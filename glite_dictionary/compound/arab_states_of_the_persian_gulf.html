Arab states of the Persian Gulf

1. NOUN

/ ˈærəb steɪts əv ðə pɜrʒən ɡʌlf / (AmE), / ˈærəb steɪts əv ðə ˈpɜːʃən ɡʌlf / (BrE)
the countries in southwestern Asia with Arab-majority populations that border the Persian Gulf, typically including Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates
Arab-majority countries bordering the Persian Gulf

Examples

Many of the Arab states of the Persian Gulf have experienced rapid economic growth due to oil exports.
The Arab states of the Persian Gulf cooperate closely on regional security issues.
Tourists are increasingly attracted to the modern cities in the Arab states of the Persian Gulf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gulf Cooperation Council - regional intergovernmental political and economic union consisting of certain Arab Persian Gulf countries
