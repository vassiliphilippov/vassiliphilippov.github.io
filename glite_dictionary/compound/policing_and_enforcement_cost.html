policing and enforcement cost

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlisɪŋ ænd ɪnˈfɔrsmənt kɑst / (AmE), / pəˈliːsɪŋ ənd ɪnˈfɔːsmənt kɒst / (BrE)
the expenses incurred to monitor compliance with a contract and to take action if one party fails to fulfill their obligations, often involving the legal system.
costs of ensuring contract compliance

Examples

The contract was carefully drafted to minimize policing and enforcement costs in case of disputes.
Effective monitoring can help reduce policing and enforcement costs in long-term business relationships.
Firms often factor in policing and enforcement cost when negotiating the terms of their agreements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transaction cost - the overall cost of completing an economic exchange, including search and information costs, bargaining costs, and enforcement costs
