off-and-on

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔf ənd ˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌɒf ənd ˈɒn / (BrE)
Happening or appearing at intervals; intermittently stopping and starting.
intermittently; not continuous

Examples

It rained off-and-on throughout the afternoon, making it hard to plan outdoor activities.
She only saw her friend off-and-on after they both started new jobs.
His interest in photography was off-and-on, and he would sometimes go months without taking a picture.

Synset Examples

fitful (or interrupted) sleep
off-and-on static

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous
  • Antonym: continuous - without interruption; unbroken
