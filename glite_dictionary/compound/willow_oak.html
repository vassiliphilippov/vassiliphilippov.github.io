willow oak

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ oʊk / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous oak tree of the eastern United States with long, narrow, willow-like leaves and soft, strong wood
oak tree with willow-like leaves

Examples

The willow oak is often planted along city streets for its attractive foliage and sturdy wood.
In autumn, the long slender leaves of the willow oak turn a soft yellow before falling.
Her backyard is shaded by a tall willow oak that was planted decades ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, known for hardwood timber and acorns
  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub with slender branches and narrow leaves, genus Salix

Etymology

Named for its willow-like leaves; 'willow' + 'oak'.
