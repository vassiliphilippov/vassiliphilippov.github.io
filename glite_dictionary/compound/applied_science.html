applied science

1. NOUN

/ əˈplaɪd ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / əˌplaɪd ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
The use of scientific knowledge and methods for practical purposes, especially in fields such as engineering and technology.
use of science for practical purposes

Examples

Many inventions are the result of breakthroughs in applied science.
She decided to specialize in applied science at university, focusing on environmental engineering.
The conference highlighted how applied science can solve real-world issues.

Synset Examples

he had trouble deciding which branch of engineering to study

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure science - science concerned with fundamental knowledge, not applications
  • Synonym: engineering - the branch of science and technology concerned with design and building

Etymology

Formed from 'applied' (from Latin 'applicare', to attach) and 'science' (from Latin 'scientia', knowledge).
