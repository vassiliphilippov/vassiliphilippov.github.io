radio observation

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ˌɑbzɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˌɒbzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
an observation of astronomical objects or phenomena made using a radio telescope that detects radio waves from space
astronomical observation with a radio telescope

Examples

The radio observation revealed new details about the distant galaxy's structure.
Scientists confirmed the source of the mysterious signal through a series of radio observations.
A successful radio observation requires precise calibration of the telescope's instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio astronomy - the study of celestial objects using radio waves
