genus Lama

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈlɑː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of South American camelids that includes the llama, alpaca, guanaco, and vicuña.
genus of South American camelids

Examples

The genus Lama includes four species of South American camelids.
Alpacas, members of the genus Lama, have been domesticated for thousands of years.
Scientists conducted a study on all animals in the genus Lama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: llama - a domesticated South American animal, member of genus Lama

Etymology

From Latin 'lama', used in scientific classification.
