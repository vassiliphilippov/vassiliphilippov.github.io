video art

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ ɑːrt / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ ɑːt / (BrE)
A form of artistic expression that uses moving images and often sound, typically produced and exhibited using video technology.
art involving moving images and sound

Examples

The museum is featuring an exhibition of contemporary video art from around the world.
She explored political themes in her video art installations.
Many emerging artists are experimenting with sound and video art to push creative boundaries.

Synset Examples

The comparison of Andrea Pozzo's illusionistic painting with today's video art aims above all at overcoming old barriers to thought and allows one to grasp the history of art as a coherent history of perception that cannot be reduced to historical classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installation art - art created for a specific site, often involving assembled media
  • Synonym: performance art - a form of art where the action of an individual or a group at a particular place and in a particular time constitutes the work
