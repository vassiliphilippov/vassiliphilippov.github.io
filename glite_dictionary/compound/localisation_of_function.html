localisation of function

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən əv ˈfʌŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən əv ˈfʌŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
The principle that specific physiological or psychological processes are associated with particular locations or regions in an organ or body part, such as the brain.
specific functions located in parts of an organ

Examples

The theory of localisation of function suggests that memory and language are controlled by specific parts of the brain.
Research into localisation of function has transformed our understanding of how the brain manages different tasks.
Some scientists argue that complete localisation of function oversimplifies how complex behaviors arise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: localization - the act or process of making something local or restricting it to a particular place

Etymology

From Latin 'localis' (of a place) and 'functio' (performance).
