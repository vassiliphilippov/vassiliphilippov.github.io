hyped up

1. ADJ

/ haɪpt ʌp / (AmE), / haɪpt ʌp / (BrE)
Presented or promoted with exaggerated or excessive publicity or enthusiasm.
excessively promoted or publicized
informal

Examples

The new smartphone was hyped up months before it was even available in stores.
Critics felt the documentary was hyped up by the media but didn't live up to expectations.
He bought the hyped up shoes, only to realize they weren't that special.

Synset Examples

The movie was hyped up before its release.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overhyped - promoted or advertised too much
  • Antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a restrained or subdued way
