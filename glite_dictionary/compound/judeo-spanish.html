Judeo-Spanish

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːdioʊ ˈspænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːdiəʊ ˈspænɪʃ / (BrE)
a dialect of Spanish traditionally spoken by Sephardic Jews, typically written in Hebrew script, also known as Ladino
Spanish dialect of Sephardic Jews

Examples

The elders in the community still speak Judeo-Spanish at home.
She is studying Judeo-Spanish to connect with her Sephardic heritage.
Many traditional songs and stories have been preserved in Judeo-Spanish.

Etymology

From 'Judeo-' (relating to Jews or Judaism) and 'Spanish', indicating its origins among Spanish-speaking Jewish communities.
