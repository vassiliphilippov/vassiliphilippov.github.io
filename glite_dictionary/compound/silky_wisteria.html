silky wisteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlki wɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlki wɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A species of wisteria native to China, known for its white, fragrant flowers and silky-haired foliage.
Chinese wisteria with white flowers

Examples

The silky wisteria in the botanical garden attracted visitors with its cascading white blossoms.
Gardeners appreciate silky wisteria for its elegant vines and delicate, fragrant flowers.
Unlike the common purple varieties, silky wisteria features clusters of white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisteria - a genus of climbing plants in the legume family with pendulous clusters of flowers
