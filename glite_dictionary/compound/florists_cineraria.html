florist's cineraria

1. NOUN

/ flɔːrɪsts sɪnəˈrɛəriə / (AmE), / flɒrɪsts sɪnəˈrɛːrɪə / (BrE)
An ornamental plant (Pericallis × hybrida), widely cultivated for its bright, profuse flowers in colors ranging from white and pink to red, purple, or blue.
ornamental plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The florist's cineraria is popular in spring gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
Grown indoors, the florist's cineraria adds a splash of blue and purple to any room.
She won a prize at the flower show for her beautiful florist's cineraria plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cineraria - a plant genus with ash-colored leaves, often referring to Pericallis species

Etymology

From 'cineraria', Latin for 'of ashes', referring to the ash-colored foliage; 'florist's' denotes its popularity as a cultivated flower.
