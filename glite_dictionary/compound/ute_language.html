Ute language

1. NOUN

/ juːt ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / juːt ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Ute people, belonging to the Shoshonean branch of the Uto-Aztecan family, spoken in parts of the western United States
language of the Ute people

Examples

She is learning to speak Ute language with the help of community elders.
The Ute language is being revitalized through immersion programs in local schools.
A dictionary of the Ute language was published to preserve the tribe's heritage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Shoshonean - branch of the Uto-Aztecan language family
  • Synonym: Ute - a member of the Native American people who speak the Ute language

Etymology

From Ute, the name of the indigenous people, combined with 'language'.
