drove chisel

1. NOUN

/ droʊv ˈtʃɪzəl / (AmE), / drəʊv ˈtʃɪzəl / (BrE)
a stonemason's chisel with a broad, flat edge used for smoothing or shaping stone surfaces
broad-edged stonemason's chisel

Examples

The mason selected a drove chisel to smooth the face of the sandstone block.
Before finishing the sculpture, she carefully worked the surface with a drove chisel.
A drove chisel is ideal for dressing and leveling larger stone areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chisel - a hand tool with a sharp, flat edge for shaping stone or wood

Etymology

From 'drove' (a row or course in stonework) and 'chisel'.
