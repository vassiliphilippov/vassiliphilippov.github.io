ground pine

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd paɪn / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd paɪn / (BrE)
A low-growing annual plant with yellow flowers marked with red, faintly aromatic of pine resin, native to Europe, the British Isles, and North Africa.
a low-growing aromatic annual plant

Examples

The ground pine gives off a light, pine-like scent as you brush past it in the meadow.
Botanists found ground pine thriving in the chalky soil of northern England.
In spring, the yellow and red flowers of ground pine brighten the coastal fields.

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd paɪn / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd paɪn / (BrE)
Any of several club mosses with long creeping stems and upright branches, especially those resembling small pine trees.
type of club moss resembling a pine

Examples

The forest floor was covered in ground pine and other low-growing plants.
Botanists collected samples of ground pine for their study of native club mosses.
Children sometimes mistake ground pine for baby Christmas trees due to its pine-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club moss - a primitive spore-bearing plant; member of the family Lycopodiaceae
