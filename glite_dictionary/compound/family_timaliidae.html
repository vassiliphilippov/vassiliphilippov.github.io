family Timaliidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli tɪˌmæliˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli tɪˌmalɪˈiːdaɪ / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized, often sociable birds known as babblers, found mainly in the Old World tropics.
family of birds called babblers

Examples

The family Timaliidae includes many species known for their chattering calls and group behavior.
Researchers discovered a new species within the family Timaliidae in Southeast Asia.
Many members of the family Timaliidae are popular among birdwatchers for their distinctive songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babbler - a small, sociable songbird of the Old World tropics

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the genus Timalia, with the suffix -idae denoting a zoological family.
