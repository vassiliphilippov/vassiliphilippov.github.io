genus Funka

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfʊŋkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈfʌŋkə / (BrE)
A genus of robust, clump-forming perennial herbs native to East Asia with racemose flowers, commonly known as plantain lilies; sometimes classified in the family Hostaceae.
genus of East Asian perennial herbs (plantain lilies)

Examples

Several species within the genus Funka are cultivated for their attractive foliage and shade tolerance.
The genus Funka is sometimes placed in the family Hostaceae, though classification may vary.
Gardeners prize members of the genus Funka for their resilience and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hosta - a plant of the same genus, widely grown as a shade-loving ornamental
