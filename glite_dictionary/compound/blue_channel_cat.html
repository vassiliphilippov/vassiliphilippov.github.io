blue channel cat

1. NOUN

/ bluː ˈtʃænəl kæt / (AmE), / bluː ˈtʃænəl kæt / (BrE)
a large species of catfish native to the Mississippi Valley, known for its bluish coloration and long whiskers
large Mississippi Valley catfish

Examples

Fishermen often hope to catch a giant blue channel cat in the Mississippi River.
The record-breaking blue channel cat weighed over 100 pounds.
As a boy, Sam learned to identify a blue channel cat by its distinctive whiskers and blue-gray color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a freshwater or marine fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
