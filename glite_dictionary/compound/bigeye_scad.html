bigeye scad

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪɡ.aɪ ˌskæd / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡ.aɪ ˌskæd / (BrE)
a species of small, silvery fish (Selar crumenophthalmus) found in Atlantic coastal waters, commonly used as bait.
a small Atlantic bait fish

Examples

Fishermen often use bigeye scad as bait to catch larger predatory fish.
The waters off the coast are abundant with bigeye scad during the spring season.
A school of bigeye scad swam past the reef, attracting several tuna.
