corporate investor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrpərət ɪnˈvɛstər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpərət ɪnˈvɛstə / (BrE)
a company that invests in or acquires control of other companies
company that invests in other companies

Examples

A major corporate investor acquired a controlling stake in the tech start-up.
The conference attracted both private individuals and large corporate investors.
As a corporate investor, the firm seeks long-term strategic gains rather than short-term profits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individual investor - a person who invests their own money, as opposed to a company
  • Synonym: shareholder - a person or organization that owns shares in a company
