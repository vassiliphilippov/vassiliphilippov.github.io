ultraviolet light

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələ t laɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt laɪt / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, often responsible for causing sunburn
form of electromagnetic radiation

Examples

Sunscreen helps protect your skin from the harmful effects of ultraviolet light.
Insects like bees can see ultraviolet light that is invisible to humans.
Special lamps emit ultraviolet light to sterilize medical equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultraviolet radiation - electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than visible light
  • Antonym: infrared - electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength longer than visible light

Etymology

From Latin 'ultra' (beyond) and 'violet' referring to light beyond the violet end of the visible spectrum.
