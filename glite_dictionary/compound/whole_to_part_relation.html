whole to part relation

1. NOUN

/ hoʊl tə pɑrt rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / həʊl tə pɑːt rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a semantic relationship in which a whole is connected to its constituent parts
semantic relationship connecting whole and parts

Examples

Linguists study the whole to part relation when analyzing how objects are described in language.
The connection between 'branch' and 'tree' is an example of a whole to part relation.
Understanding the whole to part relation helps clarify the meaning of complex terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meronymy - the semantic relationship in which a part denotes a component of something
