sign manual

1. NOUN

/ saɪn ˈmæn.ju.əl / (AmE), / saɪn ˈman.ju.əl / (BrE)
the signature of a sovereign, especially a monarch, on an official document, signifying their personal approval
signature of a monarch on an official document
legal archaic

Examples

The royal decree bore the sign manual of the queen herself.
No law was enacted without the sign manual attached to the document.
The ambassador presented a letter with the king's sign manual as proof of authenticity.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin signum manuale, meaning 'written sign by hand'.
