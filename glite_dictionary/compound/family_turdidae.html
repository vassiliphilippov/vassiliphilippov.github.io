family Turdidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmɪli tɜrˈdaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɜːˈdiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of songbirds known as thrushes; in some classifications considered a subfamily of Muscicapidae.
family of thrush birds

Examples

The family Turdidae includes familiar birds such as robins and bluebirds.
Many ornithologists debate whether the family Turdidae should be classified separately or as part of Muscicapidae.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of several species within the family Turdidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - a small to medium-sized songbird of the family Turdidae

Etymology

From New Latin Turdidae, from Turdus (Latin for thrush).
