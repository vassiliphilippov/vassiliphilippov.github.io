pneumatic tire

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmætɪk taɪr / (AmE), / njuːˈmatɪk taɪə / (BrE)
a tire made of reinforced rubber and filled with compressed air, used on vehicles such as cars and bicycles
tire filled with compressed air

Examples

Modern bicycles are equipped with pneumatic tires to provide a smoother ride over rough terrain.
The invention of the pneumatic tire revolutionized the automotive industry in the late 19th century.
If your pneumatic tire goes flat, you should check for punctures or leaks before inflating it again.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid tire - a tire not filled with air, made of solid rubber or similar material

Etymology

From Greek pneuma (air, breath), via Latin pneumaticus (of air or wind), referring to being filled with air.
