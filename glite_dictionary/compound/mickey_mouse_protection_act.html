Mickey Mouse Protection Act

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪki maʊs prəˈtɛkʃən ækt / (AmE), / ˈmɪki maʊs prəˈtɛkʃən ækt / (BrE)
an informal name for the U.S. Copyright Term Extension Act of 1998, which extended copyright protections, especially benefiting companies like Disney
U.S. copyright extension law (informal name)
informal american legal

Examples

Many legal scholars criticized the Mickey Mouse Protection Act for favoring big corporations.
The Mickey Mouse Protection Act extended copyright terms by 20 years in the United States.
Artists have debated whether the Mickey Mouse Protection Act stifles creativity or protects intellectual property.

Synset Examples

Many artists support the Mickey Mouse Protection Act.
She explained the implications of the Mickey Mouse Protection Act.
The Mickey Mouse Protection Act was created to protect copyrights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Copyright Term Extension Act - the formal name of the law extending U.S. copyright terms in 1998

Etymology

Named for the Disney character Mickey Mouse, whose copyright protection was notably extended by the act.
