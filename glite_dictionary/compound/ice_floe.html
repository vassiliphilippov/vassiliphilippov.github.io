ice floe

1. NOUN

/ aɪs floʊ / (AmE), / aɪs fləʊ / (BrE)
A flat mass of floating ice, smaller than an ice field, found in open sea or large lakes.
flat floating mass of sea ice

Examples

The ship navigated carefully to avoid the drifting ice floe in its path.
A polar bear rested on a solitary ice floe, scanning the water for seals.
Satellite images showed dozens of ice floes breaking apart each spring in the Arctic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iceberg - a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier
