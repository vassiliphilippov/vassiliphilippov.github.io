moment of inertia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv ɪnˈɜrʃə / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv ɪnˈɜːʃɪə / (BrE)
A physical property of a body that quantifies its resistance to angular acceleration, dependent on the mass distribution around an axis.
resistance to angular acceleration

Examples

The flywheel's moment of inertia allows it to keep spinning even when the engine is turned off.
Engineers increased the moment of inertia to make the satellite more stable in orbit.
A figure skater reduces her moment of inertia by pulling in her arms, spinning faster as a result.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotational inertia - resistance to change in rotational motion
  • Degree Higher: torque - a force that causes rotation

Etymology

From Latin 'momentum' (movement or significance) and 'inertia' (lack of activity or resistance to change).
