Argonne Forest

1. PROPN

/ ɑːrˈɡɑn ˈfɔrɪst / (AmE), / ɑːˈɡɒn ˈfɒrɪst / (BrE)
the site of a major World War I offensive in 1918 where American forces fought German troops
World War I battle site in France

Examples

The Battle of Argonne Forest was one of the largest operations for American troops during World War I.
Many soldiers lost their lives in the brutal fighting in the Argonne Forest in 1918.
Historians often cite the Argonne Forest offensive as a turning point in the war.

Etymology

Named after the Forest of Argonne in northeastern France, the location of the battle.
