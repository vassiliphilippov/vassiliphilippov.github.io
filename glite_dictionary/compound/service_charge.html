service charge

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of money, typically a percentage of a bill at a hotel or restaurant, added to pay for service.
percentage added to a bill for service

Examples

The restaurant automatically added a service charge to the bill for groups larger than six.
If the service charge is already included, there is no need to leave an extra tip.
Hotels in some countries include a 10% service charge in their rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a sum of money given to someone as a reward for their services

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of money paid regularly by tenants or property owners to cover costs such as electricity, lifts, and maintenance in a building or housing estate.
charge for building maintenance and services

Examples

Residents are required to pay a service charge each month to cover communal expenses.
The service charge includes the cost of cleaning and repairing the shared areas of the building.
Some tenants complained that the service charge had increased significantly this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maintenance fee - a fee paid for the upkeep of a property or facility
