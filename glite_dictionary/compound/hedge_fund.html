hedge fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒ fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒ fʌnd / (BrE)
An investment fund that uses advanced strategies, such as leverage and short-selling, and is open to a limited number of large investors.
investment fund for wealthy investors using advanced strategies

Examples

Many wealthy individuals allocate a portion of their portfolio to a hedge fund for higher returns.
The hedge fund lost millions after making a risky bet against the market.
Regulators have increased scrutiny of hedge funds following several high-profile collapses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mutual fund - an investment fund open to the general public and subject to regulations

Etymology

From 'hedge' (to limit financial risk) and 'fund' (a pool of money for investment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒ ˌfʌnd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒ ˌfʌnd / (BrE)
an investment fund typically limited to wealthy investors, using diverse and often high-risk strategies, such as short-selling and leverage, to seek high returns
high-risk investment fund for wealthy investors

Examples

After years in banking, she started her own hedge fund that specializes in emerging markets.
Many hedge funds struggled during the financial crisis due to high-risk strategies.
A minimum one-million-dollar investment is often required to join a hedge fund.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mutual fund - an investment fund open to many investors, using lower-risk strategies than a hedge fund

Etymology

From 'hedge' (to limit financial risk) and 'fund' (a pool of money for investment); financial use dates to the mid-20th century.
