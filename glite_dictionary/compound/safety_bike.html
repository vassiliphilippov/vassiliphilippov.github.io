safety bike

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti ˌbaɪk / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌbaɪk / (BrE)
a type of bicycle with two wheels of equal size and pedals connected to the rear wheel by a chain or gear mechanism, as opposed to earlier high-wheel bicycles
bicycle with equal-sized wheels and chain drive

Examples

The safety bike revolutionized personal transportation in the late nineteenth century.
Unlike the penny-farthing, the safety bike provided much greater stability for riders.
He restored a vintage safety bike for the museum's historical display.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: penny-farthing - an early bicycle with a large front wheel and a much smaller rear wheel

Etymology

Named for being a safer alternative to the 'penny-farthing' high-wheel bicycle, introduced in the late 19th century.
