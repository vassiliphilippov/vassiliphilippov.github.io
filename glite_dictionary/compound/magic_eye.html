magic eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪk aɪ / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪk aɪ / (BrE)
a transducer or indicator, often used in radio and measurement devices, that detects and shows the intensity of light or other radiation
device that measures light or radiation

Examples

The vintage radio features a magic eye to display signal strength.
Technicians used a magic eye tube to monitor the voltage levels in the circuit.
Collectors value old equipment with an intact magic eye indicator.

Etymology

So called for the glowing indicator resembling an 'eye' used in electronic devices, especially radios, from the 1930s.
