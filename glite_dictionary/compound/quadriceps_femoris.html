quadriceps femoris

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drəˌsɛps fəˈmɔr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɒ.drɪ.sɛps fəˈmɔː.rɪs / (BrE)
the large muscle group at the front of the thigh responsible for extending the leg at the knee
thigh muscle group for leg extension
medical

Examples

Strengthening your quadriceps femoris can help prevent knee injuries during sports activities.
The physical therapist recommended exercises targeting the quadriceps femoris after his knee surgery.
When you climb stairs, your quadriceps femoris muscle is heavily engaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadriceps - the large four-part muscle at the front of the thigh

Etymology

From Latin 'quadriceps' (four-headed) and 'femoris' (of the thigh).
