semicircular canal

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛm.iˈsɜr.kjələr kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌsɛm.iˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə kəˈnæl / (BrE)
any of the three curved, fluid-filled tubes in the inner ear that help maintain balance and sense of equilibrium
inner ear tube aiding balance
medical

Examples

The semicircular canal detects rotational movements of the head.
Doctors examined her inner ear, focusing on the semicircular canals to understand her balance issues.
Damage to the semicircular canal can result in dizziness and disorientation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vestibular system - sensory system contributing to balance and spatial orientation

Etymology

From Latin semi- (half) + circularis (circular) + canal (channel).
