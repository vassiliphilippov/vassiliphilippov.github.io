Overland Campaign

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌlænd kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvələnd kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
a series of major battles during the American Civil War in 1864, led by Grant against Lee, marked by heavy casualties and no decisive victor
1864 American Civil War battles (Grant vs. Lee)

Examples

The Overland Campaign was crucial in wearing down Confederate forces during 1864.
Historians often discuss the high cost in lives during the Overland Campaign.
General Grant's strategy in the Overland Campaign changed the course of the Civil War.
