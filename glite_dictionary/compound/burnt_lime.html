burnt lime

1. NOUN

/ bɝnt laɪm / (AmE), / bɜːnt laɪm / (BrE)
A white, caustic, crystalline substance (calcium oxide) produced by heating limestone, used in making cement, calcium hydroxide, and for various industrial purposes.
white crystalline oxide; calcium oxide

Examples

The workers used burnt lime to prepare mortar for the ancient stone walls.
Large quantities of burnt lime are needed in the steel manufacturing process.
Contact with water causes burnt lime to react violently, producing heat and calcium hydroxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quicklime - another name for calcium oxide
  • Degree Higher: slaked lime - calcium hydroxide, produced by adding water to burnt lime

Etymology

Term refers to limestone that has been 'burnt' (heated) to produce lime (calcium oxide).
