anise oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪs ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈænɪs ɔɪl / (BrE)
an essential oil extracted from the seeds of the anise plant, with a distinctive sweet, liquorice-like aroma and flavor
essential oil from anise seeds

Examples

Many candies and liqueurs are flavored with anise oil for its distinct taste.
Pharmacists sometimes use anise oil in cough syrups for its expectorant properties.
The strong aroma of anise oil makes it popular in aromatherapy.

Synset Examples

The most powerful flavor component of the essential oil of anise, anethole, is found in both anise and an unrelated spice called star anise (Illicium verum) widely used in South Asian, Southeast Asian, and East Asian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aniseed - the seed of the anise plant, used as a spice

Etymology

From 'anise', from Latin anisum; 'oil' from Latin oleum.
