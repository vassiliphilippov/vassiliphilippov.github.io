singletary pea

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəlˌtɛri pi / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡəltəri piː / (BrE)
A weak-stemmed winter annual plant, native to the Mediterranean region but long naturalized in the southern United States, cultivated as a cover and pasture crop.
Mediterranean-origin annual pasture/crop plant

Examples

Farmers in the South often plant singletary pea to enrich the soil during winter.
The field was covered in blooming singletary pea after the first rain.
Grazing livestock benefit from singletary pea as a nutritious winter forage.
