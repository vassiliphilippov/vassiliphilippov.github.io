organization expense

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrɡənəˈzeɪʃən ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
the costs incurred in the process of legally forming a new corporation or partnership, usually over a defined period
costs of forming a new corporation or partnership

Examples

The company recorded its organization expense as part of its initial start-up costs.
Auditors closely reviewed the organization expense to ensure compliance with accounting standards.
Many start-ups underestimate the scale of their organization expense when launching a new business.
