marsh andromeda

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ ænˈdrɑmədə / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub with clusters of white or pink hanging flowers, found in wet, acidic northern European bogs
evergreen bog shrub with hanging white or pink flowers

Examples

The marsh andromeda thrives in the acidic soils of northern peat bogs.
In spring, the marsh andromeda displays clusters of delicate pink flowers.
Researchers studied the role of marsh andromeda in wetland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin Andromeda, the plant genus, named after the mythological princess Andromeda, and 'marsh' referring to its typical habitat.
