documentary film

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːkjəˈmentəri fɪlm / (AmE), / ˌdɒkjʊˈment(ə)ri fɪlm / (BrE)
A film or television program that presents factual information about real events, people, or issues.
nonfiction film presenting facts

Examples

The documentary film explored the lives of endangered species around the world.
She directed an award-winning documentary film about the history of jazz music.
We watched a fascinating documentary film on climate change last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: docudrama - a dramatized television film based on real events
  • Antonym: feature film - a film that tells a fictional story, usually of feature length

Etymology

From 'documentary' (from French 'documentaire', from 'documenter' to document) and 'film'.
