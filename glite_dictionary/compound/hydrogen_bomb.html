hydrogen bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən bɑm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrɪdʒən bɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon that generates explosive energy by fusing hydrogen nuclei at extremely high temperatures, resulting in the formation of helium.
thermonuclear weapon

Examples

The detonation of a hydrogen bomb would cause massive destruction over a wide area.
Scientists conducted tests to measure the power of the hydrogen bomb in the 1950s.
International treaties seek to prevent the spread of hydrogen bombs to new countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic bomb - a nuclear weapon that releases energy by splitting heavy atomic nuclei (fission)
  • Synonym: thermonuclear bomb - another term for hydrogen bomb; a bomb powered by nuclear fusion
  • Antonym: conventional bomb - an explosive device that does not use nuclear reactions

Etymology

From 'hydrogen' + 'bomb', referring to the use of hydrogen nuclei in the fusion process.
