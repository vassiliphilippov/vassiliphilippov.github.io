Luwian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːwiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈluːwiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language closely related to Lycian, once spoken in ancient Anatolia
extinct Anatolian language

Examples

Scholars have discovered ancient inscriptions written in the Luwian language.
The Luwian language shares many features with other Anatolian languages such as Hittite and Lycian.
Few people today can read the hieroglyphic script of the Luwian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycian language - an extinct Anatolian language related to Luwian

Etymology

From 'Luwian', referring to the people of Luwia in ancient Anatolia.
