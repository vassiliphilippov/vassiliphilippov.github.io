cotton grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtn ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A perennial bog plant of the sedge family (genus Eriophorum), with tufts of white, cotton-like fibers at the tops of its stems, native to northern temperate regions.
bog plant with cotton-like tufts

Examples

The fields were dotted with cotton grass, swaying gently in the summer breeze.
We walked through the marsh, brushing past the soft tufts of cotton grass.
Cotton grass is a distinctive feature of peat bogs in northern Europe.
