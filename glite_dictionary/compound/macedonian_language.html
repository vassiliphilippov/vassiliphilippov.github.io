Macedonian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdəʊniən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the South Slavic language spoken mainly in North Macedonia and surrounding regions
Slavic language of North Macedonia

Examples

She learned the Macedonian language during her year abroad in Skopje.
The Macedonian language uses the Cyrillic alphabet, similar to other Slavic languages.
They discussed how the Macedonian language differs from Bulgarian and Serbian.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bulgarian language - a South Slavic language spoken primarily in Bulgaria
  • Degree Higher: Serbian language - a South Slavic language spoken mainly in Serbia

Etymology

Macedonian from Macedonia (region in Southeast Europe); see also -ian and language.
