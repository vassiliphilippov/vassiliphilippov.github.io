rank-difference correlation coefficient

1. NOUN

/ ræŋk ˈdɪf(ə)rəns kəˌroʊleɪʃn ˌkoʊɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ræŋk ˈdɪf(ə)rəns kəˌrəʊleɪʃn ˌkəʊɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to determine the degree of association between the ranks of scores on two variables
statistical measure of rank association

Examples

The rank-difference correlation coefficient is useful when analyzing non-numeric or ordinal data.
Researchers often use the rank-difference correlation coefficient to examine relationships between ranked variables.
Calculating the rank-difference correlation coefficient can help reveal monotonic associations in datasets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spearman's rank correlation coefficient - a common type of rank-difference correlation coefficient
  • Antonym: Pearson correlation coefficient - a related statistical measure for raw scores instead of ranks

Etymology

Formed from 'rank', 'difference', and 'correlation coefficient', describing the calculation method and statistical function.
