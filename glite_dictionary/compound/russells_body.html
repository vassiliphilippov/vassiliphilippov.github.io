Russell's body

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌsəlz ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈrʌsəlz ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
an inclusion body found in the cytoplasm of plasma cells, particularly in cases of certain cancers or chronic inflammatory conditions
inclusion body in plasma cells (medical)
medical

Examples

The pathologist identified a Russell's body in the biopsy sample from the patient.
Presence of multiple Russell's bodies was noted in the bone marrow smear.
A Russell's body is often associated with chronic inflammation or cancerous conditions.

Etymology

Named after British physician William Russell (1852–1940), who first described them in 1890.
