estrogen antagonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstrədʒən ænˈtæɡənɪst / (AmE), / ˈiːstrədʒən ænˈtæɡənɪst / (BrE)
A drug that blocks the activity of estrogen, often used in the treatment of estrogen-dependent breast cancer.
drug blocking estrogen, used in cancer treatment
medical

Examples

Tamoxifen is a well-known estrogen antagonist used in breast cancer therapy.
Some side effects may occur when taking an estrogen antagonist for a prolonged period.
Researchers are developing new estrogen antagonists to improve treatment outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiestrogen - a substance that inhibits the effects of estrogen

Etymology

From 'estrogen', a hormone, and 'antagonist', from Greek antagonizesthai (to struggle against).
