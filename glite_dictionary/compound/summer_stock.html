summer stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ stɑk / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə stɒk / (BrE)
theatrical productions performed by a resident company, usually in the summer, often at resorts or rural venues
theatrical productions by a resident company in summer

Examples

Many aspiring actors get their start in summer stock before moving to Broadway.
The small town comes alive each year when summer stock performances begin.
She directed three productions for summer stock last July.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock company - a repertory theatre troupe that acts as a permanent ensemble

Etymology

From 'summer' + 'stock company', where 'stock company' refers to a repertory troupe of actors.
