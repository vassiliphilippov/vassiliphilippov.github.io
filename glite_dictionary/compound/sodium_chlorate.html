sodium chlorate

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈklɔːˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈklɔːreɪt / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline salt (NaClO3) used as a weed killer and as an antiseptic
a chemical salt used as a weed killer and antiseptic

Examples

The use of sodium chlorate in agriculture is restricted due to environmental concerns.
He accidentally spilled sodium chlorate while preparing a solution for weed control.
Many industrial disinfectants contained sodium chlorate as the active ingredient.

Etymology

From sodium + chlorate, with 'chlorate' from French 'chlorate', from 'chlore' (chlorine).
