short-winded

1. ADJ

/ ʃɔrt ˈwɪndɪd / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈwɪndɪd / (BrE)
Tiring easily from exertion or being brief and lacking depth, especially in speech or writing.
easily out of breath; brief and superficial

Examples

After running up the stairs, she became short-winded and needed to rest.
His explanation was short-winded and failed to address the main issues.
As he got older, he noticed he was becoming more short-winded during exercise.

Synset Examples

He is a bit short-winded after the long run.
After jogging, I always feel a bit short-winded.
The short-winded speaker struggled to finish his talk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long-winded - talking or writing at length, often tediously so

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'long-winded', using 'short' to indicate brevity or lack of endurance.
