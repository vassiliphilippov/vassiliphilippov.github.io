baseball field

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔl ˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbɔːl ˌfiːld / (BrE)
A field on which the game of baseball is played, typically consisting of a diamond-shaped infield and a grassy outfield.
playing field for baseball

Examples

The children played catch on the baseball field after school.
The city built a new baseball field in the local park last summer.
Rain turned the baseball field into a muddy mess, delaying the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diamond - the area of a baseball field that includes the four bases and the infield
  • Synonym: ballpark - an area or stadium where baseball is played
