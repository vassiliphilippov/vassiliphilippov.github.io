burnt umber

1. NOUN

/ bɝːnt ˈʌmbɚ / (AmE), / bɜːnt ˈʌmbə / (BrE)
a medium brown to dark-brown color, typically with reddish undertones, often used in painting and art
a dark brown color

Examples

The artist mixed burnt umber with white to create a softer shade for the background.
I painted the fence a deep burnt umber to match the rustic theme of my garden.
Her dress was a rich burnt umber that complemented her complexion perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: raw umber - a natural brown earth pigment before heating

Etymology

From 'burnt', past participle of 'burn', and 'umber', from Italian 'ombra' (shade, shadow), referencing heated raw umber pigment.

2. NOUN

/ bɝːnt ˈʌmbɚ / (AmE), / bɜːnt ˈʌmbə / (BrE)
a dark brown earth pigment made by heating umber
dark brown pigment made by heating umber

Examples

The artist mixed burnt umber with other pigments to achieve a warm, rich tone.
Some shadows in the painting were deepened using layers of burnt umber.
She bought a tube of burnt umber for her next landscape artwork.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: raw umber - the natural, unheated form of the pigment umber
  • Synonym: sienna - an earth pigment containing iron oxide and manganese oxide used as a colorant

Etymology

From 'burnt', past participle of 'burn', and 'umber', from Italian 'ombra' (shade, shadow), referring to the pigment's color and method of production.
