red-green dichromacy

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈɡrin daɪˈkrɑːməsi / (AmE), / rɛd ˈɡriːn daɪˈkrɒməsi / (BrE)
A type of color vision deficiency in which a person has difficulty distinguishing between red and green hues.
type of color vision deficiency
medical

Examples

People with red-green dichromacy often cannot differentiate between similar shades of red and green.
Scientists are researching genetic therapies to address red-green dichromacy in affected individuals.
Because of his red-green dichromacy, Mark finds traffic lights challenging to interpret correctly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors, especially red and green

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) and khrōma (color), referring to seeing only two of the three basic color spectra.
