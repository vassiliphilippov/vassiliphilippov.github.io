to reverse course

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝːs kɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs kɔːs / (BrE)
to change direction, strategy, or decision, especially after having started along a particular path
change direction or decision

Examples

After several failed attempts, the company decided to reverse course and pursue a different approach.
When public opinion turned against the bill, lawmakers were forced to reverse course.
She realized her mistake halfway through and chose to reverse course before it was too late.

Synset Examples

He quickly realized he needed to reverse course before it was too late.
The government had to reverse course on the controversial policy.
After public backlash, they decided to reverse course on the plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to backtrack - to go back over the same route or reconsider decisions
  • Antonym: to stick to one's guns - to refuse to change one's decision or opinion
