point blank

1. ADV

/ pɔɪnt ˈblæŋk / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ˈblaŋk / (BrE)
in a direct and blunt manner, without evasion or hesitation
directly; bluntly

Examples

She refused to answer him point blank.
The reporter asked the politician point blank about the scandal.
He looked at me point blank and said he didn’t trust me.

Synset Examples

She refused to answer him point blank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - in a frank, straightforward manner
  • Antonym: indirectly - in a manner not direct or straightforward

Etymology

Originally from the aiming term for firing a gun at such close range that little or no aim adjustment is needed.
