wood spurge

1. NOUN

/ wʊd spɝdʒ / (AmE), / wʊd spɜːdʒ / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Euphorbia amygdaloides) with greenish-yellow clusters of flowers, typically found in woodlands.
a Eurasian perennial woodland herb

Examples

The shady corner of the garden is filled with wood spurge and ferns.
Wood spurge produces distinctive greenish-yellow flowers in the spring.
Gardeners often plant wood spurge for its colorful foliage and adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia, characterized by milky sap
