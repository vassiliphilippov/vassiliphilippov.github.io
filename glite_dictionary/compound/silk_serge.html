silk serge

1. NOUN

/ sɪlk sɜrdʒ / (AmE), / sɪlk sɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a twilled silk fabric, typically used for lining garments, especially men's coats
twilled silk fabric for garment linings

Examples

The tailor recommended silk serge as the perfect lining for my bespoke jacket.
In the museum, an ancient piece of silk serge was displayed behind glass.
He preferred suits lined with silk serge for their smooth texture and durability.

Synset Examples

The early association of silk serge, Greece, and France is shown by the discovery in Charlemagne's tomb of a piece of silk serge dyed with Byzantine motifs, evidently a gift from the Byzantine Imperial Court in the 8th or 9th century AD.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serge - a durable twilled fabric, often made from wool
  • Synonym: silk - fine, lustrous fiber produced by silkworms, often woven into textiles

Etymology

serge from Old French, from Latin 'serica' (silk), referring to twilled fabrics originally made of silk.
