halcyon days

1. NOUN

/ ˈhælsiˌɑn deɪz / (AmE), / ˈhalsɪən deɪz / (BrE)
a period of time in the past that was peaceful and happy
peaceful and happy times in the past

Examples

Many retirees look back fondly on the halcyon days of their working years.
The country has not known such halcyon days of peace and prosperity for decades.
She often told stories of the halcyon days she spent by the lake as a child.

Synset Examples

They reminisced about the halcyon days of their youth.
Her halcyon memories of summer vacations always brought her joy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden age - a period of great happiness, prosperity, or achievement

Etymology

From Greek 'alkyōn' (‘kingfisher’) by way of Latin 'halcyon', originally referring to a mythical bird said to calm the sea at midwinter.
