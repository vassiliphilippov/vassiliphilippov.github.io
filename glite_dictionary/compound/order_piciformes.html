order Piciformes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər paɪsəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə paɪsɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of birds that includes woodpeckers, jacamars, puffbirds, barbets, honeyguides, and toucans.
taxonomic order of birds (woodpeckers, toucans, etc.)

Examples

The order Piciformes comprises about 400 species, including the familiar woodpecker.
Researchers have discovered new species within the order Piciformes in South America.
Toucans and honeyguides both belong to the order Piciformes, despite their different appearances.

Etymology

From Latin 'picus' (woodpecker) + 'formes' (in the form of).
