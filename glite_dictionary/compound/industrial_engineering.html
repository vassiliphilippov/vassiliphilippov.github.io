industrial engineering

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdʌstriəl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˌdʌstri.əl ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of engineering concerned with the design, improvement, and management of integrated systems involving people, materials, and energy in productive operations
branch of engineering focused on systems and productivity

Examples

She decided to study industrial engineering because she was interested in optimizing manufacturing processes.
Many companies hire industrial engineering graduates to improve workflow efficiency.
His background in industrial engineering helped him solve complex logistical challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systems engineering - the branch of engineering dealing with complex system design and management
  • Degree Lower: mechanical engineering - the branch of engineering dealing with machines and mechanics

Etymology

From 'industrial' (relating to industry) and 'engineering'; term in use since early 20th century.
