bottom quark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəm kwɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm kwɑːk / (BrE)
a type of elementary particle, a quark with a charge of -1/3 and a mass about 10,000 times that of an electron
type of elementary particle (quark)

Examples

The discovery of the bottom quark was a significant milestone in particle physics.
Scientists study the properties of the bottom quark in high-energy collisions.
A bottom quark decays quickly into lighter particles during experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: top quark - a quark with a charge of +2/3 and the highest mass of all quarks

Etymology

Named for its position in the bottom of a classification scheme for quarks in particle physics.
