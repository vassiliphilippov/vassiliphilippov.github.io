twenty-twenty hindsight

1. NOUN

/ ˌtwɛnti ˌtwɛnti ˈhaɪndˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌtwɛnti ˌtwɛnti ˈhaɪndˌsaɪt / (BrE)
the ability to understand an event or situation clearly only after it has happened
understanding after the event

Examples

With twenty-twenty hindsight, he realized his decision to leave was premature.
We could have prevented the error, but that's easy to say with twenty-twenty hindsight.
People often offer advice using twenty-twenty hindsight rather than considering how things looked at the time.

Synset Examples

He admitted that in twenty-twenty hindsight, he made mistakes.
With twenty-twenty hindsight, we could have avoided that mess.
In twenty-twenty hindsight, I should have studied harder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hindsight - understanding of a situation only after it has happened

Etymology

From 'twenty-twenty' (20/20, normal visual acuity) and 'hindsight' (understanding of a situation after it has happened).
