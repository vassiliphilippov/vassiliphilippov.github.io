logarithmic scale

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɔːɡəˈrɪðmɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / ˌlɒɡəˈrɪðmɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale in which the actual distances from the origin are proportional to the logarithms of the corresponding values; commonly used to represent a wide range of quantities compactly
scale with distances proportional to logarithms

Examples

Earthquake magnitudes are measured on a logarithmic scale to reflect the vast range of energy released.
On a logarithmic scale, each step increases by a factor of ten rather than by a fixed amount.
The audio volume control uses a logarithmic scale to match the human ear's response to sound.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linear scale - a scale with equal distances representing equal amounts
  • Synonym: logarithm - the exponent to which a base must be raised to produce a given number

Etymology

From 'logarithm' (from Greek logos 'ratio, word' + arithmos 'number') + 'scale'.
