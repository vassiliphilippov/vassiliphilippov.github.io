genus Hakea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs həˈkiːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs həˈkiːə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs and small trees with evergreen, usually spiny leaves and dense clusters of showy flowers.
Australian genus of shrubs and trees

Examples

Botanists have discovered several new species within the genus Hakea in Western Australia.
The genus Hakea is well known for its spiny leaves and vibrant flowers.
Gardeners often choose plants from the genus Hakea for their drought resistance and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hakea - an individual plant or species of the genus Hakea

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and New Latin 'Hakea', named after German botanist Christian Ludwig Hake.
