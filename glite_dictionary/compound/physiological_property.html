physiological property

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkl ˈprɑpərti / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a characteristic or quality related to the normal functioning of a living organism or its parts
attribute related to body functioning
medical

Examples

Blood pressure is a key physiological property measured during a medical checkup.
Differences in metabolic rate illustrate how physiological properties can vary among individuals.
The study focused on the physiological property of muscle contraction in athletes.

Etymology

From Greek *physis* (nature) + *-logia* (study of) and Latin *proprietas* (ownership or quality).
