swamp gas

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˌɡæs / (AmE), / swɒmp ˌɡæs / (BrE)
a gas, chiefly methane, released from decaying organic matter in wetlands or marshy areas
methane-rich gas from wetlands

Examples

The mysterious lights were later explained by the presence of swamp gas.
Large amounts of swamp gas are produced during the decomposition of plant material in marshes.
Farmers often warn about the dangers of inhaling swamp gas near wetlands.

Synset Examples

The smell of swamp gas lingered in the air.
The scientists detected swamp gas in the marshy area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methane - a colorless, odorless flammable gas (CH4), main component of swamp gas
