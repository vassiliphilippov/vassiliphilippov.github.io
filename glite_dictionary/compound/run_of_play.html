run of play

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌn əv pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌn əv pleɪ / (BrE)
the continuous course or normal progress of a game, excluding set pieces or interruptions
normal progress of a game

Examples

During the run of play, the underdogs managed to take the lead.
His first goal came in the run of play, while his second was a penalty.
Commentators noted that most of the chances emerged from the run of play rather than set pieces.

Synset Examples

During the run of play, the team scored a surprising goal.
He scored during the run of play, not a penalty.
