spotted barramundi

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtɪd ˌbærəˈmʌndi / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd ˌbærəˈmʌndi / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish native to Australian rivers, distinguished by a red or pink spot on each scale (Scleropages leichardti)
Australian spotted freshwater fish species

Examples

The spotted barramundi is prized by anglers for its size and striking appearance.
Scientists are studying the habitat of the spotted barramundi in the Fitzroy River system.
Local guides often point out the red spots on each scale of the spotted barramundi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barramundi - a large Australian freshwater fish (Lates calcarifer)

Etymology

'Barramundi' from an Australian Aboriginal language of Queensland, meaning 'large-scaled river fish.'
