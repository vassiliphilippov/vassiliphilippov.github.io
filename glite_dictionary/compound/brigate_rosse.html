Brigate Rosse

1. PROPN

/ briˈɡɑteɪ ˈrɔseɪ / (AmE), / briˈɡɑːteɪ ˈrɒseɪ / (BrE)
An Italian Marxist-Leninist terrorist organization, active in the 1970s and 1980s, known for violent acts such as kidnappings and assassinations in pursuit of revolutionary goals.
Italian Marxist-Leninist terrorist group (Red Brigades)

Examples

The Brigate Rosse were responsible for the kidnapping and murder of Italian statesman Aldo Moro in 1978.
During the 1970s, the Brigate Rosse carried out numerous attacks against government officials and industrialists.
Reports in the media described renewed investigations into the legacy of the Brigate Rosse throughout Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Red Brigades - English name for the Brigate Rosse, Italian Marxist-Leninist terrorist group

Etymology

From Italian 'Brigate Rosse', literally 'Red Brigades'.
