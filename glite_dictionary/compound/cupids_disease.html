Cupid's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːpɪdz dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpɪdz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
an old-fashioned euphemism for syphilis, a sexually transmitted infection
euphemism for syphilis
archaic

Examples

In some Victorian novels, characters refer to syphilis as Cupid's disease to avoid explicit discussion of sexuality.
He was rumored to be suffering from Cupid's disease after years of reckless behavior.
Doctors in the 18th century sometimes whispered about cases of Cupid's disease among the aristocracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted bacterial infection

Etymology

Cupid, the Roman god of love, used humorously to refer to a disease contracted through love affairs.
