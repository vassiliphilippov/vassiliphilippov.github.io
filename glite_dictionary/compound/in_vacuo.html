in vacuo

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈvæk.wi.oʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈvæk.juː.əʊ / (BrE)
in isolation and without reference to anything else; considered separately from other factors
in isolation; considered alone

Examples

The results discussed above cannot be interpreted in vacuo, as other variables may affect them.
Philosophers rarely examine concepts in vacuo; they usually consider the broader context.
Legal rules should not be applied in vacuo without practical understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in isolation - separately, without connection to other things
  • Antonym: in situ - in its original place or position

Etymology

From Latin 'in vacuo' (in a vacuum, in emptiness).

2. ADV

/ ɪn ˈvɑːkwoʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈvækjuːəʊ / (BrE)
in a vacuum; without external factors or outside influences
without external factors or context

Examples

The samples were analyzed in vacuo to prevent any atmospheric interference.
You can't evaluate the policy in vacuo; real-world conditions must be considered.
Some chemical reactions only occur efficiently in vacuo, away from moisture and air.

Synset Examples

Her theory was tested in vacuo, without outside influences.
The experiment was conducted in vacuo to avoid contamination.
He's thinking in vacuo, ignoring practical applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in a vacuum - existing or occurring in isolation from external factors
  • Antonym: in situ - in its original place or position

Etymology

From Latin 'in' (in) and 'vacuō', ablative of 'vacuus' (empty, void).
