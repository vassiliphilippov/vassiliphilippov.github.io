language system

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
The set of linguistic units and rules that make up a particular language.
structure and rules of a language

Examples

The English language system includes complex rules for verb conjugation and word order.
Linguists study how children acquire the language system of their native tongue.
Every language system has its own unique set of phonological and grammatical rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grammar - the whole system and structure of a language
