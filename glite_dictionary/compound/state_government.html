state government

1. NOUN

/ steɪt ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / steɪt ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
the government of one of the states in the United States, responsible for governing within that state's territory
government of a U.S. state
american

Examples

The state government is responsible for maintaining public schools and highways within its borders.
After the election, the new state government implemented several reforms to improve public health.
Each state government in the U.S. has its own constitution and legislative body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federal government - the national government of the United States
