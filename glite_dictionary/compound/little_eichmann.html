little Eichmann

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.əl ˈaɪk.mən / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl ˈaɪk.mən / (BrE)
a person who follows orders blindly and without moral reflection, especially in a bureaucratic or institutional context
one who unquestioningly obeys orders

Examples

Some described the mid-level manager as a little Eichmann for enforcing harmful company policies without question.
The whistleblower warned that organizations risk breeding little Eichmanns if employees stop questioning their actions.
He dismissed his role in the scandal, insisting he was just a little Eichmann doing his job.

Synset Examples

Critics called him a little Eichmann for following orders blindly.
He was labeled a little Eichmann for his unethical decisions.
She viewed him as a little Eichmann in the corporate world.

Etymology

Coined after Adolf Eichmann, the Nazi official tried for war crimes, symbolizing the idea of the 'banality of evil.'
