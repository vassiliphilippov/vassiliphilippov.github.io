Vatican I

1. PROPN

/ ˌvætɪkən ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌvætɪkən ˈwʌn / (BrE)
the first Vatican Council (1869–1870), which proclaimed the doctrine of papal infallibility when the pope speaks ex cathedra
the first Vatican Council (1869–1870)

Examples

The doctrine of papal infallibility was formally established at Vatican I.
Many historians consider Vatican I to be a pivotal event in modern Church history.
The discussions at Vatican I lasted from 1869 to 1870.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Vatican II - the second Vatican Council (1962–1965), a major Roman Catholic council

Etymology

From Vatican (the site in Rome where the council convened) and Roman numeral I, indicating the first such council held at the Vatican.
