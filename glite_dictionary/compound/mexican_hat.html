Mexican hat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛksɪkən hæt / (AmE), / ˈmɛksɪkən hæt / (BrE)
A North American coneflower (Ratibida columnifera) with flower heads that resemble a traditional Mexican hat, featuring a tall red-brown central disk and drooping yellow or red-brown petals.
North American coneflower with distinctive head shape

Examples

A field of Mexican hats added vibrant colors to the prairie landscape.
Gardeners like to plant Mexican hat for its unique shape and drought resistance.
The Mexican hat attracts butterflies and bees throughout the summer.

Etymology

So named for its resemblance to the broad-brimmed Mexican sombrero.
