intensive care unit

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnsɪv kɛr ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪv keə ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a special department in a hospital that provides intensive care and constant monitoring for seriously ill patients
hospital department for critical care
medical

Examples

After his heart attack, he was moved to the intensive care unit for close monitoring.
Visitors are only allowed at certain hours in the intensive care unit.
The nurses in the intensive care unit are specially trained to handle emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ICU - abbreviation for 'intensive care unit'

Etymology

From 'intensive care' (specialized medical care) + 'unit' (division within a hospital).
