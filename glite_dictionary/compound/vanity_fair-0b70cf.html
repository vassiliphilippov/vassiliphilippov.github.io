Vanity Fair

1. PROPN

/ ˈvænəti fɛr / (AmE), / ˈvænɪti feə / (BrE)
a popular magazine focusing on celebrity news, fashion, culture, and current affairs
magazine on celebrities, fashion, and culture

Examples

Her photographs were published in Vanity Fair last month.
Many readers turn to Vanity Fair for insightful celebrity interviews and profiles.
The fashion spread in Vanity Fair inspired new trends among designers.

Synset Examples

The celebrity was featured on the cover of Vanity Fair.
He enjoyed the articles in Vanity Fair about fashion trends.

Etymology

Named after the fictional place in John Bunyan's 1678 novel 'The Pilgrim's Progress' and later used as the title of a novel by William Makepeace Thackeray.
