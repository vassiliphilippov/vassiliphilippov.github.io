Nestorian belief

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈstɔriən bəˈlif / (AmE), / nɛˈstɔːriən bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
the doctrines or theological principles associated with Nestorianism, a Christian sect that emphasized the distinction between the human and divine natures of Jesus Christ
doctrines of the Nestorian Christian sect

Examples

The council condemned the Nestorian belief as heretical in 431 AD.
Scholars continue to debate the influence of Nestorian belief on early Christian thought.
Some churches in the Middle East retain elements of Nestorian belief today.

Synset Examples

Nestorian beliefs influenced early Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nestorianism - the doctrine stressing the independence of the divine and human persons of Jesus
  • Antonym: orthodoxy - correct or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice

Etymology

From Nestorius, a 5th-century Archbishop of Constantinople, whose teachings led to the Nestorian controversy.
