tooth fungus

1. NOUN

/ tuːθ ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / tuːθ ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
Any fungus belonging to the family Hydnaceae, characterized by spore-bearing structures that resemble teeth or spines.
fungus of the Hydnaceae family

Examples

The hedgehog mushroom is a type of tooth fungus commonly found in forests.
Scientists discovered a rare tooth fungus species during their expedition.
Unlike most mushrooms, a tooth fungus has spines instead of gills under its cap.

Etymology

Named for the tooth-like or spiny projections on the fruiting bodies of these fungi.
