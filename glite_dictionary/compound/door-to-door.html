door-to-door

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔr tə ˈdɔr / (AmE), / ˈdɔː tə ˈdɔː / (BrE)
(of activity or service) involving visits made to each house or from the entrance of one house to the next, without omitting any.
involving visits to each house

Examples

The company is conducting a door-to-door survey to gather opinions from all residents.
He worked as a door-to-door salesman, selling vacuum cleaners in the suburbs.
A door-to-door vaccination campaign helped stop the spread of disease in the village.

Synset Examples

a door-to-door campaign
house-to-house coverage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house-to-house - involving visits or coverage from one house to the next
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00529727-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɔr tə dɔr / (AmE), / dɔː tə dɔː / (BrE)
(of services, travel, or deliveries) going directly from the point of origin to the final destination without intermediate stops.
from origin to destination directly

Examples

The company offers a door-to-door delivery service for all its online customers.
Her commute is made easy by the door-to-door shuttle bus provided by the university.
He works as a door-to-door salesperson, visiting households every day.

Synset Examples

the limousine offers door-to-door service
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00764804-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌdɔr tə ˈdɔr / (AmE), / ˌdɔː tə ˈdɔː / (BrE)
involving or relating to visiting each home in an area, especially to sell something or make inquiries
visiting each home (sales, canvassing, etc.)

Examples

They went door-to-door trying to get signatures for their petition.
Door-to-door sales can be a challenging but rewarding job.
Charity volunteers collected donations door-to-door throughout the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

The sales rep would peddle the latest gadgets door-to-door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house-to-house - from one house to the next, especially for searching or canvassing
