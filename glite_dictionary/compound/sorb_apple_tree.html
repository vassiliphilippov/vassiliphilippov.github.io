sorb apple tree

1. NOUN

/ sɔrb ˈæp.əl ˌtri / (AmE), / sɔːb ˈæp.əl ˌtriː / (BrE)
A medium-sized European tree, related to the rowan, that bears edible fruit.
European tree with edible fruit

Examples

The sorb apple tree produces fruit that can be eaten when fully ripe.
Traditional European orchards sometimes include the sorb apple tree for its unique berries.
A mature sorb apple tree can grow up to 15 meters tall in favorable conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'sorbus', the name of the tree in classical sources.
