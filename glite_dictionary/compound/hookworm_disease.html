hookworm disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkˌwɜrm dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈhʊkˌwɜːm dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation of the intestines by hookworms, typically entering the body through the skin.
intestinal disease caused by hookworms
medical

Examples

Many people in tropical regions suffer from hookworm disease due to poor sanitation.
Regular deworming programs have helped to reduce cases of hookworm disease in children.
Walking barefoot in contaminated soil increases the risk of contracting hookworm disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hookworm - a parasitic nematode worm causing hookworm disease
