chloral hydrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔrəl haɪˌdreɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəl ˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
A colorless crystalline compound used as a sedative and hypnotic drug, which can irritate the stomach and be habit-forming.
sedative and hypnotic drug
medical

Examples

The patient was given chloral hydrate to help induce sleep before surgery.
Due to its side effects, chloral hydrate is less commonly prescribed today.
Doctors monitor patients closely when administering chloral hydrate to avoid potential dependence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a substance that induces sedation by reducing irritability or excitement
  • Synonym: hypnotic - a drug that induces sleep

Etymology

From 'chloral' and 'hydrate', reflecting its chemical composition as the hydrate of chloral.
