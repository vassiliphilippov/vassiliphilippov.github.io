point woman

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt ˈwʊmən / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ˈwʊmən / (BrE)
a female soldier who goes ahead of a patrol, acting as the lead in scouting for danger
female soldier leading a patrol

Examples

The platoon assigned their most experienced point woman to lead them through the forested area.
As the point woman, she was the first to spot signs of enemy movement.
During training exercises, several soldiers practiced taking the point woman position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: point man - a soldier who goes ahead of a patrol

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'point man', with 'woman' indicating a female in the leading role.

2. NOUN

/ pɔɪnt ˈwʊmən / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ˈwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who leads or is at the forefront of an important enterprise or movement.
woman at the forefront of an enterprise

Examples

As the point woman on the project, she coordinated all the major initiatives and led the team to success.
During the negotiations, Ellen acted as the point woman for her company, handling the most critical discussions.
The committee appointed her as their point woman to spearhead the new fundraising campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: point man - person at the forefront of an enterprise

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'point man', substituting 'woman' to specify gender.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
