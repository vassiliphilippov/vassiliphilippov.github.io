elephant's ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənts ɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənts ɪə / (BrE)
a tropical plant of the genus Alocasia, known for its large, showy, basal leaves and boat-shaped flowers with reddish berries
tropical plant with large leaves (Alocasia)

Examples

The elephant's ear adds a dramatic tropical look to the garden with its huge leaves.
Many indoor plant enthusiasts choose elephant's ear for its striking foliage.
After repotting, the elephant's ear grew even larger and produced new leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfənts ɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfənts ɪə / (BrE)
A tropical South American tree with wide-spreading bipinnate leaves and coiled, ear-shaped fruits, grown for shade, ornament, and timber.
tropical tree with large leaves and ear-shaped fruits

Examples

In the park's center, a towering elephant's ear provided welcome shade from the afternoon sun.
The wood of the elephant's ear is highly prized in furniture making and construction.
Botanists studied the unique, coiled fruits of the elephant's ear tree growing along the riverbank.
