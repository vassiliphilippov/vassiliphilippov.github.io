great barracuda

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt bəˈrɑːk(j)uːdə / (AmE), / ɡreɪt bəˈræk.juː.də / (BrE)
a large, predatory tropical fish (Sphyraena barracuda) noted for its size, speed, and sharp teeth, sometimes considered dangerous to swimmers and sought after as a food and game fish
large tropical predatory fish

Examples

The great barracuda is known for its powerful jaws and swift attacks on prey.
Divers often spot a great barracuda patrolling coral reefs in tropical waters.
Fishermen prize the great barracuda for its fight and impressive size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracuda - a large, elongated predatory fish found in warm seas
