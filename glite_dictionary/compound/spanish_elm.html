Spanish elm

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ ɛlm / (BrE)
A tropical American timber tree (Cordia alliodora) valued for its strong, durable wood.
tropical American timber tree

Examples

The wood of the Spanish elm is highly prized for furniture making.
Several Spanish elms lined the avenue, providing ample shade.
Local carpenters prefer using Spanish elm because of its durability.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ ɛlm / (BrE)
A large tropical American tree of the genus Cordia, grown for its abundant creamy white flowers and valuable wood.
a tropical American tree (Cordia) with valuable wood

Examples

The Spanish elm is often prized by woodworkers for its strong, fine-grained timber.
During our trip to the Caribbean, we admired the creamy white flowers of the Spanish elm.
Many city parks in tropical regions plant Spanish elms for their shade and beauty.
