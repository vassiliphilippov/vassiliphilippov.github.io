Broca's gyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkəz ˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkəz ˈdʒʌɪrəs / (BrE)
the region of the brain in the left frontal lobe, usually responsible for speech production in most people
brain region for speech production
medical

Examples

Damage to the Broca's gyrus can result in difficulty forming coherent speech.
Researchers studied how the Broca's gyrus is activated during language tasks.
The surgeon carefully avoided the Broca's gyrus to preserve the patient's speech abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Broca's area - region in the frontal lobe involved in speech production

Etymology

Named after French physician Paul Broca (1824–1880), who discovered its role in speech.
