brain trust

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪn ˌtrʌst / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌtrʌst / (BrE)
A group of expert advisors, especially unofficial ones, who help a leader or organization, particularly in political or governmental contexts.
group of expert (often unofficial) advisors

Examples

The president consulted his brain trust before making the final decision on foreign policy.
Many companies rely on a brain trust of innovators to stay ahead of competitors.
During the crisis, he gathered a brain trust to develop a comprehensive solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisory board - a board that provides advice and guidance to an organization

Etymology

Originally used for Franklin D. Roosevelt’s circle of advisors in the 1930s, from 'brain' (intellect) and 'trust' (group organized for a purpose).
