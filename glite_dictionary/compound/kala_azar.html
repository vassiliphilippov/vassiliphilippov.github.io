kala azar

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːlə əˈzɑr / (AmE), / ˌkɑːlə əˈzɑː / (BrE)
A severe tropical disease caused by protozoa of the genus Leishmania, transmitted by sandflies, characterized by fever, weight loss, and skin sores.
tropical disease caused by Leishmania parasites
medical

Examples

The region has seen an increase in cases of kala azar due to poor sanitation.
Doctors are working hard to find better treatments for kala azar.
After months of unexplained fever, the patient was finally diagnosed with kala azar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - infection caused by Leishmania parasites

Etymology

From Hindi *kālā āzār* meaning 'black fever'.
