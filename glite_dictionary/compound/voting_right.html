voting right

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtɪŋ raɪt / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtɪŋ raɪt / (BrE)
the legal right to participate in a vote, especially to cast a ballot in an election or to vote on matters in a corporate setting as a shareholder
the legal right to participate in voting

Examples

Every citizen over the age of eighteen has the voting right in national elections.
The company offers each shareholder a voting right based on the number of shares owned.
Protests were held demanding equal voting rights for all members of the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
