primary color

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
any of three colors (red, blue, and yellow) that can be combined to produce a broad range of other colors, and from which all other colors for pigments can be obtained by mixing
basic color in pigment mixing

Examples

Red, blue, and yellow are considered primary colors for pigments in painting.
Artists often start by mixing primary colors to create a wide range of hues.
Learning to identify the primary colors is fundamental in any basic art class.

Synset Examples

the primary colors for pigments are red, blue, and yellow

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secondary color - a color produced by mixing two primary colors

Etymology

From Latin 'primarius' (principal, of the first rank) and Middle English 'colour'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.məri ˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
any of a group of colors from which all other colors can be obtained by mixing, usually red, blue, and yellow (for pigments) or red, green, and blue (for light)
color from which others are mixed

Examples

Red, yellow, and blue are each considered a primary color in traditional painting.
You can create a variety of hues by mixing any two primary colors together.
Understanding the theory of primary colors is essential for any aspiring artist.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secondary color - a color made by mixing two primary colors
