water elm

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ɛlm / (BrE)
A large ornamental tree (Planera aquatica) with graceful, spreading branches, commonly found in eastern North America, especially in wet habitats.
ornamental tree native to eastern North America

Examples

The water elm thrives in moist soils along riverbanks and swamps.
Gardeners often plant the water elm for its attractive, spreading branches.
In some regions, the water elm is a common sight near ponds and streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planertree - another name for water elm, referring to the same tree (Planera aquatica)

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ɛlm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ɛlm / (BrE)
A species of elm tree closely resembling the American elm, native to Eurasia and thriving in moist environments.
a Eurasian elm tree found in moist habitats

Examples

The water elm thrives along riverbanks and in other wetland areas.
Botanists often compare the water elm to its American counterpart due to their similar appearance.
In spring, the leaves of the water elm provide shade and shelter for local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American elm - a common North American species of elm tree
