black hickory

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / blæk ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
an American hickory tree (Carya texana) with dark bark and bitter nuts, native to the south-central United States
a species of American hickory with bitter nuts

Examples

The leaves of the black hickory are typically narrower than those of other hickories.
Wildlife generally avoid eating the bitter nuts produced by the black hickory.
The wood of the black hickory is valued for its strength and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of tree in the walnut family with hard wood and edible nuts

3. NOUN

/ blæk ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / blak ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
a smooth-barked North American hickory tree (Carya texana) with 7 to 9 leaflets and a hard-shelled edible nut
North American hickory tree species

Examples

The black hickory produces a hard-shelled nut that is valued for its taste and nutrition.
Many furniture makers prize the wood of the black hickory for its strength and durability.
A grove of black hickory trees lines the edge of the old country road.
