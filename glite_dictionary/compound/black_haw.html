black haw

1. NOUN

/ blæk hɑ / (AmE), / blæk hɔː / (BrE)
A North American deciduous shrub (Viburnum prunifolium) with dark-blue, frosted fruit and white flowers, often grown for its ornamental value.
North American deciduous shrub with blue fruit

Examples

The black haw blooms in late spring, covering its branches with creamy white flowers.
Birds are attracted to the frosted blue berries of the black haw.
She planted a black haw in her garden for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

2. NOUN

/ blæk hɔ / (AmE), / blæk hɔː / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree of the southeastern United States and Mexico, noted for its clusters of white flowers and blue-black fruit.
deciduous tree of the southeastern US and Mexico

Examples

The black haw produces clusters of edible berries in late summer.
In the wild, black haw is commonly found along woodland edges and streams.
Gardeners value the black haw for its attractive spring flowers and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Viburnum prunifolium - the scientific name for black haw
