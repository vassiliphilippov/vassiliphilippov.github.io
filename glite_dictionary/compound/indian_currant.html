Indian currant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
A North American deciduous shrub (Symphoricarpos orbiculatus) cultivated for its abundant clusters of coral-red, berrylike fruits.
North American shrub with coral-red fruits

Examples

The garden was bordered by a row of Indian currant shrubs, each laden with bright red berries.
She planted an Indian currant to attract birds and add color to her backyard in autumn.
Many native plant enthusiasts recommend Indian currant for its ornamental value and resilience.
