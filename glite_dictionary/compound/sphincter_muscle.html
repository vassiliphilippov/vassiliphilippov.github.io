sphincter muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪŋktər ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈsfɪŋktə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
A ring-shaped muscle that contracts to close or restrict an opening in the body.
ring-shaped muscle that closes openings
medical

Examples

The sphincter muscle controls the release of urine from the bladder.
Failure of the sphincter muscle can lead to digestive problems.
After surgery, her sphincter muscle needed time to regain full function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphincter - a ring of muscle that contracts to close a bodily opening

Etymology

From Greek 'sphinktēr' (one who binds tight) and Latin 'musculus' (little mouse, muscle).
