sinking fund

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkɪŋ fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ fʌnd / (BrE)
A fund set aside and accumulated over time to repay a debt or replace a large expenditure, especially by regular payments into a separate account.
fund for repaying debt or liability

Examples

The company established a sinking fund to ensure it could retire its bonds on schedule.
Most municipalities maintain a sinking fund to pay off long-term loans gradually.
Investors are attracted to bonds with a sinking fund provision because of the reduced risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt repayment - the act of paying back borrowed money

Etymology

From the verb 'sink' (to pay off or reduce, as in sinking a debt) + 'fund'; term in use since the 18th century.
