balsam poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑl.səm ˈpɑ.plɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.səm ˈpɒ.plə / (BrE)
a species of poplar tree native to northeastern North America, known for its broad, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant resinous buds
a North American poplar tree species

Examples

The balsam poplar is easily recognized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves.
In spring, sticky resin from the balsam poplar buds fills the air with a sweet scent.
Loggers often use wood from the balsam poplar for pulp and construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a type of tall, fast-growing tree of the genus Populus

Etymology

From balsam, referring to the fragrant resin, and poplar, from Old French 'poplier'.
