white separatism

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈsɛp.əˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsɛp.ər.ɪt.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The belief in or advocacy for the separation of White people from people of other races, often supporting the establishment of separate communities or institutions.
belief in racial separation for Whites

Examples

The group's advocacy for white separatism has been widely condemned by civil rights organizations.
Some communities have a troubling history of white separatism that affected local policies and social relations.
Discussions about white separatism often arise in debates on racial equality and freedom of association.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segregation - the enforced separation of different racial groups; especially as practiced in the United States
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining racial groups into a whole community, especially in society or schools
