hot stove

1. NOUN

/ hɑt stoʊv / (AmE), / hɒt stəʊv / (BrE)
A stove that is currently heated and is likely to cause burns if touched; sometimes used metaphorically to refer to a dangerous or risky situation.
a stove that is heated; risky situation (metaphor)

Examples

She reminded her son not to touch the hot stove while she was cooking.
Learning from his mistakes was like getting burned by a hot stove—he wouldn't repeat them.
After burning my hand on the hot stove once, I always check with caution.

Synset Examples

She warned him about the hot stove in the kitchen.
Be careful, the hot stove can burn you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stove - a device used for heating or cooking
  • Degree Higher: hot - having a high temperature
  • Antonym: cold stove - a stove that is not heated
