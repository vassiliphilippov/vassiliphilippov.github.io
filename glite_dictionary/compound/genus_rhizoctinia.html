genus Rhizoctinia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs raɪˌzɒkˈtɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs rɪˌzɒkˈtɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
a form genus of imperfect fungi, some species of which have been reclassified into the genera Pellicularia and Corticium after their perfect stages were identified.
form genus of imperfect fungi

Examples

The genus Rhizoctinia includes several fungi that attack the roots of plants.
Mycology students studied the classification changes within the genus Rhizoctinia.
Because its perfect stage was identified, one species was moved from the genus Rhizoctinia to Pellicularia.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) and 'ktinein' (to kill), referring to root-rotting tendency.
