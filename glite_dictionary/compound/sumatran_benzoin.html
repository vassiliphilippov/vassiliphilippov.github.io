Sumatran benzoin

1. NOUN

/ suˈmɑːtrən bɛnˈzoʊɪn / (AmE), / suˈmɑːtrən bɛnˈzəʊɪn / (BrE)
a type of fragrant resin obtained from Styrax benzoin trees, mainly grown on the island of Sumatra, used in perfumes and medicines
fragrant resin from Sumatra used in perfumes and medicine

Examples

Pharmacists often use Sumatran benzoin in the formulation of skin protectants.
The distinctive aroma of Sumatran benzoin makes it popular in high-quality perfumes.
Traditional medicine in Southeast Asia has made use of Sumatran benzoin for centuries.

Synset Examples

Benzoin Sumatra is used in pharmaceutical preparations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoin - a resin obtained from certain trees, used in perfumes and medicine

Etymology

From 'Sumatran', referring to Sumatra, the Indonesian island, and 'benzoin', from Medieval Latin 'benzoe', of Arabic origin.
