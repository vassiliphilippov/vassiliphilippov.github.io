aldermanic position

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔl.dɚˈmæn.ɪk pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɔːl.dəˈmæn.ɪk pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
An elected office or role held by an alderman in local government.
elected office of an alderman

Examples

She decided to stand for the aldermanic position in her district.
Securing an aldermanic position gave him a platform to address city issues.
Competition for the open aldermanic position was fierce during the recent elections.

Synset Examples

She ran for an aldermanic position to help her neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alderman - a member of a municipal legislative body

Etymology

From 'alderman' (from Old English 'ealdorman', meaning elder or chief) and 'position'.
