miscegenation law

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈdʒɛnəˌʃən lɔ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈdʒɛnɪˌʃən lɔː / (BrE)
a law prohibiting marriage or sexual relations between people of different races, especially as enacted in the United States before being declared unconstitutional
law prohibiting interracial marriage
legal

Examples

Many states in the early 20th century enforced miscegenation laws to prevent interracial marriages.
The Supreme Court declared miscegenation laws unconstitutional in the landmark Loving v. Virginia case.
Historians study the effects of miscegenation laws on family life and civil rights movements.

Synset Examples

They fought against miscegenation laws in their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-miscegenation law - a law prohibiting interracial marriage or relationships

Etymology

From miscegenation (from Latin miscere 'to mix' + genus 'race, kind') + law.
