Italian lira

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌtæl.jən ˈli.rə / (AmE), / ɪˌtæl.jən ˈlɪə.rə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Italy, equal to 100 centesimi, replaced by the euro in 2002
former currency of Italy

Examples

When the euro was introduced, the Italian lira was phased out as legal tender.
Old banknotes of the Italian lira are now collectors' items.
She remembers traveling in Rome when everything was priced in Italian lira.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: euro - the official currency of the eurozone, which replaced the Italian lira

Etymology

From Italian 'lira', from Latin 'libra' (a pound), also related to 'lira' used as a currency in several countries.
