resurrection fern

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən fɜrn / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈrɛkʃən fɜːn / (BrE)
a species of fern that grows on rocks or tree trunks, notable for its ability to survive dry periods by appearing dead and then reviving with moisture; found in the Americas and South Africa
fern surviving drought by reviving with moisture

Examples

The resurrection fern can appear brown and lifeless during droughts, but revives quickly after rainfall.
Scientists study the resurrection fern to understand its remarkable drought resistance.
There's a resurrection fern growing on that oak tree, clinging to the bark even through harsh summers.

Etymology

Named for its ability to revive after drying out, from 'resurrection' (rising from the dead) and 'fern'.
