antihemophilic factor

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.hɪˈmoʊ.fɪ.lɪk ˈfæk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.hiːˈməʊ.fɪ.lɪk ˈfæk.tə / (BrE)
a protein present in blood that is essential for normal blood clotting, whose deficiency causes hemophilia A; also known as Factor VIII
blood-clotting protein (Factor VIII)
medical

Examples

The patient receives regular infusions of antihemophilic factor to manage his hemophilia A.
A deficiency of antihemophilic factor leads to impaired blood clotting and increased bleeding risk.
Researchers are developing recombinant forms of antihemophilic factor to improve treatment options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Factor VIII - protein essential for blood clotting; another name for antihemophilic factor

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against), 'hemophilic' (relating to hemophilia), indicating its activity in counteracting hemophilia.
