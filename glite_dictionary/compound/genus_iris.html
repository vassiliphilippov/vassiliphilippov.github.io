genus Iris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈaɪərɪs / (BrE)
A large group of perennial flowering plants, including irises, that grow from bulbs or rhizomes.
group of perennial plants (irises)

Examples

The genus Iris includes some of the most popular ornamental garden flowers.
Many species within the genus Iris are prized for their vibrant, colorful blooms.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the genus Iris in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - any plant of the genus Iris

Etymology

From Latin 'Iris', the name of the plant and the Greek goddess of the rainbow.
