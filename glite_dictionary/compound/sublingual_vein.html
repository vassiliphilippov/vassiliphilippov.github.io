sublingual vein

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈlɪŋɡwəl veɪn / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɪŋɡwəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein located beneath the tongue that is a branch of the lingual vein
vein under the tongue
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the sublingual vein for any signs of swelling or discoloration.
A prominent sublingual vein may sometimes be visible under the surface of the tongue.
In rare cases, the sublingual vein can be used for the administration of certain medications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingual vein - a vein located in the tongue

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'lingua' (tongue).
