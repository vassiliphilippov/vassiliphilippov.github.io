perineal artery

1. NOUN

/ pɛr.ɪˈni.əl ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˈniː.əl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
A branch of the internal pudendal artery that supplies blood to the superficial structures of the perineum.
blood vessel to perineum
medical

Examples

During surgery, the perineal artery must be carefully identified to prevent excessive bleeding.
The perineal artery supplies blood to the skin and muscles of the perineum.
Damage to the perineal artery can compromise circulation in the lower pelvic region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: internal pudendal artery - main artery supplying the perineum and external genitalia

Etymology

perineal from Latin *perinaeum*, referring to the perineum; artery from Greek *arteria* (windpipe, later 'artery').
