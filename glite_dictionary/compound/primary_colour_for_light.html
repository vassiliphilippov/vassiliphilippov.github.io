primary colour for light

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkʌlɚ fər laɪt / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ˈkʌlə fə laɪt / (BrE)
any of three fundamental colors of light (red, green, and blue) from which all other colors can be created by additive mixing
basic light color in additive color mixing

Examples

When mixing stage lighting, designers start with the primary colours for light to create a range of effects.
Television screens use the primary colours for light to display every hue you see.
Understanding how primary colours for light combine is essential for anyone working in digital media.

Synset Examples

the primary colors for light are red, blue, and green

Related Terms
