family Balanidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌbæləˈnaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌbæləˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sessile (stalkless) barnacles commonly found attached to rocks and other surfaces in marine environments.
a family of stalkless barnacles

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species within the family Balanidae along the coastal reefs.
The hard shells of barnacles in the family Balanidae protect them from predators and harsh conditions.
Many marine biologists study the family Balanidae to understand sessile crustacean evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a type of marine crustacean with a hard shell, often attached to rocks

Etymology

From New Latin 'Balanidae', from 'Balanus', the type genus, ultimately from Greek 'balanos' meaning 'acorn' (referring to barnacle's shape).
