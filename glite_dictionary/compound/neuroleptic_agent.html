neuroleptic agent

1. NOUN

/ nʊˌroʊˈlɛptɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / njʊəˌrəʊˈlɛptɪk ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug that reduces nervous tension and is used to treat psychotic disorders by producing a calming effect
drug for treating psychosis with calming effect
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a neuroleptic agent to help control the patient's hallucinations.
Chronic use of a neuroleptic agent may have side effects like drowsiness or muscle stiffness.
Psychiatrists often rely on a neuroleptic agent for managing acute psychotic episodes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - drug used to manage psychosis, especially in schizophrenia

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and 'lēptikos' (taking hold of); 'agent' from Latin 'agens' (doing).
