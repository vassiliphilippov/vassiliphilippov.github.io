single nucleotide polymorphism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˈnuːkli.oʊˌtaɪd pəˈlɪmɚˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˈnjuːkliəˌtaɪd pəˈlɪməˌfɪzəm / (BrE)
A genetic variation in which a single nucleotide in the genome differs between members of a species or paired chromosomes, commonly referred to as SNP.
genetic variation in a single DNA base
medical

Examples

A single nucleotide polymorphism can help scientists identify genes associated with disease.
Researchers discovered a single nucleotide polymorphism linked to increased risk of diabetes.
The study examined how a single nucleotide polymorphism affects immune system response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SNP - abbreviation for single nucleotide polymorphism

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many), 'morph' (form), referring to variation; 'nucleotide' from 'nucleus' and 'tide' as in sugar-phosphate units.
