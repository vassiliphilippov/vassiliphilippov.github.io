Arctic fox

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrktɪk fɑks / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk fɒks / (BrE)
a fox native to Arctic regions, with thick fur that is brownish in summer and white in winter
a small fox of Arctic regions with changing fur color

Examples

The Arctic fox is well adapted to survive the freezing temperatures of the tundra.
The fur of the Arctic fox changes from brown to white as winter approaches.
Researchers observed several Arctic foxes near the icy shoreline, hunting for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red fox - a common species of fox with reddish fur
