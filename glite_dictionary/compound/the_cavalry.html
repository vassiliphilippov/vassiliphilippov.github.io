the cavalry

1. NOUN

/ ðə ˈkævəlri / (AmE), / ðə ˈkævəlri / (BrE)
used humorously to refer to help or backup arriving, especially in a difficult or critical situation
help or backup arriving in crisis

Examples

Whenever we face a tight deadline, we call in the cavalry from other departments.
Just when the negotiations fell apart, the cavalry finally arrived to resolve things.
He looked hopeless until the cavalry showed up with the correct documents.

Synset Examples

Just when we thought we were losing, the cavalry arrived.
We need the cavalry to support us in this project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reinforcements - additional people or resources sent to support or help

Etymology

From 'cavalry', originally denoting soldiers on horseback; extended metaphorically to any rescuing group.
