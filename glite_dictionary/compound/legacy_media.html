legacy media

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛɡəsi ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡəsi ˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
traditional forms of mass media, such as newspapers, radio, and television, in contrast to newer digital or online platforms
traditional, non-digital media like newspapers and TV

Examples

Despite the growth of online news sites, many people still rely on legacy media for trustworthy information.
Some politicians criticize legacy media for being slow to adapt to the digital age.
Advertisers are shifting their budgets away from legacy media and toward online platforms.

Synset Examples

He prefers legacy media over social media for news.
Many trust legacy media for accurate news reporting.
Legacy media is struggling to compete with online platforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstream media - mass media outlets with broad readership or viewership, often contrasted with independent or alternative media
  • Antonym: digital media - media that use digital technologies, such as websites, social media, and streaming services

Etymology

From 'legacy' meaning inherited or existing from the past, and 'media'.
