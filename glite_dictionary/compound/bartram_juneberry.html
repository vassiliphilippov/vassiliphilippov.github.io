Bartram Juneberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.trəm ˈdʒuːnˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.trəm ˈdʒuːnˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
An open-growing shrub of eastern North America with pure white flowers and small, waxy, almost black fruits.
shrub native to eastern North America

Examples

The Bartram Juneberry blooms early in spring, revealing clusters of white flowers before its leaves unfold.
Gardeners value the Bartram Juneberry for its attractive foliage and edible fruits.
Birds are often attracted to the dark berries produced by the Bartram Juneberry in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juneberry - shrub or small tree with edible berries, genus Amelanchier

Etymology

Named after American naturalist John Bartram (1699–1777); 'Juneberry' refers to the genus Amelanchier, whose fruits ripen in June.
