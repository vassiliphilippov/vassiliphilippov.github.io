black fritillary

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæk frɪˈtɪləri / (AmE), / ˌblæk frɪˈtɪlɪəri / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant found in the southwestern United States, noted for its dark purple, bell-shaped flowers mottled with green
wildflower with dark purple mottled flowers

Examples

The black fritillary blooms in late spring across the desert meadows.
Botanists admire the unique color pattern of the black fritillary's flowers.
We spotted several black fritillaries during our hike in New Mexico.
