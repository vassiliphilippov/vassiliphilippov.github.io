stone parsley

1. NOUN

/ stoʊn ˈpɑːrsli / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
a slender plant native to western Europe and Mediterranean regions, with parsley-like leaves, white flowers, and aromatic seeds
roadside herb with parsley-like leaves and aromatic seeds

Examples

The roadside was dotted with stone parsley blooming among the grasses.
For identification, notice how stone parsley resembles true parsley but has distinct white flowers.
Her garden included wildflowers like stone parsley and cow parsley.

2. NOUN

/ stoʊn ˈpɑːrsli / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Seseli, characterized by dense umbels of small white or pink flowers and finely divided leaves.
plant of genus Seseli

Examples

Many wildflower enthusiasts admire the delicate blooms of stone parsley in meadows during the summer.
The finely divided leaves of stone parsley can sometimes be mistaken for those of cow parsley.
Gardeners sometimes plant stone parsley to attract beneficial insects to their gardens.
