pulse timing circuit

1. NOUN

/ pʌls ˈtaɪmɪŋ ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / pʌls ˈtaɪmɪŋ ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electronic circuit designed to generate, control, or measure the timing of electrical pulses.
electronic circuit that controls pulse timing

Examples

The engineer used a pulse timing circuit to synchronize the sequence of signals.
Modern microcontrollers often integrate a pulse timing circuit for precise control of devices.
A faulty pulse timing circuit can cause delays in the transmission of electronic data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timer circuit - an electronic circuit that measures and controls time intervals
