Parkinson's disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkɪnsənz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪnsənz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A chronic, progressive disorder of the central nervous system marked by tremors, muscle rigidity, and difficulty with movement and coordination.
progressive neurological disorder
medical

Examples

Her grandfather was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease when he was in his early sixties.
The symptoms of Parkinson's disease often include tremors and stiff muscles.
New treatments for Parkinson's disease are being researched by scientists worldwide.

Etymology

Named after English physician James Parkinson (1755–1824), who first described the disease.
