genus Diervilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs daɪˈɜːr.vɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs dɪəˈvɪl.ə / (BrE)
a small genus of low, deciduous shrubs in the honeysuckle family, known as bush honeysuckles
genus of shrubs called bush honeysuckles

Examples

The genus Diervilla comprises several hardy shrubs native to North America.
Botanists are studying the pollination habits within the genus Diervilla.
Gardeners often plant members of the genus Diervilla for their yellow flowers and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - climbing or shrub plants of the genus Lonicera and related genera

Etymology

Named after N. Dierville, a French surgeon and botanist.
