swamp willow

1. NOUN

/ swɑmp ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A North American shrub or small tree of the willow family with dark bark and narrow, linear leaves, typically found near streams and lakes.
North American shrubby willow tree

Examples

The swamp willow thrives in wetland habitats along riverbanks.
We spotted a row of swamp willow trees beside the lake during our hike.
Gardeners often plant swamp willow for erosion control near ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub with slender leaves and flexible branches, typically growing by water
