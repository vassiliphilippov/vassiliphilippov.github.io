tahoka daisy

1. NOUN

/ təˈhoʊkə ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / təˈhəʊkə ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a wild aster with fernlike leaves and flower heads featuring very narrow bright purple rays, native from Alberta to Texas and Mexico
wild aster with narrow purple rays

Examples

In the Texas fields, the tahoka daisy adds a splash of purple every spring.
She planted a row of tahoka daisies along the garden path for their delicate fernlike leaves.
Botanists surveyed the spread of the tahoka daisy from Alberta to northern Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant of the daisy family with star-shaped flower heads
