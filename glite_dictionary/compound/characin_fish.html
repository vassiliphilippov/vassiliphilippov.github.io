characin fish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəsɪn fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkarəsɪn fɪʃ / (BrE)
Any freshwater fish of the family Characinidae, found mainly in Central and South America and Africa.
freshwater fish of the Characinidae family

Examples

The aquarium enthusiast added several characin fish to her collection of tropical species.
Many characin fish are prized for their vibrant colors and active swimming patterns.
In South American rivers, characin fish play an important role in the aquatic ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetra - a small, brightly colored characin fish popular in aquariums

Etymology

From family name Characinidae, derived from Greek 'charax' (a type of fish).
