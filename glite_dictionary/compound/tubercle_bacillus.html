tubercle bacillus

1. NOUN

/ təˈbɜr.kəl bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / tjuːˈbɜː.kəl bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
the bacterium (Mycobacterium tuberculosis) that causes tuberculosis in humans and animals
bacterium causing tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The tubercle bacillus was first identified by Robert Koch in 1882.
Effective control of tuberculosis depends on eliminating the tubercle bacillus from infected patients.
Under the microscope, the tubercle bacillus appears as a slender, rod-shaped bacterium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mycobacterium tuberculosis - scientific name for the bacterium causing tuberculosis
  • Synonym: tuberculosis - infectious disease caused by the tubercle bacillus

Etymology

From Latin tuberculum (small swelling) + Modern Latin bacillus (little rod), referring to its shape.
