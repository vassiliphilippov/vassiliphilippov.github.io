glottis vera

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɑtɪs ˈvɛrə / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒtɪs ˈvɪərə / (BrE)
the anatomical space between the two true vocal folds in the larynx
space between true vocal folds
medical

Examples

During endoscopic examination, the surgeon could clearly view the glottis vera.
Abnormal narrowing of the glottis vera may cause voice and breathing difficulties.
The vocal cords vibrate as air passes through the glottis vera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glottis - the space between the vocal cords in the larynx

Etymology

From New Latin: 'glottis' from Greek 'glōttis' (part of the larynx); 'vera' is Latin for 'true'.
