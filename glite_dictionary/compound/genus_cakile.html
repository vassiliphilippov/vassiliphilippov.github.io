genus Cakile

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈkaɪliː / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kəˈkaɪliː / (BrE)
a small genus of succulent annual herbs native to sandy shores in North America and Europe, known for their thick, fleshy leaves
genus of succulent coastal herbs

Examples

The genus Cakile is commonly found on sandy beaches along the Atlantic coast.
Several species within the genus Cakile are adapted to salty environments.
Botanists studied the distribution of genus Cakile plants across European shorelines.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cakile', the genus name, which derives from Arabic 'kaḵīla' (a kind of plant).
