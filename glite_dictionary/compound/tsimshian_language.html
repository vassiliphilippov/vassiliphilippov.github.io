Tsimshian language

1. NOUN

/ tɪmˈʃiːən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tɪmˈʃiːən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the indigenous language of the Tsimshian people, belonging to the Penutian language family and spoken in parts of British Columbia and Alaska
native language of the Tsimshian people

Examples

The Tsimshian language is still spoken by several hundred people in British Columbia and Alaska.
Linguists study the Tsimshian language to learn about the Penutian language family.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Tsimshian language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tsimshian - a member of an indigenous people of northwestern Canada and Alaska

Etymology

From 'Tsimshian,' the name of the indigenous people, likely from their own language, meaning 'inside the Skeena River.'
