lateral line

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬ɚəl laɪn / (AmE), / ˈlætərəl laɪn / (BrE)
a system of sense organs along the sides of fish and amphibians that detects pressure changes and movement in the surrounding water
sense organs in fish/amphibians for detecting water movement

Examples

Biologists studied the lateral line to understand how fish navigate in dark or murky waters.
The lateral line enables amphibians to sense vibrations around them in the water.
Damage to the lateral line can affect a fish’s ability to detect predators and prey.

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of the side) + 'linea' (line).
