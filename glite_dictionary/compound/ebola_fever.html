Ebola fever

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈboʊlə ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ɪˈbəʊlə ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A severe and often fatal illness in humans and some primates caused by the Ebola virus, marked by high fever, internal bleeding, and usually occurring in Africa.
severe viral disease caused by the Ebola virus
medical

Examples

The outbreak of Ebola fever in West Africa caused international concern and urgent medical response.
Doctors wore protective gear to treat patients suffering from Ebola fever in the infected region.
Early symptoms of Ebola fever include high temperature, weakness, and severe muscle pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ebola virus disease - Another term for Ebola fever; the same disease
  • Synonym: Marburg virus disease - A related viral hemorrhagic fever with similar symptoms

Etymology

Named after the Ebola River in the Democratic Republic of the Congo, where the disease was first identified in 1976.
