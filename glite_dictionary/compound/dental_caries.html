dental caries

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛntəl ˈkɛriz / (AmE), / ˈdɛntəl ˈkeəriːz / (BrE)
decay of a tooth caused by bacterial action, resulting in a soft, damaged area that can progress and destroy the tooth
tooth decay; cavity formation
medical

Examples

The dentist found dental caries during the routine examination.
Poor oral hygiene can increase the risk of developing dental caries.
Children are particularly vulnerable to dental caries if they eat too many sugary snacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - a hole in a tooth caused by decay
  • Synonym: tooth decay - the process of teeth being destroyed by bacteria

Etymology

From Latin 'caries' (decay); 'dental' from Latin 'dens, dentis' (tooth).
