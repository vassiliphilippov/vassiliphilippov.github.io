tide rip

1. NOUN

/ taɪd rɪp / (AmE), / taɪd rɪp / (BrE)
a stretch of rough, turbulent water in a river or the sea caused by the meeting or crossing of opposing currents
stretch of turbulent water caused by opposing currents

Examples

Several boats avoided the tide rip near the island because of its unpredictable waves.
Strong winds can make a tide rip even more dangerous for small vessels.
The swirling waters formed a noticeable tide rip where the tidal flows met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water causing a small whirlpool
  • Synonym: whirlpool - a rapidly rotating mass of water in a river or sea
