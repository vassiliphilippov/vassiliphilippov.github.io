to voice a grievance

1. VERB

/ vɔɪs ə ɡriˈvɑns / (AmE), / vɔɪs ə ɡriˈvɑːns / (BrE)
to express a complaint or concern, especially formally or publicly
express a formal complaint or concern

Examples

Several employees decided to voice a grievance about the new overtime policy during the meeting.
Anyone who wishes to voice a grievance should submit a written statement to human resources.
She felt empowered to voice her grievance after learning about the company's complaint process.

Synset Examples

The complain king never misses a chance to voice his grievances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: raise an objection - to state or bring up a reason against something
