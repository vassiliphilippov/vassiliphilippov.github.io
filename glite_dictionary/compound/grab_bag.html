grab bag

1. NOUN

/ ɡræb bæɡ / (AmE), / ɡrab baɡ / (BrE)
A collection or assortment of miscellaneous items, often chosen at random.
assortment of miscellaneous items
american

Examples

The garage sale was a true grab bag of treasures and oddities.
Her new novel is a grab bag of ideas, anecdotes, and personal reflections.
We received a grab bag of snacks, toys, and games at the event.

Etymology

American English, originally describing a bag used at fairs or parties, from which items were drawn at random.

2. NOUN

/ ɡræb bæɡ / (AmE), / ɡræb bæɡ / (BrE)
A container from which a person selects a wrapped item at random, not knowing what the contents are.
container for randomly choosing wrapped items

Examples

Each child took turns reaching into the grab bag to choose a surprise gift.
At the company party, employees eagerly lined up for the annual holiday grab bag.
We filled the grab bag with small toys and candies for the carnival.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03455918-n

3. NOUN

/ ɡræb bæɡ / (AmE), / ɡræb bæɡ / (BrE)
A bag containing a variety of assorted items, often chosen at random by participants; also used metaphorically to describe a miscellaneous collection.
bag with assorted random items; a miscellaneous collection

Examples

Each child got to choose a toy from the grab bag at the party.
The store offered a holiday grab bag filled with surprises for shoppers.
Her essay was a grab bag of interesting but disconnected facts.

Synset Examples

She picked a random item from the grab bag.
The party favors were a surprise grab bag for everyone.
His closet is a total grab bag of old clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miscellany - a collection of different items or things
