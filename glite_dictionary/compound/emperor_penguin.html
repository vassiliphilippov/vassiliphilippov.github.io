emperor penguin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpɚ ˌpɛŋɡwɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛmpərə ˌpɛŋɡwɪn / (BrE)
a species of large penguin native to Antarctica, known for being the largest of all penguins
largest Antarctic penguin species

Examples

The emperor penguin is famous for surviving the harsh Antarctic winter.
We watched a documentary about the chicks of the emperor penguin.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the emperor penguin across the ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - flightless seabird of the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

From 'emperor' (ruler) + 'penguin'; named for its large size and stately appearance.
