daisy chain

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a chain made by linking daisies together by their stems, often worn as a decoration
chain of daisies linked by stems

Examples

Children spent the afternoon making a daisy chain in the park.
She wore a delicate daisy chain around her head during the school festival.
A daisy chain quickly adorned the picnic table as everyone joined in the fun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (BrE)
A series of connected people, things, or events, each linked to the next in succession, often forming a loop or chain.
a series of connected things or people

Examples

A complex daisy chain of transactions was used to move the money across various accounts.
The project turned into a daisy chain of meetings, each leading to the next without resolution.
Their plan depended on a daisy chain of favors from people within different departments.

Etymology

Originally from the literal meaning: a garland made by linking daisy flower stems together.
