French franc

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ fræŋk / (AmE), / frɛntʃ fræŋk / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in France, replaced by the euro in 2002
France's former basic currency unit

Examples

The museum had a collection of old French francs on display.
When France adopted the euro, the French franc ceased to be legal tender.
She exchanged her remaining French francs for euros before leaving Paris.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - the currency that replaced the French franc in France
  • Synonym: franc - the currency unit used in various countries, including Switzerland

Etymology

From French 'franc', originally meaning 'free' and later a coin's name (14th century); the term 'French franc' distinguishes it from other currencies called franc.
