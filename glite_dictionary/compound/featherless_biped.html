featherless biped

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðərləs ˈbaɪpɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðələs ˈbaɪpɛd / (BrE)
a humorous or philosophical term for a human being, referencing the fact that humans walk on two legs and lack feathers
humorous term for a human

Examples

He jokingly described himself as a featherless biped during the debate on human nature.
The philosopher cleverly reduced the human to a featherless biped in his argument.
In the cartoon, aliens labeled the Earthlings as curious featherless bipeds.

Synset Examples

The term featherless biped humorously describes our species.
The scientist referred to humans as featherless bipeds.
As featherless bipeds, we rely on intelligence, not feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: human - a member of the species Homo sapiens
  • Degree Lower: biped - an animal that uses two legs for walking

Etymology

Popularized as a definition of 'human' by the philosopher Plato; 'biped' from Latin 'bipes' (two-footed).
