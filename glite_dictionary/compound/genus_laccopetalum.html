genus Laccopetalum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˌlækəˈpiːtələm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌlækəˈpiːtələm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants consisting of a single species, commonly known as the giant buttercup.
genus of giant buttercup plants

Examples

The genus Laccopetalum includes only one known species, the giant buttercup.
Botanists studied the distinct features of the genus Laccopetalum during their fieldwork in South America.
The genus Laccopetalum is noted for its strikingly large petals and limited geographic range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ranunculus - a genus of flowering plants commonly known as buttercups

Etymology

From Latin 'lacco', possibly meaning 'pond' or 'pit', and Greek 'petalon' meaning 'petal'. Used in botanical nomenclature.
