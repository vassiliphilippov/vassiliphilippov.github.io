adumbration of thought

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəmˈbreɪʃən əv θɔt / (AmE), / ˌadʌmˈbreɪʃn əv θɔːt / (BrE)
a suggestion or foreshadowing of ideas, often in an indirect or subtle manner
hint or foreshadowing of ideas

Examples

The critic claimed the novel's title was an adumbration of thought underlying the entire narrative.
In her early sketches, there is an adumbration of thought that prefigures later philosophical themes.
His journal often contained an adumbration of thought that emerged in his published essays.

Synset Examples

The artist's work was an adumbration of her thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreshadowing - the indication or suggestion beforehand, especially in literature

Etymology

From Latin 'adumbrare' (to cast a shadow on) plus 'thought' (from Old English 'þōht').
