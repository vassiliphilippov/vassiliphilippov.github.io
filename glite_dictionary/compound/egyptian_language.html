Egyptian language

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the ancient and now extinct language spoken in Egypt under the Pharaohs, whose written records date back to 3000 BC
ancient extinct language of Egypt

Examples

Researchers have translated many ancient texts written in the Egyptian language.
The Egyptian language was used for thousands of years before dying out in the Middle Ages.
Hieroglyphic inscriptions are a primary source of our knowledge of the Egyptian language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Coptic language - the latest stage of the Egyptian language, used by Egyptian Christians
  • Synonym: Hieroglyphics - the script used to write early forms of the Egyptian language

Etymology

Egyptian from Egypt + language; Egypt from Ancient Greek 'Aigyptos.'
