Rappahannock River

1. PROPN

/ ˌræpəˈhænək ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌræpəˈhænək ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in eastern Virginia, United States, flowing into the Chesapeake Bay through the Tidewater region
river in eastern Virginia, US

Examples

Many Civil War battles took place near the Rappahannock River.
The Rappahannock River is known for its scenic beauty and historical significance.
We spent the afternoon canoeing on the Rappahannock River.

Etymology

From an Algonquian word meaning 'river of quick, rising water'.
