placental mammal

1. NOUN

/ pləˈsɛnt(ə)l ˈmæməl / (AmE), / pləˈsɛnt(ə)l ˈmam(ə)l / (BrE)
a mammal of the group distinguished by the presence of a placenta, consisting of all mammals except monotremes and marsupials
mammal with a placenta; excludes monotremes and marsupials
medical

Examples

Humans are classified as placental mammals, unlike kangaroos or platypuses.
The majority of living mammal species today are placental mammals.
Scientists discovered a fossil of an ancient placental mammal in the rock formation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marsupial - mammal group distinguished by carrying young in a pouch
  • Antonym: monotreme - egg-laying primitive mammals such as the platypus or echidna

Etymology

From Latin placenta (cake, flat cake; in medicine, mammalian organ) + mammal, from Latin mamma (breast).
