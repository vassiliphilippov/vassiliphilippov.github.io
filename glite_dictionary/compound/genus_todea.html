genus Todea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs toʊˈdiːə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs təʊˈdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of delicate ferns in the family Osmundaceae, native to regions such as New Zealand and Australia.
a genus of delicate ferns

Examples

The genus Todea includes a few species of ferns found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere.
Botanists studied the distribution of the genus Todea in New Zealand's forests.
Members of the genus Todea are known for their delicate fronds and moist habitat preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Todea - genus of ferns in the Osmundaceae family

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Todea named after German botanist Heinrich Julius Tode.
