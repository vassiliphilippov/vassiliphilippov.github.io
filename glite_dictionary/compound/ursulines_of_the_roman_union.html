Ursulines of the Roman Union

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.sjə.laɪnz əv ðə ˈroʊ.mən ˈjuː.njən / (AmE), / ˈɜː.sjʊ.laɪnz əv ðə ˈrəʊ.mən ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
an international branch of the Ursuline order of nuns within the Roman Catholic Church, established to unify Ursuline communities under a single governance
branch of the Ursuline order of nuns

Examples

The Ursulines of the Roman Union operate schools and orphanages in dozens of countries.
She joined the Ursulines of the Roman Union after completing her studies.
The Ursulines of the Roman Union have a long tradition of educational work worldwide.

Synset Examples

They are divided into two branches, the monastic Order of St. Ursula (post-nominals O.S.U.), among whom the largest organization are the Ursulines of the Roman Union, described in this article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursuline order - a Roman Catholic religious order for women founded in 1535 by St. Angela Merici

Etymology

Ursulines from St. Ursula, legendary Christian martyr; Roman Union refers to their unification under papal authority in Rome.
