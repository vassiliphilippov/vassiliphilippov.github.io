Indian tapir

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən ˈteɪpər / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
a large herbivorous mammal with a distinctive black-and-white coloration, native to Southeast Asia, especially Malaya and Sumatra
Southeast Asian tapir species

Examples

The Indian tapir is easily identified by its striking black and white coloring.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered Indian tapir in Sumatra.
We spotted an Indian tapir at the edge of the rainforest during our trip to Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - a large plant-eating mammal with a short trunk
