warm front

1. NOUN

/ wɔrm frʌnt / (AmE), / wɔːm frʌnt / (BrE)
the leading edge of a moving mass of warmer air, especially as it advances into a region of cooler air
edge of advancing warm air mass (meteorology)

Examples

A warm front will move in tomorrow, bringing higher temperatures and rain.
As the warm front passed over the region, the snow quickly melted.
Meteorologists tracked the progress of the approaching warm front across the state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold front - the front of an advancing mass of cold air
