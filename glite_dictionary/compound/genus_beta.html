genus Beta

1. NOUN

/ ˈd͡ʒiːnəs ˈbiːtə / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒiːnəs ˈbiːtə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Amaranthaceae, including the beets, such as the common beetroot and sugar beet.
genus of plants including beets

Examples

The genus Beta includes several species important in agriculture.
Researchers studied genetic variation within the genus Beta.
Many varieties of beetroot belong to the genus Beta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - a plant with a thick root, commonly eaten or used for sugar

Etymology

From Latin *beta* (beet), used in botanical taxonomy.
