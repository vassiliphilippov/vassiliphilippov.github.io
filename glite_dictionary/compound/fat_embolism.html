fat embolism

1. NOUN

/ fæt ˈɛm.bəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / fæt ˈɛm.bə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which fat globules enter the bloodstream and block an artery, often occurring after bone fracture or fat tissue injury.
blockage of an artery by fat globules
medical

Examples

Fat embolism is a potentially fatal complication after major trauma, particularly when long bones are fractured.
Doctors monitored the patient closely for signs of a fat embolism following his surgery.
A fat embolism can rapidly cause respiratory distress if not identified and treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embolism - the blockage of a blood vessel by a foreign substance or blood clot

Etymology

From 'fat' (adipose tissue) and 'embolism' (from Greek embolismos, meaning 'insertion', referring to blockage of a vessel).
