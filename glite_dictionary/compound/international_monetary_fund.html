International Monetary Fund

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈnæʃənəl ˈmoʊnɪˌtɛri fʌnd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənl ˈmɒnɪtəri fʌnd / (BrE)
An international organization, part of the United Nations, that works to promote global monetary cooperation and financial stability, facilitate international trade, and support economic growth.
United Nations financial agency

Examples

The International Monetary Fund provided emergency funding to the country during its financial crisis.
Membership in the International Monetary Fund allows nations to access international financial resources.
The reforms were recommended in consultation with the International Monetary Fund.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Bank - A global financial institution providing loans and grants to developing countries
  • Synonym: International Development Association - A part of the World Bank Group, focusing on the world's poorest countries
