English people

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈpipəl / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈpiːpl / (BrE)
the people who are natives or inhabitants of England, especially considered as an ethnic or cultural group
natives or inhabitants of England

Examples

The English people are known for their love of tea and traditional customs.
Many historians have studied the origins of the English people and their culture.
The English people celebrated their team's victory in the streets of London.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Englishman - a man from England
  • Synonym: Briton - a native or inhabitant of Great Britain
  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from a different country
