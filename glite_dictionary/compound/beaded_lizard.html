beaded lizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːdɪd ˌlɪzərd / (AmE), / ˈbiːdɪd ˌlɪzəd / (BrE)
a large venomous lizard of western Mexico, distinguished by its black and yellow beadlike scales
a venomous lizard of western Mexico

Examples

The beaded lizard is one of only a few known venomous lizard species in the world.
Herpetologists are fascinated by the toxic bite of the beaded lizard.
The beaded lizard has raised, shiny scales that help distinguish it from other reptiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gila monster - a related venomous lizard native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico
