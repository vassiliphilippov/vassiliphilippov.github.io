phosphorous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑːsˈfɔːrəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɒsˈfɒrəs ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a clear or yellow monobasic acid (H3PO2) used in chemical synthesis and as a reducing agent
a monobasic acid (H3PO2)

Examples

Phosphorous acid is commonly used as a reducing agent in chemical reactions.
The preparation of phosphorous acid involves the hydrolysis of phosphorus trichloride.
Handling phosphorous acid requires caution due to its potential to cause burns.

Etymology

From 'phosphorous' (relating to phosphorus) + 'acid'; phosphorus from Greek 'phōsphoros' (light-bringing).
