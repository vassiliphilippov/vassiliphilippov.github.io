high bar

1. NOUN

/ haɪ bɑr / (AmE), / haɪ bɑː / (BrE)
a gymnastic apparatus consisting of a horizontal bar supported by uprights at both ends, used for gymnastic exercises and routines
horizontal gymnastics apparatus

Examples

The gymnast performed an impressive routine on the high bar during the competition.
Proper grip is essential for safety when swinging on the high bar.
He won a gold medal for his high bar performance at the Olympics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horizontal bar - another term for the high bar; same apparatus used in gymnastics

2. NOUN

/ haɪ bɑr / (AmE), / haɪ bɑː / (BrE)
a standard, requirement, or expectation that is difficult to achieve
challenging standard or requirement

Examples

The company has set a high bar for product quality this year.
Achieving this award is a high bar that only a few can reach.
She always aims for a high bar in everything she does.

Synset Examples

They set a high bar for customer service expectations.
The high bar for entry makes it hard to join.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference
  • Antonym: low bar - an easy or minimal standard or requirement
