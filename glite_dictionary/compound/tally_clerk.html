tally clerk

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæli klɜrk / (AmE), / ˈtæli klɑːk / (BrE)
a person responsible for counting and recording the quantity or weight of goods produced, shipped, or received
person who records quantities of goods

Examples

The tally clerk was responsible for ensuring every shipment was accurately recorded in the logbook.
As a tally clerk, she checked the cargo and matched it to the delivery paperwork.
The warehouse hired an additional tally clerk during the busy holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recorder - a person who keeps a record or account of something

Etymology

From 'tally' (a record or count) + 'clerk' (an office worker or record keeper).
