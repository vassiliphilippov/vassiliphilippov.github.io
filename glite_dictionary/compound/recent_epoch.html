Recent epoch

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːsənt ˈiːpɑk / (AmE), / ˈriːsnt ˈiːpɒk / (BrE)
the most recent period of geological time, approximately the last 10,000 years, also called the Holocene
geological epoch covering the last 10,000 years

Examples

During the Recent epoch, humans developed agriculture and established permanent settlements.
The Recent epoch is marked by significant climate changes and rapid evolution of civilizations.
Fossils discovered from the Recent epoch show the impact of humans on animal species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holocene - the geological epoch covering the last 11,700 years
