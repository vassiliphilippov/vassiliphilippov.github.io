Chinese cabbage

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈniz ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a vegetable with an elongated head of crisp, pale-green celery-like stalks and light green leaves, commonly used in Asian cuisine
a type of Asian leafy vegetable

Examples

She sliced some Chinese cabbage to add to the stir-fry.
The soup was garnished with tender leaves of Chinese cabbage.
You can find fresh Chinese cabbage at most Asian grocery stores.

2. NOUN

/ tʃaɪˈniz ˈkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz ˈkæbɪdʒ / (BrE)
an East Asian plant with an elongated head of broad, pale green leaves and thick stalks, used as a vegetable
East Asian leafy vegetable

Examples

She added chopped Chinese cabbage to the stir-fry for extra crunch.
Chinese cabbage grows well in cool climates and is a common ingredient in Asian cuisine.
The salad was made with fresh Chinese cabbage, carrots, and sesame dressing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napa cabbage - a type of Chinese cabbage with broad, pale green leaves
