Goidelic language

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔɪˈdɛlɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡɔɪˈdɛlɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the group of Celtic languages that includes Irish, Scottish Gaelic, and Manx, spoken mainly in Ireland, Scotland, and the Isle of Man.
Celtic language group (Irish, Scottish Gaelic, Manx)

Examples

The Irish and Scottish Gaelic are both considered Goidelic languages.
Linguists have traced the history of the Goidelic language group across several centuries.
Manx, once nearly extinct, is a revived Goidelic language spoken on the Isle of Man.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Brythonic language - group of Celtic languages spoken in Britain, such as Welsh, Breton, and Cornish
  • Synonym: Celtic language - a branch of the Indo-European language family, including the Goidelic and Brythonic branches

Etymology

From Goidelic, based on Latin 'Goídel' (Gael) with the suffix '-ic', referring to the Gaelic branch of Celtic languages.
