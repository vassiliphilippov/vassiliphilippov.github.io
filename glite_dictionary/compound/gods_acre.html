God's acre

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑdz ˈeɪkɚ / (AmE), / ɡɒdz ˈeɪkə / (BrE)
a churchyard, especially one used as a burial ground
churchyard for burials

Examples

Many poets have written about the peace and solemnity of the God's acre beside the old church.
Her ancestors were buried in the God's acre at the edge of the village.
Visitors wandered quietly among the tombstones in the historic God's acre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: churchyard - the ground adjoining a church, often used as a cemetery

Etymology

Calque of German Gottesacker, meaning ‘God’s field’.
