few-flowered leek

1. NOUN

/ fjuː ˈflaʊɚd lik / (AmE), / fjuː ˈflaʊəd liːk / (BrE)
A leek species that produces bulbils instead of flowers, native to Russia and Iran; Allium paradoxum.
a species of leek producing bulbils, not flowers

Examples

The few-flowered leek is invasive in some woodlands, quickly forming dense carpets.
Gardeners sometimes grow few-flowered leek for its edible leaves and bulbils.
Unlike common leeks, the few-flowered leek rarely produces true flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allium paradoxum - Scientific name for few-flowered leek
