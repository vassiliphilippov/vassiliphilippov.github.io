Fifth Crusade

1. PROPN

/ fɪfθ kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / fɪfθ kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
a Crusade led by European Christians from 1218 to 1221, aimed at reclaiming Jerusalem by first conquering Egypt, but ultimately unsuccessful due to internal disagreements.
a major Christian Crusade (1218–1221) to regain Jerusalem

Examples

Historians debate the strategic errors that led to the failure of the Fifth Crusade.
The Fifth Crusade initially gained territory in Egypt before internal disputes undermined its efforts.
Papal leadership played a crucial role in organizing the Fifth Crusade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crusades - a series of medieval military expeditions by Europeans to recover the Holy Land from Muslims
