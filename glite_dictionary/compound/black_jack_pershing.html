Black Jack Pershing

1. PROPN

/ blæk dʒæk ˈpɜrʃɪŋ / (AmE), / blæk dʒæk ˈpɜːʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(1860–1948) United States general who commanded American forces in Europe during World War I.
US general in World War I

Examples

General Black Jack Pershing led the American Expeditionary Forces in Europe during World War I.
The leadership of Black Jack Pershing was instrumental in shaping the outcome of the war.
Many historians regard Black Jack Pershing as one of America's greatest military leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John J. Pershing - American general and military leader during World War I

Etymology

Nickname 'Black Jack' reputedly derived from Pershing's service with African American troops; 'Pershing' is a family surname.
