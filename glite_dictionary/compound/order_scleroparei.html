order Scleroparei

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdər ˌsklɛrəˈpɛraɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌsklɛrəˈpɪərɪ / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of fishes that included scorpionfishes, sculpins, gurnards, greenlings, and flying gurnards
former fish taxonomic order

Examples

The order Scleroparei was once used to classify a diverse group of bony fishes.
Many marine biologists now use updated taxonomy instead of order Scleroparei when describing these species.
Characteristics of fishes in the order Scleroparei include spiny fins and bony cheeks.

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'parēios' (cheek), referring to bony structures in these fishes' heads.
