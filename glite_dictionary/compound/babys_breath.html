baby's breath

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪbiz ˌbrɛθ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbɪz ˌbrɛθ / (BrE)
a tall plant with slender stems and numerous tiny white or pink flowers, often used in flower arrangements
a plant with small white or pink flowers, used in bouquets

Examples

The florist added baby's breath to the bouquet for a soft, delicate touch.
Many wedding centerpieces feature baby's breath mixed with roses.
She planted baby's breath in her garden for its airy, cloud-like blossoms.

Etymology

The name refers to the delicate appearance of the flowers, likened to the lightness of a baby's breath.
