basic iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪk ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Pig iron with a high phosphorus content, used in steelmaking processes that remove phosphorus.
pig iron high in phosphorus for steelmaking

Examples

The steel plant uses basic iron as a raw material for producing quality steel.
Phosphorus must be removed from basic iron before it can be used in most applications.
This process is designed to convert basic iron into steel by eliminating impurities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pig iron - crude iron obtained from a blast furnace
