raw recruit

1. NOUN

/ rɔ rəˈkrut / (AmE), / rɔː rɪˈkruːt / (BrE)
A newly enlisted person who lacks experience and training, especially in the military or a similar organization.
inexperienced and untrained recruit

Examples

The army assigned the raw recruit to basic training as soon as he arrived.
As a raw recruit, she struggled to keep up with the rigorous drills.
The sergeant could spot a raw recruit from a mile away due to their nervousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhorn - A person who is new to or inexperienced at a particular activity
  • Antonym: veteran - A person with long experience in a particular field, especially the military
