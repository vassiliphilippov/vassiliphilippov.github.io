family Bacillaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bəˌsɪləˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bæsɪˈleɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of typically rod-shaped, usually Gram-positive bacteria that produce endospores.
a family of rod-shaped, Gram-positive bacteria
medical

Examples

Many species within the family Bacillaceae are known for forming resilient endospores.
The laboratory identified the bacteria as belonging to the family Bacillaceae.
Members of the family Bacillaceae are found in soil and water environments worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bacillus - genus of bacteria within family Bacillaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Bacillus (from Latin 'little staff') plus scientific family suffix -aceae.
