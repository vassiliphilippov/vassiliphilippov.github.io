Society of Jesus

1. PROPN

/ səˈsaɪəti əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (AmE), / səˈsaɪəti əv ˈdʒiːzəs / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic religious order founded by Saint Ignatius of Loyola in 1534, dedicated to missionary work, education, and scholarship, also known as the Jesuits.
Roman Catholic religious order (Jesuits)

Examples

He was educated by the Society of Jesus at one of their renowned schools in Paris.
The Society of Jesus played a key role in the Counter-Reformation.
Many universities around the world were founded by the Society of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jesuit - member of the Society of Jesus
