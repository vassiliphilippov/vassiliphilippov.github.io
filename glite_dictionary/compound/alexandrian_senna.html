Alexandrian senna

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛɡˈzændriən ˈsɛnə / (AmE), / əˌlɛksˈandriən ˈsɛnə / (BrE)
an erect shrub (Senna alexandrina) with racemes of tawny yellow flowers, whose dried leaves are used as a cathartic medicine
medicinal shrub; source of senna leaves
medical

Examples

The dried leaves of Alexandrian senna have long been used as a natural laxative.
Pharmacies often stock herbal remedies containing Alexandrian senna extract.
Alexandrian senna is cultivated in Egypt and Sudan for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senna - the dried leaves of various Cassia species used as a laxative

Etymology

Named after Alexandria, Egypt, a historical center of its export, and senna, from Arabic 'sanā'.
