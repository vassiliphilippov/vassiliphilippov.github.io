taxonomic group

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæksəˈnɑmɪk ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˌtæksəˈnɒmɪk ɡruːp / (BrE)
A group of organisms that are classified together based on natural relationships, typically in biological taxonomy.
group of organisms classified by taxonomy

Examples

The genus Panthera is a well-known taxonomic group that includes lions, tigers, and leopards.
Researchers discovered a new taxonomic group within the coral reef ecosystem.
Each taxonomic group shares specific characteristics that distinguish it from others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxon - a taxonomic group of any rank, such as a species, family, or class

Etymology

From 'taxonomy', which comes from Greek 'taxis' (arrangement) and 'nomos' (law).
