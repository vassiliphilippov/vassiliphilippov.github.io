mus rose

1. NOUN

/ mʌs roʊz / (AmE), / mʌs rəʊz / (BrE)
A species of rose native to the Old World, known for its faintly musk-scented leaves and white or pink flowers.
species of rose with musk-scented foliage

Examples

The mus rose is prized for its sweet scent in summer gardens.
Many old English gardens feature the mus rose among their historic plantings.
She planted a mus rose near the window, filling the room with a subtle fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rosa moschata - scientific name for mus rose

Etymology

From Latin 'rosa moschata', meaning musk-scented rose.
