California newt

1. NOUN

/ kæləˌfɔrniə ˈnut / (AmE), / kælɪˌfɔːniə ˈnjuːt / (BrE)
A species of newt (Taricha torosa) native to California, characterized by rough skin and toxic secretions.
a newt species native to California

Examples

We spotted a California newt during our hike in the coastal forest.
The California newt secretes a potent toxin to deter predators.
Researchers study the habitat preferences of the California newt to support conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small amphibian with a lizardlike appearance
