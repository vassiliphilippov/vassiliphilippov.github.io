predicate calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪt ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈprɛdɪkət ˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
a system of symbolic logic that studies the structure of statements involving predicates and quantifiers, as well as the relationships between them and individual objects
system of symbolic logic with predicates and quantifiers

Examples

Some advanced courses in logic introduce students to predicate calculus and its applications in mathematics.
The distinction between propositional calculus and predicate calculus lies in their treatment of quantification.
Automated theorem proving systems often rely on the formalism of predicate calculus to process complex logical statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propositional calculus - system of symbolic logic dealing with the logical relationships between propositions

Etymology

From Latin 'praedicatum' (predicate) and 'calculus' (small stone, reckoning, calculation).
