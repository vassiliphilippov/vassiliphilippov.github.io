Trondheim Fiord

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɑːnˌhaɪm fiˈɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtrɒn.haɪm fiːˈɔːd / (BrE)
a long, narrow inlet of the Norwegian Sea in Norway, known for its scenic beauty and important port city Trondheim.
fiord (inlet) in Norway

Examples

The cruise ship sailed through the Trondheim Fiord at sunrise, offering stunning views of the Norwegian landscape.
Researchers studied the marine life unique to the waters of Trondheim Fiord.
Tourists often visit Trondheim Fiord for its picturesque scenery and historical significance.

Etymology

From Norwegian 'Trondheimsfjorden', named after the city of Trondheim.
