margin for error

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪn fər ˈɛrər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn fə ˈerə / (BrE)
an amount that allows for slight mistakes or inaccuracies without causing failure or serious consequences
allowable range for mistakes

Examples

We need to double-check these numbers because our margin for error is extremely small.
Pilots must operate with very little margin for error during a storm.
With such a tight budget, there is no margin for error in our planning.

Synset Examples

The margin for error was razor-thin in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leeway - room to move or act freely within limits
  • Synonym: buffer - something that serves as a protective barrier; a cushion
