Australian grass tree

1. NOUN

/ əˌstreɪliən ɡræs triː / (AmE), / ɒˌstreɪliən ɡrɑːs triː / (BrE)
A stout Australian shrub with narrow leaves crowded at the ends of branches, bearing terminal clusters of white or pink flowers.
stout Australian shrub with clustered flowers

Examples

The Australian grass tree is known for its distinctive, long, narrow leaves and tall flowering spikes.
Botanists studied the resilience of the Australian grass tree during wildfires in the outback.
We admired the clusters of white flowers on the Australian grass tree during our hike in Western Australia.

2. NOUN

/ əˈstreɪliən ˈɡræs ˌtri / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈɡrɑːs ˌtriː / (BrE)
Any of several Australian evergreen perennial plants with a short, thick woody stem topped by a tuft of grasslike leaves, belonging to the genus Xanthorrhoea, and known for yielding acaroid resins.
Australian perennial plant with grassy tufted crown

Examples

The Australian grass tree is often seen dotting the dry forests of New South Wales.
Resin from the Australian grass tree has been used by Indigenous Australians for centuries.
After bushfires, Australian grass trees quickly send up new shoots from their tough trunks.
