gray hat

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪ hæt / (AmE), / ɡreɪ hæt / (BrE)
a hacker whose activities fall between ethical and unethical boundaries, often operating with ambiguous ethical standards
hacker with ambiguous ethics

Examples

Our company hired a gray hat to test the security system's vulnerabilities.
He identifies as a gray hat, choosing projects based on his own moral code.
The actions of a gray hat are often debated in the cyber security community.

Synset Examples

As a gray hat, she walks the line between ethical and unethical hacking.
Being a gray hat can lead to legal issues if not careful.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white hat - ethical hacker or security expert
  • Antonym: black hat - malicious hacker or criminal hacker
  • Synonym: hacker - someone who uses computers to gain unauthorized access to data

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'white hat' (ethical hacker) and 'black hat' (malicious hacker), using 'gray' to indicate ambiguity.
