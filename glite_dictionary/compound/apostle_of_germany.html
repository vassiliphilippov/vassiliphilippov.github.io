Apostle of Germany

1. PROPN

/ əˈpɑsəl əv ˈdʒɜrməni / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl əv ˈdʒɜːməni / (BrE)
A title given to Saint Boniface (c. 675–754), the Anglo-Saxon missionary revered for spreading Christianity in Germany.
title of Saint Boniface, Christian missionary to Germany

Examples

Saint Boniface is often called the Apostle of Germany for his missionary work.
The Apostle of Germany played a crucial role in the Christianization of central Europe.
A biography of the Apostle of Germany details his efforts among Germanic tribes.
