mirid bug

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrɪd bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmɪrɪd bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small plant-feeding insect of the family Miridae, commonly known as a leaf bug, found on a variety of plants.
plant-feeding insect of the Miridae family

Examples

The farmer discovered several mirid bugs among the tomato plants in his greenhouse.
Damage to the cotton crop was later traced back to an infestation of mirid bugs.
Researchers study how mirid bugs affect the health of different garden plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf bug - another term for mirid bug, plant-feeding insect of the Miridae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Miridae, the family name, based on Latin 'mirus' (wonderful, strange), plus 'bug'.
