family Bathyergidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli bæθiˈɜːrdʒɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli bæθɪˈɜːdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing rodents native to Africa, including mole rats and sand rats.
rodent family: African mole rats and sand rats

Examples

The family Bathyergidae comprises several genera of subterranean rodents endemic to Africa.
Researchers have studied the social behavior of different species within the family Bathyergidae.
Unlike most rodents, members of the family Bathyergidae are highly adapted to an underground lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole rat - a burrowing rodent found in Africa and Asia
  • Synonym: sand rat - small burrowing rodent from African or Asian deserts

Etymology

From New Latin Bathyergidae, based on the type genus Bathyergus (from Greek bathys 'deep' + ergon 'work'), referring to their burrowing habits.
