New World leishmaniasis

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈwɝːld liˌʃməniˈaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld liˌʃmənaɪˈæsɪs / (BrE)
a form of leishmaniasis occurring in Mexico, Central America, and South America, characterized by sores on the skin and mucous membranes
form of leishmaniasis in the Americas
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of New World leishmaniasis are most common in rural areas of Central and South America.
The patient was diagnosed with New World leishmaniasis after developing skin sores following travel to Brazil.
Unlike Old World forms, New World leishmaniasis may also affect mucous membranes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - a disease caused by protozoan parasites of the genus Leishmania

Etymology

From 'New World' denoting the Americas and 'leishmaniasis', a disease named after William Boog Leishman.
