peritoneal inflammation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ə.tɪˈni.əl ɪnˌflæm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪ.təˈniː.əl ɪnˌflæm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Inflammation of the peritoneum, the membrane lining the abdominal cavity and covering the abdominal organs.
inflammation of the peritoneum
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with peritoneal inflammation after experiencing severe abdominal pain.
Doctors often treat peritoneal inflammation aggressively to prevent complications.
Signs of peritoneal inflammation may include abdominal tenderness and fever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peritonitis - inflammation of the peritoneum (medical term)

Etymology

From 'peritoneal' (relating to the peritoneum) and 'inflammation' (Latin inflammatio, setting on fire).
