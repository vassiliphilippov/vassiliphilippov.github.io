American mink

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən mɪŋk / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən mɪŋk / (BrE)
a semi-aquatic carnivorous mammal (Neovison vison) native to North America, usually with rich dark brown fur, valued for its pelts
species of North American mink

Examples

The American mink was introduced to Europe for fur farming and has established wild populations there.
Conservationists are concerned about the impact of the American mink on native wildlife.
The rich brown fur of the American mink is highly prized in the fashion industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mink - a small carnivorous mammal valued for its fur
