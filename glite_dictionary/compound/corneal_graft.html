corneal graft

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrniəl ɡræft / (AmE), / ˈkɔːniəl ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which part or all of a damaged or diseased cornea is replaced with healthy tissue from a donor.
surgical replacement of the cornea
medical

Examples

The patient regained partial vision after undergoing a corneal graft.
Doctors recommended a corneal graft due to the severity of the corneal damage.
Some recipients of a corneal graft require lifelong medication to prevent rejection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keratoplasty - surgical repair or transplantation of the cornea
