false note

1. NOUN

/ fɔls noʊt / (AmE), / fɔːls nəʊt / (BrE)
a mistake or error in a musical or figurative performance; something that disrupts the intended harmony or effect
a mistake or error, especially in music

Examples

As the orchestra played, the clarinet unfortunately sounded a false note.
Her apology struck a false note with the audience, who doubted her sincerity.
The speech contained a false note when he praised his rivals too enthusiastically.

Synset Examples

He hit a false note during the final rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake
  • Synonym: error - a mistake, especially one that causes problems
