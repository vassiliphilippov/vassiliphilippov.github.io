freedom from self-incrimination

1. NOUN

/ ˈfri.dəm frəm ˌsɛlf ɪnˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfriː.dəm frəm ˌself ɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the legal right to refuse to answer questions or give testimony that could incriminate oneself, as protected under the Fifth Amendment in the United States
right to refuse self-incriminating testimony
legal american

Examples

The suspect invoked his freedom from self-incrimination during the police interrogation.
It is important for defendants to understand their freedom from self-incrimination when questioned in court.
The lawyer reminded her client about his freedom from self-incrimination before the trial began.
