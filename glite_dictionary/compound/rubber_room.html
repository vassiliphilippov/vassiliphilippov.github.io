rubber room

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbər ruːm / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə ruːm / (BrE)
a room, typically padded for safety, used to isolate disruptive or potentially dangerous individuals
padded isolation room for disruptive people

Examples

The hospital built a rubber room to safely contain violent patients.
After his outburst, the student was taken to the rubber room for observation.
Rumors spread that teachers on leave waited months in a rubber room.

Synset Examples

He was sent to the rubber room for acting out.
The school has a rubber room for disruptive students.
