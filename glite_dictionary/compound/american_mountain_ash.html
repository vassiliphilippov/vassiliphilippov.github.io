American mountain ash

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmaʊntən æʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈmaʊntɪn æʃ / (BrE)
A species of mountain ash native to North America, with clusters of bright red berries and compound leaves, scientifically known as Sorbus americana.
North American mountain ash tree

Examples

The American mountain ash is known for its vibrant red berries that brighten autumn forests.
Birds are attracted to the berries of the American mountain ash during the fall and winter months.
We planted an American mountain ash in our garden to add year-round interest and support local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain ash - a tree with clusters of bright berries, especially of the genus Sorbus
