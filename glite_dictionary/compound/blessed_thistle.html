blessed thistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛsɪd ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
an annual Mediterranean plant (Cnicus benedictus) with hairy stems, spiny-toothed leaves, and large heads of yellow flowers, used in herbal remedies
a Mediterranean plant used in herbal medicine

Examples

Herbalists sometimes recommend blessed thistle for digestive health.
The blessed thistle in the garden bloomed with bright yellow flowers.
She purchased dried blessed thistle to brew medicinal tea.

Etymology

Refers to its traditional use as a medicinal or 'blessed' herb in Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɛsɪd ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a tall biennial thistle (Cnicus benedictus) with white-blotched leaves and purple flower heads, native to southern Europe and widely naturalized elsewhere, historically used in herbal medicine
a medicinal European thistle plant

Examples

Blessed thistle is often used in herbal teas for its traditional healing properties.
The garden was filled with the sharp leaves and purple blooms of blessed thistle.
Some people plant blessed thistle to attract bees and support pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - any of various prickly, flowering plants of the family Asteraceae

Etymology

The name derives from the Latin 'benedictus' meaning 'blessed' due to its reputed medicinal properties.
