spreading fleabane

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɛdɪŋ ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈsprɛdɪŋ ˈfliːˌbeɪn / (BrE)
a well-branched plant of western North America with hairy leaves and stems, each bearing a solitary flower head with narrow white, pink, or lavender rays
a western North American plant with hairy leaves and lavender, pink, or white flowers

Examples

The rocky slopes were dotted with spreading fleabane in full bloom.
Gardeners appreciate spreading fleabane for its delicate white and lavender flowers.
We identified several wildflowers, including spreading fleabane, during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleabane - a common name for various daisy-like plants, formerly believed to repel fleas

Etymology

From 'fleabane', referring to plants once believed to repel fleas; 'spreading' describes the plant's growth habit.
