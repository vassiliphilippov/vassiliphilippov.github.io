eastern gray squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərn ɡreɪ ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ɡreɪ ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
a common medium-sized squirrel native to eastern North America, distinguished by its gray fur and bushy tail, and introduced to other regions such as England
North American gray squirrel species

Examples

The eastern gray squirrel is commonly seen in city parks across the United States.
In England, the eastern gray squirrel has outcompeted native red squirrels.
Researchers tagged several eastern gray squirrels to study their movement patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray squirrel - a squirrel species with gray fur found in North America
  • Antonym: red squirrel - Eurasian squirrel species with reddish fur
