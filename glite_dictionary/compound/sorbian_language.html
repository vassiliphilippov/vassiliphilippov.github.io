Sorbian language

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.bi.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.bi.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a West Slavic language spoken mainly by the Sorbs in southeastern Germany
a West Slavic language of southeastern Germany

Examples

The Sorbian language is taught in schools in certain regions of eastern Germany.
Many traditional folk songs are still sung in the Sorbian language.
He began studying the Sorbian language to better understand the culture of the Lusatian region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic language - a language belonging to the Slavic branch of the Indo-European family
  • Degree Higher: Polish language - a West Slavic language spoken in Poland
  • Degree Higher: Czech language - a West Slavic language spoken in the Czech Republic

Etymology

From Sorb, the name of the ethnic group, plus English 'language.'
