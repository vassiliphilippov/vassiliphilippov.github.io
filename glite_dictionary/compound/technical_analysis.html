technical analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛknɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛknɪkəl əˈnalɪsɪs / (BrE)
the study of past market price movements and trading volumes to forecast future price trends, especially in stocks or financial markets
forecasting prices by analyzing past market trends

Examples

Many investors rely on technical analysis to identify patterns and make informed trading decisions.
The finance course introduced students to both technical analysis and fundamental analysis of stock markets.
He used technical analysis to predict when the stock price would reach its next high point.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fundamental analysis - evaluation of a security's value based on financial and economic data

Etymology

From 'technical', relating to technique, and 'analysis', from Greek 'analusis' (a breaking up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛknɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkəl əˈnalɪsɪs / (BrE)
The study of past market prices and volume in order to predict future movements in stock or financial markets.
analyzing market data to predict price trends

Examples

Many investors use technical analysis to identify potential buying opportunities.
Unlike technical analysis, fundamental analysis focuses on company earnings and economic data.
She gave a presentation on technical analysis techniques for forecasting stock prices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fundamental analysis - analysis based on a company's financial data and economic factors
