Iceland spar

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪs.lənd ˌspɑr / (AmE), / ˈaɪs.lənd ˌspɑː / (BrE)
a transparent variety of calcite, originally found in Iceland, used especially for its optical properties in polarizing microscopes
transparent Icelandic calcite used in optics

Examples

Optical experiments often use Iceland spar to demonstrate double refraction.
The geologist examined a sample of Iceland spar under the microscope.
Historically, Iceland spar was essential in the development of polarizing equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcite - a common carbonate mineral composed of calcium carbonate

Etymology

Named after Iceland, the traditional source, and 'spar', an old term for non-metallic minerals with a crystalline appearance.
