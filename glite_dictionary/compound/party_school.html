party school

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.ti skuːl / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ti skuːl / (BrE)
a college or university known for its lively social scene and frequent parties
college or university known for social scene

Examples

She was warned that her chosen college had a reputation as a party school.
Attending a party school can be distracting for students who want to focus on academics.
Despite its label as a party school, the university offers excellent academic programs.

Synset Examples

He thrived at the party school, making lifelong friends.
They chose that university because it's known as a party school.
Graduating from a party school was quite an experience.
