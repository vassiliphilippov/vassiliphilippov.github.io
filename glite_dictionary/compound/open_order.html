open order

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpən ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a military formation in which soldiers stand with sufficient space between ranks to allow an inspecting officer to pass freely
military formation with space between ranks

Examples

The soldiers lined up in open order for their commanding officer's inspection.
During the parade, the unit was instructed to fall in at open order.
An open order allowed the inspector to move easily between the ranks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: close order - a military formation with soldiers standing close together without space for passage between ranks
