polar front

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlər frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə frʌnt / (BrE)
the boundary between cold polar air and warmer air to the south, often associated with the formation of storms
boundary between cold and warm air masses

Examples

Meteorologists often monitor the polar front to predict storm development in the northern hemisphere.
A shift in the polar front can bring colder temperatures and precipitation to temperate regions.
During winter, the polar front typically moves southward, affecting large areas with cold air.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warm front - the boundary where a warm air mass is advancing over a cold air mass
