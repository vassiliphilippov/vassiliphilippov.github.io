Border terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdər təˈrɪər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə təˈrɪə / (BrE)
a small, hardy breed of terrier with a rough coat, originally bred in Britain for fox and vermin hunting
small rough-coated British terrier breed
british

Examples

The Border terrier is known for its friendly and affectionate temperament.
My friend adopted a Border terrier from a local rescue center last year.
At the dog show, the Border terrier impressed the judges with its agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of small, active dog originally bred for hunting vermin

Etymology

Named after the border region between England and Scotland where the breed originated.
