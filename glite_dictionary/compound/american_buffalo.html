American buffalo

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈbʌfəloʊ / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkən ˈbʌfələʊ / (BrE)
a large, shaggy-haired brown bison native to the plains of North America, also known as the bison
large bison native to North America

Examples

The American buffalo once roamed the plains in enormous herds.
A photo of an American buffalo appeared on the old US nickel coin.
Many parks have reintroduced the American buffalo to help restore the prairie ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - large wild ox with a humped back, native to North America and Europe
  • Degree Higher: buffalo - large bovine animal, also refers generally to bison and related species
