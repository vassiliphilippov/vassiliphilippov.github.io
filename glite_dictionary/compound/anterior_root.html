anterior root

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪriɚ ruːt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪəriə ruːt / (BrE)
one of the two roots of a spinal nerve that emerges from the front (ventral side) of the spinal cord, carrying motor nerve fibers away from the spinal cord
front motor root of a spinal nerve
medical

Examples

Damage to the anterior root can result in the loss of voluntary muscle movement.
During surgery, the surgeon identified the anterior root to avoid nerve injury.
The function of the anterior root is to transmit motor signals from the spinal cord to the muscles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior root - the sensory root of a spinal nerve that emerges from the back (dorsal side) of the spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin 'anterior' (nearer the front) and 'root' (base or origin).
