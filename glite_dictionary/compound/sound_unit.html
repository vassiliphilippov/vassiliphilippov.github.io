sound unit

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
any unit used to measure sound, such as loudness or intensity in acoustics or audio engineering.
unit of measurement for sound

Examples

The decibel is the most common sound unit in audio engineering.
Scientists use a sound unit to quantify noise pollution in urban environments.
Each sound unit helps standardize measurements for acoustic research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decibel - a unit used to measure the intensity of a sound
