degree of a polynomial

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡri əv ə pəˈlɪn.i.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriː əv ə pəˈlɪn.i.əl / (BrE)
the highest power of the variable in a polynomial, represented by the term with the greatest exponent.
highest power in a polynomial expression
medical

Examples

The degree of a polynomial helps determine how many solutions an equation can have.
To find the degree of a polynomial, look for the term with the largest exponent.
A linear equation has a degree of a polynomial equal to one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order of a polynomial - the degree of a polynomial; another term for the same concept

Etymology

From Latin 'polynomialis', from 'poly-' (many) and 'nomial' from 'nomen' (name), and 'degree' from Latin 'gradus' (step).
