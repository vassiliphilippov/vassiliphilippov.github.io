relative clause

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv klɔːz / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv klɑːz / (BrE)
A subordinate clause introduced by a relative pronoun such as who, which, or that, which modifies a noun or pronoun in the main clause.
subordinate clause introduced by a relative pronoun

Examples

The sentence contains a relative clause beginning with 'who'.
Teachers often ask students to identify the relative clause in a sentence.
A relative clause provides additional information about a noun.

Synset Examples

‘who visits frequently’ is a relative clause in the sentence ‘John, who visits frequently, is ill’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjective clause - a clause that functions as an adjective, often called a relative clause

Etymology

From Latin 'relativus' (having reference or relation) and 'clausula' (a clause in a sentence).
