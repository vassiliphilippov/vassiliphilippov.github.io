genus Simarouba

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˌmɑːˈruːbə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs sɪˌmaruːˈbə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Simaroubaceae, consisting of tropical American trees and shrubs known for their pale, soft wood and bitter bark.
tropical American tree and shrub genus

Examples

The genus Simarouba includes several species valued for medicinal and timber uses.
Researchers studied the bark of the genus Simarouba for its potential therapeutic compounds.
The tropical forests are home to many trees from the genus Simarouba.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Simarouba', a genus name, originally from a native Caribbean term.
