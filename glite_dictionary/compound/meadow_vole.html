meadow vole

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdoʊ voʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ vəʊl / (BrE)
A small rodent found in grasslands and meadows of northern North America, known for its short tail and stocky body.
a small North American grassland rodent

Examples

A meadow vole quickly scurried through the tall grass as we walked by.
Many predators, such as hawks and owls, rely on the meadow vole as a food source.
Researchers are tracking the movements of the meadow vole to study ecosystem changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small, burrowing rodent closely related to lemmings and mice
