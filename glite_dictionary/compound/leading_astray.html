leading astray

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdɪŋ əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪŋ əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
the act of causing someone to go morally, ethically, or behaviorally wrong; the act of enticing others into wrongdoing or error
the act of enticing into wrongdoing

Examples

His constant lies were a form of leading astray that caused others to make poor choices.
Many parents worry about the leading astray of their children by negative influences.
She accused him of leading astray her younger brother with his reckless lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temptation - the desire to do something wrong or unwise
  • Synonym: corruption - the act or process of making someone or something morally depraved
  • Antonym: guidance - the act of leading or showing the right path
