curlycup gumweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrliˌkʌp ˈɡʌmˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkɜːliˌkʌp ˈɡʌmˌwiːd / (BrE)
a perennial plant of western and central North America, known for its sticky, yellow, daisy-like flowers and curled bracts, commonly found in prairies and disturbed ground
a North American perennial plant with sticky yellow flowers

Examples

The curlycup gumweed thrives in areas with poor, rocky soil across the Midwest.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of curlycup gumweed in traditional Native American remedies.
When hiking on the prairie, we noticed patches of curlycup gumweed with their distinctive yellow blooms.
