propellant explosive

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɛl.ənt ɪkˈsploʊ.sɪv / (AmE), / prəˈpɛl.ənt ɪkˈspləʊ.sɪv / (BrE)
an explosive substance used to propel projectiles from guns or to launch rockets, missiles, torpedoes, or depth charges
explosive used for propulsion in weaponry and rockets
medical

Examples

Modern artillery relies on a propellant explosive to launch shells over long distances.
Solid propellant explosive mixtures are commonly used in rocket engines for space missions.
The safety protocols for storing propellant explosive materials are extremely strict due to the high risk involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propellant - substance used to propel something
  • Synonym: explosive - substance that can cause an explosion

Etymology

From 'propellant' (driving or propelling force) + 'explosive.'
