baud rate

1. NOUN

/ bɔd reɪt / (AmE), / bɔːd reɪt / (BrE)
the rate at which symbols or signal units are transmitted per second in a communication channel, often used to describe data transmission speed in modems
rate of data transmission in symbols per second

Examples

The modem's maximum baud rate is 56,000 symbols per second.
Increasing the baud rate can improve data transfer speeds between devices.
Older telephone lines were limited in their baud rate, affecting download times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit rate - the number of bits transmitted per second

Etymology

Named after Émile Baudot, a French telegraph engineer (1845–1903), combined with English 'rate'.
