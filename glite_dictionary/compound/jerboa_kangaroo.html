jerboa kangaroo

1. NOUN

/ dʒərˈboʊ ə ˈkæŋɡəˌru / (AmE), / dʒəˈbəʊ ə ˈkæŋɡəruː / (BrE)
A small Australian marsupial, also known as the brush-tailed rat kangaroo, with long hind legs and a brush-like tail, resembling a jerboa in appearance.
small Australian marsupial (brush-tailed rat kangaroo)

Examples

The jerboa kangaroo is found in the forests of eastern Australia.
Unlike other kangaroos, the jerboa kangaroo is active mostly at dawn and dusk.
Scientists are studying the diet of the endangered jerboa kangaroo to better protect its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush-tailed rat kangaroo - another name for the jerboa kangaroo

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the jerboa (a hopping rodent) and for being a type of kangaroo.
