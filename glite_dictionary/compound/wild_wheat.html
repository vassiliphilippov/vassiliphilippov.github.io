wild wheat

1. NOUN

/ waɪld wiːt / (AmE), / waɪld wiːt / (BrE)
a type of wheat that grows naturally without cultivation, particularly those found in Palestine and considered the ancestors of domesticated wheat
wheat species growing in the wild

Examples

Many researchers study wild wheat to understand the origins of modern crops.
The genetic diversity of wild wheat makes it valuable for breeding disease-resistant varieties.
Archaeologists discovered remains of wild wheat at an ancient settlement site in Palestine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cultivated wheat - wheat that has been domesticated and grown by humans for agriculture
