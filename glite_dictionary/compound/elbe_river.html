Elbe River

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛlb riːvər / (AmE), / ˈɛlb ˌrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in central Europe, rising in the Czech Republic and flowing north through Germany to the North Sea
major river in central Europe

Examples

Many important cities, including Dresden and Hamburg, are located along the Elbe River.
During World War II, Allied and Soviet forces met at the Elbe River in 1945.
The Elbe River flows more than 1,000 kilometers from the Czech Republic to the North Sea.

Etymology

From German 'Elbe', from Latin 'Albis', of uncertain origin.
