cross-pollination of ideas

1. NOUN

/ krɑs ˌpɑləˈneɪʃən əv aɪˈdiəz / (AmE), / krɒs ˌpɒlɪˈneɪʃn əv aɪˈdɪəz / (BrE)
the exchange and blending of ideas between different people, groups, or disciplines, leading to the creation of new concepts or solutions
exchange and blending of ideas

Examples

The new research lab fosters cross-pollination of ideas among scientists from various disciplines.
International conferences are valuable for the cross-pollination of ideas between different cultures and fields.
By hiring employees with diverse backgrounds, the company encouraged cross-pollination of ideas and creativity.

Synset Examples

The conference encouraged cross-pollination of ideas between industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainstorming - the process of generating ideas collectively, typically in a group
  • Synonym: collaboration - working together with others to achieve a common goal, especially through sharing ideas

Etymology

From the botanical term 'cross-pollination' (transfer of pollen between different plants) used metaphorically for ideas.
