inverse secant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌvɜrs səˈkænt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌvɜːs sɪˈkænt / (BrE)
the inverse function of the secant; the angle whose secant is equal to a given number.
the inverse function of secant

Examples

In calculus, the inverse secant function is often denoted as arcsec.
To solve for the angle, you need to use the inverse secant of the given value.
Many scientific calculators include a button for inverse secant operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcsecant - another term for inverse secant
  • Antonym: secant - the trigonometric function, the reciprocal of cosine

Etymology

From 'inverse', from Latin 'inversus' (turned upside down), and 'secant', from Latin 'secans' (cutting).
