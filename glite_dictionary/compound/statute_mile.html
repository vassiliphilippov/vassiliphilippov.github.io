statute mile

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuːt maɪl / (AmE), / ˈstætjuːt maɪl / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 1,760 yards or 5,280 feet; exactly 1,609.344 meters, as defined by statute
British/American mile, 1,609.344 meters

Examples

The marathon course is just over twenty-six statute miles long.
Speed limits are given in statute miles per hour in the United States.
The city is approximately five statute miles from the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,609.344 meters
  • Antonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used in navigation, equal to 1,852 meters

Etymology

From 'statute', referring to its legal definition in English law (13th–16th centuries), and 'mile' from Latin 'milia passuum'.
