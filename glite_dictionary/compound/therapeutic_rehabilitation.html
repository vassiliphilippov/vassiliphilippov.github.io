therapeutic rehabilitation

1. NOUN

/ θɛrəˈpjuːtɪk ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɛərəˈpjuːtɪk ˌriːhəˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the treatment process aimed at helping disabled individuals restore function, reduce disabilities, or improve quality of life
process to help disabled people recover or improve
medical

Examples

After surgery, therapeutic rehabilitation helped her regain strength and mobility in her injured leg.
The clinic specializes in therapeutic rehabilitation for stroke patients of all ages.
Comprehensive therapeutic rehabilitation programs include physical, occupational, and speech therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehabilitation - the process of restoring someone to health or normal life

Etymology

From Greek *therapeia* (treatment) and Latin *rehabilitare* (make fit again).
