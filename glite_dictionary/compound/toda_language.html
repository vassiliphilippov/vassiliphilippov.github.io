Toda language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Dravidian language spoken by the Toda people in southern India
Dravidian language of the Toda people, India

Examples

The Toda language is noted for its unique phonetic features among Dravidian tongues.
Researchers have made significant efforts to document the rapidly disappearing Toda language.
Children in certain villages are still taught the Toda language as part of their cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dravidian - a family of languages spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka
