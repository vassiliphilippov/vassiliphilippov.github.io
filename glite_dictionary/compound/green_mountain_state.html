Green Mountain State

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡrin ˈmaʊntən steɪt / (AmE), / ˌɡriːn ˈmaʊntən steɪt / (BrE)
A nickname for the US state of Vermont, located in New England, whose capital is Montpelier.
nickname for Vermont (US state)

Examples

Skiing is a popular winter activity in the Green Mountain State.
Many tourists visit the Green Mountain State to see the vibrant fall foliage.
The Green Mountain State is famous for its maple syrup and scenic landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vermont - a state in New England, United States, whose capital is Montpelier

Etymology

Refers to Vermont's French-derived name 'Vert Mont,' meaning 'green mountain.'
