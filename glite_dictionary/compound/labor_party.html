labor party

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbɚ ˌpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a left-of-center political party formed to represent the interests of working people, often with policies supporting workers' rights and social equality
left-leaning political party for workers

Examples

He has always supported the labor party because of its stance on workers' rights.
Many working-class voters traditionally align with the labor party during elections.
The labor party introduced new policies for social welfare last year.

Etymology

From 'labor' meaning 'work' or 'workers', often referencing the British Labour Party founded in 1900.
