monkey's paw

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋkiz pɔ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkiz pɔː / (BrE)
a symbol or story motif representing unintended or unforeseen consequences resulting from a wish or desire, often with negative or ironic outcomes
(literary) object symbolizing unintended consequences

Examples

The ancient relic acted as a monkey's paw, complicating every wish made upon it.
He warned that the contract might be a monkey's paw, delivering benefits with hidden costs.
In literature, the monkey's paw serves as a cautionary tale about tampering with fate.

Synset Examples

He wished for wealth, but it was a monkey's paw.
The story of the monkey's paw is quite eerie.
The legend of the monkey's paw warns against greed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a spell or object bringing misfortune

Etymology

From the short story 'The Monkey's Paw' (1902) by W.W. Jacobs, in which a magical talisman grants wishes with tragic consequences.
