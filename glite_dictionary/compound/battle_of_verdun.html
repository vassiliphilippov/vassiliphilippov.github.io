Battle of Verdun

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv vərˈdʌn / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv vəˈdʌn / (BrE)
A major World War I battle fought in 1916 near Verdun, France, noted for being one of the longest and bloodiest engagements, where French forces halted a German offensive.
major 1916 WWI battle at Verdun, France

Examples

Many historians consider the Battle of Verdun one of the most grueling conflicts in military history.
Commemorations for those lost in the Battle of Verdun are still held annually in France.
The Battle of Verdun lasted for ten months and resulted in massive casualties on both sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Somme - a major World War I battle fought in 1916 with heavy casualties

2. PROPN

/ ˈbæt̬l əv vərˈdʌn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv vɛəˈdœ̃ / (BrE)
a major battle fought in 1916 during World War I between French and German forces, noted for being one of the bloodiest battles in history
major 1916 WWI battle in France

Examples

The Battle of Verdun is remembered as one of the longest and most brutal campaigns of World War I.
Thousands of soldiers lost their lives during the Battle of Verdun in 1916.
Historians often cite the Battle of Verdun as a symbol of French resilience and determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Somme - a major WWI battle fought in 1916 in France

Etymology

Named after Verdun, a city in northeastern France where the battle took place.
