General Services Administration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnɚl ˈsɝːvəsɪz ədˌmɪnəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəl ˈsɜːvɪsɪz ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a United States federal agency responsible for overseeing federal procurement, real property management, and information resources for the government
US federal agency for procurement and property management
american

Examples

The General Services Administration announced improvements to government office spaces across several states.
Contractors must register with the General Services Administration to bid on federal projects.
The General Services Administration manages thousands of federal buildings nationwide.
