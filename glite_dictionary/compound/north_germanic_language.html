North Germanic language

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ dʒərˈmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / nɔːθ dʒɜːˈmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the Germanic languages spoken primarily in Scandinavia and Iceland, such as Swedish, Danish, Norwegian, Icelandic, and Faroese
Germanic language group of Scandinavia and Iceland

Examples

Icelandic is a North Germanic language still spoken today.
Swedish, Norwegian, and Danish are all classified as North Germanic languages.
Scholars study the origins and differences among the North Germanic languages of Scandinavia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West Germanic language - branch of the Germanic languages spoken in Western and Central Europe (e.g., English, German, Dutch)
  • Antonym: East Germanic language - now extinct branch of Germanic languages (e.g., Gothic)

Etymology

Formed from 'North' (directional adjective), 'Germanic' (relating to Germanic peoples/languages, from Latin 'Germanicus'), and 'language' (from Latin 'lingua').
