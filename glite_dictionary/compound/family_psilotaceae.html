family Psilotaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli saɪlɒtəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli ˌsaɪlɒtəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A small family of lower ferns with nearly naked stems and minute, scalelike leaves.
family of lower ferns (Psilotaceae)

Examples

The family Psilotaceae is characterized by its simple stems and lack of true roots.
Members of the family Psilotaceae are often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
The unique features of the family Psilotaceae make it important for the study of plant evolution.

Etymology

Psilotaceae: from Greek 'psilos' (bare, naked) + botanical suffix '-aceae' (family).
