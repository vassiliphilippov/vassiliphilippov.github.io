Left Bank

1. PROPN

/ lɛft bæŋk / (AmE), / lɛft bæŋk / (BrE)
the area of Paris situated on the southern bank of the River Seine, traditionally known as a center of artistic, literary, and student activity
southern bank region of Paris, artistic center

Examples

Many famous writers and artists lived and worked on the Left Bank during the 20th century.
The café culture of the Left Bank attracted intellectuals from around the world.
Tourists flock to the Left Bank to experience its vibrant atmosphere and historic landmarks.

Etymology

From French *Rive Gauche*, literally 'left bank', referring to the southern bank of the Seine River in Paris.
