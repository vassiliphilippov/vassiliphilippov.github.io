regulator gene

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡ.jə.leɪ.t̬ɚ ˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡ.jʊ.leɪ.tə ˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that produces a repressor substance that controls or inhibits the activity of an operator gene, regulating the expression of other genes
gene controlling other genes' activity
medical

Examples

Mutations in the regulator gene can disrupt the normal control of cell growth.
The regulator gene is responsible for producing substances that suppress the activity of certain operator genes.
Researchers have identified a regulator gene that influences the expression of multiple traits in the organism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: operator gene - a gene whose expression is controlled by a regulator gene
  • Degree Higher: structural gene - a gene that codes for a protein, often regulated by a regulator gene
