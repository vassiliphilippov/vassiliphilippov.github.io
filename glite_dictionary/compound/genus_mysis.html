genus Mysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈmaɪsɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mysidae, a group of small, shrimp-like crustaceans found chiefly in freshwater and marine environments.
type genus of Mysidae family

Examples

The genus Mysis includes several species that inhabit deep freshwater lakes.
Members of the genus Mysis play an important role in aquatic food webs.
New research has identified a previously unknown species within the genus Mysis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mysidae - a family of small shrimp-like crustaceans

Etymology

From New Latin 'Mysis', used as a genus name in zoological classification.
