sanguinary ant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋɡwəˌnɛri ænt / (AmE), / ˈsaŋɡwɪnəri ænt / (BrE)
a species of slave-making ant widely distributed over the Northern Hemisphere, known for raiding other ant colonies to capture their brood
a slave-making ant species

Examples

The sanguinary ant is notorious for raiding the nests of other species to steal their larvae.
Researchers studied the social structure of a colony of sanguinary ants in northern Europe.
A single sanguinary ant colony may enslave hundreds of workers from neighboring ant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Formica sanguinea - the scientific name of the sanguinary ant

Etymology

From Latin 'sanguinarius' (bloody); so named for its aggressive behavior.
