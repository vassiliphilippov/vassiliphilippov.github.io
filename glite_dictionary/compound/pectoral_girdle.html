pectoral girdle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktɚəl ˈɡɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛktərəl ˈɡɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
the bony structure in vertebrates to which the forelimbs (arms, wings, or fins) are attached, typically consisting of the scapulae and clavicles
skeletal support for forelimbs
medical

Examples

The pectoral girdle connects the upper limb bones to the trunk of the body.
In birds, the pectoral girdle is adapted to support flight muscles.
Damage to the pectoral girdle can result in restricted arm movement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pelvic girdle - skeletal structure to which the hindlimbs are attached

Etymology

From Latin 'pectoralis' (of the chest) and 'girdle' (encircling structure).
