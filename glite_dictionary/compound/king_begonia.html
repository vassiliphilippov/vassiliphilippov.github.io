king begonia

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ bɪˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / kɪŋ bɪˈɡəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A type of ornamental hybrid begonia, often grown for its decorative, rough-textured leaves patterned in silver, bronze, purple, and red-brown, with usually inconspicuous flowers.
ornamental begonia with colorful leaves

Examples

The king begonia in her living room window displays striking silver and purple markings on its broad leaves.
Gardeners prize the king begonia for its decorative foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous blooms.
To thrive, a king begonia needs bright indirect light and high humidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Begonia rex - the botanical name for king begonia
