olfactory nerve

1. NOUN

/ ɑlˈfæktəri nɜrv / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæktəri nɜːv / (BrE)
the nerve that transmits sensory information related to smell from the nasal cavity to the brain
nerve transmitting sense of smell
medical

Examples

Damage to the olfactory nerve can result in a loss of the sense of smell.
The olfactory nerve is essential for detecting aromas in the environment.
Doctors examined the patient's olfactory nerve function after the head injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic nerve - the nerve that carries visual information from the retina to the brain
  • Synonym: auditory nerve - the nerve that carries auditory information from the ear to the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'olfactus' (sense of smell) and 'nerve'.
