Hugueninia tanacetifolia

1. NOUN

/ hjuːɡəˈniːniə tæˌnæsɪˈtoʊfiˌleɪ / (AmE), / hjuːɡɪˈniːniə tanəˌsɛtɪˈfɒliːə / (BrE)
a perennial herb with star-shaped hairy leaves and small yellow flowers, found in the mountains of southern Europe; sometimes classified in the genus Sisymbrium
herbaceous plant of southern Europe

Examples

The alpine meadows were dotted with Hugueninia tanacetifolia in full bloom.
Botanists recently reclassified Hugueninia tanacetifolia under a different genus.
Hiking through the Pyrenees, she collected a specimen of Hugueninia tanacetifolia for her study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sisymbrium - a related genus of flowering plants in the mustard family

Etymology

From the genus name Hugueninia and species epithet tanacetifolia, meaning 'with leaves like tansy' (Tanacetum).
