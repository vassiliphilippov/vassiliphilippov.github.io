Van Allen radiation belt

1. NOUN

/ væn ˈælən ˈreɪdiˌeɪʃən bɛlt / (AmE), / væn ˈælən ˈreɪdiˌeɪʃən bɛlt / (BrE)
A region of charged particles held in place by Earth's magnetic field, forming a belt around the planet.
belt of charged particles around Earth

Examples

The Van Allen radiation belt protects the planet by trapping harmful solar particles.
Satellites must be designed to withstand passing through the Van Allen radiation belt.
Scientists were surprised by the intensity of the Van Allen radiation belt discovered during the first space missions.

Etymology

Named after James Van Allen, the American physicist who discovered the belts in 1958.
