tympanic membrane

1. NOUN

/ tɪmˈpænɪk ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / tɪmˈpænɪk ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
a thin membrane separating the outer ear from the middle ear, which vibrates in response to sound waves
membrane in the ear that vibrates with sound
medical

Examples

During the examination, the doctor noticed the tympanic membrane was red and swollen.
A ruptured tympanic membrane can cause temporary hearing loss.
Sound waves strike the tympanic membrane, causing it to vibrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eardrum - the membrane in the ear that vibrates with sound

Etymology

From Latin tympanum (drum) + membrane (membrane).
