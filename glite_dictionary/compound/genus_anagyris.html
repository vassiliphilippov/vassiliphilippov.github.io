genus Anagyris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæn.əˈdʒaɪ.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˌæn.əˈdʒaɪ.rɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs native to southern Europe, characterized by backward-curving seed pods.
a genus of shrubs native to southern Europe

Examples

The genus Anagyris includes only a few species of Mediterranean shrubs.
Botanists are studying the seed dispersal mechanism of genus Anagyris.
Members of genus Anagyris are known for their distinctive curved pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anagyris - A type of Mediterranean shrub in the pea family

Etymology

From Latin 'anagyris', the classical name for a type of bean tree.
