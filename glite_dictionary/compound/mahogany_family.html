mahogany family

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɑɡəni ˈfæməli / (AmE), / məˈhɒɡəni ˈfaməli / (BrE)
a family of tropical trees and shrubs including many important timber and ornamental species, especially mahoganies (family Meliaceae)
family of tropical trees (Meliaceae)

Examples

The mahogany family includes many trees valued for their high-quality wood.
Some members of the mahogany family are grown as ornamental plants in gardens.
A botanist explained how the mahogany family is important in both forestry and horticulture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Meliaceae - the botanical family to which the mahogany family belongs
  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown timber from mahogany trees
