rope yard

1. NOUN

/ roʊp jɑrd / (AmE), / rəʊp jɑːd / (BrE)
A workplace, typically a long, narrow path or shed, where rope is manufactured.
place where rope is made

Examples

The old rope yard near the harbor is now a museum dedicated to traditional rope-making.
Sailors often worked in the rope yard to produce strong lines for ships.
He visited a historic rope yard to learn about maritime crafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ropewalk - a long, narrow building or path where ropes are made
