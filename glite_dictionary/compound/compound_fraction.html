compound fraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.paʊnd ˈfræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.paʊnd ˈfræk.ʃən / (BrE)
a fraction in which the numerator, the denominator, or both, contain one or more fractions
a fraction with fractions in its numerator or denominator

Examples

To simplify a compound fraction, first find a common denominator in the numerator and denominator.
The teacher asked the students to convert a compound fraction into a simple fraction.
A compound fraction may appear in advanced algebra problems requiring careful calculation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex fraction - a fraction where the numerator or denominator is itself a fraction
