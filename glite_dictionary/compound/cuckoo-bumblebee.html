cuckoo-bumblebee

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊku ˈbʌmbəlˌbi / (AmE), / ˈkʊkuː ˈbʌmbəlˌbiː / (BrE)
a type of bee that lays its eggs in the nests of bumblebees and relies on them to raise its young
parasitic bee that invades bumblebee nests

Examples

The cuckoo-bumblebee sneaks into bumblebee nests to lay its eggs.
Scientists observed a cuckoo-bumblebee being fed by unsuspecting worker bees.
Unlike typical bumblebees, the cuckoo-bumblebee does not collect pollen for its young.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bumblebee - a large, fuzzy bee known for its distinctive buzzing sound

Etymology

Named for its parasitic behavior, analogous to the cuckoo bird, which also lays its eggs in other species' nests.
