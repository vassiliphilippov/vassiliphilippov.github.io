atomic power

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑːmɪk ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
Nuclear energy used as a source of electricity, especially for the civilian power grid.
nuclear energy for electricity generation

Examples

Many countries have invested heavily in atomic power to meet their growing energy demands.
Debates about the future of atomic power often focus on safety and waste management.
Some environmentalists support atomic power as a low-carbon alternative to fossil fuels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear power - nuclear energy used to produce electricity
