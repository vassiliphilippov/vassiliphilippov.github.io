stannic sulfide

1. NOUN

/ stǽnɪk ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈstanɪk ˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a yellow crystalline compound, SnS₂, used as a pigment in lacquer and ceramics
a yellow compound used as a pigment

Examples

The delicate yellow color of the vase was achieved using stannic sulfide as a pigment.
Traditional lacquerware sometimes features decorations in which stannic sulfide is suspended for a rich sheen.
Researchers experimented with stannic sulfide to develop new shades for ceramic glazes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin(IV) sulfide - alternative chemical name for stannic sulfide

Etymology

From 'stannic', relating to tin (from Latin stannum), and 'sulfide', from sulfur + -ide.
