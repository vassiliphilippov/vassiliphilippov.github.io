joint liability

1. NOUN

/ dʒɔɪnt laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
Legal responsibility shared by two or more parties for a debt, claim, or judgment, meaning each party is individually and collectively liable.
shared legal responsibility for a debt or obligation
legal

Examples

All partners in the firm have joint liability for business debts.
In a lawsuit, the court imposed joint liability on both defendants.
If you co-sign a loan, you accept joint liability for repayment.

Synset Examples

A joint liability for a debt is the result of two or more parties applying jointly for credit as co-borrowers, which is implied in a general partnership.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: several liability - individual legal responsibility for a debt separate from others
