essential thrombocytopenia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsɛnʃəl ˌθrʌmboʊsaɪtoʊˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəl ˌθrɒmbəʊsaɪtəʊˈpiːnɪə / (BrE)
a primary condition characterized by an abnormally low number of platelets in the blood, not caused by other diseases or external factors
primary low platelet disorder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with essential thrombocytopenia after other causes were ruled out.
Doctors monitor blood counts closely in cases of essential thrombocytopenia to prevent complications.
Research into essential thrombocytopenia has led to new treatment approaches in hematology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombocytopenia - abnormally low number of platelets in the blood

Etymology

Thrombocytopenia from Greek 'thrombos' (clot), 'kytos' (cell), and 'penia' (poverty, lack); 'essential' meaning primary or idiopathic.
