family Theridiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli θɛˌrɪdiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæməli θɪˌrɪdiˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of spiders known as comb-footed spiders, including species such as the common house spider and black widow.
family of comb-footed spiders

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species in the family Theridiidae in South America.
The notorious black widow spider belongs to the family Theridiidae.
Members of the family Theridiidae are known for spinning irregular, tangled webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comb-footed spider - any spider of the family Theridiidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'familia' (family) and 'Theridiidae', the taxonomic name for the group.
