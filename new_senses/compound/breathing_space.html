breathing space

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːðɪŋ ˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbriːðɪŋ ˌspeɪs / (BrE)
an area or situation providing enough room to breathe easily or move freely; also, a short period of relief from pressure or difficulty
an area or period allowing more freedom or relief

Examples

After the meeting, she went for a walk to get some breathing space.
The new regulations will give small businesses some much-needed breathing space.
They cleared out the clutter to create a little breathing space in the office.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbriːðɪŋ speɪs / (AmE), / ˈbriːðɪŋ speɪs / (BrE)
a legally protected period, provided by debt-relief schemes or statute, during which certain debt obligations are paused and most creditor enforcement actions are suspended.
legal pause on debt enforcement
legal

Examples

The new law gives struggling households a twelve-week breathing space from creditor enforcement.
During the breathing space, lenders cannot start or continue most debt collection and enforcement actions.
He applied for a court-ordered breathing space to give him time to seek debt advice and restructure payments.
