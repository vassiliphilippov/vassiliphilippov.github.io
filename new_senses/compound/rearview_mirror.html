rearview mirror

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪr.vju ˌmɪr.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪə.vju ˌmɪr.ər / (BrE)
A mirror in a vehicle, usually fixed above the windshield inside the car, that allows the driver to see the area behind the vehicle through the rear window.
mirror for seeing behind in a car

Examples

A crack in the rearview mirror made it hard for her to see cars behind.
He checked the rearview mirror before changing lanes on the highway.
The driving instructor told him to always glance at the rearview mirror while reversing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side mirror - mirror on the outside of a vehicle for viewing traffic to the side and rear

A vehicle mirror system that displays a live video image from a rear-facing camera on a screen, instead of using a reflective glass surface.
camera-based rearview display

Examples

The new model comes standard with a rearview mirror that shows a bright, wide-angle camera feed at night.
She tilted the visor and the rearview mirror automatically switched to the camera view for better visibility.
Many owners replaced their old glass mirrors with a rearview mirror that streams a wider field of view.
