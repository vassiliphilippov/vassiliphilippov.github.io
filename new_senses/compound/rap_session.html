rap session

1. NOUN

/ ræp ˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ræp ˈseʃ.ən / (BrE)
an informal meeting or discussion where people openly express and share their feelings, ideas, or experiences, often in a supportive group setting
informal group discussion for sharing feelings

Examples

After the workshop, everyone stayed for a rap session to discuss their personal experiences.
The school counselor organized a rap session to help students talk about their stress.
We held a weekly rap session so everyone felt comfortable voicing their opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group therapy - a form of psychotherapy involving group discussion
  • Synonym: discussion - the action or process of talking about something to reach a decision or exchange ideas

Etymology

Derived from 'rap' (slang for talk or conversation) and 'session' (a period devoted to a particular activity), popularized in American English in the 1960s.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ræp ˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ræp ˈseʃ.ən / (BrE)
an informal musical gathering where rappers perform, often freestyling over beats or backing tracks, sometimes collaboratively or competitively.
informal gathering to perform rap
informal

Examples

After school, the crew organized a rap session where they freestyled over old-school beats.
The festival's late-night rap session drew local MCs eager to try new verses.
They held several rap sessions in the studio to record freestyles for their mixtape.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam session - an informal musical gathering where musicians improvise together
  • Synonym: open mic - an event where performers are invited to perform short pieces, often including rappers and singers

Etymology

From 'rap' (the musical style) + 'session' (a period devoted to an activity); vocal usage developed from musical practice in hip-hop culture.
