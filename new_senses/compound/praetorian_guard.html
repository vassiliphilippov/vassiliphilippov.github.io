Praetorian Guard

1. NOUN

/ priˈtɔriən ɡɑrd / (AmE), / priːˈtɔːrɪən ɡɑːd / (BrE)
the elite unit of bodyguards who protected the Roman Emperors in ancient Rome
elite Roman Emperor's bodyguard unit

Examples

During times of unrest, the loyalty of the Praetorian Guard could determine the fate of the emperor.
Historians often debate the influence wielded by the Praetorian Guard over imperial succession.
The Praetorian Guard played a significant role in the politics of ancient Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperial guard - a bodyguard of an emperor or important sovereign

Etymology

From Latin 'Praetoriani' (from 'Praetor', a Roman magistrate); 'guard' from Old French 'garde'.

2. NOUN

/ preɪˈtɔriən ɡɑrd / (AmE), / priːˈtɔːrɪən ɡɑːd / (BrE)
a member of the elite unit of bodyguards who served the Roman emperors
elite Roman imperial bodyguard

Examples

As a Praetorian Guard, he enjoyed privileges not available to ordinary soldiers in Rome.
History books often describe how the Praetorian Guard played a decisive role in the fate of many Roman emperors.
Rumors circulated that the Praetorian Guard had been bribed to support the rival claimant to the throne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bodyguard - a person employed to protect someone important

Etymology

From Latin 'praetorianus' (of a praetor) and 'guard'; 'praetor' was a Roman magistrate.

3. NOUN NEW

/ priˈtɔriən ɡɑrd / (AmE), / priːˈtɔːrɪən ɡɑːd / (BrE)
a leader's loyal inner circle of advisers, supporters, or security who protect them and often exert strong or undue influence over their decisions.
loyal inner circle with undue influence

Examples

Critics said the president's Praetorian Guard blocked reforms and discouraged dissent within the administration.
Corporate insiders described the CEO's Praetorian Guard as an unchallengeable team steering company decisions.
Reporters accused the mayor's Praetorian Guard of shielding him from accountability after the scandal.
