legal assistant

1. NOUN

/ ˈliɡəl əˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl əˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
a person trained to assist lawyers with legal work, but not qualified to practice law independently
person assisting lawyers with legal tasks

Examples

Many legal assistants specialize in specific areas such as real estate or litigation.
She has worked as a legal assistant at the firm for over five years.
The lawyer delegated the research task to her legal assistant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralegal - a person trained to assist lawyers but not authorized to practice law
