bounty hunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnti ˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti ˌhʌntə / (BrE)
a person who pursues and kills or captures animals or criminals for a reward offered by authorities or individuals
person who hunts for a reward

Examples

Historically, bounty hunters were paid to kill wolves or other predators threatening livestock.
She became a bounty hunter, specializing in capturing fugitives wanted by the sheriff.
The old West was full of bounty hunters tracking down outlaws for a living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tracker - person who pursues animals or people

Etymology

From 'bounty' (reward) + 'hunter' (one who hunts). The phrase first appeared in the late 19th century in the United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnti ˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti ˌhʌntə / (BrE)
a person who pursues fugitives or criminals in order to collect a reward offered for their capture
person seeking fugitives for reward

Examples

A bounty hunter must have sharp instincts and a strong knowledge of the law.
After reading about famous bounty hunters, she was fascinated by their risky line of work.
The bounty hunter tracked the fugitive across several states before finally catching him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fugitive - a person who has escaped from captivity or is in hiding
  • Synonym: bail bondsman - a person who provides bail for suspects in return for a fee
