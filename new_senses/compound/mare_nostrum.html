mare nostrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːr eɪ ˈnɒstrəm / (AmE), / ˌmɑːreɪ ˈnɒstrəm / (BrE)
the Mediterranean Sea, especially as referred to by the ancient Romans or in historical contexts
the Mediterranean Sea (historical term)

Examples

During World War II, Italy aspired to transform the Mediterranean into its own mare nostrum.
Historians often refer to the Mediterranean as mare nostrum when discussing Roman naval dominance.
The Romans considered the Mediterranean to be their mare nostrum.

Etymology

From Latin 'mare nostrum', meaning 'our sea', used by ancient Romans to refer to the Mediterranean Sea.
