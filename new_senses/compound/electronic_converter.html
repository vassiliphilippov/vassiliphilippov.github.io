electronic converter

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk kənˈvɝːtɚ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
A device that converts an electrical signal from one frequency to another, especially in telecommunications.
device for changing signal frequency

Examples

An electronic converter can adapt signals for a range of communication devices.
Older radios often require an electronic converter to receive modern frequencies.
The cable TV received input through an electronic converter installed behind the set.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˌlɛktrɑnɪk kənˈvɜrtər / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɒnɪk kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
A device that converts electrical energy from one form, voltage, or type to another (for example, AC to DC, DC to AC, or changing DC voltage levels), commonly used in power supplies and power-electronics systems.
device that converts electrical power (AC/DC, voltage)

Examples

The laptop's electronic converter transforms AC from the wall into the charger's DC output.
At the plant, technicians replaced a faulty electronic converter to stabilize the vehicle's battery voltage.
Solar installers used a high-efficiency electronic converter to change panel DC power into grid-ready AC.
