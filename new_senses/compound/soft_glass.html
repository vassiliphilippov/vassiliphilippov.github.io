soft glass

1. NOUN

/ sɑft ɡlæs / (AmE), / sɒft ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
Glass with a relatively low softening point, making it easier to work with at lower temperatures.
glass with a low softening point

Examples

Many artists prefer soft glass for lampworking because it melts at a lower temperature.
Soda-lime glass is a common type of soft glass used in windows and bottles.
The laboratory switched from hard glass to soft glass tubing for certain experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard glass - glass with a high softening point

2. NOUN NEW

/ sɑft ɡlæs / (AmE), / sɒft ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
(in physics and materials science) an amorphous soft material that behaves like a glassy solid, showing slow relaxation, aging, and yielding; examples include dense colloidal suspensions, concentrated emulsions, and some foams.
soft, amorphous material with glass-like rheology

Examples

Physicists model how soft glass flows under stress in colloidal suspensions.
Some concentrated emulsions behave like a soft glass and slowly relax after deformation.
The team observed aging in a sample of soft glass after repeated shear cycles.
