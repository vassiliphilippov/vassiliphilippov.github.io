capital cost

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtl kɔst / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtl kɒst / (BrE)
the cost of funds used for financing a business, typically measured as the rate of return that could be earned in an alternative investment with equivalent risk
cost of funds for business investment

Examples

A higher capital cost can discourage businesses from expanding into new markets.
Investors consider the capital cost to determine whether a proposed venture is viable.
The company carefully evaluated the capital cost before approving the new factory project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunity cost - the loss of potential gain from other alternatives when one alternative is chosen

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈkæpətəl kɔst / (AmE), / ˈkæpətəl kɒst / (BrE)
The upfront expenditure required to acquire, construct, or install fixed assets for a project or business (for example, buildings, machinery, or major equipment); also called capital expenditure (CAPEX).
upfront expenditure for fixed assets (CAPEX)

Examples

The capital cost of the new factory includes machinery purchase and installation expenses.
When preparing the budget, they underestimated the project's capital cost and ran out of funds.
Banks review expected cash flow carefully before financing high capital cost infrastructure projects.
