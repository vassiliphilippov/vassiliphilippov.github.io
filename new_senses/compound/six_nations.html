Six Nations

1. NOUN

/ sɪks ˈneɪʃənz / (AmE), / sɪks ˈneɪʃənz / (BrE)
a confederacy of Iroquois tribes originally consisting of the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca, later joined by the Tuscarora; also known as the Iroquois Confederacy
Iroquois tribal confederacy

Examples

Delegates from the Six Nations attended the treaty council in Albany.
Descendants of the Six Nations continue to live in communities in Canada and the United States.
The Six Nations played a pivotal role in the politics of colonial North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois Confederacy - another name for the Six Nations; historic alliance of Iroquois tribes
  • Degree Lower: Five Nations - the original confederacy before the Tuscarora joined

2. NOUN NEW

/ sɪks ˈneɪʃənz / (AmE), / sɪks ˈneɪʃənz / (BrE)
an annual European rugby union championship contested by six national teams (England, France, Ireland, Italy, Scotland, and Wales).
annual European rugby union championship
british

Examples

England's team aims to win the Six Nations title again this year.
The match at Twickenham could decide who tops the Six Nations this season.
Fans from Ireland and France traveled together to watch the final Six Nations match.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Six Nations Championship - alternative name for the annual rugby union competition contested by six European nations.
  • Degree Lower: Five Nations - the earlier version of the tournament before Italy joined; predecessor competition.

Etymology

Named for the six national teams; the competition took its current name after Italy joined the Five Nations in 2000.
