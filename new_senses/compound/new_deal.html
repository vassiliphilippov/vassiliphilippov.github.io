New Deal

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈdiːl / (AmE), / njuː ˈdiːl / (BrE)
the set of economic and social policies introduced by President Franklin D. Roosevelt in the 1930s to combat the effects of the Great Depression in the United States.
F.D.R.'s 1930s economic policies
american

Examples

Many historians credit the New Deal with helping the United States recover from the Great Depression.
Social Security is one of the long-lasting legacies of the New Deal.
The New Deal fundamentally reshaped the relationship between the American government and the economy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fair Deal - President Truman's economic and social policy program

Etymology

Refers to Roosevelt's promise of a 'new deal' for the American people during his 1932 presidential campaign.

2. PROPN

/ nuː ˈdiːl / (AmE), / njuː ˈdiːl / (BrE)
the program of economic and social reforms introduced in the United States by President Franklin D. Roosevelt between 1933 and 1940 in response to the Great Depression
U.S. economic and social reform program, 1933–1940

Examples

Franklin D. Roosevelt introduced the New Deal to combat the effects of the Great Depression.
Many historians still debate the effectiveness of the New Deal programs.
The New Deal brought sweeping changes to American society during the 1930s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Depression - the severe worldwide economic depression that took place mostly during the 1930s

Etymology

Refers to Roosevelt's promise of a 'new deal' for the American people; 'deal' in the sense of an arrangement or treatment.

3. NOUN NEW

/ nuː ˈdiːl / (AmE), / njuː ˈdiːl / (BrE)
a large, government-led programme of reforms or public spending, especially one presented as a bold, comprehensive response to major social or economic problems and modeled after Franklin D. Roosevelt's 1930s New Deal.
large government policy package
american

Examples

Many climate proposals today are framed as a New Deal for low-carbon industries and jobs.
The candidate promised a New Deal of public investment and training to revive the region's economy.
Progressive groups urged lawmakers to pass a New Deal focused on inequality and green infrastructure.
