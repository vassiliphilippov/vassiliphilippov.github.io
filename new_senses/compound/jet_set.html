jet set

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛt sɛt / (AmE), / dʒɛt sɛt / (BrE)
a group of wealthy and fashionable people who travel frequently for pleasure and social events
wealthy people who travel for pleasure

Examples

Despite his success, he never cared about being part of the jet set.
She always dreamed of joining the jet set and flying to glamorous destinations.
The jet set gathered in Monaco for the annual Grand Prix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialite - a person who is well known in fashionable society

Etymology

From 'jet' (airplane) and 'set' (group), popularized in the 1950s with the rise of commercial jet travel.

2. VERB NEW

/ dʒɛt sɛt / (AmE), / dʒɛt sɛt / (BrE)
to travel frequently and in style, especially by private jet, as part of a wealthy or fashionable lifestyle.
travel frequently in style
informal

Examples

Celebrities began to jet-set around the world after the film's release.
They jet-set between Cannes and New York every summer for fashion events.
After their success, she spent a year jet-setting to exclusive parties and galas.
