scale up

1. NOUN NEW

/ skeɪl ʌp / (AmE), / skeɪl ʌp / (BrE)
a company that has moved beyond the startup phase and is growing rapidly, typically expanding operations, staff, and revenues.
rapidly growing company (post-startup)

Examples

The tech scale-up hired fifty new engineers after securing Series B funding last month.
Local investors are excited to support a promising scale-up entering international markets this year.
Investors often look for a scale-up's proven ability to double revenue within two years.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth company - a business focused on rapid expansion in revenue or market share
  • Antonym: startup - a newly established company in an early development phase, often before scaling

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'scale up' (to increase in size or amount); used from late 20th/early 21st century as a business noun.
