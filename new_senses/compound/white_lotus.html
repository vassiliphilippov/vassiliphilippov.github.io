white lotus

1. NOUN

/ waɪt ˈloʊ.təs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
A water lily (Nymphaea lotus), native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, with white fragrant flowers, held sacred by the ancient Egyptians.
a white-flowered Egyptian water lily

Examples

Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the white lotus found along the Nile.
Several white lotus flowers floated gracefully on the surface of the pond.
The ancient Egyptians revered the white lotus as a symbol of purity and rebirth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blue lotus - another species of Egyptian water lily (Nymphaea caerulea)

2. NOUN NEW

/ waɪt ˈloʊ.təs / (AmE), / waɪt ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
Informal slang for a person (often privileged) who pretends to be innocent, pure, or morally superior in order to manipulate others or avoid responsibility.
person feigning innocence or virtue
slang informal

Examples

Many social media users called her a white lotus after she pretended not to know the rules.
The article argued that wealthy influencers often behave like a white lotus, playing innocent to gain sympathy.
At the meeting he was exposed as a white lotus, using fake concern to win support for his project.
