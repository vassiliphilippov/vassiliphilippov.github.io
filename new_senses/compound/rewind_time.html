to rewind time

1. VERB

/ riˈwaɪnd taɪm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwaɪnd taɪm / (BrE)
to imagine or wish that one could go back to an earlier moment in the past; to reflect on or revisit past events, often with regret or longing.
to imagine going back in time

Examples

If I could rewind time, I would have spent more time with my family.
No matter how much we want to rewind time, we have to accept the past.
Sometimes people wish they could rewind time to fix their mistakes.

Synset Examples

I wish I could rewind time and make different choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn back the clock - to return to an earlier time or situation

Etymology

Based on 'rewind' meaning to wind back (as in tapes or film), here used figuratively with 'time'.

2. VERB NEW

/ riˈwaɪnd taɪm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwaɪnd taɪm / (BrE)
(in videogames) to reverse recent events in the game's timeline so actions can be undone and retried.
reverse recent events in a game

Examples

The game lets you rewind time to retry the risky leap without losing progress.
She rewound time and avoided the trap, which saved her run in the tournament.
Many speedrunners practice by rewinding time after mistakes to perfect their routes.

Synset Examples

The game lets you rewind time to retry the risky leap without losing progress.
She rewound time and avoided the trap, which saved her run in the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undo - to reverse a previous action; in software or games, to cancel or revert a recent operation.
  • Synonym: turn back the clock - to return to an earlier time or situation; used figuratively to describe reversing events.

Etymology

Based on 'rewind' (to wind back, as with tapes); applied literally to reversing a game's timeline.
