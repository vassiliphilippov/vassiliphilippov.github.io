Maxwell's demon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæks.wɛlz ˈdiː.mən / (AmE), / ˈmæks.wɛlz ˈdiː.mən / (BrE)
an imaginary creature in thought experiments of physics, proposed by James Clerk Maxwell, that supposedly violates the second law of thermodynamics by selectively allowing fast or slow molecules to pass through a partition, thus creating a temperature difference without expending energy
an imaginary creature in physics thought experiments

Examples

One of the most famous thought experiments in thermodynamics involves Maxwell's demon.
Physicists debate whether Maxwell's demon could truly violate the second law of thermodynamics in practice.
The concept of Maxwell's demon highlights the relationship between information and entropy.

Etymology

Named after physicist James Clerk Maxwell, who proposed the thought experiment in 1867.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmæks.wəlz ˈdiː.mən / (AmE), / ˈmæks.wəlz ˈdiː.mən / (BrE)
(in experimental physics) a physical device or system that uses measurement and feedback to extract work or reduce entropy from a thermal bath, thereby implementing the idea of Maxwell's thought experiment.
experimental device implementing the thought experiment

Examples

Researchers built a nanoscale setup that acts as a Maxwell's demon, converting information into work.
The lab demonstrated a feedback-controlled engine functioning like a Maxwell's demon in the experiment.
Their paper explains how the device qualifies as an experimental Maxwell's demon under precise measurement protocols.
