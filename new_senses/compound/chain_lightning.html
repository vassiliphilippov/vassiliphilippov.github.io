chain lightning

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of lightning that travels quickly in a zigzag path, often branching at one end like a fork
lightning with a zigzag, branching path

Examples

As the thunder rumbled, a bolt of chain lightning split into several forks above the field.
Scientists studied photos of chain lightning to understand its complex, zigzagging pattern.
The storm was illuminated by streaks of chain lightning arcing across the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forked lightning - lightning appearing as a branching line in the sky

2. NOUN NEW

/ tʃeɪn ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
(in fantasy, gaming, and fiction) a magical or special attack that strikes one target and then arcs to hit additional nearby targets in sequence.
magical attack hitting multiple targets

Examples

In the final battle, the wizard unleashed chain lightning that struck five enemies in sequence.
Our party's rogue feared entering the cavern after the sorcerer used chain lightning twice.
The game's new update balances chain lightning so it no longer wipes out low-level groups.
