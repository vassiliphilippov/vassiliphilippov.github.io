tax program

1. NOUN

/ tæks ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / tæks ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
a plan or system for determining and implementing taxes in a government or organization
plan for setting and collecting taxes

Examples

Critics argued that the tax program would benefit the wealthy most.
Each political party presented its own tax program during the election campaign.
The government introduced a new tax program to simplify the tax code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax policy - government guidelines for taxation

2. NOUN NEW

/ tæks ˈproʊgræm / (AmE), / tæks ˈprəʊgræm / (BrE)
a computer program or software application used to calculate, prepare, or file tax returns and related financial documents.
tax preparation software

Examples

She bought a new tax program to help her file last year's complicated returns online.
Small business owners often rely on a reliable tax program to calculate quarterly payments accurately.
The accountant compared output from two tax program reports before submitting the client's files.
