bill of health

1. NOUN

/ bɪl əv hɛlθ / (AmE), / bɪl əv hɛlθ / (BrE)
an official document stating that a ship's crew is free from contagious disease, required to be presented at the next port of arrival
certificate showing a ship's crew is healthy
legal

Examples

Because of an outbreak onboard, the ship was denied a clean bill of health.
Every vessel arriving from overseas must present a bill of health at the port authority.
The harbor master refused entry until the bill of health was produced.

2. NOUN NEW

/ bɪl əv hɛlθ / (AmE), / bɪl əv hɛlθ / (BrE)
an authoritative statement or report saying that a person, project, organisation, or thing is healthy, safe, or fit to proceed; a formal sign of approval or clearance.
formal positive assessment or clearance

Examples

After the inspection, the committee gave the project a bill of health to proceed.
The doctor’s quick check gave him a clean bill of health to return to work.
Investors received a bill of health after the audit confirmed stable finances.
