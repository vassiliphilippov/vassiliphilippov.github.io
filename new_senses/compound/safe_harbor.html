safe harbor

1. NOUN

/ seɪf ˈhɑrbɚ / (AmE), / seɪf ˈhɑːbɑː / (BrE)
a strategy by which a company seeks protection from a hostile takeover by acquiring another company that is heavily regulated or has other attributes making the original company less attractive to the acquirer
business strategy for takeover defense
legal

Examples

Analysts discussed whether the new purchase would function as a true safe harbor for the board.
By merging with a highly regulated firm, they created a safe harbor to deter potential buyers.
The acquisition gave the company a safe harbor from aggressive takeover attempts.

Synset Examples

the acquisition gave the company a safe harbor

Etymology

From 'safe harbor' (a shelter from danger), adopted into business and legal jargon.

2. NOUN

/ seɪf ˈhɑrbɚ / (AmE), / seɪf ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
a place or situation offering protection or refuge from danger or difficulty
place or situation offering protection or refuge

Examples

Many people look for a safe harbor in times of uncertainty.
The port served as a safe harbor during the storm.
The witness was offered a safe harbor after testifying in court.

Synset Examples

The anchorage provided safe harbor for the fleet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place of shelter or protection from danger
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of safety or protection

3. NOUN NEW

/ seɪf ˈhɑrbə / (AmE), / seɪf ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
a legal or regulatory provision that protects an organization from liability or enforcement if it meets specified requirements or standards.
legal/regulatory protection from liability
legal

Examples

Many tech companies relied on the safe harbor agreement to justify cross-border user data transfers.
The regulator said firms would avoid penalties if they followed the published safe harbor procedures.
Lawyers argued the new guidance did not create a reliable safe harbor for automated profiling systems.

Synset Examples
