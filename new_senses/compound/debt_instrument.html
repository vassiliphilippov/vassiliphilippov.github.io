debt instrument

1. NOUN

/ dɛt ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / dɛt ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
a written or printed document acknowledging a loan and specifying the terms of repayment
document acknowledging a loan

Examples

A bond is a common type of debt instrument used by governments and corporations.
Before investing, you should carefully review the terms of any debt instrument you consider.
The company issued a debt instrument to raise capital for its new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a debt security in which an investor loans money to an entity for a defined period at fixed interest
  • Synonym: promissory note - a written promise to pay a specified sum of money to a certain person at a future date
