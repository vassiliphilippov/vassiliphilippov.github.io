racial profiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪʃəl ˈproʊfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəl ˈprəʊfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice by law enforcement officials of targeting or suspecting people of crimes based on their race or ethnicity
the targeting of individuals by race by police

Examples

Some argue that racial profiling undermines trust between law enforcement and minority communities.
The community protested against racial profiling by local police.
The new policy was intended to eliminate racial profiling at traffic stops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profiling - the recording and analysis of a person's psychological and behavioral characteristics
  • Synonym: discrimination - unjust or prejudicial treatment based on group characteristics

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8re\u026a\u0283\u0259l \u02c8pro\u028afa\u026al\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8re\u026a\u0283\u0259l \u02c8pr\u0259\u028afa\u026al\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
(in technology, law, and ethics) the practice of using race or race-correlated data in automated systems or data-driven processes to make decisions that disadvantage particular racial or ethnic groups.
race-based bias by automated systems
legal

Examples

Civil rights groups sued after evidence suggested the bank's algorithm performed racial profiling in loan approvals.
Researchers warned that biased training data could cause facial-recognition systems to engage in racial profiling against minorities.
The company's audit found unintentional racial profiling when automated hiring filters screened out applicants from certain neighborhoods.
