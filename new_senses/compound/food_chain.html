food chain

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːd tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈfuːd tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a series of organisms each dependent on the next as a source of food, especially within an ecological community
sequence of organisms as food sources

Examples

In science class, we learned how energy flows through the food chain from plants to predators.
Pesticides in the water have impacted the food chain, harming fish and birds alike.
The disappearance of a single species can affect the entire food chain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food web - a network of interconnected food chains in an ecosystem

2. NOUN

/ fuːd tʃeɪn / (AmE), / fuːd tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a hierarchical series of organisms each dependent on the next as a source of food, typically showing the transfer of energy and nutrients through an ecosystem
hierarchy of organisms as sources of food

Examples

Humans are often considered to be at the top of the food chain in many ecosystems.
In the ocean food chain, plankton are consumed by small fish, which are then eaten by larger predators.
The food chain can be disrupted by environmental changes such as pollution or deforestation.

Synset Examples

The food chain shows how energy moves through ecosystems.
Studying the food chain is essential for ecology.
Pollution affects the entire food chain in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food web - a system of interdependent food chains in an ecosystem

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfuːd tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈfuːd tʃeɪn / (BrE)
the system and sequence of production, processing, distribution, and sale by which food moves from producers to consumers
system for producing and supplying food

Examples

The global food chain was disrupted by the drought, causing price spikes for basic staples.
Farmers and processors met to redesign the local food chain and shorten delivery times.
Consumers demanded transparency about origins across every link in the food chain.
