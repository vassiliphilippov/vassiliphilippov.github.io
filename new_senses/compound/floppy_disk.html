floppy disk

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑːpi dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈflɒpi dɪsk / (BrE)
A thin, flexible magnetic disk enclosed in a square or rectangular plastic shell, used for storing computer data.
magnetic data storage device

Examples

Before USB drives, people commonly used a floppy disk to transfer files between computers.
He found a box of floppy disks in the attic, filled with vintage games and documents.
The old computer could only read data from a floppy disk.

Synset Examples

floppy disks are noted for their relatively slow speed and small capacity and low price

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard disk - a rigid magnetic disk used for storing computer data

Etymology

From 'floppy' (flexible or bendable) and 'disk'; named for the disk’s flexible material.

A small graphical icon (derived from the physical floppy disk) used in software interfaces to represent the command to save a file or document.
save-icon (software)

Examples

On the toolbar, she clicked the floppy disk icon to save her changes before closing.
Even though no one uses them, the small floppy disk symbol still means 'save' to most people.
He taught his grandmother to click the floppy disk button whenever she wanted to keep a document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save icon - a graphical button or symbol in a user interface that saves a file or document
  • Synonym: icon - a small pictorial symbol used in computer interfaces to represent a function, file, or object

Etymology

Named after the physical floppy disk; the disk's image was adopted as a pictorial metaphor for the 'save' function in early software.
