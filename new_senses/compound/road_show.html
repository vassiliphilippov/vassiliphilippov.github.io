road show

1. NOUN

/ roʊd ʃoʊ / (AmE), / rəʊd ʃəʊ / (BrE)
a theatrical performance, exhibition, or event that travels to various locations for public viewing.
touring show or performance

Examples

After a successful opening, the musical went on a year-long road show.
Fans lined up early to see the road show when it arrived in town.
The theater troupe launched their new road show across several major cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touring company - a company of actors or performers that travels to different locations to perform

2. NOUN NEW

/ ro\u028ad \u0283o\u028a / (AmE), / r\u0259\u028ad \u0283\u0259\u028a / (BrE)
(in business, especially finance) a series of presentations given in different locations to potential investors to promote a company or a securities offering.
series of investor presentations

Examples

Before the IPO, the executives toured Europe on a road show to meet institutional investors.
The investment bank organized a series of road show presentations to drum up interest in the offering.
After the scandal, several investors pulled out despite the company's well-prepared road show schedule.
