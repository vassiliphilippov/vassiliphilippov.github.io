bug-hunter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡ ˌhʌntər / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡ ˌhʌntə / (BrE)
A zoologist who studies insects.
a zoologist specializing in insects

Examples

A skilled bug-hunter can identify hundreds of beetle species by sight alone.
Ever since childhood, she dreamed of becoming a bug-hunter and eventually made it her career.
The bug-hunter carefully examined the rainforest's foliage for rare insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entomologist - a scientist who studies insects

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8b\u028c\u0261 \u02cch\u028cnt\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8b\u028c\u0261 \u02cch\u028cnt\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing and cybersecurity) a person who searches for and reports software bugs or security vulnerabilities, often participating in bug-bounty programs.
software vulnerability finder

Examples

A renowned bug-hunter helped the company fix critical vulnerabilities during the external security audit.
Several bug-hunters reported the memory leak on the forum, earning praise from the developer team.
She earned a reputation as a meticulous bug-hunter after reporting dozens of serious vulnerabilities.
