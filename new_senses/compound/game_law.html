game law

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪm lɔ / (AmE), / ɡeɪm lɔː / (BrE)
a law or regulation intended to manage, conserve, or protect game animals, especially by controlling hunting and trapping
law managing hunting or game animals
legal

Examples

Hunters must be familiar with local game laws before going out in the field.
The new game law has helped increase the deer population in the region.
Violating a game law can result in hefty fines or even jail time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildlife law - the body of law that governs the conservation and use of wildlife
  • Antonym: poaching - illegal hunting or capturing of wild animals

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɡeɪm lɔ / (AmE), / ɡeɪm lɔː / (BrE)
The body of law and regulation that applies to video games and related digital platforms, including licensing, intellectual property, consumer protection, monetisation (e.g., loot boxes), and privacy.
law governing video games and digital platforms
legal

Examples

The startup hired a game law attorney to navigate loot-box regulations and IP issues.
Recent changes in game law require clearer age ratings and consumer refund protections for digital purchases.
European regulators are debating whether existing game laws adequately address in-game microtransactions and privacy.
