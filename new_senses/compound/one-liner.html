one-liner

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn ˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwʌn ˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
A short and witty joke or remark delivered in a single line.
a short witty joke or remark

Examples

His stand-up routine is packed with clever one-liner jokes.
She’s famous for her sharp one-liners during meetings.
The comedian delivered a brilliant one-liner that had the audience in stitches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quip - a witty remark
  • Synonym: pun - a joke exploiting different possible meanings of a word

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈwʌnˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwʌnˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
A single line of computer code or a short command that performs a complete task or expresses a small, useful operation.
single-line code snippet

Examples

The tutorial showed a clever one-liner that sorted the list in just a single function call.
During the code review, she suggested replacing several lines with a short one-liner.
Many scripting languages let you write powerful one-liners to process text quickly on the command line.
