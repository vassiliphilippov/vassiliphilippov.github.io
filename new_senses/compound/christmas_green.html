Christmas green

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs·məs ɡriːn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs·məs ɡriːn / (BrE)
any of various club mosses with long creeping stems and upright branches, often used for decoration at Christmas.
a club moss used for Christmas decoration

Examples

Florists often sell bundles of Christmas green for seasonal arrangements.
Many people use Christmas green to create festive wreaths during the holidays.
The forest floor was covered in thick patches of Christmas green beneath the trees.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈkrɪsməs ɡriːn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsməs ɡriːn / (BrE)
a shade of green associated with Christmas; used to describe colors in decorations, clothing, or design that evoke the holiday.
festive shade of green

Examples

The designer chose Christmas green upholstery to contrast with the store's bright red decorations.
For a subtle holiday theme she painted the kitchen cabinets Christmas green instead of bright red.
He bought a sweater in deep Christmas green that matched his family's seasonal photo.
