special interest group

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp / (BrE)
an organization of people with shared interests that seeks to influence government policy or decision-making, especially through lobbying
group seeking to influence government policy

Examples

Many politicians receive funding from various special interest groups during their election campaigns.
She works as a lobbyist for a healthcare special interest group based in Washington, D.C.
The environmental special interest group launched a campaign to promote renewable energy legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobby - to seek to influence legislators or officials on behalf of a group

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp / (BrE)
a subgroup within a larger organization or community formed to focus on a specific topic, activity, or technical area (for example, standards, research, or community projects), not primarily intended for political lobbying.
subgroup focused on a specific topic

Examples

At the software conference, a special interest group focused on open-source governance presented new standards.
The university's energy special interest group organizes monthly seminars on renewable technology and policy.
Volunteers joined a local special interest group to coordinate community gardening and shared resources.
