round robin

1. NOUN

/ ˌraʊnd ˈrɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˌraʊnd ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A letter or petition that is signed by several people, often to express a collective opinion without singling out any one signatory.
letter signed by multiple people

Examples

A round robin was circulated among the students to protest the new policy.
The employees wrote a round robin to the manager requesting better working conditions.
They presented a round robin to the board, making it clear the concerns were shared by many.

Etymology

Possibly from the French 'ruban rond' (round ribbon), referring to the circular arrangement of signatures to avoid hierarchy.

2. NOUN

/ raʊnd ˈrɑː.bɪn / (AmE), / raʊnd ˈrɒb.ɪn / (BrE)
A tournament or competition in which each contestant plays against every other contestant in turn.
tournament where everyone plays everyone else

Examples

After the round robin stage, the top two players advanced to the finals.
In a round robin tournament, each team has the chance to play all the others.
The chess club organized a round robin to determine the overall winner fairly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knockout tournament - tournament where loser is eliminated each round

Etymology

From the phrase 'round robin', originally referring to a petition signed in a circle to disguise the order of signatures, later applied to circular or rotational orders in competitions.

3. NOUN NEW

/ raʊnd ˈrɑː.bɪn / (AmE), / raʊnd ˈrɒb.ɪn / (BrE)
A method of distributing tasks, connections, or requests by assigning them in turn to each server or process in a fixed, cyclical order.
rotating distribution method (computing)

Examples

The load balancer used a round robin algorithm to send each request to a different server.
DNS round robin returned multiple IP addresses, letting browsers connect to different servers for redundancy.
To prevent overload, the team configured the proxy to distribute sessions using strict round robin order among nodes.
