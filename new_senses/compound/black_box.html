black box

1. NOUN

/ blæk bɑks / (AmE), / blæk bɒks / (BrE)
an electronic device in an aircraft that records flight data and cockpit voice information, used to investigate accidents
device that records aircraft flight data

Examples

Every commercial aircraft is equipped with a black box to record flight information.
Investigators recovered the black box to learn what happened before the crash.
The data stored in the black box helped explain the cause of the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight recorder - an instrument that records various aspects of an aircraft's operation

2. NOUN NEW

/ blæk bɑks / (AmE), / blæk bɒks / (BrE)
a system, model, or process whose internal workings are hidden, unknown, or not examined, so only its inputs and outputs are observed; often used in computing, AI, and engineering.
system with hidden internal workings

Examples

Many AI models are treated as a black box because their internal reasoning is not transparent.
Regulators demanded explanations instead of relying on the bank's algorithmic black box for loan approvals.
Engineers prefer white-box testing when possible rather than treating the control system as a black box.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opaque system - a system whose internal processes are not visible or understandable
  • Antonym: white box - a system or model whose internal structure and processes are visible and can be examined

Etymology

Originally from the aircraft flight recorder (called a 'black box'); later extended metaphorically to any system with unobservable internal mechanisms.
