trace detector

1. NOUN

/ treɪs dɪˈtɛktɚ / (AmE), / treɪs dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
a device used to screen for small amounts of explosives, especially in security settings such as airports
device for detecting traces of explosives

Examples

A trace detector can provide results within seconds, making it ideal for busy security checkpoints.
Modern trace detectors are highly sensitive and can analyze even tiny amounts of chemical substances.
The airport security officer used a trace detector to check passengers' luggage for any residues of explosives.

2. NOUN NEW

/ treɪs dɪˈtɛktə / (AmE), / treɪs dɪˈtɛktə / (BrE)
a device for identifying or measuring very small amounts of chemical substances (such as pollutants, toxins, or residues) in air, water, or on surfaces.
device detecting tiny chemical traces

Examples

Public health teams used a portable trace detector to identify low levels of airborne pollutants near the factory.
Researchers deployed a water trace detector downstream to monitor chemical contamination after the spill.
The lab technician calibrated the new trace detector before measuring toxic vapors in the storage room.
