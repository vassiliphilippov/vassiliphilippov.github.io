genus Ginkgo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡɪŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈɡɪŋkəʊ / (BrE)
The only remaining genus in the family Ginkgoaceae, consisting of the species Ginkgo biloba, a unique and ancient tree.
taxonomic genus including Ginkgo biloba

Examples

Botanists study the genus Ginkgo to understand ancient plant evolution.
Only one species, Ginkgo biloba, remains within the genus Ginkgo today.
The genus Ginkgo is considered a living fossil, with no close living relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgo biloba - the only extant species within the genus Ginkgo

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and Japanese 'ginkyō' (silver apricot), from Chinese 'yínxìng' (银杏).
