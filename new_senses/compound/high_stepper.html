high stepper

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈstɛpər / (AmE), / haɪ ˈstɛpə / (BrE)
A horse trained to lift its feet unusually high off the ground while walking or trotting, often for show or competition.
horse that steps with high leg action

Examples

At the parade, the crowd admired the high stepper for its graceful and exaggerated gait.
Many trainers spend years teaching a young horse to become a skilled high stepper.
The high stepper impressed the judges with its elegant movement in the arena.

2. NOUN NEW

/ haɪ ˈstɛpər / (AmE), / haɪ ˈstɛpə / (BrE)
A person who walks or marches with exaggerated high lifts of the feet, often as part of a performance, march, or to attract attention.
person who walks with exaggerated high steps

Examples

The dancer was a high stepper, her legs lifting dramatically with every beat of the music.
At the parade, several marching band members behaved like high steppers to impress the judges.
He walked into the room as a deliberate high stepper, drawing all eyes with exaggerated steps.
