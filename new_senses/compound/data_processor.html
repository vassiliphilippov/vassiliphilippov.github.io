data processor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.tə ˌproʊ.ses.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.tə ˌprəʊ.ses.ə / (BrE)
A machine or device that performs calculations or manipulates data automatically, often as part of a computer system.
machine for automatic data calculation

Examples

Early computers served primarily as massive data processors for government and scientific research.
Our company recently upgraded its main data processor to improve performance and reliability.
The data processor automatically handles millions of transactions every second.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer - an electronic device for storing and processing data

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8de\u026a.t\u0259 \u02ccpro\u028a.ses.\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8de\u026a.t\u0259 \u02ccpr\u0259\u028a.ses.\u0259 / (BrE)
A person, company, or service that processes personal data on behalf of a data controller, under data-protection law (for example, the GDPR).
legal role processing personal data
legal

Examples

Under the GDPR, a data processor must follow the controller's instructions and protect personal data.
The vendor acted as a data processor when it stored customer records on behalf of the company.
Contracts with a data processor should specify security measures, permitted uses, and retention periods.
