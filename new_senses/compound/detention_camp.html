detention camp

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛnʃən kæmp / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən kamp / (BrE)
an institution where juvenile offenders are held temporarily, usually under the supervision of a juvenile court
temporary holding facility for juvenile offenders

Examples

Conditions at the detention camp were monitored by the state authorities.
Many young offenders spend several weeks in a detention camp before their cases are resolved.
The judge ordered the teenager to be placed in a detention camp until his next hearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juvenile detention center - a facility for holding young offenders temporarily

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈtɛnʃən kæmp / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən kamp / (BrE)
a government or official facility where refugees, asylum seekers, or migrants are held temporarily while their immigration status or entry is decided.
facility for detaining migrants or asylum seekers
legal

Examples

The government expanded several detention camps along the border to hold asylum seekers.
Humanitarian organizations criticized conditions at the remote detention camp housing hundreds of migrants.
Opposition MPs demanded inspections of every border detention camp amid reports of abuse.
