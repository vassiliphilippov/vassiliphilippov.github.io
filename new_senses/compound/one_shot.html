one shot

1. NOUN

/ wʌn ʃɑt / (AmE), / wʌn ʃɒt / (BrE)
a single charge or round of ammunition intended for firing one bullet or projectile
a single round of ammunition

Examples

After using the last one shot, they had to retreat and resupply.
Make sure you have a spare one shot when you go to the firing range.
The hunter loaded a one shot into his rifle before heading into the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartridge - a complete unit of ammunition
  • Synonym: shell - ammunition used in firearms, especially shotguns

2. ADJ NEW

/ w\u028cn \u0283\u0251t / (AmE), / w\u028cn \u0283\u0252t / (BrE)
(in machine learning) requiring or able to learn to recognise a new class or concept from a single example.
learn from a single example (ML)

Examples

Researchers developed a one shot model that recognizes new faces from a single photograph.
In robotics, engineers prefer a one shot training method to reduce data-collection time.
Her thesis compared one shot learning with few-shot techniques on image-classification tasks.
