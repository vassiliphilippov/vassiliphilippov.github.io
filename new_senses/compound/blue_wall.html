blue wall

1. NOUN

/ blu wɑl / (AmE), / bluː wɔːl / (BrE)
an unofficial code among police officers not to report or testify against a colleague’s wrongdoing, maintaining secrecy and solidarity within the force
police code of silence

Examples

After the scandal, the chief promised to break down the blue wall protecting corrupt officers.
It took a whistleblower to penetrate the blue wall and expose the cover-up.
Many critics argue that the blue wall prevents accountability within police departments.

Synset Examples

the blue wall cracked when some officers refused to take part in the cover-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code of silence - an unspoken agreement to withhold information for group protection

2. NOUN NEW

/ blu wɑl / (AmE), / bluː wɔːl / (BrE)
A bloc of voters or geographic regions that consistently support the same political party, forming a reliable electoral stronghold for that party.
electoral bloc of reliably partisan regions

Examples

Analysts warned that the blue wall of states would shape the election outcome.
Campaign strategists concentrated resources in the blue wall to secure crucial electoral votes.
After demographic shifts, some commentators argued the blue wall might no longer be reliable.

Synset Examples

Analysts warned that the blue wall of states would shape the election outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electoral bloc - a group of voters or regions that act together to support a political party or candidate
  • Antonym: red wall - a bloc of regions that historically voted for the opposing party; often used as a contrasting political term
