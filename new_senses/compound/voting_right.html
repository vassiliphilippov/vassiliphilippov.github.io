voting right

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊtɪŋ raɪt / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtɪŋ raɪt / (BrE)
the legal right to participate in a vote, especially to cast a ballot in an election or to vote on matters in a corporate setting as a shareholder
the legal right to participate in voting

Examples

Every citizen over the age of eighteen has the voting right in national elections.
Protests were held demanding equal voting rights for all members of the community.
The company offers each shareholder a voting right based on the number of shares owned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections

(in blockchain and decentralized organizations) the right to take part in governance decisions, typically granted to holders of a particular token or to those with a stake, allowing them to vote on proposals or protocol changes.
token- or stake-based governance vote

Examples

In many DAOs, token holders earn a voting right that lets them approve protocol changes.
After buying governance tokens, she received voting rights on major treasury decisions.
Debates continue about whether a voting right based on stake unfairly favors large holders.
