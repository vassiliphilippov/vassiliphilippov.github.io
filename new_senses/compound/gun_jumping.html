gun jumping

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡʌn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of starting something too soon, especially without proper authorization or before the appropriate time
acting prematurely, often without authorization

Examples

His tendency for gun jumping often led to mistakes in team projects.
The company was fined for gun jumping by releasing information before approval.
To avoid accusations of gun jumping, all procedures must be followed.

Synset Examples

His gun jumping led to several mistakes in the report.
Avoid gun jumping to ensure project success.
She was penalized for gun jumping during the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump the gun - to begin something before the proper time

Etymology

From the phrase 'jump the gun,' originally from athletics, meaning to start a race before the starting gun is fired.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɡʊn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡʊn ˈdʒʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
(in competition/antitrust law) illegal conduct by parties to a merger or acquisition that involves prohibited coordination or implementation of the transaction before receiving required regulatory clearance, for example sharing competitively sensitive information, integrating operations, or transferring control.
illegal pre‑clearance conduct in mergers
legal

Examples

Regulators fined the company for gun jumping after finding they shared strategic pricing data before clearance.
The merger stalled when officials investigated alleged gun jumping between the buyer and the target's managers.
Their legal team advised strict controls to prevent gun jumping during sensitive pre-approval discussions.

Synset Examples

Regulators fined the company for gun jumping after finding they shared strategic pricing data before clearance.
The merger stalled when officials investigated alleged gun jumping between the buyer and the target's managers.
Their legal team advised strict controls to prevent gun jumping during sensitive pre-approval discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump the gun - to begin something before the proper or agreed time
  • Synonym: antitrust violation - an act that breaches competition law, such as unlawful coordination or market‑sharing

Etymology

From the phrase 'jump the gun' (to start too soon); adopted in competition law to name premature, pre-clearance actions.
