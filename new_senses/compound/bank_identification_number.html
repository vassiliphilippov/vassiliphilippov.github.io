bank identification number

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk ˌaɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / bæŋk ˌaɪˌdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number consisting of a two-part code assigned to banks and savings associations, where the first part indicates location and the second part identifies the specific bank
two-part code for identifying banks

Examples

The bank identification number helps differentiate financial institutions during electronic funds transfers.
When opening a new account, the banker asked for the bank identification number of the branch.
You can find the bank identification number printed at the bottom of your checks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: routing number - a number used to identify a financial institution in banking transactions

/ b\u00e6\u014bk \u02cca\u026a\u02ccd\u025bnt\u026af\u026a\u02c8ke\u026a\u0283\u0259n \u02c8n\u028cmb\u0259r / (AmE), / b\u00e6\u014bk \u02cca\u026a\u02ccd\u025bnt\u026af\u026a\u02c8ke\u026a\u0283\u0259n \u02c8n\u028cmb\u0259 / (BrE)
(in payments and card processing) the initial digits of a payment card number (often six digits) that identify the card issuer and card network.
initial card digits identifying issuer (BIN/IIN)

Examples

The first six digits of her credit card showed the bank identification number identifying the issuer.
Payment gateways use the bank identification number to route transactions to the correct card network.
Fraud teams flagged the purchase because the bank identification number did not match the card's country.
