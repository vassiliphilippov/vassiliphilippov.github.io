traffic delay

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræfɪk dɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk dɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
a period during which the movement of vehicles is slowed or halted due to congestion, accidents, or other obstructions on the road
slowed or halted movement of vehicles

Examples

Morning commuters experienced a major traffic delay after an accident blocked two lanes.
There was a traffic delay on the highway due to road construction.
Unexpected traffic delays forced us to reschedule the meeting.

Synset Examples

The accident caused significant traffic delays this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congestion - the state of being overcrowded, especially with traffic
  • Synonym: traffic jam - a long line of vehicles unable to move or moving very slowly

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8tr\u00e6f\u026ak d\u026a\u02c8le\u026a / (AmE), / \u02c8tr\u00e6f\u026ak d\u026a\u02c8le\u026a / (BrE)
a delay in the transmission of data across a computer or telecommunications network, typically caused by congestion, routing, or queuing and often measured as increased latency.
delay in data transmission (network congestion)

Examples

During peak hours, traffic delay caused noticeable latency in streaming services.
Network engineers measured a ten-millisecond traffic delay between the two data centers yesterday.
To reduce traffic delays, the ISP upgraded its backbone to increase throughput and lower packet queuing.

Synset Examples

During peak hours, traffic delay caused noticeable latency in streaming services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network latency - the time it takes for data to travel from source to destination across a network
  • Synonym: congestion - a condition in a network where demand exceeds capacity, causing packet queuing and delays
  • Related: packet loss - the failure of one or more transmitted packets to arrive at their destination, often increasing effective delay
