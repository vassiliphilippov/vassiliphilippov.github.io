bricks and mortar

1. NOUN

/ brɪks ənd ˈmɔrtɚ / (AmE), / brɪks ənd ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
building material consisting of bricks held together with mortar, used in construction
building material of bricks with mortar

Examples

Modern architects sometimes prefer steel and glass to bricks and mortar when designing new buildings.
Repairing the damage required replacing sections of the original bricks and mortar.
The old church was constructed entirely of bricks and mortar.
