service call

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs kɔl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs kɔːl / (BrE)
a visit by a repairperson to a customer's location to provide maintenance or repair services
visit by repairperson for service or repairs

Examples

After my internet stopped working, I arranged a service call for the next morning.
The technician scheduled a service call to fix our broken dishwasher.
There will be a service call fee added to your bill, even if no repairs are made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house call - a visit to someone's home by a doctor or other professional

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsɜːvɪs kɔːl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs kɔːl / (BrE)
(in computing) a request sent by a client or application to a software service asking it to perform a specific operation, typically made over a network (for example, an API call or RPC).
computing request to a service

Examples

The mobile app sends a service call to the server to fetch the user's profile data.
During testing, several service calls failed because the endpoint was misconfigured and returned errors.
To speed startup, the system batches multiple service calls into a single network request.
