free energy

1. NOUN

/ fri ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / fri ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
A thermodynamic quantity representing the capacity of a system to do work, usually measured in joules or ergs.
thermodynamic capacity to do work

Examples

Gibbs free energy is an important concept in chemical thermodynamics.
Physicists calculate free energy to determine whether a reaction will occur spontaneously.
The decrease in free energy indicates that the system has become more stable.

Synset Examples

energy can take a wide variety of forms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - capacity for doing work or producing change

2. NOUN NEW

/ fri \u02c8\u025bn\u025ad\u0292i / (AmE), / fri \u02c8\u025bn\u0259d\u0292i / (BrE)
(in neuroscience and machine learning) a mathematical quantity (a variational functional) used to bound unexpected observations (surprise) and drive approximate Bayesian inference and learning.
variational bound used in Bayesian inference

Examples

Researchers use free energy minimization to train predictive coding models of perception in neuroscience.
In machine learning, minimizing free energy is equivalent to maximizing a model's evidence under observed data.
The paper showed how agents can act to reduce long-term free energy and avoid surprising sensory states.

  • Synonym: evidence lower bound (ELBO) - a quantity in probabilistic modeling equivalent to (negative) variational free energy used for model fitting
