chief of staff

1. NOUN

/ tʃiːf əv stæf / (AmE), / tʃiːf əv stɑːf / (BrE)
the senior officer in charge of a branch of the armed forces, responsible for overseeing operations and administration
senior military officer in charge of a service branch

Examples

General Martin was appointed as the new chief of staff of the army last year.
It is the responsibility of the chief of staff to coordinate all branches of the service.
The president met with the chief of staff to discuss military strategy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: commander in chief - the supreme commander of a nation's armed forces

Etymology

From 'chief' (leader) and 'staff' (group of officers assisting a commander).

2. NOUN NEW

/ tʃiːf əv stæf / (AmE), / tʃiːf əv stɑːf / (BrE)
the senior physician and administrative head of the medical staff in a hospital, responsible for overseeing clinical standards, credentialing, and medical governance.
senior medical staff leader in a hospital
medical

Examples

The hospital's chief of staff approved the new patient safety protocols.
After the review, the chief of staff recommended additional training for junior doctors.
Several chiefs of staff met to coordinate a regional response to the outbreak.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical director - a senior physician responsible for clinical leadership and oversight in a health organisation; often synonymous with hospital chief of staff.
  • Synonym: chief medical officer - a senior doctor responsible for medical policy, clinical governance, and professional standards; often closely related to the hospital chief of staff role.

Etymology

From 'chief' (leader) + 'staff' (group of assistants or employees).
