live wire

1. NOUN

/ laɪv waɪɚ / (AmE), / laɪv waɪə / (BrE)
A person who is very lively, energetic, and alert.
energetic or lively person

Examples

Everyone loves working with Jenna because she's such a live wire in the office.
He might be small, but on the field he's a real live wire.
The party really started when that live wire from marketing showed up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamo - someone who is very energetic and dynamic
  • Synonym: go-getter - an ambitious, energetic person who is determined to succeed
  • Antonym: sluggard - a lazy, slow-moving person

Etymology

From the image of an electric wire carrying a live current, figuratively applied to a person full of energy.

2. NOUN NEW

/ laɪv waɪə / (AmE), / laɪv waɪə / (BrE)
An electrical conductor or part of an electrical circuit that is carrying electric current and is therefore energized and potentially dangerous to touch.
electrically energized conductor

Examples

The technician warned to avoid touching the live wire while testing the outlet.
During the storm, a fallen live wire sparked and blocked traffic until the crew arrived.
Always switch off power before repairing a lamp to ensure you do not touch a live wire.

Synset Examples

The technician warned to avoid touching the live wire while testing the outlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot wire - a wire in an electrical circuit that is carrying current (often used interchangeably with live wire)
  • Antonym: dead wire - a wire that is not carrying electrical current; opposite of a live wire

Etymology

From the adjective 'live' (carrying electric current) + 'wire'.
