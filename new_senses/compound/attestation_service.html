attestation service

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɛsˈteɪʃən ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / əˌtɛsˈteɪʃən ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
a professional service provided by a certified public accountant (CPA) in which an independent opinion is given about the reliability of another party's written statement
CPA's professional opinion on reliability of statements

Examples

A CPA may offer an attestation service to verify compliance with regulatory requirements.
Regulators often require businesses to obtain an attestation service for certain disclosures.
The company hired a third party to perform an attestation service on its financial report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audit - an official inspection of an organization's accounts
  • Degree Lower: review - an evaluation, especially a less extensive examination than an audit

Etymology

From attest (to bear witness, from Latin attestari) + service.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u0259\u02cct\u025bs\u02c8te\u026a\u0283\u0259n \u02c8s\u025d\u02d0v\u026as / (AmE), / \u0259\u02cct\u025bs\u02c8te\u026a\u0283\u0259n \u02c8s\u025c\u02d0v\u026as / (BrE)
(in computing and cloud security) a digital service that issues cryptographic attestations proving the state, configuration, identity, or compliance of a device, virtual machine, or data.
cryptographic proof service

Examples

The cloud platform provides an attestation service to prove a VM's security posture to auditors.
Before onboarding devices, the company required each IoT device to use an attestation service.
Incident responders used the vendor's attestation service to verify configuration and cryptographic integrity of the disk.
