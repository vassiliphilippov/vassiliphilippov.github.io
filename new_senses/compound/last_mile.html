last mile

1. NOUN

/ læst maɪl / (AmE), / lɑːst maɪl / (BrE)
the final walk taken by a condemned prisoner from their cell to the place of execution.
final walk to execution (prisoner)

Examples

He dreaded the moment he would walk his own last mile.
Reading about the last mile always gives me chills; it symbolizes finality.
The prisoner took the last mile in silence, surrounded by guards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green mile - the walk to the execution chamber for condemned prisoners (used especially in U.S. prisons)

2. NOUN NEW

/ læst maɪl / (AmE), / lɑːst maɪl / (BrE)
the final stage of delivering goods, services, or data from a distribution point to the end user or customer.
final stage of delivery to customer

Examples

Logistics firms are investing heavily in the last mile to meet customer expectations for fast delivery.
Urban planners examine the last mile when deciding where to place parcel lockers and bike lanes.
Retailers open micro-fulfillment centers to shorten the last mile and lower their shipping costs.

Synset Examples

Logistics firms are investing heavily in the last mile to meet customer expectations for fast delivery.
Urban planners examine the last mile when deciding where to place parcel lockers and bike lanes.
Retailers open micro-fulfillment centers to shorten the last mile and lower their shipping costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last-mile delivery - the process of transporting goods from a local distribution center to the final consumer's address.
  • Synonym: final mile - the concluding segment of a delivery route or service chain to the customer.

Etymology

From the literal words 'last' + 'mile'; extended in logistics and telecommunications in the late 20th/early 21st century to mean the final stage of delivery or service.
