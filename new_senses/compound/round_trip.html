round trip

1. NOUN

/ raʊnd trɪp / (AmE), / raʊnd trɪp / (BrE)
a journey to a place and back again to the starting point
a journey to a place and back

Examples

A round trip from New York to Boston takes about eight hours by car.
She booked a round trip ticket to Paris for her vacation.
The special offer includes meals and a round trip airfare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-way - a journey to a place without returning

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd ˌtrɪp / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd ˌtrɪp / (BrE)
a journey from one place to a destination and back again, usually returning to the starting point
a journey to a destination and back

Examples

A round trip to Madrid costs less if you book in advance.
He bought a round trip ticket to San Francisco for the conference.
The bus schedule shows the times for the round trip to the lake and back.

3. NOUN NEW

/ raʊnd trɪp / (AmE), / raʊnd trɪp / (BrE)
(computing, networking) the complete journey of a network request from a host to a remote system and back, often measured as the time taken (also called round-trip time or RTT).
network request–response time

Examples

During the load test, the average round trip time to the database was under fifty milliseconds.
Network engineers measured the round trips for each packet to diagnose intermittent connectivity problems.
When she pinged the remote host, the round trip varied between twenty and eighty milliseconds.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round-trip time (RTT) - the time it takes for a signal or packet to travel to a destination and return to the origin
  • Synonym: latency - delay before data begins to transfer, often discussed alongside round-trip measurements
