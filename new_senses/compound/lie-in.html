lie-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ ɪn / (BrE)
an act of staying in bed later than usual in the morning, especially for rest or relaxation
staying in bed later in the morning
british

Examples

I'm looking forward to a lie-in this weekend after a busy week at work.
On Sundays, he usually has a lie-in and reads the newspaper in bed.
She treated herself to a long lie-in on her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep in - to remain asleep or in bed later than usual

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈlaɪ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ ɪn / (BrE)
a public demonstration in which people lie down, often in a public place, to draw attention to a cause or to block access.
protest where people lie down

Examples

Activists staged a lie-in in the city square to protest the council's development plan.
Dozens of students organised a lie-in outside the administration building during the strike.
Police asked the crowd to end the lie-in after it blocked the main pedestrian entrance.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sit-in - a form of protest in which people sit and refuse to move, often to occupy a place and draw attention to a cause.
  • Synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing objection to policies or actions.

Etymology

Modeled on 'sit-in' (a form of protest popularized in the mid-20th century).
