internal representation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a mental construction or image that represents external reality within the mind, often as an idea, image, or concept.
a mental idea or image of something

Examples

Children often use an internal representation to imagine the layout of a place they've never visited.
Scientists study how the brain creates an internal representation of the outside world.
The concept relies on the idea that every perception produces an internal representation in the mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental image - a picture in the mind's eye of something not actually present
  • Degree Lower: perception - the process of becoming aware through the senses
