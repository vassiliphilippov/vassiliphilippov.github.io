Indian red

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən rɛd / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən rɛd / (BrE)
a dark red pigment containing ferric oxide, commonly used in paints and cosmetics
a dark red pigment used in paints

Examples

The artist chose Indian red for the underpainting to create warm undertones.
The historic building was restored with walls painted in Indian red hues.
You can mix a shade close to Indian red by blending red and brown pigments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red ochre - a natural earth pigment containing iron oxide, similar in color to Indian red

Etymology

Named for its historical source from natural earth pigments found in India.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən rɛd / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən rɛd / (BrE)
a natural reddish pigment made from iron oxide, often used in paints and dyes
a red pigment made from iron oxide

Examples

Some older buildings are painted with a deep Indian red to mimic historical colors.
The artist mixed Indian red with other earth tones to achieve a warm background.
You can recognize Indian red by its distinctive earthy hue in traditional paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red ochre - a red pigment made from clay colored by iron oxide

Etymology

From the use of iron oxide-rich soil originally sourced from India.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən rɛd / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən rɛd / (BrE)
a reddish-brown color resembling the red soil used as body paint by some Native American peoples
a reddish-brown color

Examples

Artists have long used Indian red pigment to create deep, natural hues in their paintings.
She chose Indian red yarn for the scarf because of its rich, earthy tone.
The walls were painted a warm shade of Indian red to give the room a cozy atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red - the color at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange
  • Degree Higher: maroon - a dark brownish-red color

Etymology

Referring to the red earth pigments traditionally used in parts of India and by Native American peoples.
