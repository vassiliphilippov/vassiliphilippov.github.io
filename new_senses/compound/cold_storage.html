cold storage

1. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈstɔɹ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a place or system for keeping goods at low temperatures to preserve them, especially food or chemicals
storage at low temperatures for preservation

Examples

Due to a malfunction, the cold storage unit at the warehouse needed to be repaired.
Meat is shipped in cold storage to prevent it from spoiling during transport.
The vaccine must be kept in cold storage until it is ready to be distributed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrigeration - the process of cooling something to preserve it

2. NOUN

/ koʊld ˈstɔɹ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ / (BrE)
A state in which something is kept unused, inactive, or postponed, often temporarily.
state of being unused or postponed

Examples

After the merger talks failed, the project was put into cold storage indefinitely.
His proposal remained in cold storage until the company's priorities changed.
Many creative ideas end up in cold storage when funding runs out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abeyance - temporary inactivity or suspension
  • Synonym: on hold - postponed or suspended temporarily

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'cold storage,' extended figuratively to mean postponement or inactivity.

3. NOUN NEW

/ koʊld ˈstɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in computing) archival storage for digital data that is rarely accessed, typically kept offline or in low-cost storage tiers to reduce costs.
archival digital storage

Examples

The company moved legacy backups to cold storage to cut costs.
We transferred months of raw footage into cold storage since it was rarely accessed.
Automatic tiering sends older database snapshots to cold storage for long-term retention.

Synset Examples

The company moved legacy backups to cold storage to cut costs.

Automatic tiering sends older database snapshots to cold storage for long-term retention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archive - a collection or place for long-term storage of data; also the action of storing data for preservation
  • Antonym: hot storage - storage designed for frequently accessed data, typically higher-cost and faster than cold storage
  • Synonym: offline storage - storage kept disconnected from a network or easily accessible systems, often used for long-term retention

Etymology

From the literal phrase "cold storage" (keeping items unused or preserved); extended in computing to refer to long-term, infrequently accessed digital storage.
