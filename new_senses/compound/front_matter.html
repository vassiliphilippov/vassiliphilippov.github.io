front matter

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt ˈmætər / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈmætə / (BrE)
the written material that appears before the main text of a book, such as the title page, preface, and table of contents
preliminary pages of a book

Examples

Always check the front matter for the copyright date and publisher information.
The front matter of the book includes the dedication and the foreword.
The author's acknowledgments appeared in the front matter rather than in the main chapters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back matter - material at the end of a book, such as appendices or bibliography

2. NOUN NEW

/ frʌnt ˈmætər / (AmE), / frʌnt ˈmætə / (BrE)
a block of metadata placed at the start of a digital document or file (often in YAML or TOML) that defines properties such as title, date, layout, or tags for processing by static-site generators and publishing tools.
metadata block at top of a file

Examples

In Jekyll, the front matter at the top of a post sets layout, title, and date.
Before converting, check that the document's front matter uses valid YAML keys for metadata extraction.
The static site generator failed because the page's front matter was missing the required permalink field.
