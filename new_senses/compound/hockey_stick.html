hockey stick

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑki stɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɒki stɪk / (BrE)
A long stick with a curved or angled blade at one end, used by players to hit and control the puck or ball in hockey.
curved stick for playing hockey

Examples

Broken hockey sticks lay scattered beside the rink after the match.
She bought a new hockey stick before the start of the season.
The player gripped his hockey stick tightly as he prepared to take the shot.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8h\u0251ki st\u026ak / (AmE), / \u02c8h\u0252ki st\u026ak / (BrE)
A shape of a graph or chart that is flat or slowly rising for a long period and then turns into a rapid, sharp upward rise, resembling a hockey stick.
graph shape with sharp rise

Examples

Recent climate graphs show a hockey stick pattern, highlighting rapid warming in recent decades.
Startups often hope for a hockey stick in revenue after their product gains market traction.
Economists warned the report displayed a clear hockey stick, with growth spiking after policy changes.

Etymology
