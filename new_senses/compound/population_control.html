population control

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the practice of managing or limiting the growth of a population, often through government policies or programs
limitation of population growth

Examples

Debates about population control often involve ethical, social, and economic issues.
Some countries have adopted strict population control policies in response to resource shortages.
The government implemented population control measures to address rapid urban growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family planning - the practice of controlling the number and spacing of one's children, typically through the use of contraception

(in evolutionary computation and other population-based algorithms) a method or mechanism for limiting or managing the number or composition of candidate solutions to balance computational resources and promote effective search or convergence.
algorithmic management of candidate populations

Examples

The evolutionary algorithm implemented a new population control method to limit candidate solutions each generation.
Researchers compared several population control strategies to balance computational cost and solution quality on benchmarks.
To prevent bloating, the team applied aggressive population control after crossover and mutation steps.
