fixed-point part

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkst pɔɪnt pɑrt / (AmE), / ˈfɪkst pɔɪnt pɑːt / (BrE)
the positive fractional part of the representation of a logarithm; also called the mantissa. For example, in the expression 'log 643 = 2.808', the fixed-point part is .808.
fractional part of a logarithm (mantissa)

Examples

In logarithmic tables, only the fixed-point part is usually listed.
Scientists use the fixed-point part to simplify calculations with logarithms.
The fixed-point part of log 1000 is exactly zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantissa - the fractional part of a common logarithm

In computing, the fractional component of a fixed-point number — the digits or bits that appear after the radix (decimal) point and determine fractional precision.
fractional component of fixed-point number

Examples

When using Q15.16 format, the fixed-point part determines fractional precision for arithmetic operations.
To avoid overflow, adjust the scale so the fixed-point part fits within the available fractional bits.
During testing they compared results against floating-point and found the fixed-point part rounded differently.
