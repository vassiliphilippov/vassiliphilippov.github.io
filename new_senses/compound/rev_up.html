rev up

1. NOUN NEW

/ rɛv ʌp / (AmE), / rɛv ʌp / (BrE)
an organised or deliberate increase in activity, effort, or intensity (often to build momentum before an important event).
a surge or organised increase in activity

Examples

The company organized a major rev up to boost sales before the product launch.
Local volunteers held a weekend rev up to increase voter turnout ahead of the election.
After months of quiet work, the team launched several small rev-ups to accelerate momentum.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramp-up - a gradual or planned increase in activity or production
  • Synonym: surge - a sudden large increase in something, such as activity or demand

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'rev up' (originally from 'rev', short for 'revolution' of an engine, plus the particle 'up'); the noun is a 20th-century nominalization.
