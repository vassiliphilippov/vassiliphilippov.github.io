compound eye

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd aɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpaʊnd aɪ / (BrE)
an eye, found in insects and some crustaceans, made up of many small visual units, each contributing to the total image
eye with many visual units in insects/crustaceans

Examples

Dragonflies rely on their compound eyes to spot prey moving in different directions.
The design of the robot’s camera was inspired by the structure of a bee’s compound eye.
Under the microscope, the fly’s compound eye revealed thousands of tiny lenses packed closely together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple eye - an eye with a single lens, as in humans or spiders

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd aɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpaʊnd aɪ / (BrE)
(in engineering and imaging) a camera or sensor system that imitates an insect compound eye, consisting of an array of microlenses or photoreceptors to provide a wide field of view, improved motion detection, or redundancy.
biomimetic multi-lens camera

Examples

Engineers fitted the surveillance drone with a compound eye sensor to widen its field of view.
Researchers built a miniature compound eye camera using an array of microlenses and tiny photodiodes.
The conference featured several talks on how compound eyes improve motion detection in robotic vision systems.
