western yellow pine

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn ˈjɛloʊ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈjɛləʊ paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to western North America, known for its dark green needles in bunches of 2 to 5 and thick, dark brown bark plates when mature
a tall pine tree of western North America

Examples

In his research, Ben studied how western yellow pine adapts to changes in climate.
Many log cabins in the region are built from western yellow pine due to its sturdy wood.
The western yellow pine grows in abundance across the forests of Oregon and Washington.
