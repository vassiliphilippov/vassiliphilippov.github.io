work unit

1. NOUN

/ wɝːk ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / wɜːk ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a standard unit used for measuring work or energy, such as the joule in physics.
unit for measuring work (physics)

Examples

The scientist calculated the experiment's output in work units to ensure accurate results.
The textbook explains that a work unit defines the amount of energy transferred when a force acts through a distance.
When measuring energy consumption, engineers often refer to standard work units like joules or ergs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joule - SI unit of work or energy in physics
  • Synonym: erg - CGS unit of work or energy
