tilt angle

1. NOUN

/ tɪlt ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / tɪlt ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the angle between a rocket's direction and the vertical, especially as it follows its trajectory
angle between rocket and vertical

Examples

An incorrect tilt angle can cause a rocket to veer off course during launch.
Engineers closely monitored the tilt angle to ensure the rocket followed its planned trajectory.
The tilt angle of the rocket increased steadily as it ascended through the atmosphere.

2. NOUN NEW

/ tɪlt ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / tɪlt ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the angle at which a surface or device is tilted relative to the horizontal plane, chosen to optimise performance (for example, to maximise solar energy capture or improve antenna reception).
orientation angle of a surface

Examples

The installer adjusted the tilt angle of the solar panel to maximize energy capture.
For winter months, you should increase the tilt angle of the panels to catch lower sun rays.
Engineers set the antenna's tilt angle precisely to improve reception in urban areas.
