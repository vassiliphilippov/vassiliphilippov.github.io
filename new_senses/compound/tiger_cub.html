tiger cub

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡɚ kʌb / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə kʌb / (BrE)
a young tiger, especially one that has not yet reached adulthood
young tiger

Examples

A newborn tiger cub is completely dependent on its mother for survival.
The tiger cub playfully chased its sibling through the grass.
Visitors at the zoo watched the tiger cub take its first cautious steps outside the den.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger - a large carnivorous feline native to Asia
  • Synonym: cub - a young animal of certain species, such as a lion or bear

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ta\u026a\u0261\u025a k\u028cb / (AmE), / \u02c8ta\u026a\u0261\u0259 k\u028cb / (BrE)
(finance) a hedge fund founded by a former employee of the hedge fund Tiger Management; often one of the funds collectively known as the 'Tiger Cubs'.
hedge fund started by ex-Tiger Management staff

Examples

The newly launched tiger cub quickly attracted seed capital after a former Tiger Management analyst joined the team.
Several tiger cubs established by alumni of the fund now manage billions in assets worldwide.
When negotiating with investors, the tiger cub emphasized its experienced research team and risk controls.

Etymology

Named after the hedge fund Tiger Management (founded by Julian Robertson); 'tiger cub' refers to smaller funds established by its former employees, collectively called the Tiger Cubs.
