Sleeping Beauty

1. PROPN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
a princess in a fairy tale who falls into a deep sleep under an evil spell and can only be awakened by a prince's kiss; the story of this princess.
fairy tale of a sleeping princess

Examples

My favorite fairy tale as a child was Sleeping Beauty because of its magical storyline.
She dressed as Sleeping Beauty for the costume party, complete with a pink gown and crown.
The ballet version of Sleeping Beauty is considered one of Tchaikovsky's greatest works.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslipɪŋ ˈbyuti / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ ˈbjuːti / (BrE)
A princess who falls into a deep sleep and is awakened by a prince, from a famous fairy tale; also used figuratively to describe someone strikingly beautiful and passive or someone sleeping soundly.
princess in a fairy tale; passive or beautiful sleeper

Examples

He nicknamed his sister Sleeping Beauty because she could sleep through anything.
Many consider the old mansion a kind of Sleeping Beauty, waiting to be restored to its former glory.
The young actress played Sleeping Beauty in the school's annual fairy tale production.

Synset Examples

The play featured a modern twist on Sleeping Beauty.

He called her Sleeping Beauty because she loved to nap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a quality of being pleasing to the senses; synonym for 'Sleeping Beauty' in the figurative sense
