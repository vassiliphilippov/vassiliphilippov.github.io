tone deafness

1. NOUN

/ toʊn ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / təʊn ˈdɛfnəs / (BrE)
An inability to distinguish differences in musical pitch, making it difficult to recognize or reproduce musical notes accurately.
inability to distinguish musical pitch

Examples

Despite his tone deafness, he enjoyed listening to music and attending concerts.
His tone deafness made it impossible for him to sing in tune with the choir.
She discovered her tone deafness when she couldn't tell one note from another during music class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amusia - a musical disorder marked by the inability to recognize musical tones or reproduce them

/ to\u028an \u02c8d\u025bfn\u0259s / (AmE), / t\u0259\u028an \u02c8d\u025bfn\u0259s / (BrE)
Failure or inability to perceive, understand, or respond appropriately to the emotional tone, feelings, or social nuances in speech or situations, often causing insensitive or misaligned reactions.
insensitivity to social or emotional tone

Examples

Her tone deafness to colleagues' concerns damaged team morale during the crisis.
The politician's tone deafness about rising costs cost him voter support in the debate.
After the meeting she was criticized for tone deafness when praising budget cuts without empathy.
