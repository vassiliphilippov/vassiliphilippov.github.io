gas plant

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs plænt / (AmE), / ɡæs plɑːnt / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial herb (Dictamnus albus) with white flowers that emit flammable vapor in hot weather
herb with flammable vapor-emitting flowers

Examples

Gardeners admire the gas plant not only for its flowers but also for its aromatic foliage.
On warm evenings, the gas plant can sometimes ignite its own vapors if exposed to a flame.
The unique characteristic of the gas plant has fascinated botanists for centuries.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɡæs plænt / (AmE), / ɡæs plɑːnt / (BrE)
an industrial facility where natural gas is processed, conditioned, stored, or prepared for distribution or export.
industrial natural-gas facility

Examples

The country invested in a new gas plant to meet rising natural gas demand.
Engineers inspected the aging gas plant after a minor leak was reported near the pipelines.
Several coastal gas plants export processed LNG through specialized terminals to international customers.
