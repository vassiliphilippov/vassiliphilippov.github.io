mic drop

1. NOUN

/ maɪk drɑp / (AmE), / maɪk drɒp / (BrE)
an impressive or dramatic conclusion to a speech, performance, or statement, meant to signal that nothing more needs to be said or added
dramatic end to a statement or performance
informal

Examples

Her brilliant retort at the end of the debate was a real mic drop.
The comedian finished his set with a perfect mic drop moment that left the audience cheering.
When the CEO announced the company's record profits, it was a clear mic drop for the competition.

Synset Examples

His final joke was a true mic drop.
The performance ended with a perfect mic drop.
After her speech, she made a mic drop moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand finale - an impressive final act or event
  • Antonym: anticlimax - an unsatisfying or disappointing end that contrasts with expectation

Etymology

From 'mic', informal for 'microphone', and 'drop', referring to the gesture of dropping a microphone to mark a powerful finish.

2. VERB NEW

/ maɪk drɑp / (AmE), / maɪk drɒp / (BrE)
(informal) to end a speech, performance, remark, or online exchange with a decisive, impressive closing or gesture, implying that nothing more needs to be said.
end with an impressive closing
informal

Examples

She decided to mic drop after her last line to show she had no more arguments.
After his brilliant reply, he mic-dropped and left the stage to thunderous applause.
In the thread, users often mic drop with one-line comebacks to end the discussion.

Synset Examples

In the thread, users often mic drop with one-line comebacks to end the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclude - to bring something (a speech, event, or argument) to an end

Etymology

From 'mic' (informal for 'microphone') + 'drop', from the gesture of dropping a microphone to mark a powerful finish.
