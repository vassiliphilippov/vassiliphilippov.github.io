machine politician

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃin pəˌlɪtɪʃən / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn pəˌlɪtɪʃən / (BrE)
A politician who is part of a powerful group that controls a political party for personal or private advantage rather than public interest.
politician controlling party for private gain

Examples

As a seasoned machine politician, he controlled votes through behind-the-scenes deals.
Many reformers campaigned against the influence of machine politicians in city government.
The mayor was widely regarded as a machine politician who put party loyalty above public good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party boss - a leader in a political party organization
