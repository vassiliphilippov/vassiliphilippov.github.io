rum cherry

1. NOUN

/ rʌm ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / rʌm ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A large North American wild cherry tree (Prunus serotina) with round, black, sour edible fruit.
species of North American wild cherry

Examples

Birds are especially fond of the tart fruit produced by the rum cherry.
He used rum cherry wood to craft a durable piece of furniture.
The rum cherry thrives in forests throughout the eastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black cherry - another common name for Prunus serotina

2. NOUN NEW

/ rʌm ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / rʌm ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A cherry that has been preserved, soaked, or macerated in rum and sugar, used as a cocktail garnish or flavoring ingredient.
cherry soaked or preserved in rum

Examples

The bartender placed a bright rum cherry on the glass for a sweet finish.
She packed a jar of homemade rum cherry to give to friends at Christmas.
The recipe called for a diced rum cherry stirred into the cake batter for extra flavor.
