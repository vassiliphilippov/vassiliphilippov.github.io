opening line

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
the first line of a piece of writing, such as a book, article, or story, often designed to capture the reader's attention
the first line of a written work

Examples

Journalists spend a lot of time crafting the perfect opening line for an article.
She memorized the opening line of her speech to make a strong first impression.
The opening line of the novel instantly drew me into the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first sentence - the initial sentence of a text
