flesh-eating

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɛʃ ˌitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛʃ ˌiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of animals) feeding on the flesh of other animals; carnivorous.
carnivorous; eating flesh

Examples

Many large predators, such as lions and tigers, are flesh-eating animals.
Scientists discovered a new flesh-eating species in the rainforests of South America.
The documentary focused on the hunting strategies of flesh-eating birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnivorous - feeding on other animals; meat-eating
  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈflɛʃ ˌɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛʃ ˌiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(in medicine) (of an infection, bacterium, or other pathogen) causing rapid destruction or death of body tissue (necrosis).
causing tissue destruction (necrotizing)
medical

Examples

Doctors identified a flesh-eating infection that required immediate surgical removal of damaged tissue.
The emergency team raced to treat a patient with a rapidly progressing flesh-eating bacterial infection.
Public health warnings described several cases of flesh-eating bacteria linked to contaminated seawater.
