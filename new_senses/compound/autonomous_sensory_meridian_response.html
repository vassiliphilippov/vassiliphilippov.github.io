Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɑːnəməs ˈsɛnsəri mərˈɪdiən rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ɔːˌtɒnəməs ˈsɛnsəri mɛˈrɪdiən rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
A pleasant tingling sensation that typically begins on the scalp and moves down the neck and spine, often triggered by specific auditory or visual stimuli, resulting in deep relaxation.
pleasant tingling sensation with relaxation

Examples

Scientists are starting to study how Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response might help reduce stress or anxiety.
Some people experience Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response when they listen to soft whispering or tapping sounds.
Videos designed to trigger Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response have become very popular on the internet.

Etymology

Coined in 2010 from scientific and medical terminology: 'autonomous' (self-directed), 'sensory' (relating to the senses), 'meridian' (peak or high point), and 'response'.
