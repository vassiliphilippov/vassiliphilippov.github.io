silver standard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər ˌstændərd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌstændəd / (BrE)
A monetary system in which the standard unit of currency is defined by a specified amount of silver.
monetary system based on silver

Examples

After abandoning the silver standard, the nation experienced significant currency fluctuations.
Debate over the silver standard versus the gold standard played a major role in economic policy.
The country adopted the silver standard in the late nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gold standard - monetary system based on gold

(in machine learning and data annotation) a dataset or benchmark of lower or imperfect quality, typically produced automatically or with noisy labels, used as a practical substitute for a gold-standard annotated dataset.
imperfect ML benchmark dataset

Examples

For initial training they used a silver standard corpus created with automatic labels.
Researchers often rely on silver standards when gold annotations are too expensive or slow.
The competition provided a silver standard test set for baseline evaluation before final judging.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the phrases 'gold standard' and 'silver standard' in other domains; applied in computing to contrast a lower-quality but practical dataset with a gold-standard dataset.
