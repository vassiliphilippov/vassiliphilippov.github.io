booking agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person or company responsible for arranging and securing engagements for performers, such as musicians, actors, or speakers
person who arranges engagements for performers

Examples

Our band hired a booking agent to organize our upcoming tour dates.
She worked as a booking agent for several well-known theater companies.
The booking agent was able to secure a spot for the comedian at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent agent - a person who finds jobs for actors, musicians, or other performers
  • Degree Higher: performer - a person who entertains an audience

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbʊkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a software program or online service that automatically arranges, manages, or secures reservations or engagements on behalf of users.
automated booking service (software)

Examples

An online booking agent confirmed our restaurant reservation and sent the QR code instantly.
The festival used a simple booking agent app to schedule performers and sell tickets automatically.
Before the trip, she used a mobile booking agent to compare prices and reserve flights quickly.
