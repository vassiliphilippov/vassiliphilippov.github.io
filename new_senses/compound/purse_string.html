purse string

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝs ˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːs ˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
a string or cord used to draw together the opening of a purse or bag
string for closing a purse

Examples

He quickly loosened the purse string to retrieve a coin from his pouch.
She pulled the purse string tight to ensure nothing would fall out.
The old velvet bag still had its original purse string attached.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːs ˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːs ˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
the control over a supply of money or financial resources, especially the authority to decide how money is spent
control of financial resources

Examples

After her promotion, Jane was finally in charge of the company's purse strings.
Politicians who want new projects approved must appeal to those holding the government's purse strings.
The board of directors controls the purse strings for the entire organization.

Synset Examples

If you want a raise, talk to the one who holds the purse strings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finances - monetary resources and management
  • Synonym: budget - the amount of money available for spending

Etymology

From literal purse strings that close a purse; figurative sense of financial control dates to the 18th century.

(medicine, surgery) a suture or stitching technique in which a thread is placed in a circular line around an opening and then drawn tight, closing the opening like a drawstring.
surgical drawstring suture
medical

Examples

During the operation the surgeon placed a purse string suture around the stoma to close it.
They closed the circular wound using a purse string technique before dressing the area.
A purse string may be used in hernia repair to secure the opening without extensive dissection.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Named by metaphor from the literal 'purse string' (a drawstring for closing a purse), applied to a suture that closes an opening when pulled tight.
