big brother

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
an older brother, especially one regarded with affection or as authoritative
an older brother

Examples

As the big brother, he always looked out for his younger siblings.
My big brother taught me how to ride a bicycle when I was six.
Whenever I have a problem at school, my big brother gives me advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder brother - an older brother
  • Antonym: little brother - a younger brother

2. NOUN NEW

/ bɪg ˈbrʌðə / (AmE), / bɪg ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a powerful authority (especially a government or large organization) that watches, monitors, and controls people's behaviour or private information.
intrusive, watching authority

Examples

Many citizens worry that tech companies act as a modern big brother, collecting every movement and message.
The new law was criticized for giving governments a big brother power to access private communications without warrants.
Protesters held signs warning against a future ruled by a digital big brother controlling personal data.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surveillance state - a government or system that extensively monitors and records people's actions and communications
  • Synonym: Orwellian - resembling the oppressive, controlling society depicted in George Orwell's works, especially Nineteen Eighty-Four

Etymology

From the character and title 'Big Brother' in George Orwell's novel Nineteen Eighty-Four (1949); later used metaphorically for intrusive surveillance and control.
