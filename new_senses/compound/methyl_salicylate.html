methyl salicylate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːθəl səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəl səˈlɪsɪˌleɪt / (BrE)
a liquid organic compound with a characteristic wintergreen odor, used in topical pain relievers for muscles and joints
organic compound; wintergreen-scented pain reliever
medical

Examples

Always follow the instructions when applying methyl salicylate products to the skin.
Many muscle rubs contain methyl salicylate for its pain-relieving properties.
The scent of methyl salicylate is easily recognized as wintergreen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintergreen oil - an essential oil that contains methyl salicylate
  • Synonym: menthol - a compound used similarly in topical pain relief products

Etymology

From 'methyl', denoting the methyl group in chemistry, and 'salicylate', from Latin 'salix' (willow) relating to salicylic acid.
