mutual aid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtʃuəl eɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃu.əl eɪd / (BrE)
arrangements or actions in which two or more parties, especially nations, agree to assist each other, typically in times of need or crisis
reciprocal assistance agreement

Examples

After the earthquake, international mutual aid made recovery efforts far more effective.
The principle of mutual aid is central to many global alliances and partnerships.
The two countries signed a mutual aid treaty to support each other in case of an emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperation - working together towards a common goal
  • Antonym: self-reliance - independence and relying on oneself

(in community and activist contexts) voluntary, reciprocal help organized among neighbors or peers, especially during crises, independent of formal institutions.
grassroots reciprocal community support

Examples

During the lockdown, local mutual aid groups organized food deliveries for vulnerable neighbors every week.
Volunteers set up a mutual aid fund that helped pay bills for families who lost income.
Activists argue that mutual aid builds solidarity by allowing people to help one another without relying on institutions.
