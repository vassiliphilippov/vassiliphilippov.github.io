literary pirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərɛri ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈpaɪrət / (BrE)
A person who copies another person's literary works, ideas, or words and presents them as their own; a plagiarist.
a person who plagiarizes literary works

Examples

He was accused of being a literary pirate after publishing another writer's poems under his own name.
Many authors have taken legal action against literary pirates to protect their intellectual property.
The novel was shamelessly copied by a literary pirate and sold overseas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarist - a person who plagiarizes the work of others

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8l\u026at\u0259r\u025bri \u02c8pa\u026ar\u0259t / (AmE), / \u02c8l\u026at\u0259r\u0259ri \u02c8pa\u026ar\u0259t / (BrE)
A person who illegally copies, shares, or distributes copyrighted literary works (especially digitally), for free or for profit.
illegal distributor of literary works online

Examples

The literary pirate uploaded thousands of eBooks to a torrent site, violating multiple copyright laws.
During the investigation, agents discovered that several literary pirates sold scanned textbooks for profit.
Universities warn students that acting as a literary pirate can lead to lawsuits and career damage.
