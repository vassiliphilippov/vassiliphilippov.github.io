Grand Inquisitor

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər / (AmE), / ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtə / (BrE)
the chief official in charge of the Inquisition, especially in Spain or Portugal
chief official of the Inquisition

Examples

As Grand Inquisitor, he oversaw the trials of suspected heretics.
The Grand Inquisitor wielded immense power during the Spanish Inquisition.
The history books describe the fear inspired by the Grand Inquisitor in medieval Portugal.

Etymology

From grand (meaning 'great') + inquisitor (from Latin *inquisitor*, 'examiner').

2. PROPN NEW

/ ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər / (AmE), / ɡrænd ɪnˈkwɪzɪtər / (BrE)
In the Star Wars fictional universe, the title of the leader of the Inquisitors, agents who hunt Jedi on behalf of the Empire.
Star Wars leader of the Inquisitors

Examples

In Rebels, the Grand Inquisitor leads a squad of Inquisitors hunting Jedi across the galaxy.
Fans praised the actor who played the Grand Inquisitor for his chilling performance.
The comic book series expands the story of the Grand Inquisitor and his hunt for Jedi survivors.
