capacity unit

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpæsɪti ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪti ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement used to quantify volume or capacity
unit of measurement for volume

Examples

In the United States, the gallon is a widely recognized capacity unit.
Scientists must convert between different capacity units when sharing data internationally.
The liter is a common capacity unit used around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume unit - a unit of measurement for the space an object or substance occupies

2. NOUN NEW

/ kəˈpæsəti ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpæsəti ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
(in computing and cloud services) an allocated amount or quota of resources (such as storage, compute, or bandwidth) included in a plan or purchased for use.
digital resource quota

Examples

Our subscription includes ten capacity units of storage available per month for backups.
When load spikes the service automatically consumed an extra capacity unit to maintain performance.
You can purchase additional capacity units of compute through the cloud provider's billing portal.
