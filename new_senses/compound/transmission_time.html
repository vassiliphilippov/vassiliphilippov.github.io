transmission time

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈmɪʃ.ən taɪm / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃ.ən taɪm / (BrE)
the coordinated universal time at which a signal or communication is sent from Earth to a spacecraft or other celestial body
UTC when a signal is sent from Earth

Examples

Accurate calculation of the transmission time is crucial when sending commands to a Mars rover.
Due to the distance, there is a significant delay between transmission time and reception time.
The engineers checked the transmission time for each data packet sent to the satellite.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reception time - the UTC when a signal is received from a spacecraft or celestial body

2. NOUN NEW

/ trænzˈmɪʃ.ən taɪm / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃ.ən taɪm / (BrE)
the amount of time required to send a given amount of data across a network link or channel; often calculated from packet size divided by the link's bit rate.
duration to send data over a channel

Examples

During network testing, the transmission time for a packet was measured at two milliseconds.
High bandwidth reduced the transmission time for the large video file to under one second.
Engineers calculate the transmission time by dividing the packet size by the link's bit rate.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: latency - the overall delay experienced in a network; sometimes used loosely with transmission time, but latency can include other delays as well.
