governing body

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnɪŋ ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪŋ ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a group of people or an organization that has the authority to direct, control, or manage an institution or activity
an organization with authority to control or direct

Examples

Every sports league has a governing body that sets the official rules for competition.
She was recently elected to the university's governing body.
The school's governing body approved the new policy after much debate.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administration - the people responsible for managing an organization or institution
  • Synonym: board of directors - a group of people elected to oversee activities of a company or organization

(in computing and digital platforms) a decentralized or algorithmic system—often implemented with smart contracts, token-weighted voting, or protocol rules—that has the authority to make and enforce decisions for a platform or protocol.
decentralized/algorithmic governance system

Examples

In the blockchain project, the governing body is defined by smart contracts and token-holder voting rules.
Rather than a central committee, the platform's governing body consists of automated protocols and stakeholder votes.
Developers proposed changing the protocol, but the governing body required a two-thirds token consensus first.
