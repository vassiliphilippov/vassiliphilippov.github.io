over-the-top

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvər ðə ˈtɑp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ðə ˈtɒp / (BrE)
extremely excessive or exaggerated; more than what is considered normal or reasonable.
excessive or exaggerated

Examples

His over-the-top reaction to the news surprised everyone in the office.
The decorations at the party were so over-the-top that guests were amazed.
The film's special effects are sometimes over-the-top, but they're entertaining nonetheless.

Synset Examples

an extraordinary desire for approval
it was an over-the-top experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Antonym: moderate - kept within reasonable limits; not excessive

2. ADJ NEW

/ \u02cco\u028av\u0259r \u00f0\u0259 \u02c8t\u0251p / (AmE), / \u02cc\u0259\u028av\u0259 \u00f0\u0259 \u02c8t\u0252p / (BrE)
(in media and telecommunications) delivered over the public internet directly to users, bypassing traditional distribution channels such as cable, satellite, or telecom operator services.
delivered directly over the internet (OTT)

Examples

Many viewers prefer over-the-top streaming services to traditional cable plans for on-demand viewing.
The company launched an over-the-top app that delivers movies directly without needing a set-top box.
Regulators are debating rules for over-the-top services that compete with traditional telecommunications providers.
