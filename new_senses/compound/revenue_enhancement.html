revenue enhancement

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvəˌnu ɪnˈhæns·mənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.juː ɪnˈhɑːns.mənt / (BrE)
a charge, such as a tax or fee, imposed by a government on individuals, property, or activities to raise funds for public expenditure
government-imposed charge for public funds

Examples

Airports sometimes rely on revenue enhancement strategies such as new surcharges and fees.
Many politicians debated the effectiveness of recent revenue enhancements during the budget talks.
The city implemented a new revenue enhancement to help fund park renovations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue

Etymology

From 'revenue' (income) + 'enhancement' (increase, improvement); used in finance and government policy.
