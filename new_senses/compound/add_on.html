add on

1. NOUN NEW

/ æd ɑn / (AmE), / æd ɒn / (BrE)
a piece of software or hardware that is added to an existing program or device to provide extra features or functions, often downloadable or installable.
software or hardware extension

Examples

Users can install an add-on to enhance the editor's capabilities before publishing their work.
The browser offers many security-focused add-ons that block trackers and unwanted ads on websites.
After buying the camera, she downloaded an add-on that adds RAW-format support to the firmware.
