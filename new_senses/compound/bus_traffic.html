bus traffic

1. NOUN

/ bʌs ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / bʌs ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the flow or movement of buses on a particular route, road, or area
flow or movement of buses

Examples

After the parade, bus traffic returned to normal levels.
The city is working to improve bus traffic to reduce delays for commuters.
There was heavy bus traffic on Main Street during the morning rush hour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: traffic - the flow of vehicles, people, or goods along a route

2. NOUN NEW

/ bʌs ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / bʌs ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
(computing) the amount or rate of data transferred over a computer system bus or similar shared data pathway, indicating load or congestion on that bus.
data transfer on a computer bus

Examples

During benchmarking, the CPU stalled because unusually high bus traffic slowed memory transfers.
Engineers noted that PCIe lanes showed heavy bus traffic while the GPU rendered complex scenes.
To reduce bus traffic, they implemented caching and combined multiple small writes into one operation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data throughput - the amount of data successfully transferred over a system in a given time
  • Synonym: bus bandwidth - the maximum data transfer capacity of a system bus, often measured in bytes per second

Etymology

From the computing sense of 'bus' (short for 'omnibus'), meaning a shared data pathway, + 'traffic' (flow of data).
