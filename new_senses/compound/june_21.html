June 21

1. NOUN

/ dʒun twɛnti ˈwʌn / (AmE), / dʒuːn ˈtwɛnti wʌn / (BrE)
The date marking the summer solstice in the Northern Hemisphere, when the sun is at its northernmost point.
summer solstice date (Northern Hemisphere)

Examples

Daylight hours are usually the longest on June 21 in many countries north of the equator.
Many people in the Northern Hemisphere celebrate the summer solstice on June 21 each year.
The ancient monument Stonehenge is closely associated with sunrise on June 21.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer solstice - the longest day of the year, marking the sun's northernmost point
  • Antonym: December 21 - the date of the winter solstice, when the sun is at its southernmost point
