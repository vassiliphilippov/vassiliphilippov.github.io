black day

1. NOUN

/ blæk deɪ / (AmE), / blæk deɪ / (BrE)
A day marked by significant misfortune, disaster, or sorrow.
day of misfortune or disaster

Examples

History will remember the signing of the treaty as a black day for the country.
The stock market crash was truly a black day for investors worldwide.
When the company filed for bankruptcy, it was a black day for its employees.

Synset Examples

He remembered the black day when he lost his job.
That was a black day for our team.
The storm marked a black day in our town’s history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red-letter day - a memorable or joyous day

Etymology

From the association of the colour black with mourning, calamity, or evil.

2. NOUN NEW

/ blæk deɪ / (AmE), / blæk deɪ / (BrE)
A South Korean informal observance held on April 14 when single people gather and eat jajangmyeon (black-bean-sauce noodles) to acknowledge their single status.
South Korean singles' observance (April 14)

Examples

In Seoul, many students observe black day by sharing jajangmyeon with friends.
After his breakup, he spent black day eating noodles and texting other single classmates.
Every April 14, young people in Korea observe black day by eating jajangmyeon together.

Synset Examples

In Seoul, many students observe black day by sharing jajangmyeon with friends.
After his breakup, he spent black day eating noodles and texting other single classmates.
Every April 14, young people in Korea observe black day by eating jajangmyeon together.

Etymology

Named for the black-coloured jajangmyeon noodles traditionally eaten on the day; originated in South Korea as part of modern calendar-based social observances.
