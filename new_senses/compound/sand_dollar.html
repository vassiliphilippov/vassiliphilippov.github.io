sand dollar

1. NOUN

/ sænd ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / sænd ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
a flat, round sea urchin with a rigid skeleton, typically found on sandy sea bottoms
flat, round sea urchin

Examples

A sand dollar is often found washed up on the shore, resembling a silver coin.
Divers observed a live sand dollar partially buried in the sand on the ocean floor.
The children collected a sand dollar from the beach during their summer vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea urchin - a spiny, globular marine animal related to the sand dollar

2. NOUN NEW

/ sænd ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / sænd ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
a central-bank digital currency issued by the Central Bank of The Bahamas and used as legal tender within The Bahamas.
Bahamas central-bank digital currency

Examples

The government encouraged citizens to use the sand dollar for faster, cheaper payments across the islands.
After opening an account, she transferred her wages into the sand dollar for everyday spending.
Tourists paid the hotel bill with a Bahamian sand dollar using a mobile wallet app.

Etymology

Named after the sand dollar (a flat, round sea urchin); adopted as the name of The Bahamas' central-bank digital currency.
