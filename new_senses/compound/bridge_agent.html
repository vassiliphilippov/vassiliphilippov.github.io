bridge agent

1. NOUN

/ brɪdʒ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / brɪdʒ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
an operative who acts as a courier or intermediary between a case officer and a secret agent in a hostile area
intermediary operative in espionage

Examples

As a bridge agent, she moved discreetly between the case officer and the secret agent in enemy territory.
Security protocols for a bridge agent are especially strict to protect their identity and mission.
The intelligence agency recruited a bridge agent to deliver messages between headquarters and the undercover operative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courier - a person who transports messages, documents, or goods

2. NOUN NEW

/ brɪdʒ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / brɪdʒ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
(in computing) a software program or hardware device that connects two different systems or networks and translates data or protocols so they can communicate.
software/hardware connector between systems

Examples

The integration hub uses a bridge agent to translate data between the legacy ERP system and the cloud platform.
During deployment, the team installed multiple bridge agents to connect sensors with the central monitoring service.
If the bridge agent fails, messages may queue up and synchronization between databases will stop.
