low-level

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
having a low rank, status, or degree of importance in an organization or activity.
of low rank or importance

Examples

After graduation, she accepted a low-level position at the company to gain experience.
Most low-level employees rarely interact with senior management directly.
The committee held only low-level discussions before making a decision.

Synset Examples

a low-level job
low-level discussions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-level - having a high rank or importance

3. ADJ NEW

/ ˈloʊˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
relating to computer programming or systems that operate close to the hardware, using little abstraction or high-level language features.
close to hardware; minimal abstraction

Examples

Developers often write low-level code in assembly to optimize performance.
The operating system includes several low-level routines for direct hardware access.
Students learned low-level programming to understand how processors execute instructions.

Synset Examples
