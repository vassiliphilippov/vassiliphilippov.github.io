break-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ɪn / (BrE)
An act of illegally entering a building or premises, typically to commit a crime such as theft.
illegal entry into premises to commit a crime

Examples

After the break-in, they installed a new security system at their house.
Police are investigating a recent break-in at the local museum.
The shop owner discovered a break-in had occurred during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglary - illegally entering a building, especially to steal

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbreɪk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ɪn / (BrE)
An incident of unauthorized access to a computer system or network, often involving hacking or other security breaches.
unauthorized access to a computer or network

Examples

The security team noticed an unusual spike in traffic that suggested a break-in overnight.
After the break-in, engineers isolated the vulnerable server and began their forensic analysis.
The insurer requested a full report before covering losses from the reported break-in.
