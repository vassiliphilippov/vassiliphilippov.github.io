common carrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən ˈkæriər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkæriə / (BrE)
a person or company that transports goods or passengers for the general public and is legally responsible for any loss or damage
firm or person transporting people or goods for the public
legal

Examples

Airlines acting as common carriers must accept all reasonable requests to transport passengers.
The railway company operated as a common carrier, offering services to any member of the public.
Under the law, a common carrier has a duty to deliver goods safely to their destination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: private carrier - a carrier that transports under individual contracts, not for the public

Etymology

From 'common' (available to all) and 'carrier' (transporter); established as a legal term in the 18th–19th centuries.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8k\u0251\u02d0m\u0259n \u02c8k\u00e6ri\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8k\u0252m\u0259n \u02c6k\u00e6ri\u0259 / (BrE)
(in law and telecommunications) a service provider that transmits data or communications for the public and is subject to regulations requiring nondiscriminatory treatment of traffic.
regulated neutral data carrier
legal

Examples

The regulator classified the broadband ISP as a digital common carrier, obligating it to treat traffic equally.
Advocates argued that treating social platforms as common carriers would prevent discriminatory blocking of content.
The court must decide whether the messaging app functions as a common carrier under communications statutes.
