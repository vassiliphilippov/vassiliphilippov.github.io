plot line

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɑt ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈplɒt ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
The main storyline or sequence of events in a book, play, film, or other narrative work.
main storyline in a narrative work

Examples

Critics praised the film's inventive plot line and engaging characters.
Multiple plot lines intertwine in the second season of the series.
The central plot line of the novel kept me turning pages late into the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - the main events of a play, novel, film, or similar work

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈplɑt ˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈplɒt ˈlaɪn / (BrE)
(in data visualization) the line on a plotted graph that shows how a variable changes over time or across categories.
line on a plotted graph

Examples

The chart's plot line clearly shows median income increasing each year across the city.
During the presentation she pointed at the plot line to explain the sudden rise in demand.
To compare models, overlay both datasets so the plot lines can be examined for consistent trends.
