real-estate business

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl əˌsteɪt ˈbɪznɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ɪsˌteɪt ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
The commercial activity of buying, selling, or renting properties such as land, houses, or buildings.
the business of real estate transactions

Examples

Many people made fortunes during the boom in the real-estate business in the early 2000s.
She decided to invest in the real-estate business after studying market trends for several years.
The real-estate business can be highly competitive, especially in large cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: real estate - property consisting of land or buildings
  • Synonym: property business - the business of dealing in properties

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈriːəl əˈsteɪt ˈbɪznɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl əˈsteɪt ˈbɪznɪs / (BrE)
The market or industry concerned with buying, selling, leasing, or managing virtual land and other digital property within online platforms, games, or the metaverse.
market for virtual property

Examples

Investors are buying parcels of virtual land as part of the growing real-estate business in the metaverse.
Gaming companies sell storefronts and plots to players, creating a profitable real-estate business inside the platform.
After the crash, several startups pivoted to managing virtual property and launched new real-estate business services.
