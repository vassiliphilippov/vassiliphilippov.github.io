key signature

1. NOUN

/ ˈki ˌsɪɡ.nə.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkiː ˌsɪɡ.nə.tʃə / (BrE)
a set of sharps or flats placed at the beginning of a staff to indicate the key of a piece of music
musical notation indicating key

Examples

He forgot to check the key signature before starting the song.
The piece is written in the key of D major, so its key signature has two sharps.
When the key signature changes, musicians must adjust the notes they play.

cryptographic signature for a public key

Examples

PGP users exchange a key signature to confirm a public key belongs to its owner.
The security audit found several expired key signatures that needed to be revalidated immediately.
Before joining the network she asked a colleague to apply a trusted key signature to her key.
