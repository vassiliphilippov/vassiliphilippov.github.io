vintage recording

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ rɪˈkɔr.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.tɪdʒ rɪˈkɔː.dɪŋ / (BrE)
a sound recording made in a previous era, often valued for its distinctive quality or historical significance
old sound recording of lasting value

Examples

Collectors often pay high prices for rare vintage recordings of jazz music.
Many music enthusiasts appreciate the warmth and character of vintage recordings from the 1960s.
She digitized her grandfather's vintage recordings to preserve them for future generations.

Synset Examples

I still use my old TDK tapes for some vintage recordings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique recording - a very old or collectible recording, sometimes used similarly
  • Antonym: modern recording - a recent or current sound recording

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8v\u026an.t\u026ad\u0292 r\u026a\u02c8k\u0254r.d\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8v\u026an.t\u026ad\u0292 r\u026a\u02c8k\u0254\u02d0.d\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
(in music production) a recording made in the present that is intentionally produced to sound like recordings from an earlier era, often using analog equipment, tape saturation, or vinyl-like artifacts.
modern recording made to sound old

Examples

The producer wanted a warm, nostalgic sound, so he recorded the album as a vintage recording.
Many listeners assumed the tracks were old, but they were actually modern vintage recordings produced on tape.
To capture the era's charm, the band used analog consoles when making the vintage recording.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retro recording - a recording made to imitate the style or sound of an earlier period
  • Antonym: authentic vintage recording - a recording actually made in an earlier historical era (not just produced in a vintage style)
