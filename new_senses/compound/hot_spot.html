hot spot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt ˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌspɒt / (BrE)
a place where there is political unrest and a risk of violence or conflict
place of political unrest and potential violence

Examples

Journalists were sent to cover the story from the latest hot spot in the Middle East.
Tensions have risen in several hot spots around the globe in recent months.
The region remains a hot spot for political demonstrations and possible conflict.

Synset Examples

the United States cannot police all of the world's hot spots

Etymology

From 'hot' (intense, active) + 'spot' (place) in 20th-century English, figurative use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt ˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒt ˌspɒt / (BrE)

place of intense heat or radiation

Examples

Solar flares often originate from a hot spot on the sun's surface.
The faulty wire created a hot spot that led to the fire.
