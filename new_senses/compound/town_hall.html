town hall

1. NOUN

/ taʊn hɑl / (AmE), / taʊn hɔːl / (BrE)
A building used for the administration of a town's government, often housing official offices and sometimes used for public meetings or events.
building for town government administration

Examples

Many important decisions are made inside the town hall each month.
The mayor gave a speech at the town hall during the annual festival.
The old town hall was renovated to preserve its historic architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city hall - a government building that houses administrative offices of a city government

2. NOUN NEW

/ taʊn hɑl / (AmE), / taʊn hɔːl / (BrE)
A public meeting or forum, often organized by a government body, organization, or company, where officials or leaders address an audience and answer questions; can be held in person or virtually.
public or company meeting/Q&A

Examples

Residents filled the town hall to hear council plans for the new park.
The CEO answered staff questions during the virtual town hall about the merger.
After several complaints, the university scheduled a town hall to discuss campus safety measures.
