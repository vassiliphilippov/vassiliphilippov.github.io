traditional knowledge

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənl ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəl ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
knowledge passed down through generations within a community, especially by oral tradition, custom, or anecdote, often relating to culture, nature, or practical skills
knowledge passed down through tradition

Examples

Many indigenous communities rely on traditional knowledge to manage natural resources sustainably.
Much of the traditional knowledge about medicinal plants is handed down from elders to younger generations.
Researchers are increasingly recognizing the value of traditional knowledge in understanding local ecosystems.

Synset Examples

early peoples passed on plant and animal lore through legend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folklore - traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community
  • Synonym: oral tradition - cultural information transmitted by word of mouth

2. NOUN NEW

/ trəˈdɪʃənəl ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəl ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in law and policy) knowledge, practices, or beliefs held by Indigenous or local communities that are recognized and protected by legal regimes, often triggering requirements such as prior informed consent and equitable benefit-sharing when used by third parties.
legally recognized community knowledge
legal

Examples

Biotech companies must obtain consent before using traditional knowledge about medicinal plants in drug research.
The country's new law recognizes traditional knowledge and requires benefit-sharing with indigenous communities.
Negotiators discussed how traditional knowledge should be protected under the Nagoya Protocol's rules.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local knowledge - knowledge held by local communities about their environment, resources, or customs, closely related to traditional knowledge
