steam iron

1. NOUN

/ stiːm ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / stiːm ˈaɪən / (BrE)
An electric iron that emits steam to help remove wrinkles from fabric.
electric iron that emits steam

Examples

Be careful when using the steam iron on delicate fabrics.
She bought a new steam iron to make ironing clothes easier.
The steam iron quickly removed wrinkles from his shirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a hand-held device that presses clothes by applying heat and pressure

2. NOUN NEW

/ stiːm ˈaɪən / (AmE), / stiːm ˈaɪə(n) / (BrE)
A hair-styling device that uses steam to straighten, set, or shape hair, often claimed to reduce heat damage compared with dry-heat straighteners.
steam-based hair straightener

Examples

She used a steam iron on small sections to smooth frizz before the party.
The stylist prefers a steam iron because it leaves hair shinier and less damaged than a hot plate.
Manufacturers advertised the new steam iron as ideal for creating long-lasting straight styles without extreme heat.
