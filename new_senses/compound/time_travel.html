time travel

1. NOUN

/ taɪm ˈtrævəl / (AmE), / taɪm ˈtrævəl / (BrE)
the idea or action of traveling from one point in time to another, especially to the past or future, typically found in science fiction.
traveling to a different point in time

Examples

Many movies feature characters who use time travel to fix their mistakes.
Stories about time travel have fascinated people for more than a century.
The novel explores the paradoxes created by time travel to the past.

Synset Examples

He wishes he could time travel in a DePlorean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time machine - a device imagined for traveling through time

2. NOUN NEW

/ taɪm ˈtrævəl / (AmE), / taɪm ˈtrævəl / (BrE)
(computing, especially debugging) a technique or tool feature that lets developers move backward or forward through a program's execution or replay past program states to inspect behavior and locate bugs.
debugging technique to replay program states

Examples

The engineer used the time travel feature to step backward and find the memory leak.
Teams rely on time travel debugging in production to reproduce crashes that are hard to replicate locally.
Using time travel, she replayed the whole process step by step to locate the bug.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time-travel debugging - the specific debugging method of recording and replaying program execution to inspect past states.
  • Synonym: reverse debugging - a debugging approach that allows stepping backward through program execution to find the cause of a fault.
