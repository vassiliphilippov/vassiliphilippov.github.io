stepping stone

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpɪŋ ˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɛpɪŋ ˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a flat stone set in a marsh or shallow water, used to step on when crossing
flat stone for stepping when crossing water or marsh

Examples

A row of stepping stones led us safely across the muddy path.
She slipped off a stepping stone and splashed into the cold water.
The children leapt from one stepping stone to another as they crossed the creek.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈstɛpɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
an action, event, or experience that helps you to make progress or move towards a goal
means of advancement; aid to progress

Examples

For many new graduates, an internship is often just a stepping stone to a permanent job.
He viewed the project as a valuable stepping stone in his engineering career.
The position served as a stepping stone for her to start her own business.

Synset Examples

the job was just a stepping stone on his way to fame and riches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springboard - something that helps launch progress to something else
  • Antonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or advancement

Etymology

Originally referred to stones used to cross streams; figurative use dates to the 16th century.
