rescue operation

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛs.kju ˌɑ.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈrɛs.kju ˌɒp.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an organized activity to save people from danger or confinement
an organized act of saving from danger

Examples

Firefighters carried out a difficult rescue operation after the building collapsed in the earthquake.
The rescue operation required coordination between police, paramedics, and volunteers.
