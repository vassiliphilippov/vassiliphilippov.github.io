red shirt

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ʃɝt / (AmE), / rɛd ʃɜːt / (BrE)
a member of a socialist or leftist group, especially identified by wearing a red shirt as a symbol of socialism or communism
member of a socialist group

Examples

After joining the protest, she wore a red shirt to stand in solidarity with other socialist supporters.
In the early 20th century, being called a red shirt could endanger your job or reputation.
Some historical movements are remembered for their distinctive red shirts and leftist slogans.

Synset Examples

In college, he donned a red shirt and proudly identified as a Commie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - a person who supports or believes in the principles of communism
  • Synonym: socialist - a person who advocates or practices socialism
  • Antonym: capitalist - a person who supports or believes in capitalism

Etymology

From the practice of socialist and communist groups wearing red shirts as a symbol of their affiliation; red as a color has long symbolized leftist political movements.

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɛd ʃɜrt / (AmE), / rɛd ʃɜːt / (BrE)
(in Thailand) a member or supporter of the Red Shirt movement (United Front for Democracy Against Dictatorship), a political protest group often identified by wearing red shirts.
supporter of Thailand's Red Shirt movement

Examples

During the Bangkok rally, a red shirt led chants demanding the prime minister's resignation.
She had been a committed red shirt since the large protests began in 2010.
Journalists interviewed several red shirts outside the courthouse after the hearing.

Synset Examples

During the Bangkok rally, a red shirt led chants demanding the prime minister's resignation.
She had been a committed red shirt since the large protests began in 2010.
Journalists interviewed several red shirts outside the courthouse after the hearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United Front for Democracy Against Dictatorship (UDD) - Thai political movement and organization commonly called the Red Shirts
  • Antonym: yellow shirt - a member of an opposing Thai protest movement (People's Alliance for Democracy); politically opposed to the Red Shirts

Etymology

Named for the movement's practice of wearing red shirts; refers to supporters of the United Front for Democracy Against Dictatorship (UDD).
