rag doll

1. NOUN

/ ræɡ dɑl / (AmE), / ræɡ dɒl / (BrE)
A doll made from cloth and stuffed with soft material, often with painted or stitched features.
soft cloth doll

Examples

He collected vintage rag dolls from flea markets around the country.
She gave her daughter a handmade rag doll for her birthday.
The old rag doll was missing an arm but still loved by the child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffed animal - A toy animal made of cloth and stuffed with a soft material

2. NOUN NEW

/ ræɡ dɑl / (AmE), / ræɡ dɒl / (BrE)
(in video games and computer animation) a character model whose body is simulated with physics so it moves or falls limp and reacts dynamically to forces.
physics-simulated limp character

Examples

After the explosion, the NPC went limp and became a rag doll that tumbled down the stairs.
The developers improved the rag doll behaviour so characters react more realistically to collisions.
Players laughed when the boss's physics glitched and he flew across the map like a rag doll.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragdoll physics - a simulation technique that makes a character's body move limp and respond to forces after an impact
  • Synonym: physics-based animation - animation produced by simulating physical forces rather than by hand keyframes

Etymology

Named by analogy with the limp cloth toy 'rag doll'; applied in game development to describe physics-simulated bodies.
