sulphur hexafluoride

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˌhɛk.səˌfluːˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˌhɛksəˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless gas used mainly as an insulating material in the electrical industry and known for its strong greenhouse effect
colorless insulating gas; strong greenhouse gas
medical

Examples

Concerns have been raised about the impact of sulphur hexafluoride on global warming.
Technicians must handle sulphur hexafluoride carefully because of its high density and greenhouse potential.
The electrical industry uses sulphur hexafluoride as an effective insulating gas in high-voltage switches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse gas - a gas that contributes to global warming by trapping heat in the atmosphere

Etymology

From 'sulphur' (Latin 'sulphur') and 'hexafluoride' (hexa- meaning six + 'fluoride', from fluorine).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8s\u028clf\u025a \u02cch\u025bk.s\u0259\u02ccflu\u02d0\u02c8ra\u026ad / (AmE), / \u02c8s\u028clf\u0259 \u02cch\u025bks\u0259\u02c8fl\u028a\u0259ra\u026ad / (BrE)
(in medicine) a gas used in microbubble form as an ultrasound contrast agent to improve the visibility of organs or lesions during diagnostic imaging.
ultrasound contrast agent (microbubbles)
medical

Examples

Doctors use sulphur hexafluoride microbubbles to enhance ultrasound images of patients' livers during diagnostic scans.
The radiologist observed the sulphur hexafluoride contrast noticeably improving the tumor's visibility on ultrasound.
Clinical trials found that sulphur hexafluoride microbubbles are safe and effective as an ultrasound contrast agent.
