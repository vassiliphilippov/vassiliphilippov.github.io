cantering rhythm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɚ.ɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tər.ɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
A cardiac rhythm marked by the presence of an extra heartbeat sound, which may indicate a heart abnormality.
cardiac rhythm with extra sound
medical

Examples

A cantering rhythm is sometimes heard in patients with advanced heart failure.
After the patient complained of fatigue, the cardiologist listened for signs such as a cantering rhythm.
The doctor detected a cantering rhythm during the cardiac examination, suggesting a possible abnormality.

Etymology

From 'canter' (a horse's gait, from Latin 'cantherius') + 'rhythm' (from Greek 'rhythmos'). The term likens the abnormal heart sound to a horse's cantering gait.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈkæn.tə.rɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tər.ɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm / (BrE)
A hoofbeat-like, galloping rhythmic pattern used in music, dance, or poetry, producing a steady three-beat or gallop-like pulse.
galloping musical or poetic rhythm

Examples

The band's drummer created a cantering rhythm that pushed the song forward with relentless energy.
Her choreography emphasized a cantering rhythm, matching dancers' steps to a steady gallop-like pulse.
In the poem, the repeated syllables formed a cantering rhythm that suggested long horse strides.
