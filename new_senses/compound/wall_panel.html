wall panel

1. NOUN

/ wɔl ˈpænəl / (AmE), / wɔːl ˈpænəl / (BrE)
A flat, typically rectangular piece of material that forms part of a wall, usually as part of the interior or exterior surface.
panel forming part of a wall

Examples

Moisture-resistant wall panels are recommended for use in bathrooms and kitchens.
She chose oak wall panels for the living room to give it a classic look.
The carpenter installed a new wall panel to replace the damaged section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - material or boards used to cover walls or ceilings

2. NOUN NEW

/ wɔl ˈpænəl / (AmE), / wɔːl ˈpænəl / (BrE)
A wall-mounted electronic control panel or user interface used to operate building or home systems such as lighting, heating, security, or automation.
wall-mounted electronic control panel

Examples

The hotel installed a touch-sensitive wall panel so guests could control lighting and temperature.
Engineers mounted a secure wall panel in the lobby to manage climate and access systems.
During the tour, the building manager showed us the new wall panel for security controls.
