learning curve

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ kɝːv / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ kɜːv / (BrE)
a graph or representation showing how quickly or effectively someone becomes proficient at a task over time, often referring to the rate of improvement with experience
graph of progression in learning over time

Examples

The new software has a steep learning curve, but it's worth mastering.
They plotted the students' progress on a learning curve to measure improvement over time.
With experience, the learning curve for this equipment becomes much less daunting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: steep learning curve - a situation in which learning something is difficult and requires a lot of effort in a short time
