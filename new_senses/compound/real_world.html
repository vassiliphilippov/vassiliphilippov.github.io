real world

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəl ˌwɝːld / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˌwɜːld / (BrE)
the world of practical experience and everyday affairs, as opposed to theoretical or academic situations
the world of practical experience

Examples

After college, she found the real world far more challenging than she expected.
His research may sound impressive, but it has little relevance to the real world.
Internships provide students with valuable exposure to the real world.

Synset Examples

a good consultant must have a lot of experience in the real world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
  • Antonym: academic - related to education or scholarly pursuits instead of practical situations
