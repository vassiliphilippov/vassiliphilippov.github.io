snack bar

1. NOUN

/ snæk bɑr / (AmE), / snæk bɑː / (BrE)
A small, usually inexpensive establishment where snacks and light meals are sold and eaten.
small eating place for snacks

Examples

After the movie, we grabbed a sandwich at the snack bar in the lobby.
Many students eat lunch at the snack bar on campus between classes.
The beach had a small snack bar that sold ice cream and cold drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - self-service restaurant with simple meals
  • Synonym: diner - small, informal restaurant, especially in the US

2. NOUN NEW

/ snæk bɑr / (AmE), / snæk bɑː / (BrE)
(in computing and user-interface design) a short, temporary on-screen message, usually appearing at the edge of the screen to inform the user or offer a simple action (for example, 'undo').
temporary on-screen notification (UI)

Examples

When you save a file, a snack bar briefly appears at the bottom confirming the action.
Developers prefer a snack bar for short messages because it doesn't block the main screen.
The UX guidelines recommend using a snack bar for undo actions with a clear call to action.
