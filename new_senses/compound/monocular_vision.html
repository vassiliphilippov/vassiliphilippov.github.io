monocular vision

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑk.jə.lɚ ˈvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / məˈnɒk.jʊ.lə ˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
the ability to see with only one eye, or the use of a single eye for vision as opposed to both eyes together
vision using one eye

Examples

After the accident, he had to adjust to life with monocular vision.
Owls primarily rely on binocular rather than monocular vision to judge distances accurately.
Some species of fish have only monocular vision, limiting their depth perception.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: binocular vision - vision using both eyes together

Etymology

From Latin 'monocularis' (having one eye) + 'vision'.

(in computer vision and robotics) the analysis of a scene, including depth estimation or navigation, using input from a single camera rather than stereo cameras or other depth sensors.
single-camera scene analysis

Examples

The autonomous drone relies on monocular vision to navigate narrow corridors using a single forward-facing camera.
Researchers improved obstacle avoidance by training a network on synthetic data for monocular vision tasks.
Some autonomous cars use monocular vision as a lower-cost alternative to stereo cameras in certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monocular depth estimation - computational methods for predicting depth from a single image
  • Antonym: stereo vision - scene analysis using two cameras (a stereo pair) to compute depth from disparities

Etymology

From Latin 'monocularis' (having one eye) + 'vision'; applied metaphorically in computing to systems using one camera.
