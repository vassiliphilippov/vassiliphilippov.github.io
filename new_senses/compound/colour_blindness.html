colour blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlər ˌblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
An inability or decreased ability to distinguish between certain colours, especially as a result of a genetic condition.
inability to distinguish certain colours
british medical

Examples

Colour blindness is more common in men than in women.
He was diagnosed with colour blindness after failing a vision test at school.
Her career as a pilot was restricted due to her colour blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - Inability to distinguish between certain colours; American English spelling

A social or political stance that claims to ignore race or colour in decisions and policy, often criticised for overlooking structural inequalities.
claim to ignore race

Examples

Many people argue that colour blindness in hiring ignores structural inequalities and harms minority applicants.
Her school's policy of colour blindness failed to address the persistent achievement gap between students.
Critics say advocating colour blindness reduces complex histories to a single misleading idea in public debates.
