sound camera

1. NOUN

/ saʊnd ˈkæmərə / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈkæmərə / (BrE)
A movie camera that records audio in synchronization with the visual images.
movie camera that records synchronized sound

Examples

Early films required a sound camera to record both voices and background noises.
Replacing the old silent equipment, the studio invested in a modern sound camera.
The director insisted on using a sound camera to capture authentic dialogue on set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movie camera - a camera for recording motion pictures

2. NOUN NEW

/ saʊnd ˈkæmərə / (AmE), / saʊnd ˈkæmərə / (BrE)
A device that uses a microphone array and signal processing to create visual images or maps showing the location and intensity of sound sources.
device that visualizes sound sources

Examples

Engineers used a sound camera to locate the noise source inside the turbine housing.
At the factory, the team recorded readings with a sound camera for two hours.
The consultant showed a clear noise map captured by the sound camera during tests.
