Madagascar jasmine

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædəˈɡæskər ˈdʒæsəmɪn / (AmE), / ˌmædəˈɡæskə ˈdʒæsəmɪn / (BrE)
A twining, woody vine native to Madagascar, with thick, dark, waxy evergreen leaves and clusters of large, fragrant, waxy white flowers, widely cultivated in warm regions.
woody vine with fragrant white flowers

Examples

Gardeners love Madagascar jasmine because it thrives in warm climates and produces fragrant blossoms.
She trained the Madagascar jasmine along the trellis for its beautiful white flowers.
The greenhouse was filled with the sweet scent of blooming Madagascar jasmine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stephanotis - another name for Madagascar jasmine

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌmædəˈgæskər ˈdʒæsəmɪn / (AmE), / ˌmædəˈgæskə ˈdʒæsəmɪn / (BrE)
(in perfumery) the floral scent, extract, or ingredient obtained from the Madagascar jasmine plant, used as a perfume note or raw material in fragrances.
floral perfume ingredient/extract

Examples

The perfumer added a hint of Madagascar jasmine to the base to enhance the floral heart.
The new fragrance features Madagascar jasmine as a prominent note among citrus and sandalwood accords.
Ingredient lists often label the extract simply as Madagascar jasmine when sourced from stephanotis plants.
