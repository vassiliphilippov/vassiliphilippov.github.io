Trojan Horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒən hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən hɔːs / (BrE)
A large hollow wooden horse used by the Greeks to secretly enter the city of Troy during the Trojan War.
wooden horse from Greek legend

Examples

The expression 'beware of Greeks bearing gifts' comes from the story of the Trojan Horse.
The legend of the Trojan Horse has fascinated historians and writers for centuries.
They built a replica of the Trojan Horse for the museum exhibit.

Etymology

From the story in ancient Greek mythology, where Greeks concealed soldiers in a wooden horse to enter Troy.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8tro\u028ad\u0292\u0259n h\u0254rs / (AmE), / \u02c8tr\u0259\u028ad\u0292\u0259n h\u0254\u02d0s / (BrE)
A type of malicious software that disguises itself as legitimate or useful software to trick a user into installing or running it, thereby allowing unauthorized access, data theft, or damage.
malicious software disguised as legitimate program

Examples

Security researchers discovered a Trojan Horse that secretly stole login credentials from employees' computers.
Many phishing emails try to trick users into downloading a Trojan Horse disguised as a useful installer.
After the breach, technicians found a hidden Trojan Horse that opened a backdoor for the attackers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malware - software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise computers and networks
  • Synonym: computer virus - a type of malware that can replicate itself and spread between systems

Etymology

From the Greek myth of the Trojan Horse; used metaphorically in computing to describe malicious programs that hide harmful payloads.
