pile-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪl ʌp / (BrE)
a crash involving several vehicles, often occurring on a road or highway
multi-vehicle traffic accident

Examples

A thick fog caused a massive pile-up on the motorway this morning.
Emergency services worked quickly to clear the pile-up and help the injured.
Several lanes were closed for hours due to the pile-up involving ten vehicles.

Synset Examples

He witnessed a pile-up of cars during the storm.
There was a massive pile-up on the highway this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - collision between vehicles or with an object
  • Synonym: accident - an unexpected event, often resulting in damage or injury

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpaɪl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪl ʌp / (BrE)
(in particle physics) the occurrence of multiple particle collisions in the same detector readout, producing overlapping signals that complicate reconstruction of the primary interaction.
overlapping particle collisions (physics)

Examples

At high luminosity, the detector recorded dozens of overlapping pile-up events during each beam crossing.
To improve accuracy, analysts subtract estimated contributions from pile-up before reconstructing the primary collision.
Simulation studies showed that higher collision rates increase the background from pile-up and reduce signal clarity.

Synset Examples
