lie detector

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ dɪˌtɛktər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ dɪˌtɛktə / (BrE)
A device, commonly known as a polygraph, that measures and records physiological indicators such as blood pressure, pulse, and respiration, which are sometimes taken as evidence of whether a person is lying.
device measuring signs of lying (polygraph)

Examples

He volunteered to take a lie detector test to clear his name.
Some people question the reliability of a lie detector in court proceedings.
The police used a lie detector during their investigation to determine if the suspect was telling the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygraph - an instrument that records physiological data and is used as a lie detector

2. NOUN NEW

/  / (AmE), /  / (BrE)
(informal) An automated system, often using software or AI, that attempts to detect deception by analysing language, voice, facial expressions, or other behavioural cues rather than measuring physiological signals.
AI/software deception detector

Examples

The HR team deployed a lie detector that analyzes speech patterns during video interviews.
Researchers developed a smartphone lie detector app to flag deceptive answers in quick surveys.
Critics warn that the new lie detector cannot reliably detect dishonesty across different languages and cultures.
