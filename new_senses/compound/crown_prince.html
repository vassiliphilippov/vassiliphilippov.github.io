crown prince

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ˈprɪns / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈprɪns / (BrE)
the male heir apparent to a throne; the prince who is expected to become king
male heir to a throne

Examples

After the king fell ill, the crown prince took on more official responsibilities.
Many believed the crown prince would usher in a new era of reforms upon his accession.
The crown prince attended the state banquet in honor of the visiting dignitaries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: princess - a female member of a royal family, especially a daughter or granddaughter of a king or queen
  • Synonym: heir apparent - the person who is first in line to a throne and whose claim cannot be set aside by the birth of another

Etymology

From 'crown' referring to royal authority, and 'prince' from Old French 'prince', from Latin 'princeps' (first, chief).

2. NOUN NEW

/ kraʊn ˈprɪns / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈprɪns / (BrE)
used figuratively to refer to a person widely regarded as the likely successor or the favored candidate to take a leading position in an organization, field, or movement.
figurative designated successor

Examples

Business columnists called him the crown prince of tech after his startup's rapid success.
After the CEO announced retirement, many named the young CFO the company's crown prince.
Political commentators labeled the mayor the crown prince of the opposition after his popular speech.
