genus Parula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈruːlə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs pəˈruːlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, colorful wood warblers found in the Americas, formerly comprising several species in the family Parulidae.
a genus of wood warblers (Parulidae)

Examples

Birdwatchers often seek out members of the genus Parula during spring migration.
Scientists have revised the classification of the genus Parula based on new genetic evidence.
The genus Parula includes several North and Central American wood warbler species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Parulidae - the family of wood warblers to which genus Parula belongs

Etymology

From New Latin *Parula*, given as the genus name in scientific classification.
