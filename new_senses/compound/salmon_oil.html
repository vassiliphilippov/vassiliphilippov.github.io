salmon oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmən ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsæmən ɔɪl / (BrE)
A fatty oil extracted from the byproducts of salmon processing, used in making soap and dressing leather.
oil from salmon byproducts

Examples

Salmon oil is sometimes added to soap for its conditioning properties.
She used salmon oil to treat the leather on her boots.
The factory produced salmon oil as a byproduct during the canning process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish oil - oil obtained from the tissues of oily fish, often used as a dietary supplement

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsæmən ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsæmən ɔɪl / (BrE)
An oil obtained from salmon that is refined and sold as a dietary supplement, rich in omega-3 fatty acids and usually taken in capsules or as a liquid to support health.
dietary fish-oil supplement

Examples

She started taking salmon oil capsules to boost her omega-3 intake.
The veterinarian recommended salmon oil for the dog's dry coat and dull fur.
Many brands sell liquid salmon oil to mix into smoothies or take by spoonful daily.
