short sale

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt seɪl / (AmE), / ʃɔːt seɪl / (BrE)
the sale of securities or commodity futures that the seller does not own, with the intention of buying them back later at a lower price to make a profit
sale of securities not owned by seller

Examples

A short sale can be risky if the market moves against the trader's expectations.
He made a profit through a short sale by buying back the shares at a lower price.
Many investors lost money when their short sale positions were forced to close after the stock price rose unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short selling - the act or practice of engaging in a short sale

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u0283\u0254rt se\u026al / (AmE), / \u0283\u0254\u02d0t se\u026al / (BrE)
a real-estate transaction in which a homeowner sells a property for less than the amount owed on the mortgage, with the lender's agreement to accept the reduced payoff.
sale of property for less than mortgage

Examples

During the housing crisis, many homeowners pursued a short sale to avoid foreclosure.
The bank approved the short sale after determining it was cheaper than starting foreclosure proceedings.
They negotiated a short sale with the lender to sell the house for less than the mortgage balance.
