Il Duce

1. NOUN

/ il ˈduːtʃeɪ / (AmE), / il ˈduːtʃeɪ / (BrE)
the title adopted by Benito Mussolini, the fascist dictator of Italy from 1925 to 1945
title of Italian fascist leader Mussolini

Examples

During World War II, Il Duce formed an alliance with Nazi Germany.
Historians often debate the legacy of Il Duce and his impact on 20th-century Europe.
The Italian people were once rallied by speeches given by Il Duce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mussolini - Benito Mussolini, Italian fascist dictator (1883-1945)

Etymology

Italian, meaning 'the Leader'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ il \u02c8du\u02d0t\u0283e\u026a / (AmE), / il \u02c8du\u02d0t\u0283e\u026a / (BrE)
(informal, figurative) an epithet used to describe a political leader who behaves like a dictator; used to compare someone to Benito Mussolini.
epithet for an authoritarian leader

Examples

Several columnists warned that the prime minister was becoming the new Il Duce with strict media controls.
Protesters chanted that he was an Il Duce, accusing him of silencing opposition and removing checks on power.
The historian argued that comparing modern leaders to Il Duce risks oversimplifying complex political situations.
