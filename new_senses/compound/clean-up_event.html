clean-up event

1. NOUN

/ ˈklin ʌp ɪˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkliːn ʌp ɪˈvent / (BrE)
an organized activity in which a group of people gather to remove litter or waste from a public place
event to remove litter from a place

Examples

After the festival, a clean-up event helped restore the area to its original condition.
Dozens of volunteers attended the clean-up event at the city park last Saturday.
The school organized a clean-up event to teach students about environmental responsibility.

Synset Examples

Our local beachkeeper organizes weekly clean-up events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litter pick - an event to pick up trash from an area
  • Degree Higher: volunteer activity - an event where people volunteer to do work for the community

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8klin \u028cp \u026a\u02c8v\u025bnt / (AmE), / \u02c8kli\u02d0n \u028cp \u026a\u02c8vent / (BrE)
(in computing and IT) a scheduled or triggered maintenance operation that removes temporary files, caches, logs, or other obsolete data to free space or restore system health.
scheduled maintenance to remove temporary data

Examples

The server runs a nightly clean-up event to delete temporary files and free disk space.
After the failed update, the ops team scheduled a clean-up event to remove corrupted cache entries.
Cloud platforms often trigger automatic clean-up events when storage usage exceeds predefined thresholds.
