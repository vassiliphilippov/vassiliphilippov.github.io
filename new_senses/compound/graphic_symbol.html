graphic symbol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪmbəl / (BrE)
A written symbol used to represent a speech sound or unit in a language.
written symbol representing speech

Examples

Chinese writing uses thousands of different graphic symbols to represent words and ideas.
Each letter in the alphabet is considered a graphic symbol in linguistic studies.
Modern road signs often incorporate universally recognized graphic symbols for clarity.

Synset Examples

the Greek alphabet has 24 characters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: character - a single letter, figure, or symbol used in writing
  • Synonym: glyph - a symbolic figure or character, usually carved or inscribed
  • Antonym: phoneme - the smallest unit of sound in speech

Etymology

From Greek 'graphikos' (of writing) and 'symbolon' (mark, sign).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪk ˈsɪmbəl / (BrE)
A pictorial or digital sign (such as an icon or emoji) used in visual media or online communication to represent an idea, object, emotion, or function.
pictorial digital icon or emoji

Examples

She added a graphic symbol of a smiley face to brighten the chat.
The app displays each file type with a small graphic symbol beside its name for quick recognition.
Designers debated whether the universal graphic symbol for recycling should be simplified for small screens.

Synset Examples

She added a graphic symbol of a smiley face to brighten the chat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emoji - a small digital image or icon used to express an idea or emotion in electronic communication
  • Synonym: icon - a symbol or graphic on a screen that represents a program, file, or function
  • Synonym: pictogram - a pictorial symbol that conveys meaning through illustration rather than text

Etymology

From Greek 'graphikos' (of writing) and 'symbolon' (mark, sign).
