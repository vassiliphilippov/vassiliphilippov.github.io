investment advisor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt ədˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt ədˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
a professional who gives advice to others on how to invest their money, typically for a fee
person who advises on investments

Examples

Before making any major decisions, she consulted her investment advisor.
Many investment advisors recommend diversifying your portfolio to reduce risk.
We hired an investment advisor to help manage our retirement savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financial advisor - a person who offers financial advice, including but not limited to investments

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt ədˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt ədˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
(in finance/technology) a digital service or online platform that provides automated, algorithm-driven investment advice and portfolio management, often with little or no human involvement (also called a robo-advisor).
automated online investment service (robo-advisor)

Examples

Many beginners use a investment advisor online to automate portfolio allocation.
Their bank's investment advisor suggested a low-cost index fund portfolio via an app.
Before opening an account, she compared several investment advisor services and their annual fees.
