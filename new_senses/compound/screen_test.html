screen test

1. NOUN

/ skrin tɛst / (AmE), / skriːn tɛst / (BrE)
a filmed audition to assess an actor's suitability for a role in film or television
filmed audition of an actor or actress

Examples

He nailed his screen test and was cast as the lead in the movie.
Many famous actors got their first big break after a successful screen test.
The director asked her to come in for a screen test before making a final decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audition - a trial performance to evaluate suitability for a role

2. NOUN NEW

/ skrin tɛst / (AmE), / skriːn tɛst / (BrE)
a test of a display device (such as a monitor or TV) to check color accuracy, brightness, uniformity, or other visual performance.
test of a display's visual performance

Examples

The technicians ran a screen test to calibrate colors on the new monitor before shipping.
Before buying the TV, she performed a quick screen test to check brightness and contrast.
Factory quality control includes multiple screen tests to ensure each display meets color standards.
