to strip-mine

1. VERB

/ ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
to remove surface layers of soil or rock from an area in order to extract minerals or other resources near the surface, typically by using large machinery
remove surface layers for mining

Examples

If we strip-mine this forest, it will take decades for the ecosystem to recover.
Several companies strip-mine large areas of land to reach hidden coal deposits.
Strict regulations are needed wherever corporations strip-mine to ensure minimal environmental harm.

Synset Examples

They plan to strip-mine the area for valuable minerals.
The company is accused of strip-mining without proper regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavate - to dig out material from the ground
  • Synonym: quarry - to extract stone or other materials from a quarry

Etymology

Formed from 'strip' + 'mine'; refers to stripping away earth to access minerals.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstrɪp ˌmaɪn / (BrE)
(figuratively) to extract or harvest very large quantities of data or information from a source, often rapidly or indiscriminately, usually for commercial or analytic use.
harvest large amounts of data

Examples

The startup plans to strip-mine user data to build detailed consumer profiles for advertisers.
Regulators accused the company of strip-mining public records to sell targeted political advertising lists.
After the merger they aggressively strip-mined server logs to monetize behavioral patterns and preferences.

Synset Examples

The startup plans to strip-mine user data to build detailed consumer profiles for advertisers.
Regulators accused the company of strip-mining public records to sell targeted political advertising lists.
After the merger they aggressively strip-mined server logs to monetize behavioral patterns and preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest - to collect large amounts of data or resources for use or analysis
  • Synonym: extract - to remove or obtain data or information from a larger set or source

Etymology

From strip + mine; a metaphorical extension of physical mining to large-scale extraction of digital information.
