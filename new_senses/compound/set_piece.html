set piece

1. NOUN

/ sɛt pis / (AmE), / sɛt piːs / (BrE)
an individual item of scenery designed to stand alone as part of a stage setting
theatrical scenery item

Examples

A large painted tree served as the main set piece in the forest setting.
Each set piece had to be constructed to be both sturdy and lightweight.
The crew quickly moved the set piece into position for the next scene.

2. NOUN NEW

/ s5bt pis / (AmE), / s5bt pid0s / (BrE)
a long, carefully planned and staged sequence in a film, television show, or video game intended to display action, stunts, effects, or spectacle.
planned cinematic/action sequence

Examples

The final chase was the movie's biggest set piece, leaving audiences cheering throughout the crowded theater.
Each episode featured at least one elaborate set piece that boosted the show's ratings and social media buzz.
Players can replay the game's toughest set piece to practice the timing of their dodges and attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showpiece - a particularly impressive performance or part of a work intended to show skill or spectacle

Etymology

From the theatrical term 'set piece' (an item of stage scenery); extended to film, television, and games to mean a carefully planned dramatic or action sequence.
