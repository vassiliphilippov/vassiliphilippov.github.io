joe job

1. NOUN

/ dʒoʊ dʒɑb / (AmE), / dʒəʊ dʒɒb / (BrE)
An unskilled, low-paying, or unfulfilling job, often considered menial or undesirable.
low-paying or unfulfilling job
informal

Examples

After college, he had to take a joe job just to pay the rent.
Many people feel stuck in a joe job before finding their real career.
Tired of working a joe job, she decided to start her own business.

Synset Examples

He was stuck in a joe job, waiting for something better.
She left her joe job to pursue her passion.
Many graduates end up in a joe job after college.

Etymology

From the common given name 'Joe' used generically to refer to an average or ordinary person.

2. NOUN NEW

/ dʒoʊ dʒɑb / (AmE), / dʒəʊ dʒɒb / (BrE)
An email attack in which messages are forged to appear to come from a target (or to appear sent by the target) in order to discredit, harass, or frame them.
forged email attack to discredit someone
informal

Examples

The email was a joe job that smeared the journalist by impersonating his address.
Security teams discovered the joe job and traced the forged headers to several compromised servers.
After the politician received the joe job, supporters rushed to correct the false accusations online.

Synset Examples
