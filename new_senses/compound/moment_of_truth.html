moment of truth

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv truθ / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv truːθ / (BrE)
in bullfighting, the moment when the matador attempts to kill the bull; more generally, a critical time when an important decision or outcome occurs
critical moment, especially when the matador kills the bull

Examples

In the bullring, the moment of truth determines the matador's skill and bravery.
The announcer explained the significance of the moment of truth to those unfamiliar with bullfighting.
The crowd held its breath during the moment of truth as the matador faced the bull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climax - the most intense or important point of something
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger

Etymology

From Spanish *el momento de la verdad* (the moment of truth), originally used in bullfighting.
