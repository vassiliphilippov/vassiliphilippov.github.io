ventral fin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛntrəl fɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɛntrəl fɪn / (BrE)
either of a pair of fins attached to the underside (pelvic region) of a fish, aiding in the control of movement direction and corresponding to the hind limbs of land vertebrates
pair of fins on a fish's underside

Examples

If a fish injures its ventral fin, it may have trouble keeping its balance.
In some species, the ventral fins are larger and play a role in mating displays.
The ventral fin helps the fish stabilize itself as it swims through the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvic fin - one of the paired fins on the underside of a fish's body

Etymology

From 'ventral' (relating to the underside) and 'fin'. 'Ventral' from Latin 'venter' (belly).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvɛntrəl fɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɛntrəl fɪn / (BrE)
(in aeronautics and rocketry) a fin or set of fins mounted on the underside of an aircraft, missile, or rocket to improve directional stability and control.
underside stabilizing fin on aircraft or rockets

Examples

The model rocket gained improved yaw stability after attaching a ventral fin for flight tests.
Engineers added two small ventral fins under the fuselage to reduce unwanted rolling during high-speed flight.
During the test, the pilot felt the aircraft respond more smoothly with the new ventral fin installed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabilizer - a surface or structure on an aircraft or missile that helps maintain or control stability
  • Antonym: dorsal fin - a fin located on the upper side of a body (in aircraft terms, a fin mounted on the top of the fuselage); contrasted with a ventral fin
