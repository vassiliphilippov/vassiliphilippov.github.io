tea maker

1. NOUN

/ ti ˈmeɪkər / (AmE), / tiː ˈmeɪkə / (BrE)
a small, usually metal, container or spoon with a perforated cover used to hold tea leaves while brewing in hot water
perforated utensil for brewing loose tea

Examples

I use a tea maker to brew my favorite loose-leaf blends every morning.
Instead of tea bags, he prefers a tea maker for a fresher taste.
She bought a new tea maker shaped like a small, decorative spoon with holes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea infuser - a device for holding loose tea leaves while steeping

2. NOUN NEW

/ ti ˈmeɪkər / (AmE), / tiː ˈmeɪkə / (BrE)
an electric or programmable appliance that heats water and brews tea automatically, often with controls for strength and a keep-warm function.
electric appliance that brews tea

Examples

We gifted ourselves a modern tea maker that brews tea at the perfect strength.
The office installed a new tea maker so employees can have hot drinks during breaks.
Follow the manual to fill the water tank before operating the tea maker for the first time.
