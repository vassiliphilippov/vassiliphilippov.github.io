weapons platform

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛpənz ˌplætˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈwɛpənz ˌplætfɔːm / (BrE)
A structure or vehicle that carries or supports weapons, especially in a military context.
structure or vehicle carrying weapons

Examples

Military engineers designed the vehicle to function as a mobile weapons platform.
Missiles are often mounted on a weapons platform for increased mobility and effectiveness.
The new aircraft carrier serves as a powerful weapons platform in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch platform - structure supporting missile or rocket launch

/ \u02c8w\u025bp\u0259nz \u02ccpl\u00e6t\u02ccf\u0254rm / (AmE), / \u02c8w\u025bp\u0259nz \u02ccpl\u00e6tf\u0254\u02d0m / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) software or a networked infrastructure used to host, coordinate, or deploy cyber weapons, automated exploits, or large-scale attacks.
software or network for cyberattacks

Examples

Security researchers warned that the new botnet served as a weapons platform for automated cyberattacks.
The company sold access to its control servers, essentially renting a weapons platform to attackers.
Nation-states deploy sophisticated malware frameworks as a persistent weapons platform in espionage campaigns.
