nickel bronze

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl ˌbroʊnz / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ˌbrəʊnz / (BrE)
a type of bronze alloy containing up to 30% nickel, valued for its strength and corrosion resistance
bronze alloy with up to 30% nickel

Examples

Engineers selected nickel bronze for the ship's propeller because it resists corrosion.
Many industrial components are made from nickel bronze due to its durability.
The statue was cast in nickel bronze for added weather resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronze - a yellowish-brown alloy of copper with up to one-third tin
