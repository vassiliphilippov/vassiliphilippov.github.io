growth factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊθ ˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊθ ˌfæktə / (BrE)
a natural substance, usually a protein, that stimulates cell growth, proliferation, and differentiation
protein that stimulates cell growth
medical

Examples

Nerve cells require a specific growth factor to survive and mature.
Scientists isolated a growth factor that promotes tissue regeneration in the lab.
The absence of a vital growth factor can hinder normal development in embryos.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɡroʊθ ˌfæktər / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊθ ˌfæktə / (BrE)
a factor, condition, or variable that contributes to or causes growth in an economic, social, or technological system
cause of growth in a system

Examples

Improved digital infrastructure is a major growth factor for the regional economy.
Low interest rates acted as a key growth factor encouraging small businesses to expand rapidly.
Public investment in education proved the most important growth factor for long-term productivity gains.
