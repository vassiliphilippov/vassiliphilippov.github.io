political commissar

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪɾɪkəl ˈkɑmɪˌsɑr / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈkɒmɪˌsɑː / (BrE)
an official appointed by a Communist Party to oversee a military unit and ensure compliance with party principles and loyalty
Communist Party official supervising military unit

Examples

During the revolution, a political commissar was assigned to each regiment.
The political commissar ensured that all officers followed Communist ideology.
The army's political commissar attended every meeting to monitor loyalty to the party.

Etymology

From Russian politicheskiy komissar, combining 'political' and 'commissar.'

A non-military official in a civilian organisation who enforces loyalty to a particular political ideology or party line; often used metaphorically and pejoratively.
ideological overseer in civilian organisations
informal

Examples

The company promoted a political commissar to review editorial decisions and enforce the new ideological rules.
She joked that human resources had become a political commissar policing employees' social media posts.
Critics accused the university's dean of acting like a political commissar censoring dissenting speakers.
