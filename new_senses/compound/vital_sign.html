vital sign

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəl saɪn / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəl saɪn / (BrE)
an indicator of essential physical functions, such as heart rate, blood pressure, temperature, and breathing rate, used to assess a person's general health or physical condition
indicator of essential bodily functions
medical

Examples

A sudden drop in vital signs can indicate a medical emergency.
Medical staff closely monitor a patient's vital signs after surgery.
The nurse checked his vital signs every hour during the night.

Synset Examples

he was still alive but his vital signs were weak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vital - necessary or essential; relating to life

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvaɪtəl saɪn / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəl saɪn / (BrE)
(in non-medical contexts) a key metric used to indicate the health, viability, or performance of a system, organization, or process.
key metric of system health

Examples

Uptime is a vital sign of the software's overall performance and user experience.
Customer satisfaction scores are important vital signs for any subscription-based business.
Analysts monitor churn and revenue as vital signs of the company's market health.
