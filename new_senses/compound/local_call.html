local call

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl kɔl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl kɔːl / (BrE)
a telephone call made within a defined local area, usually charged at a lower rate than long-distance calls
telephone call within a local area

Examples

I made a local call to the restaurant to reserve a table.
In the past, payphones would only allow you to make a local call without a calling card.
There is no extra charge for a local call from this phone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long-distance call - a telephone call made to a location outside the local calling area

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8lo\u028ak\u0259l k\u0254l / (AmE), / \u02c8l\u0259\u028ak\u0259l k\u0254\u02d0l / (BrE)
(in computing) a function or procedure call that executes within the same process or address space, not across a network or between processes.
function call within same process

Examples

The compiler optimizes a local call by inlining it to eliminate function-call overhead.
When debugging, she stepped through each local call to verify the correct variable values were passed.
Microservices turned many local calls into remote requests across containers during migration.
