pop up

1. NOUN NEW

/ pɑp ʌp / (AmE), / pɒp ʌp / (BrE)
a temporary retail or event space that opens in a location for a short period, often to sell products, promote a brand, or test a market.
temporary shop or event space
informal

Examples

A trendy pop-up shop sold handmade ceramics and filled the empty storefront for two weeks.
The festival featured a food pop-up offering fusion tacos and long queues of hungry visitors.
They set up a charity pop-up in the mall to raise funds and awareness over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary shop - a shop that opens for a limited time, often to test products or markets
  • Synonym: stall - a small stand or booth selling goods, typically at a market or fair

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'pop up' (to appear suddenly); the hyphenated noun developed in the late 20th century for short-term shops and events.
