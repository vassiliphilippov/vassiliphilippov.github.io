wrist pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪst pɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪst pɪn / (BrE)
A pin that connects a piston to a connecting rod in an internal combustion engine or similar mechanism.
pin joining piston to connecting rod

Examples

A broken wrist pin can lead to severe engine damage if not replaced promptly.
During the rebuild, they inspected each wrist pin for signs of scoring or bending.
The mechanic discovered that the wrist pin had worn out, causing a knocking noise in the engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gudgeon pin - British term for wrist pin, pin joining piston to connecting rod

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈrɪst pɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪst pɪn / (BrE)
A metal pin inserted surgically to hold bones of the wrist (for example the distal radius or carpal bones) in position while they heal.
surgical pin for wrist fractures
medical

Examples

After the fall, the surgeon inserted a wrist pin to stabilize the broken distal radius.
The post‑op X‑ray showed the wrist pin was correctly placed and the bones were aligned.
They scheduled a minor procedure to remove the wrist pin once the fracture had healed.
