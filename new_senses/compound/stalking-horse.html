stalking-horse

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔkɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
A horse used by hunters as a screen to conceal themselves while approaching game.
horse used as cover by hunters

Examples

He described the technique of using a stalking-horse as both clever and resourceful.
In the past, hunters would approach wildfowl behind a stalking-horse to avoid detection.
The use of a stalking-horse allowed hunters to get close enough to their prey for a clean shot.

Etymology

From 'stalking' (pursuing stealthily) + 'horse'; historically used in hunting.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔkɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
A screen or figure, typically shaped like a horse, behind which a hunter hides while stalking game.
screen used by hunters to approach game

Examples

By hiding behind a stalking-horse, he managed to get closer to the flock without alarming them.
In the old days, a stalking-horse made from canvas was a common tool among waterfowl hunters.
The hunter crept forward slowly, using his stalking-horse to remain hidden from the deer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind - concealed shelter for hunting or observing wildlife
  • Synonym: camouflage - something used to disguise or conceal

Etymology

From the combination of 'stalking' (from the verb 'stalk') and 'horse'; originally referring to a device used to give cover to hunters.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɔkɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
a candidate or thing put forward to conceal someone's real intentions, especially one used to divide opposition or mask the true choice
decoy candidate or pretext for real intention

Examples

Rumors about a new CEO proved to be a stalking-horse to test public reaction.
She suspected he was just a stalking-horse for the company's true candidate.
The party used a stalking-horse to distract voters from their real leadership choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - something used to mislead or draw attention away from the real thing
  • Synonym: front - a person or thing used to mask someone else's activities or intentions

Etymology

Originally referred to a horse behind which a hunter concealed himself while stalking prey; figurative sense from the late 16th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˈstɔkɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
A pretext or false motive used to conceal someone's real intentions; something put forward to disguise a true purpose or plan.
pretext used to conceal real intentions

Examples

Her resignation letter was a stalking-horse to distract from deeper problems within the department.
The business merger was just a stalking-horse for the company's real interest in acquiring new technology.
The new proposal acted as a stalking-horse, allowing the negotiators to test reactions without revealing their true intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretext - a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason
  • Synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive someone; a trick
  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation put forward to defend or justify a fault or offense

Etymology

Originally referred to a horse or decoy used to conceal a hunter; later used figuratively for something that hides one's real aim.
