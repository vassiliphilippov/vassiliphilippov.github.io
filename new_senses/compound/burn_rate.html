burn rate

1. NOUN

/ bɝn reɪt / (AmE), / bɜːn reɪt / (BrE)
The rate at which an organization, especially a startup, spends its available funds, typically measured on a monthly basis.
rate at which funds are spent by a company

Examples

By cutting unnecessary expenses, they managed to lower their monthly burn rate.
Investors questioned the high burn rate during the board meeting.
The company's burn rate forced them to seek additional funding much sooner than expected.

Synset Examples

Reducing the burn rate is crucial for our survival.
They monitored their burn rate closely to extend their runway.
The startup's burn rate is alarming for investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spending rate - the rate at which money is spent
  • Degree Higher: runway - the amount of time before a company runs out of cash at its current burn rate

2. NOUN NEW

/ b\u025dn re\u026at / (AmE), / b\u025c\u02d0n re\u026at / (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and blockchain contexts) the rate at which tokens are intentionally destroyed or removed from circulation, typically measured over a set time period.
rate tokens are destroyed

Examples

The team published the monthly burn rate showing how many tokens were permanently removed.
Investors watch the token's burn rate to judge whether scarcity will increase market value.
After the hard fork, developers adjusted the protocol's burn rate to slow token deflation.

Synset Examples

The team published the monthly burn rate showing how many tokens were permanently removed.
Investors watch the token's burn rate to judge whether scarcity will increase market value.
After the hard fork, developers adjusted the protocol's burn rate to slow token deflation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: token burn - the act of permanently removing tokens from circulation by sending them to an unrecoverable address
  • Antonym: issuance rate - the rate at which new tokens are created and added to circulation (opposite effect on supply)
