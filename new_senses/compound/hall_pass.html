hall pass

1. NOUN

/ hɔl pæs / (AmE), / hɔːl pɑːs / (BrE)
a written permission given by a teacher, allowing a student to leave the classroom and be in the hallways of the school during class time
written permission to be out of class at school

Examples

She showed the principal her hall pass when he stopped her in the hallway.
The teacher gave me a hall pass so I could use the restroom during class.
Without a hall pass, students are not allowed to wander the corridors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - official permission to go somewhere

2. NOUN NEW

/ hɔl pæs / (AmE), / hɔːl pɑːs / (BrE)
(in informal speech) permission given by a partner to have sexual or romantic relations with someone outside the relationship.
permission to have sex outside a relationship
informal slang

Examples

They agreed that he could use a hall pass during the conference weekend.
After years together, she jokingly offered her husband a hall pass for one night.
Reality shows sometimes test couples by offering competing partners a hall pass on camera.
