Band-Aid

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænd eɪd / (AmE), / ˈbænd eɪd / (BrE)
A brand of small adhesive bandage used to cover and protect minor wounds such as cuts or blisters.
adhesive bandage brand for minor wounds

Examples

Do you have a Band-Aid for this blister on my heel?
He put a Band-Aid on his finger after accidentally cutting himself.
She always keeps some Band-Aids in her bag just in case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive bandage - strip of material with adhesive for covering small wounds

Etymology

From 'band(age)' and 'aid', trademarked by Johnson & Johnson in the 1920s.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbænd eɪd / (AmE), / ˈbænd eɪd / (BrE)
a quick, temporary, and often superficial solution or fix that does not address the underlying problem.
temporary superficial fix

Examples

The committee applied a Band-Aid to the budget problem instead of planning long-term reforms.
Her suggestion was dismissed as nothing more than a Band-Aid that would delay inevitable changes.
Installing another firewall felt like putting a Band-Aid on a much deeper security issue.
