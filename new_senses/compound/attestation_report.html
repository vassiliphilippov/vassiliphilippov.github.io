attestation report

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɛstəˈʃən rɪˈpɔrt / (AmE), / əˌtɛstəˈʃən rɪˈpɔːt / (BrE)
a formal statement issued by a certified public accountant expressing a conclusion about the reliability of information prepared by another party
accountant's formal conclusion on another's statement
legal

Examples

Our firm was hired to prepare an attestation report on the accuracy of the financial data.
The auditor's attestation report concluded that the internal controls were effective.
The company provided an attestation report from its CPA to satisfy regulatory requirements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audit report - formal report expressing an auditor's opinion on a company's financial statements

Etymology

From attest (to affirm as true), based on Latin 'attestari' (to bear witness), + report; used in accounting from the 20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u0259\u02cct\u025bst\u0259\u02c8\u0283\u0259n r\u026a\u02c8p\u0254rt / (AmE), / \u0259\u02cct\u025bst\u0259\u02c8\u0283\u0259n r\u026a\u02c8p\u0254\u02d0t / (BrE)
In computing and cybersecurity, a digital document (often cryptographically signed) produced by a device, secure hardware module, or system component that reports or proves the system's measured hardware, firmware, or software state to a remote verifier.
digital proof of system state

Examples

The cloud provider issued an attestation report confirming the VM's measured boot and patch level.
Before onboarding, customers request attestation reports to verify that servers run approved firmware.
The security team automated attestation report generation each time a device completed a secure boot.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote attestation - the process by which a device or platform proves its measured state to a remote verifier
  • Related: trusted platform module (TPM) - a secure hardware component that can measure and report system state and often generates attestation reports

Etymology

From attest (to affirm as true), from Latin attestāri 'to bear witness' + report; used metaphorically in computing to mean a machine-generated statement of system state.
