merry-go-round

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛri ɡoʊ raʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmɛri ɡəʊ raʊnd / (BrE)
A large rotating amusement ride with seats, often shaped like animals, that people, especially children, can ride for entertainment.
rotating amusement ride with seats

Examples

After waiting in line, we finally got to ride the merry-go-round at the festival.
She smiled as the merry-go-round spun, its lights twinkling in the evening.
The children ran excitedly toward the merry-go-round as soon as they entered the park.

Synset Examples

The kids laughed on the merry-go-round at the park.
We took a ride on the colorful merry-go-round.
The merry-go-round spun faster as the music played.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carousel - rotating amusement ride with horses or seats, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'merry' (cheerful) + 'go-round' (something that spins); originally from fairground terminology.
