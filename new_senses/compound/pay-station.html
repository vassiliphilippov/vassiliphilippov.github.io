pay-station

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
A public telephone operated by inserting coins to make a call.
coin-operated public telephone

Examples

Before the era of mobile phones, people relied on the nearby pay-station to make important calls.
He searched for loose change to use the pay-station at the train station.
The travelers found a pay-station in the lobby to call a taxi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payphone - A public telephone requiring payment to use

Etymology

From 'pay' + 'station', indicating a place where payment is required for service.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8pe\u026a \u02ccste\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / \u02c8pe\u026a \u02ccste\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
A self-service machine or kiosk that accepts payments (coins, cards, or contactless) for parking, transit fares, tickets, or similar services.
self-service payment kiosk

Examples

The driver paid at the pay-station in the lot to park for two hours.
City workers installed a new pay-station near the bus stop for contactless fare payments.
She forgot coins, so she used the smartphone app instead of the pay-station at the gate.
