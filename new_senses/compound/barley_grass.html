barley grass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrli ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɑːli ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a European annual grass, often found as a weed in waste ground, especially along roadsides and hedgerows
European annual grass, roadside weed

Examples

Barley grass quickly invades disturbed soils along rural roads.
She identified the weed in her vegetable patch as barley grass.
Some gardeners remove barley grass to prevent it from overtaking flowerbeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a cereal plant cultivated for its grain, used in food and drink
