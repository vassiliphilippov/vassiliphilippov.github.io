free-for-all

1. NOUN

/ ˈfri fɚ ˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈfriː fə ˌɔːl / (BrE)
A situation in which there is a noisy or chaotic fight, competition, or dispute involving many people, often without any rules or order.
noisy chaotic fight or competition

Examples

After the referee left, the soccer game became a chaotic free-for-all.
The argument quickly turned into a free-for-all, with people shouting and shoving each other.
The town hall meeting ended in a free-for-all as emotions ran high among attendees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy and rough fight involving several people
  • Synonym: melee - a confused fight involving many people
  • Synonym: riot - a violent disturbance by a crowd

Etymology

Originally referred to a fight or contest open to anyone, especially one without rules.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfri fəɹ ˈɔl / (AmE), / ˌfriː fə ˈɔːl / (BrE)
A situation in which there are no rules or controls and everyone can participate, often resulting in chaos or disorder.
chaotic situation with no rules

Examples

After the referee left the field, the match devolved into a complete free-for-all.
The online discussion descended into a free-for-all once the moderator left.
When the store opened its doors on Black Friday, the sale quickly became a free-for-all.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melee - a confused fight or scuffle
