chicken hawk

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkən ˌhɔk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkɪn ˌhɔːk / (BrE)
A nontechnical term for any of several hawks that are alleged to prey on poultry.
hawk believed to prey on chickens

Examples

Farmers often worry that a chicken hawk will attack their poultry.
Many people mistakenly call all large raptors chicken hawks even if they don't eat chickens.
The presence of a chicken hawk caused the hens to scatter in fright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey with broad rounded wings

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtʃɪkən ˈhɔk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkən ˈhɔːk / (BrE)
A (often pejorative) term for a person, especially a politician, who advocates military action or a hawkish foreign policy while having avoided military service.
advocate of war who avoided service
informal slang

Examples

Opponents accused the congressman of being a chicken hawk after he supported the military strike.
Critics called the policy adviser a chicken hawk for promoting war while never serving in uniform.
Some voters refuse to support chicken hawks who favour intervention but avoided conscription themselves.
