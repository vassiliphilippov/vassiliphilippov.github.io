cellulose triacetate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌloʊs traɪ.əˈsɛɪ.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊˌləʊs traɪ.əˈsiː.teɪt / (BrE)
A type of cellulose acetate that is slow to burn, used as a film base for photographic and motion-picture films instead of celluloid.
slow-burning cellulose acetate used in film

Examples

Compared to other plastics, cellulose triacetate offers greater resistance to burning.
Old motion-picture reels were often made from cellulose triacetate instead of the more flammable celluloid.
The museum has preserved several photographs printed on cellulose triacetate film bases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: celluloid - a flammable plastic formerly used as film base
  • Synonym: cellulose acetate - a non-flammable plastic derived from cellulose

Etymology

From cellulose, a polysaccharide, and triacetate, referring to the presence of three acetate groups per cellulose unit.

(in water treatment and filtration) a form of cellulose acetate polymer used to make semipermeable membranes for reverse osmosis and other filtration processes.
polymer used for filtration membranes

Examples

In high-purity water treatment, cellulose triacetate membranes are widely used for reverse osmosis desalination.
The research team tested a new cellulose triacetate film for improved chlorine resistance in desalination plants.
Engineers chose cellulose triacetate because its tolerance of oxidants extended membrane service life at the coastal facility.
