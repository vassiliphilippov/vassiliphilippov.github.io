check mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛk mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk mɑːk / (BrE)
a written mark (✓) used to indicate that something has been noted, approved, or completed
mark showing completion or approval

Examples

Please place a check mark in the box if you agree with the terms and conditions.
She put a check mark next to each item on the list as she finished it.
The teacher drew a check mark beside the students' correct answers.

Synset Examples

as he called the roll he put a check mark by each student's name

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a mark used to indicate acknowledgment or completion (chiefly British)

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8t\u0283\u025bk m\u0251rk / (AmE), / \u02c8t\u0283\u025bk m\u0251\u02d0k / (BrE)
(on social media and other online platforms) a small symbol, often blue, placed next to a user or organization name to show that the account has been verified as authentic or official.
social-media verification badge

Examples

Public figures often display a blue check mark to indicate verification.
Twitter added a policy change that removed many users' blue check mark badges last year.
She was thrilled when the platform gave her profile a check mark showing it was authentic.
