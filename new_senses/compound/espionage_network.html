espionage network

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛspiəˌnɑʒ ˈnɛtwɜrk / (AmE), / ˌɛspɪˈɒnɪʒ ˈnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
a secret organization of individuals who collect intelligence, especially for a government
network of spies

Examples

During the Cold War, many espionage networks were uncovered by counterintelligence agencies.
He was accused of being part of an international espionage network.
The government discovered an espionage network operating within its military ranks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy ring - a group of spies working together
  • Degree Lower: spy - a person who secretly collects information

Etymology

espionage from French, ultimately from espionner (to spy); network from net (web) + work.
