punch-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃ ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃ ˌʌp / (BrE)
a physical fight between people that involves punching, especially with fists
a physical fistfight
british informal

Examples

Tempers flared and they ended up having a punch-up in the street.
The football match was interrupted by a punch-up between rival fans.
There was a minor punch-up outside the pub last night.

Synset Examples

the quarrel ended in a punch-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fistfight - a fight with fists between two people
  • Degree Higher: brawl - a noisy and rough fight involving several people

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpʌntʃ ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃ ˌʌp / (BrE)
an editing session or revision in which a script, sketch, or performance is improved by adding or sharpening jokes, lines, or comedic beats.
adding or sharpening jokes in a script
informal

Examples

The script underwent a punch-up to sharpen its jokes for the final act.
After the table read, the writers scheduled a punch-up to add more one-liners and beats.
He was hired to lead a punch-up of the sitcom's pilot before it went into production.
