devil's claw

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlz klɔ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz klɔː / (BrE)
A plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, known for its large pale flowers mottled with purple and a long, curved seed pod resembling a claw.
plant with curved claw-like seed pod

Examples

After the rains, fields in Texas bloomed with rows of devil's claw flowers.
In some cultures, the devil's claw is used for ornamental purposes or in traditional crafts.
The unusual seed pod of the devil's claw caught the attention of botanists visiting the desert.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdɛvəlz klɔ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlz klɔː / (BrE)
A southern African plant (Harpagophytum procumbens) or a preparation from its root, used as an herbal remedy to relieve inflammation and joint pain.
African medicinal herb for inflammation
medical

Examples

Herbalists often recommend devil's claw root to relieve arthritis pain and stiffness.
The supplement label listed 500 mg of standardized devil's claw extract per capsule.
A recent trial found that topical devil's claw preparations reduced joint inflammation slightly in some patients.
