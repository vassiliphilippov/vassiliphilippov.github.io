turkey trot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːki trɑt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki trɒt / (BrE)
an early ragtime dance popular in the early 20th century, characterized by a jerky, trotting motion
early ragtime dance style

Examples

Historians noted that the turkey trot influenced later social dances.
In the ballroom, couples performed the turkey trot to lively ragtime music.
The turkey trot was considered quite scandalous when it first became popular in the 1910s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-step - a ballroom dance with one step per beat

Etymology

The name reflects the dance's supposed imitation of a turkey's gait; popular in the US around 1910.

Thanksgiving charity run

Examples

Every Thanksgiving morning our neighborhood hosts a turkey trot to raise money for food banks.
Thousands of runners signed up for the city's annual turkey trots, many choosing the five-kilometer route.
She trained all autumn so she could finish the local turkey trot without stopping.
