port watcher

1. NOUN

/ pɔrt ˈwɑtʃər / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to guard or monitor activity on a wharf or in a port area
person who watches over a port or wharf

Examples

As a port watcher, her main responsibility was to report any suspicious activity on the wharf.
Every large harbor employs at least one port watcher to maintain security.
The port watcher checked the shipments arriving at the dock throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchman - a guard who keeps watch, especially at night

2. NOUN NEW

/ pɔrt ˈwɑtʃər / (AmE), / pɔːt ˈwɒtʃə / (BrE)
A software program or hardware device that monitors network ports on a computer or network, reporting or logging open ports, connection attempts, and related activity for security or diagnostics.
tool that monitors network port activity

Examples

The administrator installed a port watcher to detect unauthorized port scans.
A simple port watcher alerted the team when the service unexpectedly opened a new port.
They used a cloud-based port watcher to continuously monitor thousands of virtual machines.
