megaton bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌtʌn bɑm / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡətʌn bɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon with an explosive power equivalent to one million tons of TNT.
nuclear weapon of one million tons TNT yield

Examples

During the Cold War, both sides raced to develop larger megaton bombs.
Scientists debated the environmental impact of detonating a megaton bomb above ground.
The megaton bomb was capable of wiping out an entire city in seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear bomb - a bomb whose destructive power comes from nuclear reactions
  • Degree Lower: kiloton bomb - a nuclear bomb with yield equivalent to one thousand tons of TNT

Etymology

From 'megaton', meaning one million tons, and 'bomb'. 'Megaton' derives from Greek 'mega' (large) and English 'ton'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8m\u025b\u0261\u0259\u02cct\u028cn b\u0251m / (AmE), / \u02c8m\u025b\u0261\u0259t\u028cn b\u0252m / (BrE)
A figurative expression for an event, announcement, or revelation that has an extremely large, shocking, or transformative effect.
very large, shocking event or revelation

Examples

The committee's report dropped a megaton bomb into the election campaign, forcing immediate resignations.
When the CEO admitted the fraud, it was a megaton bomb that shattered investor confidence worldwide.
Her testimony proved a megaton bomb in court, changing the trial's course overnight.

Etymology

Figurative extension of the literal phrase 'megaton bomb' (megaton + bomb), used to compare the impact of an event to the destructive power of a megaton explosive.
