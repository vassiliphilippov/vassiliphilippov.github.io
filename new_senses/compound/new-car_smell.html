new-car smell

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuː ˈkɑr smɛl / (AmE), / ˌnjuː ˈkɑː smɛl / (BrE)
a distinctive scent associated with newly manufactured or purchased automobiles, often perceived as pleasant
distinctive scent of a new car

Examples

After a few months, the new-car smell began to fade from the interior.
I love that new-car smell when I get into a brand new vehicle.
Many air fresheners try to imitate the new-car smell for older vehicles.

Synset Examples

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccnu\u02d0 \u02c8k\u0251r sm\u025bl / (AmE), / \u02ccnju\u02d0 \u02c8k\u0251\u02d0 sm\u025bl / (BrE)
a figurative sense meaning an impression or aura of freshness, novelty, or premium newness around a product, design, or presentation (not an actual scent).
figurative freshness or aura of novelty

Examples

Tech reviewers praised the new-car smell of the gadget's packaging, saying it felt premium and fresh.
The startup's launch had a new-car smell that excited investors despite the team's small size.
Despite being pre-owned, the boutique's display maintained a new-car smell of modern design and polish.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freshness - the quality of being new, clean, or recently produced
  • Synonym: novelty - the quality of being new, different, or unusual

Etymology

From the literal 'new-car smell' (the scent of a newly manufactured or purchased vehicle), used figuratively to describe an impression of freshness.
