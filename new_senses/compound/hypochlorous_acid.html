hypochlorous acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈklɔrəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈklɔːrəs ˈasid / (BrE)
a weak, unstable acid (HOCl) known only in solution or in the form of its salts; used mainly as a bleaching and oxidizing agent
weak, unstable acid and bleaching agent
medical

Examples

Hypochlorous acid is responsible for the bleaching effect of chlorine in laundry detergents.
Exposure to sunlight can rapidly break down hypochlorous acid in solution.
The disinfectant properties of hypochlorous acid are used in water treatment plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a greenish-yellow gas used as a disinfectant and bleaching agent

Etymology

From hypo- (meaning 'less than normal') + chlorous (referring to chlorine) + acid.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccha\u026apo\u028a\u02c8kl\u0254r\u0259s \u02c8\u00e6s\u026ad / (AmE), / \u02cch\u028c\u026ap\u0259\u02c8kl\u0254\u02d0r\u0259s \u02c8asid / (BrE)
(in biology and medicine) an antimicrobial oxidizing agent (HOCl) produced by neutrophils and other phagocytes during the immune response to kill or inactivate bacteria and other microbes.
antimicrobial oxidant produced by white blood cells
medical

Examples

During inflammation, hypochlorous acid is produced by neutrophils to destroy invading bacteria.
Researchers found that low concentrations of hypochlorous acid help neutrophils kill pathogens effectively.
Topical formulations containing hypochlorous acid are used to reduce infection and promote wound healing.
