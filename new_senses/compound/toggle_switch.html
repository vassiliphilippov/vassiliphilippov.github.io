toggle switch

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑɡəl swɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡəl swɪtʃ / (BrE)
a switch with a projecting lever that is moved back and forth to open or close an electrical circuit, typically with two fixed positions
switch operated by flipping a lever

Examples

Please flip the toggle switch to turn on the lights.
She replaced the toggle switch with a push-button alternative for safety.
The old radio has a metal toggle switch for power control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocker switch - a switch that rocks back and forth instead of leaning to one side

Etymology

From 'toggle' (a pin or bar that pivots) and 'switch'. 'Toggle' from earlier English meaning a pin or peg, origin unknown.
