architectural style

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkəˈtɛktʃərəl staɪl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃərəl staɪl / (BrE)
a distinctive design or method of construction that characterizes a particular period, place, or movement in architecture
distinctive form in architecture

Examples

Her research focuses on how function and tradition shape architectural style in urban environments.
Many modern buildings are influenced by the Bauhaus architectural style.
The Gothic architectural style is characterized by pointed arches and ribbed vaults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - a particular procedure by which something is done, a manner or mode

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌɑːrkəˈtɛktʃərəl staɪl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃərəl staɪl / (BrE)
(in computing) a set of constraints, conventions, and organization principles that determine how components of a software system interact and are structured.
software design constraints and conventions

Examples

The REST architectural style defines constraints for building scalable web services.
Our team decided to adopt an architectural style that emphasizes independent services and asynchronous communication.
When evaluating each architectural style, consider trade-offs for scalability, maintainability, and performance under load.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from physical architecture (design of buildings) to software design; term established in software engineering from the late 20th century.
