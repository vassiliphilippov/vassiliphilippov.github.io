safety blitz

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfti blɪts / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti blɪts / (BrE)
(in American football) a defensive play in which a safety attempts to rush the quarterback by breaking through the offensive line.
defensive play by a safety in football
american

Examples

During the third quarter, the defense executed a successful safety blitz that resulted in a sack.
Fans cheered when the unexpected safety blitz disrupted the offense's play.
The coach called a safety blitz to increase pressure on the quarterback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blitz - a defensive rush against the quarterback in football

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈseɪfti blɪts / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti blɪts / (BrE)
a short, intensive campaign or enforcement action aimed at improving safety or reducing risks (for example, by checks, inspections, or public education).
short intensive safety campaign

Examples

The city launched a safety blitz to reduce pedestrian injuries across busy intersections this summer.
Police organized a nighttime safety blitz to check seatbelts and enforce speed limits on the highway.
The hospital ran a hand-washing safety blitz to lower infection rates among patients and staff.
