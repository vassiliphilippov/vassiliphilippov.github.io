Silk Road

1. PROPN

/ sɪlk roʊd / (AmE), / sɪlk rəʊd / (BrE)
an ancient network of trade routes connecting China and the Mediterranean, used extensively from the 2nd century BCE to the 14th century CE
ancient trade routes between China and the Mediterranean

Examples

Marco Polo famously journeyed along the Silk Road to reach China in the 13th century.
Merchants from many cultures traveled the Silk Road to exchange goods and ideas.
The Silk Road helped spread not only merchandise but also religions and technologies across continents.

Etymology

Named for the lucrative trade in silk carried out along its length, from English 'silk' and 'road'.

Examples

China promotes the Silk Road as a network of ports, railways, and trade corridors across continents.

Related Terms

Etymology

Named by analogy with the historic Silk Road; applied in the early 21st century to describe China’s Belt and Road Initiative and related projects.
