health code

1. NOUN

/ hɛlθ koʊd / (AmE), / hɛlθ kəʊd / (BrE)
a set of official rules or standards, established and enforced by government, relating to public health such as sanitation, food safety, or plumbing
official public health regulations

Examples

Building inspectors review new constructions to ensure compliance with the local health code.
Changes to the city's health code have improved food safety standards in recent years.
The restaurant was shut down for violating several sections of the health code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanitary code - set of official rules about hygiene and sanitation

2. NOUN NEW

/ hɛlθ koʊd / (AmE), / hɛlθ kəʊd / (BrE)
a digital indicator (for example, a QR code or app badge) that shows a person's health status — such as vaccination, recent test results, or exposure clearance — used to permit entry, travel, or services.
digital health-status QR/badge

Examples

Many venues now require a health code scanned at the door before allowing patrons inside.
She opened the app and showed her health code, proving a recent negative test to the guard.
During the outbreak, international travelers needed an approved digital health code to board connecting flights.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccine passport - an official document or digital record showing vaccination status, used to permit travel or access
  • Synonym: health pass - a digital or physical credential indicating a person's health clearance for entry or travel

Etymology

Coined in the early 21st century by combining 'health' + 'code'; popularized during public-health responses to refer to digital QR/app status tokens.
