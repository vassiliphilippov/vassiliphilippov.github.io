face time

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪs taɪm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs taɪm / (BrE)
Time spent being physically present with others, especially in a work or professional setting.
time present with others at work

Examples

Remote work reduces the amount of face time employees have with colleagues.
She felt she needed more face time at the office to be considered for promotion.
The manager values face time with her team to promote collaboration.

Synset Examples

instead of working on the problem at home he devoted face time to it at the office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: screen time - time spent looking at a screen (computer, TV, etc.)
