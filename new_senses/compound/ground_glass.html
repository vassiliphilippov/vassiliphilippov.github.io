ground glass

1. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
glass that has been ground into fine particles, often used as an abrasive in cleaning or industrial processes
finely ground glass used as abrasive

Examples

After the accident, fragments of ground glass were scattered across the workshop floor.
Some artists incorporate ground glass into their paint to add texture to their works.
The old chemist used ground glass as a cleaning abrasive for laboratory equipment.

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊnd ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
Glass with a roughened surface that diffuses light, typically produced by abrasion or etching.
glass with diffused-surface finish

Examples

Photographers sometimes use a ground glass screen to help focus large-format cameras.
The lamp was fitted with ground glass to create a soft, diffused light in the room.
The scientist examined the solution through a piece of ground glass to reduce glare.

Etymology

Formed from 'ground' (past participle of grind) and 'glass,' referring to glass that has been ground to roughen its surface.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɡraʊnd ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
In radiology, a hazy area seen on lung imaging (especially CT) that increases density but still allows underlying blood vessels or airways to be seen; often called a ground-glass opacity.
hazy lung opacity on imaging
medical

Examples

The chest CT showed diffuse ground glass opacities consistent with viral pneumonia.
Radiologists reported a small area of ground glass in the patient's right lower lung.
Early COVID-19 often produces bilateral ground glass patterns visible on high-resolution CT scans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground-glass opacity - the medical term for a hazy lung opacity on CT or X-ray (often abbreviated GGO).
  • Synonym: opacity (radiology) - an area on an imaging scan that appears denser than normal lung tissue.
  • Antonym: consolidation - a denser area of lung on imaging that usually obscures vessels and airways; contrasts with ground-glass opacity.
