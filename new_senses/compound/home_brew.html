home brew

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm bruː / (AmE), / həʊm bruː / (BrE)
An alcoholic drink, especially beer, made at home rather than bought from a shop or bar.
beer or other alcohol made at home

Examples

He offered me a bottle of his home brew at the party.
Her father’s home brew was surprisingly good for a homemade beer.
Many people started making home brew during the lockdown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homebrewed - prepared or brewed at home

2. NOUN NEW

/ hoʊm bruː / (AmE), / həm bruː / (BrE)
Unofficial software, games, or applications created by hobbyists to run on commercial consoles, devices, or platforms.
hobbyist-made unofficial software/games
informal

Examples

He loaded a disc of home brew games onto the retro console.
The community forum hosts tutorials to help newcomers build and share their own home brew software.
At the convention, indie developers sold cartridges with experimental home brew titles to curious collectors.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homebrew - alternative one-word spelling commonly used in computing and gaming communities
  • Synonym: unofficial software - software not officially released or supported by the device or platform maker

Etymology

From 'home brew' (beer made at home); extended metaphorically in the late 20th century to mean hobbyist-created software.
