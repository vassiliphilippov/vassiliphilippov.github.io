pay as you earn

1. NOUN

/ peɪ əz ju jɝn / (AmE), / peɪ əz juː jɜːn / (BrE)
the British system of collecting income tax directly from employees' salaries by deduction before the wages are paid
British income tax withholding system
british

Examples

He checked his pay as you earn deductions on his monthly payslip.
In the UK, most employees have their taxes deducted through pay as you earn.
The pay as you earn system simplifies the tax process for both employers and workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withholding tax - tax deducted at source from income

2. NOUN NEW

/ peɪ əz ju jən / (AmE), / peɪ əz juː jɜːn / (BrE)
A U.S. federal student‑loan repayment plan that limits borrowers' monthly payments to a percentage of their discretionary income and may forgive any remaining balance after a specified period.
U.S. income‑based student loan plan
american

Examples

Many graduates enrolled in the pay as you earn plan to cap loan payments based on income.
After losing her job, she switched to pay as you earn to lower her monthly student payments.
The counselor explained how pay as you earn can lead to forgiveness of remaining balance after years.
