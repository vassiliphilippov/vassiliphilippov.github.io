fake news

1. NOUN

/ feɪk nuːz / (AmE), / feɪk njuːz / (BrE)
false or misleading information presented as news, often with the intention of deceiving people
false or misleading news reports

Examples

During the election, several websites spread fake news to influence public opinion.
He accused the television channel of broadcasting fake news about the event.
Many people were misled after reading the fake news article online.

Synset Examples

He joked that MSDNC was just fake news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinformation - false information deliberately spread to deceive people
  • Antonym: news - newly received or noteworthy information, especially about recent events

2. NOUN NEW

/ fe6ak nud0z / (AmE), / fe6ak njud0z / (BrE)
a dismissive or rhetorical label used to attack or delegitimize a news item, report, or source—often for political reasons—regardless of whether the information is true.
dismissive label attacking a report

Examples

The candidate called the opposition's report fake news, refusing to engage with its evidence.
After the study was criticised on social media, pundits labeled it as fake news without checking facts.
She accused the newspaper of publishing fake news to undermine her campaign, though the facts stood.
