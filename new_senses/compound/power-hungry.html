power-hungry

1. ADJ

/ ˈpaʊərˌhʌŋɡri / (AmE), / ˈpaʊəˌhʌŋɡri / (BrE)
having a strong and excessive desire for power; driven by the wish to control or dominate others
desiring power excessively

Examples

Her power-hungry attitude made it difficult for others to trust her intentions.
Many citizens opposed the power-hungry regime, fearing it would limit their freedom.
The power-hungry CEO was willing to do anything to climb to the top of the company.

Synset Examples

His power-hungry nature led to his downfall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ambitious - having or showing a strong desire and determination to succeed
  • Degree Higher: tyrannical - exercising power in a cruel or arbitrary way
  • Degree Higher: megalomaniacal - having a delusional sense of power or importance

2. ADJ NEW

/ \u02c8pa\u028a\u0259r\u02cch\u028c\u014b\u0261ri / (AmE), / \u02c8pa\u028a\u0259\u02cch\u028c\u014b\u0261ri / (BrE)
(of a device, system, or software) using or requiring a large amount of electrical power or energy.
uses a lot of electrical power

Examples

The gaming laptop is power-hungry, draining the battery after only an hour of play.
Updating multiple background apps made her phone feel slow and power-hungry by midday.
Large AI models can be power-hungry, requiring specialized cooling and significant electricity to run.

Synset Examples

The gaming laptop is power-hungry, draining the battery after only an hour of play.
Updating multiple background apps made her phone feel slow and power-hungry by midday.
Large AI models can be power-hungry, requiring specialized cooling and significant electricity to run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy-hungry - needing or using a lot of energy
  • Synonym: power-intensive - requiring a large amount of electrical power to operate
  • Antonym: energy-efficient - using little energy for the service provided; opposite of using a lot of power

Etymology

From the compound of power + hungry; originally metaphorical (hungry for power) and later extended to devices that 'consume' a lot of energy.
