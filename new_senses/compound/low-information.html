low-information

1. ADJ

/ loʊ ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ləʊ ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
providing little or no valuable or substantial information; lacking in useful content
lacking substantive or valuable information

Examples

Many found the guide to be low-information and not very helpful in making a decision.
The report was criticized for being too low-information and lacking important details.
Voters who rely on low-information news sources may be less informed about key issues.

Synset Examples

His presentation was interesting but ultimately low-information.
The article was criticized for being low-information and lacking depth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: informative - providing useful or interesting information

Used especially in political contexts to describe a person or group perceived as having little factual knowledge about an issue and as relying on superficial cues (slogans, personalities) rather than substantive evidence.
describing people lacking factual political knowledge
informal

Examples

Critics accused low-information voters of being swayed by slogans rather than careful consideration.
During the debate, the campaign targeted low-information districts with simple messages and ads.
Journalists warned that relying on polls of a low-information public can mislead political predictions.
