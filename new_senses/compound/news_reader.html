news reader

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuz ˌriːdər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːz ˌriːdə / (BrE)
a person who reads out news bulletins on radio or television broadcasts
person delivering news on TV or radio

Examples

Many viewers trust their favorite news reader more than the politicians being interviewed.
She trained as a news reader before becoming the main anchor for the evening program.
The news reader maintained a calm demeanor even while reporting on a crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news anchor - main presenter of a news program

A software program, app, or device that automatically fetches or aggregates news from multiple sources and displays or reads it aloud to users.
software that fetches and reads news

Examples

The car's dashboard news reader streams headlines aloud as the driver commutes.
She installed a news reader app to listen to morning headlines while making coffee.
Elderly users appreciated that the news reader could read long articles clearly and slowly.
