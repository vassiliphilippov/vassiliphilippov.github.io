high-low

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ ˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌləʊ / (BrE)
A form of poker in which the pot is split between the highest and lowest hand.
poker game where high and low hands split pot

Examples

He won the pot in high-low with both the highest and lowest hands.
In high-low poker, strategy changes since players aim for both extremes.
We're playing high-low tonight, so remember the low hand is just as important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stud poker - variant of poker where some cards are dealt face-up

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈhaɪ ˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌləʊ / (BrE)
Having a hemline that is shorter at the front and longer at the back, especially of a dress, skirt, or top.
uneven hem (short front, long back)

Examples

The designer showcased a flowing high-low dress that revealed her sandals as she walked.
She bought a black high-low skirt perfect for concerts and summer evenings.
Alterations included raising the back hem slightly on the high-low gown for better movement.

Etymology
