head-to-head

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛd tə ˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˌhɛd tə ˈhɛd / (BrE)
(of a meeting, discussion, etc.) involving only two people, especially in an intimate or private setting.
between two people; private

Examples

The two leaders engaged in a head-to-head debate behind closed doors.
Their head-to-head discussion helped resolve the misunderstanding quickly.
We had a head-to-head meeting to talk about our concerns honestly.

Synset Examples

a tete-a-tete supper
a head-to-head conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tête-à-tête - private conversation between two people
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01866830-s

2. ADV

/ ˌhɛd tu ˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˌhɛd tə ˈhɛd / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which two people or teams are competing against each other directly, especially in a very close contest
in direct, close competition

Examples

She enjoyed going head-to-head with her rival in debates.
The election results were so close that the candidates were head-to-head until the very end.
The two teams are going head-to-head for the championship title this year.

Synset Examples

the horses ran neck and neck
he won nip and tuck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neck and neck - very close in a competition; level with each other
  • Synonym: toe-to-toe - in direct confrontation or competition

Etymology

From the image of two opponents facing each other directly, as if their heads were nearly touching in opposition.
