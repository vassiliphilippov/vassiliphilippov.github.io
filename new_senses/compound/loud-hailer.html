loud-hailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊdˌheɪlər / (AmE), / ˈlaʊdˌheɪlə / (BrE)
A portable device consisting of a loudspeaker with a built-in microphone and amplifier, used to amplify a person's voice over a distance.
portable voice-amplifying device

Examples

A coach shouted instructions across the field using a loud-hailer.
The police officer used a loud-hailer to address the crowd during the parade.
They brought a loud-hailer to the protest to ensure everyone could hear the speeches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megaphone - a cone-shaped device used to amplify the voice

Etymology

From English 'loud' + 'hailer', referring to making one's voice carry loudly.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8la\u028ad\u02cche\u026al\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8la\u028ad\u02cche\u026al\u0259 / (BrE)
A person who loudly promotes, repeats, or broadcasts the views or messages of a group or individual; a public mouthpiece.
person who loudly promotes messages

Examples

The party's chief loud-hailer repeated the slogan at every rally for weeks.
As a hired loud-hailer, she amplified corporate talking points on morning radio shows.
Public debate suffered because a few loud-hailers drowned out more measured voices.

  • Antonym: independent voice - a person who expresses opinions independently rather than promoting another's message

Etymology

From English 'loud' + 'hailer'; a metaphorical extension from the device meaning to a person who 'hails' or proclaims messages loudly.
