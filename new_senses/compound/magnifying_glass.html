magnifying glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡ.nɪˌfaɪ.ɪŋ ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.nɪˌfaɪ.ɪŋ ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a simple optical instrument with a single convex lens used to make objects appear larger when viewed through it
simple lens for magnifying objects

Examples

A child might use a magnifying glass to look closely at insects in the garden.
She used a magnifying glass to read the small print on the contract.
The detective examined the crime scene with his magnifying glass.

Synset Examples

the magnifying glass was invented by Roger Bacon in 1250

Etymology

From 'magnify' (to make larger; from Latin 'magnificare') + 'glass' (referring to the lens material).
