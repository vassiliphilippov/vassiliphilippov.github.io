sleeper cell

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpər sɛl / (AmE), / ˈsliːpə sɛl / (BrE)
a secret group of agents who remain inactive within a community until instructed to carry out undercover operations, often for espionage or terrorism
secret group of inactive agents

Examples

Authorities uncovered a sleeper cell that had been operating undetected for years.
Intelligence agencies are constantly on the lookout for potential sleeper cells within the country.
The plot was orchestrated by a sleeper cell activated after years of inactivity.

Synset Examples

an al-Qaeda sleeper cell may have used Arizona as its base

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small group of people acting as a unit, especially in espionage or terrorism

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8slid0p59r s5bl / (AmE), / c8slid0p59 s5bl / (BrE)
(computing, cybersecurity) a dormant piece of malicious software, bot, or infected node within a network that remains inactive until triggered to perform harmful actions (for example, to connect with a command server, exfiltrate data, or launch attacks).
dormant malicious program or infected node

Examples

Security researchers discovered a sleeper cell of dormant malware hidden in the corporate network.
During the audit, the team removed several sleeper cell instances that activated when the user installed an update.
An advanced persistent threat sometimes plants a sleeper cell to wait for remote activation and exfiltrate data.

Synset Examples

Security researchers discovered a sleeper cell of dormant malware hidden in the corporate network.
During the audit, the team removed several sleeper cell instances that activated when the user installed an update.
An advanced persistent threat sometimes plants a sleeper cell to wait for remote activation and exfiltrate data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormant malware - malicious software present on a system but not currently performing malicious actions

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the political/military term 'sleeper cell' (a dormant group of agents), applied in cybersecurity to describe inactive malicious code or infected hosts.
