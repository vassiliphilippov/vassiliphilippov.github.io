sour cherry

1. NOUN

/ saʊɚ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / saʊə ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a small Eurasian tree that produces red to black acidic edible fruit, or the fruit itself
tree or fruit with tart cherries

Examples

A sour cherry tree can add beauty and utility to a home garden.
Many people prefer the tangy flavor of sour cherries in jams and preserves.
The pie was made with fresh sour cherries picked from the orchard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet cherry - cherry species or fruit that is sweet, not tart

3. NOUN

/ saʊɚ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / saʊə ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A variety of cherry with a tart flavor, typically used in cooking, baking, and preserves rather than eaten raw.
a tart variety of cherry used in cooking

Examples

A classic clafoutis often calls for sour cherry rather than sweet varieties.
The pie was filled with sour cherry and had a delicious, tangy flavor.
They harvested sour cherries from the orchard to make homemade jam.
