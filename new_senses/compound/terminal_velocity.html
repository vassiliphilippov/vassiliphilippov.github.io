terminal velocity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɚ.mə.nəl vəˈlɑ.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪ.nəl vəˈlɒ.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
the constant maximum speed reached by a falling object when the force of gravity is balanced by the resistance of the medium it moves through, such as air
maximum constant speed of a falling object

Examples

Raindrops typically fall at terminal velocity, dictated by their size and air resistance.
Scientists calculate the terminal velocity of different objects to predict how fast they will fall.
The skydiver reached terminal velocity before opening her parachute.

Etymology

From Latin 'terminalis' (related to an end or boundary) and 'velocity' from Latin 'velocitas' (swiftness).
