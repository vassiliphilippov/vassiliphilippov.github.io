forward pass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwɚd pæs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd pɑːs / (BrE)
a throw or pass of the ball towards the opponent's end of the field, typically from behind the line of scrimmage, often to gain distance downfield
a pass thrown downfield in sports

Examples

It is illegal to throw more than one forward pass on a single play in American football.
The quarterback threw a forward pass that was caught for a touchdown.
The team's strategy relies heavily on the forward pass to move the ball quickly down the field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral pass - a pass thrown parallel to or away from the opponent's goal line

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfɔrwərd pæs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd pɑːs / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a computation in which input data are propagated through a model’s layers to produce outputs or predictions.
ML computation propagating input through a model

Examples

During evaluation the forward pass computes output probabilities from the model's input.
The training loop performs a forward pass before computing the loss and gradients.
On complex networks, a single forward pass can take several seconds to complete.

  • Antonym: backward pass - the computation, during training, that propagates gradients backward through a model to update parameters; the complementary step to a forward pass.
