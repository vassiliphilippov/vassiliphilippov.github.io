offer price

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfər ˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒfə ˌpraɪs / (BrE)
The price at which a seller, especially a broker, is willing to sell a particular security or asset.
the price at which something is offered for sale

Examples

Investors noted that the offer price was higher than the previous day's closing price.
The offer price for the company's shares was set at $15 per share.
You should compare the offer price and the bid price before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bid price - the price at which a buyer is willing to purchase a security

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0254f\u0259r \\u02ccpra\u026as / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0252f\u0259 \\u02ccpra\u026as / (BrE)
The price proposed by a buyer when negotiating to purchase something; the buyer's bid in a sale or auction.
buyer's proposed price

Examples

They submitted an offer price of $312,000 for the house.
After negotiations, she raised her offer price to stay competitive with other buyers.
The seller rejected the initial offer price and asked for a counteroffer with repairs.
