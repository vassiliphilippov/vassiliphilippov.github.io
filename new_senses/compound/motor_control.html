motor control

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtɚ kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the process by which the nervous system coordinates muscles to produce movement
coordination of muscles and movement
medical

Examples

Early development of motor control is critical for children learning to walk.
Fine motor control is necessary to play a musical instrument accurately.
Physical therapy can help restore motor control after a stroke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordination - the ability to use different parts of the body smoothly and efficiently

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmoʊtər kəˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə kəˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
(in engineering and robotics) the subsystem of hardware and software that controls motors and actuators, using sensors and control algorithms to produce and regulate motion.
robotics subsystem that controls motion

Examples

Engineers tested the new motor control algorithm on multiple wheeled robots in the lab.
A precise motor control module keeps the robotic arm steady during delicate assembly tasks.
Modern motor control systems combine sensors, microcontrollers, and software to regulate motion accurately.
