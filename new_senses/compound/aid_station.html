aid station

1. NOUN

/ eɪd ˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / eɪd ˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a place near a battle or emergency area where first aid is given to the wounded
a first aid site near combat or emergencies

Examples

During the marathon, volunteers set up an aid station halfway through the course.
Medics established an aid station close to the front lines to treat casualties quickly.
The wounded soldiers were rushed to the aid station for immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first aid post - a place where first aid is administered

2. NOUN NEW

/ eɪd ˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / eɪd ˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a place in disaster relief or humanitarian operations where essential supplies (for example food, water, blankets) and basic services are distributed to people affected by an emergency.
disaster relief distribution point

Examples

After the flood, survivors lined up at the aid station for meals and blankets.
Volunteers set up an emergency aid station near the highway to give water and hygiene kits.
The NGO ran a mobile aid station that offered shelter information and basic medical checks.
