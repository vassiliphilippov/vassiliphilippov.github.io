golden ticket

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ˈtɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldə̃ ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
an opportunity or advantage that guarantees success or provides access to something special
guaranteed opportunity or special access

Examples

Many see a job at a major tech firm as a golden ticket to career success.
She believed the music scholarship was her golden ticket out of poverty.
Winning the science competition was his golden ticket to a top university.

Synset Examples

The scholarship was her golden ticket to college.
He thought his promotion was a golden ticket to success.
Winning the lottery felt like getting a golden ticket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open sesame - a means of immediate access or success

Etymology

Popularized by Roald Dahl's 1964 novel "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory," where golden tickets granted entrance to a magical factory.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0261o\u028ald\u0259n \u02c8t\u026ak\u026at / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0261\u0259\u028ild\u0259\u0303 \u02c8t\u026ak\u026at / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) a forged Kerberos Ticket Granting Ticket (TGT) created by an attacker that allows impersonation of arbitrary users and widespread access to a Windows domain or network resources.
forged Kerberos TGT used in attacks

Examples

The security team discovered a stolen golden ticket that allowed attackers full control of the domain.
After the breach they found several golden tickets created from a compromised domain controller image.
To prevent golden ticket attacks, admins regularly reset the KRBTGT account password and monitor tickets.

Synset Examples

The security team discovered a stolen golden ticket that allowed attackers full control of the domain.
After the breach they found several golden tickets created from a compromised domain controller image.
To prevent golden ticket attacks, admins regularly reset the KRBTGT account password and monitor tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forged Ticket Granting Ticket (TGT) - a fake Kerberos TGT created by an attacker to request service tickets and impersonate users

Etymology

Named metaphorically after Roald Dahl's golden tickets (1964), because the forged ticket grants broad, privileged access.
