double bar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl bɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl bɑː / (BrE)
A pair of adjacent vertical lines in musical notation used to mark the end of a principal section or the entire musical piece.
music: two adjacent bar lines marking section end

Examples

He drew a double bar to indicate the conclusion of the piece.
Please mark the section break with a double bar in your score.
The song ended with a double bar at the bottom of the last page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar line - a single vertical line in musical notation separating measures

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdʌbəl bɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl bɑː / (BrE)
(in programming) the symbol || used as the logical OR operator in many programming languages; it returns true if at least one operand is true.
programming: logical OR operator (||)

Examples

In most languages, the double bar returns true when either left or right expression is true.
She replaced the nested if-statements with a single double bar condition to simplify the code.
During debugging he noticed the double bar was short-circuiting, so the second function never ran.
