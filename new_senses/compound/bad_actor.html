bad actor

1. NOUN

/ bæd ˈæktɚ / (AmE), / bad ˈaktə / (BrE)
a person who behaves dishonestly, disruptively, or performs their role poorly
dishonest, disruptive, or poor performer

Examples

A single bad actor can damage the reputation of an entire group.
Authorities are working hard to prevent any bad actors from exploiting the new system.
The company took swift action to remove the bad actor from their team.

Synset Examples

A bad actor can ruin the best of scripts.
They exposed the bad actor in the organization.
The bad actor spoiled the movie for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who intentionally causes problems
  • Synonym: villain - a person who is deliberately harmful or wicked

2. NOUN NEW

/ bæd ˈæktɚ / (AmE), / bæd ˈæktə / (BrE)
(in computing and cybersecurity) a malicious non-human agent — such as malware, a bot, or an automated process — that operates within a system or network to violate rules, steal data, or cause harm.
malicious automated agent (cybersecurity)

Examples

The security team identified a bad actor that was scanning for open ports across the network.
After the breach, they discovered an automated bad actor exfiltrating customer records every night.
Lawyers argued whether the company could sue a foreign bad actor responsible for the ransomware attack.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threat actor - an individual, group, or entity (human or automated) that conducts malicious activity against systems or networks
  • Synonym: attacker - someone or something that tries to gain unauthorized access to systems or data
