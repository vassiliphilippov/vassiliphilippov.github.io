security intelligence

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjʊrəti ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊərəti ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Information relating to the identity, capabilities, and intentions of hostile individuals or organizations, often collected to prevent espionage, sabotage, subversion, or terrorism.
information on hostile threats or activities

Examples

Effective security intelligence is critical for national defense and public safety.
He works in security intelligence, analyzing data on potential terrorist threats.
The agency's security intelligence uncovered a plot against the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence - the gathering of information, especially of military or strategic value

(in cybersecurity) information collected, analysed, and organised about digital threats, attacker activity, software vulnerabilities, and indicators of compromise used to detect, prevent, or investigate cyber attacks.
information on cyber threats

Examples

The company's security intelligence team monitors network logs and indicators to prevent data breaches.

Small businesses often subscribe to threat feeds and security intelligence services to stay ahead of ransomware groups.
