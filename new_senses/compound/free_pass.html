free pass

1. NOUN

/ fri pæs / (AmE), / friː pɑːs / (BrE)
A ticket or permission allowing someone to enter a place or use a service without paying.
complimentary admission ticket or permission

Examples

After winning the contest, he received a free pass to the amusement park.
Her friend surprised her with a free pass to the theater's opening night.
The museum offers a free pass to all local schoolchildren.

Synset Examples

By giving him a free pass, you did him no favors.
She received a free pass to the concert from a friend.
With his free pass, he could access all the rides.
They offered a free pass to the museum for first-time visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complimentary ticket - A ticket given for free to provide admission

2. NOUN NEW

/ fri pæs / (AmE), / friː pɑːs / (BrE)
A figurative exemption from rules, criticism, or consequences that someone receives because of their status, influence, or past actions.
exemption from consequences
informal

Examples

Because of his celebrity, he seemed to have a free pass when scandals emerged last year.
The manager gave the intern a free pass after the mistake, citing her strong performance.
Critics argued the policy gave companies a free pass to ignore environmental regulations.

Synset Examples

Because of his celebrity, he seemed to have a free pass when scandals emerged last year.
The manager gave the intern a free pass after the mistake, citing her strong performance.
Critics argued the policy gave companies a free pass to ignore environmental regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemption - freedom from an obligation, rule, or requirement
  • Antonym: accountability - being responsible for actions and subject to consequences

Etymology

From the literal phrase 'free pass' (a complimentary ticket); extended figuratively to mean exemption from rules or consequences.
