Confederate flag

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdɚət flæɡ / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət flaɡ / (BrE)
the flag used to represent the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War, most commonly referring to the battle flag design
flag of the Confederate States of America

Examples

He wore a shirt with the Confederate flag printed on it, sparking a heated debate.
Many people view the Confederate flag as a symbol of Southern heritage, while others see it as representing racism and division.
The museum displayed an original Confederate flag from the Civil War era.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union flag - the flag representing the Union side during the American Civil War

2. NOUN NEW

/ kənˈfɛdərət flæɡ / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət flaɡ / (BrE)
(in modern public and political contexts) the Confederate flag used as a symbol of white supremacy, racial oppression, or intimidation; a hate symbol.
symbol of white supremacy / hate
american

Examples

After the protest, the city council voted to ban the Confederate flag from public buildings.
For many victims, seeing the Confederate flag is a painful reminder of racial violence.
Several stores stopped selling shirts with the Confederate flag after public backlash.
