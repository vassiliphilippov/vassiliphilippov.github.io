cloze procedure

1. NOUN

/ kloʊz prəˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / kləʊz prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a method for assessing reading ability in which words are deleted from a passage and the reader must fill in the blanks
reading test with missing words

Examples

Researchers often employ the cloze procedure in literacy assessments.
The teacher used a cloze procedure to measure the students' understanding of the passage.
Using a cloze procedure can help identify gaps in a learner's vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fill-in-the-blank - a test or question format where missing words must be supplied

Etymology

From 'cloze', coined in the 1950s, from 'closure', in reference to Gestalt psychology's closure principle.
