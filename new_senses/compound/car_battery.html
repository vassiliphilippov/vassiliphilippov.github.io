car battery

1. NOUN

/ kɑr ˈbætəri / (AmE), / kɑː ˈbætəri / (BrE)
A rechargeable battery, usually of the lead-acid type, used to start and power the electrical systems of a motor vehicle.
battery in a motor vehicle

Examples

It's important to check your car battery before going on a long road trip.
She jump-started her friend's car because the car battery had run out of power.
The mechanic said my car battery was completely dead and needed replacing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device that stores and supplies electrical energy

2. NOUN NEW

/ kɑr ˈbætəri / (AmE), / kɑː ˈbætəri / (BrE)
(in electric vehicles) a high‑capacity rechargeable battery pack, typically lithium‑ion, that supplies power to the vehicle's electric drivetrain and determines driving range.
high‑capacity EV battery pack

Examples

Electric vehicles rely on a high-capacity car battery for propulsion and driving range.
The manufacturer offers an eight-year warranty on the car battery and its capacity retention.
Replacing the heavy car battery improved acceleration but added significant weight to the vehicle.
