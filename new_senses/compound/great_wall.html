3. PROPN NEW

/ \u0261re\u026at \u02c8w\u0254l / (AmE), / \u0261re\u026at \u02c8w\u0254\u02d0l / (BrE)
(in astronomy) a very large, wall-like arrangement or filament of galaxies forming part of the universe's large-scale structure, often spanning millions of light-years (e.g., the Sloan Great Wall).
very large galaxy filament

Examples

The research team discovered a new fragment of the Great Wall during their galaxy survey last year.
Astronomers compare the Sloan Great Wall to a vast filament stretching across millions of light-years.
Mapping the distribution revealed that the Great Wall significantly affects galaxy clustering in that region.

Synset Examples

The research team discovered a new fragment of the Great Wall during their galaxy survey last year.
Astronomers compare the Sloan Great Wall to a vast filament stretching across millions of light-years.
Mapping the distribution revealed that the Great Wall significantly affects galaxy clustering in that region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmic filament - a thread-like structure of galaxies and dark matter in the universe's large-scale structure
  • Synonym: large-scale structure - the organized, web-like arrangement of matter in the universe on the largest scales

Etymology

Named by analogy with the Great Wall of China; applied in modern astronomy to very large galaxy filaments (e.g., the Sloan Great Wall).
