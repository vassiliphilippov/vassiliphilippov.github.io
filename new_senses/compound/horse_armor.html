horse armor

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
protective covering made of metal or other materials designed to shield a horse, especially in battle or tournaments
protective covering for a horse

Examples

The horse armor gleamed under the sunlight.
The knight donned his horse armor before the battle.
They displayed an ancient set of horse armor at the museum.

2. NOUN NEW

/ hɔrs ˈɑrmər / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
(in video games) a virtual, cosmetic item for a player's mount that changes the mount's appearance but does not affect protection, stats, or gameplay.
video-game cosmetic for mounts

Examples

The game's new DLC included elaborate horse armor designs sold separately in the store.
Players paid real money for shiny horse armor even though it offered no gameplay benefits.
Community forums debated whether the expensive horse armor was worth buying as a vanity item.

Synset Examples

The game's new DLC included elaborate horse armor designs sold separately in the store.
Players paid real money for shiny horse armor even though it offered no gameplay benefits.
Community forums debated whether the expensive horse armor was worth buying as a vanity item.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmetic item - a virtual item that changes appearance but provides no gameplay benefits
  • Synonym: vanity item - an in-game object purchased or equipped solely for appearance

Etymology

From the literal phrase "horse armor"; this figurative video-game sense was popularized as a term for cosmetic mount gear after the 2006 DLC for The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion.
