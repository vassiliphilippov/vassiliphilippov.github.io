high fidelity

1. NOUN

/ haɪ fəˈdɛlɪti / (AmE), / haɪ fɪˈdɛlɪti / (BrE)
the reproduction of sound that is very accurate and has little or no distortion
accurate sound reproduction

Examples

Audiophiles often invest in expensive systems to achieve high fidelity in sound reproduction.
Many music lovers appreciate the warmth and clarity of high fidelity recordings.
The new speakers are engineered for high fidelity performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hi-fi - equipment or reproduction with high fidelity sound

Etymology

From 'high' (great in amount or degree) + 'fidelity' (faithfulness; from Latin fidelitas).

2. NOUN NEW

/ haɪ fəˈdɛləti / (AmE), / haɪ fɪˈdɛləti / (BrE)
a very accurate reproduction or representation of something (for example a model, prototype, simulation, or digital media) that preserves the original's important details.
accurate representation (models, simulations, prototypes)

Examples

The simulation was designed for high fidelity to real-world behavior.
We created a high fidelity prototype to test the user interface before development.
Researchers used a high fidelity model to predict how the system would respond under stress.
