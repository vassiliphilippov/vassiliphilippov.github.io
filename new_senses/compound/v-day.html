V-day

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː deɪ / (AmE), / ˈviː deɪ / (BrE)
The day on which a victory, especially in war, is achieved or commemorated.
day marking a victory

Examples

Many veterans attend special ceremonies every V-day to honor the victory.
Newspapers around the world reported the end of the war on V-day.
The nation erupted in celebration on V-day after years of conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armistice - an agreement to stop fighting a war

Etymology

From 'V' for victory and 'day'. Popularized during World War II with terms like V-E Day (Victory in Europe Day).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈviː deɪ / (AmE), / ˈviː deɪ / (BrE)
A global social movement and series of public events that work to end violence against women and girls, often through fundraising, education, and performances.
movement to end violence against women

Examples

She joined V-day to help raise funds for survivors of gender-based violence.
The university hosted a V-day benefit reading and talk to educate students about consent.
Every year local activists organize a V-day march and performances supporting survivors.
