5D chess

1. NOUN

/ faɪv di tʃɛs / (AmE), / faɪv diː tʃɛs / (BrE)
a metaphorical expression for an extremely complex or strategic situation, often involving multiple layers of thinking or planning
extremely complex or strategic thinking

Examples

Navigating office politics in that company feels like playing 5D chess against seasoned opponents.
Sometimes her plans resemble 5D chess, with layers of tactics and contingencies.
Their legal strategy was like 5D chess; every move was calculated many steps ahead.

Synset Examples

He always approaches problems as if he's playing 5D chess.
Negotiating with them is like 5D chess; you must think ahead.
Strategizing for the merger felt like playing 5D chess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensional chess - a metaphor for complex or multi-layered strategies, originally referring to chess played on multiple boards
  • Synonym: mastermind - a person who plans and directs a complex project or scheme

Etymology

Modelled after '3D chess', with '5D' indicating multiple added dimensions of strategy; popularized through internet culture.
