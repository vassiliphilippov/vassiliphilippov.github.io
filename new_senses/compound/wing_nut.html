wing nut

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋ nʌt / (AmE), / wɪŋ nʌt / (BrE)
a nut with two large metal projections, or 'wings,' which allow it to be tightened and loosened by hand without tools
nut with winglike projections for hand tightening

Examples

Make sure the wing nut is secure before operating the machine.
She loosened the wing nut by hand to remove the panel.
The lamp's shade is held in place by a simple wing nut.

2. NOUN NEW

/ wɪŋ nʌt / (AmE), / wɪŋ nʌt / (BrE)
an informal, insulting term for a person who holds extreme or irrational political views, especially at one wing of a political party.
extreme partisan (pejorative)
informal offensive

Examples

The columnist called the activist a wing nut for denying established science.
Many voters see his proposals as extreme, calling him a wing nut who will not compromise.
She refused to debate with the wing nut who spread rumors about her record.
