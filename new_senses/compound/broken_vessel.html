broken vessel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkən ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
a person who is emotionally damaged or unable to function due to trauma or deep disappointment
emotionally damaged person

Examples

After the betrayal, he became a broken vessel, struggling to find meaning in life.
Many survivors of war feel like broken vessels, coping with invisible wounds.
She described herself as a broken vessel after the tragedy that struck her family.

Synset Examples

His anger made him a broken vessel, unable to love.
She felt like a broken vessel after the loss.
The storm left the ship a broken vessel on the shore.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from 'vessel' (a container) to denote a person, drawing on the imagery of something no longer whole or functional.
