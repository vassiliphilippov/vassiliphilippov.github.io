alpha fetoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fə ˌfiː.toʊˈproʊ.tiːn / (AmE), / ˈal.fə ˌfiː.təʊˈprəʊ.tiːn / (BrE)
a protein produced mainly by the fetal liver and yolk sac, whose elevated levels in a fetus may indicate neural tube defects such as spina bifida or anencephaly
fetal protein linked to neural tube defects
medical

Examples

Elevated levels of alpha fetoprotein may indicate certain fetal abnormalities.
Scientists are researching how alpha fetoprotein interacts with developing tissues.
The doctor ordered an alpha fetoprotein test as part of the prenatal screening.

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha' (first letter of Greek alphabet) and 'fetoprotein' (protein produced by a fetus).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u00e6l.f\u0259 \u02ccfi\u02d0.to\u028a\u02c8pro\u028a.ti\u02d0n / (AmE), / \u02c8al.f\u0259 \u02ccfi\u02d0.t\u0259\u028a\u02c8pr\u0259\u028a.ti\u02d0n / (BrE)
a serum protein measured in adults and used as a tumour marker to help detect or monitor certain cancers, especially hepatocellular carcinoma and some germ cell tumours.
serum tumour marker
medical

Examples

Clinicians monitor alpha fetoprotein levels to help diagnose and manage liver cancer.
An elevated alpha fetoprotein result prompted further imaging to check for possible hepatocellular carcinoma.
During follow-up, her alpha fetoprotein concentration fell after successful chemotherapy for a germ cell tumor.
