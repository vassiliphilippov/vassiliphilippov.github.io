preemptive bid

1. NOUN

/ priˈɛmptɪv bɪd / (AmE), / priˈɛmptɪv bɪd / (BrE)
a bid made early at a high level in a card game, especially bridge, intended to prevent opposing players from making their own bids
high bid to block opponents (in card games)

Examples

He made a preemptive bid at the three-level to keep the opponents out of the auction.
Her partner was surprised by the sudden preemptive bid during the first round.
Using a preemptive bid can be an aggressive strategy in duplicate bridge.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: opening bid - the first bid made in a round, especially in bridge

Etymology

From 'preemptive' (from Latin 'praeemptio', buying beforehand) + 'bid' (Old English 'biddan', to ask).
