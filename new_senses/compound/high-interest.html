high-interest

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / haɪ ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
(of a loan or financial product) charging a relatively large percentage in interest on the amount borrowed.
charging a high interest rate

Examples

Avoiding high-interest borrowing can help you save money in the long run.
Many people struggle to pay off their high-interest credit card debt.
The bank offered her a high-interest loan because of her poor credit history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-interest - charging a low percentage of the amount borrowed

likely to attract much attention
