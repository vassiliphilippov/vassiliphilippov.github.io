key fob

1. NOUN

/ ki fɑb / (AmE), / ki fɒb / (BrE)
A small ornament or device attached to a key ring or key chain, often to make keys easier to handle or identify.
small item attached to a key ring

Examples

He attached his new key fob to his car keys for easier access.
She lost her house keys along with the leather key fob her friend had given her.
The store offers a personalized key fob engraving service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keychain - a chain that holds keys together

2. NOUN NEW

/ ki fb / (AmE), / ki fb / (BrE)
A small electronic device, often attached to a key ring, that uses wireless signals or proximity sensing to unlock or start a car or to grant access to a building or system.
electronic access device

Examples

She waved her key fob to unlock the car doors from outside the parking lot.
At the office, everyone carries a key fob that lets them enter the secure building after hours.
Manufacturers now embed chips in key fobs so cars can be started without inserting a physical key.
