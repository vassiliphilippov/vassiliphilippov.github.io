noise level

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɪz ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪz ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
the amount or degree of unwanted or background sound, measured as its amplitude
degree of background or unwanted sound

Examples

Authorities monitor the noise level near airports to reduce disturbances for residents.
She wore headphones to block out the high noise level at her workplace.
The noise level in the restaurant made it difficult to hear our conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - the degree of loudness or the intensity of sound
  • Antonym: silence - absence of sound or noise

Examples

The engineer reduced the sensor's noise level to improve measurement accuracy during testing.
In preprocessing, they filtered the data to lower the dataset's noise level before modelling.
