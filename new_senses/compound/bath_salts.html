bath salts

1. NOUN

/ bæθ sɔlts / (AmE), / bɑːθ sɔːlts / (BrE)
a crystalline or powdery substance added to bathwater to soften the skin, scent the water, or provide a soothing effect
substance added to bathwater

Examples

Lavender-scented bath salts are popular for their calming properties.
She poured a handful of bath salts into the hot water to help her relax.
These bath salts can make your skin feel incredibly soft after a long soak.

2. NOUN NEW

/ bæθ sɔlts / (AmE), / bɑːθ sɔːlts / (BrE)
a class of synthetic stimulant drugs, often sold under the misleading label 'bath salts'; typically used recreationally and frequently illegal and dangerous.
synthetic stimulant drugs (street name)

Examples

Police issued a warning after teenagers were hospitalized following use of unknown bath salts bought online.
Paramedics treated a man who had taken bath salts and displayed violent, confused behavior in the park.
Vendors often marketed designer stimulants as bath salts, misleading buyers about their true composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthetic cathinones - a family of synthetic stimulant compounds often sold as 'bath salts'.
  • Synonym: designer drug - a synthetic substance created to mimic the effects of illegal drugs, sometimes marketed to evade laws.

Etymology

Named after deceptive marketing in the early 2000s, when synthetic stimulants were sold labeled as 'bath salts'.
