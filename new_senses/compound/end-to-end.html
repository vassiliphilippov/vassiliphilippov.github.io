end-to-end

1. ADV

/ ˌɛnd tə ˈɛnd / (AmE), / ˌɛnd tʊ ˈend / (BrE)
from the very beginning to the very end; covering the entire process or period continuously.
from beginning to end

Examples

He watched the marathon end-to-end on television, cheering for every runner.
Her commitment to excellence was visible end-to-end in the project.
The concert was entertaining end-to-end and never lost its energy.

Synset Examples

the play was excellent end-to-end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throughout - all the way through; during the whole period

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛnd tə ˈɛnd / (AmE), / ˌɛnd tʊ ˈɛnd / (BrE)
with one object placed so that its end is in contact lengthwise with the end of another object; in a continuous line with no overlap or gap
in a line, ends touching

Examples

She connected the train cars end-to-end until none were left.
The desks were arranged end-to-end along the center of the room.
The pipes were laid end-to-end to form a continuous stretch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: side-by-side - with objects placed so their sides are touching, not their ends

3. ADJ NEW

/ cc5bnd t59 c85bnd / (AmE), / cc5bnd t8a c8end / (BrE)
(in computing and information security) describing a system, connection, or process in which data or communication is protected or processed only at the origin and destination endpoints so that intermediaries cannot read, alter, or access the plaintext.
technical: protection between endpoints

Examples

The messaging app now offers end-to-end encryption to protect user privacy.
Our QA team runs end-to-end tests to ensure the whole service works under load.
Manufacturers must secure the end-to-end supply chain to prevent tampering with products.
