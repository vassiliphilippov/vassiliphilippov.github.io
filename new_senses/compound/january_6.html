January 6

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒænjuˈɛri sɪks / (AmE), / ˌdʒan.juˈɛː.ri sɪks / (BrE)
The sixth day of January, observed in many Christian traditions as Epiphany, celebrating the visit of the three wise men to the infant Jesus.
6 January; Christian feast day (Epiphany)

Examples

Church services are held on January 6 to commemorate the Magi's visit to Jesus.
In Spain, children receive gifts on January 6 to honor the arrival of the three wise men.
Many countries celebrate January 6 as Epiphany, marking the end of the Christmas season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epiphany - Christian festival commemorating the visit of the Magi to the infant Jesus, celebrated on January 6

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccd\u0292\u00e6nju\u02c8\u025bri s\u026aks / (AmE), / \u02ccd\u0292an.ju\u02c8\u025b\u02d0.ri s\u026aks / (BrE)
(in political and media contexts) a label for the attack on the U.S. Capitol on January 6, 2021; used to refer to that event and its consequences.
the Jan 6, 2021 U.S. Capitol attack

Examples

News reports compared the security failures during January 6 to other recent acts of political violence.
Survivors testified about the chaos they experienced inside the building on January 6 during congressional proceedings.
Many politicians still refer to January 6 when discussing threats to democratic institutions.
