theater of war

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər əv wɔːr / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə əv wɔː / (BrE)
the entire land, sea, and air area where military operations are conducted during a war
area where war operations are conducted

Examples

Medical units were deployed to support soldiers in the theater of war.
Military planners consider logistics throughout the entire theater of war.
The Pacific was a significant theater of war during World War II.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: battlefield - the specific site where a battle occurs
  • Degree Lower: front line - the military line or area closest to the enemy

Etymology

From 'theater' meaning place of action, and 'war'; first used in military strategy in the 18th–19th centuries.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər əv wɔr / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə əv wɔː / (BrE)
an area where active military operations and conflict are taking place during a war
area of active military conflict

Examples

Diplomatic efforts continue even as fighting intensifies in the theater of war.
During the Second World War, Europe became the main theater of war for Allied and Axis forces.
Journalists risked their lives to report from the theater of war in the Middle East.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - a place where a battle is fought during a war

(in modern military and policy contexts) a non-physical domain (for example cyberspace or outer space) regarded as a theatre of war where hostile actions or military operations occur.
non-physical domain of military conflict

Examples

Officials warned that critical infrastructure could be targeted in the new theater of war of cyberspace.
Strategists debate rules for conduct in a theater of war that has no clear national borders.
After the satellite strike, policymakers argued that outer space had become another theater of war.

Modern extension of the military phrase 'theater of war' to include non-physical domains (late 20th–21st century).
