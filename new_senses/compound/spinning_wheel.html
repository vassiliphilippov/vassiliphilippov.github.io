spinning wheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪŋ wiːl / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪŋ wiːl / (BrE)
a small domestic machine for spinning thread or yarn from fibers, typically operated by hand or foot
domestic machine for spinning thread or yarn

Examples

A spinning wheel can be powered by either hand or foot.
She learned how to use a spinning wheel to turn wool into yarn.
The antique spinning wheel in the museum was beautifully preserved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spindle - a rod used for spinning fibers manually

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈspɪnɪŋ wiːl / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪŋ wiːl / (BrE)
(in computing and user interfaces) a circular graphical indicator that spins to show that a process is in progress or the system is busy.
graphical loading indicator

Examples

The app showed a spinning wheel while it synchronized dozens of large files.
A small spinning wheel appeared on the screen, signaling the system was temporarily busy.
After clicking save, he waited as the spinning wheel completed and the document reopened.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the traditional textile spinning wheel (device).
