factor space

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæktər speɪs / (AmE), / ˈfæktə speɪs / (BrE)
A mathematical structure, often a subspace or set, formed by factors (such as variables or eigenvectors) used for analysis or problem-solving, especially in linear algebra and statistics.
mathematical structure for analysis

Examples

Finding the optimal solution required projecting the problem onto the factor space.
Researchers often use a factor space to reduce the dimensionality of large datasets.
The factor space of the matrix provided key insights during data analysis.

Synset Examples

The factor space of the matrix is crucial for analysis.
We can visualize the factor space for better insights.
Understanding the factor space helps in optimization.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfæktər speɪs / (AmE), / ˈfæktə speɪs / (BrE)
(in machine learning and recommender systems) a latent vector space produced by matrix factorization or embedding models in which entities (for example, users and items) are represented as vectors and similarity or predictions are made by comparing those vectors.
latent vector space (matrix factorization)

Examples

The recommender system maps users and items into a factor space to predict unseen ratings.
By comparing vectors in the factor space, the team identified similar products for personalized suggestions.
They trained matrix factorization until the user and item embeddings stabilized in the factor space.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latent space - a low-dimensional vector space in which data or entities are represented by continuous latent variables or embeddings
  • Synonym: embedding space - a vector space where discrete items (words, users, items) are mapped to continuous vectors for similarity and prediction
