arsenic trioxide

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrˈsiːnɪk traɪˈɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / ˌɑːˈsɛnɪk traɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a poisonous white powder (As2O3) used in glassmaking, pesticides, and formerly as a rat poison.
a toxic white chemical used in industry and as a pesticide

Examples

Arsenic trioxide is sometimes used in the production of specialty glass.
After the discovery of arsenic trioxide contamination, the plant was temporarily shut down.
The toxic properties of arsenic trioxide make it a dangerous but effective pesticide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenic - a metallic chemical element (As), often toxic
