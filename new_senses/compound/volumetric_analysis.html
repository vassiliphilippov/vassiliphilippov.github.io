volumetric analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑː.ljuˈmɛ.trɪk əˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌvɒl.juˈmɛ.trɪk əˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
a method of quantitative chemical analysis based on measuring the volume of a standard solution needed to react with a sample
chemical analysis by measuring liquid volumes
medical

Examples

Accurate pipetting is crucial for reliable results in volumetric analysis.
Laboratories often rely on volumetric analysis in clinical and environmental testing.
The student used volumetric analysis to determine the concentration of the acid solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titration - determining concentration by adding a known volume of a reagent

Etymology

From 'volumetric' (relating to measurement of volume) + 'analysis' (from Greek 'analyein', to loosen up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɑː.ljuˈmɛ.trɪk əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌvɒl.juˈmɛt.rɪk əˈnæl.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
a method in chemistry for determining the amount of a substance by measuring the volume of a gas produced or consumed in a reaction
chemical analysis by measuring gas volume
medical

Examples

He specialized in volumetric analysis for his research on reaction rates.
In chemistry class, we learned about volumetric analysis to determine the concentration of unknown substances.
The accuracy of volumetric analysis depends on precise measurement of the gas volumes involved.

Etymology

From 'volumetric', referring to measurement of volume, and 'analysis', the process of examining something methodically.
