white sage

1. NOUN

/ waɪt seɪdʒ / (AmE), / waɪt seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a perennial plant with silvery-white leaves, native to the southwestern United States and often used for ceremonial or medicinal purposes
perennial plant with silvery leaves

Examples

White sage is highly valued for its aromatic qualities and traditional uses among Native Americans.
Many people burn white sage to cleanse their homes during spiritual rituals.
The silvery leaves of white sage shimmered in the desert sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - aromatic herb of the mint family
