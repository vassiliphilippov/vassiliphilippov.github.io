defense mechanism

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
An unconscious psychological process employed to reduce anxiety arising from unacceptable or potentially harmful impulses and feelings.
unconscious process reducing anxiety
medical

Examples

Denial is a common defense mechanism people use when faced with difficult truths.
Projection can serve as a defense mechanism when someone attributes their own feelings to others.
Therapists help patients understand how a defense mechanism might be influencing their behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coping mechanism - a conscious or unconscious strategy to manage stress or anxiety

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
(in information security) a system, device, or method designed to detect, prevent, or reduce the impact of threats to computer networks, data, or systems.
security system or method

Examples

A layered defense mechanism protects the network from evolving cyber threats.
Intrusion detection is a crucial defense mechanism for large enterprises during a breach.
They deployed anomaly monitoring as an additional defense mechanism against data exfiltration.

3. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
(in biology and medicine) a physiological or behavioral trait that helps an organism protect itself from predators, pathogens, or environmental harm.
biological protective trait

Examples

Many plants rely on a chemical defense mechanism to deter herbivores from eating their leaves.
Camouflage is an effective defense mechanism that helps animals avoid detection by predators.
Fever acts as a physiological defense mechanism to slow the growth of many pathogens in the body.
