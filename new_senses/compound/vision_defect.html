vision defect

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒən dɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒ(ə)n dɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
an impairment or abnormality of the sense of sight
impairment of sight

Examples

Children with an untreated vision defect may have difficulty learning in school.
Regular check-ups can help detect a vision defect early.
The doctor diagnosed her with a mild vision defect that could be corrected by glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual impairment - reduced ability to see to a degree that causes problems not fixable by usual means
  • Degree Higher: blindness - the condition of lacking visual perception due to physiological or neurological factors

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8v\u026a\u0292\u0259n d\u026a\u02ccf\u025bkt / (AmE), / \u02c8v\u026a\u0292(\u0259)n d\u026a\u02ccf\u025bkt / (BrE)
(in computing, especially computer vision) an error or failure in a vision system or algorithm that causes incorrect interpretation of visual data.
error in a computer vision system

Examples

The autonomous vehicle stopped because a sudden vision defect misidentified a pedestrian as a shadow.
Engineers traced the frequent false alarms to a subtle vision defect in the object-detection model.
After retraining the network, the team eliminated the vision defect that caused misclassification under low light.
