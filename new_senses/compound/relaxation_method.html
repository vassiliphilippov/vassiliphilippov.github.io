relaxation method

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌlækˈseɪʃən ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / rɪˌlækˈseɪʃən ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
A mathematical technique for solving systems of equations by starting with an estimated solution and iteratively reducing errors through repeated approximation until a desired level of accuracy is achieved.
iterative technique for solving equations

Examples

By applying the relaxation method, the algorithm quickly reduces the error in each successive approximation.
Students learned how the relaxation method converges to an accurate solution through repeated calculation.
The relaxation method is widely used for solving large systems of linear equations in engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iterative method - A computational technique involving repeated application of a process to approach a desired result

Etymology

From the noun 'relaxation' (loosening, reduction of constraint) and 'method'; used since the early 20th century in mathematics.

2. NOUN NEW

A technique or procedure used to produce physical or mental calm and reduce stress, tension, or anxiety.
technique for reducing stress

Examples

The therapist introduced a relaxation method to help clients reduce daytime anxiety and tension.
They taught students a simple relaxation method of breathing to calm nerves before exams.
After trying several relaxation methods, she preferred progressive muscle relaxation for better sleep.
