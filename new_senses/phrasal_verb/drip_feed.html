to drip feed

1. VERB NEW

/ drɪp fid / (AmE), / drɪp fiːd / (BrE)
to give information, news, or material slowly and in small amounts over time, often to influence perception or build interest.
reveal information gradually

Examples

Government officials sometimes drip feed journalists small updates to control the story's direction.
The company chose to drip feed product details to build anticipation before the full launch.
During the investigation, the police drip-fed fragments of the report to the press.
