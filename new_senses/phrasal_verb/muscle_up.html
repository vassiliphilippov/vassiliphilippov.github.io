to muscle up

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌsəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl ʌp / (BrE)
to gather strength, determination, or courage in preparation for a challenge
gather strength or courage
informal

Examples

Before the interview, she told herself to muscle up and remain confident.
It's time to muscle up and face your fears head-on.
They need to muscle up if they want to overcome these obstacles.

Synset Examples

He decided to muscle up and tackle the tough project.
You need to muscle up before the big game.
To win, they must muscle up and stay focused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace oneself - prepare mentally or emotionally for something difficult
  • Synonym: psych up - make oneself mentally ready for a challenge
  • Antonym: back down - withdraw from a challenge; give up

2. VERB NEW

/ c8m8cs59l 8cp / (AmE), / c8m8csl 8cp / (BrE)
(in fitness/gymnastics) to pull oneself up and then push up over a bar or rings in one continuous movement (a pull-up followed by a dip).
perform a pull-up-to-dip movement

Examples

She finally managed to muscle up onto the high bar after weeks of practice.
He muscled up from the rings to complete the full gymnastic movement.
At the CrossFit class, they practiced muscling up quickly between sets to build endurance.

Synset Examples
