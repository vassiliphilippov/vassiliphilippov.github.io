to rush out

1. VERB

/ rʌʃ aʊt / (AmE), / rʌʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to jump or move quickly out from a place of hiding, often to surprise or attack someone
jump or move suddenly from hiding

Examples

As soon as the bell rang, the children rushed out from their hiding spots.
Suddenly, a cat rushed out from under the car, nearly scaring me to death.
The robber rushed out from behind the door and startled the security guard.

Synset Examples

The attackers leapt out from the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap out - to jump out suddenly from hiding

2. VERB NEW

/ rʌʃ aʊt / (AmE), / rʌʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to make something available quickly, especially a product, update, or statement, often in response to an event or problem.
quickly release or publish

Examples

The tech firm promised to rush out a security patch within hours of the breach.
They rushed out a brief press release before competitors announced similar features.
Every time a problem appears, the company rushes out fixes to reassure customers quickly.
