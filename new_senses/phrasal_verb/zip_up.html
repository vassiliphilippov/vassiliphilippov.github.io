to zip up

1. VERB

/ zɪp ʌp / (AmE), / zɪp ʌp / (BrE)
to close something, especially an item of clothing or a bag, by using a zipper
close with a zipper

Examples

Don’t forget to zip up your coat before going outside in the snow.
He quickly zipped up his suitcase before leaving for the airport.
She zipped up her backpack to keep everything from falling out.

Synset Examples

Zip up your jacket — it's cold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unzip - to open something with a zipper

2. VERB NEW

/ zɪp ʌp / (AmE), / zɪp ʌp / (BrE)
(in computing) to compress one or more files or folders into a ZIP archive (create a .zip file).
create a ZIP archive

Examples

Before sending the files, please zip up the project folder to save space.
She zipped up the photos and sent the archive to her colleagues yesterday.
They're zipping up each folder separately so the download size stays small for users.
