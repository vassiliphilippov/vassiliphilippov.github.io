to sign on

1. VERB

/ saɪn ɑn / (AmE), / saɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to agree to become a member of an organization or to start working for an employer, especially by signing a contract
agree to join by signing a contract

Examples

After graduation, she decided to sign on with a large law firm.
Many seasonal workers sign on during the busy holiday period.
The new coach will sign on for a two-year contract next week.

Synset Examples

They signed two new pitchers for the next season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign up - to register or enroll for something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02415071-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB NEW

/ saɪn ɑn / (AmE), / saɪn ɒn / (BrE)
(computing) to log into a computer system or online service by entering your credentials; to authenticate yourself to gain access.
log into an account or system

Examples

Users must sign on to the university portal with their student ID.
Yesterday she signed on to the new cloud service and set up two-factor authentication.
If you keep typing the wrong password, the system prevents you from signing on for a while.

Synset Examples

Users must sign on to the university portal with their student ID.
Yesterday she signed on to the new cloud service and set up two-factor authentication.
If you keep typing the wrong password, the system prevents you from signing on for a while.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: log on - to connect to a computer system or service by entering a username and password.
  • Synonym: sign in - to enter credentials to access an account or service (often used interchangeably with 'sign on').
  • Antonym: sign off - to log out of a system or end a session; often the opposite action to signing on.
