to rain check

1. VERB NEW

/ c8re6an cct835bk / (AmE), / c8re6an cct835bk / (BrE)
(informal) to postpone or decline something now while indicating a desire or intention to accept or reschedule it at a later time.
postpone or decline now, reschedule later
informal

Examples

Thanks for the invitation, but I'll have to rain check and join you another evening.
She decided to rain check the meeting after learning most team members were ill.
He texted, 'Can I rain check dinner?' and suggested rescheduling for Saturday night.

Etymology

From the noun rain check (originally a ticket given for a postponed outdoor event in late 19th-century America); later extended to a verb meaning to ask for or offer such a postponement.
