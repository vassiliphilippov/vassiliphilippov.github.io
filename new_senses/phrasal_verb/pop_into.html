to pop into

1. VERB

/ pɑp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pɒp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to visit (a place) briefly and usually unexpectedly
visit briefly and unexpectedly

Examples

I decided to pop into the café for a quick coffee before work.
If you're passing by, feel free to pop into my office for a chat.
She usually pops into the grocery store on her way home.

Synset Examples

She likes to pop into the store for snacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop in - to visit someone informally and without arrangement

2. VERB NEW

/ pɑp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pɒp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
(of a thought, idea, or memory) to occur to someone suddenly and briefly; to come into someone's mind without deliberate effort.
occur suddenly in the mind

Examples

Suddenly a useful recipe idea popped into my head while I was washing the dishes.
Every time I try to concentrate, random memories pop into my mind and distract me.
An image of their old house popped into her thoughts during the quiet meeting.

Synset Examples
