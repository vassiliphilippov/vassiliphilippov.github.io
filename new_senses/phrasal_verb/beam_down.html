to beam down

1. VERB

/ biːm daʊn / (AmE), / biːm daʊn / (BrE)
to shine brightly from above, especially referring to sunlight or another strong light source
shine brightly from above

Examples

During the summer, the sun beams down relentlessly in the desert.
Harsh lights beamed down on the stage, illuminating the performers.
The morning sun started to beam down on the quiet valley.

Synset Examples

The sun began to beam down on us during the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shine - to give out a bright light

2. VERB NEW

/ biːm daʊn / (AmE), / biːm daʊn / (BrE)
(in telecommunications) to transmit a signal, data, or broadcast downward from a satellite, aircraft, or other transmitter to a ground or lower-level receiver.
transmit data/signal downward

Examples

The satellite will beam down high-resolution photographs to the ground station every hour.
Engineers asked the probe to beam down telemetry during each scheduled contact window.
During the test, the drone beamed down encrypted commands to the remote sensor array.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmit - to send information, signals, or power from one place or device to another
  • Synonym: downlink - a transmission of data from a satellite or spacecraft to a ground station; also the act of making such a transmission
