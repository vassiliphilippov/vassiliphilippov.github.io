to knock down

1. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)
to hit or strike someone or something so that they fall to the ground
strike so as to make fall

Examples

A sudden gust of wind could easily knock down the fence.
She was accidentally knocked down by a player during the game.
The boxer managed to knock down his opponent in the third round.

Synset Examples

He decked his opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floor - to knock someone to the ground, especially in boxing
  • Synonym: deck - to knock someone down, especially with a punch

/ nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)

3. VERB

/ nɑk daʊn / (AmE), / nɒk daʊn / (BrE)
to destroy or demolish (something) as if by an explosion; to cause to collapse suddenly and forcefully
destroy or demolish as if by an explosion

Examples

The blast knocked down several walls in the factory.
The old warehouse was knocked down last week after the fire.
They plan to knock down the building to make way for new apartments.

Related Terms

4. VERB NEW

/ n51k da8an / (AmE), / n52k da8an / (BrE)
(in genetics and molecular biology) to reduce the expression level of a gene in cells, typically by using methods such as RNA interference or antisense oligonucleotides.
reduce gene expression (genetics)

Examples

Researchers used RNA interference to knock down the expression of the target gene in cultured cells.
They successfully knocked down both isoforms, which reduced the cellular protein level by seventy percent.
Knocking down expression in developing neurons allowed the team to observe changes in synaptic connectivity.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene silencing - the process of preventing a gene from producing its product (RNA or protein); often used interchangeably with reducing expression.
  • Degree Higher: knockout - a genetic technique that completely eliminates the function or expression of a gene, often by deletion or disruption.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the physical sense 'knock down', applied in molecular biology to mean reducing gene expression.
