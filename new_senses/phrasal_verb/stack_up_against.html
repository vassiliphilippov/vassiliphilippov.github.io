to stack up against

1. VERB

/ stæk ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / stak ʌp əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
(of evidence, arguments, or factors) to accumulate so as to disadvantage someone or something, or create an unfair situation.
accumulate so as to disadvantage

Examples

Negative reviews began to stack up against the new product, hurting its sales.
Several obstacles stacked up against us, making the project much harder to complete.
The evidence continued to stack up against the defendant throughout the trial.

Synset Examples

The evidence began to stack up against the suspect.
We should avoid stacking the cards against the competition.

2. VERB NEW

/ stæk ʌp əˈɡenst / (AmE), / stak ʌp əˈɡenst / (BrE)
to be compared with someone or something to determine relative quality, strength, or suitability; to measure up against competitors or a standard.
be compared; measure against

Examples

Before release, the team tested whether the app would stack up against similar products.
After six months, her performance review showed how she stacked up against the other candidates.
Consumers will compare features to see if the budget model stacks up against premium versions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compare - to examine the similarities or differences between two or more things
  • Antonym: fall short of - to not be as good as or to fail to reach a standard or expectation
