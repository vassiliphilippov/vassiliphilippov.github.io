to scratch up

1. VERB

/ skrætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / skrætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to damage the surface of something by making small cuts or marks, typically by scraping or rubbing against it
damage surface by scratching

Examples

Be careful not to scratch up the table when you move it.
The cat managed to scratch up the new sofa with its claws.
Years of neglect had scratched up the old car's paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to make thin, shallow cuts or marks on a surface

2. VERB NEW

/ skrætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / skrætʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to obtain or gather (money, supplies, or similar resources) with difficulty, by scraping together small amounts, borrowing, or making extra effort.
gather with difficulty
informal

Examples

We managed to scratch up enough money to cover the car repair.
She is trying to scratch up some spare parts from friends to fix the bike.
If we hurry, maybe we can scratch up the remaining fees before the deadline.
