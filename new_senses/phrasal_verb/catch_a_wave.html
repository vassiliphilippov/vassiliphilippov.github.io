to catch a wave

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ə weɪv / (AmE), / katʃ ə weɪv / (BrE)
to ride on a moving wave, especially as part of the sport of surfing
to surf or ride a wave

Examples

Do you know how to catch a wave when the tide is right?
Every morning, she wakes up early to catch a wave before work.
He tried for hours but couldn't catch a wave until the wind picked up.

Synset Examples

She loves to catch a wave every summer.
They all waited to catch a wave at the beach.
He managed to catch a wave just before sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surf - to ride on ocean waves using a surfboard

2. VERB NEW

/ kætʃ ə weɪv / (AmE), / katʃ ə weɪv / (BrE)
(informal) to take advantage of a rising trend, surge of interest, or new opportunity in order to gain rapid benefit or growth.
ride a trend or opportunity

Examples

The startup tried to catch a wave of interest after launching its new AI chatbot last month.
They caught a wave of consumer enthusiasm, and sales doubled within six weeks.
By catching a wave of remote-work interest, the consultancy quickly expanded its client base.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: seize an opportunity - to take immediate action to benefit from a favourable chance
