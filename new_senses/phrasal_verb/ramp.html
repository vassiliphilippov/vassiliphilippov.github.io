to ramp

1. VERB NEW

/ ræmp / (AmE), / ræmp / (BrE)
(chiefly in business/tech) to increase or cause to increase in amount, rate, capacity, or intensity, often quickly; commonly used with an object (ramp up production).
increase or escalate (something)

Examples

The factory plans to ramp up production next month to meet the holiday demand.
After the funding arrived, they began to ramp up hiring across all departments quickly.
Engineers are ramping up the server capacity ahead of the product launch.
