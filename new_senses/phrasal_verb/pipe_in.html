to pipe in

1. VERB

/ paɪp ɪn / (AmE), / paɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to transport (a substance such as gas, oil, or water) to a place through pipes
transport via pipes

Examples

During the renovation, clean water was piped in from a nearby source.
Natural gas is piped in to the factory for heating and production.
They plan to pipe in fresh air to improve ventilation in the building.

Synset Examples

We have to pipe in oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pump in - to move (a liquid or gas) into a place using a pump

/ paɪp ɪn / (AmE), / paɪp ɪn / (BrE)

4. VERB NEW

/ paɪp ɪn / (AmE), / paɪp ɪn / (BrE)
(in computing) to send or feed data, signals, or streams into a program, service, or processing pipeline, often via a software 'pipe', API, or stream interface.
feed data into a software pipeline

Examples

Engineers often pipe in real-time sensor data to the monitoring dashboard during tests.
During the demo, she piped in sample logs so the analysis tool could display errors.
To simulate traffic, the team is piping in synthetic requests through the API gateway today.
