to lust after

1. VERB

/ lʌst ˈæftɚ / (AmE), / lʌst ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to have a strong sexual desire for someone
have strong sexual desire for

Examples

He was clearly lusting after his new coworker at the party.
It was obvious she did not lust after him the way he hoped.
Tabloid stories claimed the actor was lusting after his famous costar.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - to strongly wish for something or someone
  • Synonym: crave - to long for intensely

Etymology

From the Middle English 'lust', from Old English 'lust' (desire, pleasure).

2. VERB NEW

/ lʌst ˈæftə / (AmE), / lʌst ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
to feel a very strong desire or craving for something (often abstract or material), used figuratively and not limited to sexual desire.
intensely desire (non-sexual)

Examples

The ambitious leader seemed to lust after power and prestige more than helping others.
She lusted after fame for years, accepting roles she didn't enjoy to stay in the public eye.
Many collectors are lusting after rare first editions that have skyrocketed in value.
