to bleed out

1. VERB

/ bliːd aʊt / (AmE), / bliːd aʊt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to lose so much blood from an injury that there is a risk of death or serious harm, especially if untreated.
lose a large amount of blood

Examples

If they had arrived any later, she might have bled out on the sidewalk.
The medic rushed over before the victim could bleed out from the deep wound.
We need to apply pressure here so he doesn't bleed out before the ambulance gets here.

Synset Examples

The soldier managed not to bleed out after the injury.
He was afraid he would bleed out before help arrived.
They had to stop the wound from bleeding out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exsanguinate - to drain or lose blood; medical term

2. VERB NEW

/ bliːd aʊt / (AmE), / bliːd aʊt / (BrE)
(in video games) to lose remaining health while incapacitated or downed, causing the character to die if teammates do not revive them.
lose health while downed in a game

Examples

During the match, his teammate reached him just before he bled out.
If you leave a downed player alone, they will bleed out and lose the round.
The tutorial warned new players that fallen allies can bleed out while waiting for revival.

Synset Examples

During the match, his teammate reached him just before he bled out.
If you leave a downed player alone, they will bleed out and lose the round.
The tutorial warned new players that fallen allies can bleed out while waiting for revival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; in games, to be eliminated or removed from play
  • Antonym: revive - to restore a downed player to life or activity; in games, to bring an incapacitated character back into play
