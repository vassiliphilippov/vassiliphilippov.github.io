to throw up

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ʌp / (AmE), / θrəʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth; to vomit
to vomit; eject stomach contents
informal

Examples

He felt sick and had to throw up after eating the spoiled food.
She was so nervous before the performance that she almost threw up.
The child threw up on the bus ride to school.

Synset Examples

The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: regurgitate - to bring swallowed food back up into the mouth

2. VERB NEW

/ θroʊ ʌp / (AmE), / θrəʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to cause a window, message, dialog, or other interface element to appear on a screen; to display or generate (something) in a software context.
display or generate (a dialog or message)

Examples

When the server times out, the website will throw up a message asking you to try again.
After the invalid input, the program threw up multiple warnings and prevented you from continuing.
During testing, developers kept throwing up mock dialogs to reproduce the user's problem.
