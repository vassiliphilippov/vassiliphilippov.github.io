to stick

1. VERB

/ stɪk / (AmE), / stɪk / (BrE)
to unfairly burden someone with something undesirable or disadvantageous, especially a bill or responsibility
unfairly burden with something unwelcome

Examples

She got stuck with organizing the entire fundraiser by herself.
The waiter tried to stick us with a service charge we hadn't agreed to.
They always try to stick newcomers with the least desirable jobs.

Synset Examples

They stuck me with the dinner bill
I was stung with a huge tax bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle - to burden someone with something undesirable
