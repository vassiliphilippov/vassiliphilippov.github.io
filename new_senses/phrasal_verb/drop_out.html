to drop out

1. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to stop participating in a competition, activity, or group, usually because of difficulty, defeat, or lack of hope
stop participating (esp. after difficulty)

Examples

After injuring his leg, he had to drop out of the race.
Many students drop out when the course becomes too challenging.
She didn't want to drop out, but her health gave her no choice.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give up - to stop trying to do something because it is too difficult
  • Antonym: continue - to keep doing something without stopping

2. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave school, college, or an educational program before finishing it, especially without obtaining a qualification
leave school or program before finishing

Examples

After failing several classes, Mark decided to drop out of college.
Many students drop out because they cannot afford the tuition anymore.
She regretted her decision to drop out years later when job opportunities were limited.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

5. VERB NEW

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
(in machine learning) to randomly deactivate a subset of neurons or activation units during training to reduce overfitting and improve generalization.
randomly deactivate neurons during training (ML)

Examples

During training, we drop out some neurons to prevent our model from memorizing the training data.
The team dropped out half the activations randomly each epoch as a regularization technique.
By dropping out units during training, the network learns more robust features and generalizes better.

Synset Examples

During training, we drop out some neurons to prevent our model from memorizing the training data.
The team dropped out half the activations randomly each epoch as a regularization technique.
By dropping out units during training, the network learns more robust features and generalizes better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dropout - a regularization technique that randomly deactivates neural units during training
  • Synonym: regularize - to apply methods that reduce overfitting and improve a model's ability to generalize
  • Antonym: overfitting - the tendency of a model to learn noise in the training data and perform poorly on new data

Etymology

Named in machine-learning literature (from the verb 'drop' + particle 'out'); popularized by the 2012 paper 'Dropout' by Hinton and colleagues.
