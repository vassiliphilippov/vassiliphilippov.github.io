to dope up

1. VERB

/ doʊp ʌp / (AmE), / dəʊp ʌp / (BrE)
to give a narcotic or drug to someone, often to affect their performance or consciousness
administer drugs or narcotics to someone
informal

Examples

The coach decided to dope up the athletes to enhance their performance before the big race.
The prisoner claimed that he had been doped up without his consent.
They discovered the kidnappers had doped up the guard to make their escape easier.

Synset Examples

The athletes were doped by the coach before the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drug - to administer a drug to someone, typically secretly
  • Antonym: sober up - to become less intoxicated or drugged

Etymology

From 'dope' (slang for drug/narcotic), itself from Dutch 'doop' (sauce), and 'up' as a verb particle.

2. VERB NEW

/ do\u028ap \u028cp / (AmE), / d\u0259\u028ap \u028cp / (BrE)
(in materials science and semiconductor fabrication) to introduce impurities (dopants) into a material or semiconductor to change its electrical or physical properties.
add dopants to a material

Examples

Researchers dope up silicon wafers with boron to create p-type regions for transistors.
To adjust carrier concentration, the engineer doped up the sample using ion implantation equipment.
During fabrication, technicians monitor how quickly they can dope up each wafer without causing damage.

Synset Examples

Researchers dope up silicon wafers with boron to create p-type regions for transistors.
To adjust carrier concentration, the engineer doped up the sample using ion implantation equipment.
During fabrication, technicians monitor how quickly they can dope up each wafer without causing damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doping - the intentional introduction of impurities into a semiconductor to change its electrical properties
  • Synonym: ion implantation - a technique for embedding dopant ions into a substrate to alter its conductivity
