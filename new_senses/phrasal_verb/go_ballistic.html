to go ballistic

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ bəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ bəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
to become extremely angry or furious
become extremely angry
informal

Examples

If they find out we lied to them, they'll go ballistic.
My boss went ballistic when he saw the mistakes in the report.
Try not to go ballistic over every minor inconvenience.

Synset Examples

She'll go ballistic if you break her favorite vase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose one's temper - to suddenly become very angry
  • Synonym: blow a fuse - to lose one's temper suddenly
  • Antonym: keep one's cool - remain calm instead of getting angry

Etymology

Originally referring to the trajectory of a missile; figuratively used for an explosive outburst since late 20th century.
