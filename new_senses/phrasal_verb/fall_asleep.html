to fall asleep

1. VERB

/ fɔl əˈslip / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈsliːp / (BrE)
to begin to sleep after being awake
begin to sleep

Examples

I usually fall asleep within minutes of going to bed.
She was so tired that she fell asleep during the movie.
You might fall asleep if you keep reading in that comfortable chair.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wake up - to stop sleeping and become conscious
  • Synonym: doze off - to start sleeping, especially unintentionally or briefly

3. VERB NEW

/ f54l 59c8slip / (AmE), / f54d0l 59c8slid0p / (BrE)
(of a device, program, or app) to enter a low-power or sleep state automatically after a period of inactivity.
enter low-power sleep mode (devices)

Examples

When idle for too long, the laptop will fall asleep to save battery power.
During the presentation the projector fell asleep, and we had to restart it quickly.
After months of updates, some apps refuse to fall asleep and drain phones overnight.
