to zoom in

1. VERB

/ zum ɪn / (AmE), / zuːm ɪn / (BrE)
to examine something closely or focus attention on a particular part or detail
focus closely or examine in detail

Examples

During the meeting, he zoomed in on the key issues that needed attention.
She zoomed in on the photograph to study the tiny details.
The documentary zooms in on the daily lives of the villagers.

Synset Examples

He zoomed in on the book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to focus on - to direct attention to something specific

2. VERB NEW

/ zum ɪn / (AmE), / zuːm ɪn / (BrE)
to join or attend a meeting using the Zoom video‑conferencing platform (often used with a time or place: 'zoom in at 10').
join a Zoom meeting

Examples

I'll zoom in halfway through the meeting because I have a dentist appointment this morning.
She zoomed in from home to give her presentation to the international team.
They suggested zooming in as an option for colleagues who could not travel to the conference.

Synset Examples

I'll zoom in halfway through the meeting because I have a dentist appointment this morning.
She zoomed in from home to give her presentation to the international team.
They suggested zooming in as an option for colleagues who could not travel to the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become a participant in a meeting, group, or event
  • Synonym: attend - to be present at a meeting, event, or gathering

Etymology

From the trademarked name Zoom (the video‑conferencing company); the brand name is used as a verb meaning to join a meeting on that platform.
