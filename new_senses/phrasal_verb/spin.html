to spin

1. VERB NEW

/ spɪn / (AmE), / spɪn / (BrE)
(computing) to start or provision a server, virtual machine, container, or other computing resource quickly so it is ready for use.
start or provision a virtual resource quickly

Examples

Cloud engineers can spin up a new virtual instance in minutes for testing.
She spun up a temporary database container to reproduce the customer's error during debugging.
The deployment script automatically spins up test environments whenever a developer opens a pull request.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to cause something to begin operating

  • Synonym: provision - to prepare and allocate computing resources for use

Etymology

From the verb 'spin' + particle 'up'; a metaphorical extension used in computing since the early 21st century to mean quickly bring a resource into operation.
