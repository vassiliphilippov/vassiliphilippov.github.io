to gas up

1. VERB

/ ɡæs ʌp / (AmE), / ɡæs ʌp / (BrE)
to fill a vehicle's fuel tank with gasoline
fill with gasoline
american informal

Examples

I need to gas up before we head out on the highway.
Make sure you gas up the rental car before returning it.
We stopped at the service station to gas up the truck.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuel - to fill a vehicle or machine with more fuel
  • Synonym: fill up - to make full, often used for putting fuel in a vehicle

2. VERB NEW

/ ɡæs ʌp / (AmE), / ɡæs ʌp / (BrE)
(informal, slang) to praise, flatter, or hype someone in order to raise their confidence, enthusiasm, or mood.
hype or praise to boost confidence
informal slang

Examples

Before the audition, the director gassed up the nervous singer with warm compliments.
He tried to gas up his friend before the presentation so they'd feel more confident.
Sports fans spent the evening gassing up the team on social media after the win.
