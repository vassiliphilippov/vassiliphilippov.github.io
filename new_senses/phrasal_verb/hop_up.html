to hop up

1. VERB

/ hɑp ʌp / (AmE), / hɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to increase the power, speed, or effectiveness of something, especially a motor vehicle engine, by making modifications or improvements
make (a machine) more powerful

Examples

Enthusiasts often hop up their cars to win street races.
The mechanic managed to hop up the old engine for better performance.
They hopped up the system with a new turbocharger and better fuel injectors.

Synset Examples

he souped up the old cars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soup up - make more powerful, especially an engine

2. VERB

/ hɑp ʌp / (AmE), / hɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to jump or rise quickly, often with a light, sudden movement
jump or rise quickly

Examples

He hopped up from his chair as soon as he saw her enter the room.
If I hop up on this box, I can reach the top shelf.
She watched the cat hop up onto the windowsill with ease.

Synset Examples

Can you hop up and grab that book for me?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump up - to spring into the air quickly
