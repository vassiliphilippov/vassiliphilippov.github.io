to step down

1. VERB

/ stɛp daʊn / (AmE), / stɛp daʊn / (BrE)
to resign or leave an important job or official position, especially so someone else can take over
resign from a position

Examples

After years as CEO, she decided to step down and let someone else lead the company.
Facing mounting criticism, the official finally chose to step down from his post.
The coach announced he would step down at the end of the season.

Synset Examples

The Secretary of the Navy will leave office next month
The chairman resigned over the financial scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to formally leave a job or position
  • Synonym: stand down - to leave a position or post, especially an official one

2. VERB

/ stɛp daʊn / (AmE), / stɛp daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the level, intensity, size, or scope of something.
reduce intensity or level

Examples

The government will step down restrictions now that the infection rate has fallen.
To prevent overheating, they decided to step down the power output.
You need to step down the voltage before plugging in that device.

Synset Examples

de-escalate a crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: de-escalate - to make (something) less intense or serious; to reduce the intensity of a conflict or situation
  • Antonym: step up - to increase the amount, level, or intensity of something

3. VERB

/ stɛp daʊn / (AmE), / stɛp daʊn / (BrE)
to resign from or leave an important position or job, especially one of authority
resign; leave a position of authority

Examples

After years of dedicated service, she decided to step down as chairperson.
Amid controversy, the official was pressured to step down from his post.
The company announced that their CEO would step down next month.

Synset Examples

After years of service, she decided to step down.
The CEO announced he would step down next month.
He was asked to step down from the committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to voluntarily leave a job or position
  • Degree Lower: retire - to leave one's job and stop working, often due to age
