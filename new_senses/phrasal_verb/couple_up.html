to couple up

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌpəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkʌpəl ʌp / (BrE)
to join or connect two things together; to link together as a pair.
connect or join together

Examples

Please couple up the cars before starting the train.
The engineers will couple up the two machines to improve efficiency.
We need to couple up these devices to make the system work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connect - to join together; to link

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈkʌpəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkʌpəl ʌp / (BrE)
to begin a romantic relationship with someone; to become a couple.
begin a romantic relationship
informal

Examples

After months of flirting, they finally decided to couple up and move in together.
At the party, several people seemed ready to couple up and leave with someone.
When teenagers coupled up at school, teachers sometimes worried about gossip and distractions.
