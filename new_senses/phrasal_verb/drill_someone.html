to drill (someone)

1. VERB

/ drɪl ˈsʌmwʌn / (AmE), / drɪl ˈsʌmwən / (BrE)
to make someone practice intensively and repeatedly in order to improve a skill
make someone practice intensively

Examples

She would drill her students with difficult math problems before exams.
The coach decided to drill the team on their defensive plays every afternoon.
The language tutor regularly drilled us on our pronunciation skills.

Synset Examples

The teacher decided to drill the students on their math skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - train someone to improve skills through instruction and practice
  • Synonym: train - teach someone a particular skill by practice and instruction
