to drop off

1. VERB

/ drɑp ɔf / (AmE), / drɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to retreat, withdraw, or move back from a position or place, especially gradually or unexpectedly
retreat or move back

Examples

As the enemy advanced, the soldiers had to drop off and regroup further back.
Attendance tends to drop off in the late afternoon once the main events are over.
The hikers decided to drop off from the steep ridge as night approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw or move back, especially after a defeat

/ drɑp ɔf / (AmE), / drɒp ɒf / (BrE)

informal

Examples

I must have dropped off for a minute because I missed the start of the film.
She dropped off on the sofa while waiting for his call.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to fall asleep - to enter into the state of sleep
