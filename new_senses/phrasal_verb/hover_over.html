to hover over

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an object or person) to stay in one place in the air above something or someone, often by moving only slightly.
stay in one place in the air above

Examples

A helicopter began to hover over the accident site as rescuers arrived.
I saw a butterfly hover over the blooming flowers in the garden.
The magician made the ball hover over the table, amazing the audience.

Synset Examples

He watched the drone hover over the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hover - to stay in one place in the air

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8h\u028cv\u025a \u02c8o\u028av\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8h\u028cv\u0259 \u02c8\u0259\u028av\u0259 / (BrE)
to move and hold the computer pointer or cursor over a screen element (such as an icon, link, or button) so that additional information appears or an effect is triggered.
hold cursor over a UI element

Examples

If you hover over the help icon, a tooltip appears with extra guidance.
On the website, she accidentally hovered over the menu and a submenu opened immediately.
Try to hover over the highlighted area to see the image caption without clicking.

Synset Examples

If you hover over the help icon, a tooltip appears with extra guidance.
On the website, she accidentally hovered over the menu and a submenu opened immediately.
Try to hover over the highlighted area to see the image caption without clicking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse over - to move the pointer over a UI element to trigger an event or show information; often written 'mouseover'.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'hover' (to remain in one place in the air), applied to the cursor in graphical user interfaces from the late 20th century.
