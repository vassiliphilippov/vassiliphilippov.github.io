to pop off

1. VERB

/ pɑp ɑf / (AmE), / pɒp ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) To leave a place quickly or suddenly, often without notice.
leave quickly; go away suddenly
informal

Examples

I'm just going to pop off home for lunch and come back later.
She had to pop off before the meeting ended to catch her train.
Without saying goodbye, he decided to pop off as soon as the concert finished.
