to off board

1. VERB

/ ɑf bɔrd / (AmE), / ɒf bɔːd / (BrE)
to remove or dismiss someone from a position or system, or to transition them out of a role, organization, or process
remove or transition someone out of a role

Examples

After the merger, the company had to off board several redundant employees.
It's important to properly off board team members to ensure a smooth transition.
Our IT department will off board your account at the end of your contract.

Synset Examples

It's time to off board the contractors we no longer need.
The new employees were quickly off board with the training.
They decided to off board the outdated software from the system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onboard - to integrate or acclimate someone into a new role or organization
