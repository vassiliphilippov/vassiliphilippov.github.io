to put to sleep

1. VERB

/ pʊt tə sliːp / (AmE), / pʊt tə sliːp / (BrE)
to help someone, especially a child, go to bed and fall asleep
help someone go to bed and sleep

Examples

Every night, she reads a story to put to sleep her daughter.
He gently rocked the baby to put to sleep after feeding.
Nurses sometimes put to sleep the children in the hospital ward by singing softly.

Synset Examples

2. VERB

/ pʊt tə sliːp / (AmE), / pʊt tə sliːp / (BrE)
to humanely kill (an animal), especially by giving it a drug, usually to relieve suffering
euthanize (an animal) painlessly

Examples

After a long illness, they made the difficult decision to put to sleep their beloved dog.
The veterinarian explained why it was kinder to put to sleep the suffering animal.
When her cat was no longer responding to treatment, she chose to put it to sleep to end its pain.

Synset Examples

the cat was very ill and we had to put it to sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euthanize - to put an animal to death humanely
  • Synonym: put down - to kill an animal humanely, especially when sick or old

3. VERB

/ pʊt tə sliːp / (AmE), / pʊt tə sliːp / (BrE)
to cause to become unconscious, especially by anesthesia; to euthanize an animal humanely; or to cause someone to forget or stop thinking about something.
make unconscious, euthanize, or cause to forget

Examples

After the accident, the doctor decided to put her to sleep before operating.
He tries to put to sleep the painful memories of his childhood.
It was heartbreaking when we had to put our cat to sleep last year.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euthanize - to humanely end the life of an animal or person
  • Synonym: anesthetize - to make someone unconscious for surgery with anesthesia
