to fold up

1. VERB

/ foʊld ʌp / (AmE), / fəʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to bend or lay something so that one part covers another; to make compact by folding
to make (something) compact by folding

Examples

Please fold up the map before you put it back in the drawer.
She folded up the blanket and placed it on the shelf.
The chairs are easy to fold up and store when not in use.

Synset Examples

fold up the newspaper
turn up your collar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - to bend something over on itself

2. VERB

/ foʊld ʌp / (AmE), / fəʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to bend or collapse into a compact form, especially by means of hinges or joints; to become folded up
to become folded or collapsed

Examples

Many strollers fold up for convenient transport.
The lawn chair is easy to fold up and store in the garage.
When the table is not in use, it can fold up against the wall.

Synset Examples

The bed folds in a jiffy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfold - to open or spread out something that was folded

Examples
