to hold my alcohol

1. VERB

/ hoʊld maɪ ˈælkəhɔl / (AmE), / həʊld maɪ ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
to be able to drink alcoholic beverages without becoming drunk or ill
tolerate drinking alcohol well
informal

Examples

Despite being small in stature, he can really hold his alcohol at parties.
I know my limits—I just can't hold my alcohol after two beers.
She can't hold her alcohol as well as she used to.

Synset Examples

After three drinks, I realized I couldn't hold my alcohol tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle one's liquor - to be able to drink alcohol without obvious negative effects

2. VERB NEW

/ hoʊld maɪ ˈælkəhɔl / (AmE), / həʊld maɪ ˈælkəhɔl / (BrE)
(informal, internet) (said before doing something daring or reckless) a humorous or ironic request to someone to take one's drink while the speaker attempts a bold action; used as a signal of intent to perform a stunt or show off.
signal before attempting a reckless stunt (meme)
informal

Examples

Before attempting the roof jump, he grinned and said, hold my alcohol, then leapt.
When the crowd booed, she laughed, asked someone to hold my alcohol, and climbed the fence.
Seeing the broken ladder, he shrugged and offered, hold my alcohol, before starting the stunt.
