2. VERB NEW

/ nɑk ʌp / (AmE), / nɒk ʌp / (BrE)
to make or prepare something quickly and simply, often from improvised or readily available materials.
make or prepare quickly
informal british

Examples

He managed to knock up a temporary shelter using tarps and scavenged poles before nightfall.
Could you knock up a quick salad from leftovers for tonight's guests?
They knocked up a makeshift desk from plywood and crates so she could work at home.
