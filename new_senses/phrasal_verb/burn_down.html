to burn down

1. VERB

/ bɝn daʊn / (AmE), / bɜːn daʊn / (BrE)
to be completely destroyed or consumed by fire
be destroyed by fire

Examples

If left unattended, a campfire can quickly burn down an entire forest.
Several houses burned down during the wildfires that swept through the region.
The old theater burned down last night after a short circuit started a fire.

Synset Examples

The hut burned down
The mountain of paper went up in flames

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn up - be destroyed or consumed by fire; often used for objects or smaller-scale destruction

2. VERB

/ bɝːn daʊn / (AmE), / bɜːn daʊn / (BrE)
to destroy or be destroyed by fire, especially so that only the foundation or remains are left
destroy by fire

Examples

Firefighters raced to stop the forest from burning down the nearby village.
The arsonist admitted he had burned down several abandoned buildings.
The old barn finally burned down after the lightning strike.

Synset Examples

They burned the house and his diaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set fire to - to deliberately start something burning
  • Antonym: extinguish - to put out a fire; cause to stop burning

3. VERB NEW

/ b\u025dn da\u028an / (AmE), / b\u025c\u02d0n da\u028an / (BrE)
(in Agile software development and project management) to reduce the amount of remaining work, tasks, or backlog items, typically tracked on a burn-down chart.
reduce remaining work (Agile)

Examples

In agile practice, the team aims to burn down the remaining backlog over the current sprint.
By the end of the day the developers had burned down several small tasks from the list.
The sprint review showed we were burning down work faster than the stakeholders expected.
