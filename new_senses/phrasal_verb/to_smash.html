to to smash

1. VERB

/ tə smæʃ / (AmE), / tə smæʃ / (BrE)
to ruin or destroy completely; to cause something to fail utterly
ruin or destroy completely

Examples

A single mistake was enough to smash all of her dreams.
The scandal smashed his reputation and ended his political career.
Their hopes were smashed after the unexpected defeat.

Synset Examples

He put his plans all to smash with that decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreck - to severely damage or destroy
  • Synonym: shatter - to break or destroy suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: save - to rescue from harm or destruction
