to create friction

1. VERB

/ kriˈeɪt ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / kriːˈeɪt ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)
to cause disagreement, tension, or conflict between people or groups
cause disagreement or tension

Examples

Different management styles can create friction between departments.
Her critical remarks often create friction within the team.
Unclear expectations sometimes create friction among colleagues.

Synset Examples

Their differing opinions created friction during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause tension - to create feelings of strain or conflict
  • Antonym: smooth things over - to make a situation less tense or serious

2. VERB NEW

/ krice6at cfr6ak8359n / (AmE), / krid0ce6at cfr6ak8359n / (BrE)
to cause resistance when two surfaces move against each other; to make two surfaces rub together so they resist motion.
cause physical resistance between surfaces

Examples

To test wear, rub the surfaces to create friction between them.
Designers sometimes add textured patterns so tires create friction that improves grip on wet roads.
When two metals slide without oil, they can create friction and heat that damages components.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase friction - to make the resistance between surfaces greater, often by changing texture or material
  • Antonym: lubricate - to apply a substance (like oil) to reduce friction between moving parts
