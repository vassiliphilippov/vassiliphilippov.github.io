to flash around

1. VERB

/ flæʃ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / flæʃ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to display or show off something, especially expensive or impressive possessions, in a conspicuous or boastful manner
show off possessions ostentatiously
informal

Examples

He loves to flash around his expensive watch at every opportunity.
Instead of saving money, she prefers to flash around her latest purchases.
Some people flash around their achievements to make others feel inferior.

Synset Examples

She likes to flash around her designer handbag.
He would flash around his new phone to impress friends.
Stop trying to flash around your wealth; it’s unnecessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show off - behave in a way meant to attract attention or admiration

2. VERB NEW

/ flæʃ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / flæʃ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of news, images, or other content) to spread or circulate quickly among people or across the internet.
spread quickly (online)
informal

Examples

Short, funny clips flash around social media within hours of being uploaded.
After the scandal, the video flashed around the internet before officials issued a statement.
By midnight the photo was flashing around several community forums and chat groups.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to move or be transmitted to many people or places
  • Synonym: circulate - to pass from person to person or place to place, especially information or media
