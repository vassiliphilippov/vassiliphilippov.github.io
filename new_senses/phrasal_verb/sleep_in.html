to sleep in

1. VERB

/ sliːp ɪn / (AmE), / sliːp ɪn / (BrE)
to sleep later than usual in the morning, especially by choice
sleep later than usual

Examples

After a late night out, we all decided to sleep in the next day.
I like to sleep in on Saturdays when I don't have to work.
She accidentally slept in and missed her morning class.

Synset Examples

On Sundays, I sleep in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie in - stay in bed and rest or sleep longer than usual

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sliːp ɪn / (AmE), / sliːp ɪn / (BrE)
(of a domestic worker) to live in the house where one works, rather than coming and going each day.
live at place of work (domestic)

Examples

As a nanny, Maria used to sleep in at her employer's house during the week.
Most of the housekeepers sleep in to be available early in the morning.
They offered me extra pay if I would sleep in while the family was away.

Synset Examples

our babysitter lives in, as it is too far to commute for her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live in - to reside at the place where one is employed, especially as a domestic worker

3. VERB

/ sliːp ɪn / (AmE), / sliːp ɪn / (BrE)
to wake up later than usual or stay in bed longer than usual, especially in the morning
wake up later than usual

Examples

I usually sleep in on Saturdays when I don't have work.
On holidays, the whole family enjoys sleeping in and having a late breakfast.
She accidentally slept in and missed her morning class.

Synset Examples

He loves to sleep in on the weekends.
He decided to sleep in rather than go for a run.
I love to sleep in on weekends without any plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie in - to remain in bed after the normal time for getting up
