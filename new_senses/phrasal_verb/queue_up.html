to queue up

1. VERB

/ kjuː ʌp / (AmE), / kjuː ʌp / (BrE)
to form a line or wait in a queue, often for something or to enter a place
form a line; wait in a queue

Examples

At the cinema, customers queue up to buy popcorn and drinks.
People began to queue up outside the bakery before it opened.
We had to queue up for over an hour to get tickets to the concert.

Synset Examples

Customers lined up in front of the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line up - to form a line or queue

2. VERB NEW

/ kjuː ʌp / (AmE), / kjuː ʌp / (BrE)
to add or arrange one or more items (such as tasks, media, or jobs) into a queue so they will be executed, played, or processed later.
add items to a queue

Examples

The media player will queue up the next video to play automatically.
She queued up several songs for the party before the guests arrived.
Can you queue up the print jobs so they run without manual interruption?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enqueue - to add an item to the end of a queue (especially in computing).
