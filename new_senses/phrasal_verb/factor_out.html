to factor out

1. VERB

/ ˈfæktɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfæktə aʊt / (BrE)
to consider an element as irrelevant or remove it from consideration when making a decision or calculation
exclude from consideration

Examples

If we factor out one-time events, the company's profits have been stable.
She factored out personal bias to ensure her judgment was fair and objective.
When analyzing sales trends, economists often factor out seasonal variations to see the real changes.

Synset Examples

You must factor in the recent developments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: factor in - to include as a relevant element in a decision or calculation

Etymology

From noun 'factor' (one which contributes to a result) and phrasal verb construction with 'out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00642312-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfæktɚ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfæktə aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve (a mathematical expression or number) into its factors; to express as a product of factors.
resolve into factors (math)

Examples

If you factor out x from 3x + 6x^2, you get x(3 + 6x).
Modern algorithms can factor out very large numbers much more efficiently than before.
The teacher showed us how to factor out common terms in the equation.

Synset Examples

a quantum computer can factor the number 15

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - to resolve (a number or expression) into factors (math)
  • Antonym: to expand - to multiply out an expression (opposite process in algebra)

Etymology

From 'factor' (influence, element; from Latin *factor*, 'maker') + 'out' as a verbal particle; mathematical usage since the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00642047-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8f\u00e6kt\u025a a\u028at / (AmE), / \u02c8f\u00e6kt\u0259 a\u028at / (BrE)
(in software development) to refactor code by extracting duplicated or shared code into a separate function, module, or component to reduce repetition and improve structure.
extract shared code (software)

Examples

The team decided to factor out the shared authentication logic into a separate module.
She factored out duplicate validation code to make the application easier to maintain.
While factoring out common functions, they found a bug in the error handler.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refactor - to change code structure without changing its external behavior, often to improve clarity or reduce duplication
  • Synonym: extract - to take a part out of a larger piece (in programming, move code to a separate location)

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the mathematical phrase 'factor out' (take a common factor), applied in programming to mean extracting common code.
