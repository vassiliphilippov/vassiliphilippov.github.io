to shake hands

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk hændz / (AmE), / ʃeɪk hændz / (BrE)
to take someone's hand and move it up and down as a gesture of greeting, farewell, agreement, or congratulation
to greet or congratulate by clasping hands

Examples

After agreeing to the deal, they shook hands to seal their partnership.
At the end of the meeting, both leaders shook hands in front of the cameras.
She always shakes hands with new colleagues on their first day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greet - to give a polite word or sign of welcome when meeting someone
  • Synonym: wave - move one's hand to and fro as a greeting or signal

3. VERB NEW

/ \u0283e\u026ak h\u00e6ndz / (AmE), / \u0283e\u026ak h\u00e6ndz / (BrE)
(in computing, networking, and security) to perform a protocol handshake; to exchange messages between systems to establish a connection or to agree on session parameters (for example, encryption keys).
perform a protocol handshake

Examples

During the TLS negotiation, the client and server shake hands to agree on encryption keys.
After the initial packets timed out, the devices finally shook hands and resumed secure communication.
When diagnosing connection failures, confirm the endpoints are shaking hands before checking application layers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handshake - an exchange of messages used to establish a network connection or negotiate parameters
  • Synonym: establish (a connection) - to set up a communication link between systems so they can exchange data

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the physical gesture 'shake hands', applied to the exchange of messages that 'agree' connection terms.
