to edge up

1. VERB

/ ɛdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / ɛdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to move gradually and carefully into a space, often by pushing or maneuvering oneself forward
move gradually into a space

Examples

He tried to edge up to the podium for a better view.
People continued to edge up closer to the stage as the concert began.
She managed to edge up to the front of the crowded elevator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge in - to move gradually into a space or position, often with some effort

2. VERB NEW

/ ɛdʒ ʌp / (AmE), / ɛdʒ ʌp / (BrE)
to increase slowly or slightly, especially used for prices, amounts, or rates.
increase slightly

Examples

The stock index continued to edge up gradually after the market rally.
Temperatures are expected to edge up a few degrees by the weekend.
Sales began to edge up slowly after the new advertising campaign launched.
