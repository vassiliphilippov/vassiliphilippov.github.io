to drop in

1. VERB

/ drɑp ɪn / (AmE), / drɒp ɪn / (BrE)
to visit someone informally and without making arrangements in advance
visit informally or unplanned

Examples

Feel free to drop in any time if you're in the neighborhood.
My friends often drop in for a quick chat after work.
We decided to drop in on Sarah without calling first.

Synset Examples

We frequently drop by the neighbors' house for a cup of coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop by - to visit informally and without prior arrangement

2. VERB

/ drɑp ɪn / (AmE), / drɒp ɪn / (BrE)
to visit someone informally and without prior notice
visit informally without notice
informal

Examples

He didn't want to drop in on his boss without calling first.
Neighbours often drop in unannounced for a quick chat.
Why don't you drop in for a cup of tea this afternoon?

Synset Examples

She decided to drop in unexpectedly on her friend.
Feel free to drop in anytime during business hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call in - to make a short, informal visit
  • Synonym: stop by - to pay a brief, informal visit
