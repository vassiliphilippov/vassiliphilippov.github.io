to collaborate with

1. VERB

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪt wɪθ / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪt wɪð / (BrE)
to work together with one or more people or organizations, especially on a specific project or activity
work jointly with others

Examples

Many authors collaborate with illustrators to create children's books.
Our company is collaborating with several nonprofits on this community project.
The scientist decided to collaborate with researchers from other universities to broaden the study.

Synset Examples

The rapper collaborated with several artists on his latest album.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperate with - to work together for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'collaborare' (to work together) + English preposition 'with'.

2. VERB NEW

/ kəˈlæbəreɪt wɪð / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪt wɪð / (BrE)
to work jointly with a non-human agent (such as an AI system, software, or automated tool), treating it as a partner or co-creator in tasks like content generation, analysis, or decision-making.
work jointly with AI or software

Examples

Researchers increasingly collaborate with AI systems to co-author articles and speed up analysis.
She prefers to collaborate with ocean-modeling software when predicting storm surges.
During the design sprint, the team collaborated with an AI assistant to generate layout ideas.

Synset Examples

Researchers increasingly collaborate with AI systems to co-author articles and speed up analysis.
She prefers to collaborate with ocean-modeling software when predicting storm surges.
During the design sprint, the team collaborated with an AI assistant to generate layout ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperate with - to work together for a common purpose; similar general meaning but typically implies other people or organizations.
  • Synonym: partner with - to form a partnership with someone or something to achieve a goal, often used for formal or business collaborations.

Etymology

From Latin 'collaborare' (to work together) + English preposition 'with'.
