to step in

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɪn / (AmE), / stɛp ɪn / (BrE)
to take someone else’s place, often temporarily, especially to help or act as a substitute.
act as a substitute or replacement

Examples

A substitute teacher will step in if the regular teacher is absent.
He offered to step in and look after the children while she was at work.
When the manager was away, Sarah had to step in and lead the meeting.

Synset Examples

She stood in for the soprano who suffered from a cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand in - act as a substitute for someone else
  • Synonym: fill in - to do someone’s job temporarily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02399804-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɛp ɪn / (AmE), / stɛp ɪn / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation, especially to prevent something from happening or to help resolve a problem
to intervene in a situation

Examples

Parents may sometimes step in if teachers cannot resolve a dispute.
She stepped in to help when she saw her friend struggling.
The manager had to step in when the argument got out of hand.

Synset Examples

Why did the U.S. not intervene earlier in WW II?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intervene - to become involved in order to change what is happening
  • Synonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02544431-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ stɛp ɪn / (AmE), / stɛp ɪn / (BrE)
(in computing, especially debugging) to move the program's execution into a called function or method so its code runs step-by-step under inspection by the debugger.
enter a called function in a debugger

Examples

When the function call starts, you can step in to examine local variables and execution flow.
She stepped in with the debugger to trace why the program crashed during the recursive call.
Use the debugger's 'Step' button to keep stepping in until you reach the faulty line of code.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: step into - enter the execution of a called function during debugging (often used interchangeably with this sense).
  • Antonym: to step over - execute a function call without entering its internal code (a debugger command that contrasts with 'step in').
