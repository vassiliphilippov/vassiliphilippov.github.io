to shoot a line

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt ə laɪn / (AmE), / ʃuːt ə laɪn / (BrE)
to talk boastfully, often exaggerating one's achievements or abilities; to show off through talk.
boast or show off through talk
informal

Examples

Don't be fooled—she's just shooting a line to impress everyone.
Jake likes to shoot a line about his days as a football star.
You could tell he was shooting a line when he talked about his overseas adventures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show off - behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
  • Synonym: brag - talk with excessive pride about oneself or achievements

2. VERB NEW

/ ʃuːt ə laɪn / (AmE), / ʃuːt ə laɪn / (BrE)
(informal) to send a short message to someone, especially by phone, text, or email; to contact briefly.
send a short message
informal

Examples

If you have a moment, shoot a line to me with your update.
Before you leave town, shoot a line and tell me when you'll be back.
I couldn't reach him, so I asked a friend to shoot a line to check.
