just in time

1. ADV

/ dʒʌst ɪn taɪm / (AmE), / dʒʌst ɪn taɪm / (BrE)
arriving or happening at the last possible moment, before it is too late
at the last possible moment

Examples

He finished his assignment just in time for the deadline.
She arrived just in time to catch the bus before it left.
The firefighters got there just in time to save the building.

Synset Examples

Here you are, just in time for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: too late - after the time when something could be done; no longer possible
