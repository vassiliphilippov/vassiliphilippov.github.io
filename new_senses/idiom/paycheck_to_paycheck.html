paycheck to paycheck

1. ADV

/ ˈpeɪˌtʃɛk tə ˈpeɪˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtʃɛk tə ˈpeɪtʃɛk / (BrE)
with little or no savings, relying entirely on one's next wage or salary payment to meet expenses; lacking financial security
living with no financial safety net

Examples

After being laid off, she was forced to live paycheck to paycheck for several months.
It can be challenging to save if you’re living paycheck to paycheck.
Many Americans are stuck in jobs that keep them paycheck to paycheck.

Synset Examples

After losing his job, he started to live paycheck to paycheck.
Many workers today are forced to live paycheck to paycheck.
It's stressful for families who live paycheck to paycheck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand to mouth - having only enough money to cover immediate needs; lacking savings

used to describe an economy, society, or group characterized by widespread financial insecurity, where many people have little or no savings and depend on each pay period to meet expenses.
describing an insecure economy or society

Examples

The report warned that the city had become a paycheck to paycheck economy after years of stagnant wages.
Many small towns are now part of a paycheck to paycheck society with little emergency savings.
Policymakers discussed measures to help families escape the paycheck to paycheck reality across regions.
