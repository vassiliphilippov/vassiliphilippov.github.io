wash up

1. NOUN NEW

/ wɑʃ ʌp / (AmE), / wɒʃ ʌp / (BrE)
(chiefly British, politics) the short period at the end of a parliamentary session when remaining bills and business are negotiated and hurriedly completed, often by last-minute deals.
final parliamentary negotiations (UK)
british

Examples

During the final wash up in Parliament, key bills were rushed through.
Ministers met late to negotiate the details during the wash up before prorogation.
The party used the last two days of the wash up to secure agreement on funding.
