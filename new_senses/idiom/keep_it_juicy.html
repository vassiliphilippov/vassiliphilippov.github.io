to keep it juicy

1. VERB

/ kip ɪt ˈdʒusi / (AmE), / kiːp ɪt ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
to maintain food's moisture during cooking so that it remains tender and succulent
maintain moisture in food during cooking

Examples

Chefs use marinades to keep it juicy and add extra flavor to the meat.
Cooking the steak over high heat for a short time can keep it juicy inside.
To keep it juicy, baste the turkey every 30 minutes during roasting.

Synset Examples

The chef recommends cooking the chicken with whole skin to keep it juicy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succulent - food that is juicy and tender
  • Antonym: dry out - to lose moisture and become dry (in cooking)

2. VERB NEW

/ kip ɪt ˈdʒusi / (AmE), / kiːp ɪt ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
to keep a story, report, or other content deliberately vivid, sensational, or attention-grabbing in order to maintain audience interest.
sensationalize or make content engaging
informal

Examples

Editors told the columnist to keep it juicy so readers would not stop scrolling.
She decided to keep it juicy by adding a surprising twist to the interview.
Podcasters often keep it juicy when discussing celebrity rumors to boost audience numbers.

Synset Examples
