catch me if you can

1. X

/ kætʃ mi ɪf ju kæn / (AmE), / katʃ mi ɪf ju kan / (BrE)
Used to challenge someone to try to catch or overtake you, often playfully or defiantly.
challenge to catch or follow

Examples

As the children played in the yard, one shouted, 'Catch me if you can!' before sprinting away.
During the chase, she yelled back at her friend, 'catch me if you can!'
He sent a cryptic message to the police, taunting them with 'catch me if you can'.

Synset Examples

Catch me if you can, I’m going for a run!

2. PROPN NEW

/ kætʃ mi ɪf ju kæn / (AmE), / kætʃ mi ɪf ju kæn / (BrE)
The title of a 2002 American biographical film directed by Steven Spielberg, starring Leonardo DiCaprio and Tom Hanks.
2002 film title

Examples

Last night I finally watched Catch Me If You Can and thought DiCaprio gave a brilliant performance.
The professor showed a clip from Catch Me If You Can to illustrate confidence and deception.
They planned a movie night and included Catch Me If You Can on the shortlist.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a motion picture; used here to indicate this phrase names a movie

Etymology

From the idiomatic phrase 'catch me if you can'; adopted as the title of the 2002 film directed by Steven Spielberg.
