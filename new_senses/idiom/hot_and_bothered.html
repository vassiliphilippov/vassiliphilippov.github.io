hot and bothered

1. ADJ

/ hɑt ənd bəˈðɚd / (AmE), / hɒt ənd bəˈðəd / (BrE)
feeling anxious, upset, or agitated, especially because of embarrassment, excitement, or stress
agitated or flustered
informal

Examples

Don't get hot and bothered about small mistakes—everyone makes them.
He became hot and bothered when he couldn't find his keys.
She was getting hot and bothered over the upcoming presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattled - nervous or confused, especially suddenly
  • Antonym: calm - not showing agitation or nervousness
