to go nuclear

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈnuːkliɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈnjuːkliə / (BrE)
to react with extreme anger or aggression; to respond in an intense and explosive manner
react extremely angrily or aggressively
informal

Examples

After learning he'd been betrayed, he decided to go nuclear and call everyone out.
If you keep provoking her, she might go nuclear during the meeting.
When the manager saw the mistake, he practically went nuclear in front of the whole team.

Synset Examples

If they keep lying, I might just go nuclear.
When he found out, he threatened to go nuclear on everyone.
She decided to go nuclear after being pushed too far.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose it - to suddenly lose control of one's emotions, especially anger
  • Synonym: fly off the handle - to become suddenly and extremely angry

Etymology

From nuclear (relating to atomic energy or weapons), alluding to the destructive power of nuclear weapons.

2. VERB NEW

/ \u0261o\u028a \u02c8nu\u02d0kli\u025a / (AmE), / \u0261\u0259\u028a \u02c8nju\u02d0kli\u0259 / (BrE)
(of a country, company, or organization) to decide to adopt nuclear power or to authorize the construction or operation of nuclear reactors as a major energy source.
adopt nuclear power

Examples

After years of debate, the government announced it would go nuclear to secure energy supplies.
Several coastal nations are seriously going nuclear by investing in small modular reactors for clean power.
After the crisis, the city went nuclear, approving plans for two new reactors to stabilize the grid.

Synset Examples

After years of debate, the government announced it would go nuclear to secure energy supplies.
Several coastal nations are seriously going nuclear by investing in small modular reactors for clean power.
After the crisis, the city went nuclear, approving plans for two new reactors to stabilize the grid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adopt nuclear power - to begin using nuclear energy as part of a country's or company's energy mix
  • Synonym: commission reactors - to authorize and bring nuclear reactors into operational service

Etymology

From adjective 'nuclear' (relating to atomic energy); literal extension meaning 'to adopt nuclear power'.
