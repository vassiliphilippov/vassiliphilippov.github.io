off one's feed

1. ADJ

/ ɔf wʌnz fid / (AmE), / ɒf wʌnz fiːd / (BrE)
not feeling well or having lost one's appetite; lacking usual interest or energy, especially due to illness or sadness
lacking appetite due to not feeling well
informal

Examples

After hearing the sad news, she was clearly off her feed and quiet all day.
Ever since he caught that cold, he's been off his feed and doesn't want dinner.
If your dog seems off its feed, you should probably call the vet.

Synset Examples

He looked off his feed after the bad news.
She felt off her feed and skipped the gym.
You seem off your feed; are you feeling okay?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose one's appetite - to no longer feel hungry or interested in eating

Etymology

Originally from livestock terminology, where animals refusing to eat were said to be 'off their feed.'

Informal: not actively using, checking, or engaging with one's online content feed (for example, on social media); temporarily absent from or ignoring updates and posts.
not engaging with social-media feed
informal

Examples

After taking a break, she was off her feed and didn't check Instagram for days.
Following the scandal, several journalists went off their feed and refused to repost unverified claims.
During holidays, he tries to be off his feed to spend time with family and relax.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

Originally from livestock terminology (animals refusing to eat); later extended metaphorically from physical 'feed' to the electronic 'feed' (content stream) in the 21st century.
