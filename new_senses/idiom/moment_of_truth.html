moment of truth

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv truθ / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv truːθ / (BrE)
a time when a person or thing is tested, or when an important decision must be made
decisive or critical moment

Examples

As the exam began, John realized the moment of truth had finally arrived.
The project’s moment of truth came when the prototype was revealed to investors.
When she stepped onto the stage, it was her moment of truth as a performer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch time - a critical period for making decisions or taking action
  • Synonym: turning point - the time at which a decisive change in a situation occurs

Etymology

Originally from Spanish "el momento de la verdad," a term used in bullfighting for the final act.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt əv truːθ / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt əv truːθ / (BrE)
a time when a person or situation is put to the ultimate test or an important decision or revelation is made
critical time for decision or revelation

Examples

After months of preparation, the moment of truth finally arrived for the athletes.
The moment of truth came when the jury announced its verdict.
When she opened the exam envelope, it was truly the moment of truth.

Synset Examples

The moment of truth arrived during the big presentation.
She dreaded the moment of truth at the final audition.
When the test results came, it was the moment of truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a time at which a decisive change occurs
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger requiring a decision

Etymology

Originally a translation of the Spanish 'el momento de la verdad', used in bullfighting to refer to the final sword thrust.
