to catch the sun

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ðə sʌn / (AmE), / katʃ ðə sʌn / (BrE)
to sunbathe or spend time in sunlight to enjoy its warmth
to sunbathe or enjoy sunlight

Examples

After a long winter, everyone was eager to catch the sun in the park.
Let's go outside and catch the sun before it gets cloudy.
She loves to catch the sun by the pool during summer holidays.

Synset Examples

We set up our towels to catch the sun on the beach.
He painted the garden to catch the sun's rays beautifully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunbathe - to sit or lie in the sun, especially to tan the skin

2. VERB NEW

/ kætʃ ðə sʌn / (AmE), / katʃ ðə sʌn / (BrE)
(of devices or structures) to receive or collect sunlight, especially for converting it into solar energy or heat.
collect sunlight for energy

Examples

The rooftop panels catch the sun efficiently during long summer afternoons to power the whole house.
Last winter the community microgrid caught the sun for longer than expected, boosting stored energy reserves.
By catching the sun along the south-facing facade, the building's collectors lower heating costs significantly.
