clipboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈklɪpˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a small flat board with a clip at the top for holding papers in place for writing or reading
board with a clip for holding papers

Examples

Please attach your resume to the clipboard at the front desk when you arrive.
The coach carried a clipboard to make notes during the game.
The nurse wrote down the patient's information on her clipboard as she walked between rooms.

Etymology

From 'clip' + 'board', indicating its function as a board with a clip.

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8kl6apccb54rd / (AmE), / c8kl6apccb54d0d / (BrE)
a temporary area in a computer's memory where data that has been copied or cut is held until it is pasted elsewhere.
temporary computer storage for copied data

Examples

She copied the quote to the clipboard and pasted it into her report.
When you cut the image, it replaces the previous item on the clipboard immediately.
Multiple apps can access the system clipboard to share copied text or images between programs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasteboard - the term used on some systems (notably macOS) for the clipboard — a temporary area holding copied items
  • Synonym: buffer - a region of memory used to hold data temporarily (clipboard is a specific kind of buffer)

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the physical 'clipboard' (clip + board); used to describe temporary storage for copied items in computing.
