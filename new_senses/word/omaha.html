Omaha

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊ.mə.hɑ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.mə.hɑː / (BrE)
a city in eastern Nebraska, on the Missouri River; the largest city in the state and a major transportation center in the Midwest of the United States
largest city in Nebraska, USA

Examples

After moving to Omaha, she quickly adapted to the city's vibrant culture.
Many Fortune 500 companies have their headquarters in Omaha.
The Missouri River flows along the eastern edge of Omaha.

Etymology

Named after the Omaha people, a Native American tribe native to the region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊməˌhɑ / (AmE), / ˈəʊməˌhɑː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people formerly living in the Missouri River valley in northeastern Nebraska
member of a Native American people

Examples

Many Omaha now live on reservations but maintain a strong cultural identity.
She is researching the language of the Omaha for her anthropology thesis.
The Omaha preserved many of their traditions despite relocation and changing circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - another Native American group of the same language family

Etymology

From the Omaha people's own name Umoⁿhoⁿ, meaning 'upstream people.'
