transaction

1. NOUN

/ trænˈzækʃən / (AmE), / trænˈzækʃən / (BrE)
an instance of carrying out a business or commercial exchange; the act of transacting or conducting business between parties
an act of doing business or an exchange

Examples

After completing the transaction, she received a confirmation email.
Every transaction in the database must be recorded accurately.
The bank charges a small fee for each transaction you make.

Synset Examples

no transactions are possible without him
he has always been honest in his dealings with me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal - an agreement or arrangement, especially in business
  • Synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another
  • Synonym: purchase - the act of buying something

Etymology

From Latin transactio (agreement), from transigere (to drive through, accomplish).

2. NOUN NEW

/ trænˈzækʃən / (AmE), / trænˈzækʃən / (BrE)
(in computing, especially blockchain and cryptocurrencies) a recorded operation that transfers cryptocurrency or tokens between addresses; a discrete unit of data broadcast to the network and later included in a block on the ledger.
blockchain record of value transfer

Examples

The miner confirmed the transaction on the blockchain network this morning.
Users waited as the pending transaction moved into a block and became secure on the ledger.
Each transaction includes sender, recipient, and amount fields that nodes validate before adding it.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: payment - the act of giving money in exchange for goods, services, or debt settlement; often implemented as a transaction in crypto contexts
