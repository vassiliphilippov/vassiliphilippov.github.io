rendering

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛndərɪŋ / (BrE)
A coat of plaster or stucco applied to the surface of a masonry wall, often for protection or decoration.
stucco or plaster on a wall

Examples

After the rendering had dried, the wall was ready to be painted.
Cracks appeared in the rendering due to the house settling over time.
The exterior bricks were covered with a layer of rendering for a smoother finish.

Etymology

From the verb 'render', from Old French 'rendre', from Latin 'reddere' (to give back).
