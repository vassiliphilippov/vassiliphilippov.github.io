Mods

1. NOUN

/ mɑdz / (AmE), / mɒdz / (BrE)
A British youth subculture originating in London in the early 1960s, characterized by fashionable dress, short hair, a fondness for rhythm and blues music, and traveling on motor scooters.
British 1960s youth subculture
british

Examples

A group of Mods gathered at the seaside, dressed impeccably in tailored jackets.
Many Mods were passionate about the latest rhythm and blues records.
The Mods were known for their sharp suits and love of scooter culture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rockers - A 1950s-60s British youth subculture associated with motorcycles and leather jackets, rival to Mods

Etymology

Shortened from modernist, referring to the modern jazz enthusiasts who inspired the subculture.

2. NOUN NEW

/ mɑdz / (AmE), / mɒdz / (BrE)
User-created modifications for video games or other software that change or add content, graphics, gameplay, or mechanics.
user-created game modifications

Examples

Many players download Mods to add new weapons and quests to their favourite RPG.
She learned to create simple Mods that change character skins and user interface icons.
The community rated his Mods highly for improving performance and fixing long-standing bugs.
