dupe

1. NOUN

/ duːp / (AmE), / djuːp / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or tricked, especially into doing something foolish or unlawful
person who is tricked or deceived

Examples

Con artists often search for an easy dupe in crowded tourist spots.
Don't be a dupe; always check the facts before believing wild rumors.
He became an unwitting dupe in their elaborate scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - person harmed or tricked by someone
  • Antonym: swindler - person who cheats or tricks others

Etymology

From French 'dupe' (deceived person), probably from Middle French dupe, huppe (hoopoe, a credulous bird).

2. VERB

/ duːp / (AmE), / djuːp / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone, usually by making them believe something that is not true
to deceive or trick someone

Examples

He realized too late that he had been duped by his supposed friend.
Many customers were duped by the fake advertisement.
The scammers tried to dupe her into revealing her bank details.

Synset Examples

The immigrant was duped because he trusted everyone
You can't fool me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone in order to make them do something
  • Synonym: mislead - to give someone the wrong idea or impression

Etymology

From French 'dupe', meaning a deceived person.

3. NOUN NEW

/ duːp / (AmE), / djuːp / (BrE)
(informal) a cheap imitation or replica of a branded or designer product, sold as a substitute for the original.
cheap imitation of a branded product
informal slang

Examples

She bought a dupe handbag that closely resembled the designer original but cost much less.
Beauty bloggers often review inexpensive dupes of luxury makeup to help readers save money.
If you prefer the look but not the price, consider buying a dupe instead of an authentic purse.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replica - an exact copy or close reproduction of something, often of value or art
  • Antonym: authentic - genuine; not a fake or imitation

Etymology

Shortened from 'duplicate' (copy); modern slang use in fashion and online communities.
