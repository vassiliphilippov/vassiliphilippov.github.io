rendezvous

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːn.deɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.deɪˌvuː / (BrE)
a meeting arranged between two people, especially for a romantic purpose
meeting, often romantic

Examples

Her heart raced as she prepared for the rendezvous with her long-distance partner.
Their regular Friday night rendezvous became a cherished routine.
They agreed to a secret rendezvous at the park after sundown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', meaning 'present yourselves'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːn.deɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.deɪˌvuː / (BrE)
a meeting arranged to occur at a specific time and place, often secretly or for a particular purpose
arranged meeting at a set time and place

Examples

The spy arrived early at the rendezvous point, waiting nervously.
Their secret rendezvous was discovered by the authorities.
We agreed to meet at our usual rendezvous in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - a prearranged meeting, typically formal
  • Synonym: meeting - an act of people coming together by arrangement

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', literally 'present yourselves' (imperative plural of se rendre + vous).
