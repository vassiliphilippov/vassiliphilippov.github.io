nonelective

1. ADJ

/ ˌnʌnɪˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnʌnɪˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of a position or office) filled by appointment rather than by election.
not chosen by election; appointed

Examples

Some government agencies have both elective and nonelective officials.
The city council appointed him to a nonelective position on the board.
Unlike the mayor, the treasurer holds a nonelective office.

Synset Examples

a nonelective office

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elective - appointed or filled by election, not by appointment

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'elective' (subject to election).
