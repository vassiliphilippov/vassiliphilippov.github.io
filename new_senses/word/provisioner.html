provisioner

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪʒənər / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənə / (BrE)
a person or organization responsible for supplying food and other provisions, especially to an army or large group
supplier of food or supplies, esp. to army

Examples

As a provisioner, she managed contracts for food and equipment for the troops.
During wartime, the role of the provisioner became even more critical for survival.
The army relied on their trusted provisioner to deliver rations to the front lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplier - a person or organization that provides something needed

Etymology

From 'provision' + '-er,' modeled after agent noun formation.

2. NOUN NEW

/ prəˈvɪʒənər / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənə / (BrE)
(in computing) a software component or service that automatically allocates, configures, or makes available computing resources (for example, storage volumes, virtual machines, or containers) on demand.
automated supplier of computing resources

Examples

Kubernetes uses a dynamic provisioner to allocate storage volumes on demand.
During deployment, the cloud provisioner automatically created virtual machines and attached networks.
Security audits check that third-party provisioners follow policies when they create user environments.
