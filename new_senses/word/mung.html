mung

1. NOUN

/ mʌŋ / (AmE), / mʌŋ / (BrE)
the small green seed of the mung bean plant, used as food, especially in Asian cuisine
edible seed of the mung bean plant

Examples

In vegetarian dishes, mung often serves as a good source of protein.
Sprouted mung is a popular ingredient in many Asian salads and stir-fries.
The recipe called for a handful of soaked mung to add texture to the stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the plant producing edible mung seeds

Etymology

From Hindi 'mūṅg', from Sanskrit 'mudga' (mung bean).

2. NOUN

/ mʌŋ / (AmE), / mʌŋ / (BrE)
an erect, bushy annual plant (Vigna radiata), widely grown in warm regions for its edible seeds, especially used for bean sprouts in Asian cooking
a bean plant used for edible seeds/sprouts

Examples

Mung is often used to make nutritious bean sprouts in Asian cuisine.
Farmers in the region plant mung as a valuable summer crop.
She soaked the mung beans overnight before cooking them with spices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the edible seed of the mung plant
  • Degree Lower: soybean - another major bean cultivated worldwide for its seeds

Etymology

From Hindi 'mūṅg', via Sanskrit 'mudga' (mung bean).
