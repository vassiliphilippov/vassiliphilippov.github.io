look-alike

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊk əˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈlʊk əˌlaɪk / (BrE)
a person who closely resembles another, especially a famous person
person who closely resembles another

Examples

Several fans thought he was the real celebrity, but he was just a look-alike hired for the event.
She earns a living as a Marilyn Monroe look-alike at parties and events.
The movie needed a convincing look-alike for the action star's dangerous stunts.

Synset Examples

he could be Gingrich's double
she's the very image of her mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - a person who looks like another and may substitute for them
  • Synonym: doppelganger - a person who looks exactly like someone else, often used in folklore

Etymology

From the phrase 'look alike', meaning 'to appear similar'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8l\u028ak \u0259\u02ccla\u026ak / (AmE), / \u02c8l\u028ak \u0259\u02ccla\u026ak / (BrE)
(in digital advertising) an audience segment created to include users who share key characteristics with a brand's existing customers, used to target similar potential customers.
advertising audience similar to existing customers

Examples

The advertisers built a look-alike audience to reach people similar to their top customers.
Facebook's tools let small firms create a look-alike from a list of loyal buyers.
By testing different look-alike settings, the team improved ad performance and reduced cost per conversion.
