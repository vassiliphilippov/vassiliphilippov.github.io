to depersonalise

1. VERB

/ diˈpɝːsənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈpɜːsənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something impersonal or treat someone as an object, removing personal identity or individuality
make impersonal or treat as an object

Examples

Modern technology can sometimes depersonalise communication between people.
Some critics argue that certain medical procedures depersonalise patient care.
The new policies may depersonalise the workplace, making employees feel like numbers rather than individuals.

Synset Examples

Will computers depersonalize human interactions?
Pornography objectifies women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectify - to treat a person as an object or thing
  • Antonym: humanise - to make more humane or personal

Etymology

From de- (removal) + personalise (to make personal); originally from French personnaliser and Latin persona (person).
