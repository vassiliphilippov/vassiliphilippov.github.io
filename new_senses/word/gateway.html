gateway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪtˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪt.weɪ / (BrE)
An entrance, typically one that can be closed with a gate, often marking the access to a particular area.
entrance that can be closed by a gate

Examples

A beautiful archway served as a gateway to the botanical gardens.
The large wooden gateway at the entrance of the estate was locked at night.
They walked through the old stone gateway into the town square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gate - a movable barrier for closing an opening in a fence, wall, or passage

Etymology

From Middle English gatewaye, formed from 'gate' + 'way'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɡeɪtˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪt.weɪ / (BrE)
(computing) A hardware device or software component that acts as an entry point between networks or services, routing, translating, or forwarding requests and data between different systems or protocols.
network entry point or translator (computing)

Examples

The cloud platform uses an API gateway to route requests to microservices.
To secure the network, engineers placed a new gateway between the internal and external servers.
When the protocol changed, the team updated the gateway to translate calls into the new format.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: router - a device that forwards data packets between computer networks, often based on IP addresses
  • Synonym: proxy - a server or service that acts as an intermediary for requests from clients seeking resources from other servers

Etymology

From Middle English gatewaye (gate + way); used metaphorically in computing to mean an entry point between networks.
