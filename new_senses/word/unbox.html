to unbox

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɒks / (BrE)
to remove (something) from a box
remove from a box

Examples

Please unbox all the supplies before the event starts.
She couldn't wait to unbox her birthday gifts in front of everyone.
The new phone was delivered today, and I filmed myself unboxing it.

Synset Examples

unbox the presents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: box - to put (something) in a box; to pack

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not, reverse') + box (noun).

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɒks / (BrE)
to open a new product or package, especially on video or during a live stream, often to review its contents
open a product, especially for an online video

Examples

After receiving the package, they eagerly unboxed the latest headphones during a live stream.
Millions tuned in to watch him unbox the highly anticipated gaming console.
She plans to unbox the new smartphone on her YouTube channel tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpack - to remove from a container or box

Etymology

From prefix un- (reverse an action) + box (to enclose in a box).

(in computing) to convert a boxed or wrapper object to its underlying primitive value, often automatically performed by a programming language (for example, converting an Integer to an int).
convert wrapper object to primitive (computing)

Examples

In Java, the compiler will unbox an Integer automatically when performing arithmetic with ints.
She unboxed the Long object to a long before passing it to the math function.
Modern languages often avoid errors by unboxing wrapper values safely during assignment.
