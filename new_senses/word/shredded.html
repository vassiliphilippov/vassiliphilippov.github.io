shredded

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃrɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of food) prepared by cutting or tearing into thin strips or pieces, especially with a grater.
cut or torn into thin strips (esp. food)

Examples

A bowl of shredded lettuce was served alongside the tacos.
He sprinkled shredded cheese on top of the pasta before baking it.
The recipe calls for two cups of shredded cabbage and one carrot.

Synset Examples

sliced tomatoes
sliced ham
chopped clams
chopped meat
shredded cabbage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sliced - cut into thin, flat pieces
  • Synonym: chopped - cut into small pieces

Etymology

From the verb 'shred,' from Old English 'scrēadian' (to cut or tear into shreds).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈʃrɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃrɛdɪd / (BrE)
informal (slang) having very well-defined muscles; extremely lean and muscular.
very muscular (slang)
informal slang

Examples

After months of training, the athlete looked shredded and ready for competition.
Her new gym routine left her visibly shredded, with clear muscle definition.
Many fitness influencers use supplements claiming to help you get shredded before summer.

Synset Examples

After months of training, the athlete looked shredded and ready for competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripped - having clearly visible, well-defined muscles; very fit
  • Antonym: flabby - having soft, loose flesh and lacking muscle tone; opposite of 'shredded'

Etymology

From the verb 'shred', from Old English 'scrēadian' (to cut into shreds); the slang sense is a recent metaphorical extension referring to a 'cut' or 'ripped' appearance of muscles.
