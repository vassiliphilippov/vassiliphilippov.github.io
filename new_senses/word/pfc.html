PFC

1. NOUN

/ pi ɛf si / (AmE), / piː ɛf siː / (BrE)
a perfluorocarbon; a powerful greenhouse gas emitted during aluminum production and some industrial processes
a greenhouse gas (perfluorocarbon)

Examples

Reducing industrial PFC output is a key step toward environmental sustainability.
Scientists measure PFC concentrations in the atmosphere to monitor climate change.
The aluminum industry is working to lower its PFC emissions to reduce greenhouse gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfluorocarbon - a compound of carbon and fluorine, often used refrigerant or greenhouse gas

2. NOUN NEW

/ pi \u025bf si / (AmE), / pi\u02d0 \u025bf si\u02d0 / (BrE)
(in fusion research and reactor engineering) a plasma-facing component — a part of a fusion reactor that directly contacts or faces the hot plasma and must withstand extreme heat, particle flux, and erosion.
plasma-facing component (fusion reactor)

Examples

The lab team replaced a damaged PFC after the routine plasma discharge experiment.
Engineers inspected the PFCs to ensure each tile could withstand the extreme thermal load.
A worn PFC tile can release impurities that cool and destabilize the confined plasma.
