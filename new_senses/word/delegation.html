delegation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛl.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛl.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of giving someone else, especially a subordinate, the authority to make certain decisions or perform specific tasks on your behalf.
the act of giving authority to subordinates

Examples

Effective delegation helps leaders focus on strategic planning.
He struggled with delegation because he wanted control over every decision.
Managers often practice delegation to improve efficiency within their teams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empowerment - the process of giving someone the power to do something

Etymology

From Latin delegatio (a sending on a mission), from delegare (to send as a representative).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛl.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛl.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a group of people chosen or appointed to represent others, especially at a conference or meeting
group of representatives

Examples

A French delegation attended the peace talks in Geneva.
Each country sent a delegation to participate in the summit.
The conference welcomed a delegation from South Africa for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegates - individuals representing others at a meeting or conference

Etymology

From Latin 'delegatio' (appointment, assignment), from 'delegare' (to delegate).

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccd\u025bl.\u0259\u02c8\u0261e\u026a.\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / \u02ccd\u025bl.\u026a\u02c8\u0261e\u026a.\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
(in software engineering) a design pattern in which an object passes responsibility for a task or behavior to a separate helper object instead of implementing it itself.
software design pattern

Examples

The framework uses delegation to pass tasks to a helper object.
In their codebase, they implemented delegation so different objects handle events cleanly.
During the review, she recommended delegation of authentication logic to a dedicated service.
