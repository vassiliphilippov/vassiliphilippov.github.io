to unroll

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈroʊl / (AmE), / ʌnˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to open or spread out something that has been rolled up, or to become open or spread out in this way
to open or spread out something rolled

Examples

She began to unroll the carpet in the living room.
The flag will unroll as soon as the wind picks up.
With a single motion, he unrolled the map across the table.

Synset Examples

unfurl a banner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to roll up - to form something into a roll or coil
  • Synonym: unfurl - to spread something out after it has been rolled or folded

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (reversing an action) + 'roll' (from Old French 'rolle', from Latin 'rotulus').
