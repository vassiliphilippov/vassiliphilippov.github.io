fetch

1. NOUN

/ fɛtʃ / (AmE), / fɛtʃ / (BrE)
the act of going to get something and bringing it back
the action of going to get something

Examples

After a quick fetch of the papers, he returned to the office.
Our puppy is learning to play fetch with a tennis ball.
The dog loves a good game of fetch in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retrieve - to get something and bring it back

Etymology

From Old English 'feccean', related to the verb 'fetch'.

3. VERB

/ fɛtʃ / (AmE), / fɛtʃ / (BrE)
to go to a place and bring someone or something back
go and bring back

Examples

Could you please fetch my coat from the car?

The children love to play with the dog and make it fetch the ball.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring - to take or go with someone or something to a place
  • Synonym: retrieve - to get something back or bring back

Etymology

From Old English *fetian* or *feohtan* (to fetch, bring), related to Old Frisian *fetsia* and Old High German *fezzan*.
