mailbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlˌbɒks / (BrE)
A private box, typically located outside a home or building, where mail is delivered and collected.
private box for delivery of mail

Examples

After moving to the new apartment, they had to get a new mailbox installed.
Please check the mailbox to see if the letter has arrived.
The mail carrier left a package in our mailbox this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postbox - a public box for posting mail
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03715732-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlˌbɒks / (BrE)
A public box where people can deposit outgoing letters and parcels to be collected and delivered by the postal service.
public box for depositing mail

Examples

If the mailbox is full, postal workers collect its contents several times a day.
She dropped her letter into the mailbox on the corner before heading to work.
The city installed a new mailbox outside the post office to handle increased mail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postbox - public box for depositing outgoing post
  • Synonym: letter box - another term for a public mail deposit box (especially British English)
