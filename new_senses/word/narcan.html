Narcan

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːrˌkæn / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkæn / (BrE)
A brand name for naloxone, a drug used to rapidly reverse the effects of opioid overdose.
brand name for naloxone (opioid overdose antidote)
medical

Examples

In many states, pharmacies now provide Narcan without a prescription.
Thanks to the quick use of Narcan, the patient regained consciousness within minutes.
The paramedic administered Narcan to counteract the effects of an opioid overdose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naloxone - a synthetic drug used as an opioid antagonist

Etymology

Trade name, derived from 'narcotic antagonist.'
