article

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a separate section or clause in a legal document, such as a statute, contract, or will
section of a legal document
legal

Examples

Amendments to article five were discussed in the last board meeting.
Each article of the contract must be carefully reviewed by both parties.
The constitution's first article outlines the powers of the legislative branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clause - distinct section of a legal document

Etymology

From Latin articulus (a joint, a section, a subdivision).

individual item or object (e.g., product, piece)

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a piece of nonfictional writing that forms a separate part of a newspaper, magazine, or other publication
a nonfictional work in a publication

Examples

He wrote a short article explaining the basics of cryptocurrency.
She read an interesting article about renewable energy in the magazine.
The newspaper published a front-page article on the recent election results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essay - short piece of writing on a particular subject
  • Synonym: report - an account describing in detail an event, situation, or the like
  • Antonym: story - narrative, typically fictional; distinguished from article by nonfictional focus

Etymology

From Latin articulus (a joint, division, short clause) via Old French article.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a grammatical word (such as 'the', 'a', or 'an') used with a noun to show whether the noun refers to something specific or unspecific
a grammatical word marking specificity (a/an/the)

Examples

In the sentence 'I saw a dog,' the word 'a' is an indefinite article.
Some languages, such as Russian, do not use an article before nouns.
The definite article 'the' is used when referring to something specific that both speaker and listener know about.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determiner - a word or phrase that introduces a noun and limits or clarifies its meaning

Etymology

From Latin articulus (a small joint, a part, chapter in a book).

5. VERB

/ ˈɑːr.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to bind someone by a formal contract, especially as an apprentice or trainee for a specified period
bind by contract (especially apprenticeship)
archaic legal

Examples

After university, she was articled to a law firm for her professional training.
He agreed to be articled for five years as an apprentice engineer.
Many accountants were once articled clerks before becoming fully qualified.

Etymology

From the noun 'article,' referring to the terms or items in a formal agreement; ultimately from Latin 'articulus' (a joint, division, or item).

Source: WordNet
