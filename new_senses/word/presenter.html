presenter

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɛntər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntə / (BrE)
a person who formally presents something such as a document, speech, petition, or award to others
person who presents something formally

Examples

As the presenter of the annual award, she shared a brief speech before announcing the winner.
Each presenter must ensure their document is signed before submission.
The presenter handed the petition to the mayor during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submitter - a person who submits something for consideration or judgment
  • Antonym: recipient - a person who receives something

Etymology

From present (verb) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈzɛntər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntə / (BrE)
a person who formally introduces or recommends someone, especially for an award, a degree, or an official position.
person who introduces or recommends someone

Examples

As the presenter, she introduced the guest of honor to the audience.
He acted as the presenter during the graduation ceremony, recommending the candidates for their degrees.
The presenter gave a heartfelt speech before announcing the winner of the award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introducer - a person who introduces someone

Etymology

From present + -er, meaning someone who presents.

3. NOUN NEW

/ prɪˈzɛntər / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntə / (BrE)
(in software design) a component in the Model–View–Presenter (MVP) pattern that mediates between the model and the view and contains presentation logic.
software component in MVP pattern

Examples

In the MVP pattern, the presenter coordinates updates between the model and the view.
When testing, we mocked the presenter to verify it handled user actions correctly and updated the view.
The junior developer refactored the presenter to remove UI code and keep business logic in the model.
