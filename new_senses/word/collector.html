collector

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
the electrode in a transistor through which the main current flows out, usually collecting charge carriers from the base region
electrode in a transistor

Examples

Current flows from the emitter to the collector when the base is properly biased.
In this circuit diagram, the collector is connected to the positive voltage supply.
The transistor stopped working because its collector was damaged during assembly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emitter - the electrode in a transistor from which charge carriers enter the base region
  • Synonym: base - the control electrode in a transistor that modulates flow between emitter and collector

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktər / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a person who accumulates or gathers particular items, often as a hobby or for study
person who collects things

Examples

My grandfather is an avid collector of rare stamps from around the world.
She became known as a collector of vintage jewelry after years of searching flea markets.
The museum hired a collector to acquire antique coins for its upcoming exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulator - a person who gathers things together

Etymology

From Latin 'collector', from 'collectus', past participle of 'colligere' (to gather together).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktɚ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect payments, such as rent or taxes, from others
person who collects payments as a job

Examples

A collector may represent the city or a private landlord when collecting payments.
She asked the collector for a receipt after paying her taxes.
The collector visited each property at the end of the month to gather the rent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax collector - a person whose job is to collect taxes
  • Degree Higher: landlord - person who rents out property and often collects rent

Etymology

From Latin colligere (to gather or collect), via Old French collecteur.

4. NOUN NEW

/ kəˈlɛktər / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktə / (BrE)
(in computing) a software component or service that gathers telemetry, logs, metrics, or usage data from devices or applications and forwards them for storage or analysis.
software component that gathers telemetry

Examples

The cloud service uses a collector to gather performance metrics from all nodes.
During troubleshooting, the engineers deployed several collectors to capture logs from the failing microservices.
A lightweight collector running on each device sends telemetry to the central monitoring system every minute.
