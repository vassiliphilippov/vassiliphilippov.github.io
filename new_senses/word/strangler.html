strangler

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person who kills someone by squeezing their throat tightly so that they cannot breathe
person who kills by strangling

Examples

Newspapers dubbed him the 'Midtown Strangler' because of his crimes.
The city was terrified when another strangler attack was reported.
The police finally caught the strangler after months of investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who unlawfully kills another
  • Synonym: killer - a person who kills

Etymology

From 'strangle' (verb) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlə / (BrE)
A tropical vine or tree that grows around and eventually kills its host tree by enclosing its trunk, typically by sending down aerial roots; especially a strangler fig.
plant that kills its host tree by enveloping it

Examples

Over time, the strangler can completely envelop and kill the tree it grows on.
Strangler figs are among the most well-known types of strangler plants in tropical forests.
The strangler slowly wound its roots around the host tree, cutting off its access to nutrients.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈstræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlər / (BrE)
(in software architecture) an approach to migrating or replacing a legacy system gradually by adding new components and routing new functionality to them until the old system can be retired.
software migration pattern

Examples

The team used the strangler pattern to replace the old billing system without downtime.
By routing new requests to microservices, they turned the monolith into a strangler over months.
Our architecture document recommends a strangler approach for gradual migration and risk reduction.
