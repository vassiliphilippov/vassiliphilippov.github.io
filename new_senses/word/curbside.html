curbside

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜːrb.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɜːb.saɪd / (BrE)
The side or edge of a sidewalk or street that is next to the curb.
side or edge next to a curb

Examples

The delivery driver left the package on the curbside for easy pickup.
Trash is collected from the curbside every Tuesday morning in this neighborhood.
We waited by the curbside for our taxi to arrive.

Synset Examples

policemen stood at intervals along the curbside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curb - a raised edge or border of a street, typically of stone or concrete

Etymology

Formed from 'curb' (from Old French courbe, Latin curvus, curved) + 'side'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8k\u025c\u02d0rb.sa\u026ad / (AmE), / \u02c8k\u025c\u02d0b.sa\u026ad / (BrE)
A service option in retail or food service where staff bring a customer's order to their vehicle at the curb for pickup.
pickup service at the curb

Examples

Many stores now offer curbside so customers can collect orders without entering the shop.
She chose curbside pickup and the clerk brought her groceries to the car in minutes.
During busy holiday hours, the restaurant sets aside a lane for curbside customers to collect meals.

Synset Examples

Many stores now offer curbside so customers can collect orders without entering the shop.
She chose curbside pickup and the clerk brought her groceries to the car in minutes.
During busy holiday hours, the restaurant sets aside a lane for curbside customers to collect meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curbside pickup - a service where staff bring purchases to a customer's car at the curb
  • Synonym: click-and-collect - an online order collection service where customers collect purchases from the store or a curbside point

Etymology

From 'curb' + 'side'; extended from the location sense to mean a pickup service at the curb.
