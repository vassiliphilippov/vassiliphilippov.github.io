poke

1. VERB

/ poʊk / (AmE), / pəʊk / (BrE)
to stir or move something by pushing or prodding it, especially with a stick or similar object
stir by prodding or pushing

Examples

Every few minutes, someone would poke the coals to keep them hot.
He poked the ashes to find any remaining embers.
She used a stick to poke the dying fire back to life.

Synset Examples

poke the embers in the fireplace

Etymology

Probably of Middle English origin, akin to Old English *pocian* (to poke, thrust).

2. NOUN

/ poʊk / (AmE), / pəʊk / (BrE)
A tall, coarse North American perennial herb (Phytolacca americana) with small white flowers and blackish-red berries, whose young stems are edible but berries and roots are poisonous.
a North American plant with edible shoots, poisonous berries

Examples

Although the berries of poke are attractive, they are highly poisonous to humans.
The fields behind the house were overgrown with wild poke during the summer.
Young poke shoots are cooked and eaten in some parts of the southern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pokeweed - another term for the plant Phytolacca americana

Etymology

Probably from Virginia Algonquian *pakon* (plant used as food).
