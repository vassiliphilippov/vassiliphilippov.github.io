ketamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtəˌmin / (AmE), / ˈkiːtəmiːn / (BrE)
a powerful general anesthetic and tranquilizer, often used in veterinary medicine and sometimes as a human anesthetic, known for its hallucinogenic effects at high doses
anesthetic and tranquilizer drug
medical

Examples

Doctors sometimes use ketamine as an anesthetic during certain minor medical procedures.
Some recreational users seek the hallucinogenic effects of ketamine, but misuse can be dangerous.
The veterinarian administered ketamine to sedate the injured dog before surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PCP - a hallucinogenic drug with effects similar to ketamine

Etymology

Derived from the chemical structure: ketone and amine.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8k\u025bt\u0259\u02ccmin / (AmE), / \u02c8ki\u02d0t\u0259mi\u02d0n / (BrE)
(in psychiatry) a rapid-acting antidepressant used to treat severe or treatment-resistant depression; often given by intravenous infusion or in related low-dose formulations.
rapid-acting antidepressant (psychiatry)
medical

Examples

The clinic offers ketamine infusions for patients with treatment-resistant depression and severe suicidal ideation.
After conventional medications failed, she received an IV dose of ketamine and noticed improvement in two days.
Researchers are studying whether low-dose ketamine can rapidly reduce symptoms compared with standard antidepressants.
