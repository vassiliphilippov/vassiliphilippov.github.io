retrospective

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
looking back on, or dealing with past events or situations
relating to the past

Examples

Her explanation for quitting seemed like retrospective justification rather than her true motivation at the time.
Medical studies often use retrospective data to identify patterns and outcomes from previous cases.
The report included a retrospective analysis of the company's performance over the past decade.

Synset Examples

retrospective self-justification

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prospective - relating to or likely to happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectare' (to look back), from 'retro-' (back) + 'specere' (to look).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
an exhibition showing a representative selection of an artist's work over their career
exhibition of an artist's life work

Examples

Critics praised the comprehensive retrospective for highlighting the evolution of her style.
The artist's retrospective attracted visitors from all over the world.
The museum is hosting a retrospective of Picasso's paintings next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhibition - a public display of works of art or items of interest

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectare' (to look back).

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccr\u025btro\u028a\u02c8sp\u025bkt\u026av / (AmE), / \u02ccr\u025btr\u0259\u02c8sp\u025bkt\u026av / (BrE)
(in project management, especially Agile software development) a meeting held at the end of an iteration or sprint in which a team reflects on their work and identifies improvements for future cycles.
team review meeting after a sprint

Examples

After the sprint, the team scheduled a retrospective to discuss what went well and what to change.
Many teams run short weekly retrospectives to fix problems before they affect future work.
The scrum master prepared notes to lead a focused retrospective about deployment failures and process improvements.

Synset Examples
