importer

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a person or company that brings goods or services into a country from abroad for sale
person or company that imports goods

Examples

As an importer, she attends international trade fairs to find new products.
Many importers must comply with strict government regulations.
The company recently became a leading importer of electronic goods in the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exporter - a person or company that sells goods to another country

Etymology

From Latin 'importare' (to bring in), via French 'importer'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪmˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a software tool, module, or component that reads data in an external format and converts or loads it into a system, application, or database.
software module that imports data

Examples

The data pipeline uses a importer to load JSON feeds into the warehouse.
Our CMS includes an importer that converts CSV files into structured articles automatically.
Developers created several importers to migrate legacy XML records into the new database.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ingest - to take data into a system, often for processing or storage
  • Antonym: exporter - a tool or process that writes data out of a system to an external format or location

Etymology

From Latin 'importare' (to bring in), via French 'importer'; used metaphorically in computing to mean bringing data into a system.
