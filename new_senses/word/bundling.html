bundling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of sending or moving someone quickly and often without care, especially by shoving or hurrying them
act of sending or hurrying someone off hastily

Examples

She found his bundling of her out the door rather rude.
The bundling of students onto the bus caused some commotion.
There was a lot of bundling as the parents tried to get their kids into the car.

Synset Examples

she complained about bundling the children off to school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hustling - the act of moving or pushing someone quickly

Etymology

From 'bundle' (verb), meaning to tie or gather together, + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of tying or gathering things together into a bundle
the act of binding things into a bundle

Examples

After harvest, the bundling of wheat was done by hand in the old days.
The bundling of firewood made it easier to carry through the forest.
The bundling of newspapers for recycling takes place every Monday morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bundle - a collection of things tied or wrapped together

Etymology

From 'bundle' + '-ing', where 'bundle' comes from Middle Dutch 'bondel' (sheaf).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌndlɪŋ / (BrE)
(historical) A former custom during courtship in which two unmarried people, typically a man and a woman, shared a bed fully clothed or partly clothed, often separated by a board or covering, without engaging in sexual activity.
historical courtship custom of bed-sharing
archaic

Examples

Bundling was practiced in some rural communities during the colonial period.
Historians debate whether bundling encouraged intimacy or helped preserve chastity among courting couples.
The tradition of bundling is often mentioned in American folklore and early literature.
