contract

1. VERB

/ kənˈtrækt / (AmE), / kənˈtrækt / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or a material) to become smaller or tighten by squeezing or pressing together; to make or become contracted.
to become smaller or tighter by squeezing

Examples

In cold temperatures, metals typically contract and become denser.
Pain caused the muscle to contract suddenly.
She could feel her throat contract with anxiety before her speech.

Synset Examples

she compressed her lips
the spasm contracted the muscle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expand - to become or make larger or more extensive
  • Synonym: compress - to press or squeeze something to make it smaller

Etymology

From Latin 'contractus', past participle of 'contrahere' (to draw together).
