to powerwash

1. VERB

/ ˈpaʊərˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊəˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
to clean a surface using a high-pressure water spray, especially to remove dirt, old paint, or mildew before painting
clean with high-pressure water spray

Examples

It's best to powerwash sidewalks every spring to keep them looking clean.
The contractor powerwashed the house to remove all traces of mildew and peeling paint.
We need to powerwash the deck before staining it this weekend.

Synset Examples

pressure-wash the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure-wash - to clean using a high-pressure water spray

Etymology

Formed from 'power' and 'wash', referring to the use of power-driven high-pressure washers.

(in computing, especially Chrome OS) to erase all user data and settings on a device and restore it to factory/default state; perform a factory reset.
factory-reset a device

Examples

After the malware infection, she had to powerwash the Chromebook to remove the corrupted user profile.
He powerwashed the laptop yesterday, returning it to factory settings before selling it to a friend.
If your Chromebook keeps freezing, consider powerwashing it after backing up important files to cloud storage.
