stalling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
an action or tactic intended to cause delay or to mislead, often to gain time or avoid a decision
delaying or misleading tactic

Examples

His constant stalling made negotiations drag on much longer than necessary.
The company's stalling was obvious as they avoided any commitment.
The politician's stalling frustrated reporters eager for an answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned
  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something or someone

Etymology

From the verb 'stall' (to delay), with -ing noun suffix.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈstɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing) a temporary suspension of a processor's instruction execution, usually in a CPU pipeline, caused by hazards, cache misses, or resource conflicts that delay subsequent instructions.
temporary CPU pipeline halt

Examples

During the benchmark, frequent stalling kept the CPU from reaching peak throughput.
Cache misses caused repeated stalling, so the program ran much slower than expected.
Engineers measured pipeline stalling during memory accesses to identify performance bottlenecks.
