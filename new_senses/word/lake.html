lake

1. NOUN

/ leɪk / (AmE), / leɪk / (BrE)
a large area of water, usually fresh, surrounded by land
large body of water surrounded by land

Examples

A boat floated quietly on the calm waters of the lake.
The city is famous for the beautiful lake at its center.
They spent their summer holidays fishing and swimming in the lake.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pond - a small body of still water, smaller than a lake

Etymology

From Latin 'lacus' (lake, pond, basin).

2. NOUN

/ leɪk / (AmE), / leɪk / (BrE)
a purplish-red pigment made from lac or cochineal, used especially in dyes and paints
purplish-red pigment from lac or cochineal

Examples

Historically, lake colors were highly valued for their intensity and translucency.
The artist used a deep lake pigment to achieve a vibrant shade of red in her painting.
This particular red dye, called lake, was derived from powdered cochineal insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carmine - a vivid red pigment from cochineal

Etymology

From French *laque*, from Italian *lacca*, from Persian *lāk*, referring to the red resin from insects.
