jail

1. NOUN

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
A building or place where people are held as a punishment for a crime or while waiting for trial.
prison; place of detention

Examples

Many people are held in jail while awaiting their court trial.
She spent three months in jail for tax evasion.
The suspect was taken to jail after being arrested by the police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from jail or prison

Etymology

From Old French 'jaiole', from Latin 'cavea' (cage).

2. VERB

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
to put someone in prison or to confine someone as if in prison
to put in prison; confine

Examples

Many activists were jailed after the peaceful protest.
She fears that speaking out could result in her being jailed for dissent.
The judge decided to jail the man for violating his probation.

Synset Examples

The suspects were imprisoned without trial
the murderer was incarcerated for the rest of his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprison - to put someone in prison
  • Synonym: incarcerate - to put someone in prison
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from confinement

Etymology

From Old French *jaiole* (cage, prison), from Latin *cavea* (cage).

3. NOUN NEW

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
(computing) an isolated environment or container that restricts an application's access to other parts of the system and its resources.
isolated software container

Examples

The administrator launched a jail to run the test service in isolation.
Developers deployed multiple jails to separate each microservice on the same host.
Security guidelines recommend running untrusted code inside a jail to limit system access.

Synset Examples

The administrator launched a jail to run the test service in isolation.
Developers deployed multiple jails to separate each microservice on the same host.
Security guidelines recommend running untrusted code inside a jail to limit system access.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandbox - an isolated environment for running untrusted code with restricted privileges
  • Synonym: container - a lightweight, portable unit for packaging software and its dependencies, often isolated from the host system

Etymology

From the noun 'jail' (a place of confinement); used metaphorically in computing for isolated containers (e.g., the BSD 'jail' system).
