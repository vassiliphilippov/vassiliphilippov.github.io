texture

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstʃə / (BrE)
the way a surface or substance feels when touched, especially in regard to its smoothness, softness, or roughness
the feel of a surface or fabric

Examples

She loved the silky texture of the new fabric.
The bread has a light, airy texture that melts in your mouth.
The rough texture of the bark made it easy to climb the tree.

Synset Examples

the wall had a smooth texture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - the outside layer or uppermost part of something

Etymology

From Latin textura (weaving, texture), from texere (to weave).

6. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8t\u025bkst\u0283\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8t\u025bkst\u0283\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computer graphics) a digital image applied to the surface of a 2D or 3D model to give it color, detail, or surface appearance; often used as a 'texture map'.
digital image applied to 3D surfaces

Examples

The game uses a high-resolution texture to render the rocky cliff.
She created several custom textures to give the character's clothing more realistic detail.
When the artist forgot to include a normal map, the texture looked flat on curved surfaces.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: texture map - an image file used to add color or detail to the surface of a 3D model
  • Synonym: texture mapping - the process of applying a texture (image) to a 3D surface in computer graphics

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of Latin textura (weaving, texture); applied in computing to mean an image giving surface detail.
