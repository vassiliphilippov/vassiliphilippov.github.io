LLM

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl ˌɛl ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛl ˌɛl ˈɛm / (BrE)
a postgraduate academic degree in law, typically pursued after earning a primary law degree
advanced law degree
legal

Examples

After completing her JD, she decided to pursue an LLM in international law.
He earned his LLM to specialize in tax law.
Many law firms prefer applicants with an LLM from a prestigious university.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: JD - primary law degree, especially in the US
  • Degree Higher: PhD - doctoral-level degree, highest academic rank

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Legum Magister' or 'Legum Magistra', meaning Master of Laws.
