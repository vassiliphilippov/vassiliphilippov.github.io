flagging

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
A pavement or path made of flat stones (flagstones) laid side by side.
pavement made of flagstones

Examples

His design included a mosaic pattern in the flagging leading up to the front door.
The garden's flagging was slippery after the rain, so we walked carefully.
We replaced the old brick path with new flagging around the perimeter of the house.

Synset Examples

the flagging in the garden was quite imaginative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagstone - a flat stone slab used for paving

Etymology

From flag (flat stone) + -ing (forming nouns denoting action or result).

2. ADJ

/ ˈflæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Becoming weak or losing energy, especially from exhaustion or fatigue.
weak or losing energy from tiredness

Examples

After hours of hiking, her flagging spirits needed a boost to reach the summit.
He drank a strong cup of coffee to revive his flagging energy before the meeting.
The flagging team members struggled to finish the last mile of the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - very tired, drained of energy
  • Antonym: invigorated - full of energy and life

Etymology

Present participle of 'flag', from Old Norse *flaka* (to droop or hang loosely).
