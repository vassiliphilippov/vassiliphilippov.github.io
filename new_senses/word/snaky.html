snaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈsneɪki / (AmE), / ˈsneɪki / (BrE)
Having the form or appearance of a snake; winding or sinuous like a serpent.
having a snake-like shape; winding

Examples

She admired the snaky patterns left by the tide on the beach.
The river took a snaky course through the valley.
The trail followed a snaky path through the dense jungle.

Synset Examples

a serpentine wall
snaky ridges in the sand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serpentine - winding or twisting like a serpent

Etymology

From snake + -y, meaning 'like a snake'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈsneɪki / (AmE), / ˈsneɪki / (BrE)
behaving in a secretive, deceitful, or treacherous way; showing dishonesty or bad faith.
deceitful; treacherous

Examples

The snaky negotiations revealed hidden terms that betrayed the investors' trust.
She noticed his snaky smile and realised he was not telling the whole story.
After the scandal, the board described his conduct as increasingly snakier and unprofessional.

Synset Examples

The snaky negotiations revealed hidden terms that betrayed the investors' trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treacherous - likely to betray trust; dangerously unstable or unreliable
  • Synonym: sly - having or showing a cunning and deceitful nature
  • Antonym: honest - behaving in a fair, truthful, and straightforward way; opposite of deceitful

Etymology

From snake + -y; extended figuratively from the association of snakes with slyness and treachery.
