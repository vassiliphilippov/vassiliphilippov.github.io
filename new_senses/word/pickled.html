pickled

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪkəld / (AmE), / ˈpɪkld / (BrE)
(of food) preserved in vinegar, brine, or another pickling liquid.
preserved in pickling liquid

Examples

I enjoy the sharp taste of pickled onions with my sandwich.
She served a platter of pickled cucumbers at the party.
The recipe calls for fresh or pickled ginger as a garnish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserved - kept from spoiling, especially by chemical means

2. ADJ NEW

/ c8p6ak59ld / (AmE), / c8p6akld / (BrE)
(computing) (of data or an object) serialized into a byte stream by a 'pickle' process or format so it can be stored or transmitted and later restored.
serialized (computing)

Examples

The pickled object can be restored to its original class with pickle.load after transmission.
During backup, engineers stored several pickled configurations as binary files for quick deployment.
If you receive a pickled object from an untrusted source, avoid unpickling it for security.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to serialize - to convert a data structure or object into a format that can be stored or transmitted
  • Synonym: marshal - to prepare and transform data for storage or transmission (often used interchangeably with serialize)
  • Antonym: deserialize - to convert serialized data back into its original object or data structure

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from 'pickle' (to preserve in brine); applied in computing to mean serialize, popularized by Python's 'pickle' module.
