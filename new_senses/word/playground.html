playground

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡraʊnd / (BrE)
An outdoor area provided for children to play in, often equipped with swings, slides, and other play equipment.
outdoor area for children's play

Examples

Our local playground has new swings and a slide.
She watched her son make friends at the playground every afternoon.
The children rushed to the playground after school to climb on the jungle gym.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: park - a public green area in a town used for recreation

Etymology

From 'play' + 'ground', first used in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area or place frequented by many people for recreation, holidays, or leisure activities
place popular for recreation or holidays

Examples

For decades, the Alps have been a winter playground for skiers from around the world.
The city has become a playground for tech entrepreneurs and nightlife enthusiasts.
The rich and famous have made this exclusive island their playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resort - a place where people go for holidays and recreation

Etymology

From 'play' + 'ground', originally referring to a space for children to play, later extended metaphorically.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpleɪˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A safe, isolated environment for trying out software, code, or ideas without affecting production systems.
sandbox or testing environment for code

Examples

Before merging, the junior developer tested the module in a playground to avoid breaking production.
You can paste the function into the online playground and see immediate output and errors.
Teachers used an interactive coding playground so students could experiment without installing software.
