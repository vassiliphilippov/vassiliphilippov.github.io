forfeiture

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrfɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːfɪtʃə / (BrE)
the act of losing or giving up something as a penalty for wrongdoing or failure to fulfill an obligation
loss or surrender as a penalty

Examples

She risked forfeiture of her deposit by breaking the terms of the lease.
The forfeiture of his property was the result of his failure to pay taxes.
The court ordered the forfeiture of all assets connected to the crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confiscation - the act of taking something away officially, often by authority
  • Synonym: surrender - the act of giving something up, especially as a penalty
  • Antonym: acquisition - the act of acquiring or gaining something, opposite of loss

Etymology

From Old French *forfaiture* (misdeed), from *forfaire* (to transgress).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.fɪ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.fɪ.tjʊə / (BrE)
the loss or giving up of something, especially property or a right, as a result of breaking a law or failing to comply with an obligation.
loss of something as penalty

Examples

He appealed against the forfeiture of his driver's license.
Late payment may result in the forfeiture of your deposit.
The company faced forfeiture of its assets after violating the regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penalty - a punishment for breaking a law or rule
  • Synonym: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty
  • Antonym: reward - a thing given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement

Etymology

From Old French *forfaiture* (crime, transgression), from *forfait* (crime), past participle of *forfaire* (to transgress).
