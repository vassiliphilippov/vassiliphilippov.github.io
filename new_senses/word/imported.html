imported

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔrtɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtɪd / (BrE)
(of goods) brought into a country from abroad for sale.
brought in from another country

Examples

Sales of imported cars have increased steadily over the past decade.
She prefers to buy imported cheese for special occasions.
The store offers a wide selection of imported products from Italy and France.

Synset Examples

imported wines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domestic - produced or originating within the same country; not imported

Etymology

Past participle of 'import', from Latin 'importare' (to bring in).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ɪmˈpɔrtɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtɪd / (BrE)
(in computing) brought into a program, project, or runtime environment by an import operation; used of modules, libraries, code, or datasets that originate elsewhere.
brought into a program via import

Examples

The project failed because the imported library had incompatible dependencies with the runtime environment.
She cleaned and transformed the imported dataset before feeding it into the machine-learning pipeline.
During deployment, the team discovered the imported module was missing a crucial configuration file.

Synset Examples
