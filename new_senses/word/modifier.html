modifier

/ ˈmɑːdəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)

gene altering another gene's effect

Examples

Researchers are studying how one modifier gene can lessen the impact of a harmful mutation.
Some diseases are influenced by modifier genes that affect the severity of symptoms.
The discovery of a modifier helped explain the unexpected color patterns observed in the fruit flies.

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word, especially an adjective or adverb, that adds to or changes the meaning of a noun or verb
word that qualifies the meaning of another word

Examples

Adjectives, adverbs, and certain phrases can function as a modifier in sentences.
In the phrase 'run quickly', 'quickly' acts as a modifier of the verb 'run'.
The word 'red' in 'red car' is a modifier describing the color of the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: qualifier - a word that limits or specifies the meaning of another word

Etymology

From Latin modificare (to measure or limit).
