canonization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkanənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic and Eastern Orthodox Churches) the official act of declaring a deceased person a saint.
proclamation of sainthood in Christian churches

Examples

After decades of devotion, the church announced his canonization as a saint.
Mother Teresa’s canonization ceremony was attended by thousands of people from around the world.
The process of canonization requires thorough investigation into the candidate’s life and miracles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beatification - the recognition of a deceased person's entrance into heaven and capacity to intercede on behalf of individuals who pray in their name

Etymology

From Medieval Latin canonizatio, from canonizare (to canonize), from Latin canon (rule).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌkænənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkænənɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of accepting or promoting a work, author, idea, or text as part of an established cultural or literary canon; treating it as authoritative or central.
inclusion in a cultural or literary canon

Examples

Scholars debated the canonization of post-2000 novels as part of the national literary curriculum.
The journal published an essay arguing for the canonization of previously overlooked immigrant writers.
Museums play a role in the canonization of artists by curating permanent exhibitions and retrospectives.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canonicalization - the process of making something canonical or generally accepted within a canon
  • Synonym: canon (literary) - a body of works widely accepted as important, authoritative, or representative in a culture or field

From Medieval Latin canonizatio, from canonizare (to canonize), from Latin canon (rule).
