fiducial

1. ADJ

/ fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
based on trust, especially in legal or technical contexts; relating to something that serves as a standard of reference.
based on trust; serving as a reference standard

Examples

Scientists use a fiducial marker to ensure accurate measurements in their experiments.
The agreement is fiducial in nature, relying on mutual trust between both parties.
The banker acted in a fiducial capacity, holding assets on behalf of his client.

Etymology

From Latin fiducia (trust, confidence), from fidere (to trust).

/ fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or of the nature of a trust, especially in a legal or financial context, where something is held or managed for the benefit of another person.
relating to a trust; held in trust
legal

3. ADJ

/ fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
used as a fixed standard of reference for comparison or measurement, especially in scientific or technical contexts.
serving as a fixed reference point or standard

Examples

A fiducial line was drawn to serve as the baseline for all future surveys.
Scientists rely on fiducial points in an image to calibrate their measurements.
The engineer used a fiducial marker to ensure accurate alignment of the components.

Synset Examples

a fiducial point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - a standard or point used for comparison in measurement

Etymology

From Latin fiducialis (of confidence, trustworthy), from fiducia (trust, confidence).

4. NOUN NEW

/ fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
A physical marker or reference point used in medical imaging, computer vision, surveying, or navigation to align coordinate systems or guide image registration.
reference marker used to align images or coordinates
medical

Examples

The surgeon placed a fiducial near the tumor to ensure precise alignment during radiation therapy.
Engineers used several fiducial points in the aerial images to align the maps accurately.
The algorithm detects tiny adhesive fiducial markers to register consecutive frames in real-time.
