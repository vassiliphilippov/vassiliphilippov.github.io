two-step

1. VERB

/ ˈtuˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌstɛp / (BrE)
to dance the two-step, a lively partner dance with quick and slow steps
to dance the two-step

Examples

At the wedding, everyone gathered to two-step around the dance floor.
He loves to two-step whenever he hears country music.
She taught her friends how to two-step at the country bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - to dance the waltz
  • Synonym: fox-trot - to dance the foxtrot

Etymology

From the name of the dance two-step, popularized in the late 19th century.
