chimp

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪmp / (AmE), / tʃɪmp / (BrE)
a highly intelligent ape found in the forests of central and western Africa, closely related to humans
an intelligent African ape

Examples

At the zoo, the children enjoyed watching the playful chimps swing from branch to branch.
Jane Goodall spent years observing chimps in their natural habitat.
The chimp quickly solved the puzzle, astonishing the researchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - the full name for 'chimp'; an intelligent African ape
  • Synonym: bonobo - another species of chimpanzee with distinct social behaviors

Etymology

Shortened form of 'chimpanzee', from French 'chimpanzé' (from a Bantu language in the Congo region).

2. VERB NEW

/ tʃɪmp / (AmE), / tʃɪmp / (BrE)
(informal) to look at a digital camera's LCD screen immediately after taking a photograph, often repeatedly, to check exposure, focus, or composition.
check photos on camera LCD
informal slang

Examples

After each shot, he would chimp the camera's LCD to check focus.
Many photographers chimp their images immediately, preferring to correct exposure on the spot.
She stopped chimping constantly after learning to trust her light meter and histogram.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to look over photographs or images to assess quality
  • Synonym: preview - to see or display an image or file briefly before finalizing

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'chimp' (short for 'chimpanzee'); used figuratively in photography since the digital era comparing the quick, repetitive checking to a chimpanzee's behaviour.
