diagnostics

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.əɡˈnɑstɪks / (AmE), / daɪ.əɡˈnɒstɪks / (BrE)
the branch of medical science concerned with identifying and classifying diseases
medical science of disease classification
medical

Examples

Advances in diagnostics have revolutionized the early detection of diseases.
Modern medical diagnostics combine laboratory tests and imaging technologies.
The hospital invested in the latest diagnostics equipment to improve patient care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnosis - identification of the nature of an illness or other problem

Etymology

From Greek diagnōstikos (able to distinguish), from diagnōsis (discernment, diagnosis).

2. NOUN NEW

/ daɪ.əɡˈnɑstɪks / (AmE), / daɪ.əɡˈnɒstɪks / (BrE)
a set of tests, programs, or tools used to check the condition, performance, or problems of computer systems, devices, or software.
tests for computer systems

Examples

Before shipping the laptop, the technician ran diagnostics to ensure all components were functioning.
The car's onboard system showed error codes after running diagnostics during the factory inspection.
If your phone is slow, try the built-in app that performs diagnostics and reports hardware problems.
