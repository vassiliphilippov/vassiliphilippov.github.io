custodianship

1. NOUN

/ kəˈstoʊdiənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kʌˈstəʊdiənʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, role, or responsibility of being a custodian; the act of having charge, care, or guardianship of something or someone
position or role of a custodian

Examples

During her custodianship, the museum saw major improvements in conservation methods.
He was entrusted with the custodianship of the historic estate after his father retired.
The custodianship of the records was transferred to a new department this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custody - protective care or guardianship of someone or something

Etymology

From 'custodian' plus the suffix '-ship' indicating state or condition.
