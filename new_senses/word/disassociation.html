disassociation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A psychological state in which a part of a person's experience becomes separated from the rest of their personality and functions independently.
psychological separation of mental processes

Examples

After the traumatic event, he experienced disassociation and felt detached from his surroundings.
Periods of disassociation can make it difficult to remember important personal information.
Therapists often help patients understand the triggers for their disassociation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissociation - process of separating or disconnecting from something, especially in psychology

Etymology

Formed from dis- (expressing reversal) and association.
