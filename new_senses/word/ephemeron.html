ephemeron

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛm.əˌrɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛm.ər.ɒn / (BrE)
a living thing or object that lasts only a very short time, especially an insect with a brief adult life
something short-lived; briefly living organism

Examples

He used the term ephemeron to describe trends that vanish almost as quickly as they appear.
Research into the ephemeron sheds light on the fleeting nature of some insect species.
The mayfly is a classic example of an ephemeron, living just one day as an adult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemera - things that exist or are used for only a short time

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeron (lasting only a day), from epi- (upon) + hēmera (day).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˈfɛm.əˌrɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛm.ər.ɔn / (BrE)
(in computing) a data structure used in garbage-collected systems to hold a key–value association that does not by itself keep the key alive; when the key becomes unreachable, the association can be removed so the value may be reclaimed.
short-lived key–value structure (computing)

Examples

The runtime uses an ephemeron to cache metadata that should disappear when the key is collected.
Implementers chose an ephemeron so the value would not prevent unused objects from being garbage-collected.
When the key becomes unreachable, the language's ephemeron allows both key and value to be reclaimed.

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeron ‘lasting only a day’; used metaphorically in computing to mean a short-lived association.
