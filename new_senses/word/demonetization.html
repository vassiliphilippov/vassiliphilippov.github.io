demonetization

1. NOUN

/ diˌmʌnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌmɒnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of officially removing the status of legal tender from a form of currency, such as coins or notes, so it is no longer recognized for payment in a country.
removal of legal tender status from currency
legal

Examples

Economists debated the long-term impacts of demonetization on the country's cash-dependent economy.
Following the demonetization, people had to exchange old currency notes for new ones at banks.
The government announced the demonetization of all large denomination notes to combat corruption.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remonetization - restoring legal tender status to a currency

Etymology

From French démonétisation, from dé- (removal) + monétiser (to monetize).

2. NOUN NEW

/ di\u02ccm\u028cn\u026at\u0259\u02c8ze\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / di\u02ccm\u0252n\u026ata\u026a\u02c8ze\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
The act of removing a channel's, account's, or piece of online content's ability to earn money on a digital platform.
removal of online monetization

Examples

After the policy change, several small creators reported that demonetization had destroyed their main income.
The company warned that videos violating rules could face immediate demonetization without prior notice to the uploader.
Debate about algorithm transparency grew after unexplained demonetization affected dozens of educational channels last month.
