focused

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
(of an optical system, such as a camera, eye, or opera glasses) adjusted so as to produce a clear and sharply defined image.
optics: producing a clear image

Examples

A focused camera lens is essential for taking sharp photographs.
After adjusting the lens, the image in the microscope became focused and details were clearly visible.
He waited until the binoculars were perfectly focused before looking at the birds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfocused - not adjusted to produce a clear image

Etymology

From the verb 'focus' (from Latin 'focus' meaning 'hearth, fireplace', later referring to 'point of convergence').

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
(of light rays) brought together at a single point; converging to a focal point.
light rays converging to a point

Examples

Focused sunlight through a magnifying glass can burn a hole in paper.
The focused rays coming from the projector made the image appear crisp and clear.
The scientist used a lens to create a focused beam of light in the experiment.

Synset Examples

focused light rays can set something afire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diffuse - (of light or energy) spread out widely, not concentrated

Etymology

From the verb 'focus', from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace), originally meaning center of activity or energy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00615127-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
clearly directed or having been brought into clear visual or conceptual clarity; in focus.
clearly in focus; brought into clarity

Examples

After adjusting the microscope, the specimen finally appeared focused and detailed.
Her presentation was clear and focused on the key issues.
The camera lens must be focused to capture a sharp image.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blurred - not in focus; unclear

Etymology

From the verb 'focus', ultimately from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace).

4. ADJ NEW

/ \u02c8fo\u028ak\u0259st / (AmE), / \u02c8f\u0259\u028ak\u0259st / (BrE)
(computing) (of a user-interface element) having keyboard or input focus and therefore receiving input or interaction events.
UI: having keyboard/input focus

Examples

When you click the textbox, the focused field displays a blinking cursor and accepts keyboard input.
Screen readers announce which element is focused so users know where their keystrokes will go.
You can style a focused button in CSS using the :focus selector to show it is active.

Synset Examples

From the verb 'focus', ultimately from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace); extended metaphorically to concentration and then to technical computing usage.
