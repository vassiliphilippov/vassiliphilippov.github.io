trolling

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the method of fishing by slowly pulling a baited line or lure through the water, usually behind a moving boat.
angling by pulling baited line through water

Examples

Trolling is often used to cover wide areas of water in search of schools of fish.
He spent the morning trolling for trout along the lake’s edge.
They caught several large fish by trolling slowly behind the boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trawling - fishing by dragging a net through water

Etymology

From Middle English *trollen* (to walk or stroll), later applied to fishing from the sense of 'moving about'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
informal behaviour on the internet of posting provocative, deceptive, or off-topic messages to provoke emotional reactions or disrupt discussion.
deliberate online provocation
informal

Examples

Online forums are often disrupted by trolling that aims to start angry debates among users.
Her posts were ignored because the moderators suspected she was engaging in trolling for attention.
He admitted his trolling had been intended as a joke but it upset several people.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baiting - deliberately provoking someone by saying things designed to annoy them
  • Synonym: flaming - sending angry or insulting messages online, often to provoke a hostile response

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the verb 'troll' meaning to fish with a baited line; the internet sense (baiting people online) developed in the late 20th century.
