transplanter

1. NOUN

/ trænsˈplæn.tɚ / (AmE), / trænsˈplæn.tə / (BrE)
a person who moves plants from one place and sets them in another, especially a gardener doing this as part of cultivation
a person who moves plants to new locations

Examples

Many homeowners hire a transplanter to relocate their mature shrubs without damaging them.
She worked as a transplanter during the busy spring season at the nursery.
The transplanter carefully dug up the seedlings and placed them in the new bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardener - a person who tends and cultivates a garden

Etymology

From 'transplant' (verb, to move and replant) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ trænsˈplæn.tɚ / (AmE), / trænsˈplæn.tə / (BrE)
a machine (often used in agriculture) that automatically or semi-automatically moves and plants seedlings or young plants into soil or growing beds.
machine for transplanting seedlings

Examples

The farm installed a new transplanter to speed up rice planting across the flooded paddies.
Modern transplanters can plant thousands of seedlings per hour with minimal human supervision.
Engineers tested the prototype transplanter in varied soil types before the harvest season.
