let

1. NOUN

/ lɛt / (AmE), / lɛt / (BrE)
(in tennis and similar sports) a serve that touches the net before landing in the correct service area, requiring the serve to be replayed.
tennis serve touching the net, replayed

Examples

During the match, several lets were called, slowing down the play.
If your first serve is a let, you get another chance to serve.
The umpire called a let after the ball clipped the net during the serve.

Etymology

From Old English lettan (to hinder), reflecting the sense of an interruption in play.

8. NOUN NEW

/ lɛt / (AmE), / lɛt / (BrE)
(in programming) a reserved keyword used in many languages (notably JavaScript) to declare a variable with block scope.
programming keyword for block-scoped variable

Examples

In modern JavaScript, use let to declare variables that are block-scoped within a function or block.
The tutor explained why replacing var with let prevents some unexpected hoisting bugs.
During the code review, she suggested using let for loop counters to limit their scope to the loop.
