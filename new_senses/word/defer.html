to defer

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfɜr / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜː / (BrE)
to yield to someone else's wishes, judgment, or opinion, especially out of respect or recognition of their authority
yield to someone else's wishes or opinion

Examples

He chose to defer to his manager's judgment on the issue.
She rarely defers to other people's opinions in meetings.
Sometimes it is wise to defer to experts when making complicated decisions.

Synset Examples

The government bowed to the military pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquiesce - to accept or comply reluctantly but without protest
  • Antonym: resist - to withstand, oppose, or refuse to comply

Etymology

From Latin 'deferre' (to bring down, submit), from 'de-' (down) + 'ferre' (to carry).

2. VERB

/ dəˈfɝː / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜː / (BrE)
to postpone something or delay it until a later time
postpone to a later time

Examples

Due to heavy rain, the match was deferred to the following day.
The committee decided to defer the meeting until next week.
We had to defer payment because of unforeseen expenses.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to arrange for something to take place at a later time
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward in time or make something happen sooner

Etymology

From Latin 'differre', meaning 'to postpone, delay'.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB NEW

/ dɪˈfɜr / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜː / (BrE)
(in programming) to arrange that a function or block of code is executed automatically when the current function or scope finishes, typically to perform cleanup or release resources.
schedule code to run at scope exit

Examples

Go programmers often use defer to close files automatically when a function returns.
The library automatically deferred the database close operation until the transaction finished.
The cleanup routine defers closing sockets until after the function's last statement executes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - to arrange for something to happen at a specific time or event (here, to run code later, at scope exit).
  • Synonym: postpone - to delay until a later time; in programming, to delay execution until scope exit.
