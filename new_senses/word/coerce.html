to coerce

1. VERB

/ koʊˈɜrs / (AmE), / kəʊˈɜːs / (BrE)
to make someone do something by using force or threats
compel by force or threat

Examples

He tried to coerce her into signing the contract against her will.
No one should be coerced into making a confession.
The rebels coerced villagers to provide them with supplies.

Synset Examples

She forced him to take a job in the city
He squeezed her for information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force - to make someone do something against their will
  • Synonym: compel - to pressure or drive someone to do something
  • Antonym: persuade - to convince someone to do something through reasoning or discussion

Etymology

From Latin 'coercere' (to restrain, confine), from 'co-' (together) + 'arcere' (to enclose, confine).

2. VERB NEW

/ koʊˈɜrs / (AmE), / kəʊˈɜːs / (BrE)
(in computing) to convert a value from one data type to another, often automatically or implicitly performed by a programming language or runtime.
convert data types (computing)

Examples

JavaScript will coerce '5' to the number 5 when using loose equality (==).
TypeScript prevents the compiler from coercing types implicitly without an explicit cast.
The function coerced the input into a float to ensure consistent arithmetic results.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form; in computing, to change data from one type to another
  • Synonym: cast - to explicitly change a value from one data type to another in programming
