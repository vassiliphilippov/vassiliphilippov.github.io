color blind

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚ blaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə blaɪnd / (BrE)
unable to distinguish certain colors, especially red and green, due to a deficiency or absence of particular color-sensitive cells in the eye
unable to distinguish certain colors

Examples

Because he is color blind, he finds it difficult to read certain colored charts.
His color blind condition was discovered during a routine eye exam.
The designer made her website accessible for color blind users.

Synset Examples

Being color blind, he often confuses red and green.
His color blind condition makes picking clothes challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color vision deficiency - partial or total inability to perceive colors normally

/ c8k8cl5a bla6and / (AmE), / c8k8cl59 bla6and / (BrE)
(of an attitude, policy, or person) claiming or attempting not to consider a person's race when treating or judging them; often criticized for overlooking systemic or institutional racial inequalities.
claiming not to consider race

Examples

Many companies claim a color blind hiring policy, but disparities persist in their workforce.
She argued that a color-blind approach to education ignores structural inequalities affecting minority students.
Critics say being color blind can prevent people from recognizing and addressing racial injustice.
