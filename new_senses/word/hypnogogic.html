hypnogogic

1. ADJ

/ hɪp.nəˈɡɑ.dʒɪk / (AmE), / hɪp.nəˈɡɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the state immediately before falling asleep; inducing sleep or characteristic of drowsiness experienced prior to sleep.
relating to the state before falling asleep
medical

Examples

Many people experience vivid hypnogogic imagery while drifting off to sleep.
Some artists draw inspiration from the hypnogogic sensations that occur just before sleep.
The patient's hypnogogic state was accompanied by brief auditory hallucinations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypnopompic - relating to the state of waking from sleep

Etymology

From French 'hypnagogique', from Greek 'hupnos' (sleep) + 'agōgos' (leading).

2. ADJ NEW

/ hɪpnəˈɡɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / hɪpnəˈɡɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
(in art and media) having a dreamlike, hazy, or nostalgic atmosphere reminiscent of the state just before sleep; often used of music, film, and visual art to describe an onset-of-sleep quality.
dreamlike media aesthetic

Examples

The film's hypnogogic visuals blend retro synths with hazy dreaminess to evoke nostalgia.
Critics described the album's slow textures as hypnogogic, like listening to a remembered half-dream.
Designers used hypnogogic color grading to give the commercial a soft, late-night atmosphere.
