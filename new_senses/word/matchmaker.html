matchmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmætʃˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who arranges or tries to arrange marriages between others
person who arranges marriages

Examples

Her aunt acted as a matchmaker and introduced her to several eligible bachelors.
Reality TV shows often feature a professional matchmaker helping singles find love.
The village matchmaker was respected for her keen sense of compatibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go-between - a person who acts as an intermediary, especially for relationships

Etymology

From 'match' (marriage union) + 'maker'; first recorded in English in the 17th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmætʃˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmætʃˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a service, platform, or algorithm that pairs people (for dating, friendship, work, or collaboration) by comparing profiles, preferences, or other data.
service or platform that pairs people

Examples

The dating app acted as a matchmaker, suggesting profiles that fit her interests and location.
Startup founders used a new AI matchmaker to pair developers with projects needing specific skills.
Many businesses subscribe to a matchmaker platform that recommends mentors for new employees.

  • Synonym: dating app - a mobile application designed to help people find romantic partners, often using algorithmic matching

Etymology

From 'match' (a pairing, originally for marriage) + 'maker'; originally for a person who arranges matches, extended in the 21st century to services and algorithms.
