CER

1. NOUN

/ si i ɑr / (AmE), / siː iː ɑː / (BrE)
in psychology, an emotional response that is acquired through the process of conditioning, especially classical conditioning
conditioned emotional response (psychology)
medical

Examples

By analyzing the CER, scientists can understand how emotional reactions are learned.
The researcher measured the rat's CER after it was exposed to the sound previously paired with a shock.
Therapists often discuss how a CER can trigger anxiety in patients with phobias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditioned response - a learned response to a previously neutral stimulus

Etymology

Abbreviation of conditioned emotional response.

2. NOUN NEW

/ si i ɑr / (AmE), / siː iː ɑː / (BrE)
A Certified Emission Reduction: a carbon-credit unit, equal to one metric ton of CO2-equivalent, issued under the Kyoto Protocol's Clean Development Mechanism (CDM).
carbon-credit unit (1 metric ton CO2e)

Examples

Companies can buy CER credits to meet regulatory emissions targets before the compliance deadline.
The government issued new rules on trading CER units on the international carbon market this year.
During the audit, auditors verified whether the claimed CER reductions had corresponding project documentation.
