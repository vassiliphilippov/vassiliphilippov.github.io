symbol

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəl / (BrE)
a written or printed mark, letter, or character used to represent something else, especially by convention
written or printed sign representing something

Examples

Mathematical equations often use the plus symbol to indicate addition.
On the map, each city is marked with a different symbol for easy identification.
The peace symbol is recognized around the world as a sign of nonviolence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - mark or character used to represent something
  • Synonym: emblem - symbolic object or design representing something
  • Synonym: signal - gesture, action, or sound conveying information or instructions

Etymology

From Greek 'symbolon' (token, sign).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəl / (BrE)
a mark, sign, or object that stands for or represents an idea, quality, or another object, especially something intangible or abstract
something that represents something else

Examples

Red roses are often used as a symbol of love and affection.
The dove is a symbol of peace in many cultures.
The flag serves as a powerful symbol of national unity.

Synset Examples

the eagle is a symbol of the United States

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emblem - a design or object representing a quality or concept
  • Synonym: sign - a mark or gesture conveying information
  • Synonym: metaphor - word or phrase applied to an object to suggest likeness

Etymology

From Greek *sýmbolon* (token, sign), via Latin *symbolum*.

(computing) a primitive data value that represents a unique, immutable identifier, often used as a key or token in programs.
programming unique identifier

Examples

In JavaScript, a symbol provides a unique key for private object properties and avoids name collisions.
Library functions often accept symbols to look up configuration values without risking accidental overrides.
Because each symbol is unique, comparing two created now will usually return false even if descriptions match.
