rpm

1. NOUN

/ ɑr pi ɛm / (AmE), / ɑː piː em / (BrE)
(abbreviation for revolutions per minute) The number of complete turns that an engine or other rotating device makes in one minute.
revolutions per minute; rotational speed

Examples

At high rpm, the engine produces more power but can wear out faster.
Keep the motor running at 3,000 rpm for optimal efficiency.
The tachometer shows the current rpm of the car's engine.

Synset Examples

the engine was doing 6000 revs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutions per minute - the full phrase for rpm; unit of rotational speed
  • Synonym: rev - informal abbreviation for revolution (complete turn), also used for engine speed

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'revolutions per minute'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɑr pi ɛm / (AmE), / ɑː piː em / (BrE)
(in digital advertising) revenue earned per 1,000 ad impressions; a metric showing estimated earnings per thousand impressions.
revenue per thousand ad impressions

Examples

After redesigning their site, the publisher noticed the rpm increased, boosting monthly ad revenue.
Our analytics dashboard shows daily rpm for each ad unit, letting us compare earnings.
They calculated the campaign's rpm by dividing total revenue by impressions then multiplying by one thousand.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eCPM - effective cost per mille; an estimate of revenue or cost per 1,000 impressions, functionally similar to rpm.

Etymology

Abbreviation of "revenue per mille" (mille is Latin for "thousand"); used in online advertising.
