stage

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ / (AmE), / steɪdʒ / (BrE)
a large horse-drawn coach formerly used to carry passengers and mail on scheduled routes between towns
horse-drawn passenger and mail coach
archaic

Examples

A dusty stage pulled up at the inn, dropping off passengers and mail.
He arranged for a seat on the morning stage to the city.
In the 19th century, travelers often rode the stage from one town to another.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagecoach - a horse-drawn coach that formerly carried passengers and mail along a regular route

Etymology

From Middle English stage (platform, stage of a journey), from Old French estage (dwelling, stopping place).
