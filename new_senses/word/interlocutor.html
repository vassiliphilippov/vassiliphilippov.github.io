interlocutor

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑːkjətər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɒkjʊtə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a conversation or dialogue
person in a conversation

Examples

During the interview, the interlocutor asked probing questions to clarify the issue.
In diplomatic talks, each interlocutor represents their government’s stance.
She listened attentively as her interlocutor explained his point of view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversationalist - a person who is good at or fond of engaging in conversation

Etymology

From Latin 'interloqui' meaning 'to speak between', from 'inter-' (between) + 'loqui' (to speak).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑːkjətər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɒkjʊtə / (BrE)
In a minstrel show, the performer seated in the middle who leads the conversation and engages other performers in dialogue.
central speaker in a minstrel show
archaic

Examples

During the act, the interlocutor addressed the troupe with a formal question, prompting a comic response.
In the heyday of minstrel shows, an interlocutor was considered essential for keeping the performance lively.
The interlocutor conducted the banter with perfect timing, setting up each of the end men's jokes.

Etymology

From Latin *interloqui* (to speak between), via French *interlocuteur*.
