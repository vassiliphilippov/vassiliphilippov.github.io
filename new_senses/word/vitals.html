vitals

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəlz / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəlz / (BrE)
the organs of the body that are essential for life, such as the heart, lungs, and brain
essential life-sustaining organs

Examples

The doctor explained how the vitals work together to keep us alive.
The surgeon checked the patient's vitals before proceeding with the operation.
Trauma to any of the vitals can be fatal if not treated quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organ - a part of the body that performs a specific function

Etymology

From Latin 'vitalis' (of or belonging to life, vital).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvaɪtəlz / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəlz / (BrE)
a set of important measurements or metrics that indicate the health or performance of a system, organization, or project (for example, financial, technical, or operational indicators).
key health/performance metrics

Examples

In investor meetings, she highlights the company's vitals, such as churn, ARPU, and cash burn.
The ops dashboard shows the server's vitals — CPU, memory, and network latency over the last hour.
Before the demo, the product manager reviewed the project vitals to prepare for questions about timelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: key performance indicators - quantitative measures used to evaluate the success or health of an organization or activity.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of medical 'vitals' (from Latin 'vitalis', of life) to mean essential measures of health for non‑biological systems.
