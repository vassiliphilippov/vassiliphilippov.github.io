faded

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
no longer having the original brightness, freshness, or strength of color; having become dull or washed out
having lost color or brightness

Examples

She wore her favorite pair of faded jeans to the party.
The curtains in the living room were faded after years of sunlight.
The old photograph looked faded but held many memories.

Synset Examples

sun-bleached deck chairs
faded jeans
a very pale washed-out blue
washy colors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright - having a lot of light or color; not faded
  • Synonym: washed-out - lacking color or brightness; faded

Etymology

Past participle of 'fade', from Old French 'fader', ultimately from Latin 'fatuus' (foolish, insipid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
having lost brightness, strength, or freshness; reduced in intensity or vividness, especially due to age or exposure.
diminished in brightness or strength

Examples

Her memories of childhood have faded over time.
The denim jacket looked stylishly faded around the collar and sleeves.
The old photograph showed faded colors after years of sunlight exposure.

Synset Examples

the faded tones of an old recording

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright - full of light or vivid in color

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fade', from Middle English 'faden', probably from Old French 'fader' (to vanish, diminish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ NEW

/ ˈfeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
(slang, informal) intoxicated or under the influence of drugs or alcohol; noticeably affected by substances.
intoxicated; under the influence (slang)
slang informal

Examples

After three shots and a few beers he looked completely faded and stumbled to the sofa.
Her friends helped the faded guest into a taxi after the house party ended late.
The concert photos showed the lead singer clearly faded during the second half of the set.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high - under the influence of drugs; commonly used as a slang synonym of 'faded' in some contexts
  • Synonym: drunk - intoxicated by alcohol; often used synonymously with 'faded' when alcohol is involved
  • Antonym: sober - not under the influence of alcohol or drugs; opposite of 'faded' in this slang sense

Etymology

Derived from the past participle of 'fade' (Middle English faden, probably from Old French fader, 'to vanish or diminish'); used metaphorically in recent decades to describe loss of normal mental/state control due to substances.
