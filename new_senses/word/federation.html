federation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of states or organizations that have joined together under a central government while retaining some individual independence
union of states or organizations

Examples

Australia is a federation comprised of six states and several territories.
The European Union is not a true federation, but it has some federal elements.
The newly formed federation was designed to balance local and central authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confederation - an alliance of states with a weaker central authority
  • Synonym: union - the action or fact of joining or being joined together

Etymology

From Latin foederatio (a league or pact), from foedus (treaty, agreement).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an organization consisting of several groups, states, or parties that have joined together for a common purpose but retain their individual independence
organization of united groups or states

Examples

After years of negotiation, the provinces agreed to create a federation to unify their policies.
Several trade unions formed a federation to better represent their members' interests.
The International Tennis Federation oversees major competitions and sets the rules for the sport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confederation - an alliance or league of groups or states with more autonomy

Etymology

From Latin 'foederatio' (league, alliance), from 'foedus' (treaty, pact).

3. NOUN

/ ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forming a political unity from separate states, colonies, or provinces, each retaining control over its internal affairs
union of states into one political entity

Examples

Talks are ongoing about the federation of several neighboring states for economic cooperation.
The creation of the Australian federation in 1901 united the colonies into a single nation.
The process of federation allowed each province to have its own laws while sharing a central government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confederation - an organization of states or groups united for a common purpose, often with looser ties than a federation

Etymology

From Latin 'foederatio' (a league or treaty), based on 'foedus' (treaty, league).
