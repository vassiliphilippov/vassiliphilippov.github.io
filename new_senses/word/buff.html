buff

1. NOUN

/ bʌf / (AmE), / bʌf / (BrE)
an implement made of soft material, mounted on a block, used for polishing surfaces, especially in manicuring or finishing
tool for polishing surfaces

Examples

After sanding the wood, he finished it with a buff for a glossy look.
Make sure to clean your buff regularly to prevent residue build-up.
The manicurist used a buff to smooth and shine my nails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polisher - a tool or device used to make surfaces shiny or smooth by rubbing
  • Degree Lower: file - an implement with a roughened surface used to shape or smooth surfaces, often as an alternative to a buff

2. NOUN

/ bʌf / (AmE), / bʌf / (BrE)
a soft, thick, undyed leather made from the skin of buffalo, oxen, or similar animals, often used for polishing or in military uniforms
soft, thick, undyed leather

Examples

Army officers traditionally wore gloves made of buff for formal occasions.
The bookbinding was finished with buff to give the cover a soft, durable texture.
The craftsman selected a piece of buff to polish the metal surface.

Etymology

From French 'buffle' (buffalo), related to Italian 'bufalo', originally from Late Latin 'bufalus' (buffalo).

/ bʌf / (AmE), / bʌf / (BrE)
the state of being naked; bare skin (usually used in the phrase 'in the buff')
the state of being naked
informal

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked - without clothes
  • Antonym: clothed - wearing clothes

Etymology

From the buff-colored leather, originally referring to a natural, light brown leather made from buffalo hide; the phrase 'in the buff' came to mean 'naked' as if only clothed in one's own skin.
