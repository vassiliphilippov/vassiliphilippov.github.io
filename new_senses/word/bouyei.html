Bouyei

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈjeɪ / (AmE), / bəʊˈjeɪ / (BrE)
A Tai language spoken mainly in southern and southwestern Guizhou Province in China, particularly in Buyi-Miao and Miao-Dong autonomous prefectures, as well as parts of Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.
Tai language of southern China

Examples

He traveled to Yunnan to document oral narratives in Bouyei.
Linguists have published several studies on the grammar of Bouyei.
The Bouyei language is an important means of communication among ethnic groups in Guizhou.

Etymology

From Chinese 布依 (Bùyī), the name of the Bouyei people and their language.

2. NOUN NEW

/ boʊˈjeɪ / (AmE), / bəʊˈjeɪ / (BrE)
A member of the Bouyei (Buyi) ethnic group, a Tai-speaking people native to Guizhou and nearby provinces of China.
member of the Bouyei people

Examples

A Bouyei elder showed us traditional embroidery patterns passed down through her family.
The Bouyei community in Guizhou holds an annual festival with music and dance.
Researchers interviewed three young Bouyei about language transmission and cultural identity.
