consensus

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / kənˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
a general agreement among a group of people, especially in terms of beliefs, judgments, or decisions
general agreement within a group

Examples

After hours of discussion, the committee finally reached a consensus on the best course of action.
It is difficult to achieve consensus when everyone has different priorities.
There was a growing consensus that changes needed to be made to the policy.

Synset Examples

the lack of consensus reflected differences in theoretical positions
those rights and obligations are based on an unstated consensus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties
  • Antonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or harmony, especially in opinion

Etymology

From Latin consensus (agreement, accord), from consentire (to agree).

(in computing, especially distributed systems) agreement among networked computers or processes about the state of shared data, reached by running a consensus protocol that tolerates failures and ensures consistency.
agreement among computers in a distributed system

Examples

The distributed ledger must reach consensus among all nodes before finalizing a block.
After a node failed, the cluster used Raft to reestablish consensus and resume accepting writes.
Designing fault-tolerant systems requires careful testing of the protocol that achieves consensus reliably.
