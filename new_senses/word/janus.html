Janus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪnəs / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the god of doorways, beginnings, and transitions, often depicted with two faces looking in opposite directions.
Roman god of doorways and transitions

Examples

In art, Janus is often shown with two faces, symbolizing his ability to look both to the past and the future.
Many historians associate Janus with the concept of transition and new beginnings.
The temple of Janus in Rome was kept open during times of war and closed during peace.

Etymology

From Latin 'Janus', related to 'ianua' meaning 'door'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdʒeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪnəs / (BrE)
A small particle (often at the micro- or nanoscale) whose surface has two sides with different chemical or physical properties, used in materials science and nanotechnology for directional interactions, self-assembly, or targeted delivery.
two-sided colloidal/nanoparticle

Examples

Researchers engineered Janus particles with hydrophobic and hydrophilic sides for controlled self-assembly.
The drug reached the tumor more effectively when delivered by Janus nanoparticles in trials.
Using magnetic and catalytic faces, the team propelled a single Janus particle through the fluid.
