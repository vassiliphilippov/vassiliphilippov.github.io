hologram

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɒləɡræm / (BrE)
A photographic record produced by holography, containing information used to reproduce a three-dimensional image.
photographic record for 3D image (holography)

Examples

A hologram can store far more information than a traditional photograph.
Engineers use a hologram to analyze stresses within mechanical structures.
The artist displayed a hologram that appeared to float in mid-air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holography - the technique of producing holograms

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + -gram (something written or drawn).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhoʊləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɒləɡræm / (BrE)
A three-dimensional, often life-sized projected image of a person or object produced using holographic or related projection techniques, especially one shown on stage or in a live performance.
life-sized 3D stage projection

Examples

The hologram of the late star appeared on stage to perform an encore.
Festivalgoers gasped when a lifelike hologram of the singer moved across the arena.
Technicians adjusted the lights until the three-dimensional hologram looked convincingly real to everyone.

Synset Examples

The hologram of the late star appeared on stage to perform an encore.
Festivalgoers gasped when a lifelike hologram of the singer moved across the arena.
Technicians adjusted the lights until the three-dimensional hologram looked convincingly real to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holography - the technique of producing holograms and holographic images
  • Synonym: projection - the act or result of projecting an image or light to create a visible display

Etymology

From Greek holos ‘whole’ + -gram ‘something written or drawn’; extended in recent decades to refer to visible three-dimensional projections used in performances.
