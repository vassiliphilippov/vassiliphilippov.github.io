B2

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈtu / (AmE), / biː ˈtuː / (BrE)
A standard size of paper measuring 500 x 707 millimetres, used especially in printing and publishing.
standard paper size, 500 x 707 mm

Examples

He ordered a ream of B2 size for the art project.
The poster was printed on B2 paper for better visibility.
We need several B2 sheets for the layout designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: A2 - Standard paper size, 420 x 594 mm
  • Degree Lower: B4 - Standard paper size, 250 x 353 mm
  • Degree Higher: B1 - Standard paper size, 707 x 1000 mm

Etymology

Part of the ISO 216 international paper size system, where 'B' denotes the B series of paper sizes.

2. NOUN NEW

/ bi ˈtu / (AmE), / biː ˈtuː / (BrE)
A CEFR level indicating upper-intermediate proficiency in a language (especially English), showing the ability to understand complex texts, communicate fluently, and produce clear, detailed text on many subjects.
CEFR upper-intermediate level

Examples

She achieved a B2 level in English after completing the language program.
The language school requires applicants to demonstrate B2 competency before enrolling in advanced courses.
Employers often list B2 as the minimum English requirement for customer-facing positions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: B1 - CEFR level below B2 (intermediate)
  • Degree Higher: C1 - CEFR level above B2 (advanced)

Etymology

From the CEFR abbreviation (Common European Framework of Reference for Languages); the numeral 2 denotes the specific level.
