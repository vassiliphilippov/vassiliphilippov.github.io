curator

1. NOUN

/ kjʊˈreɪtər / (AmE), / kjʊəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a person in charge of a museum, library, or other collection, responsible for its care and management
person managing a museum or collection

Examples

As a curator, she oversees the preservation of rare manuscripts in the library.
He became the curator after years of studying art history and conservation.
The curator organized a new exhibit on ancient civilizations at the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custodian - a person who has responsibility for or looks after something; caretaker

Etymology

From Latin 'curator' (overseer, guardian), from 'curare' (to take care of).

/ kj\u028a\u02c8re\u026at\u0259r / (AmE), / kj\u028a\u0259\u02c8re\u026at\u0259 / (BrE)
a person who selects, organizes, and presents online content (such as articles, videos, or posts) for a particular audience or platform.
selector of online content

Examples

The website employed a curator to choose articles that matched readers' interests each week.
As a social media curator, she organized videos into playlists for the brand's followers.
Independent curators often build audiences by selecting high-quality posts and explaining why.
