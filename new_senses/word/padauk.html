padauk

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdaʊk / (AmE), / pəˈdaʊk / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to southeastern Asia, valued for its reddish wood with distinctive black mottled or striped grain.
tropical Asian tree with reddish wood

Examples

Carvers often choose padauk for its durability and beautiful grain patterns.
In the workshop, planks of padauk lay beside other exotic hardwoods.
The furniture was crafted from padauk, giving the room a warm, rich tone.

Etymology

From Hindi 'pādauk', via Burmese.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈdʊk / (AmE), / pəˈdɑːk / (BrE)
A dense, reddish hardwood obtained from padauk trees, often used in decorative cabinetwork and furniture making.
a reddish hardwood from padauk trees

Examples

African padauk is prized for its striking color and resistance to termites.
Many luxury guitar makers choose padauk for its beauty and durability.
The cabinet was made from rare padauk, giving it a distinctive rich hue.

Synset Examples

The padauk found most often is African Padauk from Pterocarpus soyauxii which, when freshly cut, is a very bright red/orange but when exposed to sunlight fades over time to a warm brown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - hardwood from various tropical trees, used for furniture and decorative items

Etymology

From Burmese 'pa-dauk', the name of the tree.
