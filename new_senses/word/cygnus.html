Cygnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs / (BrE)
a genus of large waterbirds in the family Anatidae, comprising the swans.
genus of swans

Examples

Most species within Cygnus are known for their remarkable grace and beauty.
Scientists study the migratory patterns of birds in the Cygnus genus.
The trumpeter swan belongs to the genus Cygnus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swan - a large waterbird with a long neck and white or sometimes black feathers

Etymology

From Latin 'cygnus' (swan), itself from Ancient Greek 'kyknos'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɪgnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪgnəs / (BrE)
A northern constellation lying between Pegasus and Draco, named after the Latin word for 'swan', and containing notable stars and a black hole.
a constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

Astronomers have discovered a black hole in the direction of Cygnus.
On a clear summer night, Cygnus is visible high in the northern sky.
The constellation Cygnus is often called the Northern Cross because of its shape.

Etymology

From Latin 'cygnus' meaning 'swan'.
