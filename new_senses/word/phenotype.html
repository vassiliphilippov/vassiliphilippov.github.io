phenotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːnəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈfiːnəʊtaɪp / (BrE)
the observable physical or biochemical characteristics of an organism, as determined by both genetic makeup and environmental influences
observable characteristics of an organism
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the phenotype to understand how genetic mutations affect physical traits.
Some diseases only manifest when a certain phenotype is expressed in combination with environmental factors.
The phenotype of the plant was influenced by both its genes and the amount of sunlight it received.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genotype - the genetic makeup of an organism

Etymology

From German 'Phänotypus', coined from Greek 'phainein' (to show) + 'typos' (type).

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8fid0n59ccta6ap / (AmE), / c8fid0n598ata6ap / (BrE)
(in digital health and computational research) a set of behavioural or health-related traits inferred about a person from digital data sources (for example, smartphones, wearables, or social-media activity).
traits inferred from digital data
medical

Examples

Researchers use smartphone data to build a phenotype that reflects a patient's digital health profile.
The study derived a wearables-based phenotype showing sleep patterns and activity levels over months.
Clinicians can use a patient's social media phenotype to screen for mood changes between visits.
