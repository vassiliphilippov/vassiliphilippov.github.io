monolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːn.ə.lɪθ / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.lɪθ / (BrE)
a large single upright block of stone, especially one that forms a pillar or monument
large single upright stone block

Examples

Archaeologists studied the markings carved into the surface of the monolith.
The ancient civilization erected a gigantic monolith that still stands today.
Tourists traveled miles to view the mysterious monolith in the desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obelisk - a tall, four-sided stone pillar tapering toward a pyramidal top

Etymology

From Greek monolithos, from monos (single) + lithos (stone).
