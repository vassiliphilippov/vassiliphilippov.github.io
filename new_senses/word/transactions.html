transactions

1. NOUN

/ trænˈzækʃənz / (AmE), / trænˈzækʃənz / (BrE)
a written record or published account detailing the proceedings or activities that took place at a meeting or within an organization, often used as the title of academic society publications.
written record of meeting proceedings

Examples

Researchers often refer to the transactions of professional organizations when citing key scientific discoveries.
The annual transactions of the society include detailed reports from both the chairman and the treasurer.
The secretary compiled the transactions to provide an official record of the meeting's outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minutes - a summarized written record of what was said and decided at a meeting

Etymology

From Latin 'transactio' (a doing, a driving through), via transact (to carry out, perform).

2. NOUN NEW

/ trænˈzækʃən / (AmE), / trænˈzækʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(in computing and finance) a single recorded operation in a digital ledger that documents the transfer of value or information between parties, typically including sender, recipient, amount, timestamp, and a cryptographic signature.
digital‑ledger record of transfer

Examples

Every new transaction on the blockchain is timestamped and visible to all network participants.
The wallet showed three pending transactions that required confirmation before the funds would move.
During the audit, they traced each suspicious transaction back to its origin address on the ledger.
