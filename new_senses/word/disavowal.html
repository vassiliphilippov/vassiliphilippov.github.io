disavowal

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
the act of stating publicly that you have no knowledge of or connection with something or someone
denial of connection or knowledge

Examples

Her public disavowal of the controversial statement did little to repair her reputation.
The politician's disavowal of any involvement in the scandal was met with skepticism.
There was a clear disavowal of responsibility from the company following the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial - refusal to acknowledge or accept something
  • Antonym: avowal - a declaration or affirmation of something, especially in public

Etymology

From Old French 'desavouer' (to disavow), from Latin prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'avow' (to declare).
