Bo Hai

1. PROPN

/ boʊ haɪ / (AmE), / bəʊ haɪ / (BrE)
an inlet of the Yellow Sea located on the eastern coast of China, often considered the innermost gulf of the Bohai Sea region.
inlet of the Yellow Sea in eastern China

Examples

Cargo ships regularly pass through the Bo Hai to reach northern Chinese ports.
Major rivers such as the Yellow River flow into the Bo Hai.
Winter temperatures in the Bo Hai can lead to the formation of sea ice.

2. PROPN NEW

/ boʊ haɪ / (AmE), / bəʊ haɪ / (BrE)
the industrial and economic region surrounding the Bohai Sea in northeastern China; often used to refer to the Bohai Economic Rim (BER).
economic region around the Bohai Sea

Examples

Policy makers are promoting investment across the Bo Hai to boost the regional economy.
Many manufacturing firms relocated to the Bo Hai after incentives were offered by local authorities.
Analysts expect strong GDP growth in the Bo Hai as ports and tech clusters expand.
