chicory

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəri / (BrE)
a perennial herb with bright blue flowers, cultivated for its edible roots and leaves, often used in salads or as a coffee substitute
edible herb with blue flowers

Examples

Fresh chicory leaves add a pleasant bitterness to any salad.
Some coffee blends contain roasted chicory roots for added flavor.
The blue flowers of chicory bloom along country roads in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endive - a leafy plant related to chicory, used in salads

Etymology

From Latin 'cichorium', from Greek 'kikhórion'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.əri / (BrE)
A plant with crisp, spiky leaves that have a somewhat bitter taste, often used in salads.
crisp, bitter salad vegetable

Examples

I added some chicory to the salad for extra crunch and flavor.
Many chefs use chicory as a substitute for lettuce in gourmet salads.
The chicory leaves gave the dish a pleasantly bitter edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endive - a similar leafy vegetable, sometimes used as a synonym or variant
  • Degree Lower: lettuce - a milder-flavored leafy vegetable commonly used in salads
  • Degree Higher: radicchio - a variety of chicory with red leaves and a bitter taste

Etymology

From Latin *cichorium*, from Greek *kikhoreia* (chicory).
