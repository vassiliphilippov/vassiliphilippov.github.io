heterosexualism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əˈsɛkʃuːəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈsɛkʃuːəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
sexual attraction to, or sexual relations with, persons of the opposite sex; heterosexual orientation.
sexual attraction to the opposite sex

Examples

His views on heterosexualism sparked controversy among activists.
Some researchers study the origins of both heterosexualism and homosexuality.
The documentary explores different perspectives on heterosexualism in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterosexuality - sexual attraction toward persons of the opposite sex
  • Antonym: homosexuality - sexual attraction toward persons of the same sex

Etymology

From 'heterosexual' + '-ism', modeled on similar formations denoting orientation.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cch\u025bt\u032c\u0259\u02c8s\u025bk\u0283u\u02d0\u0259l\u02cc\u026az\u0259m / (AmE), / \u02cch\u025bt\u0259\u02c8s\u025bk\u0283u\u02d0\u0259l\u026az(\u0259)m / (BrE)
An ideological or social system that treats heterosexuality as the normal or preferred form of sexuality and that marginalizes or discriminates against non-heterosexual orientations and relationships.
ideology privileging heterosexuality

Examples

Scholars argued that state policy enforces heterosexualism by privileging traditional families over LGBTQ+ families.
Every textbook should explain how heterosexualism shapes institutions and everyday social expectations for non-heterosexual people.
Campaigners called the law an example of heterosexualism that systematically limited recognition of same-sex couples.
