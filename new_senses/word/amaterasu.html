Amaterasu

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːməˈtɛrɑːsuː / (AmE), / ˌaməˈtɛrasuː / (BrE)
In Shinto religion, the central sun goddess regarded as the mythological ancestress of the Japanese imperial family.
Shinto sun goddess and imperial ancestress

Examples

Amaterasu is revered in Shinto as the goddess of the sun and universe.
Shrines dedicated to Amaterasu can be found throughout Japan, most notably at Ise.
The emperor is traditionally said to be a direct descendant of Amaterasu.

Etymology

From Japanese 天照 (Amaterasu), short for 天照大神 (Amaterasu-ōmikami, literally ‘the great august kami who shines in the heavens’).
