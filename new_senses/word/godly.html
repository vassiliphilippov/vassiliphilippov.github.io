godly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdli / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdli / (BrE)
showing great reverence or devotion to God; devout or pious.
showing great reverence for God

Examples

He led a godly life, attending church regularly and helping those in need.
She was admired for her godly compassion and kindness toward others.
The community respected her godly wisdom and generosity.

Synset Examples

a godly man
leading a godly life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pious - devoutly religious or showing deep respect for religion
  • Antonym: ungodly - not showing reverence or respect for God; impious

Etymology

From Old English godlic, meaning 'of God', from 'God' + '-ly'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdli / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdli / (BrE)
emanating from or relating to God; divine in origin or quality.
emanating from or relating to God

Examples

Her compassion was so profound that some saw it as inspired by godly love.
Many people believed that the prophet's wisdom had a truly godly origin.
They viewed the miracle as a godly sign guiding their actions.

Synset Examples

divine judgment
divine guidance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - of, from, or like God or a god
  • Antonym: satanic - emanating from Satan; devilish

Etymology

From Old English godlic (divine), related to god ('God') + -ly (adjectival suffix).
