to refloat

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈfloʊt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfləʊt / (BrE)
to set (a boat or vessel that has run aground or been stranded) afloat again
set afloat again

Examples

After repairs were completed, they managed to refloat the sailboat successfully.
Engineers were called in to refloat the damaged ferry.
It took hours to refloat the ship after it ran aground on the sandbar.

Synset Examples

refloat a grounded boat

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From re- (again) + float (to rest or move on the surface of a liquid).

2. VERB NEW

/ ccrid0c8flo8at / (AmE), / ccrid0c8fl598at / (BrE)
(in finance) to reintroduce a company's shares onto a stock market or relist a company for public trading, often after a delisting, rescue, or restructuring.
relist on a stock market

Examples

The company announced plans to refloat its shares after completing the restructuring next month.
Investors welcomed the decision to refloat the bank because it would increase liquidity and confidence.
After the takeover the board successfully refloated the firm, returning it to public trading.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relist - to list again on a stock exchange
  • Synonym: float - to offer a company's shares to the public for trading
  • Antonym: to delist - to remove a company's shares from a stock exchange

Etymology

From re- + float (to float a company = to list its shares on a stock market).
