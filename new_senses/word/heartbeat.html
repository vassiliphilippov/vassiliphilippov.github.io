heartbeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrtˌbiːt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbiːt / (BrE)
the regular movement or sound of the heart as it pumps blood, especially as felt in the arteries
regular movement of the heart

Examples

After running up the stairs, he could feel his heartbeat pounding in his chest.
Her heartbeat quickened as she waited for the results.
The doctor listened carefully to the baby's heartbeat during the examination.

Synset Examples

he could feel the beat of her heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - the regular throbbing of the arteries caused by heart contractions

Etymology

From Middle English 'herte' (heart) + 'beat', formed in English.

4. NOUN NEW

/ c8h51rtccbit / (AmE), / c8h51d0tccbid0t / (BrE)
(in computing) a periodic signal sent between systems or components to indicate they are operating and reachable; used to detect failures and trigger recovery.
periodic liveness signal (computing)

Examples

The monitoring service sends a heartbeat every few seconds to confirm each server is still alive.
When a node stops replying to heartbeats, the cluster manager marks it as failed and reallocates tasks.
Engineers configured a watchdog to restart the service if no heartbeat arrives for thirty seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ping - a simple network request used to test whether a remote host is reachable

Etymology

From Middle English 'herte' (heart) + 'beat'; used metaphorically in computing to describe a regular 'pulse' signal.
