pew

1. NOUN

/ pju / (AmE), / pjuː / (BrE)
A long bench with a back, placed in rows in a church, for the congregation to sit on.
long bench in a church for seating

Examples

She knelt quietly in the front pew during the service.
The old wooden pews creaked as the congregation stood for the hymn.
The wedding guests filled every pew in the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bench - a long seat for several people

Etymology

From Middle French 'puie' (balcony, balustrade) and Old French, ultimately from Latin 'podium'.

2. INTJ NEW

/ pju / (AmE), / pjuː / (BrE)
An interjection or onomatopoeic exclamation imitating a small laser, ray, or gun sound, used in comics, games, online chat, and playful speech.
onomatopoeic laser/gun sound
informal

Examples

The child ran across the yard shouting pew while pretending to shoot an imaginary ray gun.
Comic book sound effects like pew and zap help show the laser beams hitting the robot.
In the chat, he typed pew to mock the game's empty laser shots after missing every target.
