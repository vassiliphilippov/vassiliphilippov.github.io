discriminative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
Able to recognize or make fine distinctions between things; showing or having good judgement in distinguishing between things.
capable of making fine distinctions

Examples

A discriminative approach is important when hiring new employees.
Her discriminative palate made her an exceptional food critic.
The test was designed to be highly discriminative between different skill levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminating - having or showing refined taste or good judgement

Etymology

From Latin discriminativus (serving to distinguish), from discriminare (to divide, separate).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
showing careful and discerning judgment or the ability to differentiate finely between things
showing careful judgment; discerning

Examples

A discriminative approach ensures that only the most relevant details are considered.
Her discriminative analysis helped distinguish subtle differences in the data.
The critic was known for his discriminative censure of new films.

3. ADJ NEW

/ dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(in machine learning) relating to models or methods that learn to distinguish or classify by predicting labels or the conditional probability P(y|x) directly, rather than modelling how the data were generated.
relating to ML models that predict classes directly

Examples

A discriminative model learns to predict labels directly from inputs in many classification tasks.
Researchers prefer discriminative approaches when accuracy on labelled data is more important than generation.
The textbook contrasted a discriminative classifier with a generative model to explain their differences.

Synset Examples

A discriminative model learns to predict labels directly from inputs in many classification tasks.
Researchers prefer discriminative approaches when accuracy on labelled data is more important than generation.
The textbook contrasted a discriminative classifier with a generative model to explain their differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classifier - a program or model that assigns labels or categories to inputs; often implemented with discriminative methods.
  • Antonym: generative (model) - a model that learns a joint or data-generating distribution (often P(x,y)); commonly presented as the opposite of discriminative models.

Etymology

From Latin discriminativus (serving to distinguish), from discriminare (to divide, separate).
