port

1. NOUN

/ pɔrt / (AmE), / pɔːt / (BrE)
an opening in the side of a wall, ship, or armored vehicle, especially one used for firing weapons through
opening for firing through in a vehicle or wall

Examples

A small port was cut into the wall so defenders could fire at attackers.
Soldiers took positions by each port to repel the assault.
The gun crew aimed through the port in the armored vehicle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrasure - an opening in a wall for firing through, especially in a fortification

Etymology

From French *porte* (door) and Latin *porta* (gate, door), reflecting its function as an opening or gateway.
