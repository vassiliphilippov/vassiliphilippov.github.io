FCS

1. NOUN

/ ɛf si ɛs / (AmE), / ɛf si ɛs / (BrE)
a cooperative nationwide system of banks and associations in the United States that provides credit to farmers and related businesses; originally federally capitalized, now owned by members and borrowers
U.S. farm credit banking system
american

Examples

Farmers joined the FCS to access competitive loan rates and cooperative ownership.
Many agricultural businesses rely on the FCS for flexible credit options and financial services.
The FCS played a crucial role in supporting rural communities during difficult economic times.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Farm Credit System, established in the United States in 1916.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɛf si ɛs / (AmE), / ɛf si ɛs / (BrE)
(in biology and laboratory work) fetal calf serum; a supplement derived from fetal blood added to cell culture media to provide growth factors and nutrients.
fetal calf serum (cell culture supplement)
medical

Examples

The cells were cultured in medium supplemented with 10% FCS to promote growth.
Before experiments, the technician thawed aliquots of FCS and stored them on ice.
Many labs prefer heat-inactivated FCS to reduce complement activity in serum.
