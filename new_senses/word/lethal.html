lethal

1. ADJ

/ ˈliθəl / (AmE), / ˈliːθəl / (BrE)
Able to cause death; deadly or fatal, especially when referring to weapons, substances, or actions.
deadly; capable of causing death

Examples

A lethal injection was administered as the method of execution.
Certain chemicals are lethal even in very small doses.
The suspect was carrying a lethal weapon during the robbery.

Synset Examples

deadly poisons
lethal weapon
a lethal injection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly - able or likely to cause death
  • Antonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm or death

Etymology

From Latin letalis (deadly), from letum (death).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈliθəl / (AmE), / ˈliːθəl / (BrE)
Informal. (figurative) Extremely effective, powerful, or impressive; causing decisive defeat or damage (not literally deadly).
figuratively extremely effective or impressive

Examples

The striker proved lethal in the final minutes, scoring twice to win the match.
Her new routine at the skate park was lethal, with flips that amazed every onlooker.
When he presents, his arguments are lethal, leaving rivals little chance to respond effectively.

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: harmless - not able to cause damage or harm; lacking power to injure or defeat
