annotator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.əˌteɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈæn.əˌteɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who adds explanatory notes or comments to a text
person who adds notes to a text

Examples

A skilled annotator can greatly enhance the understanding of a complex text.
Every scientific article in the collection was reviewed by an expert annotator.
The annotator provided additional historical context in the margins of the manuscript.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commentator - a person who writes or speaks opinions or explanations about something

Etymology

From Latin 'annotator' (one who marks or comments), from 'annotare' (to annotate).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u00e6n.\u0259\u02ccte\u026a.t\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8\u00e6n.\u0259\u02ccte\u026a.t\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing and machine learning) a person who labels, tags, or marks data (images, text, audio, etc.) to create annotated datasets used for training or evaluating AI systems.
person who labels data for machine learning

Examples

A data annotator labels thousands of images to train the model's vision system.
Many companies hire freelance annotators to tag audio clips for speech recognition projects.
The lead engineer reviewed each annotator's work to ensure consistent labels across the dataset.
