trapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræpər / (AmE), / ˈtræpə / (BrE)
a person who catches wild animals, especially for their fur, by setting traps
person who traps animals for fur

Examples

During the 19th century, a trapper could make a living selling beaver pelts.
Modern wildlife laws limit what a trapper can catch and sell.
The trapper checked his snares early every morning in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunter - a person who hunts wild animals for food or sport

Etymology

From trap + -er, dating from the 16th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtræpər / (AmE), / ˈtræpə / (BrE)
a musician or producer who performs or creates trap music, a subgenre of hip-hop noted for heavy 808 bass, rapid hi-hats, and dark lyrical themes.
performer or producer of trap music

Examples

The rising trapper from Atlanta released a high-energy trap track that topped charts.
At the studio, the trapper layered heavy 808s and skittering hi-hats over the chorus.
Fans praised the new album because the trapper blended melodic hooks with gritty street lyrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap artist - a musician who performs or records trap music
  • Synonym: trap producer - a music producer who creates beats and tracks in the trap style

Etymology

From trap (music), itself named after 'trap' (a place where drugs are sold); the musical sense emerged in Southern US hip-hop in the late 1990s–2000s.
