to infest

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛst / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛst / (BrE)
(of insects or parasites) to inhabit or overrun a place, animal, or plant, typically in large numbers and causing harm or annoyance.
to overrun or live on/in as parasites

Examples

Parasitic worms infest the intestines of their hosts, leading to health problems.
The old house was infested with termites, requiring immediate attention.
Ticks often infest dogs and can cause discomfort or disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invade - to enter forcibly or spread into a place, often causing harm
  • Synonym: inhabit - to live in or occupy

Etymology

From Latin infestare (to attack, disturb).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛst / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛst / (BrE)
(of animals, insects, or plants) to be present in large numbers in a place, especially so as to cause damage or discomfort.
to overrun in large numbers (often pests)

Examples

Certain parasites infest the fur of wild animals in this region.
If left unchecked, weeds can quickly infest a garden, choking out other plants.
The kitchen became infested with ants after we left food out overnight.

Synset Examples

the Kudzu plant infests much of the South and is spreading to the North

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invade - to enter forcefully as an enemy or in large numbers, especially in reference to living things
  • Antonym: desert - to abandon or leave a place, sometimes leaving it empty or unoccupied

Etymology

From Latin infestare 'to attack, disturb', from infestus 'hostile'.

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛst / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛst / (BrE)
(of animals or insects) to be present in large numbers, typically so as to cause damage or disease.
to invade in large numbers (animals, insects)

Examples

After the flood, mosquitoes began to infest the area, making it difficult to go outside.
If you don't keep food sealed, ants will quickly infest your kitchen.
The old barn was infested with rats and had to be cleaned thoroughly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - to cause continual trouble or distress to
  • Antonym: desert - (of animals, insects) to leave a place, opposite of infest

From Latin infestare (to attack, disturb).

overrun computer systems with malware

Examples

After the phishing attack, hackers infested the company's network with ransomware overnight.
Security software failed to detect the botnet that began infesting hundreds of IoT devices last month.
When the USB drive was plugged in, malicious scripts started to infest her laptop immediately.
