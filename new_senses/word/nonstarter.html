nonstarter

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈstɑrtər / (AmE), / nɒnˈstɑːtə / (BrE)
a person who is consistently unsuccessful, especially one who fails to succeed or be considered seriously
a consistently unsuccessful person

Examples

After three unsuccessful campaigns, some labeled the candidate a political nonstarter.
Because she never finished her assignments, her teacher called her a nonstarter.
Despite his enthusiasm, many considered him a nonstarter in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loser - a person who is habitually unsuccessful or defeated

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'starter' (one who starts), originally from horse racing jargon.

2. NOUN

/ nɑnˈstɑrtər / (AmE), / nɒnˈstɑːtə / (BrE)
(in horse racing) A horse that fails to participate in a race for which it was entered.
horse not starting in a race

Examples

Due to an injury, the favorite became a nonstarter on race day.
Several nonstarters were listed on the official race results sheet.
The trainer withdrew the nonstarter just moments before the race began.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: starter - a horse that starts in a race
