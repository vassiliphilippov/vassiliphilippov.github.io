extractive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the removal of natural resources from the earth, such as minerals, oil, or gas
relating to resource extraction from the earth

Examples

Many conflicts in the region are driven by interests in extractive resources such as copper and gold.
New regulations aim to make extractive industries more environmentally responsible.
The country's economy is heavily dependent on its extractive sector, especially mining and oil.

Synset Examples

most of the skeptics in the geology community are either older or employed in the extractive industries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - to remove or obtain something, especially using effort

Etymology

From Medieval Latin extractivus, from Latin extrahere (to draw out).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
(of an economic or political system) prioritizing short-term profit or resource extraction over long-term sustainability, often benefiting a small elite.
prioritizing short-term profit over sustainability

Examples

Critics argued that the extractive economic system undermined opportunities for sustainable growth.
Policies favoring extractive practices can lead to environmental degradation and social inequality.
The country's extractive institutions have concentrated wealth and power in the hands of a few.

Synset Examples

On the other hand, if extractive political institutions are implemented, then power will be consolidated within a small number of groups

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inclusive - (of a system) designed to benefit a broad base of the population; encouraging participation or inclusion

Etymology

From extract + -ive, modeled after the noun 'extraction' and verb 'extract'.

3. ADJ NEW

/ ɪkˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
(in natural language processing) relating to methods that create summaries or answers by selecting and reusing exact words or sentences from source text rather than generating new phrasing.
NLP method selecting existing text

Examples

The research team chose an extractive approach to summarization to preserve exact quotes from the interviews.
Many news aggregators rely on extractive methods that select full sentences rather than generate new phrasing.
Because the model is limited, it produced only an extractive summary consisting of three original article sentences.

Synset Examples

The research team chose an extractive approach to summarization to preserve exact quotes from the interviews.
Many news aggregators rely on extractive methods that select full sentences rather than generate new phrasing.
Because the model is limited, it produced only an extractive summary consisting of three original article sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extractive summarization - a summarization technique that constructs a summary by selecting and combining existing sentences or passages
  • Antonym: abstractive - creating summaries by generating new text that may paraphrase or fuse information from the source

Etymology

From extract + -ive; ultimately from Latin extrahere ‘to draw out’.
