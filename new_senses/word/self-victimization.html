self-victimization

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌvɪktɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the tendency to perceive oneself as a victim, often resulting in feelings of powerlessness and hindering personal growth or problem-solving
perceiving oneself as a victim

Examples

Constant self-victimization can damage relationships and prevent personal progress.
Her pattern of self-victimization made it difficult for her to take responsibility for her actions.
Therapists often work with clients to help them overcome self-victimization and develop resilience.

Synset Examples

He realized his self-victimization was holding him back.
Self-victimization can be a barrier to personal growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim mentality - an attitude of consistently perceiving oneself as a victim

2. NOUN NEW

/ sɛlf ˌvɪktɪməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌvɪktɪməˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in public discourse, media, or online) the deliberate presentation of oneself as a victim in order to gain sympathy, attention, or social advantage.
deliberate, performative victimhood

Examples

During the campaign, the candidate's repeated self-victimization aimed to elicit sympathy from undecided voters.
Influencers sometimes use self-victimization in posts to attract comments and boost engagement metrics.
Colleagues complained that her constant self-victimization was a tactic to avoid responsibility at work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim mentality - a general tendency to see oneself as a victim; a related but more internal psychological pattern
