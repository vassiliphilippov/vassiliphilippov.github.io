deflationary

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfleɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃənəri / (BrE)
(of economic conditions or policies) tending to cause a decrease in consumer prices or an increase in the purchasing power of money.
tending to cause falling prices or rising money value

Examples

A deflationary period can lead to reduced consumer spending and economic slowdown.
Analysts warned that the central bank's actions might have a deflationary effect on the economy.
The government introduced deflationary policies to combat excessive inflation.

Synset Examples

deflationary measures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflationary - tending to cause increases in consumer prices or decreases in purchasing power

Etymology

From 'deflation' (Latin 'deflare', to blow away) + '-ary'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ dɪˈfleɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃənəri / (BrE)
(of a cryptocurrency or token) designed so that its total supply decreases over time (for example by burning tokens), creating scarcity.
reducing a token's supply over time

Examples

Many investors prefer deflationary tokens because automatic burns reduce supply and may raise price over time.
The new cryptocurrency's deflationary design destroys a portion of each transaction to limit total circulating tokens.
Critics argue a deflationary token might encourage hoarding, reducing spending and harming the network's economy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflationary - increasing a currency's supply or tending to cause rising prices; opposite of deflationary in tokenomics.
  • Synonym: token burn - the deliberate destruction or removal of cryptocurrency tokens from circulation to reduce supply.
