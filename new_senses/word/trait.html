trait

1. NOUN

/ treɪt / (AmE), / treɪt / (BrE)
a particular quality or characteristic of someone’s personality or nature that distinguishes them from others
distinctive personal quality or feature

Examples

Her creativity is a trait admired by her colleagues.
Honesty is a valuable trait in any friendship.
Patience is not a trait that comes easily to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristic - a typical feature or quality of someone or something
  • Antonym: flaw - a fault or weakness in a person's character

Etymology

From French 'trait' (feature, stroke), from Latin 'tractus' (dragged out, line, feature).

2. NOUN NEW

/ treɪt / (AmE), / treɪt / (BrE)
(in programming) a reusable unit of methods and behavior that can be mixed into classes or types to add functionality without relying on classical inheritance.
programming unit of reusable methods

Examples

In Scala, a trait defines methods and fields that classes can reuse without full inheritance.
She used multiple traits to compose functionality across different classes in the project.
When adding logging, they created a lightweight trait that provided the necessary helper methods.
