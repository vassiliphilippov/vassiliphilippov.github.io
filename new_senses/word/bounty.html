Bounty

1. PROPN

/ ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
the British naval ship HMS Bounty, famous for the 1789 mutiny by part of its crew against Captain William Bligh
historic British ship of 1789 mutiny

Examples

After the mutiny, the Bounty was sailed to Pitcairn Island by the mutineers.
Captain Bligh showed remarkable navigation skills after being set adrift from the Bounty.
The story of the Bounty mutiny has inspired numerous books and films.

Etymology

Named HMS Bounty by the Royal Navy in 1787; 'bounty' from Middle English, meaning 'goodness, generosity'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity and software development) a reward offered by an organisation to someone who reports a security flaw, bug, or vulnerability so it can be fixed.
reward for reporting security flaws

Examples

The company offered a generous bounty to anyone who found and reported critical security bugs.
Independent researchers earned a small bounty after responsible disclosure of the website vulnerability.
Many startups run public bounty programs to encourage reporting before attackers exploit flaws.
