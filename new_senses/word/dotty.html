dotty

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑti / (AmE), / ˈdɒti / (BrE)
infatuated with or extremely fond of someone or something; obsessed.
infatuated or obsessed with
informal

Examples

Ever since he got that new game, he's been completely dotty over it.
My grandparents are still dotty about each other after fifty years of marriage.
She's absolutely dotty about classic movies and never misses a film festival.

Synset Examples

crazy about cars and racing
they are dotty about each other
gaga over the rock group's new album

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy about - wildly enthusiastic or obsessed with someone or something
  • Synonym: keen on - very much liking or interested in something or someone

Etymology

Possibly from 'dot', an old dialectal term for a small person or child, later used to mean a foolish person.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːti / (AmE), / ˈdɒti / (BrE)
informal: slightly crazy or eccentric in behavior or thinking; mildly insane.
slightly crazy or eccentric
informal

Examples

He wore mismatched socks and hats, making him appear rather dotty to the neighbors.
My grandmother can be a bit dotty at times, but everyone loves her stories.
Some people thought the old inventor was dotty, but his ideas were ingenious.

Synset Examples

it used to drive my husband balmy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged; extremely eccentric or wild
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill or eccentric

Etymology

From 'dot,' meaning 'mental feebleness,' possibly influenced by the idea of someone 'dotting' around aimlessly.
