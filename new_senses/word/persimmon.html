persimmon

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsɪm.ən / (AmE), / pəˈsɪm.ən / (BrE)
Any of several tropical or subtropical trees of the genus Diospyros, especially those bearing edible orange-red fruit also called persimmons.
a tropical tree or its orange fruit

Examples

Ripe persimmons are sweet and often used in desserts.
The leaves of the persimmon turn a vibrant red in autumn.
We planted a persimmon tree in the backyard last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: date-plum - a type of persimmon (Diospyros lotus)
  • Synonym: Diospyros - the genus of trees including persimmons

Etymology

From Powhatan (an Algonquian language) *pichamin*, meaning 'dry fruit'.

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsɪmən / (AmE), / pəˈsɪmən / (BrE)
A sweet, orange fruit resembling a plum, edible when fully ripe, produced by trees of the genus Diospyros.
type of sweet orange fruit

Examples

The persimmon must be fully ripe before eating, or it will taste very astringent.
The dessert was topped with slices of fresh persimmon and a sprinkle of cinnamon.
We picked a basket of persimmons from the tree in my grandfather's garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a sweet, fleshy fruit with a central stone

Etymology

From Powhatan (Virginia Algonquian) *pichamin* or *pushemin* (a dry fruit).

3. ADJ NEW

/ pərˈsɪm.ən / (AmE), / pəˈsɪm.ən / (BrE)
(in colour, design, and fashion) a warm orange-red colour, similar to the typical colour of ripe persimmon fruit.
warm orange-red colour

Examples

She chose a persimmon dress that brightened the evening and drew many compliments.
The interior designer painted the living room in a warm persimmon tone to energize the space.
For autumn marketing they used a persimmon background that contrasted well with the white logo.
