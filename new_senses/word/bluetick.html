bluetick

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈbluːtɪk / (BrE)
A breed of American hound known for its speed and endurance, typically white with bluish-grey mottling.
American hound breed with blue-grey mottling
american

Examples

The bluetick howled excitedly when it picked up a fresh scent on the trail.
The hunter relied on his trusted bluetick to track raccoons through the forest.
We adopted a playful bluetick from the animal shelter last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coonhound - a type of hound bred in the United States for hunting raccoons

Etymology

From 'blue' and 'tick,' referring to the coat's blue-grey mottled pattern.
