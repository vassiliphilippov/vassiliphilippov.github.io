wide-screen

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪd ˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd ˌskriːn / (BrE)
(of motion pictures or television) displayed or projected with a width significantly greater than its height, especially wider than the traditional format.
having a wide aspect ratio (film/TV)

Examples

Many modern films are shot in wide-screen format to provide a more immersive experience.
She prefers the wide-screen version of the film to see the director's full vision.
The new television has a wide-screen display, perfect for watching movies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letterbox - displaying wide-screen images on standard-size screens with black bars above and below
  • Antonym: pan-and-scan - method of adjusting wide-screen film to fit a standard-width screen
