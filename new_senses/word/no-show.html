no-show

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
a person who fails to arrive for a reservation or appointment without giving prior notice, especially at a hotel or restaurant
person failing to attend a reservation

Examples

If you can't make your appointment, please call ahead so you won't be marked as a no-show.
The hotel charges a fee if you are a no-show for your reservation.
There were three no-shows at the dinner party last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the act of cancelling or being cancelled; related to missing reservations

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ ˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ ˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
a person who is expected but fails to appear, especially for an appointment or at an event
person who fails to appear as expected

Examples

If you're a no-show for your appointment, you might be charged a fee.
The concert organizers kept a list of no-shows for future reference.
Three guests turned out to be no-shows at the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absentee - a person who is not present at a usual or expected place
  • Antonym: attendee - a person who is present at an event or place
