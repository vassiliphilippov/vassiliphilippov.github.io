2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.təl / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.tl̩ / (BrE)
A reptile with a bony or leathery shell, typically living in water or on land, and having flipper-like limbs adapted for swimming.
reptile with a hard shell, often aquatic

Examples

Children watched as the turtle poked its head out of its shell.
Many species of turtle are endangered due to habitat loss and pollution.
The turtle slowly made its way across the sandy beach toward the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortoise - A land-dwelling reptile with a hard shell

Etymology

From Late Latin 'tartarucha', from Greek 'tartarouchos', later influenced by 'tortoise'.

3. VERB

/ ˈtɝːtəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːtəl / (BrE)
(of a boat or vessel) to turn over completely so that the bottom is uppermost, especially by accident.
to overturn completely (nautical)

Examples

After the storm, they found their dinghy had turtled on the lake.
If you lean too far over the side, the canoe could turtle and throw everyone into the water.
The small sailboat began to turtle when the wind picked up suddenly.

Synset Examples

Don't rock the boat or it will capsize!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsize - to overturn (a boat) in the water
  • Antonym: right - to return a vessel to an upright position

Etymology

From the image of a turtle on its back; likely influenced by nautical slang.
