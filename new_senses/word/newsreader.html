newsreader

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌriːdər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌriːdə / (BrE)
a person who presents news broadcasts on television, radio, or other media by reading the news to the audience
person who reads the news on broadcasts

Examples

Every evening, the newsreader summarizes the day’s most important stories for viewers.
She has worked as a newsreader on national television for over a decade.
The newsreader calmly delivered the breaking news despite the chaos in the studio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newscaster - a person who presents news during a news program
  • Synonym: anchor - the main news presenter on a television or radio program

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈnuːzˌriːdər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌriːdər / (BrE)
a computer program or mobile app that gathers news items from multiple websites or web feeds (such as RSS) and displays them in one place.
app or program that aggregates news

Examples

I opened my newsreader to skim headlines from several outlets before breakfast.
The newsreader updated every hour so she never missed important international stories.
Students used a simple newsreader to collect articles for their media studies project.
