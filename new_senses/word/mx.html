Mx

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ˈɛks / (AmE), / ɛm ˈɛks / (BrE)
a cgs unit of magnetic flux equal to one maxwell, the flux perpendicular to an area of 1 square centimeter in a magnetic field of 1 gauss
unit of magnetic flux (maxwell)

Examples

A change of 1 Mx in magnetic flux induces a small electromotive force in the circuit.
Older physics textbooks often expressed magnetic flux in Mx rather than webers.
The strength of the field was measured in Mx using specialized scientific equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxwell - cgs unit of magnetic flux equal to one Mx

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'maxwell', named after James Clerk Maxwell (1831–1879).
