Go

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ / (BrE)
a board game for two players who take turns placing black or white counters on the intersections of a grid, aiming to encircle and capture the opponent's pieces
strategic board game from East Asia

Examples

Go requires deep strategic thinking and patience.
He has been playing Go competitively for ten years.
They spent the afternoon learning how to play Go together.

Etymology

From Japanese 'go', ultimately from Chinese 'wéiqí' (圍棋, encircling game).

2. PROPN NEW

/ ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ / (BrE)
a statically typed, compiled programming language created at Google, designed for simplicity, performance, and easy concurrency.
programming language by Google

Examples

She deployed a microservice written in Go to handle high traffic.
The team rewrote the backend in Go to improve performance and reduce memory usage.
Many cloud companies prefer Go for concurrent servers because its tools simplify parallel programming.
