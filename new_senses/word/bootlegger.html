bootlegger

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡə / (BrE)
a person who makes, distributes, or sells alcohol illegally, especially during Prohibition in the United States
person who illegally sells alcohol

Examples

During Prohibition, many bootleggers supplied illegal alcohol to speakeasies across the city.
Her grandfather used to tell stories about outsmarting the authorities as a bootlegger in the 1920s.
The police arrested a notorious bootlegger after months of surveillance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - person who illegally moves goods across borders
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - person who obeys the law

Etymology

From 'bootleg', originally referring to hiding liquor in a boot top.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡə / (BrE)
a person who makes, copies, or distributes unauthorized recordings or other media (such as films, music, or software) for sale or distribution.
seller of unauthorized media copies

Examples

Authorities arrested a bootlegger who distributed unauthorized films on DVD in the city.
Fans bought concert recordings from a bootlegger outside the venue despite the band's protests.
The cybersecurity team identified a bootlegger selling pirated software through several international websites.

Synset Examples

Fans bought concert recordings from a bootlegger outside the venue despite the band's protests.
The cybersecurity team identified a bootlegger selling pirated software through several international websites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - to make, distribute, or sell unauthorized copies of media or software
  • Antonym: authorized distributor - a person or company licensed to distribute media lawfully

Etymology

From 'bootleg' (originally hiding liquor in a boot); later extended from illegal alcohol trade to unauthorized recordings and copies in the 20th century.
