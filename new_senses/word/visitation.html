visitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an official visit, especially for inspection or supervision purposes
official visit for inspection or supervision

Examples

During the commissioner’s visitation, several reforms were suggested for the police precincts.
The bishop's annual visitation of the churches was an important event for the community.
The principal scheduled a visitation to ensure that all classrooms met safety standards.

Synset Examples

the commissioner made visitations to all the precinct stations
the recent visitation of the bishop to his diocese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - the act of examining or reviewing officially

Etymology

From Latin 'visitatio' (a visit), from 'visitare' (to visit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an annoying, distressing, or catastrophic event, especially one seen as a punishment or test
an affliction or catastrophe

Examples

Many in the village believed the illness was a visitation brought on by their collective misdeeds.
The early settlers dreaded every visitation of locusts that could destroy their crops.
The town suffered a visitation of flooding after weeks of heavy rain.

Synset Examples

his mother-in-law's visits were a great trial for him
life is full of tribulations
a visitation of the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribulation - a cause of great trouble or suffering
  • Synonym: affliction - something that causes pain or suffering
  • Antonym: blessing - a beneficial thing for which one is grateful

Etymology

From Latin visitatio (a going to see, visitation), from visitare ('to go to see, visit').

3. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪzɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a disaster or catastrophe, especially seen as a punishment or supernatural occurrence
disaster or catastrophe

Examples

Medieval chroniclers wrote about the visitation of the Black Death as divine retribution.
Superstitious people believed the epidemic was a visitation for the village's past sins.
The town was devastated by a visitation of floods that ruined crops and homes.

Synset Examples

a visitation of the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and often sudden damage or distress
  • Synonym: catastrophe - a sudden event causing great damage or suffering
  • Antonym: blessing - a beneficial thing for which one is grateful; something bringing well-being

Etymology

From Latin visitatio (an act of visiting); sense of 'disaster' dates to Middle English, influenced by Biblical and legal language.
