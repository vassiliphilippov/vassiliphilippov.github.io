hitchrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪtʃˌræk / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃˌræk / (BrE)
A fixed horizontal rail or post to which horses are tied to prevent them from wandering away.
rail for tying horses

Examples

Children played near the hitchrack while their parents tended to business inside.
He tied the mare to the hitchrack while he went into the general store.
The old stable still had a hitchrack out front for tying up horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hitching post - a post to which a horse may be tied

Etymology

From 'hitch' (to tie or fasten) and 'rack' (a framework or structure for holding or storage).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhɪtʃˌræk / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃˌræk / (BrE)
A rack designed to be attached to a vehicle's trailer hitch for transporting one or more bicycles.
vehicle-mounted bicycle rack

Examples

She loaded two bicycles onto the hitchrack for the cross-country trip.
He bought a heavy-duty hitchrack to carry his mountain bike safely to the trailhead.
Make sure the hitchrack is locked before you drive away with the bikes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bike rack - a device for carrying bicycles on a vehicle
  • Synonym: hitch-mounted rack - a rack that is mounted on a vehicle's trailer hitch

Etymology

Compound of hitch + rack; named for a rack that attaches to a vehicle's trailer hitch.
