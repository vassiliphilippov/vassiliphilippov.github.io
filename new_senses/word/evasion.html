evasion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the deliberate act of avoiding paying money, such as debts or taxes
deliberate avoidance of payment

Examples

His frequent evasion of rent payments led to his eviction from the apartment.
The company was fined for tax evasion after an investigation by authorities.
The new laws are designed to make financial evasion more difficult for individuals and businesses.

Synset Examples

his evasion of all his creditors
he was indicted for nonpayment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avoidance - the action of keeping away from something

Etymology

From Latin 'evasio', from 'evadere' meaning 'to escape'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a statement that avoids directly addressing an unpleasant truth, often by being indirect or misleading without being literally false
indirect way of avoiding the truth

Examples

He was caught using an evasion to avoid admitting his mistake.
Her answer was an evasion rather than a clear response to the difficult question.
When pressed for details, the politician's evasion frustrated the reporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevarication - the act of speaking or acting in an evasive way
  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being clear and straightforward

Etymology

From Latin evasio (a going out, escape), from evadere (to escape, avoid).

/ ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
