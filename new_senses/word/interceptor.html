interceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a fast and agile fighter aircraft designed to intercept and destroy enemy planes, especially bombers
fast fighter plane for intercepting enemy aircraft

Examples

During the Cold War, many countries developed advanced interceptors to protect their airspace.
The air force deployed an interceptor to challenge the approaching enemy bombers.
The new interceptor is capable of reaching supersonic speeds within minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fighter - a military aircraft designed for air-to-air combat

Etymology

From Latin 'intercipere' (to seize, intercept) via French and English forms; adapted to aircraft in the 20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌɪntərˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛptə / (BrE)
(in computing) a software component or middleware module that intercepts, inspects, and optionally modifies or handles data, requests, or responses passed between systems or components (for example, HTTP interceptors).
software component that intercepts requests

Examples

The Angular interceptor logs outgoing HTTP requests and automatically adds the user's auth token.
During testing, our interceptors blocked malformed messages before they reached the backend service.
You can implement a logging interceptor to measure response times for all API calls in production.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: middleware - software that connects or mediates between different applications or services

Etymology

From Latin 'intercipere' (to seize, intercept); the general sense 'to intercept' has been extended to computing to name components that capture and handle messages.
