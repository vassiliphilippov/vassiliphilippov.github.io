polyvalent

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a vaccine, serum, or antibody) containing several antibodies, each effective against a specific antigen.
effective against several antigens or toxins
medical

Examples

A polyvalent serum was administered to treat the mixed bacterial infection.
Doctors recommend a polyvalent antivenom for bites from snakes with varied toxins.
The new polyvalent vaccine helps protect against several different strains of the virus.

Synset Examples

a polyvalent vaccine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monovalent - containing a single type of antibody or effective against a single antigen

Etymology

From French 'polyvalent', from Greek 'poly-' (many) + Latin 'valentia' (power, capacity).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element or ion) having more than one valence, or a valence of three or higher.
having more than one valence (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Copper is considered a polyvalent element because it can have more than one valence state.
In the lab, a polyvalent ion can participate in several different types of chemical bonds.
The vaccine is polyvalent, protecting against multiple strains of the virus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monovalent - having a valence of one
  • Antonym: bivalent - having a valence of two

Etymology

From French *polyvalent*, from Greek *poly-* (many) + Latin *valentia* (strength, capacity).
