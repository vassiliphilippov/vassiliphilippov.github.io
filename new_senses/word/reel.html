reel

1. NOUN

/ ril / (AmE), / riːl / (BrE)
a lively folk dance of Scottish or Irish origin; also, the music composed for such a dance, typically in quick 2/4 or 4/4 time.
type of lively dance or its music

Examples

She learned to distinguish between a jig and a reel by listening to the rhythm.
The band played a traditional reel at the ceilidh, delighting the dancers.
The lively notes of the reel filled the hall with energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jig - a lively folk dance or its music, especially in 6/8 time

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'rèil' or Irish 'ríl', referring to the dance.
