robustness

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbʌstnəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈbʌstnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong, healthy, or able to withstand difficult conditions
strength or healthiness

Examples

His robustness made recovery from the illness much faster than expected.
The robustness of the bridge was proven during the violent storm.
These plants are known for their robustness in harsh climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • Antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From robust + -ness; ultimately from Latin robustus (strong, hardy).

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈbʌst.nəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈbʌst.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong or resilient enough to withstand intellectual challenge or criticism, especially in arguments, theories, or findings
strength or resilience to criticism or challenge

Examples

Researchers questioned the robustness of the data due to inconsistencies in the results.
The robustness of a scientific theory is tested by repeated experimentation and skepticism.
The robustness of the company's argument impressed the panel of experts.

Synset Examples

3. NOUN NEW

/ roʊˈbʌstnəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈbʌstnəs / (BrE)
(in machine learning and computer science) the ability of a model or system to maintain performance when inputs are noisy, perturbed, or subject to adversarial manipulation.
ML model resilience to noisy/adversarial inputs

Examples

Researchers measured the robustness of the model against noisy test data and adversarial attacks.
Improving a model's robustness often requires more training data or specialized regularization techniques.
The team tested the robustness of their system by adding random noise to input images.
