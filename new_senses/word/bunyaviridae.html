Bunyaviridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌniˌvɛrɪˈdiːi / (AmE), / ˌbjuːniːˌvɛərɪˈdiːiː / (BrE)
A large family of viruses, many of which are transmitted by arthropods and affect vertebrates and arthropods.
family of viruses affecting vertebrates and arthropods
medical

Examples

Researchers are studying the transmission of Bunyaviridae among mosquitoes.
Several members of the Bunyaviridae are known to be responsible for outbreaks in livestock.
The Bunyaviridae family includes viruses that can cause serious diseases in humans and animals.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Bunyamwera' (a town in Uganda; where the original virus was isolated) and the family suffix '-viridae'.
