homebrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌbru / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌbruː / (BrE)
An alcoholic beverage, especially beer, that is brewed at home rather than in a commercial brewery.
alcoholic drink made at home

Examples

At the festival, you could sample different types of homebrew from local enthusiasts.
He takes pride in the homebrew he crafts in his basement.
She gave me a bottle of her latest homebrew as a holiday gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beer - alcoholic drink made by fermenting cereal grains

Etymology

From 'home' + 'brew', indicating brewing at home rather than commercially.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhoʊmˌbru / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌbruː / (BrE)
Software or hardware created by hobbyists for consumer electronics or game consoles, typically unofficial and shared by enthusiasts.
hobbyist-made software or hardware

Examples

He downloaded a new homebrew app for his handheld game console last night.
The developer posted several homebrew plugins that add features to the smart TV.
At the meetup, enthusiasts swapped tips for creating homebrew hardware for vintage consoles.

Synset Examples

From 'home' + 'brew' (originally meaning brewed at home); extended metaphorically to hobbyist-produced software and hardware.
