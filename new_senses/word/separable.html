separable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛpərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being divided, separated, or dissociated from something else.

(in computer science, especially deep learning) describing an operation or layer in which spatial filtering and channel mixing are performed separately (for example, depthwise separable convolution), typically to reduce computation and parameters.
in deep learning: factorized operation

Examples

The model uses separable convolutions to reduce computation in the mobile vision pipeline.
Implementing separable layers allowed the team to run the network on phones with limited memory.
Researchers compared standard and separable convolutions and found similar accuracy but much faster performance.
