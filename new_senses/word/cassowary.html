cassowary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsəˌwɛri / (AmE), / ˈkæsəwəri / (BrE)
a large, black, flightless bird native to Australia and New Guinea, known for its distinctive helmet-like head crest
large flightless bird of Australia/New Guinea

Examples

The cassowary is considered one of the most dangerous birds in the world due to its powerful legs and sharp claws.
Unlike other flightless birds, the cassowary has a distinctive casque or helmet on its head.
We spotted a wild cassowary while hiking through the rainforest in northern Queensland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emu - large, flightless Australian bird related to the cassowary

Etymology

From Malay 'kasuari', referring to the bird.
