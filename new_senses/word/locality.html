locality

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈkælɪti / (AmE), / ləʊˈkælɪti / (BrE)
an area or region near or surrounding a particular place; a neighborhood or district.
a nearby area or district

Examples

After moving to a new locality, it took her a while to make friends.
Several rare plant species can be found only in this locality.
The museum is located in a quiet locality just outside the city center.

Synset Examples

the plane crashed in the vicinity of Asheville
it is a rugged locality
he always blames someone else in the immediate neighborhood
I will drop in on you the next time I am in this neck of the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicinity - the area near or surrounding a particular place
  • Synonym: neighborhood - a district or area in a city or town

Etymology

From Latin 'localitas', from 'locus' (place).

2. NOUN NEW

/ loʊˈkælɪti / (AmE), / ləʊˈkælɪti / (BrE)
(computing) The tendency for a processor to access the same or nearby memory locations within a short time; keeping data or computation physically or logically close to reduce memory/cache access time and improve performance.
data/cache locality (computing)

Examples

Modern compilers optimize locality to reduce cache misses and make programs run faster.
Good locality in data structures means related items are stored near each other in memory.
Engineers improve locality by reorganizing loops to access arrays in a cache-friendly order.

Synset Examples

Modern compilers optimize locality to reduce cache misses and make programs run faster.
Good locality in data structures means related items are stored near each other in memory.
Engineers improve locality by reorganizing loops to access arrays in a cache-friendly order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locality of reference - the principle that programs tend to access the same set of memory locations repeatedly in a short period
  • Synonym: cache locality - the property of a program's memory access pattern that makes good use of the CPU cache
