Trojan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒən / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the ancient city of Troy, especially as mentioned in classical literature
native of ancient Troy

Examples

According to legend, the Trojans were famed for their courage and endurance.
Homer's Iliad tells the story of the war between the Greeks and the Trojans.
Many artifacts have been found in the area believed to be the ancient city of the Trojans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Greek - a native or inhabitant of ancient Greece, often opposed to Trojans in classical literature

Etymology

From Latin 'Troianus', from 'Troia' (Troy).
