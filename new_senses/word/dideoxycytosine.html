dideoxycytosine

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌdiːɑksiˈsaɪtoʊˌsiːn / (AmE), / daɪˌdiːɒksiˈsʌɪtəʊˌsiːn / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection; a nucleoside analog that inhibits reverse transcriptase
antiviral drug for HIV treatment
medical

Examples

Research has shown that dideoxycytosine can effectively inhibit the replication of the virus.
Side effects associated with dideoxycytosine may include nausea and neuropathy.
The patient was prescribed dideoxycytosine as part of his HIV treatment regimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ddC - common abbreviation for dideoxycytosine
  • Degree Higher: nucleoside analog - compound resembling a natural nucleoside, used in antiviral therapy
  • Degree Higher: reverse transcriptase inhibitor - drug that blocks the enzyme reverse transcriptase

Etymology

From 'di-' (two) + 'deoxy-' (lacking oxygen) + 'cytosine' (a component of DNA), describing the molecule's structure.
