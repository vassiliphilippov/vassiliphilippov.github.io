baking

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
Very hot, as if heated in an oven; uncomfortably hot.
uncomfortably hot, like an oven

Examples

After walking for hours in the baking sun, they finally found some shade.
It was a baking afternoon, with temperatures soaring above 40 degrees Celsius.
The baking air in the small office made it hard to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorching - extremely hot
  • Antonym: freezing - extremely cold
  • Degree Lower: warm - mildly hot

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of cooking food by dry heat, especially in an oven.
cooking by dry oven heat

Examples

Modern baking techniques allow for healthier homemade treats.
She enjoys baking as a weekend hobby, especially making cakes and pastries.
The baking of fresh bread filled the house with a wonderful aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roasting - cooking food by exposing it to dry heat, especially in an oven or over a fire

Etymology

From the verb 'bake', from Old English *bacan* (to bake).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computer graphics) the process of precomputing lighting, shadows, or other shading information into texture maps (lightmaps) so that scenes render faster in real time.
precomputing lighting into textures

Examples

Texturing artists rely on baking to store complex lighting information in compact texture files.
Before export, the team performed a final baking pass to embed shadows into the game's lightmaps.
Real-time engines often use GPU-accelerated baking workflows to reduce artists' iteration time.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightmapping - the process of baking lighting into textures (lightmaps) for use in real-time rendering
  • Synonym: precomputation - the act of calculating data in advance so it can be used quickly at runtime

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the verb ‘bake’ (to cook); applied in computing to mean ‘prepare in advance’. From Old English *bacan* (to bake).
