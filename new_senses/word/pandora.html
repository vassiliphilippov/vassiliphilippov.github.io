Pandora

1. PROPN

/ pænˈdɔrə / (AmE), / pænˈdɔːrə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the first woman, created by Hephaestus at the command of Zeus, who was given a box containing all the world's evils.
first woman in Greek mythology

Examples

According to the myth, Pandora was responsible for opening the forbidden box that released evils into the world.
Many scholars analyze the symbolism of Pandora in ancient Greek literature.
The story of Pandora serves as a warning about curiosity and disobedience in mythology.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πανδώρα (Pandṓra), meaning ‘all-gifted’ or ‘all-giving’.

2. PROPN NEW

/ pænˈdɔrə / (AmE), / pænˈdɔːrə / (BrE)
the fictional moon in the film Avatar (2009), an alien world known for its bioluminescent forests, floating mountains, and diverse native life.
fictional moon in the film Avatar

Examples

In the film Avatar, explorers reach Pandora, a vibrant alien moon with glowing forests and floating mountains.
Scientists in the story study Pandora's bioluminescent ecosystem to understand its unusual plant communication.
Theme-park designers created a ride inspired by Pandora to give visitors a sense of floating mountains.
