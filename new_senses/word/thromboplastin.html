thromboplastin

1. NOUN

/ ˌθrɑːmboʊˈplæstɪn / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəʊˈplæstɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme released from blood platelets that helps to convert prothrombin into thrombin during blood clotting
enzyme involved in blood clotting
medical

Examples

A deficiency in thromboplastin can lead to disorders involving excessive bleeding.
The laboratory used thromboplastin to measure the patient's blood clotting time.
The release of thromboplastin initiates an important step in the coagulation cascade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prothrombin - a plasma protein converted into thrombin in the clotting process
  • Synonym: thrombin - an enzyme that converts fibrinogen to fibrin in the clotting process

Etymology

From Greek *thrombos* (clot) + *plastos* (formed).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cc\u03b8r\u0251\u02d0mbo\u028a\u02c8pl\u00e6st\u026an / (AmE), / \u02cc\u03b8r\u0252mb\u0259\u028a\u02c8pl\u00e6st\u026an / (BrE)
(in laboratory medicine) a reagent containing tissue factor (and usually phospholipids) used to initiate the extrinsic coagulation pathway in diagnostic clotting tests such as the prothrombin time (PT).
laboratory reagent for clotting tests
medical

Examples

In the lab, technicians used a thromboplastin reagent to start the PT assay.
To measure clotting, the pathologist added thromboplastin to the patient's plasma and timed clot formation.
Several manufacturers supply standardized thromboplastin preparations for consistent prothrombin time testing across laboratories.
