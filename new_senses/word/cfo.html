CFO

1. NOUN

/ si ɛf oʊ / (AmE), / siː ɛf əʊ / (BrE)
the executive in a company or organization responsible for managing its financial actions, planning, and reporting
executive responsible for finances

Examples

After a merger, the new CFO implemented stricter budget controls across all departments.
She was promoted to CFO after ten years of experience in finance and accounting.
The CFO presented the quarterly financial report at the shareholders' meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chief Financial Officer - the executive in charge of financial activities for an organization

Etymology

Abbreviation of Chief Financial Officer.

2. NOUN NEW

/ si \u025bf o\u028a / (AmE), / si\u02d0 \u025bf \u0259\u028a / (BrE)
informal, humorous title for a person (often in a team or small company) responsible for organizing social activities, boosting morale, or managing workplace culture rather than finances.
humorous title for person in charge of office fun
informal

Examples

The startup appointed a CFO to organize weekly game nights and surprise treats for staff.
As their CFO, Jenna planned team retreats, birthday celebrations, and morale workshops every quarter.
Nobody expected the company's CFO to lead morning yoga and free pizza Fridays.
