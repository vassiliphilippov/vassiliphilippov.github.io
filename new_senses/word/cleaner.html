cleaner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːnər / (AmE), / ˈkliːnə / (BrE)
a person or business that cleans clothes professionally, especially the operator of a dry-cleaning establishment.
professional who operates a dry-cleaning business

Examples

I dropped off my suit at the cleaner this morning before work.
Many people prefer to use an eco-friendly cleaner for their delicate clothes.
The cleaner promised my dress would be ready by Friday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launderer - person or business that washes and irons clothes

2. NOUN

/ ˈkliːnər / (AmE), / ˈkliːnə / (BrE)
a substance or preparation used for cleaning surfaces or objects
substance for cleaning

Examples

Keep all household cleaners out of reach of children.
She used a special cleaner to remove the stains from the carpet.
This cleaner is safe to use on kitchen surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detergent - a cleaning agent, especially for laundry or dishwashing

Etymology

From 'clean' + '-er', meaning something that cleans.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkliːnər / (AmE), / ˈkliːnə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to clean houses, offices, or other spaces
person whose job is cleaning

Examples

After the party, I hired a professional cleaner to restore order to my flat.
Hospitals rely on each cleaner to help prevent the spread of infection.
The office cleaner arrives early every morning to tidy up the workspace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: janitor - a person employed as a caretaker of a building; often responsible for cleaning
  • Synonym: maid - a female domestic worker who cleans homes or hotels
  • Antonym: dirty - not clean; covered or marked with an unclean substance

Etymology

From clean + -er, meaning one who cleans.
