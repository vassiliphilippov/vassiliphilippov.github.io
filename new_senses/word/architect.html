architect

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkɪˌtɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtɛkt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to design buildings and often supervise their construction
person who designs buildings

Examples

As an architect, she enjoys finding creative solutions to design challenges.
Every architect must balance artistic vision with practical needs.
The architect drew up detailed plans for the new city library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: designer - a person who plans the form, look, or workings of something before making it
  • Synonym: engineer - a person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or structures

Etymology

From Greek *arkhitektōn*, from *arkhi-* (chief) + *tektōn* (builder).

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈɑrkɪtɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪtɛkt / (BrE)
to design or plan the overall structure of a system, project, or solution, especially software or digital systems.
design system architecture

Examples

They asked the senior engineer to architect the company's new cloud infrastructure this quarter.
She has architected several microservices platforms to handle millions of daily requests.
Startups often architect solutions quickly, then refine them after initial user feedback.
