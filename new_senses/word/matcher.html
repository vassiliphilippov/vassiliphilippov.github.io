matcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmætʃə / (BrE)
a person who arranges or tries to arrange marriages between others; a matchmaker
person who arranges marriages

Examples

As a professional matcher, she attends many social events to find potential pairs.
In some cultures, a matcher is a respected figure at weddings.
The matcher introduced several couples who later married.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matchmaker - a person who arranges marriages or romantic relationships

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'match' (to bring together as partners) with agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmætʃər / (AmE), / ˈmætʃə / (BrE)
(in computing) a software component or algorithm that identifies matches between inputs and patterns or candidate items, used in pattern matching, information extraction, or record linkage.
software component that finds matches

Examples

The text processor uses a matcher to locate dates in the document.
Our email system's spam filter uses a rule-based matcher to flag suspicious messages each hour.
During testing, engineers compared two matchers to see which found duplicate records more accurately and faster.
