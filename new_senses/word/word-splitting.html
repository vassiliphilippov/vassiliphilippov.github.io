word-splitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːdˌsplɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdˌsplɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making excessively fine or trivial distinctions, often over matters of little importance
making overly fine distinctions

Examples

His word-splitting was seen as unhelpful during the negotiations.
Some critics accuse the court of word-splitting rather than addressing the core issue.
The debate turned into endless word-splitting about the definition of a single term.

Synset Examples

they didn't take his hairsplitting seriously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairsplitting - making excessively fine or trivial distinctions

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8w\u025d\u02d0d\u02ccspl\u026at\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8w\u025c\u02d0d\u02ccspl\u026at\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
In natural language processing, the process of dividing text into words or tokens for computational analysis, especially important for languages that lack explicit word boundaries.
NLP tokenization/segmentation

Examples

Before training the model, we applied word-splitting to all raw text in the corpus.
Languages like Chinese require careful word-splitting because there are no spaces between words.
The preprocessing pipeline included sentence segmentation, tokenization, and word-splitting by rule-based heuristics.
