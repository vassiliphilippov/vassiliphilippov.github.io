cancelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.səld / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səld / (BrE)
(of an event, arrangement, etc.) no longer planned or scheduled; called off.
(of events) no longer planned or scheduled

Examples

All flights were cancelled because of the strike.
Due to heavy snow, tonight's performance has been cancelled.
The meeting is cancelled and will be rescheduled for next week.

Synset Examples

the wedding is definitely off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off - (of events) cancelled or not happening
  • Antonym: scheduled - planned or arranged to happen at a particular time

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'cancel', from Latin 'cancellare' (to cross out, annul).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈkæn.səld / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səld / (BrE)
(informal) publicly criticised, ostracized, or boycotted—especially on social media—so that a person, group, or work loses support, reputation, or opportunities.
publicly ostracized on social media
informal

Examples

After the scandal, the actor was publicly cancelled and lost several endorsement deals.
A tweet criticizing his remarks meant he quickly became cancelled by many followers.
Even old films are sometimes cancelled by groups who find their content offensive today.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostracize - to exclude or banish someone from a group or society
  • Synonym: boycott - to refuse to support or buy from someone as a protest

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'cancel', from Latin 'cancellare' (to cross out, annul); extended in the 21st century to mean publicly ostracized via online backlash.
