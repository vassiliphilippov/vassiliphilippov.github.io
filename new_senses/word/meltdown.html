meltdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɛltdaʊn / (BrE)
a severe overheating of the core of a nuclear reactor, causing the core to melt and potentially release radiation
severe nuclear reactor accident

Examples

A nuclear meltdown can result in catastrophic environmental damage.
After the meltdown, large areas had to be evacuated due to radiation risk.
Engineers worked desperately to prevent a potential meltdown at the plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactor accident - an accident involving a nuclear reactor

Etymology

From 'melt' + 'down'; originally technical use referring to melting of a reactor's core.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɛltdaʊn / (BrE)
a sudden and disastrous failure, especially one that is catastrophic or total in nature
catastrophic collapse or failure

Examples

After several technical errors, the company experienced a complete meltdown.
Analysts fear that a political meltdown could destabilize the country.
The financial meltdown of 2008 had global consequences.

Synset Examples

there is little likelihood of a meltdown comparable to the American banking collapse in March 1933

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - a sudden failure or breakdown
  • Synonym: catastrophe - a sudden and widespread disaster
  • Antonym: recovery - a return to a normal state after a collapse

Etymology

From 'melt' + 'down', originally referring to the melting of a nuclear reactor's core.

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8m\u025bltda\u028an / (AmE), / \u02c8m\u025bltda\u028an / (BrE)
(in computing, especially cybersecurity) a hardware security vulnerability in many modern processors that can allow ordinary (unprivileged) programs to read protected kernel memory, breaking process isolation.
CPU hardware security flaw

Examples

Security researchers warned that the meltdown vulnerability allowed unprivileged programs to read protected memory.
After the report, companies patched systems to protect against meltdown and similar processor flaws.
Many users installed updates quickly when news of meltdown affected cloud services and laptops.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security vulnerability - a weakness in a system that can be exploited to cause harm or gain unauthorized access
  • Synonym: hardware bug - a flaw in a computer's physical components or microarchitecture that causes incorrect or insecure behaviour

Etymology

Named metaphorically after the nuclear term 'meltdown' (from 'melt' + 'down'), applied to a processor failure/security flaw.
