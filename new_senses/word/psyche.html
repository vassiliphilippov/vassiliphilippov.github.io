Psyche

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪki / (AmE), / ˈsaɪki / (BrE)
A beautiful princess in Greek mythology, loved by Cupid, who became the personification of the soul.
mythological princess and personification of the soul

Examples

Artists through the ages have depicted Psyche as the embodiment of the soul.
In the myth, Psyche must never look at Cupid, her mysterious lover.
The story of Psyche and Cupid explores themes of love and trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soul - spiritual or immaterial part of a person
  • Synonym: Cupid - Roman god of love, Psyche's lover

Etymology

From Greek Psukhē, meaning 'soul' or 'spirit'.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ˈsaɪki / (AmE), / ˈsaɪki / (BrE)
The NASA interplanetary mission and spacecraft launched to study the metal-rich asteroid 16 Psyche, intended to investigate planetary cores and metal-rich bodies.
NASA spacecraft mission to asteroid 16 Psyche

Examples

NASA's Psyche mission will travel to the metal-rich asteroid to study planetary cores.
Scientists hope the data returned by Psyche will reveal how rocky planets formed long ago.
During the lecture she showed images from Psyche to explain asteroid composition and metal distribution.
