Izanagi

1. PROPN

/ iːˌzɑːˈnɑːgi / (AmE), / iːˌzanˈɑːgi / (BrE)
a deity in Shinto mythology, regarded as the god who, with his sister Izanami, created the islands and gods of Japan
Japanese Shinto creation god

Examples

According to legend, Izanagi purified himself after visiting the underworld, giving birth to other deities.
Many shrines across Japan are dedicated to the worship of Izanagi.
The story of Izanagi and Izanami is fundamental to Japanese mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Izanami - the goddess who, with Izanagi, created the islands and gods of Japan

Etymology

From Japanese イザナギ (Izanagi), literally 'he who invites'.
