stewardship

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuɚdˌʃɪp, ˈstjuɚdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈstjʊədˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of a steward
the office or position of a steward

Examples

He was entrusted with the stewardship of several important estates.
His stewardship as head of the foundation lasted for more than a decade.
Under her stewardship, the finances of the club greatly improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steward - a person who manages the property or affairs of another

Etymology

From 'steward' + '-ship', modelled on other office/role nouns.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8stu\u025ad\u02cc\u0283\u026ap, \u02c8stju\u025ad\u02cc\u0283\u026ap / (AmE), / \u02c8stj\u028a\u0259d\u02cc\u0283\u026ap / (BrE)
(in data management) the organised management and governance of data assets, including responsibilities for data quality, privacy, lineage, and compliance.
management and governance of data assets

Examples

The company established stewardship of data to ensure quality and privacy across departments.
Effective stewardship of customer records requires clear ownership, documented lineage, and regular audits by IT.
Under her stewardship, the new data governance framework reduced duplicate records and compliance risk.
