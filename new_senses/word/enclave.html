enclave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.kleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒn.kleɪv / (BrE)
a territory, or part of a territory, surrounded by a foreign territory and culturally distinct from it
territory surrounded by another, culturally distinct

Examples

Political tensions increased after the government tried to annex the ethnic enclave.
The city is home to a vibrant enclave of artists with their own distinct traditions.
The small mountain village formed a cultural enclave within the larger region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exclave - a portion of a state or territory geographically separated from the main part by surrounding foreign territory

Etymology

From French 'enclave,' from Medieval Latin 'inclavatus' (shut in).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u025bn.kle\u026av / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0252n.kle\u026av / (BrE)
(in computing) a secure, isolated execution environment provided by hardware or software that protects code and data from the rest of the system.
secure isolated execution environment (computing)

Examples

The processor provides a trusted enclave that keeps encryption keys safe from the operating system.
Developers run sensitive algorithms inside a hardware enclave to prevent data leakage during computation.
During the audit they verified that the cloud provider isolated each tenant's keys within separate enclaves.

Synset Examples
