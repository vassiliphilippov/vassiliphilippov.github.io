showroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌruːm / (BrE)
a room or area where goods, especially vehicles, are displayed for sale to customers
room for displaying goods for sale

Examples

Many customers visit the showroom before making a final purchase decision.
The new car was displayed in the brightly lit showroom at the dealership.
They spent an hour browsing furniture in the company's spacious showroom.

Synset Examples

in Britain a showroom is called a ‘salesroom’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salesroom - a room for displaying goods for sale (used in British English)

Etymology

From 'show' + 'room', first used in the early 20th century for rooms where goods were displayed.

(in retail and e-commerce) a virtual or online space where products are presented for browsing, demonstration, or interaction, often using images, videos, or 3D models.
virtual space for displaying products

Examples

Shoppers visited the virtual showroom to explore the new line of smartphones.
The carmaker launched an online showroom with 360-degree views and custom color options.
During lockdown, many boutiques used a digital showroom so customers could browse remotely.

Metaphorical extension of 'showroom' (show + room) to digital spaces used in e-commerce and online retail.
