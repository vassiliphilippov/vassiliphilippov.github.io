symbiosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪm.baɪˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪm.baɪˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a close relationship between two different species, in which each organism benefits from the association
mutually beneficial relationship between species

Examples

Lichens are formed by a symbiosis between an alga and a fungus.
Some scientists study symbiosis to understand how cooperation evolved in nature.
The clownfish and sea anemone have a classic symbiosis that protects both species from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutualism - a type of symbiosis in which both organisms benefit equally

Etymology

From Greek 'symbiōsis', meaning 'a living together', from 'symbios' (companion).
