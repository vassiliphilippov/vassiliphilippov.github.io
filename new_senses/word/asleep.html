asleep

1. ADJ

/ əˈslip / (AmE), / əˈsliːp / (BrE)
In a state of sleep; not awake.
in a state of sleep

Examples

By midnight, I was completely asleep after a busy day.
He fell asleep during the long movie.
The children were already asleep when their parents returned home.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: awake - not in a state of sleep

Etymology

Old English on slæpe (in sleep), from 'on' (in) + 'slæpe' (sleep).

3. ADJ

/ əˈslip / (AmE), / əˈsliːp / (BrE)
(of a limb or body part) numb or lacking sensation, usually as a result of temporary interference with blood flow or nerve signals.
numb; lacking sensation in a limb

Examples

After sitting awkwardly, my hand fell asleep and I couldn't feel my fingers.
If your foot is asleep, wait a few moments for the tingling to go away.
She shook her leg because it had gone asleep during the long meeting.

Synset Examples

4. ADJ NEW

/ əˈslip / (AmE), / əˈsliːp / (BrE)
(of a device or software) in a low-power or standby state; not fully active, with operations suspended to conserve energy.
in low-power standby mode (of devices)

Examples

The laptop went asleep after five minutes of inactivity to save battery power.
After the update, the server remained asleep until a scheduled task woke it.
Make sure the phone doesn't go asleep during the presentation by changing its power settings.

Synset Examples

The laptop went asleep after five minutes of inactivity to save battery power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep mode - a low-power state in electronic devices where most activity is suspended to save energy
  • Synonym: to hibernate - a deeper power-saving state that preserves more system state to disk before shutting down hardware
  • Antonym: awake - fully active; not in a low-power or suspended state

Etymology

From Old English on slæpe (in sleep); extended metaphorically to machines and software to describe low-power or standby states.
