autoregulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The automatic adjustment of internal processes by a cell or organism to maintain a stable physiological state despite changes in the external environment.
automatic maintenance of a constant physiological state
medical

Examples

Cerebral autoregulation ensures that the brain receives a steady supply of blood under different physiological conditions.
Research into vascular autoregulation helps doctors understand how tissues adapt to reduced oxygen levels.
The kidneys use autoregulation to maintain consistent blood flow, even when blood pressure fluctuates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeostasis - the tendency to maintain a stable internal environment

Etymology

From auto- (self) + regulation; originally used in physiology.

2. NOUN NEW

(in engineering and computing) the automatic adjustment of a system's internal settings by internal feedback mechanisms so the system maintains a stable output without external control.
automatic self-regulation in technical systems

Examples

The PID controller's autoregulation prevented output oscillations during sudden load changes.
In the embedded system, autoregulation keeps voltage stable without external adjustment.
Their simulation showed effective autoregulation of temperature through an internal feedback loop.
