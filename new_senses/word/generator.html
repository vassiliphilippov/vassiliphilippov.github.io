generator

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərətə / (BrE)
a machine that produces electrical energy from mechanical energy by electromagnetic induction
machine producing electricity

Examples

A faulty generator can lead to widespread blackouts if not repaired promptly.
Many campers bring a portable generator to provide electricity in remote areas.
The hospital used a backup generator during the power outage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamo - a device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy, especially by electromagnetic induction

Etymology

From Latin 'generare' (to produce, beget), via Medieval Latin 'generator'.
