calving

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of a cow or similar animal giving birth to a calf
the birth of a calf by a cow or similar animal

Examples

During calving, farmers monitor the herd closely to assist with any complications.
Proper nutrition is essential to support healthy calving in cows.
Spring is the busiest season for calving on the ranch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birthing - the process of giving birth (animals or humans)

Etymology

From 'calf' (young of cattle) + '-ing', forming a noun of action.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈkævɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a glacier, ice shelf, or ice sheet breaks off and releases large pieces of ice (icebergs) into the sea or lake.
glacier shedding ice (forming icebergs)

Examples

Scientists recorded increasing rates of calving along the glacier's seaward edge during summer melt.
Massive calving events can cause tsunamis when large icebergs fall into the ocean near shore.
Satellite images help researchers measure seasonal calving patterns and predict future sea-level contributions.
