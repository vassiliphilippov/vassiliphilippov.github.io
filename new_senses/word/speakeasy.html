speakeasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiˌkizi / (AmE), / ˈspiːkiːzi / (BrE)
(during Prohibition in the United States) a secret or illegal bar where alcoholic drinks were sold and consumed.
secret bar during Prohibition era

Examples

During the 1920s, many New Yorkers flocked to the local speakeasy to socialize and drink.
She discovered a hidden speakeasy behind an unmarked door downtown.
The film depicted the glamour and danger of life inside a speakeasy during Prohibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a place that sells alcoholic drinks
  • Synonym: tavern - a place where people drink alcoholic beverages and often eat meals

Etymology

American English, from 'speak easy' (i.e., speak quietly to avoid detection), first used in late 19th century and popularized during U.S. Prohibition (1920–1933).

2. NOUN NEW

/ spi kizi / (AmE), / spi ki zi / (BrE)
A modern, often hidden or password-protected bar or lounge that deliberately evokes the look and atmosphere of Prohibition-era secret bars; usually legal and marketed as exclusive or themed.
modern hidden or password bar

Examples

The new cocktail lounge is a stylish speakeasy with a password entry.
Friends planned a surprise night out at an underground speakeasy downtown.
She prefers quiet, hidden speakeasy venues that focus on craft cocktails and atmosphere.
