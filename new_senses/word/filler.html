filler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlə / (BrE)
the tobacco used to form the core of a cigar, around which the binder and wrapper are rolled.
tobacco inside the core of a cigar

Examples

He carefully selected the filler leaves to ensure an even draw.
Premium cigars often use long-leaf filler for a smoother smoke.
The quality of the filler greatly affects the flavor and burn of the cigar.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlə / (BrE)
a substance used to fill cracks, holes, or gaps in a surface before finishing or painting
substance for filling gaps or holes

Examples

Before painting, apply filler to any cracks in the wall.
Most car bodywork repairs require a special filler to smooth out dents.
The carpenter used a wood filler to repair the deep scratches in the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putty - a soft material for sealing or filling holes, typically in woodwork or glass panes

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlə / (BrE)
an article or copy used to fill space between more important material in a magazine or newspaper layout
copy to fill newspaper/magazine space

Examples

Magazines often use fillers when there isn't enough content to fill the layout.
That trivia column is just a filler and not meant to be taken seriously.
The editor included a short filler to occupy the blank space at the end of the page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: padding - material added to take up space, often of lesser importance; synonym in publishing contexts

Etymology

From English 'fill' + -er, indicating something that fills.

a substance or item added to fill out a space, mass, or quantity, often to complete or bulk up something
material used to fill space or bulk up

Examples

Some TV shows add extra scenes as fillers to reach the required length.
The recipe uses breadcrumbs as a filler to make the meatloaf more substantial.
This paint includes a filler to cover small cracks in the wall.
