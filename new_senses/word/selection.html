selection

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that has been chosen from among a group
person or thing chosen

Examples

Our selection for employee of the month received a standing ovation.
She was pleased to be the committee's selection for the scholarship.
The team's final selection was announced at the press conference.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - the act of selecting or the thing selected
  • Antonym: reject - a person or thing not chosen

4. NOUN

/ səˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
(in biology) the process by which organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and reproduce, leading to evolutionary change.

Examples

Environmental pressures play a key role in selection by favoring certain traits.
The rapid adaptation of bacteria to antibiotics is a result of natural selection.
