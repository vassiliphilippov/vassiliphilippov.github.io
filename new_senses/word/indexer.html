indexer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛksər / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɛk.sə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to compile or provide indexes, especially for books or databases.
person who compiles indexes

Examples

Being a professional indexer requires attention to detail and a systematic approach.
She worked as an indexer for a major publishing company, ensuring every topic was easy to find.
The library hired an experienced indexer to organize the new digital archive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: index - a list of topics or subjects at the end of a book

Etymology

From English 'index' (list of contents) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

(computing) a software program or component that automatically builds, updates, or maintains an index of content to enable fast search and retrieval.
software that builds search indexes

Examples

The operating system's indexer runs in the background to rebuild the search index.
Our database indexer updated its mappings overnight, so queries returned results much faster this morning.
During deployment, they disabled the indexer to prevent it from interfering with bulk data imports.
