shortcut

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt.kʌt / (BrE)
a route that is shorter than the usual way, taken to save time or distance
a quicker or shorter route

Examples

He found a shortcut that cut ten minutes off his daily commute.
If you know a good shortcut, please show me.
We decided to take a shortcut through the park to get home faster.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: detour - a longer or indirect route taken to avoid something or because the usual route is blocked

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0283\u0254rt\u02cck\u028ct / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0283\u0254\u02d0t.k\u028ct / (BrE)
a clickable icon or link on a computer, phone, or other device that provides quick access to a file, program, folder, or webpage.
digital quick-access icon or link

Examples

She pinned a shortcut to her desktop for quick access to the project folder.
To open the program faster, add a shortcut to the taskbar and click it.
He sent a shortcut to the shared drive so everyone could launch the tool.
