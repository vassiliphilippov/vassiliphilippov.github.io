future

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjʊtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃə / (BrE)
a contract for the sale or purchase of a commodity or financial instrument at a predetermined price for delivery at a specified time in the future.
contract for future delivery (finance)

Examples

He invested heavily in gold futures to hedge against inflation.
Oil futures rose sharply in response to the unrest in the Middle East.
The Chicago Board of Trade specializes in commodity futures markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - a contract to buy or sell an asset at a specified future time at a price agreed today

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈfjuː.tʃə / (BrE)
the time that will come after the present; the period of time following the moment of speaking or writing
the time yet to come

Examples

No one can predict the future with absolute certainty.
She is saving money for the future.
Technological advances will shape our future in unexpected ways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: past - the time before the present; earlier times
  • Antonym: present - the period of time occurring now

Etymology

From Latin 'futurus', future participle of 'esse' (to be), meaning 'about to be'.
