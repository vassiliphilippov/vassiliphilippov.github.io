high-potential

1. ADJ

/ haɪ pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / haɪ pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of electrical equipment) operating on or powered by a high voltage.
powered by high voltage

Examples

Make sure to use insulated gloves when working with high-potential equipment.
The lab technician inspected the high-potential generator before the experiment.
The transformer is designed to operate at high-potential for industrial applications.

Synset Examples

a high-voltage generator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-voltage - powered by or involving high voltage

Etymology

Formed from 'high' and 'potential', referring to electrical potential (voltage).

2. NOUN NEW

/ haɪ pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / haɪ pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
A person, especially an employee, who is identified by an organization as having the ability and qualities to achieve rapid advancement or future leadership.
employee with strong future potential

Examples

The company identified several high-potential employees who will be fast-tracked into leadership roles.
After the assessment, Maria was named a high-potential and enrolled in the management training program.
Managers meet monthly to discuss talent and decide which high-potential should receive mentoring and stretch assignments.
