to await

1. VERB

/ əˈweɪt / (AmE), / əˈweɪt / (BrE)
to wait for or look forward to the occurrence of something, especially with anticipation
wait for; look forward to

Examples

A warm welcome awaits all visitors to the museum.
She awaited news from the hospital with growing anxiety.
We eagerly await the release of the new book next month.

Synset Examples

We were expecting a visit from our relatives
She is looking to a promotion
he is waiting to be drafted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something with pleasure
  • Synonym: expect - regard something as likely to happen

Etymology

From Old French 'awaitier', based on Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'wautier' (to watch).

2. VERB NEW

/ əˈweɪt / (AmE), / əˈweɪt / (BrE)
(in computer programming) to pause the execution of an asynchronous function until a promise or future completes, then continue with the resolved value or error handling.
pause for an asynchronous operation

Examples

In modern JavaScript, you can await a Promise to resolve inside an async function.
The function will await the API response, then update the user interface with the data.
Make sure to handle errors when you await network requests to avoid unhandled rejections.

Synset Examples
