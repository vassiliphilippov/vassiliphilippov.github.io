autonomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑː.təˈnɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔː.təˈnɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or controlled by the autonomic nervous system, which regulates involuntary body functions such as heartbeat and digestion.
relating to the autonomic nervous system
medical

Examples

His doctor explained that his fainting episodes were related to an autonomic dysfunction.
Some medications can affect autonomic responses, causing changes in blood pressure.
The autonomic nervous system regulates involuntary functions like breathing and heart rate.

Synset Examples

autonomic reflexes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - controlled by conscious will rather than by the autonomic nervous system

Etymology

From Greek 'autonomos' (having its own laws), from 'autos' (self) + 'nomos' (law), via 'autonomy'.

2. ADJ NEW

(in computing) describing software, systems, or networks that manage, configure, and heal themselves automatically with little or no human intervention.
self-managing computing systems

Examples

Many enterprises are adopting autonomic computing to reduce manual configuration and maintenance.
The new server cluster uses autonomic policies to detect failures and reconfigure itself automatically.
Research teams developed an autonomic network that optimises traffic and heals performance problems without intervention.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-managing - able to operate and maintain itself without external control
  • Synonym: self-healing - able to detect and correct faults automatically
  • Related: autonomic nervous system - the biological system that inspired the computing metaphor of automatic internal regulation

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'autonomic' (relating to the autonomic nervous system); applied to computing in the early 21st century as 'autonomic computing'.
