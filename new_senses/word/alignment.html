alignment

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
(astronomy) The apparent meeting or passing of two or more celestial bodies in the same degree of the zodiac.
apparent meeting of celestial bodies

Examples

Astrologers believe that an alignment of certain planets can influence human behavior.
During the solar eclipse, people gathered to witness the unusual alignment of the Sun and the Moon.
The rare planetary alignment was visible just before dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunction - an astronomical event when two celestial bodies appear close together

2. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the arrangement or positioning of things in a straight line or in parallel lines
arrangement in a straight or parallel line

Examples

Make sure the picture frames are in perfect alignment on the wall.
The alignment of the streetlights creates an impressive visual effect at night.
The architect checked the alignment of the columns before construction began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the way in which things are arranged

Etymology

From align + -ment, ultimately from Old French 'aligner' (to line up) and Latin 'linea' (line).

3. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the process of adjusting or positioning parts of a device so that they are properly arranged in relation to each other
adjusting parts of a device to proper position

Examples

Regular printer alignment improves the quality of printed documents.
The mechanic performed an alignment of the car's wheels to ensure safe driving.
You should check the alignment of the gears after assembling the machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - the act of making a small alteration to achieve the desired position

Etymology

From align + -ment; ultimately from Old French *aligner* and Latin *linea* (line).

The new alignment of member states was established after the treaty was signed.
The two countries formed an alignment to promote economic cooperation in the region.
