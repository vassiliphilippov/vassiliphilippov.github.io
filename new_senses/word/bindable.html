bindable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪndəbəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being fastened or secured with a rope, bond, or other means
able to be fastened or secured

Examples

Not all materials are equally bindable with standard straps.
The cables are bindable using simple twist ties for easy storage.
The instructions specify that every component must be bindable before shipment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbreakable - impossible to break; very strong
  • Antonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking

Etymology

From 'bind' + '-able', meaning 'able to be bound'.
