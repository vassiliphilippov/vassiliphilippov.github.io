champion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.pi.ən / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən / (BrE)
a person who has won first place in a competition or contest
winner of a competition

Examples

After years of hard training, she became the champion of the national tennis tournament.
Every champion remembers the moment they first held their trophy.
The reigning champion defended his title in an exciting final match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winner - a person who wins something
  • Antonym: loser - a person who loses in a competition

Etymology

From Latin 'campio' (combatant, gladiator), via Old French 'champion'.
