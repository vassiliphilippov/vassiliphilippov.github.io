CID

1. NOUN

/ si aɪ ˈdi / (AmE), / siː aɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
the United States Army's principal law enforcement agency responsible for conducting criminal investigations within the Army worldwide
US Army criminal investigation agency
american legal

Examples

She was assigned to the CID after completing her training as a military police officer.
The CID launched an inquiry into the theft from the military base.
The CID works in coordination with other branches to uphold the Army's code of conduct.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Criminal Investigation Division.

2. NOUN NEW

/ si aɪ ˈdi / (AmE), / siː aɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
Abbreviation for caller identification: a service or display that shows the telephone number and sometimes the name of an incoming caller on a device.
caller identification service

Examples

Before answering, she checked the phone's CID display to see who was calling.
Telecom regulations require networks to transmit accurate CID information with each outgoing call today.
An unknown number blocked my mother because her phone didn't show a CID for that caller.

Synset Examples
