snowshoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌʃuː / (BrE)
a flat, lightweight device attached to the foot to enable walking on deep snow by distributing weight over a larger surface area
device worn on foot for walking on snow

Examples

Early explorers relied on snowshoes to travel during harsh winters.
She strapped a snowshoe to each foot before heading out into the woods.
These modern snowshoes are made of lightweight materials for easier hiking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ski - long, narrow device attached to the foot for gliding over snow

Etymology

From 'snow' + 'shoe', first recorded in English in the 17th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈsnoʊˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌʃuː / (BrE)
to travel on snowshoes over snow-covered ground
travel on snowshoes

Examples

After lunch, we decided to snowshoe through the forest to enjoy the fresh snow.
Last winter, she snowshoed across the lake to visit her friend on the other side.
Many tourists love to snowshoe along mountain trails when visiting the Alps.

Synset Examples

After a heavy snowfall, we have to snowshoe to the grocery store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ski - to travel on skis over snow

Etymology

From 'snowshoe' (noun), combining 'snow' and 'shoe'.
