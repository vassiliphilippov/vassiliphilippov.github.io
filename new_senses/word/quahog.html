quahog

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪ.hɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊ.hɒɡ / (BrE)
an edible hard-shell clam native to the eastern coast of North America, whose heavy shells were used as money by some Native American peoples
an edible hard-shell clam of North America

Examples

Historically, some tribes used quahog shells to make wampum beads.
The restaurant served a delicious chowder made from local quahogs.
We collected fresh quahogs on the beach for tonight’s dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a marine bivalve mollusc with two shells; related shellfish

Etymology

From Narragansett *poquauhock* or *quahog*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪˌhɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈkwɑːhɒɡ / (BrE)
a large, round, hard-shelled edible clam found along the Atlantic coast of North America, often used in chowders and other dishes
a large Atlantic hard-shelled clam

Examples

Digging for quahogs is a popular summertime activity on the New England coast.
The chef stuffed the large quahog shells with a savory breadcrumb mixture.
We ordered a bowl of chowder made with fresh quahogs from the local market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a general term for bivalve mollusks, often edible

Etymology

From Narragansett (Algonquian) *poquaûhock* (hard clam).
