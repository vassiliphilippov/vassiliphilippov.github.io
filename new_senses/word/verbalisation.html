verbalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜːbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the words that are produced or expressed when articulating thoughts, feelings, or ideas in spoken form
the act or result of expressing in words

Examples

During therapy, his verbalisation became more fluent and detailed over time.
Teachers encourage the verbalisation of ideas to develop language skills in young children.
The verbalisation of her emotions helped her cope with stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings using words
  • Antonym: silence - absence of spoken words or expression

Etymology

From verb 'verbalise' + '-ation'; modelled on 'verbalize' (to express in words), ultimately from Latin 'verbum' (word).

2. NOUN

/ vɜrbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / vɜːbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of expressing something in words
expressing in words

Examples

Children develop verbalisation skills as they learn to communicate their needs.
Her verbalisation of feelings helped her therapist understand her better.
The meeting encouraged the verbalisation of ideas that had previously gone unspoken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbalization - alternate spelling (American English) for expressing something in words

Etymology

From Latin 'verbalis' (verbal) + '-ation' forming nouns of action.
