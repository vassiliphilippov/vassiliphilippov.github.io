consistent

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
marked by logical, orderly, or harmonious connection or agreement among the parts
logically or harmoniously connected

Examples

A consistent design theme ties all the rooms together beautifully.
Her explanation was consistent and easy to follow, with each point building on the last.
The novel's tone is remarkably consistent from start to finish, making it a compelling read.

Synset Examples

a coherent argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherent - logically connected and consistent
  • Antonym: incoherent - lacking orderly or logical connection among parts

Etymology

From Latin 'consistere' (to stand firm).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(often followed by 'with') in agreement with or compatible with something else; not contradictory.
in agreement or compatible with

Examples

Her actions were consistent with her words, demonstrating her honesty.
His explanation was not consistent with the facts already established.
The results of the experiment are consistent with earlier studies.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Antonym: inconsistent - not in agreement or not compatible with something else

Etymology

From Latin 'consistere' (to stand firm, stop, remain) via late Latin and Old French.

5. ADJ NEW

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(in distributed computing) maintaining a coherent, synchronized state across multiple replicas so that operations (especially reads) observe a single, agreed view of the data according to a specified consistency model.
coherent across replicas (computing)

Examples

The distributed datastore guarantees consistent reads across all replicas despite concurrent updates.
To achieve consistent state, the cluster applies each transaction to every replica synchronously.
Under the strict protocol, nodes must coordinate to remain consistent after leader failover.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronized - kept the same or coordinated across systems or processes
  • Antonym: eventual consistency - a model where replicas converge to the same state only after some time; contrasts with immediate or strong consistency

Etymology

From Latin 'consistere' (to stand firm).
