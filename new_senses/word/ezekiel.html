Ezekiel

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈziːkiəl / (AmE), / ɪˈziːkiəl / (BrE)
a Hebrew prophet of the 6th century BC, regarded as the author of the Book of Ezekiel in the Old Testament, who was exiled to Babylon.
a Hebrew prophet exiled to Babylon

Examples

In art, Ezekiel is often depicted receiving divine revelations.
Many biblical scholars have analyzed the visions of Ezekiel for their symbolic meaning.
The Book of Ezekiel describes prophecies during the Babylonian exile.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yeḥezqeʾl, meaning 'God will strengthen.'

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈziːkiəl / (AmE), / ɪˈziːkiəl / (BrE)
an Old Testament book containing prophecies attributed to the prophet Ezekiel, including predictions of the downfall and restoration of Jerusalem and Judah
Old Testament book of prophecy

Examples

Many scholars study Ezekiel to understand the religious history of ancient Israel.
She referenced Ezekiel in her sermon about hope and restoration.
The vision of the valley of dry bones appears in the book of Ezekiel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jeremiah - another Old Testament prophetic book
  • Synonym: Isaiah - Old Testament book of prophecy
  • Synonym: Ezekiel - Hebrew prophet for whom the book is named
