chad

1. NOUN

/ tʃæd / (AmE), / tʃad / (BrE)
A small piece of paper or cardboard produced when a hole is punched in a card or tape, typically falling away as waste.
waste piece punched from paper or tape

Examples

He brushed the chad off his paperwork to keep the desk tidy.
Some voting machines malfunctioned, leaving chad still attached to the ballots.
The floor was littered with chad after everyone finished punching their tickets.

Etymology

Possibly from Scots chad (gravel), origin uncertain.

2. NOUN NEW

/ tʃæd / (AmE), / tʃad / (BrE)
Informal slang for a young man regarded as conventionally attractive, confident, and socially dominant; often used jokingly or pejoratively for the 'alpha male' stereotype.
slang for stereotypical confident young man
slang informal

Examples

A couple of tall, confident chads at the party kept joking and attracting everyone's attention.
He resented the campus chad who always interrupted class and bragged about his dates.
Online, the term chad is often used jokingly to describe stereotypical alpha males.
