synapse

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnˌæps / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.æps / (BrE)
the junction between two nerve cells or between a nerve cell and a muscle, where nerve impulses are transmitted
junction between nerve cells or nerve and muscle
medical

Examples

A muscle contracts only after receiving the signal at its synapse with a nerve cell.
Learning and memory involve changes at the synapse between neurons.
The neurons communicate by releasing chemicals at the synapse.

Synset Examples

nerve impulses cross a synapse through the action of neurotransmitters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junction - a point where two or more things are joined

Etymology

From Greek synapsis (junction), from synaptein (to join together), from syn- (together) + haptein (to fasten).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsɪnˌæps / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.æps / (BrE)
(in computing, especially neuromorphic engineering) an electronic or software component that emulates a biological synaptic connection, controlling the strength or timing of signals between artificial neurons.
artificial synaptic connection (neuromorphic computing)

Examples

The neuromorphic processor uses a memristor-based synapse to adjust connection weights in real time.
Researchers trained the network by tuning thousands of artificial synapses to mimic biological learning rules.
When the chip failed, engineers replaced the faulty synapse module to restore the neural circuit's function.

Synset Examples

The neuromorphic processor uses a memristor-based synapse to adjust connection weights in real time.
Researchers trained the network by tuning thousands of artificial synapses to mimic biological learning rules.
When the chip failed, engineers replaced the faulty synapse module to restore the neural circuit's function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial synapse - a device or software element designed to perform synapse-like functions in artificial neural systems
  • Synonym: memristor - an electronic component often used to implement synapse-like behaviour in neuromorphic hardware

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the biological term 'synapse', from Greek synapsis 'junction' (from syn- 'together' + haptein 'to fasten').
