gridlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪdˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪdlɒk / (BrE)
A situation in which very severe traffic congestion blocks all movement in every direction, making progress impossible.
complete standstill of traffic

Examples

After the accident on the main road, the city center was in complete gridlock for hours.
Emergency vehicles struggled to get through the gridlock caused by the parade.
Morning commuters were frustrated by gridlock that stretched for several miles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traffic jam - a situation in which vehicles are unable to move due to congestion

Etymology

Originally U.S., from 'grid' + 'lock', after the pattern of blocked intersections forming a grid in cities.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0261r\u026ad\u02ccl\u0251k / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0261r\u026adl\u0252k / (BrE)
A situation in politics or government in which opposing parties or factions prevent decisions or legislation from being passed, causing a stalemate.
political stalemate

Examples

After weeks of negotiation, the parliament fell into gridlock, delaying the budget and public services.
Voters frustrated by gridlock blamed both parties for failing to pass meaningful reforms.
The mayor warned that federal-state gridlock could prevent essential disaster-relief funds from reaching towns.

Synset Examples

After weeks of negotiation, the parliament fell into gridlock, delaying the budget and public services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which no progress can be made or no advancement is possible
  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which opposing parties cannot reach agreement, stopping progress

Etymology

From the traffic term 'gridlock' (from 'grid' + 'lock'); extended metaphorically to describe blocked political decision-making.
