restful

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛstfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛstfʊl / (BrE)
Giving or promoting rest; making you feel relaxed or refreshed.
making you feel rested or relaxed

Examples

After a restful weekend, she felt ready to start the new week.
The soft music created a restful atmosphere in the room.
We booked a hotel in the countryside for a restful holiday.

Synset Examples

she spent a restful night at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaxing - making you feel relaxed or less tense
  • Antonym: restless - unable to rest or relax, constantly moving or uneasy

Etymology

Formed from 'rest' + '-ful', meaning 'full of rest.'

(in computing) following the REST (Representational State Transfer) architectural style; describing web services or APIs that are stateless, resource-based, and use standard HTTP methods to access resources.
following REST web-API principles

Examples

The platform offers a restful API for developers to integrate with.
They redesigned their backend into a restful system to simplify mobile app requests.
A restful service uses standard HTTP verbs and clear resource URLs for consistency.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: REST-compliant - following the principles of REST (Representational State Transfer) for web services
  • Synonym: stateless - not retaining client session state between requests; a key property of RESTful services

Etymology

From the acronym REST (Representational State Transfer), coined by Roy Fielding (2000), + suffix -ful.
