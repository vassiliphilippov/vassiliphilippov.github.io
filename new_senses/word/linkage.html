linkage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋ.kɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋ.kɪdʒ / (BrE)
an association or connection between things, especially ideas, phenomena, or groups
association or connection between things

Examples

Scientists are studying the linkage between air pollution and respiratory diseases.
The report highlighted the linkage of economic growth with environmental sustainability.
There is a strong linkage between education and income in this region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - the state of being joined or linked
  • Synonym: association - a relationship between things or events
  • Antonym: disconnection - the state of being separated or unconnected

Etymology

From English 'link' + '-age', modeled after French 'liage' (binding).

4. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
(Genetics) The tendency of genes located close together on the same chromosome to be inherited together.
tendency of genes to be inherited together
medical

Examples

Because of linkage, genes for eye color and hair color may be passed on as a group.
Researchers mapped the gene linkage on chromosome 12 to study inherited diseases.
The discovery of genetic linkage helped scientists understand how certain traits are inherited together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recombination - (Genetics) the process by which genetic material is rearranged during meiosis

Etymology

From link + -age; scientific sense dating from early 20th century.
