to speechify

1. VERB

/ ˈspiːt͡ʃɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspiːtʃɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to deliver a speech, especially in a formal, pompous, or tedious manner
give a formal or pompous speech

Examples

At every family gathering, Uncle Joe likes to speechify about his childhood adventures.
Rather than addressing the issue directly, she chose to speechify on the virtues of patience.
The senator continued to speechify long after the audience had lost interest.

Synset Examples

These ministers speechify on every occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orate - to make a formal speech, especially in a dignified or pompous manner
  • Synonym: harangue - to lecture someone at length in an aggressive and critical manner

Etymology

From 'speech' + '-ify', a suffix meaning 'to make' or 'to become'.
