insane

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈseɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪn / (BrE)
extremely foolish, reckless, or unreasonable; lacking sensible judgment.
extremely foolish or reckless

Examples

It's insane to drive so fast on icy roads.
Taking such a big risk was absolutely insane in her opinion.
They came up with an insane plan to swim across the lake at midnight.

Synset Examples

harebrained ideas
took insane risks behind the wheel
a completely mad scheme to build a bridge between two mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - extremely foolish or reckless
  • Antonym: sensible - showing sound judgment; reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'insanus' (mad, unhealthy), from 'in-' (not) + 'sanus' (healthy).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈseɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪn / (BrE)
suffering from or showing severe mental illness or derangement; extremely foolish or irrational.
mentally ill; extremely foolish

Examples

He came up with an insane plan to travel around the world in just ten days.
Her insane laughter echoed through the empty house, sending chills down my spine.
The court found him insane at the time of the crime and sent him to a psychiatric hospital.

Synset Examples

was declared insane
insane laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or extremely foolish
  • Antonym: sane - having a healthy mind; rational

Etymology

From Latin insanus (‘mad, insane’), from in- (‘not’) + sanus (‘healthy’).
