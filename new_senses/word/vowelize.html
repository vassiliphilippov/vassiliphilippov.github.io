to vowelize

1. VERB

/ ˈvaʊəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvaʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce or treat (a sound or letter) as a vowel, especially by inserting a vowel sound or quality
pronounce or treat as a vowel

Examples

In some dialects, speakers often vowelize the l in 'bottle.'
Linguists study how liquids can be vowelized between consonants in certain languages.
She demonstrated how to vowelize a consonant when teaching phonetics.

Synset Examples

between two consonants, this liquid is vowelized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonantize - to pronounce as a consonant

Etymology

From 'vowel' + '-ize', patterned after similar verbs in English.

2. VERB NEW

/ c8va8a59la6az / (AmE), / c8va8a59la6az / (BrE)
(in orthography and linguistics) to add vowel marks or diacritic signs to a consonant-based writing system (for example, Hebrew or Arabic) to show vowel sounds and indicate pronunciation.
add vowel marks to text

Examples

Scholars often vowelize medieval Hebrew manuscripts to show pronunciation for students.
The editor vowelized the Arabic text with diacritics to aid non-native readers.
Before publication, they were vowelizing poetry lines to preserve intended vocal rhythms.
