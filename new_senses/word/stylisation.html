stylisation

1. NOUN

/ staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of representing or designing something according to a particular style rather than depicting it realistically
representation according to a style, not realism
british

Examples

Her costume designs are marked by their bold stylisation and vibrant colors.
Some films use heavy stylisation to create a dreamlike atmosphere.
The painter's stylisation of landscapes gives his work a unique character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylization - the act of stylizing; causing to conform to a particular style (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'stylize' (British spelling 'stylise') + -ation, following the pattern of similar formation in English; variant spelling of 'stylization'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in computing) the process of applying a particular visual style to digital images, video, or user-interface elements using computer algorithms (for example, neural style transfer).
algorithmic application of visual style
british

Examples

The app offered stylisation of photos using neural style transfer to resemble famous paintings.
Their pipeline automates the stylisation of video frames while preserving temporal consistency across scenes.
Designers applied quick stylisation to the mockups to test different visual themes with users.
