runway

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌn.weɪ / (BrE)
a sloping chute or track along which logs are slid, especially in logging operations
log chute or slide in logging

Examples

A wooden runway was constructed to speed up the movement of heavy timber.
After the trees were cut, the logs were sent down the runway to the mill.
The workers built a runway to transport the felled logs down to the river.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌn.weɪ / (BrE)
a narrow platform that extends from the stage into the audience, used especially by models at fashion shows or performers in theaters and nightclubs
platform extending from a stage

Examples

Bright lights illuminated the runway as dancers performed to energetic music.
The model strutted confidently down the runway during the final segment of the show.
Ticket holders in the front row had the best view of the new dress as it dazzled under the runway lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catwalk - a narrow, elevated platform for models to display clothes

Etymology

From 'run' + 'way', originally meaning a path for running or traveling; sense extended to fashion by analogy.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌn.weɪ / (BrE)
a long, level surface, typically paved, used by aircraft for taking off and landing
paved strip for aircraft to take off and land

Examples

After landing, the pilot steered the jet off the runway and toward the terminal.
Heavy fog covered the runway, delaying several flights at the airport.
The plane taxied slowly onto the runway and prepared for takeoff.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: taxiway - a path for aircraft at an airport connecting runways with terminals

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈrʌnˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌn.weɪ / (BrE)
(in business, especially startups and finance) the amount of time or money a company has available before it must obtain more funding or cease operations, given its current rate of spending.
time or funds before running out

Examples

Investors asked whether the company had enough runway to reach profitability without another funding round.
With a high burn rate, the startup's remaining runway shrank to just three months of operations.
Our financial model shows that cutting costs now will extend the company's runway by six months.

Synset Examples

Our financial model shows that cutting costs now will extend the company's runway by six months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash runway - the amount of cash a company has left, usually expressed in months, before running out of money

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the literal sense 'runway' (a strip for aircraft to take off); applied to business to mean the time or funds available before a company must 'take off' or stop.
