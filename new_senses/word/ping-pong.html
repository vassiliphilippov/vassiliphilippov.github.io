Ping-Pong

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪŋ pɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪŋ pɒŋ / (BrE)
A game similar to tennis, played on a table with small paddles and a lightweight ball, where players hit the ball back and forth over a net.
table tennis game played with paddles and a light ball

Examples

He became the school Ping-Pong champion after months of practice.
They spent the afternoon playing Ping-Pong and laughing together.
We set up a Ping-Pong table in the garage for friendly matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table tennis - the sport formally known as table tennis

Etymology

Originally a trademark; possibly imitative of the sound made by the ball striking the table and paddles.

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0251\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0252\u014b / (BrE)
(informal) to send something back and forth between people or to alternate repeatedly between two options, tasks, or positions.
send or alternate back and forth (informal)
informal

Examples

The committee began to ping-pong between two proposals, delaying a final vote for weeks.
She ping-ponged the contract back and forth until both lawyers agreed on the wording.
Caught between tasks, he started ping-ponging assignments all afternoon and finished none.
