STP

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs.tiːˈpiː / (AmE), / ˌɛs.tiːˈpiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'standard temperature and pressure', used especially in chemistry and physics to refer to conditions of 0°C (273.15 K) and 1 atmosphere pressure, considered standard for measurements and calculations
standard temperature and pressure (0°C, 1 atm) in science

Examples

All gas volumes were measured at STP for consistency across experiments.
Calculations using STP conditions are common in introductory chemistry courses.
Under STP, one mole of an ideal gas occupies 22.4 liters.

Etymology

Initialism of 'standard temperature and pressure'.

Abbreviation for straight-through processing: automated, end-to-end handling of financial or business transactions so that they are completed without manual intervention.
automated end-to-end transaction processing

Examples

The bank's STP program allowed thousands of payments to settle without manual checks.
Implementing STP shortened the trade settlement cycle and reduced settlement fails across counterparties.
Regulators encourage banks to adopt STP to improve operational resilience and lower processing costs.
