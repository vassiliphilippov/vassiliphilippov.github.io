cooked

1. ADJ

/ kʊkt / (AmE), / kʊkt / (BrE)
(of food) prepared for eating by the application of heat.
prepared food by heat

Examples

All poultry should be thoroughly cooked before eating to avoid foodborne illness.
She prefers her steak well cooked rather than rare.
The cooked vegetables were served with a drizzle of olive oil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncooked - not prepared by the application of heat
  • Antonym: raw - (of food) not cooked; in natural state

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'cook,' ultimately from Old English *coc* via Latin *coquere* (to cook).

2. ADJ NEW

/ kʊkt / (AmE), / kʊkt / (BrE)
(informal) (of numbers, accounts, or data) falsified or manipulated to give a misleading impression.
falsified; manipulated (informal)
informal

Examples

The auditor discovered the quarterly revenue numbers had been cooked to hide losses from investors.
They accused the company of presenting cooked figures during the merger talks to inflate its value.
After reviewing the spreadsheet, she concluded the sales data looked cooked and needed a full audit.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsify - to change information so that it is no longer true, often to deceive
  • Synonym: manipulate - to control or influence something, often unfairly or dishonestly
  • Antonym: accurate - correct and free from error; not misleading

Etymology

From the verb 'cook', via the idiom 'cook the books' (to falsify accounts).
