purity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərəti / (BrE)
the state of being free from any contamination, adulteration, or mixture; being completely unmixed or unadulterated
state of being unmixed or unadulterated

Examples

Scientists are striving to improve the purity of the chemical compound.
She admired the purity of the snow, untouched by footsteps.
The purity of the water was tested before it was deemed safe to drink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impurity - the state or quality of being mixed with foreign matter or contaminants

Etymology

From Latin 'puritas', from 'purus' meaning 'clean, pure'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪti / (BrE)
the state of being free from moral wrongdoing or sin; lack of corruption or knowledge of evil
freedom from moral wrongdoing or sin

Examples

Her purity of heart was admired by everyone who knew her.
Some traditions associate the color white with spiritual purity.
The novel explores the theme of purity lost in a corrupt society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - the state of lacking guilt or moral wrong; blamelessness
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior

Etymology

From Old French *pureté*, from Latin *puritas* (cleanliness, chastity) from *purus* (pure).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being morally or sexually innocent, especially in reference to a woman's chastity
moral or sexual innocence, esp. female chastity

Examples

In Victorian times, a woman's purity was highly valued and closely guarded by her family.
Religious traditions often associate purity with moral virtue and chastity.
The novel explores themes of love, betrayal, and the loss of purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chastity - the state of refraining from sexual intercourse or maintaining sexual purity
  • Synonym: innocence - lack of guilt or sexual experience
  • Antonym: licentiousness - lacking legal or moral restraints, often sexual in nature

Etymology

From Latin 'puritas' (cleanness, purity), from 'purus' (pure).
