shielding

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
material such as lead or concrete used as a barrier to protect against radiation emitted during nuclear decay or similar processes.
barrier material against radiation

Examples

Concrete walls provide effective shielding against gamma rays in medical facilities.
Sufficient shielding is necessary in nuclear reactors to limit radiation reaching the control room.
The hospital's radiology department relies on heavy shielding to protect staff from X-ray exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield - object or material used to protect from harm

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃildɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
A layer or arrangement of metal mesh or plates used to protect electronic equipment from external electromagnetic interference.
protection from electromagnetic interference

Examples

Proper shielding is essential in medical imaging rooms to block external interference.
The engineers installed shielding around sensitive circuits to prevent signal disruption.
They checked the shielding to ensure compliance with safety standards for electronic equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield - a protective device or barrier

Etymology

From 'shield' + '-ing', indicating the action or result of shielding.

/ ˈʃiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
(in public health) a policy or practice of protecting people who are highly vulnerable to infection by reducing their contact with others and exposing them to fewer risks of disease.
protecting vulnerable people from infection
medical

Examples

During the pandemic, many elderly citizens relied on shielding to stay safe.
The government advised household members to support shielding by delivering groceries and medicines.
Hospitals set up separate clinics to reduce exposure and allow shielding for immunocompromised patients.
