misbehaviour

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
Improper, unacceptable, or immoral behaviour.
improper or immoral behaviour
british

Examples

His misbehaviour at the dinner party embarrassed his family.
Repeated misbehaviour will result in suspension from school.
The teacher was frustrated by the constant misbehaviour in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misbehavior - improper or immoral behavior (American spelling)
  • Synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves
  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with rules or authority

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + behaviour; influenced by French mésbehaviour, variant of behaviour.
