mangled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəld / (BrE)
(of an object or body part) having edges that are badly torn or jagged, typically as a result of injury or damage.
badly torn or jagged, usually from injury

Examples

Her mangled fingers showed the severity of the machinery accident.
Rescuers pulled a mangled bicycle from the wreckage.
The mangled remains of the car were barely recognizable after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torn - damaged by being pulled apart or ripped
  • Antonym: intact - in perfect condition; not damaged

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'mangle', from Middle English 'manglen', meaning to mutilate or cut up.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈmæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəld / (BrE)
(computing) transformed by a name-mangling process so that an identifier or symbol encodes additional information (for example about scope or type).
transformed by name-mangling (computing)

Examples

The compiler produced mangled symbol names to support function overloading.
She used a demangler to convert the mangled identifiers back to their original function names.
Reading the crash log was hard because most entries showed mangled C++ symbols.

Synset Examples

The compiler produced mangled symbol names to support function overloading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: name mangling - a compiler technique that encodes extra information into symbol names, often to support overloading
  • Antonym: demangle - to reverse name mangling and restore the original, human-readable symbol names

Etymology

From the verb 'mangle' (Middle English 'manglen', to mutilate or cut up); used metaphorically in computing to describe transformed symbol names.
