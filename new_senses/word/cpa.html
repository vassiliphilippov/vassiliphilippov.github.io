CPA

1. NOUN

/ si pi eɪ / (AmE), / siː piː eɪ / (BrE)
an accountant who has passed required exams and met state requirements to be certified as a public accountant in the United States
certified public accountant (US)
american

Examples

After passing the exam, he proudly put CPA after his name on business cards.
Every CPA must maintain their license through continuing education.
She hired a CPA to help with her business taxes.

Synset Examples

in addition to accounting and auditing, CPAs also prepare tax returns for individuals and corporations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountant - a person whose job is to keep or inspect financial accounts

Etymology

Abbreviation of Certified Public Accountant, a professional designation established in the United States in the early 20th century.
