cornsmut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌsmʌt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌsmʌt / (BrE)
a fungal disease affecting maize (Indian corn), caused by Ustilago maydis, resulting in swollen, blackish growths on the ears and kernels
fungal disease of maize

Examples

Scientists are researching ways to prevent cornsmut in commercial crops.
The farmer was dismayed to find cornsmut spreading through his maize field.
The swollen, dark growths on the corn were a clear sign of cornsmut.

the edible form of the fungus Ustilago maydis (also called huitlacoche), eaten as a delicacy in Mexican and other cuisines.
edible corn fungus (huitlacoche)

Examples

The chef prepared tacos filled with cornsmut and sautéed mushrooms for the festival.
At the market she bought fresh cornsmut to add an earthy flavour to her soup.
Traditional Mexican recipes often include cornsmut as a prized ingredient during autumn harvests.

Etymology
