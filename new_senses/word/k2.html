K2

1. PROPN

/ ˌkeɪ ˈtu / (AmE), / ˌkeɪ ˈtuː / (BrE)
a mountain in the Karakoram Range on the border between Pakistan and China, recognized as the world's second-highest peak at 8,611 meters (28,251 feet).
the world's second-highest mountain

Examples

An expedition to K2 requires careful planning and expert mountaineering skills.
Many climbers consider K2 to be even more challenging than Everest due to its harsh weather conditions.
The breathtaking view from the summit of K2 attracts adventurers from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Everest - the world's highest mountain

Etymology

Named as the second peak measured in the Karakoram Range (K for Karakoram, 2 for the second peak).
