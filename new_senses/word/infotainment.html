infotainment

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfoʊˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
a type of television or radio program, or other media content, that combines information and entertainment, often presenting facts about people or events in an engaging way
media content blending information and entertainment

Examples

Many documentaries now use elements of infotainment to capture a wider audience.
The channel focuses on infotainment rather than purely educational programming.
The morning show is a perfect example of infotainment, blending news updates with light-hearted segments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edutainment - media content designed to educate as well as entertain

Etymology

Blend of 'information' and 'entertainment', first used in the late 20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

a vehicle or device system that combines information and entertainment functions, such as navigation, media playback, phone connectivity, and apps.
in‑car information and entertainment system

Examples

The rental car's infotainment system offers maps, Bluetooth calling, and music streaming.
Before the trip, she updated her car's infotainment software to enable new apps.
Drivers can connect smartphones to the infotainment display for directions and playlists.
