chatty

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃæti / (AmE), / ˈtʃæti / (BrE)
tending to talk a lot in a friendly or trivial way; fond of casual conversation.
talkative; fond of casual conversation

Examples

Although she seemed shy at first, Jane turned out to be surprisingly chatty at the party.
My grandmother is very chatty and loves to tell stories about her youth.
The office became much livelier after our chatty new colleague joined the team.

Synset Examples

kept from her housework by gabby neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkative - inclined to talk a lot
  • Antonym: quiet - not speaking much or making little noise

Etymology

From 'chat', probably from Middle English 'chateren' (to chatter) + '-y'.

3. ADJ NEW

/ ˈtʃæti / (AmE), / ˈtʃæti / (BrE)
(in computing) describing an interface, API, or protocol that requires many small, frequent back-and-forth requests, often increasing latency or overhead.
many small requests (computing)

Examples

The mobile app's backend became slow because the REST API was too chatty with excessive small calls.
To improve performance, the engineers replaced several chatty endpoints with a single bulk-fetch method.
A chatty message protocol can significantly increase latency on mobile networks.

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: coarse-grained - describing an interface that groups work into larger requests to reduce the number of calls.

Etymology

From 'chat' (to talk) + suffix '-y'; metaphorically applied in computing to frequent small communications.
