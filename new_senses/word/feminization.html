feminization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfiːmɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an individual develops female physical characteristics, especially in a male as a result of hormonal changes or medical conditions
process of developing female characteristics
medical

Examples

Certain medical conditions may trigger feminization, such as loss of facial hair or breast development.
The feminization of the patient's body was monitored carefully during treatment.
The doctor explained that hormone therapy could lead to feminization in male patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculinization - the process of developing male characteristics

Etymology

From Latin 'fēmina' (woman) + English suffix '-ization', influenced by French 'féminisation'.

(in horticulture) the process of producing predominantly female plants or seeds, especially by treating or crossing plants so that resulting seeds grow into female individuals (often used for dioecious species).
production of female-only plants/seeds

Examples

Commercial growers often use feminization to produce seeds that grow into female plants.
Many seed catalogs advertise feminization so hobbyists can avoid male plants in small gardens.
Laboratories perform controlled pollination for feminization when producing feminized seeds for dioecious species.
