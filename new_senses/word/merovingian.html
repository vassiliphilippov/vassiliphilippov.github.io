Merovingian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (BrE)
a member of a Frankish dynasty founded by Clovis I, which ruled parts of Gaul and Germany from about 500 to 750 AD
member of early Frankish ruling dynasty

Examples

Artifacts from the Merovingian period have been discovered in northern France.
Historians study the rise and fall of the Merovingian dynasty to understand early medieval Europe.
The Merovingian kings were known for their long hair, which distinguished them from their subjects.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Carolingian - member of the Frankish dynasty succeeding the Merovingians

Etymology

From French Mérovingien, from medieval Latin Merovingi, plural of Merovech, the dynasty's semi-legendary founder.
