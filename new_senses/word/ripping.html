ripping

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal, British) Very good; excellent; of the highest quality.
excellent; very good (informal, British)
british informal

Examples

Her last novel is a ripping yarn packed with adventure and suspense.
The band gave a ripping performance that had the whole crowd cheering.
We had a ripping time at the festival this weekend.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellent - extremely good; outstanding
  • Synonym: splendid - very impressive; excellent
  • Antonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant

Etymology

Originally British slang, derived from the verb 'rip' (to move quickly or vigorously), later used for emphasis on excellence.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪp.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪp.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) resembling the noise of violent tearing or splitting, such as fabric or wood being ripped apart.
like the sound of violent tearing

Examples

She jumped at the ripping sound of fabric shredding in the next room.
The storm was accompanied by a ripping noise as trees were torn from the ground.
With a ripping crack, the lightning struck and split the old oak tree.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rending - (of a sound) resembling violent tearing or splitting

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈrɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing) The process of copying audio or other data from an optical disc (such as a CD or DVD) to a computer or other digital storage, producing digital files.
copying data from a disc to digital files

Examples

The ripping of his CD collection to MP3 took all afternoon.
She praised the software's fast ripping and accurate metadata tagging for albums.
After buying a used DVD, he asked about the legality of ripping movies for personal use.
