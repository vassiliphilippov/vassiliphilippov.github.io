promise

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to give reason to expect that something will happen or be successful
give grounds for expectation

Examples

Early sales figures promise a successful year for the company.
The new evidence does promise a breakthrough in the investigation.
The weather forecast promises sunshine for the weekend.

Synset Examples

The new results were promising
The results promised fame and glory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - to provide a formal assurance or promise
  • Antonym: disappoint - to fail to meet expectations

Etymology

From Latin promissum (a promise), from promittere (to send forth, promise).

WordNet ID: oewn-02746427-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪs / (BrE)
a statement or assurance by someone that they will definitely do or not do something in the future
a commitment to do (or not do) something

Examples

A promise is only as strong as the person who makes it.
He broke his promise and arrived late to the meeting.
She made a solemn promise to never reveal their secret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pledge - a serious or formal promise or undertaking
  • Synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
  • Antonym: threat - a statement of intention to cause harm or punishment if a condition is not met

Etymology

From Latin 'promissum' (something promised), from 'promittere' (to send forth, promise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07241205-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈprɑː.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪs / (BrE)
to state that you will definitely do or provide something or that something will definitely happen, often formally or with strong intention
to give a commitment to do something

Examples

I can't make it tonight, but I promise I'll visit you tomorrow.
She promised to help me finish the project by next week.
They promised that the repairs would be completed on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pledge - to make a formal, serious promise
  • Synonym: vow - to make a solemn promise, especially in a formal or religious context
  • Antonym: threaten - state one's intention to harm or punish

Etymology

From Latin 'promittere' (to send forth, promise) via Old French 'promettre'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00885532-v
Synset POS: v
