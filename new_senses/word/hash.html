hash

1. NOUN

/ hæʃ / (AmE), / hæʃ / (BrE)
a purified resinous substance extracted from the hemp plant, used as a drug for its hallucinogenic effects
a drug from hemp with hallucinogenic effects

Examples

He traveled to Morocco, a country known for producing high-quality hash.
Some people smoke hash by mixing it with tobacco or rolling it into cigarettes.
The police found a small amount of hash in his backpack during the search.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hashish - the resin from the cannabis plant, used as a drug
  • Synonym: marijuana - the dried leaves and flowers of the cannabis plant, smoked as a drug

Etymology

Shortened from 'hashish,' from Arabic ḥašīš (grass, hemp).

2. NOUN

/ hæʃ / (AmE), / hæʃ / (BrE)
a dish of cooked chopped meat mixed with potatoes and often onions, usually browned together in a frying pan
dish of chopped meat and potatoes

Examples

She quickly made a hash using leftovers from last night's dinner.
The diner is famous for its homemade hash served with toast.
We had corned beef hash for breakfast with a fried egg on top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hash browns - shredded or diced potatoes that are pan-fried or deep-fried until brown and crisp

Etymology

From French 'hacher', meaning 'to chop'.

3. VERB

/ hæʃ / (AmE), / hæʃ / (BrE)
to cut (food, especially meat or potatoes) into small pieces, usually in preparation for cooking
chop food into small pieces

Examples

Many recipes call for you to hash leftover turkey and vegetables for a quick meal.
She used a sharp knife to hash the onions before adding them to the stew.
The chef hashed the potatoes to make a classic breakfast hash.
