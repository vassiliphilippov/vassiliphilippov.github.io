PMS

1. NOUN

/ piː ɛm ɛs / (AmE), / piː ɛm ɛs / (BrE)
a set of physical and emotional symptoms occurring in many women before the start of menstruation, such as mood swings, fatigue, and headaches
premenstrual symptoms in women
medical

Examples

His sister warned him that her PMS made her more sensitive during that time of the month.
Many women experience PMS symptoms such as irritability and bloating before their period.
She took vitamins to help manage her PMS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premenstrual syndrome - symptoms experienced before menstruation

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'premenstrual syndrome'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ piː ɛm ɛs / (AmE), / piː ɛm ɛs / (BrE)
(in business and HR) an abbreviation for a performance management system: software or a formal process used to set goals, track performance, and manage employee reviews and development.
performance management system (HR software)

Examples

The HR department implemented a PMS to streamline annual performance reviews and goal tracking.
Managers use the PMS to set targets, approve objectives, and record development plans.
All staff must complete a self-review in the PMS before their scheduled appraisal meeting.
