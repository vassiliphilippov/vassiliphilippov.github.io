fabricator

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who invents false stories or lies; a liar
someone who tells lies

Examples

In court, the witness was exposed as a fabricator who twisted the truth.
Many believed his alibi until evidence proved him a skilled fabricator.
The journalist turned out to be a fabricator of sensational stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies

Etymology

From Latin 'fabricari' (to fashion, make), via 'fabricate'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
A person, company, or factory that makes or assembles products or parts, especially metal components or semiconductor wafers.
manufacturer or maker of parts

Examples

The fabricator announced a breakthrough in 3nm chip fabrication at the conference.
We hired a local fabricator to produce stainless steel brackets for the new handrails.
Many automotive fabricators have switched to automated welding for faster production.
