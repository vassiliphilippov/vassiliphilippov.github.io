deep-fried

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːp ˌfraɪd / (AmE), / ˈdiːp ˌfraɪd / (BrE)
(of food) cooked by immersing in hot fat or oil.
cooked by frying in oil or fat

Examples

Many people enjoy the crunchy texture of deep-fried vegetables.
She ordered a plate of deep-fried chicken with a side of coleslaw.
The fair is famous for its deep-fried desserts such as doughnuts and funnel cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fried - cooked in hot fat or oil

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈdiːp ˌfraɪd / (AmE), / ˈdiːp ˌfraɪd / (BrE)
(informal, internet slang) (of an image, video, or meme) heavily and crudely edited so that colours, contrast, sharpness, and artifacts are exaggerated, producing a garish or distorted appearance.
heavily overprocessed image/meme
informal slang

Examples

That deep-fried meme is so saturated and noisy it barely looks like the original photo.
On the forum, users joked about the deep-fried effect making the text unreadable and harsh to view.
She edited the picture until it was practically deep-fried, with blown highlights and aggressive sharpening.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oversaturated - having colors that are too intense or vivid
  • Synonym: distorted - visually altered so that the original appearance is changed or exaggerated

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the literal culinary term 'deep-fried' (cooked by immersion in hot oil), used online to describe images that look 'overcooked' by heavy editing.
