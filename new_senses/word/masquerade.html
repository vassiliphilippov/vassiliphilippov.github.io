masquerade

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a party or dance at which people wear masks and often elaborate costumes
party with masks and costumes

Examples

At the masquerade, everyone tried to guess each other's identities.
She wore a beautiful red gown to the masquerade last night.
The grand masquerade was the highlight of the social season.

Etymology

From French mascarade, from Italian mascherata, based on maschera (mask).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
a false outward appearance or display intended to conceal the truth; a pretense
false show or pretense

Examples

Her kindness was merely a masquerade to hide her real intentions.
The company’s commitment to the environment turned out to be a masquerade.
Tired of the masquerade, he decided to reveal his true feelings.

Synset Examples

a beggar's masquerade of wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit

Etymology

From French 'mascarade', from Italian 'mascherata' (masked, disguised), from 'maschera' (mask).

6. NOUN NEW

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
(in computing, especially networking) the practice or configuration of hiding private IP addresses by rewriting the source address of outgoing packets, i.e., NAT masquerading.
NAT hiding of source IP addresses

Examples

The router applied a masquerade so all internal devices shared the ISP's public address.
During the course, engineers demonstrated how NAT masquerade rewrites source addresses for outgoing traffic.
To troubleshoot connectivity, we temporarily disabled the firewall's masquerade rule and tested direct access.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network address translation (NAT) - a method for remapping one IP address space into another by modifying network address information in IP packet headers

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of French mascarade/Italian mascherata (mask, disguise); applied in computing to mean ‘hide or disguise an address’.
