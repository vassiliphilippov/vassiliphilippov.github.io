groom

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
a man who has recently been married
man who has recently married

Examples

After the ceremony, the groom joined his bride for their first dance.
The groom happily thanked all the guests for attending the wedding.
The groom looked nervous as he waited at the altar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bride - a woman who has recently been married

Etymology

From Old English *guma* (man, husband).

3. NOUN

/ ɡrum / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
a man on his wedding day, especially during the marriage ceremony; a bridegroom
man at his own wedding ceremony

Examples

After the ceremony, the groom thanked his friends for attending the wedding.
The groom waited nervously at the altar for his bride to arrive.
The groom wore a classic black suit on his big day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bride - a woman participant in her own marriage ceremony

Etymology

From Old English *guma* (man), later combined with 'bride' to form 'bridegroom'; 'groom' alone adopted this meaning by the 17th century.
