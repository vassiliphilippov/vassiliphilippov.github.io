claim

1. VERB

/ kleɪm / (AmE), / kleɪm / (BrE)
to demand or assert one's right to something, such as property or privilege
demand or assert ownership or right

Examples

After landing, he hurried to the carousel to claim his baggage.
She claimed a refund, arguing the item was defective.
You must claim your prize in person before the deadline.

Synset Examples

He claimed his suitcases at the airline counter
Mr. Smith claims special tax exemptions because he is a foreign resident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assert - to state or declare firmly
  • Antonym: relinquish - to give up or surrender a claim or right

Etymology

From Latin *clamare* (to call, cry out) via Old French *clamer* (to claim)
