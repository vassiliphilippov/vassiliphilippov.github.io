ontological

1. ADJ

/ ɑnˈtɑlədʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒlədʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy that deals with the nature of existence, being, or reality (ontology)
relating to existence or being (philosophy)

Examples

Debates about artificial intelligence often raise ontological questions about what it means to be conscious.
Her research focuses on the ontological status of mathematical objects.
The philosopher presented an ontological argument for the existence of God.

Synset Examples

ontological speculations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphysical - relating to metaphysics, a branch of philosophy concerned with the fundamental nature of reality
  • Degree Lower: epistemological - relating to epistemology, the branch of philosophy dealing with knowledge and belief

Etymology

From French 'ontologique', from Greek 'on' (being) + '-logy' (study of).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ɑnˈtɑlədʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒlədʒɪkəl / (BrE)
(in information science and artificial intelligence) relating to an ontology: a formal, explicit specification of concepts and relationships used to represent knowledge and enable automated reasoning or data integration.
relating to formal ontologies (AI, knowledge representation)

Examples

The research team built an ontological framework to standardize medical terms across different hospital databases.
She performed an ontological analysis of the dataset to identify core entities and their relationships.
Errors in the ontological model caused the AI to misclassify products in the catalog.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ontology (computing) - a formal representation of a set of concepts within a domain and the relationships between them
  • Synonym: knowledge graph - a graph-based data model that represents entities and their interrelations for retrieval and reasoning

Etymology

From French ontologique and Latin/Greek roots (Greek on ‘being’ + -logy ‘study’); applied in information science as a technical sense from the late 20th century.
