to hover

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
to remain in one place in the air, suspended as if defying gravity
remain suspended in the air

Examples

A helicopter began to hover over the accident scene, searching for a place to land.
As the magician waved his hands, the ball appeared to hover in midair.
The hummingbird seemed to hover effortlessly above the flower.

Synset Examples

The guru claimed that he could levitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levitate - to rise or float in the air, especially by means of supernatural power or as if by magic

Etymology

From Middle English hoveren, frequentative of hoven (to hover, tarry).

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
(of something negative or threatening) to remain in the air or be present in a way that causes concern or fear.
linger menacingly or threateningly above

Examples

A sense of uncertainty seemed to hover over the entire meeting.
Dark clouds hovered over the village, threatening an afternoon storm.
The fear of failure hovers over him whenever he tries something new.

Synset Examples

The terrible vision brooded over her all day long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - to hang or hover ominously (especially of something dark or menacing)

Etymology

From Middle English hoveren, frequentative of hoven (to hover, tarry).

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
to be undecided or uncertain; to waver between conflicting positions or courses of action
be undecided or waver between choices

Examples

His mind hovered over the decision, unable to commit to one path.
She tends to hover between two options whenever faced with a difficult choice.
The committee hovered between approving and rejecting the proposal for several weeks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waver - be undecided between options; vacillate

4. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
to remain in one place in the air, usually by moving wings or using special mechanisms
stay suspended in air

Examples

A helicopter can hover over the landing pad before touching down.

The hummingbird appeared to hover in front of the brightly colored flowers.

Related Terms
