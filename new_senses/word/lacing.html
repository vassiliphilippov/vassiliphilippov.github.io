lacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a beating or series of blows, especially as a form of corporal punishment
a beating; corporal punishment

Examples

He threatened to give anyone who misbehaved a good lacing.
Stories from the past sometimes mention a harsh lacing as punishment.
The boy received a severe lacing for breaking the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - an act of striking with repeated blows
  • Synonym: thrashing - a severe beating or whipping

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a cord threaded through eyelets or hooks to fasten and tighten the edges of a shoe, garment, or similar item together
cord for fastening shoes or garments

Examples

He tightened the lacing on his boots before going outside.
She replaced the worn lacing on her sneakers with a brighter color.
The corset's lacing was decorated with delicate ribbon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoelace - a string or cord used to fasten shoes

Etymology

From Middle English *lacing*, from Old French *lacier* (to lace, fasten), from *las* (lace, cord).

3. NOUN

/ ˈleɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a small amount of liquor added to a food or beverage for flavor
a dash of liquor in food or drink

Examples

She prefers her coffee with a lacing of whiskey on cold mornings.
The chef added a lacing of rum to the cake for extra flavor.
The hot chocolate came with a lacing of brandy to warm us up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - a small amount of liquid, especially alcohol, added to a drink

Etymology

From the verb 'lace', meaning to add or mix a small quantity, especially alcohol.
