doula

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːlə / (AmE), / ˈduːlə / (BrE)
A person, typically trained, who provides emotional and practical support to a woman during childbirth.
a childbirth support person

Examples

After training, she began working as a doula at a local hospital.
Many couples hire a doula to help them navigate the birth experience.
The doula provided encouragement and comfort throughout her labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwife - a person trained to assist women in childbirth

Etymology

From Modern Greek doúla (female servant or helper).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈduːlə / (AmE), / ˈduːlə / (BrE)
(in end-of-life care) a non-medical professional who provides emotional, practical and advocacy support to a dying person and their family.
end-of-life support person

Examples

Hospice programs sometimes hire a doula to support patients and their families during final days.
After her mother fell ill, she arranged for two experienced doulas to help with comfort and practical tasks.
The volunteer doula gently guided the family through conversations about wishes and funeral planning.
