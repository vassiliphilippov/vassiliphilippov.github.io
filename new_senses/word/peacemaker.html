Peacemaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a belt-fed machine gun used by United States troops in World War II and the Korean War, capable of firing more than 500 rounds per minute
U.S. military belt-fed machine gun

Examples

During training, soldiers learned to assemble and operate the Peacemaker efficiently.
The squad set up the Peacemaker on the ridge to provide covering fire.
Veterans recalled the deafening sound of the Peacemaker during intense combat operations.
