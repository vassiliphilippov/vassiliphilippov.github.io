snood

1. NOUN

/ snud / (AmE), / snuːd / (BrE)
an ornamental net or fabric bag worn at the back of a woman's head to hold the hair
net or bag for confining hair

Examples

During the 1940s, many women used a snood as a fashionable hair accessory.
She wore a delicate snood to keep her hair neat during the celebration.
The dancer's snood sparkled under the stage lights.

Etymology

From Old English snūd, meaning 'fillet or ribbon for the hair'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ snud / (AmE), / snuːd / (BrE)
a tubular knitted scarf or fabric tube worn around the neck that can be pulled up to cover the lower face or head for warmth or style.
tubular scarf or neck warmer

Examples

On windy mornings she pulled her cozy snood up over her ears and neck.
He kept a wool snood in his bag for unexpected cold evenings while traveling.
Many runners wear reflective snoods that can be pulled up to protect their faces.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neck gaiter - a tubular garment worn around the neck and sometimes pulled up over the lower face
  • Synonym: neck warmer - a piece of clothing worn around the neck to keep it warm

Etymology

From Old English snōd (a band or ribbon for the hair); the modern clothing sense extends the idea of a head/neck band to a tubular scarf.
