quintuplet

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtʌplət / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtʌplɪt / (BrE)
one of five children born at the same time to the same mother from a single pregnancy
one of five siblings born together

Examples

Each quintuplet had a distinct personality, even though they shared the same birthday.
Raising a quintuplet can be challenging due to the unique family dynamics involved.
The quintuplet sisters celebrated their birthday together with their family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadruplet - one of four children born at the same time
  • Degree Higher: sextuplet - one of six children born at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) and '-uplet' by analogy with 'triplet' and 'quadruplet'.

2. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtʌplət / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtʌplɪt / (BrE)
a set of five similar things considered as a unit
set of five similar things

Examples

Our research group formed a quintuplet of teams to tackle different parts of the project.
She collected a rare quintuplet of stamps issued in the same year.
The sculpture consisted of a quintuplet of identical bronze figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintet - a group of five people or things

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) + '-uplet', modelled after 'septuplet', 'octuplet'.
