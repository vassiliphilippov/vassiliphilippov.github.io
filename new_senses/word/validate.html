to validate

1. VERB

/ ˈvæl.ɪ.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈvæl.ɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to declare or make legally or officially valid; to confirm the legality or acceptability of something
make legally or officially valid

Examples

Her election was validated after a review confirmed the results.
The court must validate the contract before it can be enforced.
The government agency refused to validate his license to practice medicine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to invalidate - to make something invalid or null, especially legally

Etymology

From Latin validatus, past participle of validare, from validus (strong, effective).
