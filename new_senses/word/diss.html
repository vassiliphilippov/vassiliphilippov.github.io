diss

1. VERB

/ dɪs / (AmE), / dɪs / (BrE)
to treat someone with disrespect; to insult or speak rudely to someone, especially in a public or pointed way
to insult or show disrespect
slang

Examples

If you diss your boss, you might get into serious trouble at work.
She felt hurt after her friend dissed her in front of everyone.
The rapper dissed his rival in a new song that quickly went viral.

Synset Examples

He insulted her with his rude remarks
the student who had betrayed his classmate was dissed by everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect or scornful abuse
  • Synonym: offend - to cause someone to feel upset, annoyed, or resentful
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'disrespect,' first popularized in African American Vernacular English in the late 20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪs / (AmE), / dɪs / (BrE)
an insulting remark or action; (informal, especially in music) an insulting song made to call out or attack another person or artist (a 'diss track').
insult; insulting song (slang)
slang

Examples

The rapper dropped a brutal diss aimed at his rival during the award-show performance.
When he ignored her comment, she called it a public diss and walked away angrily.
Fans argued over whether the new song was a clever diss or just a publicity stunt.

Synset Examples

The rapper dropped a brutal diss aimed at his rival during the award-show performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a remark or action intended to offend or show disrespect
  • Synonym: diss track - a song created to insult or criticize another artist, often in rap or hip-hop
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration; opposite of an insult

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'disrespect,' first popularized in African American Vernacular English in the late 20th century.
