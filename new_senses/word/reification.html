reification

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of regarding or treating a person as a thing, especially by stripping away individuality or personal qualities.
treating people as things; loss of individuality

Examples

Critics argued that the film's portrayal of its characters was an example of reification.
Marxist thinkers highlight the dangers of reification in capitalist economies.
The reification of workers in industrial society can lead to widespread alienation and loss of purpose.

Synset Examples

according to Marx, treating labor as a commodity exemplified the reification of the individual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectification - regarding a person as an object, often used in feminist or sociological contexts

Etymology

From Latin res (thing) + -fication (making), via French réification.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of considering or treating an abstract concept or idea as if it were a real, concrete thing.
treating abstracts as concrete things

Examples

In sociology, reification can lead to treating social constructs as unchangeable realities.
Some philosophers criticize the reification of concepts such as freedom or justice.
The reification of emotions can make them seem more fixed than they are.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectification - treating something abstract as a concrete object
  • Antonym: abstraction - the process of considering something independently of its associations

Etymology

From Medieval Latin reificatio (making into a thing), from Latin res (thing) + facere (to make).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˌriːəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəfəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in computer science and data modeling) the process of turning a relationship, assertion, or abstract statement into a distinct, first‑class object or entity so it can be referenced, annotated, or given metadata.
making a relation a first‑class object (computing)

Examples

In the graph database, the reification of the user-item relationship created a node to store timestamps and ratings.
Developers used reification to attach provenance metadata to assertions in the RDF triple store.
Without reification, it was difficult to record who made each claim about a fact.
