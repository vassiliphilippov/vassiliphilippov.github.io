querier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛriər / (AmE), / ˈkwɪəriə / (BrE)
a person who asks a question, especially in a formal or technical context
a person who asks a question

Examples

During the meeting, the querier clarified her point by asking for further details.
Each database querier receives the requested information almost instantly.
The querier submitted his question in writing to the panel of experts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: questioner - a person who asks questions
  • Antonym: respondent - a person who replies to questions

Etymology

From the verb 'query,' itself from Latin 'quaerere' (to ask, seek), with the English agentive suffix '-er'.
