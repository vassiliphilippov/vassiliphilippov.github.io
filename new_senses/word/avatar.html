avatar

1. NOUN

/ ˈævəˌtɑr / (AmE), / ˈævətɑː / (BrE)
(in Hinduism) a manifestation of a deity or released soul in bodily form on earth; an incarnation, especially of Vishnu.
incarnation of a Hindu deity

Examples

According to legend, Krishna is considered an avatar of the god Vishnu.
Many ancient stories describe different avatars sent to restore balance on earth.
The avatar of Vishnu appeared to save the world from destruction.

Synset Examples

some Hindus consider Krishna to be an avatar of the god Vishnu

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'avatāra', meaning 'descent'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈævəˌtɑr / (AmE), / ˈævətɑː / (BrE)
a person or thing that represents or embodies an abstract idea or quality, especially a new form.
embodiment of an idea or quality

Examples

His generosity made him an avatar of kindness within the community.
In the story, the CEO is portrayed as an avatar of innovation and progress.
She became the very avatar of determination after overcoming so many obstacles.

Synset Examples

the embodiment of hope
the incarnation of evil
the very avatar of cunning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - a tangible or visible form of an idea, quality, or feeling
  • Synonym: incarnation - the embodiment of a deity or spirit as a physical form

Etymology

From Sanskrit avatāra (descent), referring to the descent of a deity to earth.
