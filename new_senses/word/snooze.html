snooze

1. VERB

/ snuːz / (AmE), / snuːz / (BrE)
to sleep lightly or for a short period of time, often during the day or unintentionally
sleep lightly for a short time

Examples

I like to snooze on the couch after lunch.
She snoozed through most of the movie last night.
The cat is snoozing in a patch of sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - to sleep for a short period, especially during the day
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00015296-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ snuːz / (AmE), / snuːz / (BrE)
a short, light sleep, especially one taken during the day and not in bed
a short, light sleep

Examples

After a long drive, she enjoyed a refreshing snooze in the car.
He likes to have a snooze during his lunch break at work.
I took a quick snooze on the sofa before dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - a short sleep, especially during the day
  • Synonym: siesta - a rest or nap taken after lunch, especially in hot countries
Source: WordNet

3. VERB NEW

/ snuːz / (AmE), / snuːz / (BrE)
to silence or delay an alarm, notification, or reminder temporarily by using a device's snooze feature.
delay or silence an alert

Examples

She decided to snooze the meeting notification until after lunch to focus.
He snoozed his alarm three times before finally getting out of bed.
Their app allows users to keep snoozing alerts for a chosen period on Fridays.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to stop something (such as a device) from making noise

Metaphorical extension from snooze 'short sleep' — applied to temporarily putting alerts 'to sleep'.

4. NOUN NEW

/ snuːz / (AmE), / snuːz / (BrE)
a device feature or the period of time by which an alarm or notification is temporarily silenced or delayed.
alarm delay feature/duration

Examples

I hit the snooze and slept another nine minutes before the alarm sounded again.
Most calendar apps show a temporary snooze option to delay meeting reminders.
She set a one-hour snooze for notifications while she finished her work.
