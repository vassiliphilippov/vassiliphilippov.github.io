farm

1. VERB

/ fɑrm / (AmE), / fɑːm / (BrE)
to work as a farmer; to cultivate land or raise animals for agricultural production
work as a farmer

Examples

His family has farmed the land for generations.
She decided to farm after graduating from college.
They farmed in the valley until the drought made it impossible.

Synset Examples

My son is farming in California

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultivate - to prepare and use land for crops or gardening

Etymology

From Old French 'fermer' (to rent, lease), from Medieval Latin 'firmare' (to fix, settle).

3. NOUN

/ fɑrm / (AmE), / fɑːm / (BrE)
an area of land and its buildings used for growing crops and raising animals, typically as a business
area of land for agriculture

Examples

After college, he decided to return home to help run the farm.
She grew up on a farm surrounded by fields and animals.
The family has owned the farm for three generations.

Synset Examples

it takes several people to work the farm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranch - a large farm, especially in North America, where cattle or other animals are bred
  • Synonym: plantation - a large farm, especially in a hot country, where crops such as coffee, sugar, and tobacco are grown

Etymology

From Old French *ferme* (farm, rent, lease), from Medieval Latin *firma* (fixed payment).

4. VERB

/ fɑrm / (AmE), / fɑːm / (BrE)
(historical) To collect fees or profits, especially by undertaking the right to do so from an authority in exchange for a fixed payment.
to collect fees or profits (historical)
archaic

Examples

He secured the right to farm customs duties at several ports.
In the 18th century, wealthy merchants would farm the taxes for the government.
The king allowed certain nobles to farm revenues from the royal estates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax farming - the practice of collecting taxes on behalf of an authority, often at a profit

Etymology

From Old French 'fermer', from Medieval Latin 'firma' (fixed payment, contract for rent), leading to the sense of collecting revenue for an authority.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

5. VERB NEW

/ fɑrm / (AmE), / fɑːm / (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and decentralized finance) to provide or lock cryptocurrency assets (for example by staking or supplying liquidity) in a protocol to earn rewards, interest, or new tokens.
earn yield on crypto assets

Examples

Investors often farm their tokens on decentralized exchanges to earn governance rewards.
She started farming stablecoins in a liquidity pool to get higher interest.
They farmed rewards from multiple protocols before withdrawing their crypto holdings.

Synset Examples

Investors often farm their tokens on decentralized exchanges to earn governance rewards.
She started farming stablecoins in a liquidity pool to get higher interest.
They farmed rewards from multiple protocols before withdrawing their crypto holdings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stake - to lock cryptocurrency in a protocol to support operations and earn rewards.
  • Synonym: liquidity mining - providing tokens to a liquidity pool on a decentralized exchange to earn fees or token rewards.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'farm' (cultivate, produce), applied in 21st-century cryptocurrency contexts; ultimately from Old French 'fermer'.
