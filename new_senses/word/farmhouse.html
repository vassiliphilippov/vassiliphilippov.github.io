farmhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrmˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house on a farm, typically used as the main residence by a farmer and their family.
house on a farm for a farmer's family

Examples

The old farmhouse was surrounded by fields of corn and wheat.
They renovated the farmhouse to preserve its rustic charm.
We spent the weekend at a cozy farmhouse in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottage - a small house, typically in the countryside

Etymology

From 'farm' + 'house', denoting a house located on a farm.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfɑrmˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house or interior designed in the farmhouse style or aesthetic, combining rustic or traditional farmhouse features (for example, exposed beams, shiplap, barn doors) with contemporary amenities and finishes.
house or interior in farmhouse style

Examples

They converted the warehouse into a modern farmhouse filled with shiplap walls and vintage lighting.
Her design blog shows suburban kitchens transformed into bright farmhouse spaces with apron sinks and open shelving.
Buyers liked the townhome’s simple farmhouse aesthetic, combining reclaimed wood beams with stainless-steel appliances.
