throttler

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːtlər / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtlə / (BrE)
a person who kills or attempts to kill by strangling
someone who kills by strangling

Examples

Detectives finally caught the throttler after weeks of terrifying the city.
Jack the Ripper was not a throttler, but other criminals in Victorian London used strangling as their method.
The police believed the murderer to be a notorious throttler known for attacking victims at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangler - a person who strangles someone, especially to death

Etymology

From the verb 'throttle' (to strangle) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈθrɑːtlər / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtlə / (BrE)
A device or software component that limits or regulates the rate of flow or activity (for example, data, requests, bandwidth, or fuel) to prevent overload or control performance.
device or software that limits rate/flow

Examples

The server uses a throttler to cap incoming requests and prevent overload.
Our API includes a throttler that limits calls per minute for each user account.
The network throttler slowed video streams during peak hours to reduce congestion.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: regulator - a device that controls or maintains a variable (such as flow, pressure, or speed) at a set level
  • Synonym: throttle (verb) - to reduce or restrict the flow or speed of something

From the verb 'throttle' (to choke, then to restrict flow) + agent suffix -er.
