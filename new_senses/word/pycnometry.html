pycnometry

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˈnɑmətri / (AmE), / pɪkˈnɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the determination of the density of a liquid by weighing it in a container (pycnometer) of known volume
method for measuring liquid density
medical

Examples

Advances in pycnometry have improved the accuracy of density measurements in research.
He wrote his thesis on the application of pycnometry in pharmaceutical analysis.
The laboratory used pycnometry to determine the exact density of the chemical solution.

Synset Examples

Measurement of the density of fine powders by pycnometry is subject to error especially when the particles are porous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pycnometer - an instrument for measuring the density of liquids

Etymology

From Greek puknos (dense, thick) + -metry (measurement).

2. NOUN NEW

/ pɪkˈnɑmətri / (AmE), / pɪkˈnɒmatri / (BrE)
(in materials science and analytical chemistry) the determination of the true or skeletal density of a solid or porous material by measuring the volume of gas displaced in a pycnometer (gas pycnometry).
gas-based measurement of solid density

Examples

The lab measured the ceramic's skeletal density using pycnometry with helium at controlled temperature.
For porous powders, pycnometry gives the true solid density by measuring displaced gas volume precisely.
They compared helium pycnometry results with mercury intrusion data to assess pore structure differences.

Synset Examples

The lab measured the ceramic's skeletal density using pycnometry with helium at controlled temperature.
For porous powders, pycnometry gives the true solid density by measuring displaced gas volume precisely.
They compared helium pycnometry results with mercury intrusion data to assess pore structure differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pycnometer - an instrument used to determine volume or density, here used for gas-displacement measurements
  • Synonym: gas pycnometry - the technique of measuring solid or skeletal density by gas displacement in a pycnometer
  • Synonym: skeletal density - the density of the solid material excluding open pores accessible to the measuring gas

Etymology

From Greek puknos (dense, thick) + -metry (measurement).
