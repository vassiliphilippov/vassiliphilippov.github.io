identity

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)
In mathematics, an operator or element that leaves another element unchanged when applied to it.
math operator leaving elements unchanged

Examples

In group theory, the identity element plays a crucial role in defining the structure.
Multiplying any number by the identity, which is 1, does not change its value.
Zero acts as the identity element in addition, as adding zero leaves numbers unchanged.

Synset Examples

the identity under numerical multiplication is 1

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inverse - an element that reverses the effect of another under an operation

Etymology

From Late Latin identitas (the same), from Latin idem (the same).

5. NOUN NEW

/ aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)

online representation and credentials

Examples

The company verifies each user's identity with two-factor authentication before allowing payments.
