balking

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔlkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
unwilling or refusing to proceed, act, or cooperate
refusing to proceed or cooperate

Examples

After much coaxing, the balking horse finally began to cross the stream.
Negotiations stalled due to a balking representative who objected to every proposal.
The balking employee refused to complete the assigned tasks without further explanation.

Synset Examples

a balking
a balky mule
a balky customer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooperative - willing to work or act together with others

Etymology

From the verb 'balk', of Middle English origin, from Old English balca ‘ridge, bank’.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbɔlkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing) the behaviour of an operation or design pattern in which a method immediately returns without taking action when required preconditions are not met.
operation that returns early when preconditions fail

Examples

The API uses balking to skip work if the input is invalid.
After detecting a race condition, the service implemented balking to avoid unsafe state changes.
Developers write unit tests to ensure a method's balking behaviour returns immediately on bad input.
