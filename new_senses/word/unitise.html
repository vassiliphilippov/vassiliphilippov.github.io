to unitise

1. VERB

/ ˈjuː.nəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to divide bulk material and process it as discrete units, often for shipping, storage, or handling purposes
divide bulk material into units

Examples

It is safer and more cost-effective to unitise heavy goods before transportation.
Modern packaging systems often unitise products for more efficient shipping.
The company decided to unitise raw materials to streamline the production process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unitize - U.S. spelling of 'unitise' (divide bulk material into units)

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ise', modeled after similar English verbs.

4. VERB NEW

/ ˈjuːnɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
(in mathematics and computing) to convert a vector or numerical quantity to unit length by dividing it by its magnitude; to normalize so the result has length one.
normalize to unit length
british

Examples

In the physics engine, we unitise the velocity vector to obtain a unit vector.
Before the simulation, she unitised every normal vector to ensure consistent shading.
When unitising direction vectors, always check for zero length to avoid division errors.
