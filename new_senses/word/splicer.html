splicer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈsplaɪsə / (BrE)
a mechanical device used to join two pieces of film, tape, or paper together
device for joining film, tape, or paper

Examples

Always clean the splicer after joining several strips of tape.
Modern studios often use a digital splicer instead of manual tools.
The film editor used a splicer to repair the damaged reel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joiner - a device or person that joins things together
  • Antonym: cutter - a device for making cuts in materials like tape or film

Etymology

From splice + -er, indicating someone or something that splices.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈsplaɪsə / (BrE)
a worker who joins ropes, cables, or similar materials together by interweaving their strands
worker who joins ropes by interweaving

Examples

As a splicer, she spent hours meticulously weaving the strands together.
Every cable-laying ship relied on a splicer to ensure strong, reliable connections.
The experienced splicer repaired the broken towline with expert skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spliceman - a person whose job is to splice things together, especially ropes or cables

Etymology

From splice + -er, indicating 'one who splices'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪsə / (BrE)
a person who joins pieces of wood together by means of a splice
person who joins wood with a splice

Examples

After years as a splicer, he could mend almost any damaged beam.
Each splicer must ensure the joints are secure and smooth before finishing the job.
The construction crew hired a skilled splicer to repair the old wooden bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joiner - a person whose occupation is making wooden fittings by joining pieces of wood

Etymology

From English 'splice' (to join by interweaving) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
