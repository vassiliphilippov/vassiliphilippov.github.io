throttling

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑt̬.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of strangling or suffocating someone by compressing their throat and restricting their airway
the act of strangling or suffocating

Examples

The detective explained that throttling requires significant force and intent.
The forensic report indicated throttling as the cause of death.
There were visible marks on her neck consistent with throttling.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈθrɑt.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing and telecommunications) the deliberate slowing or limiting of data transmission speeds by a network operator or service provider.
intentional slowing of data speeds

Examples

Many customers accused their ISP of throttling during the championship match, causing constant buffering and low quality.
Net neutrality advocates warn that secret throttling policies let providers favour some services over smaller competitors.
The technician discovered ISP throttling by comparing transfer speeds at different times of the day.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rate limiting - the practice of restricting the amount or rate of traffic to a service or API
  • Synonym: bandwidth limiting - the act of capping or reducing available bandwidth for certain services or users

Etymology

From the verb 'throttle', likely from Middle English 'throttil' (throat); later used metaphorically for restricting flow and extended to data networks.
