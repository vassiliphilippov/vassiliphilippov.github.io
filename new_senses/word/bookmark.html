bookmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbʊk.mɑːk / (BrE)
a piece of paper, ribbon, or similar material placed between the pages of a book to mark a reader's place
marker for keeping one's place in a book

Examples

I couldn't find my bookmark, so I used a receipt instead.
She slipped a bookmark between the pages before putting the novel down for the night.
The library was giving away free bookmarks designed by local artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placeholder - something used to keep a position or place for something else

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8b\u028ak\u02ccm\u0251rk / (AmE), / \u02c8b\u028ak.m\u0251\u02d0k / (BrE)
a saved marker (in a web browser, app, or document) that records a specific page, location, or link so a user can return to it later.
digital saved link or location

Examples

I added a bookmark to the article in my browser to read it later.
She saved the recipe as a bookmark in the cooking app for quick access.
He organized each research bookmark into folders to keep his sources tidy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

Compound of book + mark; originally a physical marker, later extended to digital use as web browsers and apps adopted the concept.
