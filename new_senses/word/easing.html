easing

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
a reduction in difficulty, severity, or intensity; the act of making something less strict or severe
a lessening of difficulty or severity

Examples

After hours of negotiation, an easing in tensions was finally achieved.
The central bank announced an easing of interest rates to support economic recovery.
There has been a noticeable easing of travel restrictions over the past month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightening - an increase in difficulty or severity

Etymology

From the verb 'ease', from Old French 'aisier' (to ease, assist), ultimately from Latin 'ad-' + 'iacēre' (to lie).

/ ˈiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something unpleasant less severe or intense, such as pain or discomfort
act of making something less unpleasant

Examples

The medication provided significant easing of her chronic back pain.
The new policy led to an easing of restrictions in the city.

Etymology

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computer animation and user-interface design) the use of non-linear timing curves or functions to control the speed of a motion or transition so it accelerates or decelerates in a natural way.
timing curves for smooth motion

Examples

Web developers commonly apply easing functions to smooth page transitions and improve perceived performance.
The animator adjusted the easing on the character's walk cycle to make movement feel more natural.
Modern UI frameworks include preset easing curves designers can reuse for consistent motion across components.

Synset Examples

Web developers commonly apply easing functions to smooth page transitions and improve perceived performance.
The animator adjusted the easing on the character's walk cycle to make movement feel more natural.
Modern UI frameworks include preset easing curves designers can reuse for consistent motion across components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easing function - a mathematical function that defines how a transition accelerates or decelerates over time
  • Synonym: interpolation - a method of calculating intermediate values, often used to compute in-between frames or motion values

Etymology

From the verb ease (Old French aisier), extended metaphorically in computing to mean 'make motion smoother' by the early 21st century.
