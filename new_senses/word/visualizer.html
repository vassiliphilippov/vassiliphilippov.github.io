visualizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person whose mental imagery is predominantly visual, often thinking in pictures rather than words
person who thinks mainly in images

Examples

As a visualizer, she finds it easy to recall information in the form of mental pictures.
Many artists are natural visualizers who process ideas through imagery rather than language.
Teachers use diagrams and charts to help visualizers learn more effectively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verbalizer - person whose mental imagery is chiefly verbal

Etymology

From 'visualize' + '-er', denoting one who visualizes.

a software tool or component that creates graphical, often real‑time, representations of audio, data, or system processes to help users see patterns or activity.
software that displays data or audio visually

Examples

The music player now includes a visualizer that responds to the song's beats and colors the screen.
During the presentation, she used a visualizer to show real-time graphs of the sales figures.
Audio engineers ran the track through a visualizer to inspect the frequency peaks and identify noise.
