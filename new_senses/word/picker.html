picker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpɪkə / (BrE)
a person who chooses or selects something from a group or collection
person who chooses or selects

Examples

As a fruit picker, she traveled from one farm to another during harvest season.
The book club picker selects a new title every month.
The team picker always tried to choose the best players first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selector - a person or device that selects

Etymology

From the verb 'pick' + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpɪkə / (BrE)
a person who gathers crops, fruits, or other produce by hand
person who gathers crops or fruit

Examples

Each picker is paid by the weight of fruit collected at the end of the day.
Many students worked as a picker on local farms over the summer.
The orchard hired several pickers during the busy apple harvest season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvester - a person or machine that gathers a crop
  • Antonym: planter - a person or machine that plants seeds or young plants

Etymology

From 'pick' + '-er', denoting a person who picks.
