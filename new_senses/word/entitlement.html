entitlement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtaɪtəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪtəlmənt / (BrE)
a right granted by law or contract, especially to receive benefits such as payments or services
right granted by law or contract

Examples

Changes in law may affect your entitlement to unemployment benefits.
Citizens have a legal entitlement to certain services provided by the government.
Many retirees depend on their social security entitlements for financial stability.

Synset Examples

entitlements make up the major part of the federal budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right - a legal, moral, or social entitlement to have or do something

Etymology

From English 'entitle' + '-ment', formed in the 19th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u026an\u02c8ta\u026at\u0259lm\u0259nt / (AmE), / \u026an\u02c8ta\u026at\u0259lm\u0259nt / (BrE)
(in computing and IT security) a defined permission, access right, or license that allows a user or account to use a resource, feature, or service.
IT access permission

Examples

Before granting access, the administrator checked her entitlement to the company's payroll database.
Auditors reviewed user entitlements to ensure no one had unnecessary privileges on the network.
The cloud service displays each customer's subscription entitlement and its remaining quotas for APIs.
