spinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɪnə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is to spin fibers into threads or yarn, especially using a spinning wheel or similar device
person who spins fibers into threads

Examples

In historical novels, a spinner is often depicted as sitting by the fire, twisting flax into thread.
The spinner worked diligently at her wheel, turning wool into fine yarn.
With the invention of factories, the traditional spinner gradually became less common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaver - a person who weaves fabric from threads

Etymology

From Old English *spinnere*, from *spinnan* (to spin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɪnə / (BrE)
a type of fishing lure that spins or revolves as it is pulled through the water, designed to attract fish
revolving fishing lure

Examples

Many fishermen believe that a brightly colored spinner works best in murky water.
She caught her first fish using a silver spinner she bought at the local tackle shop.
The angler tied a new spinner to his line to attract trout in the river.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnər / (AmE), / ˈspɪnə / (BrE)
a device used in board games, consisting of a dial and a pointer that is spun to determine a random outcome or the next move
board game device with a spinning pointer

Examples

Players take turns spinning the spinner to see how many spaces to move.
Some board games use dice instead of a spinner to introduce chance.
The spinner landed on red, so she had to draw a card.

Etymology

From 'spin' + '-er,' referring to something that spins.

a small animated graphic in a computer or mobile interface that rotates or moves to show that a program, page, or process is loading or working
graphical loading indicator (UI)
