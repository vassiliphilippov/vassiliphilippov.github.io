polemics

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɛmɪks / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of Christian theology concerned with refuting doctrinal errors or opposing views
theological refutation of errors

Examples

During the Reformation, polemics played an important role in religious debates.
He specialized in polemics, writing extensively against heretical movements within the church.
The university offered a course on the history and methods of polemics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apologetics - the branch of Christian theology concerned with the defense and proof of Christianity

Etymology

From Greek 'polemikos' (warlike, hostile).

2. NOUN NEW

/ poʊˈlɛmɪks / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪks / (BrE)
Hostile or strongly opinionated argument or writing that attacks or defends ideas, especially in public or political contexts.
hostile public argumentation

Examples

During the election, polemics on social media often drowned out more moderate voices.
The columnist's polemics attacked the opposition's policies with little attempt at balance.
University forums featured sharp polemics between students about the new campus speech policy.
