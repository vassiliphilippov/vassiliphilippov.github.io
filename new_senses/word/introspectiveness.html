introspectiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈspɛk.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈspɛk.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of examining or thinking carefully about your own thoughts and feelings
the quality of self-examination of thoughts and feelings

Examples

Her introspectiveness led her to keep a detailed journal of her everyday life.
His introspectiveness helped him understand the roots of his anxiety.
Writers are often praised for the introspectiveness they bring to their characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introspection - the examination of one's own mental and emotional processes

Etymology

From introspective + -ness, ultimately from Latin intrōspicere (to look within).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌɪn.trəˈspɛk.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈspɛk.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
(in computing and AI) the ability of a machine, program, or system to examine its own internal processes, outputs, or reasoning in order to detect errors, explain behavior, or improve performance.
self-examination by a system

Examples

The chatbot's enhanced introspectiveness helps it review its reasoning before replying to users.
Engineers added logging so the system's introspectiveness could detect and report performance regressions automatically.
Robots with high introspectiveness can diagnose sensor faults and adjust behavior without human help.
