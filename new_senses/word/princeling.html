princeling

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪns.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a minor or insignificant prince, especially one who rules a small or unimportant state
a minor or petty prince

Examples

Several princelings competed for control of the fragmented territory after the empire collapsed.
The politician was accused of behaving like a spoiled princeling.
The powerful king dismissed the local princeling as an insignificant ruler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prince - male royal ruler or son of a monarch

Etymology

Formed from 'prince' + diminutive suffix '-ling' to indicate smallness or insignificance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪns.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a young prince, especially one regarded as insignificant or lacking real power
a young or minor prince

Examples

Several princelings competed for influence within the royal family.
Some critics dismissed him as merely a princeling with no authority of his own.
The princeling was not involved in any matters of real importance at court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prince - a male member of a royal family, especially a son of a monarch

Etymology

Formed from 'prince' + diminutive suffix '-ling'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈprɪns.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.lɪŋ / (BrE)
an informal term for a child or young relative of a powerful political or economic elite who uses family connections to obtain positions, influence, or advantages.
child of a powerful elite; political insider

Examples

The opposition accused the government of favoring the president's princeling in the tender for the contract.
As a princeling, she relied on family connections to secure a diplomatic posting abroad.
Several princelings were promoted to senior roles despite limited experience and public criticism.
