nitrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtrəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪtrəs / (BrE)
(of a chemical substance) containing or relating to nitrogen, especially with a lower oxidation state.
containing or relating to nitrogen

Examples

Certain fertilizers contain nitrous compounds to help promote plant growth.
Engineers are studying the environmental impact of nitrous emissions from vehicles.
The doctor used nitrous oxide as an anesthetic during the procedure.

Synset Examples

nitric acid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nitric - containing or relating to nitrogen, especially with a higher oxidation state

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrosus' (full of nitre).

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8na6atr59s / (AmE), / c8n6atr59s / (BrE)
(informal) nitrous oxide (NOS), a gas injected into an internal-combustion engine to produce a temporary increase in power.
nitrous oxide for engine boost
informal

Examples

The race car's driver activated the nitrous for a sudden burst of speed down the straight.
They filled the toolbox with small tanks of nitrous before the weekend's tuning session.
Mechanics warned that using too much nitrous could seriously damage the engine's pistons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrous oxide - a chemical compound (N2O) used medically as an anesthetic and in engines to boost power
  • Synonym: NOS - abbreviation for nitrous oxide system used to increase engine power

Etymology

From adjective 'nitrous' (from Latin 'nitrosus', full of nitre); noun use is an informal shortening of 'nitrous oxide'.
