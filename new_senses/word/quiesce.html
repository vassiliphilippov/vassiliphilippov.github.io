to quiesce

1. VERB

/ kwiˈɛs / (AmE), / kwiˈɛs / (BrE)
to become quiet or less active; to settle into a state of rest or inactivity
become quiet or inactive

Examples

As the storm passed, the winds gradually quiesced and the night became calm.
The classroom quiesced when the principal entered and everyone waited for her to speak.
The patient’s symptoms may quiesce after receiving the proper treatment.

Synset Examples

The audience fell silent when the speaker entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiet - to make or become quiet

Etymology

From Latin quiescere (to rest, become quiet), from quies (rest, quiet).

2. VERB NEW

/ kwic85bs / (AmE), / kwic85bs / (BrE)
(in computing) to put a computer system, service, or storage into a controlled, temporarily quiet state by pausing or suspending normal operations so that maintenance, backups, or snapshots can be performed safely.
pause operations for maintenance (computing)

Examples

The IT team decided to quiesce the database to perform a safe backup.
Before taking the snapshot, the administrator quiesced all virtual machines to avoid data corruption.
Modern storage systems support APIs that let applications quiesce services during online upgrades.
