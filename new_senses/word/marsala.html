Marsala

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈsɑːlə / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɑːlə / (BrE)
a dark, sweet or semisweet fortified dessert wine from Sicily, often used in cooking or as a dessert wine
sweet Sicilian dessert wine

Examples

Chicken Marsala is a classic Italian-American dish featuring this flavorful wine.
I bought a bottle of Marsala to use in the sauce for tonight's dinner.
She served a glass of Marsala with the dessert course.

Etymology

Named after the town of Marsala in Sicily, Italy, where it is produced.
