crystal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.təl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.təl / (BrE)
the transparent protective cover over the face of a watch or clock
protective cover of a watch face

Examples

He replaced the broken crystal to restore the watch to its original appearance.
Many luxury watches use sapphire crystal to protect their faces.
The crystal on my watch got scratched when I dropped it.

Etymology

From Greek *krystallos* (ice, crystal), referring to the transparency of early covers, often made from rock crystal.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪstl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl / (BrE)
A solid substance in which the atoms are arranged in a highly ordered, repeating pattern, often resulting in a regular geometric shape.
solid with a regular atomic structure

Examples

A diamond is a natural crystal composed entirely of carbon atoms.
Salt forms beautiful crystals when water evaporates from a solution.
Snowflakes are unique crystals of ice that develop in the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amorphous - a solid without a regular atomic structure

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'krustallos' (ice, crystal), via Latin 'crystallum'.
