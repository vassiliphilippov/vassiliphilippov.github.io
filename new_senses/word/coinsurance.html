coinsurance

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˌkəʊɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
a type of insurance in which two or more underwriters jointly cover the same risk or policy
insurance jointly issued by multiple underwriters

Examples

Companies often prefer coinsurance when dealing with large or complex assets.
In certain cases, coinsurance allows insurers to share liability and minimize risk.
The property was protected by coinsurance held with several leading firms.

Etymology

Formed from co- (jointly) + insurance (protection against risk).

In health insurance, the portion of covered medical costs that the insured must pay, usually expressed as a percentage, after any deductible has been met.
insured's percentage of medical costs
medical

Examples

Her coinsurance for hospital bills was 20% after meeting the deductible.
Before surgery he checked that his coinsurance rate would not make treatment unaffordable.
The plan's high deductible and 30% coinsurance meant she paid much more out-of-pocket.
