placement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act or manner of positioning or arranging something in a particular space
way something is arranged spatially

Examples

Proper placement of the speakers is important for the best sound quality.
She was not satisfied with the placement of the tables in the dining room.
The placement of the artwork in the gallery created a striking effect.

Synset Examples

the arrangement of the furniture
the placement of the chairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the way in which things are organized or placed
  • Antonym: disorder - a lack of arrangement or order

Etymology

From place + -ment, modeled after French 'placement'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of putting something in a particular position or place
the act of positioning or placing

Examples

Proper placement of the painting on the wall is important for balance.
She was responsible for the careful placement of the exhibits in the gallery.
The placement of the furniture made the room feel larger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: positioning - the action of putting something in a particular place

Etymology

From French *placement*, from placer (to place).
