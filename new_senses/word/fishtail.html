fishtail

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪʃˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌteɪl / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or aircraft) to move or swerve from side to side, often due to loss of traction or control.
swerve from side to side

Examples

Be careful—if you drive too fast in the rain, your vehicle might fishtail.
The car began to fishtail on the icy road when the driver braked suddenly.
The pilot struggled to control the plane as it fishtailed on landing.

Synset Examples

The airplane fishtailed on the runway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to change direction suddenly, especially to avoid something

Etymology

From the resemblance to the motion of a fish’s tail moving side to side.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfɪʃteɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃteɪl / (BrE)
a hairstyle in which hair is braided into a two‑strand plait that produces a pattern resembling a fish's tail (often called a 'fishtail braid').
fishtail braid (hairstyle)

Examples

The model's fishtail braid drew compliments on the red carpet.
She learned to make a neat fishtail during the weekend braiding workshop.
For Sunday, he asked his sister to do his daughter's hair in a fishtail.

Synset Examples

The model's fishtail braid drew compliments on the red carpet.
She learned to make a neat fishtail during the weekend braiding workshop.
For Sunday, he asked his sister to do his daughter's hair in a fishtail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braid - hair that is woven or twisted together in strands
  • Synonym: plait - a length of hair made by braiding three or more strands, or the act of braiding

Etymology

Named for the braid's resemblance to a fish's tail.
