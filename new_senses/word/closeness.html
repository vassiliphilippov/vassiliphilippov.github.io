7. NOUN NEW

/ ˈkloʊsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsnəs / (BrE)
(in graph theory and network analysis) a centrality measure that indicates how near a node is to all other nodes, usually defined as the reciprocal of the sum of shortest-path distances from that node to every other node.
network centrality measure

Examples

In network analysis, researchers often compute closeness to find nodes that spread information quickly.
A node's low average distance to others gives it high closeness in the communication network.
Engineers ranked routers by closeness centrality to improve resilience under heavy traffic loads.
