recalcitrance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (AmE), / rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (BrE)
the quality of being stubbornly uncooperative or resistant to authority or control
stubborn resistance to authority

Examples

Despite repeated warnings, his recalcitrance only seemed to grow stronger over time.
The organization's recalcitrance in adopting new regulations frustrated government officials.
The student's recalcitrance made classroom management particularly challenging for the new teacher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - stubborn refusal to change one's opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: compliance - the act of conforming or yielding to authority

Etymology

From Latin 'recalcitrant-', meaning 'kicking back', from 'recalcitrare' (to kick back).

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (AmE), / rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (BrE)
(in biomass science and biofuel research) the inherent resistance of plant biomass to chemical or enzymatic deconstruction, which hinders conversion to fermentable sugars.
biomass resistance to breakdown

Examples

Researchers work to reduce recalcitrance in biomass to improve biofuel yields in pilot plants.
High lignin content increases plant cell wall recalcitrance, making enzymatic breakdown more difficult.
The team pretreated the straw to lower recalcitrance before fermentation in the lab.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance to deconstruction - the difficulty of breaking down biomass into its component sugars and polymers
  • Antonym: digestibility - the ease with which biomass is broken down into fermentable sugars; often considered the opposite of recalcitrance
