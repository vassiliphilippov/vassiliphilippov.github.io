helminthic

1. ADJ

/ hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (AmE), / hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or agent) capable of expelling or destroying parasitic worms.
expelling or destroying parasitic worms
medical

Examples

Certain helminthic drugs are effective against intestinal parasites in both humans and animals.
Researchers are developing new helminthic agents to combat resistant strains of parasitic worms.
The doctor prescribed a helminthic treatment for the patient's worm infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthelmintic - a substance that expels or destroys parasitic worms

Etymology

From Greek helmins, helminthos (worm) + -ic.

2. NOUN

/ hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (AmE), / hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (BrE)
a medication capable of causing the evacuation of parasitic intestinal worms from the body
medicine that expels intestinal worms
medical

Examples

Research is ongoing to develop safer and more effective helminthic drugs for children.
Some helminthics can be found over the counter, while others require a prescription.
The doctor prescribed a helminthic to treat the patient’s roundworm infection.

3. ADJ NEW

/ hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (AmE), / hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (BrE)
(in medicine) relating to the deliberate use of live parasitic worms (helminths) or their products as a treatment to modify immune responses in autoimmune or inflammatory conditions.
relating to worm-based therapy
medical

Examples

Some researchers are exploring helminthic therapies as alternatives to conventional immunosuppressants.
The clinic offered a small trial of helminthic treatment to patients with severe inflammatory bowel disease.
After months without improvement, he decided to try a monitored helminthic protocol under doctor supervision.
