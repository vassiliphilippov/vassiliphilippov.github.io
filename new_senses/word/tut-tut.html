tut-tut

1. VERB

/ tʌtˈtʌt / (AmE), / tʌtˈtʌt / (BrE)
to express disapproval or annoyance by making a quick clicking sound with the tongue
express disapproval with tongue click

Examples

He tut-tuts every time he reads bad news in the papers.
My grandmother would often tut-tut whenever she disapproved of our behavior.
The teacher tut-tutted at the late students as they entered the classroom.

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of disapproval, first recorded in the late 16th century.

2. INTJ NEW

/ t\u028ct\u02c8t\u028ct / (AmE), / t\u028ct\u02c8t\u028ct / (BrE)
An exclamation representing a short sound of disapproval or mild reproach, used to show annoyance or disapproval.
expression of mild disapproval

Examples

When she saw the messy room, she murmured tut-tut and shook her head.
The critic's review ended with a single tut-tut that made the author blush.
At the meeting, an older member clicked his tongue and said tut-tut under his breath.
