yellowhammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛl.oʊˌhæm.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛl.əʊˌhæm.ə / (BrE)
a large North American woodpecker (Colaptes auratus), also known as the northern flicker, with a red neck and yellow undersides to its wings and tail
a North American woodpecker with yellow underparts

Examples

Birdwatchers often look for the distinctive markings of the yellowhammer during spring migrations.
The bright feathers of the yellowhammer flashed as it took flight from the old oak tree.
We spotted a yellowhammer pecking at the ground in the early morning light.

Etymology

From 'yellow' + 'hammer,' possibly referring to the yellow coloring and tapping habits of the bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌhæmər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌhamə / (BrE)
A small European bunting, the male of which has bright yellow plumage.
European bunting with bright yellow male

Examples

A bright male yellowhammer perched on the fence, singing its distinctive song.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a flock of yellowhammers in the hedgerow.
The yellowhammer can be recognized by the streaks on its brown back and its vibrant yellow head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a small songbird found in Eurasia and Africa, typically with a stout bill and colorful plumage

Etymology

From Old English *geolu* (yellow) + *amora* (hammer, a kind of bird), referring to the bird's color and call.
