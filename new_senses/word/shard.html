shard

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrd / (AmE), / ʃɑːd / (BrE)
a sharp, broken piece of a brittle material such as glass, ceramic, or metal
a broken, sharp piece of something brittle

Examples

A shard from the broken window landed on the carpet.
Archaeologists found a shard of ancient pottery at the site.
He stepped carefully to avoid a shard of glass on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or detached
  • Synonym: splinter - a small, thin, sharp piece of wood, glass, etc., split or broken off from the main body

Etymology

From Old English sceard (a cut, notch, gap, fragment).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ʃɑrd / (AmE), / ʃɑːd / (BrE)
a partition of a database, ledger, or other data store that contains only a subset of the total data, used to distribute storage and processing across multiple machines.
database or blockchain partition

Examples

The web app routed requests to the correct shard to reduce database latency during peak traffic.
Developers split the user table across multiple shards so each server handles fewer records.
In some blockchains, a shard processes only a portion of transactions to increase throughput.
