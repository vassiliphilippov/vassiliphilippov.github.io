accenting

1. NOUN

/ ˈækˌsɛntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaks(ə)ntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving special importance or emphasis to something
act of highlighting or emphasizing

Examples

In music, accenting the first beat gives the piece a lively rhythm.
Modern design often involves accenting specific features to draw the viewer's attention.
The accenting of certain words in her speech made the message more powerful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphasizing - the act of giving emphasis to something
  • Antonym: downplaying - the act of making something seem less important

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'accent,' ultimately from Latin 'accentus' (song added to speech).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u00e6k\u02ccs\u025bnt\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8aks(\u0259)nt\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
the act of adding accent marks or other diacritic signs to letters or words in written text to show correct pronunciation or orthography.
adding diacritic marks to text

Examples

The editor checked the manuscript for correct accenting of French and Spanish proper names.
In typesetting, automated rules handle most accenting but manual fixes are often necessary for names.
Students practicing pronunciation benefited from careful accenting of vowels in the language exercise.
