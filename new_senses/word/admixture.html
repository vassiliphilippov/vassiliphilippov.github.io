admixture

1. NOUN

/ ædˈmɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
a substance added and mixed with another, often to improve or change its qualities
added ingredient; mixture component

Examples

Ancient concrete often contained an admixture of volcanic ash to improve durability.
The recipe calls for an admixture of herbs to enhance the flavor.
There was a noticeable admixture of optimism in his otherwise cautious remarks.

Synset Examples

the growing medium should be equal parts of sand and loam with an admixture of peat moss and cow manure
a large intermixture of sand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of two or more substances

Etymology

From Latin admiscere (to mix in), from ad- (to) + miscere (to mix).

2. NOUN

/ ædˈmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / ædˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
The state resulting from the addition of something that impairs the quality or lessens the value of something else.
impairment of quality due to an added substance

Examples

Critics believe the admixture of commercial interests has compromised the integrity of journalism.
Even a small admixture can significantly affect the purity of the chemical solution.
The admixture of low-quality materials has diminished the strength of the product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the presence of an unwanted substance that makes something impure
  • Antonym: purity - the state of being free from contamination or admixture

Etymology

From Latin admixtura, from admiscere (to mix with).

3. NOUN

/ ædˈmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / ædˈmɪkstjə / (BrE)
the act of mixing different substances together, or the substance that results from mixing
the act or result of mixing

Examples

A small admixture of foreign words enriches the English language.
Cultural admixture can create unique traditions within a community.
The admixture of water and cement forms the basis of concrete.

Synset Examples

paste made by a mix of flour and water
the mixing of sound channels in the recording studio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different things blended together

Etymology

4. NOUN NEW

/ ædˈmɪkstʃə / (AmE), / əˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
(in population genetics) the mixing of previously separate populations that produces individuals or populations with ancestry from multiple source groups; also the proportion of ancestry contributed by each source.
genetic mixing of populations

Examples

Researchers estimated the proportion of African admixture in the coastal population from genome data.
Recent studies show that ancient admixture between human groups shaped modern patterns of genetic diversity.
Population geneticists use statistical models to detect admixture and estimate ancestry proportions in individuals.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
