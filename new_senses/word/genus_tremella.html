genus Tremella

1. NOUN NEW

/ trəˈmɛlə / (AmE), / trɪˈmiːlə / (BrE)
(in cosmetics) an ingredient or extract from the fungus Tremella fuciformis (snow mushroom) used in skin-care products for its ability to attract and retain moisture.
cosmetic moisturizing ingredient

Examples

Many Korean brands add Tremella to their serums to boost hydration for dry skin.
The moisturizer lists Tremella extract among its active humectants on the ingredient panel.
She noticed visible plumping after using a night cream with Tremella for two weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humectant - a substance that attracts and holds moisture; describes Tremella's function in cosmetics

Etymology

From Latin 'tremella' (something trembling or jelly); name of the fungus from which the extract is obtained.
