Selene

1. PROPN

/ səˈliːni / (AmE), / sɛˈliːni / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess of the Moon, equated with the Roman goddess Luna.
Greek goddess of the Moon

Examples

In ancient poems, Selene is often described driving her silver chariot across the night sky.
Some artists have depicted Selene with a crescent moon above her head.
The myth of Selene and Endymion has inspired countless works of art and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luna - Roman goddess of the Moon

Etymology

From Greek Σελήνη (Selēnē), meaning 'moon'.

2. NOUN

/ səˈliːni / (AmE), / sɛˈliːni / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, including the lookdowns and moonfishes, found in warm ocean waters.
a genus of marine fish (lookdowns, moonfishes)

Examples

A striking feature of fish in the Selene genus is their silvery, reflective scales.
Scientists have studied Selene to understand adaptations to shallow coastal environments.
The Selene genus includes several fish species with laterally flattened bodies, such as the lookdown.

Etymology

From Greek Selēnē (the Moon), likely referring to the moonlike appearance of some species.

3. PROPN NEW

/ səˈliːni / (AmE), / sɛˈliːni / (BrE)
A Japanese lunar orbiter mission (Selenological and Engineering Explorer, SELENE) launched in 2007, also known as Kaguya, that mapped the Moon's surface and gravity.
Japanese lunar orbiter mission (Kaguya)

Examples

The lunar mission Selene mapped the Moon's gravity and topography during its two-year primary mission.
Scientists used data from Selene to create a high-resolution map of the Moon's crustal composition.
Japan's space agency released images taken by Selene showing previously unseen details of lunar craters.
