stovepipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊvˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈstəʊvˌpaɪp / (BrE)
a metal pipe of large diameter used to connect a stove to a flue or chimney
metal pipe connecting stove to chimney

Examples

He cleaned the stovepipe every autumn to keep the chimney clear.
Make sure the stovepipe is securely attached to prevent smoke from escaping into the room.
The old cabin had a rusty stovepipe running from the wood stove up through the roof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney - a vertical channel for conveying smoke from a fireplace or furnace

2. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊvˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈstəʊvˌpaɪp / (BrE)
a man's hat with a tall, cylindrical crown, traditionally covered with silk or beaver fur; also known as a stovepipe hat or top hat
a tall, cylindrical man's hat

Examples

Abraham Lincoln is famously depicted in portraits wearing a tall stovepipe hat.
He wore a black stovepipe to the formal banquet, giving him a distinguished appearance.
The museum displayed a genuine 19th-century stovepipe among its collection of vintage clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top hat - a tall, flat-crowned, cylindrical formal hat for men

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the vertical pipe of a stove.
