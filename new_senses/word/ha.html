HA

1. NOUN

/ eɪtʃ eɪ / (AmE), / eɪtʃ eɪ / (BrE)
In astronomy, the angular distance of a celestial object measured westward along the celestial equator from the observer's local meridian; also known as hour angle.
astronomical hour angle

Examples

Precise determination of HA is critical for celestial navigation and timekeeping.
The telescope's software automatically adjusts for changes in HA throughout the night.
To locate a star in the night sky, astronomers often calculate its HA relative to the observer's position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hour angle - angular distance along the celestial equator from the observer's meridian

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'hour angle', a term in astronomy.

2. NOUN NEW

/ eɪtʃ eɪ / (AmE), / eɪtʃ eɪ / (BrE)
(computing) Abbreviation of high availability: a system design approach that aims to keep services operational with minimal downtime, typically using redundancy and automatic failover.
high availability (IT)

Examples

The data center relies on HA architectures to ensure near-continuous uptime.
We tested the system's HA configuration by simulating server failures during peak traffic.
Implementing HA often means adding redundant components and automated failover procedures.
