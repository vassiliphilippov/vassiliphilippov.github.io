photobomb

1. VERB

/ ˈfoʊtəˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌbɒm / (BrE)
to unexpectedly or intentionally appear in someone else's photograph, often as a joke or prank, usually to spoil or add humor to the picture
to appear in someone else's photo as a prank
informal

Examples

It's always funny when someone photobombs a serious family photo.
My little brother tried to photobomb every picture we took at the party.
We were posing in front of the museum when a stranger photobombed our selfie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: videobomb - to appear unexpectedly in someone else's video, often to spoil or add humor

Etymology

Blend of 'photo' and 'bomb', first used in the early 2000s with the rise of digital photography and social media.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8fo\u028at\u0259\u02ccb\u0251m / (AmE), / \u02c8f\u0259\u028at\u0259\u028a\u02ccb\u0252m / (BrE)
An unexpected or deliberate intrusion into the frame of a photograph that spoils or adds humorous effect; also the photograph containing such an intrusion.
unexpected intrusion in a photo
informal

Examples

The couple's portrait became a viral photobomb when the best man jumped into the background.
She laughed when she found a silly photobomb of her dog peering from behind the statue.
After the ceremony they checked their phone and deleted several awkward photobombs before sharing pictures.
