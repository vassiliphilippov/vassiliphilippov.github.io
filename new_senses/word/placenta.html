placenta

1. NOUN

/ pləˈsɛntə / (AmE), / pləˈsɛntə / (BrE)
An organ in the uterus of most mammals that provides oxygen and nutrients to the developing fetus and removes waste products.
organ supplying nutrients to fetus
medical

Examples

During pregnancy, the placenta plays a vital role in supporting fetal development.
Problems with the placenta can lead to complications for both mother and child.
The doctor examined the placenta after the baby was delivered.

Etymology

From Latin 'placenta' (flat cake), from Greek 'plakount-', 'plakous' (flat cake), due to its flat shape.

2. NOUN

/ pləˈsɛntə / (AmE), / pləˈsɛntə / (BrE)
In botany, the part of the ovary of a flowering plant where the ovules develop and are attached.
botanical: ovary part where ovules form

Examples

Botanists studied the development of seeds on the placenta under various growth conditions.
In many flowering plants, the placenta is located along the inner walls of the ovary.
The arrangement of ovules on the placenta can be important for identifying different plant species.

Etymology

From Latin placenta (flat cake), originally referring to the afterbirth in mammals and later extended to similar plant structures.
