semihard

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛmiˌhɑrd / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌhɑːd / (BrE)
partly but not completely hard; having a consistency between soft and hard, often used to describe certain types of cheese or materials.
partly but not completely hard

Examples

The cheesemonger recommended a semihard cheese for the tasting platter.
They prefer mattresses that are semihard rather than very firm or too soft.
This plastic is classified as semihard, making it flexible yet sturdy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: soft - not hard; easy to compress, shape, or cut
  • Degree Higher: hard - solid, firm, and not easily broken or bent

Etymology

From semi- (Latin, meaning 'half' or 'partially') + hard.

/ c8s5bmicch51rd / (AmE), / c8s5bmicch51d0d / (BrE)
(in machine learning) (of a negative training example) moderately difficult to distinguish from a positive example; used in training methods (e.g., semihard negative mining) to improve model discrimination.
moderately difficult negative example (ML)

Examples

During training, the model sampled semihard negatives to help the embedding separate similar classes.
Researchers found that mining semihard examples improved convergence without introducing noisy false negatives in batches.
The triplet loss benefits when each batch contains at least one semihard pair alongside easy and hard pairs.
