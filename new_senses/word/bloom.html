bloom

1. NOUN

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
a healthy, rosy color in the cheeks, often suggesting good health or vitality
rosy color in cheeks

Examples

Despite her illness, there was still a bloom in her face.
Her cheeks had a healthy bloom after a brisk walk in the cold air.
The bloom of youth was visible in his complexion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance

Etymology

From Middle English *blome*, from Old Norse *blóm* (flower, blossom).

9. NOUN NEW

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
In digital imaging and computer graphics, a visual effect that simulates bright light bleeding into surrounding areas, producing a soft glow or halo around highlights.
digital glow effect

Examples

The game uses a heavy bloom effect to create glow around the street lamps.
Adjusting the bloom slider reduced the unrealistic halos around highlights in the render.
Photographers add subtle bloom during post-processing to soften bright edges and enhance atmosphere.
