learning

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
deep and extensive knowledge acquired through study or scholarship
profound scholarly knowledge

Examples

Her learning was evident in the depth of her lectures.
Many sought his advice, trusting in his vast learning.
The professor was admired for his remarkable learning in ancient history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erudition - extensive knowledge acquired through study
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information

Etymology

From Old English 'leornung' (the action of learning); related to 'learn'.
