start up

1. NOUN NEW

/ ˈstɑrtʌp / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtʌp / (BrE)
a recently formed company or venture, especially a small, innovative business in technology or entrepreneurship, typically in an early stage of development.
newly formed company, often tech

Examples

The local startup attracted investors with its innovative app and scalable revenue model.
After the accelerator program, our startup doubled its team and launched a paid beta.
Many founders dream of turning a small idea into a successful startup in a few years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: new business - a company or commercial enterprise that has been recently established
  • Antonym: established company - a business that has been operating for a long time and is no longer in its early stage

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'start up' (to begin); noun use attested from the late 20th century in business and technology contexts.
