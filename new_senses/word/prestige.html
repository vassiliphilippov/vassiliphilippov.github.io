prestige

1. NOUN

/ prɛˈstiʒ / (AmE), / prɛˈstiːʒ / (BrE)
the respect and admiration that someone or something receives because of their success, influence, or wealth
respect or admiration from success or status

Examples

He accepted the position mainly for the prestige it brought, not for the salary.
The company enjoys considerable prestige among tech enthusiasts.
Winning the award gave the scientist great prestige in her field.

Synset Examples

he wanted to achieve power and prestige

Related Terms

  • Synonym: status - social or professional position; rank or standing
  • Synonym: honor - high respect or esteem
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From French 'prestige' (illusion, magic, later 'prestige, prestige'), from Latin 'praestigiae' (illusions, conjuring tricks).

2. NOUN NEW

/ prɛˈstiʒ / (AmE), / prɛˈstiːʒ / (BrE)
(in video games) a special rank, tier, or status (often earned by reaching milestones or by resetting progress) that indicates achievement and may unlock rewards or exclusive items.
video‑game rank or tier

Examples

Many players reach prestige after completing all seasonal challenges and resetting their rank.
The tournament awarded exclusive skins to those with high prestige from previous seasons.
She debated whether to enter prestige mode and lose her unlocked weapons for a new challenge.

Synset Examples

Many players reach prestige after completing all seasonal challenges and resetting their rank.
The tournament awarded exclusive skins to those with high prestige from previous seasons.
She debated whether to enter prestige mode and lose her unlocked weapons for a new challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rank - a player's position or level in a game or system
  • Synonym: tier - a level in a ranking system, often used to sort players or rewards

Etymology

Borrowed from French prestige (respect, influence), ultimately from Latin praestigiae; applied metaphorically in gaming from the early 2000s to name a high rank or reset mechanic.
