envoy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒn.vwɑː / (BrE)
a short stanza at the end of certain poems, especially in French verse, serving as a concluding address or dedication
concluding stanza in some poems

Examples

In medieval French poetry, the envoy often names the dedicatee or patron.
The envoy serves as a formal conclusion, summarizing the poem’s main message.
The poet included an envoy at the end of the ballad to address the reader directly.

Etymology

From Old French 'envoi', meaning 'message' or 'sending'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
a diplomatic representative sent by a government, ranking below an ambassador
diplomatic representative below ambassador

Examples

An envoy delivered the official message to the foreign government.
She worked as an envoy in Eastern Europe for several years.
The president sent an envoy to negotiate peace with the neighboring country.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ambassador - highest-ranking diplomatic representative
  • Synonym: diplomat - an official representing a country abroad

Etymology

From French 'envoyé', past participle of 'envoyer' (to send), from Latin 'inviare'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
a person sent as a representative on a diplomatic or official mission
diplomatic representative or messenger

Examples

As a United Nations envoy, she traveled to regions affected by conflict.
The country sent an envoy to negotiate a peace treaty.
The president's envoy arrived in the capital for urgent talks with officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambassador - an official representative sent by a country as its chief diplomat
  • Synonym: delegate - a person authorized to represent others

Etymology

From French 'envoyé', past participle of 'envoyer' (to send), from Latin 'inviare'.
