to disenchant

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃænt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃɑːnt / (BrE)
to free someone from enchantment or illusion; to cause (someone) to stop believing in something that is not true or realistic
to free from enchantment or illusion

Examples

Her harsh words served to disenchant him with the idea of success at any cost.
The film may disenchant avid fans who hoped for a magical ending.
The magician was able to disenchant the prince with a special herb.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enchant - to fill with great delight or put under a spell

Etymology

From French 'désenchanter', from Latin 'in-' (in) with 'cantare' (to sing); related to 'enchant'.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˌdɪsənˈtʃænt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsənˈtʃɑːnt / (BrE)
(in fantasy and gaming) to remove magical properties or enchantments from an object, often to make it non-magical or to recover magical components.
remove magic from an object (gaming)

Examples

The mage decided to disenchant the cursed sword to reclaim its spellbound gems for crafting.
They disenchanted several artifacts at the workshop and sold the recovered runes to collectors.
After hours of disenchanting, she finally removed the enchantment that prevented the door from opening.
