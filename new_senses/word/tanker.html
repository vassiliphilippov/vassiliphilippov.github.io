tanker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkə / (BrE)
a soldier who operates or drives a military tank
soldier who drives a military tank

Examples

After training, she became a skilled tanker in her unit.
Many tankers participate in simulated battles to hone their driving and combat skills.
The tanker maneuvered the armored vehicle carefully through the debris-strewn street.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkə / (BrE)
a large motor vehicle designed to transport liquids, gases, or dry bulk cargo on roads.
vehicle for transporting liquids or gases

Examples

A milk tanker collected fresh milk from several farms.
Firefighters used a water tanker to supply the hoses during the wildfire.
The tanker overturned on the highway, spilling thousands of gallons of fuel.

Synset Examples

Some less visible distinctions amongst tank trucks have to do with their intended use: compliance with human food regulations, refrigeration capability, acid resistance, pressurization capability, and more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank truck - a truck for transporting liquid or gas in bulk

Etymology

From 'tank' (large container for liquids) + '-er' (denoting agent or object involved in an action).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkə / (BrE)
A large ship designed to transport liquids, especially crude oil, in bulk.
ship for carrying oil or other liquids

Examples

A massive tanker docked at the port to unload its cargo.
Environmentalists are concerned about tanker spills damaging marine life.
The tanker carried millions of barrels of crude oil across the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil tanker - a large ship for carrying oil in bulk

Etymology

From 'tank' + '-er', denoting a vehicle for carrying liquids.

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkə / (BrE)
(in gaming) a player or character who takes the 'tank' role, attracting enemy attacks and absorbing damage to protect teammates.
player who plays the tank role
informal slang

Examples

In the raid, he served as the team's tanker, soaking up damage and protecting teammates.
Good tankers keep enemy attention while healers restore the party's health under fire.
She wrote a beginner's guide explaining how to play the tanker role effectively in dungeons.
