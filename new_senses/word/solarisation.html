solarisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of being exposed to sunlight, especially resulting in changes to a material or organism
exposure to sunlight

Examples

Due to solarisation, the color of the fabric gradually faded.
Prolonged solarisation of certain plastics can make them brittle over time.
The solarisation of seeds is used to control pests before planting.

Etymology

From Latin 'solaris' (of the sun) + English '-ation'.
