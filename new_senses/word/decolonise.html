to decolonise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒl.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
To grant independence to a country or territory that was previously a colony.
grant independence to a colony

Examples

Historians have studied the movement to decolonise India in great depth.
Many African countries were decolonised in the twentieth century after years of struggle.
The process to decolonise territories often involved complex negotiations with colonial powers.

Synset Examples

West Africa was decolonized in the early 1960's

Related Terms

  • Antonym: colonise - establish a colony in or on a place

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + colonise, after French décoloniser.

2. VERB NEW

/ dɪˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒl.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
To remove or challenge colonial biases, structures, or legacies in institutions, curricula, or knowledge, often to centre indigenous or formerly marginalised perspectives.
remove colonial bias from institutions

Examples

Universities have begun to decolonise their curricula by adding indigenous authors and histories to required courses.
The museum is working to decolonise its exhibits by returning artifacts and revising explanatory labels.
Activists argue governments must decolonise education policies to centre marginalized voices and local knowledge.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decolonize - US spelling of decolonise; to remove colonial influence from institutions or knowledge
  • Antonym: colonise - to establish control over a place or people, or impose colonial structures (opposite in broad sense)

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + colonise, after French décoloniser; extended metaphorically to mean removing colonial influence from institutions and knowledge.
