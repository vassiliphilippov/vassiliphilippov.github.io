governor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvɚnɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənə / (BrE)
a device that automatically regulates the speed of a machine by controlling the supply of fuel, power, or another input.
device to regulate machine speed

Examples

A malfunctioning governor can cause fluctuations in engine speed.
Old windmills often relied on a mechanical governor to control the rotation of their sails.
The steam engine's governor keeps it from running too fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulator - a device for controlling the operation of a machine, process, or system

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənə / (BrE)
the elected official who is the head of the government of a state in the United States or other countries
head of a state government

Examples

After the hurricane, the governor declared a state of emergency and coordinated the relief efforts.
Each state in the U.S. has its own governor who leads the executive branch of the state government.
The newly elected governor promised to focus on improving education and healthcare in the state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - the elected head of a republican state

Etymology

From Old French 'governeor', from Latin 'gubernator' (steersman, governor), from 'gubernare' (to steer, to govern).
