bruiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːzər / (AmE), / ˈbruːzə / (BrE)
a large, strong, and heavyset man, often implying toughness or aggressiveness
large, strong, heavyset man

Examples

After years as a bruiser in the boxing ring, he retired undefeated.
Despite his reputation as a bruiser, he was gentle with his family.
The club hired a bruiser for security during late-night events.

Synset Examples

he was a bull of a man
a thick-skinned bruiser ready to give as good as he got

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those they perceive as vulnerable
  • Antonym: weakling - a person lacking physical strength

Etymology

From 'bruise' + '-er', originally referring to someone who gives bruises or fights.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbruːzər / (AmE), / ˈbruːzə / (BrE)
(in video games) a durable melee fighter role that soaks damage and deals steady close-range damage, often combining some offense and defense.
durable melee fighter role (gaming)

Examples

In the MOBA, the bruiser pushed into the enemy team while shrugging off incoming spells.
Their strategy relied on a fed bruiser diving the backline to finish off squishy targets.
She prefers playing a top-lane bruiser who trades damage and builds both offense and defense.

Synset Examples

In the MOBA, the bruiser pushed into the enemy team while shrugging off incoming spells.
Their strategy relied on a fed bruiser diving the backline to finish off squishy targets.
She prefers playing a top-lane bruiser who trades damage and builds both offense and defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawler - a close-combat fighter who focuses on physical damage; often used interchangeably with bruiser.
  • Synonym: tank - a character who primarily absorbs damage for the team; similar to a bruiser but usually less damage-focused.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'bruiser' (one who inflicts or receives bruises); applied in gaming to describe tough melee characters.
