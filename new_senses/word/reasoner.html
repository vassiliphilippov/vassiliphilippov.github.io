reasoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːzənər / (AmE), / ˈriːzənə / (BrE)
a person who thinks or argues in a logical, rational way
a person who reasons logically

Examples

In debates, a skilled reasoner can sometimes convince even the most stubborn opponent.
She was known as an excellent reasoner who could dissect any argument thoroughly.
The philosopher's reputation as a brilliant reasoner was recognized throughout the academic world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logician - a person skilled at logic and reasoning

Etymology

From 'reason' + '-er', with 'reason' from Old French 'raison', from Latin 'ratio'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ri\u02d0z\u0259n\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8ri\u02d0z\u0259n\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computer science, especially AI and the semantic web) a software component or program that performs automated logical inference over a knowledge base or ontology to derive new facts or check consistency.
software inference engine

Examples

The ontology was validated by a reasoner to infer hidden relationships.
During testing, their reasoner produced several new class assertions from the RDF data.
Multiple reasoners support different logic profiles, so we chose the most expressive one.
