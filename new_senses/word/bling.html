bling

1. NOUN

/ blɪŋ / (AmE), / blɪŋ / (BrE)
flashy, expensive, and ostentatious jewelry, especially as worn to attract attention or display wealth
flashy jewelry, especially to show off wealth
slang

Examples

He loved to show off his bling at every party.
Some people think too much bling looks tacky.
The celebrity's outfit was covered in diamonds and bling.

Synset Examples

the rapper was loaded with bling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bling-bling - an alternative form emphasizing the glitter of such jewelry

Etymology

Popularized in the late 1990s by hip-hop culture, possibly imitative of the sound of light reflecting from jewelry.

2. VERB NEW

/ blɪŋ / (AmE), / blɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal, slang) to decorate, outfit, or upgrade something in a showy or ostentatious way, often using shiny or expensive-looking accessories.
decorate in a flashy way
slang informal

Examples

They bling out their car with chrome rims and neon lights for the weekend show.
For the festival she blinged her phone case with rhinestones and tiny metal studs.
Local artists enjoy blinging old guitars to create one-of-a-kind stage instruments.

Synset Examples

They bling out their car with chrome rims and neon lights for the weekend show.
For the festival she blinged her phone case with rhinestones and tiny metal studs.
Local artists enjoy blinging old guitars to create one-of-a-kind stage instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embellish - to decorate or add detail to make something more attractive or showy
  • Synonym: customize - to change or add to something to make it suitable for a particular person or purpose

Etymology

From the slang noun 'bling' (flashy jewelry), popularized in late 1990s hip-hop culture; verb formed by conversion.
