interval

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tər.vəl / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
A period of time between two events or points; a measurable length of time separating two moments.
period of time between two events

Examples

After a short interval, the concert resumed with the second act.
The class is divided into three intervals of twenty minutes each.
There was a long interval between their first and second meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duration - the length of time that something continues
  • Degree Lower: moment - an exact point in time
  • Degree Higher: era - a long and distinct period of history with a particular feature or characteristic

Etymology

From Latin 'intervallum' (space between ramparts, distance, interval).
