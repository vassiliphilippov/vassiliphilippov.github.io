hotspot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌspɒt / (BrE)
a place where heat or radiation is especially intense, often compared to its surroundings
place of intense heat or radiation

Examples

Scientists measured unusually high temperatures near the hotspot on the ocean floor.
The Chernobyl reactor's core became a hotspot for radioactive contamination.
Volcanoes often form above a hotspot deep within the Earth's mantle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌspɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtspɒt / (BrE)
A place that is popular and lively, especially for entertainment or social activities.
popular place for entertainment

Examples

That new bar downtown has quickly become a local hotspot for young professionals.
The city’s arts district is a well-known hotspot for live music and trendy restaurants.
Tourists often ask locals to recommend a hotspot to experience the city’s nightlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightspot - a place open at night for entertainment, especially music or dancing
