stylus

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈstaɪləs / (BrE)
A small, pointed device, often made of metal or diamond, attached to the cartridge of a record player that makes contact with the grooves of a vinyl record to read the audio signal.
needle of a record player

Examples

A worn stylus can damage your records and affect playback fidelity.
Carefully lowering the stylus onto the spinning album, he waited for the music to begin.
The stylus on her vintage record player needed to be replaced for better sound quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needle - the small, pointed part of a record player that touches the record

Etymology

From Latin stylus (writing instrument), originally referring to a pointed instrument for engraving or writing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈstaɪləs / (BrE)
a pointed tool used for writing, drawing, or engraving, especially on wax, clay, or other surfaces
pointed writing or drawing tool

Examples

Artists in ancient times often relied on a stylus to sketch on wax tablets.
He carefully etched the pattern onto the clay tablet using a stylus.
The technician used a precise stylus to engrave serial numbers on each part.

Synset Examples

he drew the design on the stencil with a steel stylus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an instrument for writing or drawing with ink
  • Synonym: pencil - an instrument for writing or drawing, typically with graphite

Etymology

From Latin 'stylus' (writing instrument), originally from Greek 'stylos' (pillar, stake).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈstaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈstaɪləs / (BrE)
A pen-shaped instrument used to interact with a touchscreen or graphics tablet, allowing writing, drawing, or precise input; often electronic and may support pressure sensitivity.
pen-like touchscreen input device

Examples

She drafted the diagram on her tablet using a stylus with pressure sensitivity.
The designer prefers a fine-tip stylus for digital sketching because it feels more precise.
After the update, his tablet recognised the new stylus instantly and the handwriting flowed smoothly.
