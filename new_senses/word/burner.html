burner

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
A part of a stove or range that produces heat for cooking, on which pots and pans are placed.
part of stove for heating pots and pans

Examples

Always make sure the burner is turned off after you finish cooking.
She placed the kettle on the back burner to bring the water to a boil.
The stove has four burners, so you can cook several dishes at once.

Synset Examples

the electric range had one large burner and three smaller one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotplate - a flat heated surface for cooking food or keeping it warm
  • Synonym: grill - a device on which food is cooked over an open flame

Etymology

From 'burn' + '-er', indicating something that burns or produces heat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
A device or apparatus for burning fuel or refuse to produce heat or energy.
device for burning fuel or refuse

Examples

The facility installed a new waste burner designed to reduce emissions.
The gas burner in the kitchen stopped working properly this morning.
The laboratory technician adjusted the Bunsen burner to get a blue flame.

Synset Examples

a diesel engine is an oil burner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incinerator - a device for burning waste material at high temperatures
  • Synonym: stove - a device that burns fuel for heating or cooking

Etymology

From 'burn' + '-er', denoting 'one who or that which burns'.
