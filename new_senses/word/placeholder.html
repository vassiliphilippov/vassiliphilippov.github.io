placeholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪshoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈpleɪshəʊldə / (BrE)
a symbol or variable in a mathematical or logical expression that may be replaced by a specific value or element from a set
symbol to be replaced in math or logic

Examples

In the equation, x acts as a placeholder for an unknown value.
Programmers often use 'foo' or 'bar' as a placeholder name in sample code.
The placeholder can be replaced with any number from the set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variable - a symbol representing a value that may change

Etymology

From 'place' and 'holder', meaning something that holds a place for something else.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ple\u026asho\u028ald\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8ple\u026ash\u0259\u028ald\u0259 / (BrE)
A short hint or example shown inside an empty form or input field to indicate the expected type of input; typically displayed in lighter text and removed when the user begins typing.
hint text inside a form field

Examples

The login form shows a placeholder with an example email address to guide users.
Because of poor contrast, the placeholder was hard to read for older users during testing.
Remove the placeholder text when the field receives focus to avoid confusing keyboard users.
