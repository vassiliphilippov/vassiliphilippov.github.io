to containerize

1. VERB

/ kənˈteɪnəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnəraɪz / (BrE)
to package (goods or cargo) in a container, especially for transport
package goods in a container

Examples

After the goods were containerized, transporting them overseas became much simpler.
Many shipping firms now containerize products as a standard practice.
The company decided to containerize all exports to reduce shipping losses.

Synset Examples

The cargo was containerized for safe and efficient shipping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - to wrap or box goods for transport or sale

Etymology

From 'container' + '-ize', as in American English word formation.

2. VERB NEW

/ kənˈteɪnəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnəraɪz / (BrE)
(in computing) to package an application, service, or its environment into a container image or runtime so it can run consistently across different systems.
package software in a container

Examples

The team decided to containerize the microservices architecture to streamline deployment across cloud environments.
After they containerized the app, rolling updates became faster and resource use was easier to manage.
The startup began containerizing services during testing to ensure consistent behavior on developers' machines.

Synset Examples

The team decided to containerize the microservices architecture to streamline deployment across cloud environments.
After they containerized the app, rolling updates became faster and resource use was easier to manage.
The startup began containerizing services during testing to ensure consistent behavior on developers' machines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - to bundle software and its dependencies together for distribution or deployment
  • Synonym: dockerize - to put an application into a Docker container (a specific container platform)

Etymology

From 'container' + '-ize'; extended metaphorically to software deployment in the 21st century.
