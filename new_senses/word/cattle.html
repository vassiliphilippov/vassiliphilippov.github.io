cattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkætl / (BrE)
Domesticated bovine animals as a group, regardless of sex or age, often kept for milk, meat, or work.
domesticated bovine animals, collectively

Examples

A severe drought can threaten the health of cattle in many regions.
The farmers moved their cattle from one pasture to another to ensure fresh grazing.
Vaccinations help protect cattle from dangerous diseases.

Synset Examples

so many head of cattle
wait till the cows come home
seven thin and ill-favored kine
a team of oxen

Etymology

From Old French 'catel' (property, livestock), from Latin 'capitalis' (of the head, principal).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8k\u00e6tl\u0329 / (AmE), / \u02c8k\u00e6tl / (BrE)
(in computing, especially cloud and DevOps) servers or virtual machines treated as interchangeable, disposable units that are replaced automatically rather than individually maintained.
interchangeable, replaceable servers

Examples

In our cloud setup, broken machines are treated like cattle and replaced instead of repaired.
The DevOps team prefers immutable infrastructure where instances are disposable cattle, not unique pets.
When an instance fails, automated systems simply terminate the cattle and spin up replacements.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: server - a computer or virtual machine that provides data, resources, or services to other computers on a network
  • Antonym: pet (computing) - a machine treated as unique and maintained individually; contrasted with 'cattle' in cloud operations

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the animal sense 'cattle' (domesticated bovine animals); used in cloud computing in the 21st century to contrast "pets" (unique machines).
