vitalizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪt̬əˌlaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who imparts energy, vitality, or enthusiasm to others; someone who invigorates or enlivens people
person who invigorates or enlivens others

Examples

As a team leader, she became the vitalizer everyone relied on to boost morale after setbacks.
His role as a vitalizer was essential to the group's ongoing enthusiasm and drive.
The event needed a vitalizer to infuse excitement into the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invigorator - someone who gives strength or energy
  • Antonym: enervator - someone who saps energy

Etymology

From 'vitalize' (to give life or energy), formed from 'vital' (from Latin 'vitalis', of life) + '-izer', an agentive suffix.
