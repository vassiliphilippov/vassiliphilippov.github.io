underlay

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəleɪ / (BrE)
A layer of material placed underneath a carpet, flooring, or other surface to provide cushioning or insulation.
padding material beneath carpet or flooring

Examples

Before installing the new carpet, the fitter laid down a thick underlay to add comfort and insulation.
Good quality underlay can help prolong the life of your flooring.
She chose a rubber underlay to reduce noise in her apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underlayment - material placed beneath flooring for support or insulation

Etymology

From under- (beneath) + lay (to place); first recorded usage in the 19th century.

3. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈleɪ / (BrE)
to raise or support the level of printing by placing a piece of paper or cardboard under the type or plate
raise/support in printing with paper underneath

Examples

Before starting the run, the technician meticulously underlaid the plate to correct for the imperfect press bed.
Carefully underlaying the form with thin paper can solve minor alignment issues in traditional printing.
The printer had to underlay the type to ensure an even impression across the whole page.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From under- (below) + lay (to place), referring to placing something beneath.
