referable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɝː.ə.bəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɛː.rə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be assigned or attributed to a cause, source, or origin
able to be assigned or attributed to something

Examples

The changes in policy seem referable to recent economic pressures.
The mistake was clearly referable to a misunderstanding between the departments.
The patient's symptoms were referable to a viral infection.

Synset Examples

punctuation errors ascribable to careless proofreading
the cancellation of the concert was due to the rain
the oversight was not imputable to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascribable - able to be attributed to a particular cause or source
  • Synonym: attributable - able to be regarded as being caused by
  • Synonym: imputable - able to be ascribed or assigned to someone or something

Etymology

From 'refer' + '-able', ultimately from Latin 'referre' (to bring back).

warranting referral to a specialist
medical

Examples

If symptoms persist after standard treatment, the patient is referable to secondary care for specialist review.
