truthfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtruːθfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈtruːθfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being truthful and honest; the habit of telling the truth and not lying
quality of being truthful; honesty

Examples

Children should be taught the importance of truthfulness from an early age.
Her truthfulness made her a trusted friend among her colleagues.
The witness's truthfulness was never in doubt during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest
  • Antonym: deceitfulness - the quality of being deceitful

Etymology

From 'truthful' + '-ness'. 'Truthful' from 'truth' (Old English 'trēowþ') + '-ful'.
