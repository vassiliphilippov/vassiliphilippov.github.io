forked

1. ADJ

/ fɔrkt / (AmE), / fɔːkt / (BrE)
having a shape that is divided into two or more branches or parts, much like the prongs of a fork.
divided into two or more branches

Examples

Last night’s storm produced several flashes of forked lightning that lit up the sky.
She admired the bird’s distinctive forked tail as it soared above the trees.
The road ahead split into two forked paths leading in different directions.

2. ADJ

/ fɔrkt / (AmE), / fɔːkt / (BrE)
(of speech or intention) deceitful or ambiguous, intended to mislead or deceive.
deceitful or ambiguous, intended to deceive

Examples

He spoke with a forked tongue, saying one thing but meaning another.
Many fairy tales warn of villains with forked tongues who deceive innocent people.
The politician's forked statements left everyone confused about his true intentions.

Synset Examples

a sly double meaning
spoke with forked tongue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - deliberately misleading or dishonest
  • Antonym: honest - truthful and sincere

Etymology

From fork + -ed, referring figuratively to the split tongue of snakes, often symbolizing deception.

3. ADJ NEW

/ fɔrkt / (AmE), / fɔːkt / (BrE)
(in computing) created by copying a project's source code or repository so development can continue independently, resulting in divergence from the original.
created by forking (software)

Examples

The forked repository contains experimental features not present in the original.
After disagreements, the community continued development on the forked project independently.
GitHub shows that the popular package now has several forked copies with unique changes.
