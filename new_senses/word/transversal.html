transversal

1. ADJ

/ trænzˈvɜr.səl / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜː.səl / (BrE)
lying or extending across something; situated or acting in a crosswise direction, especially at right angles to the main axis.
lying or extending across

Examples

In geometry, a transversal is a line that cuts across two or more other lines.
Scientists measured the transversal vibrations of the bridge during the stress test.
The transversal beams reinforced the structure by crossing the main supports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transverse - lying or placed across something; crosswise

Etymology

From Late Latin transversalis (lying across), from Latin transversus (turned across, crosswise).

2. ADJ NEW

/ trænzˈvɜr.səl / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜː.səl / (BrE)
spanning or relating to more than one subject, discipline, or area; cross-cutting across different fields rather than confined to a single domain.
cross-disciplinary, cross-cutting

Examples

The curriculum emphasizes transversal skills, encouraging students to apply critical thinking across subjects.
Their research project took a transversal approach, combining insights from psychology, design, and computer science.
The new policy supports transversal initiatives that bridge health, education, and environmental programs.
