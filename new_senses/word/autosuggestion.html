autosuggestion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːtoʊsəˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊsəˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
a technique in which a person uses self-induced suggestions or affirmations to influence their own thoughts, feelings, or behavior, as popularized by Emile Coué in the early 20th century
self-improvement through self-suggestion

Examples

Emile Coué's theory of autosuggestion encouraged people to repeat positive phrases to themselves.
Many self-help books draw on principles of autosuggestion to promote personal growth.
She practiced autosuggestion every morning to boost her confidence before work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affirmation - a positive statement used to encourage oneself

Etymology

From French 'autosuggestion', from 'auto-' (self) + 'suggestion' (suggestion).

an automatic, algorithmic suggestion of possible completions, queries, or terms shown by a user interface (for example a search box or form) as the user types.
automatic UI suggestion (autocomplete)

Examples

The website's search bar shows autosuggestion to help users find results faster.
He disabled autosuggestions in his browser to avoid seeing irrelevant query completions.
Developers added autosuggestion so users finish long product names with fewer keystrokes.
