finalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfaɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something final or complete
the act of making final or complete

Examples

After years of negotiation, the finalization of the merger was officially announced.
The finalization of the contract took several months due to legal complexities.
We are awaiting the finalization of travel plans before booking the tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of finishing something
  • Synonym: termination - the act of bringing something to an end
  • Antonym: initiation - the act of beginning something

Etymology

From 'finalize' (from final + -ize) + -ation.

(in blockchain technology) the process by which a block or transaction becomes permanently accepted on the chain and cannot be changed after sufficient confirmations or consensus.
blockchain irreversible acceptance

Examples

The network achieves finalization when a block becomes immutable after enough confirmations.
Developers wait for finalization before releasing funds from the smart contract to avoid reorgs.
Users typically wait several confirmations to ensure finalization and avoid losing funds to reorganizations.
