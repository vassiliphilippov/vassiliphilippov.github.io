to metricise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛtrɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to express or convert (something) in terms of the metric system
express in the metric system
british

Examples

After the company metricised its packaging, exports to Europe increased.
Many countries began to metricise their road signs in the late twentieth century.
The government decided to metricise all official measurements by next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to metricize - express in the metric system (American spelling)

Etymology

Formed from 'metric' + '-ise', following the pattern of other verbs meaning 'to make' or 'apply' a certain system.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛtrɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to convert from a non-metric system of measurement to the metric system
convert to the metric system
british

Examples

After the reform, textbooks were quickly metricised to match international standards.
Many countries began to metricise their road signs in the 1970s.
The factory is planning to metricise all measurement equipment by next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to metricize - convert from a non-metric to the metric system (American spelling)

Etymology

Formed from 'metric' + '-ise', following the British English convention for forming verbs meaning 'to make or become'.

british
