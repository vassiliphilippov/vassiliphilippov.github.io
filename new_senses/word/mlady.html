m'lady

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪdi / (AmE), / məˈleɪdi / (BrE)
An old-fashioned, polite way of addressing a woman, especially one of noble rank; my lady.
old-fashioned polite address to a woman
archaic

Examples

"Good morning, m'lady," said the butler as he opened the door.
In the play, the servant respectfully addresses the countess as m'lady throughout.
The knight bowed deeply before m'lady, awaiting her command.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milady - formal address to a noblewoman

Etymology

Contraction of 'my lady', with the initial 'my' elided; found in historical and dialectal English.

sarcastic label for pretentious chivalrous person
