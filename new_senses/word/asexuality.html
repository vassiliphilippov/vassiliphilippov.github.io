asexuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˌsɛkʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌsɛkʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
the state of having no evident sex or sex organs, especially in certain animals, plants, or organisms
lack of evident sex or sex organs

Examples

Because of its asexuality, the species does not display typical mating behaviors.
Some simple organisms exhibit asexuality, lacking any visible differences between sexes.
The asexuality of certain plants allows them to reproduce without pollination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexuality - the state of having sex or sex organs or being sexually attracted

Etymology

From a- (not) + sexuality, patterned after asexual + -ity.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌeɪˌsɛkʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌsɛkʃuˈæləti / (BrE)
a sexual orientation marked by little or no sexual attraction to other people; individuals may still form romantic relationships or experience romantic attraction.
lack of sexual attraction (sexual orientation)

Examples

Many young people today openly identify with asexuality and join supportive online communities.
Her doctor listened carefully when she explained her asexuality and how it affected relationships.
Universities now include asexuality in diversity training to raise awareness and understanding.
