staging

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of discarding a stage from a multistage rocket during flight
rocket stage separation process

Examples

After successful staging, the rocket's second engine ignited to continue the ascent.
Engineers closely monitored the staging event to ensure there were no malfunctions.
Proper staging is essential for maximizing the efficiency of multistage rockets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage separation - the act of separating rocket stages

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.dʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of presenting a play or other performance on stage, including its arrangement and execution
production of a play or performance

Examples

Her staging of the musical included original choreography and lighting effects.
The director was praised for the creative staging that brought new energy to the classic script.
The innovative staging of the play impressed critics and audiences alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: production - the process of organizing and presenting a play or performance

Etymology

From 'stage' + '-ing', first used in this sense in the mid 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
a temporary structure of scaffolds used to support people and materials during the construction or repair of a building or in theatrical productions
system of scaffolds; temporary support structure

Examples

Proper safety measures are required when erecting staging on a construction site.
The lighting crew positioned themselves on the staging high above the stage.
The workers assembled the staging before beginning repairs on the facade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaffolding - a temporary structure on the outside of a building used by workers while building, repairing, or cleaning
