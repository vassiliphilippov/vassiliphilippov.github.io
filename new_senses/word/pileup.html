pileup

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlʌp / (BrE)
a crash involving several vehicles on the road, often as a result of one initial collision
road accident with multiple vehicle collisions

Examples

A chain-reaction pileup can be especially dangerous in icy weather.
The highway was closed for hours because of a massive pileup involving more than twenty cars.
Yesterday’s fog was blamed for the seven-vehicle pileup on Main Street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - an accident involving vehicles striking each other or an object
  • Synonym: collision - an instance of one moving object or person striking against another

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpaɪlʌp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlʌp / (BrE)
(in genetics and bioinformatics) a summary or view that aggregates nucleotide calls and read counts from sequencing reads aligned to a single genomic position, used to detect variants and assess evidence at that site.
aggregated sequencing reads at a genomic position

Examples

The sequencing data produced a clear pileup at chr7:142,345,900, enabling SNP calling.
During visual inspection, she noticed multiple pileups that suggested a high local sequencing depth.
The bioinformatics pipeline creates a textual pileup showing base counts and quality at each position.
