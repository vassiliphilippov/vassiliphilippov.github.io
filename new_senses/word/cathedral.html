2. NOUN

/ kəˈθiːdrəl / (AmE), / kəˈθiːdrəl / (BrE)
The principal Christian church of a bishop's diocese, containing the bishop's official seat or throne.
principal church of a bishop's diocese

Examples

The bishop held the Easter service in the grand cathedral.
The city is famous for its medieval cathedral that dominates the skyline.
Thousands of visitors tour the cathedral each year to admire its architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - a building used for Christian worship

Etymology

From Latin 'cathedra' (seat, chair), referring to the bishop's chair.

3. NOUN

/ kəˈθiːdrəl / (AmE), / kəˈθiːdrəl / (BrE)
A large and important Christian church, often the principal church of a diocese, typically where a bishop has his official seat.
large and important Christian church

Examples

After the fire, restoration work began immediately on the historic cathedral in the center of town.
The cathedral was illuminated for the evening concert, attracting hundreds of locals and tourists.
Visitors flocked to admire the soaring arches of the city’s famous cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - a building for Christian religious activities

Etymology

From Old French *cathedrale*, from Medieval Latin *cathedrālis* (of a bishop's seat).

4. NOUN NEW

/ kəˈθiːdrəl / (AmE), / kəˈθiːdrəl / (BrE)
A centralized, top-down development or organizational model—especially in software engineering—characterized by careful planning, restricted contributors, and tightly controlled releases.
centralized, top-down development model

Examples

The company favored a cathedral release process, where a small team approved every feature before launch.
Open-source contributors criticized the project's cathedral approach for slowing innovation and excluding outside feedback.
In his lecture, the architect contrasted the cathedral model with the bazaar model to explain governance differences.

Synset Examples

The company favored a cathedral release process, where a small team approved every feature before launch.
Open-source contributors criticized the project's cathedral approach for slowing innovation and excluding outside feedback.
In his lecture, the architect contrasted the cathedral model with the bazaar model to explain governance differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centralized - organized so that decision-making is concentrated within a small group or location
  • Antonym: bazaar (model) - an open, decentralized development model emphasizing many contributors and rapid iterative change

Etymology

Metaphorical use popularized by Eric S. Raymond's 1997 essay "The Cathedral and the Bazaar"; ultimately from Latin cathedra "seat".
