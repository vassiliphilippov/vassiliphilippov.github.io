snake-head

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkˌhɛd / (BrE)
A showy perennial plant of marshlands in eastern and central North America, with waxy lance-shaped leaves and flowers that are creamy white below and pale pink to deep purple above.
showy marshland plant native to North America

Examples

Gardeners appreciate the striking flowers of the snake-head for ornamental water gardens.
The snake-head thrives in moist, marshy environments across much of eastern North America.
The wetland was full of blooming snake-heads in early summer, adding splashes of color to the landscape.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsneɪkˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkˌhɛd / (BrE)
(informal) a person who illegally arranges for other people to be smuggled across borders, especially the leader of an organized smuggling group.
illegal people smuggler (informal)
informal

Examples

Police arrested the alleged snake-head who had organized dozens of illegal crossings last year.
Journalists reported that the gang's snake-head negotiated payments and travel documents for migrants.
Survivors described how smugglers tricked them and handed them over to ruthless snake-heads.
