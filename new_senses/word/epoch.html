epoch

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.pɑk / (AmE), / ˈiː.pɒk / (BrE)
(Astronomy) An arbitrarily fixed date used as a reference point for calculating or recording the positions of celestial bodies.
reference date in astronomy

Examples

Astronomers use a standard epoch to ensure data consistency across observations.
Celestial coordinates must be corrected according to the epoch of observation.
The star's position was measured at the epoch of January 1, 2000.

Etymology

From Greek *epokhē* (a stopping point, fixed point in time), via French *époque*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiˌpɑk, ˈɛˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈiː.pɒk, ˈɛp.ɒk / (BrE)
A particular period of time in history or a person's life, often marked by notable events or characteristics.
notable period in history or life

Examples

During the Victorian epoch, society underwent massive change.
Scientists debate when the current geological epoch, the Anthropocene, truly began.
The invention of the internet marked a new epoch in human communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: era - a major division of time with distinguishing characteristics
  • Synonym: age - a distinct period in history
  • Degree Lower: moment - a very short period of time

Etymology

From Greek 'epokhē' (a pause, fixed point of time), via Latin 'epocha'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːpɑk, ˈɛpək / (AmE), / ˈiːpɒk, ˈɛpək / (BrE)
a division of geological time, smaller than a period and composed of several ages
division of geological time

Examples

Many fossils found in this layer date back to the Jurassic epoch.
Scientists debate when the current geological epoch should be considered to have started.
The Paleocene is an epoch that marks the beginning of the Cenozoic Era.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: era - a major division of geological time, larger than a period
  • Degree Higher: period - a unit of geological time, larger than an epoch and smaller than an era
  • Degree Lower: age - a unit of geological time, smaller than an epoch

Etymology

From Greek ēpochē (a pause, fixed point in time).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈiː.pɑk / (AmE), / ˈiː.pɒk / (BrE)
(in machine learning) one complete pass through the entire training dataset, during which model parameters may be updated.
one training pass in machine learning

Examples

During training, the model completed its first epoch, and the engineer checked initial loss curves carefully.
They scheduled fifty epochs to ensure the network converged before fine-tuning the hyperparameters on the validation set.
If each epoch processes millions of images, training can take several hours or even days on standard GPUs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - a single traversal of the training data; often used interchangeably with 'epoch' in ML contexts
  • Synonym: iteration - one step or update in a training loop (often refers to a batch update); sometimes confused with an epoch
