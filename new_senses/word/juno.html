Juno

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnəʊ / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the queen of the gods and protector of marriage; wife and sister of Jupiter, corresponding to the Greek goddess Hera.
queen of the Roman gods; goddess of marriage

Examples

In ancient Roman legends, Juno was honored as the protector of women and marriage.
Mythology often depicts Juno as both powerful and sometimes jealous of Jupiter's other relationships.
The temple dedicated to Juno stood atop one of Rome's seven hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hera - Queen of the Greek gods; equivalent goddess in Greek mythology
  • Degree Higher: Jupiter - King of the Roman gods; husband of Juno

Etymology

From Latin Juno, the Roman goddess.

2. PROPN NEW

/ \u02c8d\u0292u\u02d0no\u028a / (AmE), / \u02c8d\u0292u\u02d0n\u0259\u028a / (BrE)
A NASA robotic spacecraft launched in 2011 to study the planet Jupiter, including its atmosphere, magnetic field, and interior structure.
NASA spacecraft studying Jupiter

Examples

NASA's Juno spacecraft entered orbit around Jupiter after a long interplanetary journey.
Scientists used data from Juno to study Jupiter's magnetic field and atmospheric composition.
During the mission, engineers adjusted Juno's orbit to improve the probe's observations of the poles.
