recovered

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkʌvərd / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvəd / (BrE)
having returned to a normal state of health after an illness or injury
free from illness or injury

Examples

After weeks in the hospital, she was finally recovered and able to return home.
Most people who catch the virus eventually become fully recovered within a few weeks.
The recovered patient expressed gratitude to the medical staff for their care.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: healed - restored to health or normal condition
  • Antonym: sick - affected by illness or disease

Etymology

Past participle of "recover" from Old French *recovrer* (to recover), from Latin *recuperare* (to regain).

/ rɪˈkʌvɚd / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvəd / (BrE)

Examples

Authorities displayed the recovered artwork, which had been missing for decades.
The recovered documents provided crucial evidence for the investigation.

3. ADJ NEW

/ rɪˈkʌvərd / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvəd / (BrE)
Describing materials or products that have been reclaimed, salvaged, or recovered from waste or previous use for reuse rather than newly manufactured.
reclaimed or salvaged material

Examples

The cafe uses recovered wood for its tables to reduce waste and lower costs.
They built the bench from recovered timber salvaged from old barns and pallets.
The manufacturer labels items made with recovered plastic to show sustainable sourcing.
