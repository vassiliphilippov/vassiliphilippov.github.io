dita

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːtə / (AmE), / ˈdiːtə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree of eastern Asia and the Philippines, known for its large leathery leaves, small green-white flowers in clustered arrangements, and bark formerly used in medicine
a tropical Asian evergreen tree

Examples

In the Philippines, the dita is often found along forest edges and roadsides.
The dita tree's bark was once widely used in traditional medicine.
The broad leaves of the dita provide excellent shade in tropical gardens.

Etymology

From Tagalog *dita*, referring to the tree Alstonia scholaris.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdiːtə / (AmE), / ˈdiːtə / (BrE)
(computing) an XML-based architecture for topic-based, structured authoring and publishing of technical documentation, used to create reusable content modules.
XML standard for technical documentation

Examples

Many software companies adopt DITA to standardize their help documentation and technical manuals.
She learned to write modular topics in DITA so content could be reused across multiple guides.
The documentation team converted the legacy files into DITA format for easier translation and publishing.
