4

1. NUM

/ fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
the number equal to three plus one; the cardinal number four
the number four

Examples

Jen has 4 brothers and no sisters.
The recipe calls for 4 eggs, so make sure you have enough.
We will meet again at 4 o'clock this afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three - the cardinal number that comes before four
  • Degree Higher: five - the cardinal number that comes after four

Etymology

From Old English feower; related to Proto-Germanic *fedwōr, Latin quattuor, and Greek tettara.
