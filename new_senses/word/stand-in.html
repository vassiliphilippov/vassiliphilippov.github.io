stand-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænd ɪn / (AmE), / ˈstænd ɪn / (BrE)
a person who takes someone else's place, especially in situations that are dangerous, difficult, or require an understudy
person who temporarily substitutes for another

Examples

The lead singer's stand-in impressed everyone during the concert.
The movie used a stand-in for the dangerous action sequence.
When the teacher was ill, a stand-in led the class for the week.

Synset Examples

the star had a stand-in for dangerous scenes
we need extra employees for summer fill-ins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another
  • Synonym: understudy - a person who learns another's role in order to serve as a replacement

Etymology

Formed from the verb phrase 'stand in' (to substitute), first recorded in the 20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈstænd ɪn / (AmE), / ˈstænd ɪn / (BrE)
a non-human substitute used in software development or testing to take the place of a real component, service, or module (for example, a mock or stub).
software substitute used in testing

Examples

During testing, the team used a stand-in service to simulate the payment API.
The developer replaced the real database with several stand-ins to run unit tests quickly.
A lightweight stand-in returned canned responses when the external service was offline.

Synset Examples

During testing, the team used a stand-in service to simulate the payment API.
The developer replaced the real database with several stand-ins to run unit tests quickly.
A lightweight stand-in returned canned responses when the external service was offline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - a simulated object used in testing to mimic the behaviour of a real component
  • Synonym: stub - a simple replacement for a component that provides fixed responses for testing

Etymology

From the phrase 'stand in' (to substitute); extended in computing to mean a non-human substitute.
