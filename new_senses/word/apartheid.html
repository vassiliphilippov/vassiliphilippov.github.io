apartheid

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːr.taɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɑː.taɪd / (BrE)
a system or policy of segregation or discrimination on grounds of race, especially the former official policy in South Africa.
racial segregation policy (esp. South Africa)

Examples

Many international communities imposed sanctions to protest apartheid policies.
She studied the effects of apartheid on education and social structure.
The end of apartheid marked a new era of equality in South Africa.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'apartheid' (separateness), from 'apart' (separate) + '-heid' (hood, state).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u0259\u02c8p\u0251\u02d0r.ta\u026at / (AmE), / \u0259\u02c8p\u0251\u02d0.ta\u026ad / (BrE)
(figurative) a system or practice that enforces separation, exclusion, or deep inequality between groups in a non-racial domain (for example, digital access, housing, education, or services).
systemic non-racial segregation or exclusion

Examples

Critics argue that unequal internet access has become a form of apartheid in the twenty-first century.
The housing policy created economic apartheid between neighborhoods with vastly different schools and services.
Many activists warn that algorithmic decisions could lead to digital apartheid by locking out disadvantaged groups.
