contention

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
the act of competing with others for something such as a prize or position
the act of competing; rivalry

Examples

After months of fierce contention, the two companies finally settled their dispute.
Several candidates dropped out, leaving only two in serious contention for the leadership.
She remains in strong contention for the gold medal despite the setback.

Synset Examples

the teams were in fierce contention for first place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the act of competing against others
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, often a synonym in some contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'contentio', from 'contendere' (to strive, contend).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
an argument or heated disagreement between people or groups; a dispute in which there is strong opposition
heated argument or dispute

Examples

After hours of contention, the committee finally reached a decision.
The issue became a source of contention among the neighbors.
There was considerable contention over who should lead the new project.

Synset Examples

they were involved in a violent argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a verbal exchange expressing opposing opinions
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or clash
  • Antonym: agreement - a situation where parties have the same opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'contentio' (a striving, rivalry), from 'contendere' (to contend).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a statement or claim that someone puts forward as part of an argument or discussion
a claim made in an argument

Examples

Her main contention was that the law was unfair to small businesses.
It is my contention that better planning could have prevented the problem.
The defense's contention that the evidence was inadmissible was overruled by the judge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a statement or series of statements for or against something
  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Antonym: concession - a point in an argument that is acknowledged or yielded

Etymology

From Latin contentio (a contest, strife), from contendere (to stretch, strive, contend).

4. NOUN NEW

/ kənˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
(in computing) the state in which two or more processes, threads, or devices attempt to use the same limited resource at the same time, causing delays, reduced performance, or interference.
competition for computing resources

Examples

During peak load, disk contention caused the database queries to take twice as long.
Developers reduced thread contention by partitioning the work and using finer-grained locks.
Network contention between virtual machines led to packet loss during the high-traffic test.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resource contention - competition among processes, threads, or devices for a shared computing resource
  • Synonym: bottleneck - a limiting point or congestion that reduces system performance

Etymology

From Latin 'contentio' (a striving, contest), from 'contendere' (to strive, contend).
