theatre

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiət̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
A region or area where active military operations are taking place.
area of active military operations

Examples

He spent two years in the European theatre during World War II.
Medical staff were urgently needed in the conflict theatre to treat the wounded.
New strategies were deployed across the Pacific theatre.

Synset Examples

the army was in the field awaiting action
he served in the Vietnam theater for three years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battlefield - the site of a battle, especially land used for fighting

Etymology

From Greek 'theatron' (place for viewing), extended in military use to mean the 'scene' or region of operations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
the art or profession of writing, producing, and performing plays in front of an audience
art of writing and producing plays

Examples

He considers theatre to be the most expressive of the arts.
Modern theatre often explores complex social issues through performance.
She has studied theatre for many years and hopes to become a playwright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drama - plays as a literary or artistic genre
  • Antonym: cinema - the art of making motion pictures

Etymology

From Greek *theatron* (a place for viewing), via Old French *theatre* and Latin *theatrum*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07019235-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈθiːətər / (AmE), / ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
A building or room where plays, shows, or movies are presented to an audience.
building for plays or shows

Examples

After the show, people gathered outside the theatre to discuss the performance.
The new theatre downtown will open next month with a popular musical.
We arrived early to get good seats at the theatre.

Synset Examples

the house was full

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinema - a building where movies are shown
  • Synonym: auditorium - a large room or hall where an audience gathers for performances or presentations

Etymology

From Greek theatron (a place for viewing), via Old French theatre and Latin theatrum.

A virtual or online venue where performances, broadcasts, or live events are presented to audiences over the internet rather than in a physical building.
online or virtual performance venue

Examples

Audiences watched the premiere in a virtual theatre hosted by the arts centre's website.
During the festival, independent companies streamed plays into an online theatre for ticket holders.
She bought a season pass to a digital theatre that screens new international productions monthly.
