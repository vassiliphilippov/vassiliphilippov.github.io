grandchild

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrændˌtʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈɡræn(d)tʃaɪld / (BrE)
a child of one's son or daughter; a son or daughter of one's own child
a child of your son or daughter

Examples

Each grandchild received a special gift on their birthday.
My grandchild drew me a picture and mailed it to me.
She was thrilled to meet her new grandchild for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grandparent - the parent of one's father or mother
  • Degree Higher: great-grandchild - a child of one's grandson or granddaughter

Etymology

Formed from 'grand-' (as in grandparent) and 'child', modelled after Latin 'nepos' (descendant, grandchild).
