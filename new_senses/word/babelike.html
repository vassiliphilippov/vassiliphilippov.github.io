2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈbeɪ.bi.laɪk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi.laɪk / (BrE)
Resembling the chaotic mixture of languages and noise associated with the biblical Babel; noisy and confusing because many languages or voices are present.
noisily multilingual; chaotic

Examples

The crowded market was babelike, traders shouting in half a dozen languages under the hot sun.
Conference calls became babelike after participants from five countries joined without a single common language.
The novel's final chapter portrayed a babelike city square where meaning dissolved into noise and laughter.

Synset Examples

The crowded market was babelike, traders shouting in half a dozen languages under the hot sun.
Conference calls became babelike after participants from five countries joined without a single common language.
The novel's final chapter portrayed a babelike city square where meaning dissolved into noise and laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babel - a scene of noisy confusion, especially of many languages or voices
  • Synonym: multilingual - involving or using several languages, often leading to a mix of voices

Etymology

From Babel (the biblical Tower of Babel) + -ike (like); literally 'like Babel'.
