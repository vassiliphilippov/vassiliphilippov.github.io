to entrain

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈtreɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtreɪn / (BrE)
to involve or bring (someone or something) into participation or alignment with a process or activity
involve or align with

Examples

It takes time to entrain new staff into the company culture.
The manager worked hard to entrain the various departments into the new workflow.
The team hopes to entrain everyone in their new initiative.

Synset Examples

They will entrain the volunteers in the upcoming project.
The new employees will entrain with the veteran staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involve - to include or engage as a necessary part
  • Synonym: to engage - to participate or become involved in

Etymology

From French *entraîner* meaning 'to draw in, drag along'.

2. VERB NEW

/ ənˈtreɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtreɪn / (BrE)
(in neuroscience, music, and related sciences) to synchronize a biological or physical rhythm or oscillation with an external rhythm or stimulus; to cause or become phase-locked.
synchronize with an external rhythm

Examples

The brain's neurons entrain to the steady rhythm of the metronome during the experiment.
By the second chorus the choir had entrained to the conductor's subtle changes in tempo.
Shift workers often use timed light exposure to entrain their circadian rhythms and improve sleep.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronize - to cause events or processes to happen at the same time or rate
  • Synonym: phase-lock - to fix the phase relationship between two oscillations so they operate in step
