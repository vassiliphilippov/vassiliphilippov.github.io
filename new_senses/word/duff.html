duff

1. NOUN

/ dʌf / (AmE), / dʌf / (BrE)
a steamed or boiled pudding made with stiff flour and usually containing dried fruit such as currants, raisins, or citron
steamed or boiled fruit pudding

Examples

After a hearty meal, the guests enjoyed slices of plum duff with their tea.
She served a traditional duff with custard for dessert on Christmas Day.
The recipe for raisin duff has been handed down for generations in my family.

Etymology

Possibly a dialectal variant of 'dough', referring to the thick texture of the pudding.

2. NOUN NEW

/ dʌf / (AmE), / dʌf / (BrE)
slang (offensive) an insulting term for a person considered the least attractive or least desirable member of a friend group; originally from the acronym 'Designated Ugly Fat Friend'.
slang: insulting label for least attractive friend
slang informal offensive

Examples

She dreaded being the duff in her circle after the makeover.
After the makeover, they stopped teasing him as a duff and he felt much happier.
Teen movies often portray one character as the duff who later becomes confident and popular.
