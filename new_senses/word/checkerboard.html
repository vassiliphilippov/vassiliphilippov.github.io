checkerboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛk.ɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛk.əˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a board marked with 64 squares of two alternating colors, used for playing games such as checkers or chess
board with 64 alternating colored squares

Examples

After the game, they carefully put away the checkerboard and the pieces.
She bought a new checkerboard with red and black squares.
The pieces were neatly arranged on the checkerboard for their next match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chessboard - board for playing chess with 64 alternating colored squares

Etymology

From 'checker' (game pieces, or a pattern of squares) + 'board'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəbɔːd / (BrE)

Examples

The black and red squares of the checkerboard made it easy to follow the moves.
They bought a new checkerboard for their family game nights.
