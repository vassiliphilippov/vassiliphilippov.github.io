goofproof

1. VERB

/ ˈɡuːfˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈɡuːfˌpruːf / (BrE)
To make (a device, process, etc.) resistant to mistakes or misuse by users; to render foolproof.
make resistant to human error

Examples

The company is trying to goofproof its application so even beginners can use it safely.
They hired an expert to goofproof the user interface before launch.
You should goofproof your passwords to prevent accidental lockouts.

Synset Examples

foolproof this appliance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolproof - make resistant to error or misuse

Etymology

Formed from 'goof' (slang for mistake or foolish person) and 'proof' (resistant to). Modeled after 'foolproof'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈɡuːfˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈɡuːfˌpruːf / (BrE)
Deliberately designed so that it is difficult or impossible for users to make mistakes when using it; foolproof.
resistant to user error
informal

Examples

The new interface is goofproof, so even first-time users navigate easily.
They bought a goofproof coffee maker that shuts off automatically when the pot is empty.
Designers aim for goofproof systems to reduce training time and user errors in hospitals.
