branded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrændɪd / (BrE)
(of goods) marked or labeled with a distinctive name or symbol, usually indicating exclusive rights or trademark.
marked with a brand name or trademark

Examples

Many consumers prefer branded products because they trust the quality.
Some stores sell both branded and generic items to cater to different customers.
The market for branded clothing has grown rapidly in recent years.

Synset Examples

branded merchandise is that bearing a standard brand name

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbranded - not marked with a brand name or trademark
  • Synonym: brand-name - having or relating to a recognized brand name

Etymology

From 'brand' (trademark or name) + -ed (past participle suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrændɪd / (BrE)
marked with a brand, especially by burning or stamping to indicate ownership or disgrace
marked with a brand

Examples

After the trial, he felt like a branded criminal in his own community.
The rancher kept his branded cattle in a separate pasture to prevent theft.
The stolen horses were quickly identified by their branded flanks.

Synset Examples

branded cattle
branded criminal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbranded - not marked with a brand

Etymology

Past participle of 'brand', from Old English *brand* (fire, torch, sword).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01484709-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ NEW

/ ˈbrændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrændɪd / (BrE)
Of content or media: created, funded, or endorsed by a commercial brand, often designed to promote the brand while resembling editorial material.
sponsored or brand-produced content

Examples

The documentary felt like branded content, with product placement throughout the episode.
Our marketing team created a branded series to showcase the new sustainable product line online.
Many publishers accept branded posts that look like editorial articles but disclose sponsorship.

Synset Examples

The documentary felt like branded content, with product placement throughout the episode.
Our marketing team created a branded series to showcase the new sustainable product line online.
Many publishers accept branded posts that look like editorial articles but disclose sponsorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponsored - paid for or supported financially, often by a company or organization
  • Synonym: advertorial - an advertisement presented in the form of editorial content

Etymology

From noun 'brand' + past-participle suffix -ed; extended in modern marketing to mean content produced or sponsored by a brand.
