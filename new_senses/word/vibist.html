vibist

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪbɪst / (AmE), / ˈvaɪbɪst / (BrE)
a musician who plays the vibraphone
musician who plays vibraphone

Examples

Many famous vibists have contributed to the evolution of modern jazz music.
She decided to become a vibist after hearing the unique sound of the instrument at a concert.
The jazz band featured a talented vibist who stole the show with his solo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibraphonist - a musician who plays the vibraphone

Etymology

Formed from 'vibes' (short for vibraphone) + '-ist' (person who practices or is concerned with something).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvaɪbɪst / (AmE), / ˈvaɪbɪst / (BrE)
informal (slang) a person who creates, curates, or projects a particular atmosphere, feeling, or mood (a 'vibe'), especially at events or online.
person who creates a vibe
informal slang

Examples

The festival's headliner was accompanied by a known vibist who curated the crowd's energy all night.
She's a real vibist, always rearranging lights and music to create the perfect party mood.
On social media, the influencer built a following by being a relaxed vibist in every video.
