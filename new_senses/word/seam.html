seam

1. VERB

/ sim / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
to join, stitch, or fasten together using a seam, especially in sewing or construction
to join with a seam

Examples

After all the panels were cut, they started seaming the sections together.
The tailor expertly seamed the edges of the fabric to create a flawless finish.
You need to seam the sleeves to the body before continuing with the dress.

Synset Examples

seam a dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - to join or fasten with thread using a needle

Etymology

From Old English sēamian (to sew together).

2. NOUN

/ siːm / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
A layer of ore or coal in the earth, thick enough to be extracted profitably.
layer of ore or coal for mining

Examples

After years of searching, geologists finally identified a promising iron ore seam.
The company invested in equipment to extract the gold seam efficiently.
The miners discovered a rich seam of coal deep underground.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - a distinct layer of mineral embedded in rock

3. NOUN

/ siːm / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
a line where two pieces of material, such as fabric or metal, are joined together
a joint where two pieces are joined

Examples

A small hole appeared along the seam of his trousers.
The metal sheets were connected by a strong seam to prevent leaks.
The tailor stitched the seam carefully to make it invisible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - a point or line where two things are joined

Etymology

From Old English 'seam', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'zoom' and German 'Saum'.

4. NOUN NEW

/ siːm / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
(in digital image processing) a connected path of pixels, usually of low energy, identified by seam-carving algorithms and removed or duplicated to change an image's dimensions while preserving important content.
pixel path used in content-aware resizing

Examples

The editor used a content-aware tool to remove a distracting seam across the horizon.
During resizing, the algorithm computes the lowest-energy seam and removes it to preserve details.
Photographers sometimes duplicate a vertical seam to widen an image without stretching important features.

Synset Examples

The editor used a content-aware tool to remove a distracting seam across the horizon.
During resizing, the algorithm computes the lowest-energy seam and removes it to preserve details.
Photographers sometimes duplicate a vertical seam to widen an image without stretching important features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seam carving - an image retargeting algorithm that removes or inserts seams to resize images while preserving important content
  • Synonym: content-aware resizing - a set of techniques for changing image dimensions by considering the importance of different pixels or regions

Etymology

Named from the term “seam carving” (Avidan & Shamir, 2007); metaphorical extension of 'seam' (a line or join) to a path of pixels in an image.
