tiddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪd.lə / (BrE)
(British, informal) A young child, especially a small one.
young child (British, informal)
british informal

Examples

She started teaching swimming lessons to tiddlers at the local pool.
The classroom was filled with excited tiddlers waiting for story time.
When I was a tiddler, I loved playing in the garden all afternoon.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8t\u026ad.l\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8t\u026ad.l\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing, especially in the context of TiddlyWiki) a small, self-contained piece of content stored as an editable item in a TiddlyWiki; it may contain text, metadata, and links and can be moved or reused within the wiki.
TiddlyWiki content unit

Examples

The developer added a new tiddler to the TiddlyWiki to store a code snippet.
During the workshop, she showed how to tag and link multiple tiddlers for better organization.
He exported the entire tiddler as JSON so others could import the note into their wiki.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a short piece of written information
  • Synonym: wiki page - a page or entry in a wiki, containing editable content and links

Etymology

Named after TiddlyWiki (a personal wiki application); the software's author coined 'tiddler' for each small, reusable wiki item.
