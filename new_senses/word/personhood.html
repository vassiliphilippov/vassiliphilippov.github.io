personhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈpɜːs(ə)nhʊd / (BrE)
the state or quality of being an individual person, especially with distinct identity and rights
state of being a person

Examples

Many legal debates center around the issue of fetal personhood and its implications.
Modern philosophy often explores what constitutes personhood beyond biological existence.
She struggled with questions of personhood while finding her place in society.

Synset Examples

finding her own personhood as a campus activist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identity - the fact of being who or what a person is

Etymology

Formed from 'person' + '-hood', denoting state or condition.
