thumbnail

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈθʌm.neɪl / (BrE)
the nail on the thumb of the hand
the nail of the thumb

Examples

He trimmed his thumbnail shorter than the others.
She accidentally broke her thumbnail while opening the can.
The painter had a streak of blue paint under his thumbnail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fingernail - the nail on any finger
  • Antonym: toenail - the nail on a toe

Etymology

From 'thumb' + 'nail', referring to the nail on the thumb.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈθʌm.neɪl / (AmE), / ˈθʌm.neɪl / (BrE)
a small, reduced-size image used to represent or preview a larger image, video, or file, typically used on websites, in galleries, or file browsers.
small preview image

Examples

The webpage displayed a thumbnail of each photo to speed up loading.
She clicked the thumbnail to view the high-resolution version of the landscape photograph.
The video editor automatically generated thumbnails to help users find the right clip quickly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preview - a short or small view of something meant to show or sample the full content
  • Synonym: icon - a small graphical representation used to represent a file, application, or function

Etymology

Originally from 'thumb' + 'nail' (the nail of the thumb); later used metaphorically in computing to mean a small preview image (late 20th century).
