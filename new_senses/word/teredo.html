Teredo

1. NOUN

/ təˈriːdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈriːdəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve molluscs in the family Teredinidae, commonly known as shipworms, that bore into and destroy submerged wood.
genus of wood-boring marine molluscs (shipworms)

Examples

Researchers studied the destructive habits of Teredo species in tropical waters.
Shipbuilders must protect their vessels from the attacks of Teredo to prevent severe wood damage.
The wooden hull of the old ship was damaged by Teredo infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - a common name for species in the genus Teredo, marine wood-boring molluscs

Etymology

From Latin *terēdō* (woodworm), ultimately from Greek *terēdōn*, meaning 'a gnawing worm'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ təˈriːdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈriːdəʊ / (BrE)
A networking protocol that provides IPv6 connectivity by encapsulating IPv6 packets inside UDP/IPv4 packets so they can traverse NATs and reach IPv6 endpoints.
IPv6-over-UDP tunneling protocol

Examples

Windows automatically configures Teredo to provide IPv6 connectivity when native IPv6 is unavailable.
When their router blocked IPv6, the system fell back to Teredo for basic internet access.
Many network tools can detect active Teredo tunnels and report them to the administrator.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teredo tunneling - the same tunneling method that encapsulates IPv6 in UDP to traverse NATs
  • Synonym: IPv6 transition technology - a class of methods (including Teredo) used to provide IPv6 connectivity over IPv4 networks

Etymology

Named after the shipworm genus Teredo, because the protocol 'tunnels' through NATs much as the mollusc bores through wood.
