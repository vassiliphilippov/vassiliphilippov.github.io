neurolysin

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈlaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈlaɪsɪn / (BrE)
a toxin that specifically affects neural tissues
toxin affecting neural tissues
medical

Examples

Exposure to the neurolysin caused rapid paralysis in laboratory mice.
Scientists are studying the effects of neurolysin on the nervous system.
Some snake venoms contain neurolysin that can disrupt neural communication.

Etymology

From 'neuro-' (relating to nerves) and Greek 'lysis' (loosening, dissolution).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccn\u028aro\u028a\u02c8la\u026as\u026an / (AmE), / \u02ccnj\u028a\u0259r\u0259\u028a\u02c8la\u026as\u026an / (BrE)
(in biochemistry and neuroscience) a zinc-dependent metallopeptidase found in the brain that cleaves or processes neuropeptides, helping regulate communication between nerve cells.
brain enzyme that processes neuropeptides
medical

Examples

Researchers have shown that neurolysin modulates neuropeptide signaling in the hippocampus of adult mice.
The study measured how neurolysin activity changed during memory formation after the learning task.
Inhibiting neurolysin increased peptide levels and altered synaptic transmission in cultured neurons.
