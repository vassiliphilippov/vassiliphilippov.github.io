ricer

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsər / (AmE), / ˈraɪsə / (BrE)
a kitchen utensil used to press cooked foods, especially potatoes, through small holes to produce a rice-like texture
kitchen tool for making foods rice-like

Examples

A ricer can also be handy for making homemade baby food.
After boiling the vegetables, he pushed them through the ricer for a finer texture.
She used the ricer to prepare smooth mashed potatoes for the family dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masher - a kitchen utensil for mashing cooked food

Etymology

From 'rice' (verb: to press through a ricer) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈraɪsər / (AmE), / ˈraɪsə / (BrE)
informal, derogatory: a person who adds large, attention-getting, usually cosmetic modifications to a car to make it look faster, rather than improving its actual performance.
pejorative for flashy car modifier
slang informal offensive

Examples

The online forum mocked the ricer who rolled up with a neon spoiler and chrome decals.
At the meet, older enthusiasts avoided the ricer whose engine sounded weak but the paint was loud.
He was labeled a ricer after adding fake vents and oversized rims without improving the brakes.
