reconciler

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛk.ənˌsaɪ.lər / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ənˌsaɪ.lə / (BrE)
a person who seeks to bring about agreement or restore friendly relations between others
person who tries to bring peace

Examples

As a skilled reconciler, she helped the two countries reach a peaceful agreement.
He saw himself as a reconciler, always seeking to heal divisions within the group.
The community respected him as a reconciler during times of disagreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who acts as an intermediary to resolve a conflict
  • Antonym: agitator - a person who stirs up conflict or trouble

Etymology

From reconcile + -er. 'Reconcile' is from Latin reconciliare (to bring together again).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈrɛk.ənˌsaɪ.lər / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ənˌsaɪ.lə / (BrE)
(in computing/data integration) a software component or service that automatically identifies, matches, and resolves differences between records or datasets from multiple systems to make them consistent.
software that aligns records across systems

Examples

The data pipeline includes a reconciler that matches customer records between the CRM and billing system.
During nightly processing, the reconcilers failed to resolve duplicate entries, causing invoice errors for several clients.
Before deployment, engineers tested the reconciler on synthetic datasets to ensure accurate record alignment.
