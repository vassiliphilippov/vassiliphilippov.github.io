envelope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
a flat, usually rectangular paper or plastic container used to enclose a letter, document, or small object
paper container for letters or documents

Examples

A thick envelope arrived in the mail this morning containing the official documents.
Please write your address on the back of the envelope.
She carefully placed the invitation inside a envelope and sealed it.

Etymology

From French 'enveloppe', from 'envelopper' meaning 'to wrap up'.
