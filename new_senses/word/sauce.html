sauce

1. VERB

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
to add sauce or relish to food to enhance its flavor
to add sauce or relish to food

Examples

He carefully sauced the steak with a rich mushroom reduction before serving it.
The chef likes to sauce each plate right at the table for extra flair.

4. NOUN

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
A liquid or semi-liquid substance served with food to add flavor, moisture, or visual appeal.
flavorful food accompaniment

Examples

Could you pass me the hot sauce, please?
He poured tomato sauce over his spaghetti before eating.
The chef created a creamy mushroom sauce to complement the steak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravy - A sauce made from meat juices, often served with roast meat or potatoes
  • Synonym: condiment - A substance such as salt, mustard, or ketchup added to food for flavor

Etymology

From Old French 'sausse', from Latin 'salsa' (salted).
