creativity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkriːeɪˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌkriːeɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
The ability to use imagination or original ideas to create something; inventiveness.
ability to create or invent

Examples

Her creativity was evident in the unique way she solved problems.
Teachers often encourage creativity in the classroom through art and writing assignments.
The company relies on the creativity of its employees to stay ahead in the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty or action of forming new ideas
  • Synonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unusual
  • Synonym: invention - the action of inventing something new

Etymology

From Latin *creare* (to create) + English suffix *-ity* (state or quality).
