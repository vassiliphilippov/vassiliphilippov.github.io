digitiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtaɪzə / (BrE)
A device that converts analogue signals into digital signals.
device converting analogue to digital signals

Examples

The engineer used a digitiser to convert the old magnetic tapes into digital files.
This laboratory recently upgraded its digitiser to improve the quality of signal processing.
Without a high-resolution digitiser, the accuracy of the data collection would be compromised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitizer - alternative spelling (US) for 'digitiser', device converting analogue to digital signals

Etymology

From 'digitize' + '-er'; 'digitize' from Latin 'digitus', meaning 'finger' or 'number'.

A person who converts physical materials (documents, photographs, manuscripts, audio or video) into digital formats, often by scanning, photographing, or otherwise creating digital files and preparing them for storage or access.
person who converts materials to digital form

Examples

The library hired a digitiser to convert its archives into searchable digital copies.
Our team of digitisers scanned and tagged thousands of photographs for the museum's online collection.
After the flood, a volunteer digitiser rescued fragile manuscripts by creating high-resolution images.
