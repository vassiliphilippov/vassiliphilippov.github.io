padding

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpædɪŋ / (BrE)
soft or resilient material used to fill, give shape, protect, or add comfort to something
soft material for filling or protection

Examples

Boxers wear gloves with thick padding to protect their hands and opponents.
She added some padding to the seat for extra comfort during long drives.
The winter jacket contains extra padding to keep you warm in cold weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuffing - material used to fill cushions or soft objects
  • Synonym: lining - a layer of material on the inside surface for comfort or protection

Etymology

From 'pad' (cushion) + -ing (forming nouns).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpædɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpædɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing) extra bytes or bits added to data so that it meets a required length or alignment, for example in block ciphers, file formats, or network frames.
extra bytes added to data

Examples

In cryptography, proper padding prevents attackers from exploiting incomplete encryption blocks.
The file format requires four bytes of padding after the header to align the data.
Network packets include padding so the payload meets the minimum frame size for transmission.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pad - to add extra bytes, characters, or space to reach a required length

Etymology

From pad + -ing; a metaphorical extension of 'pad' (to add extra) to describe adding bytes or bits.
