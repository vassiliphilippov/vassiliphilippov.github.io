turtler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːtələr / (AmE), / ˈtɜːtlə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is hunting turtles
person who hunts turtles

Examples

As a turtler, he spent months at sea searching for new nesting sites.
The experienced turtler knew all the best spots to find sea turtles along the coast.
The old journal included stories about a turtler who supplied turtles to local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunter - person who hunts animals for a living or sport

Etymology

Formed from 'turtle' (the animal) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

A player (especially in video games) who adopts an extremely defensive 'turtle' strategy, avoiding risk and staying protected rather than attacking.
player using a defensive 'turtle' strategy
slang informal

Examples

During the final round, the turtler refused to push forward and waited for opponents' mistakes.
Commentators criticized the turtler for stalling the game instead of trying to win aggressively.
In ranked play, many players accuse a turtler of ruining the match by never leaving cover.
