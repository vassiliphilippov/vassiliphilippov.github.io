dislike

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈlaɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlaɪk / (BrE)
to have or feel a strong distaste or lack of fondness for someone or something
to feel distaste or aversion

Examples

Although I disliked the movie, my friends really enjoyed it.
Many children dislike eating vegetables at dinner.
She dislikes getting up early on weekends.

Synset Examples

I really dislike this salesman

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hate - to feel intense or passionate dislike
  • Antonym: like - to find something pleasant or agreeable

Etymology

From Old English 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'like' (to please).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01780648-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlaɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlaɪk / (BrE)
a feeling of distaste or mild hostility towards someone or something; a lack of approval.
feeling of distaste or mild hostility

Examples

His dislike for crowds makes him prefer quiet gatherings.
Over time, their initial dislike of each other faded into friendship.
She has a strong dislike of spicy food and avoids it whenever possible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: like - a feeling of approval or enjoyment
  • Degree Higher: hatred - intense or passionate dislike
  • Degree Higher: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Middle English 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'like'.

3. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlaɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlaɪk / (BrE)
A feeling of aversion or distaste toward someone or something; the state of not liking.
a feeling of aversion or distaste

Examples

His open dislike for the new policy was evident in the meeting.
My dislike of horror movies started in childhood.
She has a strong dislike of spicy food.

Synset Examples

my dislike of him was instinctive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Degree Higher: hatred - intense dislike or hate
  • Antonym: like - a positive feeling of liking
  • Degree Lower: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking

Etymology

From the Middle English 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'like', after Old English 'lician'.

Source: WordNet

4. VERB NEW

/ dɪsˈlaɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈlaɪk / (BrE)
(on the internet) to express disapproval of online content by clicking a website or app's ‘dislike’ or downvote control.
indicate disapproval online

Examples

During the livestream, viewers quickly disliked the clip after the host's offensive comment.
If you dislike a comment on the post, click the down-arrow to register your opinion.
Mass disliking of the video by bots affected the recommendation algorithm for weeks.
