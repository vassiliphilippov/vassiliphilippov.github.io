tilted

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɪltɪd / (BrE)
Not in a straight or upright position; sloping or slanted away from the true vertical or horizontal.
not straight; sloping or slanted

Examples

He noticed his glasses were tilted on his nose and adjusted them.
Several tombstones in the cemetery appeared tilted after the storm.
The old picture frame on the wall was slightly tilted to the right.

Synset Examples

the leaning tower of Pisa
the headstones were tilted

Related Terms

  • Antonym: level - in a position that is horizontal or flat; not sloping
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position; standing straight up
  • Synonym: slanted - at an angle; not straight or level

Etymology

From the verb 'tilt', which comes from Old English 'tyltan' (to fall over, totter).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈtɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɪltɪd / (BrE)
Informal (especially in gaming): emotionally upset, frustrated, or mentally unsettled, so that someone performs worse or makes mistakes.
emotionally upset; frustrated (gaming slang)
informal slang

Examples

After the referee's unfair call, he was tilted and made careless mistakes for the rest of the match.
She warned her teammates not to get tilted after a few bad rounds and to focus on the next play.
Many streamers take breaks when they start feeling tilted to avoid flaming in chat and losing control.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustrated - feeling annoyed or less effective because obstacles prevent success
  • Degree Higher: angry - feeling strong annoyance or displeasure
  • Antonym: calm - not mentally upset; composed

Etymology

From the verb 'tilt' (Old English 'tyltan'), used metaphorically in modern gaming to mean being thrown off balance emotionally.
