transformer

1. NOUN

/ trænsˈfɔrmɚ / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a device used to transfer electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction, changing the voltage of an alternating current
electrical device that changes AC voltage

Examples

Many rural areas use a step-up transformer to transmit electricity over long distances.
The electrician installed a new transformer to supply power to the building.
You should never touch a transformer without proper training, as it can be very dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: converter - a device that changes something from one form to another, especially voltage

Etymology

From the noun 'transform' meaning to change in form, plus the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ trænsˈfɔrmə / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a neural network architecture that uses self-attention to process sequences of data, enabling efficient parallel training and strong performance on many natural language tasks.
neural network architecture (NLP)

Examples

Researchers trained a transformer to translate text more accurately than older RNN models.
Modern chatbots use large transformers that can generate fluent replies from short prompts.
A transformer uses self-attention to decide which words matter most when understanding a sentence.

Synset Examples

Researchers trained a transformer to translate text more accurately than older RNN models.
Modern chatbots use large transformers that can generate fluent replies from short prompts.
A transformer uses self-attention to decide which words matter most when understanding a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neural network - a computational model used in machine learning; transformers are a specific type of neural network architecture
  • Synonym: self-attention - a mechanism that lets a model weigh different parts of an input sequence; central to transformers

Etymology

Named for its role transforming input representations; term introduced in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need".
