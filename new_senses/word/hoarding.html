hoarding

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a large board used for displaying advertisements in public places, typically outdoors; a billboard
large outdoor advertising signboard
british

Examples

A giant hoarding stood at the roadside, advertising the new movie.
In London, hoardings along busy streets display colorful advertisements.
The old shop was hidden behind a temporary hoarding covered with posters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: billboard - large outdoor advertising board (US term)

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a persistent difficulty discarding or parting with possessions, leading to excessive accumulation of items and clutter; a behaviour that can cause significant distress or impairment (often clinically referred to as hoarding disorder).
compulsive collecting and clutter
medical

Examples

Her hoarding of old newspapers and mail made the apartment unsafe and cluttered.
Doctors diagnosed his severe hoarding after they found piles of belongings blocking the hallway.
Community groups organized workshops to help residents overcome hoarding and reclaim usable living space.

Synset Examples

Her hoarding of old newspapers and mail made the apartment unsafe and cluttered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarding disorder - a clinical diagnosis for severe, persistent hoarding behaviour causing distress or impairment
  • Antonym: decluttering - the process of removing unnecessary items to reduce clutter; often an opposite approach to hoarding

Etymology

From Old English 'hord' (treasure), originally meaning a hidden store or accumulation.
