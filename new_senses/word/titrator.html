titrator

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈtreɪtər / (AmE), / taɪˈtreɪtə / (BrE)
an apparatus used to perform titrations by delivering measured amounts of a solution to react with another
apparatus for performing titrations
medical

Examples

After cleaning the titrator, she prepared a new standard solution for analysis.
Modern automatic titrators can increase the accuracy and speed of analytical tests.
The laboratory technician calibrated the titrator before starting the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burette - a glass tube with a tap at one end for delivering known volumes of a liquid in titrations

Etymology

From 'titrate' (to measure or analyze by titration) + '-or' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ taɪˈtreɪtər / (AmE), / taɪˈtreɪtə / (BrE)
a person (chemist or laboratory technician) who performs titration experiments to measure the concentration or properties of a solution.
person who performs titrations

Examples

The lab hired a senior titrator to determine the solution's acidity.
During training, the new titrator learned how to prepare standard solutions carefully each morning.
As the lead titrator, she recorded every titration reading to ensure accurate results.
