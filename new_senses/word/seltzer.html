seltzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlt.sər / (AmE), / ˈsɛlt.sə / (BrE)
Naturally effervescent mineral water, sometimes used to refer to artificially carbonated water.
naturally effervescent mineral water

Examples

Many people use seltzer as a refreshing alternative to soda.
She ordered a glass of seltzer to go with her meal.
The seltzer from that spring is famous for its natural bubbles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling water - water with dissolved carbon dioxide, forming bubbles

Etymology

From German 'Selterser Wasser', after the town of Selters in Germany, famous for its mineral springs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlt.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛlt.sə / (BrE)
a fizzy drink consisting of water that has been artificially carbonated with carbon dioxide
carbonated water beverage

Examples

I prefer to drink seltzer instead of sugary sodas.
Many people use seltzer as a mixer for cocktails.
She ordered a glass of seltzer with lemon to go with her meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling water - water that contains dissolved carbon dioxide, either naturally or artificially
  • Synonym: soda water - water into which carbon dioxide has been dissolved under pressure; another term for seltzer
  • Antonym: still water - water that does not contain bubbles of gas

Etymology

From German 'Selterser', after Selters, a town in Germany known for its natural mineral springs.
