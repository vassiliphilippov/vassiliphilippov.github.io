imitator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmɪˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who copies the words, actions, or behavior of another, often to resemble or mimic them
person who copies another

Examples

She became an expert imitator of celebrities, performing their mannerisms perfectly on stage.
The comedian is known as a brilliant imitator of famous politicians and movie stars.
The young artist was called an imitator because his style resembled that of Picasso.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimic - a person who imitates others, especially for entertainment
  • Antonym: original - a person or thing from which others are copied; not an imitator

Etymology

From Latin imitator (an imitator), from imitari (to imitate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪməˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who copies or assumes the appearance, manner, or style of another, often with the intent to deceive
person who copies another, often deceptively

Examples

She exposed the con artist as merely an imitator trying to pass himself off as a famous designer.
The actor played an imitator who tricked his way into high society.
The police soon realized that the thief was an imitator who had studied the victim's habits closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impersonator - a person who pretends to be another, often for entertainment or deception
  • Antonym: originator - a person who creates or invents something new

Etymology

From Latin imitator (a copyist), from imitari (to imitate).
