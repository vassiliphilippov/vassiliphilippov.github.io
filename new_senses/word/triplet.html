triplet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪplət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɪt / (BrE)
a set of three similar things considered as a single unit
set of three similar things

Examples

A triplet of matching vases stood proudly on the mantelpiece.
The necklace was decorated with a triplet of pearls in the center.
The scientist observed a triplet of galaxies clustered closely together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trio - a group of three people or things

Etymology

Formed from 'triple' (meaning 'threefold'), patterned after 'doublet' and 'quintet'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪplət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɪt / (BrE)
one of three children or animals born at the same birth to the same mother
one of three siblings born together

Examples

Each triplet had a distinctive personality, even though they looked alike.
She was delighted to learn she would be giving birth to triplet girls.
The hospital specializes in caring for triplet and other multiple births.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twin - one of two children born at the same birth
  • Degree Higher: quadruplet - one of four offspring born at the same time

Etymology

From triple (threefold) + -et (diminutive suffix), influenced by French 'triplet'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtrɪplət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplət / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a group of three related examples — an anchor, a positive example similar to the anchor, and a negative example dissimilar to the anchor — used to train models with triplet loss or other metric-learning objectives.
three-sample unit for metric learning

Examples

During training, the model was fed a triplet consisting of an anchor, positive, and negative example.
Researchers often mine hard triplet examples to improve embedding quality in retrieval tasks.
When training with triplets, careful selection of positives and negatives is essential for convergence.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triplet loss - a metric-learning loss function that trains models using triplets (anchor, positive, negative) to shape embeddings
  • Synonym: embedding - a learned vector representation of data items used for similarity and retrieval tasks

Etymology

Extended use of 'triplet' (a group of three); adopted in 21st-century machine-learning literature for the anchor/positive/negative training unit.
