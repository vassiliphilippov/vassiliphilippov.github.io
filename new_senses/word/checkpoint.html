checkpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place, especially at a border or security area, where travellers or vehicles are stopped for inspection and clearance
place for inspection, esp. border

Examples

He was stopped at the checkpoint for a routine passport inspection.
Soldiers guarded the checkpoint at the entrance to the city.
We waited in line at the checkpoint before crossing the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border crossing - a place where people or vehicles cross a country’s border

Etymology

From 'check' + 'point'; meaning originates from military and travel usage in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtʃɛkˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a saved state of a computer program, process, or machine-learning model that can be stored and later restored to resume work from that point.
saved state in computing

Examples

After each epoch, the experiment stored a checkpoint to resume training if interrupted.
The backup system creates a checkpoint every hour so we can restore the service quickly.
When the program crashed, loading the most recent checkpoint saved several hours of work.

Synset Examples
