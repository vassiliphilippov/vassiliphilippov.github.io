wipe

1. NOUN

/ waɪp / (AmE), / waɪp / (BrE)
an act of cleaning or drying something by rubbing its surface, typically with a cloth, hand, or tissue
act of rubbing or wiping

Examples

After finishing the meal, he used a napkin to give his mouth a careful wipe.
He took a wipe at his glasses to clear the smudges.
She gave the table a quick wipe with a damp cloth.

Synset Examples

he gave the hood a quick rub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rub - the act of moving something over a surface with pressure
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00125946-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ waɪp / (AmE), / waɪp / (BrE)
to clean or dry a surface by rubbing it, typically with a cloth, your hand, etc., often using a circular or back-and-forth motion
clean or dry by rubbing

Examples

After exercising, Julia wiped the sweat from her forehead with a towel.
Before starting a new drawing, he would wipe the chalkboard carefully.
She used a damp cloth to wipe the kitchen counter clean.

Synset Examples

wipe the blackboard
He passed his hands over the soft cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard, especially with a brush and water

Etymology

From Old English wīpian (to wipe, rub), related to Dutch wrijven (to rub).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB NEW

/ wa p / (AmE), / wa p / (BrE)
(in computing) to erase or sanitize all data on a storage device or media so that the data cannot be recovered.
erase or sanitize digital data

Examples

Before disposal, they wipe the disks to protect privacy and prevent data recovery.
The technician wiped the old laptop's drive using a secure tool before reinstalling the system.
Security guidelines recommend wiping any USB stick that has held sensitive files before sharing it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erase - to remove written, recorded, or stored information so it is no longer accessible
  • Synonym: delete - to remove data or files (often less securely than a full wipe)

Etymology

From Old English wīpian (to wipe, rub); later extended metaphorically in computing to mean erase or sanitize data from storage.
