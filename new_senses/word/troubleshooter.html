troubleshooter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to find and fix problems, especially in machinery, systems, or organizations
person who finds and fixes problems

Examples

As an experienced troubleshooter, she quickly identified the problem with the engine.
Every large organization relies on a reliable troubleshooter to handle urgent crises.
The company hired a troubleshooter to resolve ongoing IT issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixer - person who solves problems or handles difficult situations

Etymology

From 'trouble' and 'shoot', originally used for railroad maintenance workers in the early 1900s U.S.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌblˌʃuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌblˌʃuːtə / (BrE)
a person employed to identify and solve problems, especially mechanical or technical issues, within a system or organization
person who locates and fixes problems

Examples

As a skilled troubleshooter, she quickly diagnosed the computer network's failure.
Engineers called in a troubleshooter when the production line came to a sudden halt.
The company hired a troubleshooter to resolve persistent issues with their manufacturing equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problem solver - a person who finds solutions to problems
  • Synonym: repairman - a person who makes mechanical repairs

Etymology

Formed from 'trouble' + 'shooter', originally used in early 20th-century America for workers fixing telegraph and telephone lines.
