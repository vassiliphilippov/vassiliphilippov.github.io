bisulcate

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈsʌlkeɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈsʌlkeɪt / (BrE)
(of hooves) divided into two parts; split or cloven.
having hooves split into two parts

Examples

Many domesticated animals, such as cattle and sheep, have bisulcate hooves.
Researchers observed the tracks left by bisulcate animals in the mud.
The goat's bisulcate hooves help it balance on rocky surfaces.

Synset Examples

bisulcate hoof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloven - split or divided, especially referring to hooves

Etymology

From Latin bisulcatus, from bis ('twice, two') + sulcare ('to furrow').

2. ADJ NEW

/ baɪˈsʌlkeɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈsʌlkeɪt / (BrE)
(in biology) having two grooves or furrows (sulci); used of microscopic or anatomical structures such as pollen grains or spores.
having two grooves (biology)

Examples

Under the microscope, the botanist noted the bisulcate pollen grains had two clear furrows around their surfaces.
Fossilized seeds displayed a bisulcate pattern, suggesting the ancient plant's reproductive structures were similar to modern relatives.
The lab manual explains how to identify bisulcate spores by locating two longitudinal grooves under high magnification.
