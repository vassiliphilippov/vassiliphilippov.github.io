private

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.vət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.vət / (BrE)
concerning matters that are deeply personal and not intended to be shared with others.
personal, not to be shared

Examples

Please respect that these are private family issues and not for public discussion.
She was careful to keep her private life out of the news.
This diary contains many private thoughts and feelings.

Synset Examples

intimate correspondence
private family matters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: public - open or accessible to all people; not private
  • Synonym: personal - relating to one's private life, thoughts, or feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'privatus' (set apart, personal), from 'privare' (to separate, deprive).

Examples

6. ADJ NEW

/ ˈpraɪ.vət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.vət / (BrE)
(in computing) restricted so that a program element (for example a variable, method, or member) is accessible only within a specified class, object, or module and not from external code.
restricted access in programming

Examples

To hide the data, declare the member as private so it is inaccessible outside the class.
The developer changed the method to private after discovering it should not be part of the API.
Many languages enforce private visibility so unit tests cannot call internal helper functions directly.
