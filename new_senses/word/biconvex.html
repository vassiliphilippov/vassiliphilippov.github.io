biconvex

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈkɑnvɛks / (AmE), / baɪˈkɒnvɛks / (BrE)
having a surface that is convex on both sides, like the shape of a lens or lentil.
convex on both sides

Examples

Microscopes often use a biconvex glass to magnify tiny objects.
The biconvex lens focuses light rays to a single point.
The seed was biconvex in shape, resembling a small lentil.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: convex - having a surface that curves outward

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' meaning 'two' and 'convexus' meaning 'arched' or 'domed'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ baɪˈkɑnvɛks / (AmE), / baɪˈkɒnvɛks / (BrE)
(in mathematics, especially optimization) describing a function or optimization problem that is convex in each variable (or block of variables) separately when the other variables are held fixed.
convex in each variable separately (math)

Examples

The optimization problem was biconvex, so alternating minimization often finds a good solution.
They proved the function f(x,y) is biconvex for fixed y and for fixed x separately.
A biconvex cost model allows alternating optimization to converge quickly in many cases.
