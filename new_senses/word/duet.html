duet

1. NOUN

/ duˈɛt / (AmE), / djuˈɛt / (BrE)
a performance by two singers, musicians, or other performers together
performance by two people

Examples

Next on the program is a duet for violin and cello.
The pianists played a beautiful duet at the concert.
They won the competition with their stunning vocal duet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solo - performance by one person
  • Degree Higher: trio - performance by three people

Etymology

From Italian duetto, diminutive of duo (two).

2. NOUN

/ duˈɛt / (AmE), / djuːˈɛt / (BrE)
a dance performed by two people, especially in ballet, typically featuring a ballerina and a male dancer
dance for two people in ballet

Examples

The final act included a challenging duet that showcased their technical skills.
The highlight of the show was a beautiful duet between the lead dancers.
They rehearsed the duet for weeks to perfect their timing and expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pas de deux - a ballet dance for two people
  • Antonym: solo - a dance performed by one person
  • Degree Higher: trio - a dance or performance for three people

Etymology

From Italian 'duetto', diminutive of 'duo' (two).

3. NOUN

/ duˈɛt / (AmE), / djuːˈɛt / (BrE)
a musical composition written for two performers, especially singers or instrumentalists
musical piece for two performers

Examples

After months of practice, they finally performed the duet perfectly.
The composer wrote a beautiful duet for piano and violin.
Their romantic duet became the highlight of the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duo - two people performing together, especially in music
  • Antonym: solo - a musical performance or composition by a single performer
  • Degree Higher: trio - a musical composition for three performers

Etymology

From Italian 'duetto', diminutive of 'duo' (duet, pair).
