comet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmət / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪt / (BrE)
An object in space made mostly of ice and dust that moves around the sun in a highly elliptical orbit and often has a visible tail when near the sun.
icy space object with a tail

Examples

A bright tail followed the comet as it approached the sun.
Scientists studied the composition of the comet as it passed close to Earth.
The comet was visible in the night sky for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asteroid - a small rocky body orbiting the sun, typically without a tail

Etymology

From Latin 'comēta', from Greek 'komētēs' (long-haired), referring to the object's tail.

(computing, web development) a technique for delivering server-to-client updates by keeping an HTTP connection open (or repeatedly long-polling) so the server can push data to the client in real time.
server-push web technique (long-lived connection)

Examples

Many early chat apps used comet techniques to deliver messages in real time.
