inscription

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
words or letters that are written, engraved, or carved on a surface, such as a monument, coin, or building
words carved or engraved on a surface

Examples

She paused to read the inscription engraved inside the wedding ring.
The inscription on the ancient tablet was barely legible after centuries of erosion.
The old statue had a Latin inscription at its base explaining its origin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a design or text cut into a hard surface
  • Synonym: epitaph - inscription on a tombstone or monument in memory of the person buried there

Etymology

From Latin inscriptionem (a writing upon, inscription), from inscribere (to write on).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the act of writing, carving, or engraving words or letters, especially on a surface such as stone, metal, or wood
act of writing or engraving words

Examples

Ancient artisans specialized in the inscription of religious texts on stone tablets.
The inscription of names on the memorial took several weeks to complete.
The inscription of the winner’s name on the trophy has become a tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - the act or process of cutting designs or words on a hard surface
  • Synonym: carving - the act of shaping or cutting a solid material, often creating letters or designs

Etymology

From Latin inscriptio (a writing upon), from inscribere (to inscribe).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a short written message, especially one in a book, photograph, or musical work, often dedicating it to someone
dedicatory message in a book or similar item

Examples

She wrote a heartfelt inscription on the title page of the book she gave me.
The composer included a brief inscription at the beginning of the score, dedicating it to his mother.
The photograph had an inscription on the back, dedicating it to his best friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedication - a statement written to dedicate a work to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'inscriptio', from 'inscribere' (to write on).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
(in blockchain technology) a piece of digital data permanently recorded on a blockchain, typically attached to a specific smallest unit of cryptocurrency (a satoshi) so the content is verifiably on‑chain.
digital data recorded on a blockchain

Examples

The artist released a digital artwork as a inscription on the Bitcoin blockchain.
Collectors verified the piece by checking the inscription attached to the satoshi's transaction history.
Marketplaces now list inscription sales alongside NFTs, emphasizing permanent on-chain ownership.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: ordinal - a protocol that assigns unique identifiers to satoshis and is often used to create inscriptions

Etymology

Extension of the earlier sense 'inscription' (writing on) to digital data; from Latin inscriptio, from inscribere 'write on'.
