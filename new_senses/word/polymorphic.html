polymorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑliˈmɔrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
Existing in or assuming several different forms; having many distinct forms.
having or occurring in several forms

Examples

Certain minerals are polymorphic, existing in different crystal structures under varying conditions.
In programming, a polymorphic function can process different data types with the same interface.
The butterfly species is polymorphic, showing a variety of color patterns.

Synset Examples

man is both polymorphic and polytypic
a polymorphous god

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monomorphic - having only one form
  • Synonym: polymorphous - having or taking many forms

Etymology

From Greek 'polymorphos' (having many forms), from 'poly-' (many) + 'morphē' (form).

3. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of a species or population) occurring in several different forms or types, independently of sexual differences.
having multiple forms within a species

Examples

Some insects are polymorphic, with distinct forms adapted to different environmental conditions.
The butterfly population is polymorphic, displaying a range of color patterns within the same species.
The study demonstrated that the plant is highly polymorphic, contributing to its resilience.

Synset Examples

a polymorphic species

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monomorphic - having only one form; not exhibiting polymorphism
