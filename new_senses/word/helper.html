helper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpə / (BrE)
a person who assists others, especially by offering support or aid, sometimes involving financial help
person who helps others

Examples

As a helper at the school fundraiser, she organized the donations table.
His grandmother was always a generous helper in the local community.
The charity relies on many dedicated helpers to distribute food to those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps another, especially in an office or work setting
  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives financial help, especially to a charity or institution
  • Antonym: hindrance - a person or thing that makes it more difficult for someone to do something

Etymology

From Middle English 'helper', from help + -er.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpə / (BrE)
a person who assists or contributes to the fulfillment of a need or the completion of a task
a person who assists or helps

Examples

As a volunteer helper, she delivers meals to elderly neighbors every week.
The new teacher was grateful to have a classroom helper to assist with projects.
We hired an extra helper during the busy holiday season to manage customer orders.

Synset Examples

my invaluable assistant
they hired additional help to finish the work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone else do a job
  • Synonym: aide - a person whose job is to assist someone
  • Synonym: supporter - someone who provides help or encouragement

Etymology

From Middle English *helpere*, from Old English *helpere* ('one who helps').

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhɛlpər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpə / (BrE)
a small software component (such as a function or module) that provides auxiliary or commonly used functionality to support other code.
small supporting software component

Examples

The helper function formats dates and calculates time zones used across the application.
Developers created several helpers to validate input and simplify form submission logic.
When debugging, she imported a small helper module that generated mock data for tests.
