chthonian

1. ADJ

/ ˈθoʊniən / (AmE), / ˈkθəʊniən / (BrE)
(chiefly literary or mythological) Relating to or inhabiting the underworld or beneath the surface of the earth.
relating to the underworld; subterranean

Examples

Ancient rituals were sometimes held to honor chthonian gods associated with the earth.
In Greek mythology, Hades is a chthonian deity ruling the underworld.
The writer described the cave’s eerie silence as a chthonian presence lurking below.

Synset Examples

nether regions

Etymology

From Greek *khthonios* (in or under the earth), from *khthōn* (earth).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈθoʊniən / (AmE), / ˈkθəʊniən / (BrE)
(astronomy) Describing an exoplanet that is the exposed rocky core of a former gas giant, left after the planet's atmosphere has been removed or stripped away.
rocky remnant of a gas giant (astronomy)

Examples

Astronomers identified a chthonian planet that likely formed when its atmosphere was stripped away.
After intense stellar radiation, the gas giant's outer layers vanished, leaving a chthonian rocky core.
They studied the chthonian world to learn how atmospheric escape transforms giant planets into small, dense bodies.
