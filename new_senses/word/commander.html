commander

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmændɚ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
an officer of a rank in the air force, typically below a group captain or equivalent, who commands a unit or group
officer rank in the air force

Examples

As commander, he was responsible for the safety of his entire unit.
She aspired to become a commander in the air force one day.
The squadron's commander briefed the pilots before the mission.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: captain - an officer rank above commander in some air forces

Etymology

From Old French *commandeor*, from Late Latin *commandare* (to command).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmændɚ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
A person in charge of a military unit, especially an officer responsible for leading soldiers or sailors.
officer in charge of a military unit

Examples

As commander of the base, she was responsible for hundreds of soldiers.
The commander gave a rousing speech before the battle began.
The commander issued strict orders to ensure the safety of the unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - an officer ranking just below a major in the army or just below a colonel in the navy
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of another within an organization

Etymology

From Old French 'comandeor', from Late Latin 'commandare' (to command).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈmændər / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
a person in an official position of authority, especially in the military, who gives orders and directs others
person with authority to command, esp. in military

Examples

As commander of the unit, she was responsible for every operation.
The commander issued strict orders before the mission began.
The navy appointed a new commander to lead the fleet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinate - a person lower in rank or position
  • Synonym: captain - an officer ranking just below a major; often a commander of a company or ship

Etymology

From Old French 'commandeor', from 'commander' (to command), from Latin 'commandare'.

4. NOUN

/ kəˈmændər / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndə / (BrE)
a commissioned naval officer who ranks above a lieutenant commander and below a captain
naval officer rank above lieutenant commander

Examples

After twenty years of service, she was promoted to commander in the navy.
He hoped one day to reach the rank of commander like his father.
The commander briefed the crew before setting sail from the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: captain - a commissioned naval officer above the rank of commander
  • Degree Lower: lieutenant commander - a commissioned naval officer below the rank of commander

Etymology

From Latin 'commandare' (to entrust, to command), via Old French and Middle English.
