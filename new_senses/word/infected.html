infected

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
containing, affected by, or resulting from disease-causing organisms such as bacteria or viruses.
containing disease-causing organisms
medical

Examples

Doctors treated the infected tissue to prevent the spread of disease.
If food is stored improperly, it can become infected with harmful bacteria.
The wound became infected after it was exposed to dirty water.

Synset Examples

a septic sore throat
a septic environment
septic sewage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septic - containing or resulting from disease-causing organisms
  • Antonym: sterile - free from living germs or microorganisms

Etymology

Past participle of 'infect', from Latin 'infectus', meaning tainted or stained.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ɪnˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
contaminated, compromised, or damaged by malicious software (malware) such as computer viruses, worms, or ransomware.
contaminated by malware

Examples

After opening the attachment, his laptop became infected with ransomware that encrypted all personal files.
The technician warned that the removable drive was infected and should be scanned before connecting it.
During the incident response, engineers found multiple servers had been infected by a fast-propagating worm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromised - breached or made insecure, often by malware or an attacker
  • Antonym: clean - free of viruses or other malware; not infected
