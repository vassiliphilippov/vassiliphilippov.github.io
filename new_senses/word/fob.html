fob

1. NOUN

/ fɑb / (AmE), / fɒb / (BrE)
A short chain or ribbon attached to a pocket watch, used to secure it to a person's vest or waistband.
chain or ribbon for attaching pocket watch

Examples

He carefully tucked the fob of his pocket watch into his vest pocket.
The antique fob was made of gold and had an engraved family crest.
Victorian gentlemen often carried their watches on a chain or fob.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch chain - a chain for attaching a watch to clothing

Etymology

Probably from Low German *Fobke* (pocket or small case).

3. VERB

/ fɑb / (AmE), / fɒb / (BrE)
to deceive or trick (someone), especially by making them accept something false or less valuable
deceive or trick someone
archaic

Examples

She suspected he was trying to fob her with a false excuse.
The merchant tried to fob me off with a cheap imitation instead of the genuine article.
They attempted to fob the customer by promising a refund they never intended to give.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive someone, usually by trickery

Etymology

Probably from Old French *forbir* (to polish, embellish deceitfully).
