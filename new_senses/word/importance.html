importance

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɔrtəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtəns / (BrE)
The quality of being significant or worthy of attention or consideration.
quality of being significant or notable

Examples

Many people underestimate the importance of regular exercise for good health.
She realized the importance of spending time with her family.
The CEO stressed the importance of meeting the project deadline.

Synset Examples

the importance of a well-balanced diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significance - the quality of being worthy of attention; importance
  • Antonym: triviality - the quality of being trivial or unimportant

Etymology

From Old French importance, from Medieval Latin importantia (being important).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɔrtəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of having a high position, influence, or prominence in society or among a group
prominent status; high social position

Examples

She enjoyed her sudden importance among the guests at the banquet.
The ambassador's arrival was an event of great importance.
The company president is a person of considerable importance within the industry.

Synset Examples

a person of importance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prominence - the state of being important or famous
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From Old French importance, from Medieval Latin importantia, from Latin importare (to bring in, carry in).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɪmˈpɔrtəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtəns / (BrE)
(in data science, machine learning) a numeric measure or score indicating how much a feature or predictor contributes to a model's predictions or performance.
measure of a feature's contribution

Examples

The team's report highlighted the importance of age and income in the prediction model.
She examined variable importance scores to decide which features to include in deployment.
After training, the data scientist plotted importance values to explain the model to stakeholders.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - the effect that one variable or factor has on another; similar to the idea of contribution in models
  • Synonym: contribution - the part played by a factor in producing a result; often used to describe a feature's role in a model

Etymology

From Old French importance, from Medieval Latin importantia (being important).
