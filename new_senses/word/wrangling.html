wrangling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɹæŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɹæŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
a heated and often prolonged argument or dispute, especially over a particular issue or point
heated or prolonged dispute

Examples

After hours of wrangling, the committee finally reached a decision.
Despite the lengthy wrangling, no agreement was reached.
Much of the wrangling centered on how to divide the inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a disagreement, especially a verbal one
  • Synonym: bickering - petty and frequent arguing

Etymology

From verb 'wrangle', Middle English *wranglen* (to dispute noisily).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈræŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of cleaning, transforming, and organizing raw data into a structured form suitable for analysis or processing.
cleaning and organizing data

Examples

Before modeling, the team spent hours on wrangling the dataset to fix inconsistencies.
Good visualizations depend on careful wrangling of missing values and inconsistent column names.
She specialized in wrangling messy logs into clean tables for the analytics pipeline.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: data preprocessing - preparing raw data for further analysis or modeling, including normalization and encoding
  • Synonym: data munging - informal term for transforming and mapping raw data into a more usable format
