2. ADJ

/ ˈlɜrnɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd / (BrE)
(of behavior or responses) acquired by conditioning or experience rather than inherited or instinctive.
acquired through learning or experience

Examples

Many phobias are the result of learned behavior in response to traumatic events.
The experiment demonstrated that the rats could develop a learned avoidance of certain flavors.
Unlike innate reflexes, a learned response requires prior experience or training.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instinctive - arising naturally, not acquired through learning
