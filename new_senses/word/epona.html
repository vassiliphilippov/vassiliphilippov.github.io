Epona

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈpoʊnə / (AmE), / ɪˈpəʊnə / (BrE)
(in Celtic and Roman mythology) a goddess chiefly associated with horses, mules, and asses.
Celtic goddess of horses

Examples

Equestrians sometimes pay tribute to Epona for good fortune in their competitions.
Many ancient statues depict Epona being surrounded by horses and holding a cornucopia.
The festival honoring Epona was celebrated by both the Romans and the Celts.

Etymology

From Latin Epona, derived from Gaulish 'epos' meaning horse.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ɪˈpoʊnə / (AmE), / ɪˈpəʊnə / (BrE)
a fictional horse in Nintendo's The Legend of Zelda video game series that serves as the player character's mount.
Zelda video-game horse

Examples

In The Legend of Zelda, Link rode Epona across the fields to reach the distant village.
Players must sing to call Epona back after she runs off during the game's side quest.
Many fans felt emotional when Epona appeared again in the series' newest installment.
