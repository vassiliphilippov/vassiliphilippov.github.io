summons

1. NOUN

/ ˈsəmənz / (AmE), / ˈsʌmənz / (BrE)
an official order requiring someone to appear at a specified place and time, especially in court
official order to appear, often in court
legal

Examples

Failure to respond to the summons could result in a fine.
She received a summons for jury duty in the mail.
The court issued a summons requiring him to testify next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subpoena - a written order requiring someone to attend court as a witness

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *somons*, from Old French *somonce*, from *somondre* (to summon), from Latin *summonēre* (to remind).

/ ˈsʌmənz / (AmE), / ˈsʌmənz / (BrE)
an official order or request commanding someone to appear, especially in a law court or for a specific purpose
official order to appear

Examples

Ignoring the summons could result in legal consequences.
She received a summons to appear in court next week.
The teacher issued a summons for the students to meet after class.

3. VERB

/ ˈsʌmənz / (AmE), / ˈsʌmənz / (BrE)
to order someone to appear, especially in a court of law or in an official situation

legal

Examples

If you are summonsed to jury duty, you must attend.

From Anglo-French 'sumunser', alteration of Old French 'somondre', from Latin 'summonere' (to remind privately, summon).
