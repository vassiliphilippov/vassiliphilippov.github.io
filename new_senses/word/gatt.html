GATT

1. NOUN

/ ɡæt / (AmE), / ɡæt / (BrE)
an international agreement under the United Nations, created to promote trade by reducing tariffs and import quotas; also refers to the agency governing this agreement
UN treaty and agency promoting international trade

Examples

Many countries benefited from membership in the GATT before the founding of the World Trade Organization.
The GATT negotiations led to significant reductions in international trade barriers.
The principles established by GATT continue to influence modern trade agreements.

Etymology

Acronym of General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u0261\u00e6t / (AmE), / \u0261\u00e6t / (BrE)
(in Bluetooth Low Energy) the Generic Attribute Profile that defines how data is organized and exchanged using services, characteristics, and descriptors.
Bluetooth Low Energy profile

Examples

Smart home hubs use GATT services to expose sensor readings to connected smartphones.
During pairing, the app discovers the device's GATT characteristics to read battery level and settings.
Engineers tested a custom GATT implementation to ensure reliable notifications from the wearable sensor.
