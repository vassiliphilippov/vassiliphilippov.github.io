to deep-fry

1. VERB

/ diːpˈfraɪ / (AmE), / diːpˈfraɪ / (BrE)
to cook food by immersing it completely in hot fat or oil
cook food by immersing in hot fat or oil

Examples

It's important to deep-fry the chicken until it turns golden brown.
She has never deep-fried vegetables before but wanted to try something new.
They deep-fried the doughnuts to achieve a crispy texture.

Synset Examples

french-fry the potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fry - to cook in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan

2. VERB NEW

/ did0pc8fra6a / (AmE), / did0pc8fra6a / (BrE)
(internet slang) to edit an image or meme very heavily—increasing saturation, contrast, sharpening, and compression artifacts—to create an exaggerated, distorted effect.
heavily edit images/memes (internet slang)
informal slang

Examples

Internet users often deep-fry memes to heighten their absurd, surreal humor.
He deep-fried the photo until the colors and noise looked completely ridiculous.
During the livestream she started deep-frying audience screenshots for comedic effect.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: oversaturate - to increase color saturation beyond natural levels, often producing garish colors

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the cooking verb 'deep-fry' (to cook by immersing in oil), used to describe intense digital processing of images.
