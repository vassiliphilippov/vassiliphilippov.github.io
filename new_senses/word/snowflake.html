snowflake

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌfleɪk / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌfleɪk / (BrE)
A single small, delicate crystal of snow, often appearing in unique, intricate shapes as it falls from the sky.
a crystal of snow

Examples

A single snowflake landed on her glove and melted quickly.
Children tried to catch a snowflake on their tongues as they played outside.
Each snowflake is unique, with its own beautiful pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow - frozen precipitation falling as white flakes

Etymology

From 'snow' + 'flake', modeled after similar compounds in English.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsnoʊˌfleɪk / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌfleɪk / (BrE)
Informal and usually derogatory: a person regarded as overly sensitive, easily offended, or believing themselves uniquely special, especially in social or political debates.
overly sensitive person (slang)
slang informal offensive

Examples

Critics mocked the commenter as a snowflake for overreacting to a short joke online.
The article argued that calling people snowflakes often prevents honest discussion about real issues.
He refused to listen and labeled anyone who disagreed a snowflake, ending the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thin-skinned - easily hurt or offended by criticism
  • Synonym: sensitive - easily affected or upset by the actions or words of others
  • Antonym: thick-skinned - not easily upset or offended; the opposite of thin-skinned

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from literal 'snowflake' (fragile, unique); attested as slang from the late 20th–early 21st century.
