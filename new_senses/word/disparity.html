disparity

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsperəti / (AmE), / dɪˈspærəti / (BrE)
A great difference or inequality, especially one that is unfair or concerning status, opportunity, or treatment.
inequality or great difference

Examples

Efforts were made to reduce the disparity in educational opportunities.
The income disparity between rural and urban areas continues to widen.
There is a significant disparity in health care access among different communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inequality - state of not being equal
  • Antonym: equality - state of being equal

Etymology

From Late Latin disparitas (inequality), from Latin dispar (unequal).

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈspɛrəti / (AmE), / dɪˈspærəti / (BrE)
(in computer and stereo vision) the horizontal difference in position between corresponding points in a pair of stereo images; this shift is used to infer depth (distance) of objects.
horizontal shift between stereo image points

Examples

Researchers calculated the disparity between corresponding pixels to build a dense depth map of the scene.
The robot used stereo cameras and measured disparity to estimate how far the obstacle was.
Small disparity values indicate distant objects, while large disparities mean objects are nearer to the cameras.
