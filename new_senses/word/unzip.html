to unzip

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈzɪp / (AmE), / ʌnˈzɪp / (BrE)
to open something by undoing its zipper
open using a zipper

Examples

Could you please unzip your jacket before coming in?
He forgot to unzip the pocket and struggled to find his keys.
She quickly unzipped her backpack to grab her notebook.

Synset Examples

unzip the bag

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zip - to close with a zipper
  • Synonym: open - to move so as to no longer be closed
  • Antonym: close - to make something not open

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (expressing reversal) and 'zip' (short for 'zipper').

2. VERB NEW

/ 8cn8z6ap / (AmE), / 8cn8z6ap / (BrE)
(in computing) to decompress or extract the contents of a ZIP archive or other compressed file so individual files can be used.
extract files from ZIP archive

Examples

I had to unzip the downloaded folder before I could edit the documents.
She unzipped the archive and copied the images into the project's assets folder.
The installer automatically unzips the package and copies files to the installation folder.

Synset Examples

I had to unzip the downloaded folder before I could edit the documents.
She unzipped the archive and copied the images into the project's assets folder.
The installer automatically unzips the package and copies files to the installation folder.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zip - to compress files into a ZIP archive (opposite operation).
  • Synonym: extract - to take files out of an archive or container.
  • Synonym: decompress - to restore compressed data to its original form.

Etymology

From the prefix un- (reverse) + zip (to compress files into a ZIP archive).
