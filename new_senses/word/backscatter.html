backscatter

1. VERB

/ ˈbækˌskætər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌskætə / (BrE)
(of radiation) to be scattered by atoms of a medium so that some of it is deflected back toward the source.
to scatter radiation back toward its source

Examples

Scientists observed how X-rays backscattered after passing through different materials.
Some imaging techniques rely on measuring how particles backscatter within the sample.
The radar signal will backscatter when it strikes rough surfaces such as terrain or vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to cause to separate and go in different directions

Etymology

From 'back' + 'scatter', indicating scattering in the backward direction.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbækˌskætər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌskætə / (BrE)
(in email security) unsolicited bounce messages returned to forged or spoofed sender addresses when those addresses are used by spammers or automated systems.
unsolicited bounced emails

Examples

The security team investigated backscatter to locate compromised mail servers used by spammers.
Many ISPs now filter backscatter automatically to reduce the load on customer inboxes.
After the phishing campaign, company logs recorded massive backscatter coming from forged sender addresses.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'back' + 'scatter' to describe messages 'scattering' back to forged senders; used in computing/security contexts.
