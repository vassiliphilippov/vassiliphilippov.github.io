hoverboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌvɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈhʌvəbɔːd / (BrE)
A board-like device, similar to a skateboard but without wheels, designed to float or hover above the ground, often depicted in science fiction and popular culture.
levitating board for personal transport

Examples

He dreamed of riding a hoverboard just like the one in his favorite movie.
In the film, the character glides through the city on a hoverboard.
Many inventors are trying to create a real hoverboard that can float above the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skateboard - a board mounted on wheels used for riding and performing tricks

Etymology

Formed from 'hover' (to stay in one place in the air) + 'board' (as in skateboard or surfboard).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhʌvərbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈhʌvəbɔːd / (BrE)
A self-balancing, battery-powered two-wheeled personal transporter that a rider stands on to move short distances.
self-balancing two-wheeled scooter

Examples

She rode a battery-powered, self-balancing hoverboard across campus to get to her morning class.
After the purchase, the store registered the warranty for both hoverboards and scooters sold that day.
Local authorities banned electric hoverboard use on sidewalks because of safety concerns during peak hours.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-balancing scooter - a two-wheeled electric personal transporter that balances automatically
  • Synonym: personal transporter - a small electric vehicle for individual short-distance travel

Etymology

Named after the fictional levitating 'hoverboard' (hover + board); applied in the 2010s to self-balancing two-wheeled electric scooters.
