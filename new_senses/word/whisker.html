/ ˈwɪskɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪskə / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪskɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪskə / (BrE)
a long, stiff hair that grows from the snout or face of some mammals, such as cats or mice, and is used for touch or sensing surroundings
long, stiff hair on a mammal's face

Examples

A mouse uses each whisker to navigate through narrow tunnels in the dark.
The cat twitched its whisker as it sensed movement nearby.
The lion's whisker stood out against its golden fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrissa - a technical term for a stiff facial hair (whisker) in mammals

Etymology

From Middle English 'wisker', related to 'wisk' meaning a quick movement.

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8w\u026ask\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8w\u026ask\u0259 / (BrE)
(in electronics and materials science) a thin, hair-like crystalline filament that grows spontaneously from metal surfaces (often tin or zinc) and can cause electrical shorts or device failures.
hair-like crystalline growth on metal

Examples

Manufacturers must inspect circuit boards for tin whiskers that could eventually cause electrical shorts.
A thin whisker growing from the connector pierced the insulating coating and triggered a failure.
Engineers developed a plating process to reduce whisker formation on metal contacts in devices.

Synset Examples

Manufacturers must inspect circuit boards for tin whiskers that could eventually cause electrical shorts.
A thin whisker growing from the connector pierced the insulating coating and triggered a failure.
Engineers developed a plating process to reduce whisker formation on metal contacts in devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin whisker - a specific type of metal whisker formed from tin plating; can cause electrical shorts
  • Synonym: metal whisker - a thin crystalline filament that grows from metal surfaces (general term)

Etymology

Named by analogy to animal 'whiskers' because of their hair-like appearance; term used in electronics and materials science since the 20th century.
