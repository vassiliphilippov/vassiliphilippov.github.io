dropper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑpɚ / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpə / (BrE)
A small tube with a rubber bulb at one end, used to draw in and release liquids in small drops.
tube with bulb for dispensing drops

Examples

Be sure to clean the dropper after each use to avoid contamination.
She added the medicine using a dropper to ensure the correct dosage.
The artist used a dropper to apply precise drops of ink onto the paper.

Synset Examples

she used an eye dropper to administer medication to the eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipette - a slender tube used to measure or transfer small amounts of liquid

Etymology

From 'drop' (noun) + agent suffix '-er', meaning 'one who drops'.

In video games and level design, a device or mechanism that releases or drops players or in-game objects from a height, often to create a challenge, obstacle, or transition between areas.
gaming device that drops players

Examples

The level designer placed a dropper at the top to send players into the challenge below.
Many Minecraft maps include steep droppers that test a player's timing and landing skills.
If you miss the moving platform, the dropper releases the player into a pit below.
