wobbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑblər / (AmE), / ˈwɒblə / (BrE)
an object or device that moves unsteadily from side to side or is likely to tip over due to instability.
an object that wobbles

Examples

A faulty table leg turned the sturdy desk into a dangerous wobbler.
The toddler played with a brightly colored wobbler that rocked back and forth on the floor.
This fishing lure is a type of wobbler designed to mimic the movement of injured prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobble - to move unsteadily from side to side

Etymology

From the verb 'wobble' (to move unsteadily) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈwɑblər / (AmE), / ˈwɒblə / (BrE)
an undecided or unsteady voter who is likely to switch support between parties or candidates.
undecided/swing voter
informal

Examples

The campaign targeted the undecided wobbler in the marginal constituency with personal canvassing.
She was labeled a wobbler by party activists after publicly criticising the platform.
Local newspapers reported a rise in wobbler support after the controversy around the incumbent.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing voter - a voter whose support is not fixed and may change between elections or candidates
  • Antonym: party loyalist - a voter who consistently supports the same political party

Etymology

From the verb 'wobble' (to move unsteadily) + agent suffix -er; used metaphorically for a person who wavers.
