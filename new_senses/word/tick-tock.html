tick-tock

1. NOUN

/ tɪk tɑk / (AmE), / tɪk tɒk / (BrE)
the regular sound produced by a clock, often symbolizing the passage of time
sound made by a clock

Examples

She found the steady tick-tock soothing as she tried to fall asleep.
The tick-tock of the grandfather clock filled the silent room.
With every tick-tock, he felt the deadline approaching.

Synset Examples

With each tick-tock, my nerves grew stronger.
She heard the tick-tock of the old clock in the hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a short, light, clicking sound, like that made by a clock

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a mechanical clock.

2. NOUN NEW

/ tɪk tɑk / (AmE), / tɪk tɒk / (BrE)
a development strategy (especially in technology and manufacturing) that alternates regularly between two types of product updates: one phase for major design or architecture changes ('tick') and the next for process, manufacturing, or optimization improvements ('tock').
alternating tech development cycle

Examples

Intel's tick-tock strategy alternated major architecture changes with manufacturing improvements.
Startups sometimes adopt a faster tick-tock cadence to keep products competitive in a crowded market.
The engineering team debated whether to break from the established tick-tock rhythm for a major redesign.

Synset Examples

Intel's tick-tock strategy alternated major architecture changes with manufacturing improvements.
Startups sometimes adopt a faster tick-tock cadence to keep products competitive in a crowded market.
The engineering team debated whether to break from the established tick-tock rhythm for a major redesign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: development cycle - a repeating series of stages in creating and improving a product
  • Synonym: release cadence - the regular timing or rhythm with which new product versions are released

Etymology

Named after the imitative clock sound 'tick‑tock'; popularized in the tech industry (notably by Intel) to describe alternating design and manufacturing cycles.
