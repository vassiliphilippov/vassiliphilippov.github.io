villager

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlədʒər / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person who lives in or comes from a village, especially one who has spent most of their life there
person living in a village

Examples

Many villagers gather at the market square every morning to trade goods.
She was known as a helpful villager who always assisted her neighbors.
The curious villager watched as tourists explored the narrow lanes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: townsman - a person who lives in a town, especially as contrasted with a villager

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *village* (village) + *-er* (denoting a person).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvɪlɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪdʒə / (BrE)
(in video games and virtual worlds) a non-player character that represents a resident of a village; typically interacts with players (for example by trading, giving quests, or providing services).
video-game non-player character

Examples

In Minecraft, a villager will trade emeralds for food and tools at the market.
Players built fences to protect the villagers from nightly zombie attacks near the farms.
The mod added dialogue options so each villager could offer unique quests to visitors.
