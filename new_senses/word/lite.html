lite

1. ADJ

/ laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
(of food or drink) containing fewer calories or less fat than the usual version; light.
lower-calorie or reduced-fat (food or drink)

Examples

Many people prefer lite beers because they have fewer calories.
She asked for a lite salad dressing with her meal.
This yogurt is labeled as lite, but still tastes creamy.

Synset Examples

light (or lite) beer
lite (or light) mayonnaise
a low-cal diet
cola light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - having less weight, calories, or intensity

Etymology

Altered spelling of 'light', adopted in marketing to emphasize reduced calories or fat.

2. ADJ NEW

/ laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
(of a product or software) having reduced features, smaller size, or lower resource requirements so it runs on older or limited devices or uses less data.
reduced-feature, lightweight product version
informal

Examples

The lite version of the app runs smoothly on older phones.
Developers released a lite edition for users with limited storage and slow connections.
She chose the lite plan to save data while still accessing essential features.

Synset Examples

The lite version of the app runs smoothly on older phones.
Developers released a lite edition for users with limited storage and slow connections.
She chose the lite plan to save data while still accessing essential features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - having less weight, size, or features; a reduced version
  • Synonym: basic - a simpler version with only essential features
  • Antonym: pro - a full-featured or professional version, often contrasted with a reduced 'lite' version

Etymology

Altered spelling of 'light' used in marketing to mean 'reduced' or 'less'; extended in late 20th–21st century to product/software variants.
