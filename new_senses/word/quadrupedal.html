quadrupedal

1. ADJ

/ kwɑːˈdrupɪdəl / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrʊpɪdəl / (BrE)
(of an animal) walking on four feet; having four limbs adapted for walking.
walking on four feet

Examples

Most mammals are quadrupedal, moving efficiently on all four limbs.
Some dinosaurs were initially bipedal but evolved to become quadrupedal for stability.
The dog is a classic example of a quadrupedal animal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bipedal - walking on two feet

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrupes' (four-footed), from 'quattuor' (four) + 'pes' (foot).
