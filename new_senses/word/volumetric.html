volumetric

1. ADJ

/ ˌvoʊ.ljuˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌvɒl.juˈmɛ.trɪk / (BrE)
relating to the measurement of volume or involving the quantity of space that a substance or object occupies.
relating to measurement by volume

Examples

The chemist performed a volumetric analysis to determine the concentration of the solution.
The engineer measured the volumetric flow rate of water through the pipe.
We use volumetric flasks in the laboratory to measure liquids precisely.

Synset Examples

volumetric analysis

Etymology

From volume + -metric, modeled after geometric.

(in media and computing) relating to the creation, capture, representation, or rendering of three-dimensional content represented as volumes rather than flat images or meshes.
relating to 3D volumetric media

Examples

The studio showcased volumetric capture technology for immersive VR scenes.
They used volumetric video to record the actor's performance for the VR experience.
Streaming platforms are beginning to support volumetric media so viewers can move within recorded scenes.
