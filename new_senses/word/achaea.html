Achaea

1. PROPN

/ əˈkiːə / (AmE), / əˈkiːə / (BrE)
a region of ancient Greece located on the north coast of the Peloponnese, known for its role in Greek history and mythology
ancient region of Greece, north Peloponnese

Examples

Archaeologists recently uncovered new artifacts in the region once called Achaea.
Many myths and legends from Achaea have influenced Western literature.
The ancient warriors of Achaea were renowned for their bravery.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀχαΐα (Akhaía), the name of the region.

2. PROPN NEW

/ əˈkiə / (AmE), / əˈkiə / (BrE)
(in modern Greece) a regional unit of Western Greece, created in the 2011 administrative reform, with Patras as its largest city and administrative centre.
modern regional unit of Greece (capital Patras)

Examples

The port of Patras is the economic hub of Achaea and draws many tourists each summer.
Local farmers in Achaea celebrated a successful harvest after months of dry weather.
The university in Achaea attracts students from across Greece and neighboring countries.
