Sucre

1. PROPN

/ ˈsuːkreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsuːkreɪ / (BrE)
the judicial capital and seat of the judiciary in Bolivia
judicial capital of Bolivia

Examples

Many tourists visit Sucre to admire its colonial architecture.
The Supreme Court of Bolivia is located in Sucre.
The government of Bolivia holds important legal proceedings in Sucre.

Etymology

Named after Antonio José de Sucre, a leader in South America's independence movement.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ˈsuːkreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsuːkreɪ / (BrE)
(in international/regional finance) a regional unit of account and electronic clearing mechanism used to settle trade and payments among member states of ALBA (Sistema Unitario de Compensación Regional).
regional unit of account (ALBA)

Examples

Several governments began settling trade in Sucre under the regional currency scheme.
The bank used Sucre balances to clear payments between member countries after the agreement.
Economists debated whether adopting the Sucre improved stability or merely complicated bilateral settlements.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regional currency - a currency or unit of account used by multiple countries within a region for trade and settlement
  • Synonym: clearing union - a system that allows multilateral clearing and settlement of payments between member countries

Etymology

Named after Antonio José de Sucre; also an acronym from Spanish 'Sistema Unitario de Compensación Regional' used for the ALBA payments mechanism.
