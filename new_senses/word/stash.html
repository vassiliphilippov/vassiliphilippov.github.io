stash

1. NOUN

/ stæʃ / (AmE), / stæʃ / (BrE)
a secret store of something valuable, such as money or goods, kept hidden for future use
a hidden supply of valuables or money

Examples

He always keeps a secret stash of cash in his desk drawer.
She pulled a chocolate bar from her private stash.
The police found a stash of stolen jewelry hidden in the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - a large amount of something valuable kept hidden

Etymology

Perhaps from Romani 'stach' (to hide), or influenced by 'stow'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13388085-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ stæʃ / (AmE), / stæʃ / (BrE)
to store or hide something, especially money or valuables, for future use, often secretly
store or hide for later use

Examples

He stashed his savings in a shoebox under the bed for emergencies.
She likes to stash chocolate bars in her desk drawer in case she gets hungry.
They have been stashing supplies in the basement all winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - to accumulate and store up for future use
  • Synonym: conceal - to hide or keep secret
  • Antonym: reveal - to make something known that was hidden

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete dialectal English stash (to store) or influenced by 'stow' and 'cache'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02310836-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ stæʃ / (AmE), / stæʃ / (BrE)
(in computing, especially version control) to save uncommitted changes temporarily so you can switch contexts and restore them later.
temporarily save uncommitted changes (VCS)

Examples

Before switching branches, she stashed her local changes to avoid merge conflicts later.
He ran git stash to save unfinished edits so he could switch branches safely.
The CI tutorial explained how to stash changes and apply them later when the build succeeds.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to record data so it can be used again later; in VCS contexts, to preserve work
  • Antonym: commit - to record changes permanently in a version-control repository (contrast with temporarily stashing them)

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the verb 'stash' (to hide or store); applied in the 21st century to temporary storage of code changes.
