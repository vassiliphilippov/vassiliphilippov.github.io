mycelium

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈsiliəm / (AmE), / mɪˈsiːlɪəm / (BrE)
the network of fine, branching filaments (hyphae) that make up the main growing part of a fungus, usually found in soil or organic material
network of fungal threads (hyphae)
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the mycelium to better understand how fungi decompose organic matter.
The mycelium spread rapidly through the damp, decaying leaves on the forest floor.
You can sometimes see white mycelium growing on old bread as it begins to rot.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypha - a single thread-like filament forming part of the mycelium of a fungus

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek mykēs (fungus) + -ēlion (diminutive suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ maɪˈsiliəm / (AmE), / mɪˈsiːlɪəm / (BrE)
A sustainable biomaterial grown from fungal mycelium and processed into panels, foams, or leather-like sheets for packaging, insulation, or products.
sustainable fungal biomaterial

Examples

The company produces lightweight mycelium packaging that biodegrades quickly after use.
They wrapped the fragile lamp in panels of grown mycelium instead of foam for shipping.
Designers developed a durable, leather-like mycelium for vegan shoes and furniture upholstery.
