Camellia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiliə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asiatic evergreen shrubs or small trees, including several species grown for their attractive flowers or for tea production
genus of tropical evergreen shrubs or small trees

Examples

Botanists study the different species within the Camellia genus for their unique characteristics.
She planted several Camellia bushes for their elegant blooms.
The Camellia genus includes both ornamental garden plants and the source of tea leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea plant - plant cultivated for producing tea (Camellia sinensis)

Etymology

Named after Jesuit botanist Georg Kamel (1661–1706).

2. NOUN NEW

/ kəˈmiliə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliə / (BrE)
(computing, cryptography) a symmetric-key block cipher algorithm used to encrypt data; standardized and widely adopted for secure communications.
symmetric block cipher algorithm

Examples

The security team implemented Camellia to encrypt sensitive customer data at rest.
Some government systems prefer Camellia because it offers strong encryption with efficient performance.
The open-source library includes support for Camellia alongside AES and other ciphers.

Etymology

Named after the camellia flower; developed by Mitsubishi and NTT in 2000 and later standardized for cryptographic use.
