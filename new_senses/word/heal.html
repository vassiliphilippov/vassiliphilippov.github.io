to heal

1. VERB

/ hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
(of an injury, wound, or person) become sound or healthy again; to recover from injury or illness.
recover or become healthy again

Examples

After her operation, she needed time to heal both physically and emotionally.
Doctors said the bone would heal faster if he rested properly.
It took several weeks for his wound to heal fully.

Synset Examples

My broken leg is mending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure - to relieve a person or animal of the symptoms of a disease or condition
  • Synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
  • Antonym: worsen - to become or make worse

Etymology

Old English hælan (make whole, sound, or well); related to whole.

5. VERB NEW

/ hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
(in computing) to repair or restore a digital system, service, or data automatically after failure or corruption, often using built-in or automated protocols.
automatically restore digital systems

Examples

The cloud platform can heal failed virtual machines automatically within minutes without human intervention.
After the power surge, the distributed database healed corrupted replicas using redundancy and checksums.
Their monitoring tool triggers healing routines that isolate faults and restore service quickly.
