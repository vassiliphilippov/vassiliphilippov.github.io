foxy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑːk.si / (AmE), / ˈfɒk.si / (BrE)
Clever at deceiving people; sly or cunning.
clever and sly, deceptive

Examples

He gave me a foxy grin, clearly up to something mischievous.
Her foxy tactics in negotiation left everyone else at a disadvantage.
The foxy lawyer quickly found a loophole to win the case.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sly - clever at deceiving people; cunning
  • Synonym: cunning - skilled at achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Antonym: naive - showing a lack of experience or sophistication; easily deceived

Etymology

From 'fox' + '-y', evoking the animal's reputation for cunning.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈfɑːk.si / (AmE), / ˈfɒk.si / (BrE)
(informal) Physically attractive or sexually appealing; having a sexy or seductive appearance.
sexually attractive (informal)
informal slang

Examples

Everyone at the party noticed her foxy dress and kept asking where she bought it.
He described the new singer as surprisingly foxy, combining old Hollywood glamour with modern confidence.
The advertisement used a foxy model to suggest the perfume was bold and seductive.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexy - sexually attractive; causing sexual desire
  • Synonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing in appearance or manner
  • Antonym: unattractive - not pleasing or appealing in appearance; opposite of physically attractive

Etymology

From 'fox' + suffix '-y'; originally evoking the animal's qualities. The sense 'sexually attractive' arose as informal/slang usage in the 20th century.
