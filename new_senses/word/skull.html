skull

1. NOUN

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
the bony structure that forms the head and encloses and protects the brain in vertebrates
the bony skeleton of the head

Examples

The archaeologists discovered an ancient skull buried beneath the ruins.
The medical student carefully examined the anatomy of the human skull.
Wearing a helmet protects your skull from injury during accidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranium - the part of the skull that encloses the brain

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skalli' (bald head).

2. NOUN NEW

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
(in video games) a gameplay modifier, often shown as a skull icon, that changes game rules or increases difficulty.
gameplay modifier increasing difficulty
slang

Examples

Activating the skull before the match made enemies much tougher and more aggressive.
She picked up a skull that doubled experience but disabled checkpoints for the run.
Speedrunners often avoid turning on any skull to keep their runs consistent and fast.
