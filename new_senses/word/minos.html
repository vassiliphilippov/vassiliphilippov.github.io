Minos

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the son of Zeus and Europa who became king of ancient Crete; he is noted for commissioning the Labyrinth and, after death, becoming a judge in the underworld.
mythical king of Crete and underworld judge

Examples

According to legend, Minos demanded tribute from Athens to feed the Minotaur in the Labyrinth.
After his death, Minos became a judge of souls in the Greek underworld.
Daedalus built the famous Labyrinth for King Minos on the island of Crete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhadamanthus - another judge of the dead in Greek mythology, Minos' brother
  • Synonym: Aeacus - another judge of the dead in the Greek underworld, Minos' brother

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μίνως (Minōs), the legendary king of Crete.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ɛm aɪ ɛn oʊ ɛs / (AmE), / ɛm aɪ ɛn əʊ ɛs / (BrE)
A particle-physics experiment (Main Injector Neutrino Oscillation Search) at Fermilab that studied neutrino oscillations using a long-baseline neutrino beam.
Fermilab neutrino-oscillation experiment (acronym)

Examples

Researchers presented new oscillation data collected by MINOS at the international physics conference.
The team compared measurements from the near and far detectors used in MINOS to study neutrino disappearance.
Funding decisions for follow-up projects were influenced by results first reported by MINOS.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: Fermilab - a U.S. national laboratory that hosts particle-physics experiments

Etymology

Acronym of Main Injector Neutrino Oscillation Search, the official name of the Fermilab experiment.
