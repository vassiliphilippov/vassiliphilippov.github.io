rematch

1. NOUN

/ ˈriˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈriːˌmatʃ / (BrE)
A second or subsequent contest or game played between the same opponents, usually to decide a winner after an earlier meeting.
a game or contest played again

Examples

Fans are already excited about the rematch between the rival teams next season.
She trained hard for the rematch, determined to win this time.
The two boxers agreed to a rematch after their first fight ended in a draw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - a contest or game between opponents

Etymology

From re- (again) + match.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈriˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈriːˌmatʃ / (BrE)
To play or arrange a second or subsequent contest or game between the same opponents; to meet again in competition.
play a second contest

Examples

After the controversial call, the teams agreed to rematch the game next month to settle the rivalry.
They rematched last season, and the defending champions won by a narrow two-point margin.
Fans hoped the singers would rematch their earlier duet with a new arrangement at the awards.
