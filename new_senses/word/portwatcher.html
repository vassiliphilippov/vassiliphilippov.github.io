portwatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtˌwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtˌwɒtʃə / (BrE)
a person assigned to guard or monitor a wharf or port area, often to ensure security or prevent theft
security guard at a wharf or port

Examples

As a portwatcher, she was responsible for monitoring activity on the dock at night.
In the 19th century, a portwatcher helped prevent cargo theft at busy wharves.
The portwatcher made his rounds every hour to ensure no one entered the restricted area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchman - a person employed to guard property at night or when otherwise unoccupied

Etymology

From 'port' (harbor) + 'watcher' (one who watches).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpɔrtˌwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtˌwɒtʃə / (BrE)
(in computing) a program or hardware device that monitors network ports for activity and reports or logs unusual connections or traffic.
software/device that monitors network ports

Examples

The security appliance includes a portwatcher that alerts administrators to unexpected inbound connections.
Before deployment, they configured the portwatcher to ignore routine traffic and only flag anomalies.
During the incident, the portwatcher recorded multiple failed login attempts on critical service ports.
