gram

1. NOUN

/ ɡræm / (AmE), / ɡræm / (BrE)
a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram
metric unit of mass

Examples

A single gram of gold can be drawn into a long wire.
Sugar should be limited to less than 50 grams per day for adults.
The recipe calls for 200 grams of flour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kilogram - metric unit of mass equal to one thousand grams
  • Degree Lower: milligram - metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a gram

Etymology

From French 'gramme', from Late Latin 'gramma', from Greek 'gramma' (a small weight, letter).
