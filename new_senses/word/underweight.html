underweight

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈweɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈweɪt / (BrE)
(of a person) extremely thin; below the normal or healthy weight.
extremely thin; below healthy weight

Examples

After his illness, he became noticeably underweight and weak.
Models are often criticized for appearing underweight on the runway.
The doctor said she was too underweight for her age and height.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, especially in an unattractive or unhealthy way
  • Synonym: bony - very thin, with bones that show easily
  • Antonym: overweight - above the normal or healthy weight

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'under-' (meaning below) + 'weight'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˌʌndəˈweɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈweɪt / (BrE)
(finance) having a smaller allocation in a portfolio than in a benchmark or than a recommended/target weight.
allocated below benchmark weight (finance)

Examples

The fund remains underweight in energy stocks relative to its benchmark index this quarter.
After research, the portfolio manager decided to hold an underweight position in small-cap companies.
Analysts recommended investors remain underweight on the sector until oil prices stabilize over months.

Synset Examples

The fund remains underweight in energy stocks relative to its benchmark index this quarter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overweight - assigned a larger allocation in a portfolio than in the benchmark or target.
  • Synonym: underallocate - to assign less capital or weight to an asset or sector than is typical or required.

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'under-' (meaning below) + 'weight'.
