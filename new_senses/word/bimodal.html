bimodal

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈmoʊ.dəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈməʊ.dəl / (BrE)
(Of a distribution, set, or phenomenon) having or characterized by two distinct modes or peaks.
having two modes; with two peaks

Examples

If a histogram is bimodal, it suggests there may be two underlying groups in the data.
Some natural phenomena exhibit bimodal patterns during certain times of the year.
The data showed a bimodal distribution, with two distinct peaks in frequency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimodal - having only one mode or peak

Etymology

From bi- (meaning 'two') + modal (relating to mode).

2. ADJ NEW

/ baɪˈmoʊ.dəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈməʊ.dəl / (BrE)
(in data science and machine learning) involving or using two different data modalities or input types (for example, text and images) in a single model, dataset, or task.
involving two data modalities

Examples

Researchers trained a bimodal model to match captions with corresponding images for better retrieval.
The app uses a bimodal approach, combining voice commands and camera input for accessibility.
During the course, students built a bimodal dataset containing paired sentences and photos for training.
