desktop

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛskˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈdɛsktɒp / (BrE)
The background area of a computer screen in a graphical user interface, on which icons, windows, and menus appear.
background area of a computer screen

Examples

After starting the computer, all the program icons appeared on the desktop.
Files saved to the desktop are easy to find quickly.
You can personalize your desktop with a wallpaper of your choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home screen - the main screen of a device displaying apps or shortcuts

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛskˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈdɛsktɒp / (BrE)
the flat upper surface of a desk, on which work may be done
the top surface of a desk

Examples

A scratch ran across the entire length of the old wooden desktop.
She cleared everything off the desktop before starting her work.
The lamp took up most of his small desktop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desktop computer - a personal computer designed to fit on a desk

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdɛsktɑp / (AmE), / ˈdɛsktɒp / (BrE)
A virtual desktop environment hosted on a remote server or in the cloud and accessed over a network, giving a user a familiar desktop interface, applications, and files as if on a local machine.
cloud-hosted virtual desktop

Examples

The IT department gave employees a cloud-hosted desktop accessible from any device.
During the pandemic, many companies switched to a virtual desktop so staff could use standardized apps remotely.
She logged into the secure desktop and found all her files and business apps ready.
