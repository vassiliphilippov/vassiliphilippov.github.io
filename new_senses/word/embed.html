to embed

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɛd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛd / (BrE)
to assign a journalist to accompany and report on a military unit during a conflict or operation.
assign (journalist) to a military unit

Examples

During the war, several journalists were embedded with front-line troops to provide real-time coverage.
She was embedded with the peacekeeping battalion for six months to document their day-to-day operations.
The government allowed select reporters to embed with the army during the conflict.

Synset Examples

The young reporter was embedded with the Third Division

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɛd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛd / (BrE)
to fix something firmly and deeply into a surrounding mass or substance so that it becomes an integral part of it
set or fix securely and deeply

Examples

Glass fragments were embedded deeply in the pavement after the accident.
The journalist was embedded with the army unit during the conflict.
Tiny sensors are embedded in the clothing to monitor heart rate and temperature.

Synset Examples

He planted a knee in the back of his opponent
The dentist implanted a tooth in the gum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implant - to insert or fix in a person's body, especially by surgery

Etymology

From Old French 'embedir', based on Latin 'in' (in) + 'batum', past participle of 'battuere' (to beat).

3. VERB NEW

/ ɪmˈbɛd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛd / (BrE)
(in computing and web publishing) to insert media, code, or content into a webpage, app, or document so that it displays and behaves as part of the host.
insert media or content into a webpage/app

Examples

The article allows readers to embed a short video directly into the story.
She embeds a live map on the site to show real-time event locations.
During the lesson, the teacher demonstrated embedding a tweet into a blog post.

Synset Examples

The article allows readers to embed a short video directly into the story.
She embeds a live map on the site to show real-time event locations.
During the lesson, the teacher demonstrated embedding a tweet into a blog post.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - to put something into something else so it becomes part of it
  • Synonym: integrate - to combine or incorporate parts so they work together as a whole

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the sense ‘fix in, set firmly’; ultimately from Old French 'embedir', based on Latin 'in' (in) + 'battuere' (to beat).
