mudder

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌdər / (AmE), / ˈmʌdə / (BrE)
A racehorse that performs well on muddy racetracks.
racehorse good on muddy tracks

Examples

Because of yesterday’s storm, several bettors placed their money on the mudder.
The jockey said his horse was a proven mudder after winning in tough conditions.
The trainer believed the mudder would have an advantage on the rain-soaked track.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racehorse - a horse bred and trained for racing

Etymology

From 'mud' + agent suffix '-er', indicating something or someone associated with mud.
