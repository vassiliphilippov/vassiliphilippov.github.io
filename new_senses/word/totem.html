totem

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.təm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təm / (BrE)
An object, animal, or plant regarded as the symbol of a family, clan, or group, especially among some Indigenous peoples.
symbolic animal, plant, or object for a clan

Examples

Each tribe selected a unique animal as its totem.
The eagle is the totem of many Native American clans.
The wolf served as a powerful totem for the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emblem - a symbolic object used as a distinctive badge

Etymology

From Ojibwa *ototeman* (his totem).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊtəm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəm / (BrE)
A group, especially a clan or tribe, identified by their kinship with or symbolic relationship to a particular totemic object, typically an animal or natural figure regarded as an emblem.
tribe identified by common totemic object

Examples

Many indigenous societies are organized around a totem that unites all members under a shared identity.
Over generations, stories about their totem were passed down to teach cultural values and ancestry.
The entire tribe regarded the eagle as their totem, symbolizing strength and courage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of families with a common ancestor

Etymology

From Ojibwe (Anishinaabe) 'ototeman' (his relative); entered English via early anthropological studies.
