to houseclean

1. VERB

/ ˈhaʊsˌklin / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkliːn / (BrE)
to clean and tidy up a house, especially as a regular or thorough activity
clean and tidy a house

Examples

She likes to houseclean thoroughly at the start of each season.
They hired someone to houseclean before their guests arrived.
We usually houseclean on Saturdays to keep the place looking fresh.

Synset Examples

She housecleans every week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make (something) free from dirt or mess

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'clean', modeled after similar compounds such as 'housekeep'.

2. VERB NEW

/ c8ha8asccklin / (AmE), / c8ha8asccklid0n / (BrE)
(informal) to remove people, practices, or procedures considered unwanted or inefficient from an organization; to carry out a thorough reorganization or purge.
purge or reorganize an organization
informal

Examples

After the scandal, the governor moved quickly to houseclean the entire administration.
The CEO housecleaned several departments, firing managers and replacing outdated procedures.
Their first act as new board members was to begin housecleaning across the organization.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purge - to remove people or things from an organization or system, often suddenly or forcefully
  • Synonym: clean house - to make big changes in an organization, typically removing people or policies

Etymology

From 'house' + 'clean'; originally literal, later extended metaphorically to mean 'clean out' an organization.
