scaling

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of arranging things in a graduated series or sequence according to size, amount, importance, etc.
arranging by degree or rank

Examples

A clear scaling of difficulty helps learners progress through the material.
Proper scaling of tasks in the project ensures that each phase is manageable.
The new salary system involves the scaling of wages according to job performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradation - a series of gradual changes or stages
  • Synonym: ranking - the process of placing in order of importance or quality

Etymology

From scale (arrange in steps or degrees), + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of climbing or ascending, especially by or as if by using a ladder
act of climbing or ascending

Examples

During the scaling of the fortress wall, the soldiers moved quickly and quietly.
He attempted the scaling of the icy mountain at dawn.
The scaling of the cliff required both skill and courage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climbing - the act of going up something, especially using hands and feet

Etymology

From the verb 'scale', ultimately from Latin 'scala' (ladder).

3. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of measuring or arranging or adjusting according to a scale
measuring or arranging by a scale

Examples

Accurate scaling is essential when converting blueprints to full-size structures.
Successful scaling of the project required adjusting resources to match the new workload.
The architect used careful scaling to ensure the model reflected the real building's proportions.

Etymology

Derived from 'scale' (measure) + -ing (forming nouns from verbs).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of increasing the size, capacity, or scope of a business, system, or operation so it can handle greater demand or growth.
increasing capacity (business/tech)

Examples

The startup is focusing on scaling its platform to support millions of users worldwide.
Effective scaling of the service required moving to distributed servers and automated deployment.
Without careful scaling, rapid growth can overwhelm customer support and harm reputation.
