interception

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(in American football) the act of catching a forward pass by a player on the opposing team, resulting in a change of possession.
catching an opponent's pass in football

Examples

He celebrated wildly after his first career interception in the NFL.
The cornerback made a crucial interception late in the fourth quarter.
Throwing an interception can drastically change the momentum of the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnover - when possession of the ball is lost to the other team

Etymology

From Latin interceptio (a taking between, seizing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of stopping or interrupting something before it reaches its intended destination
the act of stopping or interrupting

Examples

His quick interception of the ball changed the course of the game.
Police managed the interception of the suspect before he could cross the border.
The interception of the message prevented the secret from being revealed.

Synset Examples

he resorted to the interception of his daughter's letters
he claimed that the interception of one missile by another would be impossible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intercept - to stop or seize something on its way

Etymology

From Latin interceptio, from intercipere (to seize, interrupt).
