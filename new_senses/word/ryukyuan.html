Ryukyuan

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈjuːkjuən / (AmE), / raɪˈjuːkjʊən / (BrE)
the group of languages related to Japanese, spoken by the people of the Ryukyu Islands in Japan
Okinawan and related languages of the Ryukyu Islands

Examples

Efforts are being made to preserve the endangered Ryukyuan languages.
Linguists study Ryukyuan to understand its relationship with Japanese.
The Ryukyuan language is still spoken by some residents of Okinawa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese - the language spoken in Japan; related to Ryukyuan

Etymology

From Ryukyu, the name of the islands, plus the English suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ riːˈjuːkiˌuːən / (AmE), / riːˈjuːkiːuən / (BrE)
a person belonging to the indigenous people of the Ryukyu Islands, southwest of Japan
a person from the Ryukyu Islands

Examples

A significant number of Ryukyuans now live in mainland Japan as well as on the islands.
Many Ryukyuans have preserved their traditional language and customs despite outside influences.
The museum featured artifacts from the ancient Ryukyuan culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese - a person from Japan or of Japanese descent

Etymology

From Ryukyu, the name of the island group, from Japanese 琉球 (Ryūkyū), plus English suffix -an.
