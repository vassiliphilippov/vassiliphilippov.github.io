bo liao

1. ADJ

/ boʊ liːˈaʊ / (AmE), / bəʊ liːˈaʊ / (BrE)
(slang, Singapore/Malaysia) Feeling bored, unproductive, or having nothing to do; uninteresting or pointless.
feeling bored or unproductive (slang)
slang

Examples

His jokes were so bo liao that everyone stopped paying attention.
She told me she was bo liao and wanted to do something fun.
Whenever I'm bo liao, I scroll endlessly through social media.

Synset Examples

She said she was bo liao and wanted to go home.
I'm feeling a bit bo liao today; nothing seems fun.
The meeting was so boring, I was completely bo liao.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bored - feeling weary because one is unoccupied or lacks interest
  • Synonym: uninterested - not interested; indifferent or bored

Etymology

From Hokkien Chinese 無聊 (bô liâu), meaning 'boring' or 'having nothing to do'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ boʊ liːˈaʊ / (AmE), / bəʊ liːˈaʊ / (BrE)
(slang, Singapore/Malaysia) Informal: ridiculous, foolish, or obviously not credible; nonsense or absurd.
ridiculous; nonsense (slang)
slang

Examples

That plan is bo liao; nobody will take it seriously in a professional meeting.
Don't follow his advice—it's clearly bo liao and will only waste your time.
The article made so many unsupported claims that I found it rather bo liao.

Synset Examples

That plan is bo liao; nobody will take it seriously in a professional meeting.
Don't follow his advice—it's clearly bo liao and will only waste your time.
The article made so many unsupported claims that I found it rather bo liao.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculous - unreasonable or absurd; deserving to be laughed at
  • Synonym: nonsense - ideas, statements, or behaviour that are absurd or have no sensible meaning
  • Antonym: reasonable - sensible and fair; the opposite of absurd or silly

Etymology

From Hokkien Chinese 無聊 (bô liáu), originally meaning 'boring' or 'having nothing to do'; later extended in Singlish/Manglish to judge something as silly or nonsense.
