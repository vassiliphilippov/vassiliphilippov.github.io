bet

1. NOUN

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
an act of risking money or something valuable on the outcome of an event, game, or situation; a wager
an act of gambling; a wager

Examples

He lost his bet and had to pay for everyone's dinner.
On a dare and a bet, he jumped into the freezing lake.
She placed a bet on the outcome of the football match.

Synset Examples

he did it on a bet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - an act of betting or staking something on an event
  • Synonym: gamble - to bet money or valuables on an uncertain outcome

Etymology

From Old English *beot* (a vow or boast).

2. NOUN

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
an amount of money risked on the outcome of a future event, especially in gambling
money risked on a gamble

Examples

He lost his bet after the team unexpectedly lost the game.
She placed a bet on her favorite horse to win the race.
The total bet on the roulette table exceeded two thousand dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - an amount of money or something valuable risked in a bet

Etymology

Probably from the obsolete English word 'bat' meaning 'good' or 'advantage'.

informal

4. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
to state confidently that something will happen, often as if making a wager; to wager money or something of value on the outcome of an event
state with confidence; wager

Examples

He bet his friend that it would rain tomorrow.
I bet she will win the competition this year.
You can bet that prices will rise again before summer.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - to risk money or something of value on the outcome of an event
  • Degree Lower: predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Degree Higher: guarantee - promise with certainty

Etymology

From Middle English betten (to pledge as a wager), probably from Old English *beatan* (to beat), but influenced in sense by late 16th-century usage.
