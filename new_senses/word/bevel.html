bevel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛvəl / (BrE)
A hand tool with two hinged arms used for marking or measuring angles of any size.
tool for marking or measuring angles

Examples

A bevel can be set to any angle, making it a versatile tool for craftsmen.
She adjusted the bevel before marking the line on the wood.
The carpenter used a bevel to measure the precise angle for the frame.

Etymology

From Old French *baivel* (having two lips, beak-shaped), from Latin *bifarius* (double, twofold).

2. VERB

/ ˈbɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛvəl / (BrE)
to cut or shape (an edge or surface) so that it is not perpendicular to the adjacent surfaces, but instead forms a sloping edge
to cut an edge at an angle

Examples

The carpenter carefully beveled the edges of the table for a smoother finish.
The glass was beveled to create an elegant, decorative appearance.
You need to bevel the metal before welding the pieces together.

Synset Examples

bevel the surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamfer - to cut an edge or corner to make a symmetrical sloping edge

Etymology

From Old French *baivel* (with a slant), diminutive of *baif* (side of a ship).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛvəl / (BrE)
An edge or surface that is sloped at an angle other than 90 degrees, especially where two surfaces meet.
angled edge or surface

Examples

Adjust the saw to achieve the correct bevel for this joint.
He cut a bevel on the wood so the pieces would fit together smoothly.
The glass top has a decorative bevel along the edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamfer - a beveled edge connecting two surfaces

Etymology

From Old French *baivel* 'a slant, beak', from Latin *bifurcus* 'two-pronged'.
