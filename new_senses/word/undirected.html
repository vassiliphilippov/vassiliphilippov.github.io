undirected

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈrɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈrɛktɪd / (BrE)
not having a specific direction, goal, or purpose; lacking guidance or control.
lacking direction or purpose

Examples

After graduation, he felt undirected and unsure about his next move.
Her thoughts wandered in an undirected manner during the lecture.
The project's undirected efforts resulted in little progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aimless - without purpose or direction

Etymology

From un- (not) + directed, from Latin dirigere (to direct).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˌʌndɪˈrɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈrɛktɪd / (BrE)
(in mathematics and computer science) describing a graph or network whose edges have no orientation, so connections are unordered pairs and represent two-way or symmetric relationships.
of a graph: edges without orientation

Examples

Researchers modeled the network as an undirected graph to reflect symmetric connections.
In our simulation, we treated friendships as undirected relationships since ties were mutual.
An undirected edge between two nodes means either can initiate communication.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bidirectional - allowing movement or communication in both directions; often used to describe two-way connections.
  • Antonym: directed - of a graph whose edges have an orientation from one node to another; opposite of undirected.

From un- (not) + directed, from Latin dirigere (to direct).
