swarm

1. NOUN

/ swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
a large group of insects or other small things moving together in the air or on the ground
large group of moving insects or things

Examples

A swarm of bees gathered around the hive.
A swarm of tourists filled the small market square.
We watched a swarm of locusts descend upon the field.

Synset Examples

2. VERB

/ swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or insects) to move somewhere in large numbers, often in a busy or active way.
move in large numbers

Examples

Bees swarm out of their hive when disturbed.
Fans began to swarm into the stadium as soon as the gates opened.
Reporters swarmed around the celebrity, eager for a statement.
