soapiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpinəs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being soapy, or having soap on or in it
quality of being soapy

Examples

He disliked the lingering soapiness on his skin after a long shower.
The soapiness of the mixture suggested it still needed rinsing.
The soapiness of the water made it perfect for washing delicate fabrics.

Synset Examples

she could smell the soapiness of the doctor's hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapy - covered with, containing, or resembling soap

Etymology

From 'soapy' + '-ness', denoting the quality or state.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8so\u028apin\u0259s / (AmE), / \u02c8s\u0259\u028apin\u0259s / (BrE)
the quality of being sentimental, melodramatic, or exaggerated in a way that resembles a soap opera; excessive emotion or schmaltz in storytelling or presentation.
melodramatic, soap-opera–like quality

Examples

The ad's soapiness overwhelmed the message, turning a serious issue into melodrama.
Critics complained that the film's unnecessary soapiness made its characters feel unreal and exaggerated.
She avoids shows with too much soapiness; she prefers subtle, realistic drama instead.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodrama - a dramatic work or style marked by exaggerated emotions and plot
  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia in a way considered insincere or overdone

Etymology

From 'soap opera' + '-ness', originally referring to melodramatic qualities associated with soap operas.
