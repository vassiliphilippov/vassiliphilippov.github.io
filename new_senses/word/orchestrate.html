to orchestrate

1. VERB

/ ˈɔr.kəˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪ.streɪt / (BrE)
to organize and direct the elements of a complex project or event, often carefully or skillfully
plan and direct a complex activity

Examples

Authorities believe he orchestrated the entire operation from behind the scenes.
She orchestrated every detail of the event to ensure its success.
The manager skillfully orchestrated the merger between the two companies.

Synset Examples

he masterminded the robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate - to organize different elements to work effectively together
  • Synonym: manage - to be in charge of and control something
  • Antonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation (opposite of plan carefully)

Etymology

From orchestra + -ate, originally referring to arranging music for orchestra.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈɔrkɪˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪstreɪt / (BrE)
to arrange or write music for performance by an orchestra
arrange music for orchestra

Examples

After writing the main themes, she began to orchestrate the entire composition.
He spent months learning how to properly orchestrate a piano score for full orchestra.
The composer will orchestrate her new piece for the London Symphony Orchestra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange - to adapt a piece of music for a different style or ensemble

Etymology

From orchestra + -ate; based on the noun 'orchestra.'
