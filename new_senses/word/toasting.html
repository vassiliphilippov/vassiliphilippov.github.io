toasting

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊstɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of cooking something, especially bread, until it becomes brown and crisp, typically by exposure to heat or a grill.
browning food with heat or a grill

Examples

He monitored the toasting closely to ensure it wouldn't burn.
Proper toasting requires attention to heat and timing.
The toasting of the bread gives it a delicious crunch.

Synset Examples

proper toasting should brown both sides of a piece of bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baking - using dry heat in an oven to cook food

Etymology

From the verb 'toast', from Old French 'toester', from Latin 'tostare' (to parch, roast).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtoʊstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊstɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing) the act of showing brief, transient on-screen notifications (called 'toasts') that provide unobtrusive feedback to the user.
temporary on-screen notification

Examples

The app uses toasting to confirm actions without interrupting the user's workflow.
During testing, she logged every toasting so developers could check message timing and content.
Android's design guidelines recommend brief toasting for simple feedback, not long interactive dialogs.
