tavern

1. NOUN

/ ˈtævərn / (AmE), / ˈtævən / (BrE)
A place where alcoholic drinks are sold and consumed, often also serving food; a bar or pub, especially one of historical character.
place selling alcoholic drinks

Examples

Stories of pirates and treasures were often exchanged in the bustling tavern.
The old tavern in the village dates back to the 18th century.
They decided to meet at the local tavern after work for a drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pub - a place where alcoholic drinks are served, especially in Britain
  • Synonym: bar - establishment selling alcoholic beverages

Etymology

From Old French *taverne*, from Latin *taberna* (shop, inn).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtævərn / (AmE), / ˈtævən / (BrE)
A virtual gathering place in an online game, forum, or community, modelled on a traditional tavern and used for socializing, chatting, trading, or organising activities.
virtual social gathering place

Examples

After the update, the guild members met in the tavern to share tips and plan quests.
New players often hang out in virtual taverns to ask questions and make new friends.
The community created a text tavern channel for casual chat and weekend event sign-ups.
