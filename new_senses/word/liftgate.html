liftgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪftˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪftˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
a hinged rear door on a vehicle, typically sloping, that opens upward to provide access to the cargo area
upward-opening rear car door

Examples

After hitting a pothole, the liftgate wouldn't latch properly.
Please make sure the liftgate is closed before driving away.
The SUV's liftgate made it easy to load groceries into the back of the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailgate - a door at the rear of a vehicle that swings downward for loading

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈlɪftˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪftˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
a hydraulic or mechanical platform attached to the rear of a truck, van, or delivery vehicle, used to raise or lower cargo between the vehicle's cargo area and the ground or curb.
rear cargo lifting platform

Examples

The delivery truck's liftgate lowered the couch to the curb for unloading.
He stood on the liftgate while workers strapped the heavy refrigerator to the pallet.
Before loading, the driver inspected the liftgate to make sure it raised smoothly.

Synset Examples

The delivery truck's liftgate lowered the couch to the curb for unloading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tail lift - a rear-mounted platform (British term) for lifting cargo between ground and vehicle
  • Synonym: cargo lift - a platform or mechanism used to move goods between different heights; sometimes used interchangeably with liftgate

Etymology

Compound of lift + gate; originally describing a gate or platform that lifts.
