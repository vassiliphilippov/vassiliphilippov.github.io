to penalize

1. VERB

/ ˈpiːnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to punish someone or treat them unfairly for breaking a rule or law, especially by making them pay a penalty or suffer a disadvantage
impose a penalty or disadvantage

Examples

Employees may be penalized for consistently arriving late to work.
If you miss the application deadline, the university will penalize you by deducting points.
The referee decided to penalize the player for an illegal tackle.

Synset Examples

The students were penalized for showing up late for class
we had to punish the dog for soiling the floor again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punish - to cause someone to suffer because they have done something wrong
  • Antonym: reward - to give something positive to someone for good behavior or achievement

Etymology

From French *pénaliser*, from *pénal* (penal).

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8pi\u02d0n\u0259\u02ccla\u026az / (AmE), / \u02c8pi\u02d0n\u0259la\u026az / (BrE)
(in mathematics, statistics, and machine learning) to add a penalty term to an objective or loss function in order to discourage certain parameter values or model complexity, for example to reduce overfitting.
apply a mathematical penalty (regularization)

Examples

The loss function penalizes large weights to prevent the model from overfitting the training data.
To encourage sparsity, the algorithm penalizes nonzero coefficients using an L1 regularization term.
Researchers sometimes penalize complex models in experiments, balancing accuracy and simplicity through regularization.

Synset Examples

The loss function penalizes large weights to prevent the model from overfitting the training data.
To encourage sparsity, the algorithm penalizes nonzero coefficients using an L1 regularization term.
Researchers sometimes penalize complex models in experiments, balancing accuracy and simplicity through regularization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regularization - the technique of adding a penalty to a loss function to reduce model complexity and prevent overfitting
  • Synonym: regularize - to apply a penalty term or constraint to a model or objective (to control complexity)

Etymology

From French *pénaliser*, from *pénal* (penal).
