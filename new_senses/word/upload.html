to upload

1. VERB

/ ˈʌpˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌləʊd / (BrE)
to transfer a file or data from a smaller computer or local device to a central computer, server, or the internet
transfer data to a central computer or server

Examples

He uploaded his vacation photos to a shared online album.
I am uploading the new software update to the server right now.
Please upload the completed form to our website before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: download - transfer data from a central computer to a smaller or local device
  • Synonym: transfer - move data from one computer to another

Etymology

Formed in the 1980s by analogy with 'download', from 'up' (direction) + 'load' (to transfer data).

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8\u028cp\u02cclo\u028ad / (AmE), / \u02c8\u028cp\u02ccl\u0259\u028ad / (BrE)
(in science fiction and theoretical discussions) to transfer or copy a person's mind, consciousness, or mental content into a digital system or virtual environment.
transfer a mind into a digital system

Examples

Some futurists imagine humans could upload their minds into a computer simulation.
After the accident she hoped scientists might one day have a way to upload her consciousness.
Researchers discuss the ethical issues raised by uploading a person's memories into a virtual environment.

Synset Examples

After the accident she hoped scientists might one day have a way to upload her consciousness.
Researchers discuss the ethical issues raised by uploading a person's memories into a virtual environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mind uploading - the theoretical process of copying or emulating a human mind in a computer
  • Synonym: whole brain emulation - a proposed technique for replicating the structure and function of a brain in software

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the computing sense 'upload' (transfer data to a computer); applied in late 20th–21st-century futurist and scientific discussion.
