wiper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪpə / (BrE)
a conducting arm in an electrical device that rotates, sliding over a series of contacts to connect with different outlets
rotating electrical contact arm

Examples

Replacing the faulty wiper restored the switch's full functionality.
The wiper in the potentiometer adjusts the resistance as it moves across the contacts.
When the wiper loses contact, the circuit may become intermittent or fail.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪpə / (BrE)
A mechanical device, usually consisting of a blade, used to clear rain, snow, or debris from a vehicle's windshield or window.
device for cleaning windshields

Examples

Every car is equipped with at least one wiper for the windshield.
She replaced the old wiper with a new one to ensure a clear view.
The wiper stopped working, making it hard to see through the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windshield wiper - a device for wiping a vehicle's windshield

Etymology

From 'wipe' + '-er', denoting one who or that which wipes.

/ ˈwaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪpə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to clean machinery, engines, or surfaces by wiping them.
person who cleans by wiping

Examples

Maintenance crews included a wiper whose main task was to clean machinery thoroughly.
The wiper was responsible for ensuring all engine parts were free of oil and grime.
Young recruits started as a wiper before advancing to more skilled positions on the ship.

Related Terms

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈwaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪpə / (BrE)
(in computing) a program or device that securely deletes, overwrites, or destroys data on storage media so the data cannot be recovered.
secure data‑erasing tool

Examples

The IT team used a wiper to sanitize the hard drives before disposal.
A notorious malware called NotPetya acted as a wiper, destroying files across corporate networks overnight.
Before donating the laptop, she ran a certified wiper to ensure personal documents could not be recovered.
