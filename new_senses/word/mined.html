mined

1. ADJ

/ maɪnd / (AmE), / maɪnd / (BrE)
(of minerals, resources, etc.) extracted from the earth or another source, especially by mining.
extracted from the earth by mining

Examples

Much of the region's economy depends on mined copper and other minerals.
The gold was mined from deep within the mountains.
These mined resources are exported to various countries around the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmined - not having been extracted from the earth or source

2. ADJ NEW

/ maɪnd / (AmE), / maɪnd / (BrE)
(of data, information, or insights) obtained by applying data‑mining or automated analysis to large datasets.
obtained by data mining

Examples

The report drew mined insights from years of anonymized user behavior logs.
Our team used several algorithms to turn mined data into actionable marketing strategies.
After processing, the dashboard highlighted key trends found in the mined datasets.

Synset Examples

The report drew mined insights from years of anonymized user behavior logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - to obtain or remove something from a larger whole or source
  • Synonym: data mining - the automated process of discovering patterns and insights in large datasets

Etymology

From the verb 'mine' (to extract); extended in computing from the compound 'data mining' (late 20th century).
