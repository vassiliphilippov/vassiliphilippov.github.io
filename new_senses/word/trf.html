TRF

1. NOUN

/ ti ɑr ɛf / (AmE), / ti ɑː ɛf / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone controlling thyroid-stimulating hormone release
medical

Examples

A deficiency in TRF can lead to symptoms of hypothyroidism.
Researchers measured the levels of TRF in patients with thyroid disorders.
The hypothalamus secretes TRF to regulate hormone balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyrotropin-releasing hormone - a hormone that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone

Etymology

Acronym for Thyrotropin-Releasing Factor.

(in neuroscience) An abbreviation for Temporal Response Function — a model that describes how neural activity tracks or is driven by a continuous stimulus over time.
Temporal Response Function (neuroscience)

Examples

The researchers estimated a linear TRF to map speech envelopes onto EEG signals during listening tasks.
Using cross-validation, she compared different TRF models to predict participants' cortical responses.
Their paper shows that a temporal TRF can reveal how attention changes neural tracking of speech.
