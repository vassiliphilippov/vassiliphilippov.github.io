quiescence

1. NOUN

/ kwiˈɛsəns / (AmE), / kwiˈɛsəns / (BrE)
A state of quietness, inactivity, or restfulness.
state of quiet inactivity or rest

Examples

A brief quiescence in the negotiations allowed both sides to reconsider their positions.
After months of intense activity, the volcano entered a period of quiescence.
The town settled into quiescence as night fell, with hardly a sound to be heard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormancy - a state of reduced activity or suspended growth
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active or in motion

Etymology

From Latin "quiescentia" (being quiet), from "quiescere" (to rest).

3. NOUN NEW

/ kwiˈɛsəns / (AmE), / kwiˈɛsəns / (BrE)
(in astronomy) a state of a galaxy or other celestial system with little or no ongoing star formation, indicating a passive or inactive evolutionary phase.
lack of star formation in a galaxy

Examples

Astronomers measured prolonged quiescence in the galaxy, indicating star formation had effectively stopped for billions of years.
The study compared active and dormant galaxies, finding environmental factors that promote long periods of quiescence.
High central black hole activity is often linked to shutdown of star formation and the onset of quiescence in massive galaxies.
