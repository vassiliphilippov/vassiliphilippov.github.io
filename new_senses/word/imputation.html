imputation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an accusation that someone is guilty of a dishonest or criminal act
accusation of dishonesty or crime

Examples

He strongly objected to the imputation of corruption made against him.
Her reputation suffered as a result of the imputation of theft.
The company issued a statement denying any imputation of unethical behavior.

Synset Examples

he denied the imputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something illegal or wrong
  • Antonym: exoneration - the act of officially absolving someone from blame

Etymology

From Latin imputatio (an attaching, charging), from imputare (to reckon, charge).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpjuːˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌpjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of attributing something, such as a fault or responsibility, to a particular person or cause.
act of attributing responsibility or cause

Examples

Despite the imputation of dishonesty, she maintained her innocence.
He was offended by the imputation that his achievements were not earned through hard work.
The imputation of blame to the entire team was both unfair and demoralizing.

Synset Examples

the imputation that my success was due to nepotism meant that I was not taken seriously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribution - the act of assigning or regarding something as being caused by someone or something
  • Antonym: exoneration - the action of officially absolving someone from blame; opposite of imputation

Etymology

From Latin imputatio (an account, a reckoning), from imputare (to reckon, charge).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɪmˌpjuːˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌpjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(in genetics and statistics) the computational or statistical inference of unobserved genotypes or genetic variants in a dataset by using observed data together with reference panels.
inferring missing genetic data

Examples

The study used imputation to infer missing genetic variants from SNP array data for analysis.
Researchers applied imputation with a large reference panel to improve association study power.
After quality control, we performed imputation, which filled in thousands of unobserved genotypes across samples.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impute - to assign or estimate missing data values using statistical methods
  • Synonym: genotype imputation - the specific method of inferring genotypes at unobserved loci using reference panels

Etymology

From Latin imputatio (an attaching, charging), from imputare (to reckon, charge).
