bloat

1. NOUN

/ bloʊt / (AmE), / bləʊt / (BrE)
A swelling of the rumen or intestinal tract in domestic animals, caused by excessive gas accumulation.
gas swelling in animals

Examples

If bloat is not treated quickly, it can be fatal for cattle.
Sheep can develop bloat if they graze too much on lush, wet pasture.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with bloat after noticing its distended abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tympany - swelling or distention due to gas in a body cavity, especially in animals

2. VERB

/ bloʊt / (AmE), / bləʊt / (BrE)
to become swollen or distended with fluid, gas, or air; to puff up
become swollen or puffed up

Examples

After eating too quickly, his stomach began to bloat painfully.
If you let the dough sit too long, it will bloat and lose its shape.
The dead fish started to bloat after several days in the sun.

Synset Examples

The dead man's stomach was bloated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - to become larger or rounder in size, typically as a result of an accumulation of fluid
  • Antonym: to deflate - to release air or gas from something swollen or inflated

Etymology

From Middle English 'blouten' (to blow up, puff up).

3. VERB

/ bloʊt / (AmE), / bləʊt / (BrE)
to make bloated or swollen; to cause to swell or expand abnormally, especially as a result of fluid, gas, or hunger
cause to swell or become bloated

Examples

Certain medical conditions may bloat your abdomen uncomfortably.
Heavy rainfall can bloat the river until it floods the fields.
Too much salt in the diet can bloat the body and cause discomfort.

Synset Examples

Hunger bloated the child's belly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - to become larger or rounder in size, typically as a result of internal pressure
  • Antonym: to deflate - to cause to lose air or gas and thus decrease in size

Etymology

From Old Norse *blātr* (soft, swollen), related to 'blow'.

4. NOUN NEW

/ bloʊt / (AmE), / bləʊt / (BrE)
Excessive increase in the size, complexity, or resource use of a software program, typically caused by added features, redundant code, or inefficient implementation.
excessive software size or resource use

Examples

Users blamed the update for the bloat that made the app slow and use more battery.
Developers refactored the code to remove unnecessary features and reduce bloat in the system.
The startup's product was praised for being lean, with minimal bloat compared to competitors' offerings.

Synset Examples

Users blamed the update for the bloat that made the app slow and use more battery.
Developers refactored the code to remove unnecessary features and reduce bloat in the system.
The startup's product was praised for being lean, with minimal bloat compared to competitors' offerings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code bloat - excessive or inefficient code that increases program size or reduces performance
  • Synonym: feature creep - the tendency for product scope to expand by adding more features, often causing complexity and delays
  • Antonym: efficiency - the degree to which a program uses resources effectively; the opposite of bloat

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the verb 'bloat' (from Middle English 'blouten', to blow up). Applied to software in the late 20th/21st century.
