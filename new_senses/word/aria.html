aria

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈɑːriə / (BrE)
A long, elaborate song for a solo voice, typically in an opera.
elaborate solo song in opera

Examples

Her favorite aria from Puccini’s Turandot moves her to tears every time.
Students listened carefully to the famous aria as part of their music appreciation class.
The soprano sang a beautiful aria during the second act of the opera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song - a piece of music sung by the voice
  • Antonym: recitative - a style of delivery in opera where the singer adopts rhythms of ordinary speech

Etymology

From Italian aria (air, melody), from Latin aer (air).

2. NOUN NEW

/ eɪ.ɑr.aɪ.eɪ / (AmE), / eɪ.ɑː.aɪ.eɪ / (BrE)
An acronym for Accessible Rich Internet Applications: a set of HTML attributes, roles, and practices used to make dynamic web content and user interfaces accessible to people who use assistive technologies.
web accessibility attributes (ARIA)

Examples

The developer added ARIA attributes to improve screen reader navigation on the website.
Before launch, they tested the product with assistive tech and fixed several ARIA issues.
Most modern frameworks include components that automatically apply appropriate ARIA roles and attributes.

  • Synonym: accessibility - Designing websites and applications so people with disabilities can perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with them.

Etymology

Acronym of Accessible Rich Internet Applications, from the W3C specification developed in the 2000s.
