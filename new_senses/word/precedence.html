precedence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdəns / (BrE)
The condition of coming or occurring before something else in time.
the state of coming before in time

Examples

In history, the fall of the empire took precedence over minor events.
The judge gave precedence to the older case when scheduling hearings.
When two appointments overlap, precedence is given to the one made first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - the fact or condition of being regarded or treated as more important
  • Antonym: succession - the action or process of following in order

Etymology

From Latin 'praecedentia', from 'praecedere' (to go before).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdəns / (BrE)
The condition of being considered more important than someone or something else, so as to come before them in time, order, or rank.
priority in time, order, or rank

Examples

In an emergency, medical needs take precedence over routine paperwork.
Seniority gives her precedence in choosing her vacation dates.
Tradition dictates the order of precedence at the state dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - the fact or condition of being regarded or treated as more important
