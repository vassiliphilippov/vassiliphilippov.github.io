sapience

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪpiəns / (AmE), / ˈseɪpɪəns / (BrE)
the ability to apply knowledge, experience, understanding, and insight; wisdom.
wisdom; ability to apply knowledge and insight

Examples

Her sapience was evident in the advice she gave to younger colleagues.
The philosopher was admired for his sapience and thoughtful insights into life.
We hope that humanity will eventually achieve greater sapience and understanding of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge, understanding, or awareness

Etymology

From Latin sapiens (wise), from sapere (to be wise, to taste).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈseɪpiəns / (AmE), / ˈseɪpɪəns / (BrE)
(in computing and discussions of artificial intelligence) the capacity of an artificial or digital agent to demonstrate wisdom-like judgment, context-aware reasoning, or deliberative decision-making beyond mere data processing.
AI wisdom; machine judgment

Examples

Experts debate whether sapience in AI signals truly autonomous, wise decision-making.
Researchers test chatbots to see if their sapience includes moral judgment and long-term planning.
Regulators worry that claimed machine sapience could incorrectly justify high-stakes autonomous decisions.
