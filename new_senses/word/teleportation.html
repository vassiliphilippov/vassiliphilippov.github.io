teleportation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləpɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪpɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the hypothetical process of instantaneously transporting a person or object from one place to another without traversing the physical space between them
instantaneous transportation of matter by dematerializing and reassembling

Examples

Many science fiction stories feature teleportation as a convenient way for characters to travel instantly.
Researchers have discussed whether teleportation could ever become possible using quantum mechanics.
The villain escaped capture with the help of advanced teleportation technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transportation - the act of moving people or goods from one place to another

Etymology

Formed from 'tele-' (meaning 'distant') and 'portation' (related to carrying or moving), evoking the idea of moving at a distance.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌtɛləpɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛləpɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(in quantum physics and information theory) the transfer of the exact quantum state of a system from one location to another using quantum entanglement together with classical communication, without transferring the physical system itself.
transfer of a quantum state via entanglement

Examples

Scientists achieved successful teleportation of a qubit between two labs using entanglement and classical signals.
The experiment demonstrated that teleportation preserves the exact quantum state without moving the physical particle.
Researchers published a protocol describing long-distance teleportation of photons across a fiber-optic link.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum teleportation - the protocol in quantum information for transferring quantum states using entanglement and classical communication

Etymology

From tele- (distant) + portation (from Latin portare, 'to carry'); applied in modern physics as a technical extension of the original term.
