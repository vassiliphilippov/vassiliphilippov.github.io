watermark

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəmɑːk / (BrE)
a line or mark indicating the highest level reached by a body of water, especially after flooding
mark indicating water's highest level

Examples

After the river flooded, a visible watermark ran along the kitchen walls.
The old bridge still bears a watermark from the great flood of 1920.
You could see the watermark on the rocks, showing how high the lake once rose.

Etymology

From 'water' + 'mark'; originally referring to a mark left by water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəmɑːk / (BrE)
a faint design or pattern impressed into paper during manufacture, which is visible when the paper is held up to the light and serves as a distinguishing mark
faint mark in paper visible when held to light

Examples

He checked the banknote for its watermark to make sure it was not counterfeit.
High-quality stationery often contains a subtle watermark that identifies the manufacturer.
The document was authenticated by holding it up to the light to reveal the watermark.

Etymology

From water + mark, originally referring to the mark left by a wet stamp during papermaking.

identifying mark in digital media

Examples

The photographer embedded a digital watermark in the photo to deter copying.
Many stock-photo sites display a visible watermark until users purchase a licence to download.
Researchers use invisible watermark techniques to track unauthorised distribution of audio and video files.
