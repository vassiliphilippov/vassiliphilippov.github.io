parasite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpærəsaɪt / (BrE)
an animal or plant that lives in or on another organism (its host) and obtains nourishment from it, typically without benefiting or killing the host
organism living off another

Examples

Mistletoe is a parasite that grows on the branches of many trees.
Tapeworms are a type of parasite that can inhabit the intestines of animals.
The malaria parasite is transmitted to humans by mosquitoes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: host - organism that a parasite lives in or on

Etymology

From Greek parasitos, meaning 'person eating at another's table'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ə.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.saɪt / (BrE)
a person who habitually relies on or exploits others and gives nothing in return, often for personal gain
a person who exploits others for gain

Examples

He was accused of being a parasite who contributed nothing but always took advantage of others.
She realized too late that her so-called friend was just a parasite living off her generosity.
The group attracted several parasites hoping to profit from the leader's success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponger - a person who lives at others' expense
  • Synonym: freeloader - someone who takes advantage of others' generosity
  • Antonym: benefactor - someone who gives help or support

Etymology

From Latin 'parasitus', from Greek 'parasitos' (person who eats at another's table).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpærəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpærəsaɪt / (BrE)
(in computing, especially cybersecurity) a program or piece of code that attaches to or runs within another program, system, or file without authorization, often to persist, spread, or steal data.
unauthorized program attached to a host

Examples

Security researchers detected a parasite inside the mobile app that secretly exfiltrated user data.
The forensic tool managed to remove several parasites that had attached themselves to the host process.
During analysis she found a parasite that injected code into every running instance of the service.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malware - software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise computers or networks
  • Synonym: virus - a type of malicious program that can replicate and spread by attaching to other programs or files

Etymology

From Greek parasitos, 'person eating at another's table'; used metaphorically for software that lives on a host program or system.
