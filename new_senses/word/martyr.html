martyr

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
A person who chooses to suffer or die rather than renounce their religious beliefs.
person who dies for religious beliefs

Examples

She was remembered as a martyr who refused to renounce her religion.
The story of the martyr inspired generations to stay true to their beliefs.
Throughout history, many have become martyrs for their faith.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: saint - a person acknowledged as holy or virtuous

Etymology

From Greek 'martys', 'martyr-' meaning 'witness'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
to put someone to death or otherwise punish them because of their beliefs, especially for religious faith; to make a martyr of someone.
to kill for one's beliefs

Examples

He was martyred for standing up to oppression in his country.
Many saints were martyred for refusing to renounce their faith.
The regime martyred countless dissidents during the revolution.

Synset Examples

Saint Sebastian was martyred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execute - to kill someone as a punishment, especially by law

Etymology

From Old French *martir*, from Late Latin *martyr*, from Ancient Greek *martur* (witness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01607942-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
a person who suffers greatly or is killed because of their beliefs or principles, often for a religious or political cause
person who suffers or dies for their beliefs

Examples

He became a martyr to the cause of freedom after enduring years in prison.
Joan of Arc is regarded as a martyr for her faith and courage.
Some people see her as a martyr, while others view her actions more critically.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • Degree Lower: victim - a person harmed or killed as a result of an event, accident, or crime
  • Antonym: persecutor - a person who treats someone cruelly because of their beliefs

Etymology

From Greek *martys* (witness), through Late Latin *martyr*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10316279-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈmɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
to torture or torment someone, especially in a way reminiscent of a martyr; to cause to suffer greatly, often for a belief or principle
to torture or torment greatly

Examples

She felt as though fate itself sought to martyr her for her convictions.
The novelist described how the hero was martyred by those who could not accept his belief.
The regime continued to martyr its opponents, causing international outrage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain as a punishment or to force information
  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Old French *martir*, from Latin *martyr*, from Ancient Greek *martys* (witness).

5. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
Someone who exaggerates or pretends to suffer, especially to gain sympathy, praise, or special treatment.
person who seeks sympathy by feigning suffering
informal

Examples

At the team meeting he acted like a martyr, complaining about extra tasks to win sympathy.
She always plays the martyr when others get praise, hoping someone will comfort her.
Stop acting like a martyr; exaggerating your pain won't change their decision.
