to reseed

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈsiːd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsiːd / (BrE)
(of plants) to reproduce by producing and dispersing seeds, often allowing new plants to grow without human intervention.
plants reproduce by self-seeding

Examples

If left undisturbed, some vegetables will reseed and return year after year.
Perennial herbs such as dill can easily reseed in most climates.
The wildflowers in her garden often reseed themselves every spring, filling the beds with color.

Synset Examples

Some plants reseed themselves indefinitely

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) + 'seed'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01187681-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈsid / (AmE), / riːˈsiːd / (BrE)
to plant seed in (an area) again or anew
to plant seed again

Examples

After the harsh winter, the field needed to be reseeded to restore the grass.
The company plans to reseed the park with native wildflowers next spring.
You should reseed the lawn every few years for a healthier appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seed - to plant seeds in (an area) for the first time

Etymology

From re- (again) + seed (to plant seeds).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01504214-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ riˈsid / (AmE), / riːˈsiːd / (BrE)
(in computing) to give a new seed value to a random-number generator or entropy pool so it restarts or refreshes its sequence of random values.
give a new seed to an RNG

Examples

After detecting low entropy, the daemon automatically reseeded the generator to improve randomness.
You should reseed the pseudo-random number generator periodically in long-running simulations to avoid bias.
Reseeding the entropy pool requires collecting new hardware randomness before creating cryptographic keys.

Synset Examples

After detecting low entropy, the daemon automatically reseeded the generator to improve randomness.
You should reseed the pseudo-random number generator periodically in long-running simulations to avoid bias.
Reseeding the entropy pool requires collecting new hardware randomness before creating cryptographic keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed (computing) - to provide an initial value (seed) that determines the output sequence of a random-number generator
  • Synonym: initialize - to set a system or device to a starting state, often by giving initial values

Etymology

From re- (again) + seed.
