diagnostic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəgˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
(of a symptom or sign) characteristic or indicative of a particular disease or condition.
indicative of a disease or condition
medical

Examples

A persistent cough can be diagnostic of lung problems in some patients.
The presence of this antibody is highly diagnostic of the infection.
This combination of symptoms is diagnostic for the rare disorder.

Synset Examples

a diagnostic sign of yellow fever
a rash symptomatic of scarlet fever
symptomatic of insanity
a rise in crime symptomatic of social breakdown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symptomatic - showing symptoms of a disease, indicative

Etymology

From Greek diagnōstikos (able to distinguish).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəgˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
concerned with identifying the presence or nature of a condition, especially in medicine or testing.
related to diagnosis or identifying problems
medical

Examples

A diagnostic exam can reveal early signs of disease before symptoms appear.
The specialist ordered several diagnostic tests to determine the cause of her symptoms.
This software provides diagnostic information to help identify system errors quickly.

Synset Examples

a diagnostic reading test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnostic - a tool or test used for diagnosis (noun sense)
  • Antonym: therapeutic - concerned with the treatment of disease

Etymology

From Greek 'diagnōstikos' from 'diagnōsis' (discernment, diagnosis).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02987083-a
Synset POS: a
