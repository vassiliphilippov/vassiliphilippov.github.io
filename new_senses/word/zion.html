Zion

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ən / (BrE)
An imaginary place regarded as perfect or ideal, often used to refer to a utopia or heaven.
ideal or perfect place; utopia

Examples

Authors throughout history have created visions of Zion in their works of literature.
He devoted his life to making the city into a modern Zion.
Many dreamers have longed for a Zion where justice and peace prevail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined place or state of things in which everything is perfect
  • Synonym: paradise - a place or condition of great happiness where everything is exactly as you would like it to be

Etymology

From Hebrew *Ṣīyōn*, originally a hill in Jerusalem, later symbolic of the Promised Land or heavenly city.

2. PROPN

/ ˈzaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ən / (BrE)
an ancient hill in Jerusalem, originally a stronghold captured by King David, later used as a poetic or symbolic name for Jerusalem, especially in religious texts
biblical name for a hill, later Jerusalem

Examples

In many hymns, Zion represents a place of peace and spiritual renewal.
Pilgrims from many countries visit Zion each year as part of their religious journey.
The ancient prophets lamented the destruction of Zion in their writings.

Synset Examples

the inhabitants of Jerusalem are personified as ‘the daughter of Zion’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jerusalem - the ancient city central to several major religions

Etymology

From Hebrew Tsiyyon (צִיּוֹן‎), originally meaning 'castle' or 'fortress'.

3. PROPN NEW

/ ˈzaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ən / (BrE)
(in The Matrix films and related works) the last human city, a subterranean refuge and center of resistance against the Machines.
fictional last human city (The Matrix)

Examples

The crew fought their way to Zion, the last human city beneath the ruined Earth's surface.
In the final battle, ships streamed toward Zion to defend humanity from the Machine army.
Fans speculate about life in Zion, imagining its culture, politics, and daily struggles underground.
