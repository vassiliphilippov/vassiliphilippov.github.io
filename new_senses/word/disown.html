to disown

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈoʊn / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊn / (BrE)
To refuse to acknowledge, accept, or have any connection with someone or something; to reject as one's own.
refuse to acknowledge or reject relationship

Examples

After years of disagreements, his father finally disowned him.
She felt so betrayed that she decided to disown her former friend.
The company threatened to disown any product that failed to meet safety standards.

Synset Examples

She renounced her husband
The parents repudiated their son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repudiate - to refuse to accept or be associated with

Etymology

From Old French 'desavouer' (to disavow), from Latin prefix 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'own' (to possess).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈoʊn / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊn / (BrE)
to deliberately prevent (someone, especially an heir) from inheriting, often by legal means such as in a will
prevent from inheriting on purpose

Examples

By choosing to disown her grandchildren, she ensured they would receive nothing from her estate.
She decided to disown her nephew in her will after years of estrangement.
The wealthy businessman threatened to disown his son if he continued with his reckless lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinherit - to prevent deliberately from inheriting, especially by a legal act

Etymology

From prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'own.', originally meaning to refuse to acknowledge as one's own.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02321077-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ dɪsˈoʊn / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊn / (BrE)
(in computing, especially Unix-like shells) to remove a background job from the shell's job table so it is no longer controlled by the shell and can continue running after you log out.
detach a background job in a shell

Examples

After starting the background server, he ran disown so the process survived his logout.
She left the large download running and used disown to prevent it stopping when closing the terminal.
The instructor asked students to start a job and then disown it to keep it running after they disconnected.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detach - to separate or release something from control or connection
  • Synonym: disassociate - to remove an association or connection

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'disown' (to reject or remove ownership), applied to removing a process from a shell's control.
