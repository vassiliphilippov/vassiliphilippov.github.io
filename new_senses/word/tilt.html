tilt

1. NOUN

/ tɪlt / (AmE), / tɪlt / (BrE)
a slant or inclination away from a vertical or horizontal position
a slant or inclination

Examples

After the earthquake, the building's tilt was detected by engineers.
The old chair had a noticeable tilt to the left side.
You could see the tilt of the picture frame from across the room.

Synset Examples

informal

Examples

After the bad call, he went on tilt and started making risky, impulsive plays in the tournament.
Coaches teach players to stop and breathe before strong emotions push them into tilt during matches.
The poker pro explained how small losses can slowly build up to a dangerous tilt affecting decision-making.
