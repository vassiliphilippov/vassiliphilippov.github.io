unification

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of bringing things or people together to form a single unit or whole
the process of being united or made one

Examples

European unification has led to closer economic and political ties.
The unification of East and West Germany was a historic event.
The unification of the departments improved communication within the company.

Synset Examples

there is strength in union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the action or fact of joining or being joined
  • Antonym: division - the action of separating or being separated into parts

Etymology

From Late Latin unificare (to make one), from Latin unus (one) + facere (to make).
