mulberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈmʌlbəri / (BrE)
A sweet fruit, usually dark purple and resembling a blackberry, produced by trees of the genus Morus.
dark purple blackberry-like fruit

Examples

Children stained their hands purple while gathering mulberry from the bushes.
She picked a ripe mulberry from the tree and ate it immediately.
The pie was filled with sweet mulberry and served warm with cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - a soft black or dark purple fruit similar to the mulberry

Etymology

From Latin morus (mulberry tree), via Old English morberie.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈmʌlbəri / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Morus, whose edible fruit resembles the blackberry.
tree with blackberry-like edible fruit

Examples

She made a delicious jam from fresh mulberry berries.
Silkworms feed on the leaves of the mulberry plant.
The mulberry tree in their yard produces sweet fruit every summer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blackberry - a soft, edible small dark purple fruit
  • Synonym: fig - a sweet, edible fruit from the ficus tree

Etymology

From Latin morus (mulberry tree), via Old English morberie.
