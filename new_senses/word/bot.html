bot

1. NOUN

/ bɑt / (AmE), / bɒt / (BrE)
the larva of a botfly, typically developing inside the body of a horse, sheep, or sometimes a human
larva of a botfly

Examples

A single bot can cause significant discomfort to its host animal.
Sheep can suffer from internal parasites such as the bot.
The veterinarian removed a bot from the horse's stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are called bots and grow inside mammals

Etymology

From Middle English 'botte' (botfly larva), possibly of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN NEW

/ bɑt / (AmE), / bɒt / (BrE)
An automated software program or online account that performs tasks, provides information, or interacts with users without continuous human control, often on the internet or in networks.
automated software agent or online account

Examples

The bot on the company's site answered my question within seconds.
Thousands of fake bots were created to influence opinions during the election.
Developers used a bot to automatically schedule maintenance messages for the user group.

Etymology

Clipped from robot (coined from Czech robota ‘forced labour’); shortened in computing to refer to automated programs.
