mastery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəri / (BrE)
control or power to dominate, rule, or defeat someone or something
power to dominate or control

Examples

After years of conflict, the general achieved mastery over his rivals.
The country’s mastery of the region was unchallenged for decades.
Their mastery of the seas allowed them to expand their empire rapidly.

Synset Examples

mastery of the seas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - the ability or control over something
  • Synonym: dominance - the state of having power and influence over others
  • Antonym: submission - the act of yielding to the authority of another

Etymology

From Old French 'maistrie', from Latin 'magister' (master).

/ ˈmæstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəri / (BrE)
great skill or knowledge in a particular subject or activity; complete control or command of something
great skill or complete command

Examples

His mastery in mathematics impressed his professors and peers alike.
She achieved mastery of the piano after years of relentless practice.

/ ˈmæstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəri / (BrE)
(in gaming and on online platforms) a digital badge, rank, or indicator awarded to a player to show advanced skill, achievement, or high level of performance in a particular game, character, or activity.
gaming badge indicating proficiency

Examples

He earned the mastery badge after reaching the top tier in the online strategy game.
The tournament required participants to show a mastery emblem on their profiles before signing up.
After the update, players could buy cosmetic items for each mastery rank they had unlocked.
