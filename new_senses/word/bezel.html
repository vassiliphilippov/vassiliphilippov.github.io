bezel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛzəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛzəl / (BrE)
A sloping or angled edge, especially on a cutting tool, forming the sharp part used for cutting or shaping material.
sloping edge on a cutting tool

Examples

Carpenters often check the angle of the bezel before starting their work.
He adjusted the bezel to achieve a cleaner cut on the metal sheet.
The chisel's bezel must be kept sharp for precise woodworking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge - the cutting border of a blade or tool

Etymology

From French 'biseau' (sloping edge), possibly from Old French 'besel' (sloping edge of a tool).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbɛzəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛzəl / (BrE)
The rim, frame, or border surrounding the face of a device or covering a screen or display, often visible around monitors, smartphones, tablets, or watches.
rim or frame around a screen

Examples

The new smartphone has a thinner bezel that makes the display look almost edge-to-edge.
She replaced the cracked watch bezel with a stainless-steel rim at the repair shop.
Reducing the monitor's bezel improved immersion during gaming and allowed a sleeker desk setup.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frame - a structure that surrounds or supports something, such as a screen or picture
  • Synonym: rim - the outer edge or border of something
  • Synonym: border - a line or strip that marks the outer limit of an area or object

Etymology

From French 'biseau' (sloping edge); later extended from the sense 'sloping edge' to mean the rim or frame around gems, watches, and modern electronic displays.
