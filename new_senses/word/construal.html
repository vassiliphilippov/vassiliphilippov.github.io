construal

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstruːəl / (AmE), / kənˈstruːəl / (BrE)
an interpretation or explanation of the meaning of something; the act of construing
interpretation or explanation

Examples

Her unique construal of the poem revealed new layers of meaning.
Psychologists study how individual construals influence people's responses to events.
The legal team's construal of the contract differed from that of the judge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or translating something

Etymology

From construe + -al, modeled on analogous forms such as 'refusal'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ kənˈstruːəl / (AmE), / kənˈstruːəl / (BrE)
(in psychology) a mental representation or way of mentally framing an object, event, or action, usually characterized by its level of abstraction (e.g., concrete vs. abstract).
psychological mental representation (abstract vs. concrete)

Examples

Researchers measured how temporal distance changes participants' construal of future events during the experiment.
An abstract construal makes people focus on goals, while a concrete one makes them plan details.
Therapists trained students to identify different construals clients used when describing their problems.

  • Degree Lower: concreteness - a low-level, specific way of representing events or objects (opposite of abstract construal)
  • Degree Higher: abstraction - a higher-level, more general mental representation or summary of details
