shipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the business or activity of transporting goods and materials from one place to another, especially by sea, land, or air
transporting goods commercially

Examples

Due to bad weather, shipping between the two ports was temporarily suspended.
Rising fuel costs have affected the entire shipping industry this year.
The company specializes in international shipping of electronics and consumer goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft
  • Synonym: transportation - the action of transporting someone or something or the process of being transported

Etymology

From 'ship' + '-ing', originally referring to transport by ship; later broadened to all commercial transport of goods.

/ ˈʃɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the ships of a country or industry collectively, or the business or activity of transporting goods by sea
the ships of a country or industry collectively

Examples

British shipping dominated international trade during the nineteenth century.
The government imposed new regulations on foreign shipping entering its waters.
The port was crowded with shipping from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleet - a group of ships sailing together or under the same ownership

Etymology

From ship + -ing, referring originally to the act or business of sending goods by sea.

(in fan communities) the practice or activity of supporting, imagining, or promoting a romantic relationship between fictional characters; also the community interest in particular pairings.
fan support for romantic pairings
informal slang

Examples

Many viewers enjoy shipping the two main characters despite the show's ambiguous ending.
She joined an online forum dedicated to shipping minor characters into unexpected romantic plots.
Although controversial, shipping fan art can boost a series' popularity and fan engagement.
