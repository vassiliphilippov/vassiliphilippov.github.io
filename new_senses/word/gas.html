gas

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs / (AmE), / ɡæs / (BrE)
A substance in a state of matter in which it has no fixed shape and fills any container uniformly, distinguished by low density and the ability to expand and contract with temperature and pressure changes.
substance in the gaseous state of matter

Examples

Carbon dioxide is the gas we exhale when we breathe.
Oxygen is a gas at room temperature, while water is a liquid.
The balloon inflated as more gas was pumped inside.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - state of matter with fixed shape and volume
  • Antonym: liquid - state of matter with fixed volume but no fixed shape

Etymology

From Greek 'khaos' (chaos), adopted in the 17th century to describe formless substances.

7. NOUN

/ ɡæs / (AmE), / ɡæs / (BrE)
A substance in a state in which it has neither a fixed shape nor volume and expands freely to fill any space available.
substance in gaseous state

Examples

Helium, a gas, is often used to fill balloons so they float.
Oxygen is an example of a gas that we need to breathe.
Water vapor is simply water in the gas phase.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid - a state of matter with definite volume but no fixed shape
  • Antonym: solid - a state of matter with a fixed shape and volume

Etymology

From Dutch 'gas', coined in the 17th century by chemist Jan Baptist van Helmont, possibly related to Greek 'khaos' (chaos).
