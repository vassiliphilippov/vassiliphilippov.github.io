to listen

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪsən / (AmE), / ˈlɪsən / (BrE)
to give attention with the ear; to pay attention to sound in order to hear or understand something
pay attention in order to hear

Examples

He refused to listen to any advice, convinced he was right.
Please listen carefully to the instructions before starting the exam.
The children sat quietly and listened to the story their teacher was telling.

Synset Examples

Listen to your father
We must hear the expert before we make a decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hear - perceive with the ear

Etymology

From Old English *hlysnan* (to pay attention to sound).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪsən / (AmE), / ˈlɪsən / (BrE)
to give one's attention to sound; to hear something with intention or concentration
hear with intention

Examples

He was so focused that he didn't listen to anyone else's opinion.
Please listen carefully to all the instructions before you begin.
She stopped to listen to the birds singing in the trees.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hear - perceive with the ear without necessarily paying attention

3. VERB

/ ˈlɪs.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɪs.ən / (BrE)
to pay close attention to or take notice of something, especially advice or warnings
pay close attention; take notice of

Examples

He refused to listen to the warnings, which led to trouble later.
If you listen carefully to your teachers, you will learn much more.
You should listen to your doctor's advice if you want to recover quickly.

Synset Examples

Heed the advice of the old men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heed - to pay attention to and consider advice, warnings, or information

Etymology

From Old English *hlysnan* (to listen, pay attention).
