owned

1. ADJ

/ oʊnd / (AmE), / əʊnd / (BrE)
having an owner; used to describe something that is possessed, often in combination with another word (e.g., state-owned, privately owned)
having an owner; possessed

Examples

All owned vehicles must be registered with local authorities.
Several government-owned companies have recently been privatized.
The owned portion of the land was marked with clear boundary signs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possessed - owned; held as property
  • Antonym: unowned - not having an owner

2. ADJ NEW

/ oʊnd / (AmE), / əʊnd / (BrE)
(slang) completely defeated, humiliated, or dominated by someone, especially online or in games.
completely defeated (slang)
slang informal

Examples

In the final match, the rookie was completely owned by the much stronger opponent.
When his joke failed, he felt publicly owned and walked away from the conversation.
After the debate, several commenters said the candidate had been owned in the exchange.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeated - beaten in a contest or competition
  • Synonym: humiliated - made to feel ashamed or foolish, especially publicly
  • Antonym: victorious - having won a victory; opposite of being defeated

Etymology

From the verb own (originally 'to possess'); the slang sense 'to own' meaning 'to defeat or humiliate' arose in online and gaming communities in the late 1990s–early 2000s.
