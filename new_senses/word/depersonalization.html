depersonalization

1. NOUN

/ diˌpɝːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of regarding or treating a person as a thing, especially by removing their individuality or personal qualities
treating a person as an object, removing individuality

Examples

Critics argued that the company's policies led to the depersonalization of its employees.
Excessive bureaucracy often results in the depersonalization of patients in healthcare settings.
The film explores themes of depersonalization in modern urban life.

Synset Examples

according to Marx, treating labor as a commodity exemplified the reification of the individual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectification - the act of treating a person as an object or thing, especially sexually

Etymology

From de- (removal) + personal + -ization.

2. NOUN

/ diˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a psychological condition in which a person feels detached from their own thoughts, feelings, or sense of identity, often experiencing a sense of unreality or strangeness
feeling of detachment from one's self
medical

Examples

After a traumatic experience, John often struggled with depersonalization and felt disconnected from his emotions.
People experiencing depersonalization may describe feeling as if they are outside their own bodies.
Prolonged stress and anxiety can sometimes trigger episodes of depersonalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derealization - a feeling that the external world is unreal or strange

Etymology

Formed from 'de-' (removal) + 'personal' + '-ization'; first used in psychology in the early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ diˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state in which a person feels a loss of personal identity or a sense of being detached from oneself, often feeling like an anonymous part of an impersonal system.
loss of personal identity; detachment from self

Examples

After months in the corporate world, he began to experience depersonalization and felt like just another number.
Feelings of depersonalization can be common during times of extreme stress or trauma.
The novel explores themes of depersonalization in a society obsessed with conformity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alienation - a feeling of isolation or estrangement
  • Antonym: personalization - the process of making something personal, the opposite of depersonalization

Etymology

From de- (removal) + personal + -ization, influenced by French dépersonnalisation.
