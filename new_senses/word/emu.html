emu

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːmuː / (AmE), / ˈiːmjuː / (BrE)
a large, flightless bird of Australia, similar to an ostrich but smaller, with long legs and neck, and soft brown feathers
large Australian flightless bird

Examples

An emu can run at speeds of up to 50 kilometers per hour.
Farmers in Australia sometimes raise emus for their meat and oil.
The emu is the second-largest living bird by height, after the ostrich.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a large, flightless bird native to Africa; the world's largest bird

Etymology

From an early Portuguese or Arabic word for large birds, adopted via later scientific Latin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌɛmˈjuː / (AmE), / ˈiːˌɛmˈjuː / (BrE)
a system of units used to measure electrical and magnetic quantities, based on the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) system; also an abbreviation for electromagnetic unit.
system of units for electricity and magnetism

Examples

In older physics textbooks, electric current is often given in emu values.
Scientists sometimes use the emu system instead of SI units when measuring magnetic fields.
The emu is based on the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) system for electrical measurements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: SI unit - International System of Units, modern standard set of units

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'electromagnetic unit'.
