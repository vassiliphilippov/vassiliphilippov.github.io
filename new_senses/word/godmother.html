godmother

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌmʌðə / (BrE)
a woman who acts as a sponsor at a child's baptism, often taking an interest in the child's upbringing and religious education
female sponsor at baptism

Examples

My aunt was honored to be chosen as my godmother.
She invited her godmother to her graduation ceremony.
The godmother promised to guide the child in moral and spiritual matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godfather - a man who sponsors a child at baptism

Etymology

From god + mother, modeled on earlier godfather; ultimately from Old English godmōdor.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0261\u0251d\u02ccm\u028c\u00f0\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0261\u0252d\u02ccm\u028c\u00f0\u0259 / (BrE)
a woman who acts as an influential supporter, patron, or mentor of a person, project, movement, or creative field; usually an honorary or secular title rather than a religious role.
female patron or honorary mentor

Examples

She was hailed as the godmother of the indie film scene for mentoring many directors.
The tech investor became the startup's unofficial godmother, providing funding and key introductions.
At the award ceremony they called her the godmother of the movement for her lifelong support.

Synset Examples

From god + mother, modeled on the religious role of a baptismal sponsor; later used figuratively as an honorary patron.
