punk

1. NOUN

/ pʌŋk / (AmE), / pʌŋk / (BrE)
a dry, spongy substance from certain decaying woody materials used as tinder for starting a fire
material used as tinder for fire

Examples

He gathered punk to use as tinder before lighting the campfire.
The campers used some punk from a fallen log to light their fire.
You can easily start a flame if you have dry punk and a match.

Etymology

Possibly from Algonquian (Massachusett) *pôcôno*, meaning 'dust' or 'rotten wood'.

/ pʌŋk / (AmE), / pʌŋk / (BrE)
a young man, often aggressive or violent, involved in criminal or antisocial behavior
aggressive or violent young criminal
slang

Examples

He used to be a punk running with a street gang, but now he has turned his life around.
Some punk tried to steal her bike, but the neighbors caught him.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ pʌŋk / (AmE), / pʌŋk / (BrE)
a style of rock music characterized by short, fast-paced songs, often with aggressive or angry lyrics and a rebellious attitude, typically expressing social alienation or protest
style of rock music with rebellious lyrics

Examples

Many famous bands in the late 1970s played punk to challenge social norms.
She started listening to punk because of its raw energy and rebellious spirit.
The club hosts weekly punk concerts that attract fans from all over town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punk rock - a subgenre of punk music, emphasizing simplicity and raw energy
  • Antonym: progressive rock - a style of rock music that punk reacted against, characterized by complex compositions

Etymology

Originally American slang (early 20th century), later applied to the music genre in the 1970s.
