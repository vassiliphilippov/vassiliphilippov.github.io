to tessellate

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛs.əˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛs.ɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of shapes) to fit together in a repeated pattern without gaps or overlapping.
fit together in a repeated pattern

Examples

Hexagons easily tessellate to form a honeycomb pattern.
Some patterns cannot tessellate across a plane without leaving gaps.
The artist used squares that tessellate perfectly, leaving no spaces between them.

Synset Examples

triangles tessellate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tile - to cover a surface with tiles
  • Synonym: interlock - to fit or be fitted together securely

Etymology

From Latin *tessellatus* (made of small cubes), from *tessella* (small cube, for mosaic work).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛsɪleɪt / (BrE)
to cover a surface with small, usually square tiles or shapes, fitting them together in a repeated pattern without gaps or overlaps
cover with tiles in a repeated pattern

Examples

Ancient Romans would tessellate floors to create beautiful geometric patterns.
The artist decided to tessellate the bathroom walls with colorful mosaic tiles.
You can tessellate your pathway with hexagonal stones for a unique effect.

Synset Examples

tessellate the kitchen floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tile - to cover a surface with tiles

Etymology

From Latin tessellatus, from tessella (a small cube, tile), diminutive of tessera (a square tile).

(in computer graphics) to subdivide a polygonal mesh or surface into smaller polygons (triangles or quads) to increase geometric detail or allow smoother rendering, often via dedicated algorithms or hardware tessellation.
subdivide polygonal mesh for rendering

Examples

The renderer will tessellate the character's mesh to add fine detail during animation.
By enabling hardware tessellation, the game engine tessellates terrain meshes for higher-resolution geometry.
Artists often use displacement maps while tessellating models to create realistic surface wrinkles and folds.
