hygiene

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.dʒin / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.dʒiːn / (BrE)
the science or practices concerned with the prevention of illness and the maintenance of health, especially through cleanliness
science of health and cleanliness

Examples

Advances in public hygiene have greatly improved health standards worldwide.
Good hygiene is essential in preventing the spread of infectious diseases.
Many schools now teach children about personal hygiene from an early age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanitation - measures to protect public health, especially the provision of clean water and proper sewage disposal

Etymology

From French *hygiène*, from Greek *hygieinē (techne)*, meaning (art) of health.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdʒin / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdʒiːn / (BrE)
conditions or practices conducive to maintaining health and preventing disease, especially through cleanliness.
practice or condition of cleanliness for health

Examples

Good hygiene is essential to prevent the spread of infectious diseases.
Poor hygiene in the kitchen can lead to food poisoning.
She teaches her children about the importance of personal hygiene.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
