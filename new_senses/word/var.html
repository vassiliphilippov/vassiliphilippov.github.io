var

1. NOUN

/ vɑr / (AmE), / vɑː / (BrE)
a unit of reactive power in an alternating current circuit, equal to the power dissipated when one volt produces a current of one ampere (symbol: var)
unit of reactive power in AC circuits

Examples

A low var value can indicate problems with reactive power in electrical networks.
Engineers often calculate the var to assess power efficiency in AC systems.
The generator supplies 500 vars to the circuit under load.

Etymology

Acronym from 'volt-ampere reactive'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ viː eɪ ɑɹ / (AmE), / viː eɪ ɑː / (BrE)
(in football (soccer)) an official or a system that assists the on-field referee by reviewing incidents using video replays and advising on decisions.
Video Assistant Referee (football)

Examples

Late in the game the referee checked the VAR before awarding the penalty.
After a long review, the VAR overturned the goal for an offside violation.
Fans argued that the VAR ruined the flow of the match, but referees defended it.
