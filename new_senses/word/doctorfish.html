doctorfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of surgeonfish found in the West Indies, known for the sharp spines on its tail.
West Indian surgeonfish

Examples

Local fishermen in the Caribbean often catch doctorfish for both food and aquariums.
Snorkeling in the shallow reefs, we spotted a brightly colored doctorfish among the corals.
The doctorfish is easily recognized by the sharp spines near its tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgeonfish - marine fish with sharp scalpel-like spines on the tail

A small freshwater fish (especially Garra rufa) kept in spas to feed on and remove dead skin from human feet; used in fish pedicures.
freshwater spa fish used in pedicures

Examples

At the popular spa she tried a doctorfish pedicure to soften dry skin on her feet.
Regulators warned about hygiene concerns after several salons offered doctorfish treatments without proper sterilization.
Tourists flocked to the clinic where tiny doctorfish gently nibble dead skin from their heels.
