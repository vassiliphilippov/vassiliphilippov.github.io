to unlearn

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈlɝn / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɜːn / (BrE)
to intentionally discard or forget knowledge, habits, or beliefs previously acquired
intentionally discard learned knowledge or habits

Examples

It can be hard to unlearn old habits built up over many years.
She worked hard to unlearn the biases she grew up with.
Sometimes, you must unlearn what you think you know to make progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forget - to be unable to remember something
  • Antonym: to learn - to gain knowledge or skill by studying, practicing, or experiencing something

Etymology

From un- (denoting reversal) + learn, first used in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00600024-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈlɜrn / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɜːn / (BrE)
to deliberately let go of knowledge, habits, or behaviors acquired in the past, especially when they are no longer useful or appropriate
forget or discard learned habits or knowledge

Examples

It can be difficult to unlearn habits that have been with you for years.
She had to unlearn her old way of thinking to adapt to the new job.
Through therapy, he is slowly unlearning negative patterns from his childhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to learn - to gain knowledge or acquire skill in something

Etymology

From Old English 'un-' (not) + 'learn', patterned after late Latin 'dediscere' (to unlearn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00599897-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ ʌnˈlɜrn / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɜːn / (BrE)
(in computing, especially machine learning) to remove or reverse information, patterns, or the influence of specific training data from a model so it no longer retains or reproduces that learned content.
remove learned information from a model

Examples

To comply with the request, engineers plan to unlearn the user's name from the model.
After the breach, the team unlearned all training examples containing personal data to protect privacy.
Researchers are experimenting with algorithms that can quickly unlearn outdated patterns without full retraining.
