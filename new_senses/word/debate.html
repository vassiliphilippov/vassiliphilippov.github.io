debate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈbeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to discuss something formally or argue about different opinions, often in a structured way
to discuss or argue about something

Examples

The two candidates debated the issues on television last night.
They debated for hours without reaching an agreement.
We are still debating whether to accept the offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discuss - talk about something with others, especially in order to exchange ideas or reach a decision
  • Synonym: argue - give reasons for or against something, sometimes in a heated way

Etymology

From Old French 'debatre', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'battuere' (to fight).

6. NOUN NEW

/ dəˈbeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
(in AI and machine-learning) a research method in which two computational agents present opposing arguments about a question so that a judge or moderator can choose the better answer, used to surface evidence and improve model reliability.
AI research method: argument between agents

Examples

The research team staged a controlled debate between two transformer models to evaluate their claims.
A human judge reviewed each debate and decided which agent's justification was clearer and more accurate.
To test reliability, the lab ran dozens of debate sessions where agents had to cite supporting evidence.
