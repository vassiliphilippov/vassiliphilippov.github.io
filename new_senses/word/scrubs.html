scrubs

1. NOUN

/ skrʌbz / (AmE), / skrʌbz / (BrE)
loose-fitting protective garments, typically a shirt and trousers, worn by medical staff, especially surgeons, during operations or in a healthcare setting
medical clothing worn by healthcare staff
medical

Examples

All the doctors changed into their scrubs before entering the operating room.
Nurses usually wear colored scrubs to differentiate their departments.
She forgot her scrubs at home and had to borrow a set from the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gown - a long protective garment worn by surgeons
  • Synonym: lab coat - a white coat worn by laboratory workers or medical staff

Etymology

Shortened from 'scrub suit', referring to the act of scrubbing before surgery.

inexperienced or unskilled people (slang)
slang informal
