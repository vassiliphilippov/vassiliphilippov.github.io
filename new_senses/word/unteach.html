to unteach

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtitʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtiːtʃ / (BrE)
to cause someone to unlearn something, especially a habit, belief, or method
cause to unlearn something

Examples

It is difficult to unteach bad habits once they are deeply ingrained.
She tried to unteach her dog the tricks it had learned incorrectly.
The coach had to unteach the players their old, ineffective methods before introducing new techniques.

Synset Examples

unteach somebody old habits or methods

Related Terms

  • Antonym: teach - to impart knowledge or skill
  • Synonym: to unlearn - to discard knowledge or a habit from memory

Etymology

From prefix un- (reverse) + teach; modeled after "unlearn".

WordNet ID: oewn-00832159-v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈtitʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtiːtʃ / (BrE)
to cause someone to forget or discard knowledge or beliefs previously learned, especially by teaching the contrary
cause to forget or reverse what was learned

Examples

It is often harder to unteach incorrect information than to teach something new.
It took years to unteach the old ways from the staff.
The new manager hopes to unteach employees their bad habits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: teach - to give knowledge or instruction to someone
  • Synonym: to unlearn - to forget or discard previous knowledge or a habit

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'teach', indicating reversal of the teaching process.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00832009-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ ʌnˈtitʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtiːtʃ / (BrE)
(in machine learning) to cause a trained model to forget or remove specific learned information, patterns, or biases, typically by retraining, modifying, or applying algorithmic procedures that erase the model's knowledge of that information.
cause a model to forget learned information

Examples

The engineers tried to unteach the model's bias by fine-tuning with balanced datasets and constraints.
After the audit, they had untaught sensitive associations from the classifier to reduce discriminatory outputs.
Researchers are experimenting with methods for unteaching specific concepts without retraining on the entire dataset.
