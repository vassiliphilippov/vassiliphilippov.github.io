quintessence

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛsəns / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛsəns / (BrE)
In ancient and medieval philosophy, the fifth and highest element, believed to compose heavenly bodies and distinct from air, earth, fire, and water.
fifth element in ancient philosophy

Examples

In ancient cosmology, the stars were thought to be made of quintessence rather than earthly elements.
Medieval thinkers believed that quintessence filled the heavens and gave celestial objects their perfect nature.
Philosophers once debated whether quintessence was present anywhere on Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ether - the upper air or the clear sky; the material believed to fill the universe above the terrestrial sphere

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'quintessentia', from Latin 'quinta essentia' (fifth essence).

3. NOUN

the most perfect or typical example of a quality or class; the purest and most concentrated form of something
the purest or most perfect example

Examples

He is considered the quintessence of good manners in our community.
Her painting captures the quintessence of rural life.
The ballerina's grace was the quintessence of elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epitome - a person or thing that is a perfect example of a particular quality or type
  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality

Etymology

From Latin 'quinta essentia' (fifth essence), translating Greek 'pempte ousia'. Originally referring to the fifth element (ether) in addition to earth, air, fire, and water.

4. NOUN NEW

/ kwɪnˈtɛsəns / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛsəns / (BrE)
(in cosmology) a hypothetical dynamic scalar field proposed as a form of dark energy whose changing energy density can drive the universe's accelerated expansion.
hypothetical dark-energy field

Examples

Some cosmologists favor quintessence as a dynamic form of dark energy causing accelerated expansion.
The paper modeled quintessence as a slowly rolling scalar field that changes energy density over time.
If observations rule out a cosmological constant, quintessence remains a plausible alternative explanation.
