visualization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪʒuələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪʒuː(ə)laɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of forming a mental image of something, especially as an aid to understanding or achieving a goal
forming a mental image

Examples

Good visualization can help manage anxiety by picturing calm and peaceful scenes.
Many athletes use visualization techniques to enhance their performance before a competition.
Through visualization, he imagined himself successfully giving the big presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the ability to form new ideas or images in the mind
  • Antonym: hallucination - an experience of seeing something that is not present outside the mind

Etymology

From Latin "visualis" (of sight) and suffix "-ization" via French "visualisation".

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌvɪʒuələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪʒuː(ə)laɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a graphical representation of data or information (such as charts, graphs, or dashboards) used to explore, analyze, or communicate patterns, relationships, or insights.
graphical representation of data

Examples

The statistician created a visualization of the population data to reveal trends.
Their dashboard included an interactive visualization that allowed managers to explore monthly sales.
Students learned how to design effective visualizations that highlight key patterns without misleading readers.
