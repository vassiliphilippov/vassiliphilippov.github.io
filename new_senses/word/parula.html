Parula

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːlə / (AmE), / pəˈruːlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored American wood warblers, formerly regarded as the type genus of the Parulidae family.
genus of American wood warblers

Examples

Scientists have reclassified several species that once belonged to the Parula group.
Some birdwatchers travel specifically to observe members of the Parula genus in their natural habitat.
The Parula is known for its vibrant plumage and melodic songs.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Spanish 'paraulata', an old name for this type of bird.

2. NOUN NEW

/ pəˈruːlə / (AmE), / pəˈruːlə / (BrE)
(in data visualization) a colormap — a specific sequence of colours used to represent numerical data values on plots and charts; originally created for MATLAB and later adopted in other plotting libraries.
data-visualization colormap

Examples

The plot used the Parula colormap to display temperature variations across the map.
She changed the heatmap's colours to Parula because its gradient shows smooth value transitions.
Many data scientists prefer Parula over rainbow colormaps for readability and perceptual uniformity.

Etymology

Named after the Parula warbler; introduced as a colormap (popularized in MATLAB) in the 2010s.
