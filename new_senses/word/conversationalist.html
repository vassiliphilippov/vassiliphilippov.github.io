conversationalist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.vərˈseɪ.ʃən.əlɪst / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.vəˈseɪ.ʃən.əlɪst / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in holding conversations, especially one who talks in an interesting and engaging way
a person skilled at conversation

Examples

As a conversationalist, she could put anyone at ease in social situations.
Despite being a brilliant scientist, he was not much of a conversationalist.
He is such a gifted conversationalist that parties are never dull when he’s around.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially in a trivial way
  • Degree Higher: orator - a person who gives formal speeches, often very skilled

Etymology

From 'conversation' + '-alist', modelled after 'specialist'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cck\u0251\u02d0n.v\u0259r\u02c8se\u026a.\u0283\u0259n.\u0259l\u026ast / (AmE), / \u02cck\u0252n.v\u0259\u02c8se\u026a.\u0283\u0259n.\u0259l\u026ast / (BrE)
(in computing) a software program or AI agent designed to hold natural-language conversations with users, often to answer questions or perform tasks.
AI program that converses

Examples

The new customer service AI acts as a conversationalist, guiding users through complex queries.
Researchers trained a conversationalist to suggest diet plans using natural dialog and personal data.
Many companies deploy a conversationalist on their websites to handle routine customer questions.
