native

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a metal or substance) found in nature in its pure or uncombined form, not as part of a compound.
found pure in nature (of metal/substance)

Examples

Native gold is often collected from riverbeds in its native state.
Platinum is one of the few metals that can be found native in the Earth's crust.
The geologists were excited to find native copper in the old mine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
A person born in a particular place or country.
person born in a place

Examples

As a native of the city, she knows all the best places to eat.
He is a native of Brazil and speaks Portuguese fluently.
Many natives have deep-rooted traditions passed down through generations.

Synset Examples
