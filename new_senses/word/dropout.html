dropout

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who withdraws or disengages from a social group, activity, or environment, especially before completion or fulfillment
person who withdraws from a group or environment

Examples

After leaving college in his second year, he was labeled a dropout by his peers.
She became a dropout from the corporate world, seeking a simpler life in the countryside.
The community center hosts events for youth at risk of becoming dropouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quitter - a person who gives up easily or does not finish a task

Etymology

From 'drop' + 'out', first used in American English in the 1930s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who leaves school, college, or university before finishing their course of study
person who leaves school before graduating

Examples

As a high school dropout, he later returned to finish his education.
Many teenagers become dropouts due to difficult family circumstances.
The program helps dropouts reclaim their academic goals and build careers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graduate - a person who has successfully completed a course of study

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'drop out', meaning to withdraw, especially from school or a course.

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8dr\u0251\u02d0p\u02cca\u028at / (AmE), / \u02c8dr\u0252p\u02cca\u028at / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a regularization technique for neural networks in which, during training, a random subset of units (neurons) is temporarily ignored (set to zero) to reduce overfitting and improve generalization.
machine-learning regularization technique

Examples

Researchers used dropout to regularize the neural network and improve generalization during training.
The team found that applying dropout at 0.5 reduced overfitting and boosted validation accuracy.
During experiments, they compared models trained with dropout to those using only weight decay.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regularization - techniques used to reduce overfitting and improve a model's ability to generalize
  • Antonym: overfitting - when a model learns training data too closely and fails to generalize to new data

Etymology

Coined in machine-learning literature (c.2014) from the phrasal verb 'drop out' (to remove), used metaphorically for temporarily omitting units during training.
