Renoir

1. PROPN

/ rəˈnwɑr / (AmE), / rɛˈnwɑː / (BrE)
Pierre-Auguste Renoir (1841–1919), a French impressionist painter celebrated for his vibrant light and color, as well as his depictions of social life and landscapes.
French impressionist painter (1841–1919)

Examples

During her trip to Paris, she admired Renoir's paintings at the Musée d'Orsay.
Many critics praise Renoir for his unique approach to depicting sunlight.
The exhibition showcased several masterpieces by Renoir.

Etymology

From the French surname Renoir, belonging to Pierre-Auguste Renoir.
