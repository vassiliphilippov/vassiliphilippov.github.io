signature

/ ˈsɪɡ.nə.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nə.tʃə / (BrE)
a group of pages printed on a single sheet of paper, which is folded, cut, and bound with others to form a book

Examples

Damaged signatures can make an old book difficult to repair properly.
Each signature was carefully folded and stitched together during the binding process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nə.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nə.tʃə / (BrE)
(in music) the sharps or flats placed after the clef on a staff to indicate the key of a piece or passage.
musical key indicator

Examples

Each line of the score displays the signature directly after the clef symbol.
He forgot to change the signature when the music modulated to a new key.
The piece begins with a signature showing three sharps, so it's in A major.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: key signature - group of sharps or flats shown at the beginning of each staff to indicate the key

Etymology

From Medieval Latin signatura (mark), from Latin signare (to mark).
