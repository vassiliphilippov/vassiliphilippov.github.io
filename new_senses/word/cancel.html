cancel

1. VERB

/ ˈkæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl / (BrE)
to decide that something cannot be used or is no longer valid, often by marking or officially voiding it
make something invalid or void

Examples

Due to the error, all previous tickets have been cancelled and reissued.
Please cancel the order if payment is not received within a week.
The bank may cancel your check if there is a mistake on it.

Synset Examples

cancel cheques or tickets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - to declare something as having no legal force or effect
  • Antonym: reinstate - to restore something to validity or use

Etymology

From Latin cancellare (to cross out), from cancelli (lattice).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl / (BrE)
A notation in musical scores indicating that a previous sharp or flat is to be disregarded.
musical sign removing a previous accidental

Examples

A cancel is used in music to nullify a previous sharp or flat in a measure.
The composer marked a cancel before the note to restore it to its natural pitch.
When writing accidentals, a cancel can clarify the intended pitch for performers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural - the sign (♮) indicating a note is neither sharp nor flat
