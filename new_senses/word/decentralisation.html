decentralisation

1. NOUN

/ diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring power or authority from a central organization or government to local branches or authorities
transfer of authority from central to local bodies
british

Examples

Many countries have implemented decentralisation to give local governments more autonomy.
Opponents argue that decentralisation may lead to inconsistencies in policy across regions.
The decentralisation of power can improve efficiency and accountability in public administration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centralisation - the concentration of control under a single authority

Etymology

From French 'décentralisation', based on 'centre', ultimately from Latin 'centrum'.

2. NOUN

/ diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The social and economic process of moving population and industry from central urban areas to outlying regions.
movement of people and industry away from city centers

Examples

Many experts credit decentralisation with reducing the congestion and pollution of large city centers.
The city's decentralisation policies encouraged businesses to relocate to surrounding suburbs.
The government invested in infrastructure to promote decentralisation across the metropolitan area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urbanization - the process by which cities grow as more people and industry concentrate in urban areas

Etymology

From Latin 'centum' (center) with the prefix 'de-' (away from), plus '-isation' (process of making).

3. NOUN NEW

/ diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in computing and networks) the distribution of governance, data, or computational control across multiple independent nodes, reducing reliance on a single central authority and improving resilience or privacy.
distribution of control across network nodes

Examples

Tech firms are pursuing decentralisation of data to improve privacy and resilience.
The startup built a decentralisation layer so users control their own identity and keys.
Researchers studied how decentralisation improves fault tolerance across thousands of networked devices.
