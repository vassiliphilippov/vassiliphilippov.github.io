scrubber

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrʌbər / (AmE), / ˈskrʌbə / (BrE)
a brush with short stiff bristles used for heavy cleaning tasks
brush for heavy cleaning

Examples

After soaking the pot, he used a scrubber to remove the burnt residue.
She grabbed a scrubber to clean the greasy pans.
The tiles were so dirty that only a scrubber could get them clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - a tool with bristles for cleaning, painting, etc.

Etymology

From 'scrub' (to clean by rubbing) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskrʌbər / (AmE), / ˈskrʌbə / (BrE)
a device that removes impurities or pollutants from gases, especially in industrial processes
device for purifying gases

Examples

Modern power plants use scrubbers to clean harmful gases before they are released into the atmosphere.
The engineer explained how the scrubber captures sulfur dioxide during the combustion process.
The factory installed a new scrubber to reduce air pollution from its smokestacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purifier - a device that removes impurities
  • Synonym: filter - a device for removing unwanted substances

Etymology

From 'scrub' (to clean) + '-er' (agent noun suffix); named for its cleaning action on gases.
