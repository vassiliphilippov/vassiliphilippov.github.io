B1

1. NOUN

/ biː wʌn / (AmE), / biː wʌn / (BrE)
A standard size of paper measuring 707 x 1000 millimeters, used especially in printing and design.
paper size, 707 x 1000 mm

Examples

Many conference schedules are displayed on large B1 boards.
The poster was printed on B1 paper to make it more visible.
We need several B1 sheets for the architectural drawings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A1 - a standard paper size measuring 594 x 841 mm
  • Degree Lower: B2 - a standard paper size measuring 500 x 707 mm
  • Degree Higher: B0 - a standard paper size measuring 1000 x 1414 mm

Etymology

Part of the ISO 216 B series for paper sizes, where 'B' denotes the series and '1' indicates its rank in the series.

2. NOUN NEW

/ biː wʌn / (AmE), / biː wʌn / (BrE)
A level on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) indicating an intermediate level of language proficiency; a learner at B1 can deal with everyday situations, describe experiences, and give simple explanations of opinions and plans.
CEFR intermediate level

Examples

She achieved a B1 in the language exam, confirming her everyday communication skills.
The university requires a B1 certificate from all applicants for the exchange program.
At B1, students can describe experiences, briefly explain opinions, and handle travel situations.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: B2 - the CEFR upper-intermediate level, above B1
  • Synonym: intermediate - a mid-level of ability between basic and advanced

Etymology

From the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR): 'B' denotes the independent-user band and '1' indicates the lower B level (intermediate).
