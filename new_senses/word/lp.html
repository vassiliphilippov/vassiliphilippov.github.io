LP

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛlˈpi / (AmE), / ˌɛlˈpiː / (BrE)
a long-playing phonograph record designed to be played at 33⅓ revolutions per minute, typically holding more music than a standard single
long-playing phonograph record (33⅓ rpm)

Examples

Many audiophiles believe that music sounds warmer on an LP than on a CD.
She proudly showed me her vintage LP collection from the 1970s.
The band released their debut LP to critical acclaim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a disc for storing sound, especially music, for playback

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'long playing.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛlˈpi / (AmE), / ˌɛlˈpiː / (BrE)
a long-playing phonograph record, typically played at 33⅓ revolutions per minute, used for audio recordings such as albums.
a long-playing record (phonograph)

Examples

He found an old LP of The Beatles in his grandfather's attic.
Many collectors prize rare jazz LPs for their superior sound quality.
She bought a vintage turntable to play her favorite LP albums.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - a phonograph record with typically just one song on each side

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'long-playing', referring to the longer playback time compared to earlier records.
