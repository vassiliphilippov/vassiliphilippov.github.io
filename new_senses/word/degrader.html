degrader

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡreɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdə / (BrE)
a person who lowers the quality, character, or value of something
person who lowers quality or value

Examples

As the degrader of the coinage, he was quickly stripped of his powers.
Some critics saw the artist as a degrader of tradition, while others called him innovative.
The manager refused to be a degrader of standards just to cut costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debaser - a person who reduces the value or quality, especially of money or art
  • Antonym: reformer - a person who improves or restores something to a better state

Etymology

From degrade + -er (agent suffix), ultimately from Latin degradare (to reduce in rank).

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈɡreɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdə / (BrE)
(in biotechnology and pharmacology) a chemical or biological agent that promotes the breakdown or destruction of a specific target molecule, especially a protein, often by recruiting cellular degradation machinery.
biotech agent that degrades proteins
medical

Examples

Researchers designed a degrader to selectively destroy the harmful protein in disease cells.
Clinical trials showed that the novel degrader reduced toxic protein levels without major side effects.
Many modern drug discovery teams focus on creating degraders capable of targeting previously 'undruggable' proteins.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degradant - a substance that causes degradation of another substance (general term).
  • Synonym: PROTAC - a proteolysis‑targeting chimera — a designed molecule that induces degradation of a specific protein by linking it to cellular degradation machinery.

Etymology

From degrade + -er (agent suffix); ultimately from Latin degradare ‘to reduce in rank’. Extended in modern biotechnology to refer to molecules that induce target degradation.
