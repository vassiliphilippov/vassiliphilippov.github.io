yang

1. NOUN

/ jæŋ / (AmE), / jæŋ / (BrE)
(in Chinese philosophy) the active, bright, positive, and masculine principle in dualistic cosmology, often contrasted with yin.
the bright, positive principle in Chinese philosophy

Examples

He explained how yang represents strength and assertiveness, complementing the soft power of yin.
Many traditional practices aim to balance yang and yin energies for health and harmony.
The concept of yang is often associated with sunlight, warmth, and activity in Chinese philosophy.

Synset Examples

yin and yang together produce everything that comes into existence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yin - the dark, passive, negative, and feminine principle in Chinese cosmology

Etymology

From Mandarin 陽 (yáng), referring to the 'sunny side' and the positive principle in Chinese cosmology.

2. NOUN NEW

/ jæŋ / (AmE), / jæŋ / (BrE)
(in computing, especially network management) a data‑modeling language used to define configuration and state data for network devices; commonly used with protocols such as NETCONF and RESTCONF.
network data‑modeling language

Examples

Network engineers wrote a YANG module to describe the router's interface and routing settings.
The team used a YANG schema to automatically push configuration to hundreds of switches.
After upgrading, she validated the device with a YANG model to prevent configuration errors.

Synset Examples

Network engineers wrote a YANG module to describe the router's interface and routing settings.
The team used a YANG schema to automatically push configuration to hundreds of switches.
After upgrading, she validated the device with a YANG model to prevent configuration errors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data model - a formal description of the structure and meaning of data used by software or systems
  • Synonym: schema - a structured definition of how data is organized, similar to a YANG module

Etymology

From the IETF acronym YANG (said to mean ‘Yet Another Next Generation’), the name of the data‑modeling language defined in IETF specifications.
