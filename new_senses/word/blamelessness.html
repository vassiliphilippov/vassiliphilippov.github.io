blamelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪmləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈbleɪmləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being free from blame or guilt; innocence
state of being free from blame

Examples

He maintained his blamelessness despite the accusations against him.
Her blamelessness was evident to everyone who knew her true character.
The blamelessness of the child made the harsh punishment seem unjust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being not guilty or not responsible for a wrongdoing
  • Antonym: guilt - the state of having committed an offense or wrongdoing

Etymology

From 'blameless' + '-ness', where 'blameless' derives from 'blame' (from Old French 'blamer', from Latin 'blasphemare', to speak evil of).
