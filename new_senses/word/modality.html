modality

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈdæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / məʊˈdæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a particular method or type of therapeutic treatment, especially in medicine or physiotherapy, involving physical or electrical techniques
therapeutic treatment method
medical

Examples

Choosing the right modality depends on the patient's specific condition and treatment goals.
Physical therapists may combine different modalities to maximize the effectiveness of rehabilitation.
The clinic offers several modalities, including ultrasound therapy and electrical stimulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapy - treatment intended to relieve or heal a disorder
  • Synonym: treatment - medical care given to a patient for an illness or injury

Etymology

From Late Latin 'modalitas', from 'modus' meaning 'measure, manner'.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈdæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / məʊˈdæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a category that expresses the likelihood, necessity, or possibility contained in a statement, often through the use of modal verbs or expressions
category expressing necessity or possibility

Examples

Different languages use various grammatical means to indicate modality in their sentences.
In logic, modality classifies statements as necessary, possible, or impossible.
The sentence 'She might come tomorrow' expresses modality through the use of the modal verb 'might'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mood - a grammatical feature expressing modality or attitude

Etymology

From Late Latin 'modalitas' (measure, manner), from 'modālis' (of mode).

3. NOUN

/ moʊˈdæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / məʊˈdæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Linguistics) Any of the verb inflections or forms (such as mood or modal verbs) expressing the speaker's attitude or how the action or state is conceived (e.g., likelihood, necessity, or obligation).
linguistic expression of attitude or mood

Examples

Grammatical modality plays an important role in how speakers convey certainty or doubt.
In English, modal verbs such as 'can', 'must', and 'should' express different types of modality.
The modality of a verb can indicate possibility, necessity, or obligation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mood - a grammatical feature expressing the speaker's attitude toward the action (e.g., indicative, subjunctive, imperative)

From Late Latin *modalitas* (measure, mode), from Latin *modus* (measure, manner).
