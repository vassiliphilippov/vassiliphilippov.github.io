to goldplate

1. VERB

/ ˈɡoʊldˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to cover the surface of an object with a thin layer of gold, usually by chemical or electrochemical means
cover with a thin layer of gold

Examples

It is expensive to goldplate large objects due to the cost of gold.
The artist will goldplate each medal before the ceremony.
They decided to goldplate the wedding rings to give them a luxurious finish.

Synset Examples

goldplate a watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gild - to cover with a thin layer of gold
  • Synonym: silverplate - to cover with a thin layer of silver

Etymology

From 'gold' + 'plate,' referring to the process of plating with gold.
