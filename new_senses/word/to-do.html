to-do

1. NOUN

/ təˈdu / (AmE), / təˈduː / (BrE)
a commotion, disturbance, or excited outburst, especially over something unimportant
commotion or fuss

Examples

Let's not make a to-do over such a small issue.
She made a big to-do about the mistake, but it was really minor.
There was quite a to-do when the announcement was made.

Synset Examples

they were amazed by the furious disturbance they had caused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - noisy disturbance or tumult
  • Synonym: fuss - unnecessary excitement or activity

Etymology

From the phrase 'to do,' meaning something to be done, later developing into 'a fuss or commotion.'

2. NOUN NEW

/ t5cdu / (AmE), / t5cdud0 / (BrE)
An item or note indicating a task that should be done later, especially a marker in code or project trackers (often written TODO).
task note in code or tracker

Examples

The project tracker shows a to-do for implementing the search feature by next sprint.
She left a to-do comment in the code reminding herself to fix the bug later.
During the meeting they assigned several to-dos to different team members for follow-up.

Synset Examples
