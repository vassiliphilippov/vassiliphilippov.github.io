greening

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or phenomenon by which vitality and freshness are restored, especially in plants or landscapes after a dormant period
restoration of vitality or freshness (esp. in nature)

Examples

The greening of the countryside after winter was a welcome sight to the villagers.
The annual greening of the park draws visitors who come to enjoy the vibrant new growth.
There has been a noticeable greening of urban spaces thanks to new environmental policies.

Synset Examples

the annual rejuvenation of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejuvenation - the action or process of making something look or feel better, younger, or more vital again

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɡriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or movement of making an economy, industry, organisation, or system more environmentally sustainable by adopting eco‑friendly policies, practices, and technologies.
process of becoming environmentally sustainable

Examples

The greening of the economy requires investment in renewable energy and green infrastructure.
Local businesses welcomed the greening incentives that offered tax breaks for reducing emissions and waste.
Policymakers debated whether the rapid greening of industry would harm jobs without retraining programs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sustainability - the quality of causing little or no damage to the environment and being able to be maintained over the long term
  • Synonym: decarbonization - the process of reducing carbon dioxide emissions across an economy or sector

Etymology

From adjective 'green' + suffix -ing; extended from literal 'becoming green' into environmental and policy contexts in the late 20th century.
