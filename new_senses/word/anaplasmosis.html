anaplasmosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəplæzˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəplæzˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease of cattle and other animals caused by infection with Anaplasma bacteria, transmitted chiefly by ticks, leading to anemia and related symptoms.
tick-borne disease affecting cattle
medical

Examples

Effective tick control can help prevent anaplasmosis outbreaks on livestock farms.
The spread of anaplasmosis is closely linked to the presence of cattle ticks in the area.
Veterinarians diagnosed anaplasmosis in several cows after they showed signs of severe anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babesiosis - A similar tick-borne disease in cattle, also called Texas fever

Etymology

From New Latin *Anaplasma* (genus name) + -osis (denoting disease).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cc\u00e6n\u0259pl\u00e6z\u02c8mo\u028as\u026as / (AmE), / \u02cc\u00e6n\u0259pl\u00e6z\u02c8m\u0259\u028as\u026as / (BrE)
(in humans) a tick-borne infectious disease caused by Anaplasma bacteria, especially Anaplasma phagocytophilum, typically causing fever, headache and muscle aches; also called human granulocytic anaplasmosis.
tick-borne bacterial disease in humans
medical

Examples

Public health alerts advised residents after several anaplasmosis cases were reported in humans.
Doctors recommended antibiotics as soon as they suspected anaplasmosis following the tick bite.
Because symptoms can be vague, people with fever after hiking were tested for anaplasmosis.
