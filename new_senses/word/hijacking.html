hijacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒakɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of illegally seizing control of a vehicle in transit, especially by the use of force or threats.
illegal seizure of a vehicle

Examples

After the hijacking, security at the airport was significantly increased.
He was arrested in connection with the hijacking of a delivery truck.
The hijacking of the airplane shocked the entire nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carjacking - the act of violently stealing an occupied car
  • Degree Lower: theft - the act of stealing; taking another's property unlawfully

Etymology

From hijack + -ing; 'hijack' of uncertain origin, possibly related to prohibition-era slang.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ha\u026a\u02ccd\u0292\u00e6k\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8ha\u026a\u02ccd\u0292ak\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
(in computing and cybersecurity) the act of unlawfully taking control of a computer, browser, network traffic, or online account, typically to redirect users, steal data, or interfere with normal operation.
illegal control of digital systems or traffic

Examples

Browser hijacking redirects a user's searches to malicious sites without consent.
After the cyberattack, the company discovered hijacking of its DNS records that sent customers to a fake page.
Security software warned him about hijacking attempts when a suspicious extension tried to change settings.
