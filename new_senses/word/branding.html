branding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrændɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of marking someone with a hot iron, especially as a punishment or means of identification, sometimes used figuratively to mean stigmatizing or labeling someone negatively
the act of marking or stigmatizing

Examples

He spoke about the social branding of people with a criminal record.
In history books, you can find references to the branding of slaves as a means of control.
The branding of criminals was a common practice in some societies in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stigmatization - the act of describing or regarding someone as worthy of disgrace or disapproval

Etymology

From brand (to mark with a hot iron), from Old English 'brand' (burning, sword).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbrændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrændɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of creating and promoting a public, corporate, or personal identity (a 'brand') to influence how people perceive a product, service, or person.
promoting a public identity

Examples

In the digital era, personal branding is essential for freelancers seeking credibility online.
The startup invested heavily in branding to make its product stand out in crowded markets.
Good branding can change how customers perceive a company and increase willingness to pay.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand - a distinctive name, symbol, or identity used to identify a product, company, or person
  • Synonym: marketing - the activity of promoting, selling, and distributing products or services; closely related to branding

Etymology

From brand (a mark or trade name); extended in the 20th century to mean creating and promoting a commercial or personal identity.
