shook

1. NOUN

/ ʃʊk / (AmE), / ʃʊk / (BrE)
A set of staves and head pieces from a barrel or box, packed in a disassembled state for storage or shipment.
disassembled barrel or box parts

Examples

Each shook included all the necessary staves and heads for the cask.
Exporters often send shook overseas to save on transportation costs.
The cooper received a shipment of shook to assemble into barrels at the warehouse.

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *schok* (bundle of sticks).
