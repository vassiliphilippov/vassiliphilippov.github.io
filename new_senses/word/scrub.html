scrub

1. NOUN

/ skrʌb / (AmE), / skrʌb / (BrE)
Vegetation consisting of stunted trees or bushes, often forming dense, low thickets, especially in arid or semi-arid areas.
dense growth of stunted bushes or trees

Examples

Many animals find shelter in the scrub along the riverbanks.
Much of the land was covered in thick scrub that made walking difficult.
The fire quickly spread through the dry scrub near the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or trees

2. NOUN

/ skrʌb / (AmE), / skrʌb / (BrE)
the act of cleaning something by rubbing it hard, often with a brush and soap and water
cleaning by rubbing with a brush and water

Examples

After a long hike, I needed a thorough scrub in the shower.
Give the floor a good scrub before you start painting.
She gave the kitchen sink a quick scrub to make it shine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean something using water (verb)

Etymology

From Middle English 'skrobben', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'skrubba' (to scrub).

3. ADJ

/ skrʌb / (AmE), / skrʌb / (BrE)
(of domestic animals) not selectively bred; of inferior or mixed ancestry.
not selectively bred (of animals)

Examples

In some regions, scrub goats are prized for their hardiness despite their mixed lineage.
The farmer decided not to breed the scrub cattle, preferring his pedigree herd.
The show ring excludes scrub animals because they do not meet breed standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongrel - an animal of mixed or indeterminate breed
