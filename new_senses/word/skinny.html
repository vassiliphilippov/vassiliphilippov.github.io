skinny

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪni / (AmE), / ˈskɪni / (BrE)
(informal) Confidential or inside information about a topic or person.
inside or confidential information (informal)
american informal

Examples

Before making a decision, I always try to get the real skinny from someone who knows.
He gave me the skinny on what happened at the meeting after I left.
What's the skinny on our new boss? Is she easy to work with?

Synset Examples

he wanted the inside skinny on the new partner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dope - inside information (slang)
  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about other people

Etymology

Probably derived from the phrase 'the skinny', American slang from the mid-20th century, possibly referring to 'the skin' as the essence of something.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɪni / (AmE), / ˈskɪni / (BrE)
very thin, especially in a way that is unattractive or not healthy
very thin

Examples

He wore a pair of skinny jeans and a loose t-shirt.
She used to be skinny, but now she looks much healthier.
The dog looked so skinny that we knew it needed food.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Examples
