allometry

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑːmətri / (AmE), / əˈlɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the study of the relative growth of different parts of an organism in relation to the growth of the whole organism
study of proportional growth in organisms

Examples

One important insight from allometry is the relationship between brain size and body size in mammals.
The scientist's research focused on allometry to understand how animal organs grow in proportion to the body.
Through allometry, biologists can see why some body parts develop faster than others.

Etymology

From Greek 'allo-' (other) and 'metron' (measure).

Examples

Researchers found that road length scales with population according to urban allometry, following a sublinear power law.
