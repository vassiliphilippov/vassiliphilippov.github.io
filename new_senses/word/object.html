object

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.dʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.dʒɪkt / (BrE)
a person or thing to which a particular feeling, action, or thought is directed
person or thing targeted by feeling or thought

Examples

Money was never the object of her interest.
She became the object of his admiration.
The object of their investigation was never found.

Synset Examples

objects of thought
the object of my affection

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subject - the doer or experiencer of an action or state; in contrast with 'object' as the target

Etymology

From Latin objectum (thing put before), from obicere (to put before).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05818974-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑbˌdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɒbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
(computing) a discrete entity in programming that contains data and methods to operate on that data, and can interact with other such entities.
computing entity with data and methods

Examples

By modifying the object, you can update the user's information dynamically.
Each object in the program can send messages to other objects.
The object stores both a name and an age as its attributes.

Synset Examples

in object-oriented programming, objects include data and define its status, its methods of operation and how it interacts with other objects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a blueprint for creating objects in programming
  • Synonym: instance - an individualized realization of an object or class

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑbˌdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.dʒɪkt / (BrE)
(Grammar) a noun, noun phrase, or pronoun that receives the action of a verb or shows the result of an action.
grammar: noun or pronoun affected by the verb

Examples

In the sentence 'She reads books,' the object is 'books.'
Some verbs require an object, while others do not.
The grammar lesson focused on identifying the object in each example.

Synset Examples

the object of the verb

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subject - the noun, noun phrase, or pronoun performing the action of the verb

Etymology

From Latin objectum (a thing put before; neuter of obiectus, past participle of obicere ‘to set before’).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

Examples

The backup service stores each document as an object in cloud storage.
To retrieve the photo, she requested the object by its unique identifier from S3.
Large datasets are often stored as objects, each with metadata that aids searching and versioning.
