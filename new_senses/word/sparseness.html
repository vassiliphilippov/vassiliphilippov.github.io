sparseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspɑːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being thinly scattered or insufficient in amount; the state of lacking density or fullness
the state of being sparse or scanty

Examples

Critics noted the sparseness of details provided in the official report.
During winter, the sparseness of daylight hours can affect people’s moods.
The sparseness of vegetation in the desert makes survival difficult for many animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: density - the degree to which something is filled or compact
  • Synonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply; insufficiency

Etymology

From the adjective 'sparse' (from Latin 'sparsus', past participle of 'spargere', meaning to scatter), plus the noun suffix '-ness'.

(in data science and numerical computing) the degree to which a data structure (for example, a vector or matrix) contains zero or near-zero elements; the proportion of empty or negligible entries.
degree of data sparsity

Examples

The sparseness of the feature matrix reduced the model's training time and memory usage.
Researchers measured the sparseness of the user-item matrix to decide whether to use dense algorithms.
High sparseness in the sensor data meant many values were zero and the model overfit.
