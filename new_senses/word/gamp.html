gamp

1. NOUN

/ ɡæmp / (AmE), / ɡamp / (BrE)
an umbrella, especially a large or clumsy one (chiefly British, dated or informal)
umbrella (chiefly British, dated)
archaic british informal

Examples

Caught in the downpour, he opened his trusty gamp and hurried across the street.
She always carried a gamp that seemed big enough to shelter three people.
The old man waved his tattered gamp at the passing cars, hoping for a lift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella - a device for protection against rain or sun

Etymology

From the character Mrs. Gamp in Charles Dickens' novel 'Martin Chuzzlewit', who carried a large umbrella.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ɡæmp / (AmE), / ɡamp / (BrE)
(in regulated industries, especially pharmaceuticals) an industry framework of guidelines for validating, managing, and documenting computerized and automated systems to ensure data integrity and compliance.
guidelines for computerized systems (pharma)

Examples

The quality team followed GAMP guidelines when validating the new laboratory information system.
During the audit, auditors asked for documentation showing GAMP-based risk assessments and test results.
Our software vendor updated processes to align with the latest GAMP recommendations for computerized systems.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Good Automated Manufacturing Practice - the full name of the GAMP guidance framework for validation of automated systems

Etymology

From the initials of Good Automated Manufacturing Practice, a guidance framework developed for computerized systems in regulated pharmaceutical manufacturing.
