Google

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡuɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡuːɡl̩ / (BrE)
A widely used search engine on the World Wide Web that finds web pages relevant to a user's query.
widely used search engine

Examples

Google frequently updates its algorithms to improve search results.
Many people rely on Google for quick access to information.
She used Google to find the address of the new restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search engine - A program for finding information on the internet

Etymology

Coined in the late 1990s from 'googol', a mathematical term for 10^100, reflecting the company's mission to organize vast amounts of information.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈɡuɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡuːɡl / (BrE)
to search for information on the internet, especially by using the Google search engine (often used of looking something up online).
search the internet (using Google)

Examples

If you don't remember the address, just google the restaurant and check the reviews.
She googled her symptoms and decided to call a doctor after reading worrying posts.
They're googling competitors to gather public information before the meeting tomorrow.
