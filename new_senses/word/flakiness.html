flakiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪki.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being eccentric, unreliable, or mentally unstable, especially in an informal or slang context.
eccentric or unreliable behavior (informal)
informal slang

Examples

Don't mistake flakiness for genuine mental illness—sometimes people are just a little odd.
Her flakiness made it difficult to rely on her for important tasks.
Some people loved his creative ideas, while others were frustrated by his flakiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentricity - quality of being unconventional or slightly strange
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind; opposite of insanity

Etymology

From 'flaky' (slang for eccentric or unstable) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪ.ki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪ.ki.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being flaky, especially of breaking into thin crisp flakes
quality of being flaky; breaking into flakes

Examples

Proper flakiness is a sign of well-made croissants.
She judged the bread’s quality by its texture and flakiness.
The flakiness of the pastry made it especially delicious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crispness - the quality of being crisp, especially food
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth, lacking roughness or flakes

Etymology

From 'flaky' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfleɪki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki.nəs / (BrE)
(in computing) the tendency of software tests or systems to fail unpredictably and intermittently, producing nondeterministic results.
intermittent, unpredictable test failures (computing)

Examples

The CI suite shows flakiness in its tests, making builds unreliable.
Developers blamed the test flakiness for delaying the release deadline.
To fix deployment problems, the team tracked down and removed flakiness in integration tests.

Synset Examples
