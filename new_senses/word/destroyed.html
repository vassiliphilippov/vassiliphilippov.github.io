destroyed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪd / (BrE)
extremely damaged or ruined, either physically or morally
ruined or damaged beyond repair

Examples

After the war, much of the city was destroyed and uninhabitable.
He was destroyed by years of guilt and regret.
The hurricane left many homes completely destroyed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruined - severely damaged so that it is no longer able to function
  • Antonym: intact - remaining whole or undamaged

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪd / (BrE)
spoiled, ruined, or completely demolished; having been made unusable or rendered nonexistent by damage or destruction
ruined or demolished

Examples

A destroyed reputation can be difficult to rebuild.
After the hurricane, thousands of homes were destroyed by the flooding.
The old documents were found destroyed in the fire.

Synset Examples

war left many cities destroyed
Alzheimer's is responsible for her destroyed mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruined - damaged so badly that it no longer works or has value
  • Antonym: intact - remaining whole, undamaged

Etymology

Past participle of 'destroy', from Latin 'destruere' (to unbuild, demolish).

3. ADJ NEW

/ dɪˈstrɔɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪd / (BrE)
(of clothing or textiles) intentionally torn, faded, or distressed to give a worn or vintage appearance as a fashion effect.
intentionally distressed (clothing)

Examples

She wore destroyed denim to the concert, the ripped jeans matching her leather jacket.
The boutique displayed a rack of destroyed T-shirts, each piece intentionally faded and torn along the seams.
He prefers his destroyed jeans so soft from wear they look vintage and lived-in.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressed - cloth or leather intentionally aged or worn for stylistic effect
  • Synonym: ripped - clothing that has deliberate tears or holes as part of the style
  • Antonym: intact - remaining whole or undamaged; opposite of clothing made to look worn

Etymology

From the past participle of 'destroy' (from Latin destruere 'unbuild, demolish'); later extended figuratively to mean intentionally distressed clothing.
