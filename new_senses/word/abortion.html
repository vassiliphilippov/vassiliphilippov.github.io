abortion

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɔr.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈbɔː.ʃən / (BrE)
the deliberate ending of a pregnancy so that it does not result in the birth of a baby
termination of pregnancy

Examples

She made the difficult decision to have an abortion early in her pregnancy.
Some clinics provide counseling services for women considering an abortion.
The topic of abortion often sparks heated debate in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miscarriage - spontaneous loss of a pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin abortio (miscarriage), from aboriri (to miscarry).

(in computing) the premature termination of a process, transaction, or computation by a user or the system before normal completion.
premature termination (computing)

Examples

After detecting a conflict, the database performed an automatic abortion of the transaction to prevent corruption.
The application logged the unexpected abortion of the background task and restarted it immediately.
Users receive a warning when they request an abortion of the file upload during a slow connection.
