stylization

1. NOUN

/ staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of representing something according to a specific style rather than in a naturalistic or realistic way
representation in a particular style

Examples

Her novel's heavy stylization made it both challenging and rewarding to read.
In animation, stylization can be used to emphasize mood or character traits.
The artist's stylization of the landscape gave the painting a unique, modern look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylize - to represent something in a particular style

Etymology

From French 'stylisation', from 'style', ultimately from Latin 'stilus' (writing instrument).

2. NOUN NEW

/ staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / staɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in computing and digital image processing) the automated process of applying the visual style or texture of one image to another, typically using algorithms or neural networks (also called style transfer).
automatic visual style transfer

Examples

Researchers applied neural stylization to convert a daytime photo into a Monet-style painting for demonstrations.
The app uses fast stylization algorithms so users can turn selfies into artwork in seconds.
After training, the model produced convincing stylization when transferring brushwork from paintings to photographs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style transfer - the algorithmic technique of transferring the appearance or 'style' of one image onto another
  • Synonym: neural style transfer - a method using neural networks to separate and recombine image content and style

Etymology

From French 'stylisation', from 'style', ultimately from Latin 'stilus' (writing instrument). Used in computing as a metaphorical extension for algorithmic style transfer.
