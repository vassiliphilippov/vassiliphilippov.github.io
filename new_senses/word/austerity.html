austerity

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈstɛrəti / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛərəti / (BrE)
sternness or severity of manner or attitude; extreme self-denial, especially refraining from worldly pleasures.
severe self-denial; refraining from pleasures

Examples

Despite his wealth, he practiced austerity by avoiding luxury and excess.
She admired the austerity he maintained throughout his spiritual journey.
The monk's life of austerity inspired others to adopt a simpler existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence

Etymology

From Latin 'austeritas', from Greek 'austerotēs' (severity).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u0254\u02d0\u02c8st\u025br\u0259ti / (AmE), / \u0252\u02c8st\u025b\u0259r\u0259ti / (BrE)
an economic policy or period in which a government reduces public spending, services, or borrowing to lower budget deficits.
government spending cuts

Examples

Facing rising debt, the government announced new austerity measures to trim public services.
Many families struggled after years of austerity, with cuts to health and education budgets.
Opponents argued that prolonged austerity slowed economic growth and increased unemployment in the region.
