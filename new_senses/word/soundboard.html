soundboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A thin, typically wooden board in a musical instrument that vibrates to amplify and enrich the sound produced by the instrument.
vibrating board in an instrument for sound amplification

Examples

A crack in the soundboard can greatly affect the quality of a guitar's sound.
Instrument makers carefully select the wood for a violin's soundboard to achieve the desired acoustics.
The piano's soundboard is crucial for producing its rich, resonant tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonator - a device or part that increases and enriches sound vibrations

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsaʊndˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A software application or digital interface that stores and plays short pre‑recorded audio clips (sound effects, music, or voice) via clickable buttons or keyboard triggers, used in live streams, broadcasts, podcasts, or performances.
digital interface for playing audio clips

Examples

During the live podcast, she clicked the soundboard to play a short laugh track for listeners.
Streamers often organize their favorite clips into a soundboard for quick access during gameplay.
He tested several soundboards before deciding which app gave the cleanest, most reliable audio.
