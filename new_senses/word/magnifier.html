magnifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌfaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ.ə / (BrE)
an optical instrument that makes objects appear larger than they are
instrument that enlarges images

Examples

A pocket magnifier can be helpful for hobbyists and collectors.
She used a magnifier to read the tiny print on the map.
The jeweler examined the diamond closely through a magnifier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loupe - a small magnifying glass used by jewelers and watchmakers
  • Degree Higher: microscope - an instrument used to see objects that are too small for the naked eye
  • Synonym: hand lens - a small magnifying glass used to examine small objects

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare' (to magnify).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌfaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ.ə / (BrE)
(in computing) a software utility or built-in accessibility feature that enlarges part or all of a computer or mobile device screen to make text and images easier to see.
screen-enlargement software

Examples

She turned on the magnifier to enlarge the small icons during the presentation.
The tablet's built-in magnifier lets visually impaired users zoom a portion of the screen quickly.
When reading PDFs, he found the magnifier more convenient than changing the document's font size.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen magnifier - software that enlarges part or all of a display for easier viewing
  • Synonym: zoom (feature) - a function that increases the apparent size of screen content

From Latin 'magnificare' (to magnify).
