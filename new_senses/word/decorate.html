to decorate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛkəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəreɪt / (BrE)
to make something look more attractive by adding ornaments, color, or other decorative elements
to add decorations or make attractive

Examples

Every year, we decorate the office for the holidays.
She loves to decorate her cakes with fresh flowers and colorful sprinkles.
They spent the afternoon decorating their new apartment with paintings and plants.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adorn - to decorate or add beauty to something
  • Synonym: embellish - to make something more beautiful by adding details or decorations
  • Antonym: strip - to remove decorations or ornamentation from something

Etymology

From Latin 'decorare' (to adorn, embellish), from 'decus' (ornament).

5. VERB NEW

/ ˈdɛkəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəreɪt / (BrE)
(in programming) to modify or extend the behavior of a function, method, or class by applying a decorator — a callable or construct that wraps or alters it.
apply a decorator to code

Examples

In Python, you can decorate a function with @log to automatically record every call and its arguments.
She decorated the class with a validation decorator so instances validate input when created.
They avoided decorating simple functions to keep the code clear and easy to test.
