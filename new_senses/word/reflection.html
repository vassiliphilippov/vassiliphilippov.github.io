reflection

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
a thought or remark resulting from careful consideration or contemplation
carefully considered thought or remark

Examples

After some reflection, he decided to accept the job offer.
Her essay ended with a reflection on the meaning of friendship.
The book is full of wise reflections about life and happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - a verbal or written remark
  • Synonym: observation - a statement based on something one has noticed

Etymology

From Latin reflectere, 'to bend back', via Old French reflexion.

8. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) A transformation in which a figure is flipped across a line or plane, reversing the direction along one axis to produce a mirror image.
geometric transformation flipping across an axis

Examples

A reflection in mathematics results in a shape with reversed orientation.
If you apply a reflection followed by another reflection across the same line, the figure returns to its original position.
The triangle underwent a reflection over the y-axis, producing a mirror image.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rotation - A transformation that turns a figure around a fixed point.

Etymology

9. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
(in programming) the ability of a program to examine and, in some languages, modify its own classes, methods, or metadata at runtime.
programming feature to inspect/modify code at runtime

Examples

Java's reflection API allows developers to inspect classes and call methods dynamically at runtime.
Using reflection, the testing framework created mock objects for private methods during unit tests.
Overuse of reflection can make code slower and harder to understand and maintain.
