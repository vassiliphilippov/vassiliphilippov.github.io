virologist

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈrɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / vaɪˈrɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A scientist or medical specialist who studies viruses and viral diseases.
a virus expert or specialist
medical

Examples

After years of research, the virologist made a breakthrough in understanding how the disease spreads.
Hospitals often consult a virologist during outbreaks of new infectious diseases.
The virologist explained the process of virus replication to the audience at the science fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virology - the study of viruses

Etymology

Formed from 'virology' (the study of viruses) + '-ist' (person who practices or is concerned with something).

2. NOUN NEW

/ vaɪˈrɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / vaɪˈrɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) a specialist who studies computer viruses, malware, and their behaviour in order to detect, analyze, and defend against digital infections.
specialist in computer viruses

Examples

The cybersecurity firm hired a virologist to analyze how the worm moved across corporate networks.
As a virologist, she studies malware behaviour to design better antivirus and containment tools.
At the conference, the virologist presented a paper on cross‑platform virus propagation and defence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malware analyst - a professional who studies and analyzes malicious software (malware) to understand behaviour and develop defenses
  • Synonym: computer virology - the study of computer viruses, worms, and related malicious code

Etymology

From 'virology' + '-ist'; used metaphorically for the study of computer viruses and malware.
