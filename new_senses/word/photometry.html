photometry

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɑːmətri / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɒmətri / (BrE)
the scientific measurement of the properties of light, especially its luminous intensity
measurement of light intensity

Examples

Advances in photometry have improved the accuracy of satellite imaging.
Astronomers use photometry to determine the magnitude of distant stars.
The laboratory specializes in photometry, measuring the brightness of different light sources.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōt-' (light) and 'metron' (measure).

2. NOUN NEW

/ foʊˈtɑːmətri / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɒmətri / (BrE)
(in neuroscience) the measurement of light emitted by fluorescent indicators in living tissue, typically using optical fibers or microscopes to record changes that reflect neural activity.
measuring fluorescence to record neural activity

Examples

Researchers used photometry to measure fluorescent signals from dopamine neurons during the behavioral task.
The lab's fiber-optic photometry setup allowed recording in freely moving mice over several days.
After drug administration, they analyzed photometry traces to infer changes in neural population activity.
