detoxification

1. NOUN

/ diˌtɑksəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌtɒksɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing or neutralizing toxins from the body, especially as a medical treatment for poisoning or dependence
removal of toxins, especially medically
medical

Examples

After the incident, the patient underwent detoxification to eliminate the harmful substances from his bloodstream.
She checked into a clinic that specializes in detoxification for those recovering from alcohol abuse.
The liver plays a vital role in natural detoxification within the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detox - informal abbreviation for detoxification

Etymology

From de- (removal) + toxification (making toxic), based on Latin 'toxicum' (poison).

2. NOUN

/ diˌtɑksəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌtɒksɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical treatment for addiction to drugs or alcohol, which removes the physiological effects of the addictive substances from the body
medical treatment to remove addictive substances
medical

Examples

After entering the clinic, Mark underwent detoxification to rid his body of harmful substances.
Effective detoxification is often the first step in long-term addiction treatment.
The hospital provides a special unit for alcohol detoxification and recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehabilitation - the action of restoring someone to health or normal life through training and therapy after addiction
  • Antonym: intoxication - the state of being affected by drugs or alcohol

Etymology

From detoxify + -ation, based on Latin toxica (poison).

environmental removal of toxins

Examples

The factory paid for detoxification of the contaminated river to protect wildlife and downstream communities.
Engineers designed a treatment system for the detoxification of industrial wastewater before it reached municipal sewers.
Researchers study plant-based detoxification methods to remove heavy metals from urban soils near old factories.
