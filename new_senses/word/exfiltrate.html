to exfiltrate

1. VERB

/ ɛksˈfɪl.treɪt / (AmE), / ɛksˈfɪl.treɪt / (BrE)
to escape or withdraw secretly or stealthily, especially from a hostile area or under enemy control
escape or withdraw secretly

Examples

After completing the mission, the team needed to exfiltrate through the dense forest.
It took several hours for the soldiers to exfiltrate without being detected by patrols.
The agents were ordered to exfiltrate before enemy forces arrived in the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infiltrate - to enter or gain access to secretly, especially for subversive purposes

Etymology

From Latin ex- (out of) and filtrare (to filter).

2. VERB

/ ɛksˈfɪl.treɪt / (AmE), / ɛksˈfɪl.treɪt / (BrE)
to secretly remove someone or something from an area, especially from a place under enemy control, typically for protective or strategic purposes
remove secretly from hostile area

Examples

After the mission was compromised, the team quickly exfiltrated before reinforcements arrived.
The special forces managed to exfiltrate the hostages from enemy territory under the cover of darkness.
They developed a plan to exfiltrate sensitive documents without arousing suspicion.

Synset Examples

the military commander was exfiltrated on a French plane

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infiltrate - to secretly enter or gain access to an area, especially for espionage or subversion

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'infiltrate,' from Latin 'ex-' (out of) + 'filtrare' (to filter).

3. VERB NEW

/ ɛksˈfɪltreɪt / (AmE), / ɛksˈfɪltreɪt / (BrE)
(in computing, especially cyber security) to secretly transfer data or files from a computer system or network, typically without authorization, as part of a breach or attack.
secretly transfer data (cyber)

Examples

The attacker managed to exfiltrate sensitive files from the corporation's servers overnight.
Investigators discovered that the hackers had exfiltrated customer records before the ransomware spread.
While monitoring the network, she noticed a process quietly exfiltrating configuration backups to an unknown server.

Synset Examples

The attacker managed to exfiltrate sensitive files from the corporation's servers overnight.
Investigators discovered that the hackers had exfiltrated customer records before the ransomware spread.
While monitoring the network, she noticed a process quietly exfiltrating configuration backups to an unknown server.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data exfiltration - the act of illicitly transferring data out of a system or network
  • Synonym: data theft - the illicit taking or copying of digital information

Etymology

From Latin ex- (out of) + filtrare (to filter); applied in modern computing as a metaphorical extension meaning to remove data.
