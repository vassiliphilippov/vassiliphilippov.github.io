self-awareness

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈwɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈweənəs / (BrE)
conscious knowledge and understanding of your own character, feelings, motives, and individuality.
conscious knowledge of one's own individuality

Examples

Increased self-awareness can help you make better decisions and improve your relationships.
Meditation is often practiced to enhance self-awareness and reduce stress.
She displayed remarkable self-awareness during the interview, openly discussing her strengths and weaknesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-consciousness - awareness of oneself, sometimes with discomfort

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'awareness', both of Old English origin.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ccs5blf 59c8w5brn59s / (AmE), / ccs5blf 59c8we59n59s / (BrE)
(in computing and robotics) the ability of a system or agent to monitor and represent its own internal state, processes, or actions so it can adapt or control its behaviour autonomously.
system monitoring its own state

Examples

Researchers debate whether a robot's self-awareness requires subjective experience or only state-monitoring.
Modern AI systems can show limited self-awareness by tracking internal errors and adjusting strategies.
Engineers test autonomous cars for self-awareness so they respond safely to unexpected sensor failures.
