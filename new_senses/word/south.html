South

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ / (AmE), / saʊθ / (BrE)
the southern region of the United States, typically considered to be the states south of the Mason-Dixon line
the southern region of the U.S.

Examples

Many cultural traditions in the South differ from those in other regions of the country.
She grew up in the South and still loves the warm climate there.
The Civil Rights Movement had a significant impact on the South.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: the North - the northern region of the United States, especially during the Civil War era

2. NOUN

/ saʊθ / (AmE), / saʊθ / (BrE)
The group of southern states in the United States that seceded in 1861, forming the Confederacy during the American Civil War.
the US states that formed the Confederacy
american

Examples

After the defeat of the Confederacy, the South underwent a period of Reconstruction.
Many battles of the Civil War were fought in the South.
The South played a pivotal role in the outbreak of the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Confederacy - the group of southern states that seceded from the US in 1861
  • Antonym: North - the states of the northern US, especially those opposed to the Confederacy

3. NOUN NEW

/ saʊθ / (AmE), / saʊθ / (BrE)
(the Global South) the countries and regions, especially in Africa, Latin America, and parts of Asia and Oceania, that are often described as less economically developed and contrasted with the Global North.
the Global South; developing regions

Examples

Many development programs focus on the needs of the South rather than the wealthy North.
Countries in the South often face higher debt and lower access to healthcare services.
A recent report compared education outcomes between the South and the Global North.
