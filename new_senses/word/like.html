12. PART NEW

/ laɪk / (AmE), / laɪk / (BrE)
Informal discourse particle used to introduce reported speech or a thought, or to hedge/soften a statement; often appears in spoken and casual written English (as in 'he was like, "..."').
informal quotative or hedging particle
informal

Examples

When he heard the news, he was like, 'I didn't expect this at all'.
She was like going to quit, which made her manager take the problem seriously.
Kids often begin stories with like, 'You won't believe what I did yesterday,' to sound dramatic.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: say - to express something in words; introduces reported speech similarly to the quotative 'like'.
  • Synonym: hedge - to make a statement less direct or certain; 'like' can function to soften claims.

Etymology

From Old English lician (to please). The quotative/hedging use developed more recently in informal speech from the phrase 'be like' and became common in late 20th-century colloquial English.
