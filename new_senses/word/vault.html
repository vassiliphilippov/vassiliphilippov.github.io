vault

1. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
a strong room or compartment, often made of steel, used for storing valuable items securely
secure room for valuables

Examples

Access to the company’s vault is restricted to authorized personnel only.
The bank keeps gold bars in a high-security vault deep underground.
They discovered ancient coins locked away in the old family vault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safe - a strong container for storing valuables securely

Etymology

From Latin 'volta' (arched structure), via Old French 'voute'.

2. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
an act of leaping or jumping over something, especially using the hands or a pole for support
act of jumping over something

Examples

He cleared the fence with a single vault during the escape.
Her record-breaking vault secured her first place in the competition.
The gymnast's vault over the horse was flawless and impressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - a forceful jump or quick movement
  • Synonym: dive - a jump or plunge headfirst into something

Etymology

From Old French volter, from Latin volvitare, frequentative of volvere 'to roll, turn'.

7. NOUN NEW

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
a digital or software-based secure storage system for sensitive secrets such as passwords, API keys, tokens, or cryptographic keys, often providing access control and auditing.
secure digital storage for secrets

Examples

Developers store API keys in a vault to prevent accidental exposure during deployments.
The security team configured role-based access so only admins can retrieve secrets from the vault.
After the breach, they rotated all tokens stored in the central vault and revoked old keys.
