appliance

1. NOUN

/ əˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / əˈplaɪ.əns / (BrE)
A device or machine, especially one operated by electricity, that is used for a particular household job or task.
a device for a specific job, often electrical

Examples

A washing machine is an appliance used for cleaning clothes.
He unplugged the appliance before attempting to repair it.
The kitchen was filled with every modern appliance you could imagine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device - an object or tool invented for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: gadget - a small mechanical or electronic device with a practical use

Etymology

From Old French 'appliance', from 'appliquer' meaning to apply.

2. NOUN

/ əˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / əˈplaɪəns / (BrE)
a device or piece of equipment, especially electrical, used for household or office tasks such as cleaning, cooking, or computing
device used in the home or office

Examples

Businesses often invest in energy-efficient appliances to reduce electricity costs.
She bought a new appliance to help with laundry.
The kitchen was equipped with every modern appliance imaginable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device - a thing made or adapted for a particular purpose, especially a piece of mechanical or electronic equipment
  • Synonym: machine - an apparatus using mechanical power to perform work

Etymology

From Old French *appliance* and Medieval Latin *appliantia* (application, fitness), from *appliquer* (to apply).

3. NOUN NEW

/ əˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / əˈplaɪəns / (BrE)
(computing) A prepackaged, self-contained software image or virtual device that includes an application and the minimal operating environment needed to run it on hardware, a virtual machine, or in the cloud.
prepackaged software image or virtual device

Examples

The company deployed a virtual appliance to handle secure web traffic in the cloud.
Instead of installing many components, they bought a preconfigured appliance that ran on a virtual machine.
Administrators update the router appliance image before deploying it across their production cloud servers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtual appliance - a synonym often used for prepackaged software images that run on virtual machines
  • Synonym: software appliance - a packaged software product combined with a minimal operating system to run as an appliance

Etymology

From Old French 'appliance' (application), from Latin *applicare* 'to attach, apply'. Extended in the 21st century to mean a preconfigured software image or virtual device in computing.
