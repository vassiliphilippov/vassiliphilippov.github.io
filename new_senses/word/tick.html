3. NOUN

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
a small parasitic arachnid with a barbed mouthpart, feeding on the blood of warm-blooded animals, often carrying diseases
small blood-sucking arachnid

Examples

Removing a tick promptly and correctly reduces the risk of infection.
She found a tick attached to her dog's ear after their walk in the woods.
Some ticks can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - a very small arachnid, related to ticks but not parasitic on larger animals

Etymology

Old English *ticca*, related to German *Zecke*, meaning 'tick'.
