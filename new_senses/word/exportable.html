exportable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɔrtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
suitable for being exported to another country, especially in terms of goods, services, or achievements.
suitable for export

Examples

Many agricultural products are considered highly exportable due to their global demand.
The country’s exportable resources include oil, coffee, and minerals.
The government is working to develop more exportable cultural achievements to share with the world.

Synset Examples

exportable cultural achievements

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importable - suitable for being imported

Etymology

From 'export' + '-able'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00880298-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ NEW

/ ɪkˈspɔrtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
(in information technology) able to be exported from a software system to another format, platform, or application (for example data, reports, settings).
able to be exported from software

Examples

The dashboard allows users to make charts exportable to CSV, JSON, or XML for offline analysis.
Ensure all customer reports are exportable so the marketing team can review them in spreadsheets.
The new CRM stores contacts in a format that keeps them easily exportable between different systems.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: export - to transfer or send data, files, or goods out of a system or country
  • Antonym: importable - able to be imported into a system or application; opposite of exportable in data-transfer contexts

Etymology

From export + -able.
