right-winger

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who supports or belongs to the right wing of a political party or group, typically favoring conservative or traditional policies
supporter of right-wing politics

Examples

Despite being a right-winger, she supported some progressive social policies.
He has always been known as a staunch right-winger in Parliament.
The debate grew heated when right-wingers criticized the proposed reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds to traditional values and attitudes, typically in politics
  • Antonym: left-winger - a person who supports or belongs to the left wing of a political party or group

Etymology

From 'right-wing' (referring to conservative political positions) + '-er' (denoting a person).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋə / (BrE)
a player who plays on the right wing of a team in sports such as football (soccer), hockey, or rugby, responsible for attacking or defending on the right flank.
player on the right wing (sports)

Examples

The right-winger sprinted down the flank and delivered a perfect cross into the box.
Fans cheered when the right-winger cut inside and curled the ball into the top corner.
Our coach asked the right-winger to track back and support the full-back defensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winger - a player who plays on either wing (left or right) in certain team sports.
  • Antonym: left-winger - a player who operates on the left wing; positional opposite in many sports.

Etymology

From 'right wing' (the right side of a formation or field) + '-er' (one who occupies that position).
