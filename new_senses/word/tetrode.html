tetrode

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəʊd / (BrE)
a vacuum tube with four electrodes, used especially in electronic circuits to amplify or switch signals
vacuum tube with four electrodes

Examples

A tetrode differs from a triode by having an additional screen grid between the plate and the control grid.
Engineers replaced the damaged tetrode in the amplifier circuit to restore proper function.
The old radio used a tetrode to boost the weak signals it received.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: triode - a vacuum tube with three electrodes
  • Degree Higher: pentode - a vacuum tube with five electrodes

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) + '-ode' (way, path, from 'hodos').

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8t5btccro8ad / (AmE), / c8t5btr598ad / (BrE)
(in neuroscience and electrophysiology) a small neural recording probe made of four closely spaced microelectrodes used to record action potentials from individual neurons.
four-electrode neural recording probe

Examples

The researchers implanted a tetrode to record single-neuron activity in the hippocampus.
Using a bundle of four wires, she positioned the tetrode near the cortical neurons to isolate spikes.
The lab protocol describes how to clean and calibrate each tetrode before implanting it in animals.
