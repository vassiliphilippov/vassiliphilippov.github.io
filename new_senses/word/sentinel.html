sentinel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɪn.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɪ.nəl / (BrE)
a person employed to keep watch for some anticipated event; a guard or lookout
guard or person keeping watch

Examples

A sentinel stood at the gate, watching for any sign of movement in the distance.
As a sentinel, her job was to alert the team if anyone approached the camp.
The castle was protected day and night by vigilant sentinels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch or protects something or someone
  • Synonym: lookout - a person stationed to keep watch for danger or trouble
  • Antonym: intruder - a person who enters a place without permission, often opposed by sentinels

Etymology

From Old French 'sentinelle', from Italian 'sentinella', from 'sentire' (to hear).

The database cluster uses a sentinel to monitor node health and trigger automatic failover.
Our deployment includes multiple sentinel instances that alert engineers when latency exceeds thresholds.
Cloud providers offer a sentinel service to continuously check service health and restart failing nodes.
