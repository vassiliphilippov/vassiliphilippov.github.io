de novo

1. ADV

/ də ˈnoʊvoʊ / (AmE), / deɪ ˈnəʊvəʊ / (BrE)
starting from the beginning; anew, especially used in legal, scientific, or technical contexts.
from the beginning; anew

Examples

If the court finds errors, the case may be tried de novo.
Researchers grew the tissue de novo from stem cells in the laboratory.
The experiment was repeated de novo to verify the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afresh - again, in a new or different way
  • Synonym: anew - again, in a new way or from the beginning

Etymology

From Latin 'de novo' (from the new, anew).

2. ADJ NEW

/ də ˈnoʊvoʊ / (AmE), / deɪ ˈnəʊvəʊ / (BrE)
(in genetics and medical contexts) of a mutation or genetic variant: arising newly in an individual and not inherited from either parent.
spontaneous; not inherited (genetics)
medical

Examples

The diagnosis identified a de novo mutation in the gene, absent from both parents' DNA.
Researchers reported several de novo variants that likely explain the child's developmental delay.
Whole-genome sequencing can reveal previously undetected de novo changes contributing to rare diseases.
