chooser

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃuzər / (AmE), / ˈtʃuːzə / (BrE)
a person who chooses or selects something from a group or range of options
person who chooses or selects

Examples

As the appointed chooser, she picked the nominees for the award.
Every chooser in the survey had different criteria for their selections.
In the game, the chooser must decide between two difficult options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selector - a person or thing that selects

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8t\u0283uz\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8t\u0283u\u02d0z\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing) a graphical user interface control or dialog that presents options and lets the user select a file, colour, or other item.
software selection control

Examples

The chooser window allowed her to pick multiple image files at once for upload.
He opened the color chooser to find a shade that matched the company logo.
In the settings, the file chooser defaulted to the Documents folder for convenience.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selector - a UI control that lets a user choose an item from a list or options
  • Synonym: picker - a control such as a color picker or date picker used to select values

Etymology

From the verb choose + agent/instrument suffix -er; extended in computing to name interface controls (e.g., file chooser).
