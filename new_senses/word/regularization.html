regularization

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something regular, uniform, or systematic
making something regular or uniform

Examples

In mathematics, regularization techniques are used to make equations solvable.
The company implemented regularization of its procedures to ensure consistency across all departments.
The linguist studied the regularization of verb forms in the new dialect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standardization - the process of making something conform to a standard
  • Antonym: irregularity - the state or quality of being irregular

Etymology

From regular + -ization, following the pattern of 'regularize' and '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of making something regular, orderly, or standardized
act of making regular or orderly

Examples

Language reforms often involve the regularization of spelling rules.
The regularization of the schedule made meetings much easier to attend.
The new policy led to the regularization of previously informal contracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standardization - the process of making everything conform to a standard
  • Antonym: irregularity - the state of being not regular or orderly

Etymology

From regular + -ization, modeled after French régularisation.
