Stephanotis

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛfəˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌstɛfəˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World tropical woody vines, known for their fragrant, white, tubular flowers, often cultivated as ornamental plants.
genus of tropical woody vines

Examples

Because Stephanotis thrives in warm climates, it is a popular choice in tropical gardens worldwide.
Florists often use Stephanotis flowers in wedding bouquets for their beauty and delicate scent.
The Stephanotis vine is prized for its waxy, fragrant blossoms that resemble small trumpets.

Etymology

From Greek 'stephanos' (crown) and 'otos' (ear), referring to the crown-like arrangement of the flower's stamens.
