DM

1. NOUN

/ diː ɛm / (AmE), / diː ɛm / (BrE)
An abbreviation for diabetes mellitus, a disease caused by insufficient insulin and characterized by excessive urination.
abbreviation for diabetes mellitus
medical

Examples

Effective management of DM requires regular monitoring of blood glucose levels.
Many studies focus on the complications associated with DM in adults.
The nurse noted that the patient had a long history of DM managed by insulin therapy.

Synset Examples

when doctors say ‘diabetes’ they usually mean ‘diabetes mellitus’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabetes mellitus - chronic disease of high blood sugar due to insulin problems

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'diabetes mellitus', from Latin 'diabetes' (siphon) + 'mellitus' (honeyed).

2. NOUN NEW

/ diː ɛm / (AmE), / diː ɛm / (BrE)
A private message sent directly to a user on a social media platform or messaging app, not visible to the public.
direct/private message on social media
informal

Examples

She sent a DM to confirm the meeting details with her colleague.
If you have time, please check your DMs for the event link I posted.
He preferred to handle customer complaints in a DM rather than publicly on the page.

Synset Examples

She sent a DM to confirm the meeting details with her colleague.
If you have time, please check your DMs for the event link I posted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct message - a private message sent directly to a single user on a social platform
  • Synonym: private message - a message sent privately between users rather than publicly
  • Antonym: public post - a message or update visible to all followers or the public, as opposed to a private DM

Etymology

Abbreviation of "direct message" (early 21st century, from social media and messaging apps).
