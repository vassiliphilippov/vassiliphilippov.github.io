to delist

1. VERB

/ diˈlɪst / (AmE), / diˈlɪst / (BrE)
to remove (a security or company) from the official list of a stock exchange so that it is no longer publicly traded there
remove from a stock exchange listing

Examples

After repeated violations, the exchange threatened to delist the firm for failing to meet requirements.
If profits do not improve, the board may vote to delist the stock.
The company decided to delist its shares from the New York Stock Exchange.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: list - to register or admit (a security) to trading on a stock exchange

Etymology

Formed from prefix de- (removal) and list (registered item).

2. VERB NEW

/ diˈlɪst / (AmE), / diˈlɪst / (BrE)
to remove a product, app, game, film, or other content from an online store or distribution platform so it is no longer available for purchase, download, or streaming.
remove from an online platform

Examples

After licensing issues, the studio decided to delist the film from the streaming platform worldwide.
Players complained when the developer delisted the popular game and removed its in-game purchases.
Once the app was delisted, users could no longer download updates or restore purchases.

Synset Examples

After licensing issues, the studio decided to delist the film from the streaming platform worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away or make it no longer available
  • Synonym: unpublish - to take content offline so it is no longer publicly accessible
  • Antonym: list - to make a product or security available on a platform or exchange; opposite of delist

Etymology

From prefix de- (removal) + list (register or catalogue); extended from financial usage to digital platforms in the 21st century.
