dude

1. NOUN

/ duːd / (AmE), / djuːd / (BrE)
an informal term used to address or refer to a man
informal term for a man
american informal

Examples

Hey dude, are you coming to the party tonight?
Relax, dude—it's not a big deal.
The dude at the counter helped me find the right size.

Synset Examples

Say, fellow, what are you doing?
Hey buster, what's up?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guy - informal term for a man

Etymology

Originally American English, coined in the late 19th century, possibly from the word 'doodle.'

2. NOUN

/ dud / (AmE), / djuːd / (BrE)
a man who is very concerned with his dress and appearance, often considered overly fashionable or affected
man obsessed with fashion/appearance

Examples

At the party, everyone noticed the dude in the brightly colored suit and perfectly styled hair.
He likes to think of himself as a modern dude, spending hours getting ready every morning.
In the 19th century, a true dude would never be seen without his silk cravat and polished shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man unduly devoted to style, neatness, and fashion in dress and appearance
  • Synonym: fop - a man excessively concerned with his clothes and appearance

Etymology

Originally American English, late 19th century; possibly from 'doodle' as in 'Yankee Doodle'.

Examples
