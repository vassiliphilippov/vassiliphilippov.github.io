booster

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːstɚ / (AmE), / ˈbuːstə / (BrE)
the first stage of a multistage rocket, providing initial thrust to launch the vehicle
first stage of a multistage rocket

Examples

Engineers inspected the booster for signs of damage after launch.
The main booster separated after two minutes, allowing the next stage to ignite.
Without a reliable booster, the rocket cannot escape Earth’s gravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage - a section of a rocket that operates at a particular time during launch and is then discarded
