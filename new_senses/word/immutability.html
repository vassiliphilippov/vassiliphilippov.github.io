immutability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.juː.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuː.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being unchanging or unable to be altered
the state of being unchangeable

Examples

He admired the immutability of her principles, even in difficult situations.
Many religions emphasize the immutability of divine truths.
The immutability of the law means it cannot be changed by ordinary procedures.

Synset Examples

Darwin challenged the fixity of species

Related Terms

  • Antonym: changeability - the quality of being capable of change

Etymology

From Latin immutabilis (unchangeable), from in- (not) + mutabilis (changeable).

(in computing) the property of data, values, or objects that cannot be changed after they are created, often used to enable safer concurrent code and referential transparency.
unchangeable data in computing

Examples

In functional programming, immutability helps prevent bugs by ensuring data cannot be changed.
The library's API enforces immutability for its objects to simplify reasoning about state changes.
Because of immutability, concurrent threads can read shared data without locks, improving performance.

Synset Examples

From Latin immutabilis (unchangeable), from in- (not) + mutabilis (changeable). Extended in computing to describe data or objects that cannot be changed after creation.
