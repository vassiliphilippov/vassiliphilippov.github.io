teredo

1. NOUN

/ təˈriːdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈriːdəʊ / (BrE)
A marine bivalve mollusk of the genus Teredo, commonly known as a shipworm, which bores into and often destroys submerged wood.
marine mollusk (shipworm genus)

Examples

A marine biologist discovered several teredo burrows in the sunken hull.
The old pier was infested with teredo, causing extensive structural damage.
Wooden ships often suffered from teredo attacks in tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - Marine mollusk that bores into wood

Etymology

From Latin 'terēdō' (woodworm), from Greek 'tereidōn'.

(in computer networking) an IPv6-over-IPv4 tunneling protocol that allows IPv6 packets to be carried through IPv4 networks, typically using UDP to traverse NATs.

Examples

Many home routers still support teredo tunneling to give IPv6 connectivity over IPv4 networks.

  • Synonym: tunneling - sending one network protocol inside another (for example, carrying IPv6 packets inside IPv4 packets).
  • Synonym: NAT traversal - techniques that allow network traffic to pass through network address translators so hosts behind NATs can communicate.

Etymology

Named after the shipworm Teredo (a marine bivalve); the name evokes the idea of 'boring' or tunneling IPv6 traffic through IPv4 networks.
