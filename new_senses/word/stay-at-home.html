stay-at-home

1. NOUN

/ ˌsteɪ ət ˈhoʊm / (AmE), / ˌsteɪ ət ˈhəʊm / (BrE)
a person who seldom goes out or travels; someone who prefers to remain at home
person who seldom leaves home

Examples

After years of being a stay-at-home, he finally decided to travel abroad.
Being a stay-at-home isn't for everyone, but some find it very comforting.
Sarah prefers the life of a stay-at-home rather than going out to parties every weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homebody - a person who likes to stay at home

2. ADJ

/ ˌsteɪ æt ˈhoʊm / (AmE), / ˌsteɪ æt ˈhəʊm / (BrE)
(of a person) preferring to remain at home rather than travel or go out socially.
preferring to stay at home

Examples

Although her friends traveled often, Emily was a true stay-at-home who preferred her own company.
Being a stay-at-home type, he rarely went out to parties or events.
Her stay-at-home nature made her the go-to person whenever someone needed house-sitting.

/ ccste6a 59t 8ho8am / (AmE), / ccste6a 8h598am / (BrE)
(in public health/government) an official order or directive requiring people to remain at home and avoid non-essential travel or gatherings, usually to slow the spread of disease.
official order to remain at home

Examples

The government announced a nationwide stay-at-home order to curb the rapidly rising infection rate.
During the lockdown, many businesses closed after the city issued multiple stay-at-home directives.
The mayor extended the stay-at-home period by two weeks to reduce hospital admissions.
