2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɒkˌaʊt / (BrE)
An act of knocking someone unconscious with a blow, especially in boxing; a hit or punch that results in an opponent being unable to continue.
a blow that renders an opponent unconscious

Examples

His right hook delivered a knockout that left his opponent unconscious.
Spectators erupted in cheers after the unexpected knockout.
The boxer won the match with a stunning knockout in the third round.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: knockdown - a blow that knocks an opponent to the ground but not unconscious

Etymology

From the phrase 'knock out', meaning to render unconscious; first used in boxing in the late 19th century.

4. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8n\u0251\u02d0k\u02cca\u028at / (AmE), / \u02c8n\u0252k\u02cca\u028at / (BrE)
(in genetics and biomedical research) a living organism in which a specific gene has been deliberately inactivated or 'knocked out' to study that gene's function.
organism with inactivated gene

Examples

The researchers created a knockout mouse to study the gene's role in metabolism.
After breeding, they observed that the knockout showed no normal immune response to infection.
Using CRISPR, she generated a knockout line to confirm the protein's function in liver cells.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene knockout - the deliberate inactivation or removal of a specific gene in an organism for study
  • Antonym: wild type - an organism that has the typical form of a gene or phenotype found in nature; opposite of a genetic knockout

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'knock out' (to render inactive or remove), extended in late 20th-century biology to mean deliberate inactivation of a gene.
