workspace

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːrk.speɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.speɪs / (BrE)
an area or room where you do your work, especially in an office or at a computer
area allocated for work

Examples

My new office has a much larger workspace with plenty of room for my computer and files.
Please tidy your workspace before leaving at the end of the day.
The company redesigned the workspace to encourage more collaboration between employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workstation - a desk or area with equipment where one works, especially with a computer

Etymology

From 'work' + 'space', originally describing technical or office environments.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8w\u025c\u02d0rk.spe\u026as / (AmE), / \u02c8w\u025c\u02d0k.spe\u026as / (BrE)
(in computing and business) a virtual environment within software or a cloud platform where people or teams store files, share resources, and organize tasks and projects to collaborate.
virtual collaborative environment

Examples

The company migrated to a shared workspace to collaborate on documents and track tasks in real time.
She opened the project workspace and assigned new tasks to the design team.
They created two new workspaces to separate marketing and product materials.
