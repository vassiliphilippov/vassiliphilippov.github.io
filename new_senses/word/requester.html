requester

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwɛstər / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɛstə / (BrE)
a person who humbly asks or prays for something, especially as a petitioner or supplicant
person making a humble request or prayer

Examples

In ancient times, a requester would approach the king with utmost humility.
She acted as a requester, seeking assistance for her family from charitable organizations.
The requester knelt before the altar, whispering his plea for forgiveness.

Synset Examples

a suppliant for her favors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppliant - a person making a humble or earnest entreaty
  • Synonym: petitioner - a person who makes a formal, humble request

Etymology

From 'request' + '-er', modeled in analogy on forms like 'petitioner' or 'supplicant'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈkwɛstər / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɛstə / (BrE)
(in computing) an entity (for example, a program, user, or process) that initiates a request to a service, API, or network resource.
initiator of API or network requests

Examples

Before sending data, the requester must include a valid API key in the header.
The server logged many failed attempts because anonymous requesters lacked proper authentication tokens.
In the load test, each simulated requester opened dozens of concurrent connections to measure throughput.

Synset Examples
