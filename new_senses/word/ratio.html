ratio

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
the quantitative relationship between two numbers showing how many times one value contains or is contained within the other, usually expressed as a quotient
the quantitative relationship between two numbers

Examples

That company has a low debt-to-equity ratio, making it attractive to investors.
The ratio of sugar to flour in the recipe is 2 to 1.
To solve the problem, you need to simplify the ratio of boys to girls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportion - a part, share, or number considered in comparative relation to a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'ratio' (reason, calculation, proportion).
