6. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
In genetics and oncology, a mutation or genetic alteration that contributes to the initiation, growth, or progression of cancer, as opposed to a 'passenger' mutation that is incidental.
mutation that promotes cancer
medical

Examples

Researchers identified a driver mutation that accelerated cancer progression in the patient.
The oncologist explained that several driver mutations were responsible for the tumor's rapid growth.
Tumor sequencing often reveals hidden drivers that guide decisions about targeted therapies.
