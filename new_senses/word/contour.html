contour

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.tʊr / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tʊə / (BrE)
the distinctive outline, shape, or arrangement of features that defines something, especially in a complex structure
outline or arrangement of features

Examples

She described the contour of his argument with compelling clarity.
The contours of the city's economic policy shifted after the election.
The new novel traces the contour of a changing society.

Synset Examples

the contours of the melody
it defines a major contour of this administration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - the general shape or edge of something without the detail

Etymology

From French 'contour', from Latin 'contornare' (to turn around).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.tʊr / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tʊə / (BrE)
A line on a map that joins points of equal elevation above a given level, such as sea level.
line joining points of equal height on a map

Examples

A contour can show where the slope of a hill becomes steeper.
Atlantic City is only a few meters above the 10-meter contour along the coast.
The hikers followed the contour lines on the map to avoid steep climbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isoline - a line on a map connecting points having the same value of a given quantity

Etymology

From French *contour* (outline, perimeter), from Latin *contornare* (to turn around).

/ ˈkɑːn.tʊr / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tʊə / (BrE)
the outline or shape that defines the boundaries of an object or form
outline or shape of something

Examples

She traced the contour of the vase with her fingers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From French 'contour', from Latin 'contour', from 'con-' (together) + 'tornare' (to turn on a lathe).

4. VERB

/ ˈkɑnˌtʊr / (AmE), / ˈkɒntʊə / (BrE)
to shape or form the outline or surface of something, especially so as to follow its natural curves or features

Examples

She used makeup to contour her cheekbones for a more defined look.
The hills have been carefully contoured to prevent soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - to draw or mark the outer edge of something
