coiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪnər / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪnə / (BrE)
a person who makes counterfeit coins
person who makes fake coins

Examples

In the 18th century, a coiner could face the death penalty if caught.
The old novel tells the story of a clever coiner evading the law.
The police apprehended the notorious coiner who had flooded the market with fake gold pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfeiter - a person who makes fake money or goods

Etymology

From 'coin' + '-er', denoting a person who produces coins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪnər / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪnə / (BrE)
a person who invents new words or expressions
person who invents new words

Examples

He fancied himself a clever coiner, inventing terms that soon caught on.
Many tech industry leaders have become coiners of jargon and buzzwords.
Shakespeare is often regarded as a prolific coiner of English words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neologist - a person who creates new words or expressions

Etymology

From 'coin' (to invent or create), originally from Old French 'coignier' (to stamp money).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪnər / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪnə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make coins by stamping or minting metal
person who makes coins

Examples

Every coiner followed strict guidelines to ensure the coins' authenticity.
He worked as a coiner at the mint for more than two decades.
The royal coiner produced thousands of silver coins each year.

Etymology

From 'coin' + '-er', denoting a person who makes coins.
