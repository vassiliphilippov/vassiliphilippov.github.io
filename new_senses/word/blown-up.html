blown-up

1. ADJ

/ bloʊn ˈʌp / (AmE), / bləʊn ˈʌp / (BrE)
(of a photograph or image) made larger than the original size.
made larger (of a photo or image)

Examples

I hung a blown-up photograph of my grandparents in the living room.
The blown-up image showed details that were impossible to see in the original.
The blown-up map on the wall helped everyone find their way more easily.

Synset Examples

the enlarged photograph revealed many details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlarged - (of an image or object) made larger

Etymology

Formed from verb phrase 'blow up,' meaning to enlarge, plus adjectival suffix.

2. ADJ NEW

/ bloʊn ˈʌp / (AmE), / bləʊn ˈʌp / (BrE)
(informal) (of online content) that has become suddenly very popular and widely shared; viral.
viral; widely shared online
informal

Examples

The comedian's clip turned into a blown-up sensation on social media.
After the influencer shared it, the song was suddenly blown-up across multiple platforms.
They posted a short sketch that became blown-up overnight and reached millions.

Synset Examples

The comedian's clip turned into a blown-up sensation on social media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viral - rapidly and widely shared or viewed online
  • Antonym: unnoticed - not seen or paid attention to; the opposite of widely shared

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'blow up' (to become suddenly popular), extended figuratively in the age of social media.
