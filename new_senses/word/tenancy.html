tenancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnənsi / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənsi / (BrE)
The possession or occupancy of land, property, or accommodation by a tenant for a specified period under a lease or rental agreement.
the state of being a tenant

Examples

During their tenancy, the tenants are responsible for keeping the property in good condition.
He signed a one-year tenancy agreement for the apartment.
The tenancy will expire at the end of next month unless renewed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use or occupation of property during a specified period in exchange for rent
  • Synonym: occupancy - the act of living in or using a building or land as a tenant
  • Antonym: ownership - the act, state, or right of possessing property

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *tenance*, from Old French *tenir* (to hold).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtɛnənsi / (AmE), / ˈtɛnənsi / (BrE)
(in computing, especially cloud and SaaS) the arrangement by which a single software instance serves multiple customers (tenants) while keeping each tenant's data and configuration logically isolated; often referred to as multi-tenancy.
multi-tenant software arrangement

Examples

The platform's tenancy model isolates each customer's data in a separate logical partition for security.
When designing the app, they chose a multi-tenant tenancy approach to reduce infrastructure costs.
Migrating to single-tenant hosting required changing the tenancy boundaries and exporting each customer's data separately.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multi-tenancy - an architecture in which a single software instance serves multiple customers with isolated data and configurations
  • Antonym: single-tenant - an architecture in which each customer has a separate instance of the software (opposite of multi-tenancy)

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman tenance, from Old French tenir 'to hold'; used metaphorically in computing from the property sense of holding/renting space.
