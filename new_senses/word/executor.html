executor

1. NOUN

/ ɪgˈzɛk.jə.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɛk.jʊ.tə / (BrE)
a person appointed to carry out the instructions and wishes in someone’s will after their death
person carrying out a will
legal

Examples

If no executor is appointed, the court may assign someone to administer the estate.
She was named executor and had to manage all legal documents after her uncle passed away.
The executor of the will is responsible for distributing the estate according to the deceased's wishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrator - person appointed to settle an estate when there is no will

Etymology

From Latin executor (one who carries out), from exsequi (to follow out).
