low-level

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
of low rank, position, or importance within an organization or system.
of low rank or importance

Examples

He started at a low-level position in the company and worked his way up.
It was a low-level meeting involving only junior staff members.
Many low-level employees were affected by the new policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-level - of high rank, position, or importance

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
(of flight or occurrence) taking place at a relatively low altitude, especially close to the ground.
occurring at a low altitude

Examples

Helicopters often perform low-level rescues in mountainous regions.
The mission required a low-level approach to avoid radar detection.
The plane made a low-level pass over the runway during the air show.
