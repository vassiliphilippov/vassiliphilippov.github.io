sideswipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌswaɪp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdswaɪp / (BrE)
a glancing blow or collision to the side of an object, especially the side of a vehicle
glancing blow to the side, especially of a vehicle

Examples

A sideswipe between two buses caused a traffic delay downtown.
The car suffered a sideswipe when the truck drifted into its lane.
There was only minor damage from the sideswipe in the parking lot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glancing blow - a blow that strikes at an angle rather than head-on
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07425213-n

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdˌswaɪp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌswaɪp / (BrE)
to strike or hit someone or something from the side, especially while moving past
strike from the side

Examples

He accidentally sideswiped the cyclist as he turned the corner too sharply.
The bus sideswiped a parked car, leaving a long scratch down its length.
Their car was sideswiped by a truck on the highway, but fortunately no one was hurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swipe - to strike or hit with a sweeping motion

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'swipe', meaning a sweeping blow from the side.
