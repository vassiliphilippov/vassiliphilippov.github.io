takedown

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪk.daʊn / (AmE), / ˈteɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
a sharp or devastating remark that effectively humiliates or defeats someone in argument or discussion
a crushing remark or put-down
informal

Examples

Her sarcastic reply was such a perfect takedown that it left everyone speechless.
The article is a thorough takedown of the author's flawed arguments.
The comedian delivered a brilliant takedown of the politician's contradictory statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put-down - an insulting or disparaging remark
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06729735-n

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈteɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
(in wrestling) an act of bringing an opponent down to the mat from a standing position, typically scoring points.
a move to bring an opponent to the mat (wrestling)

Examples

A well-executed takedown can change the momentum of a wrestling bout.
He scored a quick takedown in the first round to earn two points.
The referee awarded her a point for each successful takedown during the match.

Synset Examples

a takedown counts two points

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin - holding an opponent's shoulders on the mat (wrestling)

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'take down'.
