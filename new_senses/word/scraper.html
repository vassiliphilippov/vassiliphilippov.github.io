scraper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpə / (BrE)
a hand tool with a flat or slightly curved blade, used for removing material from a surface by scraping
hand tool for scraping surfaces

Examples

He used a scraper to remove the old paint from the window frame.
The chef picked up a scraper to clean the griddle after cooking.
You’ll need a stiff scraper to get rid of the gum stuck to the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatula - a flat-bladed tool used for spreading or scraping
  • Synonym: trowel - a small hand tool with a flat blade, used for applying and spreading plaster or similar materials

Etymology

From Middle English 'scrapen' (to scrape) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈskreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpə / (BrE)
(computing) a software program that automatically extracts structured data from websites by downloading and parsing pages or APIs.
software that extracts website data

Examples

The marketing team used a scraper to collect product prices from several competitor websites.
After the change, the site blocked the scraper by refusing requests without a valid API key.
Developers prefer writing a custom scraper when public APIs don't provide the needed detailed information.

Synset Examples

The marketing team used a scraper to collect product prices from several competitor websites.

Developers prefer writing a custom scraper when public APIs don't provide the needed detailed information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web scraper - a program specifically designed to collect data from web pages
  • Synonym: data extractor - software that extracts specific information from larger datasets or files

Etymology

From 'scrape' + agent suffix '-er'; extended metaphorically in computing to a program that 'scrapes' data from websites.
