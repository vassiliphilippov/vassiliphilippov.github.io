pear-shaped

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛr ˌʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈpeə ˌʃeɪpt / (BrE)
(of sounds or voices) full, rich, and rounded in quality.
rich and rounded (of sounds)

Examples

He had a rich, pear-shaped voice that commanded attention.
Her pear-shaped vowels made her voice particularly pleasing to the audience.
The opera singer's pear-shaped tones filled the concert hall with warmth.

Synset Examples

orotund tones
the rotund and reverberating phrase
pear-shaped vowels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orotund - (of a voice or sound) full, round, and imposing
  • Antonym: thin - (of a voice or sound) weak and lacking fullness

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛr ʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈpeə ʃeɪpt / (BrE)
having a rounded shape that is wider at the bottom and tapers toward the top, like a pear.
shaped like a pear, tapered at one end

Examples

She chose a pear-shaped diamond for her engagement ring.
The fruit was easily identified by its pear-shaped outline.
The vase has a delicate pear-shaped body with a narrow neck.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: round - having a shape like a circle or sphere
  • Synonym: teardrop-shaped - having a shape like a teardrop, similar to pear-shaped
