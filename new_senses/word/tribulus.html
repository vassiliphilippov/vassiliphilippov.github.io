Tribulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪbjələs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪbjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbs or subshrubs found in warm regions, some species of which are known for their spiny fruits.
genus of herbs or subshrubs in warm regions

Examples

Tribulus terrestris is a species commonly found in arid regions.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Tribulus in traditional herbal remedies.
The seeds of Tribulus plants are often covered in sharp spines.

Etymology

From Latin 'tribulus' (species of this plant), from Greek 'tribolos' (water-chestnut, caltrop).

genus of herbs or subshrubs (botany)

Examples

Many species within the Tribulus genus thrive in arid and semi-arid climates.
