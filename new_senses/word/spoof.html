spoof

1. NOUN

/ spuːf / (AmE), / spuːf / (BrE)
a humorous imitation of something, especially a film, television program, or style, intended to mock or satirize the original
comedically mocking imitation

Examples

Her new book is essentially a spoof of old-fashioned detective novels.
The show opened with a clever spoof of a famous political debate.
They created a hilarious spoof of the latest blockbuster movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - a work created to imitate, make fun of, or comment on an original work
  • Synonym: satire - use of humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize people's stupidity or vices
  • Antonym: homage - something that shows respect or tribute, often by imitation

Etymology

Originated in late 19th century as a game invented by British comedian Arthur Roberts, extended to mean a hoax or parody.

2. VERB

/ spuf / (AmE), / spuːf / (BrE)
to imitate or represent someone or something in a humorous or satirical way, often as a parody
to parody or make fun of

Examples

For April Fool's Day, the newspaper spoofed a serious news article with a satirical twist.
Many websites enjoy spoofing current events to entertain their readers.
The comedy troupe spoofed popular movies during their live performance.

Synset Examples

The students spoofed the teachers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - to imitate someone or something humorously
  • Synonym: satirize - to use satire to criticize or mock
  • Synonym: imitate - to copy the manner or style of someone or something

Etymology

Probably from 19th-century English card game 'Spoof,' meaning trick or hoax.

3. NOUN NEW

/ spuːf / (AmE), / spuːf / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) a deceptive falsification of a digital identity, message, or source intended to mislead others (for example, fake emails, caller ID, or websites).
digital identity deception

Examples

The attacker sent a convincing email that contained a spoof of the bank's login page.
His phone displayed a local number because the caller used a spoof to hide their identity.
After the breach they discovered a spoof had redirected customers to a fake website.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impersonation - pretending to be another person or entity to deceive others
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

From earlier noun meaning 'hoax' or 'trick' (late 19th century); extended in computing to mean forging a digital identity.
