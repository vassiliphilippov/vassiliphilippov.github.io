mailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlə / (BrE)
a container, such as an envelope or box, designed to hold items for mailing
container for mailing items

Examples

Please use a large mailer if you need to send these documents safely.
She packed the fragile vase in a padded mailer for extra protection.
The company buys mailers in bulk for shipping their products.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlə / (BrE)
an advertisement or promotional material sent to people by mail
advertisement sent by mail

Examples

Please put the mailer in the recycling bin if you're not interested in the offer.
The company designs a different mailer for each holiday season to attract new clients.
Thousands of customers received a colorful mailer promoting the new restaurant in their neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flyer - a small handbill advertising an event or product
  • Synonym: brochure - a small book or magazine containing information and advertisements

Etymology

From 'mail', meaning to send by post, plus the agent suffix '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlə / (BrE)
a person who sends mail or posts letters and packages
person who mails letters

Examples

As the designated mailer, she visits the post office every Monday to send the company’s parcels.
Each mailer must fill out a customs form for international packages.
The mailer forgot to put a return address on the envelope, so it could not be returned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sender - a person who sends something

Etymology

From 'mail' (to send letters or packages) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
