to deprecate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛprəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛprɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to express disapproval of or belittle; to speak of as unimportant or of little value
to belittle or express disapproval

Examples

It is unkind to deprecate others for their mistakes in public.
She tends to deprecate her own achievements out of modesty.
The manager should not deprecate the team's hard work during meetings.

Synset Examples

The teacher should not deprecate his student's efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin deprecat-, past participle of deprecari (to ward off by prayer, to avert).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00857615-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛprəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛprɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval of something; to criticize or deplore, especially formally or publicly
express strong disapproval

Examples

Many leading scientists deprecate the use of outdated methods in research.
She publicly deprecated the company's decision to cut jobs.
While he did not object outright, he subtly deprecated their plan with pointed remarks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approve - to have a positive opinion of; to consent

Etymology

From Latin 'deprecari' (to avert by prayer, to pray against), from 'de-' (away) + 'precari' (to pray).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00858111-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8d\u025bpr\u0259\u02ccke\u026at / (AmE), / \u02c8d\u025bpr\u026ake\u026at / (BrE)
(in software) to mark a feature, API, or function as discouraged for use and intended to be removed or unsupported in a future release; to signal that developers should migrate to alternatives.
mark a software feature as obsolete

Examples

The library maintainers decided to deprecate the old API and document alternatives for developers.
After the security flaw, the vendor deprecated several functions slated for removal in the next release.
Many teams avoid deprecating features without offering a migration path for existing users.
