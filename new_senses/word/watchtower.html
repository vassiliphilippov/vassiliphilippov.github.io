watchtower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːtʃˌtaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌtaʊə / (BrE)
A tall structure built to provide a wide view for observing and guarding an area, often used for spotting danger or overseeing prisoners.
tower for observation or guarding

Examples

A fire was quickly spotted from the watchtower and the alert was sounded.
Several watchtowers were constructed along the prison walls to monitor the inmates.
The guard climbed the watchtower to scan the horizon for any approaching threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lookout - a place or structure from which to keep watch
  • Synonym: observation tower - a tower built for the purpose of observing an area

Etymology

From Middle English 'wacche' (to watch) + 'tour' (tower).

(in blockchain and payment-channel systems) a remote monitoring service or server that observes off-chain channel activity to detect rule breaches and, if needed, publish penalty or recovery transactions to protect users' funds.
blockchain monitoring service

Examples

The company deployed a watchtower to detect fraudulent channel closures on the Lightning Network.
Users rely on watchtowers to protect funds when they cannot watch the blockchain themselves.
After the breach, the node operator consulted logs from the watchtower to find the cheating transaction.
