chevron

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛv.rən / (AmE), / ˈʃev.rən / (BrE)
a V-shaped line or badge, especially one worn on the sleeve of a military uniform to show rank or length of service
V-shaped military rank badge

Examples

He proudly displayed the chevrons he had earned during his military career.
The sergeant's uniform had a chevron on each sleeve representing his years of service.
When she was promoted, an extra chevron was added to her uniform.

Synset Examples

they earned their stripes in Kuwait

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a line or band distinguishing rank or achievement, especially on a uniform

Etymology

From Old French 'chevron' (rafter), based on Latin 'caprio', meaning 'rafter' (from 'capra', goat, from the shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛv.rən / (AmE), / ˈʃɛv.rən / (BrE)
A shape, pattern, or insignia in the form of an inverted V, commonly used in heraldry, military insignia, or as a decorative pattern.
inverted V-shaped symbol or pattern

Examples

Medieval coats of arms often displayed a bold chevron across the shield.
The road sign featured a bright yellow chevron to warn drivers of a sharp turn.
The soldier wore a chevron on his sleeve as a sign of rank.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zigzag - a pattern made up of small connected chevrons or angles
  • Degree Lower: V-shape - a simple shape resembling the letter V; basic form of a chevron

Etymology

From Old French 'chevron' (rafter), from Latin 'caprio' (rafter).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈʃɛvrən / (AmE), / ˈʃɛvrən / (BrE)
(in user interfaces) a small V-shaped icon or mark used on-screen to indicate navigation direction, a drop-down control, or expandable/collapsible content.
V-shaped UI icon indicating navigation/expand

Examples

Tap the chevron to reveal the hidden details in the menu.
The app uses small right-pointing chevrons to show which items lead to subpages.
Designers chose a subtle down-facing chevron to indicate collapsible sections on the page.

Synset Examples

Tap the chevron to reveal the hidden details in the menu.
The app uses small right-pointing chevrons to show which items lead to subpages.
Designers chose a subtle down-facing chevron to indicate collapsible sections on the page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator - a symbol or icon that shows status, direction, or available actions in an interface
  • Synonym: drop-down arrow - an arrow-shaped icon that shows a menu or list can be expanded

Etymology

From Old French 'chevron' (rafter), originally referring to a V-shaped structural timber; extended to describe V-shaped symbols and, in modern computing, small V-shaped UI icons.
