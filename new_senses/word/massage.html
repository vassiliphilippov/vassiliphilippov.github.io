massage

1. VERB

/ məˈsɑʒ / (AmE), / məˈsɑːʒ / (BrE)
to rub, knead, or manipulate someone's body, especially the muscles, in order to relieve tension, pain, or improve circulation
rub or knead the body for relaxation or therapy

Examples

After a stressful day, she likes to massage her temples to relax.
He massaged her shoulders gently until she felt calmer.
The therapist will massage your back to relieve muscle tension.

Synset Examples

She rubbed down her child with a sponge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knead - to work or massage by pressing and squeezing with the hands

Etymology

From French 'massage', from 'masser' (to knead, to massage)

to rub and knead muscles or body parts with the hands, especially to relieve tension or pain

She likes to massage her temples to relieve headaches.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ məˈsɑʒ / (AmE), / məˈsɑːʒ / (BrE)
the act of rubbing and kneading parts of the body, especially to relax the muscles or increase circulation
rubbing and kneading of the body for relaxation or therapy

Examples

After a long day at the office, she booked a massage to help relax her muscles.
Regular massage can help relieve stress and improve circulation.
The spa offers a variety of massage techniques, including deep tissue and hot stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubdown - an act of briskly rubbing the body, often with massage
  • Degree Lower: caress - to touch or stroke gently, sometimes used similarly but generally less therapeutic

Etymology

From French 'massage', from masser (to massage), likely from Arabic 'massa' (to touch, feel, handle).

to manipulate or adjust data, figures, or information (often dishonestly) in order to present a more favorable result.
manipulate data or numbers

Examples

Auditors claimed the report tried to massage the numbers to conceal the company's losses.
The marketing team massaged sales figures slightly to make the quarter look stronger to investors.
If you keep massaging the data you risk producing results that mislead readers.
