leakage

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈliːkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the accidental escape of a liquid or gas from a container or through a hole or crack
the escape of fluid or gas from a container

Examples

Engineers detected a leakage of oil from the tank after the storm.
The plumber fixed the leakage before it could cause any major damage.
There was evidence of water leakage around the base of the old pipe.

Synset Examples

they tried to stop the escape of gas from the damaged pipe
he had to clean up the leak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leak - an unintended passage for fluid or gas to escape

Etymology

From 'leak' + '-age', formed in English (17th century).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈliːkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈliːkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the unauthorized or accidental disclosure of confidential or private information, especially in digital, cybersecurity, or data contexts.
unauthorized disclosure of data

Examples

The latest security audit revealed a data leakage affecting several clients.
The company paid fines after a leakage exposed customers' credit card numbers.
Researchers warned of possible model leakage that could reveal sensitive training data.

Synset Examples

The latest security audit revealed a data leakage affecting several clients.
The company paid fines after a leakage exposed customers' credit card numbers.
Researchers warned of possible model leakage that could reveal sensitive training data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leak - an unauthorized disclosure of confidential information; to disclose secretly or accidentally
  • Synonym: data breach - an incident in which confidential or protected information is accessed, disclosed, or stolen

Etymology

From 'leak' + '-age' (17th century); extended metaphorically in modern usage to refer to escape or disclosure of information, especially digital data.
