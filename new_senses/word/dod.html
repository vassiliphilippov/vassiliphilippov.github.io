DoD

1. NOUN

/ di oʊ di / (AmE), / diː əʊ diː / (BrE)
The federal department of the United States government responsible for safeguarding national security and overseeing the armed forces.
US Department of Defense (national security)
american

Examples

After serving in the Army, she accepted a civilian position at the DoD.
Contractors working with the DoD must have security clearance.
The DoD announced new defense initiatives during the press conference yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Department of Defense - The full name of the US federal agency responsible for national security and military forces

Etymology

2. NOUN NEW

/ di oʊ di / (AmE), / diː əʊ diː / (BrE)
(in agile software development) the agreed set of criteria that a product increment, user story, or feature must meet to be considered complete and ready for release or review.
Definition of Done (agile)

Examples

Before merging, the developer checked the feature against the team's DoD to ensure readiness.
During sprint planning they updated the DoD so completed work met the new quality standards.
The coach explained that the DoD applies to every increment, not only finished user stories.
