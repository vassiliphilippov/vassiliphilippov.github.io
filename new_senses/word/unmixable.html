unmixable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɪksəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɪksəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of substances) incapable of being mixed together to form a homogeneous mixture; not able to be combined evenly.
incapable of being mixed

Examples

Oil and water are unmixable due to their different chemical properties.
The unmixable nature of some substances is important in physical chemistry.
These two chemicals remain unmixable even when stirred vigorously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immiscible - (of liquids) not forming a homogeneous mixture when added together
  • Antonym: miscible - (of liquids) able to be mixed to form a homogeneous solution

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'mixable' (able to be mixed).

2. ADJ NEW

/ \u028cn\u02c8m\u026aks\u0259bl\u0329 / (AmE), / \u028cn\u02c8m\u026aks\u0259bl\u0329 / (BrE)
(in music and DJ culture) not able to be mixed smoothly with other tracks because of incompatible tempo, key, structure, or production, making transitions awkward or impossible.
not suitable for DJ mixing
informal

Examples

The DJ refused to play the track because it was entirely unmixable with the set.
Many producers mark songs unmixable if their tempo or key changes make transitions awkward.
He learned the track was unmixable only after trying to sync the beats live.
