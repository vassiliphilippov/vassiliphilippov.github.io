unplaced

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpleɪst / (AmE), / ʌnˈpleɪst / (BrE)
not ranked among the first three competitors in a race or contest; not achieving a top-three position.
not in the top three in a competition

Examples

Despite training hard all season, she finished unplaced in the regional finals.
He was disappointed to remain unplaced after such a strong performance.
Of the eight horses he entered in the race, three were unplaced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: placed - ranked among the first three in a competition

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'placed' (from place as in a rank or position).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ʌnˈpleɪst / (AmE), / ʌnˈpleɪst / (BrE)
(in genomics) not assigned to a specific chromosome or chromosomal position; applied to contigs, scaffolds, or sequence fragments in a genome assembly.
not assigned to a chromosome (genomics)

Examples

The genome assembly includes several unplaced contigs awaiting chromosomal assignment and validation.
Our report flagged many unplaced scaffolds that could not be positioned on any reference chromosome.
Researchers used paired reads to try to place the large unplaced sequence in the assembly map.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unassigned - not allocated to a particular group, place, or category; in genomics, not assigned to a chromosome.
  • Antonym: assigned - allocated to a specific place or category; in genomics, placed on a chromosome.

Etymology

From un- + placed; literally 'not placed', used literally in a technical genomics sense.
