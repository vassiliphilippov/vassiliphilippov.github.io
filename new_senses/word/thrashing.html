thrashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈθræʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθræʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of beating someone repeatedly as a form of punishment
a beating as punishment

Examples

After the fight, he was left sore from the unexpected thrashing.
In the past, a thrashing was often used as a form of discipline.
The schoolmaster gave the boy a severe thrashing for breaking the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - an act of striking repeatedly as punishment or attack
  • Synonym: caning - punishment by hitting with a cane
  • Antonym: reward - something given in return for good behaviour; opposite of punishment

Etymology

From the verb 'thrash', likely of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'þreskja' (to thresh).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθræʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθræʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an overwhelming or decisive defeat, especially in a competition or contest
a crushing defeat

Examples

After the thrashing they received, the players vowed to train harder for the next game.
The team suffered a humiliating thrashing in the final match of the season.
Their rivals gave them a real thrashing on the basketball court last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - the act of winning a victory over someone
  • Antonym: victory - an act of defeating an opponent

Etymology

From the verb 'thrash' (to beat), from Old English 'þrescian' (to thresh).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈθræʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθræʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing) a system performance problem in which an operating system or process spends excessive time paging or swapping memory, causing severe slowdowns and reduced throughput.
excessive paging causing slowdown

Examples

After the memory leak, the database server suffered severe thrashing and became nearly unresponsive.
System administrators diagnosed constant thrashing as processes swapped in and out of RAM endlessly.
Enabling more physical memory eliminated the thrashing that had crippled the virtual machines during peak load.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paging - the process of moving pages of memory between physical RAM and disk (swap) used by operating systems
  • Synonym: swap - to move data between RAM and disk (swap space), often causing delays when overused

Etymology

From the verb 'thrash' (originally meaning to beat or thresh); extended metaphorically to describe systems being 'beaten' by constant paging.
