tightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtnəs / (BrE)
Extreme unwillingness to spend money; mean or miserly behavior.
extreme stinginess or unwillingness to spend money

Examples

His tightness with money made holidays and gifts rare in their family.
Many criticized the company’s tightness in allocating resources to employee welfare.
People noticed her tightness when she refused to contribute to the community fund.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinginess - unwillingness to spend money
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give or spend freely

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtnəs / (BrE)
a situation in which money or credit is scarce or difficult to obtain, especially in a financial or economic context
limited availability of money or credit

Examples

Periods of financial tightness often lead to higher interest rates and reduced investment.
The central bank intervened to reduce the tightness of the money supply.
The recent tightness in the credit market has made it difficult for small businesses to secure loans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquidity - the availability of liquid assets to a market or company

3. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking space for movement or being firmly held in place
lack of space or looseness

Examples

Due to the tightness of the schedule, there was no time for a break.
She felt a tightness in her chest after running up the stairs.
The tightness of the lid made it hard to open the jar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: looseness - the quality of not being tight; more room for movement
  • Synonym: compression - the act of pressing something tightly together

Etymology

From 'tight' + '-ness', with 'tight' from Old English 'tyht' (dense, close).
