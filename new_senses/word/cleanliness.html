cleanliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklinliˌnɛs / (AmE), / ˈkliːnlɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being clean, or of keeping things clean.
quality of being clean

Examples

Children are taught the importance of cleanliness at an early age.
Regular hand washing is a simple way to maintain cleanliness.
The hotel's reputation for cleanliness attracted many guests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dirtiness - the state of being dirty or unclean
  • Synonym: hygiene - practices or conditions for maintaining health and cleanliness

Etymology

From clean +‎ -liness, based on Middle English 'clennesse' (purity, cleanliness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkliːn.lɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkliːn.lɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
the practice or quality of keeping oneself or one's surroundings clean; the state of being free from dirt and impurities.
the quality or state of being clean

Examples

Her attention to cleanliness made the house always feel welcoming and comfortable.
Regular cleanliness is essential for good health and well-being.
The restaurant was praised for its cleanliness and high standards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dirtiness - the state of being dirty
  • Synonym: hygiene - practices related to maintaining health and cleanliness
  • Synonym: purity - freedom from impurities

Etymology

From Middle English 'clennesse,' from Old English 'clǣnnes' (purity).
