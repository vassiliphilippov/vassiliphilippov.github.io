stickiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪk.i.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sticky; the property of adhering to other surfaces
the quality of being sticky

Examples

Humidity can increase the stickiness of your skin in the summer.
The stickiness of the glue determines how well it will hold the parts together.
The stickiness of the syrup made it hard to clean off the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesiveness - the quality of being able to stick to surfaces
  • Antonym: slipperiness - the quality of being slippery rather than sticky

Etymology

From 'sticky' + '-ness'; 'sticky' from Middle English 'stikky', from 'stick'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8st\u026aki.n\u0259s / (AmE), / \u02c8st\u026ak.i.n\u026as / (BrE)
(in product design and digital analytics) the degree to which a website, app, or platform keeps users engaged and returning; a measure of user retention and repeat use.
user retention/engagement (digital product)

Examples

After the redesign, the platform's stickiness improved, with more users returning each week.
Product managers measure stickiness by retention rates and how often customers perform key actions.
Good onboarding increased the app's stickiness, reducing churn and boosting long-term engagement.

Synset Examples

After the redesign, the platform's stickiness improved, with more users returning each week.
Product managers measure stickiness by retention rates and how often customers perform key actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: user retention - the proportion of users who continue to use a product over time
  • Synonym: user engagement - the extent to which users interact with a product's features and content
  • Antonym: churn - the rate at which users stop using a product or service

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'sticky' + '-ness'; used in business and technology to describe a product's ability to retain users.
