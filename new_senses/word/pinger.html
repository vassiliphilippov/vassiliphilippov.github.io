pinger

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈpɪŋə / (BrE)
a device that generates pulses of sound for use in echo sounding, particularly in sonar equipment
sonar pulse-generating device

Examples

Divers followed the signal of the pinger attached to the sunken aircraft's black box.
The research team checked the pinger regularly to ensure it was emitting strong acoustic pulses.
The vessel deployed a pinger to map the ocean floor using sonar technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonar - a system for detecting objects underwater using sound waves

Etymology

From 'ping' (imitative of a high-pitched sound) + '-er'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈpɪŋə / (BrE)
a person, program, or service that sends short messages or notifications (pings) to check status or prompt a response.
sender of pings/notifications

Examples

The app uses a pinger to nudge inactive users with short reminder notifications every morning.
Our monitoring system includes two pingers that check server response times and alert us if slow.
After no reply to his email, she sent a gentle pinger — a quick message asking for a response.
