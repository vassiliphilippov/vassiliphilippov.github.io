to generalize

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛnɚəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) to become widespread and affect the entire body rather than remaining localized.
become widespread in the body (medical)
medical

Examples

Doctors were concerned that the cancer would generalize, spreading beyond the initial tumor site.
If the infection is not treated quickly, it may generalize and affect multiple organs.
Sepsis can occur when bacteria generalize from a local site to the bloodstream.

Synset Examples

this kind of infection generalizes throughout the immune system

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪz / (BrE)
to form a general principle or conclusion from a number of specific cases or instances
draw broad conclusions from specific cases

Examples

It is not always accurate to generalize from a small sample of data.
Scientists must avoid generalizing too quickly based on limited evidence.
She tends to generalize about people after only one bad experience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specify - to state or identify clearly and definitely; to mention explicitly

Etymology

From French généraliser, from général (general).

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something popular by adapting it to the tastes or understanding of the general public; to bring into widespread or common use
make popular or common; popularize

Examples

The author aimed to generalize complex scientific ideas for a broader audience.
The organization worked hard to generalize the practice so it would be accepted in daily life.
This approach helped to generalize the once niche cuisine across the entire country.

Synset Examples

4. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make statements or write about something in a broad way rather than focusing on specific details or exceptions
to speak or write in general terms

Examples

It is easy to generalize about cultures without understanding their complexities.
Please don't generalize; every situation is different and deserves individual attention.
When people generalize from one bad experience, they often make unfair assumptions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specify - to state something in detail and with precision

Etymology

From French généraliser or Medieval Latin generalisare, from Latin generalis (general).
