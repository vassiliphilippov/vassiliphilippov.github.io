receptionist

1. NOUN

/ rəˈsɛpʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛpʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person whose main job is to greet visitors, answer the telephone, and provide information in an office or hotel
person who greets visitors and answers phones

Examples

If you have any questions, just ask the receptionist at the front desk.
She worked as a receptionist in a busy hotel for three years.
The receptionist greeted all the clients with a warm smile as they entered the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary - a person employed to assist with correspondence, keep records, etc.

Etymology

From reception + -ist (from French -iste, denoting a person engaged in a particular job).
