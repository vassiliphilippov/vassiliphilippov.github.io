cps

1. NOUN

/ si pi ɛs / (AmE), / siː piː ɛs / (BrE)
an abbreviation for cycles per second, a former unit of frequency equivalent to one hertz
cycles per second; unit of frequency

Examples

A frequency of 1,000 cps is equivalent to 1 kilohertz.
Older equipment often lists frequencies in cps rather than hertz.
The signal was measured at 60 cps, matching the local power frequency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hertz - unit of frequency equal to one cycle per second

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'cycles per second'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ si pi ɛs / (AmE), / siː piː ɛs / (BrE)
Informal abbreviation for 'clicks per second', a measure of how many times a mouse or other input is clicked in one second, used especially in gaming to gauge clicking speed.
clicks per second (gaming measure)
informal

Examples

Top players can reach over 20 cps during intense duels.
He practiced clicking to improve his cps before the online tournament next week.
The coach measured each player's sustained cps with a simple test to compare speeds.
