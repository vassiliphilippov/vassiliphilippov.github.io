tachogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈtækəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈtakəɡram / (BrE)
A graphical record showing speed and distance, produced by a tachograph.
graphical record from a tachograph

Examples

A detailed tachogram can help reconstruct the events before an accident.
Fleet managers regularly review tachograms to monitor driver performance.
The police examined the tachogram to determine if the driver had exceeded the speed limit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tachograph - an instrument for recording the speed and distance traveled by a vehicle

Etymology

From Greek 'tachos' (speed) and '-gram' (something written or recorded).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtækəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈtakəɡram / (BrE)
A record or graph showing heart rate or consecutive inter-beat (RR) intervals plotted over time, used in cardiology and physiology to analyse heart-rate variability.
graph of heart rate over time
medical

Examples

The cardiologist examined the patient's tachogram to assess beat-to-beat heart rate variability after the exercise.
The researchers compared tachograms recorded during sleep and wakefulness to study autonomic nervous system activity.
During the stress test, her tachogram showed rapid fluctuations indicating impaired variability.
