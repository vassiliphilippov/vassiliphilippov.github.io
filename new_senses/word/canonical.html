canonical

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnɑːnɪkəl / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a text or book) officially accepted as part of the biblical canon; recognized as genuine and authoritative within the Christian or Jewish religious tradition.
recognized as part of biblical canon

Examples

Only canonical gospels are read in most Christian churches during services.
Scholars debate whether certain ancient writings should be deemed canonical or apocryphal.
The Book of Daniel is considered a canonical text in the Old Testament.

Synset Examples

a canonical book of the Christian New Testament

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apocryphal - not included in the accepted biblical canon

Etymology

From Late Latin 'canonicus' (according to rule), from Greek 'kanōn' (rule, standard).
