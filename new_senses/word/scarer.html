scarer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛrər / (AmE), / ˈskɛərə / (BrE)
an object, often shaped like a human figure, placed in fields to frighten birds away from seeds or crops
object to scare away birds from crops

Examples

Despite the presence of a scarer, some birds still managed to peck at the seeds.
The farmer placed a scarer in the middle of the field to protect his corn.
They built a homemade scarer using old clothes and straw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarecrow - an object made to resemble a human figure set up to scare birds away from crops

Etymology

From the verb 'scare' + agent noun suffix '-er'.
