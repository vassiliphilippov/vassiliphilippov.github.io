rooted

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) unable to move, as if fixed in place by shock, fear, or surprise.
unable to move; fixed in place by emotion

Examples

He stood rooted in fear as the thunder roared overhead.
She was rooted to the spot with disbelief, unable to take a single step forward.
The children were rooted in amazement at the magician's final trick.

Synset Examples

frozen with horror
they stood rooted in astonishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frozen - unable to move, typically from shock or fear

Etymology

Figurative use of 'root', drawing on the image of plants held in place by roots.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈruːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪd / (BrE)
(computing) (of a device, system, or account) having root or administrator access, giving unrestricted control over the operating system and system files.
having root/administrator access (computing)

Examples

Many users install custom apps on a rooted Android phone to change system settings.
The technician warned that a rooted device can be more vulnerable to malware if misconfigured.
After she rooted her tablet, she could remove preinstalled apps and adjust hidden settings.

Synset Examples
