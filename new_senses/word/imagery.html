imagery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (BrE)
the ability to form mental pictures or images in the mind
mental image-forming ability

Examples

Athletes often use imagery techniques to improve their performance.
Guided imagery sessions can reduce stress and promote relaxation.
Her vivid imagery helped her recall every detail of the memory.

Synset Examples

he could still hear her in his imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty or action of forming new ideas, or images not present to the senses

Etymology

From Old French *imagerie* (imitation of images, imagery), from *image* (image, likeness).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒəri / (BrE)
photographs or other visual images, especially those taken from aircraft or satellites, used as data for mapping, analysis, or remote sensing.
satellite or aerial visual data

Examples

Researchers used satellite imagery to monitor urban expansion over the past decade.
Emergency teams relied on high-resolution imagery to assess flood damage and plan rescue routes.
City planners examined recent imagery to update zoning maps and forecast infrastructure needs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satellite image - a digital photograph of Earth taken by a satellite, used for mapping and analysis
  • Synonym: aerial photograph - a photograph taken from an aircraft, often used in surveying and planning

From Old French imagerie (imitation of images), from image.
