hallucination

1. NOUN

/ həˌluːsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an experience in which a person sees, hears, feels, or senses something that is not actually present, typically as a symptom of mental or physical disorder
perception of something not present
medical

Examples

After not sleeping for three days, he began to experience vivid hallucination.
She was convinced she heard voices, but it was only a hallucination.
Some medications can cause hallucinations as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin hallucinatio (wandering of the mind).

2. NOUN

/ həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an object, event, or image perceived as real during a hallucinatory episode, though it does not actually exist outside the mind
perceived object or image during a hallucination

Examples

During his illness, he often saw a hallucination of a small dog following him around the house.
She described a vivid hallucination of hearing voices that no one else could hear.
The shimmering oasis in the desert turned out to be just a hallucination.

Synset Examples

he refused to believe that the angel was a hallucination

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illusion - a false perception of something that is actually present
  • Antonym: delusion - a fixed false belief not based in reality

Etymology

From Latin 'hallucinatio' (wandering of the mind), from 'alucinari' (to wander in mind).

3. NOUN

/ həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A belief or idea that is mistaken or not based on reality.
mistaken belief or idea

Examples

His hope of immediate success was merely a hallucination with no basis in fact.
Some people mistake conspiracy theories for truth, but often they are just a hallucination.
To believe the company could double its profits overnight is a hallucination at best.
