orchestration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrkəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːkɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
an arrangement of a musical composition for performance by an orchestra or band, specifying the parts for each instrument
musical arrangement for orchestra or band

Examples

Mahler was known for his innovative orchestration, adding unusual instruments to create new sound textures.
The orchestration of the ballet was praised for its brilliant use of brass and percussion.
This new orchestration brings fresh color to the classic symphony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a musical adaptation of a piece for performance by particular instruments or voices

Etymology

From French orchestration, from orchestre (orchestra) + -ation.
