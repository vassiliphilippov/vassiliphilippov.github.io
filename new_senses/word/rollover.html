rollover

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the transfer of funds from one pension plan or retirement account to another without incurring a tax penalty
transfer of retirement funds to another plan

Examples

After leaving her job, she opted for a rollover to move her 401(k) into an IRA.
The financial advisor explained how a rollover works for employer-sponsored retirement plans.
You must complete the rollover within 60 days to avoid taxes and penalties.

Etymology

From the phrase 'roll over', meaning to transfer or move from one state to another, applied to finance in mid-20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈroʊlˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌəʊvə / (BrE)
An amount of a mobile or subscription plan (for example, data, minutes, or messages) that was unused in one billing period and is permitted to be used in a later billing period.
unused plan allowance carried forward

Examples

My plan gives 5GB and any unused 1GB rollover automatically appears next month.
They advertised free international minutes and a two-month rollover for unused call time.
Consumers complained when the provider removed the rollover feature from its cheapest plan.

Related Terms

From the phrasal verb 'roll over' meaning 'carry forward'; applied to plan allowances in late 20th–21st-century telecommunications.
