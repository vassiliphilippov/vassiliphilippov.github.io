Anunnaki

1. NOUN

/ əˌnuːˈnɑːki / (AmE), / əˌnuːˈnɑːki / (BrE)
Any of a group of powerful earth spirits or deities in Babylonian mythology, regarded as servants or agents of the gods.
Babylonian earth spirits or deities

Examples

Researchers have studied temple inscriptions describing the powers attributed to the Anunnaki.
Some believe the Anunnaki acted as intermediaries between humanity and the highest gods.
The Anunnaki played a significant role in ancient Babylonian creation myths.

Etymology

From Akkadian *Anunnaku*, a group of deities in Mesopotamian mythology.

2. NOUN NEW

/ əˌnuːˈnɑːki / (AmE), / əˌnuːˈnɑːki / (BrE)
(in modern usage, especially in conspiracy theories and popular culture) alleged ancient extraterrestrial beings said to have visited, guided, or influenced early human civilizations.
conspiracy-theory extraterrestrials

Examples

Some internet forums claim the Anunnaki were advanced aliens who taught early humans agriculture and writing.
The documentary explored fringe theories suggesting the Anunnaki engineered ancient monuments as alien technology.
Despite historians rejecting the idea, many still refer to the Anunnaki in science fiction and conspiracy blogs.
