accelerator

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtər / (AmE), / əkˈsɛləreɪtə / (BrE)
A pedal in a motor vehicle that controls the engine's power and speed by regulating the throttle; used to increase speed.
pedal controlling vehicle speed

Examples

A sudden push on the accelerator made the car lurch forward.
She pressed the accelerator to merge onto the highway quickly.
When traffic cleared, he tapped the accelerator and sped ahead of the other cars.

Synset Examples

he stepped on the gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas pedal - pedal that controls engine speed in a car
  • Antonym: brake - pedal used to slow down or stop a vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten), via French 'accélérateur'.

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtər / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.ər.eɪ.tər / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A substance that increases the speed of a chemical reaction without itself being changed.
substance that speeds up a reaction
medical

Examples

In rubber production, an accelerator is used to reduce curing time.
Some reactions are so slow that they require an accelerator to occur efficiently.
The chemist added an accelerator to the mixture to speed up the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change

Etymology

From Latin accelerare (to hasten, quicken) + English suffix -or.
