grime

1. NOUN

/ ɡraɪm / (AmE), / ɡraɪm / (BrE)
dirt or soot that makes something look dirty or unclean.
dirt or soot making things dirty

Examples

He wiped away the grime from the window to let the sunlight in.
The workers were covered in grime after a long day at the factory.
Years of grime had built up on the kitchen tiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt - any substance that makes something unclean
  • Synonym: filth - thick, unpleasant dirt
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean

2. VERB

/ ɡraɪm / (AmE), / ɡraɪm / (BrE)
to make something dirty or filthy by covering it with grime
make dirty or filthy

Examples

Working in the garage will grime your clothes quickly if you're not careful.
Years of neglect had grimed the once-bright tiled floor.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɡraɪm / (AmE), / ɡraɪm / (BrE)
A genre of UK urban music, originating in London in the early 2000s, characterized by rapid breakbeats, electronic production, and gritty, often rapid-fire vocals.
UK urban music genre
british

Examples

The club's lineup showcased grime, a raw UK urban music style.
After discovering grime, she began following emerging London MCs and underground radio shows.
The festival dedicated to grime drew fans of fast breakbeats and gritty lyrics from across Europe.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: UK garage - an electronic dance genre from which grime partly developed
  • Synonym: hip hop - a music genre whose vocal style and MCing influenced grime

Etymology

Named for its 'gritty' or raw sound; developed in early-2000s London from influences including UK garage, jungle and dancehall.
