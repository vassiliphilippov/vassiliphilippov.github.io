rickshaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪkˌʃɔ / (AmE), / ˈrɪkʃɔː / (BrE)
a small two-wheeled vehicle for carrying one passenger, typically pulled by a person on foot
small two-wheeled passenger cart, pulled by a person

Examples

After her flight, she hired a rickshaw to take her to the hotel.
Many cities in Asia still use rickshaws for transportation in narrow alleys.
The tourists took a rickshaw ride through the busy streets of the old city.

Etymology

From Japanese *jinrikisha*, from *jin* (person) + *riki* (power) + *sha* (vehicle).
