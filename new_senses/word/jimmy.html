jimmy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪmi / (BrE)
a short metal crowbar, typically used for prying open windows or doors
a short crowbar

Examples

He kept a jimmy in his toolbox for emergencies.
Police found a jimmy hidden under the suspect's coat.
The burglar used a jimmy to force open the back door.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a jimmy a ‘jemmy’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jemmy - British English term for a short crowbar

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪmi / (BrE)
to force open (a door, window, etc.), typically with a crowbar or similar tool, especially illegally or forcibly
to force open with a tool, especially illegally

Examples

After several attempts, she finally jimmied the rusty lock and got inside the shed.
The thief managed to jimmy the window open using a screwdriver.
They tried to jimmy the door instead of finding the key.

Synset Examples

The burglar jimmied the lock
Raccoons managed to pry the lid off the garbage pail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pry - to use force to move or open something
  • Synonym: force - to open something by using strength or effort

Etymology

Probably from the slang term 'jemmy', a burglar's crowbar, of unknown origin.
