electrolocation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrələʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability of certain animals to detect and locate objects in their environment by sensing natural electric fields
ability to locate objects via electric fields
medical

Examples

Scientists study how certain fish use electrolocation to navigate and find prey in murky waters.
Some aquatic animals rely on electrolocation rather than sight to understand their surroundings.
The ability of sharks to detect hidden animals is due in part to electrolocation.

Synset Examples

Sharks and rays (members of the subclass Elasmobranchii), such as the lemon shark, rely heavily on electrolocation in the final stages of their attacks, as can be demonstrated by the robust feeding response elicited by electric fields similar to those of their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroreception - the detection of electric fields by living organisms

Etymology

From electro- (electric) + location.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u026a\u02ccl\u025bktro\u028alo\u028a\u02c8ke\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / \u026a\u02ccl\u025bktr\u0259l\u0259\u028a\u02c8ke\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
(in robotics and engineering) a technique by which artificial systems emit or measure electric fields and detect their perturbations to locate objects or navigate in low-visibility environments.
artificial sensing by electric fields

Examples

Engineers fitted the autonomous underwater vehicle with active electrolocation to detect obstacles in turbid coastal waters.
Researchers published a paper demonstrating cheap sensors that enable small robots to perform electrolocation in dark environments.
The lab tested various frequencies to improve the resolution of artificial electrolocation for subsea mapping tasks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroreception - the biological detection of electric fields by living organisms
  • Synonym: active sensing - a technique where a device emits signals and measures responses to sense its environment
