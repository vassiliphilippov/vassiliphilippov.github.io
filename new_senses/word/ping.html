ping

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋ / (AmE), / pɪŋ / (BrE)
a short, sharp high-pitched resonant sound, such as that made by a sonar echo or a bullet striking metal
sharp, high-pitched resonant sound

Examples

As the submarine detected another vessel, a loud ping echoed through the control room.
He heard the sharp ping of a bullet ricocheting off the metal gate.
The microwave ended with a familiar ping, signaling that the food was ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Synonym: ring - a clear resonant sound
  • Antonym: thud - a dull sound, as of a heavy object falling

8. NOUN NEW

/ pɪŋ / (AmE), / pɪŋ / (BrE)
the measured round-trip time for a data packet to travel from one device to another and back, usually expressed in milliseconds; used to indicate network responsiveness or latency.
network round-trip time / latency

Examples

In online gaming, his ping stays under 20 ms most of the time.
Video calls drop quality when the participant's ping rises above one hundred milliseconds.
Run a network test and check the server's average ping to diagnose connectivity problems.

Synset Examples

In online gaming, his ping stays under 20 ms most of the time.
Video calls drop quality when the participant's ping rises above one hundred milliseconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latency - the delay between a request and the start of a response in a system or network
  • Synonym: round-trip time - the time it takes for a signal to go to a destination and return, often abbreviated RTT

Etymology

From the imitative word 'ping' (a short, high-pitched sound); extended in computing via the ICMP utility name 'ping' (1980s) to refer to network tests and their measured round-trip time.
