to transmigrate

1. VERB

/ trænzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (AmE), / tranzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to move from one country or region to another and settle there
move and settle in another country or region

Examples

After decades of unrest, the family decided to transmigrate to a new continent in search of peace.
Several ancient communities would transmigrate each generation, adapting to different lands as needed.
The monks chose to transmigrate to a distant region to establish their new monastery.

Synset Examples

Many Germans migrated to South America in the mid-19th century
This tribe transmigrated many times over the centuries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to migrate - to move from one region or country to another, often temporarily

Etymology

From Latin 'transmigratus', past participle of 'transmigrare' (to migrate across).

2. VERB

/ trænzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
(of the soul) to pass into a new body after death; to be born anew in another body, especially as believed in some religious and philosophical systems.
be reborn in another body

Examples

Many Eastern religions teach that souls transmigrate after death.
Some people hope they will transmigrate into a better life next time.
The belief that souls transmigrate shapes burial rituals in several cultures.

Synset Examples

Hindus believe that we transmigrate

Etymology

From Latin transmigrare (‘to move from one place to another’), from trans- (‘across’) + migrare (‘to migrate’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02704568-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ trænzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
(in biology/medicine) to move from one side of a tissue or vessel wall to the other; to cross a biological barrier (e.g., leukocytes crossing the endothelium).
move across a tissue or vessel barrier
medical

Examples

During inflammation, leukocytes transmigrate across the endothelium into the affected tissue.
Researchers transmigrated labeled T cells across the blood–brain barrier in the mouse model.
Endothelial signalling controls whether monocytes are transmigrating through vessel walls during infection.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravasate - to leak or move out from a vessel into surrounding tissue; often used for fluid or cells
  • Synonym: to migrate - to move from one place to another; in biology, to move from one location to another within an organism or between organisms
