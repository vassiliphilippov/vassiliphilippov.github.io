sanctuary

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋktʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tju.ə.ri / (BrE)
a sacred or holy place, especially within a religious building, where sacred objects are kept or religious rites occur
sacred place in a religious setting

Examples

Only a few chosen individuals were allowed to enter the holy sanctuary.
The Torah scrolls are stored in the sanctuary of the synagogue.
The ancient temple's sanctuary was reserved for priests and sacred rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block used as the focus for a religious ritual

Etymology

From Latin sanctuarium, from sanctus (holy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋktʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tju.ə.ri / (BrE)
the area in a church around the altar, reserved for the clergy and choir, often enclosed by a rail or screen
area around altar in a church

Examples

A railing separated the sanctuary from the rest of the church.
Only clergy and certain officials are allowed inside the sanctuary during the ceremony.
The choir processed into the sanctuary for the start of the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chancel - the part of a church containing the altar and seats for the clergy and choir

Etymology

From Latin sanctuarium, from sanctus (holy).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋktʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktjʊəri / (BrE)
A place of safety or shelter, especially for people in danger or distress.
place of safety; shelter

Examples

Her home became a sanctuary from the chaos of the outside world.
Many refugees sought sanctuary in the church during the war.
The wounded animal found sanctuary in the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing safety or shelter
  • Synonym: asylum - a place offering protection or shelter, especially political
  • Antonym: danger - exposure to harm or risk

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctuarium', from 'sanctus' meaning 'holy'.
