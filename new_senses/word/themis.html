Themis

1. PROPN

/ ˈθɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈθiːmɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the Titaness who was goddess of justice, order, and law.
Titaness goddess of justice (Greek mythology)

Examples

In ancient Greek tales, Themis was revered as the embodiment of justice and divine law.
Many classical statues depict Themis holding a set of scales in her hand.
The principles attributed to Themis continue to influence modern concepts of justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Justice - the quality of being fair or reasonable
  • Degree Lower: Dike - Greek goddess of moral justice, daughter of Themis

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θέμις (Thémis), meaning 'law' or 'custom'.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ˈθɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈθiːmɪs / (BrE)
A NASA space mission (Time History of Events and Macroscale Interactions during Substorms): a constellation of satellites and associated instruments that study magnetospheric substorms and related space-weather phenomena.
NASA mission studying magnetospheric substorms

Examples

NASA's Themis mission used five satellites to study auroras and magnetospheric substorms.
Data from Themis helped scientists better understand how solar wind triggers geomagnetic storms.
The conference included a presentation summarizing Themis findings on particle acceleration during substorms.
