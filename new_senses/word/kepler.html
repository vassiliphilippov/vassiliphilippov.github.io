Kepler

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɛplɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɛplə / (BrE)
(1571–1630) A German astronomer who formulated the laws of planetary motion, significantly advancing the field of astronomy.
German astronomer; laws of planetary motion

Examples

I read an article about Kepler and his contributions to astronomy.
Many astronomers regard Kepler as a pioneer in understanding the motion of planets.
The Kepler laws revolutionized how we view our solar system.

2. PROPN NEW

/ c8k5bpl5a / (AmE), / c8k5bpl59 / (BrE)
A NASA space telescope and mission (launched 2009) that searched for planets around other stars by measuring small dips in starlight when planets passed in front of them (transits).
NASA exoplanet space telescope

Examples

Astronomers used Kepler data to confirm thousands of candidate exoplanets around distant stars.
After the mission ended, scientists continued to analyze Kepler observations for new planet signals.
The university runs a pipeline that processes raw Kepler light curves to detect tiny transit dips.
