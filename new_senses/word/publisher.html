publisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃə / (BrE)
a person or company that prepares and issues books, periodicals, music, or other works for public sale or distribution
person or company issuing books, music, etc.

Examples

Many publishers now offer both print and digital versions of their books.
She sent her manuscript to a well-known publisher in New York.
The publisher decided to reprint the novel due to high demand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who prepares material for publication
  • Degree Lower: author - person who writes books or other works
  • Degree Lower: printer - person or company that physically produces printed materials

Etymology

From publish + -er, based on Latin publicare (to make public).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃə / (BrE)
a company or organization that prepares and issues books, journals, newspapers, or other works for sale or distribution
company that issues books or other works

Examples

Many authors struggle to find the right publisher for their first book.
She works for a major textbook publisher based in London.
The publisher announced a new line of science fiction novels for next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publishing house - a company that publishes books, magazines, or other works

Etymology

From publish + -er; modeled as agent noun for one who publishes, dating from the 15th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃə / (BrE)
a person or company that owns or manages a newspaper, magazine, or other publication, especially as the proprietor.
owner/proprietor of a publication

Examples

As the new publisher, she introduced several changes to modernize the newspaper.
Many famous authors have close relationships with their publishers to ensure their work is marketed effectively.
The publisher decided to invest more money in investigative journalism at the local newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who is in charge of and determines the final content of a publication
  • Synonym: owner - a person who owns something, especially a business or company

Etymology

From publish + -er; 'publish' from Latin 'publicare' (make public).
