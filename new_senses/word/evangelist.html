Evangelist

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
(when capitalized) a writer of one of the four Gospels of the New Testament: Matthew, Mark, Luke, or John.
author of the Gospels in the New Testament

Examples

Each Evangelist provides a unique perspective on the life of Jesus.
Stained glass windows often depict one Evangelist with his traditional symbol.
The four Evangelists are revered figures in Christianity.

Etymology

From Greek euangelistēs (bringer of good news), via Latin evangelista.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who enthusiastically promotes or advocates for a cause, product, technology, or idea; often used for formal roles in companies or communities (for example, a technology or developer evangelist).
enthusiastic promoter, often in tech

Examples

The company hired an evangelist to promote its new cloud platform.
As an open-source evangelist, she spoke at conferences and convinced developers to contribute regularly.
He quickly became the project's chief evangelist, winning support from partners and investors.
