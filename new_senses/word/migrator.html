migrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪˌɡreɪtər / (AmE), / mʌɪˈɡreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or animal that moves from one region or country to another, especially regularly or seasonally
person or animal that migrates

Examples

As a migrator, she had adjusted to many different cultures.
Millions of birds are migrators that travel thousands of miles each year.
Not all animals are migrators; some remain in the same habitat their whole lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migrant - a person or animal that moves from one place to another

Etymology

From Latin 'migrator' (one who moves), from 'migrāre' (to move, migrate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪɡreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪɡreɪtə / (BrE)
an animal, especially a bird or fish, that migrates between different habitats at certain times of the year
an animal that migrates seasonally

Examples

Some fish species are known as migrators because they journey upstream to spawn every spring.
Songbirds that are migrators can be seen arriving in new areas as the seasons change.
The Arctic tern is a remarkable migrator, traveling thousands of miles each year between the poles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migrant - an animal that migrates from one region to another, especially regularly by season
  • Antonym: resident - an animal that does not migrate and remains in the same area year-round

Etymology

From Latin 'migrare' (to move from one place to another).
