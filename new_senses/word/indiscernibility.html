indiscernibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌsɝːnəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌsɜːnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being impossible to perceive or distinguish.
inability to be perceived or distinguished

Examples

Philosophers debate the indiscernibility of identicals in metaphysics.
The indiscernibility of the object in the dim light made it difficult to identify.
With perfect camouflage, the animal achieves near total indiscernibility in its environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indistinguishability - the quality of being impossible to distinguish
  • Antonym: discernibility - the ability to be perceived or distinguished

Etymology

Formed from 'indiscernible' + '-ity', based on Latin 'discernere' (to separate, distinguish).

(in data privacy and statistical disclosure control) the property of a dataset in which individuals cannot be distinguished from one another by a specified set of released attributes; records form indistinguishable equivalence classes under those attributes.
privacy property: records indistinguishable by attributes

Examples

To comply with regulations, the research team ensured indiscernibility among patients' records before publishing the dataset.
K-anonymity aims to achieve indiscernibility so attackers cannot re-identify individuals from quasi-identifiers.
The privacy officer measured indiscernibility across demographic fields to estimate disclosure risk for the release.
