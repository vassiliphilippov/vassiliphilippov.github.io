to factorize

1. VERB

/ ˈfæk.təˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə.raɪz / (BrE)
to resolve a mathematical expression, especially a polynomial, into a product of simpler expressions or factors
reduce a polynomial to its factors

Examples

It is not always possible to factorize a quadratic equation with integer coefficients.
Students learned how to factorize polynomials completely in their algebra class.
The computer program can quickly factorize large numbers and polynomials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expand - to multiply out and write as a sum or more complex expression

Etymology

From factor + -ize, formed from the mathematical sense of 'factor'.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈfæk.təˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə.raɪz / (BrE)
(in linear algebra and applied mathematics) to write a matrix as a product of two or more simpler matrices (for example LU, QR, or SVD decompositions), often to make calculations or analysis easier.
decompose a matrix into factor matrices

Examples

In class, we learned to factorize a matrix into LU form to simplify solving linear systems.
The software factorized the large matrix using SVD to improve the accuracy of the model.
Researchers are factorizing user-rating matrices to predict preferences in recommendation systems.
