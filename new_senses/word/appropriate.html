appropriate

1. ADJ

/ əˈproʊpriət / (AmE), / əˈprəʊpriət / (BrE)
suitable or proper for a particular situation or purpose.
suitable; proper for a situation

Examples

Choose an appropriate response to each question in the exam.
It was not an appropriate time to ask for a raise.
Please wear appropriate clothing for the weather today.

Synset Examples

a tractor suitable (or fit) for heavy duty
not an appropriate (or fit) time for flippancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitable - right or appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation
  • Antonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From Late Latin appropriatus (made one's own), past participle of appropriare.

3. VERB

/ əˈproʊpriˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈprəʊprɪeɪt / (BrE)
to officially allocate or assign money or resources to a particular person, use, or cause
allocate resources or funds officially

Examples

Each year, Congress must appropriate money for public health programs.
The board voted unanimously to appropriate a portion of the budget for staff development.
The government decided to appropriate additional funds for disaster relief.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocate - to distribute resources for a particular purpose

From Latin appropriare, meaning 'to make one's own'.
