mintage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪn.teɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the collective coins produced by a mint; coins made in a particular period or place
coins made by a mint

Examples

Each year's mintage has distinct markings and dates.
The Roman mintage is prized by collectors around the world.
This museum houses a unique mintage from colonial times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coinage - coins collectively; the act or process of making coins

Etymology

From mint (coin-producing place) + -age (denoting a collection or result of an action).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪntɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪntɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of producing coins by stamping metal at a mint
the process of minting coins

Examples

Collectors often value coins based on their rarity and year of mintage.
During the 19th century, the mintage process was modernized to improve efficiency.
The mintage of commemorative coins increased following the anniversary celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minting - the process of making coins
  • Synonym: coinage - the act or process of making coins

Etymology

From mint (coin-producing place) + -age (forming nouns denoting an action or process).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪn.teɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee paid to a mint by the government for producing a coin
fee for minting coins

Examples

Debate arose over how the mintage was calculated in the contract with the Royal Mint.
Historically, the mintage was an important revenue source for sovereigns issuing new coins.
The government agreed to pay the mintage for each batch of coins produced.

Etymology

From 'mint' (coin-producing place) + '-age' (denoting a result or fee).
