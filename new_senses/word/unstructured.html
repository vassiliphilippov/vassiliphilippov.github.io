unstructured

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstrʌktʃɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrʌktʃəd / (BrE)
not organized or arranged in a specific or systematic way; lacking a definite structure or form.
not organized or lacking structure

Examples

Children often benefit from unstructured playtime, where they can use their imagination freely.
His essay was criticized for being too unstructured and lacking a clear argument.
The team performed well in the unstructured environment, quickly adapting to change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: structured - organized or arranged in a particular way; having structure

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'structured', meaning not structured.

Not organized or arranged in a definite or systematic way; lacking a clear structure.

3. ADJ NEW

/ ʌnˈstrʌktʃəd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrʌktʃəd / (BrE)
(in information technology) not organized according to a predefined model or schema; describing data (for example text, images, audio, or video) that does not fit a fixed table-like structure.
not organized by a schema (data)

Examples

Companies often analyze unstructured data such as emails and documents.
The research team used NLP to extract insights from a large unstructured text corpus.
Cloud providers offer tools to store and search vast amounts of unstructured multimedia content.

Synset Examples

Companies often analyze unstructured data such as emails and documents.
The research team used NLP to extract insights from a large unstructured text corpus.
Cloud providers offer tools to store and search vast amounts of unstructured multimedia content.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: structured - data organized according to a defined schema or model (e.g., rows and columns).
  • Synonym: raw data - original unprocessed data, often lacking organization or labeling.

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'structured'.
