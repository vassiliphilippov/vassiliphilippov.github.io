memorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛmərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛmərʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the action or process of learning something so that it can be recalled exactly from memory
the act of committing to memory

Examples

Actors spend hours on memorization to prepare for their performances.
Her daily memorization of vocabulary helped her excel in her language class.
The memorization of multiplication tables is often part of early math education.

Synset Examples

the actor's memorization of his lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recitation - the act of repeating something aloud from memory

Etymology

From Latin memoria (memory) + -ization.

(in machine learning) the tendency of a model to 'remember' or fit its training data too closely, storing idiosyncratic examples so it performs poorly on new, unseen data (see overfitting).
model overfitting / remembering training data

Examples

The research team discovered the model's memorization of training images caused poor performance on new datasets.
Regularization techniques are used to reduce memorization and improve a neural network's ability to generalize.
They measured memorization by comparing training accuracy with test accuracy across multiple epochs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overfitting - a model fitting training data so closely that it fails to generalize to new data
  • Antonym: generalization - the ability of a model to perform well on new, unseen data

Etymology

From Latin memoria ‘memory’ + -ization; used metaphorically in computing to describe models that 'remember' training examples.
