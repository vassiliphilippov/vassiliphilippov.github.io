paired

1. ADJ

/ pɛrd / (AmE), / peəd / (BrE)
(of animals or birds) brought together as a male and a female for the purpose of breeding.
matched or joined for breeding

Examples

At the breeding facility, each paired animal is closely monitored for compatibility.
The paired penguins returned to their nest each year to raise their chicks.
The zookeeper introduced the newly paired turtles to their enclosure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpaired - not joined or matched with a mate

2. ADJ

/ pɛrd / (AmE), / pɛəd / (BrE)
(of leaves or similar plant structures) growing in pairs on either side of a stem.
growing in pairs (botany)

Examples

Look for the paired arrangement of leaflets when identifying this species.
Many shrubs display paired flowers, creating a symmetrical appearance.
The plant is recognized by its paired leaves growing opposite each other along the stem.

Synset Examples

opposite leaves

Etymology

From the noun 'pair,' from Old French 'paire,' from Latin 'paria' (equal things).

3. ADJ

/ peɪrd / (AmE), / peəd / (BrE)
(of things) occurring in pairs or existing as a pair; matched or arranged in twos.
existing in pairs or as a pair

Examples

He bought a set of paired socks in many colors.
Her research focused on paired chromosomes during cell division.
The paired organs in the body often work together to perform vital functions.

Synset Examples

paired fangs
paired gloves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - existing alone; not in a pair
  • Degree Higher: double - consisting of two parts or items together

Etymology

From the verb 'pair' (to arrange in pairs), from Old French 'paire', from Latin 'paria' (equals).

Examples
