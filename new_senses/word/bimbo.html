bimbo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪmbəʊ / (BrE)
A young woman, typically considered attractive but regarded as unintelligent and often kept or indulged by wealthy or powerful older men.
an attractive but unintelligent young woman
informal

Examples

Many people unfairly label her a bimbo because of her looks and the company she keeps.
She proved she's not just another bimbo by launching a successful business.
The tabloids often feature stories pairing the millionaire with a glamorous bimbo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold digger - a woman who forms relationships with men purely to obtain money or gifts
  • Synonym: himbo - an attractive but unintelligent young man

Etymology

Probably from Italian *bimbo* (male baby or young child), later used in the US for a foolish man and then, from the 1920s, a woman.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbɪmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪmbəʊ / (BrE)
An online, self‑described persona or aesthetic that celebrates glamorous femininity and sexual or personal empowerment, often adopted ironically or to reclaim the insult.
reclaimed online persona
informal slang

Examples

She embraces the bimbo aesthetic as a modern empowerment statement online.
Many women proudly call themselves bimbo on social media to challenge sexist expectations.
Her essay argued that the bimbo persona can be a deliberate performance of feminine power.
