barycenter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈbarɪˌsɛntə / (BrE)
(astronomy) The common center of mass around which two or more bodies, such as planets and stars, revolve.
center of mass in astronomy

Examples

Astronomers calculate the barycenter to understand how planets and their moons move in space.
The solar system's barycenter is actually outside the surface of the Sun due to Jupiter's mass.
The two stars in a binary system orbit a barycenter located between them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center of mass - the point representing the mean position of the mass in a body or system

Etymology

From Greek barys (heavy) + center, referring to the center of mass.

(in mathematics and statistics) A generalization of the center of mass to probability distributions; a barycenter is an average or representative distribution (or point) that minimizes total distance to a given set of distributions.
average of probability distributions

Examples

Researchers computed the barycenter of several distributions to summarize the data.
In the lecture, she explained how the Wasserstein barycenter averages different probability measures.
To find a representative model, the team estimated the barycenter across all sampled distributions.
