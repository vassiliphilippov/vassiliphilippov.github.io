RFD

1. NOUN

/ ɑr ɛf di / (AmE), / ɑː ɛf diː / (BrE)
(in the US) Rural Free Delivery; a service providing free government delivery of mail to residents in rural areas.
Rural Free Delivery postal service (US)
american

Examples

Many small towns still reference the old RFD system in local historical records.
Our grandparents' farm address was just their name and RFD Route 1, before the town got street names.
The introduction of RFD made it easier for rural residents to receive mail directly at their homes.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Rural Free Delivery', a US postal service term introduced in the late 19th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɑr ɛf di / (AmE), / ɑː ɛf diː / (BrE)
(in online communities) a formal request to remove or delete a post, comment, or other content, typically submitted to moderators or administrators.
request to delete online content
informal

Examples

The moderator filed an RFD to delete the outdated question after community discussion.
Several users supported the RFD and added comments explaining why the post violated policy.
Before approving, the admin reviewed the RFD thread and checked the reported evidence carefully.
