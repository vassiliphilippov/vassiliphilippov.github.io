Rousseauan

1. ADJ

/ ruːˈsoʊən / (AmE), / ruːˈsoʊ.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the ideas or writings of the French philosopher Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712–1778).
of or relating to Jean-Jacques Rousseau

Examples

His teaching style was profoundly Rousseauan, emphasizing natural development and freedom.
Many political theorists still debate the implications of Rousseauan social contract theory.
The novel explores Rousseauan themes of innocence and the corrupting influence of society.

Etymology

From the name Jean-Jacques Rousseau, with the English adjectival suffix -an.
