GSA

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː ɛs ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː ɛs ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the General Services Administration, a United States government agency responsible for federal procurement, real property management, and information resources management
U.S. General Services Administration (government agency)
american

Examples

She got a job as an analyst at the GSA after finishing her degree in public administration.
The GSA announced new sustainability guidelines for federal office buildings.
The GSA handles government contracts and property leasing for federal agencies.

Etymology

Abbreviation of General Services Administration, a U.S. government agency established in 1949.
