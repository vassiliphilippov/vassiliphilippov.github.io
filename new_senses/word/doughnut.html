doughnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.nʌt / (BrE)
A small, usually ring-shaped cake that is deep-fried and often sweetened or coated with sugar or glaze.
deep-fried sweet ring-shaped cake

Examples

I bought a chocolate doughnut with sprinkles for breakfast.
She offered me a doughnut from the bakery box.
The kids love to eat a doughnut with their milk after school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donut - alternative spelling of doughnut

Etymology

Variant of 'donut', from 'dough' + 'nut', referring to its shape and ingredients.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8do\u028a.n\u028ct / (AmE), / \u02c8d\u0259\u028a.n\u028ct / (BrE)
A circular chart with a hole in the centre, used to show proportions or percentages of a whole; same as a 'doughnut (or donut) chart'.
circular proportion chart

Examples

The dashboard uses a doughnut chart to show market share by region.
She preferred a doughnut visualization to a pie chart when presenting sales percentages to the team.
For the report, they included three small doughnuts to compare device usage across age groups.
