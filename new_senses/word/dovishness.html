dovishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊvɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of preferring negotiation and compromise over confrontation or aggression, especially in politics or foreign policy
preference for compromise over conflict

Examples

During the debate, his dovishness resonated with voters who were weary of conflict abroad.
Her dovishness was criticized by opponents who preferred a tougher stance on national defense.
The senator's dovishness became apparent when he called for peaceful negotiations instead of military action.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hawkishness - the quality of favoring forceful or aggressive policies, especially in politics or foreign policy

Etymology

From 'dovish' (having the qualities of a dove, symbolizing peace) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting state or quality).
