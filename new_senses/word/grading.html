grading

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of changing the ground level to form a smooth, horizontal, or gently sloping surface, especially in preparation for construction or landscaping
leveling or smoothing ground surface

Examples

After the grading was finished, the land was ready for landscaping and planting.
Proper grading is essential to prevent water from collecting near the foundation of the building.
The construction crew completed the grading of the site before beginning to pour concrete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leveling - the process of making a surface level or flat

Etymology

From the verb 'grade,' ultimately from Latin 'gradus' (step).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging things in a graduated series according to a scale or rank
arranging in a graduated series

Examples

Proper grading of students' work ensures fairness throughout the course.
The grading of the gemstones depends on their color and clarity.
The grading of the produce is done before packing for export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranking - the process of arranging in order of importance or quality

Etymology

From 'grade' (to arrange in steps or ranks) + -ing (forming a noun of action or process).
