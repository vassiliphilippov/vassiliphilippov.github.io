Lucy

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːsi / (AmE), / ˈluːsi / (BrE)
the nickname given to the incomplete fossilized skeleton of an early hominid (Australopithecus afarensis) discovered in Ethiopia in 1974, significant for understanding human evolution
famous early hominid fossil discovered in 1974

Examples

Lucy is one of the most famous and complete early human ancestors ever found.
Researchers continue to study Lucy's bones to learn about the movement of early hominids.
The discovery of Lucy shed new light on the evolution of human bipedalism.

Etymology

Named after the Beatles' song 'Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds', which was played during the excavation.

2. PROPN NEW

/ c8lud0si / (AmE), / c8lud0si / (BrE)
a NASA space mission and spacecraft launched to visit and study the Trojan asteroids near Jupiter, investigating the early history of the solar system.
NASA mission to Trojan asteroids

Examples

NASA's Lucy mission will travel to the Trojan asteroids to study their origins.
Scientists hope Lucy will reveal the composition and history of Jupiter's Trojan asteroids.
At the press briefing the lead engineer explained Lucy's trajectory and planned asteroid flybys in detail.
