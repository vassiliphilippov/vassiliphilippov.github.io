rockabilly

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːk.əˌbɪl.i / (AmE), / ˈrɒk.əˌbɪl.i / (BrE)
a style of popular music combining elements of rock and roll and country, originally popular in the 1950s
style of music fusing rock and country

Examples

Her energetic dancing matched the upbeat rockabilly rhythms playing at the club.
Many musicians in Nashville were inspired by the classic rockabilly sound.
The band's new album pays tribute to rockabilly legends from the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock and roll - genre of popular music from the 1950s characterized by a strong beat
  • Degree Lower: country music - genre of popular music originating in the rural southern US

Etymology

Blend of 'rock (and roll)' and 'hillbilly' (country music).

1950s-inspired subculture and aesthetic

Examples

The club hosts a rockabilly night celebrating a modern revival of 1950s fashion and music.
She wore a vintage polka-dot dress to the rockabilly festival and danced all evening.
Their hairstyle and tattoos show they identify with the rockabilly scene and its retro aesthetic.
