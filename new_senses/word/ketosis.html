ketosis

1. NOUN

/ kiˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which there is an increased level of ketone bodies in the blood, often seen in diabetes or during periods of fasting
medical: elevated ketones in the blood
medical

Examples

Some people experience mild ketosis when following a low-carbohydrate diet.
The patient's blood tests revealed ketosis resulting from prolonged fasting.
Uncontrolled diabetes can sometimes lead to dangerous levels of ketosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidosis - a condition characterized by excessive acid in the body fluids
  • Antonym: alkalosis - a condition characterized by excessive alkalinity of body fluids

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'keto-' (relating to ketone) and '-osis' (denoting a condition).

2. NOUN NEW

/ kiˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A non-pathological metabolic state in which the body produces and uses ketone bodies for energy, typically induced by a ketogenic diet or prolonged carbohydrate restriction.
nutritional metabolic state
medical

Examples

Many dieters aim for ketosis by eating very few carbs and high fat for several weeks.
During the retreat, participants reported improved focus once their bodies entered ketosis after two days.
Doctors distinguish harmless nutritional ketosis from dangerous ketoacidosis when advising people with diabetes.
