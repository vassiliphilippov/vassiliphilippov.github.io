ichneumon

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈnuːmən / (AmE), / ɪkˈnjuːmən / (BrE)
A large mongoose native to northern Africa, historically believed to eat crocodile eggs.
northern African mongoose

Examples

An ichneumon can be difficult to spot due to its elusive behavior.
Naturalists once believed the ichneumon helped control crocodile populations along the Nile.
The ancient Egyptians revered the ichneumon for its skill in hunting crocodile eggs.

Etymology

From Greek 'ikhneúmōn', meaning 'tracker' or 'tracer'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪkˈnuːmən / (AmE), / ɪkˈnjuːmən / (BrE)
A wasp of the family Ichneumonidae; many species are parasitoids whose larvae develop in or on other insects and can help control pests.
parasitoid wasp (Ichneumonidae)

Examples

The farmer released several ichneumon to help control caterpillars in the cabbage field.
An ichneumon laid its egg inside the caterpillar, and the larva later consumed the host.
Researchers recorded an increase in beneficial ichneumon numbers during the orchard pest trial.
