harmonizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːməˌnaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who brings different people, ideas, or things into agreement or harmony; a mediator who resolves differences.
person who brings things into harmony

Examples

As a harmonizer, his calm presence eased tensions between the rival teams.
Maria acted as the harmonizer during the meeting, ensuring everyone reached a fair compromise.
The project needed a harmonizer to align the goals of the different departments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement
  • Synonym: conciliator - a person who helps bring about agreement between two parties

Etymology

From 'harmonize' + '-er', formed after 'harmony'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːməniˌzaɪə / (BrE)
a musician who sings or plays musical parts that create harmony with others
musician who creates musical harmony

Examples

As the harmonizer, she blended her voice perfectly with the lead singer.
Every band needs a strong harmonizer to enrich its overall sound.
He was known as the group's best harmonizer, always improving the depth of their music.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soloist - a musician who performs alone or has a leading part

Etymology

From 'harmonize' (to make harmonious) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
