citron

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt.rən / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.rən / (BrE)
A large, lemon-like citrus fruit with a thick, fragrant rind, often used preserved in cooking.
large lemon-like citrus fruit

Examples

I saw a display of fresh citron at the farmers' market.
She added chopped citron to the fruitcake for extra flavor.
The citron is often candied and used in desserts.

Etymology

From French 'citron', from Latin 'citrōn', from Ancient Greek 'kítron'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrən / (BrE)
A thorny evergreen tree or shrub native to India, widely cultivated for its large, lemonlike fruits with a thick, warty rind.
large lemonlike fruit tree or its fruit

Examples

A traditional fruitcake recipe sometimes calls for chopped citron as an ingredient.
In her garden, she grows a citron tree alongside lemons and oranges.
The citron is valued for its thick rind, which is often candied and used in baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow citrus fruit or its tree, related to citron

Etymology

From Old French *citron*, from Latin *citrō*, from Ancient Greek *kítron* (a kind of citrus fruit).
