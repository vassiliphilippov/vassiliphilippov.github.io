war-torn

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔr tɔrn / (AmE), / ˈwɔː tɔːn / (BrE)
(of a place) severely damaged or ruined by the effects of war.
damaged by war

Examples

After years of conflict, the war-torn city lay in ruins.
Humanitarian aid is desperately needed in the war-torn region.
Many refugees fled their war-torn homeland to seek safety elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastated - completely destroyed or ruined
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance and war

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈwɔr tɔrn / (AmE), / ˈwɔː tɔːn / (BrE)
(of a person, group, or generation) emotionally or psychologically damaged, distressed, or deeply affected by the experience or consequences of war.
emotionally scarred by war

Examples

After years in exile, she felt like part of a war-torn generation unable to trust anyone.
Therapists are trained to support veterans and families from war-torn communities struggling with trauma.
The novel paints a haunting portrait of a war-torn child carrying invisible wounds into adulthood.
