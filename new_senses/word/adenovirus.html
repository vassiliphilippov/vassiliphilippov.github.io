adenovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.n̩.oʊˈvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˌad.nəʊˈvʌɪ.rəs / (BrE)
a group of viruses that can cause respiratory infections, conjunctivitis, and other illnesses in humans and animals
group of viruses causing respiratory illnesses
medical

Examples

Researchers are developing a vaccine targeting several strains of adenovirus.
Schools often experience outbreaks of adenovirus during the winter months.
The doctor explained that the child’s cough was likely caused by an adenovirus infection.

Etymology

From Greek 'adēn' (gland) and Latin 'virus' (poison), first isolated from human adenoids.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cc\u00e6d.n\u0329.o\u028a\u02c8va\u026a.r\u0259s / (AmE), / \u02ccad.n\u0259\u028a\u02c8v\u028c\u026a.r\u0259s / (BrE)
(in medicine and biotechnology) an adenovirus used or modified as a viral vector to deliver genes, vaccine antigens, or other genetic material into cells.
adenovirus used as viral vector
medical

Examples

The trial used an adenovirus to deliver the gene therapy safely to patients' liver cells.
Scientists engineered the adenovirus to carry the vaccine antigen without causing disease in recipients.
After testing, she suggested using an adenovirus vector for a stronger immune response.
