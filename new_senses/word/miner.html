miner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who works in a mine, extracting minerals, coal, or other materials from the earth
person who works in a mine

Examples

In the 19th century, miner strikes were common in coal-producing regions.
Many miners risk their lives daily working underground for precious minerals.
The miner descended into the shaft before sunrise to start his shift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mining - the process or industry of extracting minerals from the ground

Etymology

From Middle English minor, from Old French mineur, from miner (to mine).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a person, organization, or device that uses computer hardware to validate cryptocurrency transactions and (by doing so) create or earn new cryptocurrency units or transaction fees.
person who mines cryptocurrency

Examples

The miner set up a row of GPUs to mine Ethereum in his apartment.
After electricity costs rose, several miners shut down their rigs in the industrial park.
A cloud miner rents server time so users can mine coins without owning hardware.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptocurrency miner - a person or device that performs mining on a blockchain to validate transactions and earn rewards

Etymology

From verb 'mine' + agent suffix -er; extended in the 21st century from extracting resources to extracting (creating) cryptocurrency via computing.
