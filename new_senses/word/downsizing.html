downsizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌsaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌsaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of reducing the size or costs of an organization, often by eliminating jobs, in order to improve financial stability
reducing size or costs in a business

Examples

After the downsizing, many employees were concerned about their job security.
Government agencies are also considering downsizing to reduce public spending.
The company announced a major downsizing in response to declining profits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layoff - dismissal of employees, often for economic reasons
  • Antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From the verb 'downsize' (to make something smaller), formed by combining 'down' and 'size', first popularized in the 1980s in the context of business.
