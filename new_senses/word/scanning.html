scanning

1. NOUN

/ ˈskænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskanɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of converting photographs into a digital form that can be recognized and processed by a computer
converting photos to digital format

Examples

High-resolution scanning ensures that even small details from the original photo are preserved.
Modern libraries use scanning to create digital archives of historic photographs.
The old family albums required scanning so the photographs could be stored safely on a hard drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitization - the process of converting information into a digital format

2. NOUN

/ ˈskænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskænɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of systematically moving a finely focused beam of light or electrons over a surface to produce an image for analysis or transmission
process of imaging by moving a focused beam

Examples

Advances in electron scanning have greatly improved the resolution of microscopes.
Satellite scanning enables scientists to monitor changes in the environment over time.
The laboratory uses scanning to create detailed images of the tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaging - the process of producing images of objects or surfaces
  • Degree Lower: photography - the art or practice of taking and processing photographs

Etymology

From scan + -ing; 'scan' from Latin scandere (to climb, scan).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈskænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskænɪŋ / (BrE)
(in 3D imaging and manufacturing) the process of capturing an object's three-dimensional shape (and often its color or texture) using devices such as laser or structured-light scanners, producing a digital 3D model, point cloud, or mesh.
capturing 3D shape to make a model

Examples

Manufacturers now rely on scanning to produce high-precision 3D models of components.
The museum used handheld scanning to create detailed replicas of fragile artifacts for study.
After reverse-engineering the part, engineers compared the CNC output with the original scanning data.

Synset Examples
