banger

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋər / (AmE), / ˈbæŋə / (BrE)
A firework consisting of a small explosive charge with a fuse in a heavy paper casing, producing a loud bang when ignited.
small explosive firework
british

Examples

On Bonfire Night, children set off bangers in the garden, much to their parents' dismay.
Please be careful with that banger; it can be dangerous if mishandled.
The loud noise from the banger startled everyone on the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firecracker - small explosive firework

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaŋə / (BrE)
(British informal) A pork sausage, especially as served with mashed potatoes.
British informal term for pork sausage
british informal

Examples

He bought a packet of bangers from the butcher for the weekend barbecue.
The pub serves a traditional English breakfast with eggs, banger, and beans.
We had bangers and mash for dinner last night, and it was delicious.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈbæŋɡə / (BrE)
Informal/slang: a very popular, energetic, and highly catchy song or track, especially one played in clubs, on the radio, or online.
energetic popular song (slang)
informal slang

Examples

The DJ dropped a new banger and the dance floor instantly filled with people.
Streaming playlists labeled party are full of modern bangers that keep listeners energized all night.
Critics called the chorus a perfect banger because it was catchy and impossible to forget.
