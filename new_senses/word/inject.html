to inject

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to introduce a new quality, idea, or element into something
introduce something new

Examples

His enthusiasm injected much-needed energy into the team.
She injects fresh ideas into every project she works on.
The director injected humor into an otherwise serious film.

Synset Examples

He injected new life into the performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduce - to bring something into use or operation for the first time

Etymology

From Latin 'injectus', past participle of 'inicere' (to throw in).
