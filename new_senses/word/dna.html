DNA

1. NOUN

/ di ɛn eɪ / (AmE), / diː ɛn eɪ / (BrE)
a molecule present in the nuclei of cells, composed of two long strands forming a double helix, that carries genetic information in living organisms
molecule that carries genetic information
medical

Examples

Every human being has a unique sequence of DNA in their cells.
Genetic diseases can be traced through changes in an individual's DNA.
The scientist analyzed the DNA samples to determine the suspect's identity.

Synset Examples

DNA is the king of molecules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deoxyribonucleic acid - the full chemical name for DNA

Etymology

Abbreviation of deoxyribonucleic acid, from deoxy- (lacking oxygen), ribose (a sugar), and nucleic acid.

(in computing and data archiving) synthetic DNA used as a medium for storing digital information by encoding binary data as sequences of nucleotides.
digital data stored in synthetic DNA

Examples

Researchers encoded a small file into DNA to test its potential as a long-term storage medium.
A startup stores archival photographs by synthesizing DNA strands that represent binary data and reading them later.
Advances in chemistry have made writing and reading DNA for digital backups more accurate and affordable.
