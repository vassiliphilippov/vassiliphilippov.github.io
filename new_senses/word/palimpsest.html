palimpsest

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælɪmpˌsɛst / (AmE), / ˈpælɪmp.sɛst / (BrE)
a manuscript or piece of writing material on which the original writing has been partially erased to make room for later writing but of which traces remain
manuscript with traces of earlier writing

Examples

Scholars spent years deciphering the recovered palimpsest from the monastery's library.
The ancient palimpsest revealed hidden texts when examined under ultraviolet light.
The concept of a palimpsest is often used metaphorically to describe layered histories.

Etymology

From Greek palimpsēstos (scraped again), from palin (again) + psēstos (scraped).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpælɪmpˌsɛst / (AmE), / ˈpælɪmp.sɛst / (BrE)
(in computing and digital forensics) a storage medium or digital record in which previously written data have been partially overwritten but still leave recoverable traces, producing layered information.
storage with recoverable overwritten data

Examples

The forensic team recovered deleted photos from the palimpsest hard drive as crucial evidence.
After multiple overwrites, the technician still found traces on the SSD, calling it a palimpsest.
Their report described the server log as a digital palimpsest revealing previous user activity and edits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwritten storage - a storage medium on which new writes have partially replaced earlier data

Etymology

From Greek palimpsēstos ‘scraped again’ (palin ‘again’ + psēstos ‘scraped’); extended metaphorically to digital storage in recent technical usage.
