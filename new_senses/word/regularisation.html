regularisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of making something regular or more regular, often by making its rules or system consistent or orderly
process of making regular or orderly
british

Examples

Her research focused on the regularisation of irregular verbs in the English language.
The regularisation of building permits was necessary to ensure safety standards were met.
The city's new policy led to the regularisation of hundreds of informal street vendors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regularization - American spelling of 'regularisation'

Etymology

From regular + -isation, modeled on Latin and French forms; equivalent to American 'regularization'.

(in machine learning and statistics) the technique of adding a penalty to a model's loss function to limit complexity and help prevent overfitting.
machine-learning method to reduce overfitting
british

Examples

To prevent overfitting, the team applied L2 regularisation when training the neural network.
Many textbooks explain regularisation as adding a penalty term to the model's loss function.
They compared dropout and weight-decay regularisation techniques to see which improved validation accuracy.
