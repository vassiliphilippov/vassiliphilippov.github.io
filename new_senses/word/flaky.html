flaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪki / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki / (BrE)
consisting of or resembling small, thin pieces that break away easily; made of flakes
having the texture or appearance of flakes

Examples

After drying out, the paint became rough and flaky.
She loves the flaky crust on fresh croissants.
Some types of fish have a particularly flaky texture when cooked.

Synset Examples

flaky soap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crumbly - breaking easily into small fragments
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface or texture

Etymology

From 'flake' + '-y', forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of flakes'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪki / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki / (BrE)
consisting of thin pieces that come off easily or form scales; tending to break or separate into flakes
easily forming flakes; breaks into thin pieces

Examples

After a week of sunburn, his skin became dry and flaky.
Some types of fish have a mild, flaky texture when cooked.
The croissant was perfectly flaky and buttery, just like the ones in Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crumbly - breaking into small fragments or crumbs
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or flaky

Etymology

From 'flake' + '-y', indicating the quality of forming flakes.
