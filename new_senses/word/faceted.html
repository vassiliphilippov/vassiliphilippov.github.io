faceted

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæsɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪtɪd / (BrE)
having flat surfaces (facets) cut or formed on a surface, especially on a gem or crystal
having flat surfaces (facets), especially on gems

Examples

Each faceted bead in the necklace sparkled under the sun.
Light danced off the faceted glass, creating a beautiful pattern on the wall.
The jeweler admired the faceted surface of the newly cut sapphire.

Synset Examples

a faceted diamond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faceted - having many facets or aspects; multifaceted (figurative)
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or faceted

Etymology

From 'facet' + '-ed', indicating possession of facets; 'facet' from French 'facette' (small face or side).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈfæsɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪtɪd / (BrE)
(in computing and user-interface design) describing a search or navigation interface that lets users refine results by selecting among multiple facet filters (for example: category, price, brand).
relating to search interfaces with facet filters

Examples

Users can refine results with faceted search options on the catalog site.
The library's new system shows categories and counts for quick faceted browsing across collections.
When building the shop, they added a faceted menu so customers could filter by size and brand.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: filter - to narrow results by applying criteria or selecting options.
