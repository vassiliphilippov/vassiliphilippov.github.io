pickle

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
to preserve food in a solution of vinegar or brine, often with spices
preserve food in vinegar or brine

Examples

Many people pickle cucumbers to make them last through the winter.
My grandmother taught me how to pickle vegetables using a traditional family recipe.
You can pickle onions in a mixture of vinegar and spices for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserve - to treat food so that it will not spoil
  • Synonym: ferment - to undergo or cause a process of fermentation in food

Etymology

From Middle English *pikel* (spicy sauce or brine for preserving food), probably from Middle Dutch *pekel* (brine).

/ ˈpɪk.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.əl / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relish - a condiment made of chopped pickled vegetables

Etymology

From Dutch *pekel* or Low German *pökel*, meaning brine.

4. VERB NEW

/ ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
(in computing, especially Python) to convert an in-memory object into a byte stream so it can be stored or transmitted and later reconstructed.
serialize an object (computing)

Examples

The server pickled the session data before sending it to the backup service.
Developers often pickle complex objects to store them between program runs.
To speed testing, she started pickling sample responses and reloading them locally.

/ ˈpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəl / (BrE)
(computing) a byte-stream file or object produced by serializing a program object (especially using Python's pickle format).
serialized object (byte stream)

Examples

I opened the pickle file to restore the application's user settings and cache.
The CI pipeline failed because a corrupted pickle caused the restore step to crash.
Always avoid loading an untrusted pickle because it can execute arbitrary code on your machine.
