cart

1. NOUN

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
a vehicle with wheels, typically pushed or pulled by a person, used for carrying goods or loads
wheeled vehicle for carrying loads

Examples

She loaded all her groceries into the cart before heading to the checkout.
The gardener wheeled the cart full of tools across the yard.
Vendors sold snacks from a brightly painted cart near the park entrance.

Synset Examples

he used a handcart to carry the rocks away
their pushcart was piled high with groceries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handcart - a small cart pushed by hand
  • Degree Higher: wagon - a heavier vehicle usually pulled by animals

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kartr', related to Old English 'cræt' (cart, wagon).

2. NOUN

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
a strong open vehicle, usually with two wheels, pulled by an animal and used for carrying heavy loads
large wagon for carrying loads

Examples

Donkeys were used to pull the heavy cart up the hill.
The farmer loaded hay onto his cart before heading to the market.
We saw an old wooden cart parked beside the barn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagon - a vehicle with four wheels for transporting goods or people, often pulled by animals

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kartr' (cart, wagon) and related to Old English 'cræt' (cart, chariot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02974308-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
to carry or move something, especially something heavy or cumbersome, from one place to another, often with effort
carry or move something with effort

Examples

After the picnic, we carted all the leftovers back to the car.
He carted the boxes up three flights of stairs.
She doesn't mind carting her heavy art supplies to class every week.

Synset Examples

haul stones
haul nets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul - to pull or drag something with effort or force
  • Synonym: lug - to carry or drag something heavy or cumbersome

Etymology

From Middle English 'carte', from Old Northern French 'karte', from Latin 'carta' (paper, card), referring to a vehicle for carrying goods.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01457421-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
to transport something or someone in a cart or similar vehicle; to carry or convey something, typically with effort.
transport in a cart or with effort

Examples

Farmers used to cart their produce to market every weekend.
The movers carted the old furniture out of the house.
We had to cart all the boxes up three flights of stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul - to pull or drag with effort or force
  • Synonym: carry - to transport or move something from one place to another

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kartr' or Old English 'cræt', meaning a wheeled vehicle.

5. NOUN NEW

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
a virtual container on an online store or marketplace that holds items a shopper has selected until they complete the purchase.
online shopping container

Examples

She reviewed the items in her online cart before proceeding to checkout.
He left several items in his cart, then returned later to complete the purchase.
The store sent her an email about the abandoned cart with a discount code.

Synset Examples

She reviewed the items in her online cart before proceeding to checkout.
He left several items in his cart, then returned later to complete the purchase.
The store sent her an email about the abandoned cart with a discount code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shopping cart - a container provided by online stores for collecting items to purchase; essentially synonymous with 'cart' in e-commerce.
  • Synonym: shopping basket - an online or physical container used to collect items for purchase; often used interchangeably with 'cart'.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'cart' (a vehicle for carrying goods) to mean a virtual container in online shopping.
