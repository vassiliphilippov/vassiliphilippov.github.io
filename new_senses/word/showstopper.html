showstopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌstɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌstɒpə / (BrE)
something or someone that is exceptionally impressive or attention-grabbing, often standing out because of attractiveness or impact
something exceptionally impressive or striking

Examples

Her performance was such a showstopper that the crowd gave a standing ovation.
That painting is a showstopper in the gallery—it’s what everyone comes to see.
The bride's dress was a real showstopper, dazzling everyone at the wedding.

Synset Examples

she has a show-stopper of a smile
the brilliant orange flowers against the green foliage were a showstopper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - the most outstanding part of an event or period
  • Antonym: dud - something that fails to work or excite

Etymology

Originally from the theater, referring to an act or performer that halts the show with applause; 'show' + 'stopper'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌstɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌstɒpə / (BrE)
An act, performance, or feature in a show that is so spectacular or impressive that it draws prolonged applause and attention from the audience, sometimes momentarily interrupting the performance.
outstanding or striking act in a show

Examples

Her solo in the musical was a real showstopper that had the whole audience on their feet.
That dress is a showstopper; everyone will be talking about it for weeks.
The cake at the wedding was a showstopper, both in taste and appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - the most outstanding part of an event or period
  • Antonym: anticlimax - a disappointing end to an exciting series of events

Etymology

From 'show' (performance) + 'stopper' (one who stops); originally theatrical slang from the late 1920s.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈʃoʊˌstɑpər / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌstɒpə / (BrE)
(in software development and project management) a critical defect, bug, or issue that blocks a product release or prevents progress until it is fixed.
critical blocking defect

Examples

The startup's latest build couldn't ship due to a showstopper bug during testing.
During the meeting QA listed three showstoppers that must be fixed before the product ships.
The project manager called the crash a showstopper and delayed the release until it was solved.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocking bug - a software bug that prevents a system from functioning or a release from proceeding
  • Synonym: release blocker - an issue that must be resolved before software can be released to users

Etymology

Originally theatrical (an act that stops the show); later used metaphorically in business and technical contexts to mean something that halts progress.
