abstractor

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstræk.tɚ / (AmE), / əbˈstræk.tə / (BrE)
a person who makes abstracts or summarizes information, especially by preparing a concise summary of a document
person who makes abstracts or summaries

Examples

As an abstractor of medical journals, her job is to distill complex studies into brief reports.
Every legal firm employs an abstractor to review property records and summarize key details.
The abstractor provided a concise summary of the lengthy research article.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summarizer - a person or thing that summarizes text or information
  • Synonym: abridger - a person who produces an abridged version of a text

Etymology

From 'abstract' + '-or' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ əbˈstræk.tɚ / (AmE), / əbˈstræk.tə / (BrE)
a software tool or artificial intelligence system that automatically creates concise summaries of documents, articles, or datasets.
automated summarizer (software/AI)

Examples

The company uses an abstractor to summarize thousands of medical articles.
We trained an abstractor to condense long reports into one-paragraph summaries for clinicians.
Several hospitals adopted abstractors to speed literature review across multiple departments.
