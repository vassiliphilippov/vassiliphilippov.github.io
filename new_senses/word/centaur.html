centaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɔː / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) a creature with the upper body of a human and the lower body and legs of a horse.
mythical creature, half-man half-horse

Examples

In Greek legend, a centaur was known for its wild behavior and strength.
Many stories feature a wise centaur as a mentor or guide.
The painting depicted a centaur galloping through an ancient forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satyr - a mythological creature, half man, half goat

Etymology

From Latin 'centaurus', from Ancient Greek 'kentauros' (Κένταυρος).
