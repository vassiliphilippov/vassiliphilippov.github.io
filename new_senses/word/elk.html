elk

1. NOUN

/ ɛlk / (AmE), / ɛlk / (BrE)
a large deer with broad antlers, found in the forests of Europe and Asia
large European/Asian deer

Examples

During the autumn, male elk can be heard calling across the valleys.
The dense forests are home to the majestic elk.
We spotted a herd of elk grazing near the river at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moose - large North American deer, called 'elk' in Europe

Etymology

From Old English 'eolh', related to Old Norse 'elgr' (elk).

2. NOUN

/ ɛlk / (AmE), / ɛlk / (BrE)
A large North American deer (Cervus canadensis) with a light brown body and large, branching antlers in the male.
large North American deer

Examples

A herd of elk grazed in the clearing just beyond the forest edge.
The male elk shed their antlers each winter and grow new ones in the spring.
We saw an impressive elk standing by the river during our hike in Yellowstone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moose - a very large North American and Eurasian deer (Alces alces), sometimes confused with the elk

Etymology

From Middle English 'elke', from Old English 'eolh', related to Old Norse 'elgr' and German 'Elch'.

3. NOUN

/ ɛlk / (AmE), / ɛlk / (BrE)
a large northern deer with broad, flat antlers in the male; called ‘elk’ in Europe and ‘moose’ in North America.
large northern deer; moose in North America

Examples

In North America, the animal known as a moose is called an elk in Europe.
The male elk's massive antlers are shed and regrown each year.
We saw an elk crossing the forest road during our vacation in Sweden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moose - large northern deer; North American name for elk

Etymology

From Old Norse elgr (elk, moose) and related to Old English elch (elk).

4. NOUN NEW

/ iː ɛl keɪ / (AmE), / iː ɛl keɪ / (BrE)
A software stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, and Kibana) used to collect, store, search, and visualize log and event data for monitoring and analysis.
log-management software stack

Examples

The data team deployed the ELK stack to centralize server logs and simplify incident searches.
After the outage, engineers used ELK dashboards to trace the sequence of error events across hosts.
Many startups choose ELK or cloud logging services for real-time monitoring and alerting.
