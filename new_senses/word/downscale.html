downscale

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.skeɪl / (BrE)
Intended for people with lower incomes, or relating to products or services that are less expensive and of lower quality than others in the same category.
intended for people with low incomes

Examples

Developers are constructing more downscale apartments to address affordable housing shortages.
Some restaurants offer downscale menus in addition to their gourmet selections.
The company launched a downscale line of furniture to attract budget-conscious buyers.

Synset Examples

mass-produced downscale versions of high-priced fashions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upscale - intended for people with high incomes

Etymology

Formed by combining 'down' and 'scale,' referring to a lower level on a comparative scale.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈdaʊnˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.skeɪl / (BrE)
to reduce the size, scope, or scale of something (such as operations, production, or product lines), often to cut costs or focus on core activities.
reduce size or scope

Examples

Facing shrinking demand, the company decided to downscale its product line to focus on core models.
After budget cuts, they are downscaling operations at three regional warehouses this quarter.
The management team downscaled the marketing department before seeking new investment opportunities.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downsize - to make something smaller, especially by reducing staff or output
  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in size, number, or scope; opposite of reduce or downscale

Etymology

From down + scale; formed by combining 'down' and 'scale' to mean 'reduce in size or scope'.
