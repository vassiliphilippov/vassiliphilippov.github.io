Florence

1. PROPN

/ ˈflɔːrəns / (AmE), / ˈflɒr.əns / (BrE)
a city in central Italy, the capital of Tuscany, renowned as the center of the Italian Renaissance from the 14th to 16th centuries
city in Tuscany, Italy, center of the Renaissance

Examples

During their vacation in Italy, they spent several days exploring the museums and cathedrals of Florence.
Many great artists, such as Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo, lived and worked in Florence.
The Uffizi Gallery is one of the main attractions for visitors to Florence.

Etymology

From Italian 'Firenze', from Latin 'Florentia'.
