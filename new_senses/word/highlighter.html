highlighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌlaɪtə / (BrE)
A cosmetic product applied to the face, especially the eyes or cheekbones, to accentuate features by reflecting light.
cosmetic for accentuating facial features

Examples

A touch of highlighter under the brow bone can make your eyes appear larger.
She applied highlighter to the tops of her cheekbones for a radiant look.
The makeup artist recommended using a liquid highlighter for a subtle glow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronzer - a cosmetic used to give the skin a tanned appearance

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌlaɪtə / (BrE)
a pen with fluorescent ink used to mark important parts of written text so they stand out
fluorescent pen for marking text

Examples

I used a highlighter to mark all the important sections in the report.
Please don't use a highlighter in library books, as it damages the pages.
She pulled a pink highlighter from her pencil case and started underlining key points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marker - a pen used for writing or drawing with bold, often permanent ink

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ha\u026a\u02ccla\u026at\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8ha\u026a\u02ccla\u026at\u0259 / (BrE)
(computing) A software feature or component (often in an editor or IDE) that uses colors or styles to mark and differentiate parts of source code (keywords, strings, variables) to improve readability.
software code colorizer

Examples

The IDE's highlighter colorizes keywords and strings to help developers spot errors quickly.
She enabled the highlighter to show variables in different colors while debugging the application.
Many text editors include highlighters for dozens of languages from Python to JavaScript.
