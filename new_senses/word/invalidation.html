invalidation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌvæləˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌvælɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially ending or making something legally void, especially a relationship or legal proceeding
official act of making void, especially legally
legal

Examples

After careful consideration, the judge ordered the invalidation of the will.
The invalidation of the election results led to a new round of voting.
The court's invalidation of the contract meant it was no longer enforceable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annulment - the formal invalidation of a marriage or contract
  • Antonym: validation - the action of making or declaring something legally valid

Etymology

From Latin validus (strong) via invalidate + -ion.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.vəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of rejecting, ignoring, or dismissing someone's thoughts, feelings, or experiences, often making the person feel undervalued or unimportant
rejecting or dismissing someone's feelings or thoughts

Examples

Emotional invalidation can lead to long-term psychological distress if experienced frequently in childhood.
She felt a painful sense of invalidation whenever her concerns were dismissed by her friends.
Therapists warn that repeated invalidation of a client's emotions can damage the therapeutic relationship.

Synset Examples

Invalidation is emotionally upsetting for anyone, but particularly hurtful for someone who is emotionally sensitive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: validation - recognition or affirmation that someone's feelings or opinions are worthwhile

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidare' (to make weak or null), from 'in-' (not) + 'validus' (strong).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˌɪnˌvæləˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌvæləˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in computing) the process of marking stored data, tokens, or sessions as invalid so they are refreshed, discarded, or rechecked.
marking cached data invalid (computing)

Examples

The system performs invalidation of stale cache entries to ensure fresh data.
After the password change, automatic invalidation of all active sessions logged users out.
Developers scheduled nightly invalidation of tokens so revoked credentials cannot be reused.

Synset Examples

The system performs invalidation of stale cache entries to ensure fresh data.
After the password change, automatic invalidation of all active sessions logged users out.
Developers scheduled nightly invalidation of tokens so revoked credentials cannot be reused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cache eviction - the removal of items from a cache, often to free space or because they are no longer valid
  • Synonym: refresh - to obtain or generate up-to-date data, often after invalidation forces a reload
  • Antonym: validation - the process of confirming that data or credentials are correct and acceptable; opposite of invalidation

Etymology

From Latin validus (strong) via invalidate + -ion.
