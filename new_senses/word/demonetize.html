to demonetize

1. VERB

/ diˈmɑːnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmɒnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to officially deprive a coin, note, or currency of its status as legal tender, so it can no longer be used for payment
remove legal status of currency

Examples

After the reform, all silver coins were officially demonetized and could no longer be used in shops.
Many countries have demonetized small denomination coins due to rising production costs.
The government decided to demonetize the old banknotes to combat counterfeiting.

Synset Examples

demonetize a coin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remonetize - to restore legal tender status to a coin or currency

Etymology

From French démonétiser, from dé- (removal) + monnaie (coin, money).

2. VERB NEW

/ dic8m51d0n59ccta6az / (AmE), / dic8m52n6ata6az / (BrE)
(of an online platform) to remove or disable the ability of content, a channel, or a creator to earn money from advertising, sponsorships, subscriptions, or other monetization features.
remove online monetization

Examples

After the controversy, the streaming service demonetized the creator's channel until the investigation was complete.
Small channels risk losing income when platforms demonetize videos for vague or broad guideline violations.
They warned that repeated copyright strikes could lead to demonetizing and removal of monetization features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplatform - to remove a person or their content from a website or online service, often restricting access or visibility
  • Antonym: to monetize - to earn money from content, services, or products; opposite of removing monetization

Etymology

From French démonétiser, from dé- (removal) + monnaie (coin, money); extended in the 21st century to refer to removing monetization from online content.
