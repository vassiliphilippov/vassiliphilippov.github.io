stuffing

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstʌfɪŋ / (BrE)
soft material used to fill mattresses, cushions, or upholstered furniture to make them comfortable or firm
soft filling in furniture or cushions

Examples

After years of use, the stuffing in the mattress needed to be replaced.
The stuffing in the old sofa had lost its firmness over the years.
The chair was so comfortable because the stuffing was still thick and springy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filling - material used to fill or pad something
  • Synonym: padding - soft material used for stuffing or cushioning

Etymology

From 'stuff' + -ing; 'stuff' from Old French 'estoffe', meaning material or provisions.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstʌfɪŋ / (BrE)
a mixture of seasoned ingredients, such as breadcrumbs, herbs, and vegetables, placed inside meat, poultry, or vegetables before cooking
seasoned mixture for filling meats or vegetables

Examples

I made a delicious herb stuffing for the roast turkey.
She prefers her peppers filled with a spicy vegetable stuffing.
This recipe calls for sausage and apple stuffing baked inside the chicken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filling - a substance used to fill food, especially pastries or sandwiches
