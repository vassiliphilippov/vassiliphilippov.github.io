to sanitize

1. VERB

/ ˈsænɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsænɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make something less offensive or more acceptable by removing or altering anything considered undesirable or sensitive
make less offensive by altering or removing

Examples

She asked the editor to sanitize any offensive language from the manuscript.
Some documentaries have been criticized for trying to sanitize historical events to avoid controversy.
The company decided to sanitize the official report before publishing it to the public.

Synset Examples

sanitize a document before releasing it to the press
sanitize history
sanitize the language in a book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - remove or suppress objectionable material from something
  • Synonym: expurgate - remove parts considered objectionable or offensive

Etymology

From French 'sanitiser', from Latin 'sanitas' (health, sanity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00555545-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsænɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsanɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something clean and free from bacteria or other harmful substances, especially by cleaning or sterilizing
make clean and free from bacteria

Examples

All equipment must be thoroughly sanitized between uses to prevent contamination.
It is important to sanitize your hands before preparing food.
They are sanitizing the playground every evening during the flu season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfect - to clean something using substances that kill bacteria
  • Synonym: sterilize - to make something completely free from bacteria or germs

Etymology

From Latin 'sanitas' (health), via French 'sanitiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01247616-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ ˈsænɪteɪz / (AmE), / ˈsænɪteɪz / (BrE)
(in computing and data management) to remove, mask, or modify sensitive or potentially harmful information in digital data, logs, or user input to protect privacy or security.
remove or mask sensitive digital data

Examples

Before releasing the log files, the engineer sanitized them to remove IP addresses and usernames.
They asked the data team to sanitize the dataset by replacing names with random identifiers.
To prevent SQL injection, the developer always sanitizes user input before inserting it into queries.

Synset Examples

Before releasing the log files, the engineer sanitized them to remove IP addresses and usernames.
They asked the data team to sanitize the dataset by replacing names with random identifiers.
To prevent SQL injection, the developer always sanitizes user input before inserting it into queries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymize - to remove or alter personal identifiers in data so individuals cannot be identified
  • Synonym: redact - to edit or remove sensitive information from a document or record before release

Etymology

From French 'sanitiser', from Latin 'sanitas' (health); extended to data/privacy contexts in modern computing.
