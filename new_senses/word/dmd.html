DMD

1. NOUN

/ diː ɛm ˈdiː / (AmE), / diː ɛm ˈdiː / (BrE)
Doctor of Dental Medicine; an academic degree awarded to dentists in some countries.
doctor's degree in dental medicine
medical

Examples

After four years of dental school, she earned her DMD degree.
All practicing dentists in this clinic hold either a DMD or a DDS.
The university's dental program awards a DMD upon graduation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DDS - Doctor of Dental Surgery; another common dental degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Dentariae Medicinae Doctor'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ did0 5bm c8did0 / (AmE), / did0 5bm c8did0 / (BrE)
(in data analysis and applied mathematics) a numerical method, Dynamic Mode Decomposition, used to identify and extract dominant dynamic patterns (modes) from complex time-series or simulation data.
data-analysis method (Dynamic Mode Decomposition)

Examples

Researchers used DMD to extract dominant modes from the fluid flow data.
The student applied DMD to the vibration records and found repeating patterns quickly.
Engineers ran DMD on the experiment's time series to identify unstable modes.
