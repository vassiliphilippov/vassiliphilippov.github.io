argumentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrgjəmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑːgjʊmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of discussing or reasoning systematically in support of or against a proposition or proposal
systematic reasoning or discussion

Examples

Effective argumentation requires both logical thinking and persuasive skills.
The professor emphasized the importance of clear argumentation in academic writing.
Their heated argumentation revealed deep divisions on the issue.

Synset Examples

the argument over foreign aid goes on and on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular matter
  • Synonym: discussion - a conversation about a particular topic

Etymology

From Latin 'argumentatio' (proof, reasoning), from 'argumentari' (to argue).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrɡjəmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡjʊmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of developing and presenting a reasoned argument in order to persuade others or demonstrate the truth or falsehood of a statement
systematic reasoning or logical argument

Examples

Clear argumentation is essential in academic writing to support your thesis.
Her argumentation was thorough and convinced many of those present.
The lawyer's argumentation led the jury to question the credibility of the witness.

Synset Examples

I can't follow your line of reasoning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reasoning - the process of thinking about something in a logical way
  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion with differing points of view

Etymology

From Latin 'argumentatio' (reasoning, proof), from 'argumentari' (to argue).
