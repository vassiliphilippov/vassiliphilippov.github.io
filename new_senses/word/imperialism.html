imperialism

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a policy of extending a country's power and influence over other countries, especially by direct territorial acquisition or by gaining political and economic control
extension of a country's power over others

Examples

Critics argue that economic imperialism persists through global trade practices.
Many historians debate the consequences of imperialism on indigenous cultures.
The era of European imperialism profoundly reshaped the political boundaries of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonialism - the practice or policy of acquiring full or partial political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically
  • Antonym: anti-imperialism - opposition to imperialism

Etymology

From imperial + -ism, first used in the 19th century to describe European colonial policies.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a policy or practice by which a country increases its power by gaining control over other countries, often through force or other aggressive means
policy of extending a country's power

Examples

Debates about imperialism are still relevant in contemporary geopolitics.
Some historians argue that economic motives were central to nineteenth-century imperialism.
The era of European imperialism left lasting impacts on several continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonialism - the practice of acquiring full or partial control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically
  • Antonym: self-determination - the process by which a country determines its own statehood and forms its own allegiances and government

Etymology

From French 'impérialisme', from Latin 'imperialis' (imperial).

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The policy or practice of extending a country's power and influence through colonization, use of military force, or other means.
policy of extending power or influence

Examples

Critics argue that imperialism often leads to the exploitation of weaker nations.
During the 19th century, European imperialism reshaped global political boundaries.
The museum's new exhibit examines the impact of imperialism on indigenous cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonialism - policy or practice of acquiring full or partial control over another country
  • Antonym: nationalism - loyalty and devotion to a nation; opposing policy to imperialism

Etymology

From French 'impérialisme', from 'impérial' (imperial) + '-isme' (ism).
