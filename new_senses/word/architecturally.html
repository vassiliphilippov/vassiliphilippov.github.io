architecturally

1. ADV

/ ˌɑrkɪˈtɛktʃɚəli / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃərəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to architecture or the design and style of buildings
in a manner relating to architecture

Examples

Although the hotel is modest, it is architecturally significant because of its unique art deco features.
The museum is architecturally stunning, with its sweeping glass façade drawing international praise.
This bridge, while functional, is also architecturally impressive due to its innovative design.

Synset Examples

this building is ugly, but architecturally interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architectural - relating to architecture or the art of designing buildings

(in technology) with respect to the overall structure or organization of a system, especially software or hardware components; concerning high-level design decisions rather than implementation details.
with respect to system architecture

Examples

During the meeting, they agreed the new microservices approach was architecturally sound and easy to extend.
The platform is architecturally modular, allowing teams to deploy services independently without affecting other components.
When reviewing code, she noted the feature was architecturally inconsistent with the rest of the system.
