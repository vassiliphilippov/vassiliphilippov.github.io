mocking

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
Making fun of someone or something in a cruel or contemptuous way; showing ridicule or scorn.
showing ridicule or contempt

Examples

His mocking tone made it clear he didn't take the idea seriously.
She gave him a mocking smile as she walked by.
The students burst into mocking laughter during the awkward presentation.

Synset Examples

derisive laughter
a jeering crowd
her mocking smile
taunting shouts of ‘coward’ and ‘sissy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derisive - expressing contempt or ridicule
  • Synonym: taunting - intended to provoke or mock someone
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'mock', from Old French 'moquer' (to mock or make fun of).

/ ˈmɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
Making fun of someone or something in a way that ridicules or scorns, often playfully or sarcastically.
ridiculing; making fun of

Examples

Her mocking tone made it clear she did not take him seriously.
The students burst into mocking laughter when the teacher made a mistake.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculing - making fun of someone or something
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference

Etymology

(in software development) the practice of using mock objects or simulated components to imitate real modules during automated testing so tests run independently of external systems.
use of mock objects in testing

Examples

The team adopted mocking to isolate the unit under test during development.
Our CI pipeline fails if developers rely on heavy mocking without integration tests.
Because of extensive mocking, they could simulate network errors and speed up debugging.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock object - a simulated object that mimics the behavior of real components in tests
  • Synonym: stub - a simple replacement for a software component used to provide predefined responses in tests

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'mock' (to mimic or imitate); the technical gerundal use developed with modern software testing practices.
