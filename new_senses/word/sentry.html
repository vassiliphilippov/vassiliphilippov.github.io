sentry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntri / (AmE), / ˈsɛntri / (BrE)
a soldier or guard stationed to keep watch and prevent unauthorized passage or danger
guard or lookout

Examples

A sentry spotted the approaching convoy and sounded the alarm.
During their hike, they passed an old guardhouse where a single sentry once kept watch.
The sentry stood motionless at the entrance to the castle all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch, especially a soldier protecting a place

Etymology

From French sentinelle, by alteration and influenced by 'sentinel.'

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsɛntri / (AmE), / ˈsɛntri / (BrE)
an automated or remote-controlled device (for example, a drone, turret, or sensor) placed to watch, detect, warn, or defend a place or perimeter.
automated guard device

Examples

The military base launched a sentry drone to patrol the remote perimeter at night.
Engineers installed an automated sentry turret that can identify and warn of intruders.
During the test, the new sentry sensor detected movement and sent alerts to the control room.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person or device that protects or watches over a place
  • Synonym: drone - an unmanned flying vehicle often used for surveillance or patrol

Etymology

From French sentinelle, by alteration and influenced by 'sentinel'; extended in modern usage to refer to automated guarding devices.
