incognito

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌkɑːɡˈniːtoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒɡˈniːtəʊ / (BrE)
concealing one's identity so as not to be recognized
with identity concealed

Examples

Going incognito helped the journalist get an unbiased view of the situation.
She entered the party incognito, wearing a wig and sunglasses so no one would recognize her.
The famous actor traveled incognito to avoid attracting attention from fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymous - with a name or identity that is not known

Etymology

From Italian 'incognito' (unknown), from Latin 'incognitus' (unknown, unrecognized).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɪnˈkɑːɡniːtoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒɡniːtəʊ / (BrE)
(informal, computing) a browser or application privacy mode that does not save browsing history, cookies, or other session data for the current session.
browser privacy mode

Examples

Open a new incognito window if you don't want the browser to save your history.
She checked the flight on incognito to avoid personalized price increases from trackers.
Journalists often recommend using incognito when researching sensitive topics on public computers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private browsing - a browser mode that does not save browsing history, cookies, or site data for a session
  • Synonym: private window - a browser window opened in private or incognito mode

Etymology

From Italian incognito (unknown), from Latin incognitus (unknown); extended in the 21st century as a noun short for 'incognito mode' in web browsers and apps.
