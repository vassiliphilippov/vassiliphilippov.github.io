immutableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjuːtəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːtəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unchanging or incapable of alteration.
quality of being unchangeable

Examples

Many religious doctrines are valued for their perceived immutableness.
She admired the immutableness of his principles, unchanged despite many challenges.
The immutableness of the law was emphasized by the ancient philosophers.

Synset Examples

Darwin challenged the fixity of species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable
  • Antonym: changeableness - the quality of being able to change

Etymology

From Latin 'immutabilis' (unchangeable) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˈmjuːtəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːtəblnəs / (BrE)
(in computing, especially blockchain and distributed ledgers) the property of digital data, records, or ledgers being unchangeable after writing, typically enforced by cryptographic hashes and distributed consensus mechanisms.
permanent unchangeability of digital data

Examples

Blockchain technology aims to ensure the immutableness of the transaction history.
Auditors trusted the ledger's immutableness when verifying years of financial records.
Some developers worry that rigid immutableness can prevent correcting honest mistakes in stored data.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable; in computing, the property of data that cannot be altered
  • Antonym: mutability - the property of being changeable or alterable; opposite of immutableness

Etymology

From Latin 'immutabilis' (unchangeable) + English suffix '-ness'.
