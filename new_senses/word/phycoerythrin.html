phycoerythrin

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪkoʊˈɛrɪθrɪn / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪkəʊˈɛrɪθrɪn / (BrE)
a red protein pigment found in red algae, involved in photosynthesis by capturing light energy
red pigment in red algae
medical

Examples

Researchers are studying how phycoerythrin helps red algae absorb light at different ocean depths.
The bright red color of some seaweeds is due to the presence of phycoerythrin.
The extraction of phycoerythrin from algae is important for scientific and industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorophyll - green pigment in plants, important for photosynthesis
  • Synonym: carotenoid - yellow, orange, or red plant pigments

Etymology

From Greek 'phykos' (seaweed) + 'erythros' (red), referring to a red seaweed pigment.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02ccfa\u026ako\u028a\u02c8\u025br\u026a\u03b8r\u026an / (AmE), / \u02ccf\u028c\u026ak\u0259\u028a\u02c8\u025br\u026a\u03b8r\u026an / (BrE)
(in biomedical laboratories) a bright fluorescent protein, originally from red algae, used as a dye or label (often conjugated to antibodies) for detecting molecules in flow cytometry and fluorescence imaging.
bright fluorescent protein label (PE)
medical

Examples

In flow cytometry, phycoerythrin is used as a bright fluorescent label to detect cell-surface proteins.
She conjugated the antibody to phycoerythrin so the immune cells would fluoresce under the microscope.
The lab ordered extra phycoerythrin conjugates for the multi-color staining panel next week.
