shortener

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtənər / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtənə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something shorter in length, duration, or extent
agent that makes something shorter

Examples

Exposure to harsh chemicals is a known shortener of fabric durability.
Stress is often cited as a shortener of human lifespan.
The constant delays acted as a shortener of the project's timeline.

Synset Examples

alcohol is a great shortener of life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lengthener - a person or thing that makes something longer

Etymology

From the verb 'shorten' + -er (agent suffix), formed in English.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0283\u0254rt\u0259n\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0283\u0254\u02d0t\u0259n\u0259 / (BrE)
A service or tool that converts long web addresses (URLs) into shorter links, often to simplify sharing or to track clicks.
service that shortens URLs

Examples

The platform offers a shortener that turns long URLs into short links for sharing.
She used several shorteners to compare link-tracking statistics across different social platforms.
Before posting, he checked the shortener's privacy settings to avoid exposing user data.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: URL shortener - a specific tool or online service that creates shortened versions of URLs
  • Synonym: link shortener - an alternative name for a service that shortens web links

Etymology

From the verb 'shorten' + agent suffix -er; extended in modern usage to refer specifically to web URL/link shortening services.
