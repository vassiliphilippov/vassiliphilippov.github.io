to enumerate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnuməˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːməreɪt / (BrE)
to determine the number of (items in a group), often by counting each one
to count items or list them one by one

Examples

Can you enumerate all the factors that contributed to the project's success?
The report attempts to enumerate the various challenges facing the community.
The teacher asked the students to enumerate the planets in the solar system.

Synset Examples

Can you count the books on your shelf?
Count your change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: count - to determine the total number of items
  • Synonym: list - to mention things one by one

Etymology

From Latin enumerare (to count out), from e- (out) + numerare (to count).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnuməˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːməreɪt / (BrE)
to name or list (items) one by one; to specify each individually
to list items one by one

Examples

Could you enumerate the steps we need to follow for this procedure?
In her speech, she carefully enumerated all the achievements of the team.
The report enumerates several reasons for the decline in sales.

Synset Examples

She enumerated the many obstacles she had encountered
The doctor recited the list of possible side effects of the drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: list - to make a list of items; to enumerate
  • Synonym: specify - to state or describe something clearly and exactly, often individually

Etymology

From Latin enumerare, from e- (out) + numerare (to count).

produce index–value pairs (programming)

Examples

In Python, enumerate returns index-value pairs for items in a sequence.
She used enumerate in the loop to access each element's index and value easily.
When teaching beginners, he showed how enumerate pairs numbers with items for clearer output.
