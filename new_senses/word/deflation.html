deflation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of letting air or gas out of something, such as a tire or balloon
release of air or gas from something

Examples

After the game, the deflation of the balloons began as everyone packed up.
Sudden deflation of the air mattress left us sleeping on the floor.
The deflation of the tires made the bicycle difficult to ride.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflation - the act of filling something with air or gas; the opposite process

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' meaning to blow away; influenced by French 'déflation'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
a decrease in the general level of prices in an economy, usually caused by a reduction in the supply of money or credit
fall in general price levels (economics)

Examples

Central banks often lower interest rates to combat the effects of deflation in the economy.
During the Great Depression, the United States experienced severe deflation across multiple industries.
Many economists worry that prolonged deflation can lead to higher unemployment and economic stagnation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflation - general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' (to blow away), later via French 'déflation'.

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Geology) The removal of soil, sand, and loose rocks from an area by the wind, leading to land erosion.
wind erosion of soil and rock (geology)

Examples

Because of severe deflation, large areas of the farmland turned barren and unproductive.
Geologists studied how deflation affects the formation of sand dunes in the region.
The process of deflation can create shallow depressions in desert landscapes over thousands of years.

Synset Examples

a constant deflation of the desert landscape

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' meaning 'to blow away'.

4. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and tokenomics) a mechanism or state in which the total supply of a digital token decreases over time, typically because tokens are permanently removed or 'burned', creating greater scarcity.
reduction of token supply (cryptocurrency)

Examples

The project's constant token buybacks produced a steady deflation that increased scarcity and investor interest.
After the smart contract burned millions of coins, the community welcomed the resulting deflation.
Some economists warn that token deflation can lead to hoarding and reduced network activity.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: token burn - a process that permanently removes tokens from circulation to reduce supply
  • Antonym: inflation - an increase in token supply or in general price levels; opposite concept to token deflation

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' (to blow away) via French 'déflation'; used metaphorically in the 21st century to describe intentional reductions in digital token supply.
