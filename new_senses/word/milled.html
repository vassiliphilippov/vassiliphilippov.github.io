milled

1. ADJ

/ mɪld / (AmE), / mɪld / (BrE)
(of grains, especially rice) having had the husk or outer layers removed by grinding or processing.
having husk/outer layers removed (of grains)

Examples

After harvesting, the wheat is milled to remove the tough outer layers.
Most of the rice sold in supermarkets is milled to improve its cooking properties.
This recipe calls for milled rice instead of wholegrain rice for a softer texture.

Synset Examples

polished rice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholegrain - containing all of the original kernel, including bran, germ, and endosperm
  • Synonym: refined - having impurities or unwanted elements removed by processing

Etymology

Past participle of 'mill', from Middle English 'mille', from Old English 'mylen', from Latin 'molina' (mill).

2. ADJ NEW

/ mɪld / (AmE), / mɪld / (BrE)
Shaped, cut, or finished by a milling machine or by a milling process (in manufacturing, metalwork, or woodworking).
shaped by milling machine

Examples

The engine components were milled from solid aluminum to meet strict dimensional tolerances.
The coin's edge was milled to prevent clipping and give it a textured finish.
She ordered a milled oak tabletop because she wanted precise, machine-cut grooves along its edges.

Synset Examples

The engine components were milled from solid aluminum to meet strict dimensional tolerances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machined - worked or shaped using machine tools; often used interchangeably with 'milled' in manufacturing.
  • Synonym: cut - removed or shaped by a cutting tool; a general term for material removal processes including milling.

Etymology

Past participle of 'mill' (Old English *mylen*), extended to mean worked or shaped by a milling machine.
