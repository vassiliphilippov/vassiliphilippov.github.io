incorruptibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˌrʌptəˈbɪlədi / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˌrʌptəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being incapable of corruption, especially in terms of moral or ethical integrity
incapability of being corrupted

Examples

Many admired the politician for his incorruptibility and strong moral code.
Society depends on the incorruptibility of its leaders to maintain trust and fairness.
The judge's incorruptibility made her highly respected in the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior, often involving bribery
  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles

Etymology

From Latin incorruptibilis (incapable of corruption).
