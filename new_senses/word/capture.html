capture

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə / (BrE)
the process by which a star or planet retains an object within its gravitational field
retaining an object via gravity

Examples

Planetary scientists study the capture of comets by Jupiter.
The gravitational capture of asteroids explains some planetary moons.
The theory describes how capture occurs when the object's velocity decreases below escape velocity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession, also used in astronomy

Etymology

From Latin captūra (a taking), from capere (to take).

/ ˈkæp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə / (BrE)
