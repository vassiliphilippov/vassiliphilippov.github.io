B4

1. NOUN

/ ˌbi ˈfɔr / (AmE), / ˌbiː ˈfɔː / (BrE)
A standard size of paper measuring 250 x 353 millimeters, used especially in printing and stationery.
standard paper size (250 x 353 mm)

Examples

Please print the document on B4 paper to ensure all the details fit.
The brochure was designed to fit perfectly on a B4 sheet.
The office stocks a variety of paper sizes, including B4 and A4.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A4 - A standard paper size (210 x 297 mm) commonly used in offices and printing
  • Degree Higher: B3 - A standard paper size (353 x 500 mm), one size larger than B4 in the B series

Etymology

From the ISO standard for paper sizes; 'B' denotes a specific series, and '4' its relative size.

2. ADV NEW

/ bɪˈfɔr / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔː / (BrE)
(informal, written) a text or internet shorthand meaning 'before', used to save space or type quickly.
texting shorthand for 'before'
informal slang

Examples

She texted that she'd arrive B4 noon, so we set the meeting for eleven.
Parents warned: finish your homework B4 you go online to play games tonight.
I typed B4 in the caption to save characters while keeping the meaning clear.
