tag

1. NOUN

/ tæɡ / (AmE), / tæɡ / (BrE)
a small piece of paper, card, or plastic, attached to something to give information about it, such as its owner, contents, or price
label attached to show information

Examples

Please write your name on the tag before attaching it to your suitcase.
She forgot to remove the tag from her new shirt.
The price tag showed that the book cost $20.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - piece of paper or other material attached to something to identify it or give information

Etymology

From Middle English tagge, of uncertain origin.
