lah

1. NOUN

/ lɑ / (AmE), / lɑː / (BrE)
the syllable used to name the sixth note (submediant) of a major or minor scale in solmization (as in solfège)
sixth note in solfège (music)

Examples

In solfège training, lah represents the sixth note of the scale.
She sang the melody up to lah and then came back down.
The sequence do, re, mi, fa, so, lah, ti is used to teach pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do - the syllable for the first note of the scale in solmization
  • Synonym: re - the syllable for the second note of the scale in solmization
  • Synonym: so - the syllable for the fifth note of the scale in solmization
  • Synonym: ti - the syllable for the seventh note of the scale in solmization

Etymology

From the solfège system, originated in 11th-century Italy, using syllables from a medieval hymn.

2. PART NEW

/ lɑ / (AmE), / lɑː / (BrE)
A sentence-final discourse particle used in Southeast Asian English varieties to add emphasis, soften a statement or request, reassure, or signal casual friendliness.
Southeast Asian English discourse particle
informal

Examples

Don't worry about the meeting, lah, everyone knows how busy you are today.
She smiled and said lah to make her disagreement sound less harsh and friendlier.
If you're tired, just go home lah — nobody will blame you for leaving early tonight.
