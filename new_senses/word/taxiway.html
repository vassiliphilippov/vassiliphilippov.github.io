taxiway

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksiˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtæksiˌweɪ / (BrE)
a paved strip at an airport used by aircraft to move between the runway and the parking area, hangars, or terminal
paved airport path for taxiing planes

Examples

Maintenance vehicles were seen driving on the taxiway early this morning.
Pilots must receive clearance before entering an active taxiway.
The plane left the runway and proceeded along the taxiway to the terminal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runway - a long, flat surface where aircraft take off and land

Etymology

From 'taxi' (of an aircraft: to move on the ground under its own power) + 'way'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtæksiˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtæksiˌweɪ / (BrE)
a designated low-altitude aerial corridor or route used by unmanned aerial vehicles (drones) and urban air taxis to move between takeoff, landing or delivery points.
aerial corridor for drones

Examples

City officials published maps showing new drone taxiway corridors above industrial zones for deliveries.
Operators must program their eVTOLs to follow designated taxiway routes during low-altitude flights over the city.
Emergency services rehearsed redirecting drones away from a blocked taxiway during an urban air mobility test.
