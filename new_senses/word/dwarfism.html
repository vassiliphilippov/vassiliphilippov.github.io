dwarfism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdwɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
a medical or genetic condition resulting in abnormally short stature or growth.
condition causing short stature
medical

Examples

He has a rare type of dwarfism that results in shorter limbs but a normal torso.
Some forms of dwarfism are caused by genetic mutations that affect bone growth.
The charity supports families affected by dwarfism and promotes awareness about the condition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gigantism - a condition causing excessive growth in stature

Etymology

From 'dwarf' + '-ism', modeled on medical term formation.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdwɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdwɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
(in botany and horticulture) the condition or trait in plants that results in unusually small or compact growth, whether genetic or induced.
compact or reduced plant growth

Examples

The nursery introduced a new dwarfism trait in several conifer varieties for small urban gardens.
After years of selective breeding, the rose breeder promoted dwarfism to make plants suitable for containers.
Cold stress during early development can cause temporary dwarfism in young seedlings, reducing their height.
