teal

1. ADJ

/ tiːl / (AmE), / tiːl / (BrE)
of a bluish-green color, resembling the plumage of the teal bird
bluish-green in color

Examples

He chose a teal tie to complement his navy suit.
She wore a stunning dress in teal that caught everyone's attention.
The walls were painted a soft teal to create a calming atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turquoise - a greenish-blue color
  • Synonym: aqua - a light bluish-green color

Etymology

From the name of the teal bird, whose plumage has a characteristic bluish-green color.

3. NOUN

/ til / (AmE), / tiːl / (BrE)
a small, short-necked dabbling duck found in Europe and America, known for its swift flight and often colorful plumage
small river duck of Europe and America

Examples

A flock of teal skimmed over the surface of the lake at dawn.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare green-winged teal among the ducks.
The reserve is an important wintering site for many species, including the teal.

Etymology

From Middle English *tele*, of uncertain origin, possibly imitative or related to Old French *tiele*.

4. NOUN NEW

/ til / (AmE), / tiːl / (BrE)
(in management theory) an organization that emphasizes self-management, wholeness, and an evolutionary purpose, with decentralized authority and high employee autonomy.
self-managing organization (management theory)

Examples

After revising its structure, the company became a teal organization focused on purpose and autonomy.
Consultants recommended adopting teal practices such as self-management and transparent decision-making across teams.
Many startups experiment with teal principles, giving employees more autonomy and wholeness in their roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-management - an organizational approach in which decision-making authority is distributed among employees rather than centralized
  • Antonym: hierarchy - an organizational structure with clear levels of authority, often contrasted with self-managing models

Etymology

Named metaphorically after the color teal; popularized in contemporary management writing (notably Frederic Laloux's Reinventing Organizations, 2014).
