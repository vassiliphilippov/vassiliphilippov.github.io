tracing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of discovering and describing the origin or development of something
discovering and describing a sequence of development

Examples

Historians spend years on the tracing of ancient family trees.
The tracing of the virus's spread proved essential to controlling the outbreak.
The detective began the tracing of the missing person's last known movements.

Synset Examples

the tracing of genealogies

Etymology

From trace + -ing; 'trace' from Old French tracer, from Vulgar Latin *tractiare, from Latin tractus (a pulling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00151752-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪ.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪ.sɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making a copy or outline of a drawing, plan, or diagram by following its lines or shapes, usually through a transparent sheet
the act of copying by outlining

Examples

For the art class, we practiced tracing letters using transparent paper.
She finished her tracing of the flower, carefully following every petal.
The architect made a tracing of the original blueprint for reference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copying - the act of making a copy of something
  • Synonym: drawing - the act of creating a picture by making lines on a surface

Etymology

From trace + -ing, based on the act 'to trace' (to copy by following lines of a drawing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00940235-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a drawing made by placing a semitransparent sheet over an original image and copying its lines
drawing copied using transparent sheet

Examples

Children often enjoy creating a tracing of animals or cartoons to practice their drawing skills.
She kept a tracing of her grandmother's signature as a cherished keepsake.
The architect made a tracing of the original blueprint to share with the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - a duplicate of an original, also refers to artistic reproduction

Etymology

From the verb 'trace' (Middle English, from Old French 'tracier', from Vulgar Latin *tractiare, derivative of Latin 'tractus', meaning 'dragged, drawn').

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing) the process of recording, following, and reconstructing the sequence of events, function calls, or request/data flows within a software system to diagnose problems or analyse performance.
software event recording for debugging

Examples

Developers enable tracing to visualize request paths and diagnose latency across microservices in production.
The team reviewed the tracing logs to find where the payment request failed during peak traffic.
After adding distributed tracing, they could follow user requests through three services to reproduce bugs.

Synset Examples
