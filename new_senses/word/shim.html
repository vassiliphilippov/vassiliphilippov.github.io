shim

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪm / (AmE), / ʃɪm / (BrE)
a thin piece of material, such as wood, metal, or stone, used to fill a space or make something level
thin spacer or wedge

Examples

He used a thin metal shim to fill the gap between the parts.
The carpenter slid a shim under the door to make it level.
You may need to add a shim to stop the table from wobbling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a piece of material with one thick end and one thin end used to split or hold objects apart

Etymology

From Scottish English 'shim', of uncertain origin, possibly from a dialectal word for a thin slice, related to 'shive'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ʃɪm / (AmE), / ʃɪm / (BrE)
(computing) a small piece of code or library that provides compatibility by intercepting, emulating, or translating calls so older software can run on a newer system.
software compatibility layer

Examples

The developers added a shim so the old plugin runs on the new browser.
We loaded several shims to emulate missing APIs during the migration test.
When the OS updated, a small shim intercepted calls and provided compatibility.

Etymology

From the physical noun 'shim' (a thin wedge or spacer; from Scottish English 'shim'), used metaphorically in computing to mean a small piece that fills a compatibility gap.
