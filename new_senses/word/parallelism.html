parallelism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌlɛlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛlɪzəm / (BrE)
the state of being similar or corresponding in some way, often in structure, meaning, or development
correspondence or similarity in form or meaning

Examples

The parallelism between their careers was striking, as both became leaders in their fields.
There is a noticeable parallelism in the development of these two scientific theories.
Writers often use parallelism to give rhythm and balance to their sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - resemblance or likeness
  • Antonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Greek 'parallēlismos', from 'parallēlos' (parallel).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8p\u00e6r\u0259\u02ccl\u025bl\u02cc\u026az\u0259m / (AmE), / \u02c8par\u0259l\u025bl\u026az\u0259m / (BrE)
(in computing) the use of multiple processors, cores, or devices to perform multiple computations at the same time in order to increase speed or throughput.
simultaneous computing across processors

Examples

Modern AI training relies on parallelism to scale computations across GPUs.
The software achieved faster results by exploiting parallelism across multiple CPU cores during data processing.
Students studied how parallelism affects algorithm design and how to synchronize shared memory safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallel computing - computing approach that divides problems into parts run simultaneously on multiple processors
  • Synonym: concurrency - overlapping execution of tasks that may run simultaneously or be interleaved; related but not identical to parallelism
  • Antonym: serial processing - processing tasks one after another rather than at the same time; opposite of parallelism
