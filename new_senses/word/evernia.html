Evernia

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈvɜrniə / (AmE), / ɛˈvɜːnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of lichens in the family Usneaceae, characterized by a pendulous or shrubby thallus.
genus of pendulous or shrubby lichens

Examples

Many tree branches were draped with Evernia, giving the forest a romantic appearance.
Researchers sampled Evernia thalli to study air quality in the region.
The distinctive, shrubby structure of Evernia makes it easy to distinguish from other lichens.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Greek 'evernos' (wild goat), relating to the appearance of some species.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɛˈvɜrniə / (AmE), / ɛˈvɜːniə / (BrE)
(in perfumery) an extract or ingredient obtained from the lichen Evernia prunastri, used to give fragrances oakmoss, mossy, or woody notes.
perfumery ingredient (oakmoss extract)

Examples

Perfume houses often add Evernia to give fragrances a deep, earthy oakmoss note.
Regulations now limit the use of Evernia extracts in some perfumes because of allergen concerns.
The perfumer blended amber and bergamot with Evernia to create a classic chypre accord.
