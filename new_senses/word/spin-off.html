spin-off

1. NOUN

/ spɪn ˌɔf / (AmE), / spɪn ˌɒf / (BrE)
a secondary product that is produced during the manufacture of another product; a by-product
secondary product; by-product

Examples

During the chemical process, several useful spin-offs were created alongside the main compound.
One important spin-off of the project was a more efficient method of recycling materials.
The new cleaning solvent was a spin-off from the company's research into plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by-product - a secondary product resulting from a manufacturing process
  • Antonym: main product - the primary product produced in a manufacturing process

Etymology

From the verb 'spin off,' meaning to create something as a by-product or offshoot, originally from 'spin,' referring to the act of turning fibers into thread.

2. NOUN NEW

/ spɪn ˈɔf / (AmE), / spɪn ˈɒf / (BrE)
a new company, product, television programme, or other work that is developed from an existing one, often to use its technology, characters, or market appeal.
derivative company, product, or media work

Examples

The tech company created a successful spin-off to market a new security product globally.
Fans eagerly watched the new spin-off that focused on a supporting character's life after the finale.
The university formed a research spin-off to commercialize the laboratory's renewable energy technology products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offshoot - something that has developed from something larger or earlier
  • Synonym: derivative - a product or work based on another existing work
  • Antonym: original - the primary or first work or product from which a spin-off is derived

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'spin off' (spin + off), originally meaning to separate; extended to mean a new entity formed from an existing one.
