poisoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving poison to a person or animal, especially with the intent to cause harm or death
act of giving poison to harm or kill

Examples

Improper storage of chemicals can sometimes lead to accidental poisoning in households.
Many historical figures have died as a result of deliberate poisoning.
The detectives suspected poisoning after finding a strange substance in the victim's drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes harm or death when introduced to a living organism

Etymology

From the verb 'poison', which derives from Old French 'puison', meaning a drink or potion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
A harmful physiological condition caused by the absorption of a poison or toxic substance into the body.
harmful condition from poison or toxin

Examples

Carbon monoxide poisoning can be fatal if not treated quickly and effectively.
The doctor diagnosed her condition as lead poisoning caused by exposure at work.
The patient was admitted to the hospital with acute poisoning after ingesting contaminated food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being poisoned or adversely affected by a substance
  • Synonym: overdose - an excessive and dangerous amount of a drug taken at one time

Etymology

From poison + -ing, denoting the process or result of being poisoned.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing, especially machine learning) the deliberate introduction of misleading, malicious, or corrupted data into training or input datasets to bias, degrade, or control a model's behaviour or outputs.
deliberate contamination of training data

Examples

Researchers published a report describing a successful poisoning attack on the facial-recognition training dataset.
Companies now audit datasets to prevent poisoning that could bias automated hiring systems against applicants.
After discovering subtle label flips, the team traced the model failure back to data poisoning.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data poisoning - an attack that corrupts training or input data to change a model's behaviour
  • Synonym: adversarial attack - an attempt to make a model produce wrong outputs, often by manipulating inputs or training data

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'poison' (ultimately from Old French *puison*, 'drink'); used in modern computing to mean contaminating data.
