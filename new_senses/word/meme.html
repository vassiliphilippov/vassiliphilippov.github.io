meme

1. NOUN

/ miːm / (AmE), / miːm / (BrE)
an idea, behavior, or style that spreads from person to person within a culture by imitation or other non-genetic means
cultural idea or behavior passed by imitation

Examples

Language itself can be viewed as a complex system of transmitted memes.
Some behaviors become entrenched as memes that are passed down through generations.
The concept of a meme explains how ideas can rapidly spread within a society.

Synset Examples

memes are the cultural counterpart of genes

Etymology

Coined by British biologist Richard Dawkins in 1976, formed from Greek 'mimema' (that which is imitated), on the model of 'gene'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ miːm / (AmE), / miːm / (BrE)
An image, video, piece of text, or other digital media item, often humorous or satirical, that is copied and shared widely online and modified by many users.
viral online image or media item

Examples

The meme about the distracted boyfriend spread across social media overnight.
Teenagers often create new memes by combining familiar pictures with funny, topical captions.
Marketing teams sometimes try to produce a meme that will go viral and boost engagement.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
