specialiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in and concentrates on a particular occupation or branch of learning
person expert in one field
british

Examples

As a marketing specialiser, she focuses on social media strategies for brands.
Many companies prefer to hire a specialiser rather than a generalist for technical roles.
The hospital employed a specialiser in infectious diseases to oversee the department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialist - a person who concentrates on a particular subject or activity; an expert

Etymology

From special + -ise + -er, following the British English spelling of 'specialize.'

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8sp\u025b\u0283\u0259\u02ccla\u026az\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8sp\u025b\u0283\u0259la\u026az\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing) a program that automatically produces a specialized or optimized version of another program, typically by using information about a particular architecture or input.
program that generates specialized code
british

Examples

The compiler team developed a specialiser to produce optimized binaries for embedded processors.
Using a specialiser, researchers generated input-specific versions of the interpreter to speed up tests.
Several machine-learning libraries include specialisers that tailor models to particular hardware during deployment.
