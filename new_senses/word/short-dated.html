short-dated

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔrt ˌdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a bond, security, or financial instrument) having less than five years remaining before repayment or maturity.
having less than five years to maturity (finance)

Examples

Financial advisers often recommend short-dated assets during periods of market uncertainty.
Many investors prefer to buy short-dated government bonds to reduce their exposure to interest rate risk.
The fund specializes in short-dated securities that mature within the next three years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: long-dated - having more than five years to maturity (finance)

(of food or other perishable goods) having only a short time left before the expiry or best-before date; likely to expire soon.
nearing expiry (perishables)

Examples

The supermarket discounted several short-dated yogurts to clear them before the weekend.
Farmers worried their short-dated produce would spoil during the unexpected heatwave this week.
She refused to buy the short-dated bread even though it was heavily reduced in price.
