speller

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɛl.ə / (BrE)
a person who spells words, especially in terms of spelling accuracy or ability
person who spells words

Examples

As a poor speller, he struggled with written assignments.
She has always been a good speller since childhood.
Winning the competition proved that he was the best speller in the class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthographer - a person who is skilled in correct spelling or orthography
  • Synonym: spelling bee - a competition in which participants are asked to spell words

Etymology

From 'spell' (to form words with the correct sequence of letters) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛlər / (AmE), / ˈspɛlə / (BrE)
a textbook designed to teach the correct spelling of words, especially for beginners or children
book for teaching spelling

Examples

Her first speller contained simple words and colorful illustrations.
Many nineteenth-century students learned how to read and write using a speller.
The teacher asked each child to bring a speller and a notebook to class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primer - an elementary textbook for teaching reading or basic skills

Etymology

From 'spell' (to write or name the letters of a word) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
