pasteboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪstˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈpeɪstˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a stiff, strong cardboard made by gluing together several layers of paper
stiff cardboard made of layered paper

Examples

He carefully cut the pasteboard to fit the frame for his project.
The artist mounted her sketch on a piece of pasteboard for display.
We used pasteboard to make durable signs for the school fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardboard - a stiff paper-based material for making boxes and signs

Etymology

From 'paste' + 'board', denoting a board made by pasting papers together.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpeɪstˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈpeɪstˌbɔːd / (BrE)
(computing) a temporary area in a computer's memory or operating system used to store data (text, images, files) that has been cut or copied so it can be pasted elsewhere.
digital clipboard

Examples

She copied the image to the pasteboard and pasted it into the report.
Developers use the pasteboard API to share text and images between apps on macOS.
After copying the link, check the pasteboard before closing the browser to ensure it saved.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clipboard - a software feature or area that temporarily holds cut or copied data for pasting

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the physical word 'pasteboard' to computing; used historically in systems such as NeXT/macOS to mean the clipboard.
