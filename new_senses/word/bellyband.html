bellyband

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛliˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌbænd / (BrE)
a cloth band worn around the waist, especially used for infants until the navel has healed
cloth band worn around the waist

Examples

Many traditional cultures use a bellyband for infants during the first few weeks of life.
She bought a soft cotton bellyband for her daughter after the baby's birth.
The nurse wrapped a bellyband around the newborn baby to protect his navel.

Etymology

From 'belly' (abdomen, stomach) + 'band' (strip for binding).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛliˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌbænd / (BrE)
a strap around the belly of a draft animal used to secure the shafts of a wagon or carriage
strap securing wagon shafts on draft animals

Examples

Before setting off, he tightened the bellyband around the mule's stomach.
Old carriages often used a leather bellyband to keep the shafts in place.
The farmer checked the bellyband to make sure the wagon was securely attached to the horse.

Etymology

From 'belly' + 'band', meaning a band that goes around the belly.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbɛliˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌbænd / (BrE)
a wide band, belt, or wrap worn around the abdomen during pregnancy or after childbirth to support the belly and lower back.
maternity support belt

Examples

She wore a maternity bellyband to support her growing belly during late pregnancy.
After the birth, the nurse recommended a soft postpartum bellyband to ease abdominal discomfort.
Many runners use a compression bellyband during exercise to support core muscles after surgery.
