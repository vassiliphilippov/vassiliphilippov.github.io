cross-link

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔs lɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs lɪŋk / (BrE)
a chemical bond that joins two adjacent chains of atoms within a complex molecule, often strengthening the structure (as in polymers or proteins)
chemical bond joining molecular chains

Examples

Certain proteins contain cross-links that stabilize their structure.
Scientists studied how UV light can break the cross-links in DNA molecules.
The durability of rubber is increased by introducing cross-links between polymer chains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a connection holding atoms together in molecules

Etymology

From English 'cross' + 'link', referring to linking across chains.

2. VERB

/ krɔs lɪŋk / (AmE), / krɒs lɪŋk / (BrE)
to join adjacent chains of a polymer or protein by creating covalent bonds between them
join polymer/protein chains by covalent bonds

Examples

After being cross-linked, the hydrogel became much more resistant to deformation.
Certain enzymes can cross-link proteins, altering the material's properties.
The researchers used chemicals to cross-link the polymer chains for greater stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: link - to join or connect two or more things together

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'link', referring to the linking of chains across from each other.
