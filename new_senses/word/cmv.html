CMV

1. NOUN

/ siː ɛm viː / (AmE), / siː ɛm viː / (BrE)
a type of herpes virus, cytomegalovirus, that causes enlarged epithelial cells and can lead to serious illness or birth defects, especially in people with weakened immune systems
a herpes virus causing cell enlargement and illness
medical

Examples

Patients with weakened immune systems are particularly vulnerable to severe CMV complications.
Routine screening for CMV is recommended for organ transplant recipients.
The newborn was diagnosed with CMV infection after developing persistent jaundice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytomegalovirus - a type of herpesvirus that causes enlarged cells

Etymology

Acronym of cytomegalovirus, from Greek 'cyto-' (cell) + 'megalo-' (large) + 'virus'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ siː ɛm viː / (AmE), / siː ɛm viː / (BrE)
An internet initialism (Change My View) used to label a post in which the author invites others to present arguments that might persuade them to change their opinion.
online 'Change My View' tag
informal

Examples

She posted a CMV asking why remote work isn't better for productivity in software teams.
The subreddit requires posters to include a clear argument when they tag a post with CMV.
He started his thread with CMV to invite thoughtful replies challenging his view on remote learning.
