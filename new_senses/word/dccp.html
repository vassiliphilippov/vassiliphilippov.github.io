2. NOUN NEW

/ di si si pi / (AmE), / diː siː siː piː / (BrE)
(computing, networking) the Datagram Congestion Control Protocol, a transport-layer protocol that provides congestion-controlled datagram delivery for applications where timely delivery matters more than reliable, ordered delivery (defined in RFC 4340).
congestion-controlled datagram transport protocol

Examples

Network engineers compare DCCP to TCP and UDP when discussing congestion control for real-time traffic.
Streaming services sometimes use DCCP to manage packet loss and latency for live video.
After testing, the team configured DCCP to prioritize interactive voice packets over bulk transfers.
