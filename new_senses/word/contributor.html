contributor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtrɪbjətɚ / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjʊtə / (BrE)
a person, especially a writer, whose work is published in a newspaper, magazine, or book as part of a collection or periodical
writer whose work is published in a publication

Examples

Several contributors submitted essays for the anthology.
She started as a freelance contributor before joining the newspaper staff full time.
The magazine featured an article by a regular contributor on travel destinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: author - a person who writes books or other works
  • Antonym: editor - a person who prepares written material for publication

Etymology

From Latin 'contributus', past participle of 'contribuere' (to bring together, add)

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtrɪb.jə.tɚ / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪb.jʊ.tə / (BrE)
a person who gives or pledges money for a particular purpose, such as a charity, project, or fund.
person who donates money

Examples

Every contributor will receive a receipt for their donation.
The event raised over $10,000 from generous contributors.
The museum thanked each contributor who supported the restoration project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donor - a person who gives something, especially money or blood, to help a person or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'contributus', past participle of 'contribuere' (to bring together, add).

3. NOUN NEW

/ kənˈtrɪbjətə / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjətə / (BrE)
A person who adds content, code, edits, or other material to an online collaborative project (for example, an open-source repository or a wiki).
person who adds content or code online

Examples

She became a contributor to the open-source project, submitting code and documentation.
As a regular contributor, he improves articles and answers questions on the community wiki.
Several contributors reviewed the pull request before the maintainer merged it into master.
