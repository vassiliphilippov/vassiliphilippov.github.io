2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
a horizontal structural beam or member placed at the end of an opening to support the load from the ends of joists, studs, or rafters, transferring their weight to adjacent parallel members
structural beam supporting ends of joists, etc.

Examples

A header ensures the weight from the upper floor is distributed to the adjoining wall studs.
The carpenter installed a header above the window frame to support the roof load.
When remodeling, it's important not to remove a header without proper structural reinforcement.
