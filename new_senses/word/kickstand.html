kickstand

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈkɪk.stænd / (BrE)
a metal rod attached to a bicycle, motorcycle, or similar vehicle that can be swung down to support the vehicle when it is not being ridden
support rod for bicycles or motorcycles

Examples

Please remember to flip up the kickstand before starting to ride.
She pulled down the kickstand before getting off her bike.
The motorcycle tipped over because the kickstand wasn't properly engaged.

Etymology

Formed from 'kick' and 'stand' referring to the action required to deploy it and its supporting function.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8k\u026ak\u02ccst\u00e6nd / (AmE), / \u02c8k\u026ak.st\u00e6nd / (BrE)
a small fold-out support built into or attached to an electronic device or its case that props the device upright for viewing or typing
fold-out support on an electronic device

Examples

The tablet's kickstand folds out to prop the screen at a comfortable viewing angle.
She adjusted the laptop kickstand before the video call to get the camera angle right.
Many tablet cases include a small kickstand that makes typing and watching videos easier.
