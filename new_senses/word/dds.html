DDS

1. NOUN

/ di di ɛs / (AmE), / diː diː ɛs / (BrE)
an abbreviation for Doctor of Dental Surgery, a professional doctoral degree for dentists
abbreviation for Doctor of Dental Surgery

Examples

After completing her DDS, she opened her own dental clinic in the city.
Her diploma proudly displayed the title DDS after her name.
You must earn a DDS to become a licensed dentist in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DMD - Doctor of Medicine in Dentistry, another dental degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of Doctor of Dental Surgery.

2. NOUN NEW

/ di di ɛs / (AmE), / diː diː ɛs / (BrE)
An acronym for Data Distribution Service, a middleware standard for real-time, data-centric publish–subscribe communication in distributed systems.
real-time publish–subscribe middleware

Examples

The robotics system relies on DDS for real-time, reliable data sharing between modules.
During testing the team used DDS to coordinate sensor data across multiple autonomous vehicles.
Engineers chose DDS because it provides fine-grained quality-of-service controls for time-critical messages.
