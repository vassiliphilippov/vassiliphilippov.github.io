confetti

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛti / (AmE), / kənˈfɛti / (BrE)
Small pieces or streamers of colored paper that are thrown into the air on festive occasions, such as weddings or parties.
small pieces of colored paper thrown at celebrations

Examples

After the ceremony, guests showered the newlyweds with confetti as they exited the church.
Children laughed and tossed confetti into the air during the birthday party.
The parade was lively, with colorful confetti falling from the rooftops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streamer - a long, narrow strip of material used as a decoration

Etymology

From Italian 'confetti', originally meaning 'sugared almonds', later used for paper pieces thrown at celebrations.

2. NOUN NEW

/ kənˈfɛti / (AmE), / kənˈfɛti / (BrE)
(in computing and digital design) an on-screen animated graphic of falling pieces, sparkles, or other small elements used to celebrate or reward a user action.
digital animated celebration effect

Examples

After completing the tutorial, the app celebrated my achievement with falling confetti across the screen.
The team cheered when the project tracker showed the final milestone and a burst of confetti.
Designers often add subtle confetti animations to reward users for completing small tasks.
