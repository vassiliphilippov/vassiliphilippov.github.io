sandbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈsændˌbɒks / (BrE)
a container or area filled with sand, especially for children to play in
box or area of sand for children

Examples

Her favorite playground activity is digging tunnels in the sandbox.
The children spent the afternoon building castles in the sandbox.
We put a new cover on the sandbox to keep the neighborhood cats out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpit - a British term for a children's container or area of sand to play in

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈsændˌbɒks / (BrE)
A container filled with sand, used in metal casting to shape molds.
container of sand for metal molds

Examples

A well-made sandbox ensures that the mold retains its shape during casting.
The foundry worker carefully packed the sandbox to prepare for the metal pour.
They inspected each sandbox for flaws before beginning the production process.

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8s\u00e6nd\u02ccb\u0251ks / (AmE), / \u02c8s\u00e6nd\u02ccb\u0252ks / (BrE)
(in computing) a controlled, isolated environment in which untrusted or experimental code or software can run safely without affecting the host system or production data.
isolated computing environment

Examples

Developers run experiments in a sandbox to observe behavior without risking the main system.
The security team executed the suspicious program inside a sandbox to analyze its network activity safely.
Students used a cloud-based sandbox to try coding exercises without changing the shared classroom servers.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: test environment - an environment used for testing software changes without affecting production systems

Etymology

From the children's "sandbox" (a box of sand for play); used metaphorically in computing for an isolated testing environment.
