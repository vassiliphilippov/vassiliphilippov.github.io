noisemaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɪzˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a device, such as a clapper, bell, or horn, used to make a loud noise at celebrations or parties
device for making loud noise at celebrations

Examples

Everyone received a noisemaker to blow at midnight during the New Year's Eve party.
He bought a plastic noisemaker shaped like a horn for his son's birthday celebration.
The children ran around the room with their colorful noisemakers, laughing and cheering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattle - a device that makes a rattling noise, often used for celebration or entertainment

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈnɔɪzˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzˌmeɪkər / (BrE)
a person or organization that deliberately creates public attention, controversy, or loud publicity (figurative).
person who creates public attention

Examples

The political consultant acted as a relentless noisemaker, keeping scandals in the headlines.
Local activists became noisemakers by organizing loud protests outside the city hall.
Companies sometimes hire a noisemaker to attract media attention during product launches.
