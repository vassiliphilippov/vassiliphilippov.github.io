ritonavir

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɑːnəvɪr / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒnəvɪə / (BrE)
an antiretroviral drug used as a protease inhibitor in the treatment of HIV infection, often in combination with other medications
an HIV protease inhibitor drug
medical

Examples

Patients taking ritonavir should inform their healthcare provider about all other medications they use.
Some coronavirus treatments use ritonavir in conjunction with other antiviral drugs.
The doctor prescribed ritonavir as part of her combination therapy for HIV.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lopinavir - another HIV protease inhibitor often combined with ritonavir

Etymology

From the trade name Norvir, with elements typical of pharmaceutical nomenclature.
