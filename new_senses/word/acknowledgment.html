acknowledgment

1. NOUN

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of recognizing or admitting the existence or truth of something; acceptance of the truth or validity of something
recognition or admission of something

Examples

His acknowledgment of the problem helped start the process of finding a solution.
Receiving an award is a form of acknowledgment from the community.
The team's efforts finally received acknowledgment after years of hard work.

Synset Examples

the partners were delighted with the recognition of their work
she seems to avoid much in the way of recognition or acknowledgement of feminist work prior to her own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recognition - the act of acknowledging or being recognized
  • Antonym: denial - refusal to acknowledge or accept something

Etymology

From Middle English 'aknowlegement', from Old French 'acknowledge', ultimately from Old English 'oncnāwan' (to recognize).

2. NOUN

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
a statement, usually in a book or article, in which the author expresses thanks or gives credit for help or sources of information
statement of thanks or credit in writing

Examples

She wrote a brief acknowledgment to recognize those who helped with her research.
The acknowledgment at the beginning of the book thanked several colleagues for their support.
The author included an acknowledgment section to give credit to all contributors.

Synset Examples

the student's essay failed to list several important citations
the acknowledgments are usually printed at the front of a book
the article includes mention of similar clinical cases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citation - a reference to a published or unpublished source
  • Synonym: reference - mention of a source in a written work

Etymology

From acknowledge + -ment, with acknowledge from Old French 'acknowledger', itself from Anglo-Norman and ultimately from Latin roots.
