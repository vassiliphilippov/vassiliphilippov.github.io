Rome

1. NOUN

/ roʊm / (AmE), / rəʊm / (BrE)
The leadership or authority of the Roman Catholic Church, often referred to by metonymy from its location in the city of Rome.
leadership of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

He traveled to Rome to seek guidance from the Church's highest authorities.
Many important decisions about doctrine come directly from Rome.
The bishop followed the directives issued by Rome regarding church policies.

Etymology

From the Latin 'Roma,' originally the name of the city; extended by metonymy to the Catholic Church headquartered in Rome.

2. PROPN

/ roʊm / (AmE), / rəʊm / (BrE)
The capital and largest city of Italy, located on the Tiber River; historically significant as the center of the Roman Catholic Church and formerly the capital of the Roman Republic and Roman Empire.
capital of Italy, historic city

Examples

In classical antiquity, Rome was the center of a vast empire stretching across Europe, Africa, and Asia.
Many tourists dream of visiting Rome to see its ancient monuments and vibrant culture.
The Vatican City is an independent city-state entirely surrounded by Rome.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Italian - relating to Italy or its people, language, or culture

Etymology

From Latin 'Roma', the name of the city.
