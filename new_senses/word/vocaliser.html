vocaliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.kə.laɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kə.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
an organism, especially an animal, that is capable of producing vocal sounds
organism capable of making vocal sounds

Examples

Scientists studied the bird's ability as a vocaliser to better understand communication.
Some species of lemur are not considered strong vocalisers compared to other primates.
The dolphin is a remarkably skilled vocaliser in the animal kingdom.

Synset Examples

an utterer of foul oaths
is the giraffe a vocalizer?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonvocaliser - an organism incapable of producing vocal sounds

Etymology

From 'vocalise/vocalize' (to produce vocal sounds) + '-er' (one that does).
