Tremella

1. NOUN

/ trəˈmɛl.ə / (AmE), / trɪˈmɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi, characterized by yellowish, gelatinous fruiting bodies with convoluted surfaces resembling a brain.
genus of jelly-like fungi

Examples

In some cultures, Tremella is prized as a culinary delicacy for its texture.
Mycologists studied the growth cycle of Tremella in temperate forests.
The yellow, brain-like appearance of Tremella is unmistakable on decaying wood.

Etymology

From Latin 'tremere' (to tremble), referring to the trembling, jelly-like texture of the fungi.

2. NOUN NEW

/ trəˈmɛl.ə / (AmE), / trɪˈmɛl.ə / (BrE)
(in cosmetics) a moisturizing ingredient obtained from the mushroom Tremella fuciformis, used as a humectant to help skin retain moisture.
hydrating cosmetic ingredient

Examples

Many skin-care brands now add Tremella to serums for lightweight, long-lasting hydration.
The formula uses Tremella to help the skin retain moisture and look plumper.
She bought a night cream with Tremella after reading reviews praising its hydrating effect.
