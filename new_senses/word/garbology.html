garbology

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈbɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈbɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of a society or culture by examining its refuse and waste material
study of society via garbage analysis

Examples

As a pioneer in garbology, he examined landfills for clues about urban life.
Her thesis in garbology helped reveal changing food trends over the decades.
The city hired experts in garbology to learn about residents' consumption habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From English 'garbage' + '-ology' (the study of).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɡɑːrˈbɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈbɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of a society or culture by examining its digital traces and discarded digital artifacts (for example deleted files, metadata, social-media remnants, and server logs).
study of culture via digital traces

Examples

Researchers in digital humanities often practise garbology to study deleted social-media conversations.
After the data breach, their team turned to garbology to understand users' discarded metadata.
A course on garbology taught students how to analyze deleted files and server logs ethically.
