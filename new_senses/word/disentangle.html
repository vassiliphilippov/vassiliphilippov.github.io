to disentangle

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsənˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtæŋɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
to free something or someone from being caught or twisted with something else; to untangle
free from entanglement; untangle

Examples

It took me several minutes to disentangle my headphones from the mess in my bag.
She tried to disentangle herself from the awkward conversation.
The rescue team worked tirelessly to disentangle the whale from the fishing net.

Synset Examples

Can you disentangle the cord?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untangle - to make something that is twisted or knotted straight again

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + entangle, modeled after Old French desentaler.

6. VERB NEW

/ dɪsənˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / dɪsɪnˈtæŋɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
(in machine learning and data analysis) to separate underlying factors of variation in data or learned representations so that they are independent or more easily interpreted.
separate latent factors (ML)

Examples

The research team taught the network to disentangle color and shape features in the images.
After training, the autoencoder disentangled lighting from object identity in its latent variables.
They focused on disentangling the factors of variation to improve model interpretability and control.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to factorize - to break something (e.g., data or matrices) into components or factors
  • Antonym: entangle - to twist together or involve in a complex situation

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of dis- + entangle; used in 21st-century machine-learning literature to mean separating intertwined factors in data.
