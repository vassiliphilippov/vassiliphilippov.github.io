rigidification

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪdʒɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪdʒɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming stiff or rigid, or of making something stiff or rigid
process of becoming rigid or stiff

Examples

Engineers need to prevent excessive rigidification of building materials to allow for flexibility during earthquakes.
Osteoporosis can lead to the rigidification of joints, reducing mobility in elderly patients.
The rigidification of polymers occurs when they cool below a certain temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stiffening - the act or process of making stiff or becoming stiff

Etymology

From 'rigid' (Latin rigidus, stiff) + '-ification' (forming nouns of action or process).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌrɪdʒɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪdʒɪdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in mathematics, especially algebraic geometry) the process of making an object or moduli problem 'rigid' by adding extra structure that reduces or removes automorphisms or nontrivial deformations.
mathematical process removing automorphisms

Examples

In algebraic geometry, the rigidification of the moduli stack removes unwanted automorphisms for classification.
They explained how adding level structure leads to a simpler rigidification of the parameter space.
During the lecture she showed a diagram illustrating the rigidification that eliminated all nontrivial automorphisms.
