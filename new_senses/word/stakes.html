stakes

1. NOUN

/ steɪks / (AmE), / steɪks / (BrE)
the money or valuables risked on the outcome of a bet, contest, or game
money risked in gambling or competition

Examples

He decided to raise the stakes to make the game more exciting.
The stakes were high in last night's poker game.
Whenever you gamble, you should only risk stakes you can afford to lose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - an amount of money risked on the outcome of a possible event
  • Synonym: wager - an amount of money risked in a bet

Etymology

From Old English 'staca' (stake, post); later sense of 'money risked' from the custom of marking bets with a stake or post.

(in cryptocurrency and blockchain contexts) the total amount or value of tokens that users have locked or committed to a network or protocol in order to participate (for example in consensus or governance) and to earn rewards.
tokens locked for staking

Examples

The platform reported rising stakes as more users locked tokens to earn network rewards.
She checked her wallet before increasing her stakes in the proof-of-stake pool.
Large institutional stakes can influence governance votes on many decentralized platforms.
