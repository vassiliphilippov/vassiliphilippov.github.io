reviewer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvjuːər / (AmE), / rɪˈvjuːə / (BrE)
a person who reads and evaluates manuscripts, works, or proposals to judge their suitability for publication or acceptance
person who evaluates manuscripts for publication

Examples

As a respected reviewer in her field, she receives dozens of research papers every year.
Each scientific journal relies on at least one reviewer to assess submitted papers for quality and originality.
The reviewer suggested several improvements before recommending the manuscript for publication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - someone who professionally judges or evaluates works such as books or performances
  • Antonym: author - the person who writes a manuscript; the counterpart to a reviewer in publication

Etymology

From 'review' + '-er', denoting one who reviews.

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈvjuər / (AmE), / rɪˈvjuːə / (BrE)
(in software development) a person who inspects changes to source code (often in a pull request) to check correctness, style, tests, and maintainability.
software code reviewer

Examples

Before merging the feature, the reviewer checked the code for logic errors and adherence to style guides.
Several reviewers left detailed comments on the pull request explaining necessary tests and documentation updates.
As a senior engineer, she often acts as a reviewer during sprint demos to ensure code quality.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer reviewer - a colleague who reviews another person's work (e.g., code or manuscripts) for quality and correctness
  • Synonym: code reviewer - a developer who inspects code changes, typically via pull requests, to find bugs and suggest improvements
