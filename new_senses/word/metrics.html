metrics

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrɪks / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪks / (BrE)
the study of poetic meter and the art of composing verse according to metrical structure
study of poetic meter and versification

Examples

He wrote his dissertation on metrics in Elizabethan poetry.
Her lectures on metrics made poetic structures much more accessible to students.
Understanding metrics is essential for analyzing the formal aspects of verse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosody - the patterns of rhythm and sound in poetry

Etymology

From Greek *metrikē* (art of meter), from *metron* (measure).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmɛtrɪks / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪks / (BrE)
Quantitative measures or indicators (usually numerical) used to assess performance, quality, progress, or impact in business, technology, research, or operations.
quantitative performance measures

Examples

The product team tracks several metrics every week to evaluate user engagement and growth.
Before launching, they set clear metrics to judge whether the new feature improved retention.
Researchers used financial metrics to compare company performance across different industries.
