referral

1. NOUN

/ rəˈfɝː.əl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɜː.rəl / (BrE)
the act of directing someone or something to another person, institution, or service for information, help, or a decision
act of directing someone or something elsewhere

Examples

After my initial interview, I received a referral for a second round with the hiring manager.
Most clinics require a doctor's referral before scheduling an appointment with a consultant.
The patient was given a referral to see a specialist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - a suggestion that someone or something is suitable for a particular purpose or job

Etymology

From refer + -al, first attested in the 1800s.

WordNet ID: oewn-00122724-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɜːrəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɜːrəl / (BrE)
a recommendation to consult a particular professional, organization, or service, especially from a doctor to a specialist
recommendation to see a specialist

Examples

She obtained a referral to a physiotherapist after her injury.
Some specialists require a referral from your primary care physician before accepting new patients.
Your doctor will give you a referral to see a heart specialist if necessary.

Synset Examples

the insurance company says that you need a written referral from your physician before seeing a specialist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - suggestion or endorsement of someone or something
  • Synonym: consultation - a meeting to seek advice or information, especially from a professional

Etymology

From English 'refer' + '-al', formed in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06684785-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rəˈfɜːrəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfɜːrəl / (BrE)
a person who has been sent to a specialist or professional group for consultation or treatment.
person sent to specialist

Examples

As a referral, he was offered immediate access to the specialist services.
Several referrals were waiting in the clinic to see the cardiologist.
The patient is a referral from Dr. Bones, who thought her case needed further investigation.

Synset Examples

the patient is a referral from Dr. Bones

Etymology

From refer + -al, originating from the verb 'refer' (to send or direct someone).
