throttle

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtəl / (BrE)
A device or valve that controls the supply of fuel to an engine, regulating its speed and power.
fuel control valve in an engine

Examples

A stuck throttle can cause the engine to run uncontrollably fast.
She carefully adjusted the throttle to maintain a steady speed.
When you press the accelerator, the throttle opens to allow more fuel into the engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choke - a device for restricting air flow in an engine

Etymology

From Middle English 'throtel', a variant of 'throtel' (throat), relating to the controlling function.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtəl / (BrE)
A pedal or lever in a vehicle used to control the engine's power by regulating the flow of fuel or air.
pedal for controlling engine power

Examples

She pressed the throttle to speed up the car as she merged onto the highway.
The mechanic adjusted the throttle to improve the engine's performance.

Related Terms

From Old English 'throtel', related to 'throat'; technical automotive sense developed in the late 19th century.

6. NOUN NEW

/ ˈθrɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtəl / (BrE)
A software or network mechanism that limits the rate of actions (such as API requests or data transfer) to prevent overload or ensure fair use.
software/network rate limiter

Examples

The cloud provider applied a throttle to prevent any single user from overloading the service.
You may hit the API throttle during peak hours, so space out your requests.
Engineers added a per-minute throttle that limits data transfer during backup windows.
