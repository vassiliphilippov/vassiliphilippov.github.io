spillage

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of a substance that has been accidentally spilled
amount of substance spilled

Examples

The staff quickly cleaned up the spillage in the kitchen to prevent slipping.
There was a considerable spillage of oil along the coastline after the accident.
We measured the spillage to determine how much product was lost during transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to flow or fall out of a container

Etymology

From 'spill' + '-age', modeled after similar nouns indicating action or result.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or instance of accidentally allowing a liquid or substance to escape from its container
accidental escape of a liquid or substance

Examples

Be sure to clean up any spillage promptly to prevent accidents.
The oil spillage in the harbor caused significant environmental damage.
There was a minor spillage of chemicals on the laboratory floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to cause or allow liquid to flow over the edge

Etymology

Formed from 'spill' + suffix '-age', denoting action or process.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈspɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈspɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in information security) the accidental leakage or disclosure of sensitive, confidential, or classified information to unauthorized persons, systems, or channels.
accidental disclosure of sensitive data

Examples

The compliance officer reported a spillage of customer records after the cloud backup error.
Investigators traced the accidental spillage to an insecure API that exposed user emails.
Company policy requires immediate notification when any classified spillage is discovered.
