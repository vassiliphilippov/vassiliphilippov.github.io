defacement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of spoiling or damaging the appearance or surface of something, especially deliberately
act of spoiling appearance of something

Examples

Graffiti led to the defacement of the historic train station walls.
The city passed strict laws against the defacement of public property.
The museum took measures to prevent the defacement of its ancient statues.

Synset Examples

the defacement of an Italian mosaic during the Turkish invasion
he objected to the dam's massive disfigurement of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disfigurement - the act of spoiling the appearance of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'defacemente', from Old French 'defacement', from 'defacer' (to deface).

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
(in computing) the unauthorized alteration of a website or web page, typically to display vandalistic, political, or malicious content.
unauthorized change to a website

Examples

Hackers carried out defacement of several municipal websites during the protest.
After the attack, the university reported the defacement and began restoring the affected web pages.
Visitors noticed a crude political message in the site's header, evidence of the defacement.

Synset Examples

Hackers carried out defacement of several municipal websites during the protest.
After the attack, the university reported the defacement and began restoring the affected web pages.
Visitors noticed a crude political message in the site's header, evidence of the defacement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vandalism - deliberate damage or spoiling of property or content for malicious or political reasons
  • Synonym: hacking - unauthorized access to computer systems; often associated with attacks that may include website defacement

Etymology

From Middle English 'defacemente', from Old French 'defacement' (from 'defacer', to deface); now used metaphorically in computing for damaging a site's appearance.
