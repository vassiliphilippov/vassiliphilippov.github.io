hydrated

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (BrE)
containing water that is chemically combined, especially as water of crystallization in a compound
containing chemically combined water

Examples

Many salts are found in a hydrated form, such as copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate.
The mineral is hydrated, meaning it contains water molecules within its crystal structure.
When hydrated, the compound exhibits different color and solubility compared to its anhydrous state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anhydrous - not containing water; especially not chemically combined with water

Etymology

From the verb 'hydrate', based on Greek hydor (water).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(in web development) (of server-rendered HTML or a component) enhanced by client-side JavaScript so it is interactive.
made interactive by client JavaScript

Examples

After the JavaScript ran, the previously static page was fully hydrated and responded to clicks.
We deploy server-rendered routes so their HTML arrives already hydrated for faster perceived performance.
Developers check whether the component is hydrated before attaching event listeners in production.

Synset Examples
