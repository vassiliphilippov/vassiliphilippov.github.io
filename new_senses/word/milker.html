milker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlkər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlkə / (BrE)
a cow that is kept and bred for the purpose of producing milk
a cow kept for milk production

Examples

On the farm, each milker produces about twenty liters of milk per day.
The vet checked the health of the oldest milker in the herd.
They decided to buy several new milkers to increase their dairy production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dairy cow - a cow bred for milk rather than meat
  • Antonym: beef cattle - cattle bred primarily for meat production rather than milk

Etymology

From 'milk' + '-er', indicating an agent or thing that produces or provides milk.
