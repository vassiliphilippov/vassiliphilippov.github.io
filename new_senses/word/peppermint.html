peppermint

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpəmɪnt / (BrE)
A species of red gum tree native to Tasmania, noted for its aromatic leaves; also known as Tasmanian peppermint gum.
red gum tree of Tasmania

Examples

Botanists studied how the oils from the peppermint could be used for medicinal purposes.
Many native birds nest in the branches of the peppermint throughout the year.
The peppermint is a distinctive part of Tasmania's landscape, with its tall trunk and fragrant leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpəmɪnt / (BrE)
an aromatic herb with hairy leaves and small purple or white flowers, known for its pungent oil used as a flavoring
aromatic herb producing a pungent oil

Examples

She brewed a cup of peppermint tea to soothe her stomach.
The bakery adds a hint of peppermint to its holiday chocolates.
You can grow peppermint in your garden for both culinary and medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spearmint - a similar mint plant with a milder flavor

Etymology

From Latin 'piperita', meaning 'peppered', referring to the plant's pungency.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpərˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛpəmɪnt / (BrE)
a sweet or confection that is flavored with peppermint oil, typically hard and often shaped into disks or sticks
candy flavored with peppermint oil

Examples

After dinner, I like to have a peppermint to freshen my breath.
She offered me a peppermint from the bowl on her desk.
The holiday cookies were topped with crushed peppermint for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - a confection flavored with mint or peppermint

Etymology

From 'pepper' and 'mint', originally denoting the plant.
