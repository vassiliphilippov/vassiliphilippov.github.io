collectibility

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kəˌlɛktɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being likely to be collected or paid, especially in reference to money owed.
likelihood of payment being collected

Examples

Auditors must assess the collectibility of accounts receivable before finalizing the financial statements.
Concerns about the collectibility of the debt led the company to increase its bad debt reserves.
The collectibility of revenues can significantly affect a business’s reported earnings.

Synset Examples

However, when uncertainty arises about the collectibility of revenue already recognised, the uncollectible amount is recognised as expense (bad debt).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectible - an item or sum capable of being collected

Etymology

From 'collect' + '-ibility', patterned after 'collectible'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ k\u0259\u02ccl\u025bkt\u0259\u02c8b\u026al\u026ati / (AmE), / k\u0259\u02ccl\u025bkt\u026a\u02c8b\u026al\u026ati / (BrE)
The quality of an object that makes it desirable to collectors, often determined by rarity, condition, provenance, or historical interest.
desirability to collectors

Examples

The limited-edition vinyl's collectibility increased after the band announced they would never press it again.
Experts said the toy's original box significantly improved its collectibility among serious collectors.
Condition, rarity, and provenance all affect an item's collectibility on the resale market.
