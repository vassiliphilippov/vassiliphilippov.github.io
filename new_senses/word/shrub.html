shrub

1. NOUN

/ ʃrʌb / (AmE), / ʃrʌb / (BrE)
a low-growing woody plant, usually with several stems arising from the base, that is smaller than a tree
a low woody plant with several stems

Examples

A rose is classified as a shrub because it has several woody stems.
Some shrubs produce colorful flowers in the spring.
They planted a shrub in the front yard to add some greenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush - a low dense plant, often used as a synonym for shrub
  • Degree Higher: tree - a perennial plant with a single main stem or trunk, larger than a shrub

Etymology

From Old English 'scrybb', related to Low German 'Schrubbe' and Old Norse 'skrobb' meaning 'scrub' or 'brush'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ʃrʌb / (AmE), / ʃrʌb / (BrE)
a sweetened, vinegar-based beverage made by infusing fruit (or fruit juice) with sugar and vinegar; used as a refreshing drink or a cocktail mixer.
sweetened fruit-and-vinegar drink

Examples

The bartender served a bright raspberry shrub to brighten the cocktail.
She made a jar of apple shrub by steeping fruit with sugar and vinegar.
Cocktail recipes often call for a tablespoon of shrub to add sweet-tart flavor.
