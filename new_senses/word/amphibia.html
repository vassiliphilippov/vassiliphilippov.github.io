Amphibia

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪbiə / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbɪə / (BrE)
A class of cold-blooded vertebrate animals that typically live both on land and in water at different stages of life, such as frogs, toads, newts, salamanders, and caecilians.
class of vertebrates including frogs and salamanders

Examples

Many members of the Amphibia have moist skin that assists in respiration.
Researchers recently discovered a new species belonging to the Amphibia in South America.
The Amphibia are unique because they require water for reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphibian - a member of the class Amphibia

Etymology

From Latin 'amphibia', from Greek 'amphibios' meaning 'living a double life'.
