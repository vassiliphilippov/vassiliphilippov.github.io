charr

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑr / (AmE), / tʃɑː / (BrE)
any of several small trout-like freshwater fish of the genus Salvelinus, found mainly in cold northern waters
a trout-like fish of the genus Salvelinus

Examples

Arctic charr are adapted to the cold waters of northern lakes.
Several species of charr inhabit the streams of Scandinavia.
The chef prepared smoked charr for the evening menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a freshwater fish related to salmon

Etymology

From Old Norse 'karfi', meaning a type of fish.
