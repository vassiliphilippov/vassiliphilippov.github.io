nosebleed

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊzˌbliːd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊzˌbliːd / (BrE)
An instance of bleeding from the nose.
bleeding from the nose

Examples

After running in the dry air, she suddenly got a nosebleed during class.
He suffered a nosebleed after being hit on the face with a ball.
If you have a nosebleed, lean forward and pinch your nose gently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epistaxis - medical term for nosebleed

(informal) the highest, most distant seats in a stadium, arena, or theatre, typically far from the stage or playing area and offering a poor or very distant view.
very high, distant seats
informal

Examples

From the nosebleed seats, we could barely see the performers on stage.
They paid less for nosebleed tickets, but the view was too distant to enjoy the game.
After buying cheap nosebleed seats, she wished they had splurged for lower-level tickets.
