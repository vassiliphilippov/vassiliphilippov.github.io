poi

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪ / (AmE), / pɔɪ / (BrE)
A Hawaiian food made by pounding taro root into a smooth paste, often allowed to ferment slightly.
Hawaiian dish of pounded taro root

Examples

Many locals eat poi every day, appreciating its nutritional value and unique flavor.
She served traditional Hawaiian poi at the luau, alongside roast pork and fresh fruit.
The texture of poi can vary from thick to runny, depending on personal preference.

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'poi' (mashed, as of taro).

2. NOUN NEW

/ piː.oʊ.aɪ / (AmE), / piː.əʊ.aɪ / (BrE)
(abbr., in mapping and navigation) a specific location, site, or landmark marked on a map or in an app as being of interest to users.
point of interest (map marker)

Examples

The travel app highlights nearby POIs like museums, parks, and popular restaurants for tourists.
She added her favorite café as a POI on the map before the road trip.
The GPS searches for nearby POIs to show on-screen directions and local information.
