to repaint

1. VERB

/ riˈpeɪnt / (AmE), / riːˈpeɪnt / (BrE)
to paint something again, usually to refresh or change its appearance
paint again

Examples

After years of weathering, the owner decided to repaint the old fence to make it look new.
She repainted the kitchen walls a cheerful yellow to brighten up the space.
The museum regularly repaints its galleries to keep them bright and inviting for visitors.

Synset Examples

He repainted the wall after the child smeared it with tomato sauce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paint - to cover a surface with paint

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'paint', from Old French 'peint', past participle of 'peindre'.

3. VERB NEW

/ riˈpeɪnt / (AmE), / riˈpeɪnt / (BrE)
(in computing) to cause a graphical user‑interface component or a region of the screen to be redrawn, typically after its contents change.
redraw a UI component

Examples

The GUI framework will repaint the panel after the new data arrives and the model updates.
When the window was resized, the browser repainted the affected region to show the updated layout.
Efficient graphics code avoids unnecessary repainting by only invalidating small areas that changed.

Synset Examples

The GUI framework will repaint the panel after the new data arrives and the model updates.

Efficient graphics code avoids unnecessary repainting by only invalidating small areas that changed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redraw - to draw the contents of a screen area or UI element again
  • Synonym: refresh - to update or reload a display or its data; often used for UI updates

Etymology

From re- (again) + paint; used figuratively in computing to mean ‘draw again’.
