dam

1. NOUN

/ ˈdækəˌmɛtər / (AmE), / ˈdiːkəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to ten meters
metric unit of length, ten meters

Examples

A dam is rarely used in everyday conversation, but it appears in specialized contexts.
He marked out a distance of three dams for the experiment.
The field was exactly one dam in length, making it easy to measure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decameter - a metric unit of length equal to ten meters (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From French décamètre, from Greek deka (ten) + French mètre (meter).

2. NOUN

/ dæm / (AmE), / dæm / (BrE)
the female parent of an animal, especially when referring to domestic livestock
female parent of an animal

Examples

Records showed that each calf's dam was healthy and well cared for.
The dam of the foal was a prize-winning mare.
The breeder tracked the lineage by noting both the sire and the dam.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sire - the male parent of an animal, especially livestock

Etymology

From Middle English 'dam' (mother, animal parent), from Old French 'dam', from Latin 'domina' (lady, mistress).

3. VERB

/ dæm / (AmE), / dæm / (BrE)
to obstruct or block the flow of water or another substance with a dam or similar barrier
block water or flow with a barrier

Examples

A family of beavers worked together to dam the creek with branches and mud.
Rising water levels forced engineers to dam the river to prevent flooding.
They plan to dam the stream temporarily while repairs are made downstream.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to stop movement or progress
  • Antonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction

Etymology

From Middle English 'dam', from Middle Dutch 'dam' (barrier), related to blocking water.

4. NOUN

/ dæm / (AmE), / dæm / (BrE)
A barrier built across a river or stream to hold back water, control its flow, or create a reservoir.
barrier to hold back water

Examples

Farmers rely on the dam to irrigate their fields during the dry season.
Heavy rainfall caused water to overflow the dam and flood the surrounding areas.
The construction of the dam provided electricity and water to the nearby cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levee - an embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
  • Synonym: embankment - a wall or bank of earth or stone built to prevent a river flooding an area
  • Synonym: weir - a small dam built across a river to raise the level of water upstream

Etymology

From Middle English 'dam', from Middle Dutch 'dam' (barrier, dike, obstruction).
