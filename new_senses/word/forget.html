to forget

1. VERB

/ fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
to be unable to remember something
be unable to remember

Examples

He sometimes forgets important details when he's stressed.
I often forget where I left my keys in the morning.
She tried not to forget her friend's birthday, but it slipped her mind.

Synset Examples

I'm drawing a blank
You are blocking the name of your first wife!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to remember - to keep something in mind or recall it
  • Antonym: recall - to bring a fact or event back into one's mind

Etymology

From Old English *forgietan* (to forget, neglect), related to Old High German *firgezzan*.
