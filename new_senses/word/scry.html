to scry

1. VERB

/ skraɪ / (AmE), / skraɪ / (BrE)
to predict the future or discover hidden knowledge by gazing into a crystal or other reflective surface
predict by gazing into crystals

Examples

In old legends, wizards would scry into crystal balls to uncover hidden truths.
She claimed she could scry her future using a bowl of water.
The fortune teller scried for answers before giving her prediction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - to discover or foretell by supernatural means
  • Synonym: foretell - to tell what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Old English *scrīan* (to reveal, make manifest); influenced by 'descry'.

2. VERB NEW

/ skraɪ / (AmE), / skraɪ / (BrE)
(in card games) to look at the top one or more cards of your deck and decide whether to keep them on top, put them on the bottom, or otherwise reorder them according to the game's rules.
look at top cards in a deck (card games)

Examples

In the tournament, she decided to scry the top two cards before drawing her next card.
He scried the top card and placed it on the bottom of his deck to avoid a bad draw.
During cleanup, players are scrying to improve their chances by arranging their next draws carefully.

Synset Examples
