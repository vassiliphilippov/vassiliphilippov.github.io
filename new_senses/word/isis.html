Isis

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian goddess associated with fertility, motherhood, magic, and the throne; sister and wife of Osiris and mother of Horus.
Egyptian goddess of fertility and motherhood

Examples

Priests performed rituals dedicated to Isis to ensure a fruitful harvest.
Statues of Isis often depict her holding her infant son Horus.
The ancient Egyptians worshipped Isis as the ideal mother and wife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Osiris - Egyptian god of the afterlife, husband of Isis
  • Synonym: Horus - Egyptian god, son of Isis and Osiris

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἶσις (Îsis), from Egyptian Ēset, goddess name.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ˈaɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
A name used in English for the extremist militant group that calls itself the Islamic State (commonly rendered ISIS or ISIL), active in Iraq and Syria and known for violent insurgency and terrorism.
extremist militant group (Islamic State)

Examples

Security analysts linked Isis to a series of coordinated attacks across the region last year.
She studied the rise of Isis for her university thesis on international terrorism.
The government announced new measures to prevent the spread of Isis propaganda online.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic State (ISIL) - Alternative name/acronym for the same extremist organization.
  • Synonym: terrorist organisation - An organization that uses violence and intimidation in pursuit of political aims; a descriptive category often applied to Isis.

Etymology

From the modern English acronym ISIS, from "Islamic State of Iraq and al‑Sham" (often rendered "Syria"); used in English from the 2010s to refer to the organization.
