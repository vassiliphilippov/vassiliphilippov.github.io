PIM

1. NOUN

/ pi aɪ ɛm / (AmE), / piː aɪ em / (BrE)
A type of application software that serves as a personal organizer by helping users manage and track personal information such as contacts, schedules, and notes.
software for managing personal information

Examples

He exported all his calendar events from his old PIM to the new one.
Many professionals rely on a PIM to keep track of appointments and contacts.
The introduction of smartphone apps has changed the way people use a PIM.

Synset Examples

As an information management tool, a PIM tool's purpose is to facilitate the recording, tracking, and management of certain types of ‘personal information’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal organizer - a device or software used for scheduling and managing personal information

Etymology

Acronym for Personal Information Manager.

2. NOUN NEW

/ pi aɪ ɛm / (AmE), / piː aɪ em / (BrE)
Software or a system used by retailers and manufacturers to centralize, enrich, manage, and distribute product data (such as descriptions, attributes, images, and identifiers) across channels.
product data management system

Examples

The retailer implemented a PIM to centralize and synchronize product data across channels.
Our company uses a cloud-based PIM to manage product descriptions, prices, and supplier information.
Before launch, the team enriched every SKU in the PIM to ensure consistent listings worldwide.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: product information management - the practice and systems for collecting, formatting, and distributing product data for commerce and marketing
  • Synonym: master data management - the processes and tools that ensure a single, consistent source of key business data across systems
