splat

1. NOUN

/ splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
a thin, flat piece of wood forming the central support in the backrest of a straight chair
vertical strip in a chair backrest

Examples

A decorative splat can add elegance to even a simple wooden chair.
Craftsmen paid close attention to the design of the splat in Georgian chairs.
The antique chair was missing its splat, leaving the back unsteady.

Etymology

Likely from a variant of 'slat' meaning a thin strip of wood.

2. VERB

/ splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
to make a flat, soft, and often wet noise when hitting a surface and flattening on impact
to hit and flatten on impact, making a wet noise

Examples

The paintball splatted against the window, leaving a colorful smear.
Water balloons often splat on the pavement, bursting as they hit the ground.
