recommendation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛk.ə.mɛn.dəˈʃɛn / (AmE), / ˌrɛk.ə.mɛn.dəˈʃən / (BrE)
a suggestion or proposal about the best course of action to take, especially one offered officially or formally
a suggested course of action

Examples

Following your doctor's recommendation, she started a new exercise program.
He ignored my recommendation and chose a different approach.
The committee's recommendation was to approve the project with minor changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: advice - guidance or recommendations concerning prudent action

Etymology

From Latin 'recommendatio', from 're-' (again) + 'commendare' (to commend).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkəmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a statement or suggestion expressing approval of someone or something as suitable or worthy for a particular purpose
statement suggesting approval or suitability

Examples

Can you give me a recommendation for a good Italian restaurant nearby?
Her boss wrote a glowing recommendation for her job application.
The committee followed the recommendation to hire the most experienced candidate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - an expression of support or approval
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or negative judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'recommendatio', from 're-' (again) + 'commendare' (to commend).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛk.əˌmɛn.dəˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛk.ə.mɛn.dəˈʃən / (BrE)
a quality or characteristic that gives someone acceptance or makes them favorably received
quality making acceptance likely

Examples

A friendly attitude is always a good recommendation when meeting new people.
Her kindness is a strong recommendation for the job.
His patience proved to be a valuable recommendation during the interview process.
