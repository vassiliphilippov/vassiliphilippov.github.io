chipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of breaking off small pieces from something; or removing small fragments by chipping
the act of chipping

Examples

Repeated chipping of the paint weakened the surface of the old door.
The sculptor spent hours on careful chipping to reveal the figure hidden in the stone.
There was a lot of chipping during the renovation, leaving debris everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chip - to break off a small piece from something

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'chip' + the suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtʃɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of implanting a small identification microchip in an animal or object, typically to enable permanent identification and registration.
implanting an identification microchip

Examples

The veterinary clinic offers chipping services to help owners identify lost pets quickly.
After the adoption, they paid for chipping and registered the dog's details with the national database.
Wildlife researchers use chipping to track animal movements and study migration patterns over years.
