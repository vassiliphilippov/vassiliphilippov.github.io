mail

1. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
the bags of letters and packages that are carried and delivered by the postal service
letters and packages transported by post

Examples

He sorted through the mail to find the package he was expecting.
The mail was delivered early this morning.
The company receives a large amount of mail every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - letters and parcels sent or delivered by the postal service (British English)

Etymology

From Middle English *male* (bag, pouch for letters), from Old French *male* (bag, satchel).

3. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
(historical) Flexible armor made of interlinked metal rings, worn as a protective garment.
armor of interlinked metal rings
archaic

Examples

During the Middle Ages, mail was commonly used to protect soldiers in battle.
Fragments of mail were discovered at the archaeological site, dating to the 12th century.
The knight wore a suit of mail that glittered in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain mail - armor of linked metal rings
  • Antonym: plate armor - solid metal protective armor

Etymology

From Old French 'maille' (mesh), from Latin 'macula' (mesh, net).
