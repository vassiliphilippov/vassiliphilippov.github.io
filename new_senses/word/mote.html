mote

1. NOUN

/ moʊt / (AmE), / məʊt / (BrE)
a tiny particle or speck of a substance; a very small piece of anything
tiny particle or speck

Examples

A single mote of dust floated in the beam of sunlight.
He could see not even a mote of dirt on the spotless table.
She watched a mote drift down and disappear against the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a very small spot or particle
  • Synonym: particle - a minute portion of matter

Etymology

From Old English *mote* (speck of dust), related to Middle Dutch *mot* (speck, particle).

2. NOUN NEW

/ moʊt / (AmE), / məʊt / (BrE)
(in computing and engineering) a small, low-power wireless sensor node used in a distributed network to collect and transmit environmental or system data.
small wireless sensor node

Examples

Researchers deployed a mote to monitor soil moisture across the agricultural test field.
Each mote sent temperature and humidity readings to the central server every ten minutes.
Battery life was critical, so the team replaced the motes after three months of sensing.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensor node - a device in a sensor network that collects data from its environment and communicates that data
  • Synonym: wireless sensor - a compact sensing device that transmits measurements wirelessly, often as part of a distributed network

Etymology

From 'mote' meaning a tiny speck; adopted metaphorically in computing for very small sensor devices (late 20th century).
