free-range

1. ADJ

/ friːˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / friːˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
(of livestock or domestic poultry) allowed to roam freely and forage for food, rather than being confined in an enclosure or feedlot.
allowed to roam and forage freely (livestock/poultry)

Examples

Consumers often prefer free-range eggs because they believe the chickens are treated more humanely.
He started a business raising free-range turkeys on his family farm.
The restaurant serves only free-range chicken and locally sourced vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cage-free - (of poultry, particularly laying hens) not kept in cages but indoors without outdoor access
  • Antonym: factory-farmed - raised in intensive farming conditions with little or no access to the outdoors

Etymology

From 'free' + 'range', indicating animals are free to range (roam).

2. ADJ NEW

/ friːˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / friːˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
(of a style of parenting) giving children substantial freedom to explore and make choices, with relatively little direct adult supervision.
parenting style granting children freedom
informal

Examples

Some families have adopted free-range parenting to encourage outdoor exploration and personal responsibility.
Teachers often praise children from free-range homes for their independence and problem-solving skills.
Critics argue that free-range parenting can neglect safety, while supporters stress autonomy and resilience.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permissive parenting - a parenting style characterized by low demands and high responsiveness, giving children considerable freedom
  • Antonym: helicopter parenting - an overprotective parenting style involving excessive monitoring and intervention

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the agricultural adjective 'free-range' (animals allowed to roam), applied to parenting.
