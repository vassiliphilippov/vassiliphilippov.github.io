to sanitise

1. VERB

/ ˈsænətaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsænɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something clean and free from bacteria or germs by cleaning or sterilizing
make clean or sterile
british

Examples

All surfaces should be sanitised before use in a commercial kitchen.
Please sanitise your hands before entering the ward.
The hospital staff sanitised the equipment thoroughly after each procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sanitize - to make clean and free from germs (US spelling)

Etymology

From sanitize (US spelling), from Latin 'sanitas' (health).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01247616-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsænəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsænɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something less offensive or more acceptable by removing objectionable or sensitive features
make acceptable by removing objectionable parts

Examples

Officials attempted to sanitise history by downplaying uncomfortable events.
The company tried to sanitise its report by omitting details about the accident.
The publisher chose to sanitise the novel for younger readers.

Synset Examples

sanitize a document before releasing it to the press
sanitize history
sanitize the language in a book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - to remove or suppress parts considered objectionable
  • Synonym: expurgate - to remove words or passages considered offensive from a text
  • Synonym: bowdlerize - to remove material that is considered improper or offensive from a text

Etymology

From Latin 'sanitas' (health), via French 'sanitiser' and modeled after 'sanitize'.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8s\u00e6n\u0259ta\u026az / (AmE), / \u02c8s\u00e6n\u026at\u028c\u026az / (BrE)
(in computing) to remove, escape, or neutralize unsafe or unwanted content in user input or data so it cannot cause security problems (for example, preventing cross-site scripting).
clean user input for security
british

Examples

The framework will automatically sanitise user-submitted comments before storing them.
After the breach, the team sanitised log files to remove malicious payloads and secrets.
They are sanitising all uploaded files to block scripts that could enable XSS attacks.
