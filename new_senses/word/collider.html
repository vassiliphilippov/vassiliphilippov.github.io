collider

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlaɪdər / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪdə / (BrE)
a type of particle accelerator in which two beams of particles are made to collide head-on at high speeds
particle accelerator for colliding beams

Examples

A new collider is being planned to exceed the energy of current particle accelerators.
Scientists use a collider to study the fundamental particles of the universe.
The Large Hadron Collider is one of the most sophisticated scientific instruments ever built.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accelerator - a machine that increases the speed of particles

Etymology

From collide + -er, indicating a device that causes particles to collide.

2. NOUN NEW

/ kəˈlaɪdər / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪdər / (BrE)
(in game development) a software component or shape attached to an object that defines its physical boundary used by the physics engine for collision detection and response.
game-engine collision component

Examples

In Unity, attach a box collider to the player to enable physics collisions during gameplay.
The game's performance dropped because dozens of complex colliders were active on every frame.
To fix jitter, the developer replaced the mesh with a simpler collider shape for stability.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: collision detection - the process or system that determines when two objects in a simulation intersect or contact
