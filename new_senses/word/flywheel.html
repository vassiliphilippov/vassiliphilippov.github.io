flywheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌwil / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌwiːl / (BrE)
a heavy wheel attached to a machine that stores rotational kinetic energy and helps maintain a steady speed of operation, especially in engines
rotating energy-storing wheel in machines

Examples

A damaged flywheel can cause irregular vibrations in industrial machinery.
The engine's flywheel helps keep the vehicle running smoothly even at low speeds.
The technician examined the flywheel to ensure it was properly balanced before restarting the system.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈflaɪˌwɪl / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌwiːl / (BrE)
a self-reinforcing process, mechanism, or set of activities that builds momentum and drives sustained growth or performance for a product, company, or platform.
self-reinforcing growth mechanism (business)

Examples

The startup built a flywheel of user-generated content that fuels sustained growth.
By improving onboarding, they created a flywheel where happy customers bring in new users.
Successful platforms often rely on multiple small flywheels that together increase user engagement.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the mechanical term 'flywheel' (a heavy rotating wheel that stores energy), applied in business and product strategy.
