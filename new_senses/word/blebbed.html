blebbed

1. ADJ

/ blɛbd / (AmE), / blɛbd / (BrE)
(of glass or quartzite) marred by small bubbles or small particles of foreign material.
marred by small bubbles or particles (esp. in glass or quartzite)

Examples

Collectors often reject blebbed glass marbles because of their visible flaws.
The quartzite surface appeared blebbed after exposure to intense heat.
The scientist noted that the sample was blebbed due to imperfections in the cooling process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blemished - marked by imperfections or defects

Etymology

From "bleb" (a small blister or bubble) + -ed.

2. ADJ NEW

/ blɛbd / (AmE), / blɛbd / (BrE)
(in biology/medicine) having blebs; showing small, rounded protrusions or blisters on a cell membrane, often seen during apoptosis or other membrane‑remodelling processes.
having membrane blebs (in cells)
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the damaged cancer cells looked blebbed after exposure to the drug.
During apoptosis, many neurons become blebbed, showing small membrane bulges before breaking apart.
The lab report described the plate of treated cells as severely blebbed compared with controls.
