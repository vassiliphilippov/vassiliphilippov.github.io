capsule

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.səl / (AmE), / ˈkæp.sjuːl / (BrE)
a small container, often cylindrical and usually used to hold substances such as medicine or scientific samples
small container, often cylindrical

Examples

She opened the capsule to take her daily vitamins.
The scientists stored each sample in a sterile capsule for analysis.
The time capsule was buried in the schoolyard to be opened in fifty years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: container - an object used for holding or transporting something

Etymology

From Latin 'capsula', diminutive of 'capsa' (box).
