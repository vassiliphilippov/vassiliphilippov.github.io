pm

1. ADV

/ piː ˈɛm / (AmE), / piː ˈɛm / (BrE)
used to indicate the time between noon and midnight in the 12-hour clock system.
between noon and midnight (time)

Examples

I usually have dinner around 7 pm every evening.
The meeting will start at 3 pm and end by 5 pm.
The store closes at 10 pm on weekdays.

Synset Examples

let's meet at 8 P.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: am - used to indicate the time between midnight and noon

Etymology

From Latin 'post meridiem' meaning 'after midday.'

2. NOUN NEW

/ piː ˈɛm / (AmE), / piː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a private message sent directly to another user on an online platform (such as social media, forums, or chat), not visible to the public.
private/direct message
informal

Examples

She asked him to send a pm with the meeting details.
After the public post, several followers sent the influencer a private pm with questions.
If you prefer, I can forward the document in a secure pm instead of email.

Synset Examples

She asked him to send a pm with the meeting details.
After the public post, several followers sent the influencer a private pm with questions.
If you prefer, I can forward the document in a secure pm instead of email.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct message - a private message sent directly to another user on social platforms (often abbreviated 'DM').
  • Antonym: public post - a message or post visible to all users or followers; the opposite of a private message.

Etymology

Abbreviation of "private message."
