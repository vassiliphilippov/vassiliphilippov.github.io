BC

1. ADV

/ bi si / (AmE), / biː siː / (BrE)
Used after a year to indicate a date before the birth of Christ, according to the traditional Western calendar.
indicates years before the birth of Christ

Examples

Many famous Greek philosophers lived during the 5th century BC.
The city of Rome was founded in 753 BC according to legend.
The wall was built around 200 BC to protect the settlement.

Synset Examples

in 200 BC

Related Terms

  • Antonym: AD - used for years after the birth of Christ ('Anno Domini')

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Before Christ'.

2. ADV NEW

/ bi si / (AmE), / biː siː / (BrE)
(informal) Used to mark time as occurring before the COVID-19 pandemic; 'Before COVID-19'.
before COVID-19 (informal)
informal

Examples

Many people say life felt easier BC, before lockdowns and social distancing became normal.
Companies compare sales figures from BC years to see how the pandemic changed consumer behavior.
People jokingly refer to pre-pandemic travel as the BC era when flights were easier.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pre-pandemic - the period of time before the COVID-19 pandemic
  • Antonym: post-pandemic - the period after the main COVID-19 pandemic disruptions

Etymology

Modeled on the established abbreviation 'BC' ('Before Christ'); here reinterpreted as 'Before COVID-19'.
