daemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈdiː.mən / (BrE)
a being who is part mortal and part god, often found in ancient mythology and literature
part-mortal, part-god being

Examples

In Greek mythology, a daemon could act as a guardian spirit for individuals or places.
Some ancient cultures believed a daemon could influence a person's fate or character.
The hero of the story was guided by a wise daemon who was part god, part human.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demigod - a being with one divine and one mortal parent

Etymology

From Greek *daimōn* (divine power, guiding spirit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːmən / (AmE), / ˈdiːmən / (BrE)
An evil supernatural being or spirit; often associated with mischief or harm in mythology and folklore.
evil supernatural being or spirit
archaic

Examples

In the story, the hero must confront the daemon terrorizing his dreams.
Legends tell of a daemon haunting the ruined castle at the edge of the village.
The ancient Greeks believed that a daemon could influence a person's fate, for good or ill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demon - an evil spirit or devil
  • Antonym: angel - a benevolent supernatural being

Etymology

From Greek daimōn (spirit, supernatural being), later Latin daemon, originally neutral or ambivalent in nature.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdiːmən / (AmE), / ˈdiːmən / (BrE)
(computing) a background program or autonomous software agent that runs without direct user interaction to provide services, coordinate tasks, or respond to events; (also in fiction/speculative contexts) an autonomous digital actor that controls systems or networks.
background program or autonomous software agent

Examples

The system runs several daemons to handle printing, networking, and scheduled tasks automatically.
In the novel, a hidden daemon quietly manipulates traffic signals to create chaos citywide.
Security researchers described the autonomous daemon that coordinates sensor data across thousands of devices.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background process - a program that runs behind the scenes and performs tasks without direct user interaction
  • Synonym: agent - a software entity that performs actions on behalf of users or other programs

Etymology

From Greek daimōn (spirit); term adopted in computing (1960s, MIT) for background processes, inspired by Maxwell's demon and the classical meaning of a guiding spirit.
