perk

1. VERB

/ pɝk / (AmE), / pɜːk / (BrE)
(of a person) to become or appear more lively, energetic, or cheerful, especially after feeling tired or low.
become livelier or more energetic

Examples

After a cup of coffee, she began to perk and participate in the meeting.
He didn't perk until he heard the good news from his doctor.
The children started to perk once the music began playing at the party.

Synset Examples

I picked up after a nap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perk up - to become more lively or cheerful
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00023466-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ pɝk / (AmE), / pɜːk / (BrE)
an incidental benefit or privilege given in addition to salary for certain types of employment
extra work benefit or privilege

Examples

Employees enjoy several perks including free lunches and extra vacation days.
The company gym is just one perk of working here.
Working from home is a popular perk offered by many tech companies.

Synset Examples

a limousine is one of the fringe benefits of the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perquisite - a benefit or privilege accompanying a job; a formal synonym
  • Synonym: fringe benefit - an additional benefit supplementing salary

Etymology

Shortened form of 'perquisite', from Latin 'perquisitum' (thing acquired).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ pɝːk / (AmE), / pɜːk / (BrE)
an extra benefit or advantage, especially one given to someone because of their job or position, in addition to pay or usual benefits
extra benefit from a job or status

Examples

Flexible working hours are a valuable perk for many employees.
He enjoys the perks that come with being a company executive.
One of the best perks of this job is getting free tickets to shows.

Synset Examples

Being a cyberfreak has its perks and drawbacks.
Being a rich kid has its perks and pressures.
He enjoys the perks of being a sublebrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe benefit - an additional benefit supplementing an employee's salary
  • Antonym: drawback - a disadvantage or negative feature

Etymology

Shortened form of 'perquisite,' from Latin 'perquisitum' (something acquired).

4. NOUN NEW

/ pɝk / (AmE), / pɜːk / (BrE)
(in gaming) a bonus ability or advantage granted to a player character or avatar, often passive or unlocked through progression.
gaming bonus ability

Examples

She chose the perk that reduced fall damage before entering the dungeon.
Many multiplayer shooters balance weapons by limiting certain powerful perks behind progression systems.
You can unlock a new perk every few levels to customize your character's playstyle.

Synset Examples
