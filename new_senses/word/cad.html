CAD

1. NOUN

/ siː eɪ ˈdiː / (AmE), / siː eɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
software used for computer-aided design, especially to create precise drawings in fields such as engineering and architecture.
computer-aided design software
american

Examples

Learning CAD is essential for careers in modern engineering and product design.
Many architects rely on CAD to create detailed building plans.
With CAD software, designers can quickly modify and optimize their models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: computer-aided design - the process or technique of using computer systems to assist in the creation, modification, analysis, or optimization of a design

Etymology

Acronym of computer-aided design.

2. NOUN NEW

/ siː eɪ ˈdiː / (AmE), / siː eɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
(in medicine) a computer-aided detection or diagnostic system that assists clinicians by automatically identifying or highlighting possible abnormalities on medical images.
computer-aided detection/diagnosis (medical imaging)
medical

Examples

Radiologists used the hospital's new CAD to highlight possible tumors on routine chest X‑rays.
After reviewing the scan, the doctor noted that the CAD had missed a small lesion near the liver.
Clinical trials show combining radiologist expertise with CAD improves sensitivity for detecting early-stage breast cancer.
