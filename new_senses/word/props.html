props

1. NOUN

/ prɑps / (AmE), / prɒps / (BrE)
(informal) Respect or proper acknowledgment, often used to express approval or admiration for someone's actions or achievements.
(informal) respect or acknowledgment
informal slang

Examples

Much props to the organizers for making the event a success.
She always gives props to her team for their hard work.
You have to give him props for coming up with such a creative idea.

Synset Examples

I have to give my props to the governor for the way he handled the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - admiration for someone for their abilities or qualities
  • Synonym: credit - recognition or acknowledgment for achievement

Etymology

Shortening of 'proper respect', popularized in African American Vernacular English and hip-hop culture.

2. NOUN NEW

/ prɑps / (AmE), / prɒps / (BrE)
(computing) Properties or parameters passed to a software component or function to configure its behavior, data, or appearance.
component properties (software)

Examples

In React, a component receives configuration through props to determine its displayed content and behavior.
The documentation recommends validating props types to prevent runtime errors when components receive unexpected values.
They passed the user object as props from the parent to the child component for rendering.
