paymaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person responsible for paying wages, especially to employees or troops
person in charge of paying wages

Examples

During the war, the paymaster ensured the soldiers received their wages on time.
She was appointed as paymaster for the project, handling all payments to contractors.
The company's paymaster distributed the monthly salaries to all employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasurer - person responsible for managing money or finances

Etymology

From 'pay' + 'master', combining the verb 'pay' and 'master' signifying someone in charge.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8pe\u026a\u02ccm\u00e6st\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8pe\u026a\u02ccm\u0251\u02d0st\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing, especially blockchain and web services) a software service or smart contract that pays funds or covers transaction costs on behalf of another party.
service or contract that pays fees

Examples

Developers integrated a paymaster service to cover users' gas fees on the network.
Many wallets allow a trusted paymaster to subsidize transaction costs for new users.
The startup acted as a paymaster, automatically paying transaction fees during promotional campaigns.
