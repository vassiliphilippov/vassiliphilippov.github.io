siamese

1. NOUN

/ saɪəˈmiz / (AmE), / saɪəˈmiːz / (BrE)
a connector with two or more inlets for hoses, used to supply water to a building's fire sprinkler or standpipe system
fire hose connector with multiple inlets

Examples

In an emergency, quick access to the siamese can make a crucial difference.
Maintenance crews inspected the siamese to ensure it was free from blockage.
The firefighters connected the hoses to the siamese at the front of the building.

Etymology

Refers to 'Siamese twins' due to the joined structure, from the historical term for conjoined twins.

2. ADJ NEW

/ saɪəˈmiz / (AmE), / saɪəˈmiːz / (BrE)
(in computing, especially machine learning) of or relating to a neural network architecture consisting of two or more identical subnetworks that share weights and process inputs in parallel, typically used to compare similarity between inputs or learn paired representations.
of twin neural networks (machine learning)

Examples

Researchers trained a siamese network to determine whether two face images belonged to the same person.
For plagiarism detection, they used a siamese architecture that compares document embeddings for similarity.
A siamese model learns to map different inputs into a space where similar items are close together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siamese network - a neural network architecture with two identical subnetworks that compare inputs for similarity
  • Synonym: twin network - an alternative name for paired neural networks that process inputs in parallel and share parameters

Etymology

Named by analogy with 'Siamese twins' to indicate the paired, joined structure.
