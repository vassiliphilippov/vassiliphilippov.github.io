flatness

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætnəs / (AmE), / ˈflætnəs / (BrE)
(of food or drink) lack of freshness, sharpness, or liveliness in flavor; dullness in taste.
lack of flavor or freshness (food/drink)

Examples

Many people dislike the flatness of day-old soda compared to a freshly opened can.
The flatness of the soup was apparent until she added a pinch of salt and herbs.
The chef tasted the sauce and immediately noticed its flatness, deciding to add more lemon.

Synset Examples

it needed lemon juice to sharpen the flatness of the dried lentils

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blandness - lack of strong flavor; mildness or dullness in taste

Etymology

From Middle English 'flat' + '-ness', originally meaning 'levelness' or 'lack of variation'; extended to flavor by analogy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflætnəs / (AmE), / ˈflætnəs / (BrE)
the condition or quality of having two dimensions, especially lacking in height or depth; the state of being flat
the state of being flat; two-dimensionality

Examples

Satellite images revealed the remarkable flatness of the region, with hardly any hills or valleys.
Scientists measured the flatness of the surface to ensure it would work for the experiment.
The flatness of the drawing made it appear almost abstract to the viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even or regular surface
  • Antonym: roughness - the quality of being uneven or irregular

Etymology

From Old English 'flett' (flat surface) plus suffix '-ness'.

5. NOUN NEW

/ ˈflætnəs / (AmE), / ˈflætnəs / (BrE)
(in digital design) the quality or aesthetic of a user interface that uses flat, two-dimensional elements, solid colours, minimal shading, and few depth cues; a design style contrasted with skeuomorphism.
digital-design aesthetic of two-dimensional simplicity

Examples

Many apps now embrace flatness, favoring solid colors and minimal shading over skeuomorphic depth.
Designers praised the flatness of the new site because it improved clarity and loading speed.
Critics argued that excessive flatness can harm usability by removing clear visual affordances for users.

Synset Examples

Critics argued that excessive flatness can harm usability by removing clear visual affordances for users.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flat design - a design movement that favors simple, two-dimensional elements and minimal ornamentation
  • Synonym: minimalism (design) - a design approach that reduces elements to the essentials to improve clarity and usability
  • Antonym: skeuomorphism - a design approach that mimics real-world textures and objects to suggest function; often contrasted with flatness

Etymology

Extension of 'flat' + '-ness'; used to describe a digital interface aesthetic popularized in the early 2010s.
