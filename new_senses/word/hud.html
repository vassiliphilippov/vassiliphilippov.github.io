HUD

1. NOUN

/ eɪtʃ juː ˈdiː / (AmE), / eɪtʃ juː ˈdiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development, the federal agency responsible for national policies and programs addressing housing needs and urban development
US federal agency for housing and urban development
american

Examples

Local officials met with representatives from HUD to discuss urban renewal projects.
Many low-income families receive support from HUD programs.
The HUD announced new initiatives to improve affordable housing in major cities.

Etymology

Acronym from 'Department of Housing and Urban Development', established in 1965.

2. NOUN NEW

/ eɪtʃ juː ˈdiː / (AmE), / eɪtʃ juː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a transparent or projected display that shows information (such as speed, navigation, or status) in a user's field of view, often used in aircraft, vehicles, and video games.
heads-up display

Examples

The car's HUD projected speed and navigation instructions onto the windshield at night.
During the training flight the pilot relied on the HUD to keep altitude and course stable.
Gamers customize the game's HUD to show health, map, and mission objectives on screen.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heads-up display - a transparent display presenting information within the user's line of sight

Etymology

Abbreviation of "heads-up display," originally used in aviation and military contexts to describe on-glass or projected instrumentation.
