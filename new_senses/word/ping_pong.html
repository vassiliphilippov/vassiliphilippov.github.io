ping pong

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪŋ ˌpɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪŋ ˌpɒŋ / (BrE)
a game for two or four players in which a lightweight ball is hit back and forth with small paddles across a table divided by a net
table game with paddles and a small ball

Examples

After dinner, she invited her friends to play ping pong in the basement.
He bought a new paddle for ping pong and practiced his serve every afternoon.
They set up the table in the garage and challenged each other to a game of ping pong.

Synset Examples

They played ping pong in the recreation room.
The kids enjoyed playing ping pong after school.
We had a friendly ping pong match last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table tennis - a game similar to ping pong, also the official sport's name

Etymology

Trademark from the name 'Ping-Pong', registered in 1901, imitating the sound of the ball.

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0254\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0252\u014b / (BrE)
to move, exchange, or alternate rapidly and repeatedly between two or more people, ideas, options, or positions, like the ball in the game of ping-pong.
alternate rapidly back and forth
informal

Examples

During the meeting, they ping-ponged between budget proposals until no decision was reached.
Emails ping-ponged all afternoon as the client and designer traded quick suggestions.
She kept ping-ponging her ideas between the two committees to find common ground.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounce - to move up, down, or back and forth after hitting a surface; used metaphorically for back-and-forth exchange
  • Synonym: oscillate - to move or swing repeatedly between two points or states; to alternate

Etymology

From the name of the game 'Ping-Pong' (imitative of the sound of the ball), used metaphorically to describe rapid back-and-forth exchange.
