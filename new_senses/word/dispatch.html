dispatch

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)
the act of sending someone or something to a place for a particular purpose
the act of sending off something

Examples

A team was awaiting the dispatch of new equipment from the main office.
She arranged the dispatch of the documents by courier yesterday.
The urgent dispatch of medical supplies saved many lives after the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipment - the act of sending goods to a destination

Etymology

From Italian *dispacciare* (to hasten), from Old French *despechier* (to hasten, dispatch).

10. VERB NEW

/ dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃ / (BrE)
(in computing) to send, route, or place a unit of work (such as a task or message) onto a particular handler, thread, or queue so it can be processed.
route or enqueue a task in computing

Examples

The runtime system will dispatch the incoming request to the appropriate worker.
After validation, the server dispatched the job to a background queue for later processing.
When overloaded, the application starts dispatching tasks across multiple threads to improve throughput.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: schedule - to arrange for a task or job to run at a particular time or by a particular worker
