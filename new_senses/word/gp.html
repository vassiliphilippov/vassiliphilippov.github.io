GP

1. NOUN

/ dʒiː piː / (AmE), / dʒiː piː / (BrE)
A doctor who provides general medical care to people, treating a wide variety of illnesses and conditions rather than specializing in one area.
general medical doctor (not a specialist)
british medical

Examples

If you feel unwell, you should make an appointment with your GP.
Many people rely on their GP for regular health checkups.
My GP referred me to a specialist for further tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: general practitioner - a physician who provides general medical care

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'general practitioner'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ dʒiː piː / (AmE), / dʒiː piː / (BrE)
In machine learning and statistics, an abbreviation for Gaussian process: a probabilistic model that defines a distribution over functions and is used to make predictions together with estimates of uncertainty.
Gaussian process (probabilistic model)

Examples

We used a GP to predict house prices and estimate the uncertainty of each prediction.
Before choosing a model, the team compared a GP with a neural network on small datasets.
A GP can indicate how confident it is about its predictions, helping to guide experiments.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaussian process - a collection of random variables any finite number of which have a joint Gaussian distribution; used as a prior over functions in Bayesian models
  • Synonym: probabilistic model - a statistical model that represents uncertainty about data and predictions using probability distributions

Etymology

Abbreviation of "Gaussian process" (named after mathematician Carl Friedrich Gauss).
