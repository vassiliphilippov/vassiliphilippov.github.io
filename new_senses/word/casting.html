casting

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of throwing a fishing line with a rod and reel into the water, especially while fishing
throwing a fishing line with a rod and reel

Examples

A smooth casting technique helps you reach deeper water where the fish are.
He practiced his casting every weekend at the lake.
With a flick of his wrist, his perfect casting amazed the other anglers.

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to throw), derived from Old Norse 'kasta' (to throw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of shaping material (such as metal or plaster) by pouring it into a mold and letting it harden
process of shaping material in a mold

Examples

Modern manufacturing often relies on casting to produce complex parts efficiently.
The bronze statue was created by casting molten metal into a detailed mold.
We studied the casting process in our art class to make ceramic figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molding - the process of shaping something in a mold

Etymology

From Old Norse kasta (to throw); related to the verb 'cast', referring to throwing or forming by pouring.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of selecting actors to play particular roles in a play, film, or other production
selection of actors for roles

Examples

After a tough casting session, the theater finally announced the lead roles.
She landed the part during the very first casting she attended.
The casting of the film took several months because the director wanted the perfect actors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audition - a trial performance to assess suitability for a role

Etymology

From the verb 'cast' (to assign roles), with -ing suffix forming a noun.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
an object that has been made by pouring a liquid material into a mold and letting it harden
object formed by pouring into a mold

Examples

Inspect the casting for any flaws before assembling the final product.
The bronze casting was displayed in the art gallery for everyone to admire.
This machine produces engine parts by metal casting in a sand mold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a hollow container used to give shape to molten or liquid material

Etymology

From Old Norse kasta (to throw), via cast (to throw, form by pouring into a mold).
