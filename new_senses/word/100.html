100

1. NUM

/ wʌn ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / wʌn ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
The number equal to ten times ten; one more than ninety-nine.
number ten times ten (one hundred)

Examples

She scored 100 points on her math test, which was a perfect score.
The company is celebrating its 100th anniversary this year.
There were exactly 100 guests at the wedding reception.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: 99 - the number one less than one hundred
  • Degree Higher: 101 - the number one more than one hundred

Etymology

From Old English 'hund' (hundred), from Proto-Germanic 'hundaradą'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ wʌn ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / wʌn ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
Informal slang meaning completely honest, genuine, or to the highest degree; used to say that something is true, sincere, or excellent.
slang: completely honest or genuine
slang informal american

Examples

If you want my honest opinion, I'll be 100 with you about the design.
She said the idea would work, and I told her I was 100 sure it would.
When he apologized sincerely, everyone agreed he was being 100 for once.

Etymology

From the numeral '100' ('one hundred'); the figurative slang use meaning 'complete' or 'perfectly honest' emerged in late 20th–early 21st century popular speech.
