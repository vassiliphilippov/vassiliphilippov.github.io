stutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈstʌtə / (BrE)
A speech disorder characterized by involuntary repetition, hesitation, or prolongation of sounds and syllables.
disorder with speech repetitions or hesitations

Examples

Despite his stutter, he delivered a moving speech at the ceremony.
His stutter became more noticeable when he was nervous.
The child received therapy to help manage his stutter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stammer - a similar speech disorder involving repeated or prolonged sounds

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, akin to Middle Low German *stottern* (to stutter).

2. VERB

/ ˈstʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈstʌtə / (BrE)
to speak with difficulty, often repeating the initial consonants of words or pausing frequently, typically due to a speech disorder or nervousness
speak with involuntary pauses or repetitions

Examples

Children who stutter may feel self-conscious when speaking in class.
He began to stutter nervously during his presentation.
She stuttered an apology before quickly exiting the room.

Synset Examples

The speaker faltered when he saw his opponent enter the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stammer - to speak with sudden involuntary pauses and a tendency to repeat initial letters

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly imitative; related to Middle Low German stotteren, German stottern.

Source: WordNet
