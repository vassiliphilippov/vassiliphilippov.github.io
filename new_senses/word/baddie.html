baddie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbædi / (AmE), / ˈbædi / (BrE)
the main bad character in a film, story, or play, especially in children's usage
main villain in fiction
informal

Examples

Children often cheer for the hero to defeat the baddie at the end of the story.
She always liked to play the baddie in school plays.
The baddie in the movie wore a black hat and scared everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - main evil character in a story or play
  • Antonym: hero - main good character in a story or play

Etymology

Formed from 'bad' + diminutive suffix '-ie'; widespread use from the mid-20th century, especially in children's language.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbædi / (AmE), / ˈbædi / (BrE)
informal slang for a confident, fashionable, and attractive person (often a woman) who projects strength and style.
stylish, confident person (slang)
informal slang

Examples

She posted a photo in a leather jacket and sunglasses, proving she's a real baddie.
At the party, the new DJ was the baddie everyone wanted to meet and photograph.
Fitness influencer and fashion blogger often called themselves baddies to show confidence and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: it-girl - a fashionable, admired young woman who sets trends and attracts attention
  • Antonym: wallflower - a shy or unremarkable person who does not attract attention, often at social events

Etymology

From 'bad' + diminutive suffix '-ie'; the slang sense 'bad' meaning impressive or attractive led to this usage in recent decades and became popular on social media in the early 21st century.
