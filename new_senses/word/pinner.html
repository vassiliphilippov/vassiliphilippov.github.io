pinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnə / (BrE)
A woman's cap with two long flaps that are pinned on, especially worn in the 17th and 18th centuries.
woman's cap with pinned flaps

Examples

In portraits of the 1700s, women are often shown with a pinner on their heads.
She wore a white pinner as part of her traditional costume.
The museum displayed an elegant pinner from the colonial era.

Etymology

From 'pin' + '-er', so called because it was fastened with pins.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpɪnər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnə / (BrE)
An internet user who saves or shares images, ideas, or links on the social platform Pinterest by 'pinning' them to virtual boards.
user of Pinterest
informal

Examples

She became a devoted pinner, saving fashion inspiration to themed boards.
As an experienced pinner, he knew how to create boards that attracted thousands of followers.
Many small businesses hire a pinner to manage their product images and link traffic to shops.
