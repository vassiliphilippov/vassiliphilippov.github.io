Basel

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːzəl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːzəl / (BrE)
A city in northwestern Switzerland, located on the Rhine River near the borders with France and Germany.
city in northwestern Switzerland

Examples

Many international companies have their headquarters in Basel.
She studied architecture at a university in Basel.
The art fair in Basel attracts collectors and artists from around the world.

Etymology

From Latin 'Basilia', the name of the Roman settlement at the site.

2. PROPN NEW

/ \u02c8b\u0251\u02d0z\u0259l / (AmE), / \u02c8b\u0251\u02d0z\u0259l / (BrE)
(finance) Informal name for the Basel Accords or the set of international banking regulations and supervisory standards issued by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision.
international banking regulations (Basel Accords)

Examples

Banks must hold more capital to meet the Basel III standards introduced after the financial crisis.
During the audit, the regulator found several violations of Basel requirements affecting the bank's liquidity ratios.
Analysts expect lenders to adjust lending practices to comply with forthcoming Basel reforms next year.

Synset Examples

Banks must hold more capital to meet the Basel III standards introduced after the financial crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basel Accords - International agreements (Basel I, II, III, etc.) that set standards for bank capital, liquidity, and supervision.
  • Related: Basel Committee on Banking Supervision - The international committee that develops the Basel regulatory frameworks and guidance for banking supervision.

Etymology

Named after the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision, which is based in the city of Basel, Switzerland.
