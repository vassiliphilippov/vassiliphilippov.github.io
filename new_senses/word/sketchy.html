sketchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɛtʃi / (AmE), / ˈskɛtʃi / (BrE)
giving only the main facts or details and not complete or thorough
not complete; lacking detail

Examples

At this stage, our understanding of the situation remains sketchy, but more details are expected soon.
Her notes were too sketchy to be useful for studying for the test.
The information we received about the new policy was quite sketchy and left us with many questions.

Synset Examples

a sketchy account
details of the plan remain sketchy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomplete - not having all the necessary or expected parts
  • Antonym: detailed - providing many details; thorough

Etymology

From English 'sketch' + '-y', indicating the quality of being like a sketch—broad but incomplete.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈskɛtʃi / (AmE), / ˈskɛtʃi / (BrE)
informal: appearing suspicious, unreliable, or potentially unsafe; giving reason to feel uneasy or distrustful.
suspicious; unsafe (informal)
informal slang

Examples

That alley looks sketchy at night, so let's walk the other way.
I wouldn't trust that dealer; his offer sounds sketchy and probably too good to be true.
Since the renovation, the neighborhood has become noticeably sketchier, and people avoid walking alone.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't trust that dealer; his offer sounds sketchy and probably too good to be true.
Since the renovation, the neighborhood has become noticeably sketchier, and people avoid walking alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspicious - causing a feeling that something is wrong or that someone cannot be trusted
  • Antonym: safe - not dangerous; free from risk or harm

Etymology

From English 'sketch' + suffix '-y'; the slang sense of 'suspicious/unsafe' developed by extension from the earlier sense 'incomplete' or 'uncertain'.
