POW

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiː oʊ ˈdʌbəljuː / (AmE), / ˌpiː əʊ ˈdʌbəljuː / (BrE)
a person, especially a member of the armed forces, who is captured and held by the enemy during war
prisoner captured in wartime

Examples

After the battle, several soldiers were declared POWs by the enemy forces.
He told his story of surviving as a POW during World War II.
The government worked tirelessly to negotiate the release of all POWs held overseas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner of war - a person captured and held by the enemy during war

Etymology

Acronym from 'prisoner of war'.
