to summarize

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌməraɪz / (BrE)
to state or represent the main points of something in a concise form
state concisely; give a summary

Examples

The abstract summarizes the main ideas in the paper.
The introduction summarizes each chapter’s main argument for the reader.
The report summarizes the key findings of the survey in just three paragraphs.

Synset Examples

The abstract summarizes the main ideas in the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum up - to state the essential facts briefly

Etymology

From summary + -ize, patterning after French 'résumer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02758570-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌməraɪz / (BrE)
To give a brief statement of the main points of something.
give a brief statement of main points

Examples

At the end of the meeting, she summarized the main decisions for everyone.
Can you summarize this article in one paragraph?
The teacher asked the students to summarize the chapter in their own words.

Synset Examples

he summed up his results
I will now summarize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum up - to state the most important facts or characteristics of something in a short and clear way
  • Antonym: detail - to give a full or detailed account of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin summarizāre, from Latin summa (sum, total).
