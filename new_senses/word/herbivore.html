herbivore

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrbɪˌvɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɜːbɪˌvɔː / (BrE)
an animal that feeds mainly on plants or grass
plant-eating animal

Examples

Koalas are herbivores that feed primarily on eucalyptus leaves.
Many large dinosaurs were believed to have been herbivores.
The African elephant is the world's largest land herbivore.

Synset Examples

horses are herbivores
the sauropod dinosaurs were apparently herbivores

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carnivore - an animal that feeds mainly on other animals
  • Antonym: omnivore - an animal that eats both plants and animals

Etymology

From Latin herba (grass) + vorare (to devour).

2. NOUN NEW

person who eats mainly plants (informal)
informal

Examples

She calls herself a herbivore and avoids all animal products.
After switching diets, he became known around the office as a herbivore.
Many restaurants now offer special menus for the modern herbivore seeking plant-based options.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetarian - a person who does not eat meat (often eats plant-based foods)
  • Synonym: vegan - a person who does not eat or use animal products
  • Antonym: carnivore - a person who eats primarily meat (used informally/figuratively)

Etymology

From Latin herba (grass) + vorare (to devour); originally denoting animals, later used figuratively for people.
