2. NOUN

/ ˌɛsˌtiːˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛsˌtiːˈdiː / (BrE)
a disease that is transmitted through sexual contact or genital interaction, also known as a sexually transmitted disease
sexually transmitted disease
medical

Examples

Many STDs can be treated effectively if diagnosed early.
Testing for STDs is an important part of routine healthcare for sexually active individuals.
Using condoms can greatly reduce your risk of contracting an STD.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venereal disease - disease typically transmitted through sexual contact
  • Synonym: STI - sexually transmitted infection, sometimes preferred for broader inclusion of infections

Etymology

Acronym for sexually transmitted disease.
