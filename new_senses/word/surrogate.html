surrogate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.ə.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈsʌr.ə.ɡət / (BrE)
a person who takes the place of another; a substitute, especially in a specific role or function
a substitute person

Examples

In some cultures, a surrogate may perform ceremonial duties on behalf of another person.
The organization appointed a surrogate to make decisions during her absence.
When their leader was sick, a trusted surrogate led the meeting in his place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another
  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who substitutes for another, especially in acting

Etymology

From Latin surrogatus, past participle of surrogare (to substitute).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.ə.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈsʌr.ə.ɡət / (BrE)
a person who is appointed to represent or act on behalf of someone else
person acting on behalf of another

Examples

As a child advocate, she served as a surrogate for children without legal guardians.
The ambassador acted as a surrogate for the president during the negotiations.
When the CEO was unavailable, a surrogate attended the meeting in her place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or serving in place of another
  • Synonym: agent - a person authorized to act on behalf of another
  • Antonym: principal - a person who is represented by another (especially by an agent)

Etymology

From Latin surrogatus (substituted), past participle of surrogare (to put in another’s place).

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɜːr.ə.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈsʌr.ə.ɡət / (BrE)
(of a person or role) providing care or fulfilling parental duties without being related by blood or legal ties; substitute.
acting as a substitute parent or caregiver

Examples

After his mother died, his aunt stepped in as a surrogate parent.
He acted as a surrogate father to his friend's children during difficult times.
Many children in the shelter found a surrogate family among the caring staff.
