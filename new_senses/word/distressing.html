distressing

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
causing anxiety, sorrow, or pain; upsetting or unfortunate
causing anxiety or sadness

Examples

It was a distressing experience to watch their home burn down.
She found it distressing to see her friend in so much pain.
The news reports contained several distressing images from the disaster.

Synset Examples

my finances were in a deplorable state
a lamentable decision
her clothes were in sad shape
a sorry state of affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upsetting - causing unhappiness or disappointment
  • Antonym: comforting - making someone feel less worried or more at ease

Etymology

From English 'distress' + '-ing', forming an adjective indicating the effect of causing distress.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing anxiety, worry, or emotional pain; making someone feel troubled or upset.
causing anxiety or emotional pain

Examples

It can be distressing to watch animals suffer in such conditions.
She found the news about her friend's illness particularly distressing.
The most distressing aspect of the disaster was the loss of so many lives.

  • Antonym: comforting - making someone feel less worried or troubled

Etymology

3. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
(in fashion and textiles) the process or technique of treating clothing or fabric to create an intentionally worn, aged, or weathered appearance.
fabric-wear treatment

Examples

The jeans underwent distressing to achieve a vintage, worn look for the photoshoot.
The boutique offers custom distressing on new denim so customers get a lived-in appearance.
She asked the tailor to add subtle distressing so her jacket looked older and softer.
