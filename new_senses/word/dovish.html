dovish

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊ.vɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.vɪʃ / (BrE)
favoring peaceful policies or opposed to aggression and war, especially in politics or diplomacy
favoring peace; opposed to war

Examples

His dovish stance on foreign policy has attracted support from peace activists.
The senator is considered dovish because of her consistent opposition to military intervention.
While some party members are more dovish, others strongly favor increasing the defense budget.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hawkish - favoring war or aggressive policy, especially in politics

Etymology

From 'dove' (a symbol of peace) + '-ish'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ c8do8a.v6a83 / (AmE), / c8d598a.v6a83 / (BrE)
(in economics and finance) favoring expansionary monetary policy, such as lower interest rates or other measures to stimulate the economy.
favoring easy monetary policy

Examples

Following weak data, the central bank took a dovish tone and hinted at lower rates next quarter.
Investors pushed bond yields down after the committee's dovish statement suggested more monetary stimulus might follow.
Her voting record shows she is consistently dovish, favoring cuts and stimulus during economic slowdowns.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodative - favoring easy or expansionary monetary policy (e.g., low interest rates, asset purchases).
  • Antonym: hawkish - favoring higher interest rates or tighter monetary policy to control inflation.

Etymology

From 'dove' (a symbol of conciliation) + '-ish'; extended metaphorically to describe policymakers who prefer looser monetary policy.
