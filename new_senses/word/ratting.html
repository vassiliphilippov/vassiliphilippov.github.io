ratting

1. NOUN

/ ˈræ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving incriminating information about someone to the police, typically in exchange for personal gain or leniency
act of informing on someone for police
informal slang

Examples

After weeks of ratting, he was offered a reduced sentence in exchange for testifying.
His friends turned against him after discovering his ratting had led to multiple arrests.
They accused him of ratting when the police showed up with new evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitching - the act of informing on someone, often used in criminal or informal contexts
  • Synonym: betrayal - the act of betraying someone or something

Etymology

Derived from the slang use of 'rat' meaning informant or betrayer, attested in early 20th-century American English.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈræ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing, cybersecurity) the act of using a remote access Trojan (RAT) to gain unauthorized remote access to a computer or network, typically to steal data or maintain persistent control.
use of RAT malware

Examples

Security researchers warned that attackers conducted ratting to steal data from the enterprise.
During the breach, forensic analysts found evidence of persistent ratting on multiple servers.
Companies must patch vulnerabilities quickly to reduce the risk of ratting by remote attackers.

Synset Examples

Security researchers warned that attackers conducted ratting to steal data from the enterprise.
During the breach, forensic analysts found evidence of persistent ratting on multiple servers.
Companies must patch vulnerabilities quickly to reduce the risk of ratting by remote attackers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote access Trojan (RAT) - a type of malware that gives an attacker covert, remote control of an infected computer
  • Synonym: malware - software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise computer systems

Etymology

From RAT (remote access Trojan) + -ing; RAT is an acronym for 'remote access Trojan'.
