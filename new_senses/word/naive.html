naive

1. ADJ

/ naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment; not initiated or lacking relevant experience.
lacking experience or judgment

Examples

As a naive newcomer, she made many honest mistakes at her first job.
His naive optimism led him to believe success would be easy.
Many people are naive about the risks involved in starting a new business.

Synset Examples

it seemed a bizarre ceremony to uninitiated western eyes
he took part in the experiment as a naive subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocent - lacking experience or sophistication
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having much worldly experience and knowledge

Etymology

From French 'naïf' (naive), from Latin 'nativus' (native, natural).

6. ADJ NEW

/ naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
(in machine learning and statistics) making a simplifying independence assumption about features; applied to models (e.g., naive Bayes) that assume features are conditionally independent given the class.
assuming feature independence (ML)

Examples

The team evaluated a naive Bayes classifier despite its independence assumptions in feature design.
Because the dataset was small, she chose a naive model to avoid overfitting and complexity.
Even though simple, the naive approach produced useful predictions in early prototype tests.

Synset Examples

From French 'naïf' (naive), from Latin 'nativus' (native, natural). Used in modern statistics and machine learning to label models that make a simple independence assumption (as in 'naive Bayes').
