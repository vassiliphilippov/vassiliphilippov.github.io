cam

1. NOUN

/ kæm / (AmE), / kam / (BrE)
A rotating or sliding piece in mechanical devices, shaped to convert circular motion into linear motion or vice versa.
mechanical part for converting motion

Examples

By adjusting the shape of the cam, engineers can change the movement pattern of the machine.
The bike's brakes use a cam mechanism for more stopping power.
The engine's cam controls the opening and closing of the valves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crank - a mechanical device for converting reciprocating motion into rotational motion

Etymology

Short form of 'camshaft', from Dutch 'kam' (cog, comb, cam).

2. NOUN NEW

/ kæm / (AmE), / kæm / (BrE)
Informal short for camera, especially a webcam or a device's built-in camera used to record or transmit live video or take photos.
informal short for camera (webcam)
informal

Examples

She turned on the cam to join the video chat.
The teacher asked everyone to mute microphones but keep their cam on for attendance.
He covered his laptop's cam with a sticker after hearing about privacy breaches.
