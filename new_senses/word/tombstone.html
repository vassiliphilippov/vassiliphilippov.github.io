tombstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːmˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtuːmstəʊn / (BrE)
a stone marker placed over or next to a grave, usually inscribed with the deceased person's name and dates
stone marking a grave

Examples

She spent the afternoon cleaning her ancestors' tombstone in the old churchyard.
The family gathered at the cemetery to place flowers beside the tombstone.
The name on the tombstone had faded with time, but it was still legible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravestone - a stone that marks a grave
  • Synonym: headstone - a slab of stone set up at the head of a grave

Etymology

From Middle English. 'Tomb' from Latin 'tumba' (burial mound) + 'stone'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8tud0mccsto8an / (AmE), / c8tud0mst598an / (BrE)
(in computing) a marker placed on a record or object to indicate it has been deleted, retained temporarily so that replicas and consistency protocols can process the deletion until the data is permanently removed.
deletion marker in databases

Examples

During replication, the system stored a tombstone so other nodes would know the record was deleted.
After compaction, the database permanently removed the old data and cleared the tombstones.
Investigators found that many rows still had a tombstone preventing their immediate restoration.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deletion marker - a marker indicating that a record has been deleted and should be ignored until removal
  • Synonym: soft delete - a deletion approach where records are marked as deleted but not immediately removed from storage

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the physical 'tombstone' (a grave marker); applied in computing to mark that an item is 'dead' or deleted.
