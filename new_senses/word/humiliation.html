humiliation

1. NOUN

/ hjuˌmɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / hjuˌmɪlɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling or state of shame or loss of self-respect, often caused by being made to feel foolish or inferior.
state of shame or loss of dignity

Examples

After the public apology, he felt a deep sense of humiliation that lasted for weeks.
She could not forget the humiliation of being laughed at by her classmates.
Winning the game was sweet, but the memory of last year's humiliation still lingered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness
  • Antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements

Etymology

From Medieval Latin humiliatio (making humble, abasement).
