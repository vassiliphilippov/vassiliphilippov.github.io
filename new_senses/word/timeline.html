timeline

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmlaɪn / (BrE)
a visual or conceptual representation of a sequence of related events in chronological order, often displayed along a line
chronological sequence of events

Examples

The documentary presented a detailed timeline of the artist's life and achievements.
The teacher used a timeline to help the students understand the key events of World War II.
We created a project timeline to keep track of deadlines and milestones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronology - the science of arranging events in their order of occurrence in time
  • Degree Higher: timeline - (digital, social media) a user's record of posts and activities displayed chronologically

Etymology

Formed from 'time' + 'line', first recorded in early 20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ta\u026am\u02ccla\u026an / (AmE), / \u02c8ta\u026amla\u026an / (BrE)
A social media feature or page that displays a user's posts, updates, and activity, usually in reverse-chronological order.
social media feed of posts

Examples

She posted a vacation photo on her timeline so all her friends could see it immediately.
After adjusting privacy, he reviewed his timeline to remove any old posts he didn't want public.
The journalist scrolled through the candidate's timeline to find earlier statements about education policy.
