AD

1. ADV

/ ˌeɪ ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
used before a date to indicate a year after the traditional birth of Jesus Christ; in the Christian era
indicates years in the Christian era

Examples

The Roman Empire reached its peak around AD 117.
The council was convened in AD 325 to address disputes within the early church.
This building dates back to AD 500, making it over 1,500 years old.

Synset Examples

in AD 200

Related Terms

  • Antonym: BC - used to indicate years before the traditional birth of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Latin 'Anno Domini', meaning 'in the year of our Lord'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ æd / (AmE), / æd / (BrE)
A paid announcement or sponsored content; a label indicating material is a commercial promotion, especially online or in media.
label for paid promotion

Examples

The influencer clearly marked the Instagram post as AD so followers would know it was paid promotion.
When watching tutorials, I usually skip any in-stream AD that interrupts the explanation.
The website annoyed readers with pop-ups and one large full-page AD covering the article.
