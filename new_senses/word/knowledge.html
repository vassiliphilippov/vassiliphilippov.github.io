knowledge

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
information, understanding, or skill that you get from experience or education; awareness gained by learning, perception, or reasoning.
information or skills gained from learning or experience

Examples

Gaining knowledge requires dedication and curiosity.
Her knowledge of world history impressed everyone at the interview.
We must keep updating our knowledge in this rapidly changing field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
  • Degree Higher: wisdom - the ability to use knowledge and experience to make good decisions
  • Synonym: information - facts or details about a subject

Etymology

From Old English 'cnāwleċe' (acknowledgement), related to 'know.'
