DoT

1. NOUN

/ di oʊ ti / (AmE), / diː əʊ tiː / (BrE)
Abbreviation for the United States Department of Transportation, the federal agency responsible for national transportation systems and policy.
U.S. Department of Transportation (abbreviation)
american

Examples

Many highway projects are overseen by the DoT to ensure compliance with federal standards.
Several grants are available through the DoT for infrastructure improvements.
The DoT announced new safety regulations for commercial airlines this year.

2. NOUN NEW

/ di o\u028a ti / (AmE), / di\u02d0 \u0259\u028a ti\u02d0 / (BrE)
(in video games) an effect or status that inflicts damage on a character or target continuously or repeatedly over a period of time rather than all at once.
damage-over-time effect (gaming)

Examples

During the raid, the mage applied a DoT that slowly drained the enemy's health.
In high-level play, players stack multiple DoTs to secure steady damage on the target.
Understand how resistance reduces a DoT's effectiveness to improve your character's survivability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage over time - a game effect that deals damage repeatedly to a target over a period rather than instantly
  • Antonym: burst damage - damage dealt quickly or all at once, often contrasting with sustained damage over time

Etymology

From the initialism of the phrase "damage over time" (video-game jargon).
