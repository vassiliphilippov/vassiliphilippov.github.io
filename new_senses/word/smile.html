smile

1. VERB

/ smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
to express or communicate (a feeling or message) with a smile
express with a smile

Examples

He smiled his approval at her suggestion, putting everyone at ease.
She smiled her gratitude to the audience after receiving the award.
The teacher smiled encouragement to the nervous student before the performance.

Synset Examples

She smiled her thanks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grin - to smile broadly, often showing the teeth
  • Antonym: frown - to furrow the brow in an expression of displeasure or concentration

Etymology

From Middle English smile, from Old English smīlan, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
A facial expression in which the corners of the mouth are turned up, typically indicating pleasure, friendliness, or amusement.
expression with upturned corners of the mouth

Examples

A smile spread across his face when he heard the good news.
Her smile was so infectious that everyone in the group started laughing.
She gave me a warm smile as I entered the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frown - a facial expression showing displeasure or concern
  • Synonym: grin - a broad smile often showing the teeth

Etymology

From Middle English smilen, likely of Scandinavian origin, related to the Old Norse smila (to smile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06891121-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
to make a happy or friendly expression by turning up the corners of the mouth
make a happy facial expression

Examples

He always smiles at strangers to make them feel welcome.
She couldn't help but smile when she received the good news.
The children smiled brightly as they posed for the picture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frown - to furrow the brow to show displeasure or concern

Etymology

From Middle English *smilen*, probably from a Scandinavian source, compare Norwegian *smila* (to smile).
