Midi

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdi / (AmE), / ˈmiːdi / (BrE)
the southern part of France, especially as a distinct region known for its climate and culture
the southern region of France

Examples

Cuisine in the Midi is closely tied to Mediterranean traditions.
Many tourists are drawn to the Midi for its warm weather and beautiful landscapes.
She spent her summer traveling through the picturesque villages of the Midi.

Etymology

From French 'Midi', literally meaning 'midday' or 'south'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈmiːdi / (AmE), / ˈmiːdi / (BrE)
(of a skirt or dress) having a hemline that falls around the middle of the lower leg, typically at or just below the calf.
mid-calf garment length

Examples

She chose a flowing midi dress for the afternoon wedding to balance comfort and style.
The boutique displayed several pleated midi skirts perfect for office wear in cooler months.
If you're unsure, try a high-waisted midi to see where it falls on your calves.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea-length - a dress or skirt length that falls below the knee and above the ankle, similar to a midi
  • Antonym: mini - a very short skirt or dress length that contrasts with the midi
  • Antonym: maxi - a long skirt or dress length reaching the ankles, opposite in length to the midi

Etymology

From French 'midi' (midday), used in fashion to mean 'middle' or 'middle length'.
