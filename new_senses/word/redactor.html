redactor

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdæktər / (AmE), / rɪˈdaktə / (BrE)
a person who edits or prepares text for publication, often shaping its form or structure
person who edits text for publication

Examples

As a skilled redactor, she ensured the manuscript met all editorial standards.
The redactor reviewed every article before sending it to print.
The historian credited the redactor for shaping the final version of the ancient text.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who edits text or media for publication

Etymology

From Latin 'redactor', from 'redigere' (to bring back, reduce).

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈdæktər / (AmE), / rɪˈdaktə / (BrE)
(in computing) a software program or automated system that removes, obscures, or masks sensitive information from documents, images, or datasets, often by applying rules or pattern recognition.
automated redaction software

Examples

Before publication, the legal team ran the documents through a redactor to hide client names.
The open-source redactor automatically blurred dates and Social Security numbers in the dataset.
After anonymization, auditors confirmed the redactor removed all personal identifiers from the file.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redaction tool - software used to remove or obscure sensitive information from documents.
  • Synonym: redact - to remove or obscure sensitive information from text or images.

Etymology

From Latin 'redactor', from 'redigere' (to bring back, reduce); extended in modern usage to refer to automated redaction tools.
