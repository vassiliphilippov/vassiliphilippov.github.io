printing

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the business or industry of producing printed material for sale or distribution
business of producing printed material

Examples

Advancements in technology have transformed the printing industry over the past decade.
Many small towns relied on the local printing shop for newspapers and flyers.
She started a successful printing business that specializes in booklets and brochures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publishing - the business of preparing and issuing printed material

Etymology

From Middle English 'prenten', from Old French 'preindre', from Latin 'premere' (to press).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of producing text or images by applying ink to paper, especially for publication
process of producing text/images on paper

Examples

Advances in printing have made books more affordable and widely available.
He works in the commercial printing industry, producing posters and brochures.
The printing of newspapers happens overnight so they are ready by morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publishing - the business or process of preparing and issuing books, journals, or other material for sale

Etymology

From Middle English 'prenten', from Old French 'preint', past participle of 'preindre', from Latin 'premere' (to press).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
text that is handwritten to resemble printed type, rather than written in cursive or script
handwriting in print style

Examples

He prefers printing over cursive because it is easier to read.
Her printing is so neat that it almost looks typed.
The teacher asked the students to use printing so their handwriting would be clear.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cursive - handwritten text in joined, flowing style

Etymology

From 'print' + '-ing'; 'print' from Old French 'preindre', from Latin 'premere' (to press).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈprɪn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a set of copies of a book, newspaper, or similar printed work produced at one time
set of copies produced at one time

Examples

After the first printing sold out, collectors began looking for early copies.
The publisher is planning a second printing of the popular novel due to high demand.
There were minor errors in the first printing that were corrected in later versions.

5. NOUN NEW

/ ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of producing three-dimensional objects by additive manufacturing, creating successive layers of material from a digital design (also called 3D printing).
making 3D objects by layering material

Examples

The startup uses printing to produce custom prosthetic parts at a lower cost.
Engineers tested new materials suitable for printing complex aerospace components under high stress.
As printing technology improves, schools can teach students design and rapid prototyping.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additive manufacturing - the technical term for making objects by adding material layer by layer; often used synonymously with 3D printing
  • Antonym: subtractive manufacturing - a manufacturing approach that creates parts by removing material (for example, milling or cutting); often contrasted with additive methods
