to engage

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to hire or employ someone for a job or task
hire or employ for work

Examples

The company decided to engage several consultants for the upcoming project.
They engaged a new assistant to help with the increased workload.
We are hoping to engage more staff before the holiday season starts.

Synset Examples

How many people has she employed?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hire - to employ someone for wages
  • Synonym: employ - to give someone a job to do
  • Antonym: dismiss - to remove someone from their job

Etymology

From French engager, ultimately from Latin in + vadium (pledge).
