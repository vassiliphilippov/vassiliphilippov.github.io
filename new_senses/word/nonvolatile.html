nonvolatile

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈvɑl.əˌtaɪl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
Not easily vaporized or converted into a gas at normal temperatures; having low volatility.
not easily vaporized; low volatility

Examples

For this process, choose a nonvolatile solvent to minimize evaporation losses.
The solution contains a nonvolatile acid that remains after evaporation.
Water is considered nonvolatile at room temperature compared to alcohol.

Synset Examples

a nonvolatile acid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: volatile - readily evaporating at normal temperatures

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'volatile' (from Latin 'volatilis', meaning able to fly or evaporate).

(computing) Not losing stored information when power is removed; describing memory or storage that retains data without electrical power.
computer memory that retains data without power

Examples

The server uses nonvolatile memory to prevent data loss during unexpected power cuts.
Modern smartphones include nonvolatile storage so your photos remain after a battery dies.
Engineers chose nonvolatile EEPROM chips for calibration settings that must survive power cycling.
