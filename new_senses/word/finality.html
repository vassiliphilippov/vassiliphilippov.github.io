finality

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / faɪˈnalɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being final or settled; the condition of having reached an irreversible or definite conclusion
quality of being final or definite

Examples

The finality of death is difficult for many people to accept.
The judge's decision carried an air of finality that ended the debate.
There was a sense of finality in her voice when she said goodbye.

Synset Examples

the finality of death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the state of being certain or definite
  • Antonym: indecisiveness - the quality of not being settled or final

Etymology

From Late Latin finalitas, from Latin finalis (final).

2. NOUN NEW

/ faɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / faɪˈnaləti / (BrE)
(in blockchain and distributed systems) the point at which a transaction or block is considered irreversibly confirmed by the network's agreement (consensus).
irreversible confirmation in blockchains

Examples

After six confirmations, the transaction reached finality on the blockchain network.
Network validators work to ensure block finality, so users can trust completed transactions.
Without clear finality, businesses may hesitate to accept cryptocurrency payments immediately.
