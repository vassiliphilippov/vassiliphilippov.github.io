evangelist

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith, especially by public preaching of the gospel
preacher of the Christian gospel

Examples

As a television evangelist, she reached millions of viewers around the world.
He became an evangelist after studying theology in college.
The famous evangelist spoke to a crowd of thousands at the stadium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preacher - a person who gives religious sermons
  • Synonym: missionary - a person sent on a religious mission

Etymology

From Greek euangelistēs (bringer of good news), from euangelion (good news).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who strongly promotes a technology, product, platform, or idea, aiming to persuade others to adopt or support it
enthusiastic promoter of a technology or product

Examples

The company's evangelist demonstrated the open-source framework to developers at the conference.
She was hired as a developer evangelist to grow adoption of the new API among partners.
At startup meetups, product evangelists excitedly explain how tools solve developers' real problems.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
  • Synonym: promoter - someone who supports or helps publicize a product, event, or idea

Originally from Greek euangelistēs 'bringer of good news'; now used figuratively for enthusiastic promoters of ideas and technologies.
