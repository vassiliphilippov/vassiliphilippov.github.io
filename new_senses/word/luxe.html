luxe

1. ADJ

/ lʌks / (AmE), / lʌks / (BrE)
Elegant and luxurious; characterized by great comfort, beauty, and expense.
elegant and luxurious

Examples

Every detail in the designer's collection exuded a sense of luxe sophistication.
She stayed in a luxe suite overlooking the ocean during her vacation.
The restaurant is famous for its luxe decor and impeccable service.

Synset Examples

a deluxe car
luxe accommodations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - rich and comfortable; sumptuous and expensive
  • Synonym: deluxe - of special elegance, sumptuousness, or fineness; high or highest in quality

Etymology

From French "luxe" (luxury), from Latin "luxus" (excess).

2. NOUN NEW

/ lʌks / (AmE), / lʌks / (BrE)
Luxury; expensive or high-end goods, services, or a lifestyle characterized by comfort and elegance.
luxury; high-end goods or lifestyle
informal

Examples

The brochure highlights the luxe of the resort, from private chefs to spa services.
She prefers travel that offers a little luxe instead of the cheapest possible flight and hotel.
The magazine's latest issue celebrates accessible luxe—affordable products that still feel indulgent and stylish.
