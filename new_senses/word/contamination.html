contamination

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A substance that makes something impure or unsafe by contact or mixture.
impurity-causing substance

Examples

All food products are checked for any contamination before being distributed.
Tests revealed a high level of contamination in the river water.
The drinking water was unfit due to chemical contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminant - A substance that causes contamination
  • Antonym: purity - The state of being pure and free from contamination

Etymology

From Latin contaminatio (a touching or mixing together).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the presence or introduction of harmful or unwanted substances that make something impure or unclean
the state of being contaminated

Examples

Food contamination can lead to serious health problems if not detected early.
Scientists found contamination in the water supply after the chemical spill.
The laboratory takes strict measures to prevent contamination of the samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollution - the introduction of harmful substances into the environment
  • Antonym: purification - the process of making something pure

Etymology

From Latin 'contaminatio', from 'contaminare' (to pollute).
