cycloserine

1. NOUN

/ saɪkloʊˈsɪriːn / (AmE), / saɪkləʊˈsɪəriːn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used especially to treat tuberculosis, effective against the tubercle bacillus
antibiotic for tuberculosis
medical

Examples

Researchers are studying the side effects of long-term cycloserine use.
Some strains of bacteria are resistant to cycloserine and require alternative antibiotics.
The doctor prescribed cycloserine as part of her tuberculosis treatment regimen.

Etymology

From 'cyclo-' (cycle, ring structure) and 'serine' (an amino acid), reflecting its chemical structure.
