exporter

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɔrtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːtə / (BrE)
a person or company that sells and sends goods to another country
person or business that sends goods abroad for sale

Examples

As an exporter of agricultural products, the company ships grain to several countries every year.
Many challenges face a small business trying to become an exporter for the first time.
The nation's economy relies heavily on its role as an exporter of oil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importer - a person or company that brings goods into a country from abroad

Etymology

From export + -er, formed from Latin 'ex-' (out) + 'portare' (to carry).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u026ak\u02c8sp\u0254rt\u0259r / (AmE), / \u026ak\u02c8sp\u0254\u02d0t\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing) a software component that exposes internal data or system metrics so external tools can collect, process, or store them.
software component exposing metrics

Examples

The monitoring stack uses an exporter to publish host metrics to Prometheus.
To troubleshoot performance, install a network exporter that sends bandwidth data to your dashboard.
During deployment they configured several exporters to expose application metrics for collection.

From export + -er; extended metaphorically in computing to mean a component that 'exports' data to external tools.
