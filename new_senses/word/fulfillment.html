fulfillment

1. NOUN

/ fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of satisfaction or happiness that results from achieving your desires or goals
satisfaction from achieving desires or goals

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally experienced fulfillment in her career.
Many people seek fulfillment through meaningful relationships and personal growth.
Volunteering at the animal shelter gives him a sense of fulfillment he can't find elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfaction - pleasure from achieving what you want
  • Antonym: disappointment - sadness from not achieving what you want

Etymology

From Old French *fulfilment*, based on Middle English *fulfil* (to fill up, accomplish).

2. NOUN

/ fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
the achievement or realization of something desired, promised, or expected
achievement or realization of a desire or promise

Examples

Finding meaningful work is essential for personal fulfillment.
She felt a deep sense of fulfillment after achieving her lifelong dream.
The fulfillment of the promise brought joy to everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the action or process of finishing something
  • Antonym: disappointment - the feeling of not having one's hopes or expectations fulfilled

Etymology

From Old French 'fulfillement', based on the verb 'fulfill'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ fəlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fəlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
The process in commerce of receiving, processing, packing, and shipping customer orders (order fulfillment).
processing and shipping orders

Examples

The retailer outsourced its fulfillment to a third-party provider to speed delivery.
Our startup built a local fulfillment hub to process online orders faster and cut costs.
During the holiday rush, warehouse staff struggled to keep up with fulfillment for thousands of orders.
