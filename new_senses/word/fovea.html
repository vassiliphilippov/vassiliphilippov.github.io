fovea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.vɪ.ə / (BrE)
a small depression in the retina of the eye where visual acuity is highest, containing a high concentration of cone cells
depression in retina with sharpest vision
medical

Examples

Color perception is most accurate in the fovea because of its concentration of cone cells.
Damage to the fovea can result in a significant loss of central vision.
The surgeon explained that the fovea is crucial for tasks like reading and recognizing faces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macula - area of the retina surrounding the fovea, responsible for central vision

Etymology

From Latin 'fovea' meaning 'small pit' or 'ditch'.

(in vision science and computing) the central region of a visual field or digital display corresponding to the viewer's gaze, where visual detail, resolution, or processing is prioritized.
high-attention region in a display

Examples

The game engine used eye tracking to render higher detail in the player's fovea on screen.
In VR testing, developers concentrated resources on the display's central fovea for smooth performance.
Researchers measured eye position to predict the display fovea where high-resolution rendering would be most beneficial.

  • Synonym: foveated rendering - a graphics technique that renders high detail only in the viewer's foveal region to save processing resources

Etymology

From Latin 'fovea' meaning 'small pit'. The term is extended metaphorically from the anatomical fovea to denote a central focus area in displays and gaze-tracking.
