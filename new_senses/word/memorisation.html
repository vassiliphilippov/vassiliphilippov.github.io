memorisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛm.ə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛm.ər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of committing something to memory, often verbatim.
the act of committing to memory
british

Examples

Regular memorisation of vocabulary helps language learners achieve fluency.
The play required the actor's careful memorisation of every line.
The student's success was largely due to his rapid memorisation of complicated facts.

Synset Examples

the actor's memorization of his lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorization - The act or process of committing something to memory (American spelling)
  • Synonym: recitation - The act of repeating something aloud from memory
  • Antonym: forgetting - The process of failing to remember

Etymology

From Latin 'memoria' (memory) via French or directly into English as a noun formed from 'memorise' + '-ation'.

(in machine learning) the tendency of a model to reproduce or store exact training examples or data fragments instead of learning general patterns, often causing poor performance on new data or data leakage.
model storing training data
british

Examples

Researchers warned that the model's memorisation of training examples could leak private user information.
To avoid memorisation, the team added dropout and data augmentation during model training.
Journal reviewers criticised the paper for evidence of memorisation rather than genuine generalisation to new data.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overfitting - when a model learns noise or details of the training data and performs poorly on new data
  • Antonym: generalisation - the ability of a model to perform well on unseen data; opposite of memorisation in ML contexts

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the general noun 'memorisation' (the act of committing to memory), adopted in computing and machine-learning literature in the 21st century.
