corrupting

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone or something to become morally or ethically degraded; having a damaging or contaminating influence
having a harmful or contaminating influence

Examples

He warned of the corrupting nature of money in politics.
Some believe that violent video games have a corrupting effect on children.
The corrupting influence of unchecked power can erode even the strongest values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depraving - causing moral corruption
  • Antonym: edifying - providing moral or intellectual improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'corrumpere' meaning 'to destroy, spoil'.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (BrE)
encouraging or causing someone to engage in immoral or dishonest behavior
causing someone to become immoral or dishonest

Examples

Many people feared that the corrupting influence of money would ruin the young politician.
Some critics argued that the film had a corrupting effect on impressionable audiences.
There are concerns about the corrupting power of unchecked authority in large organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality
  • Antonym: edifying - improving someone morally or intellectually

3. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (BrE)
causing harm to the mind or morals; making someone morally depraved or dishonest
harmful to morals or mind

Examples

He avoided bad company, fearing their corrupting influence on his character.
Some parents worry about the corrupting effects of violent video games on children.
The corrupting power of money can change a person's values over time.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degrading - causing a loss of self-respect; humiliating
  • Antonym: uplifting - morally or spiritually elevating; inspiring

Etymology

From the verb 'corrupt', from Latin 'corruptus' (spoiled, broken).

4. ADJ NEW

/ kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing) causing data, files, or storage to become corrupted, damaged, or to lose integrity.
causing data corruption

Examples

The malware included a corrupting payload that damaged critical files.
A faulty update introduced a corrupting bug that silently altered the database entries overnight.
Back up regularly to avoid losing important work if a corrupting error affects your drive.

Synset Examples

From Latin 'corrumpere' (to spoil, break).
