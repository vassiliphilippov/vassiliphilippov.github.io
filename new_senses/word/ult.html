ult

1. ADJ

/ ʌlt / (AmE), / ʌlt / (BrE)
of or relating to the month preceding the present one (used chiefly in formal correspondence, now rare).
of last month (archaic, formal)
archaic

Examples

His reply to my letter dated the 30th ult has not yet arrived.
The invoice sent on the 22nd ult remains unpaid.
We received your payment on the 15th ult.

Synset Examples

your letter received on the 29th ult

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inst - of the present month (used in correspondence)
  • Antonym: prox - of the next month (used in correspondence)

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin ultimo mense, meaning 'in the last month'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ʌlt / (AmE), / ʌlt / (BrE)
In online gaming, an abbreviation of 'ultimate': a character's most powerful ability or move, typically with limited uses or a long cooldown.
gaming ultimate ability
informal slang

Examples

The mage unleashed her ult to finish the boss quickly.
When the team fight began, she warned them before using her ult to stun enemies.
He saved his ults for the final round and reversed their losing streak.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimate ability - a character's strongest skill or move in a game, often decisive in combat

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'ultimate' (short for 'ultimate ability'), from the gaming community in the early 21st century.
