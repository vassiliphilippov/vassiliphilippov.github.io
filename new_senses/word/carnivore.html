carnivore

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrnəˌvɔr / (AmE), / ˈkɑːnɪˌvɔː / (BrE)
A terrestrial or aquatic mammal that eats flesh; a meat-eating animal, especially one belonging to the order Carnivora.
flesh-eating mammal

Examples

Many prehistoric carnivores left fossilized bones as evidence of their diet.
The lion is a well-known carnivore that lives and hunts in groups called prides.
Unlike herbivores, a true carnivore cannot survive on plants alone.

Synset Examples

terrestrial carnivores have four or five clawed digits on each limb

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herbivore - an animal that feeds on plants
  • Antonym: omnivore - an animal that eats both plants and animals

Etymology

From Latin 'carnivorus' (flesh-eating), from 'caro' (flesh) + 'vorare' (to devour).
