roasted

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊstɪd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊstɪd / (BrE)
(of food, especially meat) cooked by dry heat, typically in an oven or over a fire.
cooked by dry heat in an oven

Examples

She prefers her coffee beans roasted to a dark finish.
The chef served roasted vegetables alongside the main course.
We enjoyed a delicious roasted chicken for dinner last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grilled - (of food) cooked over direct heat or on a grill
  • Antonym: boiled - (of food) cooked in hot water

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'roast', from Old French 'rostir', of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈroʊstɪd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊstɪd / (BrE)
publicly ridiculed or humorously mocked, especially in a formal roast, by comedians, or on social media.
publicly mocked or ridiculed
informal

Examples

She was roasted on social media after posting the controversial video.
At the comedy night, the celebrity was lovingly roasted by old friends and comic rivals.
After his disastrous presentation the team roasted him with jokes during the coffee break.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridicule - to subject someone to contemptuous or dismissive language or behavior
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration; opposite of ridiculing

Etymology

From the verb 'roast' (originally 'to cook by dry heat'); extended figuratively in the 20th century to mean 'to subject to mocking or ridicule'.
