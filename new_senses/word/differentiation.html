differentiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˌrɛnʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which cells or tissues develop specialized structures and functions.
biological specialization of cells or tissues
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, differentiation allows cells to form tissues with specialized functions.
Muscle cells result from the differentiation of unspecialized precursor cells.
Scientists study differentiation to understand how different cell types arise in an organism.

Synset Examples

cell differentiation in the developing embryo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - the process of becoming suited for a particular function

Etymology

From Latin *differentiatio*, from *differentiare* (to make different).
