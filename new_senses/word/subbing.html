subbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of working as a substitute for someone, especially when the person is ill or on leave
working as a substitute for someone

Examples

Due to illness, he did a lot of subbing last year.
Many schools rely on subbing to cover unexpected teacher absences.
She will be subbing for the chemistry teacher next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person who takes the place of another

Etymology

From 'sub', short for 'substitute', with the present participle ending '-ing'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of creating, translating, timing, or synchronizing subtitles for video content, especially performed by fans or volunteer groups.
creating or syncing subtitles
informal

Examples

The team's weekly subbing sessions produced accurate English subtitles for the anime episodes.
She learned timing and translation techniques to improve her subbing for indie film festivals.
Volunteer subbing communities often coordinate via Discord to finish episodes quickly.

From 'subtitle' or 'subtitling' (shortened to 'sub') + -ing; popularized in fansubbing and localization communities.
