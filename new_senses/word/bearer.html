bearer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈbeərə / (BrE)
a person in possession of a check, note, bond, or document of title that is payable to whoever holds it or is endorsed to them.
person holding a negotiable financial document
legal

Examples

He lost a bond marked ‘to bearer’ and now anyone holding it can claim its value.
The check is payable to bearer, so anyone presenting it can cash it.
‘Pay to bearer’ is a common phrase on negotiable instruments.

Synset Examples

the bond was marked ‘payable to bearer’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: payee - the person to whom money is paid or is to be paid, especially by a check or note

Etymology

From Old French *bereor* (porter), from *berer* (to bear), from Latin *ferre* (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈbeə.rə / (BrE)
a person, especially one of the mourners at a funeral, who carries the coffin; a pallbearer.
person carrying a coffin at a funeral

Examples

Each of the bearers walked slowly and solemnly as they carried the coffin to the gravesite.
He was deeply honored to be asked to serve as a bearer at his grandfather’s funeral.
The bearers carefully lifted the casket from the hearse and proceeded into the chapel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallbearer - a person carrying or escorting a coffin at a funeral

Etymology

From Old French *bereor*, from Late Latin *bajulārius* (porter).

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈbeərə / (BrE)
a person who carries or presents something, especially a message or information

Examples

No one likes to be the bearer of bad tidings, but someone must deliver the message.
The bearer of this letter is authorized to collect the parcel on my behalf.

Related Terms

5. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8b\u025br\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8be\u0259r\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing, security) a credential (often called a bearer token) presented to a service to prove access rights; whoever possesses the credential can use it.
credential (bearer token) for access

Examples

The API accepts a bearer token in the Authorization header to authenticate requests.
Developers warned that storing a bearer token insecurely could let attackers access user accounts.
During testing she used several bearer tokens to simulate different permission levels for the app.
