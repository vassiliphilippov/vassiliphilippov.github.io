flight

1. NOUN

/ flaɪt / (AmE), / flaɪt / (BrE)
an act or instance of traveling by air, especially in an aircraft
an instance of air travel

Examples

He booked a last-minute flight for his business trip.
Her first flight was both thrilling and a little scary.
The flight to Paris was delayed due to bad weather.

Synset Examples

flying was still an exciting adventure for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another
  • Antonym: landing - the act of coming down to the ground after flying

Etymology

From Old English 'flyht' (act of flying), related to the verb 'fly'.
