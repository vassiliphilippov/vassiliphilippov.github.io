monetisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌnəˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The action or process of establishing something, such as gold or silver, as the legal tender of a country.
establishing something as legal tender

Examples

Historical debates centered on the monetisation of gold to stabilize the economy.
Many countries underwent monetisation processes in the 19th century as their financial systems developed.
The government announced the monetisation of silver, making it legal tender nationwide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demonetisation - withdrawal of legal tender status from a currency

Etymology

From French 'monétisation', from 'monétiser' (to coin money), ultimately from Latin 'moneta' (money).
