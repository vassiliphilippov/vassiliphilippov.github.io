ecosystem

1. NOUN

/ ˈikoʊˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈiːkəʊˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment, functioning together as a system
system of organisms and their environment

Examples

Deforestation can disrupt the balance of a forest ecosystem.
The coral reef ecosystem is home to an incredible diversity of marine life.
Wetlands are important ecosystems that purify water and support wildlife.

Etymology

Coined in the 1930s from Greek oikos (house) + systema (system).

a network of interconnected organizations, products, services, and users—especially in business or technology—that interact and create mutual value.
network of interconnected products and services

Examples

The company aimed to build an ecosystem around its API to attract developers and partners.
Startups often join a larger ecosystem to access users, tools, and distribution channels.
Apple's hardware, software, and services form an ecosystem that encourages customer loyalty.
