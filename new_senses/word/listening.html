listening

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪsɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪsənɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of paying attention to sounds or speech; hearing attentively.
the act of hearing attentively

Examples

Active listening is an important skill for effective communication.
Good listening is essential when learning a new language.
She spent the afternoon listening to her favorite podcast.

Synset Examples

you can learn a lot by just listening
they make good music — you should give them a hearing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing - the faculty or act of perceiving sound

Etymology

From the verb 'listen', which comes from Old English 'hlysnan' (to listen, hear).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈlɪsənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪsənɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing) the state of a server process or network socket that is bound to a port and waiting to accept incoming network connections.
server/socket awaiting connections

Examples

The server remained listening on port 443 for incoming client connections.
After the crash, the administrator checked whether the listening socket had been re-established automatically.
You can use netstat to see all processes that are currently listening on network ports.

Synset Examples

You can use netstat to see all processes that are currently listening on network ports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awaiting connections - the condition of waiting for incoming network connections

Etymology

From the verb 'listen', from Old English 'hlysnan' (to listen, hear).
