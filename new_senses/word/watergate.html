Watergate

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɔt̬ərˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
a major U.S. political scandal in the early 1970s, involving abuse of power and other crimes, which led to the resignation of President Richard Nixon
1970s U.S. political scandal involving Nixon

Examples

Watergate remains a defining moment in U.S. political history.
Many documentaries have explored the events and consequences of Watergate.
The Watergate scandal changed Americans' trust in their government forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandal - an action or event regarded as morally or legally wrong and causing public outrage

Etymology

Named after the Watergate complex in Washington, D.C., where the break-in occurred.
