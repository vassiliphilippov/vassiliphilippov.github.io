integration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.təˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tɪˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of incorporating people from different racial or religious groups into a community or society as equals
process of including racial or religious groups

Examples

During the 1960s, integration became a major goal of the civil rights movement in the United States.
Many communities struggled with the challenges of integration after years of enforced segregation.
The school district's policy promoted integration by encouraging students from diverse backgrounds to learn together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: segregation - the act of keeping different racial or religious groups separate

Etymology

From Latin integratio, from integrare ‘to make whole’.

2. NOUN

combining into a whole

Examples

Successful cultural integration can enrich a society and promote harmony.
Technological integration is essential for modern businesses to stay competitive.
The integration of the two departments improved efficiency and communication.
