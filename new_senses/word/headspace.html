headspace

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the space left at the top of a container, such as a bottle or jar, after it has been filled and before it is sealed
space at top of filled container

Examples

Be sure to leave enough headspace when canning fruits to ensure a proper seal.
The headspace in the jar prevents spillage when the contents expand as they cool.
Too little headspace in a bottle can cause it to burst during fermentation.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhɛdˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the amount of mental space or cognitive bandwidth a person has for thinking, planning, or dealing with tasks and emotions; one’s current frame of mind.
mental space; cognitive bandwidth
informal

Examples

After two stressful weeks she booked a weekend away to regain some headspace.
The manager gave him a lighter workload so he'd have headspace to finish the proposal.
Students need quiet time between classes to process information and protect their headspace.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental bandwidth - the amount of cognitive capacity available for thinking or processing tasks
  • Synonym: headroom - figurative freedom or capacity to think or act; similar to headspace in non-technical use

Etymology

Compound of head + space; figurative use (mental ‘space’) developed from the literal sense of space, common from the late 20th century.
