shingle

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡl / (BrE)
to cover (a roof or building) with shingles.
cover with shingles (roofing)

Examples

It took the crew three days to completely shingle the cabin.
The workers will shingle the new house next week.
They decided to shingle over the old roof rather than replace it entirely.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A thin, flat piece of wood, slate, or other material, used in overlapping rows to cover the roofs or sides of buildings.
thin roofing or siding tile

Examples

Cedar shingles are popular for both roofing and siding.
The old cottage had a roof made of wooden shingles.
They replaced the damaged shingles after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tile - a thin slab of hard material used for covering roofs, floors, or walls

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a small signboard displayed outside the office of a professional such as a lawyer or doctor, indicating their name and occupation
a professional's signboard

Examples

After passing the bar, she hung out her shingle and started her own practice.
Doctors in the past would often nail a shingle beside their door to attract patients.
When he retired, he took down his shingle after forty years of service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signboard - a board displaying information or advertising outside a place

Etymology

Originally referred to a thin piece of wood; extended to mean a small signboard from the use of wooden shingles as signs.

4. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡl / (BrE)
a mass of small, rounded stones and pebbles, especially found as a covering on a beach
coarse beach gravel or pebbly beach

Examples

Many coastal plants thrive best in the shingle at the high tide mark.
The steep bank of shingle made it difficult to walk along the beach.
Waves crashed over the shingle, creating a soothing sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pebble - a small, smooth stone found on a beach or by a river
  • Degree Lower: sand - fine granular material found on beaches and deserts
  • Synonym: gravel - loose aggregation of small waterworn or pounded stones

Etymology

From Old English 'scingel', related to German 'Schindel' (shingle, roofing tile), though this sense is now distinct.
