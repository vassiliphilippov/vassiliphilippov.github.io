disruptive

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
Causing or tending to cause unrest, disorder, or disturbance; characterized by insubordination or interruption to normal activity.
causing disorder or disturbance

Examples

His disruptive behavior in class made it hard for others to concentrate.
Security was called after a disruptive incident broke out in the audience.
The protest turned disruptive, forcing the police to intervene and restore order.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - well-arranged, peaceful, or behaving in a controlled way
  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control; disorderly or disruptive
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion

Etymology

From Latin disruptus, past participle of disrumpere (‘to break apart’).

2. ADJ NEW

/ dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
(of an innovation, technology, product, or business model) causing major change in a market or industry by creating a new market, value network, or way of doing things that displaces established competitors.
causing market-changing innovation

Examples

Investors hailed the platform as a disruptive technology poised to transform the entire industry.
The startup's disruptive business model attracted venture capital despite its unconventional pricing strategy.
Policy makers debated how disruptive innovations might improve services while unsettling established providers.

Synset Examples

Investors hailed the platform as a disruptive technology poised to transform the entire industry.
The startup's disruptive business model attracted venture capital despite its unconventional pricing strategy.
Policy makers debated how disruptive innovations might improve services while unsettling established providers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovative - introducing new ideas, methods, or products
  • Synonym: disruptive innovation - a term in business describing an innovation that creates a new market and eventually displaces established ones
  • Antonym: incremental - making small, gradual changes rather than radically changing a market or product

Etymology

From Latin disruptus, past participle of disrumpere 'to break apart'; extended in business and technology from the late 20th century to describe innovations that break existing market structures.
