eyewash

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a liquid solution used to cleanse or rinse the eyes, typically to remove irritants or foreign particles
solution for rinsing the eyes
medical

Examples

Always check the expiration date on the eyewash before using it.
She used an eyewash to soothe her irritated eyes after swimming in the pool.
The laboratory kept bottles of eyewash near every workstation for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye lotion - a liquid preparation for cleaning the eyes

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈaɪˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a statement, action, or measure that is intended to placate or impress people but is superficial or insincere and lacks real substance or effect.
superficial or deceptive measure
informal

Examples

Many voters dismissed the committee's changes as eyewash rather than meaningful safety reform.
The CEO's apology felt like mere eyewash, a public relations move with no real consequences.
After the scandal, small regulatory updates seemed to be eyewash while problems remained unresolved.
