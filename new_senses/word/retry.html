to retry

1. VERB

/ riˈtraɪ / (AmE), / riˈtraɪ / (BrE)
to hear or try (a court case) again, especially after the original trial was invalidated or ended without a verdict
try a court case again
legal

Examples

After new evidence emerged, the court agreed to retry the matter.
Due to a mistrial, the judge decided to retry the case next month.
The prosecution announced plans to retry the defendant following the hung jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to examine and judge a legal case in court

Etymology

From re- (again) + try, in the sense of 'to try' a court case.

2. VERB NEW

/ riˈtraɪ / (AmE), / riˈtraɪ / (BrE)
(in computing) to attempt an operation again after it fails, often automatically and usually using a delay or backoff strategy between attempts.
re-attempt a failed operation (computing)

Examples

The API uses retry logic to recover from transient network errors.
When the upload fails, the client automatically retries up to three times with exponential backoff.
After the timeout, the service retried the database transaction and completed successfully.
