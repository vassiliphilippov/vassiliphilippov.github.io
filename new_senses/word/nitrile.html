nitrile

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtraɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtraɪl / (BrE)
a class of organic compounds containing the cyano group (–CN)
organic compound with a cyano group

Examples

Many pharmaceutical drugs include a nitrile group to enhance their biological activity.
Polyacrylonitrile is a common type of synthetic nitrile used in manufacturing fibers.
The chemist synthesized a new nitrile during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanide - an anion containing a carbon triple-bonded to a nitrogen atom

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrum' (sodium carbonate) plus '-ile', from modern chemistry nomenclature.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈnaɪtraɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtraɪl / (BrE)
a synthetic rubber material (acrylonitrile butadiene rubber) used for gloves, seals, hoses, and other items requiring oil and chemical resistance.
synthetic rubber used for gloves and seals

Examples

Hospitals switched to nitrile gloves for better chemical resistance and latex allergy safety.
The mechanic prefers nitrile gloves when handling solvents and greasy engine parts.
A damaged nitrile seal caused oil to leak from the pump during operation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrile rubber - a synthetic rubber (acrylonitrile butadiene rubber) resistant to oils and chemicals
  • Synonym: acrylonitrile butadiene rubber (NBR) - the industrial name for the copolymer commonly called nitrile rubber

Etymology

From the chemical name 'nitrile' (compounds with a –CN group); used as shorthand for nitrile rubber (acrylonitrile butadiene rubber).
