treasury

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
the funds or revenue of a government, institution, or individual
funds of a government or institution

Examples

Donations contributed significantly to the university's treasury.
The national treasury must be managed carefully to support public services.
The organization drew from its treasury to finance the new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funds - available money or resources

Etymology

From Old French *tresorie*, from Latin *thēsaurus* (treasure, storehouse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)

government department managing public finances

Examples

Public servants in the treasury work to ensure responsible management of national funds.
The treasury announced new measures to boost the economy this week.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
a room or building where money, valuable objects, or treasures are stored securely
a place for storing valuables or treasures

Examples

The ancient temple's treasury was guarded day and night.
The museum's treasury contains rare coins and priceless artifacts.
They discovered the royal treasury hidden beneath the palace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - a secure room for storing valuables
  • Synonym: safe - a strong container for storing valuables securely
