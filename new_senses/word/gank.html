gank

1. VERB

/ ɡæŋk / (AmE), / ɡaŋk / (BrE)
(slang) In video games, to kill or defeat another character with little effort, often by surprise or with overwhelming force.
(slang) kill or defeat easily, esp. in gaming
slang

Examples

He managed to gank three players in the enemy base before anyone noticed.
If you go out alone, you're likely to get ganked by roaming opponents.
Our team kept ganking their carries, making it easy to win the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - to attack by surprise, especially from a concealed position

Etymology

Originated in online gaming slang, possibly from a blend of 'gang' and 'yank.'

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɡæŋk / (AmE), / ɡaŋk / (BrE)
(in online gaming, slang) a sudden ambush or coordinated attack by opponents, intended to kill or overwhelm a lone or vulnerable player.
gaming ambush or raid
slang

Examples

The enemy's gank in the river surprised us and secured two quick kills.
During solo lane, a sudden gank forced him to use his teleport and retreat.
The coach warned the team to watch the river for early gank opportunities to secure an advantage.
