MFA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˌɛf ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˌef ˈɑː / (BrE)
an advanced university degree in fine arts, typically awarded after completing a postgraduate program in creative or performing arts
Master of Fine Arts degree

Examples

His MFA thesis exhibition attracted local art critics and collectors.
Many creative writing instructors hold an MFA in their field.
She earned her MFA in painting from a prestigious art school.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: PhD - Doctor of Philosophy, an advanced academic degree
  • Degree Lower: BA - Bachelor of Arts, an undergraduate degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of Master of Fine Arts.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌɛm ˌɛf ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˌɛf ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a security system that requires two or more independent verification factors (for example, a password plus a code) to confirm a user's identity.
multi-factor authentication (security)

Examples

The company mandated MFA to protect employee accounts after the recent phishing attacks.
When she logged in, the system requested a code because her MFA used an authenticator app.
Small businesses can improve security significantly by enabling MFA for customer and admin portals.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-factor authentication - a specific type of MFA that requires exactly two verification factors (often abbreviated 2FA)
