jewel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuː.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuː.əl / (BrE)
a precious or semiprecious stone that has been cut and polished for use in jewelry
precious or semiprecious stone in jewelry

Examples

Each jewel was carefully chosen for its color and clarity.
She wore a sparkling jewel set in a gold ring on her finger.
The crown is decorated with rare jewels from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a precious or semiprecious stone, especially when cut and polished or engraved

Etymology

From Old French 'jouel', from Medieval Latin 'jocale' (plaything, jewel), from Latin 'jocus' (play, pastime).
