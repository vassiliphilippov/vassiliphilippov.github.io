ionophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪoʊnəfəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪənəfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the movement of charged particles in a colloid under the influence of an electric field, where positive ions move toward the cathode and negative ions toward the anode
motion of ions in an electric field

Examples

Research into ionophoresis has led to improved methods for purifying colloidal suspensions.
Scientists used ionophoresis to separate the charged particles in the laboratory experiment.
The process of ionophoresis is important in various analytical chemistry techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrophoresis - the movement of suspended particles through a fluid under the influence of an electric field

Etymology

From Greek 'ion' (going, a particle) + 'phoresis' (carrying).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cca\u026ao\u028an\u0259f\u0259\u02c8ris\u026as / (AmE), / \u02cca\u026a\u0259n\u0259f\u0259\u02c8ri\u02d0s\u026as / (BrE)
(in medicine) a technique that uses a controlled electrical current to drive therapeutic ions or charged drug molecules through the skin for local or sometimes systemic treatment.
electrical delivery of ions/drugs through skin
medical

Examples

The clinic offered ionophoresis to reduce excessive sweating in affected patients.
She tried ionophoresis sessions to deliver anti-inflammatory medication through the damaged skin area.
After treatment with ionophoresis, his localized pain decreased and the topical drug penetrated better.
