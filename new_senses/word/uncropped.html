uncropped

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkrɑpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɒpt / (BrE)
(of land or soil) not used for growing crops; left without cultivation for crops.
not used for growing crops

Examples

After the harvest, the field remained uncropped for several seasons to restore its fertility.
The researchers compared the biodiversity of uncropped land with adjacent areas planted with wheat.
Water runoff is usually higher on uncropped soil than in areas where crops are planted.

Synset Examples

uncropped soil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cropped - used for growing crops or having had crops grown on it

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'cropped' (from 'crop,' to cultivate for plant harvest).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ʌnˈkrɑpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkrɒpt / (BrE)
(of a photograph or image) not cut or trimmed; remaining in its original frame, size, or composition.
not trimmed (image)

Examples

The photographer released an uncropped version of the portrait to show the original framing.
Please upload only uncropped photos so the editors can choose the best composition.
The magazine demanded the uncropped image because the cropped version removed important context.

From prefix un- + past participle 'cropped' (from crop, 'to cut or trim').
