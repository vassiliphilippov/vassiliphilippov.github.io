saliency

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪliənsi / (AmE), / ˈseɪliənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being particularly noticeable or important; prominence
quality of standing out; prominence

Examples

His speech had such saliency that it was remembered for years afterward.
Researchers studied the saliency of different features in the image to understand attention.
The saliency of the candidate's remarks attracted widespread media coverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salience - the state or quality of being salient
  • Antonym: obscurity - the quality of being difficult to see, not prominent

Etymology

From Latin 'salire' (to leap), via 'salient' (leaping, prominent).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈseɪliənsi / (AmE), / ˈseɪliənsi / (BrE)
(in machine learning and computer vision) a numerical measure or visualization that shows which parts of an input (for example, pixels in an image) most influence a model's output; often presented as a saliency map.
measure/visualization of input importance in AI

Examples

The model's saliency showed which parts of the photo influenced the classification most.
Researchers generated a heatmap to visualize the saliency of each pixel in the image.
Before trusting predictions, we check the saliency maps to ensure the model focuses on relevant features.
