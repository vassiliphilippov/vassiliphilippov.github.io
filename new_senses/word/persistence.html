persistence

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the quality of continuing steadily or repeatedly in an opinion, course of action, or task in spite of difficulty or opposition
continued effort despite difficulty

Examples

Despite numerous setbacks, his persistence in pursuing his dream career never faltered.
Her persistence paid off when she finally completed the marathon after months of training.
The persistence of the rain ruined their plans for an outdoor picnic.

Synset Examples

his perseveration continued to the point where it was no longer appropriate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perseverance - continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties, failure, or opposition
  • Synonym: determination - firmness of purpose; resoluteness
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin persistentia, from persistere (to persist).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / pəˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
The quality of continuing steadily or firmly in a course of action despite difficulty or opposition.
firm or continued determination

Examples

Despite many obstacles, his persistence never wavered.
Her persistence eventually paid off when she secured her dream job.
The athlete's persistence inspired the entire team to work harder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perseverance - steadfastness in doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success
  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being firm in carrying out a purpose
  • Antonym: resignation - acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable

Etymology

From Latin persistentia (steadfastness), from persistere (to abide, to continue steadfastly).
