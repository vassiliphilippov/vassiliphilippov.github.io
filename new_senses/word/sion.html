Sion

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ən / (BrE)
An imaginary place considered to be perfect or ideal, often symbolizing peace, happiness, or spiritual perfection.
imaginary perfect place; ideal state

Examples

Many poets have dreamed of Sion, a land untouched by war or suffering.
She yearned for a personal Sion where peace and justice would reign.
The philosopher described his ideal society as a modern Sion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect place or state of things
  • Synonym: paradise - an ideal or perfect place or state

Etymology

Variant of 'Zion', from Hebrew *Ṣiyyôn*, originally referring to a hill in Jerusalem; later symbolic of utopia or paradise.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A hill in Jerusalem that was originally a stronghold captured by King David, later the site of the Temple, and by extension a synonym for Jerusalem itself.
hill in Jerusalem; name for Jerusalem

Examples

In biblical poetry, Sion is frequently praised as the city of God.
Pilgrims traveled to Sion to see the ancient sites of Jerusalem.
The ruins on Sion remind visitors of the city's storied past.

Synset Examples

the inhabitants of Jerusalem are personified as ‘the daughter of Zion’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zion - an alternative spelling and synonym for Sion, often used metaphorically for Jerusalem or the Jewish people

Etymology

From Hebrew *Tsiyyon*, name of the biblical hill; via Latin *Sion*, Greek *Siōn*.
