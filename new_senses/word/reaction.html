reaction

1. NOUN

/ riˈækʃən / (AmE), / riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a bodily process or response that occurs as a result of an external stimulus, such as a drug, allergen, or other agent
bodily response to a stimulus

Examples

He suffered a mild reaction to the vaccine, including redness and swelling.
Her skin showed an allergic reaction to the new soap.
The patient developed a severe reaction after receiving penicillin.

Synset Examples

a bad reaction to the medicine
his responses have slowed with age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: response - any reaction to a stimulus

Etymology

From Latin 'reactio', from 'reagere' meaning 'to act back'.

/ riˈækʃən / (AmE), / riˈækʃən / (BrE)

4. NOUN

/ riˈækʃən / (AmE), / riˈækʃən / (BrE)
a process in which one or more substances undergo a chemical change to form different substances

Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: decomposition - a reaction where a compound breaks into simpler substances

5. NOUN

/ riˈækʃən / (AmE), / riˈækʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The equal and opposite force that occurs in response to an applied force, as defined by Newton's Third Law of Motion.
equal and opposite physical force

Examples

According to Newton's laws, every reaction is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction to the action.
Engineers must consider the reaction force when designing bridges and supports.
The table exerts an upward reaction force to support the book's weight.

Synset Examples

every action has an equal and opposite reaction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: action - the initial force applied to a body, in contrast to 'reaction' in mechanics

Etymology

From Latin 'reactio' (a doing again), from 're-' (again) + 'agere' (to act).
