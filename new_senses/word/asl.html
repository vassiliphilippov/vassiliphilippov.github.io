ASL

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌɛs ˈɛl / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌɛs ˈɛl / (BrE)
American Sign Language, the primary sign language used by the deaf community in the United States and parts of Canada.
American Sign Language

Examples

Many deaf people in the United States communicate using ASL.
She has been studying ASL for three years to become an interpreter.
The presentation was simultaneously translated into ASL for the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American Sign Language - the main sign language of the United States, often abbreviated as ASL

Etymology

Abbreviation of American Sign Language.
