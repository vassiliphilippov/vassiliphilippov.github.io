faultfinding

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
inclined to criticize others, especially on moral or personal grounds
inclined to criticize others

Examples

A good leader avoids being faultfinding and instead encourages positive change.
His faultfinding attitude made it difficult for others to enjoy working with him.
She tried to ignore her colleague's faultfinding remarks during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - inclined to find fault or express disapproval
  • Antonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow the existence of opinions or behavior that one does not necessarily agree with

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the habit of continually finding small or unjustified faults or defects in others or things; persistent petty criticism
habit of persistent, petty criticism

Examples

He was criticized for his faultfinding attitude rather than offering constructive feedback.
Her constant faultfinding made it difficult to enjoy working with her.
The manager's faultfinding discouraged new ideas within the team.

Related Terms
