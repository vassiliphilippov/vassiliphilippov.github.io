moderation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of making something less severe or intense; the reduction in degree or extent of something
reducing severity or intensity

Examples

Effective moderation of noise can greatly improve the quality of urban life.
In times of crisis, the government may pursue the moderation of harsh economic measures.
The therapy focuses on the moderation of anxiety to help patients cope better.

Synset Examples

the object being control or moderation of economic depressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitigation - the action of making something less severe
  • Antonym: exacerbation - the process of making something more severe

Etymology

From Latin moderatio (guidance, self-control), from moderari (to moderate, control).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of keeping within reasonable limits; avoidance of excess or extremes, especially in one's behavior or habits
the quality of avoiding excesses

Examples

Even during celebrations, he advised moderation rather than overindulgence.
He believed that moderation in all things was the key to a healthy life.
Practicing moderation helped her maintain a balanced diet and regular exercise routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - the action or trait of holding back or controlling oneself
  • Antonym: excess - an amount or behavior that goes beyond what is allowed or usual

Etymology

From Latin moderatio (restraint, guidance), from moderari (to control, regulate).

Related Terms
