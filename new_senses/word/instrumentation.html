instrumentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.strə.men.təˈʃeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strə.men.təˈʃeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the instruments specified in a musical score or arrangement for a band or orchestra
instruments specified in a musical score

Examples

The instrumentation for the musical included brass, woodwinds, and a full string section.
The composer's innovative instrumentation gave the piece a unique and modern sound.
This symphony is known for its rich instrumentation and bold use of percussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestration - the arrangement or scoring of music for an orchestra

Etymology

From French 'instrumentation', from 'instrument'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəməntəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊmənteɪˈʃən / (BrE)
the process of arranging a piece of music for different instruments in an orchestra and assigning specific parts to each
musical arrangement for instruments

Examples

She studied instrumentation to better understand how to arrange music for various ensembles.
The choice of instrumentation in this piece highlights the woodwinds and brass.
The composer's innovative instrumentation gave the symphony a unique and vibrant sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestration - the arrangement of a musical composition for performance by an orchestra

Etymology

Formed from 'instrument' and the suffix '-ation', meaning the action or process, in the context of music arrangement.
