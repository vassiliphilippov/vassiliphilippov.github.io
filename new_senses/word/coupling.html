coupling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌplɪŋ / (BrE)
A device or mechanism that connects two things so they move together or transmit force, especially in machinery.
device that connects two things mechanically

Examples

A flexible coupling is used to absorb vibrations between connected machines.
The engineer installed a new coupling to link the two shafts together.
When the coupling broke, the train cars separated unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connector - device for joining electrical or mechanical parts
  • Synonym: fastener - device used to hold two objects together

Etymology

From the verb 'couple', from Old French *coupler*, from Latin *copulare* (to join together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌplɪŋ / (BrE)
a mechanical device used to connect the ends of two adjacent objects, such as shafts or pipes, so that they move or function together
mechanical device for connecting two objects

Examples

A faulty coupling caused the machinery to vibrate excessively during operation.
The engineer installed a new coupling to connect the two sections of the pipe.
The technician checked the coupling between the engine and the drive shaft for any signs of wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connector - a device for joining electrical circuits or components together

Etymology

From the verb 'couple' (Middle English, from Old French 'coupler', from Latin 'copulare', meaning 'to join').
