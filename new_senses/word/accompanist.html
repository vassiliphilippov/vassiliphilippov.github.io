accompanist

1. NOUN

/ əˈkʌmpənɪst / (AmE), / əˈkʌmpənɪst / (BrE)
a musician, especially a pianist, who plays music to support a singer or another performer
musician who provides accompaniment

Examples

She worked as an accompanist for the school choir during their performances.
The talented accompanist followed the singer perfectly throughout the concert.
Without the help of the accompanist, the soloist would have felt lost on stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soloist - a person who performs a musical solo

Etymology

From 'accompany' + '-ist', indicating one who performs accompaniment.
