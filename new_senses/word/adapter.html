adapter

1. NOUN

/ əˈdæptər / (AmE), / əˈdæptə / (BrE)
a musician who adapts a composition for particular voices or instruments or for another style of performance
musician who arranges compositions

Examples

As an adapter, she skillfully transformed classical songs for modern instruments.
Each Broadway revival relies on a talented adapter to update the score.
The adapter turned the symphony into a piece for jazz trio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arranger - a person who arranges music for performance

Etymology

From adapt + -er, meaning 'one who adapts'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdæptər / (AmE), / əˈdæptə / (BrE)
a device that enables one piece of equipment to be used with another that would otherwise be incompatible, or allows something to be used for a different purpose than originally intended.
device for connecting incompatible equipment

Examples

He plugged his laptop charger into the wall with an adapter he bought at the airport.
The camera's memory card required a special adapter to fit into the computer.
You will need a power adapter to use this device in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convertor - a device that changes something into a different form
  • Synonym: connector - a device for joining electrical circuits together

Etymology

From Latin 'adaptare' (to fit to), via French 'adapter'.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN NEW

/ əˈdæptər / (AmE), / əˈdæptə / (BrE)
(in molecular biology and DNA sequencing) a short DNA or RNA sequence that is attached to DNA fragments so they can be amplified, indexed, and bound to a sequencing platform during library preparation.
short DNA/RNA sequence for sequencing

Examples

The lab technician ligated sequencing adapters to the DNA fragments before PCR amplification.
To identify samples, the team added unique barcode adapters during library preparation.
She explained that a damaged adapter can prevent fragments from binding to the flow cell.
