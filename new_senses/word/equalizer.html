equalizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a weight or device used to balance or counteract another weight, especially to maintain equilibrium
device for balancing weight

Examples

A heavy equalizer was suspended from the other end of the beam to counter the load.
The mechanic added an equalizer to ensure the system remained balanced during operation.
Without an equalizer, the weight distribution would be uneven and potentially dangerous.

Etymology

From 'equal' + '-izer', meaning something that makes equal.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a goal or point that makes the score equal in a game or competition
a score that evens the match

Examples

He scored the equalizer just before halftime, bringing the crowd to its feet.
The team's hopes were revived when they got a late equalizer.
With only minutes left, she struck the equalizer to make it 2-2.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - a goal or point that puts a team or player ahead

Etymology

From 'equalize' + '-er'. 'Equalize' from Latin 'aequalis' (equal).

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪzə / (BrE)
A device or piece of electronic equipment that adjusts or reduces frequency distortion to achieve a balanced audio output.
audio device adjusting frequency balance

Examples

Adjusting the equalizer improved the clarity of the music in the car.
He bought a separate equalizer to reduce distortion in his home theater.
The new sound system includes an advanced equalizer for customizing audio settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - device or process that removes certain frequencies from a signal

Etymology

From 'equalize' + '-er', indicating something that makes levels equal.
