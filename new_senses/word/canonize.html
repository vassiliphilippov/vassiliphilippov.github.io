to canonize

1. VERB

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkænənaɪz / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) to officially declare a dead person to be a saint.
declare someone a saint

Examples

It often takes many years for the Vatican to canonize someone after their death.
Many believed he would eventually be canonized for his extraordinary acts of charity.
The church decided to canonize the nun after a lengthy investigation into her life and miracles.

Synset Examples

After he was shown to have performed a miracle, the priest was canonized

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beatify - declare a deceased person to be blessed, a step before sainthood in Catholicism
  • Synonym: sanctify - make holy or set apart as sacred

Etymology

From Old French canoniser, from Late Latin canonizare, from canon (rule, standard)

2. VERB

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkænənaɪz / (BrE)
to officially declare (someone) to be a saint, especially by the Roman Catholic Church; to treat or regard someone as being above criticism or as a perfect example
declare a saint; treat as holy

Examples

He tends to canonize his mentors, overlooking their flaws.
Some critics canonize authors whose books profoundly changed literature.
The church canonized Mother Teresa for her charitable work.

Synset Examples

He canonizes women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctify - to make holy or to treat as holy

Etymology

From Late Latin *canonizare*, from Greek *kanon* (rule, standard).

3. VERB NEW

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkænənaɪz / (BrE)
(in fiction and media) to declare a story, character, event, or element to be part of the official canon of a fictional universe.
declare part of official fiction canon

Examples

The studio decided to canonize the spin-off as part of the franchise's official storyline this season.
Fans argued that the author had privately canonized the short story by mentioning its events later.
After months of debate, the writers' room began canonizing earlier episodes to maintain continuity.

Synset Examples

The studio decided to canonize the spin-off as part of the franchise's official storyline this season.
Fans argued that the author had privately canonized the short story by mentioning its events later.
After months of debate, the writers' room began canonizing earlier episodes to maintain continuity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canon - an accepted set of works, events, or details regarded as official within a fictional universe.
  • Antonym: decanonize - to remove or declare something not part of the official canon; opposite of canonize.

Etymology

From Late Latin canonizare, from canon (rule, standard).
