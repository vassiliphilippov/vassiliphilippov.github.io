valuator

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæljuˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈvæljuːeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who officially estimates the worth, value, or quality of something, especially for legal or financial purposes
person who estimates value officially

Examples

As an experienced valuator, she specializes in antique jewelry.
Companies hire a valuator to determine the fair market value of their assets.
The bank sent a valuator to inspect the commercial property before approving the loan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraiser - a person who assesses the value of something, especially property
  • Synonym: assessor - an official who evaluates property for taxation

Etymology

From 'value' + '-ator', formed on the model of 'valuator' in Latin-based words.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvæljuˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈvæljuːeɪtə / (BrE)
a software program or online tool that automatically estimates the monetary value of an asset or property using algorithms and available data.
automated valuation tool

Examples

The online valuator estimated the antique necklace's worth in minutes.
Real estate firms run multiple automated valuators to generate quick price ranges for listings.
Before selling, she used a free valuator to check the car's current market price.

Synset Examples
