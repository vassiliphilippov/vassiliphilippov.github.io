reducer

1. NOUN

/ rəˈdusɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
A fitting used to join two pipes of different diameters by reducing the size from a larger to a smaller bore.
pipe fitting joining different diameters

Examples

A metal reducer was used to adapt the old system to the new, narrower piping.
The plumber installed a reducer to connect the large main pipe to the smaller branch pipe.
When joining pipes of different sizes, a reducer ensures a secure fit and smooth water flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adapter - fitting used to connect pipes of different types or sizes

Etymology

From 'reduce', meaning to make smaller, with the agentive '-er' suffix.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdusər / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
A substance that causes the reduction of another substance by being oxidized itself, especially used in photography to decrease the density of a negative or print.
substance causing reduction (esp. in chemistry/photography)

Examples

A common reducer in chemistry is hydrogen, which donates electrons to other substances.
The developer and the reducer were mixed separately before adding them to the photographic plate.
Using too much reducer can make your photographs appear too light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reducing agent - a substance that brings about reduction by losing electrons (chemistry)

Etymology

From reduce + -er, meaning 'one that reduces'.

function that combines values into one

Examples

The Hadoop job used a custom reducer to sum all sales values for each product id.
In Redux, every reducer receives the current state and an action, then returns new state.
