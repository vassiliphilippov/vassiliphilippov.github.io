shingling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of laying shingles, especially on a roof or wall
the laying of shingles

Examples

He learned the art of shingling from his grandfather, who was a skilled roofer.
Proper shingling ensures that the house is protected from leaks and weather damage.
The shingling of the new roof was completed in just two days.

Synset Examples

shingling is a craft very different from carpentry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roofing - the act or process of putting a roof on a building

Etymology

From 'shingle' (thin piece of building material), with suffix '-ing' indicating the action or process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
(Geology) Sediment consisting of flat pebbles that are uniformly tilted in the same direction.
sediment with uniformly tilted flat pebbles

Examples

Evidence of shingling was found in the ancient delta sediments, revealing the direction of the paleocurrents.
The geologist noted clear shingling in the riverbed deposits, with all the pebbles slanting eastward.
The presence of shingling can indicate water flow patterns during the time of sediment deposition.
