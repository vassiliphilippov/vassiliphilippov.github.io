dunk

1. VERB

/ dʌŋk / (AmE), / dʌŋk / (BrE)
to dip something quickly into a liquid, especially so as to wet, coat, or saturate it
briefly dip into liquid

Examples

Don't forget to dunk the vegetables in ice water to keep them crisp.
He quickly dunked the dirty brush in water to rinse it off.
She likes to dunk her cookies in milk before eating them.

Synset Examples

dip the garment into the cleaning solution
dip the brush into the paint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dip - to put something briefly into a liquid and remove it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01579924-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dʌŋk / (AmE), / dʌŋk / (BrE)
A shot in basketball in which the ball is thrust downwards into the basket with one or both hands above the rim.
downward basketball shot into the basket

Examples

He wowed the crowd with a spectacular dunk in the final seconds of the game.
Her first dunk during practice amazed her teammates.
The highlight reel featured an impressive dunk over two defenders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam dunk - a forceful dunk shot in basketball
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00111374-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dʌŋk / (AmE), / dʌŋk / (BrE)
to dip food quickly into a liquid, typically while eating
dip food into a liquid

Examples

He loves to dunk his cookies in milk before eating them.
She dunked the bread into her soup and took a bite.
You shouldn't dunk your doughnut for too long or it will fall apart.

Synset Examples

She dunked the piece of bread in the sauce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dip - to put something into a liquid and quickly take it out again

Etymology

Originated in US English in the early 20th century, perhaps imitative or related to 'dunk' meaning 'to submerge briefly'.

Examples
