preservation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of protecting something from harm, damage, or loss
act of protecting from harm or loss

Examples

Food preservation techniques help prevent spoilage and waste.
Strict laws have been enacted for the preservation of endangered species.
The preservation of ancient monuments is important for cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservation - the act of preventing something from being lost, wasted, damaged, or destroyed
  • Antonym: destruction - the act of damaging something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be used or repaired

Etymology

From Latin 'praeservatio' (act of guarding beforehand), from 'prae-' (before) + 'servare' (to keep).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of keeping something in its original state or preventing it from being damaged, lost, or changed.
the act of keeping something safe or unchanged

Examples

Medical advances have greatly improved the preservation of organs for transplantation.
Proper preservation of food ensures it stays fresh for longer periods.
The preservation of historic buildings is important for cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservation - the act of protecting something, especially the environment, from harm
  • Antonym: destruction - the act of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Etymology

From Latin 'praeservatio' (a keeping beforehand), from 'praeservare' (to guard or save beforehand).
