curate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊr.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjʊər.ət / (BrE)
a member of the clergy, especially in the Anglican Church, who assists a vicar or rector in the performance of religious duties
assistant clergyman in the Anglican Church

Examples

As a curate, she assisted the vicar with pastoral duties in the parish.
The curate organized a charity event to support local families in need.
The new curate was introduced to the congregation during Sunday service.

Synset Examples

clergymen are usually called ministers in Protestant churches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicar - a representative or deputy of a bishop or parish priest
  • Synonym: rector - a clergyman in charge of a parish in the Anglican Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'curatus', from Latin 'cura' (care), referring to the care of souls.

2. VERB NEW

/ kjʊˈreɪt / (AmE), / kjʊˈreɪt / (BrE)
to select, organize, and present items or content (such as artworks, articles, or playlists) as a carefully arranged collection for an audience.
select and organize content

Examples

She curates a weekly playlist of indie tracks on Spotify.
The museum director plans to curate an exhibition of local artists next summer.
He has curated a selection of articles for the course reading list this term.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to choose carefully from a number of alternatives
  • Synonym: organize - to arrange or put in order for a particular purpose or use

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'curator' / earlier 'curate', ultimately from Latin 'cura' (care).
