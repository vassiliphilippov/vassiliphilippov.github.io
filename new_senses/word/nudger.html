nudger

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈnʌdʒə / (BrE)
a person who nudges someone else, typically by giving a gentle push to get their attention or encourage an action
someone who nudges others

Examples

He can be a persistent nudger if you hesitate to make a decision.
She acted as a gentle nudger to remind the group of their goals.
The alarm clock is my daily nudger to get out of bed on time.

Synset Examples

he needs a regular nudger to keep him awake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudge - to push someone or something gently, especially with your elbow to attract their attention

Etymology

From 'nudge' (verb) + '-er' (agentive suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈnʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈnʌdʒə / (BrE)
A person who designs, implements, or applies behavioural 'nudges' — subtle changes to choice architecture or defaults — to influence people’s decisions, especially in digital products or public policy.
designer or applier of behavioural nudges

Examples

The product team hired a nudger to redesign signup defaults and increase user completions.
As a nudger, she tests small interface changes to gently steer users toward healthier options.
Government nudgers recommend default enrollment in pension plans to boost long-term savings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice architect - a person who designs environments or options to influence people's decisions
  • Synonym: behavioral designer - someone who applies psychological insights to shape user behaviour and product decisions
