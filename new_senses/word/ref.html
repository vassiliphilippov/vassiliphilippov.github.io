ref

1. NOUN

/ rɛf / (AmE), / rɛf / (BrE)
(informal) the chief official in a sporting event, responsible for enforcing the rules and ensuring fair play.
chief official in a sport (informal)
informal

Examples

After the match, the losing team blamed the ref for their defeat.
Every ref knows how hard it is to make the right call under pressure.
The players argued for a penalty, but the ref refused to stop the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referee - the chief official in sports, ensuring fair play
  • Synonym: umpire - an official who watches a game to enforce the rules (mainly used in cricket, baseball, etc.)

Etymology

Shortened form of 'referee'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɛf / (AmE), / rɛf / (BrE)
(in programming) a keyword or annotation (e.g., in C#) indicating that a function parameter is passed by reference so the function can modify the caller's variable.
programming keyword for pass-by-reference

Examples

In C#, declare a parameter with ref when you want the method to modify it directly.
She changed the tutorial to explain how the ref keyword differs from 'out' parameters in C#.
During code review, he warned that passing large structs by ref can improve performance but be error-prone.

  • Synonym: pass-by-reference - a parameter-passing mechanism where a function receives a reference to the caller's variable, allowing modification.

Etymology

Abbreviation of "reference," used in programming to mean pass-by-reference.
