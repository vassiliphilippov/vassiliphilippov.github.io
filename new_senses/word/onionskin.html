onionskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌnjənˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌnjənˌskɪn / (BrE)
a thin, strong, lightweight translucent paper, often used for making carbon copies or lightweight correspondence
thin, strong translucent paper

Examples

For archival letters, the secretary preferred onionskin because it was so light and durable.
He crumpled the onionskin sheet and tossed it into the wastebasket.
The old typewriter carbon copy is still preserved on faded onionskin paper.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in appearance and texture to the thin outer skin of an onion.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈʌnjənˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌnjənˌskɪn / (BrE)
(in animation and digital graphics) a display feature that shows semi-transparent images of previous or next frames over the current frame to help align motion and drawing.
animation overlay of previous frames

Examples

To align the next frame, the animator uses onionskin to view prior frames.
Many animation programs let you adjust onionskin opacity to see several faint frames behind the current drawing.
During the tutorial she recommended turning on onionskin to check motion and timing between frames.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onion-skinning - the technique of displaying semi-transparent previous or next frames in animation software
  • Synonym: overlay - a semi-transparent image or layer shown on top of another image

Etymology

Named by analogy with onionskin paper (thin, translucent paper); the translucent overlays in animation resemble onion skin.
