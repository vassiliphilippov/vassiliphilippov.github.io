reboot

1. VERB

/ riˈbut / (AmE), / riːˈbuːt / (BrE)
to cause a computer or other device to load its operating system and start afresh, typically by restarting it
restart a computer or device

Examples

He had to reboot the server to apply the new configuration settings.
If your phone freezes, try to reboot it to fix the problem.
The technician advised me to reboot my computer after installing the updates.

Synset Examples

boot your computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restart - to start something again, especially a machine or device

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'boot', relating to 'bootstrap' (to start up a computer).

2. NOUN NEW

/ riˈbut / (AmE), / riːˈbuːt / (BrE)
a new film, television series, game, or other media that restarts a franchise or story universe, often reimagining characters, plot, or continuity.
restart of a media franchise

Examples

The 2005 Batman reboot relaunched the franchise with a darker, more realistic tone.
After weak ratings, the studio ordered a reboot of the series to attract a new audience.
Fans argued that the director's reboot ignored previous continuity and changed key character motivations.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaunch - to start a product, series, or franchise again, often with a new marketing push or changes to reach a wider audience.
  • Synonym: remake - a new version of an earlier film or show that retells the same story; related but often preserves continuity differently from a reboot.

Etymology

From the verb 'reboot' (re- + boot), originally a computing term meaning 'start again'; later extended metaphorically to mean restarting a media franchise.
