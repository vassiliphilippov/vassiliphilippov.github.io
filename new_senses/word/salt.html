salt

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt / (AmE), / sɒlt / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed when the hydrogen of an acid is replaced by a metal or a radical that behaves like a metal
chemical compound from acid and metal

Examples

Chemists study how acids and bases combine to create different kinds of salts.
Table salt is the most commonly used seasoning in cooking.
The reaction of hydrochloric acid with sodium hydroxide produces a salt and water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: base - a substance that reacts with acids to form salts

Etymology

From Old French *sel*, from Latin *sal* (salt).

random value added before hashing

Examples

Systems store password hashes with a unique salt per user to improve security.
The developer accidentally reused the same salt, making multiple accounts vulnerable to attack.
Modern frameworks generate random salts automatically when creating password hashes for new users.
