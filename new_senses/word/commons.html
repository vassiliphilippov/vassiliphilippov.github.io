Commons

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmənz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənz / (BrE)
the common people, especially as distinguished from the aristocracy or ruling classes; often used in historical or political contexts
the common people

Examples

In medieval times, the Commons often struggled against the power of the nobility.
Reforms were demanded by the Commons, seeking greater rights and freedoms.
The House of Commons was originally the representative assembly of the common people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, especially those holding hereditary titles

Etymology

From Medieval Latin communis (common), referring to the people as a collective body.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈkɑːmənz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənz / (BrE)
resources, knowledge, or content that are shared and freely accessible to a community, especially digital materials made available under open licenses.
shared digital or community resources

Examples

Scientists publish data to the Commons to ensure open access for all.
The university library launched a new repository to store teaching materials in the Commons.
Volunteers regularly add historical documents and photos to the local digital Commons for researchers.

Synset Examples

Scientists publish data to the Commons to ensure open access for all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open access - free, immediate online access to research outputs and other materials
  • Synonym: public good - a resource or service available to all members of a community
  • Antonym: private property - resources owned and controlled by individuals or organizations rather than shared by the community

Etymology

From Medieval Latin communis (common); extended to mean resources held in common by a community.
