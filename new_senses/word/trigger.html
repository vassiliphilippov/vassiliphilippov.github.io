trigger

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
to release or pull the trigger of a gun or other device, causing it to discharge or operate
pull the trigger of a gun or device

Examples

She accidentally triggered the alarm by pulling the safety lever.
The officer triggered the rifle during the training exercise.
You should never trigger a weapon unless you are certain it is safe.

Synset Examples

Trigger a gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to discharge a gun or other firearm

Etymology

From the noun 'trigger', itself from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull).

/ ˈtrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
A small lever that is pressed to activate the firing mechanism of a gun or weapon.
lever that fires a gun

Examples

Always keep your finger off the trigger until you are ready to shoot.
He squeezed the trigger and the gun fired.
The detective examined the trigger for fingerprints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to exert a force
  • Antonym: safety catch - a device that prevents accidental firing

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull) or Middle Dutch 'trecken', relating to the idea of pulling.
