retraining

1. NOUN

/ riˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of learning new skills or being trained for a different job or occupation
training for a new job or occupation

Examples

After being laid off, he started retraining as a computer programmer.
Many companies offer retraining programs to help employees move into new roles.
The need for retraining increases as industries evolve and technology changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: training - the process of learning skills for a particular job or activity
  • Synonym: reskilling - the process of learning new skills to do a different job

Etymology

Formed from 're-' (again) + 'training'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ riˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
(in machine learning) the process of training an existing model again on new or updated data so it adapts to changed patterns, maintains accuracy, or remedies concept drift.
updating a machine-learning model

Examples

After detecting performance drop, the team scheduled retraining of the model with fresh user data.
Automated retraining pipelines run daily to prevent concept drift in production recommendation systems.
They decreased batch size during retraining to help the preexisting model learn new patterns faster.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fine-tuning - adjusting a pretrained model by further training, often on a smaller dataset, to improve task-specific performance
  • Synonym: model update - the process of changing a deployed model, often by retraining, to reflect new data or requirements
