doge

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊdʒ / (BrE)
The chief magistrate and leader in the former republics of Venice and Genoa.
historical Venetian or Genoese leader

Examples

After centuries of rule, the office of doge was finally abolished by Napoleon.
During the festivities, the doge would appear in his elaborate robes.
The doge was elected for life by the aristocracy of Venice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duke - a nobleman of high rank
  • Synonym: magistrate - a civil officer or judge with the authority to administer the law

Etymology

From Italian 'doge', from Venetian dialect, ultimately from Latin 'dux' (leader, duke).
