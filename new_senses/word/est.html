EST

1. NOUN

/ i ɛs ti / (AmE), / iː ɛs tiː / (BrE)
abbreviation for Eastern Standard Time, the standard time in the eastern part of the United States, five hours behind Greenwich Mean Time (GMT−5)
Eastern Standard Time (U.S. time zone)
american

Examples

Please note that our customer service hours are 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. EST.
Television shows often list their times in EST to avoid confusion across time zones.
The conference call is scheduled for 2:00 p.m. EST on Friday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: EDT - Eastern Daylight Time; daylight saving time for the same zone

Etymology

Abbreviation of Eastern Standard Time.

2. NOUN NEW

/ i \u025bs ti / (AmE), / i\u02d0 \u025bs ti\u02d0 / (BrE)
Abbreviation of Electronic Sell-Through: a digital purchase model in which consumers buy and permanently own a copy of digital media (for example, a film or album).
digital buy-to-own (media)

Examples

The studio reported strong EST sales after releasing the movie on digital storefronts.
Consumers prefer EST when they want to permanently own a downloaded film or TV show.
Many platforms offer movies both for rent and as EST purchases to suit different customers.

  • Synonym: digital download - a downloaded copy of digital media that a consumer can purchase and keep

Etymology

Abbreviation of Electronic Sell-Through, formed from 'electronic' + 'sell-through'.
