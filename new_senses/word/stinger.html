2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋə / (BrE)
a sharp, pointed organ found in certain animals such as wasps, stingrays, or scorpions, often connected to a poison gland and used for offense or defense
sharp organ in animals for stinging

Examples

After the bee lost its stinger, it could no longer defend itself.
Some jellyfish have a venomous stinger that can be very painful to humans.
The scorpion raised its tail, ready to strike with its stinger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - the act of wounding with a sharp-pointed organ

Etymology

From 'sting' + '-er', meaning one that stings.
