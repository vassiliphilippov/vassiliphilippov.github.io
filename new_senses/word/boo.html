boo

1. NOUN

/ buː / (AmE), / buː / (BrE)
a loud noise or cry made to show disapproval or contempt, especially from a group of people at a public event
cry to express disapproval

Examples

He let out a loud boo when the speaker made a controversial remark.
Several boos could be heard from the back of the theater when the movie ended abruptly.
The crowd erupted in boos as the referee announced his decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheer - a shout of approval or encouragement

2. VERB

/ buː / (AmE), / buː / (BrE)
to show disapproval by making a loud, deep noise, often during a performance or public event
express disapproval with a vocal sound

Examples

If you don't like the performance, please don't boo; just remain quiet.
Several fans booed the referee when he made the controversial call.
The audience began to boo loudly after the comedian's offensive joke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheer - to show approval by shouting or applauding

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself; used as an exclamation since at least the 18th century.
