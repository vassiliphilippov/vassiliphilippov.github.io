ingress

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡrɛs / (BrE)
the act or process of entering a place or entering in; the right or permission to enter
the act of entering

Examples

Authorized personnel only are allowed ingress to the restricted area.
Stormwater ingress damaged several buildings after the heavy rainfall.
The company installed security cameras to monitor all points of ingress.

Synset Examples

she made a grand entrance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of going or coming in
  • Antonym: egress - the act of going out or leaving a place

Etymology

From Latin 'ingressus' (an entering), from 'ingredi' (to enter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡrɛs / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The disappearance of a celestial body, such as a star or planet, as it moves behind another object, especially at the beginning of an eclipse or occultation.
disappearance of a celestial body before an eclipse

Examples

Accurate measurement of a star's ingress is critical in studying binary systems.
The ingress of the moon was closely observed by astronomers during the rare eclipse.
Timing the ingress of the planet helped scientists refine their orbital calculations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: egress - reappearance of a celestial body after an eclipse

Etymology

From Latin ingressus (an entry), from ingredi (to go in).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɪnˌɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡrɛs / (BrE)
(computing) A configuration, resource, or service that defines rules for routing external network traffic into internal services or applications (commonly used in Kubernetes and cloud environments).
entry point for external traffic (computing)

Examples

The cluster uses an ingress controller to route external requests to internal services.
After updating the ingress, external requests started reaching the new service correctly.
They configured TLS termination on the ingress to secure traffic entering the cluster.
