reporter

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to investigate and write or present news stories for newspapers, television, or radio.
person who reports news stories

Examples

As a crime reporter, she often worked late into the night.
Television viewers rely on the reporter for accurate and timely information.
The reporter asked several tough questions during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journalist - a person who writes for newspapers, magazines, or news websites

Etymology

From Latin 'reportare' (to bring back), via Old French 'reporteur' (narrator).

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
(in molecular biology) a gene or genetic construct that produces an easily measured signal (for example, a fluorescent protein or enzyme) used to indicate the activity or expression of another gene or regulatory sequence.
gene or construct indicating gene activity

Examples

Researchers inserted a GFP reporter to monitor promoter activity in live cells.
The lab compared several reporter constructs to find the most sensitive indicator of gene expression.
Using a luciferase reporter allowed them to measure expression with simple light readings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reporter gene - a gene attached to a regulatory sequence to indicate its activity by producing a measurable signal
  • Synonym: marker gene - a gene used to identify or indicate a particular genetic event or expression
