urn

1. NOUN

/ ɝn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
A large decorative vase, typically with a pedestal or feet, used for ornamental or ceremonial purposes.
large decorative vase with pedestal or feet

Examples

She placed a bouquet of flowers in the marble urn on the terrace.
The museum displayed an ancient Greek urn in the center of the hall.
The ornate urn stood atop a pedestal in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vase - an open container used for holding flowers or for decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'urna' (vessel, jar, urn).

3. NOUN NEW

/ juː ɑr ɛn / (AmE), / juː ɑː ɛn / (BrE)
A Uniform Resource Name: a persistent, location-independent identifier for a digital resource (a type of URI).
persistent digital resource identifier (URN)

Examples

The database assigns a URN to each digital object to enable stable citation.
Researchers prefer citing the dataset's URN because it remains valid even if the URL changes.
The metadata included a service URN that uniquely identifies the collection in archival systems.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: URI - Uniform Resource Identifier; a string that identifies a resource on the Internet (URN is a type of URI).
  • Synonym: URL - Uniform Resource Locator; a web address pointing to a resource's location (related concept, not identical).

Etymology

From the initialism of Uniform Resource Name, coined in Internet standards (IETF) in the late 1990s.
