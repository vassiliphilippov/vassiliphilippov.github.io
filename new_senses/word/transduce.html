to transduce

1. VERB

/ trænzˈdus / (AmE), / trænzˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to convert (energy, signals, or information) from one form to another, especially in technical or biological contexts
convert energy or signals to another form
medical

Examples

Cells can transduce signals from their environment into a biochemical response.
Many sensors work by transducing mechanical pressure into readable outputs.
The microphone is designed to transduce sound waves into electrical signals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'transducere' meaning 'to lead across' or 'transfer'.

2. VERB NEW

/ trænˈdus / (AmE), / trænˈdjuːs / (BrE)
(in computer science, especially functional programming) to process a collection or stream by applying a transducer (a composable transformation) to produce a new result or stream in a single pass, avoiding intermediate collections or materialization.
apply a transducer to transform a stream in one pass

Examples

The library transduced the event stream into summary statistics in a single, memory-efficient pass.
To optimize performance, the team decided to transduce logs rather than materialize intermediate collections during processing.
By composing small transformers, they were transducing multiple filters into one efficient pipeline.
