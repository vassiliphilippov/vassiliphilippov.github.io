SPF

1. NOUN

/ ɛs pi ˈɛf / (AmE), / ɛs pi ˈɛf / (BrE)
a measure of how well a sunscreen protects the skin from ultraviolet (UV) rays; expressed as a number indicating the level of protection
measure of sunscreen protection level

Examples

Always choose a sunscreen with a high SPF for maximum protection during summer.
Dermatologists recommend reapplying SPF after swimming or sweating.
The bottle is labeled with an SPF of 30, which should be sufficient for everyday use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunblock - a cosmetic preparation that protects the skin from UV rays

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'sun protection factor.'

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɛs pi ˈɛf / (AmE), / ɛs pi ˈɛf / (BrE)
An email authentication framework that lets domain owners specify which mail servers are authorized to send mail for that domain, using DNS records to help prevent address spoofing.
email authentication framework

Examples

The IT team added an SPF record to authorize their mail servers.
Before sending the campaign, she checked the company's SPF to avoid deliverability problems.
Many receiving servers reject messages that fail an SPF check as possible spam.
