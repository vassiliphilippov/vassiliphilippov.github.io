feeding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfidɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of eating or taking in food
the act of eating or taking food

Examples

Nighttime feeding can interrupt your sleep schedule.
The baby needs a feeding every three hours.
We observed the feeding habits of the wild birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eating - the act of consuming food
  • Antonym: fasting - abstaining from food

Etymology

From the verb 'feed', from Old English 'fēdan' (to feed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfidɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving food or nourishment to a person, animal, or plant
act of giving food or nourishment

Examples

Feeding young infants every few hours can be exhausting for new parents.
Proper feeding is crucial for the healthy growth of plants and livestock.
The park ranger organizes the daily feeding of the animals at noon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nourishing - providing nourishment
  • Antonym: starvation - state of extreme lack of food

Etymology

From Old English *fēdan* (to feed) + -ing (forming nouns from verbs).
