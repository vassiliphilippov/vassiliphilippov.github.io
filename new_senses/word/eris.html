Eris

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪər.ɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of discord and strife, sister of Ares.
Greek goddess of discord

Examples

According to the myth, Eris threw the golden apple that led to the Trojan War.
In Homeric poetry, Eris is depicted as a bringer of chaos to the battlefield.
The story of Eris is often cited as an example of how minor events can cause major discord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Discordia - Roman goddess of discord, equivalent to Eris

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἔρις (Éris), meaning 'strife' or 'discord'.

2. PROPN NEW

/ \u02c8\u025br.\u026as / (AmE), / \u02c8\u026a\u0259r.\u026as / (BrE)
A dwarf planet in the outer solar system, in the Kuiper belt beyond Neptune, discovered in 2005.
dwarf planet beyond Neptune

Examples

Astronomers confirmed that Eris is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper belt beyond Neptune.
The discovery of Eris led to debates about Pluto's status as a planet.
Scientists studied light from Eris to estimate its size and surface composition.
