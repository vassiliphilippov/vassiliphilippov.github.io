spectre

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktər / (AmE), / ˈspɛktə / (BrE)
A disturbing or haunting image or memory, often from the past, that troubles the mind.
haunting memory or image
british

Examples

The spectre of his past mistakes haunted him for years.
The country's economic decline raised the spectre of widespread unemployment.
Whenever she returned to the old house, the spectre of childhood fears surfaced.

Synset Examples

he looked like he had seen a ghost
it aroused specters from his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, especially as seen by the living
  • Synonym: phantom - something that is seen or sensed but has no physical reality; a ghost

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image, apparition), via French 'spectre'.
