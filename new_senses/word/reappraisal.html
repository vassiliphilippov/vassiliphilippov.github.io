reappraisal

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈpreɪzəl / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈpreɪzəl / (BrE)
the act of appraising, assessing, or evaluating something again, often with a new perspective or updated information
a new assessment or evaluation

Examples

After several years, a reappraisal of the policy revealed significant shortcomings.
He called for a reappraisal of the city’s approach to traffic management.
The museum announced a reappraisal of its entire art collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal - assessment or evaluation
  • Synonym: review - careful examination or assessment

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'appraisal' (from 'appraise', meaning to assess value).

2. NOUN NEW

/  ri preaz l / (AmE), /  ri preaz l / (BrE)
(in psychology) a cognitive emotion-regulation strategy in which a person changes their interpretation of a situation or stimulus in order to alter its emotional impact.
psychological emotion-regulation strategy

Examples

Therapists teach clients reappraisal to reinterpret stressful events and reduce negative emotions during sessions.
The study measured participants' use of reappraisal when viewing upsetting images in the laboratory.
After practice, her habitual reappraisals of criticism led to lower anxiety and better sleep.
