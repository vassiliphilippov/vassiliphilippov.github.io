blunderbuss

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌndɚˌbʌs / (AmE), / ˈblʌndəbʌs / (BrE)
an old-fashioned firearm with a short barrel and a wide, flared muzzle, used especially in the 17th and 18th centuries
historical firearm with flared muzzle

Examples

A dusty blunderbuss hung above the tavern's fireplace as a decoration.
Collectors value an antique blunderbuss for its unique design and history.
The pirate aimed his blunderbuss at the approaching ship.

Etymology

From Dutch 'donderbus' (thunder gun), from 'donderen' (to thunder) + 'bus' (pipe, tube).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8bl\u028cnd\u025a\u02ccb\u028cs / (AmE), / \u02c8bl\u028cnd\u0259b\u028cs / (BrE)
a person, method, or policy that is clumsy, indiscriminate, or lacking subtlety; a broad, scattergun approach to a problem or task.
indiscriminate, scattergun approach

Examples

The consultant's blunderbuss strategy tried to fix every problem at once and pleased no one.
Faced with tight deadlines, they used a blunderbuss marketing plan that wasted resources quickly.
His speeches were verbal blunderbusses, hitting many targets but changing nothing in practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad-brush - dealing with something in a general or undifferentiated way rather than precisely
  • Antonym: targeted approach - a precise, focused method that addresses specific issues directly

Etymology

From Dutch 'donderbus' ("thunder gun"); originally the name of the wide‑mouthed firearm, later used figuratively for a blunt or indiscriminate method.
