taint

1. NOUN

/ teɪnt / (AmE), / teɪnt / (BrE)
A trace of something that harms, contaminates, or corrupts; the state of being made impure or spoiled.
contaminated or spoiled state

Examples

Even a small taint of dishonesty can damage one's reputation.
The taint of scandal still hung over the politician's career.
There was a taint of rust in the otherwise clean water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the act or state of making something impure; the presence of a contaminant
  • Antonym: purity - the state of being pure, untainted, or uncontaminated

Etymology

From Old French *teint* (tinged, stained), past participle of *teindre*, from Latin *tingere* (to dye, stain).

2. VERB

/ teɪnt / (AmE), / teɪnt / (BrE)
to contaminate something with a disease or microorganism, making it impure or harmful
contaminate with disease or microorganism

Examples

Improper handling can taint food with harmful bacteria.
The water supply was tainted by a dangerous microorganism after the flood.
Unsterilized instruments may taint the wound with infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infect - to contaminate with a disease-causing microorganism

Etymology

From Old French *teindre* (to dye, stain), from Latin *tingere* (to dye, to tinge).

4. NOUN NEW

/ teɪnt / (AmE), / teɪnt / (BrE)
(computing, especially security) the state of a piece of data or a value that has been identified as originating from an untrusted or potentially unsafe source and therefore requiring validation or sanitization before use.
untrusted or unsafe data (computing)

Examples

The static analyzer flagged the user input as taint before it reached the database layer.
During testing they tracked taint through the application to find paths leading to injection bugs.
The security team documented every taint source and required sanitization before using any input values.
