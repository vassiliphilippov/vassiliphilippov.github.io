ombudsperson

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmbʊdspɜːrsn̩ / (AmE), / ˈɒmbʊdspɜːsn̩ / (BrE)
An official appointed by a government to investigate individuals' complaints against public authorities, especially concerning administrative injustice or maladministration.
government-appointed complaints investigator

Examples

Citizens rely on the ombudsperson to ensure government departments are held accountable.
If you feel your concerns have not been addressed, you may contact the ombudsperson for assistance.
The ombudsperson handled dozens of cases involving misuse of government funds last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ombudsman - government official who investigates citizens' complaints against administration
  • Synonym: mediator - person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement

Etymology

From Swedish 'ombudsman' (representative) with gender-neutral suffix '-person'.

An independent official within a private organisation (for example, a company, university, or NGO) who receives and investigates complaints from employees or members, helps resolve disputes, and advises on policies to ensure fair treatment.
internal complaints investigator in an organisation

Examples

The university appointed an ombudsperson to address student complaints about grading and campus policies.
Employees met with the ombudsperson after several staff raised concerns about how promotions were decided.
She contacted multiple ombudspersons before choosing one who specialized in university academic disputes.
