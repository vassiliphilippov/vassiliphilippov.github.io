1. NOUN

/ bɑm / (AmE), / bɒm / (BrE)
a strong, sealed vessel used in laboratories to measure the heat of combustion of substances

Examples

A bomb is essential equipment in the operation of a calorimetry experiment.

2. NOUN

/ bɑm / (AmE), / bɒm / (BrE)
A container filled with explosive material, designed to detonate under certain conditions and cause damage or destruction.
explosive device

Examples

An explosion occurred when the bomb was accidentally triggered.
The police discovered a bomb hidden under the bridge.
They had to evacuate the building after a bomb threat was reported.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - a substance or device that produces a sudden, violent release of energy
  • Synonym: mine - an explosive device placed on or under the ground

Etymology

From French *bombe*, from Italian *bomba*, from Latin *bombus* (a booming, humming sound), from Ancient Greek *bombos*.
