interstitial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈstɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated in the small spaces between things, especially in tissues or structures
relating to spaces between things or tissues

Examples

Fluid accumulates in the interstitial tissue around the lungs during certain illnesses.
Researchers observed interstitial defects in the silicon crystal.
The engineer studied the interstitial spaces in the new material's lattice structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interstice - a small or narrow space between things

Etymology

From Latin 'interstitium' (a space between things), from 'inter-' (between) + 'sistere' (to stand).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌɪntərˈstɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪʃəl / (BrE)
A full-screen or large-format advertisement that appears between content screens or during transitions on websites, in mobile apps, or in games.
full-screen transitional advertisement

Examples

The free app showed a full-screen interstitial after the level finished, masking the content briefly.
Users complained that multiple interstitials disrupted reading and slowed page loading on news sites.
The marketing team recommended an interstitial before checkout to promote the limited-time discount code.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From the adjective 'interstitial' (from Latin interstitium, 'space between things'); the noun use developed in digital advertising in the late 20th/early 21st century.
