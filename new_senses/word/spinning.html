spinning

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making thread or yarn from natural or synthetic fibers by drawing out and twisting them
process of making thread or yarn

Examples

Modern spinning machines can produce thousands of meters of yarn each day.
She demonstrated the art of hand spinning at the craft fair.
The village has a long tradition of spinning and weaving using local wool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaving - the process of forming fabric from yarn or thread by interlacing strands

Etymology

From Middle English 'spinnen', from Old English 'spinnan' (to draw out and twist fibers into thread).
