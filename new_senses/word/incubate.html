to incubate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbeɪt / (BrE)
to keep something, especially microorganisms, eggs, or cells, in suitable conditions so that they develop or grow
keep for development under optimal conditions

Examples

It is important to incubate the eggs at a constant temperature for successful hatching.
Researchers incubated the cells overnight to allow for proper growth.
The scientist placed the petri dishes in the warm chamber to incubate the bacterial cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurture - to care for and encourage the growth or development of something
  • Synonym: hatch - to cause (an egg) to break open so that a young animal comes out
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Latin incubare (to lie upon, brood), from in- (upon) + cubare (to lie).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪŋ.kjuˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.kjʊ.beɪt / (BrE)
(of a bird or other animal) to sit on eggs in order to keep them warm and allow them to develop and hatch.
sit on eggs to keep them warm

Examples

In the wild, only the female penguin will incubate the eggs during the harsh winter months.
Some reptiles incubate their eggs by burying them in warm sand.
The hen will incubate her eggs for about three weeks before they hatch.

Synset Examples

Birds brood
The female covers the eggs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - to sit on eggs to hatch them

Etymology

From Latin incubare (to lie upon, brood), from in- (upon) + cubare (to lie).
