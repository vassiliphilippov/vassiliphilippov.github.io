to frogmarch

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɔgˌmɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrɒgˌmɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to force someone to move forward by holding and pinning their arms behind their back or by each limb, especially against their will
to force someone to walk by pinning their arms or limbs

Examples

The guards frogmarched the protester out of the courtroom.
They frogmarched him out of the building despite his loud objections.
Two security officers frogmarched the thief down the hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - to pull someone or something along forcefully, roughly, or with difficulty

Etymology

From 'frog' (as in the animal) and 'march', originally referring to carrying someone face down like a stretched frog.

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɔɡˌmɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrɒɡˌmɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to force a person to walk somewhere by holding their arms or otherwise restraining them, typically against their will
force someone to walk by restraining them

Examples

He was frogmarched into the principal's office by two teachers.
Security staff frogmarch troublemakers off the premises to prevent further disruption.
The guards frogmarched the protester out of the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - to pull someone or something along forcefully
  • Antonym: escort - to accompany someone somewhere, sometimes forcibly

Etymology

Originally referred to a method of carrying someone face down, arms and legs held, like a frog; first recorded in late 19th-century Britain.
