restfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being restful; a feeling of peace and relaxation
the quality of being restful and calm

Examples

He decorated his room to enhance its restfulness after a long day at work.
She enjoyed the restfulness of the quiet countryside every summer.
The restfulness of the afternoon was broken only by the sound of birds.

Synset Examples

he longed for the restfulness of home

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restlessness - the quality or state of being unable to rest or relax
  • Synonym: calmness - the state of being calm and peaceful

Etymology

From 'restful' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8r\u025bstf\u0259ln\u0259s / (AmE), / \u02c8r\u025bstf\u0259ln\u0259s / (BrE)
(in computing, especially web development) the degree to which a web service or API conforms to REST (Representational State Transfer) architectural principles, such as use of standard HTTP methods, clear resource URLs, and stateless interactions.
adherence to REST API principles

Examples

After the redesign, the API's restfulness improved, making integrations far simpler for client developers.
Assessors measured the restfulness of the service by checking resource URLs, HTTP verbs, and statelessness.
High restfulness in an API often leads to better caching, scalability, and predictable client-server interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: RESTful - conforming to REST architectural principles (using HTTP verbs, resource URIs, stateless interactions)

Etymology

From REST (Representational State Transfer) + -ful + -ness; REST is an acronym coined by Roy Fielding in 2000.
