rootless

1. ADJ

/ ˈrutləs / (AmE), / ˈruːtləs / (BrE)
having no permanent home, ties, or connection to a place or community; wandering or unsettled
without permanent home or ties

Examples

After moving from city to city for years, she often felt rootless and alone.
He wrote a novel about a rootless traveler searching for meaning in life.
The rootless youth drifted from job to job without settling down.

Synset Examples

led a vagabond life
a rootless wanderer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: settled - having a fixed home or established ties to a community
  • Synonym: vagabond - wandering from place to place without a home or job

Etymology

Formed from 'root' + the suffix '-less', meaning 'without roots'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈrutləs / (AmE), / ˈruːtləs / (BrE)
(in computing) running without, or not requiring, root (superuser) privileges; of software, containers, or processes designed to operate without administrative rights.
not requiring root privileges (computing)

Examples

Many modern containers are run rootless to avoid giving apps root access.
The security team recommended deploying rootless containers to reduce the blast radius of a breach.
Developers tested the application in a rootless namespace to ensure compatibility without elevated privileges.

Synset Examples

Many modern containers are run rootless to avoid giving apps root access.
The security team recommended deploying rootless containers to reduce the blast radius of a breach.
Developers tested the application in a rootless namespace to ensure compatibility without elevated privileges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprivileged - not granted administrative or superuser privileges; lacking elevated permissions.
  • Antonym: privileged - having administrative or elevated permissions, often including root access.

Etymology

Formed from 'root' + the suffix '-less'; extended in computing to mean 'without root (superuser) privileges'.
