to democratise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑː.krə.taɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒ.krə.taɪz / (BrE)
(of a country or institution) to become more democratic; to introduce democratic principles or processes.
to become more democratic

Examples

After decades of authoritarian rule, the country began to democratise in the late 20th century.
As societies democratise, they often experience significant social and political changes.
International organizations supported efforts to democratise the electoral process in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to democratize - US spelling of democratise
  • Synonym: liberalize - to make laws or attitudes less strict and more democratic

Etymology

From Greek 'dēmokratia' (democracy) + '-ise', via French 'démocratiser'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to introduce democratic principles or reforms, especially to a country's government or political system
make democratic; introduce democratic reforms

Examples

Efforts to democratise education have made higher learning more accessible to all social groups.
International organizations supported the government's plan to democratise the judicial system.
The country has taken significant steps to democratise its political system after decades of dictatorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalize - to make laws or rules less strict; to become more open or less controlled
  • Antonym: authoritarianize - to make a system more authoritarian

Etymology

From Greek 'dēmokratia' (rule by the people) via French 'démocratiser'.

3. VERB NEW

/ dɪˈmɑː.krə.taɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒ.krə.taɪz / (BrE)
to make something (especially information, technology, or resources) widely accessible and easy to use for many people, including non-experts.
make widely accessible (data/tech)

Examples

The startup aims to democratise machine learning tools so small businesses can use them easily.
By providing user guides and APIs, they democratized access to satellite imagery for researchers worldwide.
Open-source projects are democratizing scientific software, allowing non-experts to run complex analyses at home.
