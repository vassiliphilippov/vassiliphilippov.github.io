upscale

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌskeɪl / (BrE)
suitable for or appealing to people with higher incomes; relatively expensive and of high quality
suitable for people with higher incomes

Examples

The hotel was renovated into an upscale boutique property attracting business travelers.
They decided to move to an upscale neighborhood with better schools.
This upscale restaurant is known for its gourmet menu and elegant atmosphere.

Synset Examples

an upscale neighborhood
an upscale motel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially in a way that involves great expense
  • Synonym: exclusive - restricted to a select group, often wealthy people
  • Antonym: downscale - relatively inexpensive and of lower quality

2. VERB NEW

/ 8cpccke6al / (AmE), / 8cpccke6al / (BrE)
to make (something) more stylish, expensive, or appealing to wealthier customers; to raise the quality, price, or market position of something.
make more upscale; upgrade quality/market

Examples

The hotel chain announced plans to upscale its properties to attract luxury travelers.
They decided to upscale the brand by improving packaging and raising prices next season.
Local councils are accused of upscaling neighborhoods, pushing out long-time residents through higher rents.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upgrade - to improve or raise something to a higher standard or level
  • Synonym: gentrify - to renovate and improve a district so that it attracts wealthier residents (often displacing earlier residents)

Etymology

From the adjective 'upscale' (up- + scale); used as a verb by conversion to denote making something more upscale.
