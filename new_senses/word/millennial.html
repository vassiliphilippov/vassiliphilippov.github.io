millennial

1. ADJ

/ mɪˈlɛniəl / (AmE), / mɪˈlɛniəl / (BrE)
Relating to a period of a thousand years or to a millennium.
relating to a period of a thousand years

Examples

Archaeologists discovered artifacts dating back to the millennial celebrations of the city.
Some traditions are rooted in millennial customs passed down through centuries.
The ancient civilization disappeared after a millennial reign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millennium - a period of a thousand years

Etymology

From Latin 'millennium' (thousand years) with the suffix '-al' (pertaining to).

2. NOUN NEW

/ mɪˈlɛniəl / (AmE), / mɪˈlɛniəl / (BrE)
A person born roughly between 1981 and 1996; a member of the Millennial generation (Generation Y).
person born c.1981–1996 (Millennial)

Examples

The panel included a millennial voter who grew up with smartphones.
Marketing teams study millennial attitudes toward travel and subscription services before new campaigns.
As a millennial, she often compares her student-loan experience to older relatives.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Generation Y - an alternative name for the Millennial generation

Etymology

From Latin 'millennium' (a thousand years) via the adjective 'millennial'; adopted in late 20th century as a label for the generation born around the 1980s–1990s.
