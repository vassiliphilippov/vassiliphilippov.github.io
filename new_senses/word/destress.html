to destress

1. VERB

/ diˈstrɛs / (AmE), / diˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to reduce or remove emphasis, particularly in speech or pronunciation; to make less stressed
to reduce emphasis or stress

Examples

In the second syllable, you should destress the vowel to produce a more natural pronunciation.
Linguists often destress function words when analyzing natural speech patterns.
The instructor advised the students to destress certain words in the sentence for proper intonation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
  • Antonym: stress - to place emphasis or importance on

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (removal, reversal) + stress.

2. VERB NEW

/ diˈstrɛs / (AmE), / diˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to reduce or relieve mental or emotional stress; to relax and unwind (often by doing pleasant or calming activities).
relieve mental stress
informal

Examples

After work she took a long walk to destress before preparing dinner for the family.
He destressed over the weekend with yoga classes and a friend's quiet cabin retreat.
Spa evenings and reading by the fire are her favorite ways of destressing after a busy week.
