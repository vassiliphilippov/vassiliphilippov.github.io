arb

1. NOUN

/ ɑrb / (AmE), / ɑːb / (BrE)
a person who engages in arbitrage, buying and selling securities in different markets to profit from price differences
someone who practices arbitrage in finance
informal

Examples

Many hedge funds employ at least one experienced arb on their trading desk.
The arb made a quick profit by exploiting price gaps between stock exchanges.
With his sharp eye for opportunities, he soon became known as the best arb in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrageur - a person who engages in arbitrage

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'arbitrageur', from 'arbitrage' (French), relating to financial trade.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɑrb / (AmE), / ɑːb / (BrE)
(informal, computing/finance) a computer program or algorithm that automatically identifies and executes arbitrage opportunities across markets, often by placing rapid trades to profit from small price differences.
automated arbitrage program
informal

Examples

The exchange's arb detected a price gap and placed trades across three markets.
Hedge funds deploy specialized arbs to exploit tiny, short-lived inefficiencies in cryptocurrency markets.
After the outage, the team checked whether their arb had executed any unintended cross‑exchange trades.
