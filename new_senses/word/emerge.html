to emerge

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to move out of or away from something and become visible, or to come into existence or notice
come into view or existence

Examples

After hours of searching, the diver finally saw the submarine emerge from the shadows.
As the mist cleared, the peaks of the mountains emerged against the morning sky.
New leaders often emerge during times of crisis and change.

Synset Examples

He felt new emotions emerge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appear - to become visible or come into sight
  • Antonym: disappear - to cease to be visible or to vanish

Etymology

From Latin 'emergere' (to rise out, bring forth), from 'e-' (out) + 'mergere' (to dip, plunge).

6. VERB NEW

/ ɪˈmɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(in computing, especially on Gentoo Linux) to install or build a software package using the Portage package manager (the 'emerge' command).
install/build packages with Portage

Examples

System administrators often emerge new packages on Gentoo to keep servers secure and up to date.
Before deployment, she emerged the updated library and tested it in a clean chroot.
When the build failed, the team re-ran emerge with verbose output to diagnose the error.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: install - to put software onto a computer so it is ready to use
  • Synonym: build - to compile or assemble software from source code into a usable program

Etymology

Named after the ordinary verb 'emerge'; adopted as the name of the Portage 'emerge' command in the Gentoo Linux ecosystem.
