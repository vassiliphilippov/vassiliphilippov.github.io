visualiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒ.u.əˌlaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒ.u.əˌlaɪ.zə / (BrE)
A person whose mental imagery or imagination is predominantly visual rather than verbal or abstract.
person who thinks in images

Examples

As a visualiser, she often remembers people's faces but forgets their names.
Many architects are visualisers who solve problems by imagining structures.
The artist was a natural visualiser, always picturing scenes in his mind's eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visualizer - alternate spelling, chiefly American English

Etymology

From 'visual' + '-iser', modeled after words like 'realiser' or 'organiser'.
