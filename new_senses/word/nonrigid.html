nonrigid

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
relating to an airship whose shape is maintained only by gas pressure, without an internal supporting structure.
not having a rigid internal framework (airships)

Examples

During World War I, nonrigid airships were often used for naval patrols and observation.
The Zeppelin museum displayed a famous nonrigid airship from the early twentieth century.
Unlike rigid airships, nonrigid blimps rely entirely on internal gas pressure to hold their shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blimp - a nonrigid airship (no internal frame)
  • Antonym: rigid - having a fixed, inflexible structure (contrast with nonrigid in airships)

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) + 'rigid' (stiff or inflexible).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˌnɑnˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
(in computing, image processing, and medical imaging) describing a transformation, model, or registration that allows local deformation of shape rather than only rigid motion (translation/rotation).
allowing local deformation (transformations/models)

Examples

The algorithm uses a nonrigid transformation to align distorted medical scans.
Researchers trained a nonrigid model to track facial expressions across different head poses.
During surgery planning they applied nonrigid registration to match the preoperative images with live scans.

  • Antonym: rigid - not able to be bent, stretched, or changed in shape; opposite of nonrigid
