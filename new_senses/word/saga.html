saga

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈsɑːɡə / (BrE)
A long, involved narrative about heroic deeds, families, or events, especially a traditional story from medieval Iceland; more generally, any lengthy prose story resembling such an account.
a long heroic or family narrative

Examples

The Icelandic saga of Egil Skallagrimsson is famous for its vivid storytelling.
The Star Wars films are often referred to as a cinematic saga spanning decades and galaxies.
The novel traces the saga of three generations of a family living in rural Texas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - a long narrative poem or story about heroic deeds
  • Synonym: tale - a narrative or story, often imaginative or fictitious

Etymology

From Old Norse *saga* (story, tale).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈsɑːɡə / (BrE)
(in computing, especially distributed systems) a design pattern for managing long-running transactions across multiple services by executing a sequence of local transactions and, if necessary, compensating actions to keep data consistent.
microservices transaction pattern

Examples

The development team implemented a saga to ensure orders are consistent across multiple microservices.
When a payment failed, the saga rolled back previous steps using compensation actions to maintain data integrity.
They compared orchestration and choreography approaches, then chose the saga for reliable failure handling.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saga pattern - the same design pattern for coordinating distributed, long-running transactions across services
  • Synonym: compensating transaction - an action that undoes or compensates a previous local transaction; commonly used in saga implementations to handle failures

Etymology

From Old Norse *saga* (story); used metaphorically in 21st-century computing to describe a long-running sequence of operations.
