minimalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪməˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməlɪzəm / (BrE)
A style in art, music, or design that is characterized by extreme simplicity of form, color, or content, especially a movement in painting and sculpture that began in the 1950s.
art movement focused on simplicity

Examples

He was inspired by minimalism when designing the gallery space.
Her paintings reflect the principles of minimalism in their use of simple geometric shapes.
Many critics consider minimalism a radical departure from traditional sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality or condition of being easy to understand or do; plainness

Etymology

From 'minimal' + '-ism'; originally from Latin 'minimus' (smallest).

A lifestyle or philosophy that emphasizes living with fewer possessions, reducing consumption, and keeping life and surroundings simple and uncluttered.
lifestyle of simple living

Examples

After downsizing her apartment, she found that minimalism eased both her stress and expenses.
Many blogs offer practical advice on adopting minimalism, like keeping only items that are useful or loved.
They embraced minimalism by limiting purchases and donating clothes they no longer wore each season.
