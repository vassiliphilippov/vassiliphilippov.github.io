Davy Jones

1. NOUN

/ ˌdeɪvi ˈdʒoʊnz / (AmE), / ˌdeɪvi ˈdʒəʊnz / (BrE)
a mythical place at the bottom of the sea, regarded as the burial place of drowned sailors
mythical sea grave or ocean bottom

Examples

Legends say the treasure was lost forever in Davy Jones.
Superstitious sailors believed that drowned men ended up in Davy Jones.
The ship sank, taking its secrets to Davy Jones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Davy Jones's locker - the figurative place at the bottom of the sea where drowned sailors go

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the name of a legendary sailor or a nautical slang term dating from the 18th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈdeɪvi ˈdʒoʊnz / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvi ˈdʒəʊnz / (BrE)
a fictional, often monstrous sea captain or sea-demon—commonly portrayed as the captain of the ghost ship the Flying Dutchman in modern popular culture.
fictional sea-demon captain

Examples

In the movie, Davy Jones is a fearsome sea captain with a tentacled face and a cursed heart.
Many fans argued that Davy Jones showed moments of regret despite his monstrous appearance and power.
The actor spent hours in makeup to transform into Davy Jones for the film's dramatic sea scenes.

Synset Examples

In the movie, Davy Jones is a fearsome sea captain with a tentacled face and a cursed heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Davy Jones's locker - the figurative place at the bottom of the sea where drowned sailors go; closely associated with the name 'Davy Jones'.
  • Synonym: Flying Dutchman - a legendary ghost ship whose captain is often identified as Davy Jones in modern fiction.

Etymology

From the nautical folklore name 'Davy Jones' (a figurative sea grave); later personified in literature and popular films, notably the Pirates of the Caribbean series.
