volume

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑljum / (AmE), / ˈvɒljuːm / (BrE)
the degree of loudness or intensity of a sound, often adjustable on devices
loudness or intensity of sound

Examples

Could you please turn down the volume? It's too loud in here.
She always keeps the volume low so she doesn't disturb her neighbors.
The volume on my headphones is adjustable for comfort.

Synset Examples

the kids played their music at full volume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudness - the quality of being loud; intensity of sound
  • Antonym: quietness - the quality of being quiet; low volume

Etymology

From Latin 'volumen' (roll, scroll), referring originally to a rolled-up manuscript, later extended to other senses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːl.jum / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.juːm / (BrE)
the amount of three-dimensional space that an object or substance occupies or contains; capacity
amount of 3D space occupied

Examples

Heating the air causes it to expand and increase its volume.
The architect calculated the volume of the building for heating estimates.
This container has a volume of two liters.

Synset Examples

the gas expanded to twice its original volume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacity - the maximum amount that something can contain
  • Antonym: surface area - the total area that the surface of an object occupies

Etymology

From Latin 'volumen' (roll, scroll, book), from 'volvere' (to roll); the current sense developed via the idea of a rolled object having space inside.

7. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvɑljum / (AmE), / ˈvɒljuːm / (BrE)
(in computing) a logical storage unit on a computer, server, or in the cloud that appears as a single disk or partition and usually contains a filesystem; it can be attached/mounted to a machine.
logical storage unit (computing)

Examples

The administrator attached a new volume to the virtual server to store backups.
When the disk failed, she restored the database onto a replacement volume in the cloud.
The technician increased the size of the mounted volume without shutting down the production server.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: storage - space or devices used to keep digital data

Etymology

Extended metaphorically from Latin 'volumen' (roll, scroll) and the sense of an amount or bounded space; applied in computing to mean a bounded unit of storage.
