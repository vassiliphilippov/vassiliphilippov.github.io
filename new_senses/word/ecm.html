ECM

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː siː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌiː siː ˈɛm / (BrE)
(Electronic Countermeasures) Electronic warfare activities designed to prevent or reduce an enemy's effective use of the electromagnetic spectrum.
military electronic countermeasures

Examples

Effective use of ECM can significantly reduce the threat from guided missiles.
Modern aircraft are equipped with advanced ECM suites for protection.
The military deployed ECM devices to jam enemy radar systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electronic warfare - military operations using electromagnetic energy

Etymology

Acronym of Electronic Countermeasures.

2. NOUN NEW

/ iː siː ˈɛm / (AmE), / iː siː ˈɛm / (BrE)
(in business and information technology) a set of technologies and practices for capturing, storing, organizing, managing, and delivering an organization's documents and content.
enterprise content management system

Examples

The firm deployed an ECM system to standardize its documents and workflows.
Our IT team recommended migrating records into a cloud-based ECM to improve search and compliance.
During the audit, she found missing policies that should have been captured in the company's ECM.
