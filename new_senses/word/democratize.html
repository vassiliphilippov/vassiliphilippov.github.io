to democratize

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to make a country or organization more democratic, especially by introducing political reforms that allow greater participation by citizens
make more democratic by reform

Examples

Many activists are pushing to democratize education and make it accessible to all.
Several nations in the region have begun to democratize after years of authoritarian rule.
The new government vowed to democratize the country's political system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalize - to make laws or rules less strict; to make freer or more open
  • Antonym: authoritarianize - to make more authoritarian in nature or rule

Etymology

From French 'démocratiser', from 'démocratie' (democracy) and the suffix '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
(of a country, system, etc.) to make or become more democratic, especially by allowing everyone to participate equally in making decisions.
to make or become more democratic

Examples

Efforts to democratize society met resistance from traditional power holders.
Many countries sought to democratize after the fall of the dictatorship.
Reforms were introduced to democratize the electoral process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalize - to make more liberal and open, especially regarding policies
  • Antonym: autocratize - to make more autocratic or less democratic

Etymology

From French 'démocratiser', from 'démocratie' (democracy), ultimately from Greek 'dēmokratia'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ dɪˈmɑkrətaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something (for example information, technology, or services) widely accessible and usable by many people, especially by removing barriers so it is not limited to experts or elites.
make widely accessible

Examples

Open-source projects aim to democratize software development by lowering barriers for contributors worldwide.
The nonprofit plans to democratize access to scientific data through free, online databases for researchers.
They democratized the design tool so small businesses could create their own marketing materials.
