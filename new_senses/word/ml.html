ml

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ɛl / (AmE), / ɛm ɛl / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter, commonly used to measure liquids
metric unit of liquid volume (milliliter)

Examples

Can you add 100 ml of water to the solution?
The dosage was measured precisely at 5 ml using a syringe.
This bottle holds 250 ml of shampoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milliliter - a metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'milliliter', from French 'millilitre', from Latin 'mille' (thousand) + French 'litre'.
