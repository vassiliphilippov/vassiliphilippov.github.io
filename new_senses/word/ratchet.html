ratchet

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈrætʃɪt / (BrE)
a mechanical device with a toothed wheel or bar and a pawl that allows motion in one direction only, used to prevent reversal of movement
mechanical device allowing one-way motion

Examples

A ratchet prevents the gear from turning backward.
Most wrenches include a ratchet mechanism for easier use.
The mechanic used a ratchet to tighten the bolt securely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawl - catch or lever engaging the teeth of a ratchet

Etymology

From French *rochet* (a blunt lance head), from Old French *roche* (rock, blunt object).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrætʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈratʃɪt / (BrE)
a mechanical device consisting of a toothed wheel or bar and a pawl that allows motion in only one direction
device allowing movement in one direction

Examples

A simple ratchet mechanism prevents the gear from turning the wrong way.
Be sure to engage the ratchet before attempting to lift the load.
The winch uses a ratchet to keep the cable from slipping backward.

Etymology

From French 'rochet', relating to a blunt lance head, later applied to notched wheels.

3. VERB

/ ˈrætʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈrætʃɪt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move by increments in one direction only, especially using a mechanism that prevents backward movement
move incrementally in one direction

Examples

Costs may ratchet higher if the repairs are delayed until next year.
The tension on the cable can ratchet up with each turn of the lever.
With each adjustment, the mechanism will ratchet to a tighter position.

Synset Examples

a ratcheting lopping tool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - to move forward; to progress

Etymology

From the noun 'ratchet', which comes from Old French 'rochet' (a blunt lance head or notched bar).
