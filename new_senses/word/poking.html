poking

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
A sharp or quick hand gesture, especially resembling a jab or thrust with a finger or an object.
sharp, quick jab or thrust

Examples

A gentle poking was all it took to get their attention.
He silenced his friend with a swift poking of the finger.
The child’s constant poking annoyed everyone at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

he warned me with a jab with his finger
he made a thrusting motion with his fist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jab - a quick, sharp blow or poke
  • Synonym: thrust - a sudden or forceful pushing movement

Etymology

From the verb 'poke,' of uncertain origin, possibly related to Middle Dutch 'poken' (to poke).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
(in social media and messaging) a brief, attention-getting digital nudge sent to another user to prompt a reply or show interest.
digital nudge on social media

Examples

The site's poking feature sent him a small notification he'd missed earlier.
She used poking on the app to remind her friend about dinner plans tomorrow.
After no reply for hours, he resorted to gentle poking instead of sending another long message.

Synset Examples

The site's poking feature sent him a small notification he'd missed earlier.
She used poking on the app to remind her friend about dinner plans tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudge - a gentle push or reminder intended to get someone's attention or encourage action
  • Synonym: prompt - something that encourages or causes someone to take action or reply

Etymology

From the verb 'poke' (originally 'to jab' or 'thrust'); extended metaphorically to a virtual nudge in social media platforms in the 21st century.
