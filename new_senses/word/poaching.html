poaching

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A method of cooking food by gently simmering it in liquid just below boiling point.
cooking by simmering in liquid

Examples

Poaching is often used for fruits like pears and apples in desserts.
Eggs prepared by poaching are tender and delicate in texture.
Many chefs prefer poaching fish to preserve its natural flavors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boiling - cooking food in rapidly bubbling water or other liquid
  • Synonym: simmering - cooking food gently just below the boiling point

Etymology

From Middle French *pocher* (to poach, to simmer).

The act of recruiting or enticing employees, clients, or customers away from a rival organisation, often seen as aggressive or unethical.
recruiting from competitors

Examples

The startup blamed the larger company for poaching its best engineers with higher salaries and benefits.
Industry groups warned against aggressive poaching, arguing it damages smaller firms and reduces long-term innovation.
After the announcement, the CEO accused a rival of poaching clients by copying their pricing and promises.
