decolonization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a country or region gains independence and becomes free from colonial rule.
process of gaining independence from colonial rule

Examples

Decolonization often involved both political negotiation and popular resistance movements.
Many historians study the lasting impacts of decolonization on former colonies.
The rapid decolonization of Africa changed the global political landscape in the mid-20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - freedom from control or influence of another country or government

Etymology

From de- (removal) + colonization, after French décolonisation.

The process of removing colonial biases, perspectives, and structures from institutions, curricula, or knowledge production so that Indigenous and other marginalized viewpoints are represented fairly.
removal of colonial bias from institutions

Examples

Universities worldwide are undergoing decolonization of their curricula to include Indigenous perspectives.
The museum's recent decolonization project returned artifacts and updated the exhibition labels.
Students demanded decolonization in reading lists so authors from diverse cultures were represented.
