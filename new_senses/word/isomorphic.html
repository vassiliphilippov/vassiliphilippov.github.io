isomorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈmɔr.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈmɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
Having a similar form or appearance but genetically or structurally different, especially in biological contexts.
having similar appearance but different origin

Examples

Some fungi exhibit isomorphic forms during their life cycles.
The leaves of these plants are isomorphic, making them hard to distinguish visually.
Two species may be isomorphic, appearing identical but differing genetically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homomorphic - having the same form or structure
  • Antonym: heteromorphic - having a different form or appearance

Etymology

From Greek isomorphos, from iso- (equal) + morphe (form).
