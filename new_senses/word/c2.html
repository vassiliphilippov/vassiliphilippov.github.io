C2

1. NOUN

/ si tuː / (AmE), / siː tuː / (BrE)
An international standard envelope size measuring 458 × 648 millimeters, suitable for enclosing A2-sized paper unfolded.
international envelope size (458 × 648 mm)

Examples

For large posters, please use a C2 envelope for mailing.
She purchased a pack of C2 envelopes for her architectural drawings.
The documents were shipped in a C2 envelope to avoid folding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A2 - standard paper size, 420 × 594 mm
  • Degree Lower: C3 - standard envelope size, 324 × 458 mm
  • Degree Higher: C1 - standard envelope size, 648 × 917 mm

Etymology

Part of the ISO 269 envelope size system, corresponding to the A2 paper size.

2. NOUN NEW

/ si tuː / (AmE), / siː tuː / (BrE)
A designation on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) indicating the highest level of proficiency, showing advanced, near‑native ability in a language.
CEFR highest proficiency level

Examples

Her English score placed her at C2 on the CEFR scale during the language exam.
The job listing required applicants to have a C2 level in written and spoken French.
After years of study she finally reached C2, able to participate in academic discussions effortlessly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: C1 - The CEFR proficiency level immediately below C2; indicates advanced language ability.
  • Synonym: CEFR - Common European Framework of Reference for Languages, a widely used scale of language proficiency levels (A1–C2).

Etymology

From CEFR (Common European Framework of Reference for Languages); 'C2' names the top proficiency level in the A–C scale.
