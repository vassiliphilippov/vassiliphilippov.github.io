perplexity

1. NOUN

/ pərˈplɛksəti / (AmE), / pəˈplɛksəti / (BrE)
A state of being confused or puzzled, especially because something is complex or difficult to understand.
confusion from complexity or difficulty

Examples

His sudden change of plans filled his friends with perplexity.
She stared at the complex puzzle in perplexity, unsure of where to begin.
There was perplexity on her face as she tried to follow the professor's explanation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of not being certain or clear
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin perplexitas (confusion, intricacy), from perplexus (entangled, confused).

2. NOUN NEW

/ p59rc8pl5bks59ti / (AmE), / p59c8pl5bks59ti / (BrE)
(in information theory and natural language processing) A numeric measure of how well a probabilistic model predicts a sample; lower perplexity means the model assigns higher probability to the observed data and is therefore better at prediction.
statistical measure of model uncertainty

Examples

The language model's perplexity dropped significantly after fine-tuning on the new dataset.
Researchers compare models by reporting average perplexity across multiple test corpora to judge performance.
Different corpora produce different perplexities, so lower values indicate better predictive fit.
