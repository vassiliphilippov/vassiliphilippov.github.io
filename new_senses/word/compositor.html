compositor

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑzɪtər / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪtə / (BrE)
a person who arranges type for printing written material
person who arranges type for printing

Examples

As a skilled compositor, she could set type quickly and accurately by hand.
Before digital printing, a compositor was an essential worker in publishing houses.
The compositor carefully arranged the letters for the morning newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetter - a person who sets type for printing by hand or machine

Etymology

From Latin 'componere' (to put together), via French 'compositeur'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ kəmˈpɑzətər / (AmE), / kəmˈpɔzətə / (BrE)
(in film and visual effects) a visual-effects artist who combines and integrates multiple image elements (live-action footage, CGI, matte paintings, and layers) into a single final shot, matching color, lighting, and perspective.
visual-effects artist who combines image layers

Examples

The shot required a skilled compositor to merge CGI with live-action plates and match lighting.
After color grading, the lead compositor adjusted the layers to hide the seam in the sky.
The studio hired several compositors to finish the sequence before the deadline next week.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compositing artist - a specialist who assembles and blends multiple visual elements into a final image
  • Synonym: visual effects artist - a professional who creates or integrates computer-generated imagery and other effects into film or video

Etymology

From Latin componere (to put together) via French compositeur; extended from traditional 'one who composes' to digital image composition in modern film and VFX contexts.
