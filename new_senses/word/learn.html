to learn

1. VERB

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
to be a student of a particular subject, or to acquire knowledge by studying it
be a student of a subject

Examples

He wants to learn physics to become an engineer.
Many students travel abroad to learn new subjects.
She decided to learn French at university.

Synset Examples

She is reading for the bar exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - to devote time and attention to acquiring knowledge of a subject

Etymology

Old English *leornian* (to learn); related to German *lernen* and Dutch *leren*.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
to gain knowledge or skill in a subject by study, experience, or teaching

3. VERB

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
to discover or find out about something, often by chance rather than deliberate study

Examples

I learned that the meeting was rescheduled only after I arrived at the office.
She learned about the surprise party from a mutual friend.

5. VERB NEW

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
(in computing, especially machine learning) (of a computer program or model) to improve performance, make predictions, or acquire patterns by adjusting internal parameters through training on data.
model improves by training on data

Examples

The neural network will learn useful features automatically when trained on large labeled datasets.
After several iterations, the model learned to identify fraudulent transactions with surprising accuracy.
We hope our recommendation system keeps learning from user feedback to improve suggestions over time.
