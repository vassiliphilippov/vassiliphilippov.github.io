tracker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrækər / (AmE), / ˈtrækə / (BrE)
a person who follows and locates animals or people by skillfully reading signs or trails, especially in hunting
person skilled in following trails, esp. in hunting

Examples

As a skilled tracker, she was often called upon to find missing hikers in the wilderness.
The experienced tracker led the group through the dense forest, searching for signs of wildlife.
Using subtle clues on the ground, the tracker was able to identify the direction of the herd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunter - person who hunts wild animals for food or sport

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtrækər / (AmE), / ˈtrækə / (BrE)
a small electronic device or wearable app that records and monitors physical activity or health metrics such as steps, heart rate, or sleep.
wearable activity monitor

Examples

She wore a tracker to monitor her daily steps and sleep.
Many runners compare different trackers to find the most accurate heart-rate readings.
My tracker alerted me when my resting heart rate rose unexpectedly last night.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fitness tracker - a device or app specifically designed to monitor physical activity and health metrics
  • Synonym: wearable - an electronic device worn on the body that performs computing or monitoring functions

Etymology

From track + -er; originally ‘one who tracks’, extended to devices that track activity.
