wallet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑː.lɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.ɪt / (BrE)
a small, flat case used for carrying paper money, credit cards, and personal papers, usually carried in the pocket or bag
small case for money and cards

Examples

He realized his wallet was missing after leaving the crowded train.
My wallet is full of old receipts and membership cards.
She reached into her bag and pulled out her wallet to pay for the groceries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: billfold - a folding case for holding paper money; chiefly American term

Etymology

From Middle English *walet* (bag, knapsack), of uncertain origin.

(in computing and commerce) a software application or online service that stores payment credentials, digital money, tickets, or identity details, used to make electronic or contactless payments.
digital payment app or service

Examples

She paid for coffee using her wallet on her phone.
Before traveling abroad, he downloaded several wallet apps to store foreign cards and boarding passes.
Many retailers accept contactless payment from a phone-based wallet, making checkout faster than cash.

Metaphorical extension of 'wallet' (from Middle English *walet* 'bag, knapsack') applied to software that holds payment information.
