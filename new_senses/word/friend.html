Friend

1. NOUN

/ frɛnd / (AmE), / frɛnd / (BrE)
A member of the Religious Society of Friends, a Christian movement founded by George Fox in the 17th century, also known as a Quaker.
member of the Quaker religious group

Examples

Her grandmother was a devout Friend who attended meetings regularly.
Many Friends played central roles in social reform movements of the 19th century.
The Friend community is known for its longstanding commitment to peace and equality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quaker - a member of the Religious Society of Friends

Etymology

From the name of the Religious Society of Friends, founded in England in the 17th century.

informal

Examples

I friended my new colleague on the company social site after our meeting yesterday.
She asked me to friend her so she could see my travel photos and updates.
