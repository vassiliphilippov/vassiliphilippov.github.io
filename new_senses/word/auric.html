auric

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing gold, especially with gold in the trivalent state (having a valence of three).
relating to gold, especially trivalent gold

Examples

Certain medications contain auric salts as one of their ingredients.
In chemistry, auric chloride is a well-known gold compound.
The scientist analyzed the auric compound to determine its chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: aurous - relating to gold, especially with gold in the monovalent state (valence of one)

Etymology

From Latin "aurum" (gold) with the suffix "-ic"; used in chemistry.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an aura — an energy field believed to surround a person or object, especially in spiritual, New Age, or holistic contexts.
relating to an aura or energy field

Examples

Practitioners say an auric field surrounds the body with protective energy.
He learned to sense changes in his partner's auric colours during distance healing sessions.
The therapist cleansed the client's auric layer to help balance physical and emotional symptoms.
