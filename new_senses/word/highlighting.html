highlighting

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an area of lightness in a picture, used to create contrast or emphasize certain features
bright area in a picture

Examples

Careful highlighting helps create a sense of depth and form in a drawing.
The bright highlighting on the glass made it look shiny and realistic.
The painter added highlighting to the subject's cheekbones to make her features stand out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shadow - an area of darkness in a picture, contrasting with a highlight

Etymology

From the verb 'highlight' (to emphasize), formed from 'high' + 'light'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ha\u026a\u02ccla\u026at\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8ha\u026a\u02ccla\u026at\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
(in computing and user interfaces) the act or feature of marking text or code with color, background, or emphasis to show selection, syntax categories, or important parts.
UI marking of text or code

Examples

The code editor's highlighting shows keywords and strings in different colors for easier reading.
Her careful highlighting of the contract helped the team find key clauses quickly.
When I copy text, the browser's highlighting sometimes obscures nearby buttons on mobile screens.
