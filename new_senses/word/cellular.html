cellular

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛl.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of cells, especially the basic structural units of living organisms
relating to cells

Examples

Cellular biology explores how cells grow, divide, and interact with their environment.
Some diseases are caused by abnormalities at the cellular level.
The scientist studied the cellular structure of the tissue under a microscope.

Synset Examples

cellular walls
cellular physiology

Etymology

From Latin cellula (small room), diminutive of cella (chamber, cell).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛljələr / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə / (BrE)
consisting of or divided into cells or compartments; relating to a structure or organization made up of small, separate units.
made of or containing cells or compartments

Examples

Some plants have thick, cellular walls that store water during dry periods.
The honeycomb's cellular structure provides both strength and efficiency.
The new organizational model adopted a cellular approach, with small autonomous teams.
