Pomo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊməʊ / (BrE)
the language of the Pomo people, indigenous to northern California, classified as part of the Kulanapan language family
indigenous language of the Pomo people

Examples

Researchers are working to record and preserve Pomo for future generations.
She studied Pomo at university as part of her linguistics degree.
The Pomo language is considered endangered, with only a few fluent speakers remaining.

Etymology

From Pomo, a self-designation by indigenous groups of northern California.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊməʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people of northern California, living along the Russian River valley and nearby Pacific coast.
a member of a Native American people of northern California

Examples

A Pomo community event was held along the banks of the Russian River.
Many Pomo elders work to preserve their language and cultural practices.
The Pomo have a rich tradition of basket weaving that is highly regarded worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pomoan - the language family of the Pomo people

Etymology

From the Pomoan language, meaning 'those who live at red earth hole'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊməʊ / (BrE)
(informal) an abbreviation for postmodernism or for works and ideas showing a postmodern style — for example, self‑reference, pastiche, irony, or mixing of genres.
informal: postmodernism; postmodern style
informal slang

Examples

The director's film is a quintessential Pomo work, full of self-reflexive jokes and pastiche.
In her essay she argues that postmodern novels are often labeled Pomo because they mix genres and voices.
At the gallery opening, critics dismissed the installation as trendy and overly Pomo in its approach.
