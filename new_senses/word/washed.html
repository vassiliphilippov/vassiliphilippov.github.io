washed

1. ADJ

/ wɑʃt / (AmE), / wɒʃt / (BrE)
Clean as a result of having been washed in water.
clean after being washed

Examples

She put on her washed jeans before heading out.
The washed floors were still glistening with moisture.
The freshly washed dishes sparkled in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dirty - not clean; covered or marked with unclean substances

Etymology

Past participle of 'wash', from Old English *wæscan*.

2. ADJ

/ wɑʃt / (AmE), / wɒʃt / (BrE)
(of something) made wet or clean, especially by the action of water or another liquid; often used in compound words (e.g., rain-washed).
made wet or clean by water

Examples

She admired the washed stones by the stream, glistening under the sunlight.
The artist preferred a washed canvas to create soft, natural backgrounds.
The garden looked especially beautiful after a washed rainfall had cleared the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washed-out - faded or drained of color or vitality, often from being washed repeatedly

3. ADJ NEW

/ wɑʃt / (AmE), / wɒʃt / (BrE)
Informal slang. No longer effective, successful, fashionable, or energetic; worn out or finished (often used of people, careers, or creative projects).
slang: no longer effective or fashionable
informal slang

Examples

The long-running franchise feels washed, with little new ideas and tired fan expectations.
After three tours, the singer looked washed and could no longer draw large crowds.
When he tried to compete with younger runners, everyone said he was washed and past his prime.
