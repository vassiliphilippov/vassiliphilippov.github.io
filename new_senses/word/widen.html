to widen

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdən / (BrE)
to make (an article of clothing) larger, especially by altering its seams or fabric
make clothing larger by altering

Examples

Can you widen this jacket so that it fits more comfortably?
She asked the tailor to widen her trousers at the waist after she gained weight.
They decided to widen the sleeves of the dress for a looser fit.

Synset Examples

Let out that dress — I gained a lot of weight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: let out - to make (a garment) larger by enlarging its seams

/ ˈwaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdən / (BrE)
to become or make something broader, wider, or more extensive

Examples

5. VERB NEW

/ ˈwaɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdən / (BrE)
(in computing) to convert a value from a narrower data type to a wider data type, increasing its range or precision (type promotion).
convert to a wider data type (computing)

Examples

When the compiler detects overflow risk, it will widen the operand to a larger integer type automatically.
The function widened the 16-bit value to a 32-bit integer to preserve precision during the calculation.
Avoid unnecessary casts; prefer algorithms that minimize implicit widening of floating-point values for performance.
