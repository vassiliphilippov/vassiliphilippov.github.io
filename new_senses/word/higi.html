Higi

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːɡi / (AmE), / ˈhiːɡi / (BrE)
A Chadic language spoken mainly south of Lake Chad, especially in Adamawa State, Michika LGA, and the Mandara mountains.
Chadic language of northern Nigeria

Examples

In Michika LGA, many local schools offer instruction in Higi alongside Hausa.
Researchers have studied the grammar and vocabulary of Higi for decades.
The Higi language is primarily spoken in communities south of Lake Chad.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈhiːɡi / (AmE), / ˈhiːɡi / (BrE)
A member of the Higi people, an ethnic group in northern Nigeria; also used collectively for the Higi community.
member of the Higi people

Examples

The guide described the Higi as one of the communities living near the mountains.
Several Higi artisans displayed intricate weaving at the regional cultural festival last Saturday.
My neighbor is a Higi who moved to the city to study at the university.
