descendant

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who is related to someone and lives after them, such as a child or grandchild or someone further down the family line
a person descended from an ancestor

Examples

As a descendant of immigrants, he values his family's cultural traditions.
Many people claim to be direct descendants of historical figures.
She researched her family tree to find out more about her earliest descendant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Synonym: offspring - a person's child or children

Etymology

From French 'descendant', present participle of 'descendre', from Latin 'descendere' (to climb down).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10026173-n

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
a person, plant, or animal that is descended from a particular ancestor.
offspring of an ancestor

Examples

Future descendants will benefit from the decisions we make today.
She traced her family tree to find more about her descendants.
These ancient traditions have been passed down to descendants for generations.

  • Synonym: offspring - a person's child or animal's young

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
(of something) derived or proceeding by descent from an ancestor; pertaining to descent.
proceeding by descent from an ancestor

Examples

Geneticists are studying the descendant line of the population to track mutations.
The descendant language retained only a few features of its ancestor.
This species has developed several descendant traits over generations.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestral - relating to ancestors or ancestry

Etymology

From Latin 'descendent-' (descending), from 'descendere' (to descend).

4. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
(computing) a node or element in a tree or directed graph that is reachable from another node by following one or more child (downward) links; a node that is below another in the structure.
node below another in a tree (computing)

Examples

In the DOM, a descendant node is any element reachable by following child links from a parent.
When querying the tree, the algorithm returned all descendants of the selected vertex in breadth-first order.
To update styles, add a class selector that targets any descendant elements inside the container.
