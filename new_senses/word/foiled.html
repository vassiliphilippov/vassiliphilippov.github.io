foiled

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔɪld / (AmE), / ˈfɔɪld / (BrE)
unsuccessful in achieving something because someone or something has prevented it
unsuccessful due to prevention

Examples

His foiled ambition to become an actor led him down a different career path.
Security measures ensured their foiled attempt to enter the building failed completely.
The thieves' foiled plan left them empty-handed and on the run.

Synset Examples

disappointed expectations and thwarted ambitions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thwarted - prevented from accomplishing something
  • Synonym: frustrated - feeling or expressing distress and annoyance due to being prevented from achieving something
  • Antonym: successful - accomplishing a desired aim or result

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'foil,' from Old French *fuler* 'to trample, spoil.'
