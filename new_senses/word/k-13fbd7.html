k

1. NOUN

/ keɪ / (AmE), / keɪ / (BrE)
the eleventh letter of the English alphabet, represented as K or k.
the letter 'k'

Examples

In the alphabet, k comes after 'j' and before 'l'.
She wrote a capital K at the start of the sentence.
The word 'kite' begins with the letter k.

Etymology

From Latin 'k', from Greek 'kappa', from Phoenician 'kaph'.

2. ADJ

/ keɪ / (AmE), / keɪ / (BrE)
used as an abbreviation to denote a quantity of one thousand, especially in measurements, money, or data.
abbreviation for one thousand

Examples

Her annual salary is 50k dollars.
The new printer can print up to 30k pages per month.
We reached 100k followers on social media last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thousand - the number 1,000

Etymology

From the metric prefix 'kilo-' derived from Greek 'khilioi' (thousand).
