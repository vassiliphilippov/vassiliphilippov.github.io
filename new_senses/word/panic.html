panic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk / (BrE)
A sudden, intense feeling of uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often causing irrational behavior.
sudden intense fear or anxiety

Examples

In the chaos of the accident, panic made it hard to think clearly.
She tried to control her panic before stepping onto the stage.
When the fire alarm sounded, a wave of panic swept through the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the belief that someone or something is dangerous
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of strong emotions or agitation

Etymology

From Greek 'panikos', relating to the god Pan, whose presence was believed to cause sudden fear.
