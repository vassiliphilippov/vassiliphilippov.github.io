poised

1. ADJ

/ pɔɪzd / (AmE), / pɔɪzd / (BrE)
having a calm and assured manner, in full control of oneself, especially in difficult situations
calm and self-assured

Examples

Despite the pressure of the interview, she remained poised and answered every question confidently.
Even in the midst of chaos, their leader appeared poised and in complete control.
He gave a poised performance that impressed both judges and audience alike.

Synset Examples

the witness remained collected throughout the cross-examination
perfectly poised and sure of himself
more self-contained and more dependable than many of the early frontiersmen
strong and self-possessed in the face of trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collected - calm and self-controlled; not nervous or agitated
  • Synonym: self-possessed - having control of one's feelings or actions; composed
  • Antonym: flustered - agitated or confused; lacking composure

2. ADJ

/ pɔɪzd / (AmE), / pɔɪzd / (BrE)
in a state of balance or equilibrium, and ready to move or take action
balanced and ready for action

Examples

She held her pen poised above the paper, waiting for inspiration to strike.
The mountain lion remained poised on the rock, every muscle tensed for a leap.
With his hand poised on the door handle, he listened one last time for any sounds inside.

Synset Examples

a gull in poised flight
George's poised hammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balanced - being in a state of balance
  • Antonym: unsteady - not balanced or secure; likely to fall

Etymology

Past participle of 'poise', from Old French *poisier* (to weigh), from Latin *pensare* (to weigh, consider).

3. ADJ NEW

/ pɔɪzd / (AmE), / pɔɪzd / (BrE)
(in genetics and molecular biology) in a prepared state for activation; having molecular features (for example paused RNA polymerase or specific chromatin marks) that enable rapid transcription when signalled.
genetically ready to activate

Examples

During differentiation, many developmental promoters remain poised with paused polymerase ready for rapid activation.
Chromatin modifications left the enhancer poised, enabling a quick transcriptional response when signals arrived.
Researchers found genes poised in stem cells that later switched on during early embryonic stages.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primed - prepared or made ready for rapid activation or response
  • Antonym: repressed - kept inactive or silenced at the molecular level (opposite of being ready to activate)
