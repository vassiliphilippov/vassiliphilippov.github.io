to relativise

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛləˌtɪvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪvaɪz / (BrE)
To consider or treat something as relative rather than absolute; to regard in relation to something else.
regard as relative, not absolute

Examples

He relativised his own achievements by comparing them with those of others.
Her argument seeks to relativise the concept of truth in different cultural contexts.
Some philosophers relativise moral values rather than seeing them as universal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relativize - consider or treat as relative (American spelling)

Etymology

From relative + -ise, modelled after the pattern of similar verbs.

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8r\u025bl\u0259\u02cct\u026ava\u026az / (AmE), / \u02c8r\u025bl\u0259t\u026ava\u026az / (BrE)
(in computing) to convert an absolute file path or URL into a path that is relative to a specified base path, so the reference works within a different location or environment.
convert absolute path to relative (computing)

Examples

The build tool will relativise absolute paths to make the project portable across machines.
She relativised the image URL against the site root before deploying the website.
When packaging the app, developers often avoid hard-coded paths by relativising file references.
