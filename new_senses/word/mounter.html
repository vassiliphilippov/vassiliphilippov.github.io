mounter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntə / (BrE)
a skilled worker who mounts pictures, jewels, or similar items for display or use
skilled worker who mounts items

Examples

Each mounter must have an eye for detail to ensure the artwork is properly displayed.
She found work as a mounter in a picture framing shop after finishing her apprenticeship.
The mounter carefully set the diamond into its gold setting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framer - one who frames pictures or artworks
  • Synonym: setter - person who sets jewels or stones in place

Etymology

From mount (to fix in place) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntə / (BrE)
a machine used in electronics manufacturing to place or mount electronic components onto printed circuit boards, typically at high speed and with automated accuracy.
PCB component placement machine

Examples

The factory invested in a high-speed mounter to streamline PCB assembly.
Our SMT line stopped when the mounter jammed, delaying shipments by two days.
Engineers calibrated the mounter overnight to ensure precise placement of tiny capacitors.

  • Synonym: assembler - a device or person that assembles parts; in this context, a machine used to assemble PCBs
