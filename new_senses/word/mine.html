mine

1. NOUN

/ maɪn / (AmE), / maɪn / (BrE)
An excavation in the earth made for the purpose of extracting ores or minerals.
excavation for extracting minerals or ores

Examples

After years of operation, the old gold mine was finally closed.
The workers descended into the mine at sunrise to begin their shift.
They discovered a copper mine deep in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarry - place where stone is extracted from the earth

Etymology

From Old French 'mine' (vein of ore), from Medieval Latin 'mina'.

2. NOUN

/ maɪn / (AmE), / maɪn / (BrE)
an explosive device placed in or on the ground or water, designed to explode when contacted by a person, vehicle, or ship
explosive device triggered by contact

Examples

During the war, several soldiers were injured by a hidden mine near the riverbank.
Engineers carefully cleared the area of every known mine before beginning construction.
The ship sank after striking a naval mine off the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landmine - an explosive device placed on or just under the ground, designed to explode when stepped on or driven over

4. VERB

/ maɪn / (AmE), / maɪn / (BrE)
to obtain minerals, metals, or other valuable materials from the earth by excavation
extract minerals by excavation

Examples

Much of the region's coal was mined in the early twentieth century.
The company plans to mine gold in the remote hills next year.
They are mining copper deep beneath the mountains.

Synset Examples

mine ores and metals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavate - to dig out material from the ground
  • Synonym: quarry - to extract stone or minerals by open excavation

Etymology

From Old French *miner* (to mine, tunnel), from Medieval Latin *minare* (to drive, lead); related to 'mine' (noun).

5. VERB NEW

/ maɪn / (AmE), / maɪn / (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and blockchain technology) to use computer hardware to perform cryptographic work (often proof-of-work) that validates transactions, secures the network, and can create or award new units of cryptocurrency.
produce or validate cryptocurrency by computation

Examples

Many enthusiasts now mine several cryptocurrencies using powerful GPUs and ASICs.
She mined Bitcoin for months before selling her first small amount of BTC.
The company started mining the new token on-site to help secure its blockchain network.
