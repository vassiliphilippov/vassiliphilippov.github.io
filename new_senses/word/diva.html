diva

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.və / (AmE), / ˈdiː.və / (BrE)
a distinguished female opera singer, especially a leading or celebrated soprano
celebrated female opera singer

Examples

Many aspiring singers dream of becoming an international diva one day.
The acclaimed soprano was hailed as a true diva after her stunning performance at the Met.
The legend of the diva lives on in opera houses around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prima donna - the leading female singer in an opera or opera company

Etymology

From Italian 'diva', meaning 'goddess', from Latin 'diva' (goddess, divine one).
