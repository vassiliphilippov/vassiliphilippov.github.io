feudalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːdəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A social and economic system prevalent in medieval Europe, in which land was held by lords who granted it to vassals in exchange for military service and loyalty.
medieval European social system

Examples

Feudalism shaped the political and social structures of medieval Europe for centuries.
Historians debate the origins and decline of feudalism in different regions.
Under feudalism, peasants often worked the land for protection from the local lord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serfdom - the status or condition of being a serf in a feudal society
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system characterized by private or corporate ownership of capital goods

Etymology

From Medieval Latin feudalis (relating to a fee or fief) + -ism.
