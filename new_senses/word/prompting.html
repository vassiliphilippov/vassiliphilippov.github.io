prompting

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑmptɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒmptɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of encouraging someone to take a course of action, often by suggesting or persuading
the act of suggesting or encouraging action

Examples

It was only after her friend's prompting that she decided to apply for the job.
The manager's gentle prompting helped the team come up with creative ideas.
Without any prompting, he volunteered to share his experiences with the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support, confidence, or hope
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of trying to prevent someone from taking action

Etymology

From the verb 'prompt', which comes from Latin promptus (brought forth, ready).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑmptɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒmptɪŋ / (BrE)
a cue or reminder given to a performer, often the beginning of their next line in a play or performance
a cue given to a performer

Examples

Her quiet prompting helped the nervous child finish his part in the school play.
The actor waited anxiously for the prompting he needed to remember his line.
Without the stage manager's prompting, the performance might have stalled.

Synset Examples

the audience could hear his prompting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cue - signal for a performer to begin or continue action or speech

Etymology

From the verb 'prompt', which derives from Latin 'promptus' (brought forth, ready).
