tainted

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈteɪn.tɪd / (BrE)
spoiled or corrupted by rot, decay, or contamination; no longer pure or wholesome
spoiled or corrupted; no longer pure

Examples

He refused to eat the tainted bread after noticing a strange smell and green spots.
The inspector discovered that the shipment contained tainted meat unfit for human consumption.
The scandal left the company's reputation permanently tainted in the public eye.

Synset Examples

tainted bacon
‘corrupt’ is archaic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminated - made impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • Antonym: pure - not mixed with any other substance or free from contaminants

Etymology

From Middle English tainten, from Old French teindre, from Latin tingere (to dye, to tinge).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈteɪn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈteɪn.tɪd / (BrE)
(in computing, especially security and program analysis) marked as coming from an untrusted or potentially dangerous source and therefore requiring validation or sanitization.
untrusted or potentially dangerous data (computing)

Examples

The security team marked the suspicious parameter as tainted before running the sanitization routine.
During dynamic analysis, the tool tracked which variables became tainted after receiving external network data.
Developers must treat any tainted user input as dangerous until it has been properly validated.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untrusted - not trusted; data or sources that may be unsafe or malicious
  • Antonym: sanitized - processed to remove or neutralize harmful or unsafe content

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of Middle English tainten, from Old French teindre, from Latin tingere (to dye); applied in computing to mean 'marked as untrusted'.
