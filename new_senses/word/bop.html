bop

1. VERB

/ bɑp / (AmE), / bɒp / (BrE)
To dance to bebop music, especially in a lively or improvisational style typical of jazz culture.
dance to bebop music

Examples

Crowds gathered to bop to the live jazz band at the club.
They bopped all night, swept up in the energy of the music.
Young dancers love bopping to classic bebop tracks during festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bebop - a fast style of jazz music from the 1940s

Etymology

From 'bebop,' a style of jazz; 'bop' as a shortened form in jazz slang.

3. NOUN

/ bɑp / (AmE), / bɒp / (BrE)
an early form of modern jazz, developed around 1940, characterized by fast tempo and improvisation
early form of modern jazz

Examples

Bop revolutionized jazz by introducing complex harmonies and rapid improvisation.
He has an impressive collection of bop records from the genre's early days.
Many jazz musicians of the 1940s were drawn to bop for its creative freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bebop - a style of jazz developed in the 1940s with complex harmonies and rhythms

Etymology

Shortened from 'bebop', a term imitating the two-note phrase common in the music.

4. NOUN NEW

/ bɑp / (AmE), / bɒp / (BrE)
Informal: a catchy, upbeat song that is especially danceable and enjoyable.
catchy, danceable song (slang)
informal slang

Examples

That new track is a bop, I can't stop dancing whenever it comes on.
Radio stations keep playing the bop from the summer playlist every hour.
Her playlist is full of old-school Motown bops that make everyone get up and dance.
