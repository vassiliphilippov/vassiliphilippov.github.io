LF

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ɛf / (AmE), / ɛl ɛf / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'low frequency,' referring to radio frequencies in the range of 30 to 300 kilohertz
abbreviation for low frequency (30–300 kHz)

Examples

LF transmissions are less affected by atmospheric noise than higher frequency bands.
Signals in the LF range are able to travel great distances by ground wave propagation.
The navy uses the LF band for some types of underwater communication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: HF - high frequency (3 to 30 megahertz)
  • Degree Lower: VLF - very low frequency (3 to 30 kilohertz)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'low frequency'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ 5bl 5bf / (AmE), / 5bl 5bf / (BrE)
(internet, gaming) an initialism meaning 'looking for', used in chat, forum titles, or messages to indicate someone is seeking a player, group, item, or service.
online 'looking for' (seeking players/items)
informal slang

Examples

In game chat, players typed 'LF healer for the raid starting in an hour.'
After logging on, he posted 'LF tank and healer' to quickly form a dungeon group.
The guild chat often uses LF followed by the role to request players for events.
