Sus

1. NOUN

/ sʌs / (AmE), / sʌs / (BrE)
the genus of mammals that includes domestic pigs and their wild relatives
genus of pigs and wild boars

Examples

Researchers discovered a new subspecies within the genus Sus.
The domestic pig belongs to the genus Sus.
Wild boars are classified under Sus in zoological taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pig - a domesticated animal of the species Sus scrofa domestica
  • Synonym: boar - a wild pig, especially a male one

Etymology

From Latin *Sus* (pig, swine).

2. ADJ NEW

/ sʌs / (AmE), / sʌs / (BrE)
(informal, slang) Seeming suspicious, questionable, or likely dishonest; arousing doubt or distrust.
suspicious, questionable (slang)
slang informal

Examples

Everyone in the lobby thought his explanation sounded sus, so they voted him out.
She sent a screenshot of the chat and wrote that the player's offer looked sus.
If a deal seems too good to be true, it might be sus and you should check carefully.
