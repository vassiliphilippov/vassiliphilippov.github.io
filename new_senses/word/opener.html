opener

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənə / (BrE)
a device or tool used to open sealed containers such as bottles or cans
tool for opening bottles or cans

Examples

Do you have a opener for this jar?
He used a opener to pry the lid off the can.
The bartender reached for a opener to remove the beer cap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: can opener - a tool specifically for opening cans
  • Synonym: bottle opener - a tool specifically for opening bottles

Etymology

From the verb 'open' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənə / (BrE)
the first event, activity, or item in a series, especially the first in a sequence such as a performance or a sports season
first event in a series

Examples

For her opener, the pianist chose a lively sonata.
The baseball team's home opener attracted thousands of fans to the stadium.
The festival's opener was a compelling documentary that set the tone for the week.

Synset Examples

she played Chopin for her opener
the season's opener was a game against the Yankees

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finale - the last part or event in a series, especially in a performance or season
  • Synonym: premiere - the first public performance or showing of a play, film, etc.
  • Synonym: curtain-raiser - an event or performance that takes place before the main event

Etymology

From 'open' + '-er', denoting something that opens or begins.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈoʊpənər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a relief-style pitcher who begins the game and typically pitches only the first one or two innings before a conventional starter takes over.
relief pitcher who starts a game

Examples

Before the big game, the manager announced his opener, surprising many fans.
The young opener threw two perfect innings before the regular starter entered the game.
Several teams have used openers this season to disrupt traditional pitching plans.

Synset Examples

Before the big game, the manager announced his opener, surprising many fans.
The young opener threw two perfect innings before the regular starter entered the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief pitcher - a pitcher who enters the game after the starter; openers are often relief pitchers used to start a game
  • Antonym: starting pitcher - the pitcher who traditionally begins the game and is expected to pitch multiple innings; contrasted with an opener

Etymology

From the verb 'open' + agent suffix '-er'.
