condition

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an assumption or requirement on which the validity or effect of something else depends
an assumption for validity or effect

Examples

His offer included a strict condition that no changes be made.
Payment is subject to the condition that the work is completed on time.
The contract will only be valid under this condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proviso - a condition or stipulation in a contract or agreement
  • Synonym: requirement - something essential or necessary
  • Synonym: guarantee - an assurance for the fulfillment of a condition

Etymology

From Latin 'conditio' (agreement, situation, stipulation).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)

Examples

Participants in the control condition did not receive any form of intervention.
The experiment included a placebo condition and two treatment conditions for comparison.

Related Terms
