peacock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈpiːkɒk / (BrE)
A European butterfly (Aglais io) with reddish-brown wings, each marked with a purple eyespot.
European butterfly with eyespot markings

Examples

Many gardeners welcome the peacock for its beauty and pest control abilities.
The peacock settled on a flower, displaying the brilliant eyespots on its wings.
The wings of the peacock resemble the plumage of its namesake bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacock butterfly - European butterfly (Aglais io) with eyespot markings

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈpiːˌkɒk / (BrE)
The male peafowl, notable for its crested head and very large, fan-shaped tail with iridescent eye-like spots.
male peafowl with striking tail

Examples

A peacock strutted proudly across the garden, attracting everyone's attention.
The peacock displayed its magnificent tail feathers during the mating ritual.
The vibrant colors of the peacock make it a symbol of beauty in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peahen - female of the peafowl species

Etymology

From Middle English peacocke, from Old English pæcock, from 'pea' (peafowl) + 'cock' (male bird).
