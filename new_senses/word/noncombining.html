noncombining

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.kəmˈbaɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kəmˈbaɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
Not able to or not tending to combine with something else.
not able to combine

Examples

She studied the noncombining nature of certain rare earth elements.
The noncombining gases remained separate even under high pressure.
This particular chemical is considered noncombining under normal laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: combining - able to combine

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'non-' (not) to 'combining'.

(in Unicode and typography) not participating in combining; describing a character or code point that does not accept or attach combining diacritical marks.
not combining with diacritics (Unicode)

Examples

In Unicode, a noncombining character cannot accept following combining diacritical marks.
The font treats the space as noncombining, so accents remain unattached in rendering.
When normalizing text, the parser skips noncombining code points to preserve original diacritics.
