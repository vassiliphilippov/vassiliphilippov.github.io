jagua

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈɡwɑː / (AmE), / hɑːˈɡwɑː / (BrE)
A tree native to the West Indies and northern South America, bearing edible, succulent, orange-sized fruit.
tropical fruit tree of the Americas

Examples

Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the jagua tree.
She tasted the sweet flesh of a ripe jagua during her trip to Colombia.
The jagua produces large, juicy fruits enjoyed by local communities.

Etymology

From Spanish 'jagua', of indigenous Taíno origin.

2. NOUN NEW

/ hɑːˈɡwɑː / (AmE), / hɑːˈɡwɑː / (BrE)
A natural blue-black dye made from the fruit of the jagua tree, used to create temporary tattoos or body art.
natural blue-black tattoo dye

Examples

The artist mixed the plant extract to make jagua and painted a floral design on the client's arm.
Festival-goers lined up for a stall offering quick henna alternatives using jagua for temporary body art.
After washing, her dark jagua tattoo faded to a soft blue-gray over two weeks.
