cleanse

1. VERB

/ klɛnz / (AmE), / klɛnz / (BrE)
to make (someone's body or part of it) clean, especially by washing
wash to make clean (body or part)

Examples

Before prayer, he would always cleanse his hands and face.
Doctors recommend cleansing wounds thoroughly to prevent infection.
She cleansed her skin with a gentle soap every morning.

Synset Examples

clean up before you see your grandparents
clean your fingernails before dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make something free from dirt; to remove dirt from

Etymology

From Old English *clǣnsian* (to purify, make clean).

2. VERB

/ klɛnz / (AmE), / klɛnz / (BrE)
to rid a person, place, or thing of something unwanted, especially guilt, sin, or undesirable elements
purge of undesirable elements or sins

Examples

Meditation helped her to cleanse her mind of negative thoughts.
Some political movements aim to cleanse society of unwanted ideologies.
The priest said that confession would cleanse his soul of sin.

Synset Examples

Purgatory is supposed to cleanse you from your sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purify - remove impurities or foreign elements
  • Antonym: contaminate - make something impure by exposure to or addition of a harmful substance

Etymology

From Old English 'clænsian', related to 'clean' (pure, free from dirt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00502977-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN NEW

/ klɛnz / (AmE), / klɛnz / (BrE)
A regimen or period intended to purify or rid the body, mind, or environment of unwanted substances or influences (for example, a dietary detox or a break from social media).
regimen or period to purify
informal

Examples

She completed a seven-day cleanse of juices and smoothies to try to improve her digestion.
After months online, he decided on a weekend cleanse by deleting social apps and turning off notifications.
The company underwent a management cleanse, replacing several executives to improve public trust.

Synset Examples

She completed a seven-day cleanse of juices and smoothies to try to improve her digestion.
After months online, he decided on a weekend cleanse by deleting social apps and turning off notifications.
The company underwent a management cleanse, replacing several executives to improve public trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detox - a period of dietary or medical treatment intended to remove toxins from the body
  • Synonym: digital detox - a period of time when someone voluntarily reduces or stops using digital devices or social media

Etymology

Nominal use from the verb 'cleanse', from Old English *clænsian* (to purify, make clean).
