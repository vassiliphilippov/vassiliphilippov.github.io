assignable

1. ADJ

/ əˈsaɪn.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈsaɪn.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a right or property) able to be legally transferred to the ownership of another.
able to be legally transferred
legal

Examples

Only assignable rights can be transferred in this contract.
The company holds several assignable patents valuable to potential buyers.
The lease is assignable, subject to the landlord's written consent.

Synset Examples

negotiable bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transferable - able to be transferred from one person or place to another

Etymology

From Latin 'assignare' (to mark out, assign) via French 'assignable'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ əˈsaɪnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈsaɪnəbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being allocated or given to a person, role, or resource; able to be assigned (especially of tasks, issues, or responsibilities). Does not imply legal transfer of ownership.
able to be allocated or assigned

Examples

The project backlog lists several assignable tasks for a junior developer this sprint.
Make sure each bug report is assignable to a team member with the right expertise.
Only issues marked assignable will appear in the scheduler for next week's sprint planning.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocatable - able to be allocated or distributed to someone or something
  • Antonym: transferable - able to be transferred to another owner or party (legal transfer)
