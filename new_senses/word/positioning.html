positioning

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing something in a particular position or location
act of placing something

Examples

Proper positioning of the antenna is essential for better signal reception.
The careful positioning of the sculpture enhanced the beauty of the room.
The coach discussed the positioning of the players before the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placement - the act of putting something in a particular place
  • Antonym: removal - the act of taking something away from a place

Etymology

From 'position' + '-ing'; 'position' from Latin positio (a placing).

2. NOUN NEW

/ pəˈzɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of determining the geographic location of a device or object, typically using GPS, satellite, or network signals.
determining geographic location (GPS)

Examples

The GPS positioning system provides real-time coordinates for navigation apps.
Mariners rely on satellite positioning to maintain course during foggy, low-visibility conditions.
Modern smartphones combine Wi-Fi and GPS positioning to give faster, more accurate location data.
