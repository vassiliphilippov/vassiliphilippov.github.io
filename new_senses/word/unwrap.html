to unwrap

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈræp / (AmE), / ʌnˈrap / (BrE)
to remove the covering or wrapping from something
remove the covering from

Examples

Carefully, he unwrapped the fragile vase from its many layers of paper.
She watched eagerly as he began to unwrap the present.
The children couldn't wait to unwrap their Christmas gifts.

Synset Examples

Let's unwrap the gifts!
undo the parcel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wrap - to cover or enclose something with paper, cloth, etc.

Etymology

From un- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + wrap (from Middle English 'wrappen').

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈræp / (AmE), / ʌnˈrap / (BrE)
to make information or something previously secret known to the public; to reveal or disclose
reveal or make information public

Examples

During the press conference, the CEO decided to unwrap the company's future plans.
Investigators hope to unwrap the mystery behind the missing documents soon.
The journalist tried to unwrap the truth hidden in the politician's statement.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case
The newspaper uncovered the President's illegal dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make known something that was secret or hidden
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret; to hide

Etymology

From un- (reversal) + wrap, originally referring to removing a wrapping but used figuratively for revealing information.

3. VERB NEW

/ \u028cn\u02c8r\u00e6p / (AmE), / \u028cn\u02c8rap / (BrE)
(in computing) to obtain or extract the value contained inside a wrapped data type (for example Option/Maybe/Result), usually removing the container and producing the inner value or an error if no value is present.
extract value from a wrapped data type (programming)

Examples

In Rust, you must unwrap the Option to access its value.
Rather than calling unwrap, handle the None case to avoid runtime panics in production code.
The unit tests failed because the code attempted to unwrap a Result that contained an error.

Synset Examples

From un- (prefix meaning 'reverse, remove') + wrap; used metaphorically in computing to mean removing a value from a container.
