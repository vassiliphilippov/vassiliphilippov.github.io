poison

1. VERB

/ ˈpɔɪzən / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən / (BrE)
to kill or harm someone or something by giving them poison
kill or harm with poison

Examples

Jealous rivals tried to poison the king, but he survived the attempt.
She was accused of poisoning the well to drive out the villagers.
The detective suspected that someone had poisoned the victim's tea.

Synset Examples

She poisoned her husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisonous - containing or producing poison; harmful

Etymology

From Old French *poison* and Latin *potiō* (a drink, potion).
