skunkweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌŋkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈskʌŋkˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall herb native to the Rocky Mountains, known for its sticky leaves and unpleasant odor.
a foul-smelling Rocky Mountain herb

Examples

Gardeners often avoid planting skunkweed because of its sticky leaves and strong smell.
The skunkweed in the field gave off an odor that was hard to ignore.
We found a patch of skunkweed growing along the riverbank on our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - a plant with a foul smell, also called swamp cabbage

Etymology

From 'skunk' (a mammal known for its strong odor) + 'weed' (a wild plant).
