boomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːmər / (AmE), / ˈbuːmə / (BrE)
a person born during the baby boom, especially in the years following the Second World War, typically between 1946 and 1964
member of the baby boom generation

Examples

As a boomer, she remembers the social changes of the 1960s.
Many boomers are now approaching retirement age.
The government developed new policies to address the needs of boomers.

Synset Examples

they expanded the schools for a generation of baby boomers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby boomer - someone born during the baby boom period (1946–1964)

Etymology

From 'baby boom' plus the agent noun suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbuːmər / (AmE), / ˈbuːmə / (BrE)
informal, slang. A person (often older) who is perceived as out-of-date, resistant to change, or unaware of contemporary trends; used especially online as a dismissive label.
slang: older person seen as out of touch
slang informal offensive

Examples

When they complained about new apps, the students jokingly called their teacher a boomer.
The meme calling anyone who dislikes trendy slang a boomer spread across social media quickly.
He laughed off being labeled a boomer and said age doesn't determine one's tech skills anymore.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby boomer - a member of the baby boom generation (born 1946–1964)
  • Synonym: out of touch - not aware of or concerned about modern ideas or developments
  • Antonym: zoomer - a member of Generation Z; often invoked in contrast to 'boomer' in online discourse

Etymology

From 'baby boomer' (a person born 1946–1964); extended in 21st-century internet slang to mean someone seen as out-of-touch.
