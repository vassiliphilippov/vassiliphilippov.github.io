DMZ

1. NOUN

/ di ɛm zi / (AmE), / diː ɛm zɛd / (BrE)
an area, usually established by treaty, in which military forces and activities are not permitted
demilitarized zone

Examples

Civilians are generally prohibited from entering the DMZ without special permission.
Negotiations focused on establishing a DMZ to prevent further conflict in the region.
The border between North and South Korea is one of the most heavily guarded DMZ areas in the world.

Synset Examples

tensions exist on both sides of the demilitarized zone separating North Korea and South Korea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demilitarized zone - an area where no military forces or installations are permitted

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'demilitarized zone'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ di ɛm zi / (AmE), / diː ɛm zɛd / (BrE)
(in computing, especially networking and cybersecurity) a subnetwork that separates an internal trusted network from an untrusted external network, while hosting public-facing services (for example, web or mail servers) to reduce risk to internal systems.
network subzone hosting public services

Examples

The company's public web server is placed in the DMZ to reduce risk.
Network administrators often monitor the DMZ more closely than internal segments during attacks.
Many organizations operate multiple DMZs to separate services with different security policies.

Synset Examples

The company's public web server is placed in the DMZ to reduce risk.
Network administrators often monitor the DMZ more closely than internal segments during attacks.
Many organizations operate multiple DMZs to separate services with different security policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perimeter network - a network segment that provides a boundary between an internal network and external networks, often hosting public services
  • Antonym: internal network - the trusted, private network inside an organization that the DMZ is designed to protect

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'demilitarized zone'; extended metaphorically from the military sense to describe an isolated, public-facing network segment.
