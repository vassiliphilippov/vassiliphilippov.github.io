polarity

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˈlarəti / (BrE)
the presence or manifestation of two opposed or contrasting principles or tendencies
condition of opposites or contrast

Examples

In her novel, the author explores the polarity of hope and despair.
Political debates often highlight the polarity between conservative and liberal viewpoints.
The polarity of light and darkness is a common theme in art and philosophy.

Synset Examples

he viewed it as a balanced polarity between good and evil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duality - the state of having two parts, often with opposite meanings
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole

Etymology

From Late Latin 'polaritas', from Latin 'polus' (pole).

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˈlarəti / (BrE)
the property of having distinct and opposite poles, such as positive and negative electric charges or magnetic ends
the state of having opposite poles (e.g. in electricity)

Examples

Many electrical accidents happen because people confuse the polarity of the terminals.
The battery's polarity must be matched to the circuit for proper operation.
When connecting the wires, make sure the polarity is correct to avoid damaging the device.

Synset Examples

he got the polarity of the battery reversed
charges of opposite sign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duality - the state of having two parts, often with opposite characteristics
  • Antonym: neutrality - the state of not having a positive or negative charge or pole

Etymology

From Latin polaris (of or relating to a pole), from polus (pole).

3. NOUN NEW

/ poʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˈlarəti / (BrE)
(in computing and linguistics) the orientation of sentiment expressed in a text or utterance, indicating whether it is positive, negative, or neutral.
sentiment orientation of text

Examples

The sentiment analysis tool flagged the post's polarity as positive.
Researchers measured the polarity of thousands of tweets during the election to track public mood.
Customer service uses comments to assess product polarity, helping prioritize issues for the support team.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: tone - the general character or attitude of a piece of writing or speech, often related to sentiment

Etymology

From Late Latin 'polaritas', from Latin 'polus' (pole); extended in modern computing and linguistics to mean sentiment orientation.
