stealer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstiːlə / (BrE)
a person who takes property belonging to someone else without permission, with the intention of keeping it or selling it; a thief
a person who steals; a thief

Examples

Parents talked to their children about why being a stealer is wrong and unfair to others.
The museum put new measures in place after a notorious art stealer targeted their collection.
The security camera captured the stealer running out of the store with a bag of electronics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals
  • Synonym: robber - a person who takes property illegally, often using force

Etymology

From the verb 'steal' + -er, denoting agent noun formation.

(in cybersecurity) a piece of malicious software that secretly collects sensitive data (for example, passwords, tokens, cookies) from a computer or device and sends it to an attacker.
malware that steals sensitive data

Examples

Security researchers detected a new stealer that harvested browser cookies and passwords from infected machines.
After the breach, the company removed the malicious stealer hiding in third-party extensions.
Many antivirus tools now flag popular credential stealers that attempt silent data exfiltration.
