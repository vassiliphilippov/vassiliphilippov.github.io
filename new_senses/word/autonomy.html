autonomy

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəmi / (BrE)
the ability to make your own decisions independently; personal independence or self-government
personal independence; self-government

Examples

After years of strict rules, he finally experienced the autonomy he had always desired.
Many employees value autonomy in the workplace and prefer to set their own schedules.
The teenager wanted more autonomy in making her own choices about her future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - the state of being independent, especially personal self-reliance
  • Antonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else

Etymology

From Greek 'autonomos' (self-governing), from 'autos' (self) + 'nomos' (law).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɔnəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəmi / (BrE)
the right or condition of self-government, especially in a political context; freedom from external control or influence
self-government; political independence

Examples

Cultural autonomy is important for preserving minority languages.
Many former colonies fought for autonomy in the twentieth century.
The region demanded greater autonomy from the central government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - the fact or state of being independent
  • Synonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority
  • Antonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under domination or control

Etymology

From Greek autonomia, from autonomos (having its own laws): auto- (self) + nomos (law).

3. NOUN NEW

/ əˈtɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəmi / (BrE)
(in computing, robotics, and AI) the ability of a machine, robot, or software system to perform tasks and make decisions without direct human control or continuous human guidance.
independence of machines or AI

Examples

Self-driving cars demonstrate increased autonomy in complex urban traffic during rush hour.
Engineers test the drone's autonomy by giving it unforeseen tasks without human guidance.
Concerns about safety rise as household robots gain more autonomy over daily chores.
