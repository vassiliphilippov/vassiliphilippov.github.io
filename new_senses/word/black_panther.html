Black Panther

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈpænθɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈpænθə / (BrE)
A member of the Black Panther Party, a revolutionary political organization founded in the United States in the 1960s advocating for the rights of African Americans.
member of the Black Panther Party

Examples

He proudly identified himself as a former Black Panther during the community meeting.
Many young activists became Black Panthers to fight against racial injustice in the 1960s.
The influence of the Black Panthers can still be felt in civil rights discussions today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black Panther Party - Revolutionary Black nationalist and socialist organization in the US (1966–1982)

Etymology

Named after the panther symbol adopted by the political party founded in Oakland, California, in 1966.

2. NOUN NEW

/ blæk ˈpænθə / (AmE), / blæk ˈpænθə / (BrE)
A Marvel Comics superhero (king of the fictional African nation Wakanda), who appears in comic books and films and is known for advanced technology and leadership.
Marvel superhero and Wakandan king

Examples

Fans cheered as Black Panther showcased his Wakandan technology during the film's most exciting battle scene.
The comic introduces Black Panther as T'Challa, the king and protector of the fictional nation Wakanda.
Children dressed as Black Panther for the convention, complete with a costume and mask.
