rustle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌsl / (BrE)
a soft, light sound such as the noise made by dry leaves or silk when they move or are rubbed together
soft, light noise (e.g. leaves, silk)

Examples

He heard a faint rustle behind the curtains and turned around.
The rustle of her silk dress echoed as she entered the room.
The rustle of leaves was all she could hear in the quiet forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct sound
  • Synonym: swish - a soft, rustling sound like something moving softly through air
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07407383-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈrʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌsəl / (BrE)
to search for and gather food, especially by animals foraging in the wild
search for food, forage

Examples

Every morning, the deer could be seen rustling for berries near the stream.
In autumn, wild pigs rustle through the forest, searching for acorns.
The cattle rustle for food in the open fields from dawn until dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forage - to search widely for food or provisions
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02274935-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌs.əl / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a soft, dry, crackling sound
make a soft crackling sound

Examples

As he walked, his trousers rustled softly against each other.
She heard someone rustling papers in the office next door.
The autumn leaves rustle as the wind sweeps through the park.

Synset Examples

rustling silk
the dry leaves were rustling in the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackle - make a series of small, sharp sounds
  • Degree Lower: whisper - speak very quietly

Etymology

Late Middle English, probably imitative of the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02187108-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈrʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌsəl / (BrE)
to steal (livestock, especially cattle) from someone illegally
steal livestock illegally

Examples

For decades, outlaws would rustle livestock and sell them on the black market.
He was arrested after trying to rustle a herd of sheep from the farm.
The gang planned to rustle cattle from neighboring ranches under the cover of darkness.

Synset Examples

rustle cattle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something illegally or without permission
  • Antonym: legal - permitted by law

Etymology

Originally meant 'to make a soft, fluttering sound'; sense of 'to steal cattle' developed in US English in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
