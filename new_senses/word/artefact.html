artefact

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑr.t̬ə.fækt / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.fækt / (BrE)
An object made by a human being, typically one of historical or cultural interest.
human-made object, often historical

Examples

Each artefact in the collection has its own unique story.
Researchers carefully cleaned the fragile artefact for further analysis.
The museum displayed an ancient artefact that was unearthed during the excavation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artifact - alternative spelling, especially American

Etymology

From Latin 'arte factum', meaning 'something made with skill'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0251r.t\u032c\u0259.f\u00e6kt / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0251\u02d0.t\u026a.f\u00e6kt / (BrE)
An unintended distortion, anomaly, or visible/heard error in a digital image, video, audio recording, or dataset, produced by processing, compression, transmission, or measurement.
unintended digital distortion or anomaly

Examples

The photo shows a clear artefact caused by heavy JPEG compression.
Video editors removed a distracting artefact that appeared after color grading the clip.
Signal interference produced an unusual artefact in the audio recording of the interview.
