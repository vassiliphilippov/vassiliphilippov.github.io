speaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːkər / (AmE), / ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
a person who speaks, especially someone who gives a formal talk to an audience
person who speaks; lecturer or orator

Examples

After the speaker finished his talk, the audience applauded enthusiastically.
He is a talented speaker who often gives lectures on history.
The speaker at the conference inspired the audience with her story.

Synset Examples

the speaker at commencement
an utterer of useful maxims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orator - someone who delivers formal speeches
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens

Etymology

From Old English *specere* (to speak) via Middle English *spekere*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspiːkər / (AmE), / ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
A device that converts electrical signals into audible sound, typically used to play music or other audio.
audio device that produces sound

Examples

I bought a new speaker for my home office to enjoy better sound quality.
Please lower the volume on the speaker so it's not too loud.
The band connected their instruments to the speaker for the outdoor concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudspeaker - electro-acoustic device that emits sound
  • Antonym: microphone - device that converts sound into electrical signals

a voice-activated, internet-connected audio device that can play sound and act as a virtual assistant or smart-home hub.
voice‑activated internet speaker / virtual assistant

Examples

She asked the speaker to play jazz and dim the living room lights.
The new speaker connects to Wi‑Fi and answers questions about the weather.
During the party, everyone used the speaker to queue songs by voice.
