sleep

1. NOUN

/ sliːp / (AmE), / sliːp / (BrE)
a natural, recurring state of rest in which the body and mind are inactive and consciousness is suspended
state of rest with suspended consciousness

Examples

After a long day, she enjoyed a peaceful sleep.
Getting enough sleep each night is important for good health.
The noise outside kept him from falling into a deep sleep.

Synset Examples

he didn't get enough sleep last night
calm as a child in dreamless slumber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slumber - a state of sleep
  • Antonym: wakefulness - the state of being awake

Etymology

Old English 'slǣp', related to Dutch 'slaap' and German 'Schlaf'.

5. NOUN

/ sliːp / (AmE), / sliːp / (BrE)
A state of inactivity or dormancy resembling deep sleep, often used to describe hibernation or a similar torpid state in animals or plants.
torpid or dormant state

Examples

During winter, the bear enters a deep sleep that lasts for months.
Some seeds remain in a kind of sleep until the right conditions for growth appear.
The frog's sleep during colder months helps it survive harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibernation - the state of inactivity and metabolic depression in animals during winter
  • Synonym: dormancy - a state in which something is not active but able to become active

6. VERB

/ sliːp / (AmE), / sliːp / (BrE)
to rest with eyes closed and mind and body inactive, usually at night, in a state in which one is not conscious and the body recovers strength
to rest in an unconscious state

Examples

He couldn't sleep because of the loud thunderstorm outside.
It is important to sleep at least eight hours each night for good health.
Sometimes I sleep late on weekends to catch up on rest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nap - to sleep for a short period, especially during the day
  • Degree Lower: doze - to sleep lightly or for a short time
  • Synonym: slumber - to sleep, especially peacefully or deeply
  • Antonym: wake - to emerge or cause to emerge from sleep; to be or become awake

Etymology

7. NOUN NEW

/ sliːp / (AmE), / sliːp / (BrE)
a low-power operating state of an electronic device in which most functions are suspended to conserve energy while allowing quick resumption of full operation.
device power-saving state

Examples

The laptop entered sleep after only five minutes of inactivity to save battery power.
When in sleep, the tablet uses minimal energy but resumes instantly when you press a button.
Adjust your settings so the computer goes into sleep automatically when idle for long.
