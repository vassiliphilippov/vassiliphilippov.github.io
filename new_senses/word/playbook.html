playbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌbʊk / (BrE)
a book containing the scripts of one or more dramatic plays
book of scripts for plays

Examples

She borrowed a playbook from the library to study different scenes.
The director referred to the playbook to check the actors' lines.
Their new playbook includes several classic comedies and tragedies.

Synset Examples

the 1963 playbook leaves out the whole first scene

Etymology

From 'play' (theatrical drama) + 'book'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌbʊk / (BrE)
a set of strategies or planned actions for achieving success in business, politics, or a similar competitive arena
plan or strategy guide, esp. in business or politics

Examples

The candidate's campaign seemed to use the same playbook as her predecessor.
The company followed a proven playbook to outmaneuver its competitors in the market.
They borrowed key strategies from the opposition's playbook to improve their own chances of winning.

Synset Examples

they borrowed a page from the playbook of the opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term goal
  • Synonym: tactics - carefully planned actions to achieve a specific end

Etymology

Originally from American football, where 'playbook' refers to a book of plays and tactics.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05916073-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpleɪˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌbʊk / (BrE)
(in computing, especially IT/DevOps) a file or script that defines a set of automated tasks to configure, deploy, or manage computer systems.
automation script for IT tasks

Examples

The team updated the playbook to patch all servers across environments.
She ran the Ansible playbook to deploy the new version to the staging cluster.
Before the incident, they stored their disaster-recovery playbooks in a secure repository for automation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - a program or set of commands that automates tasks on a computer
  • Synonym: runbook - a documented set of procedures for operating or recovering computer systems, often used interchangeably with automation playbooks

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from 'playbook' meaning a set of strategies (originally from American football), applied to a file of automated procedures.
