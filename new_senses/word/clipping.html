clipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
a small piece cut from a newspaper or magazine, typically containing an article, photograph, or advertisement
excerpt from a newspaper or magazine

Examples

He glued a sports clipping onto the project board for his presentation.
My grandmother keeps a clipping of my first published article in her scrapbook.
The detective found an old newspaper clipping in the victim's wallet.

Synset Examples

he searched through piles of letters and clippings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - a clipping from a newspaper or magazine

Etymology

From the verb 'clip' (to cut), + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cutting something down to the desired size or shape, or the piece that is cut off
act or result of cutting to size or shape

Examples

Grass clippings covered the yard after the lawn was mowed.
She collected the nail clipping and threw it in the trash.
The tailor made a small clipping from the cloth to show the customer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - the act of making an incision or removal by a sharp instrument

Etymology

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈklɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(in machine learning) the practice of limiting the size (magnitude) of gradients during training, to prevent unstable updates such as exploding gradients.
limiting gradient magnitude (machine learning)

Examples

To stabilize training, researchers applied clipping to the gradients during backpropagation.
Gradient clipping prevented the loss from skyrocketing when the network's parameters exploded.
Many tutorials recommend norm-based clipping as a simple fix for exploding gradients in RNNs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clip - to cut off or limit; used figuratively in computing for restricting numerical values
  • Antonym: exploding gradient - a problem in neural-network training where gradients become excessively large, which clipping is used to prevent

Etymology

From the verb 'clip' (to cut off); used metaphorically in computing to mean 'limit' (21st-century usage in machine learning).
