redraft

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˈdræft / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdrɑːft / (BrE)
a draft issued for the sum of a previously dishonored draft plus any additional charges or costs incurred for issuing it again
draft for dishonored draft plus costs

Examples

After the original check bounced, a redraft was drawn for the total amount owing.
International transactions sometimes require a redraft if the initial payment is dishonored.
The bank issued a redraft to recover the unpaid sum and the additional fees.

Etymology

Formed from prefix 're-' (again) + 'draft' (bill of exchange).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌriˈdræft / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdrɑːft / (BrE)
In fantasy sports, a league format in which teams are drafted anew each season so rosters do not carry over.
fantasy league with new draft each season

Examples

We joined a redraft league this year to rebuild our fantasy team.
Most casual players prefer a redraft format because rosters reset each season.
After several losing seasons, the manager decided to switch to a redraft competition.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: keeper league - a fantasy-sports league in which managers keep some players across seasons; opposite of a redraft league
