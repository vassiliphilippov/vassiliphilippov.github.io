controller

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊlər / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for maintaining and auditing the financial accounts of a business or organization
person who oversees business accounts

Examples

After discovering discrepancies in the budget, the controller ordered a complete audit.
The company's controller prepared the annual financial statements for the board of directors.
They hired a new controller to improve financial oversight and compliance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comptroller - an alternative term for controller, especially in government and public organizations

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'countreroller', from Old French 'contreroleur', based on 'contrerole' (copy of a roll, register).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊlɚ / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
a device or mechanism that regulates or directs the operation of a machine or system
device that controls a machine or system

Examples

If the controller fails, the entire machine can stop working.
Modern heating systems often include a digital controller for temperature regulation.
The engineer adjusted the controller to increase the speed of the conveyor belt.

Synset Examples

the speed controller on his turntable was not working properly
I turned the controls over to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulator - a device used to control the rate or quantity of something
  • Degree Higher: controller - a person who directs or manages operations

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'contreroller' (keep a copy of a roll), later extended to devices exerting regulation or control.

3. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊlər / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who directs, manages, and restrains the actions or operations of something or someone
person who directs or restrains

Examples

As a financial controller, she scrutinized every expense before approval.
He acted as the controller of the group, making key decisions and keeping everyone on track.
The controller ensured all procedures were followed correctly during the launch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person who is in charge of controlling or administering all or part of a company or similar organization

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman contreroller (to check, audit), from Old French contrerouler (to register), from Medieval Latin contrarotulare.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN NEW

/ kənˈtroʊlər / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
(in data protection law) an individual or organisation that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data and is legally responsible for compliance with data-protection obligations.
entity that determines data processing purposes
legal

Examples

Under the GDPR, the controller decides which personal data the company may collect and why.
The privacy officer explained that as controller, the nonprofit must ensure lawful processing and transparency.
When multiple organisations are involved, a joint controller agreement clarifies each party's responsibilities under law.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: data controller - the legal term for an entity that determines why and how personal data are processed
  • Antonym: data processor - an organisation or person that processes personal data on behalf of the controller
