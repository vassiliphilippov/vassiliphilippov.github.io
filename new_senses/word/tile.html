tile

1. NOUN

/ taɪl / (AmE), / taɪl / (BrE)
a small, flat, thin piece marked with characters or symbols, used as a playing piece in board games such as Mah-Jong or Scrabble.
flat piece used in board games

Examples

In Scrabble, each tile has a different point value based on the letter.
She drew a bamboo tile during her turn in Mah-Jong.
Some games require players to shuffle all the tiles before starting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: token - a piece used to represent a player or move in a board game
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04442512-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ taɪl / (AmE), / taɪl / (BrE)
a flat, thin, usually rectangular piece of hard material used for covering roofs, floors, or walls
rectangular slab for covering surfaces

Examples

She chose a blue mosaic tile for the kitchen backsplash.
The bathroom floor was covered with ceramic tile.
Workers replaced the old roof tiles after the storm damaged them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shingle - a thin, flat piece of material used for roofing, similar but typically narrower than a tile

Etymology

From Latin tegula (roof tile) via Old French tuile.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04442314-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ taɪl / (AmE), / taɪl / (BrE)
a thin, flat piece of baked clay, stone, or other material, used especially for covering roofs, floors, or walls
thin slab for roofing or covering surfaces

Examples

Each tile was carefully placed to create a patterned effect on the floor.
The house had traditional clay tiles covering its sloping roof.
The roof was repaired with new tiles after the storm damaged the old ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shingle - a thin, flat piece of wood or other material, used for covering roofs or walls

Etymology

From Latin 'tegula' (roof tile), via Old French 'tuile' and Middle English 'tile'.
