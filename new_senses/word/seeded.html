5. ADJ

/ ˈsiːdɪd / (AmE), / ˈsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a competitor in a tournament) given a place in the draw according to ranking, so as to avoid meeting other top competitors in the early rounds.
arranged by ranking in a tournament draw

Examples

All seeded teams received byes into the second round.
She was seeded second in the international tennis tournament.
The top seeded players won't face each other until the semifinals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unseeded - not ranked or assigned a preferential position in a tournament

Etymology

From the noun 'seed' (in sports), relating to the practice of 'planting' players in the draw as one would plant seeds.

(in computing) supplied or pre-populated with initial 'seed' data so the system or dataset is ready for testing, demonstration, or development.
pre-populated with initial data (computing)

Examples

The development database is seeded with user accounts and sample records for testing.
Before demos, they always ensure the demo site has been seeded with realistic example data.
The migration script seeded the new environment with default settings, admin users, and test orders.
