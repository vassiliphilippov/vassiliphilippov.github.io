garden

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
a piece of land, often next to a house, where flowers, vegetables, or other plants are grown and cared for
piece of land for growing plants

Examples

She spends all morning working in her garden, planting new flowers and pulling weeds.
The children played soccer on the lawn behind the garden.
Their garden produces fresh vegetables every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yard - an area of ground next to a house, often covered with grass
  • Degree Higher: park - a large public green area for recreation

Etymology

From Old Northern French *gardin*, variant of Old French *jardin* (garden).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
the flowers, vegetables, fruits, or herbs that are grown in a garden
plants grown in a garden

Examples

After the rain, the garden looked greener and more vibrant than ever.
She brought a basket of flowers from her own garden to decorate the kitchen.
The garden is full of ripe tomatoes and fresh herbs this summer.

Etymology

From Old Northern French *gardin* (French *jardin*), from Vulgar Latin *hortus gardinus* (enclosed garden).

3. VERB

/ ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to cultivate or care for a garden as a hobby or occupation
work in or care for a garden

Examples

He has gardened for over ten years and grows vegetables every summer.
Many people garden as a way to relax and enjoy nature.
She loves to garden on weekends when the weather is nice.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultivate - to prepare and use land for crops or gardening
  • Degree Higher: farm - to work on or manage a farm

From Old North French *gardin* (enclosure), from Vulgar Latin *hortus gardinus* (enclosed garden).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
a piece of ground, often adjoining a house, used for growing flowers, fruit, or vegetables, or as a place for recreation
a cultivated area near a house

Examples

Every morning, he waters the flowers in his garden.
She planted roses along the edge of the garden behind her house.
The children played football on the garden all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yard - an area of ground around a house, especially in American English
  • Synonym: lawn - an area of short, mown grass in a garden or park

Etymology

From Old Northern French *gardin*, from Frankish *gard* (enclosure).
