actionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.ʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈæk.ʃən.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Giving sufficient cause for legal action; subject to a lawsuit.
subject to a lawsuit
legal

Examples

His remarks were considered actionable under defamation law.
If the client's claim is actionable, the lawyer will proceed with a lawsuit.
The company discovered several actionable breaches of contract in the report.

Synset Examples

slander is an actionable offense

Etymology

From Medieval Latin actionabilis, from Latin actio (a legal action, a doing).

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈæk.ʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈæk.ʃən.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Capable of being acted on; (of information, advice, or insights) practical and specific enough to be implemented as concrete steps.
practical; can be acted on

Examples

The consultant delivered actionable recommendations that reduced costs within weeks.
We need actionable data from the survey to decide which product features to build next.
The threat intelligence report lacked actionable details such as IP addresses and timestamps.
