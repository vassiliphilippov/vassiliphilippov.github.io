overcloud

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈklaʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklaʊd / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) to become covered with clouds, making it cloudy or overcast.
become covered with clouds

Examples

As the temperature dropped, the horizon seemed to overcloud rapidly.
By mid-morning, the sun had vanished as heavy gray clouds overclouded the skyline.
The sky began to overcloud just before the afternoon storm arrived.

Synset Examples

The sky clouded over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud over - to become cloudy or overcast
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02776978-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈklaʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklaʊd / (BrE)
to make something unclear or difficult to understand; to obscure or cloud the meaning of something
make unclear or obscure

Examples

As the years passed, memory began to overcloud the details of the event.
Personal bias may overcloud his ability to judge the facts objectively.
Unnecessary technical jargon can overcloud a simple explanation.

Synset Examples

The distinction was obscured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - make unclear or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarify - make easy to understand or clear

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'cloud over'; from 'over-' + 'cloud', meaning to cover or obscure as clouds do.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00314513-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈklaʊd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈklaʊd / (BrE)
to make something less clear, especially by obscuring the main issue or making it harder to understand
make less clear; obscure main point

Examples

His lengthy explanation only overclouded the main point of the meeting.
The debate became confusing when unrelated issues overclouded the topic at hand.
Try not to overcloud your argument with irrelevant data.

Synset Examples

Let’s not overcloud the discussion with unnecessary details.
We must not overcloud the main issue with distractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: clarify - to make something clearer and easier to understand

4. NOUN NEW

/ oʊvərˈklaʊd / (AmE), / əʊvəˈklaʊd / (BrE)
(in cloud computing, esp. OpenStack) the top-level cloud deployment that provides tenant-facing services and virtual resources; typically deployed on top of an administrative 'undercloud'.
top-level cloud deployment (OpenStack)

Examples

The OpenStack team deployed an overcloud to provide virtual machines for multiple tenants.
During testing they noticed the overcloud failed to scale under heavy network traffic and load.
Operators use configuration files to customize each overcloud when provisioning resources for customers.

Synset Examples

The OpenStack team deployed an overcloud to provide virtual machines for multiple tenants.
During testing they noticed the overcloud failed to scale under heavy network traffic and load.
Operators use configuration files to customize each overcloud when provisioning resources for customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud - a network of remote servers providing computing services such as storage and virtual machines
  • Antonym: undercloud - the lower-level administrative cloud used to deploy and manage the overcloud in OpenStack

Etymology

From OpenStack usage (early 2010s); formed from over- + cloud to denote the layer above the management cloud.
