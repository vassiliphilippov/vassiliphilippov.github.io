bandwidth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændˌwɪdθ / (AmE), / ˈbæn(d)ˌwɪdθ / (BrE)
the maximum rate at which data can be transmitted over a network or internet connection, typically measured in bits per second
data transmission capacity (IT)

Examples

If too many devices are connected, the available bandwidth may not be sufficient for everyone's needs.
Our office upgraded its bandwidth to improve download speeds for all employees.
Streaming high-definition videos requires a high bandwidth internet connection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitrate - rate at which bits are transmitted over a network
  • Antonym: latency - delay before data transfer begins following an instruction

Etymology

Originally from 'band' (range) + 'width' (measure across); adopted in electronics to mean range of frequencies, later applied to data transmission.

2. NOUN NEW

informal, figurative: a person's or organization's available capacity — especially time, attention, or mental resources — to deal with tasks or responsibilities.
available personal or organisational capacity
informal

Examples

The team doesn't have the bandwidth to add another feature this quarter.
With meetings all day, she lacked the bandwidth to focus on the grant proposal.
We need to free up some bandwidth before taking on a large new client project.
