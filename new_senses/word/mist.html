mist

1. VERB

/ mɪst / (AmE), / mɪst / (BrE)
to spray something finely in the form of tiny droplets, or to cover with a light, fine spray resembling mist
spray or cover with fine droplets

Examples

After exercising, he likes to mist his face with cool water.
She used a bottle to mist the delicate leaves every morning.
The florist gently misted the flowers to keep them fresh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to send out liquid in small drops over an area

2. NOUN

/ mɪst / (AmE), / mɪst / (BrE)
A thin fog consisting of tiny water droplets suspended in the air near the ground, making it difficult to see clearly.
thin fog near the ground

Examples

A soft mist covered the meadow at dawn, making the landscape look mysterious.
She could barely see the road ahead through the thick mist of the early morning.
The mountains were shrouded in mist after the rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fog - dense vapor that obscures visibility
  • Degree Lower: haze - slight obscuration of the atmosphere, less dense than mist

Etymology

From Old English 'mist' (dimness, cloud of vapor).

3. VERB

/ mɪst / (AmE), / mɪst / (BrE)
to make something less visible or unclear by covering it with or as if with mist
make less visible or unclear

Examples

Heavy fog can mist the headlights, making driving dangerous.
The photographer worried that the rain would mist his lens and ruin the shot.
The windows began to mist over as the temperature dropped.

Synset Examples

The stars are obscured by the clouds
the big elm tree obscures our view of the valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - make something difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: clear - make or become easy to see or understand

Etymology

Old English *mist* (mist, fog).

5. NOUN NEW

/ mɪst / (AmE), / mɪst / (BrE)

cosmetic facial spray

Examples

She used a hydrating facial mist before applying her makeup.
On hot days he kept a cooling mist in his bag to freshen his skin.
The brand released a new vitamin C mist that brightened her complexion after one week.
