carrousel

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈsɛl / (BrE)
A large, rotating amusement ride with seats, often shaped like animals, for children to ride on.
rotating amusement ride for children

Examples

Brightly colored horses spun around and around on the old carrousel.
She bought tickets so they could enjoy the carrousel together.
The children rushed eagerly to ride the carrousel at the town fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carousel - a large, rotating machine with seats for children to ride on

Etymology

From French 'carrousel', itself from Italian 'carosello'.

2. NOUN

/ kæɹəˌsɛl / (AmE), / kærəˈsɛl / (BrE)
a rotating conveyor belt at an airport from which arriving passengers collect their luggage
airport luggage conveyor belt

Examples

After landing, he hurried to the carrousel to retrieve his luggage.
Her bag was the last one to arrive on the carrousel.
We waited by the carrousel for our suitcases to appear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carousel - a rotating conveyor belt for luggage, more common spelling

Etymology

From French 'carrousel', variant of 'carousel'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ kærəˈsɛl / (AmE), / kærəˈsɛl / (BrE)
A digital interface component that automatically or manually cycles through a series of images or items (for example on a website or app) to show featured content.
rotating UI image/item display

Examples

The homepage uses a rotating carrousel to display featured products.
She dismissed the site's auto-playing carrousel because it slowed the page load noticeably.
Designers debated whether the mobile carrousel improved engagement or hid important information.
