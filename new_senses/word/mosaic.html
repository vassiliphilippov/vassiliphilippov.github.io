mosaic

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
A light-sensitive layer forming the transducer surface in a television camera tube, used to convert optical images into electrical signals.
light-sensitive surface in a TV camera tube

Examples

A high-quality mosaic ensures clearer video signals in broadcast equipment.
Advances in materials technology have improved the sensitivity of the mosaic in modern cameras.
The engineer replaced the damaged mosaic in the television camera tube to restore its image quality.

Etymology

From Latin 'mosaicum', from Greek 'mousaikos' (of the Muses).
