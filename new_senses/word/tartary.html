Tartary

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑr.tə.ˌri / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
a vast historical region in Europe and Asia, controlled by the Mongols in the 13th and 14th centuries
historical region of Eurasia, Mongol-controlled

Examples

Legends about lost cities in Tartary captivated travelers and scholars for centuries.
Many European maps from the Middle Ages feature Tartary as a vast and mysterious land.
The Mongol Empire carved out Tartary after conquering much of central Asia.

Synset Examples

under Genghis Khan Tartary extended as far east as the Pacific Ocean

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Tartaria, referring to lands inhabited by the Tartars (Tatars).

2. PROPN NEW

/ \u02c8t\u0251r.t\u0259.\u02ccri / (AmE), / \u02c8t\u0251\u02d0.t\u0259.ri / (BrE)
(in contemporary online discourse and pseudohistory) a supposed hidden or suppressed ancient empire claimed by some conspiracy theories to have been globally powerful or technologically advanced.
conspiratorial 'hidden' ancient empire

Examples

Some online posts describe Tartary as a hidden ancient empire whose influence persists today.
The forum thread promoted a theory that Tartary secretly built advanced global infrastructure centuries ago.
Historians dismiss claims about Tartary as pseudohistory unsupported by primary sources or archaeology.
