elector

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a person who has the legal right to vote in an election
person with the right to vote

Examples

Every elector must present valid identification to cast a ballot.
She became an eligible elector after turning eighteen last year.
The rights of every elector are protected under the constitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voter - a person who votes or has the right to vote

Etymology

From Latin 'elector', from 'eligere' (to choose, select).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˈlɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
(in the United States) a member of the Electoral College who formally casts a state's votes for president and vice president.
member of the U.S. Electoral College
american legal

Examples

In December, the elector from Georgia cast their presidential ballot with a solemn public statement.
Each elector must sign a certificate certifying the state's vote after meeting in their capital.
A few faithless elector votes recently prompted discussion about changing the Electoral College process.
