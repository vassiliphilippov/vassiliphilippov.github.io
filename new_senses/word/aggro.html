aggro

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˈæɡrəʊ / (BrE)
Annoyance, hassle, or aggressive behavior; aggravation or trouble, especially in confrontational situations.
annoyance or aggressive trouble (informal, British)
british informal slang

Examples

I don't want any aggro with the boss today, so I'll keep quiet.
If it means less aggro, I'll just do it myself.
There was a lot of aggro at the football match last night.

Synset Examples

I skipped it because it was too much aggro

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggravation - irritation or annoyance; trouble or difficulty
  • Synonym: hassle - irritating inconvenience; bother or trouble

Etymology

Shortened from 'aggravation' or 'aggression'; British slang, mid-20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈæɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˈæɡrəʊ / (BrE)
(gaming) hostile attention or threat from one or more enemies; the state of being the target of enemy attacks or AI focus.
enemy attention in games
informal slang

Examples

In raids, players often manage aggro to control enemy focus and protect teammates.
The healer lost aggro for a moment and the boss hit them hard before recovering.
Tanks use threat abilities to build aggro quickly at the start of an encounter.

3. VERB NEW

/ ˈæɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˈæɡrəʊ / (BrE)
(gaming) to attract or draw hostile attention from enemies or AI, causing them to attack or target you.
attract enemy attention (game)
informal slang

Examples

The tank will aggro the boss to draw its attention away from the healer.
If you hit the minion, you might accidentally aggro several nearby enemies.
She tried not to aggro the patrol while sneaking through the corridor at night.
