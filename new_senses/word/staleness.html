staleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unoriginal, dull, or hackneyed, especially as a result of overuse or repetition
unoriginal or hackneyed quality

Examples

Many critics commented on the staleness of the new sitcom, describing its jokes as tired and predictable.
The advertisement campaign failed due to the staleness of its recycled ideas.
There was a certain staleness in his writing that made the novel feel uninspired.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or inventive
  • Antonym: freshness - the quality of being new or interesting

Etymology

From stale + -ness, with 'stale' in the sense of 'lacking freshness or originality'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪl.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of having lost freshness, purity, or interest as a result of aging or being exposed to air.
the quality of being stale or no longer fresh

Examples

He could detect the staleness in the air of the abandoned house.
Her jokes had a certain staleness after being repeated so many times.
The staleness of the bread made it unappetizing for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh

Etymology

From stale + -ness, forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being stale.
