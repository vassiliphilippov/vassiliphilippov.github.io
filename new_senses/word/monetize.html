to monetize

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌn.ɪ.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈmʌn.ɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to give legal value to (a coin, currency, or asset) or establish it as the legal tender of a country
to establish as legal tender

Examples

The government decided to monetize new coins to stabilize the economy.
The lira was officially monetized as the country's only legal tender in 1862.
When a new currency is introduced, the authorities must monetize it before it enters circulation.

Synset Examples

They monetized the lira

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demonetize - to deprive (a coin, note, or currency) of its status as legal tender

Etymology

From French 'monétiser', from 'monnaie' (money), ultimately from Latin 'moneta' (mint, money).

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8m\u028cn.\u026a.ta\u026az / (AmE), / \u02c8m\u028cn.\u026a.ta\u026az / (BrE)
to earn money from something (such as a website, app, data, or content) by charging, showing ads, selling access, or otherwise converting it into revenue.
make money from (content, data, attention)

Examples

Many websites monetize their content by showing ads and offering paid subscriptions to users.
After years of free access, the app was monetized and now charges for premium features.
Independent creators are increasingly focused on monetizing their videos through sponsorships and memberships.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercialize - to organize or manage something so that it makes money
  • Synonym: profit - to gain money from an activity or resource

From French 'monétiser' (to give monetary value), from 'monnaie' (money), ultimately from Latin 'moneta' (mint, money).
