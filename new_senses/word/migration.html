migration

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The nonrandom movement of an atom or group within a molecule to a new position.
movement of an atom/group within a molecule (chemistry)
medical

Examples

A rearrangement can occur by migration of a substituent to a different position in the skeleton.
Chemists study the migration of radicals during complex organic reactions.
The migration of a hydrogen atom within the molecule results in a structural change.

Etymology

From Latin migratio (a removal, change of abode), from migrare (to move).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of people moving together from one place to another, especially over a given period.
group moving from one place to another

Examples

Each year, the migration of farm workers brings new life to the rural towns.
That migration of people transformed the entire region over a single decade.
The museum featured an exhibit on the migration of Irish families during the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's own country to settle elsewhere
  • Antonym: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From Latin migratio (a moving from one place to another), from migrare (to move, migrate).
