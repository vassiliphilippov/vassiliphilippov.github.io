physique

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈzik / (AmE), / fɪˈziːk / (BrE)
the form, size, and development of a person's body, especially in relation to physical strength or appearance
the overall form of a person's body

Examples

After months of training, his physique became noticeably more muscular and toned.
She admired the athlete's physique as he sprinted across the field.
The actor was chosen for his impressive physique and screen presence.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: build - body's shape and structure
  • Synonym: frame - the structure of the body
  • Synonym: constitution - the physical makeup of a person

Etymology

From French 'physique' (physical structure), from Latin 'physica' (natural science), from Greek 'physikos' (natural).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈzik / (AmE), / fɪˈziːk / (BrE)
the size, shape, and physical condition of a person's body
the form or build of the human body

Examples

He admired the powerful physique of ancient Greek statues.
His athletic physique made him stand out on the basketball court.
The model maintained her slender physique with regular exercise and a balanced diet.

Examples
