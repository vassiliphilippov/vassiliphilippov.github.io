feat

1. NOUN

/ fit / (AmE), / fiːt / (BrE)
an impressive or notable act or achievement, especially one requiring skill, courage, or strength
impressive achievement or act

Examples

Climbing the mountain in winter was no ordinary feat.
The bridge is considered an engineering feat of its time.
Winning the championship was an incredible feat for the young athlete.

Synset Examples

he performed a great feat
the book was her finest effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something accomplished, especially by superior ability, effort, or skill
  • Synonym: accomplishment - something that has been achieved successfully
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French *fait* (deed, action), from Latin *factum* (something done, deed).

2. NOUN NEW

/ fit / (AmE), / fiːt / (BrE)
(informal, computing) a feature or new capability in software or hardware, especially as labeled in commit messages, changelogs, or release notes.
software feature (informal)
informal

Examples

The release notes list a new feat that allows users to export reports in CSV format.
Check the commit labeled feat for the UI changes on GitHub before merging.
In the changelog, feat marks functional improvements rather than simple bug fixes.
