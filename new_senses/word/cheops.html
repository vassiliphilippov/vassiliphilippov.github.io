Cheops

1. PROPN

/ ˈkiː.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈkiː.ɒps / (BrE)
An Egyptian Pharaoh of the 27th century BC, known for commissioning the Great Pyramid at Giza.
Egyptian Pharaoh who built the Great Pyramid

Examples

Archaeologists have long studied the reign of Cheops to understand the construction of the pyramids.
Many historians regard Cheops as one of ancient Egypt’s most influential rulers.
The Great Pyramid of Giza was built as the tomb of Cheops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Khufu - the original Egyptian name of Pharaoh Cheops

Etymology

From Greek 'Kheops', the Hellenized form of the Egyptian name Khufu.

2. PROPN NEW

/ c8kid0.51ps / (AmE), / c8kid0.52ps / (BrE)
A European Space Agency (ESA) space telescope mission (CHEOPS) that measures exoplanet transits to determine planet sizes and study known exoplanets.
ESA exoplanet space telescope

Examples

ESA's Cheops satellite measured the tiny dip in starlight as the planet passed in front.
The research team used data from Cheops to refine the exoplanet's radius and density estimates.
During the press briefing, the scientist announced that Cheops had found a smaller-than-expected planet.
