strafe

1. NOUN

/ streɪf / (AmE), / streɪf / (BrE)
an attack of machine-gun or cannon fire from a low-flying aircraft directed at ground targets
attack from a low-flying aircraft

Examples

Survivors described a sudden strafe that forced them to take cover.
The airfield still showed signs of strafe damage months later.
The pilot led a daring strafe against enemy tanks at dawn.

Synset Examples

the next morning they carried out a strafe of enemy airfields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombing - an attack where bombs are dropped from aircraft

Etymology

From German 'strafen' (to punish), popularized in World War I military slang.

/ streɪf / (AmE), / streɪf / (BrE)
to attack (troops or a position) repeatedly with bombs, machine-gun fire, or other weapons from low-flying aircraft
attack from low-flying aircraft

Examples

Helicopters were seen strafing the hillside in support of ground troops.
The air force strafed enemy positions at dawn, causing chaos among their ranks.

Related Terms

4. VERB NEW

/ streɪf / (AmE), / streɪf / (BrE)
(in video games) to move quickly sideways (left or right), often to dodge attacks or to reposition while aiming.
move sideways in video games

Examples

During the match he learned to strafe left and right to avoid the enemy sniper's aim.
She strafed behind the cover while shooting, which kept her alive until reinforcements arrived.
The coach taught new players how to use strafing to control recoil and make themselves harder targets.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidestep - to step quickly to one side to avoid something
  • Synonym: dodge - to move quickly to avoid being hit or seen

Etymology

From German 'strafen' (to punish), popularized in World War I military slang; later extended in late 20th-century gaming to mean moving sideways.
