synchronizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the occurrence of things happening at the same time; the act or state of being synchronized
the act of occurring at the same time

Examples

Neural synchronizing plays a significant role in certain cognitive processes.
The synchronizing of our watches before the race was essential.
The director ensured the synchronizing of sound and video for the broadcast.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrony - the state of being synchronous; simultaneous occurrence
  • Antonym: asynchrony - the state of not occurring at the same time

Etymology

From synchronize + -ing, ultimately from Greek syn- (together) + khronos (time).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of coordinating events, actions, or devices so that they occur or indicate the same time
making things happen at the same time

Examples

Synchronizing of clocks in railway stations was crucial for the smooth operation of the trains.
Proper synchronizing of team members' actions made the event look flawless.
The synchronizing of the video and audio tracks improved the viewing experience.

Synset Examples

the synchronization of their watches was an important preliminary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronization - the process of causing events to happen at the same time
  • Antonym: desynchronization - the process of causing things to no longer be synchronized

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) and 'chronos' (time), meaning 'to make simultaneous.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of causing two or more events or actions to happen at the same time or in unison
making things happen at the same time

Examples

Good synchronizing between the musicians created a flawless concert.
Regular synchronizing of the clocks ensures the trains run on time.
The synchronizing of the lights made the performance visually stunning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronization - the process of causing things to occur at the same time
  • Antonym: asynchrony - lack of synchrony; events not happening at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), via French 'synchroniser'.
