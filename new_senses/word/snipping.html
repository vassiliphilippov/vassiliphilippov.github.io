snipping

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A small piece cut off from something, especially by using scissors or shears.
small piece cut off

Examples

He tossed the paper snipping into the recycling bin.
She collected every snipping of cloth to use in her quilt.
The floor was covered with snippings from the children's craft project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snippet - a small piece or fragment

Etymology

From the verb 'snip' + '-ing', referring to the result of snipping.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsnɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A small image captured from a computer or device screen (a screenshot or screen capture), often saved or shared as a file.
screen capture; screenshot

Examples

She sent me a snipping of the error message so I could help troubleshoot.
To save time, he used the snipping tool to capture the dialog box and share it.
After the tutorial, students uploaded multiple snippings showing each step of the process for feedback.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screenshot - an image showing the contents of a computer or phone screen
  • Synonym: screen capture - the action or result of capturing a picture of a computer or device display

Etymology

From verb 'snip' (to cut off) + -ing; extended metaphorically in computing (e.g., Microsoft 'Snipping Tool') to mean a clipped image of the screen.
