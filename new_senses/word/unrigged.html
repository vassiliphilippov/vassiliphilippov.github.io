unrigged

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɪɡd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪɡd / (BrE)
(of a ship or vessel) stripped of its rigging; having had its ropes, sails, or equipment removed.
stripped of rigging (ships)

Examples

After the storm, the damaged sailboat was quickly unrigged at the harbor.
The museum displays an old unrigged schooner to illustrate its construction.
With the crew's help, the yacht was unrigged and prepared for transport.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigged - fitted or equipped with rigging (of a ship)

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'rigged', meaning stripped of rigging or equipment.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ʌnˈrɪɡd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪɡd / (BrE)
Not rigged or manipulated; fair and free from tampering, especially of elections, votes, or decision-making processes.
not manipulated; fair

Examples

Opposition leaders insisted the upcoming vote must be unrigged to restore public confidence in the system.
Activists campaigned for transparent procedures so the hiring process would remain unrigged and merit-based.
After the recount, officials declared the election unrigged, rejecting allegations of tampering.
