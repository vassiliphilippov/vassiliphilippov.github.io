fencing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛn.sɪŋ / (BrE)
materials such as wire, wood, or metal used in building fences.
materials for building fences

Examples

The farm was surrounded by sturdy fencing to keep the animals from escaping.
They bought rolls of wire fencing to repair the old paddock.
Weatherproof fencing is essential for protecting your garden from wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fence - a structure that divides or encloses an area

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
the sport or art of fighting with swords such as foils, epees, or sabres, typically to score points under established rules
sport of sword fighting

Examples

He began fencing at the age of ten and quickly excelled in competitions.
Her passion for fencing led her to travel and compete internationally.
The Olympics feature fencing events using foils, epees, and sabres.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordsmanship - skill or practice of fighting with a sword
  • Degree Higher: dueling - formal fight with weapons between two people

Etymology

From French 'fencer', from Old French 'defenser' (to defend).

/ ˈfɛnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)

material or structures forming a barrier

Examples

The fencing around the playground was recently replaced to ensure children's safety.

Related Terms

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfɛn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing, especially distributed systems and cluster management) the process or mechanism of isolating a failed or misbehaving node so it cannot access shared resources, preventing data corruption or split-brain situations.
isolating a failed node (computing)

Examples

The cluster's fencing mechanism automatically isolates the faulty node to prevent data corruption.
During the outage, engineers enabled fencing to stop the misbehaving server from writing to shared storage.
Modern orchestration tools support automatic fencing and recovery to keep enterprise clusters consistent under failure.
