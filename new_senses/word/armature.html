armature

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmətʃə / (BrE)
the part of an electric machine, such as a motor or generator, in which voltage is induced by motion through a magnetic field, typically consisting of a coil of wire
coil in which voltage is induced in a magnetic field

Examples

As the armature spins, it generates electricity within the magnetic field.
Damage to the armature can cause a generator to malfunction.
The motor's armature was replaced after it became worn out from years of use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stator - the stationary part of a motor or generator

Etymology

From Latin 'armatura' (equipment, armor), originally meaning framework.
