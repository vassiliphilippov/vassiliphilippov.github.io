Ebit

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈiː.bɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 10^18 bits or one million exabits.
unit of 10^18 bits (information)

Examples

Scientific simulations sometimes generate amounts of data approaching a few Ebits.
The new data center is capable of storing over one Ebit of information.
Transferring an Ebit of data would take decades using current consumer internet speeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exabit - A unit of information equal to 10^18 bits
  • Degree Lower: petabit - A unit of information equal to 10^15 bits
  • Degree Higher: zettabit - A unit of information equal to 10^21 bits

Etymology

Formed from 'exa-' (metric prefix for 10^18) and 'bit' (binary digit).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8i\u02d0.b\u026at / (AmE), / \u02c8i\u02d0.b\u026at / (BrE)
(in quantum information theory) a unit for the amount of entanglement between two quantum systems; one ebit is the entanglement contained in a maximally entangled pair of qubits.
unit of quantum entanglement

Examples

The experiment produced roughly 0.8 Ebit of entanglement between the two photons.
In quantum communication, sharing one maximally entangled pair gives participants one Ebit to use.
Researchers measured the system and found it retained nearly two Ebit after the decoherence event.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entanglement bit - another name for the unit of entanglement equal to one maximally entangled two-qubit state.

Etymology

From 'entanglement' + 'bit' (portmanteau), coined in quantum information theory.
