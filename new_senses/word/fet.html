FET

1. NOUN

/ ɛf i ti / (AmE), / ɛf iː tiː / (BrE)
an electronic transistor in which most current flows through a channel whose resistance is controlled by a transverse electric field; abbreviation of field-effect transistor
field-effect transistor; a type of electronic transistor

Examples

Because of its high input impedance, the FET is suitable for sensitive measurements.
Modern smartphones often use FET technology in their processors.
The amplifier circuit relies on an FET for low-noise performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transistor - a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals

Etymology

Abbreviation of field-effect transistor.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u025bf i ti / (AmE), / \u025bf i\u02d0 ti\u02d0 / (BrE)
(statistics) Abbreviation of Fisher's Exact Test, a statistical test used to determine whether there is a nonrandom association between two categorical variables in a 2×2 contingency table, especially with small sample sizes.
Fisher's Exact Test (statistical test)

Examples

The study reported a significant association using FET, with a p-value below 0.05.
When cell counts were small, they chose FET instead of the chi-square test to be conservative.
In the lab meeting she explained how to calculate odds ratios and interpret FET results.
