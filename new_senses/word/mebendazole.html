mebendazole

1. NOUN

/ məˈbɛndəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / məˈbɛndəzəʊl / (BrE)
a medication used to treat parasitic worm infestations such as hookworm, pinworm, and roundworm
antiparasitic medication for worm infestations
medical

Examples

Mebendazole is often used in regions where parasitic infections are common.
The doctor prescribed mebendazole for the child's pinworm infection.
You should take mebendazole with food to increase its effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthelmintic - a drug that destroys or expels parasitic worms

Etymology

From 'me-' (methyl), 'bend' (benzimidazole), and 'azole' indicating its chemical structure.
