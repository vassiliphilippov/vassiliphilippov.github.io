to housekeep

1. VERB

/ ˈhaʊsˌkiːp / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkiːp / (BrE)
to maintain and manage the affairs and cleanliness of a household
manage or maintain a household

Examples

He chose to housekeep full-time while his partner returned to work.
Many people housekeep and manage other household responsibilities simultaneously.
She decided to housekeep for her elderly parents after they fell ill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to housekeep - to do cleaning and running of a household
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for something properly

Etymology

Formed from 'house' and 'keep,' patterned after 'housekeeping.'

2. VERB NEW

/ c8ha8ascckid0p / (AmE), / c8ha8ascckid0p / (BrE)
(in computing and data management) to perform routine maintenance on digital systems, files, or databases; to prune, archive, reorganize, or remove obsolete data to keep storage and systems tidy and efficient.
perform digital maintenance

Examples

Each weekend the sysadmin would housekeep old log files to free up disk space for new backups.
Automated scripts now housekeep daily records by compressing, archiving, and deleting obsolete entries.
Before the audit, the team housekept the database, removing duplicates and reorganizing tables for clarity.
