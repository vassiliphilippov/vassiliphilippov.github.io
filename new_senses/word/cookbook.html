cookbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊk.bʊk / (AmE), / ˈkʊk.bʊk / (BrE)
a book that contains recipes and instructions for preparing food
a book of recipes and cooking directions

Examples

He is writing his own cookbook featuring traditional family dishes.
She gave me a beautiful cookbook for my birthday.
The cookbook includes easy-to-follow recipes for beginners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recipe book - a book containing recipes; synonym for 'cookbook'

Etymology

Formed from 'cook' + 'book' in English, first recorded in the late 19th century.

/ c8k8a k.b8a k / (AmE), / c8k8a k.b8a k / (BrE)
(in computing, especially DevOps) a collection of reusable automation recipes or configuration files used to provision, configure, or deploy software and infrastructure.
collection of automation recipes (DevOps)

Examples

The DevOps team published a cookbook for automating database backups across environments.
When onboarding new interns, we point them to the internal cookbook with deployment procedures.
You can import the Chef cookbooks to quickly configure servers with required packages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playbook - In tools like Ansible, a file describing automation tasks; similar in purpose to a cookbook.

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'cookbook' (cook + book); used in computing from the early 2000s for collections of automation 'recipes' (notably popularized by the Chef configuration-management tool).
