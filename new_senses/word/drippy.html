drippy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrɪpi / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpi / (BrE)
Leaking or dripping in drops, especially at a slow and regular rate.
leaking or dripping in drops

Examples

Her painting style is intentionally drippy, with colors running down the canvas.
The drippy faucet kept me awake all night with its constant noise.
We had to put a bucket under the drippy ceiling to catch the water.

Synset Examples

a drippy faucet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaky - allowing liquid or gas to escape; not sealed tightly
  • Antonym: watertight - closely sealed so that no water can get in or out

2. ADJ

/ ˈdrɪpi / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpi / (BrE)
slightly wet or damp, especially as a result of light rain or drizzle
slightly wet from light rain

Examples

After walking the dog on a drippy morning, I had to dry my shoes by the radiator.
She shook her umbrella before entering the hallway, leaving a drippy trail across the tiles.
The drippy weather made our picnic less enjoyable, but we stayed anyway.

Synset Examples

a sad drizzly day
a wet drippy day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drizzly - characterized by light rain; showery
  • Degree Higher: wet - covered with, or saturated with liquid; not dry
  • Synonym: damp - slightly wet, often in an unpleasant way

Etymology

From 'drip' + -y, meaning having the qualities of dripping.

4. ADJ NEW

/ ˈdrɪpi / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpi / (BrE)
(informal, slang) very fashionable or stylish, especially because of clothing, accessories, or overall personal style; showing 'drip'.
slang: very fashionable; stylish
slang informal

Examples

Everyone at the concert noticed his drippy outfit, from the designer jacket to shining chains.
She posted a photo of her new trainers and captioned it, 'Feeling drippy today' for fun.
After the vintage haul, he looked even drippier than his friends in retro jackets.
