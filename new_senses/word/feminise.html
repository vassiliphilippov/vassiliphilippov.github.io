to feminise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛmɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛmɪnʌɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to take on more feminine qualities or characteristics
make more feminine in character
british

Examples

In literature, the author sought to feminise traditional narratives by focusing on female perspectives.
Some chemicals in the environment can feminise male fish, affecting their ability to reproduce.
The company aims to feminise its branding to appeal to a broader demographic.

Synset Examples

feminized language
feminized frogs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculinise - to make something more masculine

Etymology

From Latin 'femina' (woman) + -ize, via French 'féminiser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛm.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to give a feminine quality or appearance to; to make or become more characteristic of women or girls
make or become more feminine
british

Examples

Some critics argue that modern design trends tend to feminise traditional architecture.
The new uniform policy aims to feminise the company's dress code.
The stylist used soft colors and accessories to feminise the overall look of the room.

Synset Examples

This hairdo feminizes the man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculinise - to give a masculine quality or appearance to; to make more characteristic of men or boys

Etymology

From Latin 'femina' (woman) + English suffix '-ise'.

/ c8f5bm6accna6az / (AmE), / c8f5bm6an8c6az / (BrE)
(in horticulture/plant breeding) to induce plants or flowers to develop female reproductive organs or to treat plants so that the seeds they produce are female (feminized seeds).
induce female flowers / produce feminized seeds
british

Examples

Commercial growers often feminise cannabis crops by applying hormones to produce seeds that yield female plants.
To ensure a high percentage of bud-producing plants, the lab feminised selected specimens before seed collection.
Home gardeners avoid accidental pollination by feminising mother plants to obtain reliable feminized seeds for propagation.
