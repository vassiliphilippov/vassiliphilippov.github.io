to impersonate

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɜrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsəneɪt / (BrE)
to pretend to be another person, especially to deceive others
pretend to be another person

Examples

He earned a living by impersonating famous celebrities on stage.
It is illegal to impersonate a police officer.
She tried to impersonate her professor in a prank call.

Synset Examples

She posed as the Czar's daughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitate - to copy someone’s actions or speech
  • Synonym: masquerade - to pretend to be someone you are not

Etymology

From Latin 'persona' (person, character), via 'impersonare' (to invest with a personality).

4. VERB NEW

/ ɪmˈpɜrsəneɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsəneɪt / (BrE)
(in computing or cybersecurity) to assume the digital identity of another user, account, process, or service within a system or network, either legitimately (for testing or delegated access) or maliciously to gain unauthorized access.
assume a digital identity (computing)

Examples

During security testing, the tool can impersonate a user to verify login controls.
A misconfigured script impersonates the admin account, granting the attacker full control of the system.
By impersonating the service's certificate, the malware tricked the network into trusting it.

Synset Examples

During security testing, the tool can impersonate a user to verify login controls.
A misconfigured script impersonates the admin account, granting the attacker full control of the system.
By impersonating the service's certificate, the malware tricked the network into trusting it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masquerade - in security contexts, to present oneself as a different user or system
  • Synonym: spoof - to falsify data (for example an address or certificate) to appear as another entity

Etymology

From Latin 'persona' (person, character), via Medieval Latin 'impersonare' (to personate).
