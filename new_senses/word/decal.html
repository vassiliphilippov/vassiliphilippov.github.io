decal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːˌkæl / (AmE), / ˈdiːkæl / (BrE)
A design prepared on special paper for transfer to another surface, such as glass, porcelain, or metal.
a design for transfer onto another surface

Examples

After cleaning the window, he carefully transferred the decal to the glass.
She placed a decal of a butterfly on her laptop for decoration.
The model car kit came with several decals to apply for extra detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticker - an adhesive label or design for attaching to surfaces

Etymology

Shortened from 'decalcomania,' from French 'décalcomanie' (transfer process).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8di\u02d0\u02cck\u00e6l / (AmE), / \u02c8di\u02d0k\u00e6l / (BrE)
(in computer graphics, video games, and 3D modelling) a digital image or texture applied to a surface to add markings, labels, or visual detail without altering the underlying geometry.
digital texture or sticker in graphics

Examples

The game's level designers added weather stains and graffiti decals to make the city feel lived-in.
To save memory, the artist painted detail with layered decals instead of increasing model polygon counts.
She applied a bright neon decal to the ship's hull texture during the render pass.
