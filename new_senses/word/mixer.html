mixer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪksɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪksə / (BrE)
a social event where people gather to meet, mingle, and foster social relationships, often organized for newcomers or members of a group
social gathering for mingling
american

Examples

At the alumni mixer, former classmates reconnected over drinks and shared memories.
She met her future business partner at a networking mixer last year.
The student council hosted a mixer for all the freshmen to help them make new friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social - involving friendly gatherings for pleasure
  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests, typically involving eating, drinking, and entertainment

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪksər / (AmE), / ˈmɪksə / (BrE)
an electronic device that combines two or more input signals to produce a single output signal, commonly used in audio and broadcasting applications
electronic device for combining audio signals

Examples

The sound engineer adjusted the mixer to balance the vocals and instruments during the concert.
They bought a new mixer for their home recording studio.
When connecting multiple microphones, a mixer is essential to control the audio levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixing desk - a console for combining and adjusting audio signals
  • Degree Lower: amplifier - an electronic device that increases signal strength

Etymology

From the verb 'mix' + '-er' denoting an agent or device that mixes.

/ ˈmɪksər / (AmE), / ˈmɪksə / (BrE)

Examples

The recipe called for beating the eggs with a mixer until they were fluffy.

Related Terms
