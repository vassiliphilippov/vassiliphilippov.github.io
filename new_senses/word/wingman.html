wingman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋmæn / (BrE)
a pilot who flies outside and behind the leader in a formation, supporting the lead aircraft, especially in military aviation
supporting pilot in a flying formation

Examples

Each wingman maintained his position precisely, ensuring the formation stayed tight.
In combat, a wingman provides cover and protects the lead pilot from enemy attacks.
The squadron commander relied on his trusted wingman during complex maneuvers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leader - pilot flying at the front of a formation
  • Synonym: copilot - pilot who assists the main pilot in operating an aircraft

Etymology

From 'wing' (for the position at the side of a formation) + 'man' (person performing a supporting role).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈwɪŋˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋmæn / (BrE)
a person who accompanies and helps a friend in social situations, especially to introduce them to or support them in meeting potential romantic partners.
social ally who helps with dating
informal

Examples

At the party, he asked his wingman to start a conversation with the person he liked.
Good friends act as wingmen when you want support approaching someone at a bar.
She thanked her reliable wingman for introducing her to colleagues at the networking event.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Originally from the aviation sense ('wing' + 'man'); later used figuratively to mean a supporting companion in social settings.
