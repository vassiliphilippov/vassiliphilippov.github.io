Gemini

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛmɪˌnaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmɪni / (BrE)
a constellation and zodiac sign in the Northern Hemisphere, located between Taurus and Cancer on the ecliptic
constellation and zodiac sign

Examples

Many astrologers believe that people born under Gemini have lively personalities.
The constellation Gemini is easily visible in the night sky during winter.
The two brightest stars in Gemini are called Castor and Pollux.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Taurus - a zodiac constellation preceding Gemini
  • Degree Higher: Cancer - a zodiac constellation following Gemini

Etymology

From Latin 'Gemini', meaning 'twins'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛmɪˌnaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmɪni / (BrE)
A person born between May 21 and June 20, when the sun is in the astrological sign Gemini.
person born under Gemini zodiac sign

Examples

Many Geminis are said to be adaptable and outgoing.
My brother is a Gemini and loves meeting new people.
She was born in early June, making her a Gemini by zodiac sign.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cancer - Person born when sun is in Cancer (zodiac)
  • Degree Lower: Taurus - Person born when sun is in Taurus (zodiac)

Etymology

Latin Gemini, meaning 'twins', used as a zodiac sign since antiquity.

third sign of the zodiac

Examples

As a Gemini, he enjoys learning new things and meeting new people.
Her birthday falls in Gemini, making her a typical air sign.
People born under Gemini are said to be curious and adaptable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cancer - the fourth sign of the zodiac, following Gemini
  • Degree Lower: Taurus - the second sign of the zodiac, preceding Gemini

From Latin Gemini (twins), referring to the twin brothers Castor and Pollux in mythology.
