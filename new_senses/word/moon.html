Moon

1. NOUN

/ muːn / (AmE), / muːn / (BrE)
The natural satellite of the Earth, visible at night by reflected sunlight.
Earth's natural satellite

Examples

The astronauts admired the bright Moon during their historic mission.
The full Moon illuminated the countryside with a silvery glow.
We studied the phases of the Moon in our science class.

Synset Examples

the average distance to the Moon is 384,400 kilometers
men first stepped on the moon in 1969

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satellite - an object in orbit around a planet
  • Antonym: planet - a celestial body moving in an orbit around a star

Etymology

Old English 'mōna', from Proto-Germanic '*mēnô', related to Latin 'mensis' (month).

2. VERB NEW

/ muːn / (AmE), / muːn / (BrE)
(informal, slang) (of a price, asset, or popularity) to increase very quickly and by a large amount, especially used for cryptocurrencies or meme stocks.
rise rapidly in price (crypto/meme-stock slang)
slang informal

Examples

The token mooned after the announcement, sending early investors huge unexpected profits.
Many small investors hoped the meme stock would moon after the celebrity endorsement.
When the market rallied overnight, the altcoin started mooning and attracted global headlines.

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: plummet - to fall very quickly in value or amount

Etymology

From the slang phrase 'to the moon' (to rise dramatically), popularized in online trading and cryptocurrency communities.
