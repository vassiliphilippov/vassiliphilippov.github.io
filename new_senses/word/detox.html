detox

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːˌtɑks / (AmE), / ˈdiːˌtɒks / (BrE)
a hospital ward or clinic where people with substance addictions undergo detoxification under medical supervision
hospital ward or clinic for detoxification
medical

Examples

After his overdose, Mark spent two weeks in detox before transferring to a rehabilitation program.
Many hospitals now have a dedicated detox to help patients safely manage withdrawal symptoms.
She checked herself into detox to begin her recovery from alcohol addiction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehab - a course of treatment for drug or alcohol dependence, typically at a facility

Etymology

Short for 'detoxification', from de- (removal of) + tox(ic).

2. VERB

/ ˈdiˌtɑks / (AmE), / ˈdiːˌtɒks / (BrE)
to undergo or give treatment in order to remove toxic substances or dependence on alcohol or drugs from the body
undergo or treat for withdrawal from drugs or alcohol

Examples

After years of addiction, he decided to detox at a rehabilitation center.
Many celebrities choose to detox in private clinics away from the public eye.
She is currently detoxing as part of her recovery process.

Synset Examples

He was detoxified in the clinic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detoxify - remove toxic substances from the body
  • Synonym: rehabilitate - help someone recover from addiction

Etymology

Shortened from 'detoxify', from de- (remove) + tox(ic) (poisonous).
