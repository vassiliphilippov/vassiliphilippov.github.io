biometry

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɒmɪtri / (AmE), / baɪˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the application of statistical analysis to biological data or phenomena
statistical analysis of biological data

Examples

Many graduate students in biology are now required to take courses in biometry.
Modern advances in biometry have helped scientists better understand the spread of diseases.
The team used biometry to analyze growth patterns in the population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biostatistics - the branch of statistics dealing with data relating to living organisms

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + metron (measure).

2. NOUN NEW

/ baɪˈɒmətri / (AmE), / baɪˈɒmətri / (BrE)
The use of biometric data (such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris scans) to identify or verify a person's identity, especially in security and authentication systems.
biometric identification/authentication

Examples

Many airports now use biometry to verify travelers' identities at security checkpoints.
The new phone unlocks instantly because it relies on advanced biometry instead of a password.
Privacy experts warned about storing facial scans collected for biometry without strong encryption or user consent.
