locator

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to fix or establish the boundaries of land claims, especially in mining or property law
person who fixes land claim boundaries
legal

Examples

A locator must understand both legal requirements and local geography to set accurate boundaries.
As a skilled locator, she was often hired to settle disputes over land claims in rural areas.
The locator carefully measured the land to define the property boundaries for the new mine.

Etymology

From Latin 'locare' (to place), via 'locate' + '-or' suffix indicating agent noun.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈloʊkeɪtər / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a device or software application that identifies, shows, or transmits the location of a person, object, or place, often using GPS, Bluetooth, or mapping data.
device or app that finds location

Examples

The hiker activated a locator to send her coordinates to rescuers.
Airport staff used a locator to track the missing luggage across terminals.
He bought a Bluetooth locator to help find his keys when they go missing.

  • Synonym: beacon - a transmitting device that helps determine or broadcast the location of something nearby
