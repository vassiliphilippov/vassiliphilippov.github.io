tidiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪd.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being neat, orderly, and well-organized
the quality of being neat and orderly

Examples

He was praised for his tidiness at work, always keeping his desk organized.
Her tidiness impressed everyone who visited her home.
Maintaining tidiness in common spaces is important in shared living arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neatness - the quality of being neat and free from clutter
  • Antonym: messiness - the quality of being untidy or disorganized

Etymology

From tidy + -ness, with 'tidy' from Old English 'tidig' (timely, neat).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtaɪ.di.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.di.nəs / (BrE)
(in data analysis) the state or quality of arranging data in a 'tidy' format, where each variable is a column and each observation is a row.
organization of data into tidy format

Examples

Data scientists insist on tidiness so each variable occupies one column for reproducible analysis.
The instructor emphasized tidiness when teaching how to reshape messy spreadsheets into tidy tables.
After importing the CSV, she checked tidiness to ensure each observation was in a separate row.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: data cleaning - the process of detecting and correcting or removing inaccurate, corrupted, or irrelevant data
  • Antonym: messiness - the state in which data are disorganized or inconsistently structured, making analysis difficult

From tidy + -ness; 'tidy' ultimately from Old English tidig (timely, neat).
