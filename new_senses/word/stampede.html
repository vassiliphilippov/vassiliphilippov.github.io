stampede

1. NOUN

/ stæmˈpid / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːd / (BrE)
A sudden, panicked rush of a herd of animals, usually as a result of fear or excitement.
wild rush of panicked animals

Examples

A sudden movement in the herd triggered a stampede across the plain.
The cowboys managed to stop the stampede just in time to prevent injuries.
There was a stampede when the cattle were startled by a loud noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rout - A disorderly or chaotic retreat, especially of a crowd or army

2. NOUN

/ stæmˈpid / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːd / (BrE)
a sudden and uncontrolled rush of a crowd, often triggered by panic or excitement
sudden rush of a crowd

Examples

A rumor in the marketplace triggered a stampede of shoppers.
The crowd began to stampede when the fire alarm rang.
There was a desperate stampede towards the exits after the announcement.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - state of tranquility or peace, absence of panic

Etymology

7. NOUN NEW

/ stæmˈpid / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːd / (BrE)
(in computing) a sudden, synchronized burst of requests or actions toward a service or resource that overwhelms backend systems or creates a bottleneck.
synchronized request surge (computing)

Examples

A sudden traffic surge caused a stampede of requests that brought the API to its knees.
Developers implemented locking to prevent a cache stampede from overwhelming the database under high load.
When the sale went live, the sudden stampede of connections created a backend bottleneck and many timeouts.

Synset Examples

Etymology
