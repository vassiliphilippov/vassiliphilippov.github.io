collation

1. NOUN

/ kəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the careful examination and comparison of texts or items to identify points of disagreement or difference
careful comparison to find differences

Examples

A meticulous collation is crucial when comparing multiple historical sources.
After collation of the survey results, the team identified inconsistencies in the data.
The collation of ancient manuscripts revealed several discrepancies between versions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparison - the act of examining similarities and differences between things

Etymology

From Latin 'collatio' (a bringing together), from 'collatus', past participle of 'conferre' (to bring together).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of collecting and arranging items, especially sheets or documents, in a correct numerical or logical order
arranging items in order

Examples

Automatic collation of the documents saves time and reduces errors in large print jobs.
Efficient collation is essential when preparing legal files for court proceedings.
The collation of the reports took several hours to ensure every page was in sequence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the act of putting things in a neat or required order
  • Antonym: disorder - a lack of order or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'collatio' (a bringing together), from 'collatus', past participle of 'conferre' (to bring together).

Examples

Different languages require different collations, which affects search and sort results in applications.
