Tyche

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪki / (AmE), / ˈtaɪki / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of fortune and chance, identified with the Roman goddess Fortuna.
Greek goddess of fortune

Examples

In several classical sculptures, Tyche is depicted holding a cornucopia and a rudder.
Many ancient cities worshipped Tyche as the deity who controlled their fate and prosperity.
The influence of Tyche can be seen in how people viewed luck and chance in Greek culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fortuna - Roman goddess of fortune and fate

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Τύχη (Týchē), meaning ‘fortune’ or ‘luck’.

2. PROPN NEW

/ ˈtaɪki / (AmE), / ˈtaɪki / (BrE)
(astronomy) A hypothetical giant planet in the distant outer Solar System, proposed to explain certain anomalies in the distribution of long-period comets and other objects.
hypothetical outer Solar System planet

Examples

Some astronomers suggested that Tyche could explain unusual patterns of long-period comets from the Oort cloud.
A search of infrared survey data failed to find evidence that Tyche existed within the tested mass range.
In popular articles, Tyche became shorthand for any speculative distant planet shaping comet orbits.
