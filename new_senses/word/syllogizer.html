syllogizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.əˌʤaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.əˌdʒaɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person skilled in syllogistic reasoning, especially in the use or study of syllogisms in logic
person skilled in syllogistic reasoning

Examples

In her paper, she argued like a true syllogizer, carefully constructing each logical step.
Many admired his skills as a syllogizer, able to deduce truths from the slightest premises.
The ancient philosopher was renowned as a syllogizer in the courts of Athens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logician - a person skilled in logic

Etymology

From 'syllogism', based on Greek 'syllogismos' (inference, conclusion), plus English agent suffix '-er'.
