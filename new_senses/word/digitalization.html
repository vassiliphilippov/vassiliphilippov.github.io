digitalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the administration of digitalis to a patient, especially for the treatment of certain heart disorders
administration of digitalis (medical)
medical

Examples

Careful monitoring is required during digitalization to avoid toxicity.
Rapid digitalization can be risky in elderly patients.
The patient underwent digitalization to control his irregular heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitalis - a drug obtained from the foxglove plant, used to treat heart conditions

Etymology

From digitalis (a plant genus used as a cardiac drug) + -ization.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˌdɪdʒɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of converting information, activities, or systems into digital form and using digital technologies to improve or automate them.
process of converting to digital form

Examples

The corporation's digitalization strategy aims to digitize records and automate workflows.
Small museums relied on digitalization of catalogs so people could search collections online easily.
During the pandemic, schools accelerated digitalization to deliver lessons and materials remotely to students.

Etymology

From 'digital' + the suffix -ization; 'digital' ultimately from Latin digitus 'finger'.
