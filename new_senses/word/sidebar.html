sidebar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌbɑː / (BrE)
A short news story that provides additional or supplementary information related to a major story.
supplementary news story

Examples

A sidebar on the front page explained the origins of the ongoing conflict.
Reporters sometimes write a sidebar to highlight personal stories related to the main article.
The newspaper included a sidebar describing the background of the main event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feature - a special or prominent article in a newspaper or magazine

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌbɑː / (BrE)
(law) a private conference between the judge and lawyers, held at the side of the bench out of the jury's hearing during a trial.
private legal conference during a trial
american legal

Examples

During the trial, multiple sidebar conferences were called to address objections.
The jury remained seated while the attorneys approached the bench for a sidebar.
The lawyer requested a sidebar with the judge to discuss a sensitive issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bench conference - a meeting at the judge's bench, often the same as sidebar

Etymology

From 'side' + 'bar', referring to the area beside the judge's bench in court.
