ux.

1. NOUN

/ ʌks / (AmE), / ʌks / (BrE)
(in legal contexts) wife; used chiefly as an abbreviation for the Latin 'uxor' (wife) in legal documents.
legal abbreviation for wife
legal

Examples

In early court records, 'John Doe et ux.' refers to John Doe and his wife.
Some genealogical documents use ux. to denote the wife of a male ancestor.
The deed listed John Smith and his ux. as the property's new owners.

Etymology

From Latin 'uxor' (wife).

2. NOUN NEW

/ juː ɛks / (AmE), / juː ɛks / (BrE)
Abbreviation (initialism) for 'user experience': the overall experience a person has when interacting with a product or service, especially digital interfaces; also the professional field concerned with designing and improving that experience.
user experience; design field

Examples

Tech teams optimize UX to improve product usability and overall user satisfaction.
She decided to study UX to learn how to design intuitive digital interfaces.
The company hired a senior UX designer to lead the research and testing process.
