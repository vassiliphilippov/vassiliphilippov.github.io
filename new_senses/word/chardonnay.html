Chardonnay

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑɹˈdeɪ.ni / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈdæ.nəɪ / (BrE)
A dry white wine made from Chardonnay grapes, often characterized by its crisp and fruity flavor.
type of dry white wine

Examples

The crisp notes of the Chardonnay paired perfectly with the grilled chicken.
We visited a winery famous for its award-winning Chardonnay.
Would you like a glass of Chardonnay with your seafood dish?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chablis - a dry white wine made in the Chablis region of France, also commonly made from Chardonnay grapes

Etymology

From the French place name Chardonnay, from Latin Cardonnacum (place of thistles), used as the grape's origin.

2. PROPN

/ ʃɑr.dəˈneɪ / (AmE), / ʃɑː.dəˈneɪ / (BrE)
a type of white grape used to make white wine, or the wine made from this grape
a white wine grape and wine

Examples

Many wine lovers consider Chardonnay to be a classic choice for pairing with seafood.
She poured herself a glass of chilled Chardonnay while preparing dinner.
The vineyard specializes in growing Chardonnay grapes for their award-winning white wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sauvignon Blanc - a type of white wine grape and wine
  • Antonym: Merlot - a type of red wine grape and wine

Etymology

From the French 'Chardonnay', named after the village in Burgundy, France, where the grape was first cultivated.
