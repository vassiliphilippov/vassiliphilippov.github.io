1. PROPN

/ di oʊ aɪ / (AmE), / diː əʊ aɪ / (BrE)
abbreviation for the United States Department of the Interior, the federal department responsible for conservation and management of natural resources, established in 1849.

A persistent, unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to a piece of digital content (for example, an article, dataset, or report) to provide a stable link and citation.
digital object identifier (persistent ID)

Examples

Publishers assign a DoI to each paper, ensuring a stable link even if URLs change.
When citing the study, include the DoI so readers can quickly locate the original article online.
She clicked the DoI in the dataset record and was taken directly to the publisher's page.
