actor

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɚ / (AmE), / ˈæktə / (BrE)
a person who performs in plays, films, or television shows, especially on stage or screen
theatrical performer; person who acts

Examples

Many actors spend years perfecting their craft before landing a major part.
She became a famous actor after her breakthrough role on Broadway.
The actor delivered his lines with great emotion in the final scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actress - female actor; woman who performs in theater or film

Etymology

From Latin 'actor' (doer, performer), from 'agere' (to do).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u00e6kt\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8\u00e6kt\u0259 / (BrE)
(computing) a unit of computation that has its own state and behavior and communicates with other units by sending and receiving asynchronous messages.
concurrent computational unit

Examples

In modern distributed systems, an actor processes messages asynchronously across components.
Each actor maintains its own state and responds to incoming messages independently.
We implemented several actors to handle user sessions and scale request handling.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actor model - a model of concurrent computation in which actors are the fundamental units that communicate by message-passing
  • Synonym: agent - an autonomous computational entity that performs actions and may communicate with others

Etymology

From Latin 'actor' (doer), from 'agere' (to do); used metaphorically in computing to name an independent computational entity.
