to serialise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize things in a series or sequence
arrange in sequence
british

Examples

The archivist needed to serialise the documents by date to make retrieval easier.
To ensure clarity, the teacher asked the students to serialise their ideas before presenting.
You must serialise the data before importing it into the new database.

Synset Examples

Serialize the numbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to serialize - arrange serially (American spelling)

Etymology

From serial + -ise, modelled after the word 'serial'.

(in computing) to convert a data structure or object into a linear, transportable format (for example JSON, XML, or a byte stream) so it can be stored or sent and later reconstructed.

british

Examples

The program can serialise the user profile into a JSON string for storage.
After saving, the service serialised the session data so it could be sent to another server.
They're serialising complex objects into a binary stream for faster network transmission today.
