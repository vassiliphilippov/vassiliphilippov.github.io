blotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɒtə / (BrE)
a daily written record maintained by a police station, listing events such as arrests or incidents
police station event record

Examples

A review of the police blotter showed several incidents over the weekend.
Reporters often check the blotter for news about crimes and emergencies.
The officer entered the details of the arrest in the blotter before leaving his shift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logbook - a book in which regular records of events are kept, especially in professional contexts
  • Synonym: register - an official list or record of names or events

Etymology

Originally referred to a blotting pad used to absorb ink, later applied to the record book keeping daily reports.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɒtə / (BrE)
A piece of absorbent paper used to soak up excess ink from writing.
absorbent paper for drying ink

Examples

She gently pressed a blotter on the letter to prevent the ink from smudging.
The antique desk came with an old-fashioned blotter resting on top.
The artist used a blotter to absorb the excess ink from his sketch.

Etymology

From 'blot' (to soak up ink) + '-er', denoting an agent or instrument.
