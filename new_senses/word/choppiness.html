choppiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑp.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒp.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being rough or uneven, especially referring to the surface of the sea during stormy or unsettled weather.
rough or uneven condition of the sea

Examples

Surfers enjoy some choppiness, but too much can make the waves unpredictable.
The choppiness of the water made it difficult for small boats to stay afloat.
With increasing winds, the choppiness of the sea grew more intense by the hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - the quality of being rough or uneven

Etymology

From 'choppy' (rough, with waves) + '-ness'.

The quality of video or animation appearing jerky or uneven, typically caused by low frame rate, buffering, network issues, or heavy compression.
jerky video playback

Examples

The streaming service reduced choppiness after upgrading its video encoding.
During the live call, the choppiness made it hard to follow the speaker's movements.
Gamers complain about choppiness when frame rates drop below sixty frames per second.
