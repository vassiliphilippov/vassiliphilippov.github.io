prototypal

1. ADJ

/ ˌproʊtəˈtaɪpəl / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈtaɪpəl / (BrE)
representing or constituting an original type after which other similar things are patterned; original or typical of its kind
representing the original or typical example

Examples

Her reaction was prototypal of the community's response to the crisis.
His leadership style was considered prototypal for future generations of CEOs.
The museum features a prototypal example of modernist architecture.

Synset Examples

archetypal patterns
she was the prototypal student activist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetypal - very typical of a certain kind or representing an original model

Etymology

From 'prototype' + '-al', influenced by Greek 'prototypos' (original model).

(in computing) relating to or using prototype-based programming or inheritance, in which objects inherit properties and behavior directly from other objects (prototypes) rather than from classes.
relating to prototype-based programming

Examples

JavaScript uses prototypal inheritance, so objects can inherit properties directly from other objects.
Many developers learn prototypal patterns to better understand frameworks built on prototype-based objects.
The course introduced prototypal inheritance with hands-on exercises manipulating object prototypes and properties.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prototype-based - relating to a programming model where objects inherit directly from other objects
  • Antonym: class-based - relating to a programming model where objects are instances of classes (opposite of prototype-based)

Etymology

From 'prototype' + '-al'; applied in computing to describe prototype-based inheritance.
