impression

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the mark or pattern made by pressing one thing against or into the surface of another
mark made by pressing

Examples

He examined the impression of the ancient coin in the clay.
The horse's hoof left a deep impression in the muddy ground.
You could still see the impression of her hand on the soft sand.

Synset Examples

he watched the impression of the seal on the hot wax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprint - a mark or impression made by pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'impressio' (pressing in), from 'imprimere' (to press into).
