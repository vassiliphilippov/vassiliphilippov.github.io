connectivity

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɛkˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / kəˌnɛkˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or extent of being connected or interconnected, especially the ability of systems, devices, or components to link and communicate with each other
state of being connected or interconnected

Examples

Poor internet connectivity can disrupt video calls and streaming services.
The new software improves the connectivity between devices, making it easier to share files.
The wildlife bridge was built to increase habitat connectivity for animals crossing the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - the state of being connected; a link or relationship
  • Antonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others

Etymology

From 'connect' + '-ivity', patterned after nouns like 'activity'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ kəˌnɛkˈtɪvɪti / (AmE), / kəˌnɛkˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
(in neuroscience) the pattern or degree of functional connections between brain regions, indicating synchronized activity and communication, often measured using neuroimaging methods such as fMRI.
functional connections between brain regions
medical

Examples

Researchers used fMRI to measure hippocampal connectivity during memory encoding tasks in young adults.
In patients with Alzheimer's, reduced posterior cingulate connectivity correlated with worse cognitive scores.
The study compared resting-state connectivity across brain networks after sleep deprivation in volunteers.
