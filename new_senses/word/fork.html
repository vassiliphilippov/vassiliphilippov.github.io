fork

1. NOUN

/ fɔrk / (AmE), / fɔːk / (BrE)
an act or instance of branching or dividing into two or more parts or directions
the act of branching or dividing

Examples

A river forms a wide fork as it splits around the island.
The discussion took a fork when new evidence was introduced.
There is a fork in the road just past the old church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a division or subdivision of the stem or axis of a tree, shrub, or other plant

Etymology

From Old English 'forc', from Latin 'furca' (pitchfork, forked instrument).

9. NOUN NEW

/ fɔrk / (AmE), / fɔːk / (BrE)
(in computing) a copy or split of a software project or protocol that creates an independent development line or alternate chain, often used to continue, modify, or diverge from the original project.
software or protocol split

Examples

The developers decided to fork the repository to pursue a different feature roadmap independently.
After the protocol upgrade, many participants chose the fork that preserved the original transaction history.
She uploaded bug fixes to the main project instead of maintaining her own fork on the hosting platform.
