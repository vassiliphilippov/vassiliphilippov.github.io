primer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪmər / (AmE), / ˈprɪmə / (BrE)
a small introductory textbook that covers the basic elements of a subject, used especially for beginners
introductory textbook for beginners

Examples

Her first encounter with French was through a basic language primer.
I bought a gardening primer to help me get started with planting herbs.
The children started learning to read with a primer designed for first graders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textbook - a book used as a standard work for the study of a particular subject
  • Synonym: manual - a book giving instructions or information

Etymology

From Latin 'primarius' meaning 'of the first rank', via Old French and Middle English, referring to something primary or for beginners.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪmər / (AmE), / ˈprɪmə / (BrE)
a substance or device used to ignite a propellant charge in ammunition or explosives
device that ignites a propellant

Examples

Always handle a primer with care, as it is sensitive to impact and friction.
The bullet's primer failed to ignite, leaving the round unfired.
This type of primer is commonly found in centerfire cartridges used by hunters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detonator - a device used to set off an explosive device
  • Synonym: fuse - a device for igniting explosives or propellants

Etymology

From Latin 'primarius' (of the first rank), from 'primus' (first), referencing its initiating function.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪmər / (AmE), / ˈprɪmə / (BrE)
a first or preparatory coat of paint or sealant applied to a surface to improve adhesion of subsequent layers or to protect the material
preparatory coat of paint or sealant

Examples

Applying a primer ensures that the paint adheres better to the wall.
He forgot to use primer before painting, so the finish looks uneven.
Most professionals recommend sanding the surface lightly after the primer dries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undercoat - a coat of paint applied before the final coat

Etymology

From Latin 'primus' (first); related to the word 'prime'.
