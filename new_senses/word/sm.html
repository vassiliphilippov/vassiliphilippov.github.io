SM

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˈɛm / (BrE)
an academic degree awarded for postgraduate study in science, usually called 'Master of Science'; abbreviated as 'S.M.'
master's degree in science (abbreviation)

Examples

After completing her undergraduate studies, she earned her SM in biology from a prestigious university.
He applied for the job because it required an SM or equivalent in engineering.
Many international students come to the US to pursue an SM in computer science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MS - a master's degree in science (commonly abbreviated as 'MS')
  • Synonym: MSc - a master's degree in science (Master of Science, chiefly British)

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Scientiæ Magister' ('Master of Science').
