TIA

1. NOUN

/ tiː aɪ ˈeɪ / (AmE), / tiː aɪ ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A brief episode of neurological dysfunction resulting from a temporary interruption of blood flow to the brain, often serving as a warning sign for stroke.
transient ischemic attack; brief brain ischemia
medical

Examples

After experiencing a TIA, he was prescribed medication to reduce his risk factors.
Recent research focuses on early diagnosis and treatment after a TIA to prevent long-term complications.
The doctor informed her that a TIA could be a warning sign for a future stroke.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stroke - a sudden and severe interruption in the supply of blood to the brain
  • Synonym: mini-stroke - colloquial synonym for transient ischemic attack (TIA)

Etymology

Acronym of 'transient ischemic attack'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ tiː aɪ ˈeɪ / (AmE), / tiː aɪ ˈeɪ / (BrE)
An informal internet initialism for 'This Is Africa', used in captions or comments to suggest that an image, event, or situation reflects perceived characteristics or stereotypes associated with Africa.
internet initialism 'This Is Africa' (informal)
informal slang

Examples

They posted a photo of the crowded market with the caption TIA to suggest everyday chaos.
Seeing the power cut, someone commented TIA, meaning 'This is Africa', which sparked debate.
Journalists warned that using TIA in headlines can reinforce unfair stereotypes about the continent.
