leaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈliki / (AmE), / ˈliːki / (BrE)
allowing liquid or gas to pass through in an unwanted way, because of holes, cracks, or poor construction.
allowing fluid or gas to escape

Examples

Scientists discovered a leaky pipe in the laboratory, which was releasing dangerous fumes.
The old boat was so leaky that it needed constant bailing to stay afloat.
We had to fix the leaky faucet before it caused water damage in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

a leaky roof
a leaky defense system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: watertight - preventing water from passing through
  • Antonym: airtight - preventing air or gas from passing through

2. ADJ

/ ˈliki / (AmE), / ˈliːki / (BrE)
(informal) unable to retain urine or control the bladder; experiencing involuntary leakage of urine.
unable to control urination
informal medical

Examples

After surgery, some patients feel a bit leaky and may worry about bladder control.
Being leaky can be embarrassing, but treatment is available for urinary incontinence.
He joked that he was becoming more leaky as he got older, especially after laughing hard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incontinent - unable to control urination or defecation

3. ADJ

/ ˈliːki / (AmE), / ˈliːki / (BrE)
(of a person or organization) prone to disclosing confidential or sensitive information, especially unintentionally or without authorization.
prone to disclose confidential information

Examples

A leaky organization can never maintain its competitive advantage for long.
After the leaky board meeting, the CEO struggled to control the narrative.
The committee was so leaky that sensitive details appeared in the press almost immediately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tight-lipped - unwilling to communicate confidential information
  • Antonym: secretive - inclined to conceal feelings or intentions
  • Antonym: discreet - careful not to reveal secrets or confidential information

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'leak', meaning to let out, especially information. Used figuratively in this sense since the mid-20th century.

4. ADJ NEW

/ ˈliːki / (AmE), / ˈliːki / (BrE)
(in computing) of an abstraction, API, or library: failing to hide internal implementation details so that users must understand those details to use it correctly or avoid errors.
abstraction exposing implementation details (computing)

Examples

The library's leaky abstraction forced developers to understand the network internals to fix performance issues.
They replaced the leaky API because its hidden caching behavior caused inconsistent results across platforms.
A leaky abstraction often increases debugging time, as engineers chase implementation-specific errors.

Synset Examples

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'leak' (to let out); term popularized in computing by discussions such as Joel Spolsky's 2002 essay 'The Law of Leaky Abstractions'.
