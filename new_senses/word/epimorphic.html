epimorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈmɔɹfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially an insect) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, with the same number of body segments throughout successive developmental stages.
having incomplete metamorphosis (biology)

Examples

Many wingless insects are described as epimorphic because their body segments do not change during development.
The life cycle of the cockroach is epimorphic, as it hatches with the full number of body segments.
Unlike holometabolous insects, epimorphic species do not add new segments as they mature.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + morphē (form).

/  / (AmE), /  / (BrE)
(in biology) relating to a type of regeneration in which a blastema forms at the site of injury and differentiates to recreate the lost structure.
blastema-driven regeneration

Examples

Scientists study epimorphic regeneration to understand blastema-driven limb regrowth in salamanders.
During epimorphic healing, a blastema forms at the wound and later differentiates into new tissues.
Researchers compared epimorphic regrowth with morphallactic regeneration in their recent journal article.
