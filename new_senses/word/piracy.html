piracy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəsi / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərəsi / (BrE)
The act of using or copying someone else's words or ideas without permission and presenting them as your own.
using or copying someone else's ideas without permission

Examples

Academic piracy undermines trust in research and the value of original scholarship.
He was accused of piracy when he submitted a paper using someone else's ideas.
The publishing company sued for piracy after their novel was copied without consent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarism - the practice of taking someone else's work or ideas and passing them off as one's own

Etymology

From Old French *piracie*, from Latin *piratia*, from Greek *peirateia* (brigandage, piracy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəsi / (AmE), / ˈpaɪrəsi / (BrE)
the act of attacking and taking control of a ship or aircraft by force, especially at sea, usually to steal from it
armed robbery or hijacking of ships or aircraft

Examples

He specialized in investigating cases of piracy on international waters.
Modern piracy threatens shipping routes in several parts of the world.
The cargo ship fell victim to piracy off the coast of Somalia.

Synset Examples

air piracy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hijacking - taking control of a vehicle, especially an aircraft, illegally by force

Etymology

From Latin 'piratia', from Greek 'peirateia' (piracy, piratical attack).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpaɪrəsi / (AmE), / ˈpaɪrəsi / (BrE)
The unauthorized copying, distribution, or sharing of copyrighted works (for example music, films, or software), especially via the internet.
illegal copying/distribution of copyrighted media
legal

Examples

Online piracy has cost the music industry billions in lost revenue over the past decade.
The company filed a lawsuit against several websites accused of facilitating piracy of its movies.
He admitted downloading games illegally and said he understood that was piracy.
