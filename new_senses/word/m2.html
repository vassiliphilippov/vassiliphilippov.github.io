M2

1. NOUN

/ ɛm tu / (AmE), / ɛm tuː / (BrE)
a measure of the money supply that includes M1 plus net time deposits, except large certificates of deposit
measure of the money supply (economics)

Examples

Central banks often monitor M2 to assess economic liquidity and potential inflation trends.
Economists debate whether M2 is a better indicator of future inflation than M1.
The recent policy changes led to a significant rise in M2 over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: M1 - narrower measure of the money supply

Etymology

Used as an abbreviation for 'Money 2', referring to the second, broader definition of money supply in economics.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ɛm tu / (AmE), / ɛm tuː / (BrE)
(in immunology) a macrophage activation phenotype associated with anti-inflammatory activity, tissue repair, and wound healing.
anti-inflammatory macrophage phenotype
medical

Examples

IL-4 promotes M2 macrophage polarization, aiding tissue repair and anti-inflammatory responses.
After injury, many immune cells coordinate to increase M2 numbers and support wound healing.
Researchers measured cytokine levels to confirm that the cultured cells had adopted an M2 phenotype.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: M1 - a pro-inflammatory macrophage phenotype involved in microbial killing and inflammatory responses
  • Synonym: alternatively activated macrophage - another name for M2 macrophages, emphasizing their role in anti-inflammatory responses and tissue repair

Etymology

Named in immunology from the M1/M2 classification of macrophage activation states (by analogy to Th1/Th2).
