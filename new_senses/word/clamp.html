clamp

1. VERB

/ klæmp / (AmE), / klɑːmp / (BrE)
to fasten or hold something tightly in place using a clamp
fasten or hold with a clamp

Examples

She clamped the photo frame to the table so it would not slip.
The mechanic carefully clamped the pipe to prevent it from moving during the repair.
You should clamp the two boards together while the glue dries.

Synset Examples

clamp the chair together until the glue has hardened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to fix or make something safe, especially by fastening it firmly

2. VERB

/ klæmp / (AmE), / klæmp / (BrE)
to impose or enforce (something, such as a restriction or punishment) forcefully or suddenly
impose or enforce forcefully

Examples

Authorities are clamping harsh penalties on those who break the rules.
Police quickly clamped a ban on street gatherings during the emergency.
The government decided to clamp strict regulations on the industry after the scandal.

Synset Examples

The military government clamped a curfew onto the capital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impose - to force something (such as a rule, tax, or punishment) to be accepted or put in place

3. NOUN

/ klæmp / (AmE), / klæmp / (BrE)
a device used to hold objects firmly together, often while working on them
device for holding things tightly together

Examples

Could you hand me that clamp so I can secure this joint?
The carpenter used a clamp to keep the pieces of wood in place while the glue dried.
The metal clamp held the pipes together during the repair process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clip - a device used for holding things together or attaching them

Etymology

Possible variant of Middle English clamb, from Old English clamb (bond, fetter).

4. VERB NEW

/ klæmp / (AmE), / klæmp / (BrE)
(in computing) to restrict a numeric value so that it does not go below a minimum or above a maximum; to force a number to lie within a specified range.
limit a number to a range

Examples

To avoid overflow, the function will clamp the input value between zero and 100 before processing.
She clamped the color channel values during editing so no pixel exceeded the allowed brightness.
Physics engines often clamp object positions to the world bounds to prevent visual glitches.
