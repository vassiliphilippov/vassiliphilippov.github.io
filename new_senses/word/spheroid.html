spheroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪrˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsfɪə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
A solid figure resembling a sphere, formed by rotating an ellipse about one of its axes.
solid figure formed by rotating an ellipse

Examples

In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a spheroid.
Some seeds are shaped like a spheroid, making them easy to roll on a surface.
The planet is not a perfect sphere but rather a slightly flattened spheroid.

Synset Examples

it looked like a sphere but on closer examination I saw it was really a spheroid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ellipsoid - solid figure with all cross-sections ellipses or circles
  • Degree Higher: sphere - perfectly round solid figure in three dimensions

Etymology

From Greek sphairoeidēs, from sphaira (sphere) + -oeidēs (form, likeness).

(biology) a three-dimensional cluster of cells grown in vitro that resembles a sphere and is used as a model of tissue or tumor microenvironments.
three-dimensional cell culture model

Examples

Researchers cultured a cancer cell line as a spheroid to study drug penetration.
The lab technician placed several spheroids into separate wells to compare growth under different conditions.
Using a 3D model, students observed how cancer cells in a spheroid respond differently than flat cell layers.
