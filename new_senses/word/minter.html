minter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪntər / (AmE), / ˈmɪntə / (BrE)
a person, especially a skilled worker, who produces coins by stamping or coining them, usually at a mint
person who coins or stamps money

Examples

At the old mint, visitors can see a demonstration of how a minter would strike coins by hand.
Historically, the minter played a vital role in ensuring the quality of a nation's currency.
The minter carefully inspected each coin for imperfections before approving them for circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mintmaster - the official in charge of a mint

Etymology

From Middle English mintere, from Old English mynetere, from 'mynt' (coin, money) + '-ere' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmɪntər / (AmE), / ˈmɪntə / (BrE)
a person, organization, or service that creates (mints) new digital tokens, non-fungible tokens (NFTs), or other blockchain-based assets.
creator of blockchain tokens/NFTs

Examples

The NFT project's lead minter scheduled the token drop for next Tuesday evening.
Small artists often rely on platform features that let a minter set royalty percentages.
Several minters competed to create the rare token, spurring intense bidding after the auction.

From 'mint' (to produce coins) + agent suffix -er; extended in the 21st century to mean someone who produces digital tokens on a blockchain.
