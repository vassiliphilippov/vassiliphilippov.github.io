cultivatable

1. ADJ

/ ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
(of land) able to be farmed or cultivated productively.
able to be farmed productively

Examples

Due to poor soil quality, only a small portion of the area is cultivatable.
Experts assessed whether the newly cleared fields were truly cultivatable.
The government is investing in irrigation projects to make more cultivatable land available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultivable - of land, able to be cultivated
  • Synonym: arable - land suitable for growing crops

Etymology

From 'cultivate' + '-able', modelled after 'cultivable.'

(of microorganisms, cells, or tissues) able to be grown, maintained, or reproduced in an artificial culture or laboratory conditions.
able to be grown in culture

Examples

Only the cultivatable bacteria from the soil sample produced colonies on the agar plates.
Researchers screened water samples for cultivatable strains before starting antibiotic susceptibility tests.
The report showed several cultivatable organisms, while others were detected only by sequencing.
