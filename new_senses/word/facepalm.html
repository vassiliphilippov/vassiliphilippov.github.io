facepalm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsˌpɑm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsˌpɑːm / (BrE)
The act of placing one's hand across the face to express disappointment, embarrassment, or frustration.
gesture showing disappointment or frustration

Examples

He let out a sigh and did a quick facepalm when he read the mistake.
Her disastrous comment prompted an immediate facepalm from everyone at the table.
The teacher could not help but facepalm at the student's answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head-in-hands - gesture of putting one’s head in one’s hands in despair or frustration
  • Antonym: smile - an expression of happiness or amusement

Etymology

Blend of 'face' and 'palm', originating from online culture circa early 2000s.

To put one's hand over the face as a reaction of embarrassment, disbelief, or frustration; used especially in informal and online contexts.
cover face in embarrassment
informal slang

Examples

I often facepalm at my own mistakes during video meetings with colleagues.
She facepalmed when she realized she had sent the private message to everyone.
They were facepalming during the presentation because the slides contained so many errors.
