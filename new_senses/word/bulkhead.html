bulkhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌlkˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbʌlkˌhɛd / (BrE)
a wall-like partition dividing the compartments of a ship or aircraft, providing structural support and preventing the spread of fire or flooding
partition in a ship or aircraft

Examples

A fire-resistant bulkhead separates the cockpit from the cargo area on most planes.
After the collision, water poured in but the bulkhead kept it from spreading to other compartments.
The engineers reinforced the bulkhead to improve the ship's safety in case of flooding.

Etymology

From bulk (wall) + head, originally referring to barrier partitions in ships (16th century, nautical usage).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈbʌlkˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbʌlkˌhɛd / (BrE)
(in computing and system design) an isolation boundary or design pattern that confines failures, latency, or resource exhaustion to one component so problems do not cascade to other parts of a system.
software fault‑isolation boundary

Examples

To protect the system, they added a bulkhead around each service to limit failure scope.
During the outage, the bulkhead prevented slow database responses from affecting unrelated services.
A well-designed bulkhead isolates high-latency components, keeping user-facing services responsive.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuit breaker - a related resilience pattern that stops cascading failures by opening and closing access to a component.
  • Synonym: partition - a division that separates parts of a system or space to limit interaction or failure spread.

Etymology

From the nautical term bulkhead (a partition in a ship); used metaphorically in computing as the 'bulkhead pattern' to mean an isolation boundary between components.
