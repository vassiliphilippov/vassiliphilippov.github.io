argyrodite

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrdʒɪˈroʊdaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɑːdʒɪˈrəʊdaɪt / (BrE)
a rare steel-grey mineral composed of silver, germanium, and sulfur
rare mineral containing silver and germanium

Examples

Collectors seek out argyrodite for its unique composition and rarity.
Deposits of argyrodite are considered an important source of germanium.
The geologist identified the shiny specimen as argyrodite during the mineral survey.

Etymology

From Greek argyros (silver) and -ite (mineral suffix).
