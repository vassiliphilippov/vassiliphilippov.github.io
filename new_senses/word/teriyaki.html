teriyaki

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛriˈjɑki / (AmE), / ˌtɛəriˈjɑːki / (BrE)
A Japanese dish of meat or fish that is marinated in a seasoned soy sauce and then grilled or broiled.
Japanese soy-marinated grilled dish

Examples

He enjoys making homemade teriyaki using salmon and a sweet soy glaze.
She ordered the teriyaki chicken with steamed rice for dinner.
The restaurant is famous for its beef teriyaki served with vegetables.

Etymology

Japanese, from 'teri' (glaze/shiny) + 'yaki' (grilling/broiling).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cct\u025bri\u02c8j\u0251ki / (AmE), / \u02cct\u025b\u0259ri\u02c8j\u0251\u02d0ki / (BrE)
A sweet, soy-based sauce or glaze used to flavor, marinate, or finish grilled or stir-fried foods.
sweet soy-based sauce or glaze

Examples

She brushed the chicken with teriyaki sauce and grilled it until the glaze caramelized.
He kept a jar of bottled teriyaki in the fridge to add quick flavor to stir-fries.
At the festival they offered tofu glazed with sweet teriyaki that tasted smoky and salty.
