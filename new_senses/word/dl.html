dl

1. NOUN

/ diː ˈɛl / (AmE), / diː ˈɛl / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to one tenth of a liter (deciliter)
metric unit of volume (deciliter)

Examples

Sugar is measured in dl for some European cookbooks.
The bottle contains 7 dl of water.
This recipe calls for 2 dl of milk.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cl - a metric unit of volume equal to one hundredth of a liter (centiliter)
  • Degree Higher: l - a metric unit of volume equal to one liter

Etymology

Abbreviation of deciliter, from French 'décilitre' (deci- 'tenth' + litre).
