flyover

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a flight by one or more aircraft at low altitude over a particular location, typically as part of a ceremony or public display
low-altitude aircraft flight over a place

Examples

A flyover by vintage planes marked the anniversary of the historic event.
Spectators cheered as the jets executed a precise flyover above the stadium.
The Air Force performed a flyover during the opening ceremony of the football game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flypast - a ceremonial flight of aircraft over a specific location

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A bridge or elevated structure that carries one road or railway over another at a different level.
bridge over another road/railway
british

Examples

Commuters prefer the route with the flyover because it saves time during rush hour.
The city plans to build a flyover to ease congestion at the busy intersection.
Traffic was delayed due to construction on the new flyover near the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overpass - a bridge that carries a road or railway over another road
  • Antonym: underpass - a road or pedestrian tunnel passing under another road or railway

3D aerial tour in map apps

Examples

She explored Tokyo in flyover mode on her map app.
