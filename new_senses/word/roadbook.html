roadbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd.bʊk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd.bʊk / (BrE)
a guidebook giving information about the roads of a country or region, often including maps and route details
guidebook with maps and road information

Examples

Before setting out, he made notes in his roadbook about possible rest stops.
She consulted the roadbook to plan the next stage of their journey.
The roadbook included detailed maps and recommendations for scenic routes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atlas - a book of maps or charts, often including road maps

Etymology

From 'road' and 'book', reflecting its purpose as a book about roads.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8ro\u028ad.b\u028ak / (AmE), / \u02c8r\u0259\u028ad.b\u028ak / (BrE)
(in motorsport) a navigator's notebook containing detailed route instructions, distances, diagrams (often tulip diagrams), and hazard notes used during rally stages or cross-country races.
motorsport navigator's route notebook

Examples

The navigator studied the roadbook before the stage to anticipate the next corner.
At the start she checked the competitor's roadbook for hazards and distance markers.
During the desert rally, their roadbook handed to the co-driver was smeared with dust.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: route book - a booklet of directions and distances used to navigate a course
  • Synonym: pacenote - shorthand notes describing upcoming turns and hazards used by rally co-drivers

Etymology

From the compound road + book — literally a book of route information; applied specifically to rally/navigation notebooks in motorsport.
