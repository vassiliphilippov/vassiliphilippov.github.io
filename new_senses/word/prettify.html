to prettify

1. VERB

/ ˈprɛtiˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈprɛtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make (something) look more attractive or beautiful, often in a superficial or decorative way
make more beautiful or attractive

Examples

Don't prettify the facts; tell them exactly as they happened.
She tried to prettify the old living room with fresh paint and new curtains.
The web designer used animations to prettify the site's homepage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beautify - to make beautiful or more beautiful
  • Antonym: to uglify - to make ugly or less attractive

Etymology

Formed from 'pretty' + '-fy' (from Latin '-ficare', to make).

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8pr\u025bti\u02ccfa\u026a / (AmE), / \u02c8pr\u025bt\u026afa\u026a / (BrE)
(in computing) to reformat code, JSON, or other serialized data to improve readability by adding consistent indentation, line breaks, and spacing.
format code or data for readability

Examples

Developers often prettify JSON outputs to make them easier to read during debugging sessions.
She prettified the exported CSV, adding line breaks and indentation so the data looked tidy.
Their build script automatically prettifies source files before committing to ensure consistent style.
