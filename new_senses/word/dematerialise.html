to dematerialise

1. VERB

/ diːməˈtɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diːməˈtɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of an object) to become immaterial or to cease to be visible; to disappear from sight or existence.
to become immaterial or disappear

Examples

As the mist thickened, the outline of the ship seemed to dematerialise into the fog.
In science fiction stories, characters often dematerialise and reappear elsewhere.
The magician made the coin dematerialise right before our eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disappear - cease to be visible or to exist
  • Antonym: materialize - become visible, appear in material form

Etymology

From de- (removal) + material + -ise, after French dématérialiser.
