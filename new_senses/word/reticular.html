reticular

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtɪkjələr / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
having the form of a network; resembling or forming a netlike structure.
network-like in form or structure

Examples

A leaf's veins often create a reticular pattern visible when held up to the light.
Scientists observed the reticular structure of the sponge under the microscope.
The reticular fibers form a delicate network supporting many organs in the body.

Synset Examples

the reticulate veins of a leaf
a reticulated highway system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticulate - having a network pattern; net-like

Etymology

From Latin reticulum (a small net), diminutive of rete (net).

2. ADJ NEW

/ rɪˈtɪkjələr / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjələ / (BrE)
(in materials science and chemistry) relating to the design, synthesis, or study of porous, net-like crystalline frameworks formed by linking molecular building blocks (for example, metal–organic frameworks).
relating to reticular chemistry / net-like frameworks

Examples

The team published a paper on a new reticular polymer designed for gas storage applications.
The lab applied cutting-edge chemistry to synthesize a novel reticular framework.
The porous reticular scaffold trapped molecules more effectively than non-porous materials.

Synset Examples

The team published a paper on a new reticular polymer designed for gas storage applications.
The lab applied cutting-edge chemistry to synthesize a novel reticular framework.
The porous reticular scaffold trapped molecules more effectively than non-porous materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticulate - to form or have a netlike pattern; related in meaning to reticular in describing network-like structures.
  • Synonym: reticulated - having a netlike or network pattern; often used interchangeably in technical descriptions of porous frameworks.

Etymology

From Latin reticulum (a small net), diminutive of rete (net).
