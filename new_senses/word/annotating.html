annotating

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈanəˌteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of adding explanatory notes or comments to a text, diagram, or other piece of information
the act of adding explanatory notes

Examples

Annotating the manuscript helped the editor track important changes and suggestions.
He found that annotating his lecture notes improved his understanding of the material.
Students spent the afternoon annotating the assigned article for tomorrow's discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annotation - a note or comment added for explanation
  • Synonym: commenting - the act of making remarks or observations

Etymology

From the verb 'annotate', from Latin 'annotare' (to note down).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u00e6n\u0259\u02ccte\u026at\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8an\u0259\u02ccte\u026at\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
(in data science and machine learning) the process of labeling or tagging data items (for example, images, text, or audio) to create training and evaluation datasets for models.
labeling data for machine learning

Examples

Data scientists spend hours annotating thousands of images to prepare training datasets for the new model.
For accurate results, the team developed guidelines before annotating audio clips for the speech-recognition project.
Freelancers are often hired for annotating large text corpora to create labeled examples for evaluation.
