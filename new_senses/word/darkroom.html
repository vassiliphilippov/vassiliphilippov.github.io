darkroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrkˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkˌruːm / (BrE)
a room from which normal light is excluded, used for developing photographs or film
room for developing photographs

Examples

He spent hours in the darkroom developing his latest batch of photographs.
Our school still has a fully equipped darkroom for the photography club.
The smell of chemicals filled the darkroom as the film was processed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developing room - a room for processing photographic film and prints

(in digital photography) a virtual or software environment used to edit, process, and manage digital photographs, analogous to a traditional photographic darkroom.
virtual photo-editing workspace

Examples

She treats her photo-editing suite as a digital darkroom for post-processing.
After hours of retouching, he saved the files and closed the darkroom software.
Many photographers now build a personal darkroom on their laptop using raw-processing programs.
