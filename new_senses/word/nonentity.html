nonentity

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑˈnɛntəti / (AmE), / nɒˈnɛntəti / (BrE)
a person who is considered unimportant or lacking influence or significance
an unimportant or insignificant person

Examples

Despite her qualifications, many saw her as a political nonentity.
He was treated as a nonentity by the new management team.
Once a nonentity in the industry, she eventually became a leading figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobody - an unimportant or insignificant person
  • Antonym: somebody - a person of importance or influence

Etymology

From Latin ne (not) + entitas (entity, being).

2. NOUN

/ nɑːˈnɛn.t̬ə.ti / (AmE), / nɒˈnɛn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or condition of having no existence or being regarded as unimportant or nonexistent.
the state of not existing; insignificance

Examples

He feared that his creative work would go unnoticed and slip into nonentity.
Some philosophers debate whether the concept of nonentity has any meaningful application to the universe.
The ancient civilization disappeared into nonentity over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothingness - the state of being nothing; nonexistence

Etymology

From Latin 'non entitas', meaning 'non-being'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ nɑːˈnɛntɪti / (AmE), / nɒˈnɛntɪti / (BrE)
(in natural language processing) a token or span in text that is not identified as a named entity; a label used in datasets and tagging schemes to mark items outside named-entity categories.
NLP label for non-named tokens

Examples

In the annotated corpus, most tokens are labeled nonentity because they are not proper names.
During training, the model often confuses rare names with common nonentity spans, reducing accuracy.
Annotators marked punctuation and numbers as nonentity in the dataset to simplify tagging.
