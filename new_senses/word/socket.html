socket

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒkɪt / (BrE)
a device or opening designed to receive and hold an electric plug or component, enabling a connection to a power supply or circuit
electrical receptacle for a plug or device

Examples

Be sure the lamp is switched off before inserting the bulb into the socket.
European sockets often require adapters for American appliances.
He plugged his cellphone charger into the wall socket next to the bed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plug - a device that fits into a socket to make an electrical connection

Etymology

From Old French *soc* (ploughshare, base), from Latin *soccus* (a kind of low-heeled shoe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
A hollow in a bone into which another structure fits, such as where an eye or a tooth is set.
bony hollow for a structure

Examples

After his accident, he fractured the socket of his shoulder.
The dentist examined the empty socket where the tooth had been removed.
The eye sits comfortably in its protective bony socket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - a hollow space within a body or bone

Etymology

From Old French *socquet* (diminutive of soc, plowshare), then Middle English, sense extended to anatomical hollows.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05288330-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒkɪt / (BrE)
A hollow part or receptacle into which something fits or is inserted, especially the end of a pipe, probe, or bone.
receptacle for inserting something

Examples

He twisted the bulb out of the socket to replace it.
The dentist checked the tooth socket for signs of infection.
The hip bone fits perfectly into its socket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptacle - a container or space into which something is placed

Etymology

From Old French 'socquet' (a little ploughshare), from Medieval Latin 'socca' (ploughshare).
