blending

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
a gradual transition or gradation between two or more things, with small or imperceptible differences between adjacent stages
subtle gradation or transition

Examples

In design, smooth blending ensures there are no harsh lines between different shades.
The blending from light to dark was so gradual it was almost impossible to see where one ended and the other began.
The artist achieved a realistic effect through the careful blending of colors on the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradation - a scale or series of successive changes, stages, or degrees

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of mixing or combining different substances or elements together thoroughly
thorough mixing or combining of components

Examples

Careful blending is essential for achieving consistent paint color.
Successful blending of different musical styles can create a fresh sound.
The blending of flavors gives this soup its unique taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixing - the act of combining things together

Etymology

From the verb 'blend', which originates from Old Norse 'blanda' (to mix).
