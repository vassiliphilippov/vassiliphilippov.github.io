murder

1. VERB

/ ˈmɝːdər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdə / (BrE)
to kill someone deliberately and with premeditation, especially when it is unlawful
to kill deliberately and unlawfully

Examples

He plotted for weeks before finally murdering his business partner.
Investigators believe she was murdered because she knew too much about the crime.
The mafia boss ordered his rivals to be murdered during the night.

Synset Examples

The mafia boss ordered his enemies murdered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassinate - to kill an important person for political or religious reasons
  • Synonym: slaughter - to kill in a violent or brutal way
  • Antonym: save - to prevent the death of someone

Etymology

From Old French meurtre, from Latin murdrum (secret killing).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɝːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdə / (BrE)
to alter or perform something very badly, so that it becomes almost unrecognizable, especially in a way that causes amusement or disapproval
to do something (esp. speak a language or perform) very badly

Examples

I'm sorry, I know I murder French whenever I try to speak it.
She murdered the national anthem at the concert last night.
They absolutely murdered that classic song during karaoke.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcher - to spoil something, especially a performance or a piece of work, by doing it very badly

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrdər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdə / (BrE)
the unlawful and intentional killing of one human being by another
unlawful intentional killing of a person

Examples

Investigators are still searching for clues in the murder case.
She wrote a novel about a murder that shocked the small town.
The jury found him guilty of murder after a lengthy trial.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: manslaughter - unlawful killing without premeditation
  • Degree Higher: assassination - the murder of an important or prominent person
  • Synonym: homicide - the act of killing another person
  • Antonym: suicide - the act of intentionally killing oneself

Etymology

From Old English *morðor* (secret killing, mortal sin), from Proto-Germanic *murthran*.
