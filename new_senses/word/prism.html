prism

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
an optical device with a triangular shape, made of glass or quartz, that is used to deviate a beam of light or invert an image
optical device for deviating light

Examples

A prism can invert an image as well as bend light.
Holding the prism up to the sunlight created a beautiful rainbow on the wall.
The scientist used a prism to split the beam of white light into its component colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - piece of transparent material used to focus or disperse light
  • Antonym: mirror - device that reflects light to form an image

Etymology

From Late Latin prisma, from Greek prisma (something sawed or shaped), from prizein (to saw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
a solid geometric figure with two parallel, congruent bases joined by parallelogram faces
solid geometric figure with parallel bases

Examples

In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a rectangular prism.
Light passing through a prism can be separated into a rainbow of colors.
The science museum had a display featuring a triangular glass prism to demonstrate light refraction.

Etymology

From Greek 'prisma' (something sawed or cut, prism), via Latin 'prisma'.

3. PROPN NEW

/ ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
A U.S. government electronic-surveillance program (revealed 2013) that collects internet communications from major technology providers for intelligence purposes.
U.S. surveillance program
american

Examples

The whistleblower revealed that the PRISM program collects data from major tech companies.
Journalists examined leaked documents showing how PRISM allowed agencies to access emails and chats.
Civil liberties advocates protested after learning that PRISM could gather information about ordinary citizens.
