eviction

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈvɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of legally forcing someone to leave a property that they are renting or occupying
legal removal from property

Examples

After missing several rent payments, John faced eviction from his apartment.
Rising housing costs have led to an increase in evictions across major cities.
The family fought the eviction notice in court, hoping to stay in their home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone out
  • Synonym: evict - to force someone to leave a property by legal process
  • Antonym: possession - having ownership or control of something

Etymology

From Latin 'evictio', from 'evincere' meaning 'to overcome, expel.'

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
A situation in which a landlord compels a tenant to leave the premises, especially by making them unfit for occupancy, often without using physical force or a legal process.
forcible removal of a tenant without legal process
legal

Examples

Following the landlord's intimidation tactics, the family faced an eviction without ever going to court.
She described the eviction as unofficial, since the apartment became unlivable after basic utilities were shut off.
The city is investigating cases of eviction where tenants were forced to leave due to neglected repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave a place

Etymology

From Latin 'evictio', from 'evincere' meaning 'to overcome, expel'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ɪˈvɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈvɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
(computing) the act of removing data (such as cache entries or memory pages) from a storage area to free space, typically according to a predefined policy (for example, least recently used).
removal of data from a cache

Examples

The cache uses an eviction policy to remove least recently used items.
After the memory filled, automatic eviction of old pages prevented the application from crashing.
Profiling showed frequent eviction of cache lines caused a large performance drop under load.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purge - to remove data or items (often from a cache or storage) to free space or clear stale entries

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of the legal term 'eviction' (from Latin 'evictio', from 'evincere' meaning 'to overcome, expel'), applied to removing data from storage.
