localized

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪzd / (BrE)
restricted or confined to a specific area or place
restricted to a specific location

Examples

The pain was localized to his left shoulder, making movement uncomfortable.
The storm caused localized flooding in several downtown neighborhoods.
The technician was able to identify a localized network problem within the building.

Synset Examples

the localized infection formed a definite abscess

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalized - not limited to one area; affecting many areas

Etymology

From 'local' + '-ize' + '-ed', meaning made local or restricted in place.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01428581-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Restricted or limited to a specific place, area, or region; made local in scope or effect.
limited to a specific area

Examples

A localized power outage affected only a few neighborhoods.
Heavy rainfall caused localized flooding in several parts of the city.
The infection remained localized in his left arm and did not spread.

Synset Examples

a decentralized and localized political authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local - relating to a particular area
  • Antonym: global - relating to the whole world

Etymology

Formed from 'local' (from Latin 'localis') + '-ize' + '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01111969-s
Synset POS: s
