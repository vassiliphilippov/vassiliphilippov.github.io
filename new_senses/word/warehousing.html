warehousing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛrˌhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweə.haʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of storing goods in a warehouse, especially as part of a business operation
storage of goods in a warehouse

Examples

Advancements in automation have transformed modern warehousing operations.
Many companies rely on warehousing to manage inventory before distributing products to stores.
The cost of warehousing can significantly impact overall profits for an online retailer.

Synset Examples

they decided to reposition their furniture in a recommended repository in Brooklyn
my car is in storage
publishers reduced print runs to cut down the cost of warehousing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storage - the action or method of storing something for future use

Etymology

From 'warehouse' + '-ing', referring to the act or process.
