Phoenix

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiː.nɪks / (AmE), / ˈfiː.nɪks / (BrE)
a constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near Tucana and Sculptor.
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

Astronomers used the stars of Phoenix to help orient their telescopes in the night sky.
The Phoenix constellation is best seen in the Southern Hemisphere during the spring months.
The constellation Phoenix lies between Grus and Eridanus.

Etymology

Named after the mythological bird phoenix from Greek mythology, symbolizing renewal and rebirth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːnɪks / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of pinnate-leaved palms native to Asia and Africa, including species such as the date palm.
genus of palms; includes date palm

Examples

Many decorative gardens feature a Phoenix species due to its attractive, feathery leaves.
Researchers identified a new Phoenix species growing in remote desert regions.
The Phoenix genus includes the well-known date palm, which is cultivated for its edible fruit.

Etymology

From Latin, from Greek *phoinix*, referring to the mythological phoenix or a kind of palm.
