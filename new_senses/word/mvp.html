MVP

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm vi ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌɛm viː ˈpiː / (BrE)
the player in a sports team or competition who is judged to be the most valuable or important to their team's success
most valuable player (sports)
american

Examples

After a stellar season, she was named the team's MVP.
The crowd erupted when the announcer revealed the MVP of the tournament.
Winning the MVP award was the highlight of his basketball career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: most valuable player - player judged most valuable in a sport or competition

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'most valuable player'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02cc\u025bm vi \u02c8pi / (AmE), / \u02cc\u025bm vi\u02d0 \u02c8pi\u02d0 / (BrE)
(in business and technology) an initial, minimal version of a product that contains only core features, released to early users to test assumptions and gather feedback.
minimum viable product (tech)

Examples

The startup launched an early MVP to test whether users liked the new scheduling feature.
We built a simple MVP with only the core features to measure customer interest quickly.
After feedback from beta testers, they iterated on the MVP and improved the onboarding flow.
