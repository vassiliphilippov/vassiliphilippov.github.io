civet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvət / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪt / (BrE)
a small, cat-like mammal found in Africa and Asia, known for secreting a musky substance used in perfumes
cat-like mammal producing musk

Examples

A civet can be recognized by its long, slender body and spotted coat.
In some regions, civet cats are kept on farms to collect their musk.
The civet is prized for the scent it secretes, which is often used in high-end perfumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genet - another small, cat-like carnivore of Africa and Europe

Etymology

From Old French civette, from Arabic zabād (a kind of perfume).
