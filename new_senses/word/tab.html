tab

1. NOUN

/ tæb / (AmE), / tæb / (BrE)
a short strip of material attached to something, used to facilitate opening, identifying, or handling it
a projecting strip for opening or identification

Examples

Each folder has a colored tab for easy organization.
He pulled the tab on the can of soda to open it.
The coat has a tab at the collar that you can fasten.

3. NOUN

/ tæb / (AmE), / tæb / (BrE)
the key on a typewriter or computer keyboard that moves the cursor to a predetermined position, used for tabulation or indentation
key for tabulation on keyboard

Examples

Many forms allow you to move to the next field by hitting the tab.
She pressed the tab to indent the first line of her paragraph.
To create a table, use the tab key to align the columns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tab key - the key on a keyboard for keyboard tabulation

Etymology

Short for 'tabulator', from Latin 'tabula' (tablet, list).
