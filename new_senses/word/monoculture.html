monoculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əˌkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
the cultivation or production of a single crop or species in a given area or system at a time, often to the exclusion of others
cultivation of a single crop or species

Examples

Farmers switched from monoculture to polyculture to improve soil health.
Planting the same crop every year creates a monoculture that depletes nutrients from the soil.
The reliance on monoculture has made the region's agriculture vulnerable to disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyculture - the cultivation of multiple crops or species in the same area

Etymology

From mono- (single) + culture (cultivation), first used in the early 20th century.
