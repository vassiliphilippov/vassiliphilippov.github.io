perception

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming aware of or understanding something through the senses
awareness through the senses

Examples

Her keen perception allowed her to notice details that others often missed.
Sound and smell play an important role in our perception of the environment.
The child's perception of color was tested with various shapes and patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensation - the physical process of receiving stimuli through the senses

Etymology

From Latin perceptio (perception, apprehension), from percipere (to perceive)

(in computing and robotics) a system's ability to interpret sensory data (for example from cameras, lidar, or microphones) and form a model or understanding of its environment.
machine interpretation of sensory data

Examples

The autonomous drone relies on its perception system to detect obstacles in real time.
Modern self-driving cars use multiple sensors so their perception constantly updates the driving model.
Researchers improved the robot's perception by integrating lidar, cameras, and neural networks for better mapping.
