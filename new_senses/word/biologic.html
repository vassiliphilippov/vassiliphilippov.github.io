biologic

1. ADJ

/ baɪəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əˈlɒdʒ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to biology or to life and living things; biological
relating to biology or living things

Examples

Certain biologic responses can be triggered by stress or environmental factors.
Doctors are exploring new biologic treatments for autoimmune diseases.
The researcher made several biologic discoveries in the study of marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biological - relating to biology or living organisms

Etymology

From biology + -ic, modeled on 'biological'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ baɪəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / bɪəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
a medical drug or therapeutic product derived from living organisms (for example proteins, antibodies, or cells) used to treat disease.
biotech medicine, therapeutic product
medical

Examples

The patient started a biologic to treat her rheumatoid arthritis.
Hospitals often reserve special storage for expensive biologics that require refrigeration.
After reviewing options, her doctor recommended switching to a different biologic.
