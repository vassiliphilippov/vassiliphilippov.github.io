to telepathize

1. VERB

/ tɛˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / tɛˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (BrE)
to communicate with someone by means of telepathy rather than through normal sensory channels
communicate by telepathy

Examples

Science fiction often features characters who can telepathize to share secrets instantly.
She wondered if it was possible to telepathize her thoughts to her friend during the experiment.
The twins claimed they could telepathize with each other over great distances.

Synset Examples

some people believe they can telepathize with others around the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepathy - the ability to communicate by transmitting thoughts directly without using sensory channels

Etymology

From 'telepathy' + '-ize', meaning to make or become telepathic.

2. VERB NEW

/ tɛˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / tɛˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (BrE)
(in neuroscience and technology) to transmit thoughts, sensations, or simple information directly between brains using electronic brain–computer or brain-to-brain interfaces.
send thoughts via brain–computer interface

Examples

In the experiment, volunteers could telepathize simple images using a direct brain-to-brain interface.
Researchers successfully telepathized sensory signals between participants during the controlled lab trial.
Companies are developing devices that let people telepathize through implanted brain–computer interfaces in future communications.

Synset Examples

In the experiment, volunteers could telepathize simple images using a direct brain-to-brain interface.
Researchers successfully telepathized sensory signals between participants during the controlled lab trial.
Companies are developing devices that let people telepathize through implanted brain–computer interfaces in future communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepathy - communication of thoughts or ideas without using the usual sensory channels
  • Synonym: brain–computer interface - a device or system that enables direct communication between a brain and an external device, used here as the technology enabling telepathic transmission

Etymology

From "telepathy" + the verb-forming suffix "-ize"; applied to modern neural-interface technology.
