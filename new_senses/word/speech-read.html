to speech-read

1. VERB

/ ˈspiːtʃ ˌrid / (AmE), / ˈspiːtʃ ˌriːd / (BrE)
(of a deaf or hard-of-hearing person) to understand spoken words by visually interpreting the movements of the lips, face, and tongue, also known as lipreading.
interpret speech by lipreading

Examples

During meetings, he relies on his ability to speech-read the speakers.
She learned to speech-read at a young age after losing her hearing.
Some deaf individuals prefer to speech-read rather than use sign language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lipread - interpret speech by visually observing lip movements

Etymology

Formed from 'speech' + 'read', modeled after 'lip-read'.

2. VERB NEW

/ \u02c8spi\u02d0t\u0283 \u02ccrid / (AmE), / \u02c8spi\u02d0t\u0283 \u02ccri\u02d0d / (BrE)
(in computing, especially computer vision and speech-technology) to automatically recognize or reconstruct spoken language from visual information of a speaker's lip and facial movements.
automatically lip-read by software

Examples

The security team used software that could speech-read conversations from the silent CCTV footage.
Researchers are speech-reading video to improve accuracy of lip-reading models for noisy audio conditions.
The new app speech-reads speakers in real time, creating captions when the microphone is off.
