jiujitsu

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuː.dʒɪt.su / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːˈdʒɪt.suː / (BrE)
A method of self-defense originating in China and Japan, involving unarmed techniques that use an attacker's force against them.
Japanese unarmed martial art

Examples

She has been practicing jiujitsu for five years and has earned a black belt.
The self-defense class teaches basic jiujitsu techniques suitable for beginners.
Using principles of jiujitsu, he was able to subdue the attacker without any weapons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judo - A Japanese martial art developed from jujutsu focused on throws and grappling

Etymology

From Japanese 'jūjutsu', with 'jū' meaning 'gentle' and 'jutsu' meaning 'art'.

sport grappling (Brazilian jiu-jitsu)

Examples

She earned a silver medal in jiujitsu at the international martial arts competition.
He trains five nights a week to improve his jiujitsu and prepare for local tournaments.
