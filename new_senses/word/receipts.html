receipts

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsits / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːts / (BrE)
the total amount of money received, especially as income before any deductions are made
total money received before deductions

Examples

Gross receipts must be reported before any expenses are deducted.
The company's receipts have increased steadily over the past five years.
Total receipts for the concert exceeded all expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or investments

Etymology

From Old French 'recete', from Latin 'recepta' (things received).

2. NOUN NEW

/ rɪˈsits / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːts / (BrE)
informal pieces of evidence (such as screenshots, messages, or documents) used to prove or support a claim, especially online.
informal proof or evidence (online)
informal slang

Examples

When he accused her of lying, she posted screenshots as receipts to prove she was right.
Journalists often ask for receipts before publishing claims that could damage someone's reputation.
If you can't provide receipts, people on the forum usually dismiss your accusation as baseless.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or objects that help show whether a belief or claim is true.
  • Synonym: proof - information or an argument that shows something is true or correct.

Etymology

From noun 'receipt' (a paper proving a purchase), extended figuratively to mean proof; from Old French 'recete', from Latin 'recepta'.
