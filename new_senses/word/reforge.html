to reforge

1. VERB

/ riˈfɔrdʒ / (AmE), / riˈfɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to make or form something again, especially by melting and reshaping; to reshape or reform anew, often used figuratively
make or shape again; reshape anew

Examples

After the scandal, the company had to reforge its public image to regain trust.
The sculptor decided to reforge the statue after it was damaged in the storm.
Traditions can weaken over time, but communities often strive to reforge their sense of unity.

Synset Examples

She had to recast her image to please the electorate in her home state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - to make something again or differently
  • Synonym: reshape - to shape or form again or differently

Etymology

From re- (again) + forge (from Old French *forger*, from Latin *fabricare*, to make or shape).

2. VERB NEW

/ riˈfɔrdʒ / (AmE), / riˈfɔːdʒ / (BrE)
(in video games) to change, adjust, or reroll the statistical attributes of a piece of equipment or weapon, typically using an in-game system or resource.
modify or reroll equipment stats (gaming)

Examples

Before the raid, she reforged her sword to try for a higher critical hit chance.
Many players prefer to reforge rare items until they get the ideal attribute combination.
The update added a system for reforging gear, letting users change secondary stats using gold.

Synset Examples

Before the raid, she reforged her sword to try for a higher critical hit chance.
Many players prefer to reforge rare items until they get the ideal attribute combination.
The update added a system for reforging gear, letting users change secondary stats using gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reroll - to randomize an outcome or set of attributes again, often to get better results
  • Synonym: modify - to change something slightly, especially to improve it

Etymology

From re- + forge; a modern gaming extension of 'forge' meaning to make or shape, applied to changing an item's attributes.
