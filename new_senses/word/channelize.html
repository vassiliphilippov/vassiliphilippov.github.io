to channelize

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃænəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʃænəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a stream, river, or flow) to form or be confined within a channel, especially by artificial means
cause to form a channel

Examples

Attempts to channelize the stream altered the natural ecosystem.
It is often necessary to channelize stormwater in urban areas to manage runoff.
The engineers worked to channelize the river to prevent flooding.

Synset Examples

channelize a stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canalize - to convert to a canal or provide with a canal

Etymology

From 'channel' + '-ize', meaning to make into or provide with a channel.

5. VERB NEW

/ ˈtʃænəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʃænəlaɪz / (BrE)
(in signal processing and telecommunications) to separate a wideband signal or continuous data stream into multiple distinct channels (for example, frequency or logical channels) for parallel transmission or processing.
split a signal or data stream into channels

Examples

The software will channelize the incoming data into separate frequency channels for analysis.
Engineers channelized the radio spectrum so multiple signals could be monitored simultaneously.
During processing, the system is channelizing each stream to improve throughput and reduce latency.
