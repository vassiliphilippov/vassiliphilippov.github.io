harmonisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːrmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɑːmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of singing or arranging voices in harmony
singing together in harmony

Examples

Good harmonisation requires each singer to listen carefully to the others.
The choir's harmonisation created a rich, full sound that echoed through the hall.
Their harmonisation of the melody added depth to the song's chorus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmony - combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce chords and chord progressions

Etymology

From Greek 'harmonia' (agreement, concord) via French and late Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɑːmənaɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
A musical composition arranged or adapted so that its parts are in harmony with each other.
a harmonized musical composition

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful harmonisation of the traditional folk song.
The composer's unique harmonisation gave fresh life to the old hymn.
This harmonisation introduces new chords that enrich the melody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonization - the American spelling of 'harmonisation'
  • Synonym: arrangement - a musical adaptation for performance by particular voices or instruments

Etymology

From Greek 'harmonia' (harmony), via French 'harmonisation'.
