to anneal

1. VERB

/ əˈnil / (AmE), / əˈniːl / (BrE)
to heat a material, such as metal or glass, and then allow it to cool slowly, in order to make it stronger, less brittle, or to achieve the desired properties
heat and cool a material to alter its properties

Examples

After forming the glass, it must be annealed to prevent cracks from forming.
Engineers routinely anneal copper wires in the manufacturing process for better conductivity.
The blacksmith will anneal the steel blade to improve its strength and flexibility.

Synset Examples

temper glass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temper - to improve the hardness and elasticity of metal or glass by reheating and then cooling

Etymology

From Old English *onǣlan* (to set on fire, to kindle), related to 'en-' and 'ale'.

2. VERB NEW

/ əˈnil / (AmE), / əˈniːl / (BrE)
(in computing and optimization) to apply the simulated annealing algorithm to a problem or candidate solution in order to search for or approximate a global optimum.
apply simulated annealing

Examples

Researchers anneal the solution using simulated annealing to escape local optima.
During tuning, the algorithm annealed thousands of candidate configurations to improve performance.
Their solver kept annealing the model parameters until the improvement between steps was negligible.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simulated annealing - a probabilistic optimization technique that mimics the cooling process of metallurgy to escape local optima
  • Synonym: optimize - to make a system, design, or decision as effective or functional as possible; to search for the best solution

Etymology

From Old English *onǣlan* (to set on fire, to kindle); metaphorical modern use in computing from the metallurgy sense.
