sinkhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a depression or hole in the ground caused by the collapse of a surface layer, often connecting to an underground passage, especially in regions of limestone
a natural depression formed by ground collapse

Examples

After the heavy rain, a massive sinkhole appeared in the middle of the road, swallowing a parked car.
Florida is known for its frequent sinkholes due to the area's limestone bedrock.
Scientists studied the newly formed sinkhole to understand the collapse of the underground cavern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doline - a similar term for a natural depression in karst regions
  • Degree Higher: cave - a large underground chamber, often formed by natural processes

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8s\u026a\u014bk\u02ccho\u028al / (AmE), / \u02c8s\u026a\u014bk\u02cch\u0259\u028al / (BrE)
(in cybersecurity) a server, service, or network address configured to attract, capture, and isolate malicious or unwanted internet traffic (such as botnet communications) for analysis or to prevent further harm.
decoy server that traps malicious traffic

Examples

Security researchers deployed a sinkhole to capture and analyze botnet command traffic overnight.
The ISP redirected infected devices into a sinkhole to prevent spam campaigns from reaching customers.
After discovering the domain's malicious behavior, the team registered and pointed it at a sinkhole.

  • Synonym: honeypot - a decoy computer or service set up to attract attackers and study their behavior
  • Synonym: honeynet - a network of honeypots designed to monitor and analyze malicious activity

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the geological sense 'sinkhole' (a hole that swallows things), applied in computing to systems that 'trap' malicious traffic.
