masking

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
in theatre, scenery or objects used to block the audience's view of parts of the stage intended to be hidden
stage scenery that hides parts of the stage

Examples

He designed the masking so the offstage actors remained out of sight.
Proper masking is essential to maintain the illusion on stage.
The crew positioned additional masking to hide the set changes from the audience.

Etymology

From 'mask' (to cover the face or conceal) + -ing, used in theatrical contexts from the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which the perception of one sensation is prevented or diminished by the presence of another sensation, typically used in psychology and acoustics
blocking of one sensation by another

Examples

Research in auditory masking helps improve hearing aid technology.
The phenomenon of masking makes it difficult to hear faint sounds in a noisy environment.
Visual masking can occur when one image disrupts the perception of another.

Synset Examples

he studied auditory masking by pure tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - the process in which two or more sensory inputs overlap or affect each other

Etymology

From the verb 'mask' (to conceal or cover), plus the suffix -ing.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of concealing the existence of something by obstructing its view or making it less apparent
concealing by obstructing the view

Examples

Heavy fog provided natural masking for the ship's approach to the harbor.
Proper masking is essential when painting to prevent color from reaching unwanted areas.
The soldiers used masking techniques to hide their positions from the enemy.

Synset Examples

the cover concealed their guns from enemy aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concealment - the action of hiding or keeping something secret
  • Antonym: exposure - the act of making something visible or uncovering it

Etymology

From the verb 'mask', which comes from French 'masque' (a covering to hide or protect the face).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing and data privacy) the process of hiding, replacing, or obfuscating sensitive information in databases, reports, or datasets so that the data can be used without exposing private details.
hiding sensitive data in computing

Examples

The software uses masking to protect customer data in analytics dashboards.
During testing, the team applied masking to customer phone numbers and email addresses.
Regulatory rules require masking of personal identifiers before datasets are shared with vendors.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymization - the process of removing or altering personal identifiers so individuals cannot be identified
  • Synonym: obfuscation - the deliberate making of data or code unclear to prevent understanding or reverse engineering
  • Synonym: redaction - the process of editing or removing parts of a document or dataset to hide sensitive information

Etymology

From 'mask' (to cover or conceal) + -ing; extended to computing contexts for protecting data.
