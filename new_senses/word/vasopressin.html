vasopressin

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪsoʊˈprɛsɪn / (AmE), / ˌveɪsəˈprɛsɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the posterior pituitary gland and hypothalamus that increases blood pressure and reduces urine output by affecting kidney function
hormone regulating blood pressure and urine output
medical

Examples

Doctors monitored his blood pressure after administering vasopressin in the emergency room.
Patients with diabetes insipidus may require synthetic vasopressin to control their symptoms.
The release of vasopressin helps the body retain water during dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidiuretic hormone - another name for vasopressin, emphasizes its role in reducing urine output

Etymology

From vaso- (referring to blood vessels) + pressin (from 'press', indicating its function to increase blood pressure).

medical

Examples

Researchers found that elevated vasopressin levels were linked to increased aggression in male voles during mating.
Some clinicians measure vasopressin responses to stress to study connections between hormones and social bonding.
