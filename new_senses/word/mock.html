mock

1. VERB

/ mɑk / (AmE), / mɒk / (BrE)
to imitate someone or something in a way that makes them appear foolish or ridiculous, often to ridicule or show contempt
imitate to ridicule

Examples

He mocked his brother by copying his walk in an exaggerated way.
She was upset when her classmates mocked her accent during the presentation.
The comedian mocked politicians in his stand-up routine.

Synset Examples

The children mocked their handicapped classmate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitate - to copy the actions or appearance of someone or something
  • Synonym: ridicule - to make fun of someone in a cruel or harsh way
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From Middle French *moquer* (to mock, deride).

3. ADJ

/ mɑk / (AmE), / mɒk / (BrE)
Imitating or pretending to be something, especially to appear real or genuine though not authentic.
imitation or pretend (not real)

Examples

He gave her a mock salute as a joke.
The army held a mock battle to prepare for real combat.
The children engaged in a mock trial to learn about the legal system.

Synset Examples

boys in mock battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - something that is made to look like something else
  • Antonym: genuine - actually being what it is claimed to be; authentic

Etymology

From Middle French 'moquer' (to mock, deride), via Old French, probably from Vulgar Latin *muccare (to blow the nose at).

5. VERB NEW

/ mɑk / (AmE), / mɒk / (BrE)
(in software development) to replace or simulate the behaviour of a real component (such as an API, database, or service) with a controlled stand-in during testing.
simulate a component in tests

Examples

During tests, the team mocked the payment API so failures wouldn't hit production.
I mock the email service in my unit tests to return controlled data for each case.
They were mocking the remote server during integration tests to simulate slow responses.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simulate - to imitate the behaviour or operation of a system or process
  • Synonym: stub - a small piece of code that stands in for a component during testing

Etymology

From verb 'mock' (to imitate), ultimately from Middle French *moquer*; extended in computing to mean 'simulate a component for testing'.

6. NOUN NEW

/ mɑk / (AmE), / mɒk / (BrE)
a simulated object or service used in software testing that stands in for a real component to provide predictable behaviour or data.
simulated test object

Examples

To isolate the module, the developer replaced the real database with a simple mock.
The test suite failed because the mock did not mimic the API's error responses accurately.
They created a mock of the payment gateway to run offline demonstrations for clients.

Synset Examples

The test suite failed because the mock did not mimic the API's error responses accurately.
They created a mock of the payment gateway to run offline demonstrations for clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test double - a generic term for objects that replace real components in tests (mocks, stubs, fakes)
  • Synonym: stub - a simplified implementation used in place of a real component during testing

Etymology

From verb 'mock' (to imitate), ultimately from Middle French *moquer*; used in computing to mean a test double that imitates a real component.
