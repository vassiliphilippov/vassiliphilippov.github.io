CPI

1. NOUN

/ si pi aɪ / (AmE), / siː piː aɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'consumer price index', an index measuring the average change over time in the prices paid by consumers for goods and services
consumer price index (economic indicator)

Examples

An unexpected drop in the CPI surprised financial markets.
Many economists use the CPI to track changes in the cost of living.
The CPI rose sharply last month, indicating higher inflation rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer price index - index measuring average price changes paid by consumers

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'consumer price index'.
