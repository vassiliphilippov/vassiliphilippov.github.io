cypher

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪfər / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪfə / (BrE)
a message written in a secret code
coded message

Examples

He decrypted the cypher and revealed the enemy's plan.
Spies exchanged information by sending secret cyphers during the war.
The old diary contained a cypher that nobody could solve for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cipher - message written in a secret code, alternate spelling

Etymology

From Arabic 'ṣifr' (zero, empty) via Old French and Medieval Latin.
