Leonardo

1. PROPN

/ liˈɑːrnəˌdoʊ / (AmE), / liˈɒːnədəʊ / (BrE)
Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519), Italian painter, sculptor, engineer, scientist, and architect, renowned as a preeminent genius of the Italian Renaissance.
Leonardo da Vinci, Italian Renaissance genius

Examples

Leonardo was not only an artist, but also an inventor and scientist.
Many art historians consider Leonardo one of the most influential figures in Western art.
The Mona Lisa is perhaps Leonardo's most famous painting.

Etymology

Italian given name, meaning 'brave lion', from Latin 'Leo' (lion) + 'hardus' (brave, hardy).
