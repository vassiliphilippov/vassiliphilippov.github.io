to infect

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to transmit a disease or cause an organism to become ill by introducing harmful microorganisms such as bacteria or viruses
to transmit disease to; to cause infection

Examples

A bad cough can easily infect others if you don’t cover your mouth.
Doctors warned that untreated water may infect people with cholera.
Her laughter was so genuine it seemed to infect everyone in the room with happiness.

Synset Examples

Your children have infected you with this head cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminate - to make something impure or harmful, especially by contact or mixture

Etymology

From Latin 'infectus', past participle of 'inficere' (to stain, imbue, infect), from 'in-' (in) + 'facere' (to make).

WordNet ID: oewn-00089502-v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to contaminate someone or something with a disease-causing organism or to transmit an infectious agent
to transmit disease to

Examples

Doctors took precautions to ensure the wound did not infect other parts of the body.
Food prepared in unsanitary conditions can infect large numbers of people.
It is possible for a single mosquito bite to infect a person with malaria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminate - to make something impure or harmful by contact with something unclean
  • Antonym: cure - to heal or rid of disease

Etymology

From Latin 'infectus', past participle of 'inficere' (to dye, stain, infect), from 'in-' (in) + 'facere' (to do, make).

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to influence or spread to others in a manner resembling the spread of disease, especially with strong feelings, emotions, or attitudes.
to spread feelings or attitudes to others

Examples

A sense of dread seemed to infect the crowd as the news spread.
Her enthusiasm always infects the entire team, making everyone more motivated.
His optimism infected those around him, brightening even the gloomiest days.

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
To corrupt or influence (a person, group, or place) with harmful ideas, attitudes, or ideologies.
corrupt with harmful ideas

Examples

Extremist propaganda can easily infect an impressionable community.
His speeches infected the crowd with suspicion toward outsiders.
Social media sometimes infects public opinion with divisive ideas.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to cause to become morally depraved
  • Antonym: purify - to remove harmful or undesirable elements; to make pure

Etymology

From Latin infectus (stained, tainted, corrupted), past participle of inficere (to dye, pollute, corrupt).
