hash mark

1. NOUN

/ hæʃ mɑrk / (AmE), / hæʃ mɑːk / (BrE)
a badge or insignia, often in the form of a short line or stripe, worn on a uniform to represent years of service, typically in the military or police
service insignia on a uniform

Examples

After twenty years, he wore four hash marks on his sleeve.
Each hash mark on the uniform represents three years of service.
The officer proudly pointed to the hash marks that showed his years of dedication.

2. NOUN NEW

/ hæʃ mɑrk / (AmE), / hæʃ mɑːk / (BrE)
the typographic symbol '#' used to mark numbers, create hashtags on social media, or represent the hash/number sign in computing and telephony.
the '#' symbol; number sign/hashtag

Examples

She added the hash mark before the keyword to create a trending hashtag on the platform.
On the old phone keypad, users pressed the hash mark after entering the access code.
Programmers use the hash mark to start a comment line in some scripting languages.
