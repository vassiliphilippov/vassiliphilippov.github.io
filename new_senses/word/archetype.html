archetype

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkɪˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪtaɪp / (BrE)
the original model or pattern from which all things of the same kind are copied or on which they are based; a perfect example
original model; perfect example

Examples

She became the archetype of kindness in the community.
The hero in the story is an archetype found in many myths and legends.
This building is considered an archetype of modern architecture.

Synset Examples

this painting is a copy of the original

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prototype - an original model on which later stages are based
  • Synonym: paradigm - a typical example or pattern of something

Etymology

From Greek arkhetupon (original pattern), from arkhe- (first, original) + tupos (type, model).

medical

Examples

Hospitals adopted a standard archetype to model patient data in their EHR system.
The clinical team reviewed the archetype to ensure vital signs were recorded consistently across records.
Developers published a new archetype for allergy information to improve data sharing between hospitals.
