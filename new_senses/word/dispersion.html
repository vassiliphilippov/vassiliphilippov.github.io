dispersion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of being spread or scattered over a wide area or volume, especially in a physical or geographical context
scattering over an area or volume

Examples

Scientists studied the dispersion of pollutants in the atmosphere after the explosion.
The dispersion of seeds by wind allows plants to colonize new areas.
The dispersion of the population across such a vast region makes it difficult to provide public services.

Synset Examples

worldwide in distribution
the distribution of nerve fibers
in complementary distribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribution - the way in which something is shared out or spread over an area
  • Antonym: concentration - the state of being concentrated or clustered together

Etymology

From Latin 'dispersio' (a scattering), from 'dispergere' (to scatter).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of distributing or spreading people, things, or substances over a wide area.
the act of scattering or spreading

Examples

After the festival, there was a rapid dispersion of the crowd from the main square.
Authorities studied the dispersion of pollutants in the river after the chemical spill.
The dispersion of seeds by the wind ensures the growth of new plants in different areas.

Synset Examples

the dispersion of the troops
the diffusion of knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the process by which something spreads widely
  • Antonym: concentration - the act of bringing things together in one place

Etymology

From Latin 'dispersionem' (scattering, separation), from 'dis-' (apart) and 'spargere' (to scatter).
