rosewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌwʊd / (BrE)
a hard, dark-colored wood with a streaked appearance, obtained from several tropical trees of the genus Dalbergia, used especially for fine furniture and musical instruments
hard, dark wood from Dalbergia trees

Examples

Collectors often seek out furniture made of rosewood for its beauty and lasting value.
Many high-quality guitars are made with rosewood fretboards because of the wood’s tone and durability.
The antique desk was crafted from solid rosewood and had retained its deep reddish color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - a hard, reddish-brown tropical wood used for furniture

Etymology

Named for its scent, which resembles that of a rose, and the tree from which it comes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌwʊd / (BrE)
a hard, dark reddish wood with a pronounced grain, obtained from certain tropical trees and used especially in fine furniture and musical instruments
a dark reddish hardwood used in furniture and instruments

Examples

Collectors often seek rosewood furniture for its rich color and unique grain.
Her antique desk was made of polished rosewood and gleamed in the daylight.
The craftsman chose rosewood for the guitar body because of its beauty and resonance.

Etymology

Named for its rose-like scent and reddish color; from 'rose' + 'wood'.
