IDP

1. NOUN

/ aɪ di ː pi / (AmE), / aɪ diː piː / (BrE)
automatic data processing in which data collection and all stages of processing are combined into a unified system
automatic integrated data processing system

Examples

Modern businesses rely on IDP for greater efficiency and accuracy in data workflows.
Our company upgraded to an IDP system to streamline all data management processes.
The IDP handles both input collection and real-time analysis without manual intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: EDP - electronic data processing; computer-based data handling systems

Etymology

Acronym of 'integrated data processing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13522063-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN NEW

/ aɪ diː pi / (AmE), / aɪ diː piː / (BrE)
(in computing and identity management) a service that verifies users' identities and issues identity information (assertions) to other systems so users can sign in, often enabling single sign-on (SSO).
identity provider (authentication service)

Examples

The company's IDP supports SAML-based SSO across dozens of web apps.
During the audit, engineers found the IDP misconfigured, which allowed external access to accounts.
Before logging in, the app redirected me to my organization's IDP for authentication.
