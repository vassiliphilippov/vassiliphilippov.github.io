gingko

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪŋkəʊ / (BrE)
A deciduous Chinese tree (Ginkgo biloba) with fan-shaped leaves and fleshy yellow seeds, often grown as an ornamental street tree.
ornamental Chinese tree with fan-shaped leaves

Examples

She collected fallen gingko leaves for her science project.
The gingko is valued both for its beauty and its resilience in urban environments.
The city lined its main avenue with gingko trees, which turn bright yellow in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair tree - another name for ginkgo, especially used in horticulture

Etymology

From Japanese *ginkyō*, from Chinese *yínxìng* (silver apricot).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈgɪŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈgɪŋkəʊ / (BrE)
A dietary or herbal supplement made from the leaves of the Ginkgo biloba tree, often sold in tablet or capsule form and marketed for memory or circulation support.
herbal supplement from ginkgo leaves

Examples

She started taking gingko tablets every morning after reading about possible memory benefits.
The label warned that gingko can interact with blood thinners, so he consulted his doctor first.
Pharmacies sell standardized gingko extracts in capsules, usually dosed for daily use.
