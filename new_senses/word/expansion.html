expansion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspæn.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspæn.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of increasing in size, volume, quantity, or scope
increase in size, amount, or scope

Examples

Economic expansion typically results in more jobs and higher incomes.
The company announced an expansion of its product line next year.
The rapid expansion of the city has led to impressive new skyscrapers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growth - gradual development or increase in size or amount
  • Antonym: contraction - the process of becoming smaller or narrower

Etymology

From Latin 'expansio', from 'expandere' meaning 'to spread out'.

/ ɪkˈspænʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A function written as a sum or product of terms, especially one derived from another by algebraic manipulation.

Examples

The expansion of the binomial theorem is a classic algebraic example.
