to unmask

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈmæsk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːsk / (BrE)
to remove a mask from someone or something; to reveal the true identity or nature of someone or something that has been concealed
remove a mask; reveal true identity

Examples

At midnight, all guests were required to unmask before leaving the masquerade ball.
During the play, the actor dramatically unmasked to reveal his true identity.
Investigators worked tirelessly to unmask the source of the anonymous threats.

Synset Examples

unmask the imposter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disclose - to make known or reveal
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not, opposite of) + 'mask' (covering for the face); first attested in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01361276-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈmæsk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːsk / (BrE)
to reveal the true character, intentions, or identity of someone or something, especially when they have tried to hide it
reveal someone's true identity or nature

Examples

His actions finally unmasked his true intentions to the team.
The detective worked for months to unmask the leader of the criminal organization.
The newspaper investigation helped to unmask the fraud behind the charity scam.

Synset Examples

The journal article unmasked the corrupt politician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expose - to make something visible or reveal it, especially something hidden
  • Antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From un- (not) + mask, meaning to remove a mask; first used in the late 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00937663-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB NEW

/ ʌnˈmæsk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːsk / (BrE)
(in computing, especially user interfaces) to disable character masking in an input field so the actual characters (for example a password) are shown.
reveal characters in a masked input

Examples

She clicked the eye icon to unmask her password in the login field.
The developer unmasked the input during testing so teammates could verify the authentication flow.
Accidentally unmasking credentials in a screenshot led to a serious security incident last month.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mask - to hide or replace characters (for example in a password field) so they are not visible
