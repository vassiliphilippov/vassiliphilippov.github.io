mention

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈmen.tʃən / (BrE)
an act of officially recognizing someone's merit or achievement, often in the form of a formal statement or listing
official recognition of merit

Examples

Although the team didn't win first place, they did get a mention for creativity.
He received an honorable mention in the competition for his outstanding essay.
The young soldier's bravery earned him a special mention in the unit's report.

Synset Examples

although he didn't win the prize, he did get special mention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commendation - formal praise or recognition of achievement

Etymology

From Old French *mention*, from Latin *mentio* (a calling to mind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06719256-n
Synset POS: n
