submersion

1. NOUN

/ səbˈmɝːʒən / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of putting something completely under a liquid, especially water
the act of submerging in liquid

Examples

Extended submersion of the seeds helps speed up germination.
Safety precautions are necessary during the submersion of electrical equipment.
The submersion of the sculpture in water gave it a sleek finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immersion - the act of dipping something into a liquid so it is completely covered
  • Antonym: emersion - the process of emerging from a liquid or covering

Etymology

From Latin *submersio* (a sinking), from *submergere* (to plunge under).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈmɝːʒən / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of sinking below the surface so as to be completely covered by liquid, especially water
being completely covered by water or liquid

Examples

After heavy rainfall, the valley was at risk of submersion.
Frog eggs require submersion in water to survive and hatch properly.
The complete submersion of the car made it difficult to recover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immersion - the act of plunging into a liquid so as to be surrounded by it
  • Antonym: emergence - the process of coming out from being covered or concealed, especially from water

Etymology

From Latin submersio, from submergere (to submerge).
