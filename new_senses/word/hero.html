Hero

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɪəroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhiːərəʊ / (BrE)
A Greek mathematician and inventor of the first century, known for devising a formula for the area of a triangle and for describing various mechanical devices.
Greek mathematician and inventor (1st century)

Examples

Hero of Alexandria invented many ingenious devices, including the first steam engine.
Mathematicians still use Hero's formula to calculate the area of triangles today.
The ancient writings of Hero have influenced engineering for centuries.

Etymology

Latinized form of Greek 'Hērōn', the mathematician's name.

2. PROPN

/ ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a priestess of Aphrodite who killed herself after her lover Leander drowned while swimming across the Hellespont to see her.
Greek legendary priestess and lover of Leander

Examples

In the myth, Hero waited each night for Leander's swim across the Hellespont.
Many artists have depicted Hero gazing across the sea, longing for Leander.
The tragic love story of Hero and Leander has inspired poets for centuries.

Etymology

From ancient Greek Ἡρώ (Hērṓ), a name borne by the mythological character.
