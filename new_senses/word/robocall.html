robocall

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊbəˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbəˌkɔːl / (BrE)
an automated telephone call that delivers a recorded message to a large number of people
automated telephone call

Examples

I keep getting robocalls from companies trying to sell me insurance.
She ignored the phone because she thought it was another robocall.
The government is working on regulations to limit disruptive robocalls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spam call - an unsolicited and unwanted phone call, often for advertising or fraud

Etymology

From 'robot' (automaton) and 'call'.

2. VERB NEW

/ ˈroʊbəˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbəˌkɔːl / (BrE)
to place automated, prerecorded telephone calls to many people, typically for advertising, reminders, or political messages.
place automated prerecorded calls

Examples

The campaign plans to robocall thousands of voters with a reminder before election day.
Companies sometimes robocall customers late at night with offers to renew their subscriptions.
The charity robocalled supporters yesterday, asking for donations to fund its new program.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spam - to send the same unwanted message to many people (by email, phone, etc.)
  • Synonym: automated call - a telephone call made automatically using a computerized system to deliver a prerecorded message

Etymology

From the noun 'robocall' (from 'robot' + 'call'); later used as a verb by conversion.
