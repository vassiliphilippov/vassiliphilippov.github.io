Knut

1. PROPN

/ nut / (AmE), / kˈnuːt / (BrE)
A king of Denmark, Norway, and England in the early 11th century, known for consolidating his rule over all England after 1016.
King of Denmark, Norway, and England (c. 995–1035)
archaic

Examples

Coins from Knut's reign have been discovered across England and Scandinavia.
In British folklore, Knut is often remembered for the story of commanding the waves.
Many historians regard Knut as a powerful ruler who helped stabilize northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canute - Alternate spelling; the same historical king of Denmark, Norway, and England

Etymology

Old Norse ‘Knútr’, meaning ‘knot’.

/ nut / (AmE), / kˈnuːt / (BrE)

famous Berlin Zoo polar bear cub

Examples

The Berlin crowd cheered when the keepers introduced the new cub Knut to visitors.
After he became famous, Knut appeared on magazine covers and in television reports worldwide.
Many people donated money to the zoo to help care for Knut during his early months.
