crossover

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔːsˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a piece of music, album, or an artist that combines elements from different genres or styles to appeal to a broader audience, especially by blending popular music with another genre
music or art blending multiple genres to widen appeal

Examples

He is known for his crossover work blending classical music with hip-hop rhythms.
Her new album is a successful crossover between country and pop.
The band's crossover into mainstream music brought them a wider audience.

Synset Examples

a jazz-classical crossover album

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusion - music that combines different genres or styles, especially jazz with rock or funk

Etymology

From the verb 'cross over', meaning to go from one area or category to another.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔˌsoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a voter who is registered with one political party but votes in the primary election of another party
voter switching parties in a primary election

Examples

In the last election, the party saw a surge in crossover voters from the opposition.
Many candidates adjust their platforms to appeal to potential crossover voters during the primaries.
The influence of crossover voters can sometimes determine the outcome of a close primary race.

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'over', originally meaning movement from one side to another; political use attested from the mid-20th century.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːsˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the exchange of sections between paired homologous chromosomes during prophase of meiosis, leading to genetic variation.
exchange between homologous chromosomes (genetics)
medical

Examples

A mutation can occur if there is an error during the crossover process.
During meiosis, crossover increases genetic diversity among offspring.
Scientists observed the crossover of genetic material under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recombination - the rearrangement of genetic material, especially by crossing over in chromosomes

4. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a path or structure for crossing, especially over a street or railway, from one side to the other
a path or structure for crossing

Examples

A new crossover was built to allow pedestrians to reach the other platform safely.
The engineers designed a crossover above the busy highway for schoolchildren.
We waited at the crossover until the train had passed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overpass - a bridge that carries one road or railway over another
  • Synonym: crosswalk - a marked part of a road where pedestrians can cross

Etymology

From 'cross' (to go across) + 'over' (across to the other side), orig. mid-20th century.
