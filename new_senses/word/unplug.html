to unplug

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈplʌɡ / (AmE), / ʌnˈplʌɡ / (BrE)
to disconnect an electrical device by removing its plug from a socket
disconnect by removing plug

Examples

Please unplug the coffee maker before you clean it.
She forgot to unplug her phone charger before leaving the house.
The technician instructed us to unplug all electronic devices during the storm.

Synset Examples

unplug the hair dryer after using it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plug in - to connect an electrical device to a power supply by inserting its plug

Etymology

un- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse action') + plug.

2. VERB NEW

/ ənˈplʌɡ / (AmE), / ənˈplʌɡ / (BrE)
to stop using electronic devices or online services, especially deliberately and temporarily, in order to rest or reduce stress.
disconnect from digital devices

Examples

She decided to unplug from social media for a whole weekend and feel calmer.
After work he unplugged his phone and went hiking with no notifications.
Many health experts recommend unplugging from devices before bed to improve sleep quality.

Synset Examples

She decided to unplug from social media for a whole weekend and feel calmer.
After work he unplugged his phone and went hiking with no notifications.
Many health experts recommend unplugging from devices before bed to improve sleep quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disconnect - to end or interrupt a connection, including connections to devices or networks.
  • Antonym: reconnect - to resume a connection to devices, networks, or online services; opposite of unplug (in this sense).

Etymology

From un- + plug; extended metaphorically from the literal sense of removing a plug to mean disconnecting from electronic devices or online activity.
