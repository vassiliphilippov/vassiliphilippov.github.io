attester

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛstər / (AmE), / əˈtɛstə / (BrE)
a person who affirms or vouches for the correctness, truth, or authenticity of something
person who affirms or vouches for something

Examples

A notary public often serves as an attester for legal documents.
The scientist acted as an attester to the accuracy of the research findings.
We require an attester to verify all signatures on this form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - someone who gives testimony or evidence, especially in court
  • Synonym: certifier - someone who certifies or officially confirms something

Etymology

From French 'attester', from Latin 'attestari' (to bear witness).

(in computing, especially digital attestation) a device or software component that produces cryptographic proof of a computer system's state and presents it to a remote verifier.
device/software that produces cryptographic attestation

Examples

The platform's attester reports a trusted measurement to the remote verifier during startup.
Developers updated the server's attester to include TPM-based measurements before deployment.
During the audit, the cloud provider produced logs from several attesters showing measured boot values.
