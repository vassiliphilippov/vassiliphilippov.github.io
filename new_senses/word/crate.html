crate

1. NOUN

/ kreɪt / (AmE), / kreɪt / (BrE)
the quantity of something that is contained in a crate, often used as a unit for measuring or selling goods
amount held in a crate (unit)

Examples

A single crate of wine should be enough for the party.
The farmer delivered a crate of fresh oranges to the market.
We ordered three crates of bottled water for the event.

2. NOUN

/ kreɪt / (AmE), / kreɪt / (BrE)
a strong, typically wooden box used for packing, storing, or transporting goods
strong wooden box for shipping

Examples

A wooden crate arrived on the loading dock marked 'Handle with care'.
The apples were stored in a crate to keep them fresh until market.
They packed the fragile equipment into a large crate for safer transport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - a container with flat sides and a lid, typically rectangular

Etymology

From Old French *crat* or possibly from Latin *cratis* (wickerwork, hurdle).
