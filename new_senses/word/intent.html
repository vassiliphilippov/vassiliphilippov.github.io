intent

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
the intended meaning or purpose behind words, statements, or communication
the meaning or purpose behind communication

Examples

She clarified her intent to ensure her message was not misunderstood.
The intent behind his question was to help, not to criticize.
Without knowing the speaker's intent, it's easy to misinterpret their words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intention - something that is intended; an aim or plan

Etymology

From Latin intentus (a stretching out, purpose), from intendere (to stretch out, aim).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
showing strong concentration or complete attention to something; deeply absorbed
giving complete attention; deeply focused

Examples

He became so intent on finishing his work that he forgot to eat lunch.
She listened with an intent expression, not missing a single word of the story.
The children sat intent on the magician's every move, barely blinking as he performed his tricks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrossed - so interested or involved in something that you give it all your attention
  • Synonym: absorbed - deeply interested or involved; preoccupied
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because of other things

Etymology

From Latin intentus (strained, attentive), past participle of intendere (to stretch out, aim).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
the purpose or aim that guides someone's planned actions; what someone intends to do
purpose or aim behind actions

Examples

Her intent was to help those in need, not to seek attention for herself.
The true intent behind his actions remained unclear to everyone.
You must prove criminal intent in order to convict the defendant.

Synset Examples

his intent was to provide a new translation
good intentions are not enough
it was created with the conscious aim of answering immediate needs
he made no secret of his designs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intention - something that someone plans to do or achieve
  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created
  • Synonym: aim - a goal or purpose someone tries to achieve
  • Antonym: accident - something that happens unexpectedly or unintentionally

Etymology

From Latin 'intentus' (a stretching out, a purpose), from 'intendere' (to stretch out, intend).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
(in computing) a message object used to request an action or transfer data between components of a software system (for example, Android intents used to start activities or services).
software message object (e.g., Android)

Examples

Developers send an intent to start the camera app with initial data.
The activity received the intent and opened the correct view with the supplied extras.
You can create implicit intents to request actions from other apps without specifying a package.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: message - an object or packet conveying data or a request between software components
  • Synonym: action - a requested operation or behaviour that a software component should perform

Etymology

Metaphorical adoption of the noun 'intent' (purpose); applied in computing (notably Android) to name a message expressing a requested action.
