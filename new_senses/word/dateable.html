dateable

1. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be assigned a date; possible to determine the date of occurrence or creation
able to be assigned a date

Examples

Only a few documents from the period are dateable, which makes research challenging.
Scientists use carbon dating to determine whether an artifact is dateable.
The archaeologist found pottery that was clearly dateable to the Roman era.

Synset Examples

a concrete and datable happening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: datable - able to be assigned a date; alternate spelling

Etymology

Formed from 'date' (meaning a point or period in time) plus '-able'. Variant spelling: datable.
