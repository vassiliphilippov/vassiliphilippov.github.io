dicer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪsə / (BrE)
a mechanical device used to cut food into small cubes or pieces
mechanical kitchen device for dicing food

Examples

He bought a new dicer to replace his old, broken one.
She used a dicer to quickly prepare vegetables for the salad.
The kitchen was equipped with a professional dicer that made food prep easier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chopper - a device for chopping food into pieces
  • Synonym: slicer - a device for slicing food into thin pieces

Etymology

From 'dice' (to cut into cubes) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN NEW

/ c8da6as59r / (AmE), / c8da6as59 / (BrE)
(biology) an enzyme that cuts long double-stranded RNA into short pieces (small interfering RNAs, siRNAs) used in RNA interference to regulate or silence genes.
enzyme that makes siRNA

Examples

Researchers found that the dicer enzyme cuts long double-stranded RNA into short fragments.
Without functional dicer, cells cannot produce siRNAs and gene silencing fails in experiments.
The lab technician used antibodies to detect dicer levels in the tissue samples.
