flooding

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
a psychological treatment in which a person is intensely exposed to a feared object or situation until their anxiety decreases or they become desensitized
exposure therapy technique in psychology
medical

Examples

During flooding sessions, the patient remained in the presence of dogs until his anxiety subsided.
Her therapist recommended flooding to address her severe fear of elevators.
Some people find flooding too intense and prefer more gradual forms of exposure therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic desensitization - gradual exposure therapy for phobias
  • Synonym: exposure therapy - therapy involving exposure to feared stimuli

Etymology

From the verb 'flood', metaphorically meaning 'to overwhelm', applied to therapy in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈflʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
(computing, cybersecurity) the act of overwhelming a computer system, network, or service with a large volume of traffic or requests, often to disrupt normal operation.
overload of network traffic (cyberattack)

Examples

The website suffered a massive flooding of bot requests, causing the server to crash during the sale.
Engineers traced the outage to a deliberate flooding that targeted the API endpoints overnight.
Cloud providers use filtering to mitigate flooding by blocking traffic from suspicious IP addresses.

Synset Examples

The website suffered a massive flooding of bot requests, causing the server to crash during the sale.

Cloud providers use filtering to mitigate flooding by blocking traffic from suspicious IP addresses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial-of-service attack - an attack intended to make a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users
  • Synonym: DDoS - distributed denial-of-service attack; a flooding attack originating from many distributed sources

Etymology

From the verb 'flood' (to overwhelm); extended metaphorically in computing to describe overwhelming a system with traffic.
