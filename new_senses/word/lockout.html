lockout

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈlɒkaʊt / (BrE)
an occasion when an employer prevents employees from entering the workplace until they agree to certain terms or conditions, usually during an industrial dispute
employer action to bar workers during dispute
legal

Examples

A lockout can put financial pressure on workers to accept the employer's demands.
After negotiations broke down, the company declared a lockout and shut the factory gates.
The union is preparing to take legal action in response to the lockout imposed by management.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strike - when workers stop working as a protest, often over pay or conditions

Etymology

From 'lock' + 'out', first used in labor relations in the mid-19th century.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8l\u0251\u02d0k\u02cca\u028at / (AmE), / \u02c8l\u0252ka\u028at / (BrE)
a temporary security measure that blocks a user account or access to a system after too many failed login or authentication attempts, usually enforced automatically for a set time or until unlocked by an administrator.
temporary account access block

Examples

After five incorrect passwords the website enforced a thirty-minute lockout on her account.
IT recommended enabling automatic lockout policies to reduce the risk of brute-force attacks on servers.
He waited ten minutes after the lockout before calling support to regain access to his profile.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: account lock - a restriction placed on a user account that prevents signing in or using the account
  • Synonym: suspension - a temporary removal of access or privileges, often used for accounts or services

Etymology

From 'lock' + 'out'; originally used for industrial action in the 19th century and later extended to computing to mean locking access to an account or system.
