inkling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
a slight, often vague idea or notion about something; a hint or suspicion.
slight idea or suspicion

Examples

He gave me an inkling of what the new project would involve.
I didn't have the slightest inkling about the surprise party they planned.
She had an inkling that her friend was hiding something from her.

Synset Examples

he had no inkling what was about to happen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a small piece of information that helps you guess something
  • Synonym: suspicion - a feeling or idea that something may be true, especially without proof

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *enkeling* (whisper, hint), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
(in the video game series Splatoon) a fictional humanoid squid character or member of a species that can change between squid and humanoid forms and uses ink-based weapons.
Splatoon humanoid squid character

Examples

In Splatoon tournaments, each Inkling switches between squid and humanoid forms to cover turf with ink.
The artist cosplayed as an Inkling complete with a squid hood and neon paint.
During the livestream, the player taught viewers how the Inkling abilities affect movement and strategy.

Synset Examples

During the livestream, the player taught viewers how the Inkling abilities affect movement and strategy.

Etymology

From English ink + diminutive suffix -ling; coined by Nintendo as the name for characters in the Splatoon series.
