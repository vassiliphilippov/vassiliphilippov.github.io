smuggling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmʌɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
the crime of secretly importing or exporting prohibited goods or goods on which duty has not been paid
the secret import or export of prohibited goods

Examples

Efforts to curb smuggling have led to stricter customs inspections at all ports.
She was arrested for her involvement in the smuggling of rare wildlife.
The border patrol increased security to combat smuggling of illegal substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trafficking - the illegal trade of goods or people

Etymology

From 'smuggle', probably from Low German 'smuggeln', meaning 'to sneak'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsmʌɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
the illicit or secret transfer of data or digital information across borders, between secure systems, or out of protected networks.
illegal transfer of digital data
legal

Examples

Investigators uncovered an international ring involved in the smuggling of corporate customer databases online.
New laws target smuggling of personal data across borders using encrypted messaging apps.
Companies must report incidents where employees assisted in the smuggling of classified files.
