satin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪn / (BrE)
a smooth, glossy fabric, typically made of silk or rayon, with a shiny surface and a dull back
smooth, glossy fabric

Examples

He ran his fingers across the satin and admired its smooth, shiny surface.
She wore a beautiful evening gown made of satin that shimmered in the candlelight.
The pillowcases are soft, with a satin finish that feels cool against the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk - a fine, soft, natural fiber produced by silkworms, often used to make fabric
  • Synonym: velvet - a soft, luxurious fabric with a dense pile

Etymology

From Old French *satin*, probably from Arabic *zaytūnī* (of Zaitun, a Chinese port now called Quanzhou), referring to the fabric's place of origin.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈsætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪn / (BrE)
a type of surface finish or paint sheen that gives a smooth, soft gloss with low-to-medium shine
soft, low–medium gloss finish

Examples

They chose a satin finish for the bathroom walls to hide minor imperfections.
The new lamp has a satin coating that reflects light softly without strong shine.
Paint options included gloss, matte, and satin for the kitchen cabinets.

Synset Examples

They chose a satin finish for the bathroom walls to hide minor imperfections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft shine or gloss on a surface
  • Degree Lower: matte - a finish with little or no shine, lower on the glossiness scale than satin
  • Degree Higher: gloss - a high-shine finish, higher on the glossiness scale than satin

Etymology

From the fabric name satin, used figuratively for a similar smooth, slightly shiny surface.
