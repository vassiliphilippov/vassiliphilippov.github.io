demotion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the act of reducing someone to a lower rank or less important position
the act of lowering in rank or position

Examples

After the investigation, the manager faced demotion for violating company policies.
His demotion came as a surprise to many of his colleagues.
The threat of demotion motivated employees to improve their performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: promotion - the act of raising someone to a higher rank or position

Etymology

From Latin 'demotio', from 'demovere' (to move down); influenced by 'promote' and 'promotion'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the reduction of a post's, account's, or feature's visibility, ranking, or prominence on an online platform by algorithms or moderation.
online reduction of visibility or ranking

Examples

After the controversy, the journalist complained that the platform had applied a demotion to his articles.
Algorithm changes caused a sudden demotion of many small creators, leaving their videos with far fewer views.
Moderators warned against manipulating tags to avoid a demotion that would push content out of the trending feed.

Related Terms
