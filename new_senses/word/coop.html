coop

1. NOUN

/ kup / (AmE), / kuːp / (BrE)
a small building or enclosure on a farm for keeping poultry such as chickens
small poultry shelter

Examples

After the storm, the chickens were safe inside their coop.
The farmer collected eggs from the coop every morning.
They built a new coop for their growing flock of hens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henhouse - a house or enclosure for chickens

Etymology

From Middle English coupe, from Old English cype (basket), from Latin cupa (tub, cask, barrel).

2. NOUN

/ kuːp / (AmE), / kuːp / (BrE)
a small enclosure made of wood, wire, or metal bars, in which birds or small animals are kept, especially chickens
small enclosure for birds or animals

Examples

After cleaning the coop, she refilled the water and food trays for the birds.
He built a new coop in his backyard for his rabbits.
The chickens were safe and warm inside the coop for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an enclosure for animals
  • Synonym: cage - an enclosure made of bars for confining animals or birds

Etymology

From Middle English *cope*, from Old English *cype* (basket).
