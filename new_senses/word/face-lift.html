face-lift

1. VERB

/ ˈfeɪsˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs.lɪft / (BrE)
to perform cosmetic surgery on someone's face to improve appearance
perform cosmetic surgery on the face

Examples

She decided to face-lift her appearance before her television debut.
The surgeon will face-lift several patients next week as part of the clinic's schedule.
Thousands seek a specialist every year to face-lift in hopes of looking younger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facelift - cosmetic surgery to improve the appearance of the face (noun)

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfeɪsˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs.lɪft / (BrE)
a small change or set of changes made to the appearance of something (for example a building, product, or website) to make it look more modern or attractive.
cosmetic update; modernization

Examples

The old city hall got a much-needed face-lift that brightened its exterior and fixed the roof.
After the update, the company's website received a subtle face-lift to improve navigation and style.
The cereal brand gave its packaging a quick face-lift before the product relaunched nationwide.

  • Synonym: redesign - to change the design or appearance of something, especially to improve it

Etymology

From face + lift — originally referring to cosmetic surgery on a person's face; later used metaphorically for updating appearance of objects.
