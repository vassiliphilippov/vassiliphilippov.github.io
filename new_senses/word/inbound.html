inbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɪnbaʊnd / (BrE)
moving or directed inward or toward a central point, especially approaching a place such as a city or station.
moving inward or toward a center

Examples

During rush hour, inbound trains are often crowded with commuters heading downtown.
The inbound flight from New York was delayed due to bad weather.
The company saw an inbound flow of investment from overseas clients.

Synset Examples

the inbound train
inward flood of capital

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outbound - moving or directed away from a center or starting point
  • Synonym: incoming - arriving or coming in; inward bound

Etymology

From in + bound (in the sense of 'headed toward').

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈɪnˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɪnbaʊnd / (BrE)
relating to marketing that attracts customers by creating useful content and experiences (for example via SEO, social media, or blogs) rather than by traditional outbound advertising.
attraction-based marketing

Examples

The startup focused on inbound marketing, using content to attract and nurture customers.
Our team improved the website to increase inbound leads from organic search and social posts.
They hired an agency to develop an inbound strategy centered on SEO and helpful blog posts.

Synset Examples

The startup focused on inbound marketing, using content to attract and nurture customers.
Our team improved the website to increase inbound leads from organic search and social posts.
They hired an agency to develop an inbound strategy centered on SEO and helpful blog posts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: content marketing - creating and sharing useful content to attract and retain customers
  • Antonym: outbound - marketing that pushes messages to potential customers through advertising or direct outreach; opposite of inbound in marketing

Etymology

From the phrase "inbound marketing" (popularized in the early 2000s), from in + bound ('headed toward').
