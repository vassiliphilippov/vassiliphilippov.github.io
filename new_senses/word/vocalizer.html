vocalizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.kəˌlaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kə.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who sings, especially one who uses their voice as a musical instrument
person who sings

Examples

As the main vocalizer, he often experimented with different singing styles.
Each vocalizer brought a unique tone to the choir's performance.
The band's vocalizer impressed the crowd with her powerful range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singer - a person who sings, especially professionally

Etymology

From 'vocalize' + '-er', meaning one who vocalizes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪzə / (BrE)
an organism, especially an animal, that produces vocal sounds or calls
organism that makes vocal sounds

Examples

Among mammals, the wolf is a particularly effective vocalizer when communicating within its pack.
Is the barn owl considered a vocalizer, or does it remain mostly silent?
The scientist observed that the dolphin was a skilled vocalizer during its communication experiments.

Synset Examples

an utterer of foul oaths
is the giraffe a vocalizer?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utterer - someone or something that utters or speaks

Etymology

From 'vocalize' (to utter with the voice) + '-er' (one who does).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
(computing, especially accessibility) a program or device that converts written text into spoken words (text-to-speech).
text-to-speech program or device

Examples

The screen reader includes a natural-sounding vocalizer that reads emails aloud every morning.
Hospitals now use cloud-based vocalizers to convert patient instructions into multiple languages for visitors.
Developers tested the new vocalizer on noisy recordings to improve pronunciation and clarity.
