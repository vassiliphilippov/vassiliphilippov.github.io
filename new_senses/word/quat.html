quat

1. NOUN

/ kwɑt / (AmE), / kwɑːt / (BrE)
The leaves of the shrub Catha edulis, chewed for their stimulant and euphoric effects, or brewed as tea.
leaves of Catha edulis used as a stimulant

Examples

In some communities, men gather each afternoon to chew quat together.
She drank tea brewed from fresh quat leaves.
Transporting quat across borders is illegal in many countries.

Synset Examples

in Yemen, kat is used daily by 85% of adults

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khat - the leaves of the Catha edulis shrub, chewed for stimulant effects
  • Synonym: catha edulis - an evergreen shrub native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula

Etymology

Variant of 'khat', from Arabic 'qāt' (قات).

2. NOUN NEW

/ kwɑt / (AmE), / kwɑːt / (BrE)
(informal) a quaternary ammonium compound used as a disinfectant or cleaning agent on surfaces and equipment.
quaternary-ammonium disinfectant

Examples

Hospitals prefer quat-based cleaners because they kill bacteria and are gentle on equipment.
After the spill, the staff wiped the counter with a cloth soaked in quat solution.
Farm workers warned that some plants reacted poorly to repeated use of quat sprays.
