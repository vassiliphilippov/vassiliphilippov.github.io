messenger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmesəndʒər / (AmE), / ˈmesɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person who carries and delivers messages, packages, or documents to others
person who carries messages

Examples

In ancient times, a messenger would travel for days to bring news between cities.
She handed the envelope to the messenger and asked him to deliver it quickly.
The company hired a messenger to deliver important documents across town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courier - a person who transports documents or goods; often used for urgent delivery

Etymology

From Old French messagier, from message (message).

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8mes\u0259nd\u0292\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8mes\u026and\u0292\u0259 / (BrE)
a software application or online service that lets users send and receive text, voice, or multimedia messages on phones or computers.
messaging app or service

Examples

She relies on the messenger app to coordinate teams across time zones.
When the Wi‑Fi dropped, his messenger failed to deliver the urgent files to colleagues.
She cleared space on her phone because the messenger stored hundreds of old voice messages.
