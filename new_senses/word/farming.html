farming

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or business of cultivating land and raising animals for food, wool, or other products
cultivation of land or raising animals

Examples

Many families in this region depend on farming as their main source of income.
Over the past decade, technology has transformed the field of farming.
Sustainable farming practices help protect the environment and improve food quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agriculture - the science or practice of farming, including cultivation of soil and rearing of animals

Etymology

From the verb 'farm' plus the noun-forming suffix '-ing'. Rooted in Old French 'fermer' and Medieval Latin 'firma' (fixed payment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity or business of growing crops and raising animals, especially as one's main job.
working in agriculture as an occupation

Examples

Farming requires dedication and knowledge of the land.
He returned to farming after years of working in the city.
Many rural communities depend on farming for their livelihood.

Synset Examples

farming is a strenuous life
there's no work on the land any more

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agriculture - the science or practice of farming, including cultivation of the soil and rearing of animals

Etymology

From the verb 'farm', which comes from Middle English 'fermen', from Old French 'fermier', from Medieval Latin 'firma' (fixed payment).
