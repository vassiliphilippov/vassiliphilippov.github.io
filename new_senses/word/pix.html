pix

1. NOUN

/ pɪks / (AmE), / pɪks / (BrE)
A container or small box in which the consecrated wafers for the Eucharist are kept.
container for Eucharistic wafers

Examples

During communion, the deacon carefully opened the pix to distribute the wafers.
The ornate pix was kept in the sanctuary for special occasions.
The priest placed the pix on the altar before the service began.

Etymology

From Latin 'pyxis' (box), via Old French and late Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ pɪks / (AmE), / pɪks / (BrE)

chest for coins awaiting assay
archaic

Examples

Historians studied the pix to understand old minting practices in the royal treasury.
Inspectors examined the contents of the pix before they released the currency for circulation.
The gold coins were locked in the pix until the official assay was conducted.

Etymology

From Old French *pyxide*, from Latin *pyxis* (box, chest).
