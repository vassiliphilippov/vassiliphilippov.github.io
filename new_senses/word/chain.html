chain

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪn / (AmE), / tʃeɪn / (BrE)
A series of usually metal rings or links connected together, used for fastening, pulling, supporting, or as ornamentation.
series of connected metal rings or links

Examples

She wore a delicate gold chain around her neck.
The dog was kept outside with a heavy chain attached to its collar.
The old gate was secured with a rusty iron chain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rope - a strong, thick line made of fibers or wire twisted together
  • Synonym: cable - a thick, strong rope or wire

Etymology

From Old French 'chaine', from Latin 'catena' (a chain).
