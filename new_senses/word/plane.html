plane

1. VERB

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
to smooth or shape a surface by removing thin layers, typically using a carpenter's plane
smooth or shape with a plane

Examples

Before painting the door, he carefully planed its edges to ensure a perfect fit.
She spent the afternoon planing down the wooden surface to remove any uneven spots.
The carpenter planes the rough boards until they are smooth enough for furniture making.

Synset Examples

The machine shaved off fine layers from the piece of wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shave - to remove thin layers from a surface
  • Synonym: sand - to make smooth by rubbing with sandpaper

Etymology

From Latin 'planare' (to make level), from 'planus' (flat).
