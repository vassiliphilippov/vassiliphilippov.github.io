to invoke

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪnˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to refer to a law, authority, or principle in support of an action or argument
cite as authority, law, or principle

Examples

During the debate, she invoked the Constitution to support her position.
Facing criticism, the manager invoked company policy to justify his decision.
The lawyer invoked an outdated statute to defend his client.

Synset Examples

He invoked the law that would save him
I appealed to the law of 1900
She invoked an ancient law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cite - to refer to as evidence or authority
  • Synonym: appeal to - to make a serious or urgent request to an authority or principle

Etymology

From Latin invocare (to call upon), from in- (upon) + vocare (to call).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪnˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to call upon a spirit, deity, or supernatural power to appear or help, especially as if by magic; to summon into action or bring into existence, often with the suggestion of supernatural means
summon into action or existence, as if by magic

Examples

Ancient legends say one can invoke rain by performing a sacred dance.
The magician claimed to invoke a protective force around the audience.
The shaman tried to invoke the spirits during the ritual.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjure - to call upon or command a spirit by invocation or magic
  • Synonym: summon - to call to appear, especially by supernatural means

Etymology

From Latin 'invocare' (to call upon), from 'in-' (upon) + 'vocare' (to call).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪnˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to call upon (a deity, spirit, or higher power) for help, support, or protection, especially through prayer or earnest request
call upon for help or support

Examples

During the ceremony, the priest invoked blessings upon the newlyweds.
In moments of crisis, many people invoke the help of a higher power.
The villagers would invoke the goddess before setting out to sea.

Examples

In many programming languages, you invoke a function by writing its name followed by parentheses and arguments.
The API documentation showed how to invoke the authentication method asynchronously to avoid blocking the main thread.
When the event fires, the framework automatically invokes any registered callback functions in order of priority.
