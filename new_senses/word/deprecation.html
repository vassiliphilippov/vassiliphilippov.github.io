deprecation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a prayer or act of entreaty asking for the removal or prevention of evil or disaster
prayer to avert evil
archaic

Examples

Ancient texts often include deprecations to guard against misfortune.
During the ceremony, the priest offered a solemn deprecation for protection against the coming storm.
His nightly prayers were a form of deprecation, hoping to keep his family safe.

Etymology

From Latin deprecatio (prayer for deliverance), from deprecari (to ward off by prayer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing disapproval or criticism, especially of oneself
expression of disapproval or criticism

Examples

He replied to the praise with typical self-deprecation, insisting that anyone could have done the same.
Her constant deprecation made it difficult for her colleagues to accept her compliments as genuine.
The manager's deprecation of his own achievements showed his humility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disapproval - the act of thinking something is wrong or bad
  • Antonym: approval - the act of thinking something is good or acceptable

Etymology

From Latin deprecatio (prayer against, disapproval), from deprecari (to avert by prayer, to deprecate).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˌdɛprəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
In computing, the act or state of marking a software feature, API, or function as obsolete and discouraged for use, typically with a warning and planned removal in a future release.
marking a software feature obsolete

Examples

The API's deprecation warning alerts developers that the function will be removed in a future release.
After the library's deprecation, maintainers provided migration guides and compatibility shims for users.
Browsers show console warnings when a deprecation affects a web API used by many sites.
