slicer

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈslaɪsə / (BrE)
a knife or tool specially designed for cutting particular foods into thin pieces, such as cheese or bread
knife or tool for slicing food

Examples

Be careful when cleaning the meat slicer; the blade is very sharp.
The bakery uses a bread slicer to cut loaves quickly.
This cheese slicer makes even, thin slices every time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - cutting instrument with a blade
  • Synonym: carver - knife used for carving or slicing meat

Etymology

From 'slice' + '-er', denoting a tool for making slices.

/ ˈslaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈslaɪsə / (BrE)

3. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪsər / (AmE), / ˈslaɪsə / (BrE)
A golfer whose shots typically curve to the right (for a right-handed golfer) due to unintentional spin.
golfer whose shots curve right

Examples

Among amateur golfers, being a slicer is more common than being a hooker.
He was known as a slicer, struggling to keep his drives on the fairway.
The new club helped reduce his tendency as a slicer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hooker - a golfer whose shots typically curve left (for right-handed golfers)

Etymology

From 'slice', meaning to hit a golf ball with sidespin, plus the agent suffix '-er'.
