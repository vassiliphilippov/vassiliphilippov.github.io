editing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of preparing written, visual, or audio material for publication or presentation by correcting, arranging, or revising it to an acceptable form
preparation and revision of material

Examples

She spent several hours editing her novel before submitting it to the publisher.
The documentary won an award for outstanding editing.
Video editing requires both technical skill and creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revision - the act of revising or preparing a manuscript for publication
  • Synonym: proofreading - the act of checking for errors in text before publication

Etymology

From 'edit' + '-ing'; 'edit' from Latin 'editus', past participle of 'edere' (to give out, put forth).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈedɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈedɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(in genetics) the process of making deliberate, targeted changes to an organism's DNA or genome, using techniques such as CRISPR to alter specific genes or traits.
targeted modification of DNA
medical

Examples

Researchers are refining gene editing techniques to correct genetic mutations.
Clinical trials test whether editing embryos can safely prevent inherited disorders in future generations.
The lab used CRISPR for precise editing of plant genomes to increase drought resistance.

Synset Examples

Researchers are refining gene editing techniques to correct genetic mutations.
Clinical trials test whether editing embryos can safely prevent inherited disorders in future generations.
The lab used CRISPR for precise editing of plant genomes to increase drought resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene editing - the targeted modification of specific genes within an organism's DNA
  • Synonym: genome editing - the alteration of an organism's complete set of DNA to change traits or functions

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'edit' (from Latin 'editus', past participle of 'edere', to put forth); applied to genomes in the late 20th–21st century.
