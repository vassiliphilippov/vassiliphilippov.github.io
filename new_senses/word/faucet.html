faucet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔsɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsɪt / (BrE)
A device for controlling the flow of liquid, especially water, from a pipe or container.
device for controlling water flow

Examples

He turned on the faucet and waited for the water to heat up.
Please make sure the faucet is tightly closed after you use it.
The kitchen faucet has been leaking for days, and we need to fix it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap - device for controlling the flow of liquid from a pipe (chiefly British English)

Etymology

From Old French *fausset* (a tap for drawing liquid) and possibly from Provençal *fausset*, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfɔsət / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsət / (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency) a website or online service that dispenses very small amounts of cryptocurrency to users, often for completing simple tasks or as a promotional reward.
website that gives small crypto rewards

Examples

Many users try a faucet that awards small Bitcoin rewards for simple tasks.
The security team warned students against crypto faucet sites that ask for private keys or seed phrases.
After the promotion ended, the company shut down its popular faucet that had distributed small tokens daily.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airdrop - distribution of cryptocurrency tokens to users, often for promotional purposes or community rewards

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the physical word 'faucet' (a device that dispenses liquid), applied to services that dispense small amounts of cryptocurrency.
