prop

1. VERB

/ prɑp / (AmE), / prɒp / (BrE)
to support something physically by placing an object under or against it, to prevent it from falling or to keep it in a particular position
to support something by placing something under or against it

Examples

He used a chair to prop the door open.
She propped her bike against the wall before going inside.
The old tree was propped up with wooden beams to keep it from falling.

Synset Examples

shore and buttress an old building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to hold something up so that it does not fall
  • Synonym: buttress - to support by a projecting structure or reinforcement

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'proppe', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'prop' (plug, stopper).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01221568-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ prɑp / (AmE), / prɒp / (BrE)
an object used by actors on the stage or set of a play, film, or television show, usually as part of the action or setting
object used on stage or film set

Examples

Each prop must be returned to its designated area after use.
She designed a realistic crown as a prop for the school play.
The actor forgot to pick up the prop sword before going on stage.

Synset Examples

before every scene he ran down his checklist of props

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage property - an object used in theatrical productions

Etymology

Shortened from 'property', originally meaning any item belonging to the props master in theatrical productions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04018636-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prɑp / (AmE), / prɒp / (BrE)
a device with blades that rotates to push against air or water, especially in an engine-powered aircraft or boat; short for propeller
rotating device (short for propeller)

Examples

After the crash, fragments of the plane's prop were scattered on the runway.
He carefully removed the prop from the outboard motor for maintenance.
The pilot inspected the prop before taking off to ensure it was free of damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller - device with rotating blades that pushes against air or water

Etymology

Shortened form of 'propeller'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02694945-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ prɑp / (AmE), / prɒp / (BrE)
an object placed under or against something to support it and prevent it from falling or shaking
object used to support something

Examples

A metal prop held the heavy stage scenery in place during the performance.
The worker placed a prop under the ladder to make sure it was stable.
They used a wooden prop to keep the door open during the summer breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that supports or holds up an object
  • Synonym: brace - a device that clamps things tightly together or supports them in a specific position

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'proppen', from Old French 'proper', from Latin 'proppāre' (to prop up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04017618-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN NEW

/ prɑp / (AmE), / prɒp / (BrE)
(in computing, especially in user-interface programming) a short form of 'property': a named value or attribute supplied to a component or object that controls its behavior or appearance.
software property / component attribute

Examples

In our React app we pass a prop called 'color' to change the button's appearance.
Each widget reads its prop during rendering to decide which data to display.
You can set default values for a component's prop to avoid errors when none are provided.
