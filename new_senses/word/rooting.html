rooting

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a plant develops roots and begins to grow
process of putting forth roots (plant growth)

Examples

Commercial growers use hormones to speed up the rooting of plant cuttings.
Successful rooting is essential for transplanting most garden plants.
The cuttings showed signs of rooting after two weeks in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: germination - the process of a seed developing into a new plant

Etymology

From root + -ing, indicating the process of taking root.
