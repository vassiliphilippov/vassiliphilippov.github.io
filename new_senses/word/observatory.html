observatory

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɝː.vəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / əbˈzɜː.və.tər.i / (BrE)
A building equipped for the observation and study of astronomical phenomena.
a building for astronomical observation

Examples

At night, visitors can use the telescopes at the observatory to view distant planets and stars.
She volunteered at the local observatory during their public stargazing events.
The university built a new observatory on the hill for astronomical research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planetarium - a building or device for projecting images of celestial bodies

Etymology

From Latin 'observatorius' (relating to observing).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈzɝː.vəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / əbˈzɜː.və.tər.i / (BrE)
A building or place, often situated on a height, from which a wide view of the surrounding area can be enjoyed.
building with wide view of surroundings

Examples

From the old observatory on the hill, we could see for miles in every direction.
The hotel was built with an observatory that overlooked the entire valley.
Tourists climbed to the observatory to appreciate the breathtaking landscape.

Etymology

From Latin observatorium, from observare (to observe).

A digital platform or service that collects, monitors, and analyses observational data for a specific field and provides dashboards, reports, or alerts.
digital platform for observational data

Examples

The research team published real-time maps on the new observatory tracking urban air quality.
Policy makers relied on the public health observatory for daily hospitalization and mortality trends.
Students used the climate observatory to download satellite temperature datasets for their projects.
