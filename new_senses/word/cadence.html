cadence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈkeɪd(ə)ns / (BrE)
the rhythmic flow or pattern of sounds or movements, especially in music, speech, or poetry
rhythmic flow or pattern

Examples

The cadence of her speech was calm and soothing, making the lecture easy to follow.
The poem's gentle cadence captured the peaceful mood of the evening.
The soldiers marched in perfect cadence, their boots striking the ground in unison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythm - a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound

Etymology

From Latin 'cadentia' (falling), from 'cadere' (to fall).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈkeɪd(ə)ns / (BrE)
the rhythmic accent or flow of sounds, especially in poetry or verse; the rise and fall of the voice in reading or speaking
rhythmic accent or flow in verse

Examples

An irregular cadence can disrupt the flow and mood of a poem.
He admired the cadence of ancient Greek verse, which gave the poetry its unique sound.
The poet carefully crafted each line so the cadence would evoke a musical quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythm - a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound
  • Antonym: monotone - a continuing sound, especially of a person's voice, that is unchanging in pitch

Etymology

From Latin cadentia (a falling), from cadere (to fall).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈkæd.əns / (BrE)
a sequence of chords that brings a musical phrase or section to a close; the ending of a musical passage
ending sequence in music

Examples

After every verse, the song wraps up with the same gentle cadence.
Composers often use a perfect cadence to signal the end of a section.
The symphony ended with a powerful cadence that left the audience in awe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coda - a concluding passage of a piece or movement in music

Etymology

From Italian *cadenza* and Latin *cadentia* (falling).

/ ˈkeɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈkeɪd(ə)ns / (BrE)
a regular, planned rhythm or sequence of activities, communications, or events used by a team or organization (for example, meetings, product releases, or updates).
regular scheduled rhythm (business)

Examples

The marketing team adopted a weekly cadence for customer emails and product announcements.
To improve collaboration, the startup set a monthly product-review cadence that everyone now follows.
Maintaining a reliable release cadence helped the engineering team deliver updates predictably every quarter.
