peer

1. NOUN

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
a person who is of equal status, age, or ability as another person in a particular group
person of equal status or rank

Examples

As a new team member, she wanted to be respected by her peers.
Children often learn important social skills from their peers at school.
He struggled to keep up with his academic peers throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal - a person or thing considered to be the same as another in status or quality
  • Antonym: superior - a person of higher status or rank
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person of lower rank or status

Etymology

From Latin 'par' (equal), via Old French 'peer'.

2. VERB

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
to look closely or carefully at something, especially when it is difficult to see clearly
look closely or searchingly

Examples

He peered into the box, trying to see what was inside.
She had to peer through the dense fog to find her way home.
They peered over the fence, curious about the commotion next door.

Synset Examples

We peered into the back of the shop to see whether a salesman was around

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glance - to take a quick look at something
  • Degree Higher: stare - to look fixedly or vacantly at someone or something

Etymology

From Middle English *peren*, probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Swedish *pira* (to peep, look narrowly).

3. NOUN

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
a nobleman or noblewoman who holds a hereditary or lifetime title and is a member of the British peerage
noble of the British peerage
british

Examples

He was made a peer for his contributions to science and was granted a seat in the House of Lords.
Several peers attended the opening of Parliament, dressed in ceremonial robes.
The title of peer has been passed down through generations in their family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocrat - a member of the aristocracy
  • Antonym: commoner - someone who is not a member of the nobility

Etymology

From Old French *per* (equal, peer), from Latin *par* (equal), with the title reflecting the equal standing among nobility.

device on a peer-to-peer network

Examples
