saltiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔl.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːl.ti.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or degree of tasting of or containing salt
the quality of being salty

Examples

She complained about the saltiness of the potato chips.
Some cheeses are prized for their distinctive saltiness.
The saltiness of the soup overwhelmed the delicate flavors of the vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salinity - the concentration of salt in water or soil
  • Antonym: bitterness - the quality of being bitter in taste
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of being sweet in taste

Etymology

From 'salty' + '-ness', with 'salty' from Old English 'sealt' (salt).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔlti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlti.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or sensation of tasting of salt; the presence of a salty flavor
the taste of salt

Examples

I love the saltiness of olives, especially in salads.
The saltiness of the chips made me quite thirsty.
The saltiness of the soup was overpowering, so I had to add more water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salinity - the amount of salt in a substance, especially water
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of tasting sweet
  • Synonym: salt - a white crystalline substance used to flavor food

Etymology

From 'salty' + '-ness', with 'salty' deriving from Old English 'sealt' (salt).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɔl.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːl.ti.nəs / (BrE)
(of language or humor) the quality of being earthy, witty, or slightly coarse, often in an entertaining or direct way.
earthy or slightly coarse language or humor

Examples

Her grandmother was known for the saltiness of her jokes at family gatherings.
Some critics argue that the saltiness of the novel's dialogue adds authenticity to the characters.
The comedian's saltiness was well-received by the late-night crowd.

Synset Examples

the saltiness of their language was inappropriate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthiness - plain, natural, or unrefined quality, often in language or humor
  • Synonym: coarseness - the quality of being rough or vulgar, especially in speech
  • Antonym: refinement - the process of removing vulgarity or crudeness from language, making it more polite

Etymology

Derived from 'salty,' from Old English 'sealt' (taste of salt), extended figuratively to describe earthy or coarse speech.
