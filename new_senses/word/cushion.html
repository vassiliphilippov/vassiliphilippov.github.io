cushion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈkʊʃən / (BrE)
the layer of air that supports a hovercraft or similar vehicle, keeping it above the surface
layer of air under a hovercraft

Examples

A thin cushion beneath the vehicle allows it to traverse both land and water.
Engineers improved the cushion to reduce friction and increase speed.
The hovercraft glided smoothly over the lake on a cushion of air.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈkʊʃən / (BrE)
a device that absorbs shocks or reduces the impact of sudden movements, especially in machinery or vehicles
shock-absorbing device in machines or vehicles

Examples

A new cushion was installed in the elevator system to ensure smoother stops.
The faulty cushion caused the machinery to vibrate excessively during operation.
The train's suspension uses a cushion to absorb the impact of the rails.
