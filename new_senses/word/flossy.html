flossy

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɑsi / (AmE), / ˈflɒsi / (BrE)
having a soft, fluffy, or down-like texture; resembling floss or down
soft and down-like in texture

Examples

She wore a sweater so flossy that it felt like a gentle cloud.
The flossy texture of the blanket was perfect for a cold night.
The chick's flossy feathers made it feel incredibly delicate in my hands.

Etymology

From 'floss', meaning soft threadlike fiber, with suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ NEW

/ ˈflɑsi / (AmE), / ˈflɒsi / (BrE)
(informal, slang) showy or ostentatious in appearance or behaviour, especially through expensive clothes, jewellery, or displays meant to attract attention.
showy; ostentatious
slang informal

Examples

Her outfit and jewellery were flossy enough to turn heads at the party.
After the promotion he grew flossier, buying watches and designer shirts every month.
The rapper's latest video featured a flossy parade of cars, gold chains, and champagne.
