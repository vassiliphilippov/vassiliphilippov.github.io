scuttlebutt

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌt̬lˌbʌt / (AmE), / ˈskʌt.əl.bʌt / (BrE)
rumor or gossip, especially information that is not confirmed and may be spread informally
rumor; gossip

Examples

According to the latest scuttlebutt, the company might be merging with a rival.
She tried not to listen to the scuttlebutt at the office, but it was hard to avoid.
There's a lot of scuttlebutt going around about who will be promoted next.

Synset Examples

the divorce caused much gossip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often involving rumors
  • Synonym: rumor - unverified information spread informally
  • Antonym: fact - something known or proved to be true

Etymology

Originally nautical slang: from 'scuttle butt', a cask with a hole in it for dispensing drinking water, where sailors exchanged gossip.

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈskʌtəlˌbʊt / (AmE), / ˈskʌt.əl.bʊt / (BrE)
(in computing) a peer-to-peer gossip protocol and family of systems for replicating messages or state between decentralized nodes, often associated with the Secure Scuttlebutt (SSB) project.
peer-to-peer gossip protocol

Examples

The developers implemented scuttlebutt to replicate messages across offline-capable peer-to-peer devices.
After a network partition, the node used scuttlebutt to reconcile state with its peers automatically.
Their messaging app switched to scuttlebutt so offline-first data sync would be simple and resilient.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip protocol - a communication method where nodes exchange information by repeatedly sharing updates with a subset of peers
  • Synonym: Secure Scuttlebutt (SSB) - a specific decentralized, append-only messaging and replication system that implements the scuttlebutt model

Etymology

Named after the nautical slang 'scuttle butt' (a water cask) where sailors exchanged gossip; the term was adopted in computing to name a gossip-based replication system.
