facet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæs.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæs.ɪt / (BrE)
a small, flat, and smooth surface on a cut gemstone or a bone
smooth surface on a gem or bone

Examples

Light sparkled off every facet of the gemstone, creating a dazzling effect.
The jeweler carefully examined each facet of the diamond for flaws.
The vertebrae have a small facet where they connect to each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - the surface of something, especially one that is presented to view

Etymology

From French 'facette', diminutive of 'face' (face, surface).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæsət / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪt / (BrE)
a particular aspect or feature of something, especially of a problem, situation, or subject
distinct aspect or feature

Examples

His research explores a new facet of human behavior.
She tried to consider every facet of the issue before making a decision.
The problem has many facets that need to be addressed separately.

Synset Examples

he studied every facet of the question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspect - a particular part or feature of something
  • Synonym: dimension - an aspect or feature of a situation or problem

Etymology

From French 'facette', diminutive of 'face' (face), from Latin 'facies'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈfæsət / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪt / (BrE)
(in information retrieval and user interfaces) a property or attribute of items (for example brand, price, or color) used to classify, group, and filter search results; one selectable category in faceted navigation.
filterable attribute in search

Examples

Users can refine search results by selecting a facet such as brand or price range.
The online store added a new facet for material, improving product filtering and discovery.
Some sites show multiple facets so customers can combine filters like size and color.
