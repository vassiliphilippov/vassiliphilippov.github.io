fracking

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfrækɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of extracting oil or gas from underground rock by injecting liquid at high pressure to create fractures
method of extracting oil/gas by fracturing rock

Examples

Advances in fracking technology have increased domestic oil production significantly.
Opponents of fracking argue that it can pollute groundwater and cause earthquakes.
The government approved several new fracking sites despite environmental concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydraulic fracturing - the full technical term for fracking: fracturing rock formations using pressurized liquid

Etymology

Shortened from 'hydraulic fracturing', with 'frack' as a blend of 'fracture' and '-ing'.
