endoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈen.də.skəʊp / (BrE)
a long, slender medical instrument used for examining the interior of a bodily organ or performing minor surgery, typically consisting of a flexible tube with a light and camera attached
medical instrument for internal examination
medical

Examples

During the procedure, the doctor inserted an endoscope through the patient's mouth to reach the esophagus.
Modern endoscopes provide high-definition images, making diagnosis much easier and less invasive.
The surgeon used an endoscope to inspect the patient's stomach lining for abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthroscope - instrument for examining the inside of a joint
  • Synonym: laparoscope - instrument for examining the interior of the abdomen

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'skopein' (to look).

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈɛn.dəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈen.də.skəʊp / (BrE)
(in industrial or non-medical contexts) a small rigid or flexible camera probe used to inspect the interior of machinery, pipes, engines, or other confined spaces that are hard to reach.
industrial inspection camera probe

Examples

The aviation mechanic inserted an endoscope into the engine to inspect for hidden cracks.
Plumbers often use a waterproof endoscope to find blockages deep inside narrow drain pipes.
The company bought several flexible endoscopes for inspecting complex machinery during routine maintenance checks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection camera - a camera designed to examine the interior of pipes, engines, or other confined areas for faults or blockages
