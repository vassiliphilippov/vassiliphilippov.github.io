pro forma

1. ADJ

/ proʊ ˈfɔrmə / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
done or produced as a matter of form or for the sake of formality, rather than for a substantive purpose
done as a formality or matter of form

Examples

His apology sounded pro forma rather than sincere.
It was a pro forma meeting, lasting only ten minutes with no decisions made.
The board approved the pro forma budget before reviewing the final figures.

Synset Examples

a one-candidate pro forma election

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: formal - done in accordance with rules or convention

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'for form's sake'.

(in business and accounting) describing financial statements or figures that are hypothetical, adjusted, or projected and do not represent actual historical results.
adjusted or projected financial figures

Examples

The company reported pro forma revenue that excluded one-off costs to show underlying growth.
Analysts adjusted their models after reviewing the firm's pro forma statements for next quarter.
Investors were sceptical when management presented pro forma earnings that removed restructuring expenses.
