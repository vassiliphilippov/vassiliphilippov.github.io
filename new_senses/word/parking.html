parking

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
an area or space where vehicles can be parked
space for parking vehicles

Examples

The parking fills up quickly during the morning rush hour.
There is parking available for all customers at the rear of the building.
We couldn't find any parking near the concert venue, so we had to walk several blocks.

Synset Examples

there is plenty of parking behind the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: car park - a designated area or building for parking cars
  • Synonym: parking lot - an outdoor area where vehicles can be parked

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of positioning a vehicle in a place where it can be left for a period of time
the act of positioning a vehicle for temporary storage

Examples

He found parking difficult during rush hour near the office.
She received a ticket for illegal parking on the street.
The city introduced new rules for parking to reduce congestion downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: park - to bring a vehicle to a stop and leave it temporarily

Etymology

From the verb 'park' (to bring a vehicle to a halt and leave it temporarily) plus the suffix '-ing'.

3. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8p\u0251rk\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8p\u0251\u02d0k\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
(in computing and internet business) the practice of registering a domain name and placing a temporary webpage or landing page (often with advertisements) to monetize or reserve the name.
registering a domain to display ads

Examples

The startup earns revenue by parking premium domain names on ad-supported landing pages.
After buying several domains, he tried parking them while he developed his business plan.
Domain parking can generate small income from clicks before a site is ready.

Synset Examples

The startup earns revenue by parking premium domain names on ad-supported landing pages.

Domain parking can generate small income from clicks before a site is ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain parking - the practice of hosting a placeholder page for a registered domain, usually showing ads
  • Synonym: domain monetization - methods of earning revenue from domain names, such as ads, affiliate links, or resale

Etymology

From the verb 'park' (to leave temporarily); extended metaphorically to domain names in early internet usage (late 1990s–2000s).
