redundant

1. ADJ

/ ɹɪˈdʌndənt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndənt / (BrE)
(of words or information) unnecessary because it is more than needed or repeats something already stated.
unnecessarily repetitive (of words/information)

Examples

His explanation became redundant after he repeated the same point for the third time.
The report included several redundant statements that could have been omitted.
Try to avoid using redundant expressions like 'basic fundamentals' in your writing.

Synset Examples

‘a true fact’ and ‘a free gift’ are pleonastic expressions
the phrase ‘a beginner who has just started’ is tautological
at the risk of being redundant I return to my original proposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tautological - using or containing unnecessary repetition of meaning
  • Antonym: concise - giving information clearly and in as few words as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'redundans', present participle of 'redundare' (to overflow).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈdʌndənt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndənt / (BrE)
Not needed or no longer useful because there is more than is necessary.
not needed; more than necessary

Examples

After the company installed new software, many clerical jobs became redundant.
Having three different locks on the same door seems a bit redundant.
The last paragraph in your essay is redundant and could be removed to improve clarity.

Synset Examples

trying to lose excess weight

sleeping in the spare room
supernumerary ornamentation
it was supererogatory of her to gloat
delete superfluous (or unnecessary) words
extra ribs as well as other supernumerary internal parts
surplus cheese distributed to the needy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superfluous - unnecessary, especially through being more than enough
  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important

Etymology

From Latin 'redundant-', meaning 'surging up', from the verb 'redundare'.

3. ADJ NEW

/ rɪˈdʌndənt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndənt / (BrE)
(of a system or component) duplicated or having backup elements so that function continues if one part fails; providing fault tolerance.
having backup components for reliability

Examples

The data center uses a redundant power supply to ensure uninterrupted service during failures.
Flight systems are designed with redundant controls so pilots can rely on backups if one fails.
We added a redundant network connection to maintain access while the main link undergoes maintenance.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backup - a duplicate system or component kept to replace the primary one if it fails
  • Synonym: fault-tolerant - designed to continue operating correctly even if some components fail

Etymology

From Latin 'redundans', present participle of 'redundare' (to overflow).
