BS

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈɛs / (AmE), / biː ˈɛs / (BrE)
An undergraduate academic degree awarded for completed courses in science or related fields, typically lasting three to four years.
bachelor's degree in science

Examples

After earning his BS, he decided to pursue a master's degree.
Many engineering jobs require at least a BS in a relevant field.
She graduated with a BS in chemistry from a top university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: BSc - Bachelor of Science abbreviation (mainly UK/Commonwealth)

Etymology

Abbreviation of Bachelor of Science, from Medieval Latin 'baccalaureus scientiae'.
