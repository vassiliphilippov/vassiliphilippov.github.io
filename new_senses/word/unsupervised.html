unsupervised

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈsuːpərˌvaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈsuːpəˌvaɪzd / (BrE)
not supervised or watched over by someone in authority or responsibility
not supervised or observed

Examples

Children should not be left unsupervised near the swimming pool.
Employees working unsupervised are expected to be highly responsible.
The experiment was conducted unsupervised to test the participants' independence.

Synset Examples

the school maintains unsupervised study halls during free periods
reliable workers are generally unsupervised

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supervised - under the supervision or observation of someone

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + supervised.

Examples
