velocity

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɑːsəti / (AmE), / vɪˈlɒsəti / (BrE)
the speed of something in a particular direction; the rate at which an object moves over a specific distance in a given time
speed in a given direction

Examples

The car reached a velocity of 100 miles per hour as it raced down the highway.
The scientist measured the velocity of the falling object during the experiment.
Wind velocity can greatly affect the outcome of a long jump competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates

Etymology

From Latin 'velocitas', from 'velox' (swift, speedy).
