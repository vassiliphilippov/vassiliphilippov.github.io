to impute

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpjut / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjuːt / (BrE)
to attribute (especially something undesirable) to someone or something; to assign the responsibility or cause of (often unjustly)
attribute something, especially negative, to someone

Examples

He was careful not to impute blame to any particular person for the mistake.
It would be unfair to impute any wrongdoing to her without evidence.
Some critics impute ulterior motives to the company's charitable actions.

Synset Examples

We attributed this quotation to Shakespeare
People impute great cleverness to cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribute - to regard something as being caused by someone or something
  • Synonym: ascribe - to regard a quality as belonging to someone
  • Antonym: exonerate - to absolve someone from blame for a fault or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin imputare (to charge, reckon), from in- (in, into) + putare (to think, consider).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpjut / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjuːt / (BrE)
to attribute responsibility, fault, or cause to someone or something, often without definite proof
attribute fault or responsibility to

Examples

It was unfair to impute blame to the new employee without evidence.
She resented that her motives were imputed to selfishness when she was truly trying to help.
Some historians impute the country's rapid progress to innovative government policies.

Synset Examples

The teacher imputed the student's failure to his nervousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribute - to regard something as being caused by
  • Synonym: ascribe - to assign a cause or responsibility to
  • Antonym: exonerate - officially absolve from blame

Etymology

From Latin imputare (to reckon, charge, attribute), from in- (in) + putare (to reckon).

3. VERB NEW

/ ɪmˈpjut / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjuːt / (BrE)
(in statistics and data analysis) to estimate and replace missing or incomplete values in a dataset using a statistical method.
estimate and fill missing data

Examples

Researchers impute missing income responses to avoid bias in their national survey analysis.
The software imputes average values when a few entries in the dataset are blank.
By imputing missing test scores, teachers could compare student progress across terms.
