incubator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbeɪtə / (BrE)
an enclosed apparatus used to keep its contents at a constant, controlled temperature, especially for hatching eggs or caring for premature infants
device that keeps a constant temperature for eggs or infants

Examples

Chicks hatched in an incubator during the school science project.
The laboratory uses an incubator to culture bacteria at a precise temperature.
The premature baby was placed in an incubator to help regulate her body temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brooder - heated enclosure for young animals, especially poultry

Etymology

From Latin 'incubare' (to lie upon, to brood), via Late Latin 'incubator'.
