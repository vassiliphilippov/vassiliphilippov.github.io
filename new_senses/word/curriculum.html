curriculum

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪkjʊləm / (AmE), / kəˈrɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
the subjects comprising a course of study in a school, college, or university
course of study in education

Examples

Changes to the national curriculum were introduced to improve literacy rates.
She developed a new curriculum for teaching environmental studies.
The school's curriculum includes courses in science, mathematics, and the arts.

Synset Examples

he was admitted to a new program at the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syllabus - an outline of topics covered in a course
  • Synonym: program - a planned series of future events or performances (in education, a set of related courses)

Etymology

From Latin 'curriculum' (course, career), from 'currere' (to run).

2. NOUN NEW

/ kəˈrɪkjələm / (AmE), / kəˈrɪkjələm / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a structured sequence of training examples or tasks arranged so a model learns progressively, often from easier to harder cases.
machine-learning training sequence

Examples

The researchers used a curriculum that started with simple examples before introducing complex cases.
To improve learning speed, engineers designed a progressive curriculum for training the neural network.
During experiments the team adjusted the curriculum to present harder inputs later in training.

Synset Examples

The researchers used a curriculum that started with simple examples before introducing complex cases.
To improve learning speed, engineers designed a progressive curriculum for training the neural network.
During experiments the team adjusted the curriculum to present harder inputs later in training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curriculum learning - a machine-learning approach that trains models by presenting examples in a meaningful, ordered sequence
  • Synonym: training schedule - the order and timing of training examples or tasks used to teach a model

Etymology

From Latin 'curriculum' (course, career), from 'currere' (to run).
