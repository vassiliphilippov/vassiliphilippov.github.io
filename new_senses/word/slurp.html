to slurp

1. VERB

/ slɜrp / (AmE), / slɜːp / (BrE)
to eat or drink something with a loud sucking noise
eat or drink with a loud noise

Examples

He loudly slurped his noodles, drawing curious looks from nearby diners.
Please don't slurp your soup at the table—it's considered rude.
She slurped the milkshake through her straw with obvious enjoyment.

Synset Examples

He slurped his soup

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sip - to drink (something) by taking small mouthfuls

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made when noisily sucking liquid.

2. VERB NEW

/ sl5crp / (AmE), / sl5cd0p / (BrE)
(in computing) to read, transfer, or ingest a large amount of data quickly and often automatically, typically from a file, database, or remote source, sometimes in a single operation.
ingest large amounts of data (computing)

Examples

A script can slurp thousands of records from the database in minutes for analysis.
They slurped the compressed log files into memory and began searching for errors.
The backup tool is slurping remote API responses each night to build a local cache.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ingest - to take data into a system or process for storage or processing
  • Synonym: import - to bring data into a program or database from another source

Etymology

Metaphorical computing extension of the imitative verb 'slurp' (to make a sucking noise), used to suggest 'sucking up' data.
