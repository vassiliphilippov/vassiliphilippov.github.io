diffusion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process by which social institutions, beliefs, or skills spread from one society or culture to another
spread of social institutions between societies

Examples

Anthropologists study the diffusion of religious practices from one civilization to another.
Cultural diffusion occurs when technology and customs are shared between neighboring societies.
The diffusion of democratic ideals influenced the development of new governments across continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissemination - the act of spreading something, especially ideas or information, widely

Etymology

From Latin diffusio (a pouring forth), from diffundere (to pour out, spread out).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process by which a substance moves from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration, especially as described in physics or chemistry
movement of particles from high to low concentration

Examples

In biology, diffusion plays a key role in the transport of substances between cells.
Oxygen enters the bloodstream by diffusion from the alveoli in the lungs.
The diffusion of perfume in a room demonstrates how molecules spread out over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osmosis - the movement of water across a membrane from low to high solute concentration

Etymology

From Latin 'diffusio' (a pouring forth), from 'diffundere' (to pour forth).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process or property of being spread or dispersed over a wide area, especially gradually.
state of being spread out or dispersed

Examples

As a concept, cultural diffusion explains how ideas spread between societies.
The diffusion of sunlight through the clouds gave the room a soft, even illumination.
The scent's slow diffusion made the entire house smell like fresh flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersal - the action or process of distributing things over a wide area
  • Antonym: concentration - the action or power of focusing or gathering things together

5. NOUN NEW

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
(in machine learning) a type of generative model that creates data (for example images or audio) by starting with random noise and gradually removing noise through a learned denoising process.
generative machine-learning model

Examples

Recent advances in diffusion models have produced impressive AI-generated artwork.
The research team trained a diffusion model to turn random noise into high-resolution images.
Many online tools let users generate portraits using diffusion techniques from simple text prompts.
