turmeric

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrmərɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmərɪk / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the ginger family, native to India and Southeast Asia, with aromatic yellow rhizomes used as a spice and yellow dye.
aromatic yellow spice plant

Examples

Bright yellow dye can be made from the root of turmeric.
In traditional medicine, turmeric is valued for its potential health benefits.
The chef added a pinch of turmeric to enhance the curry's color and flavor.

Etymology

From Middle English turmeryte, and Medieval Latin terra merita (meritorious earth), probably referring to the color and value of the root.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːm(ə)rɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmərɪk / (BrE)
A bright yellow spice made from the dried and ground rhizome of the turmeric plant, used especially in cooking for its flavor and color.
yellow spice from turmeric rhizome

Examples

Many Indian dishes require a pinch of turmeric for both color and flavor.
She bought organic turmeric to add to her morning smoothies for its health benefits.
The bright yellow color of mustard often comes from turmeric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curry powder - a spice mixture often containing turmeric

Etymology

From Middle English 'turmeryte', from early modern French 'turmeryte', possibly from Arabic 'curcum' (saffron).

3. ADJ NEW

/ ˈtɜrmərɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmərɪk / (BrE)
Having the warm yellow–orange colour of turmeric (the spice); used to describe shades in design, fashion, and branding.
yellow–orange colour

Examples

They painted the kitchen walls a deep turmeric to warm the small space.
She wore a turmeric dress to the exhibition and received many compliments.
The brand's logo used a muted turmeric tone that suggested warmth and approachability.
