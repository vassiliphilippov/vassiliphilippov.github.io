honeysuckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (BrE)
A climbing shrub or vine of the genus Lonicera, known for its sweetly scented, tubular flowers.
a climbing shrub or vine with fragrant flowers

Examples

Honeysuckle vines can quickly cover a trellis, creating natural shade and privacy.
She picked a sprig of honeysuckle and placed it in a vase on the windowsill.
The honeysuckle in the garden bloomed early this year, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.

Etymology

From Middle English 'hony-soukel', literally 'honey suckle', referring to the flower's sweet nectar.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌsʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪˌsʌkəl / (BrE)
a climbing shrub or vine with sweetly scented, tubular flowers and opposite leaves, often cultivated for ornamental purposes
flowering climbing shrub or vine

Examples

Bees were drawn to the honeysuckle blossoms for their nectar.
She planted a honeysuckle vine along the fence to add both shade and beauty.
The honeysuckle filled the garden with a sweet fragrance on summer evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lonicera - the botanical genus of honeysuckle plants

Etymology

From Middle English 'hony-soukil', literally 'honey-suckle', from the plant's sweet nectar.
