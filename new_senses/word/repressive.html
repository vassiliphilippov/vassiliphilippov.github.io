repressive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
restricting people's freedom or the freedom to express themselves or act
restrictive; limiting freedom

Examples

Many organizations protested the repressive laws enacted last year.
The repressive atmosphere at the school discouraged creativity.
The country suffered under a repressive government that limited free speech.

Synset Examples

a repressive regime
an overly strict and inhibitory discipline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressive - unjustly inflicting hardship and constraint
  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values; opposite of restrictive

Etymology

From French répressif, based on Latin 'reprimere' meaning 'to press back' or 'restrain'.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
controlling people or actions through the use of force, restrictions, or the limitation of freedoms
tending to suppress or restrain

Examples

Many people fled the repressive environment of their home country in search of freedom.
Strict and repressive policies often lead to unrest among the population.
The government imposed repressive measures to silence dissent and control the media.

Synset Examples

a repressive regime
an overly strict and inhibitory discipline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressive - unjustly inflicting hardship and constraint, especially on a minority or other subordinate group
  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values

Etymology

From French repressif, from Latin repressus (past participle of reprimere 'to repress').

3. ADJ NEW

/ rɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
(in biology, especially genetics and epigenetics) causing or associated with the suppression of gene expression, for example through chromatin states, histone modifications, or repressor proteins.
suppressing gene expression (biology)

Examples

The repressive chromatin state prevents transcription of nearby genes during early development.
Researchers found that specific histone modifications were repressive to gene expression in cancer cells.
Mutations in the protein complex made previously active promoters become repressive and silent.

Synset Examples

The repressive chromatin state prevents transcription of nearby genes during early development.

Mutations in the protein complex made previously active promoters become repressive and silent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silencing - the process by which gene transcription is blocked or greatly reduced
  • Antonym: permissive - in chromatin biology, allowing or promoting gene expression; opposite of repressive

Etymology

From Latin reprimere 'to press back' (via French repressif); used metaphorically in biology to mean 'suppressing transcription'.
