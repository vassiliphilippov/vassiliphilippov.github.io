orchestrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrkɪˌstreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who arranges music, especially for orchestras
person who arranges music for orchestras

Examples

As an orchestrator, she transformed the simple melody into a rich symphonic piece.
He is not only a composer but also a skilled orchestrator for many renowned orchestras.
The film's music was brought to life by a talented orchestrator who understood every instrument's role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arranger - someone who adapts or arranges music

Etymology

From 'orchestrate' + '-or', patterned after 'conductor'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8\u0254rk\u026a\u02ccstre\u026at\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0254\u02d0k\u026astre\u026at\u0259 / (BrE)
(in computing) a software system or tool that automatically deploys, schedules, manages, and scales distributed services or containers across a cluster.
software that manages deployment and scaling

Examples

The platform uses an orchestrator to coordinate microservices across the cluster.
When traffic spiked, the orchestrator automatically started more containers to handle requests.
Many teams deploy orchestrators like Kubernetes to manage scaling, updates, and service discovery.
