extractor

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstræktər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktə / (BrE)
a device or apparatus that uses centrifugal force to separate particles from a suspension, especially in industrial or laboratory settings
device that uses centrifugal force to separate particles

Examples

After harvesting honeycombs, beekeepers use an extractor to collect honey without damaging the wax.
Modern dairies often use a milk extractor to make the process more efficient.
The laboratory technician placed the blood sample in the extractor to separate the plasma from the cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrifuge - a machine that uses centrifugal force for separating substances

Etymology

From Latin 'extrahere' (to draw out) via 'extract' + '-or' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstræktɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktə / (BrE)
a tool or device used to remove tight-fitting parts or components, especially in machinery or engineering
tool for removing tight-fitting parts

Examples

A valve extractor is essential for safely taking out valves from pipes.
She purchased a screw extractor to help with stripped screw removal.
The mechanic used an extractor to remove the broken bolt from the engine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remover - a tool that removes something

Etymology

From Latin 'ex-' (out) + 'tractus' (dragged, drawn), via 'extract'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstræktər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktə / (BrE)
a mechanism in a firearm that removes an empty cartridge case from the chamber after firing, passing it to the ejector
firearm mechanism for removing cartridge cases

Examples

A faulty extractor can lead to repeated gun jams during shooting practice.
The gunsmith replaced the worn extractor to restore proper function to the firearm.
The rifle's extractor was damaged, causing the spent shell to remain stuck in the chamber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ejector - a device in a firearm that expels the empty cartridge case from the gun

Etymology

From Latin 'extrahere' meaning 'to draw out'.
