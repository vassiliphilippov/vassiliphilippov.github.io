tragicomedy

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrædʒɪˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
A literary or dramatic work combining elements of tragedy and comedy.
literary work mixing tragedy and comedy

Examples

Her new novel has been described as a tragicomedy about family life in the modern world.
Many Shakespearean works blur the line between tragedy and tragicomedy.
The play is a brilliant tragicomedy that explores love and loss with humor and pathos.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: comedy - a play or work with amusing or light content
  • Degree Higher: tragedy - a play or work with serious, somber themes

Etymology

From Italian 'tragicommedia', from Latin 'tragicomoedia', blending 'tragoedia' (tragedy) and 'comoedia' (comedy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrædʒɪˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
A play or literary work that combines elements of both tragedy and comedy, usually with tragedy more prominent.
literary work blending tragedy and comedy

Examples

Audiences often find tragicomedy relatable because it mirrors both the joys and pains of real existence.
Shakespeare’s tragicomedy ‘The Tempest’ blends elements of sorrow and humor.
The director decided to stage an original tragicomedy that reflected the complexities of modern life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farce - a comic dramatic work using buffoonery and horseplay
  • Degree Higher: tragedy - a play dealing with tragic events and having an unhappy ending
  • Degree Lower: comedy - a play intended to make the audience laugh

Etymology

From Italian *tragicommedia*, from Latin *tragicomedia* (tragedy-comedy), itself from Ancient Greek *tragōidia* (tragedy) + *komoidia* (comedy).

3. NOUN NEW

/ ˈtrædʒɪˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
An event, situation, or series of happenings in real life in which serious, tragic elements coexist with comic or absurd aspects.
real-life mix of tragic and comic elements

Examples

The protest turned into a tragicomedy of slogans and sudden downpours that made national headlines.
Watching the committee collapse under petty arguments felt like a real-life tragicomedy for everyone involved.
Their marriage, full of tender moments and ridiculous misunderstandings, became a private tragicomedy.
