F

1. NOUN

/ ɛf / (AmE), / ɛf / (BrE)
a degree unit on the Fahrenheit scale used to measure temperature
degree unit on Fahrenheit scale

Examples

He set the oven to bake at 350F for the recipe.
The weather forecast predicts a high of 85F tomorrow.
Water freezes at 32F on the Fahrenheit scale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: C - a degree unit on the Celsius scale

Etymology

From the name Fahrenheit, after German physicist Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit (1686–1736).

3. NOUN

/ ɛf / (AmE), / ɛf / (BrE)
the symbol for farad, the SI unit of electrical capacitance equal to the capacitance of a capacitor in which a charge of one coulomb produces a potential difference of one volt between the plates
symbol for farad, unit of capacitance

Examples

A typical ceramic capacitor has a value much less than 1 F.
Modern supercapacitors can reach several thousand F in capacity.
The circuit contains a 10 F capacitor for energy storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farad - SI unit of electrical capacitance

Etymology

Symbol for 'farad', named after the English scientist Michael Faraday (1791–1867).
