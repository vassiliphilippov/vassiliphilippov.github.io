Capsicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.sɪ.kəm / (AmE), / ˈkæp.sɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the nightshade family, which includes sweet and hot peppers, grown for their edible, often pungent, fruits.
genus including sweet and hot peppers

Examples

Farmers in this region grow several varieties of Capsicum for the local markets.
Scientific studies have examined the health benefits of Capsicum consumption.
The Capsicum genus includes both mild bell peppers and fiery chili peppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepper - the fruit of capsicum plants, used as a vegetable or spice

Etymology

From Latin *capsicum*, from Greek *kapto* (to bite), referring to the pungency.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæpsɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈkæpsɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical perennial shrubby plants that produce many-seeded fruits, including sweet and hot peppers.
genus of sweet and hot peppers

Examples

Botanists classify bell peppers and chili peppers in the Capsicum genus.
Many varieties of Capsicum are cultivated for their colorful and flavorful fruits.
The Capsicum genus includes both mild and extremely spicy peppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepper - the fruits of plants in the Capsicum genus, used as food or spice

Etymology

From Latin 'capsicum', the genus name, possibly from 'capsa' (box) referring to the shape of the fruit.
