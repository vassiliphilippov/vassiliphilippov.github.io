silent

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt / (BrE)
Implied or understood without being directly stated or expressed in words.
implied; not said aloud

Examples

Her parents gave their silent approval to the plan by not objecting.
Their silent consent was clear from their actions, even though nothing was discussed.
There was a silent agreement among the team members to work extra hours if needed.

Synset Examples

gave silent consent
a tacit agreement
the understood provisos of a custody agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tacit - implied or understood without being stated; unspoken

Etymology

From Latin silentem, present participle of silēre (to be quiet).
