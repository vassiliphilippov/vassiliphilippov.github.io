haematogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmətoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of blood cells in the living body, especially in the bone marrow
formation of blood cells
medical

Examples

Certain drugs can stimulate haematogenesis in patients with low blood cell counts.
Disorders of haematogenesis can lead to anemia or other blood-related conditions.
Research into haematogenesis has helped us understand how blood diseases develop.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + genesis (origin, creation).

lab production of blood cells
medical

Examples

Researchers achieved haematogenesis in vitro to generate red blood cells for transfusion trials.
The lab reported successful haematogenesis from stem cells after several weeks of culture.
A breakthrough in haematogenesis could reduce the need for donor blood in emergencies.
