depolarization

1. NOUN

/ diˌpɔlɚaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌpɔːləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of losing electrical polarity, especially in a cell membrane, resulting in a reduction or reversal of the difference in charge across the membrane.
loss of electrical polarity
medical

Examples

A rapid depolarization wave spreads across the heart to initiate a heartbeat.
During an action potential, depolarization occurs as sodium ions enter the neuron.
Muscle contraction is triggered by the depolarization of the muscle cell membrane.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repolarization - restoration of the membrane to its original polarity following depolarization

Etymology

From de- (removal) + polarization, influenced by polarize and its suffixes.
