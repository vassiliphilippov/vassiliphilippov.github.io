unlikelihood

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈlaɪklihʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪklihʊd / (BrE)
the state or quality of being unlikely to happen or be true
improbability; unlikely nature

Examples

Many doubted the team's success due to the sheer unlikelihood of winning against the champions.
She was aware of the unlikelihood that her application would be accepted, but she applied anyway.
The unlikelihood of rain convinced us to plan a picnic outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improbability - the state or quality of being improbable
  • Antonym: likelihood - the probability of something happening

Etymology

From un- (not) + likelihood (probability).
