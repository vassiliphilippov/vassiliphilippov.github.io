interpolation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɜrpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) The process of estimating the value of a function between two known values.
mathematical estimation between known values
medical

Examples

In statistics, interpolation helps fill in missing data points within a dataset.
The engineer used interpolation to estimate the temperature at a point between two measurements.
Through linear interpolation, we can determine the value of y when x is 4.5.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrapolation - estimating beyond known values in mathematics

Etymology

From Latin interpolatio (an alteration), from interpolare (to refresh, alter).

4. NOUN NEW

/ ɪnˌtɜrpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(in music) the practice of incorporating a melody, hook, or phrase from a preexisting song into a new recording by re‑performing and re‑recording it (rather than using a direct sample of the original sound recording).
musical reuse by re-recording

Examples

The artist's latest track features an interpolation of a classic chorus, replayed with a fresh arrangement.
Because the song used an interpolation, the original songwriter received credit and royalty payments.
Labels often prefer an interpolation when samples are too expensive or complex to license.
