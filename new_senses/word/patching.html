patching

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpætʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of mending a hole in a garment or fabric by sewing a patch over it
repairing fabric by sewing a patch

Examples

Patching is a practical way to extend the life of worn clothes.
He spent the afternoon patching the holes in his favorite jeans.
The tailor specializes in patching denim jackets with unique fabrics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patch - a piece of material used to cover or mend a hole in something

2. NOUN NEW

/ ˈpætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpætʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(in computing) the process of applying patches or small updates to software, systems, or devices to fix bugs, close security vulnerabilities, or add minor features.
applying software patches

Examples

The IT team announced that patching is required to fix vulnerabilities in the system.
Regular patching helped the company avoid several high-profile ransomware attacks last year.
Before the release, the developer scheduled downtime for patching the production servers overnight.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patch - a small update or fix applied to software to correct a problem or vulnerability
  • Synonym: update - a newer version of software or data that replaces or improves the previous version
  • Synonym: hotfix - a small, urgent software fix released to correct a critical problem

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of noun 'patch' (a small piece used to repair), applied to software in computing in the late 20th century.
