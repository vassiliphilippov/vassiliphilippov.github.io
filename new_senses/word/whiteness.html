whiteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally pure or innocent, without knowledge of evil or wrongdoing
moral purity or innocence

Examples

In literature, angels are often depicted with whiteness to symbolize their lack of sin.
She valued the whiteness of his character, untouched by the world's corruption.
The child's whiteness was evident in her honest and trusting nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - the state of being innocent, morally or legally
  • Antonym: guilt - the fact of having committed a specified or implied offense or crime

Etymology

From Old English hwītnes, from hwīt (“white”) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being white in color; the degree to which something resembles the color white, the achromatic color of greatest lightness
quality or state of being white in color

Examples

After several washes, the shirt lost its original whiteness and became slightly gray.
She admired the whiteness of the paint on the newly renovated walls.
The whiteness of the fresh snow made the landscape look magical.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blackness - the quality or state of being black in color
  • Degree Lower: paleness - the quality or state of being pale in color

Etymology

From Middle English whitenesse, from Old English hwītnes, from hwīt (white).

Examples

Scholars analyze whiteness as a social construct that shapes institutions and cultural norms.
