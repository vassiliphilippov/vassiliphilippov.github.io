cascara

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈkɑːrə / (AmE), / kæˈskɑːrə / (BrE)
the dried bark of the cascara buckthorn, used as a laxative
dried bark used as a laxative
medical

Examples

Many over-the-counter remedies contain cascara as an active ingredient.
Regular use of cascara should be monitored by a healthcare professional.
The herbalist recommended cascara for its gentle laxative properties.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cáscara' meaning 'bark' or 'husk'.

2. NOUN NEW

/ kæsˈkɑːrə / (AmE), / kæˈskɑːrə / (BrE)
the dried husks (skins) of coffee cherries, or a tea-like beverage brewed from those husks.
coffee cherry husks; tea-like drink

Examples

She ordered a glass of cascara, a tea-like drink brewed from coffee cherry husks.
At the café, he tried cascara for the first time and enjoyed its sweet, fruity flavor.
Local roasters sell dried cascara to brew at home like an herbal tea.
