jerking

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
an abrupt, spasmodic movement or motion, often occurring involuntarily
abrupt spasmodic movement

Examples

He could feel a jerking sensation in his leg as the muscle cramped.
Sudden jerking of the train startled the passengers.
The patient's arm began jerking uncontrollably during the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twitching - a sudden, small, involuntary muscle movement
  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction

Etymology

From the verb 'jerk', originally perhaps imitative.
