skimming

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of failing to declare income in order to avoid paying taxes on it
concealment of income for tax evasion

Examples

Auditors found evidence of skimming at multiple locations of the business.
He was charged with skimming after failing to report thousands of dollars in earnings.
The investigation uncovered a scheme involving systematic skimming by several employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax evasion - illegal nonpayment or underpayment of tax

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of reading or glancing through written material quickly to get a general idea of its content
quick reading to get the gist

Examples

Effective skimming helps students grasp the main idea of a long article without reading every word.
She practiced skimming before exams to quickly review her notes.
When pressed for time, skimming is often better than not reading at all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scanning - quickly searching for specific information in text

Etymology

From the verb 'skim', meaning to move lightly over a surface or to read quickly, dating from Middle English 'skymen'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing floating material from the surface of a liquid, often for clarification or cleaning
removal of floating material from liquid

Examples

After the milk was boiled, skimming removed the thick layer of cream from its surface.
Regular skimming is necessary in water treatment plants to remove debris from the tanks.
The cook performed skimming to ensure the soup was clear and free of fat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skim - to remove floating matter from a liquid's surface (verb)

Etymology

From the verb 'skim', from Middle English 'skymen', of Scandinavian origin.

4. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of lightly brushing against something while passing by
brushing lightly in passing

Examples

He avoided a full collision with the chair, escaping with only a slight skimming against its edge.
The bird's skimming of the water created gentle ripples across the pond.
With a quick skimming of her fingertips along the wall, she moved quietly through the dark hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grazing - the act of touching or scraping lightly in passing
  • Synonym: brushing - lightly touching in passing
  • Antonym: collision - a forceful or violent coming together

Etymology

From the verb 'skim', from Middle English 'skymen', probably of Scandinavian origin (compare Old Norse 'skima', to shine faintly, to gleam).
