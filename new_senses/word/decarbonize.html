to decarbonize

1. VERB

/ diˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈkɑːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to remove carbon deposits from (an engine or similar device)
remove carbon from an engine

Examples

After years of use, the old car needed to be decarbonized to remove built-up deposits.
It's important to decarbonize your lawn mower regularly for optimal functioning.
The mechanic had to decarbonize the engine to restore its performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to remove dirt, marks, or unwanted substances

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (removal) and 'carbon' (element name), plus '-ize'.

2. VERB NEW

/ diˈkɑrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈkɑːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to reduce or eliminate carbon dioxide or other greenhouse-gas emissions from a system, sector, process, or economy, typically through changes in technology, energy sources, or policy.
reduce greenhouse-gas emissions

Examples

The city announced plans to decarbonize its transportation sector by 2030.
Many manufacturers are trying to decarbonize production by switching to renewable electricity.
By investing in solar farms, the utility has begun to decarbonize its electricity supply.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to decarbonise - British spelling of 'decarbonize'; same meaning of lowering greenhouse-gas emissions.
