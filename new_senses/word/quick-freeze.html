quick-freeze

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɪk ˌfriz / (AmE), / ˈkwɪk ˌfriːz / (BrE)
to freeze (food) rapidly in order to preserve its natural juices, flavors, and nutritional value
freeze food rapidly to preserve quality

Examples

Chefs often quick-freeze delicate fruits for use in desserts.
Many vegetables are quick-frozen shortly after harvest to ensure freshness.
Some seafood processors quick-freeze shrimp to maintain its flavor and texture.

Synset Examples

quick-freeze the shrimp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to turn into ice or another solid as a result of extreme cold

2. NOUN NEW

/ \u02c8kw\u026ak \u02ccfriz / (AmE), / \u02c8kw\u026ak \u02ccfri\u02d0z / (BrE)
a rapid freezing process or the machine used to freeze food quickly so as to preserve texture, flavor, and nutrients
rapid freezing process or machine

Examples

The factory installed a new quick-freeze that reduced processing time and preserved delicate textures.
During shipment the strawberries underwent a rapid quick-freeze to keep their color and vitamins intact.
Research shows that the quick-freeze process locks in nutrients more effectively than gradual freezing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash-freeze - to freeze food very quickly at extremely low temperatures; a closely related technique
  • Synonym: freeze - to turn or become solid because of cold; the general process of freezing

Etymology

From the adjective 'quick' + the verb 'freeze' — a modern compound formed to denote rapid freezing.
