to sentimentize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛntəməntaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪməntaɪz / (BrE)
to act in a sentimental way or express sentimental thoughts, often excessively or self-consciously
act or express oneself sentimentally

Examples

Let's not sentimentize this occasion; we need to stay practical and focused.
She tends to sentimentize the past whenever she discusses her childhood memories.
The director was criticized for trying to sentimentize a serious social issue in the film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticize - to make something seem more appealing or romantic than it really is
  • Synonym: sentimental - characterized by feelings of tenderness or nostalgia

Etymology

Formed from 'sentiment' + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).

(in computational linguistics and data analysis) to automatically determine or assign a sentiment label (for example, positive, negative, or neutral) to a piece of text using algorithms or models.
assign sentiment labels to text (NLP)

Examples

The analytics tool can sentimentize thousands of tweets every minute for the marketing team.
They sentimentized the customer reviews to identify common complaints about delivery times.
By sentimentizing forum posts, the researcher measured public reactions to the policy change.
