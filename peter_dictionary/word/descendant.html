descendant

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who is related to someone and who lives after them, such as a child, grandchild, and so on, down the generations

Examples

Then there was North America, where American was the native speech of all except the twenty descendants of French-Canadians living on the Hudson Bay Preserve.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Synonym: offspring - a person's child or children

Etymology

From French *descendant*, present participle of *descendre* (to descend), from Latin *descendere* (to climb down).
