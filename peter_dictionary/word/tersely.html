tersely

1. ADV

/ ˈtɜrsli / (AmE), / ˈtɜːsli / (BrE)
In a brief and concise manner, often seeming abrupt or curt.

Examples

"He's there ", he got out tersely, curbing his rising excitement.

Synset Examples

a particular bird, exactly and tersely described in the book of birds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succinctly - in a clear and concise manner
  • Degree Higher: abruptly - in a sudden or curt manner

Etymology

From 'terse', which is from Latin 'tersus', past participle of 'tergere' (to polish, wipe).
