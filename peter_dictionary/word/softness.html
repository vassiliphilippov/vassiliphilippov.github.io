softness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔftnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒftnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being soft, especially in offering little resistance to pressure or being easily cut, shaped, or molded

Examples

Softness is found in crushed textures.
It was a face that had lost its childlike softness and was beginning to fold within its fragile features a harshness that belied the lyric lines of its contours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardness - the quality of being hard or difficult to press or cut

Etymology

From soft + -ness, forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being soft.
