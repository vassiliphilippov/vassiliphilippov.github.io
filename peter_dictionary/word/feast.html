feast

1. VERB

/ fiːst / (AmE), / fiːst / (BrE)
To eat and drink sumptuously, especially as part of a large, elaborate meal or banquet.

Examples

Suddenly he was interrupted in his daydreaming by a warm wetness lapping against his chin, and his eyes opened wide and long at the sight of a goat's claret tongue, feasting against the salt taste of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banquet - eat a formal and elaborate meal
  • Antonym: fast - to abstain from eating food

Etymology

From Old French *feste*, from Latin *festum* (feast, festival).

2. NOUN

/ fist / (AmE), / fiːst / (BrE)
A large and elaborate meal, often held as a celebration or in honor of a particular occasion; a ceremonial dinner party for many people.

Examples

However, this inaugural feast did its sponsors no good whatever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banquet - a formal large meal for many people, often to celebrate a particular event

Etymology

From Old French *feste*, from Latin *festum* (festival, holiday).

3. NOUN

/ fiːst / (AmE), / fiːst / (BrE)
An enjoyable or delightful experience for the senses or mind, especially something that gives great pleasure.

Examples

For so long as death was not violent, it was natural and to be welcomed, making a funeral a feast.

Synset Examples

a feast for the eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banquet - an elaborate and formal meal for many people
  • Synonym: treat - something that gives great pleasure or enjoyment

Etymology

From Old French *feste*, from Latin *festum* (feast, festival).
