foible

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔɪbəl / (BrE)
A minor weakness or eccentricity in someone's character.

Examples

The conversation of the characters creates an atmosphere suggesting the usual mixture of pleasures, foibles, irritations, and concerns which would characterize the common life of a normal village in any age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiosyncrasy - a mode of behavior or way of thought peculiar to an individual
  • Antonym: strength - a strong or positive quality or feature

Etymology

From French *foible* (weak), from Old French *feble*, from Latin *flebilis* (weak, feeble).
