Vienna

1. PROPN

/ viˈɛnə / (AmE), / viˈɛnə / (BrE)
The capital and largest city of Austria, located on the Danube River in northeastern Austria, known for its rich cultural heritage and as the home of many famous composers.

Examples

Early in 1939, Odilo Globocnik, the Gauleiter of Vienna, established SS headquarters for all of Poland.

Etymology

From the German 'Wien', the name of the city; Latin 'Vindobona', an ancient Roman settlement.
