youth

1. NOUN

/ juθ / (AmE), / juːθ / (BrE)
The early period in the development or existence of something, such as an organization, movement, or idea.

Examples

When these groups were first formed many prominent and accomplished decorators could not have had the advantage of school training since interior design courses were rare and undeveloped during their youth.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: infancy - the earliest stage in the development or existence of something
  • Degree Higher: maturity - the fully developed stage of something

Etymology

From Old English *geoguth*, related to German *Jugend* (youth).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adolescence - the period following the onset of puberty during which a young person develops from a child into an adult
  • Degree Lower: childhood - the period of life when one is a child
  • Degree Higher: maturity - the state, fact, or period of being fully grown or mature

Etymology

From Old English 'geoguþ' (the young, youth), related to German 'Jugend' and Dutch 'jeugd'.
