recrimination

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making accusations against someone who has accused you, especially as part of a mutual exchange of blame or criticism

Examples

Eternity is no time for recriminations.
Instead of emotional recrimination, loaded phrases and sloganeering, we need a dispassionate study of the facts, a better understanding of the opposite viewpoint and a more serious effort to extend the areas of agreement until a solution is reached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • Synonym: countercharge - a charge made in response to another charge

Etymology

From Medieval Latin recriminatio, from Latin recriminari (to make accusations in return).
