elute

1. VERB

/ ɪˈluːt / (AmE), / ɪˈluːt / (BrE)
to remove an adsorbed substance by washing with a solvent, especially in chromatography

Examples

The strip sections containing a given protein were pooled, eluted with 0.5 ml of isotonic saline, and the eluates were tested for antibody activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean something using water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'eluere', meaning 'to wash out'; 'e-' (out) + 'luere' (to wash).
