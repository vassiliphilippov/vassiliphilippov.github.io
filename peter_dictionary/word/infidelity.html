infidelity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfəˈdɛləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɪˈdɛlɪti / (BrE)
The act or state of being unfaithful to a partner, especially by having a sexual relationship with someone else.

Examples

It is this modulation which reveals to Jacoby his own frightful abjection and, simultaneously, his wife's infidelity.
Not once, in the time that he had known her, had he ever considered the possibility, not once, not for one one-thousandth of a second, of her infidelity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adultery - voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and someone not their spouse
  • Antonym: faithfulness - the quality of being loyal or faithful

Etymology

From Latin 'infidelitas', from 'infidelis' (unfaithful).
