advocacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈædvəˌkeɪsi / (AmE), / ˈædvəkəsi / (BrE)
public support for or recommendation of a particular cause or policy; the act of arguing for or pleading on behalf of something or someone.

Examples

Rep. Berry, an ex gambler from San Antonio, got elected on his advocacy of betting on the ponies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or backing for a person, cause, or idea
  • Antonym: opposition - resistance or dissent, in contrast to support

Etymology

From Medieval Latin advocatia (legal support), from Latin advocatus (one called to aid).
