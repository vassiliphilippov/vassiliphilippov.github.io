weather-beaten

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðɚ ˌbitn̩ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə ˌbiːtn̩ / (BrE)
Having skin or a surface tanned, roughened, or worn by exposure to the weather, especially from spending a lot of time outdoors.

Examples

With my gray hair and my weatherbeaten countenance I certainly looked the honest working stiff.

Synset Examples

a weather-beaten face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunburned - having skin red or sore from too much sun
  • Antonym: fresh-faced - having a young, healthy, and unblemished appearance

Etymology

From 'weather' (natural conditions outdoors) + 'beaten' (struck or worn by something).
