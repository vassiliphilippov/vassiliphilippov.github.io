foe

1. NOUN

/ foʊ / (AmE), / fəʊ / (BrE)
A person who is actively opposed or hostile to someone; an enemy.

Examples

In a similar vein, but writing from the opposite side, Thomas Taylor, a private in the 6 th Alabama Volunteers, in a letter to his wife, stated: "You know that my heart is with you but I never could have been satisfied to have staid at home when my country is invaded by a thievin foe By a set of cowardly Skunks whose Motto is Booty.
Friend or foe?

Synset Examples

they had been political foes for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enemy - a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone
  • Antonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

Old English 'fāh' (hostile, at feud), related to Old High German 'feind' (enemy).
