shortly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃɔrtli / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtli / (BrE)
In a short time from now; soon.

Examples

The Georgia Legislature will wind up its 1961 session Monday and head for home - where some of the highway bond money it approved will follow shortly.
Two shots apiece in the head as soon as you come out.

Synset Examples

the doctor will soon be here
the book will appear shortly
she will arrive presently
we should have news before long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soon - in a short time; before long
  • Synonym: presently - after a short time; soon
  • Degree Higher: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Degree Lower: eventually - after a period of time; in the end

Etymology

From Middle English 'shortli', from Old English 'sceortlice', meaning 'in a short manner'.

2. ADV

/ ˈʃɔrtli / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtli / (BrE)
in a short time; soon; after only a brief interval

Examples

Shortly after this study ended septic conditions resulted which required the addition of sodium nitrate.
Shortly, the doctor himself entered, his hair somewhat wet from the shower, but evidently satisfied with the outcome of their adventures.
A Federal infantryman wrote to his father shortly after his first skirmish in Virginia: "Dear Pa.
Shortly after the beginning of Sherman's Georgia campaign, an ailing Yank wrote his homefolk: "The surgeon insisted on Sending me to the hospital for treatment.
Tom Brannon had caught up with the outfit shortly after the Maguires joined it, which had been at midday.

Synset Examples

he was at the airport shortly before she was expected to arrive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soon - in a short time, quickly
  • Degree Higher: immediately - without delay; at once
  • Degree Higher: eventually - after a long time, finally
  • Synonym: presently - in a short time; soon (mainly British)

Etymology

From Middle English 'shortli', from Old English 'sceortlīce', from 'sceort' (short) + '-līce' (-ly).
