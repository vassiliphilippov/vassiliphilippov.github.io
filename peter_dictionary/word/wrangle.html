wrangle

1. VERB

/ ˈræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to quarrel or argue noisily and angrily, often in a prolonged or disruptive way

Examples

As it was, while Central wrangled among itself, Robert Tanner sneaked out of the pilots' barracks, out to the field and over to Helva's slim metal hull.

Synset Examples

The bar keeper threw them out, but they continued to wrangle on down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicker - to argue about petty and trivial matters
  • Synonym: quarrel - to have an angry argument or disagreement
  • Antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or come to a harmony

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Dutch *wrangelen*, frequentative of *wrangen* (to wring).
