turbulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.bjə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bjʊ.ləns / (BrE)
Violent or unsteady movement of air, water, or another fluid, often marked by chaotic changes in pressure and flow.

Examples

The luminosity of the water is now believed to have been caused by the stimulation of vast numbers of the luminescent organism Noctiluca miliaris by the turbulence of the sea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serenity - calm and peaceful state, especially of air or water

Etymology

From Latin 'turbulentia' (disorder), from 'turba' (crowd, confusion).
