violation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of entering another person's property without permission; an unlawful intrusion or trespass.

Examples

Since Russian was being spoken instead of Spanish, there is no violation of artistry or logic here.
Attorney General Palmer made a series of raids that sent more than 4000 so-called radicals to the jails, in direct violation of their constitutional rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespass - entry to someone's land or property without permission
  • Antonym: compliance - the act of conforming to rules or standards

Etymology

From Latin violatio (an infringement), from violare (to violate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An act that shows disrespect or irreverence for something considered sacred or important.

Examples

The charge: violation of the California Medical Practices Act by practicing medicine without a license and selling misbranded drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreverence - lack of respect, especially for things that are generally taken seriously
  • Antonym: respect - due regard for the feelings, wishes, or rights of others

Etymology

From Latin *violatio*, from *violare* (to treat with violence).

3. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an action that breaks or disregards a law, rule, agreement, or right

Examples

He claims that he was denied due process of law in violation of the Fifth Amendment, because (1) at a hearing before a hearing officer of the Department of Justice, he was not permitted to rebut statements attributed to him by the local board, and (2) at the trial, he was denied the right to have the hearing officer's report and the original report of the Federal Bureau of Investigation as to his claim.
He says that he was not permitted to rebut before the hearing officer statements attributed to him by the local board, and, further, that he was denied at trial the right to have the Department of Justice hearing officer's report and the original report of the Federal Bureau of Investigation as to his claim - all in violation of the Fifth Amendment.
Parole violation.

Synset Examples

he claimed a violation of his rights under the Fifth Amendment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breach - an act of breaking a law, agreement, or code of conduct
  • Synonym: infringement - the action of breaking the terms of a law or agreement
  • Antonym: compliance - the act of obeying a rule or law

Etymology

From Latin violatio (an injury), from violare (to harm or injure).
