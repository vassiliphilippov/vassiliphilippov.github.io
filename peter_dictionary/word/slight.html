slight

1. NOUN

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
A deliberate discourteous act or insult, often as an expression of anger or disapproval.

Examples

And it is certainly no slight to either of them to compare both their achievements and their impact.
To settle this slight, O' Banion went down to the La Salle Theatre in the Loop, where, he had learned, Dave Miller was attending the opening of a musical comedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an act or remark that is deliberately rude or offensive
  • Synonym: affront - an action or remark that causes outrage or offense
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English *slight* (trick), possibly of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse *sloegr* (cunning, crafty).
