cartilage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.t̬əl.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.tɪ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
A tough, flexible connective tissue found in various parts of the body, especially at joints, often replaced by bone as an individual matures.

Examples

Bones and cartilage transmit poorly and tend to reflect the ultrasonic signals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bone - hard, dense connective tissue; forms most of the skeleton

Etymology

From Latin 'cartilago', meaning gristle.
