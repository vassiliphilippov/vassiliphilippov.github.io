plot

1. NOUN

/ plɑt / (AmE), / plɒt / (BrE)
a small piece of land used for a particular purpose, such as growing plants, vegetables, or as a burial place

Examples

If Nikita buys a small plot in some modest rural cemetery, everyone will understand.
They held the funeral the next morning from the crossroads church and buried the little box in the quiet family plot.
You could not, of course, raise feed for the livestock on a plot this small.

It was the low yield of the Selkirk plots and the ravages of grasshoppers in 1818 that led to the dispersal of the settlement southward.

Synset Examples

a bean plot
a cabbage patch
a briar patch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patch - a small area of ground, especially one used for growing plants or vegetables

Etymology

From Old English *plot* (small area of land).

2. NOUN

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storyline - the sequence of events in a story
  • Degree Lower: subplot - a secondary or subordinate plot in a literary work

Etymology

From Old French 'complot' (combine, conspire), later specialized to story structure.
