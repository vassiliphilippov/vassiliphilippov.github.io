clomp

1. VERB

/ klɑmp / (AmE), / klɒmp / (BrE)
To walk with heavy, clumsy, and often noisy steps.

Examples

He clomped heavily up the stoop and rang the bell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clump - to walk with a heavy, noisy step
  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps
  • Antonym: tiptoe - to walk quietly on the toes
