peripheral

1. ADJ

/ pəˈrɪfɚəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated on the edge or outer boundary of something; not central or of main importance.

Examples

And in the economy of the book it is not peripheral but central.
Still, it would be surprising if what one reads did not contribute to one's ideas of right and wrong; certainly the awakened alarm over the comic books and the continuous concern over prurient literature indicate some peripheral aspects of this influence.

Synset Examples

Russia's peripheral provinces
peripheral suburbs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central - in or near the center; of primary importance
  • Synonym: marginal - relating to or at the edge; not of central importance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'peripheralis', from Greek 'periphereia' (circumference).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈrɪf.ər.əl / (AmE), / pəˈrɪf.ər.əl / (BrE)
Not central or of main importance; relating to or situated on the edge or periphery of something.

Examples

A fourteen year old girl from the Middle West observed wryly that, in her community, religion inconveniently interfered with religious activities - at least with the peripheral activities that many middle class Jews now regard as religious.

Synset Examples

a peripheral interest
energy is far from a peripheral issue in the economy
peripheral issues

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central - of the greatest importance; main or primary

Etymology

From Medieval Latin peripheralis, from Greek peripheria (circumference).
