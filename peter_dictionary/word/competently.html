competently

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑːmpətəntli / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpɪtəntli / (BrE)
In a competent or capable manner; with sufficient skill or ability.

Examples

The civil services in such societies are generally inadequate to deal competently with the problems facing them; and their members often equate a government career with security and status rather than with sacrifice, self-discipline, and competence.

Synset Examples

they worked competently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skillfully - in a skillful, expert manner
  • Antonym: incompetently - in an incompetent or unskilled manner

Etymology

From competent + -ly, formed from Latin roots: Latin 'competentem' (suitable, sufficient) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
