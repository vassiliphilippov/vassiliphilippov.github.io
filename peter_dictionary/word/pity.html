pity

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
to feel or express compassion or sorrow for the suffering or misfortune of someone or something

Examples

"Wherefore also He [ God ] drove him [ man ] out of Paradise, and removed him far from the tree of life, not because He envied him the tree of life, as some venture to assert, but because He pitied him, [ and did not desire ] that he should continue a sinner for ever, nor that the sin which surrounded him should be immortal, and evil interminable and irremediable.
Resolved that his wrongs and bereavements in Kansas, occasioned by the violence and brutality of those who were intent on the propagation of slavery in that territory, call for a charitable judgment upon his recent efforts in Virginia to undermine the despotism from which he had suffered, and commend his family to the special sympathy and aid of all who pity suffering and reverence justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathize - to share the feelings or sufferings of another
  • Synonym: compassion - a feeling of deep sympathy and sorrow for another's suffering
  • Antonym: disdain - to regard or treat with contempt; scorn

Etymology

From Old French *pitie*, from Latin *pietas* (piety, compassion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
A feeling of sympathy and sorrow for someone else's misfortune or suffering.

Examples

He felt the sweetness of pity flood through him, veining his very flesh.
In the pity for them his loneliness was gone.

Synset Examples

the blind are too often objects of pity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - A deep awareness of and sympathy for another's suffering
  • Synonym: sympathy - Feelings of sorrow for someone else's misfortune
  • Antonym: indifference - A lack of interest or sympathy

Etymology

From Old French pité (compassion), from Latin pietas (piety, pity, dutifulness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
A situation or event that is regrettable or unfortunate.

Examples

Yes, well, it's a pity to spoil your girlish figure - which all those kittens would do anyway - but I think when you 've raised these we 'll just have the vet fix it so there won n't be any more.

Synset Examples

it's a pity he couldn't do it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shame - A regrettable or unfortunate situation or event
  • Synonym: misfortune - Bad luck or an unlucky event

Etymology

From Old French *pité*, from Latin *pietas* (devotion, kindness).
