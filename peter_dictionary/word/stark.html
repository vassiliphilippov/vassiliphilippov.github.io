stark

1. ADJ

/ stɑrk / (AmE), / stɑːk / (BrE)
Bare and unembellished; presented in a direct, clear, and often unpleasant way, without any disguise or adornment.

Examples

Somehow, the pictures and stories of Soviet T-34 tanks on Cuban beaches and Russian Mig jet fighters strafing rebel troops has brought home to all of us the stark, blunt truth of what it means to have a Russian military base 90 miles away from home.

Synset Examples

the blunt truth
the crude facts
facing the stark reality of the deadline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - direct, without subtlety or embellishment
  • Synonym: crude - in a raw or unrefined state, lacking adornment
  • Antonym: softened - made less direct, milder or gentler

Etymology

From Old English 'stearc' (stiff, strong, severe).
