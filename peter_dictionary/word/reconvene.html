reconvene

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈviːn / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈviːn / (BrE)
to meet or come together again after a break or adjournment, especially for formal or official business

Examples

The tax bill will be up for reconsideration Wednesday in the House when the Legislature reconvenes.
He said Britain also proposed that the international truce commission should be reconvened, sent to New Delhi and from there to Laos to verify the cease-fire.

Synset Examples

The bill will be considered when the Legislature reconvenes next Fall

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: convene - to come together or assemble, usually for a formal meeting

Etymology

From re- (again) + convene (from Latin convenīre, to come together).
