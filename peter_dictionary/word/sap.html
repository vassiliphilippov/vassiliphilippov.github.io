sap

1. VERB

/ sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
to gradually weaken or destroy (someone's strength, power, or morale)

Examples

On the other hand, wifely supremacy demeans the husband, saps his self-respect, and robs him of his masculinity.

Synset Examples

exhaust one's savings
We quickly played out our strength

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplete - to use up the supply or resources; to reduce abundance
  • Synonym: weaken - to make or become less strong
  • Synonym: exhaust - to drain of resources or energy; to tire out completely
  • Antonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Old English 'sæpian' (to sap), related to 'sap' (the fluid in plants), with metaphorical extension to weakening.

2. NOUN

/ sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
A stupid or gullible person; someone who lacks good judgment.

Examples

Bunch of saps...

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking sense or judgment
  • Synonym: dupe - a person easily deceived
  • Antonym: wise - a person having good judgment

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'sap', from Old English sæp (juice, sap); sense of 'fool' possibly from 'saphead', deriving from the idea of having little brains or substance.
