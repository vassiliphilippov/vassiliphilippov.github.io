madam

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædəm / (AmE), / ˈmadəm / (BrE)
A polite or formal way of referring to or addressing a woman, especially one of refinement or authority.

Examples

The puzzled saleslady inquired, "How large is your house, Madam "?

Synset Examples

a chauffeur opened the door of the limousine for the grand lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ma'am - a contraction of madam used as a polite form of address to a woman

Etymology

From Old French 'ma dame' meaning 'my lady'.
