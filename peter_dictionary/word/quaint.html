quaint

1. ADJ

/ kweɪnt / (AmE), / kweɪnt / (BrE)
Attractively unusual or old-fashioned, especially in a pleasing or interesting way.

Examples

Publishers want books that will sell, recording studios want discs that will not seem strange to ears used to hillbilly and jazz music, grade and high schools want quaint, but moral, material.
How quaint it all seemed the next day.

Synset Examples

quaint dialect words
quaint streets of New Orleans, that most foreign of American cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picturesque - visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way

Etymology

From Old French *cointe* (cunning, clever), from Latin *cognitus* (known).
