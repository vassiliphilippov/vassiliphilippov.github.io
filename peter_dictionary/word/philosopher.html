philosopher

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfɚ / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfə / (BrE)
a person who studies or is an expert in philosophy, especially one who seeks wisdom or enlightenment by rational investigation of the nature of reality, existence, or knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinker - a person who thinks deeply and seriously
  • Synonym: sage - a wise person, often venerated for experience and judgment
  • Antonym: scientist - a person who is studying or has expert knowledge of one or more natural or physical sciences

Etymology

From Old French philosophe, from Latin philosophus, from Greek philosophos (philosopher, lover of wisdom).
