depreciation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a reduction in the value of an asset over time, especially due to wear, age, or obsolescence, often accounted for in business or taxation

Examples

Include as expenditures an allowance for depreciation of farm buildings and equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amortization - gradual reduction of a debt or cost over a period

Etymology

From Late Latin *depretiatio* (a lowering of value), from Latin *depretium* (to lower value).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a decrease in the value of an asset over time, especially due to wear and tear; the reduction in the price or value of something

Examples

If you are a life tenant, you deduct allowable depreciation and depletion.
If you are an income beneficiary of property held in trust or an heir, legatee, or devisee, you may deduct allowable depreciation and depletion, if not deductible by the estate or trust.

Synset Examples

depreciation of the dollar against the yen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appreciation - an increase in value or price

Etymology

From Late Latin depreciatio (a lessening in value), from Latin depretiare (to lower the price of).
