erratic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrætɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈrætɪk / (BrE)
not even or regular in pattern or movement; likely to change suddenly and unpredictably

Examples

The liquid phase photochemical exchange between chlorine and carbon tetrachloride was more reproducible than the thermal exchange, although still erratic.

Synset Examples

erratic behavior
fickle weather
mercurial twists of temperament
a quicksilver character, cool and willful at one moment, utterly fragile the next

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpredictable - unable to be predicted; changing unexpectedly
  • Antonym: consistent - acting or done in the same way over time; opposite of erratic

Etymology

From Latin erraticus (wandering), from errare (to wander).
