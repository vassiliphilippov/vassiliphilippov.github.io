authoritatively

1. ADV

/ əˈθɔːrət̬ɪˌveɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒrɪtətɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows confidence, authority, or command; in an authoritative way.

Examples

Once her trembling hand, with the pen grasped tight in it, was pressed against the paper the words came sharply, smoothly, as authoritatively as they would dropping from her own lips.

Synset Examples

she spoke authoritatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidently - in a self-assured, confident manner

Etymology

From 'authoritative' + '-ly'; 'authoritative' from Latin 'auctoritas' (authority).
