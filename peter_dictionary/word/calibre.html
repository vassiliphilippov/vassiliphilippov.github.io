calibre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
the level of quality or excellence of someone or something

Examples

Despite the clarity of his presentation, his idea was not of Einsteinian calibre.

Synset Examples

the quality of students has risen
an executive of low caliber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quality - a degree or standard of excellence
  • Synonym: merit - the quality of being particularly good or worthy

Etymology

From French 'calibre', from Italian 'calibro', ultimately from Arabic 'qālib' (mould, model).
