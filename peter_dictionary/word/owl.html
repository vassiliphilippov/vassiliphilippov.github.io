owl

1. NOUN

/ aʊl / (AmE), / aʊl / (BrE)
A nocturnal bird of prey with a large head, forward-facing eyes, and a hawk-like beak and claws.

Examples

"We found some owls had built a nest in the chimney, milord, but I promise you you 'll never have trouble of that sort again ".
"Mars and Venus, Bacchus and Ariadne, Jupiter and Io, Byron and the nymph of the owl's nest.
Was n't the owl her symbol "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - bird of prey with sharp beak and claws, active during the day
  • Synonym: eagle - large bird of prey with a powerful beak and keen eyesight

Etymology

Old English *ūle*, related to Middle Low German *ule*, *uie*.
