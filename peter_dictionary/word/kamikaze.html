kamikaze

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːməˈkɑːzi / (AmE), / ˌkamɪˈkazi / (BrE)
A Japanese aircraft used in World War II for suicide attacks, piloted with the intent to crash into enemy targets.

Examples

Fleischman with eight was to patrol the Leyte Gulf area, with his main task to get any kamikaze before they got to the ships.

Etymology

From Japanese 神風 (kamikaze), literally 'divine wind', originally referring to a legendary typhoon that spared Japan from Mongol invasion.
