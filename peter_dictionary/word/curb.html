curb

1. NOUN

/ kɝːb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
A raised edge or border, usually of stone or concrete, between a sidewalk and a roadway.

Examples

By Nov. 8, 1958, weakness, specifically involving the pelvic and thigh musculature, was pronounced, and a common complaint was "difficulty in stepping up on to curbs ".
The curb was a line of stone laid edgewise in the dirt and tilted this way and that by frost in the ground or the roots of trees.
When he turned into the highway that led to the outskirts of the city and then rose toward home, he had to pull over to the curb and wait for a few minutes, sucking in air and squinting and blinking his eyes to clear them of tears.
Cooper was beside his car, on the curb at the right, just standing there morosely; he did n't even look up.
Four cars were parked at the curb, and two of them were police radio cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kerb - British spelling; a raised edge between a pavement and the road

Etymology

From Old French *courbe* (curved, bent), from Latin *curvus* (bent, curved).

2. VERB

/ kɝb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
to control, restrain, or keep in check, especially by exerting authority or force

Examples

"He's there ", he got out tersely, curbing his rising excitement.
He will not curb his instinctual desires but release the energy within him that makes him feel truly and fully alive, even if it is only for this brief moment before the apocalypse of annihilation explodes on earth.

Synset Examples

suppress a nascent uprising
stamp down on littering
conquer one's desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrain - to hold back from action; keep under control
  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to forcibly; to subdue
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope; to stimulate

Etymology

From Old French *curbe* (a restraint for horses), from Latin *curvus* (bent, curved), originally referring to a horse's restraining strap.

3. VERB

/ kɝb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
to control or limit something in order to prevent it from increasing or becoming worse

Examples

Japan, since 1957, has been "voluntarily "curbing exports of textiles to the U. S..
The Japanese want to increase exports to the U. S. While they have been curbing shipments, they have watched Hong Kong step in and capture an expanding share of the big U. S. market.

Synset Examples

moderate your alcohol intake
hold your tongue
hold your temper
control your anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrain - to hold back from action; keep under control
  • Synonym: check - to stop or slow down the progress of something
  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over; dominate
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to; to stimulate

Etymology

From Old French *courbe* (curb, restraint) ultimately from Latin *curvus* (bent, curved), originally referring to a curved bit for a horse.
