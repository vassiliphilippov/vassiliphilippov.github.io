carriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkarɪdʒ / (BrE)
a separate, enclosed vehicle on a train for carrying passengers; a passenger car on a railway

Examples

And that was why, on the day of the performance, when a carriage from the royal stables called to take him to the palace, he did not bother to shave.
A carriage crunched by, its dim lights filtering through the gloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passenger car - a railcar for carrying passengers, used especially in American English

Etymology

From Old Northern French *cariage* (means of conveyance), from *carier* (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a vehicle with wheels, usually covered, that is pulled by horses and used for carrying people

Examples

The road seemed animated with a few more wagons than usual; a carriage raising up the choking June dust, and beyond, in a meadow, a local militia company drilling with muskets, Kentuck' rifles, every kind of horse pistol, old sword, or cutlass.
Opposite every gate was a hitching post or a stone carriage step, set with a rusty iron ring for tying a horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - a large, usually closed, four-wheeled carriage pulled by horses

Etymology

From Old Northern French *cariage* (act of carrying in a vehicle), from *carier* (to carry).
