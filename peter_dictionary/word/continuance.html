continuance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əns / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əns / (BrE)
the act or process of continuing an activity or state without interruption

Examples

That is in the continuance of the "grandfather clauses "in their membership requirements.
One is the primary purpose for which marriage exists, namely, the continuance of the race through the gift and heritage of children; the other is the paramount importance in married life of deliberate and thoughtful self-control ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuation - the process of carrying on or resuming after an interruption
  • Antonym: cessation - the process of ending or being brought to an end

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'continuance', from Latin 'continuare' (to make continuous).
