peculiarity

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌkjulˈɛrəti / (AmE), / pɪˌkjʊˈlærɪti / (BrE)
A feature or quality that makes someone or something different from others; a distinguishing trait.

Examples

Theoretical studies of the hydrogen bond generally agree that the f bond will be linear in the absence of peculiarities** of packing in the solid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristic - a feature or quality typical of a person or thing
  • Synonym: feature - an important or noticeable part or quality
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being usual or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'peculiaris' (of private property), from 'peculium' (private property).

2. NOUN

An unusual or distinctive feature or characteristic, especially one that is odd or unexpected.

Examples

  • Antonym: normality - state of being normal, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'peculiaris' (of private property), via French 'peculiarité'.
