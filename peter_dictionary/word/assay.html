assay

1. VERB

/ əˈseɪ / (AmE), / əˈseɪ / (BrE)
to analyze or test the components or quality of a substance, especially in chemistry or metallurgy

Examples

Brown (1959) has reviewed generally the various methods of assaying TSH, and the reader is referred to her paper for further information on the subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analyze - to examine methodically, often for chemical or scientific purposes
  • Synonym: test - to subject to testing; to examine for quality or composition

Etymology

From Old French 'assai', from Latin 'exagium' (a weighing or trial).

2. NOUN

/ əˈseɪ / (AmE), / əˈseɪ / (BrE)
a test or analysis, especially of a substance to determine its components or quality, most commonly used for metals and minerals

Examples

Payments shall be made only with respect to the metal content as determined by assay.

Synset Examples

they made an assay of the contents
a check on its dependability under stress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of elements or structure
  • Synonym: test - a procedure intended to establish quality or performance

Etymology

From Old French *assai* or *assayer*, from Late Latin *exagium* (a weighing), from Latin *exagium* (balance, weighing).
