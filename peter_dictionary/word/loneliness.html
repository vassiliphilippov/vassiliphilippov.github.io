loneliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnlinəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊnlinəs / (BrE)
A feeling of sadness or distress caused by being alone or abandoned.

Examples

The Secretary did not tell him at what cost, at what loneliness, he learned these things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, often without negative emotion
  • Antonym: companionship - the state of having company or friendship
  • Synonym: isolation - the state of being isolated or separated from others

Etymology

From 'lonely' + suffix '-ness'. 'Lonely' derives from 'alone' with influence from Middle English 'al one' (all one).

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnlinəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊnlinɪs / (BrE)
The state of being alone and unhappy about it; a feeling of sadness because one has no friends or company.

Examples

Loneliness tore through him like a physical pain whenever he thought of Peter Robert, Nerien, Nicholas Cop, Martin Bucer, and even the compromising Louis du Tillet.
Or was he now taking the role - the gesture and the suffering - because it was the only way to affirm his history and identity in the torpid, befogged loneliness of this land.
In the pity for them his loneliness was gone.
She would rather live in danger than die of loneliness and boredom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, often by choice; isolation
  • Antonym: company - the state of being with other people; companionship

Etymology

From 'lonely' + '-ness', with 'lonely' developing from 'lone', Middle English form of 'alone'.
