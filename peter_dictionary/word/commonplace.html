commonplace

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːmənˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌpleɪs / (BrE)
not interesting, exciting, or unusual; dull and lacking in novelty or challenge

Examples

In this article we will concentrate on the advances in the application of electronics in bio-medical research laboratories because this is where tomorrow's commonplace equipment originates.

Synset Examples

an unglamorous job greasing engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or unusual
  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Antonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable

Etymology

From common + place, originally meaning a passage or theme for general use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmənˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌpleɪs / (BrE)
A remark or statement that is dull, unoriginal, and has been made many times before; a trite or obvious observation.

Examples

As he informs Watson, "My life is spent in one long effort to escape from the commonplaces of existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful

Etymology

Late Middle English, from common + place, originally denoting a passage in a book marked for reference; later meaning a trite saying.
