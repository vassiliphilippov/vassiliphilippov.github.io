retort

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɔːt / (BrE)
to reply sharply or angrily to a remark, often in a quick or witty manner

Examples

"That will have to be in the hotel ", the doctor retorted, confirming Alex's anticipations.
"Old Crip was n't ", retorted Cobb tartly.
"All you have to do is ask ", he retorted hopefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejoin - to reply, especially to a reply; respond
  • Synonym: reply - to say or write something as an answer; respond
  • Synonym: retort - a sharp, witty, or angry reply

Etymology

From Latin 'retortus', past participle of 'retorquere' (to twist back).
