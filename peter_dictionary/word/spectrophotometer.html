spectrophotometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛktroʊfəˈtɑmətər / (AmE), / ˌspɛktrəʊfəˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument that measures and compares the intensity of light at different wavelengths, typically used to analyze the spectral properties of substances.

Examples

These were read at 280 m | m in a Beckman model DU spectrophotometer and tested for antibody activity as described above.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorimeter - device for measuring the concentration of substances by color intensity

Etymology

From spectrum (Latin 'image, apparition') + photometer ('light meter').
