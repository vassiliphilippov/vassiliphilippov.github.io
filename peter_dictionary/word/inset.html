inset

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌsɛt / (BrE)
A small picture or map that is inserted within the bounds of a larger one, often to show detail or supplementary information.

Examples

(For details see inset, fig. 5.)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - something inserted or placed within something else

Etymology

From the verb 'inset', meaning to set in or insert; based on 'in' + 'set'.
