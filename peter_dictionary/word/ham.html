ham

1. NOUN

/ hæm / (AmE), / hæm / (BrE)
meat from the upper part of a pig's hind leg, usually cured or smoked and often eaten cold

Examples

She took Glendora to the smokehouse, unlocked it and saw with satisfaction there was still a quantity of hams and sides of bacon, hanging from the smoke stained rafters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacon - cured meat from the back or sides of a pig

Etymology

Old English *ham* (back of the knee, leg of pork), of Germanic origin.
