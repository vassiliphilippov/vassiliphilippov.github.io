chambermaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbərˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəmeɪd / (BrE)
A woman whose job is to clean and take care of bedrooms, especially in a hotel.

Examples

The clocks struck seven-thirty as they approached the hotel entrance; and hopes that the chambermaid and patronne would still be abed began to rise in Alex's well exercised breast.
Although a look of alarm passed over his face, he did not arrest his movements but disappeared into the shower room just as the chambermaid emerged from number nine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housekeeper - a person, often in a supervisory role, who manages a household or hotel staff
  • Synonym: maid - a female domestic servant

Etymology

From 'chamber' (room) + 'maid' (female servant), dating from the 16th century.
