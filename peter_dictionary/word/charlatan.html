charlatan

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑrlətən / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːlətən / (BrE)
A person who falsely claims to have special knowledge or skill; a fraud or impostor, especially one who tricks or deceives others, often in a flamboyant manner.

Examples

That's why the FDA, the American Medical Association (AMA), and the National Better Business Bureau (BBB) have estimated the toll of mechanical quackery to be a substantial portion of the $ 610 million or so paid to medical charlatans annually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quack - fraudulent medical practitioner
  • Synonym: impostor - person who pretends to be someone else to deceive
  • Synonym: fraud - person who deceives others, typically for personal gain

Etymology

From Italian 'ciarlatano', probably from 'ciarlare' (to babble).
