indubitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈduːbɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːbɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Impossible to doubt; unquestionable or certain.

Examples

An elephant or a fox or a swan or a cocopalm or a banana possess in unusually high degree this quality of obvious, common-sense, indubitable identity, as do an eye or tooth or nail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unquestionable - not able to be doubted; certain
  • Antonym: doubtful - not certain; open to question

Etymology

From Latin 'indubitabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'dubitabilis' (doubtful).
