affront

1. VERB

/ əˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / əˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
To insult or treat someone with deliberate disrespect.

Examples

If, however, it would help to intensify your anguish, I can delimit the powers of a few of the divinities you 've affronted and describe the punishment they meted out in one analogous instance.

Synset Examples

He insulted her with his rude remarks
the student who had betrayed his classmate was dissed by everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak or treat with disrespect; to offend someone

Etymology

From Old French 'afronter' (to face, confront), from Latin 'ad frontem' (to the face).
