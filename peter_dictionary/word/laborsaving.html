laborsaving

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪbərˌseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbəˌseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a device or method) designed to reduce or eliminate the amount of human labor required, especially manual work.

Examples

It may not be profitable for a part-time farmer to own the labor-saving machinery that a full-time farmer can invest in profitably.

Synset Examples

laborsaving devices like washing machines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - operating by itself with minimal human intervention
  • Antonym: manual - requiring human effort rather than machinery

Etymology

From 'labor' (work) + 'saving' (reducing use).
