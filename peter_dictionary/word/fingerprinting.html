fingerprinting

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of taking inked or digital impressions of a person's fingerprints, typically for identification.

Examples

The cops would gather up Connor and the foursome on the third floor and bring us those of them who would voluntarily submit to fingerprinting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dactyloscopy - the scientific study and comparison of fingerprints for identification

Etymology

From 'fingerprint', first recorded in 1891, from 'finger' + 'print'; 'fingerprinting' as a process became common in the 20th century.
