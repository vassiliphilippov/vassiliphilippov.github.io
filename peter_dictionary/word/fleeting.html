fleeting

1. ADJ

/ ˈfliːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfliːtɪŋ / (BrE)
Lasting for only a very short time; passing quickly.

Examples

Jubal did not hear of Digby's disappearance when it was announced, and, when he did, while he had a fleeting suspicion, he dismissed it; if Mike had had a finger in it, he had gotten away with it - and what happened to supreme bishops worried Jubal not at all as long as he was n't bothered.

Synset Examples

a fleeting glance
fugitive hours
rapid momentaneous association of things that meet and pass
a momentary glimpse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentary - lasting for a very short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short period

Etymology

From Middle English *fleten* (to float, drift), from Old English *flēotan*.
