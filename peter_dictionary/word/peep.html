peep

1. VERB

/ pip / (AmE), / piÀêp / (BrE)
to look quickly and secretly at something, especially through a small opening or from a hidden place

Examples

They lay, with the birds hopping from branch to branch above them and the bright sky peeping down at them.

Synset Examples

He peeped at the woman through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peek - to look quickly, typically in a secretive way
  • Degree Lower: glance - to take a brief or hurried look
  • Degree Higher: stare - to look fixedly or vacantly at someone or something

Etymology

From Middle English 'pepen' (to chirp, peek), of imitative origin.
