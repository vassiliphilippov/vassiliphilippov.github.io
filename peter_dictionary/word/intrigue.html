intrigue

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtriɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡ / (BrE)
A secret or cunning plan, often complicated and typically intended to achieve a particular (usually dishonest or sinister) outcome.

Examples

And in the new "King of Kings "the plot involves intrigues and twists and turns that cannot be traced to the Gospels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan or scheme to accomplish some purpose
  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful

Etymology

From French 'intrigue', from Italian 'intriga', from 'intrigare' (to entangle).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtriɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡ / (BrE)
to arouse the interest or curiosity of (someone); to fascinate

Examples

She could see that Mr. Gorboduc was intrigued; the hostess in her took over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascinate - to attract and hold the attention of someone by being interesting or charming
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or tired by being dull

Etymology

From French 'intriguer', from Italian 'intricare' (to entangle).
