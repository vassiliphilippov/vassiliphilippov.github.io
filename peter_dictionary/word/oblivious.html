oblivious

1. ADJ

/ əˈblɪviəs / (AmE), / əˈblɪviəs / (BrE)
Not aware of or not concerned about what is happening around one; lacking conscious awareness of something, especially when followed by 'to' or 'of'.

Examples

He was oblivious of the form of the object actually being viewed, precisely because he could not assign it to a visual shape, already learned and held in visual memory, as persons of normal vision do.

Synset Examples

oblivious of the mounting pressures for political reform
oblivious to the risks she ran
not unmindful of the heavy responsibility

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Synonym: unaware - not aware; not realizing or conscious

Etymology

From Latin *obliviosus* (forgetful), from *oblivio* (forgetfulness).
