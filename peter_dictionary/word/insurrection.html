insurrection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
A violent uprising against an authority or government.

Examples

On Wednesday evening, November 23, 1859, in Warren, Rev. Mark Trafton of New Bedford, gave a "Mission of Sympathy "lecture in which he favorably viewed the Harper's Ferry insurrection.
Following Day was Woodbury who spoke of his disapproval of Brown's attempt at servile insurrection, his admiration of Brown's character, and his opposition to slavery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - an act of armed resistance to an established government or leader
  • Synonym: revolt - an attempt to overthrow authority by force
  • Degree Higher: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order in favor of a new system

Etymology

From Latin 'insurrectio' (a rising up), from 'insurgere' (to rise up).
