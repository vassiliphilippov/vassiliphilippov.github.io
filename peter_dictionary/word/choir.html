choir

1. NOUN

/ kwaɪɚ / (AmE), / kwaɪə / (BrE)
A group of singers who perform together, especially as part of a religious service in a church or cathedral.

Examples

His being and His will - Stevie could not divide God from his Papa - illumined every parish face, turned the choir into a band of angels, and the pulpit into the tollgate to Heaven.
Still, he did like music making and even sang in the chapel choir of the Woodberry Forest School, near Orange, Virginia, where he sounded fine but did not matriculate too well.
[PIANO MUSIC PLAYING] [CHOIR SINGING] PHOTOGRAPHER: The plaque.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorus - a group of singers or the refrain of a song

Etymology

From Old French 'quer', from Latin 'chorus' (a dance in a circle, company of dancers or singers).
