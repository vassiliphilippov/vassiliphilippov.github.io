restriction

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (BrE)
an official rule or act that limits or controls what people can do or what is allowed

Examples

Later, chlorothiazide and salt restriction became necessary to control the edema of chronic congestive failure.
And we can thank our friends in the Cuban government which has put up half the cash with the Teamsters, on a dollar for dollar basis and has relaxed restrictions on imports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - the act of limiting something or the state of being limited
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants
  • Synonym: constraint - a limitation or restriction

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictio', from 'restringere' meaning to bind fast or limit.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (BrE)
a rule or principle that limits or controls what is possible or allowed

Examples

Here, for the case of squares inscribed in plane curves, we remove the restriction to convexity and give certain other results.
In the vulnerable areas of the Pacific there should be restrictions against building homes on exposed coasts, or at least a requirement that they be either raised off the ground or anchored strongly against waves.
Similar restrictions on the strength of the Army National Guard contained in the 1960 Department of Defense Appropriation Act should likewise be dropped.
Administrative restrictions necessitated the smaller sample size in the structured schools.
It is true that, initially, the task was to remove restrictions that, it was thought, inhibited the free flow of money, goods, and labor; but even laissez-faire was a conscious policy.

Synset Examples

I am willing to accept certain restrictions on my movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - something that limits or restricts the extent, amount, or scope
  • Antonym: freedom - the absence of constraints or restrictions

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictio(n-)' meaning 'a binding back, limitation', from 'restringere' (to bind back).
