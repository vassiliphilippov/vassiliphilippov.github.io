cheerfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪə.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being noticeably happy and optimistic.

Examples

Then more than ever before did they show their fortitude and patient cheerfulness.

Synset Examples

flowers added a note of cheerfulness to the drab room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future
  • Antonym: gloominess - the state of being sad or lacking cheerfulness

Etymology

From 'cheerful' + '-ness'. 'Cheerful' from Middle English 'cherful', from Old French 'chiere' (face, mood) + '-ful'.
