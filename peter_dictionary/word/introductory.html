introductory

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.təri / (BrE)
serving as an introduction; intended to begin or lead into something

Examples

It was observed in the introductory chapter that metropolitan life had split into two trends - expanding interdependence on an impersonal basis and growing exclusiveness in local communal groupings.
Dr. A. V. Astin, NBS Director, opened the 5 - day session with introductory remarks, following which a total of twenty-six papers were given throughout the week by NBS scientists, from both the Washington and Boulder Laboratories.

Synset Examples

began the slide show with some introductory remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preliminary - serving as a preparation or introduction to something more important
  • Antonym: concluding - serving to bring something to an end or close

Etymology

From Latin introductorius (serving to introduce), from introductus, past participle of introducere (to introduce).
