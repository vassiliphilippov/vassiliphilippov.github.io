coolant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːlənt / (AmE), / ˈkuːlənt / (BrE)
A fluid, such as a gas or liquid, used to absorb and transfer heat away from a system, especially in engines or industrial processes.

Examples

In this design the anode holder is water cooled and the heat losses by conduction from the anode were determined by measuring the temperature rise of the coolant.
Temperatures of the shield and of the surface of the water cooled anode holder were measured by thermocouples to account for heat received by the coolant but not originating from the anode plug.

Synset Examples

he added more coolant to the car's radiator
the atomic reactor used a gas coolant
lathe operators use an emulsion of oil and water as a coolant for the cutting tool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrigerant - a substance, usually a fluid, used for heat transfer in refrigeration systems

Etymology

Formed from 'cool' + '-ant' (agent noun suffix), patterned after 'lubricant'.
