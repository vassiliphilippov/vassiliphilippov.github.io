culprit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.prɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.prɪt / (BrE)
A person who is responsible for a crime or other wrongdoing.

Examples

He glowed with anticipation about what would happen to the culprits when they caught them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - person who has committed an offence
  • Synonym: perpetrator - person who commits a crime or wrongdoing
  • Antonym: victim - person harmed or affected by crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman legal phrase 'culprit', meaning 'guilty: ready' (from Latin 'culpa' meaning 'fault' and 'prêt' meaning 'ready').
