misuse

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈjus / (AmE), / mɪsˈjuːz / (BrE)
the improper or excessive use of something

Examples

He is a trustee for the common good, however feeble the safeguards which the positive or municipal law of property provides against his misuse of that share of the common fund, wisely or unwisely, entrusted to his keeping.

Synset Examples

alcohol abuse
the abuse of public funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - wrong or improper use, especially to a serious degree

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + use, influenced by French *mésuser*.

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈjus / (AmE), / mɪsˈjuːz / (BrE)
to use something in the wrong way or for the wrong purpose

Examples

Religion, or the lack of it, will decide whether we use this power to build a brave new world of peace and abundance for all mankind, or whether we misuse this power to leave a world utterly destroyed.
But we must know if this is in accordance with your customs, and must have your agreement they will not misuse the power we put in their hands, to our hurt ".

Synset Examples

The words are misapplied in this context
You are misapplying the name of this religious group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to use something for a bad purpose or in a harmful way
  • Antonym: use - to put something into service or apply for a purpose

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'use'.
