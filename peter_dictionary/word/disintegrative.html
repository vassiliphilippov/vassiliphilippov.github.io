disintegrative

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈɪntɪɡrətɪv / (BrE)
Tending to cause the separation or breakup of something into its constituent parts.

Examples

The breakdown of classical structures of meaning in all realms of western culture has given rise to several generations of artists who have documented the disintegrative processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrative - relating to combining or uniting parts into a whole

Etymology

Formed from 'disintegrate' + '-ive', patterned after similar adjectives indicating causation.
