plenty

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛnti / (AmE), / ˈplɛnti / (BrE)
A large or sufficient amount or quantity; more than enough.

Examples

There is plenty of opportunity for proper education today.
He's getting plenty of rest ".
Plenty of room there now.
"We 've got plenty of time to think about that.
She's got plenty of guts, Mr. Paxton.

Synset Examples

there was plenty of food for everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - a very small supply; the state of being scarce

Etymology

From Old French 'plente', from Latin 'plenitas' (fullness), from 'plenus' (full).
