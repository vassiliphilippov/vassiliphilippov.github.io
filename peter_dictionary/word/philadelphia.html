Philadelphia

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɪləˈdɛlfiə / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈdɛlfiə / (BrE)
A large city in southeastern Pennsylvania, situated on the Delaware River; historically notable as the site where the Declaration of Independence and the US Constitution were signed.

Etymology

From Greek *philadelphos* meaning 'brother-loving', after the city in Asia Minor; adopted as the city's name in 1682.
