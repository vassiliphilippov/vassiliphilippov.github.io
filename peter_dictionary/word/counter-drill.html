counter-drill

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntər drɪl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə drɪl / (BrE)
To drill in the opposite direction, especially to enlarge or finish a hole started from the other side.

Examples

Drill all No. 50 and counter-drill all except the "A "pieces size 43.

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against, opposite) and 'drill' (to make a hole).
