batch

1. NOUN

/ bætʃ / (AmE), / bætʃ / (BrE)
A quantity of bread or baked goods baked at the same time.

Examples

He was readying a batch of sourdough biscuits for the Dutch oven.

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *bache* (a quantity of bread baked at one time), from Old English *bacan* (to bake).
