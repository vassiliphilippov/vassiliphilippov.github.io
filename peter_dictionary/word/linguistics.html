linguistics

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of language and its structure, including the study of grammar, syntax, and phonetics

Examples

Even beginning students in linguistics are made familiar with an appreciable variety of consonant systems, both in their general outlines and in many specific details.
But it is worth pondering that very little has been published on any phase of field techniques in linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philology - the study of language in historical and literary texts, especially history and development

Etymology

From linguist (specialist in language) + -ics, modeled after physics or mathematics.
