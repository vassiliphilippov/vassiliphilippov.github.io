rudder

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌdər / (AmE), / ˈrʌdə / (BrE)
a hinged vertical control surface at the tail of an aircraft, used to steer by changing the aircraft’s horizontal direction

Examples

He pushed stick and rudder and entered the overcast on his back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aileron - a hinged flight control surface on the wing of an aircraft, used to control roll

Etymology

From Old English 'rother' (steering oar), related to Dutch 'roer' and German 'Ruder.'
