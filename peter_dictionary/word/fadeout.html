fadeout

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdˌaʊt / (BrE)
A gradual or slow disappearance, especially of an image or sound.

Examples

But, in denying any physical reality for this dream, how could the brain possibly compose that realistic, vividly visual continuity uninterrupted by misty fadeout, violent break or sudden substitution?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to gradually disappear or become less distinct
  • Synonym: disappearance - the act of ceasing to be visible or present

Etymology

From 'fade' + 'out', originally from film and audio production terminology.
