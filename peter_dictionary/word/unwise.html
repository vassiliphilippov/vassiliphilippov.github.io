unwise

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwaɪz / (AmE), / ʌnˈwaɪz / (BrE)
not showing good judgment or sense; lacking wisdom; imprudent

Examples

Fire District No. 1 discussed its possible purchase in 1945, but considered it an unwise investment.
It would have been unwise policy, for instance, to apply the pound of flesh characterization to the thrifty Scotchman.

Synset Examples

an unwise investor is soon impoverished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: wise - showing good judgment or sense

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + wise (showing good judgment or sense).
