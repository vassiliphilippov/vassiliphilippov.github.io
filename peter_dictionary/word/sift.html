sift

1. VERB

/ sɪft / (AmE), / sɪft / (BrE)
to separate and remove coarse particles by passing a substance through a sieve or strainer

Examples

Frequently we are given assurance that automatically all ideas will be sifted and resifted and in the end only the good ones will survive.

Synset Examples

sift the flour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - to separate solid matter from a liquid or finer particles by filtering

Etymology

From Old English *siftan*, related to 'sieve'.
