virulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪr.jə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.jʊ.ləns / (BrE)
the severity or harmfulness of a disease or microorganism; the degree to which something is able to cause disease

Examples

It is like a mysterious epidemic which, starting first with Abigail and Parris, spreads inexorably with a dreadfully growing virulence through the whole town until all have been infected by it.
An agent would likely be selected which would possess sufficient viability and virulence stability to meet realistic minimal logistic requirements.

Synset Examples

the virulence of the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathogenicity - the ability of an organism to cause disease

Etymology

From Latin virulentia (poisonous condition), from virulentus (full of poison).
