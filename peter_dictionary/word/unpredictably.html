unpredictably

1. ADV

/ ʌn.prɪˈdɪktəbli / (AmE), / ʌn.prɪˈdɪktəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be predicted or foreseen; in an erratic or uncertain manner.

Examples

Thomas' findings are, first, "that the individual items vary greatly and unpredictably in their persistence "; but, second, "that the semantic groups are surprisingly unvarying in their average persistence " (as between M-K and I-E.

Synset Examples

economic changes are proceeding erratically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predictably - in a manner that can be predicted

Etymology

From 'unpredictable' + '-ly', with 'unpredictable' from un- (not) + predictable.
