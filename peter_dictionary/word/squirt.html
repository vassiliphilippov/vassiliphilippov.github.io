squirt

1. VERB

/ skwɝt / (AmE), / skwɜːt / (BrE)
to force a liquid to come out in a thin, fast stream, often through a narrow opening

Examples

With his mouth opened wide, he squirted the warm white milk against the roof of his mouth and his tongue savored the light, earthy taste of it.

Synset Examples

the boy squirted water at his little sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to send out liquid in small drops over a wide area

Etymology

Imitative origin, reflecting the sound of liquid being forced out.
