depth

1. NOUN

/ dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
The distance from the top or surface to the bottom of something, or the extent inward, backward, or downward.

Examples

Synset Examples

the depth of the water
depth of a shelf
depth of a closet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: height - distance from base to top; measurement upward
  • Antonym: width - measurement from side to side; breadth
  • Antonym: shallowness - the quality of having little depth

Etymology

From Old English 'dēop' (deep), with suffix '-th' forming a noun.
