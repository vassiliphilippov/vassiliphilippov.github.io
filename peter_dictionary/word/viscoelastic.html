viscoelastic

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɪskoʊɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌvɪskəʊɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
Having both viscous and elastic properties; able to exhibit characteristics of both a fluid and a solid when deforming under stress.

Examples

These are fluids which one would expect to be less viscoelastic or more Newtonian because of their lower molecular weight.
There is another means which should show the direction and relative value of the stresses in viscoelastic fluids that is not mentioned as such in the literature, and that is the shape of the suspended drops of low viscosity fluids in shear fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elastic - able to resume original shape after stretching or compression
  • Synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency like syrup

Etymology

From 'viscous' (from Latin viscum 'birdlime') + 'elastic' (from Greek elaustos 'ductile').
