secessionist

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɛʃənɪst / (AmE), / sɪˈsɛʃənɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates the withdrawal of a group from a larger political entity, especially a state or nation.

Examples

We got to one house where there were five secessionist they brok + run and Arch holored out to shoot the ornery suns of biches and we all let go at them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unionist - a person who supports the unity of a state or nation, especially in opposition to secession

Etymology

From 'secession' + '-ist'; 'secession' from Latin 'secessio' (a withdrawal).
