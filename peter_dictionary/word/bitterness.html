bitterness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənəs / (BrE)
A feeling of deep anger, resentment, and ill-will.

Examples

Oh, the irony and the bitterness of it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentment - a feeling of persistent ill will
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the act of letting go of anger or resentment

Etymology

From Old English 'biter' (sharp, biting) + '-ness', meaning the quality or state of being bitter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənəs / (BrE)
A harsh or resentful manner, attitude, or tone of voice, often showing anger or disappointment.

Examples

It's your decision ", said Juanita, holding her face very still, trying to contain the bitterness of her voice as she enunciated her words too distinctly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentment - a feeling of anger or displeasure about someone or something unfair
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of being pleasant or gentle in manner or tone

Etymology

From Old English 'biter' (biting, sharp) + suffix '-ness'.
