resignation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A formal written statement declaring that one is leaving a job or position.

Examples

Caldwell's resignation had been expected for some time.

Synset Examples

he submitted his resignation as of next month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letter of resignation - a written document officially stating that someone leaves a job or position

Etymology

From Latin resignatio (a surrender), from resignare (to unseal, annul, resign).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable, often without protest or visible emotion.

Examples

Mrs. Pastern undertook the work of going from house to house with the thoughtless resignation of an honest and traditional laborer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptance - willing acceptance of a difficult situation
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance; opposite of acceptance without protest

Etymology

From Latin resignatio (the act of giving up, surrender).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɪgˈneɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪgˈneɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The act of formally giving up a job, position, or claim, especially by notifying the relevant authority or organization.

Examples

Interviewing, checking references, training the salesmen, having them go with more experienced salesmen was expensive - and the rate of attrition due to resignations or unsatisfactory performance was too high.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retirement - the act of leaving one's job and ceasing to work, usually due to age
  • Antonym: dismissal - the act of being removed from a position by someone else

Etymology

From Latin resignatio (a surrender, giving up), from resignare (to unseal, revoke).
