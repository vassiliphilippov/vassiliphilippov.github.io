wicked

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪkɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪd / (BrE)
Morally wrong or evil; guilty of sinful or unrighteous acts.

Examples

Because he interprets the primitive state of man as one of mere potentiality or capacity and believes that Adam and Eve were created as children, Irenaeus often seems inclined to extenuate their disobedience as being "due, no doubt, to carelessness, but still wicked ".

Synset Examples

a sinful person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinful - Full of sin; morally wrong
  • Antonym: virtuous - Having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old English *wicca* (wizard, witch), later generalized to 'evil, wrong'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪkɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪd / (BrE)
Morally wrong or evil; deliberately behaving in a way that is considered bad or harmful.

Examples

She was so beautiful with her rosy mouth and haughty air that she had to be wicked.
And the old man had given a sly and wicked laugh and said, "Hell, yes!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - morally wrong or very bad; wicked
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old English *wicca* (wizard, sorcerer) and *wicce* (witch), later associated with evil behavior.
