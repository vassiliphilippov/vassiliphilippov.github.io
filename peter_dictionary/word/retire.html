retire

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to leave one's job and stop working, usually because of having reached a certain age

Examples

Dr. Clark will succeed Dr. J. R. McLemore, who will retire at the close of the present school term.
He succeeds Buck Shaw, who retired at the end of last season.
In 1931 Mrs. F. H. Briggs, agent and chief operator, who was to retire in 1946 with thirty years' service, led agency offices in sales for the year with $ 2490.
William Hitchcock, who retired in 1938, was a veteran of thirty-four years' local service.
Leaving the theatre after the performance, I had a flash of intuition that life, after all (as Rilke said), is just a search for the nonexistent cup of hot coffee, and that this unpretentious, moving, clever, bitter slice of life was the greatest thing to happen to the American theatre since Brooks Atkinson retired.

Synset Examples

He retired at age 68

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to voluntarily leave a position or job, often before retirement age
  • Synonym: retirement - the action or fact of leaving one's job and ceasing to work
  • Antonym: work - to be engaged in employment

Etymology

From French 'retirer' (to withdraw), from re- (back) + tirer (to draw).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to withdraw from active participation, often from work, a role, or public life

Examples

Saxton has made only one second-half appearance this season and that was in the Washington State game, for four plays: he returned the kickoff 30 yards, gained five yards through the line and then uncorked a 56 - yard touchdown run before retiring to the bench.
Since Election Day, Vice President Richard Nixon had virtually retired - by his own wish - from public view.
John Vernon had had all the patronage he cared for - he had prospered, but he could not retire from horsedom.
On Fridays, the day when many Persians relax with poetry, talk, and a samovar, people do not, it is true, stream into Chehel Sotun - a pavilion and garden built by Shah Abbas 2, in the seventeenth century - but they do retire into hundreds of pavilions throughout the city and up the river valley, which are smaller, more humble copies of the former.
Retiring to his beloved Mount Vernon, he returned to preside over the Federal Convention, and was the only man in history to be unanimously elected President.

Synset Examples

He retired from chess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to voluntarily leave a job or office
  • Synonym: quit - to leave or give up a job or position

Etymology

From French 'retirer', from re- (again) + tirer (to draw), meaning to draw back.

3. VERB

/ rɪˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to withdraw from a meeting, gathering, or public activity, especially to go to a more private or quiet place

Examples

After another two weeks, the first young emerge, four to eight small daughters that begin to play the role of worker bees, collecting pollen and nectar in the field and caring for the new young generation while the queen retires to a life of egg laying.

Synset Examples

We adjourned for lunch
The men retired to the library

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjourn - to suspend or break off a meeting temporarily

Etymology

From French 'retirer' (to withdraw), from re- (back) + tirer (to draw).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
(finance) to withdraw (bills, shares, bonds, or other financial instruments) from circulation, typically by redeeming or paying them off.

Examples

Union Oil Co. of California Tuesday offered $ 120 million in debentures to the public through a group of underwriters headed by Dillon, Read + Co., to raise money to retire a similar amount held by Gulf Oil Corp..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redeem - pay off or buy back (a financial instrument)

Etymology

From French *retirer*, from re- (back) + tirer (draw). The financial sense developed by analogy with withdrawing from duty.

5. VERB

/ rəˈtaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
To make someone leave their job or position because they have reached a certain age or for other reasons; to cause to retire.

Examples

The aging chancellor in all likelihood will be retired.

Synset Examples

The director was retired after the scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a job
  • Antonym: appoint - to assign someone to a job or position

Etymology

From French *retirer*, from re- (back) + tirer (draw).

6. VERB

/ rɪˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to take something out of service or use because it is no longer needed or useful

Examples

Reports that the venerable liner, which has been in service since 1936, was to be retired struck a nostalgic note in many of us.

Synset Examples

She finally retired that old coat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer useful or desirable
  • Synonym: decommission - to officially withdraw something from service, especially machinery or vehicles
  • Antonym: retain - to keep or continue to have something

Etymology

From Latin 'retirare' (to withdraw), via French 'retirer'.
