nucleolus

1. NOUN

/ nuˈkliː.ə.ləs / (AmE), / njuːˈkliː.ə.ləs / (BrE)
A small, round structure in the nucleus of a cell, composed mainly of protein and RNA, and involved in producing ribosomes.

Examples

These were increased both in number and in size, contained prominent nucleoli, and were distributed throughout the fiber (Figs. 2 - 5).
Occasionally there were small basophilic fibers that were devoid of myofibrillae and contained many vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli (Fig. 5).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleus - central part of a cell containing genetic material

Etymology

From Latin 'nucleolus', diminutive of 'nucleus' (kernel, little nut).
