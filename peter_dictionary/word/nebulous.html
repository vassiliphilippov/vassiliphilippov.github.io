nebulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛbjələs / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊləs / (BrE)
(of ideas or concepts) lacking definite form, clarity, or limits; vague or ill-defined.

Examples

As Broadway itself becomes increasingly weighted down by trite, heavy-handed, commercially successful musicals and inspirational problem dramas, the American theatre is going through an inexorable renaissance in that nebulous area known as "off-Broadway ".

Synset Examples

gropes among cloudy issues toward a feeble conclusion
nebulous distinction between pride and conceit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin 'nebulosus' (full of mist), from 'nebula' (mist, fog, cloud).
