liberally

1. ADV

/ ˈlɪbɚəli / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəli / (BrE)
in a generous or free manner, especially in a nonliteral or exaggerated way

Examples

Rector had no doubt that Hino would come back from the village bursting with information, ready to impart it with his customary gusto, liberally embellished with his active imagnation.

Synset Examples

he embellished his stories liberally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freely - without restriction or restraint
  • Antonym: sparingly - in a restricted or limited manner

Etymology

From Latin liberalis (of freedom, generous) via Old French liberal and English liberal + -ly.
