epoxy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɑksi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒksi / (BrE)
A synthetic, thermosetting resin used mainly in strong adhesives, coatings, and laminates.

Examples

The frequency used for these experiments is 15 mc. and the transducer is a specially cut crystal with an epoxy lens capable of providing beam diameters smaller than one millimeter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epoxide - a chemical compound containing an oxygen atom bonded to two adjacent carbon atoms

Etymology

Shortened from 'epoxy resin,' from 'epoxy' (prefix indicating the presence of an epoxide group), from 'epoxide' and 'oxy-' (oxygen).
