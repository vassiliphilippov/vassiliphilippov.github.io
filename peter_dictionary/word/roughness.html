roughness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being harsh, unpleasant, or grating to the senses, especially to touch or hearing.

Examples

The voices of all the characters, including that of Cherkasov, have richness, roughness or color to conform with the personalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshness - the quality of being unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being pleasant, soft, or even to the senses

Etymology

From Middle English 'roughnesse', from 'rough' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of a surface being uneven, irregular, or not smooth

Examples

Spring used a Brush surface analyzer in a metal cleaning study and showed considerable differences in soil removal, depending upon surface roughness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth or even

Etymology

From Middle English 'roughnesse', from Old English 'ruhnes'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being unpolished, unrefined, or lacking smoothness or sophistication.

Examples

It was n't the roughness and crudity and discomfort of the trip that had frightened her.

Synset Examples

the crudeness of frontier dwellings depressed her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crudeness - the quality of being in a natural or raw state; unrefined
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even, refined, or polished

Etymology

From 'rough' + '-ness'; 'rough' from Old English 'ruh'.
