straightforward

1. ADJ

/ streɪtˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / streɪtˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
(of a person, statement, or approach) direct, honest, and without evasion or compromise.

Examples

In a series of fairy tales and fantasies, Melies demonstrated that the film is superbly equipped to tell a straightforward story, with beginning, middle and end, complications, resolutions, climaxes, and conclusions.
The paper has a certain value as a comparatively easy introduction to this approach, particularly since it treats a fairly simple and straightforward phenomenon where it is possible to compare it with a more traditional (though not structural) statement.

Synset Examples

a square contradiction
he is not being as straightforward as it appears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - straight to the point; clear and unambiguous
  • Antonym: evasive - not straightforward; tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation

Etymology

From 'straight' + 'forward', meaning moving directly ahead, extended to mean direct or frank in speech or action.

2. ADJ

/ ˌstreɪtˈfɔrˌwɚd / (AmE), / ˌstreɪtˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
clear and easy to understand; free from ambiguity or complexity

Examples

This is precisely what makes lucid, straightforward music so difficult to compose - the clarity must be new, not old ".
When Prokofieff forged his new clarity of "lucid, straightforward music, so difficult to compose ", he shaped his talents to his purpose.

Synset Examples

a straightforward set of instructions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning

Etymology

Formed from 'straight' + 'forward', literally meaning going straight ahead, later used figuratively.
