compressed

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛst / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛst / (BrE)
Reduced in volume as a result of being subjected to pressure.

Examples

Note that though the ideal gas itself contains no additional energy, the compressed gas does exert an increased pressure.

Synset Examples

compressed air

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expanded - increased in volume; made larger, especially by release of pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'comprimere' meaning 'to press together'.

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛst / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛst / (BrE)
Pressed tightly together or into a smaller space; made compact by pressure.

Examples

Monsieur Favre sat down in his high-backed stall, lips compressed, eyes glinting.

Synset Examples

with lips compressed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensed - made denser or more compact
  • Antonym: expanded - made larger or more spread out

Etymology

From Latin 'compressus', past participle of 'comprimere' (to press together).
