electrophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊfəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the movement of charged particles through a fluid or gel under the influence of an electric field, used to separate substances based on their charge and size

Examples

These include low-temperature ethanol (Cohn) fractionation, electrophoresis, ultracentrifugation and column chromatography on ion exchange celluloses.

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + Greek phorēsis (being carried, from pherein, to carry).
