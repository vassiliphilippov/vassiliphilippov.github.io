breakaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəweɪ / (BrE)
an act of breaking free or withdrawing from a group or situation, especially to form a new group or pursue independent action.

Examples

It gave them all a chance to make a high-speed climbing turn attack and a break-away that would not take them into the overcast or force a tight turn recovery.

Synset Examples

there was a breakaway by the discontented members
a breaking away from family and neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secession - the action of formally withdrawing from a group or organization

Etymology

From 'break' + 'away', indicating the action of separating or escaping.
