ease

1. VERB

/ iz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
to move or place something gently or gradually, often with care to avoid sudden movement

Examples

Warren eased his rifle's safety off and gently, slowly sneaked another clip of ammunition from one of the cloth bandoleers that marked the upper part of his body with an X.
The half-breed eased the Winchester down and rested it across his lap.
And so deadheads on the payroll can be eased out at the earliest possible age.
Ease in any extra fullness evenly around.

Synset Examples

He eased himself into the chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it
  • Antonym: push - move something forward by pressing against it

Etymology

From Old French 'aisier' (to make easy), from 'ais' (ease, comfort).

2. VERB

/ iz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
to make pain, discomfort, or a problem less severe; to alleviate

Examples

Even so, he could not ease the tension of his body; the rough surface of the earth itself seemed to resist every attempt on his part to relax.
He had stupidly thought himself compelled to ease his brother's pain.
Pleasure, fame and fortune, drowning your troubles with a drink, and "living it up "with the gang are like candy bars when you 're hungry: they may ease your hunger temporarily, but they 'll never take the place of a satisfying, mouth-watering steak.
She quickly moved into cafe society, possibly easing her conscience by talking constantly of her desire to be in show business.

Synset Examples

ease the pain in your legs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relieve - to reduce or remove pain or discomfort
  • Synonym: alleviate - to make pain or problem less severe
  • Antonym: intensify - to make pain or discomfort stronger or more severe

Etymology

From Old French aise (comfort, pleasure), from Vulgar Latin *adjacens (lying near, at ease).

3. VERB

/ iz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
to make something less difficult or more manageable; to facilitate

Examples

An agreement among the Princes on a coalition government would ease their task, diplomats conceded.
The editorial concerned legislative proposals to ease the tax burden on du Pont stockholders, in connection with the United States Supreme court ruling that du Pont must divest itself of its extensive General Motors stock holdings.
When Governor Al Smith offered New York National Guard planes to fly the mail in and out of the state, it seemed a likely temporary solution, easing Burlington's bottleneck and that at Montpelier too.

Synset Examples

you could facilitate the process by sharing your knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier
  • Antonym: hinder - to make something more difficult

Etymology

From Old French aise (comfort, convenience), of unknown origin.

4. VERB

/ iz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
to make something less severe, intense, or serious; to calm or relieve.

Examples

Moreover, an eventual meeting was desirable if for no other reason than to satisfy world opinion that the U. S. was not inflexible and was sparing no effort to ease international tensions.

Synset Examples

The news eased my conscience
still the fears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alleviate - make (pain, suffering, or a problem) less severe
  • Antonym: intensify - make or become more intense

Etymology

From Old French *aisier* (to make comfortable), from *aise* (comfort, pleasure).

5. NOUN

/ iːz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
The state of being free from difficulty, effort, or hardship; comfort or simplicity in accomplishing something.

Examples

I'm just trying to set your mind at ease, that's all.

Synset Examples

he rose through the ranks with apparent ease
they put it into containers for ease of transportation
the very easiness of the deed held her back

Related Terms

  • Antonym: difficulty - the state of being hard to do or understand
  • Synonym: effortlessness - the quality of requiring little or no effort

Etymology

From Old French 'aise' (comfort, opportunity), of uncertain origin.

6. NOUN

/ iz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
A state of comfort or relief, especially after being relieved of anxiety, pain, or discomfort.

Examples

[TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] PRESIDENT: And I want to put you all at ease.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed his relief from responsibility
getting it off his conscience gave him some ease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress
  • Antonym: discomfort - a state of slight pain or lack of comfort

Etymology

From Old French *aise* (comfort, pleasure), of uncertain origin, possibly from Latin *adjacens* (lying at, nearby).
