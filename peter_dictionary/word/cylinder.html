cylinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
a solid geometric figure with straight parallel sides and a circular or elliptical cross-section, with the two ends (bases) being parallel and of equal size

Examples

Spectra were obtained from a powdered sample having the shape of a right circular cylinder with a height to diameter ratio of 4: 1.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cone - solid geometric figure with a circular base tapering to a point
  • Degree Higher: sphere - perfectly round solid figure, every point on its surface equidistant from its center

Etymology

From Greek 'kulindros' (roller, cylinder), from 'kulindein' (to roll), via Latin 'cylinder'.
