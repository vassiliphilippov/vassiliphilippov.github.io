John

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑn / (AmE), / dʒɒn / (BrE)
the fourth book of the New Testament, one of the four Gospels, traditionally ascribed to Saint John the Apostle

Examples

John, Chapter 8, Verse 12.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gospel - one of the first four books of the New Testament; account of the life and teachings of Jesus
  • Degree Lower: Matthew - the first Gospel in the New Testament
  • Degree Lower: Mark - the second Gospel in the New Testament
  • Degree Lower: Luke - the third Gospel in the New Testament

Etymology

From the name John, ultimately from Hebrew Yochanan, meaning 'Yahweh is gracious.'
