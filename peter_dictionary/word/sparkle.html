sparkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrkəl / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkəl / (BrE)
A lively brightness or vivacity, especially seen in someone's appearance or manner; a quality of animation or cheerfulness.

Examples

Somewhat to his surprise he found that one girl, whom he would never have considered for the job since she had appeared somewhat mousy and also had been in the office a relatively short time, did the most outstanding job of playing the role of receptionist, showing wit, sparkle, and aplomb.
He was fifteen years older than I - forty four - but full of spring and sparkle.

Synset Examples

he had a sparkle in his eye
there's a perpetual twinkle in his eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinkle - a brief, bright gleam or sparkle, especially in the eyes
  • Antonym: dullness - the absence of brightness or liveliness

Etymology

From Middle English 'sparklen', a frequentative of 'spark' (Middle English 'sperke'), from Old English 'spearca'.

2. VERB

/ ˈspɑr.kəl / (AmE), / ˈspɑː.kəl / (BrE)
to be lively, brilliant, or exhibit great skill, especially in performance or conversation

Examples

For this story of an American couple who meet and fall in love in Israel, Mr. Herman has written songs that are warmly melodious and dance music that sparkles.

Synset Examples

The musical performance sparkled
A scintillating conversation
his playing coruscated throughout the concert hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scintillate - to emit flashes of light; to be brilliant or witty (figurative)
  • Synonym: coruscate - to flash or sparkle; to be brilliant (figurative)

Etymology

From Middle English 'sparklen', frequentative of 'spark'.

3. VERB

/ ˈspɑrkəl / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkəl / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) to reflect bright points of light; to shine with small flashes of light.

Examples

"You see ", she said, looking past him into the room, where the highball glasses sparkled dully in the bright light, "you and I can n't understand the many hardships they have to undergo ".

Synset Examples

Unquarried marble sparkled on the hillside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or intermittent light
  • Synonym: glitter - to shine with a bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • Antonym: dull - not reflecting light; lacking brightness or sheen

Etymology

From Middle English 'sparklen', frequentative of 'spark'.
