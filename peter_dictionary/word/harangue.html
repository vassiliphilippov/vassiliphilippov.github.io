harangue

1. VERB

/ həˈræŋ / (AmE), / həˈræŋ / (BrE)
to speak to someone in a forceful, angry, or critical way for a long time

Examples

He heard himself haranguing them.
Outside, the garden, the tame wilderness, yielded a patchwork bouquet of daisies, sweet william, scented stock and lady's bedstraw, which she tied with long grasses and took back to show Rosa, who was now stirring about the kitchen and haranguing Folly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - to criticize or scold someone at length
  • Synonym: rant - to speak in a loud or angry way, often at length

Etymology

From Italian *aringare*, from Medieval Latin *arangare*, from *harenga* (public address).
