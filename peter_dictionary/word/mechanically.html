mechanically

1. ADV

/ məˈkænɪkli / (AmE), / məˈkænɪkəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to machines or the use of mechanics; by means of a machine or mechanical process.

Examples

The dirt on the soiled objects is mechanically held by surface irregularities to some extent.

Synset Examples

this door opens mechanically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manually - by hand, using human effort rather than machines

Etymology

From mechanical + -ly, formed from Latin mechanicus (relating to machines).
