cuff

1. NOUN

/ kʌf / (AmE), / kʌf / (BrE)
The folded or banded edge at the end of a sleeve or trouser leg, often turned back or finished with a separate band.

Examples

They curled around the cuff of his nightshirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hem - the edge of a piece of cloth that is folded over and sewn

Etymology

From Middle English 'cuffe', likely from Old English or Old French (origin uncertain), referring to the end of a sleeve or glove.
