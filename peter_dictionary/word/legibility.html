legibility

1. NOUN

/ lɛdʒəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / lɛdʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear enough to read or easily perceived

Examples

Only when the collage had been exhaustively translated into oil, and transformed by this translation, did Cubism become an affair of positive color and flat, interlocking silhouettes whose legibility and placement created allusions to, if not the illusion of, unmistakable three-dimensional identities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: readability - quality of written or printed material being easy to read
  • Antonym: illegibility - state of being impossible or difficult to read

Etymology

From Late Latin legibilitas (readableness), from Latin legibilis ('that can be read').
