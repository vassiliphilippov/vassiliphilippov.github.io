perspiration

1. NOUN

/ pərˌspaɪˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌspɪəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The salty fluid secreted by sweat glands, usually as a result of heat, physical activity, or stress; sweat.

Examples

And listening to such a conversation one morning while taking a cup of chocolate in a cafe, Rousseau found himself bathed in perspiration, trembling lest his authorship become known, and at the same time dreaming of the startling effect he would make if he should proclaim himself suddenly as the composer.

Synset Examples

sweat poured off his brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweat - salty moisture exuded by sweat glands

Etymology

From French *perspiration*, from Latin *perspiratio* (a breathing through or emission of moisture).
