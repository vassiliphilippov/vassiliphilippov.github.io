audit

1. VERB

/ ˈɔdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
to examine and check the accuracy of financial accounts or records

Examples

Have you audited your program recently to weed out those phases that draw least participation?

Synset Examples

audit accounts and tax returns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspect - to examine something carefully in order to check its condition

Etymology

From Latin 'auditus', past participle of 'audire' (to hear), originally meaning an official hearing of accounts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
an official examination of financial accounts and records, typically conducted by a certified accountant

Examples

Madden inquired next about the audit of the scholarship fund.
There was an annual audit, Garth informed him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - close examination or check, especially for correctness or quality
  • Synonym: review - look over or assessment, especially of accounts or finances

Etymology

From Latin 'auditus' (a hearing), past participle of 'audire' (to hear), originally referring to oral examinations of accounts.
