wagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A vehicle with four wheels, typically used for transporting goods or people, and usually pulled by animals or a tractor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cart - a two-wheeled vehicle typically pulled by animals; similar in function but smaller than a wagon
  • Synonym: carriage - a wheeled vehicle for transporting people, especially horse-drawn
  • Antonym: sleigh - a vehicle on runners for use on snow or ice, drawn by horses, reindeer, or dogs

Etymology

From Dutch *wagen* or German *Wagen*, meaning 'vehicle'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
a van used by police to transport prisoners

Examples

Gun knew it was Car 12, the wagon, returned from delivering Ingleside's drunk-and-disorderlies to the City Jail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddy wagon - police van for transporting prisoners
  • Synonym: police van - vehicle used by police to transport prisoners or personnel

Etymology

From Middle English 'wag(e)n', from Old English 'wægn', of Germanic origin.
