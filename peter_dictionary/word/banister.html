banister

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbænɪstə / (BrE)
a railing at the side of a staircase or balcony, designed to prevent people from falling

Examples

In the half darkness the banisters gleamed, and the hall seemed enormous.
Once he went after Hans and dumped his pot over the banister.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railing - a barrier consisting of a horizontal bar and supports
  • Synonym: balustrade - a row of small columns topped by a rail, on a staircase or balcony

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'baluster', from French 'balustre', from Italian 'balaustra'.
