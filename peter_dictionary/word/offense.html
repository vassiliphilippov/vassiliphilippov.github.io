offense

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A lack of politeness; an act or remark that hurts someone's feelings or shows disrespect.

Examples

Texas' 545 - yard spree against Washington State gave the Longhorns a 3 - game total offense of 1512 yards (1065 rushing and 447 passing) a new SWC high.
Completing 12 of 15 passes for 174 yards, the Aggies had a total offense of 361 yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an action or remark that causes offense or hurt
  • Antonym: courtesy - polite behavior that shows respect for others

Etymology

From Old French 'offense', from Latin 'offensa' (a striking against, injury).

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
an act that breaks a law and is punishable by law; a crime or illegal act

Examples

Lincoln understood this better than most when he said in his "Second Inaugural "that God "gives to both North and South this terrible war, as the woe due to those by whom the offense came ".

Synset Examples

a long record of crimes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act that is against the law and punishable by a government

Etymology

From Middle English, via Old French, from Latin 'offensa' (a striking against).

3. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A feeling of anger, hurt, or resentment caused by something perceived as insulting or disrespectful.

Examples

Usually she marked the few who did thank you, you did n't get that kind much in a place like this: and she played a little game with herself, seeing how downright rude she could act to the others, before they 'd take offense, threaten to call the manager.

Synset Examples

he took offence at my question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an action or remark causing outrage or offense
  • Synonym: humiliation - a feeling of being shamed or mortified
  • Degree Higher: outrage - an intense feeling of anger and shock caused by something perceived as wrong or insulting
  • Degree Lower: annoyance - a feeling of irritation or slight anger

Etymology

From Old French 'offense', from Latin 'offensa' (a striking against, injury).
