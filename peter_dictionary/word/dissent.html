dissent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsent / (BrE)
a difference of opinion, especially one that leads to disagreement or challenge of an established or majority view

Examples

When the crowd was asked whether it wanted to wait one more term to make the race, it voted no - and there were no dissents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreement - the act of having the same opinion as others

Etymology

From Latin 'dissentire' (to differ in sentiment), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'sentire' (to feel).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
(Law) An official difference of opinion, especially a judge's statement expressing disagreement with the majority decision.

Examples

Without dissent, senators passed a bill by Sen. A. R. Schwartz of Galveston authorizing establishment in the future of a school for the mentally retarded in the Gulf Coast district.

Synset Examples

he expressed his dissent in a contrary opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concurrence - A judge's agreement with the majority opinion, sometimes with a separate statement
  • Antonym: majority opinion - The official decision agreed upon by more than half the members of a court

Etymology

From Latin dissentire, from dis- ‘apart’ + sentire ‘to feel’, ‘to think’.

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
to express or hold opinions that differ from those officially or commonly held; to withhold assent or agreement

Examples

I dissent.

Synset Examples

Several Republicans dissented

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or approve

Etymology

From Latin dissentīre, from dis- (apart) + sentīre (to feel, think).
