shrine

1. NOUN

/ ʃraɪn / (AmE), / ʃraɪn / (BrE)
A place regarded as holy because of its associations with a sacred person, event, or object; often a site of worship or pilgrimage.

Examples

Slowly the procession of warriors and statesmen passes through the snow beside the black water and into the brilliantly lighted cathedral, the shrine of so many precious memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a sacred place; a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block used as the focus for a religious ritual

Etymology

From Old English 'scrin', from Latin 'scrinium' (case, chest for books or papers).
