virtuoso

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɝː.tʃuˈoʊ.soʊ / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.tjuˈəʊ.səʊ / (BrE)
a person highly skilled in a particular art, especially music, or in any field

Examples

It was like being in the concert hall in the afternoon and hearing the piano virtuoso rehearsing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation

Etymology

From Italian 'virtuoso' (skilled, learned), from Latin 'virtus' (virtue, excellence).

2. ADJ

/ ˌvɝ.tʃuˈoʊ.soʊ / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.tʃuːˈəʊ.səʊ / (BrE)
Showing supreme mastery or exceptional skill in a particular art or field.

Examples

"The artist is like a fragment of a mosaic - no, he is more than that, a virtuoso performer in some vast philharmonic.

Synset Examples

a consummate artist
consummate skill
a masterful speaker
masterful technique
a masterly performance of the sonata
a virtuoso performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterful - having great skill or authority
  • Synonym: consummate - showing a high degree of skill and flair

Etymology

From Italian 'virtuoso' (skillful), from Latin 'virtus' (excellence, virtue).
