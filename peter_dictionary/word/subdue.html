subdue

1. VERB

/ səbˈdu / (AmE), / səbˈdjuː / (BrE)
to bring under control, especially by force, or to repress or restrain; to overcome or quieten something or someone

Examples

We knew the enemy was subdued, because a flare was fired as the signal.
By subduing disparate lesser groups the nation has, to some degree at least, broadened the capacity for individual liberty.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to forcibly; to subdue or repress
  • Synonym: repress - to restrain or subdue by force
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From Latin subducere (to draw from below, withdraw), via Old French subduire.

2. VERB

/ səbˈdu / (AmE), / səbˈdjuː / (BrE)
to bring under control by force or authority; to defeat or overcome, especially by physical or authoritative means

Examples

A 24 - year-old Atlanta man was arrested Sunday after breaking into the home of relatives in search of his wife, hitting his uncle with a rock and assaulting two police officers who tried to subdue him, police said.
He was then subdued and placed in the police car to be taken to Grady Hospital for treatment of scratches received in the melee.

Synset Examples

suppress a nascent uprising
stamp down on littering
conquer one's desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to forcibly put an end to something
  • Synonym: quell - to put an end to, typically by force
  • Synonym: conquer - to overcome or take control of by force
  • Antonym: release - to set free from restraint or control

Etymology

From Latin subducere (to withdraw, draw under), via Old French souduire.
