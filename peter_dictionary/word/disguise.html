disguise

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
A way of changing your appearance or manner so as to conceal your identity or the true nature of something.

Examples

Finally, the theatrical (and perversely erotic) notions of dressing up, cosmetics, disguise, and especially change of costume (or singularity of costume, as with Cipolla), are characteristically associated with the catastrophes of Mann's stories.
It is, however, a disarming disguise, or perhaps a shield, for not only has Mercer proved himself to be one of the few great lyricists over the years, but also one who can function remarkably under pressure.

Synset Examples

the theatrical notion of disguise is always associated with catastrophe in his stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask - a covering for all or part of the face to conceal identity
  • Synonym: camouflage - something used to disguise or conceal
  • Antonym: reveal - to make known or show something that was hidden

Etymology

From Old French *desguiser* (to change one's appearance), from the prefix *des-* (indicating reversal) + *guise* (manner, style).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
to change your appearance or manner so that you cannot be recognized

Examples

Driven from the marketplace by the course of history, our hero disguises himself as a private detective.

Synset Examples

The herb masks the garlic taste
We disguised our faces before robbing the bank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask - to cover or conceal in order to prevent recognition
  • Synonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • Antonym: reveal - to make known, opposite of hide or disguise

Etymology

From Old French *desguiser*, from des- (expressing reversal) + guise (manner, fashion).
