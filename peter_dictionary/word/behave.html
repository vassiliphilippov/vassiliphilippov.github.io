behave

1. VERB

/ bɪˈheɪv / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪv / (BrE)
to act in a particular way; to conduct oneself in a specified manner

Examples

Now, apparently happier under new managers, Mays and Mantle, the perfect players, are behaving as though they 're going to pass those previous peaks.
These cut-down plants will bud and blossom in record time and will behave just as they did in early spring.
And yet we obviously also believe that the avoidance of the disaster depends in some obscure or at least uncertain way on the details of how we behave.
"Since the war I have seen the way the Germans have behaved and I think, Holy Mother, we have behaved like this for hundreds of years.

Synset Examples

You should act like an adult
Don't behave like a fool
What makes her do this way?
The dog acts ferocious, but he is really afraid of people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misbehave - to behave badly or improperly
  • Synonym: comport - to behave or conduct oneself in a particular manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'behaven', from be- (intensive prefix) + 'have' (to have, to hold, to possess), originally meaning 'to bear oneself in a certain way'.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈheɪv / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪv / (BrE)
to act in a socially correct, polite, or acceptable way; to conduct oneself properly

Examples

We shall show that the polynomials * * f behave in the manner described in the first paragraph of the proof.

Synset Examples

The children must learn to behave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misbehave - to act in a way that is not proper or acceptable

Etymology

From be- + have, meaning 'to have or hold oneself in a certain manner'.

3. VERB

/ bɪˈheɪv / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪv / (BrE)
to act or conduct oneself in a particular way, especially in terms of manners or social rules

Examples

How was she to behave, Claire wondered.
"Tell me about Minerva, how she behaved, what she did to please you ".
He did n't know how she would behave with other people.

Synset Examples

She carried herself well
he bore himself with dignity
They conducted themselves well during these difficult times

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misbehave - to act badly or improperly

Etymology

From Old French *be-* (intensive prefix) + *have* (to have, hold), meaning 'to have oneself (in a certain way)'.
