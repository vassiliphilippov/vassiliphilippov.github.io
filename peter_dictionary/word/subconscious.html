subconscious

1. ADJ

/ səbˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
(Of thoughts, feelings, or actions) existing or operating in the mind just below the level of conscious awareness.

Examples

We may conclude that all six points of information, ostensibly given by the dream priest, could have been furnished by Dr. Hilprecht's subconscious reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconscious - not conscious; lacking awareness
  • Antonym: conscious - aware and able to perceive surroundings

Etymology

Formed from Latin ‘sub-’ (under) + ‘conscious’.
