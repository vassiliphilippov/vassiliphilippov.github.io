conservatism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.vəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The political or social philosophy that promotes retaining traditional institutions, customs, or values, and opposes radical change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liberalism - political philosophy favoring reform and progressive change

Etymology

From conservative + -ism, modeled after French conservatisme.
