incapable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (BrE)
Unable to do something; lacking the ability or power to perform a particular action (usually followed by 'of').

Examples

Capable as their minds may be in some directions, these guardians of the nation's security are incapable of learning, or even of observing.
This used to be part of every child's bag of tricks, but few children can do it today; some are actually incapable of rolling forward and are completely confused when not sitting or standing upright.

Synset Examples

incapable of carrying a tune
he is incapable of understanding the matter
incapable of doing the work

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capable - having the ability or capacity to do something

Etymology

From Late Latin incapabilis, from in- (not) + capabilis (able to hold or undertake).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkeɪpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪpəbl / (BrE)
not able to do or be affected by something, usually followed by 'of' (e.g., incapable of understanding)

Examples

For both Plato and Aristotle artistic mimesis, in contrast to the power of dialectic, is relatively incapable of expressing the character of fundamental reality.

Synset Examples

incapable of solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capable - having the ability or qualities to do something; able to do something

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + capax, capac- (able to take in, capable).
