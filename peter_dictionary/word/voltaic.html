voltaic

1. ADJ

/ voʊlˈteɪɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or producing electric current by chemical action, especially as in a battery or cell.

Examples

His interest in finding a relationship between voltaic electricity and magnetism is here first indicated.
The association between electric (both electrostatic and voltaic) forces and magnetic forces had been recognized by investigators for many decades.

Synset Examples

a galvanic cell
a voltaic (or galvanic) couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanic - Producing electric current by chemical action, especially in batteries; closely related to 'voltaic' in use.

Etymology

From Italian Voltaico, named after Alessandro Volta, Italian physicist who invented the voltaic pile.
