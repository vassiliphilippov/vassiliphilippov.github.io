configuration

1. NOUN

/ kənˌfɪgjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɪgjəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement or shape of the parts of something; the outline or spatial form.

Examples

My memory has catalogued for easy reference and withdrawal the image of her pink, scented stationery and the unsloped, almost printed configurations of her neat, studious handwriting with which she invited me to recall our summer, so many sentences beginning with "Remember when "; and others concerning camp friends who resided in her suburban neighborhood, and news of her commencing again her piano lessons, her private school, a visit to Boston to see her grandparents and an uncle who was a surgeon returned on furlough, wounded, from the war in Europe.

Synset Examples

he could barely make out their shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape - the external form or appearance of someone or something
  • Synonym: form - the visible shape or configuration of something

Etymology

From Latin configuratio (an arrangement, a forming), from configurare (to shape after).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement or organization of parts or elements in a particular form or structure

Examples

We consider a rural community as an assemblage of inhabited dwellings whose configuration is determined by the location and size of the arable land sites necessary for family subsistence.

Synset Examples

the outcome depends on the configuration of influences at the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - an ordered or organized positioning of things

Etymology

From Latin configuratio (a shaping, forming), from configūrāre (to shape, fashion).
