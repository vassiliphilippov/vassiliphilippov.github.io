gauche

1. ADJ

/ ɡoʊʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊʃ / (BrE)
lacking social grace, awkward and socially inept in manners or behavior

Examples

Then I saw the father's head slightly turn; gauche rainbow shapes replaced the poignant ovals of gold.

Synset Examples

too gauche to leave the room when the conversation became intimate
their excellent manners always made me feel gauche

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease in movement or social situations
  • Antonym: polished - showing skill or refinement in manners or appearance

Etymology

From French 'gauche' meaning 'left' (as in the left hand, considered awkward), originally from Old French 'gauchir' (to turn aside).
