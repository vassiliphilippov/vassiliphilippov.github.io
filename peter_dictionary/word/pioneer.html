pioneer

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who is among the first to develop or use new methods, areas of knowledge, or activities, particularly in research, technology, or art

Examples

But it is also true that Braque was the consistent pioneer in the use of simulated textures as well as of typography; and moreover, he had already begun to broaden and simplify the facet-planes of Analytical Cubism as far back as the end of 1910.
Because from the time of the great Christopher Columbus up through the time of Enrico Fermi, right up to the present day, Italian-Americans have been pioneers in building and defending our great nation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new ideas or methods
  • Synonym: trailblazer - a person who makes a new track through wild country; a pioneer
  • Antonym: follower - a person who comes after or adopts another's ideas

Etymology

From French 'pionnier' (foot soldier, pioneer), from Old French 'peonier', from 'peon' (foot soldier).

2. VERB

/ ˌpaɪəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈnɪə / (BrE)
to develop or be the first to use or apply a new method, area of knowledge, or activity; to open up or prepare the way for others

Examples

At the present time the research team which pioneered this new technique is primarily interested in advancing and perfecting it.
Engaging mature, experienced men to pioneer or open new churches in strategic population centers.
And now Mr. Hodges has pioneered further into the economic unknown with the announcement that he thinks business has stopped sliding and that it should start going upward from this point.

Synset Examples

She pioneered a graduate program for women students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trailblaze - to be a pioneer in any field; to blaze a trail
  • Synonym: initiate - to begin or originate something, especially something new
  • Antonym: follow - to come after or act according to someone else; often the opposite of leading or pioneering

Etymology

From Middle French *pionnier* (foot soldier, pioneer), from Old French *pion* (foot soldier), from Latin *pedo* (foot soldier).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈnɪə / (BrE)
one of the first people to settle in a new area and begin living there

Examples

But Thorpe saw also the hardships of pioneer existence, the cultural poverty of the frontier settlements, and the slack morality which abounded in the new regions.

Synset Examples

they went west as pioneers with only the possessions they could carry with them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new place to live, especially as one of the first people there

Etymology

From Middle French 'pionnier', from Old French 'peonier' meaning foot soldier, from 'peon' (foot soldier).
