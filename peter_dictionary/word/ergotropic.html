ergotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɜrɡəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɜːɡəˈtrəʊpɪk / (BrE)
Relating to processes or states in the body associated with energy expenditure, arousal, or activation, such as those governed by the sympathetic nervous system.

Examples

The feeding pain antagonism seems to be based on this reciprocal relation between the tropho - and ergotropic systems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trophotropic - relating to restorative or energy-conserving physiological processes

Etymology

From Greek 'ergon' (work) + 'tropos' (turn, manner), referring to a mode of activity.
