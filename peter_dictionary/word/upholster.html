upholster

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈhoʊl.stɚ / (AmE), / ʌpˈhɒl.stə / (BrE)
to provide furniture with padding, springs, webbing, and fabric covering

Examples

One of the drawing-room shutters was partly open and he made out the shapes of chairs and sofas, which seemed to be upholstered in brown or russet velvet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reupholster - to upholster furniture again, especially with new materials

Etymology

From Middle French *houste* (cushion, mattress cover) and the verb *houster* (to cover with fabric), influenced by the English noun 'upholsterer'.
