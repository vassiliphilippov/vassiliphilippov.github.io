misplace

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈpleɪs / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪs / (BrE)
To put something in the wrong place so that it cannot be found, usually by accident.

Examples

The Mercers took up residence in Brooklyn, and Mercer found a regular job in Wall Street "misplacing stocks and bonds ".

Synset Examples

I misplaced my eyeglasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose - to fail to keep possession of something; to no longer have something because you do not know where it is

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'place'.

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈpleɪs / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪs / (BrE)
To put something in the wrong place or position.

Examples

Our comment was that this was "featherbedding "in its ultimate form and that sympathy for the railroad was misplaced since it had entered into such an agreement.

Synset Examples

misplaced modifiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: displace - to move something from its usual or proper place

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'place'; modeled after late Middle English formations.
