loose

1. ADJ

/ lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
(of a ball in sport) not in the possession or control of any player.

Examples

He broke that boy (Air Force fullback Nick Arshinkoff) in two and knocked him loose from the football ".
The Texans made themselves a comforting break on the opening kickoff when Denver's Al Carmichael was jarred loose from the ball when Dave Grayson, the speedy halfback, hit him and Guard Al Reynolds claimed it for Dallas.

Synset Examples

a loose ball

2. ADJ

/ lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
not compact or dense in structure or arrangement; spaced apart or not tightly packed.

Examples

His soil was "nothing special ", just prairie land, but he had harrowed in compost until it was loose, spongy and brown black.
He clambered out of the dwindling wash, the loose dirt flying behind him, and flashed a look about.

Synset Examples

loose gravel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dense - closely compacted in substance or arrangement
  • Antonym: compact - closely and neatly packed together; dense

Etymology

From Old Norse 'lauss' (free, unattached), related to Old English 'lēas'.

3. ADJ

/ luːs / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
not firmly or tightly fixed in place; not fitting closely or tightly

Examples

It had the features of a man bewhiskered by clumps of loose feathers.
If you go after Tattaglia, all hell will break loose.

Synset Examples

loose clothing
the large shoes were very loose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tight - closely fitted; firmly fastened or closed

Etymology

From Old Norse lauss (free, vacant); related to Old English lēas (free from, devoid of).

4. ADJ

/ lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
(of the bowels, stool, or similar) tending to empty easily or excessively, often in the form of diarrhea.

Examples

I eat too much eggs and poark it sowered [ on ] my stomack and turn loose on me ".

Synset Examples

loose bowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constipated - unable to empty the bowels easily

/ lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)

Examples

They were disturbed by his idiotic bravado - as, when his bodyguard, Yankee Schwartz, complained that he had been snubbed by Dave Miller, a prize-fight referee, chieftain of a Jewish gang and one of four brothers of tough reputation, who were Hirschey, a gambler politician in loose beer running league with Torrio and O' Banion, Frank, a policeman, and Max, the youngest.

6. ADJ

/ lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
(of interpretation, translation, or use of words) not exact or strict; not literal.

Examples

He has employed from his section rich immediate materials which in a loose sense can be termed Southern.
Of course, Norton failed to mention to the press that "bare minimum of expense" is a fairly loose term.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
  • Antonym: strict - precise; adhering closely to rules or facts
  • Synonym: free - not literal; given with considerable freedom (of translation, etc.)

7. ADV

/ lus / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or object) without restraint or confinement; freely; not fastened, tied, or confined.

Examples

Related Terms

/ luːs / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
to set free or release from confinement; to grant freedom to

Examples

Etymology

9. VERB

/ luːs / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
to release or set free, especially something that is dangerous or hard to control

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restrain - to keep under control or within limits

Etymology

From Old Norse ‘lauss’, related to Old English ‘lēas’ (free, empty, false).
