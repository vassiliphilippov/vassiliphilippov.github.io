gentry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛntri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntri / (BrE)
People of high social standing, especially the class just below the nobility in Britain; collectively, the well-born and well-bred members of society.

Examples

From the outside it was an ordinary enough house of the gentry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies
  • Degree Higher: nobility - the noble class; aristocrats
  • Degree Lower: commoner - a person not of noble rank

Etymology

From Old French 'genterie', from 'gent' (well-born, noble), from Latin 'gentilis' (of a clan, of a family).
