motivate

1. VERB

/ ˈmoʊ.tɪ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
to provide someone with a reason or incentive to act; to stimulate into action

Examples

It was neither a spirit of self-sacrifice nor a yen to encourage the downtrodden that motivated Arnold.
It motivates his behavior.
At the same time that religion exercises a conserving influence, it also energizes and motivates both individuals and groups.
It is obvious that the historian who seeks to recapture the ideas that have motivated human behavior throughout a given period will find the art and literature of that age one of his central and major concerns, by no means a mere supplement or adjunct of significant historical research.
A related question is whether unemployed workers can be motivated to take the training provided.

Synset Examples

This moved me to sacrifice my career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspire - to fill someone with the urge or ability to do something
  • Antonym: demotivate - to make someone less enthusiastic or willing to do something

Etymology

From Latin motivus (moving), from movere (to move), plus the English verb-forming suffix -ate.
