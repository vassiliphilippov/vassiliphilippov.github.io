irritate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪrɪteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪt / (BrE)
To make someone feel annoyed or a little angry, especially by causing minor repeated discomforts or disturbances.

Examples

He was not irritated.
She seemed irritated.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry; to bother
  • Synonym: bother - to give someone minor trouble or annoyance
  • Degree Higher: infuriate - to make someone extremely angry
  • Antonym: calm - to make less annoyed or upset

Etymology

From Latin 'irritare' (to provoke, excite).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪrəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪt / (BrE)
to cause a part of the body or tissue to become sore, inflamed, or painful; to excite to an abnormal condition

Examples

As cholesterol piles up, it narrows, irritates and damages the artery, encouraging formation of calcium deposits and slowing circulation.

Synset Examples

Aspirin irritates my stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflame - to cause inflammation in body tissue
  • Antonym: soothe - to relieve irritation or make less sore

Etymology

From Latin irritatus (excited, provoked), past participle of irritare.
