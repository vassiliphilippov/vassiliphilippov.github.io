lofty

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔfti / (AmE), / ˈlɒfti / (BrE)
rising to a great height; impressively tall or elevated

Examples

Many aspects of civilization were not yet sufficiently crystallized to find expression, nor could the simple economic and social foundations of this world support a lofty structure.

Synset Examples

an eminent peak
lofty mountains
the soaring spires of the cathedral
towering icebergs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towering - very high or tall, especially standing out above others
  • Synonym: eminent - standing out above others; high or elevated
  • Antonym: lowly - of low height or position

Etymology

From Old Norse *lopt* (air, sky, loft), related to Old English *loft* (air, sky).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɔfti / (AmE), / ˈlɒfti / (BrE)
of high moral, intellectual, or artistic value; elevated in character or style

Examples

We would have preferred, however, to have had the rest of the orchestra refrain from laughing at this and other spots on the recording, since it mars an otherwise sober, if not lofty, performance.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exalted - elevated in rank, character, or status
  • Synonym: noble - having high moral qualities or ideals
  • Antonym: base - lacking higher values or morals; low

Etymology

From Old Norse *lopt* (air, sky).
