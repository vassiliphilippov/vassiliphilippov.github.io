encroach

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkroʊtʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
To gradually move or advance beyond acceptable or usual limits, often in a way that is unwanted or intrusive.

Examples

The Afghans invaded; the Safavids fell from power; the capital went elsewhere; the desert encroached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrude - To come or go into a place where you are not wanted
  • Synonym: invade - To enter a place or situation in large numbers, usually unwanted

Etymology

From Old French 'encrochier' (to seize, fasten upon), from 'en-' (in) + 'crochier' (to seize with a hook), related to 'crochet' (hook).
