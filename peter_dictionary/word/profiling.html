profiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.faɪ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of recording and analyzing a person's behavior, typically psychological traits, in order to predict their abilities, suitability, or to identify them as belonging to a particular group.

Examples

"Results of psychological profile... "...violent mood swings, highly intelligent, emotionally unstable.

Etymology

From 'profile' + '-ing'; 'profile' from Italian 'profilo'.
