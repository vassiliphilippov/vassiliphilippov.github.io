assassination

1. NOUN

/ əˌsæsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsæsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The murder of an important or prominent person, especially for political or ideological reasons, usually by sudden or secret attack.

Examples

No more attempts on my father's life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murder - the unlawful killing of another person with intent

Etymology

From French *assassinat*, from Italian *assassinare* (to assassinate), from Arabic *ḥashshāshīn* (a historical sect).
