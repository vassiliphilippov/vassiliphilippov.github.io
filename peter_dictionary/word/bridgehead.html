bridgehead

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪdʒ.hɛd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdʒ.hɛd / (BrE)
an area in enemy territory that has been captured and is held to allow troops and supplies to enter or advance

Examples

One Latin American country, Cuba, has become a Soviet bridgehead ninety miles off our coast.

Synset Examples

an attempt to secure a bridgehead behind enemy lines
the only foothold left for British troops in Europe was Gibraltar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foothold - a secure position from which further progress may be made

Etymology

From 'bridge' and 'head', originally referring to the end of a bridge held by an attacking force.
