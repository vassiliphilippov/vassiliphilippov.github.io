reverberate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝbɚˌeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːbəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be repeated several times as an echo; to echo or resound through a space.

Examples

The weird, insane sound of the Rebel yell reverberated again and echoed from the distant hills.

Synset Examples

the hall resounded with laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resound - to fill a place with sound; to echo loudly
  • Synonym: echo - (of sound) to be repeated or reflected

Etymology

From Latin reverberare (to strike back), from re- (again) + verberare (to beat, strike).
