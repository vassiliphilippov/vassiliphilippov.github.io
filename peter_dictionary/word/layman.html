layman

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.mən / (BrE)
A person who is not a member of a particular profession or is not trained or qualified in a specific field, especially someone who is not a member of the clergy.

Examples

Far from it; I merely draw an etymological distinction, hoping that specialists and busy people like you will welcome such precision in a layman.
I have been using the word "vocational "as a layman would at first sight think it should be used.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: professional - a person engaged or qualified in a profession
  • Antonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy

Etymology

From Middle English layman, from 'lay' (not of the clergy) + 'man', after Old French lai and Late Latin laicus.
