diatomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈtɑ.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈtɒ.mɪk / (BrE)
(of a molecule) consisting of or relating to two atoms.

Examples

Among the most likely producers of detectable radio line spectra are the light diatomic hydrides OH and CH; somewhat less likely sources are the heavier hydrides SH, SiH, and ScH.
The first diatomic hydride investigated by the paramagnetic resonance method was the OH radical.

Synset Examples

a diatomic molecule

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monoatomic - consisting of a single atom (not two)
  • Antonym: polyatomic - consisting of multiple atoms (more than two)

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'atomic' (relating to atoms).
