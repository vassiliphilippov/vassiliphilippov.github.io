hoe

1. NOUN

/ hoʊ / (AmE), / həʊ / (BrE)
A garden tool with a flat blade attached at a right angle to a long handle, used for breaking up soil and removing weeds.

Examples

The mercenaries were little interested in farming and added nothing to the output of the farm plots on which all work was still done with hoes as late as 1818.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spade - a digging tool with a flat blade
  • Synonym: rake - a long-handled tool with teeth for gathering leaves or smoothing soil

Etymology

From Middle English 'houe', from Old French 'hoe', from Latin 'hova' (hoe, mattock).
