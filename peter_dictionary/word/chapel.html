chapel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæpəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæpəl / (BrE)
A small building or room used for Christian worship, often with its own altar, forming part of a larger institution such as a school, hospital, or palace.

Examples

A Catholic priest recently recounted how in the chapel of a large city university, following Anglican evensong, at which there was a congregation of twelve, he celebrated Mass before more than a hundred.

Etymology

From Old French 'chapel', from Medieval Latin 'cappella', diminutive of 'cappa' (cloak).
