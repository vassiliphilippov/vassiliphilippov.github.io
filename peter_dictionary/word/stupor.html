stupor

1. NOUN

/ ˈstupɚ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpə / (BrE)
A state in which a person is almost unconscious and not able to think or act normally, especially caused by shock, illness, or intoxication.

Examples

While they were away Blanche came into the office every morning, running things as she had always run them for Stanley, going through the week in a dazed stupor, getting things done automatically, out of habit.

Synset Examples

his mother's death left him in a daze
he was numb with shock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daze - a state of stunned confusion or bewilderment
  • Degree Higher: coma - deep state of unconsciousness
  • Antonym: alertness - state of being awake and attentive

Etymology

From Latin 'stupor' (numbness, astonishment), from 'stupere' (be numb or amazed).
