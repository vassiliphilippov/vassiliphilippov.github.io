crofter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒf.tə / (BrE)
a person who owns or rents and works a small farm, especially in Scotland or northern England

Examples

Thomas Douglas, fifth Earl of Selkirk, a noble humanitarian Scot concerned with the plight of the crofters of his native Highlands, conceived a plan to settle them in the valley of the Red River of the North.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm

Etymology

From 'croft' (small farm), with '-er' denoting a person.
