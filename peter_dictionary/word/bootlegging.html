bootlegging

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the illegal making, distribution, or sale of alcoholic liquor, especially in violation of restrictions such as those during Prohibition in the United States

Examples

As promptly as Torrio, O' Banion jumped into bootlegging.

Synset Examples

the Prohibition amendment made bootlegging profitable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggling - the illegal movement of goods into or out of a country

Etymology

From 'bootleg', originally meaning to conceal liquor in one's boot leg; popularized during Prohibition-era United States.
