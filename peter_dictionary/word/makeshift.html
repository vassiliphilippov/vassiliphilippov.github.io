makeshift

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪkˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkʃɪft / (BrE)
made or used as a temporary substitute, using whatever is available.

Examples

Cardboard noisemakers, substitutes for the unavailable tin models, were being hawked and bought at makeshift stands every few yards along Broadway, and one's ears were continually serenaded by the horns' rasps and bleats.

Synset Examples

crossed the river on improvised bridges
the survivors used jury-rigged fishing gear
the rock served as a makeshift hammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - made or done without preparation, using what is available
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From the phrase 'make shift', meaning to manage as best one can (17th century).
