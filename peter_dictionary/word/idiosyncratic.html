idiosyncratic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdi.oʊ.sɪnˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdi.əʊ.sɪnˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
Peculiar to a particular individual; characterized by distinctive, often unusual, traits or habits.

Examples

Scientists often turn out to be idiosyncratic, too.

Synset Examples

we all have our own idiosyncratic gestures
Michelangelo's highly idiosyncratic style of painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing rather than a group
  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange in behavior or style
  • Antonym: typical - having the usual qualities of a particular type

Etymology

From Greek idiosynkrasia (peculiarity of constitution), from idios (one's own) + synkrasis (mixture).
