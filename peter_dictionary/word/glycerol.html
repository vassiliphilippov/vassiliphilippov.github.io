glycerol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəˌrɔl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɒl / (BrE)
a thick, sweet, clear liquid, chemically a trihydroxy alcohol, obtained from fats and oils and used in food, medicine, and industry

Examples

Greasy soils, which are typified by hydrocarbons and fats (esters of glycerol with long chain organic acids).
These cells were thawed at 37 ` C for 30 min and were deglycerolized by alternately centrifuging and mixing with descending concentrations of glycerol solutions (20, 18, 10, 8, 4 and 2 %).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycerin - alternative name for glycerol, especially used in pharmaceutical and food contexts

Etymology

From French *glycérine* and *glycérol*, based on Greek *glykys* (sweet).
