wasteful

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪstfəl / (BrE)
Using more of something than is necessary or efficient; not careful in the use of resources such as time, money, or materials.

Examples

But it is a clumsy and wasteful process: it can produce negative results but not much that is positive.
Not only is this kind of duplication wasteful, but it gives the combined system the ability to take freight traffic away from the New York Central and other railroads serving the area.

Synset Examples

a clumsy and wasteful process
wasteful duplication of effort
uneconomical ebb and flow of power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: economical - using resources carefully and efficiently
  • Antonym: efficient - performing tasks in an optimal way, minimizing waste
  • Antonym: thrifty - using money and other resources carefully, not wasteful

Etymology

From Middle English, from waste + -ful; 'waste' from Anglo-Norman 'waste', from Latin 'vastus' (empty, desolate).
