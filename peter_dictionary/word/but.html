but

1. ADV

/ bʌt / (AmE), / bʌt / (BrE)
Used as an adverb to mean 'only' or 'merely.'

Examples

This year, I am told, the CBS network will continue to abide by the rule but NBC will play to a conclusion here.

Synset Examples

I was merely asking
it is simply a matter of time
just a scratch
he was only a child
hopes that last but a moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: only - no more than; just
  • Synonym: merely - simply; nothing more than
  • Antonym: more - a greater amount or degree
