wisecrack

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪzˌkræk / (AmE), / ˈwaɪzˌkræk / (BrE)
To make a witty, sarcastic, or ironic remark, often for humorous effect.

Examples

Tanner wisecracked.
He wisecracked about the captain's indoctrination of new men, took great delight in slaughtering cockroaches with ethyl chloride, and gave no thought for tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quip - a witty remark
  • Synonym: joke - a humorous remark

Etymology

Formed from 'wise' (witty, smart) + 'crack' (joke or remark), late 19th century American slang.
