bull

1. VERB

/ bʊl / (AmE), / bʊl / (BrE)
to push, force, or move forward in a determined or aggressive way, especially ignoring obstacles or opposition

Examples

He 'll even bull head-on into the rules when he is sure he's right.

Synset Examples

He bulled through his demands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - to move clumsily or forcefully, often without regard to obstacles
  • Synonym: shove - to push forcefully

2. NOUN

/ bʊl / (AmE), / bʊl / (BrE)
Slang for lies, nonsense, or talk meant to deceive; nonsense or rubbish.

Examples

Less bull the more you can do with um.

Synset Examples

I put up with a lot of bullshit from that jerk
what he said was mostly bull

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullshit - slang for nonsense, lies, or deceptive talk
  • Antonym: truth - what is factually or actually the case

3. NOUN

/ bʊl / (AmE), / bʊl / (BrE)
A large, strong, and heavyset man (often used figuratively).

Examples

This was a bull of a man.

Synset Examples

he was a bull of a man
a thick-skinned bruiser ready to give as good as he got

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruiser - a large, heavy, tough man

4. NOUN

/ bʊl / (AmE), / bʊl / (BrE)
An uncastrated adult male of domestic cattle.

Examples

She glanced around the clearing, taking in the wagon and the load of supplies and trappings scattered over the ground, the two kids, the whiteface bull that was chewing its cud just within the far reaches of the firelight.
And they had almost everything they needed: land, a house, two whiteface bulls, three horses.
I know a couple of big old bull queers that would just love to make your acquaintance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cow - adult female of domestic cattle
  • Antonym: steer - castrated male of domestic cattle

Etymology

From Old English 'bula' (bull), related to Old Norse 'boli' and German 'Bulle'.
