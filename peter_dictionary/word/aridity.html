aridity

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈrɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality of being dry or unproductive, especially in a metaphorical sense; lack of interest, emotion, or value.

Examples

The moral aridity of merchants made them loyal usually to their ledgers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrenness - quality of being unproductive or infertile
  • Antonym: fruitfulness - the quality of yielding fruit or results

Etymology

From Latin ariditas, from aridus meaning 'dry'.
