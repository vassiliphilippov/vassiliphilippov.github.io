superintend

1. VERB

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛnd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to oversee and direct the work or actions of others; to be in charge of an activity, organization, or group and ensure it is carried out properly

Examples

"Mr. Palmer will attend to any applications relating to bridges and if desired will view the proposed site, and lay out and superintend the work, or recommend a suitable person to execute it.

Synset Examples

Who is overseeing this project?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oversee - to supervise or watch over a person or activity
  • Synonym: manage - to have the responsibility of controlling or organizing something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or supervise properly

Etymology

From Latin 'superintendere', from 'super-' (over) + 'intendere' (to direct one's attention).
