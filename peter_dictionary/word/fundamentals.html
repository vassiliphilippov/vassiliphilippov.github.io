fundamentals

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlz / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlz / (BrE)
The basic principles, rules, or laws that form the foundation of a subject or system.

Examples

Obviously, the four year provision limits this to fundamentals and much desirable material must be eliminated.
He had already been studying the Bible; he knew the fundamentals, and after studying with Fletcher for a time he approached Rector, announced that he wanted to be baptized and that was that.

Synset Examples

first you must learn the fundamentals
let's get down to basics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basics - simple and essential facts or principles
  • Synonym: essentials - absolutely necessary elements or principles
  • Antonym: details - individual facts or small parts

Etymology

From Latin fundamentum (foundation), from fundare (to found).
