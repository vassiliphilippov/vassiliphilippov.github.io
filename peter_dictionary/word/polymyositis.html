polymyositis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.li.maɪ.oʊˈsaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɒl.ɪ.maɪ.əˈsaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A rare inflammatory muscle disease causing weakness and pain, especially in the limbs and neck.

Examples

The clinical impression at this time was either muscular dystrophy or polymyositis.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'myos' (muscle) + '-itis' (inflammation).
