antecedent

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntɪˈsidənt / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈsiːdnt / (BrE)
an event, condition, or cause that comes before something else; a previous occurrence or cause

Examples

The antecedents of Dipylon vases and of the Iliad lie in the Aegean past.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consequence - something that follows as a result or effect
  • Synonym: precursor - something that comes before and indicates the approach of something else

Etymology

From Latin antecedens, present participle of antecedere (to go before), from ante- (before) and cedere (to go).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
a person from whom one is descended, especially more remote than a grandparent; an ancestor

Examples

The chief literary antecedents of the Snopes clan appeared in the realistic, humorous writing which originated in the South and the Southwest in the three decades before the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from some ancestor

Etymology

From Latin antecedentem, present participle of antecedere (to go before): ante- (before) + cedere (to go).
