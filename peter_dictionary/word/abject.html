abject

1. ADJ

/ ˈæb.dʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈæb.dʒɛkt / (BrE)
(of a condition or behavior) extremely bad, unpleasant, and degrading; of the most contemptible kind.

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".

Synset Examples

abject cowardice
a low stunt to pull
a low-down sneak
his miserable treatment of his family
You miserable skunk!
a scummy rabble
a scurvy trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable

Etymology

From Latin abjectus (cast away, degraded), from abicere (to cast away).
