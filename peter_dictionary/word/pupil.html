2. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪl / (BrE)
the dark circular opening in the center of the iris of the eye, through which light passes to the retina

Examples

He was crouched over his anvil in the courtyard getting his chisels into trim, when a splinter of steel flew into his eye and imbedded itself in his pupil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - the colored part of the eye surrounding the pupil

Etymology

From Latin *pupilla*, diminutive of *pupa* (girl, doll), originally referring to the small reflection seen in someone’s eye.
