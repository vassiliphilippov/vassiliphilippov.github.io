melodramatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.ə.drəˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a story, behaviour, or style) showing exaggerated emotions or excitement, often to the point of being unrealistic or overly theatrical.

Examples

The already faded pastel charms of the naive music itself vanish entirely in Fistoulari's melodramatic contrasts between ultra vehement brute power and chilly, if suave, sentimentality.

Synset Examples

a melodramatic account of two perilous days at sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: histrionic - excessively theatrical or dramatic in character or style
  • Antonym: restrained - showing control and moderation in emotion or style

Etymology

From French mélodramatique, from mélodrame (melodrama) + -atique (-atic).
