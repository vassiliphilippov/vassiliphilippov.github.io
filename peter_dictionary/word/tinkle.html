tinkle

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to make or emit a light, high, ringing sound, typically like small bells or glass striking lightly

Examples

Fiddles screeched; a piano tinkled.
He came through from the Fleet Bar, which was stag, with the ice cubes tinkling in a glass he carried.

Synset Examples

tinkling bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jingle - to make a light, ringing sound like small metal objects shaking together
  • Degree Higher: clang - to make a loud, resonant, metallic sound

Etymology

Echoic formation imitating the sound itself.
