foreman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.mən / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mən / (BrE)
A person who supervises and directs a group of workers, especially in a factory, workshop, or construction site.

Examples

Finding him dead, Cook caught Russell's horse and rode to the cattle foreman's house to report the incident and request bloodhounds to trail the assassins.
"There is a man named Grabski who is the foreman in charge of the bricklayers at Majdanek.

Synset Examples

if you want to leave early you have to ask the foreman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervisor - person in charge of overseeing work or workers

Etymology

From Middle English 'foreman', formed from 'fore-' (before) + 'man'.
