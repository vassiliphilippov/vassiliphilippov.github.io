pilfer

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪlfər / (AmE), / ˈpɪlfə / (BrE)
to steal things of little value or in small quantities, especially repeatedly or in a sneaky way

Examples

An inmate, a former university professor, expounded to us, logically and clearly, that someone was pilfering his thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - take another person's property without permission
  • Synonym: purloin - steal, especially in a stealthy manner
  • Synonym: filch - steal, especially things of little value

Etymology

From Old French *pelfrer* (to plunder), related to *pelfre* (booty).
