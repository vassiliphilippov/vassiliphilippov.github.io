elector

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
a person who has the legal right to vote, especially in a political election

Examples

The registration figures given in Table 2 must be interpreted with caution since the estimate for eligible electors were made without the benefit of a reliable census.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voter - a person who votes or has the right to vote
  • Antonym: nonvoter - a person not eligible to vote

Etymology

From Latin 'elector' (one who chooses), from 'eligere' (to choose, select).
