treacherous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃ(ə)rəs / (BrE)
(of a situation, surface, or condition) dangerously unstable and unpredictable, likely to cause harm or danger.

Examples

He turned over impatiently and pulled the sheet over his head against the treacherous encroachment of the dawn.

Synset Examples

treacherous winding roads
an unreliable trestle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - free from danger or risk

Etymology

From Old French 'trecheros', from 'trichier' (to cheat) and '-ous'.
