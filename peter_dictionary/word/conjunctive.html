conjunctive

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (BrE)
A word, typically uninflected, used to connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences.

Examples

Say this... ...son of a bitch... ...makes a connection with us and with Axis Chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunction - a word used to join words, phrases, or clauses

Etymology

From Latin conjunctivus, from coniungere 'to join together'.
