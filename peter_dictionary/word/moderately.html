moderately

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑdɚətli / (AmE), / ˈmɒdərətli / (BrE)
To a moderate degree; not very much but not too little.

Examples

As is well known, detergent actives belong to the chemical class consisting of moderately high molecular weight and highly polar molecules which exhibit the property of forming micelles in solution.
He is aware that every moderately important problem merges imperceptibly into every other problem.

Synset Examples

pretty big
pretty bad
jolly decent of him
the shoes are priced reasonably
he is fairly clever with computers

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree
  • Degree Higher: greatly - to a large degree; very much

Etymology

From Latin 'moderatus' (within bounds, moderate) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
