sophistication

1. NOUN

/ səˌfɪstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌfɪstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing a lot of experience, knowledge, or refinement; the process or result of becoming more advanced or cultured, especially intellectually or socially

Examples

Have you examined this problem of increasing consumer sophistication from the standpoint of your own company?
However, a growing intellectual sophistication and the new certitudes imparted by courses in psychology and anthropology make the students increasingly critical of their somewhat provincial and overprotective parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refinement - the process of making something more cultured or sophisticated
  • Antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or sophistication

Etymology

From French 'sophistication', from sophistiqué (to sophisticate), from sophist (wise man, sophist).
