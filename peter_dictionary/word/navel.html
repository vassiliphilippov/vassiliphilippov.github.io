navel

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl / (BrE)
the small, central scar on the abdomen marking the spot where the umbilical cord was attached before birth; the bellybutton.

Examples

Mary was as frozen below the navel as he.
We hold safe little jobs illustrating tooth-paste ads or the salacious incidents in trivial novels, and most of our easel painting is nothing but picking the fluff out of the navel so it can be contemplated in greater purity.

Synset Examples

you were not supposed to show your navel on television
they argued whether or not Adam had a navel
she had a tattoo just above her bellybutton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellybutton - informal term for navel

Etymology

From Old English 'nafela', related to Old High German 'nabalo' and Latin 'umbilicus'.
