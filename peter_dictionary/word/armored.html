armored

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːr.mɚd / (AmE), / ˈɑː.məd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, person, or thing) protected by armor; covered with an outer layer for protection, typically in a military context.

Examples

Although the armored vest fitted the upper part of his body snugly, he felt no security.
Rocco Vecchio - a perspiring, haggard Vecchio - was standing there, flanked by two men in the uniforms of armored transport guards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unarmored - not protected by armor

Etymology

From the noun 'armor', ultimately from Old French 'armeure' (defensive covering).
