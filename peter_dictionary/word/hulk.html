hulk

1. NOUN

/ hʌlk / (AmE), / hʌlk / (BrE)
A very large or heavy person, especially one who is clumsy or awkward.

Examples

Between the ponderous hulk and himself, in the valley over which Papa-san reigned, men had hidden high explosives, booby traps, and mines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - an unusually large person; a person of great strength or size

Etymology

Originally from Middle English, meaning a clumsy, unwieldy ship; later transferred to refer to large, clumsy people.

2. VERB

/ hʌlk / (AmE), / hʌlk / (BrE)
(of a large object or form) to appear very large, heavy, or imposing, often looming or overshadowing the surroundings.

Examples

God was everywhere, in the belfry, in the steeple, in the clouds, in the trees, and in the mountains hulking on the horizon.

Synset Examples

The huge sculpture predominates over the fountain
Large shadows loomed on the canyon wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loom - to appear as a large, indistinct, often threatening shape

Etymology

From Middle English *hulke* (large ship), from Old English *hulc* (hulk, ship), from Latin *hulcus*, likely from Greek *holkas* (a ship which is towed).

3. NOUN

/ hʌlk / (AmE), / hʌlk / (BrE)
a ship that has been wrecked and abandoned, often left to decay

Examples

These were the ships of His Majesty's Navy, herding the hulks of the East Indies merchants and the yachts and ketches of the loyalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreck - remains of something that has been destroyed or ruined, especially a ship

Etymology

From Middle English 'hulke', from Old English 'hulc', originally referring to a large ship from Late Latin 'hulcus', from Greek 'holkas' (a ship towed by another).
