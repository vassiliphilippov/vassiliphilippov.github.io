engagement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
an arrangement to meet someone or be present at a particular time and place; an appointment or scheduled meeting

Examples

The engagement was supposed to be all set for the big theater in McCormick Place, which Sol Hurok, ballet booker extraordinary, considers the finest house of its kind in the country - and of course he does n't weep at the capacity, either.
Charlie Marble was back and forth on several occasions, first to confer with Andy on the advisability of cancelling the Las Vegas engagement - they decided it was wise - and later to announce that a prominent comedian, also an agency client, had agreed to fill the casino's open date.

Synset Examples

she asked how to avoid kissing at the end of a date

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - an arrangement to meet someone at a particular time and place
  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose, especially for discussion

Etymology

From French 'engagement' (a formal agreement), from 'engager' (to pledge).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
a period or arrangement in which someone is employed or booked to perform a particular job or activity, especially in the arts or entertainment

Examples

So the Kirov will fly back to Russia, minus a Chicago engagement, a serious loss for dance fans - and for the frustrated bookers, cancellation of one of the richest bookings in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booking - a reservation or arrangement for someone to perform or work
  • Synonym: employment - the state of having paid work

Etymology

From French *engagement*, from *engager* (to pledge, bind).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
A battle or fight between opposing military forces during a war.

Examples

Jonathan wrote grimly of the destruction of Harpers Ferry before they abandoned it; of their first engagement at Falling Waters after Old Jack's First Brigade had destroyed all the rolling stock of the B + O Railroad.

Synset Examples

Grant won a decisive victory in the battle of Chickamauga
he lost his romantic ideas about war when he got into a real engagement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle - an armed fight between armies, navies, or air forces
  • Degree Lower: skirmish - a short, unplanned fight between small groups of soldiers

Etymology

From French *engagement*, from engager (to engage).

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
A job or period of employment for a performer or performing group, usually for a limited time.

Examples

On that date the Musicians Emergency Fund, organized to furnish employment for musicians unable to obtain engagements during the depression and to provide relief for older musicians who lost their fortunes in the stock market crash, observed its 30 th anniversary.

Synset Examples

the play had bookings throughout the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booking - a reservation for a performance, event, or service

Etymology

From French *engagement*, from engager (to pledge, involve), originally from Old French *engagier*.

5. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
A formal agreement or promise that two people will marry each other.

Examples

There are boxed proclamations in the newspaper of watchings, pinnings, ringings, engagements, and marriages in a scrupulously graded hierarchy of felicity.
"Witt House happily announces the engagement of Fran Horowitz to Erwin Schwartz of Fife House ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrothal - formal engagement to be married
  • Degree Higher: marriage - the legal or formal union of two people as spouses

Etymology

From French 'engagement', from 'engager' (to bind by promise).
