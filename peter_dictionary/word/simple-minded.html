simple-minded

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpəlˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Lacking intelligence, subtlety, or insight; showing a lack of complex understanding.

Examples

In the field of entertainment there is no spur to financial daring so effective as audience boredom, and the first decade of the new device was not over before audiences began staying away in large numbers from the simple-minded, one minute shows.

Synset Examples

a simple-minded argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • Antonym: shrewd - having sharp powers of judgment; astute

Etymology

Formed from 'simple' + 'minded', meaning possessing a simple mind.
