fasciculation

1. NOUN

/ fəˌsɪkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˌsɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A small, involuntary muscle twitch involving a group of muscle fibers, often visible under the skin.

Examples

No fasciculations or sensory defects were found.

Etymology

From Latin *fasciculus* (a small bundle).
