inclination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪŋklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Geometry) The angle formed by a given line and the positive x-axis, measured counterclockwise from the positive half of the x-axis.

Examples

If it is assumed that the formula given by Lodge of * * f, cosec 2lc applies, the pressure difference along the major axes can be calculated from the angle of inclination of the major axis, and from this the interfacial tension can be calculated.

Etymology

From Latin inclinatio (a leaning toward).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The angle between the plane of an orbit and the plane of the ecliptic, measured in degrees.

Examples

He related events on earth to periodic celestial phenomena and indicated that the years of maximum inclination of the ecliptic coincided with the years of important electrical discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliquity - the angle between an object's rotational axis and its orbital axis

Etymology

From Latin inclinatio (a leaning, bending) from inclinare (to bend, incline).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.klɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.klɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a feeling or tendency that makes someone likely to prefer or choose one thing over others; a mental preference or disposition

Examples

Besides showing no inclination, apparently, to absent himself from his native region even for short periods, and in addition writing a shelf of books set in the region, he has handled in those books an astonishingly complete list of matters which have been important in the South during the past hundred years.
Although Faulkner was the heir in his own family to this tradition, he did not have Stark Young's inclination to romanticize and sentimentalize the planter society.

Synset Examples

he had an inclination to give up too easily
a tendency to be too strict

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tendency - an inclination or natural urge to act or behave in a particular way
  • Antonym: disinclination - a reluctance or lack of willingness

Etymology

From Latin inclinatio (a leaning, inclination), from inclinare (to lean).
