predigested

1. ADJ

/ priˈdaɪd͡ʒɛstɪd / (AmE), / priːˈdaɪdʒɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of food) having been partially digested by enzymatic or chemical action to make it easier to absorb or digest.

Examples

America is rapidly becoming a nation that uses processed, precooked and even predigested foods.

Synset Examples

predigested foods are a boon for those who are ill or have impaired digestion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preprocessed - (of food) prepared or altered before final processing or consumption

Etymology

From pre- (before) + digested, meaning digested in advance.
