misquote

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈkwoʊt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkwəʊt / (BrE)
To repeat or report someone’s words inaccurately; to quote incorrectly.

Examples

Now would you care to have us say that you were misquoted in regard to it "?

Synset Examples

He had misquoted the politician

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quote - repeat or copy the exact words of someone

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'quote'.
