invert

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɝːt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːt / (BrE)
(Music) To reverse the order of notes, intervals, or other elements in a musical composition.

Examples

The composer may reverse or invert the order of his original set of intervals (or rhythms or dynamic changes).

Synset Examples

here the theme is inverted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to turn something the opposite way, in music or general sense

Etymology

From Latin invertō, invertere (to turn upside down).
