2. NOUN

/ skwil / (AmE), / skwiːl / (BrE)
a long, high-pitched cry or sound, typically made by an animal or person when frightened, excited, or in pain

Examples

The startled animal let out a terrified squeal and thrashed around in the stall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - a loud, high-pitched, piercing cry or sound
  • Degree Lower: whimper - a low, feeble sound expressing fear or pain

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound itself.
