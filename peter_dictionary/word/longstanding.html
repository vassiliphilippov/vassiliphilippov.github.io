longstanding

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋˌstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌstændɪŋ / (BrE)
having existed or continued for a long time

Examples

It tends to support the longstanding precious sentiments, the traditional ways of thinking, and the customary ways of living.

Synset Examples

a longstanding friendship
the longstanding conflict

Etymology

From 'long' + present participle of 'stand', meaning standing or existing for a long time.
