cluster

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈklʌstə / (BrE)
A group of similar things or people positioned or occurring closely together.

Examples

About 300 yards up the creek was a cluster of Mexican houses containing six rooms in the form of a square.
Yet the violent immediacy of the wallpaper strips pasted to the paper, and the only lesser immediacy of block capitals that simulate window lettering, manage somehow to push the grape cluster back into place on the picture plane so that it does not "jump ".

Wherever you looked, you saw Committeemen running across the meadows, some away from the road, some toward it, some parallel to it; and about a mile to the west a cluster of at least fifty militia were making their way in our direction.
While this was being discussed, we saw the militia to the west of us fanning out and breaking into little clusters of two and three men as they approached the road.

Synset Examples

a bunch of trees
a cluster of admirers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunch - a number of things of the same kind growing or held together
  • Synonym: group - a number of people or things that are together or in the same place

Etymology

From Old English *clyster* (cluster, bunch), related to Old English *clust* (lump, mass).

2. VERB

/ ˈklʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈklʌstə / (BrE)
to come together in a group or mass; to gather or be grouped closely together

Examples

Synset Examples

The poets constellate in this town every summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together into one place or group
  • Antonym: disperse - to go or spread in different directions; to break up a group

Etymology

From Old English 'clyster' (a cluster), related to Middle Low German 'kluster'.

3. VERB

/ ˈklʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈklʌstə / (BrE)
to come together or gather in a close group; to cause things or people to form a cluster

Examples

I think these attributes cluster, but I have no evidence.
I think for example of three women's colleges with pitifully small enrollments, clustered within a few miles of a major Catholic university, which is also co-educational.

Synset Examples

She bunched her fingers into a fist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come or bring together
  • Antonym: disperse - to scatter in different directions

Etymology

From Middle English 'clustre' (cluster, group), from Old English 'clyster' (cluster, group, swarm).
