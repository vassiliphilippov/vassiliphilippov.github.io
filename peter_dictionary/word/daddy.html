daddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdædi / (AmE), / ˈdædi / (BrE)
An informal or affectionate term for one's father.

Examples

If Daddy's books are out of bounds his own picture books are not.
Or you could wish your daddy would really do it - kill Gratt Shafer like he said when you all the time, all along, could feel the nerve draining out of him like air out of a punctured tire when you are on a muddy road alone and it is raining and at night.
Mommy, Daddy'’s fighting again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dad - informal term for father
  • Synonym: father - male parent
  • Antonym: mommy - informal term for mother
  • Antonym: mother - female parent

Etymology

Originates as a child's variant of "dad," likely from baby talk; attested in English since the 16th century.
