outgeneral

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / aʊtˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
to surpass or defeat (an opponent) by superior military strategy or generalship

Examples

He had been a corps commander during the disastrous defeat and retreat of 1942 when the ill-prepared, ill-equipped British forces "were outmaneuvered, outfought and outgeneraled ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outmaneuver - to outwit or outsmart an opponent, especially by clever tactics

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' meaning 'to surpass' and 'general' (military leader).
