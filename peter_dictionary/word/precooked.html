precooked

1. ADJ

/ priˈkʊkt / (AmE), / priˈkʊkt / (BrE)
(of food) cooked partially or completely in advance, so that it only needs reheating or minimal preparation before eating.

Examples

America is rapidly becoming a nation that uses processed, precooked and even predigested foods.

Synset Examples

frozen precooked meals from the supermarket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw - (of food) not cooked

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'cooked'.
