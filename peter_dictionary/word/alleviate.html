alleviate

1. VERB

/ əˈli.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈliː.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to make something less severe or easier to bear, especially pain, suffering, or a problem

Examples

It has identified itself with the very tension and terror it once did so much to alleviate.

Synset Examples

you could facilitate the process by sharing your knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relieve - to reduce pain, discomfort, or difficulty
  • Synonym: mitigate - to make less severe, harsh, or painful
  • Antonym: exacerbate - to make a problem, pain, or situation worse

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviatus', past participle of 'alleviare' (to lighten), from 'ad-' (to) + 'levis' (light).

2. VERB

/ əˈliːvieɪt / (AmE), / əˈliːvieɪt / (BrE)
to make pain, suffering, or a problem less severe or more bearable

Examples

Hell's own amount of chaulmoogra oil did nothing to alleviate their torment; they expired amid indescribable fantods, imploring the Blessed One to forgive their desecration.
Vending machines can alleviate the long hike to the cafeteria during the break with resulting waste of production time.

Synset Examples

This pill will relieve your headaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relieve - to reduce pain or a problem; to lessen
  • Antonym: exacerbate - to make a problem or pain worse

Etymology

From Latin alleviare, from ad- (to) + levis (light).
