durable

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʊrəbəl, ˈdjʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
Able to last for a long time without significant deterioration or loss of function.

Examples

The stained-glass windows may have developed unpremeditated patinas, the paneling may be no more durable than the planks in a political platform.

Synset Examples

hopes for a durable peace
a long-lasting friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-lasting - lasting for a long time
  • Antonym: perishable - liable to decay or spoil quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'durabilis', from 'durare' (to last, to harden).
