heaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːvər / (AmE), / ˈhiːvə / (BrE)
A bar used as a lever, especially for twisting rope or performing heavy manual work.

Examples

Heiser, breathless and wild-eyed, brought the chilling news that the handspikes, heavers and holystones had been mysteriously removed from their customary places.
Who reported to you the disappearance of handspikes and heavers and who "-

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handspike - a heavy bar used as a lever

Etymology

From the verb 'heave' (to lift), with the agent suffix '-er'.
