frantically

1. ADV

/ ˈfræn.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a hurried and disorganized way due to fear, anxiety, or extreme emotion.

Examples

Stevie twisted and, frantically, commanded the mare to leap straight ahead.
The man shrank before the hot fury, searching frantically for the answer * * h Finnegan woke up.

Synset Examples

she fought back madly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmly - in a relaxed and composed manner
  • Synonym: madly - in an uncontrolled or wild manner

Etymology

From frantic + -ally, where frantic derives from Latin *phreneticus* via Old French.
