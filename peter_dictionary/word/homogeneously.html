homogeneously

1. ADV

/ hoʊˈmoʊdʒəniəsli / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒdʒəniəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is all of the same kind, composition, or character; uniformly or similarly throughout.

Examples

But it should be pointed out that some of the new watering places - Fire Island, Nantucket, Westhampton, Long Island, for example - tend to be homogeneously Jewish.

Synset Examples

the students at this college are homogeneously middle-class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniformly - in a way that is the same everywhere or at all times
  • Antonym: heterogeneously - in a way that is different or varied in composition

Etymology

From 'homogeneous' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'homogeneous' from Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind).
