abstruseness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstruːsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstruːsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to understand; obscurity or complexity in meaning

Examples

Apparently the feeling is that anything more would be involvement in technical abstrusenesses of possible pedantic interest but of no visible significance in practical affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'abstrusus' (hidden, concealed), from 'abstrudere' (to hide).
