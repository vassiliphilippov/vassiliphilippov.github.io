processed

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑˌsɛst / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.sɛst / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) having been subjected to a special process or treatment, especially one to preserve it or make it ready for use.

Examples

America is rapidly becoming a nation that uses processed, precooked and even predigested foods.

Synset Examples

prepared ergot
processed cheeses are easy to spread

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unprocessed - not subjected to a special process or treatment

Etymology

From the verb 'process', originating from Latin 'processus', meaning 'going forward'.
