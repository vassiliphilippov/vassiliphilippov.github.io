leech

1. NOUN

/ liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
A carnivorous or bloodsucking worm, usually found in water or damp places, typically with suckers at both ends, some species of which are used in medicine for bloodletting.

Examples

A third remarked of slowness of Southerners: "They moved about from corner to corner, as uneasy as a litter of hungry leaches on the neck of a wooden god ".

Etymology

Old English lēce (physician, leech), from Proto-Germanic *lēkijaz; the sense 'bloodsucking worm' influenced by association with its use in medicine.
