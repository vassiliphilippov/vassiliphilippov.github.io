solipsism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪpˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪpˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) the theory that only the self exists or can be known to exist.

Examples

We need a doctrine of imitation to save us from the solipsism and futility of pure formalism.

Etymology

From Latin 'solus' (alone) + 'ipse' (self).
