okay

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
Satisfactory or in a satisfactory condition; acceptable, but not especially good.

Examples

"It's okay, Mr. Paxton.
Docherty said, "It's okay, Bonfiglio, let him by ".
Everything'’s gonna be okay, Frankie, don'’t worry.

Synset Examples

an all-right movie
the passengers were shaken up but are all right
is everything all right?
everything's fine
things are okay
dinner and the movies had been fine
another minute I'd have been fine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptable - good enough to be allowed or approved
  • Synonym: fine - satisfactory or acceptable
  • Degree Higher: excellent - very good; of the highest quality
  • Degree Lower: poor - of low quality or below standard

Etymology

Probably from the initials 'O.K.', an abbreviation of 'oll korrect', a humorous alteration of 'all correct' (early 19th century U.S. slang).

2. ADV

/ oʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
In a satisfactory or adequate manner; well enough.

Examples

Come on, Anthony, I'’ll take you fishing again tomorrow, okay?

Synset Examples

she'll do okay on her own
held up all right under pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way
  • Synonym: alright - in a satisfactory or acceptable manner
  • Antonym: badly - in an unsatisfactory or poor way

Etymology

Origin uncertain; likely an abbreviation of 'all correct' with intentional misspelling as 'Oll Korrect'.
