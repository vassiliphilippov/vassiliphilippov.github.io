harshly

1. ADV

/ ˈhɑrʃli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃli / (BrE)
In a harsh or unkind manner; in a way that is severe, rough, or unfriendly.

Examples

"We 'll do it another way, then ", he said harshly.
"There's the one who's lucky "! the girl murmured harshly.

Synset Examples

`That's enough!,' he cut in harshly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindly - in a kind and gentle manner

Etymology

From 'harsh' + '-ly'. 'Harsh' from Middle English, probably from Old French *herce* (rake, harrow).

2. ADV

/ ˈhɑrʃli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃli / (BrE)
In a harsh and grating manner; in a way that is unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses.

Examples

Once he disposed of these items which screamed so harshly for attention, he could undertake the things which really counted.

Synset Examples

her voice fell gratingly on our ears

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softly - in a gentle or pleasing manner

Etymology

From 'harsh' + '-ly', with 'harsh' from Middle English 'harsk' (bitter, rough).
