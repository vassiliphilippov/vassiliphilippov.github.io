attend

1. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to be present at an event, meeting, or place, such as a class, service, or conference

Examples

Despite the warning, there was a unanimous vote to enter a candidate, according to Republicans who attended.
It would authorize the Texas Education Agency to establish county-wide day schools for the deaf in counties of 300000 or more population, require deaf children between 6 and 13 years of age to attend the day schools, permitting older ones to attend the residential Texas School for the Deaf here.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miss - not go to or be present at something
  • Synonym: participate - to take part in an event or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' (to give heed to, to stretch toward).

2. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to deal with or take responsibility for something; to give care or attention to a task or matter

Examples

At their meeting he told her not to bother about "where "- he would attend to that.
Starr Jones gets up every morning at five o' clock, milks his family cow, attends to farm chores, and then takes a two-hour train trip to New York.

Synset Examples

Could you see about lunch?
I must attend to this matter
She took care of this business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manage - to be in charge of; to deal with (a situation or task)
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to give proper care or attention

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' meaning 'to give heed to', from 'ad-' (to) + 'tendere' (to stretch).

3. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to act as a servant to or provide personal service for someone, especially in a formal or supportive capacity

Examples

Without saying so, she was really grateful; for to attend the dying was something she had never experienced, and certainly had not imagined when she thought of the duties she would have as Bobby Joe's wife.

Synset Examples

May I serve you?
She attends the old lady in the wheelchair
Can you wait on our table, please?
Is a salesperson assisting you?
The minister served the King for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - to work for or perform duties for someone, especially as a servant or employee
  • Synonym: wait on - to act as a servant or attendant for someone

Etymology

From Latin attendere (to give heed to, wait upon) via Old French atendre.

4. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to pay attention to or heed; to listen or watch carefully

Examples

The spectacle progressed towards a denouement which was obviously still remote; the audience attended.

Synset Examples

The children in the audience attended the recital quietly
She hung on his every word
They attended to everything he said

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heed - to pay careful attention to
  • Antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to

Etymology

From Latin attendere, from ad- (to) + tendere (to stretch).

5. VERB

/ əˈtɛnd / (AmE), / əˈtɛnd / (BrE)
(of a circumstance, event, or condition) To occur with or as a result of something; to accompany or follow as a consequence.

Examples

THE RECENT publicity attending the successful federal prosecution of a conspiracy indictment against a number of electrical manufacturers has evoked a new respect for the anti-trust laws that is justified neither by their rationale nor by the results they have obtained.

Synset Examples

Menuhin's playing was attended by a 15-minute standing ovation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accompany - to go together with or be combined with
  • Synonym: follow - to come after as a result or consequence

Etymology

From Latin attendere meaning ‘give heed to’, from ad- ‘to’ + tendere ‘stretch’.
