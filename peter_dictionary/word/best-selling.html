best-selling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛst ˌsɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbɛst ˈsɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
Having sold in very large numbers; extremely popular and commercially successful (especially of books, music, or other products).

Examples

The tradition reached its apex, perhaps, in the works of Thomas Nelson Page toward the end of the century, and reappeared undiminished as late as 1934 in the best-selling novel So Red the Rose, by Stark Young.

Synset Examples

a best-selling novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popular - liked or admired by many people
  • Antonym: unsuccessful - not achieving success or popularity

Etymology

Formed by combining 'best' + present participle of 'sell'.
