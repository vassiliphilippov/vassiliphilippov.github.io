emancipate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪt / (BrE)
to give someone, especially a group of people, equal rights or freedom from legal, social, or political restrictions

Examples

Our society has "emancipated "the woman, giving her new independence and new authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression
  • Antonym: oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority

Etymology

From Latin emancipatus, past participle of emancipare (to set free), from manus (hand) + capere (to take).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmænsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmansɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to set free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions; to release from slavery or servitude

Examples

For example, in 1693 the Philadelphia Yearly Meeting of Friends declared that its members should emancipate their slaves and in 1776 it determined to exclude from membership all who did not comply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manumit - to release from slavery or bondage
  • Antonym: enslave - to make someone a slave; to oppress

Etymology

From Latin emancipare (to declare someone free, transfer ownership), from e- (out) + mancipare (to transfer ownership).
