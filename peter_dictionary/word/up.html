up

1. ADV

/ ʌp / (AmE), / ʌp / (BrE)
To or in a higher position, level, or condition; from a lower to a higher place or state, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

Then Heywood Sullivan, Kansas City catcher, singled up the middle and Throneberry was across with what proved to be the winning run.
"I knew it all along ", confessed Arnold with a grin, "but I just happened to think how much nicer it would be to drop one way up there ".
Push back up and repeat.
Drop both hands to the floor and at the same time kick the right foot up in back.
Their own easier, slower tempo is especially dear to Southerners; and I have heard many say that they are content to earn a half or a third as much as they could up North because they so much prefer the quieter habits of their home town.

Synset Examples

look up!
the music surged up
the fragments flew upwards
prices soared upwards
upwardly mobile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: down - to or in a lower position, level, or state

Etymology

Old English 'up' (up, upward), from Proto-Germanic *upp.

2. ADJ

/ ʌp / (AmE), / ʌp / (BrE)
(of an amount, value, or level) higher or increased than before; improving or more vigorous.

Examples

Within a month, calls were up seventy per cent.
I doubt they'll kick up any fuss.

Synset Examples

its an up market
an improving economy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: down - lower or decreased; the opposite of 'up' in this sense

/ ʌp / (AmE), / ʌp / (BrE)
at or moving to a higher position or level; above a former position or value

Related Terms

Etymology

4. VERB

/ ʌp / (AmE), / ʌp / (BrE)
to increase or raise something, especially an amount, level, or stake

Examples

Fair Dealer Humphrey upped the ante, asked cloture power for a mere majority of Senators.
To reach a still greater audience of location-minded manufacturers, our industrial advertising budget for the fiscal year was increased from $ 32000 to $ 40000, and the Industrial Building Authority's financial participation was upped from $ 17000 to $ 20000.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - to make or become greater in amount, number, or degree
  • Synonym: raise - to lift or move to a higher position or level; to increase
  • Antonym: decrease - to make or become smaller or fewer in amount, degree, or size
