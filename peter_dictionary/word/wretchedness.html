wretchedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtʃɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛtʃɪdnəs / (BrE)
A state of great unhappiness or misery, especially caused by hardship, suffering, or misfortune.

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".

Synset Examples

the misery and wretchedness of those slums is intolerable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misery - great suffering or unhappiness
  • Antonym: bliss - perfect happiness; great joy

Etymology

From 'wretched' + '-ness', with 'wretched' from Old English 'wreccede' (accursed, miserable).
