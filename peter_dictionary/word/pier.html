pier

1. NOUN

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
A long, narrow structure built out from the shore into the water and supported by piles, used as a landing place for ships or boats, or for walking and recreation.

Examples

The big factories which are relatively near the centers of our cities - the rubber factories in Akron, Chrysler's Detroit plants, U. S. Steel's Pittsburgh works - often began on these sites at a time when that was the edge of the city, yet close to transport (river), storage (piers) and power (river).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jetty - a structure projecting into the water to protect a harbor or shoreline or to provide a landing place
  • Synonym: dock - a platform for loading and unloading ships or boats
  • Synonym: wharf - a flat structure built along the shore for ships to tie up to

Etymology

From Old French *pier*, from Latin *petra* (stone).
