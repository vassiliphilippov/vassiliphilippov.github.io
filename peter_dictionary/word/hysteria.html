hysteria

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A state of extreme emotional excitement or agitation, often with uncontrollable laughter, crying, or other behaviors.

Examples

With shout and slow dance, with tears and song, with scream and contortion, the corner group was beset by hysteria and shivering, wailing, shouting, possession of something that seemed like an alien and outside force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety

Etymology

From Greek *hystera* (womb), historically linked to ancient beliefs about causes of the condition.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / hɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A state of excessive or uncontrollable fear or emotional excitement, often affecting a group of people.

Examples

This hysteria reached its height under the leadership of Senator Joseph McCarthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often causing wildly unthinking behavior

Etymology

From Greek hystera (womb), once believed to be linked to women’s psychology.
