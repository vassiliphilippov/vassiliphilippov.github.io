thrombus

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːmbəs / (AmE), / ˈθrɒmbəs / (BrE)
A blood clot that forms inside a blood vessel and remains attached to its site of origin.

Examples

Some of the small vessels were filled with fibrin thrombi, and there was extensive interstitial hemorrhage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: embolus - a blood clot, air bubble, or other substance which has been carried in the bloodstream and lodged in a vessel

Etymology

From Greek *thrombos* (lump, clot).
