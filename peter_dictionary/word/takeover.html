takeover

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈteɪkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A sudden and decisive change of government, especially one achieved illegally or by force; a coup d'état.

Examples

Thus, typically, the first stage of a Communist takeover is to "neutralize "a country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coup d'état - a sudden and illegal seizure of government power

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'take over', meaning to assume control.
