electromagnet

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktroʊˌmæɡnət / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌmæɡnət / (BrE)
A type of magnet in which magnetic force is produced by an electric current passing through a coil of wire wrapped around an iron core.

Examples

The magnetic resonance absorption was detected by employing a Varian model f broad line spectrometer and the associated 12 - inch electromagnet** system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanent magnet - a magnet that retains its magnetic properties after the magnetizing force is removed

Etymology

From electro- (pertaining to electricity) + magnet, indicating a magnet powered by electricity.
