socialize

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to take part in social activities; to interact with others in a friendly way

Examples

But people can n't be made to integrate, socialize (the two are inseparable by Southern standards) by law.

Synset Examples

He never socializes with his colleagues
The old man hates to socialize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to associate with others socially
  • Synonym: mingle - to move among and engage with others at a social event
  • Antonym: isolate - to remain apart from others, not participating socially

Etymology

From French 'socialiser', from 'social' (pertaining to society).
