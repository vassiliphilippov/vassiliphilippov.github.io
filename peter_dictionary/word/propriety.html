propriety

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpraɪəti / (AmE), / prəˈprʌɪəti / (BrE)
conformity to conventional standards of behavior or morals; the quality of being proper or appropriate.

Examples

He said that the propriety or impropriety of such a gathering was a question that was to be settled by every man in accordance with the convictions of private judgments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impropriety - behavior that is improper or not in accordance with accepted standards
  • Synonym: decorum - behaving in keeping with good taste and propriety

Etymology

From Latin 'proprietas' (appropriateness, peculiarity), from 'proprius' (one's own, proper).
