coccidioidomycosis

1. NOUN

/ koʊkˌsɪdioɪˌdoʊmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɒkˌsɪdɪɔɪˌdəʊmaɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with the fungus Coccidioides, typically affecting the lungs and sometimes the skin, marked by symptoms such as cough and nodules.

Examples

Agents that are known to cause frequent infections among laboratory workers such as those causing Q fever, tularemia, brucellosis, glanders, coccidioidomycosis, etc., belong in this category.

Etymology

From New Latin Coccidioides (the fungal genus) + -mycosis (Greek for 'fungal infection').
