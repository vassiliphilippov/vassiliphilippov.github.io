vocalic

1. ADJ

/ voʊˈkælɪk / (AmE), / vəʊˈkælɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, consisting of, or characterized by vowels.

Examples

- Syllables are linguistic units centering in peaks which are usually vocalic but, as has been noted, are consonantal under certain circumstances, and which may or may not be combined with preceding and / or following consonants or combinations of consonants.

Synset Examples

vocalic sounds
the Gaelic language being uncommonly vocalic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonantal - relating to or consisting of consonants

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalis' (vocal), from 'vox' (voice).
