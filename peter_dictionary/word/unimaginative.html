unimaginative

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈmædʒɪnətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈmædʒɪnətɪv / (BrE)
Lacking originality, creativity, or the ability to think of new ideas.

Examples

The Hephzibah Menuhin-Amadeus Quartet (Angel) and Victor Babin-Festival Quartet (RCA Victor) editions give us superlative string playing (both in symphonic style) crippled by unimaginative piano playing.

Synset Examples

a sterile ideology lacking in originality
unimaginative development of a musical theme
uninspired writing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: creative - having the ability to create or invent things
  • Antonym: original - new and different in a way that originates from someone’s own ideas
  • Synonym: uninspired - lacking in inspiration or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'imaginative'.
