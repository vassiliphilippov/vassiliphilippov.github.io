stadium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdiəm / (BrE)
A large, usually open structure with tiers of seats for spectators, used for sporting events and entertainment.

Examples

A big-league municipal stadium at Flushing Meadow Park is in the works, and once the lease is signed the local club will be formally recognized by Commissioner Ford C. Frick.
Shea's efforts figure prominently in the new stadium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arena - an enclosed area for sports or entertainment

Etymology

From Latin 'stadium', from Greek 'stadion' (a measure of length and a racecourse).
