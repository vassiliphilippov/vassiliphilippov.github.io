instrumentality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəˌmɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊˌmɛnˈtælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of serving as a means or tool to achieve a particular purpose.

Examples

Man is first religious; the instrumentalities follow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utility - the state of being useful

Etymology

From Medieval Latin instrumentalitas, from Latin instrumentum (tool, means) + -ality (forming nouns denoting a quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstrəmɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊmɛnˈtælɪti / (BrE)
an organization or agency, especially one created by a government for a specific purpose

Examples

coordinate, correlate, and publish information with a view to advancing the development of low-cost saline water conversion projects; and cooperate with other Federal departments and agencies, with State and local departments, agencies, and instrumentalities, and with interested persons, firms, institutions, and organizations.

Synset Examples

are the judicial instrumentalities of local governments adequate?
he studied the French instrumentalities for law enforcement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agency - a government department or organization that provides a particular service
  • Synonym: organ - a part or structure that performs a specific function within an organization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin instrumentalitas, from Latin instrumentum (tool, means).
