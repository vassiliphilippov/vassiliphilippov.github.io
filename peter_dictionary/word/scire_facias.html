scire facias

1. NOUN

/ ˌskaɪri ˈfeɪʃiəs / (AmE), / ˌskɪəri ˈfeɪʃɪæs / (BrE)
A judicial writ requiring the recipient to show cause why a record should not be enforced or annulled.

Examples

If you interfere, you'll have to appear before the judge and show cause.

Etymology

From Latin 'scire facias', meaning 'let it be made known'.
