plane

1. NOUN

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
a vehicle with wings and one or more engines that enable it to fly through the air; an aeroplane

Examples

Their flight was delayed, Dunn said, when a boarding ramp inflicted some minor damage to the wing of the plane.

I saw holes in planes at the airport and in cars in the streets.
We flew in rickety planes so overloaded that we wondered why they did n't crash.

Synset Examples

the flight was delayed due to trouble with the airplane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airplane - another term for a powered flying vehicle with fixed wings
  • Synonym: jet - an aircraft powered by jet engines
  • Antonym: helicopter - a powered flying vehicle with rotary blades

Etymology

Shortened from 'aeroplane', which comes from French 'aéroplane', from Greek 'aēr' (air) and 'planos' (wandering).
