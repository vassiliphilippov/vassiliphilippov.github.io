hedonism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhiːdənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that pleasure or happiness is the highest good and proper aim of human life, especially as an ethical principle

Examples

And yet amid all the gay hedonism in Pilgrimage and Wander-Years is a cycle of short poems, "Thoughts in Loneliness ", filled with brooding, melancholy, and sombre longing ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epicureanism - philosophy advocating pleasure as the highest good
  • Antonym: asceticism - self-discipline and abstention from pleasure for religious or philosophical reasons

Etymology

From Greek hēdonē (pleasure) + -ism.
