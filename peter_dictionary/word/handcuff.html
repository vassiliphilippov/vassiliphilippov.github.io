handcuff

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌkʌf / (AmE), / ˈhændˌkʌf / (BrE)
A metal fastening that can be locked around a wrist, usually used in pairs to restrain a person's hands.

Examples

Just take off the handcuffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shackle - a device used to restrain movement, especially of the limbs

Etymology

From Middle English 'handcuff', 'hand' + 'cuff'.
