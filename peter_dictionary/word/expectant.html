expectant

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛktənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktənt / (BrE)
Having or showing an excited feeling that something pleasant or interesting is about to happen.

Examples

When in one letter Jessica informed me that her father did not like the idea of her going out alone on New Year's Eve, I knew for a moment an immense relief; but the letter went on: she had cried, she had implored, she had been miserable at his refusal, and finally he had relented - and now how happy she was, how expectant!

Synset Examples

an expectant hush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipatory - characterized by anticipation or expectation
  • Synonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring hope or optimism
  • Antonym: resigned - accepting something unpleasant that one cannot change

Etymology

From Latin exspectans, present participle of exspectare (to look out for, await).
