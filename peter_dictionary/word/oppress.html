oppress

1. VERB

/ əˈprɛs / (AmE), / əˈprɛs / (BrE)
to treat a person or group in a cruel or unfair way, especially by using power or authority; to keep someone in subservience by unjust force or restriction

Examples

The silence oppressed him, made him bend low over the horse's neck as if to hide from a wind that had begun to blow far away and was twisting slowly through the darkness in its slow search.

Synset Examples

The government oppresses political activists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persecute - to treat someone cruelly, especially because of their beliefs or ethnicity
  • Antonym: liberate - to set someone free, especially from oppression or imprisonment

Etymology

From Latin 'opprimere' (to press against, to crush), from 'ob-' (against) + 'premere' (to press).
