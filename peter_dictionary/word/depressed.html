depressed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈprɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛst / (BrE)
(of prices, markets, or areas) lower than normal or previous levels, especially due to economic factors; experiencing reduced activity or prosperity.

Examples

A training program in a depressed area may have few enrollees unless there is some apparent prospect for better employment opportunities afterwards, and the prospect may be poor if the training is aimed solely at jobs in the local community.

Synset Examples

the market is depressed
prices are down

Related Terms

  • Antonym: booming - experiencing rapid growth or high activity, especially economically
  • Degree Lower: sluggish - slow-moving or inactive, especially of markets or economies

Etymology

Past participle of 'depress', from Latin 'deprimere' (to press down).
