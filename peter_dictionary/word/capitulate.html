capitulate

1. VERB

/ kəˈpɪtʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to stop resisting an opponent or demand and officially agree to their terms, especially after a struggle; to surrender under certain conditions

Examples

His unsuccessful strivings to give up drink are represented as religious strivings; he keeps a bottle in a wardrobe at home, and "before this wardrobe Praisegod Piepsam had before now gone literally on his knees, and in his wrestlings had bitten his tongue - and still in the end capitulated ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to give up to an enemy or opponent; to yield
  • Antonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of; to oppose

Etymology

From Late Latin 'capitulare', meaning 'to draw up under headings', from Latin 'capitulum' (heading, chapter); originally referring to making terms in chapters or articles.
