quaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvə / (BrE)
A trembling or wavering sound, especially in a person's voice.

Examples

And the stiffly regal look of them, she saw grimly, lacked the quaver of age which, thwarting the efforts of her amazing will, ran through her spoken words like a thin ragged string.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrato - a rapid, slight variation in pitch in singing or playing musical instruments
  • Synonym: tremor - a slight trembling movement or sound

Etymology

From Middle English 'quaven' (to tremble).
