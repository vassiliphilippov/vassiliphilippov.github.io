fender

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛndə / (BrE)
A part of a vehicle that frames a wheel well, designed to prevent water, mud, and debris from being thrown into the air by the rotating tire.

Examples

Over the rattling of fenders, humming of tires and chattering of gears there was a charming melody of whispers and tiny giggles.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a fender a wing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wing - British term for a vehicle's wheel-arch cover

Etymology

From fend (to ward off) + -er, denoting something that wards off or protects.
