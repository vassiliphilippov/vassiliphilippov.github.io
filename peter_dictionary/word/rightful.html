rightful

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪtfʊl / (BrE)
Having a legitimate or lawful right; legally valid, proper, or deserved.

Examples

Thus, the larvae of the intruder can develop at the expense of the rightful inhabitants and the store of beebread.

Synset Examples

a rightful inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legitimate - conforming to the law or to rules
  • Antonym: unlawful - not conforming to, permitted by, or recognized by law or rules

Etymology

Formed from 'right' + suffix '-ful'; 'right' from Old English 'riht' (just, correct).
