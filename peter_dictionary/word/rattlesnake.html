rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɾlˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈrætəlˌsneɪk / (BrE)
A venomous American pit viper with a series of loosely attached horny segments at the end of its tail, which produce a rattling sound when shaken as a warning signal.

Examples

Laurence M. Klauber put length at maturity at two thirds the ultimate length for some rattlesnakes, and Charles C. Carpenter's data on Michigan garter and ribbon snakes (Thamnophis) show that the smallest gravid females are more than half as long as the biggest adults.
Klauber investigated the rattlesnakes carefully himself and also summarized what others have found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit viper - venomous snake with heat-sensitive pit organs

Etymology

From the word 'rattle' (the noise-making organ) + 'snake'. First known use in the early 17th century.
