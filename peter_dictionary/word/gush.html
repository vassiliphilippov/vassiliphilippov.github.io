gush

1. VERB

/ ɡʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌʃ / (BrE)
(of liquid or flame) to flow out suddenly and forcefully in a stream or jet.

Examples

He told Argiento to take a bird out of the cage, cut a large vein under its wing, let the blood gush into Michelangelo's injured eye.
Blood gushed from his nose, and he backed off as rapidly as he could, stumbling over his own feet in his frantic haste to get away from Curt's fists.
Powder flame gushed beneath the wagon.

Synset Examples

water gushed forth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - to force out suddenly in a brief stream
  • Degree Lower: trickle - to flow slowly in a thin stream
  • Degree Higher: pour - to flow continuously in a large stream

Etymology

Of imitative origin, representing the sound of sudden flow.

2. VERB

/ ɡʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌʃ / (BrE)
to speak or write with excessive enthusiasm, often in a way that seems over-the-top or unrestrained

Examples

"Oh, I'm so delighted to meet you ", she gushed.
"I 'd love to audition for you ", she gushed.

Synset Examples

She raved about that new restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rave - to speak or write about something with great enthusiasm or admiration
  • Synonym: enthuse - to express eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: criticize - to indicate the faults of something or someone in a disapproving way
