shrilly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃrɪli / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪli / (BrE)
In a shrill, high-pitched, and piercing manner.

Examples

Watson watched two of them flounder into the shallow water and listened to their voices beg shrilly.
Then, as he turned toward the telephone, it rang shrilly to shatter the stillness in the room and he reached for it eagerly.

Synset Examples

she sang rather shrilly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrill - high-pitched and piercing (adjective)

Etymology

From 'shrill' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
