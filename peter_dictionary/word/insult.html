insult

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsʌlt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌlt / (BrE)
to speak to or treat someone with disrespect or scornful abuse

Examples

Gouge, burn, blast, insult it as they would, could anyone really take Papa-san?
Were they to be insulted again because of the South's great victory?
It was meant to insult him, and did n't quite succeed.
Because it insults my intelligence.

Synset Examples

He insulted her with his rude remarks
the student who had betrayed his classmate was dissed by everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offend - to cause someone to feel hurt, angry, or upset by something said or done
  • Antonym: compliment - to express praise or admiration for someone

Etymology

From Latin 'insultare' (to jump on, attack), via French 'insulter'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsʌlt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sʌlt / (BrE)
a rude or offensive remark or action intended to hurt someone's feelings or dignity

Examples

Just as now anyone may hurl insults at a citizen of Mars, or even of Tikopia, and no senatorial investigation will result.

Synset Examples

when a student made a stupid mistake he spared them no abuse
they yelled insults at the visiting team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - rude or offensive language; cruel and violent treatment
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'insultare' (to leap upon, attack), via Old French 'insulte'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsʌlt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌlt / (BrE)
a remark or action that is intended to hurt someone's feelings or show a lack of respect

Examples

And I'’ll forget the insult.

Synset Examples

turning his back on me was a deliberate insult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - an act that causes someone to feel hurt or upset
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'insultare' (to jump upon, attack), from 'in-' (on) + 'saltare' (to leap).
