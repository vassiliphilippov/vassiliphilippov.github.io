coping

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.pɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.pɪŋ / (BrE)
A protective cap or cover, often of stone or brick, placed on top of a wall to prevent water from penetrating the wall.

Examples

He ran his eye along the roof copings; almost at once a figure bulked up.

Etymology

From present participle of cope, from Latin *colpus* (summit, top).
