underling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person of lower rank or status who works under the authority or control of another; a subordinate.

Examples

But aside from everything else, it would scarcely be pleasant to have dealings with one who was nominally an underling and actually held - you could say - the whip hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subordinate - a person under the authority or control of another within an organization

Etymology

From Old English 'under' (below) + 'ling' (diminutive suffix, as in 'darling'), meaning 'someone of lesser status'.
