ply

1. VERB

/ plaɪ / (AmE), / plaɪ / (BrE)
to provide or supply something (such as a service, food, or trade), especially regularly or diligently

Examples

Dice rolled, prostitutes plied their trade, thieves stole, murderers stabbed, and the ungodly blasphemed.

Synset Examples

The hostess provided lunch for all the guests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply - to make something needed or wanted available to someone
  • Synonym: furnish - to provide with what is needed

Etymology

From Old French *plier* (to bend, fold), from Latin *plicare* (to fold).
