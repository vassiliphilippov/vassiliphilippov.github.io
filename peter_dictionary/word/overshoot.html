overshoot

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈʃuːt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to aim beyond or exceed a particular target, often unintentionally

Examples

But here again, the surface declaring device both overshoots and falls short of its aim.

Synset Examples

The plan overshoots its aim

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undershoot - to aim below or fail to reach a target

Etymology

From over- (beyond) + shoot; first attested in the late Middle English period.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈʃut / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to go past or beyond a target, destination, or intended point, especially unintentionally

Examples

When the shaken Palmer finally did hit his fourth shot, he overshot the hole by 15 feet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undershoot - to fall short of a target or point

Etymology

From over- (meaning 'beyond') + shoot; first recorded in English in the 15th century.
