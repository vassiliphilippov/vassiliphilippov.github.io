paleocortex

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪli.oʊˈkɔːrtɛks / (AmE), / ˌpæli.əʊˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
The part of the cerebral cortex involved with the sense of smell, considered evolutionarily ancient.

Examples

It seems to follow that by and large an antagonism exists between the paleo - and the neocortex as far as emotional reactivity is concerned, and that the balance between the two systems determines the emotional responsiveness of the organism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neocortex - the evolutionary newer part of the cerebral cortex, involved in higher-order brain functions

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + Latin 'cortex' (bark, outer layer).
