overstrain

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈstreɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to subject something or someone to excessive strain or stress; to stretch beyond healthy or safe limits.

Examples

In any case but the last, such a course is sure to avenge itself upon the individual; the moral powers no more than the physical and mental, can bear overstraining.

Synset Examples

He overextended himself when he accepted the additional assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - to stretch something to its limit; to exert force

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'strain' (to stretch or exert), dating from the 17th century.
