thinness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪnnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having little body fat.

Examples

She made him sad some days, and he was never sure why; it was something to do with her back, the thinness of it, and the quick, jerky way she bent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leanness - the quality of being lean, especially from having little body fat
  • Antonym: obesity - the condition of being very overweight or having excess body fat

Etymology

From 'thin' + '-ness', meaning the quality or state of being thin.
