palm

1. NOUN

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɑːm / (BrE)
the inner surface of the hand between the wrist and the base of the fingers

Examples

Place flat palms on either side of the head a few inches away from the ears, fingers pointing toward the shoulders.
They lay on his lap, palms up, stiffly motionless, the tapered fingers a little thick at the joints.
Sweat popped out over him and he felt the slick between his palm and the stick grip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sole - the underside of the foot

Etymology

From Latin palma (palm of the hand, palm tree), via Old French and Middle English.

2. VERB

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɑːm / (BrE)
to conceal or hold something in the palm of one's hand, often to hide it from view

Examples

The bartender winked back at him and palmed the bill.

Synset Examples

Don't handle the merchandise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - to touch, hold, or manipulate something with the hands

Etymology

From Latin 'palma' (palm of the hand).
