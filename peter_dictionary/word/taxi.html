taxi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksi / (AmE), / ˈtæksi / (BrE)
A car with a driver that can be hired to take passengers to a chosen destination in exchange for payment.

Examples

If you use company transportation to meet trains or to haul visitors, would taxis be cheaper?
I left the party as soon as possible and got into a taxi.
It is screaming at you even in the taxis of London ".
A taxi took him back to the bar and grill where he had left his car, and a few minutes later he found a parking place across the street from his apartment.
Then they took a taxi to Trastevere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cab - another word for a taxi, especially in British English

Etymology

Shortened from taximeter cab, where 'taximeter' referred to the instrument measuring fare, from French 'taximètre'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtæksi / (AmE), / ˈtæksi / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to move slowly on the ground under its own power before takeoff or after landing.

Examples

At the prearranged time, Greg started the engine and taxied out.
The tower cleared the plane for take-off at 8 a. m., and Captain Rickards began taxiing toward the runway.

Synset Examples

The plane taxied down the runway

Etymology

From the noun 'taxi', shortened from 'taxicab'; applied to aircraft by analogy from vehicles maneuvering on the ground.
