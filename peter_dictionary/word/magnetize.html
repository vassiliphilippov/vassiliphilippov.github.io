magnetize

1. VERB

/ ˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an object or substance) magnetic; to induce magnetic properties in

Examples

Electrical literature contained numerous references to lightning that had magnetized iron and had altered the polarity of compass needles.
In the late 1700's Beccaria and van Marum, among others, had magnetized iron by sending an electrostatic charge through it.

Synset Examples

The strong magnet magnetized the iron shavings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demagnetize - to remove or reduce magnetic properties

Etymology

From magnet + -ize; based on French magnétiser and late Latin magnetizare, from Greek magnētis (magnet).
