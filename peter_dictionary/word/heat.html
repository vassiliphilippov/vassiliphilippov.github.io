heat

1. NOUN

/ hit / (AmE), / hiːt / (BrE)
A form of energy that is transferred from one body or substance to another as a result of a difference in temperature.

Examples

The high heat fluxes existing at the electrode surfaces of electric arcs necessitate extensive cooling to prevent electrode ablation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold - absence of heat or low temperature
  • Synonym: thermal energy - energy possessed by an object due to the movement of its molecules, often measured as heat
  • Degree Higher: temperature - the degree of heat present in a substance or object

Etymology

From Old English hǣtu (heat, warmth), related to hāt (hot).
