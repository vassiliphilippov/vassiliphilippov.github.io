luminous

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
shining or glowing softly with light; softly radiant.

Examples

Michelangelo found the rabbi in the room of study, a gentle old man with a white beard and luminous grey eyes, robed in black gabardine with a skullcap on his head.
They were climbing the hill in the night when the headlights abruptly probed solid blackness, became two parallel luminous tubes which broadened out into a faint mist of light and ended.
He looked at the luminous dial of his wrist watch.

Synset Examples

a house aglow with lights
glowing embers
lambent tongues of flame
the lucent moon
a sky luminous with stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glowing - emitting a steady light or warmth
  • Synonym: radiant - emitting light or heat
  • Antonym: dim - not bright; lacking light

Etymology

From Latin 'luminosus' (full of light), from 'lumen' (light).
