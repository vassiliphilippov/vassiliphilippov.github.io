casually

1. ADV

/ ˈkæʒuəli / (AmE), / ˈkaʒjuəli / (BrE)
In a relaxed or unconcerned manner; without showing anxiety or interest.

Examples

They seemed then to have had a single mind and body, a mutuality which had been accepted with the fact of their youth, casually.

Synset Examples

glanced casually at the headlines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonchalantly - in a calm and unconcerned manner
  • Antonym: deliberately - consciously and intentionally; on purpose

Etymology

From 'casual' + '-ly'; 'casual' from Late Latin 'casualis' (by chance).

2. ADV

/ ˈkæʒuəli / (AmE), / ˈkæʒjuəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not planned or methodical; without serious intention or effort.

Examples

Suddenly it seemed to him insane that they might hope to locate Gyp Carmer so casually, even were he to prove the thief.

Synset Examples

he dealt with his course work casually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessly - without taking care or attention
  • Antonym: methodically - in an orderly or systematic manner

Etymology

From 'casual' + '-ly'; 'casual' from Late Latin 'casualis' (by chance).
