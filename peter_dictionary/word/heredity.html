heredity

1. NOUN

/ həˈrɛdəti / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdəti / (BrE)
the passing of traits and characteristics from parents to their offspring through genetic transmission

Examples

In the life sciences, there has been an enormous increase in our understanding of disease, in the mechanisms of heredity, and in bio - and physiological chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - the process by which genetic information is passed from parent to child

Etymology

From Latin hereditas (inheritance), from heres (heir).
