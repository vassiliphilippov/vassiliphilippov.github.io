gaseous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæsiəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪsiəs / (BrE)
existing as or relating to a gas; having the form or characteristics of a gas rather than a solid or liquid

Examples

Carrier * * f was added and the aqueous and organic phases were separated (cells containing gaseous reactants were immersed in liquid air before opening under sodium iodide).

Synset Examples

steam is water is the gaseous state

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - existing as a solid
  • Antonym: liquid - existing as a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'gaseus', from 'gas', coined by chemist Jan Baptist van Helmont in the 17th century.
