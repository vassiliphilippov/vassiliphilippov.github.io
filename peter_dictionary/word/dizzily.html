dizzily

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪzɪli / (AmE), / ˈdɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a way that shows dizziness or light-headedness; feeling as if you might fall or lose balance.

Examples

The crowd moved back and John started dizzily down the hill.

Synset Examples

he walked around dizzily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might fall because you are not steady or balanced

Etymology

From 'dizzy' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Dizzy' from Old English 'dysig' (foolish, stupid).
