shark

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
A large, typically predatory fish with a long body, a heterocercal tail, and tough skin covered with small, toothlike scales, found mainly in the world's oceans.

Examples

He could n't see the grin that split her mouth; the teeth that shone into the phone were like a shark's.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fish - a cold-blooded aquatic animal with gills and fins

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *sharke*, possibly from German dialectal *Schurke* ('villain', 'rogue'), referring to their predatory habits.
