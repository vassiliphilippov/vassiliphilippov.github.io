keynote

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt / (BrE)
the central theme or idea in a speech or piece of writing, often setting the underlying tone or direction

Examples

Dewey Lawrence, a Tyler lawyer representing the Texas Bankers Association, sounded the opposition keynote when he said it would force banks to violate their contractual obligations with depositors and undermine the confidence of bank customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme - the subject or central idea of a talk or piece of writing
  • Synonym: motif - a dominant or recurring idea in an artistic work

Etymology

From 'key' (in music) + 'note', originally referring to the first and main note in a musical scale, later extended to mean the principal theme.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt / (BrE)
the central or most important idea in a speech, discussion, or activity

Examples

The keynotes of this style are activism and emphasis on achievements in gaining self-esteem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme - the subject or main idea in a piece of writing or discussion
  • Synonym: focus - the main point of interest or attention
  • Antonym: detail - an individual feature, fact, or item (less central than the keynote)

Etymology

From 'key' (main, principal) and 'note' (musical tone); first used in music, later figurative.

3. VERB

/ ˈkiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt / (BrE)
to set the prevailing tone or central theme of something; to serve as the foundation or leading feature of an event, activity, or creation

Examples

Design elements closely rooted to traditional forms but wearing a definite contemporary label keynote Drexel's fall 1961 group, Composite.
Dignity and comfort, in a contemporary manner, reflecting the best aspects of today's design, with substance and maturity, keynote the Perennian collection from Heritage.

Synset Examples

Comfort keynotes this designer's Fall collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominate - to have a commanding influence on; to oversee or determine the character of something

Etymology

From the noun 'keynote', which originates from music, meaning the fundamental note or tone.

4. VERB

/ ˈkiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːˌnəʊt / (BrE)
to give the main or opening address at a conference or event, usually setting the theme or tone for the proceedings

Examples

State Sen. Dick Richards will keynote; state and county committeemen, CDC directors and representatives, members of 16 area clubs, and "all residents "have been invited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address - a formal speech delivered to an audience

Etymology

From noun 'keynote', itself from 'key' (main, important) + 'note' (tone, remark). The verb arose in the 20th century from the noun sense.
