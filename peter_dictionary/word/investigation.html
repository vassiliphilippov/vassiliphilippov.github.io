investigation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvɛstəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɛstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of examining something carefully and systematically in order to discover facts or information, especially about something unknown or suspicious

Examples

The Fulton County Grand Jury said Friday an investigation of Atlanta's recent primary election produced "no evidence "that any irregularities took place.

Physicochemical investigations of anionic surfactants, including the soaps, have shown that there is little polymerization or agglomeration of the chain anions below a certain region of concentration called the critical micelle concentration.

Synset Examples

there was a congressional probe into the scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - an investigation or inquiry, especially into unusual or suspicious activities

Etymology

From Latin investigatio, from investigare (to track, search out).

From Latin investigatio (search, inquiry), from investigare (to trace, search after).
