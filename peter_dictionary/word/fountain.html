fountain

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntɪn / (BrE)
A structure from which water is forced upwards in a jet, often for decorative purposes in public places or gardens.

Examples

The fountain had brimmed over, the cannon were wet, the soldiers' monument glistened.
And when they stood by the fountain in the piazza looking at Santa Maria he had to keep a straight face, not letting on he had been there with Alberto.

Etymology

From Old French *fontaine*, from Latin *fontana* (spring, fountain), from *fons* (spring).
