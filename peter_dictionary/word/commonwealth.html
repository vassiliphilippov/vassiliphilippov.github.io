commonwealth

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.ənˌwɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌwɛlθ / (BrE)
An organized political community or state, especially one founded for the common good of its citizens.

Examples

Platonic, Stoic, early Christian, monastic, canonist or theological communism; we are concerned with Utopian communism - that is, simply communism as it appears in the imaginary commonwealth of Utopia, as More conceived it.
Moreover, it is too readily forgotten that in the Republic what gave the initial impetus to Plato's excursus into the construction of an imaginary commonwealth with its ruling-class communism of goods, wives, and children, was his quest for a canon for the proper ordering of the individual human psyche; and it is to this problem that the Republic ultimately returns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: state - a politically organized body of people under a government
  • Synonym: nation - a large body of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language, inhabiting a particular country or territory

Etymology

From Middle English common welthe (common well-being), modeled after Latin res publica.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmənˌwɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌwɛlθ / (BrE)
(in official US usage) the official term for some states (such as Massachusetts, Pennsylvania, Virginia, and Kentucky) and certain territories (such as Puerto Rico).
