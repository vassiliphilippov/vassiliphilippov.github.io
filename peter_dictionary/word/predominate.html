predominate

1. VERB

/ prɪˈdɑːmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
To be greater in number or importance than others; to be the most common or significant element present.

Examples

Initially, the flagellated protozoa predominated, but they soon gave way to the free swimming ciliated protozoa.
Colorful, bright Eastman Chromspun fabrics, with the magenta, pink and white tones predominating as well as golden shades are used with Composite.

Synset Examples

Money reigns supreme here
Hispanics predominate in this neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevail - to be widespread or generally current

Etymology

From Latin praedominari, from prae- (before) + dominari (to rule).
