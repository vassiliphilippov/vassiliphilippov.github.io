crimson

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪm.zən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.zən / (BrE)
A deep, vivid red color with a slight bluish tinge, resembling the color of blood or rubies.

Examples

Molten glare singed their eyelids an angry crimson; suffocating air sapped their strength and strained their nerves to snapping; dust choked their throats and lay like acid in their lungs.
Ben's eyes strained with the bitter hurt, his homely face slashed with gray and crimson.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red - a primary color at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange
  • Degree Lower: scarlet - a bright red color with a slightly orange tinge
  • Degree Higher: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet pigment

Etymology

From Old Spanish 'cremesín', from Arabic 'qirmizī', from 'qirmiz' (cochineal).
