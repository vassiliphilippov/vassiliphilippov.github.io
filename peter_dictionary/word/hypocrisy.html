hypocrisy

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpɑkrəsi / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform; insincerity or pretense.

Examples

The American liberal may, in the world of to-day, have a strong case; but he presents it publicly so enmeshed in hypocrisy that it is not an honest one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sincerity - honesty of mind or intention
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

From Greek hypokrisis (acting of a part), from hypokrinesthai (to play a part, to pretend).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈpɑkrəsi / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform; pretense of virtue or sincerity.

Examples

"Moriarty ", my driver suddenly exclaimed with something so definite, so final in his tone I once more repeated the absurdity, mustering all my latent powers of hypocrisy to sound convinced.
Senator, we'’re both part of the same hypocrisy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense, deceit, or hypocrisy

Etymology

From Old French hypocrisie, from Late Latin hypocrisis, from Greek hypokrisis 'acting of a part, pretense.'
