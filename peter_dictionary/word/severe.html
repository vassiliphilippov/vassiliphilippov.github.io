severe

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈvɪr / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
strict and uncompromising, especially in discipline, judgment, or manner; harsh or stern.

Examples

Only the independent art schools, that is, those not connected with any university or college, receive severe and separate investigation before accreditation by the various regional organizations.

Synset Examples

a parent severe to the pitch of hostility
a hefty six-footer with a rather severe mien
a strict disciplinarian
a Spartan upbringing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict - demanding that rules are obeyed; harshly disciplinarian
  • Antonym: lenient - permissive, merciful; not strict or harsh

Etymology

From Latin severus (stern, strict, serious).

2. ADJ

/ səˈvɪr / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪə / (BrE)
(of an illness, injury, problem, etc.) very serious; causing great harm or anxiety and potentially threatening life or well-being.

Examples

She had - he informed her - kidney trouble, liver trouble, and a severe female disorder.

Synset Examples

a dangerous operation
a grave situation
a grave illness
grievous bodily harm
a serious wound
a serious turn of events
a severe case of pneumonia
a life-threatening disease

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mild - not severe; minor in effect or degree
  • Synonym: serious - requiring careful consideration; potentially dangerous

Etymology

From Latin severus (serious, strict).
