shady

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪdi / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdi / (BrE)
(of a business, person, or activity) dishonest, corrupt, or of dubious integrity; likely to be involved in underhand or illegal dealings.

Examples

And behind every shady deal, behind every dollar earned, there was Andy, keeping the books.

Synset Examples

a shady operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unscrupulous - having or showing no moral principles; not honest or fair
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From 'shade' + -y, originally meaning 'full of shade'; figurative sense of 'dubious, questionable' dates from the late 19th century.
