raising

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing to increase in quantity or value.

Examples

The existence of conflict and of vigorous union demand for an increase in money wages does not contradict the assumption that the union is willing to settle for cost-of-living and productivity share increases as distinct from a cost raising increase in the basic wage rate.
Changes in the basic wage rate are cost raising, and they constitute an argument for raising prices.

Synset Examples

a cost-raising increase in the basic wage rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increasing - becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: lowering - causing to become less in quantity or value

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or event of causing something to move or be lifted upwards

Examples

Notice, however, that the passage of time does not permit the raising of prices per se, without an accompanying wage increase.

Synset Examples

an elevation of the temperature in the afternoon
a raising of the land resulting from volcanic activity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowering - the act or event of something moving downward

Etymology

From the verb 'raise,' which comes from Old Norse 'reisa' (to raise, lift up).
