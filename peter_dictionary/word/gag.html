gag

1. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡaɡ / (BrE)
a joke or a witty remark, especially one intended to provoke laughter, often as part of a comedy act or routine

Examples

The "wooden-leg "gag of vaudeville, another standby of this sort, had endless variations.
Another stock vaudeville gag ran: "Mother is home sick in bed with the doctor ".
The double meaning in the anatomical made it a familiar vaudeville device, as in the gags of Weber and Fields.
Apart from journalese and vaudeville gags, the anatomical is also found in jocular literature.
There was a question and answer gag that went around at that time: Q. "Who 'll carry the Forty-second and Forty-third wards "?

Synset Examples

he told a very funny joke
he knows a million gags
thanks for the laugh
he laughed unpleasantly at his own jest
even a schoolboy's jape is supposed to have some ascertainable point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to make people laugh
  • Synonym: quip - a clever or witty remark
  • Synonym: jest - a thing said or done for amusement; a joke

2. VERB

/ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)
to prevent someone from speaking or expressing their opinions, especially by using a gag or by imposing restrictions

Examples

He and Dean tied and gagged the man, using his belt and shirt for the purpose.

Synset Examples

The press was gagged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to make someone stop speaking
  • Synonym: muzzle - to prevent someone from expressing their opinions
  • Antonym: allow - to let someone speak or express themselves

Etymology

From Old Norse *gag-hals* (with the neck thrown back), later meaning 'to choke' or 'to block the mouth'.

3. VERB

/ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)
to cause someone to retch, choke, or have difficulty breathing, often by obstructing the mouth or throat

Examples

[GAGGING] [SCREAMING] BUTTON MAN: Get his head in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choke - have difficulty breathing because of a blocked throat
  • Synonym: retch - make the sound and movement of vomiting
