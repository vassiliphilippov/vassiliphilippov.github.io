citrate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪtreɪt, sɪˈtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪtreɪt, sɪˈtreɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance) to combine or treat with citric acid or to convert into a salt or ester of citric acid.

Examples

Several samples of citrated plasma were fractionated in our laboratory by Method 6 of Cohn et al.

Etymology

From citric acid, named after Latin 'citrus' (citron tree), plus suffix '-ate' used for salts and esters.
