drought

1. NOUN

/ draʊt / (AmE), / draʊt / (BrE)
A prolonged period during which there is a shortage of something, especially a desired resource or success.

Examples

Indications as late as the top of the sixth were that the Birds were to end their victory drought as they coasted along with a 3 - to-o advantage.

Synset Examples

when England defeated Pakistan it ended a ten-year drought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famine - a severe shortage of food
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something; plentifulness

Etymology

Old English *drūgath* (dryness), related to *dry*.

2. NOUN

/ draʊt / (AmE), / draʊt / (BrE)
A long period of abnormally low rainfall, resulting in a shortage of water.

Examples

Solar activities could presumably bring long periods of flood or drought.
Drought, hail, disease, and insects take their toll of crops.
