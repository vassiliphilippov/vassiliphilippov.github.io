Arianist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛəriənɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛːrɪənɪst / (BrE)
A follower of Arianism, a Christian doctrine that asserts Jesus Christ was not truly God but rather a created being distinct from God the Father.

Examples

Those who refused to believe that He was the eternal Son of God were termed Arianists.
"Arianist "! a rowdy with a big blob of a nose roared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arianism - the doctrine that Jesus was not of the same substance as God the Father

Etymology

From Arian + -ist, after Arius (c. 250–336), the founder of Arianism.
