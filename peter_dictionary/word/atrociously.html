atrociously

1. ADV

/ əˈtroʊʃəsli / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊʃəsli / (BrE)
In a very bad or shocking manner; extremely badly or unpleasantly.

Examples

Well, Jerebohm and his wife Pinkie have reached the stage of affluence that stirs a longing for the more atrociously expensive rustic simplicities.

Synset Examples

she sings terribly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - in a very bad or unpleasant manner
  • Antonym: excellently - in a very good or admirable manner

Etymology

From 'atrocious' + '-ly'; 'atrocious' from Latin 'atrox, atrocis' (fierce, cruel).
