endowment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (BrE)
A natural ability or quality possessed by a person; an innate talent or gift.

Examples

In the process, his native endowments were stretched, strengthened and disciplined to serve their human purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: gift - a natural ability or talent

Etymology

From Middle French *endowment*, from Old French *endouement*, from *endouer* (to endow).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (BrE)
the act of providing an institution, organization, or person with a permanent source of income, especially by means of a financial gift

Examples

I have here in my hand a check made out to the university and it is a magnificent endowment in the name of... [PEOPLE APPLAUDING] Uh... [MISPRONOUNCING] Anthony Vito Corleone.

Synset Examples

his generous endowment of the laboratory came just in the nick of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gift - something given voluntarily to another person or institution

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *indotamentum*, from Latin *dotare* (to endow), from *dos* (dowry).
