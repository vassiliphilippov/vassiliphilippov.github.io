circumspect

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɝːkəmˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmˌspɛkt / (BrE)
careful to consider all circumstances and possible consequences; cautious and prudent.

Examples

With a large and circumspect 20 th Century technique, he wove the materials of national heroes and events, national folklore and children's fairy tales - Slavic dances and love songs - into a solid musical literature which served his people well, and is providing much enjoyment to the World at large.

Synset Examples

circumspect actions
physicians are now more circumspect about recommending its use
a discreet investor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - acting with careful judgment about risks and consequences
  • Antonym: reckless - without regard for consequences; rash

Etymology

From Latin 'circumspicere' (to look around), from 'circum-' (around) and 'specere' (to look).
