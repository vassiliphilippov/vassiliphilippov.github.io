agglomeration

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡlɑm.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌɡlɒm.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A collection or mass formed by gathering together many separate elements; a jumble or cluster.

Examples

Physicochemical investigations of anionic surfactants, including the soaps, have shown that there is little polymerization or agglomeration of the chain anions below a certain region of concentration called the critical micelle concentration.
(2) In the critical micelle region, there is a rapid agglomeration or polymerization to give the micelles, which have a degree of polymerization averaging around 60 - 80.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregation - a group, body, or mass composed of many distinct parts
  • Synonym: cluster - a group of similar things or people positioned or occurring closely together

Etymology

From Latin 'agglomeratio', from 'agglomerare' meaning 'to wind or roll together'.
