ornament

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːnəmənt / (BrE)
an object or detail added to something to make it more attractive, often as a decoration

Examples

Ripe Geometric potters continued to employ the old syntax of ornaments and shapes and made use of the well-defined though limited range of motifs which they had inherited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something that adorns or beautifies

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, embellishment), from ornare (to equip, adorn).

2. VERB

/ ˈɔrnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːnəmənt / (BrE)
to make something more attractive by adding decorative items or details

Examples

I would not want to be one of those writers who begin each morning by exclaiming, "O Gogol, O Chekhov, O Thackeray and Dickens, what would you have made of a bomb shelter ornamented with four plaster-of-Paris ducks, a birdbath, and three composition gnomes with long beards and red mobcaps "?

Synset Examples

Decorate the room for the party
beautify yourself for the special day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorate - to make something look more attractive by putting things on it
  • Synonym: embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details
  • Synonym: beautify - to make something beautiful or more beautiful

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, decoration), from ornare (to adorn).
