creed

1. NOUN

/ krid / (AmE), / kriːd / (BrE)
a set of beliefs or principles that guides the actions or thoughts of a person or group, especially a religious faith

Examples

The Lenin tomb is obviously adequate for double occupancy, Moscow is a crowded city, and the creed of communism deplores waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group
  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • Synonym: principle - a basic idea or rule that explains or controls how something happens or works

Etymology

From Latin 'credo' (I believe).

2. NOUN

/ krid / (AmE), / kriːd / (BrE)
a formal statement of religious beliefs; the set of beliefs or aims which guide someone's actions, especially within a particular religious group

Examples

The beliefs of a religion also reflecting the values are expressed in creeds, dogmas, and doctrines, and form what Durkheim calls a credo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true, especially in religion
  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church, political party, or other group

Etymology

From Latin 'credo' meaning 'I believe'.
