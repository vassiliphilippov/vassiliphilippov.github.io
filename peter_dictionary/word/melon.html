melon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈmɛlən / (BrE)
A large, round or oval fruit with a hard rind and sweet, juicy flesh, belonging to the gourd family, such as watermelon, cantaloupe, or honeydew.

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.

Etymology

From French *melon*, from Latin *melopepo*, from Greek *mēlopepōn* (apple-shaped melon).
