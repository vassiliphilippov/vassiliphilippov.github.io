shallowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊnəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking depth in knowledge, thought, or feeling; superficiality.

Examples

It is the obverse of triviality, shallowness, emotional anaesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficiality - lack of depth of knowledge or thought or feeling
  • Antonym: depth - deep understanding or insight

Etymology

From 'shallow' + '-ness', Middle English *schalowe*.
