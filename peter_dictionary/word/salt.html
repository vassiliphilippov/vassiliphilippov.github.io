salt

1. NOUN

/ sɔlt / (AmE), / sɒlt / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed when the hydrogen of an acid is replaced by a metal or a group acting like a metal; common examples include sodium chloride.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium chloride - common salt used in food; table salt

Etymology

From Old French 'sel', from Latin 'sal' (salt).

2. NOUN

/ sɔlt / (AmE), / sɒlt / (BrE)
A white crystalline substance, especially sodium chloride, used to season and preserve food.

Examples

Later, chlorothiazide and salt restriction became necessary to control the edema of chronic congestive failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium chloride - the chemical compound forming table salt

Etymology

From Old English *sealt*, from Proto-Germanic *saltą*, from Latin *sal* (salt).

3. VERB

/ sɔlt / (AmE), / sɒlt / (BrE)
To add salt to food or something else.

Examples

Salt and pepper each breast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season - to add flavoring (such as salt, pepper, or herbs) to food

Etymology

From Old English *sealtian* (to make salty), from *sealt* (salt).
