Jacob

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪkəb / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪkəb / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the son of Isaac and grandson of Abraham, who was later named Israel and became the patriarch of the twelve tribes of Israel.

Examples

The registered owners are Jacob Lawrence, Allan Barclay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Israel - the new name given to Jacob after wrestling with God

Etymology

From Hebrew Yaʿaqōb, name of a biblical patriarch.
