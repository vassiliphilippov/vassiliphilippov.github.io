strategic

1. ADJ

/ strəˈtiːdʒɪk / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the identification and achievement of overall aims and interests, especially in military, business, or organizational contexts; concerning or affecting strategy.

Examples

Biological warfare is considered to be primarily a strategic weapon.
This aircraft, which was planned for initial operational use about 1965, would be complementary to but likewise competitive with the four strategic ballistic missile systems, all of which are scheduled to become available earlier.
Thus, the need for the B-70 as a strategic weapon system is doubtful.
- Civilized, strategic.

Synset Examples

strategic weapon
the islands are of strategic importance
strategic considerations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tactical - relating to tactics or detailed maneuvers

Etymology

From Greek stratēgikos (‘of or for a general’), from stratēgos (‘general’), from stratos (‘army’) + agein (‘to lead’).

2. ADJ

/ strəˈtidʒɪk / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk / (BrE)
Highly important to or serving as an essential part of a strategy or plan, especially relating to military operations or long-term goals.

Examples

It could halt a flood of Communist arms and strategic supplies now reaching Castro.

Synset Examples

a strategic chess move
strategic withdrawal
strategic bombing missions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tactical - concerned with or related to maneuvers or actions serving a smaller or immediate goal, often contrasted with 'strategic'

Etymology

From Greek strategikos (of a general or commander), from strategia (generalship).
