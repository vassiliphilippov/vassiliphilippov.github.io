blare

1. VERB

/ blɛr / (AmE), / blɛə / (BrE)
(of a sound) to make a loud, harsh noise, especially repeatedly or continuously.

Examples

[CAR HONKING] [ALL SHOUTING IN ITALIAN] [HORN BLARING] MICHAEL: New York City.

Synset Examples

The horns of the taxis blared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honk - to make the loud, harsh sound of a car horn or goose
  • Synonym: blast - to make a sudden, very loud noise

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative of a loud sound.
