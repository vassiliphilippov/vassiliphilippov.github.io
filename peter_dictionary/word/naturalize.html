naturalize

1. VERB

/ ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (a person) a citizen of a country to which they were not born; to grant citizenship to

Examples

George W. Cable (naturalized New Englander), writing in 1889 from "Paradise Road, Northampton " (lovely symbolic name), agitated continuously the "Southern question ".

Synset Examples

The French family was naturalized last year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denaturalize - to deprive someone of their citizenship

Etymology

From French 'naturaliser' and Medieval Latin 'naturalizare', from Latin 'natura' (birth, nature).
