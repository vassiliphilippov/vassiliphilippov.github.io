incipience

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪpiəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpiəns / (BrE)
the beginning or initial stage of existence or development; the fact of starting to appear or become apparent

Examples

Those familiar with his work will remember that he placed the incipience of the democratic faith at around 1850.

Synset Examples

he placed the incipience of democratic faith at around 1850
it is designed to arrest monopolies in their incipiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onset - the beginning of something, especially something unpleasant
  • Synonym: commencement - the beginning or start
  • Antonym: conclusion - the end or finish of an event or process

Etymology

From Latin incipientia (beginning), from incipiēns, present participle of incipere (to begin).
