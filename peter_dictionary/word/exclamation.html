exclamation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a sudden, forceful expression of emotion, usually in the form of a spoken word or phrase

Examples

As a word of caution, we should be aware that in actual practice no message is purely one of the four types, question, command, statement, or exclamation.
If the man on the sidewalk is surprised at this question, it has served as an exclamation.
All four types of message listed in Table 1 are permitted, although decorum and cocktail tradition require holding the commands to a minimum, while exclamations having complimentary intonations are more than customarily encouraged.
Questions and, particularly, exclamations are usually channeled along informal, horizontal lines not indicated in Figure 3 and seldom are carried beyond the nearest neighbor.
Muffling an exclamation, Russ sprang to the nearest steps and ran down.

Synset Examples

she gave an exclamation of delight
there was much exclaiming over it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interjection - a word or phrase expressing emotion, often standing alone

Etymology

From Latin exclamatio (a loud cry), from exclamare (to cry out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a loud utterance expressing complaint, protest, or reproach

Examples

Oh, no!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an expression of objection or disapproval
  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or grievance

Etymology

From Latin exclamatio (a cry out), from exclamare (to cry out).
