clash

1. NOUN

/ klæʃ / (AmE), / klæʃ / (BrE)
a strong disagreement or conflict between people or groups

Examples

Finally, in The Maltese Falcon among others, the clash between detective and police is carried to its logical conclusion: Sam Spade becomes the chief murder suspect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • Synonym: dispute - an argument or debate
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony in opinion or feeling

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of a violent collision, later applied metaphorically to conflicts.
