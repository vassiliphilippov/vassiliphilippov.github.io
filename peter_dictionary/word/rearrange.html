rearrange

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to change the order or position of something; to put things in a different arrangement or sequence

Examples

There was, of course, no real need to rearrange everything.
He had seen a dry, old, yellowing hand reach out, with that painful solicitude, to touch, to rearrange, to shift aimlessly, some object worth a pfennig.
He must rearrange matters so that two performers do not bump into each other.
He made a show of rearranging my forms on the shelf.

Synset Examples

Please rearrange these files
rearrange the furniture in my room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange - put things in order or organize them

Etymology

From re- (again) + arrange, from Old French *arrangier* (put in order).
