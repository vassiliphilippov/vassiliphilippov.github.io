shamrock

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæm.rɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.rɒk / (BrE)
A small clover with three-lobed leaves, especially white clover, used as a symbol of Ireland.

Examples

In the middle of the party Chandler looked up to see four smiling faces bearing down upon him, each beaming above the biggest, greenest shamrock he had ever seen.

Etymology

From Irish *seamróg* (diminutive of *seamair*, meaning 'clover').
