breeding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
Good manners and refined behavior, often indicating high social class or upbringing.

Examples

She was forty-nine at this time, a lanky woman of breeding with an austere, narrow face which had the distinction of a steeple or some architecture that had been designed long ago for a stubborn sort of prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refinement - elegance of manners or taste
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of courtesy or refinement

Etymology

From Old English 'bredan' (to breed), later extended to mean upbringing and refinement.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of animals) kept or used for the purpose of producing offspring.

Examples

To reduce vibrionic abortion in breeding sheep, feed 80 milligrams per head daily.

Synset Examples

the breeding population
retained a few bulls for breeding purposes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sterile - unable to produce offspring

Etymology

From the verb 'breed', from Old English *brēdan* (to breed, nourish).
