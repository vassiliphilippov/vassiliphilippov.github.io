primate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
A senior clergyman, especially an archbishop or high-ranking bishop of a particular country or region in certain Christian churches.

Examples

It was the first time an English Primate has done this since the 14th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archbishop - the chief bishop responsible for an archdiocese
  • Degree Lower: bishop - an ordained member of the Christian clergy ranking above priest

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *prīmās, prīmāt-* (of the first rank), from Latin *prīmus* (first).
