rubble

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.əl / (BrE)
Broken pieces of stone, brick, or other material from buildings or structures that have been destroyed or damaged.

Examples

In place of the police headquarters was a new square filled with rubble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked
  • Synonym: wreckage - the remains of something that has been badly damaged

Etymology

Probably from Old French *ruble* or Middle Dutch *rubble* (heap of stones).
