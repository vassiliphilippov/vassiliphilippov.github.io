garrison

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡærəsən / (AmE), / ˈɡærɪsən / (BrE)
A fortified military post where troops are stationed for defense or occupation.

Examples

Save for brief periods in garrison or winter quarters, soldiers rarely enjoyed the luxury of a writing desk or table.
Maneuvers over, the 7 th returned to garrison duty in Tokyo, Captain Chandler still with them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fort - a fortified building or military stronghold
  • Synonym: barracks - a building or group of buildings used to house soldiers

Etymology

From Old French 'garnison' (defense, protection), from 'garnir' (to equip, arm).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡær.ə.sən / (AmE), / ˈɡær.ɪ.sən / (BrE)
To station troops in a fort, garrison, or fortified place to defend or occupy it.

Examples

Further, it has its work cut out stopping anarchy where it is now garrisoned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - to assign troops to a post or location

Etymology

From Old French garnison (defense, protection), from garnir (to equip, to defend).
