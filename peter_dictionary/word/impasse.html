impasse

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpæs / (AmE), / ˈɪmpɑːs / (BrE)
A situation in which progress is impossible, especially because people cannot agree; a deadlock or stalemate.

Examples

Queried about the impasse, Allied Arts said: "Better cancel the Kirov for the time being.
Harold Clurman is right to say that "Waiting for Godot "is a reflection (he calls it a distorted reflection) "of the impasse and disarray of Europe's present politics, ethic, and common way of life ".

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made between opposing parties
  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which further action or progress by opposing parties is impossible

Etymology

From French 'impasse' (dead end), from 'in-' (not) + 'passer' (to pass).
