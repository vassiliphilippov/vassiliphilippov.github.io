disassemble

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəl / (BrE)
To take something apart into its separate pieces.

Examples

Now unsolder and disassemble the frame except for the two 12 "and the first two 3 - 3 4 "bars ("A "and "B "pieces), which are soldered together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assemble - to put together parts to form a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'assemble', from Old French 'assembler' (to unite).
