vogue

1. NOUN

/ voʊɡ / (AmE), / vəʊɡ / (BrE)
The prevailing fashion or general popular taste at a particular time.

Examples

These were the years when people flocked to Manchester not only to play golf, which had come into vogue, but also to witness the Ekwanok Country Club tournaments.
She sang him Scott's charming ballad "Rosabelle ", which was the vogue of the moment.

Synset Examples

leather is the latest vogue
he followed current trends
the 1920s had a style of their own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • Synonym: fashion - a currently popular style or practice, especially in clothing
  • Degree Lower: fad - an intense and widely shared enthusiasm for something, especially one short-lived

Etymology

From French 'vogue', meaning 'popular success or fashion', originally 'wave, course, direction'.
