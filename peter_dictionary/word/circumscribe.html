circumscribe

1. VERB

/ ˌsɝːkəmˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to draw a line around or enclose within boundaries, especially in geometry to construct a figure around another so that every vertex of the enclosed figure touches the enclosing one

Examples

This theorem is similar to the theorem of Kakutani that there exists a circumscribing cube around any closed, bounded convex set in **f.

Synset Examples

He drew a circle around the points

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inscribe - draw or write inside a shape, or enclose within another shape

Etymology

From Latin 'circumscribere' (to draw around), from 'circum-' (around) and 'scribere' (to write).

2. VERB

/ sɝː.kəmˌskraɪb / (AmE), / sɜː.kəmˌskraɪb / (BrE)
to limit or restrict something within boundaries; to confine narrowly

Examples

Instead of isolating the literal flatness by specifying and circumscribing it, the pasted paper or cloth releases and spreads it, and the artist seems to have nothing left but this undepicted flatness with which to finish as well as start his picture.

Synset Examples

I limit you to two visits to the pub a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - to set boundaries or restrictions
  • Synonym: restrict - to keep within limits
  • Antonym: expand - to increase in size, area, or scope

Etymology

From Latin 'circumscribere' (to draw a circle around), from 'circum-' (around) + 'scribere' (to write).
