microsomal

1. ADJ

/ maɪ.krəˈsoʊ.məl / (AmE), / mʌɪ.krəˈsəʊ.məl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from microsomes, which are small vesicular fragments of endoplasmic reticulum found in cells, especially in biochemistry or cell biology contexts.

Examples

A microsomal de-iodinase with a pH optimum of around 8, and requiring reduced triphosphopyridine nucleotide for activity, has been identified in the thyroid (Stanbury, 1957).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microsome - a small vesicle derived from fragmented endoplasmic reticulum in cells

Etymology

From 'microsome' (micro- + -some) + -al (adjectival suffix).
