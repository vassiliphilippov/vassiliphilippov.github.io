ethics

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪks / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪks / (BrE)
Moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity; motivation or system based on ideas of right and wrong.

Examples

Broad and Ross have lately contended that this fitness is one of the main facts of ethics, and I suspect they are right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
  • Antonym: immorality - the state or quality of being immoral; wickedness

Etymology

From Greek 'ethikos' (ethical), from 'ēthos' (character).
