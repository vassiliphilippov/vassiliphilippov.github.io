Plato

1. PROPN

/ ˈpleɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪtəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 428–347 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher, pupil of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle, known for his influential works on philosophy and for founding the Academy in Athens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aristotle - Ancient Greek philosopher and student of Plato

Etymology

From Greek Πλάτων (Plátōn), meaning 'broad' or 'wide', possibly referring to his broad shoulders.
