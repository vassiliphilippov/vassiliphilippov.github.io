silver

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlvər / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
Having the white, shiny, metallic sheen of silver; silvery in appearance.

Examples

Shaw and Skorich headed a group of players, coaches and team officials who received an engrossed copy of an official city citation and a pair of silver cufflinks shaped like a football.
The stethoscope glinted silver in the darkening room.
There was a blur just under my focus of vision, a crash; the car's far windshield panel turned into a silver web with a dark hole in the center.

Synset Examples

a land of silver (or silvern) rivers where the salmon leap
repeated scrubbings have given the wood a silvery sheen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silvery - having the appearance or qualities of silver

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvər / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
coins made of silver, especially collective money in the form of silver coins.

Examples

As Hartweger accepted his silver bowl, he said, "I want to thank coach's wife for talking him into letting me play ".
When he had given the call a few moments thought, he went into the kitchen to ask Mrs. Yamata to prepare tea and sushi for the visitors, using the formal English china and the silver tea service which had been donated to the mission, then he went outside to inspect the grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin - a flat, typically round piece of metal used as money
  • Degree Lower: bullion - gold or silver in bulk before coining, or valued by weight

Etymology

From Old English 'seolfor', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'zilver', German 'Silber'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
(chiefly US) Eating utensils made of or coated with silver, such as knives, forks, and spoons; silverware.

Examples

In their search for what turned out to be the right breakfast china but the wrong table silver, they opened every cupboard door in the kitchen and pantry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverware - cutlery or utensils made of or plated with silver
  • Synonym: flatware - eating utensils, especially those for use at table (US usage)

Etymology

From Old English *seolfor*, from Proto-Germanic *silubrą*, referring to the metal silver.

5. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
A soft, shiny white precious metal (chemical symbol Ag) with the highest electrical and thermal conductivity of any metal, used in coins, jewelry, tableware, and photography.

Examples

There's no greater catastrophe in the universe, she reflected dourly, impaling tender green beans on the silver fork, than the dwindling away of a family.
In February 1801, Oersted did manage to experiment with physical apparatus and reported experiments made with a voltaic battery of 600 plates of zinc and silver and of later experiments with a battery of 60 plates of zinc and lead.

Etymology

From Old English *seolfor*, related to German *Silber* and Dutch *zilver*, ultimately of unknown origin.

Examples

That tall drink of water with the silver spoon up his ass.

Synset Examples

silvery hair

Related Terms
