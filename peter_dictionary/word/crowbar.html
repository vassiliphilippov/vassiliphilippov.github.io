crowbar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌbɑː / (BrE)
a heavy iron bar with a flattened end, used as a lever for prying things apart

Examples

In fact, I hear the bite reflex is so strong, they have to pry the victim's jaws open... with a crowbar.

Etymology

From 'crow' (the bird) + 'bar,' the shape of the curved end resembling a crow's beak.
