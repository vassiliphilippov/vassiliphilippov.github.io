fecund

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːkənd, fəˈkənd / (AmE), / ˈfiːkənd, fɪˈkʌnd / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or plants) capable of producing offspring, vegetation, or new growth; fertile.

Examples

The air took on a special strength now that they 'd left the fecund warmth of the farmland behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertile - producing or able to produce abundant vegetation or offspring
  • Antonym: infertile - incapable of producing offspring or vegetation

Etymology

From Latin fecundus (fruitful, fertile).
