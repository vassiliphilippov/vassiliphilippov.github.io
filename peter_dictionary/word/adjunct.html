adjunct

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt / (BrE)
a thing added to something else as a supplementary or non-essential part

Examples

It is obvious that the historian who seeks to recapture the ideas that have motivated human behavior throughout a given period will find the art and literature of that age one of his central and major concerns, by no means a mere supplement or adjunct of significant historical research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - a thing which can be added to something else to make it more useful or attractive, but is not essential
  • Antonym: essential - something absolutely necessary; extremely important

Etymology

From Latin adjunctus, past participle of adiungere ‘to join to’.
