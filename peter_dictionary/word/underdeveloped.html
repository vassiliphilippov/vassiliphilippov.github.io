underdeveloped

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚdɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / ˌʌndədɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
(of a country or region) having a low level of industrialization, technology, and economic development, often due to a shortage of capital.

Examples

It follows that any effective policy toward the underdeveloped countries must have a realistically long working horizon.
It has erupted on the national level in the matter of including birth-control information and material in foreign aid to underdeveloped countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developing - (of a country) in the process of becoming more economically advanced
  • Antonym: developed - (of a country) having advanced industrial and economic activity

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'insufficiently') + 'developed.'
