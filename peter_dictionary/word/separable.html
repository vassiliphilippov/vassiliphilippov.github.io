separable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛpərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərəbəl / (BrE)
able to be separated or divided from something else.

Examples

The idea of community and the idea of spirit are two distinct and separable ideas.
The two have never been separable.

Synset Examples

often drugs and crime are not dissociable
the siamese twins were not considered separable
a song...never conceived of as severable from the melody

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inseparable - unable to be separated or divided from something else

Etymology

From Medieval Latin separabilis, from Latin separare (to separate).
