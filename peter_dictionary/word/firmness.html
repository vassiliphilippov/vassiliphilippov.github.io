firmness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of muscle tissue being taut, resilient, and healthy; proper muscle tone

Examples

Though his character is broader and more comically rounded than the don, he gives it a firmness and toughness - a sort of peasant dignity - too.

Synset Examples

his muscular firmness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flaccidity - lack of muscle tone or firmness, limpness
  • Synonym: tone - the normal degree of vigor and tension in muscle

Etymology

From firm + -ness, ultimately from Latin firmus (strong, stable).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being resolute, determined, and unwavering in purpose or opinion.

Examples

The very firmness of her convictions and logical clearness of her arguments captivated and stimulated him to make greater efforts; usually, this is most exasperating to men, who expect every woman to verify their preconceived notions concerning her sex, and when she does not, immediately condemn her as eccentric and unwomanly.

Synset Examples

his resoluteness carried him through the battle
it was his unshakeable resolution to finish the work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resoluteness - quality of being resolved or determined
  • Synonym: determination - quality of being firm in purpose
  • Antonym: wavering - lack of firmness; being indecisive

Etymology

From Old French 'fermeté', from Latin 'firmitas', from 'firmus' (strong, steadfast).
