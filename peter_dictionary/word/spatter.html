spatter

1. VERB

/ ˈspætər / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
to splash or dash drops of a liquid onto a surface or object, often in small particles

Examples

It should have been nearly as easy for her to remember that as it was for Big Hans to remember going after the axe while he was still spattered with Pa's yellow sick insides.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to strike or fall on something in drops or a mass
  • Synonym: spray - to scatter liquid in tiny drops over an area

Etymology

Perhaps related to Dutch spatten (to splash) or German spatten (to splash).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspætər / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
an act or instance of splashing a liquid or small particles onto a surface

Examples

During the rest of the summer my scholarly mania for making plaster casts and spatter prints of Catskill flowers and leaves was all but surpassed by the constantly renewed impressions of Jessica that my mind served up to me for contemplation and delight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - the act of liquid falling on something in drops or a short burst
  • Synonym: spray - a mass of small drops of liquid sent through the air

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, related to 'spit' or 'spat'. Similar forms found in other Germanic languages.
