long-shanked

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ ˈʃæŋkt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈʃæŋkt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having long legs.

Examples

Eyes swerved in the patronne's head, Alex coughed loudly, and the doctor, with a sforzando of chicken noises floating behind him, took to the stairs in long shanked leaps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leggy - having long legs

Etymology

From 'long' + 'shank', where 'shank' refers to the part of the leg between the knee and ankle.
