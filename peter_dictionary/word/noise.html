noise

1. NOUN

/ nɔɪz / (AmE), / nɔɪz / (BrE)
A sound, especially one that is loud, unpleasant, or lacks a clear musical quality.

Examples

The orchestra is far enough away from you that you miss the bow scrapes, valve clicks, and other noises incidental to playing.
Though Americans usually lived in groups segregated by national origin or religious belief, they liked to work and shop in the noise and vitality of downtown.
"It's safe ", Prevot had said, "and it provides cover for our noise ".
There was a hell of a noise this time of morning.
He looked for the source of the noise that had awakened him * * h It was that prick Coughlin.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed the street noises
they heard indistinct noises of people talking
during the firework display that ended the gala the noise reached 98 decibels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - vibrations perceived by the ear
  • Antonym: silence - absence of sound

Etymology

From Old French *noise* (quarrel, brawl; noise), perhaps from Latin *nausea* (seasickness), influenced by medieval Latin uses.

2. NOUN

/ nɔɪz / (AmE), / nɔɪz / (BrE)
A sound, especially one that is loud, unpleasant, or lacks musical quality; a disagreeable auditory experience.

Examples

He opened it a crack and in doing so made as much shuffling, coughing, and scraping noise as possible in order to drown emanations from the hen who had begun to protest.

Synset Examples

modern music is just noise to me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - a particular auditory impression; noise, tone, or speech
  • Antonym: music - pleasant or harmonious sound

Etymology

From Old French 'noise' (quarrel, noise), from Latin 'nausea' (sea-sickness, disgust).
