tramp

1. VERB

/ træmp / (AmE), / træmp / (BrE)
to walk or travel with heavy or noisy steps, especially over a long distance or as part of an expedition

Examples

He tramped out of the Miners Rest with his hopes plummeting, and headed doggedly for the Palace Saloon, the last place of any consequence on this side of the street.
Eleven men, a woman and a teen-age boy tramped over cold, damp, fog-enshrouded ground during a two-hour field drill in the problems of guerrilla warfare.

Synset Examples

We went tramping about the state of Colorado

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trek - to make a long, arduous journey on foot
  • Synonym: march - to walk with regular steps as a group or in a determined way
  • Degree Lower: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
  • Synonym: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps, often loudly

Etymology

From Middle English trampe (to stamp, walk heavily), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch trampen and German trampen.

2. NOUN

/ træmp / (AmE), / tramp / (BrE)
A person with no home or job who moves from place to place, often regarded as disreputable; a vagrant.

Examples

It is not having his baby nestled warm and fat against your breast and it is not having somebody that really gives a damn whether some tramp cracks your skull.

Synset Examples

a homeless tramp
he tried to help the really down-and-out bums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home who moves from place to place
  • Synonym: beggar - a person who lives by asking for money or food

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German trampen (to stamp, walk heavily).

3. NOUN

/ træmp / (AmE), / træmp / (BrE)
A woman who is considered to have many casual sexual partners; a promiscuous woman (often used as an insult).

Examples

For a no-good tramp!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slut - a woman considered to engage in many casual sexual relationships
  • Antonym: prude - a person who claims to be easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity
