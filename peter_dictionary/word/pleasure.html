pleasure

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛʒər / (AmE), / ˈplɛʒə / (BrE)
A feeling of enjoyment, satisfaction, or happiness.

Examples

The conversation of the characters creates an atmosphere suggesting the usual mixture of pleasures, foibles, irritations, and concerns which would characterize the common life of a normal village in any age.

He was again tingling with pleasure, seeing himself clearly in Slater's shoes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure; extreme satisfaction or joy
  • Antonym: displeasure - the feeling of being annoyed or dissatisfied
  • Synonym: enjoyment - the state or process of taking pleasure in something

Etymology

From Old French 'plesir', from Latin 'placere' (to please).
