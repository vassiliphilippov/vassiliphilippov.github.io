monumental

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnjəˈmɛntl̩ / (AmE), / ˌmɒnjʊˈmɛntl̩ / (BrE)
Relating to or serving as a monument; having the character or significance of a monument, especially in being large, enduring, or commemorative.

Examples

Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.

Synset Examples

the use of the arch in monumental architecture
monumental sculptures

Etymology

From Latin 'monumentalis', from 'monumentum' (monument).
