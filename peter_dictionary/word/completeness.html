completeness

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplitnəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːtnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being complete; having all parts or elements; being whole or entire

Examples

The sweep of space, the delicate counterbalance of the white masses, the over-all completeness and unity, the originality and imagination, all entitle it to be called an authentic masterpiece.
The completeness of the connections provide that, for N people, there are * * f lines of communication between the pairs, which can become a large number (1225) for a party of fifty guests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompleteness - the state of not being complete; lacking parts or elements
  • Synonym: wholeness - the quality of being whole or entire

Etymology

From late Latin 'completus' (filled up, complete) + English suffix '-ness'.
