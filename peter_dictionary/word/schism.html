schism

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪzəm, ˈsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪz.əm, ˈskɪz.əm / (BrE)
A strong division or separation within a group, organization, or religious body into opposing factions, typically due to differences in opinion or belief.

Examples

The motifs of impersonal interdependence and insulation of residential communities have polarized; the schism between central city and suburb, Negro and White, blue collar and white collar can be viewed as symptomatic of this deeper polarization of trends in the metropolis.

Synset Examples

another schism like that and they will wind up in bankruptcy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart
  • Antonym: union - the act of joining together or the state of being united

Etymology

From Greek 'skhisma' (division, cleft), via Latin 'schisma'.
