confer

1. VERB

/ kənˈfɜr / (AmE), / kənˈfɜː / (BrE)
to discuss something with other people, especially in order to reach a decision

Examples

But as November 1924 drew close the Democratic hierarchy was sorely troubled by grapevine reports that O' Banion was being wooed by the opposition, and was meeting and conferring with important Republicans.
I hoped that we could come here and reason together.

Synset Examples

We conferred about a plan of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consult - to seek advice or information from someone
  • Synonym: deliberate - to consider carefully or discuss before making a decision

Etymology

From Latin conferre, meaning 'to bring together'.
