aide

1. NOUN

/ eɪd / (AmE), / eɪd / (BrE)
an officer who acts as a military assistant to a more senior officer, especially in the armed forces

Examples

Then she took iced lemonade to Marsh's young aide where he sat in the cool of the big trees around the flower garden.
When Marsh called to his aide and the pair rode off down the River Road where the gentians burned blue, Juanita was shaken and trying not to cry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjutant - an officer assisting a more senior officer

Etymology

From French 'aide' (help, assistant), from Old French 'aidier' (to help).

2. NOUN

/ eɪd / (AmE), / eɪd / (BrE)
a person whose job is to assist someone, especially an important or senior official

Examples

As the afternoon sped toward evening, the suite saw a steady procession of Paxton aides pass in and out, each with his own special problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone else in a job, especially by doing less important work

Etymology

From French 'aide' (help, helper), from Latin 'adiutus', past participle of 'adiuvare' (to help).
