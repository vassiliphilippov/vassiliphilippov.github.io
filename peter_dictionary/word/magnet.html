magnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnət / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪt / (BrE)
A piece of iron or other material that produces a magnetic field, attracting certain metals such as iron or steel.

Examples

Like a particle drawn to a magnet he returned to that which was pressing so hard in his mind.
An attempt should be made to see if electricity, in its most latent stage, has any action on the magnet as such ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: electromagnet - a type of magnet in which the magnetic field is produced by electric current

Etymology

From Greek 'magnētis (lithos)' meaning 'Magnesian stone', referring to lodestone from Magnesia in Thessaly.
