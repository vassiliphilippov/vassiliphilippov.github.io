discontent

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
A feeling of dissatisfaction with the present situation and a desire for something better.

Examples

The careless writing was in keeping with his mood of savage discontent.
There was a certain discontent among the cardinals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction
  • Synonym: dissatisfaction - lack of satisfaction

Etymology

From Middle French 'descontent', from Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'contentus' (satisfied).
