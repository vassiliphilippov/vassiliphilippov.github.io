professorship

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfɛsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / prəˈfɛsəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, rank, or post of a professor at a university or college

Examples

In 1806 his ambition was realized and he became professor of physics at the Copenhagen University, though not realizing full professorship (ordinarius) until 1817.

Synset Examples

he was awarded an endowed chair in economics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chair - the position of a professor, especially one with an endowed post

Etymology

Formed from 'professor' + '-ship', denoting rank or office.
