inadvertence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnədˈvɜrtəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnədˈvɜːtəns / (BrE)
The act of failing to pay attention or take notice, resulting in an unintentional omission or mistake.

Examples

What I am suggesting is that when we delay, or when we fail to act, we do so intentionally and not through inadvertence or through bureaucratic or procedural difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oversight - an unintentional failure to notice or do something

Etymology

From Latin 'inadvertentia', from 'in-' (not) + 'advertere' (to turn toward).
