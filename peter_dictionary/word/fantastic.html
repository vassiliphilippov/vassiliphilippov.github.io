fantastic

1. ADJ

/ fænˈtæstɪk / (AmE), / fanˈtastɪk / (BrE)
Extremely good or impressive; excellent.

Examples

The crowd staged its own mad scene in salvos of cheers and applause and finally a standing ovation as Miss Sutherland took curtain call after curtain call following a fantastic "Mad Scene "created on her own and with the help of the composer and the other performers.
With Ritter he was exposed to the fantastic profusion of ideas that stormed through his host's fertile but disorganized mind.
Now, does that strike you as a fantastic coincidence, Mr. Dufresne, or is it just me?

Synset Examples

a fantastic trip to the Orient
the film was fantastic!
a howling success
a marvelous collection of rare books
had a rattling conversation about politics
a tremendous achievement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marvelous - extremely good or impressive
  • Synonym: tremendous - very great in amount, scale, or intensity
  • Synonym: amazing - causing great surprise or wonder; astonishing
  • Antonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant

Etymology

From Late Latin *phantasticus* (imaginary), from Greek *phantastikos* (able to create mental images).

2. ADJ

/ fænˈtæstɪk / (AmE), / fanˈtastɪk / (BrE)
Unrealistic or absurd, based on imagination rather than reality; foolish or wildly improbable.

Examples

Dreams that display events of the future with photographic detail call for a theory explaining their basic mystery and all its components, including that weird feeling of deja vu, inevitably fantastic though that theory must seem.
Which theory is more fantastic: 1.

Synset Examples

a fantastic idea of his own importance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realistic - having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected

Etymology

From Greek phantastikos (“able to imagine, create images”), via Latin and Old French.

existing only in the imagination; not real; imaginary

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French *fantastique*, from Latin *phantasticus*, from Greek *phantastikos* ('able to create images, imaginary').

4. ADJ

/ fænˈtæstɪk / (AmE), / fanˈtastɪk / (BrE)
Strange or bizarre in a way that is striking or ludicrously odd.

Examples

It was fantastic to turn from the seven men in shackles to the wardroom, where a class of apprentices awaited him.

Synset Examples

Hamlet's assumed antic disposition
fantastic Halloween costumes
a grotesque reflection in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grotesque - comically or repulsively ugly or distorted
  • Synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual, especially so as to cause interest or amusement
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Middle English *fantastik*, from Old French *fantastique*, from Latin *phantasticus* (imaginary), from Greek *phantastikos* (able to imagine).
