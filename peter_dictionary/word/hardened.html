hardened

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdənd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd(ə)nd / (BrE)
Having developed a firm or unfeeling attitude as a result of experience, especially of hardship or suffering; emotionally tough or insensitive.

Examples

He knew the faces of all, hairy or shaven, old or young, fat or thin, suffering or hardened, sad or gay, good or bad.

Synset Examples

faced a case-hardened judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling

Etymology

From the verb 'harden' + -ed; 'harden' from Old English 'heardian', from 'heard' (hard).
