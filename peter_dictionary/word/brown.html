brown

1. ADJ

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
of a color similar to that of wood or earth; having a shade ranging from dark yellowish to dark reddish-brown

Examples

His soil was "nothing special ", just prairie land, but he had harrowed in compost until it was loose, spongy and brown black.
Macrophages laden with brown pigment were seen in some of the alveoli, and the intima of some of the small arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue.
The liver weighed 2090 gm., was brown in color, and the cut surface was mottled by irregular pale areas.
Some liver cord cells contained vacuolated cytoplasm, while others had small amounts of brown hemosiderin pigment.
The gallbladder contained about 40 cc. of green brown bile and 3 smooth, dark-green calculi measuring up to 1 cm. in diameter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tan - pale brown color
  • Degree Higher: dark brown - deeper, richer brown color
  • Degree Higher: black - the darkest color, absence of light
  • Degree Lower: beige - light brown color

Etymology

From Old English brūn (brown, dusky, dark), from Proto-Germanic *brūnaz.

2. NOUN

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
A color produced by mixing red, yellow, and black, resembling that of dark wood or rich earth.

Examples

Big and dark, a melting, golden brown.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Lower: tan - a pale tone of brown
  • Degree Higher: auburn - a reddish-brown color, often used of hair
  • Degree Higher: mahogany - a rich reddish-brown color, especially of wood

Etymology

From Old English *brūn* (brown, shining), from Proto-Germanic *brūnaz*.

3. VERB

/ braʊn / (AmE), / braʊn / (BrE)
to cook food, especially in a pan, until it becomes brown in color

Examples

Watch closely while browning them, as it does n't take long.

Synset Examples

brown the meat in the pan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sear - to brown the surface of food quickly with intense heat
  • Synonym: roast - to cook food, especially meat, with dry heat, often until brown
