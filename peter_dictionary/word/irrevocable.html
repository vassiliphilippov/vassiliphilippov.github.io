irrevocable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛvəkəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvəkəbl / (BrE)
Impossible to change or reverse; cannot be undone or revoked.

Examples

Moreover, the law of the land is not irrevocable; it can be changed; it has been, many times.

Synset Examples

firm and irrevocable is my doom

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reversible - capable of being reversed or changed
  • Synonym: final - coming at the end; not able to be changed

Etymology

From Latin 'irrevocabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'revocabilis' (able to be recalled).
