petrify

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
To cause (someone or something) to become rigid, motionless, or stunned as if turned to stone, often from fear or shock.

Examples

His broad flat fingers, petrified into massive sausages by years of bricklaying, snatched the bills clumsily.

Synset Examples

The horror petrified his feelings
Fear petrified her thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralyze - to make unable to move or act
  • Synonym: stupefy - to make unable to think or feel properly
  • Antonym: animate - to give life or movement to

Etymology

From French pétrifier, from Latin petra (rock) + -fy (to make).
