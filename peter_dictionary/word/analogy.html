analogy

1. NOUN

/ əˈnælədʒi / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
a process of reasoning or inference in which similarities between two or more things are used to suggest that they are alike in other respects as well

Examples

by analogy, the church also has been regarded as entirely independent of the "world "in the sense of requiring nothing from it in order to be the church.
The situation with regard to our attitude and "control "of disease contains close analogies to problems confronting us with respect to people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simile - a figure of speech comparing two things using 'like' or 'as'
  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From Greek analogia (proportion), from ana- (upon, according to) + logos (reason, proportion).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnælədʒi / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
A comparison between two things, typically for the purpose of explanation or clarification by showing their similarities.

Examples

This hypothesis is evolved in analogy to the demonstrated action of organic actives in detergency.
One could also add to these analogies that steel loses its magnetism by heat, which proves that steel becomes a better conductor through a rise in temperature, just as electrical bodies do.

Synset Examples

the operation of a computer presents and interesting analogy to the working of the brain
the models show by analogy how matter is built up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simile - a figure of speech comparing two different things using 'like' or 'as'
  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From Greek 'analogia', meaning 'proportion' or 'correspondence'.
