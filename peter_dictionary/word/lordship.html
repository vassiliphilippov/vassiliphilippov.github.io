Lordship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdʃɪp / (BrE)
a title used to address or refer to a British peer below the rank of duke, and extended as a title of respect to a bishop or judge

Examples

She was told by the manservant who opened the door that his lordship was engaged on work from which he had left strict orders he was not to be disturbed.
His lordship was concerned in the management of Drury Lane but, if there were no opportunities there, would he read and criticize her novel?

Synset Examples

Your Lordship
His Lordship

Etymology

From Middle English lordship, from Old English *hlāfordscip* (authority of a lord), from hlāford (lord) + -scip (-ship, condition or office).
