leak

1. NOUN

/ lik / (AmE), / liːk / (BrE)
An accidental hole or crack in something that allows a fluid, gas, or light to enter or escape.

Examples

Just like the balloon would go up and you could sit all day and wish it would spring a leak or blow to hell up and burn and nothing like that would happen.

Synset Examples

one of the tires developed a leak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hole - an opening in or through something

Etymology

Possibly from Middle Dutch *lecken* or Old Norse *leka* (to drip, leak).

2. VERB

/ lik / (AmE), / liːk / (BrE)
to intentionally disclose secret or confidential information to the public or to the press, usually without authorization

Examples

Soon after Loper leaked the news that Frankie had ordered "two of everything "just "in case he spills anything ", Frankie got so mad at the chic designer that he vowed he would not wear a stitch of Loper clothing.

Synset Examples

The news were leaked to the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divulge - to make known (private or sensitive information)
  • Synonym: reveal - to make (information) known to others

3. NOUN

/ lik / (AmE), / liːk / (BrE)
(informal) An act of urinating; a pee.

Examples

Then you go to take a leak.

Synset Examples

he had to take a leak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pee - urination (informal)
  • Synonym: urinate - to discharge urine
