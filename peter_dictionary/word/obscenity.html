obscenity

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛnəti / (AmE), / əbˈsiːnəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being obscene; offensive or indecent behavior, language, or acts.

Examples

When they stood about his tent, chaffing each other, exchanging their obscenities, cursing command or weather, he had studied their faces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decency - the quality of being decent or proper
  • Synonym: vulgarity - the quality of being crude or coarse

Etymology

From Latin obscēnitās (indecency), from obscēnus (obscene).
