disruptive

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
Causing or tending to cause disorder, disturbance, or interruption; characterized by unrest or insubordination.

Examples

Nevertheless, with foresight and careful planning, some of the more disruptive and dangerous consequences of social change which have troubled other countries passing through this stage can be escaped.
As an isolated policy, land reform is likely to be politically disruptive; as part of a larger development effort, however, it may gain wide acceptance.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - well-behaved or arranged; free from disturbance
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion

Etymology

From Latin 'disruptus', past participle of 'disrumpere' (to break apart).
