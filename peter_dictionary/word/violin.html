violin

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlɪn / (AmE), / vaɪəˈlɪn / (BrE)
A bowed stringed musical instrument, typically with four strings tuned in perfect fifths, that is the highest member of the violin family and is played with a bow.

It is not news that Nathan Milstein is a wizard of the violin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiddle - another term for violin, especially used in folk music

Etymology

From Italian 'violino', diminutive of 'viola'.
