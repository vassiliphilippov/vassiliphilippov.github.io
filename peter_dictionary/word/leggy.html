leggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛɡi / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡi / (BrE)
(Of a plant) having long, thin, and often weak stems, usually as a result of not enough light or excessive growth.

Examples

The day will come, in midsummer, when you find your plants becoming "leggy ", running to tall-growing foliage at the expense of blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spindly - (of a plant or stem) long, thin, and weak-looking

Etymology

From 'leg' + -y, meaning 'having (long) legs.'
