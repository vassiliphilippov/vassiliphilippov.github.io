warhorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌhɔːs / (BrE)
A work of art, such as a musical composition, play, or literary piece, that is frequently performed and has become overly familiar or hackneyed due to repeated use.

Examples

He dropped his earlier and delightful hamming, which is about the only way to handle the old war horse called "Charley's Aunt ", and let himself go with as an appealing an "Amy "as anybody could ask.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: classic - an outstanding example of a particular style; something of enduring value

Etymology

Originally referred to a horse used in war; figurative use extended to art or music that is long-serving and frequently used.
