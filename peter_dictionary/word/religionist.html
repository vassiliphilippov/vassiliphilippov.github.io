religionist

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who is strongly devoted to religion or has zealous religious beliefs and practices

Examples

Even the non church members - the freewheelers, marginal religionists and so on - have the values of Christian civilization internalized in them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealot - a person who is fanatical and uncompromising in pursuit of their religious ideals
  • Antonym: secularist - a person who advocates separation of religion from civic affairs and government

Etymology

Formed from 'religion' + '-ist', a person who practices or is concerned with something.
