shotgun

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑtˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈʃɒtˌɡʌn / (BrE)
A firearm with a smoothbore barrel, typically fired from the shoulder and designed to shoot a spread of small projectiles (shot), often used for hunting or sport at short range.

Examples

But even on these, the palette often takes over while the shotgun cools off!
Also, he thought, I doubt if she could hit the side of a barn with a shotgun.
In Sicily women are more dangerous than shotguns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rifle - a gun with a grooved barrel for firing bullets accurately at longer distances
  • Antonym: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand

Etymology

From 'shot' + 'gun', referring to a gun firing multiple pellets (shot).
