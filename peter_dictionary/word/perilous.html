perilous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛrələs / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪləs / (BrE)
Full of danger or risk; very hazardous.

Examples

He sensed something precious in the perilous moment, something akin to the knowledge gained on his bicycle trip through the French countryside, a knowledge imprisoned in speechlessness.
Ahead were perilous, ice filled waters.

Synset Examples

dangerous waters
a parlous journey on stormy seas
a perilous voyage across the Atlantic in a small boat
the precarious life of an undersea diver
dangerous surgery followed by a touch-and-go recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - free from harm or risk

Etymology

From Old French *perillous*, from Latin *periculosus* (dangerous), from *periculum* (danger).
