sort

1. NOUN

/ sɔrt / (AmE), / sɔːt / (BrE)
a category of things or people sharing a common characteristic; a type or kind

Examples

"You often hear people talk about team spirit and that sort of thing ", Benington said in a conversation after the ceremonies, "but what this team had was a little different.
The storyline, in sort, is wildly unrealistic.
Since the hero, a sterling and upright fellow, is a rich Brown senior, while two Yalies are cast as virtual rapists, I suppose I should disqualify myself from sitting in judgment on "Where the Boys are ", but I shall do nothing of the sort.
Records sound like records because they provide a different sort of experience than live music.
It is this sort of experience that makes the concept of high fidelity of real musical significance for the home music listener.

Synset Examples

sculpture is a form of art
what kinds of desserts are there?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kind - a group of people or things having similar characteristics; type
  • Synonym: type - a category or group with shared characteristics
  • Synonym: class - a set or category of things having some property or attribute in common

Etymology

From Middle French *sorte* (kind, class), from Latin *sortem* (lot, fate, kind).

2. NOUN

/ sɔrt / (AmE), / sɔːt / (BrE)

Examples

Calenda was not the sort who walked around without one of his "boys "close at hand.

Synset Examples

what sort of person is he?

Etymology

From Old French *sorte* (lot, kind), from Latin *sors* (lot, fate).

3. NOUN

/ sɔrt / (AmE), / sɔːt / (BrE)
an act or process of arranging or separating things into groups according to a particular criterion

Examples

I will sort everything out.

Synset Examples

the bottleneck in mail delivery is the process of sorting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classify - to arrange or organize by classes; to categorize
  • Synonym: group - to put things together in a group
  • Antonym: mix - to combine or blend into one mass

Etymology

From Old French *sort* (fate, kind), from Latin *sortem* (lot, fate, share).
