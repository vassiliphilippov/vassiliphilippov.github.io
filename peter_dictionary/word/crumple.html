crumple

1. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) to break into pieces or collapse, often suddenly or completely, especially as a result of external force.

Examples

It was partially cemented by ages and pressure, yet it crumpled before the onslaught of the powerful streams, the force of a thousand fire hoses, and with the gold it held washed down through the long sluices.

Synset Examples

the building crumbled after the explosion
Negotiations broke down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crumble - to break or fall apart into small fragments

Etymology

Possibly a frequentative of 'crump', from Middle English 'crumpe' (to bend, curl up), of Germanic origin.
