shrug

1. VERB

/ ʃrʌɡ / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ / (BrE)
to raise and lower one's shoulders slightly, often to express indifference, doubt, or lack of knowledge

Examples

Scotty shrugged slightly.
Andrei shrugged.
He shrugged.
Brenner shrugged carelessly.
Phil shrugged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrug off - dismiss something as unimportant

Etymology

Of obscure origin, possibly imitative or related to Old Norse 'skrugga' (to shudder).

2. NOUN

/ ʃrʌɡ / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ / (BrE)
An act of raising and lowering the shoulders, typically as a gesture expressing indifference, doubt, or lack of knowledge.

Examples

Shrugs met that, from room clerks, from bellhops.
Shrugs met that.
Then, with a shrug of pretended indifference, she took a compact from her purse and went through the motions of fixing her make-up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrug off - to dismiss or treat as unimportant

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, referring to the motion involved.
