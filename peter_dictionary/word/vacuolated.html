vacuolated

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæk.juˌoʊ.leɪ.t̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juː.ə.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) containing one or more vacuoles or small membrane-bound cavities within the cytoplasm.

Examples

Some liver cord cells contained vacuolated cytoplasm, while others had small amounts of brown hemosiderin pigment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuole - a small cavity or sac in the cytoplasm of a cell

Etymology

From Latin vacuolatus, from vacuola (small cavity) + -ated.
