bulge

1. NOUN

/ bʌldʒ / (AmE), / bʌldʒ / (BrE)
a rounded swelling or protruding part that sticks out from a surface or surrounding area

Examples

Arkansas combined 280 yards rushing with 64 yards passing (on 5 completions in 7 tosses) and a tough defense to whip TCU, and A+M, with a 38 - point bulge against Texas Tech ran up its biggest total loop play since 1950.
Alex entered first and was followed by the doctor who, for all his care, manifested a perceptible bulge on his left side where the hen was cradled.
She would try to see over the bulge of her cheeks and somewhat under her teeth to the place where she was biting.

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protuberance - something that sticks out from a surface
  • Antonym: depression - a sunken or hollow area in a surface

Etymology

Probably from Middle French *bouge* (leather bag, swelling), from Latin *bulga* (leather bag, knapsack).

2. VERB

/ bʌldʒ / (AmE), / bʌldʒ / (BrE)
to swell or protrude outward in a rounded shape

Examples

The walls bulged, the floor trembled, the windowpanes rattled.
The clouds bulged downward and burst suddenly into a great black funnel.

Synset Examples

His stomach bulged after the huge meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protrude - to extend beyond or above a surface
  • Antonym: recede - to move back or become less prominent

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'bulgen', from Old French 'bouge' (leather bag, swelling)
