whiten

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtən / (BrE)
to become white or make something white or whiter

Examples

Johnson's left hand was pressed against the side of his head, red cheeks whitening beneath his fingers.

Synset Examples

This detergent will whiten your laundry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - to make white or lighter in color, especially by chemical means
  • Antonym: blacken - to make or become black or darker

Etymology

From Middle English 'whiten', from Old English 'hwītan', from 'hwīt' (white).
