craggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkræɡi / (AmE), / ˈkræɡi / (BrE)
having a rugged, rough, or uneven surface, often with steep rocky features; resembling a crag, especially used to describe cliffs or facial features that are strongly marked or rugged.

Examples

You have your paper horn clutched in your big, craggy fist, and for your entrance you have planned a noisy, colorful and exuberant greeting to your friends and tenants.

Synset Examples

hilly terrain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rugged - having a rough, uneven surface or strong features

Etymology

From 'crag' (steep, rugged rock), plus the suffix '-y', meaning 'characterized by'.
