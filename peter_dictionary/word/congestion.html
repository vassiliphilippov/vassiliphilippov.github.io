congestion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
an excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a body part, causing swelling or impaired function

Examples

Microscopically, there was emphysema, fibrosis, and vascular congestion.

Etymology

From Latin 'congestio' (a heaping up), from 'congerere' (to heap together).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
the state of being excessively crowded or full, especially with people or vehicles

Examples

Makes for confusion and congestion ".

Synset Examples

traffic congestion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcrowding - the condition of being too crowded

Etymology

From Latin 'congestio', from 'congerere' meaning 'to bring together'.
