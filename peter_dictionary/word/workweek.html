workweek

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌwiːk / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌwiːk / (BrE)
The number of hours or days of work in a calendar week, usually referring to the regular period that employees are expected to work.

Examples

Among them, according to the U. S. Department of Commerce, are: (1) shorter work weeks, (2) higher pay, (3) longer paid vacations, (4) better transportation, (5) earlier retirement, and (6) more education.

Synset Examples

they worked a 40-hour week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working week - The period each week during which one is expected to work (British usage)

Etymology

Formed from 'work' + 'week' in American English, early 20th century.
