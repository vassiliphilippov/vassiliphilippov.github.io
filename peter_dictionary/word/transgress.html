transgress

1. VERB

/ trænzˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / trænzˈɡres / (BrE)
to go beyond or break a law, rule, or moral code; to violate a boundary or limit

Examples

We know that in the early part of the century many Protestant congregations took positive action against members who transgressed the ethical codes to which the majority subscribed.

Synset Examples

offend all laws of humanity
violate the basic laws or human civilization
break a law
break a promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violate - break or fail to comply with a rule, law, or agreement
  • Synonym: offend - commit an illegal act; break a law
  • Antonym: obey - comply with a law, rule, or command

Etymology

From Latin transgredi (to step across, go beyond), from trans- (across) + gradi (to step).
