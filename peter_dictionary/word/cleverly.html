cleverly

1. ADV

/ ˈklɛvɚli / (AmE), / ˈklɛvəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows intelligence, skill, or ingenuity.

Examples

However well chosen and cleverly arranged, such memorabilia unfortunately amounted to more of an interruption than an auxiliary to the evening's main business, which (considering the talent at hand) should probably have been the gathering of fresh samples of the Chicago style.

Synset Examples

they were cleverly arranged
a smartly managed business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skillfully - in a skillful manner
  • Antonym: stupidly - in a stupid manner; without cleverness or intelligence

Etymology

From 'clever' + '-ly', the standard English adverbial suffix.
