grate

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt / (BrE)
a frame of iron bars in a fireplace for holding burning fuel

Examples

"Seems to me last time I was here the grate bellowed out smoke as it might have been preparing us for hell ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireplace - an open recess in a wall for a fire

Etymology

From Old French *grater* (to scrape), related to the function of holding and separating fuel.
