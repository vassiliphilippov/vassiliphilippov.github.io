lockup

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌʌp / (BrE)
a small jail, typically in a local police station, used for holding people temporarily

Examples

Brannon said, "Now the key to the lockup, Marshal ".
The door of the lockup was of oak planks and banded with strap iron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime
  • Degree Higher: prison - a large building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while waiting for trial

Etymology

From 'lock' + 'up', indicating a place where people are locked up.
