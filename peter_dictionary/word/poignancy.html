poignancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪ.njən.si / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪ.njən.si / (BrE)
the quality of evoking a keen sense of sadness or deep emotional distress

Examples

But strength and poignancy develop thenceforth, and the windmill and deathbed episodes gather the threads of realization of the wonderfulness of the old boy.

Synset Examples

a moment of extraordinary poignancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathos - a quality that evokes pity or sadness
  • Antonym: merriment - state of cheerfulness or joy

Etymology

From Old French *poignance* (painfulness), from Latin *pungere* (to prick, sting).
