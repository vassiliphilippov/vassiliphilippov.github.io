portwatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtˌwɑtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtˌwɒtʃə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to act as a watchman on a wharf, typically overseeing security and activity at a port.

Examples

The portwatchers were retired longshoremen and small time seafarers off towboats and barges who acted as watchmen on the wharves.
One reason the portwatchers let Sposato take them over was to get the protection of his musclemen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchman - a person who guards or keeps watch

Etymology

From 'port' (harbor) + 'watcher' (one who watches or guards).
