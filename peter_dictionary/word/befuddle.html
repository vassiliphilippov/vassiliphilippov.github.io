befuddle

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfʌd.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈfʌd.əl / (BrE)
to make someone confused or unable to think clearly

Examples

It bewilders and befuddles him.

Synset Examples

These questions confuse even the experts
This question completely threw me
This question befuddled even the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or to mix things up
  • Synonym: bewilder - to completely confuse or puzzle someone
  • Antonym: clarify - to make something easier to understand or less confused

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + fuddle (to confuse or make drunk), probably from German dialect.
