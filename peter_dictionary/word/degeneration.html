degeneration

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of declining to a lower level of quality, power, or vitality.

Examples

If this be true, the possibility exists that an occlusive lesion of the bronchial arteries might cause widespread degeneration of supportive tissue similar to that seen in generalized emphysema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better

Etymology

From Latin degeneratio, from degenerare (to deteriorate, become worse).
