unhurried

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɜr.id / (AmE), / ʌnˈhʌr.ɪd / (BrE)
Not rushed; done or proceeding at a slow and relaxed pace without urgency.

Examples

They were both walking towards each other, unhurried.

Synset Examples

people strolling about in an unhurried way
an unhurried walk
spoke in a calm and unhurried voice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hurried - done or acting with excessive speed or urgency

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not', and 'hurried', past participle of 'hurry'.
