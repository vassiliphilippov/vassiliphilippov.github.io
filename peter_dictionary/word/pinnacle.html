pinnacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəkəl / (BrE)
(Architecture) A slender, pointed structure rising from the top of a buttress, tower, or roof, often ornamental, used especially in Gothic architecture.

Examples

Square corner - and gate posts were an open-work pattern of cast-iron foliage; they were topped by steeples complete in every detail: high pitched roof, pinnacle, and narrow gable.

Etymology

From Old French *pinnacle*, from Latin *pinnaculum*, diminutive of *pinna* (wing, battlement).
