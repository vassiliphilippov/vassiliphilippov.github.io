crab

1. NOUN

/ kræb / (AmE), / kræb / (BrE)
A marine or freshwater crustacean with a broad, flattened shell, short projecting eyes, a small abdomen folded under the thorax, and usually five pairs of legs, the first pair often modified as pincers.

Examples

Clams, crabs, and other marine life may add interest at coastal areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - large marine crustacean with long body and claws
  • Synonym: shrimp - small slender-bodied crustacean

Etymology

From Old English crabba, related to Old High German krabbo and Dutch krab.
