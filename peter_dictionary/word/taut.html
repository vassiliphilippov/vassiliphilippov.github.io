taut

1. ADJ

/ tɑt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
Stretched or pulled tightly; not slack.

Examples

Some time in 1912, Picasso cut out and folded a piece of paper in the shape of a guitar; to this he glued and fitted other pieces of paper and four taut strings, thus creating a sequence of flat surfaces in real and sculptural space to which there clung only the vestige of a picture plane.
He wondered if it wore the old anxiety, or the old, taut stoicism.
His head undulated gradually, covering space, to come straining beneath the taut belly within the warmth of those teats.
We were off the road, gleaming barbed wire pulling taut.

Synset Examples

taut sails
a tight drumhead
a tight rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tight - firmly held or stretched; not loose
  • Antonym: slack - not taut or tight; loose

Etymology

From Old French *tét* (tight), related to Middle English *taught*, past participle of *techen* (to pull tight).

2. ADJ

/ tɔt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
Stretched or pulled tight; not slack, often under tension.

Examples

Docherty went taut: was it possible?

Synset Examples

the skin of his face looked drawn and tight
her nerves were taut as the strings of a bow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slack - loose, not tight or tense

Etymology

From Middle English 'toute', possibly of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'thūtr' (to tighten).
