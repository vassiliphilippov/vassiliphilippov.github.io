Buddhist

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdɪst / (AmE), / ˈbʊdɪst / (BrE)
A person who follows the teachings of Buddha or practices Buddhism.

Examples

He is a Buddhist, which means that to him peace and the sanctity of human life are not only religious dogma, but a profound and unshakable Weltanschauung.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hindu - follower of Hinduism
  • Antonym: Christian - follower of Christianity
  • Antonym: Muslim - follower of Islam

Etymology

From Buddha (the title of Siddhartha Gautama, founder of Buddhism) + -ist (follower or adherent).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbuːdɪst, ˈbʊdɪst / (AmE), / ˈbʊdɪst / (BrE)
relating to Buddhism or its followers

Examples

The Prime Minister paid his respects to the Buddhist monks, strode rapidly among the houses, joked with the local soldiery, and made a speech.

Synset Examples

Buddhist sculpture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - the religion and philosophy based on the teachings of Buddha

Etymology

From Buddha (name of the religion's founder) + -ist (forming nouns and adjectives)
