discerning

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing good judgment and insight; able to perceive and understand things quickly and accurately

Examples

It is this subject matter that has brought Mason a large and enthusiastic following among sportsmen, but it is his exceptional performance with this motif that commends him to artists and discerning collectors.

Synset Examples

a discerning critic
a discerning reader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - showing an ability to notice things quickly and accurately
  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing deep understanding
  • Antonym: undiscerning - lacking judgment or insight

Etymology

Present participle of 'discern', from Latin discernere (to separate, distinguish).
