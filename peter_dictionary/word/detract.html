detract

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / dɪˈtrækt / (BrE)
to make something seem less good, valuable, or important than it really is

Examples

But this does n't detract from its merit as an interesting, if not great, film.

Synset Examples

His bad manners detract from his good character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminish - to make or become less; to reduce
  • Antonym: enhance - to improve or add to the value, quality, or attractiveness of

Etymology

From Latin detract-, past participle of detrahere (to pull away).
