deficiency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
A lack or shortage of something necessary; the state of being insufficient or incomplete.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'deficientia', from 'deficiens', present participle of 'deficere' (to fail, lack).
