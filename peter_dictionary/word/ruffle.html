ruffle

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.əl / (BrE)
to disturb the smooth surface of water or another surface, causing small waves or ripples

Examples

The doctor, he noticed, was attempting a transverse movement towards the stairs, but before the movement could be completed a distinct and audible cluck ruffled the air in the hollow of the stair-well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripple - to form small waves on the surface of water

Etymology

Perhaps related to Middle English *rufelen* (to wrinkle), possibly from Old Norse *hrufla* (to scratch).

2. VERB

/ ˈrʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
to disturb the composure or calm of (someone); to upset or irritate

Examples

No question ruffles him or causes him to hesitate.

Synset Examples

ruffle somebody's composure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upset - to make someone troubled or distressed
  • Synonym: disturb - to interrupt the calm or settled state of someone
  • Antonym: calm - not agitated or upset
