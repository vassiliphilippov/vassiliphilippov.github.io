drawing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ / (BrE)
a picture or design made by marking a surface, typically paper, with lines and often shading, usually using pen, pencil, or similar tools

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a quickly made drawing giving the essential features without the details
  • Antonym: painting - a picture made with paint rather than by drawing lines

Etymology

From draw + -ing, dating to Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ / (BrE)
A picture or illustration made by marking lines on paper or another surface, especially by hand, often published in books, magazines, or newspapers.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - quick, rough drawing without much detail
  • Antonym: painting - a picture created with paint, not just lines

Etymology

From Middle English 'drawynge' (the act of drawing), from 'drawen' (to draw).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or skill of making pictures or diagrams using pen, pencil, or similar tools.

Examples

Painters left less and less of a vase in a plain dark color; instead they divided the surface into many bands or covered it by all-over patterns into which freehand drawing began to creep.
From the town surveyor, Hans learned drawing and mathematics and, from a university student, some academic subjects.

Synset Examples

he learned drawing from his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketching - making a quick, rough drawing
  • Antonym: painting - the act or art of using paint to create pictures

Etymology

From Middle English 'drawen' (to draw), from Old English 'dragan' (to drag, draw).
