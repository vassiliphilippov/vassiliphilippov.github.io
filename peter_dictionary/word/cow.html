cow

1. NOUN

/ kaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊ / (BrE)
A mature female of certain large mammals, especially cattle, whose male counterpart is called a bull.

Examples

If it's true that contented cows give more milk, why should n't happy ball players produce more base hits?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - mature male of certain large mammals, especially cattle

Etymology

Old English *cū*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *koe* and German *Kuh*.
