purgatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətəri / (BrE)
A temporary state or condition of suffering or torment, especially one that must be endured before improvement or relief.

Examples

Those persons who were lucky enough to see and hear the performance of his work at the Brest-Silevniov Festival in August, 1916, will certainly welcome his return to public notice; and it is not unlikely that, even as the great Bach lay dormant for so many years, so has the erudite, ingenious SalFininistas passed through his "purgatory "of neglect.

Synset Examples

a purgatory of drug abuse

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hell - a place or state of eternal torment or misery

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'purgatorium', meaning a place of purification.
