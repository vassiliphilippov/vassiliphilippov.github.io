yard

1. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 3 feet, or 36 inches, equivalent to 0.9144 metres.

Examples

George Kerr, the swift striding Jamaican, set a meet record in the 600 - yard run in the Knights of Columbus track meet tonight, beating Purdue's Dave Mills in a hot duel in 1.10.1.
Kerr, who set the world record earlier this month in New York with a clocking of 1.09.3, wiped out Mills's early pace and beat the young Big 10 quarter-mile king by 5 yards.

Etymology

From Old English 'geard' (rod, measure), related to Old High German 'gart' and Latin 'hortus' (garden).

2. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
An area of enclosed land, usually adjoining a house or building, used as a garden, lawn, or for other purposes.

Examples

The most beautiful bed of pansies I 've seen was in a South Dakota yard on a sizzling day.
He was able, now, to sit for hours in a chair in the living room and stare out at the bleak yard without moving.
The yards, front and back, were narrow; some were trash dumps, some had flower gardens.
He ran out into the downpour, sped across the yard and into the buggy room.
The week after Manassas the sound of horses in the yard brought Kate up in shock from an afternoon's rest when she saw the Federal soldiers from her upstairs window.

Synset Examples

it was a small house with almost no yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden - a piece of ground for growing flowers or vegetables, often in a yard
  • Synonym: courtyard - an unroofed area enclosed by walls, often adjoining a building

Etymology

From Old English *geard* (enclosure, garden), related to German *Garten* (garden).

3. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
an area of land, often enclosed and associated with a building, used for a specific purpose such as storage, work, or transport activities

Examples

By late afternoon the train inched into the marshaling yards in the railhead at Lublin, which was filled with lines of cars poised to pour the tools of war to the Russian front.
Pickings are pretty slim in the yard.

Synset Examples

they opened a repair yard on the edge of town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtyard - an enclosed area, often paved, surrounded by buildings
  • Antonym: garden - a piece of ground adjoining a house, where flowers, shrubs, vegetables, etc. are cultivated

Etymology

From Old English 'geard' (enclosure, garden); related to Old High German 'gart', Old Norse 'garðr'.
