assertiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɝːtɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈsɜːtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being confidently self-assured and able to express one’s opinions or needs in a direct, honest, and respectful way

Examples

He took advantage of the antagonism between aggressive assertiveness and anxiety and found a relatively rapid disappearance of anxiety when the former attitude was established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - feeling or showing assurance in oneself
  • Degree Higher: aggressiveness - tendency towards forceful or combative behavior
  • Degree Lower: passiveness - accepting or allowing what happens without active response

Etymology

From assertive + -ness, where 'assertive' comes from Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, affirm).
