saleslady

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlzˌleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈseɪlzˌleɪdi / (BrE)
A woman whose job is to sell goods to customers in a store.

Examples

She said to the saleslady, "I want a dress to put on around the house ".
The puzzled saleslady inquired, "How large is your house, Madam "?
This saleslady was a failure in the dress department and was transferred to the shoe department.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salesman - male salesperson
  • Synonym: salesperson - person employed to sell goods

Etymology

Formed from 'sale' + 'lady', modeled after 'salesman'.
