fiery

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəri / (BrE)
Showing strong and intense emotion; passionate or fervent.

Examples

The ballet work is on the nose, especially in the opening number by "The New Ashmolean Marching Society and Students' Conservatory Band ", along with a fiery and sultry Brazilian fantasia later.

Synset Examples

ardent love
an ardent lover
a fervent desire to change society
a fervent admirer
fiery oratory
an impassioned appeal
a torrid love affair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ardent - having or showing very strong feelings; passionate
  • Synonym: fervent - having or displaying passionate intensity
  • Synonym: impassioned - filled with intense emotion

Etymology

From Middle English *firy*, from *fire* + *-y*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəri / (BrE)
resembling or suggestive of fire, especially in color, heat, or intensity.

Examples

Two clotted balls the color of mucus rolled between fiery lids.
And the valley stretched endlessly out ahead, scorched and baked and writhing in its heat, until it vanished into the throbbing wall of fiery orange brown haze.

Synset Examples

a fiery desert wind
an igneous desert atmosphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaming - burning with or as if with flames; intensely hot or passionate
  • Synonym: blazing - burning brightly and intensely

Etymology

From Middle English 'firy', from Old English 'fȳr' (fire) + '-y'.
