gegenschein

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪɡənˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡeːɡənˌʃaɪn / (BrE)
a faint, diffuse glow in the night sky directly opposite the Sun, caused by sunlight reflecting off interplanetary dust.

Examples

Zodiacal light and the gegenschein give some evidence for such a dust blanket, a phenomenon also to be expected if the dust before capture is in circular orbits about the sun, as indicated by the trend of the smaller visible meteors.

Etymology

From German 'Gegenschein', literally 'countershine' ('gegen' meaning 'against' + 'Schein' meaning 'shine, light').
