septum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛptəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛptəm / (BrE)
Anatomy: a thin partition or wall that divides two tissues or cavities within an organism.

Examples

Etymology

From Latin septum (a partition), from sepīre (to enclose, separate).
