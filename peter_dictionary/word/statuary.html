statuary

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈstatjʊəri / (BrE)
Statues collectively, especially as a group or collection, or the art of making statues.

Examples

It bulks under a veil of thin, new grass, like some embarrassing fact of physicalness, and I think Mrs. Pastern set out the statuary to soften its meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpture - the art of making figures or designs in three dimensions, especially by carving stone or wood or by casting metal or plaster

Etymology

From Latin 'statuarius' (of statues), from 'statua' (statue).
