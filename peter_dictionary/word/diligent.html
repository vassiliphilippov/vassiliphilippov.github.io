diligent

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪlədʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Showing careful and persistent effort or work.

Examples

The American firearms and ammunition manufacturers through diligent research and technical development have replaced the muzzle loader and slow firing single shot arms with modern fast firing autoloaders, extremely accurate bolt, lever, and slide action firearms.

Synset Examples

a diligent (or patient) worker
with persevering (or patient) industry she revived the failing business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrious - hard-working and energetic
  • Antonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy

Etymology

From Latin diligens, diligent-, present participle of diligere (to value highly, take delight in).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪlədʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Showing careful and persistent effort in doing something.

Examples

Because I can state from my own knowledge and experience that Italian-Americans are among the most loyal, most law-abiding, patriotic, hard-working American citizens in this land.

Synset Examples

a diligent detective investigates all clues
a diligent search of the files

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrious - hardworking and energetic
  • Antonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy

Etymology

From Latin 'dīligēns', present participle of 'dīligere' (to value highly, love, choose).
