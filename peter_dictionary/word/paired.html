paired

1. ADJ

/ pɛrd / (AmE), / pɛəd / (BrE)
(of items such as gloves, socks, etc.) matched or combined as a pair; designed or intended to be used together in twos.

Examples

To each paired vertex and diagonal point there corresponds a unique forward corner point, i.e., the corner on C reached first by proceeding along C from the vertex in the direction of increasing t.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - not in a pair; one item alone

Etymology

From pair + -ed, meaning made into a pair.
