diminishing

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming smaller or less; decreasing in quantity, size, or importance

Examples

Over the rapidly diminishing outline of a jump seat piled high with luggage Herry's black brushcut was just discernible, near, or enviably near that spot where - hidden - more delicately textured, most beautifully tinted hair must still be streaming back in cool, oh cool wind sweetly perfumed with sagebrush and yucca flowers and engine fumes.

Synset Examples

diminishing returns
his diminishing respect for her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: increasing - becoming greater or larger
  • Synonym: reducing - making something smaller or less in amount

Etymology

Present participle of 'diminish', from Latin *diminutio* (making smaller) via Old French *diminuer*.
