self-enclosed

1. ADJ

/ sɛlf ɪnˈkloʊzd / (AmE), / sɛlf ɪnˈkləʊzd / (BrE)
(of a thing or group) enclosed or confined within itself, often by its own choice or nature; not open to outsiders.

Examples

What is noteworthy about this large group of teen-agers is that, although their attitudes hardly differentiate them from their gentile counterparts, they actually lead their lives in a vast self enclosed Jewish cosmos with relatively little contact with the non Jewish world.

Synset Examples

a self-enclosed Jewish cosmos

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-contained - not depending on others; complete in itself

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (meaning 'by oneself or itself') + 'enclosed' (shut in).
