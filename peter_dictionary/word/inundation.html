inundation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ənˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ʌnˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A flood; the covering of normally dry land by a large amount of water, especially due to the rising of a body of water.

Examples

The ancient Greeks recorded several catastrophic inundations by huge waves.

Synset Examples

plains fertilized by annual inundations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of a large amount of water beyond its normal limits

Etymology

From Latin 'inundatio', from 'inundare' (to flood).
