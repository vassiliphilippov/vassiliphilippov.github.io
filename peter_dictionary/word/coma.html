coma

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ mə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ mə / (BrE)
A state of deep and prolonged unconsciousness, typically caused by disease or injury, from which a person cannot be awakened.

Examples

If, for instance, such a change is produced by one or a few insulin comas or electroshocks, previously inhibited conditioned reactions reappear.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
  • Degree Higher: vegetative state - a condition in which a person is awake but not aware of themselves or environment

Etymology

From Greek kōma (deep sleep, trance).
