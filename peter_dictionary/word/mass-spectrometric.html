mass-spectrometric

1. ADJ

/ mæs spɛk.trəˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / mæs spɛk.trəˈmɛ.trɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving mass spectrometry or a mass spectrometer.

Examples

Mass spectrometric analysis of gases evolved upon heating to 410 ` C indicated nitrogen oxides and water vapor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass spectrometry - the technique of using a mass spectrometer to measure masses of particles

Etymology

Formed from 'mass spectrometer' (from 'mass', and 'spectrometer' from Latin 'spectrum' + Greek 'metron' meaning measure) + '-ic'.
