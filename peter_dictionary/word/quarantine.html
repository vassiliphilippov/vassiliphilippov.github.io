quarantine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːrənˌtiːn / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrənˌtiːn / (BrE)
A period or state of isolation imposed to prevent the spread of infectious disease.

Examples

Quarantine three months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others to prevent disease

Etymology

From Italian 'quaranta giorni' (forty days), referring to the period ships were isolated during the Black Death.
