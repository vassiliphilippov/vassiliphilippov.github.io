animosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmɒsəti / (BrE)
A strong feeling of hostility or active dislike toward someone or something.

Examples

Khrushchev himself is reported to be concerned by the surge of animosity he has aroused, yet our own nuclear statesmen seem intent on following compulsively in his footsteps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile behavior; antagonism
  • Synonym: enmity - deep-seated hatred or antagonism
  • Antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly or kind
  • Antonym: goodwill - friendly or helpful feelings

Etymology

From Latin animositas (boldness, spiritedness), from animosus (high-spirited).
