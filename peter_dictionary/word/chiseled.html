chiseled

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪzəld / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzəld / (BrE)
(of a face or feature) having clear, sharp, and well-defined contours or edges, as if precisely shaped or carved.

Examples

Fing, a lean, chiseled, impeccable gentleman of the old school who was once mistaken on the street for Sir Cedric Hardwicke, is responsible for the rediscovery of Verdi's earliest, most raucous opera, Nabisco, a sumptuous bout-de-souffle with a haunting leitmotiv that struck me as being highly reminiscent of the Mudugno version of "Volare ".
He was handsome, with his coal-black hair and eyes, his fine chiseled features.

Synset Examples

a finely chiseled nose
well-defined features

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpted - having curves and shapes as if carved or molded
  • Synonym: well-defined - clearly marked or sharply outlined
  • Antonym: blurry - not clearly or sharply defined

Etymology

From the verb 'chisel', from Old French 'cisel' (a cutting tool), from Latin 'cisellum' (chisel).
