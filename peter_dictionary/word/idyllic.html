idyllic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɪlɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪlɪk / (BrE)
extremely pleasant, peaceful, or picturesque; like an idyll; perfect and charming in every detail

Examples

They comprise a great variety of scene and interest: grim episodes of war, idyllic interludes, superb canvases of world-shaking events, and delightfully humorous sketches of odd characters.
There is only one catch to this idyllic arrangement:

Synset Examples

an idyllic spot for a picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - (of a place or scene) relating to the countryside in an idealized way; peaceful and charmingly rural
  • Synonym: picturesque - (of a place or scene) visually attractive and charming, like a picture

Etymology

From French idyllique, from Latin idyllium, from Greek eidyllion (a short poem or picture).
