overtake

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈteɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈteɪk / (BrE)
to move past or go ahead of someone or something that is moving in the same direction, especially in a vehicle; to surpass in progress or development

Examples

Very peculiar retribution indeed seems to overtake such jokers.
This has necessitated a continuous review and reevaluation of the defense program in order to redirect resources to the newer and more important weapons systems and to eliminate or reduce effort on weapons systems which have been overtaken by events.

Synset Examples

The sports car passed all the trucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - to go past or move beyond

Etymology

From Old English 'overtacan', from 'over' + 'tacan' ('take').

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈteɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈteɪk / (BrE)
to catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction; to become equal to or superior to in achievement or progress

Examples

The lean and leathery Oklahoma amateur, who has been playing topnotch tournament golf for many years, refused to let the Masters jitters overtake him and closed the tournament with his second straight 69.
There's only one way they can get out now and that's through the Gap - if we ride hard we can take them ".

Synset Examples

The Rolls Royce caught us near the exit ramp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch up - to reach someone ahead by moving faster
  • Synonym: outstrip - to move faster than and leave behind
  • Antonym: fall behind - to fail to maintain the same progress as others

Etymology

From Old English 'overtacan', from 'over' + 'tacan' (take).
