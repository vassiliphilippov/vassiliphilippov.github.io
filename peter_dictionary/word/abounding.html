abounding

1. ADJ

/ əˈbaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈbaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
existing in great abundance; very plentiful

Examples

For this does not account for the integral, elemental power of that which grows with abounding vigor as the play unfolds, nor does it explain the strange numinous sense of presentness which comes over those who watch the play like a spell.

Synset Examples

abounding confidence
whiskey galore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plentiful - existing in large quantities; abundant
  • Antonym: scarce - in short supply; not plentiful

Etymology

Present participle of 'abound', from Latin *abundare*, meaning 'to overflow'.
