noradrenaline

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɔːr.əˈdrɛn.əˌlin / (AmE), / ˌnɔː.rəˈdrɛn.ə.liːn / (BrE)
A hormone and neurotransmitter, also known as norepinephrine, that is produced by the adrenal medulla and released at nerve endings, playing a key role in the body's response to stress and in regulating blood pressure.

Examples

The Mecholyl and noradrenalin tests applied with certain precautions are reliable indicators of this central autonomic balance, but for the sake of correlating autonomic and clinical states, and of studying the effect of certain therapeutic procedures on central autonomic reactions, additional tests seem to be desirable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: norepinephrine - another name for noradrenaline, especially in the US

Etymology

From 'nor-' (denoting a chemical form lacking a methyl group) and 'adrenaline', in reference to its chemical structure.
