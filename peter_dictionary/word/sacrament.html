sacrament

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈsakrəment / (BrE)
A formal religious ceremony or rite considered to bestow spiritual grace, especially one of the recognized rites of the Christian Church such as baptism or the Eucharist.

Examples

Finally, it gives sanctity, more than human legitimacy, and even, through super empirical reference, transcendent and supernatural importance to some values; for example, marriage as a sacrament, much law-breaking as sinful, occasionally the state as a divine instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rite - a formal or ceremonial act or procedure

Etymology

From Latin sacramentum (a solemn oath, a religious rite), from sacrare (to consecrate).
