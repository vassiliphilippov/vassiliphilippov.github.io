herringbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.ɪŋˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.ɪŋ.bəʊn / (BrE)
A twilled fabric woven in a distinctive V-shaped pattern resembling the bones of a herring.

Examples

I wore a new double-breasted brown worsted suit with a faint herringbone design and wide lapels like a devil's ears.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the bones of a herring fish.
