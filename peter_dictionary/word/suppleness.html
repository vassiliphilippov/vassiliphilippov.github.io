suppleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌp.lə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.lɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being flexible, graceful, and able to move or bend easily.

Examples

It was enough for people to know that at one time he had looked down the street at the fleshy suppleness of a woman he had consumed - watching her become thinner and thinner in the distance, as thin as the seams on her stockings, and still thinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lissomeness - the quality of being lithe and flexible
  • Antonym: stiffness - the quality of being rigid and not easily bent
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff or inflexible

Etymology

From supple + -ness; 'supple' from Old French 'souple', from Latin 'supplex' (submissive, bending under).
