merriment

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
cheerful enjoyment and fun; lively amusement or laughter

Examples

There is a pause in the merriment as your friends gaze at you, wondering why you are staring, open-mouthed in amazement.

Synset Examples

I do it for the fun of it
he is fun to have around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fun - enjoyment, amusement, or lighthearted pleasure
  • Synonym: gaiety - state of being cheerful or lighthearted
  • Antonym: sadness - state of being unhappy or sorrowful

Etymology

From Middle English 'merymente', from 'mery' (merry) + '-ment' (suffix forming nouns).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
Cheerful laughter and lighthearted enjoyment; feelings or expressions of happiness and fun.

Examples

Once covertly looking at Simms Purdew, the only man in the world whom he hated, he had seen the heavy, slack, bestubbled jaw open and close to emit the cruel, obscene banter, and had seen the pale blue eyes go watery with whisky and merriment, and suddenly he was not seeing the face of that vile creature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaiety - cheerful and lighthearted activity or feeling
  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Middle English merymente, from earlier mery (merry) + -ment (forming nouns of action or state).
