follow-through

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːloʊ ˌθruː / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˌθruː / (BrE)
the action of continuing an activity or carrying out a plan or promise until it is fully completed

Examples

Proper follow-through requires training your own sales organization, and your distributor organizations, not only in the techniques but also in good customer relations.

Synset Examples

I appreciated his follow-through on his promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of finishing something
  • Antonym: abandonment - the act of ending or stopping something before it is finished

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'follow through', meaning to continue an action to its conclusion.
