dimensional

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɛnʃənl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or having dimensions; connected with measurement in one or more directions such as length, width, or height.

Examples

Much new equipment and many unique techniques have been developed for the quantitative exposure of experimental animals to aerosols of infectious agents contained in particles of specified dimensional characteristics.
Let T be a linear operator on the finite dimensional vector space V over the field F.
In the primary decomposition theorem, it is not necessary that the vector space V be finite dimensional, nor is it necessary for parts (a) and (b) that p be the minimal polynomial for T.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: multi-dimensional - having or relating to multiple dimensions
  • Degree Lower: one-dimensional - having or relating to a single dimension

Etymology

From 'dimension' + '-al', formed on the root of Latin 'dimensio' (a measuring).
