chlorpromazine

1. NOUN

/ klɔːrˈproʊməˌziːn / (AmE), / klɔːˈprɒməziːn / (BrE)
A synthetic drug used as an antipsychotic and sedative, especially in the treatment of schizophrenia and other mental disorders.

Examples

Specific differences exist in the action of different drugs belonging to the same group as, for instance, between reserpine and chlorpromazine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thorazine - trade name for chlorpromazine

Etymology

From 'chloro-' (referring to chlorine atoms in its structure) + 'promazine' (a class of phenothiazine derivatives).
