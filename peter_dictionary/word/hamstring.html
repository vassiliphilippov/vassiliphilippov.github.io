hamstring

1. VERB

/ ˈhæmˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhamstrɪŋ / (BrE)
To make someone or something ineffective or powerless, typically by restricting their ability to act.

Examples

I mean, do you think she'd go behind your back, try to hamstring you?

Synset Examples

The teachers were hamstrung by the overly rigid schedules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cripple - to seriously damage or impair the functioning or effectiveness of someone or something
  • Synonym: disable - to make unable to function

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'hamstring' (a tendon behind the knee); figurative use derives from the disabling effect of severing the hamstring.
