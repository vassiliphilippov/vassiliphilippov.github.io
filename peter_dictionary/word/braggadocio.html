braggadocio

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræɡəˈdoʊʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˌbræɡəˈdəʊʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
boastful or arrogant behavior that is empty or vain

Examples

That sort of braggadocio, for that sort of reason, in the view of Torrio and Capone, was a nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boasting - the act of talking with excessive pride; bragging
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Italian braggadoccio, from Braggadoccio, a vain braggart character in Spenser’s ‘The Faerie Queene’.
