embarrass

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbærəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəs / (BrE)
to make (someone) feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed

Examples

Every orthodontist sees children who are embarrassed by their malformed teeth.
I think I've embarrassed him for long enough.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: humiliate - to make (someone) feel ashamed and foolish, especially publicly

Etymology

From French *embarrasser* (to entangle, hinder), from Italian *imbarazzare*; originally from Spanish *embarazar*.
