loud

1. ADJ

/ laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
(of colours, clothes, or designs) offensively bright or showy; garish or tastelessly conspicuous.

Examples

Actions speak louder.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - ostentatiously attractive or impressive; showy
  • Synonym: gaudy - bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Antonym: muted - not bright, loud, or intense in colour; subdued
  • Synonym: garish - obtrusively bright and showy; lurid

2. ADJ

/ laʊd / (AmE), / laʊd / (BrE)
(of sounds) producing or characterized by a great volume or intensity of sound; easily heard.

Examples

You definitely hear some of the instruments close up and others farther back, with the difference in placement apparently more distinct than would result from the nearer instruments merely being louder than the ones farther back.
When he was asked a question or addressed in such a way that some response was inescapable, he would answer; if, as often happened, he had to repeat because he had spoken too softly, he would repeat his words in the same way, without emphasis or impatience, only a little louder.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noisy - making or given to making a lot of noise
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no sound
  • Degree Lower: soft - (of sounds) not loud; gentle or quiet
  • Degree Higher: deafening - extremely loud; so loud as to make hearing other things difficult

Etymology

From Old English hlūd, from Proto-Germanic *hlūdaz.
