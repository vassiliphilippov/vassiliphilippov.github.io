Alexander

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændər / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzɑːndə / (BrE)
(Alexander the Great) King of Macedon (356–323 BC), famous for conquering Greece, Egypt, and Persia and founding the city of Alexandria.

Examples

Alexander Knox.

Etymology

From the Greek name Alexandros, meaning 'defender of men'.
