tragedian

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdʒiːdiən / (AmE), / trəˈdʒiːdiən / (BrE)
a writer, especially a playwright, who composes tragedies

Examples

Hegel's profound admiration for the insights of the Greek tragedians indicates a broad channel of classical influence upon nineteenth century philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playwright - a person who writes plays
  • Antonym: comedian - a person who writes or performs comedies (contrast in genre)

Etymology

From Latin 'tragoedianus', from Greek 'tragōidios' (pertaining to tragedy).
