cannon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˈkanən / (BrE)
a large, heavy gun, typically mounted on wheels or a fixed base, used historically in warfare for firing heavy projectiles

Examples

The fountain had brimmed over, the cannon were wet, the soldiers' monument glistened.
The gilt sterns of the men-of-war becoming clearer to him, the sides of the wooden sea walls alternately painted yellow and black, the bronze cannon at the ports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare
  • Synonym: gun - a weapon that fires projectiles

Etymology

From Old Italian *cannone* (large tube), from Latin *canna* (reed, tube).
