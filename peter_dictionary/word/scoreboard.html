scoreboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈskɔːˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a large board on which the score and other information of a game or contest are displayed

Examples

Lumpe worked a walk as the first batter to face Hyde and romped around as Siebern blasted Hyde's next toss 415 feet over the scoreboard in right center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display board - a board for posting information or results

Etymology

From 'score' (to keep a record) + 'board' (a flat surface).
