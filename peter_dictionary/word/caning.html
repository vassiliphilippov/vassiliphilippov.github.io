caning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
woven material made by interlacing slender branches, especially of willow or rattan, used in furniture or basket-making.

Examples

Picasso says that he himself had already made his first collage toward the end of 1911, when he glued a piece of imitation caning oilcloth to a painting on canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicker - pliable twigs or branches woven together to make baskets or furniture

Etymology

From 'cane' (the stem of bamboo or rattan), relating to the material traditionally used.
