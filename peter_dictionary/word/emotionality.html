emotionality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊʃəˈnæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being emotional; the tendency to experience or display strong emotions

Examples

Children scoring high in compulsivity were those who gave evidence of tension or emotionality in situations where there was lack of organization or conformity to standards and expectations, or who made exaggerated efforts to achieve these goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotion - a strong feeling such as love, anger, joy, hate, or fear

Etymology

From 'emotional' + '-ity', formed after 'emotion'.
