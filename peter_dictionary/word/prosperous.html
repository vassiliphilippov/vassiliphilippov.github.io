prosperous

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːspərəs / (AmE), / ˈprɒspərəs / (BrE)
Having financial success or good fortune; well-off or wealthy.

Examples

By the time he was prosperous enough - his goals were high - he was bald and afraid of women.
Heidenstam was born in 1859, of a prosperous family.

Synset Examples

they were comfortable or even wealthy by some standards
easy living
a prosperous family
his family is well-situated financially
well-to-do members of the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable or normal

Etymology

From Latin 'prosperus' (favorable, fortunate, prosperous).

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːspərəs / (AmE), / ˈprɒspərəs / (BrE)
successful, flourishing, and financially well-off, especially as a result of being lively and profitable

Examples

While no larger than Dutch Springs, this mining supply town had the appearance of being far busier and more prosperous.

Synset Examples

flourishing businesses
a palmy time for stockbrokers
a prosperous new business
doing a roaring trade
a thriving tourist center
did a thriving business in orchids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thriving - growing or developing successfully; flourishing
  • Synonym: booming - enjoying rapid growth or expansion
  • Antonym: impoverished - having little money; poor

Etymology

From Latin 'prosperus' (favorable, fortunate).
