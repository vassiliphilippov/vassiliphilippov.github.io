scores

1. NOUN

/ skɔrz / (AmE), / skɔːz / (BrE)
A large number or quantity of something; used especially in phrases like 'scores of' to emphasize a great multitude or amount.

Examples

New Yorkers were kept informed of scores by reporters who telegraphed fifteen to twenty thousand words daily to the metropolitan newspapers.
But people differ in their religious beliefs on scores of doctrines, without taking up arms against those who disagree with them.

Synset Examples

made lots of new friends
she amassed stacks of newspapers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dozens - a set of twelve; by extension, a large number

Etymology

From Middle English 'score' (twenty), which came to mean 'a group of twenty' and then 'a large number'.
