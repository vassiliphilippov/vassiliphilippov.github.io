squatting

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of exercising by repeatedly lowering the body into a crouched position with the knees bent, usually to strengthen the leg muscles.

Examples

But for purely definition purposes - used in conjunction with your regular Squatting, Leg Curling, Leg Extensor programs - a heavy weight is not needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knee bend - an exercise involving bending the knees from a standing position
  • Synonym: lunge - a fitness exercise where one leg is positioned forward with the knee bent

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of assuming or maintaining a crouching position with the knees bent and the buttocks close to the heels.

Examples

I regained my squatting position behind the truck cab's rear window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crouching - the act of bending one's knees and bringing the upper body down, similar to squatting
  • Antonym: standing - being in an upright position on the feet, as opposed to squatting

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'squat' (from Old French esquatir 'compress, press down'), with the -ing suffix.
