chastity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæstɪti / (AmE), / ˈtʃæstɪti / (BrE)
the state or practice of refraining from sexual intercourse, especially for religious or moral reasons

Examples

Poverty imposes a kind of chastity on the ambitious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celibacy - the state of abstaining from marriage and sexual relations
  • Antonym: promiscuity - the practice of having frequent sexual relations with different partners

Etymology

From Latin *castitas* (purity), from *castus* (pure).
