loser

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːzər / (AmE), / ˈluːzə / (BrE)
A person or team who does not win in a contest, competition, or game.

Examples

Johnny Blanchard, Mantle's replacement, slammed a 2 - run homer as the Yankees routed loser Joey Jay in a 5 - run first inning.
Like Napoleon, he was the worst of losers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - person or team that wins a contest or competition

Etymology

From lose + -er, originally signifying one who loses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːzɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːzə / (BrE)
A person who is habitually unsuccessful or fails repeatedly, especially one regarded with contempt or pity.

Examples

I ain't no goddamn loser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success; a person or thing that is unsuccessful
  • Antonym: winner - a person who wins or is habitually successful
