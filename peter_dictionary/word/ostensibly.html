ostensibly

1. ADV

/ əˈstɛn.səb.li / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛn.sɪ.bli / (BrE)
As it appears or is stated to be, but perhaps not actually so; apparently, but not necessarily truly.

Examples

We may conclude that all six points of information, ostensibly given by the dream priest, could have been furnished by Dr. Hilprecht's subconscious reasoning.

Synset Examples

irrigation often produces bumper crops from apparently desert land
the child is seemingly healthy but the doctor is concerned
had been ostensibly frank as to his purpose while really concealing it
on the face of it the problem seems minor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparently - as it appears; seemingly
  • Synonym: seemingly - so as to give the impression of having a certain quality; apparently
  • Antonym: actually - in fact; truly (in contrast to what seems)

Etymology

From Latin ostendere (to show), via French ostensible.
