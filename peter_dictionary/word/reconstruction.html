Reconstruction

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌkʌnˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
The period following the American Civil War (1865–1877) when the southern states were reorganized and reintegrated into the Union.

Examples

Southern resentment has been over the method of its ending, the invasion, and Reconstruction; their fears now are of miscegenation and Negro political control in many counties.
I suppose the reason is a kind of wishful thinking: do n't talk about the final stages of Reconstruction and they will take care of themselves.

Etymology

From Latin prefix re- (again) + English 'construction', referring to the rebuilding of the South after the Civil War.
