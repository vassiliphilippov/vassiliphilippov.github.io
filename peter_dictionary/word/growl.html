growl

1. VERB

/ ɡraʊl / (AmE), / ɡraʊl / (BrE)
to make a low, guttural, and often threatening or rumbling sound, as an animal, person, or inanimate object (e.g., thunder)

Examples

One, more horrible than the rest, lunged, growling deep in his throat, his hair bristling.
"Bunch of damn jerks ", Hub growled.
From a great distance thunder growled and broke the silence.
Oso growled.

Synset Examples

he grumbled a rude response
Stones grumbled down the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snarl - to make an aggressive growling sound

Etymology

From Middle English 'growlen', probably imitative in origin.

2. NOUN

/ ɡraʊl / (AmE), / ɡraʊl / (BrE)
a low, guttural sound typically made by an animal when angry or threatened

Examples

I always felt the memory was present in both of them, stirring in their chests like a laugh or a growl, as eager as an animal to be out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snarl - a harsh, angry sound made especially by an animal

Etymology

Imitative in origin; from the Middle English *grollen*, possibly of Old French or Dutch origin.
