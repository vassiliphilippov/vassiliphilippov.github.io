unwarranted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɔɹəntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒrəntɪd / (BrE)
Not justified or authorized; incapable of being explained or defended.

Examples

Alex suppressed those expressions of relief which offered to prevail in his face and escape from his throat; unwarranted they were in any case for, as he stood facing the fille de chambre, his ears were assailed by new sounds from the interior of the shower room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason or explanation; defensible

Etymology

From un- (not) + warranted, from warrant (to justify, authorize).
