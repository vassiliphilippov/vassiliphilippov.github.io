immensity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛn.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
The extremely large size, extent, or degree of something.

Examples

Three seconds flat is the usual time, and the space is crossed by moderate mileage, while the overwhelming immensity of such journeys must be conceived as a static pulsation through an enormous number of coexistent frequencies which perpetuate all events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormity - great size or extent; enormousness
  • Synonym: vastness - the quality of being immense; great extent
  • Antonym: smallness - the quality of being small in size or extent

Etymology

From Latin immēnsitās (vastness), from immēnsus (immeasurable).
