terrestrial

1. ADJ

/ təˈrɛstriəl / (AmE), / təˈrɛstrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to the land, as distinct from the sea or air.

Examples

This and other fears of the solar system have disappeared gradually, first, with the Ptolemaic system and its built-in concept of periodicity and then, more firmly, with the Newtonian innovation of an universal force that could account quantitatively for both terrestrial and celestial motions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marine - relating to the sea
  • Antonym: aerial - relating to the air

Etymology

From Latin terrestris (of the earth), from terra (earth).

2. ADJ

/ təˈrɛstriəl / (AmE), / təˈrɛstrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to the planet Earth or its inhabitants; characteristic of the Earth as opposed to other planets or celestial bodies.

Examples

This is much higher than the nickel content of terrestrial dusts and sediments and provides a basis for the determination of the meteoritic mass influx.

Synset Examples

planetary rumblings and eructations
the planetary tilt
this terrestrial ball

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extraterrestrial - originating or located outside the planet Earth

Etymology

From Latin terrestris (of the earth), from terra (earth).
