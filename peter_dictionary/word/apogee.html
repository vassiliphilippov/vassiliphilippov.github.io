apogee

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌdʒi / (AmE), / ˈapədʒi / (BrE)
the highest point or most successful stage of something; the climax or culmination

Examples

Although it is not possible to sunder old and new in this era, I shall consider in the present chapter primarily the first decades of the eighth century and shall interpret them as an apogee of the first stage of Greek civilization.

Synset Examples

their achievements stand as a culmination of centuries of development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culmination - the highest or climactic point of something
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point; opposite of apogee

Etymology

From French 'apogée', from Latin 'apogaeum', from Greek 'apogaion (sêmeion)' meaning 'point away from earth'.
