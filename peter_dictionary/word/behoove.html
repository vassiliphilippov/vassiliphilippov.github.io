behoove

1. VERB

/ bɪˈhuv / (AmE), / bɪˈhuːv / (BrE)
to be necessary, proper, or appropriate for someone to do

Examples

Considering what is being done compared to what needs to be done, it behooves the hospital management to do some mighty careful planning toward making the best possible use of the increase granted.

Synset Examples

It behooves us to reflect on this matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incumbent - necessary for someone as a duty or responsibility
  • Synonym: require - to need or make necessary

Etymology

From Old English 'behōfian' (to be of use, need), related to 'behove.'
