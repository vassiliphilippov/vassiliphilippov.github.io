demonstrably

1. ADV

/ dɪˈmɑnstrəbli / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrəbli / (BrE)
In a way that can be clearly shown to be true or false; in an obvious and provable manner.

Examples

One was demonstrably false; the other, for the most part, true.

Synset Examples

his documentary sources are demonstrably wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood
  • Synonym: clearly - in a clear and distinct manner

Etymology

From Latin 'dēmonstrābilis' (provable), via Late Latin and Middle French.
