despotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛspəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛspətɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A system of government or rule by a despot; absolute power exercised in a cruel or oppressive way, especially through use of force or threat.

Examples

Resolved that his wrongs and bereavements in Kansas, occasioned by the violence and brutality of those who were intent on the propagation of slavery in that territory, call for a charitable judgment upon his recent efforts in Virginia to undermine the despotism from which he had suffered, and commend his family to the special sympathy and aid of all who pity suffering and reverence justice.
On shore "the freed slaves to despotism "- the town dwellers - watched the ships and waited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyranny - cruel and oppressive government or rule
  • Synonym: absolutism - a political system of unlimited, centralized authority and absolute power
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • Synonym: autocracy - government by a single person with unlimited power

Etymology

From French despotisme, from Greek despotēs (master, absolute ruler).
