run-down

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌnˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈrʌnˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of a building, place, etc.) in a poor or neglected condition as a result of overuse or lack of maintenance.

Examples

A site may be a rundown slum or a desolate piece of desert in appearance today but have excellent potentials for the future with a little development or water.

Synset Examples

a creaky shack
a decrepit bus...its seats held together with friction tape
a flea-bitten sofa
a run-down neighborhood
a woebegone old shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrepit - worn out or ruined because of age or neglect
  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin as a result of age or neglect
  • Synonym: neglected - not receiving proper care; ignored
  • Antonym: new - recently made or built; not worn or used

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'run down', meaning to decline or deteriorate; used adjectivally since the late 19th century.
