fetching

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or their appearance) very attractive; charming or captivating.

Examples

The girls, very fetching in their uniforms, are shown firing rockets from a launcher mounted on a dump truck; they are also trained with carbines, automatic weapons, pistols, rifles and other such ladies' accessories.

Synset Examples

a fetching new hairstyle
something inexpressibly taking in his manner
a winning personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming
  • Synonym: winsome - attractive or appealing in appearance or character
  • Antonym: plain - not beautiful or attractive

Etymology

From the verb 'fetch', meaning 'to attract' or 'to bring', with the adjectival suffix '-ing'.
