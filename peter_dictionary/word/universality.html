universality

1. NOUN

/ ˌjunɪvərˈsæləti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪvəˈsælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being universal; existing or applying everywhere or to all cases.

Examples

The straight line would symbolize its uniqueness, the circle its universality.
Not that such a list cannot be constructed; but the nearer it comes to attaining universality, the less significant will it be linguistically.
The theory of international law, which in the nineteenth century became common to virtually all writers in Europe and America, broke this unity and this universality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universality principle - the principle that certain laws or rights are universally valid
  • Antonym: particularity - the quality of being particular rather than universal

Etymology

From Medieval Latin universalitas, from universalis (universal).
