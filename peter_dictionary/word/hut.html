hut

1. NOUN

/ hʌt / (AmE), / hʌt / (BrE)
A small, simple, and often roughly built shelter used as a dwelling.

Examples

Wait "! cried the guard who ran from the hut to shout to other men standing about outside.
Dives, carefully repaired huts, and nicely painted and ornamented cottages were jumbled together cheek by jowl with little distinction.
This was one of the Irish women who had built their own huts down near the river.
In one of the huts a group of men were huddled together, singing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shack - a roughly built hut or cabin, typically used for temporary shelter
  • Synonym: cabin - a small simple house made of wood, usually in a rural area
  • Synonym: hovel - a small, poorly built and often dirty house

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'hutte' or Middle French 'hutte', meaning 'hut, cabin'.
