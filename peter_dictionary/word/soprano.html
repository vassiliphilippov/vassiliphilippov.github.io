soprano

1. NOUN

/ səˈprænoʊ / (AmE), / səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
a female or boy singer with the highest vocal range in classical music; also, the highest singing part or voice in a choir or ensemble

Examples

No casts are listed, but Lotte Lehmann sent word that the Negro soprano, Grace Bumbry, will sing Venus in "Tannhaeuser ".
What followed the outburst brought almost breathless silence as Miss Sutherland revealed her mastery of a voice probably unique among sopranos today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alto - the second highest singing voice in choral music

Etymology

From Italian 'soprano', from 'sopra' meaning 'above'.
