cartwheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtˌwiːl / (BrE)
An acrobatic movement in which the body turns sideways completely round, with the arms and legs spread out like the spokes of a wheel, usually performed by moving the hands and then the feet along the ground in succession.

Examples

It is made up of tumbling, which might be said to start with a somersault, run through such stunts as headstands, handstands, cartwheels, backbends, and culminate in nearly impossible combinations of aerial flips and twists and apparatus work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somersault - an acrobatic movement in which a person rolls forward or backward in a complete revolution

Etymology

From 'cart' + 'wheel', likening the movement of arms and legs to the spokes of a wheel as it turns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtˌwiːl / (BrE)
a wheel with wooden spokes and a metal rim, typically used on a cart or wagon

Examples

Through the open window they heard sounds below in the street: cartwheels, a tired horse's plodding step, voices.

Etymology

From 'cart' + 'wheel'.
