homesteader

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌstɛdər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌstɛdə / (BrE)
a person who settles on land, especially government land, with the intention of acquiring legal ownership, usually by fulfilling certain requirements such as farming or building a residence

Examples

But there were other homesteaders who passed the Lewis murder off as a personal grudge killing, the work of one of his neighbors.
The mere fact that the tall figure with the rifle and field glasses had been seen riding that way was enough to frighten three rustling homesteaders out of the Upper Laramie country in a single week.
He had made himself the personification of the Devil to the homesteaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squatter - a person who settles on land or property without legal claim

Etymology

From 'homestead' + '-er'; 'homestead' from Old English 'hāmstede' (home place).
