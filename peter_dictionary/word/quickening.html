quickening

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪk.ən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪk.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or moment of becoming alive or showing signs of life, especially the first movement of a fetus in the womb or the beginning of growth or development

Examples

In the other hemisphere it is growing colder and nymphs, those who stayed alive through the summer, are being brought into nests for quickening and more growing ".

Synset Examples

the quickening of seed that will become ripe grain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animation - the act of bringing to life or the state of being alive

Etymology

From Middle English 'quikening', from 'quiken' (to come to life), related to Old English 'cwic' (alive).
