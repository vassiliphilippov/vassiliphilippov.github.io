frightful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtfʊl / (BrE)
Very shocking, horrible, or causing fear or horror.

Examples

It is this modulation which reveals to Jacoby his own frightful abjection and, simultaneously, his wife's infidelity.
This was a training ship and the training would continue, but there was an element of frightful absurdity here which Alexander recognized.

Synset Examples

an atrocious automobile accident
a frightful crime of decapitation
an alarming, even horrifying, picture
war is beyond all words horrible
an ugly wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrible - causing horror or shock
  • Synonym: dreadful - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Antonym: delightful - giving great pleasure or delight
  • Synonym: alarming - causing fear or anxiety

Etymology

From Middle English 'frightful', from 'fright' + '-ful'.
