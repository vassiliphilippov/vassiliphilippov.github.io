worried

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜːrid / (AmE), / ˈwʌrɪd / (BrE)
Feeling anxious, troubled, or concerned, often about something that may happen or has happened.

Examples

We are worried about what people may do with them - that some crazy fool may "push the button ".
He looked at his son, his face worried.
She was very worried ".
He was no longer worried.
Precious had me worried.

Synset Examples

too upset to say anything
spent many disquieted moments
distressed about her son's leaving home
lapsed into disturbed sleep
worried parents
a worried frown
one last worried check of the sleeping children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation

Etymology

From 'worry' (verb) + -ed, originally from Old English *wyrgan* (to strangle, seize by the throat); sense shifted to mental distress in modern usage.
