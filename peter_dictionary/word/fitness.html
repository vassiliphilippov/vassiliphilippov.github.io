fitness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state of being physically healthy and strong, especially as a result of exercise and proper nutrition

Examples

If a nation wished to get a head start in physical fitness over all other nations, it would start its kindergarten students on a program of gymnastics the day they entered and thus eliminate a large number of the problems that plague American schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: health - the condition of being free from illness or injury
  • Synonym: wellness - the state of being in good health
  • Antonym: infirmity - physical or mental weakness

Etymology

From fit + -ness; 'fit' from Old Norse 'fitja' (to knit), later meaning 'suitable, healthy' in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose or situation

Examples

Broad and Ross have lately contended that this fitness is one of the main facts of ethics, and I suspect they are right.
whether there is such fitness or not, we all assume that there is, and if we do, we express in moral judgments more than the subjectivists say we do.
In such a case, any attitude would be as fitting or unfitting as any other, which means that the notion of fitness has lost all point.

Synset Examples

they had to prove their fitness for the position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitability - the quality of being right or appropriate for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: unsuitability - the quality of not being appropriate or fit for a purpose

Etymology

From fit + -ness, with 'fit' from Old Norse 'fit' (suitable, proper).
