healer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlər / (AmE), / ˈhiːlə / (BrE)
a person skilled in a particular type of therapy, especially one who treats illness or injury

Examples

Doris Hull, suffering from tuberculosis, was taken by her husband to see Otis G. Carroll, a sanipractor - a licensed drugless healer - in Spokane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapist - a person trained in methods of therapy
  • Synonym: doctor - a qualified practitioner of medicine

Etymology

From heal + -er, dating to Old English 'hælan' (to make whole).
