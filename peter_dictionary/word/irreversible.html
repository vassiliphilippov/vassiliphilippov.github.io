irreversible

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌrɪˈvɜrsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪˈvɜːsəbəl / (BrE)
Impossible to reverse or undo; unable to be changed back to a previous state or condition.

Examples

In any event, it is an irreversible step, and if we are at all honest with ourselves, we will know we have no other alternative than to live in the world in which God has seen fit to place us.

Synset Examples

irreversible momentum toward revolution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reversible - able to be reversed or returned to a previous state

Etymology

From French irréversible, from Latin in- (not) + reversibilis (that can be turned back).
