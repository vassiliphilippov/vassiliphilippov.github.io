grievance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːvəns / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvəns / (BrE)
A feeling of resentment or perceived wrong sufficiently strong to provoke a desire for retaliation or redress.

Examples

So instead of being tests of the South's loyalty, the Spanish War, the two World Wars, and the Korean War all served to overcome old grievances and cement reunion.

Synset Examples

holding a grudge
settling a score

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grudge - persistent feeling of resentment
  • Synonym: complaint - a statement expressing dissatisfaction or resentment

Etymology

From Old French 'grevance', from 'grever' (to burden, harm), from Latin 'gravare', from 'gravis' (heavy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːvəns / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvəns / (BrE)
a formal complaint or statement that something is unfair, illegal, or unjust, especially concerning an infringement of rights

Examples

Do you measure its relation to reduced absenteeism, turnover, accidents, and grievances, and to improved quality and output?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or objection

Etymology

From Old French *grevance* (harm, injury), from *grever* (to harm), from Latin *gravare* (to burden).
