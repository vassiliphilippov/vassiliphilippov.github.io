1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process by which molecules or particles spread from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration, especially as a result of random movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osmosis - movement of solvent molecules through a semipermeable membrane from low to high solute concentration

Etymology

From Latin diffundere (to pour out), from dis- (apart) + fundere (to pour).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
The process or state of being spread or dispersed widely, especially over a large area or among many people.

Examples

It has thus been a significant factor in the conservation of social values, though also in some measure, an obstacle to the creation or diffusion of new ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissemination - the act of spreading something widely; circulation
  • Antonym: concentration - the act or process of focusing or gathering together

Etymology

From Latin 'diffusio,' from 'diffundere' (to pour out, spread out).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process by which cultural practices, beliefs, or institutions spread from one society or social group to another

Examples

Some of this may prove to be true, or even considerable of it, whether by genetic ramification or by diffusion and coalescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilation - the process by which a person or group acquires the cultural traits of another group

Etymology

From Latin 'diffusionem', from 'diffundere' meaning 'to pour out'.
