damp

1. ADJ

/ dæmp / (AmE), / dæmp / (BrE)
slightly wet, often in a way that is uncomfortable or unwanted

Examples

They were damp.
Damp also.
He paced slowly, stooping, staring at the damp, slippery floor.
The waters lay muddy but placid, without a ripple of movement against the wheels; there was not a match width of damp mark to show they were receding.

Synset Examples

clothes damp with perspiration
a moist breeze
eyes moist with tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moist - slightly wet, often in a pleasant way
  • Degree Higher: wet - covered with, or saturated with liquid
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet
