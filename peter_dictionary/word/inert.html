inert

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɜrt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
(of a substance) having little or no ability to react chemically; not active in chemical processes.

Examples

In the first trial an inert substance was disseminated from a boat travelling some ten miles off shore under appropriately selected meteorological conditions.

Synset Examples

inert matter
an indifferent chemical in a reaction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reactive - able to react chemically; having a tendency to undergo chemical changes

Etymology

From Latin inert-, iners, meaning 'unskilled, inactive', from in- (not) + ars (skill).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɝːt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
Unable to move or act; lacking the ability or strength to move.

Examples

Jesus' inert body would be passive, his eyes closed.
Still the guards did not move, but stood inert, aloof from the slow scrambling man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobile - not moving; motionless
  • Antonym: active - engaging in action; capable of movement

Etymology

From Latin 'iners' (inactive, idle, helpless).
