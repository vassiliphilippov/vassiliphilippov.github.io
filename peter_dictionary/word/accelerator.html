accelerator

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛləˌreɪtə / (BrE)
A device, especially a pedal or lever in a vehicle, that controls the supply of fuel to the engine and thus regulates its speed.

Examples

Now she kept herself protectively ready to laugh again and sure enough he pointed at her with his index finger and said "Toot "! once more and roared off into the fog, his foot evidently surprising him with the suddenness with which it pressed the accelerator, just as his hand did when he worked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas pedal - pedal that controls the throttle in a vehicle
  • Antonym: brake - a device for slowing or stopping a vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten).

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
A pedal in a vehicle that is pressed by the driver to control the engine's speed by regulating the throttle.

Examples

Putting the key into the switch, pressing the accelerator with his foot, putting the car into reverse, seemed vast endeavors almost beyond the ability of his shaking body.
I let up on the accelerator, only to gradually reach again the 60 m. p. h. which would, I hoped, overhaul Herry and the blonde, and as there were cars whose drivers apparently had something more important to catch than had I, Mrs. Major Roebuck settled down to practicing on Corporal Johnson the kittenish wiles she would need when making her duty call on Colonel and Mrs. Somebody in Sante Fe.

Synset Examples

he stepped on the gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas pedal - the pedal that controls the speed of a vehicle's engine
  • Antonym: brake pedal - the pedal in a vehicle used to slow down or stop

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare', meaning to hasten, from 'ad-' (to) + 'celer' (swift).
