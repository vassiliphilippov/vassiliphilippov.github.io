parameter

1. NOUN

/ pəˈræm.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / pəˈræm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
A quantity or constant in a mathematical equation that, when varied, yields different but related curves or outcomes.

Examples

In order to reduce the number of variable parameters, all experiments were made with a constant arc length of 0.5 "and a current of 100 Amp.
The elasticity as a parameter of fluids which is not subject to simple measurement at present, and it is a parameter which is probably varying in an unknown manner with many commercial materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable - a quantity that can change or vary in a mathematical equation

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside) + 'metron' (measure) via French 'paramètre'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈræm.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / pəˈræm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
A measurable or definable factor or characteristic that helps to define a system and limits or determines its performance.

Examples

The calibration of piezoelectric sensors in terms of the particle parameters is very uncertain.
Tone languages use for linguistic contrasts speech parameters which also function heavily in nonlinguistic use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - something that contributes to a result
  • Synonym: constraint - something that limits what is possible

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside) + 'metron' (measure).
