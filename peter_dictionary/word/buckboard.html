buckboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌk.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.bɔːd / (BrE)
a horse-drawn carriage with four wheels and an open seat, mounted on a flexible board between two axles, typically used in North America in the 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

Harnessing a team to a buckboard, they drove out to a willow lined creek about a half-mile off, then climbed down and began chopping.
He heaved the dead man onto the buckboard, yelled and lashed at the team and got out of there fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a four-wheeled vehicle for conveying passengers

Etymology

From 'buck' + 'board,' referring to the flexible board that bucks or springs during motion.
