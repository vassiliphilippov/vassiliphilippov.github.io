implement

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪmɛnt / (BrE)
to put (a plan, decision, agreement, etc.) into effect; to carry out or execute something according to its purpose or design

Examples

In the last analysis, religion is the means of inducing, formulating, expressing, enhancing, implementing, and perpetuating man's deepest experience - the religious.
But none of these has been implemented.
He wanted to buy time to strengthen the U. S. and its allies and to define and begin to implement his foreign policy.

Synset Examples

implement a procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execute - to carry out or put into effect (a plan, order, or course of action)
  • Synonym: enforce - to make sure a law, rule, or obligation is carried out

Etymology

From Late Latin implementum (filling up, fulfillment), from Latin implēre (to fill up).
