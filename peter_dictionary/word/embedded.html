embedded

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbɛdɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛdɪd / (BrE)
Fixed firmly and deeply within a surrounding substance or mass.

Examples

He scored a 4 for the embedded ball, a 3 with the provisional one.

Synset Examples

found pebbles embedded in the silt
stone containing many embedded fossils
peach and plum seeds embedded in a sweet edible pulp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inserted - put or placed into something
  • Antonym: exposed - not enclosed; visible or open to view

Etymology

Past participle of 'embed', from 'em-' (variant of 'en-', meaning 'in') + 'bed'.
