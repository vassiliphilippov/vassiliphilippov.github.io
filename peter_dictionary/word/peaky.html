peaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈpiːki / (AmE), / ˈpiːki / (BrE)
(of a frequency response or sound) having especially high, sharp, or pronounced peaks, often resulting in harshness or listener fatigue.

Examples

This absence of peaky highs and beefed-up bass not only produces greater fidelity, but it eliminates listener fatigue.

Synset Examples

absence of peaky highs and beefed-up bass

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - lacking abrupt peaks or troughs in sound; even or flat in frequency response

Etymology

From 'peak' + '-y', indicating the presence of peaks.
