hepatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A disease characterized by inflammation of the liver, typically caused by a viral infection or exposure to toxins.

Examples

"I got a hundred and sixty dollars for the hepatitis fund ", she said excitedly when he walked in.

Etymology

From Greek 'hepar' (liver) + '-itis' (inflammation).
