deluge

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.judʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.juːdʒ / (BrE)
A large amount or overwhelming quantity of something occurring at once.

Examples

Teeth again flashing back at me, the driver released a deluge of Spanish in which "amigo "appeared every so often like an island in the stormy waves of surrounding sound.

Synset Examples

a flood of requests
a torrent of abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overwhelming amount of something
  • Synonym: torrent - a large and fast stream or outpouring, especially of words or feelings

Etymology

From Old French *deluge*, from Latin *diluvium* (flood).
