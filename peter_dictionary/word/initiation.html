initiation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal process or ceremony of becoming a member of an organization, group, or position

Examples

It was my initiation to war and the insane symphony war plays; for what had happened on the common was only terror and flight; but this grinning, broken head, not ten feet away from me, was the sharp definition of what my reality had become.
Initiation into combat sometimes elicited from soldier correspondents choice comments about their experiences and reactions.

Synset Examples

his initiation into the club
he was ordered to report for induction into the army
he gave a speech as part of his installation into the hall of fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction - formal admission to a position or group
  • Synonym: installation - formal placement in an office or position

Etymology

From Latin initiatio, from initiare (to begin, to initiate).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɪsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of beginning something or introducing something new for the first time

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.

Synset Examples

she looked forward to her initiation as an adult
the foundation of a new scientific society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement - the beginning or start of something
  • Synonym: inception - the establishment or starting point of something
  • Antonym: conclusion - the end or finish of something

Etymology

From Latin initiatio (a beginning), from initiare (to begin).
