disqualify

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something ineligible or unfit for a particular position, activity, or privilege, often because of a rule or requirement

Examples

Since the hero, a sterling and upright fellow, is a rich Brown senior, while two Yalies are cast as virtual rapists, I suppose I should disqualify myself from sitting in judgment on "Where the Boys are ", but I shall do nothing of the sort.

Synset Examples

Your income disqualifies you

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualify - to make eligible or fit

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'disqualificare', from Latin 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'qualificare' (to make of a certain quality).
