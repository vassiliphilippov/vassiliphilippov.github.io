Cassite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæs.aɪt / (BrE)
An ancient language spoken by the Kassite people, primarily in Mesopotamia during the second millennium BCE.

Examples

He was almost positive it was not Assyrian nor Cassite, and imagined it must have been German or English.

Etymology

From the name of the Kassite people, an ancient group in Mesopotamia.
