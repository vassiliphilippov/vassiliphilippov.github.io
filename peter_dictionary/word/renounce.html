renounce

1. VERB

/ rɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
formally give up or reject a right, claim, power, or responsibility, especially by public declaration

Examples

And all his pomps? - I do renounce them.

Synset Examples

The King abdicated when he married a divorcee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdicate - to give up a position of power, especially by a monarch

Etymology

From Old French *renoncier*, from Latin *renuntiare* ('to report, renounce')

2. VERB

/ rɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / rɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
To formally declare one's abandonment of (a claim, right, belief, or other thing), or to give up voluntarily.

Examples

I do renounce him.

Synset Examples

I am foreswearing women forever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily give up or let go of something
  • Synonym: abandon - to give up completely; forsake
  • Antonym: adopt - to take up or accept, especially as a practice or position

Etymology

From Old French *renoncer*, from Latin *renuntiare* (to announce back, reject).
