hermetic

1. ADJ

/ hɚˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / hɜːˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a container, closure, etc.) completely sealed so that no air can enter or escape; airtight.

Examples

Cubism, in its 1911 - 1912 phase (which the French, with justice, call "hermetic ") was on the verge of abstract art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airtight - not allowing air to enter or escape; sealed tightly

Etymology

From Latin 'hermeticus', from Hermes Trismegistus, associated with secret alchemical writings sealed so as to be airtight.
