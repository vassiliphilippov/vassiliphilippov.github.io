bridle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdəl / (BrE)
A piece of headgear for a horse, consisting of a headstall, bit, and reins, used by the rider or driver to control the animal.

Examples

Seizing the bridle, he tugged with all his might and forced Katie to her knees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - the mouthpiece of a bridle that goes in a horse's mouth
  • Synonym: rein - the straps attached to a bridle for controlling a horse
  • Synonym: headstall - the part of the bridle that fits around the horse's head

Etymology

Old English brīdel (restraint, curb), related to 'bridan' (to restrain, curb).
