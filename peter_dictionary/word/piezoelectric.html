piezoelectric

1. ADJ

/ ˌpiːzoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpiːzɪəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (BrE)
(of a material or device) relating to or exhibiting piezoelectricity, the property of generating an electric charge in response to applied mechanical stress.

Examples

The direct evidence on the micrometeorite environment near the Earth is obtained from piezoelectric sensors (essentially microphones) and from wire gages; these instruments are installed on rockets, satellites, and space probes.
The calibration of piezoelectric sensors in terms of the particle parameters is very uncertain.

Synset Examples

piezoelectric plates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrostrictive - relating to the property of changing shape under an electric field

Etymology

From French 'piézoélectrique', from Greek 'piezein' (to press) and 'electric'.
