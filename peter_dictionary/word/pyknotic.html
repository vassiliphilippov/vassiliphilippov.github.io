pyknotic

1. ADJ

/ pɪkˈnɑːtɪk / (AmE), / pɪkˈnɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a cell or its nucleus) showing or relating to the process of pyknosis, in which the nucleus shrinks and becomes intensely stained, often as a sign of cell death.

Examples

Such swollen fibers were deeply eosinophilic, contained a few pyknotic nuclei, and showed loss of cross striations, obliteration of myofibrils, and prominent vacuolization.
Individual nuclei were usually oval to round, though occasionally elongated, and frequently small and somewhat pyknotic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyknosis - the irreversible condensation of chromatin in the nucleus of a cell undergoing cell death

Etymology

From Greek 'pyknos' meaning 'thick' or 'dense', via medical terminology.
