treble

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛbəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛbəl / (BrE)
(of a sound, voice, or instrument) having or producing a high pitch or range, especially the highest part in choral music or among musical instruments.

Examples

Suddenly a treble auto horn tootley-toot-tootled, and, thumbing hopefully, I saw emergent in windshield flash: red lips, streaming silk of blonde hair and - ah, trembling confusion of hope, apprehension, despair - the leering face of old Herry.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bass - the lowest part in music; low-pitched

Etymology

From Old French *treble* (threefold), from Latin *triplus* (triple), originally referring to the highest of three parts in medieval music.
