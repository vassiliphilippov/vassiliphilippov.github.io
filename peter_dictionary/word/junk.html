junk

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk / (BrE)
pieces of material or remains of things that have been destroyed, broken, or discarded; debris.

Examples

Cheap clothing and junk of every sort, as far as the eye could see.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered pieces left after destruction
  • Synonym: wreckage - remains of something that has been destroyed
  • Synonym: garbage - waste material or unwanted things

Etymology

Probably from Old French 'junc' (reed, rush), comparing discarded objects to bundles of plant stalks.

2. VERB

/ dʒʌŋk / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk / (BrE)
to discard or dispose of something as useless or no longer wanted

Examples

By maintaining the nuclear deterrent, but gearing American military forces to fight conventional wars too, Secretary of State Rusk junks bluff and nuclear brinkmanship and builds more muscle and greater safety into our military position.

Synset Examples

trash these old chairs
junk an old car
scrap your old computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer useful or wanted
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of; not discard
