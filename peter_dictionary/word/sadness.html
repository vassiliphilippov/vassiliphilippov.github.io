sadness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædnəs / (AmE), / ˈsædnəs / (BrE)
The feeling of being unhappy or sorrowful.

Examples

He had had the need to understand what life lurked behind the mask of flesh, behind the oath, the banter, the sadness.
There would still be plenty of moments of regret and sadness and guilty relief.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happiness - the state of being happy
  • Degree Higher: melancholy - a deep, persistent sadness
  • Degree Lower: displeasure - a mild feeling of unhappiness or annoyance

Etymology

From sad + -ness; 'sad' from Old English sæd (sated, weary, tired), but sense shifted to 'unhappy' in Middle English.
