recklessly

1. ADV

/ ˈrɛklɪsli / (AmE), / ˈrɛkləsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of care about danger or the possible results of your actions; without regard for risk or consequences.

Examples

Recklessly hurling the bronc sidewise into an intersecting draw, he plunged forward with undiminished speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessly - without care or attention
  • Antonym: cautiously - in a careful, wary manner

Etymology

From 'reckless' + '-ly', where 'reckless' derives from Middle English 'rekeles', from Old English 'receleas' (careless, heedless).
