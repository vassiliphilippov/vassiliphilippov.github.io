restoration

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving something back to its original owner or returning something to a former condition

Examples

Moreover, as Communist power increases, the enjoyment of these conditions throughout the world diminishes pro rata and the possibility of their restoration becomes increasingly remote.

Synset Examples

upon the restitution of the book to its rightful owner the child was given a tongue lashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restitution - the return of something lost or stolen to its proper owner

Etymology

From Latin 'restauratio' (a renewing, restoration), from 'restaurare' (to renew, restore).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of returning something or someone to a satisfactory or original condition

Examples

Recognizing the limitations of such a program, the 78 th Congress in 1943 passed P. L. 113, which broadened the concept of rehabilitation to include the provision of physical restoration services to remove or reduce disabilities, and which revised the financing structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recovery - the process of becoming healthy or normal again
  • Synonym: repair - the act of fixing or mending something damaged

Etymology

From Latin restitutio, from restituere (to restore), via Old French restauration.
