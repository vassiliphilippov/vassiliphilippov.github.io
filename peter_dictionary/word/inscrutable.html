inscrutable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈskrutəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈskruːtəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to understand or interpret; mysterious or unfathomable in meaning or expression.

Examples

He was a wiry, inscrutable, silent country boy from the red clay of rural Alabama, and he spoke with the broad drawl that others normally make fun of.

Synset Examples

the new insurance policy is written without cryptic or mysterious terms
a deep dark secret
the inscrutable workings of Providence
in its mysterious past it encompasses all the dim origins of life
rituals totally mystifying to visitors from other lands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysterious - difficult or impossible to understand, explain, or identify
  • Antonym: transparent - easy to perceive or detect; obvious

Etymology

From Late Latin *inscrutabilis*, from Latin *in-* (not) + *scrutari* (to search, examine).
