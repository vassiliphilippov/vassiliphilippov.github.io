conservation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of preventing loss, injury, or change, especially to protect or preserve something valuable or important.

Examples

A stream which has all of its watershed within a national forest or other lands under good conservation practices is less likely to be affected by pollution than one passing through unrestricted logging or past an industrial area.
It has thus been a significant factor in the conservation of social values, though also in some measure, an obstacle to the creation or diffusion of new ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservation - the act of maintaining something in its original condition
  • Antonym: neglect - the state of being uncared for

Etymology

From Latin conservatio, from conservare 'to preserve'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The protection and careful management of the natural environment and natural resources to prevent exploitation, destruction, or neglect.

Examples

Present conservation practices regard wildlife, not as an expendable natural resource, but as an annual harvest to be sown and also reaped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state or in good condition
  • Antonym: depletion - serious reduction in the amount of something, especially natural resources

Etymology

From Latin conservatio (preservation), from conservare (to preserve).
