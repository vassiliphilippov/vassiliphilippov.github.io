waiting

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of people or things) ready and available for use, action, or service.

Examples

Then, one after another, the villagers tied the waiting cotton strings around our wrists.
Lolotte held a patch of leather, Rev steadied something, a tiny brad, waiting for George's poised hammer.
We have a table waiting.

Synset Examples

waiting cars and limousines lined the curb
found her mother waiting for them
an impressive array of food ready and waiting for the guests
military forces ready and waiting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - available for immediate use or action

Etymology

Present participle of 'wait', from Old Northern French *waiter*, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of staying in one place or remaining inactive until something expected happens or someone arrives

Examples

And then you worry about waiting on line to see your brother.

Synset Examples

the wait was an ordeal for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wait - a period of waiting; an instance of waiting
  • Degree Lower: anticipation - the act of anticipating; looking forward to something
  • Antonym: impatience - the lack of patience while waiting

Etymology

From the verb 'wait,' ultimately from Old Northern French 'waitier' (to watch).
