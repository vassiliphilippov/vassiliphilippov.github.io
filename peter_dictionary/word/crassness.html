crassness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being insensitive, rude, or lacking in refinement or delicacy

Examples

If the barn-burner's family produces a Flem Snopes, who personifies commercialism and materialism in hyperbolic crassness, the Compson family produces a Jason Compson 4,.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - quality of being vulgar or crude
  • Antonym: refinement - cultivated taste, elegance; opposite of crassness

Etymology

From Latin 'crassus' (thick, dense), via 'crass' + '-ness'.
