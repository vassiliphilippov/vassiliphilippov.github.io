boldness

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to take risks or face danger confidently and courageously

Examples

But of course behind his boldness he did n't feel bold at all.

Synset Examples

the proposal required great boldness
the plan required great hardiness of heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to confront fear, pain, or adversity
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Old English *beald* or *bold* (bold, courageous) + -ness (forming nouns).
