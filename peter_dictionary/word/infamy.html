infamy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fə.mi / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fə.mi / (BrE)
the state of being well known for some bad quality or deed; an evil or disgraceful reputation

Examples

That's an infámia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fame - the state of being widely known or recognized
  • Synonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for some bad quality or deed

Etymology

From Latin *infamia* (disgrace, ill repute), from *infamis* (notorious, infamous), from *in-* (not) + *fama* (reputation).
