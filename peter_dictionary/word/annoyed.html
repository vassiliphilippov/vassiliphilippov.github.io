annoyed

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪd / (BrE)
feeling slightly angry or impatient about something; irritated

Examples

He grew annoyed and at the same time surprised at that emotion.
Still another, annoyed by the brevity of a recently received missive, wrote: "Yore letter was short and sweet, jist like a roasted maget ".
He hung up with a bang, annoyed at himself for running off at the mouth like that but still terribly concerned with the situation he had helped to create.

Synset Examples

made an irritated gesture
feeling nettled from the constant teasing
peeved about being left out
felt really pissed at her snootiness
riled no end by his lies
roiled by the delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritated - feeling slight anger or impatience; annoyed
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling angry or agitated

Etymology

Past participle of 'annoy', from Old French 'anoier', from Late Latin 'inodiare' (to make loathsome).
