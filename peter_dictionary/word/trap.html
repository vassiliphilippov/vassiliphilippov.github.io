3. NOUN

/ træp / (AmE), / træp / (BrE)
a device for catching and confining animals or other creatures, typically with a mechanism that holds or restrains them

Examples

We come upon a rabbit that has been caught in one of the brutal traps in common use.
Let us suppose that the animal did not in fact fall into the trap and did not suffer at all, but that we mistakenly believe it did, and say as before that its suffering was an evil thing.

It is to be a trap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snare - a device for catching animals, especially a wire loop
  • Degree Higher: cage - a structure for confining animals

Etymology

From Old English trǣppe (a trap, a snare).

4. VERB
