familiar

1. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljər / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
Well known, easily recognized, or often encountered.

Examples

Most of the female faces are new, or at least not too familiar.
When a man recognizes a certain experience as the exact pattern of a previous dream, we have an instance of deja vu, except for the fact that he knows just why the experience seems familiar.
When we arrive at the events concerned in the vanished majority, they, of course, cannot impress us as anything familiar.
It was a part so familiar to him that he did not bother to think about it any more.
The place was evidently a familiar haunt and Claire wondered what other illicit loves had been celebrated in the comfortable rooms to which they were shown.

Synset Examples

a familiar figure
familiar songs
familiar guests

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfamiliar - not known or recognized
  • Synonym: well-known - widely recognized or known

Etymology

From Latin familiaris (of a household, domestic, intimate), from familia (family).

2. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljɚ / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
commonly encountered or experienced; usual and ordinary; not strange or rare

Examples

At a siding, another train which was a familiar sight these days.

Synset Examples

familiar ordinary objects found in every home
a familiar everyday scene
a familiar excuse
a day like any other filled with familiar duties and experiences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfamiliar - not known or recognized; not previously encountered
  • Synonym: common - occurring often; usual; not rare
  • Synonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, domestic, familiar).

3. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljər / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
well informed about or thoroughly acquainted with a person, place, subject, or thing, especially as in 'familiar with'.

Examples

Even beginning students in linguistics are made familiar with an appreciable variety of consonant systems, both in their general outlines and in many specific details.

Synset Examples

conversant with business trends
familiar with the complex machinery
he was familiar with those roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfamiliar - not well acquainted with; not knowledgeable about

Etymology

From Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, domestic; friendly), from 'familia' (family).
