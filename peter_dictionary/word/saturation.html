saturation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of thoroughly soaking or impregnating something with a substance so that no more can be absorbed.

Examples

The rf power level was maintained small enough at all times to prevent obvious line shape distortions by saturation effects.
A single value of the thermal relaxation time f at room temperature was measured by the progressive saturation** method.

Synset Examples

the impregnation of wood with preservative
the saturation of cotton with ether

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impregnation - the process of soaking or infusing thoroughly

Etymology

From Latin saturatio(n-), from saturare (to fill, to satisfy).
