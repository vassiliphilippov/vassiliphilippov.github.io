cackle

1. VERB

/ ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
(of a bird, especially a hen) to make a shrill, loud, rapid sound.

Examples

He stroked the hens and they responded with delighted clucks, he gobbled with the turkeys and they at once were all attention, he quacked with the ducks, and cackled with a pair of exceedingly flattered geese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluck - to make a short, low sound typical of a hen

Etymology

Imitative of a hen's cry, first recorded in Middle English.

2. VERB

/ ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
to laugh or speak in a shrill, harsh, or hen-like manner

Examples

"Tee-wah ", the driver cackled, his black eyes glittering behind dull silver chicken fencing.

Synset Examples

The women cackled when they saw the movie star step out of the limousine

Etymology

Imitative origin: from the sound of a hen's cry.
