learning

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of acquiring knowledge or skill through study, experience, or teaching.

Examples

"Good heavens, Adam ", he said, "I thought one thing you 'd have no trouble learning is when to get out of a place ".
Failure confirms the threat, and the intensity of anxiety is increased as the required learning becomes more difficult, so that by the time the child reaches the third grade the decrement in performance is pronounced.

Synset Examples

the child's acquisition of language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - systematic instruction or training
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information

Etymology

From Old English 'leornian' (to learn), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
deep and extensive knowledge, especially gained through study or scholarship

Examples

Sixty five per cent of the Jewish teen-agers of college age attend institutions of higher learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erudition - deep, extensive learning or knowledge
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or education

Etymology

From Old English *leornung* (the act of learning), from *leornian* (to learn).
