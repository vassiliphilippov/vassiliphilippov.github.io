seawater

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water from the sea or ocean, containing dissolved salts and minerals.

Examples

As used in this Act, the term' saline water' includes sea water, brackish water, and other mineralized or chemically charged water, and the term' United States' extends to and includes the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, and the territories and possessions of the United States.

Synset Examples

the water in the ocean is all saltwater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltwater - water containing significant concentrations of dissolved salts; another term for seawater
  • Antonym: freshwater - water with very low concentrations of dissolved salts, as found in rivers and lakes
