vellum

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛləm / (AmE), / ˈvɛləm / (BrE)
a fine-quality parchment-like material, originally made from calfskin but now often a heavy, creamy-colored paper, used for writing or printing

Examples

There she extracted two limp vellum sheets and wrote off the letters, one to Abel, one to Mark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parchment - a material made from animal skin, used as a writing surface

Etymology

From Old French *velin* (calfskin), from *veau* (calf), from Latin *vitellus* (little calf).
