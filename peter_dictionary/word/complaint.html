complaint

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpleɪnt / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪnt / (BrE)
A bodily disorder or disease, often persistent; a medical condition or ailment.

Examples

By Nov. 8, 1958, weakness, specifically involving the pelvic and thigh musculature, was pronounced, and a common complaint was "difficulty in stepping up on to curbs ".
A high protein diet, calcium lactate supplements, and norethandrolone failed to change the skeletal complaint or the severe muscle weakness.
What is the common man's complaint?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ailment - a minor illness or bodily disorder
  • Synonym: disease - a disorder of structure or function in a living organism
  • Synonym: condition - a medical problem or illness

Etymology

From Old French 'complaindre' (to lament, bewail), from Latin 'complangere' (to bewail, lament).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpleɪnt / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪnt / (BrE)
an expression of dissatisfaction, resentment, or grievance about something

Examples

Once more, Juet's complaints were the loudest.
He's supposed to be very good with a knife, but only in matters of business with reasonable complaint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grievance - a real or imagined cause for complaint or protest
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'compleinte', from Latin 'complangere' (to lament or bewail).
