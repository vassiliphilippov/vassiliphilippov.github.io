bowl

1. NOUN

/ boʊl / (AmE), / bəʊl / (BrE)
a round, deep container with a curved, open top, used for holding food or liquid; or any object with a similar concave shape

Examples

As Hartweger accepted his silver bowl, he said, "I want to thank coach's wife for talking him into letting me play ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dish - a shallow container for serving food
  • Degree Lower: cup - a small bowl-shaped drinking vessel

Etymology

From Old French *boule* (bowl), from Latin *bulla* (bubble, knob, round object).

2. NOUN

/ boʊl / (AmE), / bəʊl / (BrE)
A round, deep dish or vessel, open at the top, used chiefly for holding food or liquids.

Examples

The rows of animals and birds, in particular, suggest awareness of Oriental animal friezes, transmitted perhaps via Syrian silver bowls and textiles, but the specific forms of these rows on local vases and metal products are nonetheless Greek.
The strings were draped around flowers in tall silver bowls (page 261).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dish - a shallow, typically flat container for serving or holding food
  • Degree Lower: plate - a flat dish from which food is eaten or served
  • Degree Lower: cup - a small vessel for drinking, typically with a handle

Etymology

Old English *bolla* (bowl, cup), from Proto-Germanic *bullō*.

3. NOUN

/ boʊl / (AmE), / bəʊl / (BrE)
A round, deep dish with an open top, used for serving or eating food.

Examples

Flies swarmed around the bowl of lentils in which he mopped thick black bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dish - flat, typically shallow container for serving food
  • Degree Lower: cup - small round container, typically with a handle, used for drinking
  • Degree Higher: basin - larger, open, and often deep round container

Etymology

From Old French 'bole', from Latin 'bulla' (bubble, knob, round swelling).
