bravery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈbreɪvəri / (BrE)
Courageous behavior or character; the quality of facing danger or pain without showing fear.

Examples

The Providence Daily Journal, however, stated that Brown's courage, bravery, and heroism "in a good cause would make a man a martyr; it gives something of dignity even to a bad one ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; fearfulness

Etymology

From French *braverie*, from *brave* (brave).
