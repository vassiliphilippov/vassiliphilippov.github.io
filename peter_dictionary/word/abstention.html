abstention

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstɛnʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstɛnʃən / (BrE)
The practice of deliberately choosing not to partake in something, especially alcohol or other indulgences.

Examples

The Roman Catholic Church sanctions only abstention or the rhythm method, also known as the use of the infertile or safe period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstinence - the practice of refraining from certain indulgences, especially alcohol or sex

Etymology

From Latin 'abstentionem', from 'abstinere' (to withhold).
