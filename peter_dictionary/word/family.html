family

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A group of people related by blood, marriage, or adoption, who usually live together as a unit.

Examples

The report called racial discrimination in employment "one of the most serious causes of family breakdown, desertion, and ADC dependency ".
These, he said, are "two of the principal underlying causes for family breakups leading to ADC ".

Bill Jr., 20; Kathy, 15, and Patricia, 9, round out the Shea family.
The brushes can be cleaned and sterilized by boiling and are detachable so that every member of the family can have his own.

Synset Examples

he moved his family to Virginia
It was a good Christian household
I waited until the whole house was asleep
the teacher asked how many people made up his home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: household - people living together in the same dwelling

Etymology

From Latin 'familia' (household, family servants, those living under one roof).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈfæməli / (BrE)
A group of related plants or animals classified together in biology, ranking above genus and below order.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: genus - biological rank below family, above species
  • Degree Higher: order - biological rank above family, below class

Etymology

From Latin 'familia' (household, family).
