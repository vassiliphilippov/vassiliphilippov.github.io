beatnik

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːt.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈbiːt.nɪk / (BrE)
A person belonging to the Beat Generation or adopting its unconventional way of life, especially in dress and behavior; a nonconformist associated with the 1950s and early 1960s.

Examples

The Champs Elysees is literally littered this summer with the prostrate bodies of France's beat-up beatnik jeunes filles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bohemian - person with unconventional social habits, often artistic

Etymology

Formed from 'beat' (from 'Beat Generation') + Russian diminutive suffix '-nik', popularized in 1950s America.
