crafter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræftər / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːftə / (BrE)
A person who creates objects with skill, especially by hand, in the manual arts or crafts.

Examples

Stowey Rummel was internationally famous, a crafter of a genuine Americana in foreign eyes, an original designer whose inventive childishness with steel and concrete was made even more believably sincere by his personality.

Synset Examples

the jewelry was made by internationally famous craftsmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craftsman - person skilled in a particular craft
  • Synonym: artisan - a worker skilled in a trade, especially one that involves making things by hand

Etymology

From 'craft' (Old English cræft, meaning skill or strength) + '-er' (denoting agent).
