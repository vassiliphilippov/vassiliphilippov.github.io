disengage

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to release or detach something from a connection or entanglement; to separate oneself or something from attachment, involvement, or association

Examples

Then during washing, the greasy soil rolls back at the edges so that emulsified droplets can disengage themselves from the sorbed oil mass, with the aid of mechanical action, and enter the aqueous phase.

Synset Examples

I want to disengage myself from his influence
disengage the gears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detach - to separate something that is attached
  • Synonym: release - to set free or allow to be free
  • Antonym: engage - to occupy, attract, or involve (opposite of disengage)

Etymology

From French désengager (to set free), from dés- (dis-, un-) + engager (to engage).
