overhear

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈhɪr / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɪə / (BrE)
to accidentally or unintentionally hear what other people are saying without their knowledge

Synset Examples

We overheard the conversation at the next table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eavesdrop - to secretly listen to the private conversation of others

Etymology

From over- + hear, dating from Middle English.
