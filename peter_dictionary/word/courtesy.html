courtesy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtəsi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtəsi / (BrE)
A polite, respectful, or considerate act or gesture.

Examples

In any case, I have always been treated with the utmost courtesy by Englishmen, even in Devonshire and Cornwall, where anti Catholic feeling has supposedly existed the strongest and longest.
And social relations arising out of business ties impose courtesy, if not sympathy, toward resident and visiting Northerners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: politeness - the quality of being polite or well-mannered
  • Antonym: rudeness - the quality of being rude or impolite

Etymology

From Old French 'courtoisie', from 'courtois' (courteous), ultimately from court (court).
