prejudicial

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɛdʒəˈdɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌprɛdʒʊˈdɪʃəl / (BrE)
causing harm or injury; having a detrimental effect on someone or something, often used to describe evidence, publicity, or actions that could bias or negatively affect a situation, especially in legal contexts.

Examples

In many cases that statement "We break even on our downtown operation and make money on our branches "would be turned around if the cost analysis were recalculated on terms less prejudicial to the old store.

Synset Examples

damaging to career and reputation
the reporter's coverage resulted in prejudicial publicity for the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damaging - causing harm or injury; detrimental
  • Antonym: beneficial - producing good or helpful results; advantageous

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praeiudicialis, from Latin praeiudicium (previous judgment).
