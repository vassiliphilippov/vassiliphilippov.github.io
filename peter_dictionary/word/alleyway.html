alleyway

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈæli.weɪ / (BrE)
A narrow passage or street between or behind buildings, typically with walls or fences on both sides.

Examples

Only paved alleyways tunneled through the walls of those fortresses into the mysterious core of intimacy behind the houses where backyards owned no fences, where one man's property blended with the next to form courtyards in which no one knew privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alley - a narrow passageway between or behind buildings

Etymology

Formed from 'alley' + 'way'; 'alley' is from Old French 'alee' (a path), ultimately from Latin 'ambulare' (to walk).
