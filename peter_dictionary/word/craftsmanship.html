craftsmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræftsmənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːftsmənˌʃɪp / (BrE)
Skill and artistry in making something by hand, especially in a specific trade or craft.

Examples

He was an artisan, a man who studied his trade and developed his craftsmanship the way a goldsmith or a wood carver did.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well, expertise

Etymology

From 'craftsman' + '-ship'; 'craftsman' from Old English 'cræft' (skill) and 'man', '-ship' as a noun-forming suffix.
