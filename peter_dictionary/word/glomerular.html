glomerular

1. ADJ

/ ɡləˈmɛr.jələr / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈmɛr.jʊlə / (BrE)
Relating to or affecting glomeruli, especially those in the kidneys.

Examples

Microscopically, both kidneys showed many small cortical scars in which there was glomerular and interstitial fibrosis, tubular atrophy, and an infiltration of lymphocytes and plasma cells.
Throughout, there were marked arteriolosclerosis and hyalinization of afferent glomerular arterioles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glomerulus - a tiny ball-shaped structure in the kidney, involved in filtering blood

Etymology

From glomerulus (Latin: a small ball) + -ar (forming adjectives).
