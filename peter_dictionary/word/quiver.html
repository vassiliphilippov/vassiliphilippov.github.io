quiver

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪvə / (BrE)
to shake with slight, rapid, tremulous movement

Examples

By comparison, Fritzie and Laura Andrus were quivering fledglings.
"Deegan ", the manager said, his voice pitched low, quivering.
They were about a mile off; under me the ground quivered slightly.

Synset Examples

His nostrils palpitated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake slightly, often due to fear or cold
  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • Synonym: shiver - to shake slightly because of being cold or frightened
  • Antonym: steady - to stop from shaking; stable and not moving

Etymology

From Middle English 'quiveren' (to tremble), perhaps imitative in origin.
