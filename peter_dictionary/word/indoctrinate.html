indoctrinate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɑktrəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɒktrɪneɪt / (BrE)
to teach a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically, especially with the aim of influencing opinions or behavior

Examples

The assumptions upon which the example shown in Figure 3 is based are: (a) One man can direct about six subordinates if the subordinates are chosen carefully so that they do not need too much personal coaching, indoctrinating, etc..

Synset Examples

The Moonies indoctrinate their disciples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainwash - to manipulate someone into adopting radically different beliefs, typically forcibly

Etymology

From French 'endoctriner', from 'en-' (in) + 'doctrine' (teaching, instruction).
