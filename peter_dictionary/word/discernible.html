discernible

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnəbl / (BrE)
Able to be perceived by the senses or the mind; noticeable or detectable.

Examples

Lucretius has remarked: "The reason why all Mortals are so gripped by fear is that they see all sorts of things happening in the earth and sky with no discernable cause, and these they attribute to the will of God ".

Synset Examples

things happen in the earth and sky with no discernible cause
the newspaper reports no discernible progress in the negotiations
the skyline is easily discernible even at a distance of several miles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be seen or noticed; detectable
  • Synonym: noticeable - easily seen or noticed
  • Antonym: indiscernible - impossible to see or clearly distinguish

Etymology

From Latin discernere (to distinguish) via Medieval Latin discernibilis.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɝːnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be perceived or recognized clearly; noticeable.

Examples

Weekly samples would make any changes more readily discernible than daily samples.

Synset Examples

an essay with a meaning that was not always discernible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indiscernible - not able to be perceived or recognized

Etymology

From Latin 'discernere' (to separate, distinguish) via French 'discernable'.

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnəbl / (BrE)
Able to be perceived by the senses or understood by the intellect; noticeable or detectable.

Examples

The continuities, contrasts, and similarities discernible when past and present are surveyed together are inexhaustible and the one is often understood through the other.
Over the rapidly diminishing outline of a jump seat piled high with luggage Herry's black brushcut was just discernible, near, or enviably near that spot where - hidden - more delicately textured, most beautifully tinted hair must still be streaming back in cool, oh cool wind sweetly perfumed with sagebrush and yucca flowers and engine fumes.
In the ditch sand was white and soft looking, only an occasional pebble discernible, faintly gleaming.

Synset Examples

things happen in the earth and sky with no discernible cause
the newspaper reports no discernible progress in the negotiations
the skyline is easily discernible even at a distance of several miles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be seen or noticed; detectable
  • Antonym: indiscernible - impossible to see or clearly distinguish

Etymology

From Latin 'discernibilis', from 'discernere' (to separate, distinguish).
