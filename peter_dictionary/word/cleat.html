cleat

1. NOUN

/ klit / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
A projecting strip or stud of metal, rubber, or other material on the sole of a shoe or boot, designed to prevent slipping.

Examples

The first thing every morning when she washed her face she could see the scar on her chin where he 'd cut her with a boot cleat, and maybe she saw him heaving it again, the dirty sock popping out as it flew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stud - a small projecting part on the sole of a shoe for grip

Etymology

From Old English 'clēat' meaning 'wedge' or 'lump', related to Old Norse 'kliuti' (piece of wood).
