lane

1. NOUN

/ leɪn / (AmE), / leɪn / (BrE)
A narrow road or path, especially in the countryside.

Examples

They cannot stop to grasp and embrace and sit in the back seat of cars along a dark country lane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alley - a narrow passageway between or behind buildings
  • Degree Higher: road - a wide way leading from one place to another, especially for vehicles

Etymology

From Old English 'lanu', meaning a narrow way or path.

2. NOUN

/ leɪn / (AmE), / leɪn / (BrE)
a narrow, defined track or path, especially one used by vehicles in a road, by swimmers in a pool, or for other organized movement

Examples

"She did n't mention bringing Myra ", Mark said, maneuvering the car into the next lane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a path or course laid out for running, racing, or other movement

Etymology

From Old English 'lanu' (narrow way, path).
