coccidiosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːk.sɪ.diˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒk.sɪ.diˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A disease of animals caused by infection with protozoan parasites of the genus Coccidia, leading to diarrhea and digestive problems.

Examples

Helps control shipping dysentery and coccidiosis in lambs.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Coccidia' (a genus of protozoa) + '-osis' (disease).
