self-consciousness

1. NOUN

/ sɛlfˈkɑnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / sɛlfˈkɒnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of embarrassment or awkwardness caused by the belief that other people are watching or judging you critically.

Examples

"Do you suppose his self-consciousness is characteristic of the new Negro professionals or merely of doctors in general "?
There was the freshness of color, the freedom of perception, the lack of self-consciousness, but with a twist that made the forms leap from the page and smack you in the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shyness - the quality of being nervous or timid in the presence of others
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of self-assurance arising from one's abilities or qualities

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to oneself) and 'consciousness' (the state of being aware), originally from Latin 'conscius' (knowing, aware).
