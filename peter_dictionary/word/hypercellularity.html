hypercellularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərsɛljuˈlærɪti / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəsɛljuːˈlarɪti / (BrE)
The state of having an abnormally high number of cells, often indicating a pathological condition.

Examples

Microscopically, there were many areas of hypercellularity alternating with areas of hypocellularity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypocellularity - the state of having abnormally few cells

Etymology

From hyper- (over, excessive) + cellularity (cellularity: presence of cells).
