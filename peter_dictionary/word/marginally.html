marginally

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑrdʒɪnəli / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnəli / (BrE)
To only a small extent; slightly.

Examples

American and free-world policies can marginally affect the pace of transition; but basically that pace depends on changes in the supply of resources and in the human attitudes, political institutions, and social structure which each society must generate.

Synset Examples

marginally interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slightly - to a small degree or extent
  • Antonym: considerably - to a great extent or degree

Etymology

From 'marginal' + '-ly'. 'Marginal' from Latin 'margo' meaning 'edge, border'.
