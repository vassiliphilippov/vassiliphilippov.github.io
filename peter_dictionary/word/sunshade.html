sunshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌnʃeɪd / (BrE)
A cover or canopy used to protect people or objects from direct sunlight, typically made of fabric or similar material and supported on a frame.

Examples

There were umbrella tents, wall tents, cottage tents, station wagon tents, pup tents, Pop tents, Baker tents, tents with exterior frames, camper trailers, travel trailers, and even a few surplus parachutes serving as sunshades over entire family camps.
Moving around camp we saw all kinds of camp stoves, lanterns, coolers, bedding, games, fishing tackle, windbreaks and sunshades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasol - a light umbrella used to give shade from the sun
  • Synonym: awning - a sheet of canvas or other material to keep the sun or rain off a doorway, window, or deck

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'shade', meaning something that provides shade from the sun.
