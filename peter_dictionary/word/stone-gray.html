stone-gray

1. ADJ

/ stoʊn ɡreɪ / (AmE), / stəʊn ɡreɪ / (BrE)
Of a gray color resembling natural stone, such as slate or granite.

Examples

Brittany, that stone-gray mystery through which he traveled for thirty days, sleeping in the barns of farmers or alongside roads, had worked some subtle change in him, he knew, and it was in Brittany that he had met Pierre.

Synset Examples

the slaty sky of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slate-gray - of a dark, bluish-gray color like slate stone
  • Degree Lower: ash-gray - of a light gray color like ashes
