communism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A political and economic theory advocating collective ownership of property and the means of production, aiming for a classless society in which goods are shared equally.

Examples

The Lenin tomb is obviously adequate for double occupancy, Moscow is a crowded city, and the creed of communism deplores waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialism - a political and economic theory advocating collective ownership, typically via state control
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free market

Etymology

From French 'communisme', from 'commun' (common), ultimately from Latin 'communis'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mjuˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.juˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
A political and economic system in which all property is publicly owned, and each person works and is paid according to their abilities and needs; a form of socialism that abolishes private ownership.
