churchgoing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɝtʃˌɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃˌɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
Regularly attending and actively participating in church services or religious worship.

Examples

Now, in 1961, the Catholic population of England is still quite small (ten per cent, or 5 million); yet it represents a very considerable percentage of the churchgoing population.
Now Pat may have been a lecher and a plug-ugly, but he was a good churchgoing Catholic and he loved his little sister.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreligious - not practicing any religion or attending church services

Etymology

From 'church' + 'going', meaning the act of going to church.
