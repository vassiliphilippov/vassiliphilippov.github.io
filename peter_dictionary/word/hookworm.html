hookworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈhʊkˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic roundworm with hooked mouthparts that attaches to the intestinal wall of humans or other animals and feeds on their blood

Examples

Reduces losses from stomach, hookworm, and nodular worms by interfering with reproduction of the female worm by reducing the number of eggs laid and essentially rendering all laid eggs sterile.

Etymology

From 'hook' + 'worm', describing its hooked mouthparts.
