tension

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
A state in which something is stretched tight or the condition of being made tense, especially in physical materials or muscles.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxation - the state of being less tense or relieved from tension

Etymology

From Latin 'tensio(n-)', from 'tendere' meaning 'to stretch'.
