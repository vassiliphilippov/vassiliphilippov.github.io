edgy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒi / (BrE)
Tense, nervous, or on edge; showing signs of anxiety or restlessness.

Examples

His portrayal of an edgy head-in-the-clouds artist is virtually flawless.
The violinist, in particular, is very indulgent with swoops and slides, and his tone is pinched and edgy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tense - stretched tight; feeling or showing nervousness or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or other emotions

Etymology

From 'edge' + '-y', originally referring to being on the literal edge, later figuratively to being tense or irritable.
