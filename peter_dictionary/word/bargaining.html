bargaining

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrɡənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡənɪŋ / (BrE)
The discussion and negotiation between parties to reach an agreement, especially about the terms of a transaction or contract.

Examples

For our present purposes we assume that the sole subject of bargaining is the basic wage rate (not including productivity improvement factors or cost-of-living adjustments), and it is this basic wage rate which determines the level of costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement

Etymology

From the verb 'bargain', from Old French *bargaignier* (to haggle, trade).
