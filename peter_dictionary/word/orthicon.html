orthicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrθɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈɔːθɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
an obsolete type of television camera tube in which electrons emitted from a photoemissive surface are focused onto a target, producing an image according to the light intensity

Examples

The microscope and orthicon are both selected to operate well into the ultraviolet spectrum, which means that all lenses must be quartz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vidicon - type of television camera tube that succeeded the orthicon

Etymology

From 'ortho-' (correct, straight) and '-icon' (from 'iconoscope', an earlier television camera tube), coined in the 1930s.
