thawing

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɔː.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of changing from a solid to a liquid state as a result of warming.

Examples

That keeps in the cold, retains moisture and prevents the heaving of alternate freezing and thawing.

Synset Examples

the power failure caused a refrigerator melt that was a disaster
the thawing of a frozen turkey takes several hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freezing - the process of changing from liquid to solid due to cold

Etymology

From 'thaw' (verb), Old English thawian, meaning to melt or become liquid.
