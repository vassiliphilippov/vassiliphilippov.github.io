trudge

1. VERB

/ trʌdʒ / (AmE), / trʌdʒ / (BrE)
To walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically because of tiredness or walking through challenging conditions like mud or snow.

Examples

He trudged on, his aching eyes focused straight ahead.

Synset Examples

Mules plodded in a circle around a grindstone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plod - walk heavily or slowly with no real purpose or energy
  • Synonym: slog - walk with difficulty, as through mud

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly imitative of plodding steps or related to Middle English 'trudgen' (to walk with heavy steps).
