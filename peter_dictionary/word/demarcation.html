demarcation

1. NOUN

/ diˌmɑːrˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːmɑːˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A boundary or line that marks the limits of a specific area; the act of setting such a boundary.

Examples

Voting preparations began in the fall of 1959, although the actual demarcation and planning for the rural communes was completed in 1958.
Even the loss of hotel business to the outskirt's motel has been relatively painless; the hotel-motel demarcation is becoming harder to find every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line that marks the limits of an area
  • Synonym: limit - the furthest edge or boundary of something

Etymology

From Spanish 'demarcación', from demarcar (to mark out), itself from Latin 'de-' (off, away) + 'marcare' (to mark).
