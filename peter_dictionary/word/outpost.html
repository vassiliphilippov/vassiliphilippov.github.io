outpost

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpəʊst / (BrE)
a small military camp or position stationed at some distance from the main force, used to guard against surprise attack

Examples

You could hear them from our outpost.

Etymology

From out + post; 'post' from Latin 'postis' (door, post, station).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌpəʊst / (BrE)
a small settlement or military post located at or near the edge of territory, often in a remote or frontier area, established to guard, protect, or advance interests.

Examples

In 1819 grasshoppers again destroyed the crop at "the Forks " (Fort Douglas) and in December 1819, twenty men left Fort Daer for the most northerly American outpost at Prairie du Chien.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fort - a fortified building or strategic military construction

Etymology

From out- + post, originally in military contexts.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpəʊst / (BrE)
A small station or settlement in a remote or sparsely populated place, often serving as a base for operations or observation.

Examples

It was from the American outposts that Red River shortages of livestock were to be made good.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: base - main center for operations or activities
  • Synonym: settlement - a place where people establish a community

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (outside) and 'post' (station, position).
