contingency

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
a possible event or situation, especially one that is not certain to happen and that must be prepared for

Examples

Our military forces must be capable of contending successfully with any contingency which may be forced upon us, from limited emergencies to all-out nuclear general war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eventuality - a possible event or outcome

Etymology

From Latin contingentia (chance, possibility), from contingere (to touch, happen).
