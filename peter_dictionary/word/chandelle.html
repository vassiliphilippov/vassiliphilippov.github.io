chandelle

1. VERB

/ ʃænˈdɛl / (AmE), / ʃænˈdɛl / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to climb suddenly and steeply in a climbing turn, especially as an evasive maneuver.

Examples

When driving rain or mist socked in one valley, Fogg would chandelle up and over to reverse course and try another one, ranging from the Ottauquechee up to Danville in search of safe passage through the mountain passes.

Synset Examples

The airplane chandelled

Etymology

From French 'chandelle' (candle), referring to the upright vertical motion resembling a candle.
