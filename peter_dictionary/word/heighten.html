heighten

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make or become greater in degree, intensity, or amount

Examples

Many will add that we may find our enjoyment heightened by our understanding.

Synset Examples

This will enhance your enjoyment
heighten the tension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
  • Synonym: increase - to make greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: lessen - to make or become less

Etymology

From 'height' + '-en', using the verb-forming suffix '-en'.

2. VERB

/ ˈhaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to become or make more intense or extreme in degree or quality

Examples

This would heighten possibilities for part-time cooperative, on-the-job and extension training.

Synset Examples

The tension heightened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
  • Antonym: lessen - to become or make less in intensity or degree

Etymology

From height + -en, with -en as a verb-forming suffix.

3. VERB

/ ˈhaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈhaɪtn̩ / (BrE)
To increase the degree, intensity, or amount of something; to make or become more intense or extreme.

Examples

This strategy heightened the possibility that we would have a nuclear war.

Synset Examples

heightened interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensify - to make something increase in intensity
  • Antonym: diminish - to make or become less

Etymology

Formed from 'height' + '-en', the verb-forming suffix.
