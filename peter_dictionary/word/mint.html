mint

1. NOUN

/ mɪnt / (AmE), / mɪnt / (BrE)
a large amount of money, especially when obtained quickly or easily

Examples

She was wise enough to realize a man could be good company even if he did weigh too much and did n't own the mint.

Synset Examples

a batch of letters

a slew of journalists
a wad of money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - a large amount of money or valuable possessions
  • Antonym: pittance - a very small amount of money
  • Synonym: windfall - a sudden, unexpected piece of good fortune, often money

Etymology

From the place where money is coined; extended figuratively to mean a large sum of money.
