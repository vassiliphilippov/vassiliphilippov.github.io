tactically

1. ADV

/ ˈtæk.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈtak.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to tactics or strategic planning, especially in military or competitive situations.

Examples

If it were the enemy, tactically his position was correct.

Synset Examples

the tactically useful province is still firmly in the rebels' hands

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: strategically - in a way that relates to strategy or overall planning

Etymology

From 'tactical' + '-ly'. 'Tactical' from Greek 'taktikos' (of or for arranging or ordering).
