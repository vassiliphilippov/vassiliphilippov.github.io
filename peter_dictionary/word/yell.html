yell

1. VERB

/ jɛl / (AmE), / jɛl / (BrE)
to shout loudly, especially in a sudden or urgent way, often because of pain, excitement, or to attract attention

Examples

He saw only dimly moving figures, but when he slashed them they yelled and fled.
The men in the boats had started yelling happily at first sight of the officer, two of them calling him Billy.
He heaved the dead man onto the buckboard, yelled and lashed at the team and got out of there fast.
Even Black's old crowbait began to snort, and from the house Black yelled, "Jess!
"They have n't seen us ", Greg yelled to himself over the engine noise.

Synset Examples

she cried with pain when the doctor inserted the needle
I yelled to her from the window but she couldn't hear me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Synonym: scream - to make a loud, high-pitched cry, often from pain or fear
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *yellen*, related to Old English *giellan* (to yell, cry out).

2. VERB

/ jɛl / (AmE), / jɛl / (BrE)
to utter words or make a loud sound in a forceful or very loud voice, often as an expression of emotion or to be heard over noise or distance

Examples

Somewhere in the fog, the nigger boy was still yelling murder.
In the Presidential box someone leaned over the balustrade and yelled: "He has shot the President "!
We heard him before he ever showed, and we heard him yelling after he was out of sight.
Mike yelled to the catcher.
"Hit the bum "! somebody yelled from the Anniston bench.

Synset Examples

You don't have to yell--I can hear you just fine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something loudly; to cry out in a loud voice
  • Synonym: scream - to give a long, loud, high-pitched cry
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
  • Antonym: murmur - to say something in a low or indistinct voice

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'yellen', from Old English 'giellan' (to yell, scream).

3. NOUN

/ jɛl / (AmE), / jɛl / (BrE)
a loud, sharp shout, often made in protest, excitement, or pain

Examples

"If you spot Carmer give a yell before you move in ".

Synset Examples

the speaker was interrupted by loud cries from the rear of the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - a loud call or cry, often to attract attention
  • Antonym: whisper - a very quiet or soft way of speaking

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *yellen*, related to Old English *giellan* (to yell, scream).
