silkworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkwəːm / (BrE)
The larva of a domesticated moth, Bombyx mori, which spins a cocoon used to produce commercial silk.

Examples

In Keng Kok, the City of Silkworms, the Prime Minister bought fried chickens and fried cicadas, and two notebooks for me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bombyx mori - the species of moth whose larva is the silkworm

Etymology

From Old English 'seolcwyrm', from 'seolc' (silk) + 'wyrm' (worm).
