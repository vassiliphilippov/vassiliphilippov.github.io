harry

1. VERB

/ ˈhæri / (AmE), / ˈhæri / (BrE)
to continually annoy or attack someone, especially in a persistent or repeated way

Examples

His worries harried him up out of bed, and he lighted a cigarette and went to the window.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: molest - to bother, disturb, or persecute persistently

Etymology

From Old English *hergian* (to make war, ravage), related to Old Norse *herja* (to harry, plunder).
