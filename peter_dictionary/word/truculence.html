truculence

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌkjələns / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkjʊləns / (BrE)
an aggressive or defiant attitude; a tendency to be combative or hostile

Examples

He behaved publicly with a cocky, swaggering truculence that offended their vulpine Latin minds, and behaved towards them personally with an unimpressed insolence that enraged them beneath their blandness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belligerence - an aggressive or warlike attitude, similar in meaning to truculence
  • Synonym: aggressiveness - tendency to be aggressive or forceful; similar to truculence
  • Antonym: meekness - quietness and willingness to submit; opposite of truculence

Etymology

From Latin 'truculentia' (fierceness, savageness), from 'truculentus' (fierce, savage).
