glassy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlæsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsi / (BrE)
(of eyes) appearing dull, lifeless, or lacking expression, as if glazed or not reflecting emotion.

Examples

The woman had the glassy look of an invalid, as if she had not slept at all.

Synset Examples

empty eyes
a glassy stare
his eyes were glazed over with boredom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glazed - (of eyes) dull and lifeless, as if covered with glass
  • Antonym: lively - full of life or vitality, especially of eyes

Etymology

From 'glass' + '-y', meaning having the qualities of glass.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡlæsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsi / (BrE)
Resembling glass in smoothness, shininess, or slickness.

Examples

The chevaux de frise, those sharp stakes and barriers around the fort at the Battery, pointed to a conflict between the town and sea power rolling in glassy swells as the tide came in.

Synset Examples

the glassy surface of the lake
the pavement was...glassy with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - shiny and smooth, often reflecting light
  • Antonym: matte - having a dull or non-shiny surface

Etymology

From glass + -y, forming an adjective meaning 'like glass.'
