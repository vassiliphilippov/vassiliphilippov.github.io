sick

1. ADJ

/ sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
affected by an impairment of normal physical or mental health; unwell or ill

Examples

By preying on the sick, by playing callously on the hopes of the desperate, by causing the sufferer to delay proper medical care, these medical ghouls create pain and misery by their very activity.
Secretary of State Seward was a sick man.
The sick were always receiving medicines.
The following morning, as John entered the Place Molard on his way to visit a sick refugee, he had a premonition of danger.
Another stock vaudeville gag ran: "Mother is home sick in bed with the doctor ".

Synset Examples

ill from the monotony of his suffering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill - suffering from disease or poor health
  • Antonym: healthy - in good physical or mental health

Etymology

Old English *sēoc* (ill, unwell), related to German *siech* (sickly).

2. ADJ

/ sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
feeling nausea or about to vomit

Examples

Pa 'd been shit sick in that pot.
It should have been nearly as easy for her to remember that as it was for Big Hans to remember going after the axe while he was still spattered with Pa's yellow sick insides.
Memory flooded him the instant he opened his eyes and the sick feeling knotted his stomach.
It made him a little sick, and he let go of her.
Then he was sick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nauseous - feeling or causing a feeling of sickness in the stomach
  • Antonym: well - in good health, not feeling ill

Etymology

Old English *sēoc* (ill, diseased), related to Dutch *ziek*, German *siech*.

3. ADJ

/ sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
(of a person, mind, or behavior) affected by madness or a serious mental disturbance; mentally deranged.

Examples

We now have not only what has been called over here the comedy of menace but we also have horror jokes, magazines known as Horror Comics, and sick comedians.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; seriously mentally disturbed
  • Synonym: mad - mentally ill; suffering from madness
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; mentally healthy

4. NOUN

/ sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
people who are ill; those suffering from sickness or disease, often referred to collectively as 'the sick'.

Examples

It takes place in the household, a rite of well wishing for myriad occasions - for the traveler, a wedding, a newborn child, the sick, the New Year, for any good purpose.

Synset Examples

they devote their lives to caring for the sick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill - people who are ill (as a noun, similar collective use)
  • Antonym: healthy - people who are healthy and not suffering from illness

Etymology

From Old English *sēoc* (ill, sick).
