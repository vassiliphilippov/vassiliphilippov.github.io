foundation

1. NOUN

/ faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
an organization established with an endowment, especially one that provides funding for a particular purpose such as education, research, or charity

Examples

Not only do these quacks assume impressive titles, but represent themselves as being associated with various scientific or impressive foundations - foundations which often have little more than a letterhead existence.

The gruesome humor of the Nazis was not forgotten - the gas chamber with a sign on it with the name of a Jewish foundation and bearing a copper Star of David - nor the gratuitous sadism of SS officers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - an organization set up to help those in need
  • Synonym: trust - an organization managing assets for a specific purpose

Etymology

From Latin *fundatio* (the act of founding), from *fundare* (to lay the base of something).

2. NOUN

/ faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
The lowest load-bearing part of a building, typically below ground level, that supports the entire structure.

Examples

These are the centuries in which the inhabitants of the Aegean world settled firmly into their minds and into their institutions the foundations of the Hellenic outlook, independent of outside forces.

Synset Examples

it was built on a base of solid rock
he stood at the foot of the tower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: base - the bottom support of anything; that on which a thing stands or rests
  • Synonym: foot - the lowest part or bottom of something

Etymology

From Middle English fondacioun, from Old French fondation, from Latin fundatio (laying a base), from fundare (to found).

3. NOUN

/ faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
An underlying basis or principle; the groundwork on which something is built or established.

Examples

Many aspects of civilization were not yet sufficiently crystallized to find expression, nor could the simple economic and social foundations of this world support a lofty structure.
They had had to work on very simple foundations and had not dared to give rein to impulses.
Whether or not Plato's tale of the lost continent of Atlantis is true, skeptics concede that the myth may have some foundation in a great tsunami of ancient times.
It is a weakness of Gabriel's analysis that he never seems to realize that his so-called fundamental law had already been cut loose from its foundations when it was adapted to democracy.
This combined experience, on a foundation of very average, I assure you, intelligence and background, has helped me do things many well-informed people would bet heavily against.

Synset Examples

there is little foundation for his objections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basis - the underlying support or main idea for something
  • Synonym: groundwork - preparatory work or the basic framework for something
  • Synonym: support - something that holds up or underpins a structure or idea

Etymology

From Old French 'fondation', from Latin 'fundatio', from 'fundare' (to lay a bottom or base).
