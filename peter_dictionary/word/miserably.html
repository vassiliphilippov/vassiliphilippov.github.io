miserably

1. ADV

/ ˈmɪzərəbli / (AmE), / ˈmɪzərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or involves great unhappiness, discomfort, or distress.

Examples

"Hettie, they did n't sell you ", he said miserably.

Synset Examples

I bit my lip miserably and nodded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wretchedly - in a wretched or unhappy manner
  • Antonym: happily - in a happy or cheerful manner

Etymology

From Latin 'miserabilis' (pitiable, miserable) + English adverb suffix '-ly'.
