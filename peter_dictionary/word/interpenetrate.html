interpenetrate

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈpɛnəˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈpɛnɪtreɪt / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to penetrate each other or to be thoroughly intermixed or interlocked.

Examples

It is true that this distinction between style and idea often approaches the arbitrary since in the end we must admit that style and content frequently influence or interpenetrate one another and sometimes appear as expressions of the same insight.

Synset Examples

The territories of two married people interpenetrate a lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlock - to engage with each other so that they become firmly joined
  • Synonym: penetrate - to succeed in entering or passing into or through something

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between, among) + 'penetrāre' (to penetrate).
