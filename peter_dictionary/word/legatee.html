legatee

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛɡəˈti / (AmE), / ˌlɛɡəˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who receives a legacy, especially property or money, through a will

Examples

If you are an income beneficiary of property held in trust or an heir, legatee, or devisee, you may deduct allowable depreciation and depletion, if not deductible by the estate or trust.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: testator - a person who has made a will or given a legacy
  • Synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to the property or rank of another on that person's death

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legatarius, from Latin legatum (a legacy).
