parent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈpeərənt / (BrE)
A father or mother; a person who gives birth to, adopts, or raises a child.

Examples

Extension of the ADC program to all children in need living with any relatives, including both parents, as a means of preserving family unity.

Therefore it's a genuine pleasure to tell you about an entirely happy bodybuilder who has never had to train in secret; has never heard one unkind word from his parents; and never has been taunted by his schoolmates!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty
  • Synonym: guardian - a person who is legally appointed to care for another

Etymology

From Old French *parrent*, from Latin *parentem* (accusative of *parens*), present participle of *parere* (to bring forth).
