haggle

1. VERB

/ ˈhæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhæɡl̩ / (BrE)
to argue persistently and sometimes aggressively, especially over the price of something or the terms of an agreement

Examples

Farmers are so eager for new machinery that they 're haggling less over prices than they did a year ago, dealers report.
He might barge into a gallery, start haggling over prices without so much as a word of greeting.

Synset Examples

Let's not haggle over a few dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiate - to discuss in order to reach an agreement, especially on business or political issues
  • Synonym: bargain - to discuss the price of something in order to reach an agreement

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *haggen* (to hack, cut), implying dispute by cutting words.
