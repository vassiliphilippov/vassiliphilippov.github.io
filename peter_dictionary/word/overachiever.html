overachiever

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərəˈtʃivər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈtʃiːvə / (BrE)
A person, especially a student, who achieves more success or higher results than expected, particularly relative to their abilities or predicted potential.

Examples

The subjects for this study were randomly selected from stratified areas of the distribution, one-third as underachievers, one-third medium, and one-third over-achievers.
Over-achievers were at least one standard deviation above the regression line in their school system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underachiever - person who performs below their potential or expectations

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (exceeding) + 'achiever' (one who achieves).
