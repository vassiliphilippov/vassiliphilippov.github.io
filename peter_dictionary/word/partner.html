partner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in an activity, business, or other enterprise with another or others, sharing responsibilities, risks, or benefits

Examples

Willie's partner was Long Jim Barnes, who tried to keep count.
You should have a couple of training partners to stand by when you make your first experiments, just for safety.
Do patriots everywhere know enough about how the persecution of the Jews in Germany and later in the occupied countries contributed to terrorizing the populations, splitting apart individuals and groups, arousing the meanest and most dishonest impulses, pulverizing trust and personal dignity, and finally forcing people to follow their masters into the abyss by making them partners in unspeakable crimes?
Youngish man on the make, Madden labeled him, and was ready to guess that in a correct, not too pushing fashion, the junior partner of the firm had political ambitions; that Mrs. Garth would be impeccably suitable as the wife of a rising young lawyer; that there were three children, two boys and a girl; that she was active in the Woman's Club and he in Lions, Rotary, and Jaycee; and finally, that neither of them had harbored an unorthodox opinion since their wedding day.
ZING was the creation of two men, Louis Thor and Bill Blake, partners in ZING!

Synset Examples

the musician and the librettist were collaborators
sexual partners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborator - person who works jointly with others in an activity
  • Synonym: associate - a person who is connected with another in a business or other enterprise
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who opposes or competes against another

Etymology

From Latin 'partīri' (to share, divide), via Old French 'parçonier' (partner, sharer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person's spouse or companion in a long-term romantic relationship, especially marriage or civil partnership

Examples

The two in the bed knew each other as old people know the partners with whom they have shared the same bed for many years, and they needed to say no more.
The partners each bring to it unselfish love, and each takes away an equal share of pleasure and joy.
Instead one partner or the other dominates the sexual relationship.
Yet many psychologists and marriage counselors agree that domination of the sex relationship by one partner or the other can be unhealthy and even dangerous.
Experts say the partners in marriages like these can almost be typed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spouse - a husband, wife, or partner in a married relationship
  • Synonym: significant other - a person with whom someone has an established romantic or sexual relationship
  • Degree Lower: husband - a married man considered in relation to his spouse
  • Degree Lower: wife - a married woman considered in relation to her spouse

Etymology

From Middle English *partener*, from Old French *parçonier* or *partenaire* (associate, partner), from Latin *pars* (part).

3. VERB

/ ˈpɑːrtnər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
to join or associate with another person or organization as a partner; to provide with a partner

Examples

For Helva was destined to be the "brain "half of a scout ship, partnered with a man or a woman, whichever she chose, as the mobile half.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to join with another as a companion or colleague
  • Antonym: separate - to move or divide apart, to not join together

Etymology

From Middle English 'partener', from Old French, from 'part', meaning 'part' or 'portion'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɑɹtnɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person who shares in a business or legal partnership with others

Examples

Our friend and business partner, Hyman Roth, is in the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - a person joined with others in a business

Etymology

From Old French partenaire, from partir (to divide, share), from Latin partire.
