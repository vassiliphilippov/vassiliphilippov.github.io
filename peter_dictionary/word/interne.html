interne

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌtɝn / (AmE), / ˈɪntɜːn / (BrE)
An advanced medical student or recent graduate gaining supervised practical experience in a hospital; especially used in British or historical contexts.

Examples

Any reputable French interne can supply you with a dozen similar instances, and I 'll presently recount a case out of my own personal experience, but, for the moment, let's resume our catalogue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intern - medical trainee working in a hospital
  • Degree Higher: resident - doctor undergoing specialized medical training after internship

Etymology

From French *interne* (resident within), from Latin *internus* (inward, internal).
