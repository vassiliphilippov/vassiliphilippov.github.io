offstage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
The area of a theatre stage that is out of view of the audience, where performers wait before appearing or after exiting.

Examples

He stumbled off-stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onstage - visible part of the stage to the audience

Etymology

From 'off' (away from) + 'stage'.
