entrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛntrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛntrənt / (BrE)
a new product or commodity that enters into competition with established ones in the market

Examples

A well publicized entrant which has achieved success only recently is the built liquid detergent, with which the major problem today is incorporation of builder and active into a small volume using a sufficiently high builder / active ratio.

Synset Examples

a well publicized entrant is the pocket computer

Etymology

From French entrant, present participle of entrer (to enter), from Latin intrare.
