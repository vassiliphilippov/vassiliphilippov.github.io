Orient

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔriənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪənt / (BrE)
the countries of Asia, especially those of East and Southeast Asia; often used with reference to cultural, historical, or geographic contrasts with the West

Examples

The growing contacts between Aegean and Orient are also a phase which should be linked primarily to the remarkable broadening of Hellenic culture after 750.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Occident - the countries of the West (Europe and America), contrasted with the Orient

Etymology

From Latin orient-, oriens (rising, east), from oriri (to rise), originally referring to the rising sun in the east.
