shenanigan

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnænɪɡən / (AmE), / ʃəˈnænɪɡən / (BrE)
playful or mischievous activity, often involving trickery or pranks; reckless or deceitful behavior that causes discomfort or annoyance in others

Examples

If there is a moral lurking among the shenanigans, it is hard to find.
Instead - and not just to prove my objectivity - I hasten to report that it's a highly amusing film which probably does a fairly accurate job of reporting on the Easter vacation shenanigans of collegians down in Fort Lauderdale, and that it seems to come to grips quite honestly with the moral problem that most commonly vexes youngsters in this age group - that is to say, sex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - behavior that causes minor harm or annoyance
  • Synonym: prank - a playful trick or practical joke

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly American English slang from the mid-19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnænəɡən / (AmE), / ʃəˈnænɪɡən / (BrE)
secret or dishonest activity or maneuver, especially one intended to trick or deceive someone.

Examples

And she felt amply rewarded for her suffering when the evidence of Lee's quack shenanigans, gathered by the tape recorder under her friend's clothing, proved adequate in court for convicting Franklin D. Lee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - use of tricks to deceive
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful deception intended for personal gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Irish sionnachuighim (I play tricks).
