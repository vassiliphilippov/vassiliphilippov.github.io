umbrella

1. NOUN

/ əmˈbrɛlə / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛlə / (BrE)
A device consisting of a circular canopy of cloth on a folding metal frame, used as protection against rain or sunlight; it is held above the head by a central handle.

Examples

I work on a watercolor easel in the field, and frequently resort to a large garden umbrella to protect my eyes from undue strain.
The lieutenant's sparse brown hair was heavily pomaded, and as Killpath raked the comb through it, it stuck together in thatches so that it looked like umbrella ribs clinging to his pink skull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasol - a handheld canopy used to provide shade from the sun

Etymology

From Italian 'ombrella', diminutive of 'ombra' (shade), from Latin 'umbra' (shade).
