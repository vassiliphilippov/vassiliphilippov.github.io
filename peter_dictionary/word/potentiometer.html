potentiometer

1. NOUN

/ pəˌtɛn(t)ʃiˈɑmɪɾɚ / (AmE), / pəˌtɛn(t)siˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring electromotive force or controlling voltage in an electric circuit, often by varying resistance.

Examples

Relative incident light intensities were measured with a thermopile potentiometer system.

Etymology

From potential (in physics: electromotive force) + -meter (measuring device).
