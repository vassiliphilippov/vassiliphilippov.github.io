displace

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈpleɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to force someone or something to move from its usual or original position, often as a result of external pressure or force

Examples

A small pressure unbalance displaces the diaphragm and changes the capacitance between the diaphragm and an electrically insulated plate spaced 0.001 in. apart (for * * f).

Synset Examples

the refugees were displaced by the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place
  • Synonym: evict - to force someone to leave a place, especially a home
  • Antonym: replace - to put something new in the place of something else

Etymology

From Old French *desplacer*, from des- (dis-) + placer 'to place'.
