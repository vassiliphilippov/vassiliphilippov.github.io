wheelchair

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlˌtʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌtʃɛː / (BrE)
a chair mounted on wheels, used by people who cannot walk or have difficulty walking, typically propelled by the occupant or an attendant

Examples

At the Dumont, a guest had come in a collapsible wheel chair.

Etymology

Formed from 'wheel' + 'chair', first recorded in the mid-17th century.
