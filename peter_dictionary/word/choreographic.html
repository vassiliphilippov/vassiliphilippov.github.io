choreographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔːriəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒriəˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with choreography or the composition of dances

Examples

The party at Floyd's penthouse gave the "chorines "a chance for a nostalgic frolic through all those hackneyed routines which have become a classic choreographic statement of the era's nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choreography - the art of inventing and arranging dances

Etymology

From French 'chorégraphique', from Greek 'khoros' (dance) and 'graphein' (to write).
