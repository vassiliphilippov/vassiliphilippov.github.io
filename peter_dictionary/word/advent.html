advent

1. NOUN

/ ˈædˌvɛnt / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɛnt / (BrE)
The arrival or beginning of a notable person, thing, or event, especially one that has been eagerly awaited.

Examples

With the advent of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's Sherlock Holmes, the development of the modern private detective begins.

Synset Examples

the advent of the computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrival - the act of arriving at a place or event
  • Antonym: departure - the act of leaving, especially to start a journey

Etymology

From Latin 'adventus' (arrival), from 'advenire', meaning 'to come to'.
