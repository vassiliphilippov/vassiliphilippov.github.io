oxcart

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksˌkɑrt / (AmE), / ˈɒksˌkɑːt / (BrE)
A cart that is pulled by one or more oxen, typically used for transporting goods or people.

Examples

He at once cancels the celebrations and, buckling on his scimitar, stumbles blindly from the house, where he is hit and killed by a passing oxcart.

Etymology

From 'ox' (a bovine animal used as a draft animal) + 'cart' (a wheeled vehicle for carrying loads).
