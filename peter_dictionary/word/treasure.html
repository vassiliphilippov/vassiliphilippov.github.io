treasure

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə / (BrE)
a collection of valuable items such as money, jewels, or other precious things

Examples

Dr. H. V. Hilprecht, Professor of Assyrian at the University of Pennsylvania, dreamed that a Babylonian priest, associated with the king Kurigalzu, (1300 B.C..) escorted him to the treasure chamber of the temple of Bel, gave him six novel points of information about a certain broken relic, and corrected an error in its identification.
They went to the Flea Market, expecting to find the treasures of Europe, and found instead a duplication of that long double row of booths in Tours.
Treasure Island.

Synset Examples

the pirates hid their treasure on a small island in the West Indies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - a stockpile of valuable things often kept secret
  • Synonym: fortune - a large amount of wealth or property
  • Antonym: pittance - a very small amount of money or wealth

Etymology

From Old French *tresor*, from Latin *thēsaurus*, meaning 'hoard, treasure'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə / (BrE)
something highly valued or prized for its beauty, rarity, or excellence

Examples

True, ideas are important, perhaps life's most precious treasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a person or thing considered to be outstandingly good or valuable

Etymology

From Old French *tresor*, from Latin *thēsaurus* (store, treasure).

3. VERB

/ ˈtrɛʒər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə / (BrE)
to value something highly and hold it dear; to keep something with great affection or regard

Examples

Will his words come to be treasured and quoted through the years?

Synset Examples

I prize these old photographs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherish - to hold something dear; to care for tenderly
  • Synonym: prize - to value highly; to esteem greatly
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; to treat as unimportant

Etymology

From Old French *tresor*, from Latin *thēsaurus* (treasure, storehouse).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒə / (BrE)
a collection of valuable or precious objects, especially one that has been accumulated over time

Examples

He looked thoughtfully at his wife's trunk, holding her meager treasures.

Synset Examples

the trunk held all her meager treasures

Etymology

From Old French *tresor*, from Latin *thēsaurus* (storehouse, treasure).
