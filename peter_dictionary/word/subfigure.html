subfigure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌfɪɡjə / (BrE)
A smaller figure or diagram that forms part of a larger figure, typically in scientific or mathematical illustrations.

Examples

If, however, the figure to be discerned were complicated, composed of several interlocking subfigures, and so on, even the tracing process failed him, and he could not focus even relatively simple shapes among its parts.

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'sub-' (under, below) + 'figure' (from Latin 'figura', shape, form).
