Switzerland

1. PROPN

/ ˈswɪtsərlənd / (AmE), / ˈswɪtsələnd / (BrE)
A landlocked country in central Europe, officially a federal republic, known for its neutrality, alpine landscapes, and high standard of living.

Examples

A nation such as Switzerland could be neutralized by agreement and could be relied upon to protect its neutrality; more doubtful, but possible, (with an assist from the North) was the neutralization of the Latin American countries; out of the question was the neutralization of Asia and Africa.

Etymology

From German 'Schweiz', the name of the country, ultimately from Schwyz, one of its founding cantons.
