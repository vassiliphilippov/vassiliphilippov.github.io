hungry

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌŋɡri / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡri / (BrE)
Feeling the need or desire to eat; experiencing hunger.

Examples

The morning hawk, hungry for any eatable, killable, digestible item, kept his eyes on the ring of anchored ships that lay off the shores in the bay, sheltered by the Jersey inlets.
Are you hungry "?
"Sure, I'm hungry "!
One wrote: "[ I am so hungry ] I could eat a rider off his horse + snap at the stirups ".
A third remarked of slowness of Southerners: "They moved about from corner to corner, as uneasy as a litter of hungry leaches on the neck of a wooden god ".

Synset Examples

a world full of hungry people

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: starving - extremely hungry; suffering or dying from lack of food
  • Antonym: satiated - having had enough to eat; satisfied

Etymology

From Old English *hungor* (hunger) + *-y* (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhʌŋɡri / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡri / (BrE)
Having a strong desire or craving for something (often followed by 'for'), such as attention, success, or recognition.

Examples

A good many beef hungry settlers were accepting the death of William Lewis as proof that the warning notes were not idle threats.

Synset Examples

athirst for knowledge
hungry for recognition
thirsty for informaton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - keenly or impatiently desirous
  • Synonym: craving - an intense desire for something
  • Antonym: satisfied - content; having one's needs or desires fulfilled
