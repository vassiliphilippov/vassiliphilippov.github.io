notation

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a system of symbols or signs used to represent numbers, amounts, or elements, especially in mathematics, science, or music

Examples

In the notation of the proof of Theorem 12, let us take a look at the special case in which the minimal polynomial for T is a product of first degree polynomials, i.e., the case in which each * * f is of the form * * f.
The language and notation introduced are standard throughout the chapter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • Synonym: representation - the action of depicting or standing for something; especially using symbols

Etymology

From Latin 'notatio' (a marking, a sign), from 'notare' (to mark, note).
