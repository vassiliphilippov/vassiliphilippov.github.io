oilcloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌklɒθ / (BrE)
A fabric, typically cotton or linen, treated on one side with a drying oil or synthetic resin to make it waterproof, often used for table coverings or covering surfaces.

Examples

Picasso says that he himself had already made his first collage toward the end of 1911, when he glued a piece of imitation caning oilcloth to a painting on canvas.

Etymology

From 'oil' + 'cloth', referring to the oil-based treatment applied to the fabric.
