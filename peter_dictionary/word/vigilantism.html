vigilantism

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪdʒəˈlænˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌvɪdʒɪˈlæntɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of taking the law into one's own hands and carrying out justice according to one's own principles, often without legal authority

Examples

left vague, the spirit of vigilantism is spreading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilante - a person who takes the law into their own hands

Etymology

From 'vigilante' (Spanish, meaning 'watchman') + -ism.
