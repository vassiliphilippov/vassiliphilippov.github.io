freewheeling

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriˌwilɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌwiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
Not bound by rules or conventions; acting or done in a spontaneous or unconstrained way.

Examples

A few years ago a "Timex All Star Jazz Show "offered a broad range of styles, ranging from Lionel Hampton's big band to the free-wheeling Dukes of Dixieland.

Synset Examples

freewheeling foolishness
the versatility of his poetic freewheeling style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrestrained - not controlled or limited
  • Synonym: uninhibited - expressing oneself freely without restraint
  • Antonym: constrained - restricted or limited by rules

Etymology

From 'freewheel' (to coast freely), originally referring to bicycles with a freewheel mechanism.
