philanthropist

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlænθrəpɪst / (AmE), / fɪˈlænθrəpɪst / (BrE)
A person who seeks to promote the welfare of others, especially by making generous donations to good causes.

Examples

J. Wheaton Smith, editor of the Warren Telegraph stated that "the ends of justice must be satisfied, a solitary example must be set, in order that all those misnamed philantropists [ sic ], who, actuated by a blind zeal, dare to instigate riot, treason, and murder, may heed it and shape their future course accordingly ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - person who gives money or help to a person or cause

Etymology

From Greek philanthropia (love of mankind), from philos (loving) + anthrōpos (human being).
