Racine

1. PROPN

/ rəˈsin / (AmE), / raˈsiːn / (BrE)
(1639–1699) A French dramatist renowned for his tragedies inspired by Greek and Roman themes; a leading figure in French classical literature.

Examples

The destiny of Racine's Phedre is sealed from the beginning: she will proceed into the dark.

Etymology

Jean Racine was a French surname; his works entered English discussion through literary criticism.
