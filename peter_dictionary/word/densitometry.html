densitometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛn.sɪˈtɑ.mə.tri / (AmE), / ˌdɛn.sɪˈtɒm.ə.tri / (BrE)
The scientific measurement of the optical density of a substance, typically by shining light through it and measuring how much is transmitted.

Examples

Five milliamperes / cell were applied for 18 hr, after which the strips were stained with bromophenol blue and densitometry was carried out using a Spinco Analytrol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrophotometry - measurement of the absorption of light by a substance at different wavelengths

Etymology

From density + -metry (measurement).
