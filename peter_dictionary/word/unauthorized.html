unauthorized

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɔθəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd / (BrE)
not having official permission, approval, or authority

Examples

Representing as it did the efforts of only unauthorized individuals of the Roman and Anglican Churches, and urging a communion of prayer unacceptable to Rome, this association produced little fruit, and, in fact, was condemned by the Holy Office in 1864.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authorized - having official permission or authority

Etymology

un- (not) + authorize (from Old French autoriser, from Medieval Latin auctorizare).
