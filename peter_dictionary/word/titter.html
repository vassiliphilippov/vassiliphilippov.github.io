titter

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪtə / (BrE)
to laugh in a nervous, restrained, or half-suppressed way

Examples

Usually, they titter loudly after they have passed by.

Synset Examples

The girls giggled when the rock star came into the classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - to laugh lightly and repeatedly in a silly or nervous way

Etymology

From Middle English titeren, possibly imitative of the sound of suppressed laughter.
