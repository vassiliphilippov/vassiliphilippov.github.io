chagrin

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈɡrɪn / (AmE), / ʃəˈɡrɪn / (BrE)
A feeling of distress or embarrassment caused by failure, disappointment, or humiliation.

Examples

Thus, when the Russians sent up their first sputnik, American chagrin was human enough, and American determination to put American satellites into orbit was perfectly understandable.
The soldier's voice was muffled again, stricken with chagrin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness or shame
  • Synonym: mortification - a feeling of humiliation or shame

Etymology

From French chagrin (melancholy, anxiety, grief).
