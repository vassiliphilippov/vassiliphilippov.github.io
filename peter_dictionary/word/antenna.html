antenna

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɛnə / (AmE), / ænˈtɛnə / (BrE)
a device for sending or receiving radio or television signals, typically consisting of a wire or set of wires

Etymology

From Latin 'antenna' (sail yard); modern sense developed via Italian and French in the 19th century for electrical use.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtɛnə / (AmE), / ænˈtɛnə / (BrE)
the ability to notice and understand subtle signs, feelings, or changes, especially in a particular area or situation; sensitivity similar to that of a receptor organ

Examples

This means that the antennae of the congregation are extended into the community, picking up the wave lengths of those who will fit into the social and economic level of the congregation; the mission of the church is actually a process of informal co-optation; the lay ministry is a means to recruit like-minded people who will strengthen the social class nucleus of the congregation.
The policy officer lives with his antennae alerted for the questions which fall within his range of responsibility.

Synset Examples

he had a special antenna for public relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptivity - willingness or ability to receive new ideas or stimuli
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity or perceptiveness

Etymology

From Latin 'antenna' (sail yard), later used for the sensory organs on insects and radio devices.
