commutation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of regularly traveling between home and work or school, especially over some distance

Examples

Though the number of people flowing in and out of our metropolitan areas each day has increased tremendously since World War 2,, total annual rail commutation dropped 124 million from 1947 to 1957.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commute - to travel regularly to and from work or school

Etymology

From Latin commutatio (change, exchange), from commutare (to change).
