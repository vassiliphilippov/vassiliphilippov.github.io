inferential

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃl / (BrE)
relating to or involving inference; based on reasoning rather than direct observation.

Examples

But to go from here to the belief that those more sensitive to metaphor and language will also be more sensitive to personal differences is too great an inferential leap.

Synset Examples

the illative faculty of the mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deductive - relating to deduction or drawing logical conclusions from premises
  • Antonym: inductive - making generalizations based on specific observations

Etymology

From Medieval Latin inferentialis, from Latin inferre (to bring in, infer).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈrɛnʃ(ə)l / (BrE)
relating to or involving reasoning that moves from general premises to a specific and necessary conclusion

Examples

This is what was meant, above, by describing history as inferential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deductive - based on deduction from general principles; logical reasoning from general to specific
  • Antonym: inductive - involving reasoning from specific cases to general principles

Etymology

From Medieval Latin inferentialis, from Latin inferre (to bring in, to infer).
