microscopy

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑːskrəpi / (AmE), / mɪˈkrɒskrəpi / (BrE)
The use or study of microscopes to observe objects that cannot be seen with the naked eye.

Examples

Fig. 1 is a simplified block diagram of the ultraviolet microscopy system developed at the Medical Electronics Center of Rockefeller Institute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microscope - an instrument for viewing very small objects

Etymology

From New Latin *microscopia*, based on Greek *mikros* (small) and -scopy (viewing, observing).
