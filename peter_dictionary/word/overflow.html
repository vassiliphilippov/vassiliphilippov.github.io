overflow

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈfloʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfləʊ / (BrE)
A large or excess flow, especially of liquid, that spills over the limits of a container, or a figurative outpouring of an emotion, quality, or activity.

Examples

Some who have written on Utopia have treated it as "a learned diversion of a learned world ", "a phantasy with which More amused himself ", "a holiday work, a spontaneous overflow of intellectual high spirits, a revel of debate, paradox, comedy and invention ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - An overflowing of a large amount of water beyond its normal limits

Etymology

From Old English 'oferflōwan' (to flow over); 'ofer' (over) + 'flōwan' (to flow).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈfloʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfləʊ / (BrE)
(of a liquid, crowd, or contents) to flow or run over the edge or beyond the limits of something.

Examples

They overflowed the parking lot, making progress by automobile difficult.
The audience filled all the seats inside the Wollman enclosure and overflowed onto the lawns outside the fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to flow over the edge, especially accidentally
  • Synonym: flood - to overflow in great quantity or with water

Etymology

From Old English oferflōwan, from ofer (‘over’) + flōwan (‘to flow’).
