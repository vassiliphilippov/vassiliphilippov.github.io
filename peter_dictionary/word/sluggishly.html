sluggishly

1. ADV

/ ˈslʌɡɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡɪʃli / (BrE)
In a slow, inactive, or lethargic manner.

Examples

The smoke from that chimney rose as sluggishly as smoke from any other, and hung as sadly in the drizzle, creeping back down along the sopping canvas of the roof.

Synset Examples

the smoke rose sluggishly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowly - in a slow manner
  • Antonym: rapidly - in a rapid or fast manner

Etymology

From 'sluggish' + '-ly'. 'Sluggish' is from Middle English 'sluggi', related to 'slug' (a slow-moving creature).
