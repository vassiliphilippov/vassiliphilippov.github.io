bloodhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈblʌd.haʊnd / (BrE)
A large breed of hound, originally from Europe, with an exceptionally keen sense of smell, used especially for tracking.

Examples

Finding him dead, Cook caught Russell's horse and rode to the cattle foreman's house to report the incident and request bloodhounds to trail the assassins.
It's just like that book your Northern friend wrote - except there are n't any ice floes to cross and no bloodhounds ".

Etymology

From 'blood' + 'hound', referring to the dog's use in tracking blood trails or its noble (pure-blooded) lineage.
