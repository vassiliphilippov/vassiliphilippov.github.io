doctorate

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚət / (AmE), / ˈdɒktərət / (BrE)
A university degree of the highest level, awarded after advanced study and research.

Examples

John, an engineer and anthropologist with a doctorate from the London School of Economics, headed the rural development division of USOM, the United States Operations Mission administering U. S. aid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor's degree - an academic degree of the highest level awarded by a university
  • Synonym: PhD - Doctor of Philosophy, a common type of doctorate degree
  • Degree Lower: master's degree - an academic degree lower than a doctorate

Etymology

From Latin 'doctoratus', meaning 'having been made a doctor'.
