whipping

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech, style, etc.) smart and fashionable; lively or stylish in appearance or manner.

Examples

There are some sharp and whipping lines and some hilariously funny situations - the best of the latter being a mass impromptu plunge into a nightclub tank where a "mermaid "is performing.

Synset Examples

snappy conversation
some sharp and whipping lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snappy - (of speech, style, etc.) smart and stylish; briskly effective
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; not smart or fashionable

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of beating a person or animal with a whip, strap, or rope as a form of punishment

Examples

Pistol whipping an unarmed man might come easy to someone like Jess, but Curt could n't bring himself to do it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flogging - the act of beating with a whip or stick, especially as punishment
  • Synonym: caning - punishment by beating with a cane

Etymology

From 'whip' + '-ing', denoting the action or result of whipping.
