refill

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈfɪl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪl / (BrE)
to fill something again after it has been emptied

Examples

As the camp emptied, it was refilled by a draining of the camps and towns around Lublin, then by deportees from outside Poland.
All right, gentlemen, it'’s refill time here!

Synset Examples

refill my glass, please

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empty - to remove all contents from; to make empty

Etymology

From re- (again) + fill (to make full).
