repulsion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌlʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of dislike or aversion

Examples

To greet them with repulsion would turn what before was neutral into something bad; it would needlessly bring badness into the world; and even on subjectivist assumptions that does not seem very bright.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attraction - a feeling of liking or being drawn towards something
  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Synonym: disgust - a feeling of revulsion or profound disapproval aroused by something unpleasant

Etymology

From Latin 'repulsio' (a driving back), from 'repellere' (to drive back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌlʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlʃən / (BrE)
The force by which objects or particles push each other apart.

Examples

The great resemblance between electrical and magnetic attractions and repulsions and the similarity of their laws necessarily would bring about this comparison.
They bring an inextricable component of value within themselves, with attractions and repulsions native to their own quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attraction - the force by which bodies are drawn toward each other
  • Synonym: repel - to push away by force

Etymology

From Latin 'repulsio(n-)', from 'repellere' meaning 'to drive back'.
