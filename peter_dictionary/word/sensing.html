sensing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of perceiving or detecting that something has happened or that a particular state exists.

Examples

The chief experience is the sensing of communion, and in the higher religions, of a harmonious relationship with the supernatural power.
She walked back to the house and entered, feeling herself returning, sensing some kind of opportunity in the empty building.
More than anything, it is the therapist's intuitive sensing of these latent meanings in the stereotype which helps these meanings to become revealed, something like a spread-out deck of cards, on sporadic occasions over the passage of the patient's and his months of work together.

Synset Examples

early detection can often lead to a cure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses
  • Synonym: detection - the action or process of identifying the presence of something concealed

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix -ing to 'sense', from Latin 'sensus' (perceived, felt).
