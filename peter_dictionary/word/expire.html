expire

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaɪə / (BrE)
(of an official document, right, or agreement) to cease to be valid, typically after a fixed period of time.

Examples

The mayor's present term of office expires Jan. 1.
The outgoing members, whose four-year terms will expire a week after the April 18 primary election, received carved wooden elephants, complete with ivory tusks, to remember the state committee by.
His official term expired last summer.

Synset Examples

My passports expired last month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapse - to become invalid, especially by the passing of time or due to inaction
  • Synonym: terminate - to bring or come to an end
  • Antonym: renew - to make valid again, especially after expiration

Etymology

From Latin 'exspirare' meaning 'to breathe out, come to an end'.

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaɪə / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to die; to cease to live.

Examples

Unfortunately, Brooks' teammates were not in such festive mood as the Orioles expired before the seven hit pitching of three Kansas City rookie hurlers.
Hell's own amount of chaulmoogra oil did nothing to alleviate their torment; they expired amid indescribable fantods, imploring the Blessed One to forgive their desecration.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to cease living; stop being alive
  • Synonym: perish - to die, especially in a violent or sudden way
  • Antonym: survive - to continue to live or exist, especially after a dangerous event

Etymology

From Latin 'exspirare' (to breathe out, die).
