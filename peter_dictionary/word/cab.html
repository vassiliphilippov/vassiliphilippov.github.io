cab

1. NOUN

/ kæb / (AmE), / kab / (BrE)
the enclosed area at the front of a truck, train, or other vehicle where the driver sits

Examples

But it was only Johnson reaching around the wire chicken fencing, which half covered the truck cab's glassless rear window.
I regained my squatting position behind the truck cab's rear window.
There was no time to pick out a penny; I got a coin between my thumb and forefinger, leaned my elbows in a very natural and casual manner on top of the truck cab and flipped my little missile.
Johnson's fat hand, another bottle were protruding from the truck cab, and that self proclaimed Baptist teetotaler, had a bottle at his own lips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockpit - the area in an aircraft or boat where the pilot or driver sits

Etymology

Shortened from 'cabin,' originally from Middle French 'cabine' and Italian 'cabina.'

2. NOUN

/ kæb / (AmE), / kæb / (BrE)
a car that can be hired with a driver to carry passengers to a destination for a fare

Examples

This is my cab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxi - a car for hire with a driver that transports passengers for a fare

Etymology

Short for 'cabriolet', from French, originally referring to a type of horse-drawn carriage.
