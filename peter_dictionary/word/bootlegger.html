bootlegger

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡə / (BrE)
A person who makes, distributes, or sells alcoholic liquor illegally.

Examples

The attempt to enforce that belief ushered in a reign of bootleggers, racketeers, hijackers and gangsters that led to a breakdown of law unparalleled in our history.
Like a fair number of bootleggers he disliked alcohol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - person who illegally transports goods, especially across borders

Etymology

From 'bootleg' (to illegally make or sell alcohol), originally referring to liquor concealed in the leg of a boot.
