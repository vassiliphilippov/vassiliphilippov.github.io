disturbed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
afflicted with or marked by anxious uneasiness, trouble, or grief

Examples

There is no occasion to feel uneasy or disturbed about these people.

Synset Examples

too upset to say anything
spent many disquieted moments
distressed about her son's leaving home
lapsed into disturbed sleep
worried parents
a worried frown
one last worried check of the sleeping children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not affected by anxiety or disturbance
  • Synonym: uneasy - feeling anxiety or discomfort
  • Synonym: troubled - afflicted by worry or problems

Etymology

From the verb 'disturb', from Latin 'disturbare' meaning 'throw into disorder'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
(of a person) emotionally unstable and having difficulty coping with personal relationships or life events.

Examples

Like almost everybody else, he confused the heart, both as organ and as symbol, with the disturbed psyche, the deranged glands, and the jumpy central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troubled - (of a person) affected by emotional or mental distress
  • Antonym: stable - (of a person) emotionally steady and not easily upset

Etymology

From Latin *disturbare* via Old French *destourber*, meaning 'to throw into disorder'.
