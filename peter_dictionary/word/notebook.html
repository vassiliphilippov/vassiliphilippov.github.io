notebook

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtˌbʊk / (BrE)
A book of blank or ruled pages for writing notes in.

Examples

McFeeley greeted the parents, then studied his notebook.
In Keng Kok, the City of Silkworms, the Prime Minister bought fried chickens and fried cicadas, and two notebooks for me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a daily record of news and events
  • Synonym: diary - a book in which one keeps a daily record of events and experiences

Etymology

From note + book. The term 'notebook' has been in use since the 16th century.
