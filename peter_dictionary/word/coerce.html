coerce

1. VERB

/ koʊˈɜrs / (AmE), / kəʊˈɜːs / (BrE)
to make someone do something by using force, threats, or undue pressure

Examples

This seems like an attitude favoring a sort of totalitarian bureaucracy which, under a President of the same stamp, would try to coerce an uncooperative Congress or Supreme Court.
Just short of forty lashes we finally managed to coerce our children to this view.

Synset Examples

She forced him to take a job in the city
He squeezed her for information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force - to make someone do something against their will
  • Synonym: compel - to cause someone to do something by necessity or pressure
  • Antonym: persuade - to cause someone to do something by reasoning or argument

Etymology

From Latin coercere (to restrain, control), from co- (together) + arcere (to enclose, confine).
