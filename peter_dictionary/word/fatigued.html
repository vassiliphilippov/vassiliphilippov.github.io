fatigued

1. ADJ

/ fəˈtiɡd / (AmE), / fəˈtiːɡd / (BrE)
very tired; lacking energy or strength because of physical or mental effort

Examples

The pathetic evidence is no longer before us; and we are now so fatigued in body and mind that feeling is, as we say, quite dead.
"One of the things which makes our lives drab and empty and which leaves us, at the end of the day, fatigued and deflated spiritually is the pressure of the taxing, practical, utilitarian concern of common-sense objects.

Synset Examples

the day's shopping left her exhausted
he went to bed dog-tired
was fagged and sweaty
the trembling of his played out limbs
felt completely washed-out
only worn-out horses and cattle
you look worn out

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refreshed - feeling rested and energetic after being tired or fatigued
  • Synonym: exhausted - extremely tired; completely drained of energy
  • Degree Lower: tired - in need of sleep or rest; weary
  • Synonym: worn out - extremely fatigued; no longer able to function due to tiredness

Etymology

From French 'fatigué', past participle of 'fatiguer', from Latin 'fatigare' (to tire out).
