tighten

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
to make or become tight or tighter

Examples

The child should then bring both legs together overhead, point the toes and tighten the seat muscles.
He was standing in front of the mirror, tightening his tie.
She showed her surprise by tightening the reins and moving the gelding around so that she could get a better look at his face.
He tightened his turn.
Greg tightened his turn until the plane shuddered.

Synset Examples

Tighten the wire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosen - to make or become less tight

Etymology

From tight + -en (Old English ‘-nian’ forming verbs).

2. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
to make a rule, regulation, or control more strict or restrictive

Examples

The economist does not look for a drastic switch in the budget during this recovery and believes it "even more unlikely that the Federal Reserve will aggressively tighten monetary policy in the early phases of the upturn as was the case in 1958 ".

Synset Examples

Tighten the rules
stiffen the regulations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosen - make rules or controls less strict

Etymology

From Old English 'tītan', meaning 'to draw, pull', related to 'tight'.

3. VERB

/ ˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
to become tight or tighter; to become more firmly fixed or less flexible

Examples

Dill's voice tightened.

Synset Examples

The rope tightened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosen - to become less tight or more relaxed

Etymology

From 'tight' + '-en', a common verb-forming suffix in English.
