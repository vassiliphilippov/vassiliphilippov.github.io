morals

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəlz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlz / (BrE)
principles or standards of right and wrong behavior; ideas about what is acceptable or unacceptable in society

Examples

He is, first and foremost, a defender of public morals, a servant of society.
Beaten, bruised and exhausted, he pursues the elusive killer through the demi-monde of high society and low morals, always alone, always despised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - moral principles that govern behavior
  • Antonym: immorality - the state of being immoral or lacking morals

Etymology

From Latin 'moralis', relating to manners or morals.
