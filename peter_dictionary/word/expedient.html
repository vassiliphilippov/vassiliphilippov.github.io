expedient

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
(of an action) convenient and practical although possibly improper or immoral; suitable for achieving a particular end.

Examples

The expedient thing - yes, very true, one must make do as one could, in some situations.

Synset Examples

was merciful only when mercy was expedient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a practical way
  • Antonym: impractical - not adapted for use or action; not sensible or realistic

Etymology

From Latin 'expediens', present participle of 'expedire' (to free, disengage, prepare).
