perpetual

1. ADJ

/ pərˈpɛtʃuəl / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəl / (BrE)
Continuing forever or for a very long time without stopping; everlasting or constant.

Examples

Ann Catt was a lonely, devoted soul, never married, conducting a spotless home and devoted to her church, but a perpetual dissenter and born critic.

Synset Examples

the ageless themes of love and revenge
eternal truths
life everlasting
hell's perpetual fires
the unending bliss of heaven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting forever; without end
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time

Etymology

From Latin perpetuus (continuous, uninterrupted, permanent).
