impropriety

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɑːpriəti / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒprɪəti / (BrE)
behavior or action that is not considered proper or appropriate by social or moral standards

Examples

He said that the propriety or impropriety of such a gathering was a question that was to be settled by every man in accordance with the convictions of private judgments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: propriety - conformity to accepted standards of behavior

Etymology

From Latin improprietas via Old French improprieté, from improprius (not appropriate).
