parishioner

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪʃənər / (AmE), / pəˈrɪʃənə / (BrE)
a member of a particular parish, especially one who attends its church

Examples

"I should say it is the turning of courts of law into veritable theatres for sex dramas, involving clergymen and parishioners, psychiatrists and patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congregant - a member of a congregation, often used for those attending religious services

Etymology

From Old French *parochien* (parishioner), from Late Latin *parochianus* (parishioner), from *parochia* (parish).
