crumble

1. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌm.bl̩ / (BrE)
(of something solid) to break or fall apart into small fragments; to disintegrate.

Examples

Andy crumbled the script in his fist.

Synset Examples

the building crumbled after the explosion
Negotiations broke down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disintegrate - break into small pieces or parts; lose cohesion
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in

Etymology

From Middle English 'crumblen', frequentative of 'crumen' (to crumble), from Old English 'crymelan', related to Danish 'krumme' (to crumble).
