refund

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːfʌnd / (AmE), / ˈriːfʌnd / (BrE)
an amount of money that is given back to someone who has paid for a product or service, especially because they are not satisfied with it or there was an overpayment

Examples

If you elect to use the Standard Deduction or the Tax Table, and later find you should have itemized your deductions, you may do so by filing an amended return within the time prescribed for filing a claim for refund.
An overpayment of income and social security taxes entitles you to a refund unless you indicate on the return that the overpayment should be applied to your succeeding year's estimated tax.
If you file Form 1040 A and the District Director computes your tax, any refund to which you are entitled will be mailed to you.
Refunds of less than $ 1 will not be made unless you attach a separate application to your return requesting such a refund.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reimbursement - repayment of money spent or lost

Etymology

From Latin 'refundere' (to pour back), from 're-' (back) + 'fundere' (to pour).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌfʌnd / (AmE), / ˈriːˌfʌnd / (BrE)
the act of returning money that was paid or received, typically because of an overpayment or a returned product or service

Examples

The return for a decedent may also serve as a claim for refund of an overpayment of tax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reimbursement - the act of paying back money to someone for expenses
  • Antonym: payment - the act of paying money

Etymology

From Latin 'refundere', meaning to pour back.

3. VERB

/ riˈfʌnd / (AmE), / riˈfʌnd / (BrE)
to pay back money, especially to someone because they have returned goods or paid too much

Examples

If you file a Form 1040, you should indicate in the place provided that there is an overpayment of tax and the amount you want refunded and the amount you want credited against your estimated tax.

Synset Examples

Please refund me my money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reimburse - to pay back money to someone who has spent it for you or lost it
  • Synonym: repay - to pay back money that you owe

Etymology

From Latin *refundere*, meaning 'to pour back'.
