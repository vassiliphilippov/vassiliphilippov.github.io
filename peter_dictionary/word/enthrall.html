enthrall

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈθrɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrɔːl / (BrE)
to capture the fascinated attention of; to hold spellbound

Examples

When he came to the movies - more or less by accident - they were still cheap entertainment capable of enthralling the unthinking for an idle few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captivate - to attract and hold the interest of
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or weary

Etymology

From Middle English 'enthrallen', from 'en-' + 'thrall' (bondage, slave), originally meaning 'to enslave'.
