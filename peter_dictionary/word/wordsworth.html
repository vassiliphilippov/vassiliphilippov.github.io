Wordsworth

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɝːdzˌwɝːθ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdzˌwɜːθ / (BrE)
(1770–1850) An English Romantic poet known for his works inspired by the Lake District, and for helping launch the Romantic Age in English literature.

Examples

English philosopher Samuel Alexander's debt to Wordsworth and Meredith is a recent interesting example, as also A. N. Whitehead's understanding of the English romantics, chiefly Shelley and Wordsworth.
