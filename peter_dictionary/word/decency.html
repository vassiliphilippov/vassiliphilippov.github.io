decency

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːsənsi / (AmE), / ˈdiːsənsi / (BrE)
The quality of being polite, respectable, and conforming to accepted standards of behavior or morality.

Examples

The trick these two play upon Jacoby reveals their want not simply of decency but of imagination as well.
The red haired captain, towering above the prisoner as a symbol of decency and authority, was shocked to find himself looking with sympathy upon Philip Spencer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - correctness or appropriateness in behavior
  • Antonym: indecency - offensive or improper behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'decentia' (propriety), from 'decens' (becoming, seemly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːsənsi / (AmE), / ˈdiːsənsi / (BrE)
Behavior or standards that conform to accepted moral and social values; propriety.

Examples

I did have the decency to call up Thelma and tell her I 'd met old friends and would be home late.
This particular song at this particular time could only be interpreted as the ultimate in bad taste, callous exploitation beyond the bounds of decency.
I challenge this committee to produce any witness or evidence against me and if they do not, I hope they will have the decency to clear my name with the same publicity with which they have besmirched it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - correct or proper behavior
  • Antonym: indecency - lack of decency or propriety

Etymology

From Latin 'decentia' (becoming, proper) via French 'décence'.
