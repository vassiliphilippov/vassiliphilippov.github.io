spacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement or property of having spaces between objects or elements, especially at regular intervals

Examples

This tube could be adjusted in its axial direction by an electric drive to establish the required electrode spacing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distance - amount of space between two points or objects
  • Antonym: closeness - state of being near or having little space between objects

Etymology

From 'space' + '-ing', forming a noun indicating the result or process of spacing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the interval of time or distance between repeated events or objects

Examples

"As to the necessity ", the committee declared, "for some form of effective control of the size of the family and the spacing of children, and consequently of control of conception, there can be no question.

Synset Examples

some women do not control the spacing of their children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a period of time between two events
  • Synonym: gap - a break or interval between events or objects

Etymology

From 'space' + '-ing'.
