scathingly

1. ADV

/ ˈskeɪðɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈskeɪðɪŋli / (BrE)
In a severely critical or harsh manner.

Examples

I could evoke no further elaboration from her about this; but a few seconds later she was standing directly across the room from me, looking me in the eyes and saying in a scathingly condemnatory tone, "Your father despises you "!

Synset Examples

she criticized him scathingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scathing - severely critical; harsh

Etymology

From 'scathing' (harshly critical) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix), from Middle English 'scathe' (harm, damage), originally from Old Norse 'skaði' (harm).
