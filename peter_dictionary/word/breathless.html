breathless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɛθləs / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθləs / (BrE)
Unable to breathe easily; having difficulty breathing or out of breath, often due to exertion, excitement, or strong emotion.

Examples

What followed the outburst brought almost breathless silence as Miss Sutherland revealed her mastery of a voice probably unique among sopranos today.
At the very end, when the audience was silent and breathless, a collection was taken and then slowly everyone filed out.
Heiser, breathless and wild-eyed, brought the chilling news that the handspikes, heavers and holystones had been mysteriously removed from their customary places.

Synset Examples

breathless at thought of what I had done
breathless from running
followed the match with breathless interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winded - short of breath, especially after exercise
  • Synonym: panting - breathing quickly and heavily, especially after exertion

Etymology

From Middle English 'brethles', from 'breth' (breath) + '-less'.
