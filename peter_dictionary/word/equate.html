equate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈkweɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪt / (BrE)
(in mathematics) to set two quantities or expressions as equal to each other.

Examples

The minimum total potential energy is found by taking the derivative with respect to H and equating to zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equalize - to make equal, especially in amount or value

Etymology

From Latin 'aequatus', past participle of 'aequare' (to make equal).
