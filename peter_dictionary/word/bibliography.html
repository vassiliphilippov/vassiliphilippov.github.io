bibliography

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪbliˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a list of books, articles, or other published writings on a particular subject or by a particular author, usually with details of publication, especially one listed at the end of a scholarly work to show sources consulted or cited

Examples

Two data centers have been established for the collection, indexing, critical evaluation, and dissemination of bibliographies and critical values in the fields of transition probabilities and collision cross sections.
Selected bibliographies and tables of available data are now in preparation.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bibliographia (book-list), from Greek biblion (book) + -graphia (writing).
