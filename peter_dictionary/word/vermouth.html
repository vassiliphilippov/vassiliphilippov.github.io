vermouth

1. NOUN

/ vərˈmuθ / (AmE), / vəˈmuːθ / (BrE)
A type of white wine flavored with aromatic herbs and spices, typically used as an aperitif or in mixed drinks such as martinis.

Examples

They had Vermouth, sitting in front of a cafe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martini - cocktail made with gin and vermouth

Etymology

From French 'vermouth', from German 'Wermut' (wormwood), as the drink was originally flavored with wormwood.
