skillful

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈskɪlf(ə)l / (BrE)
having or showing skill, expertise, or proficiency in doing something

Examples

In this work, his use of non color is startling and skillful.
His counterpoint is pertinent, skillful, and rarely thick.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unskillful - lacking skill; not competent
  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing something
  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient at something

Etymology

From skill + -ful, meaning 'full of skill'.
