penance

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəns / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəns / (BrE)
A feeling of remorse or regret for one's past conduct, especially relating to wrongdoing or sin.

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repentance - remorse or regret for past actions
  • Synonym: contrition - sincere remorse for wrongdoing
  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret for a wrong committed

Etymology

From Old French *penance*, from Latin *paenitentia* (repentance, regret).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəns / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəns / (BrE)
(In the Roman Catholic Church) a sacrament involving confession of sins, repentance, and the granting of absolution.

Examples

Catholics who were truly Christians longed for the simple penance of days gone by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - the act of admitting sins to a priest as part of the sacrament of penance

Etymology

From Old French 'penance' (repentance, penance), from Latin 'poenitentia' (repentance).
