plaque

1. NOUN

/ plæk / (AmE), / plɑːk / (BrE)
(Medicine) A small, abnormal patch or area on or inside the body, often raised and differing in color or texture from the surrounding tissue.

Examples

The valves were normal except for thin yellow plaques on the inferior surface of the mitral leaflets.
The intimal surface of the aorta was covered with confluent, yellow-brown, hard, friable plaques along its entire course, and there was a marked narrowing of the orifices of the large major visceral arteries.

Etymology

From French 'plaque' (plate, slab), from Middle Dutch 'placke' (patch, slab).

2. NOUN

/ plæk / (AmE), / plɑːk / (BrE)
A flat, typically decorative plate made of brass or other metal, usually fixed to a wall or object as a memorial or to commemorate an event or person.

Examples

The coronary arteries were sclerotic and diffusely narrowed throughout their courses, and the right coronary artery was virtually occluded by a yellow atheromatous plaque 1.5 cm. distal to its origin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tablet - a flat slab of stone, clay, or other material, used as a memorial inscription

Etymology

From French plaque (plate); related to Dutch plaak, German Plack.
