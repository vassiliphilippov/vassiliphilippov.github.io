viability

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The ability of a living organism to grow, develop, and survive under normal conditions.

Examples

An agent would likely be selected which would possess sufficient viability and virulence stability to meet realistic minimal logistic requirements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonviability - incapacity to survive or develop; lack of viability

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) with the suffix '-ability.'
