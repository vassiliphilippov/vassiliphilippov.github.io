following

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
A group of people who support, admire, or are devoted to someone or something.

Examples

It is this subject matter that has brought Mason a large and enthusiastic following among sportsmen, but it is his exceptional performance with this motif that commends him to artists and discerning collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanbase - the body of admirers or devoted followers of a person or group
  • Degree Lower: admirer - a person who regards someone with respect or warm approval

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
Coming after or proceeding in the same direction as something or someone else.

Examples

A shot caught him and straightened him up in screaming pain; a following volley of shots shattered glass, ripped the ceiling, and sent him lurching heavily from the table.

Synset Examples

the crowd of following cars made the occasion seem like a parade
tried to outrun the following footsteps

Related Terms

  • Antonym: preceding - coming before in time or order

Etymology

Present participle of 'follow', from Old English 'fylgian'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
Coming immediately after in time or order.

Examples

The following April, Andy did tax returns for half the guards at Shawshank.

Synset Examples

the following day
next in line
the next president
the next item on the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: next - coming immediately after in time or order
  • Antonym: previous - coming immediately before in time or order

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
coming next in order or to be mentioned immediately after.

Examples

I wish to have the following noted for the record...

Synset Examples

the following items

Related Terms

  • Antonym: preceding - coming before in order or position

Etymology

From Middle English 'folowyng', present participle of 'folowen' (to follow), from Old English 'folgian'.
