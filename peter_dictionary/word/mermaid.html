mermaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɜːmeɪd / (BrE)
A mythical sea creature with the head and upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish.

Examples

There are some sharp and whipping lines and some hilariously funny situations - the best of the latter being a mass impromptu plunge into a nightclub tank where a "mermaid "is performing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: merman - mythical creature with the upper body of a man and the tail of a fish

Etymology

From Old English 'mere' (sea) + 'maid' (woman).
