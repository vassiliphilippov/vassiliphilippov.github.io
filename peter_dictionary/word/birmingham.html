/ ˈbɜːrmɪŋhæm / (AmE), / ˈbɜːmɪŋəm / (BrE)
A large city in central England, known as the second-largest English city and a major industrial and transportation center.

Etymology

Named after the Anglo-Saxon tribe 'Beormingas' (people of Beorma) and 'ham' (home, settlement).

Examples

He had preached a short sermon, trying to talk man-to-man to the audience, to tell them who he was, what he had done in Macon and Birmingham, and what he proposed to do here.
