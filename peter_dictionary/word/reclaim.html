reclaim

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkleɪm / (AmE), / rɪˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to take back something that was yours or recover possession or rights to something previously lost or given up

Examples

They hope, of course, to reclaim the non-Catholic population to the Catholic faith, and at every Sunday Benediction they recite by heart the "Prayer for England ":
You may reclaim your property, and the penalty on Hesperus is lifted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to get back something lost or taken away
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up possession of something

Etymology

From Old French ‘reclamer’, from Latin ‘reclamare’ (to cry out against, protest).
