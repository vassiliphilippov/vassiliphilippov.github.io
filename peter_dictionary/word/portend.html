portend

1. VERB

/ pɔrˈtɛnd / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to be a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen

Examples

Kate tried to contain her curiosity and foreboding at what the letter portended, at what involvement existed for Juanita.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bode - to be an omen of a particular outcome
  • Synonym: foreshadow - to be a warning or indication of a future event
  • Synonym: augur - to be a sign that something will happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin portendere (to foretell, indicate), from por- (forth) + tendere (to stretch).
