disappointment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
an event or action that fails to satisfy someone's expectations or hopes

Examples

Despite countless barriers and disappointments, Shea moved forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letdown - something that is disappointing; a disappointment
  • Antonym: success - an event that achieves a desired outcome, especially the opposite of disappointment

Etymology

From Middle French *desapointement* (displeasure, undoing), from Old French *desapointer* (to undo an appointment or removal from office).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of sadness or unhappiness caused by not achieving what you hoped for or expected.

Examples

The belief in immortality, where held, functions as a redress for the ills and disappointments of the here and now.
Only Mary's praise sustained him in his disappointment.
Meeting it without preparation as they did, robbed of anticipation, a common disappointment seized them.
And, in the last case, a bitter disappointment but too often meets the confiding nature ".
Then, with disappointment evident upon their faces, they moved to the work.

Synset Examples

his hopes were so high he was doomed to disappointment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfaction - feeling of contentment when expectations are met
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness
  • Synonym: frustration - feeling of upset caused by being unable to satisfy needs or expectations

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'desappointer' (to remove from office), from 'des-' (dis-) + 'appointer' (appoint).
