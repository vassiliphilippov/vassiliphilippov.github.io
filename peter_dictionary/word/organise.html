organise

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
To arrange or coordinate an activity, event, or group by systematic planning and joint effort.

Examples

Don Barzini, I want to thank you for helping me organise this meeting, and the other heads of the five families from New York and New Jersey.

Synset Examples

machinate a plot
organize a strike
devise a plan to take over the director's office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange - to put things in a neat, attractive, or required order; to organize
  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and make arrangements for in advance
  • Antonym: disorganize - to disrupt the systematic arrangement; to make disorderly

Etymology

From French 'organiser', from Medieval Latin 'organizare', from 'organum' (instrument, tool, organ).
