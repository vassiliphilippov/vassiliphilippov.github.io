inventory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.vənˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈɪn.vən.t(ə).ri / (BrE)
a detailed list of all the goods and materials that are held in stock, especially in a business or shop

Examples

Section 8 (b) (2) of the Small Business Act, as amended, authorizes the SBA to make a complete inventory of the productive facilities of small business concerns.
The Administration maintains a productive facilities inventory of small business industrial concerns that have voluntarily registered.
Purpose of this inventory is to include all eligible productive facilities in SBA's facilities register so that the small business concerns may have an opportunity to avail themselves of the services authorized by the Congress in establishing the Small Business Administration.
SBA can make complete facilities inventories of all small business concerns in labor surplus areas within budgetary and staff limitations.
True, a Mason watercolor is unmistakably a synthesis of nature rather than a detailed inventory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - the goods or merchandise kept on the premises of a shop or warehouse

Etymology

From Latin 'inventarium' (a list, inventory), from 'inventus', past participle of 'invenire' (to find).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnvənˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəntri / (BrE)
all the goods and materials that are kept in stock by a business and available for sale or use

Examples

The inventory you acquired from me is n't going to be easy to move; you can't very well sidle up to people on the street and ask if they want to buy a hot Bodhisattva.
In total, these increases in operating costs outweigh the savings that result from declining programs and from economy measures, such as reduced numbers of units and installations, smaller inventories of major equipment, and improvements in the supply and distribution systems of the Armed Forces.

Synset Examples

they carried a vast inventory of hardware
they stopped selling in exact sizes in order to reduce inventory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - the goods or merchandise kept on the premises of a shop or warehouse

Etymology

From Latin 'inventarium' (a list of what is found), via Old French 'inventaire'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.vənˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈɪn.vən.tər.i / (BrE)
the value of a company's current assets, including raw materials, work in progress, and finished goods, as recorded for accounting purposes

Examples

Computers are being used to keep branch inventories at more workable levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - goods kept by a business for sale or use

Etymology

From Latin inventarium (a list or inventory), from invenire (to find, discover).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɪnvənˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəntri / (BrE)
A complete list of items such as goods, resources, or skills in stock or possessed by an individual or organization.

Examples

The inventory of tones is much smaller, and commonly the contrasts range along one single dimension, pitch level.

Synset Examples

he dipped into his intellectual armory to find an answer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - the supply of goods available for sale or distribution
  • Synonym: catalogue - a complete list of items, typically one in alphabetical or systematic order
  • Synonym: collection - a group of accumulated items or resources

Etymology

From Latin 'inventarium' (list of what is found), from 'invenire' (to find).
