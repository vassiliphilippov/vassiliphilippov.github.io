reschedule

1. VERB

/ riˈskɛdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃɛdjuːl / (BrE)
to arrange a new time for something that was planned to happen at a different time

Examples

Can they be re-scheduled?
The year after that, they rescheduled the start of the intramural season to coincide with tax season.

Synset Examples

We had to reschedule the doctor's appointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide that an event will not take place

Etymology

From re- (again) + schedule, meaning to schedule again.
