tormented

1. ADJ

/ tɔrˈmɛntɪd / (AmE), / tɔːˈmɛntɪd / (BrE)
Experiencing intense physical or emotional suffering; afflicted by anguish or pain.

Examples

The ungainly bird thing ran away, and to David its croaking sounded like the crowing of a tormented rooster.

Synset Examples

an anguished conscience
a small tormented schoolboy
a tortured witness to another's humiliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anguished - experiencing severe mental or physical pain
  • Synonym: tortured - subjected to severe suffering or pain
  • Antonym: calm - not affected by pain or suffering

Etymology

From Latin tormentum (an instrument of torture), via Old French tormenter.
