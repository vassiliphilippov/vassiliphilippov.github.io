fondness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒnd.nəs / (BrE)
affection or liking for someone or something

Examples

The outlook for the amateur, for instance, is usually dependent on his fondness for local history or for the picturesque.
As a boy in a local school he was shy and solitary, absorbed in his fondness for nature and his visions of Sweden's ancient glory.

Synset Examples

he had a fondness for whiskey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of liking or fondness
  • Antonym: hatred - intense dislike or aversion

Etymology

From fond + -ness; 'fond' ultimately from Middle English 'fond' (foolish, affectionate).
