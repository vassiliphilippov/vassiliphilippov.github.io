confluent

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːnfluənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnflʊənt / (BrE)
(of elements) flowing or running together; merging to form a mass or whole, especially used of diseases, features, or growths that join or blend together.

Examples

The intimal surface of the aorta was covered with confluent, yellow-brown, hard, friable plaques along its entire course, and there was a marked narrowing of the orifices of the large major visceral arteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coalescent - growing or coming together to form one mass or whole

Etymology

From Latin 'confluens, confluentis' (flowing together), from 'confluere' (to flow together).
