voluntary

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɑːlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɒləntri / (BrE)
Done, given, or acting of one's own free will; not compelled or forced.

Examples

the relative effectiveness of voluntary versus mandatory reporting of accident statistics;

Synset Examples

man is a voluntary agent
participation was voluntary
voluntary manslaughter
voluntary generosity in times of disaster
voluntary social workers
a voluntary confession

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compulsory - required by law or rule; not optional

Etymology

From Latin voluntarius (of one's free will), from voluntas (will).
