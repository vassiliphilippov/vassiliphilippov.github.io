faker

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be something they are not in order to deceive others

Examples

The gadget faker operates or sells his phony machines for $ 5 to $ 10000 - anything the traffic will bear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposter - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive
  • Synonym: fraud - a person who deceives others, typically for personal gain
  • Synonym: phony - a person or thing that is fraudulent or fake
  • Antonym: genuine person - a person who is authentic and not pretending

Etymology

From 'fake' (to deceive or forge) + -er (agent suffix).
