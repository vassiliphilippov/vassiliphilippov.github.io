shanty

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃænti / (AmE), / ˈʃænti / (BrE)
A small, crudely built shelter used as a dwelling, typically made from improvised materials.

Examples

Grabski's shanty was beyond the bridge over the River Bystrzyca near the rail center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small simple building or shelter
  • Synonym: cabin - a small simple house, typically in a rural area
  • Antonym: mansion - a large, impressive house

Etymology

Probably from Canadian French *chantier* (a lumberjack's hut), originally from French, 'building site'.
