chaotic

1. ADJ

/ keɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
In a state of complete disorder and confusion; lacking any visible order or organization.

Examples

Until recently, art has withstood the pressure of chaotic things.
This, and other qualifications, make the cocktail party the most complete and most chaotic communication system ever dreamed up.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a systematic way
  • Synonym: disordered - lacking organization or arrangement

Etymology

From chaos + -ic, based on Greek khaos (abyss) and English suffix -ic.

2. ADJ

/ keɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
Extremely disordered, unpredictable, or confusing; lacking any clear order or structure.

Examples

Earlier, this woman had been so filled with a chaotic variety of introjects that at times, when she was in her room alone, it would sound to a passerby as though there were several different persons in the room, as she would vocalize in various kinds of voice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a neat, systematic way

Etymology

From chaos (Greek khaos, 'abyss, void').
