indignity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪɡnəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪɡnɪti / (BrE)
Treatment or circumstances that cause one to feel shame or to lose one's dignity; an affront to one's self-respect.

Examples

If he dwelt on the indignities he suffered he would lose all respect for her, and without the respect he might lose his view of her, too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliation - The act of making someone feel ashamed or foolish by injuring their dignity and self-respect
  • Synonym: affront - An action or remark that causes outrage or offense
  • Antonym: honor - High respect or esteem; a sense of pride in one's actions

Etymology

From Latin indignitas (unworthiness), from in- (not) + dignitas (worthiness).
