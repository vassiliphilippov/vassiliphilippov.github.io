tilt

1. VERB

/ tɪlt / (AmE), / tɪlt / (BrE)
to cause to move, lean, or incline at an angle away from a vertical or horizontal position

Examples

He paced forward unsteadily, leaning too far back, his head tilted oddly.
The curb was a line of stone laid edgewise in the dirt and tilted this way and that by frost in the ground or the roots of trees.
He tilted his homely face toward the dry bed of the river.
Farm machinery dealer Bob Houtz tilts back in a battered chair and tells of a sharp pickup in sales:
There is often a means of locking the quill and, on larger presses, the table can be tilted.

Synset Examples

She leaned over the banister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lean - to incline or bend from a vertical position
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position

Etymology

Probably from Old English *tyltan* (to totter), related to 'tilt' (noun, a cover or awning).

2. VERB

/ tɪlt / (AmE), / tɪlt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move into a sloping or slanting position; to lean or cause to lean to one side

Examples

Vernon would tilt his hat over one ear as he lounged with his feet on the dashboard, indulging in a huge cigar.

Synset Examples

The tower is tilting
The ceiling is slanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slant - to slope or incline; to have or cause a slanting direction

Etymology

From Middle English tilten, perhaps related to Old English 'tyltan' (to totter).
