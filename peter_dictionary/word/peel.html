peel

1. VERB

/ pil / (AmE), / piːl / (BrE)
to remove the outer skin or covering from a fruit, vegetable, or similar item

Examples

Peel, core, and slice across enough apples to make a dome in the pie tin, and set aside.
Nothing is peeled.

Synset Examples

pare apples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pare - to trim off the outer skin or layer, especially with a knife

Etymology

From Middle English *pelen*, from Old French *peler*, from Latin *pellis* (skin).

2. NOUN

/ pil / (AmE), / piːl / (BrE)
the outer skin or rind of a fruit or vegetable

Examples

These two slipped on a banana peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rind - the tough outer skin of certain fruits or cheese
  • Synonym: skin - the thin outer covering of some fruits or vegetables

Etymology

From Middle English *pele*, from Old French *pel*, from Latin *pellis* (skin, hide).
