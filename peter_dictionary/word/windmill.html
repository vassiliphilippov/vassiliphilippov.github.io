windmill

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn(d)mɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪn(d)mɪl / (BrE)
A building or structure with sails or blades that rotate in the wind to generate power for grinding grain or pumping water.

Examples

But strength and poignancy develop thenceforth, and the windmill and deathbed episodes gather the threads of realization of the wonderfulness of the old boy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watermill - a mill powered by running water

Etymology

From Middle English 'wyndmille', from wind + mill.
