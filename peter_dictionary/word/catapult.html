catapult

1. VERB

/ ˈkætəˌpʌlt / (AmE), / ˈkætəpʌlt / (BrE)
to hurl or launch (something or someone) suddenly and forcefully, as if thrown by a catapult

Examples

When his head came down, Curt grabbed him by the hair and catapulted him head first into the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to send something out forcefully; synonym for 'catapult' in this sense
  • Synonym: fling - to throw something with force; synonym for this sense

Etymology

From Latin *catapulta*, from Greek *katapaltēs* (shield piercer, catapult).

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French *catapulte*, from Latin *catapulta*, from Greek *katapeltēs* (down-thrower, catapult).
