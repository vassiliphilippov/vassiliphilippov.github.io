desired

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈzaɪɚd / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪəd / (BrE)
Strongly wanted; wished for intensely or sought after.

Examples

That much of what he calls folklore is the result of beliefs carefully sown among the people with the conscious aim of producing a desired mass emotional reaction to a particular situation or set of situations is irrelevant.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undesired - not wanted or wished for
  • Synonym: wanted - wished for; desired
  • Synonym: craved - longed for intensely, typically referring to something yearned for

Etymology

From Middle English 'desiren', from Old French 'desirer', from Latin 'desiderare' (to long for, to desire).
