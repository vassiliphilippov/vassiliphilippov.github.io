runaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnəˌweɪ / (BrE)
A victory or competition outcome achieved with little difficulty or opposition; a decisive win.

Examples

Since 1949, the only National League club that got off to a hot start and made a runaway of the race was the '55 Dodger team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landslide - an overwhelming victory
  • Antonym: nail-biter - a very close or tense contest

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌnəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnəweɪ / (BrE)
(of a process or thing) rapidly increasing or getting out of control; not under restraint.

Examples

Does our society have a runaway, uncontrollable growth of technology which may end our civilization, or a normal, healthy growth?
Avoiding runaway technology can be done only by assuring a humane society; and for this human beings must be firmly in control of the economics on which our society rests.
These proposals would go far toward creating the economic atmosphere favoring growth of the individual, who, in turn, would help us to cope with runaway technology.

Synset Examples

runaway inflation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchecked - not restrained or controlled
  • Synonym: uncontrolled - not under control; unrestrained

Etymology

From the phrase 'run away,' originally denoting someone or something that has escaped.
