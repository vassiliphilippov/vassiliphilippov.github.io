intimidate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɪməˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt / (BrE)
To frighten or overawe someone, especially in order to make them do what one wants, especially by threats or implied threats.

Examples

She was intimidated by the stove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to intimidate or frighten someone, often repeatedly
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone

Etymology

From Latin intimidare, from in- (in, into) + timidus (fearful).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɪməˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt / (BrE)
To frighten or overawe someone, especially in order to make them do what one wants.

Examples

At Khrushchev's door, therefore, can be placed the primary blame but also at fault are those who permitted themselves to be intimidated.
And in the role of Will Danaher, Philip Bosco roars and sneers sufficiently to intimidate not only one American but the whole British army, if he chose.

Synset Examples

Her boss intimidates her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frighten - To make someone suddenly feel afraid or anxious
  • Synonym: bully - To seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone

Etymology

From Latin *intimidare*, from in- (into) + timidus (timid, fearful).
