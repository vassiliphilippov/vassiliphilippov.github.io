superstitious

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈstɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
Having or showing belief in superstitions, such as magic, luck, or omens, rather than science or reason.

Examples

He was not a superstitious man, but he felt perhaps there was a little something in that, indeed.
But I'm a superstitious man.

Synset Examples

finally realized that the horror he felt was superstitious in origin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational - Based on reason or logic, not superstition

Etymology

From Latin superstitiosus (full of dread or superstition), from superstitio.
