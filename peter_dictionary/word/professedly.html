professedly

1. ADV

/ prəˈfɛsɪdli / (AmE), / prəˈfɛsɪdli / (BrE)
Claimed or stated to be true, often falsely or for appearances only.

Examples

In 1864 Newman professedly had to write his Apologia with his keenest feelings in order to be believed and to command a fair hearing from English readers.
It was professedly worth three thousand dollars in stock and good will, and the name was written in gold in foot high letters across each of the two display windows.

Synset Examples

is only professedly poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostensibly - as appears or is stated to be true, though not necessarily so
  • Antonym: actually - in fact; really

Etymology

From 'professed', from Latin 'professus', past participle of 'profiteri' (to declare publicly) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
