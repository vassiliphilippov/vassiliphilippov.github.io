advantage

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position

Examples

Indications as late as the top of the sixth were that the Birds were to end their victory drought as they coasted along with a 3 - to-o advantage.
The Yanks also took advantage of three Cincinnati errors.
"By winning against Bradley, Kentucky and Notre Dame on those teams' home courts, they showed that the home court advantage can be overcome anywhere and that it doesn's take a super team to do it ".
This is a characteristic of good stereo recording and one of its tremendous advantages over monaural sound.
When these groups were first formed many prominent and accomplished decorators could not have had the advantage of school training since interior design courses were rare and undeveloped during their youth.

Synset Examples

the experience gave him the advantage over me

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable or inferior condition or position

Etymology

From Old French 'avantage', from 'avant' meaning 'before' or 'in front'.

2. NOUN

/ ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
A benefit or favorable position gained as a result of some event, action, or circumstance.

Examples

They made her drink whiskey and then they tried to take advantage of her.

Synset Examples

it turned out to my advantage
reaping the rewards of generosity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavourable circumstance or condition that reduces the chances of success or effectiveness
  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • Synonym: gain - an increase or improvement; benefit

Etymology

From Old French 'avantage', from 'avant' (before), from Latin 'abante'.
