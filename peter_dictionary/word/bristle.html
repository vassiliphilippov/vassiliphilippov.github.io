bristle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
a short, stiff hair or fiber, either natural (such as on an animal) or synthetic, especially one of those making up the working part of a brush

Examples

The bristles are soft enough to massage the gums and not scratch the enamel.
It is not as convenient as the old type toothbrush and the paste tends to shimmy of the bristles.
"My brushes are different from those used by most watercolorists, for I combine the sable and the bristle.
The bristles are a Fitch 2 and a one-half inch brush shaved to a sharp chisel edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber - a threadlike structure forming tissue or material
  • Synonym: hair - a slender threadlike strand growing from the skin of an animal or human

Etymology

From Old English *brysele*, related to *bysel* (bristle, brush), akin to German *Bürste* (brush).

2. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) to be full of or teeming with a particular quality, often something undesirable or noticeable.

Examples

And although Schnabel's pianism bristles with excitement, it is meticulously faithful to Schubert's dynamic markings and phrase indications.

Synset Examples

The room abounded with screaming children
The garden bristled with toddlers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abound - to exist in large numbers or amounts

Etymology

From Old English *brysel* (bristle, brush), related to *bræs* (brass), referring originally to stiff hair.

3. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
(of hair or fur) to stand upright away from the skin, especially as a reaction to fear or anger.

Examples

One, more horrible than the rest, lunged, growling deep in his throat, his hair bristling.
The fences on both sides of the road bristled with the barbed wire.

Synset Examples

The dog's fur bristled
It was a sight to make one's hair uprise!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand on end - (of hair or fur) to become upright, especially from fright or cold
  • Antonym: smooth - to make or become smooth or flat, especially referring to hair or fur

Etymology

From Old English *brysel* (bristle), related to brush.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
A short, stiff hair, typically one of those on an animal's skin, a plant, or used in brushes.

Examples

Unruly hair goes straight up from his forehead, standing so high that the top falls gently over, as if to show that it really is hair and not bristle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fiber - a threadlike structure, narrower and softer than a bristle

Etymology

From Old English *bæristle* (stiff hair or point).
