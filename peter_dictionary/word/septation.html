septation

1. NOUN

/ sɛpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The division of a cavity or structure into separate parts by a septum.

Examples

In this instance, because of incomplete septation, the secondary lobule does not constitute in itself what appears to be a small individual lung as in type 1,.

Etymology

From Latin 'septum' (a partition) + '-ation' (indicating an action or process).
