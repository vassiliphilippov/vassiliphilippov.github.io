confrontation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑnfrənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a situation in which there is a bold challenge, conflict, or opposing forces meet or face each other, often in an argumentative or hostile way

Examples

As the first collective confrontation of the Nazi outrage, the Trial of Eichmann represents a recovery of the Jews from the shock of the death camps, a recovery that took fifteen years and which is still by no means complete (though let no one believe that it could be hastened by silence).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: agreement - a situation of harmony or consensus

Etymology

From French confrontation, from Latin confrontare (to border on, to confront).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfrənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties, especially face to face.

Examples

Mr. Dufresne, describe the confrontation you had with your wife the night she was murdered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically binding arrangement

Etymology

From French 'confrontation', from Late Latin 'confrontatio' (a bringing face to face).
