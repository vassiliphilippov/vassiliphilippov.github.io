demote

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
to move someone to a lower rank or less important position, usually as a punishment or because of poor performance

Examples

His power was so great that he even promoted and demoted gods according to whether they had given ear or been deaf to petitions.

Synset Examples

She was demoted because she always speaks up
He was broken down to Sergeant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: promote - to raise someone to a higher rank or position

Etymology

From de- (down) + promote; first attested in the early 20th century.
