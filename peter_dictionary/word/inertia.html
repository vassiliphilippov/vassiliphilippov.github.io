inertia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɜrʃə / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːʃə / (BrE)
a tendency to remain inactive or unchanged; a lack of motivation to act or change

Examples

In "The King's Ride ", Charles breaks out of a long period of petulance and inertia, regains his old self, escapes from Turkey, and finally reaches his own land after an absence of eighteen years.

Synset Examples

he had to overcome his inertia and get back to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laziness - unwillingness to work or use energy
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active; action

Etymology

From Latin 'iners' (inactive, idle), via Latin 'inertia' (unskillfulness, idleness).
