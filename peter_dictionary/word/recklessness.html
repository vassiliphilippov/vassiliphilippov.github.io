recklessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛkləsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of acting without care for the possible dangers or consequences; lack of caution or consideration for risks.

Examples

These booze customers had until then been buying their supplies from the Sheldon, Saltis-McErlane, and Druggan-Lake gangs, and now they were competing for trade with the Torrio-Capone saloons; once again O' Banion's brash recklessness had caused a proliferation of ill will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or concern; not taking care
  • Antonym: caution - care taken to avoid danger or mistakes

Etymology

From 'reckless' + '-ness', with 'reckless' from Old English 'receleas' (careless, heedless), from 'reccan' (to care).
