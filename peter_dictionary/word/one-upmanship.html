one-upmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˌwʌnˈʌpmənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌwʌnˈʌpmənʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice of outdoing or outshining others in order to gain an advantage or feel superior

Examples

Oneupmanship is practiced by both sides in a total war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - rivalry between people or groups

Etymology

Coined in English from 'one-up' + 'manship' (from 'sportsmanship'), popularized mid-20th century.
