determination

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌtɝːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being firmly committed to achieving a goal, even in the face of difficulties; resolve or firmness of purpose

Examples

Thus, when the Russians sent up their first sputnik, American chagrin was human enough, and American determination to put American satellites into orbit was perfectly understandable.
However, when there's a job to be done, I 'm a monstrosity of grim determination, I like to think.

Synset Examples

his determination showed in his every movement
he is a man of purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - strong determination or decision to do something
  • Antonym: indecision - the inability to make decisions or be resolute
  • Antonym: hesitation - uncertainty or pausing due to indecision

Etymology

From Latin determinatio (a limiting, determination), from determinare (to limit, fix).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌtɝːmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of discovering or establishing the exact nature, quantity, or value of something, often by research, calculation, or measurement

Examples

There have been few measurements specifically for the determination of the polarization of planetary radiation.
This is much higher than the nickel content of terrestrial dusts and sediments and provides a basis for the determination of the meteoritic mass influx.
Routine determinations were made for dissolved oxygen in the mixed liquor and for oxygen uptake rates.
Its staff makes a preliminary determination of the epicenter of the quake and alerts tide stations near the epicenter for a tsunami.
A hydrostatic head correction has been neither necessary nor applicable in the determination of vapor pressures or temperatures for the bulk liquid helium.

Synset Examples

the determination of molecular structures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measurement - the process of measuring something
  • Synonym: assessment - the act of making a judgment or calculation about something

Etymology

From Latin determinatio (a limiting, boundary, specification), from determinare (to limit, ascertain).

/ dɪˌtɜrmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an official decision or judgment reached after careful consideration, especially by an authority or court

Examples

As was said in Gonzales, "it is the Appeal Board which renders the selective service determination considered' final' in the courts, not to be overturned unless there is no basis in fact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - an official decision made by a court or authority

Etymology

From Latin determinatio (a limiting, conclusion), from determinare (to limit, determine).
