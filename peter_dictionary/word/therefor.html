therefor

1. ADV

/ ðɛrˈfɔr / (AmE), / ðeəˈfɔː / (BrE)
For that or for it; used in formal or legal contexts to refer to something previously mentioned.

Examples

The Commission shall notify all claimants of the approval or denial of their claims, stating the reasons and grounds therefor, and if approved, shall notify such claimants of the amount for which such claims are approved.
What do you do for a living?

Synset Examples

ordering goods and enclosing payment therefor
a refund therefor

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'there' + 'for,' parallel to 'therefore' but with distinct meaning.
