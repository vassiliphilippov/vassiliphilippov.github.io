borderline

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔrdərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdəlaɪn / (BrE)
of questionable, minimal, or barely acceptable quality or standard; not clearly passing the required threshold.

Examples

A borderline schizophrenic young man told me that to him the various theoretical concepts about which he had been expounding, in a most articulate fashion, during session after session with me, were like great cubes of almost tangibly solid matter up in the air above him; as he spoke I was reminded of the great bales of cargo which are swung, high in the air, from a docked steamship.

Synset Examples

borderline grades
marginal writing ability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marginal - of minimal quality or barely sufficient

Etymology

Formed from 'border' + 'line'; originally meaning 'along a boundary', later extended figuratively to mean nearly meeting or just failing a standard.
