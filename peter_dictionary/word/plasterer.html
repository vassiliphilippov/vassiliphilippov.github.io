plasterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstərər / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to apply plaster to walls and ceilings

Examples

O' Banion was born in poverty, the son of an immigrant Irish plasterer, in the North Side's Little Hell, close by the Sicilian quarter and Death Corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: builder - person who constructs buildings, often including various trades
  • Synonym: mason - worker skilled in building with stone, brick, or concrete

Etymology

From plaster + -er; 'plaster' from Old French *plastre*, from Latin *plastrum*, from Greek *emplastron* (salve).
