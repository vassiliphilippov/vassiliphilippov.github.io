intercede

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈsid / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsiːd / (BrE)
to act or speak on behalf of someone in order to help settle a dispute or reconcile differences

Examples

Intercede for our separated brethren, that with us in the one true fold they may be united to the chief Shepherd, the vicar of thy Son ".
Now if Hyman Roth sees that I interceded in this thing, in the Rosato brothers'’ favor, he'’s gonna think his relationship with me is still good.

Synset Examples

He interceded in the family dispute
He mediated a settlement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediate - to intervene between people in a dispute to bring about an agreement
  • Synonym: arbitrate - to settle a dispute by acting as an impartial judge

Etymology

From Latin 'intercedere' (to go between, intervene), from 'inter-' (between) + 'cedere' (to go).
