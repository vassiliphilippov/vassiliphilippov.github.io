bitterly

1. ADV

/ ˈbɪtɚli / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəli / (BrE)
With bitterness or strong resentment; in a manner expressing deep anger or disappointment.

Examples

And no doubt many people in states like the Carolinas and Georgia, which were among the most Tory in sentiment in the eighteenth century, bitterly regretted the revolt against the Crown.

Synset Examples

she complained bitterly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentfully - in a manner showing bitterness or displeasure
  • Synonym: angrily - in a manner showing anger
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic manner

Etymology

From 'bitter' + '-ly', where 'bitter' derives from Old English 'biter' (sharp, angry).

2. ADV

/ ˈbɪt̬.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈbɪt.ə.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows deep anger, sadness, disappointment, or resentment; with intense emotional pain.

Examples

Claire was bitterly disappointed but determined not to let the rebuff daunt her purpose.

Synset Examples

he was bitterly disappointed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentfully - with feelings of anger or resentment
  • Synonym: grievously - in a manner causing sorrow or pain

Etymology

From 'bitter' + -ly.
