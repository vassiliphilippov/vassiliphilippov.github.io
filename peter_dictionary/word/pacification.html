pacification

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of bringing peace or appeasing someone, especially by calming anger or agitation.

Examples

The significant things in it, at the higher religious levels, are the inner emotional, mental, and spiritual occurrences that fill the pressing human needs of self-preservation, self pacification, and self completion.

Synset Examples

a wonderful skill in the pacification of crying infants
his unsuccessful mollification of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeasement - the act of making someone less angry or hostile
  • Synonym: mollification - the act of calming or soothing someone

Etymology

From Latin pacificatio, from pacificare 'to make peace'.
