confinement

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the state or act of keeping someone within limits or restricting their liberty, especially by imprisonment

Examples

The sentence: 360 days' confinement in the county jail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being imprisoned
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free

Etymology

From French *confinement*, from confiner 'to confine', from Latin *confinare* (to border, limit).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
The final stage of pregnancy from the onset of labor contractions to the birth of a child, also known as childbirth or labor (now largely historical or formal).

Examples

"Fanny and Mrs. Godwin will probably be glad to hear that Mary has safely recovered from a very favorable confinement, and that her child is well ".

Synset Examples

she was in labor for six hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor - the process of childbirth, especially the period from the start of contractions to delivery
  • Synonym: parturition - the act or process of giving birth to offspring

Etymology

From confinare (Latin) meaning 'to border, limit'; referring here to the state of being confined during childbirth.
