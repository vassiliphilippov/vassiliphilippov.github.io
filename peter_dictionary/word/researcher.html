researcher

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɝːtʃər / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who conducts careful investigation, study, or inquiry to discover new facts or information, especially in an academic or scientific setting

Examples

A group of researchers at the University of Washington have given a paper which briefly outlines some of these techniques.
In fact, the noted psychologist and sex researcher, Dr. Albert Ellis, has declared flatly that women are "sexually superior "to men.
But since 1927, researchers digging into ancient court records and legal files have been able to find illuminating pieces of information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigator - person who investigates or examines carefully

Etymology

From 'research' + '-er'; 'research' from Old French 'recercher' (to seek out), from Latin 'circum-' (around) + 'cerchare' (to search).
