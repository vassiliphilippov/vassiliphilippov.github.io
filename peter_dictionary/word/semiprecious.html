semiprecious

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈprɛʃəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈprɛʃəs / (BrE)
(of gemstones) having a lower commercial value than precious stones; used to describe gems that are not classified as precious.

Examples

An old shipwreck, a high dam, an old covered bridge, a place to find agates or other semi-precious stones or a place to pan gold, etcetera may be of interest.

Synset Examples

such semiprecious stones as amethyst, garnet, jade, and tourmaline

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precious - of gemstones: having high value and rarity

Etymology

From 'semi-' (meaning 'half' or 'partly') + 'precious', indicating lesser value compared to precious stones.
