rebuff

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
a blunt or abrupt rejection or refusal, especially as an expression of disapproval or anger

Examples

Claire was bitterly disappointed but determined not to let the rebuff daunt her purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - an act or instance of treating someone with disdain or ignoring them
  • Synonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept or consider something or someone
  • Antonym: welcome - the act of greeting or receiving with pleasure

Etymology

From Italian *ribuffo* (a snub), from *ribuffare* (to scold, snarl at).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
to reject someone or something in an abrupt or ungracious manner

Examples

They are relentlessly rebuffed on all sides by a waitress, the police, and an intruding government tutor.
After every money argument, she rebuffed her husband's overtures until the matter was settled in her favor.
Central was rebuffed by the other two railroads in previous attempts to make it a three-way merger.
I said quietly, gaining nerve, ready to ask any question at all, no matter how intimate, ready to be rebuffed, "Then why did she leave Israel?

Synset Examples

She snubbed his proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - to rebuff, ignore, or spurn disdainfully
  • Antonym: accept - to consent to receive something or agree to a proposal

Etymology

From Italian 'ribuffare' (to scorn, snub), from 'ri-' (again) + 'buffare' (to puff out the cheeks in contempt).
