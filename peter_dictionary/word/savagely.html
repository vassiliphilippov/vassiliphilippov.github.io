savagely

1. ADV

/ ˈsævɪdʒli / (AmE), / ˈsavɪdʒli / (BrE)
In a fierce, violent, or vicious manner.

Examples

The wife, Amra, and her lover are both savagely portrayed, she as incarnate sensuality, "voluptuous "and "indolent ", possibly "a mischief maker ", with "a kind of luxurious cunning "to set against her apparent simplicity, her "bird like brain ".
Alastor was published only to be savagely attacked, contemptuously ignored.

Synset Examples

he was viciously attacked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viciously - in a cruel, violent, or ferocious manner
  • Synonym: brutally - in a harsh, cruel, or violent manner
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild, kind, or tender manner

Etymology

From savage + -ly; 'savage' from Old French 'sauvage', from Latin 'silvaticus' (wild).

2. ADV

/ ˈsævɪdʒli / (AmE), / ˈsævɪdʒli / (BrE)
In a wild, fierce, or violent manner, like an animal.

Examples

So they beat her like an animal.

Synset Examples

she cried out savagely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferociously - in a savagely fierce or violent way
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or kind manner; opposite of savagely

Etymology

From 'savage' + '-ly'; 'savage' from Old French 'sauvage', from Latin 'silvaticus' (of the woods).
