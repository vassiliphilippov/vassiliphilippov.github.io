engrossed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡroʊst / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊst / (BrE)
(of a document) written formally in large, clear script, especially for legal or official purposes.

Examples

Shaw and Skorich headed a group of players, coaches and team officials who received an engrossed copy of an official city citation and a pair of silver cufflinks shaped like a football.

Etymology

From the legal sense of 'engross', from Anglo-Norman 'engrosser' (to write in large letters).
