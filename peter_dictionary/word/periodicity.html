periodicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪriəˈdɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriəˈdɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality or fact of recurring or being repeated at regular intervals

Examples

This and other fears of the solar system have disappeared gradually, first, with the Ptolemaic system and its built-in concept of periodicity and then, more firmly, with the Newtonian innovation of an universal force that could account quantitatively for both terrestrial and celestial motions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frequency - the rate at which something occurs over a particular period

Etymology

From Latin 'periodicus' (periodic) and suffix '-ity' indicating a state or quality.
