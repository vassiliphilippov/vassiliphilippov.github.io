apron

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪprən / (AmE), / ˈeɪprən / (BrE)
A protective garment worn over the front of one's clothes and tied around the waist, usually to keep clothing clean while cooking, cleaning, or working.

Examples

Barbara bought some cotton aprons, and Harold bought shoestrings.
Winston watched her fumbling to untie her apron.
She sent Glendora back to the house, her basket and her apron laden.

Etymology

From Middle English *napron*, from Old French *naperon* (small cloth), diminutive of *nape* (cloth, napkin). Rebracketed due to historical misdivision ('a napron' > 'an apron').
