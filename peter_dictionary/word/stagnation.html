stagnation

1. NOUN

/ stægˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / stægˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A state in which activity, growth, or development stops or slows down, especially in business, art, or similar fields.

Examples

Hence, the flow conditions at the anode of free burning arcs resemble those near a stagnation point.

Synset Examples

economic growth of less than 1% per year is considered to be economic stagnation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • Antonym: growth - an increase or development
  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth

Etymology

From Latin stagnatio, from stagnare (to stand still).
