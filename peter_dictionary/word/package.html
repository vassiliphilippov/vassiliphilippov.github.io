package

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪdʒ / (BrE)
A container, usually wrapped or boxed, in which something is packed to be sent, stored, or sold.

Examples

Dr. Verdi had told him to deliver his package in person.
Too many plant officials are all too eager to buy a package program from an insurance company simply because it works for another plant.
Just a big pile of self-confidence in an almost teensy package.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parcel - a wrapped bundle or package sent by mail or carried

Etymology

From Middle French paquet (bundle), from Old French pacquet, diminutive of pacque (bundle, package).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpakɪdʒ / (BrE)
a collection of items wrapped or boxed together, often for sale, transport, or as a unit offering

Examples

Aim to balance your employee benefit package.
The blue-collar worker thus knows his insurance package, for example, costs $ 227.72.
Have the insurance company or your own accounting department break down the cost of your insurance package periodically.
The guy at the gates has a package.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parcel - a package or bundle of things wrapped together

Etymology

From Middle French paquet (bundle), from Old French pacquet, diminutive of pac, of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈpæk.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpæk.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
to put goods or items into a box or container, usually for sale or transportation

Examples

There is a trend to packaging meat at a central source, freezing it, and shipping it to outlying stores, where meat cutters will not be required.
We are slowly being regimented to having everything packaged, whether we want it or not.

Synset Examples

box the gift, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - to put into a box, usually for shipment or protection
  • Synonym: pack - to fill a container or box with items for storage or transport
