canonize

1. VERB

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkanəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
To officially declare (a person who has died) to be a saint, usually through a formal process in certain Christian churches.

Examples

The English saints are widely venerated, quite naturally, and now there is great hope that the Forty Martyrs and Cardinal Newman will soon be canonized.

Synset Examples

After he was shown to have performed a miracle, the priest was canonized

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beatify - declare a deceased person blessed and worthy of veneration, a step before canonization

Etymology

From Old French canoniser, from Late Latin canonizare, from canon (church rule or law).
