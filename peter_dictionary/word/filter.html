filter

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
to remove unwanted substances or impurities by passing a liquid, gas, light, etc., through a filter

Examples

The light was filtered by the soft glass window of the thermostat thus ensuring that only light absorbed by the chlorine and not by the carbon tetrachloride could enter the reaction cell.
The light beam from the lamp was filtered through a half standard thickness Corning 1840 filter.
In the eyepiece a Wratten 2 B filter was used to filter off residual ultra-violet light.
A red filter, Zeiss barrier filter with the code (Schott) designation BG 23, was also used in the ocular lens assembly as it improved the contrast between specific and nonspecific fluorescence.

Synset Examples

filter out the impurities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purify - to make clean by removing impurities

Etymology

From French *filtrer*, from Medieval Latin *filtrare*, from *filtrum* (felt used as a filter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
A device that removes unwanted substances from a liquid, gas, light, or other material by letting it pass through a porous material.

Examples

The light beam from the lamp was filtered through a half standard thickness Corning 1840 filter.
In the eyepiece a Wratten 2 B filter was used to filter off residual ultra-violet light.
Removal of the suspended solids by a membrane filter yielded an average effluent containing only 20 mg / l BOD.
Vent pipes also are necessary (as shown in figs. 9, 10, and 11), but filters are not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strainer - device for removing solid matter from liquid
  • Synonym: purifier - device that removes impurities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *filtrum* (felt used to strain liquid).

3. VERB

/ ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
(of light, liquid, etc.) to pass slowly through a substance or material that blocks some parts but lets others through.

Examples

A carriage crunched by, its dim lights filtering through the gloom.
Exposure problems may occur in these forest areas where uneven lighting results from shafts of sunlight filtering through the overhead branches.
They, and the two large fans which I could dimly see as daylight filtered through their vents, down at the far end of the hall, could be turned on by a master switch situated inside the office.

Synset Examples

Water permeates sand easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permeate - (of liquid or gas) to spread through something and be present in every part of it
  • Synonym: seep - to flow or leak slowly through a porous material or small holes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *filtrum* (felt for straining liquid), via French *filtrer*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
an electronic device that allows only certain frequencies of a signal to pass through, blocking others

Examples

Three different wavelengths of ultraviolet radiation are selected by the variable filters placed in front of the three mercury xenon lights which serve as the ultraviolet sources.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bandpass filter - an electronic filter that passes frequencies within a certain range and rejects frequencies outside that range
  • Degree Lower: low-pass filter - an electronic filter that passes signals with a frequency lower than a selected cutoff frequency
  • Degree Lower: high-pass filter - an electronic filter that passes signals with a frequency higher than a selected cutoff frequency
