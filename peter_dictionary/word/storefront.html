storefront

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔr.frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈstɔː.frʌnt / (BrE)
The front side of a store that faces onto the street, typically featuring display windows to attract customers.

Examples

It was a Negro section of peeling row houses, store-front churches and ragged children.
He flung off Lord's hand and attempted to push past him, inadvertently shoving him into a storefront.

Etymology

Formed from 'store' (shop) and 'front'; common in American English from the early 20th century.
