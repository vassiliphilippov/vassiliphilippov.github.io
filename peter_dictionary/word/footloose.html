footloose

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʊtˌlus / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌluːs / (BrE)
Free to go anywhere or do anything one wishes; not tied down by responsibilities or commitments.

Examples

"The world that was once foot-loose and fancy-free ", he said, "has now become screw-loose and frenzy free.

Synset Examples

Americans have always been a footloose people always moving on
a footloose young man eager to see the big city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fancy-free - without commitments or responsibilities

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' + 'loose', meaning 'free to move as one wishes'.
