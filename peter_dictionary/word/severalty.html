severalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvɚlti / (AmE), / ˈsɛv(ə)lti / (BrE)
The state of being separate or distinct; especially, ownership of property by one person alone, not jointly.

Examples

Because community not severalty of property is the law of nature no man can assert an absolutely unalterable right to what is his.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: joint tenancy - co-ownership of property by two or more parties

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman severalté, from Old French, based on 'several'.
