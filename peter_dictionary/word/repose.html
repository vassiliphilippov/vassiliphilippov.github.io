repose

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpoʊz / (AmE), / rɪˈpəʊz / (BrE)
A state of rest, sleep, or tranquility; freedom from activity, worry, or responsibility.

Examples

Her voice was ripe and full and her teeth flashed again in Sicilian brilliance before the warm curved lips met and her mouth settled in repose.

Synset Examples

took his repose by the swimming pool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest - a state of ceasing from work or movement to relax, sleep, or recover strength
  • Antonym: activity - the condition of being active or engaged in movement or work

Etymology

From Old French *reposer* (to rest), from Latin *repausare* (to rest again).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈpoʊz / (AmE), / rɪˈpəʊz / (BrE)
(formal) To place or entrust something, such as trust, confidence, or responsibility, in a person or thing.

Examples

It seemed to me that the liberals had scrapped the balanced polarity and reposed both liberty and the fundamental law in the common man.

Synset Examples

These philosophers reposed the law in the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrust - to assign responsibility for doing something to someone
  • Antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to

Etymology

From Old French reposer (to rest), from Latin repono (to put back, deposit, entrust).
