decent

1. ADJ

/ ˈdisənt / (AmE), / ˈdiːsənt / (BrE)
Behaving in a manner that conforms to accepted standards of morality or respectability; proper and virtuous.

Examples

At least the Union officer had been decent enough to provide a candle.
Then, not many years later, the Un-American Activities Committee, under the leadership of Martin Dies, pilloried hundreds of decent, patriotic citizens.
first, because it is important in itself, and, second, because he knows that the American public cares about a decent respect for the opinions of mankind.
I don'’t like to see you come out to this clean country in your oily hair dressed up in those silk suits, trying to pass yourselves off as decent Americans.

Synset Examples

from a decent family
a nice girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectable - regarded by society to be good, proper, or correct
  • Synonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: improper - not in accordance with accepted standards, especially of morality or honesty

Etymology

From Latin 'decēns' (fitting, proper), present participle of 'decēre' (to be fitting).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdiːsənt / (AmE), / ˈdiːsənt / (BrE)
Conforming to accepted standards of behavior or propriety; appropriate or proper.

Examples

There was, furthermore, the crowd of curious onlookers gathered in the street and a couple more cops to hold them at a decent distance.

Synset Examples

her becoming modesty
comely behavior
it is not comme il faut for a gentleman to be constantly asking for money
a decent burial
seemly behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improper - not conforming to accepted standards of behavior or propriety
  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From Latin 'decentem,' present participle of 'decēre' (to be fitting, proper).
