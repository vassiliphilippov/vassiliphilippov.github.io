contortion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɔr.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɔː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of twisting or bending something out of its normal shape, especially in an unnatural or strained way

Examples

With shout and slow dance, with tears and song, with scream and contortion, the corner group was beset by hysteria and shivering, wailing, shouting, possession of something that seemed like an alien and outside force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - an act of turning or bending something
  • Synonym: distortion - the act of changing from the natural, usual, or original shape

Etymology

From Latin contortio, from contorquere (to twist together).
