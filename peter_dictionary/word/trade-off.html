trade-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪd ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd ˌɒf / (BrE)
a balance achieved between two desirable but incompatible features; a compromise or concession

Examples

That's the trade-off.

Synset Examples

I faced a tradeoff between eating and buying my medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - an agreement by mutual concession

Etymology

From 'trade' + 'off', originally from economic terminology meaning a situation requiring choices that result in loss of one quality for gain of another.
