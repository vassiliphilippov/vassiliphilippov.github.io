millenarianism

1. NOUN

/ mɪˌlɛˈnɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / mɪˌlɪnəˈrɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in a coming millennium of peace and prosperity, especially as foretold in Christian doctrine and the Book of Revelation

Examples

He says: "Beside the Protestant philosophy of Progress, as expressed in radical or conservative millenarianism, should be placed the doctrine of the democratic faith which affirmed it to be the duty of the destiny of the United States to assist in the creation of a better world by keeping lighted the beacon of democracy ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millennialism - belief in a future thousand-year age of blessedness; often synonymous with millenarianism

Etymology

From Latin 'millenarius' (containing a thousand), from 'mille' (thousand) + '-arian' + '-ism'.
