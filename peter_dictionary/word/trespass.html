trespass

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɛspæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs / (BrE)
to enter someone's land or property without permission, especially unlawfully

Examples

He then got a job with the Chicago Herald-Examiner as a circulation slugger, a rough fighter employed to see that his paper's news pitches were not trespassed upon by rival vendors.
The moon had sunk below the black crest of the mountains and the land, seen through eyes that had grown accustomed to the absence of light, looked primeval, as if no man had ever trespassed before.

Synset Examples

Don't trespass on my land!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invade - to enter a place with force or as an enemy
  • Synonym: intrude - to enter without invitation or permission
  • Antonym: withdraw - to leave or remove oneself from a place

Etymology

From Old French 'trespasser' (to pass over, transgress), from 'tres-' (across) + 'passer' (to pass).
