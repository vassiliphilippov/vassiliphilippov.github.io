shot

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑt / (AmE), / ʃɒt / (BrE)
An act of hitting, striking, or propelling a ball with a club, racket, bat, cue, or hand in sports such as golf, tennis, or billiards.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - act of hitting a ball in sports such as golf or billiards

2. NOUN

/ ʃɑt / (AmE), / ʃɒt / (BrE)
an attempt or opportunity to do or achieve something, especially one chance among several or an informal try

Examples

Tonight at 8 o'clock the Cardinals, who gave the Pirates as much trouble as anyone did in 1960, breaking even with them, will get their first 1961 shot at baseball's world champions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - an attempt to do something
  • Synonym: opportunity - a chance to do something

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'sceot' (a shooting), later extended metaphorically from 'a shot' (from a gun, etc.) to mean 'an attempt.'
