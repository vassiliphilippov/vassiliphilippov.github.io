knoll

1. NOUN

/ noʊl / (AmE), / nɒl / (BrE)
A small natural hill, especially one that is rounded or isolated.

Examples

Even at this short distance they were only vague shapes, setting up the machine gun on a small knoll so that it could fire above the heads of the rest of the patrol.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high or craggy as a mountain
  • Synonym: mound - a small artificial or natural pile of earth or stones

Etymology

Old English cnoll (hilltop, small hill).
