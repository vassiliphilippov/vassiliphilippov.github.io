daily

1. ADV

/ ˈdeɪli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪli / (BrE)
Happening every day; each day.

Examples

Five of these done daily for about a week will develop the strength for one push-up.
No additional transfusions were necessary after the institution of prednisone in July, 1957, in an initial dose of 40 mg. daily with gradual tapering to 10 mg. daily.

In an attempt to reverse the downhill trend by stimulating the bone marrow and controlling any hemolytic component, triamcinolone, 16 mg. daily, was begun on Sept. 26, 1958, and continued until Feb. 18, 1959.

Synset Examples

he stops by daily

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nightly - happening every night (adverb)

Etymology

From Middle English 'dayly', from Old English 'dæġ' (day) + '-ly'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪli / (BrE)
Happening, done, or used every day; relating to each day.

Examples

Good morning, Portland Daily Bugle.

Synset Examples

daily routine
a daily paper

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: weekly - occurring every week
  • Degree Higher: hourly - occurring every hour
  • Degree Lower: nightly - occurring every night

Etymology

From Middle English *dayly*, from day + -ly.
