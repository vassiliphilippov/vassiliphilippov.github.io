stray

1. VERB

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
to move about without a fixed course, aim, or destination, often away from the place where one should be

Examples

She soared over the new pastor like an avenging angel lest he stray from the path and not know all the truth and gossip of which she was chief repository.
A dominant motive is the poet's longing for his homeland and its boyhood associations: "Not men folk, but the fields where I would stray, The stones where as a child I used to play ".

Synset Examples

The gypsies roamed the woods
roving vagabonds
the wandering Jew
The cattle roam across the prairie
the laborers drift from one town to the next
They rolled from town to town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wander - move about aimlessly or without fixed course
  • Synonym: roam - move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically
  • Antonym: stay - remain in one place, not wander

Etymology

From Old French *estraier* (to wander), from Vulgar Latin *estragare* (to go astray).

2. ADJ

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
Occurring or appearing apart from others of its kind; separated and not part of a group.

Examples

A man could be tossed outside the dimension of time by a stray bullet these days.
Alison was still sound asleep; he made fresh coffee and searched through all the desk drawers for more cigarettes before thinking of her handbag, and found a crumpled stray cigarette at its bottom, which tasted peculiarly of face powder.

Synset Examples

isolated instances of rebellion
a few stray crumbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - occurring alone or apart from others
  • Synonym: scattered - occurring or distributed at irregular intervals or locations

Etymology

From Old French *estraier* (to stray), from Vulgar Latin *extraviare* (to wander from the road).

3. NOUN

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
a domestic animal that has wandered away or is lost, especially one found without an owner

Examples

A fire had just been lighted, he saw, and things had been set out for drinks, and, like any stray, his response to these comforts was instantaneous.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: feral - an animal in the wild descended from domesticated species

Etymology

From Old French 'estraier' (to stray), from Vulgar Latin '*extraviare' (to go out of the way).

4. VERB

/ streɪ / (AmE), / streɪ / (BrE)
to move away from the correct place, path, or direction; to wander from a set course, often unintentionally

Examples

But the seven-iron shot he used to approach the green strayed into a bunker and lodged in a slight depression.

Synset Examples

The child strayed from the path and her parents lost sight of her
don't drift from the set course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wander - to move about aimlessly or without a fixed course
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in a particular place or position

Etymology

From Old French 'estraier', based on Latin 'extravagari', meaning 'wander outside'.
