vengeance

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.dʒəns / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.dʒəns / (BrE)
the act of punishing or harming someone in return for an injury or wrong, often with a sense of retribution or justice

Examples

Concerning the sentence, Foss wrote, "If it be possible that mercy shall override vengeance and that John Brown's sentence shall be commuted to imprisonment, it would be well - well for the country and for Virginia ".
The foregoing, aided by several clues I 'll withhold to keep you on your toes, will pursue you with a tenacity worthy of Inspector Javert, but before they close in, gird yourselves, I repeat, for a vengeance infinitely more pitiless.
I forgo the vengeance of my son.

Synset Examples

Vengeance is mine; I will repay, saith the Lord
For vengeance I would do nothing. This nation is too great to look for mere revenge
he swore vengeance on the man who betrayed him
the swiftness of divine retribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the action of inflicting hurt or harm on someone for an injury or wrong suffered at their hands
  • Antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone

Etymology

From Old French *venjance*, from Late Latin *vindicta* (revenge).
