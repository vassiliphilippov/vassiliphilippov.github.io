blackboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈblæk.bɔːd / (BrE)
A large, flat board with a dark surface, often made of slate, used for writing on with chalk, especially in classrooms.

Examples

These errors were then collected and written on a blackboard, condensing similar ideas.
The manager sat behind the group so he could see and count the hands that went up, and the director wrote the numbers on the blackboard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whiteboard - a smooth, white board for writing on with markers, now common in classrooms and offices

Etymology

From 'black' + 'board', originally referring to slate boards used in schools.
