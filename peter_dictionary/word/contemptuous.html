contemptuous

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛmptʃuəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptjʊəs / (BrE)
Showing or expressing deep disdain or scorn for someone or something.

Examples

They did; though contemptuous, they were still polite.
There seemed to be a contemptuous purpose in the way he sat there with his eyes glued to Drexel Street and his back in opposition to the church behind him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scornful - feeling or expressing contempt or derision
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness or deference

Etymology

From Latin 'contemptuosus', from 'contemptus' (scorn, disdain).
