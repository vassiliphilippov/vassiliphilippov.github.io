receiver

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
A device that receives radio or television signals and converts them into sound or images.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transmitter - device that sends out radio or TV signals

Etymology

From receive + -er, formed on the pattern of agent nouns in English.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
(Law) A person, usually appointed by a court, who is responsible for managing, preserving, or liquidating the assets of a business or individual for the benefit of affected parties.

Examples

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːvɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvə / (BrE)
the part of a telephone that you hold to your ear and mouth to listen and speak, which converts electrical signals into sounds

Examples

She slapped the receiver into its holder and stepped away.
He scooped up the receiver and said, "Police chief ", into the mouthpiece, and then, "Oh yes, Mr. Benson.
Casey heard the click of the distant receiver before he could open his mouth, and it took him no more than three seconds to make his decision.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French recevoir (to receive), from Latin recipere (to receive).
