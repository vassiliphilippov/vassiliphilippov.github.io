zeal

1. NOUN

/ ziːl / (AmE), / ziːl / (BrE)
excessive enthusiasm or fervor to achieve something or accomplish a goal, often to an extreme or fanatical degree.

Examples

J. Wheaton Smith, editor of the Warren Telegraph stated that "the ends of justice must be satisfied, a solitary example must be set, in order that all those misnamed philantropists [ sic ], who, actuated by a blind zeal, dare to instigate riot, treason, and murder, may heed it and shape their future course accordingly ".

Synset Examples

he had an absolute zeal for litigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Degree Higher: fanaticism - extreme and uncritical zeal or obsessive enthusiasm
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Late Latin zelus, from Ancient Greek zēlos (zeal, jealousy).

2. NOUN

/ ziːl / (AmE), / ziːl / (BrE)
great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause or objective.

Examples

In either case, they do not appreciate the private detective's zeal.

Synset Examples

they were imbued with a revolutionary ardor
he felt a kind of religious zeal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ardor - intense enthusiasm or passion
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Greek zēlos (ardor, emulation) via Latin zelus.
