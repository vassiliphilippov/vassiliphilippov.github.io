beguiling

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈɡaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
Charming or attractive in a way that delights or enchants, sometimes in a deceptive way.

Examples

Soaring to stardom along with him, Margo Felicity Brighetti, a luscious and curvaceously beguiling Italian starlet, turns in a creditable performance as an airplane mechanic.

Synset Examples

an alluring prospect
her alluring smile
the voice was low and beguiling
difficult to say no to an enticing advertisement
a tempting invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming
  • Synonym: tempting - attractive and makes you want to have or do something
  • Synonym: enticing - attractive or interesting, making you want to do or have something
  • Antonym: unattractive - not pleasing or appealing to the senses

Etymology

From Middle English *begilen* (to deceive, cheat), from be- + guile (deceit, trickery).
