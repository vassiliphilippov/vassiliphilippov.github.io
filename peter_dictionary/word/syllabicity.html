syllabicity

1. NOUN

/ sɪləˈbɪsəti / (AmE), / sɪləˈbɪsɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of consisting of or relating to syllables, especially the pattern of syllable formation in a language.

Examples

His objective is merely to determine "what distinctions of length and syllabicity it may be desirable to make explicit in a Kikuyu orthography " (59).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syllable - a unit of pronunciation typically containing a vowel sound

Etymology

Formed from 'syllable' and the suffix '-icity', modeled on 'elasticity', from Greek 'sullabē' (syllable).
