indiscriminately

1. ADV

/ ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnətli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnətli / (BrE)
In a way that is done without careful judgment, selection, or distinction; randomly or without discrimination.

Examples

Are you indiscriminantly offering unnecessary medical services - flu shots, sun lamp treatments, etc.?

Synset Examples

the houses were randomly scattered
bullets were fired into the crowd at random

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - in a way lacking plan, order, or purpose

Etymology

From 'indiscriminate' (from Latin in- 'not' + discriminatus, past participle of discriminare 'to distinguish') + -ly (adverbial suffix).
