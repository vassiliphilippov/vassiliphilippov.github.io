symmetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / ˈsɪmɪtri / (BrE)
The quality of having parts that match each other in size, shape, or arrangement, giving balance and proportion.

Examples

The aspects of physical development that catch the judges' eyes and which rightfully influence their decisions are symmetry and that hallmark of the true champion - superior definition of the muscles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asymmetry - lack of balance or equality between parts

Etymology

From Greek 'symmetria' (commensurability, symmetry), from 'syn-' (together) + 'metron' (measure).
