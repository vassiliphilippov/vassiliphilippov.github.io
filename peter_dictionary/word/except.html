except

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to prevent from being included; to leave out or exclude.

Examples

The focus of novelty in this world now lay in the south-eastern districts of the Greek mainland, and by 800 virtually the entire Aegean, always excepting its northern shores, had accepted the Geometric style of pottery.
Nobody else could catch one except me.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Antonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or set

Etymology

From Latin 'exceptus', past participle of 'excipere' (to take out, exclude).
