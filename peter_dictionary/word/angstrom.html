angstrom

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋstrəm / (AmE), / ˈæŋstrəm / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one ten-billionth of a meter (0.1 nanometer), commonly used to express wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation and sizes of atoms and molecules.
