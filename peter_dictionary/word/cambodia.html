Cambodia

1. PROPN

/ kæmˈboʊdiə / (AmE), / kæmˈbəʊdiə / (BrE)
A country in Southeast Asia, formerly part of French Indochina, bordered by Thailand, Laos, Vietnam, and the Gulf of Thailand.

Examples

Today Northern Vietnam is overtly Communist; Laos is teetering between Communism and pro Communist neutralism; Cambodia is, for all practical purposes, neutralist.

Etymology

From French 'Cambodge,' from Khmer 'Kâmpŭchéa,' the native name for Cambodia.
