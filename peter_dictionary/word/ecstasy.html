ecstasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.stə.si / (AmE), / ˈɛk.stə.si / (BrE)
A state of intense joy or happiness, in which one is carried away by overwhelming emotion.

Examples

Each high note had the crowd in ecstasy so that it stopped the show midway in the "Mad Scene ", but the real reason was a realization of the extraordinary performance unfolding at the moment.

Synset Examples

listening to sweet music in a perfect rapture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapture - a feeling of intense pleasure or joy
  • Degree Lower: bliss - perfect happiness; great joy

Etymology

From Greek *ekstasis* (standing outside oneself, trance), via Latin *ecstasis* and Old French *extasie*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.stə.si / (AmE), / ˈek.stə.si / (BrE)
A feeling or state of overwhelming happiness or joyful excitement.

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".
It was ecstasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bliss - a state of extreme happiness
  • Synonym: rapture - a feeling of intense pleasure or joy
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering and unhappiness

Etymology

From Greek ekstasis (entrancement, trance), from ek- (out) + stasis (standing).
