respite

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛspɪt / (BrE)
a short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant, especially from work or exertion

Examples

Not without a face-saving respite of at least a few minutes.

Synset Examples

we took a 10-minute break
he took time out to recuperate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - a short interval for rest
  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop in an activity or speech
  • Synonym: intermission - a break between parts of a performance or activity

Etymology

From Old French 'respite' (delay, respect), from Latin 'respectus' (consideration, refuge).
