sniper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnaɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnaɪpə / (BrE)
a person who shoots at people from a concealed position, typically with a rifle, often as a military or law enforcement role

Examples

There was a sniper's nest in a mountain cave, and it was picking off our men with devilish accuracy.
The sniper, whether psychopathic marksman or murderer by intent, would hardly have walked to his vantage point with rifle over shoulder, whistling a marching tune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marksman - a person skilled at shooting

Etymology

From 'snipe', originally describing someone who shoots snipes (a type of bird), later applied to marksmen concealed from view.
