blab

1. VERB

/ blæb / (AmE), / blæb / (BrE)
to reveal confidential information or secrets, especially by talking indiscreetly

Examples

Britain began designing the ship in 1956 but got nowhere until the American government decided to end a ban on sharing military secrets with Britain that had been imposed after Fuchs blabbed.

Synset Examples

Be careful--his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill the beans - to divulge secret information
  • Synonym: leak - to allow secret or sensitive information to become known
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret

Etymology

Possibly imitative of the sound of indiscreet talking; recorded in English since the 15th century.
