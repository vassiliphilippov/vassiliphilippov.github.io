acceptable

1. ADJ

/ əkˈseptəbəl / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptəbl / (BrE)
Satisfactory or adequate; meeting the required standard or criteria.

Examples

One or two of the schools have a five year curriculum, but the usual pattern of American education has limited most of them to the four year plan which seems to be the minimum in acceptable institutions.
The simple passage of an additional eleven months' time makes the second 3 % boost more acceptable.

Synset Examples

acceptable levels of radiation
performances varied from acceptable to excellent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unacceptable - not satisfactory or adequate; not meeting the required standard
  • Synonym: satisfactory - fulfilling expectations or needs; acceptable
  • Synonym: adequate - satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity
  • Degree Higher: excellent - extremely good; outstanding (higher degree)

Etymology

From Old French 'acceptable', from Latin 'acceptabilis' (worthy of acceptance).
