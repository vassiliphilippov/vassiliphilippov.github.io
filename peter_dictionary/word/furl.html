furl

1. VERB

/ fɜrl / (AmE), / fɜːl / (BrE)
to roll up and secure something, such as a sail, flag, or piece of cloth

Examples

He furled the slashed sides of the canvas tarpaulins, leaving the ribs and wagon open.

Synset Examples

Roll up the cloth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfurl - to unroll or spread out something that was rolled up

Etymology

From Old French *ferler*, from *fer* (up) + *ler* (to tie).
