refurbish

1. VERB

/ riˈfɝː.bɪʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈfɜː.bɪʃ / (BrE)
to clean, decorate, and repair something to make it look new or improved again

Examples

Of the 885 specimens newly mounted or refurbished, 254 were prepared for other agencies.

Synset Examples

we refurbished the guest wing
My wife wants us to renovate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovate - to restore something to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: restore - to return something to its former condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + furnish, modeled after French 'refurbir' (to polish up).
