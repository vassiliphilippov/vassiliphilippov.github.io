abstractionism

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstrækʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
A style or movement in art in which the subject matter is represented by shapes, colors, and forms rather than by realistic, pictorial depiction.

Examples

It is the abstractionism, the unrealism, of the pure idealist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract art - art that does not attempt to represent external reality precisely
  • Antonym: realism - artistic style depicting subjects as they appear in everyday life

Etymology

From 'abstraction' + '-ism', denoting a movement or style emphasizing abstraction in art.
