secularist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkjələrɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlərɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports the separation of religion from government and education; an advocate of secularism.

Examples

This group is secularist and their program tends to be technological.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secularism - the principle of separating religion from government and education

Etymology

From secular + -ist; 'secular' from Latin 'saecularis' (of a generation or age).
