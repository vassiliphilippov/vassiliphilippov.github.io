indignant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɪɡnənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪɡnənt / (BrE)
Feeling or showing anger or annoyance at something considered unjust or wrong.

Examples

The return of the Catholic hierarchy in 1850 was looked upon with indignant disapprobation and, in fact, was charged with being a gesture of disloyalty.
In its account of the Trafton lecture, the Providence Daily Post said that the remarks of Rev. Trafton made the people indignant.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong annoyance, displeasure, or hostility
  • Degree Higher: outraged - feeling fierce anger or shock, especially due to injustice
  • Degree Lower: annoyed - feeling slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

From Latin indignans, present participle of indignari (to consider as unworthy), from indignus (unworthy).
