pendulum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.dʒə.ləm / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.djʊ.ləm / (BrE)
A weight hung from a fixed point so that it can swing freely back and forth, especially one that regulates the movement of a clock.

Examples

He stared at the far morning, expecting a pendulum to swing across the horizon.

Etymology

From Latin 'pendulus' (hanging), from 'pendere' (to hang).
