Nomia melanderi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.mi.ə məˈlæn.dɛr.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.mi.ə mɛˈlæn.də.raɪ / (BrE)
A solitary bee species native to the western United States, important for pollinating alfalfa.

Examples

Nomia melanderi can be found in tremendous numbers in certain parts of the United States west of the Great Plains, for example, in Utah and central Washington.

Etymology

From the genus name Nomia (New Latin, from Greek) and the species epithet honoring American entomologist Charles T. Melander.
