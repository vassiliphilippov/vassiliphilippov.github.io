whence

1. ADV

/ wɛns / (AmE), / wɛns / (BrE)
From what place, source, or cause (used chiefly in formal or archaic English).

Examples

"Whence, then, comes the substance of the first man?

Etymology

From Middle English whannes, from Old English hwanon (‘from where’), akin to Old High German hwanāna.
