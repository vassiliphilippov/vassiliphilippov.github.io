honeymoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌmuːn / (BrE)
a holiday taken by a couple immediately after their marriage, especially one spent abroad or in a romantic location

Examples

That night the two of them left for a week's honeymoon in Acapulco.
He had picked out this pathless trail, instead of the common one, in a moment of romantic fancy, to give them privacy on their honeymoon.

Etymology

From 'honey' (signifying sweetness) + 'moon' (referring to a period of one month), first used in the 16th century to mean the initial month of marriage.

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌniˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌmuːn / (BrE)
to spend a holiday or vacation with one's spouse immediately after getting married, usually to celebrate the marriage

Examples

The couple will honeymoon in Sequoia National Park, Calif..
They were married at a lavish ceremony which was duly recorded in Parvenu and all other magazines and newspapers, and then they honeymooned in Bermuda.
They had honeymooned in Rome; everyone was very high on Rome that year.

Synset Examples

they plan to honeymoon in Hawai'i

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacation - an extended period of leisure, especially away from home or traveling
  • Antonym: work - to engage in activity involving mental or physical effort, typically for pay

Etymology

From the noun 'honeymoon', which comes from 'honey' (sweetness) and 'moon' (month), originally referring to the first month after marriage.
