lodgings

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪŋz / (BrE)
Rooms or accommodation rented for a short or temporary stay, typically as part of a house or apartment.

Examples

The King Arthur offered respectable and convenient lodgings to people from the suburbs who wanted to see a show and did n't want - heaven knew did n't want!
We shared the expenses of the studio, and we all lived within walking distance of it, in cheap lodgings of one kind or another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place where one stays or lives, especially temporarily
  • Synonym: lodging - a place to sleep or stay temporarily; often singular form

Etymology

From lodge, meaning 'to provide accommodation,' plus the plural suffix -ings.
