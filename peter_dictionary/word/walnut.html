walnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔlnʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
The edible nut of any of various trees of the genus Juglans, with a wrinkled, two-lobed seed and a hard shell.

Examples

The heavens opened, pelting them with hail the size of walnuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecan - edible nut from the pecan tree
  • Synonym: hazelnut - edible nut from the hazel tree
  • Synonym: almond - edible nut with a hard shell

Etymology

From Old English *walhhnutu*, meaning 'foreign nut', from *wealh* ('foreign') + *hnutu* ('nut').
