vomit

1. VERB

/ ˈvɑmɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɒmɪt / (BrE)
to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth

Examples

Then, as he doubled, gasping, vomiting the breakfast he had so lately eaten, Lord straightened him with an uppercut.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw up - to vomit; to be sick; informal synonym
  • Synonym: regurgitate - to bring swallowed food up again to the mouth

Etymology

From Latin *vomitare*, frequentative of *vomere* (to vomit).
