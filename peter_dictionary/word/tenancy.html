tenancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.ən.si / (BrE)
the period or fact of occupying or possessing land or property as a tenant

Examples

It should also be recognized that the problem of rural tenancy cannot be solved by administrative decrees alone.
After she had served the detectives coffee and toast (they politely declined eggs, uncomfortable about their tenancy), she settled down with a morning newspaper and began reading the stock market quotations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use or occupation of property for a specified time
  • Synonym: occupancy - the act of occupying or taking possession, especially of land or property

Etymology

From Old French *tenantie*, from tenir 'to hold', from Latin *tenere* 'to hold'.
