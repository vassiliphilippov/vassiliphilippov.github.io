affidavit

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfəˈdeɪvət / (AmE), / ˌafɪˈdeɪvɪt / (BrE)
A written statement made under oath and affirmed as true before a person legally authorized to administer an oath, used as evidence in court.

Examples

Three weeks later following his recovery, armed with a writ issued by the Catskill justice on affidavits prepared by the district attorney, Cook and Russell rode to arrest Martinez.
We have a sworn affidavit.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'affidavit' ('he has sworn'), third person singular perfect of 'affidare' (to trust).
