bastion

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstʃən / (AmE), / ˈbæstɪən / (BrE)
A group, institution, or place strongly defending or upholding particular principles or attitudes.

Examples

The person of the artist becomes a final bastion of meaning in a world rendered meaningless by the march of events and the decay of classical religious and philosophical systems.

Synset Examples

a bastion against corruption
the last bastion of communism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stronghold - a place that has been fortified to protect it against attack
  • Synonym: bulwark - a defensive wall or a strong support or protection

Etymology

From French 'bastion', diminutive of 'baston' (projecting part of a fortification), from Italian 'bastione'.
