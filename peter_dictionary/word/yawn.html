yawn

1. VERB

/ jɑn / (AmE), / jɔːn / (BrE)
To open the mouth wide and breathe in deeply, usually because of tiredness, boredom, or needing more oxygen.

Examples

"Lookit that come down, willya ", said the man, scratching himself, yawning.
Then he would yawn and stretch and shout, "All out.

Synset Examples

The child yawned during the long performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gape - to open wide, especially the mouth or a space

Etymology

From Old English *ginian* (to gape, yawn), influenced by Old Norse *gína*.
