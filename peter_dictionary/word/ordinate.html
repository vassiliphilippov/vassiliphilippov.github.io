ordinate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrd.ɪ.nət / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dɪ.nət / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The y-coordinate; the value representing the vertical position of a point in a two-dimensional coordinate system.

Examples

The roots of this equation are just the ordinates of the intersections of the graph of b with a straight line of unit slope through **f in the b-plane (the plane of the graph of b).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abscissa - the x-coordinate; the value representing the horizontal position in a coordinate system

Etymology

From Latin 'ordinatus' (ordered), via French 'ordonnée' (ordinate, ordered line).
