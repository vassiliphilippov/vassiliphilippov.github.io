territory

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪtəri / (BrE)
An area of land that is officially part of a country, region, or governed area, or is set apart for a particular administrative, political, or other purpose.

Examples

One was on a fourth down screen pass from the Mustang 21 after an incomplete pass into Gannon's territory.
Resolved that his wrongs and bereavements in Kansas, occasioned by the violence and brutality of those who were intent on the propagation of slavery in that territory, call for a charitable judgment upon his recent efforts in Virginia to undermine the despotism from which he had suffered, and commend his family to the special sympathy and aid of all who pity suffering and reverence justice.
In southern Europe, between the Israeli Republics and the Icelandic speaking peoples of northern Europe, was a thin but long stretch of territory called March.
The revenue from O' Banion's Cicero territory went up still higher, until the yield was more than the Torrio-Capone takings from the far bigger trade area of Chicago's South and West Sides.
This may be because the Athabascan divergence began earlier; or again because the Athabascan languages spread over a very much larger territory (including three wholly separated areas); or both.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - a large area of land, usually without precise boundaries
  • Synonym: district - an area marked out for administrative purposes
  • Degree Higher: state - an organized political community under one government

Etymology

From Latin territorium (land around a town, domain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.əˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
a field or area of knowledge, activity, or interest

Examples

Brooklyn College is Marjorie Morningstar territory, as much as the Bronx or Central Park West.
There was no use wandering off into a territory of utter nightmare.

Synset Examples

his questions covered a lot of territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of knowledge or activity
  • Synonym: field - an area of academic study or professional activity

Etymology

From Latin 'territorium' (land around a town, district) ultimately from 'terra' (earth, land).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈterɪtəri / (BrE)
An area of land under the jurisdiction of a ruler or state, especially a region recognized as belonging to a particular sovereign entity.

Examples

Clemenza promised the Rosato brothers three territories in the Bronx after he died.

Synset Examples

American troops were stationed on Japanese soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - area controlled by a ruler or government
  • Synonym: province - a principal administrative division of certain countries or empires
  • Synonym: colony - a country or area under the full or partial control of another country

Etymology

From Latin 'territorium', meaning land around a town.
