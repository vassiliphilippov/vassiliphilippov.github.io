histology

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɑlədʒi / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of biology that studies the microscopic structure of animal and plant tissues.

Examples

In conclusion, these findings suggest the need for a comparative physiology, pathology, and histology of mammalian lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the scientific study of the structure of living things
  • Synonym: cytology - the branch of biology that studies cells

Etymology

From Greek 'histos' (tissue) + 'logia' (study).
