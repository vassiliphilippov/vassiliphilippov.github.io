reprobate

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛprəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛprəʊbeɪt / (BrE)
A person who behaves in a morally unprincipled or depraved way.

Examples

In defiance, a chinless reprobate, Jake Camaret, marched down the aisle in St. Peter's one Sunday morning, followed by one of the women from the Bordel, whose dress and walk plainly showed the lack of any shame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a person who is dishonest or unscrupulous
  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person
  • Antonym: saint - a person regarded as morally virtuous

Etymology

From Latin reprobatus, past participle of reprobare (to disapprove, reject).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛprəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛprəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
(formal, archaic) To reject or disapprove of something, especially a document or clause, as invalid or unacceptable.

Examples

New Englanders were a bit sensitive on the subject of their complicity in Negro slavery at the time of the drafting of the Declaration of Independence, as Jefferson explained in his "Autobiography ": "The clause reprobating the enslaving the inhabitants of Africa was struck out in complaisance to South Carolina and Georgia, who had never attempted to restrain the importation of slaves, and who on the contrary still wished to continue it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to dismiss something as inadequate or invalid

Etymology

From Latin *reprobatus*, past participle of *reprobare* (to disapprove, reject), from *re-* (again, back) + *probare* (to approve).
