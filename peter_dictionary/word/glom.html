glom

1. VERB

/ ɡlɑm / (AmE), / ɡlɒm / (BrE)
(slang) To steal or take something by theft.

Examples

You then descended one story, glommed a television set from the music room - the only constructive feature of your visit, by the way - and, returning to the ground floor, entered the master bedroom.

Synset Examples

Someone snitched my wallet!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take (something) without permission or legal right and without intending to return it
  • Synonym: snitch - to steal something, especially stealthily or quickly

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang; from Scots 'glam' (to snatch), influenced by Gaelic 'glam' (to grasp, snatch).
