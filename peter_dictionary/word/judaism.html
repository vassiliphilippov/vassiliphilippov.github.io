Judaism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːdiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
The religion, philosophy, and way of life of the Jewish people, based on the teachings of the Torah and the Talmud.

Examples

However, they were hostile to Jewish Orthodoxy, professing to believe in Judaism "but in a moderate way ".
"I want to show respect for my parents' religion "was the way in which a boy justified his inhabiting a halfway house of Judaism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the person and teachings of Jesus Christ
  • Antonym: Islam - the religion founded by Muhammad, based on the Quran

Etymology

From Latin 'Iudaismus', from Greek 'Ioudaïsmos', from Hebrew 'Yehudah' (Judah).
