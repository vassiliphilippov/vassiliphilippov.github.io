coagulate

1. VERB

/ koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊˌleɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to change into a thickened or solid state, especially as a result of a chemical reaction; to clot or cause to clot.

Examples

Meanwhile, three great terrible forces were coagulating and crystallizing.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clot - to form into lumps or clumps, especially of blood
  • Antonym: liquefy - to make or become liquid

Etymology

From Latin *coagulatus*, past participle of *coagulare* (to curdle, to clot).
