linoleum

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnoʊliəm / (AmE), / lɪˈnəʊliəm / (BrE)
A material consisting of a canvas backing thickly coated with a preparation of linseed oil and powdered cork, used as a durable floor covering.

Examples

When Linda Kay had put up her breakfast dishes and mopped her linoleum rugs, she would go to the Big House.

Etymology

From Latin 'linum' (flax, linen) + 'oleum' (oil), referring to the principal ingredients.
