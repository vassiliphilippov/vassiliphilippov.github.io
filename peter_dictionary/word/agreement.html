agreement

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
compatibility or correspondence between facts, results, or statements; the state of being in accord or harmony, especially between different sets of data or observations.

Examples

Mars has been observed twice at about 3 - cm wave length, and the intensity of the observed radiation is in reasonable agreement with the thermal radiation which might be predicted on the basis of the known temperature of Mars.

In spite of all the pitfalls, there is a certain amount of agreement on some of the giants.

Synset Examples

there was no agreement between theory and measurement
the results of two tests were in correspondence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correspondence - a close similarity, connection, or equivalence
  • Antonym: discrepancy - a lack of agreement or similarity between two facts

Etymology

From Old French *agreer* (to please) + *-ment*, or directly from Medieval Latin *agreementum*.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disagreement - lack of harmony in opinions or actions
  • Synonym: consensus - general agreement; collective harmony of opinion
  • Synonym: unanimity - complete agreement by everyone

Etymology

From Old French agrément, from agreer (to agree), from Latin ad- (to) + gratus (pleasing).
