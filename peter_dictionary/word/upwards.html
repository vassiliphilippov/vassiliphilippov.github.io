upwards

1. ADV

/ ˈʌpwərdz / (AmE), / ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
In or toward a higher position or level; from a lower to a higher position, either physically or figuratively.

Examples

Arch the back upwards to make a bridge.
By lifting the seat upwards a little, the weight is taken off the neck and the back is kept rounded.
For internal political reasons, the union asks for (and accepts) increases in the basic wage rate, and would vigorously oppose a reduction in this rate, but the adjustment of the basic wage rate upwards is essentially up to the discretion of the companies of the industry.

Synset Examples

look up!
the music surged up
the fragments flew upwards
prices soared upwards
upwardly mobile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downwards - In or toward a lower position or level
  • Synonym: upward - Toward a higher place or level (alternative form, more often adjectival)

Etymology

From up + -wards, with -wards indicating direction.
