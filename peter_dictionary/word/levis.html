Levi's

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːvaɪz / (AmE), / ˈliːvaɪz / (BrE)
A brand of durable blue jeans and other clothing, originally produced by Levi Strauss & Co.

Examples

After a time, he straightened again, brushing the red Permian dust from his hands, slapping it from his six dollar levis and his tailored, twenty-five dollar shirt.

Etymology

Named after Levi Strauss (1829–1902), founder of the company.
