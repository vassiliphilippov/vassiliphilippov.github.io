acquiescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwɪˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌækwiˈɛsəns / (BrE)
The reluctant acceptance of something without protest.

Examples

He did not, however, settle back into acquiescence with things as they were.
Again, Henley's attitude of defiance which colors his ideal of self mastery is far from characteristic of a Stoic thinker like Marcus Aurelius, whose gentle acquiescence is almost Christian, comparable to the patience expressed in Milton's sonnet on his own blindness.
Thus, for non negative changes in the basic wage rate, the industry becomes the active wage setter, since any increase in the basic wage rate can occur only by reason of industry acquiescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliance - the act of conforming or yielding without protest
  • Antonym: protest - the expression of objection or dissent

Etymology

From Latin acquiescentia, from acquiescere (to rest, to become quiet).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæk.wiˈɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌæk.wiˈɛs.əns / (BrE)
the reluctant or passive acceptance of something without protest

Examples

They must do something with the acquiescence of the latter, or some of them, which amounts to an acceptance of the law in its entirety beyond all possibility of misconstruction ".

Synset Examples

he gave his assent eagerly
a murmur of acquiescence from the assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assent - agreement or approval, especially after consideration
  • Synonym: consent - permission for something to happen or agreement to do something
  • Antonym: dissent - the expression or holding of opinions at variance with those previously held

Etymology

From Latin 'acquiescentia', from 'acquiescere' (to become quiet).
