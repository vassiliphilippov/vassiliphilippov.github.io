mediocrity

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːdiˈɑːkrəti / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiˈɒkrəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being average or not very good; ordinariness due to a lack of outstanding features or ability.

Examples

Like his friend and contemporary August Strindberg he had little patience with collective mediocrity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Antonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in quality or value

Etymology

From Latin mediocritas (moderation, mediocrity), from mediocris (moderate, ordinary).
