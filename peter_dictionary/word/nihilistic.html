nihilistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnaɪəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɪhɪˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of nihilism, especially the rejection of all religious and moral principles, often believing that life is meaningless.

Examples

American newspaper reviewers like to call his plays nihilistic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nihilism - the rejection of all religious and moral principles

Etymology

From nihilism, which derives from Latin 'nihil' meaning 'nothing', plus the suffix -istic.
