heartily

1. ADV

/ ˈhɑːrtəli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtɪli / (BrE)
In an enthusiastic and energetic way, without reservation; with genuine feeling or gusto.

Examples

"Infectious hepatitis ", he shouted heartily.
This brief resume hardly does the book justice, but I heartily recommend it to all those who are engages with the major problems of our time.
From the enlisted men he pistol-whipped to the subordinate officer whose wife he tried to rape, a lot of men had plenty of reason heartily to dislike Marcus Reno.

Synset Examples

the boy threw himself heartily into his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastically - in a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: halfheartedly - without enthusiasm or real effort

Etymology

From 'hearty' + '-ly', 'hearty' from Middle English 'herti', from 'heart'.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɑːrtəli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtɪli / (BrE)
In a hearty, enthusiastic, or energetic way; with sincerity or gusto.

Examples

"Yes, ma'am ", the children chorused heartily.

Synset Examples

`Yes,' the children chorused heartily
We welcomed her warmly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warmly - in a warm or cordial manner
  • Antonym: halfheartedly - in a way that shows little enthusiasm

Etymology

From 'hearty' + '-ly'; 'hearty' from Middle English 'herti', from 'heart'.
