brightly

1. ADV

/ ˈbraɪt.li / (AmE), / ˈbraɪt.li / (BrE)
In a bright way; giving out or reflecting much light.

Examples

Directed by Grigory Kozintsev in a tempo that is studiously slow, he develops a sense of a high tradition shining brightly and passing gravely through an impious world.

Synset Examples

the stars shone brilliantly
the windows glowed jewel bright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliantly - in a very bright or dazzling way
  • Antonym: dimly - in a faint or not brightly lit way
  • Degree Higher: dazzlingly - in an extremely bright or impressive way
  • Degree Lower: faintly - in a weak or barely perceptible way

Etymology

From 'bright' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix.
