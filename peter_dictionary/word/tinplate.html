tinplate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌpleɪt / (BrE)
A thin sheet of iron or steel coated with tin to prevent rusting, commonly used to make cans and containers.

Examples

The old man's as crazy as a rat in a tin shithouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin - a soft, silvery-white metal, chemical element

Etymology

Formed from 'tin' + 'plate', referring to a metal plate coated with tin.
