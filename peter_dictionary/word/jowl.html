jowl

1. NOUN

/ dʒaʊl / (AmE), / dʒaʊl / (BrE)
The lower part of a person's or animal's cheek, especially when it is fleshy or sagging; the jaw, particularly the lower jaw or its side parts.

Examples

"You 're boiling milk, ai n't you "? soothing it with his hand, knowing the whiskered jowls and the swollen smoothness of teats that wrinkled expectantly to his touch.
Squeezing a look between Johnson's fat jowls and the car frame a handsome and still darkhaired lady inquired "Y' all drahve "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaw - the lower movable bone of the face

Etymology

From Middle English *jolle* (jaw, cheek), related to Old English *ceole* (throat, gullet).
