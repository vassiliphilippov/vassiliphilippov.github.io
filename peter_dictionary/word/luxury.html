luxury

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌɡʒəri / (AmE), / ˈlʌkʃəri / (BrE)
A state of great comfort or elegance, often involving great expense or something that is not essential but desirable.

Examples

But for many of us, it will prove an enjoyable luxury.
But can we any longer afford the luxury of such smug indignation?
In one extreme case, cited by a Pittsburgh psychologist, an office worker's wife refused to have sexual relations with her husband unless he bought her the luxuries she demanded.
Save for brief periods in garrison or winter quarters, soldiers rarely enjoyed the luxury of a writing desk or table.
They could no longer afford the luxury of the canvas sweat bag that cooled it by evaporation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: necessity - something that is needed or required

Etymology

From Latin 'luxuria' (excess, extravagance), via Old French 'luxurie'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌkʃəri, ˈlʌɡʒəri / (AmE), / ˈlʌkʃəri, ˈlʌɡzjʊri / (BrE)
a state of great comfort or elegance, especially when involving considerable expense

Examples

He, and Mrs. Dalloway, too, had never permitted themselves the luxury of joys that dug into the bone marrow of the spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opulence - great wealth or luxuriousness
  • Synonym: extravagance - lack of restraint in spending money or using resources
  • Antonym: austerity - plain and simple quality, often associated with lack of luxury

Etymology

From Latin luxuriā (excess, extravagance), from luxus (excess, abundance).
