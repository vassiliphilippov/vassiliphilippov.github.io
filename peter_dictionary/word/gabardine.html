gabardine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæbərˌdin / (AmE), / ˌɡæbəˈdiːn / (BrE)
a firm, tightly woven fabric with a twill weave, typically used for suits, uniforms, and overcoats

Examples

Michelangelo found the rabbi in the room of study, a gentle old man with a white beard and luminous grey eyes, robed in black gabardine with a skullcap on his head.

Etymology

From French 'gabardine', originally denoting a cloak or overcoat, later applied to the fabric.
