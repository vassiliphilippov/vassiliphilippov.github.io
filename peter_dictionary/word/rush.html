rush

1. VERB

/ rʌʃ / (AmE), / rʌʃ / (BrE)
to act or move quickly or with great speed, often because of urgency

Examples

Next to Leo Durocher, Dark taught Mays the most when he was a grass green rookie rushed up to the Polo Grounds 10 years ago this month, to help the Giants win a dramatic pennant.

Synset Examples

We have to rush!
hurry--it's late!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with haste
  • Antonym: dawdle - to waste time or move slowly

Etymology

From Middle English 'rushen', from Old French 'ruser' (to drive back), possibly of Latin origin.

3. NOUN

/ rʌʃ / (AmE), / rʌʃ / (BrE)
A sudden movement or action done quickly and often without care or attention.

Examples

Virtually everyone agrees, however, that the trend toward female sexual aggressiveness was tremendously accelerated with the postwar rush to the suburbs.

Synset Examples

in his haste to leave he forgot his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haste - speed or urgency of movement or action, often with carelessness
  • Antonym: leisure - freedom from rush or haste; unhurried situation

Etymology

From Middle English 'rushen', influenced by Old French 'ruser' (to drive back, dodge).
