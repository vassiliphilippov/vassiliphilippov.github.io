moralistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɔːrəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒrəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing strong or narrow opinions about what is right and wrong, often judging the moral behaviour of others by conventional standards.

Examples

Sometimes he would grunt softly to some invisible onlooker beside him, sometimes he would look stern and moralistic as his pencil did what he disapproved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgmental - having or displaying an excessively critical point of view

Etymology

From 'moral' + '-istic', patterning after 'moralist'.
