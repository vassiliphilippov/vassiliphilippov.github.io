tine

1. NOUN

/ taɪn / (AmE), / taɪn / (BrE)
A prong or slender pointed part on a fork, pitchfork, or antler.

Examples

Curt twisted to one side, and the tines of the fork bit into the floor.
The tines broke off under Jess's twisting, and he swung the handle in an attempt to knock Curt's brains out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prong - a pointed projecting part, as on a fork or antler

Etymology

From Old English 'tīn' (spike, prong), related to Old Norse 'tǐn' (tooth, spike).
