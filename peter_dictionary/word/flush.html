flush

1. VERB

/ flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
(of a person's face) to become red or pink, especially as a result of strong emotion such as embarrassment, shame, or excitement.

Examples

At the landing she saw Juanita, her face flushed pink with excitement, run down the hall from the kitchen to the front door.

Synset Examples

The girl blushed when a young man whistled as she walked by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blush - (of the face) become red, typically from embarrassment

2. ADJ

/ flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
(of two surfaces) exactly even with each other; in the same plane or level.

Examples

The wooden bracing between the roof beams is placed flush with the inside of the wall.

Synset Examples

a door flush with the wall
the bottom of the window is flush with the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level - having a flat, even surface; not sloping
  • Antonym: uneven - not level or smooth; not flush

3. ADV

/ flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
directly and squarely, especially so as to hit full-on or head-on.

Examples

Curt caught him flush on the nose with a blow which started at the floor.

Synset Examples

hit him flush in the face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squarely - directly, so as to hit fully or straight on
  • Synonym: directly - without deviation or change in direction

4. NOUN

/ flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
A period when something is at its peak, especially of prosperity, productivity, or vigor.

Examples

To have the Greek paper is not the great help that at first flush it seemed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heyday - the period of a person's or thing's greatest success, popularity, or vigor

5. NOUN

/ flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
A rosy or healthy color, especially in the cheeks, often signifying good health or excitement.

Examples

Now a little flush came on her pale homely face and enchantment in her eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blush - a reddening of the face, especially from embarrassment

6. VERB

/ flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow or be driven out in a sudden or forceful stream, especially as part of a cleaning process.

Examples

With the aid of that I could hear my man moving around, heard him cough a couple of times, heard the toilet flush, heard the sound of water running.

Synset Examples

The garbage flushed down the river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - to remove liquid from something by causing it to flow away

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *flusshen*, from Old French *flux* (a flow), from Latin *fluxus* (a flowing).
