middleman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌmæn / (BrE)
a person or business that buys goods in large quantities and sells them to retailers or other merchants rather than directly to consumers

Examples

Should I insist that all of Tattaglia's drug middlemen have clean records?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermediary - a person who acts as a link between people or groups
  • Antonym: retailer - a person or business selling goods directly to consumers
  • Synonym: wholesaler - a person or company selling goods in large quantities, usually to retailers

Etymology

From 'middle' + 'man', originally indicating someone acting in the middle between two parties.
