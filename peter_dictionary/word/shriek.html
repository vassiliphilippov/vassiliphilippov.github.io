shriek

1. NOUN

/ ʃrik / (AmE), / ʃriːk / (BrE)
A loud, high-pitched, and piercing cry, often expressing fear, pain, excitement, or laughter.

Examples

Miss Stuart is as intensely vibrant as one could wish, almost an icy shriek threatening to explode at any moment.
[CHAMPAGNE CORK POPPING] [PEOPLE LAUGHING] [SHRIEKS] [PEOPLE CHEERING] [MUSIC PLAYING] [SOFTLY]

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - a loud, piercing cry, typically of pain, fear, or excitement
  • Degree Lower: yell - a loud, sharp cry, especially of pain, surprise, or delight

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, dating from late Middle English.

2. VERB

/ ʃrik / (AmE), / ʃriːk / (BrE)
to utter a loud, high-pitched, and often sharp cry, usually in fear, excitement, or pain

Examples

Mrs. Shaefer practically shrieked.
"Mor-ee-air-teeeee ", he shrieked, his white teeth grossly counterpointing those of the glittering blonde.
Brakes shrieked behind us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - to make a loud, piercing cry, typically in fear or pain
  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly, especially to express pain, anger, or excitement
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, using one's breath rather than one's throat

Etymology

From Middle English shreken, from Old Norse skrækja (to shriek, screech).
