suspended

1. ADJ

/ səˈspɛndɪd / (AmE), / səˈspɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of small particles in a fluid) kept floating or dispersed throughout the fluid without settling, typically by buoyancy or movement.

Examples

There is another means which should show the direction and relative value of the stresses in viscoelastic fluids that is not mentioned as such in the literature, and that is the shape of the suspended drops of low viscosity fluids in shear fields.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solution - a homogeneous mixture where substances are dissolved
  • Antonym: precipitated - (of particles) settled out from a fluid

Etymology

From Latin 'suspendere' (to hang up).
