provisional

1. ADJ

/ prəˈvɪʒənəl / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənəl / (BrE)
arranged or existing for the present, possibly to be changed later; not final or definite

Examples

"I 'll do as you say, but I 'll also play a provisional ball and get a ruling ".
He scored a 4 for the embedded ball, a 3 with the provisional one.
So he picked up a stroke with the provisional ball and won the tournament by the margin of that stroke.

Synset Examples

probationary employees
a provisional government
just a tentative schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting or intended to last for only a short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin provisio (provision) via French provisionnel.
