mantle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tl̩ / (BrE)
a symbol of authority or responsibility, especially as passed from one person to another

Examples

Inheriting a more mature Mantle, who now has seen the sights on and off Broadway, Ralph Houk quietly bestowed, no pun intended, the mantle of authority on Mickey.

Synset Examples

place the mantle of authority on younger shoulders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak - something that covers or conceals, sometimes symbolic of office

Etymology

From Latin *mantellum* (cloak), referring metaphorically to a cloak representing office or authority.
