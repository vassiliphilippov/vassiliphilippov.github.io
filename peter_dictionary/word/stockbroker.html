stockbroker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌbrəʊkə / (BrE)
A person or firm that buys and sells stocks and other securities on behalf of clients.

Examples

One is an imperial London stockbroker called Jerebohm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broker - an agent who negotiates contracts of purchase and sale

Etymology

From 'stock' (referring to stocks and shares) + 'broker' (agent or intermediary).
