sept

1. NOUN

/ sɛpt / (AmE), / sɛpt / (BrE)
A division of a family or clan, especially in Ireland or Scotland, consisting of people descended from a common ancestor.

Examples

In an attempt to reverse the downhill trend by stimulating the bone marrow and controlling any hemolytic component, triamcinolone, 16 mg. daily, was begun on Sept. 26, 1958, and continued until Feb. 18, 1959.

Synset Examples

his family has lived in Massachusetts since the Mayflower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of families with a common ancestor, especially in Scottish or Irish contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'septum' (enclosure), referring to an enclosed group or clan.
