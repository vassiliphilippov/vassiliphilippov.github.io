intone

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtoʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtəʊn / (BrE)
To recite words with a musical or chanting intonation, often in a slow, solemn, or formal manner.

Examples

"The past I leave to historians ", Felix intoned, demonstrating that he could be pompous as well as happy.

Synset Examples

The rabbi chanted a prayer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chant - to sing or recite with a rhythmic, repetitive intonation

Etymology

From Latin 'intonare', meaning 'to thunder', from 'in-' (in) + 'tonare' (to thunder).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtoʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtəʊn / (BrE)
to say or recite (something) in a slow, even, and serious voice, often with little variation in pitch or tone

Examples

Finally, Orville intoned through his hawk nose, "We can n't have people running in any time they please, Sergeant ".

Synset Examples

The students chanted the same slogan over and over again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chant - to say or sing something repeatedly in a rhythmic way
  • Synonym: recite - to repeat aloud from memory, or read aloud publicly

Etymology

From Latin 'intonare' (to thunder, to sing).
