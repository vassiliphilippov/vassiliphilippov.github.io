concoct

1. VERB

/ kənˈkɑkt / (AmE), / kənˈkɒkt / (BrE)
to make a mixture or dish by combining various ingredients, especially in a creative or imaginative way

Examples

At juice bars in Los Angeles' 35 "health "stores, a new sensation is a pink, high-protein cocktail, concocted of dried eggs, powdered milk and cherry-flavored No-Cal, which sells for 59 per 8 - oz. glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine two or more substances or things together

Etymology

From Latin concoquere (to cook together, digest), from com- (together) + coquere (to cook).
