catechize

1. VERB

/ ˈkæt.ɪˌkaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkat.ɪ.kʌɪz / (BrE)
to question someone systematically, especially to instruct them or to test their knowledge, often in a religious context

Examples

"Very well ", she said, "I 'll not catechize you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogate - to ask questions formally and systematically, especially by authorities
  • Synonym: quiz - to question someone in order to test their knowledge

Etymology

From Late Latin catechizare, from Greek katēkhein (to instruct orally).
