scenery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsinəri / (AmE), / ˈsiːnəri / (BrE)
the painted structures or backgrounds used on a stage to create the setting for a play or performance

Examples

The book is by Jerome Weidman and George Abbott, music by Jerry Bock, lyrics by Sheldon Harnick, choreography by Peter Gennaro, scenery, costumes and lighting by William and Jean Eckart, musical direction by Jack Elliott, and the production was directed by Mr. Abbott.
(Several times recently I have wondered whether shows were being staged for the sake of the script or just to entertain the audience with the spectacle of scenery being shifted right in front of their eyes.
Meanwhile it looked at the scenery.

Synset Examples

they worked all night painting the scenery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set - the physical surroundings or environment of a play or film, including scenery and props
  • Synonym: backdrop - a painted cloth hung at the back of a theatre stage as part of the scenery

Etymology

From Italian *scenaria* (stage decorations), from Latin *scaena* (scene, stage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnəri / (AmE), / ˈsiːnəri / (BrE)
the natural features of a landscape or place, especially when seen as attractive or picturesque

Examples

One of the most desirable features for a park are beautiful views or scenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape - the visible features of an area of land, often considered in terms of aesthetic appeal

Etymology

From Italian *sceneria*, ultimately from Latin *scaena* (stage), also source of English 'scene'.
