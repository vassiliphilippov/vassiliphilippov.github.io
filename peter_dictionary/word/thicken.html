thicken

1. VERB

/ ˈθɪkən / (AmE), / ˈθɪkən / (BrE)
to make or become more thick or dense

Examples

The intima of the larger coronary arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue containing fusiform clefts and mononuclear cells.
Macrophages laden with brown pigment were seen in some of the alveoli, and the intima of some of the small arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue.

Synset Examples

Thicken the sauce
inspissate the tar so that it becomes pitch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thin - to make or become less thick or dense
  • Antonym: dilute - to make a liquid less concentrated or thick

Etymology

Formed from 'thick' + '-en' (a suffix forming verbs).

2. VERB

/ ˈθɪkən / (AmE), / ˈθɪkən / (BrE)
to become thick or thicker, or to make something become thick or thicker

Examples

Watching, they waited until the squall thickened and began to move in a long drifting slant across the dry burning land.
With this he felt his lips thicken and his mind go slack.

Synset Examples

The sauce thickened
The egg yolk will inspissate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thin - to become thin or thinner

Etymology

From Middle English thiknen, from Old English thiccian, from thicce (thick).
