pant

1. VERB

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
to breathe quickly and noisily with your mouth open, especially after exertion or in response to excitement or fear

Examples

It sometimes ended in death-like trances with many lying exhausted and panting on chair and floor.
Panting a little, Payne shook his head.
We ran east for about half a mile before we turned back to the road, panting from the effort and soaked with sweat.
Watson pounded to the crawling man and stopped, panting heavily.
He stood, panting, for a moment.

Synset Examples

The runners reached the finish line, panting heavily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - inhale suddenly with mouth open, as in surprise or shortness of breath
  • Synonym: wheeze - breathe with a whistling or rattling sound in the chest

Etymology

From Old French 'pantaisier' (to be out of breath, gasp), probably of imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
to say something while breathing quickly and heavily, usually from exertion or excitement

Examples

"No telling how good this horse is ", Mike panted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - to say something while catching one's breath
