ultimatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌl.təˈmeɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌʌl.tɪˈmeɪ.təm / (BrE)
a final demand or statement of terms, the rejection of which will result in retaliation or a breakdown in relations

Examples

In Poughkeepsie, N. Y., in 1952, a Roman Catholic hospital presented seven Protestant physicians with an ultimatum to quit the Planned Parenthood Federation or to resign from the hospital staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - an insistent request

Etymology

From Latin 'ultimatum', meaning 'a final statement', from 'ultimare' ('to come to an end') and 'ultimus' ('farthest, last').
