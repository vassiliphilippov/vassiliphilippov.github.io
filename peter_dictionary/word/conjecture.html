conjecture

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃə / (BrE)
An opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information; a hypothesis made without firm evidence.

Examples

Our conjecture is, then, that regardless of the manner in which school lessons are taught, the compulsive child accentuates those elements of each lesson that aid him in systematizing his work.

Synset Examples

speculations about the outcome of the election
he dismissed it as mere conjecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Antonym: fact - something that is known or proved to be true
  • Degree Higher: theory - a supposition or a system of ideas intended to explain something
  • Degree Lower: guess - an estimate or conclusion based on little or no information

Etymology

From Latin 'coniectura', from 'conicere' (to throw together, guess).

2. VERB

/ kənˈdʒɛktʃər / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛktʃə / (BrE)
to form an opinion or conclusion on the basis of incomplete information; to guess or suppose.

Examples

From the evidence "it may be conjectured that core-core marriages are the preferred unions for core males and females; core-marginal marriages still belong in the category of permissive unions; and core-Negro marriages are proscribed for core members ".

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculate - to form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Synonym: surmise - to suppose something is true without having evidence
  • Antonym: know - to have certainty or definite evidence about something

Etymology

From Latin conjectura (guess, inference), from conicere (to throw together, infer).
