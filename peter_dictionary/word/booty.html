booty

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːti / (AmE), / ˈbuːti / (BrE)
goods or money taken illegally, especially by force during war or by thieves

Examples

In a similar vein, but writing from the opposite side, Thomas Taylor, a private in the 6 th Alabama Volunteers, in a letter to his wife, stated: "You know that my heart is with you but I never could have been satisfied to have staid at home when my country is invaded by a thievin foe By a set of cowardly Skunks whose Motto is Booty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - goods taken by force, especially in war or robbery
  • Synonym: spoils - goods or benefits taken from an enemy or as the result of success
  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force in a time of war or civil disorder

Etymology

From Middle Low German *būte* (exchange, distribution, share), related to taking spoils in battle.
