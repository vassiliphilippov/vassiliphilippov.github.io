exaggeration

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzadʒəˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A statement that makes something seem better, larger, worse, or more important than it really is; extravagant overstatement.

Examples

As a consequence of the tensions thus produced in his thoughts and feelings, he wrote on the one hand sketches of idealized hunting trips and on the other an anecdote of the village of Hardscrabble, Arkansas, where no one had ever seen a piano; and he wrote also the masterpiece of frontier humor, "The Big Bear of Arkansas ", in which earthy realism is placed alongside the exaggeration of the backwoods tall-tale and the awe with which man contemplates the grandeur and the mysteries of nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperbole - Extravagant or exaggerated statements not meant to be taken literally
  • Antonym: understatement - The presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is

Etymology

From Latin exaggeratio (excess, amplification), from exaggerare (to heap up, exaggerate).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzadʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making something seem more important or impressive than it really is.

Examples

Don'’t make a big thing about it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstatement - an exaggerated statement; making something seem greater than it is
  • Antonym: understatement - making something appear less important or smaller than it really is

Etymology

From Latin exaggeratio (an exaggerating), from exaggerare ‘to heap up, amplify’.
