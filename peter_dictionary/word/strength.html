strength

1. NOUN

/ strɛŋθ / (AmE), / strɛŋθ / (BrE)
A particular skill, quality, or ability in which someone excels; a valuable or useful asset.

Examples

But strength and poignancy develop thenceforth, and the windmill and deathbed episodes gather the threads of realization of the wonderfulness of the old boy.
- This budget will provide in the fiscal year 1961 for the continued support of our forces at approximately the present level - a year-end strength of 2489000 men and women in the active forces.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forte - area in which a person excels
  • Antonym: weakness - a quality or feature regarded as a disadvantage or fault

Etymology

From Old English 'strangþu' (power, force, bodily strength), related to 'strong'.
