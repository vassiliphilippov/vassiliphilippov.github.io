shaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving or causing something to move quickly up and down or back and forth

Examples

The contents of the manifold for liquid phase experiments were then mixed by shaking, redistributed to the reaction tubes, frozen down, and each tube was then sealed off.
His legs shook, and the shaking went right on up his body through his hips to his shoulders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibration - rapid, repeated back-and-forth motion

Etymology

Present participle of the verb "shake," from Old English *sceacan* (to shake, move quickly).
