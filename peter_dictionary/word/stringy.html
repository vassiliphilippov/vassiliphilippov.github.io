stringy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
(of food, hair, or muscle) containing tough, thin pieces that are difficult to chew or separate; lean and sinewy.

Examples

There had been some coconut in it, for I remember my mother's taking a quick glance at a stringy bit of this nut on the cheek of one of them and then putting down her radish with a shiver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrous - having or consisting of fibers
  • Synonym: sinewy - lean and muscular, with visible tendons or sinews

Etymology

From 'string' + '-y', referring to resemblance to strings or fibers.
