bitch

1. NOUN

/ bɪtʃ / (AmE), / bɪtʃ / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person, especially a woman, who is greatly disliked or regarded as mean, spiteful, or unpleasant.

Examples

He'’s backing up those son of a bitches.

Synset Examples

she said her son thought Hillary was a bitch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastard - offensive term for an unpleasant or despicable person (especially a man)
  • Synonym: jerk - informal term for an annoying or contemptible person

Etymology

Old English *bicce* (female dog), later extended to an insult for a woman.

2. NOUN

/ bɪtʃ / (AmE), / bɪtʃ / (BrE)
An informal complaint or persistent objection, especially a trivial or recurrent one.

Examples

Bitch! - Dinner's on the table.

Synset Examples

I have a gripe about the service here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gripe - an informal complaint or persistent grumble
  • Synonym: moan - an informal complaint or grievance, especially persistent
