eldest

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛldɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛldɪst / (BrE)
(of people, especially among siblings) being the oldest in age, or first in order of birth.

Examples

Henrietta could easily identify herself with Jo March, although Jo was not the eldest sister.
Bertha Szold was more like Meg, the eldest March girl, who "learned that a woman's happiest kingdom is home, her highest honor the art of ruling it, not as a queen, but a wise wife and mother ".

Synset Examples

the firstborn child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: youngest - the sibling or member who is the most recently born, the opposite of 'eldest'
  • Synonym: oldest - the most advanced in age; synonym for 'eldest' when referring to people

Etymology

Old superlative form of 'old', from Old English 'yldest'.
