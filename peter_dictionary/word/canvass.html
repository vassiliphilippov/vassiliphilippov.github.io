canvass

1. VERB

/ ˈkæn.vəs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs / (BrE)
to ask people for their opinions, votes, or support, especially in order to find out how they will vote in an election or to gather public opinion

Examples

It was the day to canvass for infectious hepatitis.
The jury, which was locked up in a motel overnight, was canvassed at the request of Walker after the verdict was announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - to ask a group of people questions to gather information
  • Synonym: poll - to question people to find out their opinions or how they intend to vote

Etymology

From Old French 'canevas' (canvas), from Latin 'cannapaceus', originally referring to examining voting lists written on canvas.
