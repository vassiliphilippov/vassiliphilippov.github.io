univalent

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːˈnɪvələnt / (AmE), / ˌjuːˈnɪvələnt / (BrE)
(of a chromosome) not paired or united with its homologous chromosome during synapsis.

Examples

One type has a small univalent anion of the thiocyanate perchlorate fluoroboride type.

Synset Examples

a univalent chromosome

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'valentia' (strength, capacity).
