arm

1. VERB

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
to provide oneself or others with weapons or equipment for conflict or confrontation

Examples

Ordinary Carey Williams, armed with a pistol, stood by at the polls to insure order.
"Arm your guns, Sweeneys ".
Armed with the Nixon opinion, the Senate liberals rounded up their slim majority and prepared to choke off debate on the filibuster battle this week.
The public is now armed with sophistication and numerous competing media.

Synset Examples

The U.S. is girding for a conflict in the Middle East
troops are building up on the Iraqi border

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disarm - to deprive of weapons or the means to attack

Etymology

From Latin arma (arms, weapons).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
The upper limb of the human body, extending from the shoulder to the hand.

Examples

Chico Ruiz made a spectacular play on Alusik's grounder in the hole in the fourth and Wert came up with some good stops and showed a strong arm at third base.
In the third Push-Pull Super-Set the "push "exercise is the wide grip Pushup Between Bars, while the "pull "exercise is the Moon Bench Lateral Raise with bent arms.
They often fail, however, to develop real abdominal, back, chest, shoulder and arm strength.
For arm and shoulder strength a chinning bar is recommended.
Arm, shoulder, chest, upper and lower back strength will be aided with the Horse Kick.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leg - lower human limb
  • Degree Lower: forearm - part of the arm between the elbow and wrist
  • Degree Higher: hand - terminal part of the arm beyond the wrist

Etymology

From Old English 'earm', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'arm' and German 'Arm', from Latin 'armus' (shoulder).

3. NOUN

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
A part or projection of an object, area, or structure that extends out from the main part and resembles a human arm in shape or function.

Examples

The reactants for the gas phase experiments were first frozen out in a side arm attached to the manifold and then allowed to distill slowly into the manifold of pre-cooled reaction cells before sealing off.

Synset Examples

the arm of the record player
an arm of the sea
a branch of the sewer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a subdivision or offshoot of a main part; used similarly for structures

4. NOUN

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
the part of a chair or sofa that extends out from the side to support the sitter's elbow and forearm

Examples

She grasped the chair arms and brought her thin body upright, like a bird alert for flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armrest - a support for the arm on the side of a chair

5. NOUN

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
A weapon, particularly a firearm, used in fighting or hunting.

Examples

The American firearms and ammunition manufacturers through diligent research and technical development have replaced the muzzle loader and slow firing single shot arms with modern fast firing autoloaders, extremely accurate bolt, lever, and slide action firearms.

Synset Examples

he was licensed to carry a weapon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weapon - an instrument used in combat or to inflict harm
  • Synonym: firearm - a portable gun, such as a rifle or pistol

Etymology

From Latin 'arma' (weapons).

6. VERB

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
to supply or equip with weapons or firearms

Examples

The missiles were to be armed with an underwater bomb.

Synset Examples

The U.S. armed the freedom fighters in Afghanistan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disarm - to take weapons away from someone

Etymology

From Latin 'arma' (arms, weapons) via Old French 'armer' (to arm, furnish with weapons).
