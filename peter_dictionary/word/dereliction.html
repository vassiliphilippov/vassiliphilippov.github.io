dereliction

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛrəˈlɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrɪˈlɪkʃən / (BrE)
The deliberate failure to do what one should do, especially a duty or responsibility; willful negligence.

Examples

Nobody in his right mind punishes a quarter-century old dereliction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care over something

Etymology

From Latin derelictio (abandonment), from derelinquere (to abandon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛrəˈlɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrəˈlɪkʃən / (BrE)
A state or act of being negligent and failing to fulfill one’s duties or responsibilities.

Examples

"I should n't like to have to write you up for insubordination as well as dereliction of duty ".

Synset Examples

he inherited his delinquency from his father
his derelictions were not really intended as crimes
his adolescent protest consisted of willful neglect of all his responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - failure to care for something properly

Etymology

From Latin derelictionem (abandonment), from derelinquere (to abandon).
