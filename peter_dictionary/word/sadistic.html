sadistic

1. ADJ

/ səˈdɪstɪk / (AmE), / səˈdɪstɪk / (BrE)
Gaining pleasure, especially sexual pleasure, from inflicting pain, suffering, or humiliation on others.

Examples

Cause of all this commotion: squat, pug-nosed, balding, hopelessly ugly Jean-Pierre Bravado, a Bogartian figure, who plays a sadistic, amoral, philosophic Tasti-Freeze salesman in old New-Waver Fredrico de Mille Rossilini's endlessly provocative film, A Sour Sponge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - willingly causing pain or suffering to others
  • Antonym: masochistic - deriving pleasure from one's own pain or humiliation
  • Antonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness toward others instead of causing harm

Etymology

From German Sadismus, after the Marquis de Sade (1740–1814), whose works depicted such behavior.
