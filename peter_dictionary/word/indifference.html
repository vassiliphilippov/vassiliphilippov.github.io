indifference

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪfrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfrəns / (BrE)
Lack of interest, concern, or sympathy towards something; the state of being impartial or unconcerned.

Examples

However, even if the latent demand for demythologization is not nearly as widespread as we are claiming, at least among the cultured elements of the population there tends to be an almost complete indifference to the church and its traditional message of sin and grace.
Andrei remembered that his own reaction had been one of indifference.
Landesco thought him "just a superior sort of plugugly "but he was, in fact, with his aggression and hostility, and nerveless indifference to risking or administering pain, a casebook psychopath.
Then, with a shrug of pretended indifference, she took a compact from her purse and went through the motions of fixing her make-up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of feeling or interest
  • Antonym: concern - worry or interest
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense interest or eagerness

Etymology

From Latin 'indifferentia', from 'in-' (not) + 'differentia' (difference).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪfɚəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfrəns / (BrE)
lack of interest, concern, or sympathy; the state of showing no particular emotion or reaction to something

Examples

Scotty accepted the decision with indifference and did not enter the arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: emotion - a strong feeling such as love, anger, joy, hate, or fear

Etymology

From Latin *indifferentia* (lack of difference, impartiality), from *in-* (not) + *differentia* (difference).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪfərəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪf(ə)r(ə)ns / (BrE)
lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern about something

Examples

The image of the world tends to reflect the hostility and indifference of man or else to dissolve into empty spaces and overwhelming mystery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of feeling, emotion, or interest
  • Antonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or learn about something
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval

Etymology

From Latin *indifferentia* (lack of difference or preference).
