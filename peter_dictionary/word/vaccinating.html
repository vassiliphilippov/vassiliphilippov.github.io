vaccinating

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæksəˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvæksɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of administering a vaccine to protect against disease by stimulating the body's immune response.

Examples

A tranquilizer fed to cattle (other than lactating dairy cows) prior to their being subjected to stress conditions such as vaccinating, shipping, weaning calves, and excessive handling.

Synset Examples

doctors examined the recruits but nurses did the inoculating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inoculating - the act of introducing a vaccine or antigenic substance to induce immunity

Etymology

From 'vaccinate', which derives from Latin 'vacca' (cow), referencing the use of cowpox virus in early vaccines.
