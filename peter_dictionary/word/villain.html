villain

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlən / (AmE), / ˈvɪlən / (BrE)
A person who is deliberately wicked or evil, often the main antagonist in stories, plays, or movies.

Examples

The one sure way to tell victim and villain apart is to examine the hind legs which in the case of the idler, Psithyrus, lack the pollen baskets - naturally!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities; typically the protagonist
  • Synonym: antagonist - a character who actively opposes the protagonist
  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime

Etymology

From Old French *vilain* (peasant, churl), from Late Latin *villanus* (farmhand), originally referring to someone of low social status.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlən / (AmE), / ˈvɪlən / (BrE)
the main bad character in a film, play, or story, especially one who opposes the hero

Examples

They know from their researches into television and the movies that knights in the middle ages had beautiful flowing haircuts like Little Lord Fauntleroy, and only the villains had beards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hero - the main good character in a story, often opposing the villain
  • Synonym: antagonist - a character who opposes the main character or hero

Etymology

From Old French 'vilain', meaning 'farmhand' or 'villager', later developing the sense of 'base' or 'wicked person'.
