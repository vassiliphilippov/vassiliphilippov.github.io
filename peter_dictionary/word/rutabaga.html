rutabaga

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːtəˈbeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌruːtəˈbɑːɡə / (BrE)
A large, round root vegetable with yellowish flesh, commonly used as food; also called swede in British English.

Examples

The most conclusively identified is L - 5 - vinyl - 2 - thio oxazolidone, which was isolated from rutabaga (Greer, 1950).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swede - another name for rutabaga, mainly in British English

Etymology

From Swedish 'rotabagge' (root bag).
