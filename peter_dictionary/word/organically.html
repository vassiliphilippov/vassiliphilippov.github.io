organically

1. ADV

/ ɔrˈɡænɪkli / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that involves carbon compounds; relating to or derived from organic chemistry processes.

Examples

However, the formation of organically bound iodine, mainly mono-iodotyrosine, can be accomplished in cell-free systems.

Synset Examples

organically bound iodine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inorganically - in a way not involving carbon compounds (chemistry)

Etymology

From 'organic' + adverbial suffix '-ally'.

2. ADV

/ ɔrˈɡænɪkli / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that forms an essential or natural part of something; as an integral constituent.

Examples

No fold could be accidental, each turn of the drapery had to serve organically, to cover the Madonna's slender legs and feet so that they would give substantive support to Christ's body, to intensify her inner turmoil.

Synset Examples

the drapery served organically to cover the Madonna

Etymology

From 'organic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek organikos, relating to an organ or instrument.
