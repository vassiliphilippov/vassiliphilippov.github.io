orifice

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ə.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒ.rɪ.fɪs / (BrE)
An opening, hole, or passage, especially one of the body or of an organ.

Examples

The intimal surface of the aorta was covered with confluent, yellow-brown, hard, friable plaques along its entire course, and there was a marked narrowing of the orifices of the large major visceral arteries.
In particular, the orifices of the right renal and celiac arteries were virtually occluded, and both calcified common iliac arteries were completely occluded.

Synset Examples

the orifice into the aorta from the lower left chamber of the heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aperture - an opening, hole, or gap
  • Synonym: opening - an open space that allows passage or access
  • Antonym: closure - the act of closing or the state of being closed

Etymology

From French *orifice*, from Latin *orificium* (opening), from *os* (mouth) + *facere* (to make).
