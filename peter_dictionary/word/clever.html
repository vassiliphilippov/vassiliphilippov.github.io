clever

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɛvə / (BrE)
Showing quick intelligence and resourcefulness, often in a shrewd or self-interested way.

Examples

Such fascinating novelties in the score as the fugual treatment of "On the Side of the Angels "and "Politics and Poker "were handled splendidly, and I thought Rudy Bond and his band of tuneful ward-heelers made "Little Tin Box "even better than it was done by the New York cast; all the words of its clever lyrics came through with perfect clarity.
She had the opportunity that few clever women can resist, of showing her superiority in argument over a man.

Synset Examples

a cagey lawyer
too clever to be sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skilled at achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion
  • Synonym: crafty - clever at achieving one's aims by indirect or deceitful methods
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgement; easily fooled

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English cliver (dexterous), of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɛvər / (AmE), / ˈklɛvə / (BrE)
Quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas; mentally resourceful or intelligent.

Examples

What about that dear, clever Mr. Thynne?
I'm not that clever.

Synset Examples

an apt pupil
you are a clever man...you reason well and your wit is bold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence; clever, quick-witted
  • Synonym: resourceful - having the ability to find quick and clever ways to overcome difficulties
  • Antonym: dull - slow to understand or lacking in intelligence

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *clevere* (nimble), of uncertain origin.
