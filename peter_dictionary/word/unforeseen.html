unforeseen

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.fɔːrˈsin / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fɔːˈsiːn / (BrE)
Not expected or predicted; occurring without warning or prior indication.

Examples

In an effort to fortify himself against the unforeseen upsets sure to arise in the future, Herbert A. Leggett, banker editor of the Phoenix "Arizona Progress ", reflects upon a few of the depressing experiences of the feverish fifties.

Synset Examples

unanticipated and disconcerting lines of development
unforeseen circumstances
a virtue unlooked-for in people so full of energy
like a bolt out of the blue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expected - regarded as likely to happen; anticipated
  • Antonym: anticipated - regarded as probable; expected in advance

Etymology

From un- (not) + foreseen, past participle of foresee (to see or know about before it happens).
