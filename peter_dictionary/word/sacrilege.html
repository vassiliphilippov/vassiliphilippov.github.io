sacrilege

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækrɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsakrɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
The act of violating or showing disrespect toward something considered sacred; blasphemous or profane behavior.

Examples

If such a paragon of perfection as Palmer could commit such a scoring sacrilege, there was hope left for all.
The very proposition was sacrilege.

Synset Examples

desecration of the Holy Sabbath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desecration - the act of treating something sacred with violent disrespect
  • Synonym: blasphemy - speech or action showing disrespect for something sacred

Etymology

From Latin sacrilegium (the crime of stealing sacred things), from sacer (sacred) + legere (to steal, take).
