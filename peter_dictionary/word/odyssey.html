odyssey

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑdəsi / (AmE), / ˈɒdəsi / (BrE)
A long, adventurous and often eventful journey, especially one filled with notable experiences or hardships.

Examples

The wraith-like events of the last few days flooded David's mind and he rubbed his unshaved chin and felt again the ache in his kidneys caused by his saddle odyssey from Boston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a specific purpose

Etymology

From the Greek Odysseia, the epic poem attributed to Homer narrating the adventures of Odysseus.
