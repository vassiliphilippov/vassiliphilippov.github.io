untoward

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɔːd / (BrE)
Not in keeping with accepted standards of proper or polite behaviour; improper or inappropriate.

Examples

Gun set the captain's fifth of Hiram Walker inside the safe before he reported to Lt. Killpath, though he knew that Killpath's ulcer prevented him from making any untoward incursion on Herman Wolff's gift.

Synset Examples

was buried with indecent haste
indecorous behavior
language unbecoming to a lady
unseemly to use profanity
moved to curb their untoward ribaldry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecent - not conforming to accepted standards of morality or decency
  • Synonym: improper - not conforming to accepted rules or standards, especially of morality or honesty
  • Antonym: improper - not proper; not suitable or appropriate
  • Antonym: decorous - in keeping with good taste and propriety; polite and restrained

Etymology

From Middle English toward (hostile), influenced by the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'toward'.
