light-mindedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪtˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being frivolous, lacking seriousness, or treating important matters with inappropriate levity.

Examples

Not through fear of disobeying orders, as Eichmann kept trying to explain, but through a peculiar giddiness that began in a half acceptance of the vicious absurdities contained in the Nazi interpretation of history and grew with each of Hitler's victories into a permanent light mindedness and sense of magical rightness that was able to respond to any proposal, and the more outrageous the better, "Well, let's try it ".

Synset Examples

her mood changed and she was all lightness and joy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levity - lightness of manner or speech, especially when inappropriate
  • Synonym: frivolity - lack of seriousness; lightheartedness
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious, in contrast to frivolity

Etymology

Formed from 'light-minded' + '-ness', where 'light-minded' dates to the 17th century, meaning frivolous or lacking seriousness.
