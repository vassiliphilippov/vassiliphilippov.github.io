noticeably

1. ADV

/ ˈnoʊ.tɪ.sə.bli / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.tɪ.sə.bli / (BrE)
In a way that is easy to notice; in a manner that attracts attention or is perceptible.

Examples

Measurements of skins are of little value; every snake hide is noticeably longer than its carcass and intentional stretching presents no difficulty to the unscrupulous explorer.

Synset Examples

he changed noticeably over the years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptibly - in a perceptible manner
  • Antonym: invisibly - in a way that cannot be seen or noticed
  • Antonym: unnoticeably - in an unnoticeable manner

Etymology

From 'noticeable' + '-ly', formed from 'notice' (from Latin 'notitia', meaning 'being known') plus suffix '-able' and adverb-forming '-ly'.
