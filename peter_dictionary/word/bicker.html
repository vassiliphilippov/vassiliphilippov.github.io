bicker

1. VERB

/ ˈbɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪkə / (BrE)
to argue or quarrel about trivial matters

Examples

merchants from the bazaar bickering over a deal; a Bakhtiari khan in a cap and hacking jacket; dervishes who stand with the stillness of the blind, their eyes filmed with rheum and visions; the old Kajar princes arriving in their ancient limousines; students, civil servants, beggars, musicians, hawkers, and clowns.

Synset Examples

Let's not quibble over pennies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - to have an angry argument or disagreement
  • Degree Lower: disagree - to have a different opinion; not agree
  • Degree Higher: fight - to engage in a physical or intense verbal conflict

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'bicken' (to attack, to quarrel).
