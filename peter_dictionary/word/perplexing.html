perplexing

1. ADJ

/ pərˈplɛksɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˈplɛksɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing confusion, difficulty, or uncertainty; hard to understand or solve.

Examples

Cattle stealing and killing, again serious during the spring of 1891, placed the land grant company officers in a perplexing position.
The perplexing question still remains as to why the middle classes turn to the churches as a vehicle of social identity when their clubs and charities should fill the same need.

Synset Examples

sent confusing signals to Iraq
perplexing to someone who knew nothing about it
a puzzling statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusing - difficult to understand; causing bewilderment
  • Synonym: puzzling - causing confusion or difficulty; hard to understand
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; free from confusion

Etymology

From the verb 'perplex', from Latin 'perplexus' (entangled, confused).
