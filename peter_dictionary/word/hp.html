HP

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˈpiː / (BrE)
a unit of power equal to 746 watts, commonly used to measure the power output of engines and motors

Examples

Their study showed that it was possible to transfer 3 to 4 lb of oxygen / hr / hp.
The pilot plant was equipped with a 3 - hp turbine aerator (Figure 2).
The actual power requirements indicated 2 lb oxygen transfer / hr / hp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsepower - a unit of power equal to 746 watts

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'horsepower', coined in the late 18th century by engineer James Watt.
