pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
A word used instead of a noun or noun phrase to avoid repetition or to refer to someone or something mentioned elsewhere in the sentence or context, such as 'he', 'she', 'it', or 'they'.

Examples

All the other parts of speech: nouns, pronouns, adjectives, adverbs, and conjunctions operated under the same system as the verbs.
Ambiguity arises when the pronoun it carries a twofold reference.

Etymology

From Latin 'pronomen' (pro- 'for' + nomen 'name').
