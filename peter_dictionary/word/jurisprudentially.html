jurisprudentially

1. ADV

/ ˌdʒʊrɪsˌpruːˈdɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˌpruːˈdɛnʃəli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to jurisprudence or the philosophy of law.

Examples

National identification was reflected jurisprudentially in law theories which incorporated this Hegelian abstraction and saw law, domestic and international, simply as its formal reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jurisprudential - relating to the science or philosophy of law

Etymology

From "jurisprudential" (relating to jurisprudence) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix). Ultimately from Latin "jurisprudentia" (knowledge of law).
