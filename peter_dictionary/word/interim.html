interim

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.təˌrɪm / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tər.ɪm / (BrE)
serving as a temporary or provisional arrangement during an intervening period; intended to last until something permanent is established

Examples

The jury said it found the court "has incorporated into its operating procedures the recommendations "of two previous grand juries, the Atlanta Bar Association and an interim citizens committee.
Whether the compromises - on both sides - that made possible the interim appointment can then be repeated remains to be seen.

Synset Examples

an interim agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin 'interim' meaning 'meanwhile, in the meantime'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tə.rɪm / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tər.ɪm / (BrE)
the period of time between two events, processes, or situations

Examples

An old gentlemen next to me held a Boy Scout bugle to his lips and blasted away at every fourth step and during the interim shouted out, "V for Victory "!

Synset Examples

meanwhile the socialists are running the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a period between two events or times
  • Synonym: meantime - the time between two events
  • Synonym: interlude - an intervening period or episode

Etymology

From Latin 'interim' meaning 'in the meantime.'
