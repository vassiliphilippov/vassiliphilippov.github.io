towel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈtaʊəl / (BrE)
A piece of absorbent cloth or paper used for drying or wiping something, especially the body.

Examples

Hans had laid steaming towels over the kid's chest and stomach.
Water from the snow and water from the towels had run off the kid to the table where the dough was, and the dough was turning pasty, sticking to the kid's back and behind.
"You going to put more towels on him "?
That's why I only put towels on his chest and belly.
Colors on the towels had run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washcloth - small square cloth for washing the body or face
  • Synonym: napkin - piece of cloth or paper for wiping the mouth and fingers

Etymology

From Old French *toaille*, from Latin *tōgālia* (cloths for wiping), from *toga* (cloak).
