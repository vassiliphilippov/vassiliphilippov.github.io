cringe

1. VERB

/ krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
to bend one's body or shrink back as a reaction to fear, pain, or embarrassment

Examples

Though his inner thoughts cringed at it, he forced himself to think back, recreating the scene in which Mae claimed to have caught him molesting the child.

Synset Examples

she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - to make a quick, nervous movement as a reaction to fear or pain
  • Synonym: wince - to involuntarily react with a slight movement out of pain or distress
  • Antonym: bold - showing courage; not shrinking back from fear

Etymology

From Old English *cringan* (to yield, fall in battle).

2. VERB

/ krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
to bend one's body or crouch in fear or showing submission; to shrink back as if afraid or subordinate

Examples

They cringed under sodden covers, listening to the waves slop against the bottom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cower - to crouch or shrink away from in fear
  • Antonym: brave - to face without showing fear or submission

Etymology

Probably from Old English cringan (to yield, fall in battle).
