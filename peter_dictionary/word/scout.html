scout

1. VERB

/ skaʊt / (AmE), / skaʊt / (BrE)
to explore or investigate an area, often to gather information or locate something or someone

Examples

I do n't know whether he was after our rider, who had gone by a minute before, or whether he was simply scouting conditions; but when he passed us by, a musket roared, and he reared his horse, swung it around, and began to whip it back in the direction from which he had come.
Faced with this situation, Postmaster Charles F. McKenna of Montpelier went with Fogg on a Burlington trip, and together they scouted the terrain on the heights of Berlin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explore - to travel through an area to learn about it
  • Synonym: search - to look for something carefully

Etymology

From Old French *escouter* (to listen), from Latin *auscultare* (to listen).

2. NOUN

/ skaʊt / (AmE), / skaʊt / (BrE)
A person employed to keep watch for some anticipated event, especially to warn of danger or provide information about approaching forces.

Examples

The former scout's alibi could n't be shaken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lookout - a person who keeps watch for danger or trouble
  • Synonym: sentry - a soldier stationed to keep guard
  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch, especially a soldier or police officer

Etymology

From Old French escoute (a listener, spy), from écouter (to listen), from Latin auscultāre (to listen).
