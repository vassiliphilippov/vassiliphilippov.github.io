infallible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfæləbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfalɪbl̩ / (BrE)
Never failing; incapable of making mistakes or being wrong.

Examples

But was he infallible in all things?

Synset Examples

an infallible antidote
an infallible memory
the Catholic Church considers the Pope infallible
no doctor is infallible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *infallibilis*, from Latin *in-* (not) + *fallibilis* (liable to err).
