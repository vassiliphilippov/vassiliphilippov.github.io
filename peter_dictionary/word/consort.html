consort

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɔrt / (AmE), / kənˈsɔːt / (BrE)
(formal, often disapproving) To associate or spend time with someone, especially someone whose reputation or conduct is considered improper.

Examples

When Mayor Dever heard of the banquet he summoned Hughes for an explanation of why he had been dishonouring the police department by consorting with these felons and fixers.
This - trip of his had nothing to do with her consorting with tenants, and I am going to see that everybody at Mt. Pleasant understands that simple fact.

Synset Examples

He associates with strange people
She affiliates with her colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to spend time with or be involved with someone
  • Synonym: mingle - to mix or interact socially

Etymology

From Latin 'consortari' (to share, associate), from 'con-' (together) + 'sors' (lot, fate).
