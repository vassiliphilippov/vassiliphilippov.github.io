herculean

1. ADJ

/ hɝːˈkjuːliən / (AmE), / hɜːˈkjuːliən / (BrE)
Requiring or showing great strength or effort; displaying superhuman power, often in reference to the mythological Hercules.

Examples

Rarer still is a Mr. America or Mr. Universe of true Herculean build.

Synset Examples

herculean exertions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroic - showing extreme courage or strength

Etymology

From Latin Hercules, a hero in Roman mythology noted for his strength.

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɝː.kjuˈliː.ən / (AmE), / ˌhɜː.kjʊˈliː.ən / (BrE)
Requiring great strength or effort; extremely difficult.

Examples

But what a super Herculean task it is to winnow anything of value from the mud beplastered arguments used so freely, particularly since such common use is made of cliches and stereotypes, in themselves declarations of intellectual bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

a herculean task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - requiring great effort or endurance
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; requiring little effort

Etymology

From Latin Herculeus (of Hercules), referring to the legendary hero Hercules noted for his strength.
