geyser

1. VERB

/ ˈɡaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɡiːzə / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to gush or overflow with great force, like a geyser.

Examples

Miraculously, the bottle was still in my hand, foam still geysering over my (luckily) waterproof watch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spout - to send out liquid in a stream with force
  • Synonym: burst - to break open suddenly and forcefully, as with pressure

Etymology

From the noun 'geyser', originally from Icelandic 'Geysir', the name of a famous hot spring.
