interference

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
(in American football) the act of using your body to block or impede an opponent's progress, typically to protect a teammate carrying the ball.

Examples

"He knocked down the interference and made key stops lots of times.

Synset Examples

he ran interference for the quarterback

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈfɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈfɪə.rəns / (BrE)
the act of becoming involved in the affairs of another country, especially in a way seen as unwelcome or inappropriate

Examples

The weekly loss is partly counterbalanced by 500 arrivals each week from West Germany, but the hard truth, says Crossman, is that "The closing off of East Berlin without interference from the West and with the use only of East German, as distinct from Russian, troops was a major Communist victory, which dealt West Berlin a deadly, possibly a fatal, blow.
In a broad sense, it would reaffirm the Monroe Doctrine by opposing Communist interference in the Western Hemisphere.
Political interference in Africa and Asia and even in Latin America (though limited in Latin America by the special interest of the United States as expressed in the Monroe Doctrine, itself from the outset related to European politics and long dependent upon the "balance of power "system in Europe) was necessary in order to preserve both common economic values and the European "balance "itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intervention - the action of becoming intentionally involved in a difficult situation, often to change what is happening

Etymology

From interfere + -ence, from Latin inter- (between) + ferīre (to strike).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
Unwanted electrical or acoustic disturbance that impedes the transmission or reception of signals, especially in communications.

Examples

The radio emission of a planet was first detected in 1955, when Burke and Franklin (1955) identified the origin of interference like radio noise on their records at about 15 meters wave length as emission from Jupiter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noise - unwanted sound, disturbance in communication systems

Etymology

From interfere + -ence; 'interfere' from French 'interférer', ultimately from Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'ferīre' (to strike).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
The act of hindering, obstructing, or impeding the progress or course of something.

Examples

But with the renewal of interference in 1954 (as with its beginning in 1835), the improvement was impaired.
Curt wanted to get Jess alone, without interference from anyone, even as spineless a person as the store owner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or delay
  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or hinders
  • Antonym: assistance - the act of helping or aiding

Etymology

From interfere + -ence, ultimately from Latin inter- ‘between’ + ferire ‘to strike’.
