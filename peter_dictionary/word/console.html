console

1. VERB

/ kənˈsoʊl / (AmE), / kənˈsəʊl / (BrE)
to comfort someone at a time of grief or disappointment; to give moral or emotional support

Examples

Some liar's logic, a wisp of optimism as fragile as the scent of tropical blossoms that came through the window (a euphoria perhaps engendered by the pill Fritzie had given her), consoled her for a moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - to give relief in affliction; to console
  • Antonym: distress - to cause someone anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

From Latin consolari, from con- (together) + solari (to comfort).
