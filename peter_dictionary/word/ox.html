ox

1. NOUN

/ ɑks / (AmE), / ɒks / (BrE)
An adult castrated male of the domesticated cattle species, especially one used as a draft animal.

Examples

They were held together by pegs and withes and in later times drawn by a single ox in thills.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - an uncastrated male bovine animal
  • Antonym: cow - an adult female of domesticated cattle

Etymology

Old English *oxa* (ox), of Germanic origin.
