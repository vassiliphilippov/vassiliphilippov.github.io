retina

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtənə / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪnə / (BrE)
The thin layer of light-sensitive tissue forming the innermost surface at the back of the eyeball, continuous with the optic nerve, where images are formed and transmitted to the brain.

Examples

He can diagnose detachment of the retina where conventional methods indicate blindness due to glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optic nerve - the nerve transmitting visual information from the retina to the brain

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *rētina*, short for *tunica rētina* (net-like tunic), from Latin *rēte* (net).
