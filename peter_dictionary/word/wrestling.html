wrestling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛs.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛs.lɪŋ / (BrE)
The sport or activity of fighting someone by holding, pushing, or pulling them, usually as a test of strength and skill, especially according to specific rules.

Examples

His unsuccessful strivings to give up drink are represented as religious strivings; he keeps a bottle in a wardrobe at home, and "before this wardrobe Praisegod Piepsam had before now gone literally on his knees, and in his wrestlings had bitten his tongue - and still in the end capitulated ".

Synset Examples

they had a fierce wrestle
we watched his grappling and wrestling with the bully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grappling - the act of wresting or struggling in close combat
  • Antonym: boxing - the sport of fighting with the fists

Etymology

From Old English *wraestlian*, related to *wraestan* (to twist).
