Assyrian

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
the modern Neo-Aramaic language spoken by Assyrians, chiefly in Iraq and neighboring regions

Examples

Dr. H. V. Hilprecht, Professor of Assyrian at the University of Pennsylvania, dreamed that a Babylonian priest, associated with the king Kurigalzu, (1300 B.C..) escorted him to the treasure chamber of the temple of Bel, gave him six novel points of information about a certain broken relic, and corrected an error in its identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neo-Aramaic - the group of modern languages descended from Aramaic

Etymology

From Latin Assyrius, from Greek Assýrios, referring to ancient Assyria.

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
An inhabitant of ancient Assyria, a historical region in Mesopotamia.

Examples

He was almost positive it was not Assyrian nor Cassite, and imagined it must have been German or English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonian - inhabitant of ancient Babylonia
  • Synonym: Assyria - ancient region in Mesopotamia

Etymology

From Latin Assyrius, from Greek Assyria (Assyria), from Akkadian Aššur (the city and chief deity of Assyria).
