fracture

1. VERB

/ ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
To misuse, mangle, or violate something, especially language or rules, often in a noticeable or jarring way.

Examples

Immediately thereafter, the patient fractures her rehearsed story, veering into an oversoft, breathy, sloppily articulated, "I do n't feel like talking right now ".

Synset Examples

This writer really fractures the language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangle - to spoil or damage something, especially language, badly

Etymology

From Latin fractura (a break), from frangere (to break).
