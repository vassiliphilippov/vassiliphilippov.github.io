old-timer

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldˌtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈəʊldˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
an experienced person who has been involved in a particular activity, job, or organization for a long time

Examples

You were around the old-timers, who dreamed of how the families should be organized, basing them on the old Roman legions, and call '’em "regimes," the "capos" and "soldiers."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - someone with long experience in an activity, especially military service

Etymology

Formed from 'old' + 'timer', dating from late 19th century American English.
