nationality

1. NOUN

/ næʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / næʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
A group of people sharing common origins or traditions, often forming a nation.

Examples

it might be pointed out that the integrating function of religion, for good or ill, has often supported or been identified with other groupings - political, nationality, language, class, racial, sociability, even economic.

Synset Examples

immigrants of the same nationality often seek each other out
such images define their sense of nationality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethnicity - social group sharing a common and distinctive culture, religion, language, or the like
  • Antonym: citizenship - the legal status of being a member of a particular country

Etymology

From French 'nationalité', from Latin 'natio' (nation).
