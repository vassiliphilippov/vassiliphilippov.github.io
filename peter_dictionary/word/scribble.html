scribble

1. VERB

/ ˈskrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪbəl / (BrE)
to write or draw something quickly and carelessly, without attention to neatness or detail

Examples

H. E. Bates has scribbled a farce called "Hark, Hark, the Lark "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - to write or draw something in a sloppy or careless way
  • Degree Lower: write - to form letters, words, or symbols on a surface

Etymology

From Latin scribillare, frequentative of scribere (to write).
