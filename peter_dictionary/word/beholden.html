beholden

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈhoʊldən / (AmE), / bɪˈhəʊldən / (BrE)
Owing thanks or having a duty to someone in return for help or a service; under a moral obligation to someone.

Examples

I answer to Grissom, not to psychos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indebted - Owing gratitude or owing money; under obligation
  • Synonym: obligated - Required to do something as a result of a promise or contract

Etymology

From Middle English 'beholden', past participle of 'behold' (to observe, look at), later meaning 'to owe' in the sense of being in someone's debt.
