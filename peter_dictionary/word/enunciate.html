enunciate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnʌnsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌnsieɪt / (BrE)
to pronounce words clearly and distinctly

Examples

It's your decision ", said Juanita, holding her face very still, trying to contain the bitterness of her voice as she enunciated her words too distinctly.

Synset Examples

She pronounces French words in a funny way
I cannot say `zip wire'
Can the child sound out this complicated word?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express or pronounce distinctly
  • Antonym: mumble - to speak indistinctly

Etymology

From Latin enuntiare (to announce, declare, report).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnʌnsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌnsɪeɪt / (BrE)
to express or state (an idea, opinion, etc.) clearly and distinctly

Examples

The impression has nevertheless been given during these three days, despite Mr. Rusk's personal popularity, that the United States delegation came to Oslo in a somewhat tentative and exploratory frame of mind, more ready to listen and learn than to enunciate firm policy on a global scale with detailed application to individual danger spots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently

Etymology

From Latin enuntiare (to announce or declare), from e- (out) + nuntiare (to announce).
