sway

1. VERB

/ sweɪ / (AmE), / sweɪ / (BrE)
to influence someone so that they change their opinion or decision; to win support or approval for something

Examples

Statements by other legislators that Dallas is paying for all its water program by local bonds, and that less populous places would benefit most by the pending bill, did not sway Cotten's attack.
But Stravinsky was swayed by the attitudes of whatever culture he was reflecting.
By swaying the balance between them, he effected the alternation of the seasons.

Synset Examples

Carry all before one
His speech did not sway the voters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - to affect or change how someone behaves or thinks
  • Synonym: persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something through reasoning or argument

Etymology

From Middle English 'sweyen', of Scandinavian origin.

2. VERB

/ sweɪ / (AmE), / sweɪ / (BrE)
to move or walk in a smooth, swinging or rocking motion from side to side

Examples

He swayed like a drunkard, his arms milling in slow circles.
The man swayed on a thwart, turning his ruined eyes from side to side.
Unsinkable slowed and stopped, hundreds of brilliant white flares swayed eerily down from the black, the air raid sirens ashore rose in a keening shriek, the anti-aircraft guns coughed and chattered - and above it all motors roared and the bombs came whispering and wailing and crashing down among the ships at anchor at Bari.

Synset Examples

He swung back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - to move back and forth or from side to side while suspended or as if suspended

Etymology

From Middle English 'swayen' (to move, swing), probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'sveigja' (to bend, swing).

3. VERB

/ sweɪ / (AmE), / sweɪ / (BrE)
to move slowly or rhythmically back and forth or sideways

Examples

Calloused fingers, caressed only by the smoothness of polished rosaries, had swayed excitedly beneath puckered chins where tiny black hairs sprouted, never to be tweezed away.
It swayed with the wicker swings and screeched with the rusted hinges of screen doors.

Synset Examples

the ship was rocking
the tall building swayed
She rocked back and forth on her feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock - to move gently back and forth or from side to side
  • Synonym: swing - to move freely back and forth or from side to side while suspended or on an axis

Etymology

From Old Norse 'sveigja' (to bend, bow, sway).

4. NOUN

/ sweɪ / (AmE), / sweɪ / (BrE)
Influence or control over other people or a situation.

Examples

Circumstances gave her almost undisputed sway over child rearing, money handling and home maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - the capacity to affect the character, development, or behavior of someone or something
  • Synonym: power - the ability to control people or things
  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders and make decisions
  • Antonym: subjection - the state of being under the control of someone

Etymology

From Old Norse 'sveigja' (to bend, swing).
