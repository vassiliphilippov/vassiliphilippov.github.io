burner

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnə / (BrE)
A part of a stove, furnace, or other appliance where fuel or gas is burned to produce heat or light.

Examples

He found the pilot light and turned on one of the burners for her.

Synset Examples

a diesel engine is an oil burner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stove - an apparatus for cooking or heating by burning fuel or using electricity

Etymology

From Middle English 'brenner', from 'brennen' (to burn) + '-er'.
