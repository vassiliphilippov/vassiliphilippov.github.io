groundwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwɜːk / (BrE)
The essential foundation or basic preparation for a later stage or development.

Examples

Vocational educators do not claim that school training alone makes skilled workers, but it provides the essential groundwork for developing skills.

Synset Examples

the whole argument rested on a basis of conjecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basis - the underlying support or foundation for an idea, argument, or process
  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building, or an underlying basis for something

Etymology

From Middle English ground + work, meaning work on the ground or at the base.
