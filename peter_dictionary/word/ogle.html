ogle

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊɡl / (BrE)
to look at someone with obvious sexual or amorous interest, often in a way that is considered inappropriate or lecherous

Examples

The pretty coeds must have ogled him all day long - but he dutifully kept his eye on the gravy.
He ogled a long redhead with green eyes, but she was a shill with her money in front of her.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stare - to look at someone or something for a long time
  • Synonym: leer - to look at someone in an unpleasant or sexually interested way

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Low German *oglien* (to look sideways).
