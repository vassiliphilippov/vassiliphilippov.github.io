lover

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvər / (AmE), / ˈlʌvə / (BrE)
a person who is in a romantic or sexual relationship with another person

Examples

The wife, Amra, and her lover are both savagely portrayed, she as incarnate sensuality, "voluptuous "and "indolent ", possibly "a mischief maker ", with "a kind of luxurious cunning "to set against her apparent simplicity, her "bird like brain ".
And how anxious this lover of obscurity was for applause!
I do n't ask you who' tis you 're being unfaithful to, husband or lover.
Brains and beauty, high position in both the social and intellectual worlds, athlete, fabled lover - if ever the world was any man's oyster it was his.
But her personality was such that each affair lasted only until that lover, too, had been conquered and reduced to passivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - a person with whom one has a romantic relationship
  • Degree Higher: spouse - a person's husband or wife
  • Degree Lower: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Old English ‘lufian’ (to love) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvər / (AmE), / ˈlʌvə / (BrE)
A person who is very enthusiastic about or devoted to a particular thing, activity, or subject.

Examples

she was too indulgent a lover of dogs and grandchildren.
He was a propagandist - they were n't afraid of the word, then - satirist, nature lover, philosopher, scientist, what you will, a member of every party and of no party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - a person who has a strong interest in or admiration for a particular person or thing

Etymology

From Old English 'lufian' (to love) + '-er' (agent suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌvə / (BrE)
a person with whom one has a romantic or sexual relationship, especially one who is not married to the person

Examples

I didn't shoot my wife and I didn't shoot her lover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - person with whom one shares a close personal relationship

Etymology

From Old English 'lufian' (to love) + '-er'.
