constellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A group or arrangement of related things, people, or factors considered as a whole, especially forming a pattern or unified set.

Examples

In its place is a passionate consciousness grasped and molded to feelings of positive or negative values even as the actions of one's life are determined by constellations of process in which one is caught.

Synset Examples

the outcome depends on the configuration of influences at the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: configuration - an arrangement of elements or parts
  • Synonym: pattern - a repeated or regular arrangement
  • Synonym: collection - a group of things considered together

Etymology

From Latin 'constellatio' (set of stars), from 'con-' (together) + 'stella' (star).
