divergent

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
Tending to be different or develop in different directions from a standard, norm, or each other.

Examples

Recently I was struck anew by the divergent approaches, when in the course of one afternoon and evening I listened to no fewer than ten different performances.
For it had turned out, by a further paradox of Cubism, that the means to an illusion of depth and plasticity had now become widely divergent from the means of representation or imaging.

Synset Examples

a divergent opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergent - tending to come together or become more similar
  • Synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different

Etymology

From Latin 'divergere' (to go in different directions), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'vergere' (to turn, incline).
