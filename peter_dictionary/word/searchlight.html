searchlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrtʃˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a powerful outdoor lamp with a reflector that projects a strong, concentrated beam of light in a particular direction, used to illuminate or search an area

Examples

Prevot had said that the searchlights would be bounced off the clouds at 2230 hours, "which gives us time to get settled in position ".
Off in the distance a searchlight flashed on, its beam slashing the sky.
Above, the glowing ivory baton of their searchlight pointed at the clouds, diluting the valley's dark to a pallid light.
Point those searchlights at the tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotlight - a lamp projecting a strong beam of light onto a particular area

Etymology

Formed from 'search' + 'light,' first used in late 19th century for military applications.
