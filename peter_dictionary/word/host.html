host

1. NOUN

/ hoʊst / (AmE), / həʊst / (BrE)
a person who receives or entertains guests at a formal event, introducing speakers or events

Examples

(He played host at the Timex show already mentioned.)
"You have visited England five times in the past quarter-century, I believe ", my host said.
My host went over and stared out the window at his peacocks; then he turned to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emcee - person who acts as the master of ceremonies at an event
  • Antonym: guest - a person who is invited to stay at someone's home or attend an event

Etymology

From Latin 'hospes' (guest, host), via Old French 'hoste'.

4. NOUN
