severing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛvərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of cutting off or separating by severing; the process of ending a connection or relationship.

Examples

Nothing in all the preceding years had had the power to bring me closer to a knowledge of profound sorrow than the breakup of camp, the packing away of my camp uniforms, the severing of ties with the six or ten people I had grown most to love in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severance - the act or state of being severed; a cutting off or ending

Etymology

From 'sever', from Old French *sevrer*, from Latin *separare* (to separate).
