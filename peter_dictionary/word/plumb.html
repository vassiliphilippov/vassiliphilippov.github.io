plumb

1. VERB

/ plʌm / (AmE), / plʌm / (BrE)
to measure the depth of something, especially of water, usually with a plumb line or similar device

Examples

He evolved programs that plumbed new depths of bathos and besmirched whole networks, and quickly superseded his boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - to measure the depth of water using a line or sonar

Etymology

From Old French *plumbe* and Latin *plumbum* (lead), referring to the lead weight used on a plumb line.

2. ADV

/ plʌm / (AmE), / plʌm / (BrE)
(informal, American) Completely; used as an intensifier to emphasize the totality or extent of something.

Examples

He knew that anything a brainy little lady like her had to say would be plumb important, as well as pleasin' to the ear, and he did n't want to miss a word of it.
Figger we got to be plumb careful with any of you Highlands big shots ".

Synset Examples

clean forgot the appointment
I'm plumb (or plum) tuckered out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; in every way or as much as possible
  • Antonym: partially - in part; not completely or fully

Etymology

Originally from Middle English *plom* (lead, sounding lead), from Old French *plomb*, from Latin *plumbum* (lead); used figuratively to mean 'entirely' from the idea of measuring to the bottom.
