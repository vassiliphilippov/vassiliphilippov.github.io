overwork

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɜrk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɜːk / (BrE)
to use, perform, or develop something so much that it becomes tiresome, ineffective, or less valuable

Examples

In the same vein, a certain short-story plot has been overworked.

Synset Examples

This play has been overworked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overuse - to use something too much

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'work'.
