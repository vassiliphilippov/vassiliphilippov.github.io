trying

1. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
difficult or annoying to deal with; causing strain or hardship.

Examples

They expected greater things from him, regardless of how trying the circumstances, and they were disappointed.
Men continuously at the head of growing enterprises can acquire experiences of the most varied, complicated and trying type so that at maturation they have developed the competence and willingness to accept the personal responsibility so sorely needed now.
A man that young, less than a year to go, trying to escape.

Synset Examples

fell upon trying times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenging - demanding effort or skill; testing one's abilities
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or hard to do; uncomplicated

Etymology

Present participle of 'try', originally from Old French *trier* (to select, sift; to try), itself from Late Latin *tritare* (to wear out).
