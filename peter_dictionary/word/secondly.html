secondly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛk.ənd.li / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ənd.li / (BrE)
Used to introduce the second point or item in a series.

Examples

Secondly, to find a learned diversion and a pleasing joke in More's account of the stupid brutalities of early sixteenth century wars, of the anguish of the poor and dispossessed, of the insolence and cruelty of the rich and powerful requires a callousness toward suffering and sin that would be surprising in a moral imbecile and most surprising in More himself.
"History has this in common with every other science: that the historian is not allowed to claim any single piece of knowledge, except where he can justify his claim by exhibiting to himself in the first place, and secondly to any one else who is both able and willing to follow his demonstration, the grounds upon which it is based.

Synset Examples

second, we must consider the economy

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: firstly - used to introduce the first point or item in a series
  • Degree Higher: thirdly - used to introduce the third point or item in a series

Etymology

From 'second' + '-ly'; patterned after similar formations like 'firstly', 'thirdly'.
