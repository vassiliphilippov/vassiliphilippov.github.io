sulphur

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌlfər / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə / (BrE)
to treat (something, especially food) with sulphur, especially to preserve or bleach it.

Examples

Dried fruits are purchased from sources where they are neither sulphured nor sprayed.

Synset Examples

These dried fruits are sulphured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur - American spelling—treat with sulfur to preserve
  • Synonym: bleach - to make something lighter in color, often by chemical means

Etymology

From the noun 'sulphur', the chemical element, whose preservation properties have been known since antiquity.
