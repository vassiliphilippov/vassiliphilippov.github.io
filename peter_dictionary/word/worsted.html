worsted

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝː.stɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɜː.stɪd / (BrE)
A smooth, hard-textured woolen fabric made from long-staple wool fibers, with no nap, typically used for suits and tailored garments.

Examples

I wore a new double-breasted brown worsted suit with a faint herringbone design and wide lapels like a devil's ears.

Synset Examples

he wore a worsted suit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweed - a rough-surfaced woolen cloth, typically of mixed flecked colors, originally produced in Scotland

Etymology

From Worstead, a village in Norfolk, England, where it was first manufactured.
