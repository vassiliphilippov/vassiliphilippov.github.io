ripe

1. ADJ

/ raɪp / (AmE), / raɪp / (BrE)
(Of fruit or other food) fully developed and ready to be eaten; (of other things) fully matured or ready for a particular activity or purpose.

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.
The avocado should have a "give "to it, as you hold it, when it is ripe.
Resolved that the anti slavery sentiment is becoming ripe for resolute action.
Her voice was ripe and full and her teeth flashed again in Sicilian brilliance before the warm curved lips met and her mouth settled in repose.
Yet if he were not there, they would have missed him, as they would have missed the sounds of bees buzzing against the screen door in early June; or the smell of thick tomato paste - the ripe smell that was both sweet and sour - rising up from aluminum trays wrapped in fly dotted cheesecloth.

Synset Examples

ripe peaches
full-bodied mature wines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unripe - not fully developed or matured; not ready to be eaten or used
  • Synonym: mature - (of food, people, or things) fully developed
  • Degree Higher: overripe - past the point of optimal ripeness; starting to spoil

Etymology

From Old English *rīpe* (ready for reaping), related to reap.

2. ADJ

/ raɪp / (AmE), / raɪp / (BrE)
completely ready or prepared for a particular action or situation; eager for something to happen.

Examples

Attending the life class was my idea - or rather, Askington's idea, but I was ripe for it, and the other two would n't have gone if I had n't talked them into it.

Synset Examples

the colonists were ripe for revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - fully prepared for something
  • Antonym: unprepared - not ready or willing for something

Etymology

From Old English rīpe (ready for reaping).
