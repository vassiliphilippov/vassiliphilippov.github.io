steer

1. VERB

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
to direct the course of a vehicle, person, or process; to guide or control the movement or direction of something

Examples

With leather cups fitted in his handlebars, he steered his bicycle.
The lawyer with whom I studied law steered me off the Socialist track.
He steered me to the right track - the free enterprise track ".
Pilots would steer them in a suicide dive into the water, striking below the waterline of individual ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - to show or direct the way; to lead or direct someone
  • Synonym: drive - to operate and control the direction of a vehicle
  • Antonym: follow - to go or come after in the same direction

Etymology

From Old English 'stīeran' (to guide, govern, control).

2. VERB

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
to guide someone or something in a particular direction or towards a particular goal; to influence the course of an action or decision

Examples

I 'm not steering you wrong.

Synset Examples

The teacher steered the gifted students towards the more challenging courses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - to show or influence the way; direct
  • Antonym: mislead - to guide in a wrong direction; deceive

Etymology

From Old English *stēoran* (to guide a ship).

3. VERB

/ stɪr / (AmE), / stɪə / (BrE)
to guide or direct (oneself or someone else) in a particular direction or towards a particular goal

Examples

In steering the family toward ego-adaptive and away from maladaptive responses, the worker uses time-honored focused casework techniques of specific emotional support, clarification, and anticipatory guidance.
Let Old Knife come up and kill you and your people, or would you steer him on someone else "?
Rachel steered me along toward a school for young boys beginning to study the Torah.

Synset Examples

Steer clear of him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - to show the way by leading, directing, or advising
  • Synonym: lead - to show the way to a place or along a route

Etymology

Old English *stēran* (to guide a vessel), from Proto-Germanic *steurijaną*.
