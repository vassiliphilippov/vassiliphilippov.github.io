kneel

1. VERB

/ niːl / (AmE), / niːl / (BrE)
to rest on one's knees, usually by bending the legs and bringing the knees to the ground

Examples

John closed his eyes and saw once again the little niche in his mother's bedroom, where she had knelt to tell the good Virgin of her needs.
The blue draped Virgin was still there, but no one knelt before her now.
He knelt down at his bed as long as he could kneel.
He knelt down at his bed as long as he could kneel.
Kneeling, Cobb planted a sturdy knee in the small of his back, holding him pinned.

Synset Examples

In church you have to kneel during parts of the service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet
  • Antonym: sit - to rest with the body supported by the buttocks or thighs

Etymology

Old English *cnēowlian*, from *cnēow* (knee).
