rearguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːr.ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈrɪə.ɡɑːd / (BrE)
A group of soldiers assigned to protect the back of a main military force, especially during a retreat; also, the last line of defense in a movement or campaign.

Examples

Since American life is committed above all to productivity and a higher standard of economic life, the countervailing forces of residential and religious exclusiveness have fought a desperate, rearguard action against the expanding interdependence of the metropolis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vanguard - the foremost part of an advancing army or movement

Etymology

From rear (meaning 'back') + guard; first attested in military contexts in the 17th century.
