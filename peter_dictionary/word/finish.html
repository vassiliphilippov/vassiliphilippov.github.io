finish

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ / (BrE)
to come to an end in time, space, or amount; to reach the end of something or be completed

Examples

Mrs. Grace Butcher, of nearby Chardon, a 27 - year old housewife who has two children, finished in 2.21.6.
The big audience started applauding even before he had finished.
Now all he had to do was finish in even par to collect the trophy and the biggest single paycheck in golf.

Half an hour after he finished his round, Player holed out at the 18 th green with a 69 and a three-round total of 206, four strokes ahead of Palmer.

Synset Examples

the bronchioles terminate in a capillary bed

The symphony ends in a pianissimo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - bring something to an end; finish making or doing
  • Antonym: start - to begin; to have a beginning
  • Antonym: begin - to start or come into being

Etymology

From Middle English *finisshen*, from Old French *finiss-,* stem of *finir* (to end), from Latin *finire* (to limit, end), from *finis* (end).
