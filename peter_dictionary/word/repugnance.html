repugnance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌɡnəns / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌɡnəns / (BrE)
A strong feeling of disgust or intense aversion toward something; intense distaste.

Examples

Indeed we should say, on the contrary, that the accident of our later discovery made no difference whatever to the badness of the animal's pain, that it would have been every whit as bad whether a chance passer-by happened later to discover the body and feel repugnance or not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - A strong sense of dislike or revulsion
  • Synonym: aversion - A strong feeling of dislike or opposition
  • Antonym: attraction - A feeling of being drawn towards something

Etymology

From Latin 'repugnantia,' from 'repugnare' (to resist, oppose).
