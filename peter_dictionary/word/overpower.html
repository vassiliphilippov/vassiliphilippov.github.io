overpower

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to affect someone so strongly as to overwhelm them, especially with emotion or sensation

Examples

The home listener is overpowered, all right, but the experience is a far from pleasant one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwhelm - to overpower emotionally or physically
  • Synonym: subdue - to bring under control by force or persuasion

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + power (from Anglo-Norman pouer, ultimately from Latin posse, to be able).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to defeat or overcome someone or something by using greater strength or force

Examples

A band of robbers enters a railroad station, overpowers and ties up the telegraph operator, holds up the train and escapes.
The bad luck that can so often mar a well-played round of golf is simply overpowered and obliterated by the contemptuous boldness of these champions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • Synonym: conquer - to overcome and take control by force
  • Antonym: succumb - to fail to resist force, temptation, or pressure

Etymology

From over- (meaning 'excessively') + power, modeled after Middle French 'surpuissier'.
