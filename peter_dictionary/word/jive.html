jive

1. VERB

/ ʤaɪv / (AmE), / ʤaɪv / (BrE)
to dance to lively jazz or swing music, especially performing the jive dance

Examples

The music drove them off, or away, and he was free to walk on air in a very few moments, humming and jiving within, beating the rhythm within.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boogie - to dance energetically to music, especially to boogie-woogie
  • Synonym: swing - to dance to swing music

Etymology

Originated in African-American vernacular in the 1920s–1930s, possibly from 'jive' meaning deceptive talk, later associated with jazz and dance.
