overeat

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈit / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈiːt / (BrE)
To eat more food than is needed or than is healthy.

Examples

Physiologically, people overeat because what Dr. Jolliffe calls the "appestat "is set too high.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undereat - to eat less food than the body needs
  • Synonym: binge - to indulge in an activity, especially eating, to excess

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' (indicating excess) + 'eat'.
