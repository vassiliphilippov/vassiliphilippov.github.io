resourcefulness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɔrsfəlnɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɔːsfʊlnɪs / (BrE)
The ability to deal effectively with difficult situations by using clever and creative solutions.

Examples

As she gained composure during the second act, her technical resourcefulness emerged stronger, though she had already revealed a trill almost unprecedented in years of performances of "Lucia ".

Synset Examples

a man of great resourcefulness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuity - the quality of being clever, original, and inventive
  • Synonym: adaptability - the quality of being able to adjust to new conditions

Etymology

From 'resource' + '-fulness'; 'resource' from French 'ressource' (means of supplying a want).
