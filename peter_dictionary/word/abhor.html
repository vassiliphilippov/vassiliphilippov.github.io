abhor

1. VERB

/ əbˈhɔr / (AmE), / əbˈhɔː / (BrE)
to hate something or someone very much; to regard with disgust and hatred

Examples

Much as he abhorred slavery, Lincoln was always willing to concede to each "slave state "the right to decide independently whether to continue or end it.

Synset Examples

I loathe that man
She abhors cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for
  • Synonym: detest - to dislike intensely
  • Antonym: admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Latin abhorrēre, meaning 'to shrink back from'.
