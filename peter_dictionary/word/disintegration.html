disintegration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈteɡr̩ˌeɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtɪɡrɪˌeɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The process or state of breaking into small parts or losing cohesion; decomposition or collapse into separate elements.

Examples

It grows terribly and unavoidably in power and leaves in its wake a trail of misery, moral disintegration, and destruction.
The beautiful, the satisfying part of his disintegration, however, was the masterly way the Buddha polished him off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decomposition - the process of decaying or breaking down into simpler parts
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining parts into a whole

Etymology

From disintegrate + -ion, originally from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'integer' (whole).
