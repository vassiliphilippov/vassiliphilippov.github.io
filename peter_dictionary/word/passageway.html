passageway

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪdʒˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪdʒweɪ / (BrE)
A long, narrow way with walls on either side that connects one place to another, or a channel through which something may pass.

Examples

After finding that its coasts led nowhere, however, he turned north again, toward the main, ice filled passageway - and the crew, at first uneasy, then frightened, rebelled.

Synset Examples

the nasal passages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corridor - a long passage in a building from which doors lead into rooms
  • Synonym: hallway - a corridor or passage in a building
  • Synonym: tunnel - an underground or enclosed passageway

Etymology

Formed from 'passage' + 'way', meaning a way of passing through.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪdʒˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪdʒˌweɪ / (BrE)
A long, narrow space that connects one place to another, such as between rooms or buildings, and people can walk through.

Examples

She ate what she could and went out along the covered passageway, with the rain dripping from the vines.
The safe at Ingleside District Station stands next to the gum machine in a narrow passageway that leads to Captain Harris's office (to the left), the lieutenant's office (farther along and to the left) and the janitor's supply closet (straight ahead).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corridor - a long passage in a building from which doors lead into rooms
  • Synonym: hallway - a corridor in a building; a passageway

Etymology

Formed from 'passage' + 'way', both of French and English origin.
