playmate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌmeɪt / (BrE)
A person, especially a child, who plays with another child; a companion in play.

Examples

If he bites a playmate she says, "Danny won't like you ".
And for his playmate, Francis Coletta of West Warwick, who has a bit part, Billy.
From his playmates in Savannah, Mercer had picked up, along with a soft Southern dialect, traces also of the Gullah dialects of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

Formed from 'play' + 'mate', both from Old English roots.
