settled

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛt̬.əld / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
(of a region or area) having been inhabited or established by colonists.

Examples

The long settled areas of states like Virginia and South Carolina developed the ante-bellum culture to its richest flowering, and there the memory is more precious, and the consciousness of loss the greater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonized - (of a place) settled by colonists

Etymology

From the verb 'settle,' itself from Old English 'setlan' (to seat, place, settle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛtəld / (AmE), / ˈsɛtld / (BrE)
Established, fixed, or decided so as to be no longer in doubt or dispute.

Examples

Mark easily looked years older than himself, settled, his world comfortably categorized.
With classical art, all is settled.

Synset Examples

with details of the wedding settled she could now sleep at night

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsettled - not resolved or decided; uncertain

Etymology

From Middle English setlen, past participle of 'settle'.
