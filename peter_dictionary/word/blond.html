blond

1. ADJ

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
(of a person) having light-colored hair, often with fair skin and usually blue or grey eyes.

Examples

Mantle, the bull-necked blond switch-hitter, had one sensational triple-crown season, 1959, when he batted .365 and also led the American League in home runs, 52, and RBIs, 130.
The cyclist, by contrast, blond and blue-eyed, is simply unreflective, unproblematic Life, "blithe and carefree ".

Synset Examples

blond Scandinavians
a house full of light-haired children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blonde - female form or variant spelling, sometimes used especially for women
  • Antonym: brunette - having dark brown or black hair

Etymology

From Old French 'blond', from Medieval Latin 'blundus' (yellow, fair).
