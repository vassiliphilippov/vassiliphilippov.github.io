nominal

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a role, position, or classification) relating to or existing in name only; not reflecting the true situation or real status.

Examples

The second stage is to retain the nominal classification of "neutralist ", while in fact turning the country into an active advocate and adherent of Soviet policy.

Synset Examples

the Russian system of nominal brevity
a nominal lists of priests
taxable males as revealed by the nominal rolls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titular - holding a title or position in name only

Etymology

From Latin nominalis (pertaining to a name), from nomen (name).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɑmənl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a sum of money, fee, or amount) very small or far below the real value or cost; existing as a mere formality or in name only.

Examples

French Egalitarianism had had only nominal influence in this country before the days of Popularism.

Synset Examples

the fee was nominal
a token gesture of resistance
a toknenish gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: token - (of an amount or action) symbolic and minimal
  • Synonym: minimal - very small in amount

Etymology

From Latin nominalis (pertaining to a name), from nomen (name).

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of shares or accounts, etc.) bearing the name of a specific person; registered in someone's name.

Examples

♪ I know a cat named...

Synset Examples

nominative shares of stock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: registered - (of shares, etc.) entered in an official record with the owner's name
  • Antonym: bearer - (of shares, etc.) held by the person possessing the physical document, not registered by name

Etymology

From Latin nominālis (pertaining to a name), from nōmen (name).
