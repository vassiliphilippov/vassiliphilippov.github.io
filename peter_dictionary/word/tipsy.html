tipsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪpsi / (AmE), / ˈtɪpsi / (BrE)
Slightly intoxicated, especially from alcohol; a little drunk.

Examples

Against all expectation, Carmer was inside, clearly enjoying himself to the hilt and already so tipsy that it seemed unlikely he was bothering to note anything or anyone about him.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drunk - intoxicated from alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol

Etymology

Probably from tip (to tilt or overturn) + -sy, implying unsteadiness as if from drinking.
