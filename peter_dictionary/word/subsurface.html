subsurface

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈsɜrfɪs / (AmE), / sʌbˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
Located or situated beneath the surface of something, especially the ground or a specified material.

Examples

When sufficiently accurate and complete measurements are available, it will be possible to set limits on the thermal and electrical characteristics of the surface and subsurface materials of the moon.

Synset Examples

subsurface materials of the moon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surface - the outside or top layer of something

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + English 'surface'.
