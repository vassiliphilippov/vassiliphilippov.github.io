hotshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌʃɒt / (BrE)
A person who is exceptionally talented or skilled in a particular field, often regarded with admiration or sometimes arrogance.

Examples

A biting, pithy parable of the all pervading hollowness of modern life, the piece has been set by Mlle Lagoon to a sumptuous score (a single motif played over and over by four thousand French horns) by existentialist hot-shot Jean-Paul Sartre.
Some hotshot banker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace - someone who excels at a particular activity
  • Synonym: whizz-kid - a young person who is very skilled or successful at something

Etymology

From the phrase 'hot shot', originally referring to a projectile heated before firing, later applied metaphorically to people who are capable or outstanding.
