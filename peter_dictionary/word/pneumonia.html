pneumonia

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A serious disease in which the lungs become inflamed, often due to infection, causing cough and difficulty breathing.

Examples

Prevention of bacterial pneumonia, shipping fever, as an aid in reduction of losses due to respiratory infections (infectious rhinotracheitis - shipping fever complex).
Poor little Fredo, he'’s got pneumonia.

Etymology

From Greek pneumon (lung) via Latin pneumonia.
