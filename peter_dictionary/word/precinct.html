precinct

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriː.sɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈpriː.sɪŋkt / (BrE)
A district of a city or town established for administrative or other official purposes, especially for elections or policing.

Examples

Then, with the new affluence, there is actually a sallying forth into the wide, wide world beyond the precincts of New York.

Etymology

From Latin *praecinctum* (enclosure), from *praecingere* (to encircle).
