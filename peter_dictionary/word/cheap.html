cheap

1. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
Low in price; costing little compared to others of the same kind.

Examples

When he came to the movies - more or less by accident - they were still cheap entertainment capable of enthralling the unthinking for an idle few minutes.
Until internal combustion became cheap, he had to be near a railroad siding and a trolley line or an existing large community of lower-class homes.
The railroad siding is still important - it is usually, though not always, true that long-haul shipment by rail is cheaper than trucking.
Cheap clothing and junk of every sort, as far as the eye could see.
The place had been cheap - just the little he had left after Amelia's burial - and it would serve its purpose.

Synset Examples

it would have been cheap at twice the price
inexpensive family restaurants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexpensive - low in price
  • Antonym: expensive - costing a lot of money
  • Degree Lower: affordable - able to be afforded; not too expensive

Etymology

From Old English cēap (a bargain, sale, price), related to 'to buy' and 'purchase'.

2. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
tastelessly showy or lacking in good taste; gaudy or flashy in a way that seems of little value

Examples

Claire felt suddenly small and cheap, heroine of a trivial episode in the voluminous history of Don Juan.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - too brightly colored in a way that lacks good taste
  • Synonym: flashy - bright, noticeable and likely to attract attention, often lacking in good taste
  • Antonym: classy - stylish and sophisticated

3. ADJ

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
of very poor quality or lacking value; flimsy or inferior

Examples

Perhaps it is only an analogy, but one of the most obvious differences between cheap fiction and fiction of an enduring quality is the development of a theme or story with leisure and anticipation.
I thought you were some cheap hustler Johnny was running in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferior - of low or lower quality
  • Synonym: shoddy - badly made or done; inferior quality
  • Antonym: expensive - costing a lot of money; usually higher quality
  • Antonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially at a high cost

Etymology

From Old English cēap (a bargain, trade), later developing the sense of 'low in value or quality'.
