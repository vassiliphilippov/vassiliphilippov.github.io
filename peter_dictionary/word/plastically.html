plastically

1. ADV

/ ˈplæstɪkli / (AmE), / ˈplæstɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that allows for shaping, molding, or being formed; with pliability or flexibility.

Examples

his quill drawings had a scratchiness, suggesting skin texture; the clay he used plastically to suggest soft moving flesh, as in an abdomen, in a reclining torso; the wax he smoothed over to give the body surface an elastic pull.

Synset Examples

he used the clay plastically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elastically - in an elastic or springy manner

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-ally', after Latin 'plasticus' and Greek 'plastikos' (to mold).
