Palestine

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæləstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈpælɪstiːn / (BrE)
A historical region on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean, formerly a British mandate, which was partitioned between Jordan and Israel in 1948.

Examples

In his heart he had no desire to go to Palestine.
Only Palestine, and I will never live to see Palestine because I did not believe.

Being somewhat delicate in health, at the age of sixteen he was sent to Southern Europe, for which he at once developed a passion, so that he spent nearly all of the following ten years abroad, at first in Italy, then in Greece, Egypt, Asia Minor, and Palestine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Israel - country established in part of the former British Mandate of Palestine in 1948
  • Degree Lower: Jordan - country adjacent to and formerly part of British Mandate of Palestine/Transjordan

Etymology

From Latin 'Palaestina', from Greek 'Palaistinē', originally referring to the land of the Philistines.
