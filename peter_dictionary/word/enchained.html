enchained

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈtʃeɪnd / (AmE), / ɛnˈtʃeɪnd / (BrE)
bound or held with chains; confined as if by chains

Examples

Clumps of brush that they passed were so many enchained demons straining in anger to tear and gnaw on his bones.

Synset Examples

enchained demons strained in anger to gnaw on his bones
prisoners in chains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chained - tied or fastened with chains
  • Antonym: unchained - not bound or fastened by chains

Etymology

From en- (prefix meaning 'in, into') + chain (from Old French 'chaine') + -ed (past participle ending).
