senior

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːniɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə / (BrE)
A student in the final year of high school or college, especially in the United States.

Examples

A 208 - pound, 6 - foot 1 - inch senior from Stamford, Moritz practices nothing but place-kicking.
Since the hero, a sterling and upright fellow, is a rich Brown senior, while two Yalies are cast as virtual rapists, I suppose I should disqualify myself from sitting in judgment on "Where the Boys are ", but I shall do nothing of the sort.
Bobby Joe would be a senior this year, and he planned to graduate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: junior - a student in the next-to-last year of high school or college
  • Degree Lower: sophomore - a student in the second year of high school or college
  • Degree Lower: freshman - a student in the first year of high school or college

Etymology

From Latin 'senior', comparative of 'senex' meaning 'old'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːniɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə / (BrE)
relating to the fourth and final year of high school or college in the United States

Examples

However, in their junior and senior years, they generally forego their athletic pursuits, presumably in the interest of better academic achievement.

Synset Examples

the senior prom

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: junior - a student in the third year of high school or college in the US

Etymology

From Latin 'senior', comparative of 'senex' meaning 'old'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsiːniər / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə / (BrE)
having higher status or rank or having served longer in a position or organization; older in age or experience compared to others in the same group

Examples

In 1927 his father's business collapsed, and, rather than go bankrupt, Mercer senior turned his firm over to a bank for liquidation.
I tried to do so by calling to their attention some of the problems that a senior departmental policy officer faces.
The senior policy officer may be moved to think hard about a problem by any of an infinite variety of stimuli: an idea in his own head, the suggestions of a colleague, a question from the Secretary or the President, a proposal by another department, a communication from a foreign government or an American ambassador abroad, the filing of an item for the agenda of the United Nations or of any other of dozens of international bodies, a news item read at the breakfast table, a question to the President or the Secretary at a news conference, a speech by a Senator or Congressman, an article in a periodical, a resolution from a national organization, a request for assistance from some private American interests abroad, et cetera, ad infinitum.

Synset Examples

senior officer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: junior - younger or of lower rank or shorter service

Etymology

From Latin 'senior', comparative of 'senex' meaning 'old'.
