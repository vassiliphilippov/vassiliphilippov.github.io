tangled

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈtaŋɡəld / (BrE)
Extremely complicated or intricate, often in a way that is difficult to understand or unravel.

Examples

After Larkin has been persuaded to restock his tangled acres with pheasants, he poaches only what he needs for the nourishment of his family and local callers.

Synset Examples

the Byzantine tax structure
Byzantine methods for holding on to his chairmanship
convoluted legal language
convoluted reasoning
the plot was too involved
a knotty problem
got his way by labyrinthine maneuvering
Oh, what a tangled web we weave
tortuous legal procedures
tortuous negotiations lasting for months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - complicated; made up of many interconnected parts
  • Antonym: simple - easy to understand or deal with; not complicated

Etymology

From Middle English *tanglen* (to entangle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈtaŋɡəld / (BrE)
Twisted together in a disordered mass so that it is hard to separate or unravel.

Examples

A hay wagon moved slowly along the gutter, the top of it swept by the low boughs of the maple trees, and loose straws were left hanging tangled among the leaves.

Synset Examples

pushed back her tangled hair
the tangled ropes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untangled - not twisted together; separated

Etymology

From the verb 'tangle', likely from Middle English *tanglen*, frequentative of 'tang' meaning to entangle.
