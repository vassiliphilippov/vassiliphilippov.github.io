coyness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of feigning shyness or modesty, often in a manner intended to be alluring or provocative

Examples

"I 've seen you, though, before ", she said with enormous coyness, patting his knee.
This was delivered in a forthright way, without coyness and over pretended interest - an admirable way with children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modesty - the quality of being modest; freedom from vanity, boastfulness, etc.
  • Synonym: shyness - the quality or state of being shy
  • Antonym: boldness - willingness to take risks or act innovatively; opposite in manner to coyness

Etymology

From Middle English 'coie' (quiet, shy) + '-ness'.
