bidder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪdər / (AmE), / ˈbɪdə / (BrE)
a person or organization that offers to pay a particular amount of money for something, especially at an auction

Examples

Upon the expiration of a period deemed adequate for demonstration purposes for each plant, but not to exceed such twelve year period, the Secretary shall proceed as promptly as practicable to dispose of any plants so constructed by sale to the highest bidder, or as may otherwise be directed by Act of Congress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offeror - person who makes an offer
  • Antonym: seller - person who sells something, sometimes acting as counterpart to bidder

Etymology

From 'bid' (to offer a price) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
