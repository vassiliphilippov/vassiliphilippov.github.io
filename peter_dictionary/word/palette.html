palette

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælət / (BrE)
A thin board or slab on which an artist lays and mixes paints; also, the range of colors used by a particular artist or in a particular work.

Examples

But even on these, the palette often takes over while the shotgun cools off!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color scheme - an arrangement or combination of colors, especially as used in interior decoration or art

Etymology

From French 'palette' (small shovel, palette), diminutive of 'pale' (shovel), from Latin 'pala' (spade).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælət / (BrE)
the range of colors used or chosen by a particular artist, painting, or school of art

Examples

"My usual palette consists of top-quality colors:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color scheme - a planned combination of colors

Etymology

From French *palette* (small shovel, palette), from Old French *pale* (shovel blade), from Latin *pala* (spade, shovel).
