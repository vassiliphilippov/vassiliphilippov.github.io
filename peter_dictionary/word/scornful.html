scornful

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɔrnfəl / (AmE), / ˈskɔːnfəl / (BrE)
showing or expressing strong contempt or disdain for someone or something

Examples

And if he is so scornful of the rights of states, why not advocate a different sort of constitution that he could more sincerely support?
Papa pointed a scornful finger at the splashing youth: "Idle recreation "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuous - showing or expressing contempt; disdainful
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness or deference to others

Etymology

From 'scorn' + '-ful', with 'scorn' from Old French 'escarn'.
