conditional

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
Subject to one or more conditions or requirements being met; not absolute.

Examples

Twenty-five thousand to each of the great-nieces in Oregon (not much to blood relatives out of millions) ten thousand to this friend and that, five thousand to another; to Brian Thayer, the sum of ten thousand dollars; to the Pecks, ten thousand each; to Joan Sheldon the conditional bequest of ten thousand to be paid to her in the event that she was still in Mrs. Meeker's employ at the time of the latter's death.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditional - not subject to any conditions or limitations

Etymology

From Late Latin conditionalis, from Latin conditio (agreement, condition).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈdɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
subject to one or more conditions; not absolute

Examples

Promise me, Red, if you ever get out, find that spot.

Synset Examples

conditional acceptance of the terms
lent conditional support
the conditional sale will not be complete until the full purchase price is paid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditional - not subject to any conditions

Etymology

From Latin 'condicionalis', from 'condicio' (agreement, stipulation).
