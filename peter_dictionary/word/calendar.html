calendar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
A system for organizing and dividing time into years, months, and days, often for keeping track of events or dates.

Examples

The Senate quickly whipped through its meager fare of House bills approved by committees, passing the three on the calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronology - the arrangement of events in the order of their occurrence

Etymology

From Latin 'calendarium' (account book), from 'calendae' (the first day of the month).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ən.də / (BrE)
a written or digital schedule listing planned events, appointments, or activities

Examples

But if you keep a calendar of events, as we do, you noticed a conflict.

Synset Examples

I have you on my calendar for next Monday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - a plan listing events or times when things will happen
  • Synonym: agenda - a list of things to be done or discussed

Etymology

From Latin 'calendarium' (account book), from 'calendae' (the first day of the month).
