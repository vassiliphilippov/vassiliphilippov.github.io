modernism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdərˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənɪzəm / (BrE)
A style or movement in art, literature, and culture marked by a deliberate break from traditional forms and an emphasis on innovation and new techniques, especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

To the Traditionalists, it was a brilliant satire on modernism; to the Neo Classicists, it was a challenge to the pre-war world.
He and also Mr. Cowley and Mr. Warren have fallen to the temptation which besets many of us to read into our authors - Nathaniel Hawthorne, for example, and Herman Melville - protests against modernism, material progress, and science which are genuine protests of our own but may not have been theirs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: postmodernism - a movement in art and literature reacting against modernism
  • Antonym: classicist - advocate of classicism, traditional artistic principles

Etymology

From modern + -ism; first used in the late 19th century.
