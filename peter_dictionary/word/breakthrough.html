breakthrough

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌθru / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌθruː / (BrE)
a sudden, important development or discovery, especially one that leads to progress

Examples

The specific analogy to the dilemma of love is the problem of the "breakthrough "in the realm of art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discovery - the act of finding or learning something for the first time
  • Synonym: advance - a forward step or improvement; progress
  • Antonym: setback - an unfortunate delay or reverse in progress

Etymology

From break + through, referring to breaking through a barrier or obstacle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌθru / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌθruː / (BrE)
An important development or discovery that brings significant progress, especially in science, technology, or understanding.

Examples

Furthermore, unexpectedly rapid progress or a technological break-through on any one weapon system, in itself, often diminishes the relative importance of other competitive systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - progress or a move forward, especially in knowledge or achievement
  • Antonym: setback - an event that delays progress or reverses achievement

Etymology

From break + through, originally referring to making a gap in enemy lines; later extended to mean a significant advance in knowledge or technology.
