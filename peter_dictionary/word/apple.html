apple

1. NOUN

/ ˈæp.əl / (AmE), / ˈæp.əl / (BrE)
A round fruit with red, yellow, or green skin and firm, crisp, sweet to tart white flesh, eaten raw or used in cooking.

Examples

After a minute he went on, "People must think the curse is on me, seeing you fresh as an apple and me old and gray ".
It would be a shame, Mr. Chairman, if we allowed a few rotten apples to give a bad name to the whole barrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pear - a fruit with a rounded base tapering to a stalk, closely related to the apple

Etymology

Old English æppel; related to Dutch appel and German Apfel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpəl / (AmE), / ˈæpəl / (BrE)
A tree of the species Malus domestica, widely grown for its firm, rounded edible fruit.

Examples

A horse apple. - Bullshit. - No, horse shit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit produced by the apple tree

Etymology

Old English æppel, of Germanic origin.
