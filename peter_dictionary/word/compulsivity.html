compulsivity

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpʌlˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / kəmˌpʌlˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being compulsive; a tendency to perform acts repeatedly or without control, often as a response to urges or anxiety

Examples

The rating scale of compulsivity was constructed by first perusing the interview records, categorizing all evidence related to compulsivity, then arranging a distribution of such information apart from the case records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsiveness - the quality of being compulsive; tendency to act compulsively
  • Antonym: impulsivity - tendency to act on impulse without thinking

Etymology

From 'compulsive' + '-ity', ultimately from Latin 'compellere' (to drive together).
