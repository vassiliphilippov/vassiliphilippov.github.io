tweedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtwiːdi / (AmE), / ˈtwiːdi / (BrE)
Having the rough texture or appearance of tweed cloth, or resembling the style typically associated with those who wear tweed.

Examples

You look like a tweedy Englishman.

Synset Examples

a sweater knitted of nubbly homespun yarns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homespun - having a rough, homemade texture (especially of cloth)
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface; not rough

Etymology

From 'tweed', a type of rough, woolen cloth, plus the suffix '-y'.
