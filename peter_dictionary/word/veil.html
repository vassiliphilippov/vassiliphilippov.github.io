veil

1. VERB

/ veɪl / (AmE), / veɪl / (BrE)
to cover, obscure, or conceal something as if with a veil; to make unclear or hidden

Examples

There seems to be almost a conspiracy of silence veiling it.
And if our gospel also is veiled, it is veiled only to those who are perishing.
And if our gospel also is veiled, it is veiled only to those who are perishing.

Synset Examples

women in Afghanistan veil their faces

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unveil - to reveal or make visible; to remove a covering or obscurity

Etymology

From Old French 'veile', from Latin 'velum' (a sail, curtain, covering).

2. NOUN

/ veɪl / (AmE), / veɪl / (BrE)
A piece of fine material worn by women to cover the head or face, especially for religious, cultural, or ceremonial reasons.

Examples

In particular he sought the gentle, sweet-faced nuns, with head coverings and veils coming to the middle of their foreheads, remembering their expressions until he reached home and set them down on paper.
It bulks under a veil of thin, new grass, like some embarrassing fact of physicalness, and I think Mrs. Pastern set out the statuary to soften its meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headscarf - a square of fabric worn over the head and often around the neck
  • Synonym: burqa - a garment covering the whole body with a mesh screen to cover the face, worn by some Muslim women
  • Synonym: mask - a covering for all or part of the face, typically used as protection or disguise

Etymology

From Old French 'veile', from Latin 'velum' (a sail, curtain or covering).
