childishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldɪʃnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of behaving in a manner typical of a child, especially showing immaturity or lack of sophistication.

Examples

Stowey Rummel was internationally famous, a crafter of a genuine Americana in foreign eyes, an original designer whose inventive childishness with steel and concrete was made even more believably sincere by his personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childlikeness - the quality of being like a child in a positive or innocent way
  • Antonym: maturity - the state of being mature or fully developed

Etymology

From 'childish' + suffix '-ness', indicating a state or quality.
