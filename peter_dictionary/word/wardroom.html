wardroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrdˌrum / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdˌruːm / (BrE)
the dining and recreation quarters for officers (other than the captain) on a warship

Examples

It was fantastic to turn from the seven men in shackles to the wardroom, where a class of apprentices awaited him.

Etymology

From ward (a division or area) + room; originally naval usage.
