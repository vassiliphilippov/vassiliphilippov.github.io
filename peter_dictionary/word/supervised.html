supervised

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.vaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.vaɪzd / (BrE)
being under observation or guidance by an authority or supervisor

Examples

Our inmates, properly supervised, will be put to work outside these walls, performing all manner of public service.

Synset Examples

supervised play

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsupervised - not under observation or guidance
  • Synonym: overseen - watched or supervised by someone

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'supervise', from Latin 'supervidere', meaning 'oversee'.
