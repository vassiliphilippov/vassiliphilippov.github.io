crusade

1. NOUN

/ kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / kruːˈseɪd / (BrE)
a vigorous campaign for a cause or against an issue, often involving sustained effort to promote or achieve a principle

Examples

One of the ironies of the present crusade for Christian unity is that there are not, relatively speaking, many real Christians to unite.
The hands and their bosses saw him as a lone knight of the range, waging a dedicated crusade against a lawless new society that was threatening a beloved way of life.

Synset Examples

he supported populist campaigns
they worked in the cause of world peace
the team was ready for a drive toward the pennant
the movement to end slavery
contributed to the war effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - a series of organized activities towards a goal

Etymology

From French *croisade* (from Old French *croisé*, meaning 'marked with a cross'), originally referring to Christian military expeditions.

2. NOUN

/ kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / kruˈseɪd / (BrE)
Any of a series of religious wars from the 11th to 13th centuries in which Christian Europeans tried to capture the Holy Land from Muslim rule.

Examples

The great ultimate ends of religion have served as magnificent beacon lights that lured people toward them with an almost irresistible force, mobilizing energies and inducing sacrifices; for example, the Crusades, mission efforts, just wars.

Etymology

From French *croisade*, from Old French *croisée* (marked with a cross), from Medieval Latin *cruciata* (marked with a cross, crusade), from Latin *crux* (cross).
