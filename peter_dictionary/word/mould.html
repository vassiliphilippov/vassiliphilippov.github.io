mould

1. VERB

/ moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
to shape a substance such as clay or wax into a particular form

Examples

To what extent did it mould the morals and the social, economic, and political life and institutions of the country?

Synset Examples

model a head with clay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - to shape or form something in a particular way, often artistically
  • Synonym: sculpt - to carve or model material into a three-dimensional figure

Etymology

From Middle English 'mold' (model), from Old French 'modle', from Latin 'modulus' (a small measure, model).
