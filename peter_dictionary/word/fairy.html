fairy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri / (BrE)
A small imaginary being in human form, often depicted as having magical powers and a playful nature, found in folklore and children's stories.

Examples

Demons, fairies, angels, and a host of other spiritual beings were as much a part of the experiential world of western man as were rocks and trees and stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elf - a small, elusive supernatural being with magical powers, often mischievous
  • Synonym: pixie - a small playful fairy-like creature in folklore

Etymology

From Old French *faerie* (enchantment, fairyland), which is from Latin *fāta* (the Fates, goddesses of destiny).
