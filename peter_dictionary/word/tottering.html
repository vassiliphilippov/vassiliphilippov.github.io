tottering

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑːtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɒtərɪŋ / (BrE)
walking or moving in an unsteady or shaky way, especially due to weakness, infirmity, or old age.

Examples

his scrawny, tottering sound adds a delightful hilarity to the performance.)

Synset Examples

a tottering skeleton of a horse
a tottery old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tottery - unsteady and likely to collapse or fall

Etymology

From the verb 'totter', likely from Middle English *toteren*, of imitative origin.
