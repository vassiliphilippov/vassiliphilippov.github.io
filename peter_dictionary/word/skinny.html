skinny

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪni / (AmE), / ˈskɪni / (BrE)
very thin, often in a way that is unattractive or unhealthy

Examples

Of course he did some exercising - he's crazy about water skiing and swimming and this vigorous exercise in conjunction with the added food supplements packed pounds of solid muscle on his skinny frame.
The covers slid down his skinny neck so I saw his head, fuzzed like a dandelion gone to seed, but his face was turned to the wall - there was the pale shadow of his nose on the plaster - and I thought, Well you do n't look much like a pig-drunk bully now.
You skinny, spoiled brat.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thin - having little fat or flesh; not thick
  • Synonym: slender - gracefully thin
  • Synonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • Antonym: overweight - weighing more than is considered healthy or attractive
  • Antonym: chubby - slightly fat in a way that people usually find attractive
  • Antonym: fat - having a lot of excess flesh

Etymology

Perhaps from 'skin' + '-y', indicating 'like skin' with little flesh.
