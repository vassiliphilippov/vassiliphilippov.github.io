blushing

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) having a red face because of embarrassment, shame, or strong emotion.

Examples

The morning he walked in to announce to her, blushing, that he was married.

Synset Examples

the blushing boy was brought before the Principal
her blushful beau
was red-faced with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flushed - red in the face, often from excitement or heat

Etymology

Present participle of 'blush', from Old English *blyscan* (to blush, be ashamed).
