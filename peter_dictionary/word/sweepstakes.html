sweepstakes

1. NOUN

/ ˈswipˌsteɪks / (AmE), / ˈswiːpsteɪks / (BrE)
A lottery in which the prize consists of all the money paid by the participants.

Examples

That's like winning the sweepstakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lottery - a game of chance in which tickets are sold and prizes are drawn at random

Etymology

From 'sweep', in sense of taking all, and 'stakes', referring to the money risked; originally used in 18th-century horse racing.
