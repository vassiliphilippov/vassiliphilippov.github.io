bottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl / (BrE)
A glass or plastic container, typically cylindrical with a narrow neck and no handles, used for storing drinks or other liquids and usually closed with a cap or stopper.

Examples

His unsuccessful strivings to give up drink are represented as religious strivings; he keeps a bottle in a wardrobe at home, and "before this wardrobe Praisegod Piepsam had before now gone literally on his knees, and in his wrestlings had bitten his tongue - and still in the end capitulated ".
The argon flow from commercial bottles was regulated by a pressure regulator and measured with a gas flow rator.
The bottle was filled up with flour.
A cold supper was ordered and a bottle of port.
She had surprised Hans like she had surprised me when she said she 'd go, and then she surprised him again when she came back so quick like she must have, because when I came in with the snow she was there with a bottle with three white feathers on its label and Hans was holding it angrily by the throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flask - a small container (often with a narrow neck) used to hold liquids, typically for drinking
  • Synonym: jar - a wide-mouthed container used for holding liquids or food
  • Synonym: can - a metal container for liquids, typically with a larger opening than a bottle

Etymology

From Old French *botel*, from medieval Latin *butticula*, diminutive of *buttis* (cask, wineskin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
an amount of liquid or other substance as contained in a bottle

Examples

A news item described the launching of a ship: "Completing the ceremony, the beautiful movie star smashed a bottle of champagne over her stern as she slid gracefully down the ways into the sea ".
One said, "When I get a cold I buy a bottle of whiskey for it, and within a few hours it's gone ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle - a glass or plastic container for liquids, typically with a narrow neck
