cache

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
A hidden storage space for valuables such as money, supplies, or weapons.

Examples

Carmer's ingenious cache for his loot had been found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - a stock or store of valuables, typically kept secret

Etymology

From French 'cache' (hiding place), from cacher (to hide), from Vulgar Latin *coacticare (to press together).
