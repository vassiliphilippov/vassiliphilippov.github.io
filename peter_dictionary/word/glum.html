glum

1. ADJ

/ ɡlʌm / (AmE), / ɡlʌm / (BrE)
looking or feeling sad, disappointed, or dejected; moody and melancholic.

Examples

They were silent for a little while, each looking glum.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: morose - sullen, gloomy, or ill-tempered
  • Degree Higher: depressed - in a state of unhappiness or despondency
  • Synonym: downcast - feeling despondent or discouraged

Etymology

Perhaps related to Scots 'glum' (frown, sulk) and Middle English 'gloum' (to look sullen).
