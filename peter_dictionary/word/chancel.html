chancel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.səl / (BrE)
The part of a church near the altar, reserved for the clergy and choir, often separated from the nave by a screen or railing.

Examples

Naked girls danced in the chancel of the Abbey, the youngest and seemingly the most innocent being chosen to read a sermon filled with veiled depravities.

Etymology

From Old French 'chancel', from Latin 'cancellus' (lattice, screen).
