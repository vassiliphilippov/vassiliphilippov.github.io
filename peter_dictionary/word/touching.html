touching

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
causing strong feelings of tenderness or sympathy; arousing deep emotion, especially in a gentle or poignant way.

Examples

These trumps were more touching than they were anything else, and seemed to imply that the nights were long, her children ungrateful, and her marriage bewilderingly threadbare.
Especially touching is the chapter, "The Little Sister ", about a king's daughter who became a nun in the convent of St. Birgitta.

Synset Examples

the homecoming of the released hostages was an affecting scene
poignant grief cannot endure forever
his gratitude was simple and touching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moving - causing strong emotion, especially sadness or sympathy
  • Synonym: poignant - evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret
  • Synonym: affecting - producing strong feelings of sadness or sympathy
