immaculate

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmækjələt / (AmE), / ɪˈmækjʊlət / (BrE)
perfectly clean, neat, or free from any dirt or stain.

Examples

McFeeley noted the immaculate lawn and gardens: each blade of grass cropped, bright and firm; each shrub glazed with good health.

Synset Examples

the apartment was immaculate
in her immaculate white uniform
a spick-and-span kitchen
their spic red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotless - completely clean; without a spot or stain
  • Antonym: filthy - extremely dirty; covered with filth

Etymology

From Latin immaculatus, from in- (not) + maculatus (stained).
