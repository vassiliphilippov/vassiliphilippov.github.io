elevation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The angular distance of an object, especially a celestial body, above the horizon, measured in degrees.

Examples

Check the elevation of the ground, degree and direction of slopes, drainage, rock outcrops, topsoil types and quality, as well as subsoil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altitude - height above a reference point, especially sea level; in astronomy, often used similarly to 'elevation'

Etymology

From Latin elevatio (a raising, lifting up), from elevare (to raise).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of being raised to a higher position, level, or rank

Examples

The glorious news had been held up pending Heavenly confirmation of the elevation of a new Supreme Bishop, Huey Short - a candidate accepted by the Boone faction after lots had been cast repeatedly.
L'Unita and Hoy published identical denunciations of Short's elevation, l'Osservatore Romano and the Christian Science Monitor ignored it, Times of India snickered at it, and the Manchester Guardian simply reported it - the Fosterites in England were few but extremely militant.

Synset Examples

an elevation of the temperature in the afternoon
a raising of the land resulting from volcanic activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promotion - the act of advancing someone to a higher rank or position
  • Antonym: demotion - the act of moving someone to a lower rank or position

Etymology

From Latin elevatio (a raising up), from elevare (to raise).
