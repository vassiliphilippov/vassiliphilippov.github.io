personality

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɝːsəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
a person who is well known or notable in a particular field, especially in entertainment, sport, or the media

Examples

A crowd of 1400 is expected for the ceremonies, which will be followed by the show in which the writers will lampoon baseball personalities in skit, dance and song.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment

Etymology

From Latin personalitas (‘characteristic of a person’), via Old French personalité.

Related Terms

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'personalitas', from Latin 'persona' (character, role).
