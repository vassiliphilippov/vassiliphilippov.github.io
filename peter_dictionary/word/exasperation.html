exasperation

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzæspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɑːspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of intense irritation or annoyance.

Examples

At the end of the room there was a desk heaped with papers, and she began to riffle these, making sighs and and noises of girlish exasperation.
He straightened up, ready to vent his exasperation, then grew afraid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: annoyance - a feeling of slight anger or irritation

Etymology

From Latin exasperatio (roughness, irritation), from exasperare (to irritate, provoke).
