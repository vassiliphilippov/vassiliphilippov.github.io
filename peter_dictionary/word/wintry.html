wintry

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪn.tɹi / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tɹi / (BrE)
Characteristic of or resembling winter; cold or bleak like winter.

Examples

He is driven back by his yearning to the wintry homeland of his fathers in the forest of Tiveden ".

Synset Examples

suffered severe wintry weather
brown wintry grasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintery - alternative spelling of 'wintry'
  • Antonym: summery - characteristic of or pertaining to summer
  • Degree Lower: chilly - uncomfortably or unpleasantly cold
  • Degree Higher: freezing - extremely cold, at or below the freezing point

Etymology

From Old English 'wintrig', from 'winter' + '-y'.
