ambulance

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.bjə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈæm.bjʊ.ləns / (BrE)
A vehicle equipped for transporting people who are ill or injured to and from hospitals.

Examples

Then an ambulance comes along, and they drive Praisegod Piepsam away.
The ambulance is drawn by two "charming "little horses.
"Oh, I forgot to say that if one is taken to the funny house in the funny wagon, he is removed to a mental institution in an ambulance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency vehicle - a vehicle used for emergency purposes, such as police cars, fire engines, and ambulances

Etymology

From French 'ambulance', originally meaning 'mobile field hospital', from 'ambulant' (walking, mobile).
