remote

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
very unlikely to happen; having a slim chance of occurring

Examples

There is still the remote possibility of planetoid collision.
The kiss outraged our friends but it was done and meanwhile had released in me all the remote, exciting premonitions of lust, all the mysterious sensations that I had imagined a truly consummated kiss would convey to me.
Moreover, as Communist power increases, the enjoyment of these conditions throughout the world diminishes pro rata and the possibility of their restoration becomes increasingly remote.
The remote, cloudy, possible has values of its own - values of scope, stimulus, potential, and imagination.

Synset Examples

an outside chance
a remote possibility
a remote contingency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true
  • Antonym: likely - having a good chance of happening or being true

Etymology

From Latin remotus (removed, distant), past participle of removere (to remove).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
(of a place) far from populated areas and difficult to access.

Examples

The cores of deep sea sediments and content of collectors in remote regions are valuable in this category.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other places or people
  • Synonym: secluded - (of a place) not seen or visited by many people
  • Antonym: accessible - easy to approach or enter

Etymology

From Latin remotus, past participle of removēre (to remove).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: near - located close in space; nearby
