journalese

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɜrnəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌdʒɜːnəˈliːz / (BrE)
the distinctive style of language and writing typically used in newspapers and journalism, often characterized by clichés and formulas

Examples

Not without good reason has the anatomical been called jocular journalese.
Apart from journalese and vaudeville gags, the anatomical is also found in jocular literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newspaperese - language characteristic of newspapers, especially in style or vocabulary

Etymology

Formed from 'journal' and the suffix '-ese', modeled after names of languages; meaning 'language of journalism'.
