jibe

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
an insulting or mocking remark; a taunt.

Examples

"All too frequently ", points out James O' Gara, managing editor of Commonweal, "Catholics run roughshod over Protestant sensibilities in this matter, by failure to consider the reasoning behind the Protestant position and, particularly, by their jibes at the fact that Protestant opinion on birth control has changed in recent decades ".

Synset Examples

his parting shot was `drop dead'
she threw shafts of sarcasm
she takes a dig at me every chance she gets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taunt - an insulting or mocking remark intended to provoke someone
  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

Probably from Old French giber (to handle roughly).
