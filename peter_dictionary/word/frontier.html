frontier

1. NOUN

/ frʌnˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.tɪə / (BrE)
an undeveloped region at the edge of a settled area, especially where civilization is expanding into wilderness

Examples

For the last two years, this frontier of the arts has produced a number of so-called "non dramas "which have left indelible, bittersweet impressions on the psyche of this veteran theatregoer.
The socialism implicit in the slogan of the Roosevelt Revolution, freedom from want and fear, seems a far cry from the individualism of the First Amendment to the Constitution, or of the Jacksonian frontier.

Synset Examples

the individualism of the frontier in Andrew Jackson's day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - the edge or boundary of something, especially a country
  • Synonym: boundary - a line that marks the limits of an area; a dividing line

Etymology

From Old French 'frontiere' (front, boundary), from 'front', from Latin 'frons' (forehead, front).
