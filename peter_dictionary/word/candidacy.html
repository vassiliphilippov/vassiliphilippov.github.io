candidacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændɪdəsi / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdəsi / (BrE)
the fact or state of being a candidate for a position, especially in an election; the period during which a person runs for office

Examples

What is interesting is that his positive qualifications for the post were revealed only as a kind of tail to his candidacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - an organized effort to win an election or achieve a goal
  • Synonym: nomination - the act of being formally proposed or entered as a candidate

Etymology

From candidate + -cy; candidate from Latin 'candidatus' (clothed in white).
