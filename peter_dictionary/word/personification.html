personification

1. NOUN

/ pərˌsɑnəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɒnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A person who embodies or represents a particular quality or idea.

Examples

He had made himself the personification of the Devil to the homesteaders.

Synset Examples

she is the personification of optimism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - a tangible or visible form of an idea, quality, or feeling
  • Synonym: incarnation - a person who embodies in the flesh a deity, spirit, or quality

Etymology

From French 'personnification' and Medieval Latin 'personificatio', from Latin 'persona' (person) + 'facere' (to make).
