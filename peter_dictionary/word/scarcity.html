scarcity

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛr.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
A situation in which something is in short supply; not enough of a resource to meet demand.

Examples

Scarcity of paper caused many Southerners to adopt the practice of cross writing, i. e., after writing from left to right of the page in the usual manner, they gave the sheet a half turn and wrote from end to end across the lines previously written.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Synonym: shortage - a state or situation in which something needed cannot be obtained in sufficient amounts

Etymology

From Old French 'escars' (scanty) + '-ity', from Latin 'scarus'.
