valor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælər / (AmE), / ˈvælə / (BrE)
Great courage, especially in the face of danger, often shown in battle.

Examples

Undoubtedly none of the residents realize the influence their town has had on American military history, or the deeds of valor that have been done in its name.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; timidity in facing danger

Etymology

From Latin 'valorem', accusative of 'valor' (value, worth, courage).
