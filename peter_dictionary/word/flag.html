flag

1. NOUN

/ flæɡ / (AmE), / flæɡ / (BrE)
a type of iris plant with sword-shaped leaves and erect stalks bearing bright, usually purple or yellow, flowers

Examples

Dead fledgling birds, their squashed looking nakedness and the odor of decay that clung to the hand when they had been buried in our graveyard in front of the purple flags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of plants with showy flowers and sword-shaped leaves

Etymology

From Middle English flagge (rush, reed), possibly of Scandinavian origin.

2. VERB

/ flæɡ / (AmE), / flæɡ / (BrE)
to communicate or signal with a flag, especially as a means of attracting attention or giving a warning

Examples

It flags such possible breakdowns of communication as rehearsed dialogue, the note of disapproval, ambivalence or ambiguity, annoyance, resentment, and the disinclination to speak at all - this last often marked by a fade-in beginning of sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to convey information or instructions by means of a gesture, action, or sound

Etymology

From Old Norse *flaga* (slab, patch), possibly related to the noun 'flag'.

3. VERB

/ flæɡ / (AmE), / flæɡ / (BrE)
to mark or identify something with or as if with a flag, especially to call attention to it or designate it for special treatment

Examples

Then she catapults into "everything and everybody ", putting particular violence on "everybody ", indicating to the linguist that this is a spot to flag - that is, it is not congruent to the patient's general style of speech up to this point.

Synset Examples

Flag this file so that I can recognize it immediately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - to designate or signal something for attention or action
  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make stand out for attention

Etymology

From Old English *flagge* (piece of cloth), related to the noun 'flag'.
