lag

1. VERB

/ læg / (AmE), / læg / (BrE)
to move or develop more slowly than others, so as to fall behind in progress, movement, or development

Examples

The Bears took the lead in the first inning, as they did in Sunday's opener, and never lagged.
At any rate, Manchester did not lag far behind the first commercial system which was set up in 1844 between Baltimore and Washington.
In general, however, the timetable of travel lags considerably behind that of the student at Harvard or Smith.
"Before then, my sales during much of the year had lagged behind 1960 by 20 % ", he says.
For the year to date, sales of the company's farm equipment dealers still lag about 5 % behind 1960.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall behind - to fail to keep up with others in movement or progress
  • Antonym: catch up - to reach the same level as someone or something that is ahead

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *leggen* (to lay, delay), influenced by Scandinavian languages (cf. Norwegian *lagga*).

2. NOUN

/ læɡ / (AmE), / læɡ / (BrE)
a period or act of delay, or the state of falling behind; a slowing down or gap in progress, development, or response

Examples

Of course, nationalism has really outlived its usefulness in a country as world oriented as ours, and its continued existence reflects one of the major culture lags of the twentieth century United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - the period of time by which something is late or postponed
  • Antonym: lead - a position ahead or the act of being in front of others

Etymology

From Old Norse *lagga* (to go slowly).
