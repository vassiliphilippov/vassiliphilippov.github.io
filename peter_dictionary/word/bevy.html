bevy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛvi / (AmE), / ˈbɛvi / (BrE)
a large group of people of a particular kind, often used to refer to women or girls, or sometimes a group of birds or animals

Examples

At once a bevy of dogs was snapping and snarling around him.
A bevy of interlacing lagers, work camps, concentration camps erupted in the area.

Synset Examples

he was surrounded by a bevy of beauties in bathing attire
a bevy of young beach boys swarmed around him

Etymology

From Middle English *bevie*, of uncertain origin, possibly referring first to a group of birds.
