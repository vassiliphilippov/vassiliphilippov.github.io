solidly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑː.lɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪd.li / (BrE)
In a way that is strong, stable, and well-constructed; with strength and soundness.

Examples

Felix threw his head back and laughed a laugh that shook the timbers of even that solidly built old house.

Synset Examples

a solidly built house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - in a way that is strong or not likely to move or change

2. ADV

/ ˈsɑlɪdli / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows unity or agreement; as a single undivided group.

Examples

Until 1924 O' Banion pistoleers and knuckle-duster bullyboys had kept his North Side domain solidly Democratic.

Synset Examples

the unions voted solidly for Roosevelt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unanimously - with full agreement by everyone involved

Etymology

From solid + -ly, formed in English.
