hackneyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæk.nid / (AmE), / ˈhak.nid / (BrE)
(of a phrase or idea) lacking significance or originality because it has been overused; unoriginal or trite.

Examples

The party at Floyd's penthouse gave the "chorines "a chance for a nostalgic frolic through all those hackneyed routines which have become a classic choreographic statement of the era's nonsense.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons
his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor `hard as nails'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
  • Synonym: banal - so lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring
  • Antonym: original - fresh and unusual; not used or seen before

Etymology

From the past participle of 'hackney', originally referring to a horse for hire; later, to something that is commonplace or worn out through overuse.
