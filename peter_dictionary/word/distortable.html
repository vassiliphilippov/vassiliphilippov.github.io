distortable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɔrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Capable of being distorted; able to be altered, changed, or twisted from an original form or meaning.

Examples

Information beyond nearest neighbor is carried second -, third -, and fourth hand as a distortable rumor.

Synset Examples

our words are distortable things--as in a crooked mirror held up to nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distort - to change the shape or meaning of something so that it is no longer true or correct

Etymology

From 'distort' + suffix '-able', meaning capable of being distorted.
