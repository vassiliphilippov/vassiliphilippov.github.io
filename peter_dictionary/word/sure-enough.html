sure-enough

1. ADJ

/ ʃʊr ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / ʃɔːr ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
Used informally, especially for emphasis, to mean real, genuine, or actual; not imitation or pretended.

Examples

But after the doctor's return that night Alex could see, from the high window in his own room, the now familiar figure crouched on a truly impressive heap of towels, apparently giving its egg hatching powers one final chance before it was replaced in its office by a sure-enough hen.

Synset Examples

a real honest-to-god live cowboy
had us a high old time
went upriver to look at a sure-enough fish wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: real - actually existing as a thing; not imagined or supposed
  • Antonym: fake - not genuine; imitation or counterfeit
