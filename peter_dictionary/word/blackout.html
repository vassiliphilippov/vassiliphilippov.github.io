blackout

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
a period during which radio or television broadcasting is suspended, often to comply with regulations or for security reasons

Examples

This rule providing for a blackout of televised baseball 30 minutes before the start of a major or minor league game in any area comes from the game's top rulers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit radio or television programs

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
A period of darkness resulting from the intentional extinguishing of artificial lights, especially to prevent enemy detection during wartime or as a safety precaution.

Examples

The giant electric signs and marquees were lit up for the first time since blackout regulations had been instituted, and the atmosphere was alive with the feeling that victory was just around the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lights-out - a time when lights must be turned off, especially by regulation

Etymology

From 'black' + 'out', originally referring to covering lights for security during air raids in World War II.
