technologically

1. ADV

/ ˌtɛk.nəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌtɛk.nəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or involves the use of technology.

Examples

Related to micelle formation is the technologically important ability of detergent actives to congregate at oil water interfaces in such a manner that the polar (or ionized) end of the molecule is directed towards the aqueous phase and the hydrocarbon chain towards the oily phase.

Synset Examples

technologically impossible

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: technologically advanced - having a high level of technological development
  • Degree Lower: technologically backward - having a low level of technological development

Etymology

From 'technological' + '-ly'. 'Technological' from Greek tekhnologia (systematic treatment of an art or craft).
