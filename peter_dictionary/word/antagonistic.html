antagonistic

1. ADJ

/ ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
showing or feeling active opposition or hostility toward someone or something

Examples

Children, conditioned by this mistaken notion, have feared stepmothers, while adults, by their antagonistic attitudes, have made the role of the substitute parents a difficult one.

Synset Examples

slaves antagonistic to their masters
antipathetic factions within the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostile - showing or feeling opposition or dislike
  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant, showing support

Etymology

From Ancient Greek antagonistikos (combative), from anti- (against) + agonizesthai (to struggle).

2. ADJ

/ ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
showing or feeling active opposition or hostility toward something or someone.

Examples

Perhaps it was his misfortune, or good fortune, whichever way one looked at it, to belong to the former group, and he was struggling unconsciously to build up pressure in a world which demanded none, which was positively antagonistic to it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostile - unfriendly; antagonistic
  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; the opposite of antagonistic

Etymology

From Greek antagōnistēs (opponent), from antagōnizesthai (to struggle against).
