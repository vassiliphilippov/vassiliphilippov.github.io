dispersal

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɝːsəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsəl / (BrE)
the act or process of spreading or scattering people, animals, or things over a wide area

Examples

It was the low yield of the Selkirk plots and the ravages of grasshoppers in 1818 that led to the dispersal of the settlement southward.

Synset Examples

the dispersion of the troops
the diffusion of knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersion - the process of distributing things or people over a wide area
  • Antonym: concentration - the process of bringing things together in one place

Etymology

From Latin 'dispersus', past participle of 'dispergere' (to scatter).
