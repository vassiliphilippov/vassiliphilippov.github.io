tepid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛpɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛpɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) only slightly warm; lukewarm.

Examples

He found tepid water in a pitcher and a last bit of soap, and he lathered his face and stood stropping the razor on his broad leather belt, its buckle held firm by a knob of the bedpost.

Synset Examples

he hates lukewarm coffee
tepid bath water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lukewarm - only moderately warm; not hot
  • Degree Higher: warm - having a moderate degree of heat
  • Degree Lower: cold - lacking warmth; having a low temperature

Etymology

From Latin 'tepidus' (warm, lukewarm), from 'tepēre' (to be warm).
