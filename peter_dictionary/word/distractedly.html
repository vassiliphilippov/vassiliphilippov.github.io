distractedly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstræktɪdli / (AmE), / dɪˈstraktɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows you are not concentrating because you are worried or thinking about something else.

Examples

Ace of spades - a widow, that was what they called a widow, these low-class crooks remembered Mr. Skyros distractedly.

Synset Examples

`Come in,' he said distractedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absentmindedly - in a manner showing lack of attention or awareness
  • Antonym: attentively - in a manner showing attention or focus

Etymology

From 'distracted' + '-ly'. 'Distracted' from Latin 'distringere' (to draw apart).
