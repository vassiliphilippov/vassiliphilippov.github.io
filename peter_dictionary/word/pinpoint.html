pinpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a very brief or precise moment in time

Examples

Sensing the unseen presence of the other men in the patrol, he felt mutely united to these nine near strangers sharing this pinpoint of being with him.

Synset Examples

they were strangers sharing a pinpoint of time together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instant - a brief moment in time
  • Synonym: flash - a sudden, very short moment

Etymology

From 'pin' and 'point', originally referring to the sharp tip of a pin.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
To find or say the exact position or cause of something.

Examples

Solutions frequently suggest themselves when you accurately pinpoint your problems, whether they be in the market, in marketing methods or in marketing management.
I think it is essential, however, to pinpoint here the difference between the two concepts of sovereignty that went to war in 1861 - if only to see better how imperative is our need today to clarify completely our far worse confusion on this subject.
It is difficult to pinpoint the time of origin of the state purchasing automobiles for use of employees in Rhode Island.

Synset Examples

can you pinpoint the position of the enemy?
The chemists could not nail the identity of the chromosome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identify - to establish or indicate who or what someone or something is
  • Synonym: locate - to discover the exact place or position of something

Etymology

From the noun 'pinpoint', referring to the point of a pin; extended to mean exactness.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a very small spot or dot; a tiny point

Examples

The shapes were unmistakable and the Rising Suns were showing up, slightly brighter pinpoints in the gray gloom.

Synset Examples

the plane was just a speck in the sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a tiny spot or small particle
  • Synonym: dot - a small round mark or spot

Etymology

From 'pin' + 'point', originally referring to the sharp end of a pin.
