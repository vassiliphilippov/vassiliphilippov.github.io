phonetics

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the sounds of human speech, including their production, transmission, and perception

Examples

Everything from poetry to phonetics, history to histrionics, philosophy to party games has been adapted to the turntable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the system and pattern of sounds in a language
  • Synonym: acoustics - the branch of physics concerned with the properties of sound

Etymology

From Greek phōnētikos (vocal), from phōnē (voice, sound).
