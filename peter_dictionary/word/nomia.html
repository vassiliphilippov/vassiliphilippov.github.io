nomia

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊmiə / (AmE), / ˈnəʊmiə / (BrE)
A genus of bees in the family Halictidae, some species of which are important pollinators of legumes.

Examples

Closely related to the andrenas are the nomias or alkali bees.
I must plead guilty to a special sympathy for nomias.

Etymology

From New Latin Nomia, the genus name, likely from Greek nomia (pasture, grazing).
