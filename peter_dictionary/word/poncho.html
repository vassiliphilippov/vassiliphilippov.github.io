poncho

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑntʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒntʃəʊ / (BrE)
A garment made from a large piece of cloth with a hole in the center for the head, typically worn as a cloak and often associated with traditional South American clothing.

Examples

He saw the figure of a man in a poncho coming up the company street, with an armful of wood.
Adam watched the moisture flow from the poncho.
The head was wrapped in a turban and on top of the turban rode a great hamper across which a piece of poncho had been flung.

Etymology

From Spanish 'poncho', of Mapuche origin.
