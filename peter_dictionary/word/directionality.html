directionality

1. NOUN

/ dəˌrɛkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / daɪˌrɛkʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being more sensitive or responsive to signals coming from a particular direction, especially in microphones or antennas

Examples

Directionality is greatly exaggerated most of the time; but when the sounds of the two speakers are allowed to mix, there is excellent depth and dimension to the music.

Synset Examples

the antenna received signals with no directivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directivity - the property of favoring signals from certain directions

Etymology

From 'direction' + '-ality', modeled after 'directional'.
