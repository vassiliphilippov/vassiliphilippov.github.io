subpoena

1. VERB

/ səˈpinə / (AmE), / sʌˈpiːnə / (BrE)
(law) To officially order someone to appear in court or produce evidence by serving them with a subpoena.

Examples

After reading his statement discharging the 23 d ward case, Karns told Wexler that if the seven cases scheduled for trial also involved persons who had been subpoenaed, he would dismiss them.

Synset Examples

The witness and her records were subpoenaed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summon - to order someone to be present, especially in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin *sub poena* ('under penalty').

2. NOUN

/ səˈpinə / (AmE), / səˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A legal document ordering someone to attend a court proceeding as a witness; failure to comply may result in penalties for contempt of court.

Examples

He'’s not under subpoena and his reputation in his own country is impeccable.

Etymology

From Latin 'sub poena' meaning 'under penalty'.
