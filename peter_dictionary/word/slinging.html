slinging

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of throwing something with a wide sweeping motion, as if using a sling.

Examples

Another Yankee became so disgusted as to state: "I wish to God one half of our officers were knocked in the head by slinging them against [ the other half ] ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throwing - propelling something with force through the air by a movement of the arm

Etymology

From the verb 'sling', which derives from Old Norse 'slyngva' (to hurl, throw).
