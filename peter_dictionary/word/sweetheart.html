sweetheart

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈswiːthɑːt / (BrE)
A person with whom one is in a romantic relationship; a loved one.

Examples

This is when his troubles begin, not to mention a fledgling artist who he hires, and who turns out to have ideas of his own, with particular respect to the hero's sweetheart secretary.
A Yankee sergeant gave the following description of his sweetheart: "My girl is none of your one-horse girls.
Unmarried soldiers carried on correspondence with sweethearts at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person dearly loved
  • Synonym: lover - a person who is in a romantic relationship with another
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile to someone

Etymology

From Middle English 'swete hert', literally 'sweet heart'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈswiːthɑːt / (BrE)
A person who is liked very much by others; often used as a term of endearment for someone dear or kind.

Examples

She was such a well-rounded teenager, having been a twirler, Future Farmers sweetheart, and secretary of Future Homemakers.
In her sophomore year she had started going steady with Bobby Joe, who was a football player, Future Homemakers sweetheart, and president of Future Farmers.
"You 're a sweetheart - but leave one line open.

Synset Examples

he's a sweetheart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a dearly loved person
  • Synonym: beloved - a person who is dearly loved
  • Antonym: enemy - a person actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Middle English swete herte, literally 'sweet heart'.
