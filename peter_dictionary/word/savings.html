savings

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋz / (BrE)
Money that has been set aside, typically in a bank or similar institution, for future use rather than spent immediately.

Etymology

From 'save' + '-ing' + plural '-s', meaning things saved.
