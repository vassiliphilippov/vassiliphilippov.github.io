forefather

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
A male ancestor, especially one who founded a family, group, or nation.

Examples

Its refrain was: "Let us return to the individualistic democracy of our forefathers for our salvation ".

Synset Examples

keep the faith of our forefathers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Antonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Middle English 'forfader', from Old English 'forefæder' (fore + father).
