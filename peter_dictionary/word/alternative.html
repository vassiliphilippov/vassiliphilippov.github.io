alternative

1. ADJ

/ ælˈtɝː.nə.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ɔːlˈtɜː.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of things, plans, or possibilities) offering or necessitating a choice between two or more mutually exclusive options.

Examples

The largest hurdle the Republicans would have to face is a state law which says that before making a first race, one of two alternative courses must be taken:

Synset Examples

alternative possibilities were neutrality or war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - the act of selecting among options

Etymology

From Latin 'alternativus' (interchanging), from 'alternare' (do by turns).

2. NOUN

/ ɔlˈtɝː.nə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɒlˈtɜː.nə.tɪv / (BrE)

3. ADJ

/ ɔlˈtɜrnətɪv / (AmE), / ɔːlˈtɜːnətɪv / (BrE)
(of one thing) available as another possibility or used in place of something else.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or serving in place of another

Etymology

From Latin alternare (to do by turns), from alternus (one after another).
