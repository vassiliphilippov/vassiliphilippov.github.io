logically

1. ADV

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is consistent with the principles of logic or reason.

Examples

The long road that had taken liberals in this country into the social religion of democracy, into a worship of man, led logically to the Marxist dream of a classless society under a Socialist State.
As soon as he could think logically again he reached for the telephone directory and found Jerry Burton's home number.

Synset Examples

logically, you should now do the same to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rationally - in a logical, reasoned manner
  • Antonym: illogically - in a manner lacking logic or reason

Etymology

From logic + -ally, after the pattern of adverbs in -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses clear, sound reasoning or follows the principles of logic.

Examples

An inmate, a former university professor, expounded to us, logically and clearly, that someone was pilfering his thoughts.

Synset Examples

he acted logically under the circumstances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illogically - in an illogical manner; without clear reasoning

Etymology

From logical + -ly, following the pattern of forming adverbs from adjectives.
