proficient

1. ADJ

/ prəˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / prəˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
Having a high degree of skill, knowledge, or competence in a particular activity or field.

Examples

Just as exciting but in a more technically proficient way is Laura Stuart, whose complete control of her every movement is lovely to watch.
And the authors give numerous instances of calculated guessing on the patient's part to show how large a role it played in his process of readapting himself and how proficient he became at it.
The crime showed too much planning, the kidnappers appeared too proficient to be caught by a checklist.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having or showing skill
  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient at something
  • Antonym: incompetent - not having the necessary skills or abilities
  • Degree Higher: expert - having a high level of knowledge or skill
  • Degree Lower: beginner - a person who is starting to learn a skill or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'proficientem', present participle of 'proficere' (to advance, make progress).
