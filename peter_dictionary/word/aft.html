aft

1. ADV

/ æft / (AmE), / ɑːft / (BrE)
toward or near the stern (rear) of a ship or the tail of an airplane

Examples

Those named in the Greek paper were manufacturing reasons to steal aft under pretence of some call of duty, so as to be near Spencer, watching an opportunity to communicate with him.

Synset Examples

stow the luggage aft
ships with square sails sail fairly efficiently with the wind abaft
the captain looked astern to see what the fuss was about

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fore - toward or near the bow (front) of a ship or front of an airplane

Etymology

From Middle English 'aft', from Old English 'æftan' (behind, at the back).
