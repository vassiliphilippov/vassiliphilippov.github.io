marriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmærɪdʒ / (BrE)
The legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners in a personal relationship (traditionally a man and a woman, but now also recognized between people of the same sex in many places).

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divorce - ending of a marriage by legal process
  • Synonym: wedding - the ceremony of getting married

Etymology

From Old French 'mariage', from 'marier' (to marry), from Latin 'maritare' (to marry).
