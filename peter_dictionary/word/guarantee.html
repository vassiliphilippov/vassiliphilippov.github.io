guarantee

1. VERB

/ ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
to give an assurance or take responsibility for the fulfillment or quality of something

Examples

Pass in Review practically guarantees enjoyment, and is a dramatic demonstration of the potentialities of any stereo music system.
Again, the analyticity of the two curves guarantee that such intervals exist.
It is a program of prepayment of health costs with absolute freedom of choice guaranteed.
Much has been made of the fact that major Catholic institutions now guarantee firm tenure.
The editorial was based on a news association dispatch which said that the telegraphers had secured an agreement whereby they were guaranteed 40 hours' pay per week whether they worked or not and that a reduction in their number was limited to 2 per cent per year.

Synset Examples

I vouch for the quality of my products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrant - to officially assure or promise something
  • Synonym: vouch - to give personal assurance for something
  • Synonym: promise - to commit to doing or ensuring something will happen

Etymology

From Old French 'garantir', from 'garant' (protector, warrant), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
A formal written promise that a product or service will meet certain standards or that repair or replacement will be provided if it does not.

Examples

Until the Charter of Liberties was issued in the fall of 1958, there were no guarantees of the right to assemble or to organize for political purposes.
You may think we did n't need Nancy and Jean, but you always get what you can when you can, and we had no guarantee that a fingerprint record on them could n't be useful before we were through with this case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warranty - a written guarantee, especially for the repair or replacement of a product

Etymology

From Old French 'garantie', from garantir (to warrant), from a Germanic root related to 'warrant'.

3. VERB

/ ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
to provide a formal assurance of the quality, accuracy, or correctness of something; to promise that something will meet certain standards or conditions

Examples

The Providence Daily Journal stated that although the guilt of Brown was evident, the South must guarantee him a fair trial to preserve domestic peace.

Synset Examples

The dealer warrants all the cars he sells
I warrant this information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrant - to give a formal guarantee or assurance

Etymology

From Old French *garantir* (to protect), from *garant* (warrant, protector), from a Germanic source.

4. VERB

/ ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
to make certain that something will happen or be the case; to promise to do or provide something, giving assurance of an outcome

Examples

Mr. Dryfoos' outstanding career as a journalist guarantees that the high standards which have made the Times one of the world's great newspapers will be maintained.
Regardless of its unadjusted allotment, each State is guaranteed by law a minimum allotment each year equal to the allotment which it received in fiscal year 1954 - increased by a uniform percentage of 5.4865771 which brings total 1954 allotments to all States up to $ 23000000.
We cannot guarantee public safety.

Synset Examples

This nest egg will ensure a nice retirement for us
Preparation will guarantee success!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ensure - make certain that something will occur or be the case
  • Synonym: assure - to make sure or certain; to guarantee
  • Synonym: promise - to state that one will do something; to give assurance

Etymology

From Old French 'garantir', from 'garantie' (guarantee, warranty), ultimately of Germanic origin.

5. NOUN

/ ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
A formal assurance or promise that something will be done or is true, often as a commitment that is unconditional or legally binding.

Examples

There we held "that the statutory scheme for review, within the selective service system, entitles [ conscientious objectors ] to no guarantee that the FBI reports must be produced for their inspection ".
Guaranteeing your safety.

Synset Examples

there is no guarantee that they are not lying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warranty - written guarantee given to a purchaser
  • Synonym: assurance - a positive declaration intended to give confidence
  • Synonym: promise - declaration that something will be done

Etymology

From Old French 'garantie', from garantir (to warrant), from Old High German 'warjan' (to protect).

6. VERB

/ ˌɡærənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
to promise that something will be done or will happen, or to make something certain

Examples

There is little evidence that existing public or private training programs have any great difficulty getting students to enroll in their programs, even though they must pay tuition, receive no subsistence payments, and are not guaranteed a job.
Actually, the Communists, out of fear of a united and armed Germany, would probably be willing to agree to a disarmed Germany that would be united and neutral and have its independence guaranteed by the U. N..
I guarantee he'’ll accept what I give him.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promise - to assure someone that one will definitely do, give, or arrange something
  • Synonym: vow - to solemnly promise to do something

Etymology

From Old French *garantir* (to protect, guarantee), from *garant* (warrant, protector).
