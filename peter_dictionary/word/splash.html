splash

1. VERB

/ splæʃ / (AmE), / splæʃ / (BrE)
to cause liquid to scatter or spatter, especially by striking or moving through it forcefully

Examples

Papa pointed a scornful finger at the splashing youth: "Idle recreation "!
The water splashes into shoulder-high tracts of fernery.

Synset Examples

She splashed the water around her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinkle - to scatter drops of liquid or small particles over something

2. VERB

/ splæʃ / (AmE), / splæʃ / (BrE)
To cause liquid to strike or fall on something in irregular drops, often making noise and scattering drops in different directions.

Examples

Water splashed against his windshield as he led the flight in and out of showers.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - to scatter small drops of liquid, often randomly
  • Degree Lower: dribble - to let liquid fall in small drops

Etymology

Imitative in origin, echoing the sound made by something wet hitting a surface.

3. NOUN

/ splæʃ / (AmE), / splæʃ / (BrE)
A sound made when something strikes or falls into liquid, such as water, causing it to scatter or move noisily.

Examples

Through the splash of the rising waters, they could hear the roar of the river as it raged through its canyon, gnashing big chunks out of the banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plop - soft sound made when something drops into liquid
  • Synonym: splash - an act of splashing liquid or a mark made thereby

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by falling or striking liquid.

4. VERB

/ splæʃ / (AmE), / splæʃ / (BrE)
(of a person) to walk through mud, water, or other wet substances so that liquid is thrown up or makes a noise.

Examples

He splashed on, mud sucking at his feet with each step, until he reached the end of the drainage ditch and the beginning of the fence that enclosed the farm.

Synset Examples

We had to splosh across the wet meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wade - to walk with effort through water or another liquid or viscous substance

Etymology

Imitative formation reflecting the sound made by striking or moving in liquid.
