oscillation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of repeatedly moving back and forth between two states, directions, or positions

Examples

All this expands the oscillation between surface and depth so as to encompass fictive space in front of the surface as well as behind it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibration - a rapid back and forth movement

Etymology

From Latin 'oscillatio', from 'oscillare' (to swing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a regular periodic movement or fluctuation around a central value, commonly used in physics to describe motion or variation in systems such as waves or circuits

Examples

But when waves with a period of between 10 and 40 minutes begin to roll over the ocean, they set in motion a corresponding oscillation in a column of mercury which closes an electric circuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibration - rapid back-and-forth movement, often less regular than oscillation
  • Synonym: fluctuation - an irregular rising and falling in number or amount; variation

Etymology

From Latin oscillatio (a swinging), from oscillare (to swing).
