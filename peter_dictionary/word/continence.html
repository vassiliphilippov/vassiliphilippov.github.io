continence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːntɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɪnəns / (BrE)
self-restraint or abstinence from sexual activity, especially for moral or religious reasons.

Examples

The general board declared: "Most of the Protestant churches hold contraception and periodic continence to be morally right when the motives are right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chastity - the practice of refraining from sexual intercourse, especially for religious reasons
  • Synonym: abstinence - the practice of restraining oneself from indulging in something, typically alcohol or sex

Etymology

From Latin 'continentia' (restraint, abstinence), from 'continere' (to hold together, restrain).
