monopolization

1. NOUN

/ məˌnɑːpəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌnɒpəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of gaining exclusive control over a market or commodity, preventing others from competing.

Examples

The latter provisions proscribe any contract, combination, or conspiracy in restraint of interstate or foreign trade, and monopolization of, or attempts, combinations, or conspiracies to monopolize, such trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monopoly - exclusive control by one company or group over a commodity or service

Etymology

From 'monopolize' + '-ation', ultimately from Greek 'monos' (alone) + 'polein' (to sell).
