monopolize

1. VERB

/ məˈnɑː.pə.laɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒp.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
To have or take complete control of something so that others cannot share in it.

Examples

Flatness may now monopolize everything, but it is a flatness become so ambiguous and expanded as to turn into illusion itself - at least an optical if not, properly speaking, a pictorial illusion.
Naturally no woman can ever completely monopolize the sexual initiative.

Synset Examples

He monopolizes the laser printer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominate - to have a very strong influence over; to control
  • Antonym: share - to use, have, or enjoy something with others

Etymology

From French *monopoliser*, based on Greek *monos* (alone) + *polein* (to sell).

2. VERB

/ məˈnɑː.pəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈnɒp.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
To have or take complete control of a commodity, service, or market, often to the exclusion of others.

Examples

The latter provisions proscribe any contract, combination, or conspiracy in restraint of interstate or foreign trade, and monopolization of, or attempts, combinations, or conspiracies to monopolize, such trade.
Thus, a finding of conspiracy to restrain trade or attempt to monopolize was excluded from the Court's decision.

Synset Examples

OPEC wants to monopolize oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominate - to have a commanding influence or control over
  • Synonym: control - to exercise authoritative or dominating influence
  • Antonym: share - to have or use something with others, as opposed to monopolizing

Etymology

From French monopoliser, from 'monopole' (monopoly), from Greek monōpōlion ('sole seller').
