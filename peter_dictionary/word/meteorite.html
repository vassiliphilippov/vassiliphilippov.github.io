meteorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtiəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːtiəˌraɪt / (BrE)
A stony or metallic object from space that survives its passage through the Earth's atmosphere and lands on the Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteor - a small body of matter from outer space that enters the Earth's atmosphere, becoming incandescent as a result of friction
  • Degree Lower: meteoroid - a small body moving in the solar system that would become a meteor if it entered the Earth's atmosphere

Etymology

From French 'météorite', from 'météore' (meteor).
