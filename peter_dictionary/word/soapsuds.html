soapsuds

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌsʌdz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌsʌdz / (BrE)
a mass of small bubbles produced by mixing soap or detergent with water

Examples

The water in Thor's big swimming pool had been covered with a blanket of thick, foamy soapsuds - fashioned, of course, from ZING - Joyce had dived from the board into the pool, then swirled and cavorted in her luxurious "bath "while cameras rolled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lather - foam formed when soap is mixed with water

Etymology

From 'soap' + 'suds' (early 15th century 'suddes', meaning dregs or foam).
