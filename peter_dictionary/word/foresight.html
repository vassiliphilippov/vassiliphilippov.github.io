foresight

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsaɪt / (BrE)
The ability to anticipate what will happen or be needed in the future and to plan accordingly.

Examples

Nevertheless, with foresight and careful planning, some of the more disruptive and dangerous consequences of social change which have troubled other countries passing through this stage can be escaped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudence - the quality of being careful or wise in practical matters; caution
  • Antonym: improvidence - lack of foresight or care for the future

Etymology

From Old English 'forsihd' ('foresee') + 'sight'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.saɪt / (BrE)
The ability to anticipate what will happen or be needed in the future; the act of predicting or planning for future needs.

Examples

A man of vision and guts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prescience - the ability to know or anticipate events before they happen
  • Synonym: vision - the ability to think about or plan the future with imagination or wisdom
  • Antonym: hindsight - understanding a situation only after it has happened

Etymology

From Old English foresiht (foreseeing, providence), from fore- (before) + sight.
