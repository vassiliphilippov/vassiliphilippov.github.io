dreadfully

1. ADV

/ ˈdrɛd.fəli / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely bad, unpleasant, or alarming.

Examples

It is like a mysterious epidemic which, starting first with Abigail and Parris, spreads inexorably with a dreadfully growing virulence through the whole town until all have been infected by it.

Synset Examples

there was a dreadfully bloody accident on the road this morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awfully - in an extremely bad or unpleasant way
  • Synonym: terribly - in an extremely bad or serious way
  • Antonym: wonderfully - in an extremely good or pleasant way

Etymology

From 'dreadful' + '-ly', where 'dreadful' means 'causing fear or terror'.
