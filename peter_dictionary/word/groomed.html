groomed

1. ADJ

/ ɡruːmd / (AmE), / ɡruːmd / (BrE)
Having a neat and smart appearance; well cared for.

Examples

He was always well groomed and well tailored, and he had that rich man's look which was authentic enough and came from two good prep schools and a proper university.

Synset Examples

the manager was a beautifully groomed young man
his horse was always groomed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unkempt - having an untidy or disheveled appearance

Etymology

From the verb 'groom', meaning to clean and tidy, first recorded in 19th century English.
