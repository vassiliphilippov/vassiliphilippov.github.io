spatiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌspeɪʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌspeɪʃiˈalɪti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being related to, occupying, or characterized by space.

Examples

(2) Spatiality becomes part of the tactual sensation only by way of visual representations; that is, there is, in the true sense, only a visual space ".

Etymology

From spatial + -ity, modeled after words like 'partiality'.
