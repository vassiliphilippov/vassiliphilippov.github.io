synchronize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause two or more things to happen at the same time or to operate in unison

Examples

If their schedules were to synchronize, there was no point in wasting time.

Synset Examples

Let's synchronize our efforts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desynchronize - to cause things to be out of time with each other
  • Synonym: coordinate - to organize different elements to work together effectively

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), from 'syn' (together) + 'chronos' (time).
