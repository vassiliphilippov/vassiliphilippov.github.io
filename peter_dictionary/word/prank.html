prank

1. NOUN

/ præŋk / (AmE), / præŋk / (BrE)
behavior characterized by clownish or foolish actions intended to amuse others; buffoonery.

Examples

The Serge Prokofieff whom we knew in the United States of America was gay, witty, mercurial, full of pranks and bonheur - and very capable as a professional musician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffoonery - foolish or clownish behavior; ridicule
  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English pranken (to adorn oneself), of uncertain origin.
