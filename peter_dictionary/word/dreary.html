dreary

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrɪri / (AmE), / ˈdrɪəri / (BrE)
Dull, bleak, and lacking in liveliness, charm, or excitement; depressing or uninteresting.

Examples

That a writer who is gay cannot be serious is a common professional illusion, sedulously fostered by all too many academics who mistakenly believe that their frivolous efforts should be taken seriously because they are expressed with that dreary solemnity which is the only mode of expression their authors are capable of.

Synset Examples

her drab personality
life was drab compared with the more exciting life style overseas
a series of dreary dinner parties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - lacking brightness or interest; drearily dull
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening

Etymology

From Old English *drēorig* (gory, bloody, sorrowful), related to *drēor* (gore, blood).
