bilk

1. VERB

/ bɪlk / (AmE), / bɪlk / (BrE)
to cheat someone out of money or something owed, especially by deceit or fraud

Examples

Mr. A., her fellow townsman, also experienced a nervous breakdown just as soon as he discovered that he had been bilked of his life savings by the limited practitioner who had been treating his wife - a woman suffering from an incurable disease, multiple sclerosis - and himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Antonym: repay - to pay back money owed

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete English noun 'bilk' meaning 'thwart, frustrate' (of uncertain origin).
