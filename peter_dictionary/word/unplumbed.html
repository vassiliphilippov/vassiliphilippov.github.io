unplumbed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈplʌmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈplʌmd / (BrE)
(of depths, places, or things) not measured, explored, or understood because of great depth or obscurity; unfathomed.

Examples

On the other hand, Arnold's "The unplumbed, salt, estranging sea ", taken in its context, certainly does so.

Synset Examples

the profound depths of the sea
the dark unfathomed caves of ocean
unplumbed depths of the sea
remote and unsounded caverns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfathomed - not fully explored or understood; of unknown depth
  • Synonym: profound - very deep; extending far below the surface
  • Antonym: measured - having known or established depth or extent

Etymology

From un- (not) + plumb (to measure the depth of water with a plumb line).
