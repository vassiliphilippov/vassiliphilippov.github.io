disturbingly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstɝːbɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes worry, anxiety, or upset; in a manner that is troubling or unsettling.

Examples

It was a disturbingly familiar face, too, but I could n't remember where we had met.

Synset Examples

the details of the kidnaper's letter had sounded disturbingly convincing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reassuringly - in a way that makes one feel less worried or more comfortable

Etymology

From 'disturb' + '-ing' + '-ly', with 'disturb' from Latin 'disturbare' (to throw into disorder).
