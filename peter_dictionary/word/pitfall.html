pitfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtfɔːl / (BrE)
A hidden or unsuspected danger or difficulty.

Examples

In spite of all the pitfalls, there is a certain amount of agreement on some of the giants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - danger or risk, especially one that is hidden
  • Synonym: trap - a device or situation intended to catch or ensnare
  • Antonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position

Etymology

From Old French 'pitiéfall' (trap), from 'pit' + 'fall'.
