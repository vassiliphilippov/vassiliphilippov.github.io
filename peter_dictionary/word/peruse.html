peruse

1. VERB

/ pəˈruz / (AmE), / pəˈruːz / (BrE)
to read or examine carefully and in detail

Examples

The rating scale of compulsivity was constructed by first perusing the interview records, categorizing all evidence related to compulsivity, then arranging a distribution of such information apart from the case records.

Synset Examples

Please peruse this report at your leisure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skim - read quickly or superficially

Etymology

From Middle English 'perusen', possibly from Old French 'per-', intensive prefix, and 'user' (to use).
