bait

1. NOUN

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
Something used to entice or lure someone or something, especially to catch animals or fish, or to tempt a person into doing something.

Examples

The fish took the bait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lure - something that attracts or tempts

Etymology

From Old Norse *beita* (to cause to bite), related to Middle English *baiten*.

2. VERB

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
to deliberately annoy, provoke, or harass someone, especially by persistent criticism or teasing

Examples

Otherwise, she would be baited into a tantrum - teased and provoked until she lost control of herself, and thus lost still another battle in the maddening struggle of Tom Lord Vs. Joyce Lakewood.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful or unkind way
  • Synonym: harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge with insulting remarks

Etymology

From Old Norse *beita* (to cause to bite, hunt with bait); later used figuratively for provoking.
