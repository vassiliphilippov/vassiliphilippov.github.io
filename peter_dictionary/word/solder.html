solder

1. VERB

/ ˈsɑdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɒdə / (BrE)
to join or fuse two surfaces together using a melted metal alloy (solder) to form a secure bond, especially in metalwork or electronics.

Examples

Synset Examples

solder these two pipes together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - to join pieces of metal by applying heat or pressure
  • Synonym: braze - to join metals using a filler metal with a lower melting point

Etymology

From Old French 'souder', from Latin 'solidare' (to make solid), from 'solidus' (solid).
