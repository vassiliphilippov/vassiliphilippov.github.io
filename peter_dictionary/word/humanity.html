humanity

1. NOUN

/ hjuˈmænəti / (AmE), / hjuːˈmænɪti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being human; humanness

Examples

There is no justification for systematizing the random statements of Irenaeus about the image of God beyond this, nor for reading into his imprecise usage the later theological distinction between the image of God (humanity) and the similitude of God (immortality).

Synset Examples

he feared the speedy decline of all manhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanness - the quality or state of being human

Etymology

From Latin humanitas (human nature, humanity), from humanus (human).

2. NOUN

/ hjuˈmænəti / (AmE), / hjʊˈmænəti / (BrE)
the quality of being humane and showing compassion or kindness toward others

Examples

Yet no detail was too small to receive attention from this master, and as a result the playing here has humor, delicacy, and radiant humanity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cruelty - the opposite quality: callousness or lack of compassion
  • Synonym: compassion - sympathy and concern for the suffering of others

Etymology

From Latin humanitas (human nature, kindness, courtesy)
