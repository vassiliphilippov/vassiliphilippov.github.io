nudge

1. VERB

/ nʌdʒ / (AmE), / nʌdʒ / (BrE)
to gently push someone or something, especially with your elbow or hand, in order to attract attention or prompt a response

Examples

His mother was nudging him, but he was still falling.
Ekstrohm nudged it with a boot.
Ryan nudged it with his toe, following Ekstrohm's example.

Synset Examples

She nudged my elbow when she saw her friend enter the restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to jab or prod, especially with a finger or a sharp object
  • Degree Higher: shove - to push someone or something roughly or forcefully

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from a dialectal English word meaning 'to jog' or 'to prod'.
