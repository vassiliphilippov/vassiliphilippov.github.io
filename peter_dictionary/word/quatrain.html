quatrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːˌtreɪn / (AmE), / ˈkwætreɪn / (BrE)
A stanza of four lines, especially one with a specific rhyme scheme, used in poetry.

Examples

A verse familiar to all grammarians is the quatrain:

Etymology

From French 'quatrain', from 'quatre' meaning 'four', from Latin 'quattuor'.
