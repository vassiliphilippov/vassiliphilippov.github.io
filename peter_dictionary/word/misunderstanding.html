misunderstanding

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌʌndɚˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsʌndəˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
An incorrect interpretation or comprehension of something; a failure to understand correctly.

Examples

This is done for simplicity of commands and to bring the hidden redundancy up to where misunderstanding has almost zero possibility.

Synset Examples

his misinterpretation of the question caused his error
there was no mistaking her meaning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: understanding - correct or proper interpretation of something

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'understand.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪs.ʌn.dərˈstæn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ʌn.dəˈstæn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
A failure to understand something correctly; an incorrect interpretation or belief about what was meant, said, or intended.

Examples

What a misunderstanding!

Synset Examples

he wasn't going to admit his mistake
make no mistake about his intentions
there must be some misunderstanding--I don't have a sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong
  • Synonym: misinterpretation - an incorrect explanation or understanding of something

Etymology

From misunderstand + -ing, where misunderstand is from Middle English 'misunderstanden', from mis- (wrongly) + understand.
