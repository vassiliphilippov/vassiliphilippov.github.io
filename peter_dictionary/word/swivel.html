swivel

1. VERB

/ ˈswɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈswɪvəl / (BrE)
to turn or cause to turn around a central point or axis; to rotate on or as if on a pivot

Examples

He sat stiff-backed in a chair that did not swivel, though it was obvious to Gun that Killpath felt his position as acting captain plainly merited a swivel chair.
The head is mounted on a horizontal arm that swivels on the supporting column to position the drill bit instead of the work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotate - to turn around a central point
  • Synonym: pivot - to turn or balance on a central point

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German 'swivel' (swivel, pivot, axis).
