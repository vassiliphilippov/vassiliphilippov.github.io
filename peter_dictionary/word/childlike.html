childlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪld.laɪk / (BrE)
(of an adult, activity, or quality) having the good qualities, such as innocence or simplicity, typically associated with a young child.

Examples

It was a face that had lost its childlike softness and was beginning to fold within its fragile features a harshness that belied the lyric lines of its contours.

Synset Examples

childlike charm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childish - (of an adult or behavior) typical of a child, especially in a negative way

Etymology

Formed as 'child' + 'like', meaning 'having qualities of a child'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of an adult or their behavior) having the good qualities, such as simplicity and innocence, of a child, often in a positive or naive way.

Examples

One who invites such trials of character is either foolhardy, overconfident or too simple and childlike in faith in mankind to see the danger.

Synset Examples

childlike trust
dewy-eyed innocence
listened in round-eyed wonder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childish - (of an adult or their behavior) silly or immature, like a child, often in a negative sense

Etymology

From 'child' + 'like', meaning resembling a child.
