jail

1. NOUN

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
A building or institution where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while waiting for trial.

Examples

Four additional deputies be employed at the Fulton County Jail and "a doctor, medical intern or extern be employed for night and weekend duty at the jail ".
The sentence: 360 days' confinement in the county jail.
Attorney General Palmer made a series of raids that sent more than 4000 so-called radicals to the jails, in direct violation of their constitutional rights.
If an unlucky accident should befall him, if he should be shot by a police officer, or if he should hang himself in his jail cell, or if he's struck by a bolt of lightning, then I'm going to blame some of the people in this room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - An institution for the confinement of people convicted of serious crimes

Etymology

From Old French *jaiole*, from Latin *cavea* (cage, enclosure).

2. VERB

/ dʒeɪl / (AmE), / dʒeɪl / (BrE)
to put someone in jail as a punishment or while awaiting trial

Examples

He himself was once convicted of painting erotica and jailed for 24 days - the first three of which he spent desperately trying to make paintings on the wall with his own spittle.

Synset Examples

The suspects were imprisoned without trial
the murderer was incarcerated for the rest of his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprison - to confine someone in prison
  • Synonym: incarcerate - to put or keep someone in prison
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from jail or prison

Etymology

From Old French *jaiole* (cage, prison), from Medieval Latin *gabiola* (diminutive of *gaba* 'cage').
