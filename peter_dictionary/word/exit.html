exit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡ.zɪt, ˈɛk.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛɡ.zɪt, ˈɛk.sɪt / (BrE)
A way or passage for leaving a building, room, or other enclosed area.

Examples

As the lights came up, Andy could see that a number of patrons were already on their way toward the exit.

Synset Examples

he blocked the way out
the canyon had only one issue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: entrance - a way or passage for entering a place

Etymology

From Latin 'exitus' (a going out, departure) from 'exire' (to go out).
