hawker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔkər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkə / (BrE)
A person who travels about selling goods in public places, especially by calling out to attract customers.

Examples

The medical device pirate of today, of course, is a far more sophisticated operator than his predecessor of yesteryear - the gallus snapping hawker of snake oil and other patent medicines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peddler - a person who goes from place to place selling small goods

Etymology

From Middle Low German *hoker* (peddler).
