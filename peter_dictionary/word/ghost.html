ghost

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
A haunting memory or mental image, especially of something unpleasant or traumatic from the past.

Examples

The argument against this last approach is comparable to that which rejects stories about hoop snakes, about snakes that break themselves into many pieces and join up again, or even of ghosts that chase people out of graveyards; the mere piling up of testimony does not prove, to the scientific mind, the existence of hoop snakes, joint snakes, or ghosts.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specter - a mental image of something feared or unpleasant

Etymology

From Old English 'gāst' (spirit, soul), related to German 'Geist'.

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
to move quietly and stealthily, as if like a ghost; to glide or pass silently and almost without being noticed

Examples

They slid through the wicket in the big gate, ghosted across the dark ground.

Synset Examples

The masked men ghosted across the moonlit yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and quietly
  • Synonym: sneak - to move quietly and secretly

3. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
The spirit of a dead person that appears to the living as a visible or sensed presence.

Examples

We have enough problems in this city... ...without worrying about ghosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phantom - a ghost or apparition, often regarded as an illusion
  • Synonym: specter - a visible ghost or spirit
  • Antonym: zombie - a reanimated corpse; undead being in folklore

Etymology

From Old English 'gāst' (spirit, soul), related to German 'Geist'.
