plump

1. ADJ

/ plʌmp / (AmE), / plʌmp / (BrE)
having a pleasantly full and rounded figure; slightly fat in a way that is considered attractive or healthy

Examples

There was a glass pane in the front door, and through this he could see into a hallway where a plump woman with red hair was arranging flowers.
He ran on his plump sticks of legs, freezing now and again into the sudden startled attitudes which the camera had caught and held on the paling photographs, all carefully placed and glued and labeled, resting in the fat plush album in the bottom drawer of the escritoire.

Synset Examples

a chubby child
pleasingly plump

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chubby - pleasantly plump or slightly fat, especially in a healthy way
  • Degree Higher: fat - having a large amount of excess flesh
  • Antonym: slim - attractively thin, especially in a healthy way

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of a soft object falling, later applied to roundness of form.
