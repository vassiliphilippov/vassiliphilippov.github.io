corn

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn / (AmE), / kɔːn / (BrE)
The seeds of certain cereal plants, especially maize, that can be prepared and served as food.

Examples

They had already lost most of their corn, she thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maize - a cereal plant (Zea mays) producing large grains set in rows on a cob

Etymology

From Old English 'corn' (grain, seed), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ kɔrn / (AmE), / kɔːn / (BrE)
the dried grains or kernels of corn, used as animal feed or ground to make cornmeal

Examples

Improves utilization of low moisture corn (less than 14 %).
Greatest benefits have been associated with feeding low moisture corn in beef feeding programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maize - the plant corn, especially as a crop; Zea mays

Etymology

From Old English 'corn' (grain, seed), originally meaning any grain, but in American English referring specifically to maize.

3. NOUN

/ kɔrn / (AmE), / kɔːn / (BrE)
A tall annual cereal grass (Zea mays), widely grown for its large ears that produce kernels; commonly called 'maize' outside North America.

Examples

An Alabamian wrote: "Col. Henry is [ an ignoramus ] fit for nothing higher than the cultivation of corn ".
She looked out at the corn field, the great green deep acres of it rolled out like the sea in the field beyond the whitewashed fence bordering the grounds.
Homes and factories and schools and a big wide federal highway, instead of peaceful corn to rest your eyes on while you tried to rest your heart, while you tried not to look at the balloon and the bandstand and the uniforms and the flash of the instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maize - another term for corn, especially outside North America

Etymology

From Old English 'corn' (grain, seed), later specialized in American English to mean 'maize', originally from Latin 'granum'.
