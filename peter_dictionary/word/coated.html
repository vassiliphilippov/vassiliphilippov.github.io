coated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtɪd / (BrE)
Having a layer or film covering the surface; provided with a coating, often used in combination to describe what the covering consists of.

Examples

In the cleaning process, sorbed greasy soils become coated in this manner with an oriented film of surfactant.

Synset Examples

coated paper has a smooth polished coating especially suitable for halftone printing
sugar-coated pills

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncoated - not covered with a coating or outer layer

Etymology

From the verb 'coat' + -ed, indicating the past participle or adjectival form.
