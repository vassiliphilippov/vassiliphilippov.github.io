dairy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛri / (AmE), / ˈdeəri / (BrE)
A farm where milk and other dairy products are produced from milk-producing animals, especially cows.

Examples

There it seems that the goitrogen ingested by dairy animals is itself inactive but is converted in the animal to an active goitrogen, which is then secreted in the milk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creamery - a place where butter and cheese are made from milk

Etymology

From Old English *dæġ* (day) and Middle English *dey* (dairymaid), referring to a place where milk was handled.
