premiere

1. NOUN

/ prəˈmɪr / (AmE), / prɛˈmjɛə / (BrE)
the first public performance of a play, film, or piece of music

Examples

We attended the premieres of his concertos, symphonies, and suites; we studied, taught, and performed his piano sonatas, chamber music, gavottes, and marches; we bought his records and played them in our schools and universities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debut - the first public appearance or presentation of something

Etymology

From French 'première' (first, feminine of 'premier').
