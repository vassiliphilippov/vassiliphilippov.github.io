ending

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of bringing something to a conclusion or stop

Examples

Southern resentment has been over the method of its ending, the invasion, and Reconstruction; their fears now are of miscegenation and Negro political control in many counties.

Synset Examples

the termination of the agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termination - the act of bringing something to an end
  • Antonym: beginning - the start or commencement of something

Etymology

From Old English 'endian' (to end) plus '-ing,' a noun-forming suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the final part of a word, such as a suffix or inflectional element, added to indicate tense, number, case, or other grammatical properties

Examples

For example, the inflected forms of a word can be represented, insofar as regular inflection allows, by a stem and a set of endings to be attached.

Synset Examples

From the verb 'end' + noun suffix '-ing', originally from Old English 'endian'.
