prospect

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.pɛkt / (BrE)
the possibility or likelihood that something good will happen in the future, especially success or advancement

Examples

The record of past earnings and prospects for the future must indicate it has the ability to repay the loan out of current and anticipated income.
When necessary, we should make it clear that countries which choose to derive marginal advantages from the cold war or to exploit their potential for disrupting the security of the world will not only lose our sympathy but also risk their own prospects for orderly development.
By holding out prospects for external capital assistance, the United States can provide strong incentives to prepare for the concerted economic drive necessary to achieve self-sustaining growth.
There the matter stands with the prospect that soon Manchester may be removed from the roster of towns contributing raw sewage to its main streams.
His mind closed on that prospect, as though fog had descended to blot out a valley.

Synset Examples

his prospects as a writer are excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chance - possibility, especially of something happening
  • Synonym: opportunity - a set of circumstances that makes it possible to do something
  • Antonym: risk - possibility of loss or negative outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'prospectus', meaning view or outlook, from 'prospicere' (to look forward).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.spɛkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.pɛkt / (BrE)
the possibility or likelihood of some future event or outcome; a mental picture or expectation of what may happen

Examples

When we consider the electronic industry potential for human betterment, the prospect is staggering.
Success reduces the prospect of threat and his powers of discrimination are improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectation - a strong belief that something will happen or be the case in the future
  • Degree Lower: possibility - a thing that may happen or be the case

Etymology

From Latin 'prospectus' (view, outlook), from 'prospicere' (to look forward).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.pɛkt / (BrE)
a person who is considered likely to be chosen or succeed, especially as a candidate for a position, prize, honor, or opportunity.

Examples

He had to look for other prospects, other motives until more conclusive evidence pointing to Johnston came to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candidate - person being considered for a position, prize, or membership

Etymology

From Latin prospectus (a view, outlook); sense of 'future possibility' extended to 'potential candidate'.
