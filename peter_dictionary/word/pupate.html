pupate

1. VERB

/ ˈpjuːˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpeɪt / (BrE)
(of an insect larva) to develop into a pupa as part of the life cycle.

Examples

Each now makes a tough, papery cocoon and pupates.
The development of the Andrena larvae is very rapid, so that by the end of spring they have already pupated and become adults.

Synset Examples

the insect larva pupate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metamorphose - to undergo a major change in form or structure
  • Degree Higher: emerge - to appear or become visible after being in a pupal stage (specifically in insects)

Etymology

From pupa (Latin, meaning 'doll', used in biology), plus the suffix -ate.
