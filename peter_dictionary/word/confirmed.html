confirmed

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɝːmd / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmd / (BrE)
(of a person) having a settled habit or firmly established characteristic, not likely to change.

Examples

"Chicago and All That Jazz "may have wound up satisfying neither the confirmed fan nor the inquisitive newcomer.
Holmes is addicted to the use of cocaine and other refreshing stimulants; he is prone to semi catatonic trances induced by the playing of the vioiln; he is a recluse, an incredible egotist, a confirmed misogynist.

Synset Examples

a confirmed bachelor
a confirmed invalid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - doing something as a habit; usual or customary
  • Synonym: inflexible - unwilling to change or compromise
  • Antonym: changeable - able or likely to change; not fixed

Etymology

From Latin confirmare (to strengthen, establish) via Old French confirmer.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfɝːmd / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmd / (BrE)
Having been made definite, established, or verified; or having received the rite of confirmation in a Christian church.

Examples

According to a newspaper report of the 1961 statistics of the Church of England, the "total of confirmed members is 9748000, but only 2887671 are registered on the parochial church rolls ", and "over 27 million people in England are baptized into the Church of England, but roughly only a tenth of them continue ".

Synset Examples

confirmed reservations
received confirmed reports of casualties
a confirmed Catholic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconfirmed - not established or not verified

Etymology

From confirm + -ed; ultimately from Latin confirmare, meaning 'to strengthen, establish'.
