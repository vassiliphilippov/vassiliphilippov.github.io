condenser

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnsɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
an electrical device that stores electric charge, also known as a capacitor

Examples

Thus, 1745 corresponded to the invention of the "Leiden "jar by Kleist, 1764 that of the electrophorus by Wilcke, 1782 produced the condenser of Volta, and 1801 the voltaic pile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitor - an electrical device used to store electric charge

Etymology

From Latin 'condensare' (to condense) + English '-er'. Term used in early electrical science referring to the 'condensation' of electric charge.
