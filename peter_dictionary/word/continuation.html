continuation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌtɪn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action of carrying on with something without interruption or the state of being continued

Examples

In the area of private label competition, it is logical to expect a continuation of trends which have been under way during the first decade.
The continuation and expansion of the shooting development program will assure to some degree that national and community leaders will be made aware of the ever growing need for shooting facilities and activities for hunting and shooting in answer to public demand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interruption - the act of stopping something from continuing
  • Antonym: discontinuation - the act of ending something so that it does not continue
  • Synonym: persistence - the continued or prolonged existence of something

Etymology

From Latin continuatio (a continuation, uninterrupted succession), from continuare (to join together).
