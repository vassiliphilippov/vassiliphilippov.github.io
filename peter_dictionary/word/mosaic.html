mosaic

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
a picture or pattern produced by arranging together small pieces of colored stone, glass, or other materials, especially as a decorative art

Examples

Putting the pieces of this mosaic together, Rector had the vague outlines of a biography.
"The artist is like a fragment of a mosaic - no, he is more than that, a virtuoso performer in some vast philharmonic.

Etymology

From Old French 'mosaïque', from Italian 'mosaico', ultimately from Latin 'mosaicum' (work of the Muses), from Greek 'Mouseios'.
