aberration

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected, typically one that is unwelcome or problematic

Examples

The Poynting-Robertson effect (Robertson, 1937; Wyatt and Whipple, 1950), which is a retardation of the orbital motion of particles by the relativistic aberration of the repulsive force of the impinging solar radiation, causes the dust to spiral into the sun in times much shorter than the age of the Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard or expected
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical

Etymology

From Latin 'aberratio', from 'aberrare' meaning to wander away, stray.
