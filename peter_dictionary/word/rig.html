rig

1. NOUN

/ rɪɡ / (AmE), / rɪɡ / (BrE)
A set of equipment or machinery designed for a specific purpose, especially in industry or farming.

Examples

If an expensive and specialized piece of machinery is needed - such as a spray rig, a combine, or a binder - it is better to pay someone with a machine to do the work.
Men crowded the streets and freight rigs and teams were moving about.

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'rigga' (to equip).

2. VERB

/ rɪɡ / (AmE), / rɪɡ / (BrE)
to arrange or influence the outcome of something, especially a competition or election, by dishonest or fraudulent means

Examples

City Controller Alexander Hemphill charged Tuesday that the bids on the Frankford Elevated repair project were rigged to the advantage of a private contracting company which had "an inside track "with the city.

Synset Examples

rig an election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fix - to dishonestly arrange the outcome of a contest; to manipulate fraudulently

3. VERB

/ rɪɡ / (AmE), / rɪɡ / (BrE)
to connect or secure (something) to another object, especially in a way that allows it to function or operate, often for an illicit or dangerous purpose

Examples

It was first believed the bomb was rigged to the car's starter.

Synset Examples

They rigged the bomb to the ignition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attach - to fasten, join, or connect something
  • Antonym: detach - to disconnect or remove something attached

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *rigga* (to bind, tie), related to nautical rigging.

4. VERB

/ rɪɡ / (AmE), / rɪɡ / (BrE)
to manipulate or arrange something dishonestly or fraudulently, especially to achieve a desired result

Examples

Let us look at the heavy-electrical-goods industry in which General Electric, Westinghouse and a number of other manufacturers were recently convicted of engaging in a conspiracy to rig prices and allocate the market.

Synset Examples

rig prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fix - to dishonestly arrange the result of something, especially a competition or price
