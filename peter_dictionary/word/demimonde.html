demimonde

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmiˈmɑnd / (AmE), / ˌdɛmiˈmɒnd / (BrE)
a social group, especially of women, regarded as being on the fringes of respectable society due to dubious or promiscuous behavior, often associated with 19th- and early 20th-century high society

Examples

Beaten, bruised and exhausted, he pursues the elusive killer through the demi-monde of high society and low morals, always alone, always despised.

Etymology

From French demi-monde (literally 'half-world'), popularized in 19th-century Paris to describe women on the fringes of polite society.
