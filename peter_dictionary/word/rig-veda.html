Rig-Veda

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɪɡ ˈveɪdə / (AmE), / ˌrɪɡ ˈviːdə / (BrE)
the oldest of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of ancient Hindu hymns composed in Sanskrit before 2000 BC

Examples

His chelas were required to assume the matsyendra posture dressed in hand-woven diapers while he read aloud from Rig-Veda and an assistant guru examined their purses in another room - nothing was stolen; the purpose was less immediate.

Etymology

From Sanskrit Ṛgveda: ṛc (verse, praise) + veda (knowledge, wisdom).
