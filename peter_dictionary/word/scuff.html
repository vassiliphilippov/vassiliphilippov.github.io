scuff

1. VERB

/ skʌf / (AmE), / skʌf / (BrE)
to walk by dragging one's feet along the ground without lifting them fully

Examples

Children walking home from school would scuff through what seemed to be his foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging the feet along or without lifting them fully

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound produced when feet are dragged along the ground.
