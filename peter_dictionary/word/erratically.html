erratically

1. ADV

/ ɪˈræɾɪkli / (AmE), / ɪˈratɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is unpredictable, irregular, or lacking a fixed pattern.

Examples

The men in power are committed in principle to modernization, but economic and social changes are proceeding only erratically.

Synset Examples

economic changes are proceeding erratically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - in a random, unpredictable way
  • Antonym: predictably - in a predictable, regular way

Etymology

From 'erratic' + -ally; 'erratic' from Latin erraticus (wandering), from errare (to wander).
