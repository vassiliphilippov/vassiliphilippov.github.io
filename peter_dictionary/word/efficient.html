efficient

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
working well and achieving results without wasting time, effort, or resources

Examples

A large picnic area or camping development is most efficient in shape as a square or rectangle several hundred feet in width in preference to a long narrow area less than one hundred feet wide.
Of the two, Porter is justly the better known, for he went far beyond the vital finding of fiction for films to take the first step toward fashioning a language of film, toward making the motion picture the intricate, efficient time machine that it has remained since, even in the most inept hands.
A satisfactory cloud was produced even though these nozzles were only about 5 per cent efficient in producing an initial cloud in the size range of 1 to 5 microns.
He could only watch with a sort of gentle dismay while his body did these quick, appalling, and efficient things.
How efficient and necessary are your intra company vehicles?

Synset Examples

an efficient production manager
efficient engines save gas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficient - not achieving maximum productivity; wasting time, effort, or resources
  • Synonym: effective - successful in producing a desired result, though not necessarily without waste

Etymology

From Latin efficere (to accomplish, bring about) via Old French efficient.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
able to accomplish a task with minimal waste of time and effort; working in a well-organized and competent way

Examples

She was the only kind of Negro Laura Andrus would want around: independent, unservile, probably charging double what ordinary maids did for housework - and doubly efficient.
It looks pretty efficient and that's the important thing ".
But it is plain that a warning system, however efficient, is not enough.

Synset Examples

people who will do nothing unless they get something out of it for themselves are often highly effective persons...
effective personnel
an efficient secretary
the efficient cause of the revolution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficient - not achieving maximum productivity; wasting resources or time
  • Synonym: effective - producing a desired result; successful in achieving an intended outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'efficiens', present participle of 'efficere' (to accomplish, to effect).
