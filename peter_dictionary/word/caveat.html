caveat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.vi.æt / (AmE), / ˈkæ.vi.æt / (BrE)
a warning or caution about a particular statement, decision, or action, usually to avoid misunderstanding or risk

Examples

Here an important caveat is in order.

Synset Examples

a caveat against unfair practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - an act or statement telling someone of possible danger or trouble
  • Synonym: disclaimer - a statement that denies responsibility or limits liability

Etymology

From Latin 'caveat', third person singular present subjunctive of 'cavēre' (to beware).
