roiling

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) moving in a turbulent, swirling, or agitated manner.

Examples

There was no real sign of the river now, just a roiling, oily ribbon of liquid movement through muddy waters that reached everywhere.
She was watching a tree ride wildly down that roiling current.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or disturbance; (of a liquid) violently agitated

Etymology

Present participle of 'roil', from Old French 'rouiller' (to rust, make muddy).
