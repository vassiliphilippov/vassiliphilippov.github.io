interlard

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntɚˈlɑrd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɑːd / (BrE)
To insert words, phrases, or expressions into speech or writing, often in a way that interrupts the flow.

Examples

You know, without you... ...

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intersperse - to scatter or insert at intervals

Etymology

From Middle French *entrelarder*, from Latin *inter-* (between) + *lardum* (bacon, lard), originally 'to insert strips of fat'.
