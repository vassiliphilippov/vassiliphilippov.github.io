lurch

1. NOUN

/ lɝtʃ / (AmE), / lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
An unsteady, sudden, or awkward manner of walking or movement, especially one caused by physical difficulty.

Examples

He walked with a heavy list to the right, as that leg was four inches shorter than the other, but the lurch did not reduce his feline quickness with his guns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: sway - to move or swing unsteadily from side to side

2. VERB

/ lɝtʃ / (AmE), / lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to make an abrupt or uncontrolled movement, especially forward or sideways, often as if stumbling or staggering

Examples

A shot caught him and straightened him up in screaming pain; a following volley of shots shattered glass, ripped the ceiling, and sent him lurching heavily from the table.
He lurched on down the road despairingly, because there was no place else to go.

Synset Examples

The drunken man staggered into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall

3. VERB

/ lɝtʃ / (AmE), / lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to move with slow, sudden, and unsteady movements, especially as if out of control

Examples

The car lurched along at a snail's crawl, the left front mudguard banging and scraping against the tire, occasionally scraping against the road itself.

Synset Examples

The truck lurched down the road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: stumble - to trip or momentarily lose balance while moving

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Middle English 'lurche' (to steal, trick).
