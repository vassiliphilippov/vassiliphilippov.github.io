universalize

1. VERB

/ juˌnɪvərsəˈlaɪz / (AmE), / juːˌnɪvəˈsaɪz / (BrE)
To make something applicable or relevant to all cases, people, or things; to render universal.

Examples

His pictures generalize, though they are inspired by a particular locale; they universalize in terms of weather, skies, earth, and people.

Synset Examples

This author's stories universalize old themes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalize - To make general or broad statements; to generalize.

Etymology

From Latin 'universalis' (universal) + English '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).
