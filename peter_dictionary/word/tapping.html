tapping

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæpɪŋ / (BrE)
A light, repeated sound made by a gentle knock or touch against a surface.

Examples

The slow tapping of a cane on the stone steps coming up to the tower interrupted his reverie.
Faint at first, the tapping grew until it sounded loud against the wind.

Synset Examples

he heard the tapping of the man's cane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knocking - the action or sound of a sharp blow, especially with the knuckles
  • Synonym: pattering - a light, rapid, repeated tapping sound

Etymology

From 'tap' (to strike lightly) + -ing (forming a noun indicating an action or result).
