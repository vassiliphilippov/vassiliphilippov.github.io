stampede

1. VERB

/ stæmˈpid / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːd / (BrE)
to cause (a group of animals or people) to run away suddenly in panic, typically in a confused or uncontrolled manner

Examples

"We 'll stampede the rest.
I 'll stampede the rest of these horses so they can n't chase you ".

Synset Examples

Thunderbolts can stampede animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - to cause sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety

Etymology

From Spanish 'estampida' meaning 'crash, uproar', from 'estampar' (to stamp).
