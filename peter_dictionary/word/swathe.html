swathe

1. VERB

/ sweɪð / (AmE), / sweɪð / (BrE)
to wrap or envelop, especially in layers of cloth or fabric

Examples

The form was swathed in an army blanket, much patched, fastened at the neck with a cord.

Synset Examples

swaddled the infant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swaddle - wrap (especially an infant) tightly in cloths or blankets

Etymology

From Old English *swæþian* (to bind with a band).
