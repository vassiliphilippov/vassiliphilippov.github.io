thrust

1. NOUN

/ θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
A strong, driving force applied to move or propel something forward; the act of pushing or propelling with force.

Examples

Gannon contributed saving plays on the Falcons' aerial thrusts in the late stages.

Synset Examples

after reaching the desired velocity the drive is cut off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - to move something away by exerting force
  • Antonym: pull - to draw or haul something toward oneself

Etymology

From Old Norse *þrýsta* (“to push, squeeze”).

2. NOUN

/ θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
A force that pushes something forward, especially in physics or engineering contexts such as rockets, engines, or moving fluids.

Examples

He has gained only one thing - he has exploded a 50 - megaton bomb and he probably has rockets with sufficient thrust to lob it over the shorter intercontinental ranges.
The concert opened with another big romantic score, Schumann's Overture to Manfred, which suffered fate, this time with orchestral thrusts to the Byronic point to keep it afloat.

Synset Examples

the push of the water on the walls of the tank
the thrust of the jet engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - an act of moving something forward by pressing or exerting force
  • Antonym: pull - an act of moving something toward oneself by drawing or exerting force

Etymology

From Old Norse *þrysta* (to thrust).
