rib

1. NOUN

/ rɪb / (AmE), / rɪb / (BrE)
any of the paired curved bones extending from the spine toward or to the sternum, forming the sides of the thorax in humans and many vertebrates.

Examples

The bone of the vertebral bodies, ribs, and sternum was soft and was easily compressed.
Suddenly the Spanish became an English in which only one word emerged with clarity and precision, "son of a bitch ", sometimes hyphenated by vicious jabs of a beer bottle into Johnson's quivering ribs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sternum - the flat bone at the front center of the chest to which ribs are attached

Etymology

Old English *ribb*, from Proto-Germanic *ribjaz*.

2. NOUN

/ rɪb / (AmE), / rɪb / (BrE)
a structural support curved or straight, resembling a rib, used as a strengthening member in objects such as umbrellas, ships, or buildings

Examples

The lieutenant's sparse brown hair was heavily pomaded, and as Killpath raked the comb through it, it stuck together in thatches so that it looked like umbrella ribs clinging to his pink skull.
Behind its ornate facade the notorious dive clung like a bird's nest to the rocky ribs of the canyonside.
He furled the slashed sides of the canvas tarpaulins, leaving the ribs and wagon open.
He drew a long breath and opened the trunk and hung out her clothes and spoilables upon the wagon ribs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spar - a strong pole used as part of a structure in ships or aircraft

Etymology

From Old English ribb (rib), related to German Rippe.
