appointed

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) assigned to a position or role by official decision, rather than by election or selection by others.

Examples

"These actions should serve to protect in fact and in effect the court's wards from undue costs and its appointed and elected servants from unmeritorious criticisms ", the jury said.
The latter two were appointed secretaries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elected - chosen for a position through voting
  • Synonym: nominate - to propose or formally enter as a candidate for appointment or election

Etymology

From the past participle of the verb 'appoint', which comes from Old French 'apointer', from Late Latin 'appunctare' (to bring to a point, to arrange).

2. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
officially chosen or selected for a job or position

Examples

His addle-brained knight-errant, self appointed to the ridiculous position in an age when armor had already been relegated to museums and the chivalrous code of knight-errantry had become a joke, is, as Cervantes no doubt intended, a gaunt but gracious symbol of good, moving soberly and sincerely in a world of cynics, hypocrites and rogues.
A notable example of this was the discussion of Christian unity by the Catholic Archbishop of Liverpool, Dr. Heenan, and the Anglican Archbishop of York, Dr. Ramsey, recently appointed Archbishop of Canterbury.

Synset Examples

the one appointed for guard duty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elected - chosen by a vote for a position or office
  • Antonym: dismissed - removed from a job or position

Etymology

From Middle English 'appoynten', from Old French 'apointer', from Late Latin 'adpunctare' (to agree upon).

3. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
fixed or set officially, especially by an order or command

Examples

If death receives more than its share of attention from the theologian and if sin receives less than its share, the gift of the life eternal through Christ begins to look like the divinely appointed means of rescue from temporal, i.e., created, existence.

Synset Examples

at the time appointed (or the appointed time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheduled - arranged or planned for a particular time
  • Antonym: random - not fixed or predetermined

Etymology

From the verb 'appoint', from Old French *apointer* (to arrange, settle), from Latin *ad-* (to) + *punctum* (point, mark).

4. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a room, building, etc.) provided with furniture and fittings, especially of a tasteful or luxurious kind.

Examples

Jefferson Lawrence was alone at the small, perfectly appointed table by the window looking out over the river.

Synset Examples

a house that is beautifully appointed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-appointed - having attractive or luxurious furnishings and fittings
