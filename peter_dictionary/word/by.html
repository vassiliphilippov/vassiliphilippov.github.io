1. ADV

/ baɪ / (AmE), / baɪ / (BrE)
(adverb) So as to pass from one side to the other of a given point; past.

Examples

He came by and repeated, "Po' Chavis "!
A carriage crunched by, its dim lights filtering through the gloom.
It wasn't until a month went by that he opened his mouth to say more than two words to somebody.

Synset Examples

every hour a train goes past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past - having gone by or beyond a point

Etymology

From Old English 'bi' (near, by, beside), related to Dutch 'bij', German 'bei'.
