theorize

1. VERB

/ ˈθiəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈθɪəraɪz / (BrE)
To form or propose a theory or explanation based on incomplete or uncertain evidence.

Examples

As retinal images are conceded to be an integral function of the brain it seems logical to suppose that the nerves, between the inner brain and the eyes, carry the direct drive for cooperation from the various brain centers - rather than to theorize on the transmission of an image which is already in required location.
Police theorize that a combination of dope, drink and drugs killed her.

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculate - form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Synonym: hypothesize - propose an explanation based on limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation

Etymology

From Greek *theōria* (contemplation, speculation).
