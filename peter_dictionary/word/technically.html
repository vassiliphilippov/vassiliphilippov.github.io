technically

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛknɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to technique, skill, or the technical aspects of a subject.

Examples

Technically it was fascinating, aurally spell-binding, and dramatically quite realistic.

Synset Examples

technically lagging behind the Japanese
a technically brilliant boxer

Etymology

From technical + -ly, where 'technical' is from Greek tekhnikos (relating to art or skill).

2. ADV

/ ˈtɛknɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to technical skill, proficiency, or the use of technology

Examples

Just as exciting but in a more technically proficient way is Laura Stuart, whose complete control of her every movement is lovely to watch.

Synset Examples

a technically brilliant solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artistically - with regard to artistic skill or style

Etymology

From 'technical' + '-ly'; 'technical' from Greek tekhnikos (of art, skill).
