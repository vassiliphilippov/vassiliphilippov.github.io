salve

1. NOUN

/ sæv / (AmE), / sɑːv / (BrE)
A semisolid substance, often containing a medicinal agent, applied to the skin to soothe, heal, or protect.

Examples

John said: "I have some antiseptic salve with me, but it's too late for that ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ointment - medicated substance applied to skin for healing
  • Synonym: balm - fragrant ointment used to heal or soothe the skin

Etymology

From Old English 'sealf' (ointment, salve); related to Old High German 'salba', Old Norse 'salva'.
