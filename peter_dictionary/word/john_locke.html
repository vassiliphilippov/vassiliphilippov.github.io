John Locke

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn lɑk / (AmE), / dʒɒn lɒk / (BrE)
(1632–1704) English philosopher known for his work on empiricism and theories of knowledge, advocating that all knowledge derives from sensory experience.

Examples

That John Locke's philosophy of the social contract fathered the American Revolution with its Declaration of Independence, I believe, we generally accept.
