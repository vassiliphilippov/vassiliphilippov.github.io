swagger

1. VERB

/ ˈswæɡər / (AmE), / ˈswæɡə / (BrE)
to walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or showy way, often to impress others

Examples

He, too, cocked his cap at a jaunty angle, jingled marbles in his pocket, and swaggered down Main Street.
Among my school and neighborhood friends, during the next months, I bragged and swaggered and pompously described my impending date.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant or conceited gait

Etymology

Probably from earlier Scots 'swagger', meaning to sway or strut, of imitative origin.
