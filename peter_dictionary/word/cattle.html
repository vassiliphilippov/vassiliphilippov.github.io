cattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈkætl̩ / (BrE)
Domesticated bovine animals such as cows and bulls, regarded collectively, regardless of sex or age.

Examples

Finding him dead, Cook caught Russell's horse and rode to the cattle foreman's house to report the incident and request bloodhounds to trail the assassins.
Cattle stealing and killing, again serious during the spring of 1891, placed the land grant company officers in a perplexing position.

Below in the open bay facing Manhattan was Staten Island, gritty with clam shells and mud flats behind which nested farms, cattle barns, and berry thickets.
There was brush, and stands of pine that no grass could grow under, and places so steep that cattle would n't stop to graze.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cow - adult female of domesticated cattle
  • Synonym: bull - uncastrated adult male of domesticated cattle

Etymology

From Old French *chatel* (property, chattel), from Latin *capitalis* (of the head, principal), originally referring to personal property, especially livestock.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈkætəl / (BrE)
Large domesticated bovine animals kept for milk or meat; cattle refers to the group as a whole, regardless of sex or age.

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French 'catel' (personal property, especially livestock), from Latin 'capitale' (property, stock).
