viper

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪpə / (BrE)
A venomous snake of the family Viperidae, found in the Old World, distinguished by long, hollow, venom-conducting fangs in the upper jaw.

Examples

Before this week is out... ... we are going to knock down their doors... ... and shed the light of the law on that nest of vipers.

Etymology

From Latin 'vipera' (snake, viper), possibly from 'vivus' (alive) and 'parere' (to bear), referring historically to live birth.
