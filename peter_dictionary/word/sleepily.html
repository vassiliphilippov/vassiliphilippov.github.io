sleepily

1. ADV

/ ˈsliːpəli / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows or suggests drowsiness or a desire to sleep.

Examples

Sleepily, as if half reluctant to break the silence, he lifted his voice: "Rhu-beb ni ice fresh rhu-beb today "!

Synset Examples

the two children who were snuggled sleepily in the back of the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drowsily - in a drowsy manner, suggestive of being nearly asleep
  • Antonym: energetically - in an energetic or lively manner

Etymology

From "sleepy" + suffix "-ly".
