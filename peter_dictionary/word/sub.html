sub

1. VERB

/ sʌb / (AmE), / sʌb / (BrE)
to act as a substitute for someone, especially in a job or activity for a short time

Examples

Hector Lopez, subbing for Berra, smashed a 3 - run homer off Bill Henry during another 5 - run explosion in the fourth.

Synset Examples

The young teacher had to substitute for the sick colleague
The skim milk substitutes for cream--we are on a strict diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to serve as a replacement for someone or something

Etymology

Shortened from 'substitute', originally from Latin 'substituere' (to put in place of).
