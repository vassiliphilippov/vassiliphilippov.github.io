derrick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪk / (BrE)
A tall framework erected over an oil well to support the drilling equipment and allow drill tubes to be raised and lowered.

Examples

She saw it then, the distant derrick of the wildcat - a test well in unexplored country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil rig - platform for drilling oil wells

Etymology

Originally from the surname 'Derrick', after a 17th-century English hangman, the word came to refer to a type of gallows and later was applied to lifting devices.
