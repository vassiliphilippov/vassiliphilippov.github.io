strut

1. NOUN

/ strʌt / (AmE), / strʌt / (BrE)
A proud, stiff, and confident way of walking that shows you think you are important.

Examples

His steps were short and stiff, and, with his head thrown back, his progress was a supercilious strut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagger - a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive walk

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'strouten' (to swell, bulge out).

2. VERB

/ strʌt / (AmE), / strʌt / (BrE)
to walk in a proud or confident manner, often with the aim of impressing others

Examples

The way she strutted down the street, the Old Man would have been blind not to have noticed both.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a confident and arrogant or self-important way
  • Degree Lower: saunter - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, without hurry or effort

Etymology

Probably from Old English *strūtian* (to stand out stiffly), related to *strūt* (protuberance, display).
