athleticism

1. NOUN

/ əˈθlɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈθlɛtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
The physical qualities typical of an athlete, such as strength, energy, agility, and fitness.

Examples

And the monastic communities were supposed to be made up of volunteers selected only after a novitiate which would test their religious aptitude for monastic rigors, their spiritual athleticism.

Synset Examples

his music is characterized by a happy athleticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - the ability to move quickly and easily
  • Antonym: sluggishness - lack of energy or alertness

Etymology

From 'athletic' + '-ism'. 'Athletic' from Greek 'athlētēs' (contestant in the games).
