gusto

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌstoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌstəʊ / (BrE)
hearty or keen enjoyment, especially in doing something with enthusiastic and vigorous energy

Examples

Rector had no doubt that Hino would come back from the village bursting with information, ready to impart it with his customary gusto, liberally embellished with his active imagnation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval

Etymology

From Italian 'gusto' (taste), from Latin 'gustus' (taste).
