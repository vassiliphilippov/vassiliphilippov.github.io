chord

1. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
a group of three or more musical notes played together to produce a harmonious sound

Examples

, and Berman sifted out all alone on the stage with the ambling chords and beat of the song just whispering into being.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: triad - a chord consisting of three notes
  • Synonym: harmony - the combination of musical notes to produce chords and chord progressions

Etymology

From Greek khordē (string of a musical instrument), via Latin and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
A straight line connecting two points on a curve, especially in geometry and mathematics.

Examples

On C, from the point P at f to the point Q at f, we construct the **chord**, and upon the chord as a side erect a square in such a way that as s approaches zero the square is inside C.
