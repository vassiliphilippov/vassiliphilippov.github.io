craftsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræfts.mən / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːfts.mən / (BrE)
A person who is highly skilled in a particular craft or trade, especially one whose work is consistently of high quality.

Examples

However, in all of Prokofieff's music, young or mature, we find his profile - his "signature "- his craftsman's attitude.

Synset Examples

as an actor he was a consummate craftsman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artisan - a worker skilled in a particular craft
  • Antonym: amateur - a person lacking professional skill or expertise

Etymology

From Middle English craftsmen, from Old English cræft + man (man).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræfts.mən / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːfts.mən / (BrE)
A person who is skilled at making things by hand, especially using traditional methods and tools.

Examples

Keep the retirement age flexible so skilled craftsmen such as tool and die makers can be kept on the job for the convenience of the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artisan - a person skilled at making things by hand
  • Synonym: craftswoman - a female craftsman; a woman skilled in a craft

Etymology

From Middle English 'craftsman', from 'craft' (skill) + 'man'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkræfts.mən / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːfts.mən / (BrE)
a person who is highly skilled in a particular manual craft, such as woodworking, metalworking, or other artisanal trades

Examples

More detailed instructions may be obtained from your local building supply houses and craftsmen.

Synset Examples

the jewelry was made by internationally famous craftsmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artisan - a skilled manual worker, especially one who makes items by hand

Etymology

From craft (meaning 'skill') + man.
