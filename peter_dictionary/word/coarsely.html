coarsely

1. ADV

/ ˈkɔrsli / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsli / (BrE)
In a rough or large-grained manner; not finely or smoothly.

Examples

The surfaces were coarsely and finely granular and punctuated by clear, fluid filled cysts measuring up to 3 cm. in diameter.

Synset Examples

the surfaces were coarsely granular

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finely - in a delicate or small-grained way

Etymology

From 'coarse' + '-ly', where 'coarse' originates from Middle English 'cors', meaning rude or ordinary.
