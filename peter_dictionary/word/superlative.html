superlative

1. NOUN

/ suˈpɝːlətɪv / (AmE), / suˈpɜːlətɪv / (BrE)
An expression of the highest praise or exaggerated acclaim.

Examples

The piano performance on this Trout is one that really demands a search for superlatives.

Synset Examples

the critics lavished superlatives on it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperbole - exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally

Etymology

From Latin superlativus, from superlatus (carried above), from super (above) + latus (carried).

2. ADJ

/ suːˈpɝːlətɪv / (AmE), / sjuːˈpɜːlətɪv / (BrE)
of the highest quality; superior to all others in degree, excellence, or merit

Examples

The Hephzibah Menuhin-Amadeus Quartet (Angel) and Victor Babin-Festival Quartet (RCA Victor) editions give us superlative string playing (both in symphonic style) crippled by unimaginative piano playing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: excellent - of very high quality
  • Degree Lower: outstanding - extremely good; distinguished from others

Etymology

From Latin superlativus (exaggerated, extremely high), from superlatus (raised above).
