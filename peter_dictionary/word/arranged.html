arranged

1. ADJ

/ əˈreɪndʒd / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒd / (BrE)
Put in a particular order or pattern, often with care or intention.

Examples

It is characterized by the presence of incompletely developed secondary lobules; well defined, but haphazardly arranged, interlobular septa and a thick, remarkably vascular pleura (fig. 9).
It takes a great deal of abstraction to free oneself from the primitive impression of larger unities of power and influence and to view one's world simply as a collection of sense data arranged in such and such sequence and pattern, devoid of all power to move the feelings and actions except in so far as they present themselves for inspection.

Synset Examples

the carefully arranged chessmen
haphazardly arranged interlobular septa
comfortable chairs arranged around the fireplace

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disordered - not arranged or lacking a specific arrangement
  • Synonym: organized - arranged in a systematic way

Etymology

From the verb 'arrange', from Old French *arranger*, from *a-* (to) + *ranger* (to arrange).

2. ADJ

/ əˈreɪndʒd / (AmE), / əˈreɪn(d)ʒd / (BrE)
(of a plan, event, or situation) organized or planned in advance, often deliberately and formally.

Examples

He'’s the one who arranged this whole thing through your man Turnbull.

Synset Examples

an arranged marriage
