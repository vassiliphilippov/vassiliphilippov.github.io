glowering

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
showing an angry, brooding, or sullen expression; having a dark, threatening look

Examples

Glowering looks met them in the bar, but there was no attempt to halt them.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowling - showing an angry or bad-tempered expression
  • Antonym: smiling - showing a happy or pleasant expression

Etymology

Present participle of 'glower', from Middle English 'gloren' (to stare) of uncertain origin.
