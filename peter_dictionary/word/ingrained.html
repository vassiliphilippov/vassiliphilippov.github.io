ingrained

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
(of habits, beliefs, or ideas) deeply rooted or firmly fixed in a person or thing; difficult to change.

Examples

It'’s in their blood.

Synset Examples

deep-rooted prejudice
deep-seated differences of opinion
implanted convictions
ingrained habits of a lifetime
a deeply planted need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep-rooted - (of feelings or ideas) firmly established and difficult to change
  • Synonym: deep-seated - (of beliefs, habits, or feelings) firmly established at a deep or basic level
  • Antonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface; not deep or firmly fixed

Etymology

From Middle English grain (meaning 'grain', 'dye'), originally referring to dye fixed in fibers, later generalized to mean 'deeply fixed'.
