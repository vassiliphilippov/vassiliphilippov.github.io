verse

1. NOUN

/ vɝs / (AmE), / vɜːs / (BrE)
A piece of poetry, often forming part of a poem or song.

Examples

On Earth elections continued and a very advanced poet published a limited edition of verse consisting entirely of punctuation marks and spaces; Time magazine reviewed it and suggested that the Federation Assembly Daily Record should be translated into the medium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poem - a piece of writing expressing feelings or ideas with distinctive style and rhythm

Etymology

From Latin 'versus' (a line, row, especially in writing).

2. NOUN

/ vɝs / (AmE), / vɜːs / (BrE)
A single line of poetry, especially one with a set rhythm or meter.

Examples

A verse familiar to all grammarians is the quatrain:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - a single row of words in poetry or prose

Etymology

From Latin 'versus', meaning 'a line, row, or turn of the plow', later used for a line of writing.

3. NOUN

/ vɝs / (AmE), / vɜːs / (BrE)
Literature written in metrical form; poetry.

Examples

Equally a master of prose and verse, he recreates the glory of Sweden in the past and continues it into the present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetry - literature written in verse; poems collectively
  • Antonym: prose - written or spoken language in its ordinary, non-metrical form

Etymology

From Latin 'versus' (a line or row, especially in writing or poetry).
