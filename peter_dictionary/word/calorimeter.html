calorimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈrɪm.ɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈrɪm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the amount of heat involved in a chemical or physical process

Examples

The method of calibration employs a fixed resistance element as a calorimeter.

Etymology

From Latin 'calor' meaning 'heat' + Greek 'metron' meaning 'measure'.
