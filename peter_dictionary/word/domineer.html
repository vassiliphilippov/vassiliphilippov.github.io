domineer

1. VERB

/ ˌdɑːməˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
to assert one's will over others in an arrogant or overbearing way; to rule over someone in a harsh, autocratic manner

Examples

No one seriously contends, of course, that the domineering wife is, sexually speaking, a new character in our world.

Synset Examples

her husband and mother-in-law tyrannize her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrannize - to rule or treat (someone) despotically or cruelly
  • Synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone perceived as vulnerable
  • Synonym: subjugate - to bring under domination or control, especially by conquest

Etymology

From Dutch 'domineren' and French 'dominer', from Latin 'dominari' (to rule, to dominate).
