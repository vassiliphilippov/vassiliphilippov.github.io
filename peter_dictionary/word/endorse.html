endorse

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to express support or approval for someone or something, especially publicly or officially

Examples

Rep. Mac Barber of Commerce is asking the House in a privilege resolution to "endorse increased federal support for public education, provided that such funds be received and expended "as state funds.
Society here and abroad has been built around the dominating male - even the Bible appears to endorse the concept.
In 1958, the Conference endorsed birth control as the responsibility laid by God on parents everywhere.
I endorse pending legislation that will restore the traditional relationship between retired and active duty pay rates.
He said that the group has no candidates for the charter commission in mind at present, but that it will undoubtedly endorse candidates when the time comes.

Synset Examples

He plumped for the Labor Party
I backed Kennedy in 1960

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give approval, comfort, or help to someone or something
  • Antonym: oppose - to disagree with or actively resist something or someone

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *indorsare*, from Latin *in-* (on) + *dorsum* (back), originally meaning ‘to write on the back’.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to officially approve or certify that something meets a particular standard

Examples

Their product had been endorsed by Good Housekeeping, the A. M. A., and the Veterinary Journal, among other repositories of higher wisdom, and before much longer if you did n't have a cake of their soap in the john, even your best friends would think you did n't bathe.

Synset Examples

certified grade AAA meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certify - to formally declare or confirm to be true or to meet standards
  • Synonym: approve - to officially accept as satisfactory or meeting standards
  • Antonym: disapprove - to withhold official approval or support

Etymology

From Latin *indorsare*, from *in-* ‘upon’ + *dorsum* ‘back’.

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
To declare one's public approval or support for someone or something, such as a candidate, plan, or product.

Examples

In all probability, the council will screen and endorse candidates for the Assembly and for Congress, and then strive to put its full weight behind these pre-primary favorites.

Synset Examples

I'll second that motion
I can't back this plan
endorse a new project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give help or encouragement to
  • Antonym: oppose - to disapprove of or resist something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *indorsare* (to write on the back), from Latin *in-* (upon) + *dorsum* (back).
