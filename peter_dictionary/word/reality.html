reality

1. NOUN

/ riˈæləti / (AmE), / riˈalɪti / (BrE)
The state of things as they actually exist, rather than as they may appear or might be imagined; the totality of experiences and facts that exist independently of individual perception or belief.

Examples

One of the most widespread features of contemporary thought is the almost universal disbelief in the reality of spirit.
In such a world the words "matter "and "spirit "both referred to directly known realities in the common experience of all.

When, therefore, it turned its attention to the concrete entities with which popular imagination had peopled the world of spirit, these entities soon lost whatever status they had enjoyed as actual elements of external reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actuality - the state of being actual or real
  • Antonym: illusion - a false idea or belief; something that is not real
  • Antonym: fantasy - imagination unrestricted by reality

Etymology

From Late Latin realitas (the quality of being real), from Latin realis (actual).
