prosperity

1. NOUN

/ prɑˈspɛrəti / (AmE), / prɒˈspɛrəti / (BrE)
A state of being successful, especially in financial or economic terms; the condition of having wealth, good fortune, or economic growth.

Examples

"Uncle Sam "was, indeed, a rich uncle to Prokofieff, in those opulent, post-war victory years of peace and prosperity, bold speculations and extravaganzas, enjoyment and pleasure: "The Golden Twenties ".
Prosperity is a worthy objective; but if providing higher standards of living gets in the way of producing sufficient guns to resist Communist aggression, then material sacrifices and denials will have to be made.
[TRANSLATING] I want to thank this distinguished group of American industrialists [TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] for continuing to work with Cuba [TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] for the greatest period of prosperity [TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] in her entire history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affluence - wealth; abundant supply of money, goods, or property
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Latin prosperitas, from prosperus (favorable, fortunate).
