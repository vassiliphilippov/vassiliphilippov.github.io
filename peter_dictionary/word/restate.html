restate

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to say or express something again, often in a different way for clarity or emphasis

Examples

This restates the already widely known facts in terms of prosodies.
He plays his sax principally for beauty of tone, rather than for scintillating flights of meaningless improvisations, and he has a quiet way of getting back and restating the melody after the improvising is over.

Synset Examples

She kept reiterating her request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again
  • Synonym: paraphrase - to express the same meaning in different words

Etymology

From re- (again) + state (to express), modeled after the pattern of similar verbs.
