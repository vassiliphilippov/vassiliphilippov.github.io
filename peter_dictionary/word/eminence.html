eminence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnəns / (BrE)
the quality of being highly respected or important, especially because of great skill or achievement

Examples

One is that they were established, or gained eminence, under pressure provided by these same immigrants, from whom the old families wished to segregate their children.

Synset Examples

a scholar of great eminence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - excellence that sets someone apart from others
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From Latin 'eminentia' (prominence, distinction), from 'eminere' (to stand out).
