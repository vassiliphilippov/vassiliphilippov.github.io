deportee

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌpɔrˈti / (AmE), / dɪˌpɔːˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who is expelled from a country by legal or official order

Examples

Deportees.
As the camp emptied, it was refilled by a draining of the camps and towns around Lublin, then by deportees from outside Poland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - a person who has been banished from their country, often for political reasons

Etymology

From French déporté, past participle of déporter (to deport).
