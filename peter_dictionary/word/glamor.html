glamor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæmər / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmə / (BrE)
Attractive beauty or charm, often in a way that is exciting or alluring, sometimes with an element of sex-appeal.

Examples

Then epistolatory me was a foreign correspondent dispatching exciting cables and communiques, full of dash and wit and glamor, quoting from the books I read, imitating the grand styles of the authors recommended by a teacher in whose special, after school class I was enrolled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glamour - British spelling: alluring beauty or charm

Etymology

Altered spelling of 'glamour', from Scots 'glamer' (magic, enchantment), ultimately from Middle English 'grammar' (learning, occult knowledge).
