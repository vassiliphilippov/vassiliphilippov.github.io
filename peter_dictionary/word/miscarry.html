miscarry

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈkæri / (AmE), / mɪsˈkæri / (BrE)
(of a plan, purpose, or action) to fail or be unsuccessful.

Examples

But if his purpose was to inspire terror, his action could hardly have miscarried more obviously.

Synset Examples

Where do today's public schools fail?
The attempt to rescue the hostages failed miserably

Related Terms

  • Antonym: succeed - to achieve the intended aim or result
  • Synonym: fail - to not succeed in achieving something

Etymology

From mis- (expressing failure or error) + carry.
