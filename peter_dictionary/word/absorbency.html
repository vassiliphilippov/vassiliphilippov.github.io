absorbency

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrbənsi / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbənsi / (BrE)
The ability of a material to soak up liquids easily; the quality or property of being absorbent.

Examples

The great absorbency of this tissue and the fact that it is easier to control than a sponge makes it an ideal tool for the watercolorist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - having the ability to soak up liquids
  • Antonym: impermeability - property of not allowing liquids to pass through

Etymology

From 'absorbent' + '-cy', modeled after similar nouns in English.
