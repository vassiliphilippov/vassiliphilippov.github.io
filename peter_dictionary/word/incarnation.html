Incarnation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌkɑrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(In Christianity) The doctrine that God became human in the person of Jesus Christ.

Examples

Following the theme of Incarnation in the Gospels, the Christian artist and critic sees in the most commonplace and ordinary events "figures "of divine power and reality.

Etymology

From Latin incarnatio (making flesh), from incarnare (to make flesh).
