redeposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.dɪ.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪ.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the process of matter being removed from one place and deposited in another, especially unintentionally during cleaning or erosion

Examples

Soil redeposition is evaluated by washing clean swatches with the dirty ones.
Cleaned cloth must be protected against the redeposition of dispersed soil.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: deposition - the act of depositing something

Etymology

From re- (again) + deposition (from Latin depositio, act of laying down).
