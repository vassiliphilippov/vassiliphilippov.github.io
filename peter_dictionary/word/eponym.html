eponym

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpəˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛpənɪm / (BrE)
A person after whom a place, invention, or discovery is named or thought to be named.

Examples

Louis' restaurant in the Bronx. - Is it reliable? - My man in McCluskey's precinct.

Synset Examples

Constantine I is the eponym for Constantinople

Related Terms

  • Synonym: namesake - person having the same name as another, especially one named after another

Etymology

From Greek 'eponymos', from 'epi' (upon) + 'onyma' (name).
