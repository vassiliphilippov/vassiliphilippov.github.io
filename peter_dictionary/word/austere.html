austere

1. ADJ

/ ɔˈstɪr / (AmE), / ɔːˈstɪə / (BrE)
having a plain and simple appearance, with no decoration or comfort

Examples

She was forty-nine at this time, a lanky woman of breeding with an austere, narrow face which had the distinction of a steeple or some architecture that had been designed long ago for a stubborn sort of prayer.

Synset Examples

a stark interior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ornate - elaborately decorated, opposite of austere in appearance

Etymology

From Latin austerus (severe, strict), from Greek austēros (harsh, rough, bitter).
