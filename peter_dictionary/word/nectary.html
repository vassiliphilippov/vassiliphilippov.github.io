nectary

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.tə.ri / (BrE)
A gland in a plant, often a small protuberance or depression, that secretes nectar.

Examples

Those are the nectaries or honey glands (Fig. 26, page 74).

Etymology

From Latin 'nectarium', from 'nectar' (drink of the gods in Greek mythology).
