append

1. VERB

/ əˈpɛnd / (AmE), / əˈpɛnd / (BrE)
to add something to the end of a written document, list, or text

Examples

As a finale is appended a close-up of one of the band taking aim and firing his revolver straight at the audience.

Synset Examples

He appended a glossary to his novel where he used an invented language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: add - to put together with something else so as to increase the number, size, or amount
  • Synonym: attach - to fasten, join, or connect something

Etymology

From Latin 'appendere' (to hang upon), from 'ad-' (to) + 'pendere' (to hang).
