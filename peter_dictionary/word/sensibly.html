sensibly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛnsəbli / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəbli / (BrE)
In a way that shows good sense or judgment; reasonably or prudently.

Examples

At the first restaurant he sensibly pulled up to go in for his dinner, and as a consequence did not see Cobb strike the open range at the mouth of the canyon and head straight across the swells for Antler.

Synset Examples

he acted sensibly in the crisis
speak more sanely about these affairs
acted quite reasonably

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrationally - in an unreasonable or illogical manner
  • Synonym: reasonably - in a fair, sensible, or moderate manner

Etymology

From sensible + -ly, after Latin sensibiliter.
