remaining

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmeɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
still left after other parts have been used, removed, or dealt with.

Examples

The remaining (incomplete) components all have an even number of ordinary points at any argument, and are defined only on a proper sub-interval of [ 0, T ].
In the center of any open space remaining our grandfathers had planted syringa and sweet shrub, snowball, rose-of-Sharon and balm-of-Gilead.
On April 25, the White House reported that a total embargo of remaining U. S. trade with Cuba was being considered.
I have no wish to fill my few remaining years... ...grieving for the loss of old friends... ...or their sons.

Synset Examples

leftover meatloaf
she had a little money left over so she went to a movie
some odd dollars left
saved the remaining sandwiches for supper
unexpended provisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leftover - not used up; surplus after the main portion has been used
  • Synonym: residual - remaining after the greater part or quantity has gone
  • Antonym: spent - used up; no longer remaining

Etymology

Present participle of 'remain', from Old French 'remaindre', from Latin 'remanere' (to stay behind).
