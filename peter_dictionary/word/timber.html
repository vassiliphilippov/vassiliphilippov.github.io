timber

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə / (BrE)
Wood from trees that has been cut and prepared for use as building material.

Examples

Under this program, property sales specialists in the Small Business Administration regional offices help small business concerns to locate Federal property for sale and insure that small firms have the opportunity to bid competitively for surplus personal and real property and certain natural resources, including timber from the national forests.
Across the bay the Palisades were heavy in green timber; their rock paths led down to the Hudson.
Accordingly Selkirk's agents ordered the settlers to move north, and by October, John Halkett had torn down both posts, floating the timber to "the Forks "in rafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumber - wood that has been processed for building

Etymology

Old English 'timber' (building, structure, wood, trees for building); related to Old High German 'zimbar' (timber, building).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə / (BrE)
A large beam or structural piece of wood, especially one used in the framing of buildings or ships.

Examples

Closer still, regular barricades of barbed wire hung on timber supports.
Felix threw his head back and laughed a laugh that shook the timbers of even that solidly built old house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of wood or metal used in construction

Etymology

From Old English 'timber' (building, structure, wood for building), related to German 'Zimmer' and Dutch 'timmer'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɪm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪm.bə / (BrE)
A large, squared piece or beam of wood prepared for use in building or carpentry.

Examples

The bombproof was a low-ceilinged structure of heavy timbers covered with earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of squared timber or metal used to support weight in construction
  • Synonym: plank - a long, thin, flat piece of timber, used in building

Etymology

From Old English 'timber' (building, material for building), related to German 'Zimmer' (room/building).
