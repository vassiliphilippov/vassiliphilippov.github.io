nod

1. VERB

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
to lower and raise one's head slightly and briefly, especially in greeting, assent, or understanding

Examples

Jane nodded with a pleasant smile.
Adam nodded.
He left with all the joyous spirit of a child going on a holiday, nodding attentively as Rector gave him his final instructions.
Fletcher nodded as he listened to the instructions and said he would arrange the things Rector requested.
Johnson nodded.

Synset Examples

He nodded his approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shake - to move one's head from side to side, often signifying disagreement or refusal

Etymology

From Middle English nodden; origin uncertain, possibly imitative.

2. NOUN

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
a quick downward motion of the head as a sign of agreement, greeting, or command

Examples

They answered him in monosyllables, nods, occasionally muttering in Greek to one another, awaiting the word from Papa, who restlessly cracked his knuckles, anxious to stuff himself into his white Cadillac and burst off to the freeway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shake - side-to-side head motion to indicate refusal or disagreement

Etymology

From Middle English nodden, probably of Low German origin.

3. VERB

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
to lower and raise one's head slightly and quickly, often to show agreement, acknowledgment, or confirmation

Examples

When Joe identified himself, he nodded, unsmiling, and ushered him into a sedate living room.
Styka nodded and sniffed and blew his nose vociferously.
Vecchio was nodding and pointing at the large suitcase he held.
The doorman began to nod his head automatically, then remembered who Gilborn was, what had happened to him the night before.
Now, as he passed the open counter that divided the assembly room from the business office, he nodded and said good night to the station keeper and his clerks, not stopping to hear the day-watch playback of his chewing out.

Synset Examples

The teacher nodded when the student gave the right answer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shake - move the head from side to side to indicate 'no' or disagreement

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, akin to Middle English nodden.

4. NOUN

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
an act of moving the head up and down, usually as a gesture of agreement, greeting, or acknowledgment

Examples

The straight, black hair flopped in a vigorous nod, the slender nose plunged toward glass teeth and drew safely back.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shake - to move the head from side to side, often as a gesture of disagreement

Etymology

Probably from Middle English nodden, of imitative origin.
