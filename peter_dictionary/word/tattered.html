tattered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtætɚd / (AmE), / ˈtætəd / (BrE)
Old and torn; in poor condition due to long or hard use.

Examples

Behind them shambled a long column of weak, tattered men.

Synset Examples

a man in a tattered shirt
the tattered flag
tied up in tattered brown paper
a tattered barefoot boy
a tatterdemalion prince

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: ragged - (of cloth or clothing) old and torn
  • Antonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled

Etymology

From Middle English 'tatered', related to 'tatters', meaning torn pieces of cloth.
