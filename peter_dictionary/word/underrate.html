underrate

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndəˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈreɪt / (BrE)
to judge something or someone as less important, valuable, or capable than they really are; to make too low an estimate of their worth or ability.

Examples

Since the strength of the Mexicans had been underrated, too small a posse had been collected, and since the deputy had not been provided with search warrants, MacPherson and his men decided it was much wiser to withdraw.

Synset Examples

he underestimated the work that went into the renovation
Don't underestimate the danger of such a raft trip on this river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underestimate - to estimate something as less than the actual value or strength; to judge too low
  • Antonym: overrate - to estimate or value something more highly than it deserves

Etymology

Formed from under- (below) + rate (to estimate or value), dating from the 17th century.
