/ ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi / (BrE)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established
  • Antonym: unsteady - changing, shaking, or not regular in movement, position, or behavior

Etymology

From Middle English stedi, from Old English stedig (firm, fixed), related to 'stand.'

2. VERB

/ ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
to make something or oneself stable, firm, or less shaky; to stop something from wobbling, shaking, or being unsteady

Examples

Lolotte held a patch of leather, Rev steadied something, a tiny brad, waiting for George's poised hammer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabilize - make or become unlikely to change, fail, or give way
  • Antonym: unsteady - not stable or firmly fixed; liable to shake

Etymology

From Middle English stedi (“firm, fixed”), related to Old English stede (“place, position”).

3. VERB

/ ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
