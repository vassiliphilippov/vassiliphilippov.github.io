apprenticeship

1. NOUN

/ əˈprɛn.tɪs.ʃɪp / (AmE), / əˈprɛn.tɪs.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or period of being an apprentice, during which a person learns a trade or skill by working under a skilled employer

Examples

What we need is to get back to the group, to learning and apprenticeship, to the cafe and the school ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprentice - a person learning a trade from a skilled employer
  • Synonym: internship - a temporary position for practical work experience

Etymology

From apprentice + -ship; 'apprentice' from Old French 'aprentis', from Late Latin 'apprenticius' (someone learning).
