ballast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.æst / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əst / (BrE)
Heavy material placed in a ship or airship to improve its stability and balance.

Examples

If his mother loves him, he clings to that love as a ballast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterweight - a weight used to balance another weight

Etymology

From Middle Dutch or Low German *ballast* (cargo, weight used to steady a ship).
