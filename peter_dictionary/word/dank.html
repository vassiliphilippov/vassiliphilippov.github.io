dank

1. ADJ

/ dæŋk / (AmE), / dæŋk / (BrE)
(of a place) unpleasantly damp, cool, and often musty.

Examples

He stopped pacing, leaned against the dank, timbered wall and let his mind drift.

Synset Examples

a clammy handshake
clammy weather
a dank cellar
dank rain forests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clammy - unpleasantly damp or moist

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'dank' meaning 'wet, moist'.
