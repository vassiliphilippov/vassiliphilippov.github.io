hypertrophied

1. ADJ

/ haɪˈpɜr.trəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜː.trəˌfaɪd / (BrE)
(of an organ or body part) abnormally enlarged due to an increase in the size of its cells.

Examples

Many of the myocardial fibers were hypertrophied and had large, irregular, basophilic nuclei.

Synset Examples

hypertrophied myocardial fibers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypertrophy - the enlargement of an organ or tissue from the increase in the size of its cells

Etymology

From 'hypertrophy' + '-ed'. 'Hypertrophy' from Greek 'hyper-' (over) + 'trophē' (nourishment).
