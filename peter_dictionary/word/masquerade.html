masquerade

1. VERB

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to pretend to be someone or something that you are not, especially in order to deceive others

Examples

Professionally a lawyer, that is to say associated with dignity, reserve, discipline, with much that is essentially middle-class, he is compelled by an impossible love to exhibit himself dressed up, disguised - that is, paradoxically, revealed - as a child, and, worse, as a whore masquerading as a child.

Synset Examples

he is masquerading as an expert on the internet
This silly novel is masquerading as a serious historical treaty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: impersonate - pretend to be another person for entertainment or fraud
  • Antonym: reveal - to make known something that was secret or hidden

Etymology

From Italian 'mascherata' (masked assembly), from 'maschera' (mask) via Middle French 'mascarade'.

2. VERB

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɑːskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to pretend to be someone or something else, often to deceive; to take part in a masquerade or disguised event

Examples

The Kremlin's goal is the isolation and capture, not of Ghana, but of the United States - and this purpose may be served very well by countries that masquerade under a "neutralist "mask, yet in fact are dependable auxiliaries of the Soviet Foreign Office.
The liberal pressure bloc (which coyly masquerades under the name Democratic Study Group) had fought the committee before, and had always lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - to change appearance to conceal identity
  • Synonym: pretend - to act as if something is true when it is not

Etymology

From French *mascarade*, from Italian *mascherata*, from *maschera* (mask).

3. NOUN

/ ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɑːskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
A pretense or false display of feelings, intentions, or appearances.

Examples

I'’ll do business with you, but the fact is that I despise your masquerade, the dishonest way you pose yourself.

Synset Examples

a beggar's masquerade of wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Synonym: semblance - the outward appearance or apparent form of something
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest, not pretending or faking

Etymology

From French 'mascarade', from Italian 'mascherata' (masked ball).
