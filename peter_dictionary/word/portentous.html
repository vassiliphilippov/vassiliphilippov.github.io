portentous

1. ADJ

/ pɔrˈtɛntəs / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛntəs / (BrE)
giving a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen; full of momentous significance or awe-inspiring.

Examples

There seems to be an unfortunate assumption that an hour of Chicago style jazz in prime evening time, for example, could not be justified without the trimmings of a portentous documentary.

Synset Examples

such a portentous...monster raised all my curiosity
a prodigious vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen
  • Antonym: auspicious - suggesting a positive or successful outcome; favorable

Etymology

From Latin portentum (omen, portent) + -ous.

2. ADJ

/ pɔrˈtɛntəs / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛntəs / (BrE)
Giving a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen; ominous.

Examples

The Old Man near the corner let the shadow pass over him, sensing something portentous in it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
  • Antonym: auspicious - suggesting a positive or favorable outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'portentosus', from 'portentum' (omen, portent).
