coordination

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɔrdəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability to use different parts of the body smoothly and efficiently together

Examples

The gymnast must develop strength, flexibility, coordination, timing, rhythm, courage, discipline, persistence and the desire for perfection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: agility - ability to move quickly and easily
  • Degree Higher: dexterity - skill in performing tasks, especially with the hands

Etymology

From Latin 'coordinatio' (arrangement), from 'co-' (together) + 'ordinatio' (order).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌɔrdəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the organization of different elements or activities so that they work together effectively and harmoniously

Examples

Assistant Secretaries of State will now carry an increased burden of active formulation and coordination of policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - the act of arranging elements systematically; the process of organizing
  • Synonym: integration - the process of combining things to form a whole
  • Antonym: disorganization - the state of being disordered or lacking organization

Etymology

From French 'coordination', from Latin 'coordinatio' (arrangement with others).
