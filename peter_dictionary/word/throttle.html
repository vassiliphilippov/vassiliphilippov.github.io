throttle

1. VERB

/ ˈθrɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtəl / (BrE)
To restrict or severely limit the progress, development, or freedom of something or someone.

Examples

He is throttling the liberty my father gave his life to win "!
As the press in Cuba was gradually throttled by the Castro regime, more and more Cuban publishers, editors and correspondents were forced into exile.

Synset Examples

restrict the use of this parking lot
limit the time you can spend with your friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
  • Synonym: limit - to place limits on
  • Antonym: allow - to permit or let something happen

Etymology

From Old English 'throtu' (throat), originally meaning to choke or strangle, later extended figuratively.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtl / (BrE)
A device, typically a valve or lever, that controls the flow of fuel to an engine, thereby regulating its speed or power.

Examples

Greg pushed the radio button on his throttle.
Lots of throttle.
Greg had the stick forward and the throttle up before he heard the two "Rogers ".
Greg slammed his throttle to the fire wall and rammed up the RPM, and the engine responded as if it had been waiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accelerator - a device for increasing speed in an engine or vehicle
  • Antonym: brake - a device for slowing or stopping a vehicle or engine

Etymology

From Middle English 'throtel', a diminutive of 'throte' (throat).
