king

1. NOUN

/ kɪŋ / (AmE), / kɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who is the best or most important in a particular field, especially a top-ranked competitor

Examples

Kerr, who set the world record earlier this month in New York with a clocking of 1.09.3, wiped out Mills's early pace and beat the young Big 10 quarter-mile king by 5 yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - person who has won a competition or is the best at something

Etymology

From Old English 'cyning', of Germanic origin.
