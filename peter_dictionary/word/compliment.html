compliment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
A polite expression of praise, admiration, or approval.

Examples

(Special compliments to the double bass playing of Johann Krumpp:
And then the questions came, eager, interested questions, and many compliments on his having overcome his infirmity.
"Is that a compliment "?
I felt superior to the neighborhood friends I was leaving behind, felt older than my years, and was full of compliments for myself as I headed into the subway that was carrying its packs of passengers out of that dull borough and into the unstable, tantalizing excitement of Manhattan.
My compliments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive or insincere praise, often to gain favor
  • Antonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or act

Etymology

From Latin 'complēmentum' (that which fills up or completes), through French 'compliment' (expression of respect or congratulation).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪˌmɛnt / (BrE)
to express respect, admiration, or approval for someone or something

Examples

We feel that The Detroit News is to be complimented upon arranging for articles on these subjects and we hope that it will continue to provide material along wholesome lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for
  • Antonym: insult - to speak or act in a way that is disrespectful

Etymology

From Latin 'complēmentum' (that which fills up or completes), through Middle French 'compliment' (expression of respect, civility).
