puckish

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌkɪʃ / (BrE)
Having a mischievous or playful sense of humor; naughtily or impishly playful.

Examples

Mercer's lyrics are characterized by an unerring ear for rhythmic nuances, a puckish sense of humor expressed in language with a colloquial flair.

Synset Examples

teasing and worrying with impish laughter
a wicked prank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impish - mischievously playful or naughty

Etymology

From the name Puck, a mischievous fairy in English folklore, especially in Shakespeare's 'A Midsummer Night's Dream', plus the adjective suffix -ish.
