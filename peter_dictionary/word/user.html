user

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːzər / (AmE), / ˈjuːzə / (BrE)
a person who uses or operates something

Examples

When the words are used, we are never sure which of the traditional meanings the user may have in mind, or to what extent his revisions and rejections of former understandings correspond to ours.
And perhaps an observer of the vases will not go too far in deducing that the outlook of their makers and users was basically stable and secure.
Though far from completion, these studies indicated beyond a doubt that savings would result which would be of unprecedented benefit to the railroads concerned, their investors, their customers, their users, and to the public at large.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer - person who purchases and uses goods or services
  • Synonym: operator - person who operates equipment or machinery

Etymology

From Middle English 'user', from Anglo-Norman and Old French 'user', from Latin 'usus' (use), the noun agent of 'uti' (to use).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːzɚ / (AmE), / ˈjuːzə / (BrE)
a person who treats others selfishly or unethically, taking advantage of people for their own benefit

Examples

Angie worth his weight in gold right now, but these users, they sometimes went down fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploiter - one who unfairly takes advantage of others
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, opposite of a selfish user

Etymology

From 'use' + '-er', meaning one who uses (16th century); later also 'one who exploits'.
