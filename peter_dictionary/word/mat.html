mat

1. NOUN

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
A piece of material, typically thick and flat, used as a floor covering or to protect a surface.

Examples

The plants took zero nights in their stride, with nothing but a mat of straw over the glass to protect them.
After it has been seen, have the child start on a mat on hands and knees (a thin, inexpensive mat is quite sufficient for anything that does not require falling).

The candles were lighted, and we sat on split bamboo mats among the village notables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rug - a thick heavy fabric for covering part of a floor, typically smaller than a carpet
  • Synonym: carpet - a thick, woven floor covering that typically extends over a large area

Etymology

From Old English 'matte', from Latin 'matta' (mat, matting).

2. NOUN

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
a thick pad of material placed on the floor for use in gymnastics or other sports to cushion impacts

Examples

The parallel bars, horse, buck, springboard, horizontal bar, rings, and mats formerly in the school gyms were replaced by baseball, volleyball, basketball and football.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: padding - soft material used to cushion impact

3. NOUN

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
a border or piece of material placed around a picture within a frame, serving as a background or to enhance its appearance

Examples

Often, in working out-of-doors under all conditions of light and atmosphere, a particular passage that looked favorable in relation to the subject will be too bright, too dull, or too light, or too dark when viewed indoors in a mat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mount - a support or backing for a picture, often including a mat

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch or Middle Low German *matte* (mat, matting).
