imaging

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the mental ability or process of forming images or representations of things or events in the mind

Examples

For it had turned out, by a further paradox of Cubism, that the means to an illusion of depth and plasticity had now become widely divergent from the means of representation or imaging.

Synset Examples

he could still hear her in his imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty or action of forming new ideas or images not present to the senses
  • Synonym: visualization - the act of forming a mental image of something

Etymology

From 'image' + '-ing', used in psychological and artistic contexts from the mid-20th century.
