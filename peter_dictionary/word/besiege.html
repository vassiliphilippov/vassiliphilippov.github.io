besiege

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsidʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒ / (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force its surrender; to overwhelm someone with requests or difficulties

Examples

Today Sukarno's government is heavily besieged by avowed Communists, and for all of its "neutralist "pretensions, it is a firm ally of Soviet policy.
Whoever was out there hiding in the brushy cover was besieging the Antler house and, having spotted his approach, was determined to drive him off before he could get into the fight.

Synset Examples

The Turks besieged Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • Synonym: blockade - to seal off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving
  • Antonym: defend - to protect from attack

Etymology

From Old French 'assieger', from Vulgar Latin *sediāre, from Latin 'sedere' (to sit).
