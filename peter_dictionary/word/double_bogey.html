double bogey

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
(in golf) to score two strokes over par on a single hole; to take two more shots than the official standard for a particular hole.

Examples

After a journey through woods and stream he double-bogeyed the 13 th.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bogey - (golf) score one stroke over par on a hole
  • Degree Higher: triple bogey - (golf) score three strokes over par on a hole

Etymology

From golf terminology: 'bogey' (originally meaning one over par) plus 'double', indicating two over par.
