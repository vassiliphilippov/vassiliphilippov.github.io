verge

1. NOUN

/ vɝdʒ / (AmE), / vɜːdʒ / (BrE)
the edge or border of something, especially when marking a point at which something begins or changes

Examples

Cubism, in its 1911 - 1912 phase (which the French, with justice, call "hermetic ") was on the verge of abstract art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brink - the extreme edge, especially of land before a steep drop
  • Synonym: threshold - the point or level at which something begins or changes
  • Antonym: center - the middle point, as opposed to the edge or verge

Etymology

From Latin 'vergere' (to bend, turn), through Middle French and Old English forms.
