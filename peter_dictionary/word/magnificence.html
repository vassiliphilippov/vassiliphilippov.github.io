magnificence

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɪfəsəns / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪsəns / (BrE)
the quality of being magnificent; impressiveness of beauty, size, or style

Examples

Returning once again to the Schnabel reissue, I am beguiled anew by the magnificence of this pianist's musical penetration.

Synset Examples

the grandness of the architecture
impressed by the richness of the flora

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandeur - impressive beauty or scale
  • Synonym: splendor - magnificent and splendid appearance
  • Antonym: humbleness - the quality of being modest or unpretentious

Etymology

From Latin magnificentia (splendor, grandeur), from magnificus (great, noble).
