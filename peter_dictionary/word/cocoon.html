cocoon

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkun / (AmE), / kəˈkuːn / (BrE)
A silky covering spun by the larvae of many insects as a protective case during pupation, or by spiders to protect their eggs.

Examples

Each now makes a tough, papery cocoon and pupates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysalis - the pupal stage of butterflies, often contained within a cocoon

Etymology

From French 'cocon', from Occitan 'coucoun' (egg shell or cocoon), ultimately from Latin 'coccum' (berry, referring to the shape).
