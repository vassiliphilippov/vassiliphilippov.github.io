steeply

1. ADV

/ ˈstiːpli / (AmE), / ˈstiːpli / (BrE)
In a way that has a sharp rise or slope; at a steep angle.

Examples

The street was unpaved and rose steeply toward the center; it was mud in wet weather and dust, ankle-deep, in dry, and could be crossed only at the corner where there were stepping stones.

Synset Examples

the street rose steeply up to the castle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - suddenly or abruptly, with a noticeable change (adverb)
