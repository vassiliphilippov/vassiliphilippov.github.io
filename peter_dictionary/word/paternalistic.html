paternalistic

1. ADJ

/ pəˌtɝːnəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / pəˌtɜːnəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Showing an attitude of authority by treating people as if they were children, giving help and advice but often not allowing them to make their own decisions; benevolently but intrusively controlling.

Examples

The slaves never shared in their profits, while they did share, in a very real sense, in the profits of the slave owners: they were fed, clothed, doctored, and so forth; they were the beneficiaries of responsible, paternalistic care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maternalistic - showing care and guidance in a motherly manner

Etymology

From paternal + -istic, influenced by Latin 'paternus' (of a father).
