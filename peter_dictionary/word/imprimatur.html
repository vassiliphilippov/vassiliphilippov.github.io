imprimatur

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.prɪˈmɑː.tər / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prɪˈmɑː.tə / (BrE)
official approval or authorization, especially from an authority or institution

Examples

Almost every film bearing the imprimatur of Hollywood is physically authentic - in fact, impeccably so.

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanction - official permission or approval
  • Synonym: endorsement - publicly stated approval or support

Etymology

From Latin 'imprimatur' (let it be printed), third person singular present subjunctive passive of 'imprimere' (to print).
