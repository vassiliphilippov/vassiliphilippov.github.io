bureau

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjʊroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjʊərəʊ / (BrE)
A chest of drawers used for storing clothes, typically found in a bedroom.

Examples

From the curio cabinet on its south wall and the bureaus beneath, you abstracted seventeen ivory, metal, wood, and stone sculptures of Oriental and African origin, two snuffboxes, and a jade handled magnifying glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dresser - a piece of furniture with drawers, used for storing clothes; especially American usage
  • Synonym: chest of drawers - a furniture piece with drawers for storing clothes

Etymology

From French 'bureau' (office, desk, writing table), originally referring to a cloth covering, later to the writing table itself.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjʊəroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjʊərəʊ / (BrE)
a government department or agency responsible for a specific function or area of administration

Examples

"But when I arrived and recognised a number of notorious characters I had thrown into the detective bureau basement half a dozen times, I knew I had been framed, and withdrew almost at once ".
One of the beer runners telephoned O' Banion - on a line tapped by the detective bureau - and reported the situation.
Upon which the detective bureau despatched rifle squads to prevent trouble if O' Banion should send his gunmen out to deal with the hijacking policemen.

Synset Examples

the Central Intelligence Agency
the Census Bureau
Office of Management and Budget
Tennessee Valley Authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agency - an organization or department responsible for a specific function, often within government

Etymology

From French 'bureau' (office, desk), originally from Old French for 'cloth covering a desk', later extending to mean 'office'.
