oddly

1. ADV

/ ˈɑdli / (AmE), / ˈɒdli / (BrE)
In a way that is unusual or unexpected.

Examples

He paced forward unsteadily, leaning too far back, his head tilted oddly.
It was that oddly shaped space at the very top of the house, where ceiling heights had to accommodate themselves to the varying angles of roof slope.

Synset Examples

had a curiously husky voice
he's behaving rather peculiarly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangely - in a way that is unusual or surprising
  • Synonym: peculiarly - in a way that is odd or unusual

Etymology

Formed from 'odd' + '-ly', with 'odd' from Old Norse 'oddi' (point, triangle, odd number).
