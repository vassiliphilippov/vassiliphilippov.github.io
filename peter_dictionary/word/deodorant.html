deodorant

1. NOUN

/ diˈoʊdəˌrɑnt / (AmE), / diˈəʊdərənt / (BrE)
A substance applied to the body, especially the underarms, to mask or prevent unpleasant body odors.

Examples

Avoid the following combinations: Deodorants with baby powder, hairspray, and lipstick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiperspirant - substance reducing sweating and odor

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (removal) + 'odor' (smell) + '-ant' (agent).
