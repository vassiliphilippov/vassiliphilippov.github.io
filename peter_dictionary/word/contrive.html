contrive

1. VERB

/ kənˈtraɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪv / (BrE)
to invent or devise (an idea, plan, method, etc.) after careful thought; to succeed in creating or bringing about, often by cleverness or resourcefulness

Examples

Their kind created an American culture superior to any in the world, an industrial and technological culture which penetrated Russia as it did almost every corner of the earth without a nickel from the Federal treasury or a single governmental specialist to contrive directives or program a series of consultations of interested agencies.

Synset Examples

excogitate a way to measure the speed of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent a procedure, system, or method by careful thought
  • Synonym: excogitate - to think out, devise, or invent something
  • Synonym: plan - to design or make a scheme for doing something

Etymology

From Old French *contriver* (to devise), from Vulgar Latin *contropare* (to compare, invent).

2. VERB

/ kənˈtraɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪv / (BrE)
to plan or devise (something) with cleverness or ingenuity, often in a skillful or sometimes deceitful way

Examples

Gazing too long at the scenery could be tiring, so halts were contrived between meals.

Synset Examples

They contrived to murder their boss
design a new sales strategy
plan an attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: plan - to form a plan or scheme for
  • Antonym: improvise - to compose or perform without preparation, as an antonym to careful contrivance

Etymology

From Old French controuver (to imagine, invent), from Latin contropare (to compare), with semantic development toward 'devise, invent.'
