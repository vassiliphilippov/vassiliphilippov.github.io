tag

1. VERB

/ tæɡ / (AmE), / tæɡ / (BrE)
to attach a tag or label to something for identification

Examples

Turk Lown was tagged with the loss, his second against no victories, while Ray Moore won his second game against a single loss.
Of several methods employed for tagging chlorine with radiochlorine, the exchange of inactive chlorine with tagged aluminum chloride at room temperature was found to be the most satisfactory.
But there is hope, for Conservation Commissioner Bontempo has tagged the sanctuary as the kind of place the state hopes to include in its program to double its park space.

Synset Examples

label these bottles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - to attach a label to something

Etymology

From Middle English 'tagge' (small hanging piece), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ tæɡ / (AmE), / tæɡ / (BrE)
a small piece of paper, cardboard, plastic, or other material attached to an object to give information about it, such as its owner, nature, or price

Examples

She had dug a hole for each bulb, each tree wore a tag with her writing on it; where would she go for her gardening now?
The drug's chemical name is listed, since most states require feed processors to use this name instead of the trade name on the feed tag.
This indicates that this drug is being marketed under one trade name only or state regulatory organizations have approved its use on the feed tag.
(These enzyme preparations appear on today's feed tags as fermentation extracts of Bacillus subtilis, Apergillus orzae, Niger, and Flavus.)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - a piece of paper or other material attached to something, giving information about it
  • Synonym: ticket - a small piece of paper or card giving information about something, often a price or permit

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English tagge (tip, small object attached), of uncertain origin.

3. VERB

/ tæɡ / (AmE), / tæɡ / (BrE)
(in sports and playground games) to touch a player who has the ball, often in order to put them out or change their role in the game.

Examples

But Mike had no chance of being tagged.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out - removed from play (as in baseball, when 'tagged out')

Etymology

Probably from an alteration of Middle English taggen (to fasten or mark), related to the noun 'tag.'

4. VERB

/ tæɡ / (AmE), / tæɡ / (BrE)
to assign a name, label, or nickname to someone or something

Examples

"The forgotten 15 million "- as Sponsor tagged the Negro market in its first survey - has become a better-remembered 19 million.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - to attach or assign a descriptive name or title
  • Synonym: nickname - to give an informal or familiar name
