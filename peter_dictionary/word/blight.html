blight

1. NOUN

/ blaɪt / (AmE), / blaɪt / (BrE)
A deteriorating or run-down condition, often referring to decay in urban areas or environments.

Examples

The breakdown of social homogeneity in inner city areas and the spread of inner city blight account for the decline of central city churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of gradual deterioration or decline
  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming progressively worse

2. VERB

/ blaɪt / (AmE), / blaɪt / (BrE)
to spoil, damage, or cause to wither, especially in reference to plants; to have a severely detrimental effect on something

Examples

Three of these only were protected from us by stern commandment: the roses, whose petals might not be collected until they had fallen, to be made into perfume or rose tea to drink; the peonies, whose tight sticky buds would be blighted by the laying on of a finger, although they were not apparently harmed by the ants that crawled over them; and the poppies.

Synset Examples

Too much rain may blight the garden with mold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wither - to dry up and shrivel, especially a plant
  • Synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something
  • Antonym: nourish - to provide with food or other substances necessary for growth

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old English *bliċan* (to shine, make pale), with later development in usage.
