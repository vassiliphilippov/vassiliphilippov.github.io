brave

1. ADJ

/ breɪv / (AmE), / breɪv / (BrE)
Willing to face danger or pain without fear; showing courage.

Examples

Whenever there is a thaw or a few sunny days, you 'll be likely to find a brave little blossom or two.

Synset Examples

audacious explorers
fearless reporters and photographers
intrepid pioneers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - not deterred by danger or pain; brave
  • Synonym: fearless - without fear; brave
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened

Etymology

From Old French 'brave' (splendid, valiant) or Italian 'bravo' (bold, wild).

2. ADJ

/ breɪv / (AmE), / breɪv / (BrE)
Having or showing courage; able to face danger, pain, or difficult situations without fear.

Examples

They tried to outface him, but Joseph Simmons was as wide as two average men, and it would have taken braver men than these were to outface him.
He believed that brave boys did n't cry.
"They require, for existence, a brave spirit and a high heart, and where do you find these?
In our age of Science and Angst it seems to me more brave to stay on Earth and explore inner man than to fly far from the sphere of our sorrow and explore outer space ".
This was my second day in Vientiane, the administrative capital of Laos, and my thoughts were none too brave.

Synset Examples

Familiarity with danger makes a brave man braver but less daring
a frank courageous heart...triumphed over pain
set a courageous example by leading them safely into and out of enemy-held territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Synonym: bold - willing to take risks; confident and courageous
  • Antonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened

Etymology

From Middle French *brave* (splendid, valiant), from Italian *bravo* (courageous, bold).

3. VERB

/ breɪv / (AmE), / breɪv / (BrE)
to face or endure (danger, difficulty, or pain) with courage

Examples

I still recall the mild shock I experienced in reading material of an enthusiastic advocate of the "clean, healthful, free way of natural life in nudism ", who seemed to brave much misunderstanding and persecution in fine spirit.

Synset Examples

She braved the elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defy - openly resist or refuse to obey

Etymology

From Middle French *brave*, from Italian *bravo* (courageous, wild).
