provincialism

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪn(t)ʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˈvɪn(t)ʃ(ə)lɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Narrowness of outlook or interests that results from being concerned with or loyal to a particular region or locality rather than the whole; partiality for one’s own region.

Examples

He saw Sweden as a country of smug and narrow provincialism, indifferent to the heroic spirit of its former glory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parochialism - narrowness of outlook, especially focused on a local area

Etymology

From 'provincial' + '-ism', with 'provincial' from Latin 'provincia' (territory under Roman rule).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪn(t)ʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˈvɪn(t)ʃ(ə)lɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
behavior, attitudes, or ways of thinking regarded as characteristic of people from rural or less cosmopolitan areas, especially as resulting in a lack of sophistication or broad-mindedness.

Examples

One might argue that the ultimate purpose of literary scholarship is to correct this spontaneous provincialism that is likely to obscure the horizons of the general public, of the newspaper critic, and of the creative artist himself.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sophistication - having a refined knowledge of the world and culture

Etymology

From 'provincial' (relating to a province) + '-ism' (denoting a practice or characteristic).
