Metrazol

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrəˌzɒl / (AmE), / ˈmitrəˌzɒl / (BrE)
A trademarked drug formerly used as a circulatory and respiratory stimulant, and in shock therapy, known to induce convulsions at higher doses.

Examples

In other studies, loss of differentiation in previously established conditioned reflexes resulted from repeated convulsive (metrazol) treatments, suggesting a fundamental disturbance in the balance between excitatory and inhibitory cerebral processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentylenetetrazol - A chemical compound used as a circulatory stimulant and formerly in convulsive therapy

Etymology

Trademark name for pentylenetetrazol, first used in the 1930s.
