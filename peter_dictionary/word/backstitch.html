backstitch

1. VERB

/ ˈbækˌstɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.stɪtʃ / (BrE)
to sew using a backstitch, a method of stitching in which each stitch is sewn backward to the one before, used for strength or decorative effect

Examples

Run end of yarn through several stitches along edge to secure; backstitch pieces together close to edge.
Backstitch seam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcast - to sew with an overcast stitch to prevent fabric from fraying
  • Synonym: whipstitch - to sew with a whipstitch, a simple stitch over the edge of fabric

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stitch', describing the motion of the stitch going backward.
