cerebellum

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈbɛləm / (AmE), / sɛrəˈbɛləm / (BrE)
the part of the brain at the back of the skull in vertebrates, which is responsible for coordinating voluntary movements and maintaining balance and posture

Examples

This resulted in damage to the occipital lobe and very probably to the left side of the cerebellum also.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebrum - the principal and most anterior part of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebellum', diminutive of 'cerebrum' (brain).
