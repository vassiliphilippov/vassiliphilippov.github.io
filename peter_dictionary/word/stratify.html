stratify

1. VERB

/ ˈstrætəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstrætɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to form, arrange, or divide into layers or strata; to organize into hierarchical levels or classes

Examples

Aristotle also tended to stratify all aspects of human nature and activity into levels of excellence and, like Plato, he put the pure and unimpassioned intellect on the top level.

Synset Examples

The rock stratifies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - to arrange in layers
  • Antonym: homogenize - to make uniform or of one kind

Etymology

From Medieval Latin stratificare, from Latin stratum (layer) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈstrætɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstrætɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to divide a society or population into different social classes, castes, or groups

Examples

The populations of the various districts, or other major divisions, were stratified by type of community (rural, urban, mixed) and, where appropriate, by ethnic affiliation and by type of economy.

Synset Examples

Income distribution often stratifies a society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segregate - to separate or set apart from others, especially by race or class
  • Antonym: integrate - to combine or bring together into a whole; to end separation of groups

Etymology

From Medieval Latin stratificare, from Latin stratum (layer).
