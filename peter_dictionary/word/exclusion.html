exclusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskluʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒən / (BrE)
The state or act of preventing someone or something from entering or being included.

Examples

Mission to the metropolis contradicts survival of the congregation in the residential community, because the middle classes are fighting metropolitan interdependence with residential exclusion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included

Etymology

From Latin 'exclusio', from 'excludere' (to shut out, exclude).
