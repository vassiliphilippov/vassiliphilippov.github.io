frisk

1. VERB

/ frɪsk / (AmE), / frɪsk / (BrE)
to search someone for concealed items, especially weapons or drugs, by quickly running the hands over their clothing and through their pockets

Examples

I've frisked a thousand punks.

Synset Examples

The police frisked everyone at the airport

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pat down - to search someone by patting their clothing

Etymology

From Middle English *frisken*, from Old French *frisquer* (to move briskly), later extended to searching movements.
