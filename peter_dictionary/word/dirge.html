dirge

1. NOUN

/ dɝdʒ / (AmE), / dɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a mournful song, piece of music, or poem, especially one expressing grief for the dead at a funeral or memorial

Examples

The dark forms moved like mourners on some nocturnal pilgrimage, their dirge unsung for want of vocal chords.
Everyone with a personal or group tragedy to relate had to be given his day in court as in some vast collective dirge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - a passionate expression of grief, often in song or poem form

Etymology

From Latin 'dirige', imperative of 'dirigere' (direct), from the opening of a funeral anthem.
