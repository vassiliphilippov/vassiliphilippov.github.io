inescapably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɛskəbl̩i / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛskəbl̩i / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be avoided or escaped; inevitably or unavoidably.

Examples

The new Council was itself inescapably of political meaning, which was most clearly revealed in the absence of any U.N.F.P. members and the presence of several Istiqlal leaders.

Synset Examples

the situation slid inescapably toward disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - In a way that cannot be avoided; as is certain to happen
  • Synonym: unavoidably - In an unavoidable manner; as cannot be prevented

Etymology

From inescapable + -ly; inescapable from in- (not) + escapable (able to escape).
