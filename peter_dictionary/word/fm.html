FM

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛf ˈɛm / (BrE)
a method of transmitting radio or other signals by varying the frequency of a carrier wave; short for frequency modulation

Examples

These frequencies are amplified and detected by the FM receiver after each burst of transmitted energy and, after the "pill "has been calibrated, precise internal pressure indications can be obtained.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: AM - a method of transmitting signals by varying the amplitude of a carrier wave (amplitude modulation)

Etymology

Abbreviation of frequency modulation.
