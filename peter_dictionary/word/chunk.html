chunk

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌŋk / (AmE), / tʃʌŋk / (BrE)
a thick, solid piece or lump of something, usually larger than a chip or fragment

Examples

Booby traps to fill the head with chunks of metal.
Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.
Through the splash of the rising waters, they could hear the roar of the river as it raged through its canyon, gnashing big chunks out of the banks.

Synset Examples

a ball of mud caught him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass of a substance
  • Degree Higher: slab - a thick, flat piece of material
  • Degree Lower: chip - a small piece broken off from something

Etymology

Probably from an alteration of 'chunk', meaning a short, thick piece, of obscure origin, possibly related to 'chuck'.
