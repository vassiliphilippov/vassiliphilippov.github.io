stabilized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbɪlaɪzd / (BrE)
Having been made stable or firm; rendered unlikely to change, fall, or fail.

Examples

A wall stabilized high current arc source was constructed and used to study transition probabilities of atomic hydrogen and oxygen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unstable - prone to change, fall, or fail; not stable

Etymology

Formed from 'stabilize' + -ed; 'stabilize' from French 'stabiliser', from Latin 'stabilis' (firm, stable).
