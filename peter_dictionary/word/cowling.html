cowling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
A removable metal covering that encloses and protects an engine, especially on an aircraft.

Examples

A large piece of engine cowling vanished.

Synset Examples

there are powerful engines under the hoods of new cars
the mechanic removed the cowling in order to repair the plane's engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hood - protective covering for an engine in automobiles

Etymology

From 'cowl' (a covering), ultimately from Latin 'cucullus' (hood, cloak).
