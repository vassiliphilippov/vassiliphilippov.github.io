diagnose

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪəɡˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəɡnəʊz / (BrE)
To identify the nature of a disease, problem, or condition by examination and analysis.

Examples

(He explained that he could diagnose these ailments from squeezing her foot because all of the nervous system was connected to it.)
Carroll diagnosed Mrs. Hull by taking a drop of blood from her ear and putting it on his "radionic "machine and twirling some knobs (fee $ 50).
He can diagnose detachment of the retina where conventional methods indicate blindness due to glaucoma.
But before I try to diagnose it, I would offer other evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnosis - the identification of the nature of an illness or problem

Etymology

From Greek diagnōsis ‘discernment, distinguishing’ (from dia- ‘apart’ + gignōskein ‘to know’).

2. VERB

/ ˈdaɪəɡˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəɡnəʊz / (BrE)
to identify an illness or other problem by examining the symptoms

Examples

We may say that his problem was diagnosed but that he refused treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: treat - to give medical care to a patient for an illness or condition

Etymology

From Greek diagnōsis, from dia- (through) + gnōsis (knowledge).
