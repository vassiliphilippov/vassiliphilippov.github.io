2. VERB

/ rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
to cause a bell to sound by striking, pulling a rope, or operating a mechanism, often for a specific purpose such as musical effect or as a signal

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old English hringan (to ring, sound, clang).

5. NOUN

/ rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
a rigid circular band of metal, wood, or other material used for holding, fastening, hanging, or pulling objects

Examples

Opposite every gate was a hitching post or a stone carriage step, set with a rusty iron ring for tying a horse.
The night we first met, at one of Mrs. Monmouth's giant parties, he was wearing a brown cashmere jacket with silver buttons and a soft pink Viyella shirt; instead of a necktie he wore a leather bolo drawn through a golden ring in which was set a lump of pale pure jade.

6. VERB

/ rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
to make a telephone call to someone

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to telephone someone

Etymology

7. NOUN

/ rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
a quality of sound or tone characteristic of something, often used to describe an impression or aura (especially in phrases such as 'the ring of truth' or 'the ring of sincerity')

Examples

For a blood chilling ring of terror to the very sound of his name was the tool he needed for the job he 'd promised to do.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone - the quality or character of sound

Etymology

Old English hring (noun); the figurative sense (other than the shape) comes from the verb 'to ring,' from Old English hringan (to make a resonant or vibrating sound).
