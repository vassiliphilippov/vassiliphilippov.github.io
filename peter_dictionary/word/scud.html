scud

1. VERB

/ skʌd / (AmE), / skʌd / (BrE)
to move or run swiftly and smoothly, often driven by wind or as if sliding along

Examples

It was a delayed moon, but now the sky had cleared of scudding black and the stars sugared the silver-gray sky.

Synset Examples

She dashed into the yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dart - move suddenly or quickly in a particular direction
  • Synonym: dash - run or move somewhere in a great hurry

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'skudda' (to hurry).
