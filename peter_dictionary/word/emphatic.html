emphatic

1. ADJ

/ ɛmˈfætɪk / (AmE), / ɛmˈfætɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or words) uttered with emphasis; showing or giving emphasis.

Examples

In 1920, the Lambeth Conference repeated its 1908 condemnation of contraception and issued "an emphatic warning against the use of unnatural means for the avoidance of conception, together with the grave dangers - physical, moral, and religious - thereby incurred, and against the evils which the extension of such use threaten the race ".

Synset Examples

an emphatic word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forceful - expressed with force or conviction
  • Antonym: unemphatic - not expressed with emphasis

Etymology

From Greek emphatikos (emphatic), from emphainein (to exhibit, indicate).
