autopsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔː.tɑp.si / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tɒp.si / (BrE)
a medical examination of a dead body to determine the cause of death or study the effects of disease

Examples

The ME's boys had finished their on-the-spot examination and the body had been removed for autopsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postmortem - an examination of a dead body to determine the cause of death, especially a synonym for autopsy in British English

Etymology

From Greek autopsia, meaning ‘seeing with one’s own eyes’ (autos ‘self’ + opsis ‘sight’).
