vandalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvændəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvændəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The deliberate and malicious destruction or damage of public or private property.

Examples

Hardly a window has been broken since Dunbar first was opened (and vandalism in schools is a major problem in many slum areas).
But if I drop this fucking thing, you got me on destruction of property, too."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabotage - deliberate destruction or damage, especially for political or military advantage
  • Synonym: graffiti - writing or drawings scribbled on a wall or other surface, often as illicit damage to property

Etymology

From French 'vandalisme', from 'Vandale' (Vandal), referring to the Germanic tribe notorious for sacking Rome.
