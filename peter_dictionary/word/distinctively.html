distinctively

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstɪŋktɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is characteristic of one person, thing, or group and makes them easily recognizable; in an identifiably distinctive manner.

Examples

To say this, of course, is to take up a position on one side of a controversy going on now for some two hundred years, or, at any rate, since the beginning of the distinctively modern period in theological thought.
What makes this long and diverse tradition essentially one is that those who have belonged to it have been profoundly in earnest about being modern men in a distinctively modern world.

Synset Examples

the distinctively conservative district of the county

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristically - in a way that is typical or characteristic of someone or something
  • Antonym: indistinctly - in a way that is not clear or sharply defined

Etymology

From 'distinctive' + '-ly', with 'distinctive' from Latin 'distinctivus'.
