loathe

1. VERB

/ loʊð / (AmE), / ləʊð / (BrE)
to feel intense dislike or disgust for someone or something

Examples

How he loathed him.
He loathed the idea of drying up swamps or the chills of malaria or of leaving his natural birthright.
The children loathed him, too.

Synset Examples

I loathe that man
She abhors cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detest - to strongly dislike
  • Synonym: abhor - to regard with disgust and hatred
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection for someone or something

Etymology

From Old English *lāðian* (to hate), related to *lāð* (loath, hateful).
