humanist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
A scholar or student specializing in the study of the humanities, especially during the Renaissance, with a focus on classical literature and the liberal arts.

Examples

Similar literary exercises were the common doings of a Christian humanist of the first two decades of the sixteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicist - a person who studies the classics, especially ancient Greek and Latin literature

Etymology

From Italian 'umanista' (teacher of humanities), from Latin 'humanitas' (human nature, refinement, humanity).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhjuːmənɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Renaissance humanism, especially its revival of classical learning and values.

Examples

It is not merely a new thing; it is one of the very few new things in Utopia; most of the rest is medieval or humanist or part of an old tradition of social criticism.

Synset Examples

the humanistic revival of learning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanistic - relating to humanism or its ideals (general sense, not Renaissance-specific)

Etymology

From Latin 'humanista', referring to a teacher or student of the humanities.
