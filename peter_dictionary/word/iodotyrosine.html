iodotyrosine

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.ə.doʊˈtaɪ.rəˌsin / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əʊ.dəʊˈtɪə.rəˌsiːn / (BrE)
A compound formed when tyrosine, an amino acid, is combined with iodine, important in the synthesis of thyroid hormones.

Examples

It is assumed that the iodine released from the iodotyrosines remains in the iodide pool of the thyroid, where it is oxidised and re-incorporated into thyroglobulin.

Etymology

From iodine and tyrosine, reflecting its chemical composition.
