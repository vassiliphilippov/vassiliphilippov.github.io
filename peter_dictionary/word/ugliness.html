ugliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌɡlinəs / (AmE), / ˈʌɡlɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being unpleasant to look at; lacking beauty or attractiveness.

Examples

He filled a real need, showing society what it looked like, turning it inside out, portraying its wars and its leaders, its ugliness and its beauties, reflecting its profound religious impulses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beauty - the quality of being pleasing to the senses or mind
  • Synonym: ugly - unpleasant or repulsive in appearance

Etymology

From ugly + -ness, with 'ugly' from Old Norse 'uggligr' (dreadful).
