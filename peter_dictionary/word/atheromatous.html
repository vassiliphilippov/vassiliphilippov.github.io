atheromatous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæθəˈroʊmətəs / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈrəʊmətəs / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling atheroma; affected by or characteristic of a deposit of fatty material within the walls of arteries.

Examples

The coronary arteries were sclerotic and diffusely narrowed throughout their courses, and the right coronary artery was virtually occluded by a yellow atheromatous plaque 1.5 cm. distal to its origin.

Synset Examples

atheromatous degeneration of the arteries

Etymology

From Greek atherōma (fatty deposit) + -ous (adjective suffix).
