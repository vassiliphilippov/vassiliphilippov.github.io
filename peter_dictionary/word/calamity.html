calamity

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæməti / (AmE), / kəˈlæmɪti / (BrE)
A sudden event causing great damage, distress, or loss; a disaster.

Examples

It had just suffered a calamity, the final crisis in a long series of calamities.
It had just suffered a calamity, the final crisis in a long series of calamities.

Synset Examples

the whole city was affected by the irremediable calamity
the earthquake was a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event, such as an accident or natural catastrophe, causing great damage or loss of life
  • Synonym: catastrophe - an event causing sudden and very great harm or damage
  • Antonym: blessing - something beneficial that brings happiness or good fortune

Etymology

From Latin calamitas (damage, disaster).
