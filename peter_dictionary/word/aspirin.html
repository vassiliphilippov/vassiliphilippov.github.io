aspirin

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspərɪn / (AmE), / ˈaspərɪn / (BrE)
A drug in the form of an acetylated derivative of salicylic acid, used to relieve pain, reduce fever, and reduce inflammation.

Examples

He sat for a while with his hands on his knees, watching the bend of her back as she gathered up her things - a comb, a bottle of aspirin - to take upstairs and pack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug used to relieve pain
  • Degree Higher: ibuprofen - a common nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)

Etymology

From the German trademark Aspirin, coined by Bayer from acetylierte Spirsaure (acetylated spiraeic acid), itself from Spiraea (a plant genus, source of salicylic acid) and -in (chemical suffix).
