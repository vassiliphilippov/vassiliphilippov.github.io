semitrance

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈtræns / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈtrɑːns / (BrE)
A trancelike state in which a person can follow instructions but has weak or absent voluntary action.

Examples

It had been five days too late before he learned that she 'd gone through the wedding ceremony in a semitrance of laudanum, administered by her mother.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trance - a state of altered consciousness with lack of voluntary movement

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half' or 'partial') + trance.
