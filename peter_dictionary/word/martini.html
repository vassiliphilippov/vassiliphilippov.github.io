martini

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈtini / (AmE), / mɑːˈtiːni / (BrE)
A cocktail made with gin or vodka and dry vermouth, typically garnished with an olive or a lemon twist.

Examples

Their father, when he came back from those many business trips, just bumped their mother on the forehead with his lips and asked if anybody had thought to mix the martinis and put them in the electric icebox.
Two martinis for lunch - that was the trouble.

Etymology

Named after the American brand of vermouth 'Martini & Rossi', or possibly derived from the earlier cocktail 'Martinez'.
