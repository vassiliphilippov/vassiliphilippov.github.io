paw

1. VERB

/ pɔ / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to touch or handle something or someone clumsily or roughly, as with the paws of an animal; often in a way that is unwanted or lacking finesse

Examples

Oh, he was being queer and careful, pawing about in the drawer and holding the bottle like a snake at the length of his arm.

Synset Examples

The man tried to paw her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - to feel about or search blindly or uncertainly with the hands
  • Antonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently, often with affection

Etymology

From Middle English *pawe*, probably from Old French *poe*, of Germanic origin; related to Low German *Pfote* (paw).

2. NOUN

/ pɑ / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
The foot of an animal, especially a quadruped, that has claws or pads.

Examples

His energy was prodigious; sometimes he would be up before dawn, clad as a garbage collector and hurling pails into areaways to exasperate us, and thereafter would hurry to the Bronx Zoo to grimace at the lions and press cigar butts against their paws.
Then they had always been romping around him on these walks, yelping with delight, dashing off into the bushes on fruitless hunting expeditions, returning to jump up on him triumphantly with muddy paws.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hoof - the hard foot of a horse or similar animal

Etymology

From Old French *powe* (paw), of uncertain origin.

3. VERB

/ pɔ / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
(of an animal) to scrape, dig, or strike at something with a paw or similar limb.

Examples

The stallion had smelled the mare coming into heat and began to paw the turf, shaking his head.

Synset Examples

The bear pawed the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to scrape or dig at with sharp or hard object or body part, such as a claw or nail
