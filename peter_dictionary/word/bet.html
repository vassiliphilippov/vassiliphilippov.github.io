bet

1. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
To risk money or something of value against the outcome of an event; also, to confidently predict or assert that something will happen.

Examples

"We 're getting more' pro' letters than' con' on horse race betting ", said Ratcliff.
I 'll bet both together did.
I bet.
"This is moving day ", Winston reminded her, "and I bet you left things every which way upstairs, your clothes all over the floor and the bed not made.
Jubal would have bet his life that if Anne were to witness the Second Coming, she would memorize date, time, personae, events, and barometric pressure without batting her calm blue eyes.

Synset Examples

I bet she will be there!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - to risk something, usually money, on the outcome of an event

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from obsolete English 'abet', influenced by the Old English 'beot' (a boast or vow).

2. NOUN

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
an amount of money that is risked on the result of a game, race, or other uncertain event

Examples

To the extent, then, that declining U. S. prestige means that other nations will be tempted to place their bets on an ultimate American defeat, and will thus be more vulnerable to Soviet intimidation, there is reason for concern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stake - the amount of money placed at risk in a bet or wager
  • Synonym: wager - an amount of money or valuable object risked on the outcome of an event

Etymology

From Middle English 'bet', possibly a contraction of 'abet'; related to early 16th-century gambling terminology.

3. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
to risk money or something else on the outcome of an event or issue; to wager

Examples

GEARY: You'’ve got a bet, mister.

Synset Examples

I bet $100 on that new horse
She played all her money on the dark horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - to risk money or something valuable on the result of an event
  • Synonym: gamble - to play games of chance for money; to bet

Etymology

Possibly from early use of 'abet' (encourage, incite); origin uncertain.

4. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
To expect with confidence; to be certain of something.

Examples

I bet Russian czars never paid that for a single horse.

Synset Examples

you can count on me to help you any time
Look to your friends for support
You can bet on that!
Depend on your family in times of crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: count on - to rely on; to trust to do something
  • Synonym: depend on - to rely on with trust or confidence
  • Antonym: doubt - to disbelieve or lack confidence in

Etymology

Probably from shortening of 'abet', itself from Old French abeter (to bait, urge on).
