thoughtful

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɔt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.fəl / (BrE)
showing careful thought, consideration, or good sense in words or actions

Examples

One is the primary purpose for which marriage exists, namely, the continuance of the race through the gift and heritage of children; the other is the paramount importance in married life of deliberate and thoughtful self-control ".

Synset Examples

a sensible young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensible - showing good sense or judgment

Etymology

From 'thought' (Old English 'þoht', meaning 'thinking') + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɔt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.f(ə)l / (BrE)
Showing careful consideration or intellectual depth; characterized by deep thinking.

Examples

The term idea refers to our more reflective or thoughtful consciousness as opposed to the immediacies of sensuous or emotional experience.
Yesterday, I had the privilege of reading a thoughtful article in the U. S. News + World Report of May 8 which discussed this type of action in more detail, including both its advantages and its disadvantages.

Synset Examples

a deeply thoughtful essay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflective - marked by deep or serious thought
  • Synonym: meditative - involving deep thought or contemplation
  • Antonym: shallow - lacking depth of intellect or knowledge
