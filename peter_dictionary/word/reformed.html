Reformed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɔrm(d) / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting the branch of Protestant Christianity formed during the Reformation, especially Calvinist as distinct from Lutheran.

Examples

Peter Caroli had come to Geneva, saying that he had been a bishop of the Church of Rome and had been persecuted in Paris for his Reformed faith.

Synset Examples

Dutch Reformed theology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinist - relating to the branch of Protestantism following the teachings of John Calvin
  • Antonym: Lutheran - relating to the branch of Protestantism following the teachings of Martin Luther; contrast to Reformed

Etymology

From the past participle of 'reform,' referring to changes made during the Protestant Reformation in the 16th century.
