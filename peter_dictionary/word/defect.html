defect

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdiː.fekt / (BrE)
A fault or imperfection in an object, machine, or material that impairs its normal appearance, function, or quality.

Examples

As with the penultimate Giselle release (Wolff's abridgment for RCA Victor) I find the cleaner, less razor edged monophonic version, for all its lack of big stage spaciousness, the more aurally tolerable - but this may be the result of processing defects in my SD copies.

Synset Examples

a flaw caused the crystal to shatter
if there are any defects you should send it back to the manufacturer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaw - a mark, fault, or weakness that makes something less perfect
  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or imperfection that spoils the appearance of something
  • Antonym: perfect - having no defects or faults
  • Synonym: fault - an unattractive or unsatisfactory feature, especially in a physical object

Etymology

From Latin defectus (failure, lack), from deficere (to fail, to be lacking).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdiː.fekt / (BrE)
A fault or imperfection in a part or function of the body.

Examples

No fasciculations or sensory defects were found.
From the resulting data the doctor can determine lung defects with hitherto unknown accuracy and detail.

Synset Examples

visual defects
this device permits detection of defects in the lungs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abnormality - a deviation from what is normal in structure or function
  • Synonym: malformation - an abnormal formation or structure
  • Synonym: disease - a disorder of structure or function in a human or animal

Etymology

From Latin 'defectus' (failure, lack), past participle of 'deficere' (to fail).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈfekt / (BrE)
A fault, flaw, or deficiency that makes something less perfect or effective.

Examples

To interpret, indeed, the era from 1000 to 800 as a period mainly of consolidation may be a necessary but unfortunate defect born of our lack of detailed information; if we could see more deeply, we probably would find many side issues and wrong turnings which came to an end within the period.
Anyone who tried to remedy some of the most glaring defects in our form of democracy was denounced as a traitorous red whose real purpose was the destruction of our government.

Synset Examples

that interpretation is an unfortunate defect of our lack of information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaw - a mark, fault, or other imperfection making something less than perfect
  • Synonym: imperfection - a fault, blemish, or undesirable feature
  • Antonym: virtue - a good quality or advantage

Etymology

From Latin defectus (failure, want), from deficere (to fail).
