self-effacement

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
The quality of not drawing attention to oneself; making oneself inconspicuous or modestly withdrawing from the spotlight.

Examples

- he had to play a role of self-effacement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-deprecation - the act of undervaluing oneself or being excessively modest
  • Antonym: self-assertion - the act of expressing or defending one's rights or claims

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and 'effacement', from French 'effacer' (to erase).
