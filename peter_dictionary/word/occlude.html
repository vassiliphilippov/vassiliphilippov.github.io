occlude

1. VERB

/ əˈklud / (AmE), / əˈkluːd / (BrE)
to block or close up an opening, passage, or vessel

Examples

The coronary arteries were sclerotic and diffusely narrowed throughout their courses, and the right coronary artery was virtually occluded by a yellow atheromatous plaque 1.5 cm. distal to its origin.

Synset Examples

obstruct the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruct - block or hinder passage through something
  • Antonym: unblock - remove a blockage from something

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere', from 'ob-' (against) + 'claudere' (to close).
