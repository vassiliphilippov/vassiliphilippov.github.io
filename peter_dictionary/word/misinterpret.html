misinterpret

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsɪnˈtɝːprət / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪnˈtɜːprət / (BrE)
to understand or explain something incorrectly; to interpret in a way that is not intended or accurate

Examples

Kayabashi must feel fairly certain of his victory in order to make a visit like this, a trip which could be so easily misinterpreted by the people in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misconstrue - to interpret wrongly, often with a nuance of misunderstanding intention
  • Antonym: interpret - to explain or understand the meaning of something

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'interpret' (from Latin 'interpretari', to explain).
