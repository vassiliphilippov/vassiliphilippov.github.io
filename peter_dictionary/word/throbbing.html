throbbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒbɪŋ / (BrE)
a rhythmic, strong pulsation or sensation, especially of pain or the heart

Examples

There was the slight pain, but it was no different from the throbbing in his head.

Synset Examples

he felt a throbbing in his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulsation - a rhythmic throbbing or vibrating movement
  • Degree Lower: ache - continuous dull pain

Etymology

From the verb 'throb', of imitative origin, possibly related to Middle English 'throbben'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθrɑbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒbɪŋ / (BrE)
Pounding or beating with a strong, regular, or violent rhythm.

Examples

a dull boom and a throbbing echo.

Synset Examples

a throbbing pain
the throbbing engine of the boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulsating - expanding and contracting with a strong, regular rhythm
  • Synonym: thumping - beating or pounding strongly and loudly
