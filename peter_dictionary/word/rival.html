rival

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.vəl / (BrE)
a person, group, or thing competing with another for the same objective or for superiority in the same field

Examples

Fear of the competition - always a great motivating force in the American economy - makes retailers who do not have suburban operations exaggerate both the volume and the profitability of their rival's shiny new branches.

Synset Examples

he had respect for his rivals
he wanted to know what the competition was doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - a person or organization that competes against others
  • Synonym: opponent - a person who is against another in a contest, conflict, or argument
  • Antonym: ally - a person or organization that cooperates with or helps another
  • Antonym: friend - a person who has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Latin rivalis (a rival, originally, one who uses the same stream as another), from rivus (stream).

2. VERB

/ ˈraɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪvəl / (BrE)
To be as good as or nearly as good as someone or something else in quality or ability; to match or compete with successfully.

Examples

Soon her purring rivaled the chirping of the tree crickets, rivaled the hum of voices from inside the Pod.
Soon her purring rivaled the chirping of the tree crickets, rivaled the hum of voices from inside the Pod.
The New English Bible (the Old Testament and Apocrypha will be published at a future date) has not been planned to rival or replace the King James Version, but, as its cover states, it is offered "simply as the Bible to all those who will use it in reading, teaching, or worship ".
Near the end of the Hippodrome I came upon the Built Column, a truncated obelisk of blocks, all that remains of a monument that once rivalled the Colossus of Rhodes.

Synset Examples

Nothing can rival cotton for durability
Your performance doesn't even touch that of your colleagues
Her persistence and ambition only matches that of her parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - to be equal or similar to someone or something in quality, amount, or ability
  • Antonym: surpass - to be better than; to exceed or go beyond

Etymology

From Latin 'rivalis' (a person using the same stream as another), from 'rivus' (stream).
