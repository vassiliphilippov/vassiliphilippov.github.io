provocation

1. NOUN

/ prɑːvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / prɒvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
An action or behavior intended to make someone angry or resentful.

Examples

But these accounts do not show that Northerners have been subjected to embarrassment or provocation by Yankee hatred displayed in social gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggravation - the act of making a situation worse or causing annoyance
  • Synonym: incitement - the act of encouraging someone to behave angrily or violently
  • Antonym: calming - the act of reducing anger or agitation

Etymology

From Latin provocatio (a challenge), from provocare (to call forth, challenge).
