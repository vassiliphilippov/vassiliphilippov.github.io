conflict

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.flɪkt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.flɪkt / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more events, appointments, or activities are scheduled at the same time and cannot be attended or done together

Examples

Nine of the league's teams play in baseball parks and therefore face an early season conflict in dates.
But if you keep a calendar of events, as we do, you noticed a conflict.

Synset Examples

he noticed a conflict in the dates of the two meetings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clash - a situation in which two events happen at the same time and cannot both be attended
  • Synonym: overlap - an occasion when two events partially coincide in time

Etymology

From Latin 'conflictus', past participle of 'confligere' (to strike together).
