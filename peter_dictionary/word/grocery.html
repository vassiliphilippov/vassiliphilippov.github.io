grocery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊsəri / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsəri / (BrE)
A shop or store where food and other everyday items are sold.

Examples

The grocery the youth worked for soon tired of his depressing effect on customers, most of whom were sufficiently neurotic without the threat of incubi, and let him go.

Synset Examples

the grocery store included a meat market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supermarket - A large self-service store selling foods and household goods

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *grosserie* (wholesale goods), from *gross* (large quantity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊsəri / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsəri / (BrE)
(usually plural) food and other items sold by a grocer, especially those bought for the household.

Examples

Even my father, in the grocery store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groceries - food and other items sold by a grocer; plural form emphasizing the goods rather than the shop

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *grosserie*, from Old French *grossier* (‘wholesale dealer’).
