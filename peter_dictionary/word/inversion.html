inversion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
A meteorological condition in which a layer of cooler air is trapped near the Earth's surface by a layer of warmer air above it, impeding vertical air movement.

Examples

Neutral or inversion meteorological conditions are necessary for a cloud to travel along the surface.

Etymology

From Latin inversio (a turning upside down), from invertere (to turn over).
