perceptible

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptəbl / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptəbl / (BrE)
Able to be seen, heard, or noticed; capable of being perceived by the senses or the mind.

Examples

Alex entered first and was followed by the doctor who, for all his care, manifested a perceptible bulge on his left side where the hen was cradled.

Synset Examples

a perceptible limp
easily perceptible sounds
perceptible changes in behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperceptible - not able to be perceived; very subtle or gradual
  • Synonym: detectable - able to be detected or discovered

Etymology

From Late Latin perceptibilis, from Latin percipere (to perceive).
