salvation

1. NOUN

/ sælˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / sælˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
(in theology) the act of being saved from sin or its consequences, especially through faith in Christ; deliverance from evil or destruction.

Examples

And even as she, having indeed a husband, Adam, but being nevertheless as yet a virgin, having become disobedient, was made the cause of death, both to herself and to the entire human race; so also did Mary, having a man betrothed [ to her ], and being nevertheless a virgin, by yielding obedience, become the cause of salvation, both to herself and the whole human race ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: damnation - the state of eternal punishment in hell, especially as a consequence of sin

Etymology

From Latin 'salvatio' (a saving), from 'salvare' (to save).

2. NOUN

/ sælˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / sælˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A means of being saved from harm, loss, or an unpleasant situation.

Examples

Its refrain was: "Let us return to the individualistic democracy of our forefathers for our salvation ".

Synset Examples

tourism was their economic salvation
they turned to individualism as their salvation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - an act of saving someone or something from danger or difficulty
  • Synonym: deliverance - the action of being rescued or set free

Etymology

From Latin 'salvatio(n-)' (deliverance), from 'salvare' (to save).
