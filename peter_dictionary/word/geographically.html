geographically

1. ADV

/ dʒiːˌɑɡrəˈfɪkli / (AmE), / dʒiːˌɒɡrəˈfɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to geography or physical location.

Examples

Indeed, there are some cities where the suburban shopping pulls customers who are geographically much nearer to downtown.
These differences in turn result from the fact that my Yokuts vocabularies were built up of terms selected mainly to insure unambiguity of English meaning between illiterate informants and myself, within a compact and uniform territorial area, but that Hoijer's vocabulary is based on Swadesh's second glottochronological list which aims at eliminating all items which might be culturally or geographically determined.

Synset Examples

they are geographically closer to the center of town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topographically - in a way that relates to the arrangement of physical features
  • Synonym: geographically - with respect to geography

Etymology

From 'geographical' + '-ly', ultimately from Greek 'geōgraphia' (earth description).
