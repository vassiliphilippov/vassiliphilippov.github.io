maxim

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk-sɪm / (AmE), / ˈmæk-sɪm / (BrE)
a general statement of principle, truth, or rule of conduct, often expressed as a concise saying

Examples

This clergyman should have referred to Shakespeare's dictum: "So-so is a good, very good, very excellent maxim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proverb - a short popular saying, usually of unknown origin, that expresses effectively some commonplace truth or useful thought
  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a scientific principle, typically by a classical author
  • Synonym: dictum - a formal pronouncement from an authoritative source

Etymology

From Latin 'maxima (propositio)' meaning 'greatest (proposition)'
