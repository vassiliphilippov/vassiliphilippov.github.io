sack

1. NOUN

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
a large bag made of paper or plastic, used to hold a customer's purchases in a shop

Examples

It was like hitting a sack of salt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bag - a container made of flexible material, used for carrying things

2. VERB

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
to plunder and destroy (a town or city) after capturing it, typically by an invading army

Examples

The savage barbarian hordes of red Russian Communism descended on the Athens that was mighty Metronome, sacking and despoiling with their Bolshevistic battle cry of "Soak the rich'!

Synset Examples

the barbarians sacked Rome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of war
  • Synonym: pillage - to rob a place using violence, especially in wartime

Etymology

From French *sac* (bag), from Latin *saccus*, possibly from Italian *sacco* (in the phrase 'mettere a sacco', to plunder, literally 'to put in a bag').
