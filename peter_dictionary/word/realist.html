realist

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɪəlɪst / (BrE)
A philosopher who believes that universals or abstract concepts exist independently of individual thought or perception.

Examples

William Gilmore Simms, sturdy realist that he was, pleaded for a natural robustness such as he found in his favorites the great Elizabethans, to vivify the pale writings being produced around him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nominalist - a philosopher who denies the real existence of universals; only individual things exist

Etymology

Formed from 'real' + '-ist', denoting an adherent of realism.
