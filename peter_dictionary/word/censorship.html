censorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəʃɪp / (BrE)
The act or policy of suppressing, removing, or restricting access to information, speech, or other forms of expression considered objectionable or sensitive, often by authorities.

Examples

We would establish no censorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - a person who suppresses or edits information

Etymology

From French 'censurer' and Latin 'censura' (judgment).
