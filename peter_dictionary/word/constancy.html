constancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstənsi / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənsi / (BrE)
The quality of being unchanging, enduring, or reliable over time.

Examples

The constancy of p and the independence are the conditions most likely to give trouble in practice.

Synset Examples

early mariners relied on the constancy of the trade winds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconstancy - the quality of being changeable or lacking steadiness

Etymology

From Latin 'constantia' (firmness, steadiness), from 'constans' (standing firm).
