spearhead

1. VERB

/ ˈspɪrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈspɪəhɛd / (BrE)
to lead or initiate an activity, movement, or project

Examples

"It has become our responsibility and I hope that the Citizens Group will spearhead the movement ".

Synset Examples

She spearheaded the effort to find a cure for the disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - to be in charge or command of
  • Synonym: initiate - to begin or start something

Etymology

From 'spearhead' (noun), originally meaning the pointed head of a spear; figurative use in the 20th century for leading a movement or project.
