discarded

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɑːrdɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːdɪd / (BrE)
Having been thrown away or abandoned because no longer useful or wanted.

Examples

The discarded papers inside were sodden, there was a glint of liquid at the bottom, and the smell of whisky was strong and distinct.

Synset Examples

wearing someone's cast-off clothes
throwaway children living on the streets
salvaged some thrown-away furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandoned - left behind or deserted
  • Antonym: retained - kept or held onto

Etymology

From the verb 'discard', which comes from Middle French 'discarder', from Italian 'scartare' (to reject).
