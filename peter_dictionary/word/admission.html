admission

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of allowing someone to enter a place, institution, or organization

Examples

No one can deny that these "back door "admissions to membership provisions have been seriously abused nor that they have not resulted in the admission of downright incompetents to membership in supposedly learned societies.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of entering or being allowed to enter a place

Etymology

From Latin 'admissio' (a letting in), from 'admittere' (to let in).

2. NOUN

/ ædˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a statement in which someone recognizes or accepts that something is true, often reluctantly

Examples

Although the monarch had frequently asserted that the elections were to be without party significance, his action was an implicit admission that party identifications were a factor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - a formal statement admitting that one is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing
  • Antonym: denial - a statement claiming that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'admissio' (a letting in), from 'admittere' (to admit).
