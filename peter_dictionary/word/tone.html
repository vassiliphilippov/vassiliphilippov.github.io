tone

1. NOUN

/ toʊn / (AmE), / təʊn / (BrE)
A particular quality, shade, or tint of a color that is slightly different from another.

Examples

The citrus tones popular in clothing are also to be found afoot.
First I make preliminary watercolor sketches in quarter scale (approximately * * f inches) in which I pay particular attention to the design principles of three simple values - the lightest light, the middle tone, and the darkest dark - by reducing the forms of my subject to these large patterns.

Synset Examples

after several trials he mixed the shade of pink that she wanted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - a slight variation in color
  • Synonym: tint - a delicate or pale variation of a color
  • Synonym: hue - a particular gradation of color

Etymology

From Latin 'tonus' (sound, tone), influenced in the color sense by analogy with gradation in music.

7. NOUN
