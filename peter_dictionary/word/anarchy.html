anarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɚki / (AmE), / ˈænəki / (BrE)
A situation in which there is no government, order, or control, often resulting in lawlessness and disorder.

Examples

Only confusion, failure and anarchy result when the effort is made to impose upon the civil authority the impossible task of policing private homes to preclude the possibility of sin.
Anything is possible when anarchy has the upper hand ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: order - a situation in which rules are obeyed and authority is respected

Etymology

From Greek anarkhia (lack of a ruler), from an- (not, without) + arkhos (ruler).
