slaughter

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
A decisive or overwhelming defeat, especially in sports or competition.

Examples

He ran his string of successful conversions this season to 13 straight before one went astray last Saturday night in the 41 - 8 slaughter of Washington State.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rout - a disorderly retreat or defeating of an opponent
  • Synonym: massacre - an overwhelming or crushing defeat
  • Antonym: win - to achieve victory in a contest or competition

2. VERB

/ ˈslɔt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
to kill a large number of people indiscriminately and brutally

Examples

Men were slaughtered every day, tumbled into eternity like so many torn parcels flung down a portable chute.
If the railroads, for example, regularly slaughtered 25000 passengers each year, the high priests of the cult would have cause to tremble for their personal safety, for such a holocaust would excite demands for the hanging of every railroad president in the United States.

Synset Examples

The Hutus massacred the Tutsis in Rwanda

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - to deliberately and violently kill a large number of people
  • Synonym: butcher - to kill people in a brutal or indiscriminate way
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or harming

Etymology

From Old English *slahtrian* (to butcher), from *slēan* (to slay).

3. NOUN

/ ˈslɔtər / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
the killing of animals, especially for food

Examples

Discontinue medication 48 hours before slaughter.
Do not feed to dairy cows and do not feed within 60 days of slaughter.
Not possible, he thought; the prime minister knew who his enemy was here; he was n't going to allow himself to be led meekly to the slaughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butchery - the practice of slaughtering animals for food or selling their meat

Etymology

From Old French *esclatre*, from Old Norse *slátr* (butcher's meat).

4. VERB

/ ˈslɔːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtə / (BrE)
to kill animals, especially for food consumption, often in large numbers and sometimes in a violent or cruel manner

Examples

Additive should not be fed 72 hours before animals are slaughtered.
He wisecracked about the captain's indoctrination of new men, took great delight in slaughtering cockroaches with ethyl chloride, and gave no thought for tomorrow.

Synset Examples

They slaughtered their only goat to survive the winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butcher - to kill animals and prepare them for food

Etymology

From Middle English *slaughter* via Old Norse *slátr* (butcher’s meat).
