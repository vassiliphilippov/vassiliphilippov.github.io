desecration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsəˈkreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛsɪˈkreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act of treating a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect or irreverence.

Examples

Hell's own amount of chaulmoogra oil did nothing to alleviate their torment; they expired amid indescribable fantods, imploring the Blessed One to forgive their desecration.

Synset Examples

desecration of the Holy Sabbath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profanation - the act of treating something sacred with disrespect
  • Antonym: veneration - great respect or reverence, especially for something sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'dēsecrāre' (to make unholy, profane).
