adjudication

1. NOUN

/ əˌdʒuː.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌdʒuː.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of making a formal judgment or decision about a legal problem or disputed matter, especially in a court of law.

Examples

The Secretary of State shall certify to the Secretary of the Treasury the total cost of adjudication, not borne by the claimants, attributable to the Yugoslav Claims Agreement of 1948.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - a formal decision by a court
  • Synonym: arbitration - the use of an arbitrator to settle a dispute

Etymology

From Latin 'adjudicatio', from 'adjudicare' (to judge, assign).
