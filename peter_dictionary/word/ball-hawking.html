ball-hawking

1. ADJ

/ bɔl ˈhɔːkɪŋ / (AmE), / bɔːl ˈhɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a player) skilled at intercepting the ball or preventing the opposing team from succeeding, especially by stealing the ball or denying a hit.

Examples

Mankowski, the ball-hawking defensive expert, was cited for his performance against Bradley in St. Louis U.'s nationally televised victory.

Synset Examples

a ball-hawking center fielder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive - focused on preventing the opponent from scoring or advancing
  • Antonym: offensive - focused on attacking or scoring against the opponent

Etymology

From 'ball' + 'hawking', implying vigilance and skill in capturing the ball, by analogy with a hawk's ability to seize prey.
