neglected

1. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (BrE)
Not receiving proper care or attention; lacking a caretaker.

Examples

But it had, as was usual in southern cities of this sort, a Black Bottom, a low region near the river where the Negroes lived - servants and laborers huddled together in a region with no sewage save the river, where streets and sidewalks were neglected and where there was much poverty and crime.

Synset Examples

a neglected child
many casualties were lying unattended

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cared for - having received care or attention; attended to

Etymology

From the verb 'neglect', which comes from Latin 'neglegere', meaning 'to disregard, not heed'.

2. ADJ

/ nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktɪd / (BrE)
not receiving proper care or attention; disregarded or ignored.

Examples

It was dirty and neglected.

Synset Examples

his cries were unheeded
Shaw's neglected one-act comedy, `A Village Wooing'
her ignored advice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignored - not given attention; disregarded
  • Synonym: unattended - not looked after or supervised
  • Antonym: cared for - looked after; given attention or concern

Etymology

Past participle of 'neglect', from Latin *neglectus*, past participle of *negleger* (to neglect).
