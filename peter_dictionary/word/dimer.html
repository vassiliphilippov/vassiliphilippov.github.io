dimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪmə / (BrE)
a molecule or compound formed by the combination of two identical monomers

Examples

(1) Below the critical micelle concentration, monomers and some dimers are present.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monomer - a molecule that can join with others to form a polymer
  • Degree Higher: polymer - a substance made of many repeated monomer units

Etymology

From di- (two) and Greek meros (part), after polymer.
