idly

1. ADV

/ ˈaɪdli / (AmE), / ˈaɪdli / (BrE)
In a lazy, inactive, or unenergetic manner; without taking action or purposefully.

Examples

Instead we have stood idly by, watched our commuter railroad service decline, and have failed to offer a helping hand.
A man knocked the roulette ball about idly in its track, and another dozed at one of the card tables.

Synset Examples

this is what I always imagined myself doing in the south of France, sitting idly, drinking coffee, watching the people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actively - in an active, energetic, or engaged manner

Etymology

From idle + -ly, modeled after the formation of adverbs from adjectives in English.
