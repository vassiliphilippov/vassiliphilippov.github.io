atone

1. VERB

/ əˈtoʊn / (AmE), / əˈtəʊn / (BrE)
to make amends or reparation for a fault, mistake, or wrongdoing

Examples

It is well for us to remember that a wreath on a coffin never can atone for flowers withheld while they still can be enjoyed.

Synset Examples

expiate one's sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiate - to make amends or reparation for (guilt or wrongdoing)

Etymology

From Middle English, influenced by the phrase 'at one' (in harmony); later associated with making reparation.
