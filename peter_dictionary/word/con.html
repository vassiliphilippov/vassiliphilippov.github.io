con

1. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
an argument or consideration against something; a reason for opposing a proposal or idea

Examples

"We 're getting more' pro' letters than' con' on horse race betting ", said Ratcliff.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro - an argument or consideration in favor of something

Etymology

From Latin contra (against), via the phrase 'pro et contra' (for and against).

2. VERB

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
to deceive someone in order to get money or something valuable from them; to swindle or trick

Examples

Multiply these figures by the millions of people known to be conned by medical pirates annually.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her
the cashier gypped me when he gave me too little change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage

Etymology

Probably a shortening of 'confidence trick' or 'confidence game'.

3. NOUN

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
a person serving a sentence in a jail or prison; a convict

Examples

Just a used-up con with arthritis in both hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convict - a person found guilty of a crime and sentenced to prison

Etymology

Informal shortening of 'convict'.

4. ADV

/ kɑn / (AmE), / kɒn / (BrE)
in opposition to a proposal, opinion, or course of action

Examples

If you give up the right to remain silent, anything you say will be held against you in court.

Synset Examples

much was written pro and con

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro - in favor of something; supporting a proposal or opinion

Etymology

Shortened from Latin 'contra' (against, opposite).
