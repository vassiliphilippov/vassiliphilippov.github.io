insulate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.səˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
To protect or separate someone or something from outside influences or contact; to isolate.

Examples

Residential associations struggle to insulate themselves against intrusions.
In proportion as meanings are concrete, we can better rely on their being insulated and distinctive.

Synset Examples

They isolated the political prisoners from the other inmates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolate - to set apart or detach from others; to separate

Etymology

From Latin 'insulatus' (made into an island), from 'insula' (island).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪn.sə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to protect something by surrounding it with material that prevents the passage of heat, sound, or electricity

Examples

A small pressure unbalance displaces the diaphragm and changes the capacitance between the diaphragm and an electrically insulated plate spaced 0.001 in. apart (for * * f).
Insulate, weatherstrip, double-glaze to the maximum.

Synset Examples

We had his bedroom insulated before winter came

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolate - to keep something or someone apart from others

Etymology

From Latin 'insula' meaning 'island', via Medieval Latin 'insulatus' ('made into an island').
