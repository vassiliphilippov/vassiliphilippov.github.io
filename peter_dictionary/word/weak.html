weak

1. ADJ

/ wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
(of a drink or solution) overly diluted, thin, and lacking strength or flavor.

Examples

In addition to the rich soil they benefit by feedings of manure water every other week, diluted to the color of weak tea.
"If you will promise to make it weak ".
For he remembered too well how he had brought back the loaded drinks to Burton and then returned to the kitchen to get weaker drinks for himself.

Synset Examples

washy coffee
watery milk
weak tea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong - (of a drink or solution) concentrated, having a lot of flavor or potency
  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor or interest

Etymology

From Old English wāc, meaning 'lacking strength'.

2. ADJ

/ wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
lacking physical strength or power; not strong.

Examples

It is no harder to raise big, healthy, blooming plants than weak, sickly little things; in fact it is easier.
Those who are too weak, should climb on the chair and, starting at the top of the chin, let themselves slowly down.
If, however, the child is weak, overweight, or afraid, more help will be needed.
For a moment or two, both scenes are present simultaneously, one growing weaker, one growing stronger.
An isotropic resonance shift of f to lower applied fields indicates a weak** isotropic hyperfine contact interaction.

Synset Examples

a weak pillar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong - having great physical strength or power

Etymology

From Old English wāc (weak, pliant, soft).

3. ADJ

/ wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
(of a vowel or syllable) pronounced with little or no stress.

Examples

In I have things to do the word things makes little real contribution to meaning and has weaker stress than do.

Synset Examples

a syllable that ends in a short vowel is a light syllable
a weak stress on the second syllable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstressed - (of a syllable or vowel) not stressed in pronunciation
  • Antonym: stressed - (of a syllable or vowel) pronounced with emphasis or greater force

4. ADJ

/ wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
Lacking physical strength or energy; having little bodily or muscular power or vitality.

Examples

He'’s weak.

Synset Examples

a feeble old woman
her body looked sapless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially due to age or illness
  • Antonym: strong - having great physical power or energy

Etymology

From Old English wāc (weak, pliant, ineffectual).

5. ADJ

/ wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
Lacking moral strength, courage, or willpower; easily influenced or unable to resist temptation.

Examples

But he'’s weak and he'’s stupid, and this is life and death.

Synset Examples

I'm only a fallible human
frail humanity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong - having great moral courage or willpower

Etymology

From Old English wēac (pliant, soft), related to Old Norse veikr (weak).
