traitorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtreɪtərəs / (AmE), / ˈtreɪtərəs / (BrE)
Disloyal to one's country or cause; having the qualities of a traitor.

Examples

Anyone who tried to remedy some of the most glaring defects in our form of democracy was denounced as a traitorous red whose real purpose was the destruction of our government.

Synset Examples

the faithless Benedict Arnold
a lying traitorous insurrectionist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loyal - faithful and devoted to a person, group, or cause

Etymology

From Middle English traitourous, from Old French traitorous, from Latin traditor (traitor).
