searching

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɝːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Examining closely, thoroughly, or in a way that reveals the truth; diligent and penetrating in inquiry or investigation.

Examples

Boxell looked at Lawrence with a searching glance, the kind that a prosecuting attorney would give a man on trial.
These examples underscore the importance of even more searching evaluations of new major development programs and even more penetrating and far ranging analyses of the potentialities of future technology.

Synset Examples

a probing inquiry
a searching investigation of their past dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probing - thorough and searching, especially in inquiry or investigation
  • Antonym: superficial - not thorough or detailed; lacking depth in investigation

Etymology

From the verb 'search', from Old French 'cerchier', from Latin 'circāre' (to go around, wander).
