affiliation

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of officially connecting or associating with an organization, group, or cause.

Examples

The electoral procedure prevented the ready identification of party affiliation, but all vitally interested parties, including the government itself, were busily engaged in determining the party identifications of all successful candidates the month following the elections.
And he has proclaimed greater or less affiliation between all Western hemisphere languages.

Synset Examples

welcomed the affiliation of the research center with the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: association - the process of being connected; a link between people or organizations
  • Antonym: disaffiliation - the process of ending a formal connection or association

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatio(n-), from affiliare 'to adopt as a son'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a person's connection or association with a group, organization, or social or business entity

Examples

Both parties and the Ministry of the Interior were busily at work after the elections trying to unearth the political affiliations of the successful candidates and, thereby, give the elections a confidential but known degree of national political significance.
Simms admired the raucous tales emanating from the backwoods, but he had himself social affiliations which would not allow him to approve them fully.

Synset Examples

a valuable financial affiliation
he was sorry he had to sever his ties with other members of the team
many close associations with England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: association - a connection or relationship with an organization or group

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatio, from Latin affiliare (to adopt as a son).
