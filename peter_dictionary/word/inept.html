inept

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛpt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛpt / (BrE)
Having or showing a lack of skill, ability, or competence; generally incompetent and ineffective.

Examples

Of the two, Porter is justly the better known, for he went far beyond the vital finding of fiction for films to take the first step toward fashioning a language of film, toward making the motion picture the intricate, efficient time machine that it has remained since, even in the most inept hands.

Synset Examples

feckless attempts to repair the plumbing
inept handling of the account

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills
  • Antonym: capable - having ability or qualities necessary for a task
  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling tasks
  • Synonym: ineffective - not producing the desired result

Etymology

From Latin ineptus (unsuitable, foolish), from in- (not) + aptus (fit, suited).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛpt / (AmE), / ɪˈnept / (BrE)
Lacking elegance or grace in expression, style, or performance; clumsy or awkward.

Examples

Over the door was a board with large, inept lettering:

Synset Examples

an awkward prose style
a clumsy apology
his cumbersome writing style
if the rumor is true, can anything be more inept than to repeat it now?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill in movement or manner
  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking skill or finesse; awkward in movement or style
  • Antonym: elegant - graceful and stylish in appearance or manner

Etymology

From Latin ineptus (unsuitable, awkward), from in- (not) + aptus (fit).
