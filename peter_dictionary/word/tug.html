tug

1. NOUN

/ tʌɡ / (AmE), / tʌɡ / (BrE)
a sudden or forceful pull

Examples

As soon as an experimental tug assures you that roots have taken over, cut it off from the mother plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull - an act of drawing or hauling something toward oneself
  • Antonym: push - an act of pressing against something to move it away

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'tugga' (to pull).

2. VERB

/ tʌɡ / (AmE), / tʌɡ / (BrE)
to pull something hard, often suddenly or repeatedly

Examples

Seizing the bridle, he tugged with all his might and forced Katie to her knees.
A shot or two went wild before Cobb felt something tug at his foot.
He tugged the brim of his hat low as he approached, stepped out into the street just behind the car and strode around to the right-hand side.

Synset Examples

The prisoner tugged at the chains
This movie tugs at the heart strings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull - draw or haul something towards oneself
  • Synonym: yank - pull with a sudden, sharp movement
  • Antonym: push - move something by exerting force away from oneself

Etymology

Perhaps imitative or of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian tugga 'to pull'.
