flowering

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
the period during which something reaches its most successful or developed stage; the peak or culmination of growth or achievement

Examples

The long settled areas of states like Virginia and South Carolina developed the ante-bellum culture to its richest flowering, and there the memory is more precious, and the consciousness of loss the greater.

Synset Examples

the flowering of antebellum culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heyday - the period of greatest success, popularity, or power
  • Synonym: prime - the time of greatest vigor or success
  • Antonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, or quality

Etymology

From the noun 'flower' + suffix '-ing', originally referring to the act of producing flowers, then extended metaphorically.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or period during which a plant produces flowers; the process of blossoming.

Examples

Now the thing for us to do is to find ourselves a couple of those wonderful flowering currants such as the red Ribes sanguineum of our Pacific Northwest, or otherwise a good sloe tree, or perhaps some nice pussy willow in bloom, preferably one with male or staminate catkins.
Their appearance, next spring, coincides in an almost uncanny way with the flowering of their host plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossoming - the process of producing flowers; flowering
  • Degree Higher: fruition - the state of bearing fruit following flowering

Etymology

From 'flower' + '-ing', denoting the action or process.
