grove

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊv / (AmE), / ɡrəʊv / (BrE)
A small group of trees, often cultivated and without undergrowth, typically planted for fruit or ornamental purposes.

Examples

This cooling does not change the avocado in any way, it just delays the natural softening of the fruit until a grove like temperature (room temperature) is restored.
He came to a stretch of old orange groves, the trees dead, some of them uprooted, and then there was an outlying shopping area, and tract houses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchard - an area of land where fruit trees are grown

Etymology

From Old English 'graf' (grove, copse, thicket).

2. NOUN

/ ɡroʊv / (AmE), / ɡrəʊv / (BrE)
A small group of trees growing together with little or no underbrush.

Examples

Curt approached the place cautiously, and watched it several minutes from the protection of a grove of trees.

Etymology

From Old English *grāf* (grove, copse).
