conqueror

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋkərər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋkərə / (BrE)
A person who achieves victory over others, especially by force of arms; a person who conquers a country, people, or enemy.

Examples

Gustaf Vasa is a superb example, and Charles 10,, the conqueror of Denmark, hardly less so.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victor - a person who defeats an enemy or opponent
  • Antonym: loser - a person who is defeated in a contest

Etymology

From Old French 'conquerre' (to conquer), from Latin 'conquirere' (to seek, procure, attain).
