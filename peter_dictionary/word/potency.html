potency

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊtənsi / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtənsi / (BrE)
The power or authority to influence or control; the capacity to give orders or make decisions.

Examples

Yet such is the dramatic power of his writing that the audience is nevertheless left in the grip of the terrible power and potency of that which came over Salem.
Those who would suppress dangerous thoughts, credit ideas with high potency.

Synset Examples

he has the authority to issue warrants
deputies are given authorization to make arrests
a place of potency in the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way
  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience
  • Antonym: weakness - the state or condition of lacking strength or power

Etymology

From Latin potentia (power, might), from potens (being able).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tən.si / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tən.si / (BrE)
the capacity to produce strong physiological or chemical effects

Examples

The most active preparations obtained by these two groups of investigators appear to be similar in potency, composition and physical properties.

Synset Examples

the toxin's potency
the strength of the drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - degree to which something is strong or effective
  • Synonym: efficacy - the ability to produce a desired or intended result
  • Antonym: weakness - lack of strength or potency; less effective

Etymology

From Latin potentia (power, might), from potens (powerful).
