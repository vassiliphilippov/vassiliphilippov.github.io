dialectically

1. ADV

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈlɛktɪkli / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈlɛktɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or involves dialectic, especially as a method of argument or reasoning by examining and resolving contradictions.

Examples

His religiousness is intimately, or dialectically, connected with his sinfulness; the two may in fact be identical.

Synset Examples

his religiousness is dialectically related to his sinfulness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialectic - the art of investigating or discussing the truth of opinions; logical argumentation

Etymology

From dialectic + -ally; dialectic from Greek dialektikē (art of discourse or debate).
