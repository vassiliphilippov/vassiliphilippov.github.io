thatch

1. NOUN

/ θætʃ / (AmE), / θætʃ / (BrE)
A person's hair, especially when thick, matted, or resembling thatched roofing.

Examples

The lieutenant's sparse brown hair was heavily pomaded, and as Killpath raked the comb through it, it stuck together in thatches so that it looked like umbrella ribs clinging to his pink skull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hair - the mass of thin thread-like structures that grow on a person's head

Etymology

From Old English *þæc* (thatch, cover), related to German *Dach* (roof).
