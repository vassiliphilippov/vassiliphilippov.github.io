misjudge

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / mɪsˈdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
to form an incorrect opinion or estimate about someone or something

Examples

Solomon Chandler had n't misjudged the strength of his lungs, not at all.
"We misjudged you ", he said slowly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: judge - to form an opinion or estimate about something or someone
  • Synonym: underestimate - to estimate something as less important or smaller than it is

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'judge', from Old French 'jugier', from Latin 'iūdicāre' (to judge).
