illustrious

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlʌstriəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstriəs / (BrE)
famous and admired for past achievements; glorious or highly distinguished.

Examples

More than a beautiful visualization of the illustrious adventures and escapades of the tragi-comic knight-errant and his squire, Sancho Panza, in seventeenth century Spain, this inevitably abbreviated rendering of the classic satire on chivalry is an affectingly warm and human exposition of character.

Synset Examples

an illustrious achievement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - well known by many people
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known, unknown

Etymology

From Latin illustri-, illustrare (to make bright, illuminate, embellish).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlʌstriəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstriəs / (BrE)
well known, respected, and admired for past achievements or qualities

Examples

My heartiest congratulations go to their successors, Orvil E. Dryfoos and John B. Oakes, who can be counted upon to sustain the illustrious tradition of the New York Times.

Synset Examples

a famous actor
a celebrated musician
a famed scientist
an illustrious judge
a notable historian
a renowned painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrated - widely known and praised
  • Synonym: notable - worthy of attention or notice; remarkable
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; unknown to most people

Etymology

From Latin 'illustris' meaning 'bright, distinguished'.
