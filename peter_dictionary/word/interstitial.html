interstitial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈstɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈstɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated in the small spaces or gaps between things, especially between tissues or cells in an organism.

Examples

Some of the small vessels were filled with fibrin thrombi, and there was extensive interstitial hemorrhage.
Microscopically, both kidneys showed many small cortical scars in which there was glomerular and interstitial fibrosis, tubular atrophy, and an infiltration of lymphocytes and plasma cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interstice - a small space or gap between things

Etymology

From Latin 'interstitium' (an interval, gap) + '-al'.
