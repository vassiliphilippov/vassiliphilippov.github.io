tenet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.ɪt / (BrE)
A principle or belief, especially one of the main principles of a religion or philosophy, held to be true by an individual or group.

Examples

Each religion has its own tenets ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - A principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true

Etymology

From Latin 'tenet' (he/she/it holds), from 'tenēre' (to hold).
