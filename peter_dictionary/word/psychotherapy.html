psychotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the treatment of mental and emotional disorders using psychological methods, especially by talking with a trained therapist rather than using drugs or other physical treatments

Examples

It is believed that drug therapy and electroshock involve the former and psychotherapy the latter mechanism.
It forms the core of many, perhaps most, problems of psychotherapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counseling - professional guidance in resolving personal or psychological problems
  • Synonym: psychotherapy - treatment of mental illness by psychological means
  • Antonym: pharmacotherapy - treatment of disease through the use of drugs

Etymology

From Greek psycho- (mind) + therapeia (healing, treatment).
