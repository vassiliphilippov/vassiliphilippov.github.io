urgency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
The quality or state of requiring immediate attention; a pressing importance.

Examples

The chase in itself is a narrative; it presumes both speed and urgency and it demands cutting - both from pursued to pursuer and from stage to stage of the journey of both.
There is a new atmosphere of urgency in Washington this week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediacy - the quality of bringing about immediate results or requiring urgent attention
  • Degree Lower: importance - the state or fact of being of great significance or value
  • Degree Higher: emergency - a serious, unexpected, and often dangerous situation requiring immediate action

Etymology

From Latin urgentia (pressing, urgent quality), from urgēre (to press, urge).
