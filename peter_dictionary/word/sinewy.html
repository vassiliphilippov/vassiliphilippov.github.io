sinewy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnju.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjuː.i / (BrE)
(of meat or muscle) full of sinews; tough and difficult to chew; also, having a lean and muscular appearance with prominent sinews.

Examples

Not bulky laborers, but sinewy men.
All the while she sat there, her sinewy arms swirled before her chest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - (of meat) not easy to chew or cut; durable
  • Antonym: tender - (of meat) easy to chew or cut

Etymology

From 'sinew' (Old English *seonu*), meaning tendon or ligament, plus '-y', forming an adjective.
