honesty

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnəsti / (AmE), / ˈɒnəsti / (BrE)
The quality of being honest; the state of being truthful and free from deceit.

Examples

Parson Weems's George Washington became the symbol of honesty and the father image of the uniting States.
His tough honesty condemns him to a solitary and difficult existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles; moral uprightness
  • Antonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty; deceitfulness or untrustworthiness

Etymology

From Old French 'honeste', from Latin 'honestas' (honor, respectability).
