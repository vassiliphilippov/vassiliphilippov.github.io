reservation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈveɪʃn / (BrE)
an area of land set aside by a government for a specific purpose, especially for Native American peoples or for use by the military or government.

Examples

These were located at the homes of Government employees, in Government Offices, buildings and reservations within the trial area.
A tale of a massive reservation in the Uplands to hold several million Jews.
Observing their distressing condition, Colonel Snelling allowed these half starved immigrants to settle on the military reservation.
In 1837, 157 Red River people with more than 200 cattle were living on the reservation at Fort Snelling.
Its people, including Pierre Bottineau and other American Fur Company employees and the refugees from Fort Garry, were joined by the remaining Scots and Swiss from Fort Snelling when Major Joseph Plympton expelled them from the reservation in May 1840.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - an area of land set apart for protection or special use

Etymology

From Latin 'reservatio', from 'reservare' (to keep back, reserve), via French 'réservation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of doubt or uncertainty that makes you reluctant to accept something fully.

Examples

Adlai Stevenson expressed some reservations about this return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Synonym: misgiving - a feeling of doubt, distrust, or apprehension
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of certainty and assurance

Etymology

From Latin reservatio (a keeping back), from reservare (to keep back).
