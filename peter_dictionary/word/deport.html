deport

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɔːt / (BrE)
To officially force someone to leave a country, usually because they have broken the law or do not have the right to stay.

Examples

Death, natural or not, prison, deported...

Synset Examples

The poet was exiled because he signed a letter protesting the government's actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to force someone out (from a place)
  • Synonym: exile - to force someone to live outside their native country

Etymology

From French *déporter*, from Latin *deportare* (to carry away, banish).
