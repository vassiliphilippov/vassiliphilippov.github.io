every

1. DET

/ ˈɛvri / (AmE), / ˈɛvri / (BrE)
used before a singular countable noun to refer to all the members of a group considered individually, without exception

Examples

Every evening, I load up the cart and make my round.

Synset Examples

every person is mortal
every party is welcome
had every hope of success
every chance of winning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: each - each member of a group considered separately

Etymology

From Old English *ǽfre ǣgþer* (‘ever either, each of two’), reinforced by Old French *evere*.
