brooding

1. ADJ

/ ˈbruːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbruːdɪŋ / (BrE)
deeply or seriously thoughtful, often with a suggestion of unhappiness or serious contemplation

Examples

And it also stayed the hands of thousands of brooding incompetents who were meditating the abandonment of a sport whose frustrations were driving them to despair.
Juanita drooped about the place, wearing a haunted, brooding look, which Kate attributed to the baby's death, until the day a letter came for her addressed to "Miss Juanita Fitzroy ", bearing a Grafton postmark.
He considered them with brooding eyes, brows bunched as his brain grappled with the problem and tried to find some solution.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the 'Byronic hero' - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pensive - engaged in serious thought
  • Antonym: cheerful - marked by happiness and optimism

Etymology

Present participle of 'brood', from Old English *brōd* (offspring, hatching), extended metaphorically to describe inward dwelling on thoughts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbruːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbruːdɪŋ / (BrE)
The process in which a bird or other animal sits on its eggs to incubate and hatch them by keeping them warm.

Examples

The range is from 14 to 25 inches; the former figure is based on a somewhat unusual birth of four by a Central American female (see chapter on Laying, Brooding, Hatching, and Birth), the latter on a "normal "newly born individual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incubation - the process of maintaining eggs at the proper temperature so they develop and hatch

Etymology

From 'brood', Middle English, related to Old English *brōd* (offspring, brood).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbruːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbruːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A state of deep, persistent, and often gloomy or morbid thought, especially about problems or difficulties.

Examples

And yet amid all the gay hedonism in Pilgrimage and Wander-Years is a cycle of short poems, "Thoughts in Loneliness ", filled with brooding, melancholy, and sombre longing ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pensiveness - the state of being deep in serious thought
  • Synonym: rumination - the act of thinking deeply about something; prolonged consideration
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'brood' (verb: to think deeply, especially in a worried or troubled way), from Middle English 'broden', from Old English 'brōd' (offspring, brood).
