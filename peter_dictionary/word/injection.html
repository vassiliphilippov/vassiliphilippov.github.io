injection

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of forcing a liquid, gas, or other substance into something, usually by means of a syringe or similar device, under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infusion - the introduction of a liquid into the body, especially for medical purposes

Etymology

From Latin 'injectio', from 'inicere' (to throw in).
