chuckle

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌk.əl / (BrE)
to laugh quietly or to oneself, often in a restrained or suppressed way

Examples

He chuckled, the memory vivid.
"Four cuts at least ", he chuckled to himself, "and I owe it all to them ".
"Not this old fox ", chuckled Uncle Randolph.
What a silly, artificial way of life, Chabrier and his librettists chuckle.
His voice lost its sullen tones and he chuckled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - to laugh in a light, silly, and repeated way
  • Synonym: snicker - to laugh quietly, typically in a scornful or sly way
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously

Etymology

Imitative origin, possibly from the sound of suppressed laughter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkəl / (BrE)
a quiet or partly suppressed laugh, typically expressing mild amusement

Examples

Though there was an occasional good-natured chuckle about Marvin Goulding, the Jewish officer from Chicago, singing tearfully about the ould sod, no one really thought it was strange.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: giggle - a light, silly, and often uncontrollable laugh
  • Synonym: snicker - a partly stifled, sly laugh; similar but often more derisive

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the sound of suppressed laughter.
