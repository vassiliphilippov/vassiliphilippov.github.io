peptidase

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛp.tɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.tɪˌdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down proteins or peptides into smaller fragments or amino acids by hydrolyzing peptide bonds

Examples

The presence of several proteases and peptidases has been demonstrated in the thyroid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protease - enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of proteins
  • Synonym: proteinase - synonym for protease; enzyme splitting proteins

Etymology

From peptide + -ase (enzyme suffix).
