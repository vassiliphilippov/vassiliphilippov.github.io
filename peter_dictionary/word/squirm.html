squirm

1. VERB

/ skwɜrm / (AmE), / skwɜːm / (BrE)
to twist or wriggle the body from side to side, especially as a result of discomfort, embarrassment, or in an attempt to escape

Examples

The furor was such that people who could not possibly have squirmed their way into the rehearsals were pretending that they were intimate with the whole affair and that it would be sensational.
Joyce squirmed a little on the divan.
And so, he squirms with each play, remembering his youth.

Synset Examples

The prisoner writhed in discomfort
The child tried to wriggle free from his aunt's embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writhe - to make twisting, squirming movements or contortions of the body
  • Synonym: wriggle - to twist to and fro with quick movements, as a worm; squirm

Etymology

Perhaps imitative in origin, first recorded in the late 17th century.
