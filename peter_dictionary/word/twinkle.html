twinkle

1. VERB

/ ˈtwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
to shine with a light that changes rapidly from bright to faint or seems to move quickly

Examples

Her bright eyes were twinkling.

Synset Examples

The lights were flashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - to shine or give off bright light suddenly or intermittently
  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine with a lot of small bright flashes of light

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to Middle English 'twinklen'.
