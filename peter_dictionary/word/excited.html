excited

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing lively enthusiasm, eagerness, or emotional arousal

Examples

It made her look sweet and schoolgirlish, I was excited to be with her, but I did not know how to express it.
They were going to town, and they were both excited.
I find I'm so excited I can barely sit still or hold a thought in my head.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions; not excited
  • Degree Higher: thrilled - feeling intense excitement or pleasure
  • Degree Lower: interested - showing curiosity or concern about something

Etymology

From Latin 'excitare' meaning 'to rouse, to set in motion', via Old French 'exciter'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
very emotionally aroused, especially due to strong feelings or agitation

Examples

An excited woman was making an emergency call over the phone: "Doctor, please come over right away.
A restaurant posted a reminder to patrons "who became excited and left without paying their checks ".

Synset Examples

he would become emotional over nothing at all
she was worked up about all the noise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - free from agitation or excitement
  • Synonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From excite + -ed, where excite is from Latin excitare (to rouse, call forth).
