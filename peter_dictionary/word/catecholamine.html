catecholamine

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈkoʊləˌmin / (AmE), / ˌkatɪˈkəʊləˌmiːn / (BrE)
Any of a group of organic compounds, such as epinephrine and norepinephrine, that are produced by the adrenal glands and function as hormones or neurotransmitters.

Examples

Although in both emotions sympathetic symptoms are present, different autonomic somatic patterns underlie aggression and anxiety, respectively, as indicated by the rate of the excretion of the catecholamines, the state of the muscle tone, and the Mecholyl test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epinephrine - a hormone and neurotransmitter, also known as adrenaline
  • Synonym: norepinephrine - a hormone and neurotransmitter related to epinephrine

Etymology

From 'catechol' (a type of chemical structure) and 'amine' (organic compound containing nitrogen).
