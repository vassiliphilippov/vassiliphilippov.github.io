influx

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.flʌks / (BrE)
The arrival or entry of a large number of people or things, or the process of something flowing in.

Examples

This is much higher than the nickel content of terrestrial dusts and sediments and provides a basis for the determination of the meteoritic mass influx.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outflow - the process of flowing out

Etymology

From Latin 'influxus' (a flowing in), from 'in-' (into) + 'fluere' (to flow).
