confidentiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.fɪˌdɛn.ʃiˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˌden.ʃiˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being kept secret or private, especially to protect sensitive information from being disclosed.

Examples

It is not a medieval mental quirk or an attitude "unnourished by sense "to believe that husbands and wives should not be subjected to such a risk, or that such a possibility should not be permitted to endanger the confidentiality of the marriage relationship.

Synset Examples

you must respect the confidentiality of your client's communications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or the state of being kept secret
  • Antonym: disclosure - the act of making something known or public

Etymology

From confidential + -ity, patterned after terms denoting quality or state.
