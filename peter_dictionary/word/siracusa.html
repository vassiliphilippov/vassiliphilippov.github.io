Siracusa

1. PROPN

/ sirɑˈkuːzə / (AmE), / ˌsɪrəˈkuːzə / (BrE)
A city in southeastern Sicily, Italy, founded by Corinthians in the 8th century BC, known for its rich Greek and Roman history.

Examples

I want you to move to a villa near Siracusa...right now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Syracuse - English name for the same city in southeastern Sicily, Italy

Etymology

Italian name for the city known in English as Syracuse; from Ancient Greek Συράκουσαι (Surákousai).
