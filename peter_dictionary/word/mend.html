mend

1. VERB

/ mɛnd / (AmE), / mɛnd / (BrE)
(of a wound, injury, or part of the body) to heal or recover; return to a healthy state after damage.

Examples

Halfback Bud Priddy, slowed for almost a month by a slowly mending sprained ankle, joined TCU's workout Monday.

Synset Examples

My broken leg is mending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heal - to become healthy again; to make or become sound or healthy

Etymology

From Middle English menden, from Old French amender, from Latin emendare (to correct, free from fault).

2. VERB

/ mɛnd / (AmE), / mɛnd / (BrE)
to repair something that is broken, damaged, or torn by fixing or replacing parts

Examples

He carried the wood, carried the water, did the cooking, cleaning and mending, and occasionally got a kick in the butt for his pains.
Nothing broken that can n't be mended ".
Rev, George and Lolotte were mending shoes.
America, America, God mend thy every flaw, confirm thy soul in self control, thy liberty in law.

Synset Examples

She repaired her TV set
Repair my shoes please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - to restore something that is damaged to a good condition

Etymology

From Middle English menden, from Old French amender, from Latin emendare (to correct, free from fault).
