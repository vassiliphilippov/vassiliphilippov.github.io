meditate

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛdəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪteɪt / (BrE)
to think deeply or carefully about something, especially for a period of time

Examples

And it also stayed the hands of thousands of brooding incompetents who were meditating the abandonment of a sport whose frustrations were driving them to despair.
Tears burned behind his eyes as he prayed and meditated tonight.
It is harder, he muttered, to meditate on man (or woman) than on God.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemplate - to look thoughtfully for a long time at; to think deeply about
  • Synonym: ponder - to consider something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: speculate - to form a theory or conjecture about a subject without firm evidence

Etymology

From Latin meditari (to think about, consider), frequentative of mederi (to measure, consider).
