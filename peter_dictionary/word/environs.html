environs

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvaɪrənz / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪərənz / (BrE)
the area or surroundings near or around a particular place

Examples

Churches can be strengthened through this process of co-optation so long as the environs of the church provide a sufficient pool of people who can fit the pattern of economic integration; once the pool of recruits diminishes, the congregation is helpless - friendly contacts no longer keep it going.

Synset Examples

the country--the flat agricultural surround

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicinity - the area near or surrounding a particular place
  • Synonym: surroundings - the things and conditions around a person or place

Etymology

From French 'environ' (surroundings), from 'environner' (to surround).
