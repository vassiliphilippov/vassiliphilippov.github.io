impartial

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːʃəl / (BrE)
treating all people and groups equally; not biased or showing favoritism.

Examples

The sine qua non of the elections was naturally an impartial and standardized procedure.

Synset Examples

the cold neutrality of an impartial judge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biased - showing an unfair preference for or against something or someone
  • Synonym: neutral - not supporting or helping either side; impartial

Etymology

From Latin impar, imparis (unequal) + partial.
