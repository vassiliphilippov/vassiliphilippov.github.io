bunt

1. NOUN

/ bʌnt / (AmE), / bʌnt / (BrE)
In baseball, the act of lightly tapping the ball without swinging the bat, typically to make it roll a short distance in the infield.

Examples

Pete Ward was sent in for House and, after failing in a bunt attempt, popped to Howser on the grass back of short.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: swing - in baseball, a full forceful movement of the bat to hit the ball
