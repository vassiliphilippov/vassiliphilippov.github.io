denominationally

1. ADV

/ dɪˌnoʊməˈneɪʃənəli / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is determined by denominational (especially religious) distinctions.

Examples

This is brought out in the common religious ethos that prevails even in the denominationally diverse audiences at many secular semi-public and public occasions in the United States; and it is evidenced in the prayers offered, in the frequent religious allusions, and in the confirmation of points on religious grounds.

Synset Examples

denominationally diverse audiences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-denominationally - without regard to denomination

Etymology

From 'denominational' + '-ly'; 'denominational' from 'denomination' + '-al'.
