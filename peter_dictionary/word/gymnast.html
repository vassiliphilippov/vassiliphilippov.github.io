gymnast

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪmnæst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪmnæst / (BrE)
An athlete who is skilled in gymnastics, especially one who competes in gymnastics events.

Examples

The Russian gymnasts beat the tar out of the American gymnasts in the 1960 Olympics for one reason - they were better.

Russia's young gymnasts have studied dance before having the rigorous training on apparatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: athlete - person trained in or good at sports or physical exercise
  • Antonym: coach - someone who trains athletes or performers

Etymology

From Greek *gymnastēs* (trainer, athlete), from *gymnazein* (to exercise naked).
