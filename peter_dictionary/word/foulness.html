foulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being extremely dirty and disgusting; filthiness.

Examples

Andy crawled to freedom through 500 yards of shit-smelling foulness I can't even imagine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filth - extreme dirt or impurity
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean

Etymology

Formed from 'foul' (Middle English, from Old English 'fūl', unclean, vile) + '-ness' suffix.
