zone

1. NOUN

/ zoʊn / (AmE), / zəʊn / (BrE)
An area or region distinguished from adjacent parts by a particular feature, use, or condition.

Examples

Hal knew that when that side of Earth rotated into the night zone, Sigmen City would blaze out into space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - a large area, typically undefined by precise boundaries
  • Synonym: district - an area of a country or city, especially one regarded as a distinct unit for administration

Etymology

From French 'zone', from Latin 'zona' (belt, girdle), from Greek 'zōnē' (belt, girdle).
