scherzo

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛr.tsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈskɛːt.səʊ / (BrE)
a lively, fast-paced piece of music, usually in triple time, often serving as a movement in a symphony or sonata

Examples

The Presto ma non assai of the first trio of the scherzo is taken literally and may shock you, as the real Allegro con spirito of the finale is likely to bring you to your feet.

Etymology

From Italian scherzo (joke, jest), from scherzare (to joke).
