numinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
Having a strong spiritual quality, indicating or suggesting the presence of a divinity; evoking a sense of the sacred or supernatural.

Examples

For this does not account for the integral, elemental power of that which grows with abounding vigor as the play unfolds, nor does it explain the strange numinous sense of presentness which comes over those who watch the play like a spell.

Synset Examples

a numinous wood
the most numinous moment in the Mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystical - relating to mysticism or a sense of spiritual mystery
  • Synonym: sacred - regarded with great respect and reverence by a particular religion, group, or individual
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking spiritual or other-worldly qualities; ordinary

Etymology

From Latin numen (divine power or will) + -ous.
