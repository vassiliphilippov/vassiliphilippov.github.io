flourish

1. VERB

/ ˈflɝːɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflʌrɪʃ / (BrE)
to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way, especially as the result of a particularly favorable environment

Examples

Geneva, instead of becoming the City of God, as John had dreamed, had in the two years since he had been there, continued to be a godless place where all manner of vice flourished.
He wondered how they could go on in poverty, superstition, ignorance, with a complete lack of desire to make either their land or their lives flourish.
These my grandmother left in their places (they are still there, more persistent and longer-lived than the generations of man) and planted others like them, that flourished without careful tending.
How did it happen, for example, that the state university, that great symbol of American democracy, failed to flourish in New England as it did in other parts of the country?
Hoag stretched his left hand to the wall and fumbled for the switch: evil flourishes in the dark.

Synset Examples

The deer population in this town is thriving
business is booming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
  • Synonym: prosper - to be successful or fortunate, especially financially
  • Antonym: wither - to become dry and weak, cease to flourish

Etymology

From Old French *flourir*, from Latin *florere* (to bloom, to flower).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflʌrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflʌrɪʃ / (BrE)
A bold or extravagant gesture or action, often used to attract attention.

Examples

Some distribution costs are kept up by competitive pressure, some by the fact that the customers have come to expect certain niceties and flourishes.

Synset Examples

she entered with a great flourish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gesture - a movement of part of the body to express an idea or meaning

Etymology

From Old French *florir* (to bloom, flourish), from Latin *florere* (to bloom), from *flos* (flower).

3. VERB

/ ˈflɝː.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflʌr.ɪʃ / (BrE)
to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way, especially as the result of a particularly favorable environment; to thrive or prosper

Examples

Where only one club existed before, he says, two will flourish henceforth.

Synset Examples

The new student is thriving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
  • Synonym: prosper - to be successful, especially in financial or business matters
  • Antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful; not to thrive or flourish

Etymology

From Old French *flourir* (to blossom), from Latin *florere* (to bloom), from *flos* (flower).
