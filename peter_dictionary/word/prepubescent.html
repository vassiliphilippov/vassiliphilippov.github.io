prepubescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːpjuˈbɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːpjuːˈbɛsənt / (BrE)
(especially of a person or animal) relating to or denoting the period just before the onset of puberty, often characterized by accelerated physical growth.

Examples

The Skeletal Age curve in the lower portion of Figure 2 shows that slowing may have occurred for her during the prepubescent period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pubescent - having reached or undergoing puberty

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'pubescent' (reaching puberty).
