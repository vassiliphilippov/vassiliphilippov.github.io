lappet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪt / (BrE)
a fleshy, wrinkled, and often brightly colored fold of skin, typically hanging from the neck or throat of certain birds (such as chickens and turkeys) or lizards.

Examples

The doctor, with the air of a man whose professional interests have found scope, drew Alex's attention to those excellences which might otherwise have escaped him: the fine color in comb and wattles, the length and quality of neck and saddle hackles, the firm, wide spread of the toes, and a rare justness in the formation of the ear lappets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wattle - fleshy growth hanging from the head or neck of certain birds

Etymology

From Middle English *lap* (fold, flap) + diminutive suffix *-et*.
