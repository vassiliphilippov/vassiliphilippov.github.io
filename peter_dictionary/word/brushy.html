brushy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃi / (BrE)
(of land or vegetation) covered with or consisting of bushes or thickets.

Examples

His first glimpse of the ranch house across the brushy swells told him nothing.
Whoever was out there hiding in the brushy cover was besieging the Antler house and, having spotted his approach, was determined to drive him off before he could get into the fight.

Synset Examples

brushy undergrowth
`bosky' is a literary term
a bosky park leading to a modest yet majestic plaza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooded - covered with trees
  • Synonym: bushy - covered with or resembling a bush or bushes

Etymology

Formed from 'brush' (shrubbery or undergrowth) + '-y' (adjective suffix).
