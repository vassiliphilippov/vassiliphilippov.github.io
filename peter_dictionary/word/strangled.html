strangled

1. ADJ

/ ˈstræŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡəld / (BrE)
(of a sound or action) suppressed or produced with difficulty, as if physically restricted or choked.

Examples

The thin legs twitched convulsively once, then Kate felt the little body stiffening in her arms and heard one strangled sound.

Synset Examples

a smothered cough
a stifled yawn
a strangled scream
suppressed laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifled - (of a sound or action) suppressed or held back
  • Synonym: suppressed - (of a feeling or reaction) forcibly held in check
  • Antonym: unrestrained - (of a sound or action) not held back or suppressed

Etymology

From the verb 'strangle,' from Old French *estrangler*, from Latin *strangulare* (to strangle).
