upheaval

1. NOUN

/ ʌpˈhivəl / (AmE), / ʌpˈhiːvəl / (BrE)
a violent or sudden change or disruption to something, especially in society or politics

Examples

The storms of the past had died away, and the great upheaval which was to mark the following century had not yet begun to disturb men's minds.

Synset Examples

the industrial revolution was a period of great turbulence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and tranquility

Etymology

From up- + heave, originally meaning 'the action of heaving up'.
