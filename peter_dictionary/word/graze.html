graze

1. VERB

/ ɡreɪz / (AmE), / ɡreɪz / (BrE)
(of animals) to feed on growing grass or herbage, typically in a field or pasture.

Examples

Husbandry was bounded by snake-rail fences, and there were grazing cattle.
There was brush, and stands of pine that no grass could grow under, and places so steep that cattle would n't stop to graze.
Thousands of buffalo ("bison "they will never be to the man on the street) grazing like a mobile brown throw-rug upon the rolling, dusty-green grassland.

Synset Examples

the herd was grazing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: browse - (of animals) to feed on leaves, twigs, or other high-growing vegetation

Etymology

From Old French *greser*, from Latin *grasus* (fat), possibly via influence from Old English *grasian* (to feed on grass).
