ventilate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛn.t̬ɪ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to cause fresh air to enter and move around in (a room, building, etc.) in order to cool or freshen it

Examples

Person to Person ventilates the home lives of Johnny Mercer and Joan Collins - both in Southern California (Channel 5 at 10: 30).

Synset Examples

air the old winter clothes
air out the smoke-filled rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air - to expose to or fill with air; to ventilate

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilare', meaning 'to fan, agitate, or winnow'
