mercenary

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜr.səˌner.i / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sən.əri / (BrE)
a soldier who is hired to serve in a foreign army, often motivated chiefly by personal gain rather than loyalty to a cause or country.

Examples

The latter, members of two regiments of Swiss mercenaries transported by Great Britain to Canada to fight the Americans in the War of 1812, had settled in Montreal and Kingston at the close of the war in 1815.
The mercenaries were little interested in farming and added nothing to the output of the farm plots on which all work was still done with hoes as late as 1818.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier of fortune - person who fights for personal gain or adventure rather than allegiance to a country or cause
  • Antonym: volunteer - a person who willingly offers to take part in a task or service without pay or for a cause

Etymology

From Latin mercenarius (hired, serving for wages), from merces (wages, reward).
