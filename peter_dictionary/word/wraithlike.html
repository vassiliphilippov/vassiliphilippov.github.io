wraithlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪθˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈreɪθˌlaɪk / (BrE)
Resembling a wraith; thin, insubstantial, or ghostly in appearance or substance.

Examples

The warped, broken trees in the valley assumed wraith-like shapes.
The wraith-like events of the last few days flooded David's mind and he rubbed his unshaved chin and felt again the ache in his kidneys caused by his saddle odyssey from Boston.

Synset Examples

strange fancies of unreal and shadowy worlds
dim shadowy forms
a wraithlike column of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghostly - resembling or characteristic of a ghost
  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light in a way that seems not of this world
  • Antonym: substantial - having substance; solid or real

Etymology

From 'wraith' (a ghost or apparition) + '-like'. 'Wraith' is of Scottish origin.
