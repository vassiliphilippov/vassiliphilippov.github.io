grim

1. ADJ

/ ɡrɪm / (AmE), / ɡrɪm / (BrE)
Not easily appeased, softened, or stopped; resolute and unyielding.

Examples

Whatever the unhappy inmates of such camps are like today, Dostoevsky's companions were about as grim a lot as can be imagined.
However, when there's a job to be done, I 'm a monstrosity of grim determination, I like to think.

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - never-ceasing, unyielding
  • Synonym: stern - serious and unyielding
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy, lenient

Etymology

From Old English *grimm* (fierce, cruel, severe).

2. ADJ

/ ɡrɪm / (AmE), / ɡrɪm / (BrE)
(of humor or situations) having a harshly ironic, sinister, or sardonic quality, often about serious or unpleasant subjects.

Examples

Naval procedure, he thought, had its moments of grim humor.

Synset Examples

black humor
a grim joke
grim laughter
fun ranging from slapstick clowning ... to savage mordant wit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black - (of humor) making light of serious, distressing, or taboo subjects
  • Synonym: dark - (of humor) dealing with disturbing subjects in a satirical way
  • Synonym: mordant - (of humor) having a biting, caustic quality
