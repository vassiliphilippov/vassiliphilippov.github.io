acolyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈækəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈækəlaɪt / (BrE)
a person, typically a young person, who assists a priest or minister in religious ceremonies, especially in the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

He had been a choir boy at the Holy Name Cathedral and also served as an acolyte to Father O' Brien.

Etymology

From Greek akolouthos (follower, attendant).
