stupidity

1. NOUN

/ stuˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / stjuːˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unintelligent or showing a lack of good judgment or sense.

Examples

He refused to change his approach, and instead he attacked high and low - the officials for their not backing him, and subordinates for their laxness, stupidity, and stubbornness.
But stupidity was no consolation when it had rank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

From Late Latin stupiditas, from Latin stupidus (stupid).

2. NOUN

/ stuˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / stjuːˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
A foolish or senseless action or mistake.

Examples

Here was another human who understood the stupidity of quarreling with the inevitable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Synonym: folly - a foolish action, practice, or idea
  • Antonym: wisdom - the ability to make sensible decisions

Etymology

From Latin 'stupiditas' ('dullness, stupidity'), from 'stupidus' ('amazed, stunned').
