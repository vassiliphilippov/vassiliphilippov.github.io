modal

1. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dl̩ / (BrE)
Relating to or constituting the most frequent value in a statistical distribution.

Examples

It is convenient to classify a child's onset ages and completion ages as "advanced ", "moderate " (modal), or "delayed "according to whether the child's age equivalent "dots "appeared to the left of, upon, or to the right of the appropriate short transverse line.
Nevertheless, for most of the population of heterogeneous advanced societies, though less for the less religious portion, religion does perform certain modal individual and social functions.

Synset Examples

the modal age at which American novelists reach their peak is 30

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mode - the value that appears most frequently in a data set (statistics)

Etymology

From Latin *modus* (measure, manner). The statistical sense derives from 'mode', meaning the value that appears most frequently.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊdəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dəl / (BrE)
a verb, such as 'can', 'will', or 'should', used with another verb to express possibility, necessity, permission, or ability

Examples

Should I come over?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used in forming tenses, moods, voices, etc.

Etymology

From Latin 'modālis' (relating to a mode or manner).
