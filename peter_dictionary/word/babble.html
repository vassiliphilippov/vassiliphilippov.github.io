babble

1. VERB

/ ˈbæb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbab.əl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and foolishly, often without making much sense

Examples

The two women babbled and crooned at him.

Synset Examples

The two women babbled and crooned at the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: chatter - to talk rapidly about trivial matters
  • Degree Higher: rant - to speak or shout at length in a wild or impassioned way

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, reflecting the sound of incoherent speech.
