dispersion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɜrʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
The action or process of spreading things or people over a wide area, or of being spread in this way.

Examples

The spectrometer was adjusted to minimize the amount of dispersion mode mixed in with the absorption signal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentration - the action of bringing together or focusing into a single area

Etymology

From Latin dispersio (a scattering), from dispergere (to scatter).
