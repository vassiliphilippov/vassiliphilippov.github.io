ass

1. NOUN

/ æs / (AmE), / as / (BrE)
(informal) the fleshy part of the human body that you sit on; your buttocks.

Examples

Just about to get their asses kicked into hut Seven.
"I hit him in the ass once ".
If the acting captain wanted his acting lieutenant to sit on his ass around the station all night, Killpath would just have to go out and drag Gun back by the heels once an hour; because he 'd be damned if he was going to be a mid-watch pencil-pusher just to please his ulcerated pro-tem captain.
Watch out or I'’ll kick your Sicilian ass right into the street!

Synset Examples

he deserves a good kick in the butt
are you going to sit on your fanny and do nothing?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttocks - the fleshy part of the body you sit on
  • Synonym: butt - (slang) the human buttocks
  • Synonym: rear end - the back part of the human body; buttocks

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'assa' for 'donkey'; use for buttocks arose in American English by extension, late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ æs / (AmE), / æs / (BrE)
A stupid or foolish person, especially one who is pompous or self-important.

Examples

A Mississippi soldier wrote: "Our General Reub Davis is a vain, stuck-up, illiterate ass ".
I left it noisy, so it scares away any pain-in-the-ass innocent bystanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: genius - a person of exceptional intelligence

Etymology

From Old English *assa* (donkey); figurative use referring to foolishness is attested from the 16th century.
