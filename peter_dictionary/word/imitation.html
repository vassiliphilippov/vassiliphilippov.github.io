imitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A thing that is made to look like or resemble something else, especially something of higher value or quality; a copy or reproduction.

Examples

The sound may be good; but if you know The Real Thing, you know that what you are hearing is only a clever imitation.
Hand her a chair and she would say, "Why, it's a nice imitation of those Queen Anne chairs I inherited from Grandmother Delancy ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - a thing made to be similar or identical to another
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something, as opposed to an imitation or copy

Etymology

From Latin imitatio (a copying, imitation), from imitari (to imitate).
