uneconomical

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌikəˈnɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
not efficient or wasteful in the use of money, time, materials, or effort; not economical.

Examples

The fact seems to be that very many large branch stores are uneconomical, that the choice of location in the suburbs is as important as it was downtown, and that even highly suburbanized cities will support only so many big branches.
It will frequently be uneconomical for him to own the most up-to-date equipment.

Synset Examples

a clumsy and wasteful process
wasteful duplication of effort
uneconomical ebb and flow of power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: economical - efficient and avoiding waste or extravagance

Etymology

From un- (not) + economical.
