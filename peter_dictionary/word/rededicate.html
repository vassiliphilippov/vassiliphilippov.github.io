rededicate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈdɛdəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to commit or dedicate oneself or something again to a cause, purpose, or goal

Examples

Freedom depends upon them; Now, therefore, do i, John A. Notte, Jr., Governor of the State of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations, proclaim Saturday, May 20 th, 1961, as Armed Forces Day, reminding our citizens that we should rededicate ourselves to our Nation, respecting the uniforms as the guardians of our precious liberty.

Synset Examples

They were asked to rededicate themselves to their country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedicate - to devote to a purpose or set aside for a special use

Etymology

From re- (again) + dedicate, from Latin dedicare (to devote, consecrate).
