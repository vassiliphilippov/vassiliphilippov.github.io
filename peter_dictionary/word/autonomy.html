autonomy

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəmi / (BrE)
The right or condition of self-government, especially in a political context; independence from external control or influence.

Examples

We may thus trace the notion of individual autonomy from its manifestation in religious practice and theological reflection through practical politics and political theory into literature and the arts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - the condition of being free from outside control
  • Antonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else

Etymology

From Greek 'autonomia' (self-law, independence), from 'autos' (self) + 'nomos' (law).
