lawman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːmæn / (AmE), / ˈlɔːmæn / (BrE)
A law officer, such as a sheriff or marshal, especially one in the American West or in historical settings.

Examples

Hogan gripped the lawman's other arm.
But he brought back the sheriff and several deputies, and to the lawmen the entire affair seemed a repetition of the Lewis killing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheriff - an elected law officer in a county or town
  • Synonym: marshal - a law enforcement officer, especially in the American West
  • Antonym: outlaw - a person who has broken the law and avoids authorities

Etymology

Compound of 'law' + 'man'.
