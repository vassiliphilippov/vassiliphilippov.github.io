cocktail

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑk.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.teɪl / (BrE)
A drink made by mixing different liquors, or liquor with other ingredients such as fruit juice or cream, typically served chilled and often before a meal.

Examples

Champagne cocktails.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mocktail - a non-alcoholic mixed drink resembling a cocktail

Etymology

Probably from the combination of 'cock' and 'tail'; first used in early 19th-century America to describe mixed alcoholic drinks.
