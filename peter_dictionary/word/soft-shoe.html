soft-shoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔft ˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌʃuː / (BrE)
A style of tap dance performed in soft-soled shoes, characterized by smooth, graceful movements and often done without hard tapping noises.

Examples

One of the finest soft shoe tunes ever invented, "Once in Love with Amy "is also, of course, one of the most tantalizingly persistent of light love lyrics to come out of American musical comedy in our era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dance - dance style characterized by rhythmical tapping of the toes and heels
