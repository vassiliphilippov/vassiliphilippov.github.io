gaudy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɔdi / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdi / (BrE)
Unattractively bright or showy in a tasteless or overly elaborate way.

Examples

Dangling his gaudy trinkets before them, he told of the Rebel losses in the mountains, at Cheat and Rich mountains both, and the Federal march on Beverly.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garish - Unpleasantly bright or showy; gaudy
  • Synonym: flashy - Bright and ostentatious, tastelessly showy
  • Antonym: understated - Not showy or ostentatious; subtle

Etymology

From Middle English 'gaud' (ornament, trick), from Old French 'gaudir' (to rejoice).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɔdi / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːdi / (BrE)
(Of clothes, decoration, or style) extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless.

Examples

At 7: 25 two hotel doormen came thumping down the steps, carrying a saw-horse to be set up as a barricade in front of the haberdashery store window next to the entranceway, and as I watched them in their gaudy red coats that nearly scraped the ground, their golden, fringed epaulets and spic, red visored caps, I suddenly saw just over their shoulders Jessica gracefully making her way through the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - Bright and ostentatious in appearance
  • Synonym: garish - Unpleasantly bright or showy
  • Antonym: subdued - Not showy or bright; restrained

Etymology

Probably from Latin 'gaudium' (joy) via Middle English.
