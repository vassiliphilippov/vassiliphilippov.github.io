scab

1. VERB

/ skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
(of a wound or area of skin) to form a scab as part of the healing process.

Examples

Its ribs showed, it was a yellow nondescript color, it suffered from a variety of sores, hair had scabbed off its body in patches.

Synset Examples

the wounds will eventually scab

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heal - to become healthy or well again; to mend
