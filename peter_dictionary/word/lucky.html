lucky

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌki / (AmE), / ˈlʌki / (BrE)
Having good things happen by chance; having or resulting from good luck.

Examples

The American is very lucky if he has three.
I know that I myself felt that it was a mortal shame for a man to be torn open by a British musket ball, as Isaac had been, yet I also felt relieved and lucky that it had been him and not myself.
"I'm lucky that you were still living in Lublin ".
"You lucky kids ", he said.
Those persons who were lucky enough to see and hear the performance of his work at the Brest-Silevniov Festival in August, 1916, will certainly welcome his return to public notice; and it is not unlikely that, even as the great Bach lay dormant for so many years, so has the erudite, ingenious SalFininistas passed through his "purgatory "of neglect.

Synset Examples

a lucky escape
a lucky guess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunate - receiving good things by chance rather than through merit; synonym for 'lucky'
  • Antonym: unlucky - having bad things happen by chance; antonym of 'lucky'

Etymology

From luck + -y, first recorded in the 15th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌki / (AmE), / ˈlʌki / (BrE)
Having or bringing good fortune; resulting from or resulting in chance events that are favorable.

Examples

We collected "lucky stones "- all the creamy translucent pebbles, worn smooth and round, that we could find in the driveway.
You'’re very lucky.

Synset Examples

my lucky day
a lucky man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlucky - not having or bringing good fortune
  • Synonym: fortunate - having good fortune; favored by luck or circumstances
  • Degree Lower: fortunate - having or indicating good luck

Etymology

From luck + -y; 'luck' from Middle Dutch 'luc', and '-y' forming adjectives.
