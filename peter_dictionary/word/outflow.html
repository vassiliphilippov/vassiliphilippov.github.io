outflow

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fləʊ / (BrE)
the movement or discharge of a fluid or other substance from a container, opening, or area

Examples

The heart weighed 510 gm., and at the outflow tracts the left and right ventricles measured 19 and 3 mm., respectively.

Synset Examples

they tried to stop the escape of gas from the damaged pipe
he had to clean up the leak

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflow - the movement or entry of a fluid or substance into a container or area

Etymology

From out- + flow, patterned after in- + flow (inflow).
