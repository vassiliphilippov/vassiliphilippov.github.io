pathology

1. NOUN

/ pəˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of medical science concerned with the causes, nature, and effects of diseases.

Examples

Within certain wide limits anatomy dictates function and, if one is permitted to speculate, potential pathology should be included in this statement as well.
In conclusion, these findings suggest the need for a comparative physiology, pathology, and histology of mammalian lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: histopathology - study of diseased tissue at the microscopic level

Etymology

From Greek pathologia: pathos (suffering, disease) + -logia (study).
