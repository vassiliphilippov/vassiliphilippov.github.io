sweat

1. NOUN

/ swɛt / (AmE), / swɛt / (BrE)
The salty fluid produced by sweat glands, which emerges through the pores in the skin, especially as a response to heat, exercise, or stress.

Examples

We ran east for about half a mile before we turned back to the road, panting from the effort and soaked with sweat.
Grabski sat in a sweat saturated undershirt, cursing the excessive heat which clamped an uneasy stillness before sundown.
The sweat broke out on Mr. Skyros' forehead as he realized he had been actually thinking - hoping - planning - perhaps -
His face took on a sudden pallor, became beaded with sweat, and he seemed to have trouble with his breathing.
They brought to it all the odors that clung to men like themselves, that of their own sweat, of campfire smoke, of horses and cattle.

Synset Examples

sweat poured off his brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspiration - the process or act of sweating; also the moisture produced

Etymology

From Old English swāt (perspiration), related to Proto-Germanic *swaita- (to sweat).

2. VERB

/ swɛt / (AmE), / swɛt / (BrE)
To excrete perspiration through the pores of the skin, usually as a response to heat, exercise, stress, or illness.

Examples

With great difficulty John clambered to his feet and started to run, sweat pouring down his face.
And this handsome booby, staring and sweating, was he her bridegroom?
She had talked to him right there, with the hot sun in his face, which made him sweat and feel ashamed.
At the corroborees, when they get to dancing and sweating, you 'll see them rubbing up against a man who's supposed to have a specially good smell.
Brandy will sweat it out.

Synset Examples

Exercise makes one sweat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspire - to emit sweat through the pores of the skin

Etymology

From Old English *swātian* (to sweat) from *swāt* (sweat).

3. NOUN

/ swɛt / (AmE), / swɛt / (BrE)
Physical or mental effort; hard work, especially when strenuous.

Examples

Why should we give him the money we sweated for?

Synset Examples

he got an A for effort
they managed only with great exertion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - the use of physical or mental energy; hard work
  • Synonym: exertion - vigorous physical or mental effort

Etymology

From Old English *swāt* (perspiration), related to German *Schweiss*, from Proto-Germanic *swaitaną*.
