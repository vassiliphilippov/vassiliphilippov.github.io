preoccupation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being absorbed in or engrossed by something, often to the exclusion of other things

Examples

But here we have a distinctly modern preoccupation; we want to know why that book has kept on selling the way it has; we want to know what is perennially new about Utopia.
I guess he had a lot on his mind, trying to adapt to life on the inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - deep mental involvement or focus
  • Antonym: distraction - something that pulls attention away; opposite of focused engagement

Etymology

From Latin praeoccupatio (anticipation), from prae- (before) + occupare (to seize).

2. NOUN

/ priˌɑkjəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / priːˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
A subject or idea that dominates a person's thoughts or attention.

Examples

That is why the form itself becomes a preoccupation, because it exists as a problem separate from the material it accommodates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsession - an idea or thought that continually preoccupies the mind
  • Antonym: distraction - the process of being diverted from something else or the state of being distracted

Etymology

From Latin 'præoccupatio' (anticipation), from 'præoccupare' (to take possession beforehand).
