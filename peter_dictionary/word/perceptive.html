perceptive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
Relating to perception or the ability to perceive things; of or pertaining to the process of perceiving.

Examples

Hereby, the external object viewed by the eyes remains the thing that is seen, not the retinal image, the purpose of which would be to achieve perceptive cooperation by stirring sympathetic impulses in the other sensory centers, motor tensions, associated word symbols, and consciousness.

Synset Examples

perceptive faculties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptual - relating to the process of perceiving

Etymology

From Latin 'perceptivus', from 'percipere' (to perceive).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
having or showing sensitive insight or keen understanding; able to notice and interpret things quickly and accurately

Examples

A perceptive journalist, Sam Lubell, has phrased it in the title of one of his books as The Revolt Of The Moderates.

Synset Examples

a perceptive eye
a perceptive observation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insightful - having or showing clear understanding and perception
  • Synonym: observant - quick to notice things; attentive
  • Antonym: unperceptive - lacking insight or the ability to notice things clearly

Etymology

From Latin 'perceptivus' (able to perceive), from 'percipere' (to perceive).
