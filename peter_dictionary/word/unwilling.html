unwilling

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
Not willing or inclined to do something; reluctant.

Examples

He was unwilling to rely on Griffith's carrying his message, and he had no confidence the police would act on it.

Synset Examples

an unwilling assistant
unwilling to face facts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctant - unwilling and hesitant; disinclined
  • Antonym: willing - ready, eager, or prepared to do something

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'willing' (disposed, ready).
