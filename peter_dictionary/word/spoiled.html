spoiled

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a child) having a character or disposition that is harmed by being excessively pampered or indulged.

Examples

You skinny, spoiled brat.

Synset Examples

a spoiled child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulged - given anything they want, often excessively
  • Antonym: disciplined - having good control or restraint due to proper upbringing

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'spoil', from Old French 'espoillier', from Latin 'spoliare' (to strip, plunder).
