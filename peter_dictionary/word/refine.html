refine

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to improve or perfect something, especially by making small changes to remove faults or enhance quality

Examples

The severe yet harmonious vases of the previous fifty years, the Strong Geometric style of the late ninth century, display as firm a mastery of the principles underlying Geometric pottery; but artists now were ready to refine and elaborate their inheritance.
"In order to further refine the management of passenger vehicles, on July 1, 1958, the actual title to every vehicle was transferred, by Executive Order, to the Division of Methods, Research and Office Services.

Synset Examples

refine one's style of writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make something smooth or improve it by rubbing or careful attention
  • Synonym: improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: coarsen - to make or become rough or less delicate

Etymology

From French 'raffiner', from Old French 'refiner', from Latin 'refinare', from 're-' (again) + 'finire' (to finish).

2. VERB

/ rəˈfaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
To make an idea, method, system, or pattern more complex, intricate, or sophisticated; to add detail or improvement by making small changes.

Examples

It was the conclusion of the first phase of a process of tragic recollection, and of refining the recollection, that will last as long as there are Jews.

Synset Examples

refine a design or pattern

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simplify - make simpler or easier to understand

Etymology

From French *raffiner*, from Old French *refinir*, from Latin *refinire* ('to purify again').
