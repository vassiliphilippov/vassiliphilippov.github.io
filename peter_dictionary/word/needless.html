needless

1. ADJ

/ ˈniːdləs / (AmE), / ˈniːdləs / (BrE)
Not necessary or required; unwarranted.

Examples

Needless to say, the organic load was very low on a volumetric basis, but was 270 lb BOD / day / acre on a surface loading basis.
The effect, needless to say, is almost terrifying, and though at times a bit obscure, the film is certainly a much needed catharsis for the "repressed "movie-goer.

Synset Examples

a strikers' tent camp...was burned with needless loss of life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: necessary - required to be done, needed
  • Synonym: superfluous - unnecessary, especially through being more than enough

Etymology

From Old English nēdleas, from nēd (need) + -less (without).
