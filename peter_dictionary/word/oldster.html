oldster

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldstər / (AmE), / ˈəʊldstə / (BrE)
an elderly person, especially one who is noticeably old

Examples

Authorities say that oldsters are a prime target.
Prohibition was the law of the land, but it was unpopular (how many of us oldsters took up drinking in prohibition days, drinking was so gay, so fashionable, especially in the sophisticated Northeast!)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senior citizen - an elderly person, especially in relation to government benefits
  • Antonym: youngster - a young person, especially a child or youth

Etymology

Formed from 'old' + suffix '-ster' (denoting a person).
