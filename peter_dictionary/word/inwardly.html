inwardly

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnwərdli / (AmE), / ˈɪnwədli / (BrE)
In a way that is felt or thought internally, rather than shown openly; with respect to private thoughts or feelings.

Examples

She knew she was feeling afraid and inwardly laughed at herself.

Synset Examples

inwardly, she was raging

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outwardly - in a way that is shown or expressed openly

Etymology

From 'inward', from Old English 'inweard', meaning 'interior, internal', plus '-ly'.
