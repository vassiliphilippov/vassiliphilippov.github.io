bedground

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛd.ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbɛd.ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area of ground where a herd of cattle or sheep spends the night resting or sleeping

Examples

The herd was watered and then thrown onto a broad grass flat which was to be the first night's bedground.

Etymology

From 'bed' + 'ground', indicating ground used as a bed for animals.
