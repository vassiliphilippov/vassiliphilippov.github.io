distrust

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈtrʌst / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrʌst / (BrE)
to regard someone or something as untrustworthy; to have no confidence in or suspicion towards

Examples

When you disliked or distrusted a man, you should have a reason.
But Torrio and Capone had graver cause to hate and distrust the Irishman.
Some interfaith tensions are not occasioned by theological differences at all, but by the need of men to have persons they can blame, distrust, denounce, and even hate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trust - to believe in the reliability or truth of someone or something
  • Synonym: mistrust - to have doubts or suspicions about someone or something

Etymology

From Middle English, from dis- (expressing reversal) + trust.
