dispossessed

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪspəˈzɛst / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɛst / (BrE)
Deprived of home, land, or possessions, especially as a result of being forced to leave or having them taken away.

Examples

Secondly, to find a learned diversion and a pleasing joke in More's account of the stupid brutalities of early sixteenth century wars, of the anguish of the poor and dispossessed, of the insolence and cruelty of the rich and powerful requires a callousness toward suffering and sin that would be surprising in a moral imbecile and most surprising in More himself.

Synset Examples

made a living out of shepherding dispossed people from one country to another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeless - without a home or permanent place to live
  • Synonym: impoverished - lacking money or material possessions

Etymology

Past participle of 'dispossess', from Old French 'despossesser', from Latin 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'possidere' (to possess).
