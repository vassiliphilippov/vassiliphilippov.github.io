school

1. NOUN

/ skuːl / (AmE), / skuːl / (BrE)
A place where children or students are taught, usually a building where education is provided over a period of time.

Examples

The TEA estimated there would be 182 scholastics to attend the day school in Dallas County, saving them from coming to Austin to live in the state deaf school.

Principals of the 13 schools in the Denton Independent School District have been re-elected for the 1961 - 62 session upon the recommendation of Supt. Chester O. Strickland.

Synset Examples

the school was founded in 1900

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - a place of study or training in a special field
  • Synonym: college - a higher education institution
  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher learning

Etymology

From Old English 'scol', from Latin 'schola', from Greek 'skholē' (leisure, then place of learning).

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: educate - to give someone information or training in a particular subject or skill
  • Synonym: train - to teach a person or animal the skills for a particular activity

Etymology

From Old English *scol* (school), from Latin *schola* (school, lecture, discussion), from Greek *skholē* (leisure, that in which leisure is employed, learned discussion); the verb sense derives from the noun.
