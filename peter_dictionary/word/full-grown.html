full-grown

1. ADJ

/ fʊl ˈɡroʊn / (AmE), / fʊl ˈɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) having reached full development; mature.

Examples

The larvae, kept warm by the queen, are full grown in about ten days.
His Mary would be slender of limb and delicate of proportion, yet she must hold this full-grown man as securely and convincingly as she would a child.

Synset Examples

an adult animal
a grown woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immature - not fully developed or mature
  • Antonym: juvenile - young or not yet fully developed
  • Synonym: adult - fully developed and mature (often of people or animals)

Etymology

Formed from 'full' (completely) + 'grown' (past participle of 'grow').
