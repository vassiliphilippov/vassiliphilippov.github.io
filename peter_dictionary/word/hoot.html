hoot

1. VERB

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh shout or cry, often expressing excitement, derision, or encouragement

Examples

A train hooted.
Above, in the tiled prosceniums of the alcoves, boys sing the ghazals of Hafiz and Saadi, while at the very bottom, in the vaults, the toughs and blades of the city hoot and bang their drums, drink arak, play dice, and dance.

Synset Examples

the toughs and blades of the city hoot and bang their drums, drink arak, play dice, and dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - to shout or laugh at someone in a mocking or scornful way
  • Antonym: cheer - to shout to show approval or encouragement

Etymology

Of imitative origin, echoing the loud call made by some birds and later used of human shouts.

2. NOUN

/ huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
a loud, harsh cry or call, especially that of an owl; also, a shout or loud sound expressing amusement or derision

Examples

Dimly, he heard laughter, hoots of derision, but he could not read the racket properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - a rude and mocking remark, often shouted
  • Synonym: boo - a sound made to show disapproval or contempt
  • Antonym: cheer - a shout of encouragement or approval

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by owls and similar cries; first recorded in Middle English.
