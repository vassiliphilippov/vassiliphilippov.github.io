mystify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪstəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
To make someone feel confused or unable to understand something; to bewilder or puzzle.

Examples

One day Maeterlinck, coming with a friend upon an event which he recognized as the exact pattern of a previous dream, detailed the ensuing occurrences in advance so accurately that his companion was completely mystified.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me--I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perplex - to cause someone to feel completely baffled
  • Synonym: baffle - to totally confuse or bewilder someone
  • Antonym: enlighten - to make someone understand something clearly

Etymology

From French 'mystifier', from 'mystère' (mystery).
