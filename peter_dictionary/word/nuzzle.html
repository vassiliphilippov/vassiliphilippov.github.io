nuzzle

1. VERB

/ ˈnʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈnʌzəl / (BrE)
to gently rub or push one's nose or face against (someone or something), especially to show affection, or to settle into a comfortable, cozy position

Examples

She picked up the baby and nuzzled her fat warm little neck.

Synset Examples

We cuddled against each other to keep warm
The children snuggled into their sleeping bags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuddle - to hold close in one's arms as a way of showing love or affection
  • Synonym: snuggle - to settle or move into a warm, comfortable, and cozy position

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, frequentative of Old English 'nosel' (to nose).
