anaerobic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈroʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌanɪəˈrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism or process) living, occurring, or active in the absence of free oxygen.

Examples

The anaerobic conditions in the existing oxidation pond necessitated examination of other methods for supplying additional oxygen than by sodium nitrate.

Synset Examples

anaerobic bacteria

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aerobic - living or occurring only in the presence of oxygen

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'aēr' (air) + '-bios' (life).
