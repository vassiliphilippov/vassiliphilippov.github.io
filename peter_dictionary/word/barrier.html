barrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæriə / (BrE)
A circumstance or condition that makes progress or achievement difficult.

Examples

Despite countless barriers and disappointments, Shea moved forward.
Perhaps, the psychological barriers that had prevented her from conceiving Hal's child would no longer be present.
It is at least as important as the more dramatic attempts to break down barriers of inequality in the South.

Synset Examples

intolerance is a barrier to understanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes it difficult
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance or delay
  • Antonym: aid - something that helps progress or achievement
  • Antonym: facilitator - something that makes an action or process easier

Etymology

From Old French *barriere*, from Vulgar Latin *barrāria* (from *barra*, bar).
