inspiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a sudden good idea or creative thought, especially as the result of imaginative thinking

Examples

In all his miscalculations, Stravinsky made the fatal historical blunder of presuming that he could transform other composers' inspirations - representing many peoples, time periods and styles - into his own music by warping the harmony, melody, or form, to verify his own experiments.

Synset Examples

he had little respect for the inspirations of other artists
after years of work his brainchild was a tangible reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainchild - an idea or invention regarded as the result of one's creative work

Etymology

From Latin inspiratio (a breathing in, inspiration), from inspirare (to breathe into).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A sudden feeling or idea that encourages creativity or stimulates unusual activity in the mind.

Examples

The notion of "inspiration "is somehow cognate to this feeling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creativity - the use of imagination to create something original
  • Synonym: motivation - the reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving in a particular way
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin inspiratio (the act of breathing into or inspiring).
