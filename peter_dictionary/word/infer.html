infer

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɜr / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜː / (BrE)
to form an opinion or reach a conclusion by reasoning from specific instances to a general statement

Examples

If the mechanics are similar, we can also infer that the thermodynamics will also be similar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deduce - to reach a logical conclusion from general principles or evidence

Etymology

From Latin inferre (to bring in, deduce), from in- (in) + ferre (to carry).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɝ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜː / (BrE)
To reach a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning rather than from explicit statements.

Examples

The possibility that the absence or presence of these shunts is species dependent is therefore inferred.
Dice, for example, he inferred from black dots on a white surface.
He inferred that Hohlbein and Garth were high-priced lawyers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deduce - to reach a logical conclusion based on facts or reasoning
  • Antonym: imply - to strongly suggest the truth or existence of something not expressly stated (antonym in terms of direction)

Etymology

From Latin 'inferre', meaning 'to bring in' or 'to deduce'.
