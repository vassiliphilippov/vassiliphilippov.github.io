wide-screen

1. ADJ

/ waɪd skrin / (AmE), / waɪd skriːn / (BrE)
(of a film or television image) having a width much greater than its height, especially as projected or displayed in cinemas or on televisions.

Examples

It is truly odd and ironic that the most handsome and impressive film yet made from Miguel de Cervantes' "Don Quixote "is the brilliant Russian spectacle, done in wide screen and color, which opened yesterday at the Fifty-fifth Street and Sixty-eighth Street Playhouses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard-screen - having a more traditional or less wide aspect ratio, opposed to wide-screen
