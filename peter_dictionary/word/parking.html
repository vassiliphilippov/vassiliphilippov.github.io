parking

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
The action or process of moving a vehicle into a place where it can be left temporarily.

Examples

Raymond Vernon reports that residents of East St. Louis have been driving across the Mississippi, through the heart of downtown St. Louis and out to the western suburbs for major shopping, simply because parking is easier at the big branches than it is in the heart of town.
To the extent that the problem is merely parking, an aggressive downtown management, like that of Lazarus Brothers in Columbus, Ohio, can fight back successfully by building a garage on the lot next door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: park - to bring a vehicle to a stop and leave it temporarily

Etymology

From the verb 'park' (to leave a vehicle temporarily) plus the -ing suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
an area or space where vehicles may be parked

Examples

If you use parking attendants, can they be replaced by automatic parking gates?
If you use parking attendants, can they be replaced by automatic parking gates?

Synset Examples

there is plenty of parking behind the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: car park - an area or building where cars can be parked
  • Synonym: parking lot - an open area where cars may be parked; chiefly North American

Etymology

From the verb 'park' (to bring a vehicle to a halt and leave it temporarily), with -ing forming a noun.
