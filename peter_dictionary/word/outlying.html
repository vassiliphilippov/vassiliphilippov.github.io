outlying

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌlaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌlaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
Situated far from the center or main part; remote from the main area.

Examples

He came to a stretch of old orange groves, the trees dead, some of them uprooted, and then there was an outlying shopping area, and tract houses.

Synset Examples

outlying settlements

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central - located at, or forming, the center

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (outside) and 'lying' (present participle of 'lie').
