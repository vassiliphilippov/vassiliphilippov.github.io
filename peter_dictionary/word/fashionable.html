fashionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæʃənəbl / (AmE), / ˈfæʃnəbl / (BrE)
following or influenced by the latest styles or trends in society; popular or approved of at a particular time.

Examples

But since the gourmet insisted that it is done that way at the most fashionable dinners, the girl reluctantly agreed.
Prohibition was the law of the land, but it was unpopular (how many of us oldsters took up drinking in prohibition days, drinking was so gay, so fashionable, especially in the sophisticated Northeast!)
It would be interesting to know how much "integration "there is in the famous, fashionable colleges and prep schools of New England.

Synset Examples

fashionable clothing
the fashionable side of town
a fashionable cafe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trendy - very fashionable or up to date
  • Synonym: stylish - having a fashionable or elegant style
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not following or influenced by current trends or style

Etymology

From fashion + -able, first recorded in English in the 16th century.
