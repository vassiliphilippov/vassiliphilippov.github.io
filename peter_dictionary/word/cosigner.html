cosigner

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / kəʊˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
A person who signs a document together with one or more others, often to share responsibility or guarantee obligations.

Examples

Fifty-three of the 150 representatives immediately joined Grover as co-signers of the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signatory - a person or party that signs a document, especially a treaty or agreement

Etymology

From co- (together) + signer, meaning one who signs.
