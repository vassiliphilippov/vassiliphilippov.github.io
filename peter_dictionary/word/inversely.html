inversely

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈvɜrsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːsli / (BrE)
In a manner such that one quantity increases while another decreases, or vice versa; in an inverse or contrary manner.

Etymology

From 'inverse' + '-ly', following the pattern of adverbs, from Latin 'inversus' (turned upside down).
