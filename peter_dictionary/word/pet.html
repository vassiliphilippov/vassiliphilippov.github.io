pet

1. VERB

/ pɛt / (AmE), / pɛt / (BrE)
To stroke or caress gently, especially an animal, as a sign of affection.

Examples

He thought a moment, then inquired, "You mean petted "?

Synset Examples

pet the lamb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - to move one's hand with gentle pressure repeatedly over a surface, especially fur or hair
  • Synonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently in a loving or affectionate manner

2. NOUN

/ pɛt / (AmE), / pɛt / (BrE)
An animal that is kept in the home for companionship or pleasure, rather than for utility or food.

Examples

Done in the modern style of a "confession ", Fink tells in exquisite detail how he came to know, and, more important, love his mother's pet salamander, Alicia.
The Prince visited the hospital of Operation Brotherhood, supported by the Junior Chamber of Commerce of the Philippines, and fed rice to two pet elephants he kept at his residence at Pak Song.
It's a wonderful pet to have.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion animal - an animal kept primarily for a person's company

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *pet* (a favorite), of unknown origin; possibly from French *petit* (small), as a term of endearment.
