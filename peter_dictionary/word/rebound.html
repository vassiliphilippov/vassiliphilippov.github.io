rebound

1. VERB

/ rɪˈbaʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of an object) to spring back or move quickly away from a surface after hitting it.

Examples

It can only rebound to Mr. Hughes' discredit ".

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French rebonder (to bounce back), from re- (again) + bondir (to leap).
