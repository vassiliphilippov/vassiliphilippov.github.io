Newfoundland

1. NOUN

/ ˈnjuːfənˌland, ˈnuːfənˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfənˌlænd / (BrE)
A very large, powerful breed of dog with a thick, black or brown coat, known for intelligence and excellent swimming ability.

Examples

A Newfoundland sat solemnly beside a doghouse half his size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Bernard - large breed of working dog originally from Switzerland, known for rescue work

Etymology

Named after Newfoundland, the Canadian island where the breed originated.
