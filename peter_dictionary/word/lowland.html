lowland

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.lənd / (BrE)
An area of land that is at or near sea level and typically lower than surrounding regions.

Examples

Also, we should not even to-day discount the fact that a region such as the coastal lowlands centering on Charleston had closer ties with England and the West Indies than with the North even after independence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upland - an area of high or hilly land

Etymology

From 'low' + 'land', meaning land at a low elevation.
