insouciance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsu.si.əns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsuː.si.əns / (BrE)
A relaxed and unconcerned attitude; a cheerful lack of worry or anxiety.

Examples

Southern Liberals (there are a good many) - especially if they 're rich - often exhibit blithe insouciance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonchalance - a casual lack of concern or indifference

Etymology

From French *insouciance*, from 'in-' (not) + 'souci' (care, worry).
