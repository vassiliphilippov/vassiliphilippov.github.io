racial

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəl / (BrE)
relating to, or characteristic of, race or races, especially with reference to genetically distinguished groups of people

Examples

State and federal legislation against racial discrimination in employment was called for yesterday in a report of a "blue ribbon "citizens committee on the aid to dependent children program.
The report called racial discrimination in employment "one of the most serious causes of family breakdown, desertion, and ADC dependency ".
Among Bourbons the racial issue may have less to do with their remaining unreconstructed than other factors.
But apart from racial problems, the old unreconstructed South - to use the moderate words favored by Mr. Thomas Griffith - finds itself unsympathetic to most of what is different about the civilization of the North.
it might be pointed out that the integrating function of religion, for good or ill, has often supported or been identified with other groupings - political, nationality, language, class, racial, sociability, even economic.

Synset Examples

racial groups

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethnic - relating to a population subgroup with a common national or cultural tradition

Etymology

From French *racial*, from Latin *ratio* (reason, kind, type) via Italian *razza* (race).

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of one or more races or ethnic groups, or arising from differences between them.

Examples

And it is precisely in this poorer economic class that one finds, and has always found, the most racial friction.

Synset Examples

racial differences
racial discrimination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethnic - relating to a population subgroup with a common national or cultural tradition
  • Synonym: racialist - believing that differences between races determine cultural or individual achievement
  • Antonym: nonracial - not relating to or based on race

Etymology

From race + -al, where 'race' is from French 'race' (people of common descent).
