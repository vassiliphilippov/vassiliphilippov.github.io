niche

1. NOUN

/ nɪtʃ / (AmE), / niːʃ / (BrE)
A shallow recess, especially one in a wall for placing a statue or ornament.

Examples

John closed his eyes and saw once again the little niche in his mother's bedroom, where she had knelt to tell the good Virgin of her needs.

Etymology

From French 'niche', from Italian 'nicchia' (nook, recess).

2. NOUN

/ nɪtʃ, niːʃ / (AmE), / nɪʃ, niːʃ / (BrE)
A shallow recess, especially one in a wall to display a statue or other ornament, or simply an enclosure that is set back or indented.

Examples

A couple of years back, I occupied a Village apartment whose outer staircase contained the type of niche called a "coffin turn ".

Etymology

From French *niche* (recess in a wall), probably from Italian *nicchia* (niche, corner).

3. NOUN

/ nɪtʃ / (AmE), / niːʃ / (BrE)
a position or role that is especially suitable for someone or something; a place where a person or thing fits best

Examples

By dealing with common landscape in an uncommon way, Roy Mason has found a particular niche in American landscape art.

Synset Examples

he found his niche in the academic world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: role - function or position assigned or expected
  • Synonym: position - status or place in a group or system

Etymology

From French 'niche' (recess), from Italian 'nicchia' (nook).
