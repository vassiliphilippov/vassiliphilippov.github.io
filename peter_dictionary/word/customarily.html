customarily

1. ADV

/ ˌkʌs.təˈmer.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌkʌs.təˈmɛə.rɪ.li / (BrE)
In a way that is usual, traditional, or expected according to custom.

Examples

Then, having wrapped the lot in an afghan my dog customarily slept on, you lammed out the front door, considerately leaving it open for neighbors to discover.
All four types of message listed in Table 1 are permitted, although decorum and cocktail tradition require holding the commands to a minimum, while exclamations having complimentary intonations are more than customarily encouraged.

Synset Examples

children are custosby-the-waymarily expected to be seen but not heard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usually - under normal conditions; most of the time
  • Synonym: habitually - in a way that is habitual; by habit
  • Antonym: unusually - not common; not according to custom or expectation

Etymology

From 'customary' + '-ily', ultimately from Latin 'consuetudinarius', meaning habitual.
