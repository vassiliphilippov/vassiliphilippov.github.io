deficit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfəsɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
An amount by which something, especially money or resources, falls short of what is required or expected.

Examples

Daniel termed "extremely conservative "his estimate that it would produce 17 million dollars to help erase an anticipated deficit of 63 million dollars at the end of the current fiscal year next Aug. 31.

Synset Examples

new blood vessels bud out from the already dilated vascular bed to make up the nutritional deficit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed or expected

Etymology

From French *déficit* or Latin *deficit* (it is lacking).
