gambling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of risking money or valuables on the outcome of a game or event, in the hope of winning more money or a prize.

Examples

Undoubtedly, there was a good deal of gambling in both.
Tenements, stores, saloons, some gambling, I hope not too much.
Since then he had worked at this and that, though some said his main interest was gambling.
He wondered whether Needham was going to swear off gambling and get a steady job or whether he was counting on the income from Betty's estate to subsidize him.
We have here, finally, a witness that will further testify to Michael Corleone'’s rule over a criminal empire that controls all gambling in this country and perhaps in other countries.

Synset Examples

his gambling cost him a fortune
there was heavy play at the blackjack table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betting - the act of risking money on an event with an uncertain outcome
  • Synonym: gaming - the act of playing games of chance for money

Etymology

From the verb 'gamble', likely of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English ‘gammlen’ (to play, sport) with frequentative suffix -le.
