fantasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
The faculty or activity of imagining things, especially things that are impossible or unlikely; imagination unrestricted by reality.

Examples

It is not through space nor time that the time machine most approved by science fiction must travel for a visit to the permanent prehistoric past, or the ever existent past fantasy future.
Like Eliot, in my fantasies, I had a proud bearing and, with a skill that was vaguely continental, I would lead Jessica through an evening of dancing and handsome descriptions of my newest exploits, would guide her gently to the night's climax which, in my dreams, was always represented by our almost suffocating one another to death with deep, moist kisses burning with love.
The weeks went by, and the longer our separation grew, the more unbounded and almost unbearable my fantasies became.
If you indulge this fantasy, that's your business.

Synset Examples

a schoolgirl fantasy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the ability to form ideas or images in the mind, especially of things never seen or experienced
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (appearance, imagination), via Latin and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
A genre of fiction characterized by imaginative or supernatural elements, often including magical worlds or characters.

Examples

In a series of fairy tales and fantasies, Melies demonstrated that the film is superbly equipped to tell a straightforward story, with beginning, middle and end, complications, resolutions, climaxes, and conclusions.

Synset Examples

she made a lot of money writing romantic fantasies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: science fiction - fiction based on imagined future scientific advances or major social/environmental changes
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist, as opposed to imagination

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (imagination, appearance), via Latin and Old French.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
A belief or idea accepted by many people that is false or unrealistic.

Examples

The sound discoveries of this quixotic genius were so diluted by those of fantasy that the prize was never awarded to him.

Synset Examples

they have the illusion that I am very wealthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a deceptive appearance or impression; something that is not as it seems
  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief, often held despite evidence to the contrary

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (appearance, imagination) via Old French fantasie.
