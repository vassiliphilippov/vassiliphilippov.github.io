links

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋks / (AmE), / lɪŋks / (BrE)
A golf course located on sandy, coastal ground, typically with natural terrain and few trees.

Examples

Then he played nine imaginary holes of golf, choosing his handicap, his irons, his stance, his opponents, and his weather in detail, but the green of the links seemed faded in the light of his business worries.

Etymology

From Scottish English 'links', originally referring to sandy, undulating ground near the sea, from Old English 'hlinc' (rising ground, ridge).
