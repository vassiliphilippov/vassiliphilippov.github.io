blatant

1. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈbleɪtənt / (BrE)
(of bad behaviour) done openly and unashamedly; completely obvious and lacking in any attempt to hide.

Examples

He tried to ignore what his own common sense told him, but it was n't possible; her motives were too blatant.
For less than a dozen miles from the unplowed land of the dead man lived another settler who had ignored the warnings that his existence might be foreclosed on - a blatant and defiant rustler named Fred Powell.

Synset Examples

blatant disregard of the law
a blatant appeal to vanity
a blazing indiscretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagrant - (of an action) conspicuously offensive or obviously wrong
  • Antonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed

Etymology

First used in the late 16th century, perhaps from Latin 'blatire' (to babble).
