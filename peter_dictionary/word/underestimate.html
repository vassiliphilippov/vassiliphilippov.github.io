underestimate

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to estimate something as being less than the actual value, amount, or importance

Examples

Religious who derive their own sense of purpose through identification with the religious community rather than the academic community are prone to underestimate both the layman's reservoir of idealism and his need for this identification.
Well, you underestimate yourself.

Synset Examples

he underestimated the work that went into the renovation
Don't underestimate the danger of such a raft trip on this river

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - to estimate as being greater than the actual value or importance

Etymology

From under- (expressing insufficient degree) + estimate (from Latin *aestimare*, to value).

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to estimate something as being less than the actual amount, value, or importance, sometimes deliberately

Examples

Dr. Conant may underestimate the psychological importance of even token equality.

Synset Examples

The construction company wanted the contract badly and lowballed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - to estimate something as more than the actual amount

Etymology

From under- (less than) + estimate, influenced by Latin *aestimare* (to value).

3. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈestɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to assign a value, amount, or importance to something or someone that is lower than is accurate or deserved

Examples

But she seemed to have underestimated the number of her "boy friends ".

Synset Examples

Don't underestimate the value of this heirloom-you may sell it at a good price

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - assign too high a value to
  • Synonym: undervalue - to assign less value to than is deserved

Etymology

From under- (below) + estimate, from Latin *aestimare* (to value, rate).
