invention

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
A new device, method, or process developed from study and experimentation; something that has been invented.

Examples

Just as present technology had to await the explanations of physics, so one might expect that social invention will follow growing sociological understanding.
"The confusion is not my invention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovation - the introduction of something new; a novel idea or method
  • Antonym: discovery - the act of finding or learning something for the first time

Etymology

From Latin inventio (a finding, discovery), from invenire (to find, come upon).
