disregard

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
to pay no attention to something or treat it as unimportant

Examples

Refugees poured in, signing the Confession and rules in order to remain, and then disregarding them.
Accordingly, if it is not repealed by the Congress at its present session, I shall have no alternative thereafter but to direct the Secretary of Defense to disregard the section unless a court of competent jurisdiction determines otherwise.
We assume further that the union recognizes the possibility that price level increases may offset wage rate increases, and it does not entirely disregard the effect of price increases arising from its own wage increases upon the "real "wage rate.

Synset Examples

She cut him dead at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignore - to intentionally not acknowledge or pay attention to
  • Antonym: heed - to pay close attention to something

Etymology

From dis- (opposite of) + regard (to look at, heed)

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.rɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.rɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
to pay no attention to; to ignore or treat as unworthy of consideration

Examples

Ordinary water tides are disregarded.
Wishing to show that aviation was dependable and here to stay, Bob Fogg always made a point of taking off each morning on the dot of seven, disregarding rain, snow and sleet in true postal tradition.

Synset Examples

She dismissed his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
  • Antonym: consider - to think carefully about; opposite action to disregard

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + regard; first attested in the 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
A willful lack of care or attention for someone or something.

Examples

As to benefits to employees, it is notorious for its callous disregard except where it depends on them for services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - failure to care for properly
  • Antonym: heed - careful attention, notice

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + regard, from Old French 'regarder' (to look at).

4. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
The lack of attention or concern for something; the state of ignoring or paying no heed.

Examples

the desire to contrast the first parts of words which are alike in their last components produces an exceptional disregard of the normal patterns of stress of Chinese and Japanese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - failure to care for or give proper attention to something

Etymology

From dis- (do the opposite of) + regard, from Old French 'regarder' (to look at, consider).
