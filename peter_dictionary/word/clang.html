clang

1. NOUN

/ klæŋ / (AmE), / klæŋ / (BrE)
a loud, resonant, and repeated metallic sound

Examples

On these posts the gates swung open with a squeak and shut with a metallic clang.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clank - a dull, metallic sound, often less sharp than a clang
  • Synonym: clangor - continued, loud clanging sound

Etymology

Imitative of a ringing metallic sound; related to Latin 'clangere' (to resound).

2. VERB

/ klæŋ / (AmE), / klæŋ / (BrE)
To make a loud, resonant, and metallic sound; to cause something to produce such a noise.

Examples

It clanged shut as he sprang toward it.

Synset Examples

clanging metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clatter - make a loud, rattling noise
  • Synonym: ring - produce a clear resonant or vibrating sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself; from Latin 'clangere' (to resound, ring).
