firelight

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
the light given off by a fire, especially one burning in a fireplace or campfire

Examples

The firelight played over Eli's flowing white locks and rugged features.
She glanced around the clearing, taking in the wagon and the load of supplies and trappings scattered over the ground, the two kids, the whiteface bull that was chewing its cud just within the far reaches of the firelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candlelight - the light produced by a candle

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'light'.
