trot

1. NOUN

/ trɑt / (AmE), / trɒt / (BrE)
a slow pace of running, faster than walking but slower than running

Examples

He took one step, two, broke into a trot and then into a run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jog - an act of running at a slow, steady pace
  • Degree Higher: run - to move at a speed faster than a walk
  • Degree Lower: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn

Etymology

Probably from Old French troter, from Frankish *trotton (to trot, run).

2. VERB

/ trɑt / (AmE), / trɒt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to run at a moderately brisk, steady pace, faster than a walk but slower than a run, typically with short steps.

Examples

Stilts, a more direct cat, leaped from the bed and trotted briskly toward the kitchen.
Shadow looked surprised, wailed, and trotted after her.
Hoopla (Tar Heel-Holiday Hanover), a filly that wanted to trot, knocked herself October 31 and November 1 fighting the hopples.
She is a beautiful filly and likes to trot.
This filly is another Adios that wants to trot, and trot she did until forced to do otherwise.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
  • Degree Higher: run - to move at a speed faster than a walk, never having both feet on the ground at the same time

Etymology

From Old French troter (to trot), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ trɑt / (AmE), / trɒt / (BrE)
To ride a horse so that it moves at a gait faster than a walk but slower than a canter, lifting each diagonal pair of legs alternately.

Examples

Giving the other a dark look, he hauled his bronc around and trotted down off the street.
The first time I saw the latter filly she trotted by me and I noticed such a family resemblance that I said to myself, "that must be Hickory Ash ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: canter - to ride at a gait between a trot and a gallop
  • Degree Lower: walk - to ride at the slowest horse’s gait

Etymology

From Old French *trotter* (to trot), from Frankish or another Germanic source.
