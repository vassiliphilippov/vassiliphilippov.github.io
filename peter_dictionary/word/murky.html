murky

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝːki / (AmE), / ˈmɜːki / (BrE)
(of liquids) dark and dirty or cloudy, especially because of mud, sediment, or lack of clarity.

Examples

It may seem to some of them that success can be purchased much less dearly by fishing in the murky waters of international politics than by facing up to the intractable tasks at home.
Deputy Marshal Luke Harper still stood guard on the veranda, a forlorn, scarecrowish figure in the murky dark.

Synset Examples

a cloudy liquid
muddy coffee
murky waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudy - (of liquid) not clear, as if with sediment
  • Synonym: muddy - full of or resembling mud; not clear (of liquids)
  • Antonym: clear - (of liquid) free from cloudiness or impurities; transparent

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'murke' (dark), of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'myrkr' (darkness).
