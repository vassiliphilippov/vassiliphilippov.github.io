instinct

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌstɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stɪŋkt / (BrE)
an inborn pattern of behavior or response to certain stimuli, characteristic of a person or animal

Examples

Melies, however, out of his professional instincts as a magician, discovered and made use of a number of illusionary techniques that remain part of the vocabulary of film.
The instinct of discipline has been lost.

Synset Examples

the spawning instinct in salmon
altruistic instincts in social animals

Etymology

From Latin instinctus (impulse), from instinguere (to incite).
