sweetness

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitnəs / (AmE), / ˈswiːtnəs / (BrE)
The quality of having a sweet taste, as produced by sugar or similar substances.

Examples

He felt the sweetness of pity flood through him, veining his very flesh.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bitterness - the quality of having a bitter taste

Etymology

From Old English 'swētnes', from 'swēte' (sweet) + '-nes' (noun suffix).
