banshee

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.ʃi / (AmE), / ˈbæn.ʃi / (BrE)
(in Irish folklore) a female spirit whose wailing is believed to foretell a death in a household.

Examples

Then the pixies and the zombies took over while the banshees wailed in the distance.

Etymology

From Irish bean sídhe meaning 'woman of the fairy mound'.
