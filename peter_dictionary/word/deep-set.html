deep-set

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːpˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of eyes or other features) positioned far back in the face or surface, giving a sunken appearance.

Examples

Abel Poupin, a tall man with sunken cheeks and deep-set eyes, got to his feet.
The scar looked pure white in the half darkness; his eyes were black and deep-set, and expressionless.

Synset Examples

hunger gave their faces a sunken look

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prominent - projecting outward; standing out (especially describing eyes or features)
