toffee

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑfi / (AmE), / ˈtɒfi / (BrE)
A sweet made from sugar or molasses that is caramelized and cooled to form a hard, chewy candy.

Examples

A quarter inch this way or that for the hardbake, or the toffee, or the barley sugar, or the sardines, or the bitters, or the condensed milk, or the stationery, or the needles - what could it mean?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taffy - a chewy candy made from sugar or molasses boiled and pulled until glossy

Etymology

From English dialect toff (taffy), possibly from 'taffy', of uncertain origin.
