fountainhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntənˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntɪnˌhɛd / (BrE)
An original or abundant source of something; the principal origin of a stream of ideas, knowledge, or resources.

Examples

As the messages from Himmler, Heydrich, and Eichmann came in through Alfred Funk, Lublin's fountainhead spouted.

Synset Examples

she was a well of information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: source - place, person, or thing from which something originates
  • Synonym: origin - the point or place where something begins

Etymology

Formed from 'fountain' (spring, source of water) and 'head' (upper part, source), referring originally to the source of a stream or spring.
