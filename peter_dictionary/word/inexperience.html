inexperience

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɪkˈspɪriəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspɪəriəns / (BrE)
Lack of knowledge or skill resulting from not having enough experience.

Examples

And while her Adonis did not suffer from inexperience, satiety might well be an equal handicap.

Synset Examples

procedural inexperience created difficulties
their poor behavior was due to the rawness of the troops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: experience - practical contact with or observation of facts or events

Etymology

From in- (not) + experience, after French inexpérience or Medieval Latin inexpērientia.
