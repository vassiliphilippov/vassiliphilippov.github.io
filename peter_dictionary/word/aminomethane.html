aminomethane

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊˈmɛθeɪn / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
A chemical compound (CH3NH2) in which one hydrogen atom of methane is replaced by an amino group; also known as methylamine.

Examples

This buffer, pH 8.6, was 0.005 M in * * f and 0.039 M in tris (hydroxymethyl) - aminomethane (Tris).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methylamine - a flammable gas, the simplest primary amine; CH3NH2

Etymology

From amino- (relating to the amino group, –NH2) + methane (CH4).
