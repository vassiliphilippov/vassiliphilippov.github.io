candlewick

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændəlˌwɪk / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌwɪk / (BrE)
the cord or strand of material inside a candle that can be lit to produce a flame

Examples

Anita put out the remaining candles with a long snuffer, and in the smell of scented candlewick, the comforting awareness of each other's bodies, the retained pattern of dancers and guests remembered, their minds grew numb and then empty of images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wick - the cord in any candle, oil lamp, or lantern that can be lit to produce a flame

Etymology

From 'candle' + 'wick', both of Old English origin.
