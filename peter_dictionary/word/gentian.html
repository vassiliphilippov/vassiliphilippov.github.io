gentian

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn(t)ʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.tʃən / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Gentiana or related genera in the family Gentianaceae, typically having strikingly blue or purple, trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

When Marsh called to his aide and the pair rode off down the River Road where the gentians burned blue, Juanita was shaken and trying not to cry.

Etymology

From Latin *gentiana*, said to be named after Gentius, an ancient Illyrian king who reputedly discovered its medicinal properties.
