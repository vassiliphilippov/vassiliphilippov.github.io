commanding

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmændɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a position or viewpoint) situated so as to have a wide or advantageous view over a place or area.

Examples

The inference is overwhelming that du Pont's commanding position was promoted by its stock interest and was not gained solely on competitive merit ".

Synset Examples

a commanding view of the ocean
looked up at the castle dominating the countryside
the balcony overlooking the ballroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominant - having power or influence over others; high and controlling position
  • Synonym: overlooking - providing a view over a place from above

Etymology

From the verb 'command', influenced by the idea of commanding a view (to have control over a wide area).
