traditionalism

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief in or practice of maintaining tradition, especially in cultural or religious matters.

Examples

That he mastered every aspect of his medium according to his own great talents and contemporary judgments, is a good and solid symbol of his people under the tremendous pressures of proclaiming and practising the rigors of a new culture; and perhaps of even greater significance - his music is strong 20 th Century evidence of the effectiveness of Evolution, based on a broad Traditionalism for the creative art of music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatism - commitment to traditional values and ideas
  • Antonym: modernism - attitude or belief favoring modern ideas and practices over tradition

Etymology

From 'tradition' + '-alism', modeled after similar formations.

2. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or belief in maintaining and strictly adhering to traditional methods, customs, or teachings, especially in religion, culture, or society

Examples

How right he was; how clearly he saw the cultural defection of experimentation as an escape for those who dare not or prefer not to face the discipline of modern traditionalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservatism - the holding of traditional values and opposition to change
  • Antonym: innovation - the introduction of new ideas or methods; departure from tradition

Etymology

From 'tradition' + '-alism', modeled after similar English formations; 'tradition' from Latin 'traditio' (handing over, delivery).
