hallowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæloʊd / (AmE), / ˈhæl.əʊd / (BrE)
Regarded as holy or sacred; greatly revered and respected, especially in a religious context.

Examples

The Abbot of St. Eloi, Claude de Mommor, had been a good friend, but not even he thought Charles deserved burial in hallowed ground.

Synset Examples

the sacred name of Jesus
Jerusalem's hallowed soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacred - regarded with reverence or religious respect
  • Antonym: profane - not sacred or biblical; secular rather than religious

Etymology

Past participle of 'hallow', from Old English *hālig* (holy).
