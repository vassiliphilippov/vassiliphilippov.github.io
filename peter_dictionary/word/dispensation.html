dispensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
official permission to be released from a rule, obligation, or requirement, especially in a religious or legal context.

Examples

There were liberal provisions for dispensation where documents or records were lacking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemption - the act of freeing from an obligation or liability
  • Antonym: requirement - something that is required by a rule or law; opposite in context

Etymology

From Latin dispensationem (distribution, management), from dispensare (to distribute).
