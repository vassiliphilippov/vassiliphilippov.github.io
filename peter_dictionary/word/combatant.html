combatant

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈbætənt / (AmE), / kəmˈbætənt / (BrE)
(of a person, group, or role) engaging in or ready for combat; prepared to fight in war.

Examples

In his report to the Attorney General, the hearing officer suggested that the petitioner be exempt only from combatant training and service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-combatant - not engaged in or prepared for combat

Etymology

From Late Latin *combatantem*, present participle of *combattere* (to fight).
