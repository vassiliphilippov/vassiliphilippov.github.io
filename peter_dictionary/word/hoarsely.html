hoarsely

1. ADV

/ ˈhɔrsli / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsli / (BrE)
In a rough or harsh-sounding voice, as if suffering from a sore throat or loss of voice.

Examples

"Look, Lord ", he said hoarsely.
He hollered hoarsely, "Hang on "! and goaded the oxen as he yelled.

Synset Examples

`Excuse me,' he said hoarsely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshly - in a harsh or grating manner
  • Antonym: softly - in a gentle or quiet voice

Etymology

From 'hoarse' + '-ly'; 'hoarse' is from Middle English hors, from Old English hārs (rough, harsh).
