subliterary

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌbˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈlɪtərəri / (BrE)
(of a work, text, etc.) not considered to be literature, or not intended as such; lacking literary qualities.

Examples

Enthusiastically, Americans have swept subliterary and bogus materials like Paul Bunyan tales, Abe Lincoln anecdotes and labor union songs up as true products of our American oral tradition.

Synset Examples

subliterary works such as letters and diaries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literary - relating to literature; having qualities of literature

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'literary', meaning 'below literary quality'.
