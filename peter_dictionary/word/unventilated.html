unventilated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɛntɪˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɛntɪleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a room, space, etc.) not provided with adequate ventilation; not exposed to fresh air.

Examples

Back at the Factory-to-You with the other old maids, back there she was the youngest clerk and she was thirty-four, which made her young enough to resent the usual ideal working conditions, like the unventilated toilet with the door you had to hold shut while you sat down.

Synset Examples

stuffy unventilated rooms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ventilated - (of a space) provided with fresh air circulation

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'ventilated', from Latin 'ventilare' (to fan, to air).
