minuet

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪn.juˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌmɪn.juˈɛt / (BrE)
a slow, stately court dance of the 17th and 18th centuries, usually in triple time

Examples

Martha picked up the hem of her gown and with eyes closed she slowly began to dance a stately minuet around the ballroom.

Etymology

From French 'menuet', from 'menu' (small, delicate), after the dance's small, dainty steps.
