hiss

1. VERB

/ hɪs / (AmE), / hɪs / (BrE)
to make a sharp, sibilant sound like that of the letter 's', often as a sign of disapproval or as the sound produced by steam, snakes, or similar sources

Examples

The hostler would have the table ready and a pot of coffee hissing on the stove; then a porter from Manning's Fish House would trot in with a tray on his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boo - to yell 'boo' as a sign of disapproval

Etymology

Imitative; of echoic origin, resembling the sound it denotes.
