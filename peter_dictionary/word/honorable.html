honorable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑn.ər.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɒn.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Having strong moral principles and not disposed to cheat or deceive; honest and trustworthy.

Examples

"You would n't want me to say yes without making sure his intentions are honorable, would you "?

Synset Examples

honest lawyers
honest reporting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonest - willing to cheat, deceive, or defraud; not honest
  • Synonym: honest - free of deceit or untruthfulness; sincere and truthful

Etymology

From Old French honorable, from Latin honorabilis (worthy of honor).
