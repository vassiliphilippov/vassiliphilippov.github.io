gloomily

1. ADV

/ ˈɡluː.mɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈɡluː.mɪ.li / (BrE)
In a way that suggests sadness, depression, or lack of hope.

Examples

Only a radical change in the nature of the population in the central city would be likely to destroy this preference - and we must now turn our attention to the question of whether such a change, gloomily foreseen by so many urban diagnosticians, is actually upon us.

Synset Examples

such a change is gloomily foreseen by many

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic way

Etymology

From 'gloomy' + '-ly'. 'Gloomy' from Middle English 'glum' or 'gloome' (darkness, obscurity).
