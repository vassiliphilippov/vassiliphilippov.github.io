narcotic

1. NOUN

/ nɑrˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / nɑːˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
A drug that induces numbness or stupor, often used for pleasure or to relieve pain; use of such drugs can lead to addiction.

Examples

I also have stock in IBM and IT&T. Mr. Corleone, do you have any interests or control over gambling and narcotics in the state of New York?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - drug that relieves pain (painkiller)
  • Antonym: stimulant - drug that increases alertness or activity

Etymology

From Medieval Latin narcoticum, from Greek narkōtikos (making numb), from narkoun (to make numb).
