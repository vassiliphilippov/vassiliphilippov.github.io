superhighway

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈhaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈhaɪ.weɪ / (BrE)
A very wide and straight main road, designed for fast traffic and minimal stops, often linking major cities or regions.

Examples

The first superhighways - New York's Henry Hudson and Chicago's Lake Shore, San Francisco's Bay Bridge and its approaches, a good slice of the Pennsylvania Turnpike - were built as part of the federal works program which was going to cure the depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressway - a wide road built for fast traffic, with few or no intersections
  • Synonym: freeway - a main road for fast-moving traffic, with limited access

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (over, above, very large) + 'highway'.
