chick

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪk / (AmE), / tʃɪk / (BrE)
A young bird, especially a baby chicken or other domestic fowl.

Examples

date of laying, length of incubation period, number of chicks reaching the first week, second week, fifth week, weight of hen, size of rooster's wattles and so on, all scrawled out in a hand that looked more Chinese than English, the most jagged and sprawling Alex had ever seen.
Seems like she's willing, but the male just flops around all day like the bashful boy who took Jeannie May behind the barn and then did n't know what to do, and the people at the zoo have n't got any vulture chicks to show for their trouble.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chicken - domesticated fowl; juvenile or adult
  • Degree Lower: hatchling - very young animal that has recently emerged from an egg

Etymology

From Middle English 'chike', of imitative origin, related to the sound made by young birds.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪk / (AmE), / tʃɪk / (BrE)
(informal, slang) A young woman, especially an attractive one; sometimes considered disrespectful or dated.

Examples

He probably does it to get chicks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dame - informal term for a woman
  • Synonym: woman - adult female human
  • Antonym: guy - informal term for a man

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'chik' (young bird), later used metaphorically for young women, possibly by analogy to 'bird' as slang for woman.
