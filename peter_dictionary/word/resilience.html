resilience

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪliəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪliəns / (BrE)
The ability of a material to return to its original shape or position after being compressed, stretched, or bent, especially when the deformation does not exceed its elastic limit.

Examples

Berlin's resilience is amazing, but if it has to hire its labor in the West the struggle will be hard indeed ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed

Etymology

From Latin 'resilire' (to spring back).
