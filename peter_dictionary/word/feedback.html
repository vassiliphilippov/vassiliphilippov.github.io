feedback

1. NOUN

/ ˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
The process by which part of the output of a system is returned to its input to regulate further output.

Examples

Feed back of the energy transferred to the anode by applying gas transpiration through the anode.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - the power to influence or direct behavior or the course of events

Etymology

From 'feed' + 'back', first used in electrical engineering in the 1920s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfidˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfiːdbæk / (BrE)
information or a response given as a reaction to an inquiry, experiment, or someone's actions, often used to evaluate or improve performance or outcomes.

Examples

To practice new procedures under guided supervision and with constant feedback is the fourth step.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: response - an answer or reaction to something
  • Synonym: comment - a written or spoken remark expressing an opinion or reaction
  • Degree Higher: criticism - the expression of disapproval or analysis

Etymology

Formed from 'feed' + 'back', originally from electronic and engineering contexts in the early 20th century.
