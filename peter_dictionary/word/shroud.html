shroud

1. VERB

/ ʃraʊd / (AmE), / ʃraʊd / (BrE)
to cover or hide something as if with a cloth or shroud, often making it difficult to see or discover

Examples

They were all completely shrouded.

Synset Examples

The origins of this civilization are shrouded in mystery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or upon, especially for protection or concealment
  • Antonym: uncover - to remove a cover from; to reveal

Etymology

From Middle English shrouden, from Old English scrūd (garment, shroud).
