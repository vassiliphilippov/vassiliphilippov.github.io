underworld

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚwɝld / (AmE), / ˈʌndəwɜːld / (BrE)
the world of organized crime and its members; the criminal class considered as a whole

Examples

He was also at this time, although not so interwoven in high politics and the rackets as Torrio and Capone, the most powerful and most dangerous mob leader in the Chicago underworld, the roughneck king.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - person who has committed a crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - one who obeys the law; not involved in crime

Etymology

Formed from 'under' + 'world' to suggest a hidden or secret society beneath lawful society.
