teem

1. VERB

/ tim / (AmE), / tiːm / (BrE)
to be full of or swarming with people, animals, or things

Examples

The record teems with romance and adventure.

Synset Examples

The garden was swarming with bees
The plaza is teeming with undercover policemen
her mind pullulated with worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarm - to move or gather in large numbers, often used for insects or people
  • Synonym: abound - to exist in large numbers or amounts

Etymology

From Old English 'temian', related to Old Norse 'tæma' (to empty), likely from Proto-Germanic roots.
