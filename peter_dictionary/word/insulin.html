insulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lɪn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by the pancreas that regulates blood sugar levels by promoting the storage of glucose as glycogen in the liver and aiding the uptake of sugar into cells.

Etymology

From Latin 'insula' (island), referring to the islets of Langerhans where it is produced.
