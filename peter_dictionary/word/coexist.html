coexist

1. VERB

/ koʊˈɪɡˌzɪst / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪɡˌzɪst / (BrE)
(of people, groups, or things) to exist together at the same time or in the same place, especially in peace.

Examples

Political theoretical understanding, although almost at a standstill during this century, did develop during the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, and resulted in a flood of inventions which increased the possibility for man to coexist with man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exist - to be real or present in a place or situation

Etymology

From Latin co- (together) + existere (to exist).
