enigmatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛnɪɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛnɪɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious.

Examples

His name is Praisegod Piepsam, and he is rather fully described as to his clothing and physiognomy in a way which relates him to a sinister type in the author's repertory - he is a forerunner of those enigmatic strangers in "Death in Venice ", for example, who represent some combination of cadaver, exotic, and psychopomp.
Though it centers around the brilliant and enigmatic figure of Charles 12,, the true hero is not finally the king himself.

Synset Examples

I didn't grasp the meaning of that enigmatic comment until much later
prophetic texts so enigmatic that their meaning has been disputed for centuries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysterious - difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Synonym: obscure - not clear or hard to understand
  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; free from confusion or ambiguity

Etymology

From Latin *aenigmāticus*, from Greek *ainigmatikos* (obscure, puzzling), from *ainigma* (riddle).
