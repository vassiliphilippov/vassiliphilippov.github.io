arteriole

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪrioʊl / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriəʊl / (BrE)
A small, thin-walled branch of an artery that leads to capillaries.

Examples

Throughout, there were marked arteriolosclerosis and hyalinization of afferent glomerular arterioles.
In all of the sections examined, the arterioles and small arteries were essentially normal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • Degree Lower: capillary - a tiny blood vessel where substances are exchanged between the blood and the tissues

Etymology

From Latin 'arteriola', diminutive of 'arteria' (artery).
