relyric

1. VERB

/ riˈlɪrɪk / (AmE), / riˈlɪrɪk / (BrE)
to write new lyrics for (a song), especially replacing the words of an existing composition

Examples

Mercer's Whiteman association brought him into contact with Hoagy Carmichael, whose "Snowball "Mercer relyriced as "Lazybones ", in which form it became a hit and marked the real beginning of Mercer's song writing career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rewrite - to write something again, especially with changes
  • Synonym: parody - an imitation of a song with new, humorous lyrics

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) and lyric (words of a song).
