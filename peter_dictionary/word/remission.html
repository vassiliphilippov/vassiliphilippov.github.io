remission

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a temporary reduction or disappearance of the symptoms of a disease, especially when the disease is chronic or incurable

Examples

Some investigators have found a parallelism between remissions and return of the sympathetic reactivity of the hypothalamus to the normal level as indicated by the Mecholyl test and, conversely, between clinical impairment and increasing deviation of this test from the norm.

Synset Examples

his cancer is in remission

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relapse - the return of disease symptoms after improvement

Etymology

From Latin remissio (a sending back, relaxation), from remittere (to send back)
