circumstances

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kəmˌstænsɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəmˌstɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
the conditions or factors affecting someone's life, particularly as they influence personal situation, success, or well-being

Examples

She described herself and her circumstances unhesitatingly.
Circumstances gave her almost undisputed sway over child rearing, money handling and home maintenance.

Synset Examples

whatever my fortune may be
deserved a better fate
has a happy lot
the luck of the Irish
a victim of circumstances
success that was her portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - luck or chance as it affects one's life
  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control
  • Synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance

Etymology

From Latin 'circumstantia' (surrounding condition), from 'circumstare' (to stand around).
