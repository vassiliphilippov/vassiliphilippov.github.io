deign

1. VERB

/ deɪn / (AmE), / deɪn / (BrE)
to do something that one considers to be beneath one's dignity, usually with reluctance or a sense of superiority

Examples

Whenever he deigned to appear at the studio he was "hung over ", uncooperative, rude and insulting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condescend - to behave as if descending from a superior position; to lower oneself

Etymology

From Old French *deignier*, from Latin *dignari* (to deem worthy), from *dignus* (worthy).
