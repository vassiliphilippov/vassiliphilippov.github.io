defray

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfreɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈfreɪ / (BrE)
to pay the costs or expenses of something, especially as a means of providing financial support

Examples

He expressed the opinion the city could hire a CD director for about $ 3500 a year and would only have to put up half that amount on a matching fund basis to defray the salary costs.
The Carbondale Industrial Development Corp. has obtained a $ 500000 loan to help defray the cost of remodeling a city-owned factory to accommodate production that will provide 500 new jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pay - to give money for goods or services

Etymology

From Old French 'defrayer' (to pay expenses), from Late Latin 'disfratare', from 'de-' (completely) + 'fractus', past participle of 'frangere' (to break, in reference to expenses being 'broken off').
