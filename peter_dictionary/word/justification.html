justification

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A good reason or explanation for an action, belief, or feeling; something that proves or shows that something is reasonable or necessary.

Examples

There is no justification for systematizing the random statements of Irenaeus about the image of God beyond this, nor for reading into his imprecise usage the later theological distinction between the image of God (humanity) and the similitude of God (immortality).
As such, it was beyond politics and had no need of justification by a "message ".
There is no justification for such misrepresentation.

Synset Examples

he considered misrule a justification for revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason given to explain an offence or fault
  • Synonym: rationalization - an attempt to explain or justify behavior or an attitude with logical reasons, even if not true
  • Antonym: accusation - a claim that someone has done something wrong

Etymology

From Latin justificatio (a making just or lawful), from justificare (to make just), from justus (just) + facere (to make).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a reason, explanation, or statement that shows, explains, or defends why something was done or believed

Examples

This explains some group ends and provides a justification of their primacy.
(This provides whatever real justification there is for the talk about "reality ".)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - an explanation offered to lessen blame
  • Synonym: reason - a cause or explanation for an action
  • Antonym: accusation - a statement blaming someone for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin justificatio (an act of making just), from justificare (to justify)
