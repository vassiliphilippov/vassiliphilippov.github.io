maximum

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæksɪməm / (AmE), / ˈmæksɪməm / (BrE)
as great, complete, or intense as possible; being the greatest or highest amount, degree, or value allowed or attainable.

Examples

The bill would increase from $ 5000000 to $ 15000000 the maximum loan the state could make to a local water project.
To anyone who remembers Newport at its less than maximum violence, this view of what the boys and girls do in the springtime before they wing north for the Jazz Festival ought to prove entertaining.

The most effective political inventions seem to make maximum use of natural harbors and are aware that restraining breakwaters can play only a minor part in the whole scheme.

Synset Examples

maximal expansion
maximum pressure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimum - least or smallest possible (adjective)
  • Synonym: maximal - greatest or highest possible (more formal/technical)
  • Synonym: utmost - highest or greatest (used for degree, effort, etc.)
  • Synonym: peak - at the highest point or intensity

Etymology

From Latin maximum (n.), neuter of maximus meaning 'greatest'.
