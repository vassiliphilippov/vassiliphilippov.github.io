morphemic

1. ADJ

/ mɔrˈfimɪk / (AmE), / mɔːˈfiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of morphemes, the smallest units of meaning in a language

Examples

While the phonemes can be very easily stated, no one is likely to be satisfied with the statement until phonemic occurrences can be related in some way to morphemic units, i. e. until the morphophonemics is worked out, or at least far enough that it seems reasonable to expect success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morpheme - smallest meaningful unit in a language

Etymology

From 'morpheme' plus the suffix '-ic' meaning 'pertaining to'. 'Morpheme' is from French 'morphème', based on Greek 'morphē' (form).
