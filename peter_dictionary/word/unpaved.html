unpaved

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪvd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪvd / (BrE)
(of a road or surface) not covered with a hard material such as concrete, asphalt, or stones.

Examples

The street was unpaved and rose steeply toward the center; it was mud in wet weather and dust, ankle-deep, in dry, and could be crossed only at the corner where there were stepping stones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paved - covered with a hard surface such as concrete or asphalt

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'paved', from Latin 'pavimentum' (pavement).
