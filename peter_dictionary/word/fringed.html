fringed

1. ADJ

/ frɪndʒd / (AmE), / frɪndʒd / (BrE)
decorated with an edging of hanging cords, threads, or strips, often used to describe clothing or fabric

Examples

At 7: 25 two hotel doormen came thumping down the steps, carrying a saw-horse to be set up as a barricade in front of the haberdashery store window next to the entranceway, and as I watched them in their gaudy red coats that nearly scraped the ground, their golden, fringed epaulets and spic, red visored caps, I suddenly saw just over their shoulders Jessica gracefully making her way through the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfringed - lacking a decorative hanging edge

2. ADJ

/ frɪndʒd / (AmE), / frɪndʒd / (BrE)
Having a border or edge of something; surrounded or trimmed with a fringe or similar border, often used to describe something lined or bordered by another feature.

Examples

The unstructured schools were in a large suburban community, predominantly middle - to upper-middle class, but fringed by an industrial area.

Synset Examples

a large suburban community...fringed by an industrial area
a grass-fringed stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bordered - having a border or boundary; edged

Etymology

From the noun 'fringe', ultimately from Old French 'fringe', from Vulgar Latin *frimbia, from Latin 'fimbria' (border, edge).
