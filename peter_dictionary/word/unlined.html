unlined

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪnd / (BrE)
(of an item, especially clothing or paper) not having a lining or liner.

Examples

Many styles have perforations and an almost weightlessness achieved via unlined leathers.
At right is a casual style in a crushed unlined white leather.

Synset Examples

a thin unlined jacket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lined - (of clothing or material) having a lining

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) + lined (having a lining).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪnd / (BrE)
(of skin or a face) smooth and free from lines or wrinkles.

Examples

His face was ruddy and heavy and unlined, and when he laughed he showed his teeth, which were big and white and strong and unquestionably home-grown.

Synset Examples

his cheeks were unlined
his unseamed face

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lined - marked with lines or wrinkles (especially of the skin)

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'lined', indicating absence of lines or wrinkles.
