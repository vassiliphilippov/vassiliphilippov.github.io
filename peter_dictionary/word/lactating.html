lactating

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæk.teɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlak.teɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a female mammal) producing or secreting milk, especially to feed young offspring.

Examples

Continuous administration is not recommended for lactating cows.
A tranquilizer fed to cattle (other than lactating dairy cows) prior to their being subjected to stress conditions such as vaccinating, shipping, weaning calves, and excessive handling.

Synset Examples

a wet nurse
a wet cow
lactating cows

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry - not producing milk (of a female mammal)

Etymology

From Latin 'lactare', frequentative of 'lac, lactis' (milk).
