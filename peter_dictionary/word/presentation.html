presentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A visual display or demonstration of information, ideas, or objects to an audience.

Examples

Previous presentations have been on French, Spanish, Russian, Italian, German and Japanese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhibition - a public display of works of art or items of interest
  • Synonym: demonstration - an act of showing something clearly

Etymology

From Latin praesentatio(n-), from praesentare 'to place before'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally giving or awarding something, such as a prize, in a ceremonial manner

Examples

We shall consider these in the inverse order of their presentation.
In fact, O' Connor was honoured during the ceremony with the presentation of a $ 2500 diamond stickpin.
Despite the clarity of his presentation, his idea was not of Einsteinian calibre.
Subject to the limitations of this Act, the Secretary shall make stabilization payments to small domestic producers upon presentation of evidence satisfactory to him of their status as such producers and of the sale by them of newly mined ores, or concentrates produced therefrom, as provided in this Act.
When helped by a high degree of structure in lesson presentation, then, and only then, does such a child attain unusual success.

Synset Examples

she gave the trophy but he made the presentation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - something given as a prize or recognition
  • Synonym: presentation ceremony - an event where something is formally presented
  • Antonym: withholding - the act of refusing to give something

Etymology

From Latin praesentatio (a showing, offering), from praesentare (to present).
