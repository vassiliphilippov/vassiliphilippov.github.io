safe-conduct

1. NOUN

/ seɪf ˈkɑndʌkt / (AmE), / seɪf ˈkɒndʌkt / (BrE)
an official document or guarantee allowing a person to travel safely through a region, especially during war or in hostile territory

Examples

Watson had given his name and asked for a safe-conduct pass.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin salvus conductus (safe conduct), from Latin salvus (safe) + conductus (conducted, led).
