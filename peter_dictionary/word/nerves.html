nerves

1. NOUN

/ nɝːvz / (AmE), / nɜːvz / (BrE)
a state of nervousness or anxiety, often resulting in tenseness or shakiness; agitation due to stress, excitement, or fear

Examples

For a serious young man who plays golf with a serious intensity, Palmer has such an inherent sense of humor that it relieves the strain and keeps his nerves from jangling like banjo strings.
"Comment "? ejaculated the surprised woman, looking at Alex for an explanation but he, parting from her without ceremony, only offered a few words about the doctor's provincial American speech and a state of nerves brought on by the demands of his work.
Molten glare singed their eyelids an angry crimson; suffocating air sapped their strength and strained their nerves to snapping; dust choked their throats and lay like acid in their lungs.

Synset Examples

he suffered an attack of nerves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervousness - the quality or state of being nervous
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being relaxed and free from anxiety

2. NOUN

/ nɝːvz / (AmE), / nɜːvz / (BrE)
the ability to remain calm and in control of your emotions, especially in difficult or stressful situations

Examples

His nerves were getting a little ragged and his impatience put an edge in his voice.

Synset Examples

this kind of tension is not good for my nerves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calmness and self-control, especially under pressure
  • Degree Lower: calm - a state of not being excited, nervous, or upset
  • Degree Higher: fortitude - courage and strength in facing difficulty
  • Antonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
