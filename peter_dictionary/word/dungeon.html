dungeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌn.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈdʌn.dʒən / (BrE)
The main tower or stronghold within the walls of a medieval castle or fortress, originally used as a place of residence or last refuge.

Examples

The vast, dungeon kitchens may seem hardly worth using except on occasions when one is faced with a thousand unexpected guests for lunch.
At the end of a shaft of light, the pews appeared to be broad stairs in a long dungeon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep - the strongest or central tower of a castle or fortress

Etymology

From Old French *donjon* (great tower of a castle), later confused with 'dungeon' (prison cell) in English.
