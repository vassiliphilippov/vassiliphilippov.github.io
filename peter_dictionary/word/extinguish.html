extinguish

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
To put an end to something; to destroy or eliminate, often used figuratively for hopes, feelings, or lives.

Examples

No matter how devoted a man was, no matter how fully he gave his life to the Lord, he could never extinguish that one spark of pride that gave him definition as an individual.

Synset Examples

The Nazis snuffed out the life of many Jewish children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snuff out - to extinguish; to put an end to; to kill

Etymology

From Latin 'extinguere' (to quench, to put out).
