amusement

1. NOUN

/ əˈmjuzmənt / (AmE), / əˈmjuːzmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of enjoyment or pleasure caused by something that entertains or makes you laugh.

Examples

His appearance as Lizzy evokes not amusement but horror in the audience; it is a spectacle absolutely painful, an epiphany of the suffering flesh unredeemed by spirit, untouched by any spirit other than abasement and humiliation.
Squatting on our haunches beside the flat stone we broke them on, we were safe behind the high closed gates at the end of the drive: safe from interruption and the observation and possible amusement of the passers-by.
Lord laughed with secret amusement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entertainment - activity or event that gives enjoyment or pleasure
  • Antonym: boredom - state of being bored; lack of interest or engagement

Etymology

From French *amusement*, from *amuser* (to amuse).

2. NOUN

/ əˈmjuzmənt / (AmE), / əˈmjuːzmənt / (BrE)
an activity or experience that entertains, amuses, or gives enjoyment; something that holds the attention in a pleasant way

Examples

All these emotions were screwed up to new heights when, after acceptance and the first rehearsals, there ensued such a buzz of excitement among Parisian music lovers that Duclos had to come running to Rousseau to inform him that the news had reached the superintendent of the King's amusements, and that he was now demanding that the work be offered first at the royal summer palace of Fontainebleau.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entertainment - an event, performance, or activity designed to entertain others
  • Antonym: boredom - a state of being bored or uninterested

Etymology

From French *amusement*, from *amuser* (to amuse).
