unclasp

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈklæsp / (AmE), / ʌnˈklɑːsp / (BrE)
to release or open something by removing a clasp or fastening

Examples

He nodded, clasping and unclasping his hands over his paunch, and flicked glances at me.

Synset Examples

She clasped and unclasped her hands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clasp - to fasten or hold tightly

Etymology

From 'un-' (a reversal prefix) + 'clasp' (to fasten or grip), meaning to undo a clasp.
