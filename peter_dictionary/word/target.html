target

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑr.ɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.ɡɪt / (BrE)
An object or mark at which missiles, such as bullets, arrows, or darts, are aimed or fired.

Examples

The opinion continues here that with a 162 - game schedule, pitching spread thin through a 10 - team league and a most inviting target in Los Angeles' Wrigley Field Jr., Mantle just might break the most glamorous record on the books, Babe Ruth's 60 homers of 1927.
A trained marksman shooting five rounds at a target, all under practically the same conditions, may hit the bull's-eye from 0 to 5 times.
In the marksmanship example, a trial consists of "one round shot at a target "with outcome either one bull's-eye (success) or none (failure).
And millions of rounds of entirely new and modern small-arms ammunition, designed for today's hunting and target shooting.
Primary target would be shipments of tanks, guns, aviation gasoline and ammunition coming from Russia and Czechoslovakia.

Synset Examples

his arrow hit the mark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - a point to be aimed at or reached
  • Synonym: bull's-eye - the center of a target

Etymology

From Italian 'targhetta' (a small shield), diminutive of 'targa' (shield).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡət / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
a person, place, or thing that is selected as the aim of an attack or action, especially in military or competitive contexts.

Examples

The nuclear war is already being fought, except that the bombs are not being dropped on enemy targets - not yet.
An aggressor would use an agent against which there was a minimal naturally acquired or artificially induced immunity in a target population.
Our major problem is what an enemy might accomplish in an initial attack on a target.
One of Greg's bombs hung up, and he was miles from the target before he could get rid of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objective - something aimed at or sought; a goal
  • Synonym: goal - the object of a person’s ambition or effort; an aim or desired result
  • Antonym: source - the place where something begins, often contrasted with 'target' in technical contexts

Etymology

From Old French 'targette', diminutive of 'targe' (shield), from Frankish or Proto-Germanic.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡət / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the object of an attack, criticism, or ridicule

Examples

Authorities say that oldsters are a prime target.
He is a target of ridicule to his wife, and often - since private affairs rarely remain private - to the outside world as well.

Synset Examples

he fell prey to muggers
everyone was fair game
the target of a manhunt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed or adversely affected by an action or event
  • Synonym: prey - a person who is the aim of an attack or exploitation; victim
  • Antonym: perpetrator - a person who carries out an attack or harmful act (opposite role)

Etymology

From Italian *targhetta*, diminutive of *targa* (shield), or from Old French *targette* (small shield).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
a result or objective that a person or organization intends to achieve

Examples

The target chart quickly and briefly tells you which additives do what.
Here are some key areas to examine to make sure your pricing strategy will be on target:

Synset Examples

the sole object of her trip was to see her children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - something that you hope to achieve
  • Synonym: objective - something you are trying to achieve
  • Degree Lower: destination - the place to which someone is going or something is sent

Etymology

From Middle French *targette* (a small shield), from Old French *targe* (a shield).
