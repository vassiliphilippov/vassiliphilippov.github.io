suppress

1. VERB

/ səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
to consciously control and prevent the expression or display of an emotion, desire, impulse, or behavior

Examples

Alex suppressed those expressions of relief which offered to prevail in his face and escape from his throat; unwarranted they were in any case for, as he stood facing the fille de chambre, his ears were assailed by new sounds from the interior of the shower room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrain - to hold back or keep under control (especially emotions or impulses)
  • Antonym: express - to convey or show an emotion or feeling

Etymology

From Latin suppressus, past participle of supprimere (to press down, to restrain).

2. VERB

/ səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
To keep down or control (people or ideas), often by unjust use of authority or force.

Examples

Those who would suppress dangerous thoughts, credit ideas with high potency.

Synset Examples

The government oppresses political activists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
  • Synonym: repress - to subdue someone or something by force

Etymology

From Latin suppressus, past participle of supprimere (to press down, restrain).

3. VERB

/ səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
To forcibly put an end to (an activity, uprising, or feeling), often through the use of authority or power.

Examples

The Dominican people have known no democratic institutions and precious little freedom for a generation, and all alternative leadership has been suppressed.
One can even argue - though this is a delicate matter - that every justification existed for their returning the Public Lecture to the First Church, and so to suppress it, rather than let Parker use it as a sounding board for his propaganda when his turn should come to occupy it.
An aircraft with a load of small nuclear weapons could very conceivably be given a mission to suppress all trains operating within a specified geographic area of Russia - provided that we had used some of our ICBMs to degrade Russia's air defenses before our bombers got there.

Synset Examples

suppress a nascent uprising
stamp down on littering
conquer one's desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quell - to put an end to, typically by the use of force
  • Synonym: repress - to subdue by force
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to (someone)

Etymology

From Latin 'suppressus', past participle of 'supprimere' (to press down).
