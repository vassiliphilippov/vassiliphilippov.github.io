stiffness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪfnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪfnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being rigid and difficult to bend or move, especially as a physical property of an object, material, or part of the body.

Examples

His arm had been giving him some trouble and Rector was not enough of a medical expert to determine whether it had healed improperly or whether Hino was simply rebelling against the tedious work in the print shop, using the stiffness in his arm as an excuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidity - the quality of being unable to bend or be forced out of shape; inflexibility
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking

Etymology

From 'stiff' + '-ness', with 'stiff' from Old English 'stif' (rigid, immobile).
