boom

1. VERB

/ buːm / (AmE), / buːm / (BrE)
to hit or strike something hard, producing a loud sound or forceful impact, especially in sports

Examples

He boomed a 280 - yard drive.

Synset Examples

He smashed a 3-run homer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - to hit something with great force
  • Synonym: whack - to hit something with a loud or sharp sound

2. NOUN

/ buːm / (AmE), / buːm / (BrE)
A deep, prolonged, and loud sound, such as that made by an explosion or a distant thunder.

Examples

a dull boom and a throbbing echo.
This joke was not funny to Linda Kay, and she blushed, as she always did; then, hearing the muffled boom of Howard's laughter, blushed redder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise
  • Synonym: crash - a loud, destructive noise, especially of something being broken

Etymology

Imitative word, formed from the sound itself, first recorded in English in the late 15th century.

3. VERB

/ buːm / (AmE), / buːm / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) to make or produce a deep, loud, resonant noise.

Examples

"We were on our vacation in Canada ", Howard explained, in a muffled voice that must have been used to booming, "and the news did n't catch up with us till we were nearly home.

Synset Examples

His deep voice boomed through the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - to make a loud, deep noise, especially like a lion or crowd
  • Synonym: thunder - to make a loud, deep, rumbling noise
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak or make a sound very softly

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound itself.
