dulled

1. ADJ

/ dʌld / (AmE), / dʌld / (BrE)
Having lost intensity, sharpness, or interest, especially as a result of overexposure or repetition.

Examples

Public relations strategists everywhere, watching the reaction of the German press, the liberal press, the lunatic fringe press, listening to their neighbors, studying interviews with men and women on the street, cried out: Too much, too much - the mind of the audience is becoming dulled, the horrors are losing their effect.
But he was more than half drunk, and his faculties were dulled.

Synset Examples

the mind of the audience is becoming dulled
the benumbed intellectual faculties can no longer respond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benumbed - deprived of feeling or responsiveness
  • Synonym: blunted - having lost its edge or intensity
  • Antonym: stimulated - heightened in sensitivity or interest
