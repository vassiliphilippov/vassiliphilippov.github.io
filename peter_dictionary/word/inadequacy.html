inadequacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
A lack of sufficient quantity or number; the state of being not enough to meet a requirement or need.

Examples

This large discrepancy demonstrates the inadequacies of the experimental methods and the lack of understanding of the various phenomena involved.

Synset Examples

the inadequacy of unemployment benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficiency - the state of being inadequate or deficient; a lack

Etymology

From in- (not) + adequate + -acy, by analogy with inadequateness.
