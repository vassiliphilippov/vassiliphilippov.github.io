porterhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtərˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A large steak cut from the thick end of the short loin, containing a T-shaped bone and a large section of tenderloin.

Examples

Still existing on a "Northern Union "telegraph form is a typical peremptory message from Peru grocer J. J. Hapgood to Burton and Graves' store in Manchester - "Get and send by stage sure four pounds best Porterhouse or sirloin steak, for Mrs. Hapgood send six sweet oranges ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: T-bone steak - a steak cut from the short loin with a T-shaped bone

Etymology

Named after porterhouse restaurants in the US where this cut was commonly served.
