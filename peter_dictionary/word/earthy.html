earthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝːθi / (AmE), / ˈɜːθi / (BrE)
(especially of taste or smell) reminiscent of earth or nature; natural, unrefined, or suggestive of the soil.

Examples

With his mouth opened wide, he squirted the warm white milk against the roof of his mouth and his tongue savored the light, earthy taste of it.

Synset Examples

the earthy taste of warm milk fresh from the cow
earthy smells of new-mown grass

Etymology

From Old English eorthe (earth) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɝːθi / (AmE), / ˈɜːθi / (BrE)
(of a person or quality) hearty, robust, and uninhibited, often in an open or lusty way.

Examples

This masterpiece has gaiety, too, but it is the gaiety of dancing people: earthy, salty and humorous.

Synset Examples

an earthy enjoyment of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearty - warm, lively, and vigorous

Etymology

From Old English eorthe (earth) + -y, meaning 'of or like earth.'

3. ADJ

/ ˈɜrθi / (AmE), / ˈɜːθi / (BrE)
(of humor, language, or behavior) direct, coarse, and sometimes indecent, reflecting a lack of refinement or delicacy.

Examples

The soldiers who comprised the rank and file of the Civil War armies were an earthy people.
As a consequence of the tensions thus produced in his thoughts and feelings, he wrote on the one hand sketches of idealized hunting trips and on the other an anecdote of the village of Hardscrabble, Arkansas, where no one had ever seen a piano; and he wrote also the masterpiece of frontier humor, "The Big Bear of Arkansas ", in which earthy realism is placed alongside the exaggeration of the backwoods tall-tale and the awe with which man contemplates the grandeur and the mysteries of nature.

Synset Examples

coarse language
a crude joke
crude behavior
an earthy sense of humor
a revoltingly gross expletive
a vulgar gesture
full of language so vulgar it should have been edited

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crude - rude or offensively coarse (of language, behavior, etc.)
  • Synonym: vulgar - lacking sophistication or good taste; coarse or indecent
  • Antonym: refined - elegant and cultured; showing high moral or intellectual quality
