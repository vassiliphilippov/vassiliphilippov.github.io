accelerated

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.ə.reɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a course, process, or program) made faster than usual; taking less time than normal by proceeding at a quicker pace.

Examples

Pitcher Steve Barber joined the club one week ago after completing his hitch under the Army's accelerated wintertime military course, also at Fort Knox, Ky..

Synset Examples

in an accelerated program in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast-tracked - proceeded or promoted at a faster rate than usual

Etymology

From the verb 'accelerate,' from Latin 'acceleratus', past participle of 'accelerare' (to hasten).
