exposure

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of subjecting someone or something to a particular experience, influence, or atmosphere, especially over a period of time

Examples

The audience leaves the play under a spell, It is the kind of spell which the exposure to spirit in its living active manifestation always evokes.
It is at least possible that the capacity to postpone gratification is developed as well as expressed in a continuous and guided exposure to great literature.
In fact, I can only say this seems to me to follow from a wide, continuous, and properly guided exposure to literary art.

Synset Examples

she denounced the exposure of children to pornography

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - the state of being in communication or in physical association
  • Antonym: immunity - protection from or insensitivity to a particular disease or influence

Etymology

From French *exposer*, from Latin *exponere* (to put out, set forth).
