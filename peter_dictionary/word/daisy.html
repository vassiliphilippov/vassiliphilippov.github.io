daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A flowering plant of the family Asteraceae, typically with a yellow disk and white or colored rays, often arranged in a single whorl.

Examples

The dialogue is sharp, witty and candid - typical "do n't eat the daisies "material - which has stamped the author throughout her books and plays, and it was obvious that the Theatre-by-the-Sea audience liked it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamomile - a related plant with similar flowers, often used as a synonym for some daisies

Etymology

From Old English *dæges ēage* (day's eye), referring to the flower opening in the day.
