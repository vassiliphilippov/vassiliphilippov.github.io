carcass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.kəs / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.kəs / (BrE)
the dead body of an animal, especially after being slaughtered and prepared for food

Examples

Measurements of skins are of little value; every snake hide is noticeably longer than its carcass and intentional stretching presents no difficulty to the unscrupulous explorer.
It serves merely as an excess cushion for the rest of the carcass.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corpse - dead body of a human being
  • Synonym: remains - the parts left after most has been removed, destroyed, or used

Etymology

From Old French 'carcois', influenced by Latin 'caro' (flesh).
