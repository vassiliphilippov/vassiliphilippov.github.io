reassert

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈəˌsɝt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈəːsɜːt / (BrE)
to state again or strengthen a position, belief, or situation, especially after it has been questioned or weakened

Examples

The codes are mechanism for training in, and directing and enforcing, uniform social interaction, and for continually and publicly reasserting the solidarity of the group.
That Aristotelean-Thomistic principle experienced a thorough going-over from a number of the participants, but in the end the concept came to reassert itself.

Synset Examples

The witnesses confirmed the victim's account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reaffirm - to affirm again; to confirm more firmly
  • Antonym: undermine - to weaken or lessen the effectiveness of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + assert, from Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, affirm).
