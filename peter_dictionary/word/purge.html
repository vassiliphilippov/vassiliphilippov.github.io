purge

1. VERB

/ pɝdʒ / (AmE), / pɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to remove someone from an organization or position, especially for political reasons or as part of a campaign to eliminate dissent

Examples

(One big question: If Colmer was to be purged, what should the House do about the other three senior Mississippians who supported the maverick electors?)

Synset Examples

Deng Xiao Ping was purged several times throughout his lifetime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to force someone to leave an organization or country
  • Synonym: oust - to remove or force out of a position or place

Etymology

From Latin 'purgare' (to cleanse, purify).
