contrabass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑntrəˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrəˌbeɪs / (BrE)
the largest and lowest-pitched instrument of the violin family, also known as the double bass

Examples

Some renditions are of symphonic dimensions, with the contrabass given free rein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double bass - another term for the contrabass; the largest, lowest violin family instrument
  • Degree Higher: cello - a slightly smaller, higher-pitched member of the violin family

Etymology

From Italian 'contrabbasso', from 'contra-' (against, below) + 'basso' (bass).
