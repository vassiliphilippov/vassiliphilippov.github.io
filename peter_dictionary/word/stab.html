stab

1. VERB

/ stæb / (AmE), / stæb / (BrE)
to wound or kill someone by pushing a knife or other pointed object into their body

Examples

Dice rolled, prostitutes plied their trade, thieves stole, murderers stabbed, and the ungodly blasphemed.

Synset Examples

The victim was knifed to death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - to attack or wound with a knife

Etymology

From Middle English 'stabben', possibly of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse 'stafha' (to thrust).
