shaken

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪkən / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkən / (BrE)
Psychologically or emotionally disturbed as a result of a shock or upsetting experience.

Examples

When Marsh called to his aide and the pair rode off down the River Road where the gentians burned blue, Juanita was shaken and trying not to cry.
He drove on, wary and shaken.

Synset Examples

retrieved his named from her jolted memory
the accident left her badly shaken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unnerved - Made anxious or unsettled; deprived of courage or confidence
  • Synonym: startled - Surprised or shocked suddenly, often causing a scare
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

Past participle of 'shake', from Old English *sceacan*.
