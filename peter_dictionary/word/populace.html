populace

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs / (BrE)
the people living in a particular country or area, especially considered collectively or as a mass

Examples

It would be well to show the populace how we deal with adulterers ".
A week later the sentence of the Council was carried out: Jake Camaret and the woman were marched naked through the streets past a mocking populace.

Synset Examples

he is a hero in the eyes of the public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public - the people in general; the community at large

Etymology

From French populace, from Italian popolazza (common people), augmentative of popolo (people), from Latin populus (people).
