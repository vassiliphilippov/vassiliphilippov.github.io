idle

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
not engaged in work, activity, or movement; inactive or not being used

Examples

Texas leads in per game rushing averages, 355 yards, and passing 149 (to Baylor's 126), but idle Baylor has the best defensive record (187.5 yards per game to Texas' 189).
When he came to the movies - more or less by accident - they were still cheap entertainment capable of enthralling the unthinking for an idle few minutes.
Papa pointed a scornful finger at the splashing youth: "Idle recreation "!

Synset Examples

an idle laborer
idle drifters
the idle rich
an idle mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaged in action or work; in operation

Etymology

From Old English īdel (empty, useless, vain).

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
to spend time doing nothing; to be inactive, especially habitually or for a period of time

Examples

The veterans, idling on their benches in the Square, beneath the soldiers' monument, got to their feet when Papa approached: "Morning, Reverend "!

Synset Examples

The old man sat and stagnated on his porch
He slugged in bed all morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laze - to spend time in a relaxed, lazy, or idle manner
  • Antonym: work - to be engaged in physical or mental activity

Etymology

From Old English īdel (empty, useless).

3. ADJ

/ ˈaɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
(of a threat, fear, talk, etc.) having no real basis; not founded on reason or fact.

Examples

A good many beef hungry settlers were accepting the death of William Lewis as proof that the warning notes were not idle threats.

Synset Examples

baseless gossip
the allegations proved groundless
idle fears
unfounded suspicions
unwarranted jealousy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseless - without foundation in reason or fact
  • Synonym: unfounded - not based on fact or evidence
  • Synonym: groundless - without any good reason or cause

Etymology

From Old English īdel (empty, vain, useless).

4. VERB

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəl / (BrE)
(of an engine or machine) to run while disconnected, not under load, or not doing useful work.

Examples

A plume of smoke rose from a Central Vermont locomotive which idled behind a string of gravel cars, and little figures that were workmen labored to set the ruptured roadbed to rights.
It ticked over smoothly, idling while Fogg exchanged mails with the armed messenger from Burlington at Fort Ethan Allen, and one from Montpelier and Barre at the St. John field.

Synset Examples

the engine is idling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - to move without power or propulsion

Etymology

From Old English īdel (empty, useless, vain).
