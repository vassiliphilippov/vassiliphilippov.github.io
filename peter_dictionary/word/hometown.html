hometown

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌtaʊn / (BrE)
The town or city where you were born, grew up, or feel a strong personal connection to as your place of origin.

Examples

Their own easier, slower tempo is especially dear to Southerners; and I have heard many say that they are content to earn a half or a third as much as they could up North because they so much prefer the quieter habits of their home town.

Synset Examples

he never went back to his hometown again

Etymology

Compound of "home" and "town"; first recorded in mid-19th century American English.
