takeoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɒf / (BrE)
the action of an aircraft becoming airborne; the moment of leaving the ground during flight

Examples

The control tower gave him immediate take-off permission, and the clean roar of the engine that took him off the rough strip spoke well of the skill of Donovan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landing - the act of an aircraft returning to the ground

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'take off.'
