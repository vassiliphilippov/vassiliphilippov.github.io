impinge

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪndʒ / (BrE)
to have an effect, especially a negative one, on something or to encroach upon someone's rights or territory

Examples

Indian ghosts would not impinge upon his nights, nor would his days be haunted by the dimly-outlined, ill-conceived figure of her benighted ancestor.

Synset Examples

This impinges on my rights as an individual
This matter entrenches on other domains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infringe - to violate, encroach upon, or go beyond recognized boundaries
  • Synonym: intrude - to enter without permission or welcome, especially in a way that encroaches

Etymology

From Latin 'impingere', from 'in-' (against) + 'pangere' (to fix, drive).
