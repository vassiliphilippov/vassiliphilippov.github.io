pictorial

1. ADJ

/ pɪkˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(especially of language or writing) creating vivid images in the mind, as if painting a picture.

Examples

It appears that the dominant tendency of Mann's early tales, however pictorial or even picturesque the surface, is already toward the symbolic, the emblematic, the expressionistic.

Synset Examples

pictorial poetry and prose
graphic accounts of battle
a lifelike portrait
a vivid description

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphic - giving a clear and effective picture; vivid
  • Synonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind

Etymology

From Latin pictorius (of painting), from pictor (painter).

2. ADJ

/ pɪkˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to, expressed in, or consisting of pictures or illustrations.

Examples

It resembles, too, pictures such as Du^rer and Bruegel did, in which all that looks at first to be solely pictorial proves on inspection to be also literary, the representation of a proverb, for example, or a deadly sin.
Flatness may now monopolize everything, but it is a flatness become so ambiguous and expanded as to turn into illusion itself - at least an optical if not, properly speaking, a pictorial illusion.
The affixed elements of collage were extruded, as it were, and cut off from the literal pictorial surface to form a bas-relief.

Synset Examples

pictorial perspective
pictorial records

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustrative - serving as an illustration or example, closely related meaning

Etymology

From Latin pictorius (of a painter), from pictor (painter).
