2. ADV

/ ˈhɛvɪli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvɪli / (BrE)
In a heavy-footed manner; moving with great weight or force, often in a clumsy or noisy way.

Examples

He clomped heavily up the stoop and rang the bell.
He brushed by the idiotic boy and lumbered heavily up the stairs.
A shot caught him and straightened him up in screaming pain; a following volley of shots shattered glass, ripped the ceiling, and sent him lurching heavily from the table.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightly - in a manner that is not heavy or forceful, with little weight or effort

Etymology

From heavy + -ly, formed in Middle English.
