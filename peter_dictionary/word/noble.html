noble

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbl̩ / (BrE)
impressive in appearance; having a grand, dignified, or majestic aspect.

Examples

Not the noblest performance we have heard him play, or the most spacious, or even the most eloquent.
Heavy coated, severe looking policemen sat astride noble horses along the curbside to prevent the revellers from spilling out in front of the crawling traffic.
It celebrates the unknown architect who designed the temple of Neptune at Paestum, next to the Parthenon the noblest example of Grecian classic style now in existence.

Synset Examples

a baronial mansion
an imposing residence
a noble tree
severe-looking policemen sat astride noble horses
stately columns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stately - having a dignified, majestic appearance or manner
  • Synonym: imposing - grand and impressive in appearance
  • Antonym: humble - having a modest or unpretentious appearance

Etymology

From Latin nobilis (well-known, notable, high-born), originally meaning distinguished or renowned, later also extended to impressive appearance.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
having or showing high moral qualities or elevated character

Examples

It seems to me that the first human being to reach one of these planets may well learn what it is to be a truly great and noble species ".

Synset Examples

a noble spirit
noble deeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honorable - deserving of respect or high regard for moral qualities
  • Antonym: base - lacking moral principles; ignoble

Etymology

From Latin nobilis (well-known, famous, notable).

3. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
Of or relating to the hereditary aristocracy, especially as derived from feudal times.

Examples

Thomas Douglas, fifth Earl of Selkirk, a noble humanitarian Scot concerned with the plight of the crofters of his native Highlands, conceived a plan to settle them in the valley of the Red River of the North.
In September 1817 at Fort Daer (Pembina) Dickson met the noble lord whom, with the help of a band of Sioux, he escorted to Prairie du Chien.

Synset Examples

of noble birth

Etymology

From Latin nobilis (well-known, notable, of noble birth).
