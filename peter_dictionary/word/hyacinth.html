hyacinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.əˌsɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.əsɪnθ / (BrE)
A red or orange-yellow variety of zircon used as a gemstone.

Examples

In a week the hyacinths would spike out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zircon - a mineral used as a gemstone, often colorless but may be red, brown, or other colors

Etymology

From Latin *hyacinthus*, from Greek *hyakinthos*, originally denoting the plant but later used for the gemstone's color resemblance.
