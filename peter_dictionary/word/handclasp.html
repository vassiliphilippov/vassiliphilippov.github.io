handclasp

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌklæsp / (AmE), / ˈhændˌklɑːsp / (BrE)
the act of grasping and shaking another person's hand, typically as a greeting or to signify agreement

Examples

He disliked Garth on sight, conservative clothes and haircut, smile a shade too earnestly boyish for a man who must be well into his thirties, handclasp too consciously quick and firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handshake - an act of grasping and shaking hands as a greeting or agreement

Etymology

Compound of 'hand' and 'clasp', meaning to grasp or hold tightly.
