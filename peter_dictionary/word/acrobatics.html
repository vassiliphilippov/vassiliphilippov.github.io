acrobatics

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (AmE), / ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (BrE)
Physical exercises or movements involving balance, agility, and coordination, often performed by an acrobat as part of gymnastic or circus displays.

Examples

The backbend is of extreme importance to any form of free gymnastics, and, as with all acrobatics, the sooner begun the better the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gymnastics - exercises developing or displaying physical agility and coordination

Etymology

From French *acrobatie*, based on Greek *akrobatēs* (walking on tiptoe).
