enlightenment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlaɪtnmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtnmənt / (BrE)
The state of having gained knowledge or understanding, especially through education or experience; the spreading and acquisition of knowledge.

Examples

A contrast of the scripture reading of, let us say, St. Augustine, John Bunyan, and Thomas Jefferson, all three of whom found in such study a real source of enlightenment, can tell us a great deal about these three men and the age that each represented and helped bring to conscious expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of acquiring knowledge and skills through learning
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From the verb 'enlighten' + '-ment'; 'enlighten' from Old English 'inlihtan', influenced by Old French 'enleitter'.
