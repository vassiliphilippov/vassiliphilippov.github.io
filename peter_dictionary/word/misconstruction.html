misconstruction

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
An ungrammatical expression or arrangement of words in a sentence.

Examples

There are many grammatical misconstructions other than dangling modifiers and anatomicals which permit two different interpretations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solecism - a grammatical mistake in speech or writing
  • Antonym: construction - grammatical arrangement of words in a sentence

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + construction, modeled on similar grammatical terms.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
a misunderstanding or incorrect interpretation of words or actions, often occurring deliberately

Examples

They must do something with the acquiescence of the latter, or some of them, which amounts to an acceptance of the law in its entirety beyond all possibility of misconstruction ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misinterpretation - a wrong or mistaken interpretation
  • Antonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'construction' (interpretation); compare similar pattern in 'misconstrue'.
