mince

1. VERB

/ mɪns / (AmE), / mɪns / (BrE)
to soften or moderate one's words, especially to be less direct or blunt

Examples

But State Party Chairman James Gray of Albany said no, and he did n't mince any words.
Never given to mincing words, he places heavy blame upon the faulty, uncourageous leadership of Britain and particularly America.

Synset Examples

He moderated his tone when the students burst out in tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - to make less extreme or severe
  • Synonym: soften - to make less harsh or forceful
  • Antonym: blunt - to speak in a direct, uncompromising way

Etymology

From Old French *mincier* (to chop finely), from Vulgar Latin *minutiare* (to make small), related to 'minute' (small).
