tailback

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌbæk / (BrE)
(In American football) a player who lines up farthest behind the line of scrimmage and is typically the team's primary ball carrier.

Examples

Tailback James Saxton already has surpassed his rushing total for his brilliant sophomore season, when he netted 271 yards on 55 carries; he now has 273 yards in 22 tries during three games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfback - a backfield player in American football, sometimes synonymous with tailback
  • Antonym: fullback - a backfield player who lines up closer to the line of scrimmage than the tailback, often used for blocking

Etymology

From 'tail' + 'back', referring to the player's position at the back (tail) of the offensive formation.
