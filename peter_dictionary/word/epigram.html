epigram

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪɡram / (BrE)
A short and witty saying or poem that expresses an idea in a clever and amusing way.

Examples

It was not even in writing Latin epigrams, sometimes bawdy ones, or in translating Lucian from Greek into Latin or in defending the study of Greek against the attack of conservative academics, or in attacking the conservative theologians who opposed Erasmus's philological study of the New Testament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a general truth or principle
  • Synonym: quip - a clever or witty remark
  • Synonym: maxim - a short, pithy statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct

Etymology

From Greek *epigramma* (an inscription), from *epigraphein* (to write on).
