predilection

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛdəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a preference or special liking for something; a bias in favor of something

Examples

There is no apparent reason why we should feel bound by Swadesh's rules and procedure since his predilections and aims have grown so vast.

Synset Examples

a predilection for expensive cars
his sexual preferences
showed a Marxist orientation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one thing over another

Etymology

From French 'prédilection', from Latin 'praediligere' (to prefer), from 'prae-' (before) + 'diligere' (to choose, love).
