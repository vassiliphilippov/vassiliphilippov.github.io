underwrite

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈraɪt / (BrE)
to guarantee financial support for; to promise to finance or support something, especially a project, event, or activity

Examples

Are you underwriting expensive team trips?
Mr. Richard Preston, executive director of the New Hampshire State Planning and Development Commission, in his remarks to the Governors Conference on Industrial Development at Providence on October 8, 1960, warned against the fallacy of attempting to attract industry solely to reduce the tax rate or to underwrite municipal services such as schools when he said: "If this is the fundamental reason for a community's interest or if this is the basic approach, success if any will be difficult to obtain ".

Synset Examples

The opera tour was subvented by a bank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponsor - to support financially or by providing resources, especially for an event or activity
  • Synonym: subsidize - to support financially, especially by government or organization

Etymology

From Old English 'underwritan' (to write beneath or sign at the end), later extended in use regarding financial guarantees.

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈraɪt / (BrE)
to accept liability for financial loss or risk, especially by providing insurance or guaranteeing funding for a project or event

Examples

He said legislation for a $ 60 million bond issue to underwrite the program is expected to be introduced Monday.

Synset Examples

The insurance won't cover this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insure - to provide or obtain insurance against loss or harm

Etymology

From Old English 'underwritan', meaning 'to write under, subscribe', later used in financial sense of accepting risk by signing one's name.
