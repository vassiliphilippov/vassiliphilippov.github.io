retrial

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌtraɪəl / (AmE), / ˈriːˌtraɪəl / (BrE)
a second trial of a case in court, usually ordered when the first trial was invalid or unfair

Examples

With Tommy's testimony, I can get a new trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a formal examination of evidence in court

Etymology

From re- (again) + trial (from Anglo-Norman, based on Old French trial, from trier 'to try').
