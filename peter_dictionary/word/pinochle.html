pinochle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌnʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːnɒkəl / (BrE)
A card game for two or more players played with a deck of forty-eight cards (two of each suit for high cards), in which players score points by forming combinations of cards and by winning tricks; similar to whist.

Examples

- Sollozzo would lose our ass. - What about the negotiator? - He's playing pinochle with my men.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whist - a trick-taking card game

Etymology

From German 'Pinochle', variant of 'Binokel', from French 'binoque' (eyeglass), originally referring to a pair in the game.
