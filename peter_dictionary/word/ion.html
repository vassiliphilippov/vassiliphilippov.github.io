ion

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
an atom or molecule with an electric charge, having gained or lost one or more electrons

Examples

Etymology

From Greek *ion* (going), from *ienai* (to go), indicating movement; adopted in 19th-century scientific terminology.
