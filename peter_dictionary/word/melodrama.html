melodrama

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛləˌdrɑmə / (AmE), / ˈmɛləˌdrɑːmə / (BrE)
A dramatic or literary work characterized by exaggerated characters and exciting events intended to appeal to the emotions, often with a clear distinction between good and evil.

Examples

The Breeze And Chancellor Neitzbohr, a movie melodrama that concerns the attempts of a West German politician to woo a plaster cast of the Apollo Belvedere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drama - a play for theater, radio, or television
  • Antonym: tragedy - a serious drama with a sorrowful or disastrous conclusion

Etymology

From French *mélodrame*, from Greek *melos* (song) + French *drame* (drama).
