chronicle

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɑː.nɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɒ.nɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to record a series of events in the order in which they occurred, especially as part of a historical account

Examples

Another is a wily countryman called Larkin, whose blandly boisterous progress has been chronicled, I believe, in earlier volumes of Mr. Bates' comedie humaine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to set down in writing or some other permanent form for later reference

Etymology

From Old French chronique, from Latin chronica (annals), from Greek khronika, from khronos (time).
