provocative

1. ADJ

/ prəˈvɑkətɪv / (AmE), / prəˈvɒkətɪv / (BrE)
Causing thought, excitement, or strong reaction, especially by arousing interest, discussion, or controversy.

Examples

Cause of all this commotion: squat, pug-nosed, balding, hopelessly ugly Jean-Pierre Bravado, a Bogartian figure, who plays a sadistic, amoral, philosophic Tasti-Freeze salesman in old New-Waver Fredrico de Mille Rossilini's endlessly provocative film, A Sour Sponge.

Synset Examples

a provocative remark
a provocative smile
provocative Irish tunes which...compel the hearers to dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulating - making someone feel interested and excited
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin provocativus (calling forth), from provocare (to call forth, challenge).
