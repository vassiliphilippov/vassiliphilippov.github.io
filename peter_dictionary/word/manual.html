manual

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈmænjuəl / (BrE)
A small book or handbook that gives practical instructions on how to do or use something.

Examples

Marriage manuals and women's magazine articles began to stress the importance of the female climax.
All the manuals for setting up vocational courses stress the importance of first making a local survey of skill needs, of estimating the growth of local jobs, and of consulting with local employers on the types of courses and their content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handbook - a book giving information or instructions about a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin 'manualis' (of the hand), from 'manus' (hand); originally meant 'relating to the hand,' later used for handbooks.
