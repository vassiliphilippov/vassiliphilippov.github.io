emanate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛm.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.ə.neɪt / (BrE)
(of something perceived as intangible) to originate from or be produced by a source and spread out or issue forth.

Examples

Simms admired the raucous tales emanating from the backwoods, but he had himself social affiliations which would not allow him to approve them fully.

Synset Examples

Water emanates from this hole in the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: originate - to have a specified beginning; to arise or start
  • Synonym: proceed - to move, go, or come forward; to originate from

Etymology

From Latin emanare (to flow out, spring forth), from e- (out) + manare (to flow).
