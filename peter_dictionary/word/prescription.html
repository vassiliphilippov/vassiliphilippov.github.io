prescription

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
An instruction or rule that is officially given, especially one that is meant to be followed as a guide or regulation.

Examples

His prescription: hot and cold compresses to increase her absorption of water.
Religion has its own supernatural prescriptions that are at the same time codes of behavior for the here and now.

Synset Examples

I tried to follow her prescription for success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instruction - a direction or order, especially one given by someone in authority
  • Antonym: proscription - the action of forbidding something; prohibition

Etymology

From Latin praescriptio (an order, direction), from praescribere (to write beforehand).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
A medicine that can be obtained only with written instructions from a doctor or dentist to a pharmacist.

Examples

The pretence was that he was delivering a prescription from Dr. Verdi.

Synset Examples

he told the doctor that he had been taking his prescription regularly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: over-the-counter - medicine available without a doctor's prescription

Etymology

From Latin praescriptio (an order, direction), from praescribere (to write before).

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
A written order by a physician or dentist instructing a pharmacist to supply a specific medicine and dosage to a particular patient.

Examples

And he'’s gonna give you a prescription.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prescribe - to authorize the use of a medicine by a medical order

Etymology

From Latin praescriptio (an order, writing at the beginning), from praescribere (to write before).
