showpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌpis / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌpiːs / (BrE)
An outstanding or main item, often displayed as the best example or highlight in a collection or exhibition.

Examples

Working in a vacuum of minimal information can result only in show pieces that look good in exhibitions and catalogs and may please the public relations department but have little to do with the essence of interior design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerpiece - the most important item in a display or event

Etymology

From 'show' + 'piece', originally meaning something to be shown; first used in the early 18th century.
