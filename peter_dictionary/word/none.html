none

1. ADV

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
Used as an adverb in phrases such as 'none too' to mean 'not at all' or 'only slightly'.

Examples

This was my second day in Vientiane, the administrative capital of Laos, and my thoughts were none too brave.
- No, not at all. - It's an old habit.

Synset Examples

seemed none too pleased with his dinner
shirt looked none the worse for having been slept in
none too prosperous
the passage is none too clear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not at all - in no way; not in any degree

Etymology

From Old English nān (not one, none), from ne (not) + ān (one).
