broadcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːst / (BrE)
a radio or television program that is sent out for reception by the public

Examples

(The first Metropolitan Opera broadcast on Dec. 9 will introduce her as Lucia.)
It probably will be necessary to install an outside antenna, particularly to receive CONELRAD broadcasts.
A record should be kept of the information and instructions, including the time and date of broadcast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmission - the process of sending out a radio or television signal or program

Etymology

From broad + cast, originally referring to the spreading of seed over a wide area; applied in the early 20th century to radio transmissions.

2. VERB

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːst / (BrE)
to transmit (a program or information) by radio or television

Examples

The information is furnished by each of the guests, is sent by oral broadcasting over the air waves, and is received by the ears.
The description of the car was immediately broadcast throughout Southern Maryland on police radio.
Why don't you broadcast it?

Synset Examples

We cannot air this X-rated song

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air - to broadcast by radio or television
  • Synonym: transmit - to send out (a signal or program) electronically

Etymology

Formed from 'broad' + 'cast'; originally referred to scattering seeds widely, later applied to radio communication in the 1920s.
