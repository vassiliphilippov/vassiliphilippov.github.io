asset

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɛt / (AmE), / ˈæsɛt / (BrE)
A useful or valuable quality, skill, or resource that gives an advantage.

Examples

Thus, besides the training provided to youth in school, the existence of the school program can have supplementary benefits to industry which make it an asset to industrial development efforts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liability - a person or thing whose presence or behavior is likely to cause embarrassment or put one at a disadvantage
  • Synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable position

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman assetz, from Old French assez (enough, sufficient).
