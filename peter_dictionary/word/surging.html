surging

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɝːdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
(of waves, crowds, or other things) moving forcefully or in a swelling, rolling manner.

Examples

Or the surging whirling sounds of bats at night, when their black bodies dived into the blackness above and below the amber street lights.

Synset Examples

billowy storm clouds
the restless billowing sea
surging waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: billowy - swelling with waves or billows

Etymology

Present participle of 'surge', from Latin 'surgere' (to rise).
