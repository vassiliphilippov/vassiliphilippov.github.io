nightmare

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌmɛr / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌmɛə / (BrE)
A frightening or very unpleasant dream that wakes you up or causes distress.

Examples

It was like finally getting into one's own nightmares to punish one's dreams.
The old nightmare which had caused him so many wakeful hours came charging in on him once more, only this time he could n't pacify it with a sleeping pill and send it away.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dream - a sequence of thoughts, images, or emotions during sleep

Etymology

From Old English 'niht' (night) + 'mare' (evil spirit or goblin believed to torment sleepers), influenced by folklore.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌmɛr / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌmɛə / (BrE)
An extremely unpleasant or difficult situation, resembling the terror or distress of a bad dream.

Examples

There was no use wandering off into a territory of utter nightmare.
The search goes on through Gotham's shopping nightmare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordeal - a painful or horrific experience, especially a prolonged one
  • Antonym: dream - a series of thoughts, images, or emotions occurring during sleep; an aspiration or wish

Etymology

From Middle English 'night' + 'mare', from Old English 'mare' (goblin, incubus)
