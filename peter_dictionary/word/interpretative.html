interpretative

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɝprətətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprətətɪv / (BrE)
Providing or relating to the interpretation of something; serving to explain or clarify meaning.

Examples

I, for one, rather regret that Schnabel did n't collaborate with the Budapest Quartet, whose rugged, athletic playing was a good deal closer to this pianist's interpretative outlook than the style of the Belgian group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretive - alternative spelling, having the same meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'interpretativus', from 'interpretari' (to explain, interpret).
