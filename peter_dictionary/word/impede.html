impede

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpid / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːd / (BrE)
To delay or prevent the progress or movement of someone or something by obstructing them; to be a hindrance to.

Examples

Progress is impeded by psychological inhibitions to effective action among those in power and by a failure on their part to understand how local resources, human and material, can be mobilized to achieve the national goals of modernization already symbolically accepted.
So Enright's courting of the mettlesome Ellen is impeded considerably, thereby providing the tale which is told.

Synset Examples

She is impeding the progress of our project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to create difficulties for someone or something, resulting in delay or obstruction
  • Synonym: obstruct - to block or get in the way of something
  • Antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier

Etymology

From Latin impedire, from 'in-' (in) + 'pes, pedis' (foot), literally 'to shackle the feet'.

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpid / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːd / (BrE)
to slow down or prevent the movement, progress, or action of something or someone

Examples

Shayne stepped back to let him slump to the ground, and then dived over him through the open door into Harris who was cursing loudly and trying to drag a gun from a shoulder holster, somewhat impeded by the steering wheel.

Synset Examples

obstruct the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen
  • Synonym: obstruct - to block or prevent passage or progress
  • Antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier

Etymology

From Latin 'impedire', from 'in-' (in) + 'pes, ped-' (foot).
