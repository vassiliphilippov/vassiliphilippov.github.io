rendering

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛndərɪŋ / (BrE)
an interpretation or representation of a work of art, music, or literature, usually presented through a performance or artistic medium

Examples

More than a beautiful visualization of the illustrious adventures and escapades of the tragi-comic knight-errant and his squire, Sancho Panza, in seventeenth century Spain, this inevitably abbreviated rendering of the classic satire on chivalry is an affectingly warm and human exposition of character.

Synset Examples

her rendition of Milton's verse was extraordinarily moving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendition - an interpretation or performance of a musical or dramatic piece

Etymology

From Old French 'rendre' (to give back), from Latin 'reddere'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛndərɪŋ / (BrE)
a performance or interpretation of a musical composition, dramatic role, or work of art.

Examples

But then, after the little operetta had been given its feeble amateur rendering, everyone insisted that it was too good to be lost forever, and that the Royal Academy of Music must now have the manuscript in order to give it the really first-rate performance it merited.
An unusual, if not extraordinary, rendering of the classic myth that involves the rescue of Prometheus from the rock by the U.S. Cavalry was given last week in the warehouse of the Albany Leather Conduit Company amid cheers of "Hubba hubba "and "Yalagaloo pip pip "!

Synset Examples

they heard a live rendition of three pieces by Schubert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a play, concert, or other form of entertainment
  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of performing or explaining the meaning of a work

Etymology

From the verb 'render' (to perform, present), from Old French 'rendre' (to give back), from Latin 'reddere'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛn.dɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛn.dər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
an explanation or interpretation of something that is not immediately obvious

Examples

Usually the work studied offers us a special or even an individualized rendering or treatment of the ideas in question, so that the student finds it necessary to distinguish carefully between the several expressions of an "- ism "or mode of thought.

Synset Examples

the edict was subject to many interpretations
he annoyed us with his interpreting of parables
often imitations are extended to provide a more accurate rendition of the child's intended meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - an explanation or way of explaining
  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear

Etymology

From the verb 'render', ultimately from Old French 'rendre' (to give back) and Latin 'reddere' (to give back, return).
