quadrupling

1. NOUN

/ kwɑˈdrup.lɪŋ / (AmE), / kwɒˈdruː.plɪŋ / (BrE)
an act or instance of increasing something by four times its original amount or number

Examples

If, in a certain part of the range, it starts life 1 foot longer than do any of the other (relatively large) giants, and reaches maturity at, let us guess, 18 inches longer than the others, a quadrupling of the maturity length would result in a maximum of (nearly) 40 feet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: doubling - an act of making something twice as much
  • Degree Lower: tripling - an act of increasing something by three times
  • Degree Higher: quintupling - an act of increasing something by five times

Etymology

From quadruple (from Latin 'quadruplus', meaning fourfold) + -ing suffix forming nouns denoting an action or result.
