disruption

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɹʌp.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌp.ʃən / (BrE)
An interruption or disturbance in the normal course or continuity of something.

Examples

The provisions are also designed to avoid disruption in State programs already in operation, which might otherwise result from the allotment of funds on the basis of wealth and population alone.

Synset Examples

it was presented without commercial breaks
there was a gap in his account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - a pause or break in continuity
  • Antonym: continuity - the unbroken and consistent existence or operation of something

Etymology

From Latin disruptio, from disrumpere meaning 'to break apart'.
