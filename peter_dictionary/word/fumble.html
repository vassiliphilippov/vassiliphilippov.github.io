fumble

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
(in sports, especially American football) an act of dropping or failing to hold onto the ball while playing.

Examples

And he caused the fumble that set up our touchdown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnover - loss of possession to the opposing team in sports

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian *fumla* (to handle clumsily).

2. VERB

/ ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
to use the hands clumsily while searching for or handling something, especially without being able to see clearly or when nervous or uncertain

Examples

Winston watched her fumbling to untie her apron.
Hoag stretched his left hand to the wall and fumbled for the switch: evil flourishes in the dark.
Clyde Miller was crying softly to himself, shedding his striped suit and fumbling into the nondescript butternut pants, the worn brown shirt.

Synset Examples

She groped for her glasses in the darkness of the bedroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - to search for something by feeling with the hands, typically in the dark

Etymology

Probably from Middle English fumblen, frequentative of fumen, to handle awkwardly.

3. VERB

/ ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
to move or make one's way clumsily or blindly, often by groping or feeling for support

Examples

If, as she walked, her steps fumbled from time to time, she chose to ignore that omen.

Synset Examples

He fumbled towards the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - feel about or search blindly or uncertainly with the hands

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly imitative or related to the Middle English 'fumblen' (to grope, handle clumsily).

4. VERB

/ ˈfʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌm.bəl / (BrE)
To handle or move something in a clumsy or awkward way, often losing control of it.

Examples

[PRIEST SPEAKING LATIN] [KEYS JINGLING] [EXHALES DEEPLY] [FUMBLING WITH LOCK] [FIREWORKS EXPLODING] [CROWD CHEERING] [SPEAKING ITALIAN]

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mishandle - to manage or deal with something in a wrong or clumsy way
  • Synonym: drop - to let something fall accidentally due to losing grip

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *fumblen*, perhaps frequentative of Old Norse *fumbla* (to walk unsteadily).
