disconcertingly

1. ADV

/ ˌdɪskənˈsɝːtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈsɜːtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes unease, confusion, or embarrassment.

Examples

Again, I at first misconstrued this disconcertingly intense communication, and I quickly cast through my mind to account for her being able to speak, with such utter conviction, of an opinion held by my father, now several years deceased.

Synset Examples

he drank some sherry, his eyes disconcertingly keen as he watched her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbingly - in a way that causes worry or anxiety

Etymology

From 'disconcert' + '-ing' + '-ly', with 'disconcert' from French 'déconcerter'.
