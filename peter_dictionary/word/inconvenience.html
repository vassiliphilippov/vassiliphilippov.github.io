inconvenience

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kənˈviːn.jəns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviːn.i.əns / (BrE)
A state or instance of causing trouble, difficulty, or discomfort.

Examples

"Litigants and witnesses were put to the expense and inconvenience of going long distances to transact business; public money spent; justice delayed; nothing accomplished, and the whole distribution of justice in this county seems to be an absolute farce ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenience - freedom from trouble or difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'inconveniens' (unsuitable), from 'in-' (not) + 'conveniens' (suitable, fitting).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kənˈviːn.jəns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviːn.jəns / (BrE)
A state or instance of causing trouble, difficulty, or discomfort.

Examples

This is awkward for everybody, is n't it, we all got to put up with inconvenience sometimes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenience - the state of being able to proceed with little effort or difficulty

Etymology

From Late Latin inconveniens (unsuitable, inappropriate), from Latin in- (not) + conveniens (agreeing, suitable).
