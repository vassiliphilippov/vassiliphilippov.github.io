walkout

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk.aʊt / (BrE)
A strike in which workers leave their workplace as a form of protest.

Examples

This was the first word from Jensen on his sudden walkout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - a protest in which workers stop working to express a grievance or demand
  • Antonym: lockout - when employers prevent workers from entering the workplace during a dispute

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'walk out', used in labor contexts since the early 20th century.
