a fortiori

1. ADV

/ ˌɑː fɔːrʃiˈɔːraɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑː fɔːtɪˈɔːraɪ / (BrE)
used to indicate that a conclusion applies even more strongly to another case; with greater reason or certainty.

Examples

But, as Scripture everywhere reminds us, God does have need of his creatures, and the church, a fortiori, can ill afford to do without the talents with which the world, by God's providence, presents it.

Synset Examples

if you are wrong then, a fortiori, so am I

Etymology

From Latin, literally ‘from the stronger (argument)’.
