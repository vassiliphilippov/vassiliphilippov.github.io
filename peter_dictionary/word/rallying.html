rallying

1. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of coming together or causing people to come together to support a common cause or purpose.

Examples

This comes out in "When the Bells Ring ", which describes the rallying of the peasants in southern Sweden to repel an invasion by the Danes.

Synset Examples

the bell was a signal for the rallying of the whole neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathering for a purpose
  • Antonym: dispersal - the act of scattering or breaking up a group

Etymology

From the verb 'rally', itself from French 'rallier' (to reunite).
