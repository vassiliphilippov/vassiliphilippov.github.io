tile

1. NOUN

/ taɪl / (AmE), / taɪl / (BrE)
A flat, thin, usually rectangular slab made from materials such as fired clay, ceramic, stone, or synthetic substances, used for covering roofs, floors, or walls.

Examples

The fireplace had tiles made for Mrs. Monmouth by Picasso himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slab - a broad, flat, thick piece of stone, wood, or other solid material

Etymology

From Old French *tuile*, from Latin *tegula* ‘roof tile’.
