kickback

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪkˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkɪkˌbæk / (BrE)
a secret payment made to someone, especially as a reward for facilitating a business deal or contract, typically illegal or unethical

Examples

Kickbacks on his kickbacks.
Kickbacks on his kickbacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - money or favor given to influence someone's conduct
  • Degree Lower: commission - payment to someone for facilitating a transaction, usually legal

Etymology

From 'kick' + 'back', originally referring to giving back a portion of money received.
