freeze

1. VERB

/ friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
to become suddenly motionless or stop moving, often due to fear, surprise, or hesitation

Examples

Related to written literature, and often remaining temporarily frozen in written form, it loses its vitality when transcribed or removed from its oral existence.
He ran on his plump sticks of legs, freezing now and again into the sudden startled attitudes which the camera had caught and held on the paling photographs, all carefully placed and glued and labeled, resting in the fat plush album in the bottom drawer of the escritoire.
In life we learn to play our roles and we "freeze "into patterns which become so habitual that we are not really aware of what we do.
The sergeant froze.
Freeze!

Synset Examples

When he saw the police car he froze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halt - to stop moving or come to a stop
  • Antonym: move - to change position or go from one place to another

Etymology

From Old English 'frēosan' (to freeze), from Proto-Germanic '*freusaną'.

2. VERB

/ friz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to turn into ice or another solid as a result of extreme cold.

Examples

Recounting personal observations of clearance work, the Councilman cited instances of inefficient use of equipment or supplies by poorly trained workers and urged that plow blades be set so they do not leave behind a thin layer of snow which eventually freezes.

Insects there were on the world, but they were frozen solid during the night, only to revive and thaw in the morning sun.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melt - to change from a solid to a liquid by heating

Etymology

From Old English 'frēosan', of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'vriezen', German 'frieren', from an Indo-European root shared by Latin 'pruina' (frost).

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
to officially stop or suspend a process, activity, or habit, especially by imposing restrictions or a temporary ban

Examples

Again and again it asked the Communists to "freeze "the military situation in Laos.

Synset Examples

Suspend the aid to the war-torn country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspend - to temporarily prevent from continuing or being in force or effect
  • Synonym: halt - to bring or come to a stop
  • Antonym: unfreeze - to remove restrictions or allow to resume after a freeze

Etymology

From Old English 'frēosan' (to freeze) via Old Norse 'frjósa'. Extension of the physical sense to abstract inactivity developed in the 20th century.

5. VERB

/ friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
to make (something) become hard or solid because of very cold temperatures; to cause to become frozen

Examples

There is a trend to packaging meat at a central source, freezing it, and shipping it to outlying stores, where meat cutters will not be required.

Synset Examples

Freeze the leftover food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thaw - to make or become liquid or soft after being frozen

Etymology

From Old English *frēosan* (to turn to ice), from Proto-Germanic *freusaną*.
