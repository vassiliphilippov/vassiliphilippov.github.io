glean

1. VERB

/ ɡlin / (AmE), / ɡliːn / (BrE)
to gather (items or information) gradually and laboriously from various sources, especially what is left after the main part has been collected

Examples

The material is all basically of folk origin, gleaned from every section of Poland.
Who but Thurber can be counted on to glean such nectareous essences?

Synset Examples

harvest the grapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest - to gather a crop; to collect the results of a season's growth

Etymology

From Old French glener, from Late Latin glennare, meaning 'to glean (collect ears of corn)', from Latin glennare.
