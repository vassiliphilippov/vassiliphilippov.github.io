chip

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
A small fragment or piece that has been broken off from a larger whole.

Examples

The silver chloride was fused under vacuum in the presence of aluminum chips with the resultant product of * * f which was sublimed into a flask on the vacuum line.

Synset Examples

a bit of rock caught him in the eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or detached
  • Synonym: shard - a broken piece of a brittle material, especially ceramic or glass
  • Degree Higher: chunk - a thick, solid piece of something
  • Degree Lower: sliver - a thin, narrow piece cut or split off from something

Etymology

From Old English 'cipp', meaning a small piece of wood.

2. VERB

/ tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
to break off a small piece from the edge or surface of something, especially as a result of a blow or damage

Examples

Miss Pulova has a voice that Maria Callas once described as "like chipping teeth with a screw driver ", and her round, opalescent face becomes fascinatingly reflective of the emotions demanded by the role of Rosalie.

Synset Examples

Her tooth chipped

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crack - to break without separating into pieces
  • Degree Higher: shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces

Etymology

Possibly from Old English 'cipp' (small piece of wood).

3. VERB

/ tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
to cut or break a small piece from the edge or surface of something

Examples

From there he chipped back and sank his putt for a par 4.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nick - to make a small cut or notch in
  • Synonym: notch - to make a small cut shaped like a V

Etymology

From Old English *cippian* (to split, chip).

4. NOUN

/ tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
a small, flat, disk-shaped token used in gambling to represent a certain amount of money

Examples

Draw chips for everybody, so they can play on the house.
