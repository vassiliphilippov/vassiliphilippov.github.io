sodden

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑdən / (AmE), / ˈsɒdən / (BrE)
Thoroughly soaked with liquid; saturated and dripping wet.

Examples

The discarded papers inside were sodden, there was a glint of liquid at the bottom, and the smell of whisky was strong and distinct.
They cringed under sodden covers, listening to the waves slop against the bottom.

Synset Examples

stood at the door drenched (or soaked) by the rain
the speaker's sodden collar
soppy clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaked - thoroughly wet, saturated with liquid
  • Synonym: drenched - completely wet through

Etymology

Old English *sodden*, past participle of *seethe* (to boil).
