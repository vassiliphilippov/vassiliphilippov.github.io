ciliate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪeɪt / (BrE)
A single-celled microorganism of the phylum Ciliophora, characterized by the presence of hair-like structures called cilia used for movement and feeding.

Examples

Only once were stalked ciliates found in the mixed liquor.

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' (eyelash) plus '-ate', describing the presence of cilia.
