congestive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
relating to or affected by an excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a body part or organ, especially as a medical condition

Examples

Later, chlorothiazide and salt restriction became necessary to control the edema of chronic congestive failure.

Synset Examples

congestive heart disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congestion - the state of being congested; excessive accumulation of fluid or blood

Etymology

From Latin 'congestus', past participle of 'congerere' (to heap together), via Old French and Middle English.
