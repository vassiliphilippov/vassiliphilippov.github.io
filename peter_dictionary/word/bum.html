bum

1. NOUN

/ bʌm / (AmE), / bʌm / (BrE)
A person regarded as despicable or contemptible.

Examples

To them bums?
"Hit the bum "! somebody yelled from the Anniston bench.

Synset Examples

only a rotter would do that
kill the rat
throw the bum out
you cowardly little pukes!
the British call a contemptible person a `git'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotter - a person who is regarded as despicable or contemptible
  • Synonym: rat - an unpleasant or treacherous person
  • Synonym: git - a contemptible person (British slang)

2. NOUN

/ bʌm / (AmE), / bʌm / (BrE)
a person who has no permanent home or employment, especially one who lives by begging or doing odd jobs; a vagrant or tramp

Examples

I had studied with Burns ten years before, during the scholarship year the Manhattan gave me, along with the five-hundred dollar prize for my paintings of bums on Hudson Street.
Sit down, you bum!

Synset Examples

a homeless tramp
he tried to help the really down-and-out bums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tramp - a person who travels from place to place on foot, especially as a vagrant or beggar

Etymology

Probably from German 'Bummler' (loafer).

3. VERB

/ bʌm / (AmE), / bʌm / (BrE)
(informal) To ask for and obtain something, especially for free; to cadge.

Examples

"And I bum tickets to everything I can ", she said.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadge - to ask for or obtain something for free, often repeatedly
  • Synonym: scrounge - to seek or obtain something by begging or borrowing without intending to return it
