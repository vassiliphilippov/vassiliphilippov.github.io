self-aggrandisement

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈɡrændɪzmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈɡrændʌɪzmənt / (BrE)
The act of promoting or enhancing one's own power, status, or importance, often excessively or boastfully.

Examples

In the past year there had been too many examples of his euphoric self-confidence and self aggrandisement for their liking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-promotion - the act of furthering one's own interests or publicizing oneself

Etymology

From self- + aggrandisement, from French aggrandissement, from aggrandir (to enlarge) + -ment.
