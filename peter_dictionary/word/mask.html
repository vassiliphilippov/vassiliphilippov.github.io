mask

1. NOUN

/ mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
A covering worn over all or part of the face to conceal the identity of the wearer or to protect the face.

Examples

He had had the need to understand what life lurked behind the mask of flesh, behind the oath, the banter, the sadness.
Masks!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - something worn to change one's appearance

Etymology

From Middle French masque, from Italian maschera (mask, masquerade), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
to put a mask on something or someone; to cover or conceal with a mask

Examples

Hastening to the attic, the temperature of which was easily hotter than the Gold Coast, you proceeded to mask the windows with a fancy wool coverlet, some khaki pants, and the like, and to ransack the innumerable boxes and barrels stored there.

Synset Examples

Mask the children for Halloween

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or upon, as for protection or concealment
  • Antonym: unmask - to remove a mask from; to reveal the true identity or nature of

Etymology

From Middle French *masque*, from Italian *maschera*, ultimately from Arabic *maskhara* (buffoon) or Latin *masca* (mask, witch).

3. NOUN

/ mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
A thing used to conceal or disguise the true nature or intentions of something; a facade.

Examples

The Kremlin's goal is the isolation and capture, not of Ghana, but of the United States - and this purpose may be served very well by countries that masquerade under a "neutralist "mask, yet in fact are dependable auxiliaries of the Soviet Foreign Office.

Synset Examples

no mask could conceal his ignorance
they moved in under a mask of friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Synonym: guise - an external form or appearance concealing the true nature of something

Etymology

From Middle French *masque*, from Italian *maschera*, from Medieval Latin *masca* (mask, specter).

4. VERB

/ mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
to hide one's true feelings or intentions by presenting a false appearance

Examples

That meeting produced the "spirit of Camp David "- a spirit, it later turned out, that masked a basic misunderstanding about progress toward a Berlin settlement.

Synset Examples

He masked his disappointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - to change appearance to hide identity or true feelings
  • Antonym: reveal - to make known or show

Etymology

From Middle French masque, from Italian maschera, meaning 'covering to hide the face'.
