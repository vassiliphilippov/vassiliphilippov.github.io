lavatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈlævəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈlævətəri / (BrE)
A room or building equipped with one or more toilets; a bathroom or washroom.

Examples

The doctor shot down to the lavatory and turned the doorknob, but to no effect: the lavatory was occupied.
The doctor shot down to the lavatory and turned the doorknob, but to no effect: the lavatory was occupied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toilet - room or fixture for urination/defecation
  • Synonym: restroom - public room with a toilet and sink
  • Synonym: bathroom - room containing a toilet and often a bath or shower

Etymology

From Latin 'lavatorium', from 'lavare' meaning 'to wash'.
