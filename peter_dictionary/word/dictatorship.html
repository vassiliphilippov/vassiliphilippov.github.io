dictatorship

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪkˈteɪtɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌdɪkˈteɪtəʃɪp / (BrE)
A form of government in which absolute power is concentrated in a dictator or a small clique, and political opposition is not permitted.

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.
This notion of the General Will gave rise to the Commune of Paris in the Revolution and later brought Napoleon to dictatorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocracy - government by one person with absolute power
  • Antonym: democracy - government by the people, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

Formed from 'dictator' + '-ship', patterned after other government terms like 'kingship'. 'Dictator' from Latin 'dictator', meaning 'one who dictates'.
