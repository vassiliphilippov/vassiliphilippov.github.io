amaze

1. VERB

/ əˈmeɪz / (AmE), / əˈmeɪz / (BrE)
to surprise someone very much; to fill with wonder or astonishment

Examples

He stared at it, amazed, alarmed.
You may amaze yourself and acquire a real knack for it.
Many a sitter (in a personal sitting) has been amazed to realize that the medium was describing very vividly his state of mind.
What amazes me most is you were taken in by it.

Synset Examples

Your ability to speak six languages amazes me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: stun - to shock or overwhelm with surprise
  • Degree Lower: impress - to have a strong positive effect on someone's mind

Etymology

From Middle English 'amased', past participle of 'amasen', from Old English 'amasian' (to confuse, stupefy).

2. VERB

/ əˈmeɪz / (AmE), / əˈmeɪz / (BrE)
to fill (someone) with great surprise or bewilderment; to astonish or perplex

Examples

Albert Einstein was quoted as saying: "The workings of the woman's mind amaze me ".

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me--I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonish - to greatly surprise; synonym for amaze
  • Synonym: perplex - to cause to feel baffled or confused
  • Synonym: bewilder - to cause someone to become confused

Etymology

From Middle English *amasen*, from Old English *āmasian* (to confuse, confound).
