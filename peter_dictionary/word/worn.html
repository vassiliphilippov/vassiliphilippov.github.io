worn

1. ADJ

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
Damaged or weakened by long use or repeated action; showing signs of wear.

Examples

His captain was thin and haggard and his beautiful boots were worn and shabby.
Worn through at the knees ".
All they found of him was a muddy set of prison clothes, a bar of soap and an old rock hammer damn near worn down to the nub.

Synset Examples

worn threads on the screw
a worn suit
the worn pockets on the jacket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new - recently made or unused, not worn

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'wear,' from Old English 'werian' (to wear, clothe oneself).
