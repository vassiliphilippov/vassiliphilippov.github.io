non-discrimination

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˌdɪˌskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌdɪˌskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The practice of treating all people fairly and equally, without prejudice or bias based on race, gender, age, or other characteristics.

Examples

And both in their objectives of non-discrimination and of social progress they have had ranged against them the Southerners who are called Bourbons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discrimination - unfair or prejudiced treatment of people based on group or category

Etymology

From non- (not) + discrimination (from Latin discriminare, to divide, distinguish).
