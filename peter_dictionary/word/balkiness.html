balkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlkinəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːkɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unwilling to proceed or cooperate, often characterized by abrupt stops or resistance

Examples

This could be told chiefly from a sort of head tossing and prancing, a horse like balkiness of demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - quality of being stubborn or uncooperative

Etymology

From 'balk', meaning to stop short or refuse to proceed, with suffix '-iness' forming a noun.
