extrapolation

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˌstræpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstræpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of estimating or calculating a value beyond the known range of data by assuming that existing trends continue.

Examples

The flux of micrometeorites in the neighborhood of the Earth can be estimated by extrapolation from radar and visual meteor data.
Extrapolation of this relationship through the thirtieth magnitude covers the range of micrometeorites.
The extrapolation may be in error for several reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interpolation - estimating within the range of known data points

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'polation', modeled on 'interpolation'.
