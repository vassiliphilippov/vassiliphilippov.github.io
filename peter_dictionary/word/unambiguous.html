unambiguous

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs / (BrE)
expressed or understood in a way that is clear and has only one possible meaning; not open to more than one interpretation.

Examples

Modern man, as Dietrich Bonhoeffer has told us, has "come of age "; and though this process by no means represents an unambiguous gain and is, in fact, marked by the estrangement from the depths that seems to be the cost of human maturation, it is still a positive step forward; and those of us who so richly benefit from it should be the last to despise it.
And patterns of stress are not always unambiguous by any means.

Synset Examples

As a horror, apartheid...is absolutely unambiguous

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguous - having more than one possible meaning; unclear

Etymology

From Latin 'ambiguus', meaning 'uncertain', with the prefix 'un-' giving a negative meaning.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs / (BrE)
(of language or information) clear and precise; not open to more than one interpretation.

Examples

Because of the simplicity of the molecules, isotopic exchange reactions between elemental halogens and the corresponding carbon tetrahalides would appear to offer particularly fruitful possibilities for obtaining unambiguous basic kinetic data.

Synset Examples

unequivocal evidence
took an unequivocal position
an unequivocal success
an unequivocal promise
an unequivocal (or univocal) statement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation
  • Synonym: unequivocal - leaving no doubt; unambiguous

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'ambiguous' (from Latin 'ambiguus', uncertain).
