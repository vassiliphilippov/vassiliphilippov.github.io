anxious

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋkʃəs / (AmE), / ˈæŋkʃəs / (BrE)
Wanting very much to do or have something, especially when showing eagerness or impatience.

Examples

A Mexican justice of the peace had issue a writ against Chavez for taking part in the "murder "of Manuel Gonzales so he and his father were anxious to be taken out of danger.
And how anxious this lover of obscurity was for applause!

Synset Examples

anxious to see the new show at the museum
dying to hear who won

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - strongly wanting to do or have something
  • Synonym: keen - very interested, eager, or wanting (to do) something very much

Etymology

From Latin anxius (troubled, uneasy), from angere (to choke, distress).
