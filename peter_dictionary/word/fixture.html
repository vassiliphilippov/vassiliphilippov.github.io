fixture

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪkstʃə / (BrE)
An object that is securely attached to a building and is considered a permanent part of it, especially in a household, such as a sink, light fitting, or cabinet.

Examples

Makes necessary purchases, places contracts, supervises construction, installation, finishing and placement of furniture, fixtures and other correlated furnishings, and follows through to completion of project ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fitting - an attachment or piece of equipment fixed in place, especially in a building

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus' (fastened, fixed), past participle of 'figere' (to fix).
