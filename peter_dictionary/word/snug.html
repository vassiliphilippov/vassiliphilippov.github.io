snug

1. ADJ

/ snʌɡ / (AmE), / snʌɡ / (BrE)
Providing safety, shelter, or concealment; well protected or hidden from danger or the elements.

Examples

If we manage to keep track of a Bombus queen after she has left her feeding place, we may discover the snug little hideout which she has fixed up for herself when she woke up from her winter sleep.

Synset Examples

a snug harbor
a snug hideout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safe - protected from or not exposed to danger or risk
  • Synonym: secure - free from danger or threat
  • Antonym: exposed - not protected or sheltered from danger

Etymology

Origin unknown; attested since the late 16th century.
