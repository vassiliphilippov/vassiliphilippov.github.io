antiserum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈsɪrəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈsɪərəm / (BrE)
A blood serum containing antibodies against specific antigens, used to provide immunity to a particular disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a blood protein produced in response to an antigen

Etymology

From anti- (against) + serum (from Latin 'serum', meaning whey or serum).
