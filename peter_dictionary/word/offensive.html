offensive

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a military or other organized campaign of attack against an enemy or opponent

Examples

The Pathet Lao forces held two northern provinces and openly took the offensive in three more.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defensive - a military or protective action meant to resist attack

Etymology

From French *offensive*, from Medieval Latin *offensiva* (attack), from Latin *offendere* (to strike against).

2. ADJ

/ əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / əˈfensɪv / (BrE)
Violating or tending to violate laws, rules, or accepted standards of conduct or morality.

Examples

The sole guidance given the Court for discharging the task committed to it was this: "The judgment must therefore be reversed and the cause remanded to the District Court for a determination, after further hearing, of the equitable relief necessary and appropriate in the public interest to eliminate the effects of the acquisition offensive to the statute.

Synset Examples

violative of the principles of liberty
considered such depravity offensive against all laws of humanity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissible - allowed, lawful, or permitted by rules or standards

Etymology

From Latin offendĕre (to strike against).
