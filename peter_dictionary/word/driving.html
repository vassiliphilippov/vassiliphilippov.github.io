driving

1. ADJ

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Having great force or energy; exerting a strong and active influence or impetus.

Examples

As for the ladies, they were full of charm, and sincerity, and deep and abiding affection for this hurrying driving, honest, little man.
I lived in this onward driving contest where each day overcame a new difficulty, gained a new truth, or banished a previous error ".

Synset Examples

a driving personal ambition
the driving force was his innate enthusiasm
an impulsive force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impelling - having the power to drive or urge forward
  • Synonym: forceful - full of force; powerful
  • Antonym: restraining - holding something back or keeping it under control

Etymology

Present participle of 'drive', from Old English 'drīfan' (to drive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of controlling and steering the movement of a vehicle or animal.

Examples

Are you driving yourself, Boss?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piloting - The act of controlling the course of an aircraft or boat
  • Synonym: riding - The act of sitting on and controlling the movement of an animal, bicycle, or motorcycle

Etymology

From 'drive' + -ing; 'drive' from Old English 'drīfan' (to force, push, drive).
