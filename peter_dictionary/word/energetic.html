energetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛnərˈdʒɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈdʒɛtɪk / (BrE)
showing or involving great activity, effort, or determination; working hard and actively to achieve or promote something

Examples

The knowledge that most Americans have of folklore comes through contact with commercial propagandists and a few energetic amateurs and collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrious - diligent and hard-working
  • Antonym: idle - not active or not working; lazy

Etymology

From Greek energētikos (active, effective), from energos (active).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛnərˈdʒɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈdʒɛtɪk / (BrE)
having or showing a lot of energy; active and vigorous.

Examples

The maid was very black and very energetic, trim in a yellow pique uniform.
This favorable image of America in the minds of Russian men and women is still there despite years of energetic anti American propaganda ".
Apparently academic challenge in the structured setting creates an optimum of stress so that the child with high anxiety is able to achieve because he is aroused to an energetic state without becoming confused or panicked.

Synset Examples

an energetic fund raiser for the college
an energetic group of hikers
it caused an energetic chemical reaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: vigorous - strong, healthy, and full of energy
  • Antonym: lethargic - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Greek 'energetikos' (active, effective), from 'energein' (to work, operate).
