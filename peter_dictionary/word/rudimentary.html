rudimentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌruː.dəˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌruː.dɪˈmɛn.təri / (BrE)
existing in the earliest or most basic stage of development; not fully developed or advanced.

Examples

Since the complicated process of establishing new communes and reviewing the rudimentary plan left by the French did not even begin until the fall of 1957, this goal appears somewhat ambitious.

Synset Examples

rudimentary plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primitive - existing in or characteristic of an early stage; basic or undeveloped
  • Antonym: advanced - far along in development or progress

Etymology

From Latin 'rudimentum' (beginning, first attempt), from 'rudis' (unwrought, rough, raw).

2. ADJ

/ ˌruːdəˈmɛnteri / (AmE), / ˌruːdɪˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
involving or consisting of basic or elementary facts or principles; in an early or undeveloped stage

Examples

The most primitive feelings are rudimentary value feelings, both positive and negative: a desire to appropriate this or that part of the environment into oneself; a desire to avoid and repel this or that other part.

Synset Examples

the fundamental laws of the universe
a fundamental incomatibility between them
these rudimentary truths
underlying principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamental - forming a necessary base or core; of central importance
  • Synonym: elementary - basic and simple in nature or at an early stage

Etymology

From Latin 'rudimentum' (beginning, first attempt).
