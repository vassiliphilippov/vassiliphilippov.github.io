entirety

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtaɪərəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪərɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being whole, total, or complete

Examples

In the only sense in which badness is involved at all, whatever was bad in the first case is still present in its entirety, since all that is expressed in either case is a state of feeling, and that feeling is still there.
They must do something with the acquiescence of the latter, or some of them, which amounts to an acceptance of the law in its entirety beyond all possibility of misconstruction ".
Because if we do this, we do it all the way, 100%, nothing half-assed.

Synset Examples

he read the article in its entirety
appalled by the totality of the destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totality - the condition of being total; the whole amount
  • Antonym: part - a portion or segment of a whole

Etymology

From Old French entier (whole, complete), from Latin integer (untouched, whole).
