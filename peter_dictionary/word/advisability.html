advisability

1. NOUN

/ ədˌvaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ədˌvaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being advisable or recommended; suitability for being suggested or acted upon

Examples

Charlie Marble was back and forth on several occasions, first to confer with Andy on the advisability of cancelling the Las Vegas engagement - they decided it was wise - and later to announce that a prominent comedian, also an agency client, had agreed to fill the casino's open date.

Synset Examples

they questioned the advisability of our policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inadvisability - the quality of being inadvisable

Etymology

From 'advisable' + '-ity', modeled after similar Latin-derived nouns.
