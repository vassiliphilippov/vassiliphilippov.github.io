freight

1. NOUN

/ freɪt / (AmE), / freɪt / (BrE)
the transportation of goods commercially, especially by train, ship, truck, or aircraft, typically at rates cheaper than express delivery

Examples

Not only is this kind of duplication wasteful, but it gives the combined system the ability to take freight traffic away from the New York Central and other railroads serving the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cargo - goods carried by a ship, aircraft, or vehicle

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'vrecht', 'vracht' (freight, cargo).
