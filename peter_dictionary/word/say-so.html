say-so

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ ˌsəʊ / (BrE)
the right or authority to make a decision or give permission

Examples

I can't squeeze a drop without say-so.

Synset Examples

he has the authority to issue warrants
deputies are given authorization to make arrests
a place of potency in the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders or make decisions
  • Synonym: permission - consent or authorization to do something

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'say so', indicating that something is so because someone says it is.
