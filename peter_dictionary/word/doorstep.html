doorstep

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈdɔːstɛp / (BrE)
A horizontal piece of stone or wood at the base of a doorway, forming the bottom step as you enter or leave a building.

Examples

"You were left on the doorstep by gypsies."

Etymology

From 'door' + 'step'; first recorded in English in the 17th century.
