ablated

1. ADJ

/ əˈbleɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈbleɪtɪd / (BrE)
reduced in size, thickness, or mass by melting, erosion, or vaporization, often as a result of exposure to intense heat or friction.

Examples

Most meteoritic material, by the time it reaches the Earth's surface, has been reduced to dust or to spherules of ablated material in its passage through the atmosphere.

Synset Examples

the rocket's ablated head shield

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eroded - gradually worn away by natural forces like wind or water
  • Synonym: diminished - made smaller or less

Etymology

From the verb 'ablate', which comes from Latin ablat-, past participle of auferre, meaning 'to carry away'.
