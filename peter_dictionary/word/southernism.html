southernism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərnˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌðənɪzm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or pronunciation characteristic of the southern United States.

Examples

Her speech was barren of southernisms; she was one of Eliot Sparling's neutralized minorities, adopting the rolling R's and constricted vowels of Los Angeles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northernism - a locution or pronunciation peculiar to the northern United States

Etymology

From 'Southern' + '-ism', modeled on similar terms for regional expressions.
