uproot

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈruːt / (AmE), / ʌpˈruːt / (BrE)
to remove people forcibly from their homeland or familiar environment, often causing them to settle elsewhere

Examples

He came to a stretch of old orange groves, the trees dead, some of them uprooted, and then there was an outlying shopping area, and tract houses.

Synset Examples

The war uprooted many people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: displace - to force someone to leave their home or usual place

Etymology

From the prefix 'up-' + 'root'; originally used in a literal botanical sense, later extended metaphorically to describe displacement of people.
