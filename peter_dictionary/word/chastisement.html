chastisement

1. NOUN

/ tʃæˈstaɪzmənt / (AmE), / tʃɑːˈstaɪzmənt / (BrE)
The act of scolding or criticizing someone as a form of punishment; verbal reprimand.

Examples

It is, in fact, a relatively mild chastisement of the dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castigation - severe criticism or punishment
  • Synonym: reprimand - an official or formal scolding

Etymology

From Middle English 'chastisen', from Old French 'chastier', from Latin 'castigare' (to punish, correct).
