bleat

1. NOUN

/ blit / (AmE), / bliːt / (BrE)
the characteristic cry of a sheep, goat, or a similar sound

Examples

Cardboard noisemakers, substitutes for the unavailable tin models, were being hawked and bought at makeshift stands every few yards along Broadway, and one's ears were continually serenaded by the horns' rasps and bleats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baa - the sound made by sheep; synonym for 'bleat' (noun)

Etymology

From Old English *blǣtan* (to bleat).

2. VERB

/ blit / (AmE), / bliːt / (BrE)
(of a sheep, goat, or similar animal) to make a wavering, plaintive cry; (of a person) to complain or speak in a weak or whining tone.

Examples

As our planes land the war materiel that kills pro-Western Katangans, we stand supinely bleating while Nehru's troops smash into a five-hundred-year-old district of our NATO ally, Portugal.

Synset Examples

The lambs were bleating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
  • Synonym: murmur - to make a low, continuous, indistinct sound; to complain softly
  • Antonym: roar - to utter a loud, deep, or resonant cry

Etymology

Old English *blǣtan* (to bleat), of imitative origin.
