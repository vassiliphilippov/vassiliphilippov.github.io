merciful

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝː.sɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sɪ.fəl / (BrE)
Showing compassion or forgiveness towards someone, especially when it is within one's power to punish or harm them.

Examples

He knows that he is thinking about action in relation to a future which can be perceived but dimly through a merciful fog.

Synset Examples

sought merciful treatment for the captives
a merciful god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiful - feeling or showing sorrow for someone's suffering; compassionate
  • Synonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Antonym: cruel - wilfully causing pain or suffering to others, or feeling no concern about it

Etymology

From Old French *merciful*, from *merci* (mercy) + *-ful* (full of).
