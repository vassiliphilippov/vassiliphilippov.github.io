marking

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
a pattern or arrangement of marks, such as lines, spots, or symbols, that appear on a surface or object

Examples

And although Schnabel's pianism bristles with excitement, it is meticulously faithful to Schubert's dynamic markings and phrase indications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - a visible trace or impression on a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'mark' + suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
a symbol, line, or mark that distinguishes or identifies something or someone

Examples

When a dot appears close to the end of the transverse line, the "moderate "rating may be further classified according to the position of the dot with respect to the vertical marking denoting the mean age.

Synset Examples

the owner's mark was on all the sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - a small area on a surface having a different color from its surroundings, typically one caused accidentally or intentionally

Etymology

From verb 'mark' + -ing; ultimately from Old English *mearcian* (to mark).
