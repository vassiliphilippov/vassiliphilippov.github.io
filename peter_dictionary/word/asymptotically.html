asymptotically

1. ADV

/ ˌeɪ.sɪmˈtɑ.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪmˈtɒ.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a manner that approaches an asymptote; increasingly close to a limit but never quite reaching it.

Examples

Essentially these birefringence studies show that at low rates of shear a tension is present at 45 ` to the direction of shear, and as the rate of shear increases, the direction of the maximum tension moves asymptotically toward the direction of shear.

Synset Examples

it grew asymptotically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asymptote - a line that a curve approaches but never meets

Etymology

Formed from 'asymptote' (from Greek asymptōtos 'not falling together') + '-ically'.
