nonsense

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈnɒnsəns / (BrE)
speech, writing, or ideas that are meaningless or make no sense

Examples

The party at Floyd's penthouse gave the "chorines "a chance for a nostalgic frolic through all those hackneyed routines which have become a classic choreographic statement of the era's nonsense.
"Nonsense ", he said.
And then Eichmann was quoted as saying, in non English, that Hitler's plan to exterminate the Jews was nonsense ".
"That is only partly non nonsense ", he began.
That sort of braggadocio, for that sort of reason, in the view of Torrio and Capone, was a nonsense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubbish - spoken or written words that are nonsense or foolish
  • Antonym: sense - speech or writing that is reasonable and logical

Etymology

From Italian nonsenso, from Latin non (not) + sensus (sense).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈnɒnsəns / (BrE)
(of words or speech) having no intelligible meaning; consisting of meaningless sounds or expressions.

Examples

"If we cannot tell evil, horror, and insanity from nonsense, what is the future of humor and comedy "?
I guess I'm too old for that sort of nonsense any more.

Synset Examples

nonsense syllables
a nonsensical jumble of words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsensical - having no meaning; making no sense
  • Antonym: meaningful - having meaning; understandable

Etymology

From Italian 'non senso' (without meaning), influenced by Latin 'non' (not) and 'sensus' (sense).
