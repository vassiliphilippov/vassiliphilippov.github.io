unclaimed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkleɪmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkleɪmd / (BrE)
(of an item) not claimed or collected by its owner or assignee.

Examples

Her quarters were on the right as you walked into the building, and her small front room was clogged with heavy furniture - a big, round, oak dining table and chairs, a buffet, with a row of unclaimed letters inserted between the mirror and its frame.

Synset Examples

unclaimed luggage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: claimed - (of an item) declared as belonging to someone

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'claimed' (from Latin 'clamare', to call out).
