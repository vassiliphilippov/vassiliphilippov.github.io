swap

1. VERB

/ swɑp / (AmE), / swɒp / (BrE)
to give something to someone and receive something in exchange

Examples

Still more time was consumed while the pilot, at the radioed suggestion of Continental president Robert Six, tried to persuade the armed pair to swap the Boeing jet for a propeller-driven Douglas DC-7.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - to give something and receive something else in return

Etymology

Probably of Middle English origin, possibly from the word 'swappen' meaning 'to strike' or 'to exchange'.
