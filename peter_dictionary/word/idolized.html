idolized

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Regarded with deep admiration or love, often to the point of being treated as an idol or god-like figure.

Examples

Almost nothing is said of Charles' spectacular victories, the central theme being the heroic loyalty of the Swedish people to their idolized king in misfortune and defeat.

Synset Examples

adored grandchildren
an idolized wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adored - regarded with deep love or respect
  • Synonym: worshipped - regarded with religious reverence or intense admiration
  • Antonym: scorned - treated with contempt or disdain

Etymology

From 'idol', ultimately from Greek eidōlon (image, idol), with the suffix -ize indicating to make (into an idol) and past participle ending -ed.
