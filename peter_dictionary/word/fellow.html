fellow

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ / (BrE)
A man or boy, often used informally to refer to a person in a friendly or neutral way.

Examples

Spahn not only is a superior pitcher but a gentlemanly fine fellow, a ball player's ball player, as they say in the trade.
Since the hero, a sterling and upright fellow, is a rich Brown senior, while two Yalies are cast as virtual rapists, I suppose I should disqualify myself from sitting in judgment on "Where the Boys are ", but I shall do nothing of the sort.
The cyclist, a sufficiently commonplace young fellow, is not named but identified simply as "Life "- that and a license number, which Piepsam uses in addressing him.
Do you get the picture of the kind of fellow he was "?
"He recommended both of them for the DSM and the Detroit fellow for the Purple Heart, too, for a combat inflicted wound.

Synset Examples

that chap is your host

he's a likable cuss
he's a good bloke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chap - informal term for a man or boy
  • Synonym: bloke - British informal term for a man
  • Antonym: girl - female child or young woman (opposite gender)
  • Antonym: woman - adult female human (opposite gender)

Etymology

From Old English 'feolaga', ultimately from Old Norse 'félagi' (companion, partner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ / (BrE)
a person who shares activities, interests, or companionship with another; companion or associate

Examples

If the inner functions of religion are performed, the individual is a composed, ordered, motivated, and emotionally secure associate; he is not greatly frustrated, and he is not anomic; he is better fitted to perform his social life among his fellows.

Synset Examples

drinking companions
comrades in arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion - a person who accompanies or associates with another
  • Synonym: associate - a person with whom one is connected or works with
  • Synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Old English *feolaga*, influenced by Old Norse *félagi* (partner, companion), from *fé* (cattle, money) + *lagi* (lay, companion).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ / (BrE)
a person who belongs to the same group, class, or occupation as another; a companion, associate, or peer.

Examples

Also, I am convinced that if my company were a sole proprietorship instead of a partnership, I would have been even abler to solve long-range problems for myself and my fellow employees.
And you'’re gonna look respectable for 50 million of your fellow Americans.

Synset Examples

the surgeon consulted his colleagues
he sent e-mail to his fellow hackers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colleague - a person who works with you in the same profession or organization
  • Synonym: peer - a person who is equal to another in abilities, qualifications, age, background, and social status

Etymology

From Old English *feolaga*, from Old Norse *félagi* (partner, companion), from *fé* (money, cattle) + *lagi* (one who lays something down with another).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ / (BrE)
Used as an informal form of address for a man.

Examples

Thank you, Al. Fellows, could you step outside for a minute, please?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guy - informal term for a man
  • Synonym: man - adult male person (informal address)
