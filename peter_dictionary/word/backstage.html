backstage

1. NOUN

/ bækˈsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / bækˈsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part of a theater or performance venue that is behind the stage and out of sight of the audience

Examples

Backstage was tomb like by contrast.
As Andy reached the finale of his act, a subdued commotion backstage drew his attention to the wings.
Let'’s go see her backstage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stage - the part of a theater where performances are given

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stage', literally referring to the area at the back of the stage.

2. ADV

/ bækˈsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / bækˈsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
in or to the area behind the stage in a theater, especially where actors prepare before going on stage

Examples

We'’ll go backstage later and take her to eat.

Synset Examples

costumes were changed backstage

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'stage', referring to the area behind the stage.
