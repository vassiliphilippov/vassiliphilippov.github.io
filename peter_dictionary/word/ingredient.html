ingredient

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (BrE)
any of the substances or items that are combined to make a particular mixture, compound, or dish

Examples

In addition, many of the hard surface cleaners used for walls and woodwork had their genesis in trisodium orthophosphate, which is still the major ingredient of a number of such products.
Hydroxides, orthophosphates, borates, carbonates, and silicates are important inorganic ingredients of hard surface cleaners.
That was the new advertising angle - something about a Lloyd's of London policy to insure the secrecy of the secret ingredient.
With a new secret ingredient.

Etymology

From Latin 'ingredior' (to go into), from 'in-' (in) + 'gradior' (to step).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡriːdiənt / (BrE)
an essential part or element that combines with others to form a whole, especially in a non-physical or abstract sense

Examples

The ingredients of Faulkner's novels and stories are by no means new with him, and most of the problems he takes up have had the attention of authors before him.

Synset Examples

jealousy was a component of his character
two constituents of a musical composition are melody and harmony
the grammatical elements of a sentence
a key factor in her success
humor: an effective ingredient of a speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole
  • Synonym: factor - something that contributes to a result
  • Synonym: element - a necessary or typical part of something

Etymology

From Latin 'ingredientem', present participle of 'ingredi' (to go into, enter).
