slippery

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɪpəri / (AmE), / ˈslɪpəri / (BrE)
likely to cause slipping or sliding; difficult to hold, stand on, or move across because of a smooth or wet surface.

Examples

He paced slowly, stooping, staring at the damp, slippery floor.
He caught up with me once and grabbed me, but I was all covered with ZING - it's very slippery, you know ".

Synset Examples

slippery sidewalks
a slippery bar of soap
the streets are still slippy from the rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippy - (chiefly British and Irish) likely to cause slipping or sliding

Etymology

From Middle English slipperi, from slippen (to slip) + -y.
