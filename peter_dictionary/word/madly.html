madly

1. ADV

/ ˈmæd.li / (AmE), / ˈmad.li / (BrE)
In a wild, uncontrolled, or frenzied manner.

Examples

He dashed madly for the next elbow turn in the draw, and made it.
He sputtered back to his feet and scrambled madly to pull his bags of seed grain forward.

Synset Examples

she fought back madly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildly - in a wild or uncontrolled manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm, controlled manner

Etymology

From mad + -ly; 'mad' from Old English 'gemǣd' (insane, foolish), and '-ly' forming adverbs from adjectives.
