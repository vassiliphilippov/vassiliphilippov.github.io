overseas

1. ADV

/ ˌoʊvɚˈsiz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsiːz / (BrE)
In or to a foreign country, especially one separated by sea.

Examples

And then the Amen corner took hold, re-enacting a form of group participation in worship that stemmed from years before the Greek chorus, spreading down through the African forest, overseas to the West Indies, and then here in Alabama.

Synset Examples

He lived overseas for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abroad - In or to a foreign country

Etymology

From over + sea, originally in the sense of 'across the sea'.

2. ADV

/ ˌoʊvɚˈsiz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsiːz / (BrE)
in or to a country or place across the sea, especially a different continent

Examples

If the Department of State is to take primary responsibility for foreign policy in Washington, it follows that the ambassador is expected to take charge overseas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abroad - in or to a foreign country, especially one that is not your own

Etymology

From over + sea, indicating beyond the sea.

3. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈsiz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsiːz / (BrE)
in or to a foreign country, especially one across the sea; abroad.

Examples

I would hope that we could create the recognition in the Department and overseas that those who come across little things going wrong have the responsibility for bringing these to the attention of those who can do something about them.

Synset Examples

markets abroad
overseas markets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abroad - in or to a foreign country

Etymology

From over + seas, meaning 'across the seas'.
