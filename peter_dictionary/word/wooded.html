wooded

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of land or an area) covered with trees and bushes.

Examples

The robbers run from the hide-out, take cover in a wooded declivity, and are shot dead by the posse.

Synset Examples

wooded land
a heavily wooded tract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Antonym: treeless - lacking trees or woods

Etymology

From 'wood' (Old English 'wudu', meaning 'tree' or 'forest') + '-ed' (adjectival suffix).
