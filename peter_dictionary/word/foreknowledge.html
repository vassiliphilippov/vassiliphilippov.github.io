foreknowledge

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / fɔːˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
Knowledge or awareness of something before it happens or exists; prescience.

Examples

High sensitivity in radio telescopes is achieved by reducing the bandwidth of the receiver; therefore, only with precise foreknowledge of the line frequencies is an astronomical search for the radio spectra of these molecules feasible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precognition - knowledge of an event before it happens, especially by paranormal means

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'knowledge'.
