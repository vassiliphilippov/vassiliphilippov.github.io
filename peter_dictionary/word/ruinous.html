ruinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːənəs / (AmE), / ˈruːɪnəs / (BrE)
causing great damage, destruction, or loss, often with lasting harmful effects

Examples

As it affects the city's fiscal situation, such an interchange is ruinous; it removes forever from the tax rolls property which should be taxed to pay for the city services.

Synset Examples

a catastrophic depression
catastrophic illness
a ruinous course of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophic - extremely harmful; disastrous
  • Synonym: destructive - causing great and irreparable damage
  • Antonym: beneficial - producing good or helpful results

Etymology

From Latin 'ruinosus', from 'ruina' meaning 'ruin, downfall'.
