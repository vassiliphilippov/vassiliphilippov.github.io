brink

1. NOUN

/ brɪŋk / (AmE), / brɪŋk / (BrE)
the extreme edge or border of a steep place, or the point at which something, especially something unwelcome, is about to happen

Examples

In the unfertile land in Palestine humans broke their backs pushing will power to the brink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge - the outside limit of an object, area, or surface; a border
  • Synonym: verge - an edge, border, or margin
  • Synonym: threshold - the point or level at which something begins or changes

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'brincke' (edge, border, grassland verge).
