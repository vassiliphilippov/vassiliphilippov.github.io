underwater

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
existing, occurring, or situated beneath the surface of the water.

Examples

In the dim underwater light they dressed and straightened up the room, and then they went across the hall to the kitchen.
I 'll switch on the underwater lights.

Synset Examples

submerged rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submerged - beneath the surface of a liquid; covered by water

Etymology

From 'under' + 'water', dating from the mid-19th century.
