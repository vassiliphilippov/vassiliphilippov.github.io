invariable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛriəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈveəriəbl̩ / (BrE)
never changing; always the same in every case.

Examples

The exchange was almost invariable, and Scotty, in his bed, could hear every word of it.

Synset Examples

an invariable temperature
an invariable rule
his invariable courtesy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable - likely to change or vary; not consistent

Etymology

From Late Latin invariabilis, from in- (not) + variabilis (variable).
