jot

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑt / (AmE), / dʒɒt / (BrE)
A brief and quickly written note, often made hastily as a reminder or for communication.

Examples

The work had its beginning in 1938 with an eight bar musical strain to which Koehler set the words "There 'll be no more work / There 'll be no more worry ", matching the spiritual feeling of the jot.

Etymology

From Middle English *jote* (tiny amount), from Latin *iota*, the smallest Greek letter.
