inevitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛvɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛvɪtəbl / (BrE)
Certain to happen and impossible to avoid or prevent.

Examples

Mr. Bates shows us how this mutual education spreads its inevitable havoc.
If this aspect of death as punishment is not distinguished from the idea of death as natural termination, the conclusion seems inevitable that temporal existence itself is a form of punishment rather than the state into which man is put by the will of the Creator.
"But it was inevitable!
It's inevitable in this case, for the couple will be separated for, at the least, forty years.
To many experts, this trend was inevitable.

Synset Examples

the inevitable result

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unavoidable - impossible to avoid
  • Antonym: avoidable - able to be avoided or prevented

Etymology

From Latin inevitabilis, from in- (not) + evitabilis (avoidable).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɛvətəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛvɪtəbl / (BrE)
An event or outcome that is certain to happen and cannot be avoided.

Examples

Here was another human who understood the stupidity of quarreling with the inevitable.
Why didn't somebody tell me he had one of those... things?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - something that is certain to happen or be the case
  • Antonym: contingency - a future event that is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty

Etymology

From Latin *inevitabilis* (unavoidable), from in- (not) + *evitabilis* (avoidable).
