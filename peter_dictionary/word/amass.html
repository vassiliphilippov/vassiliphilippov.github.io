amass

1. VERB

/ əˈmæs / (AmE), / əˈmæs / (BrE)
to gather together or accumulate a large amount of something over a period of time, especially for oneself

Examples

He does not mean, in fact he addresses himself specifically to reject the proposition, that "if we took the risk of surrendering, a new generation in Britain would soon begin to amass its strength in secret in order to reverse the consequences of that surrender ".

Synset Examples

Journals are accumulating in my office
The work keeps piling up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulate - to gather together or collect, especially of something increasing gradually
  • Synonym: hoard - to collect and store large amounts, often secretly or guardedly

Etymology

From French *amasser*, from Late Latin *ammassare* (to collect into a mass), from Latin *massa* (mass, lump).
