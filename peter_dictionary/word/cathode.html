cathode

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæˌθoʊd / (AmE), / ˈkæθəʊd / (BrE)
A negatively charged electrode through which electrons enter an electrical device or circuit.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anode - the positively charged electrode through which current flows into a device

Etymology

From Greek 'kathodos' (way down), introduced in 1834 by Michael Faraday.
