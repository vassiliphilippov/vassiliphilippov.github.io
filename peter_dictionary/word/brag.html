brag

1. VERB

/ bræɡ / (AmE), / bræɡ / (BrE)
to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements or possessions

Examples

Among my school and neighborhood friends, during the next months, I bragged and swaggered and pompously described my impending date.
"A maid told us that she still bragged about getting $ 50 a date.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with pride about one's achievements
  • Antonym: humble - modest or low in talking about one's achievements

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *braggen* (to boast, brag), possibly of Scandinavian origin.
