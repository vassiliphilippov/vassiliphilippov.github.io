nonfiction

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / nɒnˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
prose writing that is based on facts, real events, and real people, such as biography or history, rather than fiction

Examples

Moreover, the most artistically successful of the nonfiction films have invariably borrowed the narrative form from the fiction feature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fiction - prose writing that describes imaginary events and people

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'fiction'.
