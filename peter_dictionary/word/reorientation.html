reorientation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of changing the direction, focus, or set of attitudes and beliefs; a new or altered orientation

Examples

The programs in infrared spectroscopy are undergoing reorientation toward wavelength standards in the far infrared, the application of infrared techniques to solid state studies, and increased emphasis on high resolution instrumentation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: orientation - the determination of the relative position or direction; the set of attitudes and beliefs

Etymology

From re- (again) + orientation (from Latin oriri, to rise or begin).
