liver

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
a large, reddish-brown glandular organ in the upper right abdomen, responsible for secreting bile and performing various vital metabolic processes, including detoxification, protein synthesis, and storage of nutrients

Examples

She had - he informed her - kidney trouble, liver trouble, and a severe female disorder.
The liver weighed 2090 gm., was brown in color, and the cut surface was mottled by irregular pale areas.
Some liver cord cells contained vacuolated cytoplasm, while others had small amounts of brown hemosiderin pigment.

Etymology

Old English *lifere*, related to Old High German *lebra*, Old Norse *lifr*, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
the large organ of an animal, especially a cow, pig, or chicken, used as food

Examples

After he 'd got out fresh liver for Bast, he paused to look at her crouched daintily over her dish.
That'’s a Ritz cracker and chopped liver!" [LAUGHING] Bring out the peppers and sardines!

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old English 'lifer', related to German 'Leber' and Dutch 'lever'.
