corporeality

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˌpɔriˈæləti / (AmE), / kɔːˌpɔːriˈælɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having a physical, bodily, or material existence rather than a spiritual or immaterial one.

Examples

At the same time, the wallpaper strips themselves seem to be pushed into depth by the lines and patches of shading charcoaled upon them, and by their placing in relation to the block capitals; and these capitals seem in turn to be pushed back by their placing, and by contrast with the corporeality of the woodgraining.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immateriality - the quality of not being physical or material

Etymology

From Late Latin corporealitas, from Latin corporeus (bodily) + -ity.
