fencing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛn.sɪŋ / (BrE)
Material or structure used to enclose or divide an area, typically forming a barrier or boundary.

Examples

But it was only Johnson reaching around the wire chicken fencing, which half covered the truck cab's glassless rear window.
"Tee-wah ", the driver cackled, his black eyes glittering behind dull silver chicken fencing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fence - a structure serving as a barrier, boundary, or enclosure

Etymology

From 'fence' + -ing; 'fence' from Middle English 'fens', from Middle French 'defens' (defense, protection).
