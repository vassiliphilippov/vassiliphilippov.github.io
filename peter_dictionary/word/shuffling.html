shuffling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌf.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of walking with slow, dragging steps without lifting the feet fully off the ground.

Examples

He opened it a crack and in doing so made as much shuffling, coughing, and scraping noise as possible in order to drown emanations from the hen who had begun to protest.

Synset Examples

from his shambling I assumed he was very old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamble - walk in a slow, dragging manner
  • Degree Higher: stagger - walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
