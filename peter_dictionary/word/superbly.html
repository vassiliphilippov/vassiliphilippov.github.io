superbly

1. ADV

/ suˈpɝːbli / (AmE), / suˈpɜːb.li / (BrE)
In an extremely good or excellent manner; to a very high standard.

Examples

This big, flexible voice with uncommon range has been superbly disciplined.
In a series of fairy tales and fantasies, Melies demonstrated that the film is superbly equipped to tell a straightforward story, with beginning, middle and end, complications, resolutions, climaxes, and conclusions.

Synset Examples

her voice is superbly disciplined
the colors changed wondrously slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellently - in an excellent manner
  • Synonym: wonderfully - in a wonderful manner; extremely well

Etymology

From Latin 'superbus' (proud, excellent) via Old French 'superbe', with the English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
