wry

1. ADJ

/ raɪ / (AmE), / raɪ / (BrE)
Using or expressing dry, especially mocking, humor.

Examples

Ever since Bambi, and, more recently, Born Free, there have been a lot of books about animals, but few compare with Max Fink's wry, understated, charming, and immensely readable My Friend, the Quizzical Salamander.

Synset Examples

dry humor
an ironic remark often conveys an intended meaning obliquely
an ironic novel
an ironical smile
with a wry Scottish wit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironic - characterized by irony, subtly mocking or contradictory

Etymology

From Old English wrīgian (to turn or bend), related to the notion of a twisted or crooked expression or humor.
