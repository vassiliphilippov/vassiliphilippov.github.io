lowly

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊli / (AmE), / ˈləʊli / (BrE)
Of low rank or status; considered inferior or humble within a hierarchy or organization.

Examples

A Massachusetts soldier, who seems to have been a Civil War version of Bill Mauldin, wrote: "The officers consider themselves as made of a different material from the low fellows in the ranks.

Synset Examples

the junior faculty
a lowly corporal
petty officialdom
a subordinate functionary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junior - of lower rank or status
  • Synonym: inferior - of lower status, quality, or rank
  • Antonym: senior - of higher rank or status
  • Antonym: superior - higher in rank, status, or quality

Etymology

From Middle English louly, from Old English lāwlic (low + -ly).
