beta

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə / (BrE)
The second letter of the Greek alphabet (Β, β), used in science, mathematics, and as a symbol in various fields.

Examples

Below these particulars was a series of alpha beta gammas connected by arrows and crosses which denoted the lineage of the breed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alpha - first letter of the Greek alphabet
  • Degree Higher: gamma - third letter of the Greek alphabet

Etymology

From Greek beta, from Phoenician beth (house).
