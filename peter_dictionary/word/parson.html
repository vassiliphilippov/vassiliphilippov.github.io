parson

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.sən / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.sən / (BrE)
A member of the clergy, especially in the Anglican Church, who is authorized to lead religious services; a parish priest or minister.

Examples

There was so much interest shown in this present-day venture that it was continued on B.B.C., where comments were equally made by an Anglican parson, a Free Church minister and a Catholic priest.

Synset Examples

clergymen are usually called ministers in Protestant churches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in certain Protestant churches
  • Synonym: priest - an ordained minister of the Catholic, Orthodox, or Anglican Church

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French 'persone', from Latin 'persona' (person, character), later used for 'parish priest'.
