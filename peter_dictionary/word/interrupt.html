interrupt

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to stop the continuous progress of an activity, process, or event, especially by speaking, causing a break, or intervening unexpectedly

Examples

If, in preparing that shot for the inevitable showing to your friends, you interrupt the sequence to paste in a few frames of the child's grandmother watching this event, you have begun to be an artist in film; you are employing the basic technique of film; you are cutting.
The slow tapping of a cane on the stone steps coming up to the tower interrupted his reverie.
Time elapsed but the doctor was obviously unconscious of its passage until an unwelcome knock on the door interrupted the processes of nature.
She was interrupted.
The telephone interrupted him.

Synset Examples

We interrupt the program for the following messages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disrupt - to cause disorder or confusion; to throw into turmoil
  • Antonym: continue - to keep going without stopping or interruption

Etymology

From Latin 'interrumpere' (to break apart), from 'inter-' (between) + 'rumpere' (to break).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪn.təˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrʌpt / (BrE)
To cause something to stop, especially by breaking the peace or continuity of it; to disturb or break the tranquility of a situation.

Examples

The Man from Mars had interrupted him with his work half finished - and that stupid jackass Short was certain to louse it up.
The coyote was calling again, and he hoped that this time there would be no other sounds to interrupt it.
Suddenly he was interrupted in his daydreaming by a warm wetness lapping against his chin, and his eyes opened wide and long at the sight of a goat's claret tongue, feasting against the salt taste of him.
The clapping they made on the concrete interrupted him in the ecstatic pleasure he knew, so that he quickly released his hold on the goat and pretended to be examining its haunches for ticks.
Tommy interrupted furiously.

Synset Examples

Don't interrupt me when I'm reading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturb - to interfere with the normal arrangement, activity, or peace of something
  • Synonym: disrupt - to interrupt the normal course or unity of
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and tranquility
  • Antonym: peace - a state or period in which there is no war or disturbance

Etymology

From Latin interruptus, past participle of interrumpere (to break apart), from inter- (between) + rumpere (to break).

3. VERB

/ ˌɪn.təˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to stop someone from continuing what they are saying or doing by saying or doing something yourself

Examples

Conrad interrupted.

Synset Examples

Please don't interrupt me while I'm on the phone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturb - to interrupt the quiet, peace, or order of; to trouble or bother
  • Synonym: disrupt - to interrupt by causing disorder
  • Antonym: continue - to persist in an activity without interruption

Etymology

From Latin interruptus, past participle of interrumpere (to break apart).
