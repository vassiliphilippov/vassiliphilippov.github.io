prod

1. VERB

/ prɑd / (AmE), / prɒd / (BrE)
To push someone or something gently, often with a finger or another object, especially to encourage them to take action or pay attention.

Examples

Mantle, it's apparent, resented Casey Stengel's attempts to push and prod him into the perfection the veteran manager saw as a thrilling possibility.

Synset Examples

She nudged my elbow when she saw her friend enter the restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudge - to push gently, especially with an elbow, to attract attention
  • Synonym: poke - to jab or prod with a finger or a sharp object

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to older dialectal 'prod' (to poke, thrust, or push).

2. VERB

/ prɑd / (AmE), / prɒd / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to take action, often by persistent suggestion or pressure

Examples

There are hordes of nubile young women there who, prodded by their impatient mothers, are determined to marry.
She was going to keep on scheming, poking, prodding, suggesting, and dictating until the cops got up enough interest in him to go back to their old neighborhood and ask questions.

Synset Examples

The other children egged the boy on, but he did not want to throw the stone through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urge - encourage strongly or press someone to do something
  • Synonym: encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone so that they act
  • Antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular action

Etymology

From Middle English 'prodden', probably of Low German origin; originally meaning 'to poke'.
