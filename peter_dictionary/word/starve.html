starve

1. VERB

/ stɑrv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to deprive a person or animal of food; to cause to suffer or die from lack of nourishment

Examples

For almost two months, the defendant and the world heard from individuals escaped from the grave about fathers and mothers, graybeards, adolescents, babies, starved, beaten to death, strangled, machine-gunned, gassed, burned.

Synset Examples

They starved the prisoners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feed - to give food to; supply with nourishment

Etymology

From Old English *steorfan* (to die), later shifting in sense to mean 'die of hunger'.

  • Antonym: feed - to give food to; to supply with nourishment
