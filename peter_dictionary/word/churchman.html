churchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɜrtʃmən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃmən / (BrE)
A male clergyman or a man who is an official in a Christian church, especially one in religious orders.

Examples

To be sure, when this is pointed out, a common response among certain churchmen is to fulminate about "the little flock "and "the great crowd "and to take solace from Paul's castigation of the "wisdom of the wise "in the opening chapter of First Corinthians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy
  • Antonym: layman - a non-ordained member of the church; not clergy

Etymology

From 'church' + 'man'.
