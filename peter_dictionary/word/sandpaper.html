sandpaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsændˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
Stiff paper coated with abrasive material, such as sand or powdered emery, used for smoothing or polishing surfaces.

Examples

He also bought a huge square of pegboard for hanging up his tools, and lumber for his workbench, sandpaper and glue and assorted nails, levels and T squares and plumb lines and several gadgets that he had no idea how to use or what they were for.

Etymology

From 'sand' + 'paper', originally describing paper covered with sand as the abrasive.
