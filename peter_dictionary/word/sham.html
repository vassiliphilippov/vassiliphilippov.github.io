sham

1. NOUN

/ ʃæm / (AmE), / ʃam / (BrE)
something that is not what it purports to be; a fake or counterfeit

Examples

Its pretense to operate in the public interest is little more than a sham.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - something that is not genuine; an imitation
  • Synonym: fraud - something intended to deceive; a falsehood
  • Antonym: genuine - something that is real or authentic

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect meaning 'a trick', possibly from 'shame'.
