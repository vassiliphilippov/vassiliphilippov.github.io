heroics

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈroʊɪks / (AmE), / hɪəˈrəʊɪks / (BrE)
exaggerated or excessively bold actions or speeches, often intended to attract attention or admiration; melodramatic or ostentatious conduct.

Examples

Just when it seems baseball might be losing its grip on the masses up pops heroics to start millions of tongues to wagging.

Synset Examples

heroics are for those epic films they make in Hollywood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravado - a show of boldness intended to impress or intimidate
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of not being boastful or showy

Etymology

From 'heroic' (adj.), modeled on the plural noun form, referring to acts worthy of a hero.
