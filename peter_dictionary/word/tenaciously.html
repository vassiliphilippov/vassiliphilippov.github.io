tenaciously

1. ADV

/ təˈneɪʃəsli / (AmE), / tɪˈneɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows great determination or persistence; with firm resolve.

Examples

Old attitudes are held more tenaciously in the Tidewater than the Piedmont; so that a line running down the length of the South marking the upper limits of tidewater would roughly divide the Old South from the new, but with, of course, important minority enclaves.

Synset Examples

he pursued her doggedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doggedly - in a manner that shows tenacity or grim persistence
  • Synonym: determinedly - in a determined manner; resolutely
  • Synonym: persistently - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action

Etymology

From 'tenacious' (from Latin tenax, tenac- ‘holding fast’, from tenere ‘to hold’) + -ly.
