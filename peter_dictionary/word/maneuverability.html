maneuverability

1. NOUN

/ məˌnuːvərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / məˌnjuːvərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be maneuvered easily or effectively, especially the ability of a vehicle or device to change direction or position with ease.

Examples

Japanese aircraft were strong on maneuverability, American on speed and firepower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - the ability to move quickly and easily
  • Antonym: stability - the quality of being stable or resistant to change, often contrasted with maneuverability

Etymology

From 'maneuver' + '-ability', combining the French 'manœuvre' (manual work, movement) and the suffix '-ability' (ability, capacity).
