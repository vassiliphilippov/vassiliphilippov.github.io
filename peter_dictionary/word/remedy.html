remedy

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛm.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈrɛm.ɪ.di / (BrE)
to correct or improve a fault, problem, or deficiency; to set right

Examples

It urged that the city "take steps to remedy "this problem.
Anyone who tried to remedy some of the most glaring defects in our form of democracy was denounced as a traitorous red whose real purpose was the destruction of our government.
Unfortunately, those who would remedy our "softness "seek to do so with calisthenics.

Synset Examples

remedy these deficiencies
rectify the inequities in salaries
repair an oversight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectify - to put right; correct
  • Synonym: repair - to fix or make right (something damaged or faulty)
  • Antonym: worsen - to make or become worse

Etymology

From Latin 'remedium' (a cure, medicine), from 're-' (again) + 'mederi' (to heal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛmədi / (AmE), / ˈrɛmɪdi / (BrE)
A medicine, treatment, or therapy that cures a disease or relieves pain.

Examples

As a result, most people do n't have more than a vague idea what folklore actually is; they see it as a potpourri of charming, moral legends and patriotic anecdotes, with a superstition or remedy thrown in here and there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure - A treatment that restores health; makes someone well again
  • Synonym: therapy - Treatment intended to relieve or heal a disorder

Etymology

From Latin remedium (a cure, medicine), from re- (again) + mederi (to heal).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛmədi / (AmE), / ˈrɛmɪdi / (BrE)
an action or method for correcting a fault, error, or harmful situation

Examples

A traditional Lao explanation is that the moon was being swallowed by a toad, and the remedy was to make all possible noise, ideally with firearms.
Strindberg's remedy for this condition was to tear down the old structures and build anew from the ground up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redress - remedy or compensation for a wrong or grievance
  • Synonym: cure - a means of healing or restoring health

Etymology

From Latin remedium (a cure, remedy), from re- (back) + mederi (to heal).
