booze

1. NOUN

/ buz / (AmE), / buÀêz / (BrE)
(informal) Alcoholic drink, especially a distilled beverage such as whiskey, vodka, or rum.

Examples

These booze customers had until then been buying their supplies from the Sheldon, Saltis-McErlane, and Druggan-Lake gangs, and now they were competing for trade with the Torrio-Capone saloons; once again O' Banion's brash recklessness had caused a proliferation of ill will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquor - distilled alcoholic beverage
  • Synonym: alcohol - ethanol or any beverage containing alcohol

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'busen' (to drink to excess) or an alteration of 'boose' (to drink heavily).
