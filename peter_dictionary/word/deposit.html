deposit

1. NOUN

/ dəˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
an accumulation of sediment, minerals, or other substances laid down by natural processes

Examples

Notice should be taken of unusual rock formations, deposits, or shapes of the earth's crust in your region.

Etymology

From Latin 'depositum', meaning 'a thing deposited' or 'that which is laid aside'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to put or place something somewhere firmly or carefully

Examples

Then, a group of eggs is deposited in a cavity in the beebread loaf and the egg compartment is closed.
When she succeeds, she usually manages to slip in unobtrusively, to deposit an egg on a completed loaf of beebread before the bumblebees seal the egg compartment.

Synset Examples

She posited her hand on his shoulder
deposit the suitcase on the bench
fix your eyes on this spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: place - to put something in a particular position
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away from where it is placed

Etymology

From Latin 'depositus', past participle of 'deponere' (to put down).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a layer or mass of material, such as sand, mud, or minerals, that has been laid down by a natural process, especially by water, wind, or glaciers

Examples

The best chance, of course, is offered by gently sloping terrain where the water remains close to the surface and where the air is dry, so that a high evaporation leaves salty deposits which permit only sparse plant growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sediment - matter that settles to the bottom of a liquid; similar to natural deposits

Etymology

From Latin deponere (to lay aside), via Old French deposer.

4. VERB

/ dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to put money or something valuable into a bank account or safe place for safekeeping

Examples

The forced sale of the General Motors stock owned by or allocable to Christiana, Delaware, and the stockholders of Delaware, and deposited with the trustee, would result in a tax to those parties at the capital gains rate.
For the convenience of guests bundle centers have been established throughout the city and suburbs where the donations may be deposited between now and the date of the big event.

Synset Examples

She deposits her paycheck every month

Related Terms

  • Antonym: withdraw - to take money out of a bank account

Etymology

From Latin depositus, past participle of deponere (to lay aside).

5. VERB

/ dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to put or lay down (something, especially a substance) in a particular place, often gradually or as part of a process

Examples

"A remarkable substance ", says Dr. Keys, "quite apart from its tendency to be deposited in the walls of arteries ".
When thus deposited, Keys says that cholesterol is mainly responsible for the arterial blockages that culminate in heart attacks.
Explains Keys: As the fatty protein molecules travel in the bloodstream, they are deposited in the intima, or inner wall of a coronary artery.

Synset Examples

lodge a bullet in the table
stick your thumb in the crack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulate - to gather or collect over time

Etymology

From Latin deponere (to lay aside, put down), via French déposer.

6. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑːzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a sum of money placed in a bank or similar financial institution for safekeeping or to earn interest

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin depositum (a thing deposited), from deponere (to put aside).
