exterminate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɝːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
To destroy or kill a group of living beings completely, especially on a large scale.

Examples

And then Eichmann was quoted as saying, in non English, that Hitler's plan to exterminate the Jews was nonsense ".
In other words, the promulgators of the murder plan made clear that physically exterminating the Jews was but an extension of the anti-Semitic measures already operating in every phase of German life, and that the new conspiracy counted on the general anti-Semitism that had made those measures effective, as a readiness for murder.
"Exterminatin' cow thieves is just a business proposition with me ", he 'd blandly announce.

Synset Examples

Hitler wanted to exterminate the Jews, Gypsies, Communists, and homosexuals of Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eradicate - to destroy or remove completely

Etymology

From Latin exterminare (to drive out, expel, destroy), from ex- (out of, from) + terminus (boundary).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɝːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or get rid of completely; to eliminate all traces of (something or someone)

Examples

Watch out, so we don't exterminate you!

Synset Examples

the vestiges of political democracy were soon uprooted
root out corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eradicate - to destroy completely; put an end to
  • Synonym: annihilate - to destroy utterly; obliterate
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep safe from harm or destruction

Etymology

From Latin exterminare (to drive out, expel), from ex- (out of) + terminus (boundary).
