creditable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛdɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪtəbəl / (BrE)
Deserving praise or approval, though not necessarily outstanding.

Examples

Soaring to stardom along with him, Margo Felicity Brighetti, a luscious and curvaceously beguiling Italian starlet, turns in a creditable performance as an airplane mechanic.

Synset Examples

the student's effort on the essay--though not outstanding--was creditable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commendable - deserving praise; worthy of commendation
  • Synonym: praiseworthy - worthy of praise
  • Antonym: blameworthy - deserving blame or criticism

Etymology

From Medieval Latin creditabilis, from Latin creditum (a loan, a thing entrusted).
