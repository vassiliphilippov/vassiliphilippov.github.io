tornado

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˈneɪdoʊ / (AmE), / tɔːˈneɪdəʊ / (BrE)
A violent, rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground, typically causing significant damage in a narrow path.

Examples

The basement or belowground shelters also will serve for tornado or hurricane protection.
I was in the path of the tornado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurricane - a large, powerful tropical storm with strong winds
  • Synonym: cyclone - a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Spanish 'tornado' (thunderstorm), from 'tornar' (to turn), influenced by Spanish 'tronada' (thunderstorm).
