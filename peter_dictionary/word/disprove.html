disprove

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈpruv / (AmE), / dɪsˈpruːv / (BrE)
To show that something is not true or is incorrect, especially by providing evidence or argument.

Examples

It is this conclusion that we challenge; we do so by disproving the presumption on which it is based.

Synset Examples

The physicist disproved his colleagues' theories

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prove - to show that something is true by providing evidence

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'probare' (to prove).
