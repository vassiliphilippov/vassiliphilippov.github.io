blindly

1. ADV

/ ˈblaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndli / (BrE)
In a way that is done without being able to see or without looking.

Examples

But he groped blindly.
He at once cancels the celebrations and, buckling on his scimitar, stumbles blindly from the house, where he is hit and killed by a passing oxcart.

Synset Examples

he felt around his desk blindly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sightlessly - in a way that lacks the use of sight; without seeing

Etymology

From 'blind' + '-ly', forming the adverb.

2. ADV

/ ˈblaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndli / (BrE)
Without thinking, preparation, or sufficient information; without a rational basis.

Examples

But is that not like going to a chemistry laboratory and blindly pouring out liquids and powders from an array of bottles and then, after stirring, expecting a new wonder drug inevitably to result?

Synset Examples

they bought the car blindly
he picked a wife blindly

Etymology

From 'blind' (lacking sight) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
