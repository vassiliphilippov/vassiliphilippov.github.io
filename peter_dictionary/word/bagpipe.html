bagpipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɡˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈbæɡˌpaɪp / (BrE)
a traditional musical instrument with pipes and a bag that is inflated by the mouth or a bellows, producing sound when air is squeezed out through pipes

Examples

The boy's fingers and mouth operated with the skilled unity of a bagpipe player, pressing and pulling, delighting in what he did.

Etymology

From Middle English 'bagpipe', from 'bag' + 'pipe'.
