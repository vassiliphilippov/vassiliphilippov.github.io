hard-boiled

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrd ˌbɔɪld / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌbɔɪld / (BrE)
(of a person) tough and unfazed, typically as a result of harsh experience; showing little emotion or sympathy.

Examples

In the American "hardboiled "detective story of the' 20 s and' 30 s, the spirit of the mad genius from Baker Street lives on.
Surrounded by crime and violence everywhere, the "hardboiled "private eye can retain his purity only through a life of self-imposed isolation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - strong and not easily affected or influenced
  • Antonym: soft-hearted - easily moved to pity or compassion

Etymology

Originally referring to eggs, the figurative use for people developed in early 20th-century American English, suggesting someone who is 'cooked hard'—i.e., toughened by adversity.
