pillory

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈpɪl.əri / (BrE)
To publicly criticize or ridicule someone, especially as a way to shame or humiliate them.

Examples

Then, not many years later, the Un-American Activities Committee, under the leadership of Martin Dies, pilloried hundreds of decent, patriotic citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridicule - to make fun of or mock
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French *pilori*, ultimately from Medieval Latin *pilloria* (stone barrier).
