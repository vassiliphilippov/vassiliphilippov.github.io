spit

1. VERB

/ spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
to say something in a forceful, angry, or contemptuous way

Examples

That is, when Mr. Milstein thrust straight to the core of the music, sparks flying, bow shredding, violin singing, glittering and sometimes spitting, Mr. Hendl could go along.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snarl - to speak in an angry, aggressive way
  • Synonym: sneer - to speak with mocking contempt

2. VERB

/ spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
to force saliva, phlegm, or other substances from the mouth, often as a sign of anger or contempt

Examples

"Bastards ", he said aloud, spitting on them.
"Yehhh ", said Coughlin, practically spitting on him.
Watson spat on the ground.
Fuzzy caterpillars, snails with their sensitive horns, struggling grasshoppers held by their long hind legs and commanded to "spit tobacco, spit ".

Synset Examples

The father of the victim spat at the alleged murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectorate - to cough or spit out from the throat or lungs

Etymology

Old English *spittan*, of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
Saliva; the clear liquid produced in the mouth that moistens it and begins the digestion of starches.

Examples

Your life won't be worth spit!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saliva - the liquid produced by salivary glands in the mouth

Etymology

Old English *spittan* (to eject saliva), related to German *spitzen* (to spit).
