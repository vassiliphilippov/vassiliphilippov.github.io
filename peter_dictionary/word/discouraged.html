discouraged

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɝːrɪdʒd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒd / (BrE)
Lacking confidence or hope; having lost enthusiasm or the will to continue, especially due to setbacks or difficulties.

Examples

When he gets the answers to his questions he will be discouraged.

Synset Examples

desperate demoralized people looking for work
felt discouraged by the magnitude of the problem
the disheartened instructor tried vainly to arouse their interest

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraged - having confidence or hope, inspired to continue

Etymology

From the prefix dis- (expressing reversal) + courage, from Old French *coragier* (to inspire with courage).
