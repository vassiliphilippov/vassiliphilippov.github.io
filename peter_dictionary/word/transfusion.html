transfusion

1. NOUN

/ trænsˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process of transferring blood or blood plasma into a person's vein or artery, typically for medical treatment

Examples

No additional transfusions were necessary after the institution of prednisone in July, 1957, in an initial dose of 40 mg. daily with gradual tapering to 10 mg. daily.

Etymology

From Latin 'transfusio', from 'transfundere' (to pour from one vessel into another), from 'trans-' (across) + 'fundere' (to pour).
