bankruptcy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌrʌptsi / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkrʌptsi / (BrE)
A complete lack or failure of a particular quality; the state of having none of a specific abstract property.

Examples

But what a super Herculean task it is to winnow anything of value from the mud beplastered arguments used so freely, particularly since such common use is made of cliches and stereotypes, in themselves declarations of intellectual bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

spiritual bankruptcy
moral bankruptcy
intellectual bankruptcy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success or falling short of a standard
  • Synonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage of something needed

Etymology

From bankrupt + -cy, with bankrupt from Italian *banca rotta* ‘broken bench’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkrʌptsi / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkrəptsi / (BrE)
The legal state of being unable to pay debts as they become due.

Examples

But to the cattlemen who had been facing bankruptcy from rustling losses and to the cowboys who had been faced with lay-offs a few years earlier, he was becoming a vastly different type of legendary figure.
We may celebrate in bankruptcy court.

Synset Examples

the company had to declare bankruptcy
fraudulent loans led to the failure of many banks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvency - the state of being unable to pay debts owed

Etymology

From Italian *bancarotta*, from *banca* (bench, bank) and *rotta* (broken), referring to a broken bench—symbolizing a banker's insolvency.
