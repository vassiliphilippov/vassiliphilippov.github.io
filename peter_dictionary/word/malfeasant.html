malfeasant

1. NOUN

/ mælˈfiːzənt / (AmE), / mælˈfiːzənt / (BrE)
A person who is guilty of malfeasance or wrongdoing, especially in a public or official capacity.

Examples

The innocent malfeasant, filled with that supreme sense of honor found in bars, insisted upon replacing the destroyed monocle - and did, over the protests of the former owner - with a square monocle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • Synonym: wrongdoer - a person who does something immoral or illegal
  • Synonym: malefactor - a person who commits a crime or some other wrong

Etymology

From malfeasance, from Latin mal- (bad) + Old French faisance (doing).
