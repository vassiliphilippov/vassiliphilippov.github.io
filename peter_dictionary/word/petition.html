petition

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
A formal written request, typically one signed by many people, appealing to an authority for a particular cause or action.

Examples

His petition charged mental cruelty.
The petition listed the mayor's occupation as "attorney "and his age as 71.
The petition said that the couple has not lived together as man and wife for more than a year.
Henry L. Bowden was listed on the petition as the mayor's attorney.
In the Blue Ridge meeting, the audience was warned that entering a candidate for governor would force it to take petitions out into voting precincts to obtain the signatures of registered voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - the act of asking for something formally or politely
  • Synonym: application - a formal request, especially to an authority or organization

Etymology

From Latin petitionem (a request, entreaty), from petere (to seek, request).

2. VERB

/ pəˈtɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / pəˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
to make a formal written request to an authority or official body, especially by collecting signatures or following a prescribed format

Examples

If, in the trustee's judgment, "reasonable market conditions "did not prevail during any given year, he was to be allowed to petition the court for an extension of time within the ten year period.
Its building was first proposed in 1791, when a group of citizens, mostly Newburyport men, petitioned the General Court for an act of incorporation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to ask for something politely or formally
  • Degree Higher: demand - to ask for something forcefully, not as polite or formal as petition

Etymology

From Latin petitionem (a request, solicitation), from petere (to seek, request).
