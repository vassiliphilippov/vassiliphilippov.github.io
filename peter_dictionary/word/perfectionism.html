perfectionism

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency to demand perfection in oneself or others, and to regard anything less than perfect as unacceptable.

Examples

The parent was asked to describe the child's typical behavior in certain standard situations in which there was an opportunity to observe tendencies toward perfectionism in demands upon self and others, irrational conformity to rules, orderliness, punctuality, and need for certainty.

Synset Examples

his perfectionism seemed excessive to his students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfectionist - a person who refuses to accept any standard short of perfection
  • Antonym: imperfectionism - lack of concern with achieving perfection

Etymology

From 'perfectionist' + '-ism', modeled after 'perfection'. Ultimately from Latin 'perfectio' (completeness, perfection).
