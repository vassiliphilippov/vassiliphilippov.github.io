opposing

1. ADJ

/ əˈpoʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpəʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of people, teams, or forces) competing, fighting, or acting against each other.

Examples

The opposing aircraft continued to come on.
The guards on the opposing teams all remembered to bring their W-2s.

Synset Examples

opponent (or opposing) armies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - completely different or contrary in position or meaning

Etymology

Present participle of 'oppose', from Latin 'opponere' (to set against).
