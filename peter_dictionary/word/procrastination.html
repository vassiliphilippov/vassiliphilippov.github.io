procrastination

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌkræstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌkræs.tɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of delaying or postponing something that should be done, often unnecessarily

Examples

All political interests supported electoral planning, although there are some signs that the inherent uncertainties of a popular judgment led to some procrastination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - a period of waiting before an event happens or before something is done
  • Synonym: dilly-dallying - wasting time, especially by being slow or indecisive

Etymology

From Latin procrastinatio (a putting off), from procrastinare (to put forward until tomorrow).
