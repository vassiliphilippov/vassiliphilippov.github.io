validate

1. VERB

/ ˈvælɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvælɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to officially confirm or make something legally valid, especially by formal approval or sanction

Examples

One validated acts of school districts.
If the indenture is accepted, the authority will proceed to validate a bond issue repayable from revenue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalidate - to make something invalid, especially legally; cancel
  • Synonym: authorize - to give official permission or approval for something

Etymology

From Latin validatus, past participle of validare, from validus (strong, effective).

2. VERB

/ ˈvælɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvælɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to demonstrate or confirm that something is sound, true, or worthwhile; to establish the validity of

Examples

There is much research evidence to validate the use of the instrument in differentiating individuals who are likely to manifest anxiety in varying degrees.
The hypothesis ventured here is that it does, and that evidence is accumulating validating that proposition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalidate - to show something is not valid; to make invalid
  • Synonym: confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something

Etymology

From Latin validatus, past participle of validare (to make strong), from validus (strong).
