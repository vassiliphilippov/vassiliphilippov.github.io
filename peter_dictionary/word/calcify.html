calcify

1. VERB

/ ˈkælsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(of tissue or other organic matter) to become hardened or impregnated with calcium salts, often resulting in loss of flexibility.

Examples

In particular, the orifices of the right renal and celiac arteries were virtually occluded, and both calcified common iliac arteries were completely occluded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ossify - to turn into bone or become bony; to become rigid or inflexible

Etymology

From French *calcifier*, from Latin *calx* (lime, limestone) + *-fy* (to make).
