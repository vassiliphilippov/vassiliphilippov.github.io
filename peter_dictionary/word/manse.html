manse

1. NOUN

/ mænz / (AmE), / mɑːns / (BrE)
A large, impressive house, often used to refer to the residence of a clergyman.

Examples

It's a wonder, really, to how much mendacious trouble Larkin puts himself to sell the Jerebohms that preposterous manse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house or residence

Etymology

From Latin 'mansio' (a dwelling), via Old French and Medieval Latin 'mansus'.
