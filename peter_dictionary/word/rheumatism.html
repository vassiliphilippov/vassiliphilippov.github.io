rheumatism

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈruːmətɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A disease marked by inflammation and pain in the joints, muscles, or connective tissue, especially in the elderly.

Examples

Says Wallace F. Jannsen, director of the FDA's Division of Public Information: "Quacks are apt to direct their appeal directly to older people, or to sufferers from chronic ailments such as arthritis, rheumatism, diabetes, and cancer.
When her right hand was incapacitated by the rheumatism, Sadie learned to write with her left hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of the joints, causing pain and stiffness

Etymology

From Late Latin rheumatismus, from Greek rheumatismos (subject to flows or discharges).
