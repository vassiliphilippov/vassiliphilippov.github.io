animate

1. VERB

/ ˈænəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈænɪmeɪt / (BrE)
To heighten or intensify (a feeling, quality, expression, etc.); to make more lively, spirited, or energetic.

Examples

In reference to Brown's raid she wrote, "though we are non-resistants and religiously believe it better to reform by moral and not by carnal weapons we know thee was anemated [ sic ] by the most generous and philanthropic motives ".

Synset Examples

These paintings exalt the imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspire - to fill with the urge or ability to do or feel something; to stimulate emotionally
  • Synonym: exalt - to raise in rank, character, or status; to elevate or intensify
  • Antonym: dampen - to make less sustained or intense; to diminish

Etymology

From Latin animatus, past participle of animare (to give life, make spirited), from anima (soul, spirit).
