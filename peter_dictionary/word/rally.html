rally

1. NOUN

/ ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
a large public gathering of people, especially one intended to arouse enthusiasm or support for a cause, political campaign, or movement

Examples

State Party Chairman James W. Dorsey added that enthusiasm was picking up for a state rally to be held Sept. 8 in Savannah at which newly elected Texas Sen. John Tower will be the featured speaker.
LaGuardia's multi lingual rallies, when he is running for Congress, are well staged, and wind up in a wild Jewish folk-dance that is really great musical theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstration - a public meeting or march expressing political views or protest
  • Synonym: protest - a public expression of objection or disapproval, often a gathering
  • Degree Lower: celebration - a gathering to mark a joyful occasion

Etymology

From French 'rallier' (to assemble, unite), from Old French, from re- (again) + 'allier' (to unite).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
an increase or renewed effort after a decline, especially a sudden burst of activity in sports, finance, or a comeback in a contest

Examples

Jackie Brandt singled deep into the hole at short to start the rally.

Synset Examples

he singled to start a rally in the 9th inning
he feared the rallying of their troops for a counterattack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comeback - a return to a former position or condition, especially after a setback
  • Synonym: resurgence - an increase or revival after a period of little activity, popularity, or occurrence
  • Antonym: collapse - a sudden failure or breakdown after an effort

Etymology

From French *rallier* (to reunite, rally), from Latin *ad* (to) + *alligāre* (to bind).

3. VERB

/ ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
to bring people together for a common purpose, cause, or action; to muster support or resources

Examples

This is demonstrated especially when there is awareness of radically different value orientation elsewhere; for example Americans rally to Christian values vis-a-vis those of atheistic communism.
For nearly a century the cry has never failed to rally the fighting men of the regiment.
Against that other man he could rally his anger; against this bent man in the chair he was powerless.

Synset Examples

drum up support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemble - to gather together in one place for a common purpose
  • Antonym: disperse - to break up and scatter in different directions

Etymology

From French 'rallier', from Old French 'ralier' (to assemble again), from re- (again) + 'allier' (to unite).

4. VERB

/ ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
to bring together and motivate people, especially troops, for a common purpose or action, often in preparation for battle or conflict

Examples

Before he could return to Burma, Field Marshal Slim had to rally the defeated remnants of a discouraged army and unite them with fresh recruits.
When they learn you 're in the hills though, they 'll rally, do n't worry about that ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemble - to bring people together in one place for a common purpose
  • Antonym: disperse - to break up and move in different directions

Etymology

From French 'rallier' (to reassemble), from re- (again) + 'allier' (to unite).

5. VERB

/ ˈræli / (AmE), / ˈræli / (BrE)
to recover strength, spirits, or position after a decline or setback

Examples

Beatie, getting no sympathy for her misfortune, soon rallies and finds that although she has lost a lover she has gained her freedom.

Synset Examples

The jilted lover soon rallied and found new friends
The stock market rallied

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to return to a normal state after difficulty
  • Antonym: relapse - to fall back into a former (usually worse) state

Etymology

From Old French 'rallier', from re- (again) + 'allier' (to unite).
