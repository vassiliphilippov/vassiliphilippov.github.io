2. VERB

/ ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
to measure or regulate the amount, rate, or flow of something using a meter

Examples

The sewage flow into the treatment plant was metered and continuously recorded on 24 - hr charts.

Synset Examples

meter the flow of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measure - to ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From French 'mètre', from Greek 'metron' (measure).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring and indicating the amount, degree, or rate of something.

Examples

There is a possibility that battery powered radios with built-in radiation meters may become available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - instrument for measuring or testing something
  • Synonym: detector - device for discovering the presence or amount of something

Etymology

From Greek 'metron' (measure) via French 'mètre'.
