voyage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long journey to a distant place, especially by sea or in space

Examples

"I had no idea that you, too, would be on this glorious voyage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a specific purpose, often involving exploration

Etymology

From Old French *voiage* (journey), from Latin *viaticum* (provision for a journey).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪ.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long journey by sea or water, especially in a ship

Examples

She carried, besides her captain, a crew of twenty-one and provisions for a voyage of exploration of the Arctic waters of North America.
The man who now commanded her had started the voyage as an ordinary seaman.
Not enough to do away with all doubts, but sufficient to give a fairly accurate picture of the events of the voyage.
This is the story of his last tragic voyage, as nearly as we are able - or ever, probably, will be able - to determine:
The purpose of this fourth voyage was clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another; more general than 'voyage'
  • Synonym: odyssey - a long and adventurous voyage or journey

Etymology

From Old French *voiage* (journey), from Latin *viaticum* (provisions for a journey).
