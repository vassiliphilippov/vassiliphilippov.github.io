leprosy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛprəsi / (AmE), / ˈlɛprəsi / (BrE)
A chronic infectious disease, chiefly found in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by inflamed nodules under the skin and degeneration of body tissues, caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae.

Examples

Eight or ten years ago, a couple of French hoods stole a priceless Khmer head from the Musee Guimet, in Paris, and a week later crawled into the Salpetriere with unmistakable symptoms of leprosy.

Etymology

From Late Latin lepra (leprosy), from Greek lepros (scaly).
