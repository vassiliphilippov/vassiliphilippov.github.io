moss

1. NOUN

/ mɑs / (AmE), / mɒs / (BrE)
A small, green, flowerless plant that typically grows in dense clumps or mats on damp or shady surfaces.

Examples

Most species seem to prefer a ready-made hollow such as a deserted mouse nest, a bird house, or the hole made by a woodpecker; some show a definite liking for making their nest in moss.

Etymology

Old English 'mos' (bog, marsh), related to Old Norse 'mosi'.
