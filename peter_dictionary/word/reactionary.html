reactionary

1. NOUN

/ riˈækʃəˌneri / (AmE), / riˈakʃənəri / (BrE)
A person who holds extremely conservative views and opposes social or political progress or liberalism.

Examples

The two main charges levelled against the Bourbons by liberals is that they are racists and social reactionaries.
He had never heard the word reactionary before his life as a POW began.
When I talked to Ching about it, he said, Everyone can learn, if he is not a Reactionary or lazy.
It is plain as hell Johnson is no Reactionary.
Why did n't the damn Reactionaries bump him off?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - a person advocating reform or progress, especially in politics

Etymology

From French réactionnaire, from réaction (reaction), referring to opposition to revolutionary change.

2. ADJ

/ riˈækʃəˌneri / (AmE), / riˈækʃənəri / (BrE)
Marked by strong opposition to social or political change; extremely conservative.

Examples

Turn the bastards over to me - to me and my boys - no nigger ever got what would be comin to them - reactionary bastards.
Lousy Reactionary bastards been tryin to fuck up the Program for months.
Dirty Reactionary bastards comin down here in the night and bumpin off ol Slater and those other poor bastards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new, liberal ideas
  • Degree Lower: conservative - disposed to preserve existing conditions, institutions, etc., or to restore traditional ones

Etymology

From English 'reaction', modeled on French 'réactionnaire' (opposed to revolution), from 'réaction' (reaction).
