under

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə / (BrE)
(in combinations) lower in rank, power, or authority than someone or something else; subordinate.

Examples

Before daylight Sunday morning, a posse of twenty-three men under the leadership of Deputy Sheriff Frank MacPherson of Catskill followed the trail to the house of Francisco Chaves, where 100 to 150 Mexicans had gathered.

Synset Examples

an under secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subordinate - lower in rank or position

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə / (BrE)
Situated below or beneath something else.

Examples

It was a fine broody hen, white, with a maternal eye and a striking abundance of feathers in the under region of the abdomen.
It looks like there'’s - $100 under my hat.

Synset Examples

nether garments
the under parts of a machine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper - situated above or higher than something else

3. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə / (BrE)
In or into a state of being controlled, ruled, or subject to something or someone.

Examples

He'’s not under subpoena and his reputation in his own country is impeccable.

Synset Examples

we must keep our disappointment under

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subject to - being under the control or authority of something or someone

4. ADV

/ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə / (BrE)
At or to a lower amount, number, or level than; less than a specified quantity or limit.

Examples

Now the price for the license is less than $20,000, am I right?

Synset Examples

fifty dollars or under

Related Terms

  • Synonym: below - less than a given number or amount
  • Antonym: over - more than a given number or amount
  • Antonym: above - more than a given number or amount
