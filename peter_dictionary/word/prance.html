prance

1. VERB

/ præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
to walk or move with high steps in a lively or proud way, often to attract attention or impress others

Examples

He was prancing along the hall, heading for the next flight of stairs.
This could be told chiefly from a sort of head tossing and prancing, a horse like balkiness of demeanor.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk stiffly and proudly, often to impress
  • Synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a very confident and arrogant way

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *prauncen*, of uncertain origin.
