dissension

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
Disagreement or conflict of opinions, especially among people who are expected to work together.

Examples

Instead of quelling the dissension, as many captains of the era would have done (Sir Francis Drake lopped a man's head off under similar circumstances), Hudson decided to be reasonable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discord - disagreement or lack of harmony
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or consensus among people

Etymology

From Latin 'dissensio', from 'dissentire' (to disagree).
