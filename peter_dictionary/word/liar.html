liar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪə / (BrE)
A person who tells lies, especially habitually.

Examples

Some liar's logic, a wisp of optimism as fragile as the scent of tropical blossoms that came through the window (a euphoria perhaps engendered by the pill Fritzie had given her), consoled her for a moment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truth-teller - a person who always tells the truth

Etymology

From Old English lēogere, from lēogan (to lie).
