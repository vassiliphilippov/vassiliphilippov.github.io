dovetail

1. VERB

/ ˈdoʊvˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊvteɪl / (BrE)
to fit or combine together perfectly or harmoniously, as if by interlocking parts

Examples

He says the state, in order to proceed with economic development, must develop an understanding of how the various parts of its economy fit together and dovetail into the national economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlock - to connect or fit together with another part
  • Antonym: contradict - to be in opposition to, not fit together

Etymology

From the noun 'dovetail', referring to a type of woodworking joint resembling a dove's tail, where parts fit tightly together.
