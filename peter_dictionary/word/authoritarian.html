authoritarian

1. ADJ

/ əˌθɔːrɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
Favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority, especially that of the government, at the expense of personal freedom; characteristic of an absolute ruler or system.

Examples

Why, in the first place, call himself a liberal if he is against laissez-faire and favors an authoritarian central government with womb-to-tomb controls over everybody?
Nevertheless, impulses still exist among the ruling elite to rationalize and thus to perpetuate the need for centralized and authoritarian practices.

Synset Examples

an authoritarian regime
autocratic government
despotic rulers
a dictatorial rule that lasted for the duration of the war
a tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - characterized by absolute power or authority; domineering
  • Synonym: dictatorial - typical of a ruler with total power; domineering
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles

Etymology

From authority + -arian, influenced by earlier use of authority as an adjective.
