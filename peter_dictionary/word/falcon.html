falcon

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæl.kən, ˈfɔl.kən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.kən, ˈfæl.kən / (BrE)
A diurnal bird of prey with long, pointed wings adapted for swift flight, belonging to the family Falconidae.

Examples

"Such as' sending the cat to guard the mice', or' the falcon to protect the dove', or most terribly sharp of all,' the human being to save humanity "'.
Sam Spade joins forces with a band of adventurers in search of a priceless jeweled statue of a falcon; but when the bird is found at last, it turns out to be a fake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - bird of prey with broader wings and different hunting habits

Etymology

From Old French 'faucon', from Late Latin 'falco' (a falcon), related to 'falx' (sickle), referring to the shape of the bird's claws or wings.
