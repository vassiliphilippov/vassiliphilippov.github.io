industrialism

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
An economic and social system in which industry and manufacturing are the dominant sectors, rather than agriculture or craft production.

Examples

The riotous onrush of industrialism after the War for Southern Independence and the general secular drift to the Religion of Humanity, however, prepared the way for a reception of the French Revolution's socialistic offspring of one sort of another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industrialization - the process of developing industries on a wide scale

Etymology

From 'industrial' + '-ism', modeled after similar formations like 'capitalism'.
