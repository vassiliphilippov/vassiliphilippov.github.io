chuck

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌk / (AmE), / tʃʌk / (BrE)
(informal) Food or a meal, especially as used in casual or rustic contexts.

Examples

"We 'll ride out as soon as we 've had chuck ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grub - informal word for food or a meal
  • Synonym: chow - slang for food or a meal

Etymology

Possibly from English dialect 'chuck' meaning food or to throw (as in 'chucking' food to animals), but exact origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ tʃʌk / (AmE), / tʃʌk / (BrE)
To throw something casually or carelessly.

Examples

Chuck a length of 1 8 "dia. drill rod into a drill press or some similar turning device and while it is rotating file the end square and then file a slight taper 1 8 "long.
Chuck a 2 "or 3 "piece of 1 8 "dia. drill rod in a drill press or electric hand tool.

Synset Examples

chuck the ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or casually
  • Degree Higher: hurl - to throw something with force
  • Degree Lower: lob - to throw in a high arc, often gently

Etymology

Origin obscure; possibly imitative or related to the Scottish 'chuck' meaning 'to toss or throw'.
