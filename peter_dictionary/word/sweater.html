sweater

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛtər / (AmE), / ˈswɛtə / (BrE)
A warm, knitted or crocheted garment, usually with long sleeves, worn on the upper part of the body.

Examples

The children rushed off to get rid of their sweaters; and Arlene began tapping the kitchen door open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumper - A British term for a knitted garment, especially with no opening at the front

Etymology

From 'sweat' + '-er', originally referring to a garment intended to induce sweating during exercise (late 19th century).
