epitaph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌtæf / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪtɑːf / (BrE)
An inscription written on a tombstone or monument, usually in memory of the person buried there.

Examples

Henrietta's feeling of identity with Sara Sullam was crowned by her discovery of the coincidence that Sara's epitaph in the Jewish cemetery in Venice referred to her as "the Sulamite ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obituary - a notice of a death, usually with a brief biography of the deceased

Etymology

From Greek epitaphion (funeral oration), from epi (upon) + taphos (tomb).
