lined

1. ADJ

/ laɪnd / (AmE), / laɪnd / (BrE)
having a border formed by a line or row of something, such as trees, shrubs, or buildings.

Examples

And wherever the new thruways go up their banks are lined by neat glass and metal and colored brick light industry.
Harnessing a team to a buckboard, they drove out to a willow lined creek about a half-mile off, then climbed down and began chopping.
I followed a shrubbery lined gravel path alongside the house to the pool.

Synset Examples

tree lined streets

2. ADJ

/ laɪnd / (AmE), / laɪnd / (BrE)
(of skin or a face) marked with lines or wrinkles; showing visible lines or seams, especially as a result of age, wear, or emotion.

Examples

He looked at the lined face with vague interest; he felt he was noting it, as if it were something he might think about when he grew stronger.

Synset Examples

their lined faces were immeasurably sad
a seamed face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkled - (of skin or fabric) having many small lines because of age, use, or being pressed

Etymology

Past participle of 'line', from Old English 'līn' (flax, linen) but used here in the sense of 'to mark with lines'.
