stall

1. NOUN

/ stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
a small enclosed area set off by partitions for a particular purpose, such as selling goods at a market or for seating in a theatre, church, or stable

Examples

His head hung over the boards of Katie's stall; before it was sprawled the mangled corpse of the bearded stranger.
Drenched and shaking, he stood near the sweet-smelling stall and dared to pat her muzzle.
Monsieur Favre sat down in his high-backed stall, lips compressed, eyes glinting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booth - a small temporary structure used for selling goods or providing information

Etymology

From Old English 'steall' (place, position, stall).

2. NOUN

/ stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
A small booth or stand in a market or fair where goods are displayed and sold.

Examples

Adam watched his own hands make the caressing, anxious movement that, when rain falls and nobody comes, and ruin draws close like a cat rubbing against the ankles, has been the ritual of stall vendors, forever.
At the same time he started walking the streets, peering at the people passing or shopping at the stalls, storing up fresh impressions of what they looked like, how they moved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booth - a temporary stall or small enclosure for selling or exhibiting

3. VERB

/ stɑl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
to delay taking action or avoid doing something in order to gain time or put off making a decision

Examples

He stalled for a half-hour longer, hoping to hear something from Vecchio about the ransom money.
In going away Debonnie got behind several lengths, stalling at the start - she is a little fussy.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastinate - to delay or postpone action; put off doing something
  • Antonym: hasten - to move or act quickly

4. VERB

/ stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
to deliberately delay taking action or making a decision, usually in order to gain time or avoid something

Examples

I stalled him off.

Synset Examples

she doesn't want to write the report, so she is stalling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastinate - delay or postpone action; put off doing something
  • Antonym: expedite - make an action or process happen sooner or be accomplished more quickly

5. NOUN

/ stɑl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
a compartment in a stable or barn where a single animal, such as a horse or cow, is kept and fed

Examples

The fact that Jess's horse had not been returned to its stall could indicate that Diane's information had been wrong, but Curt did n't interpret it this way.
Reaching across the side of the stall, he slapped the buckskin on the rump.
The startled animal let out a terrified squeal and thrashed around in the stall.
He slapped the buckskin again and it kicked wildly, its hoofs rattling the side of the stall.
As he crossed to the side of the stall, Curt drew his gun and clicked back the hammer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - small enclosed area for confining animals
  • Synonym: sty - enclosure for pigs

Etymology

From Old English steall (place, position), related to Old High German stall (place, stable).

6. VERB

/ stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
(of an engine, vehicle, or process) to come to a stop, typically because of a problem or obstruction.

Examples

The education bill appears to be temporarily stalled in the Rules Committee, where two Northern Democratic members who usually vote with the Administration are balking because of the religious controversy.

Synset Examples

The car stalled in the driveway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to cease moving or operating
  • Antonym: start - to begin moving or operating
