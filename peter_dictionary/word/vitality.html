vitality

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈtæləti / (AmE), / vaɪˈtalɪti / (BrE)
energy and enthusiasm; the quality of being lively, spirited, or vigorous in manner or style.

Examples

It is really as though the Russians have seen in this character the oftentimes underlying vitality and courage of supposed buffoons.
He injected more vitality into the score than it has revealed in many years.
Not altogether a successful play, "Epitaph for George Dillon "overcomes through sheer vitality and power what in a lesser work might be crippling.
Related to written literature, and often remaining temporarily frozen in written form, it loses its vitality when transcribed or removed from its oral existence.
Though Americans usually lived in groups segregated by national origin or religious belief, they liked to work and shop in the noise and vitality of downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacity - the quality of being attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: energy - vigor or the capacity for vigorous activity
  • Synonym: spirit - enthusiasm or liveliness
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'vitalitas' (life, liveliness), from 'vitalis' (of life).

2. NOUN

/ vaɪˈtæləti / (AmE), / vaɪˈtælɪti / (BrE)
The state of being strong and active; energy and enthusiasm, especially in relation to physical and mental health.

Examples

The host of novel applications of electronics to medical problems is far more thrilling because of their implication in matters concerning our health and vitality.

Synset Examples

jogging works off my excess energy
he seemed full of vim and vigor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - the ability to be active; physical or mental strength
  • Synonym: vigor - physical strength and good health
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'vitalitas', from 'vitalis' (of life).
