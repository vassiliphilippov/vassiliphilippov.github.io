shield

1. NOUN

/ ʃild / (AmE), / ʃiːld / (BrE)
A protective covering or structure designed to guard a person or object from harm, damage, or danger.

Examples

To reduce heat transfer from the hot gas to this anode holder outside the region of the arc, a carbon shield was attached to the surface providing an air gap of 1/16 inch between the plate and the surface of the anode holder.
In addition, the inner surface of the carbon shield was covered with aluminum foil to reduce radiation.
Temperatures of the shield and of the surface of the water cooled anode holder were measured by thermocouples to account for heat received by the coolant but not originating from the anode plug.
Shields open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - something that blocks or protects
  • Synonym: armor - protective covering, often for the body

Etymology

From Old English *scield*, related to Old Norse *skjǫldr*, Old High German *scilt* (shield).

2. VERB

to protect someone or something from danger, harm, or unwanted attention

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
  • Synonym: defend - to resist an attack; to protect from harm

Etymology

From Old English *scieldan* (to protect, defend), from *scield* (shield).

3. NOUN

/ ʃild / (AmE), / ʃiːld / (BrE)
A broad piece of armor, often made of wood or metal, carried on the arm to protect against blows or missiles.

Examples

I even ferreted out the materials from which shields were made - linden wood covered with leather - so I 'd get the light reflections accurate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckler - small round shield held by a handle or worn on the arm

Etymology

Old English 'scield', from Proto-Germanic *skelduz.
