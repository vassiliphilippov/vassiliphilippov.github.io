misbehavior

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
Behavior that is improper or unacceptable, especially by not following rules or standards.

Examples

But even mother's loving attitude will not always prevent misbehavior.
What can a mother do then to prevent misbehavior?
Remove the child from the scene of his misbehavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: good behavior - behavior that is proper or acceptable
  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or illegal behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (badly, wrongly) + 'behavior'.
