transmissible

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪsɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or agent) capable of being passed from one individual to another, especially by infection.

Examples

Wound tumor virus is a leafhopper transmitted virus not easily transmissible by mechanical inoculation (Black, 1944; Brakke et al., 1954).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious - (of a disease) spread by direct or indirect contact
  • Synonym: infectious - (of a disease) likely to be transmitted to people, organisms, etc.

Etymology

From French 'transmissible', based on Latin 'transmittere' (to send across).
