susceptible

1. ADJ

/ səˈsɛptəbəl / (AmE), / səˈsɛptɪbəl / (BrE)
likely to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing; easily affected or influenced.

Examples

This area is highly susceptible to infection.
The New York Central notes: "The freight traffic most susceptible to raiding by the C. + O. - B. + O. provides the backbone of Central's revenues.

Synset Examples

susceptible to colds
susceptible of proof

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immune - not affected or influenced by something

Etymology

From Late Latin susceptibilis (‘capable of taking in or receiving’), from Latin suscipere (‘to receive, take up’).
