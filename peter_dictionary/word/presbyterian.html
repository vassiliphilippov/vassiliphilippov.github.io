Presbyterian

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzbɪˈtɪriən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzbɪˈtɪəriən / (BrE)
A member or follower of the Presbyterian Church, a Protestant denomination governed by elders and based on Calvinist doctrine.

Examples

They put Kate to bed and wired Jonathan and sent for the young Presbyterian minister.

Etymology

From 'Presbyter', from Greek 'presbyteros' (elder), reflecting church governance by elders.
