sentient

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnʃənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnʃiənt / (BrE)
Able to perceive or feel things; having awareness or consciousness.

Examples

"That it had found a planet on which human beings could live and which was already inhabited by sentient beings "! said Hal, forgetting in his enthusiasm that he had not been asked to speak.

Synset Examples

the living knew themselves just sentient puppets on God's stage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscious - aware of and able to think; possessing consciousness
  • Antonym: insentient - lacking sensation, feeling, or awareness

Etymology

From Latin 'sentient-', present participle of 'sentire' (to feel).
