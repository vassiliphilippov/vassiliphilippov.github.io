ignorant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡnərənt / (AmE), / ˈɪɡnərənt / (BrE)
Lacking knowledge or awareness in a particular subject or field; uneducated or uninformed about something specific.

Examples

They are often ignorant as well as underprivileged.
In its propagandistic and commercial haste to discover our folk heritage, the public has remained ignorant of definitions such as this.

Synset Examples

she is ignorant of quantum mechanics
he is musically illiterate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write; uneducated in a particular field

Etymology

From Latin 'ignorans', present participle of 'ignorare' (not know).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡ.nɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɪɡ.nə.rənt / (BrE)
Lacking knowledge, education, or awareness about a particular subject or in general; uneducated or unsophisticated.

Examples

If one wishes to discuss a literary figure who uses folklore in his work, the first thing he must realize is that the literary figure is probably part of this ignorant American public.
I have heard people talk with contempt about the British regulars, but that only proves that a lot of people talk about things of which they are deplorably ignorant.
This ignorant estimate, I repeat, is not of any interest in itself; but it becomes very important if foreign peoples react the way human beings typically do - namely, by taking steps to end up on what appears to be the winning side.
Know how to read, you ignorant fuck?

Synset Examples

an ignorant man
nescient of contemporary literature
an unlearned group incapable of understanding complex issues
exhibiting contempt for his unlettered companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneducated - lacking education; not having received much schooling
  • Antonym: knowledgeable - having a great deal of knowledge; well informed
  • Antonym: learned - having a lot of knowledge because of study

Etymology

From Latin 'ignorantem', present participle of 'ignorare' meaning 'not to know'.
