reseal

1. VERB

/ riˈsil / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsiːl / (BrE)
to seal something again after it has been opened or unsealed

Examples

This chlorine carbon tetrachloride solution was illuminated for a day following which the flask was resealed onto a vacuum system and the excess chlorine distilled off.

Synset Examples

reseal the bottle after using the medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to close something tightly so that it cannot be opened without breaking the seal

Etymology

From re- (again) + seal (to close tightly)
