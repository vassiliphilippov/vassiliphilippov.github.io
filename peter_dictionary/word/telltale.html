telltale

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛlˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛlˌteɪl / (BrE)
revealing something that is meant to be kept secret; showing or indicating something unintentionally

Examples

The son and heir of a prominent family marries a girl who has tell-tale shadows on the half-moons of her finger nails.

Synset Examples

a telling smile
a telltale panel of lights
a telltale patch of oil on the water marked where the boat went down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revealing - making something known that was hidden
  • Synonym: incriminating - suggesting guilt or involvement
  • Antonym: concealing - keeping something secret or hidden

Etymology

From 'tell' (to reveal) + 'tale' (story); originally meaning someone who reveals secrets or evidence, later extended to objects or signs.
