putout

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈpʊtˌaʊt / (BrE)
(Baseball) an out made by a fielder not resulting from a strikeout, such as by catching a fly ball or tagging a runner.

Examples

Jay Porter drew a base on balls to fill the bases but Don Wert's smash was knocked down by Rudolph for the putout.

Synset Examples

the first baseman made 15 putouts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out - an instance of a batter or runner being retired in baseball

Etymology

Formed from 'put' + 'out', referring to putting a player out in the field of play.
