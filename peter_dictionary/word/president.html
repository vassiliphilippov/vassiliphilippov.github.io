President

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (BrE)
the head of state and chief executive officer of the United States of America, elected every four years

Examples

a former Democratic candidate for President, a New Deal poet, the magazine's chief editorial writer, two newspaper columnists, head of a national broadcasting company, a popular Protestant evangelist, etc..

Synset Examples

a President is elected every four years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - leader of a republic, company, or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'praesidens' (presiding, ruling), present participle of 'praesidēre'.
