Maine

1. PROPN

/ meɪn / (AmE), / meɪn / (BrE)
a state in the northeastern United States, part of New England, known for its forests, coastline, and maritime history

Examples

After a "busy "overture, the curtain rises on a farm scene - the Ranavan Valley in northern Maine.
The state universities of Maine, New Hampshire, And Vermont are older and more "respectable "; they had less immigration to contend with.
By the power vested in me by the state of Maine, I hereby order you to serve two life sentences, back to back.
Maine National and New England First.
At the base of that wall, you'll find a rock that has no earthly business in a Maine hayfield.

Etymology

Named after the French province of Maine or possibly from a nautical term meaning 'mainland'.
