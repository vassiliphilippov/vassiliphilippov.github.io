pantomime

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpæn.tə.maɪm / (BrE)
A theatrical performance or entertainment in which the story is told by expressive bodily or facial movements, without spoken words.

Examples

Frederick Seward said his father was sleeping, and then went through a pantomime at his father's door, to prove the statement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - a performance or performer using only gestures and movement; silent acting

Etymology

From Latin pantomimus, from Greek pantomimos (imitator of all), from pan (all) + mimos (imitator, actor).

2. VERB

/ ˈpæn.t̬əˌmaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpæn.təˌmaɪm / (BrE)
to express or represent something by exaggerated gestures without speaking

Examples

In the same way he pantomimed grasping a mantel and bouncing cleanly off that, pressing his hands against the floor and bouncing cleanly off that.

Synset Examples

The acting students mimed eating an apple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - to use gestures and body movements to act without words

Etymology

From French 'pantomime' and Latin 'pantomimus', from Greek 'pantomimos' (mime actor).
