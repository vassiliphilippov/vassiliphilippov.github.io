smack

1. VERB

/ smæk / (AmE), / smæk / (BrE)
to strike something or someone with a quick, sharp blow, often making a loud sound

Examples

There was the freshness of color, the freedom of perception, the lack of self-consciousness, but with a twist that made the forms leap from the page and smack you in the eye.
Then, in the fifth, Anniston's kid catcher caught onto a curve and smacked the ball into left center field.

Synset Examples

The teacher smacked the student who had misbehaved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - to hit with an open hand or flat object
  • Degree Higher: punch - to strike with a closed fist

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a sharp slap or blow; recorded since the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ smæk / (AmE), / smæk / (BrE)
to have a quality or suggestion that is strongly reminiscent of something, often in a disapproving or negative sense

Examples

They might well indicate conjugal felicity, but in musical terms that smack of Hollywood.

Synset Examples

his speeches smacked of racism
this passage smells of plagiarism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resemble - to be similar to or have qualities of something
  • Synonym: evoke - to bring to mind or suggest a feeling or quality
