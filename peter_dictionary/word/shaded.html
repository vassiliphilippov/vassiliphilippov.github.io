shaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪd / (BrE)
Partly protected from direct sunlight or light, typically by being under trees, buildings, or another object casting a shadow.

Examples

The road leading south along the river was shaded with old trees, and in the moonlight the silvery landscape was like a setting for trolls and wood gods rather than the Hudson River Valley of his boyhood memories.
Love and hatred and fear were one here, shaded only by fig trees and grape vines.

Synset Examples

shaded avenues
o'er the shaded billows rushed the night

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunny - exposed to much sunlight
  • Synonym: shadowed - cast into shadow; darkened

Etymology

From the verb 'shade' + -ed, denoting the state of being in shade.
