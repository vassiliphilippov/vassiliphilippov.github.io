asymmetry

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / ˌæˈsɪmətri / (BrE)
The lack of symmetry; the state of being unequal or unbalanced in shape, arrangement, or proportion.

Examples

The lines are asymmetric and over the range of field f gauss and temperature f the **asymmetry** increases with increasing **f and decreasing T.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symmetry - exact correspondence on opposite sides of a dividing line or around a center

Etymology

From Greek 'asymmetria', from 'a-' (not) + 'symmetria' (symmetry).
