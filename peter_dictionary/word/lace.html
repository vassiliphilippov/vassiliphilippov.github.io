lace

1. VERB

/ leɪs / (AmE), / leɪs / (BrE)
to intertwine or thread together by winding, twisting, or crossing.

Examples

The elements that did were the introspective slow movement, the beautiful transition to the third movement, and the passages of filigree that laced through the bigger moments of the opening movement and the final Rondo.

Synset Examples

intertwine the ribbons
Twine the threads into a rope
intertwined hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intertwine - to twist or twine together
  • Synonym: twine - to twist together (threads, etc.)

Etymology

From Middle English lacs, from Old French laz (a noose, string), from Latin laqueus (noose, snare).
