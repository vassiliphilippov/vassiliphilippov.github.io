pulsate

1. VERB

/ pʌlˈseɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌlseɪt / (BrE)
to expand and contract or beat in a steady rhythm, as the heart or arteries do

Examples

The half-man lay on the ground, arteries pulsating prettily behind the half-transparent film which protected his abdominal cavity.

Synset Examples

The baby's heart was pulsating again after the surgeon massaged it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throb - to beat or sound with a strong, regular rhythm
  • Synonym: palpitate - to beat rapidly, strongly, or irregularly, especially the heart

Etymology

From Latin pulsatus, past participle of pulsare, meaning 'to beat, strike repeatedly'.
