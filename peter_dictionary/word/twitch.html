twitch

1. VERB

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to make a sudden, quick, involuntary movement or spasm, especially of a muscle or part of the body

Examples

The figure was close enough now for him to see the nose twitching to dislodge the drop clinging there.
The thin legs twitched convulsively once, then Kate felt the little body stiffening in her arms and heard one strangled sound.
Of four dark brown ears, one twitched slightly at this.
I saw the clergyman kneel for a moment by the twitching body of the man he had shot, then run back to his position.

Synset Examples

his face is twitching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to move with a sudden sharp motion
  • Synonym: spasm - an involuntary contraction of a muscle

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *twicchen* (to pluck, pull), related to Old English *twiccian*.

2. VERB

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to make a sudden, quick, and usually small movement, often without control

Examples

She twitched her leg away.
A second twitched his shirtsleeve, and he felt a brief burn on his upper arm.

Synset Examples

The patient's legs were jerkings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to move or cause to move with a sharp sudden motion

Etymology

Middle English twicchen, related to Old English twiccian (to pluck, pull sharply).
