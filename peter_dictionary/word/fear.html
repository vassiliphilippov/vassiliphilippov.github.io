fear

1. VERB

/ fɪr / (AmE), / fɪə / (BrE)
To be afraid of (someone or something) as likely to be dangerous, painful, or threatening; to feel anxiety or apprehension about a possible or probable event.

Examples

Many local citizens feared that there would be irregularities at the polls, and Williams got himself a permit to carry a gun and promised an orderly election.
We are not now afraid of atomic bombs in the same way that people once feared comets.
Small wonder, then, that we fear.
"I fear explanations explanatory of things explained ", he said, leaving the biter bit - and bitter.
But time is running out, and many of Stravinsky's admirers begin to fear that he will never find terra firma.

Synset Examples

I fear she might get aggressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dread - to anticipate with great apprehension or fear
  • Antonym: hope - to desire with expectation of obtainment or fulfillment (opposite emotional reaction to fear)
  • Degree Lower: worry - to feel or cause to feel anxious or troubled about actual or potential problems

Etymology

From Old English *fǣran* (to terrify), related to *fǣr* (sudden danger, fear).

2. NOUN

/ fɪr / (AmE), / fɪə / (BrE)
An unpleasant emotion caused by the belief that someone or something is dangerous, likely to cause pain, or a threat.

Examples

In doing so science has unquestionably cleared up widespread misconceptions, removed extraneous and illusory sources of fear, and dispelled many undesirable popular superstitions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: terror - A feeling of intense fear; extreme fright
  • Synonym: anxiety - A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease, often about something with an uncertain outcome
  • Antonym: courage - The ability to do something that frightens one; bravery

Etymology

Related Terms

Etymology

5. VERB

/ fɪr / (AmE), / fɪə / (BrE)
(formal) Used to introduce a statement expressing regret or apology, often when giving bad news or an unpleasant truth.

Examples

"More arrests, I fear ".
I don't have her stuffed down the front of my pants right now, I'm sorry to say.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regret - to feel sorrow or remorse for something
