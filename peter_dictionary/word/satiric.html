satiric

1. ADJ

/ səˈtɪrɪk / (AmE), / səˈtɪrɪk / (BrE)
Using satire to expose or criticize folly or vice; characterized by mocking or ridiculing human weaknesses.

Examples

At the door she turned back, her Roman nose looking very long now and satiric.

Synset Examples

a persistent campaign of mockery by the satirical fortnightly magazine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satirical - using or relating to satire

Etymology

From Latin satiricus, from satira (satire).
