torment

1. VERB

/ ˈtɔrˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtɔːmənt / (BrE)
to cause someone to experience severe emotional or mental suffering

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - to cause great physical or mental pain
  • Synonym: harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation repeatedly
  • Antonym: soothe - to gently calm or relieve pain

Etymology

From Latin tormentum (an instrument of torture).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtɔːmɛnt / (BrE)
severe physical or mental suffering; extreme distress

Examples

They marched with bayonets fixed, and as fixed on their faces was anger, fear, and torment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anguish - severe mental or physical pain or suffering
  • Synonym: agony - intense physical or mental pain
  • Synonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Antonym: relief - alleviation of pain or distress

Etymology

From Old French torment and Latin tormentum (instrument of torture, anguish), from torquere (to twist).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtɔːmɛnt / (BrE)
Severe physical or mental suffering; intense pain or anguish.

Examples

They caused my love for Jessica to become warmer and at the same time more hopeless, as if my adolescent self knew that only torment would ever bring me the courage to ask to see her again.

Synset Examples

an agony of doubt
the torments of the damned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony - extreme physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Antonym: relief - alleviation or removal of pain or distress

Etymology

From Old French torment, from Latin tormentum (an instrument of torture, a rack, torture, pain), from torquere (to twist).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtɔːmɛnt / (BrE)
Extreme and unbearable physical pain.

Examples

Hell's own amount of chaulmoogra oil did nothing to alleviate their torment; they expired amid indescribable fantods, imploring the Blessed One to forgive their desecration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony - intense pain, especially mental or physical suffering
  • Synonym: anguish - severe physical or mental pain
  • Antonym: relief - the removal or alleviation of pain or distress

Etymology

From Latin tormentum (instrument of torture, pain) from torquere (to twist).
