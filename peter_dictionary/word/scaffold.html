scaffold

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæfəld / (AmE), / ˈskæfəʊld / (BrE)
a raised platform used for the public execution of criminals, especially by hanging or beheading

Examples

The unfortunate queen mounted the scaffold; the headsman swung his axe; the head dropped off; end of film.
Extraordinary precautions were taken so that no stranger be allowed in the city and no citizen within the enclosure surrounding the scaffold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallows - structure for hanging criminals
  • Synonym: guillotine - machine for beheading, used in executions

Etymology

From Old French *eschafault*, from Vulgar Latin *scadafalcum* (scaffold, platform).
