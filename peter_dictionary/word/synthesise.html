synthesise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪnθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to combine different elements or substances to form a new, more complex whole

Examples

First, is the circulating form of the hormone the same as that found in the gland where it is synthesised and stored?

Synset Examples

his operas synthesize music and drama in perfect harmony
The liver synthesizes vitamins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analyze - to separate something into its constituent parts for study

Etymology

From Greek *synthesis* (putting together), via Latin *synthesizare*.
