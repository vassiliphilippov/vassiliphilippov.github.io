tending

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of taking care of someone or something; looking after or providing care.

Examples

These my grandmother left in their places (they are still there, more persistent and longer-lived than the generations of man) and planted others like them, that flourished without careful tending.

Synset Examples

no medical care was required
the old car needs constant attention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - the process of providing what's necessary for the health, welfare, maintenance, and protection of someone or something
  • Antonym: neglect - the state or fact of being uncared for

Etymology

From the verb 'tend' (to care for), derived from Latin *tendere* (to stretch, extend).
