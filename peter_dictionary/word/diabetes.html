diabetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈbitiz / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
a disease in which the body has abnormally high glucose (sugar) levels in the blood, often resulting in excessive urination and persistent thirst

Examples

Says Wallace F. Jannsen, director of the FDA's Division of Public Information: "Quacks are apt to direct their appeal directly to older people, or to sufferers from chronic ailments such as arthritis, rheumatism, diabetes, and cancer.

Etymology

From Greek 'diabetes' (siphon), referring to excessive discharge of urine.
