melting

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
The process by which a solid substance becomes liquid as a result of heat.

Examples

However, technical difficulties arise by melting at local hot spots.

Synset Examples

the power failure caused a refrigerator melt that was a disaster
the thawing of a frozen turkey takes several hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freezing - the process of a liquid becoming a solid due to cold
  • Synonym: thawing - the process of warming and therefore softening or liquidizing something frozen

Etymology

From the verb 'melt', ultimately from Old English 'meltan' (to liquefy, digest).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
Becoming liquid by the action of heat; in the process of changing from solid to liquid.

Examples

Then he slipped and went down on his hands and knees in the melting snow.
I'm melting!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freezing - the process of becoming solid from a liquid
  • Antonym: solidifying - becoming solid

Etymology

Present participle of 'melt', from Old English 'meltan' (to liquefy, dissolve).
