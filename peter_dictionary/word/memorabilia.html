memorabilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛmərəˈbɪliə / (AmE), / ˌmɛmərəˈbɪliə / (BrE)
Objects collected or kept because of their historical interest or association with memorable people or events.

Examples

However well chosen and cleverly arranged, such memorabilia unfortunately amounted to more of an interruption than an auxiliary to the evening's main business, which (considering the talent at hand) should probably have been the gathering of fresh samples of the Chicago style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event

Etymology

From Latin 'memorabilia', neuter plural of 'memorabilis' (memorable).
