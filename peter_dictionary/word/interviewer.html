interviewer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tɚ.vjuː.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vjuː.ə / (BrE)
a person who asks questions in an interview, especially as part of a job, research, or news assignment

Examples

"Senator ", said an interviewer, "your constituents can n't understand from your speech last night just how you stand on the question ".
The interviewers were instructed not to suggest answers and, as much as possible, to record the parents' actual words as they described the child's behavior in home situations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interviewee - the person who is interviewed

Etymology

From 'interview' + '-er' suffix, denoting 'one who interviews'. 'Interview' is from French 'entrevue', from 'entrevoir' meaning 'to see between'.
