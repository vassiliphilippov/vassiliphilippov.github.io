senor

1. NOUN

/ seɪˈnɔr / (AmE), / seˈnjɔː / (BrE)
a Spanish title or form of address for a man, equivalent to 'Mr.' or 'sir' in English

Examples

WOMAN: Gracias, senor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: señorita - a Spanish title for an unmarried woman
  • Antonym: madam - form of address for a woman, especially in formal contexts

Etymology

From Spanish 'señor' (lord, sir, mister), from Latin 'senior' (older, elder).
