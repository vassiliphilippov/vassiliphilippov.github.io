misinterpretation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsɪntɚprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪntɜːprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
An incorrect or mistaken understanding or explanation of something.

Examples

They never troubled themselves about us while we were playing, because the fence formed such a definite boundary and "Do n't go outside the gate "was a command so impossible of misinterpretation.

Synset Examples

his misinterpretation of the question caused his error
there was no mistaking her meaning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something
  • Synonym: misunderstanding - a failure to understand correctly; a mistaken idea

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'interpretation' (from Latin interpretatio: explanation, translation).
