slantwise

1. ADV

/ ˈslæntˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈslɑːntˌwaɪz / (BrE)
In a direction that is at an angle; diagonally or obliquely rather than straight.

Examples

The sun moved slant-wise across the sky and down; the trees' shadows circled from street to sidewalk, from sidewalk to lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonally - at an angle; along a diagonal line or direction
  • Antonym: straight - in a straight or direct line; not at an angle

Etymology

Formed from 'slant' + 'wise', meaning 'in the manner of a slant'.
