verve

1. NOUN

/ vɜːrv / (AmE), / vɜːv / (BrE)
Energy, enthusiasm, and lively spirit, especially in artistic or creative expression.

Examples

And little Zeme North, a Dora with real spirit and verve, was fascinating whether she was singing of her love for Floyd, the cop who becomes sewer commissioner and then is promoted into garbage, or just dancing to display her exuberant feelings.
What remained lacked the original verve but it was at least dignified, as befitting the tragic circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacity - liveliness and animated spirit
  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and full of life or energy
  • Antonym: spiritlessness - lack of spirit or enthusiasm

Etymology

From French 'verve' (liveliness; enthusiasm), ultimately from Latin 'verbum' (word).
