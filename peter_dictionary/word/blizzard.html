blizzard

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪzɚd / (AmE), / ˈblɪzəd / (BrE)
A severe snowstorm with strong winds and heavy snowfall, reducing visibility and causing difficult conditions.

Examples

"I soon came back to my senses ", he said, aloud, to the young blizzard, proudly, drawing himself up, as if making a report to some important superior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowstorm - a storm with a large amount of falling snow

Etymology

Probably of American origin, perhaps from the earlier sense 'violent blow' or from a regional word for a blow or blast.
