romance

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəˈmæns / (BrE)
A love affair; a relationship between two people who are in love, often characterized by passion or emotional attachment.

Examples

He imagined they were the kind whose tax returns were never examined (if they were, they were never penalized), whose children had no unhappy romances, whose names never knew scandal.
Again among those jubilantly reunited bunkmates, I was shy with Jessie and acted as I had during those early Saturday mornings when we all seemed to be playing for effect, to be detached and unconcerned with the girls who were properly our dates but about whom, later, in the privacy of our bunks, we would think in terms of the most elaborate romance.
A weekly newspaper reported a local romance: "and the couple were married last Saturday, thus ending a friendship which began in their schooldays ".
The moonlit night was made for romance, and he had been looking at her soulfully for some time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affair - a love affair, often secret or illicit
  • Synonym: relationship - a romantic involvement between two people
  • Antonym: friendship - a platonic relationship, as opposed to a romantic one

Etymology

From Old French 'romanz', originally referring to verse narrative, from Medieval Latin 'romancia', related to Rome and the vernacular (Roman) languages.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæns / (BrE)
An exciting and mysterious quality, especially one reminiscent of heroic times or adventures.

Examples

The record teems with romance and adventure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventure - an unusual and exciting or daring experience
  • Antonym: monotony - lack of variety and interest; tedious repetition and routine

Etymology

From Old French 'romanz' (narrative), from Latin 'romanice' (in the Roman language).
