tomato

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A glossy red or occasionally yellow, pulpy edible fruit that is typically eaten as a vegetable in salads, sauces, and other dishes.

Examples

A salad with greens and tomato is a popular and wonderfully healthful addition to a meal, but add an avocado and you have something really special.
He found a jar of preserved tomatoes and one of eggs that they had meant to save.
Throw in some tomatoes, tomato paste, fry it and make sure it doesn't stick.

Etymology

From Spanish *tomate*, from Nahuatl *tomatl*.
