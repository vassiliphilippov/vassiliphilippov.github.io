freeman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːmən / (AmE), / ˈfriːmən / (BrE)
A person who is not a slave or a serf, especially in historical contexts.

Examples

The colonial period has generally left people believing that government can, if it wishes, provide all manner of services for them - and that with independence free men do not have to work to realize the benefits of modern life.
All I ever wanted was to be a free man in my own country.
I think it's the excitement only a free man can feel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system
  • Antonym: slave - a person who is legally owned by someone else and forced to work for them

Etymology

From Old English 'frēoman', literally 'free man' (contrasted with 'theow', or slave).
