woeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwoʊ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈwəʊ.fəl / (BrE)
Full of or expressing sorrow, grief, or misery.

Examples

My parents' welcoming arms would seem woeful, inadequate, unwanted.

Synset Examples

his sorrow...made him look...haggard and...woebegone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserable - feeling or showing great unhappiness or suffering
  • Antonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment

Etymology

From Middle English 'woful', from 'wo' (woe) + '-ful'.
