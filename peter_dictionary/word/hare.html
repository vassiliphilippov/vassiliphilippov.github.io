hare

1. NOUN

/ hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
A fast, timid mammal with long ears, longer hind legs, and a divided upper lip, larger than a rabbit, whose young are born fully furred and with open eyes.

Examples

The Zeitgeist is not crazy as a loon or mad as a March hare; it is manic as a man ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small, burrowing mammal with long ears and short tail, related to the hare but smaller and with different breeding habits

Etymology

Old English *hara*, related to German *Hase* and Dutch *haas*.
