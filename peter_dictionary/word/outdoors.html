outdoors

1. ADV

/ aʊtˈdɔɹz / (AmE), / aʊtˈdɔːz / (BrE)
In or to the open air; outside a building.

Examples

He dressed, and sped outdoors.
How soon you may go outdoors.

Synset Examples

in summer we play outside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indoors - inside a building; not outdoors

Etymology

From the words 'out' + 'doors', originally meaning 'out of doors'.

2. NOUN

/ aʊtˈdɔrz / (AmE), / aʊtˈdɔːz / (BrE)
The open air; the natural environment outside buildings or shelter.

Examples

I think a man working outdoors feels more like a man if he can have a bottle of suds.

Synset Examples

he wanted to get outdoors a little
the concert was held in the open air
camping in the open

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indoors - inside a building; within a house or shelter

Etymology

From the preposition 'out' and 'doors', originally indicating outside the door or house.
