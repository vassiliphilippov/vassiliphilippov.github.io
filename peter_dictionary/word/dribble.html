dribble

1. VERB

/ ˈdrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow slowly in small drops or a thin stream.

Examples

As reports dribbled in, William Weigand tossed them into the centrifuge which had become his head.

Synset Examples

water trickled onto the lawn from the broken hose
reports began to dribble in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickle - to flow slowly in a thin stream or drops
  • Antonym: spurt - to flow out suddenly and forcefully

Etymology

Probably from dialectal drib or driblet (a small drop), of unknown origin.
