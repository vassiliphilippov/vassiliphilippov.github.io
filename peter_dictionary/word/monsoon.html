monsoon

1. NOUN

/ mɑnˈsun / (AmE), / mɒnˈsuːn / (BrE)
A seasonal wind in southern Asia that brings heavy rains in summer from the southwest and dry weather in winter from the northeast.

Examples

He spoke of the season of dryness and dust, brought by the monsoon from the northeast, in harmony with the season of rain and mud, brought by the monsoon from the southwest.
He spoke of the season of dryness and dust, brought by the monsoon from the northeast, in harmony with the season of rain and mud, brought by the monsoon from the southwest.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'monção', from Arabic 'mawsim' (season).
