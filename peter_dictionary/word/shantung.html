shantung

1. NOUN

/ ʃænˈtʌŋ / (AmE), / ʃænˈtʌŋ / (BrE)
A heavy silk fabric with a rough, uneven surface, traditionally made from wild silk, or an imitation fabric made from cotton.

Examples

In the tintable group are high and little heels, squared and oval throats, and shantung like textures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pongee - a thin, soft, plain-woven silk fabric

Etymology

From Shantung (now Shandong), a province in China where the fabric originated.
