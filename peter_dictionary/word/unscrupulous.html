unscrupulous

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈskruːpjələs / (AmE), / ʌnˈskruːpjʊləs / (BrE)
Having or showing no moral principles; not honest or fair.

Examples

Measurements of skins are of little value; every snake hide is noticeably longer than its carcass and intentional stretching presents no difficulty to the unscrupulous explorer.

Synset Examples

unscrupulous politicos who would be happy to sell...their country in order to gain power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scrupulous - having moral integrity or principles

Etymology

From un- (not) + scrupulous (having moral integrity); scrupulous from Latin 'scrupulus' (scruple, a small sharp stone, anxiety).
