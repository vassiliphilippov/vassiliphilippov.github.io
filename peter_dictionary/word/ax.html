ax

1. NOUN

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
A tool with a heavy, sharp-edged blade fixed at a right angle to a handle, used for chopping wood or as a weapon.

Examples

Andy Ross had just started swinging an ax at his second willow when the distant blast of a rifle sounded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatchet - a small ax with a short handle for use with one hand
  • Synonym: cleaver - a large, broad-bladed knife or tool used for chopping

Etymology

From Old English 'æx', related to Old High German 'achs' and Latin 'ascia' (axe).
