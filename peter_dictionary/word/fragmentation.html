fragmentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræɡ.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of breaking or separating something into small parts or fine particles.

Examples

Thus the image of man has suffered complete fragmentation in personal and spiritual qualities, and complete objectification in sub-human and quasi mechanistic powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomization - the process of breaking into tiny particles or droplets

Etymology

From Latin 'fragmentum' (a piece broken off) + English '-ation' (forming nouns of action/process).
