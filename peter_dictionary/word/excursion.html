excursion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskɝːʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɜːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A short journey or trip, especially one taken as a leisure activity.

Examples

On these excursions, Papa instructed him on man's chief end, which was his duty to God and his own salvation.

Synset Examples

many summer excursions to the shore
it was merely a pleasure trip
after cautious sashays into the field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outing - a short journey taken for pleasure or education
  • Synonym: trip - a journey or excursion, especially for pleasure

Etymology

From Latin 'excursio' (an invasion, raid), from 'excurrere' (to run out).
