anastomotic

1. ADJ

/ əˌnæstəˈmɑtɪk / (AmE), / əˌnæstəˈmɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting anastomosis; involving or characterized by the connection or joining of two tubular structures, such as blood vessels or other channels in the body.

Examples

As in type 1,, it provides arterial blood to the interlobular septa, and an extremely rich anastomotic pleural supply is seen (figs. 9, 10).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anastomosis - a surgical or natural connection between two structures, such as blood vessels or loops of intestine

Etymology

From anastomosis + -ic, from Greek 'anastomosis' (a providing with a mouth, outlet) + English adjectival suffix '-ic'.
