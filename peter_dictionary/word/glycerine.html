glycerine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪsəriːn / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsəriːn / (BrE)
a thick, sweet, colorless, and odorless liquid used in food, medicine, and cosmetics, chemically known as glycerol

Examples

After blotting out most of the liquid around the sections, the latter were mounted in buffered glycerine (7 parts glycerine to 3 parts of PBS).

Unstained sections mounted in buffered glycerine or sections treated only with NS or * * f did not show such green fluorescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycerol - the chemical name for glycerine; a trihydroxy alcohol

Etymology

From French glycérine, from Greek glykys (sweet), referring to its sweet taste.
