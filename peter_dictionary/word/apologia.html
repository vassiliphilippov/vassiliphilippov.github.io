apologia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈloʊdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌapəˈləʊdʒɪə / (BrE)
a formal written defense of one's opinions, actions, or beliefs

Examples

In 1864 Newman professedly had to write his Apologia with his keenest feelings in order to be believed and to command a fair hearing from English readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apology - expression of regret or a defense of an idea

Etymology

From Late Latin *apologia* (a speech in defense), from Greek *apologia* (a speaking in defense).
