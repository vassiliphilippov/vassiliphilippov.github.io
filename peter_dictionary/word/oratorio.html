oratorio

1. NOUN

/ ɔɹ.əˈtɔɹ.i.oʊ / (AmE), / ɒɹ.əˈtɔː.ri.əʊ / (BrE)
a large-scale musical composition for voices and orchestra, usually based on a religious theme, performed without costumes, scenery, or action

Examples

And she wrote the libretto for an oratorio on the subject of Judas Maccabeus performed at the Hanukkah festival which came in December.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantata - a musical composition for voices and instruments, typically with solos, chorus, and orchestra, but generally shorter and less dramatic than an oratorio

Etymology

From Italian 'oratorio', meaning 'oratory', originally a place of prayer, influenced by performances in the Oratory of St Philip Neri in Rome.
