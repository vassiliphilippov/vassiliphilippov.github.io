litter

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
a group of offspring born at one time to a multiparous animal, especially mammals such as cats, dogs, or pigs

Examples

In litters of eight mice from similar parents, the number of mice with straight instead of wavy hair is an integer from 0 to 8.
For the marksman, we study sets of five shots (* * f); for the mice, we restrict attention to litters of eight (* * f); and for the aces, we toss three dice (* * f).
A third remarked of slowness of Southerners: "They moved about from corner to corner, as uneasy as a litter of hungry leaches on the neck of a wooden god ".
He seemed to remember reading somewhere that Abyssinians had large litters, and suffered a dismaying vision of the apartment overrun with a dozen kittens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - a family of young animals, especially birds, produced at one hatching or birth

Etymology

From Middle English *lytyr* (bed), from Anglo-Norman *litiere*, from Old French *litiere* (bed, bedding), ultimately from Latin *lectus* (bed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
waste such as paper, cans, and bottles left carelessly in public places

Examples

However, the litter remained, augmented by several dozen lunchroom suppers.
There was no confusion and no litter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trash - waste material, especially in American English
  • Synonym: garbage - discarded waste, especially in American English
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean and free from dirt or waste

Etymology

From Middle English *littere* (bed), from Old French *litiere*, later extended to mean 'things strewn about'.

3. VERB

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
to make a place untidy or messy by scattering rubbish or things around

Examples

The Champs Elysees is literally littered this summer with the prostrate bodies of France's beat-up beatnik jeunes filles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clean - to remove dirt or make neat and tidy

Etymology

From Middle English 'litteren', from Old French 'litere', related to a bed of straw or refuse.

4. VERB

/ ˈlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
to scatter or leave rubbish or objects untidily over a surface or place

Examples

Cigarette butts littered the floor.

Synset Examples

Cigar butts littered the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to throw or spread things over a wide area
  • Antonym: collect - to gather things together

Etymology

From Middle English 'litteren', from Old French 'litiere' (bed, litter), from Medieval Latin 'lectaria'.
