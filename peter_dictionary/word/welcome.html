welcome

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛlˌkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
to accept or receive someone or something gladly or willingly

Examples

Far from it; I merely draw an etymological distinction, hoping that specialists and busy people like you will welcome such precision in a layman.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - to accept or support willingly or enthusiastically
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or consider

Etymology

From Old English *wilcuma* (one whose coming is pleasing), from *wil* (pleasure, desire) + *cuma* (guest).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɛlkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
(of something or someone) pleasing and gladly received, or allowed or permitted with pleasure.

Examples

Chicago was also a welcome host: there, in 1921, Prokofieff conducted the world premiere of the Love for Three Oranges, and played the first performance of his Third Piano Concerto.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwelcome - not gladly received or accepted

Etymology

From Old English *wilcuma* (one who is welcome, a guest), from *will* (pleasure, desire) + *cuma* (guest, comer).

3. VERB

/ ˈwɛlkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
to receive someone with pleasure or hospitality into one's home or company

Examples

New England academies welcome fugitives from the provinces, South as well as West.

Related Terms
