contented

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛntɪd / (AmE), / kənˈtɛntɪd / (BrE)
Feeling or expressing happiness and satisfaction with what one has or with the way things are.

Examples

If it's true that contented cows give more milk, why should n't happy ball players produce more base hits?
Also, perhaps, table-tennis and other indoor sports to keep them fit and contented.

Synset Examples

a contented smile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discontented - feeling dissatisfied or unhappy with circumstances
  • Synonym: satisfied - pleased because a need or desire has been fulfilled

Etymology

From past participle of 'content', from Latin 'contentus' (contained, satisfied).
