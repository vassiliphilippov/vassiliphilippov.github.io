Florida

1. PROPN

/ fləˈrɪdə / (AmE), / fləˈrɪdə / (BrE)
A state in the southeastern United States, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico, known for its warm climate and popular tourist destinations.

Etymology

From Spanish 'florida', meaning 'flowery', named by Spanish explorer Juan Ponce de León in 1513.
