pedigree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪɡriː / (BrE)
the recorded ancestry or lineage of a person or animal

Examples

It would not be easy to discover a more thoroughly Southern pedigree than that of his family.
I 'm well aware that you 've got a pedigree as long as my leg, and that I do n't amount to anything.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry or pedigree
  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Degree Lower: descendant - a person, plant, or animal that is descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'pedegre', from Old French 'pie de grue' (foot of a crane), referring to the appearance of genealogical charts.
