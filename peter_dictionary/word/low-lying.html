low-lying

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ ˌlaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌlaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
Situated at a small elevation above the ground or sea level; not far above the surrounding area.

Examples

We tumbled to a stop in Deacon Gordon's cow hole, a low-lying bit of pasture with a muddy pool of water in its middle.

Synset Examples

low-lying clouds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-lying - situated at a high elevation above the ground or sea level
