irresistible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪrɪˈzɪstəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈzɪstəbl̩ / (BrE)
So attractive, appealing, or powerful that it cannot be resisted or refused.

Examples

The great ultimate ends of religion have served as magnificent beacon lights that lured people toward them with an almost irresistible force, mobilizing energies and inducing sacrifices; for example, the Crusades, mission efforts, just wars.

Synset Examples

irresistible (or resistless) impulses
what happens when an irresistible force meets an immovable object?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistible - able to be resisted

Etymology

From Latin 'irresistibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'resistere' (to resist) + '-ibilis' (-ible).
