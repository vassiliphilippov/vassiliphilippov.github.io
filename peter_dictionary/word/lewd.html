lewd

1. ADJ

/ lud / (AmE), / luːd / (BrE)
(of behavior, speech, etc.) crude and offensive in a sexual way; indecent or obscene.

Examples

Before them stalked the beadle, proclaiming as he went, "Thus the Council deals with those who break its laws - adulterers, thieves, murderers, and lewd persons.

Synset Examples

lewd whisperings of a dirty old man
an indecent gesture
obscene telephone calls
salacious limericks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecent - not conforming with accepted standards, especially regarding sex
  • Synonym: obscene - offensively or grossly indecent
  • Antonym: chaste - refraining from sexual activity; morally pure

Etymology

From Old English *lǣwede* (lay, unlearned, base), later shifting to 'vile' and 'lustful'.
