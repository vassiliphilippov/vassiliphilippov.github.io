conversational

1. ADJ

/ kənˌvɜrˈseɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˌvɜːˈseɪʃənəl / (BrE)
(of language or style) characteristic of informal spoken language or conversation; suitable for casual communication rather than formal writing.

Examples

It began invariably in low tones, almost conversational, and then gradually worked up to high, shrill appeals to God and man.

Synset Examples

wrote her letters in a colloquial style
the broken syntax and casual enunciation of conversational English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloquial - (of language) used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary

Etymology

From 'conversation' + '-al', first recorded in the 18th century.
