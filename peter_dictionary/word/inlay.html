inlay

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪnleɪ / (BrE)
to decorate a surface by setting pieces of another material into it, typically wood, stone, or metal, to create a pattern or design

Examples

Rounded posts give a soft, sculptured look, paneled doors have decorative burl panels or cane insets plus softening arches, table tops are inlaid in Macassar ebony or acacia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embed - to fix an object firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass

Etymology

From Middle English inlaien, from in- + lay, meaning 'to lay in'.
