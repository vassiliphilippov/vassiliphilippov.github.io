turf

1. NOUN

/ tɝf / (AmE), / tɜːf / (BrE)
The surface layer of soil that is covered with grass and held together by its roots.

Examples

The females like to burrow in the short turf of well-kept lawns, where their little mounds of earth often appear by the hundreds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sod - a section of grass-covered surface soil held together by roots

Etymology

From Old English 'turf', related to Old Norse 'torf', meaning sod or peat.
