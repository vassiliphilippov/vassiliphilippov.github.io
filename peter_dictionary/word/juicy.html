juicy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒusi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
(of a job, contract, or opportunity) offering large profits or rewards; lucrative.

Examples

Then why not the juicy booking Hurok had held for us?

Synset Examples

a juicy contract
a nice fat job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit
  • Synonym: profitable - yielding financial gain or advantage

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːsi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
Full of juice; containing a lot of liquid, especially said of fruit or meat.

Examples

Fresh, warm, sweet and juicy, sweet lovin sixteen, she was.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry - lacking moisture or juice

Etymology

From juice + -y.

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʒusi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
(informal) Sexually attractive or appealing; having strong sexual allure.

Examples

She was a juicy one.

Synset Examples

juicy barmaids
a red-hot mama
a voluptuous woman
a toothsome blonde in a tight dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voluptuous - sexually attractive, especially because of a curvy figure
  • Synonym: seductive - tempting and attractive in a sexual way
  • Antonym: plain - lacking strong sexual attractiveness; not striking in appearance
