slippage

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (BrE)
a reduction in power or motion in a mechanical system caused by a component slipping, especially when a belt or similar part fails to grip properly

Examples

The slippage of the drive belts caused the aerator to slow down and reduce oxygen transfer as well as the mixing of the raw sewage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - to lose one's grip or traction

Etymology

From 'slip' + '-age', denoting a process or result of slipping.
