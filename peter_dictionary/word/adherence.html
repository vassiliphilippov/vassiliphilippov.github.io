adherence

1. NOUN

/ ədˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / ədˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
faithful support or commitment to a cause, belief, or set of rules

Examples

But except for a vague adherence to the basic storyline - i.e., that Ruth remained with Naomi and finally wound up with Boaz - the film version has little to do with the Bible.
It is his relentlessness and unwavering adherence to this revolutionary artistic philosophy that has enabled him to paint such pictures as "The Invasion of Cuba ".

Synset Examples

attachment to a formal agenda
adherence to a fat-free diet
the adhesion of Seville was decisive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attachment - emotional or supportive connection to something
  • Synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause

Etymology

From French *adhérence*, from Latin *adhaerentia*, from *adhaerēre* (to stick to).
