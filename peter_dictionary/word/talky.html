talky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔki / (AmE), / ˈtɔːki / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) inclined to talk a lot, especially about trivial matters; containing much talking or conversation.

Examples

It is awfully talky, for instance, and not all of the talk is terribly impressive.

Synset Examples

kept from her housework by gabby neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatty - talking a lot in a friendly way; fond of chatting
  • Synonym: garrulous - excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters

Etymology

Formed from 'talk' + '-y', meaning characterized by talking.
