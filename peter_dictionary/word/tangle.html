tangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a confused mass of things twisted together in an untidy way

Examples

At the slope's base coils of concertina stretched out of eye range like a wild tangle of children's hoops, stopped simultaneously, weirdly poised as if awaiting the magic of the child's touch to start them all rolling again.

Synset Examples

they carved their way through the tangle of vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - a tightly interlaced loop or lump in rope, string, etc.
  • Synonym: mess - a disordered or untidy state or mass

Etymology

From Middle English *tanglen*, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish *tangla* ‘to entangle’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡəl / (BrE)
a complicated or confused situation; a mess or state of disorder

Examples

Yet had he not visited the girl at Saw Buck he would never have been involved in this latest tangle.

Synset Examples

a tangle of government regulations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a dirty, untidy, or confused state
  • Synonym: snarl - a confused or complicated situation, especially one that is hard to unravel

Etymology

Probably from Middle English tangen (to entangle), of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse tǫng (pliers, tongs).
