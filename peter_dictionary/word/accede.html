accede

1. VERB

/ əkˈsiːd / (AmE), / əkˈsiːd / (BrE)
to agree to a request or demand, especially after initially refusing or hesitating; to yield to another's wish or opinion

Examples

Where this approach becomes critical, the industry can be expected to put much emphasis on this as evidence of its sincerity in "resisting "the wage pressures of a powerful union, requesting tariff relief after it has "reluctantly "acceded to the union pressure.

Synset Examples

The government bowed to the military pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
  • Synonym: submit - to accept or yield to a superior force or to the will of another
  • Antonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something; to oppose

Etymology

From Latin accedere (to approach, to assent), from ad- (to) + cedere (to go, yield).
