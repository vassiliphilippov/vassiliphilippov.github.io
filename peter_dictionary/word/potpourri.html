potpourri

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊpʊˈri / (AmE), / ˌpɒpʊˈriː / (BrE)
A collection of different or miscellaneous things; a mixture or assortment.

Examples

As a result, most people do n't have more than a vague idea what folklore actually is; they see it as a potpourri of charming, moral legends and patriotic anecdotes, with a superstition or remedy thrown in here and there.

Synset Examples

a great assortment of cars was on display
he had a variety of disorders
a veritable smorgasbord of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assortment - a collection of different kinds, a variety
  • Synonym: medley - a varied mixture of things
  • Synonym: variety - a number of different types of things; a mixture

Etymology

From French pot-pourri, literally ‘rotten pot’, referring to a stew, later used for a mixture of dried flowers and then metaphorically for a miscellany.
