descendants

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndənts / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənts / (BrE)
A person or animal that is descended from a particular ancestor; all offspring of a specific progenitor.

Examples

Lincoln was historian and economist enough to know that a substantial portion of this wealth had accumulated in the hands of the descendants of New Englanders engaged in the slave trade.

Synset Examples

we must secure the benefits of freedom for ourselves and our posterity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Synonym: progeny - a descendant or the descendants of a person, animal, or plant

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere' (to climb down).
