headline

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.laɪn / (BrE)
a title or caption at the top of a newspaper or magazine article, designed to attract attention or summarize the content

Examples

Once, Andrus walked by it, hastily scanned the bold black headline and the five column lead of the article (by Duane Bosch, staff correspondent - age not given), and muttered: "We a buncha national celebrities ".
Since brevity is the soul of ambiguity as well as wit, newspaper headlines continually provide us with amusing samples.
Several years ago headlines were made by a small radio transmitter capsule which could be swallowed by the patient and which would then radio internal pressure data to external receivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the name of a book, article, or other work
  • Synonym: caption - a brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + 'line', meaning the line at the head of an article.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
To publicize widely or prominently, especially to announce as a main attraction or feature.

Examples

The Corn Palace at Mitchell, S. Dak., "the world's corniest building ", has a carnival through Sept. 23 headlining the Three Stooges and Pee Wee Hunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feature - to give special prominence to (someone or something)

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'headline', dating from the 19th century in journalism.
