roller

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A ball that is hit softly and rolls along the ground, especially through the infield.

Examples

Chuck Hinton tripled to the rightfield corner, Cliff Cook and Dan Pavletich singled and Gaines' infielder roller accounted for the tallies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grounder - a batted baseball that bounces or rolls along the ground
