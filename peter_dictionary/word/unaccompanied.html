unaccompanied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnəˈkʌmpənid / (AmE), / ʌnəˈkʌmpənid / (BrE)
not having anyone or anything with one; without an escort or companion.

Examples

A further regulation is that commands always go down, unaccompanied by statements, and statements always go up, unaccompanied by commands.
A further regulation is that commands always go down, unaccompanied by statements, and statements always go up, unaccompanied by commands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accompanied - having someone or something with one; having an escort or companion

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'accompanied' (from Old French accompagner 'to associate with').
