camp

1. NOUN

/ kæmp / (AmE), / kæmp / (BrE)
An area where people stay temporarily in tents, trailers, or other temporary shelters, especially when traveling or on vacation in the countryside.

Examples

Nearly level areas are required for parking areas, beaches, camp areas, ball fields, etcetera.
There were umbrella tents, wall tents, cottage tents, station wagon tents, pup tents, Pop tents, Baker tents, tents with exterior frames, camper trailers, travel trailers, and even a few surplus parachutes serving as sunshades over entire family camps.
Moving around camp we saw all kinds of camp stoves, lanterns, coolers, bedding, games, fishing tackle, windbreaks and sunshades.

Camp meals are no great problem.

Synset Examples

level ground is best for parking and camp areas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campsite - a place used for camping, often equipped with facilities

Etymology

From Latin 'campus' (field, open space) via French 'camp' (field, military camp).

2. NOUN

/ kæmp / (AmE), / kamp / (BrE)
a place where people are confined as a form of punishment or persecution, especially for forced labor; a penal institution such as a concentration camp or labor camp.

Examples

Whatever the unhappy inmates of such camps are like today, Dostoevsky's companions were about as grim a lot as can be imagined.
As the first collective confrontation of the Nazi outrage, the Trial of Eichmann represents a recovery of the Jews from the shock of the death camps, a recovery that took fifteen years and which is still by no means complete (though let no one believe that it could be hastened by silence).

Synset Examples

China has many camps for political prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - a type of camp where large numbers of people are imprisoned, especially for political reasons or as part of a policy of oppression
  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are legally held as punishment for crimes
  • Synonym: labor camp - a camp where prisoners are forced to work

Related Terms

Etymology
