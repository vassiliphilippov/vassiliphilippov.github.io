apologetically

1. ADV

/ əˌpɑːlədʒəˈtɪkli / (AmE), / əˌpɒlədʒəˈtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or expresses an apology or regret.

Examples

Scotty would reply softly and his father, apologetically, would ask him to repeat.
He clutched the staff, and his dark eyes blinked apologetically.
Macneff raised his hands apologetically and said, "You are horrified, of course.

Synset Examples

he spoke apologetically about his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regretfully - in a way that expresses regret
  • Antonym: defiantly - in a way that resists or defies

Etymology

From 'apologetic' + '-ally', from Late Latin apologeticus (defending, vindicating).
