web

1. NOUN

/ wɛb / (AmE), / wɛb / (BrE)
A complex and often deceptive situation or set of circumstances that is difficult to escape from.

Examples

Sixty thousand Jewish prisoners of war disappeared into Lublin's web.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device or setup designed to catch or deceive
  • Synonym: snare - a situation that catches or traps someone, often unexpectedly

Etymology

From Old English *webb* (woven fabric, tapestry), from Proto-Germanic *wabjam*.

2. NOUN

/ wɛb / (AmE), / wɛb / (BrE)
The flattened, weblike part of a feather, made up of a series of barbs extending from either side of the shaft.

Examples

Somehow, all the other passengers (also supposed to have died in this web of elaborate frauds to cover up the disappearance of the personnel of the Gabriel) had simultaneously "agreed "to die.

3. NOUN

/ wɛb / (AmE), / wɛb / (BrE)
A complex, interconnected network or pattern that resembles something formed by weaving.

Examples

There was a blur just under my focus of vision, a crash; the car's far windshield panel turned into a silver web with a dark hole in the center.

Synset Examples

the trees cast a delicate web of shadows over the lawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network - an interconnected system or group
  • Synonym: mesh - material made of a network of wire or thread

Etymology

From Old English 'webb' (woven fabric, web), from Proto-Germanic *wabjam.
