cross-sectional

1. ADJ

/ krɔs ˈsɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to or involving a cross section; describing the shape, size, or properties of something as seen in a slice or section cut across its main axis.

Examples

Consider a shear field with a height of H and a cross-sectional area of A opposed by a manometer with a height of h (referred to the same base as H) and a cross-sectional area of a.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - relating to the side or sides (sometimes contrasted with cross-sectional)

Etymology

Formed from 'cross section' + '-al'.
