jealousy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛl.ə.si / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛl.ə.si / (BrE)
A feeling of envy and resentment towards someone, often because of a rival or out of fear of losing someone's affection or position to another.

Examples

Check with one of them, go ahead - there's no professional jealousy here ".
Bitter, unreasoning jealousy?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envy - a feeling of discontent or resentful longing aroused by someone else's possessions, qualities, or luck

Etymology

From Old French *jalosie*, from *jalous* (jealous), from Late Latin *zelosus* (full of zeal).
