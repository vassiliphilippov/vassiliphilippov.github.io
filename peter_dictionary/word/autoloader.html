autoloader

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔtoʊˌloʊdər / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊˌləʊdə / (BrE)
A firearm that automatically reloads itself after each shot, allowing for rapid consecutive firing.

Examples

The American firearms and ammunition manufacturers through diligent research and technical development have replaced the muzzle loader and slow firing single shot arms with modern fast firing autoloaders, extremely accurate bolt, lever, and slide action firearms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-loading rifle - a rifle that reloads itself after firing
  • Antonym: manual firearm - firearm that must be reloaded by hand after each shot

Etymology

Formed from 'auto-' (self) + 'loader' (one that loads).
