indentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌdɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a shallow concave cut, recess, or notch in a surface or edge

Examples

When Papa's slender fingers removed the spectacles, there were red indentations on the bridge of the strong nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - a small cut or indentation on an edge or surface
  • Antonym: protrusion - something that sticks out from a surface, opposite of indentation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin indentatio, from Latin indentare (to notch) + -ion.
