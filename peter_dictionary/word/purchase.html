purchase

1. VERB

/ ˈpɝːtʃəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
to acquire something by paying money for it; to buy

Examples

Synset Examples

The family purchased a new car
The conglomerate acquired a new company
She buys for the big department store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buy - to obtain in exchange for payment
  • Antonym: sell - to give something in exchange for money (opposite process)

Etymology

From Old French *pourchacier* (to seek, pursue), from Latin *purchasare*.
