guts

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌts / (AmE), / ɡʌts / (BrE)
Courage, determination, or strength of character; the ability to face danger or difficulty without fear.

Examples

Warren thought of all the men out that night who, like himself, had left their protective ridge and - fear working at their guts - picked their way into the area beyond.
She's got plenty of guts, Mr. Paxton.
You'’ve got a lot of guts.

Synset Examples

he didn't have the guts to try it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Synonym: bravery - brave behaviour or actions
  • Synonym: backbone - strength of character, determination
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage

Etymology

Colloquial use of 'guts' in this sense dates from the late 16th century, referring metaphorically to internal organs as a symbol of courage.
