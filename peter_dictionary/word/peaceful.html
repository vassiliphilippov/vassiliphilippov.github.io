peaceful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpis.fəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːs.fəl / (BrE)
Free from disturbance by strife, conflict, or war; tranquil and calm.

Examples

Anyone's identification with an international struggle, whether warlike or peaceful, requires absurd oversimplification and intense emotional involvement.
Charming and peaceful - but what were charm and peace compared to high adventure?
In the village of Soukhouma, which means "Peaceful ", we had a baci.
Homes and factories and schools and a big wide federal highway, instead of peaceful corn to rest your eyes on while you tried to rest your heart, while you tried not to look at the balloon and the bandstand and the uniforms and the flash of the instruments.
And I'm willing to do whatever is necessary to find a peaceful solution.

Synset Examples

a peaceful nation
peaceful times
a far from peaceful Christmas
peaceful sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquil - calm; free from disturbance
  • Synonym: calm - not agitated; peaceful and free from disturbance
  • Antonym: restless - unable to rest or relax; uneasy or agitated
  • Antonym: violent - using or involving physical force intended to hurt or damage

Etymology

Formed from 'peace' + '-ful', with 'peace' from Old French 'pais' (from Latin 'pax, pacis', meaning 'peace').
