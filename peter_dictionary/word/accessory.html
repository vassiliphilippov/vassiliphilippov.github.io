accessory

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛs.ɚ.i / (AmE), / əkˈsɛs.ər.i / (BrE)
A small item of clothing or object worn or carried to complement or enhance an outfit, but not essential to it.

Examples

The girls, very fetching in their uniforms, are shown firing rockets from a launcher mounted on a dump truck; they are also trained with carbines, automatic weapons, pistols, rifles and other such ladies' accessories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adornment - an accessory or ornament worn to enhance appearance
  • Antonym: garment - an item of clothing (main item, not accessory)

Etymology

From Latin accessorius (additional), from accedere (to approach, to be added).

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
an additional item that is not essential but adds to the usefulness, convenience, or effectiveness of something

Examples

Examples are in public utilities, making military aircraft and accessories, or where the investment and risk for a proprietorship would be too great for a much needed project impossible to achieve by any means other than the corporate form, e. g. constructing major airports or dams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole
  • Synonym: attachment - an additional part that can be attached to something to perform a function

Etymology

From Latin accessorius, from accedere (to approach) + -ory.
