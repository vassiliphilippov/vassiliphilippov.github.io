disdain

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈdeɪn / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to regard or treat with contempt; to consider someone or something unworthy of respect or attention

Examples

He disdains external restrictions - conventional syntax, traditional metre.
Unlike the heroes and flappers of the lost generation, they disdain the art of "necking "and "petting ".

Synset Examples

He despises the people he has to work for
The professor scorns the students who don't catch on immediately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorn - to treat with open dislike or disrespect
  • Synonym: despise - to feel contempt or a strong dislike for
  • Antonym: admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Old French 'desdeignier', from Latin 'dedignari' (to scorn).
