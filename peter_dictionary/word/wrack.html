wrack

1. NOUN

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
Dried seaweed, especially that which has been washed ashore by the tide.

Examples

One soft evening - that marvelous sea blessed time when the sun's departing warmth lingers and a smell of spume and wrack haunts everything - Amy had picked herself off the floor and begun to walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - Any marine plant or algae, especially those used as food or fertilizer

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *wrak* (something driven), referring to seaweed or wreckage washed up by the sea.

2. VERB

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
to smash, damage, or break forcefully; to subject to severe destruction or ruin

Examples

Had U. S. warships not appeared off the Dominican coast, there is every possibility that the country would now be wracked by civil war.

Synset Examples

The kid busted up the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreck - to destroy or severely damage
  • Synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly it no longer exists or cannot function
  • Synonym: shatter - to break suddenly into many small pieces

Etymology

From Middle English *wrak* (wreck, ruin), of Old English origin.
