divergence

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the act or process of moving or spreading apart from a common point or from each other

Examples

This may be because the Athabascan divergence began earlier; or again because the Athabascan languages spread over a very much larger territory (including three wholly separated areas); or both.

Synset Examples

an angle is formed by the divergence of two straight lines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergence - the process of coming together or moving toward a common point
  • Synonym: diversification - the process of becoming more varied or different

Etymology

From Latin 'divergere' (to go in different directions), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'vergere' (to turn).
