commission

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an official group of people who have been given authority to consider, investigate, or make decisions about a particular matter

Examples

An amazing article in the Manchester Guardian of last November, entitled "Fate of Redundant Churches ", states than an Archbishops' Commission "reported last month that in the Church of England alone there are 790 churches which are redundant now, or will be in 20 years' time.
Fishing interest calls for a check of the species found, quantity and size, the season they are available, and the stocking program of the fish commission.
It is not that the facts of the persecution were unavailable; most of the information elicited in Jerusalem had been brought to the surface by the numerous War Crimes tribunals and investigating commissions, and by reports, memoirs, and survivors' accounts.
They, perhaps, gave the pitch of their position in the preface where it was said that Eisenhower requested that the Commission be administered by the American Assembly of Columbia University, because it was non-partisan.
The Commission seems to represent the viewpoint of what I would call the unconscious liberal, but not unconscious enough, to invoke the now taboo symbolism of socialism.

Synset Examples

a committee is a group that keeps minutes and loses hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: committee - a group of people appointed for a specific function by a larger group

Etymology

From Latin commissio (sending together, delegation of business), from committere (to commit, entrust).

2. VERB

/ kəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
to formally appoint or equip for service, especially referring to assigning someone as an officer or preparing a ship or other entity for active duty

Examples

Chandler had been commissioned in the Medical Service Corps and was serving as a personnel officer for the Kansas City Medical Depot when he decided that if he was going to make the Army his career, he wanted to be in the fighting part of it.
There were nine qualified scouts sitting around collecting base pay the day Helva was commissioned.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decommission - to withdraw something, especially a ship or installation, from service

Etymology

From Latin commissio (entrusting, sending together), from committere (to entrust).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a fee paid to an employee or agent for services rendered, usually calculated as a percentage of the amount of business transacted, as opposed to a fixed salary

Examples

Outside salesmen deduct all expenses attributable to earning a salary, commission, or other compensation.

Synset Examples

he works on commission

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salary - fixed regular payment, typically paid monthly or biweekly, not based on percentage or commission

Etymology

From Latin commissio (entrusting, delegation).

4. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal act of granting authority to perform certain duties or hold an office

Examples

Among us, we three handled quite a few small commissions, from spot drawings for advertising agencies uptown to magazine work and quick lettering jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone
  • Antonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from office

Etymology

From Latin commissio, from committere (to entrust).

5. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
An official group of people appointed to investigate, consider, or regulate a particular matter.

Examples

The licenses were grandfathered in, so there was no problem with the Gaming Commission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: committee - group of people appointed for a specific function

Etymology

From Latin commissio (entrusting, delegation), from committere (to entrust, commit).
