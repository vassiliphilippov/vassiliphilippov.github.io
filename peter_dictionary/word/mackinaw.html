mackinaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmakɪnɔː / (BrE)
A short, thick woolen coat, often with a plaid pattern, designed to provide warmth in cold weather.

Examples

He left his home the day after New Year's wearing a mackinaw and sheepskin mittens and without a hat.

Etymology

From Mackinac, a trading post in Michigan, USA, where such coats were originally made.
