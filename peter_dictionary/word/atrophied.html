atrophied

1. ADJ

/ ˈætrəˌfiːd / (AmE), / ˈætrəˌfiːd / (BrE)
(of a body part or organ) diminished in size or strength, typically as a result of disease, injury, or lack of use.

Examples

This connection was clarified by Smith and Smith (1922), who showed that saline extracts of fresh bovine pituitary glands could re-activate the atrophied thyroids of hypophysectomised tadpoles.

Synset Examples

partial paralysis resulted in an atrophied left arm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasted - reduced in size and strength (of body part)
  • Antonym: normal - having typical size and strength (of body part)

Etymology

From Greek 'atrophia' ('a-' meaning 'not' + 'trophē' meaning 'nourishment').
