fleet

1. ADJ

/ fliːt / (AmE), / fliːt / (BrE)
moving very fast; able to move swiftly

Examples

We blushed and were flustered, and it turned out to be the fleetest brush of lips upon cheek.

Synset Examples

fleet of foot
the fleet scurrying of squirrels
a swift current
swift flight of an arrow
a swift runner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swift - moving very fast; marked by speed
  • Synonym: fast - moving or capable of moving at high speed
  • Antonym: slow - moving or operating at a low speed; not fast

Etymology

From Old English *flēotan* (to float, swim); related to Old Norse *fljōta*, reflecting swiftness or rapid movement.

2. NOUN

/ flit / (AmE), / fliːt / (BrE)
A group of aircraft operating together under the same ownership or command.

Examples

Also, the program for fleet modernization will be stepped up in 1961 causing an increase in expenditures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squadron - a unit or group of aircraft in an air force, typically smaller than a fleet

Etymology

From Old English *fleot* (a ship, fleet) related to *fleotan* (to float).
