trinket

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋkət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋkɪt / (BrE)
A small, inexpensive item of jewelry or ornament, often showy but of little value.

Examples

Dangling his gaudy trinkets before them, he told of the Rebel losses in the mountains, at Cheat and Rich mountains both, and the Federal march on Beverly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'trenket' (a kind of knife), later applied to small objects.
