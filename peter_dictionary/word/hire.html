hire

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to obtain the temporary use of something in exchange for payment; to rent (especially goods or services)

Examples

Berlin's resilience is amazing, but if it has to hire its labor in the West the struggle will be hard indeed ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to pay for the use of something, especially property or goods, temporarily

Etymology

From Old English hȳrian (to hire, let for pay), related to Old High German huor (payment).

2. VERB

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to employ someone to do a particular job for a period of time in exchange for payment

Examples

This is when his troubles begin, not to mention a fledgling artist who he hires, and who turns out to have ideas of his own, with particular respect to the hero's sweetheart secretary.
Great actors and actresses - the most notable being Sarah Bernhardt - were hired to repeat their stage performances before the camera.
Orvis even needed to hire an assistant, Clark J. Wait.
They hire a good deal of local labor, including two members of our Trustee Board.
Sposato could n't wait to get me hired.

Synset Examples

They hired two new secretaries in the department
How many people has she employed?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employ - to give someone work and pay them for it
  • Antonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job

Etymology

From Old English *hȳrian* (to hire, let, lease), related to Old Norse *hӯra* (payment, wages).
