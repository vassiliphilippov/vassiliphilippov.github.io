racket

1. NOUN

/ ˈræk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈræk.ɪt / (BrE)
a loud and unpleasant noise

Examples

I could n't make out what his racket was.
Dimly, he heard laughter, hoots of derision, but he could not read the racket properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • Antonym: quiet - absence of noise or sound

Etymology

Possibly from Middle French 'raquette' meaning noise or from Old French 'racquet', but the exact origin is uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrækɪt / (AmE), / ˈrækɪt / (BrE)
an illegal or dishonest business or activity, often involving organized crime, carried on for profit

Examples

He was also at this time, although not so interwoven in high politics and the rackets as Torrio and Capone, the most powerful and most dangerous mob leader in the Chicago underworld, the roughneck king.
Through our newspaper contacts we've been able to put out material about McCluskey's link with Sollozzo in the drug rackets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud, especially for making money
  • Synonym: fraud - the crime of deceiving people to gain money or goods
  • Antonym: business - commercial activity, especially legitimate trade

Etymology

Probably from Italian *racchetta* or *raccatto* (a fraudulent scheme), though exact origin is uncertain.
