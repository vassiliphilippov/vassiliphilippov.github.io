gregarious

1. ADJ

/ ɡrəˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
(Of animals) tending to form groups or live in flocks with others of the same species.

Examples

Although Andrena is gregarious, so that we may find hundreds and hundreds of burrows together, we must still call it a solitary bee.

Synset Examples

gregarious bird species

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solitary - (of animals) tending to live alone rather than in groups

Etymology

From Latin 'gregarius' (of a flock or herd), from 'grex' (herd, flock).

2. ADJ

/ ɡrɪˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɛːriəs / (BrE)
(of a person) fond of the company of others; sociable.

Examples

It was a ridiculous situation and Rector knew it, for Hino, frankly partisan, openly gregarious, would make a poor espionage agent.

Synset Examples

he is a gregarious person who avoids solitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociable - willing to talk and engage with other people; friendly
  • Antonym: introverted - more interested in one's own thoughts and feelings than in external things, especially social situations

Etymology

From Latin gregarious, from grex (flock, herd).
