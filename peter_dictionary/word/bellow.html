bellow

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛləʊ / (BrE)
to shout in a loud and powerful voice, often without restraint

Examples

It was Mr. Jack, bellowing out in the hall.
"Got the upstairs guy ", he bellows.
He bellowed orders and watched the alert response of some of his men and watched, too, the way a dozen or more turned their heads questioningly toward the shackled figure as though for further instruction.
While he was at it, the philosopher of the Kremlin contributed an additional assist to the rule of reason by bellowing at those in the west who can n't appreciate coexistence thru suicide.
Musmanno bellowed to his Italian crewmen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly, typically in anger or excitement
  • Synonym: roar - to shout or utter in a deep, loud voice
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly

Etymology

From Old English *bylian* (to bellow, roar), related to animal sounds and loud cries.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛləʊ / (BrE)
a very loud shout or roar, especially one made by a person or animal

Examples

Then they let out a bellow of anguish and rage and, cursing, screaming and hollering "Garryowen "! they charged into the enemy like wild men.
With a bellow Carmer lunged at him.

Synset Examples

his bellow filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - a loud, deep sound made by an animal or person
  • Antonym: whisper - a very quiet way of speaking or making sound

Etymology

From Old English 'bylgan' (to bellow) and Old Norse 'bölva' (to curse), related to imitation of a roaring sound.
