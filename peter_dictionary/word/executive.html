executive

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
having the function or authority to carry out plans, decisions, or laws; relating to the execution or administration of activities or policies

Examples

The change may require different products, pricing, packaging, warehousing, salesmanship, advertising and executive attention - practically every link in the marketing network may have to be adjusted.
However, it determined that neither this factor, nor "the fact that all concerned in high executive posts in both companies acted honorably and fairly, each in the honest conviction that his actions were in the best interests of his own company and without any design to overreach anyone, including du Pont's competitors ", 353 U. S., at 607, outweighed the Government's claim for relief.

Synset Examples

the executive branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrative - relating to management or administration
  • Antonym: legislative - relating to the making of laws (government); opposite in governmental contexts
  • Antonym: judicial - relating to the administration of justice (government); opposite in governmental contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'executivus', from 'exsequi' meaning 'to carry out'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
a person who has a high-level position in an organization and is responsible for making important decisions, especially in business

Examples

We find too many sales and marketing executives so burdened with detail that they are short-changing planning.
Indeed, as already noted, the Court proceeded on the assumption that the executives involved in the dealings between du Pont and General Motors acted "honorably and fairly "and exercised their business judgment only to serve what they deemed the best interests of their own companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • Synonym: administrator - a person responsible for managing and organizing work, especially within a business or government

Etymology

From Latin 'executivus', from 'exsequi' (to carry out).

3. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
The branch or group of people in government responsible for carrying out and enforcing laws.

Examples

The legislative mills have been grinding ever since, and when its cumbersome processes were no longer adequate to the task, a limited legislative authority was delegated in one form or another, to the executive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legislature - the branch of government that makes laws
  • Antonym: judiciary - the branch of government that interprets laws

Etymology

From Latin executivus (of execution), from exsequi (to follow out).
