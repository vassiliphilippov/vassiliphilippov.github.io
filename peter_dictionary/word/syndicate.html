syndicate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪn.dɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.dɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
a group of companies or organizations formed to pursue a particular business purpose or project together

Examples

By springtime, he was supported by a rich merchant syndicate under the patronage of Henry, Prince of Wales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consortium - an association of several businesses or organizations for a common purpose

Etymology

From French *syndicat* (council), from Medieval Latin *syndicatus*, from *syndicus* (representative), based on Greek *syndikos* (helping in a court of justice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.dɪ.kət / (BrE)
an organized group that controls or coordinates illegal activities, especially those related to crime

Examples

But he still showed no intention of sharing with the syndicate.
Commissioner Gordon informs me that he has targeted those businesses... ... suspected of fronting for the syndicate in this city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - a large organized crime group
  • Synonym: gang - an organized group of criminals
  • Synonym: cartel - an association of businesses or crime groups

Etymology

From French *syndicat* (council, association), from Late Latin *syndicus* (representative), from Greek *syndikos* (caretaker of an issue).
