singularity

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋɡjəˈlɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪŋɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being unique or one of a kind

Examples

Finally, the theatrical (and perversely erotic) notions of dressing up, cosmetics, disguise, and especially change of costume (or singularity of costume, as with Cipolla), are characteristically associated with the catastrophes of Mann's stories.

Synset Examples

that singularity distinguished him from all his companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being unique
  • Antonym: commonness - the quality of being common or widespread

Etymology

From Late Latin singularitas, from Latin singularis (single, unique).
