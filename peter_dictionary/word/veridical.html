veridical

1. ADJ

/ vəˈrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / vəˈrɪdɪkəl / (BrE)
(of perceptions, statements, etc.) corresponding to reality; truthful or accurate.

Examples

This behavior is more "veridical "- or true than other testing behavior for some types of evaluation, and so can give quick and accurate estimates of complex functioning.

Synset Examples

perceptual error...has a surprising resemblance to veridical perception

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illusory - not real; produced by illusion

Etymology

From Latin veridicus ‘truthful’, from verus ‘true’ + dicere ‘to say’.
