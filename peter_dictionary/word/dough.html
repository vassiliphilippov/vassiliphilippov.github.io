dough

1. NOUN

/ doʊ / (AmE), / dəʊ / (BrE)
(informal) Money.

Examples

Investors breathed more freely when it was learned that this acrobatic dancer had turned magician and was only doing a best seller book to make some dough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bread - informal term for money
  • Synonym: cash - ready money in the form of notes and coins
  • Synonym: loot - slang for money; profits or winnings

2. NOUN

/ doʊ / (AmE), / dəʊ / (BrE)
A mixture of flour and liquid, often with other ingredients, that is stiff enough to knead or roll and used as the base for bread, pastries, and similar baked products.

Examples

I knew better but I was thinking of the Pedersen kid mother-naked in all that dough.
Water from the snow and water from the towels had run off the kid to the table where the dough was, and the dough was turning pasty, sticking to the kid's back and behind.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: batter - a mixture of flour and liquid that is thin enough to pour, used in baking and frying

Etymology

From Old English *dag* (dough), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *deeg* and German *Teig*.
