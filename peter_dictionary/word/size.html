size

1. NOUN

/ saɪz / (AmE), / saɪz / (BrE)
The physical magnitude or dimensions of something; how large or small something is.

Examples

"Only a relative handful of such reports was received ", the jury said, "considering the widespread interest in the election, the number of voters and the size of this city ".
Physique contests are rarely won on muscle size alone.
These specialists, I learned, have done a great deal of work to improve the size and health of the plants and the resulting flowers.
Their seeds produce vigorous blooming plants half again the size of the unimproved strains.
It is brought to packing houses, cleaned and graded as to size and quality, and packed in protective excelsior.

Synset Examples

a wolf is about the size of a large dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - amount of space an object occupies
  • Synonym: dimension - measurable extent, such as length, width, or height
  • Antonym: weight - the amount something weighs
  • Antonym: smallness - the quality of being small in size

Etymology

From Old French *sise*, from Anglo-Norman, shortened from *assise* (a financial levy, assessment).

2. NOUN

/ saɪz / (AmE), / saɪz / (BrE)
a numbered or lettered category indicating the measurements or dimensions of an item, especially clothing or shoes

Examples

All sizes: big ones, some wired to set off a whole field, little ones, hand grenade size.
All sizes: big ones, some wired to set off a whole field, little ones, hand grenade size.
These blocks are sold in various sizes so it seldom is necessary to cut a block to fit.

Synset Examples

he wears a size 13 shoe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measurement - the act of measuring or the size determined by measuring

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'sis', Old French 'assise' (a setting or measure).
