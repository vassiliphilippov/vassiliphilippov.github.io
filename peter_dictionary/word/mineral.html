mineral

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnərəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl / (BrE)
A naturally occurring solid substance that is not of animal or vegetable origin, has a definite chemical composition, and is typically formed through geological processes.

Examples

Calcium, phosphorus and iron are present in worthwhile amounts, and eleven other minerals also have been found in varying trace amounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ore - a type of mineral from which metals can be extracted
  • Degree Higher: rock - a natural solid aggregate of minerals or mineraloids

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'minerale', meaning 'substance obtained by mining'.
