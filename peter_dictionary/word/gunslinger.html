gunslinger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌslɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌslɪŋə / (BrE)
A person, especially in the American Old West, who is skilled with a gun and is known for engaging in gunfights; often portrayed as a professional gunfighter or killer.

Examples

I ai n't a gunslinger ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunfighter - person known for fighting with guns, esp. in the Old West
  • Synonym: outlaw - person who has broken the law, especially in the Old West

Etymology

From 'gun' + 'slinger', referring to one who slings or handles a gun.
