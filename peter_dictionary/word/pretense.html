pretense

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriːˌtɛns / (AmE), / ˈpriːtɛns / (BrE)
An attempt to make something that is not the case appear true; the act of giving a false appearance.

Examples

Its pretense to operate in the public interest is little more than a sham.

Synset Examples

his conformity was only pretending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simulation - the act of imitating or pretending
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Old French 'pretense' (pretended claim), from Medieval Latin 'praetensus', past participle of Latin 'praetendere' (to stretch forth, pretend).
