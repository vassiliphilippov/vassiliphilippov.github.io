cast

1. NOUN

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
The group of actors who perform in a play, film, or other production.

Examples

No casts are listed, but Lotte Lehmann sent word that the Negro soprano, Grace Bumbry, will sing Venus in "Tannhaeuser ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ensemble - a group of actors, musicians, or dancers who perform together

Etymology

From Old Norse kasta (to throw), later developed into various senses including 'to assign a role'.

2. VERB

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
to choose actors, singers, or dancers for particular roles in a play, film, or other performance

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audition - a tryout for performers to show their suitability for a role
  • Synonym: assign - to designate someone for a specific role or function

From Old Norse 'kasta' (to throw).

4. NOUN

/ kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
a container or mold into which liquid material is poured to form a particular shape as it hardens

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a hollow container used to give shape to molten or hot liquid material when it cools and hardens

Etymology

From Middle English 'casten', from Old Norse 'kasta' (to throw). The noun sense developed from the idea of something that is cast (thrown) or shaped.
