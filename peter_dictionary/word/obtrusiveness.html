obtrusiveness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈtruːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əbˈtruːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being noticeable in an unwelcome or intrusive way.

Examples

The sets are remarkably elaborate for a road-show that does n't pause long in any one place, and they are devised so that they shift with a minimum of interruption or obtrusiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrusiveness - the quality of intruding or being unwelcome
  • Antonym: subtlety - the state of being not obvious or conspicuous

Etymology

From 'obtrusive' + '-ness'. 'Obtrusive' from Latin 'obtrudere' (to thrust upon).
