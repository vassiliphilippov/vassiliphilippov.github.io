buck

1. NOUN

/ bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
a gymnastic apparatus consisting of a padded, elongated support without pommels, used for vaulting exercises

Examples

The parallel bars, horse, buck, springboard, horizontal bar, rings, and mats formerly in the school gyms were replaced by baseball, volleyball, basketball and football.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaulting horse - a gymnastic apparatus for vaulting; often with pommels

2. VERB

/ bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
To strive energetically or make determined efforts to achieve something, especially to gain promotion or advancement (often used with 'for').

Examples

The troop received a new leader, Lieutenant Robert M. Carroll, fresh out of ROTC and bucking for Regular Army status.
One of the more seriously wounded was Lieutenant Carroll, the young officer bucking for the Regular Army.

Synset Examples

John is bucking for a promotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strive - to make great efforts to achieve something

3. VERB

/ bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
to resist or oppose something, especially a prevailing trend, tendency, or pressure

Examples

However, in the same three-month period, toll-road bonds, as a group, have bucked this trend.

Synset Examples

buck the trend

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conform - to comply with rules, standards, or laws; to go along with trends or norms

4. NOUN

/ bʌk / (AmE), / bʌk / (BrE)
(informal) A dollar; a unit of U.S. or Canadian paper currency.

Examples

Five bucks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dollar - the standard unit of money in the U.S., Canada, Australia, and some other countries
