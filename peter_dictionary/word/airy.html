airy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈɛə.ri / (BrE)
(of a room or space) well-ventilated and filled with fresh air; spacious and open to fresh air.

Examples

That is why it is important to invest in comfortable, airy types of shoes.
The apex, the study where Eugene slept, was light and bright and airy and cheerful.
He could no longer build anything, whether a private residence in his Pennsylvania county or a church in Brazil, without it being obvious that he had done it, and while here and there he was taken to task for again developing the same airy technique, they were such fanciful and sometimes even playful buildings that the public felt assured by its sense of recognition after a time, a quality of authentic uniqueness about them, which, once established by an artist as his private vision, is no longer disputable as to its other values.
Airy and bright, the apartment was furnished with good modern furniture, rugs, and draperies.

Synset Examples

airy rooms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stuffy - (of a room or space) lacking fresh air; airless or poorly ventilated

Etymology

From air + -y, originally meaning 'consisting of air' or 'light as air'.
