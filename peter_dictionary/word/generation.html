generation

1. NOUN

/ ˌd͡ʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌd͡ʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
all the people born and living about the same time, regarded collectively as a group distinguished by shared characteristics or experiences

Examples

This is especially in evidence among the present generation of the suburban middle class.
What actually occurred in the past is seldom as important as what a given generation feels must have occurred.
She was the last living of the older generation.
In Berlin he published his views of the chemical laws of nature in German and this was issued in French translation (Paris, 1813) under the title Recherches sur l' identite des forces chimiques et electriques, a work held in very high esteem by the new generation of research chemists.
As the generations move on, clothes become more suitable for the enjoyment of outdoor sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohort - a group of people banded together or treated as a group, often of similar age
  • Synonym: age group - a group of people of the same age

Etymology

From Latin 'generatio', from 'generare' (to beget, create).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)

Examples

Synset Examples

they had to wait a generation for that prejudice to fade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: age cohort - a group of people born and living at about the same time

Etymology

From Latin *generatio* (begetting, generation), from *generare* (to beget).
