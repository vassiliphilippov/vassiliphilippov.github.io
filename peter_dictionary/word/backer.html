backer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækər / (AmE), / ˈbækə / (BrE)
a person or organization that provides money, support, or resources for a project, especially in the arts, business, or entertainment; an investor.

Examples

He was not the sort of sailor Hudson wanted his backers to see on board and he had Greene wait at Gravesend, where the Discovery picked him up.
Hudson named the capes Digges and Wolstenholme, for two of his backers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponsor - a person or organization that provides funds for a project or event

Etymology

From 'back' (to support) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
