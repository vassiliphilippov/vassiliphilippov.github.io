noisy

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔɪzi / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzi / (BrE)
making or full of loud, unpleasant, or distracting sounds.

Examples

You have your paper horn clutched in your big, craggy fist, and for your entrance you have planned a noisy, colorful and exuberant greeting to your friends and tenants.
"Speaking of nonism: the other day, in a story about a sit-down demonstration, the Paris Herald Tribune wrote,' The non violence became noisier'.
Without monitoring, a military hookup becomes a noisy party.
I left it noisy, so it scares away any pain-in-the-ass innocent bystanders.

Synset Examples

a noisy cafeteria
a small noisy dog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
  • Degree Higher: loud - producing a lot of noise
  • Degree Lower: silent - making no sound at all

Etymology

From noise + -y (adjective suffix); noise from Old French noise (quarrel, uproar), from Latin nausea (sea-sickness).
