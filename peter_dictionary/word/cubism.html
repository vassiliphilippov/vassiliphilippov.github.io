cubism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (BrE)
A style of art and an early 20th-century movement originating in France, characterized by the depiction of objects using geometric forms and the breakdown of subjects into abstract planes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: futurism - an early 20th-century artistic movement focused on speed and technology
  • Antonym: impressionism - an art movement featuring visible brushstrokes and emphasis on light

Etymology

From French 'cubisme', derived from 'cube'.
