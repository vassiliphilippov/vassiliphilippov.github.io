woe

1. NOUN

/ woʊ / (AmE), / wəʊ / (BrE)
Great sorrow or distress; misery resulting from affliction or hardship.

Examples

That parallelism affects his picture of man's disobedience too; for as it was Christ, the Word of God, who came to rescue man, so it was disobedience to the word of God in the beginning that brought death into the world, and all our woe.
Lincoln understood this better than most when he said in his "Second Inaugural "that God "gives to both North and South this terrible war, as the woe due to those by whom the offense came ".
He hears your tale of woe and quite naturally wants to cheer you up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffering - the state of undergoing pain, distress, or hardship
  • Synonym: misery - a state of great suffering and unhappiness
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Middle English *wo*, from Old English *wā* (interjection expressing sorrow).
