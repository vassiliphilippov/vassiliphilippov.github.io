unsold

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsoʊld / (AmE), / ʌnˈsəʊld / (BrE)
(of goods, property, etc.) not purchased or remaining without a buyer.

Examples

The safe is a repository for three dead flashlight batteries, a hundred and fifty unused left-hand fingerprint cards, a stack of unsold Policemen's Ball tickets from last year, and thirty-seven cents in coins and stamps.

Synset Examples

the house has been on the market almost a year and is still unsold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sold - (of goods, property, etc.) bought or disposed of by purchase

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'sold', the past participle of 'sell'.
