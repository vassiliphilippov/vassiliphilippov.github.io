foam

1. NOUN

/ foʊm / (AmE), / fəʊm / (BrE)
a lightweight material consisting of a mass of small gas bubbles trapped in a solid or liquid, often used for cushioning, insulation, or packaging

Examples

For added comfort some of the Italian designed sandals have foam padded cushioning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge - a lightweight, porous material used for absorption or cleaning

2. NOUN

/ foʊm / (AmE), / fəʊm / (BrE)
a mass of small bubbles formed on the surface of or within a liquid, often as a result of agitation or fermentation

Examples

Exclusive of esthetic values, such as high - or low foam level, perfume content, etc., the requirements for the organic active used in washing textiles are high.
Miraculously, the bottle was still in my hand, foam still geysering over my (luckily) waterproof watch.
I saw Johnson's bottle snatched from his hand, saw it go in a swirl of foam just behind the second car.

Synset Examples

the beer had a thick head of foam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: froth - a mass of small bubbles on a liquid, often light and white

Etymology

From Old English 'fām' (foam, froth), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ foʊm / (AmE), / fəʊm / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to produce or cover with a mass of small bubbles; to become frothy or bubbly.

Examples

The beer foamed furiously.

Synset Examples

The boiling soup was frothing
The river was foaming
Sparkling water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: froth - to produce or become covered with small bubbles

Etymology

From Old English 'fām' (foam; froth), of Germanic origin.
