astronomical

1. ADJ

/ əˌstrɑːnəˈmɪkəl / (AmE), / əˌstrɒnəˈmɪkəl / (BrE)
Extremely large in amount, size, or degree; immense.

Examples

The expense and time involved are astronomical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size, quantity, or extent
  • Synonym: colossal - extremely large or great

Etymology

From astronomical, originally meaning 'pertaining to astronomy', from astronomy + -ical.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæstrəˈnɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the science of astronomy or celestial objects

Examples

High sensitivity in radio telescopes is achieved by reducing the bandwidth of the receiver; therefore, only with precise foreknowledge of the line frequencies is an astronomical search for the radio spectra of these molecules feasible.

Synset Examples

astronomic telescope

Etymology

From Latin 'astronomicus', from Greek 'astronomikos', from 'astronomia' (astronomy).
