alabaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.əˌbæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈæl.əˌbɑː.stə / (BrE)
A compact, fine-textured, usually white form of gypsum, often used for carving and ornamental work.

Examples

Flanked by marble urns and alabaster lamps, they seemed to be posing for a tribal portrait.
The alabaster cockatoo ".
And it ain't alabaster, either.

Etymology

From Old French 'alabastre', from Latin 'alabaster', from Greek 'alábastros' (vase of alabaster).
