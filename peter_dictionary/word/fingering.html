fingering

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement or placement of the fingers when playing notes or passages on a musical instrument.

Examples

For example, to move (as the score requires) from the lowest F major register up to a barely audible N minor in four seconds, not skipping, at the same time, even one of the 407 fingerings, seems a feat too absurd to consider, and it is to the flautist's credit that he remained silent throughout the passage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finger - the part of the hand used for playing instruments and forming fingerings

Etymology

From 'finger' + '-ing', indicating the action or result related to fingers.
