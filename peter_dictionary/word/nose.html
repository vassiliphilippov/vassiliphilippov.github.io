nose

1. NOUN

/ noʊz / (AmE), / nəʊz / (BrE)
the part of the face that sticks out above the mouth and contains the nostrils, used for breathing and smelling

Examples

Moritz does n't even have a nose guard or hip pads but he's one of the most valuable members of the Longhorn team that will be heavily favored Saturday over Oklahoma in the cotton bowl.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nostril - either of the two external openings of the nose

Etymology

From Old English *nosu* (nose), related to Dutch *neus* and German *Nase*.

2. VERB

/ noʊz / (AmE), / nəʊz / (BrE)
to search or inquire into something in a meddlesome or prying way, often used with 'around' or 'about'

Examples

Hub, nosing about, spotted reporters in the lobby, so Andy was hustled away quietly through the hotel's service entrance in a strange car which Hub had procured somewhere.

Synset Examples

This guy is always nosing around the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pry - to inquire too closely into a person's private affairs
  • Synonym: meddle - to interfere in or busy oneself unduly with something that is not one's concern

Etymology

From the noun 'nose'; figurative use based on the idea of sniffing around like an animal.

/ noʊz / (AmE), / nəʊz / (BrE)
to move the front part of something forward slowly and carefully

Examples

Etymology
