lord

1. NOUN

/ lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
A man who has power and authority over others, especially in a feudal, religious, or noble context.

Examples

"So that the man should not have thoughts of grandeur, and become lifted up, as if he had no lord, because of the dominion that had been given to him, and the freedom, fall into sin against God his Creator, overstepping his bounds, and take up an attitude of self-conceited arrogance towards God, a law was given him by God, that he might know that he had for lord the lord of all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master - person who has control or authority over others
  • Antonym: servant - person who works for another, typically in a subordinate position

Etymology

From Old English hlāford (master, ruler), from hlāf (bread) + weard (keeper).

2. NOUN

/ lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
A man who has a rank of nobility, especially a peer in the British system.

Examples

In September 1817 at Fort Daer (Pembina) Dickson met the noble lord whom, with the help of a band of Sioux, he escorted to Prairie du Chien.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lady - female equivalent of a lord; a woman of noble rank

Etymology

From Old English hlāford, meaning 'master' or 'ruler'.
