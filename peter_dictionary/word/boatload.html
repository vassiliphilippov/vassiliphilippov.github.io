boatload

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊtˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊtˌləʊd / (BrE)
an amount of cargo or number of passengers that a boat or ship can carry; often used informally to mean a large quantity of something

Examples

When McFeeley was halfway to the door, the proprietor emerged - a mountainous, dark man, his head thick with resiny black hair, his eyes like two of the black olives he imported in boatloads.

Synset Examples

he imported wine by the boatload

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truckload - the amount that a truck can carry
  • Synonym: carload - the amount that a car or railcar can carry

Etymology

Formed from 'boat' + 'load'; modeled on similar compounds such as 'truckload'.
