feverishly

1. ADV

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃli / (BrE)
In a way that is full of intense excitement, energy, or agitation; with great urgency or activity.

Examples

Feverishly, he tried to brush away this intuition.
Reaching the porch rail beyond view of the bar windows, he feverishly scanned the busy street below.

Synset Examples

she worked feverishly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantically - in a hurried, excited, or disorganized way
  • Antonym: calmly - in a relaxed, tranquil, or unhurried manner

Etymology

From 'feverish', itself from Middle English 'feverisch', based on 'fever' (from Latin 'febris', fever) + '-ish'.
