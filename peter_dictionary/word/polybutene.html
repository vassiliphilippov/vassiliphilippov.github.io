polybutene

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈbjuːtin / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbjuːtiːn / (BrE)
A synthetic polymer made from butylene, used in lubricants and synthetic rubber.

Examples

By this method it was determined that the normal pressure exerted by a sample of polybutene (molecular weight reported to be 770) was over half an atmosphere.
The two fluids which gave the small negative pressures were polybutenes with molecular weights which were stated to be 520 and 300.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyethylene - a polymer made from ethylene
  • Synonym: polypropylene - a polymer made from propylene

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many) and 'butene' (a hydrocarbon); refers to a polymer formed from butene monomers.
