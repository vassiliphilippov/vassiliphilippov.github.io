dense

1. ADJ

/ dɛns / (AmE), / dɛns / (BrE)
(of a substance or object) allowing little or no light to pass through because it is thick or closely packed.

Examples

They dislike dense vegetation.
It seemed to her, as it seemed each night, that the gloom drew itself in and became densest at the table's empty chairs, giving her the frequent illusion that she dined with shadows.
Every morning early, in the summer, we searched the trunks of the trees as high as we could reach for the locust shells, carefully detached their hooked claws from the bark where they hung, and stabled them, a weird faery herd, in an angle between the high roots of the tulip tree, where no grass grew in the dense shade.

Synset Examples

dense smoke
heavy fog
impenetrable gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • Antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen

Etymology

From Latin 'densus' meaning thick, crowded.

2. ADJ

/ dɛns / (AmE), / dɛns / (BrE)
having a high relative density or specific gravity; closely compacted in substance

Examples

Solid concrete blocks, relatively heavy and dense, are used for this shelter.

Synset Examples

dense as lead

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light - having low relative density or specific gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'densus' meaning 'thick, dense'.
