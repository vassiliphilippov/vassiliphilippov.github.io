uninjectable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈdʒɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈdʒɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) not capable of being injected into the body.

Examples

It is distinctly possible, therefore, that simultaneous pressures in all three vessels would have rendered the shunts inoperable and hence, uninjectable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: injectable - able to be injected into the body

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) and injectable (able to be injected).
