manic

1. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˈmænɪk / (BrE)
Showing wild, very excited, or uncontrolled behavior, often as a result of extreme mental excitement or mania.

Examples

The Zeitgeist is not crazy as a loon or mad as a March hare; it is manic as a man ".
"The time has come ", the walrus said, "To speak of manic things, Of shots and shouts, and sealing dooms Of commoners and kings ".

Synset Examples

a frenzied attack
a frenzied mob
the prosecutor's frenzied denunciation of the accused
outbursts of drunken violence and manic activity and creativity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - marked by wild and hurried activity
  • Antonym: calm - free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From Latin maniacus, from Greek maniakos (mad, frenzied), related to mania.
