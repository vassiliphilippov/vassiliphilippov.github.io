infinitive

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪnətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪnətɪv / (BrE)
The basic form of a verb, usually preceded by 'to' in English, that is not inflected for tense, person, or number.

Examples

Thus, the masculine animate infinitive dabhumaksanigalu'ahai, meaning to live, was, in the perfect tense, ksu'u'peli'afo, and, in the future, mai'teipa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerund - A verbal noun form ending in -ing and functioning as a noun

Etymology

From Late Latin infinitivus (unlimited, indefinite), from Latin infinitivus, from infinitus (infinite, without end).
