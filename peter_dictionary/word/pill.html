pill

1. NOUN

/ pɪl / (AmE), / pɪl / (BrE)
A small, solid dose of medicine intended to be swallowed.

Examples

Some liar's logic, a wisp of optimism as fragile as the scent of tropical blossoms that came through the window (a euphoria perhaps engendered by the pill Fritzie had given her), consoled her for a moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tablet - a flat, compressed dose of medicine
  • Synonym: capsule - a small container with medicine inside for swallowing

Etymology

From Middle English pille, from Old French, from Latin pilula, diminutive of pila (ball).
