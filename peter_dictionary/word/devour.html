devour

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvaʊɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊə / (BrE)
(often figurative) To destroy or consume something completely, especially with great force or violence.

Examples

Our enemy is also threatening to devour us.

Synset Examples

Fire had devoured our home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consume - to use up or destroy something completely
  • Synonym: destroy - to ruin or put an end to something's existence

Etymology

From Latin *devorare*, from *de-* (down) + *vorare* (to swallow).
