euphoria

1. NOUN

/ juˈfɔriə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːriə / (BrE)
A feeling or state of intense excitement and happiness, often exaggerated or abnormal.

Examples

Some liar's logic, a wisp of optimism as fragile as the scent of tropical blossoms that came through the window (a euphoria perhaps engendered by the pill Fritzie had given her), consoled her for a moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elation - a feeling of great joy or pride
  • Degree Higher: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness or joyful excitement
  • Degree Lower: happiness - the state of being happy
  • Antonym: depression - a state of severe despondency and dejection

Etymology

From Greek *euphoria* (power of enduring easily), from *eu-* (well) + *pherein* (to bear).
