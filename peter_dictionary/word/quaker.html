Quaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkə / (BrE)
a member of the Religious Society of Friends, a Christian movement founded in the 17th century by George Fox, known for its emphasis on pacifism and simplicity

Examples

On November 7, 1859, the Providence Daily Journal reprinted a letter sent to John Brown from "E. B. ", a Quaker lady in Newport.

Etymology

From the verb 'quake', originally a term of ridicule referring to those who 'trembled' at the word of God.
