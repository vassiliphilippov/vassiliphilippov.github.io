along

1. ADV

/ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
(used with 'with') In addition to; together with something else.

Examples

"Along with the fruit they did also fall under the power of death, because they did eat in disobedience; and disobedience to God entails death.
From his playmates in Savannah, Mercer had picked up, along with a soft Southern dialect, traces also of the Gullah dialects of Africa.
With my baton up your ass!

Synset Examples

we sent them food and some clothing went along in the package
along with the package came a bill
consider the advantages along with the disadvantages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: together with - in addition to; as well as
  • Synonym: besides - in addition to; apart from

Etymology

From Middle English 'alonge', from Old English 'andlang', meaning 'continuous, extended'.

2. ADV

/ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
moving forward in a particular direction, especially following a path, road, or line; with a forward motion.

Examples

They germinate quickly, the tiny plants appearing in a week, and grow along lustily.
They had traveled only a short distance when they spotted five Mexicans riding along a horse-trail across the stream just ahead of them.
Soon they were picking their way along the edge of the stream which glowed in the night.
In the very front rank, two men were wounded and staggered along, trailing blood behind them.
"I do not care if your beliefs take you along a path of religion or a path of labor or a path of activism.

Synset Examples

we drove along admiring the view
the horse trotted along at a steady pace
the circus traveled on to the next city
move along
march on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onward - moving forward or continuing towards a place or position
  • Synonym: forward - moving ahead in the direction one is facing

Etymology

From Middle English 'along', from Old English 'andlang' (entire, continuous, extending in length).
