spill

1. VERB

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
to cause or allow a liquid or other substance to flow over the edge of its container, often accidentally

Examples

Soon after Loper leaked the news that Frankie had ordered "two of everything "just "in case he spills anything ", Frankie got so mad at the chic designer that he vowed he would not wear a stitch of Loper clothing.
You're spilling it.

Synset Examples

spill the milk
splatter water

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pour - to cause liquid to flow in a steady stream, typically in a controlled way
  • Degree Higher: splatter - to splash liquid or a thick substance over a surface, especially with force

Etymology

Old English *spillan* (to kill, destroy, waste), related to Old Norse *spilla*.

2. VERB

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
to cause or allow (a solid substance) to flow or fall out of a container, especially by accident

Examples

To solve the problem of the wheat grains spilling on the floor and getting underfoot, a ball of maple syrup boiled to candy consistency was invented to hold the grains.

Synset Examples

spill the beans all over the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - throw loosely about; separate and strew
  • Antonym: pour - cause a liquid to flow

Etymology

From Old English *spillan* (to destroy, kill, waste), later shifting to the modern meaning.

3. VERB

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to flow, run, or fall out of a container, often by accident, and become lost or scattered.

Examples

It was dark inside the room but enough light spilled from the restaurant behind her to enable her to make out a round table with a green cloth top.

Synset Examples

The milk spilled across the floor
The wine spilled onto the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to flow over the brim or edge
  • Synonym: leak - to escape accidentally from a container

Etymology

Probably of Low German origin; related to German 'spielen' (to play), in the sense 'to shed or let fall'.

4. VERB

/ spɪl / (AmE), / spɪl / (BrE)
to reveal secret or confidential information, especially unintentionally

Examples

The next morning, right about the time Raquel was spilling her little secret, a man nobody ever laid eyes on before strode into the Maine National Bank.

Synset Examples

If you don't oblige me, I'll talk!
The former employee spilled all the details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divulge - to make known something secret
  • Synonym: disclose - to make something known publicly
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret; not reveal
