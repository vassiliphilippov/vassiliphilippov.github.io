smug

1. ADJ

/ smʌɡ / (AmE), / smʌɡ / (BrE)
Having or showing an excessive pride in oneself or one's achievements; self-satisfied in an irritating or complacent way.

Examples

But can we any longer afford the luxury of such smug indignation?
He saw the smug eyes of the Home Army chief, Roman, and all the Romans and the faces of the peasants who held only hatred for him.
He saw Sweden as a country of smug and narrow provincialism, indifferent to the heroic spirit of its former glory.

Synset Examples

a smug glow of self-congratulation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complacent - uncritically satisfied with oneself or one's achievements
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble view of one's achievements

Etymology

Perhaps from Low German *smuk* (trim, neat).
