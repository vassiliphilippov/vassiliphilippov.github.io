inconveniently

1. ADV

/ ɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt.li / (AmE), / ɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that causes difficulty or discomfort; not conveniently.

Examples

A fourteen year old girl from the Middle West observed wryly that, in her community, religion inconveniently interfered with religious activities - at least with the peripheral activities that many middle class Jews now regard as religious.

Synset Examples

he arrived at an inconveniently late hour

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conveniently - in a way that suits one's purposes

Etymology

From 'inconvenient' + '-ly'. 'Inconvenient' from Latin 'inconveniens' (unsuitable, improper).
