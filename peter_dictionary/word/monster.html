monster

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstə / (BrE)
a person or animal that is markedly unusual or deformed, often regarded with horror or disgust

Examples

Oversized monsters are never brought home either alive or preserved, and field measurements are obviously open to doubt because of the universal tendency to exaggerate dimensions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freak - a person or animal with unusual physical deformity or abnormality

Etymology

From Latin 'monstrum' (portent, unnatural event, monster), from 'monere' (to warn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstə / (BrE)
A person, animal, or thing that is extremely large and powerful, often in a way that is unnatural or frightening.

Examples

There was the slate roof of the church; ivy climbed the red brick walls like a green scaled monster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a very large, strong, or powerful person or thing
  • Antonym: dwarf - a person or thing of unusually small size or power

Etymology

From Latin monstrum (portent, abnormal shape, monster), related to monstrare (to show).
