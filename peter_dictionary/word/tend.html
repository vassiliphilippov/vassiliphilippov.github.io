tend

1. VERB

/ tɛnd / (AmE), / tɛnd / (BrE)
to regularly or usually behave in a particular way, or to be likely to happen in a particular situation

Examples

It is not as convenient as the old type toothbrush and the paste tends to shimmy of the bristles.
However, even if the latent demand for demythologization is not nearly as widespread as we are claiming, at least among the cultured elements of the population there tends to be an almost complete indifference to the church and its traditional message of sin and grace.
Schnabel's emphasis on structural clarity, his innate rhythmic vibrancy, and impetuous intensity all tend to stamp his reading as a symphonic one.
The race problem has tended to obscure other, less emotional, issues which may fundamentally be even more divisive.
In agriculture, for example, despite the advances in biology, elaborate rituals tend to persist along with a continued sense of the imminence of some natural disaster.

Synset Examples

She tends to be nervous before her lectures
These dresses run small
He inclined to corpulence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incline - to have a tendency or preference toward something
  • Synonym: be prone to - to be likely to do something or suffer from something

Etymology

From Latin *tendere* (to stretch, aim at), via Old French *tendre*.

2. VERB

/ tɛnd / (AmE), / tɛnd / (BrE)
to care for or look after someone or something; to attend to the needs of

Examples

He tended the new colts Beau had sired.
Livestock must be tended every day, routinely.
You take care of me?

Synset Examples

She tends to the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look after - to take care of someone or something
  • Synonym: care for - to provide for and watch over
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, to attend to) via Old French 'tendre' (to attend to).

3. VERB

/ tɛnd / (AmE), / tɛnd / (BrE)
to manage or look after something such as a business, shop, or land

Examples

The small half-heartedly tended fields of men who 'd spent more time rustling cattle than farming were lying fallow.

Synset Examples

tend a store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manage - to be in charge of, administer, or run (something)
  • Synonym: oversee - to supervise or watch over (something, especially work or workers)

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, extend).
