battered

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæt̬ɚd / (AmE), / ˈbætəd / (BrE)
Worn out or damaged by repeated use or rough treatment.

Examples

Straightening up, his eyes ablaze, he held out the battered Stetson.

Synset Examples

his battered old hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worn - showing signs of long use; no longer fresh or new
  • Antonym: new - recently made, bought, or used; not damaged

Etymology

From batter (to strike repeatedly), from Old French batre, from Latin battuere (to beat).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbætərd / (AmE), / ˈbætəd / (BrE)
Damaged by repeated blows or rough use; worn out and shabby from long or hard use.

Examples

"By the way ", said Mr. Blatz, packing his tools into a battered carrier, "them power tools needs extra voltage.

Synset Examples

a battered old car
the beaten-up old Ford

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaten-up - damaged or in poor condition, especially from rough treatment or long use
  • Antonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled, not damaged

Etymology

From Middle English 'batre' (to beat), from Old French 'batre', from Latin 'battuere' (to beat).
