cross-legged

1. ADV

/ ˈkrɔsˌlɛgd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌlɛgd / (BrE)
With the legs crossed, especially sitting with the knees bent and the feet tucked close to the body.

Examples

I have more than once sat cross-legged in the grass through a long summer morning and watched without touching while a poppy bud higher than my head slowly but visibly pushed off its cap, unfolded, and shook out like a banner in the sun its flaming vermilion petals.

Synset Examples

he sat on the floor cross-legged and meditated

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'legged', referring to the position of the legs.
