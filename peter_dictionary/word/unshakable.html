unshakable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃeɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃeɪkəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be changed, weakened, or made to doubt; extremely firm in purpose, belief, or loyalty

Examples

He is a Buddhist, which means that to him peace and the sanctity of human life are not only religious dogma, but a profound and unshakable Weltanschauung.

Synset Examples

firm convictions
a firm mouth
steadfast resolve
a man of unbendable perseverence
unwavering loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • Antonym: wavering - becoming weaker or undecided

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'shakable' (able to be shaken).
