calculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈkalkjʊləs / (BrE)
A hard mass formed by the accumulation of mineral salts, usually found in organs or ducts of the body, such as the kidneys or gallbladder.

Examples

The gallbladder contained about 40 cc. of green brown bile and 3 smooth, dark-green calculi measuring up to 1 cm. in diameter.

Synset Examples

renal calculi can be very painful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - a hard mineral mass or concretion that forms in the body

Etymology

From Latin *calculus* (small stone, pebble), diminutive of *calx* (limestone).
