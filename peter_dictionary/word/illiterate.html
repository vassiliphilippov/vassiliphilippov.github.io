illiterate

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪt̬ərət / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
Unable to read or write.

Examples

Though it may exist in either literate or illiterate societies, it assumes a role of true cultural importance only in the latter.
One would need to test this proposition carefully; after all, the large (and probably unreliable) Reader's Digest literature on the "most unforgettable character I ever met "deals with village grocers, country doctors, favorite if illiterate aunts, and so forth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literate - able to read and write

Etymology

From Latin 'illiteratus' meaning 'unlearned, ignorant,' from 'in-' (not) + 'literatus' (lettered, educated).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪt̬.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪt.ər.ət / (BrE)
Lacking knowledge or education in a particular subject or art; uneducated in the fundamentals of a specific field.

Examples

A Mississippi soldier wrote: "Our General Reub Davis is a vain, stuck-up, illiterate ass ".
These differences in turn result from the fact that my Yokuts vocabularies were built up of terms selected mainly to insure unambiguity of English meaning between illiterate informants and myself, within a compact and uniform territorial area, but that Hoijer's vocabulary is based on Swadesh's second glottochronological list which aims at eliminating all items which might be culturally or geographically determined.

Synset Examples

she is ignorant of quantum mechanics
he is musically illiterate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or in a specific field

Etymology

From Latin 'illiteratus' (unlearned, ignorant), from 'in-' (not) + 'litteratus' (lettered).
