unsolder

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈsɑdɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɒdə / (BrE)
to remove solder from (a joint or connection), usually to separate components that have been joined by soldering

Examples

Now unsolder and disassemble the frame except for the two 12 "and the first two 3 - 3 4 "bars ("A "and "B "pieces), which are soldered together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solder - to join or fuse with solder

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'reverse') to 'solder'. 'Solder' from Old French *soudure*, from Latin *solidare* (to make solid).
