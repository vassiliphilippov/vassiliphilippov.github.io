implant

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to insert or fix (something) securely and deeply, especially in a part of the body or in the mind

Examples

Already firmly implanted internally, it is a growing factor in external matters.

Synset Examples

He planted a knee in the back of his opponent
The dentist implanted a tooth in the gum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - to put something into something else
  • Antonym: remove - to take away or get rid of

Etymology

From Latin implantare, from in- (into) + plantare (to plant).
