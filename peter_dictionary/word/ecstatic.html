ecstatic

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈstætɪk / (BrE)
Feeling or expressing overwhelming happiness or joyful excitement.

Examples

The clapping they made on the concrete interrupted him in the ecstatic pleasure he knew, so that he quickly released his hold on the goat and pretended to be examining its haunches for ticks.
At its most ecstatic moments, husband and wife are elevated far above worldly cares.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delighted - feeling or showing great pleasure
  • Synonym: thrilled - extremely pleased or excited
  • Synonym: overjoyed - extremely happy
  • Antonym: miserable - very unhappy or uncomfortable

Etymology

From Greek ekstatikos (entranced), from ekstasis (ecstasy).
