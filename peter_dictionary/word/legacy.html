legacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛɡəsi / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡəsi / (BrE)
A gift of money or property left to someone in a will; a bequest.

Examples

The right to leave legacies should be substantially reduced and ultimately eliminated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bequest - a gift of property or money left to someone in a will
  • Synonym: inheritance - assets received from someone after their death

Etymology

From Latin 'legatum' (bequest), via Medieval Latin 'legacia' and Old French 'legacy'.
