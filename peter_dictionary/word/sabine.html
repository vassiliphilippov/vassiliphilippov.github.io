Sabine

1. PROPN

/ səˈbiːn / (AmE), / səˈbiːn / (BrE)
A river in eastern Texas that flows south into the Gulf of Mexico, forming part of the border between Texas and Louisiana.

Examples

They were west of the Sabine, but only God knew where.

Etymology

Named after the Spanish word *sabina* meaning ‘cypress tree’, relating to the cypress trees along the river.
