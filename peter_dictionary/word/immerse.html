immerse

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɝs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to involve or absorb oneself deeply in an activity or interest

Examples

Miss Sutherland appeared almost as another person in this scene: A much more girlish Lucia, a sensational coloratura who ran across stage while singing, and an actress immersed in her role.

Synset Examples

He immersed himself into his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engage - to occupy oneself with something
  • Synonym: absorb - to take up all one's attention; engross
  • Antonym: distract - to divert attention away; the opposite of immerse

Etymology

From Latin 'immergere', from 'in-' (into) + 'mergere' (to plunge).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmɜrs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to put someone or something completely under the surface of a liquid

Examples

Carrier * * f was added and the aqueous and organic phases were separated (cells containing gaseous reactants were immersed in liquid air before opening under sodium iodide).

Synset Examples

Immerse yourself in hot water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submerge - to put under water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'immersus', past participle of 'immergere' (to plunge in).
