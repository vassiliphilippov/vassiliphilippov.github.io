volatile

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɑːlətl / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance) evaporating readily at normal temperatures and pressures.

Examples

The average volatile suspended solids in the effluent was 75 mg / l while MLSS averaged 170 mg / l volatile suspended solids.
The average volatile suspended solids in the effluent was 75 mg / l while MLSS averaged 170 mg / l volatile suspended solids.

Synset Examples

volatile oils
volatile solvents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonvolatile - not evaporating readily at normal temperatures and pressures

Etymology

From Latin 'volatilis' (fleeting, flying), from 'volare' (to fly).
