hyperbolically

1. ADV

/ haɪ.pɚˈbɑ.lɪk.li / (AmE), / haɪ.pəˈbɒl.ɪ.kəl.i / (BrE)
In a manner involving exaggeration or overstatement.

Examples

For example, a writer in a recent number of The Queen hyperbolically states that "of the myriad imprecations the only one which the English Catholics really resent is the suggestion that they are' un English "'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggeratedly - in an exaggerated manner

Etymology

From 'hyperbolic', from Greek 'hyperbolē' (excess, exaggeration).
