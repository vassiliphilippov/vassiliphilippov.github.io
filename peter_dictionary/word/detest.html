detest

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛst / (BrE)
to dislike intensely; to feel strong hatred or antipathy for someone or something

Examples

Thus some provinces contained tribes which detested each other, and to them independence meant an opportunity for war.
This would be by far the better choice, when "it is a question of allowing the human race to survive, possibly under the domination of a regime which most of us detest, or of allowing it to destroy itself in appalling and prolonged anguish ".

Synset Examples

I hate Mexican food
She detests politicians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hate - to feel intense or passionate dislike for
  • Synonym: loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for
  • Antonym: like - to find agreeable or enjoyable
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection or strong positive feeling for

Etymology

From Latin detestari (to curse while calling a deity to witness), from de- (away) + testari (to witness).
