evasive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
Tending to avoid giving a clear or direct answer or statement; deliberately vague or ambiguous.

Examples

Above him slid the evasive shadow of a storm cloud.
Replies to requests for character reference are notorious for their evasive double-entendre.

Synset Examples

his answers were brief, constrained and evasive
an evasive statement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - straightforward and clear in communication
  • Synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning
  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined

Etymology

From Latin evasivus, from evadere (to escape).
