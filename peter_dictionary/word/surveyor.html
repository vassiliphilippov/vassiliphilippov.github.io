surveyor

1. NOUN

/ sərˈveɪər / (AmE), / səˈveɪə / (BrE)
an engineer or specialist who measures land areas and determines boundaries, elevations, and features of land or structures, often for construction, legal, or mapping purposes

Examples

"You are about to become a first-class surveyor ", Rector said.
From the town surveyor, Hans learned drawing and mathematics and, from a university student, some academic subjects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineer - a person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or structures

Etymology

From Middle English 'surveiour', from Anglo-Norman 'surveier' (to oversee).
