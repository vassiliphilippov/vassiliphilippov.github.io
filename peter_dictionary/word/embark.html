embark

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑrk / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to begin a new project, activity, or course of action, especially one that is important or demanding

Examples

To speak of this underlying view of the world is to embark upon matters of subjective judgment.

Synset Examples

she embarked upon a new career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commence - to begin or start something
  • Synonym: undertake - to take on a responsibility or task
  • Antonym: conclude - to bring to an end or finish something

Etymology

From French *embarquer*, from em- (in) + barque (ship).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɑrk / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to go on board a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle; to begin a journey or course, especially one that is significant or challenging

Examples

In contrast to this Stoic patristic view, Utopia implies that the nature of man is such that to rely on individual conscience to supply the deficiencies of municipal law is to embark on the bottomless sea of human sinfulness in a sieve.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disembark - to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle

Etymology

From French embarquer, from en- (in) + barque (boat).
