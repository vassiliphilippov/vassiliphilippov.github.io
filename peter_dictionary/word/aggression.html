aggression

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a feeling or tendency to behave in a hostile or forceful way, often with the intention of attacking or confronting others

Examples

Although in both emotions sympathetic symptoms are present, different autonomic somatic patterns underlie aggression and anxiety, respectively, as indicated by the rate of the excretion of the catecholamines, the state of the muscle tone, and the Mecholyl test.
Prosperity is a worthy objective; but if providing higher standards of living gets in the way of producing sufficient guns to resist Communist aggression, then material sacrifices and denials will have to be made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic attitude or behavior
  • Antonym: peacefulness - the absence of hostility or aggression

Etymology

From Latin aggressio (an attack), from aggredi (to approach, attack).

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
violent or hostile action, often without provocation, intended to dominate or harm others; an act of attack or invasion

Examples

After this holocaust, a changing world occupied the minds of men; a world beset with new boundaries, new treaties and governments, new goals and methods, and the age-old fears of aggression and subjugation - hunger and exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent act against a person or place
  • Antonym: defense - the act of protecting against attack

Etymology

From Latin 'aggressio', from 'aggredi' meaning 'to attack'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
A feeling of anger or hostility that may lead to thoughts of attacking or harming others.

Examples

Landesco thought him "just a superior sort of plugugly "but he was, in fact, with his aggression and hostility, and nerveless indifference to risking or administering pain, a casebook psychopath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly feeling or behavior
  • Antonym: peacefulness - state or feeling of peace or calm

Etymology

From Latin aggressio (attack, approach).
