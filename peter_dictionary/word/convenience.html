convenience

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvinjəns / (AmE), / kənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
the quality of being useful, easy, or suitable for a particular purpose

Examples

"At three o'clock if it will be of convenience to you at that time ".

Synset Examples

they offered the convenience of an installment plan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconvenience - the quality of being troublesome or difficult

Etymology

From Latin convenientia (agreement, suitability), from convenire (to come together, be suitable).
