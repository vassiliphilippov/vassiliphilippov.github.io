lush

1. ADJ

/ lʌʃ / (AmE), / lʌʃ / (BrE)
(of vegetation, gardens, or growth) growing thickly and in great abundance; rich and profuse.

Examples

Gazing at her husband's drugged body, his chest rising and falling in mindless rhythms, she saw the grandeur of his fictional world, that lush garden from which he plucked flowers and herbs.

Synset Examples

their riotous blooming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxuriant - (of plants) growing thickly, very lush and healthy
  • Antonym: barren - (of land) too poor to produce much or any vegetation, not lush
