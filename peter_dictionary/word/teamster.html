teamster

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːm.stər / (AmE), / ˈtiːm.stə / (BrE)
a person who drives a team of draft animals, especially horses, used for hauling goods

Examples

I found a trooper once the Apache had spread-eagled on an ant hill, and another time we ran across some teamsters they 'd caught, tied upside down on their own wagon wheels over little fires until their brains was exploded right out o' their skulls.
There were no less than six or seven saloons in Ganado, not counting the lower class dives, all vying for the trade of celebrating miners and teamsters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagoner - driver of a wagon, especially in historical contexts

Etymology

From 'team' (a group of draft animals) + agent suffix '-ster', originally Middle English.
