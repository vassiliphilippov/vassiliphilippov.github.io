qualm

1. NOUN

/ kwɑm / (AmE), / kwɑːm / (BrE)
a feeling of doubt or uneasiness about whether one is doing the right thing

Examples

Even Hudson, experienced in Arctic sailing and determined as he was, must have had qualms as he slid down the Thames.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scruple - a feeling of doubt or hesitation regarding the morality of an action

Etymology

From Old English cwealm (pain, death, suffering), though the modern sense developed later.
