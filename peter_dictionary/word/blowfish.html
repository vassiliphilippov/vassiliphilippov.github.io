blowfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of fish that can inflate itself and contains a deadly poison, especially in its ovaries and liver; considered a delicacy in some cultures despite the risk.

Examples

She glinted suspiciously at the dish before her: "Blowfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pufferfish - another name for blowfish; a fish that inflates itself when threatened
