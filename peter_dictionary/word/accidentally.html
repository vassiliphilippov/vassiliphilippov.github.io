accidentally

1. ADV

/ ˌæksɪˈdɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌæksɪˈdɛntəli / (BrE)
In a way that happens by chance or without intention; without advance planning.

Examples

He had not even thought about her much except once or twice at night in bed when his slowly ranging thoughts would abruptly, almost accidentally, encounter her.

Synset Examples

they met accidentally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intentionally - in a way that is planned or deliberate
  • Antonym: deliberately - on purpose, in a planned way

Etymology

From accident + -ally, modeled on the pattern of adverbs formed from adjectives ending in -al.
