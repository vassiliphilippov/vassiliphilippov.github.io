glasses

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæsɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsɪz / (BrE)
A pair of lenses set in a frame resting on the nose and ears, used to correct or aid defective eyesight.

Examples

The door was answered by a slender man in his sixties - straight backed, somewhat clerical in manner, wearing rimless glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectacles - another word for glasses (lenses to correct eyesight)
  • Synonym: contact lenses - thin lenses placed directly on the eyes to correct vision

Etymology

Plural of 'glass', in reference to the use of glass lenses in the frames.
