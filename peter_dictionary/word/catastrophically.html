catastrophically

1. ADV

/ kəˌtæstrəˈfɪkli / (AmE), / kəˌtæstrəˈfɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that causes or involves catastrophe or disaster; with disastrous or ruinous consequences.

Examples

This movement of industry away from the central cities is not so catastrophically new as some prophets seem to believe.
Each failed catastrophically in an invasion of Russia and each brought ruin on the country that worshipped him.

Synset Examples

catastrophically complex

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrously - in a disastrous manner; causing disaster

Etymology

From 'catastrophic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'katastrophē' (overturning, disaster).
