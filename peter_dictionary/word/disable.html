disable

1. VERB

/ dɪˈseɪbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪbəl / (BrE)
to make unable to function, act, or perform a certain action; to render inoperative or incapable

Examples

The jurors said they realize "a proportionate distribution of these funds might disable this program in our less populous counties ".

Synset Examples

disable this command on your computer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enable - to make able to do something; to make possible

Etymology

From Old French *desabler* (to disable), from Latin *dis-* (away, opposite) + *habilis* (able).
