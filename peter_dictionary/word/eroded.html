eroded

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈroʊdɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdɪd / (BrE)
worn away by the action of water, wind, ice, or other natural forces

Examples

Beyond the forest all our paths merge into a single great highway which ends in the barren, eroded hills of Judea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathered - worn or changed by exposure to the weather; used especially of rocks and surfaces

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'erode', from Latin *erodere* 'to gnaw away'.
