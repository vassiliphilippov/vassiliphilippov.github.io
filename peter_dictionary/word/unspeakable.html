unspeakable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspiːkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈspiːkəbəl / (BrE)
Too extreme, bad, or great to be expressed or described in words.

Examples

We all seek the same thing through different ways - an end to this long night of two thousand years of darkness and unspeakable abuses which will continue to plague us until the Star of David flies over Zion ".

Synset Examples

indefinable yearnings
indescribable beauty
ineffable ecstasy
inexpressible anguish
unspeakable happiness
unutterable contempt
a thing of untellable splendor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe; too unusual or extreme for words
  • Synonym: inexpressible - impossible to express in words
  • Synonym: ineffable - too great or extreme to be expressed in words
  • Antonym: utterable - able to be expressed in words

Etymology

From un- (not) + speak + -able, meaning 'not able to be spoken.'

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspiːkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈspiːkəbl̩ / (BrE)
So bad, shocking, or extreme as to be indescribable; exceptionally bad or appalling.

Examples

Do patriots everywhere know enough about how the persecution of the Jews in Germany and later in the occupied countries contributed to terrorizing the populations, splitting apart individuals and groups, arousing the meanest and most dishonest impulses, pulverizing trust and personal dignity, and finally forcing people to follow their masters into the abyss by making them partners in unspeakable crimes?

Synset Examples

atrocious taste
abominable workmanship
an awful voice
dreadful manners
a painful performance
terrible handwriting
an unspeakable odor came sweeping into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - exceptionally bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad or serious
  • Antonym: wonderful - extremely good; inspiring delight

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'speakable' (able to be spoken).
