interjection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
a word or short phrase, often expressing strong emotion, inserted into speech or writing as an exclamation, such as 'oh', 'wow', or 'ouch'

Examples

REEVES: Oh, I don'’t know.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclamation - a sudden cry or remark expressing surprise, anger, or pain

Etymology

From Latin 'interjectio', from 'intericere' meaning 'to throw between'.
