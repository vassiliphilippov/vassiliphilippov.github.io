tilth

1. NOUN

/ tɪlθ / (AmE), / tɪlθ / (BrE)
The physical condition of soil in relation to its suitability for planting or growing crops, particularly its texture and structure after tillage.

Examples

I fingered it and had the feeling of adequacy that comes with the right texture, tilth and body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil structure - the arrangement of soil particles

Etymology

From Old English 'tilth' (act of tilling), from 'tilian' (to till, cultivate).
