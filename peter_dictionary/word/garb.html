garb

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑrb / (AmE), / ɡɑːb / (BrE)
Clothing of a particular style, especially worn for a specific purpose or on a special occasion.

Examples

Since the Protestant clergy for the most part wear gray or some variant from the wholly black suit, my Roman collar and black garb usually identify me in England as a Roman Catholic cleric.

Synset Examples

formal attire
battle dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attire - clothes, especially fine or formal ones
  • Synonym: costume - a set of clothes for a particular occasion, purpose, or period
  • Synonym: clothing - items worn to cover the body

Etymology

From Italian 'garbo' (grace, elegance) or French 'garbe'; ultimately from Old High German 'warb' (dress, manner).
