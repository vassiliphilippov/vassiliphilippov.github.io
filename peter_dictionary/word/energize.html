energize

1. VERB

/ ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒʌɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something feel full of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

At the same time that religion exercises a conserving influence, it also energizes and motivates both individuals and groups.

Synset Examples

Coffee and tea stimulate me
This herbal infusion doesn't stimulate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulate - to encourage activity or increase energy in someone or something
  • Synonym: invigorate - to make someone feel healthier, stronger, or more energetic
  • Antonym: drain - to make someone feel very tired or lacking energy

Etymology

From Greek 'energein' (to work, operate), via French 'énergiser' and English 'energy'.
