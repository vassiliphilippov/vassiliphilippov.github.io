muffled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈmʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a sound) not loud because of being obstructed or muted; made softer or less distinct.

Examples

The sound of his footsteps was like a muffled drum.
Far off, in the dusk, he heard voices singing, muffled but strong.
The soldier answered in a curious, muffled voice, his lips barely moving.
The soldier's voice was muffled again, stricken with chagrin.
"We were on our vacation in Canada ", Howard explained, in a muffled voice that must have been used to booming, "and the news did n't catch up with us till we were nearly home.

Synset Examples

the dull boom of distant breaking waves
muffled drums
the muffled noises of the street
muted trumpets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muted - of sound made softer or quieter; not loud
  • Synonym: dull - of a sound lacking sharpness, resonance, or clarity
  • Antonym: ringing - (of sound) clear and resonant; strong

Etymology

From Middle English 'muffle' (to wrap so as to cover or make quiet), probably from Old French 'moufler'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈmʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) wrapped up, especially to protect from cold or to obscure identity.

Examples

As he neared the square, a round figure muffled in a long, black cape whisked by.

Synset Examples

children muffled almost to the eyebrows

Etymology

Past participle of 'muffle', from Middle English 'moflen' (to wrap up, cover).
