ohmic

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.mɪk / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, measured in, or having characteristics of ohms, especially in accordance with Ohm's law.

Examples

This is used as a reference for comparing the ohmic heating and the electrical energy obtained from the measured current through the element and the measured voltage across the element.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ohm - the SI unit of electrical resistance

Etymology

From ohm (the unit of electrical resistance) + -ic.
