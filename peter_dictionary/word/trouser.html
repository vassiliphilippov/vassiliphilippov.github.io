trouser

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraʊzɚ / (AmE), / ˈtraʊzə / (BrE)
A piece of clothing that covers the body from the waist to the knees or ankles, with a separate part for each leg; usually worn in pairs.

Examples

He carried three guns - one in the right trouser pocket, one under his left armpit, one in the left outside coat pocket - and was equally lethal with both hands.
Even before his mind had rounded out the idea, he thrust one hand into his trousers pocket and pulled out the six slugs he had taken from the revolver.
- I heard he had you shitting in your pants.

Synset Examples

he had a sharp crease in his trousers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pants - another word for trousers, especially in American English
  • Degree Lower: shorts - short trousers ending above the knee

Etymology

From earlier 'trouse', from Scottish Gaelic 'triubhas' (close-fitting shorts or breeches).
