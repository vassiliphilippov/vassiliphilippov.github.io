knead

1. VERB

/ niːd / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
to work and press a soft substance, such as dough or clay, with the hands, especially to make it uniform or smooth

Examples

Knead on lightly floured board and roll out to form a * * f-inch rectangle.

Synset Examples

knead dough
work the clay until it is soft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work - to shape or form something by repeated movement or action

Etymology

From Old English *cnedan*, related to Dutch *kneden* and German *kneten*.
