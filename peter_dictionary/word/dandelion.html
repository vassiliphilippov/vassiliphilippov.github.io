dandelion

1. NOUN

/ ˈdændɪˌlaɪən / (AmE), / ˈdændɪlʌɪən / (BrE)
A common wild plant of the genus Taraxacum, with deeply notched leaves, bright yellow flowers that turn into fluffy seed heads, and a long taproot.

Examples

The covers slid down his skinny neck so I saw his head, fuzzed like a dandelion gone to seed, but his face was turned to the wall - there was the pale shadow of his nose on the plaster - and I thought, Well you do n't look much like a pig-drunk bully now.

Etymology

From Old French 'dent de lion' (lion's tooth), referring to the jagged shape of the leaves.
