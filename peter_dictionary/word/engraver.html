engraver

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡreɪvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪvə / (BrE)
A person who carves or etches designs, letters, or images onto a surface, especially as a skilled profession.

Examples

His father, a professional engraver and an amateur landscape painter, took his sons on numerous hunting expeditions, and imparted to them his knowledge and love of nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etcher - a person who engraves designs using acid or a similar agent
  • Synonym: sculptor - an artist who carves figures or designs, especially from stone or wood

Etymology

From 'engrave' + '-er', formed from Middle English 'grave' (to carve) and Old French 'engraver'.
