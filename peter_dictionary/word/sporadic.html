sporadic

1. ADJ

/ spəˈrædɪk / (AmE), / spəˈrædɪk / (BrE)
Occurring at irregular or infrequent intervals; scattered or unpredictable in time or place.

Examples

This sporadic type of planetary radiation is discussed by Burke (chap. 13) and Gallet (chap. 14).
Eventually such incidents became more sporadic, and more sharply demarcated from her day-after-day behavior, and in one particular session, after several minutes of such behavior - which, as usual, went on without any accompanying words from her - she asked, eagerly, "Did you see Granny "?
More than anything, it is the therapist's intuitive sensing of these latent meanings in the stereotype which helps these meanings to become revealed, something like a spread-out deck of cards, on sporadic occasions over the passage of the patient's and his months of work together.

Synset Examples

a city subjected to sporadic bombing raids

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frequent - Happening often or at short intervals
  • Degree Lower: occasional - Occurring from time to time, less frequent than regular

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sporadicus', from Greek 'sporadikos' (scattered).
