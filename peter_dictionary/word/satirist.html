satirist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæ.t̬əˌrɪst / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪ.rɪst / (BrE)
A writer, artist, or humorist who uses ridicule, irony, or sarcasm to expose or criticize people's stupidity or vices.

Examples

He was a propagandist - they were n't afraid of the word, then - satirist, nature lover, philosopher, scientist, what you will, a member of every party and of no party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize or expose

Etymology

From French *satiriste*, from Latin *satira* (satire).
