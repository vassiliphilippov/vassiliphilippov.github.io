attendant

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛndənt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to look after, serve, or help others, especially in a particular place or situation

Examples

In the railway station at Berlin, a uniformed attendant was chanting,' Foreigners this way!
If you use parking attendants, can they be replaced by automatic parking gates?
Hotel attendants pulled parked bicycles into the lobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person employed to do domestic duties for others
  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps or supports someone
  • Antonym: customer - the person being served, opposite role in service situations

Etymology

From Old French 'atendant', present participle of 'atendre' (to attend), from Latin 'attendere'.
