greedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡridi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːdi / (BrE)
Having an excessive or insatiable desire for wealth, possessions, or food.

Examples

From what I was able to gauge in a swift, greedy glance, the figure inside the coral colored boucle dress was stupefying.

Synset Examples

they are avaricious and will do anything for money
casting covetous eyes on his neighbor's fields
a grasping old miser
grasping commercialism
greedy for money and power
grew richer and greedier
prehensile employers stingy with raises for their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avaricious - having an extreme desire for wealth
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or share; not selfish

Etymology

From Old English grēdig (hungry, eager), related to 'greed'.
