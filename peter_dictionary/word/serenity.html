serenity

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɛnɪti / (AmE), / səˈrɛnɪti / (BrE)
A state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled; the absence of mental stress or anxiety.

Examples

Throughout the work of the later ninth century a calm, severe serenity displays itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquillity - a state of calm and peace
  • Antonym: stress - state of mental or emotional strain

Etymology

From Latin *serenitas* (clearness, calmness), from *serenus* (clear, calm).

2. NOUN

/ səˈrɛnəti / (AmE), / sɪˈrɛnɪti / (BrE)
The state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled; a calm and unruffled disposition.

Examples

A third idea is that artistic literature serves to reduce emotional conflicts, giving a sense of serenity and calm to individuals.
Agnese had been sitting quietly, listening with the serenity of the unaware.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquility - state of calm and quiet
  • Antonym: agitation - state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From Latin serenitas (calmness, clearness), from serenus (clear, calm).
