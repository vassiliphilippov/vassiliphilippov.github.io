nonfunctional

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈfʌŋkʃənl / (AmE), / nɒnˈfʌŋkʃənl / (BrE)
Not working or serving any useful purpose; not performing its intended function.

Examples

Nakamura (' 58), Verloop (' 48), Marchand, Gilroy and Watson (' 50), von Hayek (' 53), and Tobin (' 52) have all claimed their normal but relatively nonfunctional existence in the human being.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: functional - working; serving a useful function

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'functional'.
