conscientious

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnʃiˈenʃəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒnʃiˈenʃəs / (BrE)
Wishing to do one's work or duty well and thoroughly; taking care to do things correctly and carefully.

Examples

A conscientious girl became the secretary of a doctor.
"Patrolman Accacia is an alert and conscientious law-enforcement officer.

Synset Examples

conscientious application to the work at hand
painstaking research
scrupulous attention to details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careful - making sure to avoid mistakes or accidents; paying close attention
  • Synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Antonym: negligent - failing to take proper care in doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'conscientiosus', from 'conscientia' (knowledge within, conscience).
