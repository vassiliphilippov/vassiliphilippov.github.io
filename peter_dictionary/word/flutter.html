flutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə / (BrE)
a quick, light movement back and forth or up and down, especially repeated or trembling slightly

Examples

From above one could only occasionally catch a glimpse of life on the floor of this green sea: a neighbor's gingham skirt flashing into sight for an instant on the path beneath her grape-arbor, or the movement of hands above a clothesline and the flutter of garments hung there, half-way down the block.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flap - a quick, light movement up and down or back and forth
  • Synonym: wave - a movement back and forth or up and down, especially in a smooth, regular manner

Etymology

From Middle English floteren, from Old English floterian (to flutter, float about).

2. VERB

/ ˈflʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə / (BrE)
to move quickly and lightly, often with a trembling or wavering motion

Examples

Lester's hand fluttered to Cabot's shoulder.

Synset Examples

The hummingbird flitted among the branches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flit - to move lightly and swiftly from place to place
  • Synonym: dart - to move suddenly and quickly in a particular direction

Etymology

From Old English 'floterian' (to flutter, float), frequentative of 'float'.
