daring

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeərɪŋ / (BrE)
willingness to take risks or face danger, especially courage shown in challenging or dangerous situations.

Examples

In the field of entertainment there is no spur to financial daring so effective as audience boredom, and the first decade of the new device was not over before audiences began staying away in large numbers from the simple-minded, one minute shows.

Synset Examples

the proposal required great boldness
the plan required great hardiness of heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks or act innovatively
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness or fearfulness

Etymology

From Old English *diernan* or *dearr* (to dare) with the suffix -ing.
