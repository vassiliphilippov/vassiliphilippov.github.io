sodium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A soft, silvery-white metallic element of the alkali metal group, symbol Na, that reacts vigorously with water and is commonly found in sea water and rock salt.

Examples

These widely advertised products, which are used primarily for washing clothes, are based on high-sudsing, synthetic organic actives (sodium alkylbenzenesulfonates) and contain up to 50 % by weight of sodium tripolyphosphate or a mixture of sodium tripolyphosphate and tetrasodium pyrophosphate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium - a similar alkali metal element (K)
  • Synonym: salt - a compound of sodium and chlorine (NaCl)

Etymology

From New Latin *sodium*, coined from soda + -ium, influenced by Latin and Arabic sources.
