dodging

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of avoiding something unpleasant or a duty, especially by deceit or trickery.

Examples

The influence of Mass was less pervasive than that of the congested, slum tenements among the bawdy houses, honkytonks, and sawdust saloons of his birthplace; he ran wild with the child gangs of the neighbourhood, and went through the normal pressure-cooker course of thieving, police dodging, and housebreaking.

Synset Examples

his evasion of his clear duty was reprehensible
that escape from the consequences is possible but unattractive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something; avoidance
  • Antonym: compliance - the act of conforming or yielding; opposite of evasion

Etymology

From 'dodge' (to avoid), of uncertain origin.
