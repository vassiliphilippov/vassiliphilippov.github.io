ally

1. NOUN

/ ˈælaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælaɪ / (BrE)
a person or organization that cooperates with or helps another in a particular activity, often for mutual benefit

Examples

Had the situation been reversed, had, for instance, England been the enemy in 1898 because of issues of concern chiefly to New England, there is little doubt that large numbers of Southerners would have happily put on their old Confederate uniforms to fight as allies of Britain.

Synset Examples

he's a good ally in fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - a person who takes part in an undertaking with another
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Latin *alligare* (to bind to).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælaɪ / (BrE)
a country that formally agrees to cooperate and support another country, especially in war or for a common purpose.

Examples

Virginia and Rachel talked to each other quietly now, as allies who are political rather than natural might in a war atmosphere.
Today Sukarno's government is heavily besieged by avowed Communists, and for all of its "neutralist "pretensions, it is a firm ally of Soviet policy.
Then suddenly we found ourselves in the middle of another fight, an irrational, an indecent, an undeclared and immoral war with our strongest (and some had thought noblest) ally.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enemy - a country that is actively opposed or hostile to another
  • Synonym: partner - a country or entity that works together with another, but not necessarily in formal alliance

Etymology

From Latin 'alligare' (to bind to).
