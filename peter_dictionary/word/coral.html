coral

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
a shade of deep pink or pinkish-orange, resembling the color of some coral reefs

Examples

From what I was able to gauge in a swift, greedy glance, the figure inside the coral colored boucle dress was stupefying.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: salmon - a pale pinkish-orange color
  • Degree Higher: vermilion - a vivid red to reddish-orange color
  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color

Etymology

From the color of red or pink coral (marine invertebrates); the word derives from Latin 'corallium', originally from Greek 'korallion'.
