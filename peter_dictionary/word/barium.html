barium

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛriəm / (AmE), / ˈbeəriəm / (BrE)
A soft, silvery-white metallic element of the alkaline earth group, used in various industrial applications and in medical imaging.

Examples

In Chicago, some time ago, Mr. H., age 27, a diabetic since he was six, stopped using insulin because he had bought a "magic spike "- a glass tube about the size of a pencil filled with barium chloride worth a small fraction of a cent - sold by the Vrilium Company of Chicago for $ 306 as a cure-all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strontium - a soft, silvery metallic element in the alkaline earth group
  • Synonym: calcium - a soft gray metal, another alkaline earth element

Etymology

From New Latin 'barium', from Greek 'barys' (heavy).
