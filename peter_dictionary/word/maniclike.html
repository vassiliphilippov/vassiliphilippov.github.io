maniclike

1. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪkˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkˌlaɪk / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of mania, especially as seen in manic-depressive illness.

Examples

Important as these differences are, they should not obscure the basic fact that by shifting the hypothalamic balance sufficiently to the parasympathetic side, we produce depressions, whereas a shift in the opposite direction causes excitatory effects and, eventually, maniclike changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manic - affected by or relating to mania
  • Antonym: depressive - related to or suffering from depression

Etymology

From 'manic' (relating to mania) + 'like' (having the characteristics of).
