bully

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
a person who habitually seeks to harm, intimidate, or intimidate those who are weaker or vulnerable

Examples

The covers slid down his skinny neck so I saw his head, fuzzed like a dandelion gone to seed, but his face was turned to the wall - there was the pale shadow of his nose on the plaster - and I thought, Well you do n't look much like a pig-drunk bully now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tormentor - a person who inflicts suffering on others
  • Synonym: persecutor - someone who harasses others persistently
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or intimidated by a bully

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch boel (lover, brother) or Middle English bulli (sweetheart), later shifting to a pejorative meaning.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
(dated, informal) Very good; excellent.

Examples

"Bully for you ", Gibby said.

Synset Examples

he did a bully job
a neat sports car
had a great time at the party
you look simply smashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great - of very high quality; excellent
  • Synonym: smashing - excellent; very impressive

Etymology

Possibly from the earlier noun 'bully', meaning 'good fellow', itself from Middle Dutch 'boele' (lover).
