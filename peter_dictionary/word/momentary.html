momentary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊməntɛri / (AmE), / ˈməʊməntəri / (BrE)
Lasting for a very short period of time; brief or fleeting.

Examples

So we may conceive the coexistence of the infinite number of universal, apparently momentary states of matter, successive one after another in consciousness, but permanent each on its own basic phase of the progressive frequencies.
This man's isolation is not merely momentary, it is permanent.

Synset Examples

a fleeting glance
fugitive hours
rapid momentaneous association of things that meet and pass
a momentary glimpse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleeting - lasting for a very short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Late Latin 'momentarius' (lasting a moment), from 'momentum' (moment).
