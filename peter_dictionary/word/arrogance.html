arrogance

1. NOUN

/ ˈærəɡəns / (AmE), / ˈærəɡəns / (BrE)
The quality of having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities and showing contempt for others; overbearing pride.

Examples

"So that the man should not have thoughts of grandeur, and become lifted up, as if he had no lord, because of the dominion that had been given to him, and the freedom, fall into sin against God his Creator, overstepping his bounds, and take up an attitude of self-conceited arrogance towards God, a law was given him by God, that he might know that he had for lord the lord of all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of not being too proud or confident about oneself or one's abilities

Etymology

From Latin 'arrogantia' (presumption), from 'arrogare' (to claim for oneself).
