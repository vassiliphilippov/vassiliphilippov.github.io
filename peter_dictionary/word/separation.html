separation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The distance or space that exists between two or more objects.

Examples

But contest definition - that dramatic muscular separation of every muscle group that seems as though it must have been carved by a sculptor's chisel - is something quite different.

Synset Examples

fragile items require separation and cushioning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an empty space or opening between two objects
  • Synonym: interval - a space or period between two objects or events
  • Antonym: connection - state of being joined or linked

Etymology

From Latin separatio (a dividing, parting), from separare (to separate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsepəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)

Examples

  • Antonym: reunion - the act of coming together again after separation

Etymology

From Latin separatio (a separation), from separare (to separate).
