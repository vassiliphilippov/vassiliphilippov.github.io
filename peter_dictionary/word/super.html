super

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuːpər / (AmE), / ˈsuːpə / (BrE)
(informal) Of the highest quality; excellent.

Examples

"By winning against Bradley, Kentucky and Notre Dame on those teams' home courts, they showed that the home court advantage can be overcome anywhere and that it doesn's take a super team to do it ".
And in its engineers' frantic attempts to achieve maximum dynamic impact and earsplitting brilliance, the recording sounds as though it had been "doctored for super high fidelity ".
My lovely caller - Joyce Holland was her name - had previously done three filmed commercials for ZING, and this evening, the fourth, a super production, had been filmed at the home of Louis Thor.

Synset Examples

an ace reporter
a crack shot
a first-rate golfer
a super party
played top-notch tennis
an athlete in tiptop condition
she is absolutely tops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellent - of the highest quality; outstanding
  • Synonym: superb - excellent; of the highest quality
  • Antonym: inferior - of lesser quality

Etymology

From Latin 'super' (above, over).

2. ADV

/ ˈsuːpər / (AmE), / ˈsuːpə / (BrE)
Extremely; to an extreme or very high degree (used as an intensifier, especially in informal speech).

Examples

Mary's super critical attitude toward Byron had nothing to do with his moral disrepute.
But what a super Herculean task it is to winnow anything of value from the mud beplastered arguments used so freely, particularly since such common use is made of cliches and stereotypes, in themselves declarations of intellectual bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

extremely cold
extremely unpleasant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree; used for emphasis
  • Synonym: very - to a high degree (used as an intensifier)
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree (opposite of extreme)

Etymology

Shortened from 'super-' meaning 'above, beyond', from Latin 'super' (above).
