derived

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈraɪvd / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪvd / (BrE)
(of something) formed or developed from something else; not original.

Examples

With completion of filling, net-like anastomoses were noted to be present between these separately derived branches.

Synset Examples

the belief that classes and organizations are secondary and derived

Related Terms

  • Antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not derived from anything else

Etymology

Past participle of 'derive', from Latin 'derivare' (to draw off, divert).
