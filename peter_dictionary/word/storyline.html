storyline

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːriˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪˌlaɪn / (BrE)
The sequence of events that make up the plot of a book, play, film, or other narrative work.

Examples

The storyline, in sort, is wildly unrealistic.
But except for a vague adherence to the basic storyline - i.e., that Ruth remained with Naomi and finally wound up with Boaz - the film version has little to do with the Bible.
He talked about unauthentic storylines too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - the main events that make up a story

Etymology

Formed from 'story' + 'line', meaning the line or thread of a narrative.
