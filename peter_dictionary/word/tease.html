tease

1. VERB

/ tiz / (AmE), / tiːz / (BrE)
to annoy or provoke someone repeatedly or persistently, especially in a playful or mocking way

Examples

William R. Stillwell, an admirable Georgian whose delightful correspondence is preserved in the Georgia Department of Archives and History, liked to tease his wife in his letters.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone, often in a hurtful way
  • Synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge someone with insulting remarks
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Old English tǣsan (to pluck, pull apart).

2. NOUN

/ tiz / (AmE), / tiːz / (BrE)
A person who habitually makes fun of others or provokes them in a playful or mocking way.

Examples

Joseph Jastrow, the younger son of the distinguished rabbi, Marcus Jastrow, was a friendly, round-faced fellow with a little mustache, whose field was psychology, and who was also a punster and a jolly tease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tormentor - a person who causes someone to suffer or feel pain
  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable

Etymology

From Middle English 'teasen', from Old English 'tǣsan' (to pluck, pull apart).

3. VERB

/ tiz / (AmE), / tiːz / (BrE)
to annoy or provoke someone persistently, often in a playful or mocking way

Examples

Otherwise, she would be baited into a tantrum - teased and provoked until she lost control of herself, and thus lost still another battle in the maddening struggle of Tom Lord Vs. Joyce Lakewood.

Synset Examples

The children teased the boy because of his stammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule
  • Synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge in an insulting manner
  • Degree Higher: torment - to cause severe distress or suffering
  • Antonym: compliment - to praise or express approval

Etymology

Old English tǣsan (to pluck, tease out wool).

4. VERB

/ tiz / (AmE), / tiːz / (BrE)
To mock or make fun of someone in a playful or lighthearted way.

Examples

You know, Mama used to tease me.

Synset Examples

the flirting man teased the young woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taunt - to mock or provoke someone with insulting remarks

Etymology

From Middle English *tesen*, from Old English *tǣsan* (to pull, pluck).
