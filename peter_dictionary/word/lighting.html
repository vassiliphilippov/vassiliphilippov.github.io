lighting

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
equipment and arrangement used to provide artificial light, especially in a theatre, film, or television production to create particular effects or to ensure visibility.

Examples

The book is by Jerome Weidman and George Abbott, music by Jerry Bock, lyrics by Sheldon Harnick, choreography by Peter Gennaro, scenery, costumes and lighting by William and Jean Eckart, musical direction by Jack Elliott, and the production was directed by Mr. Abbott.
When improvements are recommended in working conditions - such as lighting, rest rooms, eating facilities, air-conditioning - do you try to set a measure of their effectiveness on productivity?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - the act of lighting something up, especially for visibility or decoration
  • Antonym: blackout - a period when stage or theatre lights are turned off

Etymology

From the verb 'light' + -ing, meaning the act or means of producing light.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement or use of artificial lights, especially as a specialized skill in interior design, theater, or film

Examples

The interior designer, then, must first be an artist but also understand carpentry and painting and lighting and plumbing and finance.

Synset Examples

an interior decorator must understand lighting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - the provision of light to make something visible or bright

Etymology

From the verb 'light' + -ing, meaning the process or result of providing light.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
The arrangement or supply of light, especially for a space, event, or production.

Examples

Lighting.
Lighting is an important consideration.
Continuous low-level lighting may be provided in the shelter by means of a 4 - cell hot-shot battery to which is wired a 150 - milliampere flashlight type bulb.

Synset Examples

they played as long as it was light
as long as the lighting was good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - the provision of light to make something visible

Etymology

From 'light' (verb/noun) + -ing (forming nouns indicating action or result).
