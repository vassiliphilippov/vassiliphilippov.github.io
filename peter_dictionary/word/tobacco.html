tobacco

1. NOUN

/ təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
The dried leaves of the tobacco plant, prepared for smoking, chewing, or snuffing.

Examples

Al's Little Cafe was small, dark, narrow, and filled with the mingled scent of beer, tobacco smoke, and Italian cooking.
Early in 1822 he was at Fort Garry offering to bring in pork, flour, liquor and tobacco.
Fuzzy caterpillars, snails with their sensitive horns, struggling grasshoppers held by their long hind legs and commanded to "spit tobacco, spit ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cigarette - a thin cylinder of finely cut tobacco rolled in paper for smoking
  • Synonym: snuff - powdered tobacco for inhalation

Etymology

From Spanish 'tabaco', likely from Arawakan (Caribbean) 'tabako' (a kind of roll of tobacco leaves).
