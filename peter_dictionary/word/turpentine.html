turpentine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːpənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːpəntaɪn / (BrE)
a volatile, flammable liquid distilled from turpentine oleoresin, used chiefly as a paint thinner, solvent, and sometimes medicinally

Examples

By the year Kennedy was shot, Andy had transformed a storage room smelling of rat turds and turpentine into the best prison library in New England, complete with a fine selection of Hank Williams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white spirit - a petroleum-derived clear, colorless liquid used as a paint thinner

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'terebinthina', from 'terebinthus' (the terebinth tree).
