percussive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈkʌsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈkʌsɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by percussion or the striking of one object against another, especially referring to sound or music involving percussion instruments.

Examples

Some clue to the character of London's approach in these discs may be gained immediately from the fact that ten of the 12 titles include the word "percussion "or "percussive ".
The Percussive Twenties (SP 44006) stirs pleasant memories with well-known songs of that day, and Johnny Keating's Kombo gives forth with tingling jazz in Percussive Moods (SP 44005).

Synset Examples

percussive music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion - musical instruments played by striking or shaking

Etymology

From Latin 'percussio' (a striking), from 'percutere' (to strike through).
