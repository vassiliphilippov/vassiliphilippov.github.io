thermogravimetric

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɝː.moʊˌɡræ.vəˈmɪ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məʊˌɡræ.vɪˈmɪ.trɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the measurement of changes in weight as a function of changes in temperature, especially as applied in scientific analysis of materials.

Examples

Thermogravimetric analysis showed a weight loss of 1.8 % centered at 337 ` C and another weight loss of 10.8 % at 463 ` C.

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + gravimetric (relating to measurement of weight or mass).
