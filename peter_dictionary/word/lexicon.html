lexicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛksɪkən / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkən / (BrE)
The complete set of words known and used by an individual speaker or writer, or by a particular group or language community.

Examples

It also overlooks the fact that in a rational lexicon, and quite clearly in More's lexicon, the opposite of serious is not gay but frivolous, and the opposite of gay is not serious but solemn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocabulary - the body of words known to an individual or used within a particular field

Etymology

From Greek lexikon (book of words), from lexis (word, speech), from legein (to speak).
