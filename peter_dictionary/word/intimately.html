intimately

1. ADV

/ ˈɪntəmətli / (AmE), / ˈɪntɪmətli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a very close connection or association

Examples

His religiousness is intimately, or dialectically, connected with his sinfulness; the two may in fact be identical.
It now becomes evident that the denominational church is intimately involved with the economy of middle-class culture, for it serves to crystallize the social class identity of middle-class residential groupings.

Synset Examples

the two phenomena are intimately connected
the person most nearly concerned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closely - in a way that shows little distance or much connection

Etymology

From 'intimate' + '-ly'; 'intimate' from Latin 'intimatus' (make known, announce).

2. ADV

/ ˈɪn.t̬ə.mət.li / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.mət.li / (BrE)
with great or especially detailed personal knowledge; in a manner showing close familiarity

Examples

but Old Johnny knows these places like the back of his hand!

Synset Examples

we knew them well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way; with skill or expertise

Etymology

From Latin intimatus (made known, announced), past participle of intimare (to make known).
