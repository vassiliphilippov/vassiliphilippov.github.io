inoculation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɑkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɒkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of introducing a vaccine or antigenic substance into the body to produce immunity against a specific disease.

Examples

The entrance and retention of infectious particles in the alveoli amounts almost to an intra tissue inoculation.
Wound tumor virus is a leafhopper transmitted virus not easily transmissible by mechanical inoculation (Black, 1944; Brakke et al., 1954).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccination - the act of administering a vaccine to stimulate an immune response

Etymology

From Latin 'inoculatio', from 'inoculare' meaning 'to graft' or 'to implant'.
