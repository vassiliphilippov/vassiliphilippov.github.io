gymnastics

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnæstɪks / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnastɪks / (BrE)
A sport involving exercises that demonstrate strength, balance, and agility, often performed on specialized apparatus.

Examples

The Russians are all trained as dancers before they start to study gymnastics.
But why gymnastics at all?
It is more than just lack of dance training that is our problem, for just as gymnastics can learn from dance, dance has some very important things to learn from gymnastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrobatics - spectacular gymnastic feats and stunts, usually performed in entertainment

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnastikos' (fond of or skilled in bodily exercise), from 'gymnazein' (to exercise).
