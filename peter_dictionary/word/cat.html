cat

1. NOUN

/ kæt / (AmE), / kat / (BrE)
A small domesticated mammal with soft fur, a short snout, and retractable claws, kept as a pet or for catching mice; also, any member of the family Felidae, including wildcats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feline - relating to cats or the cat family
  • Antonym: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal often contrasted with cat

Etymology

From Old English *catt*, from Late Latin *cattus* (cat), probably from an Afro-Asiatic language (cf. Egyptian *catta*).
