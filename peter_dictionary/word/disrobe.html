disrobe

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈroʊb / (AmE), / dɪsˈrəʊb / (BrE)
To remove all or part of one's clothing; to get undressed.

Examples

He hesitated for a moment when Michelangelo asked him to disrobe, so Michelangelo gave him a piece of toweling to wrap around his loins, led him to the kitchen to take off his clothes.

Synset Examples

please don't undress in front of everybody!
She strips in front of strangers every night for a living

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undress - to remove one's clothes
  • Antonym: dress - to put on clothes

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'robe' (from French 'robe'), meaning 'to un-robe'.
