Kent

1. PROPN

/ kɛnt / (AmE), / kɛnt / (BrE)
a county in southeastern England, bordering the English Channel, known for its history as an Anglo-Saxon kingdom and its early colonization by the Romans

Examples

Greene was in actuality a young ruffian from Kent, who had broken with his parents in order to keep the company he preferred - pimps, panders and whores.

Etymology

From Old English Cant, from Latin Cantium, the Roman name for the area.
