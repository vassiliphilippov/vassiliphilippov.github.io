disloyalty

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
lack of loyalty or faithfulness to a person, group, or cause

Examples

The return of the Catholic hierarchy in 1850 was looked upon with indignant disapprobation and, in fact, was charged with being a gesture of disloyalty.
Hudson's reply was to accuse the mate of disloyalty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'loyalty'.
