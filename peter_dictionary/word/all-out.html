all-out

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˌɔːlˈaʊt / (BrE)
(of an action or effort) made with maximum effort or commitment; using all available resources or force.

Examples

"Spahnie does n't know how to merely go through the motions ", remarked Enos Slaughter, another all-out guy, who played rightfield that day and popped one over the clubhouse.
Our military forces must be capable of contending successfully with any contingency which may be forced upon us, from limited emergencies to all-out nuclear general war.

Synset Examples

all-out war
a full-scale campaign against nuclear power plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full-scale - as complete or thorough as possible; not limited in scope
  • Antonym: limited - restricted in extent or amount; not complete or unlimited
