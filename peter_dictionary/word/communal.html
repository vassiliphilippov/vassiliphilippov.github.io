communal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəl / (BrE)
Relating to a town, local administrative district, or community, especially as a unit of local government.

Examples

The overall effect was probably to stimulate more party activity in the communal elections than might have otherwise taken place.

Synset Examples

communal elections in several European countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: municipal - relating to a city or town, especially its governing body

Etymology

From French 'communal', from Latin 'commūnis' (common).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunəl / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəl / (BrE)
shared by, or relating to, a group of people rather than individuals; involving the collective participation or use by members of a community.

Examples

It was observed in the introductory chapter that metropolitan life had split into two trends - expanding interdependence on an impersonal basis and growing exclusiveness in local communal groupings.
Attention will be given in the next chapter to the style of association in the denominational churches; this style is characteristically an expression of the communal style of the middle classes.

Synset Examples

dipping each his bread into a communal dish of stew
a communal settlement in which all earnings and food were shared
a group effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collective - done by people acting as a group

Etymology

From Late Latin communalis, from Latin communis (common).
