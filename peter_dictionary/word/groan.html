groan

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊn / (BrE)
A low sound made in the throat to express pain, discomfort, or disapproval.

Examples

Cobb unleashed a single powerful jab that sent Gyp reeling wildly and crashing down with a whining groan.
[SCREAMING] [CURSING IN ITALIAN] [GUN FIRING] [CAR ENGINE STARTING] [WARNING] [GROANING] Michael, say goodbye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moan - a long, low sound expressing physical or mental suffering
  • Degree Lower: sigh - an audible breath expressing sadness or relief
  • Degree Higher: cry - to make a loud, high sound expressing strong emotion

Etymology

From Old English 'grānan' (to murmur, grunt).

2. VERB

/ ɡroʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊn / (BrE)
to make a low, mournful sound expressing pain, discomfort, or displeasure; to complain audibly or react with such a sound

Examples

Timothy Rourke groaned dismally as he slid under the wheel.
They lived mainly in the kitchen; they moved Maggie's bed and the baby's basket there, and the rest of them undressed by the stove and ran groaning and shivering to the upper polar regions and plunged into icy beds.
[GROANS]

Synset Examples

The students groaned when the professor got out the exam booklets
The ancient door soughed when opened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moan - make a low sound of pain or complaint

Etymology

From Old English grānian (to lament, murmur), related to groan.
