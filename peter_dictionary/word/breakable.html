breakable

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəbl / (BrE)
an item, especially a household article, that is fragile and easily broken

Examples

"Winston ", she said, "get the basket for the breakables ".

Synset Examples

pack the breakables separately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: unbreakable - not able to be broken easily

Etymology

From break + -able, originally used as an adjective, later also as a noun for objects.
