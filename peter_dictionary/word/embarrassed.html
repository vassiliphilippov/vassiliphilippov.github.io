embarrassed

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbærəst / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəst / (BrE)
Feeling awkward, self-conscious, or uncomfortable, especially in a social situation.

Examples

He drew back, embarrassed and pleased.

Synset Examples

felt abashed at the extravagant praise
chagrined at the poor sales of his book
was embarrassed by her child's tantrums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling guilt or disgrace for wrongdoing
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction from achievements

Etymology

From French *embarasser* (to perplex, make difficult), from Old Italian *imbarazzare* (to entangle, embarrass).
