draft

1. VERB

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
to prepare an outline or preliminary version of a document, plan, or speech

Examples

The bill, which Daniel said he drafted personally, would force banks, insurance firms, pipeline companies and other corporations to report such property to the state treasurer.
Council president Frank SanAntonio said yesterday he may ask the council to formally request Town Solicitor Michael A. Abatuno to draft the ordinance.
Hamilton was bent over his desk, drafting a legal paper by the light of a candle.
But though the Southern States, when drafting a constitution to unite themselves, narrowed the difference to this fine point by omitting to assert the right to secede, the fact remained that by seceding from the Union they had already acted on the concept that it was composed primarily of sovereign states.

Synset Examples

draft a speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compose - to create or put together a written work
  • Synonym: write - to produce text or a document

Etymology

From Middle English 'draught', from Old English 'dræft' (act of drawing or pulling), related to 'draw'.

2. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
a written order directing a person or bank to pay a specified sum of money to another; a bill of exchange.

Examples

By early summer, he wrote from Laramie that he was suffering from the wound inflicted in the ambush and was in a bad way financially, so Pels sent him a draft for $ 100, warning that it was still not wise for him to return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of exchange - a written order to pay a specified amount to a designated party
  • Synonym: check - a written order directing a bank to pay money

Etymology

From Middle English 'draught', related to Old English 'dragan' (to draw); meaning influenced by the idea of drawing money.

3. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
A preliminary sketch or outline of a design, picture, or plan, typically made as the first version before the final work.

Examples

When this linear draft is completed, I dust it down to a faint image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a rough or unfinished drawing or painting
  • Synonym: outline - a line or set of lines indicating the shape of an object or figure

Etymology

From Middle English *draught* (drawing, pull), from Old English *dræht*, *dræht* (drawing, pull), from draw.

4. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
A current of air, especially one that moves through a room, chimney, or vehicle, often causing discomfort.

Examples

Sarah had begun to tell Lucien of Emile, she had begun to question and a little draft had crept across the room from the bedroom door, open barely enough to show a rim of blackness in the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draught - British spelling of 'draft' in this sense (air current)

Etymology

From Old English *dræft* (act of drawing or pulling); related to 'draw'.

5. VERB

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
to select or order someone to join the armed forces, especially by compulsory enlistment.

Examples

Drafted into the Austrian army, he rebelliously rejected discipline, wangled a Vienna billet, went on painting.
In 1954 I was drafted and after serving two years honorably on Active Duty I was not required to participate in any further Army Reserve activities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enlist - to voluntarily join the armed forces

Etymology

From Middle English 'draught', from Old English 'dræht' or 'dræft', meaning 'act of pulling'. Extended to the military sense in American English in the 19th century.
