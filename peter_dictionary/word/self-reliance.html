self-reliance

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf rɪˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / sɛlf rɪˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
the ability to rely on one's own powers and resources rather than those of others; personal independence

Examples

By virtue of his self-reliance, his individualism and his freedom from external restraint, the private eye is a perfect embodiment of the middle class conception of liberty, which amounts to doing what you please and let the devil take the hindmost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - freedom from reliance on or control by others
  • Antonym: dependence - reliance on or being controlled by someone or something else

Etymology

From 'self' + 'reliance', meaning reliance on oneself, first attested in early 19th century.
