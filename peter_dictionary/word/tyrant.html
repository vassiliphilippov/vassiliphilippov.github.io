tyrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt / (BrE)
A ruler who exercises power in a harsh, cruel, and oppressive way.

Examples

From being a hated tyrant and madman he was now the symbol of all that was noblest and best in the history of Sweden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despot - a ruler with absolute power, typically one who exercises it in a cruel or oppressive way
  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power, especially one who has obtained it by force
  • Antonym: benevolent ruler - a ruler who governs kindly and justly

Etymology

From Latin tyrannus, from Ancient Greek tyrannos (absolute ruler).
