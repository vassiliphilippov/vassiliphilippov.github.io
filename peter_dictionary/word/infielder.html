infielder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌfiːldər / (AmE), / ˈɪnfiːldə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a player who occupies a position in the infield, such as first baseman, second baseman, shortstop, or third baseman.

Examples

Chuck Hinton tripled to the rightfield corner, Cliff Cook and Dan Pavletich singled and Gaines' infielder roller accounted for the tallies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outfielder - player who occupies a position in the outfield (baseball)

Etymology

From 'infield' + '-er', denoting a person associated with the infield.
