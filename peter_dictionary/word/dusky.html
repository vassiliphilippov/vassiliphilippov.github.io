dusky

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌski / (AmE), / ˈdʌski / (BrE)
Dim, shadowy, or partially dark; illuminated as if by twilight.

Examples

a lighted lamp stood on the table that dusky, drizzling day.

Synset Examples

The dusky night rides down the sky/And ushers in the morn
the twilight glow of the sky
a boat on a twilit river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilit - dimly lit as if by twilight
  • Synonym: gloomy - partially or totally dark; dismal
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting a lot of light

Etymology

From dusk + -y; 'dusk' from Middle English, perhaps from Old Norse 'dǫskva' (to grow dark).
