bitter

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə / (BrE)
Feeling or showing strong resentment, anger, or cynicism, often as a result of pain or disappointment.

Examples

In all the bitter in-fighting, the squabbles over election procedures, the complicated numbers game that East and West played on the assistant secretaries' theme, the gentleman from Burma showed himself both as a man of principle and a skilled diplomat.
Mr. A has recovered, but he is, justifiably, a bitter man.
He was bitter when he missed.
"I fear explanations explanatory of things explained ", he said, leaving the biter bit - and bitter.
Possibly, the coconut containing dessert had brought up bitter problems of administration.

Synset Examples

an acrimonious dispute
bitter about the divorce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentful - feeling or expressing bitterness or indignation
  • Synonym: acrimonious - sharp or harsh in language or tone, especially in disputes
  • Antonym: forgiving - ready to forgive; not bitter

Etymology

From Old English *bitter* (sharp, biting), related to biting or sharpness, later applied figuratively to emotions.

5. ADJ

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə / (BrE)
Characterized by strong hostility, animosity, or resentment; involving intense conflict or opposition.

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology
