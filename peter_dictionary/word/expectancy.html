expectancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛk.tən.si / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛk.tən.si / (BrE)
the state or quality of being expected, or something that is expected to happen, especially on the basis of statistical norms or probability.

Examples

As stated earlier in the paper, Onsets and Completions - particularly the former - provide a different tool or indicator of expectancy in osseous development, each within a limited age period.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin expectantia (expectation, waiting for).
