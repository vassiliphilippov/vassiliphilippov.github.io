substitution

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of replacing one person or thing with another.

Examples

But, in denying any physical reality for this dream, how could the brain possibly compose that realistic, vividly visual continuity uninterrupted by misty fadeout, violent break or sudden substitution?

Synset Examples

the replacement of lost blood by a transfusion of donor blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - the act of replacing one thing with another
  • Synonym: exchange - an act of giving one thing and receiving another in return

Etymology

From Latin substitutio (a putting in place of another).
