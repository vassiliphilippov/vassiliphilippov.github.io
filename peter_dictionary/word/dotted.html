dotted

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒtɪd / (BrE)
marked with dots or gaps; consisting of a series of small spots or spaces rather than a continuous line

Examples

Thus, the dotted line shown in figure 4 was taken as typical for the temperature distribution for all blowing rates.

Synset Examples

sign on the dotted line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dashed - marked with dashes or short lines, rather than dots

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒtɪd / (BrE)
Having a pattern or design of small round spots or dots.

Examples

Yet if he were not there, they would have missed him, as they would have missed the sounds of bees buzzing against the screen door in early June; or the smell of thick tomato paste - the ripe smell that was both sweet and sour - rising up from aluminum trays wrapped in fly dotted cheesecloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - having small marks or patches; covered with spots

Etymology

From the verb 'dot' (to mark with a dot) + adjectival suffix '-ed'.
