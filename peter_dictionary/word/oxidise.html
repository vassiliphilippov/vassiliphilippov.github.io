oxidise

1. VERB

/ ˈɑksɪˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to add oxygen to a substance or to combine with oxygen, especially in a chemical reaction

Examples

It is assumed that the iodine released from the iodotyrosines remains in the iodide pool of the thyroid, where it is oxidised and re-incorporated into thyroglobulin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxidize - add oxygen to or combine with oxygen (American spelling)
  • Antonym: reduce - remove oxygen or add electrons in a chemical reaction (antonym, chemistry context)

Etymology

From French *oxyder*, from oxyd(e) 'oxide'.
