gee

1. NOUN

/ dʒi / (AmE), / dʒi / (BrE)
a unit of acceleration equal to the acceleration produced by gravity at the Earth's surface, approximately 9.8 meters per second squared, used to express force or acceleration experienced by a body

Examples

The Gabriel pointed towards her destination and, under one gee acceleration, began to build up towards her ultimate velocity, 99.1 percent of the speed of light.
Then, after slowing the vessel considerably, the drive would adjust to a one gee deceleration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: g-force - the force of gravity or acceleration on a body

Etymology

From the symbol 'g' for gravity, pronounced as 'gee'.
