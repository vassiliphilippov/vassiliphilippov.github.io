estranging

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈstreɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
Making someone feel alienated or out of place; causing a feeling of separation or distance.

Examples

On the other hand, Arnold's "The unplumbed, salt, estranging sea ", taken in its context, certainly does so.

Synset Examples

the landscape was estranging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alienating - making someone feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

From the verb 'estrange', from Old French *estrangier*, from Latin *extraneare* ('to treat as a stranger').
