fool

1. NOUN

/ ful / (AmE), / fuﾋ人 / (BrE)
A person who lacks good sense or judgment; a person who acts unwisely.

Examples

We are worried about what people may do with them - that some crazy fool may "push the button ".
The teacher was a pliant fool.
Oh, you can submit a requisition for a miracle if you want to make a fool of yourself.
It would n't matter to a fool like you.
Oh, God, the name repeated over and over, anybody to hear - Not being a fool, Mr. Skyros knew why.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Antonym: wise man - a person of great wisdom

Etymology

From Old French *fol*, from Latin *follis* (bellows, windbag, hence, a silly or empty-headed person).

2. VERB

/ ful / (AmE), / fuﾋ人 / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone into believing something that is not true

Examples

"Fooled you, huh?
I guess fooling Hans and me took doing.
Brenner's voice was oily, but Summers was n't fooled.
Fly cutters can fool you into putting your hand too close to the tool, so if you want to avoid nicked fingers, keep your hands well out of the way.
No girl would go this far to fool a man so she could kill him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick someone, often by making them look foolish

Etymology

From Old French 'fol', from Latin 'follis' (bellows, windbag, fool).

3. NOUN

/ ful / (AmE), / fuﾋ人 / (BrE)
A person who is easily tricked or deceived, lacking good judgment.

Examples

What a fool he had been to think of his brother!
You 're a fool.
He's an even bigger fool than you are.
A fool could see what was going on.
And he certainly could n't have guessed that she would resist his demand for the gold or that she was not the yielding - yes, and credible fool he had every right to expect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a person of low intelligence; a very stupid person
  • Synonym: dupe - a person who is easily deceived or tricked
  • Antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual or creative power

Etymology

From Old French *fol*, from Latin *follis* (bellows, empty-headed person).

4. NOUN

/ ful / (AmE), / fuﾋ人 / (BrE)
a historical entertainer, especially one kept in royal or noble households in the Middle Ages to amuse with jokes, tricks, or antics; a jester

Examples

I went and, mum and nervous, all but made a fool of myself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jester - a professional entertainer in royal courts; court fool

Etymology

From Old French *fol* (fool, madman), from Latin *follis* (bellows, windbag, fool).
