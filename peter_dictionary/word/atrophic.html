atrophic

1. ADJ

/ əˈtrɑːfɪk / (AmE), / əˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by atrophy; marked by the wasting away or degeneration of tissue, organs, or body parts.

Examples

The mucosa of the stomach was atrophic and irregularly blackened over a 14 cm. area.
These changes were more marked in the atrophic right kidney than in the left.
The muscles of the extremities, chest wall, neck, and abdominal wall were soft, pale, and atrophic.

Synset Examples

atrophic arthritis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophy - wasting away or decrease in size of a body part, tissue, or organ
  • Antonym: hypertrophic - relating to or characterized by hypertrophy (excessive growth of tissue or organ)

Etymology

From atrophy + -ic, based on the Greek 'atrophia' (lack of nourishment).
