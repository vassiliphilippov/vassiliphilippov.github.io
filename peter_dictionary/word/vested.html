vested

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪd / (BrE)
(Of a right, power, or interest) completely and legally established; not contingent or subject to conditions.

Examples

By the power vested in me by the state of Maine, I hereby order you to serve two life sentences, back to back.

Synset Examples

a vested right

Etymology

Past participle of 'vest', from Latin 'vestire' (to clothe).
