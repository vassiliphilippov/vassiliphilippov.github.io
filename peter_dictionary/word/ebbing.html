ebbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛbɪŋ / (BrE)
A gradual decline or lessening in strength, number, size, or power.

Examples

After an earthquake in Japan in 1793 people on the coast at Tugaru were so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • Synonym: waning - a gradual decrease or decline in strength or intensity
  • Antonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement

Etymology

From 'ebb' (to flow back or recede), ultimately from Old English 'ebbian'.
