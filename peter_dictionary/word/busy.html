busy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
Intrusively meddling in other people's affairs; overly interested or involved in matters that do not concern one.

Examples

The game opened a busy week for Washington.

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: officious - meddling or assertive of authority in an annoying way
  • Synonym: interfering - meddling in others' affairs without invitation

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
Actively or fully engaged or occupied; having a lot to do or many things happening at once.

Examples

And Anthony was busy most of the time courting this girl and that.
They had lunch at a sidewalk cafe overlooking the intersection of two broad, busy, unpicturesque streets, and coming home they got lost in the Metro; it took them over an hour to get back to the station where they should have changed, in order to take the line that went to the Place Redoute.
"I would like to enact a little tableau this afternoon ", Rector said, He explained about the visit and the effect he wished to create, the picture of a very busy mission.
"I would like them to appear very busy today, not busy exactly, but joyous, exuberant, full of life.

Synset Examples

busy with her work
a busy man
too busy to eat lunch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idle - not working, unoccupied; not busy

Etymology

From Old English *bysig* (occupied, diligent).

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
(of a design, picture, or scene) containing too many details or elements, making it appear cluttered or overcrowded.

Examples

After a "busy "overture, the curtain rises on a farm scene - the Ranavan Valley in northern Maine.

Synset Examples

a busy painting
a fussy design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fussy - excessively decorated or overly detailed, especially in design or style

4. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
Crowded with or characterized by much activity or movement.

Examples

Reaching the porch rail beyond view of the bar windows, he feverishly scanned the busy street below.

Synset Examples

a very busy week
a busy life
a busy street
a busy seaport

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quiet - not busy or crowded; free from activity or noise

Etymology

From Middle English 'bisī', from Old English 'bysig' (diligent, industrious, busy).

5. VERB

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
to occupy oneself with a task or activity, often in order to pass the time or avoid idleness

Examples

San Francisco firemen busied themselves last week with their annual voluntary task of fixing up toys for distribution to needy children.

Synset Examples

She busies herself with her butterfly collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupy - to keep oneself engaged with an activity

Etymology

From Middle English 'busien' (to be busy), from 'busy' (adjective).
