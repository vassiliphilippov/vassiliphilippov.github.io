little

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl / (BrE)
Used with mass nouns to indicate a small quantity or degree; not much or almost none. Often used to emphasize a shortfall or limited amount.

Examples

But questions with which committee members taunted bankers appearing as witnesses left little doubt that they will recommend passage of it.
"They can be going along, doing little damage, then bang, bang - they can hit a couple of passes on you for touchdowns and put you in trouble ".

For my seedbed I use good garden soil with a little sand added to encourage rooting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: much - large in quantity or degree
  • Antonym: few - small in number (with count nouns)
  • Synonym: slight - small in degree or amount

Etymology

From Old English lȳtel (small), related to Old Saxon luttil, Old High German luzzil.
