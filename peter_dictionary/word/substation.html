substation

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a subsidiary station where electricity is transformed to a lower voltage for distribution to consumers

Examples

The latter now furnishes the area with electricity distributed from a modern sub-station at Manchester Depot which was put into operation February 19, 1930 and was improved in January 1942 by the installation of larger transformers.

Etymology

From 'sub-' meaning 'under' or 'secondary' and 'station', denoting its role as a subsidiary station.
