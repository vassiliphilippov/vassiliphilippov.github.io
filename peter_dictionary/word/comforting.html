comforting

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌm.fər.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.fət.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Making someone feel less worried, anxious, or upset; giving comfort.

Examples

The Texans made themselves a comforting break on the opening kickoff when Denver's Al Carmichael was jarred loose from the ball when Dave Grayson, the speedy halfback, hit him and Guard Al Reynolds claimed it for Dallas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or afraid
  • Antonym: distressing - causing anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

From the verb 'comfort', from Old French 'conforter', from Latin 'confortare' (to strengthen greatly).
