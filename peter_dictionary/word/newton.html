Newton

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːtən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtən / (BrE)
Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727), English mathematician and physicist famous for developing calculus, the law of gravitation, and the three laws of motion.

Examples

Is the future of psychology akin to the rich future of physics at the time of Newton?

Etymology

From the surname of Sir Isaac Newton.
