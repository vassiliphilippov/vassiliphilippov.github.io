inglorious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡlɔriəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡlɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
Deserving or causing shame or disgrace; not bringing honor.

Examples

The so-called "noble experiment "came to an inglorious end.

Synset Examples

Man...has written one of his blackest records as a destroyer on the oceanic islands
an ignominious retreat
inglorious defeat
an opprobrious monument to human greed
a shameful display of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glorious - bringing honor or fame
  • Synonym: ignominious - deserving public shame or disgrace
  • Synonym: shameful - causing shame or disgrace

Etymology

From Latin 'ingloriosus', from 'in-' (not) + 'gloriosus' (glorious).
