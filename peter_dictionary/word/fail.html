fail

1. VERB

/ feɪl / (AmE), / feɪl / (BrE)
to not do something that you should do, or to leave something undone

Examples

They often fail, however, to develop real abdominal, back, chest, shoulder and arm strength.

Synset Examples

She failed to notice that her child was no longer in his crib
The secretary failed to call the customer and the company lost the account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - to not give proper attention or care to something required

Etymology

From Old French *faillir*, from Latin *fallere* (to deceive, disappoint).
