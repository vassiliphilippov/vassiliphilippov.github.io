unselfconsciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌsɛlfkənˈʃɑsnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌsɛlfkənˈʃɒsnəs / (BrE)
The state of being not self-conscious; a lack of awareness of oneself or of how others perceive one.

Examples

Thus shielded, we played many foolish games in comfortable unselfconsciousness; even when the fences became a part of the game - when a vine embowered gate-post was the Sleeping Beauty's enchanted castle, or when Rapunzel let down her golden hair from beneath the crocketed spire, even then we paid little heed to those who went by on the path outside.

Synset Examples

he had the unselfconsciousness of a child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: self-consciousness - the state of being excessively aware of oneself

Etymology

From 'unselfconscious' + '-ness', with 'un-' (not), 'self-conscious' and 'ness' (state of being).
