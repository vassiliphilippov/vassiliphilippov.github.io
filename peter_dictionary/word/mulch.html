mulch

1. NOUN

/ mʌltʃ / (AmE), / mʌltʃ / (BrE)
A protective covering of organic material, such as decaying leaves or straw, spread over soil to conserve moisture, reduce weed growth, and improve soil quality.

Examples

A light, porous mulch applied now keeps the roots cool and the soil soft during these early days of growth.
They need mulch.
We put a light mulch over the seedlings; now we must use a heavy one.
When winter comes be ready with additional mulch.
Give them food, some shade, mulch, water and more food, and they 'll repay your solicitude with beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compost - decayed organic matter used as fertilizer

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Middle English *molsh* (soft, decaying).
