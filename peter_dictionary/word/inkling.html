inkling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
A slight suggestion, hint, or vague idea about something.

Examples

"I do n't know, Mr. Brenner ", he said haltingly, beginning to get an inkling of Brenner's plans.

Synset Examples

he had no inkling what was about to happen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect suggestion

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'enklen' (to hint), of uncertain origin.
