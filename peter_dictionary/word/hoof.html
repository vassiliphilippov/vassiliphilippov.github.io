hoof

1. NOUN

/ hʊf, huf / (AmE), / hʊf, huːf / (BrE)
The hard, horny covering at the end of the foot of an ungulate mammal such as a horse, cow, or deer.

Examples

Again Katie reared, and now, wickedly, he compelled her to bring her hooves down again and again upon the sprawled figure of the stranger.
Cows were kept in backyard barns, boys were hired to drive them to and from the pasture on the edge of town, and familiar to the ear, morning and evening, were the boys' coaxing voices, the thud of hooves, and the thwack of a stick on cowhide.
There were no tracks of either hoofs or boots.
Again he stood in the darkness listening, but there was only the scrape of a shod hoof on a plank floor.
He slapped the buckskin again and it kicked wildly, its hoofs rattling the side of the stall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claw - curved, pointed appendage at the end of the toe in animals
  • Degree Lower: paw - the foot of an animal having claws or pads

Etymology

Old English *hof*; related to Dutch *hoef*, German *Huf* (hoof).

2. NOUN

/ hʊf / (AmE), / hʊf / (BrE)
The hard, horny covering protecting the end of the foot in animals such as horses, cows, and deer.

Examples

600,000 dollars on four hoofs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claw - curved, pointed appendage at the end of animal limbs

Etymology

From Old English *hof* (hoof), related to German *Huf* and Dutch *hoef*.
