untenable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛnəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛnəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a theory, argument, or position) not able to be defended against criticism or objection; impossible to justify or maintain.

Examples

If the demand for demythologization is unavoidable and so must be accepted by theology unconditionally, the position of the "right "is clearly untenable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indefensible - impossible to defend against attack or criticism
  • Antonym: defensible - able to be defended or justified

Etymology

From un- (not) + tenable (from French, from tenir 'to hold').
