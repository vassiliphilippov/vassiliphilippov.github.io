governing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌvɚnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪŋ / (BrE)
Having authority to make and enforce rules or laws; having control or influence over a people, organization, or process.

Examples

Its function is to separate from the base ruled mass, among whom private ownership prevails, the governing warrior elite.
Denouncing the view that the sexual union is an end in itself, the Conference declared: "We steadfastly uphold what must always be regarded as the governing considerations of Christian marriage.

Synset Examples

governing bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruling - having control or authority over people or a place
  • Antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or position; under the authority of another

Etymology

Present participle of 'govern', from Old French 'governer', from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, direct).
