carelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
failure to give sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors; lack of care or attention.

Examples

Because he interprets the primitive state of man as one of mere potentiality or capacity and believes that Adam and Eve were created as children, Irenaeus often seems inclined to extenuate their disobedience as being "due, no doubt, to carelessness, but still wicked ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something; neglect
  • Antonym: attentiveness - the quality of paying careful attention

Etymology

From 'careless' + '-ness', with 'careless' derived from Old English 'carleas' (free from care).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkeələsnəs / (BrE)
Lack of attention, thought, or effort in doing something; the state or quality of being careless.

Examples

"The hero of his next poem is Napoleon Bonaparte ", said Claire, with slightly overdone carelessness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: careful - showing thought and attention; opposite of 'careless'
  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care; neglect

Etymology

From 'careless' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
