necktie

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.taɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.taɪ / (BrE)
A long, narrow piece of cloth worn around the neck under a collar and tied in a knot at the front, usually as part of formal or business attire.

Examples

Dark gray sports jacket, lighter gray slacks, pink flannel shirt, black silk necktie.
The night we first met, at one of Mrs. Monmouth's giant parties, he was wearing a brown cashmere jacket with silver buttons and a soft pink Viyella shirt; instead of a necktie he wore a leather bolo drawn through a golden ring in which was set a lump of pale pure jade.

Synset Examples

he stood in front of the mirror tightening his necktie
he wore a vest and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tie - a strip of cloth worn around the collar and tied at the throat, often used interchangeably with 'necktie' in American English

Etymology

Formed from 'neck' + 'tie', indicating a tie worn around the neck.
