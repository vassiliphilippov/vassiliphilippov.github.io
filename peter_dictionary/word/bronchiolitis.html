bronchiolitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɑːŋki.oʊˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkɪ.əˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the membranes lining the bronchioles, usually causing difficulty in breathing.

Examples

One might also wonder if monkeys are capable of developing bronchiolitis as we know it in man or the horse.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bronchitis - inflammation of the larger bronchial tubes

Etymology

From 'bronchiole' (a small branch of the bronchial tubes) + '-itis' (Greek, meaning inflammation).
