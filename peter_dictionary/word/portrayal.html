portrayal

1. NOUN

/ pɔɹˈtreɪ.əl / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act of performing as a particular character in a play, film, etc., by using speech, movement, and expression

Examples

His portrayal of an edgy head-in-the-clouds artist is virtually flawless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a role or piece in a play or film
  • Synonym: depiction - the representation of someone or something in a work of art or literature

Etymology

From verb 'portray' + noun-forming suffix '-al', modeled after French 'portraial'.

2. NOUN

/ pɔːrˈtreɪəl / (AmE), / pɔːˈtreɪəl / (BrE)
A description or depiction of a person's appearance and character, especially in writing or art.

Examples

In B. M. Spinley's portrayal of the underprivileged and undereducated youth of London, a salient finding was the inability to postpone gratification, a need to satisfy impulses immediately without the pleasure of anticipation or of savoring the experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the action of depicting something, especially in art

Etymology

From the verb 'portray', from Old French *portraire* (to depict), from Latin *protrahere* (to draw forth).
