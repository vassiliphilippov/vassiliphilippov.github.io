giggle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡl / (BrE)
To laugh in a light, nervous, or silly way, often in a high-pitched tone.

Examples

Mose giggled.
Miss Ada had giggled, and she went sweeping and rustling to the couch and sank down.
But when some of the squeals had subsided and she had been through one of those sessions that are so indispensable to the young female - six girls sprawled on one bed, drinking Cokes and giggling - she came back to the kitchen to talk with me a minute.
[GIGGLING] MICHAEL: I don'’t want you going!

Synset Examples

The girls giggled when the rock star came into the classroom

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself
  • Synonym: snicker - to laugh in a suppressed or partly stifled way
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously

Etymology

Imitative; possibly from Middle English 'gigen' (to trill, to laugh).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡl / (BrE)
a light, silly, or nervous laugh

Examples

Over the rattling of fenders, humming of tires and chattering of gears there was a charming melody of whispers and tiny giggles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckle - a quiet or suppressed laugh
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - a loud, boisterous laugh

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of such laughter.
