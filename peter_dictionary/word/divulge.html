divulge

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvʌldʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈvʌldʒ / (BrE)
to make known (something private, secret, or previously unknown); to disclose or reveal

Examples

The Washington state supreme court ruled that the state's occupation tax applied to sales, made at cost to an oil company, by a wholly-owned subsidiary set up to purchase certain supplies without divulging the identity of the parent.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make previously unknown information known to others
  • Synonym: disclose - to make secret information known
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep from being discovered or known
  • Antonym: hide - to keep out of sight or secret

Etymology

From Latin 'divulgare' (to make public), from 'di-' (widely) + 'vulgare' (to make known, publish, from 'vulgus' people).
