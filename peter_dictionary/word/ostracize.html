ostracize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑstrəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəsaɪz / (BrE)
To exclude someone from a group or community, often by a collective social agreement.

Examples

To the extent that he was ostracized or even reviled, we solace ourselves by saying he asked for it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shun - to deliberately avoid or stay away from someone

Etymology

From Greek *ostrakizein* (to banish by voting with potsherds), from *ostrakon* (shell, potsherd).
