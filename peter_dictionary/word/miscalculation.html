miscalculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌkælkjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.kæl.kjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An error or mistake in calculating or estimating; a misjudgment, especially involving numbers or outcomes.

Examples

If this capacity had not failed them, they would see that their enemy has made a disastrous miscalculation.
In all his miscalculations, Stravinsky made the fatal historical blunder of presuming that he could transform other composers' inspirations - representing many peoples, time periods and styles - into his own music by warping the harmony, melody, or form, to verify his own experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calculation - the process of using mathematics; careful planning
  • Synonym: error - a mistake or wrong decision

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'calculation', from Latin 'calculatio', meaning 'a reckoning'.
