dismember

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈmɛmbɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
to cut or tear the limbs from a person's or animal's body

Examples

Simply out of bloodlust, their murderers dismembered the bodies and tossed the remains into the river.

Synset Examples

the tiger dismembered the tourist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutilate - to inflict serious injury to a body, especially by removing parts

Etymology

From Old French *desmembrer*, from Latin *dismembrare* (to cut off the limbs), from *dis-* (apart) + *membrum* (limb).
