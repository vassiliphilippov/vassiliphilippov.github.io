torch

1. NOUN

/ tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A portable light source, usually consisting of a stick with material that can be set on fire at one end and carried in the hand.

Examples

Below he could see the bright torches lighting the riverbank.
Watson spoke bewilderedly to the dark night flecked with pine-knot torches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashlight - a portable electric light, also called a 'torch' in British English

Etymology

From Old French torche (twisted thing), from Latin torqua (something twisted, a wick).
