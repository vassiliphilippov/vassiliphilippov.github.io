fineness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely good or excellent

Examples

It all began on an autumn afternoon - and who, after all these centuries, can describe the fineness of an autumn day?

Synset Examples

the inn is distinguished by the fineness of its cuisine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Synonym: superiority - the state of being better or higher in quality
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or ordinary (contrasting)

Etymology

From fine + -ness, patterned after Middle English finenesse, from Old French finesse.
