pound

1. NOUN

/ paʊnd / (AmE), / paʊnd / (BrE)
a unit of weight equal to 16 ounces, used in the United States and other countries that use the imperial system

Examples

The 6 - foot 3 inch Hansen checked in close to 200 pounds, 15 pounds lighter than his reporting weight last spring.

The husky 6 - 3, 205 - pound lefthander, was in command all the way before an on the scene audience of only 949 and countless of television viewers in the Denver area.

Synset Examples

he got a hernia when he tried to lift 100 pounds

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ounce - a unit of weight equal to 1/16 of a pound
  • Degree Higher: stone - a unit of weight equal to 14 pounds (mainly British)
  • Synonym: kilogram - the metric unit of mass, equivalent to about 2.2046 pounds

Etymology

From Old English *pund*, from Latin *pondus* (weight).

4. NOUN

/ paʊnd / (AmE), / paʊnd / (BrE)
the main unit of currency in the United Kingdom and Northern Ireland, equal to 100 pence

Examples

One man remarked that if he had a hundred pounds, he would give ninety of them to be back in England.
Up spoke carpenter Staffe, who said he would n't give ten pounds to be home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterling - British money, especially the pound sterling
  • Antonym: euro - the currency used by many European Union countries
  • Antonym: dollar - the standard monetary unit in the US, Canada, Australia and some other countries

Etymology

From Latin 'pondo' (by weight), influenced by the use of 'libra pondo' (a pound in weight) for monetary reckoning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: flutter - to beat quickly and lightly, as a heart might
