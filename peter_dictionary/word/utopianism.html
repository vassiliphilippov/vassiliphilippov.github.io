utopianism

1. NOUN

/ juːˈtoʊpiənɪzəm / (AmE), / juːˈtəʊpiənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief in or pursuit of an ideal society in which everything is perfect, often considered unrealistic or impractical.

Examples

And let me add, utopianism, also.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - Pursuit of high or noble goals, sometimes impractical
  • Antonym: realism - Acceptance of facts and practical approaches

Etymology

From 'Utopia', coined by Sir Thomas More in 1516 from Greek 'ou' (not) + 'topos' (place), and '-ism'.
