rack

1. NOUN

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
A framework, often with bars, hooks, or shelves, used for holding, storing, or displaying things.

Examples

In the lower center rack an 8 - channel recorder indicates the percentage of carbon dioxide and nitrogen from the upper and lower lobes of one lung, the total volume of inhalation per breath, the flow of air from both lobes, and the pressure of the two lobes with respect to each other.
He walked up to the lieutenant's office, leaned wearily against the gun rack that housed four rifles and a gas gun nobody remembered having used and a submachine gun that was occasionally tried out on the Academy Range.
Haying time was close at hand, and they needed some strong branches to repair a hay rack.
Quickly but carefully lowering my duffel bag over the low side rack, I stepped on the running board; it flopped down, sprang back up and gouged my shin.
I seized the rack and made a western style flying mount just in time, one of my knees mercifully landing on my duffel bag - and merely wrecking my camera, I was to discover later - my other knee landing on the slivery truck floor boards and - but this is no medical report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelf - flat surface for storing or displaying items, often attached to a wall

Etymology

From Middle English *rakke*, from Old English *racu* (framework), related to Dutch *rek* and German *Reck*.

2. VERB

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
to subject someone or oneself to intense strain or effort, especially mentally; to stretch or torture, often used in the phrase 'rack one's brains'

Examples

At the end of the half-hour, racking his brains, thinking over and over again of Kitti, her friends, her past, he left the bedroom.

Synset Examples

rack one's brains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - to make strenuous or excessive effort
  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering

Etymology

From Middle English *rakken* (to stretch), related to the noun 'rack' (a device for stretching).

3. VERB

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
(of a horse) to move at a rack, a particular smooth, fast gait in which each foot strikes the ground separately.

Examples

Although they were forced to maintain a sharper watch, this activity enabled them to ride in and rack their broncs without any particular attention being paid them.
This Sparky can rack and single-foot and he's the fastest thing in Washoe County.

Synset Examples

the horses single-footed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: single-foot - (of a horse) to move in a manner where each foot strikes the ground separately; a smooth gait, synonym for 'rack' in this context

Etymology

From the noun 'rack' (a kind of horse gait), of uncertain origin.
