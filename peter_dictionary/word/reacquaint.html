reacquaint

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make yourself or someone else familiar with someone or something again after a period of not knowing or using them

Examples

Only when the newest Mr. America or Mr. Universe rediscovers them and puts them into practice are we reacquainted with them and once again see how effective they really are.

Synset Examples

We reacquainted ourselves with this technique

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: acquaint - to make someone aware of or familiar with

Etymology

From re- (again) + acquaint, from Old French acointier (to make known), from Latin accognitare.
