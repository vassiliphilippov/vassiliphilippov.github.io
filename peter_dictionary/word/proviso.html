proviso

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvaɪzoʊ / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪzəʊ / (BrE)
A condition or stipulation added to an agreement, document, or law, usually specifying a limitation or qualification.

Examples

Two millions were added to what had been set aside for it in Mrs. Meeker's lifetime, and the proviso made that as long as Brian Thayer continued to discharge his duties as administrator of the fund to the satisfaction of the board of trustees (hereinafter appointed by the bank administering the estate) he was to be retained in his present capacity at a salary commensurate with the increased responsibilities enlargement of the fund would entail.
Not as long as we have over one billion dollars invested in this country.

Synset Examples

he accepted subject to one provision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condition - something that must be fulfilled for something else to happen; requirement
  • Synonym: stipulation - a specific demand or requirement in an agreement

Etymology

From Latin proviso (‘it being provided’), from providere (‘to provide’).
