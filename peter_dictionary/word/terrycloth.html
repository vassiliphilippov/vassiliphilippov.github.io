terrycloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛriˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈtɛriˌklɒθ / (BrE)
a thick, soft cotton fabric with uncut loops on both sides, commonly used for making bath towels and bathrobes

Examples

The infant, in white terry-cloth bathrobe, her face intense and purposeful, had essayed a few wobbly steps toward her father.

Etymology

From 'terry', a shortening of French 'terci' (past participle of 'tercer', to twist, from Latin 'tertius', third, referring to a three-ply weave), and 'cloth'.
