thresh

1. VERB

/ θrɛʃ / (AmE), / θrɛʃ / (BrE)
to move or thrash about violently, especially in an uncontrolled or agitated way

Examples

The bodies in the truck threshed and bawled without human speech when the dromozoa struck them.

Synset Examples

The feverish patient thrashed around in his bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to move about or strike violently

Etymology

From Old English 'þrescan' (to separate grain by beating), later used figuratively for violent movement.
