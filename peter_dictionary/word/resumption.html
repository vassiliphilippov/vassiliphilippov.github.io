resumption

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzʌmpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act of starting something again after a pause or interruption

Examples

These gentlemen are calling for a resumption of testing - in the atmosphere - on the greatest possible scale, all in the name of national security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restart - the act of starting again after stopping
  • Antonym: pause - a temporary stop or break in activity

Etymology

From Latin resumptio (a taking up again), from re- (again) + sumere (to take).
