garbed

1. ADJ

/ ɡɑrbd / (AmE), / ɡɑːbd / (BrE)
dressed or clothed, especially in a specified way or attire (often used in combination).

Examples

They trailed him across the wide hallway to the parlor, four roughly garbed and tough looking men who probably had never before ventured into such a house.

Synset Examples

the elegantly attired gentleman
neatly dressed workers
monks garbed in hooded robes
went about oddly garmented
professors robed in crimson
tuxedo-attired gentlemen
crimson-robed Harvard professors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attired - dressed in a particular way
  • Synonym: appareled - clothed, dressed (esp. in fine clothing)
  • Synonym: garmented - wearing garments or clothing

Etymology

From the verb 'garb,' ultimately from Italian *garbare* (to please, suit), via French *garbe* (style, fashion, dress).
