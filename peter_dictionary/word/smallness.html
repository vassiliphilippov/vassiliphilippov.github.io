smallness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being small in size, amount, or extent.

Examples

Left in their previous atom-like smallness, they would have cut away too abruptly into depth; and the broad, opaque shapes of pasted paper would have been isolated in such a way as to make them jump out of plane.
That world was in scale with my own smallness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: littleness - the quality of being little; an alternative for smallness
  • Antonym: bigness - the quality or state of being big or large

Etymology

From 'small' + '-ness', a common English suffix forming nouns denoting a state or condition.
