deglycerolize

1. VERB

/ diˈɡlɪsəˌrɔːˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈɡlɪsərəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
To remove glycerol from a substance, typically as part of a purification process in scientific or medical contexts.

Examples

These cells were thawed at 37 ` C for 30 min and were deglycerolized by alternately centrifuging and mixing with descending concentrations of glycerol solutions (20, 18, 10, 8, 4 and 2 %).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glycerolize - to add glycerol to (a substance)

Etymology

Formed from de- (removal) + glycerol + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
