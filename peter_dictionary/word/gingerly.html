gingerly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɪndʒɚli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒəli / (BrE)
In a careful or cautious manner; with great delicacy.

Examples

They advanced in a line across the entrance hall to the stairway and up, with gingerly steps, towards the first landing.

Synset Examples

they proceeded with gingerly footwork over the jagged stones
the issue was handled only in a gingerly way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a careful, cautious manner
  • Antonym: recklessly - without regard to danger or consequences

Etymology

Perhaps from earlier sense of 'delicate' or 'dainty',[influenced by the word ginger]. Exact origin uncertain.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒɪndʒɚli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒəli / (BrE)
In a careful or cautious way to avoid harm or risk.

Examples

Mr. Crombie poked at it gingerly with his foot.

Synset Examples

gingerly I raised the edge of the blanket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - in a way that deliberately avoids harm or danger
  • Antonym: recklessly - in a careless or heedless manner

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from Old French 'gingibre' (ginger), with a sense of delicacy or carefulness associated with ginger root.
