suffering

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfərɪŋ / (BrE)
The experience of mental or physical pain or distress.

Examples

And she felt amply rewarded for her suffering when the evidence of Lee's quack shenanigans, gathered by the tape recorder under her friend's clothing, proved adequate in court for convicting Franklin D. Lee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical or emotional discomfort
  • Degree Higher: agony - extreme physical or mental suffering
  • Degree Lower: discomfort - slight pain or uneasiness
  • Antonym: relief - removal or alleviation of pain or distress

Etymology

From Old English 'sufrian', via Old French 'soufrir', from Latin 'sufferre' (to endure, bear).
