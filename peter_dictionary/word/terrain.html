terrain

1. NOUN

/ təˈreɪn / (AmE), / təˈreɪn / (BrE)
A stretch of land, especially with regard to its physical features or suitability for a particular purpose.

Examples

The best chance, of course, is offered by gently sloping terrain where the water remains close to the surface and where the air is dry, so that a high evaporation leaves salty deposits which permit only sparse plant growth.
But with all the changes in philosophy, dress and terrain - a few things remain constant, including the devotion of Americans to the great field sports, hunting and fishing.

Synset Examples

they decided to attack across the rocky terrain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape - all the visible features of an area of land
  • Synonym: topography - the arrangement of the natural and artificial physical features of an area

Etymology

From French *terrain* (ground, land), from Latin *terrenum* (land, earth).
