heist

1. VERB

/ haɪst / (AmE), / haɪst / (BrE)
To steal something in a burglary or robbery, especially involving an elaborate or daring theft.

Examples

First of all, the six figures of the Buddha you heisted - four Siamese heads, a black obsidian statuette in the earth touching position, and a large brass figure of the Dying Buddha on a teakwood base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglarize - to break into and steal from a building
  • Synonym: rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

Probably from dialectal heist, variant of hoist (to lift).
