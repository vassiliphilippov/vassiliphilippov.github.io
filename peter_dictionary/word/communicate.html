communicate

1. VERB

/ kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmjuː.nɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to share or transmit information, thoughts, or feelings to others, especially by speaking, writing, or using other signals

Examples

Nor is it necessary to look for such evidence in the great urban centers of our culture that are admittedly almost entirely secularized and so profoundly estranged from the conventional forms in which the gospel has been communicated.
By the same means he perceives this fact as having communicated itself to the audience; he collapses, and dies.

Various methods of pulsing, scanning, and displaying these sound waves are used to detect submarines, map ocean floors, and even communicate under water.
A Peace Corps official described the editorial as "precisely the message we need to communicate to the men and women who will soon be Peace Corps volunteers ".

Synset Examples

Please communicate this message to all employees
pass along the good news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convey - to communicate or make known (a message or information)
  • Synonym: inform - to give information to; to tell
  • Synonym: notify - to formally or officially communicate information
  • Antonym: withhold - to hold back or refuse to communicate information

Etymology

From Latin communicare (to share, impart), from communis (common).

2. VERB

/ kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to share or exchange information, ideas, or feelings with others, especially by speaking, writing, or using other signals

Examples

The United States can help by communicating a genuine concern with the problems these countries face and a readiness to provide technical and other appropriate forms of assistance where possible.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convey - to make feelings, ideas, or information known to someone
  • Synonym: express - to make known one's thoughts or feelings

Etymology

From Latin communicare (to share, impart), from communis (common).
