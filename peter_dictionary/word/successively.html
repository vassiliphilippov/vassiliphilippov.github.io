successively

1. ADV

/ səkˈsɛsɪvli / (AmE), / səkˈsɛsɪvli / (BrE)
Following one after another in sequence; consecutively.

Examples

In the testing room he was to make, successively, three presentations to three different people.

Synset Examples

talked to each child in turn
the stable became in turn a chapel and then a movie theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutively - following one after another without interruption

Etymology

From Latin 'successivus' (following in order), from 'succedere' (to follow).
