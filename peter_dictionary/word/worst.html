worst

1. ADJ

/ wɜrst / (AmE), / wɜːst / (BrE)
Most bad in quality, value, or condition; of the poorest or lowest standard.

Examples

The club that overcame the worst start in a comparable period to win the pennant was New York's '51 Giants, who dropped 11 of their first 13.
Certainly there would be less anxiety, fewer accidents (it is the clumsy child who sustains the worst injuries), and higher scholastic averages, since alert children work better.
The best could not escape from the worst and the worst nestled cosily beside the better.
The best could not escape from the worst and the worst nestled cosily beside the better.
That goddamn redheader was the worst.

Synset Examples

the worst player on the team
the worst weather of the year

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bad - of poor quality or low standard; negative
  • Degree Lower: worser - nonstandard or archaic comparative of 'bad'; more bad
  • Antonym: better - comparative of 'good'; more desirable or of higher standard
  • Antonym: best - superlative of 'good'; highest in quality or achievement

Etymology

Superlative form of 'bad,' from Old English 'wyrst' (worst, most bad).

2. NOUN

/ wɝst / (AmE), / wɜːst / (BrE)
The least favorable or most undesirable outcome or situation.

Examples

Chandler understood this and expected the worst.
Once he had abandoned himself to the very worst, once he had quieted all the dragons of worry and suspense, there would n't be very much for Mae to do.

Synset Examples

the worst that could happen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: best - the most desirable outcome; opposite of 'worst' as a noun

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrst', superlative of 'yfel' (evil; ill).
