aluminum

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪnɪəm / (BrE)
A light, silvery-white, ductile metallic element used in making many products such as cans, foils, kitchen utensils, and aircraft parts, known for its strength and resistance to corrosion.
