immature

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪməˈtʃʊr, ɪˈmætʃər / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtjʊə, ɪˈmatjʊə / (BrE)
not fully developed in body or mind; showing a lack of emotional, physical, or intellectual maturity

Examples

She is even prone to regard the college girl as immature.
And just as "Laurie "Lawrence was first attracted to bright Jo March, who found him immature by her high standards, and then had to content himself with her younger sister Amy, so Joe Jastrow, who had also been writing Henrietta before he came to Johns Hopkins, had to content himself with her younger sister, pretty Rachel.

Synset Examples

immature behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mature - having reached full development or maturity

Etymology

From Latin immaturus, from in- (not) + maturus (ripe, mature).
