diversity

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜrsəti / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having many different forms, types, ideas, or people; variety.

Examples

With the diversity of religious viewpoints, there are differences of opinion as to the essential features of religion; and there are different opinions as to the essential functions of religion.
Everywhere within the common pattern, however, one finds local diversity; Greek history and culture were enduringly fertilized, and plagued, by the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors.

Synset Examples

a diversity of possibilities
the range and variety of his work is amazing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse; a number of different things
  • Antonym: uniformity - the quality or state of being the same or uniform

Etymology

From Latin 'diversitas', from 'diversus' (turned different ways, diverse).
