bounds

1. NOUN

/ baʊndz / (AmE), / baʊndz / (BrE)
limits or boundaries that restrict or define the extent of something, especially what is considered acceptable or possible.

Examples

This particular song at this particular time could only be interpreted as the ultimate in bad taste, callous exploitation beyond the bounds of decency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line indicating the limits of an area
  • Synonym: limit - the furthest point or edge; a restriction
  • Synonym: confines - the borders or limits of an area or subject

Etymology

Plural of 'bound', from Middle English 'bounde', from Old French 'bonde/borne' (boundary).
