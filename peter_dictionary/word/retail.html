retail

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl / (BrE)
The sale of goods to the public in relatively small quantities for use or consumption rather than for resale.

Examples

We have already witnessed great changes through mergers and acquisitions in the food industry - at both the manufacturing and retail ends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale - the selling of goods in large quantities, typically to be retailed by others

Etymology

From Old French *retaillier* (to cut off, divide), from re- + *tailler* (to cut).

2. VERB

/ ˈriː.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈriː.teɪl / (BrE)
(of goods) to be sold to the public in small quantities, usually at a set price determined by the retailer rather than wholesale.

Examples

It is a simple task to haul a boat fifty or one hundred miles to a lake or reservoir on the new, light, strong, easy-to-operate trailers which are built to accommodate almost any kind of small boat and retail from $ 100 to $ 2000.

Synset Examples

These gems retail at thousands of dollars each

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale - to be sold in large quantities, typically to retailers or professional users, not directly to the public

Etymology

From Old French 'retaillier' (to cut off, pare), from re- (back) + tailler (to cut).
