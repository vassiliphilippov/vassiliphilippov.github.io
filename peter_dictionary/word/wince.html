wince

1. VERB

/ wɪns / (AmE), / wɪns / (BrE)
to make a sudden facial expression showing embarrassment, pain, or dislike

Examples

He winced.

Synset Examples

She winced when she heard his pompous speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimace - to make a twisted facial expression indicating pain or disgust

Etymology

From Middle English *wincen*, from Old North French *wincier* (to turn aside, recoil).

2. VERB

/ wɪns / (AmE), / wɪns / (BrE)
To make a slight, involuntary movement of the body as a reaction to pain or discomfort, or from embarrassment or fear.

Examples

The fat man winced.
When one of the men in the hall behind us spat on the floor and scraped his boot over the gob of spittle I noticed how the clerk winced.

Synset Examples

she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - draw back or shrink from pain or fear
  • Synonym: cringe - bend one’s head and body in fear or embarrassment
  • Synonym: recoil - suddenly spring or flinch back

Etymology

From Middle English *wynsen*, possibly from Old North French *wincier* (to turn aside, recoil).
