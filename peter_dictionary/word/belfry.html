belfry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlfri / (AmE), / ˈbɛlfri / (BrE)
A tower or part of a building, usually for housing bells, especially one that stands apart from the main structure.

Examples

God was everywhere, in the belfry, in the steeple, in the clouds, in the trees, and in the mountains hulking on the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campanile - an Italian-style freestanding bell tower
  • Synonym: steeple - a tall tower of a church or other building, sometimes containing bells

Etymology

From Old French *berfrei*, later associated with *belle* (bell), originally meaning a watchtower.
