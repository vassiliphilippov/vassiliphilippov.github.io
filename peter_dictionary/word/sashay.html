sashay

1. VERB

/ sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / sæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
To move in a confident, playful, or ostentatious way, often with exaggerated movements such as swinging the hips; sometimes to move sideways or in a gliding manner.

Examples

A quarter of a century has gone by since this bevy of walking dreams sashayed up and down the staircases of the old New Amsterdam Theater, N. Y..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidle - to move sideways, often in a timid or unobtrusive manner
  • Synonym: strut - to walk in a proud or confident way

Etymology

From French 'chassé', past participle of 'chasser' (to chase); adopted into English from dance terminology.
