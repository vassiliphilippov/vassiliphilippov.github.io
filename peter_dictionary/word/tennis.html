tennis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnɪs / (BrE)
A game in which two or four players use rackets to hit a ball over a net stretched across a rectangular court.

Examples

Watching her, he felt like a spectator at a tennis game, with the ball being bounced back and forth.

Etymology

From French 'tenez' ('take, receive, hold'), traditionally called out by the server.
