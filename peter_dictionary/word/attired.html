attired

1. ADJ

/ əˈtaɪɚd / (AmE), / əˈtaɪəd / (BrE)
wearing clothes, especially of a particular kind or of fine quality; dressed.

Examples

Then Rector, attired in his best blue serge suit, sat in a chair out on the lawn, in the shade of a tree, smoking a cigarette and waiting.

Synset Examples

the elegantly attired gentleman
neatly dressed workers
monks garbed in hooded robes
went about oddly garmented
professors robed in crimson
tuxedo-attired gentlemen
crimson-robed Harvard professors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressed - wearing clothes; clothed
  • Synonym: clad - clothed, dressed in a particular way
  • Antonym: undressed - not wearing clothes

Etymology

From the verb 'attire', from Old French 'atirer' (to equip, attire).
