facetiously

1. ADV

/ fəˈsiʃəsli / (AmE), / fəˈsiːʃəsli / (BrE)
in a manner that is intended to be humorous or not meant to be taken seriously; jokingly.

Examples

The episode in which Sancho Panza concludes the joke that is played on him when he is facetiously put in command of an "island "is one of the best in the film.

Synset Examples

I meant it facetiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jokingly - in a joking manner
  • Antonym: seriously - in a serious manner

Etymology

From English 'facetious' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; ultimately from French 'facetieux', from Latin 'facetia' (jest, wit).
