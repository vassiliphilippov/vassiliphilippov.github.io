charity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
an organization set up to provide help and raise money for those in need or to promote the public good

Examples

The writers' Gold Tee Award will go to John McAuliffe of Plainfield, N. J., and Palm Beach, Fla., for his sponsorship of charity tournaments.
The perplexing question still remains as to why the middle classes turn to the churches as a vehicle of social identity when their clubs and charities should fill the same need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonprofit - an organization not aiming to make a profit, often for social good

Etymology

From Old French 'charité', from Latin 'caritas' meaning dearness, high value, or affection.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəti / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
an activity or gift intended to help the public or those in need

Examples

Charity as she knew it was complex and reciprocal, and almost every roof she saw signified charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philanthropy - the desire to promote the welfare of others, often through generous donations

Etymology

From Old French 'charité', from Latin 'caritas' (dearness, affection, charity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.rɪ.ti / (BrE)
A kindly and forgiving attitude toward others; leniency or tolerance.

Examples

Charity as she knew it was complex and reciprocal, and almost every roof she saw signified charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward others
  • Antonym: harshness - severity or strictness toward others

Etymology

From Old French charité, from Latin caritas (dearness, affection), from carus (dear).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʃær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
An organization set up to provide help and raise money for those in need.

Examples

They like his charity balls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonprofit - an organization not intending to make a profit
  • Synonym: foundation - an institution that supports causes or people with funds

Etymology

From Old French *charité* (Christian love, charity), from Latin *caritas* (costliness, esteem, affection), from *carus* (dear).
