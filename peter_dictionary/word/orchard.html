orchard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrtʃərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːtʃəd / (BrE)
A piece of land planted with fruit trees.

Examples

The water was there, so much of it that it spread all through the dead orchard.
There was an artificial lake just out of sight in the first stand of trees, fed by a half dozen springs that popped out of the ground above the hillside orchard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grove - a small group of trees

Etymology

From Old English 'orceard', likely from Latin 'hortus' (garden) and a Germanic element meaning 'enclosure'.
