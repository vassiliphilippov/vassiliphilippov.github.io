unlace

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈleɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈleɪs / (BrE)
to loosen or undo the laces of something, such as shoes or clothing

Examples

He had just finished unlacing the right one when the telephone rang again.

Synset Examples

They untied the prisoner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lace - to fasten or bind with a lace
  • Synonym: untie - to undo a knot or fastened thing

Etymology

From un- (not) + lace (from Old French las, from Vulgar Latin *laceum, from Latin laqueus ‘noose, snare’).
