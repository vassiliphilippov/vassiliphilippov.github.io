yacht

1. NOUN

/ jɑt / (AmE), / jɒt / (BrE)
A large and often expensive boat or ship, propelled by sail or power, used for private cruising, racing, or pleasure trips.

Examples

These were the ships of His Majesty's Navy, herding the hulks of the East Indies merchants and the yachts and ketches of the loyalists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boat - a small vessel for traveling over water, usually not as large as a ship
  • Degree Higher: ship - a large vessel for transporting people or goods by sea

Etymology

From Dutch 'jacht' (short for 'jachtschip', literally ‘hunting ship’).
