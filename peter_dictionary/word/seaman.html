seaman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.mən / (AmE), / ˈsiː.mən / (BrE)
A person, especially a man, who works as a sailor on a ship.

Examples

The man who now commanded her had started the voyage as an ordinary seaman.
Hands on Bible, seaman Lodley and carpenter Staffe swore that Juet had tried to persuade them to keep muskets and swords in their cabins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship as part of the crew
  • Antonym: landsman - a person who is not familiar with the sea or sailing

Etymology

From Old English sæman, sæmann (sea + man).
