transmit

1. VERB

/ trænˈsmɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪt / (BrE)
to serve as a medium for conveying or spreading something such as light, sound, heat, a disease, or information from one place or person to another

Examples

Wound tumor virus is a leafhopper transmitted virus not easily transmissible by mechanical inoculation (Black, 1944; Brakke et al., 1954).
The rows of animals and birds, in particular, suggest awareness of Oriental animal friezes, transmitted perhaps via Syrian silver bowls and textiles, but the specific forms of these rows on local vases and metal products are nonetheless Greek.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convey - to transport or carry to a place
  • Synonym: carry - to support and move (someone or something) from one place to another
  • Antonym: receive - to be given, presented with, or paid (something)

Etymology

From Latin transmittere, from trans- (across) + mittere (to send).
