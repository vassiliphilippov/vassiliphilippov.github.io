distastefully

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈteɪstfəlli / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪstfəli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes dislike or disgust; in an unpleasant or offensive way.

Examples

He put the basket down distastefully, muttering softly and thoroughly disgusted with himself and his plan that had seemed so foolproof.

Synset Examples

a distastefully explicit report on the Royal couple's marital life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpleasantly - in an unpleasant manner
  • Antonym: tastefully - in a tasteful or pleasing manner

Etymology

From 'distasteful' + '-ly'; 'distasteful' from 'distaste' + '-ful'.
