click

1. NOUN

/ klɪk / (AmE), / klɪk / (BrE)
A short, sharp, light sound, typically made by a switch, button, or small metal object striking another.

Examples

The orchestra is far enough away from you that you miss the bow scrapes, valve clicks, and other noises incidental to playing.
Casey heard the click of the distant receiver before he could open his mouth, and it took him no more than three seconds to make his decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clack - a sharp, abrupt sound, especially from two hard objects striking
  • Synonym: snap - a sudden sharp sound, such as a small object breaking or being released
  • Degree Higher: clang - a loud resonant metallic sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself; early 17th century.

2. VERB

/ klɪk / (AmE), / klɪk / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a short, sharp sound, especially as a result of movement or striking

Examples

The secretary sighed with relief and then the telephone clicked in Rector's hand.
Out of long experience I have found that incidental figures and other objects like trees, logs, and bushes can be traced from the original sketch and moved about in the major areas on the final sheet until they occupy the right position, which I call' clicking'.
A second scene flashed before his mind, the interior of the garage at the new house and the young Bartlett girl turning startled to meet him, the dim dark and the sudden confusion and fear and then the brightness as Mae had clicked on the light.
As he crossed to the side of the stall, Curt drew his gun and clicked back the hammer.

Synset Examples

he clicked on the light
his arm was snapped forward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - make a sudden, sharp cracking sound
  • Degree Lower: tick - make a light, regular, repeated sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced.

3. VERB

/ klɪk / (AmE), / klɪk / (BrE)
to make a short, sharp sound like that made by a switch, a button, or two hard objects striking each other

Examples

Rachel clicked her tongue behind her teeth.

Synset Examples

The clock ticked away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - to make a light, regular, repeated sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, first recorded in the early 17th century.
