nestling

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛslɪŋ / (BrE)
A young bird that has not yet developed feathers or strength enough to leave the nest.

Examples

Jubal's conversations coming and going - Jubal's words troubled him most; he studied them, compared them with what he had been taught as a nestling, struggling to bridge between languages, the one he thought with and the one he was learning to think in.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fledgling - a young bird that has just fledged or left the nest

Etymology

From verb 'nestle' + -ing, referring to one who nestles (remains in nest).
