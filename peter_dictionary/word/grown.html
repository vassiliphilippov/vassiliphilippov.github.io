grown

1. ADJ

/ ɡroʊn / (AmE), / ɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) fully developed; mature.

Examples

He's been married and got this half grown kid.
This nettled the children into the revelation of exact truth, a sacrifice of their secret superiority over grown people, but a victory in the wide fields of perpetration and illegitimate accomplishment.

Synset Examples

an adult animal
a grown woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immature - not fully developed; not mature

Etymology

Past participle of 'grow', from Old English 'grōwan' (to grow).
