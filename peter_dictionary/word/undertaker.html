undertaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌteɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to arrange and manage funerals, prepare bodies for burial or cremation, and provide related services

Examples

"If you can fix it up with the undertaker ", returned the politician, "it's all right with me ".
That luscious tan... ... those ruby lips... ... and hair color so natural, only your undertaker knows for sure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortician - person trained to prepare the dead for burial or cremation and to arrange funerals
  • Synonym: funeral director - person responsible for managing funerals and related arrangements

Etymology

From 'undertake' (to take upon oneself) + '-er' (agent suffix); originally meaning one who undertakes tasks, later specialized to funerals.
