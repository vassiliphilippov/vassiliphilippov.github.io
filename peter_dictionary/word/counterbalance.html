counterbalance

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəbæləns / (BrE)
to offset or compensate for the effect of something by exerting an equal but opposite influence

Examples

The weekly loss is partly counterbalanced by 500 arrivals each week from West Germany, but the hard truth, says Crossman, is that "The closing off of East Berlin without interference from the West and with the use only of East German, as distinct from Russian, troops was a major Communist victory, which dealt West Berlin a deadly, possibly a fatal, blow.

Synset Examples

engineers will work to correct the effects or air resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensate - to make up for something by counteracting it
  • Synonym: offset - to balance the effect of something by exerting an opposite force

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'balance', from Old French 'balance', from Vulgar Latin *bilancia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌbæləns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəbæləns / (BrE)
A weight that balances another weight, especially to maintain equilibrium.

Examples

The sweep of space, the delicate counterbalance of the white masses, the over-all completeness and unity, the originality and imagination, all entitle it to be called an authentic masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterweight - a weight used to balance another weight

Etymology

From counter- (against) + balance, modeled after French 'contrebalancer'.
