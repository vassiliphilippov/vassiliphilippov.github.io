inculcation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kəlˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kʌlˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of teaching or instilling an idea, attitude, or habit by frequent instruction or repetition.

Examples

When we see the steady and methodical inculcation into humanity of the idea of man's worthlessness - until redeemed - the necessity of the Devil may become evident as a weapon, a weapon designed and used time and time again in every age to whip men into a surrender to a particular church or church-state ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge

Etymology

From Latin inculcare (to tread in, press in) via Medieval Latin inculcatio.
