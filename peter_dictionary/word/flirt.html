flirt

1. VERB

/ flɝt / (AmE), / flɜːt / (BrE)
to behave or talk in a playful or romantic way, often without serious intent

Examples

Once there had been big glittering rooms where Ulanys bowed and kissed the ladies' hands as they flirted from behind their fans.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: court - to try to win the affection of someone for marriage or romance

Etymology

Possibly from the Old French 'fleureter', meaning to talk sweetly.
