Delaware

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛləˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.ə.weə / (BrE)
a river in the eastern United States flowing south from southeastern New York, forming part of the borders of Pennsylvania and New Jersey, and emptying into Delaware Bay

Examples

South of Laurel Grove Cemetery, and below the junction of the Neversink and the Delaware, was the Tri-State Rock, from which Stevie could spy New Jersey and Pennsylvania, as well as New York, simply by spinning around on his heel.

Etymology

Named after Thomas West, 3rd Baron De La Warr, an early colonial governor of Virginia.
