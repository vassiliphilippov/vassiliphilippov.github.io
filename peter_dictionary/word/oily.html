oily

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔɪli / (AmE), / ˈɔɪli / (BrE)
Containing, covered with, or resembling oil or grease; greasy.

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.
Obviously hydrophobic (oleophilic) substances such as greases, oils, or particles having a greasy or oily surface are more at home in the center of a micelle than in the aqueous phase.
Related to micelle formation is the technologically important ability of detergent actives to congregate at oil water interfaces in such a manner that the polar (or ionized) end of the molecule is directed towards the aqueous phase and the hydrocarbon chain towards the oily phase.

Synset Examples

greasy hamburgers
oily fried potatoes
oleaginous seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greasy - containing or covered with grease; similar in meaning to 'oily'

Etymology

From oil + -y; oil from Old French *olie*, from Latin *oleum* (olive oil).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔɪli / (AmE), / ˈɔɪli / (BrE)
(of a person, their speech, or manner) unpleasantly smooth, flattering, or insincere; excessively ingratiating.

Examples

Brenner's voice was oily, but Summers was n't fooled.

Synset Examples

buttery praise
gave him a fulsome introduction
an oily sycophantic press agent
oleaginous hypocrisy
smarmy self-importance
the unctuous Uriah Heep
soapy compliments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuous - excessively or ingratiatingly flattering; oily
  • Synonym: smarmy - excessively flattering or insincerely polite
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine, without pretense or falseness
