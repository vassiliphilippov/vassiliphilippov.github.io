deer

1. NOUN

/ dɪr / (AmE), / dɪə / (BrE)
A large, graceful, hoofed mammal, the male of which usually has solid, branching antlers that are shed annually, found in forests and grasslands worldwide.

Examples

He conjures herds of deer, and wild birds crowding the air.
Muller, nakedly exposed at the bright window like a deer pinned in a car's headlights, threw down the rifle and turned to jump from the table; his face wore a look of outrage.

Etymology

Old English *dēor* (wild animal), related to Old High German *tior*, Old Norse *dýr* (animal).
