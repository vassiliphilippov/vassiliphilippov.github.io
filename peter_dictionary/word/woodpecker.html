woodpecker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌpɛkɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌpɛkə / (BrE)
A bird with strong claws, a stiff tail for climbing, and a hard, chisel-like bill used for boring into wood to find insects.

Examples

Most species seem to prefer a ready-made hollow such as a deserted mouse nest, a bird house, or the hole made by a woodpecker; some show a definite liking for making their nest in moss.

Etymology

Formed from "wood" + "pecker", describing its habit of pecking at wood.
