endowed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdaʊd / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊd / (BrE)
having a particular quality or feature, especially one given by nature or inheritance

Examples

Man, we are told, is endowed with reason and is capable of distinguishing good from bad.

Synset Examples

a well-endowed college
endowed with good eyesight
endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gifted - having a special ability or talent
  • Antonym: deprived - lacking the possession of something

Etymology

Past participle of 'endow', from Old French 'endouer', from Latin 'dotare' (to endow, portion).
