foil

1. NOUN

/ fɔɪl / (AmE), / fɔɪl / (BrE)
A very thin and flexible sheet of metal, usually aluminium or tin, used for wrapping, covering, or other purposes.

Examples

Hans cut the foil off finally and unscrewed the cap.
The opening action is obtained when a capacitor, charged to high voltage, is suddenly discharged through the foil.

Etymology

2. VERB

/ fɔɪl / (AmE), / fɔɪl / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to enhance or emphasize the qualities of something by providing a strong contrast with it.

Examples

Long observation has taught Mason that most landscape can be reduced to three essential planes: a foreground in sharp focus - either a light area with dark accents or a dark one with lights; a middle distance often containing the major motif; and a background, usually a silhouetted form foiled against the sky.

Synset Examples

In this picture, the figures are foiled against the background

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - difference that emphasizes distinct qualities

Etymology

From Old French *foille*, from Latin *folium* (leaf), originally referring to a thin leaf of metal placed under a gem to enhance its brightness.
