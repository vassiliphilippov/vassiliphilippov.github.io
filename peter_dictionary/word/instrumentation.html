instrumentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.strə.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strə.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of providing, arranging, or using instruments, especially for a particular purpose such as a scientific procedure or a musical composition

Examples

In orchestration, he stretches the limits of instrumentation with good judgment and a fine imagination for color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the action of arranging something in a particular way, especially for music

Etymology

From French 'instrumentation' and Latin 'instrumentum' (tool, instrument).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.strə.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strə.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the set or system of instruments or measuring devices used for a particular purpose, especially in scientific or technical contexts

Examples

A balanced resistance bridge and a pen recorder are all the electronic instrumentation needed.
A simplified version of the instrumentation for this procedure is shown in Fig. 2.
The programs in infrared spectroscopy are undergoing reorientation toward wavelength standards in the far infrared, the application of infrared techniques to solid state studies, and increased emphasis on high resolution instrumentation.

Etymology

From French 'instrumentation', from 'instrument' (tool, device) + '-ation'.
