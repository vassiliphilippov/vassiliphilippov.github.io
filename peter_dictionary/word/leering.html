leering

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
Looking or smiling in an unpleasant, sly, or malicious way, often suggesting sexual interest or ill intent.

Examples

Suddenly a treble auto horn tootley-toot-tootled, and, thumbing hopefully, I saw emergent in windshield flash: red lips, streaming silk of blonde hair and - ah, trembling confusion of hope, apprehension, despair - the leering face of old Herry.

Synset Examples

she had run in fear of...his evil leering eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leer - to look or smile in an unpleasant, sly way
  • Degree Lower: smirking - smiling in an irritatingly smug, conceited way

Etymology

From the verb 'leer' (to look with a sly or malicious expression), probably from Middle Dutch 'leren' (to teach, read), influenced by obsolete English leer (cheek).
