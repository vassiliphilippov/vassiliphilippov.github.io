flocculate

1. VERB

/ ˈflɑːkyəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈflɒkyəˌleɪt / (BrE)
to form or cause to form into small clumps or masses, especially in a liquid

Examples

As shown in Figure 3, the protoplasm of other fibers was pale, granular, or flocculated and invaded by phagocytes.

Synset Examples

the protoplasms flocculated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulate - to change from a liquid to a thickened or solid state
  • Synonym: precipitate - to cause a substance to be deposited in solid form from a solution

Etymology

From Latin *flocculus* (a small tuft of wool) via Medieval Latin *flocculatus* (having tufts).
