doorbell

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈdɔːbɛl / (BrE)
A push button or electric device near an outer door that makes a ringing or buzzing sound to alert those inside when pressed.

Examples

Like the bell at Mass, the doorbell was pitched too high.
[DOORBELL CHIMING] [SINGING SOFTLY IN ITALIAN] [BABY CRYING] [CONTINUES CRYING]

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzzer - an electric signaling device that makes a buzzing sound
  • Synonym: chime - a device that makes a musical ringing sound, often used for doorbells

Etymology

Formed from 'door' + 'bell'.
