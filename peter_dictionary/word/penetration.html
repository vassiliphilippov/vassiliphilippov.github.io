penetration

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability to understand or perceive things deeply and clearly; keen insight

Examples

Returning once again to the Schnabel reissue, I am beguiled anew by the magnificence of this pianist's musical penetration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insight - the capacity to gain an accurate and deep understanding
  • Synonym: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses or mind
  • Antonym: superficiality - lack of depth of knowledge or thought

Etymology

From Latin 'penetratio', from 'penetrare' (to enter, penetrate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of entering into or passing through something

Examples

Certain other properties of small particles, in addition to those already mentioned in connection with penetration of the respiratory tract, are noteworthy in defense considerations.

Synset Examples

the penetration of upper management by women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of entering or going in
  • Antonym: exit - the act of going out or leaving a place

Etymology

From Latin 'penetratio', from 'penetrare' (to enter, to penetrate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
A military attack or operation that succeeds in entering enemy territory.

Examples

Once the full extent of this Russian military penetration of Cuba was clear, President Kennedy announced we would take whatever action was appropriate to prevent this, even if we had to go it alone.
A naval blockade would be thoroughly in line with the Monroe Doctrine, would be a relatively simple operation to carry out, and would bring an abrupt end to Soviet penetration of our hemisphere ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incursion - an invasion or attack, especially a sudden one, into foreign territory
  • Synonym: invasion - an instance of invading a country or region with an armed force

Etymology

From Latin *penetratio* (a piercing, entering) from *penetrare* (to enter, pierce).
