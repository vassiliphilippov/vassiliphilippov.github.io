classical

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæs.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈklæs.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to the culture, art, or literature of ancient Greece or Rome, or to the most highly developed stage of an earlier civilization.

Examples

In tularemia produced by aerosol exposure, one would not expect to find the classical ulcer of "rabbit fever "on a finger.
The portable record player with a pile of classical records beside it.
The classical lines of the church which Napoleon thought of as a Temple of Glory, dominated all the scene where we sat.
With classical art, all is settled.
In classical drama, such problems do not arise.

Synset Examples

classic Cinese pottery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - Judged over a period of time to be of the highest quality and outstanding of its kind

Etymology

From Latin classicus (of the highest class), originally referring to citizens of the highest class in ancient Rome.

2. ADJ

/ ˈklæs.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈklæs.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
regarded as representing an established standard; authoritative or exemplary in quality and tradition, often serving as a model of excellence.

Examples

The breakdown of classical structures of meaning in all realms of western culture has given rise to several generations of artists who have documented the disintegrative processes.
The person of the artist becomes a final bastion of meaning in a world rendered meaningless by the march of events and the decay of classical religious and philosophical systems.

Synset Examples

the definitive work on Greece
classical methods of navigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - judged over a period of time to be of the highest quality and outstanding of its kind

Etymology

From Latin classicus (belonging to the highest class of citizens), later used to describe the artists and authors regarded as models of excellence.
