evolutionary

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌiːvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from the process of evolution; describing something that concerns or is produced by gradual development or change, especially in biology.

Examples

Again the student of evolutionary biology will find a fascinating, if to our minds grotesque, anticipation of the theory of chance variations and the natural elimination of the unfit in Lucretius, who in turn seems to have borrowed the concept from the philosopher Empedocles.

Synset Examples

evolutionary biology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolutional - relating to or characteristic of evolution

Etymology

From evolution + -ary; "evolution" from Latin evolutio (an unrolling).
