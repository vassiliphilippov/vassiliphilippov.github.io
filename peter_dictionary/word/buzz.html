buzz

1. NOUN

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
a continuous, low, humming sound produced by rapid vibration, such as that made by bees, machinery, or electronics

Examples

With these gadgets - impressive to the gullible because of their flashing light bulbs, ticks, and buzzes - he then carries out a vicious medical con game, capitalizing on people's respect for the electrical and atomic wonders of our scientific age.

Synset Examples

the buzz of a bumble bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - a low, steady continuous sound
  • Synonym: drone - a deep, continuous humming sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound it describes.

2. NOUN

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
a feeling or situation of excitement and lively activity, especially as created by social interest or gossip

Examples

All these emotions were screwed up to new heights when, after acceptance and the first rehearsals, there ensued such a buzz of excitement among Parisian music lovers that Duclos had to come running to Rousseau to inform him that the news had reached the superintendent of the King's amusements, and that he was now demanding that the work be offered first at the royal summer palace of Fontainebleau.

Synset Examples

the buzz of excitement was so great that a formal denial was issued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrill - a sudden feeling of excitement and pleasure
  • Synonym: stir - a commotion or feeling of excitement
  • Antonym: calm - a state or period of quiet and tranquility

3. VERB

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
to make a continuous, low, humming or vibrating sound, like that of a bee or a small motor

Examples

Yet if he were not there, they would have missed him, as they would have missed the sounds of bees buzzing against the screen door in early June; or the smell of thick tomato paste - the ripe smell that was both sweet and sour - rising up from aluminum trays wrapped in fly dotted cheesecloth.
[DOORBELL BUZZES]

Synset Examples

bees were buzzing around the hive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - make a low, continuous steady sound
  • Synonym: drone - make a dull, continuous, low sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, first recorded in Middle English.

4. VERB

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to fly low over a place, especially in a way that startles people or draws attention.

Examples

Inland, outboard motorists welcome cooler weather and the chance to buzz over Colorado River sandbars and Lake Mead.

Synset Examples

Planes buzzed the crowds in the square

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hover - to remain in one place in the air

5. VERB

/ bʌz / (AmE), / bʌz / (BrE)
to be full of activity or excitement, often with people or things moving around and making noise

Examples

She picked her own Middle-Eastern friends from the flock of ardent Egyptians that buzzed around her.

Synset Examples

This office is buzzing with activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - be filled with a low continuous noise or activity

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of buzzing.
