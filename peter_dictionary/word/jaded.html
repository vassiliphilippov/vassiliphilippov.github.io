jaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪdɪd / (BrE)
No longer interested or excited, usually because of having experienced too much of something; dulled or wearied by excess or overindulgence.

Examples

The jaded amorist conjured up pictures of the blasphemous rites with relish.
Aging but still precocious, French feline enfant terrible Francoisette Lagoon has succeeded in shocking jaded old Paris again, this time with a sexy ballet scenario called The Lascivious Interlude, the story of a nymphomaniac trip-hammer operator who falls hopelessly in love with a middle-aged steam shovel.

Synset Examples

the amoral, jaded, bored upper classes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: world-weary - tired of the world from too much experience or exposure
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From obsolete jade (to tire, weary), likely from earlier sense of jade as a worn-out horse.
