hair

1. NOUN

/ hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
The fine, threadlike strands growing from the skin of humans or animals, especially those covering the head or body.

Examples

It lacks pollen baskets and possesses only a large number of long, branched hairs on its legs, on which the pollen grains will collect.
In litters of eight mice from similar parents, the number of mice with straight instead of wavy hair is an integer from 0 to 8.
It is natural from the marksman's viewpoint to call a bull's-eye a success, but in the mice example it is arbitrary which category corresponds to straight hair in a mouse.
Teachers - men who wore brown suits and had gray hair and pleasant smiles - came to their table to talk shop and to be introduced to Scotty and Rachel.
Scotty regarded the ear and the grizzled hair around it with a moment of interest.

Synset Examples

he combed his hair
each hair consists of layers of dead keratinized cells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur - the thick hair covering the bodies of some animals
  • Antonym: bald - lacking hair on the scalp or body

Etymology

From Old English hǣr, related to German Haar and Dutch haar.

2. NOUN

/ hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
Any of the fine, thread-like strands growing from the skin of a mammal, especially on the head of a person or animal.

Examples

I don'’t like to see you come out to this clean country in your oily hair dressed up in those silk suits, trying to pass yourselves off as decent Americans.

Synset Examples

there is a hair in my soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur - soft thick hair covering the bodies of some animals
  • Antonym: bald - lacking hair on the scalp

Etymology

From Old English hær, related to Dutch haar and German Haar.
