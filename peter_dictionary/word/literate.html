literate

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.ət / (BrE)
able to read and write; having the ability to read and write written language.

Examples

Though it may exist in either literate or illiterate societies, it assumes a role of true cultural importance only in the latter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illiterate - unable to read and write

Etymology

From Latin 'litteratus' (learned, lettered), from 'littera' (letter).
