mendacious

1. ADJ

/ mɛnˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɛnˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Not telling the truth; habitually lying or deceptive.

Examples

It's a wonder, really, to how much mendacious trouble Larkin puts himself to sell the Jerebohms that preposterous manse.

Synset Examples

a mendacious child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth
  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; deceiving or fraudulent

Etymology

From Latin 'mendax, mendacis' (lying, false).
