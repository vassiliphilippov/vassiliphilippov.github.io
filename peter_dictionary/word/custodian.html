custodian

1. NOUN

/ kʌˈstoʊdiən / (AmE), / kʌˈstəʊdiən / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for taking care of a building, grounds, or animals and ensuring their proper upkeep and supervision.

Examples

Indeed, of all that is his every man is by nature and reason and therefore by conscience obligated to regard himself as a custodian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - person employed to look after a building or land
  • Synonym: janitor - person employed to clean and maintain a building, especially a school or office building

Etymology

From Latin 'custodia' (guardianship), from 'custos' (guardian).
