profusely

1. ADV

/ prəˈfjusli / (AmE), / prəˈfjuːsli / (BrE)
In large amounts; to a great or excessive degree.

Examples

After years of digging, nights and weekends, he put together the big, profusely illustrated book, Of Garryowen and Glory, which is probably the most complete history of any military unit.

Synset Examples

they were abundantly supplied with food
he thanked her profusely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundantly - in large quantities; plentifully
  • Antonym: sparsely - in small amounts or numbers; thinly distributed

Etymology

From Latin 'profusus' (lavish, profuse), from 'profundere' (to pour forth).
