pie

1. NOUN

/ paɪ / (AmE), / paɪ / (BrE)
A baked dish consisting of a pastry-lined pan filled with sweet or savory ingredients, often covered with a pastry top.

Examples

"So that's sculpture ", commented Argiento wryly, when he had sluiced down the floor for a week, "making mud pies ".
He's got his fingers in a lot of pies, I hear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tart - open pastry dish, usually with a fruit or custard filling

Etymology

From Middle English *pye*, of uncertain origin, possibly from Old French *pie* (magpie), by analogy with mixed contents.
