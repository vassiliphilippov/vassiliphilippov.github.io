paucity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːsəti / (AmE), / ˈpɔːsəti / (BrE)
A very small or insufficient amount or number of something; scarcity.

Examples

Only the amethystine python and the anaconda must be excluded for lack or paucity of data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'paucitas' (fewness), from 'paucus' (few).
