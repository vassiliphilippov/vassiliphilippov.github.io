keyboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkiːbɔːd / (BrE)
A set of keys arranged for operating a musical instrument, computer, or other device by manual pressing.

Examples

Turning to the more modern versions, Curzon's (London) offers the most sophisticated keyboard work.
(Babin has acquired some of Schnabel's keyboard manner, but his playing is of limited insight.)

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: keypad - small set of keys, usually numeric, on a device
  • Synonym: piano - musical instrument with a keyboard

Etymology

From 'key' + 'board', originally in reference to piano-like musical instruments.
