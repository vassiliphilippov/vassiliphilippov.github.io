menacing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛnəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛnəsɪŋ / (BrE)
suggesting the presence of danger or the intention to harm; threatening

Examples

Giant, menacing, supernatural form.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds
his tone became menacing
ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior
ugly black clouds
the situation became ugly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threatening - having a hostile or intentionally frightening quality
  • Synonym: sinister - giving the impression that something harmful or evil is happening or will happen
  • Synonym: forbidding - unfriendly or threatening in appearance

Etymology

From English noun 'menace' (threat), from Old French 'manace', from Latin 'minacia' (threats), from 'minax' (jutting out, threatening).
