discard

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd / (BrE)
to get rid of something that is no longer useful or wanted

Examples

Following single dose treatment, milk should be discarded for 4 days following treatment.
One by one, the openings in the coast that promised a passage through had been explored and discarded.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispose - to get rid of something
  • Antonym: retain - to keep in possession

Etymology

From Old French *descarder* (to dismiss, reject), from *des-* (dis-) + *carder* (to throw away a card in a game).
