emboss

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑs / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒs / (BrE)
to decorate (a surface) with a raised design or pattern

Examples

Both, of course, were remarkable feats and further embossed the fact that baseball rightfully is the national pastime.

Synset Examples

embossed stationery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: engrave - to cut or carve (a text or design) on a surface
  • Synonym: imprint - to mark or stamp with pressure

Etymology

From Old French 'embosser' (to raise in relief), from 'en-' (in) + 'bosse' (boss, swelling).
