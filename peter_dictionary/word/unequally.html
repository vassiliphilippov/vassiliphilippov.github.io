unequally

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈiːkwəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːkwəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not equal; in a manner that treats parties or things differently or unfairly.

Examples

To guard against the tyranny of a numerical majority, Calhoun developed his theory of "concurrent majority ", which, he said, "by giving to each portion of the community which may be unequally affected by the action of government, a negative on the others, prevents all partial or local legislation ".
that the Court order does not unequally affect the Southern region?

Synset Examples

profits were distributed unevenly
angry at being dealt with so unequally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equally - in an equal or identical manner

Etymology

From 'unequal' + '-ly', patterning after standard English adverb formation.
