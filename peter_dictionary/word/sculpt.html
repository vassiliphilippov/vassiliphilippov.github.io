sculpt

1. VERB

/ skʌlpt / (AmE), / skʌlpt / (BrE)
to create a figure or design by carving or shaping materials such as stone, wood, or similar substances

Examples

The dumbbell variation develops a most classically sculptured outline to the pecs.
They look as if they had been sculptured with an unsharpened chisel.

Synset Examples

sculpt a swan out of a block of ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carve - to cut a hard material to produce an object or design
  • Synonym: model - to shape a material into a form

Etymology

From Latin 'sculpere' (to carve), via French 'sculpter'.
