prevalence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛvələns / (AmE), / ˈprɛvələns / (BrE)
The fact or condition of being widespread or common in a particular area or at a particular time.

Examples

The expense of this type of organization in religious life, when one recalls the number of city churches which deteriorated beyond repair before being abandoned, raises fundamental questions about the principle of Protestant survival in a mobile society; nonetheless, the prevalence of economic integration in congregations illumines the nature of the Protestant development.

Synset Examples

he was surprised by the prevalence of optimism about the future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidence - the rate or frequency of occurrence, especially of something undesirable
  • Antonym: rarity - the state of being rare or infrequent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praevalentia, from Latin praevalēre (to be stronger).
