testimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪməni / (BrE)
something that serves as evidence of a fact, quality, or event, especially as proof or demonstration of something

Examples

But the most impressive testimony to Schnabel's distinction as a teacher is reflected by the individuality which marks each student's approach as distinctly his own.
There is much talk in theological circles about the "Church as Mission "and the "Church's Mission "; theologians have been stressing the fact that the Church does not exist for its own sake but as a testimony in the world for the healing of the world.

Synset Examples

his effort was testimony to his devotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or objects that help to prove something
  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement
  • Antonym: contradiction - the act of saying the opposite or denying a statement

Etymology

From Latin 'testimonium' (proof, evidence), from 'testis' (witness).
