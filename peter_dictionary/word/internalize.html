internalize

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an attitude, belief, or value) part of one's own way of thinking or understanding, so that it influences behavior or feelings

Examples

Even the non church members - the freewheelers, marginal religionists and so on - have the values of Christian civilization internalized in them.
"Culturally induced social cohesion resulting from common norms and values internalized by members of the group "is operative in the boundary maintenance of the group as well as in the process of socialization.

Synset Examples

internalize a belief

Related Terms

  • Antonym: externalize - to project (something internal, such as feelings or beliefs) outward

Etymology

From Latin 'internus' (inward, internal) + '-ize'.
