suffrage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfrɪdʒ / (BrE)
The right to vote in political elections, especially as established or extended by law.

Examples

Some date it from woman suffrage, others from when women first began to challenge men in the marketplace, still others from the era of the emancipated flapper and bathtub gin.

Synset Examples

American women got the vote in 1920

Related Terms

  • Synonym: franchise - the right to vote in public elections
  • Antonym: disenfranchisement - the state of being deprived of the right to vote

Etymology

From Latin suffragium (vote, support).
