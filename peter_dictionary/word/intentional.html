intentional

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
Done on purpose; deliberate and planned, rather than accidental.

Examples

Biological warfare is the intentional use of living microorganisms or their toxic products for the purpose of destroying or reducing the military effectiveness of man.
Measurements of skins are of little value; every snake hide is noticeably longer than its carcass and intentional stretching presents no difficulty to the unscrupulous explorer.

Synset Examples

intentional damage
a knowing attempt to defraud
a willful waste of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • Antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not planned

Etymology

From Latin intentio (stretching, purpose) + -al.
