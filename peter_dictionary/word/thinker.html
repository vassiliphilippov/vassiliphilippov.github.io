thinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkə / (BrE)
A person who is known for deep thought or intellectual activity, often someone whose ideas have significant influence.

Examples

Again, Henley's attitude of defiance which colors his ideal of self mastery is far from characteristic of a Stoic thinker like Marcus Aurelius, whose gentle acquiescence is almost Christian, comparable to the patience expressed in Milton's sonnet on his own blindness.

Synset Examples

the great minds of the 17th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - a person with a highly developed intellect
  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy

Etymology

From Middle English 'thinken' (to think) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
