ferromagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɛroʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or exhibiting ferromagnetism; having the property of being strongly attracted to magnets or capable of being magnetized.

Examples

Since f is ferromagnetic**, we felt that any results obtained from the magnetically contaminated **f would be suspect.
Magnetic analyses by R. G. Meisenheimer of this laboratory indicated no ferromagnetic impurities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paramagnetic - relating to weak magnetic attraction
  • Degree Lower: diamagnetic - not attracted by a magnet

Etymology

From Latin 'ferrum' (iron) and 'magnetic' (relating to magnetism).
