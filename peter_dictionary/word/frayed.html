frayed

1. ADJ

/ freɪd / (AmE), / freɪd / (BrE)
Worn at the edges or unravelled, typically from use or age.

Examples

A soldier held the end of a frayed rope.

Synset Examples

frayed cuffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tattered - torn or ragged, especially at the edges

Etymology

From Old French *freer* (to rub, chafe), from Latin *fricare* (to rub).
