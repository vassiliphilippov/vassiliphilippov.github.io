revision

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a version of something, especially a text or document, that has been rewritten or edited

Examples

Two tax revision bills were passed.

Synset Examples

the rewrite was much better

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edition - a particular version or release of a text
  • Synonym: draft - a preliminary version of a written work

Etymology

From Latin 'revisio' (a seeing again), from 'revidere': re- (again) + videre (to see).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the process of changing, improving, or updating something, especially a piece of writing, a plan, or an opinion

Examples

When the words are used, we are never sure which of the traditional meanings the user may have in mind, or to what extent his revisions and rejections of former understandings correspond to ours.
Because Christianity teaches not only salvation in history, but salvation by the history of Christ, such an interpretation of death would require a drastic revision of the Christian understanding of the work of Christ.
It seems time to consider a revision of operational procedures for lexicostatistic studies on a more humble, solid, and limited basis.

Synset Examples

it would require a drastic revision of his opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amendment - a minor change or addition to a text or piece of legislation
  • Synonym: alteration - the action or process of changing something

Etymology

From Latin revisio (a looking back), from revidere (to see again).

3. NOUN

/ rəˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the process of changing or correcting something written to improve it or make it more accurate

Examples

You may find certain coverage costing much more than is economically feasible, thereby alerting you to desirable revisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edit - to prepare written material by correcting or revising it
  • Synonym: draft - a preliminary version of a written work

Etymology

From Latin 'revisio' (a seeing again), from 'revidere' (to see again).
