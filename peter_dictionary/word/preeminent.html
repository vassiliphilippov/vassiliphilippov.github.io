preeminent

1. ADJ

/ priˈɛmɪnənt / (AmE), / priˈɛmɪnənt / (BrE)
More important, skilled, or distinguished than others in a particular group, field, or area; surpassing all others.

Examples

This, however, did not bar finding that du Pont had become pre-eminent as a supplier of automotive fabrics and finishes to General Motors; that these products constituted a "line of commerce "within the meaning of the Clayton Act; that General Motors' share of the market for these products was substantial; and that competition for this share of the market was endangered by the financial relationship between the two concerns:

Synset Examples

our greatest statesmen
the country's leading poet
a preeminent archeologist

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eminent - well-known and respected; standing out in quality or achievement
  • Degree Lower: distinguished - recognized for excellence; notable
  • Degree Higher: supreme - highest in rank or authority; above all others

Etymology

From Latin 'praeeminens', present participle of 'praeeminere' (to project, stand out), from 'prae-' (before) + 'eminent' (standing out).
