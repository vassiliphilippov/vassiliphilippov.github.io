grovel

1. VERB

/ ˈɡrɑːvəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒvəl / (BrE)
to behave in an excessively submissive or self-abasing manner, especially to gain favor or forgiveness

Examples

What had he thought of, to go to John, grovel and beg understanding?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fawn - to flatter or act submissively to gain someone's favor
  • Synonym: cringe - to behave in a servile or overly submissive way

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse grūfa (to lie face downward) + -le (frequentative suffix).
