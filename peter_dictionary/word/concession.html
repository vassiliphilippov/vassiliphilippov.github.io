concession

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a contractual right granted to operate a business or service, especially within another company's property or at an event

Examples

Will your union accept seniority concessions in assigning work for older or disabled employees?
At last, even the controlled Torrio was unable to hold still, and he tentatively suggested that O' Banion should take a percentage in the Stickney brothels in return for one from his Cicero beer concession.

Synset Examples

he got the beer concession at the ball park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: franchise - the right or license granted to sell a company's goods or services in a particular place

Etymology

From Latin concessio (granting, yielding), via Old French concession.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of conceding or yielding something, often in response to demands or as a compromise

Examples

The secretary's tone indicated that an appointment at such short notice was a concession for which Madden should be duly grateful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yielding - giving way under pressure or granting something
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of declining to yield or grant

Etymology

From Latin concessio (allowance, granting), from concedere (to concede).
