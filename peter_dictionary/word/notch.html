notch

1. NOUN

/ nɑtʃ / (AmE), / nɒtʃ / (BrE)
A V-shaped cut or indentation made on an edge or surface.

Examples

So choosing a good tree, he clambered up into it, found a comfortable notch, and curled up in it to sleep, like the tousled bear he was, with his hands across his chest, as though surfeited with honey.

Synset Examples

mandibular notch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nick - a small cut or notch

Etymology

Possibly from Old French 'noche' (a notch), of unknown origin.

2. VERB

/ nɑtʃ / (AmE), / nɒtʃ / (BrE)
to cut or make a notch or indentation in the edge or surface of something

Examples

After notching it for the keelson, chines and battens, the half-inch plywood transom is secured to it with glue and the same type nails.
The frames are notched only for the keelson and the chines.
If notched for the battens, they would require more work, be weakened and limber holes would have to be bored so that bilge water could flow through.

Synset Examples

notch the rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nick - to make a small cut or indentation
  • Antonym: smooth - to make free of notches or roughness

Etymology

Probably from Middle English nocche (a notch); related to Old French noche.
