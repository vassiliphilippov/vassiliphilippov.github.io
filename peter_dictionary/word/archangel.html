archangel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑɹkeɪndʒəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːkeɪndʒəl / (BrE)
A high-ranking angel in the celestial hierarchy, typically considered to be above other angels.

Examples

Fosterite bishops, after secret conclave, announced the Church's second Major Miracle: Supreme Bishop Digby had been translated bodily to Heaven and spot-promoted to Archangel, ranking with-but-after Archangel Foster.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: angel - a spiritual being acting as a messenger of God

Etymology

From Late Latin 'archangelus', from Greek 'arkhángelos', meaning 'chief angel'.
