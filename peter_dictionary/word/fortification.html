fortification

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrtəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a strong structure, such as a wall or mound, built to defend a place against attack

Examples

They possessed no outer fortifications, no hard shells of confidence; they had enough difficulty getting from day to day, let alone having an awful crime thrust upon them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampart - a broad embankment raised as a fortification
  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification

Etymology

From Latin 'fortificatio', from 'fortificare' (to strengthen a place).
