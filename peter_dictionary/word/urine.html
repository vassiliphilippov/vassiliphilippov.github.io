urine

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrɪn / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪn / (BrE)
the pale yellowish liquid waste that is excreted by the kidneys and passed out of the body through the urethra

Examples

The place smelled strongly of rank, fertile earth, rotting wood and urine.

Synset Examples

there was blood in his urine
the child had to make water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pee - informal term for urine

Etymology

From Latin 'urina' (urine), from Proto-Indo-European root *awer- (to moisten, pour).
