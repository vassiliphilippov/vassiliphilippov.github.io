snort

1. VERB

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
To inhale a drug or other substance through the nose, typically for recreational purposes.

Examples

Watson snorted and then laughed aloud.

Synset Examples

The addict was snorting cocaine almost every day
the kids were huffing glue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huff - to inhale fumes from chemicals or drugs for intoxication

2. VERB

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
to make a sudden, forceful sound with the nose or mouth to show contempt, annoyance, or amusement

Examples

For a moment she held her face to the empty doorway; then she snorted and groped for her fork.
"The way you were careful "? he snorted.
[SNORTS]

Synset Examples

she snorted her disapproval of the proposed bridegroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoff - to speak to someone or about something in a scornfully derisive or mocking way
  • Synonym: sneer - to smile or speak in a contemptuous or mocking manner

Etymology

Of imitative origin, representing the sound made.

3. VERB

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
to make a sudden, harsh noise by forcing air out through the nose, often as a sign of contempt, anger, or excitement

Examples

The horse continued to snort.
Even Black's old crowbait began to snort, and from the house Black yelled, "Jess!

Synset Examples

The critic snorted contemptuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow - to expel air forcefully through the nose or mouth

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin; first recorded in the 15th century.
