rehash

1. VERB

/ riˈhæʃ / (AmE), / riˈhæʃ / (BrE)
to present or use something again with little or no change, especially in a way that lacks originality

Examples

The work's two movements, one melodically sentimental, the other brightly capricious, are clever enough in a Ravel-like style, but they rehash a wornout idiom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reuse - to use something again
  • Synonym: recycle - to process and use again

Etymology

From re- (again) + hash (to chop up); hash from French 'hacher', to chop.
