mica

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.kə / (BrE)
a group of minerals consisting of hydrous silicates of aluminum, potassium, or other elements, characterized by their ability to split easily into thin, flexible sheets and used especially as electrical insulators

Examples

And some mica.

Etymology

From Latin *micare* (to glitter).
