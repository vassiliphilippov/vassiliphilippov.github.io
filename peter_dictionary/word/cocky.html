cocky

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑki / (AmE), / ˈkɒki / (BrE)
Confident in an overly bold or arrogant way; excessively self-assertive.

Examples

He behaved publicly with a cocky, swaggering truculence that offended their vulpine Latin minds, and behaved towards them personally with an unimpressed insolence that enraged them beneath their blandness.
Even the mess sergeant, Bill Brown, a dapper, cocky transfer from an airborne division, went out on the range.
Look at Castro now - cockier than ever with arms and agents to threaten the Americas.
Cocky as hell.

Synset Examples

a very cocky young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble estimate of one's abilities

Etymology

Probably from cock (the bird), suggesting a strutting or self-important manner.
