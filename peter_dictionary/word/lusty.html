lusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌsti / (AmE), / ˈlʌsti / (BrE)
full of strong, healthy energy; vigorous and robust in body or mind.

Examples

Time plays an essential part in our mortality, and suddenly for no reason he could imagine (or admit) the image of Peg laughing filled his mind - so desirable, so lusty, so full of nuances of pleasure and joy.

Synset Examples

a hearty glow of health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearty - showing strong, healthy energy or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
  • Antonym: frail - physically weak or delicate

Etymology

From Old English 'lustig', meaning 'pleasant, cheerful', derived from 'lust' (pleasure, desire).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌsti / (AmE), / ˈlʌsti / (BrE)
Full of strong, vigorous passion or enthusiasm; robustly energetic.

Examples

Later, riding in for some lusty enjoyment of the liquor and professional ladies of Cheyenne, he laid claim to the killing with the vague insinuations he made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief
  • Synonym: vigorous - strong, healthy, and full of energy
  • Antonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Middle English *lusti*, from *lust* (pleasure, desire) + *-y*.
