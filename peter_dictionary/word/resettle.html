resettle

1. VERB

/ riˈsɛtəl / (AmE), / riˈsetl / (BrE)
to settle or cause to settle in a new place, especially after being made to leave a previous home

Examples

Here the peoples spoke the tongue of Iceland because that island had gotten the jump on the Hawaiian Americans who were busy resettling North America and the western half of South America after the Apocalyptic War.

Synset Examples

The immigrants had to resettle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relocate - to move to a new place and establish one's home or business there

Etymology

From re- (again) + settle, originating in Middle English from Old English 'setlan'.
