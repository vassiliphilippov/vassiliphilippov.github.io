prolonged

1. ADJ

/ prəˈlɔŋd / (AmE), / prəˈlɒŋd / (BrE)
Continuing for a long time or longer than usual; extended in duration.

Examples

Another source of NBC pride was its rare film clip of Bix Beiderbecke, but this view of the great trumpeter flew by so fast that a prolonged wink would have blotted out the entire glimpse.
The element of public danger which enters so largely into architectural certification, however, would demand a prolonged study of structure.

Synset Examples

a drawn-out argument
an extended discussion
a lengthy visit from her mother-in-law
a prolonged and bitter struggle
protracted negotiations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-lived - lasting for only a short time
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period
  • Antonym: brief - of short duration
  • Synonym: extended - made longer or greater in duration or size
  • Synonym: protracted - lasting for a long time or longer than expected

Etymology

From the verb 'prolong' (from Latin 'prolongare', from 'pro-' meaning 'forth' and 'longus' meaning 'long').
