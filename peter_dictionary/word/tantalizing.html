tantalizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæn.t̬əˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæn.tə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely appealing or tempting, especially by arousing desire or expectation.

Examples

I felt superior to the neighborhood friends I was leaving behind, felt older than my years, and was full of compliments for myself as I headed into the subway that was carrying its packs of passengers out of that dull borough and into the unstable, tantalizing excitement of Manhattan.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing aroma
a tempting repast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempting - attractive or appealing, especially in a way that makes people want to have or do something

Etymology

From the myth of Tantalus in Greek mythology, who was tormented by being kept just out of reach of desirable things.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæn.t̬əˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæn.təˌlaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Arousing desire or interest by presenting something that is attractive but out of reach or difficult to obtain.

Examples

First, they wanted to clarify a tantalizing, bizarre enigma.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing taste of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempting - attractive or appealing but not necessarily unattainable
  • Synonym: teasing - provoking interest or desire while withholding satisfaction

Etymology

From Tantalus, a figure in Greek mythology who was kept eternally just out of reach of food and drink.
