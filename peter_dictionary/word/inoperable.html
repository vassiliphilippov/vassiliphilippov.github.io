inoperable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɑpərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɒpərəbəl / (BrE)
Not able to function as intended or perform its normal function.

Examples

It is distinctly possible, therefore, that simultaneous pressures in all three vessels would have rendered the shunts inoperable and hence, uninjectable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: operable - able to function or operate as intended

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + operabilis (workable).
