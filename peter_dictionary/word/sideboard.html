sideboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdbɔːd / (BrE)
a removable board attached to the side of a wagon to increase its load-carrying capacity

Examples

I ducked just as the first strand broke somewhere down the line and came whipping over the sideboards.

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'board'.
