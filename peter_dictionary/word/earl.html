earl

1. NOUN

/ ɜrl / (AmE), / ɜːl / (BrE)
A British nobleman ranking below a marquess and above a viscount.

Examples

Thomas Douglas, fifth Earl of Selkirk, a noble humanitarian Scot concerned with the plight of the crofters of his native Highlands, conceived a plan to settle them in the valley of the Red River of the North.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: marquess - a British nobleman ranking above an earl
  • Degree Lower: viscount - a British nobleman ranking below an earl
  • Synonym: count - continental European nobleman equivalent to an earl

Etymology

From Old English *eorl* (nobleman, warrior), possibly of Germanic origin.
