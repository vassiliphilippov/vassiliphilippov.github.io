2. VERB

/ ʃeɪk / (AmE), / ʃeɪk / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with short, quick, irregular movements, often as a result of cold, fear, excitement, or weakness

Examples

Wilson again went downtown to a different banker, an intelligent young white man who seemed rather sympathetic, but he shook his head.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety or excitement
  • Synonym: quiver - to shake with a slight, rapid motion
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become steady; opposite of shake

Etymology

Old English *sceacan* (to move quickly), of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *schokken* (to shake, jolt), German *schocken* (to jolt).
