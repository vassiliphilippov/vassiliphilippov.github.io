gloat

1. VERB

/ ɡloʊt / (AmE), / ɡləʊt / (BrE)
to dwell with excessive satisfaction or pleasure, especially over one's own success or another's misfortune.

Examples

He gloated, and his lips slavered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
  • Antonym: mourn - to feel or express deep sorrow or regret

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Middle English *glouten* (to look askance).
