corporal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.pɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.əl / (BrE)
a noncommissioned officer of low rank in the Army, Air Force, or Marines, above private but below sergeant

Examples

The Rebs were, to a Massachusetts corporal, "fighting madmen or not men at all but whiskey + gunpowder put into a human frame ".
The same command is repeated as many times as there are levels in rank from general to corporal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sergeant - a noncommissioned officer ranking above corporal
  • Degree Lower: private - the lowest military rank, below corporal

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'corporalis,' meaning 'of the body,' originally referring to the leader of a body of soldiers.
