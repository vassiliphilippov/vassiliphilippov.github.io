indispensable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈspɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspɛnsəb(ə)l / (BrE)
Absolutely necessary; essential and impossible to do without.

Examples

The concern of Utopia is with the optimo reipublicae statu, the best ordering of a civil society; and it is again and again made clear that Utopian communism provides the institutional array indispensable to that best ordering.

Synset Examples

essential tools and materials
funds essential to the completion of the project
an indispensable worker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary, extremely important
  • Antonym: dispensable - able to be replaced or done without, not necessary

Etymology

From Late Latin indispensabilis, from Latin in- (not) + dispensabilis (able to be managed or spared).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈspɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspɛnsəb(ə)l / (BrE)
Absolutely necessary; essential and impossible to do without.

Examples

Arlene became indispensable; nobody could have told why.
It may be, of course, that such objectives can be pursued consisently with a policy designed to overthrow Communism; my point is that where conflicts arise they must always be resolved in favor of achieving the indispensable condition for a tolerant world - the absence of Soviet Communist power.

Synset Examples

foods indispensable to good nutrition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Synonym: necessary - needed to achieve a certain result or purpose
  • Antonym: dispensable - able to be replaced or done without; not essential

Etymology

From Middle French *indispensable*, from Latin *indispensabilis*, from *in-* (not) + *dispensare* (to dispense).
