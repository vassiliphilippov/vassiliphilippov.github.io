grandma

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrænˌmɑ / (AmE), / ˈɡranˌmɑː / (BrE)
(informal) One's grandmother; the mother of one's father or mother.

Examples

Linda Kay told him he could n't do anything like that with his Grandma dying, and he said well they had to eat, did n't they, they were n't all dying.
"Grandma was n't expected to like it ", Dr. Marion Hilliard, the late Toronto gynecologist, once summed up the attitude of the' 90 s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandmother - the mother of one's father or mother (formal term)
  • Antonym: grandpa - informal term for grandfather

Etymology

Shortened and altered from 'grandmother'.
