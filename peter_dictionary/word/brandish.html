brandish

1. VERB

/ ˈbrændɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrændɪʃ / (BrE)
to wave or swing something, especially a weapon, in a threatening or excited manner

Examples

The man had come floundering aboard the flat-bottomed barge at the last instant, brandishing the flag of truce.
They poured through the opening in the valley, then spread out in a long line to come at us, brandishing their lances and filling the morning with their spine-chilling scalp cry.

Synset Examples

She waved her gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - move something back and forth in the air

Etymology

From Old French brandir ‘to wave a sword’, from brand ‘sword’.

2. VERB

/ ˈbræn.dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbræn.dɪʃ / (BrE)
to wave or display (something, especially a weapon) in a threatening or aggressive manner

Examples

Tillotson had reported the man to Gansevoort and an hour later, with back turned, had been attacked by Wilson, brandishing the weapon.

Synset Examples

brandish a sword

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wield - to hold and use (a weapon or tool)
  • Synonym: flourish - to wave something around to attract attention

Etymology

From Old French brandir (to flourish a sword), from brand (sword).
