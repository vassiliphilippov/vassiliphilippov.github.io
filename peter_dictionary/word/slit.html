slit

1. NOUN

/ slɪt / (AmE), / slɪt / (BrE)
A long, narrow opening or cut, especially one made intentionally in a surface or fabric.

Examples

Pressing his cigarette out in the earth, Warren walked to the slit and scanned the jagged hills.
Two slits enabled observers to watch across the river.
There was no place to sit, but Watson walked slowly from the ladder to the window slits and back, stooping slightly to avoid striking his head on the heavy beams.
And the cast shell of a locust, straw colored and transparent, weighing nothing, fragile but entire, with eyes like bubbles and a gaping slit down its back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - an opening made by cutting
  • Degree Higher: hole - a hollow place or opening in a surface

Etymology

From Old English 'slittan' (to split or tear), related to Old Norse 'slita' (to tear).

2. NOUN

/ slɪt / (AmE), / slɪt / (BrE)
(vulgar, offensive) An obscene term for the female genitals.

Examples

The slit breeches had to go.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunt - an extremely offensive term for the female genitals
  • Synonym: vagina - anatomical term for the female genital canal

3. VERB

/ slɪt / (AmE), / slɪt / (BrE)
to make a long, narrow cut or opening in something

Examples

Then he looked at his finger, at the wrinkled, heavy knuckle and the thick nail he used like a knife to pry up, slit, and open.

Synset Examples

slit the throat of the victim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to divide something with a sharp edge; more general than 'slit'

Etymology

From Old English *slitan* (to split, tear apart), related to Old Norse *slita* (to slit, tear), both from Proto-Germanic roots.
