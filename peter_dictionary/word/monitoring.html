monitoring

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnɪtɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of observing, supervising, or keeping track of something, often systematically and sometimes recording the results.

Examples

Great stress is placed on the role that the monitoring of information sending plays in maintaining the effectiveness of the network.
By monitoring, we mean some system of control over the types of information sent from the various centers.
The monitoring is the highest and most restrictive of any organization in existence.
This monitoring is necessary because, on a parade ground, everyone can hear too much, and without monitoring a confused social event would develop.

Synset Examples

the monitoring of enemy communications plays an important role in war times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surveillance - close observation, especially of a suspected person or group
  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination or scrutiny

Etymology

From the verb 'monitor', which comes from Latin 'monitor' (one who admonishes, warner, overseer).
