absorptive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsɔrptɪv, æbˈsɔrptɪv / (AmE), / əbˈsɔːptɪv, æbˈsɔːptɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to absorb or soak up liquids, energy, or other substances

Examples

Even here there is room for some variation, for metal surfaces vary in smoothness, absorptive capacity, and chemical reactivity.

Synset Examples

as absorbent as a sponge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquid easily

Etymology

From Latin absorptivus, from absorbere (to suck in).
