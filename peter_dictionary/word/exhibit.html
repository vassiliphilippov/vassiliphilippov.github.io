exhibit

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (BrE)
An object or document presented in a court of law as evidence during a trial.

Examples

People who take us for suckers are like the Westerner who had on exhibit his superior marksmanship in the form of a number of bull's-eye achievements.

Etymology

From Latin *exhibere* (to hold out, exhibit), from *ex-* (out) + *habere* (to have).
