mustache

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.tæʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.tɑːʃ / (BrE)
Hair grown on the upper lip, usually deliberately, by men.

Examples

He crossed the tracks to Delaware House, where ladies in gay dresses and men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah.
Brown eyes, eyebrow mustache.
Pa had his head up, glaring, his teeth gnawing at the place where he 'd grown a mustache once.
Joseph Jastrow, the younger son of the distinguished rabbi, Marcus Jastrow, was a friendly, round-faced fellow with a little mustache, whose field was psychology, and who was also a punster and a jolly tease.

Synset Examples

he looked younger after he shaved off his mustache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard - hair growing on the cheeks and chin
  • Antonym: clean-shaven - having no facial hair

Etymology

From French 'moustache', from Italian 'mostaccio'.
