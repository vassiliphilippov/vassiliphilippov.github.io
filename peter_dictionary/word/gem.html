gem

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛm / (AmE), / dʒɛm / (BrE)
A person, thing, or especially a work of art or a comment, highly valued for its outstanding beauty, excellence, or perfection.

Examples

When I 'd delivered myself of that gem there was nothing to do but order up another drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jewel - something or someone highly valued or prized
  • Synonym: treasure - something of great worth or value

Etymology

From Old French 'gemme', from Latin 'gemma' (bud, jewel).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɛm / (AmE), / dʒɛm / (BrE)
A precious or semiprecious stone that has been cut or polished and is used in jewelry.

Examples

Seven dollars in any rock and gem shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jewel - a precious stone used in jewelry
  • Synonym: stone - a precious stone used as a gem

Etymology

From Old French 'gemme', from Latin 'gemma' meaning 'bud, jewel, gem'.
