chinked

1. ADJ

/ tʃɪŋkt / (AmE), / tʃɪŋkt / (BrE)
(of a structure, especially a log cabin) having its narrow gaps or openings filled with a suitable material, such as mud or mortar, to make it weather-tight.

Examples

Beyond that misty gray of the rain, he saw the stretching hutment, low diminutive log cabins, chinked with mud, with doorways a man would have to crouch to get through, with roofs of tenting laid over boughs or boards from hardtack boxes, or fence rails, with cranky chimneys of sticks and dried mud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daubed - (of a wall or surface) covered or coated thickly, especially with a soft or sticky substance

Etymology

From the verb 'chink' meaning to fill the cracks, itself from 'chink' (a narrow opening).
