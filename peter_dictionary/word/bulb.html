bulb

1. NOUN

/ bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
A rounded underground part of certain plants, such as onions and tulips, that stores food and produces shoots and roots for new plants to grow.

Examples

Instead they learn their dissection on the bulbs of plants.
She had dug a hole for each bulb, each tree wore a tag with her writing on it; where would she go for her gardening now?

Etymology

From Latin bulbus (bulb, onion), from Greek bolbos (bulbous plant).

2. NOUN

/ bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
a rounded, often hollow part at the end of a cylindrical instrument, used for holding or dispensing liquids or for measurement

Examples

However, the surface temperature gradient can produce erroneous vapor-pressure measurements for the bulk liquid helium unless precautions are taken to isolate the tube (which passes through the surface to the vapor pressure bulb) from the liquid helium surface.

Synset Examples

the bulb of a syringe

Etymology

From Latin bulbus, from Greek bolbos (bulbous root).

3. NOUN

/ bʌlb / (AmE), / bʌlb / (BrE)
An electric lamp with a glass housing that contains a wire filament that produces light when heated by electricity; commonly called a light bulb.

Examples

Continuous low-level lighting may be provided in the shelter by means of a 4 - cell hot-shot battery to which is wired a 150 - milliampere flashlight type bulb.
He paused on the landing to steady his breathing and then bent to examine the single door by the light of the weak bulb overhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light bulb - electric lamp with a glass housing and filament

Etymology

From Latin bulbus (bulb, onion), later applied to the glass housing of lamps due to its shape.
