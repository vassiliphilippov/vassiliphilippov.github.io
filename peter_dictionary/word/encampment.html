encampment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (BrE)
A place where people set up tents or temporary shelters, especially while on holiday or during travel.

Examples

The Fort Lauderdale encampment for drinking is foreign to most Brooklyn College boys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campsite - an area where tents can be pitched, especially for recreation

Etymology

From French 'encampement', from 'encamper' (to encamp).
