caravan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrəˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈkærəˌvæn / (BrE)
a group of people, vehicles, or animals traveling together in a line, typically for safety or companionship, especially across a desert or through hostile territory

Examples

In contrast to the caravan of the previous night, there were only four cars parked across the street.

Synset Examples

we were part of a caravan of almost a thousand camels
they joined the wagon train for safety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convoy - a group of vehicles or ships traveling together for protection

Etymology

From Persian *karwān* (group of desert travelers) via French and Italian.
