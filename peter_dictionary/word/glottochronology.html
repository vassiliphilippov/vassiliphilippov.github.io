glottochronology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɑːtoʊkrəˈnɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒtəʊkrəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of estimating the time at which languages diverged from a common ancestral language by comparing their core vocabulary

Examples

If of the founders of glottochronology Swadesh has escaped our steady plodding, and Lees has repudiated his own share in the founding, that is no reason why we should swerve.

Synset Examples

he mapped the glottochronology of the Romance languages

Etymology

From Greek 'glōtta' (tongue, language) and 'chronology' (science of time arrangement).
