lazy

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪzi / (AmE), / ˈleɪzi / (BrE)
Unwilling to work or use energy; disinclined to effort or activity.

Examples

He looked lazy, spoiled, a little querulous.
When I talked to Ching about it, he said, Everyone can learn, if he is not a Reactionary or lazy.

Synset Examples

faineant kings under whose rule the country languished
an indolent hanger-on
too lazy to wash the dishes
shiftless idle youth
slothful employees
the unemployed are not necessarily work-shy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolent - avoiding activity or exertion; habitually lazy
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *lasich* (slack, idle) or Middle Dutch *lasich* (feeble).

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪzi / (AmE), / ˈleɪzi / (BrE)
Moving slowly and gently; unhurried and relaxed in motion or pace.

Examples

He was calm, drugged, and lazy.
The name fell with lazy affectionate remembrance from her lips.
The lazy sing-song was spaced in time like the drone of a bumble-bee.

Synset Examples

up a lazy river
lazy white clouds
at a lazy pace
