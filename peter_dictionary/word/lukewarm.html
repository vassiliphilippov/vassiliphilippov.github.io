lukewarm

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːk.wɔrm / (AmE), / ˈluːk.wɔːm / (BrE)
Showing little enthusiasm or interest; unenthusiastic or indifferent.

Examples

Arnold Lodley and Michael Perse were like the rest - lukewarm, ready to swing against Hudson in a crisis.

Synset Examples

a halfhearted effort
gave only lukewarm support to the candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfhearted - lacking enthusiasm or effort
  • Synonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest or concern
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - having or showing intense interest or excitement
  • Synonym: tepid - showing little enthusiasm; also barely warm

Etymology

From Middle English 'lewkewarme', from Old English 'hlēoc' (tepid) + 'warm'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈluːkˌwɔrm / (AmE), / ˈluːkˌwɔːm / (BrE)
(of liquid or food) only slightly warm; tepid.

Examples

Glendora put down a dish of lukewarm rice.

Synset Examples

he hates lukewarm coffee
tepid bath water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tepid - slightly warm, especially so as to be unpleasant
  • Degree Higher: warm - having or giving out heat to a moderate or comfortable degree
  • Antonym: cold - of a relatively low temperature

Etymology

From Middle English 'leukewarme', from Old English 'hlēowe' (warm) + 'warm'.
