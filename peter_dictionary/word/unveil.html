unveil

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈveɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪl / (BrE)
to remove a veil or covering from something, especially as part of a public ceremony; to reveal or make known for the first time

Examples

It is the AID's intention to create in the library "a miniature museum of Americana "before completed refurbishing is unveiled early this fall.

Synset Examples

Women must not unveil themselves in public in Islamic societies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make something known or visible that was secret or hidden
  • Antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From un- (not) + veil, from Old French 'veile' (veil), from Latin 'velum' (sail, covering).
