electrolysis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
The process of causing a chemical decomposition by passing an electric current through a liquid or solution containing ions.

Etymology
