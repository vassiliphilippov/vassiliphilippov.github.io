championship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæm.pi.ən.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃæm.pi.ən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the status or condition of being a champion, especially in a sport or competition

Examples

At a ceremony in the reception room of Mayor Richardson Dilworth, the Eagles were honored for winning the championship.
Definition of the thighs at the uppermost part is quite commonly seen in most championship Olympic lifters which is easily understandable.

Synset Examples

he held the title for two years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the distinction of being a champion, often in a sporting context

Etymology

From 'champion' + '-ship', the latter indicating a state or condition.
