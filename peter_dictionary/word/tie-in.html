tie-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ ɪn / (BrE)
a device or connection used to join or fasten two things together, especially in construction or utilities

Examples

For a time following the abandonment of the local plant, electric current for Manchester was brought in from the south with an emergency tie-in with the Vermont Marble Company system to the north.

Synset Examples

the walls are held together with metal links placed in the wet mortar during construction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: link - a connecting element or means of joining

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'tie in', meaning to connect or link by tying.
