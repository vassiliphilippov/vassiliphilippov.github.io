pact

1. NOUN

/ pækt / (AmE), / pækt / (BrE)
a formal agreement between two or more parties, especially between countries or sovereigns

Examples

It was as though he had made a pact with the devil himself, but it was not yet time to pay the price.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treaty - a formally concluded and ratified agreement between states
  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties

Etymology

From Latin pactum (agreement, contract), from pacisci (to agree).
