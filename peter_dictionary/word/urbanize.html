urbanize

1. VERB

/ ˈɝː.bəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
To make an area more characteristic of a city, typically by increasing population density or developing industries, infrastructure, and services associated with urban life.

Examples

Was it the party's intention, for example, to abolish gradually the kolkhoz system and replace it by uniformly wage-earning sovkhozes, i. e., state farms (which were, moreover, to be progressively "urbanized ")?

Synset Examples

The area was urbanized after many people moved in

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suburbanize - make more suburban in character
  • Antonym: ruralize - make more rural in character

Etymology

From urban + -ize, ultimately from Latin urbānus (of a city).
