nightfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtfɔl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtfɔːl / (BrE)
The time in the evening when it becomes dark; the period immediately after sunset.

Examples

Hansen arrived just before nightfall, two hours late, in company with Lee MacPhail; J. A. W. Iglehart, chairman of the Oriole board of directors, and Public Relations Director Jack Dunn.
Cover the whole building, bury us all, by nightfall.
Brannon timed it so that they rode in an hour after nightfall.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight just before night
  • Degree Lower: sunset - the time when the sun disappears below the horizon
  • Synonym: twilight - the period of partial darkness after sunset

Etymology

From 'night' + 'fall', referring to the coming or 'falling' of night.
