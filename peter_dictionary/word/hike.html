hike

1. NOUN

/ haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
a long walk, especially in the countryside or wilderness, taken for exercise or pleasure

Examples

In a few minutes she was making the ten foot hike unaided; soon she was parading around the house, flaunting her new skill.
Vending machines can alleviate the long hike to the cafeteria during the break with resulting waste of production time.
Come on, girls, take a hike, eh.

Synset Examples

she enjoys a hike in her spare time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - an act of traveling on foot, typically for pleasure or exercise
  • Synonym: trek - a long and difficult journey on foot, especially in remote areas

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in American English in the early 19th century.

2. VERB

/ haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
To increase something, especially suddenly or by a large amount, such as prices, taxes, or rates.

Examples

The transition from segregation to finances might already be in progress, in the form of an administration proposal to hike the state sales tax from 2 per cent to 3 per cent.

Synset Examples

The landlord hiked up the rents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to increase the amount, level, or strength of something
  • Antonym: reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Etymology

Originally meaning 'to walk a long distance,' later extended figuratively to increases in prices or rates.
