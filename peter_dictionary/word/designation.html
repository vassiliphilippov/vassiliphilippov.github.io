designation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛzɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdezɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a name, title, or term by which someone or something is identified or distinguished

Examples

Men of more delicate sensibilities referred to this condition as "looseness of the bowels "; but a much more common designation was "the sh-ts ".
In the first instance, "mimesis "is here used to mean the recalling of experience in terms of vivid images rather than in terms of abstract ideas or conventional designations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appellation - a name or title by which someone or something is known
  • Synonym: title - a name that describes someone's position or job
  • Synonym: label - a word or phrase used to describe or identify something

Etymology

From Latin designatio, from designare meaning 'to mark out'.
