wag

1. VERB

/ wæɡ / (AmE), / wæɡ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move rapidly and repeatedly from side to side or up and down

Examples

Just when it seems baseball might be losing its grip on the masses up pops heroics to start millions of tongues to wagging.
His stethoscope was on the table by Scotty's bed and he picked it up and wagged it at Scotty.
The clerk wagged his head toward the captain's office.

Synset Examples

The happy dog wagged his tail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - to move back and forth or from side to side, often in an arc
  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly up and down or from side to side

Etymology

From Middle English waggen, of Germanic origin; related to Old English wagian.
