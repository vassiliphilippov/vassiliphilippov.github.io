resift

1. VERB

/ riˈsɪft / (AmE), / riˈsɪft / (BrE)
to sift again; to pass something through a sieve or similar process another time to further separate, sort, or refine it

Examples

Frequently we are given assurance that automatically all ideas will be sifted and resifted and in the end only the good ones will survive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sift - to separate or remove by passing through a sieve

Etymology

From re- (again) + sift (to strain or separate by sieve).
