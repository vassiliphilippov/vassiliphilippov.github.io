socialized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an institution, service, or economic asset) placed under group or government ownership or control rather than private ownership.

Examples

Constitutional government, popular vote, trial by jury, public education, labor unions, cooperatives, communes, socialized ownership, world courts, and the veto power in world councils are but a few examples.

Synset Examples

socialized ownership
socialized medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalized - brought under control or ownership of a national government

Etymology

From the verb "socialize," itself from French socialiser ('to make social'), based on Latin socialis ('sociable, allied').
