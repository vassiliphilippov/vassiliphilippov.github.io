starving

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑrvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɑːvɪŋ / (BrE)
suffering severely or dying from lack of food; extremely hungry.

Examples

Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hungry - feeling or showing the need for food
  • Degree Higher: famished - extremely hungry, to the point of starvation

Etymology

Present participle of 'starve', from Old English *steorfan* (to die).
