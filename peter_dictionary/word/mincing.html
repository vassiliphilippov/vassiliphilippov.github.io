mincing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person's movements or manner) affectedly dainty, refined, or delicate, often in an exaggerated or unnatural way.

Examples

He caught her eye, came back around the car with the boot wearer's teetering, half mincing walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dainty - delicately small and pretty, or affectedly refined
  • Antonym: natural - not affected or artificial in manner

Etymology

From the verb 'mince', ultimately from Old French *mincier* 'to chop finely'.
