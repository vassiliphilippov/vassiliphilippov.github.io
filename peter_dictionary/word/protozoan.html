protozoan

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
A microscopic single-celled organism, usually nonphotosynthetic, belonging to diverse groups formerly classified as Protozoa.

Examples

When the temperature reached 32 ` F all protozoan activity ceased; but as the temperature rose, the numbers of protozoa increased rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoa - plural form; group of single-celled microscopic organisms

Etymology

From New Latin Protozoa, from Greek prōtos (first) + zōion (animal).
