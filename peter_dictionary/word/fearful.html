fearful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪə.fəl / (BrE)
Causing fear, dread, or terror; frightening or alarming.

Examples

Resolved, that we find in this fearful tragedy at Harper's Ferry a reason for more earnest effort to remove the evil of slavery from the whole land as speedily as possible.

Synset Examples

the awful war
an awful risk
dire news
a career or vengeance so direful that London was shocked
the dread presence of the headmaster
polio is no longer the dreaded disease it once was
a dreadful storm
a fearful howling
horrendous explosions shook the city
a terrible curse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrifying - causing extreme fear
  • Synonym: dreadful - causing fear or suffering
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less afraid or worried

Etymology

From Middle English 'ferful', from 'fere' (fear) + '-ful'.
