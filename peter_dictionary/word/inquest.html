inquest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌkwɛst / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkwɛst / (BrE)
a legal inquiry to determine the cause of a person's unexpected or suspicious death

Examples

An inquest was held, and after a good deal of testimony about the anonymous notes, the county coroner estimated that the shooting had been done from a distance of 300 yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coroner's inquest - an inquest conducted specifically by a coroner
  • Degree Lower: autopsy - postmortem examination to discover cause of death

Etymology

From Old French *enqueste*, from Latin *inquirere* (to inquire).
