transaction

1. NOUN

/ trænˈzækʃən / (AmE), / trænˈzækʃən / (BrE)
an instance of buying, selling, or otherwise conducting business; the act of carrying out a business, financial, or other exchange between parties

Examples

It ignores the sordid financial aspects (quite conveniently, too, for his audience, who could indulge in moral indignation without visible, or even conscious, discomfort, their money from the transaction having been put away long ago in a good antiseptic brokerage).
This section prevents the military departments and the Office of Civil and Defense Mobilization from carrying out certain transactions involving real property unless they come into agreement with the Committees on Armed Services of the Senate and the House of Representatives.

Synset Examples

no transactions are possible without him
he has always been honest is his dealings with me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deal - an instance of buying or selling something
  • Synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another

Etymology

From Latin transactio (accomplishment, settlement), from transigere (to drive through, accomplish).
