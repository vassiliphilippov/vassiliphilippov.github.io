uproar

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌrɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌrɔː / (BrE)
A state of noisy confusion, excitement, or commotion.

Examples

This brought in everybody from both sides, while the spectators stood up and added to the uproar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumult - a loud, confused noise, especially one caused by a large mass of people

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *uproer* (tumult, commotion), from *op* (up) + *roer* (roar, disturbance).
