laxness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæksnəs / (AmE), / ˈlæksnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being careless, negligent, or not sufficiently strict.

Examples

He refused to change his approach, and instead he attacked high and low - the officials for their not backing him, and subordinates for their laxness, stupidity, and stubbornness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strictness - the quality of being strict or severe

Etymology

From 'lax' + '-ness'. 'Lax' from Latin 'laxus' (loose, slack).
