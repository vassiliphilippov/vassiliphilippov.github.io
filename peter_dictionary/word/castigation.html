castigation

1. NOUN

/ kæstəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / kæstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of criticizing or reprimanding someone severely

Examples

To be sure, when this is pointed out, a common response among certain churchmen is to fulminate about "the little flock "and "the great crowd "and to take solace from Paul's castigation of the "wisdom of the wise "in the opening chapter of First Corinthians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval
  • Synonym: scolding - the act of angrily criticizing someone

Etymology

From Latin castigatio (rebuke, correction), from castigare (to chastise, correct).
