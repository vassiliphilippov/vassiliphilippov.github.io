whiteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being white in color; the attribute of having the greatest lightness with little or no hue

Examples

In this way, numerical values for the relative efficacy of various detergent formulations can be obtained by measuring the reflectance (whiteness) of the cloth swatches before and after washing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blackness - the quality or state of being black in color

Etymology

Derives from the adjective 'white' + the suffix '-ness', indicating a state or quality.
