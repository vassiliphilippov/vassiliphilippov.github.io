prejudiced

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛdʒədɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɛdʒʊdɪst / (BrE)
(of a person or their views) influenced by preconceived opinions or feelings that are not based on reason or actual experience.

Examples

For hundreds of years, the evidence available consisted of (1) the captain's fragmentary journal, (2) a highly prejudiced account by one of the survivors, (3) a note found in a dead man's desk on board, and (4) several second-hand reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just

Etymology

From 'prejudice' (Middle English), from Old French, from Latin 'præjudicium' (prior judgment).
