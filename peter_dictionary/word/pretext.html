pretext

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriˌtɛkst / (AmE), / ˈpriːtɛkst / (BrE)
A false reason or excuse given to hide the real purpose or motive for something.

Examples

He was, in the words of Irenaeus, "beguiled by another under the pretext of immortality ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or offense
  • Synonym: pretense - a claim that is not supported by fact; a false display
  • Antonym: pretense - a claim or assertion that is not true (as antonym for honesty)
  • Antonym: reason - the actual cause or explanation for something

Etymology

From Latin praetextus (pretense), past participle of praetexere (to disguise, to pretend).
