aplomb

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɑm / (AmE), / əˈplɒm / (BrE)
self-confidence and assurance, especially in demanding or difficult situations

Examples

Somewhat to his surprise he found that one girl, whom he would never have considered for the job since she had appeared somewhat mousy and also had been in the office a relatively short time, did the most outstanding job of playing the role of receptionist, showing wit, sparkle, and aplomb.

Synset Examples

keep your cool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calmness and self-possession, especially in difficult situations
  • Synonym: poise - graceful and elegant bearing in a person, often under pressure
  • Synonym: confidence - belief in oneself and one's abilities

Etymology

From French 'aplomb' (perpendicularity, assurance), from 'à plomb' ('according to the plummet', i.e. upright).
