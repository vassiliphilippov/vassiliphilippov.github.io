gout

1. NOUN

/ ɡaʊt / (AmE), / ɡaʊt / (BrE)
A painful medical condition in which defective metabolism of uric acid causes arthritis, especially in the big toe and foot, due to accumulation of uric acid crystals in the joints.

Examples

Mrs. Hewlitt led the birthcontrol league, Mrs. Ryerson was arthritis, and way in the distance could be seen the slate roof of Ethel Littleton's house, a roof that signified gout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthritis - inflammation of one or more joints causing pain and stiffness

Etymology

From Old French *goute*, from Latin *gutta* (a drop), referring to humor drops in ancient medical theory.
