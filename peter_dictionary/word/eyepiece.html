eyepiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌpis / (AmE), / ˈaɪpiːs / (BrE)
the set of lenses through which a person looks in an optical instrument such as a microscope or telescope

Examples

In the eyepiece a Wratten 2 B filter was used to filter off residual ultra-violet light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocular - another term for the eyepiece of an optical instrument

Etymology

eyepiece: from 'eye' + 'piece', indicating the part used for viewing with the eye.
