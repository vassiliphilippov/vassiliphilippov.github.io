wildcatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪldˌkætər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldˌkætə / (BrE)
A person who drills wells in areas not known to contain oil, taking significant financial risks in the hope of making a discovery.

Examples

A wildcatter had to be prepared for almost any emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilman - a person involved in the oil industry, especially in drilling or production

Etymology

From 'wildcat' (to engage in speculative business) + -er (agent noun suffix); originally American, referring to speculative drilling.
