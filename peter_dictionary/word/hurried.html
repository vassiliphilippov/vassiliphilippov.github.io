hurried

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɜːr.id / (AmE), / ˈhʌr.id / (BrE)
Done or acting with excessive speed or in a rushed manner.

Examples

His aim was hurried; so the pitchfork whistled over Curt's head.

Synset Examples

a hurried trip to the store
the hurried life of a city
a hurried job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rushed - done in a hurried fashion; not leisurely
  • Antonym: leisurely - unhurried, taking plenty of time

Etymology

From the verb 'hurry' + -ed, early 18th century origin.
