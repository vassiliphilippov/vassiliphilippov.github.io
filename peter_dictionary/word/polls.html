polls

1. NOUN

/ poʊlz / (AmE), / pəʊlz / (BrE)
the place where people go to vote in an election

Examples

Ordinary Carey Williams, armed with a pistol, stood by at the polls to insure order.
"Being at the polls was just like being at church.
Many local citizens feared that there would be irregularities at the polls, and Williams got himself a permit to carry a gun and promised an orderly election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polling station - a building where voting takes place during an election

Etymology

From Middle English 'polle' (head), used in the sense of counting heads in voting; later evolved to mean the act or place of voting.
