acetonemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsɪtəˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌæsɪtəʊˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally increased level of ketone bodies in the blood, often occurring in diabetes mellitus or certain metabolic disorders.

Examples

For the prevention or treatment of acetonemia (ketosis) in dairy cows.
For the prevention of acetonemia (ketosis) feed 1 4 pound per day beginning at calving and continuing for 6 weeks.
Prevents and treats acetonemia (ketosis) in dairy cows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ketonemia - an abnormal increase of ketone bodies in the blood
  • Synonym: ketosis - a metabolic state with raised levels of ketone bodies, often in diabetes or starvation

Etymology

From acetone + -emia (from Greek haima 'blood'), indicating the presence of acetone in the blood.
