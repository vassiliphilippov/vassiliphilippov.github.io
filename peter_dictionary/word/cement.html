cement

1. NOUN

/ səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a substance that hardens to act as an adhesive material, bonding objects or surfaces together

Examples

The physical film is cut with a knife at the end of one complete sequence, and the cut edge is joined physically, by cement, to the cut edge of the beginning of the next sequence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glue - an adhesive substance used for sticking objects together
  • Synonym: adhesive - a substance used for sticking objects or materials together

Etymology

From Latin 'caementum' (quarry stone), later meaning 'bonding material'.

2. VERB

/ səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to join or bind together firmly, as if using cement; to strengthen or make a relationship, agreement, or situation more solid or permanent

Examples

So instead of being tests of the South's loyalty, the Spanish War, the two World Wars, and the Korean War all served to overcome old grievances and cement reunion.
It was partially cemented by ages and pressure, yet it crumpled before the onslaught of the powerful streams, the force of a thousand fire hoses, and with the gold it held washed down through the long sluices.

Synset Examples

We cemented our friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to make safe or stable
  • Antonym: weaken - to make less strong or solid
  • Synonym: reinforce - to make something stronger

Etymology

From Latin 'caementum' (stone chips used in making mortar), via Old French 'ciment'.

3. NOUN

/ səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a powdered substance made from calcined limestone and clay, used as a binder in building materials like concrete and mortar when mixed with water, sand, or gravel

Examples

They hurled a cement barge against a freighter in Honolulu Harbor, knocked down telephone lines, marooned automobiles, flooded lawns, killed six cows.
We send shovels, cement, nails, and corrugated iron for roofs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concrete - a building material made from cement, water, and aggregate

Etymology

From Old French *ciment*, from Latin *caementum* (rough stone, quarry stone), later meaning 'cement'.

4. NOUN

/ səˈmɛnt / (AmE), / sɪˈmɛnt / (BrE)
A hard, gray building material made from a mixture of calcined limestone and clay, used for making concrete; commonly refers to concrete pavement or surfaces, especially in informal use.

Examples

But the fences were still in place fifty odd years ago, and when we stood on the gate to look over, the sidewalk under our eyes was not cement but two rows of paving stones with grass between and on both sides.
Two uniformed officers, a couple of plain-clothesmen I knew, and two other men stood on a gray cement area next to the pool on my left.

Synset Examples

they stood on the grey cement beside the pool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concrete - building material made from cement, sand, and gravel

Etymology

From Latin 'caementum' (rough stone, building stone).
