vanguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈvænˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈvæn.ɡɑːd / (BrE)
the leading or forward position in a movement or field, especially the foremost part of an advancing army.

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rearguard - the soldiers stationed at the back of an army or movement

Etymology

From Old French 'avantgarde', from 'avant' (before) + 'garde' (guard).
