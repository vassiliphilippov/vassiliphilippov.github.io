counterbalanced

1. ADJ

/ ˈkaʊntərˌbælənst / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəbælənst / (BrE)
Brought into balance or equilibrium by means of a weight, force, or influence that offsets another.

Examples

It seems clear, from the counter-balanced shape of the series of arrows in Figure 5 that there was about an equal number of early and late Onsets and Completions for the 34 girls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balanced - in a state of equilibrium or proper proportions
  • Antonym: unbalanced - not in balance, lacking equilibrium

Etymology

From 'counterbalance,' itself from 'counter-' (against) + 'balance'.
