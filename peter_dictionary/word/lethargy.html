lethargy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛθərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɛθəˌdʒi / (BrE)
A state of sluggishness, inactivity, and lack of energy; extreme drowsiness or apathy.

Examples

Now Spencer, seeming with effort to shake himself from lethargy, spoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torpor - a state of physical or mental inactivity; sluggishness
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; energetic activity

Etymology

From Greek *lēthargia* (forgetfulness), from *lēthargos* (forgetful).
