barnful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrn.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːn.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to the capacity of a barn.

Examples

But is the result new barnsful of tested knowledge on the basis of which we can with confidence solve our domestic and international problems?

Etymology

Formed from 'barn' + 'ful', following the pattern of 'cupful', 'spoonful', etc.
