mucus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjukəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkəs / (BrE)
A thick, slippery substance produced by mucous membranes in the body, serving to protect and lubricate surfaces such as the nose, throat, lungs, and digestive tract.

Examples

Two clotted balls the color of mucus rolled between fiery lids.
Trachea clear of mucus and foreign objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlegm - thick mucus produced in the respiratory passages, especially during illness

Etymology

From Latin 'mucus' (slime, snot).
