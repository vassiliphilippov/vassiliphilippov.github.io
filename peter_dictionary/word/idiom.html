idiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəm / (BrE)
the characteristic style or expression of a particular artist, school, or movement in art, music, or literature

Examples

By 1937 he had clarified his intentions to serve his people: "I have striven for clarity and melodious idiom, but at the same time I have by no means attempted to restrict myself to the accepted methods of harmony and melody.

Synset Examples

an imaginative orchestral idiom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - a manner of expression characteristic of a particular person, group, period, or movement

Etymology

From Greek *idiōma* (peculiarity, special feature), from *idios* (one's own, private).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəm / (BrE)
a manner of expression or style of speaking that is characteristic or natural to native speakers of a language

Examples

Prokofieff might well emerge as a cultural hero, who, by the force of his creative life, helped preserve the main stream of tradition, to which the surviving idioms of current experimentalism may be eventually added and integrated.
economic security and cultural opportunities, incisive idioms, social fermentations for a new national ideology - a sympathetic public and a large body of performers especially trained to fulfill his purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parlance - a particular way of speaking or using words in a group
  • Synonym: dialect - a particular form of a language specific to a region or group

Etymology

From Greek *idióma* (peculiarity, special feature), from *idiousthai* (to make one's own).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəm / (BrE)
A form of language or a set of expressions and vocabulary characteristic of a specific group, region, or community; a dialect.

Examples

The Negro composer Hall Johnson studied the Americanegro Suite and said of it, "Of all the many songs written by white composers and employing what claims to be a Negroid idiom in both words and music, these six songs by Harold Arlen and Ted Koehler easily stand far out above the rest.

Synset Examples

the immigrants spoke an odd dialect of English
he has a strong German accent
it has been said that a language is a dialect with an army and navy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a regional or social variety of a language

Etymology

From Greek *idiōma* (peculiarity, special feature), from *idios* (one's own, private).
