xylem

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈzaɪləm / (BrE)
The tissue in vascular plants that conducts water and dissolved nutrients upward from the roots and also helps to form the woody element of the stem.

Examples

The autofluorescence from the walls of the xylem cells was particularly brilliant.
Frequently a few isolated thick walled cells or, rarely, groups of such cells in the xylem region, were also specifically stained, but there was no such staining in epidermis, cortex, most xylem cells, ray cells, or pith.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phloem - the tissue in plants that conducts food from the leaves to the rest of the plant

Etymology

From German Xylem, from Greek xylon (wood).
