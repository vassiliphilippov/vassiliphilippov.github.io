mocking

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
Making fun of someone or something in a cruel or contemptuous way; showing ridicule or scorn.

Examples

A week later the sentence of the Council was carried out: Jake Camaret and the woman were marched naked through the streets past a mocking populace.

Synset Examples

derisive laughter
a jeering crowd
her mocking smile
taunting shouts of `coward' and `sissy'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derisive - expressing ridicule or mockery
  • Synonym: jeering - making rude and mocking remarks
  • Synonym: taunting - provoking or mocking in a contemptuous way

Etymology

From verb 'mock' (late Middle English), from Old French 'mocquer' (to mock, deride).
