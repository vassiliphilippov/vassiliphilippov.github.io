tutor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
A person who gives private instruction to one or a small group, especially in a particular subject or skill.

Examples

They are relentlessly rebuffed on all sides by a waitress, the police, and an intruding government tutor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - a person who trains or instructs, especially in sports or specific skills
  • Synonym: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school

Etymology

From Latin 'tutor' (guardian, watcher), from 'tueri' (to watch, guard).

2. VERB

/ ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
to teach or give private instruction to an individual or a small group, especially as a supplement to formal teaching

Examples

They supplemented their income by small government assistance, by tutoring and economizing wherever they could.

Synset Examples

She tutored me in Spanish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - to train or instruct someone in a particular subject or skill
  • Synonym: teach - to impart knowledge to or instruct someone

Etymology

From Latin 'tutor' (guardian, watcher, protector), from 'tueri' (to watch, protect).
