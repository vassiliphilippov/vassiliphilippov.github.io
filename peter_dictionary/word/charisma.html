charisma

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪzmə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪzmə / (BrE)
a compelling charm or attractiveness that enables someone to influence or inspire others

Examples

And the common man was developing mythic power, or charisma, on his own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - a personal quality of attracting or fascinating others

Etymology

From Greek kharisma (favor, divine gift), from kharis (grace).
