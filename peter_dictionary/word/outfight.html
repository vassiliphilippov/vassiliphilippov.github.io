outfight

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈfaɪt / (AmE), / aʊtˈfaɪt / (BrE)
To fight better than (an opponent); to defeat in fighting by superior combat skill.

Examples

He had been a corps commander during the disastrous defeat and retreat of 1942 when the ill-prepared, ill-equipped British forces "were outmaneuvered, outfought and outgeneraled ".
He could never have outfought Santino.

Synset Examples

the Rangers outfought the Maple Leafs
The French forces outfought the Germans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a contest or battle
  • Antonym: lose - to be defeated in a fight, game, or contest

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'more than') + 'fight'.
