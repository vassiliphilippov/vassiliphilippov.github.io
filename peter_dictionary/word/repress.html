repress

1. VERB

/ rɪˈprɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛs / (BrE)
to subdue, control, or suppress (something, especially opposition, ideas, or feelings) by force or intimidation

Examples

New ideas were dangerous and must be repressed, no matter how.
Hence, the only defensible procedure is to repress any and every notion, unless it gives evidence that it is perfectly safe.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to forcibly put an end to
  • Synonym: quash - to put an end to forcibly; suppress
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to (opposite action)

Etymology

From Latin reprimere, repress-, from re- (back) + premere (to press).
