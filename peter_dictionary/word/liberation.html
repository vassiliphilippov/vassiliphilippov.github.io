liberation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of setting someone or something free from imprisonment, slavery, or oppression.

Examples

During the Brown trial, however, the state's most powerful Democratic newspaper, the Providence Daily Post, stated that Brown was a murderer, a man of blood, and that he and his associates, with the assistance of Republicans and Abolitionists, had plotted not only the liberation of the slaves but also the overthrow of state and federal governments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emancipation - the act of freeing someone from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • Antonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control

Etymology

From Latin 'liberatio', from 'liberare' (to set free).
