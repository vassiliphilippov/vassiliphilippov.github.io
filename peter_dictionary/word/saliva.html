saliva

1. NOUN

/ səˈlaɪ.və / (AmE), / səˈlaɪ.və / (BrE)
The clear, watery liquid produced by glands in the mouth that moistens food and aids in digestion.

Examples

The dust thick saliva came from his mouth like balled cotton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - saliva, especially when ejected from the mouth
  • Degree Higher: sputum - mixture of saliva and mucus coughed up from the respiratory tract

Etymology

From Latin 'saliva' (spittle).
