repulsive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
Extremely unpleasant or offensive to the mind or morals; causing strong dislike or disgust.

Examples

But even if we cannot see the repulsive characteristics in this new image of America, foreigners can; and our loss of "prestige "abroad is the direct result.

Synset Examples

an abhorrent deed
the obscene massacre at Wounded Knee
morally repugnant customs
repulsive behavior
the most repulsive character in recent novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abhorrent - causing disgust or hatred; morally repugnant
  • Synonym: disgusting - arousing revulsion or strong dislike
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing; appealing to the mind or senses

Etymology

From Latin 'repulsus', past participle of 'repellere' meaning 'to drive back'.
