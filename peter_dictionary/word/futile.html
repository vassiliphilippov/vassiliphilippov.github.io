futile

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːtəl, ˈfjuːtaɪl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtaɪl, ˈfjuːtɪl / (BrE)
incapable of producing any useful result; pointless

Examples

An argument with Orville Torrence Killpath was as frustrating and as futile as a cap pistol on a firing range.

Synset Examples

a futile effort
the therapy was ineffectual
an otiose undertaking
an unavailing attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffectual - not producing the desired effect
  • Synonym: useless - not fulfilling any useful purpose
  • Antonym: effective - successful in producing a desired result

Etymology

From Latin 'futilis' (leaky, vain, worthless).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːtəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtaɪl / (BrE)
Incapable of producing any useful result; pointless or ineffective.

Examples

This habit had become so fixed over the years that it seemed futile to do anything for which no one was waiting.

Synset Examples

a fruitless search
futile years after her artistic peak
a sleeveless errand
a vain attempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruitless - failing to achieve the desired result
  • Synonym: vain - producing no result; useless
  • Antonym: effective - successful in producing a desired result

Etymology

From Latin 'futilis', meaning 'leaky, vain, worthless'.
