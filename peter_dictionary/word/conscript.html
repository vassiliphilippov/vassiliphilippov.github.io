conscript

1. VERB

/ kənˈskrɪpt / (AmE), / kənˈskrɪpt / (BrE)
to force someone by law to serve in the armed forces; to enroll someone compulsorily into service, especially the military

Examples

The miserable people of China, the largest cast ever conscripted to enact an ideological passion play, cannot themselves resist overtly.

Synset Examples

The men were conscripted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - to select for mandatory military service
  • Synonym: enlist - to voluntarily join the armed forces
  • Antonym: volunteer - to offer oneself for service without compulsion

Etymology

From Latin 'conscribere' (to enroll, enlist), from 'con-' (together) + 'scribere' (to write).
