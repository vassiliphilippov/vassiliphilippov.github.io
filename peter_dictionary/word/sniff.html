sniff

1. VERB

/ snɪf / (AmE), / snɪf / (BrE)
to inhale air audibly through the nose, especially to detect a smell, show disapproval, or because of a cold

Examples

Styka nodded and sniffed and blew his nose vociferously.
Harvey Dent has been sniffing around one of our front companies.

Synset Examples

the sick student was sniffling in the back row

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sniffle - to make repeated small sniffing sounds, usually because of a cold or crying
  • Degree Higher: snort - to force air violently through the nose, often making a loud sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of inhaling through the nose.

2. VERB

/ snɪf / (AmE), / snɪf / (BrE)
to perceive an odor by inhaling through the nose, often audibly or deliberately

Examples

"I like to sniff a place, and reproduce what it really smells and looks like, its color, its particular kind of life ".
Sniffing its sweet smell and letting it fall to show its good crumbly consistency, he pointed to the nearby driveway and said, "This soil used to be like that hard packed road over there ".
Their heads were in the air sniffing.
But he knew; he sniffed the air and licked it on his lip and knew as a vintner knows a vintage.
On this trip to the South he wants, above all else, to sniff the effluvium of backwoods-and-sand-hill subhumanity and to see at least one barn burn at midnight ".

Synset Examples

sniff the perfume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell - to perceive or detect an odor by the nose
  • Synonym: inhale - to breathe in
  • Degree Lower: sniffle - to breathe through the nose with short audible inhalations

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the sound produced when sniffing.
