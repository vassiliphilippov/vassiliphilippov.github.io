backlog

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈbæk.lɒg / (BrE)
a quantity of work or orders that have accumulated and are yet to be completed or fulfilled

Examples

Despite the successful rehabilitation of over a half million disabled persons in the first eleven years after 1943, the existing program was still seen to be inadequate to cope with the nation's backlog of an estimated two million disabled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - a line or sequence of people or things waiting their turn
  • Synonym: pending - something waiting to be done or settled
  • Degree Higher: excess - an amount of something that is more than necessary or usual

Etymology

Originally referred to a large log placed at the back of a fire, later figuratively meaning something kept in reserve.
