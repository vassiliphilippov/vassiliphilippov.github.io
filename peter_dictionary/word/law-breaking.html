law-breaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of violating or disregarding the law; committing crimes or offenses.

Examples

Finally, it gives sanctity, more than human legitimacy, and even, through super empirical reference, transcendent and supernatural importance to some values; for example, marriage as a sacrament, much law-breaking as sinful, occasionally the state as a divine instrument.

Synset Examples

a long record of crimes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an action or omission which constitutes an offense and is punishable by law
  • Synonym: offense - a breach of a law or rule; an illegal act

Etymology

From 'law' + 'breaking', meaning the breaking or violation of the law.
