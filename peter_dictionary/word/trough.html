trough

1. NOUN

/ trɔf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
A long, narrow channel attached to the edge of a roof to collect and carry away rainwater; a gutter.

Examples

Together they also developed a new form of voltaic cell in which the wooden trough was replaced by one of copper, thereby producing stronger currents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gutter - a channel at the edge of a roof for carrying away rainwater

Etymology

From Old English *troh* (trough, hollow vessel), related to Old Norse *trogr*.

2. NOUN

/ trɑf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
a long, narrow depression, especially one between waves or in the earth's surface

Examples

Their amplitude sometimes is as little as two feet from trough to crest.
This is followed by a tremendous suck of water away from the shore as the first great trough arrives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crest - the top or highest point of a wave or curve

Etymology

From Old English 'troh', related to Old Norse 'trog' and Old High German 'troh', all meaning a hollow or trough.
