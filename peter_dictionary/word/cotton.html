cotton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
A soft, white, fibrous substance surrounding the seeds of the cotton plant, used as a textile fiber and in making fabric, thread, and other products.

Examples

One gram of cotton has been found to have a specific surface area of * * f.
Barbara bought some cotton aprons, and Harold bought shoestrings.
Still, he had liked the way she had looked, in a fresh, neat cotton dress - citron yellow, if he remembered.
The preparations were elaborate: flowers, candles, incense sticks, rice wine, dozens of delicacies, and pieces of white cotton string.
Then, one after another, the villagers tied the waiting cotton strings around our wrists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - soft, curly hair fiber from sheep, used as textile

Etymology

From Arabic 'quṭn', via Old Italian or French; entered English in the Middle Ages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtən / (AmE), / ˈkɒtn / (BrE)
A soft white fabric woven from the fibers of the cotton plant, commonly used for clothing, bedding, and other textiles.

Examples

A mist was walking on the water, white as cotton, but with a blending and merging grace.
She was wearing a brown cotton dress, cut across the hips in a way that was supposed to make her look slimmer, a yoke set into the skirt and flaring pleats below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linen - fabric made from flax fibers
  • Synonym: wool - fabric made from the fleece of sheep or goats
  • Synonym: silk - fine, smooth fabric made from silkworm threads
  • Antonym: polyester - synthetic fabric commonly used in textiles

Etymology

From Arabic 'quṭn', via Old French 'cotton' and Middle English.
