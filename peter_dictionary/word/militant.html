militant

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪlɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
Favouring confrontational or aggressive methods in support of a cause; combative or disposed to engage in warfare or conflict.

Examples

L'Unita and Hoy published identical denunciations of Short's elevation, l'Osservatore Romano and the Christian Science Monitor ignored it, Times of India snickered at it, and the Manchester Guardian simply reported it - the Fosterites in England were few but extremely militant.

Synset Examples

militant nations
hawkish congressman
warlike policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkish - supporting aggressive or warlike policies
  • Antonym: peaceful - not seeking or involving conflict or war

Etymology

From Latin militans, present participle of militare 'to serve as a soldier', from miles 'soldier'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪlətənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
aggressively active in support of a cause or ready to fight for a belief or purpose

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.

Synset Examples

highly competitive sales representative
militant in fighting for better wages for workers
his self-assertive and ubiquitous energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combative - ready or eager to fight; aggressively uncooperative
  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving war or violence; tranquil

Etymology

From Latin militant-, militans, present participle of militare (to serve as a soldier)
