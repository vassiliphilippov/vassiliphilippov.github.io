pitiable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtiəbl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiəbl / (BrE)
Deserving or arousing pity, often mixed with contempt; so poor, weak, or inadequate as to be almost contemptible.

Examples

After the collapse of that desperate and ill-fated campaign the character of the king degenerated for a time into a futility that was not merely pitiable but often ridiculous.

Synset Examples

their efforts were pathetic
pitiable lack of character
pitiful exhibition of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathetic - arousing pity, often contempt; deserving pity or scorn
  • Synonym: pitiful - deserving or arousing pity; very small or poor in quality

Etymology

From Middle English 'pitiable', from Old French, from pitié ('pity').
