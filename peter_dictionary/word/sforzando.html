sforzando

1. NOUN

/ sfɔrˈtsɑndoʊ / (AmE), / sfɔːtˈsændəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) a note or chord played with sudden, strong emphasis.

Examples

Eyes swerved in the patronne's head, Alex coughed loudly, and the doctor, with a sforzando of chicken noises floating behind him, took to the stairs in long shanked leaps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accent - an emphasis on a note or chord

Etymology

From Italian sforzando, gerund of sforzare ‘to force’.
