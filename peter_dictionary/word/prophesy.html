prophesy

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːfəsaɪ / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪsaɪ / (BrE)
to predict or reveal something, especially by or as if by divine inspiration

Examples

Because he spoke openly with what Channing had prophesied someone might - with daring hyperbole - Parker vindicated Channing's further prophecy that he who committed this infraction of taste would promptly discover how little mercy liberals were disposed to allow to libertarians who appeared to them libertines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predict - to say that something will happen in the future; often used more generally than 'prophesy'

Etymology

From Old French 'prophecier', from Late Latin 'prophetizāre', from Greek 'prophēteuein' (to be a prophet).
