stairwell

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛrˌwɛl / (AmE), / ˈstɛəˌwɛl / (BrE)
A vertical shaft or space in a building that contains a staircase.

Examples

The doctor, he noticed, was attempting a transverse movement towards the stairs, but before the movement could be completed a distinct and audible cluck ruffled the air in the hollow of the stair-well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staircase - a set of stairs and its surrounding structure

Etymology

Formed from 'stair' + 'well', where 'well' refers to a shaft or deep space in a building.
