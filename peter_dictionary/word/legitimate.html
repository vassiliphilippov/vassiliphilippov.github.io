legitimate

1. ADJ

/ ləˈdʒɪtəmət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
(of children or marriages) recognized as lawful according to the law or rules; born to legally married parents.

Examples

Rather, it is typical of the thousands of quacks who use phony therapeutic devices to fatten themselves on the miseries of hundreds of thousands of Americans by robbing them of millions of dollars and luring them away from legitimate, ethical medical treatment of serious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegitimate - not recognized as lawful; contrary to law or custom (especially of children or marriages)

Etymology

From Late Latin legitimatus, past participle of legitimare (to make lawful), from Latin legitimus (lawful).

2. ADJ

/ lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
Conforming to the law or to rules; authorized or sanctioned by law.

Examples

In 1930, the Lambeth Conference again affirmed the primary purpose of marriage to be the procreation of children, but conceded that, in certain limited circumstances, contraception might be morally legitimate.
"In five years the Corleone family "will be completely legitimate."

Synset Examples

a legitimate government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawful - permitted or recognized by law
  • Antonym: illegitimate - not authorized by law; unlawful

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'legitimatus', past participle of 'legitimare' (to make lawful), from Latin 'legitimus' (lawful).

3. ADJ

/ ləˈdʒɪtɪmət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
Conforming to accepted rules, standards, or principles.

Examples

Leadership is lacking in our society because it has no legitimate place to develop.

Synset Examples

legitimate advertising practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proper - fitting, right, or appropriate according to standards
  • Antonym: illegitimate - not in accordance with accepted standards; not legitimate

Etymology

From Latin 'legitimus' (lawful), from 'lex' (law).

4. ADJ

/ lɪˈdʒɪtəmət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
(of a reason, claim, or action) justified or reasonable according to the facts, circumstances, or accepted standards.

Examples

He had a legitimate reason for wearing it.

Synset Examples

rain was a logical expectation, given the time of year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justifiable - able to be shown to be right or reasonable
  • Synonym: reasonable - having sound judgment; fair and sensible
  • Antonym: illegitimate - not authorized by the law; not in accordance with accepted standards

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legitimatus, past participle of legitimare 'make lawful', from Latin legitimus 'lawful', from lex, legis 'law'.
