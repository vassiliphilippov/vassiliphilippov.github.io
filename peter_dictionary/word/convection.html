convection

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the transfer of heat through a fluid (such as a liquid or gas) by the movement of its particles, often resulting in the circulation of the fluid

Examples

In plasma generators the superimposed forced convection may modify the picture somewhat.
This arrangement had the purpose to prevent heated gas to reach the thermocouple by natural convection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conduction - the transfer of heat through direct contact between substances
  • Antonym: radiation - the transfer of energy as electromagnetic waves

Etymology

From Latin 'convectio' (a bringing together), from 'convehere' (to carry together).
