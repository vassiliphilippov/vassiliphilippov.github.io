whistle

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
(of an object or entity) to make a whining, ringing, or high-pitched sound similar to that of a whistle.

Examples

The Catholics had the largest cemetery, near the Neversink River where Main Street ran south; Stevie whistled when he passed these alien grounds.

Synset Examples

the kettle was singing
the bullet sang past his ear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sing - to make a high-pitched or musical sound, especially of an object

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
To produce a clear, high-pitched sound by forcing breath through pursed lips or through a device.

Examples

I decided I hated the Pedersen kid too, dying in our kitchen while I was away where I could n't watch, dying just to entertain Hans and making me go up snapping steps and down a drafty hall, Pa lumped under the covers at the end like dung covered with snow, snoring and whistling.
Lord whistled tunelessly as he fought the steering wheel.

Synset Examples

He lay there, snoring and whistling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoot - to make a short, loud, high sound (like a horn or owl)

Etymology

Old English 'hwistlian', of imitative origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
to utter or express something by making a whistling sound, especially a tune or melody

Examples

The sniper, whether psychopathic marksman or murderer by intent, would hardly have walked to his vantage point with rifle over shoulder, whistling a marching tune.
[PEOPLE EXCLAIMING, CHEERING] [FIREWORKS WHISTLING, EXPLODING] [MAN SHOUTING IN SPANISH] [SPEAKING SPANISH]

Synset Examples

She whistled a melody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sing - to produce musical tones with the voice
  • Synonym: hum - to make a low continuous sound, often when expressing a tune with closed lips

Etymology

From Old English *hwistlian*, of imitation origin, related to the sound produced.

4. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
to move through the air with or as if with a whistling sound

Examples

His aim was hurried; so the pitchfork whistled over Curt's head.

Synset Examples

The bullets whistled past him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - to move quickly making a rushing noise
