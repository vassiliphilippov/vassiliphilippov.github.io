acclimatize

1. VERB

/ əˈklaɪ.məˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈklɪ.mə.taɪz / (BrE)
to become accustomed to a new climate, place, or situation; to adapt to new conditions.

Examples

Today however, the "outsider "is likely to have professional qualifications of the highest order (otherwise the college would not be interested in hiring him) and to be acclimatized to the democratic processes of the secular or state university.

Synset Examples

They never acclimatized in Egypt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclimate - to become accustomed to a new climate or environment

Etymology

From French *acclimater* (to acclimate), based on Latin *clima* (region, climate).
