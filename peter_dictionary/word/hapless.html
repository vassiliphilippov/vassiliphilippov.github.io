hapless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæpləs / (AmE), / ˈhæplɪs / (BrE)
Unlucky and deserving pity; unfortunate.

Examples

First, the student trains on his hapless parents the heavy artillery of his newly acquired psychological and sociological insights.

Synset Examples

a hapless victim
miserable victims of war
the shabby room struck her as extraordinarily pathetic
piteous appeals for help
pitiable homeless children

a wretched life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlucky - having or marked by bad luck
  • Antonym: fortunate - having good luck

Etymology

From Middle English *hap* (luck, fortune) + *-less* (without).
