pardon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
The act of forgiving or excusing someone for a mistake or offense.

Examples

"A thousand pardons ", said Hal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven
  • Antonym: condemnation - the act of blaming or punishing somebody

Etymology

From Old French 'pardon', from Late Latin 'perdonare' (to grant freely, forgive).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to forgive or excuse someone for a mistake or offense

Examples

"Please pardon me again, Sandalphon ", said Hal.
If laborers are merely commodities competing against each other in a market place like so many bags of wheat and corn (unsupported, by the way, by any agricultural subsidy), then they may be pardoned for reacting with complete antagonism to a system that imposes such status upon them.
Pardon me, but that's not a denial.

Synset Examples

Please excuse my dirty hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense, flaw, or mistake
  • Synonym: excuse - to seek or grant pardon for; to overlook a fault or offense
  • Antonym: blame - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong

Etymology

From Old French *pardoner*, from Medieval Latin *perdonare* (to give thoroughly, forgive).

3. VERB

/ ˈpɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to officially forgive someone for an offense or crime, especially by a head of state or authority, and free them from punishment

Examples

The military also had demanded pledges that there would be no changes in the laws passed by the junta and no leaders of the Menderes regime now in prison would be pardoned.
Mrs. Blanche Dunkel, 60, who has spent 25 years in the Dwight reformatory for women for the murder in 1935 of her son-in-law, Ervin Lang, then 28, appealed for a parole at a hearing yesterday before two Illinois pardon and parole board members, John M. Bookwalter and Joseph Carpentier.

Synset Examples

Ford pardoned Nixon
The Thanksgiving turkey was pardoned by the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward (someone) for an offense

Etymology

From Old French pardoner, from Medieval Latin perdonare ‘to grant forgiveness’.
