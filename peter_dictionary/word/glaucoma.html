glaucoma

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaʊˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ɡlɔːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A disease of the eye characterized by damage to the optic nerve, often associated with increased pressure within the eyeball, leading to impaired vision or blindness if untreated.

Examples

He can diagnose detachment of the retina where conventional methods indicate blindness due to glaucoma.

Synset Examples

contrary to popular belief, glaucoma is not always caused by elevated intraocular pressure

Etymology

From Greek glaukōma, meaning 'opacity of the lens'.
