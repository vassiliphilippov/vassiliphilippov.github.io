jangle

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒæŋɡəl / (BrE)
(of metal objects or similar) to make a harsh, discordant ringing or clanging sound.

Examples

For a serious young man who plays golf with a serious intensity, Palmer has such an inherent sense of humor that it relieves the strain and keeps his nerves from jangling like banjo strings.

Synset Examples

The keys were jingling in his pocket

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jingle - make a light, quick, ringing sound like small bells or coins

Etymology

Probably imitative of a harsh or discordant sound; related to Middle English 'janglen' (to chatter or dispute noisily).
