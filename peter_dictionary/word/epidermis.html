epidermis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɝːmɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɜːmɪs / (BrE)
The outermost layer of skin covering the body of vertebrates, serving as a protective barrier.

Examples

Frequently a few isolated thick walled cells or, rarely, groups of such cells in the xylem region, were also specifically stained, but there was no such staining in epidermis, cortex, most xylem cells, ray cells, or pith.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dermis - the thick layer of living tissue below the epidermis

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) + 'derma' (skin).
