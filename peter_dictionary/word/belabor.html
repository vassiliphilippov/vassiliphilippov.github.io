belabor

1. VERB

/ bɪˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪbə / (BrE)
To discuss or treat a subject at excessive length or with more detail than is necessary.

Examples

The world might ring with the struggle, if we devoted ourselves exclusively to belaboring each other ".

Synset Examples

belabor the obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwell on - to speak or write at length about a particular topic

Etymology

From Middle English 'belaboren', from be- (intensive prefix) + labor.
