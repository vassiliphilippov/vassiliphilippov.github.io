orthodox

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔr.θə.dɑks / (AmE), / ˈɔː.θə.dɒks / (BrE)
following or believing in what is generally accepted as right or true; traditional or conventional, especially in matters of religion or ideology.

Examples

After all, Shelley is no "orthodox "or Hellenic Platonist, and even his "romantic "Platonism can be distinguished from that of his contemporaries.
Or a Protestant, or one of those amusing dogmatic atheists, or a strict orthodox Communist.
Devout, orthodox and plain like a family she might meet in Brooklyn or Malta or Ireland.

Synset Examples

an orthodox view of the world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unorthodox - not conforming to established or traditional beliefs

Etymology

From Greek orthodoxos (‘having the right opinion’), from orthos (‘right, true’) + doxa (‘opinion’).
