button-down

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌtən ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌtən ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of a person, attitude, or style) unimaginatively conventional or very restrained and traditional.

Examples

The Ivy League enjoys no easy dominion here, and the boys are as likely to dress in rather foppish Continental fashion, or even in nondescript working class manner, as they are in the restrained, button-down Ivy way.

Synset Examples

a colorful character in the buttoned-down, dull-grey world of business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - following traditional forms and genres
  • Antonym: unconventional - not conforming to established customs or attitudes

Etymology

Figurative use from the button-down (collared shirt), associated with conservative business attire.
