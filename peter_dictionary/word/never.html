never

1. ADV

/ ˈnɛvər / (AmE), / ˈnevə / (BrE)
At no time in the past, present, or future; not ever.

Examples

Although he never gets to play while the clock is running, he gets a big kick - several every Saturday, in fact - out of football.
I never did miss one in a playoff game - I kicked about 20 in the five playoff games my last two years ".
The Bears took the lead in the first inning, as they did in Sunday's opener, and never lagged.
He said he had never talked to Liston ".
The Continental League never got off the ground, but after two years it forced the existing majors to expand.

Synset Examples

I have never been to China
I shall never forget this day
had never seen a circus
never on Sunday
I will never marry you!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ever - at any time
  • Antonym: always - at all times; on all occasions

Etymology

From Old English 'nǣfre' (‘not ever’), from 'ne' (‘not’) + 'ǣfre' (‘ever’).

2. ADV

/ ˈnɛvər / (AmE), / ˈnɛvə / (BrE)
Not at any time; not on any occasion; in no circumstances.

Examples

There would be no way, Michael, no way you could ever forgive me.
There would be no way, Michael, no way you could ever forgive me.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ever - at any time; on any occasion

Etymology
