pray

1. VERB

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
to speak to God or another deity, especially to give thanks or ask for help

Examples

Open your heart to Him and pray, Stephen, pray!
Open your heart to Him and pray, Stephen, pray!
He strove to think of God and His eternal wrath; he must pray to be spared.
Not even Varnessa; she, too, prayed only to God.
Tears burned behind his eyes as he prayed and meditated tonight.

Synset Examples

pray to the Lord

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worship - show reverence and adoration for a deity
  • Degree Lower: meditate - focus thoughts as a spiritual practice

Etymology

From Latin 'precari' (to ask, entreat, pray).

2. VERB

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
To make an earnest or formal request for something, especially in official or legal contexts; to entreat or beseech.

Examples

"We, the Subscribers, do agree, that as soon as a convenient Number of Persons have subscribed to this, or a similar Writing, We will present a petition to the Hon' ble General Court of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, praying for an Act incorporating into a Body politic the subscribers to such Writing with Liberty to build such a Bridge, and a Right to demand a Toll equal to that received at Malden Bridge, and on like Terms, and if such an Act shall be obtained, then we severally agree each with the others, that we will hold in the said Bridge the several shares set against our respective Names, the whole into two hundred shares being divided, and that we will pay such sums of Money at such Times and in such Manners, as by the said proposed Corporation, shall be directed and required ".

Synset Examples

I beg you to stop!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully

Etymology

From Old French *preier*, from Latin *precari* (to entreat, beg).
