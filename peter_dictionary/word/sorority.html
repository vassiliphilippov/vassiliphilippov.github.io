sorority

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɔrəti / (AmE), / səˈrɒrəti / (BrE)
a social organization for female students at a college or university, primarily in the United States, whose members usually live together and participate in group activities

Examples

Parents will drive on Friday night to pick up their daughters after a sorority or House Plan meeting.
And the school newspaper sells space to jubilant fraternities, sororities, and houses (in the House Plan Association) that have good news to impart.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fraternity - a social club for male undergraduates

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sororitas (sisterhood), from Latin soror (sister).
