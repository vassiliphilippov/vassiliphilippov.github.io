lager

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːɡə / (BrE)
(historical) A camp defended by a circular formation of wagons, especially as used by settlers or military groups for protection.

Examples

A bevy of interlacing lagers, work camps, concentration camps erupted in the area.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'laer' (camp), from Dutch 'leger' (camp, army).
