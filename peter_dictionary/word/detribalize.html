detribalize

1. VERB

/ diˈtraɪbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈtraɪbəlaɪz / (BrE)
To cause the members of a tribe or social group to lose their cultural identity or traditional way of life.

Examples

Brooklyn College is unequivocally Jewish in tone, and efforts to detribalize the college by bringing in unimpeachably midwestern types on the faculty have been unavailing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilate - cause to become part of a different society or culture

Etymology

From de- (removal, reversal) + tribal + -ize.
