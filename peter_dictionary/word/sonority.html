sonority

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔrəti / (AmE), / səˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
the quality of having a rich, full, and resonant sound

Examples

The music is always allowed the living space needed to attain its full sonority; yet the hall never intrudes as a quasi performer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality of being deep, full, and reverberating

Etymology

From Latin 'sonoritas', from 'sonorus' (sonorous).
