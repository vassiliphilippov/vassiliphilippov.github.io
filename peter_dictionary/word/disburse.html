disburse

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈbɜrs / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːs / (BrE)
to pay out money, especially from a fund or as part of an official process

Examples

All payments authorized under section 7 of this Title shall be disbursed from the proper fund, as the case may be, and all amounts covered into the Treasury to the credit of the aforesaid funds are hereby permanently appropriated for the making of the payments authorized by section 7 of this Title.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expend - to spend or use up resources such as money, time, or energy
  • Antonym: reimburse - to pay back money that has been spent or lost

Etymology

From Old French *desbourser* (to pay out), from des- (dis-) + bourse (purse).
