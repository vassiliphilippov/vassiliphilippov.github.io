lash

1. VERB

/ læʃ / (AmE), / læʃ / (BrE)
to strike or beat forcefully with a whip, rod, or similar object, especially as a form of punishment

Examples

For the remainder of the movie, Chancellor Neitzbohr proceeds to lash the piano stool with a slat from a Venetian blind that used to hang in the pre-war Reichstag.
He heaved the dead man onto the buckboard, yelled and lashed at the team and got out of there fast.
Fairmount Park Commission Does each tentacle of the octopus of City Government reach out and lash at whatever it dislikes or considers an annoyance?

Synset Examples

The teacher often flogged the students
The children were severely trounced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flog - to beat with a whip or stick as punishment
  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a flexible instrument such as a whip
  • Degree Lower: spank - to slap on the buttocks as a punishment
  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly, typically as punishment

Etymology

From Middle English *lashen*, of uncertain origin; possibly imitative or related to Scandinavian forms.
