flu

1. NOUN

/ flu / (AmE), / flu / (BrE)
A common, highly contagious viral infection causing fever and body aches; short for influenza.

Examples

Harvey Haddix, set back by the flu this season, will start against his former Cardinal mates, who might be playing without captain Kenny Boyer in tonight's game at Busch Stadium.
Are you indiscriminantly offering unnecessary medical services - flu shots, sun lamp treatments, etc.?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influenza - technical term for flu, acute viral infection

Etymology

Shortened form of 'influenza,' from Italian 'influenza' (influence), originally referring to the influence of the stars on epidemics.
