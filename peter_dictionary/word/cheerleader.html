cheerleader

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪrˌliːdər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəˌliːdə / (BrE)
A person, typically at a sporting event, who leads and encourages the crowd to cheer for a team, often as part of an organized group performing chants and routines.

Examples

I made my bones when you were out with cheerleaders.

Etymology

From 'cheer' + 'leader', meaning 'one who leads cheers', first recorded in the early 20th century in American English.
