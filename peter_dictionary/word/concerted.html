concerted

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɝːtɪd / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːtɪd / (BrE)
(of actions) done together by two or more people, groups, or organizations working towards a common goal; planned or carried out in cooperation with others.

Examples

By holding out prospects for external capital assistance, the United States can provide strong incentives to prepare for the concerted economic drive necessary to achieve self-sustaining growth.

Synset Examples

concerted action
the conjunct influence of fire and strong wind
the conjunctive focus of political opposition
a cooperative effort
a united effort
joint military activities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperative - involving mutual assistance in working towards a common goal
  • Synonym: joint - involving two or more people together
  • Antonym: individual - done by a single person, not in cooperation

Etymology

From French 'concerter' (to arrange by agreement), from Italian 'concertare' (to harmonize).
