browbeat

1. VERB

/ ˈbraʊˌbit / (AmE), / ˈbraʊˌbiːt / (BrE)
to intimidate or bully someone into doing something, typically through stern or overbearing words or manner

Examples

He had n't shown up too well in their eyes, letting himself be browbeaten by a woman.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to persecute or intimidate someone weaker

Etymology

From 'brow' (forehead) + 'beat', originally meaning to coerce by stern looks.
