self-control

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌself.kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
The ability to control your emotions and actions, especially in difficult situations.

Examples

One is the primary purpose for which marriage exists, namely, the continuance of the race through the gift and heritage of children; the other is the paramount importance in married life of deliberate and thoughtful self-control ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willpower - the ability to control oneself and determine one's actions
  • Synonym: discipline - the ability to control behavior and obey rules
  • Antonym: impulsiveness - tendency to act on sudden urges without control

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'control,' first recorded in English in the 18th century.
