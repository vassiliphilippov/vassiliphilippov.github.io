suave

1. ADJ

/ swɑv / (AmE), / swɑːv / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) charming, confident, and elegant, especially in a way that is seen as smooth or sophisticated.

Examples

The already faded pastel charms of the naive music itself vanish entirely in Fistoulari's melodramatic contrasts between ultra vehement brute power and chilly, if suave, sentimentality.

Synset Examples

a debonair gentleman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debonair - charming, confident, and stylish
  • Synonym: urbane - courteous and refined in manner
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking social grace or ease
  • Antonym: clumsy - lacking finesse or grace

Etymology

From Latin 'suavis' meaning 'sweet, agreeable'.

2. ADJ

/ swɑv / (AmE), / swɑːv / (BrE)
(of a person or manner) charming, confident, and elegant, especially in a way that seems smooth and sophisticated.

Examples

Even so, Madden's dislike of the suave, correct lawyer deepened.

Synset Examples

he was too politic to quarrel with so important a personage
the manager pacified the customer with a smooth apology for the error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - having an even, polished, or effortless manner
  • Synonym: urbane - notably polite or polished in manner
  • Antonym: coarse - lacking refinement or sophistication

Etymology

From Latin suavis (sweet, agreeable).
