derivative

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɪvətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪvətɪv / (BrE)
A mathematical expression representing the rate of change of a function with respect to one of its variables; the result of differentiation.

Examples

The minimum total potential energy is found by taking the derivative with respect to H and equating to zero.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integral - a function representing the area under the curve of another function; the reverse of derivative

Etymology

From Medieval Latin derivativus (drawn off), from Latin derivare (to derive).
