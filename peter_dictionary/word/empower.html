empower

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaʊə / (BrE)
To give someone the power or authority to do something.

Examples

It seemed like a good time for officials to use a recently-passed law empowering the post office department to contract for the transport of first class mail by air.

Synset Examples

She authorized her assistant to sign the papers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorize - to give official permission or power to someone
  • Synonym: enable - to make able or give the means to do something

Etymology

From Old French 'empouwer', formed from 'em-' (variant of 'en-') + 'power'.
