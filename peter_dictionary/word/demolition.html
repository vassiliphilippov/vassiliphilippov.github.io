demolition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
The act of deliberately destroying a building or other structure, or the process of doing this.

Examples

And in the last five years, the "Methodist chapel committee has authorized the demolition or, more often, the sale of 764 chapels ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying or state of being destroyed

Etymology

From Latin 'demolīrī' (to tear down), via Old French 'demolir'.
