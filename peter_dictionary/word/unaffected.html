unaffected

1. ADJ

/ ʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
not changed or influenced by something; remaining the same when acted upon.

Examples

And so the authors conclude: "The conduct of the patient in his every-day life and in his work, even more than the foregoing facts [ mentioned above under 1 ], leave positively no room for doubt that the sense of touch, in the ordinary sense of the word, was unaffected; or, to put the same thing in physiological terms, that the performance capacity of the tactual apparatus, from the periphery up to the tactual centers in the brain, - that is, from one end to the other - was unimpaired ".

Synset Examples

entirely unaffected by each other's writings
fibers remained apparently unaffected by the treatment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: altered - changed in form or character
  • Antonym: changed - made different in some way

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'affected', from Latin 'affectus' (influenced, touched).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
not influenced, changed, or moved emotionally by something; indifferent or unaware

Examples

Not a man on the brig, loyal or villainous, could be unaffected by the sight of seven men involved in the crime of mutiny.

Synset Examples

insensible to the suffering around him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or concern; unconcerned
  • Synonym: insensible - unaware of or indifferent to; not affected by something
  • Antonym: moved - emotionally affected

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + affected (influenced or changed)
