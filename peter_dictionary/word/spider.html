spider

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə / (BrE)
A small predatory arachnid with eight legs, two poison fangs, and usually two silk-spinning organs, known for spinning silk webs to trap prey or protect eggs.

Examples

Also fine are Sue Lawless, as a mother more protective and belligerent than a female spider and just as destructive, Harold Cherry, as her scratchy spouse, and Hildy Weissman, as a vegetable in human form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arachnid - A class of joint-legged invertebrate animals including spiders, scorpions, and ticks

Etymology

Old English 'spīthra', related to 'spinnan' (to spin), referring to silk-spinning behavior.
