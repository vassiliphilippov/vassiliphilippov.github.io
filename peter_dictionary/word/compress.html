compress

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmprɛs / (BrE)
a thick, soft piece of cloth that is pressed against a part of the body to reduce pain, swelling, or fever, often after being heated or cooled

Examples

His prescription: hot and cold compresses to increase her absorption of water.
Argiento never left his side, keeping the water boiled, applying hot compresses throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing - a piece of material used to cover and protect a wound

Etymology

From French *compresse*, from Old French *compresser* (to press together), from Latin *comprimere*.
