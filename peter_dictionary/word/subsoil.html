subsoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɔɪl / (BrE)
the layer of soil immediately beneath the topsoil and above the bedrock, typically containing less organic material

Examples

Check the elevation of the ground, degree and direction of slopes, drainage, rock outcrops, topsoil types and quality, as well as subsoil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: topsoil - the uppermost layer of soil, rich in organic matter

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'soil'.
