capo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːˌpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpəʊ / (BrE)
(in the Mafia) the head of a branch of an organized crime syndicate; a high-ranking member who typically leads a group of soldiers.

Examples

You were around the old-timers, who dreamed of how the families should be organized, basing them on the old Roman legions, and call '’em "regimes," the "capos" and "soldiers."

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boss - the leader of a criminal organization; the top ranking member
  • Degree Lower: underboss - the second-in-command in a Mafia family

Etymology

From Italian 'capo' meaning 'head' or 'chief', from Latin 'caput' (head).
