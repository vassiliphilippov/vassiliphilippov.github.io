slat

1. NOUN

/ slæt / (AmE), / slæt / (BrE)
a thin, narrow strip of wood, metal, or other material, often used in furniture, window blinds, or similar structures

Examples

For the remainder of the movie, Chancellor Neitzbohr proceeds to lash the piano stool with a slat from a Venetian blind that used to hang in the pre-war Reichstag.
And, when the slat finally shatters, we see him count the fragments, all the while muttering, "He loves me, he loves me not ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - a long, narrow piece of material
  • Degree Higher: plank - a long, flat piece of timber, thicker and wider than a slat

Etymology

Probably from Old French esclat (splinter, chip), related to 'slat' meaning 'split piece'.
