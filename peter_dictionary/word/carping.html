carping

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪŋ / (BrE)
The action of continually finding petty or unjustified fault with someone or something.

Examples

But Blanche had been able to maintain a serene and assured composure in the face of her widowed mother's continued carping, had been able to resist her urgings to date anyone who offered the slightest possibility of matrimony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval based on perceived faults
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English 'carpen' (to talk, prattle, find fault), from Old Norse 'karpa' (to boast).
