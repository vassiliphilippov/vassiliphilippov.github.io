pemmican

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛmɪkən / (AmE), / ˈpɛmɪkən / (BrE)
A mixture of lean dried meat pounded fine and blended with melted fat, traditionally prepared by Indigenous peoples of North America as a high-energy food.

Examples

Others carried pemmican from "the Forks "to St. Paul and goods from St. Paul to Red River, as in the summer of 1847 when one trader, Wells, transported twenty barrels of whisky to the British settlement.

Etymology

From Cree 'pimîhkân', from 'pimî' (fat, grease).
