skim

1. VERB

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to move quickly and lightly just above or along the surface, especially of water

Examples

Moments later the V-1 skimmed across the landing strip, edging closer and closer to a touchdown - then in a streamer of dust it landed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - move smoothly and continuously along a surface

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *skima* (to shine, glitter), related to moving lightly over a surface.

2. VERB

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to move or pass swiftly and lightly over or along the surface of something

Examples

Often, threading through the overcast, he was forced to fly close to the ground by a low ceiling, skimming above the Winooski or the White River along the line of the broken railroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort or resistance
  • Synonym: float - to rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'skymen', from Old Norse 'skima' (to shine, gleam) or related to 'skum' (foam, scum).

3. VERB

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to remove a substance from the surface of a liquid, especially by gliding a utensil lightly across it

Examples

There are a hundred different ways to skim off the top.

Synset Examples

skim cream from the surface of milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - to pick something up and remove it with a spoon or ladle; synonym in the context of liquids

Etymology

Probably from Old French eskimer (to clear away), of Germanic origin.
