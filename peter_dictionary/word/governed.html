governed

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnd / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənd / (BrE)
The people who are subject to the authority of a government; citizens or inhabitants ruled by a governing body.

Examples

The Declaration of Independence says that "governments derive their just powers from the consent of the governed ".
The phrase "consent of the governed "needs a hard look.
Is the consent of the governed a numerical majority?
Who will deny that in a vast portion of the South the Federal action is incompatible with the Jeffersonian concept of "the consent of the governed "?

Synset Examples

governments derive their just powers from the consent of the governed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: governor - person who governs or rules
  • Synonym: government - the governing body or authority over the governed

Etymology

From the past participle of 'govern', ultimately from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, to rule).
