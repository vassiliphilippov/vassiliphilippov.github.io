sharecropper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛrˌkrɑpər / (AmE), / ˈʃeəˌkrɒpə / (BrE)
A farmer who works land owned by another person and pays a share of the crops raised as rent.

Examples

"They knew I was a good sharecrop farmer back in Carolina, but out West was a chance to build a real farm of our own.

Etymology

From 'sharecrop', referring to sharing the crop as payment, plus '-er'.
