bas-relief

1. NOUN

/ bɑː rəˈlif / (AmE), / bɑː rəˈliːf / (BrE)
A type of sculptural relief in which the figures project only slightly from the background and are not undercut.

Examples

The affixed elements of collage were extruded, as it were, and cut off from the literal pictorial surface to form a bas-relief.

Etymology

From French bas-relief, from 'bas' (low) + 'relief' (raised work).
