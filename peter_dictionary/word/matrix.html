matrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (BrE)
A rectangular array of numbers or symbols arranged in rows and columns, used in mathematics to represent and manipulate data according to specific rules.

Examples

A set of tables containing spectral intensities for 39000 lines of 70 elements, as observed in a copper matrix in a d-c arc, was completed and published.

Etymology

From Latin matrix (breeding female, womb, source), from mater (mother).
