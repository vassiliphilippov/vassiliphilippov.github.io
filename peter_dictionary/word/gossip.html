gossip

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːsɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪp / (BrE)
a rumor or report, often malicious, about the private affairs or behavior of other people

Examples

The monk Savonarola, brought over from the Renaissance and placed against the background of Munich at the turn of the century, protests against the luxurious works displayed in the art shop of M. Bluthenzweig; in particular against a Madonna portrayed in a voluptuous style and modeled, according to gossip, upon the painter's mistress.
And the forked tongue of gossip licked its sinister way from back porch to back porch.

Synset Examples

the divorce caused much gossip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumor - unsubstantiated information or report
  • Degree Lower: news - information about recent events or happenings

Etymology

From Old English *godsibb* (godparent, literally 'God-relative'), later shifting in meaning to refer to talkative acquaintances at christenings and social events.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑsɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪp / (BrE)
casual or light informal conversation, especially about other people's private affairs

Examples

She soared over the new pastor like an avenging angel lest he stray from the path and not know all the truth and gossip of which she was chief repository.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chitchat - casual talk or informal conversation
  • Synonym: chat - informal conversation or talk

Etymology

From Old English 'godsibb,' meaning a godparent or close family friend, later extended to refer to a person who spreads rumors.
