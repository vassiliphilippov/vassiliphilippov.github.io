New Orleans

1. PROPN

/ nu ˈɔrlɪnz, ˈnju ˈɔrlinz, ˌnu ɔrˈliːnz, nu ɔrˈliːənz / (AmE), / njuː ɔːˈliːnz, ˌnjuː ɔːˈliːənz / (BrE)
a major city and port in southeastern Louisiana, USA, known for its vibrant culture, jazz music, and annual Mardi Gras celebrations

Examples

The young William Faulkner in New Orleans in the 1920's impressed the novelist Hamilton Basso as obviously conscious of being a Southerner, and there is no evidence that since then he has ever considered himself any less so.
She had lost a bottle of opium - but that was on the trip from New Orleans.

Etymology

From French 'La Nouvelle-Orléans', named in honor of Philippe II, Duke of Orléans.
