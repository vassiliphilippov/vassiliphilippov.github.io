delivery

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the act of bringing goods, mail, or other items to a person or place; the process of distributing something to designated recipients

Examples

The major defensive problems are concerned with the possibility of overt military delivery of biological agents from appropriate disseminating devices.
The same delivery vehicles - whether they be airplanes, submarines or guided missiles - should be usable.
Biological agents are, moreover, suitable for delivery through enemy sabotage which imposes many problems in defense.
"If you put up 3-4,000 dollar investment, we can make 50,000 distributing."

Synset Examples

his reluctant delivery of bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among recipients
  • Antonym: collection - the act of picking things up or gathering items to be delivered

Etymology

From Old French 'delivrer' (to set free), from Latin 'de-' (from) + 'liberare' (to set free).
