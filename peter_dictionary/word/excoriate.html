excoriate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈskɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɔːrɪeɪt / (BrE)
to criticize or denounce severely and publicly

Examples

We today are not entitled to excoriate honest men who believed Parker to be downright pernicious and who barred their pulpits against his demand to poison the minds of their congregations.

Synset Examples

We condemn the racism in South Africa
These ideas were reprobated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemn - to express complete disapproval, often in public
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of

Etymology

From Latin 'excoriare' (to strip the skin), from 'ex-' (out) + 'corium' (skin).
