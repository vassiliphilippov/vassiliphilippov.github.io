intentionally

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɛnʃənəli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃənəli / (BrE)
In a deliberate or purposeful manner; with intention.

Examples

What I am suggesting is that when we delay, or when we fail to act, we do so intentionally and not through inadvertence or through bureaucratic or procedural difficulties.

Synset Examples

he used that word intentionally
I did this by choice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberately - in a deliberate or planned way; not by accident
  • Antonym: accidentally - in an accidental or unintended way; by chance

Etymology

From 'intentional' + '-ly', where 'intentional' comes from Latin 'intentio' (an aim or purpose) + '-al'.
