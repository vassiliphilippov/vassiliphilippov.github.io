reevaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of assessing or judging something again, especially in light of new information or circumstances

Examples

This has necessitated a continuous review and reevaluation of the defense program in order to redirect resources to the newer and more important weapons systems and to eliminate or reduce effort on weapons systems which have been overtaken by events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaluation - the process of making a judgment about something's value or quality

Etymology

From re- (again) + evaluation, formed after evaluate.
