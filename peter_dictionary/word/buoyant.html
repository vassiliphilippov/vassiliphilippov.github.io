buoyant

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪənt, ˈbuːjənt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪənt, ˈbjuːjənt / (BrE)
cheerful and lively; showing high spirits and lightheartedness

Examples

Hindemith's joust with Weber tunes was a considerably more serious misfortune, for it demands translucent textures, buoyant rhythms, and astringent wit.

Synset Examples

buoyant spirits
his quick wit and chirpy humor
looking bright and well and chirpy
a perky little widow in her 70s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloomy - lacking in cheer, feeling sad or pessimistic

Etymology

From French *bouyant*, present participle of *bouyer* (to float), ultimately from Spanish *boyar* (to float). Extended in English to mean 'cheerful, lively'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪənt, ˈbuːjənt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪənt, ˈbjuːjənt / (BrE)
Able to float or rise easily in a liquid or gas.

Examples

Social invention did not have to await social theory any more than use of the warmth of a fire had to await Lavoisier or the buoyant protection of a boat the formulations of Archimedes.

Synset Examples

buoyant balloons
buoyant balsawood boats
a floaty scarf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floaty - tending to float; light and able to remain on the surface

Etymology

From French *bouyant*, present participle of *bouyer* (‘to float’), from Spanish *boyar*, ultimately from Latin *bullire* (‘to bubble, boil’).
