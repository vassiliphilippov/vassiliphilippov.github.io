sirloin

1. NOUN

/ sɜrˌlɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.lɔɪn / (BrE)
a cut of beef from the upper part of the loin, just in front of the rump

Examples

Still existing on a "Northern Union "telegraph form is a typical peremptory message from Peru grocer J. J. Hapgood to Burton and Graves' store in Manchester - "Get and send by stage sure four pounds best Porterhouse or sirloin steak, for Mrs. Hapgood send six sweet oranges ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porterhouse - a thick cut of steak from the short loin

Etymology

From Old French *surlonge* (above the loin), from sur- (over) + longe (loin).
