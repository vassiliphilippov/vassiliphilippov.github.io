corticosteroid

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔrtəkoʊˈstɛrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈstɛərɔɪd / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex or a synthetic equivalent, used medically to reduce inflammation and suppress immune responses

Examples

Muscle weakness is now recognized as an uncommon though serious complication of steroid therapy, with most of the synthetic adrenal corticosteroids in clinical use.

Synset Examples

adrenal cortical steroids are used to treat many different conditions

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glucocorticoid - a type of corticosteroid involved in glucose metabolism
  • Degree Lower: mineralocorticoid - a type of corticosteroid involved in mineral and water balance

Etymology

From cortex (Latin, 'bark, outer layer') + steroid.
