superficial

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
Not thorough, deep, or complete; concerned only with what is obvious or on the surface, lacking depth emotionally or intellectually.

Examples

"Since 1692 a great but superficial change has wiped out God's beard and the Devil's horns, but the world is still gripped between two diametrically opposed absolutes.
The result has been neglect, fumbling efforts, or superficial treatment.

Synset Examples

superficial similarities
a superficial mind
his thinking was superficial and fuzzy
superficial knowledge
the superficial report didn't give the true picture
only superficial differences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallow - lacking depth emotionally or intellectually
  • Antonym: profound - showing great depth or insight
  • Antonym: deep - having or showing depth of intellect, emotion, or knowledge

Etymology

From Latin superficialis, from superficies ‘surface’.
