stagger

1. VERB

/ ˈstæɡər / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall, often due to weakness, injury, or difficulty

Examples

In the very front rank, two men were wounded and staggered along, trailing blood behind them.
Fists pummeled him as he staggered forward.
Being a teacher of American literature, I remembered Whittier's "Massachusetts to Virginia ", where he said: "But that one dark loathsome burden ye must stagger with alone, And reap the bitter harvest which ye yourselves have sown ".
He staggered over and dropped it on the table.
She got to her feet, staggered, and almost fell.

Synset Examples

He staggered along in the heavy snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totter - to walk unsteadily as if about to fall
  • Synonym: lurch - to make an abrupt, uncontrolled movement, especially forward or sideways
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction (opposite stability)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'staggen', related to Old Norse 'stakra' (to push, stagger).

2. VERB

/ ˈstæɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
To arrange events, times, or objects so that they are not all at the same time or in exactly the same position, often to avoid crowding or overlap.

Examples

Can staggered lunch periods relieve the capacity strain on your feeding facilities?

Synset Examples

stagger the chairs in the lecture hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternate - to arrange or occur by turns or in succession

Etymology

From Old Norse *stakra*, meaning 'to push, shove'.

3. VERB

/ ˈstæɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to walk or move unsteadily, as if unable to control one's movements, often as a result of being ill, injured, drunk, or shocked

Examples

McBride staggered into the street, flopped sprawling in the stinging dust.
It gave Curt time to stagger to his feet.
Pat saw Gyp Carmer staggering forward, a half filled bottle upraised as if to strike.
He staggered into the back seat and lay back, fighting for breath.
He staggered back to the group.

Synset Examples

The drunken man staggered into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - to move in a way that is not steady, as if you are going to fall
  • Synonym: lurch - to make a sudden, unsteady movement forward or sideways

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse *stakra* (to push, stagger).
