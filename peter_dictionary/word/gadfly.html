gadfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæd.flʌɪ / (BrE)
a person who persistently annoys or provokes others, especially by criticism or questioning

Examples

"My beloved ward, my perennial gadfly ", said the whining voice.
Once out in the street, the traffic was a gadfly maze in which he wandered stricken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a person or thing causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Synonym: provocateur - person who provokes others, especially to action

Etymology

Originally denoting an insect that bites livestock; applied figuratively to people in the 17th century.
