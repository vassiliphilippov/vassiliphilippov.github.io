landowner

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈlændˌəʊnə / (BrE)
A person who owns land, especially large amounts of land.

Examples

From there it looked across at Westchester County and the Hudson River where the manor houses, estates, and big farms of the original (non Indian) landowners began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprietor - a person who owns something, such as land, a business, or property

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + 'owner', combining Old English 'land' (ground, earth) and 'owner' from Old English 'āgen' (to possess).
