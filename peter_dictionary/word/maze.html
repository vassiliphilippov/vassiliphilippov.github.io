maze

1. NOUN

/ meɪz / (AmE), / meɪz / (BrE)
A network of winding or interconnected paths, designed to be difficult to navigate or to find the way out of.

Examples

Once out in the street, the traffic was a gadfly maze in which he wandered stricken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labyrinth - a complicated, irregular network of passages or paths

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *mǣs* (delusion, bewilderment).
