electrophorus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑfərəs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒfərəs / (BrE)
a device used to generate static electricity by induction, consisting of a metal plate and an insulating material

Examples

Thus, 1745 corresponded to the invention of the "Leiden "jar by Kleist, 1764 that of the electrophorus by Wilcke, 1782 produced the condenser of Volta, and 1801 the voltaic pile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: van de graaff generator - device that produces high voltages using a moving belt to accumulate charge
  • Synonym: leiden jar - early device used to store static electric charge

Etymology

From Greek 'ēlektron' (amber, for electricity) + '-phoros' (bearing, carrying).
