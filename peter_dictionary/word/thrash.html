thrash

1. VERB

/ θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
to move around in a violent or uncontrolled way, often because of pain, fear, or agitation

Examples

The startled animal let out a terrified squeal and thrashed around in the stall.

Synset Examples

The feverish patient thrashed around in his bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flail - to wave or swing violently; to move arms or legs in an uncontrolled way
  • Synonym: writhe - to twist and turn, especially from pain or discomfort

Etymology

Probably from an earlier sense meaning 'beat repeatedly', from Old English 'þrescian' (to thresh).
