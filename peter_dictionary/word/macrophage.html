macrophage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækɹəˌfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmak.ɹəˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
A large white blood cell that engulfs and digests foreign substances, bacteria, and dead or dying cells, playing a key role in the immune system.

Examples

Macrophages laden with brown pigment were seen in some of the alveoli, and the intima of some of the small arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phagocyte - cell that engulfs and digests particles and bacteria
  • Degree Lower: lymphocyte - type of white blood cell involved in the immune response
  • Degree Lower: neutrophil - type of phagocytic white blood cell

Etymology

From French *macrophage*, from Greek *makro-* (large) + *phagein* (to eat).
