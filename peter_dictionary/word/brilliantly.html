brilliantly

1. ADV

/ ˈbrɪljəntli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪljəntli / (BrE)
In an extremely intelligent or clever manner.

Examples

She topped the sextet brilliantly.

Synset Examples

he solved the problem brilliantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleverly - in a clever or ingenious manner
  • Antonym: stupidly - in a foolish or unintelligent manner

Etymology

From 'brilliant' (from French brillant, present participle of briller 'to shine') + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈbrɪliəntli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪljəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is very bright or shining.

Examples

At the end of the performance, Dave and Max came out into the brilliantly lit foyer among a surge of gowned and tuxedoed first nighters.
During a 1933 tsunami in Japan the sea glowed brilliantly at night.
Slowly the procession of warriors and statesmen passes through the snow beside the black water and into the brilliantly lighted cathedral, the shrine of so many precious memories.

Synset Examples

the stars shone brilliantly
the windows glowed jewel bright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightly - in a bright manner

Etymology

From 'brilliant' + '-ly'; 'brilliant' from French 'brillant', from 'briller' (to shine).
