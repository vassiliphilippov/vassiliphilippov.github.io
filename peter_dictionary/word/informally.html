informally

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈfɔrməli / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːməli / (BrE)
In a casual or relaxed manner, without observing formal rules or procedures.

Examples

(Music often sounds best to me when I can dress informally and sit in something more comfortable than a theatre seat.)
The doctor, since Scotty was no longer allowed to make his regular trips into town to see him, came often and informally to the house.

Synset Examples

he visited us informally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formally - in a formal or ceremonious manner

Etymology

From 'informal' + '-ly'. 'Informal' from Late Latin 'informalis' (unformed) + English '-ly' adverbial suffix.
