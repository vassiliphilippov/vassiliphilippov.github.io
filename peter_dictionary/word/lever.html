lever

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːvər, ˈlɛvər / (AmE), / ˈliːvə, ˈlɛvə / (BrE)
A rigid bar that is used to lift or move a load by being pivoted on a fixed point (fulcrum).

Examples

The American firearms and ammunition manufacturers through diligent research and technical development have replaced the muzzle loader and slow firing single shot arms with modern fast firing autoloaders, extremely accurate bolt, lever, and slide action firearms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulcrum - the point on which a lever pivots

Etymology

From Old French *levier*, from lever 'to lift', from Latin *levare* 'to raise'.
