adsorb

1. VERB

/ ædˈzɔrb / (AmE), / ədˈzɔːb / (BrE)
(of a solid or liquid) to accumulate molecules of a gas or liquid on its surface.

Examples

However, f adsorbs** water from the atmosphere and this may account for the low chromium analysis and high total weight loss.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up completely; not just on the surface

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'sorbēre' (to suck in).
