debut

1. NOUN

/ deɪˈbjuː / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bjuː / (BrE)
the first public appearance or introduction of a person or thing, especially in a professional or formal context

Examples

Rabb, the former Louisiana State field general, came off the bench for his debut with the Bills Sunday and directed his new team to a 22 - 12 upset victory over the Houston Oilers, defending league champions.
Even Joan Sutherland may not have anticipated the tremendous reception she received from the Metropolitan Opera audience attending her debut as Lucia in Donizetti's "Lucia di Lammermoor "Sunday night.
Her debut over, perhaps the earlier scenes will emerge equally fine.
The performance also marked the debut of a most promising young conductor, Silvio Varviso.
It was a brilliant debut, so much so indeed that it aroused a new vitality in the younger poets, as did Byron's Childe Harold.

Synset Examples

they looked forward to the debut of their new product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - the first public performance or showing of a work

Etymology

From French 'début' (a first appearance), from 'débuter' (to start, begin).

2. VERB

/ ˈdeɪˌbju / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bjuː / (BrE)
(of a performer, work, product, etc.) to appear or be presented in public for the first time.

Examples

La Russell's run in "Skylark ", debuting next week at Drury Lane, already is a sellout.

Synset Examples

The new ballet that debuts next months at Covent Garden, is already sold out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - to give the first public performance of (a play, film, etc.)

Etymology

From French *débuter* (to make a first appearance), from *début* (first appearance).

3. VERB

/ deɪˈbju / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bjuː / (BrE)
To present or introduce something for the first time to the public.

Examples

Dave Trager, who is quite a showman and boss of Chicago's new pro basketball Packers, is debuting a new International club, for the exclusive use of season ticket holders, in the Stock Yards Inn.
Vivacious redhead debuts another shop, her sixth, in the Governor's Club Hotel this week.

Synset Examples

The band debuts a new song or two each month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - to perform or present for the first time
  • Synonym: launch - to introduce or start a new product or venture

Etymology

From French *débuter* (to make the first move in a game), from *début* (a first appearance).
