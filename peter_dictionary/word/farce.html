farce

1. NOUN

/ fɑrs / (AmE), / fɑːs / (BrE)
A type of comedy that uses exaggerated, absurd situations and broad humor, often characterized by improbability and satire.

Examples

H. E. Bates has scribbled a farce called "Hark, Hark, the Lark "!
"Litigants and witnesses were put to the expense and inconvenience of going long distances to transact business; public money spent; justice delayed; nothing accomplished, and the whole distribution of justice in this county seems to be an absolute farce ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: satire - a genre using humor, irony or exaggeration to criticize
  • Antonym: tragedy - serious drama representing sad events

Etymology

From French *farce* (stuffing, joke), from Late Latin *farcīre* (to stuff).
