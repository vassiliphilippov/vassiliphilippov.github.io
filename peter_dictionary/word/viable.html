viable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be done or achieved successfully; feasible.

Examples

The Aristotelian notion of catharsis, the purging of emotion, is a persistent and viable one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • Antonym: impractical - not sensible or realistic; unworkable

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) via French 'viable' (capable of living).
