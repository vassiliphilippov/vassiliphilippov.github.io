waterline

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtɚˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
The line on the hull of a ship or boat that marks the level that the surface of the water reaches when the vessel is afloat on an even keel.

Examples

The area may provide good springs or opportunities for a well or be near to municipal water lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plimsoll line - a marking on a ship's side showing safe water levels
