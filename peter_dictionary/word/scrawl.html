scrawl

1. VERB

/ skrɔl / (AmE), / skrɔːl / (BrE)
To write or draw something in a hurried, careless, or untidy way, often making it hard to read.

Examples

date of laying, length of incubation period, number of chicks reaching the first week, second week, fifth week, weight of hen, size of rooster's wattles and so on, all scrawled out in a hand that looked more Chinese than English, the most jagged and sprawling Alex had ever seen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribble - write or draw something quickly or carelessly

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect skråla (to scrawl, scribble).
