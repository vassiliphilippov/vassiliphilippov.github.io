butcher

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃə / (BrE)
to kill animals, usually for food consumption

Examples

Concerning the sentence the editor asked, "What else can Virginia do than to hang the men who have defied her laws, organized treason, and butchered her citizens ".

Synset Examples

They slaughtered their only goat to survive the winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughter - to kill animals for food, often in large numbers

Etymology

From Old French 'bochier', from 'bouc' (he-goat), referring to a slaughterer of goats and other animals.
