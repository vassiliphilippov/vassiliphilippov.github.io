reload

1. VERB

/ riˈloʊd / (AmE), / riːˈləʊd / (BrE)
to load something, such as a gun or device, again after it has been used or emptied

Examples

Meanwhile, I reloaded my gun, as the other men were doing.

Synset Examples

She reloaded the gun carefully

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: load - to put something into a device or container so it is ready for use

Etymology

From re- (again) + load.

2. VERB

/ riˈloʊd / (AmE), / riˈləʊd / (BrE)
to load something again, especially after it has been emptied or used; to put a new amount of something into a container, vehicle, or device

Examples

That means that he fired the gun empty, and then stopped to reload... ..so that he could shoot each of them again.

Synset Examples

The movers reloaded the truck

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unload - to remove a load from something
  • Degree Lower: load - to put a load in or on

Etymology

From re- (again) + load (to put a load on).
