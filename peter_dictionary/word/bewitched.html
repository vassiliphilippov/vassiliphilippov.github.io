bewitched

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈwɪtʃt / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪtʃt / (BrE)
Under a spell; enchanted or charmed, as if by magic.

Examples

Not everyone is bewitched by Byron's caliphs and harem beauties ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanted - Under a spell; magically charmed
  • Synonym: spellbound - Having one's attention held by something magical or fascinating
  • Antonym: cursed - Affected by a curse or evil spell

Etymology

From the verb 'bewitch', itself from be- (expressing effect) + 'witch' (from Old English 'wicce', meaning 'female magician or sorceress').
