icy

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪsi / (AmE), / ˈaɪsi / (BrE)
(of a manner, expression, or tone) cold and unfriendly; lacking warmth or cordiality.

Examples

Miss Stuart is as intensely vibrant as one could wish, almost an icy shriek threatening to explode at any moment.
And, slowly, feeling himself icy yet burning, Hal turned.
You strike me as a particularly icy and remorseless man, Mr. Dufresne.

Synset Examples

a frigid greeting
got a frosty reception
a frozen look on their faces
a glacial handshake
icy stare
wintry smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frigid - very cold in manner or feeling; lacking warmth or emotion
  • Synonym: frosty - showing or feeling no warmth; cold in manner
  • Antonym: warm - showing enthusiasm, affection, or kindness

Etymology

From ice + -y, originally referring to the physical properties of ice, later applied figuratively.

2. ADJ

/ ˈaɪ.si / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.si / (BrE)
covered with or consisting of ice; having a surface that contains or is coated by ice.

Examples

I learned, for example, that he made a practice of yapping at dogs he encountered and, in winter, of sprinkling salt on the icy pavement to scarify their feet.

Synset Examples

icy northern waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosty - covered with frost or very cold; similar to icy
  • Degree Lower: slippery - difficult to walk on due to smoothness, often from ice
  • Degree Higher: glacial - extremely cold or icy; resembling ice in characteristics

Etymology

From 'ice' + '-y', forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈaɪsi / (AmE), / ˈaɪsi / (BrE)
Extremely cold; resembling or suggestive of ice in temperature.

Examples

And that's how it came to pass... that on the second-to-last day of the job, the convict crew that tarred the plate-factory roof in the spring of '49... wound up sitting in a row at ten o'clock in the morning, drinking icy-cold, Bohemia-style beer... courtesy of the hardest screw that ever walked a turn at Shawshank State Prison.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body
  • Degree Higher: freezing - at or below 0°C; extremely cold, at the freezing point

Etymology

From ice + -y, forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of ice'.
