chamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a room used by a judge for official business, often private and separate from the main courtroom

Examples

Gov. Vandiver is expected to make the traditional visit to both chambers as they work toward adjournment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courtroom - main room in a court where trials are conducted
  • Synonym: office - a room where a particular type of work is done

Etymology

From Old French *chambre*, from Latin *camera* (vaulted room).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)

Examples
