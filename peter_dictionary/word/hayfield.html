hayfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌfild / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.fiːld / (BrE)
A field where grass or alfalfa is grown to be cut and dried for use as hay, typically for feeding livestock.

Examples

A fringe of housing and gardens bearded the top of the heights, and behind it were sandy roads leading past farms and hayfields.
At the base of that wall, you'll find a rock that has no earthly business in a Maine hayfield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow - area of grassland, often natural and not specifically managed for hay

Etymology

From 'hay' (Old English hēg) + 'field' (Old English feld).
