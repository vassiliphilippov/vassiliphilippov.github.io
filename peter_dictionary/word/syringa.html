syringa

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A hardy shrub with showy, strongly fragrant creamy-white flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.

Examples

In the center of any open space remaining our grandfathers had planted syringa and sweet shrub, snowball, rose-of-Sharon and balm-of-Gilead.

Etymology

From New Latin *syringa*, from Greek *syrinx* (pipe, tube), referring to the plant's hollow stems.
