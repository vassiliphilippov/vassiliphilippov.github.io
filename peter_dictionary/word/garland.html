garland

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɑr.lənd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.lənd / (BrE)
to decorate or adorn someone or something with a wreath or bands of flowers or leaves

Examples

In it was a stone Tibetan Buddha I had picked up in Bombay, and occasionally, to make merit, my wife and I garlanded it with flowers or laid a few pennies in its lap.

Synset Examples

They garlanded the statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreathe - to cover or surround with a wreath or something resembling a wreath

Etymology

From Old French 'guirlande', probably from Italian 'ghirlanda' (wreath, braid).
