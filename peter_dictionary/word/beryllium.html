beryllium

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɪliəm / (AmE), / bəˈrɪlɪəm / (BrE)
a light, strong, brittle, grey, toxic metallic element (chemical symbol Be) with atomic number 4, used in aerospace and other high-technology applications

Examples

Similar measurements are being initiated to resolve a large discrepancy in the heat of formation of another important combustion product, beryllium fluoride.

Etymology

From New Latin *beryllia*, from Greek *bēryllos* (beryl), because it was first detected as an oxide in beryl.
