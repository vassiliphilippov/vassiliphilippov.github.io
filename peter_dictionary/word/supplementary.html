supplementary

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌpləˈmɛnˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmɛntri / (BrE)
serving as an addition or support to something; providing extra help or reinforcement

Examples

Thus, besides the training provided to youth in school, the existence of the school program can have supplementary benefits to industry which make it an asset to industrial development efforts.

Synset Examples

the main library and its auxiliary branches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary - giving assistance or support; additional
  • Antonym: main - chief or principal; most important

Etymology

From Latin 'supplementarius', from 'supplementum' meaning 'that which fills up or completes'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsʌpləˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmɛn.təri / (BrE)
Added to complete something or to make up for a deficiency; additional.

Examples

We also have, as a special added attraction, the Sierra Boys Choir who have chosen a certain special song and a special arrangement to honor their host, Mr. Michael Corleone.

Synset Examples

produced supplementary volumes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additional - added, extra, or supplementary
  • Synonym: complementary - serving to complete or enhance

Etymology

From Latin supplementarius, from supplementum (that which fills up or completes).
