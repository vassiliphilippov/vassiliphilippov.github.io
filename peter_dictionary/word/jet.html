jet

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛt / (AmE), / dʒɛt / (BrE)
A sudden or forceful stream of liquid or gas released into the air or another medium.

Examples

The transpiring gas ejected from the anode formed a jet directed axially towards the cathode below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - a sudden, brief stream or burst (as of liquid)

Etymology

From Old French 'jet', from Latin 'jactus', past participle of 'jacere' (to throw).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɛt / (AmE), / dʒɛt / (BrE)
An aircraft powered by one or more jet engines.

Examples

Somehow, the pictures and stories of Soviet T-34 tanks on Cuban beaches and Russian Mig jet fighters strafing rebel troops has brought home to all of us the stark, blunt truth of what it means to have a Russian military base 90 miles away from home.
A squadron of Navy jets and another of long-range patrol planes would add support to the carrier task forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet plane - an airplane powered by jet engines
  • Antonym: propeller plane - an airplane powered by propeller engines

Etymology

From jet propulsion, named after the propulsion method (jet of gas).
