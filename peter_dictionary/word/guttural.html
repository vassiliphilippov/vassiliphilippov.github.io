guttural

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌt̬ɚəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtərəl / (BrE)
(of a sound) having a harsh, throaty quality like that made by frogs or crows; produced in the throat.

Examples

"Slice o' mutton, bhoy "? she had queried in her soft guttural.

Synset Examples

a guttural voice
acres of guttural frogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croaky - having a rough, harsh-sounding voice or sound, like a frog or crow
  • Synonym: raspy - having a harsh, grating sound

Etymology

From Latin 'guttur' meaning 'throat'.
