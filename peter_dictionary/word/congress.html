congress

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
a formal meeting of elected or appointed representatives, often to discuss or decide on important issues

Examples

He had sat beside Alexander Brandel at the rostrum of a congress of Zionists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conference - a large formal meeting for discussion, often academic or professional
  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'congressus' (a meeting, encounter), from 'congrēdī' (to come together).
