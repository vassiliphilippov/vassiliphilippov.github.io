unrestricted

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.rɪˈstrɪk.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌn.rɪˈstrɪk.tɪd / (BrE)
Not limited or controlled by rules or restrictions; free or open.

Examples

A stream which has all of its watershed within a national forest or other lands under good conservation practices is less likely to be affected by pollution than one passing through unrestricted logging or past an industrial area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restricted - limited or controlled by rules or boundaries

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'restricted' (limited).

2. ADJ

/ ʌn.rɪˈstrɪk.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌn.rɪˈstrɪk.tɪd / (BrE)
Not limited or controlled; free of any restrictions.

Examples

Seemingly, with an unrestricted flow of ideas, all will be well, and we are even assured that "an idea a day will keep the sheriff away ".

Synset Examples

I had unrestricted access

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restricted - limited or controlled by rules or conditions
  • Antonym: limited - not allowed to go beyond a certain amount or scope

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'restricted' (limited, controlled).
