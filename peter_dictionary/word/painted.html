painted

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪd / (BrE)
Covered with a layer of paint.

Examples

Dives, carefully repaired huts, and nicely painted and ornamented cottages were jumbled together cheek by jowl with little distinction.
The gilt sterns of the men-of-war becoming clearer to him, the sides of the wooden sea walls alternately painted yellow and black, the bronze cannon at the ports.

Synset Examples

freshly painted lawn furniture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpainted - not covered with paint

Etymology

From the past participle of 'paint'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of an emotion, expression, or feature) appearing artificial, superficial, or lacking genuine feeling, as if applied like paint.

Examples

He straightened his tie at the mirror with a shaking hand; the genial smile seemed painted on his face.

Synset Examples

in public he wore a painted smile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be, authentic; not artificial or fake
  • Synonym: fake - not real or genuine; artificial; made to look like something else

Etymology

From the verb 'paint' + -ed, originally describing something covered with paint; figurative use dates from the 18th century.
