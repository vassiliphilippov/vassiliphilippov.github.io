stairway

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛrˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈsteəˌweɪ / (BrE)
A set of steps that allow people to go up or down between different levels of a building or structure.

Examples

They advanced in a line across the entrance hall to the stairway and up, with gingerly steps, towards the first landing.
Plans for the shelter, with either a stairway or hatchway entrance, are shown on pages 26 and 27.
He started toward the stairway, then turned to add, "Tell her to come to Adams's room, that Adams is in trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staircase - a set of stairs inside a building, usually with a banister
  • Antonym: ladder - a structure for climbing up or down, with rungs between two side pieces

Etymology

Formed from 'stair' + 'way', meaning a path or way made of stairs.
