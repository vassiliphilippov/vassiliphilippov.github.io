prechlorination

1. NOUN

/ priˌklɔrəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / priˌklɔːrɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process of adding chlorine to a substance before a subsequent chemical treatment or process, especially in water treatment or purification.

Examples

It should be possible to free carbon tetrachloride of any interfering substances by the usual purification methods followed by prechlorination prior to addition of radioactive chlorine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorination - the process of treating something with chlorine

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'chlorination' (treatment with chlorine).
