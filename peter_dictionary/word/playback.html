playback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌbak / (BrE)
the act or process of reproducing recorded sound or images, especially for review or enjoyment

Examples

Now, as he passed the open counter that divided the assembly room from the business office, he nodded and said good night to the station keeper and his clerks, not stopping to hear the day-watch playback of his chewing out.

Synset Examples

he was allowed to hear the playback of his testimony

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recording - the process of capturing sound or images for later playback

Etymology

From 'play' + 'back,' first used in 20th-century audio technology.
