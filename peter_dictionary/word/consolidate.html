consolidate

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɑː.lɪ.deɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to combine or unite things into a single, more effective or coherent whole

Examples

The general intellectual outlook which had appeared in the eleventh century was now consolidated to a significant degree.
This Congress will see Premier Khrushchev consolidating his power and laying the groundwork for an orderly succession should death or illness remove him from the scene in the next few years.
The Institute of Public Administration, in its report to the State Fiscal Study Commission in 1959, recommended "consolidating and centralizing all aspects of property tax administration in a single state agency professionally organized and equipped for the job ".

Synset Examples

The companies consolidated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unify - to make or become united or whole

Etymology

From Latin consolidare, from com- (together) + solidare (to make firm).

2. VERB

/ kənˈsɑː.lɪ.deɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to make something stronger or more secure, especially to strengthen a position of power or success

Examples

He has traversed provinces and kingdoms, but he has not consolidated them behind him, nor does he control them.
Is it true that in the year 1950, you devised the murder of the heads of the so-called "Five Families" in New York to assume and consolidate your nefarious power?

Synset Examples

consolidate one's gains
consolidate one's hold on first place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strengthen - to make stronger or more powerful
  • Antonym: weaken - to make less strong or secure

Etymology

From Latin consolidare, from com- (together) + solidare (to make solid).
