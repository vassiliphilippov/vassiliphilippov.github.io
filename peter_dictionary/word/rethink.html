rethink

1. VERB

/ riˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / riˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
To consider something again, especially in order to change a previous decision or opinion.

Examples

The historian's business is to penetrate to the inside of the actions with which he is dealing and reconstruct or rather rethink the thoughts which constituted them.
It is a characteristic of thoughts that, in re-thinking them we come, ipso facto, to understand why they were thought ".

Synset Examples

He rethought his decision to take a vacation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconsider - to think again about a decision or opinion

Etymology

Prefix re- (again) + think.
