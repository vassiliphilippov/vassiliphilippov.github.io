presage

1. VERB

/ ˈprɛsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdʒ / (BrE)
to be a sign or warning of a future event; to foreshadow or predict

Examples

Figurines and simple chapels presage the emergence of sculpture and architecture in Greece; objects in gold, ivory, and bronze grow more numerous.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreshadow - to indicate or suggest a future event before it happens
  • Synonym: predict - to say what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin 'praesagium' (omen), from 'praesagire' (to foreknow).
