unworthy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɝði / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːði / (BrE)
Not deserving respect, attention, or a particular treatment; lacking value or merit.

Examples

And she begins to regard him as savage, bestial and unworthy.

Synset Examples

dispel a student whose conduct is deemed unworthy
unworthy of forgiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worthy - having value or merit; deserving

Etymology

From Old English unweorþ (not worthy); 'un-' (not) + 'worthy'.
