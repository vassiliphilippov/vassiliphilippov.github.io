scratchiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrætʃi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being rough or coarse, causing a sensation of abrasion or irritation when touched or experienced.

Examples

his quill drawings had a scratchiness, suggesting skin texture; the clay he used plastically to suggest soft moving flesh, as in an abdomen, in a reclining torso; the wax he smoothed over to give the body surface an elastic pull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughness - the quality of being uneven or coarse to the touch

Etymology

From "scratchy" + suffix "-ness".
