psychopath

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkəˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəˌpæθ / (BrE)
A person with a personality disorder characterized by persistent antisocial behavior, lack of empathy, remorse, and often criminality; now more often referred to as a sociopath.

Examples

Landesco thought him "just a superior sort of plugugly "but he was, in fact, with his aggression and hostility, and nerveless indifference to risking or administering pain, a casebook psychopath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociopath - a person with a sociopathic personality disorder

Etymology

From Greek psykho- (mind, soul) + -pathēs (suffering, feeling).
