incidental

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
Happening as a minor or less important part of something else; occurring as a chance result or consequence; subordinate or secondary in significance.

Examples

Out of long experience I have found that incidental figures and other objects like trees, logs, and bushes can be traced from the original sketch and moved about in the major areas on the final sheet until they occupy the right position, which I call' clicking'.

Synset Examples

incidental expenses
the road will bring other incidental advantages
extra duties incidental to the job
labor problems incidental to a rapid expansion
confusion incidental to a quick change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accidental - happening by chance; not planned
  • Antonym: major - more important; primary in significance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'incidentalis', from Latin 'incidentem' (happening).
