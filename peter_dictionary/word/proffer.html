proffer

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈprɒfə / (BrE)
to offer or present something to someone for acceptance or rejection, often in a formal or polite manner

Examples

He slid in at her side, tucked a cigar into his mouth, and politely proffered one to her.
Although they are forbidden to sit with the customers, the dancers are sometimes proffered drinks, and most of them can bolt one down in mid-shimmy.

Synset Examples

She offered us all a cold drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - to present or propose something for acceptance or rejection

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'profrer', altered from Old French 'porfreir', from Latin 'proferre' (to bring forward).
