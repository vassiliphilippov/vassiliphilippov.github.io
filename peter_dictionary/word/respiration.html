respiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in living organisms of exchanging gases with the environment, especially the intake of oxygen and release of carbon dioxide; or, at a cellular level, the metabolic process by which energy is produced and carbon dioxide released

Examples

The intra nasal instillation of a fluid suspension of infectious agent in an anesthetized animal is far different from exposure, through natural respiration, to aerosolized organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breathing - the act of inhaling and exhaling air

Etymology

From Latin 'respiratio' (breathing), from 'respirare' (to breathe again).
