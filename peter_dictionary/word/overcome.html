overcome

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkʌm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkʌm / (BrE)
to succeed in dealing with or gain victory over a difficulty, problem, opponent, or emotion

Examples

The club that overcame the worst start in a comparable period to win the pennant was New York's '51 Giants, who dropped 11 of their first 13.

It was at that party that, finally overcoming my timidity, inspired by tales only half understood and overheard among older boys, I asked Jessie to spend New Year's Eve with me.

Synset Examples

You must overcome all difficulties
defeat your enemies

Her anger got the better of her and she blew up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conquer - to take control of by force; defeat completely
  • Antonym: succumb - to fail to resist; yield to a stronger force
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up to an opponent or authority

Etymology

From Old English ofercuman (to get the better of), from ofer- (over) + cuman (to come).
