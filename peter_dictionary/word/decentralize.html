decentralize

1. VERB

/ diˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛntrəlaɪz / (BrE)
To distribute administrative powers or functions away from a central authority.

Examples

They must continue to satisfy basic capital needs; and there persists the dual problem of maintaining operational unity around a national program of modernization while simultaneously decentralizing participation in the program to wider and wider groups.

Synset Examples

After the revolution, food distribution was decentralized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centralize - to bring under a single, central authority or control

Etymology

From de- (removal) + centralize, modeled on French décentraliser.
