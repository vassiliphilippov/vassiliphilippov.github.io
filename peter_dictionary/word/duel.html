duel

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːəl / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəl / (BrE)
a formal and prearranged fight with deadly weapons between two people, usually to settle a matter of honor

Examples

George Kerr, the swift striding Jamaican, set a meet record in the 600 - yard run in the Knights of Columbus track meet tonight, beating Purdue's Dave Mills in a hot duel in 1.10.1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - a violent confrontation or struggle
  • Synonym: duelist - a person who participates in a duel
  • Antonym: peace - absence of conflict or fighting

Etymology

From Latin 'duellum' (war, combat between two) via Old French 'duel'.
