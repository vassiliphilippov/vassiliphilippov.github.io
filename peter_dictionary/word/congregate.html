congregate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːŋɡrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrɪˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to come together in a group, often for a particular purpose

Examples

Related to micelle formation is the technologically important ability of detergent actives to congregate at oil water interfaces in such a manner that the polar (or ionized) end of the molecule is directed towards the aqueous phase and the hydrocarbon chain towards the oily phase.
Department stores congregated in the "one hundred per cent location ", where all the transit lines converged.

Synset Examples

The crowds congregated in front of the Vatican on Christmas Eve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemble - to gather together in one place for a common purpose
  • Antonym: disperse - to go or spread in different directions

Etymology

From Latin congregare ‘to collect into a flock’, from com- ‘together’ + gregare ‘to collect’, from grex, greg- ‘flock’.
