extricate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛk.strɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a difficult or complicated situation; to release from entanglement or constraint

Examples

Balanced properly between optimism and practicality, the nondefeatist attitude of the shell people led them to extricate themselves, their ships and personnel, from bizarre situations.

Synset Examples

I cannot extricate myself from this task

Related Terms

  • Antonym: entangle - to cause to become twisted together or caught in difficulties

Etymology

From Latin extricatus, past participle of extricare (to disentangle): ex- (out of) + tricae (tricks, perplexities).
