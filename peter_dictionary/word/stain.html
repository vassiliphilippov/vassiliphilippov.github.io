stain

1. NOUN

/ steɪn / (AmE), / steɪn / (BrE)
a mark or discoloration on a surface, especially one that is difficult to remove

Examples

Stains, which include the wide variety of nonparticulate materials which give color even when present in very low concentration on the soiled object.
Greases, stains, and miscellaneous soils are usually sorbed onto the soiled surface.
The other constituents in a built detergent assist in this and in the removal of dirty stains and the hydrophilic sticky or dried soils.

Synset Examples

the wine left a dark stain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round or irregular mark, typically one that is composed of dirt
  • Synonym: smudge - a smear or dirty mark
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean; absence of stains or dirt

Etymology

From Old French 'teindre' (to dye), from Latin 'tingere' (to dye, color).
