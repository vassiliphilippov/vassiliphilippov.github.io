unreconstructed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌriːkənˈstrʌktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌriːkənˈstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
Still holding views or attitudes that are considered outdated or outmoded, especially after major change or reform.

Examples

I suspect that there are far more unreconstructed ones than the North likes to believe.
Probably a larger percentage of Virginians and South Carolinians remain unreconstructed than elsewhere, with Georgia, North Carolina, and Alabama following along after them.
Among Bourbons the racial issue may have less to do with their remaining unreconstructed than other factors.
But apart from racial problems, the old unreconstructed South - to use the moderate words favored by Mr. Thomas Griffith - finds itself unsympathetic to most of what is different about the civilization of the North.
But those among the Bourbons who remain unreconstructed go much further than this.

Synset Examples

peasants are still unreconstructed small capitalists at heart
there are probably more unreconstructed Southerners than one would like to admit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - strongly opposed to change; ultra-conservative
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or promoting social reform or new ideas

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'reconstructed' (reformed, changed), referring originally to the post-Civil War South.
