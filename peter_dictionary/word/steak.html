steak

1. NOUN

/ steɪk / (AmE), / steɪk / (BrE)
A thick, flat slice of meat or fish, typically cut across the muscle, often cooked by grilling, frying, or broiling.

Examples

Still existing on a "Northern Union "telegraph form is a typical peremptory message from Peru grocer J. J. Hapgood to Burton and Graves' store in Manchester - "Get and send by stage sure four pounds best Porterhouse or sirloin steak, for Mrs. Hapgood send six sweet oranges ".
"Why, I could n't even cook a piece of antelope steak; I never even saw any ".
Do you speak English, butt steak?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fillet - a boneless piece of meat or fish
  • Degree Higher: roast - large piece of meat cooked by roasting

Etymology

From Middle English steike, from Old Norse steik (roast, steak), related to the verb 'to stick' (as in to pierce for roasting).
