blandly

1. ADV

/ ˈblændli / (AmE), / ˈblændli / (BrE)
In a bland, unemotional, or indifferent manner; without excitement or strong feeling.

Examples

Another is a wily countryman called Larkin, whose blandly boisterous progress has been chronicled, I believe, in earlier volumes of Mr. Bates' comedie humaine.
"Exterminatin' cow thieves is just a business proposition with me ", he 'd blandly announce.

Synset Examples

his blandly incompetent attempts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferently - without emotion or interest; synonym of 'blandly' in this sense
  • Antonym: emotionally - with strong emotion; antonym of 'blandly'

Etymology

Formed from 'bland' + '-ly'. 'Bland' from Latin 'blandus' (smooth, mild).
