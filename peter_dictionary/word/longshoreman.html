longshoreman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌʃɔrmən / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌʃɔːmən / (BrE)
a person employed to load and unload ships at a dock or port

Examples

The portwatchers were retired longshoremen and small time seafarers off towboats and barges who acted as watchmen on the wharves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevedore - a person employed at a dock to load and unload cargo from ships

Etymology

From 'longshore' (by or on the shore) + 'man', refers to work done along the shore.
