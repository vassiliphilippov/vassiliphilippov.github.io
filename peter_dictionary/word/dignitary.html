dignitary

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪtəri / (BrE)
a person who holds a high rank or office, especially in government or the church

Examples

The oyabun was entertaining a group of dignitaries, the secretary said, businessmen from Tokyo for the most part, and Kayabashi wished to show them the mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: VIP - very important person; influential or prominent figure

Etymology

From Latin 'dignitas' (worthiness, rank, dignity).
