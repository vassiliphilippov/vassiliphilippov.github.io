scolding

1. NOUN

/ ˈskoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
An angry or harsh reprimand or rebuke.

Examples

He gives me shit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From the verb 'scold', which comes from Middle English 'scolden', from Old Norse 'skald', meaning 'to scold, insult'.
