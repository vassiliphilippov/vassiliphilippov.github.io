haiku

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪku / (AmE), / ˈhaɪkuː / (BrE)
A traditional Japanese poem consisting of three short lines, typically with a syllable pattern of 5-7-5, often evoking nature or seasons.

Examples

The latest and, significantly, greatest fruit of this theatrical vine is The, an adaptation of Basho's classic frog haiku by Roger Entwhistle, a former University of Maryland chemistry instructor.

Etymology

From Japanese 俳句 (haiku), coined in the late 19th century from 'hai' (playful) and 'ku' (verse).
