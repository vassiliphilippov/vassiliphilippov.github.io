brucellosis

1. NOUN

/ bruːsɛˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / bruːsɛˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious bacterial disease transmitted to humans from infected animals or animal products, characterized by fever, headache, and malaise

Examples

Agents that are known to cause frequent infections among laboratory workers such as those causing Q fever, tularemia, brucellosis, glanders, coccidioidomycosis, etc., belong in this category.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undulant fever - another name for brucellosis, referring to its recurring fevers

Etymology

From New Latin Brucella (the bacteria genus, named after microbiologist David Bruce) + -osis (disease suffix).
