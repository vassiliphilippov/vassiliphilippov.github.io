scratch

1. VERB

/ skrætʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ / (BrE)
to cut or wear away the surface of something, often by rubbing or scraping

Examples

The bristles are soft enough to massage the gums and not scratch the enamel.
Following reaction the cells were scratched with a file and opened under a 20 % aqueous sodium iodide solution.
He would lay in wait in the garage, and when the terrier came scratching around, he 'd let fly with a cherry bomb.
He scratched his mosquito plagued neck.
"I could scratch her eyes out ", Eileen cried and stamped her foot when I came back from the phone booth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - to remove material from a surface by rubbing
  • Synonym: abrade - to wear down or rub away by friction
  • Antonym: polish - to make the surface smooth and shiny, often by rubbing (opposite effect)

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound made by scratching; Middle English 'scracchen', related to Middle Low German 'kratzen' (to scratch).

to rub your skin with your nails or with something sharp, usually in order to stop it itching

The words ran crazily in his head: Mollie the Mutton is scratching her nose in the rain.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rub - to move against the surface with pressure and movement

Etymology

Originated from Middle English 'scratchein', related to Old High German 'krazen' (to scratch).

4. VERB

/ skrætʃ / (AmE), / skrætʃ / (BrE)
To rub or scrape a part of the body, typically with fingernails or an object, especially to relieve itching.

Examples

"That is undoubtedly a hell of a quote ", said the writer, scratching his head.

Related Terms
