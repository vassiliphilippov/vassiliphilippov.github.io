vet

1. NOUN

/ vɛt / (AmE), / vɛt / (BrE)
A person qualified and licensed to treat sick or injured animals; a veterinarian.

Examples

Yes, well, it's a pity to spoil your girlish figure - which all those kittens would do anyway - but I think when you 've raised these we 'll just have the vet fix it so there won n't be any more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veterinarian - animal doctor; specialist in veterinary medicine

Etymology

Shortened from 'veterinarian,' which comes from Latin 'veterinarius' (of cattle), from 'veterinae' (beasts of burden).
