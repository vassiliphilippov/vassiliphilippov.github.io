hopeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpləs / (BrE)
Having no hope; feeling or showing a lack of possibility for comfort, improvement, or success.

Examples

In this manner they side-step the seemingly hopeless investigation of the greater depths of mystery in which all of us grope continually.
They caused my love for Jessica to become warmer and at the same time more hopeless, as if my adolescent self knew that only torment would ever bring me the courage to ask to see her again.
It all seemed rather hopeless.

Synset Examples

in an agony of hopeless grief
with a hopeless sigh he sat down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desperate - showing a loss of hope and willingness to take any risk
  • Antonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring optimism about a future event
  • Synonym: discouraged - having lost confidence or enthusiasm; disheartened

Etymology

Formed from 'hope' + 'less', after Middle English 'hopeles'.
