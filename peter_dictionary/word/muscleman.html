muscleman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmʌslmæn / (BrE)
a man employed for his strength, typically as a bodyguard or enforcer; a strongman or thug

Examples

One reason the portwatchers let Sposato take them over was to get the protection of his musclemen.

Synset Examples

the drug lord had his muscleman to protect him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strongman - a man of great physical strength, often employed for security or intimidation
  • Synonym: thug - a violent criminal or ruffian employed to enforce or intimidate

Etymology

From 'muscle' (referring to physical strength) + 'man'; modeled after similar compounds like 'businessman'.
