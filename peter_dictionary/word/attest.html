attest

1. VERB

/ əˈtɛst / (AmE), / əˈtɛst / (BrE)
to affirm or declare that something is true, genuine, or correct, especially in an official or legal capacity

Examples

On the contrary, even in the heart of "the Bible belt "itself, as can be attested by any one who is called to work there, the industrial and technological revolutions have long been under way, together with the corresponding changes in man's picture of himself and his world.

Synset Examples

I attest this signature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certify - to confirm formally as true or accurate
  • Synonym: verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true
  • Antonym: deny - to declare something is not true

Etymology

From Latin attestari, from ad- (to) + testari (to bear witness).

2. VERB

/ əˈtɛst / (AmE), / əˈtɛst / (BrE)
to provide evidence for or bear witness to something; to show or prove to be true, often by personal experience or external evidence

Examples

However, Biblical scholars frequently attested to its numerous inaccuracies, as old manuscripts were uncovered and scholarship advanced.
Fortunately, the honor of the denomination can attest that Cyrus Bartol defended Parker's sincerity, as did also Gannett and Chandler Robbins; whereupon Parker broke down into convulsions of weeping and rushed out of the room, though not out of the Fellowship.
We have here, finally, a witness that will further testify to Michael Corleone'’s rule over a criminal empire that controls all gambling in this country and perhaps in other countries.

Synset Examples

His high fever attested to his illness
The buildings in Rome manifest a high level of architectural sophistication
This decision demonstrates his sense of fairness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testify - give evidence as a witness in a court or other setting; serve as evidence for

Etymology

From Latin 'attestari', from 'ad-' (to) + 'testari' (to bear witness), from 'testis' (witness).
