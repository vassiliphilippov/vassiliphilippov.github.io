divertimento

1. NOUN

/ dəˌvɝtəˈmɛntoʊ / (AmE), / dɪˌvɜːtɪˈmɛntəʊ / (BrE)
A light and entertaining musical composition consisting of several movements, typically for a small ensemble and without a fixed form.

Examples

Moreover, the piece is written in five movements, rather than the conventional four of most quintets, and this gives the opus a serenade or divertimento flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenade - a musical composition, typically calm and light, performed in the evening or outdoors

Etymology

From Italian 'divertimento' (amusement, diversion), from 'divertire' (to amuse).
