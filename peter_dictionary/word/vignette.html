vignette

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈnjɛt / (AmE), / vɪˈnjɛt / (BrE)
A brief and evocative description, account, or episode, especially in a literary work.

Examples

Pueri Aquam De Silvas Ad Agricolas Portant, a delightful vignette set in the unforgettable epoch of pre Punic War Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a short descriptive literary piece
  • Degree Higher: portrait - a detailed description or representation, often of a person

Etymology

From French 'vignette', diminutive of 'vigne' meaning 'vine', referring originally to decorative vine patterns in books.
