gore

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔr / (AmE), / ɡɔː / (BrE)
coagulated blood, especially that from an injury or wound, often clotted and dark red in color

Examples

You should have put more gore under the fangs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blood - the red liquid that circulates in the arteries and veins of humans and other vertebrate animals
  • Synonym: clot - a thick mass of coagulated liquid, especially blood

Etymology

From Old English 'gor' (dirt, dung, blood), related to Old Norse 'gor' (dirt, dung) and Middle Dutch 'goor' (dirt, mud).
