guzzle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌzəl / (BrE)
To drink (something) greedily or in large quantities, as if very thirsty.

Examples

The plane should have started at the scheduled time and left Sinatra and Martin to guzzle.

Synset Examples

The boys guzzled the cheap vodka

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - to swallow quickly or greedily
  • Antonym: sip - to drink (something) in small quantities

Etymology

Probably from Old French *gosillier* (to drink greedily), related to *gosier* (throat).
