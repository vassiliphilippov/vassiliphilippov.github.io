pearly

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝːli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːli / (BrE)
Having a lustrous, white or iridescent quality like that of a pearl.

Examples

The way his red rubber lips were stretched across his pearly little teeth I though he was only having a little joke, but, no, he wanted me to bend down from the roar of wind so he could roar something into my ear.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: milky - resembling milk in color or consistency
  • Degree Higher: iridescent - showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles

Etymology

From 'pearl' + '-y', suffix forming adjectives.
