willful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfʊl / (BrE)
(of a bad action) done deliberately, especially with the intention of causing harm; intentional or deliberate.

Examples

I 've given willful hurt to no man.

Synset Examples

the insult was intentional
willful disobedience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • Antonym: accidental - happening by chance; not intended

Etymology

From Middle English wilful, from will (desire) + -ful (full of).
