clarinet

1. NOUN

/ ˌklærəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌklærɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
A woodwind musical instrument with a single reed and a straight cylindrical tube, producing sound when air is blown across the reed.

Examples

Clarinet up, up, up!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxophone - a woodwind instrument with a single reed, usually made of brass
  • Synonym: oboe - a woodwind instrument with a double reed
  • Synonym: flute - a woodwind instrument without a reed, producing sound by the flow of air across an opening

Etymology

From French clarinette, diminutive of clairon (a type of trumpet), from Latin clarus (clear, bright).
