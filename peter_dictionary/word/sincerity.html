sincerity

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛrɪti / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɛrəti / (BrE)
The quality of being genuine, honest, and without pretense or deceit.

Examples

As for the ladies, they were full of charm, and sincerity, and deep and abiding affection for this hurrying driving, honest, little man.
His claim was therefore "so highly exaggerated ", the Department concluded, that it "cast doubt upon his veracity and, consequently, upon his sincerity and good faith ".
Sir, my client has answered every question asked by this committee with the utmost sincerity.

Synset Examples

his sincerity inspired belief
they demanded some proof of my sincerity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
  • Antonym: deceit - the act or practice of deceiving; concealment or distortion of the truth
  • Antonym: hypocrisy - the practice of claiming to have moral standards to which one's own behavior does not conform

Etymology

From Latin sinceritas (genuineness, purity), from sincerus (clean, pure, genuine).

2. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɛr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being natural and simple, without artificiality or affectation.

Examples

Thoroughly modern in treatment, they are at the same time, full of simple sincerity which invariably characterizes genuine Negro folk-music and are by no means to be confused with the average' Broadway Spirituals' which depend for their racial flavor upon sundry allusions to the' Amen Corner',' judgement day',' Gabriel's horn', and a frustrated devil - with a few random' Hallelujahs' thrown in for good measure.

Synset Examples

the simple sincerity of folk songs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality of being plain, natural, or easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin sinceritas, from sincerus (pure, genuine).

3. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɛrəti / (BrE)
the quality of being free from pretense or deceit; genuine feeling or honesty

Examples

Where this approach becomes critical, the industry can be expected to put much emphasis on this as evidence of its sincerity in "resisting "the wage pressures of a powerful union, requesting tariff relief after it has "reluctantly "acceded to the union pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit
  • Antonym: insincerity - the quality of being not sincere; hypocrisy or deceit

Etymology

From Latin sinceritas (genuineness, purity).
