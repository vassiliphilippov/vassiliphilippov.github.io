discourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪs.kɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔːs / (BrE)
Written or spoken communication or debate, especially one that is extended or formal.

Examples

How, for example, could a Voltaire understand the strange predicament in which a Rousseau would find himself when, soon after the furor of his first Discourse, he acquired still another title to fame?
He opens his discourse, however, with a review of the Eisenhower inaugural festivities at which a sympathetic press had assembled its massive talents, all primed to catch some revelation of the emerging new age.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: conversation - an informal spoken exchange between people
  • Degree Higher: lecture - a formal talk on a subject, especially at a university
  • Degree Lower: dialogue - a conversation between two or more people

Etymology

From Latin 'discursus' (a running to and fro, conversation), from 'discurrere' (to run about).
