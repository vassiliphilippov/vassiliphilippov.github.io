analyticity

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˌlɪˈsɪɾəti / (AmE), / ˌænəˌlɪˈsɪtɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being analytic, especially in mathematics, referring to a function or expression that can be represented by a convergent power series.

Examples

Again, the analyticity of the two curves guarantee that such intervals exist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analytic - relating to or using analysis or logical reasoning

Etymology

Formed from analytic + -ity, after analogous formations in Latin and French.
