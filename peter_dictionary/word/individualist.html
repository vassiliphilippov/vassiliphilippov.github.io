individualist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or practices independent thought or action, valuing self-reliance and personal freedom.

Examples

He is an individualist but not an anarchist; he is a public servant but not a cop.
Sherlock Holmes is not merely an individualist; he is very close to being a mental case.
At first glance, this hero seems to be more rather than less of an individualist than any of his predecessors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformist - person who does not conform to established customs or attitudes
  • Antonym: conformist - person who conforms to accepted behavior or established practices

Etymology

From 'individual' + '-ist', based on Latin 'individuus' (indivisible).
