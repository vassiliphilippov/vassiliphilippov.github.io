demonstrative

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑnstrətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv / (BrE)
a pronoun that is used to indicate or point to a specific person, thing, or idea, such as 'this', 'that', 'these', or 'those'

Examples

Other classes are included only by myself (interrogatives, adverbs) or only by Swadesh and Hoijer (pronouns, demonstratives).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that replaces a noun

Etymology

From Latin demonstrativus, from demonstrare (to point out).
