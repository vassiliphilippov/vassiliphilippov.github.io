fraternity

1. NOUN

/ frəˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
A social organization for male students at a college or university, often with its own house, traditions, and membership requirements.

Examples

And the school newspaper sells space to jubilant fraternities, sororities, and houses (in the House Plan Association) that have good news to impart.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sorority - a social club for female undergraduates

Etymology

From Latin fraternitas, from frater (brother), emphasizing brotherhood.
