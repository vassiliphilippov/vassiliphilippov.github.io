bleakly

1. ADV

/ ˈblikli / (AmE), / ˈbliːkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of hope or encouragement; in a despondent or grim manner.

Examples

How far could it be, Watson thought bleakly, how far can a blind man crawl?
He had a round, frank Irish face, creased in a jovial grin that stayed bleakly in place even when he was pumping bullets into someone's body.
"Penny's always glad to see me over there ", he mused bleakly.

Synset Examples

he wondered bleakly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a way that shows happiness or optimism

Etymology

From 'bleak' + '-ly', where 'bleak' is from Old Norse 'bleikr' (pale, shining, bleak).
