migration

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the movement of people from one country, region, or locality to another, especially for the purpose of settling temporarily or permanently in a new location

Examples

There are a half dozen reasons helping to account for the migration to the campgrounds.
Was it supposed, perchance, that A + M (vocational training, that is) was quite sufficient for the immigrant class which flooded that part of the New England world in the post Civil War period, the immigrants having been brought in from Southern Europe, to work in the mills, to make up for the labor shortage caused by migration to the West?

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country
  • Degree Lower: emigration - the act of leaving one's own country to settle permanently in another

Etymology

From Latin 'migratio' (a removal, change of abode), from 'migrare' (to move, migrate).
