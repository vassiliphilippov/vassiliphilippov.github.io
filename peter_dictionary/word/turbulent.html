turbulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜrbjələnt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊlənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or air) moving unsteadily or violently, full of sudden or confused movement.

Examples

Turbulent tides rose as much as fifty feet.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - (of water or air) not moving or agitated, peaceful
  • Synonym: agitated - moving with a violent or irregular action, especially of a fluid

Etymology

From Latin turbulentus (full of commotion, disorder).
