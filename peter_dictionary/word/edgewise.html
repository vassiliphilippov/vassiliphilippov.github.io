edgewise

1. ADV

/ ˈɛdʒˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒ.wʌɪz / (BrE)
with the edge forward, or in such a way that only the edge is facing something or in use

Examples

The curb was a line of stone laid edgewise in the dirt and tilted this way and that by frost in the ground or the roots of trees.

Synset Examples

he sawed the board edgeways
held it edgewise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sideways - with the side facing forward or to the side

Etymology

From 'edge' + '-wise', indicating manner or direction.
