lemonade

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛməˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɛmənˌeɪd / (BrE)
A sweetened drink made from lemon juice, water, and sugar, usually served cold.

Examples

Then she took iced lemonade to Marsh's young aide where he sat in the cool of the big trees around the flower garden.

Etymology

From French *limonade*, from *limon* (lemon).
