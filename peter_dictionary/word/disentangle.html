disentangle

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsənˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a difficult situation or from being twisted together; to extricate or untangle.

Examples

The problem is to disentangle the linguistic features of pitch from the co-occurring nonlinguistic features.

Synset Examples

I cannot extricate myself from this task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extricate - to free from a constraint or difficulty
  • Antonym: entangle - to cause to become twisted together or caught up

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + entangle.

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪsənˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtæŋɡəl / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a tangled or complicated situation; to untangle

Examples

But no other two phonologic systems are as difficult to disentangle as are tone and intonation in some languages.

Synset Examples

Can you disentangle the cord?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: entangle - to twist together or complicate, so as to be hard to separate
  • Synonym: extricate - to free from a constraint or difficult situation

Etymology

From Late Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'intangulare' (to entangle).
