bracing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A structural component used to reinforce or stiffen a framework, providing stability and support.

Examples

The wooden bracing between the roof beams is placed flush with the inside of the wall.
Mortar is poured between this bracing and the 4 - inch blocks on edge to complete the wall thickness for radiation shielding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - a structural component designed to resist compression
  • Synonym: support - something that bears the weight of a structure

Etymology

From 'brace' + '-ing', where 'brace' comes from Old French 'brace' (arm), from Latin 'brachium'.
