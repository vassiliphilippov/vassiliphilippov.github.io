hubbub

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌb.əˌbʌb / (AmE), / ˈhʌb.ʌb / (BrE)
A loud, confused noise made by a crowd of people or multiple sources.

Examples

Most of the letters were written in the hubbub of camp, on stumps, pieces of bark, drum heads, or the knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting vehemently
  • Antonym: silence - complete absence of sound

Etymology

Probably from Irish "ubub," an exclamation of lament, or imitative of tumultuous noise.
