going

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of leaving a place; a departure

Examples

The meat wagon, therefore, was not out in front of the house any more, but the cluster of squad cars was still there and there was a cop on the door downstairs to screen any comings and goings.
MICHAEL: I thought you were going to tell me that you were moving your family to Vegas and that you'’d been offered the, uh, Vice Presidency of the Houstan hotels there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: departure - the act of leaving, especially to start a journey
  • Antonym: arrival - the act of coming to a place

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'go'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of something such as a business, rate, or rule) current or in operation; existing or prevailing at the present time.

Examples

The single union which faces the industry does not restrict its membership, and there is an adequate supply of labor available to the firms of the industry at the going wage rate.

Synset Examples

a going concern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: current - existing or happening now; of the present time
  • Synonym: prevailing - existing at a particular time; widespread

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of making progress, especially when referring to how easy or difficult it is to advance toward a goal.

Examples

Mikey, what the hell is going on anyway?

Synset Examples

persuading him was easy going
the proposal faces tough sledding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a destination or goal
  • Antonym: difficulty - the state of being hard to accomplish, deal with, or understand (as the opposite of 'easy going')
  • Degree Lower: easy - achieved without great effort; presenting few difficulties; often used in 'easy going'
  • Degree Higher: tough - requiring great effort to deal with or manage; often used in 'tough going'
