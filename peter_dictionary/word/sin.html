1. NOUN

/ sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
An immoral act considered to be a transgression against divine law; estrangement or separation from God, especially in religious contexts.

Related Terms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virtue - a quality considered morally good or desirable in a person (opposite of sin)

Etymology
