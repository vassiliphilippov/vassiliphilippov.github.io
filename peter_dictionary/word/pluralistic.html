pluralistic

1. ADJ

/ plʊr.əˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / plʊə.rəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or advocating a system in which multiple groups, principles, or sources of authority coexist or have influence, especially in society or philosophy.

Examples

It should attempt to communicate both an appreciation of professional standards and an understanding of the tremendous powers and potentialities of genuinely open and pluralistic societies.

Synset Examples

a pluralistic culture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monistic - relating to the doctrine that reality is one unified whole

Etymology

From 'pluralism' + '-istic', with 'pluralism' from Latin 'pluralis' (of or pertaining to more than one).
