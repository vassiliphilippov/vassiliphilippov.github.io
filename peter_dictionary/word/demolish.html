demolish

1. VERB

/ dəˈmɑːlɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
to completely destroy a building or other structure; to ruin or put an end to something

Examples

A further 260 Anglican churches have been demolished since 1948 ".
Mortars had demolished the defense ministry and set fire to the American Embassy next door.

Synset Examples

the wrecking ball demolished the building
demolish your enemies
pulverize the rebellion before it gets out of hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it

Etymology

From French démolir, from Latin demoliri (to tear down).
