alienation

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.li.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.li.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of being isolated or estranged from other people or from society.

Examples

His alienation is far more acute than Holmes'; he is not an eccentric but rather an outcast.
With Rex Stout's Nero Wolfe, alienation is represented on a purely physical plane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrangement - the state of being alienated or separated from a group or individual
  • Antonym: belonging - the feeling of being connected to or accepted by others

Etymology

From Latin alienatio(n-), from alienare 'to estrange', from alienus 'of another'.
