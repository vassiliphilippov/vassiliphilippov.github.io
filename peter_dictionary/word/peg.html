peg

1. NOUN

/ pɛɡ / (AmE), / pɛɡ / (BrE)
A short, cylindrical or tapered piece of wood (or sometimes metal or plastic), used for fastening, hanging, or marking by being pushed or driven into a surface.

Examples

They were held together by pegs and withes and in later times drawn by a single ox in thills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowel - A pin, usually made of wood, used for holding two pieces together
  • Degree Higher: nail - A thin pointed piece of metal used as a fastener

Etymology

From Middle English *pegge*, possibly of Low German origin.
