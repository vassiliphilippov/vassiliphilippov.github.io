institutionalized

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃə.nəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nəˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
Officially placed in or committed to a specialized institution, such as a hospital, prison, or care home.

Examples

Recently, by the way, I received a questionnaire in which I was asked whether or not I was non institutionalized ".

Synset Examples

had hopes of rehabilitating the institutionalized juvenile delinquents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: committed - admitted or consigned for treatment, care, or safekeeping (in an institution)

Etymology

From 'institution' + '-alize' + '-ed'; 'institution' from Latin 'institutio' (arrangement).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnstəˈtuːʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a custom, practice, or process) established as part of an official system, structure, or institution.

Examples

When the power of the latter was made both limited and explicit - when norms were clarified and made more precise and the creation of new norms was placed exclusively in parliamentary hands - two purposes were served: Government was made subservient to an institutionalized popular will, and law became a rational system for implementing that will, for serving conscious goals, for embodying the "public policy ".

Synset Examples

institutionalized graft
institutionalized suicide as practiced in Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic - done according to a fixed plan or system; methodical

Etymology

From institution + -al + -ize + -ed, meaning made into or incorporated as an institution.
