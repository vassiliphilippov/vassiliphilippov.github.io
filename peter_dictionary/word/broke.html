broke

1. ADJ

/ broʊk / (AmE), / brəʊk / (BrE)
Having no money; without funds.

Examples

Hanging over the bar was an oil painting of a nude Al had accepted from a student at the Corcoran Gallery who needed to eat and drink and was broke.
An only child, he had done all the things that young men do who have been born to money and social position until his father double-crossed him by dying broke.

Synset Examples

`skint' is a British slang term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penniless - having no money at all
  • Synonym: skint - having no money (British informal)
  • Antonym: flush - having plenty of money
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a lot of money or resources

Etymology

Past tense of 'break,' in use since the late 19th century for 'financially ruined.'
