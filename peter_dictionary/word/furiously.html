furiously

1. ADV

/ ˈfjʊəriəsli / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In an extremely wild, energetic, or violent manner.

Examples

The snow was tumbling down furiously now.
North and south, east and west, back and forth he sailed in the land-locked bay, plowing furiously forward until land appeared, then turning to repeat the process, day after day, week after week.
She yanked away from him furiously.

Synset Examples

winds were blowing furiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - in a forceful, intense, or aggressive manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful, gentle, or relaxed way

Etymology

From Latin 'furiosus' (full of rage, mad), via Old French 'furieus' + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈfjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a manner characterized by intense or violent energy; with great force, speed, or anger.

Examples

The beer foamed furiously.
I drank furiously.

Synset Examples

the boys fought furiously
she went peddling furiously up the narrow street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - in a way that involves extreme force or intensity
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or unhurried manner

Etymology

From 'furious' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'furiosus' (full of rage).
