exile

1. VERB

/ ˈɛɡˌzaɪl, ˈɛkˌsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪl / (BrE)
to expel someone from their native country, typically for political or punitive reasons

Examples

Jay had participated in the decision that exiled his old friend Van Schaack.

Synset Examples

The poet was exiled because he signed a letter protesting the government's actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banish - to send someone away from a place as punishment; to exile
  • Antonym: repatriate - to return someone to their own country

Etymology

From Latin exilium (banishment), from exul (banished person).
