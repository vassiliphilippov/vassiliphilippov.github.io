excretion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskriʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskriːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms eliminate waste products from the body

Examples

Although in both emotions sympathetic symptoms are present, different autonomic somatic patterns underlie aggression and anxiety, respectively, as indicated by the rate of the excretion of the catecholamines, the state of the muscle tone, and the Mecholyl test.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secretion - the process of producing and releasing substances (often for use in the body, not as waste)

Etymology

From Latin excretio (separation), from excerne (to sift out, separate).
