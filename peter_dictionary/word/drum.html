drum

1. NOUN

/ drʌm / (AmE), / drʌm / (BrE)
A musical percussion instrument consisting of a hollow cylinder with a membrane stretched tightly over one or both ends, which is played by striking with the hands or sticks.

Examples

Drums, xylophones, castanets, and other percussive instruments are reproduced remarkably well.
The sound of his footsteps was like a muffled drum.
The boundless blue air was stretched over the zenith like the skin of a drum.
Not one of them born... whose asshole wouldn't pucker up tighter than a snare drum when you asked them for funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tambourine - a small drum with metal disks around the edge, played by shaking or striking
  • Synonym: bongo - a pair of small connected drums played with the hands
  • Synonym: cymbal - a percussion instrument consisting of a thin, round metal plate that is hit with a stick or clashed together

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *tromme*, akin to Middle High German *trumme* (drum).

2. VERB

/ drʌm / (AmE), / drʌm / (BrE)
to make a rhythmic beating sound, especially by striking repeatedly or quickly

Examples

Standing in the shelter of the tent - a rejected hospital tent on which the rain now dripped, no longer drumming - Adam watched his own hands touch the objects on the improvised counter of boards laid across two beef barrels.

Synset Examples

Rain drummed against the windshield
The drums beat all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - strike repeatedly to make a sound

3. VERB

/ drʌm / (AmE), / drʌm / (BrE)
to play a drum or other percussion instrument, especially by striking it with sticks or the hands

Examples

One of those delightful surprise additions, which so frequently occur in jazz programs, was an excellent stint at the drums by the great Joe Jones, drumming to "Old Man River ", which seems to have been elected the favorite solo for the boys on the batterie at this year's concerts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percuss - to strike or tap repeatedly, especially as in medical examination
