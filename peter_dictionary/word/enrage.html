enrage

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈreɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈreɪdʒ / (BrE)
to make someone extremely angry; to put into a state of rage

Examples

He behaved publicly with a cocky, swaggering truculence that offended their vulpine Latin minds, and behaved towards them personally with an unimpressed insolence that enraged them beneath their blandness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anger - to make angry
  • Degree Higher: infuriate - to make extremely angry; even more intense than enrage
  • Antonym: calm - to make calm; opposite of enrage

Etymology

From French *enrager*, from *en-* (make) + *rage* (anger).
