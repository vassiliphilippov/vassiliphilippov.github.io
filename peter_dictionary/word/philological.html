philological

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪləˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to philology, especially the study of language in written historical sources and literary texts.

Examples

It was not even in writing Latin epigrams, sometimes bawdy ones, or in translating Lucian from Greek into Latin or in defending the study of Greek against the attack of conservative academics, or in attacking the conservative theologians who opposed Erasmus's philological study of the New Testament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linguistic - relating to language or linguistics

Etymology

From Late Latin philologicus, from Greek philologikos, from philología (love of learning, literature).
