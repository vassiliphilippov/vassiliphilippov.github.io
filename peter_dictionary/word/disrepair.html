disrepair

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈper / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈpeə / (BrE)
The state of being in poor condition or in need of repairs.

Examples

The rest of Black Bottom was a rabbit warren of homes in every condition of neglect, disrepair and careful upkeep.
The feet wore army shoes, in obvious disrepair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - the state of being uncared for or unattended
  • Synonym: decay - the gradual decline in condition or quality
  • Antonym: repair - the action of fixing or restoring to good condition

Etymology

From Old French 'desrepaire', based on 'repair' meaning to restore.
