alundum

1. NOUN

/ əˈlʌndəm / (AmE), / əˈlʌndəm / (BrE)
A synthetic substance made of fused alumina, used as an abrasive and in high-temperature applications.

Examples

Spectra were also obtained from a third sample of f which had been diluted to three times its original volume with powdered, anhydrous alundum** (**f).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alumina - a compound of aluminum and oxygen (Al2O3)
