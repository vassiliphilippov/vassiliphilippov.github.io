gasoline

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsəliːn / (AmE), / ˈɡæsəliːn / (BrE)
A volatile, flammable liquid derived from petroleum, used mainly as a fuel in internal-combustion engines.

Examples

Primary target would be shipments of tanks, guns, aviation gasoline and ammunition coming from Russia and Czechoslovakia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrol - British English term for gasoline, used as fuel in engines

Etymology

From earlier gasolene, probably from 'gas' + chemical suffix '-ine', modeled after similar compounds.
