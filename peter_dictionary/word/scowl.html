scowl

1. VERB

/ skaʊl / (AmE), / skaʊl / (BrE)
to frown in an angry or displeased way

Examples

Ekstrohm scowled.
He scowled at her flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frown - to make a facial expression showing displeasure or concentration

Etymology

Perhaps of Middle English origin, possibly from a Scandinavian source such as Old Norse 'skūla' (to cover, protect), related to facial expressions.
