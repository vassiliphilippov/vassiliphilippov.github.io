wary

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛri / (AmE), / ˈweəri / (BrE)
careful and alert to possible dangers or problems; showing cautious watchfulness

Examples

He drove on, wary and shaken.
The altercation in the coffee house had done little to dampen his spirits, but he was still a little wary around Rector for they had not yet discussed the incident.

Synset Examples

they were wary in their movements
a wary glance at the black clouds
taught to be wary of strangers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - being careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • Synonym: careful - exercising care and attention to avoid risks
  • Antonym: trusting - willing to rely on others without suspicion

Etymology

From Old English wær (aware, cautious); related to Old High German giwar (wary).
