limp

1. ADJ

/ lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
not firm, stiff, or strong; lacking rigidity or structure

Examples

He stood stiffly erect, clutching the staff, his body half hidden by the limp cloth.
There she extracted two limp vellum sheets and wrote off the letters, one to Abel, one to Mark.
At the pool's far end was the little cabana Joyce had mentioned, and on the water's surface floated scattered lavender patches of limp looking lather.

Synset Examples

wilted lettuce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaccid - soft or hanging loosely; lacking firmness
  • Antonym: stiff - firm or rigid; not easily bent or changed in shape

2. VERB

/ lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, object, or process) to move or progress slowly and with difficulty, often due to damage or impairment.

Examples

Seventeen months later, on September 6, 1611, an Irish fishing boat sighted the Discovery limping eastward outside Galway Bay.

Synset Examples

the boat limped into the harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: struggle - to make forceful or strenuous efforts to move or achieve something

3. VERB

/ lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
to walk with difficulty, typically because of an injury or physical disability affecting the legs or feet

Examples

He limps, and the man who hit you and took the cane, he limped.
He limps, and the man who hit you and took the cane, he limped.

Synset Examples

The old woman hobbles down to the store every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobble - to walk with difficulty, typically because of pain or injury

Etymology

Perhaps of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian *lempe* (to move gently, limp).
