bloom

1. VERB

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) to produce or yield flowers; to come into blossom.

Examples

As soon as they are large enough to move, I put mine 9 inches apart where they are to bloom.
The pansies I cherished most bloomed for me in February during a particularly cold winter.
In response to the lengthening days of February they budded, then bloomed their 4 - inch velvety flowers.
Pick the flowers, keep the soil dampened, and each of the pegged-down branches will take root and become a little plant and go on blooming for the rest of the season.
They stick to their principles, insist upon their due, but grow and bloom with dependable regularity if given it.

Synset Examples

The cherry tree bloomed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossom - (of a tree or plant) to produce flowers

Etymology

From Middle English *blomen*, from Old Norse *blóma* (to bloom, flower).

2. NOUN

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
A flower, especially one having showy or colorful parts; the reproductive structure of a flowering plant.

Examples

That cold frame was my morale builder; its mass of bright bloom set in a border of snow made my spirits rise every time I looked at it.
The blooms of Ribes and of the willow and sloe are the places where large numbers of our early insects will assemble: honeybees, bumblebees, and other wild bees, and also various kinds of flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - the reproductive structure of a plant, typically colorful
  • Synonym: blossom - the flower of a plant, especially of a fruit tree

Etymology

From Old Norse blóm (flower, blossom), related to Old English blōma (flower).

3. NOUN

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
the process of producing or bearing flowers

Examples

Probably no one needs to tell you that the way to stop all bloom is to let the blossoms go to seed.
Many people think that pansies last only a few weeks, then their period of growth and bloom is over.

Synset Examples

you will stop all bloom if you let the flowers go to seed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering - the action of producing flowers

Etymology

From Old Norse *blóm* (flower, blossom).

4. NOUN

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
The period of greatest freshness, vitality, or beauty, especially in a person's youth.

Examples

Her hair was dyed, and her bloom was fading, and she must have been crowding forty, but she seemed to be one of those women who cling to the manners and graces of a pretty child of eight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime - the period in life of greatest health, vigor, or strength
  • Antonym: decline - the process of deteriorating or losing strength, especially with age

Etymology

From Old Norse 'blóm', related to the idea of flourishing or blossoming.

5. NOUN

/ bluːm / (AmE), / bluːm / (BrE)
A healthy, rosy glow, especially in the cheeks, suggesting good health or vitality.

Examples

Related Terms
