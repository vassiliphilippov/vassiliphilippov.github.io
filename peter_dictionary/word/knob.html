knob

1. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
A round handle that you turn or pull to operate or open something, such as a door, radio, or drawer.

Examples

Carroll diagnosed Mrs. Hull by taking a drop of blood from her ear and putting it on his "radionic "machine and twirling some knobs (fee $ 50).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - part of an object designed to be held or operated by hand

Etymology

Probably from Middle English knobbe, of Middle Dutch or Low German origin.

2. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
a small, rounded handle or projection, often used for opening doors, drawers, or as an ornamental feature

Examples

He found tepid water in a pitcher and a last bit of soap, and he lathered his face and stood stropping the razor on his broad leather belt, its buckle held firm by a knob of the bedpost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - part of an object designed to be held or operated by hand

Etymology

From Middle English knobbe (lump, knob), probably from Middle Low German knobbe (knot, bud, swelling).
