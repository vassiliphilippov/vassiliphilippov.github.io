lavish

1. ADJ

/ ˈlævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃ / (BrE)
Giving or spending generously and on a grand scale; marked by excessive or abundant generosity.

Examples

Speakers at a Tipoff Club dinner dealt lavish praise to a group of St. Louis University players who, in the words of Coach John Benington, "had more confidence in themselves than I did ".

Synset Examples

distributed gifts with a lavish hand
the critics were lavish in their praise
a munificent gift
his father gave him a half-dollar and his mother a quarter and he thought them munificent
prodigal praise
unsparing generosity
his unstinted devotion
called for unstinting aid to Britain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous
  • Synonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more than is necessary or expected
  • Synonym: extravagant - lacking restraint in spending money or using resources

Etymology

From Middle English *lavasse* (torrent of rain), from Old French *lavasse*, from *laver* (to wash).

2. VERB

/ ˈlævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃ / (BrE)
To give or spend something generously or excessively, often in large quantities or amounts.

Examples

Hence the attention they 're lavishing on the CDC.
Grant all this - still, when modern Unitarianism and the Harvard Divinity School recall with humorous affection the insults Parker lavished upon them, or else argue that after all Parker received the treatment he invited, they betray an uneasy conscience.

Synset Examples

He was showered with praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heap - to give or pour on in large quantities
  • Synonym: bestow - to give generously or present as a gift
  • Antonym: withhold - to refuse to give or hold back

Etymology

From Middle English *lavache*, from Old French *lavasse*, from *laver* (‘to wash’), reflecting the sense of pouring out.
