megaton

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌtʌn / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡətʌn / (BrE)
a unit of explosive power equal to that of one million tons of TNT, used especially for measuring the power of nuclear weapons

Examples

The biggest nuclear device the United States has exploded measured some 15 megatons, although our B-52s are said to be carrying two 20 - megaton bombs apiece.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kiloton - a unit of explosive power equal to one thousand tons of TNT

Etymology

Formed from 'mega-' (meaning million) and 'ton', reflecting its definition.
