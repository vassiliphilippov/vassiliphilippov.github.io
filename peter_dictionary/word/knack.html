knack

1. NOUN

/ næk / (AmE), / næk / (BrE)
A particular talent or special skill for doing something easily or well.

Examples

Pa had a knack for hiding.
A smart, shrewd and ambitious young man, well connected, and with a knack for getting in the good graces of important people, he was bound to go far.

Synset Examples

he had a bent for it
he had a special knack for getting into trouble
he couldn't get the hang of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: flair - natural ability to do something well
  • Synonym: habitude - a habitual way of behaving or doing

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *knak* (sharp sound, clever trick).
