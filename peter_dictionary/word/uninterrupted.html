uninterrupted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪn.təɹˈʌp.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪn.təˈrʌp.tɪd / (BrE)
not stopped, broken, or disturbed; continuing without interruption or disturbance.

Examples

But, in denying any physical reality for this dream, how could the brain possibly compose that realistic, vividly visual continuity uninterrupted by misty fadeout, violent break or sudden substitution?
Have you investigated the possibility of moving midweek holidays forward to Monday or back to Friday in order to have an uninterrupted work week?

Synset Examples

a convalescent needs uninterrupted sleep

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interrupted - stopped or broken in continuity
  • Synonym: continuous - without interruption or break; ongoing

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'interrupted', past participle of 'interrupt' (from Latin 'interrumpere').
