scarify

1. VERB

/ ˈskærəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈskærɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make shallow cuts or incisions in the skin, often for ritual, medical, or identification purposes

Examples

I learned, for example, that he made a practice of yapping at dogs he encountered and, in winter, of sprinkling salt on the icy pavement to scarify their feet.

Synset Examples

The men in some African tribes scarify their faces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incise - to cut into or carve (a surface, especially with a sharp instrument)
  • Synonym: cut - to make an opening, incision, or wound in something

Etymology

From Old French *scarifier*, from Late Latin *scarificare* (to scratch open).
