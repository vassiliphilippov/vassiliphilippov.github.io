unspoken

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspoʊkən / (AmE), / ʌnˈspəʊkən / (BrE)
(of thoughts, feelings, or intentions) not expressed in words; implied or understood without being said.

Examples

Pausing in the outside door to glance behind him, Pat looked his unspoken warning and stepped out.

Synset Examples

a mute appeal
a silent curse
best grief is tongueless
the words stopped at her lips unsounded
unspoken grief
choking exasperation and wordless shame

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoken - expressed in speech or words

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'spoken' (past participle of 'speak').
