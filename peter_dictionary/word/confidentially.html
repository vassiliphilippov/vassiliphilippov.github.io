confidentially

1. ADV

/ ˌkɑːn.fɪˈden.ʃəli / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˈden.ʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that is meant to be private or secret; so that only certain people are intended to know.

Examples

One has to talk confidentially with some of the directors of vocational high schools to realize that a boy cannot just say, "I want to be a plumber ", and then, by doing good work, find a job.
Nodding approvingly and swelling with importance, the old lady whispered confidentially.

Synset Examples

spoke to him intimately and confidentially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - in a way that is kept hidden from others
  • Antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret or hidden

Etymology

From 'confidential' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix, from Latin 'confidentia' (trust).
