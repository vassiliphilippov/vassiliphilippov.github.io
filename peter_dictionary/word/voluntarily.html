voluntarily

1. ADV

/ ˌvɑːlənˈtɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌvɒlənˈteərəli / (BrE)
in a way that is done by choice, without being forced or compelled

Examples

The Administration maintains a productive facilities inventory of small business industrial concerns that have voluntarily registered.
The cops would gather up Connor and the foursome on the third floor and bring us those of them who would voluntarily submit to fingerprinting.

Synset Examples

he voluntarily submitted to the fingerprinting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: involuntarily - in a way that is done unwillingly or without control

Etymology

From Latin voluntarius (of one's free will), from voluntas (will).
