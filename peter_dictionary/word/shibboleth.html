shibboleth

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪbəˌlɛθ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪbəlɪθ / (BrE)
A phrase, principle, or belief that distinguishes a particular group of people and is often used as a test for membership or loyalty.

Examples

When I question them as to what they mean by concepts like liberty and democracy, I find that they fall into two categories: the simpler ones who have simply accepted the shibboleths of their faith without analysis; and the intelligent, cynical ones who scornfully reply that these things do n't count any more in the world of to-day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motto - a short sentence or phrase expressing a belief or ideal

Etymology

From Hebrew *šibbōleth*, meaning 'ear of grain' or 'stream', used as a test of nationality in the Bible.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪbəˌlɛθ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪbəlɪθ / (BrE)
a word, expression, or custom that distinguishes a particular group of people, especially one regarded as outmoded or no longer important

Examples

But the most notable thing about the incantation of these ex liberals was that the one-time shibboleth of socialism was conspicuously absent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catchword - a word or phrase associated with a particular group or movement
  • Synonym: password - a secret word or phrase that must be used to gain admission

Etymology

From Hebrew *šibbōlet* (ear of grain, stream), used in the Bible to distinguish members of different groups by pronunciation.
