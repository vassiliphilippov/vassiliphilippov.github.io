buttocks

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtəks / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəkz / (BrE)
the fleshy parts of the human body that form the bottom and on which a person sits.

Examples

With a tiny fur-piece wrapped around her shoulders, she wiggled her satin covered buttocks down the street before him and did n't stop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluteus maximus - the main muscle of the buttocks

Etymology

Plural of 'buttock', from Middle English 'buttok', possibly diminutive of 'butt' (end, stump).
