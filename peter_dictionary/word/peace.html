peace

1. NOUN

/ pis / (AmE), / piːs / (BrE)
A state or period in which there is no war or fighting in a country or region; the absence of conflict.

Examples

He is a Buddhist, which means that to him peace and the sanctity of human life are not only religious dogma, but a profound and unshakable Weltanschauung.
The Providence Daily Journal stated that although the guilt of Brown was evident, the South must guarantee him a fair trial to preserve domestic peace.

"Uncle Sam "was, indeed, a rich uncle to Prokofieff, in those opulent, post-war victory years of peace and prosperity, bold speculations and extravaganzas, enjoyment and pleasure: "The Golden Twenties ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: war - a state of armed conflict between countries or groups
  • Synonym: truce - an agreement between enemies or opponents to stop fighting for a certain time

Etymology

From Old French 'pais', from Latin 'pax' (peace).

/ pis / (AmE), / piːs / (BrE)
a state of calm or tranquility; the absence of mental stress or anxiety

  • Antonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: stress - mental or emotional strain or tension

Etymology
