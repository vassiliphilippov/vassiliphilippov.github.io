quip

1. VERB

/ kwɪp / (AmE), / kwɪp / (BrE)
to make a witty or humorous remark; to say something clever or sarcastic

Examples

"That permitted us to start controlling the ball right away ", said Stram, quipping, "I think I 'll put that play in the book ".

Synset Examples

The students were gagging during dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - say something to provoke laughter
  • Synonym: jest - speak or act in a playful or humorous way
  • Synonym: banter - exchange playful remarks

Etymology

Possibly from Latin quippe (indeed, to be sure), used ironically.
