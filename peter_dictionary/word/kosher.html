kosher

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃə / (BrE)
(of food or premises) conforming to the dietary laws of Judaism.

Examples

It appears that an Orthodox girl in the community disrupted plans for an outing sponsored by one of the Jewish service groups because she would not travel on Saturday and, in addition, required kosher food.

Synset Examples

kosher meat
a kosher kitchen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halal - (of food) permissible under Islamic law

Etymology

From Yiddish *kosher*, from Hebrew *kāshēr* (proper, fit, correct).
