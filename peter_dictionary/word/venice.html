Venice

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɛnɪs / (BrE)
A city in northeastern Italy, built on a group of 118 small islands separated by canals and linked by bridges; known for its historical significance, architecture, and as a major tourist destination.

Examples

Henrietta's feeling of identity with Sara Sullam was crowned by her discovery of the coincidence that Sara's epitaph in the Jewish cemetery in Venice referred to her as "the Sulamite ".

Etymology

From Italian 'Venezia,' from Latin 'Venetia.'
