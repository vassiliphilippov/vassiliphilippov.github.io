castigate

1. VERB

/ ˈkæstəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæstɪˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to criticize or reprimand someone severely, especially in a formal or public way

Examples

Washington castigated his critic, General Conway, as being capable of "all the meanness of intrigue to gratify the absurd resentment of disappointed vanity ".

Synset Examples

She chastised him for his insensitive remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chastise - to rebuke or reprimand severely
  • Synonym: criticize - to express disapproval or find fault with
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of

Etymology

From Latin 'castigatus', past participle of 'castigare' (to chastise, to correct).
