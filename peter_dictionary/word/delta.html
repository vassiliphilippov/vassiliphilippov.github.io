delta

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛltə / (AmE), / ˈdɛltə / (BrE)
A low, usually triangular area of land formed at the mouth of a river where it divides into several branches before entering a larger body of water.

Examples

After three weeks' swift sailing, however, the ship entered an area of shallow marshes and river deltas.

Synset Examples

the Mississippi River delta
the Nile delta

Etymology

From Greek *delta*, the name of the Greek letter Δ, due to the shape's resemblance to the letter.
