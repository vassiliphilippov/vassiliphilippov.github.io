propeller

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɛlər / (AmE), / prəˈpɛlə / (BrE)
A mechanical device with blades that rotates to push against air or water, commonly used to move ships, boats, or aircraft forward.

Examples

The Jap's propeller flew off in pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screw - a similar device used primarily for propulsion in ships
  • Synonym: rotor - rotating part of a helicopter or other rotating-wing aircraft

Etymology

From propel + -er, ultimately from Latin *propellere* (to drive forward).
