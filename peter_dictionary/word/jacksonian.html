Jacksonian

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒækˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒækˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
Relating to Andrew Jackson, his presidency, or the political principles he espoused, especially popular democracy and individualism in early 19th-century America.

Examples

The socialism implicit in the slogan of the Roosevelt Revolution, freedom from want and fear, seems a far cry from the individualism of the First Amendment to the Constitution, or of the Jacksonian frontier.

Etymology

From the name Andrew Jackson (1767–1845), seventh President of the United States, with the suffix -ian.
