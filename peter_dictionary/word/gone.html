gone

1. ADJ

/ ɡɑn / (AmE), / ɡɒn / (BrE)
No longer present; destroyed, finished, or lost.

Examples

"I know when my reflexes are gone and I'm not going to be any 25th man on the ball club ".
Suddenly my reflexes are gone.
Gone is the tabula rasa of the mind.
Now the hope was gone.

Synset Examples

we are gone geese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - no longer possessed or retained; unable to be recovered
  • Synonym: destroyed - completely ruined or put an end to
  • Synonym: ruined - severely damaged so as to be useless or no longer existing

2. ADJ

/ ɡɑn / (AmE), / ɡɒn / (BrE)
No longer alive; dead (used as a euphemism or in expressions of loss).

Examples

The child was gone.
One by one, our old friends are gone.

Synset Examples

he is deceased
our dear departed friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead - no longer alive
  • Synonym: deceased - having died, no longer living
  • Antonym: alive - living, not dead

3. ADJ

/ ɡɑn / (AmE), / ɡɒn / (BrE)
No longer present, existing, or available; having departed or disappeared.

Examples

But still... the place you live in is that much more drab and empty that they're gone.
That kid's long gone.

Synset Examples

gone with the wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - no longer possessed or accessible
  • Antonym: available - able to be used or obtained; not gone

Etymology

Past participle of 'go', from Old English 'gān'.
