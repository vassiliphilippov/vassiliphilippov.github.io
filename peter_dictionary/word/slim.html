slim

1. ADJ

/ slɪm / (AmE), / slɪm / (BrE)
(of a person or object) attractively thin; having a slender or delicate build.

Examples

Carrying it to the living room, she imagined the picture she made: tall and roundly slim, a bit sophisticated in her yellow sheath, with a graceful swingy walk that she had learned as a twirler with the school band.
The slim pirogues in harmony with the majestically meandering Mekong River.
She was wearing a brown cotton dress, cut across the hips in a way that was supposed to make her look slimmer, a yoke set into the skirt and flaring pleats below.

Synset Examples

she was slender as a willow shoot is slender
a slim girl with straight blonde hair
watched her slight figure cross the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slender - gracefully thin in form
  • Synonym: thin - having little fat or flesh; not thick
  • Degree Higher: skinny - very thin, often unattractively so
  • Antonym: chubby - slightly fat in an endearing way

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian *slim* (slender, thin).

2. ADJ

/ slɪm / (AmE), / slɪm / (BrE)
small in amount, size, or degree; not plentiful or abundant

Examples

Pickings are pretty slim in the yard.

Synset Examples

slender wages
a slim chance of winning
a small surplus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scant - barely sufficient; very limited in quantity
  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality; not enough
  • Antonym: plentiful - existing in large amounts
