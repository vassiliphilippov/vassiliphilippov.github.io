rigorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪɡərəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡərəs / (BrE)
Demanding strict attention to rules, procedures, or standards; thorough and exacting.

Examples

Russia's young gymnasts have studied dance before having the rigorous training on apparatus.

Synset Examples

rigorous discipline
tight security
stringent safety measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stringent - strict, precise, and exacting
  • Synonym: strict - enforcing or demanding adherence to rules
  • Antonym: lenient - more permissive or tolerant

Etymology

From Latin 'rigorosus' (stiff, rigorous), from 'rigor' (stiffness).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪɡərəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡərəs / (BrE)
Extremely thorough, accurate, or strict in procedure or attention to detail; allowing no deviation from a standard.

Examples

The Mathematical Appendix presents the rigorous argument, but is best read after Part 1, in order that the assumptions underlying the equations may be explicit.

Synset Examples

rigorous application of the law
a strict vegetarian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict - demanding exact observance of rules or standards
  • Antonym: lenient - not strict or harsh; permissive

Etymology

From Latin 'rigorosus' (harsh, severe), from 'rigor' (stiffness).
