sanctified

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæŋktɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktɪˌfaɪd / (BrE)
made or declared holy; set apart or consecrated for a sacred purpose

Examples

These are ways of jointly participating in significantly symbolized, standardized, and ordered religiously sanctified behavior.

Synset Examples

a consecrated church
the sacred mosque
sacred elephants
sacred bread and wine
sanctified wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrated - made or declared sacred, usually formally
  • Synonym: sacred - regarded with reverence, especially due to a connection to religion
  • Antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; secular or irreverent

Etymology

From Latin sanctificare (to make holy), from sanctus (holy) + facere (to make).
