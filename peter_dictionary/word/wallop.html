wallop

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑːləp / (AmE), / ˈwɒləp / (BrE)
to strike or hit someone or something very hard

Examples

The spectacular Mays, who reaches a decade in the big leagues come May 25, joined six other sluggers who walloped four home runs in a span of nine innings.

Synset Examples

The teacher whacked the boy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whack - to strike or slap forcibly with a sharp blow
  • Synonym: smack - to strike with a sharp, resounding blow

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, first attested in the late Middle English period.
