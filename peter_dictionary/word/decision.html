decision

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
(in boxing and some other sports) a victory awarded on points by the judges when there is no knockout or technical knockout.

Examples

The Orioles tonight retained the distinction of being the only winless team among the eighteen Major-League clubs as they dropped their sixth straight spring exhibition decision, this one to the Kansas City Athletics by a score of 5 to 3.
Kenny Lane of Muskegon, Mich., world's seventh ranked lightweight, had little trouble in taking a unanimous decision over Rip Randall of Tyler, Tex., here Monday night.

Synset Examples

had little trouble in taking a unanimous decision over his opponent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knockout - a victory in boxing when an opponent is unable to continue after being struck

Etymology

From Latin 'decisio' (a cutting off, decision), from 'decidere' (to decide).
