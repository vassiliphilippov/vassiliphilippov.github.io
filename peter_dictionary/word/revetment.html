revetment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɛtmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛtmənt / (BrE)
A protective barrier or facing, especially one built of earth, concrete, or sandbags to protect against explosions or prevent erosion.

Examples

The truck dropped them off at the various revetments spread through the jungle.

Etymology

From French 'revetement', from 'revêtir' meaning 'to clothe or cover'.
