misanthrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪs.ənˌθroʊp / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.ənˌθrəʊp / (BrE)
A person who dislikes and avoids other people; someone who has a general distrust or hatred of humankind.

Examples

Such was the impromptu that Voltaire gave to howls of laughter at Sans Souci and that was soon circulated in manuscript throughout the literary circles of Europe, to be printed sometime later, but with the name of Timon of Athens, the famous misanthrope, substituted for that of Rousseau.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: philanthropist - person who seeks to promote the welfare of others; loves humankind

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpos ('hating mankind'), from misein ('to hate') + anthrōpos ('man, human').
