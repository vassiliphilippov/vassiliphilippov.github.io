neutrality

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈtræləti / (AmE), / njuːˈtræləti / (BrE)
the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict or war

Examples

A nation such as Switzerland could be neutralized by agreement and could be relied upon to protect its neutrality; more doubtful, but possible, (with an assist from the North) was the neutralization of the Latin American countries; out of the question was the neutralization of Asia and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonalignment - the policy or practice of not aligning with either side in a conflict, especially in international relations
  • Antonym: belligerency - the state of being at war or in conflict

Etymology

From French 'neutralité', from Latin 'neutralitas', from 'neutralis' (neither).
