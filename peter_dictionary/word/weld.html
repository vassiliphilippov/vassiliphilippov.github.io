weld

1. VERB

/ wɛld / (AmE), / wɛld / (BrE)
to join pieces of metal or plastic together by heating the surfaces and pressing them so that they fuse

Examples

It took me a moment to realize what was odd about that panel: there was a gimbaled compass welded to it, which rocked gently back and forth as the Land Rover bounced about.

Synset Examples

weld metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solder - join metals by melting a filler metal at a lower temperature
  • Synonym: braze - join metals using a filler metal with a lower melting point than the workpieces

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'welden' (to weld, wield), possibly influenced by Swedish 'välla' (to boil, weld).
