edify

1. VERB

/ ˈɛdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to instruct or improve (someone) morally or intellectually; to enlighten or provide knowledge

Examples

Less respect for the legal conventions was displayed by Castro's right hand man, Che Guevara, who edified the Inter-American Economic and Social council meeting in Montevideo by reading two secret American documents purloined from the United States embassy at Caracas, Venezuela.

Synset Examples

Can you enlighten me--I don't understand this proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding

Etymology

From Latin *aedificare* (to build), later used figuratively for 'building up' knowledge or character.
