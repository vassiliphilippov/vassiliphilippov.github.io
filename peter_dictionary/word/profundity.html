profundity

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfʌndəti / (AmE), / prəˈfʌndɪti / (BrE)
Great depth of insight, knowledge, or understanding; the quality of being profound.

Examples

In fact, it is in just such a situation that the profundity of Bultmann's argument is disclosed.

Synset Examples

the depth of my feeling
the profoundness of the silence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth - the quality of being deep (of thought or knowledge)
  • Antonym: shallowness - lack of depth (in thought or knowledge)

Etymology

From Latin 'profunditas' (depth, intensity), from 'profundus' (deep).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈfʌn.də.ti / (AmE), / prəˈfʌn.dɪ.ti / (BrE)
great depth of insight, wisdom, or understanding, especially when it is difficult to grasp or obscure

Examples

He read Henry James and used to pretend profundity through eye-beamings at people.

Synset Examples

the anthropologist was impressed by the reconditeness of the native proverbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth - great intensity or profundity, especially intellectual or emotional
  • Antonym: shallowness - lack of depth or profundity

Etymology

From Late Latin 'profunditas' (deepness, depth), from 'profundus' (deep).
