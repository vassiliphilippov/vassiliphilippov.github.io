fruitfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfruːtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfruːtfʊlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of producing good results or promoting healthy growth; fertility or productivity.

Examples

Why did Prokofieff expand in stature and fecundity, while Stravinsky (who leaped into fame like a young giant) dwindled in stature and fruitfulness?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertility - the ability to produce abundantly
  • Antonym: barrenness - the state of being unproductive or infertile

Etymology

From 'fruitful' + '-ness', ultimately from Old French 'fruit' (produce, offspring) and Latin 'fructus' (enjoyment, produce).
