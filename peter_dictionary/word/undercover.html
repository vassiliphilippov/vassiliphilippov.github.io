undercover

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈkʌvər / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkʌvə / (BrE)
(of an activity or person) done or acting in secret, especially in order to obtain information for the police, government, etc.

Examples

Fearful of inviting a military showdown with the West which they could not win, the Communists seek to undermine Western power where the nuclear might of the West is irrelevant - in backwoods guerrilla skirmishes, in mob uprisings in the streets, in parliaments, in clandestine meetings of undercover conspirators, at the United Nations, on the propaganda front, at diplomatic conferences - preferably at the highest level.

Synset Examples

clandestine intelligence operations
cloak-and-dagger activities behind enemy lines
hole-and-corner intrigue
secret missions
a secret agent
secret sales of arms
surreptitious mobilization of troops
an undercover investigation
underground resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially for illicit purposes
  • Synonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed
  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret or hidden

Etymology

From under + cover, literally meaning 'beneath a covering', applied figuratively to secret or hidden operations since the early 20th century.
