revamp

1. VERB

/ riˈvæmp / (AmE), / riˈvæmp / (BrE)
to improve, renovate, or revise something, often by making significant changes or repairs

Examples

Consider what you can afford to spend and what your goals are before setting up or revamping your employee benefit program.
Foreign aid programs were revamped to give greater emphasis to economic aid and to encourage political reform in recipient nations.
In fact, they often revamped their social activities to include class members previously unknown.
Buy some worthless old boat, fix it **up** new.

Synset Examples

They revamped their old house before selling it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovate - to restore to a good state; to renew or repair
  • Synonym: restore - to bring back to original condition
  • Synonym: repair - to fix or mend something that is broken

Etymology

Probably a blend of 're-' and 'vamp' (meaning to patch or repair).
