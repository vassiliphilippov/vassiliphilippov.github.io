tantalize

1. VERB

/ ˈtæn.təˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtan.tə.laɪz / (BrE)
To torment or tease someone by showing them something desirable but keeping it out of reach.

Examples

I sat there with the faint odor of charcoal-broiled steaks tantalizing my nostrils and occasionally catching the aroma of coffee.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful or irritating way

Etymology

From the mythological character Tantalus in Greek mythology, who was punished by being kept just out of reach of food and drink.
