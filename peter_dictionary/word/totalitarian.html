totalitarian

1. ADJ

/ toʊˌtæləˈtɛriən / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
Relating to a system of government that is centralized and dictatorial and requires complete subservience to the state.

Examples

This seems like an attitude favoring a sort of totalitarian bureaucracy which, under a President of the same stamp, would try to coerce an uncooperative Congress or Supreme Court.

Synset Examples

a totalitarian regime crushes all autonomous institutions in its drive to seize the human soul

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to government by the people, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

From Italian 'totalitario', from Latin 'totus' (whole, entire).
