pop

1. VERB

/ pɑp / (AmE), / pɒp / (BrE)
(in baseball) to hit a ball high into the air but not far, typically resulting in an easy catch for a fielder.

Examples

Pete Ward was sent in for House and, after failing in a bunt attempt, popped to Howser on the grass back of short.
Just when it seems baseball might be losing its grip on the masses up pops heroics to start millions of tongues to wagging.
"Spahnie does n't know how to merely go through the motions ", remarked Enos Slaughter, another all-out guy, who played rightfield that day and popped one over the clubhouse.

Synset Examples

He popped out to shortstop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly out - hit a fly ball that is caught by a fielder for an out (in baseball)

2. VERB

/ pɑp / (AmE), / pɒp / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a sudden sharp, explosive sound

Examples

Stengel probably preached too much in the early days when the kid wanted to pop his bubble gum and sow his oats.
[CHAMPAGNE CORK POPPING] [PEOPLE LAUGHING] [SHRIEKS] [PEOPLE CHEERING] [MUSIC PLAYING] [SOFTLY]

Synset Examples

The cork of the champagne bottle popped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - make a sudden sharp cracking sound
  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise, often from impact or explosion
  • Degree Higher: explode - burst or cause to burst loudly and violently

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, first recorded in the 14th century.

3. NOUN

/ pɑp / (AmE), / pɒp / (BrE)
(informal) Father; a familiar or affectionate term for one's dad.

Examples

He said more loudly, "I'm full, old Pop ".
SONNY: You'’re goddamn right, that'’s Pop talking!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dad - informal term for father
  • Synonym: father - male parent

Etymology

Probably from baby talk, imitative of the sounds made by infants.

You 'll have the neighbor's eyes popping as well as their mouths watering!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protrude - to stick out from a surface
