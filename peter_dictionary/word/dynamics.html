dynamics

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / daɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mechanics that deals with the forces and their effect on motion in physical systems

Examples

However, the dynamics of airflow, from morphologic considerations alone, may conceivably be different in the monkey than in the horse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: statics - the branch of mechanics concerned with bodies at rest and forces in equilibrium

Etymology

From Greek dynamis (power, force), via French dynamique.
