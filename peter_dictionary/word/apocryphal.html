apocryphal

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɑkrəfəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒkrɪfəl / (BrE)
(of a story or statement) widely circulated but of doubtful authenticity; probably not true.

Examples

A story, no doubt apocryphal, for Mercer himself denies it, has him sporting a monocle in those Village days.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authentic - genuine; of undisputed origin

Etymology

From late Latin apocryphus (secret, not canonical), from Greek apokryphos (hidden).
