afferent

1. ADJ

/ ˈæf.ɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
(of nerves, vessels, or signals) carrying sensory information or fluid toward a central organ or point, especially toward the central nervous system.

Examples

Throughout, there were marked arteriolosclerosis and hyalinization of afferent glomerular arterioles.

Synset Examples

afferent nerves
afferent impulses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: efferent - carrying information or signals away from a central organ or point

Etymology

From Latin afferens, afferent- ‘bringing toward’, from ad- ‘to’ + ferre ‘to carry’.
