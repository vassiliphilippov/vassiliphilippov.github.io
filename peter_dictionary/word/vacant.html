vacant

1. ADJ

/ ˈveɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a person's expression or mind) showing or suggesting a lack of thought, knowledge, or intelligence; blank or empty.

Examples

By 3: 00 A.M. they reached his house and found it vacant.

Synset Examples

a vacant mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blank - (of an expression) showing no emotion or understanding
  • Antonym: expressive - showing thought, feelings, or meaning in expression

Etymology

From Latin vacans, vacantis, present participle of vacare (to be empty).
