finished

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of materials or goods) having been brought to the desired or complete final state; completed, not in progress or unfinished.

Examples

Those who saw his finished Pieta would take the place of the biblical witnesses.
There was no display of either works in progress or of finished work.
The Man from Mars had interrupted him with his work half finished - and that stupid jackass Short was certain to louse it up.
The finished - and drastically cut - product would begin with a hazy longshot of Joyce entering the suds, then bursting above the pool's surface clad in layers of lavender lather, and I had a hunch this item was going to sell tons and tons of soap; even to clean men and boys.
McKenzie, the art editor, took one look at my finished sketch and said, "Nothing doing, Rufus.

Synset Examples

a finished product

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfinished - not brought to a final or desired state; incomplete
  • Synonym: complete - to make whole or finished

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'finish,' from Middle English 'finisshen', from Old French 'finir', from Latin 'finire' (to limit, end).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
Having been brought to an end; completed.

Examples

Then I plan my attack: the parts I will finish first, the range of values, the accenting of minor details - all in all, mechanics of producing the finished job with a maximum of crispness.
Stanley had filled out the return and because, when he was finished, it was close to the lunch hour, he had politely asked Kitti to join him, never expecting her to accept.
I'm just about finished, sir.

Synset Examples

are you finished?
gave me the finished manuscript

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completed - fully finished; brought to a conclusion
  • Antonym: unfinished - not completed; still in progress

Etymology

From the verb 'finish', from Middle English 'finisshen', from Old French 'finiss-', stem of 'finir', from Latin 'finire' (to end).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɪn.ɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɪn.ɪʃt / (BrE)
No longer able to succeed or continue; brought to ruin or defeat.

Examples

You're finished.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruined - Brought to a state of destruction or irreparable loss
  • Synonym: done for - Certain to fail or suffer defeat
  • Antonym: successful - Having achieved success or favorable outcome
