platitudinous

1. ADJ

/ plætɪˈtjudɪnəs / (AmE), / plætɪˈtjuːdɪnəs / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) dull, trite, and lacking originality, especially while trying to sound important or significant.

Examples

The first sentence, with its platitudinous irony, announces an emblematic intent: "The way to the churchyard ran along beside the highroad, ran beside it all the way to the end; that is to say, to the churchyard ".

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
  • Antonym: original - new and different in a good way; not copied

Etymology

From 'platitude' + '-inous', modeled after 'platitude' (from French 'platitude', from 'plat' meaning 'flat').
