important

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔrtnt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtnt / (BrE)
Having great significance or value; deserving particular attention or consideration.

Examples

Because of its important game with Arkansas coming up Saturday, Baylor worked out in the rain Monday - mud or no mud.
That is why it is important to invest in comfortable, airy types of shoes.
Orange and lemon are considered important as are such pastels as blue and lilac.
The appointment of U Thant of Burma as the U.N.'s Acting Secretary General - at this writing, the choice appears to be certain - offers further proof that in politics it is more important to have no influential enemies than to have influential friends.
During the making recently of an important Biblical film, some 40 volumes of research material and sketches not only of costumes and interiors, but of architectural developments, sports arenas, vehicles, and other paraphernalia were compiled, consulted, and complied with.

Synset Examples

important people
the important questions of the day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significant - sufficiently great or important to be worthy of attention
  • Degree Higher: crucial - decisive or critical, especially in the success or failure of something
  • Antonym: unimportant - not significant or noteworthy
  • Degree Lower: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'importans', present participle of 'importare' (to bring in, signify).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔɹtənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
Of great significance or value; crucial to achieving a particular result or resolving a situation.

Examples

How can you cash in on this fast-growing type of outlet and still maintain relationships with older existing outlets which are still important?
The most important quality of the meanings is that they should be as definable as possible.

Synset Examples

a crucial moment in his career
a crucial election
a crucial issue for women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucial - decisive or critical, especially in the success or failure of something
  • Synonym: vital - absolutely necessary or essential
  • Synonym: significant - sufficiently great or important to be worthy of attention
  • Antonym: unimportant - not important; lacking significance
  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Antonym: minor - less important, serious, or significant

Etymology

From Latin *importans*, present participle of *importare* (to bring in, carry).

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔɹtənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
Having great effect, influence, or meaning; significant or notable.

Examples

This is important to understanding the position that doctrinaire liberals found themselves in after World War 2, and our great democratic victory that brought no peace.
This is an important consideration.
Sometimes strong stress serves to focus an important secondary relationship.
The important thing was that Burton had gone somewhere to meet a blackmailer with a gun in his pocket.

Synset Examples

a significant change in tax laws
a significant change in the Constitution
a significant contribution
significant details
statistically significant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significant - having meaning or importance
  • Degree Higher: crucial - extremely important or essential
  • Degree Lower: major - greater in size, extent, or importance
  • Antonym: insignificant - not important or lacking significance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *importantem*, present participle of Latin *importare* ('to bring in').
