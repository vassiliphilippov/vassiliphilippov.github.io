unreason

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈriːzən / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːz(ə)n / (BrE)
The state or quality of being irrational or lacking reason; absence of logical or rational thought.

Examples

Their artistic rationale is given to the witness of unreason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrationality - the quality of being illogical or unreasonable
  • Antonym: reason - the power of the mind to think, understand, and form judgments logically

Etymology

From un- (not) + reason.
