shepherd

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpəd / (BrE)
A clergyman, especially in Christian contexts, who watches over and guides a group of people or congregation.

Examples

Intercede for our separated brethren, that with us in the one true fold they may be united to the chief Shepherd, the vicar of thy Son ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastor - a Christian minister or priest in charge of a congregation
  • Antonym: flock - a group of people under the care of a clergyman, especially a congregation

Etymology

From Old English 'sceaphierde', meaning 'sheep herder', later used metaphorically for spiritual leaders.

2. VERB

/ ˈʃɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpəd / (BrE)
to guide or direct someone or something carefully and protectively, like a shepherd guiding sheep

Examples

Are there individuals in your organization who can shepherd a new product through to commercialization; who can develop reliable estimates of sales volume, production, and distribution costs; and translate the whole into profit and loss and balance sheet figures which management can act on with some assurance?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - to show the way or direct; to lead
  • Synonym: lead - to direct or guide toward a goal

Etymology

From Old English *sceaphierde* (sheep herder), combining 'sceap' (sheep) + 'hierde' (herder).
