powdery

1. ADJ

/ ˈpaʊdəri / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəri / (BrE)
Consisting of or resembling fine, dry particles like powder.

Examples

Off and on, all day, someone would be wiping at the powdery gray dust that settled over everything.

Synset Examples

powdered cellulose
powdery snow
pulverized sugar is prepared from granulated sugar by grinding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powdered - made into or resembling powder

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-y', denoting an adjective form.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpaʊdəri / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəri / (BrE)
(of a color or appearance) having a soft, dull quality as if lightly covered with powder.

Examples

She glanced at the man nodding beside her, a man with weather cracks furrowed into his lean cheeks, with powdery pale eyes reflecting all the droughts he had seen, reflecting the sky and the drought which must follow now in August - yes, with eyes predicting the drought and here it was only June, only festival time again and thoughts of Gratt Shafer would not leave her.

Synset Examples

a powdery blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalky - having a dull, pale, powder-like appearance

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-y'.
