halter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltə / (BrE)
A rope or straps placed around the head of a horse or other animal, used for leading or tying it.

Examples

Turning his back, Curt crossed to the stall, reached over to untie the buckskin's halter rope, and waved his hand in the animal's face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridle - A piece of equipment for controlling a horse, which includes a bit and reins

Etymology

From Old English *hæltor* (rope for leading an animal).
