forgo

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈgoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːˈgəʊ / (BrE)
to decide not to do or have something; to go without or give up voluntarily

Examples

I would have foregone my romantic chances rather than leave a friend sweltering and dusty and - Well, at least I would n't have shouted back a taunt.
I forgo the vengeance of my son.

Synset Examples

We are dispensing with formalities
relinquish the old ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily give up a claim or right
  • Synonym: waive - to choose not to demand something
  • Synonym: surrender - to give up something to another person under compulsion or voluntarily
  • Antonym: claim - to demand or assert one's right to something

Etymology

From Old English 'forgān' ('for-' expressing negation + 'gān' to go).
