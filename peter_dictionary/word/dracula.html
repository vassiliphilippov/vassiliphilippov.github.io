Dracula

1. PROPN

/ drəˈkjuːlə / (AmE), / drəˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
A fictional vampire and the main antagonist in the 1897 gothic horror novel by Bram Stoker.

Examples

Welcome, Count Dracula.

Etymology

Named after the historical figure Vlad Dracula (Vlad the Impaler), from Romanian 'dracul' meaning 'dragon', 'devil'.
