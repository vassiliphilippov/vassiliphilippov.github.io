absentee

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.sənˈti / (AmE), / ˌæb.sənˈtiː / (BrE)
A person who is not present in a place where they are expected to be or usually are.

Examples

I came home to vote in the presidential election because they wouldn'’t give me an absentee ballot.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present - person who is in attendance

Etymology

From Latin *absens*, present participle of *abesse* (to be away).
