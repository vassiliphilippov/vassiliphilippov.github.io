entice

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈtaɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪs / (BrE)
to attract or persuade someone to do something, often by offering something appealing or by making false or exaggerated promises

Examples

Although the false glamour surrounding bourbon or other whisky commercials is possibly no more fatuous than the pseudo-sophistication with which TV soft-drinks are downed or toothpaste applied, there is a sad difference between enticing a viewer into sipping Oopsie-Cola and gulling him into downing bourbon.

Synset Examples

He lured me into temptation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempt - to try to persuade someone to do something, especially something wrong or unwise
  • Antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Old French 'enticier', from Vulgar Latin '*intitiare', of uncertain origin.
