freshman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃmən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃmən / (BrE)
A person who is new to an activity, job, or group; a newcomer or beginner.

Examples

Hansen, who slugged the 1960 Oriole high of 22 homers and drove in 86 runs on a .255 freshman average, completes the Birds' spring squad at 49 players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginner - A person just starting to learn a skill or take part in an activity
  • Antonym: veteran - A person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From fresh + man, originally denoting a first-year student.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃmən / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃmən / (BrE)
A student in their first year at a college or university.

Examples

Moritz came to Texas in 1954 but his freshman football efforts were hampered by a knee injury.
A freshman girl's father not too long ago called a dean at Brooklyn College and demanded the "low-down "on a boy who was going out with his daughter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sophomore - a student in their second year at college or university

Etymology

From 'fresh' + 'man', originally in the sense 'newcomer', dating from the mid-16th century.
