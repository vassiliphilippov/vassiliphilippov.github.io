pith

1. NOUN

/ pɪθ / (AmE), / pɪθ / (BrE)
the soft, spongy tissue found in the center of the stems of most flowering plants, often used for storing and transporting nutrients

Examples

Frequently a few isolated thick walled cells or, rarely, groups of such cells in the xylem region, were also specifically stained, but there was no such staining in epidermis, cortex, most xylem cells, ray cells, or pith.

Etymology

Old English pitha (soft inner part of plants), related to Old High German pfiza.
