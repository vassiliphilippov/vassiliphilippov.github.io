acquaint

1. VERB

/ əˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make (someone) aware of or familiar with something

Examples

Other THC activities followed, conducted by shopping centers, department stores, recreation equipment dealers, radio - TV stations, newspapers, and other organizations interested in the need existing to acquaint youngsters with the proper use of sporting firearms and the development of correct attitudes and appreciations related to hunting and wise use of our natural resources.

Synset Examples

you should acquaint yourself with your new computer
We familiarized ourselves with the new surroundings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiarize - to make familiar or knowledgeable about something

Etymology

From Old French 'acointier' (to make known), from Latin 'accognitare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'cognoscere' (to know).

2. VERB

/ əˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make someone aware of or familiar with someone or something

Examples

A series of high school assemblies to acquaint junior and senior students with the Junior Achievement program begins at St. Thomas Aquinas Monday.

Synset Examples

permit me to acquaint you with my son
introduce the new neighbors to the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiarize - to make someone or oneself familiar with something
  • Synonym: introduce - to make someone known to another by name

Etymology

From Old French 'acointer', from Latin 'accognitare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'cognoscere' (to know).
