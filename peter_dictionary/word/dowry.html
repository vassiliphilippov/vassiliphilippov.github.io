dowry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊəri / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəri / (BrE)
Property or money that a bride brings to her husband when they marry.

Examples

"O Blessed Virgin Mary, Mother of God and our most gentle queen and mother, look down in mercy upon England, thy "dowry ", and upon us all who greatly hope and trust in thee.
As he went out he told Freddie the dinner was perfect, and when he got his hat and coat from Nancy Parks and put a fifty-piece piece in the slot, he told her to be sure that it went toward her dowry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bride price - money or property given by the groom or his family to the bride's family
  • Synonym: dower - a widow's share of her husband's estate

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'dowarie', from Latin 'dotarium' (portion, dowry), from 'dos' (gift, marriage portion).
