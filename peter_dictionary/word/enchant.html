enchant

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtʃænt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːnt / (BrE)
to fill someone with great delight or charm; to captivate or hold spellbound

Examples

"Heaven forbid "! cried the ladies, enchanted by his music.
Now Gavin Maxwell's ring of bright water has widened to enchant the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - to attract or delight greatly
  • Synonym: captivate - to attract and hold the attention of someone
  • Antonym: bore - to make uninterested or uninterested

Etymology

From Old French enchanter, from Latin incantare (to chant a magic formula).
