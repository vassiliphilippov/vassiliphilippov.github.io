New Waver

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈweɪvər / (AmE), / njuː ˈweɪvə / (BrE)
A film maker who follows the principles and style of the New Wave movement, especially in cinema.

Examples

Cause of all this commotion: squat, pug-nosed, balding, hopelessly ugly Jean-Pierre Bravado, a Bogartian figure, who plays a sadistic, amoral, philosophic Tasti-Freeze salesman in old New-Waver Fredrico de Mille Rossilini's endlessly provocative film, A Sour Sponge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auteur - an artist, especially a film director, regarded as the author of their work
  • Synonym: New Wave - a movement in French cinema of the late 1950s and 1960s characterized by experimental techniques

Etymology

From 'New Wave', a term for revolutionary artistic movements, especially in film (e.g., French Nouvelle Vague), with '-er' denoting a person associated with it.
