alligator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈælɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
Leather made from the hide of an alligator, often used for shoes, bags, and accessories.

Examples

When a customer asked for alligator shoes, she said, "What size is your alligator "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - leather from the hide of a crocodile

Etymology

From Spanish *el lagarto* (“the lizard”), referring to the animal, later applied to the leather made from it.

2. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈalɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
A large, amphibious reptile of the genus Alligator, related to crocodiles but having a shorter and broader snout, native to the Americas and China.

Examples

When a customer asked for alligator shoes, she said, "What size is your alligator "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large reptile with longer, narrower snout

Etymology

From Spanish 'el lagarto' (the lizard), influenced by Latin 'lacerta' (lizard).
