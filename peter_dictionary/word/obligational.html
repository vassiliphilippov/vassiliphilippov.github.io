obligational

1. ADJ

/ əˌblɪɡəˈʃənəl / (AmE), / ɒˌblɪɡəˈʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or able to create a legal or financial obligation; pertaining to obligations, especially in law or finance.

Examples

New obligational authority for 1961 recommended in this budget for aircraft procurement (excluding amounts for related research and construction) totals $ 4753 million, which is $ 1390 million below that enacted for 1960.

Synset Examples

obligational authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligatory - required by a legal, moral, or other rule; compulsory

Etymology

From 'obligation' + '-al', following standard English adjective formation.
