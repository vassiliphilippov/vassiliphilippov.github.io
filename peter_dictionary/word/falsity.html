falsity

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæl.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being false; untruth or lack of conformity to fact or reality.

Examples

When Peter B. Kyne (Pride of Palomar, 43) informed us in 1921 that we had an instinctive dislike for the Japanese, did the heated debates of the Californians settle the truth or falsity of the proposition?

Synset Examples

argument could not determine its truth or falsity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true
  • Synonym: falsehood - a false statement; the act of lying

Etymology

From Latin falsitas, from falsus (false).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔl.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
A statement that is untrue; the quality of being false.

Examples

Will argument and debate decide its truth or falsity?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsehood - a lie or a false statement
  • Antonym: truth - statement or quality of being true

Etymology

From Latin 'falsitas', from 'falsus' (false).
