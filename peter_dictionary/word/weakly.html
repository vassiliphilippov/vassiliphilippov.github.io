weakly

1. ADV

/ ˈwiːkli / (AmE), / ˈwiːkli / (BrE)
In a weak or feeble manner; with little strength, force, or conviction.

Examples

The man wheezed weakly, his fetid breath beating softly against Watson's neck.

Synset Examples

weakly agreed to a compromise
wheezed weakly
he was weakly attracted to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feebly - in a feeble or weak manner
  • Antonym: strongly - in a strong or forceful manner

Etymology

Formed from 'weak' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix.
