incubus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbəs / (BrE)
A male demon or evil spirit in folklore, believed to lie on sleeping persons and to have sexual intercourse with sleeping women.

Examples

The grocery the youth worked for soon tired of his depressing effect on customers, most of whom were sufficiently neurotic without the threat of incubi, and let him go.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: succubus - female demon believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping men

Etymology

From Late Latin incubus (nightmare, evil spirit), from Latin incubare (to lie upon).
