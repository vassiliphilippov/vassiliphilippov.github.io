presentment

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɛntmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntmənt / (BrE)
(Law) A formal statement or report made by a grand jury declaring that a crime has been committed, usually made on the jury's own initiative rather than in response to a prosecutor's request.

Examples

The jury further said in term end presentments that the City Executive Committee, which had over-all charge of the election, "deserves the praise and thanks of the City of Atlanta "for the manner in which the election was conducted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indictment - formal charge or accusation of a serious crime

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *presentement*, from présenter (to present).
