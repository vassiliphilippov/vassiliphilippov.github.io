spell

1. VERB

/ spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
to say, write, or name the letters of a word in the correct order

Examples

Yes, we know, they 're professionals, men paid to play, and they should n't care how they 're handled, just as long as their names are spelled correctly on the first and fifteenth of each month.
Also you can spell, without consulting a dictionary for every other word.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misspell - to spell incorrectly

Etymology

Old English *spellian* (to tell, relate) and later influenced by Middle English *spellen* (to read letter by letter).

2. NOUN

/ spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
A state of enchantment or trance as if caused by magic or supernatural power; a condition in which a person feels captivated or under the influence of something mysterious.

Examples

The audience leaves the play under a spell, It is the kind of spell which the exposure to spirit in its living active manifestation always evokes.

Etymology

3. VERB

/ spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
(of an action, event, or situation) to mean or be a sign of (usually something unwelcome or significant); to signify or indicate.

Examples

Polyphosphates gave renewed life to soap products at a time when surfactants were a threat though expensive, and these same polyphosphates spelled the decline of soap usage when the synergism between polyphosphates and synthetic detergent actives was recognized and exploited.
Smith's first workout with stresses, pitches, and junctures was based on mother, which spells, in our culture, a good deal more than bread alone.

Synset Examples

I'm afraid this spells trouble!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to be a sign that something exists or is about to happen
  • Synonym: portend - to be a warning or indication of (something, especially something momentous or calamitous)

4. NOUN

/ spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
a short period of time spent doing a particular activity, especially work, before being relieved by someone else

Examples

Would you permit Juanita to walk about the grounds with me for a short spell, Mrs. Lattimer "?

Synset Examples

it's my go
a spell of work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a set period of work or duty

Etymology

Old English *spell* (story, discourse), but this sense may derive from Old Norse *spöl* (a period or stretch of time).

5. VERB

/ spɛl / (AmE), / spɛl / (BrE)
to write or say the letters of a word in the correct order

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
