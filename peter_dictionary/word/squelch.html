squelch

1. VERB

/ skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to suppress or put an end to something forcefully; to crush or silence completely

Examples

Fights sprang up and were quickly squelched.

Synset Examples

squelch any sign of dissent
quench a rebellion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quash - to put an end to forcibly; suppress
  • Synonym: crush - to suppress or subdue completely
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to (opposite sense)

Etymology

Of imitative origin, echoing the sound produced by something being squashed or crushed.
