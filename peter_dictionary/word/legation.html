legation

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a diplomatic mission, especially one lower in rank than an embassy, or the official residence or office of a diplomatic representative (legate)

Examples

The legation was generously set back from the building line; if the angle of fire were too great the jutting buildings on either side would interfere.
From this side he could see farther into the legation's third story window, but he saw no faces; the room's occupants were still seated or they had been called into the hallway by an alarmed police captain.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: embassy - the official residence or office of an ambassador; a diplomatic mission of the highest rank

Etymology

From Latin 'legatio' (the act of sending an ambassador or deputy), from 'legare' (to delegate).
