boomerang

1. VERB

/ ˈbuːmərˌæŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːməræŋ / (BrE)
(of an action, remark, or situation) to return to its origin, often with negative or unforeseen consequences; to rebound on the originator.

Examples

Literal flatness now tends to assert itself as the main event of the picture, and the device boomerangs: the illusion of depth is rendered even more precarious than before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - to bounce back or return to the original place or condition

Etymology

From the noun 'boomerang', originally from Dharug (an Australian Aboriginal language).
