potato

1. NOUN

/ pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A round or oval edible tuber with brown, yellow, or red skin and white or yellow flesh, cultivated as a staple food.

Examples

He'’s small potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - a starchy tuber of a climbing plant, similar to a potato

Etymology

From Spanish 'patata', from Taíno 'batata' (sweet potato).
