detached

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtætʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃt / (BrE)
Not influenced by emotional involvement; objective or aloof in attitude or feeling.

Examples

Again among those jubilantly reunited bunkmates, I was shy with Jessie and acted as I had during those early Saturday mornings when we all seemed to be playing for effect, to be detached and unconcerned with the girls who were properly our dates but about whom, later, in the privacy of our bunks, we would think in terms of the most elaborate romance.
For better or worse we cannot regard "imitation "in the arts in the simple mode of classical rationalism or detached realism.
Presupposed in Plato's system is a doctrine of levels of insight, in which a certain kind of detached understanding is alone capable of penetrating to the most sublime wisdom.
This rather detached attitude toward life's encumbrances has seemed to be the dominant trait in Mercer's personality ever since.

Synset Examples

adopted a degage pose on the arm of the easy chair

an uninvolved bystander

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant
  • Synonym: uninvolved - not taking part or not connected emotionally
  • Antonym: involved - emotionally engaged or connected
  • Antonym: attached - emotionally connected or invested

Etymology

From Old French *detacher* (to detach), from des- (dis-) + attacher (to attach).
