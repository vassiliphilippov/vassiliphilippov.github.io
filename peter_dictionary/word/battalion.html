battalion

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtæljən / (AmE), / bəˈtæliən / (BrE)
A large military unit typically consisting of several companies and forming part of a regiment or brigade.

Examples

For example, it was a battalion of the 7 th Cavalry under Colonel George Armstrong Custer that had been wiped out at the Battle of The Little Big Horn.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: regiment - a larger military unit made up of battalions
  • Degree Lower: company - a smaller military unit, part of a battalion

Etymology

From French 'bataillon', from Italian 'battaglione', from 'battaglia' meaning 'battle'.
