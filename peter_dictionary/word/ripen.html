ripen

1. VERB

/ ˈraɪpən / (AmE), / ˈraɪpən / (BrE)
to make something fully mature or ready to eat or use; to cause to reach full development

Examples

The flavor of Baltimore's Florida Grapefruit League news ripened considerably late today when the Orioles were advised that Ron Hansen has fulfilled his obligations under the Army's military training program and is ready for belated spring training.

Synset Examples

The sun ripens the fruit
Age matures a good wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mature - to bring to full development

Etymology

From Middle English ripen, from ripe + -en (verb forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈraɪpən / (AmE), / ˈraɪpən / (BrE)
(of fruit, crops, etc.) to become or make ripe; to reach full maturity, especially so as to be ready to eat or use.

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.

Synset Examples

The plums ripen in July

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mature - to become fully developed or fully grown
  • Antonym: decay - to rot or decompose as ripeness is lost

Etymology

From Middle English ripen, from ripe (adjective) + -en (verb-forming suffix).
