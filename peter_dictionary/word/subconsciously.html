subconsciously

1. ADV

/ səbˈkɑn.ʃəs.li / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒn.ʃəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is influenced by or originates from the subconscious mind, without the person being fully aware.

Examples

A "mental image "subconsciously impressing us from beneath its language symbols in wakeful thought, or consciously in light sleep, is actually not an image at all but is comprised of realities, viewed not in the concurrent sensory stream, but within the depths of the fourth dimension.

Synset Examples

the image came to him subconsciously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consciously - in a way that is fully aware or intentional

Etymology

Formed from 'subconscious' (from Latin sub- ‘below’ + conscious) + adverbial suffix -ly.
