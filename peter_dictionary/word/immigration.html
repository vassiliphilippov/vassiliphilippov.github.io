immigration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
The action of coming to live permanently in a foreign country.

Examples

The state universities of Maine, New Hampshire, And Vermont are older and more "respectable "; they had less immigration to contend with.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's country to live in another
  • Synonym: migration - movement from one place to another, often for settlement

Etymology

From Latin 'immigratio', from 'immigrare' (to go into, to immigrate).
