flog

1. VERB

/ flɑg / (AmE), / flɒg / (BrE)
to beat someone severely with a whip or rod as a punishment or for discipline

Examples

Finally men arrested Him, gave Him a mock trial, flogged Him, nailed Him on a cross and hung Him between earth and heaven; and God allowed it.

Synset Examples

The teacher often flogged the students
The children were severely trounced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a whip as a form of punishment
  • Synonym: birch - to beat with a bundle of twigs as corporal punishment
  • Degree Lower: spank - to strike on the buttocks, usually lightly, as a punishment
  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly

Etymology

Probably from Latin *flagellare* (to whip) via late 18th-century slang.
