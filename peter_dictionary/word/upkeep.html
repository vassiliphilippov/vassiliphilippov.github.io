upkeep

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌkip / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkiːp / (BrE)
The process or cost of keeping something in good condition through regular maintenance.

Examples

The rest of Black Bottom was a rabbit warren of homes in every condition of neglect, disrepair and careful upkeep.
You can keep your machinery investment down by buying good secondhand machinery, by sharing the cost and upkeep of machinery with a neighbor, and by hiring someone with machinery to do certain jobs.

Synset Examples

he wrote the manual on car care

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maintenance - action of keeping something in good condition
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to care for something

Etymology

Formed from 'up' (sense of 'continuing') and 'keep' (to maintain); first used in 19th century.
