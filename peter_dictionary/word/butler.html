butler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtlə / (BrE)
The chief male servant in a large household, responsible for managing the household's wines, serving meals, and supervising other staff.

Examples

The spaciousness of the Tudor cooking areas, for example, will provide needed space for the extra television sets required by modern butlers, cooks and maids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housekeeper - a person, usually a woman, employed to manage a household
  • Degree Lower: maid - a female domestic servant

Etymology

From Old French *bouteillier* (cup-bearer, bottle-bearer), from *bouteille* (bottle).
