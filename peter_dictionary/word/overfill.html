overfill

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈfɪl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɪl / (BrE)
to fill something beyond its capacity, so that it cannot contain any more

Examples

Do not overfill the chamber.

Synset Examples

overfill the baskets

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fill - to make something full; to put something into a container until it is full
  • Degree Higher: overflow - to spill over the edge because there is too much liquid or material

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'fill'.
