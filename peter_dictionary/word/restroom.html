restroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈrɛstruːm / (BrE)
A room containing one or more toilets, typically in a public place, for use by the public.

Examples

When improvements are recommended in working conditions - such as lighting, rest rooms, eating facilities, air-conditioning - do you try to set a measure of their effectiveness on productivity?
Rest room break, boss?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavatory - a room or compartment with a toilet and washbasin; a synonym used in British English
  • Synonym: toilet - a fixture or room for urination and defecation; general synonym worldwide
  • Synonym: bathroom - a room containing a toilet and usually a sink and sometimes a bathtub or shower (esp. American English)
  • Synonym: public convenience - public toilet (British English)

Etymology

From 'rest' + 'room', originally a euphemism for a place to rest; became associated with public toilets in American English.
