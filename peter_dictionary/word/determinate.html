determinate

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɜrmɪnət / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnət / (BrE)
precisely determined, limited, or defined; fixed by rule or by a specific and constant cause

Examples

States were free to enact, within broad though (perhaps) determinate limits, their own rules as to the application of foreign law by their courts, to vary the law merchant, and to enact legislation with regard to many claims arising on the high seas.

Synset Examples

a determinate distance
a determinate number
determinate variations in animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indeterminate - not exactly determined or established; not precise or fixed

Etymology

From Latin determinatus, past participle of determinare (to limit, fix).
