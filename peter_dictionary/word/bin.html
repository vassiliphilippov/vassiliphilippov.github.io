bin

1. NOUN

/ bɪn / (AmE), / bɪn / (BrE)
A container, often with a lid, used for storing items such as waste, materials, or provisions.

Examples

Supplies of sheet metal were neatly stacked in bins.
A refuse bin at the Dumont turned up a florist's box - a very long box for very long stemmed flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: container - an object that can hold or store things
  • Synonym: basket - a container made of interwoven materials

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French 'bin' (basket, bin), of Celtic origin.
