Wilsonian

1. ADJ

/ wɪlˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / wɪlˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
Relating to Woodrow Wilson or his principles, policies, or era, especially his advocacy of self-determination and internationalism.

Examples

Never mind whether the Kikiyus and the Bantus enjoyed Wilsonian self-determination: the point is that in the struggle for the world that vast land mass was under the domination and influence of the West.

Etymology

From the name of Woodrow Wilson (1856–1924), 28th President of the United States, plus the suffix -ian.
