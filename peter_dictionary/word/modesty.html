modesty

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdəsti / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəsti / (BrE)
The quality of not being proud or vain about your abilities or achievements; freedom from conceit.

Examples

No, she would not pretend modesty, but neither must she be crudely bold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humility - the quality of having a low view of one's importance
  • Antonym: vanity - excessive pride in one's appearance or achievements

Etymology

From Latin 'modestia' (moderation, modesty), from 'modestus' (moderate, restrained).
