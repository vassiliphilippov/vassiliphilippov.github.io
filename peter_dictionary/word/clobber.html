clobber

1. VERB

/ ˈklɑːbər / (AmE), / ˈklɒbə / (BrE)
to strike someone or something violently and repeatedly, often with great force

Examples

"What I always say is what if somebody clobbers you in a little car like that?
What if one or more of them turn irrational or suddenly, coolly, decide to clobber the Russians?

Synset Examples

She clobbered the man who tried to attack her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike someone or something with force
  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in British English in the mid-20th century.
