conduction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
The process by which heat, electricity, or sound is transmitted through a material without the material itself moving.

Examples

Heat transfer by molecular conduction as well as by radiation from the arc column.
In this design the anode holder is water cooled and the heat losses by conduction from the anode were determined by measuring the temperature rise of the coolant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convection - the transfer of heat by the movement of a fluid
  • Antonym: radiation - the emission of energy as electromagnetic waves

Etymology

From Latin conductio (a bringing together), from conducere (to lead together).
