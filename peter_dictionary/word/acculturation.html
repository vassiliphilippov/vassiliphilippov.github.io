acculturation

1. NOUN

/ əˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkʌltʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person or group adopts the cultural traits or social patterns of another group, typically by adapting to the surrounding culture

Examples

And acculturation into the world at large is likely to occur for the Brooklyn College student after college rather than during the four school years.

Synset Examples

the socialization of children to the norms of their culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilation - the process of fully absorbing and integrating into a different culture

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'cultura' (cultivation).
