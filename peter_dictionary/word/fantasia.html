fantasia

1. NOUN

/ fænˈteɪʒə / (AmE), / fænˈteɪzɪə / (BrE)
a musical composition with a free form, often incorporating several familiar themes, allowing for improvisation and imaginative variation

Examples

The ballet work is on the nose, especially in the opening number by "The New Ashmolean Marching Society and Students' Conservatory Band ", along with a fiery and sultry Brazilian fantasia later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - a similar type of musical composition with an imaginative, improvisatory style
  • Antonym: fugue - a structured contrapuntal musical composition

Etymology

From Italian 'fantasia' (imagination), from Greek 'phantasia' (appearance, imagination).
