/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) having a kind, gentle, or charming nature; endearing or lovable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kind - having a friendly, generous, or considerate nature
  • Synonym: gentle - mild and kind in temperament or behavior
  • Antonym: mean - unkind or spiteful

Etymology

Old English *swēte* (pleasant, agreeable), related to Proto-Germanic *swōtja*.

2. ADJ

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
(of a smell) having a pleasant, natural fragrance; fragrant; aromatic.

Examples

It hung in all her day clothes, sweet and strong; sometimes when he was pressing, Winston raised her dresses to his face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Synonym: aromatic - having a noticeable and pleasant smell
  • Synonym: scented - having a fragrance, especially an added one
  • Synonym: odorous - having a smell (often used for strong or distinctive smells, not always pleasant)
  • Antonym: malodorous - having an unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Old English swēte, meaning 'pleasing to the senses.'
