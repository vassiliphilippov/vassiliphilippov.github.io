slapstick

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæp.stɪk / (AmE), / ˈslæp.stɪk / (BrE)
a type of boisterous comedy characterized by exaggerated physical actions, practical jokes, and humorous chases or collisions

Examples

The cowboy films, the cops and robbers films, and the slapstick comedy films culminating in an insane chase are not only catering to what critics may assume to be a vulgar taste for violence; these films and these sequences are also seeking out - instinctively or by design - the peculiarly cinematic elements of narrative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farce - comedy that aims at entertaining the audience through situations that are highly exaggerated and improbable

Etymology

From a device used in physical comedy: a pair of flexible wooden slats that slap together to produce a loud sound, from Italian 'batacchio' (slapstick).
