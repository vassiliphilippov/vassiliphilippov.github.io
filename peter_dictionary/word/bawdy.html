bawdy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔdi / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdi / (BrE)
(of language, jokes, or songs) humorously indecent or vulgar, especially in a way that is meant to be funny or playful; often referring to sexual matters.

Examples

It was not even in writing Latin epigrams, sometimes bawdy ones, or in translating Lucian from Greek into Latin or in defending the study of Greek against the attack of conservative academics, or in attacking the conservative theologians who opposed Erasmus's philological study of the New Testament.

Synset Examples

bawdy songs
off-color jokes
ribald language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribald - referring to coarse or indecent humor
  • Synonym: lewd - crude and offensive in a sexual way
  • Antonym: clean - not referring to sexual or indecent matters

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'baudi', of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old French 'bald' (bold, audacious).
