citron

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrən / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrən / (BrE)
A large, lemon-like citrus fruit with a thick, aromatic rind, often used in preserves or candied.

Examples

Still, he had liked the way she had looked, in a fresh, neat cotton dress - citron yellow, if he remembered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow, oval citrus fruit with acidic juice
  • Degree Lower: lime - a small, green citrus fruit

Etymology

From French 'citron', from Italian 'citro', from Latin 'citrus' (citron tree).
