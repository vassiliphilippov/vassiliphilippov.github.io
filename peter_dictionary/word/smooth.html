smooth

1. ADJ

/ smuð / (AmE), / smuːð / (BrE)
Having a surface with no roughness, bumps, or irregularities; even and flat to the touch or sight.

Examples

"Everything went real smooth ", the sheriff said.
Spectators in white crush textures dip toe and heel in smooth black, navy and taffy tan.
I dig it, rake it smooth, sow the seeds and wet them down with a fog spray.
One of these is the "dissolve ", which makes possible a visually smooth transition from scene to scene.
Not always, though, does the development of a bumblebee colony take place in the smooth fashion we have just described.

Synset Examples

smooth skin
a smooth tabletop
smooth fabric
a smooth road
water as smooth as a mirror

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or irregular surface

Etymology

From Old English smōð (smooth), related to Old Norse smjōðr.

2. ADJ

/ smuð / (AmE), / smuːð / (BrE)
moving or operating with ease and without abruptness, interruptions, or roughness.

Examples
