orphan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
A child whose parents are both dead.

Examples

one asked for help for the blind, another help for orphans, and the third for relief for the war refugees.
Make that baby an orphan before he's born?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widow - a woman whose spouse has died
  • Synonym: orphaned - having become an orphan; losing both parents

Etymology

From Late Latin orphanus, from Greek orphanos (bereaved, orphaned).
