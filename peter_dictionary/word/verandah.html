verandah

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrændə / (AmE), / vəˈrændə / (BrE)
A roofed platform along the outside of a house, typically level with the ground floor and often partly enclosed by a railing or decorative screen.

Examples

He crossed the tracks to Delaware House, where ladies in gay dresses and men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porch - a covered shelter projecting in front of the entrance of a building

Etymology

From Hindi *varaṇḍā*, probably via Portuguese *varanda* (railing, balustrade).
