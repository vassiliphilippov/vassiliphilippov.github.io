Gypsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪp.si / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪp.si / (BrE)
(Offensive) A member of a traditionally itinerant ethnic group of people, mainly living in Europe and North America, with South Asian origins, who speak Romany; also called Roma or Romani. The term 'Gypsy' is now considered offensive by many people.

Examples

You'’re no gypsy, Fredo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roma - the Romani people
  • Synonym: Romani - a member of the Romani people

Etymology

Probably from Egyptian, based on the mistaken belief that the people came from Egypt.
