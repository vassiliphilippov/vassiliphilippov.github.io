pageant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædʒənt / (AmE), / ˈpadʒənt / (BrE)
a public entertainment consisting of a procession of people in elaborate costumes, often representing historical events or scenes

Examples

They want to own a junior-grade castle, or a manor house, or some modest little place where Shakespeare might once have staged a pageant for Great Elizabeth and all her bearded courtiers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, especially one celebrating a special day or event

Etymology

From Middle English 'pagyn', from Latin 'pagina' (a page, scene), later influenced by sense development related to tableaux and spectacle.
