righteousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪtʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪtʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being morally right or justifiable; adherence to moral principles.

Examples

We will have righteousness "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong

Etymology

From Old English 'rihtwīsnes' (state of being just or righteous), from 'riht' (right) + 'wīs' (wise).
