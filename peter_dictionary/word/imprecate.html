imprecate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmprɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmprɪˌkeɪt / (BrE)
To wish harm or invoke evil upon someone or something, especially through words or prayers.

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to invoke supernatural harm; to wish evil upon

Etymology

From Latin 'imprecari' meaning 'to invoke, to pray for (evil)'.
