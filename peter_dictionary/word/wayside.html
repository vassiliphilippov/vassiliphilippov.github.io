wayside

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
the edge or border of a road, path, or highway

Examples

Shy, slender-waisted girls at the loom in harmony with the frangipani by the wayside.

Synset Examples

flowers along the wayside

Etymology

From Middle English way (road) + side (edge or border).
