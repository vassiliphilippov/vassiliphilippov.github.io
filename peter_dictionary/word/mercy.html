mercy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)
A kind or compassionate feeling that leads one to show forgiveness or pity rather than harm or punishment.

Examples

"O Blessed Virgin Mary, Mother of God and our most gentle queen and mother, look down in mercy upon England, thy "dowry ", and upon us all who greatly hope and trust in thee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings or misfortunes of others
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain and suffering

Etymology

From Old French 'merci' (pity, reward, thanks), from Latin 'merces' (reward, wages, favor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)
The act of showing compassion or forgiveness toward someone, especially an offender, by a person or authority in a position to punish.

Examples

We are desperately in the need of such invention, for man is still very much at the mercy of man.
Concerning the sentence, Foss wrote, "If it be possible that mercy shall override vengeance and that John Brown's sentence shall be commuted to imprisonment, it would be well - well for the country and for Virginia ".
For His mercy and His guidance to spare you from evil and eternal punishment in the Lake of Fire ".
He could throw himself on the mercy of the Police Department.

Synset Examples

he threw himself on the mercy of the court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clemency - merciful or lenient treatment, especially by a person in authority
  • Synonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected
  • Antonym: harshness - the quality of being cruel or severe, especially in punishing

Etymology

From Old French 'merci', from Latin 'mercedem' (reward, favor, pity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːsi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsi / (BrE)
A disposition or quality of being kind and forgiving towards someone, especially when one has the power to punish or harm.

Examples

Generally, however, in such marriages as those cited, the husband is at his wife's mercy.

Synset Examples

in those days a wife had to depend on the mercifulness of her husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - a feeling of deep sympathy and concern for others' suffering
  • Synonym: clemency - merciful or lenient treatment
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference or causing suffering

Etymology

From Old French *merci* (pity, thanks), from Latin *mercedem* (reward, wages, favor, pity).
