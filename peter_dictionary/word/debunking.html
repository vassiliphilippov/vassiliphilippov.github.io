debunking

1. NOUN

/ diˈbʌŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈbʌŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of exposing the falseness or pretensions of a belief, idea, or person

Examples

The scientific debunking of the spirit world has been in a way too successful and too thorough.

Synset Examples

the debunking of religion has been too successful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debunk - to expose the falseness or hollowness of a myth, idea, or belief

Etymology

From the verb 'debunk', which derives from 'bunk' (nonsense), itself popularized from 'bunkum', originally from Buncombe County, North Carolina.
