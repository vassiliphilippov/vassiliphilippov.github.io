ethanol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθ.əˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.ənɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile liquid alcohol (C2H5OH) that is the intoxicating agent in alcoholic drinks and is also used as a solvent, in fuel, and in industry

Examples

These include low-temperature ethanol (Cohn) fractionation, electrophoresis, ultracentrifugation and column chromatography on ion exchange celluloses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethyl alcohol - alternative name for ethanol
  • Synonym: alcohol - general term for organic compounds including ethanol

Etymology

From ethane + -ol (alcohol suffix), following chemical naming conventions.
