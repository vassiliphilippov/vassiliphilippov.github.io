adore

1. VERB

/ əˈdɔɹ / (AmE), / əˈdɔː / (BrE)
To love someone or something very much; to feel deep affection and admiration for.

Examples

Yet I adored this man, Letch Feeley, why, I cannot say.

Synset Examples

he just adored his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: love - to feel strong affection or deep attachment
  • Degree Higher: worship - to show great respect, admiration, or devotion to
  • Degree Lower: like - to find agreeable or enjoyable, have mild affection for

Etymology

From Latin 'adorare' (to worship, to adore), from 'ad-' (to) + 'orare' (to speak, to pray).
