bend

1. VERB

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
to form a curve; to shape or force something so that it is no longer straight

Examples

When the front knee is straight and locked, allow it to bend again until you feel the bar come lightly into contact with the sides of the Power Stands.
As strength improves start in a standing position with legs wide apart and upper body bent forward.
At the peak of the arch, tip the head back and bend the knees in an effort to touch toes to head.
Watson bent awkwardly and lifted the man to his feet.
When he was bent over behind the wheel of the station wagon, feeling in his trouser cuffs for the ignition key which he had dropped a moment before, she came out of the house with an enormous Rumanian shawl over her head, which she had bought in that country during one of their trips abroad, and handed him a clean handkerchief through the window.

Synset Examples

The stick does not bend

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straighten - make or become straight

Etymology

Old English 'bendan' (to bend a bow), from Proto-Germanic 'bandijan'.

2. VERB

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bend / (BrE)
to change direction by curving or angling, especially about a fixed point or along a line; to lean or incline the body or part of it

Examples

Scotty murmured, "No, thanks ", so softly his father had to bend his gaunt height across the table and turn a round brown ear to him.
Rachel had to bend toward Scotty and ask him to repeat.
The doctor had to bend close to hear; his delicate hand, as veined as a moth's wing, rested absently on Scotty's chest.
Rachel came close to the bed, bent as if she would kiss him, then moved away.
She made him sad some days, and he was never sure why; it was something to do with her back, the thinness of it, and the quick, jerky way she bent.

Synset Examples

The road bends

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

Old English 'bendan' (to bend, bow, confine); related to German 'biegen' (to bend).

3. NOUN

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
a movement or shape in which something curves or changes direction; a curved part or section

Examples

He sat for a while with his hands on his knees, watching the bend of her back as she gathered up her things - a comb, a bottle of aspirin - to take upstairs and pack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - a smoothly flowing, continuous line or surface that deviates from straightness

4. VERB

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
to shape something that is flexible or pliable so that it becomes curved or so that one part is at an angle to another

Examples

His eye had fastened on her leg; bending, he touched her knee.
Use well-wedged clay, free of air bubbles and pliable enough to bend without cracking.
This will prevent the clay from twisting or bending, causing warping when fired.
Bend handle; press scored handle ends firmly in place using dowel to reinforce container while pressing; use slip to join.

Synset Examples

bend the rod
twist the dough into a braid
the strong man could turn an iron bar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straighten - to make something straight
  • Synonym: flex - to bend or move something, especially a limb or muscle, by contraction

Etymology

Old English bendan (to bend, confine with a string or band).

5. NOUN

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
A curved section or segment of something such as a road, river, or railway line.

Examples

The first part of the road was steep, but it leveled off after the second bend and curled gradually into the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - something that bends or deviates from a straight line, especially a rounded change in direction

6. VERB

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
to change direction from a straight line or to curve instead of continuing straight

Examples

But it is the wooden sculpture from Bali, the one representing two men with their heads bent backward and their bodies interlaced by a fish, that I particularly call to your attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straighten - make or become straight
  • Synonym: curve - to form or cause to form a curve

Etymology

Old English bendan (to curve, bow), from Proto-Germanic *bandijaną.

7. VERB

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
to move your body so that your back is not straight, usually by leaning forward from the waist

Examples

He paused on the landing to steady his breathing and then bent to examine the single door by the light of the weak bulb overhead.
The way his red rubber lips were stretched across his pearly little teeth I though he was only having a little joke, but, no, he wanted me to bend down from the roar of wind so he could roar something into my ear.
He bent down, a black cranelike figure, and put his mouth to the ground.

Synset Examples

he crouched down
She bowed before the Queen
The young man stooped to pick up the girl's purse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crouch - to lower the body by bending the knees and leaning forward
  • Synonym: stoop - to bend one's body forward and down

8. NOUN

/ bɛnd / (AmE), / bɛnd / (BrE)
A curved part of something, especially a road or river, where it changes direction.

Examples

They watched the tree until it twisted sharply on a bend.

Synset Examples

a bend in the road
a crook in the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - a smoothly flowing, continuous line or surface that deviates from straightness
  • Antonym: straight - something not curved or bent; without bends
