picnic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
an occasion when people eat a meal outdoors, especially as part of a social gathering or excursion

Examples

A body of water is usually the center of interest at parks which attract the greatest picnic and camping use.
One day he followed the Irish Jasper Greens, the town band, to a picnic and spent the entire day listening, while his family spent the day looking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - outdoor meal where food is cooked over a fire or grill

Etymology

From French pique-nique (a social gathering where each guest brings a share of the food), dating from the 18th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
to eat a meal outdoors, especially as part of a social occasion

Examples

They picnicked by the pool, then lay back on the grass and looked at stars.

Synset Examples

We picnicked near the lake on this gorgeous Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dine - to eat dinner or the main meal of the day

Etymology

Derived from French pique-nique, of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
(informal, figurative) An undertaking or task that is very easy or pleasant to do, often used in negative expressions to indicate something difficult (e.g. 'no picnic').

Examples

Marketing in the new decade will be no picnic - for the sixties will present possibly the most intense competitive activity that you have experienced in the last 20 - 25 yr..

Synset Examples

marketing this product will be no picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinch - something that is very easy to do
  • Synonym: breeze - something easily accomplished
  • Synonym: walkover - something easy to achieve or win
  • Antonym: challenge - a task that is difficult or demanding

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɪk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.nɪk / (BrE)
An informal meal eaten outdoors, often on an excursion or in the countryside.

Examples

We went there for a picnic and made love under that oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - A meal or gathering at which meat and other foods are cooked outdoors over an open fire or grill

Etymology

From French 'pique-nique' (originally meaning a social gathering with each guest contributing food), likely formed from 'piquer' (to pick) and 'nique' (a small thing).
