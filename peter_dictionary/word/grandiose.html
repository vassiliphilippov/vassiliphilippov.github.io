grandiose

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrændiˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡrændiəʊs / (BrE)
Impressive or magnificent in appearance or style, especially in an exaggerated or pretentious way; excessively grand or ambitious.

Examples

There was the day Uncle Izaak had, in an unexpected grandiose gesture, handed over the pretty sloop to Abel for keeps, on condition that he never fail to let his brother accompany him whenever the younger boy wished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - trying to impress by displaying greater importance or worth than is actually possessed
  • Antonym: modest - not showing excessive pride or extravagance in appearance or behavior

Etymology

From French grandiose, from Italian grandioso, from grande (great).
