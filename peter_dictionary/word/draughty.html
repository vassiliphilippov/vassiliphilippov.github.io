draughty

1. ADJ

/ ˈdræfti / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːfti / (BrE)
(of a room or building) uncomfortable because cold air is entering it through gaps.

Examples

He ate what he felt like, slept as much or as little as he pleased, and moved about the draughty rooms of the house, when he was not in bed, with slow dubious steps, like an elderly tourist in a cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drafty - (of a building or room) letting in cold air; not airtight (American spelling)

Etymology

From Middle English *draught*, meaning a current of air.
