generosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnəˈrɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəˈrɒsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being kind and willing to give money, help, or time freely to others

Examples

The same month that Alastor was published, Murray sold twenty thousand copies of The Siege of Corinth, a slovenly bit of Byronism that even Shelley's generosity rebelled at.
Why do I deserve this generosity?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - the voluntary giving of help, typically money, to those in need
  • Antonym: stinginess - unwillingness to give or spend; meanness

Etymology

From Latin generositas (nobleness, generosity), from generosus (noble in birth).
