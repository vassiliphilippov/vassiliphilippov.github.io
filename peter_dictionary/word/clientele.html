clientele

1. NOUN

/ klaɪənˈtɛl / (AmE), / klɪˈɑːntɛl / (BrE)
all the customers or clients of a business or professional person considered as a group.

Examples

Mr. Phillips took a razor to Gonzalez, Prop., but left the promise that Spanish would be understood because he thought it meant that Spanish clientele would be welcome.

Synset Examples

they have an upper class clientele

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customers - people who buy goods or services from a shop or business
  • Synonym: patrons - regular customers of a shop, restaurant, or service

Etymology

From French 'clientèle', from Latin 'clientela' (clientship, body of clients).
