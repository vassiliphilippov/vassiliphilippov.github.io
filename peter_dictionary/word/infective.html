infective

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of an agent or substance) able to cause infection or disease.

Examples

Milk production may be increased by the anti infective properties of this drug.
Milk production may be increased by the anti infective properties of this drug.

Synset Examples

infective agents
pathogenic bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - able to transmit disease or infection
  • Antonym: noninfective - not able to cause infection or disease

Etymology

From Latin infectivus (able to infect), from inficere (to stain, taint, infect).
