beguile

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡaɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪl / (BrE)
to influence or deceive someone by slyness or cunning

Examples

He was, in the words of Irenaeus, "beguiled by another under the pretext of immortality ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Degree Lower: enchant - to attract and hold the attention by charm or delight
  • Synonym: dupe - to trick or cheat someone

Etymology

From Middle English beguile, from be- (intensive prefix) + guile (deceit)

2. VERB

/ bɪˈɡaɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪl / (BrE)
To charm or attract someone in a way that is often enchanting or captivating.

Examples

Returning once again to the Schnabel reissue, I am beguiled anew by the magnificence of this pianist's musical penetration.

Synset Examples

She captured all the men's hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captivate - to attract and hold the interest of someone
  • Synonym: enchant - to fill someone with great delight or charm
  • Antonym: repel - to drive back or discourage

Etymology

From Middle English begilen, from be- (about, around) + Old French guiler (to deceive).
