salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
A small amphibian resembling a lizard, typically living on land but returning to water to breed.

Examples

Done in the modern style of a "confession ", Fink tells in exquisite detail how he came to know, and, more important, love his mother's pet salamander, Alicia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a type of small, brightly colored salamander

Etymology

From Greek *salamandra*.
