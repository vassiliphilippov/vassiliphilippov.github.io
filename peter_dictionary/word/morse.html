Morse

1. NOUN

/ mɔrs / (AmE), / mɔːs / (BrE)
A system of telegraph communication in which letters and numbers are represented by sequences of short and long signals, known as dots and dashes.

Examples

The old Morse system was replaced locally by the Simplex modern automatic method in 1929, when Ellamae Heckman (Wilcox) was manager of the Western Union office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Morse code - the code in which letters and numbers are transmitted as dots and dashes

Etymology

Named after Samuel F. B. Morse (1791–1872), the American inventor.
