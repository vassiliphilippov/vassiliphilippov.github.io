substantive

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌb.stən.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.stən.tɪv / (BrE)
important, meaningful, or considerable; having a firm basis in reality and not imagined or illusory.

Examples

No fold could be accidental, each turn of the drapery had to serve organically, to cover the Madonna's slender legs and feet so that they would give substantive support to Christ's body, to intensify her inner turmoil.

Synset Examples

substantial equivalents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
  • Antonym: insubstantial - lacking strength or solidity; not real or important

Etymology

From Latin 'substantivus' (serving as a substance or essential), from 'substantia' (substance).
