berth

1. NOUN

/ bɝːθ / (AmE), / bɜːθ / (BrE)
a job or position in an organization, especially in a team or competition

Examples

If you are thinking of giving him a berth, be sure to make it a wide one ".
Carroll was sharp and military, but he was up against tough competition for that RA berth, and he wanted to play it cool.

Synset Examples

he occupied a post in the treasury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - a job or position in an organization
  • Synonym: position - a job or role within an organization

Etymology

Originally from the nautical term meaning a place assigned to a ship or sailor; extended to mean a position or job.
