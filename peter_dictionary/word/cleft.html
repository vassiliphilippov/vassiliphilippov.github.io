cleft

1. NOUN

/ klɛft / (AmE), / klɛft / (BrE)
A narrow split or indentation, especially in a surface, such as a fissure in the body (e.g. palate or chin) or in another object.

Examples

The intima of the larger coronary arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue containing fusiform clefts and mononuclear cells.
The mouth was thin lipped and wide, the long cleft in the upper lip like a slide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or crack
  • Synonym: crack - a narrow opening, break, or split

Etymology

From Old English *cleft* (past participle of *cleofan* 'to split').
