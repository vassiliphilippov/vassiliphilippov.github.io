depersonalize

1. VERB

/ diˈpɜrsənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈpɜːsənəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something impersonal or treat as an object, stripping away individuality or personality.

Examples

As the dancer is depersonalized, his accouterments are animized, and the combined elements give birth to a new being.

Synset Examples

Will computers depersonalize human interactions?
Pornography objectifies women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectify - to treat as an object, especially in a way that strips individuality

Etymology

From de- (removal) + personalize, formed on the model of 'personalize'.
