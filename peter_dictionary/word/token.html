token

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
An individual occurrence of a symbol, word, or element, especially in language, computing, or mathematics.

Examples

While many tokens point forward, the main achievements stand as a culmination of the simple patterns of the Dark ages.
In this third year at the university, Hans, in 1797, was awarded the first important token of recognition, a gold medal for his essay on "Limits of Poetry and Prose ".

Synset Examples

the word`error' contains three tokens of `r'

Related Terms

  • Antonym: type - a class or category to which an instance (token) belongs

Etymology

From Old English 'tacen', meaning sign or symbol.
