comportment

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɔrtmənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɔːtmənt / (BrE)
the way in which a person behaves, especially in a dignified or formal manner

Examples

Not that he gave a damn what the grapevine sent out about Killpath's little speech on the comportment of platoon commanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearing - the way one carries or conducts oneself
  • Synonym: demeanor - outward behavior or bearing

Etymology

From French *comportement*, from comporter (to behave), from Latin *comportare* (to bring together).
