fidelity

1. NOUN

/ fəˈdɛlɪti / (AmE), / fɪˈdɛlɪti / (BrE)
the degree of accuracy with which a system, such as one for sound or image reproduction, reproduces its input signal

Examples

This absence of peaky highs and beefed-up bass not only produces greater fidelity, but it eliminates listener fatigue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accuracy - the quality or state of being correct or precise
  • Synonym: high fidelity - the reproduction of sound with little distortion

Etymology

From Latin fidelitas (faithfulness), from fidelis (faithful). The technical use developed in electronic contexts in the 20th century.
