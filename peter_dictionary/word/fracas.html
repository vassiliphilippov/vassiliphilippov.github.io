fracas

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪkəs, frəˈkɑː / (AmE), / ˈfrækɑː, ˈfrækəs / (BrE)
A noisy argument, fight, or disturbance involving several people.

Examples

The only performance which was too soft for me was that of Yvette Mimieux, but since someone had to become the victim of despoilers, just to emphasize that such things do happen at these fracases, I suppose this was the attitude the part called for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a rough or noisy fight, typically in a public place

Etymology

From Italian 'fracasso' meaning 'crash, uproar'.
