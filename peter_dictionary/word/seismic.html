seismic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪzmɪk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by earthquakes or earth vibrations.

Examples

Within about an hour with the help of reports from seismic stations in Alaska, Arizona and California, the quake's epicenter was placed at 51 degrees North latitude and 158 degrees East longitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tectonic - Relating to the structure of the earth's crust and the large-scale processes which take place within it, such as earthquakes and volcanic activity

Etymology

From Greek seismos (earthquake), from seiein (to shake).
