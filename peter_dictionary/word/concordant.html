concordant

1. ADJ

/ kənˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / kənˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
in agreement or harmony; consistent with something else

Examples

If the distribution of the 71 items were wholly concordant in the two families, the distance would of course be 0.
And irrespective of the outcome in centuries elapsed since splitting, calculations obviously carry more concordant and comparable meaning if they deal with the most stable units than with variously unstable ones.

Synset Examples

salaries agreeable with current trends
plans conformable with your wishes
expressed views concordant with his background

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - not in agreement; disagreeing or clashing

Etymology

From Latin 'concordare' (to agree, to be of one mind), from 'con-' (together) + 'cor', 'cordis' (heart).
