brace

1. NOUN

/ breɪs / (AmE), / breɪs / (BrE)
a device that supports or strengthens a part of the body or object, especially to aid healing or stability

Examples

writes: Does a brace help in sciatica?

Synset Examples

he wore a brace on his knee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splint - a rigid device used to support and immobilize a broken bone

Etymology

From Old French *brace* (arm), from Latin *bracchia* (arms).

2. VERB

/ breɪs / (AmE), / breɪs / (BrE)
To support, steady, or prepare (oneself or something) for impact or a difficult situation.

Examples

The Utopians brace conscience with legal sanctions.
One of the most appealing of the rooftop canvases is "Sun and Wind on the Roof ", with a woman and child bracing themselves against flapping clothes and flying birds.

Synset Examples

brace your elbows while working on the potter's wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady - make or become steady; stabilize or sustain
  • Synonym: fortify - to make stronger or more secure
  • Antonym: weaken - make or become less strong or steady

Etymology

From Old French *bracer* (to embrace, support), from the noun *brace* (pair, support), ultimately from Latin *brachium* (arm).

3. VERB

/ breɪs / (AmE), / breɪs / (BrE)
to prepare yourself mentally or physically for something unpleasant or difficult

Examples

He braced himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steel oneself - prepare oneself mentally for something difficult
  • Synonym: fortify - to make oneself mentally stronger for a challenge
  • Antonym: relax - to become less tense or anxious

Etymology

From Old French *brace* (arm), from Latin *bracchia* (arms); the sense evolved from 'support, make firm'.

4. VERB

/ breɪs / (AmE), / breɪs / (BrE)
to support or strengthen (a structure or object) by providing additional support

Examples

After the frames and transom are set up on the jig and temporarily braced, a piece of three-inch-wide mahogany (only widths will be given since the 13 16 - inch thickness is used throughout) is butted between frames one and two below the line of the keelson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to bear the weight of; hold up
  • Synonym: reinforce - to strengthen with additional material or support

Etymology

From Old French *brace* (arm), from Latin *bracchium* (arm).
