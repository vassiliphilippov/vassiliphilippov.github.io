content

1. VERB

/ kənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
(formal) To make (someone) satisfied or pleased; to satisfy. Often used reflexively (content oneself) to mean accept or be satisfied with what is available.

Examples

For Larkin is already wonderfully contented with his lot.
He would have to work without questioning the motives which made him work and content himself with the thought that the eventual victory, however it was brought about, would be sweet indeed.

Synset Examples

I am contented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfy - To fulfill the desires, needs, or expectations of

Etymology

From Latin contentus, past participle of continēre (to hold together, contain), meaning 'satisfied.'

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / kənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
the ideas, facts, or information contained in something, especially as distinct from its form or appearance

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substance - the essential meaning or most important part of something
  • Antonym: form - the manner or style in which something is expressed or performed, as opposed to its content

Etymology

From Latin 'contentum', meaning 'that which is contained', from 'continere' (to contain).
