ago

1. ADV

/ əˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / əˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
used to indicate how much time has passed since a particular event; before the present time, measured back from now

Examples

Pitcher Steve Barber joined the club one week ago after completing his hitch under the Army's accelerated wintertime military course, also at Fort Knox, Ky..
Named by Mayor Wagner three years ago to head a committee that included James A. Farley, Bernard Gimbel and Clint Blume, Shea worked relentlessly.
A couple of weeks ago, he scored a monstrous 12 on a par 5 hole.
Until a few weeks ago, however, Arnold Palmer was some god-like creature who had nothing in common with the duffers.
Broglio's 4 - 0 won-lost record and 1.24 earned-run mark against Pittsburgh a year ago; 2.

Synset Examples

long ago
sixty years ago my grandfather came to the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: since - from a particular time in the past until now

Etymology

From Old English 'āgān' (to pass, to go by), related to 'gone'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / əˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
(of a period of time) before the present; earlier; used after a measurement of time to indicate how much time has passed since something happened.

Examples

I was in the olive oil business with his father, but that was a long time ago.

Synset Examples

two years ago
`agone' is an archaic word for `ago'

Related Terms

  • Antonym: since - from a past time up to the present; often marks the start point

Etymology

From Old English āgān (to pass away, depart), past participle of āgān (to go by, pass).
