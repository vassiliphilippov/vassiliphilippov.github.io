dean

1. NOUN

/ din / (AmE), / diÀên / (BrE)
the head of a division or faculty at a university or college, responsible for administration and academic matters

Examples

A freshman girl's father not too long ago called a dean at Brooklyn College and demanded the "low-down "on a boy who was going out with his daughter.

Etymology

From Old French *deien*, from Late Latin *decanus* (head of ten), originally used for a leader of a group of ten monks.
