monotonous

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑtə.nəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒt.ən.əs / (BrE)
Dull and repetitious; lacking in variety and interest.

Examples

To them he could have been the broken bell in the church tower which rang before and after Mass, and at noon, and at six each evening - its tone, repetitive, monotonous, never breaking the boredom of the streets.

Synset Examples

a humdrum existence; all work and no play
nothing is so monotonous as the sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humdrum - lacking excitement; dull and monotonous
  • Antonym: varied - showing variety; not monotonous
  • Synonym: dreary - dull, bleak, and lifeless; depressing

Etymology

From Greek monotonos, from monos (single) + tonos (tone).
