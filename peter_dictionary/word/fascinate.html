fascinate

1. VERB

/ ˈfæs.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæs.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
To render someone motionless or unable to act, as if by a fixed stare, or by arousing awe, terror, or intense attention.

Examples

I was fascinated; just listening to him made me feel intelligent.

Synset Examples

The snake charmer fascinates the cobra

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesmerize - to hold the complete attention of someone as if by magic or hypnosis

Etymology

From Latin fascinare (to bewitch or enchant).

2. VERB

/ ˈfæsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to attract and hold the attention and interest of someone deeply

Examples

For some compulsive reason which would have fascinated Dr. Freud, Communists of all shapes and sizes almost invariably impute to others the very motives which they harbor themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrigue - to arouse the curiosity or interest of; fascinate
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or tired due to dullness

Etymology

From Latin fascinare (to bewitch).
