far-off

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑr ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈfɑː ˌɒf / (BrE)
Distant in space or time; very remote.

Examples

Far off, in the dusk, he heard voices singing, muffled but strong.
This time Lewis had his own rifle in his hands, and he threw some answering fire back at the mysterious far-off shot, then spent most of the day searching out the area.

Synset Examples

faraway mountains
the faraway future
troops landing on far-off shores
far-off happier times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote - distant in space or time; removed
  • Antonym: nearby - close in distance; not far
