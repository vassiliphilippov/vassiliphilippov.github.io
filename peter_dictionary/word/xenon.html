xenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːnɑn, ˈzɛnɑn / (AmE), / ˈziːnɒn, ˈzɛnɒn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Xe), a colorless, odorless, inert gas found in trace amounts in the Earth's atmosphere

Examples

Three different wavelengths of ultraviolet radiation are selected by the variable filters placed in front of the three mercury xenon lights which serve as the ultraviolet sources.

Etymology

From Greek xenos (strange, foreign) via New Latin xenon, named for its rarity.
