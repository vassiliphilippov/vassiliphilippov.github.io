huckster

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌkstɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌkstə / (BrE)
to sell or offer for sale aggressively, often by going from place to place

Examples

He milks the latest scientific advances, translating them into his own special Buck Rogers vocabulary to huckster his fake machines as a cure-all for everything from hay fever to sexual impotence and cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peddle - to sell goods by going from place to place

Etymology

From Middle English *hokster*, from Middle Dutch *hokester* (female peddler).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌkstər / (AmE), / ˈhʌkstə / (BrE)
A person who sells cheap or shoddy goods, often in an aggressive or dishonest way; a peddler.

Examples

When the hay wagon had gone, and an interval passed, a huckster's cart might turn the corner.
The horse walked, the reins were slack, the huckster rode with bowed shoulders, his forearms across his knees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peddler - a person who travels about selling goods; especially one who sells small wares
  • Synonym: hawker - a person who sells goods on the street or in public places

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'hokester' (pedlar, huckster), related to 'hoken' (to peddle).
