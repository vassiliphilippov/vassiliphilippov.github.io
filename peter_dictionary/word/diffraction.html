diffraction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
the bending and spreading of waves, especially light or sound, as they pass around sharp edges or through narrow openings, often creating patterns of alternating light and dark bands

Examples

The photochemical reaction cells consisted of 10 mm. i.d. Pyrex tubing, 5.5 cm. long, diffraction effects being minimized by the fact that the light passed through only liquid glass interfaces and not gas glass interfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refraction - the bending of a wave as it passes from one medium to another

Etymology

From Latin *diffringere* (to break into pieces), from dis- (apart) + frangere (to break).
