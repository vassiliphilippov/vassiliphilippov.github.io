update

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something more current by adding new information or changing it to reflect recent developments

Examples

Publisher Richardson has updated the Blue Book "but it still remains the compact reference book used by so many for those ever-changing telephone numbers, addresses, other residences, club affiliations and marriages ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernize - make something more modern or up-to-date
  • Antonym: outdate - to make outdated; to be older than the current standard

Etymology

Formed in 1940s from 'up-' (to a higher or later position) + 'date' (time reference).

2. VERB

/ ʌpˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something more modern, or bring it up to date by adding new information or features

Examples

Until professional planners meet this situation squarely and update the concepts of zoning in a manner acceptable to the courts, I hope we in East Greenwich can continue to shape our own destiny.
Their house was a centuries-old Colonial which they had had restored (guided by an eminent architect) and updated, and added on to.

Synset Examples

We updated the kitchen in the old house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernize - to make something more modern
  • Synonym: upgrade - to raise something to a higher standard

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'date', meaning to bring up to the present date.
