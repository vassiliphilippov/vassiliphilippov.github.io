adept

1. ADJ

/ əˈdɛpt / (AmE), / əˈdɛpt / (BrE)
very skilled or proficient at something; having or showing great knowledge and skill.

Examples

Yanks were equally adept at figurative expression.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing something
  • Synonym: expert - a person or adjective denoting someone highly skilled
  • Antonym: inept - having or showing no skill

Etymology

From Latin 'adeptus' (having obtained), from 'adipisci' (to obtain).
