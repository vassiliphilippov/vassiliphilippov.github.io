fearfully

1. ADV

/ ˈfɪr.fəli / (AmE), / ˈfɪə.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows fear or is full of fear.

Examples

Once more, Katie reared, and whinnied in fear.
She remembered McClellan's last proclamation as she hurried fearfully down the stairs.
Black called fearfully.

Synset Examples

she hurried down the stairs fearfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timidly - in a way that shows shyness or lack of confidence
  • Antonym: boldly - in a way that shows courage; without fear

Etymology

From 'fearful' + '-ly', forming an adverb from the adjective.
