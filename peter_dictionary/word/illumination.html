illumination

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount or quality of light in a place, especially as it affects visibility

Examples

In all cases there was readily measurable exchange after as little as one hour of illumination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lighting - the arrangement or effect of lights in an environment

Etymology

From Latin illuminatio (a lighting up), from illuminare (to light up).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
Spiritual or intellectual enlightenment, especially a sudden or divine awareness or understanding.

Examples

At the beginning of the play she has partial illumination and at the end she has complete illumination, but there has been no question but that she moves toward the dark.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlightenment - the state of gaining spiritual or intellectual insight

Etymology

From Latin illuminatio (enlightenment), from illuminare (to light up, make clear).
