agitate

1. VERB

/ ˈædʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈædʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
to campaign or take action in order to arouse public feeling or influence public opinion about a particular issue, especially in a political context

Examples

George W. Cable (naturalized New Englander), writing in 1889 from "Paradise Road, Northampton " (lovely symbolic name), agitated continuously the "Southern question ".
Legislators always get restless for a special session (whether for the companionship or the $ 22.50 per diem is not certain) and if they start agitating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - to work in an organized and active way toward a particular goal, often political

Etymology

From Latin agitare (to drive, stir up), frequentative of agere (to do, drive).
