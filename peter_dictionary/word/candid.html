candid

1. ADJ

/ ˈkændɪd / (AmE), / ˈkændɪd / (BrE)
Speaking honestly and openly, without holding back or trying to hide the truth; frank and direct.

Examples

The dialogue is sharp, witty and candid - typical "do n't eat the daisies "material - which has stamped the author throughout her books and plays, and it was obvious that the Theatre-by-the-Sea audience liked it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frank - honest and direct in speech or writing
  • Synonym: blunt - saying what is true even if it is impolite or shocks people
  • Antonym: evasive - not honest or direct, tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation

Etymology

From Latin 'candidus' (bright, clear, honest).
