quench

1. VERB

/ kwɛntʃ / (AmE), / kwɛntʃ / (BrE)
to satisfy one's thirst by drinking

Examples

The room filled with smoke, and Maggie's head throbbed with excitement and fatigue, but Stuart had such a happy, earnest look of proud possession on his face that Maggie could n't bear to do anything to quench it.

Synset Examples

The cold water quenched his thirst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slake - to satisfy thirst or desire
  • Antonym: thirst - desire for drink

Etymology

From Old English 'cwencan' (to extinguish), related to Middle English 'quenchen'.
