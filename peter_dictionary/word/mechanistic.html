mechanistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛkəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛkəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Explained in terms of physical processes or mechanical laws, often ignoring the role of consciousness or purpose.

Examples

Thus the image of man has suffered complete fragmentation in personal and spiritual qualities, and complete objectification in sub-human and quasi mechanistic powers.

Synset Examples

a mechanistic universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanical - relating to machinery or physical forces rather than organic or emotional factors

Etymology

From 'mechanism' + '-istic', modeled after French 'mécanistique'.
