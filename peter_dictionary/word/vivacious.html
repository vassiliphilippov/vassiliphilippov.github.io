vivacious

1. ADJ

/ vɪˈveɪʃəs / (AmE), / vɪˈveɪʃəs / (BrE)
(especially of a woman or her manner) attractively lively and animated.

Examples

But both were high-spirited and vivacious, both had tempers to control, both loved languages, especially English and German, both were good teachers and wrote for publication.

Synset Examples

a vibrant group that challenged the system
a charming and vivacious hostess
a vivacious folk dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animated - full of life or excitement; lively
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'vivax' (lively), from 'vivere' (to live).
