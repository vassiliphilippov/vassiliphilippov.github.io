clip

1. NOUN

/ klɪp / (AmE), / klɪp / (BrE)
a metal device for holding cartridges together, used to load them into the magazine of a firearm

Examples

Others carried extra clips for the Browning Automatic Rifle, which was in the hands of a little Mexican named Martinez.
Warren eased his rifle's safety off and gently, slowly sneaked another clip of ammunition from one of the cloth bandoleers that marked the upper part of his body with an X.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a chamber for holding cartridges to be fed into a firearm

2. NOUN

/ klɪp / (AmE), / klɪp / (BrE)
a small device, typically metal or plastic, used for holding objects or papers together

Examples

Our pin-curl clips and self-locking nuts achieved dominance in just a few years time, despite substantial, well established competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - a device that closes or secures something
  • Degree Lower: paperclip - a small wire or plastic device for holding papers together

3. VERB

/ klɪp / (AmE), / klɪp / (BrE)
to cut off or remove something by pinching or snipping, especially with scissors or other sharp tools

Examples

A carbine cracked more loudly, and a slug clipped fragments from the brush off at one side.
Different cards are used for males and females, and a corner is clipped from the cards of adults, and of children when they reach puberty.

Synset Examples

nip off the flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snip - to cut with small quick strokes, especially using scissors
  • Synonym: nip - to pinch or bite sharply, or to cut off sharply
  • Synonym: trim - to cut off unwanted parts to make something neat

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *klippa* (to cut or snip).
