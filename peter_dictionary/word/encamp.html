encamp

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkæmp / (AmE), / ɪnˈkamp / (BrE)
to set up or settle in a camp, especially in tents or temporary shelters, often for a group or army

Examples

Though an host should encamp against me, my heart shall not fear:
Yes indeed, we too can see a warlike host of infidels encamped against us.

Synset Examples

Can we go camping again this summer?
The circus tented near the town
The houseguests had to camp in the living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - to stay or dwell in a tent or shelter outdoors
  • Synonym: bivouac - to camp temporarily without shelter or in an improvised way

Etymology

From French 'encamper', from en- (in) + 'camp' (from Latin 'campus', field).
