oleophobic

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊlioʊˈfoʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊliəˈfəʊbɪk / (BrE)
Not having an affinity for oils; repellent to oil.

Examples

Thus, for aqueous media, we can think of the idealized organic active as an oleophilic or hydrophobic surface-active agent, and of an idealized builder as a oleophobic or hydrophilic surface-active agent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hydrophobic - repellent to water
  • Antonym: oleophilic - having an affinity for oil

Etymology

From Greek 'oleo-' (oil) and '-phobic' (having a fear or aversion).
