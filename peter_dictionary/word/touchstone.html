touchstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A standard or criterion by which something is judged or recognized.

Examples

Indeed, contract was the dominant legal theme of the century, the touchstone of the free society.

Synset Examples

the schools comply with federal standards
they set the measure for all subsequent work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - something used as a measure, norm, or model
  • Synonym: criterion - a principle or standard by which something may be judged or decided
  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference against which things may be compared

Etymology

Originally a stone used to test the purity of gold or silver by the streak left on it; from touch + stone.
