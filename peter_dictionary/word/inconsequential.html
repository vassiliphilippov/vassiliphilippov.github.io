inconsequential

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌkɑnsəˈkwɛnʃuəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃuəl / (BrE)
Not important or worth considering; insignificant.

Examples

"It was always the spirit with Christ; matters such as leavened or unleavened bread are inconsequential.
Yet Andy plowed ahead, mouthing the inconsequential words as if they possessed real meaning, and gradually his listeners warmed to him.
The film they did after his return was an inconsequential bit of nothing titled Out of This World, a satire on the Sinatra bobby-soxer craze.

Synset Examples

his work seems trivial and inconsequential
the quite inconsequent fellow was managed like a puppet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: significant - important, meaningful, or having a notable effect
  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary, extremely important
  • Synonym: unimportant - not important or not relevant; insignificant

Etymology

From Latin *in-* (not) + *consequentia* (sequence, consequence).
