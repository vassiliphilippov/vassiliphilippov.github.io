veined

1. ADJ

/ veɪnd / (AmE), / veɪnd / (BrE)
marked with lines or patterns that resemble the veins of a leaf or the blood vessels in animals

Examples

The doctor had to bend close to hear; his delicate hand, as veined as a moth's wing, rested absently on Scotty's chest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veinless - lacking visible veins or vein-like markings

Etymology

From 'vein' + '-ed', meaning 'having veins or vein-like markings'.
