ruined

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːɪnd / (AmE), / ˈruːɪnd / (BrE)
Having been destroyed or damaged so badly that repair is impossible or pointless; devastated physically or morally.

Examples

The man swayed on a thwart, turning his ruined eyes from side to side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyed - completely destroyed or annihilated
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired; in its original condition

Etymology

Past participle of 'ruin', from Old French 'ruiner', from Latin 'ruina' (a collapse, downfall).
