versatility

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrsəˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɜːsəˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the ability to adapt or be adapted to many different functions or activities; having a wide variety of skills or uses

Examples

Good taste and versatility, plus safety from spray poisons would be enough to recommend the frequent use of such a fruit, even if its nutritional values were limited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptability - the ability to adjust to new conditions
  • Synonym: flexibility - willingness or ability to change or compromise
  • Antonym: specialization - the act of concentrating on a limited range of skills or activities

Etymology

From Latin 'versatilis' (turning easily; versatile) via French 'versatile'.
