furtively

1. ADV

/ ˈfɝːtɪvli / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that attempts to avoid notice or attention; secretly or stealthily.

Examples

From the east to the west coast of the Korean peninsula was a strip of land in which fear filled men were at that same moment furtively crawling through the night, sitting in sweaty anticipation of any movement or sound, or shouting amidst confused rifle flashes and muzzle blasts.

Synset Examples

the soldiers were furtively crawling through the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - in a secret manner
  • Antonym: openly - in an open and unconcealed manner; opposite of secretly or furtively

Etymology

From 'furtive' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'furtivus' (stolen, secret).
