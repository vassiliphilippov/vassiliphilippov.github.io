confidential

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnfɪˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of information or a relationship) entrusted with private matters or secrets; intended to be kept private or secret.

Examples

Both parties and the Ministry of the Interior were busily at work after the elections trying to unearth the political affiliations of the successful candidates and, thereby, give the elections a confidential but known degree of national political significance.
One of these is the solidarity and the confidential relationship of marriage.

Synset Examples

a confidential secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden from others; not meant to be known
  • Antonym: public - meant for or open to all people; not private

Etymology

From Latin 'confidentia' (trust, confidence), from 'confidere' (to trust).
