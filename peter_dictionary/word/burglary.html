burglary

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrɡləri / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡləri / (BrE)
The act of illegally entering a building, especially at night, with the intention of committing a crime, usually stealing.

Examples

O' Banion's first conflict with the police came in 1909, at seventeen, when he was committed to Bridewell Prison for three months for burglary; two years later he served another three months for assault.
Those were his only interludes behind bars, although he collected four more charges on his police record in 1921 and 1922, three for burglary and one for robbery.
Six-to-twelve, armed burglary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From Anglo-Latin 'burglaria', from 'burglar' (house-breaker).
