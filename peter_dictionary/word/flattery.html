flattery

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæt.əɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈflæt.ər.i / (BrE)
Excessive or insincere praise given to someone, often to gain favor or advantage.

Examples

No man ever had a better opinion of himself and indeed, with one so favored, flattery could hardly seem overdone.
The Boulevard de la Madeleine, the Boulevard Malesherbes, and the Rue Royale ran to it with graceful flattery, bearing tidings of the Age of Reason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • Antonym: insult - an offensive remark or action

Etymology

From Old French 'flaterie', from 'flater' (to flatter).
