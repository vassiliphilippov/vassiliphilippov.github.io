chronologically

1. ADV

/ krəˈnɑlədʒɪkli / (AmE), / krəˈnɒlədʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the order in which events occurred in time.

Examples

Fourth, the two indicators are for the most part widely separated chronologically, with the extensive age gap occurring during childhood for all but one growth center.

Synset Examples

he is chronologically older

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequentially - in sequence; in a specific order, often following in time or logic
  • Antonym: randomly - in a random manner; without any specific order

Etymology

From 'chronology', which comes from Greek *khronos* (time) and *-logia* (study of).
