brandy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrændi / (AmE), / ˈbrændi / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice.

Examples

They had brandy in the library.
When something unexpected happened, one always asked for water if one were a woman, brandy if one were a man.
He claimed to be visiting the waterfront saloon at the crossroads to play cards and drink with his cronies, but Kate had not smelled brandy on him since Mrs. Lattimer's funeral.
Brandy will sweat it out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognac - type of brandy from the Cognac region of France
  • Antonym: whisky - spirits made from grain, not fruit or wine

Etymology

From Dutch 'brandewijn' meaning 'burnt wine', referring to the distillation process.
