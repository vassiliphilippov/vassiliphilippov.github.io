manipulation

1. NOUN

/ məˌnɪpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌnɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of skillfully or deviously controlling or influencing people or situations, especially for one's own advantage

Examples

After how many generations is such wealth (mounting all the while through the manipulations of high finance) purified of taint?

Synset Examples

his manipulation of his friends was scandalous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploitation - using someone or something unfairly for your own advantage
  • Synonym: coercion - the act of persuading someone forcefully or by threat
  • Synonym: deception - the act of causing someone to accept as true what is false

Etymology

From French 'manipulation', from manipuler (to handle), ultimately from Latin 'manus' (hand).
