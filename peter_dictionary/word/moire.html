moire

1. NOUN

/ mwɑːr / (AmE), / mwɑːr / (BrE)
A silk fabric with a rippled or wavy surface pattern, often produced by pressing the fabric to create a shimmering effect.

Examples

But before she left her room she dug into her big moire bag, took out the envelope holding her marriage contract and the wax seal had been broken.

Etymology

From French moire, from moirer 'to produce a watered (wavy) appearance'.
