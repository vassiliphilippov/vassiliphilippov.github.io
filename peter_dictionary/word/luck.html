luck

1. NOUN

/ lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
An unpredictable force or phenomenon that seems to cause events to happen in a particular way, beyond a person's control.

Examples

"Just our luck "! exclaimed Stram.
Here's where luck would normally step in.
Some people have luck and some have no luck and that, whatever people who prefer order say, is the size of it.
Some people have luck and some have no luck and that, whatever people who prefer order say, is the size of it.
As luck had it, he had not gone twenty feet in the street before Pat appeared.

Synset Examples

bad luck caused his downfall
we ran into each other by pure chance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chance - the occurrence of events without apparent cause
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as a force affecting people
  • Synonym: fate - the development of events outside a person's control

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *luc* (happiness, good fortune).

2. NOUN

/ lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
The success or failure brought by chance rather than through one's own actions; the overall circumstances of life as influenced by fortune.

Examples

His money was tied up in a Nassau hotel, an Ohio pottery works, and a detergent for window-washing, and luck had been running against him.
"What luck, Cobb "? he said swiftly.
The truth was, the puncher was both bewildered and dismayed by his own mixed luck.
Damn his luck.
But as the tree passed, she lifted an arm in gesture of better luck and farewell.

Synset Examples

whatever my fortune may be
deserved a better fate
has a happy lot
the luck of the Irish
a victim of circumstances
success that was her portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - success or failure brought by chance
  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as determined by a supernatural power
  • Degree Lower: bad luck - misfortune; unfavorable outcome by chance
  • Degree Higher: good luck - favorable outcome by chance

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *luk* (happiness, good fortune), influenced by Middle Dutch *luc*, first used in English in the 15th century.

3. NOUN

/ lʌk / (AmE), / lʌk / (BrE)
The force that causes good things to happen to a person by chance, rather than through their own actions or efforts.

Examples

Luck was with him.
I wish all of you good luck.

Synset Examples

it was my good luck to be there
they say luck is a lady
it was as if fortune guided his hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as a controlling power in human affairs

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *luc*, a short form of *gheluc* (happiness, good fortune).
