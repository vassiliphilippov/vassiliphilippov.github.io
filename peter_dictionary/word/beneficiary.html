beneficiary

1. NOUN

/ bɛnəˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / bɛnɪˈfɪʃiəri / (BrE)
A person who receives money, property, or other advantages from someone else, especially as designated in a will, trust, or insurance policy.

Examples

The slaves never shared in their profits, while they did share, in a very real sense, in the profits of the slave owners: they were fed, clothed, doctored, and so forth; they were the beneficiaries of responsible, paternalistic care.
If you are an income beneficiary of property held in trust or an heir, legatee, or devisee, you may deduct allowable depreciation and depletion, if not deductible by the estate or trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to receive property or money after someone dies
  • Synonym: legatee - a person who receives a legacy (property or money) under a will
  • Antonym: trustee - a person who holds property or assets on behalf of another (opposite role)

Etymology

From Latin 'beneficiarius' (enjoying a favor or benefit), from 'beneficium' (benefit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnəˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃəri / (BrE)
In linguistics, the participant in a sentence who is intended to benefit from the action described by the verb.

Examples

You'’ve always been good to me, ever since I came here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recipient - person who receives something (linguistics)

Etymology

From Latin beneficium (benefit) + -ary (relating to).
