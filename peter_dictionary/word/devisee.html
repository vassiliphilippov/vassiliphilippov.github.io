devisee

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvaɪˈzi / (AmE), / dɪˌvaɪˈziː / (BrE)
a person to whom property, especially real estate, is left in a will

Examples

If you are an income beneficiary of property held in trust or an heir, legatee, or devisee, you may deduct allowable depreciation and depletion, if not deductible by the estate or trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legatee - person who receives a legacy (personal property) by will

Etymology

From Anglo-French *devisee*, feminine past participle of *deviser* (to devise).
