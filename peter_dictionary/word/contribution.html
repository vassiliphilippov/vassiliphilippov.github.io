contribution

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A voluntary gift of money, service, or ideas, especially to a cause, organization, or effort.

Examples

On the clock given him was the inscription, "For Outstanding Contribution to Billiken Basketball, 1960 - 61 ".
This may be unfortunate, perhaps, from the standpoint of David Hedison, Providence's contribution to Hollywood, who is appearing by special arrangement with 20th Century-Fox.
Oldenburg's contributions were soon exhausted and the boys had to turn to a wider circle of the town's learned, such as the pastor, to supplement the simple teaching.
"Sometimes we 'd have trouble persuading her to make tax-exempt charitable contributions, and I 've known her to quarrel with a plumber over a bill for fixing a faucet; the next moment she 'd put another half million into the scholarship fund or thirty thousand into something as impractical as this unfortunate Johnston affair.
And also to thank that boy'’s family for a magnificent contribution to the State.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something that is given to a charity or cause, usually money
  • Synonym: gift - something given without payment or return, often to a cause
  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given for a particular purpose, often by an organization

Etymology

From Latin 'contributio' (a bringing together, contribution), from 'contribuere' (to bring together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
something that a person gives or does to help achieve or produce something, especially as part of a group effort; the part played by someone in bringing about a result.

Examples

About the Pro Arte's contribution I am less happy.
The assisting musicians from the Vienna Octet are somewhat lacking in expertise, but their contribution is rustic and appealing.
But marriage experts say that such mutual contribution and mutual joy are seldom achieved.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: input - something put into an activity or process to achieve a result
  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt to do something
  • Degree Higher: expenditure - the use of energy, time, or resources in order to achieve something

Etymology

From Latin 'contributio' (a bringing together), from 'contribuere' (to bring together, contribute).
