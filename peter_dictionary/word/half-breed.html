half-breed

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌbrid / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌbriːd / (BrE)
An offensive and now outdated term for a person whose parents are of different races, especially one of mixed Caucasian and Native American ancestry.

Examples

He was tall and dark-skinned, a half-breed, Wilson thought.
The half-breed eased the Winchester down and rested it across his lap.
The half-breed did n't answer this time.
He watched the half-breed as he turned silently.

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'breed', reflecting mixed ancestry; first recorded in the 18th century.
