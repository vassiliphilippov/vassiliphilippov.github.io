footing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
The condition or status of a relationship between people or groups, especially as a basis for interaction (often used in phrases such as 'on an equal footing').

Examples

Catholic priests have frequently appeared on television programs, sometimes discussing the Christian faith on an equal footing with Protestant clergymen.

Synset Examples

on good terms with her in-laws
on a friendly footing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terms - state or basis of a relationship (as in 'on good terms')

Etymology

From foot + -ing, originally referring to the act of setting one’s feet or position; figurative use developed later.
