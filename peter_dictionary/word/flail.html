flail

1. NOUN

/ fleɪl / (AmE), / fleɪl / (BrE)
a manual tool with a long handle and a free-swinging stick at the end, used for threshing grain by hand

Examples

He thought of Simms Purdew, who once had risen at the edge of a cornfield, a maniacal scream on his lips, and swung a clubbed musket like a flail to beat down the swirl of Rebel bayonets about him.

Etymology

From Old French *flael*, from Latin *flagellum* (whip).

2. VERB

/ fleɪl / (AmE), / fleɪl / (BrE)
to beat or strike something hard and repeatedly, especially with a swinging motion

Examples

He could not grasp that Lord had withdrawn from the fight minutes ago, and that his leaden arms were flailing at nothing but the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to beat repeatedly and violently

Etymology

From Old English *fleogel* (flail), related to Old French *flael*, from Latin *flagellum* (whip, scourge).
