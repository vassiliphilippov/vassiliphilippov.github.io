progressivism

1. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
A political and social philosophy advocating progress, reform, and improvement in government and society, typically emphasizing social justice, equality, and modernization.

Examples

I used his polarity to illustrate what I thought had happened to us in that form of liberalism we call Progressivism.
And with Progressivism the Religion of Humanity was replacing what Gabriel called Christian supernaturalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalism - Political philosophy favoring individual rights and equality; often overlaps with progressivism

Etymology

From 'progressive' + '-ism', indicating a system of beliefs or practices.
