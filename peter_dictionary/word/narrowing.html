narrowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
An instance or process of becoming or making something narrower.

Examples

The intimal surface of the aorta was covered with confluent, yellow-brown, hard, friable plaques along its entire course, and there was a marked narrowing of the orifices of the large major visceral arteries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: widening - an instance of becoming wider

Etymology

From the verb 'narrow' + -ing suffix, forming a noun.
