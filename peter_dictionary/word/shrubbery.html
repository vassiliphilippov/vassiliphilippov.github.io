shrubbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrʌbəri / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌbəri / (BrE)
An area planted with shrubs, often as part of a garden or landscaped grounds.

Examples

While several yards from it, still concealed by the shrubbery, she 'd seen two men on her left at the pool's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a low woody plant, smaller than a tree

Etymology

From shrub + -ery, denoting a place where shrubs are grown.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrʌb.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌb.əri / (BrE)
An area or border of land planted with shrubs, especially as part of a garden or landscape design.

Examples

I followed a shrubbery lined gravel path alongside the house to the pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a small to medium-sized woody plant
  • Synonym: hedge - a fence or boundary formed by closely growing bushes or shrubs

Etymology

From shrub + -ery, patterned after the formation of words such as 'ornery' or 'brewery'.
