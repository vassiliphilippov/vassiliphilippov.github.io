mute

1. ADJ

/ mjuːt / (AmE), / mjuːt / (BrE)
(of an action, feeling, or expression) conveyed or expressed without speech or spoken words; silent.

Examples

After a few tortuous moments of wondering who "he "is, the camera pans across the room to the plaster statue, and we realize that Neitzbohr is trying to redeem himself in the eyes of a mute piece of sculpture.

Synset Examples

a mute appeal
a silent curse
best grief is tongueless
the words stopped at her lips unsounded
unspoken grief
choking exasperation and wordless shame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silent - not making or accompanied by any sound
  • Synonym: unspoken - not voiced or expressed in words
  • Synonym: wordless - expressed without words

Etymology

From Latin mutus (silent, unable to speak).
