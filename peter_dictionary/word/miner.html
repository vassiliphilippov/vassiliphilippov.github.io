miner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
A person who works in a mine, extracting minerals, coal, or other materials from the earth.

Examples

The injured German veteran was a former miner, twenty-four years old, who had been wounded by shrapnel in the back of the head.
There were no less than six or seven saloons in Ganado, not counting the lower class dives, all vying for the trade of celebrating miners and teamsters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mining - the process or industry of extracting minerals from the earth

Etymology

From Middle English *minour*, from Old French *mineur* (miner), from *mine* (mine).
