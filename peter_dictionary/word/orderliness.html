orderliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdərlɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being methodical, organized, and systematic; the tendency to maintain order.

Examples

The parent was asked to describe the child's typical behavior in certain standard situations in which there was an opportunity to observe tendencies toward perfectionism in demands upon self and others, irrational conformity to rules, orderliness, punctuality, and need for certainty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disorderliness - the quality of lacking order or organization
  • Synonym: orderly - neat and arranged in an organized way

Etymology

From 'orderly' + '-ness', after 'order' from Old French 'ordre', from Latin 'ordo'.
