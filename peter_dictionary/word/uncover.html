uncover

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to remove a covering from something, or to reveal something that was hidden or unknown

Examples

It was a sort of poetic justice that at the time of his own demise a new plot to overthrow the Venezuelan government, reportedly involving the use of Dominican arms by former Venezuelan Dictator Marcos Perez Jimenez, has been uncovered and quashed.
Only ingenuity will uncover it.
Thanks for continuing to capture the attention and uncover so many areas of need in this amazing world.
Thus it was that Barco, apprehended for mere larceny, now began to suspect that one or another of his murders had been uncovered.

Synset Examples

Summer brings out bright clothes
He brings out the best in her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make known or show something previously secret or hidden
  • Synonym: expose - to make something visible, typically something hidden
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden or secret

Etymology

From un- (not) + cover, meaning to remove a cover or make known.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to remove clothing from all or part of the body so that it is exposed

Examples

However, Biblical scholars frequently attested to its numerous inaccuracies, as old manuscripts were uncovered and scholarship advanced.

Synset Examples

uncover your belly
The man exposed himself in the subway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expose - to make something visible, especially something hidden or secret
  • Antonym: cover - to put something over or on a part of the body

Etymology

From un- (opposite of) + cover; 'cover' from Old French 'covrir,' from Latin 'cooperire'.
