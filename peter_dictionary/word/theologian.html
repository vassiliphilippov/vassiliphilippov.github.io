theologian

1. NOUN

/ θiˈoʊlədʒən / (AmE), / θɪˈɒlədʒən / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in theology or who studies and writes about religious beliefs

Examples

This emphasis upon death rather than sin as man's fundamental problem Irenaeus shares with many early theologians, especially the Greek speaking ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious belief

Etymology

From Late Latin theologianus, from Greek theologia (study of the divine).
