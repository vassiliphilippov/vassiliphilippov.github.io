endeavor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
An attempt to achieve a goal; a purposeful or industrious undertaking, especially one that requires effort or boldness.

Examples

Some 80 reaction tubes from 13 manifold fillings were illuminated in the temperature range from 40 to 85 ` in a further endeavor to determine the cause of the irreproducibility and to obtain information on the activation energy and the effect of light intensity.
Presumably a cocktail party is expected to fulfill the host's desire to get together a number of people who are inadequately acquainted and thereby arrange for bringing the level of acquaintance up to adequacy for future cooperative endeavors.
Putting the key into the switch, pressing the accelerator with his foot, putting the car into reverse, seemed vast endeavors almost beyond the ability of his shaking body.

Synset Examples

he had doubts about the whole enterprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - an act of trying to achieve something
  • Synonym: undertaking - a task or project, especially one that is difficult or requires effort
  • Antonym: idleness - the state of being inactive or avoiding effort

Etymology

From Middle English 'endeveren', from Old French 'endevoir' (to make it one's duty), based on Latin 'debere' (to owe).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
an attempt or effort to achieve a goal, especially one that is earnest and conscientious

Examples

Synset Examples

she gave it a good try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Synonym: try - an attempt to accomplish something

Etymology

From Old French *endeverer* (to make an effort), from phrase *en deveir* (in duty), from Latin *debitum* (duty).

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
to try hard to do or achieve something

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strive - to make great efforts to achieve something
  • Synonym: attempt - to try to do something, especially something difficult

Etymology

From Middle English 'endeveren', from Anglo-French 'endeverer', meaning 'to make an effort'.
