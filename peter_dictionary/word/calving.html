calving

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.vɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːv.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of a cow or other bovine giving birth to a calf

Examples

For the prevention of acetonemia (ketosis) feed 1 4 pound per day beginning at calving and continuing for 6 weeks.
For prevention of ketosis, feed 1 4 pound per head daily for 6 weeks commencing at calving time.
Then, feed preventive dose until 6 weeks after calving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parturition - the act of giving birth; especially in mammals
  • Synonym: lambing - the act of a sheep giving birth to a lamb

Etymology

From 'calf' (the young of cattle) plus the suffix '-ing' indicating the action or process.
