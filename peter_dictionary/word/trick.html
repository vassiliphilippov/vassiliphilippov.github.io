trick

1. NOUN

/ trɪk / (AmE), / trɪk / (BrE)
a skilful act or illusion that appears to be magical, often performed to entertain

Examples

This discovery of Melies was vastly more important than his sometimes dazzling, magician's tricks produced on film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality
  • Synonym: conjuring - performance of magical tricks as entertainment

2. NOUN

/ trɪk / (AmE), / trɪk / (BrE)
a cunning act or scheme intended to deceive or mislead someone, especially to make them do something foolish or imprudent

Examples

The trick these two play upon Jacoby reveals their want not simply of decency but of imagination as well.

Synset Examples

that offer was a dirty trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruse - a clever plan used to trick someone
  • Synonym: prank - a playful trick or practical joke
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or tricking
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest, not tricking or deceiving

Etymology

From Old North French *trique* (deception, trick), related to Old French *trichier* (to cheat).

3. NOUN

/ trɪk / (AmE), / trɪk / (BrE)
an amusing, often silly or grotesque act performed for fun or entertainment

Examples

he liked children, in a loathsome kind of way; the two youngest in our family always had to be brought in and put through tricks for his entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunt - a daring or entertaining action performed for amusement
  • Synonym: antic - a ludicrous or eccentric act

Etymology

From Old North French *trique* (deception, trickery), related to Old French *trichier* (to cheat).

4. NOUN

/ trɪk / (AmE), / trɪk / (BrE)
a clever or deceitful act intended to deceive or outwit someone

Examples

Too many husbands, Dr. Schillinger continues, worry about "how well they 're doing ", and fear that their success depends on some trick or technique of sexual play.
Time is a queer thing and memory a queerer; the tricks that time plays with memory and memory with time are queerest of all.
That's a new trick.

Synset Examples

he played a trick on me
he pulled a fast one and got away with it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruse - a clever plan intended to deceive someone
  • Synonym: prank - a playful, mischievous trick
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

Probably from Old French tricher (to cheat), from Vulgar Latin *trica (trick, deceit).

5. NOUN

/ trɪk / (AmE), / trɪk / (BrE)
a period or shift of work or duty, especially in jobs requiring continuous coverage such as the military, railroads, or ranch work

Examples

Two of the new hands, a Mexican named Jose Amado and a kid known only as Laredo, were picked for the first trick of riding night herd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a scheduled period of work

6. VERB

/ trɪk / (AmE), / trɪk / (BrE)
to deceive or cheat someone by making them believe something that is not true

Examples

It was the night Clayton had tricked them in the poker game.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something untrue
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick someone

Etymology

Probably from Old North French *trique* (a trick, artifice) or Middle English *trick* (deceit, cunning).
