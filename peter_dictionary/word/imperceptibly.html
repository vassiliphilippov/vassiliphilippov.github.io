imperceptibly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪm.pɚˈsɛp.tə.bli / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈsɛp.tɪ.bli / (BrE)
In a way that is so slight, gradual, or subtle as to be almost unnoticeable.

Examples

He is aware that every moderately important problem merges imperceptibly into every other problem.

Synset Examples

the power of the Secretary of State in London increased gradually but imperceptibly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradually - in a slow or gradual manner
  • Antonym: perceptibly - in a way that can be perceived; noticeably

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'percipere' (to perceive) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
