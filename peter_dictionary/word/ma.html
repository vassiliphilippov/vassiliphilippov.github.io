MA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈɑː / (BrE)
an academic degree awarded by universities for completion of postgraduate study in arts, humanities, or social sciences; abbreviation for Master of Arts

Examples

On December 9, 1862, Sergeant Edwin H. Fay, an unusual Louisianan who held A. B. and M. A. degrees from Harvard University and who before the war was headmaster of a private school for boys in Louisiana, wrote his wife:

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: BA - Bachelor of Arts degree
  • Degree Higher: PhD - Doctor of Philosophy degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Magister Artium' (Master of Arts).
