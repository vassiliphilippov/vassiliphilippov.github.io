rhetorician

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtəˈrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtəˈrɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in the art of rhetoric, especially one who delivers speeches or is an expert in the use of persuasive language

Examples

There are many types of ambiguity and many of them have been described by rhetoricians under such names as amphibology, parisology, and other ologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orator - a person who delivers formal speeches, especially one skilled in public speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'rhetoricianus', from Greek 'rhētorikós' (of oratory), related to 'rhētōr' (orator).
