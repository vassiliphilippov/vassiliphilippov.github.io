cooperative

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑːpərətɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
A business or organization that is owned and run jointly by its members, who share the profits or benefits.

Examples

Constitutional government, popular vote, trial by jury, public education, labor unions, cooperatives, communes, socialized ownership, world courts, and the veto power in world councils are but a few examples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: co-op - an informal or abbreviated term for 'cooperative' (member-owned organization)

Etymology

From Latin 'cooperari' (to work together), via French 'coopérative'.

/ koʊˈɑːpərətɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
involving mutual assistance in working toward a common goal; done together with others for mutual benefit

Examples

  • Antonym: independent - acting alone or separately, not cooperating with others
