dais

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.ɪs / (BrE)
A low platform at the front of a hall or room, often used for speeches or ceremonies to give prominence to the speaker or honored guests.

Examples

Shea, the chairman of Mayor Wagner's Baseball Committee, will be joined on the dais by Warren Spahn, the southpaw pitching ace of the Milwaukee Braves; Frank Graham, the Journal-American sports columnist; Bill Mazeroski, the World Series hero of the Pittsburgh Pirates, and Casey Stengel, the former manager of the Yankees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stage - an area raised above ground level, especially for performances

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *deis*, from Latin *discus* (table, disk).
