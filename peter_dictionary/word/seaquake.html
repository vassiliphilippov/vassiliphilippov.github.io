seaquake

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌkweɪk / (BrE)
An earthquake that takes place beneath the sea, typically at the seabed.

Examples

On some of the islands' beaches the waves came in gently; they were steepest on the shores facing the direction of the seaquake from which the waves had come.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthquake - a sudden shaking of the ground caused by seismic waves

Etymology

Formed from "sea" and "quake" (as in "earthquake"), modeled on similar compounds.
