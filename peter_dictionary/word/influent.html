influent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnfluənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnfluənt / (BrE)
flowing inward; entering, especially as a fluid into a system or channel

Examples

The BOD of the influent to the pilot plant varied between 110 and 710 mg / l with an average of 350 mg / l.
The organic concentration in the influent raw sewage ranged from 160 to 270 mg / l of BOD with an average of 230 mg / l.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effluent - flowing out; liquid waste or outflow from a process

Etymology

From Latin influentem, present participle of influere (to flow into).
