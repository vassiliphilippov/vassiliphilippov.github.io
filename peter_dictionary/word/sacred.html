sacred

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd / (BrE)
connected with religion or considered to be holy; regarded with great respect and reverence, especially in a religious context.

Examples

Durkheim noted long ago that religion as "a unified system of beliefs and practices relative to sacred things - unite [ s ] into one single moral community - all those who adhere to them ".
As Yinger has pointed out, the "reliance on symbols, on tradition, on sacred writings, on the cultivation of emotional feelings of identity and harmony with sacred values, turns one to the past far more than to the future ".
But William continued to find a bitter joy in smashing images and tearing down symbols sacred to the Old Church.
He is the conveyor of a sacred reality by which he has been grasped.

Synset Examples

sacred texts
sacred rites
sacred music

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profane - not sacred; relating to nonreligious or secular things
  • Synonym: holy - dedicated to God or a religious purpose; sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'sacer' (holy, consecrated).

2. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd / (BrE)
regarded with great respect or deserving of reverence and dedication

Examples

What he really wants is to find "a sacred cause "to which he can honestly devote himself.

Synset Examples

saw motherhood as woman's sacred calling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holy - regarded as sacred or spiritually pure
  • Antonym: profane - not sacred; showing disrespect toward what is sacred

Etymology

From Latin sacer (holy, consecrated).

3. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd / (BrE)
Regarded with great respect and reverence, especially because of association with religion or divinity.

Examples

To the Italian people that's a very sacred, close relationship.

Synset Examples

the sacred name of Jesus
Jerusalem's hallowed soil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; secular or irreverent
  • Synonym: holy - dedicated or consecrated to God or a religious purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'sacratus', past participle of 'sacrare' (to consecrate, make holy).
