literalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being literal; strict adherence to the exact or primary meaning of words or representations.

Examples

Depicted, Cubist flatness is now almost completely assimilated to the literal, undepicted kind, but at the same time it reacts upon and largely transforms the undepicted kind - and it does so, moreover, without depriving the latter of its literalness; rather, it underpins and reinforces that literalness, re-creates it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literalism - adherence to the exact letter or the literal sense
  • Antonym: figurativeness - the quality of being figurative; not literal

Etymology

From 'literal' + '-ness'. 'Literal' from Latin 'litteralis' (relating to letters).
