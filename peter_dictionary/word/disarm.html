disarm

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈɑrm / (AmE), / dɪsˈɑːm / (BrE)
to remove weapons or military capability from a person, group, or country; to deprive of means of attack or defense

Examples

This is why I say we just can't go ahead and disarm the Germans and pull down our own defenses.
No need to kill an entire city and all its people because we lacked the precision and reconnaissance to selectively disarm the enemy's military force.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arm - to equip with weapons

Etymology

From Old French *desarmer*, from Latin *dis-* (apart) + *arma* (arms, weapons).
