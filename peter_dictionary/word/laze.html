laze

1. VERB

/ leɪz / (AmE), / leɪz / (BrE)
to spend time in a relaxed and lazy way, doing very little

Examples

You can cross an ocean in a fully equipped craft, sail, power, or both, or laze away a fine day in a small dinghy on a local pond.

Synset Examples

The old man sat and stagnated on his porch
He slugged in bed all morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing or being inactive
  • Synonym: relax - to rest and be calm, not work or worry
  • Antonym: work - to engage in physical or mental activity

Etymology

Probably back-formation from 'lazy'.
