resurface

1. VERB

/ riˈsɝːfɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
to cover (something) with a new surface, especially as part of repair or renovation

Examples

The roof of the license-plate factory needs resurfacing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repave - to pave again, especially a road or path

Etymology

From re- (again) + surface (noun and verb).

2. VERB

/ riˈsɜrfɪs / (AmE), / riˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
to appear or become visible or active again after being absent or hidden

Examples

Until Grissom... resurfaces... ... I'm the acting president.

Synset Examples

The missing man suddenly resurfaced in New York

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reappear - to appear again after disappearing

Etymology

From re- (again) + surface (to appear at the surface).
