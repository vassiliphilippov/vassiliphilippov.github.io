flashlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃ.laɪt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃ.laɪt / (BrE)
A small portable electric lamp powered by batteries, used to provide light when needed.

Examples

Where was my flashlight?
Continuous low-level lighting may be provided in the shelter by means of a 4 - cell hot-shot battery to which is wired a 150 - milliampere flashlight type bulb.
A flashlight or electric lantern also should be available for those periods when a brighter light is needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torch - British term for a portable battery-powered electric lamp

Etymology

From 'flash' + 'light'; originated in early 20th-century America when early devices emitted quick flashes rather than a steady beam.
