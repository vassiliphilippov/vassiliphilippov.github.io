blade

1. NOUN

/ bleɪd / (AmE), / bleɪd / (BrE)
the flat, extended part of a leaf, especially a grass leaf, distinct from the stem or stalk

Examples

McFeeley noted the immaculate lawn and gardens: each blade of grass cropped, bright and firm; each shrub glazed with good health.

Etymology

From Old English blæd (leaf, leaf-like part of a plant), related to Proto-Germanic *bladan.

2. NOUN

/ bleɪd / (AmE), / bleɪd / (BrE)
a long, narrow, thin piece of something, especially one that resembles a blade of grass

Examples

There were a few blades of lint on the shoulder.

Synset Examples

a blade of lint on his suit

3. NOUN

/ bleɪd / (AmE), / bleɪd / (BrE)
(historical) A dashing or fashionable young man, especially one known for lively or reckless behavior.

Examples

Occasionally gay young blades would write vividly to boon companions at home about their amorous exploits in Richmond, Petersburg, Washington, or Nashville.

Synset Examples

gay young blades bragged of their amorous adventures

4. NOUN

/ bleɪd / (AmE), / bleɪd / (BrE)
the flat, sharp-edged part of a tool, weapon, or machine used for cutting

Examples

Alone in the dark with nothing but your thoughts, time can draw out like a blade.

Etymology

From Old English blæd (leaf, blade of a sword), related to Proto-Germanic *bladan.
