declaim

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkleɪm / (AmE), / dɪˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to recite or speak aloud with rhetorical effect, often in a loud, formal, or dramatic way

Examples

As Sir Giles Overreach (how often had he had to play that part, who did not believe a word of it), he raised his arm and declaimed: "Where is my honour now "?
As he declaimed the sonorous measures, it was as much as Claire could do to restrain herself from bursting out with her dramatic tidings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recite - to repeat aloud from memory, often formally or publicly
  • Synonym: orate - to speak formally or give an oration; to make a speech

Etymology

From Latin 'dēclāmāre', meaning 'to practice oratory, declaim'.
