abreaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbriˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbriːˈækʃən / (BrE)
In psychoanalysis, the process of releasing or purging pent-up emotional tension by reliving a traumatic experience.

Examples

We pointed out that emotional excitement may lead to psychosomatic disorders and neurotic symptoms, particularly in certain types of personality, but it is also known that the reliving of a strong emotion ("abreaction ") may cure a battle neurosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catharsis - the process of releasing strong or repressed emotions

Etymology

From German 'Abreaktion', from 'ab-' (off, away) + 'Reaktion' (reaction).
