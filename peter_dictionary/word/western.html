Western

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən / (BrE)
A film genre set in the American West, typically during the late 19th century, depicting frontier life, cowboys, and outlaws.

Examples

On the other we have the flag wavers and the national sentimentalists who have been willing to use any patriotic, "frontier western "or colonial material willy-nilly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oater - informal synonym for a Western film

Etymology

From 'western', meaning 'of or relating to the West', in reference to the western United States.
