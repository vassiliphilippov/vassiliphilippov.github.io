spray

1. NOUN

/ spreɪ / (AmE), / spreɪ / (BrE)
a container or device that emits liquid in a fine mist, often used for applying perfume, water, or cleaning solutions

Examples

I dig it, rake it smooth, sow the seeds and wet them down with a fog spray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomizer - device for converting a liquid into a fine spray

2. NOUN

/ spreɪ / (AmE), / spreɪ / (BrE)
A liquid preparation, such as a pesticide or other chemical, suspended or dissolved for application by spraying.

Examples

It is thick, much like an egg plant's skin, so that poison sprays, if they are used, present no hazard to the consumer.
Good taste and versatility, plus safety from spray poisons would be enough to recommend the frequent use of such a fruit, even if its nutritional values were limited.
If an expensive and specialized piece of machinery is needed - such as a spray rig, a combine, or a binder - it is better to pay someone with a machine to do the work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosol - A substance enclosed under pressure and released as a fine spray

3. VERB

/ spreɪ / (AmE), / spreɪ / (BrE)
to send out liquid in a stream of tiny drops, or to cover something with a fine mist of liquid

Examples

By 1952 he was convinced he would no longer spray.
Dried fruits are purchased from sources where they are neither sulphured nor sprayed.
Apples come from a farm in Vermont where they are not sprayed.

Synset Examples

Water sprayed all over the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mist - to spray very finely with liquid

Etymology

From Dutch *spray* (sprinkle), related to Middle Dutch *sprayen* (to sprinkle).

4. VERB

/ spreɪ / (AmE), / spreɪ / (BrE)
to scatter liquid or small particles in a mass or jet of droplets

Examples

The heavy glass did n't break, but the top flew off; sugar sprayed with a hiss that was loud in the silence.
He tossed the bag into the pit and watched dry dust spray up around it.

Synset Examples

spray water on someone
spray paint on the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirt - to eject liquid in a thin, fast stream
  • Degree Lower: mist - to cover with tiny droplets of liquid
  • Degree Higher: drench - to cover thoroughly with liquid

Etymology

From Dutch 'spuiten' (to spout) and Middle Dutch 'spraeyen' (to sprinkle, spray).
