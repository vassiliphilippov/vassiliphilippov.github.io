mismanage

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪsˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to control or handle something badly or incompetently

Examples

Mr. Nixon, he believes, has no particular political philosophy and mismanaged his own campaign.

Synset Examples

The funds were mismanaged

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manage - to be in charge of or run something

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'manage' (from Old French 'manager', to handle or control).
