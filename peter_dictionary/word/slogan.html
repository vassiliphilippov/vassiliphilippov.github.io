slogan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsloʊɡən / (AmE), / ˈsləʊɡən / (BrE)
A distinctive phrase or motto used by a group, political movement, or organization to express its ideals, purpose, or rallying point.

Examples

The socialism implicit in the slogan of the Roosevelt Revolution, freedom from want and fear, seems a far cry from the individualism of the First Amendment to the Constitution, or of the Jacksonian frontier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motto - a short sentence or phrase expressing a principle or ideal

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'sluagh-ghairm' (battle cry).
