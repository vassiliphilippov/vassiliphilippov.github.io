flicker

1. VERB

/ ˈflɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
to move or shift quickly and repeatedly back and forth or from side to side

Examples

Garth's glance began to flicker to his watch.
During the five-month lull, civil war smoldered and flickered throughout the Congo.

Synset Examples

the candle flickered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flutter - to move with a light, quick, wavering or flapping motion

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, possibly related to Middle English 'flikeren' (to flutter, flicker).
