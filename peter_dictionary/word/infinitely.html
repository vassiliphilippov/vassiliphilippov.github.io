infinitely

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnfənətli / (AmE), / ˈɪnfɪnətli / (BrE)
To an unlimited or immeasurable degree; without end or limit.

Examples

Rabbi Melzi smiled at him with infinitely old but merry eyes.

Synset Examples

there are infinitely many possibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endlessly - without an end; lasting forever
  • Synonym: forever - for all time; for always
  • Antonym: finitely - in a limited way; having limits

Etymology

From Latin infinitus (unbounded) via Late Latin infinitē (without limit).

2. ADV

/ ˈɪn.fə.nɪt.li / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.nɪt.li / (BrE)
To an unlimited or immeasurable degree; without bound or limit.

Examples

The foregoing, aided by several clues I 'll withhold to keep you on your toes, will pursue you with a tenacity worthy of Inspector Javert, but before they close in, gird yourselves, I repeat, for a vengeance infinitely more pitiless.

Synset Examples

he is infinitely wealthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundlessly - without boundaries; limitlessly
  • Synonym: endlessly - having or seeming to have no end; continually
  • Antonym: finitely - within limits or bounds

Etymology

From Latin infinitus (without limit) + English adverbial suffix -ly.
