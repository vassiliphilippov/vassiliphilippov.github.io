conjoin

1. VERB

/ kənˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to join together or combine; to make contact or come together

Examples

By "image "is meant not only a visual presentation, but also remembered sensations of any of the five senses plus the feelings which are immediately conjoined therewith.

Synset Examples

The two roads join here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge to form a single unit
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to divide

Etymology

From Latin 'conjungere' (to join together), from 'con-' (together) + 'jungere' (to join).
