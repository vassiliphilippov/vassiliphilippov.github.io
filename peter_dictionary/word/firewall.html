firewall

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwɔːl / (BrE)
(Colloquial, aviation) The application of maximum thrust, especially by advancing the throttle fully forward.

Examples

Greg slammed his throttle to the fire wall and rammed up the RPM, and the engine responded as if it had been waiting.

Synset Examples

he moved the throttle to the firewall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full throttle - at the maximum speed or power; maximum throttle opening

Etymology

Colloquial use from aviation, derived from the literal firewall in aircraft separating the engine from the cockpit, as the throttle was often pushed fully forward toward the firewall.
