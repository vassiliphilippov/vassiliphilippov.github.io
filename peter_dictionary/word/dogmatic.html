dogmatic

1. ADJ

/ dɔgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / dɒgˈmætɪk / (BrE)
Asserting opinions in an authoritative and inflexible way, often without proof.

Examples

Or a Protestant, or one of those amusing dogmatic atheists, or a strict orthodox Communist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertive - confidently stating opinions or claims
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas or opinions

Etymology

From late Latin dogmaticus, from Greek dogmatikos (disposed to teach or as a doctrine).
