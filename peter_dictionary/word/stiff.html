stiff

1. NOUN

/ stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
(informal) An ordinary man, especially a working-class person; a worker or laborer.

Examples

Sposato needed a front, some labor stiff with a clean record to act as business agent of the Redhook local.
With my gray hair and my weatherbeaten countenance I certainly looked the honest working stiff.

Synset Examples

a lucky stiff
a working stiff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worker - a person who works, especially manual or industrial labor
  • Synonym: laborer - a person doing unskilled manual work
  • Antonym: toff - a wealthy or upper-class person (informal, often contrasted)

2. ADJ

/ stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) formal and lacking in ease or naturalness; not relaxed or friendly.

Examples

The stiff figure in the corner no longer blocked his thoughts.

Synset Examples

a starchy manner
the letter was stiff and formal
his prose has a buckram quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starchy - very formal or prim in manner, especially excessively so

3. ADJ

/ stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
not able to move or be moved easily; difficult to bend, fold, or operate

Examples

In the stiff neutral lines of the telephone poles he saw the no-nonsense pen strokes of Aunt Jessica's letter.
His face was stiff with anger when they let go of his arms.

Synset Examples

a stiff hinge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible
  • Antonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking

Etymology

From Old English *stif* (rigid, inflexible), related to Dutch *stijf* and German *steif*.

4. ADJ

/ stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
(of a wind, drink, penalty, etc.) strong or powerful; having a high intensity.

Examples

"I have come to talk with you about the future of humor and comedy ", I told him, at which he started slightly, and then made us each a stiff drink, with a trembling hand.

Synset Examples

a stiff current
a stiff breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strong - having great power or intensity

5. ADJ

/ stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
not easily bent or changed in shape; rigid or difficult to move or bend.

Examples

Looked like a stiff breeze would blow him over.

Synset Examples

a rigid strip of metal
a table made of rigid plastic
a palace guardsman stiff as a poker
stiff hair
a stiff neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigid - not able to be bent; stiff
  • Antonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking

Etymology

From Old English *stif* (rigid, inflexible).

6. ADV

/ stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
used after an adjective to emphasize a strong degree; extremely.

Examples

Our people with the New York detectives said he was half dead, scared stiff and talking out loud that you'’d turned on him.

Synset Examples

bored stiff
frightened stiff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead - intensifier in expressions like 'dead tired', 'dead right'
