cheery

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəri / (BrE)
Bright, pleasant, and likely to make people feel cheerful or happy.

Examples

Out of compulsion to say something cheery, Ben Prime blurted, "Well, we were lucky to be on soft ground when the first floodheads hit.

Synset Examples

a cheery hello
a gay sunny room
a sunny smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening

Etymology

Formed from 'cheer' + '-y' suffix, meaning 'full of cheer'.
