calculating

1. ADJ

/ ˈkælkjəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkælkjʊleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
acting in a scheming and often shrewd or coldly clever way to achieve personal advantage; carefully planning actions for selfish benefit.

Examples

Adele, like Amy, the youngest of the Marches, was the rebellious, mischievous, rather calculating and ambitious one.

Synset Examples

the most calculating and selfish men in the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheming - given to making secret and elaborate plans to achieve one's aims
  • Synonym: manipulative - exercising control or influence over others in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • Antonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From the verb 'calculate', from Latin *calculare* (to count, reckon).
