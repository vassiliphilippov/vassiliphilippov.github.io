aspire

1. VERB

/ əˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / əˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to have a strong desire or ambition to achieve something significant or high; to aim for a lofty goal

Examples

Each aspired to be a god in human form, but with each it was a different kind of god.
This may not be the greatest but it certainly comes close to being the greatest lesson Sam Rayburn's career, up to this hour, teaches all of us who would aspire to distinction in political life under our processes of government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - to want something strongly
  • Synonym: yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something

Etymology

From Latin 'aspirare' (to breathe upon, to strive for), from 'ad-' (toward) + 'spirare' (to breathe).
