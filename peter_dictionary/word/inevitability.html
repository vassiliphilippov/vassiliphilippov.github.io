inevitability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌɛvɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌɛvɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being certain to happen and impossible to avoid.

Examples

He was closer to understanding it in English now, although it could never have the inevitability of the Martian concept it stood for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the state of being completely confident or having no doubt about something
  • Antonym: contingency - a future event or circumstance that is possible but cannot be predicted with certainty

Etymology

From inevitable + -ity; ultimately from Latin *inevitabilis* (unavoidable) + -itas (suffix forming nouns).
