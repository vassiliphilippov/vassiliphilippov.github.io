biting

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of remarks, criticism, etc.) sharply hurtful, caustic, or cutting; capable of wounding emotionally or intellectually.

Examples

I must say, however, that I preferred the acting that had something of a biting edge to it.
A biting, pithy parable of the all pervading hollowness of modern life, the piece has been set by Mlle Lagoon to a sumptuous score (a single motif played over and over by four thousand French horns) by existentialist hot-shot Jean-Paul Sartre.

Synset Examples

a barbed compliment
a biting aphorism
pungent satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - severely critical or sarcastic (of remarks)
  • Synonym: scathing - witheringly harsh or critical (of remarks)
  • Antonym: gentle - kind and mild, not harsh or severe (of remarks)

Etymology

Present participle of 'bite', from Old English 'bītan'. Used figuratively for painful sharpness of speech since the 16th century.
