relief

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlif / (AmE), / rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
a person who replaces another in a job, game, or other role, especially to provide rest or support in a difficult situation

Examples

Bobby Waters of Sylvania, Ga., relief quarterback for the San Francisco 49ers of the National Football League, will undergo a knee operation tomorrow at Franklin Hospital here.
"Sir, Vaughn knows better than to leave the station without a relief.

Synset Examples

the star had a stand-in for dangerous scenes
we need extra employees for summer fill-ins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who substitutes for another, especially in acting or other roles
  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or used in place of another
  • Synonym: replacement - a person who takes over another's role or position

Etymology

From Old French *relief*, from Latin *relevare* (to raise, lighten, relieve).
