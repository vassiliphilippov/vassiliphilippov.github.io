livelihood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪvliˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvlihʊd / (BrE)
A means of securing the necessities of life, such as food, shelter, and income; a way of earning money in order to live.

Examples

Hino was the fourth son of an elderly farmer who lived on the coast, in Chiba, and divided his life between the land and the sea, supplementing the marginal livelihood on his small rented farm with seasonal employment on a fishing boat.

Synset Examples

each child was expected to pay for their keep
he applied to the state for support
he could no longer earn his own livelihood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employment - paid work or occupation
  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or investments
  • Antonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job or means of earning a living

Etymology

From Middle English 'lyvelood', alteration of 'liflode' meaning 'course of life, sustenance', from Old English 'līflād' (life + way).
