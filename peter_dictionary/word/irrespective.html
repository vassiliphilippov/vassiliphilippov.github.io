irrespective

1. ADV

/ ɪrɪˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪrɪˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
Without taking something into account; regardless.

Examples

And irrespective of the outcome in centuries elapsed since splitting, calculations obviously carry more concordant and comparable meaning if they deal with the most stable units than with variously unstable ones.

Synset Examples

he carried on regardless of the difficulties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regardless - without paying attention to circumstances or consequences

Etymology

From Latin 'ir-' (not) + 'respective', from 'respect', meaning heed or regard.
