secondhand

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsekəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of goods) previously owned or used by someone else.

Examples

You can keep your machinery investment down by buying good secondhand machinery, by sharing the cost and upkeep of machinery with a neighbor, and by hiring someone with machinery to do certain jobs.

Synset Examples

bought a secondhand (or used) car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: used - (of goods) not new; previously owned or utilized
  • Antonym: new - (of goods) not previously owned or used

Etymology

From the phrase 'at second hand', originally meaning obtained from another person, attested from the late 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of information or accounts) not obtained directly from the original source; derived from someone else's report or experience.

Examples

For hundreds of years, the evidence available consisted of (1) the captain's fragmentary journal, (2) a highly prejudiced account by one of the survivors, (3) a note found in a dead man's desk on board, and (4) several second-hand reports.

Synset Examples

a secondhand report
a secondhand account of a memory of something once read
most of our knowledge is secondhand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firsthand - direct from the original source; experienced personally

Etymology

From 'second' (following the first in sequence) + 'hand' (person involved in action); originally relating to goods, later applied to information.

3. ADV

/ ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
By indirect means; not directly from the original source.

Examples

Information beyond nearest neighbor is carried second -, third -, and fourth hand as a distortable rumor.

Synset Examples

I heard about it only secondhand or thirdhand
he prefers to buy secondhand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firsthand - directly from the original source

Etymology

From 'second hand', originally meaning having had a previous owner, later extended to mean indirect or not directly from the source.
