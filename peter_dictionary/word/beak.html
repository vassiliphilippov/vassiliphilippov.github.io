beak

1. NOUN

/ bik / (AmE), / biÀêk / (BrE)
The hard, pointed, projecting mouth part of a bird, used for pecking, eating, and other functions.

Examples

Just enough to wet my beak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - another word for a bird's beak, especially in some species

Etymology

From Middle English 'bek', from Old French 'bec', from Latin 'beccus'.
