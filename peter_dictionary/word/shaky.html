shaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪki / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪki / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) likely to tremble, wobble, or collapse; not firm, strong, or well-constructed.

Examples

He was a little pale and shaky.

Synset Examples

a rickety table
a wobbly chair with shaky legs
the ladder felt a little wobbly
the bridge still stands though one of the arches is wonky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rickety - (of an object or structure) poorly made and likely to collapse
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established

Etymology

From shake + -y, dating to the late 16th century.
