Related Terms

Etymology

From Old English boga (arch, bend), referring to the curved shape of the knot.

2. NOUN

/ boʊ / (AmE), / bəʊ / (BrE)
A slightly curved stick with taut horsehair, used to play musical notes on stringed instruments such as the violin or cello.

Examples

That is, when Mr. Milstein thrust straight to the core of the music, sparks flying, bow shredding, violin singing, glittering and sometimes spitting, Mr. Hendl could go along.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violin bow - bow specifically for a violin

Etymology

From Old English *boga* (bow, arch), related to bending shape; specialized for musical use in the 18th century.

5. NOUN

/ boʊ / (AmE), / bəʊ / (BrE)
A weapon for shooting arrows, consisting of a curved piece of resilient wood with a taut cord connecting its ends, used to propel arrows.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crossbow - a weapon with a horizontal bow mounted on a stock

Etymology

Old English boga (arch, anything bent or arched); related to Old Norse bogi, German Bogen.
