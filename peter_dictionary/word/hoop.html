hoop

1. NOUN

/ huːp / (AmE), / huːp / (BrE)
A rigid circular band of metal, wood, or other material used for holding, fastening, or encircling objects.

Examples

At the slope's base coils of concertina stretched out of eye range like a wild tangle of children's hoops, stopped simultaneously, weirdly poised as if awaiting the magic of the child's touch to start them all rolling again.

Synset Examples

there was still a rusty iron hoop for tying a horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - circular band, often made of metal, used as an ornament or fastening

Etymology

From Old English 'hōp' (ring, band), origin uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ hup / (AmE), / huːp / (BrE)
a light, curved framework worn under a skirt to make it spread out; especially used in women's fashion in the 18th and 19th centuries

Examples

Maude swooped up the cup and hiked up her top hoop as if about to take off with a racing start.
