elegant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛlɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡənt / (BrE)
Pleasantly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner; showing refined taste or simplicity.

Examples

Promptly at one-thirty he entered Hohlbein and Garth's elegant suite of offices in Medfield's newest professional building.
"I 'll get an elegant pair of thin soled Italian shoes tomorrow, Carla ".

Synset Examples

elegant handwriting
an elegant dark suit
she was elegant to her fingertips
small churches with elegant white spires
an elegant mathematical solution--simple and precise and lucid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - having elegance or beauty of form, manner, or movement
  • Synonym: refined - with elegant and cultured manners or taste
  • Synonym: tasteful - showing good aesthetic judgement or appropriate style
  • Antonym: clumsy - lacking grace or elegance in movement or posture
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill in manner or movement

Etymology

From Latin 'elegans', 'elegantis' (tasteful, choice, fine, select).
