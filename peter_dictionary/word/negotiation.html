negotiation

1. NOUN

/ nəˌɡoʊʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɪˌɡəʊʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
formal discussions between two or more parties with the aim of reaching an agreement

Examples

Then, abandoning the studies in the face of their promising outlook for all concerned, B. + O. entered on-again-off-again negotiations with C. + O. which resulted in the present situation.
In wage negotiations, the industry bargains as a unit with a single union.
He said that you were being tough on the negotiations but if they could get a little help and close the deal fast, it...

Synset Examples

the buyout negotiation lasted several days
they disagreed but kept an open dialogue
talks between Israelis and Palestinians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargaining - discussion aimed at reaching agreement, especially on price or conditions

Etymology

From Latin negotiatio (business, negotiation), from negotiari (to carry on business).
