adversity

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvɝːsəti / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
A difficult or unpleasant situation; a state of misfortune or hardship.

Examples

There are, however, some wonderful chapters at the beginning of the second part, concerning the reactions of the Swedes in adversity.

Synset Examples

debt-ridden farmers struggling with adversity
a life of hardship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardship - severe suffering or privation
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being prosperous or successful

Etymology

From Latin 'adversitas', from 'adversus' meaning 'turned toward, unfavorable'.
