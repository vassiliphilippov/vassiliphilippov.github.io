overcoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkəʊt / (BrE)
A long, heavy coat worn over other clothes, especially in cold weather.

Examples

My eyes were so bleary I could barely see him but there he was, a little smooth olivefaced guy in a new spring overcoat and a taffy colored fedora.
He had on his gray tweed overcoat and his city hat, and his brief case lay on the bench.
Hatless, in an overcoat of rough blue wool, I was given a proud farewell by my mother and father, and I set out into the strangely still streets of Brooklyn.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: topcoat - a lightweight coat worn over a suit or jacket
  • Degree Higher: greatcoat - a very heavy, long overcoat, often used historically by soldiers

Etymology

From 'over' + 'coat', indicating a coat worn over other garments. First recorded in English in the early 18th century.
