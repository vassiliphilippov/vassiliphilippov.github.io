preacher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpritʃər / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃə / (BrE)
a person, especially a member of the clergy, whose job is to deliver religious sermons or preach the gospel

Examples

For the old preacher who had been there twenty-five years was dead, and the city mourned him.
There was no church like Drew Church, no preacher like Papa, who was intimate with Him, and could consign sinners to hellfire.
"He'll make a preacher out of you "!
"Somebody's got to be a preacher in the family.
He started down the steps to meet the near-blind preacher, who had been one of the early Gospelers in Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy who leads religious services
  • Synonym: sermon - a religious speech or discourse delivered by a preacher

Etymology

From Middle English prechour, from Old French precheor, from Late Latin praedicātor (preacher).
