paramagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrəˌmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌparəˌmaɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) having or relating to the property of being weakly attracted by an externally applied magnetic field, forming internal, induced magnetic fields in the same direction as the applied magnetic field.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diamagnetic - not attracted to a magnetic field; tending to be repelled by it
  • Degree Higher: ferromagnetic - (of a substance) strongly attracted to a magnetic field

Etymology

From para- (beside, alongside) + magnetic, referring to alignment beside a magnetic field.
