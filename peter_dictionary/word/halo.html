halo

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊ / (BrE)
A circle or ring of light shown around the head of a holy person, especially in religious art, to signify sanctity.

Examples

There would be no halos, no angels.
Foster shook his halo.
Otherwise, straighten your halo, square your wings, and dig in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aureole - a radiant circle or disk around the head in religious art

Etymology

From Latin 'halōs', from Greek 'halōs' (threshing floor, disk, halo around the sun or moon).
