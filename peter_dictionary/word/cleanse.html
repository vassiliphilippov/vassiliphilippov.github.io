cleanse

1. VERB

/ ˈklɛnz / (AmE), / ˈklɛnz / (BrE)
to clean one's body or part of it thoroughly, especially by washing

Examples

He cleansed his mouth with a small quantity.

Synset Examples

clean up before you see your grandparents
clean your fingernails before dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make something free from dirt or impurities

Etymology

From Middle English clensen, from Old English clǣnsian, related to 'clean'.
