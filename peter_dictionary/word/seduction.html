seduction

1. NOUN

/ səˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
The act of enticing someone to behave in a way that is considered morally wrong or improper.

Examples

The casual seduction of a seventeen year old bluestocking seemed tame by comparison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temptation - the desire or act of tempting someone to do something wrong
  • Synonym: persuasion - the act of convincing someone to do something, often by appealing to emotions or desires
  • Antonym: resistance - the act of opposing or withstanding temptation or seduction

Etymology

From Latin seductio, from seducere meaning 'to lead aside'.
