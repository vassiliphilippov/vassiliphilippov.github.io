delighted

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
very pleased; feeling or showing great pleasure and satisfaction

Examples

I was delighted with Paula Prentiss' comedy performance, which was as fresh and unstilted as one's highest hopes might ask.
He was delighted to see them so happy.
He stroked the hens and they responded with delighted clucks, he gobbled with the turkeys and they at once were all attention, he quacked with the ducks, and cackled with a pair of exceedingly flattered geese.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pleased - feeling or showing pleasure and satisfaction
  • Degree Higher: thrilled - extremely pleased or excited
  • Antonym: displeased - not satisfied; feeling or showing annoyance

Etymology

From the verb 'delight', from Old French 'deliter', from Latin 'delectare' (to please, to charm).
