cryostat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.oʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.əʊ.stæt / (BrE)
An apparatus for maintaining very low temperatures, especially to preserve or process biological samples.

Examples

Root and stem tumors from sweet clover plants infected with WTV were quick-frozen in liquid nitrogen, embedded in ice, and cut at 3 - 6 | m in a cryostat maintained at - 16 ` to - 20 `.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (frost, cold) + 'stat' (standing, regulating).
