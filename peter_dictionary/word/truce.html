truce

1. NOUN

/ truːs / (AmE), / truːs / (BrE)
An agreement between enemies or opponents to stop fighting for a certain period, usually so that negotiations can take place.

Examples

So, truce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armistice - an agreement made by opposing sides in a war to stop fighting for a certain time
  • Synonym: ceasefire - an order or agreement to stop fighting temporarily
  • Antonym: war - a state of armed conflict between different countries or groups

Etymology

From Old French truce, treues, plural of treue (agreement, assurance), from Medieval Latin treuga.
