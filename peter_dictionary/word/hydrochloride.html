hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A compound formed when an organic base combines with hydrochloric acid, commonly used in pharmacy and chemistry.

Examples

As an aid in reducing incidence and severity of bloat, provide 75 milligrams of oxytetracycline hydrochloride per animal daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloride - a compound of chlorine with another element or group

Etymology

From hydro- (water) + chloride, indicating a compound with hydrochloric acid.
