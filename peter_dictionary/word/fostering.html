fostering

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːstərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒstərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of encouraging or promoting the development or growth of something.

Examples

The Roman Catholic natural-law tradition regards as self-evident that the primary objective purpose of the conjugal act is procreation and that the fostering of the mutual love of the spouses is the secondary and subjective end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope; synonym for fostering in this sense

Etymology

From foster (verb), from Old English *fostrian* (to nourish, rear).
