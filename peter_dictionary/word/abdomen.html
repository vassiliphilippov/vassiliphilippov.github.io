abdomen

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdoʊmən, ˈæbdəmən / (AmE), / ˈæbdəmən, æbˈdəʊmən / (BrE)
the part of the body between the chest and the pelvis, containing the stomach, intestines, and other organs

Examples

his quill drawings had a scratchiness, suggesting skin texture; the clay he used plastically to suggest soft moving flesh, as in an abdomen, in a reclining torso; the wax he smoothed over to give the body surface an elastic pull.
It was a fine broody hen, white, with a maternal eye and a striking abundance of feathers in the under region of the abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belly - the front part of the human trunk below the ribs
  • Synonym: stomach - the organ in the abdomen where food is digested or the area on the front of the body below the chest
  • Antonym: thorax - the part of the body between the neck and the abdomen
  • Antonym: pelvis - the lower part of the trunk of the human body between the abdomen and the thighs

Etymology

From Latin abdomen (belly, paunch).
