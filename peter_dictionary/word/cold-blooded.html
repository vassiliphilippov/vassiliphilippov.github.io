cold-blooded

1. ADJ

/ koʊld ˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or action) showing no emotion or pity; cruel and without remorse.

Examples

His name became synonymous with cold-blooded cruelty.
This was revenge... of a much more brutal and cold-blooded nature.

Synset Examples

in cold blood
cold-blooded killing
insensate destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others
  • Antonym: compassionate - feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others; the opposite of cold-blooded

Etymology

From 'cold' + 'blooded', originally used with reference to animals whose body temperature is not regulated internally; later used metaphorically for lack of emotion.
