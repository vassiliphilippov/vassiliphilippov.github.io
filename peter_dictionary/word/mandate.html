mandate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
an official order or document giving authority or instruction to act in a particular way

Examples

General Motors comprehensively contended that the Government plan would not be "in the public interest "as required by the mandate of this Court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Synonym: directive - an official instruction or order
  • Antonym: prohibition - an official ban or forbidding order

Etymology

From Latin mandatum (command, order), from mandare (to command).

2. VERB

/ ˈmænˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
to assign (a territory or colony) to be administered by another country or authority under a mandate, especially as authorized by an international body

Examples

The Congo should have been mandated, because it was not ready for independence.

Synset Examples

mandate a colony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administer - to manage or supervise affairs of a territory or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'mandatum' (command, order), from 'mandare' (to order, commit to one's charge).
