uninitiate

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪˈnɪʃiət / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈnɪʃɪət / (BrE)
A person who has not been introduced to or does not have experience in a particular field or activity.

Examples

This may both divert the attention of the uninitiate and cause confusion for the more knowledgeable.

Synset Examples

it diverts the attention of the uninitiate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: initiate - a person who has been admitted to knowledge of a particular field or activity

Etymology

From un- (not) + initiate (from Latin 'initiare' meaning to begin or admit).
