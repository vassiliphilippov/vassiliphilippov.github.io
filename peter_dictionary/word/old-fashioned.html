old-fashioned

1. ADJ

/ oʊld ˈfæʃənd / (AmE), / əʊld ˈfæʃənd / (BrE)
Not in style or fashion; belonging to an earlier time and considered no longer modern.

Examples

An old-fashioned mother said to her modern daughter, "You must have gotten in quite late last night, dear.
He'’s old-fashioned.

Synset Examples

a suit of rather antique appearance
demode (or outmoded) attire
outmoded ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdated - no longer current or fashionable
  • Antonym: modern - characteristic of the present or recent times
  • Synonym: antique - belonging to an earlier period, often valuable
  • Antonym: contemporary - living or occurring at the same time, especially present day
