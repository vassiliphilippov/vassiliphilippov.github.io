wandering

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a group, people, or animal) tending to move from place to place without a fixed home; migratory or nomadic.

Examples

Old Order, acted and atonally sung by Grunnfeu Arapacis, the lovely Serbantian import, then entered and delivered the well-known invocation to the god Phineoppus, whereupon the stage is quite unexpectedly visited by a company of wandering Gorshek priests, symbolizing Love, Lust, Prudence and General Motors, respectively.
This is no longer a way of life for the bearded logger and the wandering cowboy.

Synset Examples

a restless mobile society
the nomadic habits of the Bedouins
believed the profession of a peregrine typist would have a happy future
wandering tribes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - moving from place to place rather than settling permanently
  • Antonym: settled - not moving from place to place; having a fixed residence

Etymology

Present participle of 'wander,' from Old English 'wandrian' (to move about, wander).
