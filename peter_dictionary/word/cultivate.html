cultivate

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌl.tɪ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
to try to develop or improve something through attention, care, or effort; to foster the growth of (a quality, skill, relationship, etc.)

Examples

So Prokofieff was able to cultivate his musical talents and harvest a rich reward from them.
The psychological strategists in Taiwan stress the great sense of family, cultivated in China over thousands of years.
Mr. Kennedy did not neglect to cultivate the personal friendship of reporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurture - to care for and encourage the growth or development of
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Latin *cultivare* (to till, to tend), from *cultus* (cultivated, cared for).

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌl.tɪ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
to prepare and work on land in order to grow crops; to improve and fertilize soil for planting

Examples

For larger plantings, you 'll need some kind of power for plowing, harrowing, disking, and cultivating.

Synset Examples

Work the soil
cultivate the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: till - to prepare and cultivate land for crops
  • Synonym: plow - to turn over the soil with a plow as part of preparing land for crops

Etymology

From Latin 'cultivare', from 'cultus' (cultivated, tilled), from 'colere' (to till, cherish).
