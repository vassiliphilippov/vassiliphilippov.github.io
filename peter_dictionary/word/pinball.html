pinball

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A game in which a ball is propelled on a sloping board, hitting various obstacles and scoring points, typically played on a coin-operated machine.

Examples

Now, you no doubt regard the preceding as pap; you 're tooling around full of gage in your hot rods, gorging yourselves on pizza and playing pinball in the taverns and generally behaving like Ubermenschen.

Etymology

From 'pin' + 'ball', referring to the pins and ball used in the game.
