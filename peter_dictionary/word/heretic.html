heretic

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrətɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛrətɪk / (BrE)
A person who holds religious beliefs that are in opposition to the official doctrine of a church, especially the Roman Catholic Church.

Examples

"Heretic "!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodox - following traditional or established beliefs, especially in religion

Etymology

From Greek hairetikos (able to choose), via Latin haereticus (heretical person).
