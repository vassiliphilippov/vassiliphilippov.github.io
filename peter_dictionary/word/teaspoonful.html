teaspoonful

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːˌspuːnfʊl / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌspuːnfʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to what a teaspoon can hold, used especially as a standard measure in cooking or medicine.

Examples

Harold put a teaspoonful of powdered coffee in his cup and filled it with hot water, and then, stirring, he sat back in his chair.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tablespoonful - an amount equal to what a tablespoon can hold

Etymology

From 'teaspoon' + 'ful', indicating the content held by a teaspoon.
