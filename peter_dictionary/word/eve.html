eve

1. NOUN

/ iv / (AmE), / iːv / (BrE)
The day or period immediately before an event or occasion, especially a significant one.

Examples

The story opens on the eve of his fifty-third birthday, as he prepares for the two weeks of festivities that are to follow.
On the eve of his return to their native Naxos he speaks with his wife of the masterpiece which rises before them in its completed perfection.
It'’s New Year's Eve. Come on, just for a minute.

Synset Examples

he always arrives on the eve of her departure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening - the period of time at the end of the day, usually from about 6 p.m. to bedtime

Etymology

From Old English ǣfen (evening), later influenced by French 'ève'.
