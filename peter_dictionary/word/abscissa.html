abscissa

1. NOUN

/ æbˈsɪs.ə / (AmE), / æbˈsɪs.ə / (BrE)
(In coordinate geometry) the horizontal coordinate or the distance of a point from the vertical (y) axis, usually measured along the x-axis.

Examples

Each point with abscissa t on the graph represents an intersection between C and **f.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordinate - vertical coordinate in geometry

Etymology

From New Latin, feminine of Latin 'abscissus' (cut off), from 'abscindere' (to cut off).
