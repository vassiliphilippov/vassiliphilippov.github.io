dynamite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnəmaɪt / (BrE)
A powerful explosive consisting of nitroglycerin absorbed in an inert material such as wood pulp, used for blasting in construction and mining.

Examples

In the search for oil and gas, we make similar waves under controlled conditions with dynamite and learn from them where there are buried rock structures favorable to the accumulation of these resources.

Etymology

Coined by Alfred Nobel from Ancient Greek dýnamis (power).

2. VERB

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnəmaɪt / (BrE)
to blow up or destroy with dynamite

Examples

In Nashville, a school was dynamited.

Synset Examples

The rock was dynamited

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow up - to cause to explode or be destroyed by explosion

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamis' (power); the noun was coined by Alfred Nobel for the explosive, and then verb use followed.
