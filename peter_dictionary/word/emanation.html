emanation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of emitting or something that is emitted or flows out from a source

Examples

He opened it a crack and in doing so made as much shuffling, coughing, and scraping noise as possible in order to drown emanations from the hen who had begun to protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emission - the act of sending out gas, heat, light, etc.
  • Synonym: effluence - the act of flowing out, or something that flows out

Etymology

From Latin emanatio (a flowing out), from emanare (to flow out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a substance, quality, or influence that is emitted or radiated from a source, such as light, gas, or an abstract quality.

Examples

Law was seen as an emanation of the "sovereign will ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhalation - something exhaled or given off, especially as vapor or gas
  • Synonym: radiation - the emission of energy as electromagnetic waves or as moving subatomic particles

Etymology

From Latin emanatio (a flowing out), from emanare (to flow out).
