trimming

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
an embellishment or decoration added to something, such as clothing, food, or objects, often to make it more attractive or festive

Examples

There seems to be an unfortunate assumption that an hour of Chicago style jazz in prime evening time, for example, could not be justified without the trimmings of a portentous documentary.

Synset Examples

the children had to be in bed before it was time for the trimming of the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornament - a decoration added to something to make it more attractive
  • Synonym: adornment - something that decorates or adds beauty

Etymology

From the verb 'trim' (to decorate or adorn), which derives from Middle English 'trimmen' (to strengthen, make ready).
