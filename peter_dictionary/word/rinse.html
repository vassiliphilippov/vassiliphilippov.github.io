rinse

1. VERB

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)
To clean something quickly, lightly, or with a liquid, especially to remove soap, dirt, or a residue after washing.

Examples

Rinsing the sections with PBS before layering the intermediate sera did not improve the staining reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean with water or another liquid, often using soap

Etymology

Probably from French 'rincer', from Vulgar Latin '*recentiare' (to wash out).

2. VERB

/ rɪns / (AmE), / rɪns / (BrE)
To wash something using clean water in order to remove soap, dirt, or residue.

Examples

Clean it meticulously in boiling water and detergent, rinse thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean something using water or other liquid, often with soap

Etymology

From Old French *rincer*, from Vulgar Latin *recentiare*, from Latin *recentare* (to make fresh).
