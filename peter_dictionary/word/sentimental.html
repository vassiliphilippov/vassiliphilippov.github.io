sentimental

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛntəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
Having or showing tender, emotional feelings, often excessively or in a way that may seem insincere or overly nostalgic.

Examples

The sentimental pure heart of Galahad is gone with the knightly years, but I still believe in the heart of the George Meredith character that was not made of the stuff that breaks ".
I have a sentimental weakness for my children, and I spoil them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotional - showing strong feelings
  • Antonym: unsentimental - not influenced by emotions or sentimentality
  • Degree Higher: maudlin - self-pityingly or tearfully sentimental
  • Degree Lower: cold - lacking emotion, feeling, or sympathy

Etymology

From French *sentimental*, from Medieval Latin *sentimentalis*, from Latin *sentire* (to feel).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.t̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmɛn.təl / (BrE)
(especially of people or artworks) marked by excessive or insincere emotion, often in a way that is self-indulgent or lacking in genuine feeling.

Examples

Only the vain and incurably sentimental among us will lose sleep simply because foreign peoples are not as impressed by our strength as they ought to be.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maudlin - overly sentimental, especially in a self-pitying way
  • Synonym: schmaltzy - excessively sentimental, especially in art or music
  • Antonym: unemotional - lacking emotion or not showing feelings

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *sentimentalis* (pertaining to feeling), from Latin *sentire* (to feel).
