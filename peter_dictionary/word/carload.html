carload

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːˌləʊd / (BrE)
A group of people or amount of goods that fills a car, especially an automobile.

Examples

One afternoon, as the women sat clucking softly, a new carload of people pulled up at the gate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truckload - amount that fills a truck
  • Synonym: busload - number of people or amount of things that fill a bus

Etymology

Formed from 'car' + 'load', modeled after other combinations indicating a capacity or amount.
