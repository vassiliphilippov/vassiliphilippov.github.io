bias

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.əs / (BrE)
A strong feeling in favor of or against one group of people or one side in an argument, often not based on fair judgement; a tendency to prefer or oppose something or someone in a way that prevents objective consideration.

Examples

The last two writers introduce strong political bias into their works, and not unlike the union leaders that we will discuss soon, see folklore as a reservoir of protest by a downtrodden and publically silenced mass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - an unreasonable dislike or preference for a person, group, custom, etc.
  • Antonym: impartiality - fairness; lack of bias or prejudice

Etymology

From Old French *biais* (slant), possibly ultimately from Greek *epikarsios* (oblique, slanting).
