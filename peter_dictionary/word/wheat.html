wheat

1. NOUN

/ wiːt / (AmE), / wiːt / (BrE)
A cereal plant of the grass family, grown chiefly for its edible grain used in food products such as flour, bread, and pasta.

Examples

With these completed and ice gone from the St. Peter's River (present-day Minnesota river) their 250 bushels of wheat, 100 bushels of oats and barley and 30 bushels of peas and some chickens were loaded onto the flat-bottomed boats and rowed up the river to Big Stone Lake, across into Lake Traverse, and down the Red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - cereal plant with similar uses
  • Synonym: rye - grass cultivated for its grain

Etymology

Old English *hwǣte*, related to 'white' due to the color of the flour.
