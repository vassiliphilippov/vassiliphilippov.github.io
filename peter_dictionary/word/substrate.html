substrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.streɪt / (BrE)
a substance on which an enzyme acts during a chemical reaction

Examples

Wet cleaning involves an aqueous medium, a solid substrate, soil to be removed, and the detergent or surface-active material.
This appears to result from both a reduced amount of the iodine substrate and a more rapid secretion of newly iodinated thyroglobulin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactant - a substance that takes part in and undergoes change during a reaction

Etymology

From Latin substratum, from sub- (below) + stratum (something spread or laid down)
