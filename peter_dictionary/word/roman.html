Roman

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of ancient Rome or its people

Examples

Jacopo Galli introduced him into several Roman homes.
He was able to discern the body lines of the Roman women under their robes.

Synset Examples

Roman virtues
his Roman bearing in adversity
a Roman nose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Greek - relating to ancient Greece or its people

Etymology

From Latin Romanus, meaning 'of Rome'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of the city of Rome, especially in modern times.

Examples

He saw the smug eyes of the Home Army chief, Roman, and all the Romans and the faces of the peasants who held only hatred for him.
