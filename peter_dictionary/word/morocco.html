morocco

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / məˈrɒkəʊ / (BrE)
A soft, flexible, and durable leather with a pebbled grain, made from goatskin and traditionally used for book bindings, shoes, and other items of fine craftsmanship.

Examples

These narratives of coarse action and crude language appeared first in local newspapers, as a rule, and later found their way between book covers, though rarely into the planters' libraries beside the morocco bound volumes of Horace, Mr. Addison, Mr. Pope, and Sir Walter Scott.

Etymology

From the country name Morocco, as the leather was originally imported from there.
