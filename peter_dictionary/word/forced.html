forced

1. ADJ

/ fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
(of a process or motion) produced by or involving the application of force, especially in contrast to that which occurs naturally.

Examples

In plasma generators the superimposed forced convection may modify the picture somewhat.

Synset Examples

forced-air heating
furnaces of the forced-convection type
forced convection in plasma generators

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - occurring without artificial or external force; opposite of forced, esp. in technical contexts

Etymology

Past participle of 'force', from Old French *forcer*, from Late Latin *fortiare*, from Latin *fortis* (strong).

2. ADJ

/ fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
done or compelled by force, not voluntary; resulting from pressure or coercion rather than free choice.

Examples

Heydrich, in opening the Conference, followed the reasoning and even the phraseology of the order issued earlier by Goering which authorized the Final Solution as "a complement to "previous "solutions "for eliminating the Jews from German living space through violence, economic strangulation, forced emigration, and evacuation.
Safe products are flying in as Gotham City goes on a forced fast.

Synset Examples

promised to abolish forced labor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - done by free will, not forced
  • Synonym: compulsory - required by law or rule; obligatory
  • Synonym: coerced - persuaded or forced to do something by use of threats or pressure

Etymology

From the verb 'force', which comes from Old French 'forcer', from Latin 'fortia', meaning 'strong' or 'force'.
