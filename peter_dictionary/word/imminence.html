imminence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪm.ə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈɪm.ɪ.nəns / (BrE)
The state or quality of being about to happen; the fact that something is likely to occur very soon.

Examples

In agriculture, for example, despite the advances in biology, elaborate rituals tend to persist along with a continued sense of the imminence of some natural disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediacy - the quality of being immediate or directly about to occur
  • Antonym: remoteness - the fact of being distant in time; the opposite of imminence

Etymology

From Latin imminentia (overhanging, impending), from imminēre (to overhang, impend).
