hackwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈhækˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈhækˌwɜːk / (BrE)
work produced mechanically and without inspiration, especially for payment rather than artistic merit

Examples

But it is characteristic of him, we are told, "his little artifice ", to be able to introduce "into a fairly vulgar and humorous piece of hackwork a sudden phrase of genuine creative art ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potboiler - a work created merely to make money
  • Antonym: masterpiece - a work of outstanding artistry or skill

Etymology

From 'hack' (a person who does routine work) + 'work'.
