dying

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
in the process of passing from life or coming to an end; approaching death or extinction

Examples

Without saying so, she was really grateful; for to attend the dying was something she had never experienced, and certainly had not imagined when she thought of the duties she would have as Bobby Joe's wife.
This, he was sure, was the way they would act; laughing at a dying man, laughing as a man was beaten to death.
Get busy living or get busy dying.

Synset Examples

a dying man
his dying wish
a dying fire
a dying civilization

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dead - having ceased to live; not alive
  • Antonym: living - still alive; not dead

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'die', from Old Norse 'deyja'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The ending or fading away of something, especially hopes, feelings, or a way of life.

Examples

And he knew in that moment, with a cold sinking of despair, a dying of old hopes, that Mae had spread some kind of word there among the neighbors.

Synset Examples

it was the death of all his plans
a dying of old hopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - The permanent end or cessation of life or existence
  • Synonym: demise - The end or failure of an enterprise or institution

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'die', from Old English *dīegan* (to die).
