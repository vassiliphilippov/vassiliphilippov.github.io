relearn

1. VERB

/ riˈlɝn / (AmE), / riˈlɜːn / (BrE)
to learn something again, especially after forgetting or neglecting it

Examples

"Usually at this point a man is withdrawn from society and one of my jobs is to see that he relearns to mingle with his fellow men ", Mrs. Marr explained.

Synset Examples

After the accident, he could not walk for months and had to relearn how to walk down stairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learn - to gain knowledge or skill by study or experience

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) + learn.
