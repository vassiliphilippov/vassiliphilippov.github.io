disbelieve

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsbɪˈliv / (AmE), / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to not accept something as true; to refuse to believe

Examples

Why do we not realize that no ideology believes so much in itself as it disbelieves in something else?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: believe - to accept that something is true

Etymology

Formed from 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'believe', with roots in Old English 'gelefan'.
