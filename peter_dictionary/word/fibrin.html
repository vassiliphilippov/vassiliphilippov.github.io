fibrin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbrɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪbrɪn / (BrE)
a white, insoluble fibrous protein formed during blood clotting, which helps to form a mesh that traps blood cells

Examples

Some of the small vessels were filled with fibrin thrombi, and there was extensive interstitial hemorrhage.
A section of the colon revealed intense hyperemia and extensive focal ulcerations of the mucosa, associated with much fibrin and many neutrophils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrinogen - the soluble blood plasma protein converted to fibrin during clotting

Etymology

From French 'fibrine', from Latin 'fibra' (fiber).
