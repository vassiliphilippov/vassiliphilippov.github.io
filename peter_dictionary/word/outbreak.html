outbreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌbreɪk / (BrE)
A sudden onset or occurrence, especially of something unwelcome such as disease or conflict.

Examples

They argue further (and somewhat contradictorily) that our knowledge and resources in preventive medicine would make it possible to control such an outbreak of disease.

Synset Examples

the outbreak of hostilities

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: epidemic - widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community

Etymology

From out + break; modeled after German Ausbruch.
