mixture

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪks.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪks.tʃə / (BrE)
A combination of different things or kinds, often forming a single whole.

Examples

The conversation of the characters creates an atmosphere suggesting the usual mixture of pleasures, foibles, irritations, and concerns which would characterize the common life of a normal village in any age.
The sections were mounted on cold slides smeared with Haupts' adhesive (Johansen, 1940) in earlier experiments, and in later experiments with a different mixture of the same components reported by Schramm and Rottger (1959).

Synset Examples

a great assortment of cars was on display
he had a variety of disorders
a veritable smorgasbord of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - a mixture in which substances are combined so that the constituent parts are indistinguishable
  • Antonym: pure - not mixed; free from any other element or substance

Etymology

From Latin 'mixtūra', from 'mixtus', past participle of 'miscēre' (to mix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪks.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɪks.tʃə / (BrE)
