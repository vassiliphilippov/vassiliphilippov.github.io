satiate

1. VERB

/ ˈseɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈseɪʃɪeɪt / (BrE)
to satisfy a desire or appetite fully; to fill until nothing more is wanted

Examples

The annual butchery of 40000 American men, women and children to satiate its blood-lust is excessive; a quota of 25000 a year would be more than sufficient.

Synset Examples

I am sated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfy - to fulfill a desire, need, or appetite
  • Synonym: fill - to make full; to occupy completely

Etymology

From Latin satiatus, past participle of satiare (to fill, satisfy), from satis (enough).
