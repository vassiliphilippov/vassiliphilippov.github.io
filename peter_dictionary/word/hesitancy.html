hesitancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛzɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈhɛzɪtənsi / (BrE)
A state of being uncertain or indecisive about what to do; reluctance or delay caused by doubt or lack of confidence.

Examples

He was about to hang up the phone, but a note of hesitancy in the secretary's voice left the conversation open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitation - the action or state of pausing before saying or doing something, typically through uncertainty
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling or belief that one can rely on someone or something; firm trust

Etymology

From Latin *haesitare* (to hesitate) + English suffix -ancy.
