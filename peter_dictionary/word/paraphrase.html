paraphrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌfreɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌfreɪz / (BrE)
A restatement of a text or passage using different words, typically for clarity or to aid understanding.

Examples

Do you want to hear the paraphrase "?
"Just barely ", he said, and repeated his paraphrase:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restatement - the act of expressing something in different words
  • Synonym: translation - the process of rendering text into another language (sometimes by analogy)

Etymology

From Greek paraphrasis 'a way of expressing in other words', from para- 'beside' + phrazein 'to tell'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpærəˌfreɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəfreɪz / (BrE)
to express the same meaning using different words, often to clarify the meaning

Examples

"I woke up this morning ", Moreland said, "paraphrasing Lewis Carroll.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reword - to state something again in different words
  • Degree Lower: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Greek 'paraphrasis' (additional expression), via Latin 'paraphrasis'.
