coffin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːfɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒfɪn / (BrE)
A long, narrow box in which a dead body is buried or cremated.

Examples

All of Poland is a coffin.
A couple of years back, I occupied a Village apartment whose outer staircase contained the type of niche called a "coffin turn ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casket - box for burying a dead body (especially in American English)

Etymology

From Old French 'cofin' (basket, chest), from Latin 'cophinus' (basket).
