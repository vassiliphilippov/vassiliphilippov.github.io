institutionalize

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.stəˈtuː.ʃə.nəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to send or commit someone to be cared for or confined in an institution such as a hospital, prison, or similar facility, especially involuntarily

Examples

She suffered a nervous breakdown and had to be institutionalized.

Synset Examples

After the second episode, she had to be committed
he was committed to prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commit - to send someone to an institution, such as a hospital or jail

Etymology

From institution + -alize, originally meaning 'to make into an institution,' later developing the sense of sending to an institution.
