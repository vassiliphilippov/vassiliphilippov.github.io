aperture

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpərtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈapətjʊə / (BrE)
A device or opening that controls the amount of light admitted, especially in cameras or optical instruments.

Examples

The shutter aperture may be made larger or smaller by changing the foil area and adjusting the electrical energy input to the foil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris diaphragm - mechanism that controls aperture size in cameras/microscopes

Etymology

From Latin 'apertura' (an opening), from 'aperire' (to open).
