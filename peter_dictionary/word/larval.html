larval

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑr.vəl / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.vəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the immature form of an animal, especially an insect or amphibian, in its early developmental stage before metamorphosis.

Examples

But they are still enclosed in their larval cells and remain there throughout the summer, fall, and winter.

Synset Examples

larval societies
larval crayfishes
the larval stage

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pupal - relating to the pupa stage between larva and adult in insects
  • Degree Higher: adult - fully mature form of an organism after metamorphosis

Etymology

From Latin 'larva' (ghost, mask; later used in scientific Latin for insect stage) + '-al'.
