Missouri

1. PROPN

/ mɪˈzʊəri / (AmE), / mɪˈzʊəri / (BrE)
A state in the central United States, known as a border state during the American Civil War.

The principal speaker will be Senator Stuart Symington, Democrat of Missouri.

Etymology

Named after the Missouri River, which is of Siouan origin (*ouemessourita*: 'people with wooden canoes').

2. PROPN

/ mɪˈzʊri / (AmE), / mɪˈzʊəri / (BrE)
the longest river in the United States, rising in Montana and flowing southeast to join the Mississippi River at St. Louis

Examples

A river, wide as the Missouri, where it ran by his place.

Synset Examples

The Missouri and Mississippi Rivers together form the third longest river in the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mississippi - a major river in North America that the Missouri joins at St. Louis

Etymology

Named after the Missouri people, from an Algonquian language via French.
