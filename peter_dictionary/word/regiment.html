regiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒəmənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪmənt / (BrE)
A military unit that is typically composed of several battalions and is smaller than a division.

Examples

Adam thought of the hutments, regiment after regiment, row after row, the thousands of huts, stretching away into the night.
Adam thought of the hutments, regiment after regiment, row after row, the thousands of huts, stretching away into the night.
The latter, members of two regiments of Swiss mercenaries transported by Great Britain to Canada to fight the Americans in the War of 1812, had settled in Montreal and Kingston at the close of the war in 1815.
His head cannot contain enough sense to command a regiment, much less a corps.
The truth is, however, that when Mel Chandler first reported to the regiment the only steed he had ever ridden was a swivel chair and the only weapon he had ever wielded was a pencil.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: battalion - an army unit smaller than a regiment
  • Degree Higher: division - an army unit larger than a regiment

Etymology

From Latin 'regimentum' (rule, government), from 'regere' (to rule).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛdʒ.əˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒ.ɪ.mənt / (BrE)
to organize and control strictly, especially by imposing uniformity and discipline

Examples

We are slowly being regimented to having everything packaged, whether we want it or not.

Synset Examples

regiment one's children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organize - to arrange systematically; order
  • Synonym: discipline - to train or control by enforcing rules
  • Antonym: free - not under control or restriction

Etymology

From Latin 'regimentum' (rule, guidance), related to 'regere' (to rule).
