northerly

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrðərli / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðəli / (BrE)
Located in or facing toward the north; situated to the north.

Examples

In 1819 grasshoppers again destroyed the crop at "the Forks " (Fort Douglas) and in December 1819, twenty men left Fort Daer for the most northerly American outpost at Prairie du Chien.

Synset Examples

the northern suburbs
going in a northerly direction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southerly - situated in or oriented toward the south
  • Synonym: northern - situated in or toward the north

Etymology

From Middle English 'north' + '-erly', formed by analogy to 'easterly', 'southerly', etc.
