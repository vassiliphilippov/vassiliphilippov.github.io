sinister

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnɪstər / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
Suggesting or foreshadowing harm, evil, or misfortune; giving the impression that something bad or unpleasant will happen.

Examples

At the same time the multiple transvestitism involved - the fat man as girl and as baby, as coquette pretending to be a baby - touches for a moment horrifyingly upon the secret sources of a life like Jacoby's, upon the sinister dreams which form the sources of any human life.
His name is Praisegod Piepsam, and he is rather fully described as to his clothing and physiognomy in a way which relates him to a sinister type in the author's repertory - he is a forerunner of those enigmatic strangers in "Death in Venice ", for example, who represent some combination of cadaver, exotic, and psychopomp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
  • Synonym: menacing - suggesting the presence of danger; threatening
  • Antonym: benign - gentle and kindly, not harmful

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' (left, on the left side), later associated with evil or ill omen.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
Giving the impression that something harmful, evil, or wicked is happening or will happen.

Examples

And the forked tongue of gossip licked its sinister way from back porch to back porch.

Synset Examples

black deeds
a black lie
his black heart has concocted yet another black deed
Darth Vader of the dark side
a dark purpose
dark undercurrents of ethnic hostility
the scheme of some sinister intelligence bent on punishing him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malevolent - having or showing a wish to do evil to others
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm
  • Antonym: benign - gentle and kind, not harmful

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' meaning 'left, on the left side', later associated with inauspiciousness and evil.
