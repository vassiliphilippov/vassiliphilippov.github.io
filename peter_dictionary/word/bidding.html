bidding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving an authoritative command or instruction, especially to be obeyed by someone else

Examples

Even as the conviction of truth roared through him, shattering his last hope of safety, he was reaching to release the hand brake, to head up the road for home, doing her bidding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - an order given with authority
  • Synonym: order - an authoritative direction or instruction to do something
  • Degree Lower: request - an act of asking politely or formally for something

Etymology

From Middle English 'biding', from Old English 'bīdan' (to ask, command), related to 'bid'.
