self-determination

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.dɪˌtɜːr.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the right of a people or nation to govern itself without outside interference

Examples

Freedom, in the sense of self-determination, is a worthy objective; but if granting self-determination to the Algerian rebels entails sweeping that area into the Sino-Soviet orbit, then Algerian freedom must be postponed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomy - independence or freedom, as of the will or one's actions
  • Synonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority, especially of a state
  • Antonym: colonization - the action or process of settling among and establishing control over the indigenous people of an area

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'determination'; 'determine' from Latin 'determinare' (to enclose, to set limits to).
