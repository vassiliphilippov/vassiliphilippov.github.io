apology

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a statement expressing regret for having done something wrong or causing inconvenience; an admission of fault or an expression of remorse

Examples

I was having lunch not long ago (apologies to N. V. Peale) with three distinguished historians (one specializing in the European Middle Ages, one in American history, and one in the Far East), and I asked them if they could name instances where the general mores had been radically changed with "deliberate speed, majestic instancy " (Francis Thompson's words for the Hound of Heaven's pursuit) by judicial fiat.
This committee owes an apology!

Synset Examples

he wrote a letter of apology to the hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation put forward to defend or justify a fault or offense
  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment over something that has happened or been done

Etymology

From Late Latin apologia (a speech in defense), from Greek apologia (a speech in defense, an answer).
