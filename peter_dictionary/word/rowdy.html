rowdy

1. ADJ

/ ˈraʊdi / (AmE), / ˈraʊdi / (BrE)
noisy, rough, and likely to cause trouble or disturb others, especially in a public place.

Examples

In his teens O' Banion was enrolled in the vicious Market Street gang and he became a singing waiter in McGovern's Cafe, a notoriously low and rowdy dive in North Clark Street, where befuddled customers were methodically looted of their money by the singing waiters before being thrown out.

Synset Examples

a raucous party
rowdy teenagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raucous - making a disturbingly harsh and loud noise
  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful; rowdy
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; tranquil; quiet

Etymology

Probably from obsolete row (noisy disturbance) + -y.
