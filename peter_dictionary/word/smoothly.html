smoothly

1. ADV

/ ˈsmuðli / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðli / (BrE)
In a way that happens without problems, difficulties, or interruptions; in an even and continuous manner.

Examples

Linking the smoothly changing images together, the eye itself endows them with the illusion of movement.
Once her trembling hand, with the pen grasped tight in it, was pressed against the paper the words came sharply, smoothly, as authoritatively as they would dropping from her own lips.
A district station can n't run smoothly, unless "- He interrupted himself, looking around Gun at the doorway.

Synset Examples

put the plans into effect quickly and smoothly
despite of some mishaps, everything went swimmingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seamlessly - in a way that is smooth and without seams or interruptions
  • Antonym: roughly - in a way that is not smooth or is marked by problems

Etymology

From "smooth" (Old English smōth) + "-ly", forming the adverb.
