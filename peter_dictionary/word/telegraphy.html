telegraphy

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛɡrəfi / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛɡrəfi / (BrE)
the science or practice of transmitting messages over long distances by wire, especially using an electrical telegraph

Examples

Manchester's unusual interest in telegraphy has often been attributed to the fact that the Rev. J. D. Wickham, headmaster of Burr and Burton Seminary, was a personal friend and correspondent of the inventor, Samuel F. B. Morse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telecommunication - communication over a distance by cable, telegraph, telephone, or broadcasting

Etymology

From French 'télégraphie', based on Greek 'tele-' (far) + 'graphein' (to write).
