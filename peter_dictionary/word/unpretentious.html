unpretentious

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.prɪˈtɛn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prɪˈtɛn.ʃəs / (BrE)
Not attempting to impress others with an appearance of greater importance, talent, or culture than is actually possessed; simple and modest.

Examples

Leaving the theatre after the performance, I had a flash of intuition that life, after all (as Rilke said), is just a search for the nonexistent cup of hot coffee, and that this unpretentious, moving, clever, bitter slice of life was the greatest thing to happen to the American theatre since Brooks Atkinson retired.

Synset Examples

an unpretentious country church
her quiet unpretentious demeanor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modest - having or showing a moderate estimate of one's abilities; not boastful
  • Antonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by showing greater importance or merit than is actually possessed

Etymology

From un- (not) + pretentious, from French prétentieux, from prétention (pretension).
