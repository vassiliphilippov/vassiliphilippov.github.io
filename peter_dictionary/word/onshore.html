onshore

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌʃɔː / (BrE)
(of winds) blowing from the sea toward the land.

Examples

The spraying operation was conducted from the rear deck of a small Naval vessel, cruising two miles off-shore and vertical to an on-shore breeze.

Synset Examples

an inshore breeze
an onshore gale
sheltered from seaward winds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offshore - (of winds) blowing from the land toward the sea

Etymology

Formed from 'on' + 'shore'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɑnˌʃɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌʃɔː / (BrE)
(of movement or location) on or towards the land, especially from the sea.

Examples

The issue was settled on shore, Greene winning and Wilson remaining ashore, determined to catch the next fishing boat back to England.

Synset Examples

they were living onshore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offshore - away from or at a distance from the land, towards the sea
