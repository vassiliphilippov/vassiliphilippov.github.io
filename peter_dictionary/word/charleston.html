Charleston

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑːrlstən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlstə(n) / (BrE)
The capital city of West Virginia, located in the central part of the state on the Kanawha River.

Examples

Also, we should not even to-day discount the fact that a region such as the coastal lowlands centering on Charleston had closer ties with England and the West Indies than with the North even after independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charleston - port city in southeastern South Carolina, USA

Etymology

Named after Charles Clendenin, father of an early settler, or possibly after Charles Town in Virginia.
