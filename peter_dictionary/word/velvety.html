velvety

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛlvəti / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvəti / (BrE)
Having a smooth, soft texture, appearance, or sound like that of velvet.

Examples

Pansies are supposed to like it cool, but those great velvety flowers were healthy and perky in the glaring sun.
In response to the lengthening days of February they budded, then bloomed their 4 - inch velvety flowers.
A. armata is much more beautiful in color, at least in the female of the species: a rich, velvety, rusty red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky - having a smooth, soft texture like silk

Etymology

From 'velvet' + '-y', denoting 'having the quality of velvet'. 'Velvet' comes from Old French 'velu', meaning 'shaggy', and Medieval Latin 'villotus'.
