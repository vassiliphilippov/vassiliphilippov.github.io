accomplished

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɑmplɪʃt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmplɪʃt / (BrE)
Successfully completed or brought to an end.

Examples

That done, her work is accomplished and she ignores the plant.

Synset Examples

his mission accomplished he took a vacation
the completed project
the joy of a realized ambition overcame him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completed - finished; brought to an end
  • Antonym: unfinished - not completed; not brought to an end

Etymology

From accomplish + -ed, formed from Old French 'accomplir', from Latin 'ad' (to) + 'complere' (to fill up, complete).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkɑmplɪʃt / (AmE), / əˈkɒmplɪʃt / (BrE)
very skilled or highly trained in a particular art, activity, or field

Examples

When these groups were first formed many prominent and accomplished decorators could not have had the advantage of school training since interior design courses were rare and undeveloped during their youth.

Synset Examples

an accomplished pianist
a complete musician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proficient - skilled and competent in a particular field
  • Antonym: unskilled - lacking skill or expertise

Etymology

From Latin 'accomplere' (to complete) via Old French 'acomplir'.
