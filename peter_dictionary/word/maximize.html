maximize

1. VERB

/ ˈmæk.sɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make the best or most effective use of (something)

Examples

Since a national interpretation cannot be avoided it is unfortunate that the elections were not held in a way to maximize party responsibility and the educational effect of mass political participation.
We made the most of it.

Synset Examples

He maximized his role

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimize - make the best or most effective use of a situation or resource
  • Antonym: minimize - reduce to the smallest possible amount or make least use of

Etymology

From Latin 'maximus' (greatest), via English 'maximum.'

2. VERB

/ ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make something as large, great, or important as possible

Examples

A former du Pont official became a General Motors vice president and set about maximizing du Pont's share of the General Motors market.
For the industry of this model, the effect of such public pressures in the past has been to hold the price well below the short-run profit maximizing price (given the wage rate and the level of GNP), and even below the entry limited price (but not below average cost).

Synset Examples

Maximize your profits!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimize - to make something as small or unimportant as possible

Etymology

From Latin maximus (greatest) via English maximum + -ize.
