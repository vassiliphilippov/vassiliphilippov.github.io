iodine

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪəˌdin / (AmE), / ˈaɪədiːn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol I), a dark gray or black solid halogen used especially in medicine, photography, and dyes

Examples

The precise mechanism for organification of iodine in the thyroid is not as yet completely understood.
However, the formation of organically bound iodine, mainly mono-iodotyrosine, can be accomplished in cell-free systems.
In addition, depending on availability of dietary iodine, thyroglobulin may contain varying quantities of iodine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromine - a chemical element, halogen similar to iodine
  • Synonym: fluorine - a chemical element, halogen with similar properties
  • Synonym: chlorine - a chemical element, another halogen

Etymology

From French iode, from Greek ioeidēs (violet-colored), referring to its violet vapors.
