lustre

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
a thin, shiny coating applied to ceramics or porcelain to produce a metallic or iridescent effect

Examples

They come in crisp basket weaves in natural honey hues, along with lacy open weaves with a lustre finish in natural, white, black and a whole range of colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaze - a vitreous coating applied to pottery for decoration or waterproofing

Etymology

From Old French *lustre* (radiance), from Latin *lustrum* (purification).
