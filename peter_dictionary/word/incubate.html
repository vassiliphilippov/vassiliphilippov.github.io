incubate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbeɪt / (BrE)
to keep cells, eggs, bacteria, or other organisms under conditions that promote their growth or development, especially in a controlled environment

Examples

The saline and albumin tests were performed as described for the ABO samples except that the mixture was incubated for 1 hr at 37 ` C before centrifugation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culture - to grow microorganisms in a prepared medium

Etymology

From Latin incubare (to lie upon, brood), from in- (upon) + cubare (to lie).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪn.kjəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.kjʊ.beɪt / (BrE)
(of a bird or other animal) to sit on or keep (eggs) warm until they hatch.

Examples

The queen afterward keeps incubating and guarding her eggs like a mother hen, taking a sip from time to time from the rather liquid honey in her honey pots.

Synset Examples

Birds brood
The female covers the eggs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - to sit on eggs to hatch them (of birds)

Etymology

From Latin incubare (to lie upon, brood), from in- (upon) + cubare (to lie).
