flap

1. VERB

/ flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
to move with a rising and falling or back-and-forth motion, especially in a wavy or undulating way, as when something loose moves in the air or wind

Examples

- flapped as it ran.
He stopped automatically at the street corners, waiting for the traffic lights to change, unheeding of other people, his coat open and flapping.
Along about 4: 30, just when it was getting to be about time to turn the audience over and toast them on the other side, Judy came on singing, in a short-skirted blue dress with a blue and white jacket that flapped in the wind.
One medium saw two sheets flapping on a line and found that the name Shietz was significant to the sitter.

Synset Examples

The curtains undulated
the waves rolled towards the beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - move to and fro or up and down repeatedly
  • Synonym: flutter - move or fall with a light, irregular motion
  • Synonym: undulate - move with a smooth, wave-like motion

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound or action of flapping; first recorded in Middle English (14th century).

2. VERB

/ flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
to move up and down or back and forth with a sudden or noisy motion, especially of something loose or flexible

Examples

She went downstairs and received another curious shock, for when Glendora flapped into the dining room in her homemade moccasins, Sarah asked her when she had brought coffee to her room and Glendora said she had n't.
Glendora flapped away.

Synset Examples

flags flapped in the strong wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flutter - to move lightly and quickly from side to side or up and down

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound or motion; related to Middle English flappen, meaning 'to strike, hit'.

3. VERB

/ flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
(of wings or other flat objects) to move quickly up and down or back and forth, often producing a noise or as part of flying.

Examples

It was like some beautiful bird flapped into our drab little cage and made those walls dissolve away.

Synset Examples

The bird's wings were flapping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flutter - move with quick, light, or irregular motions

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of flapping.
