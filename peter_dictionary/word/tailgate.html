tailgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪlɡeɪt / (BrE)
The hinged rear door of a vehicle, such as a truck or station wagon, which can be lowered or opened for loading and unloading.

Examples

He let down the tailgate and was knocked over by the sluice of water.

Etymology

From 'tail' (the rear part) and 'gate' (a movable barrier), referring to its position and function.
