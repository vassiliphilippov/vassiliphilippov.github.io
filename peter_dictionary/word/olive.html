olive

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːlɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv / (BrE)
Of a yellowish-green or brownish-green color, like that of an unripe olive, especially used to describe skin complexion.

Examples

She smiled, and the teeth gleamed in her beautifully modeled olive face.
She was a living doll and no mistake - the blue-black bang, the wide cheekbones, olive flushed, that betrayed the Cherokee strain in her Midwestern lineage, and the mouth whose only fault, in the novelist's carping phrase, was that the lower lip was a trifle too voluptuous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sallow - of an unhealthy yellow or pale brown color
  • Synonym: tan - pale brown to brownish-yellow color, especially of skin

Etymology

From Old French olive, from Latin oliva (olive, olive tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑlɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪv / (BrE)
A small, oval fruit from the olive tree, typically green or black, eaten as food or used to produce olive oil.

Examples

We'’ll send him olive oil from here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive oil - oil pressed from olives, used in cooking and dressings

Etymology

From Latin oliva, from Greek elaia (olive tree).
