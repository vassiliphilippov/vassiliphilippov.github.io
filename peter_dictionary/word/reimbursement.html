reimbursement

1. NOUN

/ ˌriɪmˈbɝsmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈbɜːsmənt / (BrE)
the act of paying back money to someone for expenses, damages, or losses they have incurred

Examples

There shall be deducted from the amount of each payment made pursuant to subsection (c) of section 8, as reimbursement for the expenses incurred by the United States, an amount equal to 5 per centum of such payment.

Synset Examples

he received reimbursement for his travel expenses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - money or goods given to make up for a loss or injury

Etymology

From reimburse + -ment, from French rembourser (to repay).
