technician

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
A person whose job requires special training or skill in a particular technical process or field, especially involving the practical application of scientific or engineering principles.

Examples

During the night, a phone company technician had deadened the bells and installed red blinkers on the phones.
Annual authorizations of $ 15 million were added for area vocational education programs that meet national defense needs for highly skilled technicians.
The National Manpower Council of Columbia University has estimated that three out of five skilled workers and one out of five technicians have not been formally trained.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: engineer - person trained in engineering, typically with broader or more theoretical responsibilities than a technician
  • Degree Lower: mechanic - a person who repairs and maintains machinery, often with less formal training than a technician

Etymology

From French 'technicien', based on Greek 'technikós' (of art or skill).
