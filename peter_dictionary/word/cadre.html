cadre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdrə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
a small group of people specially trained for a particular purpose or as the core of a larger organization, especially political or military

Examples

The major studios maintain a cadre of film librarians and research specialists who look to this matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small group acting as a nucleus of a larger political movement

Etymology

From French cadre (frame, framework), from Italian quadro (square, frame), from Latin quadrum (square).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdrə / (BrE)
A core group of trained military personnel around which a larger group can be formed or expanded.

Examples

Its entire complement of non-commissioned officers on the platoon level had departed as cadre for another unit, and its vehicles were still those used in the drive across Luzon in World War 2,.

Etymology

From French *cadre* (framework), originally from Italian *quadro* (frame).
