sculpture

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptʃə / (BrE)
a work of art made by shaping or carving materials such as stone, metal, or wood into a three-dimensional form

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptura' (carving), from 'sculpere' (to carve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlp.tʃə / (BrE)
the art or practice of creating figures or designs in three dimensions, especially by carving stone or wood or by casting metal or plaster

Examples

Figurines and simple chapels presage the emergence of sculpture and architecture in Greece; objects in gold, ivory, and bronze grow more numerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carving - the act of shaping something by cutting
  • Synonym: modeling - forming a shape, as in sculpture, with a soft material
  • Antonym: painting - the art of applying pigment to a surface

Etymology

From Latin sculptura (carving), from sculpere (to carve).
