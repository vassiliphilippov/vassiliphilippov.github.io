enclosure

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkloʊʒər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
an area that is surrounded by a barrier, fence, or wall, especially for a particular purpose

Examples

Extraordinary precautions were taken so that no stranger be allowed in the city and no citizen within the enclosure surrounding the scaffold.
Bunks, or materials to build them, may have to be put inside the enclosure before the shelter walls are completed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - a small enclosure for animals

Etymology

From Old French 'enclos', from Latin 'inclus-' (shut in, enclosed).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of enclosing something within something else, especially a document or item included with a letter or package

Examples

Sometimes these servants wrote or dictated for enclosure with the letters of their soldier masters messages to their relatives and to members of their owners' families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclusion - the act of including someone or something within a whole

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *enclos* (enclosed), from Latin *inclusio* (enclosure).
