downpour

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌpɔː / (BrE)
A sudden, heavy fall of rain.

Examples

He ran out into the downpour, sped across the yard and into the buggy room.
Lighted windows glowed jewel bright through the downpour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainstorm - a storm with a lot of rain
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain falling in very fine drops
  • Degree Higher: deluge - a severe flood or very heavy rain

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'to pour down'.
