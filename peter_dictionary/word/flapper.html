flapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæpɚ / (AmE), / ˈflæpə / (BrE)
A young woman in the 1920s who wore unconventional clothes and behaved in a way that was considered shocking for the time.

Examples

Some date it from woman suffrage, others from when women first began to challenge men in the marketplace, still others from the era of the emancipated flapper and bathtub gin.

Etymology

From 'flap', possibly referring to the perceived energetic or flapping movements made by these women; popularized in the 1920s.
