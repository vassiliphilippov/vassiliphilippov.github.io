excommunicate

1. VERB

/ ˌɛkskəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
To officially exclude someone from participation in the sacraments and services of a Christian Church.

Examples

Parker was never excommunicated.
The Roman Catholic Church has excommunicated one of its priests, Father Feeney, for insisting that there is no salvation outside the visible church.

Synset Examples

The gay priest was excommunicated when he married his partner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to force someone to leave an organization or community

Etymology

From Latin excommunicatus, past participle of excommunicare (to put out of the community).
