acute

1. ADJ

/ əˈkut / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt / (BrE)
(of an illness, symptom, or condition) having a rapid onset and a short but severe course.

Examples

This is particularly acute for those who attended Midwood High School directly across the street from Brooklyn College.

Synset Examples

acute appendicitis
the acute phase of the illness
acute patients

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronic - (of an illness) persisting for a long time or constantly recurring

Etymology

From Latin *acutus* (sharp, pointed, acute), from *acuere* (to sharpen).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkjut / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt / (BrE)
(of a feeling or situation) extremely sharp or intense; severe in degree or effect.

Examples

His alienation is far more acute than Holmes'; he is not an eccentric but rather an outcast.

Synset Examples

acute pain
felt acute annoyance
intense itching and burning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intense - of extreme force, degree, or strength
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or extreme

Etymology

From Latin acutus (made sharp, sharpened), past participle of acuere (to sharpen).
