punitive

1. ADJ

/ ˈpjuːnɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈpjuːnɪtɪv / (BrE)
intended as punishment or meant to punish someone for wrongdoing.

Examples

Even death, therefore, has a providential as well as a punitive function.

Synset Examples

punitive justice
punitive damages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retributive - relating to or involving retribution as punishment
  • Antonym: rewarding - providing a reward or benefit, rather than punishment

Etymology

From Latin 'punitivus', from 'punire' (to punish).
