colloquialism

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊkwiəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈləʊkwiəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A word or phrase that is used in informal language or everyday conversation rather than in formal speech or writing.

Examples

Hey, I just came in to clean up a little, you know?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiom - a phrase with a meaning different from its literal meaning

Etymology

From late Latin 'colloquium' (conversation) + '-ism'.
