plaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
A soft mixture of lime, gypsum, sand, and water that hardens to a smooth solid, used for coating walls and ceilings.

Examples

The covers slid down his skinny neck so I saw his head, fuzzed like a dandelion gone to seed, but his face was turned to the wall - there was the pale shadow of his nose on the plaster - and I thought, Well you do n't look much like a pig-drunk bully now.
Bill Weigand was good and tired of the wall opposite, and the crack in the plaster.

Etymology

From Old French *plastre*, from Latin *plastrum*, variant of *emplastrum* (bandage, plaster).

2. VERB

/ ˈplæstər / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
to cover a surface thickly or conspicuously, especially by pasting or spreading material such as posters or substances on it

Examples

As you have doubtless guessed already, the plot is plastered with Freudian, Jungian, and Meinckian theory.

Synset Examples

The demonstrators plastered the hallways with posters
She let the walls of the apartment be beplastered with stucco

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover something with a layer of a substance
  • Synonym: smear - to spread a substance thickly over a surface
