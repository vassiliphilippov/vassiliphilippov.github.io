mad

1. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mad / (BrE)
angry or very annoyed; having strong feelings of displeasure or rage

Examples

He's mad at the world ".
He's mad at a world he did not make.
That would make him mad enough if he was sober.
He was beginning to see he was too mad to sleep.
And she was made to fall in love with him again there in the rutted dirt driveway standing in the cold fog, mad as she was at his going away when he really did n't have to, mad at their both got older in a life that seemed to have taken no more than a week to go by.

Synset Examples

stayed huffy a good while
she gets mad when you wake her up so early
mad at his friend
sore over a remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong annoyance or displeasure
  • Degree Higher: furious - extremely angry
  • Degree Lower: annoyed - slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

From Old English *gemǣd* (out of one's mind, insane). The sense 'angry' developed in North American English.

2. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mad / (BrE)
Showing or characterized by uncontrolled excitement, enthusiasm, or emotion.

Examples

The crowd staged its own mad scene in salvos of cheers and applause and finally a standing ovation as Miss Sutherland took curtain call after curtain call following a fantastic "Mad Scene "created on her own and with the help of the composer and the other performers.

Synset Examples

a crowd of delirious baseball fans
something frantic in their gaiety
a mad whirl of pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delirious - in a state of wild excitement or ecstasy
  • Synonym: frantic - conducted in a hurried, excited, or chaotic way

Etymology

From Old English 'gemæd' (insane, foolish).

3. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mæd / (BrE)
Mentally ill or insane; affected by madness or insanity.

Examples

"I'm mad ", shouted Payne, as he ran out into the hall.
"I'm mad ", and only wished he had been.
But he was not mad.
The creature in feathers looked around and David saw the mad eyes, glazed with an insane fear.
These shapes might have been mad, but there was no telling.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - suffering from mental illness; not sane
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill

Etymology

From Old English 'gemæd' (insane, foolish).
