instillation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing a liquid into a body or container drop by drop, often for medical or scientific purposes

Examples

The intra nasal instillation of a fluid suspension of infectious agent in an anesthetized animal is far different from exposure, through natural respiration, to aerosolized organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injection - the act of forcing a liquid into something, often quickly or under pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'instillatio', from 'instillare' meaning 'to drip in'.
