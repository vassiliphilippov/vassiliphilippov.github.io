buckwheat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌwiːt / (AmE), / ˈbʌkwiːt / (BrE)
An annual Asian plant with clusters of small pinkish-white flowers, grown for its edible triangular seeds, which are used whole or ground into flour.

Examples

Yeah, I don't need no banker to tell me where the bears shit in the buckwheat.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *boecweite* ('beech wheat'), after the shape of the seeds resembling beechnuts.
