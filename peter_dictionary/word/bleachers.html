bleachers

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːtʃɚz / (AmE), / ˈbliːtʃəz / (BrE)
a set of uncovered, tiered benches providing seating for spectators at an outdoor sports event or venue

Examples

In the third Frank Robinson hammered a long home run deep into the corner of the bleachers in right center, about 400 feet away, with two men on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandstand - a large seating area, typically covered, for spectators at a sports event

Etymology

Originally American English, from 'bleach' (to whiten by sunlight), as the seats were exposed to sun.
