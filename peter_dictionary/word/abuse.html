abuse

1. VERB

/ əˈbjus / (AmE), / əˈbjuːz / (BrE)
to use something for a purpose it was not intended for, especially in a way that causes harm or damage

Examples

No one can deny that these "back door "admissions to membership provisions have been seriously abused nor that they have not resulted in the admission of downright incompetents to membership in supposedly learned societies.
This retelling by Louis Zara of the brief, anguished life of Stephen Crane - poet and master novelist at 23, dead at 28 - is in novelized form but does not abuse its tragic subject.

Synset Examples

Don't abuse the system
The director of the factory misused the funds intended for the health care of his workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misuse - to use something in the wrong way or for the wrong purpose
  • Antonym: use - to employ for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French *abuser*, from Latin *abūsāre* (to misuse, to use wrongly).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbjus / (AmE), / əˈbjuːs / (BrE)
cruel or violent treatment of a person or animal, especially regularly or repeatedly

Examples

We all seek the same thing through different ways - an end to this long night of two thousand years of darkness and unspeakable abuses which will continue to plague us until the Star of David flies over Zion ".

Synset Examples

the child showed signs of physical abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistreatment - the act of treating someone badly or unfairly
  • Synonym: neglect - failure to care for properly

Etymology

From Old French 'abuser', from Latin 'abūsus', past participle of 'abūtī' (to misuse).

3. VERB

/ əˈbjus / (AmE), / əˈbjuːz / (BrE)
to treat a person or thing badly, especially in a cruel or harmful way

Examples

We out here do n't see enough of the conference to know he is being abused.
Then it will be a "fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God "if you have abused Him in your hands.
But I knowingly abuse their confidence.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistreat - to treat someone or something badly or unfairly
  • Synonym: maltreat - to treat a person or animal cruelly or with violence
  • Antonym: cherish - to care for and protect someone lovingly

Etymology

From Old French 'abuser', from Latin 'abūsāre' (to use up, misuse), from 'ab-' (away) + 'ūsus' (use).
