brevity

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈbrɛv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being brief or concise in expression, especially in speech or writing.

Examples

Since brevity is the soul of ambiguity as well as wit, newspaper headlines continually provide us with amusing samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciseness - the quality of being concise; expressing much in few words

Etymology

From Latin 'brevitas' (shortness), from 'brevis' (short).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛvəti / (AmE), / ˈbrɛvəti / (BrE)
The quality of being brief or concise in expression or duration.

Examples

Still another, annoyed by the brevity of a recently received missive, wrote: "Yore letter was short and sweet, jist like a roasted maget ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciseness - quality of expressing much in few words
  • Antonym: lengthiness - quality of being lengthy or prolonged

Etymology

From Latin brevitās (shortness), from brevis (short).
