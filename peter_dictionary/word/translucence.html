translucence

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsluːsəns / (AmE), / trænˈsluːsəns / (BrE)
The quality of allowing light to pass through but diffusing it so that objects on the other side are not clearly visible.

Examples

Next he turned to wax because there was a similarity of wax to marble in tactile quality and translucence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: transparency - quality of allowing light to pass so that objects can be clearly seen through
  • Antonym: opacity - quality of not allowing light to pass through

Etymology

From Latin translucēns, present participle of translucēre (to shine through), from trans- (through) + lucēre (to shine).
