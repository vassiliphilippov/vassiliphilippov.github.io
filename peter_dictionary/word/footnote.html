footnote

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.nəʊt / (BrE)
A note printed at the bottom of a page in a book or document, providing additional information or references related to the main text.

Examples

His love of folklore has romanticism in it, and he does n't care much about the dollar sign or the footnote.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endnote - a note placed at the end of a document or section rather than at the bottom of the page

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'note', referring to a note at the foot of the page.
