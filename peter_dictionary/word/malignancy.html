malignancy

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡ.nən.si / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡ.nən.si / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the presence or development of malignant cells or tumors, typically resistant to treatment and potentially fatal.

Examples

She thought again of her children, those two who had died young, before the later science which might have saved them could attach even a label to their separate malignancies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - a disease caused by uncontrolled division of abnormal cells
  • Antonym: benign - not harmful in effect; in tumors, not malignant

Etymology

From Latin malignus (malignant, wicked) + -ancy (suffix forming nouns).
