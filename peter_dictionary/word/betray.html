betray

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
(of a feeling, quality, or emotion) to reveal or make known unintentionally; to show something, often a feeling, without meaning to.

Examples

Afraid at one and the same time that his work might be turned down - which would be a blow to his pride even though no one knew he was the author - and that the work would be accepted, and then that his violent feelings in the matter would certainly betray how deeply concerned he was in spite of himself.
They offered no opinions, volunteered nothing, betrayed no emotions.
At the same time, his voice betrayed uncertainty about their being here, and conveyed an appeal to whatever is reasonable, peace-loving, and dependable in everybody.
Their eyes betrayed too much of their emotions, she thought sadly.
She was a living doll and no mistake - the blue-black bang, the wide cheekbones, olive flushed, that betrayed the Cherokee strain in her Midwestern lineage, and the mouth whose only fault, in the novelist's carping phrase, was that the lower lip was a trifle too voluptuous.

Synset Examples

Her smile betrayed her true feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make something known or visible that was previously secret or unknown
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret; to hide

Etymology

From Old French *betrayer* ('to deceive'), derived from *trahir* ('to betray, to hand over').

2. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to deliver someone or something to an enemy by treachery

Examples

"He is aware he will not live for twenty-four hours if he betrays you ".

Synset Examples

Judas sold Jesus
The spy betrayed his country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sell out - to betray someone for personal gain or advantage

Etymology

From Old French *betrar*, based on Latin *tradere* (to hand over, deliver).

3. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to be sexually unfaithful to one's spouse or partner; to have a sexual relationship outside the commitment of marriage or partnership

Examples

He felt no anger towards Kitti, no sense that she had betrayed him.

Synset Examples

She cheats on her husband
Might her husband be wandering?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat (on) - to be sexually unfaithful to one's partner
  • Antonym: be faithful - to remain loyal and not be sexually unfaithful

Etymology

From Old French *betrayer*, from be- + *trahir* (to betray), from Latin *tradere* (to hand over, deliver up).

4. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
(of a quality or feeling) to fail or disappoint someone, especially when something expected or relied upon does not happen; to let down.

Examples

The mind has betrayed them, reason is the foe of life; they will trust only their physical sensations, the wisdom of the body, the holy promptings of the unconscious.

Synset Examples

His sense of smell failed him this time
His strength finally failed him
His children failed him in the crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fail - to disappoint or prove undependable to; to not meet expectations
  • Antonym: support - to give assistance or help to; not disappoint or let down

Etymology

From Old French 'betrayer', based on 'trahir', from Latin 'tradere' (to hand over, surrender).
