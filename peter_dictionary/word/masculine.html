masculine

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæskjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪn / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of men or boys; associated with men and not with women.

Examples

They saw completely masculine and obviously virile men performing with incredible grace.
May their first child be a masculine child.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feminine - associated with women and not with men

Etymology

From Latin masculinus, from masculus (male, man).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmæskjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪn / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting a grammatical gender, typically used for words referring to male persons or animals, or for words arbitrarily assigned this gender in some languages.

Examples

Thus, the masculine animate infinitive dabhumaksanigalu'ahai, meaning to live, was, in the perfect tense, ksu'u'peli'afo, and, in the future, mai'teipa.
Plus the fact that Siddo not only had the normal (to Earthmen) three genders of masculine, feminine, and neuter, but the two extra of inanimate and spiritual.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feminine - relating to or denoting the grammatical gender typically used for female persons or things

Etymology

From Latin masculinus (male, manly).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæskjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪn / (BrE)
the grammatical gender referring chiefly to male beings or things classified as male

Examples

And I hope that their first child will be a masculine child.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feminine - the grammatical gender denoting females
  • Antonym: neuter - the grammatical gender denoting neither male nor female

Etymology

From Latin masculinus, from masculus (male, manly).
