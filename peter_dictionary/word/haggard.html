haggard

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæɡərd / (AmE), / ˈhæɡəd / (BrE)
Looking very thin and tired, especially because of worry, suffering, illness, or hunger.

Examples

His captain was thin and haggard and his beautiful boots were worn and shabby.

Synset Examples

emaciated bony hands
a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - very thin and bony, especially from hunger or illness
  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially due to illness or lack of food
  • Antonym: healthy - in good health or physical condition

Etymology

Probably from Old French *hagard* (wild, untamed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæɡɚd / (AmE), / ˈhæɡəd / (BrE)
Looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue, worry, or suffering.

Examples

Rocco Vecchio - a perspiring, haggard Vecchio - was standing there, flanked by two men in the uniforms of armored transport guards.

Synset Examples

looking careworn as she bent over her mending
her face was drawn and haggard from sleeplessness
that raddled but still noble face
shocked to see the worn look of his handsome young face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careworn - showing signs of worry or exhaustion
  • Synonym: drawn - looking strained or tired
  • Antonym: fresh-faced - looking healthy and energetic

Etymology

Probably from Old French *hagard* (wild, unruly).
