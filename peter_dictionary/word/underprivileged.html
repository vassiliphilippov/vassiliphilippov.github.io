underprivileged

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈprɪv(ə)lɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈprɪv(ə)lɪdʒd / (BrE)
Lacking basic social rights, opportunities, or advantages enjoyed by others in society.

Examples

They are often ignorant as well as underprivileged.
In B. M. Spinley's portrayal of the underprivileged and undereducated youth of London, a salient finding was the inability to postpone gratification, a need to satisfy impulses immediately without the pleasure of anticipation or of savoring the experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disadvantaged - lacking basic resources, rights, or opportunities compared to others
  • Antonym: privileged - having special rights, advantages, or immunities

Etymology

From prefix 'under-' (meaning 'below' or 'insufficient') + 'privileged' (from Old French *privilegie*, from Latin *privilegium*, meaning 'law affecting one person').
