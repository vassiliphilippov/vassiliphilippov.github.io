electron microscopic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛktrɒn maɪkrəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɒn mʌɪkrəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving an electron microscope, especially in terms of examination or study at very high magnification

Examples

Electron microscopic examination of the **f sample showed it to be composed of nearly isotropic particles about 0.3 lm in diameter.
We found no obvious effects due to preferred orientation of the crystallites in this sample nor would we expect to on the basis of the shape found from electron microscopic examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron microscope - a microscope that uses a beam of electrons to create an image of the specimen

Etymology

Formed from 'electron' (from Greek elektron 'amber') and 'microscopic' (from Greek mikros 'small' + skopein 'to look at').
