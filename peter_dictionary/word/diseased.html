diseased

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈzizd / (AmE), / dɪˈziːzd / (BrE)
affected by or showing signs of disease; having a condition caused by illness or pathology.

Examples

As he applied the applicator extending from the machine - which consisted of seven differently colored neon tubes superimposed on a rectangular base - to the supposedly diseased portions of Mrs. Shaefer's body, Lee kept up a steady stream of pseudo scientific mumbo-jumbo.

Synset Examples

diseased tonsils
a morbid growth
pathologic tissue
pathological bodily processes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - in good physical or mental condition; not diseased

Etymology

From Middle English 'desesed', past participle of 'deseasen', from 'disease'.
