tribunal

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈbjunəl / (AmE), / trʌɪˈbjuːn(ə)l / (BrE)
A court of justice or an assembly with authority to adjudicate disputes and make judgments, especially one convened for a specific legal or administrative purpose.

Examples

It is not that the facts of the persecution were unavailable; most of the information elicited in Jerusalem had been brought to the surface by the numerous War Crimes tribunals and investigating commissions, and by reports, memoirs, and survivors' accounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a place or body where legal cases are heard and decided

Etymology

From Latin 'tribunal', originally a platform for magistrates, from 'tribunus' (tribune).
