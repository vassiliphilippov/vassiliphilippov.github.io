sir

1. NOUN

/ sɝː / (AmE), / sɜː / (BrE)
A polite or respectful form of address for a man, often used when speaking to someone whose name you do not know, or as a formal title.

Examples

Hell, sir.
- Sir?
- The last six months, sir?
- Yes, sir.
- Yes, sir.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: madam - formal term of address for a woman

Etymology

From Middle English *sir*, variant of *sire*, from Old French *sieur* or *sire* (lord, master).
