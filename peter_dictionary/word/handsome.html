handsome

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæn.səm / (AmE), / ˈhæn.səm / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a man) attractive in appearance, typically in a way that is strong or well-proportioned rather than delicate.

Examples

And this handsome booby, staring and sweating, was he her bridegroom?
First thing I knew he was in the kitchenette cooking up the breakfast and I was handing Eileen her coffee-cup and she was lying there handsome as a queen among her courtiers.
Winston watched him hurry down the drive to his car; a handsome, fine-looking man it made him proud to see.
And he was handsome, despite the long thin scar that slanted across his cheek.
He was handsome, with his coal-black hair and eyes, his fine chiseled features.

Synset Examples

a fine-looking woman
a good-looking man
better-looking than her sister
very pretty but not so extraordinarily handsome
our southern women are well-favored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good-looking - attractive in appearance
  • Degree Lower: pretty - attractive, usually in a delicate or charming way
  • Degree Higher: beautiful - very attractive in a perfectly formed or outstanding way
  • Antonym: ugly - unpleasant or unattractive in appearance

Etymology

From Middle English 'handsom' (easy to handle, appropriate), from 'hand' + '-some'. Original sense evolved from 'easy to handle' to 'good-looking'.
