scrounge

1. VERB

/ skraʊndʒ / (AmE), / skraʊndʒ / (BrE)
to search for and collect things, especially food or money, usually by begging, borrowing, or seeking among discarded items

Examples

For years he wore hand-me-down suits and homemade paper collars, was even driven to scrounging for cigarette butts in Vienna's gutters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forage - search widely for food or provisions
  • Synonym: beg - ask for food or money as charity

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly dialectal or imitative, first recorded in early 1900s.
