compass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs / (BrE)
the range or scope within which something acts, operates, or is effective; limits, bounds

Examples

Prokofieff was guided in a consistent direction by the life of his own people - by the compass of their national ideas.
He is already beginning to box the compass of alternative lines of action, including doing nothing.

Synset Examples

the range of a supersonic jet
a piano has a greater range than the human voice
the ambit of municipal legislation
within the compass of this article
within the scope of an investigation
outside the reach of the law
in the political orbit of a world power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scope - the extent of the area or subject matter something deals with
  • Synonym: ambit - the range or limits of something
  • Synonym: range - the area covered by something
  • Synonym: extent - the area covered by something; scope

Etymology

From Latin 'compassus', past participle of 'compassare' (to pace out, measure), from 'com-' (together) + 'passus' (step).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs / (BrE)
an instrument used for finding direction, typically consisting of a magnetic needle that points to the magnetic north

Examples

Electrical literature contained numerous references to lightning that had magnetized iron and had altered the polarity of compass needles.
Oersted's own earlier experiments were unimpressive, possibly because he had, like other experimenters, laid the conducting wire across the compass needle instead of parallel with it.
The ship's compass was useless because of the nearness of the magnetic North Pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyrocompass - navigational instrument that uses a spinning wheel and Earth's rotation rather than magnetism
  • Synonym: GPS - global positioning system, an electronic system for determining exact location and direction

Etymology

From Old French *compas* (circle, pair of compasses), from Late Latin *compassus* (circle, embrace), from Latin *com-* (together) + *passus* (a step).
