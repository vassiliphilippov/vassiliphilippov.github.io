trunk

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋk / (AmE), / trʌŋk / (BrE)
the main woody stem of a tree, typically covered with bark and supporting the branches and leaves

Examples

Every morning early, in the summer, we searched the trunks of the trees as high as we could reach for the locust shells, carefully detached their hooked claws from the bark where they hung, and stabled them, a weird faery herd, in an angle between the high roots of the tulip tree, where no grass grew in the dense shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bole - the trunk of a tree, especially when large and clearly defined

Etymology

From Old French *trunc* (trunk of a tree), from Latin *truncus* (trunk, stem, stalk, mutilated, cut off).

2. NOUN

/ trʌŋk / (AmE), / trʌŋk / (BrE)
A large strong box or case for holding clothes and personal items, used when traveling or for storage.

Examples

He looked thoughtfully at his wife's trunk, holding her meager treasures.
That's a good trunk.
He drew a long breath and opened the trunk and hung out her clothes and spoilables upon the wagon ribs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitcase - a portable rectangular container with a handle, used for carrying clothes
  • Synonym: chest - a large strong box, typically used for storage

Etymology

From Old French 'tronc' (box, trunk), from Latin 'truncus' (stem, trunk, wooden box).
