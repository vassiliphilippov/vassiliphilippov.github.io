crafty

1. ADJ

/ ˈkræfti / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːfti / (BrE)
good at achieving one's aims by deception or indirect methods; clever and deceitful.

Examples

This would make anyone crafty and cruel, capable of fiendish revenge.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
deep political machinations
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - clever and deceitful; skilled in achieving one's aims by deceit
  • Synonym: wily - skilled at gaining an advantage, especially deceitfully
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English 'crafti', from Old English 'cræftig' meaning 'strong, skillful', from 'cræft' (strength, skill).
