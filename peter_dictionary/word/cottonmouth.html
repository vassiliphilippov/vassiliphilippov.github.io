cottonmouth

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːt̬nˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
A venomous, semiaquatic snake (Agkistrodon piscivorus), found in swamps and wetlands of the southern United States, known for its white-lined mouth.

Examples

These three were much alike: lined snake (Tropidoclonion), one year and nine months; red-bellied snake (Storeria), two years; cottonmouth (Ancistrodon), two years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water moccasin - another name for the cottonmouth snake

Etymology

Named for the cotton-white interior of its mouth, which it displays when threatened.
