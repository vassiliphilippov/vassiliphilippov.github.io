funnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌnəl / (BrE)
a conical object or shape, wide at the top and narrow at the bottom, used for guiding liquid or powder into a small opening

Examples

The clouds bulged downward and burst suddenly into a great black funnel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - a solid or hollow object shaped like a cone

Etymology

From Old French fonel, from Latin fundibulum (a funnel, hopper).

2. VERB

/ ˈfʌn.l̩ / (AmE), / ˈfʌn.əl / (BrE)
to move or pour something through or as if through a funnel, typically guiding it into a narrow space or channel

Examples

Clerfayt, driving, sees himself "a volcano whose cone funneled down to hell "; the Briton Stirling Moss, one of the greatest virtuosi of all time, believes that ultra-fast road-circuit driving is an art form related to ballet.
I channel it, filter it, funnel it.

Synset Examples

funnel the liquid into the small bottle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channel - to direct or guide along a particular route

Etymology

From Old French 'founil', from Latin 'fundibulum' (funnel).
