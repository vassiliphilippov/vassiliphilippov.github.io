fancy-free

1. ADJ

/ ˌfæn.siˈfri / (AmE), / ˌfæn.siˈfri / (BrE)
having no commitments, responsibilities, or cares; carefree

Examples

"The world that was once foot-loose and fancy-free ", he said, "has now become screw-loose and frenzy free.

Synset Examples

a fancy-free bachelor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from anxiety or responsibility

Etymology

From 'fancy' (imagination, liking) and 'free,' meaning free from fancies or attachments. First recorded in the 16th century in Shakespeare's works.
