momentous

1. ADJ

/ moʊˈmɛn.təs / (AmE), / məʊˈmɛn.təs / (BrE)
Of great importance or significance, especially in its effect on the future.

Examples

It was to be expected that Stanley would be shy, slow in taking such a momentous step.

Synset Examples

deciding to drop the atom bomb was a very big decision
a momentous event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significant - having meaning, especially great meaning or importance
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'momentum' (movement, importance) via late Latin 'momentosus'.
