atop

1. ADV

/ əˈtɑp / (AmE), / əˈtɒp / (BrE)
on or at the top of; on top of something

Examples

We rode over roads so rough that our Jeep came to rest atop the soil between ruts, all four wheels spinning uselessly.
Batman's head on a lance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on - physically in contact with and supported by a surface

Etymology

Formed by combination of the preposition 'at' and 'top,' first recorded in the 19th century.
