moisten

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔɪsən / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪsən / (BrE)
To make something slightly wet or damp.

Examples

He moistened his lips uneasily.
Coney Island sauce: finely chop several onions and add enough catsup to moisten well; add prepared mustard to suit taste.
Check thickness of clay and build up thin areas by moistening surface with a little water and adding small pieces of clay.

Synset Examples

The dew moistened the meadows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dampen - make slightly wet or less dry
  • Antonym: dry - remove moisture or make not wet

Etymology

From Middle English moisten, from Old French moistenier, from moiste (moist).
