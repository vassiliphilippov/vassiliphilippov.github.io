selective

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
making careful choices; choosing only the best, most suitable, or preferred items or people from a larger group

Examples

"Selective selling "- concentrating sales on the larger accounts - has been used effectively by some manufacturers.
It is far more difficult in many communities to obtain admission to an apprentice program which involves union approval than to get into the most selective medical school in the nation.
"We aim t' be see-lective, y' know?

Synset Examples

an exceptionally quick and selective reader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminating - having or showing good judgment or taste
  • Antonym: indiscriminate - done at random or without careful judgment

Etymology

From Latin selectus (chosen, selected) + English suffix -ive.
