self-awareness

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈwɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˌself əˈweənəs / (BrE)
conscious knowledge and understanding of one's own character, feelings, motives, and individuality

Examples

The epic poems, the consolidation of the Greek pantheon, the rise of firm political units, the self-awareness which could permit painted and sculptured representations of men - all these had to await the progress of following decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-consciousness - awareness of oneself as an individual, especially as a distinct and separate being; often with a focus on being observed by others

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'awareness', first recorded in the mid 20th century.
