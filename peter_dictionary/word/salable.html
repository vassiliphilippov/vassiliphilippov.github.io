salable

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪləb(ə)l / (BrE)
Fit to be sold; capable of being sold.

Examples

Despite this danger, however, we are informed on every hand that ideas, not machines, are our finest tools; they are priceless even though they cannot be recorded on a ledger page; they are the most valuable of commodities - and the most salable, for their demand far exceeds supply.

Synset Examples

saleable at a low price

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsalable - not fit for sale; not capable of being sold
  • Synonym: marketable - able or fit to be sold or marketed
  • Synonym: saleable - alternative spelling of 'salable'

Etymology

From Latin 'salabilis', from 'salare' (to salt, preserve), then influenced by 'sale' (the act of selling) in English.
