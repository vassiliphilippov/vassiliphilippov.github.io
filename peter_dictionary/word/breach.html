breach

1. NOUN

/ briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
A violation or failure to fulfill a law, promise, agreement, or duty.

Examples

The misplaced modifier is another species more honored in the observance of obscurity than in the breach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violation - an act of breaking a law, rule, or agreement
  • Antonym: observance - the action or practice of fulfilling a law or custom (opposite sense in legal/contractual context)

Etymology

From Old French *breche*, from Old English *bryce* (a breaking).

2. VERB

/ briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
to make an opening or gap in a barrier, wall, or defense; to break through

Examples

There were two possible methods of breaching the conservative barriers around the Rules Committee: 1) to pack it with additional liberals and break the conservative-liberal deadlock, or 2) to remove one of the conservatives - namely Mississippi's 14 - term William Meyers Colmer (pronounced Calmer).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break through - to forcefully make a way through a barrier or obstacle

Etymology

From Old French *breche* (a breaking), from *brecher* (to break), from Germanic origins.

3. VERB

/ briːtʃ / (AmE), / briːtʃ / (BrE)
to break a law, rule, agreement, or promise; to violate a standard or obligation

Examples

A Philadelphia distiller is currently breaching the customary prohibition against hard-liquor advertising on TV and radio.

Synset Examples

offend all laws of humanity
violate the basic laws or human civilization
break a law
break a promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or formal agreement
  • Antonym: observe - to comply with or follow a law, rule, or promise

Etymology

From Old French *breche*, from *brëche* (a breaking), from Frankish *breka* (a break).
