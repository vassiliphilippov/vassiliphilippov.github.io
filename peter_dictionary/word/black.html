black

1. ADJ

/ blæk / (AmE), / blæk / (BrE)
Of the color that is darkest, resembling coal or pitch; having little or no hue due to the absorption of nearly all light.

Examples

Spectators in white crush textures dip toe and heel in smooth black, navy and taffy tan.
Since the Protestant clergy for the most part wear gray or some variant from the wholly black suit, my Roman collar and black garb usually identify me in England as a Roman Catholic cleric.

It is not difficult to see that the stamens of the catkin are always arranged in pairs, and that each individual flower is nothing but one such pair standing on a green, black tipped little scale.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white - of the color that reflects nearly all visible light; the opposite of black

Etymology

From Old English 'blæc' (black, dark) of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'blak' (black) and German 'blach' (black, dark).

2. NOUN

/ blæk / (AmE), / blæk / (BrE)
The quality or state of being black in color; the darkest color, due to the absence or complete absorption of light.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whiteness - the quality or state of being white in color
  • Synonym: darkness - the partial or total absence of light; dark quality or state

Etymology
