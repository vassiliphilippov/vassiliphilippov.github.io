2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.trəm / (BrE)
A broad range of related things, such as objects, values, qualities, ideas, or activities.

Examples

There is a broad spectrum of organisms from which selection for a specified military purpose might be made.
Those antisera shown by immunoelectrophoresis to be of the "broad spectrum "type were selected for used in the present study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: range - a set of different things of the same general type

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image), originally meaning 'specter' or 'apparition'.
