saturated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsatʃ(ə)reɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution) containing the maximum possible amount of a dissolved substance at a given temperature, so that no more can dissolve.

Examples

The precipitate was washed twice with an 80 % saturated solution of * * f, dissolved in a small quantity of 0.1 M neutral phosphate buffer, dialyzed against cold distilled water till free from ammonium ions, and lyophilized using liquid nitrogen.

Synset Examples

a saturated solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaturated - (of a solution) able to dissolve more of a substance; not saturated

Etymology

From Latin saturatus, past participle of saturare (to fill, to satisfy).
