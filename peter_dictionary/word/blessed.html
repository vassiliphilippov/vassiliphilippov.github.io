blessed

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɛsɪd / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd / (BrE)
(In religious contexts) regarded as holy and worthy of worship; venerated.

Examples

"O Blessed Virgin Mary, Mother of God and our most gentle queen and mother, look down in mercy upon England, thy "dowry ", and upon us all who greatly hope and trust in thee.

Synset Examples

the Blessed Trinity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holy - dedicated or consecrated to God or a religious purpose; sacred
  • Synonym: sacred - connected with God or dedicated to a religious purpose and so deserving veneration

Etymology

From Old English 'blētsian', meaning to consecrate or make holy.

2. ADJ

/ ˈblɛsɪd / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd / (BrE)
(informal, expletive) Used as an intensifier, especially in negative contexts, to add emphasis or express irritation.

Examples

If we cluster together, the redcoats can make an advantage out of it, but there's not a blessed thing they can do with two or three of us except chase us, and we can outrun them ".

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot
it's a blamed shame
a blame cold winter
not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - (informal) used as an intensifier or expletive
  • Synonym: blasted - (informal) used as a mild expletive or intensifier

3. ADJ

/ ˈblɛsɪd, blɛst / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd, blɛst / (BrE)
Having special favor or fortune, especially as bestowed by God or a higher power.

Examples

One soft evening - that marvelous sea blessed time when the sun's departing warmth lingers and a smell of spume and wrack haunts everything - Amy had picked herself off the floor and begun to walk.
Blessed art thou amongst women.

Synset Examples

our blessed land
the blessed assurance of a steady income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunate - receiving good fortune; lucky
  • Antonym: cursed - subject to misfortune or divine disfavour

Etymology

From Old English blētsian (to consecrate, make holy), related to blood rituals of blessing.

4. ADJ

/ ˈblɛsɪd / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) officially recognized as having entered heaven and thus worthy of public veneration, though not yet canonized as a saint.

Examples

Blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: saint - a person officially recognized as holy by the Church after canonization

Etymology

From Old English 'blētsian', meaning 'to consecrate or make holy'.
