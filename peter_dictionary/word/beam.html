2. NOUN

/ biːm / (AmE), / biːm / (BrE)
A long, narrow stream of light, especially one emitted from a source such as a lamp, flashlight, or beacon.

Examples

Off in the distance a searchlight flashed on, its beam slashing the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a thin line of light or energy
  • Synonym: shaft - a ray or beam of light passing through something

Etymology

From Old English *bēam* (tree, beam, ray), later influenced by Old Norse *beimr* (tree, beam).
