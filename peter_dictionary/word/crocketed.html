crocketed

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɑːkɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒkɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a gable or spire) decorated with crockets—small projecting ornamental features, typically in the form of curved or bent foliage.

Examples

Thus shielded, we played many foolish games in comfortable unselfconsciousness; even when the fences became a part of the game - when a vine embowered gate-post was the Sleeping Beauty's enchanted castle, or when Rapunzel let down her golden hair from beneath the crocketed spire, even then we paid little heed to those who went by on the path outside.

Synset Examples

a crocketed spire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocket - an architectural ornament resembling curved foliage

Etymology

From "crocket," from Old Northern French *croquet* (a little hook), referencing the shape of the ornament.
