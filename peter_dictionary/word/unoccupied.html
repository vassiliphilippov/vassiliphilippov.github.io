unoccupied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a space or place) not held, filled, or in use; empty or vacant.

Examples

On unoccupied roadway the bottle shattered into a small amber flash.
Mr. Blatz sat down in the only unoccupied kitchen chair.

Synset Examples

an unoccupied telephone booth
unoccupied hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupied - (of a space or place) held, filled, or in use
  • Synonym: vacant - (of a seat, house, or place) not occupied; empty

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + occupied (from occupy, from Middle French occuper, from Latin occupare ‘seize’).
