nag

1. VERB

/ næɡ / (AmE), / næɡ / (BrE)
(of a thought, feeling, doubt, etc.) to worry or trouble someone persistently or constantly

Examples

The thought came back, the one nagging at him these past four days.

Synset Examples

nagging concerns and doubts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - cause unnecessary worry or concern
  • Synonym: plague - cause persistent trouble or distress to

2. VERB

/ næɡ / (AmE), / næɡ / (BrE)
To complain or criticize persistently, especially about small or unimportant matters.

Examples

Conchita nagged at the younger children, attempting without success to keep her thoughts off Tom Brannon.

Synset Examples

She nags her husband all day long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pester - to annoy or bother someone repeatedly
  • Synonym: harass - to disturb or trouble persistently

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian nagga (to gnaw, irritate).
