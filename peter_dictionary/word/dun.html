dun

1. NOUN

/ dʌn / (AmE), / dʌn / (BrE)
A horse with a dull brownish grey coat, often with darker markings on the mane, tail, and legs.

Examples

There were two horses in the barn, a sway-backed dun and Jess Crouch's buckskin.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: buckskin - a horse of a yellowish or gold coat with black mane, tail, and lower legs

Etymology

From Middle English 'don', from Old English 'dunn' (dull brown), of Celtic origin.
