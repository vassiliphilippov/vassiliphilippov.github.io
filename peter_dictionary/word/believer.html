believer

1. NOUN

/ bəˈliːvər / (AmE), / bɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
A person who accepts that something is true or has faith in a particular idea or cause.

Examples

Brevet Major General George Armstrong Custer was the regiment's first permanent commander and, like such generals as George S. Patton and Terry de la Mesa Allen in their rise to military prominence, Custer was a believer in blood and guts warfare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skeptic - a person who doubts or questions accepted beliefs or claims
  • Synonym: supporter - a person who advocates, backs, or accepts something

Etymology

From believe + -er, forming agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈliːvər / (AmE), / bɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
A person who has religious faith or accepts a particular set of religious beliefs.

Examples

If it were primarily a believing fellowship, it would recruit believers from all social and economic ranks, something which most congregations of the New Protestantism (with a few notable exceptions) have not been able to do.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonbeliever - a person who does not have religious faith

Etymology

From believe + -er, meaning 'one who believes'. 'Believe' from Old English *belȳfan*, related to German *glauben*.
