alignment

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the arrangement of objects or parts in a straight line or in parallel lines

Examples

Thus, the alignment of the "dots "and "tips ", respectively, indicate individual variability of the 21 growth centers of each child with respect to the mean values for these boys and girls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - a row of people or things arranged one after the other
  • Synonym: arrangement - the way in which things are positioned or organized

Etymology

From French 'alignement', from 'aligner', from 'à' (to) + 'ligne' (line).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
an alliance or grouping of people, countries, or organizations united by a formal agreement or shared interests, especially in politics or international relations

Examples

Essentially this imposed two conditions: First, international law had to recognize and be compatible with an international political system in which a number of states were competitive, suspicious, and opportunistic in their political alignments with one another; second, it had to be compatible with the value system that they shared.
On the one hand, the major European nations had to maintain vis-a-vis each other an emphasis upon sovereignty, independence, formal equality - thus insuring for themselves individually an optimal freedom of action to maintain the "flexibility of alignment "that the system required and to avoid anything approaching a repetition of the disastrous Napoleonic experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations
  • Synonym: coalition - a temporary alliance for combined action, especially of political parties
  • Antonym: opposition - the act of opposing or resisting, especially an opposing group in politics

Etymology

From align + -ment, based on align from Old French aligner, from a- (to) + ligne (line)
