handful

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæn(d)ˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈhæn(d)fʊl / (BrE)
A small number or amount of something, usually fewer than expected; as much as can be held in one hand.

Examples

"Only a relative handful of such reports was received ", the jury said, "considering the widespread interest in the election, the number of voters and the size of this city ".
Outside, only a handful of reporters remained.

Synset Examples

only a handful of responses were received

Related Terms

  • Synonym: few - a small number of people or things, not many

Etymology

From hand + ful (Middle English), literally 'as much as a hand can hold.'
