glanders

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlændɚz / (AmE), / ˈɡlændəz / (BrE)
A contagious and often fatal bacterial disease affecting horses, mules, and donkeys, which can also be transmitted to humans.

Examples

Agents that are known to cause frequent infections among laboratory workers such as those causing Q fever, tularemia, brucellosis, glanders, coccidioidomycosis, etc., belong in this category.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melioidosis - an infectious disease similar to glanders, caused by a related bacterium

Etymology

From Middle English glandres, from Old French glandres (plural of glandre, 'gland, swelling').
