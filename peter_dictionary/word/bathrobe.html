bathrobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθˌroʊb / (AmE), / ˈbɑːθ.rəʊb / (BrE)
A loose, comfortable robe made of absorbent material, worn after bathing or swimming.

Examples

He rose late and went down in his bathrobe and slippers to have breakfast either alone or with Rachel.
The infant, in white terry-cloth bathrobe, her face intense and purposeful, had essayed a few wobbly steps toward her father.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing gown - a robe worn before dressing or in informal situations

Etymology

Formed from 'bath' + 'robe', indicating a robe to wear after bathing.
