zoom

1. VERB

/ zum / (AmE), / zuÀêm / (BrE)
to move very quickly, especially with a loud humming or buzzing sound

Examples

Samples zoomed into closeup range in regular succession, like telephone poles passing on the highway, while representative music reinforced the mood of the late teens and 1920's.
For this reason, he says, the density of the universe always remains the same even though the galaxies are zooming away in all directions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - to move quickly
  • Synonym: race - to move rapidly or at top speed
  • Antonym: crawl - to move very slowly

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of something moving fast, first used in the early 20th century.
