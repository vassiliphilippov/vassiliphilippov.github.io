parliamentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.tɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.təri / (BrE)
Relating to, enacted by, or characteristic of a parliament or its members.

Examples

For, if so, the path leads through a complex process of parliamentary diplomacy which adds still another dimension to the problem.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislative - relating to the making of laws
  • Antonym: executive - relating to the branch of government that implements laws

Etymology

From parliament + -ary, ultimately from Anglo-Norman and Old French 'parlement' (a meeting, assembly).

Relating to a system of government in which the supreme legislative power rests with a cabinet of ministers chosen from and responsible to the parliament.
