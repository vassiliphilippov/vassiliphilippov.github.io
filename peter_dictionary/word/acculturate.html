acculturate

1. VERB

/ əˈkʌltʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈkʌltʃəreɪt / (BrE)
To assimilate or adopt the cultural traits or social patterns of another group.

Examples

In addition, this country has been settled by many peoples of many heritages and their lore has become acculturated slowly, in an age of print and easy communication, within an ever expanding and changing society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilate - to absorb and integrate into a wider society or culture

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'cultura' (cultivation).
