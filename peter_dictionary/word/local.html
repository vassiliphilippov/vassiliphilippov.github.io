local

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or concerned with a town, city, or district, rather than a wider area; associated with the immediate area or locality.

Examples

After Larkin has been persuaded to restock his tangled acres with pheasants, he poaches only what he needs for the nourishment of his family and local callers.
One of the local callers, a retired brigadier apparently left over from Kipling's tales of India, does not approve of the way Larkin gets his birds.
The popularity of local color literature before the Spanish-American War, the steady currency of the Lincoln myth, the increased emphasis on the frontier west in our mass media are cases in point.
The outlook for the amateur, for instance, is usually dependent on his fondness for local history or for the picturesque.
- liberal considers that the need for a national economy with controls that will assure his conception of social justice is so great that individual and local liberties as well as democratic processes may have to yield before it.

Synset Examples

local taxes
local authorities

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: regional - relating to a large area or region, broader than local
  • Degree Higher: national - relating to an entire nation, broader than local or regional
  • Synonym: municipal - relating to a city or town or its governing body

Etymology

From Latin localis (relating to a place), from locus (place).

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəl / (BrE)
a train or bus that stops at all stations or stops along its route, as opposed to an express service

Examples

Sposato needed a front, some labor stiff with a clean record to act as business agent of the Redhook local.
He would not ride the eight-thirty local to the city each morning.

Synset Examples

the local seemed to take forever to get to New York

Related Terms

  • Antonym: express - a train or bus that makes few stops and travels faster than a local train or bus

3. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
relating to, characteristic of, or belonging to a particular place, area, or neighborhood, especially as distinct from a wider area.

Examples

The Prime Minister paid his respects to the Buddhist monks, strode rapidly among the houses, joked with the local soldiery, and made a speech.
More detailed instructions may be obtained from your local building supply houses and craftsmen.
In the cow camps, Tom Horn was regarded as a hero, as the same kind of champion he was when he entered and invariably won the local rodeos.
You might try some of the local drinks, you know.

Synset Examples

local customs
local schools
the local citizens
a local point of view
local outbreaks of flu
a local bus line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regional - relating to or characteristic of a region
  • Antonym: national - relating to a whole nation as opposed to a part
  • Antonym: global - relating to or involving the whole world

Etymology

From Latin 'localis', from 'locus' (place).
