meat

1. NOUN

/ mit / (AmE), / miÀêt / (BrE)
the flesh of animals, including fish and birds, used as food

Examples

Human nature was not a piece of meat you could tell was bad by its smell.
The meat wagon, therefore, was not out in front of the house any more, but the cluster of squad cars was still there and there was a cop on the door downstairs to screen any comings and goings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vegetable - a plant or part of a plant used as food
  • Degree Lower: fish - the flesh of fish used as food
  • Degree Higher: beef - the flesh of cattle used as food

Etymology

Old English *mete* (food, meat), from Proto-Germanic *mati-*.
