seminarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛməˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
a student who attends a seminary, especially one studying to become a priest in the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

Some Catholic priests lecture there; Catholic seminarians attend tutorials and row on the Cherwell with non-Catholic students.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theologian - a person who studies theology, especially religious theory and doctrine

Etymology

From Latin seminarium (seed plot, nursery) + -an.
