profitably

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑfɪtəbli / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪtəbli / (BrE)
In a way that brings profit or advantage; in a manner that yields beneficial results.

Examples

It may not be profitable for a part-time farmer to own the labor-saving machinery that a full-time farmer can invest in profitably.
The high cost of land, supplies, and labor make it difficult to farm profitably on a part-time basis.
A part-time farmer and his family can use their spare time profitably.

Synset Examples

they worked together productively for two years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: productively - in a productive way
  • Antonym: unprofitably - in a way that does not bring profit or advantage

Etymology

From 'profitable' + '-ly'. 'Profitable' from Old French 'profitable', from Latin 'profitalis', from 'proficere' (to accomplish, make progress).
