prophet

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑfɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt / (BrE)
a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God, or one who is believed to predict or foretell future events, especially by divine inspiration

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".
This movement of industry away from the central cities is not so catastrophically new as some prophets seem to believe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seer - person who claims to see or predict the future
  • Synonym: oracle - person regarded as a source of wise counsel or prophecy
  • Synonym: predictor - someone who predicts future events

Etymology

From Old French *prophete*, from Latin *propheta*, from Greek *prophētēs* (one who speaks for a god, interpreter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪt / (BrE)
A person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God, often believed to speak by divine inspiration.

Examples

Could he honestly believe it would be good for Carla to have those old prophets gripping her imagination now?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seer - a person who is supposed to be able to foresee the future or is endowed with spiritual insight
  • Synonym: oracle - a person through whom a deity is believed to speak
  • Antonym: false prophet - a person who falsely claims divine inspiration

Etymology

From Old French *prophete*, from Latin *propheta*, from Greek *prophētēs* (one who speaks for a god).
