carpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑɹ.pɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪt / (BrE)
A thick woven material covering the floor of a room, often with a nap or pile, used for warmth or decoration.

Examples

They tracked mud on the oaken floor, on the carpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rug - small floor covering, often not wall-to-wall

Etymology

From Old French *carpite*, from Medieval Latin *carpita* (thick woolen cloth), from *carpere* (to pluck).
