lefty

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛfti / (AmE), / ˈlɛfti / (BrE)
A person, especially an athlete, who is left-handed and uses their left hand with greater skill than their right.

Examples

Dick McAuliffe cracked the first of his two doubles against Lefty Don Rudolph to open the Bear's attack.
Lefty Wyman Carey, another Denver rookie, will be on the mound against veteran John Tsitouris at 8 o'clock Tuesday night.

Synset Examples

their pitcher was a southpaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southpaw - another informal term for a left-handed person, especially in sports
  • Antonym: righty - a right-handed person

Etymology

Diminutive formed from 'left' + '-y'.
