self-respect

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌself.rɪˈspekt / (BrE)
Pride and confidence in oneself; a feeling that one is behaving with honor and deserves esteem.

Examples

On the other hand, wifely supremacy demeans the husband, saps his self-respect, and robs him of his masculinity.

Synset Examples

it was beneath his dignity to cheat
showed his true dignity when under pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-esteem - confidence and satisfaction in oneself
  • Antonym: self-contempt - lack of respect for oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'respect', based on the Latin 'respectus' (regard).
