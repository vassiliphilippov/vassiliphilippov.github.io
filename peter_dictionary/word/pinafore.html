pinafore

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəˌfɔː / (BrE)
A sleeveless dress, often resembling an apron, typically worn over other clothes, especially by girls or women.

Examples

On the Christophers' lawn, little girls in white pinafores were playing grownups at a tea party.

Etymology

From 'pinafore', originally referring to an apron pinned to the front (from 'pin' + 'afore').
