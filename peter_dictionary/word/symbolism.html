symbolism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities; the practice of giving things symbolic meaning.

Examples

The religious symbolism, and especially the closely related rites and worship forms, constitute a powerful bond for the members of the particular faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word is applied to an object to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From French 'symbolisme', from 'symbole', from Latin 'symbolum'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The use of symbols or a system of symbolic representations to express ideas or qualities.

Examples

The Commission seems to represent the viewpoint of what I would call the unconscious liberal, but not unconscious enough, to invoke the now taboo symbolism of socialism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iconography - the visual images and symbols used in a work of art or the study of such imagery

Etymology

From French *symbolisme*, from Greek *symbolon* (symbol).
