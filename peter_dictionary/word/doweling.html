doweling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or technique of joining or fastening pieces of wood together using dowels

Examples

He went down to Mills and Bradley's Hardware Store and bought a full set of carpenter's tools, including a rotary power saw and several other pieces of power machinery that Mr. Mills said were essential for babbiting and doweling, whatever they were.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowel - a cylindrical rod used to fasten or align pieces of material

Etymology

From 'dowel' + '-ing', where 'dowel' comes from Middle English 'doule', referring to a pin for joining.
