hysterical

1. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
Experiencing or showing uncontrolled extreme emotion, especially as a result of a psychological disturbance or hysteria.

Examples

Besides, there was something hysterical and silly, something almost childish about an attempt to frighten her.
Maude was neither hysterical nor silly and Sarah rather doubted if she had ever been childish.
He thought once that he identified the somewhat hysterical voice of Fleischman claiming a kill.

Synset Examples

during hysterical conditions various functions of the human body are disordered
hysterical amnesia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing abnormal or irrational emotion

Etymology

From Greek hysterikos (of the womb), from hystera (womb).

2. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
Displaying excessive or uncontrollable emotion, often to the point of irrationality.

Examples

Hysterical.

Synset Examples

hysterical laughter
a mob of hysterical vigilantes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions; not agitated
  • Antonym: composed - having control of one's emotions; not agitated

Etymology

From Latin 'hystericus', from Greek 'hystera' (womb), reflecting outdated beliefs about emotional excess.
