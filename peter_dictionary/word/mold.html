mold

1. VERB

/ moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
to form or shape a material such as clay or wax into a particular form

Examples

Whenever artists, indeed, turned to actual representations or molded three-dimensional figures, which were rare down to 800 B.C., they tended to reflect reality (see Plate 6a, 9b); a schematic, abstract treatment of men and animals, by intent, rose only in the late eighth century.
In its place is a passionate consciousness grasped and molded to feelings of positive or negative values even as the actions of one's life are determined by constellations of process in which one is caught.
How literature does this, or for whom, is certainly not clear, but the content, form, and language of the "message ", as well as the source, would all play differentiated parts in giving and molding a sense of purpose.
The music director of the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra, William Steinberg, has molded his group into a prominent musical organization, which is his life.

Synset Examples

model a head with clay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - to make or shape a representation of something, often in clay or wax
  • Synonym: cast - to shape material by pouring it into a mold

Etymology

From Old French *moule*, from Latin *modulus* (a small measure, model).

2. NOUN

/ moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
A hollow container used to give shape to molten or liquid material as it cools and hardens.

Examples

But for all his erudite confidence, Sergeant Early was right out of the Garryowen mold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast - an object formed by pouring a liquid into a mold and letting it harden
  • Synonym: template - a shaped piece for guiding the form or shape of something
  • Synonym: model - a representation or copy in three dimensions, typically on a smaller scale

Etymology

From Old French *molde*, from Latin *modulus* (measure, model).

3. NOUN

/ moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
A particular style, form, or character by which something or someone is made or described.

Examples

For this reason, he appears as an independent and self-reliant figure, whose rugged individualism need not be pressed into the mold of a 9 to 5 routine.

Synset Examples

pottery of this cast was found throughout the region

4. VERB

/ moʊld / (AmE), / məʊld / (BrE)
(of food or organic material) to become covered with mold and begin to spoil, especially because of excess moisture.

Examples

It was spoiled now for seed, and it would sour and mold in three days if they failed to find a place and fuel to dry it.

Synset Examples

The furniture molded in the old house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rot - (of food or material) to decay or decompose through the action of bacteria or fungi
