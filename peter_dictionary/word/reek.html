reek

1. VERB

/ rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
to smell strongly and unpleasantly; to give off an offensive odor

Examples

The hedge reeked of coffee dregs thrown against it.

Synset Examples

The building reeks of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stink - to have a strong unpleasant smell
  • Synonym: smell - to emit an odor (neutral, can be pleasant or unpleasant)
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Antonym: scent - a pleasant smell

Etymology

From Old English rēocan (to emit smoke or vapor).

2. VERB

/ rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
to be strongly suggestive of something unpleasant or undesirable

Examples

Such tactics reek of totalitarianism!

Synset Examples

his speeches smacked of racism
this passage smells of plagiarism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - to have a trace or flavor of something (especially unpleasant)
  • Synonym: stink - to be strongly suggestive of something bad; to seem corrupt

Etymology

Old English *rēocan* (to emit smoke, steam)
