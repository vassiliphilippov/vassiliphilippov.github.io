cerebral

1. ADJ

/ səˈribɹəl, səˈriːbɹəl / (AmE), / sɪˈriːbrəl, səˈriːbrəl / (BrE)
Relating to the intellect, reason, or mental effort rather than emotions or instinct.

Examples

Is a dream simply a mental or cerebral movie?
The entire concept of cerebral imagery as the physical basis of a mental image can find no logical support.

Synset Examples

a cerebral approach to the problem
cerebral drama

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - relating to the intellect rather than emotions or instinct
  • Antonym: emotional - relating to emotions rather than intellect or reasoning

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain).

2. ADJ

/ səˈribɹəl, ˈsɛɹəˌbɹəl / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl, ˈsɛr.ɪ.brəl / (BrE)
relating to the brain or the cerebrum, the main part of the brain

Examples

In other studies, loss of differentiation in previously established conditioned reflexes resulted from repeated convulsive (metrazol) treatments, suggesting a fundamental disturbance in the balance between excitatory and inhibitory cerebral processes.

Synset Examples

cerebral hemisphere
cerebral activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain - organ of soft nervous tissue in the skull of vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebralis', from 'cerebrum' (brain).
