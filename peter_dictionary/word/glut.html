glut

1. VERB

/ ɡlʌt / (AmE), / ɡlʌt / (BrE)
To eat or consume in excess, especially to the point of being overly full or unable to take more.

Examples

Frankie was fairly glutted with ideas, as he had hinted upon his arrival: "It's really tremendous when you think Ella Fitzgerald is coming from Australia.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gorge - to eat greedily or to excess
  • Synonym: stuff oneself - to eat until very full
  • Antonym: fast - to abstain from food

Etymology

From Latin 'gluttire' (to swallow), related to 'gluttus' (greedy).
