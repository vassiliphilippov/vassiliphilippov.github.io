fluoroboride

1. NOUN

/ ˌflʊroʊˈbɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌflʊərəʊˈbɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that is a salt derived from fluoroboric acid

Examples

One type has a small univalent anion of the thiocyanate perchlorate fluoroboride type.

Etymology

From 'fluoro-' (containing fluorine) + 'boric' (referring to boron) + '-ide' (a suffix for salts and compounds).
