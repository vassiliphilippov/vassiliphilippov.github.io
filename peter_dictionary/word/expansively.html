expansively

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that is impressive, open, or spacious, often involving elaborate or generous style or scale

Examples

The Jewish working girl almost invariably works in an office - in contradistinction to gentile factory workers - and, buttressed by a respectable income, she is likely to dress better and live more expansively than the college student.

Synset Examples

she managed to live rather expansively on her modest income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a rich, elaborate, or luxurious manner
  • Antonym: modestly - in a simple, small, or humble manner

Etymology

From 'expansive' + '-ly'; 'expansive' from Latin 'expansus', past participle of 'expandere' (to spread out).
