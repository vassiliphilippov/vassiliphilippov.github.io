snapshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnæpˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈsnæpʃɒt / (BrE)
An informal photograph, typically taken quickly and without formal posing, often with a small hand-held camera.

Examples

Not long ago a newspaper advised those taking part in a contest that "snapshots must be of a person not larger than * * f inches ".

Synset Examples

my snapshots haven't been developed yet
he tried to get unposed shots of his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photograph - a picture made using a camera
  • Degree Higher: portrait - a photograph or painting of a person's face

Etymology

From 'snap' (to take a quick photograph) + 'shot' (photograph); first used in the late 19th century.
