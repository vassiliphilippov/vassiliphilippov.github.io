oval

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəl / (BrE)
having a rounded and slightly elongated shape, like that of an egg

Examples

In the tintable group are high and little heels, squared and oval throats, and shantung like textures.
Individual nuclei were usually oval to round, though occasionally elongated, and frequently small and somewhat pyknotic.
Matching photographs in oval frames of Mme Bonenfant and an elderly man who must be Alix's grandfather.
Oval face without an expression in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elliptical - having the shape of an ellipse; oval-shaped
  • Antonym: circular - having the shape of a circle

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' (egg) via Medieval Latin 'ovalis'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəl / (BrE)
a closed, curved shape that is broad and rounded at one end and tapers to a point at the other, resembling the outline of an egg or an ellipse

Examples

Then I saw the father's head slightly turn; gauche rainbow shapes replaced the poignant ovals of gold.
I looked back at pale ovals framed in the elongated oval of the car's rear window.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ellipse - a regular oval shape; a closed curve
  • Degree Lower: circle - a round plane figure whose boundary consists of points equidistant from the center

Etymology

From French *oval*, from Medieval Latin *ovalis* (egg-shaped), from Latin *ovum* (egg).
