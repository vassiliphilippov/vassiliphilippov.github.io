distract

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstrækt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrækt / (BrE)
to prevent someone from giving their full attention to something by drawing their mind or attention away

Examples

While Crosby distracted the Beardens, stewardesses Carnegey and Toni Besset dropped out of a rear door.
He tried to turn right to the society page in each one, but interesting stories kept cropping up to distract him.
Something else distracted him, yet there was no sound, only tomblike silence.

Synset Examples

The thief distracted the bystanders
He deflected his competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divert - to turn aside from a course or direction (including attention)
  • Antonym: absorb - to occupy or engage the attention fully
  • Antonym: focus - to direct attention or effort to a particular task or subject

Etymology

From Latin distractus, past participle of distrahere (to pull apart, distract).
