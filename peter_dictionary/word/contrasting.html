contrasting

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtræstɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈtrɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
Strikingly different from something else in close association, creating a noticeable difference.

Examples

Finally, if the mission of the Trial was to convict anti-Semitism, how could it have failed to post before the world the contrasting fates of the countries in which the Final Solution was aided by native Jew haters - i.e., Germany, Poland, Hungary, Czechoslovakia - and those in which it met the obstacle of human solidarity - Denmark, Holland, Italy, Bulgaria, France?

Synset Examples

contrasting (or contrastive) colors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similar - having a likeness or resemblance
  • Synonym: distinct - recognizably different from something else of a similar type

Etymology

From the verb 'contrast', which comes from Latin 'contrastare' (to stand against).
