outface

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈfeɪs / (AmE), / aʊtˈfeɪs / (BrE)
to defeat, intimidate, or cause someone to submit by confronting them boldly, especially by staring at them without flinching

Examples

They tried to outface him, but Joseph Simmons was as wide as two average men, and it would have taken braver men than these were to outface him.
They tried to outface him, but Joseph Simmons was as wide as two average men, and it would have taken braver men than these were to outface him.

Synset Examples

He simply stared down his opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare down - to make someone back down by glaring at them
  • Synonym: browbeat - to intimidate by overbearing looks or words

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (beyond) + 'face', meaning to face beyond or more boldly than another.
