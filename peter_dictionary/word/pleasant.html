pleasant

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈplɛzənt / (BrE)
Giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment; agreeable or pleasing.

Examples

The Percussive Twenties (SP 44006) stirs pleasant memories with well-known songs of that day, and Johnny Keating's Kombo gives forth with tingling jazz in Percussive Moods (SP 44005).
The home listener is overpowered, all right, but the experience is a far from pleasant one.
Teachers - men who wore brown suits and had gray hair and pleasant smiles - came to their table to talk shop and to be introduced to Scotty and Rachel.
Mickie had a pleasant glow as he said, "You see, both of them, I mean the President and Jeff Lawrence, are romantics.
Jane nodded with a pleasant smile.

Synset Examples

we had a pleasant evening together
a pleasant scene
pleasant sensations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or agreeable
  • Synonym: enjoyable - giving delight or pleasure
  • Degree Higher: delightful - highly pleasing or charming
  • Synonym: nice - giving pleasure or satisfaction
  • Antonym: disagreeable - not pleasant or enjoyable

Etymology

From Old French plaisant (pleasing), from plaisir ('to please'), from Latin placere ('to please').

2. ADJ

/ ˈplɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈplɛzənt / (BrE)
(of a person) having friendly, polite, or agreeable manners or behavior.

Examples

All she did was write me a pleasant little note about how it was beautiful while it lasted but that now life had parted our ways and it was goodbye forever.

Synset Examples

I didn't enjoy it and probably wasn't a pleasant person to be around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeable - (of a person) pleasant, likable, or easy to get along with
  • Antonym: unpleasant - (of a person) unfriendly, disagreeable, or off-putting

Etymology

From Old French plaisant, present participle of plaisir (to please), from Latin placēre (to please).
