contest

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɛst / (BrE)
An event in which people compete against each other in order to win, often with a prize or recognition for the winner.

Examples

Then Dick Hyde, submarine ball hurler, entered the contest and only five batters needed to face him before there existed a 3 - to 3 deadlock.
John Powell, batting for Adair, fanned after fouling off two 2 - and 2 pitches, and Buddy Barker, up for Stepanovich, bounced out sharply to Jerry Lumpe at second to end the 2 - hour and - 27 - minute contest.
Skinny Brown and Hoyt Wilhelm, the Flock's veteran knuckleball specialists, are slated to oppose the American League champions in tomorrow's 8 P.M. contest.
This is the kind of chest that invariably wins contests that steel edged "carved out of solid rock "looks of the great champions.
Physique contests are rarely won on muscle size alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - an event or situation in which people compete
  • Synonym: tournament - a series of contests between competitors

Etymology

From Latin 'contestari' (to call to witness), via French 'contester' (to contest, dispute).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɛst / (BrE)
A struggle or competition between rivals, often to achieve victory or superiority.

Examples

I lived in this onward driving contest where each day overcame a new difficulty, gained a new truth, or banished a previous error ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the activity or condition of striving to gain or win something by defeating others
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • Antonym: cooperation - the process of working together to the same end

Etymology

From Latin 'contestari' (to call to witness, to contest), from 'con-' (together) + 'testari' (to witness).

3. VERB

/ kənˈtɛst / (AmE), / kənˈtɛst / (BrE)
to formally dispute or challenge (a decision, outcome, claim, etc.), often in a legal or official context

Examples

Nevertheless he had ample opportunity to contest the statement before the appeal board.
An agreement between the leaders of four parties which contested indecisive elections on Oct. 15 was reached after almost 18 hours of political bargaining under the threat of an army coup d' etat.
A rule on the Federal deductibility of state taxes is contested.

Synset Examples

They contested the outcome of the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to question the validity or legality of something; to dispute

Etymology

From French 'contester', from Latin 'contestari' (to call to witness, to challenge).
