jazzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒazi / (BrE)
Having the characteristics of jazz, especially in rhythm or style; lively, bright, or flashy.

Examples

When he was fifteen John H. Mercer turned out his first song, a jazzy little thing he called "Sister Susie, Strut Your Stuff ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy; animated
  • Synonym: showy - attracting attention by being bright or flashy

Etymology

From 'jazz' + '-y', meaning 'like jazz'.
