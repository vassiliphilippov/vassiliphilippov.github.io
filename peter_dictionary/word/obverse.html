obverse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.vɜrs / (AmE), / ˈɒb.vɜːs / (BrE)
the more prominent or easily noticed of two sides, particularly the front or principal face of a coin, medal, or object; figuratively, the opposite or corresponding aspect of something.

Examples

It is the obverse of triviality, shallowness, emotional anaesthesia.

Synset Examples

the obverse of this issue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reverse - the back or less prominent side of a coin or object; the opposite of the obverse

Etymology

From Latin 'obversus', past participle of 'obvertere' (to turn toward).
