bazaar

1. NOUN

/ bəˈzɑr / (AmE), / bəˈzɑː / (BrE)
a market or shop, especially one in the Middle East or Asia, where a variety of goods are sold

Examples

The young poet painter reproduces the French life of the streets; he tells stories of the Thousand and One Nights, and conjures up before us the bazaars of Damascus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an open space or building where goods are bought and sold, especially a regular gathering of people for the purchase and sale of provisions, livestock, and other commodities

Etymology

From Persian *bāzār* (market) via Italian and French.
