slingshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪŋˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈslɪŋˌʃɒt / (BrE)
A Y-shaped stick with elastic material attached to the ends, used as a toy or simple weapon to shoot small objects by pulling and releasing the elastic.

Examples

Ludie took his slingshot and climbed to the rooftop to shoot at crows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catapult - Device for launching projectiles, especially a large weapon or British term for slingshot

Etymology

Formed from 'sling' + 'shot', dating from the 19th century.
