ingroup

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌɡrup / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a small, closely connected group of people with shared interests or identity who often exclude others from their group

Examples

At the same time, there is a good deal of self congratulation at attending a good college - they are even inclined to exaggerate its not inconsiderable virtues - and they express pleasure at the cozy in-group feeling that the college generates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outgroup - a group to which a person does not belong or with which they do not identify
  • Synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people

Etymology

Formed from 'in' + 'group', indicating a group one belongs to.
