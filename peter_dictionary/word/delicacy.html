delicacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛlɪkəsi / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪkəsi / (BrE)
The quality of being delicate and attractive in appearance or manner.

Examples

Yet no detail was too small to receive attention from this master, and as a result the playing here has humor, delicacy, and radiant humanity.
But just when she seemed to have sunk into some depravity of peasanthood she would disappear and come down bathed, brushed, and taking breaths of air, and even with her broken nails her hands would come to rest on a table or a leaf with a thoughtless delicacy, a grace of history, so to speak, and for an instant one saw how ferociously proud she was and adamant on certain questions of personal value.

Synset Examples

the daintiness of her touch
the fineness of her features

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daintiness - the quality of being delicate and refined
  • Antonym: crudity - the quality of being crude or lacking refinement

Etymology

From Latin delicatus (alluring, dainty, fastidious) via Old French delicatesse and Middle English delicacie.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛlɪkəsi / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪkəsi / (BrE)
a rare or luxurious food item considered especially pleasing or desirable

Examples

The preparations were elaborate: flowers, candles, incense sticks, rice wine, dozens of delicacies, and pieces of white cotton string.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat - an item of food that is especially pleasant or rare
  • Synonym: dainty - something delicious or choice to eat

Etymology

From Latin delicatus (delicate, dainty).
