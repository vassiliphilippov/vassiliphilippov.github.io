unsympathetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnsɪmˈpæθətɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌnsɪmˈpæθətɪk / (BrE)
not showing understanding or compassion for others; not inclined to feel or express sympathy

Examples

Time's editor, Thomas Griffith, in his book, The Waist High Culture, wrote: "most of what was different about it (the Deep South) I found myself unsympathetic to ".
But apart from racial problems, the old unreconstructed South - to use the moderate words favored by Mr. Thomas Griffith - finds itself unsympathetic to most of what is different about the civilization of the North.

Synset Examples

unsympathetic officialdom
people unsympathetic to the revolution
his dignity made him seem aloof and unsympathetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympathetic - showing understanding or compassion for others
  • Degree Higher: compassionate - feeling or showing sympathy and concern for others
  • Degree Lower: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'sympathetic'.
