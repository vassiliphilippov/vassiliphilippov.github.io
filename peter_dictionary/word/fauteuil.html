fauteuil

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɜɪj / (AmE), / foˈtœj / (BrE)
An upholstered armchair, especially one of a style originating in France with open sides and arms.

Examples

Eugene was not entirely silent, or openly rude - unless asking Harold to move to another chair and placing himself in the fauteuil that creaked so alarmingly was an act of rudeness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armchair - a comfortable chair with side supports for a person's arms

Etymology

From French 'fauteuil' (armchair).
