parasite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpærəsaɪt / (BrE)
an animal or plant that lives in or on another (the host), drawing nourishment from it without providing any benefit or causing immediate harm; commonly refers to organisms that live off others.

Examples

The only exception to this is certain bees that have become parasites.
Some members of the bee family have become idlers, social parasites that live at the expense of their hardworking relatives.
The female parasite spends much time in her efforts to find a nest of her host.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symbiont - an organism in a mutually beneficial relationship with another
  • Antonym: host - the animal or plant which a parasite lives on or in

Etymology

From Greek *parasitos* (‘person eating at another’s table’), from *para-* (‘beside’) + *sitos* (‘food’).
