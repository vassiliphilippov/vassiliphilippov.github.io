reimburse

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪmˈbɝːs / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈbɜːs / (BrE)
to pay back money to someone who has spent or lost it, especially for expenses incurred

Examples

If your employer reimburses you for expenses incurred, you deduct such expenses if they otherwise qualify.

Synset Examples

Can the company reimburse me for my professional travel?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repay - to pay back money that has been borrowed or spent
  • Synonym: compensate - to give someone something in recognition of loss, suffering, or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'reimborsare' (to repay), from 're-' (back) + 'imborsare' (to put into a purse).
