dysentery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈdɪsəntri / (BrE)
An infection of the intestines that causes severe diarrhea, often with blood and mucus.

Examples

Helps control shipping dysentery and coccidiosis in lambs.

Etymology

From Greek dusenteria, from dus- (bad) + enteron (intestine).
