Hanukkah

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.nə.kə / (AmE), / ˈhæn.ə.kə / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish festival commemorating the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem in 165 BCE, celebrated with the lighting of the menorah.

Examples

And she wrote the libretto for an oratorio on the subject of Judas Maccabeus performed at the Hanukkah festival which came in December.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Festival of Lights - another name for Hanukkah

Etymology

From Hebrew חֲנֻכָּה‎ (ḥanukká), meaning 'dedication'.
