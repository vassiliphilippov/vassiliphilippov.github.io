dishwasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪʃˌwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈdɪʃˌwɒʃə / (BrE)
A machine for washing dishes, cutlery, and other kitchen utensils automatically.

Examples

Hard surface cleaning in household application is represented by two classes of alkaline products: (1) the formulations made expressly for machine dishwashers, and (2) the general-purpose cleaners used for walls and woodwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing machine - a machine for washing laundry

Etymology

From 'dish' + 'washer', originally referring to a person who washes dishes; now chiefly refers to the machine.
