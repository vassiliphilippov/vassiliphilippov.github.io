retch

1. VERB

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
to make the movements and sounds of vomiting without necessarily bringing anything up from the stomach

Examples

Hoag stumbled back into the hall, leaned against the wall, and started to retch.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
