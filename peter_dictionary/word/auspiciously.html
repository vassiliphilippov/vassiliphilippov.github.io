auspiciously

1. ADV

/ ɔːˈspɪʃəsli / (AmE), / ɔːˈspɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that suggests a positive or successful outcome; favorably or with good omens.

Examples

Skorich began his new job auspiciously today.

Synset Examples

he started his new job auspiciously on his birthday

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inauspiciously - in an inauspicious manner; unfavorably or with bad omens

Etymology

From 'auspicious' + '-ly'; 'auspicious' from Latin 'auspicium' (augury).
