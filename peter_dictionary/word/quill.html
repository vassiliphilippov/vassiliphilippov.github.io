quill

1. NOUN

/ kwɪl / (AmE), / kwɪl / (BrE)
a pen made from a bird's feather, traditionally used for writing by dipping its pointed end in ink

Examples

his quill drawings had a scratchiness, suggesting skin texture; the clay he used plastically to suggest soft moving flesh, as in an abdomen, in a reclining torso; the wax he smoothed over to give the body surface an elastic pull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an instrument for writing or drawing with ink

Etymology

From Middle English quil (hollow stalk), possibly of Low German or Scandinavian origin.
