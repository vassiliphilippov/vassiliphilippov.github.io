gravestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪv.stəʊn / (BrE)
A stone marker placed at a grave, usually bearing an inscription in memory of the deceased.

Examples

The effect was as depressing as a gravestone, the applause irresolute and short-lived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headstone - a stone placed at the head of a grave
  • Synonym: tombstone - a stone that marks a gravesite, often inscribed

Etymology

From 'grave' (burial place) + 'stone'.
