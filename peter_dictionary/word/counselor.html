counselor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnsələr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnsələ / (BrE)
a person who gives professional advice or guidance, especially in solving personal, social, or psychological problems

Examples

In a growing number of American homes, marriage counselors report, the wife is taking a commanding role in sexual relationships.
Yet many psychologists and marriage counselors agree that domination of the sex relationship by one partner or the other can be unhealthy and even dangerous.
More typical is the case of a suburban Long Island housewife described by a marriage counselor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisor - person who gives advice in a professional context

Etymology

From Latin consiliarius (advisor), from consilium (counsel, advice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.səl.ə / (BrE)
a person responsible for supervising and guiding children or teenagers at a summer camp

Examples

It is significant that the Catskills, which used to be the summer playground for older teen-agers, a kind of summer suburb of New York, no longer attracts them in great numbers - except for those who work there as waiters, bus boys, or counselors in the day camps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervisor - person in charge of others; general term

Etymology

From Latin consiliarius (one who gives counsel), via Old French conseiller and Middle English counseilour.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnsələr / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnsələ / (BrE)
A lawyer who represents and pleads cases in a court of law; especially used as a formal mode of address for such a lawyer.

Examples

Yeah, Counselor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in business or legal matters
  • Synonym: barrister - a type of lawyer entitled to practice as an advocate in higher courts, mainly used in the UK
  • Synonym: solicitor - a lawyer who advises clients and prepares cases but does not usually appear in court, mainly used in the UK

Etymology

From Latin 'consiliarius' (adviser), from 'consilium' (counsel, advice).
