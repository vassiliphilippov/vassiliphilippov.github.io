falsify

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔl.səˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to change information, evidence, or records in order to make them false and deceive others

Examples

The two major newspapers in Providence continued, throughout the crisis, to accuse each other of misrepresenting the facts and attempting to falsify history.
It is so easy to falsify sentiment.
Shout at Eichmann though he might, the Prosecutor could not establish that the defendant was falsifying the way he felt about Jews or that what he did feel fell into the generally recognized category of anti-Semitism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - to make a false copy of something in order to deceive
  • Synonym: fabricate - to invent or create (a story, information) in order to deceive
  • Antonym: authenticate - to prove something is genuine or true

Etymology

From Latin falsificare, from falsus (false) + facere (to make).
