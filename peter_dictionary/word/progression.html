progression

1. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
A sequence of events, numbers, or stages that follow each other in a particular order or pattern.

Examples

When consciousness deserts the sleeping body and the wakeful world, it continues in the myriad progressions of the ever-present past and future, in a life as vibrant and real as the one left when the body tired and required sleep.
In following this general principle, Mason provides the observer with a natural eye progression from foreground to background, and the illusion of depth is instantly created.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a set of related events or items that follow each other in order
  • Synonym: series - a number of things or events of the same class coming one after another

Etymology

From Latin 'progressio' (an advance, progression), from 'progredi' (to go forward).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
The act of moving forward or advancing from one stage, process, or point to another.

Examples

that the dream was a reality on the infinite progressions of universal, gradient frequencies, across which the modern professor and the priest of ancient Nippur met?

Synset Examples

he listened for the progress of the troops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - forward movement or development
  • Antonym: regression - return to a previous state; movement backward

Etymology

From Latin progressio (an advance), from progredi (to go forth, advance).

3. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of moving forward or developing towards a particular goal or higher stage

Examples

X-ray films of the vertebral column showed progression of the demineralization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - forward movement towards a destination or goal
  • Antonym: regression - return to a previous or less developed state

Etymology

From Latin progressio (an advance, progression), from progredi (to go forward).
