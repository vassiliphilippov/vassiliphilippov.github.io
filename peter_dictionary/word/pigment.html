pigment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a dry coloring substance, usually in powder form, that is mixed with a liquid to produce paint or other coloring materials.

Examples

Macrophages laden with brown pigment were seen in some of the alveoli, and the intima of some of the small arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue.
Some liver cord cells contained vacuolated cytoplasm, while others had small amounts of brown hemosiderin pigment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - substance used to color materials, usually soluble

Etymology

From Latin pigmentum (coloring), from pingere (to paint).
