composite

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑːzət / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
A thing made up of different parts or elements; a whole formed by combining several different things.

Examples

Lung type 3, (fig. 3) is to some degree a composite of types 1, and 2,.
Composite samples were collected at weekly intervals.
The composite samples were normally collected over a 6 - hr period, but an occasional 24 - hr composite was made.

Synset Examples

the complex of shopping malls, houses, and roads created a new town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different substances or things
  • Antonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole

Etymology

From Latin compositus (put together, composite), past participle of componere (to put together).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
Made up of separate parts or elements that are combined to form a whole.

Examples

He drew toward the composite design from his meticulously accurate memory, without need to consult his sketches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - made up of two or more parts or elements
  • Antonym: simple - not made up of multiple parts; uncomplicated

Etymology

From Latin 'compositus', past participle of 'componere' (to put together).
