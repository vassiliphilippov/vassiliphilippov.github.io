drawn

1. ADJ

/ drɔn / (AmE), / drɔːn / (BrE)
(of a person or their face) looking strained as a result of worry, suffering, or tiredness.

Examples

And once Bake slipped in, pale and drawn, last night's liquor still on his breath with some of today's added to it.

Synset Examples

looking careworn as she bent over her mending
her face was drawn and haggard from sleeplessness
that raddled but still noble face
shocked to see the worn look of his handsome young face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggard - looking tired or worn, especially from fatigue or worry
  • Synonym: careworn - showing signs of anxiety or worry in one's appearance
  • Antonym: rested - looking refreshed and not tired or strained

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'draw', from Old English 'dragan' (to pull, drag).

2. ADJ

/ drɔn / (AmE), / drɔːn / (BrE)
(of curtains or draperies) closed or pulled shut so as to cover a window or opening.

Examples

There was a light in Black's front room, but drawn curtains prevented any view of the interior.

Synset Examples

the drawn draperies kept direct sunlight from fading the rug

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - (of curtains or draperies) not closed, allowing passage of light or visibility

Etymology

Past participle of 'draw', from Old English 'dragan' (to draw, pull).
