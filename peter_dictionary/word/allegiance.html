allegiance

1. NOUN

/ əˈlidʒəns / (AmE), / əˈliːdʒəns / (BrE)
loyalty or commitment to a group, cause, or course of action.

Examples

To forestall any change of allegiance, the Democrats hastily organised a testimonial banquet for O' Banion, as public reward for his past services and as a reminder of where his loyalties lay.

Synset Examples

his long commitment to public service
they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal; strong feeling of support or allegiance
  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being faithful; fidelity
  • Antonym: treason - betrayal of allegiance or loyalty to one's country or sovereign

Etymology

From Old French 'ligance', related to 'liege', from Medieval Latin 'ligantia' (bond, connection).
