diminution

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪməˈnuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪmɪˈnjuːʃən / (BrE)
A reduction in the size, amount, or importance of something.

Examples

Further, the corpuscular radiation, i.e., the solar wind protons, must sputter away the surface atoms of the dust and cause a slow diminution in size, with a resultant increase in both the Poynting-Robertson effect and the ratio of the repulsive force to the gravitational force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: increase - a rise in size, amount, or degree
  • Synonym: decrease - an instance of becoming smaller or fewer

Etymology

From Latin 'diminutio' (a lessening), from 'diminuere' (to diminish).
