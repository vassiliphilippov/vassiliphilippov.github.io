lull

1. NOUN

/ lʌl / (AmE), / lʌl / (BrE)
a temporary period of quiet, calm, or decreased activity, especially during an otherwise busy or turbulent time

Examples

He had n't realized that there would be so much time to think, so many lulls.

Synset Examples

there was never a letup in the noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a short period in which something stops before starting again
  • Synonym: hiatus - a pause or break in activity, work, or a process
  • Synonym: respite - a short period of rest or relief from something difficult
  • Antonym: flurry - a brief period of activity or excitement (opposite of lull)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *lullen* (to quieten, soothe), imitative in origin.

2. VERB

/ lʌl / (AmE), / lʌl / (BrE)
To make someone feel calm or safe, often by giving them false assurance or misleading them.

Examples

The waves are separated by intervals of 15 minutes to an hour or more (because of their great length), and this has often lulled people into thinking after the first great wave has crashed that it is all over.
The spirit served chiefly to lull the West while Moscow made inroads into the Middle East.

Synset Examples

Don't let yourself be lulled into a false state of security

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Antonym: alarm - to make someone feel worried or fearful
