analytically

1. ADV

/ əˌnælɪˈtɪkli / (AmE), / əˌnælɪˈtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner involving or based on analysis; by means of analysis.

Examples

Tone systems are certainly more complex than the number of units would suggest, and often analytically more difficult than much larger consonantal systems.

Synset Examples

assuming that the distinction is maintained one may ask which is to be analytically prior?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analytical - relating to or using analysis or logical reasoning

Etymology

From 'analytical' + '-ly'; ultimately from Greek 'analutikos' (able to loosen up, analytic).
