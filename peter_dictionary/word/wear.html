wear

1. VERB

/ wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
to have clothing or other items on one's body as a covering or adornment

Examples

Since the Protestant clergy for the most part wear gray or some variant from the wholly black suit, my Roman collar and black garb usually identify me in England as a Roman Catholic cleric.
Rachel wore a smart hat and, because she had been warned recently about smoking, puffed at her cigarettes through a long ivory holder stained with lipstick.
Teachers - men who wore brown suits and had gray hair and pleasant smiles - came to their table to talk shop and to be introduced to Scotty and Rachel.
And another time, without accusation: "You never wore that scarf I bought you ".
They were carpeted, but made for pumps and congress gaiters, not the great clodhoppers he wore.

Synset Examples

She was wearing yellow that day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remove - to take off clothing from the body
  • Antonym: put on - to place clothing on the body

Etymology

Old English *werian* (to clothe, cover, defend).

4. VERB

/ wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
(of an object or material) to become damaged, thin, or weak as a result of continuous use or friction.

Examples

We collected "lucky stones "- all the creamy translucent pebbles, worn smooth and round, that we could find in the driveway.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erode - to gradually wear away by natural forces or friction
  • Synonym: abrade - to scrape or wear away by friction or erosion

Etymology

Old English werian (to clothe, carry), but the sense of 'deteriorate by use' developed later.

5. VERB

/ wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
(of a person) to have a particular expression or look on one's face, especially as an indication of mood or feeling.

Examples

Madden's dark face wore a meditative look.
Muller, nakedly exposed at the bright window like a deer pinned in a car's headlights, threw down the rifle and turned to jump from the table; his face wore a look of outrage.

Synset Examples

He always wears a smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show - to display or allow to be perceived; to exhibit an expression or feeling
  • Synonym: display - to show an emotion or expression outwardly

Etymology

Old English 'werian' (to clothe, put on), related to German 'tragen' (to carry, wear).

6. VERB

/ wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
(of an object or material) to last and remain in good usable condition, especially through use or over a period of time.

Examples

Tougher than plastic, it wears well.

Synset Examples

This dress wore well for almost ten years

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wear out - to become unusable or damaged from long use
