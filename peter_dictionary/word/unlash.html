unlash

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈlæʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈlæʃ / (BrE)
to untie or remove a lashing or binding, especially one made with rope or cord

Examples

He unlashed the dipper and drew water from a barrel.

Synset Examples

unlash the horse

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lash - to tie or bind with a rope or cord

Etymology

From 'un-' (a prefix meaning 'reverse, remove') + 'lash' (verb: to tie with a rope or cord).
