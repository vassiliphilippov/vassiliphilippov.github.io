immunoelectrophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjuːnoʊɪˌlɛktroʊfəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊɪˌlɛktrəʊfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
A laboratory technique in which antigens and antibodies are separated and analyzed using electrophoresis to identify specific proteins in a sample.

Examples

Those antisera shown by immunoelectrophoresis to be of the "broad spectrum "type were selected for used in the present study.

Etymology

From immune + electrophoresis; 'immune' relating to immunity and 'electrophoresis' from Greek 'phoresis' (being carried) via 'electro-' (relating to electricity).
