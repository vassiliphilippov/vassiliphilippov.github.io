memorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛmɚɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛmərʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of learning and remembering something exactly as it is, especially by repeating it many times

Examples

The child with high anxiety may first direct his anxiety released energy toward achievement, but because his distress severely reduces the abilities of discrimination and memorization of complex symbols, the child may fail in his initial attempts to master the problem.

Synset Examples

the actor's memorization of his lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning - the acquisition of knowledge or skills through experience or study
  • Antonym: forgetting - the loss or failure to remember information

Etymology

From memorize + -ation, ultimately from Latin memor (mindful, remembering).
