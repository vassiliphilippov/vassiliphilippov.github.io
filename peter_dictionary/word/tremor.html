tremor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
A slight or minor earthquake.

Examples

A sharp tremor was followed by a jerky roll.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: earthquake - a sudden and violent shaking of the ground

Etymology

From Latin tremor (a shaking, trembling), from tremere (to tremble).
