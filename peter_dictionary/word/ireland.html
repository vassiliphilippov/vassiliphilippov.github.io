Ireland

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪərlənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪələnd / (BrE)
A country in northwestern Europe, occupying most of the island of Ireland, established as an independent republic in 1921.

Examples

Mongi Slim of Tunisia and Frederick Boland of Ireland were early favorites in the running, but France did n't like the former and the Soviet Union would have none of the latter.
In Ireland's County Limerick, near the River Shannon, there is a quiet little suburb by the name of Garryowen, which means "Garden of Owen ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Northern Ireland - region in the northeast of the island; part of the United Kingdom

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Ériu', a goddess personifying the island, Latinized as 'Hibernia'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈaɪərlənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪələnd / (BrE)
An island situated to the west of Great Britain, comprising the Republic of Ireland and Northern Ireland.

Examples

One day the dogs of Ireland will do that too and perhaps also the pigs ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Great Britain - large island to the east of Ireland, comprising England, Scotland, and Wales

Etymology

From Old Irish Ériu (a goddess/personification of the land), via Latin Hibernia.
