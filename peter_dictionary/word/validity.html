validity

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / vəˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being legally binding or officially recognized as effective

Examples

The bond issue will go to the state courts for a friendly test suit to test the validity of the act, and then the sales will begin and contracts let for repair work on some of Georgia's most heavily traveled highways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalidity - the lack of legal validity or force

Etymology

From Latin 'validitas' (strength, power), from 'validus' (strong, powerful).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / vəˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being logically or factually sound, or in accordance with reason or principle; the state of being valid and rigorous

Examples

Being based on so few events, these results are of dubious validity.
We have heard that after seeing Mr. Quasimodo's work it will be virtually impossible to deny the artistic validity and importance of the whole abstract movement.
The necessary step between diagnosis and training is acceptance of the validity of the criticisms.
To accept the validity of the judgments of others is the second step.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soundness - the quality of being based on valid reason or good judgment
  • Antonym: invalidity - the quality or state of being invalid or without legal or logical force

Etymology

From Latin validitas (strength, potency), from validus (strong).
