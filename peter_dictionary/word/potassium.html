potassium

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtæsiəm / (AmE), / pəˈtasiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic chemical element (symbol K), belonging to the alkali metal group, that reacts rapidly with water and oxygen and is found widely in nature, especially in compounds such as salts.
