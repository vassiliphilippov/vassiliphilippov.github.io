founder

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
A disease in horses and other hooved animals in which inflammation of the laminated tissue of the hoof leads to pain and lameness; also known as laminitis.

Examples

An aid in getting cattle and sheep on full feed, in improving feed conversion and growth, in reducing bloat and founder, and in controlling scours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laminitis - inflammation of the laminae in the hoof of a horse or similar animal

Etymology

Apparently from Old French fondeure (sinking, collapse), related to the verb 'founder' meaning to sink or collapse, reflecting the effect of the disease.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
a person who establishes an institution, organization, or enterprise

Examples

If of the founders of glottochronology Swadesh has escaped our steady plodding, and Lees has repudiated his own share in the founding, that is no reason why we should swerve.

Synset Examples

George Washington is the father of his country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cofounder - a person who founds something jointly with another
  • Antonym: successor - a person who takes over after the founder

Etymology

From Old French *fondeur*, from *fonder* (to found), from Latin *fundare* (to lay the bottom, establish), from *fundus* (bottom, base).
