centerfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌfiːld / (BrE)
(in baseball or softball) the area of the outfield directly beyond second base, between left field and right field.

Examples

"Take a ride on this one ", Brooks Robinson greeted Hansen as the Bird third sacker grabbed a bat, headed for the plate and bounced a third inning two run double off the left centerfield wall tonight.
Then, in the fifth, Anniston's kid catcher caught onto a curve and smacked the ball into left center field.

Synset Examples

he hit the ball to deep center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center fielder - the player who defends centerfield in baseball

Etymology

Formed from 'center' + 'field', referring to the central section of the outfield.
