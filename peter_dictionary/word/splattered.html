splattered

1. ADJ

/ ˈsplætɚd / (AmE), / ˈsplætəd / (BrE)
Marked or covered with scattered drops or patches of a liquid or another substance.

Examples

Wiry and burr headed, with steel blue eyes and a chest splattered with medals, Chandler is the epitome of the old-time trooper.

Synset Examples

waves dabbled with moonlight
a blood-spattered room
gardens splashed with color
kitchen walls splattered with grease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spattered - marked with small drops or spots, especially of a liquid
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains

Etymology

From the verb 'splatter', which is imitative of the sound produced when a liquid hits a surface.
