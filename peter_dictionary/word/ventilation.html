ventilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛn.təˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of allowing fresh air to enter and move through a room, building, or enclosed space, and removing stale or foul air

Examples

If you do use a cold frame be sure that its ventilation is adequate.
Ventilation.
Ventilation is provided in a concrete block basement shelter by vents in the wall and by the open entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airing - the process of exposing something to fresh air

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilatio', from 'ventilare' (to fan, agitate air).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The system or process by which fresh air is supplied to and stale air is removed from a building, often using mechanical means.

Examples

This behavior on her part subsided only after I had come to see the uncomfortably close similarity between, on the one hand, her arranging the ventilation of the common living room to her own liking, or turning the television off or on without regard to the wishes of the others, and on the other hand, my own coming stolidly into her room despite her persistent and vociferous objections, bringing my big easy chair with me, usually shutting the windows of her room which she preferred to keep in a very cold state, and plunking myself down in my chair - in short, behaving as if I owned her room.

Synset Examples

she was continually adjusting the ventilation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air conditioning - system for controlling temperature and humidity of air
  • Synonym: exhaust - system that removes air or gases from a space

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilatio', from 'ventilare' (to fan, winnow, agitate).
