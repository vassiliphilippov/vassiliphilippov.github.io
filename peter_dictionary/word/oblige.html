oblige

1. VERB

/ əˈblaɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈblaɪdʒ / (BrE)
To make someone feel morally or legally bound to do something; to put someone in a position where they are indebted to act.

Examples

I should be obliged if you could make other arrangements for your daughters.

Synset Examples

He's held by a contract
I'll hold you by your promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligate - to make someone legally or morally bound to do something
  • Antonym: release - to free someone from an obligation or debt

Etymology

From Old French obliger, from Latin obligare (to bind, to oblige).

2. VERB

/ əˈblaɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈblaɪdʒ / (BrE)
to make someone feel bound or forced to do something, especially because of a rule, law, or moral duty

Examples

It was the doctor, dressed and ready for the expedition to the market, and Alex was obliged to prepare himself in haste.
The doctor sat down rather wearily, caressing the hen and remarking that the city was not the place for a poultry loving man, but no sooner was the remark out than a knock at this door obliged him to cover the hen with his greatcoat once more.
It has been endlessly rephrased, but I may here put it thus: at what point do the tolerant find themselves obliged to become intolerant?
The district officials, along with their other duties, were obliged to organize special demographic inquiries in selected areas and to supervise the annual tabulations of demographic statistics.

Synset Examples

We compel all students to fill out this form

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compel - to force someone to do something

Etymology

From Old French *obliger*, from Latin *obligare* (to bind).

3. VERB

/ əˈblaɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈblaɪdʒ / (BrE)
to do something as a favor for someone; to accommodate or help by providing a service or action

Examples

Well, my friend, are you ready to do me this service?

Synset Examples

We had to oblige him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodate - to do a favor or service for
  • Synonym: assist - to help someone
  • Antonym: refuse - to decline to do something requested

Etymology

From Old French *obliger*, from Latin *obligare* (to bind).
