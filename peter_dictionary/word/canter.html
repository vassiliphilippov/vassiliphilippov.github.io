canter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tə / (BrE)
A smooth, moderate-paced three-beat gait of a horse, between a trot and a gallop.

Examples

Clapping spurs to the bronc he set off at a sharp canter, with growing alarm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gallop - the fastest gait of a horse, faster than a canter
  • Degree Lower: trot - a gait of a horse that is slower than a canter but faster than a walk

Etymology

From the shortened form of 'Canterbury gallop', named after pilgrims riding to Canterbury, England.

2. VERB

/ ˈkæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tə / (BrE)
(of a horse or rider) to move at a canter, a pace between a trot and a gallop.

Examples

They cantered down the peninsula, not slackening until the coach reached Woodside where the Santa Cruz uplands begin.

Synset Examples

The men cantered away

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gallop - (of a horse) to run fast with all feet leaving the ground together
  • Degree Lower: trot - (of a horse) to move at a pace slower than a canter but faster than a walk

Etymology

From noun 'canter', short for 'Canterbury gallop', named after the city of Canterbury.
