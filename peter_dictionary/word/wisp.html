wisp

1. NOUN

/ wɪsp / (AmE), / wɪsp / (BrE)
A small thin or delicate bunch, strand, or lock, especially of hair, smoke, or thread.

Examples

Some liar's logic, a wisp of optimism as fragile as the scent of tropical blossoms that came through the window (a euphoria perhaps engendered by the pill Fritzie had given her), consoled her for a moment.

Synset Examples

wisps of hair

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Middle English 'wisp' (bundle of hay or twigs).
