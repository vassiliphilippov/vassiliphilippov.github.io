engrave

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡreɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪv / (BrE)
to cut or carve a design, letters, or pattern onto the surface of something

Examples

Folklore, as used by such writers, really reflects images engraved into it by the very person using it.

Synset Examples

engrave a pen
engraved the trophy cupt with the winner's
the lovers scratched their names into the bark of the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etch - to engrave with acid or a sharp tool

Etymology

From French *engraver*, from en- (in) + graver (to carve).
