theoretically

1. ADV

/ θiːəˈrɛtɪkli / (AmE), / θɪəˈrɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is based on theory or abstract principles rather than practical application.

Examples

This is a theoretically complicated dipole interaction which causes any extremely small uncharged particle to agglomerate with other small uncharged particles, or to stick to an uncharged surface.

Synset Examples

he worked the problem out theoretically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practically - in a way that relates to actual practice; as opposed to theoretically

Etymology

From 'theoretical' + '-ly', with 'theoretical' from Late Latin 'theoreticus'.

2. ADV

/ θiˈɛrətɪkli / (AmE), / θɪəˈrɛtɪkəli / (BrE)
According to theory rather than experience or practice; in principle.

Examples

Theoretically, they are capable of extracting their required oxygen either from the surrounding air (Ghoreyeb and Karsner,' 13) or from pulmonary arterial blood (Comroe,' 58).
This way of escape is theoretically possible, but since it has grave difficulties of its own and has not, so far as I know, been urged by positivists, it is perhaps best not to spend time over it.

Synset Examples

on paper the candidate seems promising

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practically - in practice; in fact, as opposed to theoretically

Etymology

From 'theoretical' + '-ly'. 'Theoretical' from Late Latin 'theoreticus', from Ancient Greek 'theōrētikós' (contemplative, theoretical).
