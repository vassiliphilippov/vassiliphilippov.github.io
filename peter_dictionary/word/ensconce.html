ensconce

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈskɑns / (AmE), / ɪnˈskɒns / (BrE)
to settle or place someone or oneself comfortably, safely, or securely

Examples

As the plans were laid, some several thousand fat cats were to be ensconced in the armory's $ 100 seats and in 68 ringside boxes priced at $ 10000 each.

Synset Examples

He ensconced himself in the chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: install - to set up or establish in a position or place

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, variant of 'esconcen' (to conceal), from Old French 'esconcer', from 'es-' (out) + 'concer' (to hide).
