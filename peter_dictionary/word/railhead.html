railhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a railroad terminal, especially in a war zone, where military supplies are unloaded for further distribution

Examples

By late afternoon the train inched into the marshaling yards in the railhead at Lublin, which was filled with lines of cars poised to pour the tools of war to the Russian front.

Etymology

From 'rail' (referring to railway) + 'head' (end or terminus).
