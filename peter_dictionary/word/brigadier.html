brigadier

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɪɡəˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌbrɪɡəˈdɪə / (BrE)
a military officer ranking above a colonel and below a major general, typically commanding a brigade

Examples

One of the local callers, a retired brigadier apparently left over from Kipling's tales of India, does not approve of the way Larkin gets his birds.
But the brigadier dines on the birds with relish.
He was brigadier of the club's locker-room light infantry, and at one time or another declared war on Russia, Czechoslovakia, Yugoslavia, and China.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: major general - military rank above brigadier
  • Degree Lower: colonel - military rank below brigadier

Etymology

From French 'brigadier', from 'brigade' (troop) + '-ier' (agent noun suffix).
