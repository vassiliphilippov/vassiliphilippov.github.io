conjure

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑndʒər / (AmE), / ˈkʌndʒə / (BrE)
To summon into existence, often as if by magic; to call upon or bring forth, especially through supernatural or mysterious means.

Examples

I conjured him... out of thin air.

Synset Examples

raise the specter of unemployment
he conjured wild birds in the air
call down the spirits from the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summon - to call forth or order the presence of someone or something
  • Synonym: evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
  • Antonym: dispel - to drive away or scatter something, such as a belief or feeling

Etymology

From Latin 'coniūrāre' (to swear together, to conspire), via Old French 'conjurer'.
