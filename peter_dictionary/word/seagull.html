seagull

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌɡʌl / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌɡʌl / (BrE)
A mostly white aquatic bird with long pointed wings and short legs, commonly found near the sea.

Examples

We were camping a few weeks ago on Cape Hatteras Campground in that land of pirates, seagulls and bluefish on North Carolina's famed Outer Banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a general term for birds of the family Laridae

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'gull', referring to a gull found near the sea.
