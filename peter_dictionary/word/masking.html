masking

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of concealing or covering something so that it cannot be seen or noticed.

Examples

All of the releases, however, are recorded at a gratifyingly high level, with resultant masking of any surface noise.

Synset Examples

the cover concealed their guns from enemy aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concealment - the act of hiding or keeping something secret
  • Antonym: exposure - the condition of being revealed or made visible

Etymology

From the verb 'mask', which comes from Middle French 'masque', from Italian 'maschera'.
