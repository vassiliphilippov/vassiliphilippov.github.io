theatrical

1. ADJ

/ θiˈætrɪkəl / (AmE), / θɪˈætrɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the stage or theater; suited to performance in plays or dramatic productions.

Examples

Finally, the theatrical (and perversely erotic) notions of dressing up, cosmetics, disguise, and especially change of costume (or singularity of costume, as with Cipolla), are characteristically associated with the catastrophes of Mann's stories.

Synset Examples

a theatrical pose
one of the most theatrical figures in public life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dramatic - relating to drama or the performance of plays
  • Degree Lower: theatrical (overly dramatic) - overly dramatic or exaggerated (different sense)

Etymology

From Greek theatron (theater) via late Latin theatricalis.
