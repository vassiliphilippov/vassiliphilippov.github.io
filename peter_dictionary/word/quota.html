quota

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwoʊtə / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtə / (BrE)
A fixed or limited number or amount of something, officially allowed or required.

Examples

The difficulty was that each day seemed to produce its quota of details which must be cleaned up immediately.

Synset Examples

all the salesmen met their quota for the month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the process or action of distributing something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quota (pars) 'how large (a part)', from Latin quota, feminine of quotus 'of what number'.
