staff

1. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
the group of teachers and administrative personnel working at a school, college, or other educational institution

Examples

A good feeling prevailed on the SMU coaching staff Monday, but attention quickly turned from Saturday's victory to next week's problem: Rice University.

Synset Examples

the dean addressed the letter to the entire staff of the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faculty - the teaching staff of a university or college in American English

Etymology

From Old English *stæf* (stick, rod; also a symbol of office or authority); sense of 'group of workers' developed in the 18th–19th centuries.

2. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
All the people employed by an organization or working under a particular leader, especially those assisting in carrying out work or tasks.

Examples

"Our interior line and our linebackers played exceptionally well ", said Stram Monday after he and his staff reviewed movies of the game.

Synset Examples

the hospital has an excellent nursing staff
the general relied on his staff to make routine decisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crew - a group of people who work together, especially on a ship or plane

Etymology

From Old English 'stæf' (rod, staff), from Proto-Germanic '*stabaz'.

3. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
A strong stick or rod, often used as a support when walking or carried as a symbol of authority.

Examples

He stood stiffly erect, clutching the staff, his body half hidden by the limp cloth.
He clutched the staff, and his dark eyes blinked apologetically.

Synset Examples

he walked with the help of a wooden staff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cane - a stick used as an aid in walking
  • Synonym: rod - a straight, slender stick or bar

Etymology

From Old English stæf (staff, stick, rod); related to Old High German stab, Gothic stabs, and Old Norse stafr.
