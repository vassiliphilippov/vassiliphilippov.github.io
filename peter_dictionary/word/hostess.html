hostess

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊstɪs / (BrE)
a woman who is the owner or manager of an inn, hotel, or similar establishment

Examples

"Vous voulez vos petits dejeuners tout de suite alors "? their hostess enquired.
I remember standing in a corner, watching Jessica act the hostess, serving soft drinks to her guests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: host - a man who is the owner or manager of an inn, hotel, or similar establishment

Etymology

From host + -ess, from Old French hostesse, feminine of hoste (host).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊstɪs / (BrE)
(historically) a woman employed on an aircraft to look after passengers; a female steward on an airplane (now often called a flight attendant).

Examples

Finally she had come down; Winston had heard her shaking out the skirt of her new pink silk hostess gown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight attendant - a person whose job is to look after passengers on an aircraft
  • Synonym: stewardess - female steward on a passenger airplane or ship

3. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊstɪs / (BrE)
A woman who receives or entertains guests at her home or elsewhere, or who acts as the person in charge of guests at an event or venue.

Examples

She could see that Mr. Gorboduc was intrigued; the hostess in her took over.
The lawyer did n't know him very well although he saw him occasionally at some dinner party - Thayer, like himself, Madden reflected, was the extra man so prized by hostesses - and found him easy enough to talk to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - a person who receives or entertains guests, regardless of gender

Etymology

From host + -ess (feminine suffix), patterned after 'host' (from Latin 'hospes', guest, host).
