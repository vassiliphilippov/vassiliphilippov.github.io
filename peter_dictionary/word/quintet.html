quintet

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
A piece of music written for five instruments or voices.

Examples

Moreover, the piece is written in five movements, rather than the conventional four of most quintets, and this gives the opus a serenade or divertimento flavor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - musical composition for four performers
  • Degree Higher: sextet - musical composition for six performers

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', from 'quinto' (fifth), itself from Latin 'quintus'.
