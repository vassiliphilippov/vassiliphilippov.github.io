detail

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiˌteɪl, dɪˈteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdiːteɪl, dɪˈteɪl / (BrE)
A single, specific fact or item considered separately from the whole, often as part of a larger set of information.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particular - a specific item or fact, considered individually
  • Antonym: generalization - a broad statement that applies to a group based on individual instances

Etymology

From French détail, from détailler (to divide, cut in pieces), from de- (apart) + tailler (to cut).

2. VERB

/ ˈdiːˌteɪl, dəˈteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdiːteɪl, dɪˈteɪl / (BrE)
to describe or list all the facts or particulars about something; to give detailed information

Examples

One day Maeterlinck, coming with a friend upon an event which he recognized as the exact pattern of a previous dream, detailed the ensuing occurrences in advance so accurately that his companion was completely mystified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enumerate - to name things one by one; enumerate details
  • Synonym: describe - to give a detailed account of something; describe in full
  • Antonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points; opposite of detailing fully

Etymology

From French 'détailler' (to cut up in pieces), from 'dé-' (apart) + 'tailler' (to cut).
