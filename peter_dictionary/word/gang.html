gang

1. NOUN

/ ɡæŋ / (AmE), / ɡaŋ / (BrE)
A group of friends who regularly spend time together for social purposes.

Examples

So it is too with many other spirits which we all know: the spirit of Nazism or Communism, school spirit, the spirit of a street corner gang or a football team, the spirit of Rotary or the Ku Klux Klan.

Synset Examples

he still hangs out with the same crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a group of people gathered together, typically in a disorganized or informal way
  • Synonym: crew - a group of people who work closely together or share activities, especially informally

Etymology

Old English *gang* (a going, journey, way, passage, company), from Germanic origins.

3. NOUN

/ ɡæŋ / (AmE), / ɡæŋ / (BrE)
a group of workers who form a team, especially on a particular job or task

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crew - a group of people who work together, especially on a ship or aircraft
  • Synonym: team - a group of people working together on a task

Etymology

From Old English *gang* (a going, a journey, a way, a passage), related to the verb *gangen* (to go).
