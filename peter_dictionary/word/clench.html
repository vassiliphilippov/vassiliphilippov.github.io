clench

1. VERB

/ klɛntʃ / (AmE), / klɛntʃ / (BrE)
to hold something tightly and firmly, usually because of anger or determination

Examples

He does not know whether to look up or look aside, to put his hands in his pockets or to clench them at his side, to cross the street, or to continue on the same side.

Synset Examples

clench a steering wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to hold firmly
  • Antonym: release - to let go of something held

Etymology

From Middle English *clenchen*, of Germanic origin, related to 'clinch'.

2. VERB

/ klɛntʃ / (AmE), / klɛntʃ / (BrE)
to close or squeeze together tightly, especially with the hands, teeth, or other body parts, often as a reaction to emotion or exertion

Examples

He clenched his fists and faced the terror.

Synset Examples

clench one's jaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to take and keep a firm hold of; to grasp tightly
  • Antonym: loosen - to make or become less tight or firm

Etymology

Probably from Old English *clencan* (to hold fast), related to *clingan* (to cling).
