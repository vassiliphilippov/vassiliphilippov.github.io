retrospective

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
An exhibition that displays a representative selection or survey of an artist's work, typically spanning a long period of their career.

Examples

I went to a retrospective of his work when I was eighteen, and I thought he was a contemporary of Cezanne's ".

Etymology

From Latin retrospicere (to look back).
