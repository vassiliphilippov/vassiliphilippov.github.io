unbreakable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbreɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈbreɪkəbl / (BrE)
Not able to be broken or damaged, especially under normal use.

Examples

Immediately, the film improved and it improved because in narrative it found a content based on time to complement its own unbreakable connection with time.

Synset Examples

unbreakable plastic dinnerwear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: breakable - able to be broken or damaged
  • Degree Lower: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Degree Higher: indestructible - impossible to destroy or break; extremely durable

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'breakable' (able to be broken).
