elegance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪɡəns / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡəns / (BrE)
The quality of being graceful and stylish in appearance or manner; the quality of being pleasingly ingenious or simple.

Examples

Of course, it can be argued that an ability to write English correctly and with some degree of elegance is a marketable skill.
Unlike his younger brother, Joe, he never presumed to address her more familiarly than as "My dear friend ", although he praised and envied the elegance and purity of her style.
Their presence fouled the elegance of that room.

Synset Examples

she conveys an aura of elegance and gentility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grace - simple elegance or refinement of movement
  • Synonym: taste - refined aesthetic sensibility
  • Antonym: crudity - the quality of lacking refinement or taste

Etymology

From Latin elegantia (taste, propriety), from elegans (tasteful, fine).
