redemption

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdɛmpʃən / (BrE)
(Theology) the act of being saved from sin, error, or evil.

Examples

"The recognition of complicity is the beginning of innocence ", where innocence, I think, means about the same thing as redemption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salvation - deliverance from sin and its consequences

Etymology

From Latin redemptio, from redimere 'to buy back, redeem'.
