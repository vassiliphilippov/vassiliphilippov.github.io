falloff

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔlˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlɒf / (BrE)
A noticeable decline in the level, amount, or quality of something.

Examples

On last May 1, the Cardinals stood at 7 - 6, ending a two season fall-off on that milestone.
These contour diagrams also suggest a rather rapid falloff in the radio brightness with latitude.

Synset Examples

the team went into a slump
a gradual slack in output
a drop-off in attendance
a falloff in quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slump - a sudden decrease or decline in activity or performance
  • Synonym: drop-off - a decrease or reduction in amount or level
  • Antonym: increase - a rise in the level, amount, or quality of something

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'fall off' (to decrease), converted to a noun.
