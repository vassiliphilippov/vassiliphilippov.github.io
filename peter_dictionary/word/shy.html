shy

1. VERB

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a horse) to start back or move suddenly to one side, typically from fear or surprise.

Examples

The horse shied at the dreadful thing and flared its nostrils.

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *sceoh* (timid) or related Scandinavian roots, but precise origin uncertain.

2. ADJ

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
(after a number or quantity) just short of; lacking the specified amount.

Examples

Just shy of half a mile.

Synset Examples

eleven is one shy of a dozen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short - not having enough; less than required amount
