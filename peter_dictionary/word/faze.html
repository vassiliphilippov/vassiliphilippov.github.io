faze

1. VERB

/ feɪz / (AmE), / feɪz / (BrE)
to disturb or disconcert someone; to make someone feel unsettled or uneasy

Examples

That is an evening of music-making that would faze many a younger man; Mr. Elman is 70 years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unnerve - to make someone lose courage or confidence
  • Synonym: disturb - to interrupt the calm or order of
  • Synonym: daunt - to make someone feel intimidated or apprehensive
  • Antonym: calm - free from nervousness or disturbance

Etymology

Probably from dialectal phase, feeze, meaning 'to drive away', from Old English fēsian.
