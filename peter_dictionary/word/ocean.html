ocean

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
A very large area of salt water that covers most of the Earth's surface, separating continents and constituting a principal part of the hydrosphere.

Etymology

From Old French *ocean*, from Latin *oceanus*, from Greek *ōkeanos* (great river or sea surrounding the earth).
