chivalrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪvəlrəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəlrəs / (BrE)
(of a man or his behavior) courteous, gallant, and attentive toward women, like that of an ideal knight.

Examples

His addle-brained knight-errant, self appointed to the ridiculous position in an age when armor had already been relegated to museums and the chivalrous code of knight-errantry had become a joke, is, as Cervantes no doubt intended, a gaunt but gracious symbol of good, moving soberly and sincerely in a world of cynics, hypocrites and rogues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallant - brave, heroic, or chivalrous, especially towards women
  • Synonym: courteous - polite, respectful, or considerate in manner
  • Antonym: discourteous - rude and lacking in courtesy or respect

Etymology

From Old French 'chevalerous', from 'chevalier' (knight), from Late Latin 'caballarius' (horseman).
