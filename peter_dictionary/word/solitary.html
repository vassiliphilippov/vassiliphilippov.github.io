solitary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑː.lə.ˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
(of plants or animals) living or growing alone, not in groups, colonies, or clusters.

Examples

Although Andrena is gregarious, so that we may find hundreds and hundreds of burrows together, we must still call it a solitary bee.

Synset Examples

solitary bees

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gregarious - (of animals, plants, or people) sociable; living in groups or colonies

Etymology

From Latin *solitarius* (alone, lonely).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑː.ləˌteri / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪ.təri / (BrE)
Preferring to be alone; tending to spend time alone rather than with others.

Examples

Michelangelo hurried to Sangallo's solitary bachelor room with his sketches, asked the architect to design a stand which would simulate the seated Madonna.
As a boy in a local school he was shy and solitary, absorbed in his fondness for nature and his visions of Sweden's ancient glory.
His tough honesty condemns him to a solitary and difficult existence.

Synset Examples

a lone wolf
a lonely existence
a man of a solitary disposition
a solitary walk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gregarious - fond of company; sociable
  • Synonym: lonely - feeling sad because of being alone
  • Synonym: lone - without companions; solitary

Etymology

From Latin 'solitarius' (alone, lonely), from 'solus' (alone).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːləˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪˌtɛːri / (BrE)
(informal) Solitary confinement; a punishment in which a prisoner is kept alone and isolated from other prisoners.

Examples

Sir, I... Solitary.

Synset Examples

he was held in solitary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary confinement - confinement of a prisoner in isolation from other prisoners
