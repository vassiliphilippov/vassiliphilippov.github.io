northland

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrθˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːθˌlænd / (BrE)
a region or area that is situated in or toward the north; a northern part of a country or territory.

Examples

No suburban shopping-center branch - not even Hudson's vast Northland outside Detroit - does anything like the unit volume of business or carries anything like the variety of merchandise to be found in the home store.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southland - a region lying in or toward the south

Etymology

Formed from 'north' + 'land', meaning land lying to the north.
