naturalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being natural or in accordance with nature; the state of not being artificial, forced, or affected

Examples

The embarrassment of these theories over the naturalness of death is an illustration of the thesis that death cannot be only a punishment, for some termination seems necessary in a life that is lived within the natural order of time and change.

Synset Examples

he accepted the naturalness of death
the spontaneous naturalness of his manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial instead of natural
  • Synonym: genuineness - the quality of being genuine or authentic

Etymology

From 'natural' + '-ness', with 'natural' from Latin 'naturalis' (by birth, natural).
