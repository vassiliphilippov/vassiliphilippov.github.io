specialty

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlti / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlti / (BrE)
A particular subject, skill, or area of work that a person knows a lot about or has a special ability in.

Examples

The story is of a famous strip cartoonist, an arty individual, whose specialty is the American boy and who adopts a 10 - year old to provide him with fresh idea material.
Each of us had his own specialty besides.
But this is a specialty item.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forte - a person's strong point or talent
  • Antonym: weakness - an area where someone lacks ability or skill

Etymology

From Old French 'especialté', from Latin 'specialitas'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlti / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlti / (BrE)
A particular area of work or study to which someone has devoted themselves or in which they have specialized, especially as a career.

Examples

That'’s my specialty, right?

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - the process of becoming an expert in a particular area or field
  • Antonym: generalist - a person competent in many fields rather than specializing in one

Etymology

From Latin 'specialitas', from 'specialis' (special).
