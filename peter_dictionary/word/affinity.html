affinity

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɪn.ə.ti / (AmE), / əˈfɪn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Immunology) The degree of attraction or binding strength between an antigen and an antibody.

Examples

It has been suggested that the plasma thyroxine binding proteins, which have an extremely high affinity for thyroxine, compete with thyroglobulin for thyroxine (Ingbar and Freinkel, 1957).

Etymology

From Latin *affinitas* (relationship by marriage, connection).
