sash

1. NOUN

/ sæʃ / (AmE), / sæʃ / (BrE)
the frame that holds the panes of glass in a window, fitting within the main window frame and capable of being moved or fixed

Examples

He raced to the window and yanked at the sash.

Etymology

Probably from Arabic *shāsh* (muslin, gauze), used in reference to window frames with thin divisions.

2. NOUN

/ sæʃ / (AmE), / sæʃ / (BrE)
a long strip or band of fabric worn around the waist or over one shoulder, often as part of a garment for decoration or to hold clothing together

Examples

At that moment the bathroom door flew open and Eugene came out, with his face lathered for shaving, and strode down the hall, tying the sash of his dressing gown as he went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt - a strip of leather or other material worn around the waist to support or hold in clothes

Etymology

From Arabic *shāsh* (muslin, fine linen).
