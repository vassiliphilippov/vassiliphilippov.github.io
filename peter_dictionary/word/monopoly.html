monopoly

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːpəli / (AmE), / məˈnɒpəli / (BrE)
Exclusive control or possession of something, especially where others are excluded.

Examples

Particularly when based upon a single dominant party, governments may respond to such a situation by claiming a monopoly of understanding about the national interest.
It is the concern of the Department of State that the American people are safe and secure - defense is not a monopoly concern of the Department of Defense.

Synset Examples

They have no monopoly on intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - the power to influence or direct something
  • Synonym: dominion - the power or right of governing and controlling; sovereign authority
  • Antonym: sharing - dividing and distributing something among two or more people

Etymology

From Greek monopōlion, from monos (alone) + pōlein (to sell).

2. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːpəli / (AmE), / məˈnɒpəli / (BrE)
A situation in which a single company or organization has exclusive control over a particular commodity or service, allowing it to limit competition and set prices.
