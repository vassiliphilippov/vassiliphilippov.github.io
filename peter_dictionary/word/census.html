census

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
An official count or survey of a population, typically recording various details of individuals.

Examples

The registration figures given in Table 2 must be interpreted with caution since the estimate for eligible electors were made without the benefit of a reliable census.
Return to your cell blocks for evening count.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - a detailed inspection or investigation, especially one conducted by asking questions

Etymology

From Latin census (a registering, a surveying, a tax assessment), from censere (to assess).
