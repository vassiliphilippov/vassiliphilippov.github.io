partake

1. VERB

/ pɑrˈteɪk / (AmE), / pɑːˈteɪk / (BrE)
(formal) To have or show some of the characteristics or qualities of something.

Examples

All of her movements were careful and methodical, partaking of the stealth of a criminal who has plotted his felony for months in advance and knows exactly which step to take next in the course of the final execution of his crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resemble - to be similar in appearance or quality to something

Etymology

From Middle English parten ('to share') + take; influenced by Old English partian and Latin participare ('to share in').
