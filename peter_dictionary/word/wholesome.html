wholesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊl.səm / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl.səm / (BrE)
Promoting or conducive to physical or moral health; suggestive of good health or virtue.

Examples

A farm provides a wholesome and healthful environment for children.
We feel the quality of these powers initially as in some degree wholesome or threatening.

Synset Examples

wholesome attitude
wholesome appearance
wholesome food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: healthy - in good health; promoting health
  • Antonym: unwholesome - harmful to physical or moral well-being

Etymology

From Middle English 'holsom', from 'whole' + '-some'.
