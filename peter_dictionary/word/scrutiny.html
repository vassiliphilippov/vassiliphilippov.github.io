scrutiny

1. NOUN

/ ˈskruːtəni / (AmE), / ˈskruːtɪni / (BrE)
a prolonged and intense look at someone or something

Examples

She answered him precisely, missing not a beat in her scrutiny of the financial reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination or look
  • Degree Lower: glance - brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Latin 'scrutinium', meaning a search, inquiry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskruːtəni / (AmE), / ˈskruːtəni / (BrE)
Careful and thorough examination or inspection of something, especially to find mistakes or faults.

Examples

But the pseudo capitalism which dictates our whole economy as well as our politics and social life, will not stand close scrutiny.
Can they stand rigid scrutiny?
The model of this paper considers an industry which is not characterized by vigorous price competition, but which is so basic that its wage price policies are held in check by continuous critical public scrutiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination or investigation
  • Synonym: examination - detailed inspection or investigation
  • Antonym: overview - general summary or review

Etymology

From Latin scrutinium (a search or inquiry), from scrutari (to search), related to scruta (rubbish, odds and ends).
