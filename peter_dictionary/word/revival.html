revival

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvaɪvəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪvəl / (BrE)
an instance of something becoming active, popular, or important again after a period of decline or obscurity.

Examples

But now, under the guidance of the contemporary composer Marc Schlek, Jr., a major revival is under way.

Synset Examples

the revival of trade
a revival of a neglected play by Moliere
the Gothic revival in architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resurgence - a rising again into life, activity, or prominence
  • Synonym: reawakening - a renewed interest or revival after a period of inactivity
  • Antonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value

Etymology

From French 'revival' or directly from Medieval Latin 'revivalis', from Latin 'revivere' (to live again).
