unleash

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈliʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈliːʃ / (BrE)
To suddenly release or set free something powerful, such as an emotion, force, or action.

Examples

Rivers of cold sweat were suddenly unleashed within him.
Cobb unleashed a single powerful jab that sent Gyp reeling wildly and crashing down with a whining groan.

Synset Examples

unleash one's anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to set free from confinement or control
  • Antonym: restrain - to hold back or keep under control

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'leash' (a restraining cord for an animal), originally meaning to remove a leash from a dog.
