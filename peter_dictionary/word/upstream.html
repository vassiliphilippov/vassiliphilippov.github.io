upstream

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌpˈstriːm / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈstriːm / (BrE)
situated or moving in the direction against the current of a stream or river

Examples

A shielded thermocouple was used to measure the upstream temperature of the transpiring gas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downstream - in the same direction as the current of a stream or river

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'stream', patterning after compounds used in navigation and hydrology.

/ ʌpˈstriːm / (AmE), / ʌpˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the direction toward the source of a river or stream; against the current.
