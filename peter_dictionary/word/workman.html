workman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːrk.mən / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.mən / (BrE)
a man employed to do manual or industrial work, especially one who is skilled in a particular trade

Examples

"Workmen.
Everything was orderly and it seemed to be arranged for the workman's comfort, convenience and efficiency.
Here, Prokofieff became a workman in the vineyards of Socialism - producing music for the masses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worker - a person who works, especially in manual or industrial labor (gender-neutral)

Etymology

From Old English 'weorcman', compound of 'weorc' (work) + 'mann' (man).
