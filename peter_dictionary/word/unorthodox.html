unorthodox

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔrθəˌdɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːθədɒks / (BrE)
Not conforming to established customs, beliefs, or practices; independent or unconventional in behavior or thought.

Examples

They for their part are convinced that Holmes is too "unorthodox "and "theoretical "to make a good detective.
Why do the police find Holmes "unorthodox "?

Synset Examples

she led a somewhat irregular private life
maverick politicians

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional - following accepted customs and traditions
  • Antonym: orthodox - conforming to established doctrine or practice
  • Synonym: maverick - an independent-minded person, nonconformist

Etymology

From un- (not) + orthodox (from Greek orthos 'straight, correct' + doxa 'opinion, belief').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔrθəˌdɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːθədɒks / (BrE)
Not conforming to established customs, beliefs, or practices; breaking with convention or tradition.

Examples

Youngish man on the make, Madden labeled him, and was ready to guess that in a correct, not too pushing fashion, the junior partner of the firm had political ambitions; that Mrs. Garth would be impeccably suitable as the wife of a rising young lawyer; that there were three children, two boys and a girl; that she was active in the Woman's Club and he in Lions, Rotary, and Jaycee; and finally, that neither of them had harbored an unorthodox opinion since their wedding day.

Synset Examples

an unorthodox lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodox - following established or traditional beliefs or practices

Etymology

From un- (not) + orthodox, from Greek orthos (straight, correct) + doxa (opinion).
