moderation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.dəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒd.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The quality of being moderate and avoiding extremes; restraint or temperance in action, feeling, or consumption.

Examples

I waved with discretion and moderation to the vague golden faces fading through rising dust and the distortions of the back window glass.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excess - lack of moderation; an amount that is more than necessary
  • Synonym: temperance - moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating and drinking

Etymology

From Latin moderatio (restraint, guidance), from moderari (to moderate).
