save

1. VERB

/ seɪv / (AmE), / seɪv / (BrE)
To rescue or protect someone or something from harm, ruin, or destruction.

Examples

Nor were there any rules to save him.
A compromise will leave both sides without the glow of triumph, but it will save Berlin.
He would save her.
If there was a fire or a flood he would save Mama first and Violet next.
I wish our doctor could have saved her ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - to save someone from a dangerous or harmful situation
  • Antonym: endanger - to put someone or something in danger

Etymology

From Latin salvare (to make safe), via Old French sauver.
