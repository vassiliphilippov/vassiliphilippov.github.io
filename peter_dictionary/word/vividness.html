vividness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪvɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being vivid, especially in terms of being lively, striking, or full of life, color, or intensity.

Examples

A word taken in its dictionary meaning, a photographic image of a recognizable object, the mere picturing of a "scene "tends to lose experiential vividness and to connote such conventional abstractions as to invite neutral reception without the incitement of value feelings.

Synset Examples

the Puritan Period was lacking in color
the characters were delineated with exceptional vividness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dullness - the quality of lacking interest or intensity

Etymology

From Latin vividus (lively), from vivere (to live) + English -ness.
