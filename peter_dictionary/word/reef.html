reef

1. NOUN

/ riːf / (AmE), / riːf / (BrE)
A long, narrow ridge of rock, sand, or coral that lies just below or near the surface of the sea.

Examples

Reefs are left high and dry, and the beaches are covered with stranded fish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: coral reef - a reef made chiefly of coral

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'rif', meaning 'rib' or 'reef (in water)'.
