invalid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.və.lɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.və.lɪd / (BrE)
a person made weak or disabled by chronic illness or injury, often requiring care

Examples

The woman had the glassy look of an invalid, as if she had not slept at all.

Etymology

From Latin invalidus (weak), from in- (not) + validus (strong).
