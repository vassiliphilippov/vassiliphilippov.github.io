labour

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
The group of people in society who do manual work or work for wages, regarded collectively as a social class.

Examples

On one of his 1921 ventures he was actually come upon by a Detective Sergeant John J. Ryan down on his knees with a tool embedded in a labour office safe in the Postal Telegraph Building; the jury wanted better evidence than that and he was acquitted, at a cost of $ 30000 in bribes, it was estimated.

Synset Examples

there is a shortage of skilled labor in this field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
  • Synonym: proletariat - workers or working-class people regarded collectively

Etymology

From Old French 'labour' (work, toil), from Latin 'labor' (toil, trouble, work).
