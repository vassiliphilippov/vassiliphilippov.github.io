naturalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæ.tʃə.rəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ.ər.ə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The philosophical belief that everything can be explained by natural causes and laws, without involving supernatural or spiritual elements.

Examples

He specifies, "In the middle period of the Nineteenth Century it was colored by Christian supernaturalism, in the Twentieth Century it was affected by naturalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supernaturalism - the belief that events or phenomena cannot always be explained by natural laws and may involve supernatural forces

Etymology

From French *naturalisme*, from Latin *natura* (nature) + *-ism*.
