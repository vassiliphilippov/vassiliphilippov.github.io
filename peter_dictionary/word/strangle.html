strangle

1. VERB

/ ˈstræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstraŋɡl / (BrE)
To kill someone by squeezing their throat so tightly that they cannot breathe.

Examples

For almost two months, the defendant and the world heard from individuals escaped from the grave about fathers and mothers, graybeards, adolescents, babies, starved, beaten to death, strangled, machine-gunned, gassed, burned.
Deputy coroner says it looks like he sucked in a big pile of those thick suds and strangled on' em.

Synset Examples

he tried to strangle his opponent
A man in Boston has been strangling several dozen prostitutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choke - to prevent breathing by blocking the airway
  • Synonym: asphyxiate - to deprive someone of oxygen, causing unconsciousness or death

Etymology

From Latin strangulare (to strangle), from Greek strangaloun (to choke).
