gladiator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlædiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlædɪeɪtə / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome) a person, often a professional combatant or a captive, who fought other people or wild animals for public entertainment.

Etymology
