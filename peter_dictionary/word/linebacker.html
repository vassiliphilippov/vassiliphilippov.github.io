linebacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnˌbækər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnˌbækə / (BrE)
A defensive player in American football who positions themselves behind the line of scrimmage and is responsible for both defending against the run and covering passes.

Examples

"Our interior line and our linebackers played exceptionally well ", said Stram Monday after he and his staff reviewed movies of the game.

Etymology

From 'line' (referring to the line of scrimmage) + 'back' (referring to a player positioned back from the line).
