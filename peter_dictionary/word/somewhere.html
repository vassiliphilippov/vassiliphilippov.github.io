somewhere

1. ADV

/ ˈsʌmˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.wɛə / (BrE)
In or at or to an unspecified or unknown place.

Examples

Under the religious impulse, whether theistic or humanistic, men have joy in living; life leads somewhere.
Tonight a group of men, tomorrow night he himself, would go out there somewhere and wait.
Somewhere over there another patrol had need of light.
Above him somewhere were the bedrooms.
Somewhere in the fog, the nigger boy was still yelling murder.

Synset Examples

she must be somewhere

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anywhere - in or at or to any place (used for emphasis, or in negative statements)
  • Antonym: everywhere - in or to all places
  • Synonym: someplace - informal term for somewhere

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌm.wɛr / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.wɛə / (BrE)
An unspecified or unknown place.

Examples

He must be somewhere in New York.

Synset Examples

they moved to somewhere in Spain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anywhere - any place whatever (noun or adverb)
  • Antonym: nowhere - no place; not anywhere (noun or adverb)
