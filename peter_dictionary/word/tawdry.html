tawdry

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔdɹi / (AmE), / ˈtɔːdɹi / (BrE)
Showy but cheap and of poor quality; having a gaudy and tasteless appearance.

Examples

"If you are trying to get us out of the brothel, the dustbin, the kitchen sink, and the tawdry living-room, you are probably wasting your time ", Moreland told me.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Synonym: garish - obtrusively bright and showy; lurid
  • Antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way

Etymology

From late Middle English 'tawdry lace', a contraction of 'St. Audrey's lace', sold at St. Audrey's (Æthelthryth's) fair at Ely, England.
