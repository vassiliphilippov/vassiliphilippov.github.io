freshly

1. ADV

/ ˈfrɛʃli / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃli / (BrE)
Very recently; only a short time ago.

Examples

He was freshly shaved, and if there had been any alcohol in him we could never have missed detecting some scent of it on the massive gusts of his laughter.

Synset Examples

they are newly married
newly raised objections
a newly arranged hairdo
grass new washed by the rain
a freshly cleaned floor
we are fresh out of tomatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newly - very recently

Etymology

From 'fresh' + '-ly', forming an adverb.
