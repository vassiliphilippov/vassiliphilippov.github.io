friendliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛn(d)linəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛndlɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being friendly; a feeling of liking for others and enjoyment in their company.

Examples

The attitudes which the Rebs and Yanks took toward each other were very much the same and ranged over the same gamut of feeling, from friendliness to extreme hatred.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendliness or antagonism
  • Synonym: amiability - the quality of being amiable or pleasant

Etymology

From 'friendly' + '-ness', dating from Middle English, with 'friendly' from Old English 'frēondlic'.
