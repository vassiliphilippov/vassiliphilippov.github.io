brisk

1. ADJ

/ brɪsk / (AmE), / brɪsk / (BrE)
(of weather or air) pleasantly stimulating, invigorating, and imparting vitality and energy.

Examples

A bright sun and brisk wind had the track in a fast condition for the first time this week and 8280 St. Patty Day celebrants bet $ 842617 on the well prepared program.

Synset Examples

the bracing mountain air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracing - refreshing and invigorating (especially of air)

2. ADJ

/ brɪsk / (AmE), / brɪsk / (BrE)
Quick and energetic in movement or action.

Examples

During summers, business was so brisk that Mrs. Wilcox had two assistants and a messenger.
Someone said that while we were standing here and arguing about it, the British would be gone; but Cousin Simmons said he had watched them marching west early in the morning, and moving at a much brisker pace it had still taken half an hour for their column to pass, what with the narrowness of the road and their baggage and ammunition carts.

Synset Examples

a brisk walk in the park
a lively gait
a merry chase
traveling at a rattling rate
a snappy pace
a spanking breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: slow - not quick or energetic in movement
  • Synonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Antonym: sluggish - moving or reacting slowly; lacking energy

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'bruske', perhaps from Old Norse 'brask' (a bustle, commotion).
