simplex

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪm.plɛks / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.pleks / (BrE)
(of a communication system) allowing transmission of signals, messages, or data in only one direction at a time.

Examples

The old Morse system was replaced locally by the Simplex modern automatic method in 1929, when Ellamae Heckman (Wilcox) was manager of the Western Union office.

Synset Examples

simplex system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: duplex - (of communication systems) allowing simultaneous two-way communication

Etymology

From Latin simplex (simple, single).
