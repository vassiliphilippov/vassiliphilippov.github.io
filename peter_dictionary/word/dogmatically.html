dogmatically

1. ADV

/ dɒɡˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / dɒɡˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is stubbornly assertive of unproved principles or beliefs, often without consideration of evidence or other opinions; in a dogmatic way.

Examples

"Almost everybody in the senior class is married ", students say dogmatically.
The Court's opinion dogmatically asserts that the husband wife conspiracy doctrine does not in fact protect this relationship, and that hence the doctrine "enthrone [ s ] an unreality into a rule of law ".

Synset Examples

he is a dogmatically opinionated critic of Modern Art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertively - in a confident and forceful manner
  • Antonym: flexibly - in a manner that is adaptable to new ideas or circumstances

Etymology

From 'dogmatic' + '-ally'; 'dogmatic' from Greek 'dogmatikos', from 'dogma' (opinion, tenet).
