earnest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrnɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪst / (BrE)
a sum of money or other token given as a pledge to bind a contract or show commitment

Examples

In deadly earnest, the besiegers methodically stripped away portions of the roof and tossed lighted rags inside, only to have most stamped out by the women as soon as they hit the floor.

Etymology

From Old French *ernest*, from Latin *arra*, meaning a pledge or earnest money.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɝː.nɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜː.nɪst / (BrE)
Showing sincere and serious intention or conviction; not joking or playful.

Examples

Resolved, that we find in this fearful tragedy at Harper's Ferry a reason for more earnest effort to remove the evil of slavery from the whole land as speedily as possible.
He was in earnest conversation with her father and the old vaquero, Luis Hernandez.

Synset Examples

both sides were deeply in earnest, even passionate
an entirely sincere and cruel tyrant
a film with a solemn social message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serious - not joking or playful; thoughtful or grave
  • Synonym: sincere - genuine in feeling; not pretending or deceitful
  • Synonym: solemn - formal and dignified; serious
  • Antonym: joking - not taking things seriously; treating as a joke
  • Antonym: frivolous - not having any serious purpose or value; carefree and not serious

Etymology

From Middle English *ernest*, from Old English *eornost* (seriousness, zeal).
