withe

1. NOUN

/ wɪð / (AmE), / wɪð / (BrE)
A flexible band or rope made by twisting or plaiting together twigs or stems, typically used for tying or binding.

Examples

They were held together by pegs and withes and in later times drawn by a single ox in thills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withy - flexible branch or twig, especially of willow

Etymology

Old English *wiðig* (willow; withy), referring to pliant willow twigs.
