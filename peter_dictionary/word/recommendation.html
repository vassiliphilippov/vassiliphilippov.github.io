recommendation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛk.ə.mɛnˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛk.ə.mɛnˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a suggestion or proposal as to the best course of action, especially one put forward formally or officially

Examples

The jury said it found the court "has incorporated into its operating procedures the recommendations "of two previous grand juries, the Atlanta Bar Association and an interim citizens committee.
It was one of a series of recommendations by the Texas Research League.
Principals of the 13 schools in the Denton Independent School District have been re-elected for the 1961 - 62 session upon the recommendation of Supt. Chester O. Strickland.
The report, culminating a year long study of the ADC program in Cook county by a New York City welfare consulting firm, listed 10 long range recommendations designed to reduce the soaring ADC case load.
Other recommendations made by the committee are:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advice - guidance or recommendation offered
  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
  • Antonym: order - a command or directive to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'recommendatio', from 're-' (again) + 'commendare' (to commend).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkəmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A quality, attribute, or statement that makes someone or something seem suitable or worthy of approval or support.

Examples

For once radicalism was a recommendation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commendation - a formal or explicit expression of praise

Etymology

From Latin recommendatio, from recommendare ‘commend again, present with approval’.
