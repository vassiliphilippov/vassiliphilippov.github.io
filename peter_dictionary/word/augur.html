augur

1. VERB

/ ˈɔɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡə / (BrE)
(of an event or sign) to be a sign that something will happen, especially something good or bad.

Examples

He has, moreover, another qualification which augurs well for the future.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bode - to be an omen or sign of a particular outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'augur' (soothsayer), originally a religious official who interpreted omens.
