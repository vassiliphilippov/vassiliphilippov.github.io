query

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɛri / (AmE), / ˈkwɪəri / (BrE)
to ask a question, especially in order to express doubt or to request information

Examples

Queried about the impasse, Allied Arts said: "Better cancel the Kirov for the time being.
"Slice o' mutton, bhoy "? she had queried in her soft guttural.
"Is that so "? he queried.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ask - to put a question to someone
  • Synonym: inquire - to seek information by asking

Etymology

From Latin 'quaerere' (to seek, to ask) via Late Latin 'quaerita' and English noun 'query'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪri / (AmE), / ˈkwɪəri / (BrE)
A question, especially one addressed to an official or organization, or an expression of doubt or inquiry.

Examples

At the end of work one day, the personnel man took the applicants one at a time, asked them to sit behind the receptionist's desk and he then played the role of a number of people who might come to the receptionist with a number of queries and for a number of purposes.

Synset Examples

there was a question about my training
we made inquiries of all those who were present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - a sentence, phrase, or gesture that seeks information; an inquiry
  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information; a request for information
  • Antonym: answer - a response or reply to a question or query

Etymology

From Latin 'quaerere' (to seek, ask).
