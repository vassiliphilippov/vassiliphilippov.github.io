Athabascan

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθ.əˈbæsk.ən / (AmE), / ˌaθ.əˈbæsk.ən / (BrE)
A family of related Indigenous languages spoken in western North America, including parts of Alaska, northwest Canada, and the southwestern United States.

Examples

There are more stems per item in Athabascan, which expresses the fact that the Athabascan languages have undergone somewhat more change in diverging from proto Athabascan than the Yokuts languages from proto Yokuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athabaskan - alternative spelling of Athabascan; refers to the same language family

Etymology

From Lake Athabasca in Canada, named from Woods Cree; the language family term was coined by Edward Sapir.
