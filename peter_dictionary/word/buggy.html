buggy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡi / (BrE)
a small, light carriage with four wheels, usually drawn by a single horse, typically used in the past for personal transportation

Examples

When Papa went out to do God's work, Stevie often accompanied him in the buggy, which was drawn by Violet, the new black mare.
He ran out into the downpour, sped across the yard and into the buggy room.

Etymology

Probably from 'buggy', a dialectal English word for a low, light wagon (19th century).
