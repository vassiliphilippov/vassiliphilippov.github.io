volleyball

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑliˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈvɒlibɔːl / (BrE)
A game in which two teams, separated by a high net, use their hands to hit an inflated ball back and forth, aiming to ground the ball on the opponent's side to score points.

Examples

The parallel bars, horse, buck, springboard, horizontal bar, rings, and mats formerly in the school gyms were replaced by baseball, volleyball, basketball and football.

Etymology

Formed from "volley" (strike or hit rapidly) + "ball" in early 20th-century U.S. usage.
