Hellenic

1. ADJ

/ hɛˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to ancient Greece, its people, language, or culture, especially as distinguished from later Greek developments.

Examples

Greek civilization was swirling toward its great revolution, in which the developed qualities of the Hellenic outlook were suddenly to break forth.
The growing contacts between Aegean and Orient are also a phase which should be linked primarily to the remarkable broadening of Hellenic culture after 750.

Synset Examples

classical mythology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - of or relating to Greece, Greeks, or Greek language and culture

Etymology

From Greek Hellēnikos, from Hellēn (a Greek).

2. ADJ

/ hɛˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the classical Greek civilization, especially its language, culture, or art.

Examples

These are the centuries in which the inhabitants of the Aegean world settled firmly into their minds and into their institutions the foundations of the Hellenic outlook, independent of outside forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - Of or relating to Greece, its people, or language

Etymology

From Greek *Hellēnikos*, from *Hellēn* (a Greek).

3. NOUN

/ hɛˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛnɪk / (BrE)
The branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Greek and its dialects, both ancient and modern.

Examples

After all, Shelley is no "orthodox "or Hellenic Platonist, and even his "romantic "Platonism can be distinguished from that of his contemporaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - The language of the Greek people, ancient or modern

Etymology

From Greek Hellēnikos, from Hellēn (a Greek).
