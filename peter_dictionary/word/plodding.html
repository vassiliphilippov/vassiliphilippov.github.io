plodding

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɑdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplɒdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of movement) slow, heavy, and laborious; lacking in speed or liveliness.

Examples

Through the open window they heard sounds below in the street: cartwheels, a tired horse's plodding step, voices.

Synset Examples

leaden steps

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brisk - quick and energetic in movement
  • Synonym: leaden - dull, heavy, or sluggish (often of movement)

Etymology

Present participle of 'plod', originally of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative.
