remuda

1. NOUN

/ rəˈmjudə / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːdə / (BrE)
A herd of horses from which mounts are selected for use, especially on a ranch or during a cattle drive.

Examples

The rest of the crew offsaddled their mounts and turned them into the remuda.

Etymology

From Spanish 'remuda' (change of horses).
