2. NOUN

/ skɪl / (AmE), / skɪl / (BrE)
The ability to do something well, especially as a result of experience or training.

Examples

Benington recalled that he once told Hartweger that he doubted Gordon would ever play much for him because he seemed to be lacking in all of the accepted basketball skills.

Synset Examples

the sweet science of pugilism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • Degree Higher: expertise - expert skill or knowledge in a particular area
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of ability or skill

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skil' (distinction, discernment), related to the verb 'skilja' (to separate, understand).
