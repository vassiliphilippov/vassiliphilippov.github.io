drag

1. NOUN

/ dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
the force that opposes the motion of an object through a fluid such as air or water

Examples

Whipple (1955) extends the effects to include the solar corpuscular radiation pressure, which increases both the minimum particle size and the drag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friction - the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another

2. VERB

/ dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to pull something or someone along with effort or difficulty, especially against resistance

Examples

Half crazed by the weight dragging, the dust, and the heat, the horse leaped our wall, dashing out the rider's brains against it, and leaving him lying there among us - while the horse crashed away through the brush.
If the acting captain wanted his acting lieutenant to sit on his ass around the station all night, Killpath would just have to go out and drag Gun back by the heels once an hour; because he 'd be damned if he was going to be a mid-watch pencil-pusher just to please his ulcerated pro-tem captain.
Apart from the personal equation, another reason advanced in favor of the meeting was that too often in the past the U. S. appeared to have been dragged reluctantly to the summit.
The only hope which good teachers have for being paid their due is to stop dragging the dead weight of poor teachers up the economic ladder with them.
The horses were groomed to a high gloss; departing, they stepped solemnly with knees lifted to the jaw, for they had been trained to drag at important funerals.

Synset Examples

He dragged the big suitcase behind him
These worries were dragging at him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull - to take hold of something and move it toward oneself
  • Synonym: haul - to pull or drag with effort or force
  • Antonym: push - to move something away from oneself by pressing against it

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *dragan* (to draw, drag), related to Old Norse *draga* and Old High German *tragan*.

/ dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to pull someone or something along with effort or difficulty, especially along the ground; to move slowly or with resistance

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to move or progress more slowly than others, or lag or linger behind

Examples

But in so many other areas we still are dragging.

Synset Examples

But in so many other areas we still are dragging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lag - to fail to keep up or move as quickly as others
  • Synonym: trail - to be behind or move more slowly than someone or something
  • Antonym: forge ahead - to move forward quickly, to get ahead of others

Etymology

From Old English *dragan* (to draw, drag), related to Old Norse *draga* (to pull).

5. VERB

/ dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to inhale air or smoke through a cigarette, cigar, or pipe by drawing it in

Examples

As he talked about himself, time and again stuffing and dragging on his pipe, Steinberg began to relax and the initial hurried feeling grew faint and was dispelled.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

6. VERB

/ dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to search the bottom of a body of water for something lost or valuable, often using nets or similar equipment

Examples

The police dragged that river for three days, and nary a gun was found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dredge - to clear or search the bottom of (a body of water) by scooping out mud, weeds, and rubbish
