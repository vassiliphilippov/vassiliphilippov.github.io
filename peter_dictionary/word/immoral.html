immoral

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
Not conforming to accepted standards of morality; not adhering to ethical or moral principles.

Examples

As for states' rights, they have never counted in the thinking of my liberal friends except as irritations of a minor and immoral nature which exist now only as anachronisms.
Then suddenly we found ourselves in the middle of another fight, an irrational, an indecent, an undeclared and immoral war with our strongest (and some had thought noblest) ally.

Synset Examples

base and unpatriotic motives
a base, degrading way of life
cheating is dishonorable
they considered colonialism immoral
unethical practices in handling public funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unethical - not conforming to accepted standards of conduct
  • Synonym: amoral - lacking a moral sense; unconcerned with rightness or wrongness
  • Antonym: moral - concerned with right and wrong behavior and the goodness or badness of human character
  • Antonym: ethical - relating to accepted principles of right and wrong

Etymology

From Latin immoralis, from in- (not) + moralis (moral).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
not following accepted standards of right and wrong; deliberately behaving in a way considered wrong or wicked

Examples

Up to the turn of the century, contraception was condemned by all Christian churches as immoral, unnatural and contrary to divine law.
It was, too, an optimistic philosophy, and, though it separated law from morality, it was by no means an immoral or amoral one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moral - following standards of right and wrong; virtuous
  • Degree Lower: amoral - not concerned with morality; lacking a sense of right and wrong

Etymology

From Latin 'immoralis': 'in-' (not) + 'moralis' (moral).
