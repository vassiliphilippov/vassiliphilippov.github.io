confrere

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈfrɛr / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.freə / (BrE)
A fellow member of the same profession, organization, or group; a colleague.

Examples

We must build a corps of highly professional teachers of interior design who have had education, experience in the profession and are willing to take on the usual accompaniments of teaching - minimal income and minimal status among their confreres.

Synset Examples

the surgeon consulted his colleagues
he sent e-mail to his fellow hackers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colleague - a person with whom one works, especially in a profession or business

Etymology

From French confrère, from Latin confrater (comrade, fellow brother).
