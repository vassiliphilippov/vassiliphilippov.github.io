impudent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmpjədənt / (AmE), / ˈɪmpjʊdənt / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect and a rude disregard for others; impertinent.

Examples

The posse's retreat encouraged the Mexicans to be overbearing and impudent.
For three years, since the liquor territorial conference, Torrio had, with his elastic patience, and because he knew that retaliation could cause only violent warfare and disaster to business, tolerated O' Banion's impudent double-crossing.

Synset Examples

a flip answer to serious question
the student was kept in for impudent behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinent - not showing proper respect; rude
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect and consideration for others

Etymology

From Latin 'impudens', from 'im-' (not) + 'pudens' (ashamed, modest).
