dregs

1. NOUN

/ drɛɡz / (AmE), / drɛɡz / (BrE)
the small solid particles that sink to the bottom of a liquid, such as coffee grounds or wine sediment, and are left when the liquid is nearly finished

Examples

The hedge reeked of coffee dregs thrown against it.
He was so long thinking that my mother had time to inspect her sherry for dregs.
She laughed a little and tossed the dregs rakishly around in her glass.

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse dregg (sediment), related to Middle Low German dregg (dregs).
