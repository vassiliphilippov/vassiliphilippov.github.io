flea

1. NOUN

/ fliː / (AmE), / fliː / (BrE)
A small, wingless, bloodsucking parasite known for its ability to jump long distances, often found on mammals and birds.

Examples

The women said they had seen him wave an exhausted farewell; but he might have been shooing away the fleas that hopped from his yellow dog onto him.

Etymology

Old English fleā, related to Dutch vlo and German Floh.
