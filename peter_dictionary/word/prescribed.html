prescribed

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈskraɪbd / (AmE), / prɪˈskraɪbd / (BrE)
ordered or set down as a rule, requirement, or guide, especially by an authority or regulation.

Examples

The last exercise of Roland Claude's prescribed program for Henri is a single exercise, done in individual sets with a bit longer pause between sets.
Held: On the record in this case, the administrative procedures prescribed by the Act were fully complied with; petitioner was not denied due process; and his conviction is sustained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; compulsory
  • Antonym: proscribed - forbidden, especially by law

Etymology

From Latin praescribere (to write before, order), via past participle prescribed.

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈskraɪbd / (AmE), / prɪˈskraɪbd / (BrE)
fixed, determined, or established, especially by rule, law, or authoritative order

Examples

If you elect to use the Standard Deduction or the Tax Table, and later find you should have itemized your deductions, you may do so by filing an amended return within the time prescribed for filing a claim for refund.

Synset Examples

at the time appointed (or the appointed time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointed - fixed or set officially, especially by authority
  • Synonym: stipulated - specified or demanded, usually formally or legally
  • Antonym: optional - not required or set; left to choice

Etymology

Past participle of 'prescribe', from Latin 'praescribere' (to write before, order).
