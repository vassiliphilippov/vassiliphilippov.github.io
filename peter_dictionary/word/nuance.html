nuance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.ɑːns, ˈnjuː- / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.ɑːns / (BrE)
a subtle or slight difference in meaning, expression, or sound

Examples

Time plays an essential part in our mortality, and suddenly for no reason he could imagine (or admit) the image of Peg laughing filled his mind - so desirable, so lusty, so full of nuances of pleasure and joy.
Mercer's lyrics are characterized by an unerring ear for rhythmic nuances, a puckish sense of humor expressed in language with a colloquial flair.

Synset Examples

without understanding the finer nuances you can't enjoy the humor
don't argue about shades of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - a slight difference or variation in meaning, feeling, or tone
  • Synonym: subtlety - the quality of being subtle; a fine detail or distinction

Etymology

From French nuance (shade, subtle difference), from nuer (to shade), from Latin nubes (cloud).
