abstain

1. VERB

/ əbˈsteɪn / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪn / (BrE)
(of someone eligible to vote) to deliberately not cast a vote either for or against a proposal, motion, or candidate.

Examples

On July 14 the Security Council - with France and Great Britain abstaining - voted the resolution which drew the U. N. into the Congo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vote - to formally express a choice or opinion in an election or meeting
  • Antonym: participate - to take part, to be involved, especially by voting

Etymology

From Latin 'abstinere', meaning 'to withhold'.

2. VERB

/ əbˈsteɪn / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately choose not to do or have something, especially something enjoyable or unhealthy, such as consuming food, drink, or drugs

Examples

More recently, the chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission, Dr. Glenn T. Seaborg, "admitted "to a news conference in Las Vegas, Nevada, that the US might fall behind Russia (he apparently meant in weapons development) if the Soviets continue to test in the atmosphere while we abstain.

Synset Examples

I abstain from alcohol

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indulge - to allow oneself to enjoy the pleasure of something

Etymology

From Latin abstinere, from ab- ‘from’ + tenere ‘to hold’.
