vault

1. NOUN

/ vɔlt / (AmE), / vɔːlt / (BrE)
an underground room or chamber used for burial

Examples

The guns are fired, the hymns are sung, and the body of Charles is carried down to the vault and laid beside the tombs of his ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crypt - stone chamber beneath the floor of a church used as a burial place
  • Synonym: mausoleum - a stately or impressive building housing a tomb or tombs
  • Synonym: tomb - a place for burying the dead

Etymology

From Old French 'voute', from Latin 'voluta' (a scroll, vault).
