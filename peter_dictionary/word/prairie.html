prairie

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri / (AmE), / ˈpreəri / (BrE)
a large, treeless, grassy plain, especially characteristic of central North America

Examples

His soil was "nothing special ", just prairie land, but he had harrowed in compost until it was loose, spongy and brown black.
The dangerous current upon the prairie ceased, but the water stood and kept on rising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steppe - vast, usually treeless plain especially in southeastern Europe or Siberia

Etymology

From French *prairie* (meadow), from Late Latin *prataria* (pasture), from Latin *pratum* (meadow).
