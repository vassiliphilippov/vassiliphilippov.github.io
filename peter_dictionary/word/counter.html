counter

1. ADJ

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə / (BrE)
indicating opposition or resistance to something; acting in the opposite direction or with an opposing effect.

Examples

And now, of course, the hue and cry for counter escalation is being raised on our side.
What have we to show by way of counter successes?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supportive - giving support or approval; not opposing

Etymology

From Old French 'contre', from Latin 'contra' (against).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə / (BrE)
A long flat surface or piece of furniture, often with shelves or drawers, on which food is prepared or objects are placed, especially in a kitchen, shop, or restaurant.

Examples

Standing in the shelter of the tent - a rejected hospital tent on which the rain now dripped, no longer drumming - Adam watched his own hands touch the objects on the improvised counter of boards laid across two beef barrels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffet - a piece of furniture with shelves and drawers for storage, usually in a dining room

Etymology

From Middle French *compter* (to count), via Old French *conteor* (table to count money).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
A long, flat surface in a shop, bank, café, or similar establishment across which goods or services are served, payments are made, or business is conducted.

Examples

Then he spread out the last list on the counter.
Now, as he passed the open counter that divided the assembly room from the business office, he nodded and said good night to the station keeper and his clerks, not stopping to hear the day-watch playback of his chewing out.
When several minutes had passed and Curt had n't emerged from the livery stable, Brenner re-entered the hotel and faced Summers across the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a counter in a pub or restaurant where drinks or food are served

Etymology

From Middle English countour (table used by a money changer), from Anglo-Norman, from Medieval Latin computatorium (place of reckoning), from Latin computare (to calculate).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
A device or instrument that records the number of times a particular process or event occurs.

Examples

Commonly used electronic devices which are found in practically every hospital are closed-circuit TV and audio systems for internal paging and instruction, along with radiation counters, timers, and similar devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tally - a count or record of a number of items
  • Synonym: recorder - a device that records information

Etymology

From the verb 'count' + '-er', indicating an agent or device.

5. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
To act in advance to prevent or neutralize; to deal with ahead of time.

Examples

Have you actually checked out (not just mentally tested) different selling approaches designed to counter the price competition problem?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipate - to foresee and act in advance
  • Antonym: ignore - to intentionally pay no attention to

Etymology

From Old French *contre* (against), from Latin *contra* (against).

6. ADV

/ ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
In the opposite direction; in opposition.

Examples

Demagogues of this sort found communist bogeys lurking behind any new idea that would run counter to stereotyped notions.

Synset Examples

run counter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - opposed in nature, direction, or meaning
  • Antonym: with - in the same direction as

Etymology

Ultimately from Latin 'contra' (against).

7. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
a small disc or piece, often made of wood, plastic, or ivory, used in board games or card games to keep score or reserve a space

Examples

We must avoid the notion, suggested to some people by examples such as those just mentioned, that ideas are "units "in some way comparable to coins or counters that can be passed intact from one group of people to another or even, for that matter, from one individual to another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: token - a piece used in place of money or to represent value or position in games

8. VERB

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə / (BrE)
To respond to a statement, argument, or action with an opposing or contrasting statement or argument.

Examples

"But Fogg ", they countered, "we can n't get over there.
"It is easy for you to talk "; countered a twenty year old law student, "you travel around the world.

Synset Examples

He countered with some very persuasive arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reply - to answer, especially in speech, to a question or statement
  • Synonym: retort - to reply sharply or angrily

Etymology

From Old French contre (against) and Latin contra (against).
