telescope

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
To become crushed together or collapse, especially by sliding into itself like the parts of a telescope; to shorten or compress by overlapping or sliding parts together.

Examples

The accumulation of such devices, however, soon had the effect of telescoping, even while separating, surface and depth.

Synset Examples

In the accident, the cars telescoped
my hiking sticks telescope and can be put into the backpack

Etymology

From the noun 'telescope', modeled after how a telescope contracts by sliding its sections together.
