contingent

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪndʒənt / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒɪnt / (BrE)
A group of people representing a larger group, organization, or country, especially at a meeting or event.

Examples

Baltimore's bulky spring training contingent now gradually will be reduced as Manager Paul Richards and his coaches seek to trim it down to a more streamlined and workable unit.

Synset Examples

each nation sent a contingent of athletes to the Olympics

Etymology

From Latin 'contingent-', meaning 'befalling, happening by chance', from 'contingere' ('to touch, befall').
