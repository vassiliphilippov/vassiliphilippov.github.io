hobby

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈhɒbi / (BrE)
An activity done regularly in one's leisure time for pleasure.

Examples

Folklore is his hobby, and he, all too rightly, wishes it to remain as such.
What you were looking for (unless you make a hobby of collecting old tennis rackets and fly screens) eludes me, but to judge from phonograph records scattered about a fumed oak Victrola, you danced two tangos and a paso doble, which must have been fairly enervating in that milieu.
It turns out Andy's favorite hobby was toting his wall out into the exercise yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocation - a hobby or minor occupation
  • Antonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one that involves prolonged training and formal qualification

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'hobyn' (small horse or pony); later applied figuratively to a favorite pastime (hobby-horse), giving rise to current meaning.
