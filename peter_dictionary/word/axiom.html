axiom

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksiəm / (AmE), / ˈæksiəm / (BrE)
A statement or saying that is widely accepted as true or self-evident without proof; a maxim.

Examples

The content of his words may lapse back into monotonous repetition, as if a phonograph needle were stuck in one groove; only seldom is it so simple as to be a matter of his obviously parroting some timeworn axiom, common to our culture, which he has evidently heard, over and over, from a parent until he experiences it as part of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxim - a general truth, fundamental principle, or rule of conduct
  • Synonym: aphorism - a short, witty statement expressing a general truth
  • Synonym: proverb - a short pithy saying in general use expressing a commonly held idea

Etymology

From Greek axiōma (that which is thought worthy or fit), from axios (worthy).
