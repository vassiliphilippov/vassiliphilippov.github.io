equality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɑlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɒlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities.

Examples

I used to love this country and believe that someday we 'd win our battle for equality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inequality - the state of being unequal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • Synonym: parity - the state or condition of being equal, especially regarding status or pay

Etymology

From Latin aequalitas, from aequalis meaning 'equal'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɑləti / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɒlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities; the condition of being essentially the same or balanced in degree or value.

Examples

On the one hand, the major European nations had to maintain vis-a-vis each other an emphasis upon sovereignty, independence, formal equality - thus insuring for themselves individually an optimal freedom of action to maintain the "flexibility of alignment "that the system required and to avoid anything approaching a repetition of the disastrous Napoleonic experience.

Synset Examples

on a par with the best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parity - state of being equal, especially in status or pay
  • Antonym: inequality - state of being unequal or uneven

Etymology

From Latin aequalitas (likeness, parity), from aequalis (equal).
