cookie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki / (AmE), / ˈkʊki / (BrE)
A small, flat, sweet baked treat, typically made with flour, sugar, and fat, and often containing chocolate chips, nuts, or dried fruit. In British English, called a 'biscuit'.

Examples

Virginia treated him with attention and tried to tempt his appetite with special food: biscuits, cookies, candies - the result of devoted hours in the tiled kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biscuit - British term for a small, sweet baked treat

Etymology

From Dutch 'koekje' meaning 'little cake'.
