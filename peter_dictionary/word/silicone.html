silicone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪləˌkoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪˌkəʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic compound made from silicon, oxygen, carbon, and hydrogen, used for its stability and flexibility in products such as lubricants, adhesives, sealants, and medical implants.

Examples

Figure 3 shows the data on a silicone fluid, labeled 12500 cps which gave a high positive normal pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicon - a chemical element, Si, used in electronics and as a base for silicones

Etymology

From silicon, with a suffix -one to indicate similarity to ketones, though the chemical structure is different.
