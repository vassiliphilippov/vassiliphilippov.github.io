albumin

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjumɪn / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːmɪn / (BrE)
a simple water-soluble protein found in many animal tissues and fluids, such as blood, egg white, and milk

Examples

The second contained incomplete antibody and showed titers of 1: 256 in albumin and 1: 2048 by the indirect Coombs test.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: globulin - a group of simple proteins in the blood, differing from albumin

Etymology

From Latin 'albumen' (egg white, literally 'whiteness').
