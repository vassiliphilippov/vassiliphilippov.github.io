incurably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be cured, changed, or remedied.

Examples

Only the vain and incurably sentimental among us will lose sleep simply because foreign peoples are not as impressed by our strength as they ought to be.

Synset Examples

she was incurably optimistic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curably - in a way that can be cured or remedied

Etymology

From 'incurable' + '-ly'; 'incurable' from Latin 'incūrābilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'cūrāre' (to care for).
