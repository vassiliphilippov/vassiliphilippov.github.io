west

1. NOUN

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
The direction towards the point of the horizon where the sun sets, opposite to east; one of the four main points of the compass (270 degrees).

Examples

Nomia melanderi can be found in tremendous numbers in certain parts of the United States west of the Great Plains, for example, in Utah and central Washington.
To the west of this road was another low bluff, forty or fifty feet high, covered with scrub oak and other brush.
Annisberg was about seventy-five miles west of Birmingham, near the Georgia border and on the Tallahoosa River, a small and dirty stream.
And now the redcoats were coming, and the gunfire was a part of the dust cloud on the road to the west of us.
Wherever you looked, you saw Committeemen running across the meadows, some away from the road, some toward it, some parallel to it; and about a mile to the west a cluster of at least fifty militia were making their way in our direction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east - the direction opposite to west; one of the four main points of the compass (90 degrees)

Etymology

From Old English 'west', from Proto-Germanic 'westrą', related to Latin 'vesper' (evening).

2. ADJ

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
Situated in, facing, or moving toward the west direction.

Examples

Someone said that while we were standing here and arguing about it, the British would be gone; but Cousin Simmons said he had watched them marching west early in the morning, and moving at a much brisker pace it had still taken half an hour for their column to pass, what with the narrowness of the road and their baggage and ammunition carts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east - situated in or facing or moving toward the east
  • Antonym: north - situated in or facing or moving toward the north
  • Antonym: south - situated in or facing or moving toward the south

Etymology

From Old English *west*, from Proto-Germanic *westrą*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *wes- (evening, west).

4. NOUN

/ wɛst / (AmE), / wɛst / (BrE)
The direction toward the point of the horizon where the sun sets; one of the four principal compass points, opposite to east.

Examples

We have to find a spot on the West Side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east - the direction opposite west; cardinal compass point
  • Antonym: north - perpendicular cardinal compass point; at right angles to west and east
  • Antonym: south - perpendicular cardinal compass point; at right angles to west and east

Etymology

From Old English 'west', from Proto-Germanic 'westrą', ultimately from Proto-Indo-European 'wes-', meaning 'evening' or 'west'.
