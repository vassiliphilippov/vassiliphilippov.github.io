qualitatively

1. ADV

/ ˈkwɑːlɪtəˌtɪvli / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪtəˌtɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is measured by the quality of something rather than its quantity.

Examples

Have you estimated your sales manpower needs for the future (both quantitatively and qualitatively)?

Synset Examples

this discoloration qualitatively suggests that the substance is low in inorganic iron

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quantitatively - in a quantitative manner (in terms of quantity rather than quality)

Etymology

From 'qualitative' + '-ly'; 'qualitative' from Medieval Latin qualitativus.
