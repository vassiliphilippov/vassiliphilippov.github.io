bowstring

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
the strong cord that is attached to both ends of an archer’s bow and is used to launch the arrow

Examples

Nerves tight as a bowstring, he paused to gather his wits.

Etymology

From 'bow' + 'string'; 'bow' from Old English 'boga', 'string' from Old English 'streng'.
