juror

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊrər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊərə / (BrE)
a member of a jury who is selected to hear and decide a case in a court of law

Examples

The jurors said they realize "a proportionate distribution of these funds might disable this program in our less populous counties ".
Nevertheless, "we feel that in the future Fulton County should receive some portion of these available funds ", the jurors said.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jury - a group of people selected to give a verdict in a legal case

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French jurour, from Latin *jurare* (to swear).
