brute

1. ADJ

/ bruːt / (AmE), / bruːt / (BrE)
(of force, power, or behavior) resembling a beast; lacking reasoning or human sensibility; purely physical or animal-like.

Examples

The already faded pastel charms of the naive music itself vanish entirely in Fistoulari's melodramatic contrasts between ultra vehement brute power and chilly, if suave, sentimentality.

Synset Examples

beastly desires
a bestial nature
brute force
a dull and brutish man
bestial treatment of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bestial - resembling a beast; lacking reason or sensibility
  • Synonym: beastly - cruel; resembling a beast
  • Antonym: gentle - kind; showing human warmth or compassion

Etymology

From Latin brutus (heavy, dull, stupid).

2. NOUN

/ brut / (AmE), / bruːt / (BrE)
A person who is savagely violent or cruel.

Examples

What more could be asked for a Trial intended to warn the world against anti-Semitism than this opportunity to expose the exact link between the respectable anti-Semite and the concentration-camp brute?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beast - a person who behaves in a cruel, savage, or brutish way
  • Synonym: monster - a cruel or inhuman person

Etymology

From Latin 'brutus' (dull, stupid, insensible).
