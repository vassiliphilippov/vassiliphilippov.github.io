inventive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
(of a person or their ideas) having the ability to create or design new things or to think originally.

Examples

Stowey Rummel was internationally famous, a crafter of a genuine Americana in foreign eyes, an original designer whose inventive childishness with steel and concrete was made even more believably sincere by his personality.

Synset Examples

an imaginative use of material
the invention of the knitting frame by another ingenious English clergyman
an ingenious device
had an inventive turn of mind
inventive ceramics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative - having the ability or power to create; imaginative
  • Synonym: original - new and not derived from something else; innovative
  • Antonym: uninventive - lacking creativity or originality

Etymology

From Latin inventivus (able to discover), from invenire (to come upon, find, invent).
