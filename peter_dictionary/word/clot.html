clot

1. VERB

/ klɑt / (AmE), / klɒt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, especially blood) to thicken or solidify from a fluid state, often by forming clumps or lumps.

Examples

Blood samples were allowed to clot at room temperature for 3 hr, centrifuged and the serum was removed.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulate - to change from a fluid to a thickened mass

Etymology

From Old English 'clott' (lump, mass), related to Middle Dutch 'klot' (clod, ball).

2. NOUN

/ klɑt / (AmE), / klɒt / (BrE)
a thick, sticky lump formed when a liquid, especially blood, changes into a semi-solid mass

Examples

Brain examined for thrombosis, clot or hemorrhage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombus - a medical term for a blood clot within the vascular system
  • Antonym: dissolve - to break up or become liquid again (opposite of clot in some contexts)

Etymology

From Old English 'clott' (mass, clod).
