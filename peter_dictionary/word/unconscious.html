unconscious

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑːn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
(followed by 'of') not aware or not realizing; failing to notice or perceive something.

Examples

Time elapsed but the doctor was obviously unconscious of its passage until an unwelcome knock on the door interrupted the processes of nature.

Synset Examples

happily unconscious of the new calamity at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness about something

Etymology

From un- (not) + conscious; conscious from Latin 'conscius' (knowing with, aware).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
Not done or experienced consciously; occurring or existing without deliberate thought or intention.

Examples

"I paint the nothing ", he said once to Franz Kline and myself, "the nothing that is behind the something, the inexpressible, unpaintable' tick' in the unconscious, the' spirit' of the moment resting forever, suspended like a huge balloon, in non time ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conscious - aware of and responding to one's surroundings or actions

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'conscious', from Latin 'conscius' (knowing with others or in oneself).

4. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
the part of the mind that contains thoughts, feelings, and memories of which a person is not aware, often influencing behavior

Examples

As I have been intimating, in the schizophrenic - and perhaps also in the dreams of the neurotic; this is a question which I have no wish to take up - condensation is a phenomenon in which one finds not a condensed expression of various feelings and ideas which are, at an unconscious level, well sorted out, but rather a condensed expression of feelings and ideas which, even in the unconscious, have yet to become well differentiated from one another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subconscious - existing or operating in the mind beneath or beyond conscious awareness

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'conscious' (aware), denoting what is not in conscious awareness.
