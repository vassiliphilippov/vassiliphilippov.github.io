debilitate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈbɪlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
To make someone or something physically weak or feeble.

Examples

The body was that of a well developed, somewhat debilitated white man weighing 108 lb. There were bilateral pterygia and arcus senilis, and the mouth was edentulous.

Synset Examples

Life in the camp drained him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enfeeble - to make very weak
  • Antonym: strengthen - to make stronger or more powerful

Etymology

From Latin 'debilitat-', past participle of 'debilitare' (to weaken), from 'debilis' (weak).
