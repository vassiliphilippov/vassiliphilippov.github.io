bleach

1. VERB

/ bliːtʃ / (AmE), / bliːtʃ / (BrE)
to remove color from something, typically by exposing it to sunlight or by using a chemical agent, usually in order to make it lighter or white

Examples

New York lay bleaching in the summer sun, and the morning fish hawk, flying in the heated air, saw below him the long triangular wedge of Manhattan Island.

Synset Examples

The sun bleached the red shirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiten - make or become white
  • Antonym: darken - make or become dark

Etymology

From Old English *blǣcan* (to whiten), from *blāc* (pale, shining).
