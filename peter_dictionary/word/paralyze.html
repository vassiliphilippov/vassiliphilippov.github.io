paralyze

1. VERB

/ ˈpæ.rə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈpar.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
To make someone or something powerless or unable to function; to bring to a complete standstill.

Examples

He was n't troubled with the ordinary, rank-and-file fear that overcomes and paralyzes and sends individual soldiers and whole companies under fire running in panic.

Synset Examples

The bureaucracy paralyzes the entire operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobilize - to prevent something from moving or operating
  • Antonym: enable - to make able or possible

Etymology

From French 'paraliser' and Late Latin 'paralysi', from Greek 'paralysis'.
