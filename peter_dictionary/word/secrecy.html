secrecy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkrəsi / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrəsi / (BrE)
The state of keeping something secret or the act of not revealing information.

Examples

Our weapons production, our world prestige, our ideas of democracy, our actions of trust or stubbornness or secrecy or espionage?
That was the new advertising angle - something about a Lloyd's of London policy to insure the secrecy of the secret ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidentiality - the quality of keeping information private or secret
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being open and transparent

Etymology

From Latin 'secretus' (set apart, hidden).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkrəsi / (AmE), / ˈsiːkrəsi / (BrE)
The state or condition of being concealed or hidden from others; the act of keeping something secret.

Examples

Since the magnitude of the plan made secrecy impossible, once the wheels had began to turn, persons controlling German industries, social institutions, and armed forces became, through their anti-Semitism or their tolerance of it, conscious accomplices of Hitler's crimes; whether in the last degree or a lesser one was a matter to be determined individually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privacy - the state of being free from being observed or disturbed by others
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of not being secretive or hidden

Etymology

From Latin secretus (set apart, hidden), via Old French secrète and Middle English secreci.
