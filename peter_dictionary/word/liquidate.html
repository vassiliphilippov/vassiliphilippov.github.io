liquidate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to eliminate or kill someone, especially as a deliberate or systematic act, often for political reasons

Examples

If he had been "liquidated "in some way, he would have become a martyr, a rallying point for people who shared his ideas.
Perhaps Khrushchev is in a more difficult position than any since 1957, when the "anti-party group "nearly liquidated him.

Synset Examples

The mafia liquidated the informer
the double agent was neutralized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eliminate - to remove or get rid of someone or something, especially by deliberate action
  • Synonym: neutralize - to prevent someone from being a threat, sometimes by killing them

Etymology

From Latin liquidare (to melt, make liquid), later extended in jargon to 'clear away'.
