beggary

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡəri / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəri / (BrE)
the state or condition of living by begging for money or food, especially in public

Examples

Reduced to beggary, he at last got a job as office boy to a television producer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begging - the act of asking for money or food as charity

Etymology

From Middle English beggerie, from beggar + -y, meaning the state or condition of being a beggar.
