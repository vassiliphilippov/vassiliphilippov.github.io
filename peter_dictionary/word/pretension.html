pretension

1. NOUN

/ priˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
The act of claiming or presenting oneself as having a certain quality or importance, especially when this claim is false or not supported by facts.

Examples

Today Sukarno's government is heavily besieged by avowed Communists, and for all of its "neutralist "pretensions, it is a firm ally of Soviet policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - act of pretending or making a false display
  • Synonym: affectation - behavior that is artificial and intended to impress
  • Antonym: humility - modest or low view of one's own importance

Etymology

From French prétention, from Medieval Latin praetensio (a stretching forth, pretension), from Latin praetendere (to pretend).
