heedless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhidləs / (AmE), / ˈhiːdləs / (BrE)
not paying attention to warnings or advice; careless or thoughtless.

Examples

When a husband is sexually selfish and heedless of his wife's desires, she is cheated of the fulfillment and pleasure nature intended for her.

Synset Examples

We have always known that heedless self-interest was bad morals; we know now that it is bad economics
heedless of danger
heedless of the child's crying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention to something

Etymology

From Old English hǣdlēas, from 'heed' (care, attention) + '-less' (without).
