sorrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊ / (BrE)
A feeling of deep distress or sadness, especially caused by the loss of someone or something loved.

Examples

Her mother wrote Kate of her grief at the death of Kate's baby and at Jonathan's decision to go with the South "And, dear Kate ", she wrote, "poor Dr. Breckenridge's son Robert is now organizing a militia company to go South, to his good father's sorrow.
Nothing in all the preceding years had had the power to bring me closer to a knowledge of profound sorrow than the breakup of camp, the packing away of my camp uniforms, the severing of ties with the six or ten people I had grown most to love in the world.
In our age of Science and Angst it seems to me more brave to stay on Earth and explore inner man than to fly far from the sphere of our sorrow and explore outer space ".

Synset Examples

he tried to express his sorrow at her loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness

Etymology

From Old English *sorh* (grief, regret), related to Old High German *sorga* (care, worry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəʊ / (BrE)
A cause of great unhappiness or distress.

Examples

Whosoever violates our rooftree, the legend states, can expect maximal sorrow.

Synset Examples

her death was a great grief to John

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death; synonym for 'sorrow'
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling or cause of great happiness; antonym of 'sorrow'

Etymology

Old English *sorg* (grief, regret), related to Old High German *sorga*.
