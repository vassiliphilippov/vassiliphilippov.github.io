scoop

1. NOUN

/ skuːp / (AmE), / skuːp / (BrE)
An amount that can be contained in a scoop or scooped up at one time.

Examples

I put a little scoop of pulverized phosphate rock or steamed bone meal into each hole with the plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoonful - an amount held by a spoon
  • Synonym: handful - an amount that can be held in one hand

Etymology

From Middle English *skope* (scoop, ladle), from Middle Dutch *schope* or Old Norse *skopa* (to scoop, ladle).
