derision

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪʒən / (BrE)
The act of ridiculing or mocking someone or something; contemptuous laughter or criticism.

Examples

Dimly, he heard laughter, hoots of derision, but he could not read the racket properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockery - the act of ridiculing or making fun of someone or something
  • Synonym: contempt - feeling that someone or something is worthless or beneath consideration
  • Antonym: respect - admiration or regard for someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'derisio', from 'deridere' (to mock, to laugh at).
