indefatigable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈfætɪɡəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfætɪɡəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or their efforts) persisting tirelessly; unable to be fatigued or wearied.

Examples

But perhaps this was a part of the eternal plan, that man's ambition when linked with God would be a driving, indefatigable force for good in the world.

Synset Examples

an indefatigable advocate of equal rights
a tireless worker
unflagging pursuit of excellence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tireless - showing great effort or persistence without fatigue
  • Antonym: exhausted - very tired; drained of strength or energy

Etymology

From Latin indefatigabilis, from in- (not) + defatigare (to tire out).
