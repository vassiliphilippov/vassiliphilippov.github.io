remunerative

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmjunjəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːn(ə)rətɪv / (BrE)
Providing payment or financial reward; offering compensation for work or services.

Examples

To assist the States, therefore, in rehabilitating handicapped individuals, "so that they may prepare for and engage in remunerative employment to the extent of their capabilities ", the 83 rd Congress enacted the Vocational Rehabilitation Amendments of 1954 (P. L. 565).

Synset Examples

a paying job
remunerative work
salaried employment
stipendiary services

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensable - eligible to be compensated; deserving payment
  • Synonym: paying - providing payment or salary

Etymology

From Latin 'remunerativus', from 'remunerari' (to reward).
