threadbare

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛdˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdbɛə / (BrE)
(of an idea, expression, or argument) overused and unoriginal; lacking freshness or originality due to excessive use.

Examples

These trumps were more touching than they were anything else, and seemed to imply that the nights were long, her children ungrateful, and her marriage bewilderingly threadbare.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons
his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor `hard as nails'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackneyed - (of phrases or ideas) unoriginal through overuse
  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness, overused
  • Antonym: fresh - (of ideas, expressions) new and original

Etymology

Originally referring to cloth worn so thin that the threads show, from 'thread' and 'bare'.
