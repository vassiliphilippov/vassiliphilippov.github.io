portrait

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtrət / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtrɪt / (BrE)
a representation or image of a person, especially of the face, created in any medium such as painting, drawing, photograph, or sculpture

Examples

There were many letters of strong protest against the portrait of the Anglican clergyman, who was indeed portrayed as a man not particularly concerned with religious matters and without really very much to do as clergyman.
He tried to order the words of the three Union officers, seeking to create some coherent portrait of the dead boy.

Synset Examples

the photographer made excellent portraits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likeness - a representation or image of someone or something
  • Antonym: caricature - a picture or image exaggerating peculiarities or defects

Etymology

From Middle French *portrait*, past participle of *portraire* (to depict), from Latin *protrahere* (to draw forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔr.trɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.trɪt / (BrE)
A description or representation of a person’s appearance and character in words.

Examples

My interviews with teen-agers confirmed this portrait of the weakening of religious and ethnic bonds.
The planter aristocracy has appeared in literature at least since John Pendleton Kennedy published Swallow-Barn in 1832 and in his genial portrait of Frank Meriwether presiding over his plantation dominion initiated the most persistent tradition of Southern literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profile - a short written description of someone or something
  • Degree Higher: caricature - a description or imitation that exaggerates for comic effect

Etymology

From Middle French *portrait* (a likeness), from Old French *portraire* (to portray).
