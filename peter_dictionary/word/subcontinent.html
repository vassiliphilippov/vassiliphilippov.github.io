subcontinent

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈkɑntɪnənt / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒntɪnənt / (BrE)
A large and distinct landmass that forms a subdivision of a continent, often due to geographical, cultural, or historical factors.

Examples

They hail from Travancore, a state in the subcontinent where Kali, the goddess of death, is worshiped.

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'continent' (from 'continentia', landmass).
