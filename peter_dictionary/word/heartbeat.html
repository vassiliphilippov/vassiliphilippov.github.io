heartbeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbit / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbiːt / (BrE)
the regular movement or pulsation felt as the heart contracts and pumps blood through the body

Examples

The original electrocardiograph primarily indicates irregularities in the heartbeat, but today's techniques allow exact measurements of the flow of blood through the aorta, dimensioning of the heart and its chambers, and a much more detailed study of each heartbeat.

Synset Examples

he could feel the beat of her heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - the regular throbbing of arteries as blood is propelled through them, typically felt at the wrist or neck

Etymology

From 'heart' + 'beat', indicating the beating of the heart.
