barefoot

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɛə.fʊt / (BrE)
Wearing nothing on the feet; without shoes or socks.

Examples

She had had a dignity about her, even barefoot and almost too tan.
But he was looking forward to snow - seeing for himself that each tiny crystal of the water of life was a unique individual, as he had read - walking barefoot, rolling in it.

Synset Examples

the barefoot boy
shoeless Joe Jackson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoeless - not wearing shoes

Etymology

From Middle English 'bare' (without covering) + 'foot'.

2. ADV

/ ˈbɛrˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈbeəfʊt / (BrE)
Without wearing any shoes or other covering on the feet.

Examples

Now Pathet Lao propagandists were reported marching him barefoot from village to village, as evidence of evil American intervention.

Synset Examples

he chased her barefoot across the meadow

Etymology

From Old English 'bǣrfot', literally 'bare' + 'foot'.
