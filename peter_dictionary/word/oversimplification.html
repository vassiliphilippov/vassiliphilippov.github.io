oversimplification

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˌsɪmpləfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of simplifying a situation, problem, or explanation so much that it misrepresents or distorts the true nature of the subject

Examples

Anyone's identification with an international struggle, whether warlike or peaceful, requires absurd oversimplification and intense emotional involvement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to understand

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + simplification.
