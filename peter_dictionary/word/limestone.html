limestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪmstəʊn / (BrE)
A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate, often formed from the remains of marine organisms.

Examples

Limestone.

Etymology

From Middle English 'lymeston', from 'lime' (referring to lime or calcium) + 'stone'.
