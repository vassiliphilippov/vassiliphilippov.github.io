theft

1. NOUN

/ θɛft / (AmE), / θɛft / (BrE)
The act of taking another person's property illegally and without permission.

Examples

You know your oldest boy, Victor, was picked up in Reno for some petty theft you don'’t even know about.

Synset Examples

the thieving is awful at Kennedy International

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larceny - theft of personal property
  • Synonym: robbery - theft involving force or threat
  • Synonym: burglary - illegal entry to commit theft

Etymology

From Old English thēofth, related to 'thief'.
