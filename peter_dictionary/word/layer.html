layer

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪə / (BrE)
a sheet or stratum of material, substance, or tissue that lies over or under another

Examples

The results of present observations of the thermal radio emission of the moon are consistent with the very low thermal conductivity of the surface layer which was derived from the variation in the infrared emission during eclipses (e.g., Garstung, 1958).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratum - a layer or a series of layers of rock or other material

Etymology

From Middle English layere (one who lays), from Old English lecgan (to lay).
