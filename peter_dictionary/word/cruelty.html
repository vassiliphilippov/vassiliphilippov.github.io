cruelty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːəlti / (AmE), / ˈkruːəlti / (BrE)
The quality of having extreme heartlessness or indifference to suffering; behavior or feelings showing a lack of compassion.

Examples

His petition charged mental cruelty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From Old French cruelté, from Latin crudelitas, from crudelis (cruel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːəlti / (AmE), / ˈkruːəlti / (BrE)
Deliberate and unnecessary infliction of pain or suffering; cruel behavior or a cruel act.

Examples

Secondly, to find a learned diversion and a pleasing joke in More's account of the stupid brutalities of early sixteenth century wars, of the anguish of the poor and dispossessed, of the insolence and cruelty of the rich and powerful requires a callousness toward suffering and sin that would be surprising in a moral imbecile and most surprising in More himself.
cruelty, meanness, self-will.
His name became synonymous with cold-blooded cruelty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate; the opposite of cruelty

Etymology

From Old French cruauté, from Latin crudelitas (cruelty).
