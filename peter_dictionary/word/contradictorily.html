contradictorily

1. ADV

/ kənˌtrædəˈktɔrəli / (AmE), / kənˌtrædəˈktɒrəli / (BrE)
In a way that involves contradictions or is inconsistent with itself.

Examples

They argue further (and somewhat contradictorily) that our knowledge and resources in preventive medicine would make it possible to control such an outbreak of disease.

Synset Examples

he argued contradictorily

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistently - in a way that is always the same or in agreement
  • Synonym: paradoxically - in a seemingly absurd or self-contradictory way

Etymology

From 'contradictory' + '-ly'. 'Contradictory' from Latin 'contradictorius' (asserting the contrary).
