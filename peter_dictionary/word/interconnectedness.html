interconnectedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛktɪdnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being connected with each other; mutual connection or relationship between things.

Examples

As long as perception is seen as composed only of isolated sense data, most of the quality and interconnectedness of existence loses its objectivity, becomes an invention of consciousness, and the result is a philosophical scepticism.

Synset Examples

an interconnection between the two buildings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - a link or relationship between things

Etymology

Formed from 'interconnect' + '-ed' + '-ness', originally from Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'connectere' (to bind together).
