claustrophobia

1. NOUN

/ klɔstrəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / klɔːstrəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
an extreme or irrational fear of being in enclosed or confined spaces

Examples

It was only a fifteen minute flight, but before it was through Greg felt himself developing a case of claustrophobia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agoraphobia - fear of open or public spaces

Etymology

From Latin 'claustrum' (a closed space) + Greek 'phobos' (fear).
