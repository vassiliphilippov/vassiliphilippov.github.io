plunder

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndə / (BrE)
Goods or money taken illegally, especially by force or during war; stolen property.

Examples

His plunder is therefore far higher - running into hundreds of millions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - goods stolen or taken by force, especially in war

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *plunderen* (to pillage), related to the word for 'household goods'.

2. VERB

/ ˈplʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndə / (BrE)
to take or steal something, especially intellectual property or ideas, illegally or dishonestly

Examples

These voters view the political process as a secret conspiracy, the object of which is to plunder them ".

Synset Examples

This writer plundered from famous authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take (something) without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: appropriate - to take something for one's own use, typically without permission

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *plunderen* (to plunder, to pillage), related to *plunder* (household goods)
