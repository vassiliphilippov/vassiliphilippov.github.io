faint

1. ADJ

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
not clear, distinct, or easily perceptible; slight or indistinct in degree, quality, or appearance.

Examples

After these treatments the conjugate did not stain healthy or crown gall sweet clover tissues or stained them a very faint green which was easily distinguishable from the bright yellow-green specific staining.
He looked out through windowpanes turned a faint violet by sun and weather, looked out at King's Bridge toward Westchester.
I wore a new double-breasted brown worsted suit with a faint herringbone design and wide lapels like a devil's ears.
When this linear draft is completed, I dust it down to a faint image.

Synset Examples

a dim figure in the distance
only a faint recollection
shadowy figures in the gloom
saw a vague outline of a building through the fog
a few wispy memories of childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dim - not bright or clear; lacking distinctness
  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or perceived
  • Antonym: distinct - clearly defined or easy to perceive
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, see, or understand
  • Synonym: shadowy - not clearly visible or distinct

Etymology

From Old French *feint* (feigned, weak), from Latin *fingere* (to shape, form, feign).

2. ADJ

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
(of a sight, sound, smell, or feeling) barely perceptible; lacking strength, brightness, clarity, or loudness.

Examples

Faint at first, the tapping grew until it sounded loud against the wind.
One could hear a very faint, ladylike sigh of relief.
Cool air moving slowly through the open or smashed out side windows hinted of blooming roadside vegetation, and occasionally a faint fragrance of perfume swirled from the back seat.

Synset Examples

a faint outline
the wan sun cast faint shadows
the faint light of a distant candle
weak colors
a faint hissing sound
a faint aroma
a weak pulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking strength or power; not strong
  • Synonym: dim - not bright or clear
  • Antonym: strong - having great intensity, brightness, or power
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; vivid

Etymology

From Old French *feint* ‘false, feigned’, from Latin *fingere* ‘to shape, pretend’.

3. ADJ

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
(of a person or their state) feeling weak and close to losing consciousness.

Examples

Planes made her feel faint, and in Tokyo, where she had gone that summer, she had been given raw fish for breakfast and so she had come straight home.

Synset Examples

suddenly felt faint from the pain
was sick and faint from hunger
felt light in the head
a swooning fit
light-headed with wine
light-headed from lack of sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might fall or lose balance
  • Synonym: light-headed - feeling faint or as if you might lose consciousness
  • Antonym: strong - having physical power or feeling robust

Etymology

From Old French *faint* (weak, cowardly), from Latin *fingere* (to shape, devise, feign).

4. ADJ

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
(of a sound, feeling, effort, or quality) lacking strength, force, or intensity; weak or slight.

Examples

They were climbing the hill in the night when the headlights abruptly probed solid blackness, became two parallel luminous tubes which broadened out into a faint mist of light and ended.
To his faint surprise Russ held up his hand.

Synset Examples

damning with faint praise
faint resistance
feeble efforts
a feeble voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeble - lacking strength or force; weak
  • Antonym: strong - having great strength or intensity

Etymology

From Old French 'feint' (feigned, false), from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, pretend).

5. VERB

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
to suddenly lose consciousness for a short time, usually because of weakness, pain, or emotional distress

Examples

The attempt had failed because, when endeavoring to cut his wrists, this murderer of seven women had fainted at the sight of blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass out - to lose consciousness temporarily
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down and become unconscious or unable to move
  • Antonym: revive - to come back to consciousness after fainting

Etymology

From Old French 'feindre', later influenced by Old French 'faint', meaning weak or feeble.
