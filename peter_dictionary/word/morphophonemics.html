morphophonemics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔrfoʊfəˈnimɪks / (AmE), / ˌmɔːfəfəˈniːmɪks / (BrE)
The branch of linguistics that studies how the phonological form of morphemes changes in different contexts, especially how allomorphs are realized in speech.

Examples

While the phonemes can be very easily stated, no one is likely to be satisfied with the statement until phonemic occurrences can be related in some way to morphemic units, i. e. until the morphophonemics is worked out, or at least far enough that it seems reasonable to expect success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the system of sounds in a language

Etymology

From 'morpho-', relating to form, and 'phonemics', from Greek phōnēma (sound).
