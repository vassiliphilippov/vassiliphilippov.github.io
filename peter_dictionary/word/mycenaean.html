Mycenaean

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈsɪniən / (AmE), / maɪˈsiːniən / (BrE)
of or relating to ancient Mycenae or its inhabitants and culture, especially in the Late Bronze Age (c. 1600–1100 BC) in Greece.

Examples

In this process the Minoan Mycenaean inheritance had been transmuted or finally rejected; the Aegean world which had existed before 1000 differed from that which rises more clearly in our vision after 800.

Synset Examples

Mycenaean bronzes

Etymology

From Mycenae, the ancient Greek city, plus the English suffix -an.
