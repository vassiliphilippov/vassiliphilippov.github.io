mistrust

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈtrʌst / (AmE), / mɪsˈtrʌst / (BrE)
to suspect someone or something is untrustworthy; to lack trust or confidence in

Examples

They were always leaping to light cigarettes, open car doors, fill plates or glasses, and I mistrusted the whole lot of them to the same degree that I mistrusted bake shops that called themselves "Sanitary Bake Shops ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distrust - to have no trust or confidence in; be suspicious of
  • Antonym: trust - to believe in the reliability or honesty of someone or something

Etymology

From mis- (expressing negation) + trust.
