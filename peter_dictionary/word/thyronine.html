thyronine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪrəˌniːn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪrənɪn / (BrE)
a phenolic amino acid from which thyroxine is derived, found in the thyroid gland

Examples

In the thyroid gland it appears that proteins (chiefly thyroglobulin) are iodinated and that free tyrosine and thyronine are not iodinated.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thyroxine - a hormone derived from thyronine, produced by the thyroid gland

Etymology

From 'thyroid' + '-ine' (chemical suffix).
