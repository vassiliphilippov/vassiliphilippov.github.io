rhino

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈraɪnəʊ / (BrE)
A large, heavy animal found in Asia and Africa, with thick skin and one or two horns on its nose.

Examples

"If you saw the drama called Rhinoceros ", I said, "think of the effect it would have on an audience of rhinos when the actor on stage suddenly begins turning into a rhinoceros.
The rhinos would panic, screaming' Help'!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinoceros - large plant-eating mammal with thick skin and one or two horns

Etymology

Shortened from 'rhinoceros', from Greek 'rhinokerōs' ('rhino-' meaning 'nose' + 'keras' meaning 'horn').
