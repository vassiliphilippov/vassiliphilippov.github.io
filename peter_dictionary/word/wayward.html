wayward

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪ.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.wəd / (BrE)
Difficult to control or predict because of unusual or stubborn behavior; resistant to guidance or discipline.

Examples

Each of the four wayward shots cost him two strokes.
If it could be shown that judgments of good and bad were not judgments at all, that they asserted nothing true or false, but merely expressed emotions like "Hurrah "or "Fiddlesticks ", then these wayward judgments would cease from troubling and weary heads could be at rest.

Synset Examples

Mary Mary quite contrary
an obstinate child with a violent temper
a perverse mood
wayward behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - opposing or stubborn in nature
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: tractable - easily managed or controlled

Etymology

From Middle English wayward, likely from 'way' + '-ward', meaning turned away from the right way.
