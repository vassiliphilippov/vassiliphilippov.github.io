awarding

1. NOUN

/ əˈwɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈwɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of officially granting something, especially money or compensation, as decided by a court or other authority

Examples

The jury also commented on the Fulton ordinary's court which has been under fire for its practices in the appointment of appraisers, guardians and administrators and the awarding of fees and compensation.

Synset Examples

he criticized the awarding of compensation by the court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - an amount or item granted by decision, especially by a court

Etymology

From the verb 'award', which comes from Anglo-Norman 'awarder', from Old French 'awardier', ultimately from 'a-' (to) + 'ward' (guard, keep).
