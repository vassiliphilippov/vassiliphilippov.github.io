doomed

1. NOUN

/ duːmd / (AmE), / duːmd / (BrE)
People who are certain to die soon, often due to unavoidable circumstances or fate.

Examples

Then Pentangeli is a dead man.

Synset Examples

the agony of the doomed was in his voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemned - people sentenced to death

Etymology

From the past participle of 'doom', meaning to condemn or sentence, from Old English 'dōm' (judgment, law).
