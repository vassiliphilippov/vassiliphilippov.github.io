aerobic

1. ADJ

/ ɛˈroʊbɪk / (AmE), / ɛəˈrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
(of a process or organism) requiring free oxygen or air for growth, survival, or functioning.

Examples

It was possible to maintain aerobic conditions in the pond by regular additions of sodium nitrate until the temperature decreased and the algae population changed from blue-green to green algae.

Synset Examples

aerobic fermentation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaerobic - not requiring free oxygen or air

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'bios' (life); coined in late 19th century.
