hex

1. NOUN

/ hɛks / (AmE), / hɛks / (BrE)
A spell or curse that is believed to bring bad luck or harm.

Examples

Additionally, since you 're going to be hors de combat pretty soon with sprue, yaws, Delhi boil, the Granville wilt, liver fluke, bilharziasis, and a host of other complications of the hex you 've aroused, you must n't expect to be lionized socially.

Synset Examples

a witch put a curse on his whole family
he put the whammy on me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a prayer or invocation for harm or injury to come upon someone
  • Antonym: blessing - a prayer or invocation for good or protection; the opposite of a curse

Etymology

Probably from Pennsylvania German *hexe* (to practice witchcraft), from German *Hexe* (witch).
