apprehend

1. VERB

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to understand or grasp the meaning, significance, or nature of something

Examples

There is no framework or structure of thought with respect to which we can organize it and no part of reality, as we know and apprehend it, with respect to which we can refer this experience.
Though they are often heard clairaudiently, as if a voice were speaking them, in other cases they are apprehended visually as symbols: a slope to signify the name "Hill ", for instance.

Synset Examples

Do you comprehend the meaning of this letter?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehend - to understand, grasp mentally
  • Synonym: understand - to perceive the meaning of something
  • Antonym: misunderstand - fail to interpret or understand correctly

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere (to lay hold of, seize; to understand), from ad- (to) + prehendere (to seize).

2. VERB

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌaprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
(of the police or authorities) to arrest and take someone into custody.

Examples

Thus it was that Barco, apprehended for mere larceny, now began to suspect that one or another of his murders had been uncovered.

Synset Examples

the police nabbed the suspected criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrest - to seize someone by legal authority and take into custody
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from custody

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere, from ad- (to) + prehendere (to seize).
