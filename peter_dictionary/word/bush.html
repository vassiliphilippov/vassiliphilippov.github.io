bush

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
Dense vegetation made up of stunted trees or shrubs, typically forming an area that is difficult to cross or cultivate.

Examples

A strange wood creature came floating up from a patch of berry bushes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or trees
  • Synonym: scrub - vegetation consisting mainly of stunted trees and bushes; brush

2. NOUN

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
A low woody plant, typically with several stems rising from or near the ground, smaller than a tree.

Examples

Out of long experience I have found that incidental figures and other objects like trees, logs, and bushes can be traced from the original sketch and moved about in the major areas on the final sheet until they occupy the right position, which I call' clicking'.
Then they had always been romping around him on these walks, yelping with delight, dashing off into the bushes on fruitless hunting expeditions, returning to jump up on him triumphantly with muddy paws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrub - a low woody plant with several stems; synonym for bush

Etymology

From Old English 'bysc', of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ bʊʃ / (AmE), / bʊʃ / (BrE)
(particularly in Australia or Africa) an area of wild, uncultivated country or forest, especially one covered with vegetation and not settled by people.

Examples

For a year the boy had lived in the bush in a boxcar.
Did many of Sam's countrymen live in boxcars in the bush?
