surpass

1. VERB

/ sərˈpæs / (AmE), / səˈpɑːs / (BrE)
To do or be better than someone or something; to exceed or excel beyond a particular limit or achievement.

Examples

Tailback James Saxton already has surpassed his rushing total for his brilliant sophomore season, when he netted 271 yards on 55 carries; he now has 273 yards in 22 tries during three games.
Those would be reserved for the orchestra's great nights when the soloist can surpass himself.

Synset Examples

She excelled in math

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excel - To be exceptionally good at an activity or subject

Etymology

From Old French surpasser, from sur- (over) + passer (to pass).

2. VERB

/ sərˈpæs / (AmE), / səˈpɑːs / (BrE)
to do or be better or greater than someone or something; to exceed or go beyond

Examples

During the rest of the summer my scholarly mania for making plaster casts and spatter prints of Catskill flowers and leaves was all but surpassed by the constantly renewed impressions of Jessica that my mind served up to me for contemplation and delight.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceed - to be greater than a particular number or amount
  • Synonym: outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Antonym: fall short - to fail to reach a required standard or amount

Etymology

From Old French surpasser, from sur- (over) + passer (to pass).
