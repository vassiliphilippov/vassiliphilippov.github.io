spume

1. NOUN

/ spjum / (AmE), / spjuÀêm / (BrE)
froth or foam formed on the sea or ocean, especially as a result of waves breaking

Examples

One soft evening - that marvelous sea blessed time when the sun's departing warmth lingers and a smell of spume and wrack haunts everything - Amy had picked herself off the floor and begun to walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed in or on a liquid
  • Synonym: froth - a mass of small white bubbles on the top of a liquid

Etymology

From Latin spuma (foam, froth).
