monochrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.krəʊm / (BrE)
A painting or artwork executed in varying tones of a single color.

Examples

Everywhere else his ideas lay or hung in visible form: his models, drawings, ten foot canvases in monochromes from his painting days, and underfoot a windfall of broken-backed books that looked as though their insides had been ransacked by a maniac.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polychrome - artwork or surface decorated in several colors

Etymology

From French *monochrome*, from Greek *monokhrōmos* (of one color): *mono-* (one) + *khrōma* (color).
