ailing

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
being ill, especially with a persistent or chronic condition; in poor health.

Examples

He missed the 1955 season because of an operation on the ailing knee, then played 77 minutes in 1956.
Shortly after the beginning of Sherman's Georgia campaign, an ailing Yank wrote his homefolk: "The surgeon insisted on Sending me to the hospital for treatment.

Synset Examples

my poor ailing grandmother
feeling a bit indisposed today
you look a little peaked
feeling poorly
a sickly child
is unwell and can't come to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill - suffering from disease or poor health
  • Antonym: healthy - free from illness or injury

Etymology

From the verb 'ail', which comes from Old English *eglan* (to trouble, pain).
