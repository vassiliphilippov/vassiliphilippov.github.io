intellectual

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.t̬əˈlɛk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
a person who is highly developed intellectually, especially one who engages in critical thinking, research, or reflection about ideas

Examples

The capacity of intellectuals and members of the new professional classes to contribute creatively to national development is likely to be destroyed by a constraining sense of inferiority toward both their own political class and their colleagues and professional counterparts in the West.
At a party an English intellectual - so-called - asked me why I write always about distress.
An intellectual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinker - a person who thinks deeply and seriously
  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy
  • Antonym: ignoramus - an ignorant person

Etymology

From Latin intellectualis, from intellectus (understanding).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.t̬əˈlɛk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or involving the use of the intellect or the ability to think and reason.

Examples

in the Republic we have to do with an elite of physical and intellectual athletes, in the apostolic and monastic communities with an elite of spiritual and religious athletes.
Brains and beauty, high position in both the social and intellectual worlds, athlete, fabled lover - if ever the world was any man's oyster it was his.
Conrad's words had intellectual meaning for him only.

Synset Examples

intellectual problems
the triumph of the rational over the animal side of man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental - relating to the mind

Etymology

From Latin intellectualis, from intellectus (understanding).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.t̬əlˈɛk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or involving the use of the intellect; appealing to the reasoning or thinking abilities rather than emotions or instinct.

Examples

However, a growing intellectual sophistication and the new certitudes imparted by courses in psychology and anthropology make the students increasingly critical of their somewhat provincial and overprotective parents.

Synset Examples

satire is an intellectual weapon
intellectual workers engaged in creative literary or artistic or scientific labor
has tremendous intellectual sympathy for oppressed people
coldly intellectual
sort of the intellectual type
intellectual literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental - relating to the mind or intellect
  • Antonym: emotional - appealing to the emotions rather than intellect

Etymology

From Latin intellectualis (relating to the understanding), from intellectus 'understanding'.
