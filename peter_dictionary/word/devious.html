devious

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːviəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːviəs / (BrE)
Showing a skillful use of underhanded tactics to achieve goals; not straightforward or direct.

Examples

Something indirect, mixed, reconciling, tensional might well be the stratagem, the devious technique by which a poet indulged in all kinds of talk about love and anger and even in something like "expressions "of these emotions, without aiming at their incitement or even uttering anything that essentially involves their incitement ".

Synset Examples

used devious means to achieve success
gave oblique answers to direct questions
oblique political maneuvers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; intending to deceive or cheat
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin 'deviare' (to turn aside), from 'de-' (away from) + 'via' (way).
