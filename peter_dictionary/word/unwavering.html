unwavering

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈweɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈweɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
not changing, steady and determined; not becoming weaker or less certain

Examples

It is his relentlessness and unwavering adherence to this revolutionary artistic philosophy that has enabled him to paint such pictures as "The Invasion of Cuba ".

Synset Examples

firm convictions
a firm mouth
steadfast resolve
a man of unbendable perseverence
unwavering loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady - firm and unwavering in position or action
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • Antonym: wavering - becoming weaker or indecisive

Etymology

From un- (not) + wavering (from 'waver', of Scandinavian origin, meaning to flicker or be unsteady).
