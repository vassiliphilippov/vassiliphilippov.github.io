reckless

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛkləs / (BrE)
Showing a lack of care about danger or the consequences of one's actions.

Examples

She was taken up in worry for the reckless old man.
Curt, angry enough to be a little reckless, raised his hands shoulder high.

Synset Examples

foolhardy enough to try to seize the gun from the hijacker
became the fiercest and most reckless of partisans
a reckless driver
a rash attempt to climb Mount Everest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers

Etymology

From Middle English rekeles, from Old English rēcelēas (careless), from rēca (care) + -lēas (without).
