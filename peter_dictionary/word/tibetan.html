Tibetan

1. ADJ

/ tɪˈbɛtən / (AmE), / tɪˈbiːtən / (BrE)
of or relating to Tibet, its people, or their language

Examples

A Tibetan swami from Palermo, Sicily, announced in Beverly Hills a newly discovered, ancient yoga discipline for ripple breathing which increased both pranha and cosmic attraction between sexes.

Synset Examples

Tibetan monks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibet - a region in Asia

Etymology

From Tibet + -an.

2. NOUN

/ tɪˈbɛtən / (AmE), / tɪˈbɛtən / (BrE)
The Sino-Tibetan language spoken in Tibet and neighboring regions.

Examples

In it was a stone Tibetan Buddha I had picked up in Bombay, and occasionally, to make merit, my wife and I garlanded it with flowers or laid a few pennies in its lap.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Chinese - the Sinitic language spoken in China

Etymology

From Tibet + -an, referring to the language and people of Tibet.
