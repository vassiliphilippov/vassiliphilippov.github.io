possessive

1. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
wanting to own or control things or people; showing a strong desire to keep someone or something for oneself and not share with others

Examples

The old man was almost too possessive.

Synset Examples

small children are so possessive they will not let others play with their toys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealous - feeling or showing envy of someone who has something; suspicious of losing someone's affection
  • Degree Lower: protective - wanting to protect someone from harm; watching over someone closely
  • Degree Higher: controlling - exerting excessive authority or dominance over others

Etymology

From Latin possessivus, from possidere (to possess).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a word, form, or grammatical construction) showing or relating to ownership or possession.

Examples

In of all the suggestions that were made, his was the silliest the possessive his represents his suggestion and is stressed.

Synset Examples

possessive pronouns
the genitive endings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genitive - grammatical case marking possession or close association

Etymology

From Latin possessivus, from possessio ('possession').
