unrequited

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.rɪˈkwaɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌn.rɪˈkwaɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a feeling, especially love) not returned or reciprocated by the other person or party.

Examples

He also spoke of "the wealth piled by the bondsman's two hundred and fifty years in unrequited toil ".

Synset Examples

unrequited (unanswered) love

Related Terms

  • Antonym: requited - (of feeling, return, or favor) given in return; reciprocated

Etymology

From re- + quited, after the archaic verb 'requite' (to return or repay).
