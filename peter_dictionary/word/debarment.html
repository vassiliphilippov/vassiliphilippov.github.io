debarment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈbɑrmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑːmənt / (BrE)
The state or act of being officially excluded from doing something, especially by law or regulation.

Examples

You won'’t be deprived of anything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of excluding or the state of being excluded

Etymology

From debar + -ment, modeled after legal usage; debar from Old French 'desbarer'.
