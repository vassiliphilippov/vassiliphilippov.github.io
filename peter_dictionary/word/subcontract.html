subcontract

1. VERB

/ sʌbˈkɑn.trækt / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒn.trækt / (BrE)
to arrange for part or all of the work required by a contract to be done by another party

Examples

Have you checked the cost of subcontracting your cafeteria operation in order to save administrative costs?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsource - to buy goods or services from outside suppliers, especially from overseas; similar in meaning to subcontract though broader

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (under) and 'contract', first used in the late 19th century.
