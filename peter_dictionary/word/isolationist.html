isolationist

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the policy of isolationism, especially in politics or foreign policy.

Examples

It is an understandable paradox that most American history and most American literature is today written from an essentially egocentric and isolationistic point of view at the very time America is spreading her dominion over palm and pine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: internationalist - supporting greater cooperation among nations

Etymology

From isolation + -ist, patterned after isolationism.
