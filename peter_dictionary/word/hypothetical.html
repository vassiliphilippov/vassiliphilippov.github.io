hypothetical

1. ADJ

/ haɪˌpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / haɪˌpɒˈθɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Based primarily on a suggested idea or theory, rather than on actual evidence; existing only as a possibility or in the imagination.

Examples

The received radiation is compared with the radiation from a hypothetical black-body which subtends the same solid angle as the visible disk of the planet.
A hypothetical example will illustrate this point.
Local jobs can be seen and counted, while opportunities elsewhere are regarded as more hypothetical.

Synset Examples

theories about the extinction of dinosaurs are still highly conjectural
the supposed reason for his absence
suppositious reconstructions of dead languages
hypothetical situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretical - concerned with ideas and theory rather than practical application
  • Synonym: conjectural - based on guessing or incomplete information
  • Antonym: actual - existing in fact; real and not imagined or theoretical

Etymology

From Greek hypothetikos (‘pertaining to a hypothesis’), from hypothesis (‘base, supposition’).
