Khartoum

1. PROPN

/ kɑːrˈtuːm / (AmE), / kɑːˈtuːm / (BrE)
The capital city of Sudan, situated at the meeting point of the Blue Nile and White Nile rivers.

Examples

Whether the pair of Sudanese ivory carvings you lifted really possess the juju to turn your livers to lead, as a dealer in Khartoum assured me, I am not competent to say.

Etymology

From Arabic الخرطوم (al-Khurṭūm), meaning ‘the trunk (of an elephant)’, referring to the shape of the land at the river confluence.
