gallon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælən / (AmE), / ˈɡælən / (BrE)
A unit of liquid capacity used in the US, equal to 4 quarts or approximately 3.785 liters.

Examples

In this test, 130 gallons of a suspension, having a count of * * f organisms per ml, or a total of approximately * * f spores, was aerosolized.
A 10 - gallon garbage can, with a tightly fitting cover, could be used to keep the wastes until it is safe to leave the shelter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quart - Unit of liquid capacity equal to one fourth of a gallon
  • Degree Lower: liter - Metric unit of volume (1 gallon ≈ 3.785 liters)
  • Degree Lower: pint - US unit of volume equal to one eighth of a gallon

Etymology

From Old Northern French *galon*, of uncertain origin.
