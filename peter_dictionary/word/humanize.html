humanize

1. VERB

/ ˈhjuːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone more humane, or to give human qualities to something

Examples

Among the Moliere specialists of some years ago, Louis Jouvet tried to humanize some of the clowns, while Fernand Ledoux, often performing at the Comedie, made them more gross than Moliere may have intended.

Synset Examples

The mayor tried to humanize life in the big city

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dehumanize - to deprive of human qualities or make less humane

Etymology

From Latin humanus (human) via French humaniser.
