rickety

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪkəti / (AmE), / ˈrɪkəti / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) poorly made and likely to collapse or shake from weakness or defect.

Examples

We flew in rickety planes so overloaded that we wondered why they did n't crash.

Synset Examples

a rickety table
a wobbly chair with shaky legs
the ladder felt a little wobbly
the bridge still stands though one of the arches is wonky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobbly - unstable and likely to shake or move unsteadily
  • Antonym: sturdy - strongly and solidly built, not likely to give way

Etymology

Probably from English dialect 'rick' (to shake, totter), origin uncertain.
