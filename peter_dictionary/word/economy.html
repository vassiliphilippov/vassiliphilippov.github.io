economy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
The system by which a country or region organizes the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services.

Examples

"If you destroy confidence in banks, you do something to the economy ", he said.
- liberal considers that the need for a national economy with controls that will assure his conception of social justice is so great that individual and local liberties as well as democratic processes may have to yield before it.
The Office of Business Economics (OBE) of the U.S. Department of Commerce provides basic measures of the national economy and current analysis of short-run changes in the economic situation and business outlook.
Such measures are essential to its job of presenting business and Government with the facts required to meet the objective of expanding business and improving the operation of the economy.
We should encourage the governments to develop their own technical assistance to communities, state and provincial governments, rural communities, and other smaller groups, making certain that no important segment of the economy is neglected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an area or arena in which commercial dealings are conducted
  • Degree Higher: macroeconomy - the economy viewed as a whole, including broad measures such as GDP and national income
  • Degree Lower: microeconomy - the part of the economy concerned with single factors and individual decisions

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management), from oikos (house) and nemein (manage).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
the use of resources such as money, materials, or effort in a careful and efficient way, avoiding waste

Examples

To light industry, the economies of being on one floor are much slighter, but efficiency engineers usually believe in them, and manufacturers looking for ways to cut costs cannot be prevented from turning to efficiency engineers.

Synset Examples

economy of effort

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wastefulness - the inefficient or careless use of resources
  • Synonym: thrift - the quality of using resources carefully and not wastefully
  • Synonym: frugality - the quality of being economical with resources

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management), from oikos (house) + nomos (management).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
a measure taken to reduce spending or save resources; an act of economizing

Examples

There may be possible economies at any one of a number of links in your marketing and distribution chain.

Synset Examples

it was a small economy to walk to work every day
there was a saving of 50 cents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saving - an amount of money or resource saved

Etymology

From Greek oikonomia (household management) via Latin and Old French.

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmi / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
The careful use of money, resources, or effort, aiming to avoid waste; frugality.

Examples

But make no mistake about it, the first reason people turn to camping is one of economy.

Synset Examples

the Scots are famous for their economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrift - The quality of using money and resources carefully and not wastefully
  • Antonym: wastefulness - The opposite quality of spending resources freely or carelessly

Etymology

From Greek *oikonomia* (household management).
