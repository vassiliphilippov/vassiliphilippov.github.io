tailored

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪlərd / (AmE), / ˈteɪləd / (BrE)
(of clothing or other items) made with simple, clean lines and a fitted or custom cut, often appearing neat and precisely finished.

Examples

Stacked heels are also popular on dressy or tailored shoes.
He was always well groomed and well tailored, and he had that rich man's look which was authentic enough and came from two good prep schools and a proper university.
After a time, he straightened again, brushing the red Permian dust from his hands, slapping it from his six dollar levis and his tailored, twenty-five dollar shirt.

Synset Examples

a neat tailored suit
tailored curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fitted - (of clothing) made to fit closely to the body
  • Antonym: loose - (of clothing) not fitting closely, baggy

Etymology

From 'tailor', ultimately from Old French 'taillour' (cutter), from 'tailler' (to cut).
