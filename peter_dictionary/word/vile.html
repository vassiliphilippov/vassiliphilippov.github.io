vile

1. ADJ

/ vaɪl / (AmE), / vaɪl / (BrE)
Morally extremely bad; wicked or despicable.

Examples

Once covertly looking at Simms Purdew, the only man in the world whom he hated, he had seen the heavy, slack, bestubbled jaw open and close to emit the cruel, obscene banter, and had seen the pale blue eyes go watery with whisky and merriment, and suddenly he was not seeing the face of that vile creature.

Synset Examples

would do something as despicable as murder
ugly crimes
the vile development of slavery appalled them
a slimy little liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despicable - deserving hatred and contempt
  • Synonym: loathsome - causing hatred or disgust
  • Antonym: honorable - deserving respect or high regard

Etymology

From Latin 'vilis' (cheap, worthless).
