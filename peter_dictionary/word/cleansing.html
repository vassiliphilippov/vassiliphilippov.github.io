cleansing

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to the act of cleaning, especially by washing; often referring to washing the body as part of a ritual or religious practice.

Examples

"In addition to the usual tools, I make constant use of cleansing tissue, not only to wipe my brushes, but to mop up certain areas, to soften edges, and to open up lights in dark washes.

Synset Examples

ablutionary rituals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purifying - removing impurities or contaminants

Etymology

From the verb 'cleanse', from Old English 'clǣnsian' (to purify, cleanse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɛn.zɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of making something clean; the process of removing dirt, impurities, or harmful substances.

Examples

The cleansing tissues she had been using had been falling on the floor, and he got up and picked up one, then another, hoping she would notice what he was doing.

Synset Examples

he gave his shoes a good cleaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaning - the act of removing dirt or impurities

Etymology

From the verb 'cleanse' + noun-forming suffix '-ing'.
