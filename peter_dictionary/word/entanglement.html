entanglement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtæŋɡəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡl̩mənt / (BrE)
A complex situation or arrangement that traps or ensnares someone, making escape or disentanglement difficult.

Examples

But when she called he had thought better of the matter and decided not to involve himself in a new entanglement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snare - a device or situation that catches or traps someone or something
  • Synonym: trap - a device or plan for catching someone or something by surprise
  • Antonym: liberty - the state of being free within society, opposite of being trapped or entangled

Etymology

From the verb 'entangle' (to tangle up), itself from 'en-' (in) + 'tangle'.
