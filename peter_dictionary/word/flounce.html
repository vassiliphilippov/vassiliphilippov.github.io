flounce

1. VERB

/ flaʊns / (AmE), / flaʊns / (BrE)
to move or walk with exaggerated, jerky, or emphatic motions, often to show annoyance or impatience

Examples

- and flounced to the sink, where she began noisily to wash her hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - to move angrily or forcefully in a dramatic way

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian origins, related to Norwegian *flunsa* (to hurry).
