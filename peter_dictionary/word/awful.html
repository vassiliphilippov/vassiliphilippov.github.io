awful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔfəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːf(ə)l / (BrE)
Very bad or unpleasant; extremely disagreeable or displeasing.

Examples

As the play unfolds, however, the audience is subtly brought into the grip of an awful evil which grows with ominously gathering power and soon engulfs the community.
The comfort was short-lived, yet she found herself returning to the assurance whenever her imagination forced images on her too awful to contemplate without the prop of illusion.
They possessed no outer fortifications, no hard shells of confidence; they had enough difficulty getting from day to day, let alone having an awful crime thrust upon them.
Some people really got it awful!
It pissed the Warden off something awful.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: dreadful - causing fear or suffering; very bad
  • Antonym: wonderful - extremely good or pleasing

Etymology

From 'awe' + '-ful'; originally 'full of awe' (inspiring dread or wonder), later shifting to 'remarkably bad'.

/ ˈɔfəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːfəl / (BrE)
(informal) Extremely or very, used to emphasize a characteristic or quality.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - used as an intensifier (very, extremely)
  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely (intensifier)

Etymology

From Middle English 'awful' (inspiring awe), from 'awe' + '-ful'. The intensifier use developed later in colloquial speech.

4. ADJ
