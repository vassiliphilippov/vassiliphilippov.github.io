boon

1. ADJ

/ bun / (AmE), / buːn / (BrE)
(of a companion or friendship) very close and convivial; characterized by cheerful friendliness or fellowship.

Examples

Occasionally gay young blades would write vividly to boon companions at home about their amorous exploits in Richmond, Petersburg, Washington, or Nashville.

Synset Examples

boon companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close - near in relationship or friendship
  • Synonym: intimate - closely acquainted; familiar, close
  • Antonym: distant - not close in relationship; unfriendly or remote

Etymology

From Middle English 'bone', from Old French 'bon' (good), from Latin 'bonus' (good).

2. NOUN

/ bun / (AmE), / buːn / (BrE)
A thing that is helpful or beneficial; a blessing or advantage.

Examples

Mr. Blatz had been at least sober enough to remember to telephone and he turned out to be the greatest boon that had come into Mr. Crombie's life since he moved to Highfield, in spite of the fact that he did n't work very fast or very long at a time, and he did n't like to work at all unless Mr. Crombie hung around and talked to him.

Synset Examples

enjoy the blessings of peace
a spanking breeze is a boon to sailors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blessing - something that brings good or helps
  • Antonym: curse - something that brings harm or trouble

Etymology

From Middle English 'bone', from Old Norse 'bón' (a prayer, request, favor).
