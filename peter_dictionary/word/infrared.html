infrared

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd / (BrE)
(of electromagnetic radiation) having wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves, lying just beyond the red end of the visible spectrum; also, relating to or using such radiation.

Examples

The programs in infrared spectroscopy are undergoing reorientation toward wavelength standards in the far infrared, the application of infrared techniques to solid state studies, and increased emphasis on high resolution instrumentation.
The programs in infrared spectroscopy are undergoing reorientation toward wavelength standards in the far infrared, the application of infrared techniques to solid state studies, and increased emphasis on high resolution instrumentation.

Synset Examples

infrared radiation
infrared photography

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ultraviolet - (of electromagnetic radiation) having wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays

Etymology

From Latin 'infra' (below) + 'red', referring to its position below red in the visible spectrum.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd / (BrE)
the part of the electromagnetic spectrum with wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves, typically perceived as heat.

Examples

The programs in infrared spectroscopy are undergoing reorientation toward wavelength standards in the far infrared, the application of infrared techniques to solid state studies, and increased emphasis on high resolution instrumentation.

Synset Examples

they could sense radiation in the infrared

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ultraviolet - region of the electromagnetic spectrum with wavelengths shorter than visible light

Etymology

From infra- (Latin, meaning 'below') + red, referring to wavelengths below (longer than) red light.
