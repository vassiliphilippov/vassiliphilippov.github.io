spotless

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑt.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈspɒt.lɪs / (BrE)
Completely clean; without any dirt, marks, or stains.

Examples

Ann Catt was a lonely, devoted soul, never married, conducting a spotless home and devoted to her church, but a perpetual dissenter and born critic.
Rector asked him to move it for the time being; he wanted the mission compound to be effortlessly spotless.
She had made curtains for all the windows of her little house, and she had kept it spotless and neat, shabby as it was, and cooked good meals for Bobby Joe.

Synset Examples

the apartment was immaculate
in her immaculate white uniform
a spick-and-span kitchen
their spic red-visored caps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from dirt or error
  • Antonym: dirty - not clean; covered or marked with dirt
  • Degree Lower: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains
  • Antonym: filthy - extremely dirty

Etymology

From 'spot' (mark, stain) + '-less' (without).
