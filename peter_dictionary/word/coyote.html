coyote

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈoʊti / (AmE), / kaɪˈəʊti / (BrE)
A small wild canine native to North America, closely related to the wolf and known for its yelping howl.

Examples

The coyote was calling again, and he hoped that this time there would be no other sounds to interrupt it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf - large wild carnivorous mammal related to dogs
  • Synonym: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal, Canis familiaris

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'coyote', from Nahuatl 'coyōtl'.
