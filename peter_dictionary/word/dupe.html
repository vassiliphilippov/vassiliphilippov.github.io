dupe

1. VERB

/ duːp / (AmE), / djuːp / (BrE)
to deceive or trick someone into believing something that is not true; to fool

Examples

If the would-be joiner asks these questions he is not likely to be duped by extremists who are seeking to capitalize on the confusions and the patriotic apprehensions of Americans in a troubled time.

Synset Examples

The immigrant was duped because he trusted everyone
You can't fool me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
  • Synonym: gull - to deceive or cheat; to trick
  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or possessions
  • Synonym: trick - to deceive someone, especially as a joke or in order to gain an advantage

Etymology

Probably from French 'dupe' (a gull, simpleton), of uncertain origin.
