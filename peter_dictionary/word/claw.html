claw

1. NOUN

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
a pincer-like, grasping structure found on the limbs of crustaceans and other arthropods, used for seizing, holding, or fighting

Examples

Every morning early, in the summer, we searched the trunks of the trees as high as we could reach for the locust shells, carefully detached their hooked claws from the bark where they hung, and stabled them, a weird faery herd, in an angle between the high roots of the tulip tree, where no grass grew in the dense shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pincer - a gripping organ or limb of an animal resembling the action of tongs or pincers

Etymology

From Old English 'clawu', related to Dutch 'klauw' and German 'Klaue'.

2. NOUN

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
A mechanical device, often curved or hooked, designed to suspend, grip, or pull something.

Examples

The fingers of his right hand twisted into a claw, but he did n't reach for the gun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hook - a curved or bent tool for catching, holding, or pulling

3. VERB

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
to clutch or grasp at something frantically or desperately, as with claws

Examples

They were covered with tiny white blossoms, their scant roots clawing at the stony ground, and wild birds darted in and about and through them so they were nearly alive with the rustle and cry.

Synset Examples

She clawed the doorknob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take hold of something suddenly or roughly

Etymology

Old English *clawu* (claw, talon), related to German *Klaue*.

4. VERB

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
(of a person) to make progress or move, especially with effort or difficulty, as if by using one's claws to grasp, seize, or dig.

Examples

Ten years older than Mitch Barton, he had clawed his way up from mucker in the pits to manager of the operation.

Synset Examples

They clawed their way to the top of the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrabble - to scratch or grope around with one's fingers to find, collect, or hold onto something

Etymology

From Old English 'clawu', meaning claw or talon.

5. NOUN

/ klɔ / (AmE), / klɔː / (BrE)
a sharp, curved, horny growth at the end of the toe in birds, some mammals, and reptiles, used for grasping or tearing

Examples

We've got a flying mouse to kill... ...and I want to clean my claws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talon - a claw, especially one belonging to a bird of prey

Etymology

Old English *clawu* (claw, talon), related to Old Norse *kló*, Dutch *klauw*.
