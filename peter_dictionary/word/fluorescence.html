fluorescence

1. NOUN

/ fluəˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛsəns / (BrE)
The emission of visible light by a substance that has absorbed light or other electromagnetic radiation of a different, usually invisible, wavelength, typically as a result of excitation by ultraviolet light.

Etymology

From Latin 'fluo' (to flow) and 'fluor' (fluor spar), originally coined to name the effect observed in fluorite.
