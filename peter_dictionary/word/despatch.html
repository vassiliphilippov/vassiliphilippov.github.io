despatch

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈspatʃ / (BrE)
To send someone or something off to a particular destination for a specific purpose, often quickly or officially.

Examples

Upon which the detective bureau despatched rifle squads to prevent trouble if O' Banion should send his gunmen out to deal with the hijacking policemen.
It had required the approval of President Franklin Delano Roosevelt before the John Harvey could be loaded with 100 tons of mustard gas and despatched to the Italian warfront.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispatch - To send someone or something off to a destination; alternative, more common spelling.

Etymology

Alternative spelling of 'dispatch', from Italian 'dispacciare' (to expedite), from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'pactum' (pact, agreement).
