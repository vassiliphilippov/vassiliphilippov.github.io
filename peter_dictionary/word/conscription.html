conscription

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the practice of ordering people by law to serve in the armed forces; compulsory enlistment for military service

Examples

In this world-wide conscription of men, minds, and machines, Prokofieff was recalled to his native land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - the system of selecting people for compulsory military service (especially in the US)

Etymology

From Latin 'conscriptionem', from 'conscribere' (to enlist, to write together).
