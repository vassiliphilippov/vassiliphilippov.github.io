seaweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌwiːd / (BrE)
A general term for various types of algae or plants growing in the sea, especially large marine algae.

Examples

There was the smell of the coast, like a primeval memory, composed of equal parts salt water, clams, seaweed and northern air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweed

Etymology

Formed from 'sea' + 'weed', recorded in English since the 16th century.
