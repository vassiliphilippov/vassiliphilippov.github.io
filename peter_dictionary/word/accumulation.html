accumulation

1. NOUN

/ əˌkjumjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a number of things that have been collected or have gradually increased over time

Examples

Yet it is the accumulation of distortion, the fitting together of fractional bits until the total reaches the threshold of our awareness, that makes records sound like records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of things or people gathered together

Etymology

From Latin accumulatio, from accumulare ('to heap up').

From Latin accumulatio (a heaping up), from accumulare (to heap up).
