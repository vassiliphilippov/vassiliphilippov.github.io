curl

1. VERB

/ kɝl / (AmE), / kɜːl / (BrE)
to twist or form something into coils or ringlets

Examples

They curled around the cuff of his nightshirt.

Synset Examples

curl my hair, please

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight; to remove bends or curves

Etymology

From Middle English 'curlen', probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'krulla' (to curl).

2. VERB

/ kɝl / (AmE), / kɜːl / (BrE)
to wind or twist around into coils or loops

Examples

The first part of the road was steep, but it leveled off after the second bend and curled gradually into the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coil - to wind into a spiral or series of loops
  • Antonym: unwind - to reverse the action of winding or coiling

Etymology

From Middle English 'curlen', of uncertain origin, perhaps from Middle Dutch or Middle Low German.

3. VERB

/ kɜrl / (AmE), / kɜːl / (BrE)
to form into a curved or spiral shape; to bend or twist into a coil or loop

Examples

Reaching for an old clay pot, relic of pioneer days, he tore the envelope in pieces, dropping them into it, touching the little pyre to flame, watching it curl, the red sealing wax melting and bubbling in the feathery ash.

Synset Examples

the cigar smoke curled up at the ceiling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From Middle English 'curlen', probably from Middle Dutch 'krul' (curly or twisted).
