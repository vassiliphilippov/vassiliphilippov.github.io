foolhardy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfuːlˌhɑːrdi / (AmE), / ˈfuːlhɑːdi / (BrE)
showing reckless boldness or disregard for danger or consequences

Examples

One who invites such trials of character is either foolhardy, overconfident or too simple and childlike in faith in mankind to see the danger.

Synset Examples

foolhardy enough to try to seize the gun from the hijacker
became the fiercest and most reckless of partisans
a reckless driver
a rash attempt to climb Mount Everest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers

Etymology

From Old French folhardi, from fol (foolish) + hardi (bold), ultimately from Latin follis (bellows, fool) and hardīre (to harden).
