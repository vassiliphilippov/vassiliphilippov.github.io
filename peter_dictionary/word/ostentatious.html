ostentatious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːstənˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɒstənˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
characterized by a showy or pretentious display meant to attract notice or admiration.

Examples

This dinner was the start of a new blatancy in the relationship between the gangs and the politicians, which, prior to 1924, says Pasley, "had been maintained with more or less stealth ", but which henceforth was marked by these ostentatious gatherings, denounced by a clergyman as "Belshazzar feasts ", at which "politicians fraternized cheek by jowl with gangsters, openly, in the big downtown hotels ".

Synset Examples

an ostentatious sable coat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit than is actually possessed
  • Antonym: modest - not showing excessive pride or attention-seeking; the opposite of ostentatious

Etymology

From Latin 'ostentatio(n-)' meaning 'display', from 'ostentare' (to display).
