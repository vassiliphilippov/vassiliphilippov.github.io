caretaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person employed to look after a building, property, or sometimes a person, by keeping it in good condition, maintaining it, and ensuring its security

Examples

It all seemed - if one could have peeked in at him through one of his windows - as though this broken nosed man with the muscular arms and wrestler's neck was merely the caretaker trying his hand at the boss's work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custodian - a person who is responsible for or looks after something, especially a building
  • Synonym: janitor - a person employed to take care of a building, responsible for cleaning and minor repairs

Etymology

From take care, used in the sense of a person who takes care; first recorded in the 18th century.
