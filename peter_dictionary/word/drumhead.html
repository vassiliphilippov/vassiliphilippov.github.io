drumhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌhɛd / (BrE)
the membrane that is stretched tightly across one or both ends of a drum, producing sound when struck

Examples

Most of the letters were written in the hubbub of camp, on stumps, pieces of bark, drum heads, or the knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumskin - membrane or surface of a drum
