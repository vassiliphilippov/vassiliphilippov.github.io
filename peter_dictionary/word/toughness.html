toughness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʌfnəs / (BrE)
The quality of having enduring strength, energy, or resilience.

Examples

Though his character is broader and more comically rounded than the don, he gives it a firmness and toughness - a sort of peasant dignity - too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resilience - the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness
  • Antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From tough + -ness; 'tough' from Middle English 'togh', from Old English 'tōh' (firm, resistant).
