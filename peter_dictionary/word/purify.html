purify

1. VERB

/ ˈpjʊrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove impurities or unwanted elements from a substance, especially by a process such as distillation or filtration

Examples

There is no conclusive evidence yet that either of the proteases has been prepared in highly purified form nor is their specificity known.
On Feb. 12, 1959, purified corticotropin (ACTH Gel), 20 units daily intramuscularly, was started but had to be discontinued 3 weeks later because of excessive fluid retention.

Synset Examples

purify the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refine - to remove unwanted elements from a substance, especially in an industrial process
  • Antonym: contaminate - to make something impure by adding harmful or undesirable substances

Etymology

From Old French 'purifier', from Latin 'purificare' (to make pure), from 'purus' (pure) + 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈpjʊr.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊə.rɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) morally pure or free from sin or guilt

Examples

After how many generations is such wealth (mounting all the while through the manipulations of high finance) purified of taint?

Synset Examples

he left the monastery purified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleanse - remove impurities or sin from someone or something
  • Antonym: taint - to contaminate morally or spiritually

Etymology

From Old French *purifier*, from Latin *purificare* (to make pure), from *purus* (pure) + *facere* (to make).
