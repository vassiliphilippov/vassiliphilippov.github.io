disfigured

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈfɪɡɚd / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɪɡəd / (BrE)
Having had the appearance spoiled or made less attractive, often by damage or injury.

Examples

He expected Concetta's thin hand to reach down to grasp the boy, and her shrill, impetuous voice to sound against the rotundity of his disfigured flesh that was never sure of hearing anything.

Synset Examples

a disfigured face
strip mining left a disfigured landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marred - damaged or spoiled to a certain extent; less perfect, attractive, useful, etc.
  • Synonym: scarred - marked by scars, especially after healing from wounds or injury
  • Antonym: unblemished - not spoiled, flawless in appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'disfigurare' (to disfigure) via Old French 'desfigurer'.
