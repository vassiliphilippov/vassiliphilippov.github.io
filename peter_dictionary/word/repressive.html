repressive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
Restricting freedom or limiting actions, often by enforcing strict controls or the use of force.

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.

Synset Examples

a repressive regime
an overly strict and inhibiting discipline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressive - unjustly inflicting hardship or constraint, especially on a minority or other subordinate group
  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values; favoring maximum individual liberty

Etymology

From French 'répressif' or Medieval Latin 'repressivus', from Latin 'reprimere' (to press back).
