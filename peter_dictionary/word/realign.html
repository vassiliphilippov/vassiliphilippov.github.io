realign

1. VERB

/ riˈə.laɪn / (AmE), / riˈə.laɪn / (BrE)
to change the position or direction of something so that it is in a better or correct alignment; to align again.

Examples

When Richard's parents told him they wanted to take him to an orthodontist - a dentist who specializes in realigning teeth and jaws - their young son was interested.

Synset Examples

The surgeon realigned my jaw after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: align - to place or arrange things in a straight line or in correct relative positions
  • Antonym: disalign - to put out of alignment

Etymology

From re- (again) + align (to bring into line).
