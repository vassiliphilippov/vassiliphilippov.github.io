mutuality

1. NOUN

/ mjuːtʃuˈæləɾi / (AmE), / mjuːtʃuˈalɪti / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being mutual; a shared feeling, action, or relationship between two or more parties

Examples

They seemed then to have had a single mind and body, a mutuality which had been accepted with the fact of their youth, casually.

Synset Examples

the mutuality of their affection was obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocity - the practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit

Etymology

From mutual + -ity, ultimately from Latin mutuus (borrowed) + -itas (state or quality).
