rustic

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈrʌstɪk / (BrE)
Having the qualities or appearance typical of the countryside; simple, plain, or unsophisticated in style, often in a way that is considered charming or attractive.

Examples

Well, Jerebohm and his wife Pinkie have reached the stage of affluence that stirs a longing for the more atrociously expensive rustic simplicities.

Synset Examples

countrified clothes
rustic awkwardness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urban - relating to a city or town

Etymology

From Latin rusticus (of the country), from rus (country).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈrʌs.tɪk / (BrE)
(especially of a person or manner) awkwardly simple or provincial; lacking sophistication or polish.

Examples

The assisting musicians from the Vienna Octet are somewhat lacking in expertise, but their contribution is rustic and appealing.

Synset Examples

bumpkinly country boys
rustic farmers
a hick town
the nightlife of Montmartre awed the unsophisticated tourists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsophisticated - lacking refinement or worldly experience
  • Antonym: urbane - having refined, polished, and elegant manners

Etymology

From Latin 'rusticus' (of the country, rural), from 'rus' (country).
