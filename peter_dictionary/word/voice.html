voice

/ vɔɪs / (AmE), / vɔɪs / (BrE)
the expression or ability to express an opinion, feeling, or point of view

Examples

And I select this sentence as its pertinent summation: "In essence the drama of his (Eisenhower's) Presidency can be described as the ordeal of a nation turned conservative and struggling - thus far with but limited and precarious success - to give effective voice and force to that conservatism ".

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinion - a belief or judgment that is not based on certainty
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of expressed opinion or speech

Etymology

From Old French 'voiz', from Latin 'vox' (voice, sound).
