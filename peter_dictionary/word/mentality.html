mentality

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / mɛnˈtalɪti / (BrE)
a habitual or characteristic way of thinking or mental attitude that determines how a person interprets and responds to situations

Examples

These are mentalities which crave action - and they are beginning to get it, as Messrs. Salsich and Engh report on page 372.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mindset - a particular way of thinking; an attitude or set of opinions

Etymology

From French *mentalité*, from Latin *mens* (mind).

2. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / mɛnˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the ability to think, reason, and understand; mental capacity

Examples

Although he is perfectly willing to cooperate with Scotland Yard, Holmes has nothing but contempt for the intelligence and mentality of the police.

Synset Examples

he's got plenty of brains but no common sense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • Synonym: wit - keen intelligence or mental sharpness
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of mental ability or alertness

Etymology

From French mentalité, from Latin mentalis (of the mind).
