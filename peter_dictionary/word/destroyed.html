destroyed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪd / (BrE)
physically or morally ruined; having been damaged completely and left without its former qualities or function

Examples

In the cruel clearness of her memory the boy remained unchanged, quick with the delight of laughter, and the pain with which she recalled that short destroyed childhood was still unendurable to her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruined - severely damaged or destroyed; no longer able to function
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired; in its original state

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪd / (BrE)
Having been ruined, spoiled, or demolished; no longer in a usable or original state.

Examples

The innocent malfeasant, filled with that supreme sense of honor found in bars, insisted upon replacing the destroyed monocle - and did, over the protests of the former owner - with a square monocle.

Synset Examples

war left many cities destroyed
Alzheimer's is responsible for her destroyed mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired; complete

Etymology

Past participle of the verb "destroy," from Old French *destruire*, from Latin *destruere* (to unbuild).
