sandal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsæn.dl̩ / (BrE)
A light shoe consisting of a sole held to the foot by straps that go over the instep or around the ankle.

Examples

For added comfort some of the Italian designed sandals have foam padded cushioning.
In the meantime, six sandals, stained an ocher, the same color as Pompeii's shaved hair, edged up close to him.
He knew at a glance that the biggest sandals belonged to Niobe, the neatest ones to Concetta, and the laced ones to Romeo, Concetta's idiot brother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip-flop - casual sandal with thong between the toes
  • Degree Higher: shoe - general term for covering for the foot
  • Degree Higher: boot - footwear covering the ankle or leg
  • Degree Lower: slipper - light, comfortable footwear for indoors

Etymology

From Latin 'sandalium' and Greek 'sandalion', meaning a type of footwear.
