languid

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪd / (BrE)
Displaying or having a disinclination for physical exertion or effort; slow, relaxed, and without energy or enthusiasm.

Examples

Watson glanced briefly at him, seeing only a body rigidly erect behind the languid banner.
He saw the Starbird as she lay, her slender mast up and gently turning, its point describing constant languid circles against a cumulus sky.

Synset Examples

a lackadaisical attempt
a languid mood
a languid wave of the hand
a hot languorous afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

From Latin 'languidus' (faint, weak), from 'languere' (to be weak or faint).
