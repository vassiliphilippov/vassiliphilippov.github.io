unsteadily

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈstɛdəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɛdɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is not steady, firm, or balanced.

Examples

He paced forward unsteadily, leaning too far back, his head tilted oddly.

Synset Examples

he walked unsteadily toward the exit
The wounded soldier was swinging unsteadily on his legs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steadily - in a steady manner

Etymology

From 'unsteady' + '-ly'. 'Unsteady' from un- (not) + steady (from Middle English stedfast, from Old English stedefæst).
