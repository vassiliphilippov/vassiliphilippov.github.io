jeopardy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛpɚdi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛpədi / (BrE)
A situation in which there is a risk of harm, loss, or danger.

Examples

"There can be no compromise when souls are in jeopardy ".
It is n't what the ideologist believes in, but what he hates, that puts the world in jeopardy.

Synset Examples

drinking alcohol is a health hazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of suffering harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger or harm

Etymology

From Old French 'jeu parti' (a divided game, a contest with uncertain outcome).
