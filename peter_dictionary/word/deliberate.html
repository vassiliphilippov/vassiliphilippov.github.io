deliberate

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɪbərət / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərət / (BrE)
Done consciously and intentionally; carefully thought out or planned in advance.

Examples

Mark held the wheel loosely, but his fingers curved around it in a purposeful way and the deliberate set of his body spoke plainly of the figure he 'd make in the years to come.
One is the primary purpose for which marriage exists, namely, the continuance of the race through the gift and heritage of children; the other is the paramount importance in married life of deliberate and thoughtful self-control ".
Is it deliberate?

Synset Examples

a calculated insult
with measured irony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done with intention or on purpose
  • Antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not planned or intended
  • Synonym: premeditated - planned in advance, especially of a crime

Etymology

From Latin deliberatus, past participle of deliberare (to weigh, consider).

2. ADJ

/ dəˈlɪbərət / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərət / (BrE)
Done in a slow, careful, and unhurried way, often showing intention and dignity.

Examples

Communist political warfare, we must remember, is waged insidiously and in deliberate stages.
I was having lunch not long ago (apologies to N. V. Peale) with three distinguished historians (one specializing in the European Middle Ages, one in American history, and one in the Far East), and I asked them if they could name instances where the general mores had been radically changed with "deliberate speed, majestic instancy " (Francis Thompson's words for the Hound of Heaven's pursuit) by judicial fiat.

Synset Examples

walking at the same measured pace
with all deliberate speed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careful - showing attention or caution
  • Antonym: hasty - done with excessive speed or urgency

Etymology

From Latin deliberatus, past participle of deliberare (to consider carefully).
