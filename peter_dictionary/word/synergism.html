synergism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnərˌdʒɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪnədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
The interaction or cooperation of two or more agents, substances, or factors to produce a combined effect greater than the sum of their separate effects.

Examples

Polyphosphates gave renewed life to soap products at a time when surfactants were a threat though expensive, and these same polyphosphates spelled the decline of soap usage when the synergism between polyphosphates and synthetic detergent actives was recognized and exploited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synergy - the interaction of elements that creates a greater effect together than individually
  • Antonym: antagonism - opposing or inhibiting interaction between agents or substances

Etymology

From Greek 'synergos' (working together) via Modern Latin 'synergismus'.
