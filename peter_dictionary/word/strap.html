strap

1. VERB

/ stræp / (AmE), / stræp / (BrE)
to fasten, secure, or tie something with a strap

Examples

Stranger, Uncle Randolph began riding home nights with a jug strapped to his saddle, drunkenly singing "Old Dan Tucker "at the top of his voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to fasten or make safe
  • Synonym: fasten - to attach or close securely

Etymology

From Middle Dutch strapen or Low German strappen, meaning a strap or thong.

2. NOUN

/ stræp / (AmE), / stræp / (BrE)
A long, narrow strip of leather or other flexible material used for fastening, holding, or carrying things.

Examples

The door of the lockup was of oak planks and banded with strap iron.
"I 've got her as neat as I can ", Donovan said, as he dropped the straps of the Seton harness over Greg's shoulders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt - a band of material worn around the waist or used for fastening

Etymology

From Old English *stropp* (strap, band), from Latin *stroppus* (strap, band), related to Greek *strophos* (twisted band).
