forgetful

1. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɛtfəl / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛtfəl / (BrE)
Unable or tending not to remember; apt to forget.

Examples

After he had been away from home about a year he wrote: "[ Dear Wife ] If I did not write and receive letters from you I believe that I would forgit that I was married I do n't feel much like a maryed man but I never forgit it sofar as to court enny other lady but if I should you must forgive me as I am so forgitful ".

Synset Examples

a short memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent-minded - habitually forgetful, inattentive
  • Synonym: unretentive - having a memory that is not retentive
  • Antonym: mindful - conscious or aware, especially in remembering
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention; alert

Etymology

From Old English forgytel (negligent, heedless), from forgietan (to forget).
