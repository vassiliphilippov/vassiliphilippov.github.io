distally

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪstəli / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəli / (BrE)
At or toward the farthest point from the center or point of attachment, especially in anatomy.

Examples

Distally the bronchus is situated between a pulmonary artery on one side and a pulmonary vein on the other, as in type 1, (fig. 24).

Synset Examples

the bronchus is situated distally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proximally - closer to the center or point of attachment (anatomical term)

Etymology

From distal + -ly, distal from Latin distare ‘to stand apart’.
