undersecretary

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˈsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsɛkrətəri / (BrE)
A government official who is immediately subordinate to the head of a department, often responsible for assisting in administering the department's activities.

Examples

The undersecretary's in there.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: secretary - head of a government department
  • Degree Higher: deputy secretary - official ranked between undersecretary and secretary

Etymology

From 'under-' (meaning below) + 'secretary' (from Latin 'secretarius', a confidential officer).
