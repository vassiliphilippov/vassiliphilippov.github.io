splendor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplen.də / (BrE)
Great beauty or magnificence; impressive grandeur or brilliance.

Examples

He suggests that Gore Court embodies all the glories of Tudor splendor.
The Pope, in the splendor of his great intellect, had neglected them a little.

Synset Examples

for magnificence and personal service there is the Queen's hotel
his `Hamlet' lacks the brilliance that one expects
it is the university that gives the scene its stately splendor
an imaginative mix of old-fashioned grandeur and colorful art
advertisers capitalize on the grandness and elegance it brings to their products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificence - the quality of being magnificent or splendid or grand
  • Synonym: grandeur - impressive or awesome appearance; stateliness; majesty
  • Antonym: humbleness - the quality of being humble or modest

Etymology

From Latin 'splendor', from 'splendere' (to shine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɛn.də / (BrE)
Brilliant or impressive beauty or excellence; a quality of magnificent appearance or grandeur.

Examples

In the fullness of her vocal splendor, however, she could sing the famous scene magnificently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificence - Great beauty or elaborate richness; similar meaning
  • Synonym: luster - A gentle sheen or soft glow; radiance
  • Antonym: dullness - Lack of brightness, luster, or beauty; opposite quality

Etymology

From Latin splendorem (brightness, brilliance), from splendēre (to shine).
