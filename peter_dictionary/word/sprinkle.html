sprinkle

1. VERB

/ ˈsprɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to scatter small drops or particles of a substance over a surface or area, especially in a light or uneven way

Examples

I learned, for example, that he made a practice of yapping at dogs he encountered and, in winter, of sprinkling salt on the icy pavement to scarify their feet.
Sprinkle over top of chicken breasts.
Serve each breast on a thin slice of slow-baked ham and sprinkle with Thompson seedless grapes.
Keep ashes from one barbecue to the next to sprinkle over coals if they are too hot, and to stop flames that arise from melting grease.
If desired, sprinkle with 1 teaspoon drained crushed pineapple.

Synset Examples

He scattered gun powder under the wagon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to throw loosely about; distribute irregularly
  • Synonym: dust - to lightly cover with a fine powder or particles
  • Degree Higher: pour - to flow or make something flow in a steady stream

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen (variant of sprengen 'to sprinkle, scatter'), from Old English sprengan.

2. VERB

/ ˈsprɪŋ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋ.kəl / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to be scattered or spattered about, especially with some force

Examples

He got the pint of liquor out of his pocket and unscrewed the top, sprinkled the pungent stuff liberally over both men, and then tossed the open bottle in on the front seat.

Synset Examples

She splashed the water around her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to strike or spatter noisily

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen, frequentative of Old English sprengan (to sprinkle, scatter).
