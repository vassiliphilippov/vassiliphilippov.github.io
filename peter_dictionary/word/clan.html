clan

1. NOUN

/ klæn / (AmE), / klæn / (BrE)
A group of people united by actual or perceived kinship, especially one with a common ancestor or family name.

Examples

The chief literary antecedents of the Snopes clan appeared in the realistic, humorous writing which originated in the South and the Southwest in the three decades before the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family - a group of people related by blood or marriage, especially parents and children

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'clann' meaning 'children, offspring, family.'
