consolidated

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɑː.lɪ.deɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / kənˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of organizations, groups, or things) joined together into a single whole or made more solid and unified.

Examples

In this respect, public education in the large cities differs from education in the smaller cities and consolidated school districts.

Synset Examples

United Industries
the amalgamated colleges constituted a university
a consolidated school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amalgamated - joined together to form a single entity, especially in business or organizations

Etymology

From Latin 'consolidare', meaning 'to make firm or solid'.
