tightly

1. ADV

/ ˈtaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that is firmly or closely fixed, held, or pressed; in a tight or constricted manner.

Examples

They keep their wings and feet pressed tightly against their bodies, and in spite of their often colorful attire you may very well mistake them for lumps of dirt.
He was frightened; his fists clutched so tightly that his knuckles hurt.
Narrow four story buildings ran the length of the block like books tightly packed on a shelf.

Synset Examples

a tightly packed pub

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosely - in a loose or relaxed manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'tight' + -ly adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈtaɪt.li / (AmE), / ˈtaɪt.li / (BrE)
In a way that is firmly or securely fixed, fastened, or held together.

Examples

When Felix first opened the door on it, all these shades were tightly drawn and the whole studio was as dark as night.

Synset Examples

the window was tightly sealed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosely - in a way that is not firmly or securely fixed
  • Synonym: securely - in a safe or fixed manner

Etymology

From tight + -ly, with 'tight' from Old Norse 'þétt'.
