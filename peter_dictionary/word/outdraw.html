outdraw

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈdrɔ / (AmE), / aʊtˈdrɔː / (BrE)
To draw a gun more quickly than another person, especially in a gunfight.

Examples

"I heard how you outdrew Chico.

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'better than') and 'draw' (to pull out), as in drawing a weapon.
