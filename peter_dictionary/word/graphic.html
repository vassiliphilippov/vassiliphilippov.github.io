graphic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
(of descriptions or images) giving very explicit and detailed accounts of nudity, sexual activity, or violence, often considered shocking or disturbing.

Examples

He did not wish to deceive Kayabashi exactly, just to display the mission activities in a graphic and impressive manner.

Synset Examples

graphic sexual scenes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explicit - leaving no room for confusion or doubt, especially in describing sexual or violent content
  • Degree Lower: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind
  • Degree Higher: pornographic - intended to arouse sexual excitement, often explicit

Etymology

From Latin graphicus, from Greek graphikos (of drawing or writing).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
(of symbols, devices, etc.) written, drawn, or engraved, especially as opposed to spoken or purely abstract.

Examples

I have often searched for a graphic way of impressing our superiority on those Americans who have doubts, and I think Mr. Jameson Campaigne has done it well in his new book American Might and Soviet Myth.
He concludes that some changes can made in the current orthography which will appreciably improve its usefulness, but hesitates to suggest precise graphic devices to effect these changes.

Synset Examples

graphic symbols

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictorial - relating to or consisting of pictures

Etymology

From Greek graphikos (pertaining to drawing or writing), from graphein (to write, draw).
