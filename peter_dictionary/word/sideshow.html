sideshow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdʃəʊ / (BrE)
A minor entertainment or attraction that is part of a larger event, especially one at a circus or fair.

Examples

And without so much as an apology... ...you ran off with that sideshow phony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main event - the principal or most important performance or attraction at a show

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'show', indicating a subsidiary or additional show aside from the main event.
