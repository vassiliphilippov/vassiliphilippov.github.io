maladroit

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæləˈdrɔɪt / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈdrɔɪt / (BrE)
clumsy or lacking skill; not adroit

Examples

Meager and shabby by-products linger to haunt our memories of a once mighty protagonist; a maladroit reharmonization of our National Anthem (The Star-Spangled Banner); a poor attempt to write an idiomatic jazz concerto; a circus polka for elephants; his hopes that the tunes from his old music might be used for popular American commercial songs!

Synset Examples

a maladroit movement of his hand caused the car to swerve
a maladroit translation
maladroit propaganda

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adroit - skillful and clever
  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling

Etymology

From French 'maladroit' (clumsy), from 'mal-' (badly) + 'adroit' (skillful).
