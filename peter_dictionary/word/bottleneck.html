bottleneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəlˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəlˌnɛk / (BrE)
A narrow section of a road, pipe, or other channel that reduces the flow of traffic, liquid, or other material; also used figuratively for anything that impedes progress or flow.

Examples

Not discussed here are some military problems of modern times such as undersea warfare, where the surveillance, sending, transmitting, and receiving are all so inadequate that networks and decision making are not the bottlenecks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constriction - a narrowing that limits or restricts something
  • Synonym: barrier - something that blocks movement or progress

Etymology

From 'bottle' + 'neck'; named for the narrow opening of a bottle which restricts flow.
