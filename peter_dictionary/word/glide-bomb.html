glide-bomb

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlaɪd bɑm / (AmE), / ˈɡlaɪd bɒm / (BrE)
To drop a bomb from an aircraft while gliding towards the target, often using a bomb designed for unpowered flight after release.

Examples

Finally, as time began to run out, he headed into Ormoc and glide-bombed a group of houses that Intelligence had thought might contain Japanese supplies.

Etymology

Formed from 'glide', to move smoothly and continuously, and 'bomb'; refers to a bombing method using gliding motion.
