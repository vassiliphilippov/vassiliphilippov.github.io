myofibril

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈfaɪ.brəl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈfaɪ.brɪl / (BrE)
A threadlike contractile structure within a striated muscle fiber, composed of repeating sections of sarcomeres, responsible for muscle contraction.

Examples

Such swollen fibers were deeply eosinophilic, contained a few pyknotic nuclei, and showed loss of cross striations, obliteration of myofibrils, and prominent vacuolization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcomere - the basic structural and functional unit of a myofibril responsible for muscle contraction

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + Latin 'fibra' (fiber) + English '-il'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈfaɪ.brəl / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈfɪ.brɪl / (BrE)
A threadlike structure composed of protein that forms part of a muscle fiber and is responsible for muscle contraction.

Examples

In contrast to the nuclear changes described above, another change in muscle nuclei was seen, usually occurring in fibers that were somewhat smaller than normal but that showed distinct cross striations and myofibrillae.
Occasionally there were small basophilic fibers that were devoid of myofibrillae and contained many vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli (Fig. 5).

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: muscle fiber - cell forming the basic unit of muscle tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'fibrilla', diminutive of Latin 'fibra' (fiber).
