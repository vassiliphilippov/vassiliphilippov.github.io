layout

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.aʊt / (BrE)
the arrangement or plan of how something is arranged or set out, such as a page, room, or design

Examples

The American Institute of Interior Designers has published a recommended course for designers and a percentage layout of such a course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: design - a plan or drawing showing the look and function of something before it is made

Etymology

From 'lay out', meaning to arrange or plan, first used in late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈleɪaʊt / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging or planning the design or organization of something

Examples

The layout of the sewer lines was designed by Henry W. Taylor, who was the engineer for the Manchester Village disposal plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for achieving or arranging something

Etymology

From 'lay out' (phrasal verb), meaning to arrange, with the noun form attested from the late 19th century.
