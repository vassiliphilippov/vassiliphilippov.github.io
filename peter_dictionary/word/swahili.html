Swahili

1. NOUN

/ swɑːˈhiːli / (AmE), / swɑːˈhiːli / (BrE)
A Bantu language widely spoken in East Africa, serving as the official language of Kenya and Tanzania and as a lingua franca across East and Central Africa.

Examples

His mind turned the globe swiftly for him, and he visualized Africa, which used Swahili south of the Sahara Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kiswahili - Swahili language as named in its native form

Etymology

From Arabic sawāḥil (coasts), referring to the coastal regions of East Africa where the language originated.
