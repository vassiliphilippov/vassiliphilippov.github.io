imperil

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɛrəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrəl / (BrE)
To put someone or something at risk of being harmed or destroyed; to endanger.

Examples

When the recent Katangan outbreaks imperiled these solutions Mr. Hammarskjold, despite the danger, flew to exert a calming influence.

Synset Examples

The pollution is endangering the crops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endanger - to put someone or something in danger
  • Synonym: jeopardize - to put in danger or at risk

Etymology

From Medieval Latin imperilare, from Latin in- (in, upon) + periculum (danger).
