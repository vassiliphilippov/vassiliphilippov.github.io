enamel

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməl / (BrE)
the hard, white, outer substance that covers the crown of a tooth

Examples

The bristles are soft enough to massage the gums and not scratch the enamel.

Etymology

From Old French esmal, from Latin smaltum, from Greek smaltos (enamel).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnaməl / (BrE)
to coat, inlay, or cover a surface with enamel, especially as a decorative or protective finish

Examples

"I 'm also studying enameling with Hajime Iijima ", he went on, "and twice a week I go to a life class taught by Pendleton ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquer - to coat with lacquer for a protective or decorative finish

Etymology

From Old French *esmail*, from Frankish *smalt* (enamel), related to Old High German *smalz* (enamel).
