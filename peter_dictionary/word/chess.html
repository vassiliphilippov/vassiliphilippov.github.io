chess

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛs / (AmE), / tʃɛs / (BrE)
A board game for two players, each moving 16 pieces according to specific rules, with the objective of checkmating the opponent's king.

Examples

The man likes to play chess.

Etymology

From Old French 'esches', plural of 'eschec', from Medieval Latin 'scaccus', from Arabic 'shāh', from Persian 'shāh' (king).
