multiplicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltɪˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈplɪsɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being numerous or having many parts; in mathematics, the number of times a value or component occurs.

Examples

The number of ordinary values of the function f (t) at t will be called its multiplicity at t.
Further, we see by Lemma 2 that the multiplicity of f can only change at a tangent point, and at such a point can only change by an even integer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plurality - the state of being plural or having more than one part or number

Etymology

From Late Latin multiplicitas, from Latin multiplex (having many folds).
