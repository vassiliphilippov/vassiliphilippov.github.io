boot

1. NOUN

/ buːt / (AmE), / buːt / (BrE)
A type of footwear that covers the whole foot and part of the lower leg, often worn outdoors or for specific activities such as riding or work.

Examples

I hear the boot of Lucifer, I see his filthy face "!
They had stripped him of his musket and equipment, and now they were pulling his boots and jacket off.
His captain was thin and haggard and his beautiful boots were worn and shabby.
The plank floor was slimed beneath Watson's boots.
They were stocky men, well fed and clean-shaven, with neat uniforms and sturdy boots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoe - a covering for the foot, typically not extending above the ankle
  • Degree Lower: slipper - a light, low shoe for indoor wear

Etymology

From Old French *bote*, of uncertain origin, possibly from a Germanic source.

2. NOUN

/ buːt / (AmE), / buːt / (BrE)
(British) the enclosed space at the back of a car for carrying luggage; the trunk.

Examples

Cursing himself for having ridden out the last few days without a rifle in his saddle boot, Russ drew his Colt and examined it briefly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trunk - the main luggage compartment of a car (American English)

Etymology

Possibly from Latin *butis* (cask, bottle), but this car-related sense originated in British English in the early 20th century, perhaps from 'boot locker' on horse-drawn carriages.
