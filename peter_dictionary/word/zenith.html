zenith

1. NOUN

/ ˈziː.nɪθ, ˈzɛn.ɪθ / (AmE), / ˈziː.nɪθ, ˈzɛn.ɪθ / (BrE)
the point in the sky or celestial sphere directly above an observer, opposite the nadir

Examples

The boundless blue air was stretched over the zenith like the skin of a drum.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nadir - the point directly beneath the observer, opposite the zenith

Etymology

From Arabic 'samt' (direction, path) via Old French 'cenith' and Medieval Latin 'cenit'.
