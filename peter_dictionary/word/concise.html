concise

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsaɪs / (AmE), / kənˈsaɪs / (BrE)
Giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words; brief but comprehensive.

Examples

About one-third as long, it is less intimate and detailed, but better coordinated, more concise and more dramatic.

Synset Examples

a concise explanation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succinct - expressed clearly and briefly
  • Synonym: terse - using few words, sometimes in a way that seems unfriendly
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than needed
  • Antonym: wordy - using or expressed in too many words

Etymology

From Latin 'concisus' (cut short), from 'concidere' (to cut up).
