twist

1. NOUN

/ twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
an unexpected or surprising change in the development of a story, situation, or event

Examples

And in the new "King of Kings "the plot involves intrigues and twists and turns that cannot be traced to the Gospels.
But now what crazy twists and turns of his emotions!

Synset Examples

events suddenly took an awkward turn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn - an unexpected change in a situation or story

2. VERB

/ twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
to move in a contorted or writhing motion, especially when struggling or in discomfort

Examples

Used in several sets of high reps once or twice each week it will not be long before your entire upper leg takes on a razor-sharp definition in which the muscles look like wire cables writhing and twisting under the skin!
Stevie twisted and, frantically, commanded the mare to leap straight ahead.
"It was a terrible loss to me ", said Kate quietly, feeling the pain twist again at the mention, knowing now that Juanita must have written to him at Grafton.
The fingers of his right hand twisted into a claw, but he did n't reach for the gun.
Jess's coarse features twisted in a surprised grin which was smashed out of shape by Curt's fist.

Synset Examples

The prisoner writhed in discomfort
The child tried to wriggle free from his aunt's embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writhe - to make twisting, squirming movements, as in pain or struggle
  • Synonym: squirm - to wriggle or twist the body, especially from discomfort or to escape

Etymology

Old English 'twist' (a rope, cord), from 'twis-' (double, divided), related to the verb in sense of turning or winding.

3. NOUN

/ twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
An act of rotating or turning something rapidly.

Examples

It is made up of tumbling, which might be said to start with a somersault, run through such stunts as headstands, handstands, cartwheels, backbends, and culminate in nearly impossible combinations of aerial flips and twists and apparatus work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - an act of turning around quickly
  • Synonym: turn - an act of moving something in a circular direction

4. VERB

/ twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
to cause something flexible or plastic to bend, curl, or assume a crooked or angular form, often by turning or pulling

Examples

Earthquakes result when movements in the earth twist rocks until they break.
Once, then - for how many years or how few does not matter - my world was bound round by fences, when I was too small to reach the apple tree bough, to twist my knee over it and pull myself up.
This will prevent the clay from twisting or bending, causing warping when fired.
Mickey's guts twisted with the effort, but he kept his eye on the weapon.

Synset Examples

bend the rod
twist the dough into a braid
the strong man could turn an iron bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - to shape or force something straight into a curve or angle
  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

Old English twist (a rope, cord, or thread); related to 'twine'.

7. VERB

/ twɪst / (AmE), / twɪst / (BrE)
to turn something or a part of the body in the opposite direction, or to move it so that it faces a new direction

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotate - to turn around a central point or axis
  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight, not twisted

Etymology
