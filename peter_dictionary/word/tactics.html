tactics

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪks / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tɪks / (BrE)
(in military science) The art and science of organizing and maneuvering forces and equipment in order to achieve objectives set by strategy.

Examples

Though he knew no more about military science and tactics than any other desk officer, he managed to get transferred to the combat forces.
Strategy and tactics of the U. S. military forces are now undergoing one of the greatest transitions in history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strategy - the overall plan or policy to achieve long-term goals in war or other contexts
  • Synonym: manoeuvre - a movement or series of moves requiring skill and care, especially in military operations

Etymology

From French ‘tactique’ (tactics), from Greek ‘taktikē’ (art of arrangement).
