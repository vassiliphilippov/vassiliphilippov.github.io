chivy

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪvi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪvi / (BrE)
To annoy or pester someone continually or repeatedly.

Examples

Well, the odious little toad went along chivying animals and humans who could n't retaliate, and in due course, as was inevitable, overreached himself.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harass - to subject someone to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Synonym: pester - to trouble or annoy with frequent or persistent requests

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Romani 'chiv' (to throw) or related to British dialect.
