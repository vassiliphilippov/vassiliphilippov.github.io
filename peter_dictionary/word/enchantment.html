enchantment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæntmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of great delight and fascination caused by something wonderful or unusual.

Examples

Now a little flush came on her pale homely face and enchantment in her eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captivation - the state of being intensely interested or charmed
  • Synonym: bewitchment - the state of being controlled or strongly affected as if by magic
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of feeling uninterested or lacking in excitement

Etymology

From Old French *enchantement*, from Latin *incantare* (to chant a magic spell).
