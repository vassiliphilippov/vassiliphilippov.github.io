dull

1. ADJ

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
(of a color) lacking brightness or intensity; having very low saturation; not vivid or bright.

Examples

The males are much duller.

Synset Examples

dull greens and blues

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vivid - (of colors) intensely deep or bright
  • Degree Higher: bright - (of colors) having a lot of light or saturation
  • Synonym: pale - (of colors) light in color or washed out

2. ADJ

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
lacking in liveliness, excitement, or interest; boring or monotonous

Examples

Scotty looked at the children, his mouth slightly opened, his eyes dull.
But unlike Holmes, he feels his society to be not merely dull but also corrupt.
While five minutes ago the place had presented a scene of easy revelry, with Gyp Carmer a prominent figure, it was now as somnolent and dull as the day before payday.

Synset Examples

he was so dull at parties
a dull political campaign
a large dull impassive man
dull days with nothing to do
how dull and dreary the world is
fell back into one of her dull moods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; tedious
  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

From Old English *dol* (foolish, dull-witted).

3. ADJ

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
(of a sound) being less loud, clear, or distinct; muffled or muted.

Examples

a dull boom and a throbbing echo.

Synset Examples

the dull boom of distant breaking waves
muffled drums
the muffled noises of the street
muted trumpets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muffled - (of a sound) not loud because of being obstructed in some way
  • Synonym: muted - (of sound) quieted or softened
  • Antonym: sharp - (of a sound) clear, distinct, and loud

4. ADJ

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) not bright, shiny, or reflective; emitting or reflecting little light.

Examples

It gave the rubberized fabric a dull gleam, like metal.
Often, in working out-of-doors under all conditions of light and atmosphere, a particular passage that looked favorable in relation to the subject will be too bright, too dull, or too light, or too dark when viewed indoors in a mat.
When these had been pocketed, we could still spend a morning cracking open other pebbles for our delight in seeing how much prettier they were inside than their dull exteriors indicated.
"Tee-wah ", the driver cackled, his black eyes glittering behind dull silver chicken fencing.

Synset Examples

a dull glow
dull silver badly in need of a polish
a dull sky

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shiny - bright or glossy in appearance
  • Synonym: matte - having a dull or non-glossy surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'dul', probably from Old English 'dul' (slow-witted), of unknown origin.

5. ADJ

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
Lacking interest or excitement; boring or tedious.

Examples

I felt superior to the neighborhood friends I was leaving behind, felt older than my years, and was full of compliments for myself as I headed into the subway that was carrying its packs of passengers out of that dull borough and into the unstable, tantalizing excitement of Manhattan.
It is dull business, detecting, and hard on feet.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - not interesting, causing boredom
  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome
  • Antonym: interesting - holding one's attention; stimulating

Etymology

From Old English 'dul' (dull, foolish), of Germanic origin.

6. VERB

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
to make or become less bright, shiny, or intense in appearance

Examples

Jack Frost is not really responsible for this great seasonal spectacle; in fact, a freezing autumn dulls the blaze.

Synset Examples

Age had dulled the surface

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brighten - to make or become brighter or more vivid

7. VERB

/ dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
(of a surface, feeling, or activity) to become less bright, shiny, intense, or sharp; to lose luster or vividness.

Examples

The pain dulled as he moved, and he steadied inside.

Synset Examples

the varnished table top dulled with time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brighten - to become or make brighter or more vivid

Etymology

From Middle English dul, from Old English dol (foolish, dull).
