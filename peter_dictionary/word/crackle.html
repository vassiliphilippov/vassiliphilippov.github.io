crackle

1. VERB

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
to make a rapid succession of short, sharp, or snapping sounds, like something being crushed or burning.

Examples

(Norman Mailer), but no one can deny that the screen crackles with electricity whenever he is on it.

Synset Examples

his shoes were crunching on the gravel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - make a sudden sharp cracking sound
  • Synonym: crunch - make a crushing or grinding noise

Etymology

From Middle English cracklen, frequentative of cracken (to crack).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrækəl / (AmE), / ˈkrækəl / (BrE)
a series of short, sharp, snapping sounds

Examples

Amid the crackle of small arms and automatic weapons, I heard the thumping of mortars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - a sudden, sharp cracking sound
  • Synonym: pop - a short, sharp, explosive sound

Etymology

Imitative formation based on 'crack'.
