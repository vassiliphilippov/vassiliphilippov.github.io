enervate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛnərˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌveɪt / (BrE)
To make someone feel drained of energy or vitality; to weaken mentally or morally.

Examples

What you were looking for (unless you make a hobby of collecting old tennis rackets and fly screens) eludes me, but to judge from phonograph records scattered about a fumed oak Victrola, you danced two tangos and a paso doble, which must have been fairly enervating in that milieu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaken - to make or become weaker in power, resolve, or strength
  • Antonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Latin 'enervare' (to weaken), from 'ex-' (out of) + 'nervus' (sinew, nerve).
