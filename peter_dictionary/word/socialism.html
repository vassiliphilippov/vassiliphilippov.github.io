socialism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political and economic system in which the means of production, such as industries and land, are owned and controlled by the state or the community as a whole, with the aim of achieving equality and reducing differences between social classes

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: communism - a political and economic system aiming for collective ownership, often with abolition of class
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership of the means of production

Etymology

From French socialisme, from social (social) + -isme (-ism).
