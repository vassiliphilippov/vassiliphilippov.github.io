East German

1. NOUN

/ ist ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the former republic of East Germany (the German Democratic Republic, 1949–1990)

Examples

When East Germans fled to the West by the thousands, paeans of joy rose from the throats of Western publicists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: West German - a native or inhabitant of the former West Germany

Etymology

Formed from 'East' (directional adjective) and 'German' (relating to Germany), referring specifically to the German Democratic Republic (1949–1990).

2. ADJ

/ ist ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of East Germany (the former German Democratic Republic) or its people

Examples

The weekly loss is partly counterbalanced by 500 arrivals each week from West Germany, but the hard truth, says Crossman, is that "The closing off of East Berlin without interference from the West and with the use only of East German, as distinct from Russian, troops was a major Communist victory, which dealt West Berlin a deadly, possibly a fatal, blow.
