laundering

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔn.dɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːn.dər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of washing clothes, bed linens, and other washable fabrics

Examples

Early in the war it was not uncommon for planters' sons to retain in camp Negro "body servants "to perform the menial chores such as cooking, foraging, cleaning the quarters, shining shoes, and laundering clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing - the act of cleaning something with water and usually soap

Etymology

From 'launder' (to wash), from Old French *lavandier* (launderer), from Latin *lavare* (to wash).
