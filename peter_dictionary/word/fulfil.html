fulfil

1. VERB

/ fʊlˈfɪl / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪl / (BrE)
to do or carry out something that has been promised, required, or expected; to put into effect

Examples

Each ally will have to carry out obligations long since laid down, but never completely fulfilled.
Nixon fulfilled his assignment with grace, then went beyond the required "sufficient declaration ".

Synset Examples

carry out a task
execute the decision of the people
He actioned the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carry out - to perform or complete a task or duty
  • Synonym: execute - to carry out a plan, order, or course of action
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to carry out a duty or obligation

Etymology

From Old English 'fullfyllan', from 'full' + 'fyllan' (to fill).
