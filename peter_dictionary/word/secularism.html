secularism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkjələrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlərɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that religion should not play a role in government, education, or other public parts of society, or the principle of separating religion from civic affairs and public institutions.

Examples

A century ago, Newman saw that liberalism (what we now might call secularism) would gradually but definitely make its mark on English Protestantism, and that even high Anglicanism would someday no longer be a "serviceable breakwater against doctrinal errors more fundamental than its own ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheism - belief that there is no god

Etymology

From secular + -ism, based on Latin saecularis (worldly).
