synergistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪnɚˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
(of substances, actions, or agents) working together so that their combined effect is greater than the sum of their individual effects, especially referring to drugs, muscles, or systems.

Examples

Upon consideration of the variety of soils and fabrics normally encountered in the washing process, it is little wonder that the use of a number of detergent constituents having "synergistic "properties has gained widespread acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antagonistic - (of drugs, muscles, etc.) acting to oppose or reduce the action or effect of another
  • Synonym: synergy - the interaction or cooperation that produces a combined effect greater than the sum of individual effects

Etymology

From Greek 'synergos' (working together) via synergy + -istic.
