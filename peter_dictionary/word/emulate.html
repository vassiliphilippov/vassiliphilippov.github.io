emulate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛm.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to try to equal or surpass someone or something, especially by imitating them

Examples

When one powerful nation strives to emulate the success of another, it is only natural.
The Senate's overwhelming (64 - 13) vote to support locally controlled educational TV efforts should be emulated in the lower house.

Synset Examples

He is emulating the skating skills of his older sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitate - to copy someone or something

Etymology

From Latin emulari (to rival, strive to equal or excel)
