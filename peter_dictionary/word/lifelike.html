lifelike

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌlaɪk / (BrE)
very similar to or accurately representing real life, especially in appearance, detail, or effect.

Examples

I am sure that nothing within me is capable of composing that life-like sequence, so complete in detail, from the hodge-podge of news pictures I have seen.
His hands lay loosely, yet stiffly - they were like wax hands: almost lifelike, not quite - folded in his lap; his mouth hung slightly open.

Synset Examples

pictorial poetry and prose
graphic accounts of battle
a lifelike portrait
a vivid description

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is accurate and true to life
  • Antonym: unrealistic - not true to life; not accurately representing reality

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'like', meaning like life.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪflaɪk / (BrE)
Very similar to real life in appearance or effect; natural and free from artificiality.

Examples

And a gray-haired man whose glance - direct, lifelike, and mildly accusing - was contradicted by the gilt and black frame.

Synset Examples

a lifelike pose
a natural reaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not artificial
  • Antonym: artificial - not genuine or natural; made to imitate something natural

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'like'.
