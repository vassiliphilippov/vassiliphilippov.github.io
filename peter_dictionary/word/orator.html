orator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrətər / (AmE), / ˈɒrətə / (BrE)
a person who delivers speeches, especially someone skilled in public speaking

Examples

But the famous orator felt more than vague recognition for the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaker - a person who speaks, especially one who makes a formal speech
  • Synonym: declaimer - a person who recites or delivers speeches with rhetorical effect

Etymology

From Latin orator (speaker), from orare (to speak, plead).
