phenothiazine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛnoʊˈθaɪəziːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnəʊˈθaɪəziːn / (BrE)
a synthetic chemical compound used primarily as a veterinary medicine to treat internal parasites in farm animals; also the parent substance of a class of antipsychotic drugs

Examples

Feeder cattle - 2 - 5 grams of phenothiazine daily; beef calves - .5 to 1.5 grams daily depending on weight of animal.
For continuous control, feed 1 part phenothiazine to 9 parts minerals or salts.
To include in feed, add phenothiazine to supply 0.5 to 1 gram per sheep daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthelmintic - a drug that destroys or expels parasitic worms

Etymology

From pheno- (relating to benzene) + thiazine (a type of heterocyclic compound).
