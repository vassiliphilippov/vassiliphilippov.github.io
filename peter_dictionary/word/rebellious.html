rebellious

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈbɛliəs / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛliəs / (BrE)
unwilling to obey authority, rules, or control; showing a desire to resist or fight against authority or tradition

Examples

Adele, like Amy, the youngest of the Marches, was the rebellious, mischievous, rather calculating and ambitious one.

Synset Examples

temperamentally rebellious
a rebellious crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - openly resisting authority or opposition
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with authority or rules

Etymology

From Old French *rebellius*, from Latin *rebellis* (waging war again, rebellious).
