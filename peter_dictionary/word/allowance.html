allowance

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an amount of money given regularly or as a one-time payment, especially to reimburse expenses incurred.

Examples

Schley County Rep. B. D. Pelham will offer a resolution Monday in the House to rescind the body's action of Friday in voting itself a $ 10 per day increase in expense allowances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reimbursement - repayment for money spent or expenses incurred
  • Synonym: stipend - fixed regular sum paid as a salary or allowance

Etymology

From Old French *alouance* (act of allotting), from *alouer* (to allot, allow).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an amount of money or quantity given regularly for a particular purpose, especially to cover expenses or as a benefit

Examples

She was moving up to the allowance department after winning a $ 10000 claiming event.
"Imagine you won't get your allowance if you 're caught not smiling - or smiling with your lips trembling too much ", Arlene suggested.

Synset Examples

travel allowance
my weekly allowance of two eggs
a child's allowance should not be too generous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stipend - fixed regular sum paid as a benefit or allowance
  • Synonym: grant - sum of money given, often for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: salary - regular payment for employment, differs by context but related

Etymology

From Old French *alouance*, from *alouer* (to allow).

3. NOUN

/ əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an amount of money added to or deducted from a sum for a specific reason, such as depreciation, profit, or contingencies

Examples

Include as expenditures an allowance for depreciation of farm buildings and equipment.

Synset Examples

an allowance for profit

Etymology

From Old French *alouance* (allowing), from *alouer* (to allow), from Latin *allaudare* (to praise, to approve).

4. NOUN

/ əˈlaʊ.əns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ.əns / (BrE)
an amount or margin by which something is permitted to vary from a standard or limit; a permitted difference or tolerance

Examples

Hoijer's Athabascan and my Yokuts share 71 identical meanings (with allowance for several near synonyms like stomach belly, big large, long far, many much, die dead, say speak).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerance - the allowable deviation from a standard, especially in measurement

Etymology

From Old French 'alouance', from 'alouer' (to allocate), based on 'allouer'.
