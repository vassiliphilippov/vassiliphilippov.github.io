scoundrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊndrəl / (AmE), / ˈskaʊndrəl / (BrE)
A person who behaves dishonestly or wickedly; a rogue.

Examples

Furthermore, he's something of a scoundrel, an artist whose mind and feelings are all finger-tips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person
  • Synonym: rogue - a dishonest or unprincipled person
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courageous or noble qualities

Etymology

Probably from Scots *scunner* (disgust, aversion) plus diminutive suffix -el.
