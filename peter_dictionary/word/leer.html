leer

1. VERB

/ lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
to look or gaze in an unpleasant, sly, sexually suggestive, or malicious way

Examples

Looming over all, Papa-san leered down at him, threatening a hundred hidden malevolencies.
The stranger leered.

Synset Examples

The men leered at the young women on the beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ogle - to look at someone with obvious sexual interest

Etymology

From Middle English *leren*, probably related to obsolete 'leer' (the cheek), or from Old English *hlǣran* (to learn, teach), with shift in meaning influenced by association with sly looks.
