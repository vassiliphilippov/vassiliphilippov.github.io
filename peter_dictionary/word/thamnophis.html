Thamnophis

1. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɑːfɪs / (AmE), / θamˈnɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of non-venomous snakes commonly known as garter snakes, found throughout North and Central America.

Examples

Laurence M. Klauber put length at maturity at two thirds the ultimate length for some rattlesnakes, and Charles C. Carpenter's data on Michigan garter and ribbon snakes (Thamnophis) show that the smallest gravid females are more than half as long as the biggest adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garter snake - a common name for snakes of genus Thamnophis

Etymology

From Greek 'thamnos' (bush) and 'ophis' (snake).
