sympathize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪz / (BrE)
To share or understand the feelings of another person, especially in times of sorrow or trouble.

Examples

Therefore, he decided he was unfair to the young man and should make an effort to understand and sympathize with his point of view.
We sympathize with Mr. Kennedy, but we feel bound to say that his budget review does n't please us either, although for very different reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling), via Late Latin sympathizare.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪz / (BrE)
to feel or express understanding and compassion for someone else's situation or feelings

Examples

I tell you, I know how it is with you, my friend, I sympathize, and I 'll make it a special point - a special favor - get in touch, and get some stuff just for you.

Synset Examples

You don't need to explain--I understand!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings
  • Synonym: condole - to express sympathy, especially in times of sorrow

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling), via Medieval Latin sympathizare and French sympathiser.
