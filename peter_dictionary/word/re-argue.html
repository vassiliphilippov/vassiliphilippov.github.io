re-argue

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈɑrgju / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɑːgjuː / (BrE)
to argue something again, especially after it has already been discussed or decided

Examples

"He is wrong to inject Eisenhower into this campaign ", he said, "because the primary is being waged on state issues and I will not be forced into re arguing an old national campaign ".

Synset Examples

This politician will be forced into re-arguing an old national campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argue - to give reasons or cite evidence in support of an idea, action, or theory

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' meaning 'again' and the verb 'argue'.
