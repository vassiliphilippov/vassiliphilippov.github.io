unacceptable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəkˈsɛptəbl / (AmE), / ˌʌnəkˈsɛptəbl / (BrE)
Not satisfactory or not allowed; too bad to be accepted.

Examples

Representing as it did the efforts of only unauthorized individuals of the Roman and Anglican Churches, and urging a communion of prayer unacceptable to Rome, this association produced little fruit, and, in fact, was condemned by the Holy Office in 1864.
Hairspray won't do it alone.

Synset Examples

the coach told his players that defeat was unacceptable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptable - satisfactory or allowed

Etymology

From un- (not) + acceptable, from Latin acceptabilis (fit to be accepted).
