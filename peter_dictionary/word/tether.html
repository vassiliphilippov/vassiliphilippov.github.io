tether

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛðə / (BrE)
to tie (an animal or object) with a rope or chain to restrict its movement to a certain area

Examples

They were tethered, army style, on stable lines.

Synset Examples

tether horses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untether - to release from a tether; to set free

Etymology

From Old Norse *tjǫðr* (tether, rope); related to Old English *teþer*.
