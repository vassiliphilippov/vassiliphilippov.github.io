mastermind

1. VERB

/ ˈmæstərˌmaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəmaɪnd / (BrE)
To plan and direct a complex and often secret or difficult project or undertaking.

Examples

(Jelke later served 21 months when he was found guilty of masterminding a ring of high-priced call girls.)

Synset Examples

he masterminded the robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestrate - to arrange and direct the elements of a complex project
  • Synonym: engineer - to skillfully arrange for something to happen, especially secretly

Etymology

From 'master' (having control or authority) and 'mind' (intellect, plan); first used as noun in late 19th century, verb use developed in 20th century.
