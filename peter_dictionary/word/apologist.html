apologist

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who defends or supports a particular policy, belief, or institution, especially by argument

Examples

Faulkner's is not the mind of the apologist which Mr. O' Donnell implies that it is.

Synset Examples

an apologist for capital punishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defender - a person who defends someone or something
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disagreements or disapproval

Etymology

From Greek 'apologia' (a speech in defense), via Late Latin 'apologista'.
