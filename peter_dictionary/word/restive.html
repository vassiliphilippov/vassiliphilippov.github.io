restive

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛstɪv / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪv / (BrE)
Unable to remain still, silent, or submissive, especially because of impatience, dissatisfaction, or nervousness.

Examples

The men were restive, he wrote, ready to take the battle to the enemy as Jackson wished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edgy - tense, nervous, or easily irritated
  • Antonym: placid - calm and not easily excited or disturbed

Etymology

From French *restif* (unruly, stubborn), from Latin *restare* (to remain behind, resist).
