quilted

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɪltɪd / (BrE)
(of fabric or a garment) made with two or more layers of material held together by lines of decorative stitching, often forming a padded effect.

Examples

It was amazing how they had herded together for protection: an enormous matriarch in a quilted silk wrapper, rising from the breakfast table; a gross boy in his teens, shuffling in from the kitchen with a sandwich in his hands; a girl in her twenties, fat and sullen, descending the marble staircase; then all four gathering on the sofa to face the inquisitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: padded - filled with soft material for comfort or protection
  • Synonym: stitched - made with or joined by stitches

Etymology

From 'quilt' + -ed, indicating made in the manner of a quilt. 'Quilt' from Middle English quilte, from Old French cuilte, from Latin culcita (mattress, cushion).
