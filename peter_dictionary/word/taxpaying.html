taxpaying

1. ADJ

/ ˈtækˌpeɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæksˌpeɪɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, business, etc.) required to pay taxes; not exempt from taxation.

Examples

In the case of taxpaying corporate stockholders, the measure would be the lesser of the fair market value of the shares or du Pont's tax basis for them, which is approximately $ 2.09 per share.

Synset Examples

after training they became productive taxpaying citizens

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tax-exempt - exempt from paying taxes

Etymology

From 'tax' + present participle of 'pay' (from Old French 'paier', from Latin 'pacare', to appease, settle).
