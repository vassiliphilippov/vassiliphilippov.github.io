seedbed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsidˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈsiːdbɛd / (BrE)
a prepared area of soil where seeds are sown and seedlings are grown before being transplanted to their final location

Examples

I like to make a seedbed right in the open, though many people start them successfully in cold frames.
For my seedbed I use good garden soil with a little sand added to encourage rooting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nursery - area where young plants or trees are grown before transplanting

Etymology

From 'seed' + 'bed', indicating a bed for seeds.
