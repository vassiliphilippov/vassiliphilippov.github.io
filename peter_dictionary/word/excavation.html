excavation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.skəˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛks.kəˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of digging out and removing earth, especially as part of an archaeological investigation or construction project.

Examples

"How many is that, Jim "? asked Willie at one stage of his excavation project.
The historian can only point out those lines which were major enough to find reflection in our limited evidence, and must hope that future excavations will enrich our understanding.

Synset Examples

there's an interesting excavation going on near Princeton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digging - the act of making a hole in the ground by removing soil

Etymology

From Latin excavatio (a hollowing out), from excavare (to hollow out), from ex- (out) + cavare (to hollow).
