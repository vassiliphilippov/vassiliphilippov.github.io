incitement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
something that encourages someone to act or stimulates a particular action or feeling

Examples

The kind of religious experience that most moderns seek not only provides, clarifies, and relates human yearnings, values, ideals, and purposes; it also provides facilities and incitements for the development of personality, sociality, and creativeness.

Synset Examples

the result was a provocation of vigorous investigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of attempting to prevent something by showing disapproval or creating difficulties

Etymology

From Old French *encitement*, from Latin *incitamentum* (‘incentive, stimulus’), from *incitare* (‘to urge on’).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging, urging, or provoking someone to take action, especially to do something bad or unlawful
