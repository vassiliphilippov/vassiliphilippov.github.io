heal

1. VERB

/ hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
to become healthy again; to return to a normal state after injury or illness

Examples

Bevo was congratulated for his efforts to stay in shape so that he could help the team if his knee healed in time.

Synset Examples

The wound is healing slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health or strength
  • Antonym: deteriorate - to become worse in health or condition

Etymology

Old English hælan (make whole, sound, or well).

2. VERB

/ hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
(of an injury, wound, or person) to become healthy or normal again; to recover from damage or injury.

Examples

His arm had been giving him some trouble and Rector was not enough of a medical expert to determine whether it had healed improperly or whether Hino was simply rebelling against the tedious work in the print shop, using the stiffness in his arm as an excuse.
That breach was healed 20 years later by merger of the American Federation of Labor and the Congress of Industrial Organizations.

Synset Examples

My broken leg is mending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure - to restore health; make well
  • Synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health or strength
  • Antonym: injure - to do harm or damage to

Etymology

Old English hælan (to make whole, sound, or well), related to 'whole'.
