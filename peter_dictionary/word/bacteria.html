bacteria

1. NOUN

/ bækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Microscopic single-celled organisms, typically lacking chlorophyll, that reproduce by fission and can be found in various shapes such as spheres, rods, or spirals; some cause disease, while others play important roles in the environment.

Examples

The vegetables were not readily metabolized by the bacteria in the aeration unit and tended to float on the surface.
The bacteria formed typical activated sludge floc.
Many cells, bacteria, and other microorganisms are transparent to visible light and must be stained for microscopic investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - singular form of bacteria; a single microscopic organism of the group

Etymology

Plural of bacterium, from Greek baktērion (small staff, rod).
