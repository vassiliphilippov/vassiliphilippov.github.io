perpetuation

1. NOUN

/ pərˌpɛtʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌpɛtjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of causing something to continue or be sustained for a long period.

Examples

Historically, religion has also functioned as a tremendous engine of vindication, enforcement, sanction, and perpetuation of various other institutions.
For most Brooklyn College students, college is at once a perpetuation of their ethnic attachments and a breaking away from the cage of neighborhood and family.

Synset Examples

there was an indefinite prolongation of the peace talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolongation - the act of making something last longer than usual or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetuatio', from 'perpetuare' (to make perpetual).
