wreckage

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the parts of something such as a vehicle, building, or structure that remain after it has been badly damaged or destroyed

Examples

Not until they sailed home at the end of the day, through a sea strewn with bodies and the wreckage of houses, were they aware of what had happened.

Synset Examples

they searched the wreckage for signs of survivors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debris - scattered pieces of waste or remains

Etymology

From 'wreck' + '-age', possibly via Old French 'wreck', ultimately from Old Norse 'wrek'.
