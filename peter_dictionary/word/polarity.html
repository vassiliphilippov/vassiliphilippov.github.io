polarity

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˈlarəti / (BrE)
the property of having distinct and opposite poles, such as positive and negative electrical charges, or the direction of a magnetic or electrical field

Examples

Electrical literature contained numerous references to lightning that had magnetized iron and had altered the polarity of compass needles.

Synset Examples

he got the polarity of the battery reversed
charges of opposite sign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bipolarity - the state of having two poles or opposite characteristics
  • Antonym: neutrality - the state of having no polarity or having balanced charges

Etymology

From Late Latin polaris (of or relating to a pole) + -ity.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˈlarəti / (BrE)
The state of having two opposite or contradictory tendencies, attributes, or opinions.

Examples

And he describes it as a balanced polarity between the notions of the free individual and what he called the fundamental law.
I used his polarity to illustrate what I thought had happened to us in that form of liberalism we call Progressivism.
It seemed to me that the liberals had scrapped the balanced polarity and reposed both liberty and the fundamental law in the common man.

Synset Examples

he viewed it as a balanced polarity between good and evil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposition - the state of being in conflict or contrast
  • Antonym: unity - the state of being united or forming a whole

Etymology

From Late Latin *polaritas*, from Latin *polaris* ('of or pertaining to a pole').
