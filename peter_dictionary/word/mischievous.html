mischievous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs / (BrE)
(of a person, action, or expression) causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful or teasing way.

Examples

I think, too ", he said, his dark eyes mischievous, "that you will find there some clue to the secret of the cathedrals about which you have spoken ".

Synset Examples

teasing and worrying with impish laughter
a wicked prank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impish - playfully mischievous or cheeky
  • Synonym: naughty - badly behaved, especially in a playful way
  • Synonym: wicked - playfully or mischievously evil or morally wrong

Etymology

From Old French meschevous (unfortunate, wicked), from meschief (misfortune).
