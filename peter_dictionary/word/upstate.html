upstate

1. ADV

/ ˌʌpˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈsteɪt / (BrE)
in or toward the northern or more rural parts of a state, especially in the United States

Examples

Or was it her own first ball as mistress of this big house, a Van Rensselaer bride from way upstate near Albany, from Rensselaerwyck.
They transferred him to a minimum-security hospital upstate.

Synset Examples

he lives upstate New York

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downstate - in or toward the southern or more urban parts of a state

Etymology

Formed from 'up' + 'state', modeled after expressions like 'up country'.
