congenital

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) present from birth, but not necessarily inherited; acquired during fetal development.

Examples

Similar findings have been noted in a patient with congenital absence of the organification enzymes, whose thyroid tissue could only concentrate iodide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hereditary - (of a trait or condition) passed from parent to offspring through genes

Etymology

From Latin 'congenitus,' past participle of 'congignere' (to be born together).
