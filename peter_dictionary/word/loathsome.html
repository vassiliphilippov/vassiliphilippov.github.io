loathsome

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊð.səm / (AmE), / ˈləʊð.səm / (BrE)
causing intense disgust or revulsion; extremely unpleasant or offensive, sometimes to the point of causing nausea.

Examples

he liked children, in a loathsome kind of way; the two youngest in our family always had to be brought in and put through tricks for his entertainment.

Synset Examples

a nauseating smell
nauseous offal
a sickening stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nauseating - causing nausea or extreme disgust
  • Synonym: disgusting - causing a strong sense of dislike or disapproval
  • Antonym: delightful - highly pleasing or charming

Etymology

From Middle English 'lothsum', from 'loath' (to hate, be unwilling) + '-some' (characterized by).

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊðsəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊðsəm / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant; causing hatred or strong disgust.

Examples

Being a teacher of American literature, I remembered Whittier's "Massachusetts to Virginia ", where he said: "But that one dark loathsome burden ye must stagger with alone, And reap the bitter harvest which ye yourselves have sown ".

Synset Examples

a disgusting smell
distasteful language
a loathsome disease
the idea of eating meat is repellent to me
revolting food
a wicked stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgusting - arousing revulsion or strong dislike
  • Synonym: repulsive - causing strong dislike or aversion
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Middle English 'lothsum', from 'loath' + '-some'.
