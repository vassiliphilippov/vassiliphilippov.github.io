premonitory

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈmɑːnɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / prɪˈmɒnɪtəri / (BrE)
Giving warning or indication of a future event, especially of misfortune.

Examples

The revolution was well under way before 700 B.C., and premonitory signs go back virtually across the century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precursory - serving as a forerunner; indicative or warning of what is to come

Etymology

From Latin praemonitorius, from praemonere 'to warn beforehand'.
