Virginian

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniən / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of the state of Virginia in the United States.

Examples

Probably a larger percentage of Virginians and South Carolinians remain unreconstructed than elsewhere, with Georgia, North Carolina, and Alabama following along after them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia - A state in the eastern United States

Etymology

From Virginia, the U.S. state, named after Queen Elizabeth I (the 'Virgin Queen').
