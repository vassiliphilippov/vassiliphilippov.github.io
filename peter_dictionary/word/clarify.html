clarify

1. VERB

/ ˈklɛrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈklærɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something clearer and easier to understand; to remove confusion or uncertainty from something

Examples

By 1937 he had clarified his intentions to serve his people: "I have striven for clarity and melodious idiom, but at the same time I have by no means attempted to restrict myself to the accepted methods of harmony and melody.

Synset Examples

clarify the mystery surrounding her death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explain - make something clear by describing it in detail
  • Antonym: obscure - make unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Late Latin clarificare (to make clear), from clarus (clear) + facere (to make).
