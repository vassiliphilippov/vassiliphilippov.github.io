anemic

1. ADJ

/ əˈniːmɪk / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪk / (BrE)
Lacking in vigor, energy, or vitality; weak or feeble.

Examples

The Giants who had been anemic with the bat in their windy Candlestick Park suddenly found the formula in Milwaukee's park.

Synset Examples

an anemic attempt to hit the baseball

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vigorous - full of energy, strong and active

Etymology

From medical use: ultimately from Greek anaïmos (bloodless), from an- (without) + haima (blood).
