belly

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
the part of the body of a vertebrate between the chest and the pelvis; the abdomen

Examples

They 'd cut their mothers' belly open.
Isaac Pitt, one of the men from Lincoln, had taken a musket ball in his belly; and though he had found the strength to run with us, now he collapsed and lay on the ground, dying, the Reverend holding his head and wiping his hot brow.
That's why I only put towels on his chest and belly.
His head undulated gradually, covering space, to come straining beneath the taut belly within the warmth of those teats.
But the boy could see only the goat's belly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdomen - technical term for the part of the body between the chest and pelvis

Etymology

Old English *belig* (bag, pouch), related to Old High German *balla* (ball).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
A large or protruding stomach or abdomen, especially when sticking out more than usual.

Examples

The Old Man's son threw himself down, belly first, upon a concrete step, taking in the coolness of it, and dreaming of the day he would be rich.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomach - abdomen, the front part of the body below the chest
  • Synonym: abdomen - the part of the body containing digestive organs, between the chest and pelvis

Etymology

From Old English 'belig' (bag, pouch), related to the idea of swelling or being distended.
