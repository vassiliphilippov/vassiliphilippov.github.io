flashy

1. ADJ

/ ˈflæʃi / (AmE), / ˈflæʃi / (BrE)
(of clothes, objects, or people) attracting attention by being very bright, noticeable, or showy, often in a tasteless way.

Examples

The principal tactic in controlling the ball was giving it to Abner Haynes, the flashy halfback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - drawing attention by being bright, colorful, or ostentatious
  • Antonym: modest - not drawing attention; humble, not showy

Etymology

From 'flash' + '-y', originally meaning 'showy or ostentatious', first recorded in the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈflæʃi / (AmE), / ˈflæʃi / (BrE)
Bright or showy in a way that lacks good taste or subtlety; intended to attract attention, often in a tasteless manner.

Examples

Yet a moment did come that night when the adventurous letter writer and fantasist seemed to stride off my flashy pages, out of my mind, and plant himself in reality.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - excessively showy or tastelessly bright
  • Synonym: garish - unpleasantly bright or showy
  • Antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way, the opposite of showy

Etymology

From 'flash' (meaning to emit a sudden brief light) + -y (forming adjectives).
