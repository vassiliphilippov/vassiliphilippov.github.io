/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
(of behavior) caused by or showing evidence of a mentally disturbed condition; compulsive and abnormal.

Examples

The foregoing remarks imply that the hypothalamic balance plays a crucial role at the crossroads between physiological and pathological forms of emotion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsive - resulting from or relating to an irresistible urge, especially one that is against one's conscious wishes
  • Synonym: obsessive - of the nature of an obsession; dominating a person's thoughts
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; not abnormal or pathological

Etymology

From Greek pathologikos ('treating of diseases'), from pathos ('suffering') + -logia ('study of').
