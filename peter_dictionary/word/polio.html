polio

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
An acute infectious viral disease that affects the nervous system and can cause temporary or permanent paralysis, particularly in children; also called poliomyelitis.

Examples

The merits of the Salk anti polio vaccine were not established on the forensic platform or in newspaper editorials, but in the laboratory and by tests in the field on thousands of children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poliomyelitis - medical term for polio, acute viral infection affecting the nervous system

Etymology

Shortened from 'poliomyelitis', from Greek 'polios' (gray) and 'myelos' (marrow).
