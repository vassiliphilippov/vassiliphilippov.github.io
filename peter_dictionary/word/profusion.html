profusion

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / prəˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
an extremely large quantity or abundance of something

Examples

With Ritter he was exposed to the fantastic profusion of ideas that stormed through his host's fertile but disorganized mind.

Synset Examples

the profusion of detail
the idiomatic richness of English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply

Etymology

From Latin 'profusionem', from 'profundere' meaning 'to pour forth'.
