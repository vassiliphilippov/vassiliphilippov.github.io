condemnatory

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdɛm.nəˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm.nə.tər.i / (BrE)
expressing strong disapproval or criticism; containing or imposing condemnation or censure.

Examples

I could evoke no further elaboration from her about this; but a few seconds later she was standing directly across the room from me, looking me in the eyes and saying in a scathingly condemnatory tone, "Your father despises you "!

Synset Examples

a condemnatory decree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - expressing or involving an analysis or evaluation, often adverse
  • Antonym: approving - expressing or showing acceptance and support

Etymology

From condemn +‎ -atory, ultimately from Latin 'condemnare' (to sentence, condemn).
