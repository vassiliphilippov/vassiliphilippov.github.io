midshipman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌʃɪpmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdʃɪpmən / (BrE)
A rank for a young officer in training in the navy, typically held temporarily before full commissioning.

Examples

Those of us present, the Perry brothers, Deslonde and the other midshipmen now have the responsibility of the Somers.
No action of his could be interpreted in his favor and four midshipmen, prior to their knowing the significance of the Greek paper, had seen it in Cromwell's hands while Spencer whispered explanations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ensign - lowest commissioned officer in the navy

Etymology

From 'midship' + 'man', originally referring to a ship's apprentice stationed amidships.
