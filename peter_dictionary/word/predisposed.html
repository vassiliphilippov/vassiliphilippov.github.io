predisposed

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːdɪˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
Having a tendency or inclination to be affected by something, especially a disease or a particular condition.

Examples

Because of the authentic homogeneity of his early Nationalistic materials, and his flair for orchestrations - his brilliant Petruchka, his savage Sacre du Printemps, his incisive Les Noces - the world kept hoping that he could recapture the historical direction for which his native talents were predisposed.

Synset Examples

because of conditions in the mine, miners are predisposed to lung disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
  • Antonym: immune - not susceptible to something; protected from

Etymology

From prefix pre- (before) + disposed, from Latin disponere (to arrange).
