divan

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvæn / (AmE), / dɪˈvæn / (BrE)
A long, low sofa without a back or arms, typically placed against a wall and often topped with cushions.

Examples

Only then, when his glance focused on the divan and saw that it was empty, did he remember his earlier problem.
The wastebasket stood near the wall next to the divan, and the instant Casey picked it up he knew what had happened.
I guided her to the divan, turned off the TV, faced her.
I sat by her on the divan.
Joyce squirmed a little on the divan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a comfortable seat wide enough for two/three people
  • Synonym: couch - a long upholstered seat for reclining

Etymology

From Turkish 'divan', originally meaning 'council' or 'council chamber', where such couches were used.
