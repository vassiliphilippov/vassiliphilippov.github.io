unpunished

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpʌnɪʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈpʌnɪʃt / (BrE)
Not having received a punishment for an offence or wrongdoing.

Examples

Somehow more terrible than the certainty that he was about to die was the knowledge that Lord would probably not suffer for it: the murder would go unpunished.

Synset Examples

would he forget the crime and let it go unpunished?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punished - having received a punishment

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'punished' (past participle of 'punish').
