tailor-make

1. VERB

/ ˈteɪlərˌmeɪk / (AmE), / ˈteɪləˌmeɪk / (BrE)
to make or create something, especially clothing, to fit the specific requirements of an individual or purpose

Examples

The director helped tailor-make a check list of the district manager's errors by asking various observers to write out sentences commenting on the mistakes they felt he made.

Synset Examples

Can the seamstress sew me a suit by next week?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom-make - to make to fit the specific requirements of a customer

Etymology

Formed from 'tailor' and 'make', indicating made by a tailor (custom-made).
