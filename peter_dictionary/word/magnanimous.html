magnanimous

1. ADJ

/ mæɡˈnænɪməs / (AmE), / mæɡˈnænɪməs / (BrE)
generous and forgiving, especially toward a rival or less powerful person; showing nobility of mind and spirit.

Examples

The colossal prick even managed to sound magnanimous.

Synset Examples

a heart big enough to hold no grudges
that's very big of you to be so forgiving
a large and generous spirit
a large heart
magnanimous toward his enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something, especially money or time, than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Antonym: mean - unwilling to give or share things, especially money; not generous

Etymology

From Latin magnanimus, from magnus (great) + animus (spirit, mind).
