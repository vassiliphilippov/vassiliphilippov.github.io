unmated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal or organism) not having formed a mating pair or copulated sexually.

Examples

It is on them alone that the future of their race depends, for all their relatives (mothers, husbands, brothers, and unmated sisters) have perished with the arrival of the cold weather.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mated - (of an animal or organism) having formed a mating pair or copulated sexually

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'mated', past participle of 'mate'.
