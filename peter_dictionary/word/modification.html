modification

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a changed or slightly altered version of something; an adjustment or adaptation that is not a completely new creation

Examples

The instrument used for this work was a slight modification of that previously described.

Synset Examples

a modification of last year's model

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variant - something that differs in some way from others of the same kind
  • Synonym: adaptation - a changed form or version of something, often for a new use

Etymology

From Latin modificatio, from modificare (to limit, prescribe).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑd.ə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒd.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of changing something, usually to improve it or make it more suitable for a particular purpose

Examples

Modifications of the last technique have been applied by several groups of investigators.
A more detailed description of dictionary operations - text lookup and dictionary modification - give a clearer picture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alteration - the act of making changes to something
  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to suit new conditions
  • Synonym: change - the process of becoming different

Etymology

From Latin modificatio, from modificare (to limit, restrain, modify).
