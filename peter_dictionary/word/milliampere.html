milliampere

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪliˈæmpɛr / (AmE), / ˌmɪliˈæmpɪə / (BrE)
A unit of electric current equal to one thousandth of an ampere.

Examples

Five milliamperes / cell were applied for 18 hr, after which the strips were stained with bromophenol blue and densitometry was carried out using a Spinco Analytrol.
Continuous low-level lighting may be provided in the shelter by means of a 4 - cell hot-shot battery to which is wired a 150 - milliampere flashlight type bulb.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ampere - the base unit of electric current in the International System of Units

Etymology

From 'milli-' (Latin, meaning 'thousandth') and 'ampere', named after French physicist André-Marie Ampère.
