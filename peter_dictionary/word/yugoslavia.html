Yugoslavia

1. PROPN

/ juˌɡoʊˈslɑviə / (AmE), / juːˌɡɒˈslɑːviə / (BrE)
A former country in southeastern Europe, originally composed of six republics, which underwent several changes in borders and names throughout the 20th century before dissolving in the 1990s.

Examples

He was brigadier of the club's locker-room light infantry, and at one time or another declared war on Russia, Czechoslovakia, Yugoslavia, and China.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Serbia - country in southeast Europe, once part of Yugoslavia
  • Degree Lower: Montenegro - country in southeast Europe, once part of Yugoslavia

Etymology

From Serbo-Croatian *Jugoslavija*, meaning 'Land of the South Slavs' (*jug* 'south' + *Slavija* 'Slavia').
