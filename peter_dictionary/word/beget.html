beget

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɛt / (BrE)
(Of a man) to become the father of a child; to father or sire (offspring), especially in biblical or literary use.

Examples

Instead of Catholics' being obliged or even encouraged to beget the greatest possible number of offspring, as many non-Catholics imagine, the ideal of responsible parenthood is stressed.
Of his own will he has begotten us by the word of truth.

Synset Examples

Abraham begot Isaac
Men often father children but don't recognize them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: father - to become the male parent of a child
  • Antonym: mother - to become the female parent of a child

Etymology

From Old English *begietan* (to get, acquire), related to 'get.'
