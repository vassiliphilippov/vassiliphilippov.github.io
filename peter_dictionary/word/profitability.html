profitability

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑfətəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌprɒfɪtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of yielding a financial gain, benefit, or profit; the degree to which an activity or enterprise generates income beyond its costs.

Examples

Fear of the competition - always a great motivating force in the American economy - makes retailers who do not have suburban operations exaggerate both the volume and the profitability of their rival's shiny new branches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unprofitability - the quality of not yielding profit or gain
  • Synonym: profitableness - the quality of being profitable; similar in meaning to profitability

Etymology

From 'profitable' + '-ity', formed after French 'profitabilité', tracing ultimately to Latin 'profĭtābilis'.
