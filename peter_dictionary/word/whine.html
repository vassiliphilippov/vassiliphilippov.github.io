whine

1. VERB

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
(of an object or projectile) to move while making a high-pitched, complaining or buzzing sound.

Examples

When it was followed by a second, whining even closer, Cobb swerved sharply aside into a depression.

Synset Examples

The bullets were whining past us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistle - to move making a high, clear, musical sound
  • Synonym: buzz - move with a buzzing sound

Etymology

From Old English hwīnan (to whistle, whiz, rush), imitative in origin.

2. VERB

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
to complain or talk in a high-pitched, tearful, or feeble manner, especially in a way that is annoying or suggests self-pity

Examples

Wires whined as a cold November blast rocked the silver wings, but the engine roar was reassuring to the pilot bundled in the open cockpit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • Synonym: moan - to complain or grumble, often in a prolonged manner

Etymology

Old English *hwīnan* (to whistle, whiz, or whine); related to Old Norse *hvīna* (to whiz).
