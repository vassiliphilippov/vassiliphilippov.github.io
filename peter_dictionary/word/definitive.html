definitive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɪnətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪnətɪv / (BrE)
clearly defined or formulated; serving as a fixed, final, or authoritative example or version.

Examples

Though it is not easy to apply the evidence of the Iliad to any specific era, this marvelous product of the epic tradition had certainly taken definitive shape by 750.
the success of Communism's war against the West does not depend on such spectacular and definitive conquests.

Synset Examples

the plain and unequivocal language of the laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: final - coming at the end; last in a series
  • Antonym: indefinite - not clearly defined or determined; vague

Etymology

From Latin 'definitivus', from 'definire' (to define).
