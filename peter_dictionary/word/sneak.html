sneak

1. VERB

/ snik / (AmE), / sniːk / (BrE)
to put, bring, or take something somewhere in a secretive or stealthy manner, especially to avoid detection or disapproval

Examples

Warren eased his rifle's safety off and gently, slowly sneaked another clip of ammunition from one of the cloth bandoleers that marked the upper part of his body with an X.

Synset Examples

sneak a look
sneak a cigarette

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggle - to move goods secretly, especially to avoid the law
  • Antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret or hidden

Etymology

Probably from Old English *snīcan* (to creep, crawl).

2. VERB

/ snik / (AmE), / sniːk / (BrE)
to move or go somewhere in a quiet, stealthy, or secret way, especially to avoid being seen or caught

Examples

You should have gone to work today,' stead of sneaking around spying on the Dronk house ".
The cops caught him sneaking TV sets out the back door of a JC Penney.

Synset Examples

..stead of sneaking around spying on the neighbor's house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully, especially to avoid being heard or noticed
  • Synonym: slip - to go somewhere quietly or without being noticed
  • Synonym: stalk - to move silently or cautiously, often when hunting or observing
  • Antonym: march - to walk in a deliberate, regular manner, as a soldier

Etymology

Perhaps of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect snike (to creep).
