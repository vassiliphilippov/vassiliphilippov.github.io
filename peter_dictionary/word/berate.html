berate

1. VERB

/ bəˈreɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈreɪt / (BrE)
to scold or criticize someone angrily and at length

Examples

All of them were there in this loosely knit association of diversified ideologies, and each berated the other and beat his breast for his own approaches.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to rebuke or criticize angrily
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin *beratare*, possibly a blend of Old English *be* (thoroughly) and *rate* (to scold).
