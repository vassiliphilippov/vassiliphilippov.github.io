slump

1. NOUN

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
a period of decline or decrease in activity, performance, or quality

Examples

It will forever be a baseball mystery how a team will suddenly start hitting after a distressing slump.
Manager Hemus, eager to end a pitching slump that has brought four losses in the five games on the current home stand, moved Gibson to the Wednesday night starting assignment.

Synset Examples

the team went into a slump
a gradual slack in output
a drop-off in attendance
a falloff in quality

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid growth or increase (opposite of a slump)

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish slump ‘a fall, a slouch’.

2. VERB

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
to sit, lean, or fall heavily and limply, often with a drooping posture due to tiredness or dejection

Examples

He slumped against a log fence rail, then tried to lift himself.
Shayne stepped back to let him slump to the ground, and then dived over him through the open door into Harris who was cursing loudly and trying to drag a gun from a shoulder holster, somewhat impeded by the steering wheel.
As he started to slump over, another warrior swung him onto his horse.
He was riding between two warriors, who held him erect when he started to slump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often due to weakness
  • Antonym: straighten - to become or make straight or erect after bending or slumping

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative or related to earlier 'slump' meaning 'to fall or sink heavily'.

3. VERB

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
to fall, sink, or sit down heavily and suddenly, often because of tiredness or dejection

Examples

He let go of the shirt, and Jess slumped to the floor.

Synset Examples

He slumped onto the couch
My spirits sank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: sink - to go down below the surface or to a lower position

4. VERB

/ slʌmp / (AmE), / slʌmp / (BrE)
to fall suddenly and noticeably in amount, value, or quality; to decrease sharply

Examples

Traffic on that road slumped sharply in January and February, as compared with those same months in 1960.

Synset Examples

The real estate market fell off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • Antonym: rise - to increase or go up in amount, value, or quality

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish dialect slumpa (to fall or come suddenly).
