italicize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈtælɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to print or write (text) in italics, or to change it into italic type or style

Examples

In the written language then can be underlined or italicized to guide the reader here, but much of the time the written language simply depends on the reader's alertness, and a careless reader will have to back up and reread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underline - to draw a line under text, often for emphasis
  • Antonym: romanize - to print or set in roman (upright) type

Etymology

From 'italic' + '-ize', meaning to make text italic.
