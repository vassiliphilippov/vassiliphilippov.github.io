pit

1. NOUN

/ pɪt / (AmE), / pɪt / (BrE)
A large hole in the ground, especially one made by digging.

Examples

Somewhere, beyond, where shadows lurked, must be the yawning pit of which Papa preached and the dreadful Lake of Fire.
What in the name of God was he doing, crouched in a timbered pit on the wrong bank of the river?

Synset Examples

they dug a pit to bury the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hole - an opening or hollow space in something solid or a surface

Etymology

Old English *pyt*, from Latin *puteus* (well, pit).

2. VERB

/ pɪt / (AmE), / pɪt / (BrE)
to set someone or something in competition or conflict with another

Examples

Individual human strength is needed to pit against an inhuman condition.

Synset Examples

let them match their best athletes against ours
pit a chess player against the Russian champion
He plays his two children off against each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - to set in competition against; to pair for rivalry
  • Synonym: oppose - to place in conflict or contrast with
