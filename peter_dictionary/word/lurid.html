lurid

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʊr.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈljʊə.rɪd / (BrE)
Unpleasantly bright in colour or overly vivid; especially marked by sensationalism or shocking details.

Examples

During much of the fifteen mile ride they had watched a lurid display of lightning in the sky to the east.

Synset Examples

lurid details of the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensational - intended to arouse strong interest or excitement, often in a lurid way
  • Antonym: subdued - not intense; restrained or softened in tone

Etymology

From Latin *lūridus* (pale yellow, ghastly).
