thrill

1. NOUN

/ θrɪl / (AmE), / θrɪl / (BrE)
a sudden strong feeling of excitement or pleasure

Examples

The crowd at the twenty-first annual K. of C. Games, final indoor meet of the season, got a thrill a few minutes earlier when a slender, bespectacled woman broke the one week old world record in the half-mile run.
It was a thrill he felt no part in.

Synset Examples

they got a great bang out of it
what a boot!
he got a quick rush from injecting heroin
he does it for kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or stimulant effect
  • Synonym: kick - an enjoyable or exciting experience
  • Antonym: bore - a feeling of dullness or lack of excitement (the opposite state)

Etymology

From Middle English 'thrillen' (to pierce), from Old English 'þyrlian' (to pierce, perforate).

2. VERB

/ θrɪl / (AmE), / θrɪl / (BrE)
to cause someone to experience a sudden intense feeling of excitement or pleasure

Examples

There will be thousands who will thrill to the loveliness of Alastor.

Synset Examples

The men were thrilled by a loud whistle blow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excite - to cause strong feelings of enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel weary and uninterested

Etymology

From Middle English *thrillen* (to pierce), from Old English *þyrlian* (to pierce, to perforate).

3. NOUN

/ θrɪl / (AmE), / θrɪl / (BrE)
A sudden feeling of excitement and pleasure, sometimes accompanied by a slight sense of fear.

Examples

It had been shut again, but the lock was broken; he noted with a thrill of fear that the door moved under his touch.

Synset Examples

a frisson of surprise shot through him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frisson - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or fear; a shiver or thrill
  • Degree Lower: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger or harm
  • Degree Higher: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness or joyful excitement

Etymology

From Middle English 'thrillen', from Old English 'þyrlian' (to pierce, penetrate).

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
