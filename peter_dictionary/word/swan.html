swan

1. NOUN

/ swɑn / (AmE), / swɒn / (BrE)
A large, stately waterbird with a long neck, broad wings, and usually white plumage as an adult.

Examples

An elephant or a fox or a swan or a cocopalm or a banana possess in unusually high degree this quality of obvious, common-sense, indubitable identity, as do an eye or tooth or nail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose - a large waterbird related to the swan

Etymology

Old English swan, from Proto-Germanic *swanaz.
