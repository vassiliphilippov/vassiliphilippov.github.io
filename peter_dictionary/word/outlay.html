outlay

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.leɪ / (BrE)
an amount of money spent on a particular purpose; the act of spending money on something

Examples

Operating budget for the day schools in the five counties of Dallas, Harris, Bexar, Tarrant and El Paso would be $ 451500, which would be a savings of $ 157460 yearly after the first year's capital outlay of $ 88000 was absorbed, Parkhouse told the Senate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - an amount of money spent; the act of spending money
  • Synonym: disbursement - the payment of money from a fund

Etymology

From out + lay, originally meaning to lay out (money).
