pew

1. NOUN

/ pjuː / (AmE), / pjuː / (BrE)
A long bench with a back, used for seating members of a congregation in a church.

Examples

At the end of a shaft of light, the pews appeared to be broad stairs in a long dungeon.

Etymology

From Old French *puie* (balcony) and Middle English *pewe* (raised, enclosed seat), possibly of Germanic origin.
