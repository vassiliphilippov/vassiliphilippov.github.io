lapse

1. VERB

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
to return to a previous, typically less desirable, state or behavior, especially after improvement

Examples

The fruit is then cooled to 42 ` F., a temperature at which it lapses into a sort of dormant state.

Synset Examples

Those who recidivate are often minor criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially a bad habit or illness

Etymology

From Latin 'lapsus' (a slip or fall), past participle of 'labi' (to slip).

2. NOUN

/ læps / (AmE), / læps / (BrE)
a temporary failure of concentration, memory, or judgement; a mistake resulting from inattention

Examples

It will rise during lapse conditions.
There was a slight lapse condition, a moderate fog, and 100 per cent relative humidity.
Philip Spencer had cold-bloodedly planned the murder of his captain, yet it seemed in order to chide him for a lapse of proper address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgement that is wrong or incorrect
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake

Etymology

From Latin lapsus (a slip, fall, or error), from labi (to slip).

3. VERB

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
to fall or slip to a lower level, especially in morals, standards, or quality

Examples

The content of his words may lapse back into monotonous repetition, as if a phonograph needle were stuck in one groove; only seldom is it so simple as to be a matter of his obviously parroting some timeworn axiom, common to our culture, which he has evidently heard, over and over, from a parent until he experiences it as part of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially of illness or bad habits

Etymology

From Latin 'lapsare' (to slip or fall), frequentative of 'labi' (to slip).

4. NOUN

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
a temporary failure to maintain a standard of behavior or a higher state

Examples

"Mr. Hohlbein and I have noticed some lapses since, though.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - a return to a former bad habit or condition after improvement
  • Synonym: error - a mistake or incorrect action

Etymology

From Latin lapsus (a slip or fall), past participle of labi (to slip).

5. VERB

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
(of a right, privilege, activity, etc.) to cease to be effective, valid, or in force, especially by passage of time or through neglect.

Examples

Many of them had once been members of a church or at least had been given instruction in Christianity but for one or another reason had allowed the connexion to lapse.

Synset Examples

The correspondence lapsed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expire - to come to an end, especially by passage of time (of rights, contracts, etc.)
  • Antonym: continue - to persist, remain in force or effect

Etymology

From Latin lapsus ('a slipping, fall'), past participle of labi ('to slip, glide').

6. VERB

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
to gradually pass or slip into a particular state or condition, especially a less desirable one

Examples

Having arrived he blinked sleepily and friendlily at them, and lapsed back into the restful stupor from which he had emerged.

Synset Examples

He sank into nirvana

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially a worse one after improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'lapsus' (a slipping, falling), past participle of 'labi' (to slip, fall).
