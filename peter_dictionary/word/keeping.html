keeping

1. NOUN

/ ˈkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
responsibility for the care or protection of someone or something; guardianship.

Examples

He is a trustee for the common good, however feeble the safeguards which the positive or municipal law of property provides against his misuse of that share of the common fund, wisely or unwisely, entrusted to his keeping.

Synset Examples

he left his car in my keeping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custody - protective care or guardianship of someone or something
  • Synonym: guardianship - the position of being legally responsible for someone

Etymology

From Middle English keping, from keep + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the condition of being consistent or in harmony with something, especially as used in the phrase 'in keeping with'

Examples

The careless writing was in keeping with his mood of savage discontent.

Synset Examples

his behavior was not in keeping with the occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accordance - conformity or agreement with a rule or request
  • Synonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion or feeling
  • Antonym: discrepancy - a lack of compatibility or similarity

Etymology

From verb 'keep' + -ing, originally meaning 'guarding, care'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of holding, retaining, or maintaining something.

Examples

And behind every shady deal, behind every dollar earned, there was Andy, keeping the books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retention - the continued possession, use, or control of something

Etymology

From Middle English keping, gerund of keep.
