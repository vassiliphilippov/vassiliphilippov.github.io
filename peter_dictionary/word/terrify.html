terrify

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛr.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
To fill someone with intense fear; to frighten greatly.

Examples

When the automobile was in its embryonic stage, such roads as existed were pretty much open roads with the tacit understanding that horses should not be unduly terrified being about the only rule governing where, when and how fast a car could go.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frighten - to make someone afraid or anxious
  • Synonym: scare - to cause sudden fear or alarm
  • Degree Higher: petrify - to paralyze with extreme fear
  • Degree Lower: alarm - to make someone feel worried or afraid but less intense than terrify

Etymology

From Latin 'terrificare', from 'terrore' (terror) + '-ficare' (to make).
