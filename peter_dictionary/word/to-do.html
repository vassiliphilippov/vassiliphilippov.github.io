to-do

1. NOUN

/ təˈdu / (AmE), / təˈduː / (BrE)
A state of agitation, commotion, or fuss, usually over something unimportant.

Examples

- Very nice, sir. - Big charity to-do up Portland way.

Synset Examples

they were amazed by the furious disturbance they had caused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuss - needless or excessive commotion
  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance

Etymology

From the phrase 'to do', meaning a matter requiring attention or action.
