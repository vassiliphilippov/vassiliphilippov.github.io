censure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɛnʃə / (BrE)
the expression of strong disapproval or criticism, especially in a formal statement

Examples

Our Northern brethren also I believe felt a little tender under those censures; for though their people had very few slaves themselves, yet they had been pretty considerable carriers of them to others ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - a sharp criticism or reprimand
  • Antonym: commendation - praise or formal approval

Etymology

From Latin 'censura' (judgment, oversight), from 'censere' (to assess, judge).
