hover

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒvə / (BrE)
to remain in one place in the air, or to stay very close to someone or something, often moving slightly back and forth or showing anxiety or protectiveness

Examples

She would hover over him and, looking like her brother, anxiously watch the progress of Scotty's fork or spoon.
He hovered over her to shield her, for spent bullets were thudding against the rear walls.

Synset Examples

The shy student lingered in the corner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linger - to stay in one place longer than necessary, often because of reluctance to leave

Etymology

From Middle English 'hoveren', frequentative of 'hovien' (to hover, linger).

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌvər / (AmE), / ˈhɒvə / (BrE)
to remain suspended in the air in one place, as if defying gravity

Examples

In my recollection, there was a long interval between the death of the officer and the appearance of the first of the retreating redcoats, and in that interval the dust cloud over the road seems to hover indefinitely.

Synset Examples

The guru claimed that he could levitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levitate - to rise or float in the air, especially by supernatural means

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'hoveren', frequentative of 'hove' (to tarry, linger).

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
To be undecided or waver between different actions, choices, or positions.

Examples

"The pattern ", says Dr. Morton Schillinger, psychologist at New York's Lincoln Institute for Psychotherapy, "is for the husband to hover about anxiously and eagerly, virtually trembling in his hope that she will flash him the signal that tonight is the night ".
Didi and Gogo hover on the edge of suicide; Hamm's world is death and Clov may or may not get out of it to join the living child outside.

Synset Examples

He oscillates between accepting the new position and retirement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillate - to swing between two points or opinions; to waver
  • Synonym: waver - to be undecided between two opinions or courses of action
  • Antonym: decide - to come to a resolution in the mind as a result of consideration

Etymology

From Middle English 'hoveren', frequentative of 'hove' (to hover, float), origin uncertain.

4. VERB

/ ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
to remain suspended or floating in the air in one place; to stay in the same position above something

Examples

When I fell on my back, I saw a vulture hovering.
And there is also something intangible that hovers around the table.

Etymology

From Middle English 'hoveren', frequentative of 'hove' (to remain poised), of uncertain origin.
