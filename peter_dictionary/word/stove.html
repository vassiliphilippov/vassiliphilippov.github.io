stove

1. NOUN

/ stoʊv / (AmE), / stəʊv / (BrE)
A device or apparatus for heating, typically burning fuel or using electricity, found in buildings to warm rooms or spaces.

Examples

Seated on the platform were Amos C. Barstow, ex-mayor of Providence and a wealthy Republican stove manufacturer; Thomas Davis, an uncompromising Garrisonian; the Reverend Augustus Woodbury, a Unitarian minister; the Reverend George T. Day, a Free-Will Baptist; Daniel w. Vaughan, and William H. H. Clements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heater - a device for warming the air or water
  • Synonym: oven - enclosed compartment for heating or baking food; distinct but related appliance
  • Degree Higher: furnace - a large apparatus for heating a building

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'stoof' meaning heated room, via Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ stoʊv / (AmE), / stəʊv / (BrE)
A kitchen appliance used for cooking food, typically consisting of one or more burners or heating elements and often an oven.

Examples

She was intimidated by the stove.
Every few minutes she would awaken for a moment to review things: Stowey, yes, was on his way south, and the two boys were away in school, and nothing was burning on the stove, and Lucretia was coming for dinner and bringing three guests of hers.
Moving around camp we saw all kinds of camp stoves, lanterns, coolers, bedding, games, fishing tackle, windbreaks and sunshades.
He opened the inner door; the cooking odors were stronger - all over the city, at this hour, housewives would be fussing over stoves.

Synset Examples

dinner was already on the stove

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oven - enclosed compartment for baking or roasting food

Etymology

From Middle English 'stove', meaning heated room, from Old French, of Germanic origin (cf. Middle Low German 'stove' meaning heated room).
