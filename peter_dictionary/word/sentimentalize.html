sentimentalize

1. VERB

/ ˌsɛntəməntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to view or present something in a sentimental or emotional manner, often excessively so, sometimes making it seem more appealing or nostalgic than it is in reality

Examples

Although Faulkner was the heir in his own family to this tradition, he did not have Stark Young's inclination to romanticize and sentimentalize the planter society.

Synset Examples

Don't sentimentalize the past events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticize - to view or present something in a romantic or idealized way

Etymology

From sentimental + -ize, modeled after French 'sentimentaliser'.
