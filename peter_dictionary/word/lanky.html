lanky

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋki / (AmE), / ˈlaŋki / (BrE)
(of a person) ungracefully thin and tall.

Examples

Nikolai Cherkasov, the Russian actor who has played such heroic roles as Alexander Nevsky and Ivan the Terrible, performs the lanky Don Quixote, and does so with a simple dignity that bridges the inner nobility and the surface absurdity of this poignant man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangly - awkwardly tall and thin
  • Degree Lower: slender - gracefully thin; slim

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæŋki / (AmE), / ˈlæŋki / (BrE)
(of a person) ungracefully tall and thin, with long slender limbs.

Examples

She was forty-nine at this time, a lanky woman of breeding with an austere, narrow face which had the distinction of a steeple or some architecture that had been designed long ago for a stubborn sort of prayer.

Synset Examples

a gangling teenager
a lanky kid transformed almost overnight into a handsome young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangly - tall, thin, and awkward in movement or posture
  • Antonym: stocky - short and broad or sturdily built

Etymology

Probably from dialectal lank (thin, slender) + -y.
