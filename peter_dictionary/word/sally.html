sally

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæli / (AmE), / ˈsæli / (BrE)
A sudden or witty remark; a quip or wisecrack.

Examples

"Well, Mrs. Charles - Sally - has phoned too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisecrack - a witty or clever remark
  • Synonym: quip - a clever or witty remark

Etymology

From Old French 'saillie' (a rush, a sally), from 'sauter' (to leap), from Latin 'salīre' (to leap).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæli / (AmE), / ˈsæli / (BrE)
an excursion or venture into unfamiliar or new territory, especially as a departure from routine or safety

Examples

Then, with the new affluence, there is actually a sallying forth into the wide, wide world beyond the precincts of New York.

Synset Examples

a sally into the wide world beyond his home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foray - a sudden or brief attempt or venture, especially into new territory
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a particular purpose, especially into the unknown

Etymology

From Old French *saillie* (a rush, an attack), from *sailir* (to leap), from Latin *salīre* (to leap, jump).
