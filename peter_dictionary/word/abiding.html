abiding

1. ADJ

/ əˈbaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈbaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a feeling or memory) lasting a long time; enduring.

Examples

As for the ladies, they were full of charm, and sincerity, and deep and abiding affection for this hurrying driving, honest, little man.
While it is easy enough to ridicule Hawkins' pronouncement in Pleas of the Crown from a metaphysical point of view, the concept of the "oneness "of a married couple may reflect an abiding belief that the communion between husband and wife is such that their actions are not always to be regarded by the criminal law as if there were no marriage.

Synset Examples

an abiding belief
imperishable truths

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fleeting - lasting for a very short time

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'abide', from Old English 'abīdan'.
