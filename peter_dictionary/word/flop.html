flop

1. VERB

/ flɑp / (AmE), / flɒp / (BrE)
to fall, drop, or move in a loose, uncontrolled, or heavy way

Examples

His head flopped back.
Seems like she's willing, but the male just flops around all day like the bashful boy who took Jeannie May behind the barn and then did n't know what to do, and the people at the zoo have n't got any vulture chicks to show for their trouble.
The straight, black hair flopped in a vigorous nod, the slender nose plunged toward glass teeth and drew safely back.
Quickly but carefully lowering my duffel bag over the low side rack, I stepped on the running board; it flopped down, sprang back up and gouged my shin.

Synset Examples

He flopped into a chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plop - to fall or drop with a soft, splashing sound
  • Synonym: drop - to fall vertically; to let something fall

Etymology

Probably imitative of a sudden soft sound; related to 'flip' and 'flap'.

2. VERB

/ flɑp / (AmE), / flɒp / (BrE)
to fall or drop suddenly, often in an uncontrolled or heavy way

Examples

McBride staggered into the street, flopped sprawling in the stinging dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in suddenly
  • Synonym: plop - to fall or drop with a soft sound

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly of imitative origin, echoing the sound of something falling
