deflated

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfleɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪtɪd / (BrE)
Feeling less confident or enthusiastic; having one's spirit or morale brought low.

Examples

"One of the things which makes our lives drab and empty and which leaves us, at the end of the day, fatigued and deflated spiritually is the pressure of the taxing, practical, utilitarian concern of common-sense objects.

Synset Examples

left us fatigued and deflated spiritually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discouraged - having lost confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: elated - in high spirits; extremely happy or proud

Etymology

From the verb 'deflate', from Latin 'deflare' (to blow away).
