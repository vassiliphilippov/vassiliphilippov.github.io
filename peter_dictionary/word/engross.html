engross

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊs / (BrE)
to absorb all the attention or interest of someone; to occupy fully

Examples

Unconcerned with the practical function of his actions, the dancer is engrossed exclusively in their "motional content ".

Synset Examples

He immersed himself into his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorb - to take in or engage completely (of attention, interest, etc.)
  • Antonym: distract - to divert (attention) from something

Etymology

From Middle English 'engrossen', from Anglo-Norman 'engrosser', from Old French 'en-' (in) + 'gros' (large, wholesale). Originally referred to buying up wholesale, later to occupying fully.
