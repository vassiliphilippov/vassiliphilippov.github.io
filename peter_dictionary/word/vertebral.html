vertebral

1. ADJ

/ vərˈtiːbrəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.brəl / (BrE)
Relating to or consisting of the vertebrae, the series of small bones forming the backbone.

Examples

The bone of the vertebral bodies, ribs, and sternum was soft and was easily compressed.
The marrow of the vertebral bodies was pale and showed areas of fatty replacement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal - relating to the spine or backbone

Etymology

From New Latin vertebralis, from Latin vertebra (joint, backbone).
