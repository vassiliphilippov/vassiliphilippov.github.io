reclassify

1. VERB

/ riˈklæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / riˈklɑːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to assign (someone or something) to a different category or class; to classify again.

Examples

In a periodic review, the local board on July 30, 1953, reclassified him 1, - A and upheld this classification after a personal appearance by petitioner, because of his willingness to kill in defense of his church and home.
He was again reclassified 1, - A by the local board.

Synset Examples

The zoologists had to reclassify the mollusks after they found new species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classify - to arrange in classes or categories

Etymology

From re- (again) + classify (from French classifier, from classe 'class' + -fier 'to make').
