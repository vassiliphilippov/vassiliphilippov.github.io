Crown

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn / (AmE), / kraʊn / (BrE)
(in the UK and other monarchies) the reigning monarch or the monarchy as a symbol of state power and authority.

Examples

And no doubt many people in states like the Carolinas and Georgia, which were among the most Tory in sentiment in the eighteenth century, bitterly regretted the revolt against the Crown.

Synset Examples

the colonies revolted against the Crown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throne - the position and power of a monarch

Etymology

From Old French 'corone', from Latin 'corona' (garland, wreath, crown).
