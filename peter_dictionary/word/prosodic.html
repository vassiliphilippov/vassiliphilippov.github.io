prosodic

1. ADJ

/ prəˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / prəˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to the rhythm, stress, and intonation patterns of spoken language, or to features such as pitch, stress, and juncture in speech.

Examples

But it would seem more intended as a tract advocating the prosodic theory than a paper directed to the specific problems of Igbo phonology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosody - the patterns of rhythm and sound in poetry or speech

Etymology

From Greek 'prosōidikos', from 'prosōidia' meaning 'song sung to music, accent'.
