Janus-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪnəs feɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪnəs feɪst / (BrE)
Having two contrasting or contradictory aspects; characterized by duality or ambiguity.

Examples

The era, however, is Janus-faced.

Synset Examples

a Janus-faced view of history
a Janus-faced policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-faced - hypocritical or deceitful; having two different aspects

Etymology

From Janus, the Roman god depicted with two faces, symbolizing duality.
