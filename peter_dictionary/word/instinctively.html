instinctively

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈstɪŋktɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɪŋktɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that is based on instinct rather than conscious thought; done automatically or naturally.

Examples

The cowboy films, the cops and robbers films, and the slapstick comedy films culminating in an insane chase are not only catering to what critics may assume to be a vulgar taste for violence; these films and these sequences are also seeking out - instinctively or by design - the peculiarly cinematic elements of narrative.

Synset Examples

he instinctively grabbed the knife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatically - without conscious thought or intention
  • Antonym: deliberately - in a way that is done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From 'instinctive', which derives from Latin 'instinctus' (impulse, instinct) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
