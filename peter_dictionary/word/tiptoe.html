tiptoe

1. ADV

/ ˈtɪpˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɪp.təʊ / (BrE)
In a manner using the balls of the feet and raising the heels, especially in order to move quietly or cautiously.

Examples

They waited three minutes and then crept out on tip-toe; the halls were empty and they passed down the stairs to number nine and listened at the door.

Synset Examples

standing tiptoe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stealthily - in a stealthy or sneaky manner

Etymology

From Middle English tipto (with 'tip' meaning small point and 'toe').

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪpˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɪptəʊ / (BrE)
to walk quietly and carefully on the balls of one's feet, especially to avoid making noise

Examples

The girls seemed to spend half their time tiptoeing in "to see if Mike was all right "and they were too preoccupied to cook, much less be secretaries.

Etymology

From the noun 'tiptoe', from Middle English 'tippe' (tip) and 'toe'.
