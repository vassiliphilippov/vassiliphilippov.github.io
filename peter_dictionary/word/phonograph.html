phonograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an early machine for reproducing sound from records, in which a stylus tracks grooves on a rotating disc, and the vibrations are amplified acoustically or electronically

Examples

The phonograph today, for all its high fidelity and stereophonic sound, is precisely what the early art purveyors in the movies wished to make of the camera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record player - a device for playing sound recordings; mostly used for modern varieties
  • Synonym: gramophone - a British term for a device similar to a phonograph

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) + -graph (something written or drawn), referring to recording of sound.
