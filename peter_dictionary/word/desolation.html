desolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of complete emptiness, decay, or destruction, especially as a result of abandonment or disaster.

Examples

He finds it in utter misery and desolation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - The act of completely destroying or laying waste.
  • Synonym: ruin - A state of complete destruction or disrepair.
  • Antonym: prosperity - The state of being successful or flourishing, especially economically.

Etymology

From Old French *desolacion*, from Latin *desolatio* ('loneliness, abandonment').
