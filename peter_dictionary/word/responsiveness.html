responsiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈspɑnsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of reacting quickly and positively to a stimulus, suggestion, or need

Examples

Moreover, the responsiveness of the hypothalamus to nociceptive stimulation is greatly increased under these conditions.
It seems to follow that by and large an antagonism exists between the paleo - and the neocortex as far as emotional reactivity is concerned, and that the balance between the two systems determines the emotional responsiveness of the organism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsivity - the degree to which something responds to a stimulus (especially in science/engineering)
  • Antonym: unresponsiveness - lack of reaction or response to stimulus
  • Synonym: sensitivity - the capacity to respond to physical or emotional stimuli

Etymology

From 'responsive' + '-ness', both from Latin roots via Old French and Middle English.
