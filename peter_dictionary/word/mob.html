mob

1. NOUN

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
a large, disorderly crowd of people, especially one that may become violent or unruly

Examples

A British writer, Richard Haestier, in a book, Dead Men Tell Tales, recalls that in the turmoil preceding French Revolution the body of Henry /4,, who had died nearly 180 years earlier, was torn to pieces by a mob.
In the audience a man named Ferguson lost his head and tried to rescue a little girl from the mob, on the same principle which had led Miss Harris to demand water.
Somehow Dr. Charles Leale was forced through the mob and squeezed out into the dingy corridor.
El Dorado was surrounded by a mob.
However, it was virtually impossible to screen the mob outside, even if Bonner had manpower available for the purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together, typically in a disorganized or random way
  • Synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people or animals
  • Synonym: rabble - a disorderly or noisy crowd; the lowest class of people

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the movable common people), later shortened in English slang.

2. NOUN

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
an organized group of criminals, especially one involved in illegal activities such as gambling, extortion, or trafficking; the Mafia or similar criminal syndicate

Examples

He was also at this time, although not so interwoven in high politics and the rackets as Torrio and Capone, the most powerful and most dangerous mob leader in the Chicago underworld, the roughneck king.
What about the theory that the Batman is a Mob enforcer?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mafia - an organized international body of criminals operating originally in Sicily and now especially in Italy and the US

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the movable common people), originally referring to a disorderly crowd; later developed to mean a criminal organization.

3. NOUN

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
A group of people involved in organized crime; a criminal gang.

Examples

Our police have rounded up all of the Joker's men.

Synset Examples

police tried to break up the gang
a pack of thieves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gang - an organized group of criminals
  • Synonym: syndicate - an association of people to promote a common interest, often criminal

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the fickle crowd), shortened to 'mob' in late 17th century English; later used for criminal gangs in American English.
