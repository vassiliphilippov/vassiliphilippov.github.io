overestimation

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˌɛstəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˌɛstɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
An assessment or calculation that rates something as greater or more important than it actually is.

Examples

It is often stated that the largest snakes require five years to attain maturity, but this apparently is an overestimation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimation - an assessment that is too low
  • Synonym: overrate - to assign too high a value or importance to something

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'estimation', from Latin 'aestimatio' (valuation, assessment).
