fluorine

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʊrˌin / (AmE), / ˈflɔːrɪn / (BrE)
A nonmetallic chemical element (symbol F), a poisonous pale yellow gas that is extremely reactive, belonging to the halogen group.

Examples

Another recent achievement was the successful development of a method for the complete combustion in a bomb calorimeter of a metal in fluorine when the product is relatively non-volatile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a similar halogen element, a greenish-yellow gas (Cl)

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow), referring to the mineral fluorite from which it was first isolated.
