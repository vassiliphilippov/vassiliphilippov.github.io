ROTC

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr oʊ ti si / (AmE), / ɑːr əʊ tiː siː / (BrE)
(in the US) a college program that trains students to become commissioned officers in the armed forces, typically while completing their undergraduate degree.

Examples

The troop received a new leader, Lieutenant Robert M. Carroll, fresh out of ROTC and bucking for Regular Army status.

Etymology

Acronym for Reserve Officers' Training Corps, established in the United States.
