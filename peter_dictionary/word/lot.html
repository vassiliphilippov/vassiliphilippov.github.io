lot

1. NOUN

/ lɑt / (AmE), / lɒt / (BrE)
a person's overall circumstances or condition in life, including everything that happens to them

Examples

For Larkin is already wonderfully contented with his lot.
At Fort Garry some of the Swiss also decided to cast their lot with the United States, and in 1823 several families paid guides to take them to Fort Snelling.
Debate is not likely to resolve the tensions and make the lot of the stepchild a happier one.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events outside a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external, arbitrary force affecting human affairs
  • Synonym: destiny - the events that will necessarily happen to a particular person or thing in the future

Etymology

From Old English 'hlot' (object used to determine someone's share), via Germanic origins.

3. NOUN
