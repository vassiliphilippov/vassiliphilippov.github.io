predomination

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛdəˌmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːdəˌmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or condition of being predominant over others; having greater power, influence, or authority

Examples

The predomination of free swimming ciliated protozoa is indicative of a high bacterial population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predominance - the state of being greater in number or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'praedominari' meaning 'to have greater power' with the suffix '-tion' indicating a state or condition.
