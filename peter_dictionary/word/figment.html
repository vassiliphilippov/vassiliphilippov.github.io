figment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡmənt / (BrE)
something invented, imagined, or made up and not real

Examples

If they ever trace any of those accounts, they're going to wind up chasing a figment of my imagination.

Synset Examples

a figment of the imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invention - something that has been invented; a creation of the mind
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin 'figmentum' (something formed or invented), from 'fingere' (to form, shape).
