crane

1. VERB

/ kreɪn / (AmE), / kreɪn / (BrE)
to stretch or extend one's neck in order to see something more clearly

Examples

Shayne drove westward from the Boulevard slowly, letting Rourke crane his head out the window and watch for street numbers.

Synset Examples

The women craned their necks to see the President drive by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - look closely or with difficulty

Etymology

From Old English cran (the bird 'crane'), alluding to the bird's long neck.
