cantle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæntəl / (AmE), / ˈkæntəl / (BrE)
the raised back part of a saddle seat, providing support to the rider

Examples

The others put on old coats or ducking jackets, whichever they carried behind their saddle cantles.

Etymology

From Old French *cantel* (corner, piece), from Latin *canthus* (iron tire of a wheel, rim).
