invasion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
An instance of entering a place by force with an army, especially to conquer or plunder.

Examples

Southern resentment has been over the method of its ending, the invasion, and Reconstruction; their fears now are of miscegenation and Negro political control in many counties.
Each failed catastrophically in an invasion of Russia and each brought ruin on the country that worshipped him.
This comes out in "When the Bells Ring ", which describes the rallying of the peasants in southern Sweden to repel an invasion by the Danes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - the act of using force to try to hurt or damage something or someone
  • Antonym: defense - the act of protecting from or resisting attack

Etymology

From Latin invasio, from invadere (to go into, attack).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
An instance of entering or spreading into an area or domain previously unoccupied, often in large numbers or in a way considered intrusive.

Examples

Immediately, the religious groups of the city were embroiled in an angry dispute over the alleged invasion of a man's right to freedom of religious belief and conscience.

Synset Examples

an invasion of tourists
an invasion of locusts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of entering where one is not welcome
  • Synonym: incursion - a sudden entry or invasion into a place
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing from an area or position

Etymology

From Latin 'invadere' (to go into, attack).
