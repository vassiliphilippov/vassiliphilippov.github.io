preoccupied

1. ADJ

/ priˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / priˈɒkjəˌpaɪd / (BrE)
Thinking so much about something that you are not aware of other things around you.

Examples

The girls seemed to spend half their time tiptoeing in "to see if Mike was all right "and they were too preoccupied to cook, much less be secretaries.

Synset Examples

as distant and bemused as a professor listening to the prattling of his freshman class
lost in thought
a preoccupied frown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracted - Unable to concentrate because one's mind is preoccupied
  • Synonym: engrossed - Fully absorbed or occupied by something
  • Antonym: unconcerned - Not interested or worried about something

Etymology

From Latin 'praeoccupatus', past participle of 'praeoccupare' (to seize beforehand).

2. ADJ

/ priˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / priˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
Thinking about something so much that you are not aware of other things; very absorbed or involved in something.

Examples

If we look at recent art we find it preoccupied with form.

Synset Examples

became more and more haunted by the stupid riddle
was absolutely obsessed with the girl
got no help from his wife who was preoccupied with the children
he was taken up in worry for the old woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbed - deeply interested or involved; preoccupied
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because something else is taking one's attention

Etymology

From Latin praecoccupatus, past participle of praecoccupare (to seize beforehand).
