idler

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdlɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪdlə / (BrE)
a person who habitually avoids work or activity; someone who is lazy or inactive

Examples

Some members of the bee family have become idlers, social parasites that live at the expense of their hardworking relatives.
The one sure way to tell victim and villain apart is to examine the hind legs which in the case of the idler, Psithyrus, lack the pollen baskets - naturally!

Synset Examples

a lazy bum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loafer - a person who avoids work and spends their time lazily
  • Antonym: worker - a person who works, especially hard or diligently

Etymology

From idle + -er, dating from Middle English.
