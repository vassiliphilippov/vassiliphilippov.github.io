soothe

1. VERB

/ suð / (AmE), / suːð / (BrE)
To make someone feel calmer, less anxious, or more comforted emotionally.

Examples

Acting soothed him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - to make someone feel better or less sad
  • Antonym: agitate - to make someone feel anxious or disturbed

Etymology

Probably from Old English sōthian ('to verify, prove to be true'); influenced by sooth ('truth').
