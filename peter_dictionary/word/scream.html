scream

1. NOUN

/ skɹim / (AmE), / skɹiːm / (BrE)
A loud, sharp, and piercing cry, often made in fear, pain, or excitement.

Examples

With shout and slow dance, with tears and song, with scream and contortion, the corner group was beset by hysteria and shivering, wailing, shouting, possession of something that seemed like an alien and outside force.
There was no mistaking that scream.
Yet this scream had a different note in it.
He thought of Simms Purdew, who once had risen at the edge of a cornfield, a maniacal scream on his lips, and swung a clubbed musket like a flail to beat down the swirl of Rebel bayonets about him.
She stood up, pulled the coat from her shoulders and started to slide it off, then let out a high-pitched scream and I let out a low-pitched, wobbling sound like a muffler blowing out.

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - a high-pitched, piercing cry
  • Synonym: yell - a loud, sharp cry, especially of pain, surprise, or delight
  • Antonym: whisper - a very soft or quiet speech or sound

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin; related to Middle English 'scremen' and Old Norse 'skrækja' (to shriek).

2. VERB

/ skrim / (AmE), / skriːm / (BrE)
to utter something or declare in a very loud, piercing voice, often from fear, excitement, pain, or strong emotion

Examples

The nigger boy fled down the stairs, screaming, "Murder ".
"You bastard "! the Anniston catcher screamed.

Synset Examples

You don't have to yell--I can hear you just fine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to speak or call out loudly
  • Synonym: yell - to cry out or call loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

Etymology

From Middle English 'scremen', probably from Old Norse 'skræma' (to terrify).

to make a loud, high-pitched cry, especially because of strong emotion such as fear, pain, or excitement

Then they let out a bellow of anguish and rage and, cursing, screaming and hollering "Garryowen "! they charged into the enemy like wild men.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Synonym: yell - to cry out loudly, especially in pain or anger
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very quietly

Etymology

Perhaps imitative in origin; first recorded in Middle English.
