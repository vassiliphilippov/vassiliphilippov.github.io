politic

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑː.lə.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
(of an action or decision) showing prudence, shrewdness, or tact, especially in practical matters; judiciously diplomatic or expedient.

Examples

Congress is full of politicians, and if you want to get along with them, you have to be politic.

Synset Examples

it is neither polite nor politic to get into other people's quarrels
a politic decision
a politic manager
a politic old scoundrel
a shrewd and politic reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - acting with careful judgment and wisdom; cautious and sensible
  • Antonym: imprudent - not showing care for the consequences of an action; rash
  • Synonym: tactful - having or showing skill in dealing with people or difficult situations

Etymology

From Old French politique, from Latin politicus, from Greek politikos (relating to citizens).
