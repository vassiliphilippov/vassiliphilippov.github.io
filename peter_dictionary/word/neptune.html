Neptune

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛp.tjun / (AmE), / ˈnɛp.tjuːn / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the sea, equivalent to the Greek god Poseidon.

Examples

It celebrates the unknown architect who designed the temple of Neptune at Paestum, next to the Parthenon the noblest example of Grecian classic style now in existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poseidon - Greek god of the sea; counterpart to Neptune

Etymology

From Latin 'Neptūnus', name of the Roman sea god.
