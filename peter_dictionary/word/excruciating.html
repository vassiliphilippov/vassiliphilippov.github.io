excruciating

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskruːʃiˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈskruːʃɪˌeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely painful; causing intense physical or mental suffering.

Examples

As we think of the long and excruciating pain it must have suffered, we are very likely to say: "It was a bad thing that the little animal should suffer so ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agonizing - causing great physical or mental pain
  • Degree Lower: painful - causing physical pain
  • Degree Higher: unbearable - impossible to endure

Etymology

From Latin excruciatus, past participle of excruciare (to torment, to torture).
