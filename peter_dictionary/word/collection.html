collection

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
A group of items, often of a similar type, that are gathered together and kept as a set.

Examples

This recording surely belongs in everyone's collection.
In the upper center of Braque's first collage, Fruit Dish (in Douglas Cooper's collection), a bunch of grapes is rendered with such conventionally vivid sculptural effect as to lift it practically off the picture plane.
Manthey maintained a valuable collection of physical and chemical apparatus which was at Oersted's disposal during and after his graduation.
Primary feelings of the world come neither as a collection of clearly known objects (houses, trees, implements, etc.) nor a collection of isolated and neutral sensory qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemblage - a collection or gathering of things or people
  • Synonym: selection - a collection of things chosen from a larger number
  • Antonym: dispersal - the act of distributing things over a wide area, opposite of gathering items

Etymology

From Latin collectio (a gathering together), from colligere (to gather together).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
an act or instance of asking people for money, especially for charity or a specific cause.

Examples

At the very end, when the audience was silent and breathless, a collection was taken and then slowly everyone filed out.

Synset Examples

an appeal to raise money for starving children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solicitation - the act of asking someone for money or support, especially formally or persistently

Etymology

From Latin collectio (a gathering together), from colligere (to collect).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a publication that contains a variety of written works, such as stories, poems, or essays, typically by one or more authors

Examples

Rather than from a first-hand study of Jewish people, his delineation of Shylock stems from a collection of Italian stories, Il Pecorone, published in 1558, although written almost two centuries earlier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing

Etymology

From Latin collectio (a gathering together), from colligere (to gather together).

/ kəˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of gathering things together

Examples

Two data centers have been established for the collection, indexing, critical evaluation, and dissemination of bibliographies and critical values in the fields of transition probabilities and collision cross sections.

Etymology

From Latin collectio (a gathering), from colligere (to gather together).
