prepupal

1. ADJ

/ priˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / priˈpjuːpəl / (BrE)
relating to the inactive stage in the life cycle of some insects occurring between the larval and pupal stages.

Examples

The life history of the alkali bee is similar to that of Andrena, but the first activity of the adults does not take place until summer, and the individuals hibernate in the prepupal stage.

Synset Examples

the prepupal stage

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pupal - relating to the pupa stage of insects, which follows the prepupal stage
  • Degree Lower: larval - relating to the larva stage of insects, which precedes the prepupal stage

Etymology

From pre- (before) + pupa (Latin for 'doll', used in biology for the inactive insect stage).
