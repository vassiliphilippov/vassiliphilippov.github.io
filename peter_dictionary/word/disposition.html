disposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the usual attitude or mood that a person has; a person's temperament.

Examples

For that matter, Stan Musial is rare, possessing the disposition that enabled him to put out the same for seven managers, reserving his opinions, but not his effort.
This happy, always smiling lad with the sunny disposition is our new Junior Mr. Canada - Henri de Courcy.

Synset Examples

he has a happy disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temperament - a person's or animal's nature or usual mood
  • Synonym: personality - the combination of characteristics or qualities that form an individual's character

Etymology

From Latin dispositio (arrangement), from disponere (to arrange).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of getting rid of something, especially by sale or transfer

Examples

All moneys received from dispositions under this section shall be paid into the Treasury as miscellaneous receipts)
If you get rid of Sollozzo, everything falls into line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disposal - the action or process of getting rid of something

Etymology

From Latin dispositio (arrangement), from disponere (to arrange).
