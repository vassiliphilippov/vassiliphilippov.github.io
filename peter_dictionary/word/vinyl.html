vinyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪnɪl / (BrE)
A univalent chemical group (—CH=CH2) derived from ethylene, found in many polymers and chemical compounds.

Examples

The most conclusively identified is L - 5 - vinyl - 2 - thio oxazolidone, which was isolated from rutabaga (Greer, 1950).

Etymology

From Latin 'vinum' (wine) + '-yl', by analogy with alcohol derivatives.
