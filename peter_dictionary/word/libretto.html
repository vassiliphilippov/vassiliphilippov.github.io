libretto

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈbrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / lɪˈbretəʊ / (BrE)
The text of an opera or other long vocal work, including all spoken or sung words.

Examples

And she wrote the libretto for an oratorio on the subject of Judas Maccabeus performed at the Hanukkah festival which came in December.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: score - the music for an opera or musical play

Etymology

From Italian 'libretto', diminutive of 'libro' (book), from Latin 'liber' (book).
