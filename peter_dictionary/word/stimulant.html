stimulant

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪmjələnt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊlənt / (BrE)
something that encourages activity, interest, or enthusiasm; an event or information that arouses action or response.

Examples

It was a real stimulant to a lot of guys I know who have moved past the 2 - score year milestone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incentive - something that motivates or encourages someone to do something
  • Antonym: discouragement - something that makes someone less likely to do something

Etymology

From Latin stimulans, present participle of stimulare (to goad, incite).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪmjələnt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊlənt / (BrE)
A drug or substance that temporarily increases physiological or nervous activity in the body.

Examples

Holmes is addicted to the use of cocaine and other refreshing stimulants; he is prone to semi catatonic trances induced by the playing of the vioiln; he is a recluse, an incredible egotist, a confirmed misogynist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depressant - a drug that reduces physiological or nervous activity in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'stimulans', present participle of 'stimulare' (to goad, stimulate).
