parch

1. VERB

/ pɑrtʃ / (AmE), / pɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to make or become very dry, especially through intense heat

Examples

The dust clogged their throats, and the heat parched them, so that the women were always making ice water.

Synset Examples

The sun parched the earth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wither - to become dry and shriveled
  • Antonym: drench - to make thoroughly wet

Etymology

From Middle English *parchen*, likely from Old French *parcher* (to roast), from Latin *percoquere* (to cook thoroughly).
