treadmill

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛdˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd.mɪl / (BrE)
a piece of exercise equipment with a moving belt that allows a person to walk or run in place

Examples

Usually the patient breathes into a mouthpiece while walking a treadmill, standing still, or in some other medically significant position.

Etymology

Originally referred to a kind of mill powered by walking on a wheel or belt, from 'tread' + 'mill'.
