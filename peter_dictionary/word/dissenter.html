dissenter

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛntər / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛntə / (BrE)
a person who disagrees with or refuses to conform to an established policy, authority, or majority opinion

Examples

Ann Catt was a lonely, devoted soul, never married, conducting a spotless home and devoted to her church, but a perpetual dissenter and born critic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to established customs or policies

Etymology

From English 'dissent' (to differ in opinion) plus '-er' (one who). Ultimately from Latin 'dissentire' (to disagree).
