morosely

1. ADV

/ məˈroʊsli / (AmE), / məˈrəʊsli / (BrE)
In a sullen, gloomy, or ill-tempered manner.

Examples

Cooper was beside his car, on the curb at the right, just standing there morosely; he did n't even look up.

Synset Examples

he fell morosely on the bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomily - in a pessimistic or melancholy manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy, joyful manner

Etymology

From 'morose', from Latin morosus (peevish, sullen).
