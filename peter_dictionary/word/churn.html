churn

1. VERB

/ tʃɝn / (AmE), / tʃɜːn / (BrE)
to stir cream or milk vigorously in order to make butter

Examples

With the exception of a few spots, Milhaud's music mostly churns away with his usual collection of ditties, odd harmonies, and lumbering, satiric orchestration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir - to mix a substance by moving it around with a spoon or stick

Etymology

From Old English 'cyrin', related to Old Norse 'kirna' (butter churn).
