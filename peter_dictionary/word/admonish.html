admonish

1. VERB

/ ədˈmɑː.nɪʃ / (AmE), / ədˈmɒ.nɪʃ / (BrE)
To firmly warn or counsel someone about their behavior or actions.

Examples

Instead of admonishing him to let the weights alone they personally took him to that master Montreal bodybuilding authority, Professor Roland Claude.

Synset Examples

I warned him not to go too far
I warn you against false assumptions
She warned him to be quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warn - to inform someone in advance of possible danger or problems
  • Degree Higher: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism of someone
  • Degree Lower: advise - to offer suggestions about the best course of action to someone

Etymology

From Old French *amonester*, from Latin *admonere* (to warn, advise).
