airs

1. NOUN

/ ɛrz / (AmE), / eəz / (BrE)
Affected or pretentious behavior, typically intended to impress others or show superiority.

Examples

She gave herself fancy airs!

Synset Examples

don't put on airs with me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretensions - claims or behavior intended to impress others
  • Antonym: modesty - the opposite quality: not behaving to impress others

Etymology

From Old French 'air' (appearance, manner), ultimately from Latin 'aer' (air).
