improvised

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.prə.vaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prə.vaɪzd / (BrE)
Created or put together quickly using whatever materials are at hand, rather than being planned or prepared in advance.

Examples

Standing in the shelter of the tent - a rejected hospital tent on which the rain now dripped, no longer drumming - Adam watched his own hands touch the objects on the improvised counter of boards laid across two beef barrels.

Synset Examples

crossed the river on improvised bridges
the survivors used jury-rigged fishing gear
the rock served as a makeshift hammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: makeshift - serving as a temporary substitute, especially due to an emergency
  • Synonym: jury-rigged - constructed in a makeshift way with available materials
  • Antonym: planned - made or arranged in advance, not improvised

Etymology

From improvise, ultimately from French *improviser*, from Italian *improvvisare*, from Latin *improvisus* ('unforeseen').
