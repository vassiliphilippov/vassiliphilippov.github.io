remind

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmaɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈmaɪnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to remember something or to make someone think of something

Examples

But, as Scripture everywhere reminds us, God does have need of his creatures, and the church, a fortiori, can ill afford to do without the talents with which the world, by God's providence, presents it.

We are reminded, however, that freedom of thought and discussion, the unfettered exchange of ideas, is basic under our form of government.

Synset Examples

Remind me to call Mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - bring back into one's mind; remember
  • Antonym: forget - fail to remember; lose from memory

Etymology

From re- (again) + mind, modeled after remind in French and Late Latin.
