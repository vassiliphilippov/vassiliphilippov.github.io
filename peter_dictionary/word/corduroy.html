corduroy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdəˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəˌrɔɪ / (BrE)
A durable cotton fabric with a ridged, raised pattern of vertical ribs (wales), commonly used for clothing and upholstery.

Examples

He 'd put on his old brown corduroy coat and it was already soaked.

Etymology

From French *corde du roi* ('cord of the king'), possibly referring to the fabric's origin or quality.
