freezing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːzɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of becoming solid as a result of extreme cold; the change of a substance from liquid to solid due to the withdrawal of heat.

Examples

That keeps in the cold, retains moisture and prevents the heaving of alternate freezing and thawing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melting - process whereby a solid turns into a liquid by heat

Etymology

From verb 'freeze' + -ing, from Old English 'frēosan' (to freeze).
