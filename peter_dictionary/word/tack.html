tack

1. VERB

/ tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
(of a sailing boat or its crew) to change course by turning the bow into and through the wind.

Examples

The men were at first puzzled, then angered by the aimless tacking.

Synset Examples

The sailors decided to tack the boat
The boat tacked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jibe - (also 'gybe') to change course by turning a sailing boat's stern through the wind

Etymology

From Middle French *taquer*, related to the nautical sense of changing direction.

2. VERB

/ tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
to fasten or attach something with tacks (small nails or pins)

Examples

This was the message found tacked to the cabin door.
Tack solder all the 1 2 "sq. pieces to the 10 "and 12 "members.
Cap with a * * f bar and tack solder in place.

Synset Examples

tack the notice on the board

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - to fasten with a nail

Etymology

From Middle English *takke*, probably from Middle Dutch *tacke* (hook, nail).
