salesmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlzmənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈseɪlzmənʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill or ability of persuading people to buy goods or services

Examples

The change may require different products, pricing, packaging, warehousing, salesmanship, advertising and executive attention - practically every link in the marketing network may have to be adjusted.
Salesmanship is still necessary, but it's a different brand of salesmanship.
Salesmanship is still necessary, but it's a different brand of salesmanship.

Synset Examples

he read a book on salesmanship but it didn't help

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuasion - the act of convincing someone to do or believe something

Etymology

From 'salesman' + '-ship', denoting the qualities or skills of a salesman.
