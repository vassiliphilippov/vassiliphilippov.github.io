roost

1. VERB

/ ɹuːst / (AmE), / ɹuːst / (BrE)
(of a bird) to settle or rest on a perch, especially for the purpose of sleeping.

Examples

This carried over into the more urbanized late 19 th and early 20 th centuries, when the man ruled the roost in the best bull roaring Life With Father manner.

Synset Examples

The birds perched high in the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - to sit or rest on something high or narrow

Etymology

From Middle English 'rooste', from Old English 'hrōst' (roof beam, perch).
