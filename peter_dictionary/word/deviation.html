deviation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A difference from what is usual, expected, or normal; a departure from a standard or accepted norm.

Examples

However, one must expect that vaporization and ejection of material by hypervelocity impacts would cause a deviation from a linear relationship.
Some investigators have found a parallelism between remissions and return of the sympathetic reactivity of the hypothalamus to the normal level as indicated by the Mecholyl test and, conversely, between clinical impairment and increasing deviation of this test from the norm.

Synset Examples

the deviation from the mean

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or standard
  • Synonym: variance - statistical measure of deviation from a mean

Etymology

From Late Latin *deviationem* (a turning away), from Latin *deviare* (to turn aside).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount by which a single measurement differs from a fixed value such as the mean or expected value, especially in statistics and mathematics

Examples

The 34 arrows, denoting onset age plus completion age deviations, have been arrayed in an Onset sequence which begins with girl 18 who had the earliest Onset of the 34 girls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variance - a measure of the spread between numbers in a data set
  • Synonym: standard deviation - a measure of the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of values

Etymology

From Latin 'deviatio', from 'deviāre' (to turn aside).
