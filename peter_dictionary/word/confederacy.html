Confederacy

1. PROPN

/ kənˈfɛdɚəsi / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərəsi / (BrE)
the group of southern US states that seceded from the Union in 1861, forming a separate government during the American Civil War

Examples

Prior to 1954 I imagine that a majority of Southerners would have voted against the Confederacy.

Etymology

From Latin *confoederatiō* (alliance), via Old French *confederacie*.
