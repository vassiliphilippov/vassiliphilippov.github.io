incorporation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkɔrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of combining two or more things into a single whole; unification into one body or entity

Examples

A well publicized entrant which has achieved success only recently is the built liquid detergent, with which the major problem today is incorporation of builder and active into a small volume using a sufficiently high builder / active ratio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integration - the act of combining parts to make a whole
  • Antonym: separation - the act of moving things apart or keeping things distinct

Etymology

From Late Latin incorporatio, from Latin incorporare (to embody).
