anonymity

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈnɪməti / (AmE), / ˌanəˈnɪmɪti / (BrE)
The state of having one's identity unknown or concealed.

Examples

And thus torn between his desire to be known as the composer of a successful opera and the necessity of remaining true to his proclaimed desire for anonymity, Rousseau suffered through several painful weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anonymous - having an unknown or concealed identity

Etymology

From Greek anōnymia, from anōnymos (nameless).
