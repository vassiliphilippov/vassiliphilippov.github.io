drowse

1. VERB

/ draʊz / (AmE), / draʊz / (BrE)
to sleep lightly or be half-asleep; to doze, especially in a relaxed or lazy way

Examples

The street that is full now of traffic and parked cars then and for many years drowsed on an August afternoon in the shade of the curbside trees, and silence was a weight, almost palpable, in the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly or nap briefly
  • Synonym: nap - to sleep for a short period, especially during the day

Etymology

From Middle English 'drowsen', related to 'drowsy'.
