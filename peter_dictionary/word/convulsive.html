convulsive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvʌl.sɪv / (AmE), / kənˈvʌl.sɪv / (BrE)
(of movements or actions) characterized by or resulting from sudden, involuntary muscular contractions; resembling a spasm.

Examples

Thus cortico-fugal discharges induced by topical application of strychnine to a minute area in the neocortex summate with spikes present in the hypothalamus and cause increased convulsive discharges.
In other studies, loss of differentiation in previously established conditioned reflexes resulted from repeated convulsive (metrazol) treatments, suggesting a fundamental disturbance in the balance between excitatory and inhibitory cerebral processes.

Synset Examples

convulsive motions
his body made a spasmodic jerk
spastic movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasmodic - occurring in brief, irregular bursts; resembling a spasm
  • Synonym: spastic - relating to or affected by muscle spasm

Etymology

From Latin 'convulsivus', from 'convellere' (to tear loose).
