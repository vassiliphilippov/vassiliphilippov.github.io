sworn

1. ADJ

/ swɔrn / (AmE), / swɔːn / (BrE)
Formally bound or committed by an oath; having made a solemn or official promise.

Examples

The important result, however, was that Juet and Francis Clemens, the deposed boatswain, became Hudson's sworn enemies.
Will he come forward and be sworn, sir?

Synset Examples

according to an early tradition became his sworn brother
sworn enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pledged - firmly committed or promised

Etymology

Past participle of 'swear', from Old English swerian.

2. ADJ

/ swɔrn / (AmE), / swɔːn / (BrE)
(of a statement, testimony, or person) formally declared or promised as true under oath.

Examples

We have a sworn affidavit.

Synset Examples

now my sworn friend and then mine enemy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsworn - not made or given under oath

Etymology

Past participle of 'swear', from Old English 'swerian'.
