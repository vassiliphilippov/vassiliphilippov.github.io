dragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
A mythical creature typically depicted as a large, fire-breathing reptile, often with wings, found in European folklore and legends.

Examples

Once he had abandoned himself to the very worst, once he had quieted all the dragons of worry and suspense, there would n't be very much for Mae to do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wyvern - a mythical two-legged dragon with wings
  • Synonym: serpent - a large snake, sometimes used for mythical creatures

Etymology

From Old French dragon, from Latin dracō, from Greek drákōn (serpent, dragon).
