refocusing

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˈfoʊkəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfəʊkəsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of focusing again, either literally (as with a camera or light) or figuratively (as with attention or strategy).

Examples

It was the season's first night game and an obvious refocusing of the lights are in order.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: focus - the act of focusing or the state of having been focused

Etymology

From re- (again) + focusing (from focus, Latin focus 'hearth, fireplace').
