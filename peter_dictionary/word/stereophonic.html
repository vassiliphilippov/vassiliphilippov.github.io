stereophonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsterioʊˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌstɛəriəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of sound or audio equipment) using two or more channels to create the impression of sound coming from different directions, as in natural hearing.

Examples

The phonograph today, for all its high fidelity and stereophonic sound, is precisely what the early art purveyors in the movies wished to make of the camera.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mono - single-channel sound reproduction
  • Synonym: stereo - audio system using two channels for sound reproduction

Etymology

From Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'phonic' from Greek 'phōnē' (sound, voice).
