wizard

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzɚd / (AmE), / ˈwɪzəd / (BrE)
A person who is extremely skilled or talented in a particular area.

Examples

It is not news that Nathan Milstein is a wizard of the violin.
An early film by a competitor of the Wizard of Menlo Park simply showed a long kiss performed by two actors of the contemporary stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace - a person who excels at something
  • Synonym: expert - a person with extensive knowledge or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From Middle English 'wysard', alteration of 'wys' (wise) + '-ard'.
