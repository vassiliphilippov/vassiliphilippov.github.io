esthetic

1. ADJ

/ ɛsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / iːsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty

Examples

This would, naturally, lengthen their courses far beyond the largely esthetic demands of interior designer's training.
Exclusive of esthetic values, such as high - or low foam level, perfume content, etc., the requirements for the organic active used in washing textiles are high.

Synset Examples

the aesthetic faculties
an aesthetic person
aesthetic feeling
the illustrations made the book an aesthetic success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aesthetic - relating to beauty or artistic taste (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Greek *aisthētikos* (perceptive, sensitive), via French *esthétique* or German *ästhetisch*.
