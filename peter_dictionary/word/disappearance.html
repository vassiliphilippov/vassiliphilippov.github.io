disappearance

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpɪr.əns / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpɪə.rəns / (BrE)
The process of gradually ceasing to be visible or no longer able to be seen.

Examples

He took advantage of the antagonism between aggressive assertiveness and anxiety and found a relatively rapid disappearance of anxiety when the former attitude was established.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Antonym: emergence - the process of becoming visible after being concealed

Etymology

From dis- + appearance, after appear; dating from the early 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɪərəns / (BrE)
the act or event of someone or something ceasing to be visible or present

Examples

Death is not permanent defeat and disappearance; man has a second chance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appearance - the act or event of someone or something becoming visible or present

Etymology

From French *disparaître* (to disappear), from Latin *disparere*: *dis-* (apart, away) + *parere* (to appear).
