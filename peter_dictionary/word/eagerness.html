eagerness

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡərnəs / (AmE), / ˈiːɡənəs / (BrE)
a strong feeling of excitement and enthusiasm to do or have something

Examples

They moved toward the skiffs with shocking eagerness, elbowing and shoving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or lack of enthusiasm

Etymology

From 'eager' + noun-forming suffix '-ness'. 'Eager' from Old French 'eigre', from Latin 'acer' (sharp, keen).
