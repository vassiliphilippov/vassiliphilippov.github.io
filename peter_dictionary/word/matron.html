matron

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪtrən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtrən / (BrE)
A married woman, typically middle-aged and dignified, often with children.

Examples

Not seeing her dark intelligent face, one would have gauged the voice as that of a Westwood Village matron, ten years out of Iowa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matriarch - an older woman who is the head of a family or community

Etymology

From Latin 'matrona' (married woman), from 'mater' (mother).
