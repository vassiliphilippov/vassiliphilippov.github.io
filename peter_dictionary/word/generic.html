generic

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛrɪk / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrɪk / (BrE)
A product, especially a drug or consumer item, that is sold without a brand name; an unbranded item often sold at a lower price than branded equivalents.

Examples

He's been using brand X.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brand-name - a product sold under a particular manufacturer's name

Etymology

From Latin genericus (of a genus or kind), from genus (birth, kind, class).
