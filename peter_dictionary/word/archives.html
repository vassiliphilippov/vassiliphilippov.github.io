archives

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrˌkaɪvz / (AmE), / ˈɑːkʌɪvz / (BrE)
A collection of historical records and documents, especially those of an institution, organization, or government, preserved for their long-term value.

Examples

At this date, it seems probable that the name of Serge Prokofieff will appear in the archives of History, as an effective Traditionalist, who was fully aware of the lure and danger of experimentation, and used it as it served his purpose; yet was never caught up in it - never a slave to its academic dialectics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archive - a place where public records or documents are kept

Etymology

From French 'archives', from Latin 'archīva', from Greek 'arkheia' (public records).
