letdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɛt.daʊn / (BrE)
A feeling of disappointment or dissatisfaction caused by unmet expectations.

Examples

The parties were on the whole unprepared for elections, while the people were still experiencing post independence let-down and suffering the after effects of poor harvests in 1957.

Synset Examples

his hopes were so high he was doomed to disappointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disappointment - a feeling of sadness or disappointment
  • Antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation after stress or disappointment

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'let down', meaning 'to disappoint', later used as a noun (early 20th century).
