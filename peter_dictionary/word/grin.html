grin

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪn / (BrE)
A broad smile in which the corners of the mouth are turned up, usually showing amusement or pleasure.

Examples

"I knew it all along ", confessed Arnold with a grin, "but I just happened to think how much nicer it would be to drop one way up there ".
He had a round, frank Irish face, creased in a jovial grin that stayed bleakly in place even when he was pumping bullets into someone's body.
He could n't see the grin that split her mouth; the teeth that shone into the phone were like a shark's.
Jess's coarse features twisted in a surprised grin which was smashed out of shape by Curt's fist.
But with new Joker Brand... ... I get a grin again... ... and again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smile - a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression with the mouth turned up
  • Antonym: frown - a facial expression with the brows knitted, expressing displeasure or worry

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse grina ('to grin, to snarl').

2. VERB

/ ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪn / (BrE)
to smile broadly by drawing back the lips and showing the teeth, often in amusement or pleasure, but sometimes in a grimace or snarl

Examples

See, he's already snapping it up, the dumb jerk * * h Coughlin grinned, feeling supremely on top of things * * h He watched the snow once again.

Michelangelo grinned.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: smile - to make a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression, typically without showing the teeth
  • Degree Higher: beam - to smile broadly and radiantly with happiness
  • Antonym: frown - to make a facial expression indicating displeasure or concentration by contracting the brow

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *grennian* (to show the teeth in pain or anger).
