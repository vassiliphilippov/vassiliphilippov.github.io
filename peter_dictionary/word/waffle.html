waffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒfəl / (BrE)
A crisp, leavened batter cake baked in a patterned iron and eaten hot, often with syrup or other toppings.

Examples

We would attend a film and, later on, I stated, we might go to the Mayflower Coffee Shop or Child's or Toffenetti's for waffles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancake - a thin, flat cake of batter fried on both sides in a pan

Etymology

From Dutch *wafel*, related to Middle Low German *wāfel*.
