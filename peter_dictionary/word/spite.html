spite

1. NOUN

/ spaɪt / (AmE), / spaɪt / (BrE)
A feeling of wanting to hurt, annoy, or upset someone, often out of malice or resentment.

Examples

Here he was, suddenly caught up in the delirium of a war, in the spite and calumny of Whigs and Tories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - desire to harm others; ill will
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Old French *despit*, from Latin *despectus* (a looking down on).

2. NOUN

/ spaɪt / (AmE), / spaɪt / (BrE)
A feeling of malice or a desire to hurt, annoy, or offend someone, often for petty reasons.

Examples

We're not murderers, in spite of what this undertaker says.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil or cause harm
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being kind and well-meaning

Etymology

From Old French 'despit' (contempt), from Latin 'despectus', past participle of 'despicere' (to look down on).
