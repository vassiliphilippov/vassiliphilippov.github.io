providence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.vɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.vɪ.dəns / (BrE)
(in Christian theology and other religions) the protective care and guidance of God or of a divine power over the universe or its creatures.

Examples

But, as Scripture everywhere reminds us, God does have need of his creatures, and the church, a fortiori, can ill afford to do without the talents with which the world, by God's providence, presents it.
Thus ideas like "grace ", "salvation ", and "providence "cluster together in traditional Christianity.

Synset Examples

divine providence

Etymology

From Latin 'providentia' (foresight, prudence), from 'providere' (to foresee). The theological sense developed in late Middle English.
