obscurely

1. ADV

/ əbˈskjʊrli / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not clear, easily understood, or explicit; in an obscure manner.

Examples

It is worth dwelling in some detail on the crisis of this story, because it brings together a number of characteristic elements and makes of them a curious, riddling compound obscurely but centrally significant for Mann's work.

Synset Examples

this work is obscurely written

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clearly - in a clear and easy to understand manner

Etymology

From 'obscure' + '-ly'; 'obscure' from Latin 'obscurus' (dark, dim, unclear).
