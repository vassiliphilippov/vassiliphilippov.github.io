unfettered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɛtərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛtəd / (BrE)
Not confined or restricted; free from limitations, especially referring to being not physically bound or, more commonly, free in actions or thoughts.

Examples

We are reminded, however, that freedom of thought and discussion, the unfettered exchange of ideas, is basic under our form of government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - not under the control or in the power of another; able to act or be as one wishes
  • Antonym: restricted - limited in extent, number, scope, or action

Etymology

From un- (not) + fettered, from Middle English 'feter' (a chain or shackle for the feet).
