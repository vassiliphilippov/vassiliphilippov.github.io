disproportionate

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪs.prəˈpɔr.ʃə.nət / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.prəˈpɔː.ʃən.ət / (BrE)
Too large or too small in comparison with something else; not in correct or balanced proportion.

Examples

"Failure to do this will continue to place a disproportionate burden "on Fulton taxpayers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportionate - having the correct or balanced proportion

Etymology

From dis- (expressing negation) + proportionate, from proportion.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsprəˈpɔrʃənət / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃənət / (BrE)
too large or too small in comparison with something else; not in correct or fair proportion.

Examples

Accounting procedures which continue to charge a disproportionate overhead and warehouse expense to the main store make the branches seem more profitable than they are.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportionate - in correct or appropriate proportion
  • Synonym: inordinate - excessive; unrestrained

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart, not) + 'proportionatus' (proportioned).
