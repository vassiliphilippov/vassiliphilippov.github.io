playable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being played or suitable for play, as in a musical piece, a game, or a sporting situation

Examples

A spectator picked up the ball and handed it to a small boy, who dropped this suddenly hot potato in a very playable lie.

Synset Examples

a playable lie in golf
the baseball fan reached out and caught a foul that was judged playable
the ball field was playable
harpsichord music is readily playable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplayable - not able to be played or unsuited for play

Etymology

From 'play' + '-able', with '-able' from Latin '-abilis', indicating possibility or suitability.
