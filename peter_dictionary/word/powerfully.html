powerfully

1. ADV

/ ˈpaʊ.ɚ.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə.fəli / (BrE)
In a powerful manner; with great force, strength, or influence.

Examples

He called in the pitcher who had been pitching, and a big, heavy, powerfully built right hander moved out to the mound for Anniston.

Synset Examples

the federal government replaced the powerfully pro-settler Sir Godfrey Huggins with the even tougher and more determined ex-trade unionist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weakly - in a weak manner; lacking strength or power
