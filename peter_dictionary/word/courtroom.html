courtroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtˌruːm / (BrE)
A room where legal cases are heard and decided in a court of law.

Examples

I stood in the courtroom like a fool.

Synset Examples

television cameras were admitted in the courtroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - official assembly where legal cases are tried

Etymology

Formed from 'court' (from Old French *cort*, from Latin *cohors*, enclosure) + 'room' (Old English *rum*)
