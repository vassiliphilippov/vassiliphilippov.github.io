aloes

1. NOUN

/ ˈæloʊz / (AmE), / ˈaləʊz / (BrE)
A bitter, purgative substance made from the leaves of the aloe plant, used in medicine and perfumes, especially in historical and literary contexts.

Examples

He brought with him a mixture of myrrh and aloes, of about a hundred pounds' weight.
Perhaps after the soldiers had laid him on the ground, while Joseph of Arimathea was at Pontius Pilate's asking for Christ's body, Nicodemus was gathering his mixture of myrrh and aloes, and the others had gone home to mourn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloe - a succulent plant of the genus Aloe

Etymology

Plural form of 'aloe,' from Latin 'aloë', from Greek 'aloē', referring to both the plant and its resin.
