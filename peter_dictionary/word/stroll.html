stroll

1. NOUN

/ stroʊl / (AmE), / strəʊl / (BrE)
a leisurely walk, typically taken in a public place for pleasure.

Examples

Alex explained that they had been out for a stroll before breakfast while the doctor edged around behind him, attempting to hide the protuberance at his left side behind Alex's arm and back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - an act of traveling on foot at a regular pace
  • Antonym: march - a walk with a steady, regular step, often for a purpose

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps related to German dialect strollen (to loaf) or strollen (to roam).

2. VERB

/ stroʊl / (AmE), / strəʊl / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, typically for pleasure and without a particular destination

Examples

His legs suddenly feel heavy and unaccountably weary, as if he had walked for miles, instead of strolling a few hundred yards along the old campus paths.
He strolled... like a man in a park, without a care or worry in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saunter - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, often without purpose
  • Degree Higher: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
  • Antonym: rush - to move with urgent haste

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from dialectal Scandinavian, or related to German 'strolchen' (to roam).
