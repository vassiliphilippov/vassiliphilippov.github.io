Pole

1. NOUN

/ poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Poland, or a person of Polish descent

Examples

Before he went into battle Andrei had told Alex, "I only want to be a Pole.
They were not Poles.
Until I was transferred into the Seventh Ulanys I was like every other Pole in my feeling about Jews.
What I mean is, he was a Pole and the greatest soldier in the Ulanys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polish - relating to Poland, its people, or language

Etymology

From Polish 'Polak' (male person from Poland), via French 'Pôle' or German 'Pole'.
