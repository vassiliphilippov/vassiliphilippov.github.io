procrastinate

1. VERB

/ proʊˈkræstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / prəʊˈkræstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to delay or postpone action, especially something that must be done; to put off doing something out of reluctance or laziness

Examples

Those in power tend to procrastinate and even to repudiate the electoral process.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to postpone or cause something to happen at a later time
  • Antonym: expedite - to make (an action or process) happen sooner; to speed up

Etymology

From Latin procrastinatus, past participle of procrastinare, from pro- (forward) + crastinus (of tomorrow).
