brad

1. NOUN

/ bræd / (AmE), / bræd / (BrE)
A small, slender nail with a small head, used in woodwork or furniture making.

Examples

Lolotte held a patch of leather, Rev steadied something, a tiny brad, waiting for George's poised hammer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - a small metal spike driven into wood

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from Old Norse 'broddr' (spike, point).
