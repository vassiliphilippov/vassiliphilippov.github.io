lordly

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔrdli / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdli / (BrE)
Having the qualities of or characteristic of a lord; noble, dignified, or stately.

Examples

The lordly poet was at low-water mark.

Synset Examples

heir to a lordly fortune
of august lineage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: august - respected and impressive; majestic
  • Synonym: noble - having or showing fine personal qualities or high moral principles
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance

Etymology

From Middle English lordly, from lord + -ly.
