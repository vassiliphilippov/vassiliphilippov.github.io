impel

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɛl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛl / (BrE)
to drive, urge, or force someone to do something, especially by moral pressure or necessity

Examples

At one time she felt impelled to make dances that "moved all over the stage ", much as Pollock's paintings move violently over the full extent of the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compel - to force or oblige someone to do something
  • Antonym: deter - to discourage someone from doing something

Etymology

From Latin impellere, from in- (towards) + pellere (to drive, push).
