disarray

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈreɪ / (BrE)
A state of disorder or confusion in thoughts, feelings, or organization.

Examples

Harold Clurman is right to say that "Waiting for Godot "is a reflection (he calls it a distorted reflection) "of the impasse and disarray of Europe's present politics, ethic, and common way of life ".

Synset Examples

a confusion of impressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - lack of clarity or order in the mind or situation
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged methodically

Etymology

From Old French 'desaree' (disorder), from 'des-' (dis-) + 'aree' (array).
