arduous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrdʒuəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːdjuːəs / (BrE)
involving or requiring strenuous effort; very difficult and tiring, especially physically

Examples

By fall, 443 survivors of this arduous journey were clustered about Fort Snelling, but most of them were sent on to Galena and St. Louis, with a few going as far as Vevay, Indiana, a notable Swiss center in the United States.

Synset Examples

worked their arduous way up the mining valley
a grueling campaign
hard labor
heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or requiring little effort
  • Synonym: grueling - extremely tiring and demanding
  • Synonym: demanding - requiring much skill or effort

Etymology

From Latin arduus (steep, difficult).
