vandal

1. NOUN

/ ˈvændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvændl̩ / (BrE)
A person who deliberately destroys or damages property belonging to others.

Examples

Furthermore - and this, to me, strikes an especially warming note - it shall avail the vandals naught to throw away or dispose of their loot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hooligan - a violent or destructive young person

Etymology

From the name of the Vandals, a Germanic people who sacked Rome in 455 AD; later used figuratively to mean a destroyer of property.
