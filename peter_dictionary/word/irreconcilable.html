irreconcilable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪˌrɛkənˈsaɪləbəl / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛkənˈsaɪləbl / (BrE)
Impossible to bring into agreement or harmony; unable to be reconciled.

Examples

Let it be granted then that the theological differences in this area between Protestants and Roman Catholics appear to be irreconcilable.

Synset Examples

irreconcilable differences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reconcilable - able to be reconciled or brought into agreement

Etymology

From French 'irréconciliable', from Latin 'ir-' (not) + 'reconciliare' (to bring together).
