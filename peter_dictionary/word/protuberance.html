protuberance

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtuːbərəns / (AmE), / prəˈtjuːbərəns / (BrE)
A part of something that sticks out or projects from a surface.

Examples

Alex explained that they had been out for a stroll before breakfast while the doctor edged around behind him, attempting to hide the protuberance at his left side behind Alex's arm and back.

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - a rounded swelling or projection
  • Synonym: bump - a rounded protuberance on a surface

Etymology

From Latin protuberare 'to swell outward', from pro- 'forward' + tuber 'swelling, lump'.
