upgrade

1. VERB

/ ˈʌpɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌpɡreɪd / (BrE)
to improve something by making it newer, better, or more effective

Examples

Many companies have upgraded their sales manpower and tested new selling, distribution, and promotion techniques to gain a bigger competitive edge.

Synset Examples

I've upgraded my computer so I can run better software
The company upgraded their personnel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: downgrade - to lower to a less important or less valuable level

Etymology

From up + grade, originally meaning to improve the grade or level of something.

2. VERB

/ ˈʌpˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌpɡreɪd / (BrE)
to raise something to a higher standard, rank, or value; to improve or enhance the quality or status of someone or something

Examples

Stations programing to Negro listeners are having to upgrade their shows in order to keep pace with rising educational, economic, and cultural levels.
This tied in closely with the current attempt to upgrade state-owned cars to the extent that vehicles are not retained beyond the point where maintenance costs (in light of depreciation) become excessive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promote - to raise someone to a higher position or rank
  • Antonym: downgrade - to lower someone or something to a lower grade, rank, or status

Etymology

From up + grade, meaning to move to a higher step, c. 19th century.
