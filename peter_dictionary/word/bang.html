bang

1. NOUN

/ bæŋ / (AmE), / bæŋ / (BrE)
a sudden very loud noise, such as that made by an explosion, a gunshot, or something striking a hard surface

Examples

That tap had a slight bang ish quality.
He hung up with a bang, annoyed at himself for running off at the mouth like that but still terribly concerned with the situation he had helped to create.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boom - a deep, prolonged loud noise
  • Synonym: crash - a sudden loud sound as of something breaking
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or quiet sound

2. NOUN

/ bæŋ / (AmE), / bæŋ / (BrE)
a section of hair cut short and straight across the forehead

Examples

She was a living doll and no mistake - the blue-black bang, the wide cheekbones, olive flushed, that betrayed the Cherokee strain in her Midwestern lineage, and the mouth whose only fault, in the novelist's carping phrase, was that the lower lip was a trifle too voluptuous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe - hair that is cut and hangs over the forehead (British English equivalent)

Etymology

Originally US English, possibly from the manner of cutting hair 'bang-off' (completely off, all at once).

3. VERB

/ bæŋ / (AmE), / bæŋ / (BrE)
to close or shut something violently and noisily

Examples

The 7: 45 bell rang and he could hear the outside doors bang shut, closing in the assembled day watch.
and Phil banged his locker door shut and spun around.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam - to shut forcefully and noisily
  • Synonym: shut - to close something, especially a door or lid

4. VERB

/ bæŋ / (AmE), / baŋ / (BrE)
to hit or strike something violently, often causing a loud noise

Examples

The car lurched along at a snail's crawl, the left front mudguard banging and scraping against the tire, occasionally scraping against the road itself.
Above, in the tiled prosceniums of the alcoves, boys sing the ghazals of Hafiz and Saadi, while at the very bottom, in the vaults, the toughs and blades of the city hoot and bang their drums, drink arak, play dice, and dance.

Synset Examples

slam the ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam - to shut forcefully or strike hard, often with noise
  • Synonym: hit - to strike something with force
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly, with a gentle touch
  • Degree Higher: smash - to break or strike with great force

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a sudden blow or impact.

6. NOUN

Examples

The two little bangs meant that he was getting impatient to have a crowd of customers waited on and that if he had to he would jerk open the door and drag out, by the opposite door handle which she would be clutching, whichever-the-hell clerk it was who thought she could waste so much store time on the pot.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knock - a sharp blow or impact, especially as a sound
  • Synonym: bash - a heavy blow, especially with the fist
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light touch or blow
