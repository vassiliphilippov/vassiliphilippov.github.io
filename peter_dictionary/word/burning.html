burning

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of setting something on fire or undergoing combustion

Examples

On the street outside, Hieronymus envisions a holocaust of the vanities of this world, such a burning of artistic and erotic productions as his namesake actually brought to pass in Florence, and prophetically he issues his curse: "Gladius Dei super terram cito et velociter ".

Synset Examples

the burning of leaves was prohibited by a town ordinance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combustion - the process of burning

Etymology

Present participle of 'burn', from Old English 'byrnan' (to burn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɝː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
A pain or sensation that feels hot, as if the affected area were on fire.

Examples

They march you in, naked as the day you were born, skin burning and half-blind from that delousing shit they throw on you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sting - a sharp, burning pain or sensation
  • Degree Lower: tingling - a slightly prickling or stinging feeling
