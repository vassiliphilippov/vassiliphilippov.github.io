breakup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
the ending or disintegration of a relationship, especially between people or nations

Examples

These, he said, are "two of the principal underlying causes for family breakups leading to ADC ".
Nothing in all the preceding years had had the power to bring me closer to a knowledge of profound sorrow than the breakup of camp, the packing away of my camp uniforms, the severing of ties with the six or ten people I had grown most to love in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the state of being apart; the action of separating people or things
  • Synonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage
  • Antonym: reconciliation - restoration of harmonious relations

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'break up', combining 'break' and 'up'.

the act or process of breaking apart or separating into pieces; the state of being broken up

Examples

(Some suspensions break up before they are near to the direction of shear, and some become asymptotic to it without breakup.)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart
  • Synonym: disintegration - the process of losing cohesion or strength

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'break up', formed by combining 'break' + 'up'.
