sect

1. NOUN

/ sɛkt / (AmE), / sɛkt / (BrE)
A group that has separated from an established religion, typically holding its own distinct beliefs and practices.

Examples

These groups have varied widely from mere families, primitive, totemic groups, and small modern cults and sects, to the memberships of great denominations, and great, widely dispersed world religions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denomination - a recognized autonomous branch of the Christian Church
  • Degree Lower: cult - a relatively small religious group regarded as unorthodox or spurious

Etymology

From Latin secta (a course, school of thought, sect), from sequi (to follow).
