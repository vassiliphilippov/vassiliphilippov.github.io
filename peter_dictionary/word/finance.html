finance

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnaɪns, ˈfaɪnæns / (AmE), / fəˈnɑːns, ˈfaɪnæns / (BrE)
the commercial activity of providing funds and managing capital, especially for businesses or governments.

Examples

The interior designer, then, must first be an artist but also understand carpentry and painting and lighting and plumbing and finance.
Finally, a balance must be effected among project finance, utilization of agricultural surpluses, and general balance of payments support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funding - the act of providing financial resources
  • Synonym: investment - the act of putting money into something to earn profit

Etymology

From Old French *finance* (payment, settlement of a debt), from finer (to pay, to end), from Latin *finis* (end).

2. VERB

/ faɪˈnæns, fəˈnæns / (AmE), / faɪˈnæns, fɪˈnɑːns / (BrE)
to provide or obtain the money needed for something, especially a project or business
