estuary

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈɛstjʊəri / (BrE)
The wide part of a river where it meets the sea, and fresh and salt water mix.

Examples

R. R. Mole and F. W. Urich give approximately 20 inches as the average length of a brood of thirty from the region of the Orinoco estuaries.

Etymology

From Latin *aestuarium* (creek, tidal inlet), from *aestus* (tide, surge).
