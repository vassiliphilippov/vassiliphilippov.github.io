dignify

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something or someone appear worthy of respect or honor; to confer dignity upon

Examples

Proponents of Zen to the West emphasize disproportionately the amount of Mahayana Buddhism in Zen, probably in order to dignify the indisputably magical Taoist ideas with more respectable Buddhist metaphysic.

Synset Examples

He was dignified with a title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ennoble - to give someone or something a higher status or dignity
  • Antonym: debase - to reduce in dignity or quality

Etymology

From Latin dignificare, from dignus (worthy) + ficare (to make).
