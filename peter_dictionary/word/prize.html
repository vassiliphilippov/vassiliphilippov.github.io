prize

/ praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reward - something given in return for effort or service

Etymology

From Middle English prise, from Old French (also related to 'prise' meaning 'a taking'), from Latin pretium (price, value).

2. VERB

/ praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
to value highly; to regard as very important or precious

Examples

The lawyer did n't know him very well although he saw him occasionally at some dinner party - Thayer, like himself, Madden reflected, was the extra man so prized by hostesses - and found him easy enough to talk to.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherish - to feel or show affection for something; to hold dear
  • Synonym: value - to estimate the worth of; to consider something important
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; to ignore or undervalue

Etymology

From Middle English 'prisen', from Old French 'prisier', from 'pris' (price).
