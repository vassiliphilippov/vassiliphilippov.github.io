opposed

1. ADJ

/ əˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / əˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
(of people or groups) being in opposition or having conflicting aims, beliefs, or interests.

Examples

Everywhere within the common pattern, however, one finds local diversity; Greek history and culture were enduringly fertilized, and plagued, by the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors.

Synset Examples

two bitterly opposed schools of thought

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supportive - giving active help or encouragement; providing support
  • Synonym: hostile - actively opposed or unfriendly

Etymology

From Latin 'opponere' (to set against), via Old French 'opposer'.
