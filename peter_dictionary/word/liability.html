liability

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈæbɪlɪti / (AmE), / laɪˈabɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being legally responsible for something, especially by law.

Examples

If word classes differ in their resistance or liability to stem replacement within meaning slot, it is conceivable that individual meanings also differ with fair consistence trans lingually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsibility - the state or fact of having a duty to deal with something or of having control over someone
  • Antonym: asset - something valuable that an entity owns, benefits from, or has use of

Etymology

From Old French *liabilité*, from *lier* 'to bind', from Latin *ligare* 'to bind'.
