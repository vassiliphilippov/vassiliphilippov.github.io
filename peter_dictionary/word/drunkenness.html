drunkenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌŋkənnəs / (AmE), / ˈdrʌŋkənnəs / (BrE)
The state of being drunk as a result of consuming too much alcohol.

Examples

Drunkenness was no longer tolerated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, not affected by alcohol

Etymology

From Middle English 'drunkennesse', from 'drunken' (past participle of 'drink') + '-ness'.
