importunity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pɔɹˈtuː.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɔːˈtjuː.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
persistent and insistent requests or demands, especially in a way that is annoying or difficult to refuse

Examples

Women themselves have come to look upon matters in the same light as the outside world, and scarcely find any wrong in submitting to the importunities of a stronger will, even when their affections are withheld.

Synset Examples

his importunity left me no alternative but to agree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insistence - the act of stating or demanding something forcefully
  • Synonym: entreaty - an earnest or humble request
  • Degree Lower: request - the act of asking formally for something
  • Degree Higher: demand - an insistent and forceful request

Etymology

From Latin 'importunitas' (troublesomeness), from 'importunus' (inconvenient, troublesome).
