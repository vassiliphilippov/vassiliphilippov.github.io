architecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
The art and science of designing and constructing buildings, especially as a discipline or profession.

Examples

Without comparing the relative merits of the two courses - architecture versus interior design - let us examine the educational needs of the interior designer.

Synset Examples

architecture and eloquence are mixed arts whose end is sometimes beauty and sometimes use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architect - a person who designs buildings

Etymology

From Latin architectura, from Greek arkhitekton (chief builder).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
the art and profession of designing and planning buildings and environments, especially considering their appearance and function

Examples

Considerable specialization in teaching subjects such as architecture, furniture design, textiles and color is also desirable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architect - a person who designs buildings

Etymology

From Latin architectura, from Greek arkhitekton (chief builder).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkɪˌtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a building or other structure designed by an architect; an architectural work

Examples

Figurines and simple chapels presage the emergence of sculpture and architecture in Greece; objects in gold, ivory, and bronze grow more numerous.
On this principle of division I must postpone the evolution of sculpture, architecture, society, and politics; for the developments in these areas make sense only if they are connected to the age of revolution itself.
She was forty-nine at this time, a lanky woman of breeding with an austere, narrow face which had the distinction of a steeple or some architecture that had been designed long ago for a stubborn sort of prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - something that is built or constructed
  • Synonym: building - a structure with a roof and walls

Etymology

From Latin architectura, from Greek arkhitektōn (chief builder, architect).
