bouncing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsɪŋ / (BrE)
The action or process of rebounding off a surface after impact, or a series of such rebounding movements.

Examples

An hour of bouncing, a brief stop in a village to inspect a new school or dispensary.
More bouncing, another stop, a new house for teachers, a new well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - bounce back after hitting a surface

Etymology

From 'bounce' (verb) + '-ing' (present participle suffix). 'Bounce' probably of imitative origin.
