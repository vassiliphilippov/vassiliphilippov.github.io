straightaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtəweɪ / (BrE)
a straight section of a road or racecourse, especially one used for acceleration or overtaking

Examples

Then Kerr, a graduate student from Illinois, moved past him on a straightaway and held off Mills's challenge on the final turn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curve - a bend in a road or racecourse

2. ADV

/ ˈstreɪtəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtəˌweɪ / (BrE)
At once; without delay or hesitation; immediately.

Examples

His hubris, deficiency of taste, and sadism carried him straightaway to the top.

Synset Examples

he answered immediately
found an answer straightaway
an official accused of dishonesty should be suspended forthwith
Come here now!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without any delay
  • Synonym: forthwith - immediately; without hesitation
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end; after a period of time

Etymology

From 'straight' + 'away', indicating immediate movement or action.
