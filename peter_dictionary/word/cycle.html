cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
a set of poems or songs composed around a single theme or narrative and intended to be performed or read as a cohesive unit

Examples

The "Ring "cycles are July 26, 27, 28 and 30, and Aug. 21, 22, 23 and 25.
And yet amid all the gay hedonism in Pilgrimage and Wander-Years is a cycle of short poems, "Thoughts in Loneliness ", filled with brooding, melancholy, and sombre longing ".

Synset Examples

Schubert's song cycles

Etymology

From Greek kyklos (circle, wheel), via Latin cyclus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a single complete occurrence of a recurrent process or phenomenon; one round of a sequence that is repeated periodically

Examples

It is curious that even centuries of repetition of the yearly cycle did not induce a sufficient degree of confidence to allow people to abandon the ceremonies of the winter solstice.

Synset Examples

a year constitutes a cycle of the seasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - an interval of time during which a recurring event takes place

Etymology

From Greek kyklos (circle, wheel) via Latin cyclus.

Examples

This was followed by a comprehensive series of observations of the 1.25 - cm emission of the moon over three lunar cycles by Piddington and Minnett (1949).
There are, of course, certain times during the 24 - hour daily cycle when most of these conditions will be met.
After the male and female andrenas have mated, the cycle is repeated.
It was assumed that the shift in autonomic hypothalamic balance occurring spontaneously in neuropsychiatric patients from the application of certain therapeutic procedures follows the pattern known from the sleep wakefulness cycle.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - a length or portion of time
  • Synonym: sequence - a series of related things or events

Etymology

From Greek kyklos (circle, wheel).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəl / (BrE)
a unit of frequency, equal to one complete oscillation or repetition occurring in a specified time period, especially per second (now replaced in the SI system by the hertz)

Examples

At 100 Amp the 360 cycle ripple was less than 0.5 V (peak to peak) with a resistive load.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hertz - unit of frequency equal to one cycle per second

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle, wheel, cycle).

a series of events that are regularly repeated in the same order; a sequence occurring again and again

Examples

  • Synonym: pattern - a repeated or regular way in which something happens or is done

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle, wheel).
