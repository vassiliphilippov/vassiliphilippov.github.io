expostulate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɑstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express strong disagreement or to argue earnestly with someone in order to try to dissuade them from a course of action

Examples

I'’ll reason with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remonstrate - to protest or object forcefully
  • Synonym: reason with - to try to change someone's mind through logical argument

Etymology

From Latin expostulatus, past participle of expostulare (to demand, to dispute).
