crop

1. NOUN

/ krɑp / (AmE), / krɒp / (BrE)
a plant or group of plants grown and harvested in large quantities, especially as food, during a single season

Examples

Man may also be injured secondarily by damage to his food crops or domestic animals.
The farmer's life must be arranged to meet the demands of crops and livestock.
Drought, hail, disease, and insects take their toll of crops.
Is the land suited to the crops you intend to raise?
Soil type, drainage, or degree of slope can make the difference between good crops and poor ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest - the process or period of gathering in crops

Etymology

Old English *cropp* (sprout, cluster, ear of corn).

2. VERB

/ krɑp / (AmE), / krɒp / (BrE)
to cut (hair, plants, or similar) very short

Examples

He had only a fringe of hair and he wore it cropped short.

Synset Examples

She wanted her hair cropped short

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - to make something neat by cutting away irregular parts

Etymology

From Old English *cropp* (a sprout or head of a plant), later extended to cutting short.
