hopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑpɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒpə / (BrE)
A funnel-shaped container from which materials are discharged by gravity, typically into another receptacle below.

Examples

The resolution, which Barber tossed into the House hopper Friday, will be formally read Monday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funnel - a device for guiding liquid or powder into a small opening

Etymology

Probably from 'hop' + '-er', referring to its mechanism of dropping or letting material fall.
