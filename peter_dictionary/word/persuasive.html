persuasive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsweɪsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈsweɪsɪv / (BrE)
Good at persuading someone to do or believe something through reasoning or the force of argument.

Examples

Rousseau is so persuasive that Voltaire is almost convinced that he should burn his books, too.

Synset Examples

persuasive eloquence
a most persuasive speaker
a persuasive argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convincing - capable of causing someone to believe that something is true or real
  • Antonym: unconvincing - not capable of causing someone to believe that something is true or real

Etymology

From Medieval Latin persuasivus, from Latin persuadere (to persuade).
