drowsily

1. ADV

/ ˈdraʊzəli / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzɪli / (BrE)
In a way that shows sleepiness or lack of full alertness.

Examples

"I think I 'll sleep in this morning ", she said drowsily, and as she snuggled against him, he wondered if she ever went to church.

Synset Examples

`Time to get up,' she said drowsily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepily - in a sleepy manner
  • Antonym: energetically - in a lively, vigorous manner

Etymology

From "drowsy" + "-ly", forming the adverbial form.
