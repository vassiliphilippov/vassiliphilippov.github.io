incidentally

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪˈdentəli / (BrE)
Used to introduce a new or related topic, or to add information that is not directly relevant to the main subject.

Examples

Incidentally, only two did it before a home audience.
That, incidentally, might give you some idea of what Felix was like.

Synset Examples

incidentally, I won't go to the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by the way - used to introduce a new topic or add information

Etymology

From 'incident' + '-ally', modeled after 'accidentally'.

2. ADV

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈdɛn.t̬ə.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈden.təl.i / (BrE)
In a way that is of minor or subordinate importance; not central to the main issue.

Examples

We shall not be able entirely to pass over these connections to the East as we consider Ripe Geometric pottery, the epic and the myth, and the religious evolution of early Greece; the important point, however, is that these magnificent achievements, unlike those of later decades, were only incidentally influenced by Oriental models.

Synset Examples

these magnificent achievements were only incidentally influenced by Oriental models

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marginally - to a limited extent, slightly, or as a secondary matter

Etymology

From 'incidental' + '-ly'; 'incidental' from Medieval Latin 'incidentalis'.
