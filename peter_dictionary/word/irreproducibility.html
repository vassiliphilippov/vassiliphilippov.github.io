irreproducibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪɹˌiːpɹoʊˌdusəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌriːprəˌdjuːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible to reproduce or duplicate, especially when referring to experimental results.

Examples

No correlation between these variables and the irreproducibility of the results was found.
Some 80 reaction tubes from 13 manifold fillings were illuminated in the temperature range from 40 to 85 ` in a further endeavor to determine the cause of the irreproducibility and to obtain information on the activation energy and the effect of light intensity.

Synset Examples

he could not explain the irreproducibility of the results of his experiment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reproducibility - The quality of being able to be reproduced, especially experimental results.

Etymology

From 'ir-' (not) + 'reproducibility' (ability to be reproduced).
