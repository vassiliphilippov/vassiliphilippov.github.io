repatriate

1. VERB

/ riˈpeɪ.tri.eɪt / (AmE), / riˈpæ.tri.eɪt / (BrE)
to send someone back to their own country, especially against their will or by official order

Examples

All I do any more is think of ways to break my parole... ..so maybe they'd send me **back**.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deport - to expel someone from a country, often for legal reasons

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (back) + 'patria' (fatherland, native country).

2. VERB

/ riˈpeɪtriˌeɪt / (AmE), / riˈpætrieɪt / (BrE)
to allow or accept someone to return to their own country; to admit back into one's country

Examples

Now that the war is over, this boy, Enzo, they want to repatriate him back to Italy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deport - to expel someone from a country

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (back) + 'patria' (native land).
