recapture

1. VERB

/ riˈkæptʃɚ / (AmE), / riˈkæptʃə / (BrE)
to experience or feel (a feeling, quality, or condition) again after a period of absence

Examples

Because of the authentic homogeneity of his early Nationalistic materials, and his flair for orchestrations - his brilliant Petruchka, his savage Sacre du Printemps, his incisive Les Noces - the world kept hoping that he could recapture the historical direction for which his native talents were predisposed.
No matter how often a man goes back to the scenes of his youth and strength, they can never be recaptured again.
As another Thanksgiving draws near, let us take time out from the often hectic pace of our lives to try and recapture the feelings that filled the hearts of the Pilgrims on the first Thanksgiving.

Synset Examples

She could not recapture that feeling of happiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relive - to experience something again in one's mind
  • Synonym: recover - to regain possession or a state that was lost

Etymology

From re- (again) + capture, ultimately from Latin 'captura' (a taking).

2. VERB

/ riˈkæptʃər / (AmE), / riˈkæptʃə / (BrE)
to experience or bring back again (a feeling, idea, or quality) that existed in the past

Examples

It is obvious that the historian who seeks to recapture the ideas that have motivated human behavior throughout a given period will find the art and literature of that age one of his central and major concerns, by no means a mere supplement or adjunct of significant historical research.

Synset Examples

The author recaptures an old idea here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to remember or bring back into one's mind
  • Synonym: relive - to experience again in one's imagination or memory

Etymology

From re- (again) + capture, from Latin 'recaptura' (a retaking).
