rigorously

1. ADV

/ ˈrɪɡ.ə.rəs.li / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡ.ər.əs.li / (BrE)
In a very thorough and strict manner; with great attention to detail and discipline.

Examples

Argiento had been trained so rigorously by the Jesuits that Michelangelo was unable to change his habits: up before dawn to scrub the floors, whether they were dirty or not; water boiling on the fire for washing laundry every day, the pots scoured with river sand after each meal.

Synset Examples

he had been trained rigorously by the monks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strictly - in a strict or exact way, similar in meaning to 'rigorously'
  • Antonym: leniently - in a mild or permissive manner, the opposite of 'rigorously'

Etymology

From 'rigorous' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'rigorosus' (stiff, severe, rigorous).
