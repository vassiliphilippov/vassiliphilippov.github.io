blonde

1. NOUN

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
A woman or girl with pale or yellowish hair and often fair skin.

Examples

"Mor-ee-air-teeeee ", he shrieked, his white teeth grossly counterpointing those of the glittering blonde.
I let up on the accelerator, only to gradually reach again the 60 m. p. h. which would, I hoped, overhaul Herry and the blonde, and as there were cars whose drivers apparently had something more important to catch than had I, Mrs. Major Roebuck settled down to practicing on Corporal Johnson the kittenish wiles she would need when making her duty call on Colonel and Mrs. Somebody in Sante Fe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blond - man or boy with pale or yellowish hair

Etymology

From French *blonde* (feminine of blond), ultimately from Medieval Latin *blundus* (yellow, blond).

2. ADJ

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
Having very light or pale hair and often fair skin, typically used to describe people, especially women, with such features.

Examples

Suddenly a treble auto horn tootley-toot-tootled, and, thumbing hopefully, I saw emergent in windshield flash: red lips, streaming silk of blonde hair and - ah, trembling confusion of hope, apprehension, despair - the leering face of old Herry.

Synset Examples

blond Scandinavians
a house full of light-haired children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brunette - having dark brown or black hair
  • Synonym: blond - having very light or pale hair (variant spelling, especially for males)

Etymology

From French 'blonde' (feminine of 'blond'), from Old French, of Germanic origin.
