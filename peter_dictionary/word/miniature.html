miniature

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnətʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪətjʊə / (BrE)
A very small copy or model of something, especially one resembling the original in detail.

Examples

The ghetto, a miniature of Warsaw's, was emptied into the camp in the Majdan-Tartarski suburb called Majdanek.

Etymology

From Italian *miniatura* (small painting or illumination), from Latin *miniare* (to color with red lead).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪniətʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪətjʊə / (BrE)
very small; much smaller than usual, especially as a smaller version or replica of something.

Examples

And his eyes - those miniature sundials of variegated yellow - had not altered their expression or direction.
Looks like a miniature pickaxe.

Synset Examples

a miniature camera

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - extremely small
  • Synonym: mini - very small, sometimes specifically a smaller version of something standard
  • Antonym: huge - extremely large
  • Antonym: gigantic - very large, enormous

Etymology

From Italian *miniatura* (manuscript illumination), from Latin *miniare* (to color with cinnabar).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniəˌtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪətjʊə / (BrE)
A small, finely detailed painting or drawing, especially one included in a book such as an illuminated medieval manuscript.

Examples

George did wonderful complicated pen-and-ink drawings like something out of a medieval miniature: hundreds of delicate details crammed into an eight by ten sheet and looking as if they had been done under a jeweler's glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - a decorative illustration in a manuscript

Etymology

From Italian *miniatura* (manuscript illumination), from Latin *miniare* (to color with red lead).
