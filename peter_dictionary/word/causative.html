causative

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔzətɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzətɪv / (BrE)
(especially of a factor, agent, or condition) producing an effect, particularly as a cause of disease or a result.

Examples

Let us assume that it would be possible for an enemy to create an aerosol of the causative agent of epidemic typhus (Rickettsia prowazwki) over City A and that a large number of cases of typhus fever resulted therefrom.

Synset Examples

poverty as a causative factor in crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causal - relating to or acting as a cause

Etymology

From Medieval Latin causativus, from Latin causa (cause).
