intrinsic

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɪnsɪk / (BrE)
belonging to the essential nature of a thing; inherent

Examples

More potent a charm to bring back that time of life than this record of a few pictures and a few remembered facts would be a catalogue of the minutiae which are of the very stuff of the mind, intrinsic, because they were known in the beginning not by the eye alone but by the hand that held them.
No one will deny that such broad developments and transitions are of great intrinsic interest and the study of ideas in literature would be woefully incomplete without frequent reference to them.

Synset Examples

form was treated as something intrinsic, as the very essence of the thing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrinsic - not part of the essential nature of something; coming from outside
  • Synonym: inherent - existing as a permanent, essential part

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'intrinsecus' (inward, interior), from Latin 'intra' (within).
