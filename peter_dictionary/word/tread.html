tread

1. NOUN

/ trɛd / (AmE), / trɛd / (BrE)
a step in walking or running, especially as heard or felt

Examples

His eyes now fixed on White's solid figure, Warren could hear behind him the tread of another.
It was then that they heard the tread of one descending and, in some perturbation glancing up, saw the patronne coming towards them as they gained the landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footstep - the sound or act of a step made by the foot

Etymology

Old English 'tredan' (to step, tread) of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ trɛd / (AmE), / trɛd / (BrE)
to put your foot down on or in something, especially while walking; to step or walk in a specified way

Examples

"Well ", remarked one gentleman who had been forced to accompany his wife, "the good Lord can eat where angels fear to tread ".

Synset Examples

For fools rush in where angels fear to tread
step on the brake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step - to move by lifting and setting down each foot in turn while walking

Etymology

Old English 'tredan', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'treden' and German 'treten'.
