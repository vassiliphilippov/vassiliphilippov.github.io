masted

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæstɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstɪd / (BrE)
(of a ship) having or equipped with one or more masts, often used in combination (e.g. 'three-masted').

Examples

On April 17, 1610, the sturdy little three masted bark, Discovery, weighed anchor in St. Katherine's Pool, London, and floated down the Thames toward the sea.

Synset Examples

probably was so masted when she set forth
a three-masted bark
