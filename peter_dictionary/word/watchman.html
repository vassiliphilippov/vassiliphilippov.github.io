watchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃ.mən / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.mən / (BrE)
A person employed to guard a building, property, or area, especially at night.

Examples

The portwatchers were retired longshoremen and small time seafarers off towboats and barges who acted as watchmen on the wharves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - person who protects or oversees a place or people
  • Synonym: sentinel - a person or thing that stands watch

Etymology

From Middle English 'waccheman', from 'watch' + 'man'.
