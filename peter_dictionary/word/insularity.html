insularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈlær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sjʊˈlær.ə.ti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being isolated or detached, especially culturally or socially.

Examples

The Skopas people seemed to him of that breed of human beings whose insularity frees them from tragedy.

Synset Examples

the insulation of England was preserved by the English Channel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
  • Antonym: connectedness - state of being connected or linked

Etymology

From Late Latin insulāritās, from Latin insula (island).
