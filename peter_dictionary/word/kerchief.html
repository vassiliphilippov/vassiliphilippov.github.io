kerchief

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.tʃɪf / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tʃɪf / (BrE)
A square piece of cloth, usually folded into a triangle and worn over the head or around the neck.

Examples

She drank and pushed back her gingham bonnet to wet a kerchief and wipe her face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarf - A length of fabric worn around the neck or head for warmth, sun protection, cleanliness, fashion, or religious reasons.
  • Synonym: bandana - A large, usually brightly colored handkerchief, often worn as a head covering or neckerchief.

Etymology

From Old French *couvrechief*, from *couvrir* (to cover) + *chief* (head).
