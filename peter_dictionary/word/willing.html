willing

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
ready or prepared to do something, especially of one's own free choice; inclined or disposed to act.

Examples

I'm willing to stake my political career on it ".
But he had in Walter Hendl a willing conductor able only up to a point.
They are willing to settle, however, in anything that offers pheasants to shoot at and peasants to work at.
He is willing to sell it just to get it off his hands.
And the Jerebohms are more than willing to buy it.

Synset Examples

a willing participant
willing helpers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwilling - not ready or inclined to do something
  • Degree Higher: eager - strongly wanting to do or have something
  • Antonym: reluctant - hesitant or unwilling to do something

Etymology

From Old English *willung* (wish, will), from *willian* (to wish, will).
