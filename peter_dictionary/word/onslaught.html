onslaught

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌslɔt / (AmE), / ˈɒn.slɔːt / (BrE)
A sudden and violent attack; an intense and forceful onset of something unpleasant or difficult to deal with.

Examples

Bumblebees can thus suffer severely from the onslaughts of Psithyrus, the "cuckoo bumblebee "as it is called in some European countries.
After heavy rains and an onslaught of mice, snow fell on October 15, 1825, and remained on the ground through a winter so cold that the ice on the Red was five feet thick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • Synonym: assault - a violent physical or verbal attack

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *onslag* (“attack, assault”).
