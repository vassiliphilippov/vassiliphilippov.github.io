nibbed

1. ADJ

/ nɪbd / (AmE), / nɪbd / (BrE)
(of a pen) having a nib or writing point, especially of a specified kind.

Examples

"Please come down as soon as you conveniently can ", the upright letters stalked from the broad nibbed pen, "I have an important matter to discuss with you ".

Synset Examples

a broad-nibbed pen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nibless - lacking a nib (of pens)

Etymology

From 'nib' (the pointed end of a pen) + '-ed' suffix (forming adjectives from nouns).
