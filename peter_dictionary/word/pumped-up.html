pumped-up

1. ADJ

/ pʌmpt ʌp / (AmE), / pʌmpt ʌp / (BrE)
Feeling intense excitement or enthusiasm, often due to a surge of adrenaline.

Examples

As you can see, in this Push-Pull Super Set the entire chest back shoulder area is vigorously exercised in alternate sectors by alternate exercises, so the complete torso remains pumped-up all the time!
By this time Henri's entire chest back - lat shoulder area is pumped-up to almost bursting point, and Claude takes time to do a bit more pectoral front deltoid shaping work.

Synset Examples

we were really pumped up for the race
he was so pumped he couldn't sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excited - feeling or showing excitement
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety

Etymology

From 'pump up' (verb), meaning to increase pressure or intensity, often extended figuratively.
