perennially

1. ADV

/ pəˈrɛniəli / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəli / (BrE)
In a way that continues or recurs year after year; enduringly or continually over a long period.

Examples

But here we have a distinctly modern preoccupation; we want to know why that book has kept on selling the way it has; we want to know what is perennially new about Utopia.

Synset Examples

We want to know what is perennially new about the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continually - repeatedly over a long period
  • Synonym: periodically - at regular intervals

Etymology

From 'perennial' + '-ly'. 'Perennial' from Latin 'perennis' (lasting through the year).
