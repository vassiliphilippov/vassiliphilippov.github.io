magnify

1. VERB

/ ˈmæɡnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaɡnɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to make something appear larger than it is; to increase the size, amount, or importance of something

Examples

The observational data determining the concentration distribution have a range of error which is magnified in the extension into the micrometeorite region.
After a while, however, a person's mind gets fed up and that magnifies all of the disagreeable physical symptoms which go with that sort of an assignment.

Synset Examples

Her terror was magnified in her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlarge - to make something bigger
  • Antonym: diminish - to make or become smaller or less

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare', from 'magnus' (great, large) + '-ficare' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something appear larger, especially by using lenses or a microscope

Examples

These wavelengths are reflected in sequence through the specimen by the rotating mirror; the specimen is magnified by the microscope.

Synset Examples

blow up an image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlarge - to make larger in size or scale

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare', from 'magnus' (great) + 'facere' (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to exaggerate or represent something as greater or more important than it really is

Examples

Privately, he created and magnified an image of himself as a hired assassin.

Synset Examples

tended to romanticize and exaggerate this `gracious Old South' imagery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as being larger or greater than it really is
  • Antonym: understate - to represent something as less important or smaller than it really is

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare,' from 'magnus' (great) + 'facere' (to make).
