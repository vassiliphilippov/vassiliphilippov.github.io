drain

1. NOUN

/ dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
The act or process of making something empty by allowing liquid to run out.

Examples

As the camp emptied, it was refilled by a draining of the camps and towns around Lublin, then by deportees from outside Poland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emptying - the act of making something empty

Etymology

From Old English *drēahnian* (to drain, strain), related to Old Norse *drekkja* (to drown).

2. VERB

/ dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to flow off gradually from somewhere; to be removed slowly by flowing away.

Examples

Or you could wish your daddy would really do it - kill Gratt Shafer like he said when you all the time, all along, could feel the nerve draining out of him like air out of a punctured tire when you are on a muddy road alone and it is raining and at night.
The sink in the hinged panel above the bunk drains into the head and a five-gallon water tank is mounted on the bulkhead above the sink.

Synset Examples

The rain water drains into this big vat

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trickle - to flow in a small stream or drops

Etymology

From Old French *dreiner* (to drain), from Latin *draganare* (to drag, drain).

3. VERB

/ dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
to remove liquid from something, or cause it to become empty of liquid

Examples

Poach the apples in this syrup for twelve minutes, drain them, and cool.

Synset Examples

We drained the oil tank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty - remove everything from inside, especially liquid

Etymology

From Old English drēahnian (to strain, filter water), related to drēahn (to drip, leak).
