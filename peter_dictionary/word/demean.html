demean

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmin / (AmE), / dɪˈmiːn / (BrE)
to make someone or something lose their dignity or the respect of others

Examples

On the other hand, wifely supremacy demeans the husband, saps his self-respect, and robs him of his masculinity.

Synset Examples

She tends to put down younger women colleagues
His critics took him down after the lecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degrade - to treat or regard someone with contempt or disrespect
  • Synonym: humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish

Etymology

From Old French *demener* 'to conduct oneself', from Latin *deminere* (to lower, diminish), from *de-* (down) + *minuere* (to lessen).
