wink

1. NOUN

/ wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
A very short period of time.

Examples

Another source of NBC pride was its rare film clip of Bix Beiderbecke, but this view of the great trumpeter flew by so fast that a prolonged wink would have blotted out the entire glimpse.

Synset Examples

if I had the chance I'd do it in a flash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - a very brief period of time
  • Synonym: instant - an extremely short period of time
  • Antonym: eternity - an infinitely long time

Etymology

From Old English wincian (to wink, to blink).

2. VERB

/ wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a light) to gleam, flash, or glow for short periods at intervals; to appear and disappear in quick succession.

Examples

Houses winked as the cars rolled beside a little depot.
Kate had no idea what they were talking of, although she had seen the blue lights and strange fires burning and winking on the ridges at night, had heard horsemen on the River Road and hill trails through the nights till dawn.
Without an instant's transition, the green light that meant full fusion power winked on the board.

Synset Examples

The lights were flashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - to give off a sudden, brief burst of light
  • Synonym: glimmer - to shine with a faint, wavering light

Etymology

Old English 'wincian', meaning 'to blink' or 'to close the eyes quickly'.

3. VERB

/ wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
to signal or communicate something by briefly closing and opening one eye

Examples

The latter, thanking her for the coffee, had winked and muttered, "Sure' nuff, honey ".
I think I 've taken it about fifty times already "! winking at Joel's look of shock.
Pa was on his side, looking at me, his eyes winking, the hand that had hit me a fist in the pillow.
She held herself that way and turned her head towards them and laughed and winked.
"Imagine being able to laugh and wink when you 're like the top part of that picture frame at home ", one of them said.

Synset Examples

She winked at him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to close and open both eyes quickly
  • Antonym: stare - to look fixedly with eyes wide open

Etymology

From Old English wincian (to wink, blink), related to Dutch wenken (to nod, wink).

4. VERB

/ wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
to shut and open one eye quickly, often as a signal or to indicate something in a playful way; or to briefly shut both eyes involuntarily or deliberately

Examples

she asked, winking wildly.

Synset Examples

The TV announcer never seems to blink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to shut and open both eyes quickly

Etymology

Old English 'wincian', meaning 'to wink or blink'.
