cicada

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkeɪdə, sɪˈkɑdə / (AmE), / sɪˈkɑːdə / (BrE)
A stout-bodied insect with large transparent wings, known for the loud, high-pitched songs produced by males.

Examples

In Keng Kok, the City of Silkworms, the Prime Minister bought fried chickens and fried cicadas, and two notebooks for me.

Etymology

From Latin *cicada* (tree cricket, cicada).
