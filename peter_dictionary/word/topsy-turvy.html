topsy-turvy

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɑpsi ˈtɝvi / (AmE), / ˌtɒpsi ˈtɜːvi / (BrE)
In a state of utter disorder or confusion; upside down or chaotic.

Examples

Some psychologists, in fact, suggest that career bound husbands often are more to blame for topsy-turvy marriages than their wives.

Synset Examples

a disorderly pile of clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotic - completely confused or disordered
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged in a neat or regular way

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a reduplication based on "top" with playful inversion of syllables, first recorded in the 16th century.
