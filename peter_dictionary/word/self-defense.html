self-defense

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌself dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
The act of protecting oneself from physical harm or danger, especially by using force.

Examples

His friends advised that it would be only a question of time until either the Mexicans killed him by ambuscade or he would be compelled to kill them in self-defense, perpetuating the troubles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-protection - the act of protecting oneself
  • Antonym: assault - a violent physical attack

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'defense'.
