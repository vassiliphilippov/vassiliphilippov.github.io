damn

1. ADJ

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes vulgar) Used to emphasize or express annoyance or anger; used as an intensifier before nouns or adjectives.

Examples

I was the first to get my squad on the ball, and anybody thinkin it was easy is pretty damn dumb.
Damn good.
Why did n't the damn Reactionaries bump him off?
Did I start the damn war?
Did I start the damn war?

Synset Examples

oh, damn (or goddamn)!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - used for emphasis or to show annoyance (intensifier)

Etymology

From Old French 'damner', from Latin 'damnare' (to condemn, harm).

2. NOUN

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
A thing regarded as having very little or no value (typically used in negative constructions).

Examples

Not that he gave a damn what the grapevine sent out about Killpath's little speech on the comportment of platoon commanders.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuck - something of little value; a thing considered worthless
  • Synonym: red cent - a very small amount of money; a thing of little value

Etymology

From the verb 'damn', meaning to condemn, expressing worthlessness or contempt in this sense.

3. VERB

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
To wish harm or misfortune upon someone or something; to invoke condemnation or express strong disapproval.

Examples

"Damn you, Adams "- Jess was beginning to recover from his initial shock.
Damn his luck.
"Damn the world ", she thought.

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to call down harm or misfortune on

Etymology

From Latin 'damnare' (to condemn, to inflict loss upon).

4. ADJ

/ dæm / (AmE), / dæm / (BrE)
Used informally as an intensifier to express emphasis, annoyance, or admiration, often considered mildly vulgar.

Examples

All they found of him was a muddy set of prison clothes, a bar of soap and an old rock hammer damn near worn down to the nub.

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot
it's a blamed shame
a blame cold winter
not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blasted - used informally as an intensifier
  • Synonym: darn - used informally as a milder intensifier

Etymology

From Old French *damner*, from Latin *damnare* (to condemn).
