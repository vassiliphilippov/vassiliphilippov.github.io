exquisite

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskwɪzɪt, ˈɛkskwɪzɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskwɪzɪt, ˈɛkskwɪzɪt / (BrE)
Extremely intense or sharply felt; keenly experienced.

Examples

Done in the modern style of a "confession ", Fink tells in exquisite detail how he came to know, and, more important, love his mother's pet salamander, Alicia.

Synset Examples

suffered exquisite pain
felt exquisite pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute - sharp or severe in effect; intense
  • Antonym: mild - not intense or sharp; gentle

Etymology

From Latin exquisitus (carefully sought out), from exquirere (to seek out).
