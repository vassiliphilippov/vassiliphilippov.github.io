shawl

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔl / (AmE), / ʃɔːl / (BrE)
A large piece of cloth, often oblong, worn around the shoulders or head, typically for warmth or decoration.

Examples

When he was bent over behind the wheel of the station wagon, feeling in his trouser cuffs for the ignition key which he had dropped a moment before, she came out of the house with an enormous Rumanian shawl over her head, which she had bought in that country during one of their trips abroad, and handed him a clean handkerchief through the window.
She stood clutching her shawl around her shoulders until he had swung the car onto the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarf - a strip of fabric worn around the neck or head

Etymology

From Persian *shāl*, meaning 'draped garment'.
