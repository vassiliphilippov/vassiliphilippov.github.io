abundant

1. ADJ

/ əˈbʌndənt / (AmE), / əˈbʌndənt / (BrE)
present in large quantities; more than enough; plentiful

Examples

For this period, as for earlier centuries, pottery remains the most secure source; the ceramic material of the age is more abundant, more diversified, and more indicative of the hopes and fears of its makers, who begin to show scenes of human life and death.

Synset Examples

an abundant supply of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plentiful - existing in large amounts
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient in quantity; hard to find

Etymology

From Latin abundans, abundantis, present participle of abundare (to overflow).
