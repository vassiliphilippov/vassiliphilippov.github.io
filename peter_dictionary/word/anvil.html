anvil

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.vɪl / (AmE), / ˈæn.vɪl / (BrE)
a heavy iron or steel block with a flat top and typically a pointed end, used as a surface for shaping metal objects by hammering.

Examples

He was crouched over his anvil in the courtyard getting his chisels into trim, when a splinter of steel flew into his eye and imbedded itself in his pupil.

Etymology

From Old English 'anfilte', related to Old High German 'anefalz' and Latin 'incus' (meaning 'anvil').
