bankroll

1. VERB

/ ˈbæŋkˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkˌrəʊl / (BrE)
to provide the money needed for a project, venture, or person; to finance.

Examples

You did that because we bankrolled your casino and the Molinari family guaranteed his safety.

Synset Examples

Who will bankroll the restoration of the former East German economy?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - to provide funding for a person or project
  • Synonym: fund - to provide money for
  • Synonym: sponsor - to support financially

Etymology

From the noun 'bankroll', originally meaning a roll of banknotes, hence 'to provide money'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈbaŋk.rəʊl / (BrE)
a roll of paper money; the available money supply or resources of a person, organization, or business

Examples

Don't leave out his very large bankroll.

Synset Examples

he shot his roll on a bob-tailed nag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funds - available money or financial resources
  • Synonym: capital - wealth in the form of money or assets

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial establishment) + 'roll' (bundle of paper money), originally late 19th century American slang.
