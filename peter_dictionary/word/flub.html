flub

1. VERB

/ flʌb / (AmE), / flʌb / (BrE)
to do something badly or make a careless mistake; to botch or blunder

Examples

But he also achieved something that endeared him to every duffer who ever flubbed a shot.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: bungle - to do something clumsily or ineptly
  • Antonym: succeed - to achieve a desired aim or result

Etymology

Perhaps a variant of 'flubdub,' American slang for 'nonsense,' or imitative in origin.
