undiminished

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dəˈmɪn.ɪʃt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈmɪn.ɪʃt / (BrE)
Not reduced or lessened; remaining at the same level or intensity.

Examples

The tradition reached its apex, perhaps, in the works of Thomas Nelson Page toward the end of the century, and reappeared undiminished as late as 1934 in the best-selling novel So Red the Rose, by Stark Young.
Recklessly hurling the bronc sidewise into an intersecting draw, he plunged forward with undiminished speed.

Synset Examples

unrelieved suffering

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diminished - Reduced or made smaller
  • Antonym: reduced - Made smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'diminished' (past participle of diminish).
