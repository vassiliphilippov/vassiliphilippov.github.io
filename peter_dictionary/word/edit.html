edit

1. VERB

/ ˈɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪt / (BrE)
to prepare written material for publication or presentation by correcting, revising, or adapting the content

Examples

Young Morris, who, while attending the University of Pennsylvania, also taught and edited a paper, found time to write Henrietta twenty page letters on everything that engaged his interest, from the acting of Sarah Bernhardt in Philadelphia to his reactions to the comments of "Sulamith "on the Jewish reform movement being promulgated by the Hebrew Union College in Cincinnati.

Synset Examples

Edit a book on lexical semantics
she edited the letters of the politician so as to omit the most personal passages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revise - to reconsider and alter something, especially written text
  • Degree Higher: publish - to prepare and issue for public distribution

Etymology

From Latin editus, past participle of edere (to put forth, bring out, publish).
