vigor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
Physical strength, energy, or forcefulness in action or movement.

Examples

For this does not account for the integral, elemental power of that which grows with abounding vigor as the play unfolds, nor does it explain the strange numinous sense of presentness which comes over those who watch the play like a spell.

Synset Examples

he plays tennis with great energy
he's full of zip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'vigorem' (liveliness, activity, force).
