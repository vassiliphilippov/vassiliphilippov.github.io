outdo

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈdu / (AmE), / aʊtˈduː / (BrE)
to do something better than someone else; to surpass or excel

Examples

It is greatly to Berman's credit that he made no attempt to outdo Ray Bolger.
This is n't surprising when we consider that over 29 percent of the 11 - year old boys in America cannot chin themselves once, and that English school girls outdo them in almost every test (even dashes and endurance).

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surpass - to be greater than or go beyond
  • Synonym: excel - to be exceptionally good at or proficient in an activity
  • Synonym: beat - to defeat or do better than someone

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'beyond') and 'do'.
