bombproof

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌpruːf / (BrE)
A reinforced structure, often underground, designed to protect people from bombing attacks, usually equipped with living supplies and used during air raids.

Examples

The bombproof was a low-ceilinged structure of heavy timbers covered with earth.
Watson had presented his pouch and been led to the bombproof.
He had hoped to be alone in the bombproof, but the soldier had followed him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb shelter - A structure built to protect people from bombs, especially during air raids

Etymology

From 'bomb' + 'proof', indicating ability to withstand bombs; originally used in military contexts.
