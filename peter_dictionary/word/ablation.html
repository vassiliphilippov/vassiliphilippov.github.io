ablation

1. NOUN

/ əˈbleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈbleɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a body part, tissue, or harmful material from the body

Examples

The anode ablation could be reduced to a negligible amount.
The high heat fluxes existing at the electrode surfaces of electric arcs necessitate extensive cooling to prevent electrode ablation.

Etymology

From Latin ablatio (a removal), from ablatus, past participle of auferre (to carry away).
