Holland

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑː.lənd / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.ənd / (BrE)
A region and former province on the western coast of the Netherlands, commonly used in English to refer to the entire country of the Netherlands, though technically only part of it.

Examples

Finally, if the mission of the Trial was to convict anti-Semitism, how could it have failed to post before the world the contrasting fates of the countries in which the Final Solution was aided by native Jew haters - i.e., Germany, Poland, Hungary, Czechoslovakia - and those in which it met the obstacle of human solidarity - Denmark, Holland, Italy, Bulgaria, France?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Netherlands - the country of which Holland is a region

Etymology

From Old Dutch *holt land* ('wood land'), referring originally to the woodland region.
