overdrive

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈdraɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdraɪv / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to work, perform, or be driven excessively hard or beyond normal capacity

Examples

In vying with him, Mr. D' Albert also seemed to be overdriving his tone.

Synset Examples

The teacher is overworking his students
Overdriving people often suffer stress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwork - to work too hard or cause to work too hard
