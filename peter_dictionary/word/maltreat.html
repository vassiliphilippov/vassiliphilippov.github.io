maltreat

1. VERB

/ mælˈtrit / (AmE), / mælˈtriːt / (BrE)
to treat someone or something badly or cruelly, especially by causing physical or emotional harm

Examples

Well, ordinarily he is, except (as the Wheel of the Law specifies) toward impious folk who steal, disturb, or maltreat the Presence.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistreat - to treat someone or something badly
  • Synonym: abuse - to use something or treat someone in a harmful way
  • Synonym: ill-treat - to treat someone or something in a cruel or unkind way

Etymology

From French 'maltraiter', from 'mal' (badly) + 'traiter' (to treat).
