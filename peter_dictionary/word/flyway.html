flyway

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌweɪ / (BrE)
A route regularly used by large numbers of migratory birds when traveling between breeding and wintering grounds.

Examples

The site may be on one of the major flyways of migratory birds or have its own resident bird life.

Etymology

Formed from 'fly' + 'way', meaning a path or route for flying.
