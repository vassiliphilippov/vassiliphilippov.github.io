felon

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛlən / (AmE), / ˈfɛlən / (BrE)
A person who has been convicted of a serious crime, especially a felony.

Examples

When Mayor Dever heard of the banquet he summoned Hughes for an explanation of why he had been dishonouring the police department by consorting with these felons and fixers.
You are convicted felons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime

Etymology

From Old French 'felon' (evil-doer), from Medieval Latin 'fellō' (wicked person).
