forth

1. ADV

/ fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
(literary) out from a starting point and onward into view, activity, or existence.

Examples

Greek civilization was swirling toward its great revolution, in which the developed qualities of the Hellenic outlook were suddenly to break forth.
The letters which poured forth from camps were usually written under adverse circumstances.

Synset Examples

ran away from the lion
wanted to get away from there
sent the children away to boarding school
the teacher waved the children away from the dead animal
went off to school
they drove off
go forth and preach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - toward a position ahead or onward

Etymology

From Old English 'forð', related to German 'fort' and Dutch 'voort'.
