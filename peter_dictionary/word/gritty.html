gritty

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɪti / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪti / (BrE)
having a texture made up of or covered with small, hard particles; grainy or granular in consistency.

Examples

Below in the open bay facing Manhattan was Staten Island, gritty with clam shells and mud flats behind which nested farms, cattle barns, and berry thickets.

Synset Examples

granular sugar
the photographs were grainy and indistinct
it left a mealy residue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grainy - having a texture of small, grain-like particles
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface without roughness or projections

Etymology

From grit (small particles of stone) + -y (adjective suffix).
