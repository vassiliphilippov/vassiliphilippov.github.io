trend

1. NOUN

/ trɛnd / (AmE), / trɛnd / (BrE)
a general direction in which something is changing or developing

Examples

Zodiacal light and the gegenschein give some evidence for such a dust blanket, a phenomenon also to be expected if the dust before capture is in circular orbits about the sun, as indicated by the trend of the smaller visible meteors.
In an attempt to reverse the downhill trend by stimulating the bone marrow and controlling any hemolytic component, triamcinolone, 16 mg. daily, was begun on Sept. 26, 1958, and continued until Feb. 18, 1959.
The trends have been in evidence for many years - population shifts to the Southwest and Far West, and from city to suburbs.
Have you examined these trends, forecast the effects, and planned your marketing strategy to compete effectively under changing circumstances?
In the area of private label competition, it is logical to expect a continuation of trends which have been under way during the first decade.

Synset Examples

the shoreward tendency of the current
the trend of the stock market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tendency - an inclination toward a particular direction or course
  • Synonym: drift - a gradual shift in position or opinion

Etymology

From Middle English 'trenden' (to turn, revolve), from Old English 'trendan'.

2. NOUN

/ trɛnd / (AmE), / trɛnd / (BrE)
the general direction or course in which something moves, develops, or lies

Examples

Do you know the trend in your cost of maintaining access roads and parking lots?

Synset Examples

the river takes a southern course
the northeastern trend of the coast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: course - the path, direction, or route something follows
  • Synonym: direction - the line or path along which something moves, lies, or points

Etymology

From Old English 'trendan' (to revolve, roll), related to Middle English 'trenden' (to turn, revolve).

3. NOUN

/ trɛnd / (AmE), / trɛnd / (BrE)
a general direction in which something develops, changes, or moves, especially a gradual tendency; a prevailing tendency or inclination

Examples

Virtually everyone agrees, however, that the trend toward female sexual aggressiveness was tremendously accelerated with the postwar rush to the suburbs.

Synset Examples

not openly liberal but that is the trend of the book
a broad movement of the electorate to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - a general tendency or course of events
  • Synonym: pattern - a regular and repeated way in which something happens or is done

Etymology

From Middle English 'trenden' (to turn or revolve), from Old English 'trendan'.
