infirmity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrməti / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːməti / (BrE)
physical or mental weakness, especially as a result of age or illness.

Examples

And then the questions came, eager, interested questions, and many compliments on his having overcome his infirmity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frailty - the condition of being physically weak and delicate

Etymology

From Latin 'infirmitas' (weakness), from 'infirmitas', from 'in-' (not) + 'firmus' (strong).
