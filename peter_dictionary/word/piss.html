piss

1. VERB

/ pɪs / (AmE), / pɪs / (BrE)
to discharge urine from the body; to urinate (very vulgar or informal)

Examples

I 've got a quarter of a million Germans in my state, and those krautheads tune in on Father Werther every night, and if he tells them to go out and piss in the public square, that's what they do.

Synset Examples

Again, the cat had made on the expensive rug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urinate - to discharge urine from the body

Etymology

From Middle English 'pissen', from Old French 'pissier', from Vulgar Latin *pissiare (to urinate), imitative of the sound of urination.

2. NOUN

/ pɪs / (AmE), / pɪs / (BrE)
(informal, vulgar) An act of urinating.

Examples

I'’d give four million just to be able to take a piss without it hurting.

Synset Examples

he took a pee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pee - informal term for urination
  • Synonym: urination - the act of discharging urine

Etymology

From Middle English *pissen*, from Old French *pissier*, of imitative origin.
