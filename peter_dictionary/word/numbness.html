numbness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌmnəs / (AmE), / ˈnʌmnəs / (BrE)
A partial or complete lack of physical sensation in a part of the body, often caused by nerve damage or dysfunction.

Examples

writes: Does numbness in the left hand at night, which awakens the person, indicate brain tumor?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paresthesia - abnormal skin sensations such as tingling or prickling

Etymology

From 'numb' + '-ness'; 'numb' from Middle English 'nom', from Old English 'numen' (past participle of 'niman', to take), meaning 'taken, seized, deprived of sensation'.
