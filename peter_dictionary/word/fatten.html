fatten

1. VERB

/ ˈfætən / (AmE), / ˈfætən / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) fatter or plumper, usually by feeding them more or specially; to increase the size, profit, or supply of something.

Examples

Rather, it is typical of the thousands of quacks who use phony therapeutic devices to fatten themselves on the miseries of hundreds of thousands of Americans by robbing them of millions of dollars and luring them away from legitimate, ethical medical treatment of serious diseases.

Synset Examples

We will plump out that poor starving child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slim - to make or become thin or thinner
  • Synonym: plump - to make or become fuller or rounder in shape

Etymology

From Old English *fættian* (to make fat), from *fætt* (fat, plump).
