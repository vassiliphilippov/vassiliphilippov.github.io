verdict

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
the decision or finding made by a jury in a court of law regarding the facts of a case

Examples

How could supplying Eichmann with a platform on which to maintain that one could collaborate in the murder of millions of Jews without being an anti-Semite contribute to a verdict against anti-Semitism?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - the official decision or order of a court following a trial

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *verdit* (truth), from Old French *verdit* (decision), from Latin *verum* (truth) + *dictum* (saying).
