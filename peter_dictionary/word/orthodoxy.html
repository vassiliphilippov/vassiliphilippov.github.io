orthodoxy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.θəˌdɑk.si / (AmE), / ˈɔː.θəˌdɒk.si / (BrE)
The quality or practice of conforming to established or traditional beliefs, especially in religion.

Examples

However, they were hostile to Jewish Orthodoxy, professing to believe in Judaism "but in a moderate way ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterodoxy - nonconformity to established beliefs or doctrines

Etymology

From Greek orthodoxia, from orthos (right, true) + doxa (opinion, belief).
