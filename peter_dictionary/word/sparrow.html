sparrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
A small, typically brownish or grey songbird with a short beak, found in many parts of the world and known for feeding on seeds and insects.

Examples

Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.

Etymology

Old English *spearwa*, of Germanic origin.
