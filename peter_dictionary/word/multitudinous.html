multitudinous

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltɪˈtjuːdənəs / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈtjuːdɪnəs / (BrE)
Very numerous; consisting of a great many parts, individuals, or items.

Examples

It is as large as all of art, interdependent, varied, multitudinous ".

Synset Examples

incalculable riches
countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars
untold thousands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too numerous to be counted
  • Synonym: innumerable - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: myriad - countless, extremely numerous

Etymology

From Latin 'multitudin-', stem of 'multitudo' (multitude) + '-ous'.
