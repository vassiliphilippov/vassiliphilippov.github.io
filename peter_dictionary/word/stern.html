stern

1. ADJ

/ stɝn / (AmE), / stɜːn / (BrE)
Serious and unrelenting, especially in enforcing rules or demanding obedience.

Examples

He was stern and overbearing with his flock, but obsequious and conciliatory with the whites, especially the rich who partly supported the church.
Three of these only were protected from us by stern commandment: the roses, whose petals might not be collected until they had fallen, to be made into perfume or rose tea to drink; the peonies, whose tight sticky buds would be blighted by the laying on of a finger, although they were not apparently harmed by the ants that crawled over them; and the poppies.

Synset Examples

an exacting instructor
a stern disciplinarian
strict standards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict - demanding that rules are obeyed, not allowing for leniency
  • Antonym: lenient - permissive or tolerant, not severe or strict

Etymology

From Old English 'stearn' (severe, strict), related to Old Norse 'stirinn' (stiff, rigid).

2. ADJ

/ stɝn / (AmE), / stɜːn / (BrE)
having a serious, strict, or forbidding manner or appearance

Examples

The stern face would relax, the black clad arms would embrace him, "My son "!
Stern faced, he inspected the prisoners, satisfying himself that they were clean, well fed and comfortable within reason.

Synset Examples

an austere expression
a stern face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severe - very strict or harsh in manner
  • Antonym: gentle - kind or mild in manner or appearance

Etymology

From Old English *styrne* (severe, strict), related to Old High German *sterni* (strict).

3. NOUN

/ stɝn / (AmE), / stɜːn / (BrE)
the rear or back part of a ship or boat

Examples

The gilt sterns of the men-of-war becoming clearer to him, the sides of the wooden sea walls alternately painted yellow and black, the bronze cannon at the ports.
A news item described the launching of a ship: "Completing the ceremony, the beautiful movie star smashed a bottle of champagne over her stern as she slid gracefully down the ways into the sea ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bow - the front part of a ship or boat

Etymology

From Middle English *sterne*, from Old English *stiern* (steering part of a ship), related to steer (to guide).

4. ADJ

/ stɝn / (AmE), / stɜːn / (BrE)
Strict and severe; showing determination and unwillingness to change or be moved by pleas.

Examples

Sometimes he would grunt softly to some invisible onlooker beside him, sometimes he would look stern and moralistic as his pencil did what he disapproved.

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lenient - tolerant, not strict or harsh
  • Synonym: grim - stern and unrelenting in manner or appearance
  • Synonym: relentless - persistently severe or strict

Etymology

From Old English *styrne* (severe), related to German *stirn* (forehead).
