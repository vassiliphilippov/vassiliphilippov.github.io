sizzling

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪzlɪŋ / (BrE)
Very hot; hot enough to produce a hissing sound, or intensely hot or exciting.

Examples

Sizzling temperatures and hot summer pavements are anything but kind to the feet.
The most beautiful bed of pansies I 've seen was in a South Dakota yard on a sizzling day.

Synset Examples

a sizzling steak
a sizzling spell of weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorching - extremely hot
  • Synonym: blistering - intensely hot
  • Antonym: cool - moderately cold, not warm or hot

Etymology

From the verb 'sizzle', echoic of the sound produced by frying or burning.
