mortar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
a short, portable muzzle-loading cannon that fires explosive shells at high angles and short ranges, used especially in warfare

Examples

Then we pull out under our mortar and artillery cover, but nobody pulls out until I say so.
Amid the crackle of small arms and automatic weapons, I heard the thumping of mortars.
Mortars had demolished the defense ministry and set fire to the American Embassy next door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howitzer - a type of artillery piece with a barrel shorter than a cannon's, for firing shells at moderate angles and ranges
  • Degree Higher: cannon - a large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels, firing projectiles with a flat trajectory

Etymology

From Old French *mortier*, from Latin *mortarium* (bowl for pounding); the sense of the weapon relates to its shape, like a bowl.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
A mixture of lime or cement, sand, and water used to bond bricks or stones together or to cover walls.

Examples

Mortar is poured between this bracing and the 4 - inch blocks on edge to complete the wall thickness for radiation shielding.
The walls are held together with metal ties placed in the wet mortar as the walls are built.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cement - powdered material used as a binder in mortar and concrete

Etymology

From Old French *mortier*, from Latin *mortarium* (bowl for mixing).

3. VERB

/ ˈmɔrtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
to fasten or cover with mortar, especially in building or construction

Examples

The outside 4 - inch space is filled by mortaring blocks on edge.
The roof blocks are in two layers and are not mortared together.
When the roof blocks are all in place, the final rows of wall blocks are mortared into position.

Synset Examples

mortar the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - to cover a surface, especially walls, with plaster or a similar material
