mutiny

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtəni / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪni / (BrE)
an open rebellion against authority, especially by soldiers or sailors against their commanding officers

Examples

On the third voyage, a near mutiny rising from a quarrel between Dutch and English crew members on the Half Moon had almost forced him to head the ship back to Amsterdam in mid Atlantic.
Not a man on the brig, loyal or villainous, could be unaffected by the sight of seven men involved in the crime of mutiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - an act of violent or open resistance to an established government or ruler

Etymology

From Old French *mutiner* 'to revolt', from *mutin* 'insurgent, rebellious', from Middle French.
