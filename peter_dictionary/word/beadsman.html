beadsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbidzmən / (AmE), / ˈbiːdzmən / (BrE)
A man who was paid or supported to pray for the souls of others, especially in a religious or charitable institution; an almsman.

Examples

Alas, all that belonged to the age of "Devil Dashwood "and "Wicked Wilkes ", abbot and beadsman of the Order!

Etymology

From 'bead' (in the sense of prayer) + 'man'; Middle English 'bede' (prayer) relates to Old English 'bēd'.
