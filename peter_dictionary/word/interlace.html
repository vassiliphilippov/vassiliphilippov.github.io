interlace

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈleɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈleɪs / (BrE)
to twist, weave, or wind together two or more things so that they cross each other

Examples

A bevy of interlacing lagers, work camps, concentration camps erupted in the area.
But it is the wooden sculpture from Bali, the one representing two men with their heads bent backward and their bodies interlaced by a fish, that I particularly call to your attention.
Furthermore, the network in Figure 3 is only the basic net through which other networks pertaining to logistics and the like are interlaced.

Synset Examples

intertwine the ribbons
Twine the threads into a rope
intertwined hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intertwine - to twist or twine together closely
  • Synonym: weave - to interlace threads or strips to form fabric or a pattern
  • Antonym: untangle - to separate things that have been twisted together

Etymology

From Old French entrelacer, from entre- (between) + lacer (to lace).
