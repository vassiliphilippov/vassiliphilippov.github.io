informer

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a person who secretly gives information about someone, especially to the authorities, often in return for money or other reward

Examples

Get our informers to find out where it's going to be held.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes wrongdoing within an organization, especially to the public or authorities
  • Synonym: snitch - a person who gives information about others, especially for personal gain or to the authorities (informal, often derogatory)

Etymology

From Old French 'enformer', derived from Latin 'informare' (to inform, shape).
