insurance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
An arrangement in which a company provides financial protection or reimbursement to individuals or organizations in the event of specified loss, damage, illness, or death, usually in exchange for regular payments (premiums).

Examples

When did you last compare your present premium costs with the costs of insurance from other sources?
The blue-collar worker thus knows his insurance package, for example, costs $ 227.72.
Have the insurance company or your own accounting department break down the cost of your insurance package periodically.
Use deductable insurance wherever feasible.
It can put an end to marginal claims which play havoc with your insurance rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assurance - a similar arrangement or promise of payment, especially in British English

Etymology

From insure + -ance, ultimately from Old French *ensurer* (to make sure) and Latin *securus* (safe).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
A written contract or certificate that provides evidence of insurance coverage.

Examples

Do your employees understand all the benefits to which your insurance entitles them?
Do n't go overboard on insurance that pays benefits only upon death.

Synset Examples

you should have read the small print on your policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policy - A written document detailing the terms of an insurance contract

Etymology

From Middle English 'ensurance', from Old French 'ensurance' (assurance), from 'ensurer' (to assure).
