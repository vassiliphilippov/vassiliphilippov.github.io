detection

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the action of discovering or identifying the presence or existence of something, especially something difficult to perceive.

Examples

The study of the radio emission of the moon and planets began with the detection of the thermal radiation of the moon at 1.25 - cm wave length by Dicke and Beringer (1946).
One of the most gratifying applications of an important technique of submarine detection is in the exploration of the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discovery - the act of finding or learning something for the first time
  • Antonym: concealment - the action of hiding or keeping something secret

Etymology

From Latin detectio (uncovering), from detegere (to uncover).
