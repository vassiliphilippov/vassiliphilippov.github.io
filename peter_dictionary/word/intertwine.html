intertwine

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to twist or wind two or more things together, or to become twisted together

Examples

He was not sure how much of this desire was due to his devotion to the church and how much was his own ego, demanding to be satisfied, for the two were intertwined and could not be separated.
When Siamese cats are intertwined it is difficult to tell where one leaves off and another begins.

Synset Examples

intertwine the ribbons
Twine the threads into a rope
intertwined hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entwine - to twist or wind together (especially closely and intricately)

Etymology

From inter- (between, together) + twine (to twist), influenced by Latin inter (between) and English twine.
