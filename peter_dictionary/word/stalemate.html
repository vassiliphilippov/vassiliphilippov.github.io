stalemate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪlˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlmeɪt / (BrE)
a situation in which neither side in a dispute or contest can make progress or win, resulting in a deadlock

Examples

- No more stalemate.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made; a dead end
  • Synonym: impasse - a situation with no escape or progress possible; dead end
  • Antonym: breakthrough - an instance of achieving significant progress; overcoming a deadlock

Etymology

Originally a chess term from 'stale' (obsolete: stalemate, deadlock) and 'mate' (checkmate).
