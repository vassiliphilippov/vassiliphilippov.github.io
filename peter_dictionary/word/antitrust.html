antitrust

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈtrʌst / (BrE)
Relating to laws or actions intended to prevent unfair competition and monopolies in trade and commerce.

Examples

The threat of effective anti-trust action, provoked by "gouging the public "through price increases not justified by cost increases, and fears of endangering relations with customers, Congress, the general public and the press, all operate to keep price increases in some relation to cost increases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monopoly - exclusive control of a commodity or service
  • Synonym: competition law - the body of law promoting or maintaining market competition

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'trust' (as in 'business trust', a monopolistic company or group).
