coaxial

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈæksiəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈæksiəl / (BrE)
(of objects or wires) having a common axis; placed on or around the same straight line.

Examples

Argon was also blown at low velocities (mass flow rate f) through a tube coaxial** with the cathode as an additional precaution against contamination of the arc by air.

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (together) + 'axis' (axle), referring to sharing the same axis.
