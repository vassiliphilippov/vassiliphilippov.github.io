engineering

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
The branch of science and technology concerned with the design, building, and use of engines, machines, and structures, especially as applied in industry and commerce.

Examples

Like the recent Scheherazade from London (High Fidelity, Sept. 1961), it is successful because emphasis has been placed on good musical and engineering practices rather than on creating sensational effects.
The engineering as such never obtrudes upon your consciousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technology - the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes

Etymology

From engineer + -ing; engineer from Old French 'engigneor', from Latin 'ingeniator' (contriver, deviser).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of science and technology concerned with the design, building, and use of engines, machines, and structures by applying scientific principles to practical problems

Examples

The engineering provides exactly the support needed for such a result.

Synset Examples

he had trouble deciding which branch of engineering to study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering science - scientific study underlying engineering practice
  • Synonym: technology - application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes

Etymology

From engineer + -ing, originally from Old French 'engigneor' (constructor of military engines), from Latin 'ingenium' (talent, device).
