laudanum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔdənəm / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdənəm / (BrE)
A solution of opium in alcohol, formerly used as a narcotic painkiller.

Examples

It had been five days too late before he learned that she 'd gone through the wedding ceremony in a semitrance of laudanum, administered by her mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opium - a narcotic drug obtained from the opium poppy

Etymology

From Latin 'laudanum', possibly coined by Paracelsus, based on Latin 'laudare' (to praise).
