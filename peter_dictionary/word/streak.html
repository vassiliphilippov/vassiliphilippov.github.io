streak

1. NOUN

/ striːk / (AmE), / striːk / (BrE)
A continuous series of similar events, especially successes or failures, without interruption.

Examples

It was Nischwitz' third straight victory of the new season and ran the Grizzlies' winning streak to four straight.

Synset Examples

had a streak of bad luck
Nicklaus had a run of birdies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - a continuous period or series of the same thing, such as success or failure

Etymology

From Middle English *strek*, from Old English *strica* ('stroke, line').

2. NOUN

/ stɹik / (AmE), / stɹiːk / (BrE)
A distinctive or prominent quality, especially one that is part of someone's character or personality.

Examples

The seventh man was Red Hogan, a wiry little puncher with a wild streak and a liking for hell raising.

Synset Examples

he has a stubborn streak
a streak of wildness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing quality or characteristic
  • Synonym: characteristic - a typical feature or quality

Etymology

From Old English 'strica' (a line, stroke), influenced by Middle Dutch 'streke'.

3. VERB

/ stɹik / (AmE), / stɹiːk / (BrE)
to move very quickly in a straight line; to flash past

Examples

At 12000 feet, Fiedler signaled "release ", and started the roaring pulse-jet engine - then streaked away from beneath the Heinkel.
Here in the cool darkness Mr. Podger could still feel the warmth of midday, could still see the yellow butterflies dancing over the road, could still see the friendly grin on the young, sun-browned face as the driver looked back over his shoulder for a moment before the car streaked out of sight.

Synset Examples

The plane streaked across the sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dart - to move or shoot out suddenly and quickly in a straight line
  • Synonym: zip - to move very quickly
  • Synonym: race - to move at great speed
