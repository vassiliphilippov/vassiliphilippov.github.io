prototype

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtətaɪp / (BrE)
a first or preliminary version of a device or vehicle from which other forms are developed, or a typical example of a class or category

Examples

A prototype which fulfills the requirements was developed and thoroughly tested.
It is expected that in 1963 two prototype aircraft will be available for flight testing.

Synset Examples

he is the prototype of good breeding
he provided America with an image of the good father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetype - an original model or pattern after which other things are patterned

Etymology

From French *prototype*, from Late Latin *prototypon* (original form), from Greek *prōtotypon*, from *prōtos* (first) + *typos* (model, type).
