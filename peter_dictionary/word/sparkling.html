sparkling

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑrk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑː.klɪŋ / (BrE)
Shining with bright, flashing points of light resembling stars or glitter.

Examples

Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.
A very great Pope, this one, the old woman explained, her black eyes sparkling.

Synset Examples

sparkling snow
sparkling eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shining - emitting or reflecting bright light
  • Antonym: dull - not bright or shiny; lacking luster

Etymology

Present participle of 'sparkle', from Middle English 'sperklen', a frequentative of 'spark'.
