afraid

1. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
(used to soften an unpleasant statement) used to express regret or polite concern about something, especially when delivering bad news or a negative reply.

Examples

The first substantially complete stereo Giselle (and the only one of its scope since Feyer's four-sided LP edition of 1958 for Angel), this set is, I'm afraid, likely to provide more horrid fascination than enjoyment.
The young banker looked at him with a certain surprise, and then he said flatly: "I'm afraid I can't tell you anything in particular about Kent House.
"I am afraid there is not much to amuse small children here.

Synset Examples

I'm afraid I won't be able to come
he was afraid he would have to let her go
I'm afraid you're wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorry - used to express apology or regret

Etymology

From Old English āfrǣdan, possibly influenced by Middle English affray (frighten), from Old French effraier.

2. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
feeling fear or worry about something or someone; filled with apprehension.

Examples

If, however, the child is weak, overweight, or afraid, more help will be needed.
We get some clue from a few remembrances of childhood and from the circumstance that we are probably not much more afraid of people now than man ever was.
We are not now afraid of atomic bombs in the same way that people once feared comets.
Time to become afraid.
By the time he was prosperous enough - his goals were high - he was bald and afraid of women.

Synset Examples

afraid even to turn his head
suddenly looked afraid
afraid for his life
afraid of snakes
afraid to ask questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scared - feeling frightened or worried
  • Synonym: fearful - experiencing or showing fear
  • Antonym: brave - having or showing courage
  • Antonym: fearless - without fear; very brave

Etymology

From Old English æfǣred, past participle of āfǣran (to terrify).

3. ADJ

/ əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
worried or concerned about something that might happen, or feeling insecure.

Examples

In I wanted to tell him, but I was afraid to the final to is lightly stressed because it represents to tell him.
It'’s not his words I'’m afraid of.

Synset Examples

She was afraid that I might be embarrassed
terribly afraid of offending someone
I am afraid we have witnessed only the first phase of the conflict

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worried - feeling anxious or concerned about something
  • Antonym: confident - feeling sure about oneself or something

Etymology

From Old English āfrǣdan, related to 'frighten'.
