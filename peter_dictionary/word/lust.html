lust

1. NOUN

/ lʌst / (AmE), / lʌst / (BrE)
A very strong sexual desire or appetite.

Examples

The kiss outraged our friends but it was done and meanwhile had released in me all the remote, exciting premonitions of lust, all the mysterious sensations that I had imagined a truly consummated kiss would convey to me.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: desire - a strong feeling of wanting to have something
  • Synonym: passion - intense sexual or romantic feeling
  • Antonym: chastity - the state of refraining from sexual activity

Etymology

From Old English *lust* (desire, pleasure), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ lʌst / (AmE), / lʌst / (BrE)
Intense or uncontrolled sexual desire, regarded as one of the seven deadly sins in Christian tradition.

Examples

Old Order, acted and atonally sung by Grunnfeu Arapacis, the lovely Serbantian import, then entered and delivered the well-known invocation to the god Phineoppus, whereupon the stage is quite unexpectedly visited by a company of wandering Gorshek priests, symbolizing Love, Lust, Prudence and General Motors, respectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - a strong feeling of wanting to have something or wishing for something to happen
  • Antonym: chastity - the state or practice of refraining from extramarital, or especially from all, sexual intercourse; considered the opposing virtue to lust

Etymology

From Old English 'lust' (desire, pleasure), of Germanic origin.
