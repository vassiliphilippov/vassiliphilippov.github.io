licensed

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪsənst / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsənst / (BrE)
Officially given permission or approval by an authority to do, own, or use something.

Examples

Doris Hull, suffering from tuberculosis, was taken by her husband to see Otis G. Carroll, a sanipractor - a licensed drugless healer - in Spokane.
Ten students act as managers for a high-school football team, and of these managers a proportion p are licensed drivers.
They're licensed to carry firearms.

Synset Examples

an accredited college
commissioned broker
licensed pharmacist
authorized representative

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlicensed - not officially approved or permitted

Etymology

From 'license' (noun), from Latin 'licentia' (freedom, leave), from 'licere' (to be permitted).
