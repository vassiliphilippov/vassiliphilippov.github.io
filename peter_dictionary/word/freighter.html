freighter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪtə / (BrE)
A ship or boat designed for carrying goods rather than passengers; a cargo ship.

Examples

They hurled a cement barge against a freighter in Honolulu Harbor, knocked down telephone lines, marooned automobiles, flooded lawns, killed six cows.

Synset Examples

they did much of their overseas trade in foreign bottoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cargo ship - A ship that carries goods or cargo

Etymology

From 'freight' (goods transported in bulk) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
