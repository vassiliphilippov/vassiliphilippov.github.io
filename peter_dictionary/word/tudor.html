Tudor

1. PROPN

/ ˈtuː.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.də / (BrE)
The English royal dynasty that ruled from 1485 to 1603, including monarchs from Henry VII to Elizabeth I.

Examples

He suggests that Gore Court embodies all the glories of Tudor splendor.
The spaciousness of the Tudor cooking areas, for example, will provide needed space for the extra television sets required by modern butlers, cooks and maids.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Stuart - the royal house that succeeded the Tudors in Britain

Etymology

From the Welsh personal name *Tudur*, adapted as a surname by descendants of Ednyfed Fychan; Henry VII was the first monarch of the Tudor line.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtuː.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.də / (BrE)
Relating to a style of architecture in England during the late 15th and early 16th centuries, characterized by half-timbering, steeply pitched roofs, and elaborate masonry.

Examples

There remained a family named Kahler, owners of a two story Tudor style house on the south side of the Andrus home.

Synset Examples

half-timbered Tudor houses
Tudor furniture

Etymology

From the Tudor dynasty, which ruled England from 1485 to 1603.
