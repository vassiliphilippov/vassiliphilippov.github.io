stately

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪtli / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtli / (BrE)
Impressive in appearance or manner; dignified and majestic.

Examples

Martha picked up the hem of her gown and with eyes closed she slowly began to dance a stately minuet around the ballroom.

Synset Examples

a baronial mansion
an imposing residence
a noble tree
severe-looking policemen sat astride noble horses
stately columns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - Having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Synonym: imposing - Impressive in appearance or manner
  • Synonym: grand - Magnificent and impressive in appearance
  • Antonym: humble - Having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; unpretentious

Etymology

From Middle English statly, from state (“dignity”).
