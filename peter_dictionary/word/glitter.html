glitter

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪtə / (BrE)
to shine with a bright, shimmering, reflected light, often as if wet or covered with tiny points of light

Examples

That is, when Mr. Milstein thrust straight to the core of the music, sparks flying, bow shredding, violin singing, glittering and sometimes spitting, Mr. Hendl could go along.
Below the moon splashed world rolled away to insurmountable white peaks; above him the deep blue sky glittered with stars.
"Tee-wah ", the driver cackled, his black eyes glittering behind dull silver chicken fencing.
I walked to the right around it to buildings containing illuminated manuscripts and came to the Treasury, which houses such things as coffee cups covered with diamonds, jewelled swords, rifles glittering with diamonds and huge divan-like thrones as large as small beds, on which the sultans sat cross-legged.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glisten - to shine with a sparkling, reflected light, especially as if wet
  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine with bright flashes of light
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or shine; not reflecting light

Etymology

Middle English, from Old Norse glitra (to shine or sparkle), related to gleam and glisten.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪtə / (BrE)
A bright, shimmering reflected light; the quality of shining with brilliance or sparkle.

Examples

He thought of the old man laughing under the glitter of the great chandelier.
She smiled at Winston, and he saw the hateful hard glitter in her eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or faint appearance of light
  • Synonym: sparkle - to shine with brief flashes of light
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or shine

Etymology

From Middle English 'gliteren', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'glitra' (to glitter).
