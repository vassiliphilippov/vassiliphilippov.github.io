bilateral

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
Having identical or matching parts on each side of a central axis; relating to or affecting both sides.

Examples

The body was that of a well developed, somewhat debilitated white man weighing 108 lb. There were bilateral pterygia and arcus senilis, and the mouth was edentulous.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilateral - relating to, occurring on, or affecting only one side

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'lateralis' (of the side).
