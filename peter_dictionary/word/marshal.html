marshal

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
to arrange or assemble people, resources, or things in order or for a particular purpose; to prepare systematically for action

Examples

On Election Day - November 4 - he energetically marshalled his force of bludgeon men, bribers, and experts in forging repeat votes.

Synset Examples

marshal resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deploy - to move or arrange resources or people into position for action
  • Synonym: organize - to arrange systematically; to put in order
  • Antonym: disperse - to scatter or spread over a wide area

Etymology

From Old French 'marichal', from Frankish *marhskalk ('horse servant'), but the verb sense is later, developing from the noun 'marshal' (a person who arranges order or leads).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
a law officer with duties similar to those of a sheriff, especially one responsible for enforcing court orders

Examples

They reined in before the town marshal's office, a box sized building on Main Street.
"Let's go, Marsh al ", Brannon said, and took him by the arm.
"Put your gun on the desk, Marshal ".
Brannon said, "Now the key to the lockup, Marshal ".
Maybe if the marshal hears this himself, it 'll make a difference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheriff - an official responsible for keeping the peace and enforcing the law in a county

Etymology

From Old French 'mareschal', from Frankish '*marhskalk' (horse servant, stable keeper).
