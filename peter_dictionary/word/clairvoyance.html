clairvoyance

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɛrˈvɔɪəns / (AmE), / ˌkleəˈvɔɪəns / (BrE)
the supposed ability to gain information about an object, person, or event through means other than the known senses

Examples

Dreams present many mysteries of telepathy, clairvoyance, prevision and retrovision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepathy - supposed communication of thoughts by means other than the senses

Etymology

From French 'clairvoyance', from 'clair' (clear) + 'voyance' (seeing); related to 'clairvoyant'.
