dispossession

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspəˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
The action of depriving someone of land, property, or other possessions, especially through legal process.

Examples

If some future Khrushchev decided to rake up the misdeeds of his revered predecessor, would not the factory workers pass the same resolutions applauding his dispossession?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eviction - the action of expelling someone from property; similar process, usually by legal means
  • Synonym: expropriation - the action of taking away property, especially by the state

Etymology

From Old French 'despossessio(n)', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'possidēre' (to possess).
