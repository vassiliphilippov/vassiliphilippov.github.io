paean

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.ən / (AmE), / ˈpiː.ən / (BrE)
a song, speech, or written piece that expresses enthusiastic praise, tribute, or thanksgiving, especially in a formal or literary style

Examples

When East Germans fled to the West by the thousands, paeans of joy rose from the throats of Western publicists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encomium - a formal expression of high praise; eulogy
  • Synonym: panegyric - a public speech or published text in praise of someone or something
  • Antonym: dirge - a mournful song, especially for a funeral

Etymology

From ancient Greek παιάν (paian), a hymn of thanksgiving to Apollo or other gods.
