plastic

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstɪk / (BrE)
A synthetic material that can be molded into solid objects, films, or filaments, commonly used to make products such as containers, toys, and adhesives.

Examples

Such an instrument is expected to be especially useful if it could be used to measure the elasticity of heavy pastes such as printing inks, paints, adhesives, molten plastics, and bread dough, for the elasticity is related to those various properties termed "length ", "shortness ", "spinnability ", etc., which are usually judged by subjective methods at present.
Mr. Flannagan manufactured plastic tongue depressors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymer - a substance consisting of large molecules made up of many repeating units

Etymology

From Greek *plastikos* (fit for molding), from *plassein* (to mold).

2. ADJ

/ ˈplæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪk / (BrE)
capable of being shaped or molded, especially when soft; able to undergo a permanent change of form without breaking

Examples

(1) As far down as 400 miles below the surface the material should be hot enough to be plastic and adjust itself to twisting forces by sluggish flow rather than by breaking, as rigid surface rocks do.

Synset Examples

plastic substances such as wax or clay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleable - easily shaped or molded (often of metals or other substances)
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to be changed or shaped; stiff

Etymology

From Greek plastikos (fit for molding) via Latin plasticus.

3. ADJ

/ ˈplæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) capable of being shaped or molded; easily influenced or shaped.

Examples

But, as my plastic surgeon always said, "If you've got to go... "...go with a smile."

Synset Examples

a formative influence
a formative experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleable - easily shaped or bent
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to be bent or changed

Etymology

From Greek plastikos (fit for molding), from plassein (to mold or form).
