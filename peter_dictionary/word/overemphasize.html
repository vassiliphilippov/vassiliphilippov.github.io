overemphasize

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɛm.fəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɛmfəsaɪz / (BrE)
To give something more importance or attention than is necessary or appropriate.

Examples

The effects of earthquakes on civilization have been widely publicized, even overemphasized.
Mr. Paray resisted the temptation to over-emphasize the melodic elements of the score.

Synset Examples

I cannot overemphasize the importance of this book

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: emphasize - give special importance or prominence to something
  • Antonym: underemphasize - fail to give enough importance or attention to something

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) and 'emphasize', which derives from Greek 'emphainein' (to display, show).
