breeches

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈbriːtʃɪz / (BrE)
Short trousers that cover the hips and thighs and end just above or at the knees, traditionally worn by men in previous centuries.

Examples

The slit breeches had to go.

Etymology

From Old French *braies* (breeches), from Latin *bracae* (trousers).
