valve

1. NOUN

/ vælv / (AmE), / vɑːlv / (BrE)
A device in a brass wind instrument for changing the length of the air column to alter the pitch of the sound.

Examples

The orchestra is far enough away from you that you miss the bow scrapes, valve clicks, and other noises incidental to playing.

Etymology

From Latin 'valva' (leaf of a door).

2. NOUN

/ vælv / (AmE), / vɑːlv / (BrE)
a structure in a hollow organ, such as the heart, that has a flap to allow fluid to flow in one direction only and prevent backflow

Examples

This viewpoint is further supported by Verloop's (' 48) demonstration of thickened bronchial artery and arteriolar muscular coats which are capable of acting as valves.
The valves were normal except for thin yellow plaques on the inferior surface of the mitral leaflets.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin valva (leaf of a door, folding door).
