ordeal

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈdil / (AmE), / ɔːˈdiːl / (BrE)
A very difficult or unpleasant experience, especially one that lasts for a long time.

Examples

And I select this sentence as its pertinent summation: "In essence the drama of his (Eisenhower's) Presidency can be described as the ordeal of a nation turned conservative and struggling - thus far with but limited and precarious success - to give effective voice and force to that conservatism ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a test of patience, endurance, or character, especially through hardship
  • Synonym: test - a challenging situation to prove ability or endurance
  • Antonym: relief - the alleviation or removal of pain or distress; the state after an ordeal

Etymology

From Old English *ordāl* (judicial trial by ordeal), from Proto-Germanic *uzdailijaną* (to share out, allot).
