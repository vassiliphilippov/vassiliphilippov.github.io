organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an electronic musical instrument designed to simulate the sound of a pipe organ

Examples

Mary Dobbs Tuttle was back at the organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe organ - large musical instrument with pipes sounded by air
  • Degree Lower: electric piano - electronic keyboard instrument sounding similar to a piano

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a part of a living animal or plant that has a particular purpose or function, such as the heart or liver in animals, or a leaf or root in plants

Examples

A colossal campaign opened to sell more sexual organs of plants and Mrs. Joseph Shadow of Greatness Douglas was quoted as saying: "I would no more sit down without flowers on my table than without serviettes ".
For one thing, the organs of speech of the Ozagen natives differed somewhat from Earthmen's; the sounds made by these organs were, therefore, dissimilar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tissue - an ensemble of similar cells that together carry out a specific function; biological tissue
  • Degree Higher: system - a group of organs that work together to perform a complex function

Etymology

From Latin organum (instrument, tool), from Greek organon (implement, organ).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
an official body or agency of government or an organization that performs a specific function

Examples

The infamous Wansee Conference called by Heydrich in January 1942, to organize the material and technical means to put to death the eleven million Jews spread throughout the nations of Europe, was attended by representatives of major organs of the German state, including the Reich Minister of the Interior, the State Secretary in charge of the Four Year Plan, the Reich Minister of Justice, the Under Secretary of Foreign Affairs.

Synset Examples

The Census Bureau is an organ of the Commerce Department

Etymology

From Latin 'organum', meaning 'instrument' or 'tool', later used figuratively for parts of a body or organization.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
A large musical instrument, typically found in churches, in which sound is produced by air passing through a series of pipes controlled by one or more keyboards and pedals.

Examples

[DOOR SLAMMING] [GULLS CAWING] [SINGING IN ITALIAN] [CHURCH ORGAN PLAYING] [PRIEST RECITING IN LATIN] [SPEAKING LATIN] [UPBEAT MUSIC PLAYING] MAN: Did you bring the car keys?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonium - A free-reed keyboard instrument similar to a small organ
  • Synonym: piano - A large keyboard musical instrument with strings struck by hammers

Etymology

From Latin *organum* (instrument, tool), from Greek *organon* (implement, musical instrument).
