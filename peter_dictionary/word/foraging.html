foraging

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.ɪdʒ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪdʒ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of searching for food and provisions in the wild, especially by animals or people

Examples

Early in the war it was not uncommon for planters' sons to retain in camp Negro "body servants "to perform the menial chores such as cooking, foraging, cleaning the quarters, shining shoes, and laundering clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunting - the activity of pursuing and killing wild animals for food or sport
  • Synonym: gathering - the act of collecting food, especially wild plants or fruit, from the environment

Etymology

From 'forage', from Old French *forager* (to forage), from Late Latin *fodicare* (to dig, scratch).
