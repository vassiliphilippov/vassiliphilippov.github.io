mortician

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / mɔːˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
A person whose job is to arrange and manage funerals, including the preparation of the deceased for burial or cremation.

Examples

The Macmillan government might be willing to let him go, but he has been dead seventy-eight years and even the Soviet morticians could not make him look presentable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertaker - person whose job is to arrange funerals

Etymology

From Latin 'mort-' (death) + '-ician' (denoting a profession or practitioner).
