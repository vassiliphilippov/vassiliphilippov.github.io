systematically

1. ADV

/ ˌsɪstəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
In a methodical and organized way; following a fixed plan or system.

Examples

The argon flow through the porous anode was varied systematically between **f.
Separating, they took different sides of the main drag and systematically combed the bars.

Synset Examples

they systematically excluded women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methodically - in an orderly or systematic manner

Etymology

From 'systematic' + '-ally'; 'systematic' from French systématique, from Greek systematikos, from systema (system).
