hanker

1. VERB

/ ˈhæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈhæŋkə / (BrE)
To desire something strongly or persistently, especially something not easily attained.

Examples

Before we get through he 'll have the Blackfeet hankerin' for our hair and our goods.
I had always, I said, hankered after working hard with my hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something
  • Synonym: crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
  • Synonym: long - to want something very much, especially something unattainable or distant

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Dutch 'hunkeren' (to hanker, to long for).
