wean

1. VERB

/ wiːn / (AmE), / wiːn / (BrE)
To gradually stop feeding a baby or young mammal on its mother's milk and start giving it other food.

Examples

In West Africa, for example, where meat is a luxury and babies must be weaned early to make room at the breast for later arrivals, a childhood menace is kwashiorkor, or "red Johnny ", a growth-stunting protein deficiency (signs: reddish hair, bloated belly) that kills more than half its victims, leaves the rest prey for parasites and lingering tropical disease.

Synset Examples

she weaned her baby when he was 3 months old and started him on powdered milk
The kitten was weaned and fed by its owner with a bottle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaning - the process of accustoming an infant or young mammal to food other than its mother's milk

Etymology

Old English wenian ‘accustom, train’
