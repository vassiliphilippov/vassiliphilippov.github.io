virulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪr.jələnt / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.jʊ.lənt / (BrE)
Extremely poisonous or injurious; capable of causing serious harm or death, especially by the action of a toxin or venom.

Examples

In regard to Eichmann, it was to be found in the Nazi outlook, which contained a principle separate from and far worse than anti-Semitism, a principle by which the poison of anti-Semitism itself was made more virulent.

Synset Examples

venomous snakes
a virulent insect bite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venomous - producing venom; capable of injecting poison

Etymology

From Latin virulentus (full of poison), from virus (poison).
