ink

1. NOUN

/ ɪŋk / (AmE), / ɪŋk / (BrE)
A colored liquid used for writing, printing, or drawing.

Examples

In the South, after the first year of the war, paper and ink were very poor.
Another Reb writing under similar circumstances before Atlanta reported: "The Yankees keep Shooting so I am afraid they will knock over my ink, so I will close ".
The ink on your divorce isn'’t dry yet, and you'’re getting married?

Etymology

From Latin 'encaustum', from Greek 'enkauston', meaning 'burnt in', originally referring to purple or red ink used by Roman emperors.
