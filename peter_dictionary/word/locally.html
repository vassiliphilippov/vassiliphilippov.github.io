locally

1. ADV

/ ˈloʊkəli / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəli / (BrE)
In or related to a particular place or locality, rather than at a national or global level.

Examples

The old Morse system was replaced locally by the Simplex modern automatic method in 1929, when Ellamae Heckman (Wilcox) was manager of the Western Union office.
The selection of materials depends on skills of available labor for installation, cost of materials available locally, and your own preference.

Synset Examples

it was locally decided

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionally - in a particular region, rather than nationally or globally
  • Antonym: nationally - in relation to an entire nation
  • Antonym: globally - in relation to the whole world

Etymology

From 'local' + '-ly', formed from Latin 'localis' (relating to a place).
