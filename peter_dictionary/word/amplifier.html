amplifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.pləˌfaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈæm.plɪˌfaɪ.ə / (BrE)
An electronic device that increases the strength of electrical signals passing through it, especially in audio and radio equipment.

Examples

Three separate amplifiers then drive a 21 - inch tricolor tube.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attenuator - device that reduces the strength of signals

Etymology

From 'amplify' + '-er', meaning 'one that amplifies'.
