shred

1. VERB

/ ʃrɛd / (AmE), / ʃrɛd / (BrE)
To tear or cut something into long, thin strips; to break or cut into shreds.

Examples

That is, when Mr. Milstein thrust straight to the core of the music, sparks flying, bow shredding, violin singing, glittering and sometimes spitting, Mr. Hendl could go along.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or damage by force
  • Synonym: rip - to tear or split apart quickly or forcefully

Etymology

From Old English 'screadian' (to cut, pare), related to Middle Low German 'schrēden' (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ʃrɛd / (AmE), / ʃrɛd / (BrE)
A very small piece or amount of something.

Examples

In a like sense whatever bits or shreds of previous conceptions one may find in it, Utopian communism remains, as an integral whole, original - a new thing.
Perhaps - perhaps - there is a shred of conscience left in the human race.
The act, cut to shreds and hastily patched together during the afternoon, had not been rehearsed sufficiently by anyone.
If I'm going to move on this, there can't be the least little shred of doubt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small piece broken off or separated from something
  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or amount of something

Etymology

From Old English screade (piece, fragment); related to 'shred' (to cut into strips).
