demure

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmjʊr / (AmE), / dɪˈmjʊə / (BrE)
Affecting a modest or shy manner, especially in a playful or flirtatious way.

Examples

All evening Eileen had been as demure as a little girl getting ready for her first communion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coy - pretending to be shy or modest, often in a provocative way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, of Anglo-Norman origin, perhaps from Old French *demorer* (to delay, linger, stay).
