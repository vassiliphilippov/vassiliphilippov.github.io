cufflink

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌfˌlɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈkʌf.lɪŋk / (BrE)
A decorative fastener, typically one of a pair, used to secure the cuffs of a shirt.

Examples

Shaw and Skorich headed a group of players, coaches and team officials who received an engrossed copy of an official city citation and a pair of silver cufflinks shaped like a football.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stud - A small fastener used for fastening clothing, similar in function to a cufflink

Etymology

From 'cuff' + 'link', referring to a fastener joining the two sides of a shirt cuff.
