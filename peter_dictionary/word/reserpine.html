reserpine

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɜrpin / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːpiːn / (BrE)
a drug used to lower high blood pressure, obtained from the roots of certain plants, especially Rauwolfia serpentina

Examples

Specific differences exist in the action of different drugs belonging to the same group as, for instance, between reserpine and chlorpromazine.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Rauwolfia + -ine, denoting a chemical compound.
