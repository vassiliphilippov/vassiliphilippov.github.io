fermentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɜr.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜː.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A state of agitation or turbulent change, especially in society or politics.

Examples

economic security and cultural opportunities, incisive idioms, social fermentations for a new national ideology - a sympathetic public and a large body of performers especially trained to fulfill his purpose.

Synset Examples

the political ferment produced new leadership
social unrest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • Synonym: unrest - a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, and agitation in a group of people
  • Synonym: ferment - a state of agitation or excitement, especially in society

Etymology

From Latin *fermentatio*, from *fermentare* (to leaven, to cause to rise).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɜr.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜː.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A process in which an agent, usually a microorganism, causes an organic substance to break down into simpler substances, especially the anaerobic conversion of sugar to alcohol.

Examples

(These enzyme preparations appear on today's feed tags as fermentation extracts of Bacillus subtilis, Apergillus orzae, Niger, and Flavus.)

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: putrefaction - decomposition of organic matter, especially producing a foul smell
  • Antonym: respiration - the aerobic breakdown of substances, typically in cells

Etymology

From Latin fermentatio, from fermentare (to leaven or cause to rise), from fermentum (leaven).
