hideout

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdaʊt / (BrE)
a place where someone goes to hide or avoid detection, especially a place used by criminals or those avoiding authorities

Examples

A posse is formed and pursues the robbers, who, having made their escape, are whooping it up with some wild, wild women in a honky-tonk hide-out.
The robbers run from the hide-out, take cover in a wooded declivity, and are shot dead by the posse.
If we manage to keep track of a Bombus queen after she has left her feeding place, we may discover the snug little hideout which she has fixed up for herself when she woke up from her winter sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lair - a secret or private place where a wild animal lives or a person hides
  • Synonym: den - a hidden or secret place used for shelter or concealment
  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing shelter or protection from danger
  • Synonym: retreat - a place of privacy or safety

Etymology

From 'hide' (to conceal oneself) + 'out', indicating a place for hiding outside normal view.
