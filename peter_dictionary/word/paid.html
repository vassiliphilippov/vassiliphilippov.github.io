paid

1. ADJ

/ peɪd / (AmE), / peɪd / (BrE)
(of work or activity) involving payment for one’s services; done as a job rather than as a hobby.

Examples

Yes, we know, they 're professionals, men paid to play, and they should n't care how they 're handled, just as long as their names are spelled correctly on the first and fifteenth of each month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpaid - not receiving payment for work or activity

Etymology

Past participle of 'pay', from Old French 'paier', from Latin 'pacare' (to pacify, appease, satisfy a debt).

2. ADJ

/ peɪd / (AmE), / peɪd / (BrE)
(of work, a job, or a position) for which payment is received; remunerated.

Examples

In your sales force, will a smaller number of higher-priced, high quality salesmen serve you best, or can you make out better with a larger number of lower paid salesmen?
Do you try to maintain the principle of employee contributed (as opposed to fully company paid) programs?

Synset Examples

paid work
a paid official
a paid announcement
a paid check

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpaid - not receiving payment or salary

Etymology

Past participle of 'pay', from Old French 'paier', from Latin 'pacare' (to pacify or settle).
