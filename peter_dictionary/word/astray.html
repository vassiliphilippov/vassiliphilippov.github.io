astray

1. ADV

/ əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
away from the correct path or direction

Examples

He ran his string of successful conversions this season to 13 straight before one went astray last Saturday night in the 41 - 8 slaughter of Washington State.

Synset Examples

he was led astray

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stray - to move away from the correct path or place (verb)

Etymology

From Old French 'estraié', past participle of 'estraier' (to stray), from Vulgar Latin 'estragare'.
