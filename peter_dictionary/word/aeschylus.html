Aeschylus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛskɪləs / (AmE), / ˈiːskɪləs / (BrE)
(c. 525–456 BC) An ancient Greek tragedian, considered the father of Greek tragic drama.

Examples

We do not deny originality to the Agamemnon because Aeschylus found the tales of the house of Atreus among the folk lore of the Greeks.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰσχύλος (Aiskhýlos).
