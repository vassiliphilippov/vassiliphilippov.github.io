ballroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlˌrum / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlˌruːm / (BrE)
A large room in which formal dances and social gatherings are held.

Examples

In the ballroom below, the dark had given way to moonlight coming in through the bank of French windows.
She came to the ballroom and stood on the two carpeted steps that led down to it.
Martha picked up the hem of her gown and with eyes closed she slowly began to dance a stately minuet around the ballroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance hall - a place or hall where people gather to dance, often less formal than a ballroom
  • Synonym: banquet hall - large hall used for banquets or formal gatherings, sometimes used for dances

Etymology

From 'ball' (formal dance) + 'room'. 'Ball' from French *bal*, from Latin *ballare* (to dance).
