smallpox

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlpɒks / (BrE)
A highly contagious and often fatal viral disease characterized by fever and a distinctive skin rash with pustules that can leave permanent scars.

Examples

Her mother had done it before her, and even her old grandmother, who had collected money for smallpox and unwed mothers.
Tell him he has smallpox.

Etymology

From 'small' + 'pox', distinguishing it from 'great pox' (syphilis).
