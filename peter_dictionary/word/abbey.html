abbey

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbi / (AmE), / ˈæbi / (BrE)
A building or group of buildings where monks or nuns live, worship, and work, often serving as a church as well.

Examples

Within two weeks Warren was ringing the bell at the abbey gate.

Etymology

From Old French 'abbaye', from late Latin 'abbatia', meaning 'abbot's house'.
