fill-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.ɪn / (BrE)
a person who temporarily takes the place of someone else, especially when that person is absent or when a job is difficult or undesirable

Examples

Which is more economical for your plant - a vacation shutdown or spaced vacations that require extra employees for vacation fill-ins?
Can vacations be spaced throughout the 12 months to minimize the number of employee fill-ins?

Synset Examples

the star had a stand-in for dangerous scenes
we need extra employees for summer fill-ins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who takes the place of another, especially in a performance
  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another
