implacable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈplækəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈplækəbl / (BrE)
Unable to be placated, soothed, or appeased; relentless or unstoppable.

Examples

One moment, the road was filled with disciplined troops, marching four by four with a purpose as implacable as death; the next, a cloud of gun smoke covered a screaming fury of sound, out of which the redcoat soldiers emerged with their bayonets and their cursing fury.
With all his heart he had loved the Navy and now he must act in accordance with the Navy's implacable laws.

Synset Examples

an implacable enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - oppressively constant; persistent
  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy or pity
  • Antonym: placable - able to be placated or appeased

Etymology

From Latin implacabilis (not to be appeased), from in- (not) + placabilis (able to be placated).
