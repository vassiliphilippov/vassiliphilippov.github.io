confused

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuzd / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
Unable to think clearly or to understand what is happening; feeling bewildered or perplexed by conflicting situations or statements.

Examples

But explanations leave him confused and unmoved.
The policeman got a confused, funny look on his face, and he had answered kind of politely, "Now, look here, lady: I know you got to entertain these kids and all.
Apparently academic challenge in the structured setting creates an optimum of stress so that the child with high anxiety is able to achieve because he is aroused to an energetic state without becoming confused or panicked.
She looked confused at this, and I felt sure it had been a wrong response for me to make.
Then he was striding across the room, his thoughts confused but the worry building swiftly inside him as he snatched up the note.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewildered - deeply confused or puzzled
  • Antonym: clear-headed - able to think clearly and calmly

Etymology

From confuse + -ed, past participle of Latin confūsus (confused).

3. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuzd / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, events, or situations) lacking clear order or continuity; disorganized or unclear.

Examples

From the east to the west coast of the Korean peninsula was a strip of land in which fear filled men were at that same moment furtively crawling through the night, sitting in sweaty anticipation of any movement or sound, or shouting amidst confused rifle flashes and muzzle blasts.
All told, they offered a highly confused picture.
It has moved on various levels, it has been clamorous and confused.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking order or structure
  • Synonym: scattered - lacking order or unity; dispersed
  • Antonym: orderly - neatly arranged or organized
  • Antonym: coherent - logically connected and consistent

Etymology

Past participle of 'confuse', from Latin 'confusus', past participle of 'confundere' (to mingle, mix together).

(of a place, group, or situation) being in a state of disorder or lacking clear organization.

Examples

This monitoring is necessary because, on a parade ground, everyone can hear too much, and without monitoring a confused social event would develop.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disordered - in a state of confusion or lack of order
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a neat, methodical way

Etymology

From confuse + -ed; ultimately from Latin confūsus (confounded, mixed together).
