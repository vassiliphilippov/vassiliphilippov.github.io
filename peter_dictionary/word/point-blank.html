point-blank

1. ADJ

/ pɔɪntˈblæŋk / (AmE), / pɔɪntˈblaŋk / (BrE)
(of speech or a manner) direct and blunt, without subtlety or evasion.

Examples

If he wanted to know anything, he would end up asking about it point-blank, but in this guileless manner he would probably receive more truthful answers than if he tried to get them by indirection.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - direct and uncompromising in speech or manner
  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward; frank
  • Antonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly

Etymology

Originally from the term for firing a gun at close range, metaphorically extended to directness in speech.
