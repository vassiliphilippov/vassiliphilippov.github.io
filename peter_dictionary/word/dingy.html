dingy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɪndʒi / (BrE)
Dirty, discolored, or lacking brightness, often as a result of ingrained dirt or soot.

Examples

Somehow Dr. Charles Leale was forced through the mob and squeezed out into the dingy corridor.
I did book jackets and covers for paperback reprints: naked girls huddling in corners of dingy furnished rooms while at the doorway, daring the cops to take him, is the guy in shirt sleeves clutching a revolver.

Synset Examples

a miner's begrimed face
dingy linen
grimy hands
grubby little fingers
a grungy kitchen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimy - covered with or characterized by grime, dirty
  • Degree Higher: filthy - very dirty or covered in filth
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal dindgy (dingy, dirty), of uncertain origin.
