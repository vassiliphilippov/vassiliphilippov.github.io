wingback

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋˌbæk / (BrE)
A player positioned near the side of the field who combines the roles of defender and attacker, especially in football.

Examples

Wingback Jack Collins injured a knee in the Washington State game but insists he 'll be ready for Oklahoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fullback - defensive player in football, positioned behind the main defensive line
  • Synonym: halfback - player usually in the middle of the back lines in football

Etymology

From 'wing' + 'back', referring to a back positioned toward the wing or side of the field.
