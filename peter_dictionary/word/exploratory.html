exploratory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsplɔːrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɒrəˌtəri / (BrE)
Serving to investigate, examine, or discover something new or unknown.

Examples

These experiments can be considered exploratory only.

Synset Examples

an exploratory operation
exploratory reconnaissance
digging an exploratory well in the Gulf of Mexico
exploratory talks between diplomats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigative - intended to examine or inquire closely
  • Antonym: confirmatory - serving to confirm or verify

Etymology

From Latin 'exploratorius', from 'explorator' (scout, explorer), from 'explorare' (to search out).
