rationing

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
The controlled allocation or limitation of scarce resources, goods, or services, especially during times of shortage, often by an authority.

Examples

Family rationing probably will be necessary.
Water rationing will be difficult and should be planned carefully.

Synset Examples

during the war the government imposed rationing of food and gasoline

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allotment - the act of giving a share or portion
  • Synonym: allocation - the process of distributing something

Etymology

From 'ration' + '-ing'; 'ration' from Latin 'ratio' (reckoning, calculation).
