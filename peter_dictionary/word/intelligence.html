intelligence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
The ability to learn, understand, and think in a logical way about things; the capacity to acquire and apply knowledge and skills.

Examples

With intelligence.
Intelligence jabbed at him accusingly.
Although he is perfectly willing to cooperate with Scotland Yard, Holmes has nothing but contempt for the intelligence and mentality of the police.
This combined experience, on a foundation of very average, I assure you, intelligence and background, has helped me do things many well-informed people would bet heavily against.
For purposes of sample selection only (individual tests were given later) we obtained group test scores of reading achievement and intelligence from school records of the entire third grade population in each school system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - the ability to think quickly and cleverly
  • Antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence

Etymology

From Latin 'intelligentia' (understanding, knowledge), from 'intelligere' (to understand).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
an organization or department responsible for collecting and analyzing information, especially about enemies or threats, often for military or government purposes

Examples

Finally, as time began to run out, he headed into Ormoc and glide-bombed a group of houses that Intelligence had thought might contain Japanese supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence agency - an organization responsible for gathering secret information for a government

Etymology

From Latin intelligentia (understanding), from intelligere (to understand).
