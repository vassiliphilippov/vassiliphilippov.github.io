silly

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪli / (AmE), / ˈsɪli / (BrE)
Showing a lack of seriousness; inclined to frivolous or foolish behavior.

Examples

There have been cooing doves, chattering magpies, thieving jackdaws, a proud peacock, a silly goose, and a harpy eagle - whom I was silly enough to mate with and who is now busy tearing at my vitals ".
Then he laughed himself silly.

Synset Examples

a dizzy blonde
light-headed teenagers
silly giggles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frivolous - not having any serious purpose or value
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or thoughtful in character or manner

Etymology

From Old English 'sælig' (happy, fortunate), later influenced to mean 'pitiable', then 'foolish'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪli / (AmE), / ˈsɪli / (BrE)
Having or showing a lack of good sense, judgment, or reason; foolish or absurd.

Examples

There have been cooing doves, chattering magpies, thieving jackdaws, a proud peacock, a silly goose, and a harpy eagle - whom I was silly enough to mate with and who is now busy tearing at my vitals ".
"That's silly - lemon in mouth ", she said.
But I would not pamper myself in that silly way.
In of all the suggestions that were made, his was the silliest the possessive his represents his suggestion and is stressed.
It's silly, childish, running after them like that.

Synset Examples

gave me a cockamamie reason for not going
wore a goofy hat
a silly idea
some wacky plan for selling more books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgement
  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable or illogical
  • Antonym: sensible - having or showing good sense or judgement
  • Antonym: serious - not joking or silly; thoughtful

Etymology

From Middle English 'sely', meaning happy or fortunate, later meaning innocent or foolish.
