canape

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænəˌpeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkænəpeɪ / (BrE)
A small piece of bread or toast with a savory topping, served as an appetizer.

Examples

A kid comes up to me in a white jacket, gives me a Ritz cracker with chopped liver and says, "canapes."

Etymology

From French 'canapé', meaning 'sofa', referring to the topping sitting on the bread like people on a sofa.
