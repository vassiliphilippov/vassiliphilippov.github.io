reorder

1. VERB

/ riˈɔrdər / (AmE), / riːˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to arrange something again in a different sequence or order

Examples

"Speed in painting a picture is valid only when it imparts spontaneity and crispness, but unless the artist has lots of experience so that he can control rapid execution, he would do well to take these first sketches and soberly reorder their design to achieve a unified composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange - to put things in a particular order or position
  • Synonym: shuffle - to mix up or rearrange, especially in a random way

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' meaning 'again' and 'order', from Old French 'ordre', from Latin 'ordo'.
