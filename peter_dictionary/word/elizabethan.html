Elizabethan

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən / (BrE)
A person who lived during the reign of Elizabeth I of England (1558–1603), often used with reference to figures in literature, drama, and culture from that era.

Examples

William Gilmore Simms, sturdy realist that he was, pleaded for a natural robustness such as he found in his favorites the great Elizabethans, to vivify the pale writings being produced around him.

Synset Examples

William Shakespeare was an Elizabethan

Etymology

From Elizabeth, the name of Queen Elizabeth I of England (reigned 1558–1603), with the suffix -an denoting association.
