fertile

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɝːtəl / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtaɪl / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or land) able to produce offspring, seeds, or crops; capable of reproduction or growth.

Examples

The place smelled strongly of rank, fertile earth, rotting wood and urine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infertile - unable to reproduce or produce offspring
  • Antonym: barren - (of land, animals, or people) unable to produce offspring or crops
  • Synonym: productive - producing or capable of producing large amounts

Etymology

From Latin fertilis (fruitful), from ferre (to bear).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɜrt̬l / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtaɪl / (BrE)
(of a mind, imagination, etc.) able to produce many new and original ideas; intellectually productive.

Examples

With Ritter he was exposed to the fantastic profusion of ideas that stormed through his host's fertile but disorganized mind.

Synset Examples

a prolific writer
a fecund imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolific - producing many works, results, or ideas
  • Antonym: barren - not producing ideas or results; unproductive

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful), from 'ferre' (to bear, carry).
