pleasantly

1. ADV

/ ˈplɛzəntli / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is enjoyable, agreeable, or gives pleasure.

Examples

Badura-Skoda-Vienna Konzerthaus (Westminster) and Demus-Schubert Quartet (Deutsche Grammophon) are both warm toned, pleasantly lyrical, but rather slack and tensionless.
"Just you wait ", said Arlene again, as though she were discovering the pleasantly tingling insinuations of that handy little sturdy statement.

Synset Examples

we spent a pleasantly lazy afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeably - in a pleasant or pleasing manner
  • Antonym: unpleasantly - in an unpleasant or disagreeable way

Etymology

From 'pleasant' + '-ly'; 'pleasant' from Old French plaisant, present participle of 'plaisir' (to please).

2. ADV

/ ˈplɛzəntli / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəntli / (BrE)
In a cheerful, friendly, or agreeable manner.

Examples

"Bonjour, messieurs, vous etes matinals ", she greeted them pleasantly.
"I do n't mind washing dishes now and then ", he said pleasantly.

Synset Examples

`I'll do the dishes,' he said pleasantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfully - in a happy and positive way
  • Antonym: gruffly - in a rough or surly manner (opposite)

Etymology

From 'pleasant' + suffix '-ly'; 'pleasant' from Old French plaisant (pleasing).
