roleplay

1. VERB

/ ˈroʊlˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌpleɪ / (BrE)
To act out or perform the part of a character, especially as a training exercise or in a staged scenario.

Examples

The first eight of these eighteen statements, which received at least one-half of the votes, were duplicated to form an analysis checklist for the particular manager, and when this particular manager roleplayed in other situations, the members checked any items that appeared.

Synset Examples

She acts in this play
He acted in `Julius Caesar'
I played in `A Christmas Carol'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - to perform a role in a play or scenario

Etymology

From 'role' (a part played by an actor) + 'play' (to perform or act), both in use since the 19th century.
