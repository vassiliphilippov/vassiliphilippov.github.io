self-evident

1. ADJ

/ sɛlf ˈɛvɪdənt / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈɛvɪdənt / (BrE)
Clearly true and not needing to be demonstrated or explained.

Examples

The Roman Catholic natural-law tradition regards as self-evident that the primary objective purpose of the conjugal act is procreation and that the fostering of the mutual love of the spouses is the secondary and subjective end.
I do not mean to suggest that these assumptions are self-evident, in the sense that everyone agrees with them.

Synset Examples

an axiomatic truth
we hold these truths to be self-evident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - Easily perceived or understood; clear, self-explanatory
  • Synonym: axiomatic - Evident without proof or argument; taken as a given
  • Antonym: questionable - Open to doubt or challenge; not self-evident

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'evident', first attested in the 17th century.
