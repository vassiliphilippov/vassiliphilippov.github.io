torpor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrpər / (AmE), / ˈtɔːpə / (BrE)
A state of physical and mental inactivity; lethargy, with partial or total suspension of feeling or activity.

Examples

Apart from the honeybee, practically all bees and bumblebees hibernate in a state of torpor.

Synset Examples

he fell into a deep torpor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; lively energy

Etymology

From Latin torpor (numbness, sluggishness), from torpēre (to be numb or sluggish).
