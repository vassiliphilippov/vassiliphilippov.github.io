banjo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.dʒoʊ / (AmE), / ˈban.dʒəʊ / (BrE)
A stringed musical instrument with a long neck and a circular body, typically played by plucking or strumming.

Examples

For a serious young man who plays golf with a serious intensity, Palmer has such an inherent sense of humor that it relieves the strain and keeps his nerves from jangling like banjo strings.

Etymology

Probably of West African origin, possibly from the Kimbundu word 'mbanza' (a type of guitar).
