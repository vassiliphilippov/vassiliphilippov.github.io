revile

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪl / (BrE)
to criticize or speak about someone or something in a very angry, insulting, or abusive way

Examples

To the extent that he was ostracized or even reviled, we solace ourselves by saying he asked for it.

Synset Examples

The Nazi propaganda vilified the Jews

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vilify - to speak or write about in a disparaging or defamatory manner
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'reviler' (to despise), from re- (again) + vil (vile).
