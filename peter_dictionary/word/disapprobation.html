disapprobation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌæprəˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌæprəˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
Strong disapproval, typically on moral grounds.

Examples

The return of the Catholic hierarchy in 1850 was looked upon with indignant disapprobation and, in fact, was charged with being a gesture of disloyalty.

Synset Examples

his uncompromising condemnation of racism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemnation - an expression of very strong disapproval; censure
  • Antonym: approbation - approval or praise

Etymology

From Latin disapprobatio, from dis- (expressing reversal) + approbatio (approval).
