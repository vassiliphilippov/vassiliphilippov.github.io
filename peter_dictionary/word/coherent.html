coherent

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or an argument) logical and consistent, with all parts connected in a clear and orderly way.

Examples

He tried to order the words of the three Union officers, seeking to create some coherent portrait of the dead boy.
The first half of The Charles Men, ending on the climax of the battle of Poltava in 1709, is more dramatically coherent than the second.

Synset Examples

a coherent argument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incoherent - lacking logical connection or consistency

Etymology

From Latin 'cohaerent-' (sticking together), from 'cohaerere' (to stick together).

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈhɪr.ənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪə.rənt / (BrE)
(of a person) able to think and express oneself clearly and logically.

Examples

There was nothing coherent about his thinking.
He could n't see; he was long past the point of coherent thinking.

Synset Examples

a lucid thinker
she was more coherent than she had been just after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucid - able to think clearly; expressed clearly and easy to understand
  • Antonym: incoherent - unable to speak or think in a clear and orderly way

Etymology

From Latin cohaerentem, present participle of cohaerēre (to stick together, be connected).
