savage

1. ADJ

/ ˈsævɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsavɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) fierce, violent, and cruel; inclined to inflict pain or suffering.

Examples

The careless writing was in keeping with his mood of savage discontent.
"While Henry Morgan was escorting Miss Vera Green from the church social last Saturday night, a savage dog attacked them and bit Mr. Morgan on the public square ".
And she begins to regard him as savage, bestial and unworthy.
He was uttering threats in a low but savage voice when they closed and padlocked the door.

Synset Examples

a barbarous crime
brutal beatings
cruel tortures
Stalin's roughshod treatment of the kulaks
a savage slap
vicious kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutal - violent, cruel, lacking compassion
  • Synonym: barbarous - cruel and uncivilized
  • Synonym: vicious - deliberately cruel or violent
  • Antonym: gentle - kind, not violent or cruel

Etymology

From Old French *sauvage* (wild), from Latin *silvaticus* (of the woods).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsævɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsavɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of animals or actions) wild, untamed, and menacing; fierce or ferocious in manner or appearance.

Examples

Because of the authentic homogeneity of his early Nationalistic materials, and his flair for orchestrations - his brilliant Petruchka, his savage Sacre du Printemps, his incisive Les Noces - the world kept hoping that he could recapture the historical direction for which his native talents were predisposed.

Synset Examples

a pack of feral dogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feral - existing in a wild or untamed state
  • Antonym: tame - domesticated, not wild or ferocious

Etymology

From Old French sauvage, from Latin salvaticus (wild, untamed), from silva (forest).
