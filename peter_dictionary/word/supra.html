supra

1. ADV

/ suːˈprɑː / (AmE), / suːˈprɑː / (BrE)
Used chiefly in legal and academic writing to refer to a previously cited source or passage earlier in the text.

Examples

We found in Gonzales v. United States, supra, that this was the controlling reason why copies of the recommendation should be furnished a registrant.
Gonzales v. United States, supra.
But this is fully answered by United States v. Nugent, supra.

Synset Examples

see above

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infra - referring to material later in the text (especially in legal writing)

Etymology

From Latin supra (above, over, earlier).
