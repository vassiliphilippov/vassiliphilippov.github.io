reliability

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌlaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / rɪˌlaɪ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being trustworthy or performing consistently well; the degree to which someone or something can be depended on.

Examples

By 1965, several or all of these systems will have been fully tested and their reliability established.
Reliability and validation work with the Children's Anxiety Scale by Castaneda, et al. demonstrated results closely similar to the findings with the adult scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependability - the quality of being trustworthy and reliable
  • Antonym: unreliability - the quality of being untrustworthy or inconsistent

Etymology

From reliable + -ity, where reliable is from rely + -able.
