summation

1. NOUN

/ sʌˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a final summary or statement, especially one given at the end of a legal case or as a concluding overview

Examples

And I select this sentence as its pertinent summation: "In essence the drama of his (Eisenhower's) Presidency can be described as the ordeal of a nation turned conservative and struggling - thus far with but limited and precarious success - to give effective voice and force to that conservatism ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Medieval Latin summation-, summatio, from Latin summare (to sum up).
