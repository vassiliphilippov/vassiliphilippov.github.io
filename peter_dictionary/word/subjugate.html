subjugate

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌbdʒəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒʊɡeɪt / (BrE)
to bring under control or domination, especially by force

Examples

Right now, they are pushing a resolution which would have UN use its forces to invade and subjugate Katanga.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repress - to subdue or put down by force
  • Synonym: dominate - to have control or power over
  • Antonym: liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression

Etymology

From Latin subjugare (to subdue), from sub- (under) + jugum (yoke).
