antigen

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪ.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.dʒən / (BrE)
A substance that, when introduced into the body, stimulates the production of antibodies as part of the immune response.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system in response to an antigen

Etymology

From French antigène, from Greek anti- (against) + -gen (producer).
