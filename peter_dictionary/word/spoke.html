spoke

1. NOUN

/ spoʊk / (AmE), / spəʊk / (BrE)
each of the bars or rods radiating from the center of a wheel (the hub) to the outer edge (the rim), providing support and structure

Examples

He moved back to the wheel and stood there blowing, grasping the top of a spoke to still the trembling of his played-out limbs.
The spokes were tight again, the iron tires gripped onto the wheels as if of one piece.

Etymology

From Old English *spāca* (spoke of a wheel).
