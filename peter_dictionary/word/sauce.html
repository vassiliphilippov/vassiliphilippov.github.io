sauce

1. NOUN

/ sɔs / (AmE), / sɔːs / (BrE)
A flavorful liquid or semi-liquid served as a relish, dressing, or topping to accompany food.

Examples

They ate the cafeteria food with its orange sauces and Scotty gazed without interest at his food, the teachers, the heroic baronial windows, and the bright ranks of college banners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravy - a sauce made from meat juices, often thickened and served with meat
  • Synonym: condiment - a substance such as a sauce or spice added to food for flavor

Etymology

From Old French 'sauce', from Latin 'salsa' (salted).
