dross

1. NOUN

/ drɑs / (AmE), / drɒs / (BrE)
Worthless, useless, or impure material that remains after valuable parts have been removed, especially in metal refining; metaphorically, anything considered worthless or inferior.

Examples

Will mere debate on that proposition, even though it be free and untrammeled, remove the dross and leave a residue of refined gold?

Synset Examples

there were impurities in the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impurity - a substance that makes something impure
  • Synonym: refuse - waste material; garbage

Etymology

Old English drōs (dregs, scum), related to the process of melting metal.
