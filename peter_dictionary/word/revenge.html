revenge

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
The act of harming someone in return for a perceived injury or wrongdoing.

Examples

So John had refused to agree to his appointment as a preacher, and now Caroli sought revenge.
This would make anyone crafty and cruel, capable of fiendish revenge.
I want you to help me take my revenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retaliation - the act of returning an injury or offense; reprisal
  • Synonym: vengeance - punishment inflicted in return for an injury or offense
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven; letting go of resentment

Etymology

From Old French *revengier*, from Latin *vindicare* (to claim, avenge).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
To inflict harm or punishment on someone in retaliation for a wrong or injury suffered.

Examples

I want you to help me take my **revenge**.

Synset Examples

He wants to avenge the murder of his brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avenge - to inflict punishment on behalf of someone

Etymology

From Old French 'revengier', from 're-' (again) + 'venger' (to avenge), from Latin 'vindicare' (to claim, avenge).
