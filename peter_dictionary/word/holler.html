holler

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑlər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
to shout or cry out loudly, often in a sudden and forceful way

Examples

Then they let out a bellow of anguish and rage and, cursing, screaming and hollering "Garryowen "! they charged into the enemy like wild men.

Synset Examples

she cried with pain when the doctor inserted the needle
I yelled to her from the window but she couldn't hear me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Synonym: yell - to cry out loudly, often in pain or anger
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very quietly

Etymology

Perhaps a variant of 'holla' from French 'holà' (stop!), used as an exclamation to attract attention.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑlər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
to shout or call out loudly, often in a forceful or urgent way

Examples

Gracias ", I hollered, my first long swallow filling me with confidence and immediately doubling the size of my Spanish vocabulary.
He hollered hoarsely, "Hang on "! and goaded the oxen as he yelled.

Synset Examples

He hollered out to surrender our weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to speak or call out in a loud voice
  • Synonym: yell - to cry out loudly, often in pain, anger, or excitement
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'holla', from French 'holà' (stop! hold on!), from 'ho' (ahoy!) + 'là' (there).
