suspend

1. VERB

/ səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to temporarily prevent someone from carrying out their job, attending school, or participating in an activity as a form of punishment or while an investigation is carried out.

Examples

Prior to the featured race, the stewards announced that apprentice James P. Verrone is suspended ten days for crowding horses and crossing the field sharply in two races on Wednesday.
Three agreed, but four declined and were suspended.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: expel - to officially force someone to leave a school, organization, or country permanently

Etymology

From Latin suspendere, from sub- 'up from under' + pendere 'to hang'.
