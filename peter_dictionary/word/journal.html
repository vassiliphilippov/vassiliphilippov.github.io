journal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nəl / (BrE)
a record of personal experiences, thoughts, or observations, written daily or regularly

Examples

For hundreds of years, the evidence available consisted of (1) the captain's fragmentary journal, (2) a highly prejudiced account by one of the survivors, (3) a note found in a dead man's desk on board, and (4) several second-hand reports.
The mate, Robert Juet, who had kept the journal on the half Moon, was experienced - but he was a bitter old man, ready to complain or desert at any opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diary - a daily record of personal experiences; synonym of this sense

Etymology

From Old French *journal* (daily), from Latin *diurnalis* (daily), from *diurnus* (of a day).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nəl / (BrE)
A magazine or periodical published regularly that deals with a particular subject or professional activity, often academic or technical in nature.

Examples

He also drew precise crisp spots, which he sold to various literary and artistic journals, The New Yorker, for instance, or Esquire.

Synset Examples

he reads the medical journals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations, often on a particular subject

Etymology

From Old French 'journal' (daily), from Latin 'diurnalis' (of the day).
