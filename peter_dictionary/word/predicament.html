predicament

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈdɪkəmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪkəmənt / (BrE)
a difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation from which it is hard to escape

Examples

How, for example, could a Voltaire understand the strange predicament in which a Rousseau would find himself when, soon after the furor of his first Discourse, he acquired still another title to fame?

Synset Examples

finds himself in a most awkward predicament
the woeful plight of homeless people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plight - a dangerous, difficult, or otherwise unfortunate situation
  • Synonym: quandary - a state of perplexity or uncertainty over what to do in a difficult situation

Etymology

From Late Latin praedicamentum (category, predicament), from praedicare (to proclaim).
