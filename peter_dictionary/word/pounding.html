pounding

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
A rapid, strong, and repeated throbbing or pulsation, especially of the heart, head, or pulse.

Examples

For a moment he thought of going into Crosson's office to explain that he had to leave, but there was now such a pain in his chest, such a pounding in his head, that he decided to let it go.

Synset Examples

he felt a throbbing in his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throbbing - a regular, strong pulsation or beat

Etymology

From the verb 'pound', of Germanic origin, meaning to strike repeatedly and heavily.
