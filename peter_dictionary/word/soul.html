soul

1. NOUN

/ soʊl / (AmE), / səʊl / (BrE)
Deep feeling or emotion, often regarded as the animating or essential element of a person's character.

Examples

It is into this household, one eroded by irritations that have tortured the souls out of its people, that George Dillon enters at the beginning of the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit - the non-physical part of a person, often considered the seat of emotions and character
  • Synonym: heart - the seat of emotion and feeling (in figurative usage)

Etymology

From Old English 'sāwol', related to Old High German 'sēula', Gothic 'saiwala'.
