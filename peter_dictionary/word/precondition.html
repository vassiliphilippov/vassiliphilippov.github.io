precondition

1. VERB

/ priːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / priːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to put something into the required state or condition in advance

Examples

It is a proposal that justice now be served by means other than those that have ever preconditioned the search for it, or preconditioned more positive means for attaining it, in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • Synonym: condition - to bring something into a desired state

Etymology

From pre- (before) + condition (state of something).
