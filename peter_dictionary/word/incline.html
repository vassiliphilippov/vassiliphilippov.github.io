incline

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈklaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to have a tendency or be likely to behave in a particular way or do a particular thing

Examples

Cherkasov does not caricature him, as some actors have been inclined to do.
But the Latin American republics who have been rather inclined to drag their feet on taking action against Castro also reacted swiftly last week by finally throwing Cuba off the Inter-American Defense Board.
He was also, if Pam North was right, a closer acquaintance of Lauren Payne's than she, now, was inclined to admit.
In the past these same Southerners were inclined to look the other way.

Synset Examples

She tends to be nervous before her lectures
These dresses run small
He inclined to corpulence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tend - to be likely to behave in a particular way or have a particular characteristic
  • Synonym: lean - to be likely or disposed toward a course or opinion

Etymology

From Latin *inclinare* (to lean toward).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈklaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪn / (BrE)
A sloping surface or ramp that connects two levels.

Examples

He has Henri do from four to six sets of the Incline Bench Press (note the high incline).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramp - a sloping surface joining two levels

Etymology

From Latin *inclinare* (to bend toward), via Old French *encliner*.
