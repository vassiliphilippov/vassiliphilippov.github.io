fascism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfæʃɪzəm / (BrE)
An authoritarian and nationalistic system of government and social organization, characterized by strong centralized power, suppression of opposition, and often a policy of belligerent nationalism and racism.

Examples

Theirs is no mere lack of sympathy, but something closer to the passionate hatred that was directed against Fascism.
The world exploded when Fascism challenged all concepts of peace and liberty, and the outraged, freedom loving peoples of the Capitalist and Socialist worlds combined forces to stamp Fascist tyranny into cringing submission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarianism - a political system with absolute centralized control by the state
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

From Italian *fascismo*, from *fascio* meaning 'bundle, group', used for political groups; influenced by Latin *fascis* ('bundle').
