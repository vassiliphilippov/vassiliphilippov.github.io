sting

1. NOUN

/ stɪŋ / (AmE), / stɪŋ / (BrE)
A sudden, sharp pain or discomfort, either physical or emotional, like that caused by a sting.

Examples

It is the wages of sin, and its sting is the law.
She was biting into a small red radish; and that action always caused her to lift her lip from the sting of the thing.

Synset Examples

the sting of death
he felt the stinging of nettles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prick - a small, sharp pain like that of being pricked
  • Degree Lower: ache - a continuous or dull pain

Etymology

Old English sting (a thrust), related to Old Norse sting (a prick, stab), from Proto-Germanic *stinganą (to pierce, stab).

2. VERB

/ stɪŋ / (AmE), / stɪŋ / (BrE)
to burden someone with something unpleasant or disadvantageous, especially unexpectedly or unfairly

Examples

After an unspeakable siege, lasting the better part of two months, it was announced that the studio "owed "the government a tax debt in excess of eight million dollars while I, who had always remained aloof from such iniquitous practices as paying taxes on the salary I had earned and the little I legally inherited as Morris' helpless relict, was "stung "with a personal bill of such astronomical proportions as to "wipe out "all but a fraction of my poor, hard-come-by savings.

Synset Examples

They stuck me with the dinner bill
I was stung with a huge tax bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle - to burden someone with an unwanted responsibility or cost
  • Antonym: let off - to relieve someone from an expected penalty or burden

Etymology

From Old English *stingan* (to pierce, stab).

3. VERB

/ stɪŋ / (AmE), / stɪŋ / (BrE)
to cause a sharp, often sudden pain or discomfort; to hurt with or as if with a sting, either physically or emotionally

Examples

Her reply stung me, but this was too important to let my hurt make any difference.

Synset Examples

The sun burned his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to cause or feel a sensation of heat or pain, often sharply
  • Synonym: hurt - to cause pain, injury, or distress

Etymology

Old English *stingan* (to stab, prick).
