saint

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
A person of exceptional holiness, virtue, or benevolence, typically recognized for their moral integrity or devotion.

Examples

Everyone in Salem, saint and sinner alike, is swept up by it.
No, he was indeed a saint now.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sinner - a person who transgresses divine law or principles; an immoral person

Etymology

From Old French 'saint', from Latin 'sanctus' (holy, consecrated).

2. NOUN

/ seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
A person officially recognized by the Christian Church as being holy and having entered heaven, often after death and canonization.

Examples

The English saints are widely venerated, quite naturally, and now there is great hope that the Forty Martyrs and Cardinal Newman will soon be canonized.
Barstow concluded that as Rhode Island's John Brown became a canonized hero, if not a saint, so would it be with John Brown of Harper's Ferry.
Yes, the Pope could die and quickly be made a saint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martyr - person killed for their religious beliefs

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctus' (holy).

3. NOUN

/ seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
A person regarded as a model of excellence or perfection in a particular field or quality.

Examples

An Alabama soldier whose feminine associations were of the more admirable type wrote boastfully of his achievements among the Virginia belles: "They thout I was a saint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality
  • Synonym: ideal - a person or thing regarded as perfect

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctus' (holy, consecrated).
