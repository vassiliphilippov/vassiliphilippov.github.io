crow

1. NOUN

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
A large, black bird of the genus Corvus, known for its harsh, cawing cry and high intelligence.

Examples

Ludie took his slingshot and climbed to the rooftop to shoot at crows.
The ungainly bird thing ran away, and to David its croaking sounded like the crowing of a tormented rooster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raven - a large black bird closely related to the crow, typically larger and with a heavier bill

Etymology

From Old English *crāwe*, imitative of the bird’s call.

2. VERB

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
to express triumph or boastful satisfaction, often in an overt or gleeful manner

Examples

The Federals is making everybody take the oath of loyalty around these parts too ", he crowed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloat - to dwell on one's own success or another's misfortune with pleasure
  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about oneself or achievements
  • Antonym: humble - to cause someone to feel less proud or lower in dignity

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the sound made by a crow (the bird).

3. VERB

/ kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
(of a rooster or cock) to make a loud, shrill sound, especially at dawn.

Examples

On some distant farm a rooster crowed and, far down the valley, an associate answered.

Synset Examples

The cocks crowed all morning

Etymology

Old English *crāwan*, of imitative origin.
