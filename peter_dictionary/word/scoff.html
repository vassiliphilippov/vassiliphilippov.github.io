scoff

1. VERB

/ skɑf / (AmE), / skɒf / (BrE)
to speak to someone or about something in a scornfully mocking or derisive way

Examples

But the day of the deadline came and passed, and the men who had scoffed at the warnings laughed with satisfaction.
"And add fever to our troubles "? she scoffed.
"In the past anyone who said that 90 % of all malocclusion is hereditary was scoffed at; now we know that family characteristics do affect tooth formation to a large extent ", he says.
They didn'’t know it was Pop'’s... [SCOFFS]

Synset Examples

The crowd jeered at the speaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - to make rude and mocking remarks
  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *scoffen*, of Middle Dutch or Old Norse origin.
