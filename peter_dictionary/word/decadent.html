decadent

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkədənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛkədənt / (BrE)
Characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline, often through excessive self-indulgence or luxury.

Examples

Bravado has been alternately described as "a symbol of the new grandeur of France and myself " (De Gaulle) and "a decadent, disgusting slob "!

Synset Examples

a decadent life of excessive money and no sense of responsibility
a group of effete self-professed intellectuals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degenerate - having lost moral or cultural qualities, morally corrupt
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From French *décadent*, from Latin *decadere* (to fall down, decay).
