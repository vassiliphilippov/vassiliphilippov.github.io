catastrophic

1. ADJ

/ kætəˈstrɑfɪk / (AmE), / kætəˈstrɒfɪk / (BrE)
Causing sudden and severe damage or suffering; disastrous or extremely harmful.

Examples

The ancient Greeks recorded several catastrophic inundations by huge waves.

Synset Examples

a catastrophic depression
catastrophic illness
a ruinous course of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrous - causing great damage or suffering; very harmful
  • Synonym: devastating - highly destructive or damaging
  • Antonym: fortunate - bringing good fortune or luck; resulting in a positive outcome

Etymology

From Greek 'katastrophē' (an overturning, ruin).
