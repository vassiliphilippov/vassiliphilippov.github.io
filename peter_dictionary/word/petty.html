petty

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛti / (AmE), / ˈpɛti / (BrE)
Of little importance; trivial or minor.

Examples

You know your oldest boy, Victor, was picked up in Reno for some petty theft you don'’t even know about.

Synset Examples

a fiddling sum of money
a footling gesture
our worries are lilliputian compared with those of countries that are at war
a little (or small) matter
a dispute over niggling details
limited to petty enterprises
piffling efforts
giving a police officer a free meal may be against the law, but it seems to be a picayune infraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Synonym: minor - lesser in importance or seriousness
  • Antonym: significant - important; of considerable meaning or effect

Etymology

From Middle French *petit* (small), from Old French *petit* (small, little).
