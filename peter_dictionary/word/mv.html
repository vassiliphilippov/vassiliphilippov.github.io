mV

1. NOUN

/ ɛm viː / (AmE), / ɛm viː / (BrE)
A unit of electric potential equal to one thousandth of a volt.

Examples

The current was regulated by means of a variable resistor and measured with a 50 mV shunt and millivoltmeter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: volt - the SI unit of electric potential; symbol V

Etymology

Abbreviation of millivolt, from Latin "mille" (thousand) + "volt" (after Alessandro Volta).
