concussion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / kənˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
A temporary injury to the brain caused by a blow or jolt to the head, often resulting in loss of consciousness or confusion.

Examples

Before the fight was over, the Harlem boy had a concussion and Trig was cut up badly.

Etymology

From Latin 'concussio' (a shaking), from 'concutere', from 'com-' (together) + 'quatere' (to shake).
