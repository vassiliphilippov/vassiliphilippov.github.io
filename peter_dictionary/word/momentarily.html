momentarily

1. ADV

/ ˌmoʊ.mənˈter.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌməʊ.mənˈteə.rɪl.i / (BrE)
Very soon; at any moment.

Examples

Momentarily the Reds were back in the ball game, trailing only 6 - 3, but the drive fizzled when John Edwards fouled out with men on second and third and two out.
They'll be out any second.

Synset Examples

she will be with you momently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imminently - happening very soon; about to occur
  • Synonym: soon - in or after a short time; shortly

Etymology

From Latin 'momentarius' (momentary), via late Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˌmoʊmənˈtɛrəli / (AmE), / ˌməʊmənˈteərəli / (BrE)
For a very short time; for a moment or instant.

Examples

In fact, the recent warnings about the use of X-rays have introduced fears and ambiguities of action which now require more detailed understanding, and thus in this instance, science has momentarily aggravated our fears.
Forced behind him momentarily, Russ followed at once and halted two steps inside.

Synset Examples

we paused momentarily before proceeding
a cardinal perched momently on the dogwood branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefly - for a short time, not lasting long
  • Synonym: temporarily - for a limited period; not permanently

Etymology

From Latin 'momentarius' (lasting a moment) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
