solemn

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑləm / (AmE), / ˈsɒləm / (BrE)
serious and dignified in manner or appearance; showing sincere and earnest intention or feeling

Examples

The car was just about to us, its driver's fat, solemn face intent on the road ahead, on business, on a family in Sante Fe - on anything but an old pick-up truck in which two human beings desperately needed rescue.

Synset Examples

a grave God-fearing man
a quiet sedate nature
as sober as a judge
a solemn promise
the judge was solemn as he pronounced sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grave - serious and dignified in manner or character
  • Antonym: lighthearted - cheerful and carefree in manner

Etymology

From Latin 'sollemnis' (customary, ceremonial, solemn).
