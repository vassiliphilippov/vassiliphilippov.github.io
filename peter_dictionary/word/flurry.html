flurry

1. VERB

/ ˈflɜri / (AmE), / ˈflʌri / (BrE)
to move in an agitated, hurried, or confused manner

Examples

One such wife, Dr. Linden says, became disgusted with her weak husband and flurried through a series of extramarital affairs in the hope of finding a stronger man.
When you pass a church on an Irish bus, all the hands flurry in the sign of the cross.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with speed
  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Antonym: dawdle - to move slowly and without hurry

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɜri / (AmE), / ˈflʌri / (BrE)
A light, brief fall of snow or a sudden gust of wind, or a sudden, brief burst or commotion of activity or things.

Examples

Glendora dropped a chicken and a flurry of feathers, and went with her through the drizzle, to the storehouse.

Synset Examples

he had to close the window against the flurries
there was a flurry of chicken feathers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gust - A brief, strong rush of wind
  • Synonym: burst - A sudden brief occurrence of something

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of a disturbance or burst; compare with 'flurr' in Scottish dialect.
