ICBM

1. NOUN

/ aɪ siː biː ɛm / (AmE), / aɪ siː biː ɛm / (BrE)
an intercontinental ballistic missile, a long-range missile capable of traveling from one continent to another and delivering nuclear or conventional warheads

Examples

The first Atlas ICBM's are now operational, the first two Polaris submarines are expected to be operational this calendar year, and the first Titan ICBM's next year.

The Minuteman solid fueled ICBM is planned to be operational about mid 1963.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile - a weapon that is forcibly propelled at a target

Etymology

Acronym from intercontinental ballistic missile.
