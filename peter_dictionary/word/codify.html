codify

1. VERB

/ ˈkoʊ.dəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.dɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to arrange laws, rules, or principles into a systematic code or ordered body; to organize systematically

Examples

Furthermore, even the highly trained law clerk who was a part of Jack's total make-up could not understand how the principle could ever be codified.

Synset Examples

Hammurabi codified the laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematize - to arrange according to a plan or system

Etymology

From Latin codex (book of laws) + -fy (to make).
