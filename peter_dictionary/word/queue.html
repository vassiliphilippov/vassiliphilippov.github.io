queue

1. VERB

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
to form or join a line of people or vehicles waiting for something; to stand in line

Examples

I'’ve got to talk to him and get a few things straight and I can'’t wait on line.

Synset Examples

Customers lined up in front of the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line up - to form or stand in a line
  • Synonym: wait - to stay where one is or delay action until a particular time or event

Etymology

From Old French 'cue', meaning tail, from Latin 'cauda'.
