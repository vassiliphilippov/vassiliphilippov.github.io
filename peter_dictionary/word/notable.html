notable

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Deserving attention or notice; remarkable or significant.

Examples

A notable example of this was the discussion of Christian unity by the Catholic Archbishop of Liverpool, Dr. Heenan, and the Anglican Archbishop of York, Dr. Ramsey, recently appointed Archbishop of Canterbury.
By fall, 443 survivors of this arduous journey were clustered about Fort Snelling, but most of them were sent on to Galena and St. Louis, with a few going as far as Vevay, Indiana, a notable Swiss center in the United States.
If it were primarily a believing fellowship, it would recruit believers from all social and economic ranks, something which most congregations of the New Protestantism (with a few notable exceptions) have not been able to do.
But the most notable thing about the incantation of these ex liberals was that the one-time shibboleth of socialism was conspicuously absent.
Notable in this category are the Jupiter and Thor intermediate range ballistic missiles, which have been successfully developed, produced, and deployed, but the relative importance of which has diminished with the increasing availability of the Atlas intercontinental ballistic missile.

Synset Examples

a noteworthy advance in cancer research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remarkable - worthy of attention or striking
  • Synonym: noticeable - easily seen or noticed; clear or obvious
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or surprising; not worth noticing

Etymology

From Middle French notable, from Latin notabilis (that can be noted).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Widely known and respected; celebrated or famous.

Examples

Great actors and actresses - the most notable being Sarah Bernhardt - were hired to repeat their stage performances before the camera.

Synset Examples

a famous actor
a celebrated musician
a famed scientist
an illustrious judge
a notable historian
a renowned painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - known about by many people
  • Synonym: renowned - known and admired by many people for some special quality or achievement
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; unknown to most people

Etymology

From Middle English notable, from Old French, from Latin notabilis (worthy of note), from notare (to mark, note).

3. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbl̩ / (BrE)
a person who is famous or distinguished, especially one who is regarded as an inspiration or prominent in a particular field

Examples

The candles were lighted, and we sat on split bamboo mats among the village notables.

Synset Examples

he was host to a large gathering of luminaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment or sport
  • Synonym: luminary - a person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere

Etymology

From Old French *notable*, from Latin *notabilis* (noteworthy).
