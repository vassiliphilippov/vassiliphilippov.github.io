harshness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːʃ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being physically rough and likely to cause abrasions; the state of being harsh to the touch.

Examples

It was a face that had lost its childlike softness and was beginning to fold within its fragile features a harshness that belied the lyric lines of its contours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softness - the quality of being smooth and gentle to the touch

Etymology

From harsh + -ness, denoting the state or quality.
