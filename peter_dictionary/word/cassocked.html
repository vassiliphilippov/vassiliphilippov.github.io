cassocked

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæsɒkt / (AmE), / ˈkæsɒkt / (BrE)
Wearing a cassock, especially as part of clerical or monastic dress.

Examples

He regaled Claire with an account of the mock mass performed by the cassocked bloods, which he had had at firsthand from old Bud Dodington, one of the leaders of the so-called "Order ".

Synset Examples

cassocked monks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassock - a long, close-fitting garment worn by members of the clergy

Etymology

From 'cassock,' ultimately from French 'casque' (a kind of garment).
