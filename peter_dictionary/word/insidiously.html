insidiously

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsɪdiəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪdiəsli / (BrE)
In a gradual and subtle way, but with harmful effects.

Examples

Communist political warfare, we must remember, is waged insidiously and in deliberate stages.
And the rebellion of these third generation Jews is not the traditional conflict of culture but, rather, a protest against a culture that they view as softly and insidiously enveloping.

Synset Examples

these drugs act insidiously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: openly - In a way that is not secret or hidden

Etymology

From insidious + -ly; ultimately from Latin insidiosus (deceitful, cunning).
