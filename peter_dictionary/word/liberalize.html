liberalize

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪbərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make laws, rules, or systems less strict or more open to change; to make more liberal

Examples

I would like to suggest that the landlords and Commissioners get together and consider liberalizing the practice of prohibiting dogs in apartments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tighten - to make laws, rules, or restrictions stricter

Etymology

From liberal + -ize, meaning to make more liberal.
