hazard

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæzərd / (AmE), / ˈhazəd / (BrE)
A source of danger; something that can cause harm, risk, or loss.

Examples

It is thick, much like an egg plant's skin, so that poison sprays, if they are used, present no hazard to the consumer.
Look at the trees as to size and interest, the amount of shade they provide, how healthy they are, the problems of maintenance, fire hazards, wind throw, etcetera.
the causes of injuries and health hazards in metal and nonmetallic mines (excluding coal and lignite mines);

Synset Examples

drinking alcohol is a health hazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Synonym: risk - the chance or possibility of loss or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from or unlikely to cause danger

Etymology

From Old French *hasard* (game of chance, risk), probably from Arabic *az-zahr* (the dice).

2. VERB

/ ˈhæzɚd / (AmE), / ˈhazəd / (BrE)
to make or offer (a guess, suggestion, or opinion) despite possible risk of error or criticism

Examples

Again Steinberg was cautious and replied with a smile that he was not exposed to it enough to hazard comments.

Synset Examples

I am guessing that the price of real estate will rise again
I cannot pretend to say that you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venture - to dare to do or say something that involves risk
  • Synonym: suggest - to put forward an idea or opinion for consideration

Etymology

From Old French hasard (game of chance), probably from Arabic al-zahr (the dice).
