stubbornness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌbɚnnəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌbənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being determined not to change one's ideas, attitude, or actions, even when others think they are wrong or unreasonable

Examples

Our weapons production, our world prestige, our ideas of democracy, our actions of trust or stubbornness or secrecy or espionage?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or unyielding
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being willing to change or compromise
  • Synonym: intransigence - refusal to change one's views or to agree

Etymology

From 'stubborn' + '-ness'; 'stubborn' from Middle English 'stobur', possibly from Old French 'estoborn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌbɚnnəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌbənnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unwilling to change one's attitude or position, even in the face of reason or persuasion.

Examples

He refused to change his approach, and instead he attacked high and low - the officials for their not backing him, and subordinates for their laxness, stupidity, and stubbornness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or unyielding
  • Antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or adapt

Etymology

From 'stubborn' + '-ness'. 'Stubborn' from Middle English 'stoborn', probably from 'stob', meaning stub or stump.
