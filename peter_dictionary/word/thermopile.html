thermopile

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːməˌpaɪl / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌpaɪl / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring heat radiation, consisting of a series of thermocouples connected in series.

Examples

Relative incident light intensities were measured with a thermopile potentiometer system.

Etymology

From Greek 'thermo-' (heat) and 'pile' (from French 'pile', meaning battery or stack).
