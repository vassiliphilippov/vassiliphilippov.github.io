frigid

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
Extremely cold; lacking warmth or heat.

Examples

Treat them right and they 'll make a showing every month in the year except the frigid ones.

Synset Examples

an arctic climate
a frigid day
gelid waters of the North Atlantic
glacial winds
icy hands
polar weather

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: freezing - very cold; at or below the freezing point
  • Synonym: icy - covered with or consisting of ice; very cold
  • Antonym: scorching - extremely hot

Etymology

From Latin 'frigidus' (cold, chilly), from 'frigere' (to be cold).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
Lacking sexual responsiveness or desire; emotionally or sexually unresponsive.

Examples

She would weep in private, he was sure, for she loved him in her frigid way, though in public she would be dry eyed.
They are n't "frigid "and they are n't homosexual; they 're just restrained in all of life.

Synset Examples

was cold to his advances
a frigid woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong sexual desire

Etymology

From Latin 'frigidus' meaning 'cold'.
