disgusted

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɡʌstɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌstɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing strong revulsion or distaste, often as a result of being sickened or fed up by something

Examples

One such wife, Dr. Linden says, became disgusted with her weak husband and flurried through a series of extramarital affairs in the hope of finding a stronger man.
He put the basket down distastefully, muttering softly and thoroughly disgusted with himself and his plan that had seemed so foolproof.

Synset Examples

grew more and more disgusted
fed up with their complaints
sick of it all
sick to death of flattery
gossip that makes one sick
tired of the noise and smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fed up - annoyed or bored, no longer able to tolerate something
  • Synonym: sickened - made to feel sick, especially with revulsion; disgusted
  • Antonym: pleased - feeling happy or satisfied
  • Degree Higher: enraged - very angry, furious
  • Degree Lower: annoyed - slightly angry; bothered or irritated

Etymology

From Latin 'disgustare' (to dislike), via Old French 'desgouster'.
