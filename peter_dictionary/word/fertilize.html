fertilize

1. VERB

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to supply (soil or land) with substances that provide nutrients and help plants grow

Examples

Everywhere within the common pattern, however, one finds local diversity; Greek history and culture were enduringly fertilized, and plagued, by the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors.

Synset Examples

We should fertilize soil if we want to grow healthy plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enrich - to improve the quality of soil by adding nutrients

Etymology

From French *fertiliser*, from Latin *fertilis* (fruitful, fertile).
