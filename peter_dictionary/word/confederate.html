Confederate

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɛdərət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd(ə)rət / (BrE)
Relating to the southern Confederacy that fought against the Union in the American Civil War (1861–65).

Examples

Had the situation been reversed, had, for instance, England been the enemy in 1898 because of issues of concern chiefly to New England, there is little doubt that large numbers of Southerners would have happily put on their old Confederate uniforms to fight as allies of Britain.
It had been a big battle, and the Confederate forces had won.

Synset Examples

Confederate soldiers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union - of or relating to the northern states of the U.S. during the Civil War

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛdɚət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd(ə)rət / (BrE)
a person who supported or fought for the Confederate States of America during the American Civil War (1861–1865)

Examples

A Confederate observed that the Yankees were: "thicker than lise on a hen and a dam site ornraier ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Unionist - supporter of the United States (Union) during the American Civil War

Etymology

From Latin *confoederatus*, meaning 'allied, united by a treaty'.
