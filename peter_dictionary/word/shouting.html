shouting

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
Vocal encouragement, cheers, or cries from a crowd or spectators to support or celebrate someone or something.

Examples

When the shouting ended, the bill passed, 114 to 4, sending it to the Senate, where a similar proposal is being sponsored by Sen. George Parkhouse of Dallas.
There was much shouting and screaming.

Synset Examples

it's all over but the shouting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheering - loud cries of encouragement or approval

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃaʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
The action of uttering a loud, often inarticulate, cry, typically as a result of pain, excitement, anger, or to attract attention.

Examples

There was a clump of trees that appeared to provide cover right up to the road, and the shouting and gunfire never slackened.
Mikey, after three and a half months... [SHOUTING] Can'’t you give me a straight answer anymore?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yelling - the act of uttering a loud cry, especially of pain, anger, or excitement
  • Antonym: whispering - the act of speaking very quietly
