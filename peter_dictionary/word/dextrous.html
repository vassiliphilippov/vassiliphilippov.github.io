dextrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkstrəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛkstrəs / (BrE)
Skillful and quick in physical movements, especially with the hands.

Examples

His hand went to her shoulder and pushed aside the knotted scarf that surmounted the striped poplin gown; then, to better purpose, he took hold of the knot and with dextrous fingers, untied it.
He was an expert florist, tenderly dextrous in the arrangement of bouquets and wreaths.

Synset Examples

a deft waiter
deft fingers massaged her face
dexterous of hand and inventive of mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deft - skillful and quick in movement
  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or in handling things

Etymology

From Latin 'dexter' (right, as in right-handed, skillful).
