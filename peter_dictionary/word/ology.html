ology

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
An informal term for a branch of knowledge or academic study, often used humorously to refer to any discipline ending with '-ology'.

Examples

There are many types of ambiguity and many of them have been described by rhetoricians under such names as amphibology, parisology, and other ologies.

Etymology

From words ending in '-ology', which come from Greek '-logia' (the study of).
