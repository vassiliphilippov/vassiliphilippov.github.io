sheaf

1. NOUN

/ ʃif / (AmE), / ʃiːf / (BrE)
A bundle of objects, especially papers or stalks of grain, tied together for carrying or storage.

Examples

In the living room, Miss Ada was standing by the window with a sheaf of lists in her hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bundle - a collection of things tied or wrapped together
  • Synonym: stack - a neat pile, typically of objects of the same kind

Etymology

Old English *sceaf* (bundle of grain), related to Old Norse *skauf* (sheaf).
