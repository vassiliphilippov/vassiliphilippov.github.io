patriarchal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪ.triˈɑːr.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpeɪ.triˈɑː.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a system or society in which men hold power and authority, especially as family heads, and descent or inheritance is traced through the male line.

Examples

And all this too shall pass away: it came to him out of some dim corner of memory from a church service when he was a boy - yes, in a white church with a thin spur steeple in the patriarchal Hudson Valley, where a feeling of plenitude was normal in those English Dutch manors with their well-fed squires.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarchal - relating to a society or family structure where women hold authority

Etymology

From Late Latin 'patriarchalis', from 'patriarcha' (chief or father of a family or tribe), from Greek 'patriarkhēs'.
