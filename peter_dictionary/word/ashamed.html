ashamed

1. ADJ

/ əˈʃeɪmd / (AmE), / əˈʃeɪmd / (BrE)
Feeling shame, guilt, or embarrassment about something one has done or failed to do.

Examples

Almost immediately she was ashamed of herself for feeling vain, at such a time, in such a place, and she tossed back her long yellow hair, smiling shyly as she entered the room.
She had talked to him right there, with the hot sun in his face, which made him sweat and feel ashamed.
Arlene was so ashamed that she hung her head when she said, "Yes, sir ".
Your kindness to that widow made me ashamed of myself.

Synset Examples

are you ashamed for having lied?
felt ashamed of my torn coat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of achievements

Etymology

From Old English 'āscamian' (to be ashamed).
