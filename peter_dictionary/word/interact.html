interact

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntəˈrækt / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækt / (BrE)
to act together or communicate with others, or to have an effect on each other

Examples

Other pairs of phonologic subsystems also interact or overlap in this way; for example, duration sometimes figures in both the vowel system and the intonation.
In the frame are two sets of bars which interact with each other to prevent the operator from making dangerous moves.

Synset Examples

He should interact more with his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communicate - to share or exchange information, news, or ideas

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + agere (to act), through interaction.
