fugitive

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdʒətɪv / (BrE)
A person who has escaped from a place or is hiding, especially to avoid arrest or persecution.

Examples

New England academies welcome fugitives from the provinces, South as well as West.

Synset Examples

fugitives from the sweatshops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escapee - someone who has escaped, especially from prison or captivity
  • Synonym: refugee - a person who flees to escape danger or persecution

Etymology

From Latin fugitivus (fleeing, running away), from fugere (to flee).
