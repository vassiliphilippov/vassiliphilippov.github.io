pyramid

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪrəmɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪərəmɪd / (BrE)
To use paper profits from existing investments as margin to buy more of the same asset, especially during a price rise, thereby enlarging one's holdings.

Examples

Owen went over to the crap table and the dice were hot, but he could n't pyramid with any consecutive success.

Etymology

From the shape of a pyramid, referencing the increasing size of holdings; from Greek 'pyramis', meaning 'pyramid'.
