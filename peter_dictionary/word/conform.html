conform

1. VERB

/ kənˈfɔrm / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to be in agreement or consistent with something; to match or be similar in form, type, or character

Examples

The voices of all the characters, including that of Cherkasov, have richness, roughness or color to conform with the personalities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: differ - to be unlike or distinct in nature, form, or characteristics
  • Synonym: agree - to correspond or be compatible or consistent; to match

Etymology

From Latin 'conformare' (to form, to shape), from 'con-' (together) + 'formare' (to form).

2. VERB

/ kənˈfɔrm / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to adapt oneself or one’s behavior to new or different conditions or rules

Examples

Jesus replied,' let it be so for the present; we do well to conform this way with all that God requires'.

Synset Examples

We must adjust to the bad economic situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adapt - to change to suit new circumstances
  • Synonym: comply - to act in accordance with rules or requests
  • Antonym: rebel - to resist authority or rules; to not conform

Etymology

From Latin 'conformare' (to shape, fashion), from 'con-' (together) + 'formare' (to form).
