jovial

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊ.vi.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.vi.əl / (BrE)
cheerful and friendly; full of high-spirited good humor or merriment.

Examples

He had a round, frank Irish face, creased in a jovial grin that stayed bleakly in place even when he was pumping bullets into someone's body.

Synset Examples

when hearts were young and gay
a poet could not but be gay, in such a jocund company
the jolly crowd at the reunion
jolly old Saint Nick
a jovial old gentleman
have a merry Christmas
peals of merry laughter
a mirthful laugh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merry - cheerful and lively
  • Synonym: jolly - happy and cheerful
  • Antonym: gloomy - sad or depressed

Etymology

From Latin Iovialis, meaning 'of Jupiter (Jove)', who was associated with good humor.
