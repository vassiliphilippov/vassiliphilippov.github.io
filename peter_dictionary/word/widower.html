widower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊə / (BrE)
a man whose wife has died and who has not remarried

Examples

He is a widower, his three children are dead, he has no one left on earth; also he is a drunk, and has lost his job on that account.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: widow - a woman whose husband has died and who has not remarried

Etymology

From Middle English *widowere*, modeled after 'widow', with Old English and Romance roots.
