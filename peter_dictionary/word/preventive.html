preventive

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv / (BrE)
Intended to stop something harmful, especially disease, from happening.

Examples

Then, feed preventive dose until 6 weeks after calving.

Synset Examples

preventive medicine
vaccines are prophylactic
a prophylactic drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophylactic - designed to prevent disease

Etymology

From Latin praevenire (to come before, prevent) via Late Latin preventivus.
