hurrying

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɜriɪŋ, ˈhʌriɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌriɪŋ / (BrE)
moving or acting with great haste

Examples

As for the ladies, they were full of charm, and sincerity, and deep and abiding affection for this hurrying driving, honest, little man.

Synset Examples

affection for this hurrying driving...little man
lashed the scurrying horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rushing - moving swiftly with urgency
  • Antonym: leisurely - unhurried, taking plenty of time

Etymology

Present participle of 'hurry', which is of uncertain origin, possibly related to the Scandinavian word for 'rush'.
