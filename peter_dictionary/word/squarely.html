squarely

1. ADV

/ ˈskwɛrli / (AmE), / ˈskweəli / (BrE)
In a way that is direct, clear, or without evasion; not ambiguously or by avoidance.

Examples

Although they have also been concerned to stand squarely within the tradition of the apostolic church, they have exhibited no willingness whatever to sacrifice their modernity to their Christianity.
This brings us squarely to the problem of power, and the uses a nation makes of power.

Synset Examples

to face a problem squarely
the responsibility lies squarely with them
spoke forthright (or forthrightly) and to the point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - without deviating; in a straightforward manner
  • Antonym: indirectly - not in a direct way; circuitously

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'square' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Square' here refers to being straightforward.
