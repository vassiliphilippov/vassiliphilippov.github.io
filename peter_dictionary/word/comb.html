comb

1. VERB

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
To untangle or arrange (hair or fibers) by drawing a comb through it.

Examples

David finished shaving, washed his face clean of lather, and combed and retied his hair.

Synset Examples

comb your hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - to arrange or detangle hair with a brush

Etymology

Old English *camb* (comb, crest) from Proto-Germanic *kambaz*.

2. NOUN

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
the fleshy red crest on the head of a domestic fowl or other similar birds

Examples

The doctor, with the air of a man whose professional interests have found scope, drew Alex's attention to those excellences which might otherwise have escaped him: the fine color in comb and wattles, the length and quality of neck and saddle hackles, the firm, wide spread of the toes, and a rare justness in the formation of the ear lappets.

Etymology

From Old English *camb* (comb, crest, ridge); related to German *Kamm* and Dutch *kam*.

3. NOUN

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
a flat device with a row of narrow pointed teeth at one edge, used for untangling or arranging hair

Examples

He sat for a while with his hands on his knees, watching the bend of her back as she gathered up her things - a comb, a bottle of aspirin - to take upstairs and pack.
The lieutenant's sparse brown hair was heavily pomaded, and as Killpath raked the comb through it, it stuck together in thatches so that it looked like umbrella ribs clinging to his pink skull.
The lieutenant eyed Gun's reflection in the mirror over the washbowl and then glanced back at his own face, moving the comb methodically around his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - an implement with bristles or other filaments used for grooming hair

Etymology

From Old English *camb*, from Proto-Germanic *kambaz* (comb, crest).

4. VERB

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
to use a comb or similar tool to smooth or untangle hair, wool, or other fibers

Examples

Gun went to the connecting door, which was open, and stood at attention while Orville Torrence Killpath, in full uniform, finished combing his hair.

Synset Examples

comb your hair before dinner
comb the wool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - to use a brush to smooth or arrange hair or fur

Etymology

Old English *camb* (comb, crest), from Proto-Germanic *kambaz*.

5. VERB

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
to search a place or area very thoroughly in order to find someone or something

Examples

Separating, they took different sides of the main drag and systematically combed the bars.

Synset Examples

They combed the area for the missing child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ransack - to search a place violently or thoroughly for something
  • Synonym: scour - to search a place or things thoroughly in order to find something
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice something while searching or examining

Etymology

From Old English 'camb', related to Old High German 'kamb' (comb); figurative sense (search as with a comb) dates from early 20th century.
