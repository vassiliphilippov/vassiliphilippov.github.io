inexhaustible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɪɡˈzɔstəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪɡˈzɔːstəbl̩ / (BrE)
Impossible to use up completely; unable to be exhausted.

Examples

The continuities, contrasts, and similarities discernible when past and present are surveyed together are inexhaustible and the one is often understood through the other.

Synset Examples

an inexhaustible supply of coal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exhaustible - able to be used up completely
  • Synonym: infinite - limitless or endless in extent or size

Etymology

From Latin inexhaustibilis ('in-' not + 'exhaurire' to exhaust + '-ibilis' able).
