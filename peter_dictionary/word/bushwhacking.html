bushwhacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃˌwækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of attacking or ambushing from a concealed position, especially in rough terrain or under cover of vegetation

Examples

"Just let me meet up with that damned bushwhackin' coward face-to-face "! he exploded.

Synset Examples

bushwhacking guerrillas attacking from ambush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - the act of attacking by surprise from a concealed position

Etymology

From 'bushwhack', which combines 'bush' (shrubby country) and 'whack' (to strike), originally referring to fighting in bushy or wooded areas.
