analytical

1. ADJ

/ əˈnælɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌænəˈlɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
using or involving careful examination and logical reasoning by breaking down a complex whole into its basic parts; skilled in analysis.

Examples

Fractions from the column which were to be subjected to analytical ultracentrifugation were concentrated by negative pressure dialysis and dialyzed for 16 hr in the cold against at least 500 volumes of phosphate buffered saline, pH 7.2, ionic strength 0.154.

Synset Examples

an analytic experiment
an analytic approach
a keenly analytic man
analytical reasoning
an analytical mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic - relating to combining parts to form a whole; opposite approach of analysis

Etymology

From Greek analytikos (skilled in analysis), via Medieval Latin analyticus.
