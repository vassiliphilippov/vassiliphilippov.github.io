dictum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪktəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪktəm / (BrE)
an authoritative or formal statement; a pronouncement, especially one considered to carry weight or authority

Examples

In blind panic of grief she accepted Jonathan's dictum, and believed in her desperation that she had been cursed by God.
This clergyman should have referred to Shakespeare's dictum: "So-so is a good, very good, very excellent maxim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronouncement - a formal or authoritative statement
  • Synonym: maxim - a general truth or rule of conduct expressed in a concise way

Etymology

From Latin dictum (something said), from dicere (to say).
