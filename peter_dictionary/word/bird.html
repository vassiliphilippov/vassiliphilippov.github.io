bird

1. NOUN

/ bɝd / (AmE), / bɜːd / (BrE)
A warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate animal distinguished by feathers, wings, a beak, and typically capable of flight.

Examples

He conjures herds of deer, and wild birds crowding the air.
One of the local callers, a retired brigadier apparently left over from Kipling's tales of India, does not approve of the way Larkin gets his birds.
This keeps it cool and moist and protects it from birds.
The fish, animals, and birds which may be found at the site are another interest.
The site may be on one of the major flyways of migratory birds or have its own resident bird life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avian - relating to birds
  • Antonym: mammal - warm-blooded vertebrate animal distinguished from birds by fur and live birth

Etymology

Old English 'brid' (young bird or chick), later generalized to all birds.

2. NOUN

/ bɝd / (AmE), / bɜːd / (BrE)
The flesh of a bird or fowl, wild or domestic, used as food.

Examples

But the brigadier dines on the birds with relish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poultry - domesticated birds raised for meat or eggs

Etymology

From Old English 'brid' (young bird), related to Middle English 'bird' and 'byrd'.
