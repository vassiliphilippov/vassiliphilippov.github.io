hoist

1. VERB

/ hɔɪst / (AmE), / hɔɪst / (BrE)
to raise or lift something, especially by using ropes, pulleys, or machinery

Examples

A Dr. Charles Taft clambered up on the stage and got the actors to hoist him up to the box.

Synset Examples

hoist the bicycle onto the roof of the car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position; opposite of 'hoist'
  • Synonym: lift - to raise to a higher position; synonym of 'hoist'

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *hijsen* (to hoist).
