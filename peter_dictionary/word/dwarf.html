dwarf

1. NOUN

/ dwɔrf / (AmE), / dwɔːf / (BrE)
A person of unusually small stature, especially one whose height results from a medical or genetic condition.

Examples

He was n't a dwarf but he was a bit of a comic figure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: giant - a person of very great size
  • Synonym: midget - a person who is unusually short, often considered offensive

Etymology

From Old English 'dweorg', of Germanic origin; related to German 'Zwerg' and Dutch 'dwerg'.

2. VERB

/ dwɔɹf / (AmE), / dwɔːf / (BrE)
to make something seem small or insignificant by comparison

Examples

The dimensions of these waves dwarf all our usual standards of measurement.
The building was dwarfed by the scene outside.
Red man or white man, pacifist or killer, the forest would accept them all - knowing that it could thrive equally well on slaughter and beneficence; knowing that its ageless mass would always dwarf the short span of time allotted to any man.

Synset Examples

This year's debt dwarfs that of last year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overshadow - to appear much more important or impressive than something else

Etymology

From Old English 'dweorg' (a diminutive being), later used figuratively as a verb.
