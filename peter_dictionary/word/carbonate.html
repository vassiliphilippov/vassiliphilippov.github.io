carbonate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbəneɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of carbonic acid containing the ion CO3²⁻

Examples

Hydroxides, orthophosphates, borates, carbonates, and silicates are important inorganic ingredients of hard surface cleaners.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bicarbonate - a salt containing the HCO3− anion, related to carbonates

Etymology

From French *carbonate*, from Italian *carbonato*, from *carbonare* (to make carbonic), from *carbonio* (carbon).
