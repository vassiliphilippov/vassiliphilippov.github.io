neighborliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbɚrlinɪs / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəlɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or disposition of being friendly, helpful, and considerate toward one's neighbors.

Examples

fearful of being mistaken for a Jew, he seeks protection in his Nazi uniform; clinging to the enemy Jew idea, he is forced to overcome habits of politeness and neighborliness; once in power he begins to give vent to a criminal opportunism that causes him to alternate between megalomania and envy of those above him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendliness or opposition

Etymology

From 'neighbor' + '-liness', patterned after 'friendliness'.
