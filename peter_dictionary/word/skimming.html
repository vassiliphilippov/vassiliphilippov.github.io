skimming

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing floating material from the surface of a liquid, often to clean or process the liquid

Examples

A skimming device at the effluent weir prevented loss of most of these light solids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skim - to remove a substance from the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From the verb 'skim', which comes from Middle English skimmen, from Old French escumer (to skim, clear of foam), from escume (scum, foam).
