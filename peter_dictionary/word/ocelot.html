ocelot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.səˌlɑːt / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.lɒt / (BrE)
A small, nocturnal wildcat found in Central and South America, recognized by its dark-spotted, buff-brown coat.

Examples

Well, a man had tried, at the King Arthur, to register with an ocelot.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *ōcēlōtl* (jaguar).
