thrombosis

1. NOUN

/ θrəmˈboʊsɪs / (AmE), / θrɒmˈbəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel, which can obstruct the flow of blood

Examples

Brain examined for thrombosis, clot or hemorrhage.

Etymology

From Greek *thrombōsis* (coagulation, clotting), from *thrombos* (clot).
