rubric

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.brɪk / (AmE), / ˈruː.brɪk / (BrE)
an established rule, instruction, or set of guidelines, especially one of an authoritative or formal nature

Examples

What goes on in the cage will occupy our attention under the rubric of the organization church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guideline - a general rule, principle, or piece of advice
  • Synonym: rule - a prescribed guide for conduct or action

Etymology

From Latin 'rubrica' (red ochre, heading in red), as rules and instructions were often written in red in manuscripts.
