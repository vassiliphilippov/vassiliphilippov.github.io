peroxide

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A pale blue, viscous liquid with strong oxidizing properties (hydrogen peroxide), used as a bleaching agent, mild disinfectant, and, in higher concentrations, as an oxidizer in rocket fuel.

Etymology
