feint

1. VERB

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
To make a deceptive or pretended movement, especially to mislead an opponent.

Examples

This was going to be it now, any second, and what he had to remember was to keep his eye on the razor, no matter what, even if Roberts should feint with a kick to the groin, the deadly hand was his exclusive concern.

Synset Examples

The midfielder feinted to shoot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - to pretend or falsely present an action or emotion

Etymology

From French 'feindre' (to pretend), from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, pretend).
