landmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlɑːndmɑːk / (BrE)
a prominent or easily recognizable object or feature in a particular landscape that serves as a point of reference

Examples

But it was a landmark.

Synset Examples

the church steeple provided a convenient landmark

Etymology

From land + mark, originally indicating a mark made in the landscape, later applied to prominent features.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlændˌmɑːk / (BrE)
an event or development that marks an important stage or turning point in history or progress

Examples

His various aesthetic postulates remain as landmarks of a house divided against itself: Supra Expressionism, Neo Paganism, Neo Classicism, Neo Romanticism, Neo Jazz, Neo Ecclesiasticism, Neo Popularism, and most recently, Post Serialism - all competing with each other within one composer!

Synset Examples

the agreement was a watershed in the history of both nations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milestone - an important event in the development or history of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'landmerk', from Old English 'landmearc' (land boundary, boundary marker).
