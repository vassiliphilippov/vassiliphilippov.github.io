tulip

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
A bulbous perennial plant of the lily family, with showy, typically cup-shaped flowers of many colors, widely grown in gardens.

Examples

The tulips and the big pink peonies had been blooming along the drive, and he had walked up from the bus almost singing.

Etymology

From French *tulipe*, from Turkish *tülbent* (muslin), influenced by Persian *dulband* (turban), referring to the flower's turban-like shape.
