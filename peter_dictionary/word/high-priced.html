high-priced

1. ADJ

/ haɪ praɪst / (AmE), / haɪ praɪst / (BrE)
costing a lot; having a high price

Examples

In your sales force, will a smaller number of higher-priced, high quality salesmen serve you best, or can you make out better with a larger number of lower paid salesmen?
He inferred that Hohlbein and Garth were high-priced lawyers.

Synset Examples

costly jewelry
high-priced merchandise
much too dear for my pocketbook
a pricey restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expensive - costing a lot of money
  • Synonym: costly - requiring large expenditure; expensive
  • Antonym: cheap - costing very little money
  • Antonym: low-priced - having a low price or cost, not expensive
