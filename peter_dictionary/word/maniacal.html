maniacal

1. ADJ

/ məˈnaɪ.əkəl / (AmE), / məˈnaɪ.əkəl / (BrE)
Behaving in a wild, frenzied, or violently irrational way, as if affected by madness.

Examples

He thought of Simms Purdew, who once had risen at the edge of a cornfield, a maniacal scream on his lips, and swung a clubbed musket like a flail to beat down the swirl of Rebel bayonets about him.

Synset Examples

a maniacal frenzy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; wildly irrational
  • Synonym: frenzied - wildly excited or uncontrolled
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or other strong emotions

Etymology

From mania (madness) + -ical (adjectival suffix); mania from Greek *maniā* (madness).
