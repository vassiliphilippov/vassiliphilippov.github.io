obsess

1. VERB

/ əbˈsɛs / (AmE), / əbˈsɛs / (BrE)
to continually and anxiously preoccupy or fill the mind of; to haunt persistently with an idea, feeling, or emotion

Examples

He was obsessed by disease and poverty, by the melancholy of old age and the tyranny of lust.
It is sex that obsesses them, sex that is at the basis of their aesthetic creed.

Synset Examples

Fear of illness haunts her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunt - to continually trouble or preoccupy the mind
  • Synonym: preoccupy - to occupy the mind to the exclusion of other thoughts
  • Antonym: free - to release from preoccupation or obsession

Etymology

From Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidere' (to besiege, occupy).
