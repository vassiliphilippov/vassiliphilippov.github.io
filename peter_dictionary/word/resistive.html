resistive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪstɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (BrE)
exhibiting or relating to electrical resistance; having the property of resisting the flow of electric current.

Examples

At 100 Amp the 360 cycle ripple was less than 0.5 V (peak to peak) with a resistive load.

Synset Examples

resistive load

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conductive - having the property of conducting electricity

Etymology

From 'resist' + '-ive', patterned after 'resistance'.
