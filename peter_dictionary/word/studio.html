studio

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəʊ / (BrE)
A room or building where an artist, photographer, musician, or dancer works or teaches.

Examples

The major studios maintain a cadre of film librarians and research specialists who look to this matter.
The men did not object to his sketching them while they went about their work, but no one could be persuaded to come to his studio to pose.
Scrub the studio once a week.
Felix took us into his studio.
When Felix first opened the door on it, all these shades were tightly drawn and the whole studio was as dark as night.

Synset Examples

she ran a dance studio
the music department provided studios for their students
you don't need a studio to make a passport photograph

Etymology

From Italian 'studio' (study, studio), from Latin 'studium' (study, zeal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːdiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəʊ / (BrE)
A room or building where movies, television shows, or radio programs are produced and recorded.

Examples

But this... man won't give it to me, the head of the studio. -

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recording studio - room or building specifically equipped for recording music or sound

Etymology

From Italian 'studio' (room for study or work), from Latin 'studium' (study).
