cue

1. VERB

/ kju / (AmE), / kjuÀê / (BrE)
To assist someone acting or reciting by suggesting the next words when they are forgotten or imperfectly learned.

Examples

Cue Becky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - to supply forgotten words to an actor or speaker

Etymology

Probably from the use of 'cue' in stage directions, from the letter 'q' for 'quando' (Latin for 'when'), marking entrances.
