scour

1. VERB

/ ˈskaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊə / (BrE)
To clean the surface of something by rubbing it hard, typically with an abrasive or detergent.

Examples

Argiento had been trained so rigorously by the Jesuits that Michelangelo was unable to change his habits: up before dawn to scrub the floors, whether they were dirty or not; water boiling on the fire for washing laundry every day, the pots scoured with river sand after each meal.

Synset Examples

She scrubbed his back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to clean by rubbing hard, typically with a brush

Etymology

From Middle English *scouren*, from Middle Dutch *scuren* or Middle Low German *schūrēn*, meaning 'to clean'.

2. VERB

/ ˈskaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊə / (BrE)
to search a place, book, or source thoroughly and carefully in order to find something

Examples

We scour literature for them; here we find stored the wisdom of great minds.

Synset Examples

The police scoured the country for the fugitive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look for something carefully and systematically

Etymology

From Middle English 'scouren', probably from Middle Dutch 'schuren' (to rub, polish, clean).
