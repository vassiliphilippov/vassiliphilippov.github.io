hearth

1. NOUN

/ hɑrθ / (AmE), / hɑːθ / (BrE)
the floor of a fireplace or the area in front of it, where a fire is made

Examples

Slowly, like a man grown old, he took Eli's hand and led him below to the tower study, guiding him to a chair beside the little hearth where a fire still burned.
He thought of Simms Purdew snoring on his bunk while Pullen James crouched by the hearth, skirmishing an undershirt for lice, and a wet log sizzled.

Synset Examples

the fireplace was so large you could walk inside it
he laid a fire in the hearth and lit it
the hearth was black with the charcoal of many fires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireplace - a structure for holding an open fire indoors

Etymology

Old English *heorð* (hearth, fireplace), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ hɑrθ / (AmE), / hɑːθ / (BrE)
(literary) The home or the comfort of home life, symbolized as or by the area in front of a fireplace.

Examples

He saw Mose squatting by the hearth, breaking up hardtack into a pan.

Synset Examples

driven from hearth and home
fighting in defense of their firesides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireside - the area near the fire in a home; often used as a symbol of home life

Etymology

Old English *heorð* (fireplace, hearth), related to German *Herd*.
