idiosyncrasy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdi.oʊˈsɪŋ.krə.si / (AmE), / ˌɪd.i.əˈsɪŋ.krə.si / (BrE)
A distinctive or peculiar feature or characteristic of an individual, especially in their behavior or manner.

Examples

A brief list of the great detective's little idiosyncrasies would provide Dr. Freud with ample food for thought.
"Old people have their idiosyncrasies ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quirk - a peculiar behavioral habit

Etymology

From Greek idiosynkrasia, meaning a peculiar temperament or physical constitution, from idios (one's own) + synkrasis (mixture).
