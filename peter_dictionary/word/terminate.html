terminate

1. VERB

/ ˈtɜːrməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to bring to an end or cause to stop; to finish or conclude something

Examples

This dissatisfaction led to Howsam's request that the video not be terminated before the end of the game.
They plan to become county people who know the proper way to terminate a fox's life on earth.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - to bring something to a conclusion or stop
  • Synonym: cease - to stop happening or existing
  • Antonym: begin - to start or commence

Etymology

2. VERB

/ ˈtɜːrməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to bring to or come to an end; to finish; to conclude, either in time, space, or extent, literally or figuratively

Examples

All orders originate with the officer of highest rank and terminate with action of the men in the ranks.
The authority of the Secretary of the Interior under this joint resolution to construct, operate, and maintain demonstration plants shall terminate upon the expiration of twelve years after the date on which this joint resolution is approved.

Synset Examples

the bronchioles terminate in a capillary bed
Your rights stop where you infringe upon the rights of other
My property ends by the bushes
The symphony ends in a pianissimo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - to come to or bring to a conclusion
  • Synonym: cease - to stop happening or existing
  • Antonym: begin - to start; to come into existence

Etymology

From Latin terminare (to set boundaries, limit), from terminus (boundary, end).
