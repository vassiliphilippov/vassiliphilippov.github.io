paleocortical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪli.oʊˈkɔrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpali.əʊˈkɔːtɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting the olfactory cortex of the cerebrum, a region in the brain involved primarily with the sense of smell.

Examples

After this brief discussion of neo -, paleocortical, and cortico-hypothalamic relations, let us return once more to the problem of hypothalamic balance and its physiological and pathological significance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neocortical - relating to the neocortex, the most recently evolved part of the cerebral cortex

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'cortex' (outer layer) + '-al' (adjectival suffix).
