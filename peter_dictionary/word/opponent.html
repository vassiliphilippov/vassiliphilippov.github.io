opponent

1. NOUN

/ əˈpoʊnənt / (AmE), / əˈpəʊnənt / (BrE)
A person who competes, fights, or argues against another in a contest, conflict, or debate.

Examples

Opponents generally argued that the ballot could n't give enough information about tax proposals for the voters to make an intelligent choice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supporter - a person who supports or is in favor of someone or something
  • Synonym: adversary - an enemy or opponent in a contest or conflict
  • Synonym: rival - a person who is competing for the same goal as another; a competitor

Etymology

From Latin 'opponens', present participle of 'opponere' (to oppose)

2. NOUN

/ əˈpoʊnənt / (AmE), / əˈpəʊnənt / (BrE)
a person, team, or group that competes against another in a contest, game, or argument

Examples

But to imitate an opponent when he has made the mistake of his life would be a new high in statesmanlike folly.
Then he played nine imaginary holes of golf, choosing his handicap, his irons, his stance, his opponents, and his weather in detail, but the green of the links seemed faded in the light of his business worries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rival - a person or group competing with another for the same objective
  • Synonym: adversary - someone who opposes or competes with another
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who supports or is on the same side as another

Etymology

From Latin 'opponens' (present participle of 'opponere', meaning 'to set against').
