honky-tonk

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑŋ.ki tɑŋk / (AmE), / ˈhɒŋ.ki tɒŋk / (BrE)
A cheap bar, nightclub, or dance hall, especially one featuring country music and associated with rowdy behavior.

Examples

A posse is formed and pursues the robbers, who, having made their escape, are whooping it up with some wild, wild women in a honky-tonk hide-out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a place where alcoholic drinks are served
  • Synonym: saloon - a public room or building for people to gather and drink

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of a piano, first used in the US South and West in the late 19th century.
