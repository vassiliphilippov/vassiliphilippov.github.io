flamboyantly

1. ADV

/ flæmˈbɔɪəntli / (AmE), / flamˈbɔɪəntli / (BrE)
In a very showy, colorful, or exuberant manner.

Examples

The girls are prone to dress far more flamboyantly than their counterparts out of town, and eye shadow, mascara, and elaborate bouffant hairdos - despite the admonitions of cautious guidance personnel - are not unknown even in early morning classes.

Synset Examples

he dresses rather flamboyantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostentatiously - in a showy or pretentious way
  • Antonym: modestly - in a modest or unassuming way

Etymology

From flamboyant + -ly; 'flamboyant' from French 'flamboyant', present participle of 'flamboyer' (to blaze, to flame).
