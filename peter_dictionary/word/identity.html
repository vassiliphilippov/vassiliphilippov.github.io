identity

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɛntəti / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪti / (BrE)
The state of being exactly the same as something else; exact sameness.

Examples

Every community, if it is alive has a spirit, and that spirit is the center of its unity and identity.
But in such an important question, we would be satisfied if the judgment were that the principal objection to the identity of forces which produce electricity and magnetism were only a difficulty, and not a thing which is contrary to it.

Synset Examples

they shared an identity of interests

Related Terms

  • Antonym: difference - the state or condition of being different

Etymology

From Late Latin identitas (sameness), from Latin idem (the same).
