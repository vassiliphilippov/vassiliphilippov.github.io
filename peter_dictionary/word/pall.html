pall

1. VERB

/ pæl / (AmE), / pɔːl / (BrE)
(of an experience or feeling) to become less interesting, enjoyable, or attractive; to lose its appeal.

Examples

Apparently Fellini caught the crowd when its parties had begun to pall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel tired and uninterested

Etymology

From Old French paleir (to become pale), from Latin pallescere (to become pale).
