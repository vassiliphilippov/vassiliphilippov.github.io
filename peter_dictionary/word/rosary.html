rosary

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzəri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzəri / (BrE)
A string of beads used for counting prayers, especially in the Roman Catholic tradition.

Examples

Calloused fingers, caressed only by the smoothness of polished rosaries, had swayed excitedly beneath puckered chins where tiny black hairs sprouted, never to be tweezed away.

Etymology

From Latin *rosarium* (rose garden, garland of roses), later used figuratively for the prayer sequence.
