bandoleer

1. NOUN

/ ˌbændəˈlɪr / (AmE), / ˌbændəˈlɪə / (BrE)
A wide belt worn over the shoulder and across the chest, fitted with small pockets or loops for carrying cartridges.

Examples

Warren eased his rifle's safety off and gently, slowly sneaked another clip of ammunition from one of the cloth bandoleers that marked the upper part of his body with an X.

Etymology

From French 'bandoulière', ultimately from Spanish 'banda' (sash).
