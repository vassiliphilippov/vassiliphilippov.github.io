floc

1. NOUN

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
A small, loosely aggregated mass of particles suspended in or precipitated from a liquid, commonly occurring in water treatment and chemical processes.

Examples

The bacteria formed typical activated sludge floc.
The floc particles were all small as the heavier floc settled out.
The floc particles were all small as the heavier floc settled out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floccule - a small loosely aggregated mass (synonym for floc)

Etymology

From Latin floccus (a tuft of wool).
