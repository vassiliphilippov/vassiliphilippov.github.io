amplification

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmpləfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌamplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding more information, detail, or illustration in order to clarify, explain, or elaborate on something

Examples

size at which life is started and at which sexual maturity is reached; time required to reach maturity; rate of growth both before and after this crucial stage; and maximum length, with confirmation or amplification of Oliver's figures.

Synset Examples

a few remarks added in amplification and defense
an elaboration of the sketch followed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaboration - the process of adding more detail or information

Etymology

From Latin amplificatio (enlargement), from amplificare (to enlarge).
