legitimacy

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi / (BrE)
The quality of being legal or in accordance with law; lawfulness or validity as recognized by rules, standards, or authority.

Examples

Finally, it gives sanctity, more than human legitimacy, and even, through super empirical reference, transcendent and supernatural importance to some values; for example, marriage as a sacrament, much law-breaking as sinful, occasionally the state as a divine instrument.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegitimacy - the state of not being lawful or sanctioned by law
  • Synonym: validity - the quality of being valid, especially legally recognized

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legitimitas, from Latin legitimus (lawful, legitimate).
