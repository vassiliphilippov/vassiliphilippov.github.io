de-iodinate

1. VERB

/ diˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / diˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪt / (BrE)
to remove iodine from a substance, especially as a biochemical process

Examples

This de-iodinating enzyme is effective against mono - and di-iodotyrosine, but does not de-iodinate thyroxine or tri-iodothyronine.
Further, the gland is able to re-use a larger fraction of the thyroid hormone de-iodinated peripherally.

Synset Examples

de-iodinate the thyroxine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iodinate - to treat or combine with iodine

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'de-' (indicating removal) to 'iodinate' (to treat or combine with iodine).
