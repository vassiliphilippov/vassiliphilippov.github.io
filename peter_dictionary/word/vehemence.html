vehemence

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːəməns / (AmE), / ˈviːɪməns / (BrE)
The display of strong feeling, passion, or intensity, especially in speech or expression.

Examples

He swung toward the front to give the news to Hettie, then stopped, barred from her by the vehemence of her blame and hate.

Synset Examples

the vehemence of his denial
his emphasis on civil rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensity - degree of strength or force, especially of emotion or expression
  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'vehementia' (impetuosity, violence), from 'vehemens' (vehement).
