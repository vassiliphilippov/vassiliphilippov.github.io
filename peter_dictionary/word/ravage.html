ravage

1. NOUN

/ ˈrævɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈravɪdʒ / (BrE)
(usually plural) the severe and damaging effects of something; acts of destruction or devastation.

Examples

It was the low yield of the Selkirk plots and the ravages of grasshoppers in 1818 that led to the dispersal of the settlement southward.

Synset Examples

the ravages of time
the depredations of age and disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage
  • Synonym: depredation - an act of attacking or plundering

Etymology

From French ravager, from ravage (devastation), from ravir (to seize) and -age.
