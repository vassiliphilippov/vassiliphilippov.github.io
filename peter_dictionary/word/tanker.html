tanker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkə / (BrE)
A large ship designed for transporting liquids in bulk, especially crude oil.

Examples

I am recommending additional acquisitions of the improved version of the B-52 (the B-52 H with the new turbofan engine) and procurement of the B-58 supersonic medium bomber, together with the supporting refueling tankers in each case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil tanker - a tanker specifically designed to transport crude oil

Etymology

From 'tank' + '-er', originally referring to vehicles or vessels holding tanks for liquids.
