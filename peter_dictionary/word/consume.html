consume

1. VERB

/ kənˈsuːm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to destroy completely, especially by fire or another force

Examples

When the house was about half consumed, his comrade ran to the door and threw up his hands, declaring repeatedly that he did not know the whereabouts of Manuel.

Synset Examples

The fire consumed the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devour - to eat up or consume hungrily or destructively
  • Synonym: incinerate - to burn something completely to ashes
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something intact or from being destroyed

Etymology

From Latin consummare (to complete, finish); via Latin consumere (to take up, consume).

2. VERB

/ kənˈsum / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
To eat or drink something in large quantities, often in an excessive or immoderate way.

Examples

It was enough for people to know that at one time he had looked down the street at the fleshy suppleness of a woman he had consumed - watching her become thinner and thinner in the distance, as thin as the seams on her stockings, and still thinner.
In this same society, the plain citizen can with an average of only one-fifth his income buy more calories than he can consume.
We consume tons of aspirin and tranquilizers and sleeping pills in order to get a moment's relief from the tensions that are tearing us apart.

Synset Examples

Some people can down a pound of meat in the course of one meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devour - to eat greedily or ravenously
  • Synonym: gorge - to eat large amounts greedily
  • Antonym: abstain - to deliberately avoid eating or drinking

Etymology

From Latin 'consumere', meaning 'to use up, eat, waste', from 'com-' (altogether) and 'sumere' (take).

3. VERB

/ kənˈsuːm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to eat or drink something, especially in a habitual or regular manner

Examples

Animals consuming 20 pounds feed daily receive 2 grams Dynafac).
This may be incorporated in complete feeds at the level of 0.4 milligram of diethylstilbestrol per pound of ration - assuming animal consumes about 25 pounds daily.
His rage had built up as he made his way here from the second floor, helped by the quantity of champagne he had consumed.

Synset Examples

Have another bowl of chicken soup!
I don't take sugar in my coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingest - to take food or drink into the body
  • Degree Higher: devour - to eat hungrily or quickly

Etymology

From Latin consumere (to take wholly, devour), from com- (altogether) + sumere (to take).

4. VERB

/ kənˈsum / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to use up a resource, especially in large amounts or extravagantly; to spend or waste (something) such as time, energy, or resources

Examples

Still more time was consumed while the pilot, at the radioed suggestion of Continental president Robert Six, tried to persuade the armed pair to swap the Boeing jet for a propeller-driven Douglas DC-7.
The Abernathy furnace consumed fuel like a giant ravenous maw that had to be appeased by hurling tons of coal into its evil red depths, and no matter how much coal they put in the house remained cold.

Synset Examples

waste not, want not

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - to use something carelessly or without purpose, especially in excess
  • Antonym: conserve - to protect from loss or waste; to use sparingly

Etymology

From Latin consumere, from com- (altogether) + sumere (to take)
