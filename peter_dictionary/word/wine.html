wine

1. NOUN

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
An alcoholic drink made from the fermented juice of grapes, typically containing around 12–15% alcohol by volume.

Examples

When Napoleon's ship had borne him to Elba, French wines had started to cross the Channel, the first shipments in a dozen war-ridden years, but the supplies had not yet reached rural hostelries where the sweet wines of the Spanish peninsula still ruled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beer - an alcoholic drink made from fermented cereals, especially barley
  • Degree Higher: champagne - sparkling wine from the Champagne region of France

Etymology

From Old English 'wīn', from Latin 'vinum' (wine, vine).

2. NOUN

/ waɪn / (AmE), / waɪn / (BrE)
A deep red color, similar to that of red wine.

Examples

The velvet smoking jackets, pearl gray, wine, and blue, which Miss Ada had bought him hung brushed and unworn in the closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgundy - a deep red color, similar to wine
  • Degree Lower: crimson - a strong, rich red color inclining to purple
  • Degree Lower: maroon - a dark brownish-red color

Etymology

Named after the color of red wine.
