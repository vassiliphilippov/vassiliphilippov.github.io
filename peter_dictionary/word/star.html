star

1. NOUN

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
A person who is exceptionally talented or skilled in a particular field, often attracting public attention or admiration.

Examples

Ramsey has a thing or two to mutter about himself, for the Dallas defensive unit turned in another splendid effort against Denver, and the Texans were able to whip the dangerous Broncs without the fullbacking of a top star, Jack Spikes, though he did the team's place-kicking while nursing a knee injury.
A few years ago a "Timex All Star Jazz Show "offered a broad range of styles, ranging from Lionel Hampton's big band to the free-wheeling Dukes of Dixieland.
Certainly its composer was an ascending star on a new world horizon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: superstar - a person who is extremely famous and talented
  • Synonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual or creative power
  • Degree Lower: novice - a person who is new to and lacks experience in a field
  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment or sports

Etymology

From Old English steorra (star), probably from Proto-Germanic *sternô, with extended use for persons of great fame or talent from the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
(of a performer or role) being the most important or prominent in a production or group.

Examples

There are 12 of these to choose from, all of them of popular music except for the star release, Pass in Review (SP 44001).

Synset Examples

the leading man
prima ballerina
prima donna
a star figure skater
the starring role
a stellar role
a stellar performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leading - most important; chief (as in 'leading role')
  • Antonym: supporting - less important; secondary performer or role

Etymology

From the noun 'star', meaning a prominent or famous person, extended to an attributive adjective sense.

3. NOUN

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
A large, distant celestial body composed of hot gases that emits light and other energy as the result of nuclear reactions in its core, typically visible in the night sky as a fixed point of light.

Examples

Demons, fairies, angels, and a host of other spiritual beings were as much a part of the experiential world of western man as were rocks and trees and stars.
Below the moon splashed world rolled away to insurmountable white peaks; above him the deep blue sky glittered with stars.
It was a delayed moon, but now the sky had cleared of scudding black and the stars sugared the silver-gray sky.
Mark stopped the car and switched off the lights and they sat looking at the water, which, there being no moon out, at first could be distinguished from the sky only by an absence of stars.
They picnicked by the pool, then lay back on the grass and looked at stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sun - the star at the center of the solar system

Etymology

Old English *steorra*, related to Old High German *sterno*, Greek *astēr* (see 'aster').

4. NOUN

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
an actor who plays the most important role in a film, play, or show

Examples

The stage had been empty, except for Harry Hawk, doing his star monologue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - the main actor in a play or movie
  • Degree Lower: supporting actor - an actor in a less prominent role

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'steorra' (star), extended figuratively in the 19th century to mean a prominent or celebrated actor, akin to a celestial star's prominence.

5. VERB

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
(of a performer) to have the principal role in a film, play, or other show; to feature (someone) as the leading actor or performer.

Examples

Birgit Nilsson will be starred.
He is starred against Alvin Alcorn, trumpet; Phil Gomez, clarinet; Cedric Haywood, piano; Julian Davidson, guitar; Wellman Braud, bass, and Minor Hall, drums.
He read a special announcement whereby Hurrays would feature a special floorshow at three a. m. starring Adele (The Body) Brenner and fourteen glamorous schoolgirls.

Synset Examples

The movie stars Dustin Hoffman as an autistic man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feature - to give a prominent role to; to present as a special attraction

Etymology

From Old English *steorra* (star); figurative use for lead performer since the 19th century.

6. NOUN

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
A performer, especially in film, theater, or music, who is given prominent billing and widespread recognition; a leading celebrity or main attraction.

Examples

I spent hundreds of thousands of dollars on her, to make her a big star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment

Etymology

Metaphorical use from 'star' (celestial body), symbolic of brightness and prominence.

7. VERB

/ stɑr / (AmE), / stɑː / (BrE)
To be the main performer or most prominent actor in a play, film, show, etc.

Examples

He's starring in that new film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feature - to have a prominent or important role in something
