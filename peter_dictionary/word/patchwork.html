patchwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætʃˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈpætʃˌwɜːk / (BrE)
A theory, argument, or plan composed of various ideas or elements that do not necessarily fit together well.

Examples

What a patchwork of proclamations and renunciations!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hodgepodge - a confusing mixture of different things
  • Synonym: hotchpotch - a jumbled mixture (chiefly British)
  • Synonym: jumble - a confused mixture or collection

Etymology

From 'patch' + 'work'; originally referring to something sewn from pieces of cloth, now extended metaphorically to mixtures of diverse elements.
