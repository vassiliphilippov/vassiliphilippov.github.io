careen

1. VERB

/ kəˈrin / (AmE), / kəˈriːn / (BrE)
to move swiftly and in an uncontrolled way, often swaying or lurching from side to side, as if unable to control one's movements

Examples

Roberts careened backward, his back arched, fought for balance and, failing, stumbled against the newel post at the foot of the stairs.

Synset Examples

The drunken man staggered into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: lurch - to make an abrupt, uncontrolled movement or series of movements

Etymology

From French *cariner* 'to put (a ship) on a careen', from Italian *carena* 'keel' (of a ship).
