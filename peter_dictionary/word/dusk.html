dusk

1. NOUN

/ dʌsk / (AmE), / dʌsk / (BrE)
The period of the evening just after the sun has set, when the light begins to fade but it is not yet fully dark.

Examples

His first model arrived at dusk.
The surgeon came back at dusk, cut the vein of a second pigeon, again washed out the eye.
She stopped at the Surcliffes' after dusk, and had a Scotch-and-soda.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the time between sunset and darkness
  • Antonym: dawn - the time just before sunrise, the start of morning light

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English 'duske' meaning 'obscure' or 'dark'.
