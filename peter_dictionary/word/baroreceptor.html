baroreceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæroʊrɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌbærərəˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a sensory nerve ending in the body that detects changes in blood pressure

Examples

Similar effects can be induced reflexly via the baroreceptor reflexes in man and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanoreceptor - a receptor that responds to mechanical pressure or distortion

Etymology

From Greek 'baros' (weight, pressure) + 'receptor' (from Latin 'recipere', to receive).
