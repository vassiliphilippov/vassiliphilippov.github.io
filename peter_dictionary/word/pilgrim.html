pilgrim

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɡrəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɡrɪm / (BrE)
a person who travels to a foreign land, especially for religious or moral purposes or in pursuit of spiritual meaning

Examples

The hero, who is himself, is represented as a pilgrim in the storied lands of the East, a sort of Faustus type, who, to quote from Professor Bo ^ o ^ k again, "even in the pleasure gardens of Sardanapalus can not cease from his painful search after the meaning of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler - someone who travels, especially to distant or foreign places
  • Degree Lower: tourist - person who travels for pleasure or sightseeing

Etymology

From Latin 'peregrinus' (foreigner, traveler), later adopted into Old French as 'pelerin', then Middle English 'pilgrim'.
