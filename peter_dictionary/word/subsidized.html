subsidized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌb.sɪˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd / (BrE)
Receiving partial financial support, especially from public or government funds.

Examples

The causes of the decline of the commuter railroads are many and complex - high taxes, losses of revenue to Government subsidized highway and air carriers, to name but two.

Synset Examples

lived in subsidized public housing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funded - provided with financial resources

Etymology

From Latin 'subsidium' (aid, support), via French 'subsidier' + -ed.
