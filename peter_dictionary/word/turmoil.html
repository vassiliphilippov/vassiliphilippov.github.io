turmoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.mɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɔɪl / (BrE)
a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty, especially when violent or intense

Examples

A British writer, Richard Haestier, in a book, Dead Men Tell Tales, recalls that in the turmoil preceding French Revolution the body of Henry /4,, who had died nearly 180 years earlier, was torn to pieces by a mob.
Where their sharp edges seemed restless as sea waves thrusting themselves upward in angry motion, Papa-san sat glacier like, his smooth solidity, his very immobility defying all the turmoil about him.
Alexander guessed that they had planned confusion and turmoil, thinking it the ideal climate in which to begin battle and bloodshed.

Synset Examples

the convulsions of the stock market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption
  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: calm - a state of tranquility or lack of disturbance

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'termoyle', of uncertain origin; possibly related to Old French 'tremouille' (mill hopper).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.mɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɔɪl / (BrE)
A state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty; violent agitation or commotion.

Examples

No fold could be accidental, each turn of the drapery had to serve organically, to cover the Madonna's slender legs and feet so that they would give substantive support to Christ's body, to intensify her inner turmoil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption to something
  • Antonym: calm - absence of agitation or disturbance

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle French 'tourmoil' (commotion, stir) or Old French 'tremouille'.
