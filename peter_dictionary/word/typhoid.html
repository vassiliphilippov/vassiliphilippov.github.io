typhoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪˌfɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease causing fever, inflammation, and ulcers in the intestines, contracted from food or water contaminated with Salmonella typhi.

Examples

There was typhoid and malaria.

Etymology

From Greek 'typhos' (smoke, stupor), referring to the feverish state.
