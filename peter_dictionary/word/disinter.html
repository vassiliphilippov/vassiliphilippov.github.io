disinter

1. VERB

/ dɪsɪnˈtɜr / (AmE), / dɪsɪnˈtɜː / (BrE)
to dig up a body or remains that have been buried, especially for reburial or medical examination

Examples

And in England, after the Restoration, the body of Cromwell was disinterred and hanged at Tyburn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhume - to dig out something buried, especially a corpse

Etymology

From French *désenterrer*, from dés- (dis-) + enterrer (to bury), from Latin *in terrā* (in the earth).
