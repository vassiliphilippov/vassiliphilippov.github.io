car

1. NOUN

/ kɑr / (AmE), / kɑː / (BrE)
A road vehicle with four wheels, powered by an internal combustion engine or electric motor, used for carrying passengers.

Examples

For instance, the dreamer sees himself seated behind neighbor Smith and, with photographic realism, sees Smith driving the car; whereas, it is a matter of fact that Smith cannot drive a car.

Synset Examples

he needs a car to get to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automobile - a four-wheeled motor vehicle for passenger transport
  • Antonym: truck - a motor vehicle for transporting goods
  • Antonym: motorcycle - a two-wheeled motor vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'carrus' (wheeled vehicle), via Old Northern French 'car'.

2. NOUN

/ kɑr / (AmE), / kɑː / (BrE)
a separate vehicle, usually part of a train, that runs on railroad tracks and is used for carrying passengers or goods

Examples

Houses winked as the cars rolled beside a little depot.
By late afternoon the train inched into the marshaling yards in the railhead at Lublin, which was filled with lines of cars poised to pour the tools of war to the Russian front.

Synset Examples

three cars had jumped the rails

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railcar - a railroad vehicle for passengers or freight
  • Degree Lower: freight car - railroad car used for carrying goods
  • Degree Higher: passenger car - railroad car for carrying passengers

Etymology

From Latin *carrus* (wheeled vehicle), through Old Northern French *car*.
