climax

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
the most intense, exciting, or important point of a story, play, or other narrative; the turning point at which the main conflict reaches its peak.

Examples

In a series of fairy tales and fantasies, Melies demonstrated that the film is superbly equipped to tell a straightforward story, with beginning, middle and end, complications, resolutions, climaxes, and conclusions.
The first half of The Charles Men, ending on the climax of the battle of Poltava in 1709, is more dramatically coherent than the second.

Synset Examples

the deathbed scene is the climax of the play

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticlimax - a disappointing or less exciting conclusion after a build-up

Etymology

From Greek *klimax* (ladder), via Latin *climax*, originally meaning ‘ladder’ or ‘staircase’, later used metaphorically for a rising sequence or culmination.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪˌmæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
the most intense, exciting, or important point of something; the culmination or apex of an experience, event, or process

Examples

The clear and searching sweep of sun on the lawns was like a climax of the year's lights.
Like Eliot, in my fantasies, I had a proud bearing and, with a skill that was vaguely continental, I would lead Jessica through an evening of dancing and handsome descriptions of my newest exploits, would guide her gently to the night's climax which, in my dreams, was always represented by our almost suffocating one another to death with deep, moist kisses burning with love.

Synset Examples

the climax of the artist's career
in the flood tide of his success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culmination - the highest or climactic point of something, especially as attained after a long time
  • Synonym: peak - the highest point or level, especially of a mountain or process
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point in the fortunes of a person or organization

Etymology

From Greek klimax (ladder, staircase), via Latin climax (ladder, climax).

3. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
the moment of greatest sexual pleasure during intercourse; orgasm

Examples

Marriage manuals and women's magazine articles began to stress the importance of the female climax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgasm - the climax of sexual excitement

4. VERB

/ ˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
To reach the most intense, exciting, or important point of something; to come or bring to a climax.

Examples

Their activities will be climaxed in the spring of 1962 when they go to Europe.
To climax her Roman revels, she was thrown out of the swanky Hotel Excelsior after she had run naked through its marble halls screaming for help.

Synset Examples

The meeting culminated in a tearful embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culminate - reach a climactic or decisive point

Etymology

From Greek *klimax* (ladder, staircase), via Latin *climax*.
