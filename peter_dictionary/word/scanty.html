scanty

1. ADJ

/ ˈskæn.ti / (AmE), / ˈskæn.ti / (BrE)
small in quantity or insufficient in amount; barely adequate.

Examples

Data on the former are scanty, but there can be little doubt that the latter is sometimes born at a length greater than that of any of the others, thereby lending support to the belief that the anaconda does, indeed, attain the greatest length.

Synset Examples

a bare livelihood
a scanty harvest
a spare diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities

Etymology

From Latin *scantus*, past participle of *scandere* (to climb, mount), later influenced by Old Norse *skamt* (scant, short).
