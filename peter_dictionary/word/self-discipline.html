self-discipline

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈdɪsəplɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈdɪsəplɪn / (BrE)
the ability to control one's own behavior, emotions, and desires, especially in order to achieve a goal or adhere to standards

Examples

The civil services in such societies are generally inadequate to deal competently with the problems facing them; and their members often equate a government career with security and status rather than with sacrifice, self-discipline, and competence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-control - the ability to restrain one's impulses, emotions, or desires

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'discipline', from Latin 'disciplina' (instruction, training).
