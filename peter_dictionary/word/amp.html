amp

1. NOUN

/ æmp / (AmE), / æmp / (BrE)
A unit of electric current equal to one ampere, used in measuring the flow of electric charge.

Examples

The dependence of the arc voltage upon the mass flow velocity of the transpirating gas was investigated for various arc lengths and currents between 100 Amp and 200 Amp.

Etymology

Abbreviation of ampere, from French André-Marie Ampère, French physicist.
