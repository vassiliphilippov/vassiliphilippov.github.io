interceptor

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.tɚˈsɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
a fast, maneuverable fighter aircraft designed to intercept and destroy enemy aircraft, especially bombers, before they reach their target

Examples

Another example is the recent cancellation of the F-108, a long range interceptor with a speed three times as great as the speed of sound, which was designed for use against manned bombers in the period of the mid 1960's.

Etymology

From intercept + -or, meaning ‘one who intercepts’.
