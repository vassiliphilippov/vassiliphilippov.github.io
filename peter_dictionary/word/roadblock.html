roadblock

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌblɒk / (BrE)
A circumstance or condition that prevents or hinders progress towards a goal.

Examples

Here again we run into the roadblock that Negroes do not like to be designated as Negroes in the press.

Synset Examples

intolerance is a barrier to understanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that obstructs or hinders progress
  • Synonym: barrier - an impediment or obstacle to progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction

Etymology

From ‘road’ + ‘block’, originally referring to a physical barrier placed across a road.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌblɒk / (BrE)
a temporary barrier across a road set up by police or authorities to stop traffic for inspection, search, or security purposes

Examples

Of course, I doubt they'll toss up any roadblocks for that.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checkpoint - a place where vehicles or people are stopped and checked
