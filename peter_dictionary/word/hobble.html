hobble

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑb.əl / (AmE), / ˈhɒb.əl / (BrE)
to hamper or restrict the action or progress of someone or something

Examples

The Secretary General must have, subject to the constitutional direction of the Security Council and the General Assembly, the power to act, to propose action and to organize action without being hobbled by advisers and assistants acting on someone else's instructions.

Synset Examples

The chairman was hobbled by the all-powerful dean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impede - to delay or prevent someone or something by obstructing them; synonym of 'hobble' in this sense
  • Synonym: hamper - to hinder or restrict movement or progress

Etymology

From Middle English hobelen (to walk with difficulty), probably imitative or related to 'hob' (a limp).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈhɒbəl / (BrE)
to walk with difficulty, usually because of injury or physical limitation

Examples

Mike tested the leg and found that he was able to hobble around on it.

Synset Examples

The old woman hobbles down to the store every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - to walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *hobelen*, frequentative of *hobben* (to rock up and down).
