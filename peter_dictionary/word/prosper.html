prosper

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑː.spɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.pə / (BrE)
to be successful, especially financially or in career; to grow and develop in a vigorous way

Examples

John Vernon had had all the patronage he cared for - he had prospered, but he could not retire from horsedom.

Synset Examples

The new student is thriving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrive - to grow or develop well; to flourish
  • Antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful; to not achieve something

Etymology

From Latin prosperare (to cause to succeed), from prosperus (favorable, fortunate).
