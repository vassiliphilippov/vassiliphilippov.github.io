housebreaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of illegally entering a building, especially to commit theft; burglary.

Examples

The influence of Mass was less pervasive than that of the congested, slum tenements among the bawdy houses, honkytonks, and sawdust saloons of his birthplace; he ran wild with the child gangs of the neighbourhood, and went through the normal pressure-cooker course of thieving, police dodging, and housebreaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglary - illegal entry into a building to commit a crime, especially theft

Etymology

From 'house' + 'breaking'; attested from the late 16th century, referring to the breaking into of a house.
