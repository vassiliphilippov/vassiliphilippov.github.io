dynasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnəsti / (AmE), / ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
A succession of rulers or leaders from the same family, often maintaining power for several generations.

Examples

Without saying or seeming to say that in portraying the Sartoris and the Compson families Faulkner's chief concern is social criticism, we can say nevertheless that through those families he dramatizes his comment on the planter dynasties as they have existed since the decades before the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - descent from a common ancestor or family line
  • Synonym: monarchy - a form of government with a monarch at the head

Etymology

From Greek *dunasteia* (power, lordship), from *dynastēs* (ruler).
