football

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfʊtbɔːl / (BrE)
A team game played with a ball (round or oval), in which two teams try to score goals or points by moving the ball into the opponents’ goal or end zone through kicking, carrying, or passing, according to different codes and regions.

Examples

During his college career, Dr. Clark was captain of his basketball team and was a football letterman.
Although he never gets to play while the clock is running, he gets a big kick - several every Saturday, in fact - out of football.
Moritz came to Texas in 1954 but his freshman football efforts were hampered by a knee injury.
"Nelson played magnificent football ", Meek praised.
The early Southwest Conference football leaders - Texas, Arkansas and Texas A+M - made a big dent in the statistics last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soccer - team sport known as association football in most of the world
  • Synonym: rugby - team sport similar to football but with different rules and handling
  • Synonym: American football - team sport played mainly in the US using an oval ball
  • Antonym: baseball - team sport using bat and ball, not a type of football

Etymology

From English 'foot' + 'ball', referring to a ball game played on foot (rather than on horseback).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌbɔːl / (BrE)
An oval-shaped, inflated ball used in playing American football.

Examples

He broke that boy (Air Force fullback Nick Arshinkoff) in two and knocked him loose from the football ".
Shaw and Skorich headed a group of players, coaches and team officials who received an engrossed copy of an official city citation and a pair of silver cufflinks shaped like a football.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rugby ball - oval ball used in rugby

Etymology

From English 'foot' + 'ball'; in this sense, specifically the ball used in American football.
