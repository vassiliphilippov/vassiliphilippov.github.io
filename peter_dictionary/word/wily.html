wily

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪli / (BrE)
Skilled at gaining an advantage, especially by using clever or deceitful methods.

Examples

Another is a wily countryman called Larkin, whose blandly boisterous progress has been chronicled, I believe, in earlier volumes of Mr. Bates' comedie humaine.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
deep political machinations
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - having or showing skill in achieving a goal by deceit or evasion
  • Synonym: crafty - clever at achieving one's aims by indirect or deceitful methods
  • Synonym: sly - having or showing a cunning and deceitful nature
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Old English *wil* (trick, craft, fraud) + -y.
