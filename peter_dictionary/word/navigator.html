navigator

1. NOUN

/ ˈnævɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnævɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
the officer on a ship responsible for planning and directing the course of the vessel

Examples

In 1610, Hudson was probably in his early forties, a good navigator, a stubborn voyager, but otherwise fatally unsuited to his chosen profession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - person who steers a ship, especially in difficult waters

Etymology

From Latin 'navigator' (sailor), from 'navigare' (to sail).
