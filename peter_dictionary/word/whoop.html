whoop

1. VERB

/ wup / (AmE), / wuÀêp / (BrE)
to shout loudly and excitedly, especially with joy or enthusiasm

Examples

He came spurring and whooping down the road, his horse kicking up clouds of dust, shouting:

Synset Examples

The children whooped when they were led to the picnic table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout in encouragement or joy
  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly, often with excitement or anger
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, opposite of shouting

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of a shout or call; influenced by Middle English 'whopen'.
