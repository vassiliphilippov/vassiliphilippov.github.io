eccentricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɛnˈtrɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sənˈtrɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
Unusual or unconventional behavior; the quality of being odd or peculiar.

Examples

Not only was Haumd's intonation and phrasing without flaw, but he seemed to take every tonal eccentricity in stride.
His eccentricity begins as a defense against boredom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - the quality of being strange or peculiar
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal; conventional behavior

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *eccentricitas*, from Greek *ekkentros* (out of the center).
