vacate

1. VERB

/ ˈveɪˌkeɪt, vəˈkeɪt / (AmE), / vəˈkeɪt, ˈveɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to leave a job, post, or position, especially so that someone else can take it

Examples

Five candidates seek the place vacated by Secretary Hugh G. Stout.
Mr. Simpkins will move into the post being vacated by Thomas B. Finan, earlier named attorney general to succeed C. Ferdinand Sybert, who will be elevated to an associate judgeship on the Maryland Court of Appeals.

Synset Examples

She vacated the position when she got pregnant
The chairman resigned when he was found to have misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to formally leave a job or position voluntarily

Etymology

From Latin vacātus, past participle of vacāre (to be empty, free).

2. VERB

/ ˈveɪkeɪt / (AmE), / vəˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to leave a place or position so that it is empty, especially so that someone else can use it

Examples

I discovered that the girls had shrewdly vacated the kitchen, and were playing quietly in the living room.

Synset Examples

You must vacate your office by tonight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupy - to fill or take possession of a place or position

Etymology

From Latin vacare (to be empty), via past participle vacatus.
