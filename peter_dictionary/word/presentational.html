presentational

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with a presentation, especially as used in psychology or philosophy.

Examples

What Hume calls "sensation "is what Whitehead calls "perception in the mode of presentational immediacy "which is a sophisticated abstraction from perception in the mode of causal efficacy.

Synset Examples

what Whitehead calls `perception in the presentational immediacy'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presentationalism - the philosophical doctrine relating to presentational immediacy

Etymology

From 'presentation' + '-al', forming adjectives meaning 'of or pertaining to'.
