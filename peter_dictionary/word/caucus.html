caucus

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔːkəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəs / (BrE)
to hold or participate in a meeting to select a candidate or promote a political policy

Examples

Caucusing, the liberals decided to go after Colmer, which actually was the more drastic course, since seniority in the House is next to godliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confer - to have a discussion or exchange opinions
  • Synonym: nominate - to propose as a candidate for election

Etymology

From North American English, originally referring to a political faction or meeting.
