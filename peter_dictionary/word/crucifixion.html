crucifixion

1. NOUN

/ ˌkruːsəˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
A method of execution in which a person is bound or nailed to a cross and left to die, used especially in ancient times.

Examples

His death is his alone, yet each man can see his own death in the crucifixion of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the carrying out of a death sentence

Etymology

From Late Latin crucifixio, from Latin crucifixus, past participle of crucifigere (to fasten to a cross).
