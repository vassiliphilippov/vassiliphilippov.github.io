equator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪtə / (BrE)
an imaginary line around the Earth that is equally distant from the North and South Poles, dividing the Earth into the northern and southern hemispheres

Examples

"Of the humans we left at the equator, one has discorporated and the others are sad ".

Synset Examples

the equator is the boundary between the northern and southern hemispheres

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'aequator' (equalizer), from Latin 'aequare' (to make equal).
