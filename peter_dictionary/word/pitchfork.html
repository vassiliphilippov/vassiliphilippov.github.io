pitchfork

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃˌfɔrk / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃˌfɔːk / (BrE)
a long-handled tool with sharp, widely spaced prongs used for lifting and throwing hay or similar material

Examples

When his eyes began to focus, he saw Jess charging at him with a pitchfork.
His aim was hurried; so the pitchfork whistled over Curt's head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fork - a tool with two or more prongs used for lifting or carrying

Etymology

From Old English *pic* (pitch) + *fork* (forked tool); 'pitch' meaning to throw.
