inappropriate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnəˈproʊpriət / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈprəʊpriət / (BrE)
not suitable or proper for a particular situation or purpose.

Examples

The Warren Telegraph stated that many of Rev. Trafton's remarks were inappropriate and savored strongly of radicalism and fanaticism.
His fear of making some inane or inappropriate remark.

Synset Examples

noise seems inappropriate at a time of sadness
inappropriate shoes for a walk on the beach
put inappropriate pressure on them

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appropriate - suitable or proper for the situation

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + appropriate, from appropriatus (made one's own).
