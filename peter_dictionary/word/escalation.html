escalation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛskəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛskəˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A rapid or steady increase in the intensity, seriousness, or extent of something, especially in a conflict or problematic situation.

Examples

They predicted escalation, and escalation is what we are getting.
They predicted escalation, and escalation is what we are getting.
And now, of course, the hue and cry for counter escalation is being raised on our side.
So escalation proceeds, ad infinitum or, more accurately, until the contestants begin dropping them on each other instead of on their respective proving grounds.
The escalation must end sometime, and probably quite soon.

Synset Examples

higher wages caused an escalation of prices
there was a gradual escalation of hostilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - the act of becoming larger or greater
  • Antonym: de-escalation - a reduction in the intensity or seriousness, especially of conflict

Etymology

From escalate (to increase), patterned after 'elevator', from Italian 'scala' (ladder).
