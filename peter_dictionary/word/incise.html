incise

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪz / (BrE)
to cut into a surface or object, especially with a sharp instrument; to make an incision.

Examples

If he foresaw any problem because of the quality of the hymen, it was recommended that simple procedures be undertaken at once to incise the hymen or, preferably, to dilate it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carve - to cut a shape or pattern into a surface
  • Synonym: engrave - to cut or etch designs into a hard surface

Etymology

From Latin incīdere, from in- (into) + caedere (to cut).
