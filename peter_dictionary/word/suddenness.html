suddenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌdənnəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌdənnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of occurring or being done quickly and unexpectedly

Examples

Now she kept herself protectively ready to laugh again and sure enough he pointed at her with his index finger and said "Toot "! once more and roared off into the fog, his foot evidently surprising him with the suddenness with which it pressed the accelerator, just as his hand did when he worked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptness - quality of happening suddenly and unexpectedly

Etymology

From 'sudden' + '-ness', with 'sudden' from Middle English, from Old French 'soudain', from Latin 'subitaneus' (sudden).
