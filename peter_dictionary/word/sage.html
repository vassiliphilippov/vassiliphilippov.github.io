sage

1. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a profoundly wise person, especially one who gives advice in spiritual or philosophical matters

Examples

These sages include poet Carl Sandburg, statesman Jawaharlal Nehru and sculptor Jacques Lipchitz, in Volume One, and playwright Sean O'Casey, David Ben-Gurion, philosopher Bertrand Russell and the late Frank Lloyd Wright in the second set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy, especially as an academic discipline

Etymology

From Old French *sage* (wise), from Latin *sapius* (wise).

2. NOUN

/ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / seɪdʒ / (BrE)
An aromatic plant with grey-green leaves, widely used in cooking, especially as a seasoning for meat and poultry.

Examples

He then sold her minerals to cure her kidney ailment, a can of sage "to make her look like a girl again ", and an application of plain mud to take her wrinkles away.

Etymology

From Old French sauge, from Latin salvia (healing plant), related to salvus (safe, healthy).
