helper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpə / (BrE)
a person who assists or supports others in completing a task or achieving a purpose

Examples

He wants to be called sweet, good, considerate and mother's little helper.

Synset Examples

my invaluable assistant
they hired additional help to finish the work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps in a job or task
  • Synonym: aid - a person or thing that provides help
  • Synonym: supporter - someone who supports or helps
  • Antonym: hinderer - a person who impedes progress or makes things more difficult

Etymology

From the verb 'help' + -er suffix indicating a person who does an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpə / (BrE)
a person who assists or gives support to others, especially by providing financial help

Examples

Whereas, John Brown has cheerfully risked his life in endeavoring to deliver those who are denied all rights and is this day doomed to suffer death for his efforts in behalf of those who have no helper:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives money or other help to a person or cause

Etymology

From Middle English 'helpere', from Old English 'helpan' (to help) + '-er' (agent suffix).
