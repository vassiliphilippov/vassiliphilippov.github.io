fiberglass

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbərˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbəˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A lightweight, strong material made from thin glass fibers embedded in resin, used as a construction, insulation, or reinforcing material.

Examples

The side slopes were coated with fiberglas matting coated with asphalt to prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glass fiber - A single strand of glass used to reinforce materials

Etymology

From 'fiber' + 'glass', first used in the early 20th century to describe glass-reinforced plastics.
