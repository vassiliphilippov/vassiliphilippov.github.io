electron

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɑn / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn / (BrE)
A subatomic particle with a negative electric charge, found in all atoms and acting as the primary carrier of electricity in solids.

Examples

Heat of condensation (work function) plus kinetic energy of the electrons impinging on the anode.
The use of high voltages and low currents by proper design to reduce electron heat transfer to the anode for a given power output.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proton - A subatomic particle with a positive charge found in atomic nuclei.
  • Antonym: neutron - A subatomic particle with no electric charge found in atomic nuclei.

Etymology

From electr- (from 'electric') + -on (suffix for subatomic particles).
