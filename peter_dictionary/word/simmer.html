simmer

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmə / (BrE)
to cook or keep food just below boiling point, usually so that small bubbles form slowly and gently

Examples

After this has simmered an hour, add two tablespoons each of Worcestershire, catsup, and chutney, two pickled walnuts, and a pint of Sherry.
Then simmer fifteen minutes longer.
Simmer about 10 minutes, and keep warm for serving.
Simmer 15 minutes.
Cover; simmer 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sauce is of desired consistency.

Synset Examples

simmer the sauce
simmering water

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boil - to cook (food) by immersing it in water or other liquid that is heated until bubbles rise continuously to the surface
  • Degree Lower: poach - to cook food gently in liquid just below the boiling point

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *simiren*, imitative of the sound of gentle boiling.
