oilfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌfiːld / (BrE)
An area of land or seabed with significant deposits of petroleum, often with active oil wells.

Examples

It was payday for Highlands, and he was packing a lot of money back into the oil fields.
Oil-field workers were a rough tough lot.

Etymology

Formed from 'oil' + 'field', referring to a field where oil is extracted.
