gruesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈgruːsəm / (AmE), / ˈɡruːsəm / (BrE)
Causing horror or disgust; shockingly repellent or grisly.

Examples

But let us return, after this gruesome interlude, to our willow catkins in the spring; there are other wild bees that command our attention.
The gruesome humor of the Nazis was not forgotten - the gas chamber with a sign on it with the name of a Jewish foundation and bearing a copper Star of David - nor the gratuitous sadism of SS officers.
Come on, you gruesome son of a bitch.

Synset Examples

ghastly wounds
the grim aftermath of the bombing
the grim task of burying the victims
a grisly murder
gruesome evidence of human sacrifice
macabre tales of war and plague in the Middle ages
macabre tortures conceived by madmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghastly - shockingly frightful or dreadful
  • Synonym: macabre - disturbing because of involvement with death or injury
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

Perhaps from Scots, variant of 'gruesome', from 'grue' (shudder, feel horror).
