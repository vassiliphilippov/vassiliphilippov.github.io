revulsion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvʌlʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈvʌlʃən / (BrE)
A strong feeling of disgust or intense aversion.

Examples

He looked over at him, lying there, asleep, and he felt a wave of revulsion.
Swiftly, came the revulsion, the protest.
Then suddenly there was a tremendous revulsion of popular feeling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - a strong feeling of dislike or disapproval
  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or unwillingness

Etymology

From Latin 'revulsio' (a pulling away), from 'revellere' (to pluck away).
