condone

1. VERB

/ kənˈdoʊn / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊn / (BrE)
to accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong or offensive to continue without protest or punishment

Examples

In other words, atrocities by savages wearing the uniform of the central government might be condoned, had the victims been serving the cause of dissident Katanga.
That could be understood, if not condoned.

Synset Examples

excuse someone's behavior
She condoned her husband's occasional infidelities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - to pardon or overlook an offense or mistake
  • Synonym: overlook - to ignore or disregard (a fault or offense)
  • Antonym: condemn - to express strong disapproval of

Etymology

From Latin 'condōnāre' (to forgive, remit), from 'con-' (altogether) + 'dōnāre' (to give).
