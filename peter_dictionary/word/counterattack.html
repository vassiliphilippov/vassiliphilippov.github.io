counterattack

1. VERB

/ ˌkaʊntərəˈtæk / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərəˈtæk / (BrE)
to attack in response to an enemy's attack; to make a retaliatory attack

Examples

They found it at the junction between Troops H and G, and prepared to counterattack.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attack - to take aggressive action against someone or something

Etymology

From noun 'counterattack', itself from 'counter-' (against) + 'attack'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntəɹəˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtæk / (BrE)
A military attack made in response to an enemy's offensive, typically to regain lost ground or halt an enemy's advance.

Examples

At the present time, the counter-attack takes the line that there's no more of the true spirit of "integration "in the North than in the South.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attack - an aggressive action or offensive against an enemy

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'attack'.
