whack

1. VERB

/ wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
To strike something or someone hard.

Examples

But the bullets whacked home before he finished his battle cry and Marvin Goulding fell dead.
Clayton swung into the saddle and whacked the stallion's rump.

Synset Examples

The teacher whacked the boy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike with force
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly
  • Degree Higher: smash - to strike with even greater force, often causing destruction
