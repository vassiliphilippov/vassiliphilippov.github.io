disband

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈbænd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbænd / (BrE)
(of an organization or group) to cease functioning or operating as a unit; to come to an end as an organized body.

Examples

Anderson operated three Havana automobile service stations and was commander of the Havana American Legion post before it disbanded since the start of Fidel Castro's regime.

Synset Examples

The political wing of the party dissolved after much internal fighting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to formally end the existence or operation of an organization or assembly
  • Degree Lower: disintegrate - to break up into parts or lose cohesion
  • Antonym: form - to bring together into an organized group; opposite of disband

Etymology

From Old French desbander (to disband, to break up a band or troop), from des- (apart) + bander (to band together).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈbænd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbænd / (BrE)
to cause (an organization, group, or formal body) to break up and stop functioning

Examples

When the end did come, and the schools were disbanded and abandoned, we felt and hoped that the machinery of psychological warfare would not be allowed to rust.

Synset Examples

the principal disbanded the political student organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to make an organization or official group cease to exist

Etymology

From Old French desbander (to break up an assembly), from des- (dis-) + bander (to band together).
