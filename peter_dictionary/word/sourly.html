sourly

1. ADV

/ ˈsaʊrli / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows resentment, displeasure, or ill-temper.

Examples

The door swung open, and Jess said sourly, "What the hell's the matter with you? "

Synset Examples

he complained sourly that the new rules only benefitted the managers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumpily - in a bad-tempered or sulky way
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic way
