coalition

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
An organization of individuals, groups, or countries united for a common purpose, especially through a formal pact or treaty.

Examples

The coalition was to prove inadvisable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a group formed by agreement for mutual benefit
  • Synonym: confederation - an organization consisting of parties or groups united in an alliance
  • Synonym: union - an organized association formed by uniting

Etymology

From Latin 'coalitio' (a uniting, growing together), from 'coalescere' (to grow together).
