wreath

1. NOUN

/ ɹiːθ / (AmE), / ɹiːθ / (BrE)
An arrangement of flowers, leaves, or other materials twisted or woven into a circular shape, typically used for decoration or ceremonial purposes.

Examples

He was an expert florist, tenderly dextrous in the arrangement of bouquets and wreaths.
Hence the title of the book, referring to the soldiers and subjects of the king; on the fatal battlefield of Poltava, to quote from the novel, "the wreath he twined for himself slipped down upon his people ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garland - a wreath of flowers and leaves, worn on the head or hung as a decoration

Etymology

Old English 'writha' (band, fillet), related to 'writhan' (to twist).
