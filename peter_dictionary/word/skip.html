2. VERB

/ skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to intentionally not attend or take part in (an event, activity, or obligation)

Examples

She had skipped her lunch hour in the fear that he might call while she was out.

Synset Examples

cut class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss - to fail to attend or be present at
  • Antonym: attend - to be present at

3. VERB

/ skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to move by making light, quick jumps or leaps, often in a playful or lively way

Examples

She hesitated, she hopped, she roll and rocked, skipped and jumped, but in some two weeks she started to pace, From that time to this she has shown steady improvement and now looks like one of the classiest things on the grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - to jump on one foot or to make a short, quick jump
  • Synonym: jump - to spring off the ground by a muscular effort of the legs and feet

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'skopa' (to run, leap).
