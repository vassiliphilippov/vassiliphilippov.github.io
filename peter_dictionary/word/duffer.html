duffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌf.ər / (AmE), / ˈdʌf.ə / (BrE)
A person who is incompetent or clumsy, especially in amateur sports such as golf.

Examples

But he also achieved something that endeared him to every duffer who ever flubbed a shot.
From the standpoint of the army of duffers, however, this was easily the most heartening exhibition they had had since Ben Hogan fell upon evil ways during his heyday and scored an 11 in the Texas open.
Until a few weeks ago, however, Arnold Palmer was some god-like creature who had nothing in common with the duffers.

Synset Examples

as a golfer he was only a duffer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungler - a person who habitually does things clumsily or unsuccessfully
  • Antonym: expert - a person who is very knowledgeable or skilled in a particular area

Etymology

Probably from dialectal 'duff' (to do poorly or fail) or possibly from 'duffer' (counterfeit coin) in British slang.
