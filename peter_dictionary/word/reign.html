reign

1. VERB

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
To hold and exercise sovereign power, especially as a monarch or ruler over a country or people.

Examples

Between the ponderous hulk and himself, in the valley over which Papa-san reigned, men had hidden high explosives, booby traps, and mines.
The old woman, stubbornly reigning in the house above the crashing waters took on an ominous reality.
I now ever look for Christ acording to His promises and those of the Old Testament as well, to appear again in glory to put away all sin and to reign in righteousness over the whole earth.

Synset Examples

Henry VIII reigned for a long time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - to exercise authority or control over; to govern
  • Synonym: govern - to conduct the policy, actions, and affairs of a state or organization
  • Antonym: abdicate - to renounce one's throne or sovereign power

Etymology

From Old French 'reignier', from Latin 'regnare' (to rule), from 'regnum' (kingdom, rule).

2. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
A period during which a person, group, or thing is dominant, influential, or exercises power.

Examples

The attempt to enforce that belief ushered in a reign of bootleggers, racketeers, hijackers and gangsters that led to a breakdown of law unparalleled in our history.
The reign of crime is over.

Synset Examples

he was helpless under the reign of his egotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - period during which someone or something exercises control or authority
  • Degree Higher: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control

Etymology

From Old French *reign*, from Latin *regnum* (kingdom, rule), from *rex* (king).

3. VERB

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
To be the most important, powerful, or influential; to predominate or prevail.

Examples

In the realm of physical sensations, sex reigns supreme.

Synset Examples

Money reigns supreme here
Hispanics predominate in this neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predominate - to be the greatest in number, importance, or influence
  • Synonym: dominate - to have a commanding influence over; to be the most noticeable
  • Antonym: subside - to become less intense or to lose importance (antonym)

Etymology

From Latin 'regnare' (to rule), from 'regnum' (kingdom).
