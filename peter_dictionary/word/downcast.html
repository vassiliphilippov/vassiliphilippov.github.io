downcast

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌkɑːst / (BrE)
(of a person's eyes or look) directed downward, especially in a manner suggesting loss of hope or modesty.

Examples

The eyes, blue and always somewhat downcast, possessed a sullen quality.

Synset Examples

a downcast glance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uplifted - (of eyes or gaze) directed upward

Etymology

From 'down' + 'cast', meaning 'to cast downward'.
