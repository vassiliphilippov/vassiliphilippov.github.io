vigilante

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪdʒɪˈlænti / (AmE), / ˌvɪdʒɪˈlanti / (BrE)
A person who takes the law into their own hands and acts without legal authority, often as a member of a self-appointed group.

Examples

We were discussing winged vigilantes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-appointed - acting without official authority or appointment

Etymology

From Spanish 'vigilante' (watchman, guard), from Latin 'vigilans' (keeping awake, watchful).
