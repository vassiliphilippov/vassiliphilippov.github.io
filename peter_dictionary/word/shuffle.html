shuffle

1. VERB

/ ˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌfəl / (BrE)
to walk by dragging one's feet along the ground rather than lifting them fully

Examples

He pulled with all his strength at the heavy, brass bound door, and shuffled along the wainscoted wall.
It was amazing how they had herded together for protection: an enormous matriarch in a quilted silk wrapper, rising from the breakfast table; a gross boy in his teens, shuffling in from the kitchen with a sandwich in his hands; a girl in her twenties, fat and sullen, descending the marble staircase; then all four gathering on the sofa to face the inquisitor.
He mumbled at her but let himself be led off inside the house, shuffling mightily to make it clear how weak and aged he was and how he was buffeted about by those who still had their wicked strength.

Synset Examples

he shuffled out of the room
We heard his feet shuffling down the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically from exhaustion
  • Antonym: stride - to walk long, decisive steps in a brisk way

Etymology

Perhaps of imitative origin or related to Middle English shovelen (to shove, push).

2. VERB

/ ˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
to move things or people around repeatedly or back and forth, often with a sense of uncertainty or to achieve a particular arrangement

Examples

We shuffle a large percentage of the cars across the river twice.

Synset Examples

He shuffled his funds among different accounts in various countries so as to avoid the IRS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rearrange - change the order or position of something

Etymology

Early 16th century, possibly related to 'shovel' (move with the feet), or from Middle English shoflen.
