defensible

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛn.sə.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛn.sɪ.bəl / (BrE)
Able to be defended against attack, criticism, or challenge.

Examples

Hence, the only defensible procedure is to repress any and every notion, unless it gives evidence that it is perfectly safe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indefensible - unable to be defended

Etymology

From Medieval Latin defensibilis, from Latin defendere (to defend).
