impromptu

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑmptu / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒmptjuː / (BrE)
Done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed; spontaneous or improvised.

Examples

There are some sharp and whipping lines and some hilariously funny situations - the best of the latter being a mass impromptu plunge into a nightclub tank where a "mermaid "is performing.

Synset Examples

his ad-lib comments showed poor judgment
an extemporaneous piano recital
an extemporary lecture
an extempore skit
an impromptu speech
offhand excuses
trying to sound offhanded and reassuring
an off-the-cuff toast
a few unrehearsed comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spontaneous - performed or occurring as a result of a sudden impulse; not planned
  • Antonym: prepared - made ready beforehand

Etymology

From French 'impromptu', from Latin 'in promptu' (in readiness, at hand).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɑmptu / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒmptjuː / (BrE)
A short speech, remark, or piece performed without advance preparation; something spoken or done extemporaneously.

Examples

Such was the impromptu that Voltaire gave to howls of laughter at Sans Souci and that was soon circulated in manuscript throughout the literary circles of Europe, to be printed sometime later, but with the name of Timon of Athens, the famous misanthrope, substituted for that of Rousseau.

Synset Examples

a witty impromptu must not sound premeditated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extemporization - the act of speaking or performing without preparation
  • Antonym: prepared - deliberately made or planned beforehand (as an antonym for spontaneous)

Etymology

From French 'impromptu', from Latin 'in promptu' (in readiness).
