snare

1. NOUN

/ snɛr / (AmE), / snɛə / (BrE)
A situation or thing that is deceptively attractive or traps someone unexpectedly; a trap or entanglement, especially one involving deception.

Examples

But it is becoming increasingly evident that such a hope is a snare.

Synset Examples

the exam was full of trap questions
it was all a snare and delusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device or situation that catches people unawares
  • Synonym: pitfall - a hidden or unsuspected danger or difficulty

Etymology

From Old English *snearu* (a snare, noose) and Old Norse *snara* (a snare).

2. VERB

/ snɛɚ / (AmE), / snɛə / (BrE)
to catch or entangle (someone or something) as if in a trap

Examples

She had been snared here by a vile sensuality that writhed around her throat in ever-tightening circles.

Synset Examples

The men trap foxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - to catch using a trap or in a trap

Etymology

From Middle English snare, from Old Norse snara (a noose, snare).
