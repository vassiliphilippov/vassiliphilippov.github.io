electroshock

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktroʊˌʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌʃɒk / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which a strong electric current is passed through the brain to induce a brief seizure, used especially for certain mental illnesses

Examples

It is believed that drug therapy and electroshock involve the former and psychotherapy the latter mechanism.
If, for instance, such a change is produced by one or a few insulin comas or electroshocks, previously inhibited conditioned reactions reappear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - a medical treatment in which seizures are electrically induced in anesthetized patients

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + shock (sudden disturbance), in reference to the electric current used in the procedure.
