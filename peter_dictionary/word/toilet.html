toilet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlət / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪt / (BrE)
A large bowl fitted with plumbing, used for the disposal of bodily waste.

Examples

Provision for emergency toilet facilities and disposal of human wastes will be an unfamiliar problem.
A covered container such as a kitchen garbage pail might do as a toilet.
They've got an old-fashioned toilet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavatory - another word for a room or receptacle for urination and defecation
  • Synonym: restroom - (primarily US) a room containing toilets and sinks for public use
  • Synonym: bathroom - (in US English) a room containing a toilet and usually a sink and bath or shower

Etymology

From French *toilette*, originally meaning a small cloth, later referring to dressing and grooming, and in English extended to the fixture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔɪlət / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪt / (BrE)
A room or small building containing a toilet or toilets, typically used for defecation and urination.

Examples

Back at the Factory-to-You with the other old maids, back there she was the youngest clerk and she was thirty-four, which made her young enough to resent the usual ideal working conditions, like the unventilated toilet with the door you had to hold shut while you sat down.
A lock on the toilet would encourage malingering and primping.
The toilet had n't had a sincere scrubbing in years and there were things written on the walls of the little boxed in place because you could n't keep the public out - entirely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restroom - public room with toilets (chiefly North American usage)
  • Synonym: lavatory - room with a toilet, especially in public or formal use
  • Synonym: bathroom - room containing a toilet and typically a bath or shower (esp. North American)
  • Synonym: outhouse - small outdoor building containing a simple toilet

Etymology

From French 'toilette' (small cloth; later dressing room), diminutive of 'toile' (cloth).
