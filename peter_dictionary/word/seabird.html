seabird

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A bird that lives near or on the sea, especially one that frequents coastal waters and the open ocean, such as gulls, pelicans, and albatrosses.

Examples

To starboard was a cape a thousand feet high, patched with ice and snow, populated by thousands of screaming sea birds.

Etymology

From sea + bird, referring to birds associated with marine environments.
