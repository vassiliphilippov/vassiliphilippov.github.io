infuriating

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfjʊriˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjʊə.ri.eɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely annoying or making someone very angry.

Examples

How infuriating all this was!
It's infuriating, this feeling that one is being picked on, continually, constantly.

Synset Examples

his cavelier curtness of manner was exasperating
I've had an exasperating day
her infuriating indifference
the ceaseless tumult of the jukebox was maddening

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: annoying - causing mild irritation or bother
  • Degree Higher: enraging - causing intense anger; making someone extremely angry

Etymology

From Latin *furia* (fury); related to the verb 'infuriate' (to make furious).
