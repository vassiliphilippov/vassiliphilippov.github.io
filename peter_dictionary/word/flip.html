flip

1. NOUN

/ flɪp / (AmE), / flɪp / (BrE)
An acrobatic movement in which the body rotates in the air, bringing the feet over the head and returning to an upright position, either forward or backward.

Examples

It is made up of tumbling, which might be said to start with a somersault, run through such stunts as headstands, handstands, cartwheels, backbends, and culminate in nearly impossible combinations of aerial flips and twists and apparatus work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somersault - acrobatic movement where the body rotates over the head and lands upright

2. VERB

/ flɪp / (AmE), / flɪp / (BrE)
To toss something, especially a coin, lightly into the air so that it turns over and lands to show which side is face up, often to make a choice or decision.

Examples

There was no time to pick out a penny; I got a coin between my thumb and forefinger, leaned my elbows in a very natural and casual manner on top of the truck cab and flipped my little missile.
At Fort Ethan Allen the ever-present wind off Lake Champlain could readily flip a puny man-made thing like an airplane if the pilot miscalculated.
There was no cash around; everyone was flipping silver dollars.

Synset Examples

I don't know what to do--I may as well flip a coin!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or casually

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing sound or action of quick movement.

3. VERB

/ flɪp / (AmE), / flɪp / (BrE)
To move (a switch, control, etc.) quickly in order to turn something on, off, or to a different setting.

Examples

On impulse, he flipped up the heat control on his coverall and slid back the hatch of the bubble.

Synset Examples

switch on the light
throw the lever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - to turn (a device) on or off by pressing or flipping a control
