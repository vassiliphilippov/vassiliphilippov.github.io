cultured

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌltʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌltʃəd / (BrE)
Having or showing good taste, manners, and a broad knowledge of the arts, literature, or other intellectual pursuits.

Examples

However, even if the latent demand for demythologization is not nearly as widespread as we are claiming, at least among the cultured elements of the population there tends to be an almost complete indifference to the church and its traditional message of sin and grace.
And yet this is exactly the risk we run when we assume, as we too often do, that we can continue to preach the gospel in a form that makes it seem incredible and irrelevant to cultured men.

Synset Examples

cultivated speech
cultured Bostonians
cultured tastes
a genteel old lady
polite society

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncultured - lacking refinement or good manners; not cultured
  • Synonym: refined - showing or having good taste and manners
  • Synonym: civilized - having an advanced stage of social, cultural, and moral development

Etymology

From Latin 'cultūra' (cultivation, culture), via French 'culturer'.
