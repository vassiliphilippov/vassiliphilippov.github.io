Victrola

1. NOUN

/ vɪkˈtroʊlə / (AmE), / vɪkˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
A brand name for an early type of phonograph or record player, especially one with a large horn, widely used in the early 20th century.

Examples

What you were looking for (unless you make a hobby of collecting old tennis rackets and fly screens) eludes me, but to judge from phonograph records scattered about a fumed oak Victrola, you danced two tangos and a paso doble, which must have been fairly enervating in that milieu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonograph - an early device for playing recorded music using records

Etymology

Trademark, introduced by Victor Talking Machine Company in 1906.
