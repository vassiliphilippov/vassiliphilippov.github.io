axle

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksəl / (AmE), / ˈæksəl / (BrE)
A rod or shaft that passes through the center of a wheel or group of wheels, enabling rotation or support.

Examples

Then he hunkered down on the heels of his handmade boots, peered into the orderly chaos of axle, shock absorber, and spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaft - a long, narrow part serving as an axis or handle

Etymology

From Middle English axel, from Old Norse *öxull*, related to Old English *eaxl* (shoulder, axis).
