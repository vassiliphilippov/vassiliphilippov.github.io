coax

1. VERB

/ koʊks / (AmE), / kəʊks / (BrE)
to gently persuade or influence someone to do something, often by using flattery or gradual encouragement

Examples

Or a wife may make sudden demands that she be courted, flattered or coaxed, simply because she needs her ego lifted.
Maggie could n't seem to get her strength back or catch up with herself with all she had to do: there was the big basket of clothes to be coaxed through the rackety old washer and lugged out and lugged back; there was the daily round of household chores in which Maggie insisted on participating.

Synset Examples

He palavered her into going along

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cajole - to persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery
  • Synonym: wheedle - to use flattery or trickery to persuade someone to do something
  • Antonym: force - to make someone do something against their will

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete Dutch coksen (to fondle, to coax), or related to cock (rooster), referring to soothing or petting.
