costly

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑstli / (AmE), / ˈkɒstli / (BrE)
Entailing great loss or sacrifice; obtained at a high price, especially in terms of suffering or effort.

Examples

Marshall Formby of Plainview, former chairman of the Texas Highway Commission, suggested a plan to fill by appointment future vacancies in the Legislature and Congress, eliminating the need for costly special elections.
That tumultuous, painful and costly experience shows clearly that a law expressing a moral judgment cannot be enforced when it has little correspondence with the general view of society.
I'll decide... - War is costly.

Synset Examples

a dearly-won victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expensive - involving a high monetary cost

Etymology

From cost + -ly; Middle English, from Old French coster (to cost).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑstli / (AmE), / ˈkɒstli / (BrE)
having a high price; expensive

Examples

He was standing there, he thought, in Virginia, in the thickening dusk, in a costly greatcoat that had belonged to another Jew.

Synset Examples

costly jewelry
high-priced merchandise
much too dear for my pocketbook
a pricey restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expensive - costing a lot of money; high-priced
  • Antonym: cheap - costing little money; low-priced

Etymology

From Middle English costli, from cost (cost) + -ly (adjectival suffix).
