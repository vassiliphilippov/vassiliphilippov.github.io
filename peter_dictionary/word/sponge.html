sponge

1. VERB

/ spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
to clean or moisten something by wiping it with a sponge

Examples

After selecting a sheet and inspecting it for flaws (even the best sometimes has foreign' nubbins' on its surface), I sponge it thoroughly on both sides with clean, cold water.
For an antique effect on jars, brush Creek-Turn brown toner on bisque ware and sponge it off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wipe - to clean or dry a surface by rubbing

Etymology

From Latin 'spongia' via Old French 'esponge' and Middle English 'spunge'.

2. NOUN

/ spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
A porous material, often made from the skeleton of certain marine animals or manufactured from rubber or cellulose, used to absorb liquids or clean surfaces.

Examples

The great absorbency of this tissue and the fact that it is easier to control than a sponge makes it an ideal tool for the watercolorist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubber - a tool used for cleaning surfaces, especially rougher than a sponge

Etymology

From Latin 'spongia', from Ancient Greek 'spongia' (sponge).
