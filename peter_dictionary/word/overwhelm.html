overwhelm

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to have a strong emotional or sensory effect on someone so that they are unable to respond or cope; to overpower emotionally or mentally

Examples

At that moment Kipling was overwhelmed with awed amazement, suddenly recalling that these identical details of scene, action and word had occurred to him in a dream six weeks earlier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overpower - to defeat or have a very strong effect on emotionally or physically
  • Synonym: submerge - to immerse or cover completely, especially with water or feeling
  • Antonym: calm - to make or become less agitated or disturbed emotionally

Etymology

From Middle English 'overwhelmen', from 'over-' + 'whelmen', meaning 'to overturn, capsize'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to give someone too many tasks, responsibilities, or problems so that they become unable to cope

Examples

The crisis later on when debts seemed about to overwhelm me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to give someone a heavy load of tasks or responsibilities

Etymology

From Old English *oferhwhelmian* (to submerge), from *ofer* (over) + *hwhelmian* (to cover, overwhelm).
