sunken

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌŋkən / (AmE), / ˈsʌŋkən / (BrE)
(of facial features or a surface) having fallen to a lower level than the surrounding area; hollow or depressed.

Examples

Abel Poupin, a tall man with sunken cheeks and deep-set eyes, got to his feet.
He was a square brick of a man with a moon round face and sunken Polish features.

Synset Examples

hunger gave their faces a sunken look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollow - having a space or depression inside; not solid
  • Antonym: protruding - sticking out or projecting

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'sink', from Old English 'sincan' (to descend, subside).
