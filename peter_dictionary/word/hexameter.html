hexameter

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæmɪtər / (AmE), / hɛkˈsæmɪtə / (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of six metrical feet, especially used in classical Greek and Latin poetry.

Examples

At the least, however, one may conclude that Geometric potters sensed a logical order; their principles of composition stand very close to those which appear in the Homeric epics and the hexameter line.

Etymology

From Latin *hexameter*, from Ancient Greek *hexametros* (having six measures), from *hex* (six) + *metron* (measure).
