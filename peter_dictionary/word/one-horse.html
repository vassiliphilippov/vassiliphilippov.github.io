one-horse

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʌnˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwʌnˌhɔːs / (BrE)
(of a town, institution, etc.) small, insignificant, or provincial; lacking importance or sophistication.

Examples

A Yankee sergeant gave the following description of his sweetheart: "My girl is none of your one-horse girls.

Synset Examples

a jerkwater college
passed a series of poky little one-horse towns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerkwater - small and remote; insignificant (US, informal)
  • Synonym: poky - unpleasantly small and cramped; provincial
  • Synonym: backwater - a place or condition in which no development or progress is occurring

Etymology

Originally referring to a vehicle or town served by only one horse (i.e., humble or minor in capacity).
