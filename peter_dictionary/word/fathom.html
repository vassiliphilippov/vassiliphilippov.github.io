fathom

1. VERB

/ ˈfæðəm / (AmE), / ˈfaðəm / (BrE)
to understand or comprehend something difficult or mysterious; to figure out

Examples

What it was Kate could not fathom.
How foolish it was to try to fathom the truth in an area where only faith would suffice.
No, Kayabashi was bringing his associates here for a specific purpose and Rector would not be able to fathom it until they arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasp - to understand fully
  • Synonym: comprehend - to understand
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to understand

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'fæðm' (outstretched arms, embrace), later used metaphorically for measuring depth and understanding.
