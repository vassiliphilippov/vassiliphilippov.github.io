fleshy

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɛʃi / (AmE), / ˈflɛʃi / (BrE)
(of a person or part of the body) having a lot of flesh; plump or with substantial soft tissue, often implying a larger build that can support extra weight.

Examples

It was enough for people to know that at one time he had looked down the street at the fleshy suppleness of a woman he had consumed - watching her become thinner and thinner in the distance, as thin as the seams on her stockings, and still thinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plump - slightly fat in a way that looks healthy and attractive
  • Degree Higher: fat - having a lot of excess flesh
  • Antonym: thin - having little flesh or fat on the body

Etymology

From Old English 'flǣsc' (flesh) + '-y'.
