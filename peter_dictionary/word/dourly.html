dourly

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊrli / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəli / (BrE)
In a sullen, gloomy, or stern manner.

Examples

There's no greater catastrophe in the universe, she reflected dourly, impaling tender green beans on the silver fork, than the dwindling away of a family.

Synset Examples

he sat in his chair dourly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullenly - in a sulky, gloomy manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a joyful, happy manner

Etymology

Formed from 'dour' + '-ly', with 'dour' from Middle English, from Scottish, probably from Latin 'durus' (hard).
