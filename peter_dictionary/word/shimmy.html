shimmy

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
to shake or tremble with quick, slight movements

Examples

It is not as convenient as the old type toothbrush and the paste tends to shimmy of the bristles.

Synset Examples

His voice wobbled with restrained emotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move to and fro with quick movements
  • Synonym: vibrate - to move back and forth rapidly

Etymology

Variant of 'chemise' or possibly from English dialect 'shimmy', meaning a dance involving shaking movements.

2. VERB

/ ˈʃɪmi / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmi / (BrE)
to perform a dance involving rapid shaking or trembling of the body or shoulders, especially the shoulders and hips

Examples

She also taught them to sing "I wish I could shimmy like my sister Kate ".

Etymology

Originally 1910s, probably an alteration of 'chemise' (referring to a type of dance or movement of the dress).
