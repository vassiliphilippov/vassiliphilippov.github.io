corrode

1. VERB

/ kəˈroʊd / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊd / (BrE)
(of water, air, or acid) to slowly destroy or damage (something, especially metal) by a chemical action; also, to gradually weaken or destroy something non-physical.

Examples

But bitterness had more cause to remain, even increasingly to corrode.

Synset Examples

The acid corroded the metal
The steady dripping of water rusted the metal stopper in the sink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erode - to gradually wear away or be worn away, especially by natural forces like wind or water
  • Synonym: rust - to corrode, especially iron or steel, through oxidation
  • Synonym: decompose - to break down or rot, especially through chemical processes

Etymology

From Latin 'corrōdere', from 'com-' (together) + 'rōdere' (to gnaw).
