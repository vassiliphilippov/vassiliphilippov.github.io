divest

1. VERB

/ daɪˈvɛst / (AmE), / daɪˈvɛst / (BrE)
to deprive someone of power, rights, possessions, or property, especially by legal or formal means

Examples

The editorial concerned legislative proposals to ease the tax burden on du Pont stockholders, in connection with the United States Supreme court ruling that du Pont must divest itself of its extensive General Motors stock holdings.

Synset Examples

The Nazis stripped the Jews of all their assets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deprive - to take something away from someone
  • Synonym: strip - to remove possessions, rights, or qualities from someone

Etymology

From Latin 'divestire', from 'dis-' (apart) + 'vestire' (to clothe).
