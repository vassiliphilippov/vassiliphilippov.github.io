unerring

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɛːrɪŋ / (BrE)
Always accurate or never making mistakes; not liable to error.

Examples

Mercer's lyrics are characterized by an unerring ear for rhythmic nuances, a puckish sense of humor expressed in language with a colloquial flair.

Synset Examples

the Church was...theoretically inerrant and omnicompetent
lack an inerrant literary sense
an unerring marksman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infallible - incapable of making mistakes or being wrong
  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From un- (not) + erring, from Latin errare (to wander, to err).
