taint

1. NOUN

/ teɪnt / (AmE), / teɪnt / (BrE)
a trace or state of contamination or impurity

Examples

After how many generations is such wealth (mounting all the while through the manipulations of high finance) purified of taint?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the state of being contaminated
  • Antonym: purity - the state of being pure, free from contamination

Etymology

From Old French *teint* (stain), from Latin *tingere* (to dye, color).

2. VERB

/ teɪnt / (AmE), / teɪnt / (BrE)
to contaminate something so that it becomes harmful or carries disease, especially by introducing microorganisms or pathogens

Examples

The Joker's tainted hundreds of chemicals at the source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infect - to affect with a disease-causing agent
  • Antonym: cleanse - to purify or remove contamination

Etymology

From Old French 'teint', past participle of 'teindre' (to dye), from Latin 'tingere' (to dye or tinge).
