norethandrolone

1. NOUN

/ nɔˌrɛθˈændrəˌloʊn / (AmE), / nɔˌrɛθˈændrəˌləʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic progestational hormone used in oral contraceptives and in the treatment of endometriosis.

Examples

A high protein diet, calcium lactate supplements, and norethandrolone failed to change the skeletal complaint or the severe muscle weakness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: norethindrone - A similar synthetic progestin hormone, also used in contraceptives and related treatments

Etymology

From 'nor-' (indicating chemical modification by removal of a group), 'ethan(d)-' (referring to ethyl/ethane), and 'androlone' (from androgen, a class of steroid hormones).
