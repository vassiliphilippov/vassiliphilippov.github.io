upland

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌp.lænd / (AmE), / ˈʌp.lənd / (BrE)
(of land) situated in high or hilly areas, away from lowlands or the coast.

Examples

Close to two million game birds were harvested on 1500 commercial and private shooting preserves, and on State Game Commission controlled upland game areas during the 1960 - 61 season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowland - land that is low and usually level, especially in contrast to upland

Etymology

From Old English 'ūp' (up) + 'land' (land).
