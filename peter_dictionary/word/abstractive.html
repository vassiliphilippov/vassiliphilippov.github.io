abstractive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / əbˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or having the ability to abstract; involving the process of generalizing by extracting common features from specific examples.

Examples

Later abstractive and rational processes may indicate errors of judgment in these apprehensions of value, but the apprehensions themselves are the primary stuff of experience.

Synset Examples

abstractive analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical existence

Etymology

Formed from 'abstract' + '-ive', following the pattern of Latin-derived adjectives.
