simulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the imitation of the operation or behavior of a process or situation, usually for the purposes of training, research, or testing

Examples

In certain other pictures, however, Braque began to paint areas in exact simulation of wood graining or marbleizing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something

Etymology

From Latin simulatio, from simulare (to imitate), from similis (like).
