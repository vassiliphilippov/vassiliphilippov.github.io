nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.treɪt / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.treɪt / (BrE)
a compound containing the nitrate group (NO₃), such as a salt or ester of nitric acid

Examples

The small reaction occurring at 337 ` C is probably caused by decomposition of occluded nitrates, and perhaps by a small amount of some hydrous material other than **f.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nitrite - compound containing the nitrite group (NO₂)

Etymology

From French nitrate, from nitre (niter) + -ate (indicating a salt or ester).
