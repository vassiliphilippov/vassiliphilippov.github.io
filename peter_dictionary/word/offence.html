offence

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
the action of attacking an enemy, especially in war or sport; an aggressive action or attack

Examples

There will be no mitigation of these offences until all art schools, whether independent or attached to universities have separate accreditation - as do medical schools - by an art accreditation group such as the "National Association of Schools of Art ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defence - the action of protecting against attack or harm
  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive action; an act of attacking
  • Synonym: offense - American spelling of 'offence' meaning attacking action

Etymology

From Old French 'offense', from Latin 'offensa' (a striking against, injury).
