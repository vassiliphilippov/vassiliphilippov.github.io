well-read

1. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈrɛd / (AmE), / wɛl ˈrɛd / (BrE)
Having a lot of knowledge about many subjects, especially as a result of reading a lot of books and other materials.

Examples

This conception was taken up by the early Church Fathers and by canon lawyers and theologians in the Middle Ages; and More was far too well read not to have come across it in one or several of the forms thus given it.

Synset Examples

respect for a well-read man
well-read in medieval history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learned - having acquired much knowledge by study
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general
