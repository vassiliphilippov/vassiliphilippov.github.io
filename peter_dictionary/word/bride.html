bride

1. NOUN

/ braɪd / (AmE), / braɪd / (BrE)
a woman on her wedding day or just after the marriage ceremony

Examples

Or was it her own first ball as mistress of this big house, a Van Rensselaer bride from way upstate near Albany, from Rensselaerwyck.
Enjoy being a bride while you can ".
There was no room for company in the tiny Weaning House (where the Albright boys always took their brides, till they could get a house and a farm of their own).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: groom - a man on his wedding day or just after the marriage ceremony

Etymology

Old English brȳd (bride), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ braɪd / (AmE), / braɪd / (BrE)
A woman on her wedding day or just before and after the event of her marriage.

Examples

The bride, the groom and the angel... -

Related Terms

  • Antonym: groom - a man on his wedding day or just before and after the event of his marriage

Etymology

Old English brȳd (bride, betrothed woman); related to Dutch bruid, German Braut.
