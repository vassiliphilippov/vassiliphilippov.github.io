degas

1. VERB

/ diˈɡæs / (AmE), / diˈɡɑːs / (BrE)
to remove gas from (a liquid or solid, especially by means of reduced pressure or heating)

Examples

The mixture was then extracted with alkali and with water following which the carbon tetrachloride was distilled on a Vigreux column, a 25 % center cut being retained which was then degassed under vacuum in the presence of * * f.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outgas - release gas, particularly from a solid or liquid

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (meaning removal) and 'gas'.
