postscript

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
A note added at the end of a letter, after the signature.

Examples

She added a postscript begging me to be careful about drinking.
PS. Tell Heywood I'm sorry I put a knife to his throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PS - abbreviation for postscript

Etymology

From Latin 'postscriptum' meaning 'written after'.
