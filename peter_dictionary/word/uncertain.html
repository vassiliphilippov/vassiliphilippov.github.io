uncertain

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɝːtən / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːtən / (BrE)
not known for sure or not definite; not established beyond doubt or still undecided

Examples

For example, there was sheet music with the word "jazz "in the title, to illustrate how a word of uncertain origin took hold.
And yet we obviously also believe that the avoidance of the disaster depends in some obscure or at least uncertain way on the details of how we behave.
The calibration of piezoelectric sensors in terms of the particle parameters is very uncertain.
A free man at the start of a long journey whose conclusion is uncertain.

Synset Examples

an uncertain future
a manuscript of uncertain origin
plans are still uncertain
changes of great if uncertain consequences
without further evidence his story must remain uncertain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certain - known for sure; established beyond doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'incertus', from 'in-' (not) + 'certus' (certain).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɝːtən / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːtn̩ / (BrE)
not feeling confident or sure about something; lacking certainty or assurance.

Examples

Even Mr. Miller himself seems uncertain on this score.
Dr. Hilprecht was uncertain as to the language used by the ancient priest in his dream.
Does that not suggest to you an uncertain and uneasy, not to say confused, state of the public mind?
Hernandez looked suddenly uncertain.
A great deal of labor we have as well, for we are too uncertain of where trust may be placed ".

Synset Examples

uncertain of his convictions
unsure of himself and his future
moving with uncertain (or unsure) steps
an uncertain smile
touched the ornaments with uncertain fingers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty; sure of oneself
  • Synonym: unsure - not certain or confident; doubtful or hesitant

Etymology

From Latin 'certus' (sure, settled), with prefix 'un-' meaning 'not'.
