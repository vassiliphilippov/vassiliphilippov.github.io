soundproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaʊndˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd.pruːf / (BrE)
(of a room, building, or material) not allowing sound to pass through or in; impervious to sound.

Examples

Guns were going off all over Washington City these days, because of the celebrations, and the theatre was not soundproof.

Synset Examples

a soundproof room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soundproofed - made so as to not allow sound to pass through
  • Synonym: noiseproof - impervious to noise
  • Antonym: sound-permeable - allowing sound to pass through

Etymology

Formed from 'sound' + 'proof', meaning resistant to the passage of sound.
