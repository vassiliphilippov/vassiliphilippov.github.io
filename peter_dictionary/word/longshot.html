longshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌʃɒt / (BrE)
A photograph or film shot taken from a considerable distance, showing a broad view of the scene.

Examples

The finished - and drastically cut - product would begin with a hazy longshot of Joyce entering the suds, then bursting above the pool's surface clad in layers of lavender lather, and I had a hunch this item was going to sell tons and tons of soap; even to clean men and boys.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: close-up - a photograph or film shot taken at close range

Etymology

Formed from 'long' (distance) + 'shot' (photographic/film capture).
