pompously

1. ADV

/ ˈpɑːmpəsli / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows self-importance and excessive dignity or pride.

Examples

Among my school and neighborhood friends, during the next months, I bragged and swaggered and pompously described my impending date.

Synset Examples

he pompously described his achievements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogantly - in a way that shows an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: modestly - in a way that is humble or not boastful

Etymology

From 'pompous' (from Old French *pompos*, from Late Latin *pomposus*, from Latin *pompa* 'pomp') + -ly.
