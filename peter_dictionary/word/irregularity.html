irregularity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛɡjəˈlɛrəti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛɡjʊˈlarəti / (BrE)
An instance or act of behavior that breaks rules, etiquette, custom, or accepted standards, especially in a formal process or procedure.

Examples

The Fulton County Grand Jury said Friday an investigation of Atlanta's recent primary election produced "no evidence "that any irregularities took place.
The September October term jury had been charged by Fulton Superior Court Judge Durwood Pye to investigate reports of possible "irregularities "in the hard-fought primary which was won by Mayor-nominate Ivan Allen Jr..
Many local citizens feared that there would be irregularities at the polls, and Williams got himself a permit to carry a gun and promised an orderly election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violation - an act that breaks a law, rule, or agreement
  • Synonym: transgression - an act that goes against a law, rule, or code of conduct
  • Antonym: compliance - conformity with rules or standards

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *irregularitas*, from Latin *irregularis* (not according to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛɡjʊˈlarəti / (BrE)
an uneven or asymmetrical feature in shape or spatial pattern; a lack of regular or smooth form

Examples

The dirt on the soiled objects is mechanically held by surface irregularities to some extent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symmetry - the quality of being made up of exactly similar parts facing each other or around an axis
  • Antonym: regularity - the state of being even, uniform, or in a regular pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'irregularitas', from 'irregularis' (not regular).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛɡjəˈlærɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not occurring at regular intervals or lacking a fixed pattern; unevenness or unpredictability.

Examples

The original electrocardiograph primarily indicates irregularities in the heartbeat, but today's techniques allow exact measurements of the flow of blood through the aorta, dimensioning of the heart and its chambers, and a much more detailed study of each heartbeat.
When irregularity and variation prevent summarizing, information is written in complete detail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regularity - state of being regular or consistent

Etymology

From Latin 'irregularitas', from 'ir-' (not) + 'regularis' (regular).
