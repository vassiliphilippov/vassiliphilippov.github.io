decree

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkri / (AmE), / dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
an official order issued by a legal authority, especially a court or government, that has the force of law

Examples

It should also be recognized that the problem of rural tenancy cannot be solved by administrative decrees alone.
The District Courts, in the framing of equitable decrees, are clothed' with large discretion to model their judgments to fit the exigencies of the particular case'.
The Government submitted its proposed decree on October 25, 1957.
The decrees proposed by the amici curiae were filed in August of 1958.

Synset Examples

a friend in New Mexico said that the order caused no trouble out there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Synonym: injunction - a judicial order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific act

Etymology

From Old French *decre*, from Latin *decretum* (something decided), from *decernere* (to decide).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈkri / (AmE), / dɪˈkriː / (BrE)
to order or decide something officially, especially by a formal legal or authoritative order

Examples

Furthermore, Bern decrees that we must do as we are ordered by the Council, preach only the word of God and stop meddling in politics "!
Two Americans and seven Cubans were executed by firing squads today as Castro military tribunals began decreeing the death penalty for captured invasion forces and suspected collaborators.

Synset Examples

The King only can decree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - to give an authoritative instruction
  • Synonym: proclaim - to declare officially or publicly
  • Antonym: revoke - to officially cancel a decree or order

Etymology

From Old French 'decreer', from Latin 'decretum' (decision, edict).
