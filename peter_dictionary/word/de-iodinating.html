de-iodinating

1. ADJ

/ diːˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an enzyme, agent, or process) removing iodine from a compound, especially in a chemical or biological context.

Examples

Besides proteolytic enzymes the thyroid possesses de-iodinating enzymes.
This de-iodinating enzyme is effective against mono - and di-iodotyrosine, but does not de-iodinate thyroxine or tri-iodothyronine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iodinating - adding iodine to a compound

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (removal of) and 'iodinate' (to treat or combine with iodine); 'iodine' from French 'iode', from Greek 'ioeidēs' (violet-colored).
