maggot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡət / (AmE), / ˈmæɡət / (BrE)
the larval stage of a fly, especially the housefly or blowfly, typically found in decaying organic matter

Examples

Still another, annoyed by the brevity of a recently received missive, wrote: "Yore letter was short and sweet, jist like a roasted maget ".
We could see maggots moving.
I haven't seen such a sorry-looking heap of maggot shit in all my life.

Etymology

From Middle English *magot*, possibly a diminutive of *Magge*, a pet form of the name Margaret, alluding to the worm-like movement.
