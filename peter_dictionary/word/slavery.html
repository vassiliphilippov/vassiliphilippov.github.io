slavery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvəri / (BrE)
The state or condition of being owned by and forced to work for someone else without having the freedom to leave; the practice or system of owning slaves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free from control or restriction
  • Synonym: servitude - the condition of being a servant or subject to someone else

Etymology

From slave + -ery. Ultimately from Medieval Latin 'sclavus' (slave).
