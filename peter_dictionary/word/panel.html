panel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænəl / (AmE), / ˈpænəl / (BrE)
a flat, usually rectangular piece that forms a distinct part of a surface or structure, such as a door, wall, or vehicle

Examples

There was a blur just under my focus of vision, a crash; the car's far windshield panel turned into a silver web with a dark hole in the center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: section - a distinct part or subdivision of something

Etymology

From Old French 'panel', diminutive of 'panne' meaning piece of cloth, from Latin 'pannus'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpænəl / (AmE), / ˈpænəl / (BrE)
to cover or decorate (a surface, especially a wall) with panels

Examples

While Mr. Blatz was putting up the pegboards and starting the workbench, Mr. Crombie told him of this idea about paneling the whole end of the cellar.

Synset Examples

panel the walls with wood
