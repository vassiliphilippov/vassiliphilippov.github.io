mousy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊzi / (BrE)
(of a person) quiet, timid, and lacking confidence or presence; easily overlooked.

Examples

Somewhat to his surprise he found that one girl, whom he would never have considered for the job since she had appeared somewhat mousy and also had been in the office a relatively short time, did the most outstanding job of playing the role of receptionist, showing wit, sparkle, and aplomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others
  • Antonym: bold - showing ability to take risks; confident and courageous

Etymology

From 'mouse' + '-y', likening a person to the characteristics of a mouse.
