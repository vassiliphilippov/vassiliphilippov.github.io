subsidy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.sə.di / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.di / (BrE)
A sum of money granted by a government or other authority to help an industry or business keep the price of a commodity or service low or to support its activities.

Examples

Have you considered gradual withdrawal of subsidies to your in plant feeding operation?

Synset Examples

a subsidy for research in artificial intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given by an organization for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: tax - a compulsory financial charge imposed by a government

Etymology

From Latin subsidium (support, assistance).
