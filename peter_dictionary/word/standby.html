standby

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænd.baɪ / (AmE), / ˈstænd.baɪ / (BrE)
a thing or person that can be depended on when needed, especially as a substitute or backup

Examples

The "wooden-leg "gag of vaudeville, another standby of this sort, had endless variations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backup - an alternative in case the first option fails
  • Synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main source of support

2. ADJ

/ ˈstændˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstændˌbaɪ / (BrE)
Kept ready to be used in case of an emergency or when needed.

Examples

A police captain is on call 24 hours a day.

Synset Examples

a standby generator
a standby crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - kept back for future or emergency use
