befit

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈfɪt / (BrE)
to be appropriate for or suitable to; to be proper for

Examples

As befits a queen, a bumblebee female is rather choosy and may spend considerable time searching for a suitable nesting place.
What remained lacked the original verve but it was at least dignified, as befitting the tragic circumstances.

Synset Examples

This kind of behavior does not suit a young woman!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suit - to be appropriate or fitting for someone or something
  • Antonym: misbecome - to be unsuitable or improper for

Etymology

From be- (prefix) + fit (suitable), dating to Middle English.
