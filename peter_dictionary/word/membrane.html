membrane

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
A thin, flexible sheet of material that acts as a barrier or lining, often used in biological, technical, or industrial contexts.

Examples

Removal of the suspended solids by a membrane filter yielded an average effluent containing only 20 mg / l BOD.

Etymology

From Latin membrāna (parchment, membrane), from membrum (limb, member).
