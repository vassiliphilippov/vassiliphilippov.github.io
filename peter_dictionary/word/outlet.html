outlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɛt / (BrE)
A shop or store that sells goods directly to the public, often at lower prices, especially one that is part of a chain or specializes in discounted merchandise.

Examples

Moreover, the larger and more aggressive mass distribution outlets and chain stores have insisted on high quality - and the customer seems to have caught on.
How can you cash in on this fast-growing type of outlet and still maintain relationships with older existing outlets which are still important?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - a place where goods are sold to the public
  • Synonym: shop - a business that sells goods to the public

Etymology

From Middle English 'outlet', formed from 'out' + 'let'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɛt / (BrE)
A socket in a wall or other surface where electrical devices can be plugged in to receive power.

Examples

"Now ", he said, "we got to put in some outlets for them power tools; then a couple of fluorescent lamps over the workbench an' I guess we 're about through down here ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socket - a device in a wall allowing electrical equipment to be connected to the power supply
