trademark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdˌmɑːk / (BrE)
A symbol, word, or phrase officially registered or established by use as representing a company or product.

Examples

It must be marked by a patience and persistence which have not always been its trademark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand - a name, term, or symbol identifying a seller's products

Etymology

From trade + mark, where 'mark' means a sign or symbol.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdmɑːk / (BrE)
A distinctive feature or characteristic identifying a person, group, or thing.

Examples

Though merely clear glass, it was a distinctive trade mark for an aspiring actor who hoped to imprint himself upon the memories of producers.
Rumor had it he slipped two small rocks under each victim's head as a sort of trademark.
The expression was his trade-mark, his open sesame to good luck, and his prayer that pilot and plane would always return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallmark - a distinctive feature, especially one characteristic of an individual or group

Etymology

From 'trade' + 'mark', originally referring to a legally registered sign.
