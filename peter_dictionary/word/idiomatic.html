idiomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdiəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Using, containing, or conforming to expressions that are natural to a native speaker of a language; characteristic of an idiom or particular language.

Examples

Meager and shabby by-products linger to haunt our memories of a once mighty protagonist; a maladroit reharmonization of our National Anthem (The Star-Spangled Banner); a poor attempt to write an idiomatic jazz concerto; a circus polka for elephants; his hopes that the tunes from his old music might be used for popular American commercial songs!

Synset Examples

idiomatic English

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense without metaphor or idiom
  • Synonym: colloquial - (of language) used in ordinary or familiar conversation, not formal or literary

Etymology

From Greek idiōmatikos (peculiar, characteristic), from idiōma (peculiarity, idiom).
