screaming

1. ADJ

/ ˈskriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of pain, emotion, etc.) extremely intense or severe; so strong as to provoke or seem to provoke a scream.

Examples

A shot caught him and straightened him up in screaming pain; a following volley of shots shattered glass, ripped the ceiling, and sent him lurching heavily from the table.

Synset Examples

in screaming agony
a screaming rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intense - of extreme force, degree, or strength
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or strong

2. NOUN

/ ˈskriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
A loud, sharp, and piercing cry, often expressing pain, fear, or excitement.

Examples

There was much shouting and screaming.

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shouting - the act of speaking or calling out loudly
  • Synonym: yelling - the act of uttering a loud cry
  • Antonym: whispering - the act of speaking very softly
