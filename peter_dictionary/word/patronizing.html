patronizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a manner, tone, or behavior) displaying an attitude of superiority, often treating others as if they are less intelligent or important.

Examples

The collective by which I address you in the title above is neither patronizing nor jocose but an exact industrial term in use among professional thieves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condescending - showing that you consider yourself better or more intelligent than others
  • Antonym: respectful - showing deference and respect to others

Etymology

From 'patronize' + '-ing', from French 'patroniser', from Latin 'patronus' (patron).
