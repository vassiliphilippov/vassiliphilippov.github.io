unvarying

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɛriɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈveəriɪŋ / (BrE)
not changing or varying; remaining constant or uniform

Examples

Thomas' findings are, first, "that the individual items vary greatly and unpredictably in their persistence "; but, second, "that the semantic groups are surprisingly unvarying in their average persistence " (as between M-K and I-E.

Synset Examples

maintained a constant temperature
principles of unvarying validity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - remaining the same; not changing
  • Synonym: changeless - without change; always the same
  • Antonym: variable - able or likely to change
  • Synonym: invariable - never changing; unalterable

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) + varying (present participle of vary, from Latin variāre).
