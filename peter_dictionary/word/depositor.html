depositor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑːzɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪtə / (BrE)
a person who places money in a bank or similar financial institution for safekeeping

Examples

Dewey Lawrence, a Tyler lawyer representing the Texas Bankers Association, sounded the opposition keynote when he said it would force banks to violate their contractual obligations with depositors and undermine the confidence of bank customers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: withdrawer - a person who withdraws money from a bank

Etymology

From deposit + -or, indicating a person who deposits.
