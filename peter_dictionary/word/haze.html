haze

1. NOUN

/ heɪz / (AmE), / heɪz / (BrE)
A thin mist, smoke, or dust in the air that makes it difficult to see clearly.

Examples

And the valley stretched endlessly out ahead, scorched and baked and writhing in its heat, until it vanished into the throbbing wall of fiery orange brown haze.
His blue eyes sought the shimmering sea of haze ahead.
To his puzzlement, there suddenly was no haze.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fog - thick cloud of tiny water droplets or smoke near the ground reducing visibility
  • Degree Lower: mist - fine spray or cloud of water droplets in the air
  • Synonym: smog - fog mixed with smoke or other pollutants

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete Dutch *haze* or Low German *hasen* (to dry).
