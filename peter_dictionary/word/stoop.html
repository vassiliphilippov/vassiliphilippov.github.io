stoop

1. VERB

/ stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
to bend one's upper body forward and downward, especially from the waist, often to avoid something or to reach a lower position

Examples

There was no place to sit, but Watson walked slowly from the ladder to the window slits and back, stooping slightly to avoid striking his head on the heavy beams.
He paced slowly, stooping, staring at the damp, slippery floor.
To demonstrate the soil of his vegetable gardens as it is today, Mr. Clark stooped to scoop up a handful of rich dark earth.
Gavin's lips moved so that Clayton had to stoop to catch the words.

Synset Examples

he crouched down
She bowed before the Queen
The young man stooped to pick up the girl's purse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crouch - to bend the knees and bring the upper body down close to the ground
  • Antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet

Etymology

From Middle English *stoupen* ‘to stoop, bow down’, from Old Norse *stúpa* ‘to stoop, bow’
