facsimile

1. NOUN

/ fækˈsɪməli / (AmE), / fækˈsɪməli / (BrE)
An exact copy or reproduction of a document, book, artwork, or other item.

Examples

M + R Dietetic Laboratories, Inc., Columbus, gives all its workers a facsimile checkbook - each check showing the amount the company spends on a particular fringe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - a thing made to be similar or identical to another
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest version from which copies are made

Etymology

From Latin fac simile, meaning 'make similar.'
