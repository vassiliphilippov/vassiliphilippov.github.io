shamble

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈʃæmbəl / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, awkward way, dragging the feet

Examples

Behind them shambled a long column of weak, tattered men.

Synset Examples

he shuffled out of the room
We heard his feet shuffling down the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to walk by sliding the feet along the ground
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from earlier noun meaning 'a butcher's stall', influenced by the notion of awkward movement.
