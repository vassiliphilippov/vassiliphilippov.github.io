dogwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
A tree or shrub of the genus Cornus, often having showy white or pink bracts that resemble petals.

Examples

And the dogwood in early May, for Miss Ada's alfresco party; and after that the Japanese cherries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornel - another tree or shrub of the Cornus genus

Etymology

From 'dog' + 'wood'. The reason for the name is uncertain, possibly relating to traditional uses or the texture of the wood.
