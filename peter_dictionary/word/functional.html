functional

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or disorder) relating to the way in which an organ or system works, not involving changes to its structure or physiology.

Examples

There are many possibilities, including poor circulation, a variety of neurological conditions, and functional disorders.

Synset Examples

functional deafness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic - (of a disorder) caused by structural or physiological change in an organ or tissue

Etymology

From function + -al, deriving from Latin 'fungi' (to perform) via French 'fonction'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (BrE)
Designed to be practical and useful rather than decorative, or able to perform a particular function or purpose.

Examples

In other words, the anastomoses between the bronchial artery and pulmonary artery should be considered as functional or demand shunts.
He showed convincingly that anxiety is a learned (conditioned) reaction and is the basis of experimental and clinical neuroses and assumed, therefore, that the neuronal changes which underlie the neuroses are functional and reversible.
Furthermore, a functional antagonism exists between an aggressive attitude and a state of anxiety.

Synset Examples

a style of writing in which every word is functional
functional architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice
  • Antonym: decorative - intended only to look attractive rather than be useful

Etymology

From Latin 'functio' (performance, execution) + -al.
