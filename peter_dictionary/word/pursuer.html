pursuer

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsuːər / (AmE), / pəˈsjʊə / (BrE)
a person or thing that pursues someone or something, especially in order to catch, overtake, or capture them

Examples

The chase in itself is a narrative; it presumes both speed and urgency and it demands cutting - both from pursued to pursuer and from stage to stage of the journey of both.
She was the pursuer as clearly as was Venus in Shakespeare's poem.

Synset Examples

always before he had been able to outwit his pursuers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pursued - a person or thing being pursued
  • Synonym: pursue - to follow or chase in order to catch

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman and Old French *porsuere*, from *poursuivre* (to pursue).
