repentance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɛntəns / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛntəns / (BrE)
the feeling of sincere regret or remorse for past wrongdoing, often accompanied by a wish to make amends or seek forgiveness

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - a deep sense of guilt or regret for a wrong committed

Etymology

From Old French *repentance*, from the verb *repentir* (to feel regret).
