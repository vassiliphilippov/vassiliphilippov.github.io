Hebrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːbru / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː / (BrE)
an ancient Semitic language of the Hebrews, revived in modern times as the official language of Israel

Examples

All around the Mediterranean Sea, Asia Minor, northern India, and Tibet, Hebrew was the native tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebraic - relating to the Hebrew language or culture

Etymology

From Old French Hebreu, from Latin Hebraeus, from Hebrew ʿivrī (relating to the Hebrews).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhiːbru / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruː / (BrE)
relating to the Hebrews or their language, culture, or history

Examples

In the following year her father undertook to give a course in Hebrew theology to Johns Hopkins students, and this brought to the Szold house a group of bright young Jews who had come to Baltimore to study, and who enjoyed being fed and mothered by Mamma and entertained by Henrietta and Rachel, who played and sang for them in the upstairs sitting room on Sunday evenings.

Synset Examples

the old Hebrew prophets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebraic - relating to the Hebrews; alternative form

Etymology

From Old French Ebreu/Abreu, from Latin Hebraeus, from Greek Hebraios, from Hebrew ‘Ivri (Hebrew).
