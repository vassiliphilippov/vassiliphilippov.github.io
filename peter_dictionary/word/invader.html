invader

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪdər / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪdə / (BrE)
A person or group that enters a place, especially a country, by force in order to conquer or occupy it.

Examples

"If the day should ever come that foreign invaders swarm ashore along the Gulf Coast ", the account reads, "they can count on heavy opposition from a group of commando trained telephone employees - all girls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - a person who attacks; may not imply conquest
  • Antonym: defender - a person who defends against attackers or invaders

Etymology

From Latin invadere (to go into, attack), via Old French envadeor.
