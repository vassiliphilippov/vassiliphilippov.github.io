concierge

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈsjɛrdʒ / (AmE), / kɒnˈsjɛːʒ / (BrE)
an employee, especially in a hotel or apartment building, whose job is to assist guests, manage mail, and oversee security and maintenance services

Examples

The concierge called out to them as they were passing through the foyer.
The suitcases had come while they were out, and had been put in their room, the concierge said.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porter - person who carries luggage and assists guests, especially in hotels

Etymology

From French 'concierge' (caretaker or janitor).
