umpire

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈʌm.paɪə / (BrE)
a person chosen to judge or settle a dispute or a contested issue, especially one with the authority to make a final decision

Examples

- Are you a dance judge or something?

Synset Examples

the critic was considered to be an arbiter of modern literature
the arbitrator's authority derived from the consent of the disputants
an umpire was appointed to settle the tax case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrator - a person officially appointed to settle a dispute
  • Synonym: judge - a person who makes a formal decision in a dispute or competition

Etymology

From Middle English *oumpere*, from Old French *nonper* (not equal, arbitrator), evolving through misdivision to 'umpire'.
