Ukrainian

1. NOUN

/ juˈkreɪniən / (AmE), / juˈkreɪniən / (BrE)
the East Slavic language spoken mainly in Ukraine

Examples

The Death's-Head Corps took in Ukrainian and Baltic Auxiliaries, and the Einsatzkommandos waded knee-deep in blood and turned into drunken, dope ridden maniacs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian - the East Slavic language spoken in Russia and neighboring countries
  • Synonym: Belarusian - the East Slavic language spoken in Belarus

Etymology

From Ukraine + -ian, denoting language or nationality.
