dispute

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspjut / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
A serious argument or disagreement about something important.

Examples

The former county school superintendent, George P. Callan, shot himself to death March 18, four days after he resigned his post in a dispute with the county school board.
Immediately, the religious groups of the city were embroiled in an angry dispute over the alleged invasion of a man's right to freedom of religious belief and conscience.

Synset Examples

he had a dispute with his wife
there were irreconcilable differences
the familiar conflict between Republicans and Democrats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a verbal exchange expressing opposing views
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or clash
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or consensus in opinion

Etymology

From Old French 'disputer', from Latin 'disputare' (to discuss, examine).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈspjut / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
to question the truth or validity of something; to argue or take exception to a statement, claim, or decision

Examples

This was no man's land, disputed by the Haijac Union and the Israeli Republic, a potential source of war for the last two hundred years.
"With no strong men and no parliament to dispute his will, he was the government ".
But few who have experienced marriage can dispute the fact that the focus of interpersonal relationships is different in marriage than in a pre-marital situation.

Synset Examples

She challenged his claims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to dispute the truth or validity of; to call into question
  • Antonym: accept - to consent to receive or agree with; to not dispute

Etymology

From Old French *disputer*, from Latin *disputare* (to discuss, examine, dispute).
