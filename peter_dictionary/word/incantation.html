incantation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kænˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a series of words spoken or chanted as part of a ritual, believed to have magical power

Examples

But the most notable thing about the incantation of these ex liberals was that the one-time shibboleth of socialism was conspicuously absent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spell - a spoken word or form of words held to have magic power
  • Antonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm

Etymology

From Latin 'incantatio', from 'incantare' meaning 'to chant upon, to bewitch'.
