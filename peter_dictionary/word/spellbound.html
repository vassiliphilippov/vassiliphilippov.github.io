spellbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛlˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈspɛlˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
having one's attention completely held by something, as if under a magical spell; entranced or captivated

Examples

The others, the ones in the stands, were spellbound, for hearing the mayor was for them like listening to a symphony was for sophisticated folks in New York City.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entranced - mesmerized or filled with wonder and delight
  • Synonym: fascinated - greatly interested or attracted
  • Antonym: bored - feeling uninterested or unengaged

Etymology

From 'spell' (charm or magic words) + 'bound' (being constrained or held); literally, bound by a spell.
