wobble

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒbəl / (BrE)
to move unsteadily from side to side or up and down, often in an irregular or shaky manner

Examples

Oh, you 'll wobble and weave quite a bit at first.
He drove, and the road wobbled, familiar scenes crept past on either side.
A workbench had a heavy top and sturdy legs, but how did you attach sturdy legs to a heavy top so that the whole thing did n't wobble like a newborn calf and ultimately collapse when you leaned on it?
He hit Roberts with his left fist in the ribs and the razor cut toward him feebly, then wobbled in mid-air.

Synset Examples

His knees wobbled
The old cart wobbled down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become stable, not wobbling or unsteady

Etymology

Probably imitative of a shaky or unstable movement, first recorded in English in the 17th century.
