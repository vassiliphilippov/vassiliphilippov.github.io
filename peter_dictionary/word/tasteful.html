tasteful

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪst.fəl / (AmE), / ˈteɪst.fəl / (BrE)
Showing or having good taste; aesthetically pleasing and appropriate.

Examples

He merely said, "Any good decorator these days can make you a tasteful home ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tasteless - lacking good taste or aesthetic judgment

Etymology

From 'taste' + '-ful', modeled after other adjectives formed with '-ful'.
