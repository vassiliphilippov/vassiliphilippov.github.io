confidant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.fəˌdænt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.dænt / (BrE)
A person with whom one shares private matters or secrets, trusting them not to repeat them to others.

Examples

So she could n't choose Rev as a confidant; it must be Lucien.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidante - female form of 'confidant', a trusted person for secrets
  • Synonym: confessor - a person to whom one confesses, especially in a religious or confidential context

Etymology

From French 'confidant', from Italian 'confidante', ultimately from Latin 'confīdēns' (trusting).
