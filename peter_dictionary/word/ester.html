ester

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɛstə / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed by the reaction between an acid and an alcohol, with the elimination of water

Examples

Greasy soils, which are typified by hydrocarbons and fats (esters of glycerol with long chain organic acids).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt - compound formed from an acid and a base by removal of water

Etymology

From German 'Ester', coined in the 19th century by chemist Leopold Gmelin.
