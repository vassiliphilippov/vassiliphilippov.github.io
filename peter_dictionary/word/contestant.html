contestant

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛstənt / (AmE), / kənˈtɛstənt / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a competition or contest

Examples

So escalation proceeds, ad infinitum or, more accurately, until the contestants begin dropping them on each other instead of on their respective proving grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - person who competes against others
  • Synonym: opponent - person who opposes another in a contest
  • Antonym: judge - person who assesses a contest, not a participant

Etymology

From 'contest' + '-ant'; patterned after French 'contestant', present participle of 'contester'.
