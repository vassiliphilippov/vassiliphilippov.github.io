intimacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntəˌməsi / (AmE), / ˈɪntɪməsi / (BrE)
A close, warm, and personal relationship or feeling of connection, often involving deep understanding or affection.

Examples

Only paved alleyways tunneled through the walls of those fortresses into the mysterious core of intimacy behind the houses where backyards owned no fences, where one man's property blended with the next to form courtyards in which no one knew privacy.

Synset Examples

the absence of fences created a mysterious intimacy in which no one knew privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiarity - close acquaintance or friendship

Etymology

From Latin *intimitas*, from *intimus* meaning 'inmost, most intimate.'
