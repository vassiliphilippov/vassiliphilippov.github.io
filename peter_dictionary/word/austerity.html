austerity

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈstɛrəti / (AmE), / ɒˈstɛərɪti / (BrE)
a simple and strict way of life that involves avoiding physical pleasures and luxuries, often for moral or religious reasons

Examples

Why had not this success come to him before he had plunged into his Discourse, and before he had committed himself to a life of austerity and denial?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence, often for religious reasons
  • Antonym: indulgence - the practice of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasures

Etymology

From Latin 'austeritas' (severe, harsh), from Greek 'austeros' (harsh, severe).
