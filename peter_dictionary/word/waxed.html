waxed

1. ADJ

/ wækst / (AmE), / wækst / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) having been treated, polished, or coated with wax.

Examples

He crossed the tracks to Delaware House, where ladies in gay dresses and men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah.

Synset Examples

waxed floors
waxed mustache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polished - made smooth and shiny by rubbing

Etymology

From the verb 'wax', ultimately from Old English 'weaxian' (to grow, wax); 'wax' in the sense of a substance from Old English 'weax' (wax, beeswax).
