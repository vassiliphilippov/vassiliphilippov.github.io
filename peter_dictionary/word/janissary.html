janissary

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒænɪˌsɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒanɪsəri / (BrE)
A devoted follower or loyal supporter, especially one who is unquestioningly obedient. Originally, a member of an elite Turkish infantry unit.

Examples

In Plato's Republic communism is - to speak anachronistically - a communism of Janissaries.

Synset Examples

every politician has a following of janissaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who backs, follows, or champions another
  • Synonym: adherent - a person who supports a party, person, or set of ideas
  • Synonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas

Etymology

From French 'janissaire', from Italian 'giannizzero', from Ottoman Turkish 'yeniçeri' (new soldier).
