eardrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪr.drʌm / (AmE), / ˈɪə.drʌm / (BrE)
the thin membrane inside the ear that vibrates when sound waves reach it, enabling hearing

Examples

Honey Bunny, you'’re going to break your eardrums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tympanic membrane - technical term for the eardrum
