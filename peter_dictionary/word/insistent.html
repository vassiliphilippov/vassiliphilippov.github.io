insistent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
Continuing to demand attention, especially in a repetitive and persistent way.

Examples

He was aware of insistent inner beatings, as if prisoners within sought release from his rigid body.
Not until the words had been spoken did Abel suddenly see the old house and the insistent sea, and feel his contrition blotted out in one shameful moment of covetousness.

Synset Examples

the bluejay's insistent cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action
  • Synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention

Etymology

From Latin 'insistere' (to persist, to stand upon).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
Demanding attention or persistent in requesting something; not allowing refusal or distraction.

Examples

John was quietly insistent.

Synset Examples

clamant needs
a crying need
regarded literary questions as exigent and momentous
insistent hunger
an instant need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action
  • Degree Higher: urgent - requiring immediate attention

Etymology

From Latin 'insistere' meaning 'to persist, to press upon'.
