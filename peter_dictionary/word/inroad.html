inroad

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌrəʊd / (BrE)
An advance, encroachment, or intrusion into a place, activity, or area of interest; often used figuratively to describe a gradual or significant impact or influence.

Examples

By making inroads in the name of law enforcement into the protection which Congress has afforded to the marriage relationship, the Court today continues in the path charted by the recent decision in Wyatt v. United States, 362 U. S. 525, where the Court held that, under the circumstances of that case, a wife could be compelled to testify against her husband over her objection.

Synset Examples

they made inroads in the United States market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incursion - a sudden or brief invasion or attack
  • Synonym: intrusion - an act of intruding; an unwelcome entry or interference

Etymology

From Old English 'inrād' (a raid, invasion).
