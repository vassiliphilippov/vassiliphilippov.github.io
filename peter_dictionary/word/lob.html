lob

1. VERB

/ lɑb / (AmE), / lɒb / (BrE)
to throw or hit (a ball or other object) in a high, arcing trajectory

Examples

He has gained only one thing - he has exploded a 50 - megaton bomb and he probably has rockets with sufficient thrust to lob it over the shorter intercontinental ranges.

Synset Examples

lob the tennis ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toss - to throw something lightly or casually
  • Degree Higher: hurl - to throw with great force

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps imitative or from dialectal English lob (to throw slowly or heavily).
