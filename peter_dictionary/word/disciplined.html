disciplined

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsəˌplɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪplɪnd / (BrE)
Showing self-control and obedience to rules or authority; well-trained or controlled.

Examples

This big, flexible voice with uncommon range has been superbly disciplined.
They were better trained, better looking, better built, better disciplined - and something else - they were better dancers.
One moment, the road was filled with disciplined troops, marching four by four with a purpose as implacable as death; the next, a cloud of gun smoke covered a screaming fury of sound, out of which the redcoat soldiers emerged with their bayonets and their cursing fury.
The Church considers this to be the method provided by nature and its divine Author: It involves no frustration of nature's laws, but simply an intelligent and disciplined use of them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orderly - well-organized, showing order or control
  • Antonym: undisciplined - not controlled or lacking obedience to rules

Etymology

From discipline + -ed, formed from Latin disciplina (instruction, training).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsəplɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪplɪnd / (BrE)
trained mentally or physically by instruction or exercise; exhibiting self-control or orderly behavior as a result of training

Examples

In answering these questions, we need to ask not merely whether Communist troops have crossed over into territories they did not occupy before, and not merely whether disciplined agents of the Cominform are in control of governments from which they were formerly excluded:

Synset Examples

the beautiful coordination of his disciplined muscles
a disciplined mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trained - having been taught a particular skill or type of behavior through practice and instruction
  • Synonym: self-controlled - having control over one's own behavior and emotions
  • Antonym: undisciplined - lacking in self-control or trained behavior

Etymology

From discipline + -ed, meaning someone who has been subjected to discipline or training.
