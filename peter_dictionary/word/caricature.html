caricature

1. VERB

/ ˈkærɪkətʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈkærɪkətjʊə / (BrE)
to represent or describe someone or something in a way that exaggerates certain features, often for comic or satirical effect

Examples

Cherkasov does not caricature him, as some actors have been inclined to do.

Synset Examples

The drawing caricatured the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satirize - to use satire to criticize or mock
  • Synonym: parody - to imitate in a humorous or satirical way

Etymology

From Italian *caricatura* (loaded picture), from *caricare* (to load).
