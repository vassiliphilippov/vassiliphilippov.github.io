exorcise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛksɚˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛksɔːˌsaɪz / (BrE)
to expel an evil spirit or demon from a person or place by prayer, ritual, or command

Examples

Perhaps it would be better to speak to him, since silence could not exorcise his form.

Synset Examples

exorcise evil spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banish - to send someone away and forbid them to return
  • Synonym: dispel - to drive away (a feeling, thought, or belief)

Etymology

From Late Latin exorcizare, from Greek exorkizein, meaning 'to bind by oath'.
