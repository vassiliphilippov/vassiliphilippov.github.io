rutted

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌtɪd / (AmE), / ˈrʌtɪd / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially a road or path) full of long, narrow tracks made by the repeated passage of wheels.

Examples

And she was made to fall in love with him again there in the rutted dirt driveway standing in the cold fog, mad as she was at his going away when he really did n't have to, mad at their both got older in a life that seemed to have taken no more than a week to go by.

Synset Examples

rutty farm roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free of ruts or roughness

Etymology

From 'rut' (track or groove) + '-ed' (adjectival suffix indicating 'having'), from Old French 'route' (track) via Latin 'rupta' (broken way).
