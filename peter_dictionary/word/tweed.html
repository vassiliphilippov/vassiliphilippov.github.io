tweed

1. NOUN

/ twid / (AmE), / twiÀêd / (BrE)
A rough-surfaced, thick woolen fabric, usually of mixed flecked colours, originally made in Scotland.

Examples

The tweed coat, maybe.
He had on his gray tweed overcoat and his city hat, and his brief case lay on the bench.
His tweed suit was a baggy gray and green.

Etymology

From the River Tweed in Scotland, near where the fabric was first produced.
