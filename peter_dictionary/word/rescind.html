rescind

1. VERB

/ rɪˈsɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪnd / (BrE)
to officially cancel or withdraw (a law, order, agreement, etc.) so that it is no longer valid

Examples

Schley County Rep. B. D. Pelham will offer a resolution Monday in the House to rescind the body's action of Friday in voting itself a $ 10 per day increase in expense allowances.
As of Sunday night, there was no word of a resolution being offered there to rescind the action.
His London contract was rescinded, and now, he explains cheerfully, as a bright smile lightens his intense, mobile face, "I conduct only one hundred and twenty concerts "!

Synset Examples

He revoked the ban on smoking
lift an embargo
vacate a death sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revoke - to officially cancel or annul (a law, order, etc.)
  • Synonym: annul - to declare invalid (an official agreement, decision, or result)
  • Antonym: enact - to make into law or put into effect (opposite of rescind)

Etymology

From Latin rescindere: re- (again) + scindere (to cut or split).
