calmness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːm.nəs / (BrE)
The state of being free from agitation or strong emotion; composure, especially under stress.

Examples

The calmness and detachment of his tone suggested unawareness of how implicit was his own guilt in the words he had used to defend Cromwell.

Synset Examples

he accepted their problems with composure and she with equanimity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - the state of being calm and in control of oneself
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From 'calm' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin 'cauma' (heat of the day, rest in the heat).
