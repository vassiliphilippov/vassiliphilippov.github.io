compulsively

1. ADV

/ kəmˈpʌlsɪvli / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌlsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that results from or relates to an irresistible urge, especially one that is against one's conscious wishes.

Examples

Khrushchev himself is reported to be concerned by the surge of animosity he has aroused, yet our own nuclear statesmen seem intent on following compulsively in his footsteps.

Synset Examples

he cleaned his shoes compulsively after every walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsessively - in an obsessive manner, with persistent, intrusive thoughts

Etymology

From 'compulsive' + '-ly'; 'compulsive' from Latin 'compulsus', past participle of 'compellere' (to drive together).
