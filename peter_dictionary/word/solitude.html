solitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
The state of being alone, especially when this is peaceful and pleasant.

Examples

In the heat of the summer, the garden solitudes were ours alone; our elders stayed in the dark house or sat fanning on the front porch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
  • Antonym: company - the state of being with others

Etymology

From Latin *solitudo* (loneliness, solitude), from *solus* (alone).
