bleb

1. NOUN

/ blɛb / (AmE), / blɛb / (BrE)
(Pathology) a small blister or bubble on the skin or an organ surface, filled with serous fluid.

Examples

On the surfaces of both lungs there were emphysematous blebs measuring up to 3 cm. in diameter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister - a small bubble on the skin filled with fluid, caused by friction or burns

Etymology

From Greek *blábē* (damage).
