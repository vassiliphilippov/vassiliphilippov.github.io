brutal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbruː.təl / (AmE), / ˈbruː.təl / (BrE)
(of people or their actions) cruel, harsh, and likely to cause pain or suffering; showing a lack of pity or compassion.

Examples

Russ gave him a brutal thrust that tumbled him over flat on his stomach.
This was revenge... of a much more brutal and cold-blooded nature.

Synset Examples

a barbarous crime
brutal beatings
cruel tortures
Stalin's roughshod treatment of the kulaks
a savage slap
vicious kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - willing to cause pain to others without pity
  • Antonym: merciful - showing kindness and forgiveness towards others
  • Synonym: barbarous - savagely cruel; exceedingly brutal
  • Antonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *brutalis* (dull, stupid, like a beast), from Latin *brutus* (heavy, dull, irrational).
