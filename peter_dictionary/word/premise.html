premise

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪs / (BrE)
a statement or proposition that is assumed to be true and from which a conclusion is drawn, especially in logic or argument

Examples

The basic premise of all mystery stories is that the distinction between good and bad coincides with the distinction between legal and illegal.

Synset Examples

on the assumption that he has been injured we can infer that he will not to play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - something taken for granted or accepted as true without proof
  • Antonym: conclusion - a judgment or decision reached by reasoning

Etymology

From Latin *praemissa*, past participle of *praemittere* (to send before), from *prae-* (before) + *mittere* (to send).
