prie-dieu

1. NOUN

/ priˈdiːoʊ / (AmE), / priːˈdjəʊ / (BrE)
A low bench with a kneeling shelf and a sloping shelf for books or elbows, used for private prayer.

Examples

To confess with a canvas chair as a prie-dieu, gouging at his heart until a rough and stupid hand bade him rise and go?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kneeler - a cushion or bench for kneeling, especially in a church

Etymology

From French 'prie-Dieu', meaning 'pray [to] God'.
