water-wash

1. VERB

/ ˈwɔtər wɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə wɔʃ / (BrE)
to wash something using water, especially to remove impurities or residue

Examples

A very fine, gray solid (about 15 g) is formed, water-washed by centrifugation, and dried at 110 ` C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rinse - to wash something quickly with clean water
