cascade

1. VERB

/ kæˈskeɪd / (AmE), / kæsˈkeɪd / (BrE)
(of something, especially water or hair) to flow, fall, or hang down in large or abundant quantities in a manner resembling a waterfall.

Examples

Leila (Malia Phillips), 25, is a Greenwich Village painter of Persianesque miniatures who has red hair that cascades almost to her ankles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - to move downward freely by the force of gravity

Etymology

From French 'cascade', from Italian 'cascata' (waterfall), from 'cascare' (to fall).
