parole

1. NOUN

/ pəˈroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊl / (BrE)
the conditional release of a prisoner before the end of their sentence, under the promise of good behavior and compliance with certain conditions

Examples

...but towards the end, he was paroled to help with the American war effort, so for six months he's worked in my pastry shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probation - a period of supervision instead of prison

Etymology

From French 'parole' (word, speech, promise).
