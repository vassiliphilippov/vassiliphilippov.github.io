shelve

1. VERB

/ ʃɛlv / (AmE), / ʃɛlv / (BrE)
to decide not to continue with a plan, project, etc., either temporarily or permanently; to postpone or put aside for future consideration

Examples

We have seen good new products shelved because no one had the assignment to develop such facts and plans - and management could n't make up its mind.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Synonym: defer - to put off to a later time
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide not to proceed with something

Etymology

From shelf + -e, originally referring to placing something on a shelf.
