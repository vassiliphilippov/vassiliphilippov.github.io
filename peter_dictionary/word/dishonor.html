dishonor

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈɑnər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
to bring shame or disgrace upon; to cause someone or something to lose honor or respect

Examples

He finished the worship service as if there had been no brazen attempt to dishonor God and man.
The men of your company would not allow your name dishonored.

Synset Examples

he dishonored his family by committing a serious crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - to cause someone to lose respect or honor
  • Antonym: honor - to show great respect or bring pride to someone or something

Etymology

From Old French deshonorer, from Latin dishonorare, from dis- (opposite of) + honorare (to honor).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑnɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
A state of shame or disgrace resulting from loss of respect or reputation.

Examples

I consider it a great dishonor to me personally to have to deny that I am a criminal.

Synset Examples

he was resigned to a life of dishonor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honor - high respect; esteem; opposite of dishonor

Etymology

From Old French deshonor, from Latin dishonor (from dis- 'apart, not' + honor 'honor').
