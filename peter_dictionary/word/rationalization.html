rationalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of attempting to explain or justify behavior or an attitude with logical reasons, even if these are not appropriate; making something seem consistent with reason.

Examples

It was merely a rationalization and ordering of new institutions of popular government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justification - the action of showing something to be right or reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'ratio' (reason) + English '-alization' (forming nouns meaning 'the process of'), influenced by French 'rationalisation'.
