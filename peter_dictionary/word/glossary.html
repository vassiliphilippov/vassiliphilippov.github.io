glossary

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑːsəri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsəri / (BrE)
An alphabetical list of technical or specialized terms with their definitions, usually placed as an appendix in a book or manual.

Examples

This is in large part a code of behavior and a glossary of values: what is it that people do and should do and how one should regard it.
This initiates the second step of glossary lookup - connecting the information cell of forms in the text form list to dictionary forms.
The first stage of translation after glossary lookup is structural analysis of the input text.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictionary - a reference book containing words and their meanings

Etymology

From Latin 'glossarium', from 'glossa' meaning 'word requiring explanation', from Greek 'glōssa' (tongue, language, word).
