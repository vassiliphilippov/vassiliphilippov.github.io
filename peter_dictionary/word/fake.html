fake

1. ADJ

/ feɪk / (AmE), / feɪk / (BrE)
fraudulent or not genuine; intended to deceive by giving a misleading appearance

Examples

He milks the latest scientific advances, translating them into his own special Buck Rogers vocabulary to huckster his fake machines as a cure-all for everything from hay fever to sexual impotence and cancer.
According to the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), "Doctor "Ghadiali, Dr. Albert Abrams and his clique, and Dr. Wilhelm Reich - to name three notorious device quacks - succeeded, respectively, in distributing 10000, 5000, and 2000 fake health machines.
Sarah Gross, a dress shop proprietor, paid $ 1020 to a masseur, and Mr. A., a laborer, paid $ 4200 to a chiropractor for treatment with two fake health machines - the "radioclast "and the "diagnometer ".
The Postmaster General recently reported that mail order frauds - among which fake therapeutic devices figure prominently - are at the highest level in history.
But it is our health - more precious than all the money in the world - that these modern witch doctors with their fake therapeutic gadgets are gambling away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genuine - real; actually what it is said to be
  • Antonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine
  • Synonym: fraudulent - obtained or done by deceit, especially criminal deceit
  • Synonym: bogus - not genuine or true (used in a disapproving sense)

Etymology

Probably from the Thieves’ Cant 'fake' (to steal, contrive, or make), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ feɪk / (AmE), / feɪk / (BrE)
an object made to look like something valuable or genuine in order to deceive people; a counterfeit

Examples

Sam Spade joins forces with a band of adventurers in search of a priceless jeweled statue of a falcon; but when the bird is found at last, it turns out to be a fake.
That thing'’s gotta be a fake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgery - a copy of a document, signature, artwork, etc. made to deceive
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Antonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine

Etymology

Possibly from Latin facere (to make) or from 19th-century thieves' slang.

3. VERB

/ feɪk / (AmE), / feɪk / (BrE)
to make (something) appear real in order to deceive; to forge or counterfeit

Examples

In the case of academic personnel the "feeling "of participation can hardly be "faked ".

Synset Examples

he faked the signature
they counterfeited dollar bills
She forged a Green Card

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - to produce a fraudulent copy or imitation, especially of a document
  • Synonym: counterfeit - to make an imitation with intent to deceive, especially in relation to money or documents
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic; real; not fake

Etymology

Probably from dialectal feak ('to beat, strike') or from German fegen ('to polish, sweep'), shifting to the sense of 'to trick' in 18th-century English.
