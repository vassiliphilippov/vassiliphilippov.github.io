octahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑktəˈhidɹən / (AmE), / ˌɒktəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A solid figure with eight flat faces.

Examples

The O atoms in each sheet are close packed and each Cr atom is surrounded by a distorted octahedron of O atoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: polyhedron - solid figure with many flat faces
  • Degree Lower: cube - solid figure with six square faces
  • Degree Higher: dodecahedron - solid figure with twelve flat faces

Etymology

From Greek oktáedron, from oktō (eight) + hédra (seat, base).
