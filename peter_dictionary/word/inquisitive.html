inquisitive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪzətɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a strong desire to learn or know more

Examples

"Chicago and All That Jazz "may have wound up satisfying neither the confirmed fan nor the inquisitive newcomer.

Synset Examples

if someone saw a man climbing a light post they might get inquisitive
raised a speculative eyebrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curious - having a strong desire to know or learn

Etymology

From Late Latin inquisitivus (searching, examining), from Latin inquirere (to seek after).
