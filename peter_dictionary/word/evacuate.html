evacuate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvæk.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvæk.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
To remove all contents from (a place or container); to empty completely.

Examples

The data reported here are either from spectra from which the adsorbed water resonance could easily be eliminated or from spectra of samples evacuated and sealed off at 375 ` C which contain no adsorbed water.

Synset Examples

evacuate the bottle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty - to remove all contents from

Etymology

From Latin evacuare (to empty), from ex- (out) + vacuus (empty).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvækjueɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvækjueɪt / (BrE)
to move people from a place of danger to a safer location, often because of an emergency such as a fire, natural disaster, or war

Examples

"Nobody really expects to evacuate.

Synset Examples

After the earthquake, residents were evacuated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relocate - to move to a different place for safety or other reasons
  • Antonym: return - to go back to a place after leaving, as in after an evacuation

Etymology

From Latin *evacuare* (to empty out), from *ex-* (out) + *vacuus* (empty).
