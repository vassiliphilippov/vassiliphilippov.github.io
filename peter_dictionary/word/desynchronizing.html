desynchronizing

1. NOUN

/ diːˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈsɪŋkrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
A condition or process in which things are not synchronized or do not occur at the same time; lack of synchronization.

Examples

It has further been shown that: (1) an experimental neurosis in its initial stages is associated with a reversible shift in the central autonomic balance; (2) drugs altering the hypothalamic balance alter conditioned reactions; (3) in a state of depression, the positive conditioned stimulus may fail to elicit a conditioned reaction but cause an increased synchrony instead of the excitatory desynchronizing (alerting) effect on the EEG.

Synset Examples

the stimulus produced a desynchronizing of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronization - the process of causing things to occur at the same time

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + synchronizing, from Greek 'syn' (together) and 'chronos' (time).
