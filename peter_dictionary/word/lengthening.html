lengthening

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛŋθənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something longer in time or space; an extension or prolongation

Examples

However, this would require a lengthening of the season from thirteen to fourteen weeks.

Synset Examples

there was an indefinite prolongation of the peace talks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shortening - the act of making something shorter

Etymology

From the verb 'lengthen' + -ing, originally from Old English 'lengþan'.
