columned

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑl.əmnd / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.əmd / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having columns or resembling columns; often used in combination to specify the type of columns present.

Examples

He would not be like the "rich Americans "who lived in white columned houses on the other side of the park.

Synset Examples

a columned portico
trees with columned trunks
white-columned houses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillar - a tall vertical structure, usually of stone or concrete, used to support a building

Etymology

From 'column' + '-ed' suffix, indicating possession or presence of columns.
