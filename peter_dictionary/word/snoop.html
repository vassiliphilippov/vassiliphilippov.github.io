snoop

1. VERB

/ snup / (AmE), / snuÀêp / (BrE)
to investigate or look around secretly, especially trying to find out private information about someone or something

Examples

How dared they demand to "snoop "in private financial records, disbursements, confidential contracts and agreements?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - to observe secretly, often for hostile purposes
  • Synonym: pry - to inquire impertinently into the affairs of others

Etymology

Probably imitative or formed as a variant of Dutch *snoepen* (to pry, snack).
