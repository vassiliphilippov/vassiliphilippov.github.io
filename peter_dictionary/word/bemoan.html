bemoan

1. VERB

/ bɪˈmoʊn / (AmE), / bɪˈməʊn / (BrE)
to express deep regret or sorrow over something; to lament or complain about a loss, situation, or fact

Examples

There is a trend today to bemoan the fact that Americans are too "soft ".
Westbrook further bemoans the Southern writers' creation of an unreal image of their homeland, which is too readily assimilated by both foreign readers and visiting Yankees: "Our northerner is suspicious of all this crass evidence [ of urbanization ] presented to his senses.

Synset Examples

I deplore this hostile action
we lamented the loss of benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret; mourn
  • Synonym: deplore - to regret strongly; to disapprove of deeply
  • Antonym: celebrate - to honor or praise with joy; show happiness for

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + moan; Middle English; related to Old English 'bemǣnan'.
