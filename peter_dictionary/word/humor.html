a habitual or temporary state of mind or feeling; mood or disposition (archaic or literary use)

Synset Examples

whether he praised or cursed me depended on his temper at the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • Synonym: temper - a person's state of mind seen in terms of being angry or calm

Etymology

From Latin *umor* (moisture, fluid), originally referring to bodily fluids thought to affect temperament.

4. VERB

/ ˈhjuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmə / (BrE)
to comply with or indulge (someone's wishes or mood) in order to keep them content or avoid conflict

Examples

Jenny's aversion to having Dr. Dunne, a former admirer, seemed silly to him, but he would humor her, get anybody she wanted, the best never being too good for her.
The chances were against his being here to humor her when her time came, she was sure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulge - to allow oneself or another to enjoy the pleasure of something

Etymology

From Latin 'umor' meaning 'moisture'; sense developed in English from the former belief that mood was governed by bodily fluids called 'humors'.
