pickaxe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkˌæks / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.æks / (BrE)
a heavy hand tool with a long wooden handle and a curved metal head that is pointed at both ends, used for breaking up hard surfaces such as rocks or soil

Examples

Pickaxe? - For rocks.

Synset Examples

they used picks and sledges to break the rocks

Etymology

From Middle English 'picas', from Anglo-Norman 'picasse', influenced by Old French 'pioche' (pick) and 'axe'.
