deep-seated

1. ADJ

/ ˈdip ˌsitɪd / (AmE), / ˈdiːp ˌsiːtɪd / (BrE)
(of feelings, beliefs, or problems) firmly established and difficult to change; deeply rooted.

Examples

He washed it down with beer and produced a deep-seated belch.

Synset Examples

deep-rooted prejudice
deep-seated differences of opinion
implanted convictions
ingrained habits of a lifetime
a deeply planted need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingrained - (of habits, beliefs, or attitudes) firmly fixed; difficult to change
  • Synonym: deep-rooted - (of feelings, beliefs, or traditions) firmly established and difficult to change
  • Antonym: superficial - not deep or thorough; existing or occurring at or on the surface

Etymology

From 'deep' + 'seated', meaning literally 'seated deep'. Often confused with 'deep-seeded', but 'seated' is correct.
