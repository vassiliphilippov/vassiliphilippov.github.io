pun

1. NOUN

/ pʌn / (AmE), / pʌn / (BrE)
A humorous use of a word or words so as to suggest different meanings or applications, or a play on words.

Examples

Inheriting a more mature Mantle, who now has seen the sights on and off Broadway, Ralph Houk quietly bestowed, no pun intended, the mantle of authority on Mickey.

Synset Examples

I do it for the pun of it
his constant punning irritated her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wordplay - the clever or witty use of words and their meanings

Etymology

Perhaps from Italian 'puntiglio' (fine point, quibble), or from earlier English 'pundigrion', of uncertain origin.
