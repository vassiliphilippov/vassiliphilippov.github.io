indirection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the use of indirect methods or procedures to achieve something, especially instead of stating or acting directly

Examples

If he wanted to know anything, he would end up asking about it point-blank, but in this guileless manner he would probably receive more truthful answers than if he tried to get them by indirection.

Synset Examples

he tried to find out by indirection

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being direct or straightforward
  • Synonym: deviousness - the quality of being underhanded or indirect

Etymology

From Latin 'indirectus' (not direct), with the suffix '-ion' forming a noun.
