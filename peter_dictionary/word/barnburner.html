barnburner

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnˌbɝnɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌbɜːnə / (BrE)
a person who burns down barns, often as an act of arson or protest

Examples

If the barn-burner's family produces a Flem Snopes, who personifies commercialism and materialism in hyperbolic crassness, the Compson family produces a Jason Compson 4,.

Etymology

From barn + burner; originally used in 19th-century American politics to describe radical members of the Democratic Party who would destroy system structures to reform them.
