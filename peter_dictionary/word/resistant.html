resistant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
(of a living organism or strain) having immunity or insusceptibility to a disease, infection, or damaging agent.

Examples

It is possible that certain mutational forms may be produced such as antibiotic resistant strains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing (especially disease)

Etymology

From Latin 'resistens', present participle of 'resistere' (to resist).
