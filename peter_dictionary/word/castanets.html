castanets

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæstəˈnɛts / (AmE), / ˌkæstəˈnɛts / (BrE)
A percussion instrument consisting of a pair of small, hollow pieces of wood, ivory, or plastic, typically held in the hand and clicked together rhythmically, especially as used by Spanish dancers.

Examples

Drums, xylophones, castanets, and other percussive instruments are reproduced remarkably well.

Etymology

From Spanish 'castañeta', diminutive of 'castaña' meaning 'chestnut', referring to the shape.
