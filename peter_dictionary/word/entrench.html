entrench

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
to establish something firmly so that it is difficult to change or remove

Examples

The ten or more dangerous parties singled out for prosecution were still at large, and Pels realized that if these men entrenched themselves in their adobe houses, defending themselves through loopholes, it would be most difficult to capture them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embed - to fix something firmly in a surrounding mass
  • Synonym: implant - to insert or fix firmly

Etymology

From Old French 'en-' (in) + 'trenchier' (to cut, to dig a trench).
