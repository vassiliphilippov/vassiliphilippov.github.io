constricted

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
narrowed, tightened, or made smaller or more tense than usual; often used to describe physical spaces or voices and speech sounds produced by such narrowing

Examples

Her speech was barren of southernisms; she was one of Eliot Sparling's neutralized minorities, adopting the rolling R's and constricted vowels of Los Angeles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tight - firmly held; not loose; tense
  • Antonym: relaxed - not tense or tight; at ease

Etymology

From Latin 'constrictus', past participle of 'constringere' (to bind together, tighten).
