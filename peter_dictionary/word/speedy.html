speedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈspiːdi / (AmE), / ˈspiːdi / (BrE)
Moving or able to move quickly; characterized by high speed.

Examples

The Texans made themselves a comforting break on the opening kickoff when Denver's Al Carmichael was jarred loose from the ball when Dave Grayson, the speedy halfback, hit him and Guard Al Reynolds claimed it for Dallas.

Synset Examples

a rapid movement
a speedy car
a speedy errand boy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quick - moving fast or doing something in a short time
  • Antonym: slow - moving at a low speed; not quick
  • Synonym: rapid - happening or done quickly or in a short time

Etymology

From 'speed' + -y suffix, meaning 'having the quality of'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspiːdi / (AmE), / ˈspiːdi / (BrE)
Done or happening quickly; taking little time to complete.

Examples

Alex told her that there was no hurry for their breakfasts, trying at the same time to effect a speedy separation of the persons before and behind him.

Synset Examples

was quick to make friends
his quick reaction prevented an accident
hoped for a speedy resolution of the problem
a speedy recovery
he has a right to a speedy trial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quick - moving or happening with great speed
  • Synonym: fast - happening or moving at high speed
  • Antonym: slow - not quick or fast; taking longer than usual

Etymology

From 'speed' + '-y', dating back to late Middle English.
