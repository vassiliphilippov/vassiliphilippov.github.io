static

1. ADJ

/ ˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˈstatɪk / (BrE)
not moving or changing; characterized by a lack of motion or activity

Examples

Contract - conceived as the free bargain of formal equals - replaced the implied obligations of a more static and status conscious society.

Synset Examples

the inertia of an object at rest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stationary - not moving or not intended to be moved
  • Antonym: dynamic - characterized by constant change, activity, or progress

Etymology

From Greek statikos (causing to stand), from histanai (to make stand).
