rupture

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌptʃər / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃə / (BrE)
to break or burst suddenly; to cause something to separate or split abruptly, especially by force.

Examples

One thing should be clear to both husband and wife - neither pain nor profuse bleeding has to occur when the hymen is ruptured during the first sex act.

Synset Examples

The rope snapped
tear the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or split by force; to rip
  • Synonym: snap - to break suddenly with a sharp sound
  • Antonym: heal - to become healthy again after damage or rupture

Etymology

From Latin ruptūra (a breaking), from rumpere (to break).
