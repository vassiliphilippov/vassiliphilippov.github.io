chromatogram

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈmætəˌɡræm / (AmE), / krəˈmatəɡram / (BrE)
a visible record produced by chromatography, showing the separation of a mixture's components as spots or bands on a strip or column

Examples

The incomplete antibody activity appeared in the early part of the chromatogram; the complete, in the latter part.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatograph - an instrument used for chromatography

Etymology

From Greek 'chroma' (color) + 'gramma' (something written), reflecting colored bands in early forms.
