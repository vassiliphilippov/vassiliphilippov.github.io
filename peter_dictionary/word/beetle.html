beetle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
An insect with hard, usually shiny front wings (elytra) that cover the delicate hind wings and protect the body; member of the order Coleoptera.

Examples

The nest itself, the structure that in some cases housed about 2000 individuals when the season was at its peak, is now rapidly destroyed by the scavenging larvae of certain beetles and moths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bug - insect (informal or general term)

Etymology

Old English *bitela*, from *bītan* (to bite), referring to its biting mouthparts.
