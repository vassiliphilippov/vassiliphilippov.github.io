hock

1. VERB

/ hɑk / (AmE), / hɒk / (BrE)
to leave an object with a pawnbroker as security for money lent; to pawn

Examples

I raised some kale by hocking the good clothes I had left over from my respectable uptown life, but when that was gone I did n't have a cent.

Synset Examples

pawn your grandfather's gold watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawn - to deposit an object at a pawnbroker's in exchange for money

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Yiddish 'hokn' (to peddle, haggle) or from Dutch 'hok' (prison, pen), relating to keeping something confined.
