abandon

1. VERB

/ əˈbændən / (AmE), / əˈbændən / (BrE)
to stop maintaining, using, or supporting something, especially an idea, belief, or claim

Examples

When we say that a mythological mode of thought must be completely abandoned, we mean it must be abandoned as the sole or proper means for presenting the Christian understanding of existence.
She wrote again and now, abandoning for the moment the theme of love, she asked for help in the matter of her career.
On the other hand, if he wishes to continue in his chosen profession, he must abandon his own code and sacrifice his precious individualism.
Then, abandoning the studies in the face of their promising outlook for all concerned, B. + O. entered on-again-off-again negotiations with C. + O. which resulted in the present situation.
What was that old sign, supposed to be painted over a door somewhere, Abandon hope, all ye who enter here?

Synset Examples

He abandoned the thought of asking for her hand in marriage
Both sides have to give up some claims in these negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renounce - to formally give up a claim, belief, or way of behaving
  • Synonym: give up - to stop doing or having something; to surrender
  • Antonym: embrace - to accept an idea, belief, or change willingly

Etymology

From Old French abandoner, from a bandon (at one’s control, disposal), from Late Latin bandum (proclamation, ban).

2. VERB

/ əˈbændən / (AmE), / əˈbændən / (BrE)

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forsake - to abandon or desert someone or something
  • Synonym: desert - to leave someone in a difficult situation; to abandon

Etymology

From Old French abandoner, from a bandon (at one’s disposal), from Latin ad + bandon (control).

4. NOUN

/ əˈbændən / (AmE), / əˈbændən / (BrE)
A complete lack of inhibition or restraint; the quality of behaving with reckless freedom or exuberance.

Examples

in his motherland; in the spacious hunting grounds of "Uncle Sam "; in the exciting salons of his lovely, brilliant Paris - mistress of gaiety - excess and abandon - world theatre of new found freedoms in tone, color, dance, design, and thought.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old French *abandoner* (to relinquish control), from Latin *ab* (away) + *bandum* (a decree, ban).
