revelry

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvəlri / (AmE), / ˈrɛvəlri / (BrE)
Lively and noisy festivities, especially when these involve drinking alcohol and unrestrained behavior.

Examples

While five minutes ago the place had presented a scene of easy revelry, with Gyp Carmer a prominent figure, it was now as somnolent and dull as the day before payday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festivity - the celebration of something in a joyful and exuberant way
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober or restrained

Etymology

From Old French *revelrie* (wild merry-making), from *reveler* (to revel).
