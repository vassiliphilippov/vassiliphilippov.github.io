illicit

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
Not allowed by law, rules, or social custom, especially relating to something considered morally wrong or improper.

Examples

The place was evidently a familiar haunt and Claire wondered what other illicit loves had been celebrated in the comfortable rooms to which they were shown.

Synset Examples

an illicit association with his secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - not allowed by law
  • Antonym: lawful - allowed by law or morality

Etymology

From Latin 'illicitus', from 'in-' (not) + 'licitus' (lawful).
