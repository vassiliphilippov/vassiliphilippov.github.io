atm

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌti ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌtiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to the pressure exerted by a column of mercury 760 mm high at sea level and 0°C; standard atmosphere

Examples

Over a temperature range from 25 to 200 * * f and at pressures up to 250 atm, an overload of 300 psi, applied for a period of one day, results in an uncertainty in the pressure of, at most, one millimeter of mercury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard atmosphere - unit of pressure (101,325 pascals)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'atmosphere', in the sense of 'standard atmosphere'; from Greek 'atmos' (vapor) + 'sphaira' (sphere).
