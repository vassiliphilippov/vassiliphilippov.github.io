midday

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.deɪ / (BrE)
The middle part of the day, typically around noon.

Examples

Tom Brannon had caught up with the outfit shortly after the Maguires joined it, which had been at midday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noon - twelve o'clock in the day; the time of midday

Etymology

From Middle English 'midday', from Old English 'middæg' (mid + day).
