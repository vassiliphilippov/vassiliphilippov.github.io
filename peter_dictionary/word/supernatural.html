supernatural

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈnætʃ.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈnætʃ.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a force, event, or being) not existing in nature or explained by natural laws; attributed to powers beyond the physical world.

Examples

The chief experience is the sensing of communion, and in the higher religions, of a harmonious relationship with the supernatural power.
Finally, it gives sanctity, more than human legitimacy, and even, through super empirical reference, transcendent and supernatural importance to some values; for example, marriage as a sacrament, much law-breaking as sinful, occasionally the state as a divine instrument.
Religion has its own supernatural prescriptions that are at the same time codes of behavior for the here and now.
Giant, menacing, supernatural form.

Synset Examples

supernatural forces and occurrences and beings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranormal - unexplained by science or natural laws; outside the range of normal experience
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or derived from nature; not made or caused by humankind

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'supernaturalis', from Latin 'super-' (above) + 'naturalis' (natural).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈnætʃ.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈnætʃ.ər.əl / (BrE)
Phenomena or forces that are beyond or outside the laws of nature, such as ghosts, deities, or magic.

Examples

At the same time that religion binds the individual helpfully to the supernatural and gives him cosmic peace and a sense of supreme fulfillment, it also has great therapeutic value for him.
Some of the earlier episodes have touches of the supernatural, as suited to the legendary background.

Synset Examples

She doesn't believe in the supernatural

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranormal - Events or phenomena beyond scientific explanation; supernatural
  • Antonym: natural - Existing in or caused by nature; not supernatural

Etymology

From Medieval Latin supernaturalis, from Latin super (above) + naturalis (natural).
