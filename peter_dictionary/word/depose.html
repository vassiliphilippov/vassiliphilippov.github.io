depose

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to remove someone, especially a leader or ruler, from a position of power or office, often by force

Examples

Hudson deposed Juet and cut his pay.
But in any event, I submit that the power to depose belongs to Congress, not to this Court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dethrone - to remove a monarch from the throne
  • Synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a job or position
  • Antonym: appoint - to assign someone to a position or office

Etymology

From Latin *deponere* (to put down, lay aside), influenced by Medieval Latin *deponere* in sense 'to depose from office'.
