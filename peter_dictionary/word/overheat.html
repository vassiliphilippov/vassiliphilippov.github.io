overheat

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈhit / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhiːt / (BrE)
(of an object or body) to become excessively hot, often to an undesirable or dangerous degree.

Examples

The walk and his fears had served to overheat him and his sweaty armpits cooled at the touch of the night air.

Synset Examples

The car engines overheated

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: heat up - to become warm or hot

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'heat' (to make hot).
