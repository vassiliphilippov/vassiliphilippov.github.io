shim

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪm / (AmE), / ʃɪm / (BrE)
A thin, often wedge-shaped piece of material inserted into a space, especially to fill gaps or adjust the fit of parts.

Examples

It consists of two blocks with flat surfaces held apart by shims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a piece of material with one thick end that tapers to a thin edge, used for splitting or adjusting
