veritable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛr.ɪ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛr.ɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
Genuine; true; real, often used to emphasize the aptness of a description.

Examples

"I should say it is the turning of courts of law into veritable theatres for sex dramas, involving clergymen and parishioners, psychiatrists and patients.

Synset Examples

an authentic signature
a bona fide manuscript
an unquestionable antique
photographs taken in a veritable bull ring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authentic - real or genuine; not counterfeit
  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic

Etymology

From Old French veritable, from Latin veritas (truth).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛrətəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪtəbəl / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something is real or true to a significant degree; genuine or actual, often as an intensifier.

Examples

Crossing the 4000 - foot width of the Mekong at Champassak, on a raft with an outboard motor, we took off our dusty shirts and enjoyed a veritable ocean breeze.

Synset Examples

a regular morass of details
a regular nincompoop
he's a veritable swine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: real - actually existing or occurring; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From Old French 'veritable', from Latin 'veritabilis', from 'veritas' (truth).
