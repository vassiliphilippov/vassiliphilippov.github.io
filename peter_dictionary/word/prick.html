prick

1. NOUN

/ prɪk / (AmE), / prɪk / (BrE)
An insulting and vulgar term for a contemptible, unpleasant, or irritating person.

Examples

He looked for the source of the noise that had awakened him * * h It was that prick Coughlin.
All pricks like Coughlin run it anyway, one way or another.
She's fucking this prick, see, this golf pro.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - (informal) a contemptible or annoying person
  • Synonym: asshole - (vulgar slang) an obnoxious or detestable person

Etymology

Originally a slang term referring to the male genitalia; use as an insult dates from the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ prɪk / (AmE), / prɪk / (BrE)
to make a small hole in something with a sharp point, such as a needle or thorn

Examples

Strenuous efforts were made to remove pin pricking from administration statements.
Prick frankfurters with fork; place in sauce.

Synset Examples

The nurse pricked my finger to get a small blood sample

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puncture - to pierce or make a hole, typically with a pointed object

Etymology

Old English *prician* (to prick, pierce), of Germanic origin.
