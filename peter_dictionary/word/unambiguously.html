unambiguously

1. ADV

/ ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.juəs.li / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is clear and not open to more than one interpretation; leaving no doubt.

Examples

It is clear that patterns of stress sometimes show construction unambiguously in the spoken language where without the help of context it would be ambiguous in the written.
They isolate out easily, naturally, and unambiguously from the continuum of nature and existence; and they should be given priority in the basic list as long as they continue to show these qualities.

Synset Examples

she stated her intentions unequivocally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguously - in a way that is open to more than one interpretation

Etymology

From 'unambiguous' (not ambiguous) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix); 'ambiguous' from Latin 'ambiguus' (doubtful, shifting).
