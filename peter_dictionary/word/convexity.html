convexity

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛksəti / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛksəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being convex; the property of having a curved or outwardly rounded surface.

Examples

Here, for the case of squares inscribed in plane curves, we remove the restriction to convexity and give certain other results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concavity - the state of being concave; inward curve or hollow

Etymology

From Late Latin convexitas, from Latin convexus (arched, vaulted).
