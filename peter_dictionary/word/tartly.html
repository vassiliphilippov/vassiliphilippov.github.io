tartly

1. ADV

/ ˈtɑrtli / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtli / (BrE)
In a manner that is sharp, bitter, or cutting, especially in speech.

Examples

"Old Crip was n't ", retorted Cobb tartly.

Synset Examples

`Never mind your immortal soul,' she said tartly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - in a manner that is abrupt or harsh
  • Antonym: sweetly - in a pleasant, gentle, or kind manner

Etymology

From "tart" (sharp in taste or manner) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
