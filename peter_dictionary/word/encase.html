encase

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkeɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪs / (BrE)
To completely cover or surround something, especially to protect it.

Examples

Griffith was trying to clear his head of the champagne fuzz that encased it.

Synset Examples

my feet were encased in mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclose - to surround or close off on all sides

Etymology

From en- (put in) + case, from Old French 'en-' (in) and 'cas' (box, container).
