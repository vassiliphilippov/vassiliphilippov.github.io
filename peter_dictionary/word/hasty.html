hasty

1. ADJ

/ ˈheɪsti / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti / (BrE)
Done with excessive speed or lack of care; excessively quick.

Examples

Implements of wood and iron are available for close and hasty combat no matter where a man stands.

Synset Examples

made a hasty exit
a headlong rush to sell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rash - done without careful consideration of the possible consequences; impulsive
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally; slow and careful

Etymology

From Middle English *hasti*, from Old French *hasti* (quick, speedy, rash), from Frankish *hastig* (violent, hasty).
