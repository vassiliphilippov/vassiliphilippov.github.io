forgetfulness

1. NOUN

/ fərˈɡɛtfəlnəs / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛtf(ʊ)lnəs / (BrE)
the tendency or state of being likely to forget things

Examples

The degree of circumstance, the ratio of memory to forgetfulness, determines whether a dream will be a recognized, fulfilled prevision, or the vaguely, effective source of the weird deja vu feeling.
There are, he thought, so few true means of forgetfulness in this life that why should he shun the medicine even when the medicine seemed, as it did, a little crude?
Maybe one could be startled into forgetfulness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: memory - the ability to remember; opposite of forgetfulness
  • Degree Higher: amnesia - partial or total loss of memory; severe forgetfulness
  • Synonym: absent-mindedness - habitual inattentiveness or tendency to forget

Etymology

From 'forgetful' + '-ness'. 'Forgetful' from Old English 'forgietan'.
