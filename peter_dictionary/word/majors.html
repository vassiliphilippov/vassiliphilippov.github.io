majors

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.dʒɚz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.dʒəz / (BrE)
the most important professional league or leagues in a sport, especially baseball in the United States

Examples

The Continental League never got off the ground, but after two years it forced the existing majors to expand.
Stengel inherited DiMaggio, Rizzuto, but he brought up Mantle from Class C to the majors, from Joplin to New York.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minor leagues - lower-level professional sports leagues

Etymology

From 'major league', with 'major' from Latin 'maior' (greater).
