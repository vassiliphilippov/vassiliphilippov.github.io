hydride

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdraɪd / (BrE)
a compound in which hydrogen is combined with another element, especially a binary compound where hydrogen acts as the more electronegative part

Examples

Among the most likely producers of detectable radio line spectra are the light diatomic hydrides OH and CH; somewhat less likely sources are the heavier hydrides SH, SiH, and ScH.
