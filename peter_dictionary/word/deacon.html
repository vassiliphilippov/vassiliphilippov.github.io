deacon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːkən / (AmE), / ˈdiːkən / (BrE)
A member of some Christian churches, especially Protestant churches, appointed to assist the minister and perform various duties within the congregation.

Examples

Wilson made up his mind that he must receive at least $ 2500, but when he mentioned this to the Deacons they said nothing.
The deacon of the church, Carlson, was its janitor.

Etymology

From Greek diakonos (servant, minister), via Latin diaconus and Old English deacon.
