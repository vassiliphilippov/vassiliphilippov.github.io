testimonial

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstɪˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
A written or spoken statement that serves as evidence of someone's character, qualifications, or the value and quality of a product or service.

Examples

Seeing so many illustrations and reading so many testimonials to the value of Quick-Wate and Super-Protein, those two wonder-working Weider food supplements, he decided to try them and see what they could do for him.
The ninth century was in its artistic work "the spiritually freest and most self-sufficient between past and future ", and the loving skill spent by its artists upon their products is a testimonial to their sense that what they were doing was important and was appreciated.

Synset Examples

his effort was testimony to his devotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - a statement suggesting someone or something as worthy or fit
  • Synonym: reference - a statement describing someone's abilities and character

Etymology

From Latin 'testimonium' (evidence, witness, testimony), via Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstəˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
something given or done to express praise, respect, or appreciation for someone's achievements or qualities

Examples

To forestall any change of allegiance, the Democrats hastily organised a testimonial banquet for O' Banion, as public reward for his past services and as a reminder of where his loyalties lay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribute - gift or act showing respect or gratitude

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *testimonialis*, from Latin *testimonium* (evidence, proof).
