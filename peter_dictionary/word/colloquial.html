colloquial

1. ADJ

/ kəˈloʊkwiəl / (AmE), / kəˈləʊkwɪəl / (BrE)
(of language) used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary.

Examples

Mercer's lyrics are characterized by an unerring ear for rhythmic nuances, a puckish sense of humor expressed in language with a colloquial flair.

Synset Examples

wrote her letters in a colloquial style
the broken syntax and casual enunciation of conversational English

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formal - suitable for serious or official speech and writing

Etymology

From Latin 'colloquium' (conversation) via late Latin 'colloquialis'.
