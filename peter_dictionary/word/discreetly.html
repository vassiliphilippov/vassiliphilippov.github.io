discreetly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈskritli / (AmE), / dɪˈskriːtli / (BrE)
In a careful and prudent way, showing good judgment and self-restraint so as to avoid causing offense or drawing attention.

Examples

In these readings, the double bass is either kept discreetly in the background, or it is dressed in clown's attire - the musical equivalent of a bull in a china shop.

Synset Examples

I sent for the sergeant of the platoon both men were in and asked him to try to find out discreetly what lay behind this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudently - in a way that shows careful good judgment
  • Antonym: indiscreetly - in a careless or thoughtless way, lacking discretion

Etymology

Formed from 'discreet' (from Latin 'discretus', meaning separated, distinct) + '-ly'.
