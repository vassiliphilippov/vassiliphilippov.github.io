lifelong

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.lɒŋ / (BrE)
lasting or continuing for the whole of a person's life; enduring throughout one's life.

Examples

His life-long friend, Serge Koussevitzky, gave unreservedly of his praise and brilliant performances in Boston, New York, and Washington, D. C., to which he added broadcastings and recordings for the whole nation.
FBI MAN 1: You'’ve got a great home here, Frankie, for the rest of your life.

Synset Examples

a lifelong friend
from lifelong habit
his lifelong study of Greek art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpetual - never ending or changing; lasting forever

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'long', meaning 'as long as a life'.
