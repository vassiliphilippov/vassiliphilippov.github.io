aggressiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡresɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having hostile feelings or a tendency to attack or confront

Examples

2) As a country superimposes its cultural and political attitudes on others, it searches its heritage in hopes of justifying its aggressiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or antagonistic attitude
  • Antonym: passiveness - state of being passive, lacking aggression

Etymology

From Latin 'aggressio' (attack) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being bold, forceful, or likely to take strong action, especially in pursuing one's goals or confronting others

Examples

Virtually everyone agrees, however, that the trend toward female sexual aggressiveness was tremendously accelerated with the postwar rush to the suburbs.
"When the husband becomes passive in the face of his wife's aggressiveness ", Dr. Linden says, "the wife, in turn, finds him inadequate.
Sometimes a burst of aggressiveness will sweep over a man - or his wife - because he or she feels age creeping up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggression - hostile or violent behavior or attitudes; readiness to attack or confront
  • Antonym: passivity - acceptance of what happens without active response or resistance

Etymology

From aggressive + -ness, forming a noun meaning the state or quality of being aggressive.
