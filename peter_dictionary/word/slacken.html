slacken

1. VERB

/ ˈslækən / (AmE), / ˈslækən / (BrE)
to become slower or less active; to decrease in speed or intensity

Examples

There was a clump of trees that appeared to provide cover right up to the road, and the shouting and gunfire never slackened.
But it probably will be less because of a usual slackening during the last weeks of each month, Smythe said.
They cantered down the peninsula, not slackening until the coach reached Woodside where the Santa Cruz uplands begin.

Synset Examples

Production slowed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow down - to reduce speed or activity
  • Synonym: slow - to reduce speed; to become slower
  • Antonym: speed up - to increase speed or activity

Etymology

From Middle English slaken, from Old English slacian (to become loose, slacken), related to slack.
