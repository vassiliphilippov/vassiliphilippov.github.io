cattleman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈkatəlman / (BrE)
A person, especially a man, who owns, raises, or tends cattle for a living.

Examples

I heard o' Texas cattlemen wrappin' a cow thief up in green hides and lettin' the sun shrink' em and squeeze him to death.
But to the cattlemen who had been facing bankruptcy from rustling losses and to the cowboys who had been faced with lay-offs a few years earlier, he was becoming a vastly different type of legendary figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rancher - person who owns or works on a ranch, often raising cattle or sheep
  • Synonym: cowboy - person who herds and tends cattle, typically on horseback

Etymology

From 'cattle' + 'man', meaning a man involved with cattle.
