allegation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement claiming that someone has done something wrong or illegal, often made without proof and sometimes in a court of law.

Examples

However, this Court put to one side without consideration the Government's appeal from the dismissal of its Sherman Act allegations.

Synset Examples

an allegation of malpractice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a statement saying that someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'allegatio' (a bringing forth as evidence), from Latin 'allegare' (to allege).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement or claim that someone makes without necessarily having proof, often one that they are prepared to try to prove in a legal or formal context.

Examples

While there are now allegations of the withholding of "favorable evidence developed at the hearing "and a denial of a "full and fair hearing ", no such claim was made by petitioner at any stage of the administrative process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin ‘allegatio’ (affirmation, declaration), from ‘allegare’ (to allege).
