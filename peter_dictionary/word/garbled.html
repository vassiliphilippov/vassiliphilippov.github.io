garbled

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑrbəld / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbəld / (BrE)
(of information, communication, or text) distorted or jumbled to the point of being unclear or nonsensical; lacking coherence or continuity.

Examples

Accounts were garbled at the telegraph office when they sent old George down to Parkersburg for the news.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - lacking clarity or coherence
  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking order or structure

Etymology

From Middle English garbelen, meaning 'to sift, select', from Medieval Latin garbellare, of Arabic origin (from *gharbala*, to sift).
