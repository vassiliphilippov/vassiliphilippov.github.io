omission

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
something that has been left out or excluded.

Examples

Angelina stepped back, her eyes roaming the tray for omissions.

Synset Examples

she searched the table for omissions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act or state of leaving something out
  • Antonym: addition - the act of adding something; an item that is included rather than omitted

Etymology

From Latin omissio(n-), from omittere ‘to let go, neglect’.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əʊˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a failure to do something that should have been done, especially resulting in a mistake through neglect or oversight.

Examples

The omission might look peculiar to outsiders, but Andy could not bring himself to go through the motions simply for the sake of appearances.
He wore no gun - a strange ommission for a peace officer in this country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: commission - an act of doing something (esp. a crime), opposite of omission

Etymology

From Latin omissio, from omittere ‘to let go, neglect’.

3. NOUN

/ əˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act or process of leaving out sounds or words in speech or writing.

Examples

Omission of a subordinator pronoun, however, does not result in an increase in stress on a prepositional adverb for which the subordinator pronoun would be object.

Etymology

From Latin 'omissio', from 'omittere' (to let go, to neglect).
