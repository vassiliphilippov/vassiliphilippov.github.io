feudal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːdəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the social and economic system of feudalism, especially as it existed in medieval Europe.

Examples

Burly leathered men and wrinkled women in drab black rags carried on in a primitive way, almost unchanged from feudal times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feudalistic - relating to or resembling feudalism

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'feudalis', from 'feodum' (fief, estate).
