cardboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a stiff, thick paper material often used for making boxes and signs

Examples

Cardboard noisemakers, substitutes for the unavailable tin models, were being hawked and bought at makeshift stands every few yards along Broadway, and one's ears were continually serenaded by the horns' rasps and bleats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paper - a thin material made from wood pulp, used for writing, printing, or wrapping
