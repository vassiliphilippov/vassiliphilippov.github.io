excise

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪz / (BrE)
to remove something, especially by erasing, cutting out, or striking from a text, record, or material

Examples

Jack crawled under the boards and restored the six feet of lead line he had excised from the Nernst generator switch.

Synset Examples

Please strike this remark from the record
scratch that remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delete - to remove or erase written or printed material
  • Antonym: insert - to add or include something, especially in text

Etymology

From Latin 'excidere' meaning 'to cut out', from 'ex-' (out) + 'caedere' (to cut).
