mildly

1. ADV

/ ˈmaɪldli / (AmE), / ˈmaɪldli / (BrE)
To a moderate or slight degree; not strongly or intensely.

Examples

"Sic semper tyrannis ", it said mildly.
And a gray-haired man whose glance - direct, lifelike, and mildly accusing - was contradicted by the gilt and black frame.
"You owe it to Penny to give her a chance to explain that she was defending you, really ", he observed mildly.

Synset Examples

he was mildly interested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderately - to a moderate extent or degree
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree; a little
  • Degree Higher: strongly - with great intensity or force

Etymology

From 'mild' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix.
