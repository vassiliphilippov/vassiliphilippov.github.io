perfectibility

1. NOUN

/ pərˌfɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / pəˌfɛktɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being capable of becoming perfect or attaining perfection

Examples

Outstanding among these is the idea of human nature itself, including the many definitions that have been advanced over the centuries; also secondary notions such as the perfectibility of man, the depravity of man, and the dignity of man.

Synset Examples

he believes in the ultimate perfectibility of man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfectness - the quality of being perfect

Etymology

From French perfectibilité, from Late Latin perfectibilitas, from perfectus (perfect).
