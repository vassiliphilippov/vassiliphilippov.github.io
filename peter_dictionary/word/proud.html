proud

1. ADJ

/ praʊd / (AmE), / praʊd / (BrE)
Feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of one's own achievements, qualities, or those of someone with whom one is closely associated.

Examples

And what leadership a proud Mantle has given so far.
It is extraordinary that a people as proud and warlike as Southerners should have been as docile as they have.
He was fortunate, and proud.
The thing about Eugene is that he's very proud ".
He was proud that he had never worn a wig.

Synset Examples

proud parents
proud of his accomplishments
a proud moment
proud to serve his country
a proud name
proud princes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arrogant - having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's own importance

Etymology

From Old English *prut* (excellent, splendid, arrogant), of unclear Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ praʊd / (AmE), / praʊd / (BrE)
Having or displaying great dignity, nobility or grandeur.

Examples

Many English Catholics are proud of their Catholicism and know that they are in a new ascendancy.

Synset Examples

a gallant pageant
lofty ships
majestic cities
proud alpine peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Synonym: lofty - of imposing height or noble nature
  • Antonym: humble - modest or having a low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Old English *prūd*, from Old French *prud* (courageous, valiant), from Late Latin *prōdus* (useful, virtuous).
