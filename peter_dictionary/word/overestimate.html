overestimate

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to judge or guess the value, amount, or importance of something as being greater than it really is

Examples

The statement is often made that when Bultmann argues in this way, he "overestimates the intellectual stumbling-block which myth is supposed to put in the way of accepting the Christian faith ".

Synset Examples

He overestimated his own powers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimate - to judge something as less than its actual value or size

Etymology

From over- (above, excessively) + estimate, from Latin aestimare (to value).
