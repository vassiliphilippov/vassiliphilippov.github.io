fisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is catching fish, especially for a living

Examples

On the shores north and south, the fishers and mooncursers - smugglers - lived along the churning Great South Bay and the narrow barrier of sand, Fire Island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fisherman - a person, usually a man, who catches fish for a living
  • Synonym: angler - a person who catches fish with a hook and line

Etymology

From Old English *fiscere* (one who fishes).
