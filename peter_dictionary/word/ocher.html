ocher

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a moderate yellow-orange to orange color

Examples

In the meantime, six sandals, stained an ocher, the same color as Pompeii's shaved hair, edged up close to him.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow - the color between green and orange in the spectrum, next to ocher
  • Degree Higher: orange - the color between red and yellow in the spectrum, deeper than ocher

Etymology

From Greek 'ōkhra' (pale yellow), via Latin 'ochra'.
