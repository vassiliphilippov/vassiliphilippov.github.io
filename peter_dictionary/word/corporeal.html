corporeal

1. ADJ

/ kɔrˈpɔriəl / (AmE), / kɔːˈpɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
having a physical or material form; relating to the body rather than the mind or spirit

Examples

By its greater corporeal presence and its greater extraneousness, the affixed paper or cloth serves for a seeming moment to push everything else into a more vivid idea of depth than the simulated printing or simulated textures had ever done.

Synset Examples

that which is created is of necessity corporeal and visible and tangible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorporeal - lacking material form; not composed of matter
  • Synonym: physical - relating to material things; having a physical presence

Etymology

From Latin corporeus (of the body), from corpus, corporis (body).
