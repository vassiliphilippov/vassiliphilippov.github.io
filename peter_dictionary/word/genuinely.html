genuinely

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛn.ju.ɪn.li / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ju.ɪn.li / (BrE)
In a real, sincere, or honest way; truly or actually rather than superficially or falsely.

Examples

It should attempt to communicate both an appreciation of professional standards and an understanding of the tremendous powers and potentialities of genuinely open and pluralistic societies.
And when they put you in that cell, and those bars slam home... that's when you know it's for real.

Synset Examples

she was now truly American
a genuinely open society
they don't really listen to us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truly - in a truthful or genuine manner
  • Synonym: really - in actual fact; truly
  • Antonym: falsely - in a false or insincere way

Etymology

From 'genuine' + '-ly'; 'genuine' from Latin genuinus (innate, natural, authentic).
