formative

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
A linguistic unit, such as a prefix, suffix, or inflection, that has a syntactic or morphological function but is not necessarily a word by itself.

Examples

Historically re is the formative that is employed also in republic, and al is the common suffix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affix - a morpheme attached to a word stem to form a new word or word form
  • Synonym: morpheme - the smallest grammatical unit in a language

Etymology

From Latin 'formativus' (serving to form), from 'formare' (to form).
