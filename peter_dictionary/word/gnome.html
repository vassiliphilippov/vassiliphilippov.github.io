gnome

1. NOUN

/ noʊm / (AmE), / nəʊm / (BrE)
A legendary creature resembling a small, old man who is said to live underground and guard buried treasure.

Examples

I would not want to be one of those writers who begin each morning by exclaiming, "O Gogol, O Chekhov, O Thackeray and Dickens, what would you have made of a bomb shelter ornamented with four plaster-of-Paris ducks, a birdbath, and three composition gnomes with long beards and red mobcaps "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwarf - a mythical small humanoid, often found in folklore
  • Synonym: elf - a supernatural creature of folk tales, typically with pointed ears

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'gnomus', coined in the 16th century by Paracelsus, possibly from Greek 'gnōmē' (thought, intelligence).
