incurable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbl / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) impossible to cure or treat successfully.

Examples

He is confronted with the recurrent crises, such as great natural catastrophes and the great transitions of life - marriage, incurable disease, widowhood, old age, the certainty of death.

Synset Examples

an incurable disease
an incurable addiction to smoking

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curable - able to be cured

Etymology

From Latin 'incurabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'curabilis' (able to be cured).
