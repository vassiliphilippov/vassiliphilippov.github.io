almost

1. ADV

/ ˈɔl.moʊst / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.məʊst / (BrE)
not quite; very nearly, but not entirely or exactly.

Examples

The escheat law cannot be enforced now because it is almost impossible to locate such property, Daniel declared.
"He almost brought it back all the way ".
Halfback Bud Priddy, slowed for almost a month by a slowly mending sprained ankle, joined TCU's workout Monday.
The old man was almost too possessive.
Many styles have perforations and an almost weightlessness achieved via unlined leathers.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nearly - very close to but not exactly or completely
  • Synonym: virtually - almost or nearly as described, but not completely or according to strict definition
  • Antonym: completely - in every way or as much as possible; totally
  • Antonym: entirely - wholly; completely

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'all-most' (all + most).
