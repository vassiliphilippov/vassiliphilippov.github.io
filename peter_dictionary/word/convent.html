convent

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.vənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vənt / (BrE)
a building or set of buildings in which a community of nuns lives and worships

Examples

Especially touching is the chapter, "The Little Sister ", about a king's daughter who became a nun in the convent of St. Birgitta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a residence for monks
  • Synonym: nunnery - a convent or building where nuns live
  • Synonym: abbey - a building occupied by a community of monks or nuns, often headed by an abbot or abbess

Etymology

From Old French *convent*, from Latin *conventus* (assembly, community).
