crave

1. VERB

/ kreɪv / (AmE), / kreɪv / (BrE)
to feel a very strong desire for something, especially food, affection, or an experience

Examples

These are mentalities which crave action - and they are beginning to get it, as Messrs. Salsich and Engh report on page 372.
That glory, craved for so long, was now forbidden to him.
The beatniks crave a sexual experience in which their whole being participates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - to strongly wish for something
  • Synonym: yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something
  • Antonym: loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for

Etymology

From Old English 'crafian' (demand, implore); related to Old Norse 'krefja' (to demand).
