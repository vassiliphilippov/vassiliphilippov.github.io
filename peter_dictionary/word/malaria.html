malaria

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛriə / (AmE), / məˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease caused by parasites transmitted to humans by the bite of infected Anopheles mosquitoes, characterized by recurring episodes of chills and fever.

Examples

There was typhoid and malaria.
He loathed the idea of drying up swamps or the chills of malaria or of leaving his natural birthright.

Etymology

From Italian 'malaria', literally 'bad air', from 'mal' (bad) + 'aria' (air), originally believed to be caused by swamp air.
