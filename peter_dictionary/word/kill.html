kill

1. VERB

/ kɪl / (AmE), / kɪl / (BrE)
to prevent the progress or success of (a bill, proposal, plan, etc.), especially by putting an end to it

Examples

Rep. Wesley Roberts of Seminole, sponsor of the poll idea, said that further delay in the committee can kill the bill.

Synset Examples

kill a motion
he shot down the student's proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thwart - to prevent someone from accomplishing something
  • Synonym: defeat - to win against someone or something, causing them to fail
  • Antonym: approve - to agree with or officially accept (often of a proposal or bill)
