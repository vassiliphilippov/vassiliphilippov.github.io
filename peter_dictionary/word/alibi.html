alibi

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælɪbaɪ / (BrE)
A claim or piece of evidence that someone was elsewhere when a crime was committed, used as a defense in law.

Examples

The former scout's alibi could n't be shaken.
Once again, he shook his head, kept his face expressionless and his voice very calm, and had a strongly supported alibi ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason given to justify a fault or offense

Etymology

From Latin 'alibi' (elsewhere), ablative of 'alius' (other).
