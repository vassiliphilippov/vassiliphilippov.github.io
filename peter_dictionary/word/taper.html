taper

1. VERB

/ ˈteɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
to become gradually smaller or less; to diminish in thickness, amount, or intensity slowly and steadily

Examples

No additional transfusions were necessary after the institution of prednisone in July, 1957, in an initial dose of 40 mg. daily with gradual tapering to 10 mg. daily.

Synset Examples

Interested tapered off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminish - to make or become less
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Old French taper (to diminish), from Latin tapēre (to be diminished).
