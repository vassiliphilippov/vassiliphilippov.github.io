careerism

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈrɪərɪzəm / (BrE)
The pursuit of personal advancement in one's career, often by putting career goals above ethical considerations or personal integrity.

Examples

After Gagarin became the Greatest Man in the World, for a nation that does not believe in the cult of personality or in careerism, Moreland wrote me a letter in which he said: "I am not interested in how long a bee can live in a vacuum, or how far it can fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunism - the practice of taking advantage of opportunities with little regard for principles
  • Antonym: idealism - the belief in and pursuit of noble ideals, often at the expense of practical considerations

Etymology

From 'career' + '-ism', denoting a system or practice of career advancement, first attested in the mid-20th century.
