covetousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvətəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪtəsnɪs / (BrE)
An intense or envious desire to possess something belonging to another; greed and longing for what one does not have.

Examples

Not until the words had been spoken did Abel suddenly see the old house and the insistent sea, and feel his contrition blotted out in one shameful moment of covetousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Synonym: envy - desire for what someone else has

Etymology

From Old French *covetousnesse*, from *covetous* (from Latin *cupiditas*, desire, longing).
