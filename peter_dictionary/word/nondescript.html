nondescript

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑndɪˈskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈskrɪpt / (BrE)
Lacking distinctive or interesting features or characteristics; dull and unremarkable.

Examples

The Ivy League enjoys no easy dominion here, and the boys are as likely to dress in rather foppish Continental fashion, or even in nondescript working class manner, as they are in the restrained, button-down Ivy way.

Synset Examples

women dressed in nondescript clothes
a nondescript novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featureless - lacking notable features or characteristics
  • Synonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or special
  • Antonym: distinctive - having a quality that makes something clearly different and easy to recognize

Etymology

From Latin 'nondēscriptus' (not described, not marked out).
