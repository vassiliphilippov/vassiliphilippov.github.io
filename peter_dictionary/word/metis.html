Metis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.tɪs, məˈtiː / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.tɪs, məˈtiː / (BrE)
A member of a group of people in western Canada of mixed Indigenous (primarily Cree or Ojibwe) and European (typically French or Scottish) ancestry, recognized as one of the three distinct Aboriginal peoples in Canada.

Examples

When late in the summer the full extent of the damage was assessed, all but fifty of the Scots, Swiss and metis moved up the Red to the mouth of the Pembina river.

Etymology

French métis, from Latin mixticius (of mixed race).
