embassy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmbəsi / (AmE), / ˈɛmbəsi / (BrE)
The official building or offices where an ambassador and their staff work, representing their country in a foreign nation.

Examples

Mortars had demolished the defense ministry and set fire to the American Embassy next door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consulate - an office representing a government, headed by a consul, typically in a foreign city

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'ambassia', from Latin 'ambactus' (servant, vassal), influenced by Old French 'ambassade'.
