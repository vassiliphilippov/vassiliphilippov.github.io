album

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bəm / (AmE), / ˈæl.bəm / (BrE)
a collection of recorded music tracks released together as a single item, originally on vinyl record and later on cassette, CD, or digital formats

Examples

There's more specialization and a narrower purpose in two albums recently issued by Dover Publications.
The respective vocabularies "essential for travel "are available in separate albums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - musical recording, especially a single track or song

Etymology

From Latin 'album' (white tablet), originally used for a blank book for signatures or memorabilia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bəm / (AmE), / ˈæl.bəm / (BrE)
a book with blank pages or pockets for keeping photographs, stamps, or similar collectibles

Examples

He ran on his plump sticks of legs, freezing now and again into the sudden startled attitudes which the camera had caught and held on the paling photographs, all carefully placed and glued and labeled, resting in the fat plush album in the bottom drawer of the escritoire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrapbook - a book used for sticking cuttings, pictures, or other mementos

Etymology

From Latin 'album' (white board or tablet for public notices).
