spraying

1. NOUN

/ ˈspreɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspreɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of dispersing fungicides, insecticides, or fertilizer in fine particles over crops, often using special equipment or low-flying aircraft.

Examples

The spraying operation was conducted from the rear deck of a small Naval vessel, cruising two miles off-shore and vertical to an on-shore breeze.
Spraying continued along a two mile course.
He may have to depend upon custom service for specialized operations, such as spraying or threshing, and for these, he may have to wait his turn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crop-dusting - the aerial application of agricultural chemicals to crops

Etymology

From the verb 'spray' (to scatter in drops or particles) plus the suffix '-ing', forming a noun.
