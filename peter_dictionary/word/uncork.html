uncork

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkɔrk / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔːk / (BrE)
To release or let loose suddenly, especially an action, remark, or emotion.

Examples

Saxton has made only one second-half appearance this season and that was in the Washington State game, for four plays: he returned the kickoff 30 yards, gained five yards through the line and then uncorked a 56 - yard touchdown run before retiring to the bench.

Synset Examples

uncork his anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to set free or allow to be expressed
  • Antonym: bottle up - to restrain or suppress (an emotion, remark, etc.)

Etymology

From 'un-' (not, opposite of) + 'cork' (the stopper of a bottle), originally meaning to remove a cork, later used figuratively.
