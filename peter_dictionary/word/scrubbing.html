scrubbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskrʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of cleaning a surface by rubbing it with a brush, soap, and water.

Examples

The toilet had n't had a sincere scrubbing in years and there were things written on the walls of the little boxed in place because you could n't keep the public out - entirely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard, especially with a brush and soap

Etymology

From 'scrub' (to rub hard in cleaning), with the suffix '-ing' forming a noun of action.
