armadillo

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrməˈdɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˌɑːməˈdɪləʊ / (BrE)
A burrowing, chiefly nocturnal mammal native to the Americas, characterized by a body covered with strong, horny plates.

Examples

A dead armadillo, the sole occupant of the stage, symbolizes the crisis and destruction of the Old Order.
The night we saw it, a rather unpleasant situation arose when the soloist refused to approach the armadillo, complaining - in ad-lib - that "it smelled ".

Etymology

From Spanish 'armadillo', diminutive of 'armado' meaning 'armored', from Latin 'armatus' (armed, armored).
