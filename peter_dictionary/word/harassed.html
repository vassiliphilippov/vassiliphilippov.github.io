harassed

1. ADJ

/ həˈræst, ˈhær əst / (AmE), / həˈræst, ˈhær əst / (BrE)
Feeling or showing the effects of persistent annoyance, stress, or pressure, especially from repeated petty disturbances.

Examples

Harassed state park officials often have more campers than they know what to do with.

Synset Examples

harassed working mothers
a harried expression
her poor pestered father had to endure her constant interruptions
the vexed parents of an unruly teenager

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: annoyed - slightly irritated or bothered
  • Synonym: harried - feeling strained as a result of persistent demands

Etymology

From harass, derived from French *harasser* (to tire, vex).
