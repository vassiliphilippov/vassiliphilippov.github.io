confidante

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfɪˈdænt, ˈkɑːnfɪˌdænt / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfɪˈdænt, ˈkɒnfɪˌdænt / (BrE)
A woman with whom one shares a secret or private matter, trusting her not to repeat it to others; a female confidant.

Examples

Each was her mother's assistant and confidante; and each stood out conspicuously in the family picture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidant - a person, especially a man, in whom one confides

Etymology

From French 'confidante', feminine of 'confidant'. Ultimately from Italian 'confidante' and Latin 'confidens' (trusting).
