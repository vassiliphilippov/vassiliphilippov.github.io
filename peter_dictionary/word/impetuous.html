impetuous

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛtʃuəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛtjuːəs / (BrE)
Acting or done quickly and without thought or care; marked by impulsiveness or rashness.

Examples

Schnabel's emphasis on structural clarity, his innate rhythmic vibrancy, and impetuous intensity all tend to stamp his reading as a symphonic one.
He expected Concetta's thin hand to reach down to grasp the boy, and her shrill, impetuous voice to sound against the rotundity of his disfigured flesh that was never sure of hearing anything.

Synset Examples

a hotheaded decision
liable to such impulsive acts as hugging strangers
an impetuous display of spending and gambling
madcap escapades

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rash - acting without careful consideration of the possible consequences
  • Synonym: impulsive - acting or done without forethought
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Late Latin impetuosus (violent, forceful), from Latin impetus (attack, impulse).
