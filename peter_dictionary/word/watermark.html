watermark

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəmɑːk / (BrE)
A line or mark showing the highest level reached by a body of water, especially as a visible stain or line left on objects after flooding or submersion.

Examples

He examined the water marks on the iron tires when the animals were finished.

Etymology

From 'water' + 'mark', referring to a visible mark left by water.
