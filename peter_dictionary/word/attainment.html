attainment

1. NOUN

/ əˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of achieving or accomplishing something, especially a goal or aim

Examples

Many people seem hopeful, yet it is difficult to predict whether or not there will be any more real attainment of Christian unity in 1961 than there was in 1861.

Synset Examples

the attainment of independence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something accomplished, especially by superior ability, special effort, or great courage
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French *atainement*, from *ataindre* (to attain), from Latin *attingere* (to touch upon).

2. NOUN

/ əˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of reaching or achieving a particular stage or level; arrival at a new stage in development or progress

Examples

A similar analysis of Figure 6 for the 34 boys would necessitate quite a different conclusion about the predictive value of onset age in forecasting their attainment of the pubescent stage.

Synset Examples

his attainment of puberty was delayed by malnutrition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - the act of achieving something successfully
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in doing or achieving something

Etymology

From Old French *atainment*, from *ataindre* (to attain), from Latin *attingere* (to reach, touch upon).
