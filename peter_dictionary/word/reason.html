reason

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːzən / (AmE), / ˈriːzən / (BrE)
A cause, explanation, or justification for an action or belief.

Examples

Solly Hemus announced a switch in his starting pitcher, from Bob Gibson to Ernie Broglio, for several reasons:
In arguing in this way, we are obviously taking for granted that a demythologized restatement of the kerygma can be achieved; and that we firmly believe this will presently become evident when we set forth reasons to justify such a conviction.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motive - a reason for doing something, especially one that is hidden or not obvious

Etymology

From Old French raison, from Latin ratio (reckoning, understanding, cause).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːzən / (AmE), / ˈriːzən / (BrE)
A cause or explanation for something that has happened or is true.

Examples

Each high note had the crowd in ecstasy so that it stopped the show midway in the "Mad Scene ", but the real reason was a realization of the extraordinary performance unfolding at the moment.
While it must be said that these same Protestants have built some new churches during this period, and that religious population shifts have emptied churches, a principal reason for this phenomenon of redundancy is that fewer Protestants are going to church.

The "chase "as a standard film device probably dates from The Great Train Robbery, and there is a reason for the continued popularity of the device.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause - something that brings about an effect or result
  • Synonym: excuse - an explanation offered to justify or lessen blame
  • Antonym: result - a consequence, often as the opposite of a cause

Etymology

From Old French *raison*, from Latin *ratio* (reckoning, reason), from *reri* (to reckon, calculate).

3. VERB

/ ˈriːzən / (AmE), / ˈriːzən / (BrE)
to think about something in a logical way in order to form a conclusion or make a decision

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deduce - to arrive at a conclusion by reasoning
  • Synonym: infer - to deduce or conclude from evidence and reasoning

Etymology

From Old French *raisonner*, from Latin *rationari* (to reckon, calculate), from *ratio* (reason).

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason given to justify a fault or offense

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *raison*, from Latin *ratio* (reckoning, calculation, reason).
