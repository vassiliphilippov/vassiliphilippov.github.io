undue

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuː / (BrE)
more than is appropriate, permitted, or necessary in the circumstances; unwarranted or excessive.

Examples

Obviously, the Interstate Commerce Commission will not force the New York Central to further curtail its commuter operations by giving undue competitive advantages to the lines that wish to merge.

Synset Examples

undue influence
I didn't want to show undue excitement
accused of using undue force

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is reasonable or necessary
  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: necessary - needed or required

Etymology

From Latin 'indebitus' (not owed), via Old French 'indue', influenced by English 'due.'
