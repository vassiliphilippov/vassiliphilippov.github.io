incapacitate

1. VERB

/ ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something unable to move, act, or function in the normal way

Examples

When her right hand was incapacitated by the rheumatism, Sadie learned to write with her left hand.

Synset Examples

disable this command on your computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disable - to make incapable or unable, especially physically
  • Synonym: impair - to weaken or damage something, especially a function or ability
  • Antonym: enable - to make able; to give the means or ability

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incapacitatus, past participle of incapacitat-, from incapax (incapable).
