landlocked

1. ADJ

/ ˈlænd.lɑkt / (AmE), / ˈlænd.lɒkt / (BrE)
(of a country, region, or body of water) entirely or almost entirely surrounded by land, having no direct access to the sea.

Examples

North and south, east and west, back and forth he sailed in the land-locked bay, plowing furiously forward until land appeared, then turning to repeat the process, day after day, week after week.

Synset Examples

a landlocked country

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coastal - located on or near a coast

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + past participle of 'lock', meaning enclosed or shut in by land.
