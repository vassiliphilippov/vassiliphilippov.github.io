confusing

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
difficult to understand; causing someone to feel bewildered or unclear.

Examples

Tonal morphophonemics is much more confusing to the beginning analyst than consonantal morphophonemics, even when the total number of rules is no greater.

Synset Examples

sent confusing signals to Iraq
perplexing to someone who knew nothing about it
a puzzling statement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand; not confusing
  • Synonym: perplexing - causing confusion or puzzlement
  • Synonym: puzzling - difficult to understand or explain

Etymology

From confuse + -ing, with 'confuse' from Latin 'confundere' (to mingle, mix up, confuse).
