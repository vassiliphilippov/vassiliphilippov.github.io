justifiably

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒʌstəˌfaɪəbli / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is reasonable or has good justification; for a valid or defensible reason.

Examples

Mr. A has recovered, but he is, justifiably, a bitter man.
Of course, it goes without saying that no student of ideas can justifiably ignore the contemporary scene.

Synset Examples

he is justifiably bitter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unjustifiably - in a way lacking good reason or justification

Etymology

From 'justifiable' (from Late Latin justificabilis, from justificare 'to justify') + adverbial -ly.
