whimper

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪmpər / (AmE), / ˈwɪmpə / (BrE)
To cry or make a series of low, feeble sounds expressing fear, pain, or unhappiness; to complain in a weak or soft voice.

Examples

Above all, disabuse yourselves of any thought that I propose to vent moral indignation at your rifling my residence, to whimper over the loss of a few objets d'art, or to shame you into rectitude.

Synset Examples

she wailed with pain

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wail - cry out with a long, loud sound expressing pain, grief, or anger
  • Degree Higher: sob - cry noisily while taking short breaths
  • Degree Lower: moan - make a low sound expressing pain or suffering
