preliterate

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriˈlɪt̬ərət / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
(of a society or culture) existing before the development of a system of writing.

Examples

The place of religion in the simple, preliterate societies is quite definite; as a complex it fits into the whole social organization and functions dominantly in every part of it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

From Latin prefix *pre-* (before) + English *literate* (able to read and write).

2. ADJ

/ ˌprɛˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
(of a person or society) not yet having learned to read and write; existing before the development of writing.

Examples

I am not aware of great attention by any of these authors or by the psychotherapeutic profession to the role of literary study in the development of conscience - most of their attention is to a pre-literate period of life, or, for the theologians of course, to the influence of religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read and write

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'literate' (able to read and write).
