twirl

1. VERB

/ twɝl / (AmE), / twɜːl / (BrE)
to turn, spin, or rotate something or oneself quickly around a central point or axis

Examples

Carroll diagnosed Mrs. Hull by taking a drop of blood from her ear and putting it on his "radionic "machine and twirling some knobs (fee $ 50).
She had picked up the quirt and was twirling it around her wrist and smiling at him.

Synset Examples

The leaves swirled in the autumn wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly; to rotate rapidly
  • Synonym: whirl - to move or make something move quickly in a circular or spinning motion
  • Synonym: twist - to turn something in a circular motion

2. VERB

/ twɜrl / (AmE), / twɜːl / (BrE)
to cause something to spin around quickly and lightly

Examples

At one side of the stage a dancer jumps excitedly; nearby, another sits motionless, while still another is twirling an umbrella.

Synset Examples

spin a coin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to turn around rapidly on an axis
  • Synonym: whirl - to move or cause to move rapidly around and around

Etymology

Perhaps imitative, or a blend of 'twist' and 'whirl'.
