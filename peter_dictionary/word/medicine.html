/ ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈmɛdsɪn / (BrE)

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'medicina' (the healing art, medicine), from 'medicus' (physician).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
A substance or preparation used to treat, prevent, or relieve the symptoms of disease or illness.

Examples

The sick were always receiving medicines.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin medicīna (the healing art, remedy), from medicus (physician).
