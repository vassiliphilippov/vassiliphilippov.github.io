outcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A person who has been rejected or excluded from society or a social group.

Examples

His alienation is far more acute than Holmes'; he is not an eccentric but rather an outcast.
The Corleone family would be outcasts!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah - a person who is despised or avoided by others; social outcast
  • Antonym: insider - a person accepted as a member of a group or society

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'outcasté' (to cast out), influenced by 'cast', meaning to throw or reject.
