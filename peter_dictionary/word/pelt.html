pelt

1. VERB

/ pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
to throw objects at someone or something repeatedly and forcefully

Examples

The heavens opened, pelting them with hail the size of walnuts.

Synset Examples

They pelted each other with snowballs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack repeatedly with missiles or bombs
  • Synonym: shower - to throw or send down in large amounts or numbers

Etymology

Middle English, perhaps from Old French *peloter* (to strike with a ball), from *pelote* (ball).
