selectman

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɛktmən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktmən / (BrE)
An elected member of the executive board in New England towns, responsible for municipal administration.

Examples

The 1946 town meeting voted to have the Selectmen appoint a committee to investigate and report on the feasibility of some system of sewage disposal and a disposal plant to serve Manchester Center, Depot, and Way's Lane.

Etymology

From 'select' + 'man': originally referring to men specially chosen for civic duty.
