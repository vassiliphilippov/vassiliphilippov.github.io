skylight

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a window set in a roof or ceiling, designed to admit natural light

Examples

At each angle of its pitch a big skylight had been fitted into the roof and all these skylights were fitted with systems of multiple screens and shades.
At each angle of its pitch a big skylight had been fitted into the roof and all these skylights were fitted with systems of multiple screens and shades.
He quickly fixed that, rolling back the shades on some of the skylights and adjusting screens on the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rooflight - another term for a window set in a roof

Etymology

Formed from 'sky' + 'light' to denote a light-admitting window in the roof.
