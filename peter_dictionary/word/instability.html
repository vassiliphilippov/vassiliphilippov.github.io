instability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unreliable due to lack of stability; tendency to change, break down, or fail.

Examples

Besides, he can hardly avoid musing on the instability of death which, what with exhumations and rehabilitations, seems to match that of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stability - the quality of being stable or reliable
  • Synonym: unreliability - the quality of not being reliable or dependable

Etymology

From Latin instabilitas, from in- (not) + stabilis (stable).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being unstable; lack of stability, firmness, or security.

Examples

Need for novelty may be a symptom of cultural fatigue and instability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable or firm
  • Synonym: precariousness - the quality of being uncertain or insecure

Etymology

From Latin 'instabilitas', from 'instabilis' (unsteady, unstable).
