emeritus

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɛrɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛrɪtəs / (BrE)
(of a person) having retired from professional life but allowed to retain their title as an honor, especially in academic or professional settings.

Examples

Mr. Oakes succeeds Charles Merz, editor since 1938, who now becomes editor emeritus.

Etymology

From Latin *emeritus* (having served one's time, veteran), past participle of *emereri* (to earn by service).
