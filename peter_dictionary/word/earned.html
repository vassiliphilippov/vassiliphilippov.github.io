earned

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝːnd / (AmE), / ˈɜːnd / (BrE)
gained or acquired, especially as a result of one's own effort or merit

Examples

Dr. Clark holds an earned Doctor of Education degree from the University of Oklahoma.
"That's a lot of hard earned money to lose ", he says today.
And behind every shady deal, behind every dollar earned, there was Andy, keeping the books.

Synset Examples

a well-earned reputation for honesty
earned income
an earned run in baseball

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unearned - not gained by effort or merit

Etymology

Past participle of 'earn', from Old English *earnian* (to earn, deserve).
