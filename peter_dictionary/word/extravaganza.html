extravaganza

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstrævəˈɡænzə / (AmE), / ɪkˌstrævəˈɡænzə / (BrE)
a lavish or spectacular entertainment or production, typically involving elaborate staging, costumes, or effects

Examples

"Uncle Sam "was, indeed, a rich uncle to Prokofieff, in those opulent, post-war victory years of peace and prosperity, bold speculations and extravaganzas, enjoyment and pleasure: "The Golden Twenties ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectacle - an impressive or striking public display or performance

Etymology

From Italian *stravaganza* (extravagance), from *stravagante* (extravagant).
