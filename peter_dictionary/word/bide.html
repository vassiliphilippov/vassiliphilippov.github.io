bide

1. VERB

/ baɪd / (AmE), / baɪd / (BrE)
(literary or archaic) To stay or dwell in a place; to remain somewhere.

Examples

They would have to bide their official time and see, trusting that the massive doses of shell-psychology would suffice her, too, as the necessary bulwark against her unusual confinement and the pressures of her profession.

Synset Examples

You can stay with me while you are in town
stay a bit longer--the day is still young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to continue to be in a particular place; to remain
  • Synonym: wait - stay in place in expectation of something

Etymology

Old English 'bīdan', meaning 'to remain, wait, dwell'.
