solemnly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑː.ləm.li / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.əm.li / (BrE)
In a serious, formal, and dignified manner.

Examples

A Newfoundland sat solemnly beside a doghouse half his size.
When she had wiped some of the lipstick from her mouth, she stared solemnly at her image in the mirror.
Indeed he did, Sam said solemnly, trying to get Carla's eye.

Synset Examples

the judge sat there solemnly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravely - in a serious and dignified manner
  • Synonym: seriously - in a serious manner

Etymology

From 'solemn' + '-ly'; 'solemn' from Latin 'solemnis' (customary, ceremonial).
