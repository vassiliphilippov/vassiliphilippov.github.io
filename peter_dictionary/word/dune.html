dune

1. NOUN

/ duːn / (AmE), / djuːn / (BrE)
A hill or ridge of sand formed by the wind, commonly found in deserts or near the sea or lakes.

Examples

Naturally, a park site with scenic views, a good lake, trees, and sand dunes, will attract more people than a nearby area with only trees and dunes.

Etymology

From French 'dune', from Middle Dutch 'dūne' (sand hill).
