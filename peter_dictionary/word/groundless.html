groundless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡraʊndləs / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndləs / (BrE)
Having no basis in reason, fact, or evidence.

Examples

On the theory before us, such relief would be groundless, for in that suffering itself there was nothing bad at all, and hence in its nonoccurrence there would be nothing to be relieved about.

Synset Examples

baseless gossip
the allegations proved groundless
idle fears
unfounded suspicions
unwarranted jealousy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseless - without foundation in fact
  • Synonym: unfounded - having no foundation or basis
  • Synonym: unsubstantiated - not supported by evidence

Etymology

Formed from 'ground' (basis) + '-less' (without).
