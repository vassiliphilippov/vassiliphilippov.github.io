precipice

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛs.ə.pɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛs.ɪ.pɪs / (BrE)
A very steep or vertical cliff or rock face, especially a high one.

Examples

To port was a point 200 feet high rising behind to a precipice of 2000 feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliff - a steep rock face, especially at the edge of the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'praecipitium' (a precipice, steep place), from 'praeceps' (headlong, steep).
