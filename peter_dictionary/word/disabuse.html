disabuse

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈbjuːz / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbjuːz / (BrE)
to persuade someone that what they believe is not true; to free someone from a mistaken or false idea

Examples

Above all, disabuse yourselves of any thought that I propose to vent moral indignation at your rifling my residence, to whimper over the loss of a few objets d'art, or to shame you into rectitude.
Thus he will be in a position to disabuse the Soviet leader of any notions he may have about grave Allied disunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding
  • Antonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true

Etymology

From French désabuser (to undeceive), from dés- (dis-) + abuser (to abuse, deceive)
