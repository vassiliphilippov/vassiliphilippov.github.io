2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(dated, offensive in modern use) Having dark skin, especially as a member of a racial group with high levels of melanin pigment; formerly used as a term for people of African descent.

Examples

Despite the opposition of the city newspapers, the Pratt Hall meeting "brought together a very respectable audience, composed in part of those who had been distinguished for years for their radical views upon the subject of slavery, of many of our colored citizens, and of those who were attracted to the place by the novelty of such a gathering ".

Synset Examples

National Association for the Advancement of Colored People

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black - of or belonging to a racial group with dark skin, especially of African descent
  • Synonym: African American - an American of African and especially Black African descent
  • Antonym: white - of or belonging to a racial group with light skin

Etymology

Derived from the past participle of the verb 'color', meaning to apply color.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(offensive, archaic, US) A term once used in the United States to refer to a Black person; now considered offensive.

Examples

In my city we would keep the traffic to the coloured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black - A person of African descent; accepted modern term
