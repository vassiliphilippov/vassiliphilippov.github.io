meronymy

1. NOUN

/ məˈroʊnəmi / (AmE), / məˈrɒnəmi / (BrE)
(Linguistics) the semantic relationship between a part and the whole to which it belongs; for example, 'wheel' is a meronym of 'car'.

Examples

You were just being strong for all of us, the way Papa was.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holonymy - the relation of a whole to its parts

Etymology

From Greek μέρος (meros, 'part') + ὄνομα (onoma, 'name').
