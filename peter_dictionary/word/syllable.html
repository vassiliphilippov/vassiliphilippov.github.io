syllable

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.bəl / (BrE)
A unit of pronunciation having one vowel sound, with or without surrounding consonants, forming the whole or a part of a word.

Examples

But still Mel Chandler was not completely convinced that men would really die for a four syllable word, "Garryowen ".

Etymology

From Old French 'silabe', from Latin 'syllaba', from Ancient Greek 'syllabē' (that which is held together, a syllable).
