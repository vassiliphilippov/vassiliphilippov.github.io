flint

1. NOUN

/ flɪnt / (AmE), / flɪnt / (BrE)
A hard, gray or black form of quartz that breaks with a conchoidal fracture and was used in the past to make tools and fire-starting devices.

Examples

I wanted to wipe my flint, but I did n't dare to, the state my hands were in, just as I did n't dare to do anything about the priming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard mineral consisting of silicon dioxide, often found as crystals

Etymology

Old English *flint*; related to Old Norse *flinte* and Old High German *flins* (hard stone).
