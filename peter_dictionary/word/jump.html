jump

1. VERB

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
(of something) to be immediately and strongly noticeable or attract attention, often suddenly or unexpectedly.

Examples

Yet the violent immediacy of the wallpaper strips pasted to the paper, and the only lesser immediacy of block capitals that simulate window lettering, manage somehow to push the grape cluster back into place on the picture plane so that it does not "jump ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand out - to be easily seen or noticed; to be prominent
  • Antonym: blend in - to not be easily noticed; to become indistinguishable from the surroundings

2. VERB

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to move or start suddenly, often as a reaction to surprise or fear

Examples

Mr. McKinley, for all his sprawling and his easy familiarity, was completely alert to his son, eyes always on the still face, jumping to anticipate Scotty's desires.
Startled, he jumped up to pull hen and case out of view, and Alex went to the door.
She had jumped away from his shy touch like a cat confronted by a sidewinder.
She hesitated, she hopped, she roll and rocked, skipped and jumped, but in some two weeks she started to pace, From that time to this she has shown steady improvement and now looks like one of the classiest things on the grounds.
Within half an hour I jumped a six-point buck that hop-skipped through a rhododendron thicket, and I caught him just behind the left foreleg at 60 yards.

Synset Examples

She startled when I walked into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: startle - to cause (someone) to feel sudden shock or alarm
  • Synonym: flinch - to make a quick movement, often from pain or fear

3. VERB

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to push oneself off the ground and into the air by using the muscles in one's legs and feet, usually in order to move over something or to reach a higher position

Examples

They set bouncing betties to jump and explode at testicle level while we more mercifully had them go off at the head.
He jumped.
That po'k, it was so full of skippers it would jump and run and not come when you say,' Hoo-pig'.
I was ready to jump but when Ma said she 'd get the whisky it surprised him like it surprised me, and he ran down.
Then, they said General Burnside was going to jump over his horse's head; and they did some somersaults.

Synset Examples

The horse bounded across the meadow
The child leapt across the puddle
Can you jump over the fence?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - to jump a long way, high or in a great hurry
  • Degree Lower: hop - to make a short jump or jumps, usually on one foot
  • Degree Higher: vault - to jump over something using the hands or a pole for support

Etymology

Probably of Middle English origin, from Old French 'jumper' (to jump), possibly of imitative origin.

4. VERB

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to enter or become involved in something eagerly or suddenly

Examples

As promptly as Torrio, O' Banion jumped into bootlegging.

Synset Examples

He jumped into the game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunge - to enter or engage in something suddenly or enthusiastically

5. VERB

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
(of a quantity, price, or amount) to increase suddenly and significantly.

Examples

Expect their own production volume, and presumably sales, to jump 30 percent in 1961.
The normal rate of suicides in East Berlin was one a day, but since the border was closed on August 13 it has jumped to 25 a day!

Synset Examples

Prices jumped overnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soar - to rise rapidly
  • Antonym: plummet - to decrease suddenly and steeply
  • Degree Lower: rise - to increase

Etymology

From Middle English 'jumpe', probably of Scandinavian origin.

7. VERB

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to rise or increase suddenly in rank, status, or position.

Examples

Carson McCullers, after a long, painful illness that might have crushed a less-indomitable soul, has come back with an absolute gem of a novel which jumped high on best-seller lists even before official publication.

Synset Examples

Her new novel jumped high on the bestseller list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soar - to rise quickly and smoothly to a high level
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall suddenly and quickly from a high level or position

8. NOUN

/ dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
an act of pushing oneself off the ground or another surface by using the muscles in one's legs and feet

Examples

You'd cut a loud fart, he'd jump three feet in the air.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - an act of jumping a long distance or to a great height
  • Degree Lower: hop - a short or small jump, often on one foot
