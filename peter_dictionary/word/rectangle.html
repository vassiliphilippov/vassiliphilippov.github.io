rectangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkˌtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛktæŋɡl / (BrE)
A four-sided flat shape with four right angles and opposite sides of equal length.

Examples

A large picnic area or camping development is most efficient in shape as a square or rectangle several hundred feet in width in preference to a long narrow area less than one hundred feet wide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: square - a rectangle with all sides of equal length

Etymology

From Latin 'rectangulus' (having right angles), from 'rectus' (right) + 'angulus' (angle).
