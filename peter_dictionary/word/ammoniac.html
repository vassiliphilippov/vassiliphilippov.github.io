ammoniac

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / əˈmɒniˌak / (BrE)
A gum resin obtained from the ammoniac plant, used historically in medicine and incense.

Examples

Leaves were burning somewhere and the smoke smelled, for all its ammoniac acidity, of beginnings.

Etymology

From Latin 'ammoniacum', from Greek 'ammoniakon' (gum of Ammonia), referring to the region near the Temple of Ammon in Libya.
