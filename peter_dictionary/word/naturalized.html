naturalized

1. ADJ

/ ˈnætʃɚɚˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnatʃərəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, etc.) introduced from another region and able to thrive and reproduce in a new environment without human cultivation.

Examples

The police were disqualified along with certain categories of naturalized citizens, criminals and those punished for Protectorate activities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: invasive - species tending to spread and cause damage in a new environment
  • Antonym: native - originally occurring in a particular place

Etymology

From 'naturalize' + -ed, meaning to make or become established as if native.
