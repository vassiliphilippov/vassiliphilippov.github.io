rationalize

1. VERB

/ ˈræʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
To try to explain or justify behavior or an attitude with logical reasons, even if these are not true or appropriate.

Examples

1) Every age rewrites the events of its history in terms of what should have been, creating legends about itself that rationalize contemporary beliefs and excuse contemporary actions.
Nevertheless, impulses still exist among the ruling elite to rationalize and thus to perpetuate the need for centralized and authoritarian practices.
This action was rationalized on the basis of a small survey which indicated that a high percentage of married freshmen women on our campus never become sophomores.

Synset Examples

rationalize the child's seemingly crazy behavior
he rationalized his lack of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justify - to show or prove to be right or reasonable
  • Synonym: excuse - to seek to lessen the blame by giving reasons

Etymology

From Medieval Latin rationalizare, from Latin rationalis (rational).

2. VERB

/ ˈræʃ.ə.nəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
To remove unnecessary or unwanted items, especially to make a system or process more efficient.

Examples

We will recall that the still confident liberals of the Truman administration gathered with other Western utopians in San Francisco to set up the legal framework, finally and at last, to rationalize war - to rationalize want and fear - out of the world: the United Nations.

Synset Examples

We had to lose weight, so we cut the sugar from our diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streamline - to make an organization or process more efficient by simplifying or eliminating unnecessary steps

Etymology

From Latin *rationalis* (rational), via French *rationaliser* (to rationalize).
