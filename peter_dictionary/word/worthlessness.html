worthlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːθ.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːθ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having no value, usefulness, or importance.

Examples

When we see the steady and methodical inculcation into humanity of the idea of man's worthlessness - until redeemed - the necessity of the Devil may become evident as a weapon, a weapon designed and used time and time again in every age to whip men into a surrender to a particular church or church-state ".

Synset Examples

the drill sergeant's intent was to convince all the recruits of their worthlessness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: value - The quality of being useful or important
  • Antonym: usefulness - The quality of being useful
  • Synonym: ineptitude - Lack of skill or ability

Etymology

Formed from 'worthless' + '-ness', where 'worthless' comes from 'worth' (value) + '-less' (without).
