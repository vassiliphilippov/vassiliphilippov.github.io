lobularity

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbjʊləˌrɪti / (AmE), / ləˈbjʊlærɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having lobules, especially as a structural feature in tissues or organs.

Examples

Lobularity is extremely well developed in type 1,; absent in type 2,; imperfectly developed in type 3,.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobule - a small lobe or subdivision of a lobe

Etymology

From 'lobule' (a small lobe) + '-arity' (forming nouns indicating a quality or state).
