falsehood

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔlshʊd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlshʊd / (BrE)
A statement that is untrue; a lie or fabrication.

Examples

It'’s a complete falsehood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - an intentionally false statement
  • Antonym: truth - a true statement or fact

Etymology

From Old English 'falsheod', from 'false' + '-hood'.
