quietness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪətnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətnɪs / (BrE)
A state of peace and calm, free from noise, disturbance, or activity.

Examples

The children had to play away from the house (in the barn loft or the pasture behind the barn), to maintain a proper quietness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquillity - a state of calm and peacefulness
  • Synonym: silence - the absence of sound or noise
  • Antonym: noise - a loud or distracting sound; opposite of quietness

Etymology

From Middle English quietnesse, from Old French quietness, from Latin quietus (calm, at rest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪətnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being quiet; the absence of noise or sound.

Examples

The primary quality of that view seems, now, to have been its quietness, but that cannot at the time have impressed us.
It became so monotonous as to seem a part of the quietness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - complete absence of sound
  • Antonym: noise - unwanted sound or loudness

Etymology

From 'quiet' + '-ness', with 'quiet' from Latin 'quietus' (calm, at rest).
