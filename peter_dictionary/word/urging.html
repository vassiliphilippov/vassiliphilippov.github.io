urging

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of earnestly encouraging, persuading, or pressing someone to take a particular action

Examples

When the family business failed, Mercer left school and on his mother's urging - for she hoped that he would become an actor - he joined a local little theater group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From the verb 'urge', which is from Latin urgēre (to press, push).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜː.dʒɪŋ / (BrE)
an attempt to persuade or encourage someone strongly to do something

Examples

But Blanche had been able to maintain a serene and assured composure in the face of her widowed mother's continued carping, had been able to resist her urgings to date anyone who offered the slightest possibility of matrimony.

Synset Examples

the ceaseless prodding got on his nerves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support or confidence
  • Antonym: dissuasion - the act of persuading someone not to do something

Etymology

From the verb 'urge', which comes from Latin urgēre (to press, drive, urge).
