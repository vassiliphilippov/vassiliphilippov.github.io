hard-fought

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd fɔt / (AmE), / hɑːd fɔːt / (BrE)
(of a contest, battle, or competition) contested with great effort and determination by both sides; requiring vigorous struggle to achieve.

Examples

The September October term jury had been charged by Fulton Superior Court Judge Durwood Pye to investigate reports of possible "irregularities "in the hard-fought primary which was won by Mayor-nominate Ivan Allen Jr..

Synset Examples

a hard-fought primary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fierce - showing intense and forceful effort (of competition)
  • Antonym: easy - not requiring much effort; the opposite of hard-fought

Etymology

Formed from 'hard' (in the sense of 'with great force or effort') and the past participle of 'fight'.
