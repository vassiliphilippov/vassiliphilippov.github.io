grieve

1. VERB

/ ɡriv / (AmE), / ɡriːv / (BrE)
to feel intense sorrow, especially because of someone's death or a great loss

Examples

Liz Peabody, he thought, might spend some time grieving for her lost lover, but he doubted that she would launch an investigation.
I have no wish to fill my few remaining years... ...grieving for the loss of old friends... ...or their sons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourn - to express sorrow, especially for someone who has died
  • Synonym: lament - to express passionate grief or sorrow

Etymology

From Old French *grever* (to burden, afflict), from Latin *gravare* (to burden, from *gravis*, heavy).
