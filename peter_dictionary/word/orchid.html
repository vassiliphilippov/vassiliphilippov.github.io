orchid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
A plant of the large family Orchidaceae, typically having showy or unusual flowers with a complex structure.

Examples

Wolfe refuses to ever leave his own house, and spends most of his time drinking beer and playing with orchids.

Etymology

From modern Latin Orchidaceae, from Greek orchis (testicle), referring to the shape of the plant's root tubers.
