amenable

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəbəl / (BrE)
willing to be influenced or controlled; open to suggestion or persuasion.

Examples

They are in general those fears that once seemed to have been amenable to prayer or ritual.

Synset Examples

someone amenable to persuasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliant - willing to agree or obey
  • Antonym: uncooperative - not willing to cooperate

Etymology

From Old French *amenable*, from Latin *minari* (to threaten), by influence of 'amener' (to lead).
