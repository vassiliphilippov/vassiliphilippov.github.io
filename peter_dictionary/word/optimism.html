optimism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːptəmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪmɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or feeling that good things will happen or that a situation will end well

Examples

Some liar's logic, a wisp of optimism as fragile as the scent of tropical blossoms that came through the window (a euphoria perhaps engendered by the pill Fritzie had given her), consoled her for a moment.
Her optimism gave me heart.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimism - the belief that bad things will happen or expect the worst

Etymology

From French optimisme, from Latin optimum (the best).
