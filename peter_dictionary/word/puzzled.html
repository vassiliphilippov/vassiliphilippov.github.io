puzzled

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌzəld / (AmE), / ˈpʌzəld / (BrE)
Unable to understand or make sense of something; feeling confused or bewildered.

Examples

McFeeley looked puzzled.
The guards lowered their rifles and their rifles and peered at Watson with sullen, puzzled faces.
The puzzled saleslady inquired, "How large is your house, Madam "?

Synset Examples

at a loss to understand those remarks
puzzled that she left without saying goodbye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly; bewildered
  • Synonym: perplexed - completely baffled; very puzzled
  • Antonym: certain - completely sure and confident; not confused or in doubt

Etymology

From the verb 'puzzle' (to bewilder or confuse), itself possibly from Old French *pusle* (confuse), of uncertain origin.
