expressly

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈsprɛsli / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsli / (BrE)
For a specific purpose; with deliberate intention or specificity.

Examples

Hard surface cleaning in household application is represented by two classes of alkaline products: (1) the formulations made expressly for machine dishwashers, and (2) the general-purpose cleaners used for walls and woodwork.

Synset Examples

she needs the money expressly for her patients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specifically - in a way that is precise and clear for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: deliberately - consciously and intentionally; on purpose
  • Antonym: accidentally - without intention; by chance

Etymology

From 'express' (to state clearly) + -ly; 'express' from Latin 'expressus' (clearly presented).
