phonic

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the phonic method of teaching reading, which emphasizes the association between sounds and letters or groups of letters.

Examples

We speculate that compulsives in the unstructured schools are under greater strain because of the lack of systemization in their school setting, but that their need to organize (for comfort) is so intense that they struggle to induce the phonic rules and achieve in spite of the lack of direction from the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonics - a method of teaching reading based on sound-letter correspondence

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (voice, sound); related to 'phone' (sound).
