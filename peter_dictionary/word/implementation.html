implementation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmpləˌmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of putting a plan, decision, or policy into effect.

Examples

Implementation of Georgia's automobile title law was also recommended by the outgoing jury.
It urged that the next Legislature "provide enabling funds and re-set the effective date so that an orderly implementation of the law may be effected ".

Synset Examples

the agency was created for the implementation of the policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the carrying out or putting into effect of a plan, order, or course of action

Etymology

From Medieval Latin implementatio (the act of filling up, completion), from Latin implementum (filling up, employment, fulfilment).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪmpləˌmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of putting a plan, decision, or agreement into effect; the act of carrying something out.

Examples

Implementation of the latter requires storage of information needed only for synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the act of carrying out or putting into effect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'implementatio', from 'implementum' (action of filling up, means of fulfilling).
