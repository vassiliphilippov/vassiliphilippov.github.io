impractical

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
Not sensible or workable in practice; unable or unwilling to deal with practical matters effectively.

Examples

This shelter could be built in regions where water or rock is close to the surface, making it impractical to build an underground shelter.
"Sometimes we 'd have trouble persuading her to make tax-exempt charitable contributions, and I 've known her to quarrel with a plumber over a bill for fixing a faucet; the next moment she 'd put another half million into the scholarship fund or thirty thousand into something as impractical as this unfortunate Johnston affair.

Synset Examples

refloating the ship proved impractical because of the expense
he is intelligent but too impractical for commercial work
an impractical solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - sensible and realistic; likely to succeed in practice

Etymology

From im- (not) + practical; 'practical' from Late Latin practicus (practical), from Greek praktikós.
