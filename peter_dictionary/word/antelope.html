antelope

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.t̬əˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.ləʊp / (BrE)
A fast-running, graceful hoofed mammal found in Africa and Asia, typically with long legs and horns that curve backward; includes species such as gazelles, impalas, and springboks.

Examples

"Say, did you know they 're fixing to have a two day antelope season on the Double X "?
He was talking about antelope again when they woke up.
"Listen, I never had a chance to kill an antelope.
She was going to tell Bobby Joe about how mistaken she had been, but he brought one of the cousins home for supper, and all they did was talk about antelope.
"Cook a whole antelope "? she exclaimed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazelle - a small, slender antelope known for speed and grace

Etymology

From Italian 'antilope', from Latin 'ant(h)alopus', from Greek 'anthólops', a mythical animal.
