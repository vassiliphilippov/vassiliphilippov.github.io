certiorari

1. NOUN

/ sɜːrʃiəˈrɛəri / (AmE), / sɜːtɪ.əˈrɑːraɪ / (BrE)
a writ issued by a higher court ordering a lower court to deliver the record of a case for review

Examples

We granted certiorari in view of the importance of the questions in the administration of the Act.

Etymology

From Latin 'certiorari', meaning 'to be made more certain'.
