bullyboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊliˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlibɔɪ / (BrE)
A violent or intimidating man, especially one acting as an enforcer or agent for a political faction or organization.

Examples

Until 1924 O' Banion pistoleers and knuckle-duster bullyboys had kept his North Side domain solidly Democratic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal
  • Synonym: ruffian - a violent, lawless person; a bully
  • Synonym: enforcer - a person who imposes obedience or discipline, often violently

Etymology

From 'bully' (a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate) + 'boy'; used since the late 19th century.
