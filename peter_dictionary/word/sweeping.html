sweeping

1. ADJ

/ ˈswipɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
Extending or moving over a wide area; covering a broad or comprehensive range.

Examples

Sweeping a look around, he saw that he was safe for the moment.

Synset Examples

a sweeping glance
a wide-sweeping view of the river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad - covering a large area; wide in extent
  • Antonym: narrow - limited in width or extent; not wide

Etymology

From the verb 'sweep' + -ing, in sense of moving widely or broadly.
