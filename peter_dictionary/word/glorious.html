glorious

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɔriəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːriəs / (BrE)
Deserving or conferring great fame, admiration, or honor; bringing or characterized by glory.

Examples

Richard Tucker sang Edgardo in glorious voice.
In his great and glorious wisdom, he knew that our enemies the Israelites must not be able to read therein what we planned ".
"I had no idea that you, too, would be on this glorious voyage.
One of my virtues or vices is a sort of three-dimensional imagination complete with sound effects and glorious living color.

Synset Examples

a long and glorious career
our glorious literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificent - grand or impressive in appearance or quality
  • Synonym: splendid - impressive in beauty, excellence, or magnificence
  • Antonym: inglorious - causing shame or a lack of honor; not glorious

Etymology

From Latin 'gloriosus' (full of glory), from 'gloria' (glory).
