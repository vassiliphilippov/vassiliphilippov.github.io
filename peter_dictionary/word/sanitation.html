sanitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of making something sanitary by removing or neutralizing germs, especially through sterilization or cleaning.

Examples

- In addition to its recreation interests, water is needed for drinking, sanitation, and irrigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterilization - the process of making something free from bacteria or other living microorganisms
  • Synonym: disinfection - the process of cleaning something to destroy bacteria
  • Antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances or germs

Etymology

From French *sanitation* or Medieval Latin *sanitatio* (health), from Latin *sanitas* (health).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsænɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
Measures or conditions relating to the maintenance of cleanliness and public health, especially regarding the disposal of sewage and waste.

Examples

Sanitation in the confines of the family shelter will require much thought and planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hygiene - conditions or practices conducive to maintaining health and preventing disease

Etymology

From Latin 'sanitas' (health).
