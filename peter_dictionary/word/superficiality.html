superficiality

1. NOUN

/ suːˌpɪfərʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌsuːpɪˌfɪʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
The quality of lacking depth in knowledge, thought, or feeling; concern only with surface or obvious features.

Examples

His orthographic recommendations are no simplistic acceptance of phonemics on the one hand or of superficiality on the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallowness - lack of depth of emotion or understanding

Etymology

From Late Latin superficialitas (appearance of being only on the surface), from superficial.
