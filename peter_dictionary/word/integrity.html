integrity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (BrE)
The state of being whole and undivided; the condition of being complete or unimpaired.

Examples

When the smaller facet-planes of Analytical Cubism were placed upon or juxtaposed with the large, dense shapes formed by the affixed materials of the collage, they had to coalesce - become "synthesized "- into larger planar shapes themselves simply in order to maintain the integrity of the picture plane.
Self's integrity was and is and ever had been.
Proprietorships can establish a unity and integrity of control; corporations, being more amorphous, cannot.

Synset Examples

the integrity of the nervous system is required for normal development
he took measures to insure the territorial unity of Croatia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking or being broken into parts; loss of wholeness

Etymology

From Latin integritas (wholeness, completeness), from integer (whole, untouched).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛɡrəti / (BrE)
the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles; moral uprightness

Examples

They visited the shipyards at Brest and Pierre had to sign the register, vouching for the integrity of the visiting foreigner.

Synset Examples

he expects to find in us the common honesty and integrity of men of business
they admired his scrupulous professional integrity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being honest
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonesty or moral decay

Etymology

From Latin integritas (wholeness, uprightness), from integer (whole, complete)
