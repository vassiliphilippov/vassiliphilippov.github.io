Ribes

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪbiːz / (AmE), / ˈriːbiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs that includes currants and gooseberries, native to the northern hemisphere.

Examples

The blooms of Ribes and of the willow and sloe are the places where large numbers of our early insects will assemble: honeybees, bumblebees, and other wild bees, and also various kinds of flies.

Etymology

From Latin *ribes*, referring to currants, ultimately via Arabic *ribās* (rhubarb).
