l

1. NOUN

/ ɛl / (AmE), / ɛl / (BrE)
The symbol for liter, a metric unit of capacity equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters or approximately 1.75 pints.

Examples

Research at Fayette, Missouri on oxidation ponds has shown that the BOD in the treated effluent varied from 30 to 53 mg / l with loadings from 8 to 120 lb BOD / day / acre.
The BOD of the influent to the pilot plant varied between 110 and 710 mg / l with an average of 350 mg / l.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liter - metric unit of capacity, equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
  • Degree Lower: ml - symbol for milliliter, one thousandth of a liter
  • Degree Higher: kiloliter - metric unit of capacity, 1,000 liters

Etymology

From French *litre*, adopted as the symbol for the liter in the metric system.
