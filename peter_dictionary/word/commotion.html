commotion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / kəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
a state of noisy confusion and disorder; a sudden outburst of activity or tumult

Examples

The commotion had brought her into the wings.
As Andy reached the finale of his act, a subdued commotion backstage drew his attention to the wings.
Cause of all this commotion: squat, pug-nosed, balding, hopelessly ugly Jean-Pierre Bravado, a Bogartian figure, who plays a sadistic, amoral, philosophic Tasti-Freeze salesman in old New-Waver Fredrico de Mille Rossilini's endlessly provocative film, A Sour Sponge.

Synset Examples

they were amazed by the furious disturbance they had caused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumult - a loud, confused noise, especially one caused by a large mass of people
  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Antonym: tranquility - the quality or state of being calm and peaceful

Etymology

From Latin commotionem, from com- (together) + motio (motion, movement).
