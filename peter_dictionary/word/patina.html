patina

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə / (BrE)
a thin layer that forms on the surface of metals like copper and bronze through oxidation, often giving a green or brown appearance, and sometimes valued for its aesthetic or protective qualities

Examples

The stained-glass windows may have developed unpremeditated patinas, the paneling may be no more durable than the planks in a political platform.

Etymology

From Latin patina (shallow dish, pan), referring to the appearance of old bronze vessels.
