moreover

1. ADV

/ mɔːrˈoʊvər / (AmE), / mɔːˈrəʊvə / (BrE)
Used to introduce additional information or a further point, especially in formal writing.

Examples

It is being fought, moreover, in fairly close correspondence with the predictions of the soothsayers of the think factories.
He has, moreover, another qualification which augurs well for the future.
Moreover, for those few there was almost no ecclesiastical representation in the city to care for their religious needs.
Moreover, the piece is written in five movements, rather than the conventional four of most quintets, and this gives the opus a serenade or divertimento flavor.
It is, moreover, a perfectly integrated ensemble effort.

Synset Examples

computer chess games are getting cheaper all the time; furthermore, their quality is improving
the cellar was dark; moreover, mice nested there
what is more, there's no sign of a change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furthermore - used to add more information; in addition
  • Synonym: besides - in addition; as well
  • Antonym: however - used to introduce a contrast or exception

Etymology

From Middle English, from more + over.
