nasal

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəl / (BrE)
relating to the nose; situated in or affecting the nose.

Examples

The intra nasal instillation of a fluid suspension of infectious agent in an anesthetized animal is far different from exposure, through natural respiration, to aerosolized organisms.

Synset Examples

nasal passages

Etymology

From Latin 'nasalis', from 'nasus' meaning 'nose'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈneɪzl̩ / (AmE), / ˈneɪzl̩ / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) having a quality as if produced with the nose blocked; resonant or twangy, as if the nose were pinched.

Examples

"I called the station at three this morning ", Killpath's nasal voice pronounced.

Synset Examples

a whining nasal voice

Etymology

From Latin nasalis, from nasus (nose).
