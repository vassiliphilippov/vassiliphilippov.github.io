czar

1. NOUN

/ zɑr / (AmE), / zɑː / (BrE)
A male monarch or emperor of Russia, especially before the 1917 revolution.

Examples

I bet Russian czars never paid that for a single horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsar - Alternative spelling for Russian emperor
  • Synonym: emperor - Supreme male monarch of an empire
  • Synonym: king - Male sovereign, especially of a nation or territory

Etymology

From Russian 'tsar', from Latin 'Caesar' (emperor).
