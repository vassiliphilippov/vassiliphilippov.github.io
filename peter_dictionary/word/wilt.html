wilt

1. VERB

/ wɪlt / (AmE), / wɪlt / (BrE)
(of a person or plant) to become weak, tired, or less confident; to lose strength or freshness.

Examples

But its relentless discursiveness and determined complexity are so overwhelming that after an hour and a half a listener's stamina begins to wilt.

Synset Examples

My opponent was wilting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wither - (of a plant) become dry and shriveled
  • Antonym: flourish - grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way

Etymology

Variant of 'welten', Middle English, from Old English 'wyltan', meaning to fade or wither.
