comet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmət / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪt / (BrE)
A small celestial body of ice and dust that orbits the sun, typically developing a bright tail of gas and dust when near the sun.

Examples

We are not now afraid of atomic bombs in the same way that people once feared comets.

Etymology

From Latin 'comēta', from Greek 'komētēs' (long-haired), referring to the comet's tail.
