faded

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a color, sound, or quality) having become weaker or less distinct; reduced in strength or intensity.

Examples

The twenty-five year old recording offers rather faded string tone, but the balance between the instruments is good and the transfer is very quiet.
The already faded pastel charms of the naive music itself vanish entirely in Fistoulari's melodramatic contrasts between ultra vehement brute power and chilly, if suave, sentimentality.

Synset Examples

the faded tones of an old recording

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attenuated - weakened; reduced in force, effect, or value
  • Antonym: intense - having strong effect or strength; not faded

Etymology

Past participle of 'fade', from Old French 'fader'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪdəd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdɪd / (BrE)
Having lost freshness, brightness, or brilliance of color, often as a result of exposure to sunlight or frequent use.

Examples

"They were all faded.

Synset Examples

sun-bleached deck chairs
faded jeans
a very pale washed-out blue
washy colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleached - whitened or lightened by exposure or chemicals
  • Synonym: washed-out - very faded in color or intensity
  • Antonym: bright - having intense color or light

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'fade', from Old French 'fader' (to fade, vanish), from Vulgar Latin *fatidare.
