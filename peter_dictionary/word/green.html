green

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin / (AmE), / ɡriːn / (BrE)
an area of open grassy land, especially one in or near a town, often used for recreation or community events.

Examples

Arnold's iron shot from the tee burrowed into the bunker guarding the green, an embankment that had become soft and spongy from the rains, thereby bringing local rules into force.

Synset Examples

they went for a walk in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: park - an area of public land for recreation

Etymology

From Old English *grēne*, originally referencing color, extended to grassy areas.

2. NOUN

/ ɡrin / (AmE), / ɡriːn / (BrE)
A color between blue and yellow in the spectrum, resembling that of fresh grass.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blue - color between green and violet in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: yellow - color between green and orange in the spectrum

Etymology

Old English grēne, of Germanic origin, related to grow.
