framework

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪmˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈfreɪmwɜːk / (BrE)
A set of ideas, rules, or beliefs from which something is developed, or which provides the underlying structure for thinking about or dealing with something.

Examples

There is no framework or structure of thought with respect to which we can organize it and no part of reality, as we know and apprehend it, with respect to which we can refer this experience.
Only within the framework of a mature relationship characterized by honest appraisals of performance can we provide telling assistance.
Within this framework, what followed was strained, even macabre.

Synset Examples

the computer program was based on a model of the circulatory and respiratory systems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement or organization of parts to form an entity
  • Synonym: system - a set of connected things or parts forming a complex whole

Etymology

From frame + work, likely modeled after analogies with physical support structures.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪmˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈfreɪmwɜːk / (BrE)
A basic structure underlying a system, concept, or text, providing support and shape.

Examples

We will recall that the still confident liberals of the Truman administration gathered with other Western utopians in San Francisco to set up the legal framework, finally and at last, to rationalize war - to rationalize want and fear - out of the world: the United Nations.

Synset Examples

providing a factual framework for future research
it is part of the fabric of society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement of and relations between the parts of something complex

Etymology

From frame + work, first used in the late Middle English period.
