forbidden

1. ADJ

/ fərˈbɪdən / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdn / (BrE)
Not allowed by law, rule, or custom; excluded from use or mention.

Examples

Substitute approved objects for forbidden ones and keep telling him how he is to act.
We were forbidden to swing on the gates, lest they sag on their hinges in a poor-white-trash way, but we could stand on them, when they were latched, rest our chins on the top, and stare and stare, committing to memory, quite unintentionally, all the details that lay before our eyes.
For Hammer, nothing is forbidden.
In the calm which follows the reading of a poem, for example, is the effect produced by the enforced quiet, by the musical quality of words and rhythm, by the sentiments or sense of the poem, by the associations with earlier readings, if it is familiar, by the boost to the self-esteem for the semi-literate, by the diversion of attention, by the sense of security in a legitimized withdrawal, by a kind license for some variety of fantasy life regarded as forbidden, or by half conscious ideas about the magical power of words?
It's not like gambling or liquor or even women, which is something that most people want, but is forbidden by the Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibited - not allowed, especially by law
  • Antonym: permitted - allowed, especially by law or rule
  • Antonym: allowed - permitted, not forbidden

Etymology

From Old English *forboden*, past participle of *forbēodan* (to forbid).
