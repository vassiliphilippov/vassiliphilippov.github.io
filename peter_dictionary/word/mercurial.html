mercurial

1. ADJ

/ mərˈkjʊriəl / (AmE), / məˈkjʊəriəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) subject to sudden or unpredictable changes of mood or mind; lively and unpredictable.

Examples

The Serge Prokofieff whom we knew in the United States of America was gay, witty, mercurial, full of pranks and bonheur - and very capable as a professional musician.

Synset Examples

erratic behavior
fickle weather
mercurial twists of temperament
a quicksilver character, cool and willful at one moment, utterly fragile the next

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erratic - liable to unpredictable change
  • Synonym: fickle - constantly changing in feelings, loyalties, or affections

Etymology

From Latin 'Mercurialis', relating to Mercury (the Roman god), traditionally associated with eloquence and changeability.
