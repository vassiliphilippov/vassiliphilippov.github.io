chute

1. NOUN

/ tʃut / (AmE), / ʃuːt / (BrE)
A sloping channel or passage down which people or things can slide or be dropped.

Examples

Men were slaughtered every day, tumbled into eternity like so many torn parcels flung down a portable chute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - a smooth sloping surface for sliding
  • Degree Higher: conveyor - a mechanical apparatus for moving articles

Etymology

From French 'chute' meaning 'fall', from Old French 'cheoir' (to fall), from Latin 'cadere'.

2. NOUN

/ tʃuːt / (AmE), / tʃuːt / (BrE)
an informal term for a parachute; a device that fills with air and slows the descent of a person or object through the air

Examples

Donovan snatched Greg's chute from him with a belligerent motion and almost ran to the plane with it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachute - a device filling with air to slow a fall

Etymology

Shortened from 'parachute', from French 'parachute' (para- as protection + chute fall).
