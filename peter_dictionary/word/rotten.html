rotten

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑːtən / (AmE), / ˈrɒtən / (BrE)
Very bad; extremely unpleasant or unsatisfactory.

Examples

"That was a rotten thing to do ".
I feel rotten about telling you this...

Synset Examples

a lousy play
it's a stinking world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awful - very bad; unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad; unpleasant
  • Antonym: wonderful - extremely good; excellent

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɑtən / (AmE), / ˈrɒtən / (BrE)
Decayed or decomposed, usually with foulness or an offensive smell; no longer fresh or good to use.

Examples

It would be a shame, Mr. Chairman, if we allowed a few rotten apples to give a bad name to the whole barrel.

Synset Examples

dead and rotten in his grave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putrid - decayed with a foul smell
  • Antonym: fresh - not decayed or spoiled

Etymology

From Old English 'rotian' (to rot or decay).
