helplessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlpləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlplɪsnəs / (BrE)
The state of being unable to take action or protect oneself; powerlessness.

Examples

To act otherwise would be to admit his helplessness.

Synset Examples

in spite of their weakness the group remains active

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - the condition of having no power or control
  • Antonym: empowerment - the process of becoming stronger or more confident, especially in controlling one's life

Etymology

From 'helpless' + '-ness'. 'Helpless' from Middle English, from Old English 'help' + '-less'.
