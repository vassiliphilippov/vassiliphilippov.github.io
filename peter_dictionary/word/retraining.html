retraining

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of being taught new skills, especially to prepare for a different job or occupation.

Examples

Unemployed older workers who have no expectation of securing employment in the occupation in which they are skilled should be able to secure counseling and retraining in an occupation with a future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: training - the process of learning the skills you need for a particular job or activity

Etymology

From 'retrain' (to train again) + '-ing'.
