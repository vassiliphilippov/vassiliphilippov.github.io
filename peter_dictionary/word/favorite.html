favorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
A person or thing that is especially liked or preferred over others.

Examples

Mongi Slim of Tunisia and Frederick Boland of Ireland were early favorites in the running, but France did n't like the former and the Soviet Union would have none of the latter.
Sometimes it takes several "eatings "of avocado to catch that delightful quality in taste that has made it such a favorite throughout the world.
One of my favorites is A. armata, a species very common in England, where it is sometimes referred to as the lawn bee.
One of his favorites: "Guitar Boogie ".
William Gilmore Simms, sturdy realist that he was, pleaded for a natural robustness such as he found in his favorites the great Elizabethans, to vivify the pale writings being produced around him.

Synset Examples

that book is one of my favorites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another or others
  • Antonym: least favorite - the person or thing liked the least among a group

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *favoritus* (favored), from Latin *favorem* (favor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
A person who is especially loved, trusted, or preferred above others.

Examples

His easy good looks made him a favorite with the ladies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person who is dearly loved

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *favorit*, past participle of *favorir* (to favor).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
Regarded with special liking or preference; especially appealing to the general public.

Examples

The newspaper too is the favorite habitat of the anatomical.

Synset Examples

a favorite tourist attraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popular - liked or admired by many people or by a particular person or group

Etymology

From Middle English, via Old French 'favorit', from Italian 'favorito', past participle of 'favorire' (to favor).

4. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
Preferred above all others; most liked or favored.

Examples

One would need to test this proposition carefully; after all, the large (and probably unreliable) Reader's Digest literature on the "most unforgettable character I ever met "deals with village grocers, country doctors, favorite if illiterate aunts, and so forth.

Synset Examples

the favored child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preferred - regarded as more desirable than others
  • Synonym: best-loved - loved more than others
  • Antonym: disliked - not liked; regarded with aversion

Etymology

From Middle English 'favourite', from Old French, from Latin 'favorem' (favor).
