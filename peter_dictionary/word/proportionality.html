proportionality

1. NOUN

/ prəˌpɔːrʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / prəˌpɔːʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or condition in which two quantities have a constant ratio to each other.

Examples

Direct proportionality of the rate to the incident intensity has also been assumed in obtaining the value in the last column for the fourth sample of series /2, where the light intensity was reduced by use of a screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportion - a part, share, or amount considered in comparative relation to a whole
  • Antonym: inversely proportional - a relationship where one value increases as the other decreases, resulting in a constant product

Etymology

From 'proportional' + '-ity', formed from Latin 'proportionalis' (having due proportion).
