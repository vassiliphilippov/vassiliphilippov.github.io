orgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɔːdʒi / (BrE)
an occasion or activity involving excessive or unrestrained indulgence in something, often used figuratively

Examples

The characteristic thing about this church was its Amen corner and the weekly religious orgy.

Synset Examples

an orgy of shopping
an emotional binge
a splurge of spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binge - a period of excessive indulgence in an activity
  • Synonym: splurge - an act of spending money freely or extravagantly
  • Antonym: restraint - moderation or self-control, especially in indulgence

Etymology

From Greek orgia (secret rites).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɔːdʒi / (BrE)
(in ancient Greece or Rome) a secret rite involving singing, dancing, drinking, and sexual activity, often as part of religious worship.

Examples

And so they went, he choosing of all places an inn near Medmenham Abbey, scene a generation ago of the obscene orgies of the Hellfire Club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchanalia - ancient Roman festival of Bacchus involving revelry

Etymology

From Greek ‘orgia’ (secret rites), plural of ‘orgion’.
