excel

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsɛl / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛl / (BrE)
to be exceptionally good at an activity or subject; to do something very well or to surpass others

Examples

I wonder if anyone ever bothered to make the point that when it comes to boats and their motors, Americans excel over any country in the world in the long run.
Where the pool excels is in its compilation of maintenance and cost-data studies and analyses.

Synset Examples

She excelled in math

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surpass - to be greater than or go beyond in achievement or quality
  • Synonym: outperform - to perform better than others
  • Antonym: fail - to not succeed in achieving something

Etymology

From Latin 'excellere' (to rise, be eminent), from 'ex-' (out, above) + 'cellere' (to rise, project).
