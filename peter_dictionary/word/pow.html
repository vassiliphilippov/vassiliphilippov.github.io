POW

1. NOUN

/ pi oʊ ˈdʌblju / (AmE), / piː əʊ ˈdʌbljuː / (BrE)
A person, especially a member of the armed forces, who is captured and held by the enemy during war.

Examples

He had never heard the word reactionary before his life as a POW began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner of war - Person captured and held by an enemy during war

Etymology

Acronym of 'prisoner of war'.
