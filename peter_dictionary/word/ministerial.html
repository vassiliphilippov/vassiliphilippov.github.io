ministerial

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪnɪˈstɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌmɪnɪˈstɪəriəl / (BrE)
Relating to a minister of religion or the duties of a minister.

Examples

He again stated that he was "a regular minister "; that he was "devoting an average of 100 hours a month to actual preaching publicly ", in addition to 50 to 75 hours in other ministerial duties, and that he opposed war in any form.

Synset Examples

ministerial duties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerical - relating to the clergy or a cleric

Etymology

From Latin ministerialis, from minister (servant, attendant).
