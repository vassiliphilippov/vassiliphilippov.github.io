exodus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksədəs / (AmE), / ˈɛksədəs / (BrE)
A situation in which a large number of people leave a place at the same time, especially to escape a hostile environment.

Examples

Central city areas have become progressively poorer locales for the major denominations since the exodus of middle-class people from most central cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: migration - the movement from one region or place of habitat to another

Etymology

From Greek 'Exodos' (exit, way out), from 'ex-' (out) and 'hodos' (way).
