pursue

1. VERB

/ pərˈsu / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː / (BrE)
to go after or search for something or someone, especially in order to catch, achieve, or attain it

Examples

John Adams took to heart the advice given him by his legal mentor, Jeremiah Gridley, to "pursue the study of the law, rather than the gain of it ".

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seek - to try to find or obtain something
  • Synonym: hunt - to chase or search for something in order to catch or obtain it
  • Antonym: give up - to stop pursuing or stop trying to obtain something

Etymology

From Old French 'poursuivre', from Latin 'prosequi' (to follow, pursue).

2. VERB

/ pərˈsu / (AmE), / pəˈsjuː / (BrE)
to follow someone or something in order to catch or overtake them, especially in a chase

Examples

They were pursuing him.
Beaten, bruised and exhausted, he pursues the elusive killer through the demi-monde of high society and low morals, always alone, always despised.

  • Antonym: flee - to run away from someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *poursuivre*, from Latin *prosequi* (to follow, prosecute).
