Streptococcus anhemolyticus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptəˈkɑkəs ˌænˌhiːməˈlɪtɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈkɒkəs ˌanˌhiːməˈlɪtɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of Streptococcus bacteria that does not cause hemolysis (the breaking down of red blood cells) in blood agar cultures.

Examples

Cultures taken from the jejunum yielded Monilia albicans, Pseudomonas pyocanea, Aerobacter aerogenes, and Streptococcus anhemolyticus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Streptococcus - genus of spherical, chain-forming bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin: Greek streptos (twisted) + kokkos (berry) referring to chain-shaped bacteria; an- (not) + hemolyticus (related to breaking down red blood cells).
