mindful

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪnd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd.f(ə)l / (BrE)
consciously aware of something; keeping something in mind or attentive to it

Examples

Therefore, the scholar, as he looks at our national folklore of the last 60 years, will be mindful of two facts.
Properly mindful of all the cultures in existence today throughout the world, we must employ these resources without war or violent revolution.
The change of emphasis from conventional type to missile type warfare must be made with care, mindful that the one type of warfare cannot be safely neglected in favor of the other.

Synset Examples

ever mindful of her health
mindful of his responsibilities
mindful of these criticisms, I shall attempt to justify my action

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heedless - not paying attention; careless; antonym of 'mindful'
  • Synonym: attentive - paying close attention to something; synonym of 'mindful'

Etymology

From mind + -ful, meaning 'full of mind' or 'bearing in mind'.
