forte-piano

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔːrt.eɪˈpjænoʊ / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.teɪˈpjɑː.nəʊ / (BrE)
An early type of piano, with a lighter sound and construction than the modern piano, used chiefly from the 18th to early 19th centuries.

Examples

There was one of the new forte-pianos in the room and, as Claire rose to go, he asked her to sing him one song before she left.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: piano - a large musical instrument with a keyboard, developed from the forte-piano

Etymology

From Italian 'forte' (loud) + 'piano' (soft), reflecting the instrument's expressive range.
