pirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
a person who wrongfully copies or uses another's work, ideas, or inventions as their own without permission

Examples

The medical device pirate of today, of course, is a far more sophisticated operator than his predecessor of yesteryear - the gallus snapping hawker of snake oil and other patent medicines.
Multiply these figures by the millions of people known to be conned by medical pirates annually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarist - a person who copies another's work or ideas without permission

Etymology

From Latin 'pirata', from Greek 'peiratēs' (sea-attacker, brigand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərət / (BrE)
A person who attacks and robs ships at sea, typically without the authority of any nation.

Examples

We were camping a few weeks ago on Cape Hatteras Campground in that land of pirates, seagulls and bluefish on North Carolina's famed Outer Banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privateer - a person authorized by a government to attack enemy ships during wartime

Etymology

From Latin 'pirata', from Greek 'peiratēs' meaning 'brigand, sea robber'.
