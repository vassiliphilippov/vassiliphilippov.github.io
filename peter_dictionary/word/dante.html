Dante

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / ˈdænteɪ / (BrE)
(1265–1321) An Italian poet best known for the 'Divine Comedy', an epic poem describing a journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Paradise.

Examples

"It's not for making pretty speeches about Dante those actresses get paid so good ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Divine Comedy - an epic poem by Dante describing a journey through the afterlife

Etymology

From Italian Dante (short for Durante) Alighieri.
