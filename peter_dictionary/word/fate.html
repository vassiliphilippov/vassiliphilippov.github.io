fate

1. NOUN

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
A power that is believed to control what happens in the future, often regarded as predetermining events beyond human control.

Examples

The concert opened with another big romantic score, Schumann's Overture to Manfred, which suffered fate, this time with orchestral thrusts to the Byronic point to keep it afloat.
"The great Greek tragedies are concerned with man against Fate, not man against man for the prize of a woman's body.

Synset Examples

we are helpless in the face of destiny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destiny - the predetermined course of events regarded as beyond human power

Etymology

From Latin 'fatum' (that which has been spoken, decree), from 'fari' (to speak).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destiny - the events that will necessarily happen to a particular person or thing in the future
  • Antonym: chance - the occurrence and development of events in the absence of any obvious design

Etymology

From Latin 'fatum' meaning 'that which has been spoken', from 'fari' (to speak).
