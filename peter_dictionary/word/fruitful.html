fruitful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrut.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfruːt.fəl / (BrE)
producing good or useful results; productive or conducive to abundance.

Examples

Because of the simplicity of the molecules, isotopic exchange reactions between elemental halogens and the corresponding carbon tetrahalides would appear to offer particularly fruitful possibilities for obtaining unambiguous basic kinetic data.

Synset Examples

be fruitful and multiply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: productive - producing or able to produce large amounts or results
  • Antonym: barren - not productive or fruitful

Etymology

From Middle English fruitfull, from fruit + -ful.
