spelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or activity of writing or naming the letters of a word in the correct sequence according to accepted conventions.

Examples

The Stanford Achievement Test, Form J, was administered by classroom teachers, consisting of a battery of six sub tests: Paragraph Meaning, Word Meaning, Spelling, Language, Arithmetic Computation, and Arithmetic Reasoning.
A random storage scheme, based on the spelling of forms, provides an economical way to compile this text form list.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthography - the conventional spelling system of a language

Etymology

From the verb 'spell' (to name or write letters in order), which comes from Old French 'espeller', from Frankish 'spellon' (story, report).
