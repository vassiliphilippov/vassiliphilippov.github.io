bankrupt

1. VERB

/ ˈbæŋ.krʌpt / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.krʌpt / (BrE)
to cause a person, business, or organization to become insolvent or unable to pay their debts

Examples

Yet some, like Morris Manderscheid, would bankrupt themselves for the new ideas.

Synset Examples

My daughter's fancy wedding is going to break me!
The slump in the financial markets smashed him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - to destroy or severely damage financially
  • Synonym: impoverish - to make someone poor

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* (broken bench), via French *banqueroute* (bankruptcy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbæŋ.krʌpt / (AmE), / ˈbæŋ.krʌpt / (BrE)
Unable to pay debts; having been financially ruined.

Examples

In 1927 his father's business collapsed, and, rather than go bankrupt, Mercer senior turned his firm over to a bank for liquidation.

Synset Examples

a bankrupt company
the company went belly-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* (broken bench), referring to collapsed moneylenders’ benches.
