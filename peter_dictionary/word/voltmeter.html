voltmeter

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊltˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈvɒltˌmiːtə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring the electrical potential difference between two points in an electric circuit.

Examples

The arc voltage was measured with a voltmeter whose terminals were connected to the anode and cathode holders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammeter - instrument measuring electric current

Etymology

From 'volt', the unit of electric potential (named after Alessandro Volta), and 'meter', from Greek 'metron' (measure).
