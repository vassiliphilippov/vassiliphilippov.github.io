mouthpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (BrE)
A part of an instrument, device, or apparatus that is placed over or into the mouth to allow breathing, speaking, or the passage of substances.

Examples

Usually the patient breathes into a mouthpiece while walking a treadmill, standing still, or in some other medically significant position.

Synset Examples

the mouthpiece of a respirator

Etymology

Formed from 'mouth' + 'piece', meaning the part of an object designed for the mouth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (BrE)
the part of a telephone that you speak into

Examples

He scooped up the receiver and said, "Police chief ", into the mouthpiece, and then, "Oh yes, Mr. Benson.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receiver - the part of a telephone that you listen to

Etymology

From 'mouth' + 'piece', signifying a part used with the mouth.
