constable

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstəbəl / (BrE)
A police officer, usually with less authority or jurisdiction than a sheriff, especially in the United Kingdom and some Commonwealth countries.

Examples

So much for the tiresome facts, as familiar to you, I'm sure, as to the constables and state troopers who followed in your wake.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sheriff - a law officer with more authority and jurisdiction than a constable
  • Synonym: police officer - general term for a member of the police force

Etymology

From Old French 'conestable', from Late Latin 'comes stabuli' (officer of the stable).
