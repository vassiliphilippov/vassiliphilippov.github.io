stacked

1. ADJ

/ stækt / (AmE), / stækt / (BrE)
arranged in a neat pile or stack, with one item placed on top of another

Examples

Supplies of sheet metal were neatly stacked in bins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piled - arranged in a pile; accumulated in a heap

Etymology

Past participle of 'stack', from Middle English 'stak', from Old Norse 'stakkr' (haystack).
