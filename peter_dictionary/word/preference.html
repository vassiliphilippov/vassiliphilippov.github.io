preference

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛfərəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛfərəns / (BrE)
A greater liking for one alternative over another or others; a tendency to choose or favor one thing.

Examples

Only a radical change in the nature of the population in the central city would be likely to destroy this preference - and we must now turn our attention to the question of whether such a change, gloomily foreseen by so many urban diagnosticians, is actually upon us.
His aptitudes and preferences could be given more weight in selecting the proper training.
The Sisters have taken quite a liking to you.

Synset Examples

my own preference is for good literature
the Irish have a penchant for blarney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penchant - a strong or habitual liking for something or tendency to do something
  • Synonym: liking - a feeling of regard or affection for something; fondness
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praeferentia' (preference), from Latin 'praeferre' (to carry in front, prefer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛfərəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛfərəns / (BrE)
A greater liking or favorable attitude toward one thing or option over others.

Examples

The selection of materials depends on skills of available labor for installation, cost of materials available locally, and your own preference.
Counsel for du Pont indicated a preference for the submission of detailed plans by both sides at an early date.

Synset Examples

a predilection for expensive cars
his sexual preferences
showed a Marxist orientation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predilection - a strong liking or preference for something
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin 'praeferre' (to prefer), via French 'préférence'.
