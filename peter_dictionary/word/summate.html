summate

1. VERB

/ səˈmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmeɪt / (BrE)
to add together; to find the total of several numbers or items

Examples

Religion can summate, epitomize, relate, and conserve all the highest ideals and values - ethical, aesthetic, and religious - of man formed in his culture.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum - to add together; calculate the total of numbers or items
  • Synonym: add - to combine numbers or amounts to get a total

Etymology

From Latin 'summatus', past participle of 'summare', from 'summa' (sum, total).
