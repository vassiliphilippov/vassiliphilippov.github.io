inspector

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈspɛktər / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɛktə / (BrE)
a high-ranking officer in the police, usually responsible for supervising other officers or investigating crimes

Examples

The date, October 8, 1957, immediately caught the inspector's eye.
The inspector nodded, doubting this.
The inspector knew as he left that this was wishful thinking.
The inspector declined.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sergeant - a police officer ranking below inspector
  • Degree Higher: superintendent - a police officer ranking above inspector

Etymology

From Latin 'inspector', meaning 'an overseer'.
