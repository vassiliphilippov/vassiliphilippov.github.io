snap

1. VERB

/ snæp / (AmE), / snæp / (BrE)
to break or cause to break suddenly and completely, typically making a sharp cracking sound

Examples

She snapped five tenths of a second off the mark set by Helen Shipley, of Wellsley College, in the National A.A.U. meet in Columbus, Ohio.
The imposition of yet another pressure, a strong one, from the outside, might cause it to snap.
Oso reached up, jerked the buck from the bale and snapped his neck.

Synset Examples

The rope snapped
tear the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain
  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or in pieces by force

Etymology

Probably imitative of a sharp cracking sound, first recorded in the early 15th century.
