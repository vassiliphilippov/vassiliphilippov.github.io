Stalin

1. PROPN

/ stəˈlɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɑːlɪn / (BrE)
Joseph Stalin (1879–1953), Soviet leader who succeeded Lenin and ruled the Soviet Union with a totalitarian regime, notably purging political opposition.

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.

Etymology

Adopted name from Russian 'Stalin', meaning 'man of steel', from 'stal' (steel).
