repressed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈprɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛst / (BrE)
(of emotions, desires, or impulses) kept under control, not expressed or released; suppressed, often due to social or psychological factors.

Examples

The effect, needless to say, is almost terrifying, and though at times a bit obscure, the film is certainly a much needed catharsis for the "repressed "movie-goer.

Synset Examples

her severe upbringing had left her inhibited
a very inhibited young man, anxious and ill at ease
their reactions were partly the product of pent-up emotions
repressed rage turned his face scarlet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressed - (of emotions or desires) forcibly put an end to
  • Synonym: inhibited - (of a person or their behavior) unable to relax or express feelings naturally
  • Antonym: expressed - conveyed or made known, not held back

Etymology

From Latin repressus, past participle of reprimere (to press back, restrain).
