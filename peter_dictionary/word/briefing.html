briefing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbri.fɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbriː.fɪŋ / (BrE)
A meeting or session at which detailed instructions or information are given, especially before undertaking a task or operation.

Examples

"Our objective ", the colonel had said that day of the briefing, "is Papa-san ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: debriefing - a meeting for questioning someone after a mission or event to discuss what happened

Etymology

From 'brief' (noun) + -ing; influenced by military usage since the 20th century.
