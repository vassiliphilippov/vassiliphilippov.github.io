dissection

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛkʃən, daɪˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkʃən, daɪˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of carefully cutting something, especially a dead body or plant, in order to study its internal parts

Examples

It is like medical schools in India where, in that fairy land of religious inhibition, the dissection of dead bodies is frowned upon.
Instead they learn their dissection on the bulbs of plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopsy - examination of a dead body to determine cause of death
  • Synonym: vivisection - cutting or operation on a live animal for scientific investigation

Etymology

From Latin dissectio (a cutting apart), from dissecare (to cut apart).
