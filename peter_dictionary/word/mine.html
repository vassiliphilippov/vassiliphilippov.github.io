mine

1. NOUN

/ maɪn / (AmE), / maɪn / (BrE)
an explosive device designed to detonate on contact or by remote control, used to destroy vehicles, ships, or to harm personnel

Examples

Between the ponderous hulk and himself, in the valley over which Papa-san reigned, men had hidden high explosives, booby traps, and mines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land mine - an explosive mine buried just under the surface of the ground
  • Synonym: sea mine - an explosive device placed in water to damage or sink ships or submarines
  • Synonym: booby trap - an explosive or other device designed to harm someone unknowingly triggering it

Etymology

From Middle English, possibly from Old French 'mine' (underground passage), later extended to mean an explosive device placed underground.

2. NOUN
