propylthiouracil

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌpɪlˌθaɪoʊˈjʊrəsɪl / (AmE), / prəʊˌpɪlˌθaɪəʊˈjʊərəsɪl / (BrE)
A crystalline compound used as an antithyroid drug for the treatment of conditions such as goiter and hyperthyroidism.

Examples

In spite of normal thyroid function tests, a trial of propylthiouracil, 400 mg. daily for one week, was given but served only to intensify muscle weakness.

Etymology

From 'propyl' (chemical group) + 'thio-' (containing sulfur) + 'uracil' (a nitrogenous base), indicating its chemical structure.
