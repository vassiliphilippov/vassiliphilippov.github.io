vibrate

1. VERB

/ ˈvaɪˌbɹeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.breɪt / (BrE)
to move continuously and rapidly backwards and forwards or from side to side

Examples

The room vibrated as if a giant hand had rocked it.

Synset Examples

the needle on the meter was oscillating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillate - to move or swing back and forth at a regular speed
  • Synonym: quiver - to shake with a slight, rapid motion

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrare' (to move quickly to and fro, shake).
