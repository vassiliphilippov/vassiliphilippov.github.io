contraband

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːntrəˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrəˌbænd / (BrE)
distributed, sold, or imported illegally; prohibited by law from being traded or possessed.

Examples

Shipments of arms from Western countries could similarly be seized as contraband.

Synset Examples

the black economy pays no taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law; not permitted
  • Antonym: lawful - permitted by law

Etymology

From Spanish 'contrabanda', from 'contra' (against) + 'bando' (proclamation, ban).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːntrəˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrəbænd / (BrE)
Goods that are imported, exported, or possessed illegally, especially because their trade is forbidden by law.

Examples

Some contraband here, but nothing to get in a twist over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggled goods - goods illegally imported or exported
  • Antonym: legal goods - goods that are lawfully imported, exported, or possessed

Etymology

From Italian 'contrabbando', from Latin 'contra' (against) + 'bannum' (proclamation, ban).
