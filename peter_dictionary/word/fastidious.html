fastidious

1. ADJ

/ fæˈstɪdiəs / (AmE), / fæˈstɪdiəs / (BrE)
Very attentive to and concerned about accuracy, detail, or cleanliness; hard to please due to demanding standards.

Examples

Moreover, it should not be so fastidious in its growth requirements as to make production on a militarily significant scale improbable.
Garth was prepared to be helpful in what he referred to with fastidious distaste as this unfortunate Johnston affair, which would not, he said more than once, have ever come about if Mrs. Meeker had only seen fit to consult Mr. Hohlbein or him about it.

Synset Examples

a fastidious and incisive intellect
fastidious about personal cleanliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Synonym: scrupulous - diligent, thorough, and extremely attentive to details
  • Antonym: sloppy - careless and lacking attention to detail

Etymology

From Latin fastidiosus (disdainful), from fastidium (loathing).
