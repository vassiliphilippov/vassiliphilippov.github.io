reclassification

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌklɑːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of classifying something again, usually by assigning it to a different category

Examples

Upon administrative approval of the reclassification, he was ordered to report for induction on June 11, 1956, but failed to do so.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging into classes or categories

Etymology

From re- (again) + classification.
