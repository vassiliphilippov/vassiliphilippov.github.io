contusion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtuːʒən / (AmE), / kənˈtjuːʒən / (BrE)
A region of injured tissue or skin in which blood capillaries have been ruptured, typically resulting in a bruise but without breaking the skin.

Examples

Recently many doctors have installed ultrasonic vibration machines for deep massage of bruises, contusions, and simple bursitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruise - an injury to body tissue without a break in the skin, causing discoloration

Etymology

From Latin contusio (a bruising), from contundere (to bruise).
