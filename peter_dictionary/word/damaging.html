damaging

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdamɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing harm or injury; having a negative or detrimental effect.

Examples

One of the most damaging tsunami on record followed the famous Lisbon earthquake of November 1, 1755; its waves persisted for a week and were felt as far away as the English coast.

Synset Examples

damaging to career and reputation
the reporter's coverage resulted in prejudicial publicity for the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmful - causing or likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: beneficial - producing good or helpful results

Etymology

From the verb 'damage' + -ing.
