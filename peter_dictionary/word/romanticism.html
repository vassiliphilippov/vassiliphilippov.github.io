romanticism

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to have or display impractical or idealized romantic attitudes and ideals.

Examples

His love of folklore has romanticism in it, and he does n't care much about the dollar sign or the footnote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - the practice of forming or pursuing ideals, often unrealistically
  • Antonym: realism - the attitude of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From French *romantisme*, from *romantique* (romantic).
