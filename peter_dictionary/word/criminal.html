criminal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
A person who has committed a crime or has been legally convicted of a crime.

Examples

The police were disqualified along with certain categories of naturalized citizens, criminals and those punished for Protectorate activities.
Although admitting Brown's guilt on legal grounds, Day said that, "Brown is no common criminal; his deed was not below, but above the law ".
He catches criminals not merely because he is paid to do so (frequently he does not receive a fee at all), but because he enjoys his work, because he firmly believes that murder must be punished.
At the same time, because the personal code of the detective coincides with the legal dictates of his society, because he likes to catch criminals, he is in middle class eyes a virtuous man.
Another, more interesting explanation, is hinted at by Watson when he observes on several occasions that Holmes would have made a magnificent criminal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: lawbreaker - person who breaks the law
  • Antonym: innocent - person not guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Latin criminalis (relating to crime), from crimen (accusation, crime).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnl̩ / (BrE)
Guilty of crime or a serious offense; involving or relating to crime.

Examples

On Wednesday morning, November 2, 1859, the Providence Daily Journal stated that although Brown justly deserved the extreme penalty, no man, however criminal, ought to suffer the penalty without a fairer trial.

Synset Examples

criminal in the sight of God and man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense

Etymology

From Latin 'criminalis' (pertaining to crime), from 'crimen' (accusation, crime).

3. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəl / (BrE)
Relating to crime or unlawful acts; involving or characteristic of crime.

Examples

We have here, finally, a witness that will further testify to Michael Corleone'’s rule over a criminal empire that controls all gambling in this country and perhaps in other countries.

Synset Examples

a criminal offense
criminal abuse
felonious intent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: felonious - involving or relating to serious crime (felony)
  • Antonym: legal - relating to the law or conforming to the law

Etymology

From Latin criminalis (‘relating to crime’), from crimen (‘accusation, crime’).
