Mozart

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊzɑrt / (AmE), / ˈməʊzɑːt / (BrE)
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (1756–1791), an Austrian composer renowned as a child prodigy and master of the Classical period, celebrated for his prolific output and influence on Western music.

Examples

I had Mr. Mozart to keep me company.

Etymology

From German, the surname of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart.
