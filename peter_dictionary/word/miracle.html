miracle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪrəkəl / (BrE)
An extraordinary and surprising event that is not explicable by natural or scientific laws and is therefore often attributed to a divine agency; more generally, any amazing or wonderful occurrence.

Examples

But barring a miracle, and do n't hold your breath for it, Chicago will not see the Leningrad-Kirov Ballet, which stems from the ballet cradle of the Maryinsky and is one of the great companies of the world.
Not a chance of opening here Nov. 28 - barring that miracle.
"A miracle, a revelation, it was like a curtain suddenly torn away to reveal something nude ".
Tolek Alterman had returned from the colonies in Palestine and, before the national leadership, exalted the miracles of drying up swamps and irrigating the desert.
Trial and error are better placed in the preliminary sketch than in hoping for miracles in the final painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - a feeling of surprise mingled with admiration, caused by something beautiful, unexpected, or unfamiliar
  • Synonym: phenomenon - a remarkable person, thing, or event
  • Antonym: disaster - a sudden accident or a natural catastrophe

Etymology

From Latin miraculum (object of wonder), from mirari (to wonder at).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪrəkəl / (BrE)
A marvellous event believed to be caused by a supernatural power or divine agent, often regarded as unexplainable by natural laws.

Examples

Oh, you can submit a requisition for a miracle if you want to make a fool of yourself.
Lord, it's a miracle!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - a feeling of amazement and admiration caused by something beautiful, remarkable, or unfamiliar

Etymology

From Latin miraculum (object of wonder), from mirari (to wonder at).
