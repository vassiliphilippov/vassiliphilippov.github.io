tempo

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛm.poʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.pəʊ / (BrE)
The speed at which a piece of music is played or should be played.

Examples

Directed by Grigory Kozintsev in a tempo that is studiously slow, he develops a sense of a high tradition shining brightly and passing gravely through an impious world.
Why, for example, does he favor two tempos, rather than one, for the third movement?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pace - the speed or rate of an activity or movement
  • Synonym: rhythm - a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound

Etymology

From Italian 'tempo' meaning 'time', from Latin 'tempus' (time).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəʊ / (BrE)
the speed or rate at which something happens, especially the timing of repeated events or actions

Examples

Their own easier, slower tempo is especially dear to Southerners; and I have heard many say that they are content to earn a half or a third as much as they could up North because they so much prefer the quieter habits of their home town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pace - the speed at which something happens or is done
  • Synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
  • Antonym: slowness - the quality of moving or happening at a low speed
  • Synonym: velocity - the speed of something in a given direction

Etymology

From Italian *tempo* (time, rate), from Latin *tempus* (time).
