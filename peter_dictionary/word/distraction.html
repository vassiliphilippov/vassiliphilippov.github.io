distraction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
A state of mental agitation, distress, or confusion; extreme anxiety or loss of focus due to overwhelming emotions or circumstances.

Examples

I'm glad to say there's none of that distraction in this "Fiorello! "
The concentration of effective power in Rabat leads not only to party bickering, but to distraction from local activity that might have had many auxiliary benefits in addition to contributing to more meaningful elections.

Synset Examples

he drives me to distraction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace or tranquility

Etymology

From Latin distractio (a pulling apart), from distrahere (to pull in different directions).
