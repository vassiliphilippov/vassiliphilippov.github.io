supplicate

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to ask or beg for something earnestly and humbly, especially in prayer or with great respect

Examples

White shirted and conservatively cravated drivers stared conspicuously toward the eastern horizon and past my supplicating and accusing gaze.
The poodle came gleefully to Henrietta and begged for the flowers, supplicating the air with prayerful forepaws.

Synset Examples

He supplicated the King for clemency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: implore - to beg earnestly or desperately

Etymology

From Latin supplicare (to kneel, entreat), from sub- (under) + plicare (to fold).
