commoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmənər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənə / (BrE)
A person who does not have a title of nobility; an ordinary member of society.

Examples

"The time has come ", the walrus said, "To speak of manic things, Of shots and shouts, and sealing dooms Of commoners and kings ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noble - a person of high social rank by birth or title

Etymology

From Middle English 'comuner', from Anglo-Norman 'communer', from Old French 'comunier' (one who shares with others), from 'comun' (common).
