Olympics

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪmpɪks / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪks / (BrE)
the international sports competition held every four years in which athletes from around the world compete in various events, known as the Olympic Games

Examples

The Russian gymnasts beat the tar out of the American gymnasts in the 1960 Olympics for one reason - they were better.
After all, we did pretty well in some other areas of the Olympics competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paralympics - international multi-sport event for athletes with disabilities
  • Synonym: Olympic Games - full name for the Olympics, major international sporting event

Etymology

From Greek 'Olympikos', relating to Olympia, where the ancient games were held. The modern games revived in the late 19th century.
