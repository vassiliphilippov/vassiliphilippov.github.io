cracking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrækɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of breaking or causing something to break, especially with a sharp sound; in particular, opening something by force, as in 'safe cracking.'

Examples

Unlike the Sicilians, he additionally conducted holdups, robberies, and safe cracking expeditions, and refused to touch prostitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fracture - the cracking or breaking of a hard object or material

Etymology

From crack + -ing, forming a noun from the verb.
