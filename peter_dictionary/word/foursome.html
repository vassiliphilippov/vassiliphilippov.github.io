foursome

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌsəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsəm / (BrE)
A group or set of four people considered together as a unit.

Examples

The cops would gather up Connor and the foursome on the third floor and bring us those of them who would voluntarily submit to fingerprinting.

Synset Examples

he joined a barbershop quartet
the foursome teed off before 9 a.m.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: threesome - group of three people considered as a unit
  • Synonym: quartet - group of four people, especially singers or musicians

Etymology

Formed from 'four' + '-some' indicating a group.
