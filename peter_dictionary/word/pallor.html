pallor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælər / (AmE), / ˈpalə / (BrE)
An unhealthy or unnatural paleness of the skin, often caused by illness, shock, or emotional distress.

Examples

His face took on a sudden pallor, became beaded with sweat, and he seemed to have trouble with his breathing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleness - the quality of being pale in color

Etymology

From Latin 'pallor', from 'pallēre' (to be pale).
