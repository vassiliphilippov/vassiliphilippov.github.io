featured

1. ADJ

/ ˈfitʃərd / (AmE), / ˈfiːtʃəd / (BrE)
given special prominence or importance; highlighted or made a central part of something

Examples

State Party Chairman James W. Dorsey added that enthusiasm was picking up for a state rally to be held Sept. 8 in Savannah at which newly elected Texas Sen. John Tower will be the featured speaker.
Prior to the featured race, the stewards announced that apprentice James P. Verrone is suspended ten days for crowding horses and crossing the field sharply in two races on Wednesday.

Synset Examples

a featured actor
a featured item at the sale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlighted - emphasized or made prominent
  • Synonym: starred - given a leading or prominent role

Etymology

Formed from 'feature' (to give prominence to) + '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfiːtʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈfiːtʃəd / (BrE)
(in combination) Having facial features of a particular kind; used with adjectives to specify the type (e.g., 'regular-featured', 'sharp-featured').

Examples

Mr. Skyros did not at all like the look on Angelo's regular featured, almost girlishly good-looking face - or indeed anything about Angelo.

Synset Examples

a grim-featured man
