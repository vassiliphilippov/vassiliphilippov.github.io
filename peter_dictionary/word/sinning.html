sinning

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of committing a sin; a transgression of divine or moral law.

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sin - an act against divine law or moral principles

Etymology

From the verb 'sin', from Old English 'syngian', from 'syn' (sin).
