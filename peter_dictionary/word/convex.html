convex

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvɛks / (BrE)
Having a surface or outline that curves or bulges outward, like the exterior of a sphere or circle.

Examples

This theorem is similar to the theorem of Kakutani that there exists a circumscribing cube around any closed, bounded convex set in **f.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concave - having a surface that curves inward

Etymology

From Latin 'convexus' (vaulted, arched, rounded), from 'con-' (together) + 'vehere' (to carry).
