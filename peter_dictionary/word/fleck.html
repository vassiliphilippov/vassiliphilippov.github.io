fleck

1. NOUN

/ flɛk / (AmE), / flɛk / (BrE)
a very small spot, mark, or fragment, especially one that has become detached from something larger

Examples

When he was going to town, nothing was good enough - he had cursed at Winston once for leaving a fleck of polish on his shoelace.

Synset Examples

a bit of rock caught him in the eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speck - a tiny spot or small particle
  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or fragment of something
  • Synonym: flake - a small, thin piece that has broken away from a larger object

Etymology

From Middle English flekke, probably from Old Norse flekkr (spot, stain).
