acutely

1. ADV

/ əˈkjuːtli / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtli / (BrE)
In a way that is intensely or sharply felt or experienced.

Examples

The collector enjoys the contact with rural life; he hunts folklore for the very "field and stream "reasons that many persons hunt game; and only rarely is he acutely concerned with the meaning of what he has located.
We cannot listen to a conversation for five minutes without being acutely aware of the confusion.
Such speech differences made him acutely aware of the richness and expressivness of language.

Synset Examples

she pitied her sister acutely
acutely aware

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keenly - in an intense or sharp manner

Etymology

Formed from 'acute' (from Latin acūtus) with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ əˈkjuːtli / (AmE), / əˈkjuːtli / (BrE)
In a manner that is sudden and severe, especially with rapid onset (typically of an illness or symptom).

Examples

An enemy might choose an acutely debilitating microorganism, a chronic disease producer or one causing a high rate of lethality.

Synset Examples

an acutely debilitating virus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chronically - in a persistent or long-lasting manner (especially of disease)

Etymology

From 'acute' (sharp, severe) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix), ultimately from Latin 'acutus' (sharp).
