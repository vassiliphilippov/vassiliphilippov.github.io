calumny

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləmni / (AmE), / ˈkæləmni / (BrE)
A false and malicious statement made to damage someone's reputation; slander or defamation.

Examples

Here he was, suddenly caught up in the delirium of a war, in the spite and calumny of Whigs and Tories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person’s reputation
  • Synonym: libel - a published false statement that damages a person's reputation
  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation

Etymology

From Latin *calumnia* (false accusation, trickery).
