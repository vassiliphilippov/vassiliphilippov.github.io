red

1. ADJ

/ rɛd / (AmE), / rɛd / (BrE)
Of a color resembling that of blood, rubies, or ripe tomatoes; at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange.

Examples

Now the thing for us to do is to find ourselves a couple of those wonderful flowering currants such as the red Ribes sanguineum of our Pacific Northwest, or otherwise a good sloe tree, or perhaps some nice pussy willow in bloom, preferably one with male or staminate catkins.
A. armata is much more beautiful in color, at least in the female of the species: a rich, velvety, rusty red.
The firm red spleen weighed 410 gm., and its surface was mottled by discrete, small patches of white material.
A red filter, Zeiss barrier filter with the code (Schott) designation BG 23, was also used in the ocular lens assembly as it improved the contrast between specific and nonspecific fluorescence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: scarlet - bright red with a slightly orange tinge
  • Degree Higher: crimson - deep, rich red color
  • Degree Lower: pink - pale red or reddish color
  • Degree Higher: orange - color between red and yellow in the spectrum

Etymology

From Old English 'rēad', of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'rood' and German 'rot'.

2. ADJ

/ rɛd / (AmE), / rɛd / (BrE)
(especially of the face) having become red or reddened as a result of emotion or exertion.

Examples

His face was bloated with drugging, redder than normal.
His eyes were old and they never saw well, but heated with whisky they 'd glare at my noise, growing red and raising up his rage.
This joke was not funny to Linda Kay, and she blushed, as she always did; then, hearing the muffled boom of Howard's laughter, blushed redder.

Synset Examples

crimson with fury
turned red from exertion
with puffy reddened eyes
red-faced and violent
flushed (or crimson) with embarrassment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flushed - (of the face) red from emotion or exertion
  • Antonym: pale - (of the face) lacking color, as from fear or illness

Etymology

From Old English 'rēad', of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ rɛd / (AmE), / rɛd / (BrE)
A color at the end of the visible spectrum, resembling the hue of blood or a ripe tomato.

Examples

The video signal is amplified and then switched, in synchronism with the three ultraviolet light sources which are sequenced by the rotating mirror so that during one-twentieth of a second only one wavelength, corresponding to red, green, or blue, is seen.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crimson - a deep, rich red color inclining to purple
  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale shade of red
  • Antonym: green - the color of grass, complementary to red

Etymology

From Old English 'rēad', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'rood' and German 'rot'.

4. NOUN

/ rɛd / (AmE), / rɛd / (BrE)
A person who supports radical, revolutionary, or communist ideas, especially used historically as an emotional or derogatory term for such individuals.

Examples

Anyone who tried to remedy some of the most glaring defects in our form of democracy was denounced as a traitorous red whose real purpose was the destruction of our government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - a person who supports or believes in the principles of communism
  • Synonym: radical - a person with extremist political views, particularly advocating for thorough or complete political or social reform
  • Antonym: reactionary - a person opposing political or social progress or reform

Etymology

From the association of the color red with socialist and communist movements, particularly after the Russian Revolution.
