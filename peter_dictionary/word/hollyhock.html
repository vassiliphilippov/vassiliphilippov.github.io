hollyhock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑliˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌhɒk / (BrE)
A tall, flowering plant of the genus Althaea, similar to but with smaller flowers than those of the genus Alcea.

Examples

Alley fences were made of solid boards higher than one's head, but not so high as the golden glow in a corner or the hollyhocks that grew in a line against them.

Etymology

Old English *holegn* (holly) + *hok* (hook, possibly referring to the shape), later influenced by Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑliˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɒlihɒk / (BrE)
A tall garden plant of the genus Alcea, bearing large, showy, variously colored flowers, widely cultivated and originally native to the Middle East.

Examples

Other flowers we might gather as we pleased: myrtle and white violets from beneath the lilacs; the lilacs themselves, that bloomed so prodigally but for the most part beyond our reach; snowballs; hollyhock blossoms that, turned upside down, make pink petticoated ladies; and the little, dark blue larkspur that scattered its seed everywhere.

Etymology

From Middle English 'holihoc', from 'holi' (holy) + 'hoc' (mallow).
