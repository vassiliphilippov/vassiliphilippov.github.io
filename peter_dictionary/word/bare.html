bare

1. ADJ

/ bɛr / (AmE), / bɛə / (BrE)
not more than what is necessary; minimal in amount or degree

Examples

Just the barest suggestion of a heel is found on teenage pumps.
The twenty-three (or twenty-two with the Paynes themselves omitted) provided merely a place to start, and their identification was the barest of starts.

Synset Examples

a bare livelihood
a scanty harvest
a spare diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scanty - insufficient in amount; barely adequate
  • Synonym: meagre - lacking in quantity or quality; barely sufficient
  • Antonym: ample - enough or more than enough; plentiful

Etymology

From Old English bær (bare, naked).

2. ADJ

/ bɛr / (AmE), / beə / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) not covered by clothing; completely unclothed.

Examples

Bare feet are better for such work than any form of slipper.
The hoofmarks of cattle and the prints of bare feet in the mud or in the dust were as numerous as the traces of shod horses.

Synset Examples

bare bodies
naked from the waist up
a nude model

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked - not wearing any clothes; without covering
  • Antonym: clothed - wearing clothes; covered with clothing

Etymology

From Old English *bær* (bare, naked).

3. ADJ

/ bɛr / (AmE), / bɛə / (BrE)
just enough; only the minimum amount required or possible

Examples

These men can learn the value of an honest day's labor, while providing a valuable service to the community and... at a bare minimum of expense to Mr. and Mrs. John Q Taxpayer.

Synset Examples

a bare majority
a marginal victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minimum - the least or smallest amount possible

Etymology

From Old English bær (bare, uncovered).

4. ADJ

/ bɛr / (AmE), / bɛə / (BrE)
Not decorated or embellished; plain and without ornamentation.

Examples

Of course, Norton failed to mention to the press that "bare minimum of expense" is a fairly loose term.

Synset Examples

a plain hair style
unembellished white walls
functional architecture featuring stark unornamented concrete

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - simple, not decorated or elaborate
  • Antonym: ornate - highly decorated, elaborately adorned

Etymology

From Old English bær (naked, uncovered).
