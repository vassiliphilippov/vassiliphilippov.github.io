timbered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
(of land or an area) covered with growing trees or woodland.

Examples

What in the name of God was he doing, crouched in a timbered pit on the wrong bank of the river?

Synset Examples

thickly timbered ridges clothed with loblolly pine and holly
hills timbered up to their summits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooded - covered with trees or forest

Etymology

From 'timber' (wooded land, trees) + -ed, indicating the presence of something.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
(of a building, wall, or structure) furnished with or made of wood or timbers, especially as a visible part of the construction.

Examples

He stopped pacing, leaned against the dank, timbered wall and let his mind drift.

Synset Examples

timbered walls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: half-timbered - (of a building) having a wooden frame with spaces filled with masonry, often seen in historic European architecture

Etymology

From 'timber' + '-ed', indicating made of or provided with timber.
