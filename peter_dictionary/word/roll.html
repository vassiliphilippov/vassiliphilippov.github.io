roll

1. VERB

/ roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undulate - to move in a smooth, wavelike motion

11. VERB

/ roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
(of a sound) to emit, produce, or utter a deep, prolonged, reverberating noise.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumble - produce a continuous deep, resonant sound

Etymology

From Old French *roller*, from Latin *rotulus* (a little wheel), relating to the sense of turning or moving in a curved way, which extended metaphorically to sounds echoing or reverberating.
