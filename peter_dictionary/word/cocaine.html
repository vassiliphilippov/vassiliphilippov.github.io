cocaine

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkeɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˈkeɪn / (BrE)
A powerful, addictive narcotic drug derived from the leaves of the coca plant, used medically as a local anesthetic and also taken illicitly for its stimulant effects.

Examples

Holmes is addicted to the use of cocaine and other refreshing stimulants; he is prone to semi catatonic trances induced by the playing of the vioiln; he is a recluse, an incredible egotist, a confirmed misogynist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a form of cocaine that can be smoked

Etymology

From Spanish 'coca', from Quechua 'kuka' (the coca plant), with '-ine' as a standard chemical suffix.
