camouflage

1. VERB

/ ˈkæməˌflɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaməˌflɑːʒ / (BrE)
to disguise or conceal by using camouflage, especially by making something blend in with its surroundings

Examples

Greg wished the Air Corps had continued to camouflage planes.

Synset Examples

The troops camouflaged themselves before they went into enemy territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceal - to keep something hidden; not allow to be seen
  • Antonym: reveal - to make something known or visible that was hidden

Etymology

From French 'camouflage' (disguise), from camoufler (to disguise).
