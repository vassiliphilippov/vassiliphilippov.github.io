align

1. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to support or identify with a particular group, cause, or way of thinking; to associate oneself with the views or stance of others

Examples

We have aligned ourselves with that "liberal "tradition in Protestant Christianity that counts among the great names in its history those of Schleiermacher, Ritschl, Herrmann, Harnack, and Troeltsch, and more recently, Schweitzer and the early Barth and, in part at least, Bultmann.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side with - to support or favor one side in a dispute
  • Antonym: oppose - to act against or stand in contrast to a group or viewpoint

Etymology

From French *aligner*, from Latin *ad* (to) + *linea* (line).

2. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to place or arrange things in a straight line or in correct relative positions

Examples

Put the 12 tappets and some * * f locking bar spacers in the frame to help align all the components before you tighten the screws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjust - to alter or move something slightly to achieve the desired fit or appearance
  • Antonym: misalign - to place out of line or incorrect position

Etymology

From French *aligner*, from *à* (to) + *ligne* (line), from Latin *linea* (line).

3. VERB

/ əˈlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaɪn / (BrE)
To place or arrange things in a straight line or so that they are parallel or properly positioned relative to each other.

Examples

Aligning all the teeth may take a year or more.

Synset Examples

align the car with the curb
align the sheets of paper on the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straighten - to make or become straight
  • Synonym: position - to place in a particular position

Etymology

From French 'aligner', from 'à' (to) + 'ligne' (line), ultimately from Latin 'linea'.
