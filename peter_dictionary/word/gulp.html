gulp

1. VERB

/ ɡʌlp / (AmE), / ɡʌlp / (BrE)
to swallow something hurriedly, greedily, or in one large mouthful

Examples

He came home overheated, ran straight to the ice-chest, and gulped shivery cold water.
My car gulped 20 gallons without even wiping its mouth.
Timothy Rourke gulped down the whiskey hastily and joined him, asking, "Who are we going to call on in the Northeast section "?
[GULPS]

Synset Examples

The men gulped down their beers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sip - to drink slowly by taking small mouthfuls
  • Synonym: swig - to drink something in large gulps

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of swallowing, first recorded in the late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌlp / (AmE), / ɡʌlp / (BrE)
An act of swallowing a large amount of liquid or food quickly, often in a noisy or audible way.

Examples

I asked, taking a final gulp of my drink, and handing him the empty glass.
Glendora gave a gulp.

Synset Examples

he finished it at a single gulp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swig - a large sip or swallow, often of a drink
  • Degree Lower: sip - a small mouthful of liquid taken at one time

Etymology

Probably imitative, first recorded in the 16th century.
