constraining

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstreɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈstreɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
imposing restrictions or limitations on something; limiting the freedom of action.

Examples

The capacity of intellectuals and members of the new professional classes to contribute creatively to national development is likely to be destroyed by a constraining sense of inferiority toward both their own political class and their colleagues and professional counterparts in the West.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - limiting or controlling something
  • Antonym: liberating - freeing from restrictions or limitations

Etymology

Present participle of 'constrain,' from Old French 'constraindre,' from Latin 'constringere' (to bind together).
