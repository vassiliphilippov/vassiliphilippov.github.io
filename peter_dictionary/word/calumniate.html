calumniate

1. VERB

/ kəˈlʌm.ni.eɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlʌm.ni.eɪt / (BrE)
to make false and malicious statements about someone; to slander or defame

Examples

Ezra Stiles Gannett, an honorable representative of the sanhedrin, addressed himself frankly to the issue in 1845, insisting that Parker should not be persecuted or calumniated and that in this republic no power to restrain him by force could exist.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defame - to damage the good reputation of someone by making false statements
  • Synonym: slander - to make false spoken statements damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: libel - to publish false statements that damage a person's reputation
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin calumniari (to accuse falsely, slander).
