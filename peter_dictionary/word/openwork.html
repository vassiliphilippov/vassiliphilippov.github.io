openwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a decorative design in which parts are left open, often forming a pattern of holes or openings, as in embroidery, metalwork, or woodwork

Examples

Square corner - and gate posts were an open-work pattern of cast-iron foliage; they were topped by steeples complete in every detail: high pitched roof, pinnacle, and narrow gable.

Etymology

From 'open' + 'work', denoting work (craft) with open spaces.
