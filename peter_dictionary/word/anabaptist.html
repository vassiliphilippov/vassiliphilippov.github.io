Anabaptist

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈbæp.tɪst / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈbap.tɪst / (BrE)
A member of a Christian movement that originated in the 16th century, advocating adult baptism and rejecting infant baptism.

Examples

Two Anabaptists, Caroli and Benoit, are to challenge you and William to a debate before the Council.
But Michael Sept had unmasked him, revealing he had never been a bishop, but was an Anabaptist, afraid to state his faith, because he knew John Calvin had written a book against their belief that the soul slept after death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anabaptism - the Christian doctrine and practices of the Anabaptists

Etymology

From Late Latin 'anabaptista', from Greek 'anabaptizein' (to baptize again).
