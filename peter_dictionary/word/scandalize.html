scandalize

1. VERB

/ ˈskændəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskændəlaɪz / (BrE)
to shock or horrify (someone), especially by offending their sense of morality or propriety

Examples

The clock on the mantel piece was scandalized and ticked so loudly that he glanced at it over his shoulder and then quickly left the room.

Synset Examples

The scandalous behavior of this married woman shocked her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to cause surprise and usually horror or disgust
  • Synonym: outrage - to arouse fierce anger, shock, or indignation in

Etymology

From Old French *scandaliser*, from Late Latin *scandalizare* (to cause to stumble, give offense), from Greek *skandalizein*.
