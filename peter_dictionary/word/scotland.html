Scotland

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɑtlənd / (AmE), / ˈskɒtlənd / (BrE)
A country forming the northern part of the island of Great Britain and one of the four nations comprising the United Kingdom, known for its distinctive culture, history, and landscapes.

Examples

Here they built huts and a stockade named Fort Daer after Selkirk's barony in Scotland.
His father, George A. Mercer, was descended from an honored Southern family that could trace its ancestry back to one Hugh Mercer, who had emigrated from Scotland in 1747.

Etymology

From Old English Scottaland, meaning 'land of the Scots,' from Latin Scoti (the Gaels).
