downward

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnwərd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwəd / (BrE)
Moving or directed toward or onto a lower surface or base.

Examples

The "push "exercise of this Push-Pull Super-Set is the Bench Press done with elbows well pulled back and with a greater downward stretch of the pectorals not possible with the barbell variation.

Synset Examples

he lay face downward
the downward pull of gravity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upward - directed toward or onto a higher place or surface

Etymology

From Old English 'dūnweard', meaning 'toward the hill' or 'downward'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnwɚd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwəd / (BrE)
Towards a lower place, level, or condition; in or to a descending direction, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

This gives a wide flare to the pecs, causing them to flow dramatically upward into deltoids and dramatically downward into the serratus and lats.
The clouds bulged downward and burst suddenly into a great black funnel.

Synset Examples

don't fall down
rode the lift up and skied down
prices plunged downward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upward - towards a higher place, level, or condition

Etymology

From Old English 'dūnweard', from 'dūn' (down, hill) + 'weard' (toward).
