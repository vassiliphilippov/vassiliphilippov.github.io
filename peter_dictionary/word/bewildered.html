bewildered

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈwɪldərd / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪldəd / (BrE)
confused and unable to understand something clearly, often because of many conflicting situations or statements

Examples

The figure on the earth halted, seemingly bewildered.
The truth was, the puncher was both bewildered and dismayed by his own mixed luck.

Synset Examples

obviously bemused by his questions
bewildered and confused
a cloudy and confounded philosopher
just a mixed-up kid
she felt lost on the first day of school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or to understand what is happening
  • Synonym: lost - unable to understand or uncertain about something
  • Antonym: certain - sure and confident, not confused

Etymology

From 'bewilder', formed by prefix 'be-' + 'wilder' (to lead astray, perplex); 'wilder' from Middle English, related to 'wilderness'.
