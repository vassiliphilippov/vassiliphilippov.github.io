coalescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊəˈlɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌkəʊəˈlɛsəns / (BrE)
the act or process of coming together and uniting into one; the merging of distinct elements into a single entity

Examples

Some of this may prove to be true, or even considerable of it, whether by genetic ramification or by diffusion and coalescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusion - the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity
  • Antonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Latin coalescere (to grow together).
