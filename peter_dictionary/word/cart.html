cart

1. NOUN

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
A small vehicle with wheels, typically pushed or pulled by hand, used for carrying loads.

Examples

Someone said that while we were standing here and arguing about it, the British would be gone; but Cousin Simmons said he had watched them marching west early in the morning, and moving at a much brisker pace it had still taken half an hour for their column to pass, what with the narrowness of the road and their baggage and ammunition carts.

Synset Examples

he used a handcart to carry the rocks away
their pushcart was piled high with groceries

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wagon - a larger, typically horse-drawn vehicle for transporting goods

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kartr', related to Old English 'cræt' (cart, wagon).

2. NOUN

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
a strong open vehicle with two or four wheels, typically drawn by a horse or other animal, used for carrying loads

Examples

His trading goods came from Canada to the Forks of Red River and from Selkirk's settlement he brought them south in carts.
These carts were of a type devised in Pembina in the days of Alexander Henry the Younger about a decade before the Selkirk colony was begun.
In 1802 Henry referred to "our new carts "as being about four feet off the ground and carrying five times as much as a horse could pack.
When the hay wagon had gone, and an interval passed, a huckster's cart might turn the corner.
Every evening, I load up the cart and make my round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wagon - a larger, covered or open vehicle for transporting goods, usually with four wheels

Etymology

From Latin 'carrus' (wagon, cart), via Old French 'carrete'.

3. VERB

/ kɑrt / (AmE), / kɑːt / (BrE)
to carry or transport something, especially with effort or using a cart or similar vehicle

Examples

With their customary source of supply cut off, the Fort Garry free traders engaged three men to cart goods to them from the Mississippi country.

Synset Examples

haul stones
haul nets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul - to pull or drag with effort or force

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kartr', related to the vehicle used for carrying goods.
