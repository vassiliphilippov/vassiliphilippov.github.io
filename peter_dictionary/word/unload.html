unload

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈloʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊd / (BrE)
to remove cargo, goods, or passengers from a vehicle, ship, or aircraft

Examples

At times, three ships a day from the Soviet bloc are unloading in Cuban ports.
It gave him a chance to unload the stuff and get it down to the cellar without a barrage of acid comments.
Richardson wondered when it would be unloaded.

Synset Examples

unload the cargo
drop off the passengers at the hotel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: load - to put cargo or passengers onto a vehicle or container

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + load, dating to the late 15th century.
