tarnish

1. VERB

/ ˈtɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
To lose or cause to lose luster or shine, especially as a result of exposure to air or moisture; also, to damage or spoil the quality or reputation of someone or something.

Examples

Tardily the Government here came to understand how this country's own reputation was tarnished by the association with repression.

Synset Examples

The silver was tarnished by the long exposure to the air
Her reputation was sullied after the affair with a married man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sully - to damage the purity or integrity of; defile

Etymology

From Old French *ternir* ‘to make dull’, from Latin *terere* ‘to rub’ or ‘wear’ (via Vulgar Latin).
