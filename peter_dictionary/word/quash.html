quash

1. VERB

/ kwɑʃ / (AmE), / kwɒʃ / (BrE)
To put an end to something, especially by force, authority, or intimidation.

Examples

It was a sort of poetic justice that at the time of his own demise a new plot to overthrow the Venezuelan government, reportedly involving the use of Dominican arms by former Venezuelan Dictator Marcos Perez Jimenez, has been uncovered and quashed.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to activities or actions, often by force
  • Synonym: subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to, the opposite of putting down by force

Etymology

From Old French *quasser*, from Latin *quassare* (to shatter).
