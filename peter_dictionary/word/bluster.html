bluster

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌstər / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
loud, aggressive, or swaggering talk or behavior intended to impress or intimidate, often lacking real courage or substance

Examples

The Vice President said with a slight bluster, "There is n't anyone who loves the President more than I do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravado - a bold manner or a show of boldness intended to impress or intimidate
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'blustren', of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'blisteren'.

2. VERB

/ ˈblʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
to talk in a loud, aggressive, or boastful way, often with little effect

Examples

"I never felt better in my life ", Fiske blustered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about achievements
  • Synonym: brag - to speak proudly about what one has done

Etymology

Probably from Middle English blusten (to blow violently).
