blast

1. NOUN

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
(Baseball, informal) A very long fly ball, especially a home run.

Examples

In the ninth, Robinson led off with his second double of the night, a blast off the fence 375 feet deep into left.
Al Smith's 340 - blast over left in the fourth - his fourth homer of the campaign - tied the score and Carreon's first major league home run in the fifth put the Sox back in front.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home run - hit allowing batter to round all bases, scoring a run

2. VERB

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
to hit something with great force, especially in sports

Examples

Lumpe worked a walk as the first batter to face Hyde and romped around as Siebern blasted Hyde's next toss 415 feet over the scoreboard in right center.
"Virdon has been blasting the ball.

Synset Examples

He smashed a 3-run homer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - to hit something with great force

3. VERB

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh, or strident sound, especially by blowing a horn or similar instrument

Examples

Their secondary hero is another pro, Willie Chisholm, who drank his lunch during another Open and tried to blast his way out of a rock strewn gully.
From the east to the west coast of the Korean peninsula was a strip of land in which fear filled men were at that same moment furtively crawling through the night, sitting in sweaty anticipation of any movement or sound, or shouting amidst confused rifle flashes and muzzle blasts.
An old gentlemen next to me held a Boy Scout bugle to his lips and blasted away at every fourth step and during the interim shouted out, "V for Victory "!

Synset Examples

She tended to blast when speaking into a microphone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blare - to make a loud and harsh noise

Etymology

From Old English 'blǣstan' (to blow or puff), related to 'blǣst' (a blowing, blast).

4. VERB

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
to use explosives to destroy, break apart, or clear away something

Examples

Gouge, burn, blast, insult it as they would, could anyone really take Papa-san?

Synset Examples

The enemy has been shelling us all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow up - to destroy something with explosives
  • Synonym: demolish - to completely destroy a structure, often with explosives

5. NOUN

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
a sudden loud and explosive noise

Examples

Andy Ross had just started swinging an ax at his second willow when the distant blast of a rifle sounded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise, similar in effect

Etymology

From Old English blǣst (a blowing, puff of wind), related to 'blow'.

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
(informal) To fire a shot, especially with force or repeatedly, often with a gun.

Examples

Synset Examples

the gunman blasted away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - to fire a gun or discharge a bullet

/ blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
to create an opening, passage, or destruction by detonating explosives

Examples

Then you come back blasting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
