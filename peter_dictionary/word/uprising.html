uprising

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌraɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
An act of resistance or rebellion against those in power, often by a group seeking to challenge or overthrow authority.

Examples

Fearful of inviting a military showdown with the West which they could not win, the Communists seek to undermine Western power where the nuclear might of the West is irrelevant - in backwoods guerrilla skirmishes, in mob uprisings in the streets, in parliaments, in clandestine meetings of undercover conspirators, at the United Nations, on the propaganda front, at diplomatic conferences - preferably at the highest level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - organized resistance to authority, especially by force
  • Synonym: insurrection - violent uprising against an authority or government
  • Synonym: revolt - an attempt to end the authority of a person or body by rebelling

Etymology

From 'up' + 'rising'; recorded in English since the 13th century for the literal act of rising up, and since the 16th century for insurrection.
