correction

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something right by removing or fixing a mistake, error, or fault

Examples

Corrections were applied for modulation broadening, apparatus background, and field shift.
Asked which institution most needs correction, I would say the corporation as it exists in America today.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: error - a mistake or incorrect action
  • Synonym: amendment - a minor change or improvement

Etymology

From Latin correctio, from corrigere 'to make straight, set right'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
an amount or change made to account for or fix an error or inaccuracy

Examples

The fraction of exchange was determined as the ratio of the counts / minute observed in the carbon tetrachloride to the counts / minute calculated for the carbon tetrachloride fractions for equilibrium distribution of the activity between the chlorine and carbon tetrachloride, empirically determined correction being made for the difference in counting efficiency of * * f in * * f and * * f.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amendment - a minor change or addition designed to improve a text or document
  • Synonym: adjustment - a small alteration or movement made to achieve a desired fit or result
  • Antonym: error - a mistake or inaccuracy

Etymology

From Latin 'correctio', from 'corrigere' meaning 'to make straight, put right'.

3. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
a value added or subtracted from a scientific measurement to increase its accuracy

Examples

A hydrostatic head correction has been neither necessary nor applicable in the determination of vapor pressures or temperatures for the bulk liquid helium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - a small change made to achieve a desired fit or result

Etymology

From Latin correctio (a making straight, correction).
