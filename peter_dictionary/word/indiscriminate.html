indiscriminate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (BrE)
Done without careful judgment or making distinctions; lacking selectivity.

Examples

And if the affection for the suburban branch reflects a desire to shop with "nice people ", rather than with the indiscriminate urban mass which supports the downtown department store, the central location may be in serious trouble.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discriminate - To recognize or make distinctions; to differentiate

Etymology

From Latin indiscriminatus, from in- (not) + discriminatus (distinguished).
