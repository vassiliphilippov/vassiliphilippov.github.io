alley

1. NOUN

/ ˈæli / (AmE), / ˈæli / (BrE)
A narrow street or passageway, typically between or behind buildings, usually with walls or fences on both sides.

Examples

Alley fences were made of solid boards higher than one's head, but not so high as the golden glow in a corner or the hollyhocks that grew in a line against them.
His parents were murdered in that alley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lane - a narrow road or passage, especially in the countryside
  • Degree Higher: street - a public road in a city or town

Etymology

From Old French 'alee' (a walk or passage), from 'aler' (to go).
