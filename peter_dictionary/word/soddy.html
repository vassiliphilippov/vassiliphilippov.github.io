soddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑdi / (AmE), / ˈsɒdi / (BrE)
A house built from blocks of sod, common on the American prairies in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

The company herds were being raided less often, and cabins and soddies all over the range were standing deserted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adobe house - a house built from sun-dried mud bricks

Etymology

From 'sod' (a piece of turf) + -y (forming nouns).
