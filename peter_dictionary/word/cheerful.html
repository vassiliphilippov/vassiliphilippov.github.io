cheerful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪɚfəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəfəɫ / (BrE)
Happy and showing a positive attitude or good spirits; causing feelings of happiness.

Examples

Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.
The apex, the study where Eugene slept, was light and bright and airy and cheerful.
He felt cheerful again, refreshed; presentable in his wide-cut brown suit, the well-made riding boots.

Synset Examples

her cheerful nature
a cheerful greeting
a cheerful room
as cheerful as anyone confined to a hospital bed could be

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment
  • Antonym: sad - feeling or showing sorrow; unhappy
  • Synonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness
  • Antonym: gloomy - feeling distressed or pessimistic

Etymology

From cheer + -ful, where 'cheer' derives from Old French 'chiere' (face, expression, mood).
