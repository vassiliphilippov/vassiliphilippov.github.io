scaled

1. ADJ

/ skeɪld / (AmE), / skeɪld / (BrE)
(of an animal or object) having the body covered or partially covered with thin, flat horny plates (scales), as in some fish and reptiles.

Examples

There was the slate roof of the church; ivy climbed the red brick walls like a green scaled monster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaly - having scales or covered with scales

Etymology

From 'scale' (protective plate) + -ed (adjectival suffix).
