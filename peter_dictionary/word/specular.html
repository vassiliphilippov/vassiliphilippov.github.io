specular

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
Reflecting light in a mirrorlike way; capable of producing a clear image as a mirror does.

Examples

The reflection of radiation originating from the anode holder and reflected back to it by the surrounding metal surfaces should also be small because of the specular characteristic of the metal surfaces and of the specific geometry.

Synset Examples

mirrorlike surface of the lake
a specular metal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diffuse - scattering light in many directions rather than reflecting it like a mirror

Etymology

From Latin 'specularis' (of a mirror), from 'speculum' (mirror).
