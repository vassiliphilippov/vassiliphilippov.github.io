referee

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈri / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈriː / (BrE)
An official in sports who ensures the rules are followed and fair play is maintained, especially in games such as boxing, football, or soccer.

Examples

They were disturbed by his idiotic bravado - as, when his bodyguard, Yankee Schwartz, complained that he had been snubbed by Dave Miller, a prize-fight referee, chieftain of a Jewish gang and one of four brothers of tough reputation, who were Hirschey, a gambler politician in loose beer running league with Torrio and O' Banion, Frank, a policeman, and Max, the youngest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umpire - an official who enforces the rules in sports, especially in baseball, cricket, and tennis

Etymology

From French 'référé', past participle of 'référer' (to refer), from Latin 'referre' (to refer, relate).
