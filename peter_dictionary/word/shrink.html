shrink

1. VERB

/ ʃɹɪŋk / (AmE), / ʃɹɪŋk / (BrE)
to become or make smaller in size, usually as a result of losing moisture; to shrivel

Examples

In February, 1959, during the second admission to The New York Hospital, a biopsy specimen of the left gastrocnemius showed striking increase in the sarcolemmal sheath nuclei and shrunken muscle fibers in several sections.
I heard o' Texas cattlemen wrappin' a cow thief up in green hides and lettin' the sun shrink' em and squeeze him to death.
If only this could be done more often - with such heartening results - many of the earth's "big problems "would shrink to the insignificances they really are.

Synset Examples

The fruit dried and shriveled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrivel - to become wrinkled or shrunken, especially through loss of moisture
  • Synonym: wither - to dry up or become shriveled, often due to lack of moisture

Etymology

From Old English scrincan (to contract, wither).

2. VERB

/ ʃrɪŋk / (AmE), / ʃrɪŋk / (BrE)
to move back or away, especially because of fear or pain; to flinch or recoil

Examples

The man shrank before the hot fury, searching frantically for the answer * * h Finnegan woke up.

Synset Examples

she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - to make a small sudden movement because of pain or fear
  • Synonym: recoil - to move back suddenly, often from fear or disgust

Etymology

Old English 'scrincan' (to dwindle, shrink), related to German 'schrumpfen'.

3. VERB

/ ʃrɪŋk / (AmE), / ʃrɪŋk / (BrE)
to become or make something smaller in size, especially gradually or as a result of heat, moisture, or age

Examples

Rather, they are impressed with the British Government's success in forcing - and helping - the British textile industry to shrink and to change over to other products.
But that heritage is shrinking even faster.

Synset Examples

Hot water will shrink the sweater
Can you shrink this image?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - to become smaller or narrower, often as a result of cold; synonym in the context of size reduction
  • Antonym: expand - to increase in size, number, or importance; antonym on the size spectrum

Etymology

From Old English 'scrincan' (to shrivel up, contract).

4. VERB

/ ʃrɪŋk / (AmE), / ʃrɪŋk / (BrE)
to become smaller in size, amount, or value, or to contract or draw together, often as a result of heat, cold, or moisture

Examples

Do not use wood as it will not shrink with the clay and would cause breakage.

Synset Examples

The fabric shrank
The balloon shrank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - to become smaller or shorter, especially as a result of cooling or drying
  • Antonym: expand - to become larger in size, number, or amount

Etymology

From Old English scrincan (to contract, wither).
