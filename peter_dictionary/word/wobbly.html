wobbly

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑbli / (AmE), / ˈwɒbli / (BrE)
liable to shake or move unsteadily, especially because of weakness, instability, or defect.

Examples

The infant, in white terry-cloth bathrobe, her face intense and purposeful, had essayed a few wobbly steps toward her father.

Synset Examples

a rickety table
a wobbly chair with shaky legs
the ladder felt a little wobbly
the bridge still stands though one of the arches is wonky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - not steady or firm; likely to change or fall
  • Synonym: rickety - poorly made and likely to collapse or shake; unstable
  • Antonym: stable - firmly fixed or not likely to move or change
