bell

1. NOUN

/ bɛl / (AmE), / bɛl / (BrE)
A hollow device, typically made of metal, that produces a ringing sound when struck, often used for signaling, in music, or as a decorative object.

Examples

Within two weeks Warren was ringing the bell at the abbey gate.
Like the bell at Mass, the doorbell was pitched too high.
For an instant John longed for the sound of the bells of Noyon-la-Sainte, the touch of his mother's hand, the lilt of Charles's voice in the square raftered rooms, his father's bass tones rumbling to the canons, and the sight of the beloved bishop.
Quickening his steps, John entered the vast church and climbed the tower steps to the bells.
Underneath the big one, in the silent moonlight, lay a dead pigeon, and on the smaller bell, the Clemence, two gray and white birds slept huddled together in the cold winter air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chime - musical instrument or device that produces a ringing sound, typically in a set of tuned bells
  • Synonym: gong - metal disk that makes a resonant sound when struck

Etymology

From Old English 'belle', related to Middle Dutch 'belle' (bell).

2. NOUN

/ bɛl / (AmE), / bɛl / (BrE)
A push button at an outer door that makes a ringing or buzzing sound to signal someone inside.

Examples

He clomped heavily up the stoop and rang the bell.
In fact, I was watching you on that little seventeen inch screen when you rang my bell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorbell - electric bell or buzzer rung from outside a door

Etymology

From Old English *belle*; related to Middle Dutch *belle*.

3. NOUN

/ bɛl / (AmE), / bɛl / (BrE)
the sound produced by a bell when it is struck

Examples

The 7: 45 bell rang and he could hear the outside doors bang shut, closing in the assembled day watch.

Synset Examples

saved by the bell
she heard the distant toll of church bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chime - a melodious ringing sound, especially from a bell or set of bells
  • Synonym: toll - the sound produced by a large bell, especially one that is struck slowly
