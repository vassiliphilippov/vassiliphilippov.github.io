rectitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the quality of being morally correct and honest

Examples

Above all, disabuse yourselves of any thought that I propose to vent moral indignation at your rifling my residence, to whimper over the loss of a few objets d'art, or to shame you into rectitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uprightness - the quality of being honest and morally upright
  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or unethical conduct by a person in power

Etymology

From Latin 'rectitudo' meaning 'rightness, straightness', from 'rectus' (right, straight).
