Russia

1. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
The largest and most influential constituent republic of the former Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR), occupying eastern Europe and northern Asia, officially known as the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic (RSFSR).

Examples

He was brigadier of the club's locker-room light infantry, and at one time or another declared war on Russia, Czechoslovakia, Yugoslavia, and China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soviet Union - federation of socialist republics that existed from 1922 to 1991
  • Degree Higher: Russian Federation - modern country that succeeded the RSFSR after 1991

2. PROPN

/ ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
A former empire in eastern Europe and northern Asia, established in the 14th century with Moscow as its capital, and later Saint Petersburg; notable for its expansion and power under rulers such as Peter the Great and Catherine the Great; overthrown by revolution in 1917.

Examples

Each failed catastrophically in an invasion of Russia and each brought ruin on the country that worshipped him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian Empire - historical monarchy and empire ruled by the Romanovs, preceding the Soviet Union
  • Antonym: Soviet Union - the socialist state that succeeded the Russian Empire after 1917

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Russia, from Rus', the medieval East Slavic state.
