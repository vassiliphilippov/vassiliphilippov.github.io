botch

1. VERB

/ bɑtʃ / (AmE), / bɒtʃ / (BrE)
To carry out a task badly or carelessly; to spoil or ruin something through incompetence or clumsiness.

Examples

Very, very frightened that they botched it.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungle - carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • Synonym: mishandle - manage or deal with something badly or incompetently
  • Antonym: fix - repair or correct something properly

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'bocchen' (to mend), of uncertain origin.
