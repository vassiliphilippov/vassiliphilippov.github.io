idealist

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdiəlɪst / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who is guided more by ideals or principles than by practical considerations

Examples

Emerson - Platonist, idealist, doctrinaire - sounded a high Transcendental note in his "Boston Hymn ", delivered in 1863 in the Boston Music Hall amidst thundering applause: "Pay ransom to the owner and fill the bag to the brim.
It is the abstractionism, the unrealism, of the pure idealist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realist - someone who is guided more by practical considerations than ideals

Etymology

From ideal + -ist, first used in English in the 18th century.
