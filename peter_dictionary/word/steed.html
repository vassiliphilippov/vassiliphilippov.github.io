steed

1. NOUN

/ stid / (AmE), / stiːd / (BrE)
(literary) A spirited horse, especially one ridden into battle or used ceremonially.

Examples

The truth is, however, that when Mel Chandler first reported to the regiment the only steed he had ever ridden was a swivel chair and the only weapon he had ever wielded was a pencil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - large domesticated animal used for riding or racing

Etymology

From Old English stēda (stallion), related to 'stud'.
