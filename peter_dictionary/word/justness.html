justness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌstnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being correct or proper according to a standard of aesthetics or propriety.

Examples

The doctor, with the air of a man whose professional interests have found scope, drew Alex's attention to those excellences which might otherwise have escaped him: the fine color in comb and wattles, the length and quality of neck and saddle hackles, the firm, wide spread of the toes, and a rare justness in the formation of the ear lappets.

Synset Examples

it was performed with justness and beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correctness - the quality of being correct or accurate
  • Synonym: propriety - the quality of conforming to accepted standards; decorum

Etymology

From 'just' + '-ness', modeled after Middle English and Latin roots for 'just' (Latin 'justus': righteous, lawful).
