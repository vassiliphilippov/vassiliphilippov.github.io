replant

1. VERB

/ riˈplænt / (AmE), / riˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to plant (a plant or crop) again, either in the same place or in a new location

Examples

Uncle Randolph and Joel had replanted the bottom lands with difficulty, for more of the slaves, including Annie, had sneaked off when the soldiers broke camp.

Synset Examples

They replanted the land
He replanted the seedlings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plant - to put a seed, bulb, or plant in the ground to grow

Etymology

From re- (again) + plant (from Latin plantare, to plant).
