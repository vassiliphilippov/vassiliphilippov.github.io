cosecant

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈsiːkənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈsiːkənt / (BrE)
The trigonometric function equal to the ratio of the length of the hypotenuse to the length of the side opposite a given angle in a right-angled triangle, or the reciprocal of the sine of the angle.

Examples

If it is assumed that the formula given by Lodge of * * f, cosec 2lc applies, the pressure difference along the major axes can be calculated from the angle of inclination of the major axis, and from this the interfacial tension can be calculated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secant - trigonometric function relating to the ratio of hypotenuse to adjacent side
  • Antonym: sine - trigonometric function; ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (complementary) + secant, modeled after 'cosine'.
