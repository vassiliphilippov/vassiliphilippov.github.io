stomp

1. VERB

/ stɑmp / (AmE), / stɒmp / (BrE)
to walk with heavy, deliberate steps, often showing anger or energy

Examples

A short time later, Lieutenant Bonner stomped into the dressing room.
The final group included folk songs from back home, stomped out, shouted and chanted with irresistible spirit and in cowboy costume.

Synset Examples

The men stomped through the snow in their heavy boots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tramp - walk heavily or noisily
  • Antonym: tiptoe - walk quietly or stealthily
