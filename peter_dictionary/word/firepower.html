firepower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌpaʊə / (BrE)
the ability of a military force to deliver effective gunfire or missile attacks on a target

Examples

Japanese aircraft were strong on maneuverability, American on speed and firepower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - shots fired from weapons, especially in combat

Etymology

Formed from 'fire' (as in firearms or gunfire) + 'power', first recorded in early 20th century military contexts.
