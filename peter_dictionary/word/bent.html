bent

1. NOUN

/ bɛnt / (AmE), / bɛnt / (BrE)
A strong natural tendency, talent, or inclination for a particular type of work or activity.

Examples

By her eighteenth birthday her bent for writing was so evident that Papa and Mamma gave her a Life of Dickens as a spur to her aspiration.
With her son evidencing so strong a musical bent his mother could do little else but get him started on the study of music - though she waited until he was ten - beginning with the piano and following that with the trumpet.

Synset Examples

the set of his mind was obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - a person's natural tendency or urge to act in a particular way
  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill

Etymology

From Old English 'bendt', meaning 'a curve' or 'a leaning'; related to the verb 'bend'.
