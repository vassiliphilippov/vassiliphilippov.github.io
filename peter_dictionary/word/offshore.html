offshore

1. ADV

/ ˌɔfˈʃɔr / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈʃɔː / (BrE)
At or towards a position away from the shore or land; out at sea.

Examples

The spraying operation was conducted from the rear deck of a small Naval vessel, cruising two miles off-shore and vertical to an on-shore breeze.

Synset Examples

cruising three miles offshore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inshore - closer to the shore or land

Etymology

From off + shore, indicating movement or location away from the shore.
