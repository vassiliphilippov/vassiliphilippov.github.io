azimuth

1. NOUN

/ ˈæz.ə.mɪθ / (AmE), / ˈæz.ɪ.məθ / (BrE)
The angular distance of an object around the horizon from a reference direction, usually measured clockwise from the north; typically used in astronomy and navigation.

Examples

The measurements made with the NRL 50 - foot reflector, which is altitude azimuth mounted, would have shown a systematic change with local hour angle in the measured intensities of Venus and Jupiter if a substantial part of the radiation had been linearly polarized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altitude - the elevation angle of a celestial body above the horizon

Etymology

From Arabic 'as-sumut' (the directions, pl. of 'summ', direction or way).
