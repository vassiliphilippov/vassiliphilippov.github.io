ominously

1. ADV

/ ˈɑː.mə.nəs.li / (AmE), / ˈɒ.mɪ.nəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that suggests that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen.

Examples

As the play unfolds, however, the audience is subtly brought into the grip of an awful evil which grows with ominously gathering power and soon engulfs the community.
"Do you remember the woman in the French Alps who was all alone with her sheep one day when the sun darkened ominously?

Synset Examples

the sun darkened ominously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threateningly - in a way that expresses a threat or menace

Etymology

From 'ominous' + '-ly'; 'ominous' from Latin 'omen' (foreboding, sign).
