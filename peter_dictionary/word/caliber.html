caliber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
the internal diameter of a tube, especially the barrel of a gun, usually expressed in hundredths or thousandths of an inch or in millimeters.

Examples

The beaming rok was carrying a thirty caliber machine gun; another man lugged the tripod and a box of ammunition.
Well, where I get hazy is where the cleaning woman shows up the following morning and finds your wife in bed with her lover, riddled with .38 caliber bullets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bore - the hollow part inside a gun barrel or tube; diameter of that part

Etymology

From French 'calibre', possibly from Italian 'calibro', origin uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæləbər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
the level of quality or ability that someone or something has, especially compared to others of the same kind

Examples

Have you studied the caliber and sales approaches of your sales force in relation to requirements for effective marketing?
Has your company developed selection and training processes that are geared to providing the caliber of salesmen you will need in the next 10 yr.?

Synset Examples

the quality of students has risen
an executive of low caliber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quality - general standard or level of excellence
  • Synonym: stature - reputation or standing, especially in terms of achievement or quality
  • Synonym: merit - the quality of being good, deserving praise

Etymology

From French calibre (degree of worth, diameter of a bullet), originally referring to the internal diameter of a gun barrel.
