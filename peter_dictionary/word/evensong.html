evensong

1. NOUN

/ ˈivənˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːvənˌsɒŋ / (BrE)
a service of evening prayers, psalms, and canticles, typically held in the Anglican Church; also known as vespers.

Examples

A Catholic priest recently recounted how in the chapel of a large city university, following Anglican evensong, at which there was a congregation of twelve, he celebrated Mass before more than a hundred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vespers - a service of evening prayer in the Divine Office; especially in Western Christian tradition

Etymology

From Old English 'æfensang' (evening song).
