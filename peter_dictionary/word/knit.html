knit

1. VERB

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
To tie, link, or bring together closely, especially in a way that forms a strong bond; often used figuratively to describe close relationships or unity.

Examples

All of this happening in a time of their lives when their youth and their brotherhood knitted them together as no other time or circumstance could.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unite - bring or come together for a common purpose
  • Synonym: bond - establish a close relationship
  • Antonym: separate - move or be apart; stop being joined

Etymology

From Old English cnyttan (to tie in a knot).

2. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
A piece of fabric or a garment produced by knitting yarn or thread together.

Examples

He wore a brown knit sports shirt with no tie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knitted fabric - fabric produced by interlocking loops of yarn

Etymology

From the verb 'knit', which comes from Old English *cnyttan* (to tie in a knot).

3. VERB

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
to make fabric or garments by interlocking loops of yarn with needles or a machine

Examples

Some women can sit and sew, crochet, tat or knit by the hour, and look calm and relaxed and turn out beautiful work.

Synset Examples

knit a scarf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crochet - to make fabric by looping yarn with a hooked needle
  • Synonym: sew - to join fabric together with needle and thread

Etymology

Old English *cnyttan* (to tie, knot), related to knot.
