harsh

1. ADJ

/ hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
Unpleasantly rough, jarring, or irritating to the senses; causing discomfort or unease.

Examples

The South's antipathy to Northern civilization includes such charges as poor manners, harsh accents, lack of appreciation of the arts of living like gastronomy and the use of leisure.
He ai n't gone far "! a harsh cry floated to him across the brush.

Synset Examples

the harsh cry of a blue jay
harsh cognac
the harsh white light makes you screw up your eyes
harsh irritating smoke filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grating - (of sound) harsh and unpleasant
  • Antonym: gentle - not harsh; soft or mild to the senses

Etymology

From Middle English harsk (rough, coarse, dry) of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse harskr (rough, dry).

2. ADJ

/ hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
Unpleasantly strict, severe, or unkind in manner, action, or effect.

Examples

All too often our language is unduly harsh.
But when a board of inquiry was called to look into the charges of cowardice made against him, the men who had seen Reno leave the battlefield and the officer who had heard Reno suggest that the wounded be left to be tortured by the Sioux, refused to say a harsh word against him.

Synset Examples

wild and harsh country full of hot sand and cactus
the nomad life is rough and hazardous

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gentle - kind and mild in manner or effect
  • Synonym: severe - very great; intense or strict in manner

3. ADJ

/ hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
Unpleasantly severe, strict, or difficult to accept; rigorously disagreeable.

Examples

There's a harsh truth to face.

Synset Examples

the harsh facts of court delays
an abrasive character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severe - very great; intense or strict
  • Antonym: gentle - mild and kind, not harsh or severe
