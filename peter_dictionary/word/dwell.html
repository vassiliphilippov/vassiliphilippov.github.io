dwell

1. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
to think, speak, or write at length or with deep focus about something, especially something troubling or significant

Examples

It is worth dwelling in some detail on the crisis of this story, because it brings together a number of characteristic elements and makes of them a curious, riddling compound obscurely but centrally significant for Mann's work.
I know I do not have to dwell here on the absurdity of that contention.
If he dwelt on the indignities he suffered he would lose all respect for her, and without the respect he might lose his view of her, too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - to think deeply or worry persistently about something
  • Synonym: ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention or give no thought to something

Etymology

From Old English 'dwellan' meaning 'to lead astray, hinder', later 'to delay, linger'.

2. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
to live in a particular place; to inhabit

Examples

"There is a spark dwells deep within my soul.
Or the mode of love to this fragment by a recent poet: "Know ye, fair folk who dwell on earth Or shall hereafter come to birth, That here, with dust upon his eyes, Iraj, the sweet-tongued singer, lies.

Synset Examples

People lived in Africa millions of years ago
The people inhabited the islands that are now deserted
this kind of fish dwells near the bottom of the ocean
deer are populating the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhabit - to live in a specific place
  • Synonym: reside - to live in a place for a long time

Etymology

From Old English *dwellan* (to lead astray, hinder; later to remain, inhabit).

3. VERB

/ dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
to exist, reside, or be present in a particular place, situation, or state

Examples

They dwell, in short, in the doltish twilight in which peasants and serfs of the past are commonly reported to have lived.
To have Christ dwelling through faith in your hearts.
Lawrence Lipton, in The Holy Barbarians, stresses that for the beat generation sex is more than a source of pleasure; it is a mystique, and their private language is rich in the multivalent ambiguities of sexual reference so that they dwell in a sexualized universe of discourse.

Synset Examples

The problems dwell in the social injustices in this country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reside - to have one's permanent home in a place
  • Synonym: inhabit - to live or dwell in a place

Etymology

From Old English 'dwellan' (to lead astray, hinder, delay), later 'to remain, dwell'.
