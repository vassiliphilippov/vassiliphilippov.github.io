commendable

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɛndəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɛndəbl / (BrE)
Deserving praise or approval; worthy of commendation.

Examples

We may say that his attitude was foolish, since he may have been a success had he learned some human relations skills; or we may say that his attitude was commendable, showing his independence of mind, in his refusal to adjust to the opinions of others.

Synset Examples

applaudable efforts to save the environment
a commendable sense of purpose
laudable motives of improving housing conditions
a significant and praiseworthy increase in computer intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudable - deserving praise or commendation
  • Synonym: praiseworthy - deserving approval and praise
  • Antonym: blameworthy - deserving censure or blame

Etymology

From Latin commendare (to recommend, entrust).
