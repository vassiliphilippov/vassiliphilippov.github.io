richness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of a sensation—such as a sound, color, or flavor—that is pleasingly full, deep, or intense.

Examples

The voices of all the characters, including that of Cherkasov, have richness, roughness or color to conform with the personalities.

Synset Examples

the music had a fullness that echoed through the hall
the cheap wine had no body, no mellowness
he was well aware of the richness of his own appearance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fullness - the quality of being full or complete, especially in sound or appearance
  • Antonym: thinness - the quality of lacking richness; being weak or insubstantial (of sound, color, etc.)

Etymology

From 'rich' + '-ness'; 'rich' from Old French riche, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being abundant or plentiful in some desirable attribute, such as detail, color, or language

Examples

Such speech differences made him acutely aware of the richness and expressivness of language.
If art is to release us from these postulated things [ things we must think symbolically about ] and bring us back to the ineffable beauty and richness of the aesthetic component of reality in its immediacy, it must sever its connection with these common sense entities ".
This set his tone: richness of texture and color, and another kind of richness as well, for his clothing and decorations would have paid the Brush-off's rent for a year.

Synset Examples

the profusion of detail
the idiomatic richness of English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor or lacking in something
  • Synonym: profusion - an extremely abundant supply or quantity

Etymology

From 'rich' + '-ness', with 'rich' from Old French riche, from Frankish *rīki (powerful, wealthy).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃ.nəs / (BrE)
The state of having abundant wealth or valuable resources.

Examples

This set his tone: richness of texture and color, and another kind of richness as well, for his clothing and decorations would have paid the Brush-off's rent for a year.

Synset Examples

they studied forerunners of richness or poverty
the richness all around unsettled him for he had expected to find poverty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affluence - abundant wealth or great prosperity
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor; lack of wealth

Etymology

From 'rich' + '-ness'; 'rich' from Old English 'rīce' (powerful, wealthy).
