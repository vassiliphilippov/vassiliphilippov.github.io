staccato

1. ADJ

/ stəˈkɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / stəˈkɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(of musical notes or sounds) performed with each note sharply separated from the others; disconnected and crisp.

Examples

He demonstrated by playing an imaginary piano, doing a staccato passage with a broadly exaggerated attack.

Synset Examples

staccato applause
a staccato command
staccato notes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legato - (music) in a smooth, connected manner

Etymology

From Italian 'staccato', past participle of 'staccare' (to detach).
