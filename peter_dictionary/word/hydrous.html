hydrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪdrəs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəs / (BrE)
(of a substance) containing water, especially water chemically combined as in a hydrate.

Examples

The small reaction occurring at 337 ` C is probably caused by decomposition of occluded nitrates, and perhaps by a small amount of some hydrous material other than **f.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anhydrous - not containing water, especially as a hydrate

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' meaning 'water' plus the suffix '-ous'.
