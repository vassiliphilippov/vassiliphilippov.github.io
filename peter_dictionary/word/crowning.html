crowning

1. ADJ

/ ˈkraʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
representing the highest or most important achievement or moment; supreme or culminating

Examples

Of course, the crowning event that has dramatically upset the traditional pattern of English religious history was the friendly visit paid by Dr. Fisher, then Anglican Archbishop of Canterbury, to the Vatican last December.

Synset Examples

the crowning accomplishment of his career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supreme - highest in rank or authority
  • Synonym: ultimate - being the best or most extreme example
  • Synonym: culminating - reaching a climax or point of highest development

Etymology

From the verb 'to crown,' originally from Old French 'coroner' and Latin 'coronare' (to crown).
