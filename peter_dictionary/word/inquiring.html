inquiring

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
having a strong desire to know or learn something; showing curiosity or an eagerness to investigate.

Examples

All artistic and mythological representations, therefore, are "imitations of imitations "and are completely superseded by the truth value of "dialectic ", the proper use of the inquiring intellect.

Synset Examples

an inquiring mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curious - eager to know or learn something
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or concern

Etymology

From the verb 'inquire', from Latin 'inquirere' (to seek for, search for).
