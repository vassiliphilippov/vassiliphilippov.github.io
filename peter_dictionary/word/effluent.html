effluent

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛfluənt / (AmE), / ˈɛfluənt / (BrE)
Liquid waste or sewage discharged into a river or the sea.

Examples

Research at Fayette, Missouri on oxidation ponds has shown that the BOD in the treated effluent varied from 30 to 53 mg / l with loadings from 8 to 120 lb BOD / day / acre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewage - waste water and excrement conveyed in sewers
  • Antonym: influent - water or material that flows into a facility

Etymology

From Latin effluere (to flow out).
