conjoined

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɔɪnd / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɔɪnd / (BrE)
joined or connected together; combined as a single entity

Examples

Everywhere within the common pattern, however, one finds local diversity; Greek history and culture were enduringly fertilized, and plagued, by the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors.

Synset Examples

the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors
social order and prosperity, the conjoint aims of government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combined - joined or mixed together
  • Antonym: separate - not joined or connected; divided

Etymology

From Latin 'coniunctus', past participle of 'coniungere' (to join together) via Old French 'conjoindre'.
