labyrinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæbəˌrɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈlæb.ər.ɪnθ / (BrE)
A complicated network of winding passages or paths in which it is easy to get lost; a maze.

Examples

There remained only the delicate task of maneuvering the laws through the labyrinth of Palace politics and making a small number of policy decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maze - A confusing network of paths; something designed to be difficult to navigate.

Etymology

From Greek labyrinthos (labyrinth, maze), later adopted into Latin and then Old French and English.
