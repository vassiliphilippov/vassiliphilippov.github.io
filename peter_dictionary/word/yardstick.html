yardstick

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌstɪk / (BrE)
a standard or principle by which something is judged or compared

Examples

In any case, Dr. Calderone remarks, such problems are a couple's own affair, and can n't always be measured by a general yardstick.

Synset Examples

on what kind of yardstick is he basing his judgment?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or reference point for comparison
  • Synonym: criterion - a standard by which something may be judged
  • Synonym: standard - something established as a rule for measuring or judging

Etymology

Originally from 'yard' (unit of length) + 'stick', referring to a measuring stick one yard long.
