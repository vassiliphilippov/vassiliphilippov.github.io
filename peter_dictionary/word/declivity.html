declivity

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɪv.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / dɪˈklɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A downward slope, especially of the ground.

Examples

The robbers run from the hide-out, take cover in a wooded declivity, and are shot dead by the posse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascent - an upward slope or climb
  • Synonym: descent - a dropping or downward movement; a downward slope

Etymology

From Latin declivitas, from declivis (sloping downward).
