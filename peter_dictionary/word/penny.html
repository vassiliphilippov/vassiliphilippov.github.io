penny

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ˈpɛni / (BrE)
A coin or monetary unit in the United Kingdom and formerly in Ireland, equal to one hundredth of a pound sterling.

Examples

In it was a stone Tibetan Buddha I had picked up in Bombay, and occasionally, to make merit, my wife and I garlanded it with flowers or laid a few pennies in its lap.
One morning, we discovered not only that the pennies were missing from the idol but that a cigarette had been stubbed out in its lap.
- Every penny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a coin of the US or other decimal currencies, equal to one hundredth of a dollar/euro/etc.

Etymology

Old English *penning*, from Germanic origins, related to Old High German *pfennig*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ˈpɛni / (BrE)
A coin worth one-hundredth of the basic unit of currency in many countries, such as a cent in the US or a one-penny coin in the UK.

Examples

There was no time to pick out a penny; I got a coin between my thumb and forefinger, leaned my elbows in a very natural and casual manner on top of the truck cab and flipped my little missile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a coin worth one-hundredth of the basic monetary unit (especially in the US)

Etymology

From Old English *pening*, from Proto-Germanic *panningaz*.
