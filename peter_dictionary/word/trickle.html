trickle

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow slowly in drops or a thin stream.

Examples

Something wet and hot was trickling on her wrists.

Synset Examples

water trickled onto the lawn from the broken hose
reports began to dribble in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribble - flow or fall in drops, especially about liquid
  • Degree Higher: pour - flow rapidly in a steady stream

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *trikken* (to drip or fall in drops).
