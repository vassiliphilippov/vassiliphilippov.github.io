attachment

1. NOUN

/ əˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
a supplementary part or accessory that can be added to a machine or device to perform a particular function

Examples

If you have a planting of half an acre or more you may want to buy a small garden tractor (available for $ 300 to $ 500 with attachments, 1960 prices).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - an additional item that can be added to something else to make it more useful
  • Synonym: add-on - an extra part that can be added to improve or expand something

Etymology

From French 'attachement', from 'attacher' (to attach).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈtatʃmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of affection or fondness for someone or something, especially a person or institution.

Examples

For most Brooklyn College students, college is at once a perpetuation of their ethnic attachments and a breaking away from the cage of neighborhood and family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Antonym: detachment - lack of emotional involvement or interest

Etymology

From Old French 'attachement', from 'attacher' meaning 'to attach'.
