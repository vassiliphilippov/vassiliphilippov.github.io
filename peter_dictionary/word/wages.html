wages

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈweɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
Retribution or reward for actions, especially as punishment for wrongdoing or recompense for virtue, chiefly used in literary, religious, or proverbial contexts.

Examples

The wages of sin is death.
It is the wages of sin, and its sting is the law.

Synset Examples

the wages of sin is death
virtue is its own reward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reward - something given in return for effort or achievement
  • Antonym: punishment - the infliction of a penalty as retribution for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *wages*, plural of *wage*, from Old French *gage* (pledge, guarantee).
