menial

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːniəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːniəl / (BrE)
(of work) not requiring much skill and lacking prestige; typically referring to tasks of a low status, especially domestic work.

Examples

Early in the war it was not uncommon for planters' sons to retain in camp Negro "body servants "to perform the menial chores such as cooking, foraging, cleaning the quarters, shining shoes, and laundering clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humble - of low social, administrative, or economic rank
  • Antonym: skilled - requiring skill or training

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *mesnial* ‘domestic’, from *mesnie* ‘household’.
