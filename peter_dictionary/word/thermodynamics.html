thermodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɝːmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the relations between heat, work, and other forms of energy

Examples

If the mechanics are similar, we can also infer that the thermodynamics will also be similar.

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + dynamics (study of forces and motion).
