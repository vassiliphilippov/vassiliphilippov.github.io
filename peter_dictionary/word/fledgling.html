fledgling

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, organization, or activity) new, young, and lacking experience; just beginning to develop.

Examples

This is when his troubles begin, not to mention a fledgling artist who he hires, and who turns out to have ideas of his own, with particular respect to the hero's sweetheart secretary.

Synset Examples

a fledgling enterprise
a fledgling skier
an unfledged lawyer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexperienced - lacking experience or practice; new to something
  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: experienced - having knowledge or skill from doing or seeing things

Etymology

From the noun 'fledgling', itself from 'fledge' (to acquire feathers for flight) + -ling (diminutive suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a person or organization that is new to a particular activity or field and lacks experience

Examples

By comparison, Fritzie and Laura Andrus were quivering fledglings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: veteran - person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From 'fledge' + '-ling', originally referring to a young bird just acquiring feathers; by extension, a metaphor for someone new.

3. ADJ

/ ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a young bird) having just acquired its flight feathers and typically just learning to fly.

Examples

Dead fledgling birds, their squashed looking nakedness and the odor of decay that clung to the hand when they had been buried in our graveyard in front of the purple flags.

Synset Examples

a fledgling robin

Etymology

From 'fledge' (to acquire feathers necessary for flight) + '-ling' (diminutive suffix).
