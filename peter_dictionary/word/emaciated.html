emaciated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmeɪsiˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈmeɪsiˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Extremely thin, especially as a result of illness, starvation, or lack of nourishment.

Examples

William Beebe reports 26 inches and 2.4 ounces (this snake must have been emaciated) for the length and the weight of a young anaconda from British Guiana.

Synset Examples

emaciated bony hands
a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - very thin, especially from hunger or suffering
  • Synonym: skeletal - so thin as to resemble a skeleton

Etymology

From Latin emaciatus, past participle of emaciare (to make thin), from e- (out) + macies (leanness).
