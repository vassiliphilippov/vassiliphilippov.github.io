explosive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
(of a situation, issue, or temperament) likely to lead to sudden change or violent outburst; unstable or volatile.

Examples

"We always like to keep the ball as much as we can against Denver because they have such an explosive attack ", explained Stram.
Along Wappinger Creek in Dutchess County, past the white church at Fishkill, past Verplanck's Point on the east bank of the Hudson, to the white salt crusted roads of the Long Island Rockaways there was a watching and an activity of preparing for something explosive to happen.
Yet Laos was now one of the most explosive headaches of statesmen around the globe.

Synset Examples

an explosive issue
a volatile situation with troops and rioters eager for a confrontation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volatile - likely to change rapidly and unpredictably, especially for the worse
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established

Etymology

From Latin explosivus (tending to drive out by clapping), from explosus, past participle of explodere.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance, device, emotion, etc.) able or likely to explode, or liable to cause an explosion; liable to cause a sudden, forceful outburst.

Examples

He had a deep voice which was full of good food she had cooked, and good humor; an explosive laugh which always carried everything before it.
She was like charcoal, he thought - dark, opaque, explosive.

Synset Examples

an explosive device
explosive gas
explosive force
explosive violence
an explosive temper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volatile - liable to change rapidly and unpredictably, especially for the worse

Etymology

From Latin explosivus ‘fit for driving off by clapping’, from explodere (to drive out by clapping).
