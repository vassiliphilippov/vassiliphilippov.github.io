interfering

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) tending to involve oneself in affairs that do not concern them, especially in an annoying or unwelcome way.

Examples

It should be possible to free carbon tetrachloride of any interfering substances by the usual purification methods followed by prechlorination prior to addition of radioactive chlorine.

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meddlesome - fond of interfering with others' affairs
  • Synonym: officious - assertively interfering in a domineering way
  • Antonym: hands-off - not interfering; deliberately refraining from involvement

Etymology

Present participle of 'interfere', from French 'interférer', from Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'ferire' (to strike).
