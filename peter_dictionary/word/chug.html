chug

1. VERB

/ tʃʌɡ / (AmE), / tʃʌɡ / (BrE)
(of an engine or machine) to make a series of dull, explosive sounds, typically as it moves or operates steadily.

Examples

He was torn between the excitement in the sun inflamed waters and a little engine chugging northward on the Monticello Branch.

Synset Examples

the engine chugged down the street

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: purr - (of an engine) to make a low, continuous, soft sound
  • Degree Higher: roar - (of an engine or machine) to make a loud, deep, continuous sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound produced by engines or similar machinery.
