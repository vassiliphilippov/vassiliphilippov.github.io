screenplay

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrinˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈskriːnˌpleɪ / (BrE)
The script of a film, including the dialogue and descriptions of characters, settings, and action.

Examples

Loew's Theater presents "Where the Boys are ", an MGM picture produced by Joe Pasternak and directed by Henry Levin from a screenplay by George Wells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - The written text of a play, broadcast, or speech

Etymology

Formed from 'screen' + 'play', first used in the early 20th century with the rise of cinema.
