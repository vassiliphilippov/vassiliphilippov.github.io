snout

1. NOUN

/ snaʊt / (AmE), / snaʊt / (BrE)
the long, projecting nose or muzzle of an animal, especially one that is prominent or elongated

Examples

One female, collected on an island off the coast of Nicaragua, was gravid and measured 4 feet 8 inches from snout to vent (her tail should be between 6 and 7 inches long).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muzzle - the projecting part of the face, including the nose and mouth, of an animal such as a dog or horse
  • Degree Lower: beak - the hard, pointed mouthpart of a bird

Etymology

Middle English, possibly from Old English *snūt* or of Low German origin, related to Dutch *snuit* (nose, snout).
