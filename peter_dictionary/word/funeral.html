funeral

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuː.nɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈfjuː.nər.əl / (BrE)
a ceremony, usually religious, held to commemorate and dispose of a dead person's body by burial or cremation

Examples

They held the funeral the next morning from the crossroads church and buried the little box in the quiet family plot.
He claimed to be visiting the waterfront saloon at the crossroads to play cards and drink with his cronies, but Kate had not smelled brandy on him since Mrs. Lattimer's funeral.
The boyhood summers preceding their uncle's funeral might never have been.
Did you go to their funeral "?
Thus, you avoid headaches when an employee wants off for his fourth cousin's funeral.

Synset Examples

hundreds of people attended his funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burial - the act or process of putting a dead body into the ground
  • Synonym: cremation - the process of burning a dead body
  • Antonym: wedding - a ceremony for joining a couple in marriage (as a contrasting type of ceremony)

Etymology

From Latin 'funus' (funeral, burial, death rites), via Old French 'funerailles'.
