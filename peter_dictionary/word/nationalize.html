nationalize

1. VERB

/ ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring a private business, industry, or asset under state ownership or control

Examples

One result was to nationalize much that had been regarded as the law of nations.
In 1960 Ceylon nationalized its sectarian - preponderantly Christian - schools, to the rejoicing of most of its 7000000 Buddhists and the lament of its 800000 Roman Catholics.

Synset Examples

Mitterand nationalized the banks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: privatize - transfer from state to private ownership

Etymology

From 'nation' + '-alize', formed on the pattern of similar verbs indicating conversion to state control.
