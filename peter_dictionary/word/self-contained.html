self-contained

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf.kənˈteɪnd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kənˈteɪnd / (BrE)
(of a thing) complete and independent, not needing or depending on others for support or functioning.

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.
He could not see objects as unified, self-contained, and organized figures, as a person does with normal vision.

Synset Examples

the university is like a self-contained city with shops and all amenities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - free from outside control; self-governing

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'contained'; 'contained' from Latin 'continere' (to hold together, enclose).
