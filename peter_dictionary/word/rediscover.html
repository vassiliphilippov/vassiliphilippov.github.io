rediscover

1. VERB

/ ˌriːdɪˈskʌvər / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈskʌvə / (BrE)
To find or realize something again that was previously known, understood, or experienced, often after having forgotten or ignored it.

Examples

Only when the newest Mr. America or Mr. Universe rediscovers them and puts them into practice are we reacquainted with them and once again see how effective they really are.
Rachel, observing, would say, "He has to rediscover his own capacity.

Synset Examples

I rediscovered the books that I enjoyed as a child

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: discover - find or become aware of (something) for the first time

Etymology

From re- (again) + discover; modeled after 'discover.'
