subvert

1. VERB

/ səbˈvɝt / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to try to destroy or undermine the power and authority of an established system, government, or institution

Examples

Despite the lip service paid by local governments, the anti-secrecy statutes have been continuously subverted by reservations and rationalizations.

Synset Examples

The Czar was overthrown
subvert the ruling class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overthrow - to remove forcefully from power
  • Antonym: support - to give assistance or uphold, often to a government or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'subvertere' (to turn upside down), from 'sub-' (from below) + 'vertere' (to turn).
