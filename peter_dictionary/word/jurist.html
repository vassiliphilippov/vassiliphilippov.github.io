jurist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊrɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊərɪst / (BrE)
A person with expert knowledge of law; especially, a legal scholar or an authority on law.

Examples

A Virginia judge a while back cited a Roman jurist to the effect that ten years might be a reasonable length of time for such a change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - an official with authority to decide legal cases
  • Synonym: lawyer - a professional who practices or studies law

Etymology

From Latin *iūrāta* (oath), from *iūs* (law, right).
