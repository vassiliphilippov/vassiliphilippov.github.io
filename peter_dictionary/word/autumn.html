autumn

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.təm / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təm / (BrE)
The season of the year between summer and winter, when leaves fall from deciduous trees; chiefly identified as the period from September to November in the northern hemisphere and from March to May in the southern hemisphere.

Examples

I must add at once that these animals are what we call "queens ", young females that have mated in the previous summer or autumn.
But the liaison successfully started in the last days of autumn was now languishing.

Synset Examples

in the fall of 1973

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - the season between summer and winter; chiefly American English term for autumn
  • Antonym: spring - the season after winter and before summer
  • Antonym: summer - the warmest season of the year, preceding autumn
  • Antonym: winter - the coldest season of the year, following autumn

Etymology

From Latin 'autumnus', meaning 'autumn' or 'fall'.
