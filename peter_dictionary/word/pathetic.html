pathetic

1. ADJ

/ pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
arousing feelings of pity or sadness; deserving or inciting pity

Examples

The pathetic evidence is no longer before us; and we are now so fatigued in body and mind that feeling is, as we say, quite dead.

Synset Examples

a hapless victim
miserable victims of war
the shabby room struck her as extraordinarily pathetic
piteous appeals for help
pitiable homeless children
a pitiful fate
Oh, you poor thing
his poor distorted limbs
a wretched life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piteous - arousing pity, especially by being sad or distressing
  • Synonym: pitiful - deserving or arousing pity
  • Synonym: wretched - in a very unhappy or unfortunate state
  • Antonym: admirable - deserving praise and respect

Etymology

From Greek *pathētikos* (liable to suffering), from *pathos* (suffering, feeling).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
arousing feelings of both pity and contempt, often due to inadequacy, weakness, or failure.

Examples

The wife's attempt at control, these psychologists contend, is sometimes merely a pathetic effort to compel her husband to pay as much attention to her as he does to his job.

Synset Examples

their efforts were pathetic
pitiable lack of character
pitiful exhibition of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiful - arousing pity, often through inadequacy or sadness
  • Synonym: pitiable - deserving or arousing pity
  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable
  • Antonym: admirable - arousing respect or approval

Etymology

From Greek pathētikos (capable of feeling, sensitive), from pathos (feeling).
