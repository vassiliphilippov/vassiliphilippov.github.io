transact

1. VERB

/ trænˈsækt / (AmE), / trænˈzækt / (BrE)
to conduct or carry out business, negotiations, or other dealings

Examples

"Litigants and witnesses were put to the expense and inconvenience of going long distances to transact business; public money spent; justice delayed; nothing accomplished, and the whole distribution of justice in this county seems to be an absolute farce ".
The tragedy is that it will not be able to transact that business in any responsible manner.
Because I have to do business.

Synset Examples

transact with foreign governments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiate - discuss to reach an agreement
  • Synonym: conduct - organize and carry out (an activity or business)

Etymology

From Latin 'transactus', past participle of 'transigere' (to drive through, accomplish).
