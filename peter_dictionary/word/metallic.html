metallic

1. ADJ

/ məˈtælɪk / (AmE), / məˈtælɪk / (BrE)
Containing, made of, or having the characteristics of metal.

Examples

The rows of animals and birds, in particular, suggest awareness of Oriental animal friezes, transmitted perhaps via Syrian silver bowls and textiles, but the specific forms of these rows on local vases and metal products are nonetheless Greek.

Synset Examples

a metallic compound
metallic luster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metal - a solid material that is typically hard, shiny, and a good conductor of heat and electricity

Etymology

From Latin metallicus, from Greek metallikos (of metal).

2. ADJ

/ məˈtælɪk / (AmE), / məˈtælɪk / (BrE)
having the characteristics of metal, such as being shiny, hard, or containing metal; or made from or resembling metal

Examples

On these posts the gates swung open with a squeak and shut with a metallic clang.
Surface temperature gradients have been found to exist in liquid helium baths contained in 15 - and 25 - liter metallic storage dewars.

Synset Examples

a metallic compound
metallic luster
the strange metallic note of the meadow lark, suggesting the clash of vibrant blades

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metal - a solid material which is typically hard, shiny, and malleable
  • Antonym: nonmetallic - not containing or resembling metal

Etymology

From French métallique, from Latin metallicus, from Greek metallikos (of metal).
