Homer

1. PROPN

/ ˈhoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmə / (BrE)
An ancient Greek epic poet, traditionally said to be the author of the Iliad and the Odyssey, who is thought to have lived around the 8th century BC.

Examples

The landscape of Greek history broadens widely, and rather abruptly, in the eighth century B.C., the age of Homer's "rosy fingered Dawn ".

Etymology

From Greek Ὅμηρος (Hómēros).
