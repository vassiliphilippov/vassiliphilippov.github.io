serviette

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.viˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.viˈet / (BrE)
a small piece of cloth or paper used at a meal for wiping the mouth and fingers or to protect clothing; a napkin

Examples

A colossal campaign opened to sell more sexual organs of plants and Mrs. Joseph Shadow of Greatness Douglas was quoted as saying: "I would no more sit down without flowers on my table than without serviettes ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napkin - a small piece of table linen used at meals

Etymology

From French 'serviette' (diminutive of 'servir', to serve).
