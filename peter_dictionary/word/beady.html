beady

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːdi / (AmE), / ˈbiːdi / (BrE)
(of a person's eyes) small, round, and shining, often suggestive of alertness or suspicion.

Examples

He rocked back in the chair, knee locked against stomach, his beady eyes fixed on Matson.

Synset Examples

bright beady eyes
black buttony eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttony - resembling a button; small, round, and shiny (esp. of eyes)

Etymology

From 'bead' + -y; 'bead' from Old English 'bēad' meaning 'prayer bead'.
