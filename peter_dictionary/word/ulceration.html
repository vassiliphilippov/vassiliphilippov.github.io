ulceration

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌl.sɚˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌl.sərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A lesion on the skin or a mucous membrane characterized by inflammation, tissue loss, and often the formation of pus.

Examples

A section of the colon revealed intense hyperemia and extensive focal ulcerations of the mucosa, associated with much fibrin and many neutrophils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulcer - an open sore or lesion on the skin or mucous membrane

Etymology

From Latin ulcērātiō (an ulcerating, a sore), from ulcus (ulcer).
