contaminate

1. VERB

/ kənˈtæməˌneɪt / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make something impure by adding harmful or undesirable substances

Examples

Since f is ferromagnetic, we felt that any results obtained from the magnetically contaminated** **f would be suspect.
They 're contaminating the earth's atmosphere including their own via mighty megaton bombs but their own peasants still do n't know about it!
The present attempts of the politicians to contaminate ordinary Britons shows that this British common sense is unwilling to pull somebody else's chestnuts out of the fire by new military adventures ".
Its primary advantage is that it is a moral choice; one which, should it fail, will not have contaminated the conscience.

Synset Examples

The industrial wastes polluted the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollute - to make (land, air, water, etc.) dirty or harmful by adding waste material
  • Antonym: purify - to remove contaminants from, to make pure

Etymology

From Latin 'contaminatus', past participle of 'contaminare' (to pollute, defile).

2. VERB

/ kənˈtæm.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / kənˈtæm.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to make something radioactive by introducing radioactive material

Examples

The second choice, full testing, has become even more risky just because the current Soviet tests have already dangerously contaminated the atmosphere.

Synset Examples

Don't drink the water--it's contaminated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollute - to make impure or harmful by introducing contaminants
  • Antonym: purify - to remove impurities or contaminants

Etymology

From Latin contaminare (to make impure), from con- (together) + tangere (to touch).
