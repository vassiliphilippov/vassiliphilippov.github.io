mingle

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to combine or mix different things or substances together, or to cause them to become mixed; or to move among people and talk to different individuals at a social gathering

Examples

Al's Little Cafe was small, dark, narrow, and filled with the mingled scent of beer, tobacco smoke, and Italian cooking.
He went on to the next building and found what he expected - the mingled cooking aromas of a public vestibule.
"Usually at this point a man is withdrawn from society and one of my jobs is to see that he relearns to mingle with his fellow men ", Mrs. Marr explained.
Using a wide stage resourcefully he mingled music and dance with Shakespeare's words in a spirited mixture.

Synset Examples

resourcefully he mingled music and dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend two or more things
  • Synonym: blend - to mix smoothly and inseparably together
  • Antonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart

Etymology

From Middle English menglen, frequentative of 'mengen', from Old English mengan (to mix), related to Old Norse 'mengja'.
