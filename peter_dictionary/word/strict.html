strict

1. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
(of rules or standards) stringently enforced; requiring rigid compliance or observance.

Examples

She was told by the manservant who opened the door that his lordship was engaged on work from which he had left strict orders he was not to be disturbed.
"And you know, Angelo, Pretty, he always keeps it a strict cash basis, like they say "-

Synset Examples

hard-and-fast rules

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lenient - not strict; permissive or tolerant in enforcing rules

Etymology

From Latin 'strictus' (drawn tight), past participle of 'stringere' (to draw tight).

2. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
Demanding that rules or standards are closely and exactly followed; allowing no deviation or relaxation.

Examples

You 've already sent your daughter to Miss X's select academy for girls and your son to Mr. Y's select academy for boys, and you can be as liberal as you please with strict impunity.
They give strict interpretation to William James' statement that "Every idea that enters the mind tends to express itself ".
Or a Protestant, or one of those amusing dogmatic atheists, or a strict orthodox Communist.

Synset Examples

rigorous application of the law
a strict vegetarian

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lenient - permissive, not strict or rigorous

Etymology

From Latin 'strictus', past participle of 'stringere' (to draw tight, bind).

3. ADJ

/ strɪkt / (AmE), / strɪkt / (BrE)
demanding that rules or requirements are followed exactly and severely, with little or no allowance for deviation or leniency.

Examples

I must have strict assurance from Corleone.

Synset Examples

an exacting instructor
a stern disciplinarian
strict standards

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lenient - permissive; allowing exceptions to rules
  • Synonym: stern - serious and unrelenting in discipline or attitude
  • Synonym: rigorous - extremely thorough or accurate; strict

Etymology

From Latin strictus, past participle of stringere (to draw tight).
