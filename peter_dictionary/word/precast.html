precast

1. ADJ

/ ˈpriˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌkɑːst / (BrE)
(of structural components) cast in their final shape at a location other than the place of installation, especially referring to concrete or metal members made off-site and transported for assembly.

Examples

Pre shaped corrugated metal sections or pre-cast concrete can be used for shelters either above or below ground.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cast-in-place - concrete poured and set at the construction site

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'cast' (to shape in a mold).
