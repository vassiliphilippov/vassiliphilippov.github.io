buffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfər / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə / (BrE)
A solution or substance that resists changes in pH when an acid or base is added, commonly used in chemistry and biology to maintain a stable pH environment.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌfɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə / (BrE)

Etymology

From Latin *buffare* (variant root meaning 'to soften', later applied to neutralizing agents in chemistry).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə / (BrE)
a device or mechanism that absorbs shock or reduces impact, often by providing a cushion between two objects

Examples

This witness has had no buffer between himself and Michael Corleone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cushion - something used to soften or absorb impact

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French buffe (a blow, a puff of wind) or from the verb 'to buff' (to soften a blow).
