joust

1. NOUN

/ dʒaʊst / (AmE), / dʒaʊst / (BrE)
a medieval contest in which two knights on horseback fought with lances

Examples

Hindemith's joust with Weber tunes was a considerably more serious misfortune, for it demands translucent textures, buoyant rhythms, and astringent wit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tournament - a series of contests or battles, especially of knights

Etymology

From Old French 'joster', from Latin 'juxtare' (to approach, to be next to).
