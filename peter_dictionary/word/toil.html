toil

1. VERB

/ tɔɪl / (AmE), / tɔɪl / (BrE)
to work extremely hard, typically over a long period

Examples

He also spoke of "the wealth piled by the bondsman's two hundred and fifty years in unrequited toil ".

Synset Examples

She was digging away at her math homework
Lexicographers drudge all day long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor - work, especially hard, physical work
  • Synonym: drudge - to do hard, menial, or tedious work
  • Antonym: relax - to rest or do something enjoyable after work or effort

Etymology

From Old French 'toiler' (struggle), probably from Latin 'tudiculare' (to crush), from 'tudicula' (machine for crushing olives).
