whatever

1. DET

/ wʌˈtɛvɚ / (AmE), / wɒˈtɛvə / (BrE)
Used to refer to any one or every one of a group, regardless of which; anything or everything that.

Examples

Whatever mistakes I've made, I've paid for them and then some.

Synset Examples

give me any peaches you don't want
not any milk is left
any child would know that
pick any card
any day now

at twilight or any other time
beyond any doubt
need any help we can get
give me whatever peaches you don't want
no milk whatsoever is left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: any - used to refer to one or some of a thing or number of things, no matter how much or how many

Etymology

From Middle English 'what ever' (whatsoever), combining 'what' + 'ever'.
