improved

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
made better, more desirable, or more valuable, especially by being prepared for use, marketing, or greater usefulness.

Examples

The rangy, Albany (Cal.) native, a surprise slugging sensation for the Flock last year as well as a defensive whiz, set "improved fielding "as his 1961 goal.
"Is his appetite improved "?
Have you permitted it to become a giveaway program rather than one that has the goal of improved employee morale and, consequently, increased productivity?

Synset Examples

new houses are springing up on an improved tract of land near the river
an improved breed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enhanced - increased or intensified in value or quality
  • Antonym: deteriorated - made worse in quality, value, or condition
  • Synonym: upgraded - raised to a higher standard

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'improve', which comes from Anglo-French 'emprower' (to make better).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
having become or been made better in quality or condition

Examples

Do you measure its relation to reduced absenteeism, turnover, accidents, and grievances, and to improved quality and output?
I am recommending additional acquisitions of the improved version of the B-52 (the B-52 H with the new turbofan engine) and procurement of the B-58 supersonic medium bomber, together with the supporting refueling tankers in each case.
New and improved Joker products!

Synset Examples

was proud of his improved grades
an improved viewfinder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: better - of a higher standard or quality
  • Antonym: worsened - become or made worse in quality or condition

Etymology

Past participle of 'improve', from Anglo-Norman 'emprouwer' (to turn to profit).
