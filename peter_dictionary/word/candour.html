candour

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændɚ / (AmE), / ˈkændə / (BrE)
The quality of being open, honest, and straightforward in speech or expression.

Examples

O' Banion was a complex and frightening man, whose bright blue eyes stared with a kind of frozen candour into others'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankness - quality of being open and honest
  • Synonym: openness - quality of being honest and accessible
  • Antonym: disingenuousness - quality of being insincere or deceitful
  • Antonym: insincerity - quality of not expressing genuine feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'candor' (whiteness, brightness, openness) via Old French and Middle English.
