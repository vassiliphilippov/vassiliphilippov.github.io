drone

1. NOUN

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
a male bee in a colony of social bees, such as honeybees, whose only function is to mate with the queen and which does not have a stinger

Examples

Simultaneously, males or drones are produced, mostly from the unfertilized eggs of workers, although a few may be produced by the queen.
The young queens and drones leave the nest and mate, and after a short period of freedom, the fertilized young queens will begin to dig in for the winter.

Etymology

Old English drān (male bee), related to Old High German treno (drone), from Proto-Germanic *dranan.

2. NOUN

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
a continuous, low, monotonous sound or tone that does not change in pitch

Examples

The lazy sing-song was spaced in time like the drone of a bumble-bee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotone - a sound or voice that does not vary in pitch

Etymology

From Old English 'drān', meaning 'male bee', later extended to monotonous sound (from the buzzing of a male bee).
