confirm

1. VERB

/ kənˈfɜrm / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to establish the truth or validity of something by providing new evidence or facts; to strengthen an idea, belief, or opinion through additional proof

Examples

An enthusiastic audience confirmed the "live "character of the hour, and provided the interaction between musician and hearer which almost always seems to improve the quality of performance.
Although the * * f calculation is obvious by analogy with that for gravitational field and osmotic pressure, it is interesting to confirm it by a method which can be generalized to include related effects.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true, accurate, or justified
  • Antonym: refute - to prove that something is incorrect or false

Etymology

From Latin confirmare, from con- (together) + firmare (to make firm).

2. VERB

/ kənˈfɝːm / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːm / (BrE)

Examples

Etymology

From Latin confirmare (to strengthen, establish), from con- (together) + firmare (to make firm).

3. VERB

/ kənˈfɝːm / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to formally approve someone for a position or office, especially by an official body such as a legislature

Examples
