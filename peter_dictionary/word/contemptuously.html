contemptuously

1. ADV

/ kənˈtɛmptʃuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptjuəsli / (BrE)
In a manner showing disdain or lack of respect; scornfully.

Examples

Alastor was published only to be savagely attacked, contemptuously ignored.

Synset Examples

she spoke of him contemptuously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectfully - in a manner showing respect
  • Synonym: scornfully - in a way that shows scorn; synonym for 'contemptuously'

Etymology

From 'contemptuous' + '-ly'. 'Contemptuous' from Latin 'contemptuosus' (full of contempt).
