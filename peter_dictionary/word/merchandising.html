merchandising

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tʃən.daɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or business of selling goods to customers, especially by promoting and displaying them effectively

Examples

Display merchandising, backed by pre selling through advertising and promotion, will continue to make strides in the sixties.
The "distributive education "program operates in a similar way, with arrangements between the school and employers in merchandising fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retailing - the sale of goods to the public in small quantities
  • Degree Lower: wholesaling - the sale of goods in large quantities, usually to retailers

Etymology

From 'merchandise' (goods for sale), from Old French 'marchandise', from 'marchand' (merchant).
