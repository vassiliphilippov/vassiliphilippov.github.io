Ancistrodon

1. NOUN

/ ænˈsɪstrədɑn / (AmE), / ænˈsɪstrədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of venomous pit vipers found in North America and Asia, including copperheads and cottonmouths.

Examples

These three were much alike: lined snake (Tropidoclonion), one year and nine months; red-bellied snake (Storeria), two years; cottonmouth (Ancistrodon), two years.

Etymology

From Greek ankistron (hook) + odōn (tooth).
