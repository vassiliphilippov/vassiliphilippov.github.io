perfectionist

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
A person who refuses to accept any standard short of perfection; someone who strives for flawlessness and sets excessively high performance standards.

Examples

Did men become perfectionists as they grew older, polishing, polishing, reluctant to let go?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealist - a person guided more by ideals than by practical considerations
  • Antonym: pragmatist - a person guided more by practical considerations than by ideals

Etymology

From perfection + -ist, meaning one who strives for or believes in perfection.
