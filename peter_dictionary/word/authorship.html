authorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔθərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or fact of being the writer of a book, article, or other written work; the origin or creation of a written work.

Examples

And listening to such a conversation one morning while taking a cup of chocolate in a cafe, Rousseau found himself bathed in perspiration, trembling lest his authorship become known, and at the same time dreaming of the startling effect he would make if he should proclaim himself suddenly as the composer.

Synset Examples

writing was a form of therapy for him
it was a matter of disputed authorship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writing - the activity or art of composing text

Etymology

From author + -ship, modeled after other state nouns (e.g. friendship).
