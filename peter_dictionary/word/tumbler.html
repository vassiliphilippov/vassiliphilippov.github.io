tumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmblər / (AmE), / ˈtʌmblə / (BrE)
A gymnast who performs rolls, somersaults, and twisting movements, especially on the floor.

Examples

Even if gymnastics are not the ultimate goal, the good tumbler will be a better dancer, a better athlete, and a human being with a greater margin of safety in any activity.

Etymology

From Middle English tumbler (acrobat), from tumblen (to tumble) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌm.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.blə / (BrE)
A drinking glass with a flat bottom and no handle or stem, typically used for serving water, whisky, or other drinks.

Examples

Hans poured some in a tumbler.

Etymology

Originally referred to a glass with a rounded base that would 'tumble' over. From Middle English *tomblen*, related to 'tumble'.
