appealing

1. ADJ

/ əˈpilɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
attractive or interesting; able to draw positive attention or interest

Examples

It has a fast pace, excellent music, expert direction, and not only a good comedian, but an appealing person in his own right, Mr. Berman.
The assisting musicians from the Vienna Octet are somewhat lacking in expertise, but their contribution is rustic and appealing.
Your competition is now proportionately greater - you are competing not only against manufacturers in the same field but also against a vast array of manufacturers of other appealing consumer products.

Synset Examples

He added an appealing and memorable figure to popular American mythology
an appealing sense of humor
the idea of having enough money to retire at fifty is very appealing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or interesting
  • Synonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses
  • Synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in manner or appearance

Etymology

From appeal + -ing, originally meaning 'making an appeal', later 'attractive'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈpiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
Attracting interest or sympathy; evoking a favorable emotional response, especially in relation to characters or personalities in literature, drama, or real life.

Examples

He dropped his earlier and delightful hamming, which is about the only way to handle the old war horse called "Charley's Aunt ", and let himself go with as an appealing an "Amy "as anybody could ask.

Synset Examples

the sympathetic characters in the play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathetic - evoking compassion or understanding
  • Synonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses
  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or not evoking sympathy

Etymology

From the verb 'appeal' (to attract or request), based on Old French *apeler* ‘to call’ (see appeal).
