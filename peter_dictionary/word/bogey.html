bogey

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
(chiefly British) An evil spirit or imaginary being used to frighten children; a bugbear.

Examples

Demagogues of this sort found communist bogeys lurking behind any new idea that would run counter to stereotyped notions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugbear - an imaginary being invoked to frighten children; source of irrational fear
  • Synonym: bogeyman - a mythical evil being used to frighten children

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'bugge' (frightening specter), source also of 'bugbear'.

2. VERB

/ ˈboʊ.ɡi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.ɡi / (BrE)
(of a golfer) to score one stroke over par on a particular hole.

Examples

He bogeyed the 10 th.
He bogeyed the 15 th by missing a short putt and finally scrambled through the last three holes without further mishap for a 2 - over-par 74 and a 72 - hole total of 280.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: par - the standardized number of strokes in which a golfer is expected to complete a hole
  • Degree Higher: birdie - (in golf) to score one stroke under par on a hole

Etymology

Originally from Scots bogey, a specter; the golfing use developed in the late 19th century, referring to the 'bogey score,' once meaning the score a good player might be expected to make on a hole.
