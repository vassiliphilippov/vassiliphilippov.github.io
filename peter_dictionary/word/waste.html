waste

1. NOUN

/ weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
The use of something valuable in a way that is careless or unnecessary, so that it is not used effectively or is lost.

Examples

The Lenin tomb is obviously adequate for double occupancy, Moscow is a crowded city, and the creed of communism deplores waste.
There's a lot of waste going on here.
These factors can make the difference between waste and efficiency in any benefit program.
Vending machines can alleviate the long hike to the cafeteria during the break with resulting waste of production time.
So you go on and stamp your form, sonny, and stop wasting my time.

Synset Examples

if the effort brings no compensating gain it is a waste
mindless dissipation of natural resources

Related Terms

  • Antonym: economy - careful management or use of resources
  • Antonym: conservation - careful preservation or protection of something

Etymology

From Old French *waster*, from Latin *vastare* (to lay waste).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbage - wasted or spoiled food and other refuse, as from households or businesses
  • Antonym: resource - a source or supply that can be used when needed; the opposite of 'waste' in the context of value or utility

Etymology
