resuspension

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.səˈspɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.sʌsˈpɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making previously settled particles become suspended again in a fluid

Examples

The increase in oxygen uptake rates from 1.2 to 2.6 mg / l / hr which followed an increase in rotor speed was believed to be related to resuspension of solids which had settled at the lower rotor speeds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: suspension - the state in which solid particles are dispersed in a liquid without dissolving

Etymology

From re- (again) + suspension, based on Latin 'suspendere' (to hang up).
