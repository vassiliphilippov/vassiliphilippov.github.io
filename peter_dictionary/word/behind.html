behind

1. ADV

/ bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
In or to the place or condition that someone or something has departed from; left after someone or something has gone.

Examples

Remember what I said about going out to get anybody left behind?
"To Be Left Behind "was printed at the top in Miss Ada; fine hand.
In one cartoon a family is shown outside a theater with the head of the family addressing the doorman: "Excuse me, but when we came out we found that we had left my daughter's handbag and my wife's behind ".

Synset Examples

when he died he left much unfinished work behind
left a large family behind
the children left their books behind
he took off with a squeal of tires and left the other cars far behind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ahead - in a position further forward, especially in progress or movement

Etymology

From Old English 'behindan', from 'be-' (by) + 'hindan' (from behind, at the rear).

2. ADV

/ bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
at or toward the rear of a place or object; in a position further back than someone or something else

Examples

His eyes now fixed on White's solid figure, Warren could hear behind him the tread of another.
Her hat had come off and fallen behind her shoulders, held by the string, and he could see her face more clearly than he had at any time before.
To port was a point 200 feet high rising behind to a precipice of 2000 feet.
And so, after a flight southeast to Savannakhet, we found ourselves bouncing along in a Jeep right behind the Land-Rover of Prince Boun Oum of Champassak, a tall man of Churchillian mien in a bush jacket and a ten-gallon hat from Texas.
From behind, he had put his arms on her shoulders, turned her around, and pressed her to him, so close she could n't breathe.

Synset Examples

he followed behind
seen from behind, the house is more imposing than it is from the front
the final runners were far behind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in front - positioned ahead or at the front

Etymology

From Old English be hindan (‘at the back, in the rear’), from be (‘by, at’) + hindan (‘from behind’).

3. NOUN

/ bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhɑːɪnd / (BrE)
the fleshy part of the human body that you sit on; the buttocks (informal and euphemistic)

Examples

Water from the snow and water from the towels had run off the kid to the table where the dough was, and the dough was turning pasty, sticking to the kid's back and behind.

Synset Examples

he deserves a good kick in the butt
are you going to sit on your fanny and do nothing?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttocks - the two round fleshy parts of the human body forming the lower rear
  • Synonym: rear end - informal term for the buttocks
  • Synonym: bottom - the part of the body you sit on

Etymology

From Middle English behinde, from Old English behindan ('at the back'). Noun usage by extension from the adverb meaning 'at the back'.

4. ADJ

/ bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
having a lower score or position in a contest, competition, or comparison; not ahead

Examples

The evidence suggests that foreign peoples believe the United States is weaker than the Soviet Union, and is bound to fall still further behind in the years ahead.

Synset Examples

behind by two points
the 8th inning found the home team trailing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ahead - having a higher score or position
