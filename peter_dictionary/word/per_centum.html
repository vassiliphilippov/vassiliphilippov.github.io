per centum

1. NOUN

/ pər ˈsɛntəm / (AmE), / pə ˈsɛntəm / (BrE)
A proportion in relation to a whole, especially out of one hundred; percentage (used chiefly in legal or formal contexts).

Examples

Written evidence that the claimant and any such attorney have agreed to the amount of the attorney's fees shall be conclusive upon the Commission: Provided, however, That the total amount of the fees so agreed upon does not exceed 10 per centum of the total amount paid pursuant to the award.
There shall be deducted from the amount of each payment made pursuant to subsection (c) of section 8, as reimbursement for the expenses incurred by the United States, an amount equal to 5 per centum of such payment.
to make additional payment of not to exceed 25 per centum of the unpaid principal of awards in the principal amount of more than $ 1000;

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percentage - a rate, number, or amount in each hundred
  • Synonym: percent - one part in every hundred (common form)

Etymology

From Latin 'per centum' meaning 'by the hundred'.

2. NOUN

/ pər ˈsɛntəm / (AmE), / pə ˈsɛntəm / (BrE)
A proportion or rate in relation to a whole, typically expressed as a number out of one hundred; percentage.

Examples

Driving new Cadillacs, paying 50 per cent on a bet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percentage - a proportion or rate per hundred
  • Synonym: percent - in or for every hundred; one part in every hundred

Etymology

From Latin per centum, meaning 'by the hundred.'
