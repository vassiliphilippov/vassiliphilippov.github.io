delay

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
the act of making something happen later than planned or expected; a period when something does not happen as soon as expected

Examples

Rep. Wesley Roberts of Seminole, sponsor of the poll idea, said that further delay in the committee can kill the bill.
Such an indicator, or indicators, are needed as means of recognizing specific periods of delay in skeletal developmental progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the action of delaying something; deferral
  • Antonym: expedition - quick and efficient action; the opposite of delay

Etymology

From Old French ‘delay’, from ‘delayer’ (to delay), from Latin ‘dilatare’ (to defer, put off).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
to cause something to happen at a later time than planned or expected; to make something or someone late or slow

Examples

Their flight was delayed, Dunn said, when a boarding ramp inflicted some minor damage to the wing of the plane.
This cooling does not change the avocado in any way, it just delays the natural softening of the fruit until a grove like temperature (room temperature) is restored.
Color was delayed until 1935, the wide screen until the early fifties.
"Litigants and witnesses were put to the expense and inconvenience of going long distances to transact business; public money spent; justice delayed; nothing accomplished, and the whole distribution of justice in this county seems to be an absolute farce ".
Black would have little trouble getting out, but it might delay him a few minutes.

Synset Examples

Traffic was delayed by the bad weather
she delayed the work that she didn't want to perform

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to arrange for something to happen at a later time
  • Antonym: expedite - to make something happen sooner or faster
  • Antonym: hasten - to cause something to happen sooner

Etymology

From Old French *delayer*, from Latin *dilatare* (to put off, defer).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to arrange for something to happen at a later time; to delay
  • Antonym: expedite - to make something happen sooner or more quickly

5. NOUN

/ dɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
a period of time by which something is postponed or made late

Examples

The Providence Daily Post thought that there were probably good reasons for the haste in which the trial was being conducted and that the only thing gained by a delay would be calmer feelings.
There will of course be times for delay and inaction.

Synset Examples

he ordered a hold in the action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - action of delaying something; period of being postponed
  • Antonym: expedition - speed or promptness in action; doing something quickly

Etymology

From Old French *delai* (delay, postponement), from *delaier* (to delay), based on Latin components: *de-* (away) + *lacere* (to let).
