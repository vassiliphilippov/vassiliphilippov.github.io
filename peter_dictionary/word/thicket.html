thicket

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪkət / (AmE), / ˈθɪkɪt / (BrE)
A dense group of bushes or small trees growing closely together.

Examples

Below in the open bay facing Manhattan was Staten Island, gritty with clam shells and mud flats behind which nested farms, cattle barns, and berry thickets.
But he had found all of the thickets and points of cover deserted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copse - a small group of trees
  • Synonym: grove - a small group of trees without underbrush

Etymology

From Old English 'þiccet', diminutive of 'þicce' (thick).
