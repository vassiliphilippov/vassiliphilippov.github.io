motor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A machine that converts forms of energy, such as electricity or fuel, into mechanical energy to produce motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine - machine that converts energy into mechanical work (especially via combustion)

Etymology

From Latin 'motor' (mover), from 'movēre' (to move).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tə / (BrE)
(of nerves or pathways) conveying impulses from the central nervous system to muscles, causing movement.

Examples

Moreover, the dominance in parasympathetic action (with reciprocal inhibition of the sympathetic) at the hypothalamic level induces, by its peripheral action, the autonomic symptoms of sleep and, by its action on the cortex, a lessening in the reactivity of the sensory and motor apparatus of the somatic nervous system.

Synset Examples

motor nerves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensory - (of nerves) conveying information from sense organs toward the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin 'motor' (mover), from 'movēre' (to move).

3. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
to travel or be transported in a motor vehicle, especially for leisure or a journey

Examples

Vernon was serviceable on the botanical field trips, but he could arrange no schedule with the cooks, and he was glad when the trips dropped off, and the botanists began to motor out by themselves.

Synset Examples

We drove to the university every morning
They motored to London for the theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - to operate and travel in a motor vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'motor' (mover), adopted into English in the late 19th century with the rise of automobiles.
