ordinance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.dɪn.əns / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dɪ.nəns / (BrE)
an authoritative rule or law issued by a government or other authority, especially a municipal regulation

Examples

But this is a public park and it's a city ordinance that the statues cannot be crawled on ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body

Etymology

From Old French 'ordenance', from Medieval Latin 'ordinantia', from Latin 'ordinare' (to put in order).
