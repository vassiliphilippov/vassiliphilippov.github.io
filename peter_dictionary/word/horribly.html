horribly

1. ADV

/ ˈhɔrəblɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɒrəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely unpleasant, shocking, or dreadful.

Examples

She was deeply, horribly sure that Lucien had filled it with opium.

Synset Examples

there was a dreadfully bloody accident on the road this morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - in an extremely bad or unpleasant way
  • Synonym: dreadfully - in a way that causes great fear or suffering

Etymology

From "horrible" + adverbial suffix "-ly"; ultimately from Latin "horribilis" (dreadful, fearful).
