ken

1. NOUN

/ kɛn / (AmE), / kɛn / (BrE)
the range of knowledge or understanding that someone has

Examples

Better minds than Hale's were - and still are - convinced that there is a society of spirits beyond our ken ".
On these pillars rested that solid basis for life and thought which was soon to be manifested in the remarkably unlimited ken of the Iliad.

Synset Examples

beyond my ken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowledge - facts, information, and skills acquired through experience or education
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Scottish and Northern English, from Old English cennan (‘to make known, declare’); related to Old Norse kenna (‘to know, perceive’).
