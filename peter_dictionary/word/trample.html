trample

1. VERB

/ ˈtræmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtræmpəl / (BrE)
To tread or stomp heavily on something, typically causing damage or crushing it.

Examples

They rode around and around to trample the figure into the sand.
He had no apparent comprehension of morality; he divided humanity into "right guys "and "wrong guys ", and the wrong ones he was always willing to kill and trample under.
"They 'll trample it down.

Synset Examples

The soldiers trampled across the fields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - press or squeeze forcefully so as to break or injure
  • Synonym: stamp - to bring down (one's foot) heavily on the ground

Etymology

From Middle English tramplen, frequentative of trampen (to stamp, tread heavily).

2. VERB

/ ˈtræmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtræmpəl / (BrE)
to injure someone or something by stepping heavily on them, especially intentionally or with a large crowd or animal

Examples

The hospitals contain patients trampled by elephants or run over by sports cars.

Synset Examples

The passerby was trampled by an elephant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to deform or injure by pressing or squeezing forcefully
  • Synonym: stamp - to bring down (one’s foot) heavily; to crush or flatten by stepping

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'trampling', frequentative of 'tramp'.
