shack

1. NOUN

/ ʃæk / (AmE), / ʃæk / (BrE)
A small, crudely built shelter or dwelling, often made from rough materials.

Examples

He had caught her coming out of the shack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small, simple shelter or dwelling, often temporary
  • Synonym: cabin - a small, simple house, typically made of wood in a rural area
  • Antonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
