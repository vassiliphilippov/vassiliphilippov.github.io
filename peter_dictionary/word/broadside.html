broadside

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.saɪd / (BrE)
a large sheet of paper printed on one side only, used for announcements, advertisements, or proclamations intended for wide distribution

Examples

What he was about became clear to her with the circulation of another broadside proclamation by General McClellan, threatening reprisals against Rebel guerrillas.

Synset Examples

he mailed the circular to all subscribers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circular - an advertisement (usually printed on a page or leaflet) intended for wide distribution
  • Synonym: poster - a large printed notice or picture used for advertising or decoration

Etymology

From broad (wide) + side, originally referring to the broad surface of a ship or a large sheet of paper.
