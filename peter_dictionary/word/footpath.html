footpath

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtpæθ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtpɑːθ / (BrE)
A narrow path for people to walk on, usually in the countryside or through fields, not typically for vehicles.

Examples

Thus, on the highroad, a troop of soldiers "marched in their own dust and sang ", while on the footpath one man walks alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidewalk - a paved path for pedestrians, especially in North American cities

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'path', indicating a path made or used by those on foot.
