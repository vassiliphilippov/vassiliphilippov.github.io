corrupt

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
Willing to act dishonestly in return for money or personal gain; lacking in integrity.

Examples

"To have become so corrupt ", he says, "surely you must have studied many arts and sciences ".
But unlike Holmes, he feels his society to be not merely dull but also corrupt.
The latter are either too stupid to catch the killer or too corrupt to care.

Synset Examples

humanity they knew to be corrupt...from the day of Adam's creation
a corrupt and incompetent city government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; disposed to lie, cheat, or steal
  • Antonym: incorruptible - not susceptible to corruption, especially by bribery

Etymology

From Latin 'corruptus', past participle of 'corrumpere' (to destroy, spoil, bribe).

2. VERB

/ kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
To make someone or something morally depraved or dishonest, typically through bad influences or immorality.

Examples

Complete power not only corrupts but it also attracts the mad.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauch - to lead into a life of depravity or sensual pleasure
  • Synonym: subvert - to undermine the power, authority, or integrity of
  • Antonym: reform - to improve morally or correct bad behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'corruptus', past participle of 'corrumpere' (to destroy, spoil, bribe).
