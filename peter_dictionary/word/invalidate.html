invalidate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvælɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvælɪdeɪt / (BrE)
To make something officially invalid or no longer legally acceptable; to prove that something is not correct or true.

Examples

Whereas Bultmann's "center "position is structurally inconsistent and is therefore indefensible on formal grounds alone, the general position of the "right ", as represented, say, by Karl Barth, involves the rejection or at least qualification of the demand for demythologization and so is invalidated on the material grounds we have just considered.

Synset Examples

The contract was annulled
void a plea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: validate - to make or declare valid

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidare' (to weaken), from 'in-' (not) + 'validus' (strong, valid).

to make something no longer legally valid or effective; to take away its legal force

Examples

His passport'’s been invalidated, except for return to the United States.

Synset Examples

invalidate a contract

Related Terms

  • Antonym: validate - to make or declare legally valid

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidare', from 'in-' (not) + 'validus' (strong, valid).
