estrangement

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈstreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
The state of being separated from someone or something, especially as a result of hostility or conflict.

Examples

Modern man, as Dietrich Bonhoeffer has told us, has "come of age "; and though this process by no means represents an unambiguous gain and is, in fact, marked by the estrangement from the depths that seems to be the cost of human maturation, it is still a positive step forward; and those of us who so richly benefit from it should be the last to despise it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations after alienation or estrangement
  • Synonym: alienation - the experience of being isolated or separated, especially as a result of hostility

Etymology

From Old French *estrangement* (separation), from Latin *extraneare* (to treat as a stranger).
