jest

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛst / (AmE), / dʒɛst / (BrE)
A humorous remark, story, or act; a joke made to provoke laughter or amusement.

Examples

Such ambiguous exercises compound confusion by making it worse compounded, and they are sometimes expanded until the cream of the jest sours.

Synset Examples

he told a very funny joke
he knows a million gags
thanks for the laugh
he laughed unpleasantly at his own jest
even a schoolboy's jape is supposed to have some ascertainable point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to amuse people
  • Synonym: quip - a witty remark
  • Synonym: gag - a humorous story or trick

Etymology

From Middle English *jeste*, from Old French *geste*, from Latin *gesta* (deeds, exploits).

2. VERB

/ dʒɛst / (AmE), / dʒɛst / (BrE)
to speak or act in a humorous or playful way; to tell jokes

Examples

Mr. Khrushchev was jesting in the expansive mood of the successful banker.

Synset Examples

He often jokes even when he appears serious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - to say something to cause amusement; to tell a joke
  • Degree Higher: mock - to make fun of or ridicule

Etymology

From Middle English *jesten* (to joke), from Anglo-Norman *gester*, from Latin *gesta* (deeds, exploits).
