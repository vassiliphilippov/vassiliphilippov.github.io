objectification

1. NOUN

/ əbˌdʒɛktəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / əbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of representing an abstract idea or quality as a concrete or physical thing.

Examples

The objectification of the world of spirit in popular superstition had certainly gone far beyond what the experience of spirit could justify or support.
Thus the image of man has suffered complete fragmentation in personal and spiritual qualities, and complete objectification in sub-human and quasi mechanistic powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personification - attributing human characteristics to something nonhuman

Etymology

From 'objectify' + '-ation'; based on Latin 'objectum' (thing presented).
