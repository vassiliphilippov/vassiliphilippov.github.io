miscarriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌkɛrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
the spontaneous loss of a pregnancy before the fetus can survive outside the womb

Examples

Then you'’ll forget about this miscarriage and we'’ll have another child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abortion - termination of a pregnancy, either spontaneous or induced
  • Antonym: stillbirth - birth of a baby that has died in the womb after 20 weeks of pregnancy

Etymology

From mis- (wrong) + carriage, originally 'failure to carry (a pregnancy) to term'.
