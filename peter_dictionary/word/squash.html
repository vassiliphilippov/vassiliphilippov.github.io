squash

1. VERB

/ skwɑʃ / (AmE), / skwɒʃ / (BrE)
to compress or crush something with force so that it becomes flat, out of shape, or loses its normal condition

Examples

His eyes blinked hard, snapping on and squashing some bad things that were trying to push their way into him.

Synset Examples

crush an aluminum can
squeeze a lemon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or loses its shape
  • Synonym: flatten - to make or become flat or flatter

Etymology

Perhaps alteration of 'quash', from Old French 'quasser', from Latin 'quassare' (to shatter).
