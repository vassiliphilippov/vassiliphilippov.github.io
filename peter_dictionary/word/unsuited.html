unsuited

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsuːtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsuːtɪd / (BrE)
Not appropriate or well-matched for a particular purpose, situation, or combination; not easy to combine harmoniously.

Examples

In 1610, Hudson was probably in his early forties, a good navigator, a stubborn voyager, but otherwise fatally unsuited to his chosen profession.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suitable - appropriate or right for a particular situation

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + suited (from 'suit', meaning to be appropriate).
