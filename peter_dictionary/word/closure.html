closure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
the process or rate at which two moving objects approach each other, resulting in a decreasing distance between them

Examples

With the rapid rate of closure, the approach from below, the side, and ahead, there would be only a moment when damage could be done.

Synset Examples

the ship's rapid rate of closing gave them little time to avoid a collision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - the act of coming nearer in distance or time

Etymology

From Old French *closure* (closing), from Latin *clausura* (a closing or enclosure).
