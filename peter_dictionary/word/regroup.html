regroup

1. VERB

/ riˈɡruːp / (AmE), / riˈɡruːp / (BrE)
to organize again, especially after being disorganized or after a setback

Examples

The Koreans fell back, but regrouped at the top of the hill and pinned down the cavalrymen with a screen of fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reorganize - to organize again or differently
  • Antonym: disband - to break up an organized group

Etymology

From re- (again) + group (from French groupe), meaning to group again.
