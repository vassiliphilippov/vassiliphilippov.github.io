incensed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnst / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnst / (BrE)
Very angry; greatly enraged, especially at something unjust or wrong.

Examples

Monsieur de Cury was incensed, of course.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong annoyance or displeasure
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger

Etymology

From Latin 'incensare' (to set on fire), from 'incendere' (to burn).
