smirk

1. VERB

/ smɝk / (AmE), / smɜːk / (BrE)
to smile in an affected, smug, or self-satisfied way, often conveying scorn or self-importance

Examples

Plunking themselves down on the front bench, they turned to smirk at those around them.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grin - to smile broadly, especially so as to show the teeth
  • Degree Higher: leer - to look or smile in an unpleasant, sly, or lascivious way

Etymology

From Middle Low German 'smirken' (to smile), related to 'smile'.
