hot-blooded

1. ADJ

/ hɑt ˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / hɒt ˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) prone to strong emotions, especially passion or anger; excitable or impulsive.

Examples

I submit that this was not a hot-blooded crime of passion.

Synset Examples

hot-blooded Latin-Americans

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold-blooded - emotionless or unfeeling; not easily affected by emotion

Etymology

Formed from 'hot' and 'blooded', suggesting warmth or intensity of feelings.
