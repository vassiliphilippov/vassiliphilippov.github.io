elongate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlɔŋˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to make something longer, or to become longer, especially by stretching or pulling

Examples

Individual nuclei were usually oval to round, though occasionally elongated, and frequently small and somewhat pyknotic.

Synset Examples

stretch the fabric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthen - to make or become longer
  • Antonym: shorten - to make or become shorter

Etymology

From Latin elongare (to lengthen), from e- (out) + longus (long).
