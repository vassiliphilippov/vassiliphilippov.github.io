lobular

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːbjələr / (AmE), / ˈlɒbjʊlə / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a lobule, especially in anatomical or biological contexts.

Examples

In addition, it would be difficult to imagine chronic generalized emphysema occurring in a cow, considering its marked lobular development but, conversely, not difficult to imagine this occurring in the horse or the dog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobule - a small lobe or a subdivision of a lobe

Etymology

From Latin 'lobulus', diminutive of 'lobus' (a lobe), with English suffix '-ar'.
