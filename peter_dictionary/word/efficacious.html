efficacious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛfɪˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɛfɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having the power to produce the intended result; effective in achieving a desired outcome.

Examples

These desires presuppose a sense of causally efficacious powers in which one is involved, some working for one's good, others threatening ill.

Synset Examples

written propaganda is less efficacious than the habits and prejudices...of the readers
the medicine is efficacious in stopping a cough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effective - successful in producing a desired or intended result
  • Antonym: ineffective - not producing the desired effect

Etymology

From Latin efficax, efficacis (effectual, powerful), from efficere (to accomplish).
