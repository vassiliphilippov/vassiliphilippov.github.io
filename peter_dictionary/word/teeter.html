teeter

1. VERB

/ ˈtitər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtə / (BrE)
to move or stand unsteadily, often rocking back and forth, as if about to fall

Examples

Today Northern Vietnam is overtly Communist; Laos is teetering between Communism and pro Communist neutralism; Cambodia is, for all practical purposes, neutralist.
He caught her eye, came back around the car with the boot wearer's teetering, half mincing walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totter - to walk or move in an unsteady way
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become stable or balanced

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the action or related to Old English 'teter' (to totter).
