stun

1. VERB

/ stʌn / (AmE), / stʌn / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) temporarily unable to act or respond, as from a blow or shock; to render senseless or dizzy

Examples

It was not so much that the shot had stunned the audience, as that they had been stunned already.
It was not so much that the shot had stunned the audience, as that they had been stunned already.
I'll knock you dizzy.

Synset Examples

stun fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daze - to make unable to think or react properly; to confuse or stun
  • Synonym: knock out - to cause someone to become unconscious, especially by a blow

Etymology

From Old French *estoner* (to stun, daze), from Latin *extonare* (to thunder out).

2. VERB

/ stʌn / (AmE), / stʌn / (BrE)
to hit someone or something with great force, as if with a sandbag; to shock or knock out by a heavy blow

Examples

For an instant John was stunned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to surprise or disturb greatly
  • Synonym: daze - to stun or confuse, especially temporarily

3. VERB

/ stʌn / (AmE), / stʌn / (BrE)
to shock or astonish someone so greatly that they are temporarily unable to react

Examples

The fashion world was stunned today... ...by the sudden deaths of models Candy Walker and Amanda Keeler.

Synset Examples

The news stunned her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaze - to surprise greatly; fill with wonder
  • Synonym: astonish - to surprise or impress greatly
  • Synonym: startle - to cause sudden surprise or alarm
  • Antonym: calm - to make less agitated or shocked

Etymology

Probably from Old French *estoner* 'to stun, daze', from Latin *extonare* 'thunder out'.
