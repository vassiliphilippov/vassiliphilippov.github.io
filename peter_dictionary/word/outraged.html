outraged

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing strong anger or indignation at something perceived as unjust or wrong.

Examples

The world exploded when Fascism challenged all concepts of peace and liberty, and the outraged, freedom loving peoples of the Capitalist and Socialist worlds combined forces to stamp Fascist tyranny into cringing submission.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indignant - feeling or showing anger over something unjust or unworthy
  • Synonym: incensed - very angry; enraged
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger; peaceful

Etymology

From French 'outrager', from Old French 'outrage' (excess, insult), from Latin 'ultraticus'.
