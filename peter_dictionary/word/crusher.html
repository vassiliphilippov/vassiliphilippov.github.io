crusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʌʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌʃə / (BrE)
A machine or device designed to break or compress something into smaller pieces or a different form, typically by applying force.

Examples

The world's best golfer, shooting below par, came to the last hole of the opening round of the Los Angeles open with every intention of delivering a final crusher.
Ice baths, electric shocks, lashings, wild dogs, testicle crushers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grinder - a machine used for grinding something into smaller pieces
  • Antonym: crusher-resistant - resistant to being crushed; the opposite quality

Etymology

From 'crush' + '-er', denoting an agent or instrument.
