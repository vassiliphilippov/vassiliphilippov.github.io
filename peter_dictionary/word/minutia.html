minutia

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈnuːʃə / (AmE), / mɪˈnjuːʃə / (BrE)
A small or precise detail; an individual feature, fact, or item that is minor or subtle.

Examples

More potent a charm to bring back that time of life than this record of a few pictures and a few remembered facts would be a catalogue of the minutiae which are of the very stuff of the mind, intrinsic, because they were known in the beginning not by the eye alone but by the hand that held them.

Synset Examples

he had memorized the many minutiae of the legal code

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detail - an individual fact or item; a particular

Etymology

From Latin *minutia* (smallness, petty detail), from *minutus* (small).
