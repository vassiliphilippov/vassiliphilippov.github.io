inhabited

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhæbɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) having people or animals living there; occupied.

Examples

We consider a rural community as an assemblage of inhabited dwellings whose configuration is determined by the location and size of the arable land sites necessary for family subsistence.

Synset Examples

the inhabited regions of the earth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uninhabited - (of a place) with nobody living there; not inhabited

Etymology

From Latin *inhabitare*, from *in-* (in) + *habitare* (to dwell).
