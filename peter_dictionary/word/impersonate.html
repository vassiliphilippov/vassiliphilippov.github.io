impersonate

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɜrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsəneɪt / (BrE)
to pretend to be another person by imitating their appearance, voice, or manner, especially as entertainment or to deceive

Examples

Art Lund, a fine big actor with a great head of blond hair and a good voice, impersonates Enright.
Colonel Frederick W. Lander, impersonated, will again make his break-neck ride down the steep declivity of Talbott's (now College) Hill and thunder across the bridge to join Colonel Benjamin F. Kelley's (West) Virginia Infantry, then swarming through the streets in pursuit of the retreating Confederates.

Synset Examples

She impersonates Madonna
The actor portrays an elderly, lonely man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitate - copy or mimic someone or something
  • Synonym: portray - represent or play the role of a character

Etymology

From Latin impersonare (to give an appearance in person), from in- (in) + persona (person).
