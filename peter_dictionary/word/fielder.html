fielder

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːldər / (AmE), / ˈfiːldə / (BrE)
A player in baseball or cricket whose position is in the field while the team is not batting, responsible for catching, stopping, or retrieving the ball.

Examples

By the time the fielder got his hands on the ball Deegan was rounding third base and heading for home.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: batter - a player whose turn it is to bat

Etymology

From the verb 'field' (to catch or stop a ball) + -er (agent noun suffix).
