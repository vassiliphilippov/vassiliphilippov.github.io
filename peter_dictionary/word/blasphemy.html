blasphemy

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæsfəmi / (AmE), / ˈblɑːsfəmi / (BrE)
The act or offense of speaking disrespectfully about God or sacred things; profane language or utterance against what is held to be holy.

Examples

John's first impulse was to denounce their blasphemy.
Rule number one: No blasphemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profanity - profane or irreverent language, especially about religion
  • Synonym: sacrilege - violation or profanation of something sacred

Etymology

From Greek *blasphēmia* (slander, profane speech), via Late Latin *blasphemia* and Old French *blasfemie*.
