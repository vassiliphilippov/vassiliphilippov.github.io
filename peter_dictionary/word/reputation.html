reputation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛp.jəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛp.jʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the opinion that people have about what someone or something is like, based on past behavior or achievements; the state of being widely respected or admired

Examples

Martin Tahse has established quite a reputation for himself as a successful stager of touring productions.
"The man's true reputation is his work ".
I kept saying, "If I could just build up a reputation for myself, make some real money, get to be well known as an illustrator - like Peter Askington, for instance - then I could take some time off and paint ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repute - the opinion generally held of someone or something; reputation
  • Antonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for something bad

Etymology

From Latin 'reputatio' (consideration, reckoning), from 'reputare' (to think over, reckon up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The beliefs or opinions that are generally held about someone or something, especially regarding a particular characteristic.

Examples

They were disturbed by his idiotic bravado - as, when his bodyguard, Yankee Schwartz, complained that he had been snubbed by Dave Miller, a prize-fight referee, chieftain of a Jewish gang and one of four brothers of tough reputation, who were Hirschey, a gambler politician in loose beer running league with Torrio and O' Banion, Frank, a policeman, and Max, the youngest.
It is possible, although highly doubtful, that he killed none at all but merely let his reputation work for him by privately claiming every unsolved murder in the state.
"My reputation's my stock in trade ", Tom mentioned more than once.

Synset Examples

his reputation for promiscuity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for some bad quality or deed
  • Antonym: stigma - a mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance, quality, or person

Etymology

From Latin 'reputatio' (consideration), from 'reputare' (to reckon, consider).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The opinion or judgment that people in general have about someone's character or abilities, based on what has happened in the past.

Examples

It will ruin your already useless reputation.

Synset Examples

he acquired a reputation as an actor before he started writing
he was a person of bad report

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repute - the state of being highly regarded or well thought of
  • Antonym: infamy - the state of being well known for something bad

Etymology

From Latin reputatio (consideration), from reputare (to consider, reckon).
