trio

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtriː.əʊ / (BrE)
A group or set of three people or things.

Examples

The trio who shared the most-valuable honors were introduced by Bob Broeg, sports editor of the Post-Dispatch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duo - a group of two people or things
  • Degree Higher: quartet - a group of four people or things

Etymology

From Italian *trio*, from Latin *tres* (three).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtriːoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtriːəʊ / (BrE)
a musical composition written for three performers or instruments

Examples

The Presto ma non assai of the first trio of the scherzo is taken literally and may shock you, as the real Allegro con spirito of the finale is likely to bring you to your feet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duet - a musical composition for two performers
  • Degree Higher: quartet - a musical composition for four performers

Etymology

From Italian 'trio', from Latin 'tres' (three).
