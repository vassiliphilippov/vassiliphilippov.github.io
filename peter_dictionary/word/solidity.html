solidity

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / səˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being solid, firm, or substantial; the consistency of a solid object.

Examples

Where their sharp edges seemed restless as sea waves thrusting themselves upward in angry motion, Papa-san sat glacier like, his smooth solidity, his very immobility defying all the turmoil about him.
The shapes of the men ahead of him lacked solidity, as if the whip had stripped them of their very flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solidity - state or quality of being solid (synonym sense)
  • Synonym: firmness - quality of being strong or firm (synonym sense)
  • Antonym: softness - quality of being soft or yielding (antonym sense)

Etymology

From Latin soliditas, from solidus (solid).
