ligament

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪɡəmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡəmənt / (BrE)
A tough band or sheet of fibrous connective tissue that connects bones, supports joints, or holds organs in place.

Examples

There is definitely some ligament damage in his knee ".

Etymology

From Latin 'ligamentum' (band, tie), from 'ligare' (to bind).
