pharmacist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrməsɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməsɪst / (BrE)
A person qualified to prepare and dispense medicinal drugs, especially one licensed to do so in a pharmacy.

Examples

At any cost, he must leave the dreary Pennsylvania mining town where his father was a pharmacist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemist - British term for pharmacist or a person who prepares and dispenses medicines

Etymology

From French *pharmacien*, from Greek *pharmakon* (drug, medicine).
