secularize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
To make something non-religious or remove its religious character or influence.

Examples

Nor is it necessary to look for such evidence in the great urban centers of our culture that are admittedly almost entirely secularized and so profoundly estranged from the conventional forms in which the gospel has been communicated.

Synset Examples

Ataturk secularized Turkey

Related Terms

  • Antonym: religionize - to make religious or imbue with religious character

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'saecularizare' (to make secular), from Latin 'saecularis' (worldly, non-religious).
