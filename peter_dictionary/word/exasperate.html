exasperate

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæspəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːspəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to irritate or provoke to a high degree; to annoy or anger greatly

Examples

His energy was prodigious; sometimes he would be up before dawn, clad as a garbage collector and hurling pails into areaways to exasperate us, and thereafter would hurry to the Bronx Zoo to grimace at the lions and press cigar butts against their paws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritate - to make someone annoyed or impatient
  • Synonym: annoy - to make someone a little angry; to bother
  • Antonym: appease - to calm or pacify, especially by giving in to demands

Etymology

From Latin exasperare, from ex- (thoroughly) + asper (rough).
