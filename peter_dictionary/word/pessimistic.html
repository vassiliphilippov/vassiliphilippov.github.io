pessimistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛsɪˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛsɪˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
expecting that only bad things will happen or emphasizing the negative aspects of a situation

Examples

There followed a long and sometimes bitter discussion of the feasibility of elections for the fall of 1957, in which it appears that the Minister of the Interior took the most pessimistic view and that the Istiqlal was something less than enthusiastic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optimistic - expecting the best possible outcome; having a positive outlook

Etymology

From French pessimiste (pessimist) + -ic, from Latin pessimus (worst).
