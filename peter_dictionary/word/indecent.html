indecent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdisənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsənt / (BrE)
not conforming to accepted standards of propriety or morality; improper or offensive in polite society

Examples

While the Daily Post continued to accuse Republicans and the Daily Journal continued to accuse Democrats, the Woonsocket Patriot complained that the Virginia authorities showed indecent and cowardly haste to condemn Brown and his men.
Then suddenly we found ourselves in the middle of another fight, an irrational, an indecent, an undeclared and immoral war with our strongest (and some had thought noblest) ally.

Synset Examples

was buried with indecent haste
indecorous behavior
language unbecoming to a lady
unseemly to use profanity
moved to curb their untoward ribaldry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards; proper
  • Synonym: unseemly - not proper or appropriate; unbecoming
  • Synonym: indecorous - not in keeping with good taste and propriety

Etymology

From Latin indecens, indecent- (unbecoming), from in- (not) + decens (seeming, fitting).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdisənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːsənt / (BrE)
not conforming to accepted standards of morality, especially by being offensive or inappropriate in a sexual way

Examples

I use the phrase advisedly because there was something positively indecent about our relationship.

Synset Examples

an earthy but not indecent story
an indecent gesture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards; proper or respectable
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards, especially relating to sex

Etymology

From Latin indecent-, indecens, from in- (not) + decent- (fitting, appropriate).
