hesitating

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛzɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛzɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
lacking decisiveness or certainty; slow to act or decide

Examples

One handled the salesman in a friendly manner, another in a rough manner, and the third in a hesitating manner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisive - able to make decisions quickly and confidently

Etymology

Present participle of 'hesitate', from Latin 'haesitare' (to stick fast, hesitate).
