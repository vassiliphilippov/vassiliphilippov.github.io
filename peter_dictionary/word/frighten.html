frighten

1. VERB

/ ˈfraɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid or anxious; to cause fear in someone

Examples

The question frightened Andrei.
So suppose somebody only wished to frighten her, so she would leave Honotassa!
It was merely an attempt to frighten her.
She could n't see any reason why Maude would attempt to frighten her.
Besides, there was something hysterical and silly, something almost childish about an attempt to frighten her.

Synset Examples

The stranger who hangs around the building frightens me
Ghosts could never affright her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scare - cause someone to feel sudden fear
  • Degree Higher: terrify - cause someone to feel extreme fear

Etymology

From Middle English 'frighten', from 'fright' (fear) + '-en' (verbal suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈfraɪtən / (AmE), / ˈfraɪt(ə)n / (BrE)
to drive (someone or something) away by causing fear

Examples

The mere fact that the tall figure with the rifle and field glasses had been seen riding that way was enough to frighten three rustling homesteaders out of the Upper Laramie country in a single week.
I left it noisy, so it scares away any pain-in-the-ass innocent bystanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scare off - to cause someone or something to leave by making them afraid
  • Synonym: intimidate - to frighten someone, especially in order to make them do what one wants

Etymology

From Middle English frighten, frequentative form of fright, from Old English fyrhtan (to fear).
