sine qua non

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪn.eɪ kwɑ ˈnɑn / (AmE), / ˌsɪneɪ kwɑː ˈnɒn / (BrE)
an essential condition or element; something that is absolutely necessary

Examples

The sine qua non of the elections was naturally an impartial and standardized procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - the fact of being required or indispensable
  • Synonym: precondition - a condition that must be fulfilled before something else can happen
  • Antonym: luxury - something inessential but conducive to pleasure and comfort

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'without which not' (i.e., an indispensable condition).
