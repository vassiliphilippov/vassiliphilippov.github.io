addressee

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.rɛˈsi / (AmE), / ˌæd.rɛˈsiː / (BrE)
The person to whom something, such as a letter, speech, or message, is addressed.

Examples

I think that I did things to myself, to hurt myself, so that you'’d know that I could hurt you.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sender - the person who sends something, especially a message
  • Synonym: recipient - person who receives something

Etymology

Formed from 'address' + the suffix '-ee' (denoting the person affected).
