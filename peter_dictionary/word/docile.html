docile

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːsəl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsaɪl / (BrE)
quiet and easy to control; ready and willing to be taught or led

Examples

It is extraordinary that a people as proud and warlike as Southerners should have been as docile as they have.
Furthermore, Dr. Calderone says, a certain number of docile, retiring men always have been around.

Synset Examples

the docile masses of an enslaved nation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient - willing to do as told or instructed
  • Synonym: compliant - inclined to agree with others or obey rules
  • Antonym: rebellious - resisting control or authority
  • Antonym: stubborn - unwilling to change or be led

Etymology

From Latin docilis (easily taught), from docere (to teach).
