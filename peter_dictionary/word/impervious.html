impervious

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɝviəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːviəs / (BrE)
Not allowing liquid, gas, or other substances to pass through; not affected or influenced by something.

Examples

And it presented itself to him as it would to any stranger, impervious, complete in itself.

Synset Examples

a material impervious to water
someone impervious to argument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permeable - allowing substances to pass through
  • Synonym: resistant - unaffected by something

Etymology

From Latin 'impervius' (not to be passed through), from 'in-' (not) + 'pervius' (letting through).
