ungainly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡeɪnli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡeɪnli / (BrE)
Lacking grace or ease in movement or posture; awkward or clumsy.

Examples

Since his Christ was to be life size, how was Mary to hold him on her lap without the relationship seeming ungainly?
The ungainly bird thing ran away, and to David its croaking sounded like the crowing of a tormented rooster.

Synset Examples

a gawky lad with long ungainly legs
clumsy fingers
what an ungainly creature a giraffe is
heaved his unwieldy figure out of his chair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graceful - showing grace and elegance in movement
  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + gainly (graceful, attractive), from Middle English gainly (direct, convenient, graceful), probably from Old Norse gegn (straight, direct).
