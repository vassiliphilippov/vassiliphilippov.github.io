prediction

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
A statement or act of saying what will happen in the future, often based on reasoning, evidence, or prior knowledge.

Examples

It is being fought, moreover, in fairly close correspondence with the predictions of the soothsayers of the think factories.
Beyond a few million kilometers from the Earth, but still in the region of the Earth's orbit, a prediction of the flux of dust is even more unreliable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forecast - a prediction based on knowledge or analysis, often related to weather or business
  • Synonym: prophecy - a prediction, often with supernatural connotations
  • Degree Lower: guess - an estimate or conjecture without sufficient information

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + dicere (to say).
