pidgin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
A simplified language that develops as a means of communication between speakers of different native languages, typically for trade or practical purposes.

Examples

Its citizens spoke all of the world's surviving tongues, plus a new one called Lingo, a pidgin whose vocabulary was derived from the other six and whose syntax was so simple it could be contained on half a sheet of paper.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: creole - A stable, fully developed natural language that evolved from a pidgin

Etymology

From English 'business', altered by Chinese Pidgin English pronunciation.
