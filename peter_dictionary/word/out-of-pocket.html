out-of-pocket

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
(of expenses or costs) paid directly by an individual in cash, rather than by a third party or reimbursed later.

Examples

And at the end of the year, you figure the tax wrong, you pay them out of your own pocket.

Synset Examples

his out-of-pocket costs were $10

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reimbursed - (of expenses or costs) paid back to the person who spent them

Etymology

From the phrase 'out of pocket', meaning 'from one's own money'.
