placid

1. ADJ

/ ˈplæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæsɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) not easily upset or excited; calm and not easily irritated.

Examples

Jane asked in her placid, interested way.

Synset Examples

an equable temper
not everyone shared his placid temperament
remained placid despite the repeated delays

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Latin 'placidus' meaning 'pleasing, gentle'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈplæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæsɪd / (BrE)
(of a body of water) calm and free from disturbance or agitation; not rough or turbulent.

Examples

But Greg's area remained as placid as a Florida dawn.
The waters lay muddy but placid, without a ripple of movement against the wheels; there was not a match width of damp mark to show they were receding.

Synset Examples

a ribbon of sand between the angry sea and the placid bay
the quiet waters of a lagoon
a lake of tranquil blue water reflecting a tranquil blue sky
a smooth channel crossing
scarcely a ripple on the still water
unruffled water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions; (of water) smooth, not rough
  • Synonym: tranquil - free from disturbance; calm
  • Antonym: rough - (of water) having many waves or being agitated

Etymology

From Latin placidus (gentle, calm).
