carton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.tən / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.tən / (BrE)
the amount of something contained in a carton, especially as a standard unit for sale or transport

Examples

Our suspicions eventually centered, by the process of elimination, on a grocer's boy, a thoroughly bad hat, who delivered cartons to the people overhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - the contents held in a box

Etymology

From French 'carton' (cardboard, cardboard box), from Italian 'cartone' (pasteboard), from 'carta' (paper).
