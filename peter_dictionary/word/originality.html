originality

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪdʒəˈnæləti / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒɪˈnæləti / (BrE)
The quality of being inventive or capable of thinking and acting independently; the ability to create or do something new or unique.

Examples

We do not deny originality to the Agamemnon because Aeschylus found the tales of the house of Atreus among the folk lore of the Greeks.
The sweep of space, the delicate counterbalance of the white masses, the over-all completeness and unity, the originality and imagination, all entitle it to be called an authentic masterpiece.
His repeated experimentation with the techniques of fiction testifies to an independence of mind and an originality of approach, but it also shows him touching at many points the stream of literary development back of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creativity - the use of imagination or original ideas to create something
  • Antonym: conformity - compliance with standards, rules, or laws; behaving in ways that are conventional

Etymology

From French originalité, from original (original) + -ité (-ity).

2. NOUN

/ əˌrɪdʒəˈnæləti / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being new, unique, or inventive; not derived from something else.

Examples

She concluded by asking for a brief interview - "to settle with you where "- and she threw in a tribute to his "gentle manners "and "the wild originality of your countenance ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual
  • Antonym: conventionality - conformity to established practice or accepted standards

Etymology

From French originalité, from original + -ity.
