amber

1. ADJ

/ ˈæmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈæmbə / (BrE)
a medium to dark brownish yellow color, resembling that of the fossilized resin amber

Examples

Or the surging whirling sounds of bats at night, when their black bodies dived into the blackness above and below the amber street lights.
On unoccupied roadway the bottle shattered into a small amber flash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden - of a bright, yellowish color like gold

Etymology

From Old French ambre, from Medieval Latin ambra (amber, fossil resin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈæmbə / (BrE)
A deep yellow or orange-yellow color, resembling that of amber resin.

Examples

The Indian's arm whipped sidewise - there was a flash of amber and froth, the crash of the bottle shattering against the side of the first car.

Synset Examples

an amber light illuminated the room
he admired the gold of her hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold - a deep yellow metallic color
  • Degree Lower: yellow - the color between green and orange in the visible spectrum
  • Degree Higher: orange - a bright reddish-yellow color

Etymology

From Old French 'ambre', originally meaning amber resin.
