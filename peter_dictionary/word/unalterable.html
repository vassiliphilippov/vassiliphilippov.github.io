unalterable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔltɚəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːltərəbl / (BrE)
Impossible to change; not capable of being altered or modified.

Examples

Because community not severalty of property is the law of nature no man can assert an absolutely unalterable right to what is his.

Synset Examples

unalterable resolve
an unalterable ground rule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutable - unchanging over time or unable to be changed
  • Synonym: changeless - remaining the same; unchanging
  • Antonym: alterable - capable of being altered or changed

Etymology

un- (not) + alterable, from Latin alterare (to change).
