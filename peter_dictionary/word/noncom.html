noncom

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑnˌkɑm / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌkɒm / (BrE)
an informal term for a noncommissioned officer in the military, referring to an enlisted member who has earned authority but is not a commissioned officer

Examples

But what made the load lighter was the realization that every officer, non-com and trooper was ready and willing to help him carry it, for the good of the troop and the regiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noncommissioned officer - military officer appointed from enlisted ranks; not holding a commission

Etymology

Shortened form of 'noncommissioned officer', dating from early 20th century.
