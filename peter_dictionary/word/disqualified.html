disqualified

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkwɑːlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
Declared ineligible or unfit to participate, typically due to a law, rule, or provision.

Examples

The police were disqualified along with certain categories of naturalized citizens, criminals and those punished for Protectorate activities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eligible - fit or qualified to be chosen or to participate

Etymology

From disqualify + -ed, based on the Latin prefix dis- (expressing reversal) and qualify.
