dignified

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪd / (BrE)
Having or showing a composed or serious manner that is worthy of respect; marked by dignity or stateliness in appearance or demeanor.

Examples

What remained lacked the original verve but it was at least dignified, as befitting the tragic circumstances.

Synset Examples

her dignified demeanor
the director of the school was a dignified white-haired gentleman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undignified - lacking dignity; not behaving in a worthy or respectable way

Etymology

From the verb 'dignify', ultimately from Latin 'dignitas' (worthiness, dignity).
