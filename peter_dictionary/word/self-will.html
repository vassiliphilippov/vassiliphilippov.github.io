self-will

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈwɪl / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈwɪl / (BrE)
the determination to follow one's own wishes or ideas, often in defiance of others' advice or opinions

Examples

cruelty, meanness, self-will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - refusal to change one's attitude or position
  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or difficult to move
  • Antonym: compliance - willingness to agree or yield to others

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'will', indicating the will of oneself.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈwɪl / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈwɪl / (BrE)
The ability or trait of resolutely controlling one’s own actions or behavior according to personal will or decision.

Examples

A state, the highest form of human organization in fact and theory, could be subjected to Law only by a manifestation of self-will, or consent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-control - the ability to control one’s own emotions and behavior

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'will'.
