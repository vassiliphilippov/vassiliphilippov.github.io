shriveled

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
Dried out and wrinkled, often as a result of losing moisture; withered.

Examples

When she returned to life in the big house she felt shriveled of all emotion save dedication to duty.

Synset Examples

dried-up grass
the desert was edged with sere vegetation
shriveled leaves on the unwatered seedlings
withered vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - dried up and shriveled, especially due to loss of moisture
  • Antonym: plump - full and rounded in form

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrivelen', likely from Middle Low German 'schrivel' meaning 'to wrinkle'.
