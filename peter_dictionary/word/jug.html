jug

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌɡ / (AmE), / dʒʌɡ / (BrE)
a large container with a narrow mouth and a handle, used for holding and pouring liquids

Examples

Stranger, Uncle Randolph began riding home nights with a jug strapped to his saddle, drunkenly singing "Old Dan Tucker "at the top of his voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher - a container for holding and pouring liquids, typically with a spout and handle

Etymology

From late Middle English, perhaps from Old French 'joug', from Latin 'jugum' meaning 'yoke', by resemblance to the shape.
