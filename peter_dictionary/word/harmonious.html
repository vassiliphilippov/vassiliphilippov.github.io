harmonious

1. ADJ

/ hɑrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
pleasing to the ear; having a pleasant or agreeable combination of musical notes or sounds

Examples

The chief experience is the sensing of communion, and in the higher religions, of a harmonious relationship with the supernatural power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - having a harsh or jarring sound

Etymology

From Latin 'harmoniosus', from Greek 'harmonios' (harmonious, fitting together).

2. ADJ

/ hɑːrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒniəs / (BrE)
having parts that are well matched, balanced, or forming an aesthetically pleasing or consistent whole; exhibiting equivalence or correspondence among constituents.

Examples

The severe yet harmonious vases of the previous fifty years, the Strong Geometric style of the late ninth century, display as firm a mastery of the principles underlying Geometric pottery; but artists now were ready to refine and elaborate their inheritance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balanced - having different elements in the correct proportions
  • Antonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement among parts

Etymology

From Latin harmoniosus, from Greek harmonia (harmony, agreement).
