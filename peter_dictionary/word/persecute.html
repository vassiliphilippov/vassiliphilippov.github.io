persecute

1. VERB

/ ˈpɜrsɪˌkjut / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsɪkjuːt / (BrE)
to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, especially because of their race, religion, or political beliefs

Examples

Peter Caroli had come to Geneva, saying that he had been a bishop of the Church of Rome and had been persecuted in Paris for his Reformed faith.
Ezra Stiles Gannett, an honorable representative of the sanhedrin, addressed himself frankly to the issue in 1845, insisting that Parker should not be persecuted or calumniated and that in this republic no power to restrain him by force could exist.

Synset Examples

Jews were persecuted in the former Soviet Union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppress - keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority

Etymology

From Latin persecūtus, past participle of persequī (to pursue, persecute).
