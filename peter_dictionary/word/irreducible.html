irreducible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪrɪˈduːsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdjuːsəbl̩ / (BrE)
Not able to be made smaller, simpler, or reduced in size or extent; impossible to simplify or diminish further.

Examples

Let p be the minimal polynomial for T, * * f where the * * f are distinct irreducible monic polynomials over F and the * * f are positive integers.

Synset Examples

an irreducible minimum
an irreducible formula
an irreducible hernia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reducible - able to be made smaller or simpler

Etymology

From Late Latin 'irreducibilis', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'reducere' (to bring back).
