fertilizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance added to soil to increase its fertility and promote plant growth, often containing nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium

Examples

As a substitute for this, organic fertilizer dissolved in water to half the strength in the directions, may be used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manure - animal dung used for fertilizing land

Etymology

From French *fertiliser* or Latin *fertilis* (fertile) plus the agent suffix '-er'.
