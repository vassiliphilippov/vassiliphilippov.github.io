flout

1. VERB

/ flaʊt / (AmE), / flaʊt / (BrE)
to openly disregard (a rule, law, or convention) with contempt; to show disdain for by not obeying

Examples

If the moral code were flouted, the proper balance of the universe would be upset, and the disastrous result could be floods, plague, or famine.

Synset Examples

flout the rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • Antonym: obey - to comply with or follow a rule or command

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Middle English 'flouten' (to play the flute, jeer, mock).
