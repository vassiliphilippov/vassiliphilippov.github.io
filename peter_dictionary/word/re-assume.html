re-assume

1. VERB

/ ˌriː əˈsum / (AmE), / ˌriː əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to take on or adopt again, especially after an interruption or lapse

Examples

By the death of his father in 1888 he had come into possession of the family estate and had re-assumed its traditions.

Synset Examples

He re-assumed his old behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resume - to take up or begin again after an interruption
  • Degree Lower: assume - to take on, adopt, or accept (a role, position, responsibility, etc.)

Etymology

From re- (again) + assume (from Latin assumere, to take up).
