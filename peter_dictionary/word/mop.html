mop

1. VERB

/ mɑp / (AmE), / mɒp / (BrE)
to clean or wipe a surface, especially by using a mop or by rubbing with a cloth

Examples

He got out of there in a hurry, brushing past another man in the door, mopping his brow.

Synset Examples

Mop the hallway now
He mopped her forehead with a towel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wipe - to clean or dry by rubbing
  • Synonym: scrub - to rub hard in order to clean
  • Antonym: dirty - make dirty or unclean

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Middle English moppe (rough cloth).

2. NOUN

/ mɑp / (AmE), / mɒp / (BrE)
a cleaning tool consisting of absorbent material attached to a handle, used for washing floors

Examples

Keep your eyes on your mop, man.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broom - tool with bristles for sweeping

Etymology

From Middle English moppe (bundle of coarse yarn, etc. used for cleaning), of obscure origin.
