smolder

1. VERB

/ ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
(of a fire or emotion) to burn or exist slowly and without a flame, sometimes with smoke, or (of emotion) to remain intense but hidden.

Examples

Where it is not actually erupting, it rumbles and smolders in sullen resentment like a volcano, ready to explode at any moment.
Her face was pale but set and her dark eyes smoldered with blame for Ben.
During the five-month lull, civil war smoldered and flickered throughout the Congo.

Synset Examples

a smoldering fire

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: flare - to burn with a sudden, strong flame
  • Antonym: extinguish - to put out a fire or end something burning

Etymology

From Middle English smolderen, related to Scandinavian words like Danish smuldre (to crumble) and Norwegian smuldra (to smolder).
