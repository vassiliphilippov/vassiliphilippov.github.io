irksome

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝk.səm / (AmE), / ˈɜːk.səm / (BrE)
Annoying or tedious; causing irritation or weariness due to monotony or repetitiveness.

Examples

The family was Protestant, but for me it was only irksome and I let it go.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome
  • Synonym: boring - uninteresting; causing boredom
  • Antonym: engaging - attractive or interesting; able to get your attention

Etymology

From Middle English irken (to grow weary) + -some (forming adjectives).
