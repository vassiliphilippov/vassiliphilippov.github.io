planted

1. ADJ

/ ˈplæn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːn.tɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, feelings, or needs) deeply established; firmly fixed in the mind or character.

Examples

A need so deeply planted, asking for direction, so to speak, is likely to be gratified by the vivid examples and heroic proportions of literature.

Synset Examples

deep-rooted prejudice
deep-seated differences of opinion
implanted convictions
ingrained habits of a lifetime
a deeply planted need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep-rooted - firmly established and difficult to change
  • Synonym: ingrained - (of habits, beliefs, or attitudes) firmly fixed; difficult to change
  • Antonym: shallow - not deeply established or held, easily changed
