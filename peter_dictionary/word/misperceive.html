misperceive

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪspərˈsiv / (AmE), / ˌmɪspəˈsiːv / (BrE)
To interpret or understand something incorrectly; to perceive in a mistaken way.

Examples

Any organism that falters or misperceives the signals or weakens is done.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perceive - to become aware of through the senses

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'perceive', ultimately from Latin 'percipere' (to seize, understand).
