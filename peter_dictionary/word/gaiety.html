gaiety

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a feeling or atmosphere of cheerfulness, high spirits, and lively celebration

Examples

He was seeing, somehow, the face of a young boy, the boy Simms Purdew must once have been, a boy with sorrel hair, and blue eyes dancing with gaiety, and the boy mouth grinning trustfully among the freckles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merriment - cheerful fun, joy, and laughter
  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness
  • Antonym: sadness - the state of being unhappy

Etymology

From French 'gaieté', from Old French 'gai' (joyful, merry).
