terminal

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnəl / (BrE)
A point on an electrical device, such as a battery or circuit, where electric current enters or leaves.

Examples

The arc voltage was measured with a voltmeter whose terminals were connected to the anode and cathode holders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connector - device for joining electrical circuits together

Etymology

From Latin terminālis (of or pertaining to a boundary or limit), from terminus (end, boundary).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnəl / (BrE)
A station where buses, trains, ships, or airplanes arrive and depart, and passengers or goods are loaded or unloaded.

Examples

These revenues make it possible to provide essential freight and passenger service over the entire New York Central system as well as the New York area commuter and terminal freight services.
With a cop patrolling the road Muller would have to be inside a building - if he was here at all, and not waiting for the prime minister somewhere between this street and the terminal building at La Guardia Airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - a place where a bus, train, or other vehicle regularly stops for passengers or goods

Etymology

From Latin terminalis (of or pertaining to a boundary or end), from terminus (end, boundary).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɝːmɪnl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnl̩ / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) predicted to lead to death, especially slowly; incurable and likely to result in death.

Examples

ROCCO: His medical condition is reported as terminal.

Synset Examples

a terminal patient
terminal cancer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incurable - (of a disease) not able to be cured
  • Synonym: fatal - resulting in death
  • Antonym: benign - (of a disease or tumor) not harmful in effect

Etymology

From Latin terminālis ('pertaining to a boundary or end'), from terminus ('end, boundary').
