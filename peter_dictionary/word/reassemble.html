reassemble

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈsɛmbəl / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsembəl / (BrE)
to put something back together again after it has been taken apart

Examples

There was no weapon Early could not take apart and reassemble blind-folded.
Now reassemble the frame, using * * f roundhead steel screws and nuts.
In vain his mind groped to reassemble the bones of the relationships he had sought so desperately, but they would not come to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemble - to put together the parts of something
  • Antonym: disassemble - to take something apart into its separate components

Etymology

From re- (again) + assemble, ultimately from Latin assemblare (to bring together).
