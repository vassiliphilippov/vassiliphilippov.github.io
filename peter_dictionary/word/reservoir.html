reservoir

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzərˌvwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəvwɑː / (BrE)
A large natural or artificial tank or storage area used for collecting and storing liquids, especially water or oil.

Examples

Natural gas public utility companies would be given the right of eminent domain, under a bill by Sen. Frank Owen /3, of El Paso, to acquire sites for underground storage reservoirs for gas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - a large container for storing liquid or gas

Etymology

From French réservoir, from réserver (to reserve) and ultimately from Latin reservare (to keep back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzərˌvwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəvwɑː / (BrE)
An artificial or natural lake used to store water for use by a community, especially for supplying water to households, industry, or agriculture.

Examples

If the site is on a reservoir, the level of the water at various seasons as it affects recreation should be studied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake - a large body of water surrounded by land
  • Degree Lower: pond - a small body of still water, smaller than a lake

Etymology

From French *réservoir* (storehouse, tank), from *réserver* (to reserve), from Latin *reservare* (to keep back).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛz.ər.vwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛz.ə.vwɑː / (BrE)
A large or extra supply of something that can be drawn upon when needed.

Examples

The last two writers introduce strong political bias into their works, and not unlike the union leaders that we will discuss soon, see folklore as a reservoir of protest by a downtrodden and publically silenced mass.
In the absence of a reservoir of political consensus each organized political group hopes that the elections will give them new prominence, but in a system where there is as yet no place for the less prominent.

Synset Examples

a reservoir of talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - an amount of something kept for future use
  • Synonym: stockpile - a large accumulated stock of goods or materials

Etymology

From French 'réservoir', from Old French ‘reservoir’, from ‘reserver’ (to reserve), from Latin ‘reservare’.
