thirsty

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɜrsti / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsti / (BrE)
needing or lacking moisture; suffering from a need for water or liquid.

Examples

There was the suggestion of ice water, and - in spite of the protest "We 're not really thirsty "- Linda Kay, to escape the stuffy air and the smothering soft voices, hurried to the kitchen.

Synset Examples

thirsty fields under a rainless sky

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: parched - very dry, especially from heat or lack of water
  • Degree Lower: dry - lacking moisture or liquid

Etymology

From Old English 'thurstig', from 'thurst' (thirst) + '-ig' (adjective suffix).
