doom

1. NOUN

/ duːm / (AmE), / duːm / (BrE)
A terrible or inevitable fate, especially one resulting in destruction or ruin.

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".
"The time has come ", the walrus said, "To speak of manic things, Of shots and shouts, and sealing dooms Of commoners and kings ".

Synset Examples

everyone was aware of the approaching doom but was helpless to avoid it
that's unfortunate but it isn't the end of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as determined by a supernatural power
  • Synonym: destiny - the events that will necessarily happen to a particular person or thing in the future
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external, arbitrary force affecting human affairs

Etymology

Old English *dōm* (judgment, decree, fate), from Proto-Germanic *dōmaz*.
