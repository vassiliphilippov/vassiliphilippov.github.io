troupe

1. NOUN

/ truːp / (AmE), / truːp / (BrE)
A group of performers, especially actors or dancers, who work and travel together.

Examples

It was signed by everyone in the troupe.
When the troupe traveled to New York to participate in a one act play competition - and won - Mercer, instead of returning with the rest of the company in triumph, remained in New York.

Synset Examples

the traveling company all stayed at the same hotel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - a group of actors, singers, or dancers performing together

Etymology

From French 'troupe' (band, troop), of Germanic origin.
