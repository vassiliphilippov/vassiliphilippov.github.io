framed

1. ADJ

/ freɪmd / (AmE), / freɪmd / (BrE)
(of an object) having a frame around it for support, protection, or decoration.

Examples

On the third floor one of the two windows was lighted; it was framed in maroon drapes, and no faces were visible.

Synset Examples

there were framed snapshots of family and friends on her desk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unframed - not provided with a frame

Etymology

From the verb 'frame', dating to Middle English from Old English 'framian' (to profit, be useful), later 'frame' in the sense of constructing or forming.
