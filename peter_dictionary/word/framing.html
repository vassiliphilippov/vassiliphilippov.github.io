framing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of formulating plans or setting out the important parts of something, especially in a structured or detailed manner.

Examples

The District Courts, in the framing of equitable decrees, are clothed' with large discretion to model their judgments to fit the exigencies of the particular case'.

Synset Examples

the framing of judicial decrees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formulation - the action of creating or devising a plan or proposal
  • Synonym: drafting - preparing a preliminary version of a plan or document

Etymology

From the verb 'frame' (Middle English *fraimen*, from Old English *framian* 'to profit, avail'), with the noun suffix '-ing'.
