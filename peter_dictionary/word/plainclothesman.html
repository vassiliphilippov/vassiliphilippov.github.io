plainclothesman

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnˌkloʊðzˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌkləʊðzˌmæn / (BrE)
A police officer, typically a detective, who works in civilian clothing instead of a uniform while on duty.

Examples

Two uniformed officers, a couple of plain-clothesmen I knew, and two other men stood on a gray cement area next to the pool on my left.
Lieutenant Rawlins, one of the plain-clothesmen, spotted me and said, "Hi, Shell ", and walked toward me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a police officer who investigates crimes

Etymology

From 'plainclothes' (civilian dress) + 'man', referring to an officer out of uniform.
