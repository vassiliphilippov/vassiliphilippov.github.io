tallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtæləʊ / (BrE)
A hard, fatty substance made from rendered animal fat, used especially in making soap, candles, and lubricants.

Examples

The next time we saw him, he was a changed person; he had aged thirty years, and his face, the color of tallow, was crisscrossed with wrinkles, as though it had been wrapped in chicken wire.

Etymology

From Old English *tealg*, related to Old High German *zalg* (fat).
