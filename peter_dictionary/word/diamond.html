diamond

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəmənd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd / (BrE)
A transparent precious stone consisting of a clear and typically colorless crystalline form of pure carbon, highly valued for its brilliance and used especially in jewelry.

Examples

He was heralded as "Sportsman of the Year "by Sports Illustrated, and last night was acclaimed in Rochester as the "Professional Athlete of the Year ", a distinction that earned for him the $ 10000 diamond - studded Hickok Belt.
In fact, O' Connor was honoured during the ceremony with the presentation of a $ 2500 diamond stickpin.
Then O' Banion was presented with a platinum watch set with rubies and diamonds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a precious or semiprecious stone, especially when cut and polished or engraved
  • Antonym: cubic zirconia - synthetic gemstone used as an imitation of diamond

Etymology

From Old French 'diamant', from Latin 'adamas, adamant-', from Greek 'adamas' meaning 'invincible, untameable (hardest metal)', referring to its hardness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəmənd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd / (BrE)
a precious, very hard clear stone made of pure carbon, often used in jewelry

Examples

And most of all it is not having the only man you could love, whether he drives a bread truck or delivers the mail or checks the berry crates down at the sheds, or owns seventeen oil wells and six diamond mines, for if you are anybody what he is or does makes no difference if he is the one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - precious or semi-precious stone cut and polished for use in jewelry
  • Degree Lower: ruby - red precious stone used as a gemstone
  • Degree Lower: pebble - small stone, often used figuratively for something of little value

Etymology

From Old French diamant, from Latin adamas (invincible, diamond), from Greek adamas (untameable, hard metal or stone).
