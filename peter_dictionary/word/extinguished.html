extinguished

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a conditioned response) no longer elicited, typically because reinforcement has been withdrawn or absent.

Examples

A previously extinguished conditioned reaction was restored in monkey A and was associated with typical signs of emotional excitement including sympathetic discharges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditioned - subject to learning through association or reinforcement

Etymology

From Latin 'extinguere' (to quench, put out), past participle 'extinctus'.
