fizzle

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪzəl / (BrE)
to end in a weak or disappointing way, especially after a promising start

Examples

Momentarily the Reds were back in the ball game, trailing only 6 - 3, but the drive fizzled when John Edwards fouled out with men on second and third and two out.

Synset Examples

The music just petered out--there was no proper ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peter out - to gradually come to an end or stop; diminish
  • Synonym: fail - to be unsuccessful; not achieve the desired result

Etymology

Probably imitative of a sputtering or hissing sound; related to 'fizz'.
