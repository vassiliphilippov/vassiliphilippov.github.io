lamb

1. NOUN

/ læm / (AmE), / læm / (BrE)
A young sheep, especially one that is less than one year old.

Examples

1.0 gram premix per head per day for promoting growth, feed conversion, and getting lambs on full feed earlier.
Treat lambs with 12 grams per head for lambs weighing up to 50 pounds; treat lambs over 50 pounds and adults with 24 grams per animal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sheep - a domesticated ruminant animal with a thick woolly coat
  • Degree Higher: ewe - a female sheep, especially an adult
  • Degree Higher: ram - a male sheep, especially an adult

Etymology

From Old English *lamb* (young sheep), related to German *Lamm* and Dutch *lam*.
