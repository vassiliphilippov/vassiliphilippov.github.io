rivet

1. VERB

/ ˈrɪvət / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪt / (BrE)
to hold (someone's attention) firmly; to focus or concentrate one's attention on something

Examples

Tenderly and rather tediously, the camera rivets on the abrupt, deep love of a pretty nurse and a uniformed teacher, complicated by nothing more than a friend they do n't want to hurt.

Synset Examples

Please focus on your studies and not on your hobbies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to pay close attention; direct attention to
  • Synonym: concentrate - to direct all one's attention to

Etymology

From Old French *riveter*, from *rivet* (nail or fastener). Figurative use of 'rivet' meaning to hold attention is based on the sense of fastening or fixing firmly.
