moralist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person, especially a philosopher, who is concerned with or specializes in morals and moral problems

Examples

Others writing on Faulkner have found the phrase "traditional moralist "either inadequate or misleading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethicist - a person who studies or is an expert in ethics

Etymology

From French *moraliste*, from Latin *moralis* (relating to morals).
