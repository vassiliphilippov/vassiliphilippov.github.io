spade

1. VERB

/ speɪd / (AmE), / speɪd / (BrE)
to dig or turn over soil using a spade

Examples

I spade lots of compost into their bed; lacking that, decayed manure spread over the bed is fine.

Synset Examples

I spade compost into the flower beds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - to break up and move earth with a tool or machine

Etymology

From Old English 'spadu', related to Old Norse 'spaði', meaning a spade or shovel.

2. NOUN

/ speɪd / (AmE), / speɪd / (BrE)
one of the four suits in a standard deck of playing cards, marked with a black symbol shaped like a pointed leaf with a stalk

Examples

"This deal with the ace o' spades.
Anything to do with an ace o' spades, bad luck ".

Synset Examples

she led a low spade
spades were trumps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club - a suit of playing cards, typically marked with black clover-like symbols (♣)
  • Synonym: heart - a suit of playing cards, usually marked with red heart-shaped symbols (♥)
  • Synonym: diamond - a suit of playing cards, usually marked with red rhombus-shaped symbols (♦)

Etymology

Perhaps from the Italian *spada* (sword), from Latin *spatha* (broad sword), reflecting the original shape of the symbol.

3. NOUN

/ speɪd / (AmE), / speɪd / (BrE)
A sturdy hand tool with a flat blade, used for digging, lifting, and moving earth or other materials, typically operated by pushing into the ground with the foot.

Examples

It was nine o' clock in the morning: the hour which, like a spade turning clods of earth, exposed to the day a myriad of busy creatures that had lain dormant in the quiet night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovel - a tool with a broad blade and upturned sides for lifting and moving material

Etymology

From Old English *spadu*, related to Dutch *spade* and German *Spaten* (spade, shovel).
