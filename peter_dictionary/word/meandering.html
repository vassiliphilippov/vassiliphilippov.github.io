meandering

1. ADJ

/ miˈændərɪŋ / (AmE), / miˈændərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a road, river, or path) following a winding and turning course; not straight.

Examples

The slim pirogues in harmony with the majestically meandering Mekong River.
Swinging up then, and bending forward over the horn, he urged his mount down the meandering draw.

Synset Examples

meandering streams
rambling forest paths
the river followed its wandering course
a winding country road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winding - having a twisting or turning course
  • Antonym: straight - extending or moving in one direction without curving

Etymology

Present participle of 'meander', from Latin 'Maeander', a winding river in Phrygia (present-day Turkey).
