midair

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪdˈɛr / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˈeə / (BrE)
a point in the air above the ground or above a surface; the space in the air, not touching the ground or any object.

Examples

For a moment, boy and mount hung in midair.

Synset Examples

the planes collided in midair

Etymology

Formed from 'mid-' (middle) + 'air'.
