oil

1. NOUN

/ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ɔɪl / (BrE)
a slippery or viscous liquid or substance that does not mix with water, often used as a lubricant or fuel

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grease - a thick, oily substance used as a lubricant
  • Degree Higher: oil - petroleum; crude oil used as a fuel
  • Antonym: water - a transparent, tasteless, odorless, and nearly colorless chemical substance

Etymology

From Old French 'oile', from Latin 'oleum' (olive oil, oil), from Greek 'elaion' (olive oil).

3. NOUN

/ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ɔɪl / (BrE)
A dark, thick liquid consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, found underground and used as a fuel and raw material for various products; petroleum.

Examples

We should have expected it after the oil embargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petroleum - crude oil; naturally occurring hydrocarbon liquid
  • Synonym: crude oil - unrefined petroleum; raw natural oil

Etymology

From Latin 'oleum' (oil), from Greek 'elaion' (olive oil).
