chela

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪlə / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪlə / (BrE)
A disciple or pupil of a Hindu spiritual teacher (swami), especially in the context of Indian religious practice.

Examples

His chelas were required to assume the matsyendra posture dressed in hand-woven diapers while he read aloud from Rig-Veda and an assistant guru examined their purses in another room - nothing was stolen; the purpose was less immediate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher or leader

Etymology

From Hindi *celā*, meaning 'pupil' or 'disciple'.
