interrogative

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.t̬əˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrɒɡ.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
A sentence or expression in the form of a question, especially one that is used to seek information.

Examples

Other classes are included only by myself (interrogatives, adverbs) or only by Swadesh and Hoijer (pronouns, demonstratives).

Synset Examples

he asked a direct question
he had trouble phrasing his interrogations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - a sentence meant to elicit information

Etymology

From Latin interrogativus (pertaining to asking questions), from interrogare (to ask).
