seriousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being earnest and sincere in attitude, manner, or intention.

Examples

Instead, the audience can sit back at ease and, from the perspective of an enlightened time which no longer believes in such things, enjoy the dead seriousness with which the characters in the play take the witches and devils which are under discussion.
Perhaps if Felix had first come upon us when this boy was not cavorting so gaily up and down the hall outside the murdered woman's apartment, we might have had less trouble convincing Felix of our seriousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earnestness - the quality of being earnest or sincere
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness; lightness in manner

Etymology

From 'serious' + '-ness', with 'serious' from Latin 'seriōsus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being alarming or distressing, often causing fear or concern.

Examples

Not realizing the seriousness of the wound, the besiegers warned that if he did not surrender the house would be burned down around him.

Synset Examples

he learned the seriousness of his illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the quality of being serious or solemn (in the sense of importance or severity)

Etymology

From serious + -ness; 'serious' from Latin 'serius' (earnest, grave).
