Permian

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.mi.ən / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.ən / (BrE)
A geologic period lasting from about 299 million to 252 million years ago, following the Carboniferous and preceding the Triassic; characterized by the diversification of reptiles and the largest mass extinction in Earth's history.

Examples

After a time, he straightened again, brushing the red Permian dust from his hands, slapping it from his six dollar levis and his tailored, twenty-five dollar shirt.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Carboniferous - The geologic period preceding the Permian
  • Degree Higher: Triassic - The geologic period following the Permian

Etymology

From the province of Perm in Russia, where rocks from this period were first studied.
