enveloping

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛləpɪŋ / (BrE)
surrounding and closing in on or hemming in something or someone

Examples

And the rebellion of these third generation Jews is not the traditional conflict of culture but, rather, a protest against a culture that they view as softly and insidiously enveloping.

Synset Examples

the army's enveloping maneuver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encircling - surrounding in a circle; enclosing all around
  • Synonym: closing - blocking or shutting in completely

Etymology

From the verb 'envelop,' which comes from Old French 'enveloper,' from 'en-' (in) + 'voloper' (to wrap).
