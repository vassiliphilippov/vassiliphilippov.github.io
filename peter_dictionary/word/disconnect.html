disconnect

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to pull the plug of an electrical device so that it stops working; to separate (an appliance, etc.) from the supply of power or a network.

Examples

The phone had been disconnected but telegrams came for him and notes by special messenger.

Synset Examples

unplug the hair dryer after using it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unplug - to pull the plug from an electrical device
  • Antonym: connect - to join or link together, or supply with power

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + connect (from Latin connectere, to bind together).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to detach or separate something so that it is no longer joined, united, or connected; to unfasten or disjoin

Examples

There was even a bare chance that the phone had not been disconnected.
Can you disconnect the tubes? - That's out of the question! - Do you know my father?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connect - to join together or fasten; to link or unite

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + connectere (to bind together).
