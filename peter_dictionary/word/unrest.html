unrest

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈrɛst / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛst / (BrE)
A state of disturbance, agitation, or dissatisfaction, especially involving a group of people and resulting in protest or disorder.

Examples

Add this to the unrest in the countries around us where oppressed peoples would be ready to turn on us at the first opportunity.

Synset Examples

the political ferment produced new leadership
social unrest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement
  • Synonym: disturbance - interruption of a state of peace or order
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and quiet; absence of disturbance

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'rest' (peace, tranquility), meaning 'absence of rest'.
