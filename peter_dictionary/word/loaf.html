loaf

1. NOUN

/ loʊf / (AmE), / ləʊf / (BrE)
A quantity of bread that is shaped and baked in one piece, typically sliced for eating.

Examples

Then, a group of eggs is deposited in a cavity in the beebread loaf and the egg compartment is closed.
When she succeeds, she usually manages to slip in unobtrusively, to deposit an egg on a completed loaf of beebread before the bumblebees seal the egg compartment.
Several sections of a loaf of dark bread; butter; jam; a tiny cake of ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bun - a small, round or oval bread roll

Etymology

From Old English *hlāf* (bread, loaf).

2. VERB

/ loʊf / (AmE), / ləʊf / (BrE)
to spend time in an idle or lazy way; to be inactive or avoid work

Examples

That day they loafed around, just getting the feel of the city.

Synset Examples

Her son is just bumming around all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - not active or not working; avoiding activity
  • Synonym: laze - to spend time in a relaxed, lazy way
  • Antonym: work - to engage in physical or mental activity to achieve a result

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete 'loaf' (to walk idly), origin uncertain, but possibly from German dialect 'laufen' (to run, loaf about).
