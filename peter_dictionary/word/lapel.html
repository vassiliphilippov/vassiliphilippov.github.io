lapel

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɛl / (AmE), / ləˈpɛl / (BrE)
Either of the two folded flaps of cloth on the front of a coat or jacket, extending from the collar downwards.

Examples

I wore a new double-breasted brown worsted suit with a faint herringbone design and wide lapels like a devil's ears.

Etymology

From English 'lap' (flap of a garment) + diminutive suffix '-el'.
