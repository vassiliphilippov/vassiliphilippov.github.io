corral

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræl / (AmE), / kəˈrɑːl / (BrE)
an enclosure or pen for confining cattle or other livestock

Examples

Arriving at daybreak, they found Julio in his corral and demanded that he surrender.
After walking out to his corral that morning, he 'd been amazed to see the dust puff up in front of his feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an enclosure for domestic animals

Etymology

From Spanish 'corral', from Vulgar Latin *currāle (enclosure for vehicles, stable), from Latin 'currus' (cart).

2. VERB

/ kəˈræl / (AmE), / kəˈrɑːl / (BrE)
to gather or enclose (animals, people, or things) in a corral or confined area; to bring together and control.

Examples

A bunch of young buckaroos from out West, who go by the name of Texas Boys Choir, loped into Town Hall last night and succeeded in corralling the hearts of a sizable audience.

Synset Examples

corral the horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - to gather animals together and drive them as a group
  • Antonym: release - to set free from confinement or control

Etymology

From Spanish 'corral' (enclosure for animals), from Vulgar Latin *currale.
