clam

1. NOUN

/ klæm / (AmE), / klæm / (BrE)
A burrowing marine mollusk with a hinged shell, typically found in sand or mud and known for its firm-closing shell.

Examples

Clams, crabs, and other marine life may add interest at coastal areas.
There was the smell of the coast, like a primeval memory, composed of equal parts salt water, clams, seaweed and northern air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a type of bivalve mollusk similar to a clam but with a longer, thinner shell
  • Synonym: oyster - an edible bivalve mollusk with a rough shell, related to the clam

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *clam* (bond, fetter) referring to its clamping shell.
