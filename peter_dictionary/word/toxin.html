toxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A poisonous substance produced naturally by living organisms such as bacteria, plants, or animals.

Examples

Botulinal toxin, for example, is several thousand-fold more toxic by this route than when given per os.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - A substance that causes harm or death when introduced to a living organism

Etymology

From Greek *toxikon* (poison for arrows), from *toxon* (bow).
