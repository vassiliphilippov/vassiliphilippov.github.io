suffix

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈsʌfɪks / (BrE)
An affix added to the end of a word to form a new word or to alter its grammatical function.

Examples

The many linguistic techniques for reducing the amount of dictionary information that have been proposed all organize the dictionary's contents around prefixes, stems, suffixes, etc..
Historically re is the formative that is employed also in republic, and al is the common suffix.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prefix - an affix added to the beginning of a word
  • Synonym: affix - a morpheme added to a word to change its form or meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'suffixus', past participle of 'suffigere' (to fasten on from below), from 'sub-' (under) + 'figere' (to fix).
