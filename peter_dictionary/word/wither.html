wither

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪðər / (AmE), / ˈwɪðə / (BrE)
(of a plant, part of the body, etc.) to become dry and shriveled, often due to loss of moisture.

Examples

Fourteen of the sculptures you took posses properties of a most curious and terrifying nature, as you will observe when your limbs begin to wither and your hair falls out in patches.

Synset Examples

The fruit dried and shriveled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrivel - to become wrinkled or shrunken, especially due to loss of moisture
  • Synonym: wilt - to lose freshness and become limp, especially of plants

Etymology

From Old English 'wiðerian' (to dry up, shrivel).
