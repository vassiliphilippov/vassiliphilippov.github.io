prowl

1. VERB

/ praʊl / (AmE), / praʊl / (BrE)
To move about quietly and stealthily, as if searching for prey or something to steal.

Examples

While prowling around these buildings, two of the posse recognized the voice of Gonzales speaking to the people inside.
I have just asked these questions in the Pentagon, in the White House, in offices of key scientists across the country and aboard the submarines that prowl for months underwater, with neat rows of green launch tubes which contain Polaris missiles and which are affectionately known as "Sherwood Forest ".

Synset Examples

The suspicious stranger prowls the streets of the town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalk - To pursue or approach stealthily

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *prowlen*, of unknown origin; related to 'prow' (to hunt for prey).

2. VERB

/ praʊl / (AmE), / praʊl / (BrE)
to move or wander about with no particular purpose, often in a leisurely or aimless way

Examples

It did not care what sort of person prowled its woods, plucked at its bark or stripped the berries from its bushes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loiter - to stand or wait around idly without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: wander - to move about without a fixed course or purpose

Etymology

From Middle English 'prowlen' (to move about stealthily), of uncertain origin.
