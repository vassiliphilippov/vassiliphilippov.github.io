indoors

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdɔrz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːz / (BrE)
In or into a building; inside rather than outside.

Examples

Often, in working out-of-doors under all conditions of light and atmosphere, a particular passage that looked favorable in relation to the subject will be too bright, too dull, or too light, or too dark when viewed indoors in a mat.

Synset Examples

in winter we play inside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outdoors - outside; in the open air

Etymology

Formed from 'in' + 'doors' (the interior side of doors), dating from the 18th century.
