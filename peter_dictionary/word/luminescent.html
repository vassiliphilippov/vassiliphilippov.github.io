luminescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌluː.məˈnɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌluː.mɪˈnɛs.ənt / (BrE)
Emitting light not produced by heat; showing luminescence.

Examples

The luminosity of the water is now believed to have been caused by the stimulation of vast numbers of the luminescent organism Noctiluca miliaris by the turbulence of the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphorescent - emitting light after exposure to radiation and continuing without heat
  • Synonym: fluorescent - emitting visible light when exposed to ultraviolet light

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light) + English '-escent' (becoming).
