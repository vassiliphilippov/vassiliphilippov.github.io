congested

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɛstɪd / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) abnormally full or blocked, especially with blood or other fluid.

Examples

The influence of Mass was less pervasive than that of the congested, slum tenements among the bawdy houses, honkytonks, and sawdust saloons of his birthplace; he ran wild with the child gangs of the neighbourhood, and went through the normal pressure-cooker course of thieving, police dodging, and housebreaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - obstructed, not able to move or flow
  • Antonym: uncongested - not congested, free from blockage or crowding

Etymology

From Latin congestus, past participle of congerere (to bring together, heap up).
