undereducated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈɛdʒəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɛdʒʊkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having received less education than is considered necessary or desirable.

Examples

In B. M. Spinley's portrayal of the underprivileged and undereducated youth of London, a salient finding was the inability to postpone gratification, a need to satisfy impulses immediately without the pleasure of anticipation or of savoring the experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneducated - lacking education or schooling
  • Antonym: educated - having received a good or satisfactory education

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (not enough) + 'educated'.
