supersonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈsɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of speed) greater than the speed of sound in a given medium, especially air.

Examples

One of the important and difficult decisions which had to be made in this budget concerned the role of the B-70, a long range supersonic bomber.
I am recommending additional acquisitions of the improved version of the B-52 (the B-52 H with the new turbofan engine) and procurement of the B-58 supersonic medium bomber, together with the supporting refueling tankers in each case.

Synset Examples

a supersonic bomber flies so fast that it must release its bombs while the target is still over the horizon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subsonic - slower than the speed of sound

Etymology

From Latin 'super-' (above) + 'sonic' (from Latin 'sonus', meaning sound).
