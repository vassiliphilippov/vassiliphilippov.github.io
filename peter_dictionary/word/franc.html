franc

1. NOUN

/ fræŋk / (AmE), / fræŋk / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit used in several countries, formerly including France, Switzerland, and Belgium, equal to 100 centimes.

Examples

Oersted remodeled Ritter's notes into an essay in French which was submitted to the Institut de France for its annual prize of 3000 francs.

Etymology

From French 'franc', originally meaning 'free', later used as the name of a coin issued in France.
