juxtaposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or placement of two or more things close together, especially to emphasize their differences or create an effect

Examples

The differences between onset age and completion age with respect to the corresponding mean age have been brought into juxtaposition by means of a series of arrows.

Synset Examples

it is the result of the juxtaposition of contrasting colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different; often used in relation to juxtaposition

Etymology

From French juxtaposition, from Latin juxta (near) + positio (position).
