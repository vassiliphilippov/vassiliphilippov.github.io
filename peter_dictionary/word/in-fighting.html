in-fighting

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Secret or hidden conflict and rivalry among members of the same group, organization, or team.

Examples

In all the bitter in-fighting, the squabbles over election procedures, the complicated numbers game that East and West played on the assistant secretaries' theme, the gentleman from Burma showed himself both as a man of principle and a skilled diplomat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discord - disagreement or conflict among people
  • Antonym: cooperation - working together for a common purpose

Etymology

From 'in-' (internal) + 'fighting', meaning fighting within a group.
