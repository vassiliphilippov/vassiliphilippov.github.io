gavel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡævəl / (AmE), / ˈɡævəl / (BrE)
a small hammer used by a judge or presiding officer to signal for attention or order

Examples

[PEOPLE APPLAUDING] [POUNDING GAVEL] MAN: The chairman is calling the meeting back to order.

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from medieval English dialects denoting a tool or implement.
