domain

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
an area of activity, interest, or knowledge; a particular sphere of influence or expertise

Examples

Even in domains where detailed and predictive understanding is still lacking, but where some explanations are possible, as with lightning and weather and earthquakes, the appropriate kind of human action has been more adequately indicated.
There are many domains in which understanding has brought about widespread and quite appropriate reduction in ritual and fear.
In addition, the size distribution obtained from visual and radar observations of meteors may be extrapolated to the micrometeorite domain.
The difference is important, for although the older law of nations did cover relationships among sovereigns, this was by no means its exclusive domain.

Synset Examples

his social sphere is limited
it was a closed area of employment
he's out of my orbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphere - a particular area of activity or interest
  • Synonym: realm - a field or domain of activity or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'dominium' (property, ownership), from 'dominus' (lord, master).

2. NOUN

/ doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
An area of territory owned or controlled by a ruler or government; also, a sphere of influence or control.

Examples

The particles appeared rough and undoubtedly the single crystal domains are smaller than this.
Until 1924 O' Banion pistoleers and knuckle-duster bullyboys had kept his North Side domain solidly Democratic.

Synset Examples

his domain extended into Europe
he made it the law of the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realm - a kingdom or field or domain of activity

Etymology

From Latin 'domin(i)um' (property, right of ownership), from 'dominus' (lord, master).
