siding

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a short stretch of railroad track that is connected to a main line and is used for storing, loading, or unloading trains, or for allowing trains to pass each other

Examples

At a siding, another train which was a familiar sight these days.

Etymology

From the verb 'side' + -ing, originally referring to the side tracks off a main railway line.
