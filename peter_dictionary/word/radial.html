radial

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪ.di.əl / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.di.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or moving along the direction of a radius; extending outward from a central point.

Examples

The radial velocity varies inversely as the particle size - a 1000 - | m diameter particle near the orbit of Mars would reach the sun in about 60 million years.

Synset Examples

radial velocity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: axial - relating to or around an axis

Etymology

From Latin radius (ray, spoke of a wheel).
