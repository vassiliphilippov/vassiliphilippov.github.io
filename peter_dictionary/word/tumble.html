tumble

1. VERB

/ ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəl / (BrE)
to fall suddenly and helplessly, often rolling or turning over as you descend

Examples

Then last season the Birds tumbled as low as 11 - 18 on May 19 before recovering to make a race of it and total 86 victories.
He smashed it in and tumbled into darkness.
Thunder crashed; barrels tumbled down the mountainsides, and bounced and bounced till their own fury split them open.
The snow was tumbling down furiously now.
We tumbled to a stop in Deacon Gordon's cow hole, a low-lying bit of pasture with a muddy pool of water in its middle.

Synset Examples

The tower of the World Trade Center tumbled after the plane hit it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - to move downward, typically rapidly and freely without control
  • Synonym: topple - to fall over, usually by turning onto its side
  • Antonym: rise - to move upward or get up from a lower position

Etymology

Late Middle English: probably of Low German or Dutch origin; related to Dutch tuimelen 'to tumble'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəl / (BrE)
an acrobatic movement involving rolling or turning over end to end, often as part of gymnastics or play

Examples

Arlene had taught them a new way to have fun in their little private area; and they had told their mother about the tumbles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somersault - an acrobatic movement in which a person turns head over heels in the air or on the ground

Etymology

From Middle English tumblen, probably of Germanic origin, related to 'tumble' (verb).

3. VERB

/ ˈtʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to roll over and over, especially in a haphazard or uncontrolled manner

Examples

Stevens was grunting over the last empty pocket when Russ abruptly rose and lunged toward Carmer's hat, which had tumbled half-a-dozen feet away when he first fell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll - move by turning over and over along a surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'tumblen', probably of Germanic origin, related to Old High German 'tūm(b)ōn' (to turn, tumble).

4. VERB

/ ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəl / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to fall or turn over suddenly, especially by pushing

Examples

Russ gave him a brutal thrust that tumbled him over flat on his stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topple - to cause to become unsteady and fall

Etymology

From Middle English tumblen, probably of Germanic origin.
