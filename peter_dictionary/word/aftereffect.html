aftereffect

1. NOUN

/ ˈæftərɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɑːftərɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
A delayed result or consequence that appears after a particular event or action, especially one that is undesirable.

Examples

The parties were on the whole unprepared for elections, while the people were still experiencing post independence let-down and suffering the after effects of poor harvests in 1957.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: side effect - secondary, typically undesirable, effect of a process or drug
  • Antonym: cause - that which gives rise to an effect

Etymology

Formed from 'after' + 'effect', first recorded in English in the 17th century.
