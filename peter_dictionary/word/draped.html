draped

1. ADJ

/ dreɪpt / (AmE), / dreɪpt / (BrE)
covered with or as if with cloth or something similar, especially in loose folds; wrapped or arranged in a flowing or decorative way

Examples

Shot six or eight times the body was draped with Russell's pistol, belt, and cartridges.
The blue draped Virgin was still there, but no one knelt before her now.
The strings were draped around flowers in tall silver bowls (page 261).

Synset Examples

leaf-clothed trees
fog-cloaked meadows
a beam draped with cobwebs
cloud-wrapped peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloaked - covered or concealed, especially in a way that hides
  • Synonym: wrapped - covered or enclosed in a material or cloth

Etymology

Past participle of 'drape,' from French *drap* (cloth, drapery).
