irremediable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌrɪˈmiːdiəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪˈmiːdiəbl / (BrE)
Impossible to cure, correct, or put right; beyond remedy or repair.

Examples

"Wherefore also He [ God ] drove him [ man ] out of Paradise, and removed him far from the tree of life, not because He envied him the tree of life, as some venture to assert, but because He pitied him, [ and did not desire ] that he should continue a sinner for ever, nor that the sin which surrounded him should be immortal, and evil interminable and irremediable.

Synset Examples

an irremediable error
irremediable defects of character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incurable - impossible to cure or heal
  • Synonym: irreparable - impossible to repair or put right
  • Antonym: remediable - capable of being remedied or corrected

Etymology

From Late Latin irremediabilis (not to be cured), from Latin in- (not) + remediabilis (remediable).
