publicise

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
To make something known to the general public; to announce or broadcast publicly.

Examples

That no proof linking me to any criminal conspiracy whether it is called Mafia or Cosa Nostra or whatever other name you wish to give has ever been made public.

Synset Examples

She aired her opinions on welfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicize - to make something known to the public (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'public' + '-ise', modeled after French 'publiciser'.
