peak

1. NOUN

/ pik / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
The period or point of greatest success, activity, or productivity.

Examples

Now, apparently happier under new managers, Mays and Mantle, the perfect players, are behaving as though they 're going to pass those previous peaks.
The nest itself, the structure that in some cases housed about 2000 individuals when the season was at its peak, is now rapidly destroyed by the scavenging larvae of certain beetles and moths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zenith - the time at which something is most powerful or successful
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point of fortune; opposite of peak

Etymology

From Old English *pēac* or Middle French *pic* (pointed top, summit).

2. NOUN

/ pik / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
The highest or greatest possible level or value of something, especially in relation to a measurable quantity such as power or intensity.

Examples

At 100 Amp the 360 cycle ripple was less than 0.5 V (peak to peak) with a resistive load.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ pik / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
the pointed top or highest part of a mountain or hill

Examples

Below the moon splashed world rolled away to insurmountable white peaks; above him the deep blue sky glittered with stars.
