warlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔrˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌlaɪk / (BrE)
disposed to war or inclined towards warfare, aggression, or conflict; favoring military solutions or policies

Examples

Anyone's identification with an international struggle, whether warlike or peaceful, requires absurd oversimplification and intense emotional involvement.
It is extraordinary that a people as proud and warlike as Southerners should have been as docile as they have.

Synset Examples

militant nations
hawkish congressman
warlike policies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving war or violence; inclined to peace
  • Synonym: militant - combative and aggressive in support of a cause

Etymology

From Old English 'wer' (war) + 'lic' (like, having the nature of).
