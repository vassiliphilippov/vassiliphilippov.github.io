meditative

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛdəˌteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪtətɪv / (BrE)
thinking deeply or seriously, especially in a focused or reflective way

Examples

Madden's dark face wore a meditative look.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the 'Byronic hero' - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemplative - thinking about something very carefully or for a long time
  • Synonym: thoughtful - engaged in or involving deep thought
  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention or thoughtful

Etymology

From Latin 'meditativus', from 'meditari' (to meditate).
