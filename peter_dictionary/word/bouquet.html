bouquet

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / buːˈkeɪ / (BrE)
A carefully arranged collection of flowers, often given as a gift or used for decoration.

Examples

But in his dressing room was a large bouquet and a card that read, "We 're with you all the way ".
He was an expert florist, tenderly dextrous in the arrangement of bouquets and wreaths.

Etymology

From French 'bouquet' (small wood; bunch of flowers), from Old French 'bosc' (wood, grove).
