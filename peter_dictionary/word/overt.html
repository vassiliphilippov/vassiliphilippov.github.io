overt

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈvɜrt / (AmE), / əʊˈvɜːt / (BrE)
Done or shown openly; not secret or hidden.

Examples

The major defensive problems are concerned with the possibility of overt military delivery of biological agents from appropriate disseminating devices.

Synset Examples

an overt lie
overt hostility
overt intelligence gathering
open ballots

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed; hidden

Etymology

From Old French *overt* (open), from Latin *apertus* (open, uncovered).
