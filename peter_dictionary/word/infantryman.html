infantryman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnfəntrimæn / (AmE), / ˈɪnfəntrimən / (BrE)
A soldier who fights on foot, typically using small arms, as part of an infantry unit.

Examples

A Federal infantryman wrote to his father shortly after his first skirmish in Virginia: "Dear Pa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantrywoman - a female soldier who serves in the infantry
  • Antonym: cavalryman - a soldier who fights on horseback

Etymology

From 'infantry' (from Italian 'infanteria', from 'infante' meaning child or foot soldier) + 'man'.
