birthright

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜːrθˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθˌraɪt / (BrE)
A right or privilege that a person is entitled to from birth.

Examples

He loathed the idea of drying up swamps or the chills of malaria or of leaving his natural birthright.

Synset Examples

free public education is the birthright of every American child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - something received from parents or ancestors
  • Synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage
  • Antonym: disinherit - to deprive of a birthright or inheritance

Etymology

Formed from 'birth' + 'right', indicating a right from birth.
