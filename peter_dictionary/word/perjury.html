perjury

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːdʒɚi / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
The offense of willfully telling a lie or making a misrepresentation under oath, especially in a court of law.

Examples

He can corroborate our charges on enough counts for this committee to recommend a charge of perjury against Michael Corleone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false witness - the act of giving untrue testimony under oath

Etymology

From Old French perjurie, from Latin perjurium (false oath).
