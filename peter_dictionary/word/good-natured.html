good-natured

1. ADJ

/ ɡʊd ˈneɪtʃərd / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈneɪtʃəd / (BrE)
Having a pleasant, easygoing, and cheerful personality; friendly and amiable.

Examples

Though there was an occasional good-natured chuckle about Marvin Goulding, the Jewish officer from Chicago, singing tearfully about the ould sod, no one really thought it was strange.

Synset Examples

too good-natured to resent a little criticism
the good-natured policeman on our block
the sounds of good-natured play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiable - having a friendly and pleasant manner
  • Antonym: ill-natured - having an unpleasant or unfriendly disposition

Etymology

Formed from 'good' and 'natured', meaning 'having a good nature'.
