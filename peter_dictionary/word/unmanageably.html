unmanageably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈmænɪdʒəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈmanɪdʒəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is difficult or impossible to control or manage.

Examples

What had been an unmanageably powerful introject was now, despite its continuing charge of energy disconcerting to me, sufficiently within control of her ego that she could use it to show me what this introjected mother was like.

Synset Examples

`This house is unmanageably large,' she complained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmanageable - not able to be controlled or handled
  • Antonym: manageably - in a manageable way; in a way that can be controlled or handled

Etymology

From 'unmanageable' + '-ly'.
