splicing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsplaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A joining or junction where two materials, such as lengths of tape, film, or rope, have been connected together.

Examples

There is a break in continuity just before the fourth variation in the "Forellen "movement, and I suspect that this is due to imperfect splicing between sides of the original SPs.

Synset Examples

the break was due to an imperfect splice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splice - to join two ends together, especially of rope or film

Etymology

From the verb 'splice', which comes from Dutch 'splissen' (to splice, to split strands for joining).
