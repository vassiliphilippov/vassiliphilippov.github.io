aptitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈæptɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈæptɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
A natural ability or talent for learning or doing something.

Examples

And the monastic communities were supposed to be made up of volunteers selected only after a novitiate which would test their religious aptitude for monastic rigors, their spiritual athleticism.
Young Mercer showed a remarkable lack of aptitude for both instruments.
His aptitudes and preferences could be given more weight in selecting the proper training.
"Aptitudes include science, chemistry, and art."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: ability - the capacity to do something
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of ability or skill

Etymology

From Latin 'aptitudo' meaning 'fitness' or 'adaptation', from 'aptus' (fit).
