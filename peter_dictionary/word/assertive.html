assertive

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɝːtɪv / (AmE), / əˈsɜːtɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a confident and forceful personality; able to express one's opinions or needs clearly and firmly.

Examples

On the other hand, a husband who always has been vigorous and assertive may suddenly become passive - asking, psychologists say, for reassurance that his wife still finds him desirable.

Synset Examples

an energetic assertive boy who was always ready to argue
pointing directly at a listener is an assertive act

Related Terms

  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Degree Higher: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; characterized by or resulting from aggression
  • Antonym: passive - accepting or allowing what happens without active response or resistance

Etymology

From Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' meaning 'to affirm, assert'.
