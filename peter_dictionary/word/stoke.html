stoke

1. VERB

/ stoʊk / (AmE), / stəʊk / (BrE)
to add fuel to or move fuel in (a fire, furnace, etc.) in order to keep it burning strongly; to stir up a fire or maintain its heat

Examples

Ramsey has stoked up Harry Truman, Henry Cabot Lodge, the King of Morocco, Clement Atlee and other shiny characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - to supply with material for combustion; to feed a fire

Etymology

From Dutch 'stoken' (to stoke, to poke) or Middle Dutch 'stoken' (to stick, thrust)
