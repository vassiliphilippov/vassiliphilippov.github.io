deadlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈdɛd.lɒk / (BrE)
a situation in which progress is impossible because the people involved cannot agree or because conditions prevent advance

Examples

Then Dick Hyde, submarine ball hurler, entered the contest and only five batters needed to face him before there existed a 3 - to 3 deadlock.
In affirming this we have already taken the decisive step in breaking the deadlock into which Bultmann's attempt to formulate such a theology has led.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalemate - a position in which no progress can be made; drawn contest
  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which no progress is possible, especially due to disagreement

Etymology

From dead +‎ lock, originally referring to a lock that could not be moved; figurative sense from the 18th century.
