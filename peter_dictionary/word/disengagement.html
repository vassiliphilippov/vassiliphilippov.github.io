disengagement

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of separating or freeing something or someone from an attachment, connection, or involvement

Examples

This air of disengagement carried over to his apparent attitude toward his things, and people often mistook it for boredom in him or a surrender to repetitious routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detachment - the state of being separate or aloof
  • Antonym: engagement - the act of being engaged or involved

Etymology

From French 'désengagement', from 'désengager' (to disengage), ultimately from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'ingaggiare' (to engage).
