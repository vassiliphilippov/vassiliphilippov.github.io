gymnastic

1. ADJ

/ dʒɪmˈnæstɪk / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnæstɪk / (BrE)
involving or relating to vigorous physical exercises that require strength, flexibility, and agility; energetic or athletic in nature

Examples

Another reason gymnastic study is valuable is that it can be started very early in life.
Russia knows this, and that is why there were over 800000 competing for places as candidates for the Olympic gymnastic team.
Dance teachers can respond to President Kennedy's request not only through their regular dance work, but also through the kind of basic gymnastic work that makes for strength and flexibility.
The last essential to the beginner's gymnastic program is the somersault, or forward roll.

Synset Examples

an acrobatic dance
an athletic child
athletic playing
gymnastic exercises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: athletic - physically strong, active, and fit; relating to athletes or sports
  • Synonym: acrobatic - performing, involving, or adept at spectacular gymnastic feats

Etymology

From Greek gymnastikos (fond of athletic exercises), from gymnazein (to exercise naked, train in athletic exercises).
