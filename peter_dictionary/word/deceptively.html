deceptively

1. ADV

/ dɪˈsɛptɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛptɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that gives a false or misleading impression; so as to make something seem different from what it really is.

Examples

Female individuals of Psithyrus look deceptively like the workers and queens of the bumblebees they victimize.

Synset Examples

the exam looked deceptively easy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misleadingly - in a way that causes someone to believe something that is not true

Etymology

Formed from 'deceptive' + '-ly'; 'deceptive' comes from Latin 'deceptivus', from 'decipere' (to deceive).
