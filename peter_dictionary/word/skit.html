skit

1. NOUN

/ skɪt / (AmE), / skɪt / (BrE)
a short, humorous performance or scene, usually as part of a variety show or revue

Examples

A crowd of 1400 is expected for the ceremonies, which will be followed by the show in which the writers will lampoon baseball personalities in skit, dance and song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a short, humorous scene or play; similar in meaning to 'skit'

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the early 18th century.
