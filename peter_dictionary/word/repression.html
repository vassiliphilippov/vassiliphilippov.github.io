repression

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or state of controlling people or groups by force, often limiting their freedoms or rights

Examples

Many Americans reacted irrationally to the challenge of Russia and turned to the repression of ideas by force.

Synset Examples

the long repression of Christian sects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppression - the act of stopping something by force
  • Synonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free

Etymology

From Latin repressio (a pressing back), from reprimere (to press back).
