leavened

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛvənd / (AmE), / ˈlɛvənd / (BrE)
(of bread or other baked goods) made light and porous by the addition of a leavening agent such as yeast or baking powder.

Examples

"It was always the spirit with Christ; matters such as leavened or unleavened bread are inconsequential.

Synset Examples

leavened bread
well-leavened
yeast-leavened breads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unleavened - (of bread or other baked goods) made without a leavening agent such as yeast; not risen

Etymology

From 'leaven', from Old French *levain*, from Latin *levamen*, from *levare* (to raise).
