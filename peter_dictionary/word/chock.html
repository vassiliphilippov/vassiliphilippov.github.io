chock

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑk / (AmE), / tʃɒk / (BrE)
a block, typically of wood or metal, placed against a wheel or heavy object to prevent it from moving or rolling

Examples

From the time the chocks were pulled until the plane was out of sight, he knew Donovan would keep his back to the strip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a piece of wood, metal, or other material tapered to a thin edge and used for splitting, lifting, or tightening objects

Etymology

Probably from Old French choque (block), of uncertain origin.
