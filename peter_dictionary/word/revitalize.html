revitalize

1. VERB

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to give new life, energy, or strength to something or someone; to make something vigorous or active again

Examples

But those who would revitalize so complex an organization must, first of all, overcome the resistance of layers of officials wedded to traditional procedures, suspicious of innovation and fearful of mistakes.

Synset Examples

This food revitalized the patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reinvigorate - to give new energy or strength to someone or something
  • Antonym: weaken - to make or become less strong

Etymology

Formed from 're-' (again) and 'vitalize' (give life), first attested in the late 19th century.
