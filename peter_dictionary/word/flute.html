flute

1. NOUN

/ fluːt / (AmE), / fluːt / (BrE)
A high-pitched woodwind musical instrument consisting of a slender tube, typically closed at one end, with finger holes and a mouthpiece where air is blown across an opening.

Examples

He adjusted the hat, lifted the horn to his lips as though it were a flute, and fell in alongside our fingerprint expert to cavort with him.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: piccolo - a very small flute, pitched an octave higher

Etymology

From Old French 'flaüte', from Old Provençal 'flaüt', probably imitative of the sound produced or from Latin 'flare' (to blow).
