handling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændlɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of moving, carrying, or dealing with something, especially using the hands or machinery.

Examples

The grand jury took a swipe at the State Welfare Department's handling of federal funds granted for child welfare services in foster homes.
The use of bulk handling is continuously growing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: management - the process of dealing with or controlling things or people

Etymology

From the verb 'handle' + -ing, first recorded in Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of touching, holding, or manipulating something with the hands or by mechanical means

Examples

For prevention of these diseases during periods of stress such as shipping, excessive handling, vaccination, extreme weather conditions: 350 milligrams per head per day for 30 days only.
A tranquilizer fed to cattle (other than lactating dairy cows) prior to their being subjected to stress conditions such as vaccinating, shipping, weaning calves, and excessive handling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manipulation - the act of handling or controlling something skillfully
  • Synonym: touch - to make physical contact with something, especially with the hand

Etymology

From 'handle' + -ing, where 'handle' is from Middle English 'handlen', from hand.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhændlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of managing, controlling, or dealing with something or someone

Examples

Circumstances gave her almost undisputed sway over child rearing, money handling and home maintenance.

Synset Examples

the handling of prisoners
the treatment of water sewage
the right to equal treatment in the criminal justice system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: management - the process of dealing with or controlling things or people
  • Synonym: treatment - the manner in which someone behaves toward or deals with someone or something

Etymology

From 'handle' + '-ing', originally Middle English 'handlen' (to touch, manage) from Old English 'handlian'.
