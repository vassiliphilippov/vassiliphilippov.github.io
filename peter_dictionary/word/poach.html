poach

1. VERB

/ poʊtʃ / (AmE), / pəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to hunt or catch animals illegally, especially on land that belongs to someone else or in protected areas

Examples

After Larkin has been persuaded to restock his tangled acres with pheasants, he poaches only what he needs for the nourishment of his family and local callers.

Synset Examples

people are poaching elephants for their ivory

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hunt - to pursue and kill or capture animals; general sense of hunting

Etymology

Probably from Middle French *pocher* (to poke), related to trespassing or encroaching.

2. VERB

/ poʊtʃ / (AmE), / pəʊtʃ / (BrE)
To cook food, especially an egg, fish, or fruit, gently in simmering or nearly boiling liquid.

Examples

Poach the apples in this syrup for twelve minutes, drain them, and cool.

Synset Examples

poached apricots

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boil - cook food in rapidly boiling water
  • Synonym: simmer - cook in gently bubbling liquid

Etymology

From Middle French *pocher* (to boil eggs without their shell).
