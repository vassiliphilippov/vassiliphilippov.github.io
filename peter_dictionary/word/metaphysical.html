metaphysical

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy that deals with the fundamental nature of reality and existence; of or pertaining to metaphysics.

Examples

If the fourth dimension is a physical concept and not purely metaphysical, through what medium does it extend?
While it is easy enough to ridicule Hawkins' pronouncement in Pleas of the Crown from a metaphysical point of view, the concept of the "oneness "of a married couple may reflect an abiding belief that the communion between husband and wife is such that their actions are not always to be regarded by the criminal law as if there were no marriage.

Synset Examples

metaphysical philosophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosophical - relating to philosophy

Etymology

From Greek meta- (beyond) + physika (physics), referring to the works of Aristotle that came 'after the Physics.'
