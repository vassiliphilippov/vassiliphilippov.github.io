calcification

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkalsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which calcium or calcium salts build up in body tissue, causing the tissue to harden.

Examples

Third, the process of calcification is seen to begin later and to continue much longer for these boys than for the girls, a fact which confirms data for other groups of children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ossification - the process of bone formation; a related type of hardening in tissues

Etymology

From Latin 'calx' (lime, limestone) and the English suffix '-ification'.
