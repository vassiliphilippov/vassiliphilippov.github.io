reprisal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpraɪzəl / (AmE), / rɪˈprʌɪzəl / (BrE)
an act of retaliation, especially against an enemy or opponent, often in the context of war or conflict

Examples

What he was about became clear to her with the circulation of another broadside proclamation by General McClellan, threatening reprisals against Rebel guerrillas.
It would be the most severe reprisal, short of declared war, that the United States could invoke against Castro.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retaliation - the act of returning like for like, especially revenge
  • Synonym: revenge - the action of inflicting hurt or harm in return for an injury or wrong

Etymology

From Old French 'reprisaille', from Medieval Latin 'reprensalia', meaning 'act of taking back'.
