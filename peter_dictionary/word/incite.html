incite

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to encourage or stimulate someone to take action, often by urging or provoking them

Examples

Sections of tumors incited by WTV were not similarly stained with conjugated normal serum or conjugated antiserum to potato yellow-dwarf virus.

Synset Examples

This moved me to sacrifice my career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivate - to provide someone with a reason for doing something
  • Synonym: provoke - to stimulate or incite someone to do or feel something, especially by arousing anger
  • Antonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm for something

Etymology

From Latin incitare, from in- (toward) + citare (to put in motion, urge).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to encourage or provoke someone to act in a violent or unlawful way; to stir up strong feelings or actions.

Examples

On October 31, 1859, John Brown was found guilty of treason against the state of Virginia, inciting slave rebellion, and murder.

Synset Examples

incite a riot
set off great unrest among the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke - to stimulate or give rise to a reaction or emotion
  • Synonym: instigate - to bring about or initiate (an action or event)
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone or something peaceful or less agitated

Etymology

From Latin incitāre (to urge on, excite).
