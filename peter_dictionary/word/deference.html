deference

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfɚəns / (AmE), / ˈdɛfərəns / (BrE)
polite respect shown for the wishes or opinions of someone else

Examples

At the inn, which was situated close to a broad weir, Byron was greeted by the landlord with obsequious deference and addressed as "milord ".

Synset Examples

his deference to her wishes was very flattering
be sure to give my respects to the dean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
  • Antonym: disrespect - lack of respect or courtesy

Etymology

From French *déférence*, from déférer (to yield), from Latin *deferre* (to yield, to bring down).
