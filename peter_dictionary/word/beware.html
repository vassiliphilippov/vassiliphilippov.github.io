beware

1. VERB

/ bɪˈwɛr / (AmE), / bɪˈweə / (BrE)
To be careful or cautious about something or someone that may be dangerous or cause trouble.

Examples

Let evildoers contemplate their ways, and let every man beware "!
Also, beware of open end policies, especially in the medical field.
Beware!

Synset Examples

Beware of telephone salesmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch out - to look out for danger or be careful
  • Synonym: caution - care taken to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: ignore - to disregard or pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Middle English 'be ware', meaning 'be wary or cautious'.
