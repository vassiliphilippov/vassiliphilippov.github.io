acidity

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being acidic; the degree to which a substance has acid properties or tastes sour.

Examples

Leaves were burning somewhere and the smoke smelled, for all its ammoniac acidity, of beginnings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalinity - the quality of being alkaline; opposite of acidity

Etymology

From French 'acidité', from Latin 'aciditas', from 'acidus' (sour, acid).
