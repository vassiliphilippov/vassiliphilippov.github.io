clumsily

1. ADV

/ ˈklʌmzəli / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzɪli / (BrE)
In a clumsy way; in a manner that lacks skill or grace.

Examples

His broad flat fingers, petrified into massive sausages by years of bricklaying, snatched the bills clumsily.

Synset Examples

he snatched the bills clumsily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardly - in an awkward manner
  • Antonym: gracefully - in a graceful manner
  • Antonym: skillfully - with skill and dexterity

Etymology

Formed from 'clumsy' + '-ly'; 'clumsy' from Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'klumsa'.
