smuggler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmʌɡlər / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɡlə / (BrE)
a person who imports or exports goods illegally, especially to avoid paying customs duties or taxes

Examples

On the shores north and south, the fishers and mooncursers - smugglers - lived along the churning Great South Bay and the narrow barrier of sand, Fire Island.
No doubt John Hancock would do well now; war was a smugglers' heaven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trafficker - a person who deals or trades in something illegal
  • Antonym: customs officer - an official who checks goods entering or leaving a country

Etymology

From 'smuggle' (to import or export secretly), probably of Low German origin (compare Low German 'smuggeln').
