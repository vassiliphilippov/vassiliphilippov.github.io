lobby

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑː.bi / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.i / (BrE)
a large entrance or reception area in a building, such as a hotel, theater, or office, where people may wait or gather

Examples

Hub, nosing about, spotted reporters in the lobby, so Andy was hustled away quietly through the hotel's service entrance in a strange car which Hub had procured somewhere.
Better stay in the hotel lobby, where the walls looked good and thick.
Hotel attendants pulled parked bicycles into the lobby.
His letter had suggested we meet at my hotel at noon on Sunday, and I came into the lobby as the clock struck twelve.
He crossed the lobby and rode up in the elevator lost in his own thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foyer - an entrance hall or open area in a public building, especially a theater or hotel
  • Degree Lower: hallway - a corridor or passage in a building

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *laubia* (covered walk, arcade), from Old High German *laubja* (arbor, porch).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɑː.bi / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.i / (BrE)
to try to influence a politician or government official on an issue, especially for political or economic advantage

Examples

As far as they were aware, the Connecticut Association of Real Estate Boards had not officially opposed the bill's passage or lobbied in its support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petition - to officially request or urge for action, especially in writing

Etymology

From lobby (noun), originally referring to the entrance hall where such influencing would take place.
