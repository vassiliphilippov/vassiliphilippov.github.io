gap

1. NOUN

/ ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡap / (BrE)
a narrow opening or space between two objects or surfaces

Examples

The gap between the bookshelf and the record cabinet grows smaller with each new recording catalogue.
To reduce heat transfer from the hot gas to this anode holder outside the region of the arc, a carbon shield was attached to the surface providing an air gap of 1/16 inch between the plate and the surface of the anode holder.

Synset Examples

he opened the window a crack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - a very narrow opening or split in something

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gap' (opening, chasm), related to Old English 'gæpian' (to gape).

2. NOUN

/ ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡap / (BrE)
a conspicuous difference between two amounts, groups, or ideas, especially one that is significant or problematic

Examples

the first hearing was fascinating and the second disillusioning as the gap between sound and substance became clearer.
Fourth, the two indicators are for the most part widely separated chronologically, with the extensive age gap occurring during childhood for all but one growth center.
As the historic processes of modernization gradually gain momentum, their cohesion will be threatened by divisive forces, the gaps between rulers and subjects, town and country, will widen; new aspirants for power will emerge whose ambitions far exceed their competence; old rulers may lose their nerve and their sense of direction.

Synset Examples

gap between income and outgo
the spread between lending and borrowing costs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparity - a great difference
  • Synonym: divide - a difference or separation between groups
  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar; likeness

3. NOUN

/ ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡæp / (BrE)
An open or empty space in or between things; a break or opening in a surface, object, or area.

Examples

But there remained one mysterious, unexplored gap, far to the north.

Synset Examples

there was a small opening between the trees
the explosion made a gap in the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening - an open space that allows passage or access
  • Synonym: hole - an opening through something
  • Degree Lower: crack - a narrow opening, usually caused by splitting or breaking
  • Degree Higher: chasm - a deep fissure or gorge; a much larger gap

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gap', meaning 'chasm, opening'.
