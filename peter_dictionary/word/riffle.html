riffle

1. VERB

/ ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
to turn over or move through something, such as papers or pages, quickly and lightly; to flip or flutter through.

Examples

At the end of the room there was a desk heaped with papers, and she began to riffle these, making sighs and and noises of girlish exasperation.

Synset Examples

the paper flicked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip through - to turn through pages quickly, usually browsing or searching
  • Synonym: flick - to move something quickly with a light movement

Etymology

Perhaps an alteration of 'ripple', influenced by 'ruffle'. First attested in the 18th century.
