essay

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛseɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛseɪ / (BrE)
a short attempt or try at doing something, especially in a new or unaccustomed field

Examples

Still, the network's willingness to experiment in this musical field is to be commended, and future essays happily anticipated.
He worked in oil for years before beginning his work in watercolor, and his first public recognition and early honors, including his election to the Academy, were for his essays in the heavier medium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attempt - an effort to achieve or complete something
  • Synonym: trial - a test or experiment to see how something works

Etymology

From French *essai* (trial, attempt), from Late Latin *exagium* (weighing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛˌseɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛseɪ / (BrE)
a short piece of writing on a particular subject, especially one done by students as part of their studies or as a literary form expressing the author's argument or point of view

Examples

When I try to work out my reasons for feeling that this passage is of critical significance, I come up with the following ideas, which I shall express very briefly here and revert to in a later essay.
A second major point of this essay is to examine the formal arrangements for the elections.
He is in a hurry to write another essay against culture.
In this third year at the university, Hans, in 1797, was awarded the first important token of recognition, a gold medal for his essay on "Limits of Poetry and Prose ".
He completed his training in pharmacy also, taking his degree with high honors in 1797, and in 1799 was awarded the degree of Doctor of Philosophy along with a prize for an essay in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: article - a piece of writing included with others in a newspaper, magazine, or other publication

Etymology

From French 'essai' (trial, attempt), from Late Latin 'exagium' (a weighing), from Latin 'exigere' (to examine, test).

3. VERB

/ ɪˈseɪ / (AmE), / ɪˈseɪ / (BrE)
to make an attempt or effort to do something, especially something difficult or unfamiliar

Examples

The infant, in white terry-cloth bathrobe, her face intense and purposeful, had essayed a few wobbly steps toward her father.

Synset Examples

He tried to shake off his fears
The infant had essayed a few wobbly steps
The police attempted to stop the thief
He sought to improve himself
She always seeks to do good in the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to attempt to do something
  • Synonym: attempt - to make an effort to accomplish something

Etymology

From Old French 'essayer' (to attempt), from Late Latin 'exagiare' (to weigh); related to the noun 'essay'.
