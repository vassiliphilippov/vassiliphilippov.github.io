impunity

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpjunəti / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjuːnəti / (BrE)
Exemption from punishment, harm, or loss; freedom from the consequences of an action.

Examples

You 've already sent your daughter to Miss X's select academy for girls and your son to Mr. Y's select academy for boys, and you can be as liberal as you please with strict impunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - protection or exemption from something, especially an obligation or penalty
  • Antonym: punishment - the infliction or imposition of a penalty as retribution for an offense

Etymology

From Latin 'impunitas' (freedom from punishment), from 'im-' (not) + 'poena' (punishment).
