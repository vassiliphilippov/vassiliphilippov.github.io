apiece

1. ADV

/ əˈpis / (AmE), / əˈpiːs / (BrE)
for or to each person or thing; for each one individually

Examples

The biggest nuclear device the United States has exploded measured some 15 megatons, although our B-52s are said to be carrying two 20 - megaton bombs apiece.
They get all the glory and most of the pay and do n't earn ten cents apiece on the average, the drunken rascals ".
Two shots apiece in the head as soon as you come out.

Synset Examples

they received $10 each

Related Terms

  • Synonym: each - used to refer to every one of two or more people or things, regarded and identified separately

Etymology

From Middle English 'a piece' (for each piece), using the preposition 'a' meaning 'per' or 'for each'.
