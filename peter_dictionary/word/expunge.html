expunge

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspʌndʒ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌndʒ / (BrE)
to erase or remove something completely, especially from a record or written document

Examples

In this manner, he seeks to expunge from his own soul the guilt pangs caused by his personal assaults against the English at Dunkirk.

Synset Examples

Please strike this remark from the record
scratch that remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erase - to remove written or recorded marks completely
  • Synonym: delete - remove or wipe out, especially from written material or records

Etymology

From Latin expungere (to prick out, blot out), from ex- (out) + pungere (to prick).
