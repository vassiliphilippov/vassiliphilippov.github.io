suspicious

1. ADJ

/ səˈspɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃəs / (BrE)
Openly distrustful of someone or something; unwilling to confide in or trust others.

Examples

With that he hurried up the stairs, followed by her suspicious gaze.
Essentially this imposed two conditions: First, international law had to recognize and be compatible with an international political system in which a number of states were competitive, suspicious, and opportunistic in their political alignments with one another; second, it had to be compatible with the value system that they shared.
And we are positive of so few and suspicious of so many ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trusting - inclined to trust other people easily
  • Synonym: leery - openly distrustful and unwilling to confide

Etymology

From Medieval Latin suspiciosus, from Latin suspicere (to look up at, mistrust).

2. ADJ

/ səˈspɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃəs / (BrE)
Causing a feeling that something is wrong, dishonest, or not as expected; arousing suspicion or distrust.

Examples

"Surely, Captain, you did not find my name on any suspicious paper or anything ".

Synset Examples

there was something fishy about the accident
up to some funny business
some definitely queer goings-on
a shady deal
her motives were suspect
suspicious behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fishy - seeming suspicious or not quite right
  • Synonym: shady - dishonest or of dubious character
  • Synonym: questionable - doubtful or suspicious in character
  • Antonym: trustworthy - deserving of trust or confidence

Etymology

From Latin suspiciosus ‘full of suspicion.’
