internationally

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈnæʃ.ən.əli / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈnæʃ.nəli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or affects two or more countries; across the world.

Examples

Stowey Rummel was internationally famous, a crafter of a genuine Americana in foreign eyes, an original designer whose inventive childishness with steel and concrete was made even more believably sincere by his personality.
If we cannot stop warfare in our own economic system, how can we expect to abolish it internationally?

Synset Examples

She is internationally known

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationally - within a single nation; domestically

Etymology

From 'international' + '-ly', formed on the pattern of 'nationally'. 'International' from inter- (between) + national.
