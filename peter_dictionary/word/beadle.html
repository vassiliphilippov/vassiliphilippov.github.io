beadle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːdəl / (AmE), / ˈbiːdəl / (BrE)
a minor parish official, especially in the Church of England, who carries out various ceremonial duties

Examples

Before them stalked the beadle, proclaiming as he went, "Thus the Council deals with those who break its laws - adulterers, thieves, murderers, and lewd persons.

Etymology

From Old French *bedel*, from Medieval Latin *bedellus*, from Old High German *bital* meaning 'herald, messenger'.
