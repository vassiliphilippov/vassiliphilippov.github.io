thyrotoxic

1. ADJ

/ ˌθaɪrəˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌθaɪrəʊˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by hyperthyroidism; having signs or symptoms caused by an excessive production of thyroid hormones.

Examples

The importance of knowing in what chemical forms the hormone may exist is accentuated by the recent observation that there exists an abnormally long-acting TSH in blood drawn from many thyrotoxic patients (Adams, 1958).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperthyroid - having or relating to an overactive thyroid gland
  • Antonym: hypothyroid - having or relating to an underactive thyroid gland

Etymology

From 'thyro-' (referring to the thyroid gland) + 'toxic' (poisonous or harmful).
