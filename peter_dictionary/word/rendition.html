rendition

1. NOUN

/ rɛnˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / rɛnˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular performance or interpretation of a musical composition, dramatic role, or similar work

Examples

Some renditions are of symphonic dimensions, with the contrabass given free rein.
Certain pianistic traits are common to all five Schnabelian renditions, most notably the "Schnabel trill " (which differs from the conventional trill in that the two notes are struck simultaneously).

Synset Examples

they heard a live rendition of three pieces by Schubert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performance - the act of performing a piece of music or a role in a play
  • Synonym: interpretation - a way of performing or expressing a work of art; a particular style or understanding

Etymology

From Latin *redditionem* (a giving back, a restoring), via Old French *rendition*.
