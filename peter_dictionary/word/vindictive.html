vindictive

1. ADJ

/ vɪnˈdɪktɪv / (AmE), / vɪnˈdɪktɪv / (BrE)
Having or showing a strong or unreasoning desire for revenge; motivated by a wish to hurt or punish others.

Examples

You have unwittingly set in motion forces so malign, so vindictive, that it would be downright inhumane of me not to warn you about them.

Synset Examples

a despiteful fiend
a truly spiteful child
a vindictive man will look for occasions for resentment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiteful - full of spite; showing malicious ill will
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm
  • Antonym: forgiving - willing to forgive; not vindictive

Etymology

From Latin vindicta (revenge) via medieval Latin vindictivus.
