self-sacrifice

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlfˌsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfˌsak.rɪ.faɪs / (BrE)
The act of giving up one's own interests, needs, or wishes for the sake of others or for the greater good.

Examples

It was neither a spirit of self-sacrifice nor a yen to encourage the downtrodden that motivated Arnold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selflessness - lack of concern for one's own interests, especially for the benefit of others
  • Antonym: selfishness - concerned excessively or exclusively with oneself

Etymology

From 'self' + 'sacrifice', the latter from Latin sacrificium (an offering to a deity).
