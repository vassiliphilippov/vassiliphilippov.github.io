noninterference

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
The practice or policy of not becoming involved in the affairs or disputes of others, especially in political or international contexts.

Examples

After only eighteen years of non-interference, there were already indications of melioration, though "in a slight degree ", to be sure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interference - the act of intervening in affairs of others
  • Antonym: intervention - the action of becoming involved in a situation to influence it

Etymology

From non- (not) + interference, from interfere (from French 'interférer', from Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'ferire' (to strike)).
