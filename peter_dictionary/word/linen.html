linen

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnən / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
A fabric made from flax fibers, known for its strength, coolness, and smooth texture, used especially for clothing and household items.

Examples

Here he sketched, sitting in their flowing gowns of linen and silk, young girls not yet twenty, some about to be married, some married a year or two.
Argiento made him lie down on the bed, brought a pan of hot water, dipped some clean white linen cloth and applied it to extract the splinter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - soft, white fibrous substance from the cotton plant, used to make fabric

Etymology

From Latin linum (flax), via Old French lin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnən / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
A high-quality paper made from linen fibers or with a linen-like texture, often used for stationery or official documents.

Examples

"My nephews will be coming down ", she said that evening as Angelina brought her dinner into the dining room, the whole meal on a vast linen covered tray.

Etymology

From Latin *linum* (flax), referring to the plant used to make the fabric, later applied to the paper made from its fibers.
