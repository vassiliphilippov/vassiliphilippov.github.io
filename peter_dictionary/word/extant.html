extant

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈstænt / (AmE), / ɛkˈstænt / (BrE)
(especially of documents, artifacts, or species) still in existence; surviving, not destroyed or lost.

Examples

It is possible, of course, to work on extant or projected buildings where either architect or owner will explain their necessities so that the student may get "the feel "of real interior design demands.

Synset Examples

extant manuscripts
specimens of graphic art found among extant barbaric folk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extinct - no longer in existence

Etymology

From Latin 'exstans', present participle of 'exstāre' (to stand out, exist).
