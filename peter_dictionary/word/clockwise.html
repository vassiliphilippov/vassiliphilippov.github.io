clockwise

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɑkˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈklɒkˌwaɪz / (BrE)
Moving or tending to move in the same direction as the hands of a clock.

Examples

If the vertex is at f, and if the interior of C is on the left as one moves in the direction of increasing t, then every such corner can be found from the curve obtained by rotating C clockwise** through 90 ` about the vertex.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticlockwise - in the opposite direction to the rotating hands of a clock
  • Antonym: counterclockwise - in the opposite direction to the rotating hands of a clock (American English)

Etymology

Formed from 'clock' + 'wise' (direction or manner).
