suffocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of killing or causing death by depriving someone of oxygen; asphyxiation.

Examples

"He's just heard from the pathologist who says Mrs. Meeker apparently died from suffocation ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphyxiation - death or unconsciousness by lack of oxygen

Etymology

From Latin suffocatio, from suffocare (to choke).
