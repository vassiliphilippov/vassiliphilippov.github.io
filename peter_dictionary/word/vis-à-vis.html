vis-à-vis

1. ADV

/ vi zə ˈvi / (AmE), / viː zɑː ˈviː / (BrE)
Used as an adverb to mean 'face-to-face with' or 'in relation to; compared with'.

Examples

This is demonstrated especially when there is awareness of radically different value orientation elsewhere; for example Americans rally to Christian values vis-a-vis those of atheistic communism.

Synset Examples

they sat vis-a-vis at the table
I found myself vis-a-vis a burly policeman

Etymology

From French 'vis-à-vis' meaning 'face to face'.
