disillusioning

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈljuːʒənɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to lose their illusions or false beliefs; making someone realize that something is not as good as they thought.

Examples

the first hearing was fascinating and the second disillusioning as the gap between sound and substance became clearer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disappointing - not as good as hoped, leading to feelings of letdown
  • Antonym: enchanting - delighting or charming someone, often by creating illusions or unrealistic hope

Etymology

From 'disillusion' + '-ing', where 'disillusion' comes from French 'désillusion' (disappointment) and earlier from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'illusio' (mockery, illusion).
