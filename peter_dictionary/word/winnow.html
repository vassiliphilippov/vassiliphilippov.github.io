winnow

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ / (BrE)
to remove chaff from grain by blowing air or allowing the wind to blow through it, so that only the grain remains

Examples

But what a super Herculean task it is to winnow anything of value from the mud beplastered arguments used so freely, particularly since such common use is made of cliches and stereotypes, in themselves declarations of intellectual bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

She stood there winnowing chaff all day in the field

Etymology

Old English *windwian*, from *wind* ('air in motion')
