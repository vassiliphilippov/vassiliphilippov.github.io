gleam

1. VERB

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
to shine with a soft or reflected light, often as if wet or polished

Examples

In the half darkness the banisters gleamed, and the hall seemed enormous.
She smiled, and the teeth gleamed in her beautifully modeled olive face.
Her little brown face wrinkled up, her brown eyes gleamed, and with her little gestures she said all the courteous things.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glitter - shine with a bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • Synonym: glow - give out a steady light
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny; lacking brightness

Etymology

Possibly of Germanic origin; related to Middle English 'glemen' (to shine, gleam), and Old English 'glēam' (joy, brightness).

2. NOUN

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
A small, bright or reflected light, especially one that shines briefly or faintly.

Examples

It gave the rubberized fabric a dull gleam, like metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimmer - a faint or wavering light
  • Synonym: shine - to reflect or emit light
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or shine

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *gleme* (radiance, brightness), from Old English *glǣm* (brightness, splendor).

3. VERB

/ ɡlim / (AmE), / ɡliːm / (BrE)
To shine with a bright or steady light, especially with a soft or reflected glow.

Examples

O beautiful for patriot dream that sees beyond the years thine alabaster cities gleam undimmed by human tears.
I was proud of the new buildings which housed Diario now: the rotogravures, gleaming behind glass doors; the thump and whir of our new presses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - emit a steady light without flame
  • Synonym: shine - emit or reflect light
  • Degree Higher: flash - shine briefly or suddenly
  • Degree Lower: glimmer - shine faintly with a wavering light

Etymology

From Middle English *glemen* (to shine), from Old English *glǣman* (to shine, gleam), related to *glæm* (gleam, brightness).
