cognizance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑɡ.nɪ.zəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.nɪ.zəns / (BrE)
Knowledge or awareness of something; conscious perception or recognition.

Examples

The Outdoor Education Project took cognizance of the fact, so often overlooked, that athletic activities stressed in most school programs have little or no relationship to the physical and mental needs and interests of later life.

Synset Examples

he had no awareness of his mistakes
his sudden consciousness of the problem he faced
their intelligence and general knowingness was impressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awareness - knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Old French 'cognissance' (knowledge), from Latin 'cognoscere' (to know).
