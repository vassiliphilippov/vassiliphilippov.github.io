whiskey

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic drink distilled from fermented grain mash, especially barley, rye, or corn, and typically aged in wooden casks.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Irish uisce beatha 'water of life', via Gaelic uisge beatha.
