epiphysis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪfəsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪfɪsɪs / (BrE)
The rounded end part of a long bone, which is initially separated from the main bone by cartilage and later fuses after ossification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diaphysis - the shaft or central part of a long bone

Etymology

From Greek epiphysis, from epi- (upon) + physis (growth).
