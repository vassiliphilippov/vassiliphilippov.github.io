war-ridden

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔr ˌrɪdən / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌrɪdən / (BrE)
(of a place, period, or society) severely affected by war; engaged in or suffering from ongoing conflict or warfare.

Examples

When Napoleon's ship had borne him to Elba, French wines had started to cross the Channel, the first shipments in a dozen war-ridden years, but the supplies had not yet reached rural hostelries where the sweet wines of the Spanish peninsula still ruled.

Synset Examples

belligerent (or warring) nations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - free from war or conflict

Etymology

Formed from 'war' and the adjectival suffix '-ridden', meaning afflicted or dominated by.
