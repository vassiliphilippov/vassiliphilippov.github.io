spy

1. VERB

/ spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
to catch sight of something or someone, especially after searching or with difficulty

Examples

South of Laurel Grove Cemetery, and below the junction of the Neversink and the Delaware, was the Tri-State Rock, from which Stevie could spy New Jersey and Pennsylvania, as well as New York, simply by spinning around on his heel.
A driver cruising easily at 70 m. p. h. in Lane A of a four-lane freeway spies an incipient traffic jam ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espy - to catch sight of, especially at a distance or after searching
  • Synonym: notice - become aware of; observe or pay attention to
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to see or notice

Etymology

From Old French espier (to observe), from Frankish *spāhōn, related to Old High German spāhōn (to spy) and Old English spīwan (to look out).

2. NOUN

/ spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to collect secret information about the activities, plans, or intentions of a foreign government, enemy, or competitor

Examples

"Oh he's just an international spy ", Eileen would shout with her screechy laugh.
He refused to bring Claire to it even as an occasional visitor, claiming that his every move was watched by spies of the Milbankes.
A similar tone of underlying futility and despair pervades the spy thrillers of Eric Ambler and dominates the most famous of all American mystery stories, Dashiell Hammett's The Maltese Falcon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another; especially in secret or espionage contexts
  • Synonym: informant - a person who provides secret or confidential information
  • Synonym: mole - a spy who achieves ongoing access by infiltrating an organization
  • Antonym: counterspy - a person who detects or thwarts spies

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French *espier* (to watch), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
to watch or observe someone or something secretly, especially for information gathering or surveillance purposes

Examples

You should have gone to work today,' stead of sneaking around spying on the Dronk house ".
What on earth was in Mae's mind, that she wanted him up there spying on what the cops were doing?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observe - to watch carefully, especially for a purpose
  • Synonym: monitor - to watch and check a situation carefully over time

Etymology

From Old French *espier* (to watch), from Frankish *spāhōn* (to watch, examine); related to Dutch *spioneren* and German *spähen*.
