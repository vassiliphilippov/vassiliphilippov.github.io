thyrotrophin

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪrəˈtrəʊfɪn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the activity of the thyroid gland.

Examples

The hormone has also been called thyrotrophin or thyrotrophic hormone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyrotropin - another term for thyrotrophin, a thyroid-stimulating hormone

Etymology

From Greek 'thureos' (shield, referring to the thyroid) + 'trophos' (nourisher).
