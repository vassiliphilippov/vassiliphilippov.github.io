somersault

1. NOUN

/ ˈsəmərˌsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməsɔːlt / (BrE)
an acrobatic movement in which a person turns head over heels in the air or on the ground and lands on their feet or back

Examples

It is made up of tumbling, which might be said to start with a somersault, run through such stunts as headstands, handstands, cartwheels, backbends, and culminate in nearly impossible combinations of aerial flips and twists and apparatus work.
The last essential to the beginner's gymnastic program is the somersault, or forward roll.
First, Arlene had put them through some rapid somersaults.
Then, they said General Burnside was going to jump over his horse's head; and they did some somersaults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip - an acrobatic jump or movement where the body is thrown into a full rotation

Etymology

From obsolete French 'sombresault', from Old Occitan 'sobressalt' (jump), from Latin 'super' (over) + 'saltus' (jump).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmərˌsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməsɒlt / (BrE)
An acrobatic movement in which the body rolls forward or backward turning heels over head and finishing on the feet.

Examples

Later on, the children told her further about somersaulting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip - A similar acrobatic movement involving a quick turn or rotation of the body

Etymology

From French 'sombresault', modified from Old Occitan 'sobressaut' (a leap).
