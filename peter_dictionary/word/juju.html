juju

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːˌdʒuː / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːˌdʒuː / (BrE)
(in West African contexts) supernatural power or magic associated with objects, charms, or rituals; also, a charm or amulet believed to have magical power.

Examples

Whether the pair of Sudanese ivory carvings you lifted really possess the juju to turn your livers to lead, as a dealer in Khartoum assured me, I am not competent to say.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - an object believed to have magical powers
  • Synonym: amulet - an ornament or small piece of jewelry thought to give protection against evil, danger, or disease
  • Synonym: magic - the power of apparently influencing events by mysterious or supernatural forces

Etymology

From West African languages, such as Hausa jùju or Yoruba jíjú.
