thrall

1. NOUN

/ θrɔl / (AmE), / θrɑːl / (BrE)
The state of being in the power of someone; the condition of being under the control or influence of another person.

Examples

Forgive me, but with you gone, me and Pete will come under Barzini's thumb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondage - the state of being bound or under restraint, especially as a slave or serf
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants

Etymology

From Old Norse *þræll* (slave), later Middle English *thral* (bondman, serf).
