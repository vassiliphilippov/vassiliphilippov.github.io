long-lived

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋˌlɪvd, ˈlɔːŋˌlaɪvd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌlɪvd, ˈlɒŋˌlaɪvd / (BrE)
Existing or lasting for a long time; having a long duration or lifespan.

Examples

These my grandmother left in their places (they are still there, more persistent and longer-lived than the generations of man) and planted others like them, that flourished without careful tending.

Synset Examples

hopes for a durable peace
a long-lasting friendship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-lived - existing or lasting for only a short time
  • Synonym: durable - able to withstand wear or damage over time; lasting
  • Synonym: perennial - lasting or existing for a long or apparently infinite time

Etymology

Formed from 'long' + 'lived' (past participle of 'live'), meaning 'having lived long'.
