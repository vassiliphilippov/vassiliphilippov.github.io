lone

1. ADJ

/ loʊn / (AmE), / ləʊn / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) characterized by or preferring solitude; solitary.

Examples

In the street, walking as quickly as he could, Stanley Gilborn was a lone figure.

Synset Examples

a lone wolf
a lonely existence
a man of a solitary disposition
a solitary walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary - enjoying or characterized by solitude
  • Antonym: gregarious - enjoying the company of others; sociable

Etymology

Alteration of "alone," from Middle English "al one" (all one).

2. ADJ

/ loʊn / (AmE), / ləʊn / (BrE)
Without companions; solitary or single; lacking company or companionship.

Examples

The hands and their bosses saw him as a lone knight of the range, waging a dedicated crusade against a lawless new society that was threatening a beloved way of life.

Synset Examples

he was alone when we met him
she is alone much of the time
the lone skier on the mountain
a lonely fisherman stood on a tuft of gravel
a lonely soul
a solitary traveler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - Without anyone else, solitary
  • Synonym: solitary - Existing or living alone, without others
  • Degree Higher: lonely - Sad because one has no friends or company

Etymology

Contracted form of 'alone', from Middle English all one.
