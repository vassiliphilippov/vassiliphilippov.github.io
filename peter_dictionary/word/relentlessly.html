relentlessly

1. ADV

/ rɪˈlɛntləsli / (AmE), / rɪˈlɛntləsli / (BrE)
In a way that does not stop or yield; persistently and without giving up or becoming less severe.

Examples

Named by Mayor Wagner three years ago to head a committee that included James A. Farley, Bernard Gimbel and Clint Blume, Shea worked relentlessly.
The small car flew on relentlessly.
They are relentlessly rebuffed on all sides by a waitress, the police, and an intruding government tutor.

Synset Examples

he worked relentlessly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistently - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action

Etymology

From 'relentless' + '-ly'; 'relentless' from 'relent' + '-less', from Latin 'lentus' (slow, flexible).
