relativistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛl.ə.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɛl.ə.tɪˈvɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or determined by the special or general theory of relativity, especially as applied in physics.

Examples

The Poynting-Robertson effect (Robertson, 1937; Wyatt and Whipple, 1950), which is a retardation of the orbital motion of particles by the relativistic aberration of the repulsive force of the impinging solar radiation, causes the dust to spiral into the sun in times much shorter than the age of the Earth.

Synset Examples

relativistic quantum mechanics
relativistic increase in mass
radiation from relativistic particles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relativity - the dependence of various physical phenomena on relative motion of the observer and the observed objects, especially as described in the special or general theory of relativity

Etymology

Formed from 'relativity' + '-istic'; 'relativity' from Latin 'relativus' (having reference or relation).
