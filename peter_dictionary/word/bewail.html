bewail

1. VERB

/ bɪˈweɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈweɪl / (BrE)
to express deep regret or sorrow for something; to lament strongly

Examples

You can sit back and moan and bewail your lot.

Synset Examples

I deplore this hostile action
we lamented the loss of benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express grief or sorrow for something
  • Synonym: deplore - to feel or express strong disapproval or sorrow about something
  • Antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology

From Old English 'beweƿlian', where 'be-' is an intensifier and 'weƿlian' means 'to wail, lament'.
