hurricane

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝː.ɪ.keɪn / (AmE), / ˈhʌr.ɪ.kən / (BrE)
A violent tropical storm with strong winds of 73–136 knots (force 12 on the Beaufort scale) and heavy rain, typically occurring in the Caribbean and western Atlantic.

Examples

The basement or belowground shelters also will serve for tornado or hurricane protection.
He had never seen clouds like them before, but he had the primitive feel of danger that gripped a man before a hurricane in Carolina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhoon - violent tropical cyclone occurring in the western Pacific
  • Synonym: cyclone - general term for a storm system with a rotating wind pattern

Etymology

From Spanish 'huracán', from Taino (Arawakan) 'hurakán' (god of the storm).
