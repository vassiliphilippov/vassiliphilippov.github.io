glint

1. VERB

/ ɡlɪnt / (AmE), / ɡlɪnt / (BrE)
(of light or a shiny surface) to give out a small, bright flash of light, often as if wet or polished.

Examples

The stethoscope glinted silver in the darkening room.
But dully glinting on the dark form were the buttons and badge of a policeman.
In the Canadian Rockies, great groves of aspen are already glinting gold.
There were lights glinting in the city, too, even though it was now dark enough for a few stars to become visible.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glitter - shine with bright, shimmering reflected light
  • Synonym: gleam - shine with a soft or gentle light
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or sheen

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse glitra ‘to glitter’.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɪnt / (AmE), / ɡlɪnt / (BrE)
A brief or momentary flash or gleam of light.

Examples

The discarded papers inside were sodden, there was a glint of liquid at the bottom, and the smell of whisky was strong and distinct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or faint light, especially a steady one
  • Synonym: flash - a sudden, brief burst of light

Etymology

Probably from Middle English glenten (to shine), akin to Scandinavian languages (compare Norwegian glinta ‘to shine’).
