sanction

1. VERB

/ ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
To give official permission or approval for something.

Examples

In America also all of our major religious bodies officially sanction a universalistic ethic which is reflective of our common religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorize - to give official permission or approval for
  • Antonym: forbid - to refuse to allow; to prohibit

Etymology

From Latin sanctionem, nominative sanctio (a decree or ordinance), from sancire (to make sacred, ratify).

2. VERB

/ ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
to give official permission or approval for; to authorize

Examples

Historically, religion has also functioned as a tremendous engine of vindication, enforcement, sanction, and perpetuation of various other institutions.
All the additives listed here are sanctioned for use by the Food and Drug Administration of the federal government.
The Roman Catholic Church, however, sanctions a much more liberal policy on family planning.
The Roman Catholic Church sanctions only abstention or the rhythm method, also known as the use of the infertile or safe period.
Their strategy was sound enough and, he reasoned, had been defeated only by Philip Spencer's unwillingness to sanction an idea he had not originated.

Synset Examples

I approve of his educational policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approve - to officially accept or agree to
  • Synonym: authorize - to give official permission for something
  • Antonym: forbid - to order not to do something; to prohibit

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctio', from 'sanctus' meaning 'made holy'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
Official permission or approval for an action or policy.

Examples

Among primitive peoples the sanctions and dictates of religion were more binding than any of the other controls exercised by the group; and in modern societies such influence is still great.
My object, rather, is to alert you to an aspect or two of the affair that could have the gravest implications for you, far beyond the legal sanctions society might inflict.

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - favorable opinion or assent
  • Synonym: endorsement - expression of support or approval
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctio' (decree), from 'sanctus' (made holy).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
a measure or penalty used to enforce rules or standards in a society, often to encourage conformity or punish disobedience

Examples

The Utopians brace conscience with legal sanctions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a law or rule
  • Antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement (opposite effect as sanction)

Etymology

From Latin sanctio (an ordinance, decree), from sancire (to make sacred, to ratify).
