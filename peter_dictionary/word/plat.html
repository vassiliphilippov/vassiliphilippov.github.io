plat

1. VERB

/ plæt / (AmE), / plæt / (BrE)
to make a plat of land, especially to map or lay out the divisions of a town or subdivision

Examples

The resultant town, platted in 1847 and named for the patron of Father Galtier's mission, St. Paul, was to become an important center of the fur trade and was to take on a new interest for those Selkirkers who remained at Red River.

Synset Examples

Plat the town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - to measure and map land
  • Synonym: parcel - to divide into parcels or lots

Etymology

From Middle English *platt* (flat surface, map), from Old French *plat* (flat), from Latin *plattus* (flattened), influenced by the meaning of 'plan' or 'map'.
