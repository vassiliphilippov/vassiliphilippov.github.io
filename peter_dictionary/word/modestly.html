modestly

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑdəstli / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪstli / (BrE)
In a modest way; without boasting or showing off.

Examples

For sheer ambition, take the Decca series titled modestly "Wisdom ".
The great detective modestly agrees.

Synset Examples

the dissertation was entitled, modestly, `Remarks about a play by Shakespeare'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humbly - in a humble manner; without pride or arrogance

Etymology

From 'modest' + '-ly', with 'modest' from Latin 'modestus' (keeping measure, moderate).
