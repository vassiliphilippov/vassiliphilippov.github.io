boisterous

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪstərəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪstərəs / (BrE)
noisy, energetic, and lacking in restraint; full of rough and exuberant spirit

Examples

Another is a wily countryman called Larkin, whose blandly boisterous progress has been chronicled, I believe, in earlier volumes of Mr. Bates' comedie humaine.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambunctious - uncontrollably exuberant; boisterous
  • Synonym: unruly - disorderly, not easily controlled
  • Antonym: calm - free from noise or disturbance

Etymology

From Middle English 'boistous' (rough, noisy), of uncertain origin.
