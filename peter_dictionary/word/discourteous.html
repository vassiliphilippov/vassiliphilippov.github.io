discourteous

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɝːtiəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜːtiəs / (BrE)
rude and lacking in courtesy; showing a lack of politeness or respect.

Examples

It was not a discourteous question, Lawrence decided.

Synset Examples

a distant and at times discourteous young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rude - impolite; showing a lack of respect or courtesy
  • Antonym: courteous - polite and showing respect for others

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + courteous, from Old French 'courtois', from court ('court').
