unchanged

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒd / (BrE)
Remaining the same; not altered or modified.

Examples

Burly leathered men and wrinkled women in drab black rags carried on in a primitive way, almost unchanged from feudal times.
The presumption in the literature would appear to be that the basic wage rate would be unchanged in this case, on the grounds that it is "clearly "not in the interest of the industry to raise wages gratuitously.

Synset Examples

the causes that produced them have remained unchanged

Related Terms

  • Antonym: changed - Made different; altered from a previous state

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'changed', past participle of 'change'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒd / (BrE)
remaining in an original or unaltered state; not changed or modified

Examples

In the cruel clearness of her memory the boy remained unchanged, quick with the delight of laughter, and the pain with which she recalled that short destroyed childhood was still unendurable to her.

Synset Examples

persisting unaltered through time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: changed - having been altered or modified
  • Synonym: unaltered - remaining unchanged

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'changed', meaning not changed.
