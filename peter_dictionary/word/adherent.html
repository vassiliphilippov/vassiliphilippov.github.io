adherent

1. NOUN

/ ædˈhɪr.ənt / (AmE), / ədˈhɪə.rənt / (BrE)
A person who supports a particular party, person, or set of ideas.

Examples

The Jews for 2500 years have been a prime example, though the adherents of any world or inter people religion are cases in point.
The second stage is to retain the nominal classification of "neutralist ", while in fact turning the country into an active advocate and adherent of Soviet policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas
  • Synonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher, leader, or philosophy
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who opposes or competes against another

Etymology

From Latin adhaerentem, present participle of adhaerēre (to stick to).
