detain

1. VERB

/ dɪˈteɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈteɪn / (BrE)
to officially keep someone in a place and prevent them from leaving, especially for questioning or as a punishment

Examples

Excellent in their way, they lack the wide appeal of The Charles Men, and need not detain us here.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from confinement

Etymology

From Latin detinere, from de- (down) + tenere (to hold).
