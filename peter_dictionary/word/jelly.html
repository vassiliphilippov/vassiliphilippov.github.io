jelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
A soft, sweet or savory food made with gelatin, often served as a dessert, salad base, or a coating for foods.

Examples

Now, driving the horse and sulky borrowed from Mynheer Schuyler, he felt as if every bone was topped by burning oil and that every muscle was ready to dissolve into jelly and leave his big body helpless and unable to move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gelatin - a substance used to make jelly; a colorless, tasteless protein obtained from animal collagen

Etymology

From Old French *gelée* (frost, jelly), from Latin *gelata* (frozen, jelly), from *gelare* (to freeze).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
A sweet, clear preserve made by boiling the strained juice of fruit with sugar until it gels.

Examples

Their jams and jellies had not suffered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a preserve consisting of fruit stewed with sugar
  • Synonym: marmalade - a preserve made from citrus fruit, especially oranges, and sugar

Etymology

From Old French *gelée* (frost, jelly), from Latin *gelata* (frozen), from *gelare* (to freeze).
