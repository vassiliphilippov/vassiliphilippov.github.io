negligent

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛɡlɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Failing to take proper care over something; careless, especially in a way that results in harm or risk.

Examples

I would have been negligent and a goddam lousy cop to boot, if I 'd sat around this station all night when somebody got away with murder in my district.

Synset Examples

negligent parents
negligent of detail
negligent in his correspondence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'negligent-', present participle of 'negligere' (to neglect).
