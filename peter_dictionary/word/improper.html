improper

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑpər / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpə / (BrE)
Not conforming to accepted standards of legality, morality, or social convention.

Examples

Mr. Chairman... Mr. Chairman, I think this statement is totally out of order at this time.

Synset Examples

an unconventional marriage
improper banking practices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proper - conforming to accepted standards; appropriate or suitable
  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From Latin 'improprius' (not one's own, unsuitable).
