interpreter

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːprətɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to translate spoken words from one language to another

Examples

During the following week, six tons of hay belonging to one rancher were burned; some buildings, farm tools, two horses, plows, and hay owned by Bonito Lavato, a friendly interpreter for the company, and Pedro Chavez' hay were stolen or destroyed; and a store was broken into and robbed.
Richard Brinsley Sheridan said, "I think the interpreter is the hardest to be understood of the two ".
"We "were Bill Garrett of the National Geographic Illustrations Staff, whose three cameras and eight lenses made him look as formidable as any fighting man we met; Boun My, our interpreter; and myself.
Acting only as interpreter Carla, her hands folded on her lap, was utterly impersonal.
[PEOPLE CHATTERING IN VARIOUS LANGUAGES] Interpreter!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: translator - a person who translates written texts from one language to another

Etymology

From Latin interpretari (to explain, translate), via Old French interpréter.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːprətɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪtə / (BrE)
A person who expresses or represents something through their artistic work, such as an artist, performer, or musician who conveys the meaning or character of a subject.

Examples

Living with his watercolors is a vicarious experience of seeing nature distilled through the eyes of a sensitive interpretor, a breath and breadth of the outdoor world to help man honor the Creator of it all.

Synset Examples

his paintings reveal a sensitive interpreter of nature
she was famous as an interpreter of Shakespearean roles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artist - a person who creates art as a means of expression
  • Synonym: performer - a person who presents or interprets artistic works for an audience

Etymology

From Latin interpretari (to explain, expound, understand).
