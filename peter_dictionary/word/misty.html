misty

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪsti / (AmE), / ˈmɪsti / (BrE)
full of or covered with mist; characterized by a cloud of fine droplets in the air that makes it difficult to see clearly.

Examples

Beyond that misty gray of the rain, he saw the stretching hutment, low diminutive log cabins, chinked with mud, with doorways a man would have to crouch to get through, with roofs of tenting laid over boughs or boards from hardtack boxes, or fence rails, with cranky chimneys of sticks and dried mud.

Synset Examples

a brumous October morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foggy - full of or covered with fog
  • Antonym: clear - not covered by mist or fog; easy to see through

Etymology

From Old English 'mistig' (misty), from 'mist' + '-y'.
