incandescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt / (BrE)
Emitting light as a result of being heated; glowing with intense heat.

Examples

There was about that song something incandescent, for this Brahms was Milstein at white heat.

Synset Examples

an incandescent bulb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminous - emitting or reflecting light, especially in the dark

Etymology

From Latin 'incandescere' (to become white with heat), from 'in-' (into) + 'candescere' (to grow white).
