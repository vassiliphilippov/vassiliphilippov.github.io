chivalry

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪvəlri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəlri / (BrE)
Polite and kind behavior, especially by men towards women, traditionally associated with the qualities of knights.

Examples

More than a beautiful visualization of the illustrious adventures and escapades of the tragi-comic knight-errant and his squire, Sancho Panza, in seventeenth century Spain, this inevitably abbreviated rendering of the classic satire on chivalry is an affectingly warm and human exposition of character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallantry - polite attention or respect shown to women by men

Etymology

From Old French *chevalerie* (knighthood), from *chevalier* (knight), based on Latin *caballarius* (horseman).
