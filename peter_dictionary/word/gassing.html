gassing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡasɪŋ / (BrE)
the process in textile manufacturing of passing yarn or fabric through a gas flame to remove loose fibers and achieve a smooth finish

Examples

There is no dyeing or waxing or gassing needed.

Etymology

From 'gas' + '-ing', referring to the use of a gas flame in the process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡasɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate poisoning of a person or animal with gas, especially as a method of execution or killing

Examples

Ought not an edifying Trial have made every effort to demonstrate this once and for all by showing how representative types of "mere "anti-Semites were drawn step by step into the program of skull bashings and gassings?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the carrying out of a death sentence
  • Synonym: poisoning - the act of causing injury or death by poison

Etymology

From the verb 'gas' (to poison with gas) + -ing suffix.
