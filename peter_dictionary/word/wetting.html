wetting

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of making something wet, especially by applying a liquid to a surface; in science, the ability of a liquid to maintain contact with a solid surface.

Examples

Indeed, when the proper inorganic constituents are employed, practically any wetting or surface-active agent will do a reasonably good job when present in sufficient amount in a hard surface cleaning formulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dampening - the act of making slightly wet
  • Antonym: drying - the process of removing moisture; opposite of wetting

Etymology

From 'wet' + '-ing', forming a gerund or verbal noun.
