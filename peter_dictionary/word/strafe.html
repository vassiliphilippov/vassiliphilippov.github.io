strafe

1. VERB

/ streɪf / (AmE), / streɪf / (BrE)
to attack (an area or people) with machine-gun or cannon fire from a low-flying aircraft

Examples

Somehow, the pictures and stories of Soviet T-34 tanks on Cuban beaches and Russian Mig jet fighters strafing rebel troops has brought home to all of us the stark, blunt truth of what it means to have a Russian military base 90 miles away from home.

Synset Examples

civilians were strafed in an effort to force the country's surrender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack with bombs or heavy fire, especially from above

Etymology

From German 'strafen' (to punish), popularized during World War I.
