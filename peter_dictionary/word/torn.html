torn

1. ADJ

/ tɔrn / (AmE), / tɔːn / (BrE)
(of a person) feeling emotionally disrupted or conflicted by opposing desires or loyalties.

Examples

He was torn between the excitement in the sun inflamed waters and a little engine chugging northward on the Monticello Branch.
And thus torn between his desire to be known as the composer of a successful opera and the necessity of remaining true to his proclaimed desire for anonymity, Rousseau suffered through several painful weeks.

Synset Examples

torn between love and hate
torn by conflicting loyalties
torn by religious dissensions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflicted - experiencing conflicting emotions or desires
  • Synonym: undecided - not having made a decision
  • Antonym: resolved - having reached a firm decision

Etymology

Past participle of 'tear', from Old English 'teran'.

2. ADJ

/ tɔrn / (AmE), / tɔːn / (BrE)
having edges that are irregular or jagged as a result of being torn, injured, or ripped

Examples

Men were slaughtered every day, tumbled into eternity like so many torn parcels flung down a portable chute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacerated - having edges that are torn or deeply cut
  • Antonym: smooth - having a surface without irregularities or roughness

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'tear', from Old English *teran* (to tear).
