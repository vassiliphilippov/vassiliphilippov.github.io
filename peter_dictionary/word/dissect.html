dissect

1. VERB

/ daɪˈsɛkt, dɪ- / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkt, daɪ- / (BrE)
to cut open or cut apart (an animal, plant, or other organism) in order to study its internal parts

Examples

Dissect it "?

Synset Examples

dissect the bodies for analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analyze - to examine in detail for purposes of explanation and interpretation
  • Synonym: examining - inspecting something closely, especially to determine its nature or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'dissectus', past participle of 'dissecare' (to cut apart), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'secare' (to cut).
