metamorphose

1. VERB

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrˌfoʊz / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəʊz / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to change completely in form, structure, or character; to undergo or cause a complete transformation.

Examples

The depersonalization continued as the dancer was further metamorphosed by the play of lights upon his figure.

Synset Examples

In Kafka's story, a person metamorphoses into a bug
The treatment and diet transfigured her into a beautiful young woman
Jesus was transfigured after his resurrection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transform - to change in form, appearance, or structure
  • Synonym: transfigure - to change the appearance of someone or something completely

Etymology

From Greek metamorphoun, from meta- (change) + morphē (form).
