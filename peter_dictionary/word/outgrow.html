outgrow

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈɡroʊ / (AmE), / aʊtˈɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to become too large or mature for something; to no longer be suitable or appropriate due to development or growth

Examples

"A child with a certain type of head and teeth will outgrow tooth deformity ".

Synset Examples

I have outgrown these clothes
She outgrew her childish habits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outgrew - past tense of outgrow

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (beyond) + 'grow'.
