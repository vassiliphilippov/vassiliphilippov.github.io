carefully

1. ADV

/ ˈkɛɹ.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows attention to detail or care; with caution or thoroughness.

Examples

That much of what he calls folklore is the result of beliefs carefully sown among the people with the conscious aim of producing a desired mass emotional reaction to a particular situation or set of situations is irrelevant.
It seems likely that the Indian python comes out ahead because records of its growth have been made more carefully and frequently; it responds exceptionally well to captivity and does not reach proportions that make it hard to keep.
Klauber investigated the rattlesnakes carefully himself and also summarized what others have found.
He looked very carefully at every piece of furnishing, as though hoping to store this information carefully in his mind.
He looked very carefully at every piece of furnishing, as though hoping to store this information carefully in his mind.

Synset Examples

they watched carefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a way that shows caution or wariness
  • Antonym: recklessly - in a way that shows no regard for danger or the consequences

Etymology

From 'careful' + '-ly', with 'careful' originating from 'care' + '-ful'.

2. ADV

/ ˈkɛr.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fə.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows care, caution, or attention to detail.

Examples

He moved ahead carefully, his left hand in front of him, and came to a wooden partition.
He took a long but carefully controlled draught.

Synset Examples

she ventured cautiously downstairs
they handled the incident with kid gloves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a careful, wary manner
  • Antonym: recklessly - without care or regard for danger or consequences

Etymology

From 'careful' + '-ly', where 'careful' derives from 'care' (Old English 'caru', concern, anxiety) + '-ful', and '-ly' is an adverb-forming suffix from Old English '-līce'.
