spice

1. NOUN

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
Aromatic substances of plant origin, used especially in the past as preservatives or in religious or burial practices.

Examples

They took Jesus's body, then, and wrapped it in winding-clothes with the spices; that is how the Jews prepare a body for burial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - plant used for flavoring, food, medicine, or fragrance; similar in use to spice

Etymology

From Old French *espice*, from Latin *species* (kind, sort, spice).

2. VERB

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
To make something more interesting, lively, or exciting, often by adding a new or unexpected element.

Examples

And even if they stay in resorts part of the time, they might, if the right salesman gets them in tow, develop a yearning to spice the usual vacation fare with a camping trip into the wide open spaces.

Synset Examples

Spice up the evening by inviting a belly dancer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enliven - make more lively or interesting
  • Antonym: dull - make less lively or interesting

Etymology

From Old French 'espice', from Latin 'species' (kind, sort, appearance), originally relating to spices used in cooking.
