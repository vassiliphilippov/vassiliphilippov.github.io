xylophone

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈzaɪləfəʊn / (BrE)
a musical instrument consisting of a series of wooden bars of varying lengths, which are struck with small mallets to produce different pitches

Examples

Drums, xylophones, castanets, and other percussive instruments are reproduced remarkably well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marimba - a larger, deeper-toned percussion instrument similar to a xylophone

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood) + 'phōnē' (voice, sound).
