unobtrusively

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnəbˈtruːsɪvli / (AmE), / ˌʌnəbˈtruːsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that does not attract attention or cause disruption; without being conspicuous.

Examples

When she succeeds, she usually manages to slip in unobtrusively, to deposit an egg on a completed loaf of beebread before the bumblebees seal the egg compartment.

Synset Examples

messengers were moving unobtrusively over the jet-black mountain ranges, bearing confidential tidings from sheikhdom to sheikhdom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discreetly - in a careful and unobtrusive manner
  • Antonym: conspicuously - in a way that is easily noticed or attracts attention

Etymology

Formed from 'unobtrusive' (not attracting attention), with adverbial suffix '-ly'.
