plentiful

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɛn.tɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.tɪ.fəl / (BrE)
existing or available in large quantities; more than enough.

Examples

That encourages rooting, and the better developed the roots, the larger and more plentiful the flowers.
Thereafter seed and food became more plentiful and the colony remained in the north the year round.

Synset Examples

had ample food for the party
copious provisions
food is plentiful
a plenteous grape harvest
a rich supply

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scarce - in short supply; not enough of something
  • Synonym: abundant - present in large amounts
  • Synonym: copious - large in quantity; abundant

Etymology

From Old French *plentevouz*, from Latin *plentivus*, from *plentus* (full, filled, abundant).

2. ADJ

/ ˈplɛn.tɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.tɪ.fʊl / (BrE)
existing in large amounts; more than enough for what is needed

Examples

Juet had made plentiful enemies, several men stepped forward.
Evidence is plentiful that early and later also he has been indebted to the Gothic romancers, who deal in extravagant horror, to the symbolists writing at the end of the preceding century, and in particular to the stream-of-consciousness novelists, Henry James and James Joyce among them.

Synset Examples

rhinoceroses were once plentiful here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundant - existing in large quantities; more than enough
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient in quantity; hard to find
  • Degree Higher: abundant - existing in large quantities; more than enough
  • Degree Lower: ample - enough or more than enough; plentiful

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'plentif', from Latin 'plenus' (full).
