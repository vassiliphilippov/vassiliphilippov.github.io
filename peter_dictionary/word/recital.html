recital

1. NOUN

/ rəˈsaɪt̬əl / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪtəl / (BrE)
A performance of music or dance, typically by a soloist or a small group, often before an audience.

Examples

Certainly not in Orchestra hall where he has played countless recitals, and where Thursday night he celebrated his 20th season with the Chicago Symphony orchestra, playing the Brahms Concerto with his own slashing, demon-ridden cadenza melting into the high, pale, pure and lovely song with which a violinist unlocks the heart of the music, or forever finds it closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concert - a musical performance, typically by a large ensemble or orchestra
  • Synonym: gig - a live performance, especially popular or jazz music, usually informal or for a small venue

Etymology

From Latin 'recitare' (to read aloud), via French 'récital'.
