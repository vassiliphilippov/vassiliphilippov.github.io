politically

1. ADV

/ pəˈlɪtɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to politics, government, or social relationships involving power or authority.

Examples

As an isolated policy, land reform is likely to be politically disruptive; as part of a larger development effort, however, it may gain wide acceptance.

Synset Examples

politically correct clothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political - relating to politics or government; adjective form

Etymology

From 'politic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'politikós' (of citizens) via Latin and Old French.

2. ADV

/ pəˈlɪtɪkli / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to government, politics, or public affairs.

Examples

The unifying effect of religion is also brought out in the fact that historically peoples have clung together as more or less cohesive cultural units, with religion as the dominant bond, even though spatially dispersed and not politically organized.

Synset Examples

politically organized units

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political - relating to government or public affairs

Etymology

From political + -ly; ultimately from Greek politikos (of citizens or the state).
