governess

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌvərnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənɪs / (BrE)
a woman employed to teach and care for children in a private household, typically within the family home

Examples

For example, when the film is only four minutes old, Neitzbohr refers to a small, Victorian piano stool as "Wilhelmina ", and we are thereupon subjected to a flashback that informs us that this very piano stool was once used by an epileptic governess whose name, of course, was Doris (the English equivalent, when passed through middle Gaelic derivations, of Wilhelmina).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tutor - private teacher, especially in a student's home
  • Degree Lower: nanny - woman employed to take care of a child in its own home

Etymology

From Old French 'governeresse', from 'governer' (to govern), ultimately from Latin 'gubernare' (to steer, to govern).
