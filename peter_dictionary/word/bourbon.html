Bourbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːbən / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbən / (BrE)
(historical, US) A reactionary or conservative politician, especially one from the Southern United States, typically resistant to social and political change; originally used for members of the post-Civil War Democratic Party leadership in the South.

Examples

And both in their objectives of non-discrimination and of social progress they have had ranged against them the Southerners who are called Bourbons.
The nature of the opposition between liberals and Bourbons is too little understood in the North.
Among Bourbons the racial issue may have less to do with their remaining unreconstructed than other factors.
The two main charges levelled against the Bourbons by liberals is that they are racists and social reactionaries.
There is much truth in both these charges, and not many Bourbons deny them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactionary - a person opposing political or social progress or reform
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new ideas

Etymology

From the Bourbon dynasty of France, known for its resistance to reform; applied to intransigent Southern U.S. politicians after the Civil War.
