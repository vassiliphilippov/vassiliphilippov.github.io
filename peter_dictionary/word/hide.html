hide

1. NOUN

/ haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
the skin of an animal, especially when tanned or dressed

Examples

Measurements of skins are of little value; every snake hide is noticeably longer than its carcass and intentional stretching presents no difficulty to the unscrupulous explorer.
Effectively controls cattle grubs which damage hides and can reduce gains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - the outer layer of tissue covering an animal's body
  • Degree Higher: leather - animal skin made into a durable material

Etymology

Old English hȳd, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch huid and German Haut.

2. NOUN

/ haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
the skin of an animal, especially a large one, after it has been removed and treated for use as leather

Examples

Cook had discovered a beef in his possession a few days earlier and, when he could not show the hide, arrested him.
I heard o' Texas cattlemen wrappin' a cow thief up in green hides and lettin' the sun shrink' em and squeeze him to death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelt - the skin of an animal with the fur, hair, or wool still on it
  • Degree Higher: leather - animal hide that has been tanned or dressed for use

Etymology

From Old English hýd (skin, hide), related to German Haut (skin).

3. VERB

/ haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
to put or keep something or someone out of sight; to conceal so as not to be seen or discovered

Examples

A bullet fired by one of the Mexicans hiding in a little chicken house had passed through his head, tearing a hole two inches square on the outgoing side.
The heavy branches in his front yard would hide and then reveal them.
Between the ponderous hulk and himself, in the valley over which Papa-san reigned, men had hidden high explosives, booby traps, and mines.
The others likewise had hidden themselves in the grass and the brush.
He heard the words: "Rock of Ages, cleft for me, Let me hide myself in Thee!

Synset Examples

Muslim women hide their faces
hide the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
  • Synonym: bury - to cover so as to hide from view
  • Antonym: expose - to make something visible or uncovered
  • Antonym: reveal - to make known or visible

Etymology

Old English hȳdan (to conceal), related to Old High German hūtan (to guard).

4. VERB

/ haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
to go or stay somewhere so as not to be seen or found, especially for protection or safety

Examples

If any had escaped expulsion by hiding, they certainly would not frequent the market-place.
He had the feeling that he should abandon the car and run off somewhere to hide.
Probably his horse would be close to where he was hiding.
Though they saw the old-style chemical rockets lift up from the field beyond B' dikkat's cabin, they did not make plans to hide among the frozen crop of transmuted flesh.
He'’ll hide out in the Bronx!

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceal - to keep something out of sight; to hide
  • Antonym: reveal - to make something visible; to show

Etymology

From Old English *hȳdan* (to conceal), related to Old High German *hūtan* (to guard).
