figurine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪɡjəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌfɪɡjʊˈriːn / (BrE)
a small figure or model of a human, animal, or other subject, typically made of porcelain, clay, metal, or plastic

Examples

Figurines and simple chapels presage the emergence of sculpture and architecture in Greece; objects in gold, ivory, and bronze grow more numerous.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: statue - a carved or cast figure of a person or animal, especially one that is life-size or larger

Etymology

From French 'figurine', diminutive of 'figure'.
