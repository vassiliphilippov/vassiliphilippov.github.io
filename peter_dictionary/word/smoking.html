smoking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of inhaling and exhaling the smoke of tobacco or other substances, especially as a habit.

Examples

Rachel wore a smart hat and, because she had been warned recently about smoking, puffed at her cigarettes through a long ivory holder stained with lipstick.

Synset Examples

he went outside for a smoke
smoking stinks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vaping - the act of inhaling and exhaling vapor from an electronic cigarette
  • Synonym: smoke - a noun meaning a single instance or period of smoking

Etymology

From the verb 'smoke' + -ing, denoting the action.
