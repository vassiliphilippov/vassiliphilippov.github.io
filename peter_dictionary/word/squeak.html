squeak

1. NOUN

/ skwik / (AmE), / skwiːk / (BrE)
a short, high-pitched sound or noise, often produced by something being pressed, rubbed, or moving quickly

Examples

On these posts the gates swung open with a squeak and shut with a metallic clang.

Synset Examples

the squeak of shoes on powdery snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound, especially made by a small bird
  • Degree Higher: screech - a loud, harsh, piercing cry or noise
  • Degree Lower: hum - a low, continuous sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself; related to Middle English 'squeken'.

2. VERB

/ skwik / (AmE), / skwiːk / (BrE)
to make a short, high-pitched, sharp sound

Examples

A wheel squeaked on a hub, was still, and squeaked again.
A wheel squeaked on a hub, was still, and squeaked again.

Synset Examples

The door creaked when I opened it slowly
My car engine makes a whining noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creak - to make a long, high-pitched, grating sound
  • Degree Higher: screech - to make a loud, harsh, piercing cry

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, first recorded in the 16th century.
