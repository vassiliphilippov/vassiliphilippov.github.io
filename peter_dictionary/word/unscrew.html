unscrew

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈskru / (AmE), / ʌnˈskruː / (BrE)
To loosen or remove (something) by turning it, especially in a counterclockwise direction.

Examples

Hans cut the foil off finally and unscrewed the cap.
You can unscrew this tube and replace it with a smoothbore insert for use with .22 shotshells - to break the little Targo clay targets.

Synset Examples

unscrew the outlet plate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: screw - to fasten or tighten by turning a screw

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (reversing the action) + 'screw' (to fasten by turning).

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈskru / (AmE), / ʌnˈskruː / (BrE)
to loosen or remove something, such as a lid or a fastener, by turning it, usually in the opposite direction to screwing in

Examples

He got the pint of liquor out of his pocket and unscrewed the top, sprinkled the pungent stuff liberally over both men, and then tossed the open bottle in on the front seat.

Synset Examples

unscrew the bottle cap

Related Terms

  • Antonym: screw - to fasten or tighten by turning, usually with a screw or lid

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'reverse, do the opposite of') + screw (from Middle French 'escroue', meaning 'a screw').
