swindle

1. VERB

/ ˈswɪn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈswɪn.dl̩ / (BrE)
to cheat someone out of money or property by deliberate deception

Examples

You think that Highlands swindled you and I helped' em do it.
Perhaps, as Mr. Freeman says, American agriculture may stop the Communists, but it is also swindling the American taxpayer.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her
the cashier gypped me when he gave me too little change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: scam - to deceive someone, usually for financial gain

Etymology

Probably from German *schwindeln* (to be dizzy, to swindle), related to *schwindel* (fraud).
