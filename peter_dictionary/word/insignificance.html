insignificance

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unimportant or having little or no significance.

Examples

The transmutation of mission to co-optation is further indicated by the insignificance of educational activities, worship, preaching, and publicity in reaching new members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significance - the quality of being important or meaningful
  • Synonym: unimportance - the state or fact of not being important

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + significance, meaning 'not significant'.
