skirt

1. VERB

/ skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with or addressing directly (a duty, question, or issue); to evade or sidestep

Examples

And he ignored him, skirting the parked cars and walking up the path to the Skopas house.
Sometimes it was to skirt a gulley.

Synset Examples

He dodged the issue
she skirted the problem
They tend to evade their responsibilities
he evaded the questions skillfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evade - to avoid or escape from (a duty, question, etc.), often by cleverness
  • Synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) by a sudden or clever move; to avoid addressing (a question, issue)
  • Antonym: confront - to face or deal with a problem or difficult situation

2. NOUN

/ skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
A piece of clothing that hangs from the waist and covers all or part of the legs, typically worn by women or girls.

Examples

Finally she had come down; Winston had heard her shaking out the skirt of her new pink silk hostess gown.
She was wearing a brown cotton dress, cut across the hips in a way that was supposed to make her look slimmer, a yoke set into the skirt and flaring pleats below.
She smoothed the skirt, sat down, then stood up and went back to the windows.
He lifted the skirt of Macklin's coat, took his gun from its holster, tossed it onto the desk.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skyrta' (shirt), passed into Middle English via Old French 'escarte'.

3. VERB

/ skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
to go around or move along the edge or border of something, rather than through its center

Examples

For the first three weeks, the ship skirted up the east coast of Great Britain, then turned westward.

Synset Examples

The boat skirted the coast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - to form or be situated along the edge of something

Etymology

From Old Norse *skyrta* (shirt, tunic), but the verb sense developed in the 17th century from the noun ‘skirt’ (edge of a garment).

4. NOUN

/ skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
A piece of clothing that hangs from the waist and covers all or part of the legs, worn especially by women and girls.

Examples

From above one could only occasionally catch a glimpse of life on the floor of this green sea: a neighbor's gingham skirt flashing into sight for an instant on the path beneath her grape-arbor, or the movement of hands above a clothesline and the flutter of garments hung there, half-way down the block.
So you sit in the car and listen to the air run out and listen to the rain and see the mud in front of the headlights, waiting for you, for your new spectator pumps, waiting for you to squat by yourself out there in your tight skirt, crying and afraid and trying to get that damned son-of-a-bitch tire off, because that is being an old maid too, if you happen to drive a car, it is changing the tire yourself in the night, and in the mud and the rain, hating to get out in it but afraid to stay and afraid to try to walk out for help.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dress - a one-piece garment for a woman or girl covering the body and extending down over the legs
  • Synonym: kilt - a knee-length skirt-like garment with pleats at the back, originating from Scotland
  • Antonym: pants - a piece of clothing covering each leg separately, typically not a skirt

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skyrta' (shirt).
