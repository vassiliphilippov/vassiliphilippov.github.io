vocation

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular occupation or profession, especially one requiring special training or skills

Examples

My advice, if you live long enough to continue your vocation, is that the next time you 're attracted by the exotic, pass it up - it's nothing but a headache.
In a sense almost all high school and college courses could be considered as vocational to the extent that later in life the student in his vocation (which may be a profession) will be called upon to use some of the skills developed and the competence obtained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: career - the course or progress of one's professional life
  • Synonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one requiring advanced education and training
  • Antonym: avocation - a hobby or minor occupation

Etymology

From Latin 'vocatio' (a calling), from 'vocare' (to call).
