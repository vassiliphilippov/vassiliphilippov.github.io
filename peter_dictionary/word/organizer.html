organizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who arranges and coordinates the details of an activity, event, or enterprise

Examples

They were not diplomats or jazz musicians, or even organizers of reading-rooms and photo montage displays, but rugged capitalist entrepreneurs like Henry Ford, Hugh Cooper, Thomas Campbell, the International Harvester Co., and David W. Griffith.

Synset Examples

she was the organizer of the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinator - a person who organizes people or groups so that they work together properly and well

Etymology

From organize + -er; 'organize' from French organiser, from Medieval Latin organizare.
