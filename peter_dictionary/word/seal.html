seal

1. VERB

/ sil / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
to close or fasten something securely, often by means of a seal, adhesive, or similar device so that it cannot be opened or leaked

Examples

By comparing reaction cells sealed from the same manifold temperature dependency corresponding to activation energies ranging from 11 to 18 * * f was observed while dependence on the first power of the light intensity seemed to be indicated in most cases.
When she succeeds, she usually manages to slip in unobtrusively, to deposit an egg on a completed loaf of beebread before the bumblebees seal the egg compartment.
The hosts never seem to recognize that something is amiss, so that the compartment afterward is sealed normally.

Synset Examples

She sealed the letter with hot wax

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - to make something no longer closed or sealed

Etymology

From Old French 'seeler', from Latin 'sigillare', from 'sigillum' (a seal).

2. VERB

/ sil / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
to decide or make something definite, especially so that it cannot be changed (often used figuratively of fate, destiny, or outcomes)

Examples

"The time has come ", the walrus said, "To speak of manic things, Of shots and shouts, and sealing dooms Of commoners and kings ".
The destiny of Racine's Phedre is sealed from the beginning: she will proceed into the dark.

Synset Examples

sealing dooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determine - to decide or settle conclusively
  • Synonym: finalize - to make final or conclusive
  • Antonym: undo - to reverse or make not final

Etymology

From Old French *seel*, from Latin *sigillum* (a little sign), the source of the sense 'to close up and make secure.' Figurative use for fate/outcome is by extension.

3. NOUN

/ siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
a device bearing an engraved design, used to make an impression in wax, paper, or other material to authenticate or secure a document

Examples

Like many others, he had to work hard, long hours in a struggling family business which, though it was allied to art of a kind - the design and production of engraved seals - bore no relation to the painting of pictures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - an instrument for impressing a mark, especially for official purposes
  • Synonym: signet - a small seal, especially one set in a ring, used instead of or in addition to a signature for authentication

Etymology

From Old French *seel*, from Latin *sigillum* (small signet, seal), diminutive of *signum* (sign).

4. NOUN

/ siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
a small piece of wax, resin, or similar substance used for fastening and securing a letter or document, typically stamped with a design or emblem while soft

Examples

But before she left her room she dug into her big moire bag, took out the envelope holding her marriage contract and the wax seal had been broken.
Rev did n't need to break the wax seal, read the contract and find out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealing wax - a resinous substance used for making seals on documents

Etymology

From Old French *seel*, from Latin *sigillum* (small image, seal).

5. VERB

/ siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
to close or secure something tightly, especially to prevent the passage of air, liquid, or gas

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French *selaer*, from Latin *sigillare* (to seal).
