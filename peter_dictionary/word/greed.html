greed

1. NOUN

/ ɡriːd / (AmE), / ɡriːd / (BrE)
Intense and selfish desire for wealth, possessions, or power, especially when regarded as morally wrong.

Examples

Gotham's greed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give and share unselfishly

Etymology

From Middle English 'grede', from Old English 'grǣd' (hunger, greed).
