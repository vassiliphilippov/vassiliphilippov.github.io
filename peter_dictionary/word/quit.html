quit

1. VERB

/ kwɪt / (AmE), / kwɪt / (BrE)
to leave (a place, job, or position) permanently or temporarily

Examples

An injury forced Skorich to quit after the 1948 season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Synonym: resign - to voluntarily leave a job or position
  • Antonym: remain - to stay in the same place or position

Etymology

From Old French *quiter*, from Latin *quietare* (to make quiet).

2. VERB

/ kwɪt / (AmE), / kwɪt / (BrE)
To stop or discontinue an activity, especially to leave a job, habit, or state; to put an end to something.

Examples

Now Eileen really would have to settle down to love honor and obey, and she 'd have to quit drinking.
An Ohio soldier who, from a comrade just returned from leave, received an unfavorable comment on the conduct of his sister, took pen in hand and delivered himself thus: "[ Dear Sis ] Alf sed he heard that you and hardy was a runing together all the time and he though he wod gust quit having any thing mor to doo with you for he thought it was no more yuse.
Indeed, in some periods of our history and in some neighborhoods the job opportunities have been so good that undoubtedly a great many boys who were potential members of the professions quit school at an early age and went to work.
Curt was in almost as bad shape, but he would n't quit.
Actually, there was a lot of force in him, which is why I kept on in that class instead of quitting after a week.

Synset Examples

Quit teasing your little brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to cease operation or activity; to bring or come to an end
  • Antonym: continue - to persist in an activity or process without stopping

Etymology

From Middle English 'quiten', from Old French 'quiter', from Latin 'quietare' (to quieten), from 'quietus' (at rest).

3. VERB

/ kwɪt / (AmE), / kwɪt / (BrE)
to leave, give up, or retire from a position or job, especially voluntarily

Examples

In Poughkeepsie, N. Y., in 1952, a Roman Catholic hospital presented seven Protestant physicians with an ultimatum to quit the Planned Parenthood Federation or to resign from the hospital staff.
Well, if you don'’t feel like that, why don'’t you just quit college and go to join the army!

Synset Examples

The Secretary of the Navy will leave office next month
The chairman resigned over the financial scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to formally give up a job or position
  • Synonym: retire - to stop working, especially because of age
  • Antonym: take up - to begin or accept a position or job

Etymology

From Old French *quiter* (to release, free, clear), from Latin *quietare* (to make quiet), from *quietus* (calm, at rest).
