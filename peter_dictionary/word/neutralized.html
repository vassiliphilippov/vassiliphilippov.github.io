neutralized

1. ADJ

/ ˈnutʃrəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪzd / (BrE)
Made neutral; deprived of distinctive or distinguishing characteristics.

Examples

Her speech was barren of southernisms; she was one of Eliot Sparling's neutralized minorities, adopting the rolling R's and constricted vowels of Los Angeles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: distinctive - having a quality that makes something clearly different and easy to recognize

Etymology

From the verb 'neutralize', which is from French 'neutraliser', from Latin 'neutralis' (neutral).
