imminent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪnənt / (BrE)
About to happen; likely to occur very soon.

Examples

The 53 - year old Shea, a prominent corporation lawyer with a sports background, is generally recognized as the man most responsible for the imminent return of a National League club to New York.

Synset Examples

retribution is at hand
some people believe the day of judgment is close at hand
in imminent danger
his impending retirement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impending - about to happen; imminent
  • Degree Lower: immediate - occurring at once; instant
  • Degree Higher: inevitable - certain to happen; unavoidable

Etymology

From Latin imminentem, present participle of imminēre (to overhang, impend).
