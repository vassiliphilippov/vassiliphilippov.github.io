metro

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛt.roʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛt.rəʊ / (BrE)
an underground electric railway system, typically serving a city; subway

Examples

They had lunch at a sidewalk cafe overlooking the intersection of two broad, busy, unpicturesque streets, and coming home they got lost in the Metro; it took them over an hour to get back to the station where they should have changed, in order to take the line that went to the Place Redoute.

Synset Examples

in Paris the subway system is called the `metro' and in London it is called the `tube' or the `underground'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subway - an underground railway system in a city
  • Synonym: underground - the railway system below London's streets
  • Synonym: tube - the London Underground railway system

Etymology

From French métro, short for métropolitain.
