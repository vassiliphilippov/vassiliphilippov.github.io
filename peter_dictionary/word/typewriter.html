typewriter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪpˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpˌraɪtə / (BrE)
A machine with keys that, when pressed, print letters or characters one at a time onto paper; used especially before the advent of computers for writing documents.

Examples

The figures on the worksheet paper in front of her were jumping and waving around so badly it was all she could do to make them out clearly enough to copy them with the typewriter.
It contained a desk, files, a typewriter on a stand, and two big leather armchairs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: keyboard - set of keys for typing on a device

Etymology

Formed from 'type' + 'writer', first attested in the 19th century as a name for the machine.
