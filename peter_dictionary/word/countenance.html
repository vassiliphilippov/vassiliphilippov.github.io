countenance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntənəns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntənəns / (BrE)
formal approval or support; sanction or encouragement, especially in a public or official context.

Examples

The editor of the Daily Journal warned, "that if such a demonstration be made, it will not find support or countenance from any of the men whose names are recognized as having a right to speak for Providence ".

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanction - official approval or permission
  • Synonym: endorsement - expression of support or approval

Etymology

From Old French 'contenance' (demeanor), from Latin 'continentia' (restraint), later shifting to 'approval' or 'encouragement' in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntənəns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntɪnəns / (BrE)
a person's face or facial expression, especially as an indication of mood or emotion

Examples

The boy shuddered at the deathly pale countenance with its wrinkles and gray hair.
With my gray hair and my weatherbeaten countenance I certainly looked the honest working stiff.
She concluded by asking for a brief interview - "to settle with you where "- and she threw in a tribute to his "gentle manners "and "the wild originality of your countenance ".

Synset Examples

a pleasant countenance
a stern visage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visage - the face or facial expression of a person
  • Synonym: expression - the look on someone's face conveying a thought or feeling

Etymology

From Old French 'contenance' (demeanor, bearing), from Latin 'continentia' (restraint).
