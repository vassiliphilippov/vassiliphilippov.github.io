backwards

1. ADV

/ ˈbækwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈbækwədz / (BrE)
Toward the back or rear; in the direction behind.

Examples

Then, Arlene threw herself backwards and wiggled in a way that was just wonderful.

Synset Examples

he moved back
tripped when he stepped backward
she looked rearward out the window of the car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forward - toward the front or ahead

Etymology

From 'backward' + -s, with addition of the adverbial '-s' suffix; late Middle English.
