justly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒʌstli / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstli / (BrE)
in a fair or morally right way; with justice or fairness

Examples

Of the two, Porter is justly the better known, for he went far beyond the vital finding of fiction for films to take the first step toward fashioning a language of film, toward making the motion picture the intricate, efficient time machine that it has remained since, even in the most inept hands.

Synset Examples

he was rightly considered the greatest singer of his time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rightly - in a way that is correct or morally right
  • Antonym: unjustly - in an unfair or morally wrong way

Etymology

From Middle English justly, from just + -ly; just from Latin iustus (lawful, righteous).

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒʌstli / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstli / (BrE)
In a manner that conforms to moral or social standards; in a fair or rightful way.

Examples

On Wednesday morning, November 2, 1859, the Providence Daily Journal stated that although Brown justly deserved the extreme penalty, no man, however criminal, ought to suffer the penalty without a fairer trial.

Synset Examples

that serves him right
do right by him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unjustly - in a manner not conforming to moral or social standards; unfairly

Etymology

From 'just' + '-ly', where 'just' is from Latin 'justus' (lawful, fair).
