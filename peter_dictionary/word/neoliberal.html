neoliberal

1. ADJ

/ ˌniː.oʊˈlɪb.ər.əl / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈlɪb.ər.əl / (BrE)
believing in or relating to a policy model that emphasizes free-market capitalism and economic growth alongside traditional liberal values such as individual liberty and limited government intervention

Examples

Finally, we may also mention the several members of the self-consciously "neoliberal "movement that developed at the University of Chicago and is heavily indebted philosophically to the creative work of Alfred North Whitehead.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liberal - supporting individual liberties, open markets, and limited government intervention

Etymology

Formed from 'neo-' (new) and 'liberal'; originally from mid 20th-century political usage.
