bravely

1. ADV

/ ˈbreɪvli / (AmE), / ˈbreɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that shows courage or fearlessness; with bravery.

Examples

It was thickly settled by fifteen thousand citizens and laid out into pig infested streets, mostly around the Battery, going bravely north to Wall Street, but giving up and becoming fields and farms in the region of Harlem Heights.

Synset Examples

bravely he went into the burning house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageously - in a courageous manner
  • Synonym: fearlessly - without fear; boldly
  • Antonym: timidly - in a manner showing lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'brave' + '-ly', where 'brave' ultimately from Old French 'brave', likely from Italian 'bravo' (bold).
