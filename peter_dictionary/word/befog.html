befog

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfɑg / (AmE), / bɪˈfɒg / (BrE)
to make something less clear or visible; to obscure or cloud over, either literally or figuratively

Examples

Or was he now taking the role - the gesture and the suffering - because it was the only way to affirm his history and identity in the torpid, befogged loneliness of this land.

Synset Examples

The stars are obscured by the clouds
the big elm tree obscures our view of the valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make less visible or difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: clear - to make or become easy to see or understand

Etymology

From be- (a prefix) + fog, originally meaning to cover with or as if with fog.
