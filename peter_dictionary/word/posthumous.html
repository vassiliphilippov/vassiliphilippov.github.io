posthumous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑstjəməs / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊməs / (BrE)
Occurring, awarded, or appearing after the death of the person concerned.

Examples

Contemplating these posthumous punishments, Stalin should not lose all hope.

Synset Examples

a posthumous award
a posthumous book
a posthumous daughter

Etymology

From Latin posthumus, later altered from postumus (last, born after the father's death), from post (after) + humus (ground, earth).
