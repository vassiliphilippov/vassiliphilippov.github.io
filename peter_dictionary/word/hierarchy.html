hierarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌrɑrki / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəˌrɑːki / (BrE)
A system in which people or groups are ranked one above the other according to status or authority, especially within an organization or administrative body.

Examples

The return of the Catholic hierarchy in 1850 was looked upon with indignant disapprobation and, in fact, was charged with being a gesture of disloyalty.
But as November 1924 drew close the Democratic hierarchy was sorely troubled by grapevine reports that O' Banion was being wooed by the opposition, and was meeting and conferring with important Republicans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain of command - the official hierarchy of authority in an organization

Etymology

From Greek hierarkhia (rule of a high priest), from hierarkhēs (leader of sacred rites).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌrɑrki / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərɑːki / (BrE)
A system in which people or things are arranged or ranked one above another according to status, authority, or importance.

Examples

Even Rector himself was prey to this spirit of competition and he knew it, not for a more exalted office in the hierarchy of the church - his ambitions for the bishopry had died very early in his career - but for the one clear victory he had talked about to the colonel.
There are boxed proclamations in the newspaper of watchings, pinnings, ringings, engagements, and marriages in a scrupulously graded hierarchy of felicity.

Synset Examples

put honesty first in her hierarchy of values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecking order - informal term for a hierarchy among people or animals

Etymology

From Greek 'hierarkhia' (rule of a high priest), from 'hierarkhēs' (president of sacred rites), from 'hieros' (sacred) + 'arkhē' (rule).
