headache

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.eɪk / (BrE)
A continuous pain in the head, often caused by tension, illness, or other physical or psychological factors.

Examples

Severe headaches were frequent.
My advice, if you live long enough to continue your vocation, is that the next time you 're attracted by the exotic, pass it up - it's nothing but a headache.
She had left the party early, pleading a headache.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: migraine - a severe, recurring headache often accompanied by nausea and sensitivity to light and sound

Etymology

From head + ache; 'ache' from Old English 'æce', related to Proto-Germanic 'akin' (pain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.eɪk / (BrE)
An annoying or difficult problem; something or someone that causes worry or trouble.

Examples

It has multiple implications and possible headaches for your marketing program.
If competition beats you to it, this exciting new product era can have real headaches in store.
Thus, you avoid headaches when an employee wants off for his fourth cousin's funeral.
Coffee breaks can be a real headache if not regulated.
Yet Laos was now one of the most explosive headaches of statesmen around the globe.

Synset Examples

New York traffic is a constant concern
it's a major worry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problem - a matter or situation regarded as unwelcome or harmful
  • Synonym: nuisance - a person, thing, or circumstance causing inconvenience or annoyance

Etymology

From 'head' + 'ache', originally referring to pain in the head, later used figuratively for problems.
