Dewar

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːər / (AmE), / ˈdjuːə / (BrE)
A vacuum-insulated flask for storing very cold liquids such as liquid air or helium, used especially in scientific experiments.

Examples

Surface temperature gradients have been found to exist in liquid helium baths contained in 15 - and 25 - liter metallic storage dewars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum flask - a flask with a vacuum between double walls, used to keep liquids hot or cold

Etymology

Named after Sir James Dewar (1842–1923), Scottish chemist and physicist who invented the flask.
