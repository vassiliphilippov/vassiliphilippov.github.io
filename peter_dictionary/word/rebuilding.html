rebuilding

1. NOUN

/ riˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or act of building something again after it has been damaged or destroyed.

Examples

As for rebuilding, well, that might be looked into, but there was no hurry, no hurry at all.
Soon they will fight their way into the lower middle-class suburbs, and the churches will experience the same decay and rebuilding cycle which has characterized their history for a century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + build + -ing, meaning the act of building again.
