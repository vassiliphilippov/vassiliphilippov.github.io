tungsten

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋstən / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋstən / (BrE)
a heavy, hard, greyish-white metallic element, chiefly used in electrical applications and alloyed in steel; chemical symbol W.

Examples

The cathode consisted of a 1/4 "diameter thoriated tungsten rod attached to a water cooled copper tube.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Swedish 'tung sten' meaning 'heavy stone'.
