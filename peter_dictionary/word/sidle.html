sidle

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
to move sideways or in a quiet, unobtrusive, or furtive manner

Examples

The inventory you acquired from me is n't going to be easy to move; you can't very well sidle up to people on the street and ask if they want to buy a hot Bodhisattva.

Synset Examples

The young man began to sidle near the pretty girl sitting on the log

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slink - to move smoothly and quietly with gliding steps, in a stealthy or sensuous manner
  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and quietly in order to avoid being noticed

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'sidelen', from 'side' + '-le' (frequentative suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
to move sideways, often in a cautious or unobtrusive way

Examples

Everywhere he went in town, people sidled up, gave him the guttural bit or broke into a frightening Tarzan yodel.
She sidled along the booths one step at a time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - to move quietly or unobtrusively

Etymology

Perhaps from 'side' + -le, after older dialect 'sideling' meaning 'sideways'.
