wardrobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːr.droʊb / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.drəʊb / (BrE)
the collection of clothes owned by a particular person

Examples

While white is the coolest summer shade, there are lots of pastel hues along with tintable fabrics that will blend with any wardrobe color.

Etymology

From Old French *warderobe*, from warder (to guard) + robe (robe, garment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌdroʊb / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdrəʊb / (BrE)
a tall, often freestanding piece of furniture with doors used for storing clothes, typically fitted with rails or shelves for hanging or placing garments

Examples

His unsuccessful strivings to give up drink are represented as religious strivings; he keeps a bottle in a wardrobe at home, and "before this wardrobe Praisegod Piepsam had before now gone literally on his knees, and in his wrestlings had bitten his tongue - and still in the end capitulated ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closet - a small room or enclosed recess for storing clothes or household supplies
  • Synonym: cupboard - a cabinet with shelves for storing dishes, food, or clothes

Etymology

From Old French *garde-robe* ('ward' + 'robe').
