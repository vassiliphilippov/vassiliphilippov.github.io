Louisianan

1. NOUN

/ luˌiːziˈænən / (AmE), / luˌiːziˈænən / (BrE)
a native or resident of Louisiana (a state in the southern United States)

Examples

On December 9, 1862, Sergeant Edwin H. Fay, an unusual Louisianan who held A. B. and M. A. degrees from Harvard University and who before the war was headmaster of a private school for boys in Louisiana, wrote his wife:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Louisianian - alternative term for a native or resident of Louisiana

Etymology

Formed from 'Louisiana' (the U.S. state) + the suffix '-an' denoting origin or association.
