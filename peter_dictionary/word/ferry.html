ferry

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)
to transport people or goods regularly between two places, especially across water, usually by boat or ship

Examples

From Burlington, outgoing mail could be ferried across Lake Champlain to the railroad at Port Kent, N. Y..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transport - to carry someone or something from one place to another

Etymology

From Middle English *ferien* (to carry, convey), from Old English *ferian* (to carry, transport).
