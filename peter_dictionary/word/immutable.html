immutable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjutəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːtəbəl / (BrE)
unable to be changed; unchanging over time or unable to be altered.

Examples

Six wardens have been through here in my tenure, and I've learned one immutable, universal truth.

Synset Examples

the view of that time was that all species were immutable, created by God

Related Terms

  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed or altered

Etymology

From Latin 'immutabilis' (unchangeable), from 'in-' (not) + 'mutabilis' (changeable).
