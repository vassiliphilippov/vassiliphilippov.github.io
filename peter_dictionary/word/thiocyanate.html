thiocyanate

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪ.oʊˈsaɪ.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌθaɪ.əʊˈsaɪ.ə.neɪt / (BrE)
A salt or ester of thiocyanic acid, typically formed when an alkaline cyanide reacts with sulfur.

Examples

One type has a small univalent anion of the thiocyanate perchlorate fluoroboride type.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cyanate - a salt or ester of cyanic acid

Etymology

From 'thio-' (indicating sulfur) + 'cyanate' (from cyanide).
