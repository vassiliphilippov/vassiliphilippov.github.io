missive

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
A formal or literary term for a written message or letter, especially a long or official one, sent to someone.

Examples

Still another, annoyed by the brevity of a recently received missive, wrote: "Yore letter was short and sweet, jist like a roasted maget ".

Synset Examples

mailed an indignant letter to the editor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letter - a written message sent to a person or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'missivus', from 'mittere' (to send).
