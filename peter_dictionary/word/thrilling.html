thrilling

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a feeling of excitement and pleasure; producing a surge of emotion or excitement.

Examples

Mantle, it's apparent, resented Casey Stengel's attempts to push and prod him into the perfection the veteran manager saw as a thrilling possibility.
The host of novel applications of electronics to medical problems is far more thrilling because of their implication in matters concerning our health and vitality.

Synset Examples

she gave an electrifying performance
a thrilling performer to watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; dull and tiresome

Etymology

Present participle of 'thrill', from Middle English 'thrillen' (to pierce), later figuratively 'to affect with emotion'.
