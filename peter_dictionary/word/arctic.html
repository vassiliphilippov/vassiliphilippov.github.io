Arctic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrktɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk / (BrE)
the region around the North Pole, north of the Arctic Circle, characterized by cold climate and polar conditions

Examples

She carried, besides her captain, a crew of twenty-one and provisions for a voyage of exploration of the Arctic waters of North America.
Second, they believed it important to determine the fate of the captain - a man whose name is permanently stamped on our maps, on American towns and counties, on a great American river, and on half a million square miles of Arctic seas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Antarctic - the region around the South Pole, opposite the Arctic

Etymology

From Greek 'arktikos' (northern), from 'arktos' (bear, referring to the constellation Ursa Major, prominent in northern skies).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrktɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the regions around the North Pole; characteristic of or found in the Arctic

Examples

Even Hudson, experienced in Arctic sailing and determined as he was, must have had qualms as he slid down the Thames.

Synset Examples

the Arctic summer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antarctic - of or relating to the regions around the South Pole

Etymology

From Greek *arktikos* (of the bear, northern), from *arktos* (bear), referring to the constellation near the North Pole.
