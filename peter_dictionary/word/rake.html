rake

1. VERB

/ reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
to make ground or a surface level and smooth using a rake

Examples

I dig it, rake it smooth, sow the seeds and wet them down with a fog spray.

Synset Examples

rake gravel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - to make a surface even or flat

2. VERB

/ reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
to move something through or across an area, or to draw something over a surface in a raking motion, as with a rake or with fingers

Examples

The lieutenant's sparse brown hair was heavily pomaded, and as Killpath raked the comb through it, it stuck together in thatches so that it looked like umbrella ribs clinging to his pink skull.

Synset Examples

She raked her fingers through her hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comb - to untangle or arrange hair with a comb

Etymology

From Old Norse raka (to scrape together, rake), related to Old English racian.
