liberate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəreɪt / (BrE)
To grant equal rights or freedom to, especially to a group that has been oppressed or marginalized.

Examples

If the raw population figures are crucially relevant, then it is idle to think of liberation, as idle as to suppose that Poland might liberate Russia.
with China attacking Formosa and the North Koreans and North Vietnamese liberating the southern half of their respective countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emancipate - to set free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • Antonym: oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by the unjust exercise of authority

Etymology

From Latin liberatus, past participle of liberare (to set free), from liber (free).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəreɪt / (BrE)
to set someone or something free, especially from imprisonment or oppression

Examples

There were three other men within this prison whom Barton would have liked to liberate, but they were in other cell blocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - to release from captivity or constraint
  • Antonym: imprison - to confine in prison or captivity

Etymology

From Latin liberatus, past participle of liberare (to set free), from liber (free).
