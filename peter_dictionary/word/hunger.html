hunger

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
A physical sensation caused by the need for food; the discomfort or weakness resulting from prolonged lack of food.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: starvation - severe or prolonged lack of food
  • Degree Lower: appetite - natural desire for food
  • Antonym: satiety - state of being fully satisfied, especially with food

Etymology

Old English *hungor*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *honger* and German *Hunger*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
A strong desire or craving for something, such as power, knowledge, or affection.

Examples

But there was a contrast even more decisive than a hunger for fact between the Trial in Jerusalem and those in Moscow and New York.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From Old English hungor, related to Old High German hungar.
