fulfillment

1. NOUN

/ fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
The achievement or realization of a desire, promise, or goal; the completion of a required task or duty.

Examples

For example, in accordance with the fashion of the times, most transitional societies have announced economic development plans of varying numbers of years; such is the mystique of planning that people expect that fulfillment of the plan will follow automatically upon its announcement.
A goal was fixed, as given in Table 2, and attention focused on its fulfillment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of finishing something
  • Synonym: achievement - the successful completion of something or attainment of a goal
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French *fulfiler* (to fulfill), from full + fill. Modern spelling influenced by Middle English *fulfille* (to fill up, complete).

2. NOUN

/ fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of happiness and satisfaction because you are doing something important or using your abilities; the achievement of desired goals or ambitions.

Examples

Religion at its best also offers the experience of spiritual fulfillment by inviting man into the highest realm of the spirit.
At the same time that religion binds the individual helpfully to the supernatural and gives him cosmic peace and a sense of supreme fulfillment, it also has great therapeutic value for him.
When a husband is sexually selfish and heedless of his wife's desires, she is cheated of the fulfillment and pleasure nature intended for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfaction - the pleasure derived from fulfilling a desire or need
  • Antonym: disappointment - sadness or displeasure caused by the non-fulfillment of one's hopes or expectations

Etymology

From Old English 'fullfyllan' (to fill full) via Middle English; influenced by 'full' and 'fill'.
