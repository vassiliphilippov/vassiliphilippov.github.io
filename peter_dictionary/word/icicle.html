icicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪsɪkəl / (BrE)
A long, pointed piece of ice hanging down from a surface, formed by the freezing of dripping water.

Examples

A seeping coldness entered Holden's being; his nerves seemed frost-bitten down to the tips of his tingling fingers and his spine felt stiff and glass like, liable to break like an icicle at any moment.

Etymology

Formed from 'ice' + diminutive suffix '-cle', modelled after 'particle'.
