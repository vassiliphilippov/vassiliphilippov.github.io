out

1. ADJ

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
(in baseball or similar sports) no longer allowed to continue to bat or run, having been put out according to the rules.

Examples

The Flock added a pair of tallies in the third on three straight hits after two were out.
Billy Gardner's line double, which just eluded the diving Minnie Minoso in left field, drove in Jim Lemon with the winning run with two out in the last of the ninth to give the Minnesota Twins a 6 - 5 victory over the Chicago White Sox Monday.
Momentarily the Reds were back in the ball game, trailing only 6 - 3, but the drive fizzled when John Edwards fouled out with men on second and third and two out.

Synset Examples

he was tagged out at second on a close play
he fanned out

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - not out, allowed to continue to bat or run in baseball

2. ADV

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
Not at home; away from one's residence or place of usual stay.

Examples

He got no relief from drink because, though sometimes Precious would buy himself a drink if he went out with us in the evening, he 'd leave it on the table untouched.
As a result, he was persuaded out to dinner.
But he was always persuaded out.
Upon which the detective bureau despatched rifle squads to prevent trouble if O' Banion should send his gunmen out to deal with the hijacking policemen.
They thought it would be a chance for you to make a life out where nobody will be thought any better than the next except for just what's inside of them.

Synset Examples

they went out last night

Related Terms

  • Antonym: home - at one's place of residence

3. ADJ

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
(of a fire, light, or similar) extinguished; no longer burning or shining.

Examples

And make sure it's out when you leave in the morning ".
Be sure it's out when you leave.
Lights out!

Synset Examples

threw his extinct cigarette into the stream
the fire is out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinguished - no longer burning or active
  • Antonym: alight - burning; on fire
  • Antonym: lit - burning or giving light

4. ADJ

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
not regarded as a possible or acceptable option; eliminated from consideration.

Examples

He walked rapidly along the buildings scanning their facades: one was a club - that was out; two others he ruled out because all their windows were lighted.

Synset Examples

a picnic is out because of the weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off the table - not available for consideration or discussion
  • Antonym: in - regarded as possible or allowed

5. ADV

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
indicating movement away from an enclosed or hidden place into the open or visible area.

Examples

Come on out, let'’s play some Hearts.

Synset Examples

the cat came out from under the bed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in - indicating movement to an enclosed or hidden place

6. VERB

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
(of the truth or information) to be made known or disclosed; to become public knowledge.

Examples

When the landlord found out, he got mad and told her to leave.

Synset Examples

The truth will out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make something known that was secret or hidden
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

7. ADJ

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
not permitted or excluded from consideration or use; forbidden.

Examples

No, you're out of the family business.

Synset Examples

forbidden fruit
in our house dancing and playing cards were out
a taboo subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbidden - not allowed; banned
  • Synonym: taboo - prohibited or restricted by social custom
  • Antonym: permitted - allowed; given permission

8. VERB

/ aʊt / (AmE), / aʊt / (BrE)
to expose someone's secret or identity, especially relating to their sexuality or private life, without their consent

Examples

We've been ratted out, boys.

Synset Examples

The gay actor was outed last week
Someone outed a CIA agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expose - to make something visible, typically something secret or hidden
  • Antonym: keep secret - to refrain from revealing information about someone
