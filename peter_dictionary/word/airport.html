airport

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈeəˌpɔːt / (BrE)
A large area of land where aircraft take off, land, and are maintained, usually equipped with buildings, hangars, control towers, and facilities for passengers and cargo.

Examples

Regarding Atlanta's new million dollar airport, the jury recommended "that when the new management takes charge Jan. 1 the airport be operated in a manner that will eliminate political influences ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airfield - a place where aircraft can land and take off, usually without passenger facilities

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'port', following the pattern of 'seaport' for ships.
