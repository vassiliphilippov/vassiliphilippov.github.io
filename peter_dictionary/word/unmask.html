unmask

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈmæsk / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːsk / (BrE)
to reveal the true character or hidden identity of someone or something, especially by exposing deception or pretense

Examples

But Michael Sept had unmasked him, revealing he had never been a bishop, but was an Anabaptist, afraid to state his faith, because he knew John Calvin had written a book against their belief that the soul slept after death.

Synset Examples

The journal article unmasked the corrupt politician

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mask - to conceal or disguise the identity or nature of

Etymology

From un- (not) + mask; dating to 16th century, literally to remove a mask.
