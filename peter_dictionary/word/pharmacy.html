pharmacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːrməsi / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməsi / (BrE)
the science and practice of preparing, dispensing, and formulating drugs and medicines

Examples

He completed his training in pharmacy also, taking his degree with high honors in 1797, and in 1799 was awarded the degree of Doctor of Philosophy along with a prize for an essay in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacology - the branch of medicine concerned with the uses, effects, and modes of action of drugs

Etymology

From Greek pharmakeia (use of drugs, medicine) via late Latin pharmacia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːr.mə.si / (AmE), / ˈfɑː.mə.si / (BrE)
A shop where medicinal drugs are prepared or sold, and often other health-related products are available.

Examples

Eager as he was to pursue this promising line, he was so loaded down with the management of the pharmacy and lectures in the medical and pharmaceutical faculties at the university that he could devote only Sunday afternoons to "galvanizing ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drugstore - A shop where medicines and miscellaneous articles are sold, especially in North America

Etymology

From Greek 'pharmakeia' (the use of drugs, medicine) via Old French and Medieval Latin.
