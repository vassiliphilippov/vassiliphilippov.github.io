3. NOUN

/ fæst / (AmE), / fɑːst / (BrE)
A period during which someone abstains from all or certain kinds of food, often for religious or health reasons.

Examples

Although she weighed only 108 pounds when she visited him, Carroll permitted her to go on a 10 - day fast in which she took nothing but water.
Safe products are flying in as Gotham City goes on a forced fast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feast - A large meal, typically a celebratory one; frequently occurs as the opposite of a fast in religious or ritual contexts.

Etymology

From Old English *fæstan* (to fast, observe abstinence), related to Old Norse *fasta*, German *fasten*.
