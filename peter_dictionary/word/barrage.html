barrage

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
a concentrated artillery bombardment over a wide area intended to saturate the target zone rather than strike specific targets

Examples

The powerful New York Yankees won their 19th world series in a 5 - game romp over outclassed Cincinnati, crushing the Reds in a humiliating 13 - 5 barrage Monday in the loosely played finale.

Synset Examples

they laid down a barrage in front of the advancing troops
the shelling went on for hours without pausing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardment - a continuous attack with bombs, shells, or other missiles

Etymology

From French 'barrage' (barrier), from 'barrer' (to bar, to block), originally applied to defensive barriers, later extended to military fire.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈrɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈbærɑːʒ / (BrE)
A rapid and continuous outpouring of something, especially a large quantity of spoken or written communication such as questions, criticisms, or comments.

Examples

Nevertheless, there are notably frequent instances of deja vu, in which our recognition of an entirely novel event is a feeling of having lived through it before, a feeling which, though vague, withstands the verbal barrage from the most impressive corps of psychologists.
It gave him a chance to unload the stuff and get it down to the cellar without a barrage of acid comments.

Synset Examples

a barrage of questions
a bombardment of mail complaining about his mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardment - an overwhelming and continuous attack, especially with questions or criticisms

Etymology

From French 'barrage' (barrier), originally from 'barrer' (to bar).
