spoonful

1. NOUN

/ ˈspunˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈspuːn.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount of something that a spoon can hold.

Examples

Eugene put a spoonful of powdered coffee into his cup and then filled it with hot water.

Synset Examples

he added two spoons of sugar

Etymology

Formed from 'spoon' + 'ful', modeled on words denoting quantity.
