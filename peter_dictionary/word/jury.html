jury

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.i / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊə.ri / (BrE)
A group of people chosen by law to consider the evidence in a legal case and decide whether someone is guilty or not guilty of a crime, or to give a judgment in a civil case.

Examples

The jury further said in term end presentments that the City Executive Committee, which had over-all charge of the election, "deserves the praise and thanks of the City of Atlanta "for the manner in which the election was conducted.
The September October term jury had been charged by Fulton Superior Court Judge Durwood Pye to investigate reports of possible "irregularities "in the hard-fought primary which was won by Mayor-nominate Ivan Allen Jr..
"Only a relative handful of such reports was received ", the jury said, "considering the widespread interest in the election, the number of voters and the size of this city ".
The jury said it did find that many of Georgia's registration and election laws "are outmoded or inadequate and often ambiguous ".
However, the jury said it believes "these two offices should be combined to achieve greater efficiency and reduce the cost of administration ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: judge - person who presides over a court and makes legal decisions
  • Synonym: panel - a group assembled, especially to decide or judge

Etymology

From Old French *juree* (oath, inquiry), from Latin *jurare* (to swear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A group of people chosen to judge and decide the winners of a competition, contest, or exhibition.

Examples

(Small wonder a Spokane jury awarded the husband $ 35823 for his wife's death.)
On playing some typical situations before a jury of his peers he showed some characteristics rated as unsatisfactory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panel - group of people judging something (competition or discussion)

Etymology

From Old French 'juree' (oath, panel of jurors), from jurer 'to swear', from Latin 'jurare'.
