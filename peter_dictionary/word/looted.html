looted

1. ADJ

/ ˈlutɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːtɪd / (BrE)
Having been wrongfully robbed or stripped of valuables.

Examples

As the robbers leave the looted train, the film suddenly cuts back to the station, where the telegrapher's little daughter arrives with her father's dinner pail only to find him bound on the floor.

Synset Examples

the robbers left the looted train
people returned to the plundered village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundered - robbed forcefully of goods or valuables
  • Synonym: pillaged - robbed using violence, especially in wartime

Etymology

From the verb 'loot', which is from Hindi 'lūṭ' (to plunder).
