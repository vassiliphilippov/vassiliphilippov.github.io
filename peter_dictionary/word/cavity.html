cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈkæv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A large hole or hollow space, especially one in the ground or within a solid object.

Examples

Then, a group of eggs is deposited in a cavity in the beebread loaf and the egg compartment is closed.

Synset Examples

they dug a pit to bury the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pit - a large hole in the ground
  • Synonym: crater - a large bowl-shaped cavity in the ground, often from an explosion or impact

Etymology

From Latin 'cavitas', from 'cavus' meaning 'hollow'.
