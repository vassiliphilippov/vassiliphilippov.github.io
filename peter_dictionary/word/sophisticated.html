sophisticated

1. ADJ

/ səˈfɪstəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having a great deal of worldly experience and knowledge of culture and fashion; showing refinement, taste, and complexity.

Examples

Turning to the more modern versions, Curzon's (London) offers the most sophisticated keyboard work.
The medical device pirate of today, of course, is a far more sophisticated operator than his predecessor of yesteryear - the gallus snapping hawker of snake oil and other patent medicines.
In December I wrote her with authority that we would meet on the steps of the Hotel Astor, a rendezvous spot that I had learned was the most sophisticated.
The early works were conceived for a sophisticated, international audience; the later works were conceived to affirm a way of life for fellow citizens.
Average consumer is becoming more sophisticated regarding product and advertising claims, partly because of widespread criticism of such assertions.

Synset Examples

sophisticated young socialites
a sophisticated audience
a sophisticated lifestyle
a sophisticated book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmopolitan - familiar with and at ease in many different countries and cultures
  • Synonym: refined - showing cultured elegance and good taste
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *sophisticatus*, past participle of *sophisticare* (to tamper with), from Greek *sophos* (wise).

2. ADJ

/ səˈfɪstɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a machine, system, or method) developed to a high degree of complexity; advanced or intricate.

Examples

A still more sophisticated system has been devised for determining the effective power of the heart itself.

Synset Examples

advanced technology
a sophisticated electronic control system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advanced - having developed or progressed to a higher stage; highly developed
  • Synonym: complex - consisting of many different and connected parts; complicated
  • Antonym: simple - easy to understand or not complex; uncomplicated

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sophisticatus (tampered with), from Latin sophisticare (to tamper with), relating to sophisticus (skilled, wise).
