parchment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtʃmənt / (BrE)
A stiff, flat material made from the skin of sheep or goats and prepared for writing on; also, a superior paper made to resemble this material.

Examples

The sharp wind slapped at him and his feet felt like ice as the snow penetrated the holes of his shoes, his only ones, now patched with folded parchment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vellum - fine parchment made from calf skin

Etymology

From Latin 'pergamentum' (from Pergamum, an ancient city where it was reputedly invented).
