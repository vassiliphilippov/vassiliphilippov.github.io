flocculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌflɑːkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌflɒkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which fine particles in a liquid clump together to form larger masses or flocs, often resembling woolly clouds.

Examples

Freed soil must be dispersed and protected against flocculation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulation - the process by which a liquid becomes solid or semi-solid, especially in chemistry or biology

Etymology

From New Latin flocculatio (a flocculating), from Latin flocculus (a small tuft of wool).
