midsummer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌsʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsʌmə / (BrE)
The middle of summer; often refers to the period around the summer solstice (June 21 in the Northern Hemisphere), when the sun reaches its highest point in the sky.

Examples

The day will come, in midsummer, when you find your plants becoming "leggy ", running to tall-growing foliage at the expense of blossoms.

Etymology

From Middle English midsomer, from Old English midsumor, from mid (middle) + summer.
