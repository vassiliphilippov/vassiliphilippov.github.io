commutator

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmjəˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊteɪtə / (BrE)
A device in an electric motor or generator that reverses the direction of electric current between the rotor and the external circuit.

Examples

Because of light leakage from one ultraviolet source to another, the lights are switched by a commutator like assembly rotated by a synchronous motor.

Etymology

From Latin 'commutator', from 'commutare' (to change, exchange).
