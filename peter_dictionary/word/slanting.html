slanting

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslɑːn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Having an oblique or slanted direction or position.

Examples

The first slanting rays of the new day cannot yet dispel all the dark shadows which lie across the Aegean world; but our evidence grows considerably in variety and shows more unmistakably some of the lines of change.
Snow buried the streets and covered the slanting rooftops, as John trudged toward St. Peter's.
Seeing the slanting hand, Kate knew uneasily that it was from the Yankee colonel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aslant - at an angle; sloping
  • Synonym: slanted - sloping or inclined from a straight line or level
  • Antonym: horizontal - parallel to the plane of the horizon; not slanted
  • Antonym: vertical - at right angles to the horizon; perpendicular

Etymology

Present participle of 'slant', from Middle English 'slenten' (to slope, incline).
