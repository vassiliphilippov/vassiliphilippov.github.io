intermediary

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmiːdiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiəri / (BrE)
a person or organization that mediates or acts as a link between two or more parties to help them reach an agreement or understanding

Examples

Furthermore, religion tends to integrate the whole range of values from the highest or ultimate values of God to the intermediary and subordinate values; for example, those regarding material objects and pragmatic ends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement
  • Synonym: go-between - someone who acts as an intermediary or messenger between people or groups
  • Antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something (as opposed to a mediator)

Etymology

From French 'intermédiaire', from Latin 'intermedius' meaning 'lying between'.
