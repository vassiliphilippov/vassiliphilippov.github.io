critical

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
Involving a turning point, crisis, or decisive moment; crucial for the outcome of a situation.

Examples

The quantity of water flow may be critical; a stream or pond which is attractive in the springtime may become stagnant or dry in late summer.
When I try to work out my reasons for feeling that this passage is of critical significance, I come up with the following ideas, which I shall express very briefly here and revert to in a later essay.

Synset Examples

a critical point in the campaign
the critical test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucial - decisive or critical, especially in the success or failure of something
  • Synonym: decisive - settling an issue; producing a definite result
  • Antonym: unimportant - not of great significance or value

Etymology

From Greek kritikós (able to discern, decisive), via Latin criticus.
