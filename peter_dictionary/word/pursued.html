pursued

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsuːd / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːd / (BrE)
Chased or followed, especially with intent to harm or capture.

Examples

All the families would come after you.

Synset Examples

running and leaping like a herd of pursued antelopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunted - followed or chased as prey

Etymology

Past participle of 'pursue', from Old French 'poursuivre', from Latin 'prosequi' (to follow).
