articulation

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the way in which speech sounds are formed by bringing together articulatory organs such as the tongue, lips, and palate

Examples

Of course, something of the same sort occurs with other sectors of the phonology: consonantal articulations have both a linguistic and an individual component.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enunciation - clear and distinct pronunciation of words
  • Synonym: pronunciation - the way in which a word is pronounced

Etymology

From Latin articulatio (a joint, division), from articulare (to divide into joints).
