discriminating

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing refined taste or good judgment; able to discern fine distinctions

Examples

Thereafter the audience waxed applause happy, but discriminating operagoers reserved judgment as her singing showed signs of strain, her musicianship some questionable procedure and her acting uncomfortable stylization.

Synset Examples

the discriminating eye of the connoisseur

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discerning - having or showing good judgment
  • Antonym: indiscriminate - done at random without careful judgment

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'discriminate' + -ing, from Latin 'discriminare' (to divide, distinguish).
