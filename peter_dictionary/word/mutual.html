mutual

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjutʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃu.əl / (BrE)
(of a feeling, action, or relationship) experienced or done by each of two or more parties toward the other or others; shared in common.

Examples

Mr. Bates shows us how this mutual education spreads its inevitable havoc.
But marriage experts say that such mutual contribution and mutual joy are seldom achieved.
But marriage experts say that such mutual contribution and mutual joy are seldom achieved.

Synset Examples

a common friend
the mutual interests of management and labor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocal - given, felt, or done in return; mutual
  • Synonym: shared - held or experienced in common by two or more
  • Antonym: one-sided - involving only one side; not mutual

Etymology

From Latin mutuus (borrowed, lent, reciprocal).
