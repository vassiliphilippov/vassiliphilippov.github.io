indistinctly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktli / (BrE)
in a way that is not clear or easy to see, hear, or understand; unclearly.

Examples

[MAN SPEAKING INDISTINCTLY ON TV] MAN: [ON TV] ...pick up of two by Holden.

Synset Examples

we perceived the change only dimly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unclearly - in a way that is not clear
  • Antonym: clearly - in a clear manner, easy to perceive or understand; antonym

Etymology

From 'indistinct' + '-ly', where 'indistinct' is from Latin 'indistinctus' (not distinct).
