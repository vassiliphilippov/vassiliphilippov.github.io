pork

1. NOUN

/ pɔrk / (AmE), / pɔːk / (BrE)
the meat from a pig, especially when eaten as food

Examples

Early in 1822 he was at Fort Garry offering to bring in pork, flour, liquor and tobacco.
I eat too much eggs and poark it sowered [ on ] my stomack and turn loose on me ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beef - meat from cattle
  • Synonym: lamb - meat from a young sheep
  • Synonym: ham - meat from the upper part of a pig's leg

Etymology

From Old French *porc* (pig, pork), from Latin *porcus* (pig).
