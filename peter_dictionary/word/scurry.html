scurry

1. VERB

/ ˈskɜri / (AmE), / ˈskʌri / (BrE)
to move quickly and hurriedly with short, rapid steps, often because of fear or excitement

Examples

After an earthquake in Japan in 1793 people on the coast at Tugaru were so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground.

Synset Examples

so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scamper - to run or move quickly with light steps, especially playful animals or children

Etymology

Probably from earlier dialectal 'scurry' (to hurry), of imitative origin.
