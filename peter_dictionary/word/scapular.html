scapular

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæpjələr / (AmE), / ˈskapjʊlə / (BrE)
a feather or group of feathers covering the shoulder region of a bird

Examples

That first entry there is the Vermont Flumenophobe, the earliest and one of the most successful of my eighty-three varieties - great big scapulars and hardly any primaries at all.

Etymology

From Late Latin scapularis, from Latin scapula (shoulder blade).
