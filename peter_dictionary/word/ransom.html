ransom

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.səm / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
a sum of money demanded or paid for the release of a captive

Examples

Kidnapper Spurns Ransom; Amy Still Missing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extortion - the act of obtaining something, especially money, through force or threats
  • Antonym: release - the action of freeing someone from captivity

Etymology

From Old French 'rançon', from Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back, redemption).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræn.səm / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
A sum of money demanded or paid for the release of a person who has been kidnapped or captured.

Examples

Emerson - Platonist, idealist, doctrinaire - sounded a high Transcendental note in his "Boston Hymn ", delivered in 1863 in the Boston Music Hall amidst thundering applause: "Pay ransom to the owner and fill the bag to the brim.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free

Etymology

From Old French *rançon*, from Latin *redemptio* (a buying back, redemption).
