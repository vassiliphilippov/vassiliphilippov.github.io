evolve

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to develop gradually by learning or experience, especially in skills, ideas, or qualities

Examples

Mass spectrometric analysis of gases evolved upon heating to 410 ` C indicated nitrogen oxides and water vapor.

Synset Examples

I acquired a strong aversion to television
Children must develop a sense of right and wrong
Dave developed leadership qualities in his new position
develop a passion for painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: develop - to grow or cause to grow or become more mature

Etymology

From Latin evolvere (to unroll, unfold, develop), from e- (out) + volvere (to roll).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form

Examples

This hypothesis is evolved in analogy to the demonstrated action of organic actives in detergency.
Gradually Schiele evolved a somber style of his own - and he had few inhibitions about his subject matter.

Synset Examples

We have developed a new theory of evolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: develop - to grow or cause to grow and become more mature, advanced, or elaborate
  • Degree Higher: progress - to move forward or onward in space or time
  • Degree Lower: emerge - to become apparent, important, or prominent

Etymology

From Latin evolvere (to unroll, to unfold).

3. VERB

/ ɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
(of a living thing or system) to develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form.

Examples

Life has evolved from simple combinations of molecules in the sea to complex combinations in man.
Thus Faulkner reminds us, and wisely, that the "new "South has gradually evolved out of the Old South, and consequently its agrarian roots persist.
The theme may be the formation of a shape from which other shapes evolve.

Synset Examples

Modern man evolved a long time ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: develop - to grow or cause to grow and become more mature, advanced, or elaborate

Etymology

From Latin evolvere (to unroll, unfold), from ex- (out of) + volvere (to roll).
