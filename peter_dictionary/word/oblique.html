oblique

1. ADJ

/ əˈbliːk, oʊˈbliːk / (AmE), / əˈbliːk, ɒˈbliːk / (BrE)
Slanting or inclined in direction, course, or position; neither parallel, perpendicular, nor forming a right angle.

Examples

Thus we obtain g (t) by introducing an oblique g (t) - axis in the b-plane.

Synset Examples

the oblique rays of the winter sun
acute and obtuse angles are oblique angles
the axis of an oblique cone is not perpendicular to its base

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slanting - being at a slant, not level or straight
  • Antonym: perpendicular - at a right angle to a given line or surface
  • Antonym: parallel - lines or surfaces that are always the same distance apart and never meet

Etymology

From Latin obliquus (slanting, sideways).
