kilometer

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈlɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / kɪˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to 1,000 meters, or approximately 0.62 miles.

Examples

Beyond a few million kilometers from the Earth, but still in the region of the Earth's orbit, a prediction of the flux of dust is even more unreliable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: meter - the base metric unit of length
  • Degree Higher: mile - an imperial unit of length (about 1,609 meters)

Etymology

From French 'kilomètre', from Greek 'khilioi' (thousand) + French 'mètre' (meter).
