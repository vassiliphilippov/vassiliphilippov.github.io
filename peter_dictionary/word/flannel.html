flannel

1. NOUN

/ ˈflænəl / (AmE), / ˈflæn.əl / (BrE)
A soft, lightweight woolen or cotton fabric, often used for clothing and bed sheets.

Examples

Dark gray sports jacket, lighter gray slacks, pink flannel shirt, black silk necktie.
Changing his clothes, he put on his dark-blue flannel suit, and laid away the gray jacket with the feeling that he might be putting it aside for good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweed - rough-surfaced woolen cloth, often used for suits and jackets

Etymology

From Old French 'flanelle', from Welsh 'gwlanen' (woolen article).
