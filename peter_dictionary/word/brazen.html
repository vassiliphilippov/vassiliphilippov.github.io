brazen

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪzən / (AmE), / ˈbreɪzən / (BrE)
Shamelessly bold and without any attempt to hide one's behavior; showing a lack of respect and not being restrained by social conventions or propriety.

Examples

He finished the worship service as if there had been no brazen attempt to dishonor God and man.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shameless - having no shame; showing a lack of embarrassment or remorse
  • Synonym: audacious - showing a willingness to take bold risks or being impudently bold
  • Antonym: modest - having or showing a moderate or humble opinion of oneself; not boastful

Etymology

From Old English *bræsian* (to make of brass), from *bræs* (brass), originally referring to something made of or like brass, later extended to mean 'shameless'.
