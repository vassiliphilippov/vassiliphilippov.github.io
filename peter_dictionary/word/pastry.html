pastry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪstri / (AmE), / ˈpeɪstri / (BrE)
A baked food made from dough or batter, such as pies, tarts, or croissants; also, the dough used for such foods.

Examples

...but towards the end, he was paroled to help with the American war effort, so for six months he's worked in my pastry shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cake - sweet baked food made from a mixture of flour, sugar, and other ingredients
  • Synonym: pie - baked dish with a pastry crust and filling
  • Degree Lower: bread - staple food made of flour and water, baked

Etymology

From Middle French *pasterie* (baked food), from *pasté* (paste, dough), from Latin *pasta* (dough, paste).
