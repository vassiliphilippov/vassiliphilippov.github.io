coverage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount and type of insurance provided by a policy

Examples

You may find certain coverage costing much more than is economically feasible, thereby alerting you to desirable revisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance - a contract providing compensation for loss or damage

Etymology

From 'cover' + '-age', indicating the state or amount of being covered.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
The extent to which something is included, dealt with, or covered, such as in reporting, a study, or a reference work.

Examples

The former is intended to decrease the amount of work necessary to extend dictionary coverage.

Synset Examples

the dictionary's coverage of standard English is excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclusion - the act of including something within a whole
  • Antonym: exclusion - the act of leaving something out or not covering it

Etymology

From 'cover' + '-age', originally American English (late 19th cent.).
