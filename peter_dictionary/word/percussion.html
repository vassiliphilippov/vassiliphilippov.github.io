percussion

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
the act or technique of playing percussion instruments, or the use of such instruments in a musical composition

Examples

Some clue to the character of London's approach in these discs may be gained immediately from the fact that ten of the 12 titles include the word "percussion "or "percussive ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumming - the act of playing drums

Etymology

From Latin percussio (a striking, percussion), from percutere (to strike through).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
the act of igniting or exploding a percussion cap, especially in firearms or explosives

Examples

Thus he wired J. P. Lower and Sons of Denver: "Have you any percussion hand grenades for throwing in a house or across a well loaded with balls or shrapnel shot?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detonation - the action of causing a bomb or explosive to explode
  • Antonym: misfire - failure of an explosive device to detonate as intended

Etymology

From Latin percussio(n-), from percutere 'to strike through'.
