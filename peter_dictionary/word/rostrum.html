rostrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑstrəm / (AmE), / ˈrɒstrəm / (BrE)
a raised platform on which a person stands to make a speech or receive an award, giving them prominence above the audience

Examples

He had sat beside Alexander Brandel at the rostrum of a congress of Zionists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dais - a low platform for a lectern, seats of honor, or a throne
  • Synonym: podium - a small platform on which a person stands to be seen by an audience, as when giving a lecture or conducting an orchestra
  • Synonym: platform - a raised level surface on which people or things can stand

Etymology

From Latin 'rostrum' (beak, platform for orators in Ancient Rome).
