Tropidoclonion

1. PROPN

/ troʊˌpɪdəˈkloʊni.ɑn / (AmE), / trəʊˌpɪdəˈkləʊni.ɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small North American colubrid snakes commonly known as lined snakes.

Examples

These three were much alike: lined snake (Tropidoclonion), one year and nine months; red-bellied snake (Storeria), two years; cottonmouth (Ancistrodon), two years.

Etymology

From Greek 'tropis' (keel) + 'klonion' (small branch), referring to the keeled scales and slender body structure.
