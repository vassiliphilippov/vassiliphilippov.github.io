eggplant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛgˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈɛgˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant of the nightshade family, grown for its large, edible, often dark purple, egg-shaped fruit.

Examples

It is thick, much like an egg plant's skin, so that poison sprays, if they are used, present no hazard to the consumer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aubergine - the same vegetable, chiefly British usage

Etymology

From 'egg' + 'plant', referring to the white, egg-shaped varieties first cultivated in Europe.
