Marines

1. PROPN

/ məˈrinz / (AmE), / məˈriːnz / (BrE)
A branch of a country's armed forces trained to serve on land and at sea, especially known for amphibious operations.

Examples

I'’ve enlisted in the Marines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier - member of an army
  • Synonym: sailor - person who serves in a navy

Etymology

From marine, from Latin 'marinus' (of the sea). The plural form 'Marines' denotes the military branch.
