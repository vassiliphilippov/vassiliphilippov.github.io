staginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being artificial, theatrical, or affected, as if staged or performed for effect.

Examples

And when he came to examine the scene, there was a certain staginess to it, it had the smell of planning, and a swift suspicion darted into his mind.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naturalness - the quality of being natural or unaffected
  • Synonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial

Etymology

From 'stage' + '-iness', formed by analogy with 'stagey' (theatrical, affected) + '-ness'.
