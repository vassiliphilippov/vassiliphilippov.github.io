jam

1. NOUN

/ dʒæm / (AmE), / dʒam / (BrE)
A sweet preserve made by boiling crushed fruit and sugar, often spread on bread or used in baking.

Examples

Several sections of a loaf of dark bread; butter; jam; a tiny cake of ice.
Their jams and jellies had not suffered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jelly - A sweet preserve made by boiling fruit juice and sugar until it sets

Etymology

From Old French 'jambe' (leg), mistaken in early 18th century for a French word for fruit preserve, possibly influenced by obsolete 'jamb' meaning to squeeze, related to the fruit being crushed.
