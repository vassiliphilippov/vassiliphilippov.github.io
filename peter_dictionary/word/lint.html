lint

1. NOUN

/ lɪnt / (AmE), / lɪnt / (BrE)
small, soft fibers that come loose from cotton, linen, or similar fabrics, often collecting on clothes or in the dryer

Examples

There were a few blades of lint on the shoulder.
"Many minute hemorrhages in the lungs; particles of lint and thread in the mouth and nostrils.
Scrapings from the bed linen identical with the lint and thread found in the nasal and oral cavities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuzz - tiny fluffy particles or fibers, especially on surface of fabric
  • Synonym: fluff - small loose particles of soft material, especially from fabric

Etymology

From Middle English *lynnet*, from Old English *līn* (flax, linen).
