Tahiti

1. PROPN

/ təˈhiti / (AmE), / təˈhiːti / (BrE)
an island in the south Pacific, the largest and most important island of French Polynesia, known for its natural beauty and cultural significance

Examples

To many a Frenchman - they came 95 years ago, colonized, and stayed until Laos became independent in 1953 - the land had been even more delightfully tranquil than Tahiti.

Etymology

From Tahitian Tahiti, the indigenous name for the island.
