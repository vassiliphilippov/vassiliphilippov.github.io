ossify

1. VERB

/ ˈɑː.sə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒ.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(of tissue or cartilage) to turn into bone; to become bony.

Examples

Although the standard deviation values on which spread of the lines are based are relatively larger for those centers which begin to ossify early (Table 1), there are considerable differences in this value between centers having the closely timed Onsets.

Synset Examples

The tissue ossified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcify - to become hardened by deposition of calcium salts

Etymology

From Latin ossificare, from os (bone) + facere (to make).
