indolence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndoʊləns / (AmE), / ˈɪndələns / (BrE)
habitual laziness or avoidance of activity due to a dislike of work

Examples

In the care-free indolence of the East he sees the last reflection of the old happy existence, and for that reason he loves it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laziness - unwillingness to work or use energy; idleness
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'indolentia' (freedom from pain), from 'in-' (not) + 'dolere' (to feel pain).
