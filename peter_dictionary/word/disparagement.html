disparagement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɛrədʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈspærɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of speaking about someone or something in a way that shows strong disapproval or makes them seem less important

Examples

Some of the choicest remarks made by soldiers in their letters were in disparagement of unpopular officers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemnation - expression of very strong disapproval
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'desparagement' (disadvantage, contempt), from 'desparagier' (marry someone of inferior rank).
