quickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɪknəs / (BrE)
Skill or ability to perform something quickly and efficiently.

Examples

He walked with a heavy list to the right, as that leg was four inches shorter than the other, but the lurch did not reduce his feline quickness with his guns.

Synset Examples

his quick adeptness was a product of good design
he was famous for his facility as an archer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adeptness - skillful performance or proficiency
  • Synonym: facility - ease or skill in doing something
  • Antonym: clumsiness - lack of skill, awkwardness

Etymology

From Middle English 'quiknesse', from 'quik' (alive, moving) + '-ness'.
