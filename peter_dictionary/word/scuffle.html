scuffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈskʌfəl / (BrE)
A short, confused fight or struggle at close quarters, typically involving minor physical contact or disorder.

Examples

He had left after the scuffle; had been seen to leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a rough or noisy fight, typically in a public place
  • Synonym: melee - a confused fight among several people
  • Degree Higher: fight - a violent confrontation involving physical force
  • Degree Lower: quarrel - a heated argument or disagreement, typically less physical

Etymology

Perhaps frequentative of 'scuff', meaning to drag the feet; of uncertain origin.
