merciless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝː.sɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sɪ.ləs / (BrE)
Showing no mercy or compassion; cruel or pitiless.

Examples

Entirely concerned with efficiency, he was merciless in criticizing people who made mistakes, condemning them to too great an extent.

Synset Examples

the merciless enemy
a merciless critic
gave him a merciless beating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiless - showing no pity or compassion
  • Synonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion for others
  • Antonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness to others

Etymology

Formed from 'mercy' + '-less' (without), dating to the late Middle English period.
