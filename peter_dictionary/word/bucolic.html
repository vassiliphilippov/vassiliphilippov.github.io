bucolic

1. ADJ

/ bjuˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / bjuːˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the pleasant aspects of the countryside and country life; idyllically rustic.

Examples

It had a bucolic atmosphere that it has lost long since.

Synset Examples

a country life of arcadian contentment
a pleasant bucolic scene
charming in its pastoral setting
rustic tranquility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - Relating to the idealized life of shepherds or country people
  • Synonym: rustic - Having the simplicity and charm typical of the countryside

Etymology

From Latin *bucolicus*, from Greek *boukolikos* (pastoral, rustic), from *boukolos* (herdsman).
