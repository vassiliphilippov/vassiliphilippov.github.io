rattling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrætlɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid succession of short, sharp, loud sounds, often resembling a series of small knocks or clatters; sometimes used to describe abnormal sounds in the lungs heard with a stethoscope

Examples

Their onrush and retreat, he reported, was accompanied by a great hissing, roaring and rattling.
Over the rattling of fenders, humming of tires and chattering of gears there was a charming melody of whispers and tiny giggles.

Synset Examples

the death rattle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattle - a rapid series of short, sharp sounds
  • Synonym: clatter - a loud, rattling sound, as of hard objects falling or striking each other

Etymology

From the verb 'rattle', which is of imitative origin, suggesting the sound itself.
