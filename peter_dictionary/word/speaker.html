Speaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːkɚ / (AmE), / ˈspiːkə / (BrE)
The presiding officer of a legislative or deliberative assembly, especially in a parliament or similar body.

Examples

Under Formby's plan, an appointee would be selected by a board composed of the governor, lieutenant governor, speaker of the House, attorney general and chief justice of the Texas Supreme Court.

Synset Examples

the leader of the majority party is the Speaker of the House of Representatives

Etymology

From Middle English *spekere*, meaning 'one who speaks'.
