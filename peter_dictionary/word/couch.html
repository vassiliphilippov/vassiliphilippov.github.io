couch

1. NOUN

/ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
A long, comfortable upholstered seat for more than one person, typically with a back and arms.

Examples

Fritzie was on the couch reading; Laura was sitting in an easy chair about eight feet away.
Sangallo studied the drawings and improvised a trestle couch.
Miss Ada had giggled, and she went sweeping and rustling to the couch and sank down.
While Johnny made himself comfortable on the couch, I 'd play the tunes for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - an upholstered seat for more than one person

Etymology

From Old French couche (a bed, lair), from coucher (to lie down), from Latin collocare (to lay, place).

2. VERB

/ kaʊtʃ / (AmE), / kaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to express something in a particular style or wording; to phrase or word something in a specific way

Examples

Naturally, he couched the provision in obscure language.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't put it that way
She cast her request in very polite language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrase - to express or word in a particular way
  • Synonym: word - to choose and use words to express something

Etymology

From Middle English 'couchen' (to lay, place), from Old French 'coucher', from Latin 'collocare' (to lay, place).
