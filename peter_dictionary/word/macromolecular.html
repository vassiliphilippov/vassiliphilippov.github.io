macromolecular

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæ.kroʊ.məˈlɛk.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊ.məˈlɛk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of macromolecules; involving large complex molecules with relatively high molecular weight.

Examples

A study of their activity on thyroglobulin has shown that thyroxine is not preferentially released and that the degradation proceeds stepwise with the formation of macromolecular intermediates (Alpers, Petermann and Rall, 1956).

Synset Examples

macromolecular compounds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: micromolecular - relating to or consisting of small molecules

Etymology

From 'macromolecule' + -ar, with 'macromolecule' from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'molecule' from Latin 'moles' (mass).
