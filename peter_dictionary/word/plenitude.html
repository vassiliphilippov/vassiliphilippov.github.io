plenitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛnɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈplɛnɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a state of being full or having an abundance of something; a full supply

Examples

And all this too shall pass away: it came to him out of some dim corner of memory from a church service when he was a boy - yes, in a white church with a thin spur steeple in the patriarchal Hudson Valley, where a feeling of plenitude was normal in those English Dutch manors with their well-fed squires.

Synset Examples

there was plenty of food for everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply; shortage

Etymology

From Latin plenitudo (fullness), from plenus (full).
