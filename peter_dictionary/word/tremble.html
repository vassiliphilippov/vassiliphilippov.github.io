tremble

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɛmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmbəl / (BrE)
to shake slightly and uncontrollably as a result of anxiety, excitement, weakness, or cold

Examples

The walls bulged, the floor trembled, the windowpanes rattled.
They both tried to keep smiling and winking for a long time, but it made their lips and eyelids tremble.
They also caught on a little bit on how to smile a lot without your lips trembling.
"Imagine you won't get your allowance if you 're caught not smiling - or smiling with your lips trembling too much ", Arlene suggested.

Synset Examples

His hands were trembling when he signed the document

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move or cause to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements

Etymology

From Old French *trembler*, from Latin *tremulare*, frequentative of *tremere* (to tremble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmbl / (BrE)
A shaking or quivering movement of the body, usually caused by cold, fear, or excitement.

Examples

[SIGHS] [VOICE TREMBLING] I always wanted to be thought of as a brother by you, Mikey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiver - a slight shaking movement caused by fear, cold, or excitement
  • Synonym: quake - a violent shaking movement

Etymology

From Old French *trembler*, from Latin *tremulare*, frequentative of *tremere* (to tremble).
