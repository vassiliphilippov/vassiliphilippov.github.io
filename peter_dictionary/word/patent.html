patent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt, ˈpeɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈpætnt, ˈpeɪtənt / (BrE)
an official legal document that gives an inventor the exclusive right to make, use, or sell an invention for a certain period of time

Examples

It is interesting to note how many of the plants on Massachusetts' Route 128 draw most of their income either from the government in non-competitive cost-plus arrangements, or from the exploitation of patents which grant at least a partial monopoly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyright - exclusive legal right to reproduce a literary or artistic work
  • Synonym: trademark - legal protection for a brand name, logo, or symbol

Etymology

From Latin 'patent-', meaning 'lying open', from 'patere' (to lie open).

2. VERB

/ ˈpætənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt / (BrE)
to obtain a government grant protecting the exclusive rights to an invention or process for a certain period.

Examples

Timothy Palmer, who invented and later patented the arch type of construction for wooden bridges, was the genius who planned and supervised the building of the Essex, or "Deer Island "bridge although the actual work was carried out under the direction of William Coombs, who received 300 as recompense.

Synset Examples

Should I patent this invention?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyright - obtain a legal right to exclusively publish or reproduce creative work

Etymology

From Latin 'patent-', present participle of 'patere' (to lie open); applied in legal sense since the 15th century.
