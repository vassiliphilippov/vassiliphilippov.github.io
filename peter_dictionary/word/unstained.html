unstained

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsteɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪnd / (BrE)
(of a material or object) not treated with a coating of stain or varnish.

Examples

The unstained strips were then marked, using the stained ones as a guide, and cut transversely so as to separate the various protein bands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stained - having a coating of stain or varnish

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'stained' (treated with stain or varnish).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsteɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪnd / (BrE)
not marked, tainted, or discolored by a stain; not stained

Examples

Stained or unstained sections were examined under dark field illumination in a Zeiss fluorescence microscope equipped with a mercury vapor lamp (Osram HBO 200).
Unstained sections mounted in buffered glycerine or sections treated only with NS or * * f did not show such green fluorescence.

Synset Examples

An apron keeps his clothing unstained

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stained - marked or discolored by something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + stained.
