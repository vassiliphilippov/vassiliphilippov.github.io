drug

1. NOUN

/ drʌɡ / (AmE), / drʌɡ / (BrE)
a substance used as a medicine or narcotic to treat, prevent, or diagnose disease, or to alter physical or mental states

Examples

The charge: violation of the California Medical Practices Act by practicing medicine without a license and selling misbranded drugs.
Huge industries are involved also in the production of biological products, drugs and cosmetics which are liable to this type of attack.
Antithyroid drugs are of two general types.
The other group of antithyroid agents or drugs is typified by thiouracil.
These drugs have no effect on the iodide concentrating mechanism, but they inhibit organification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine - a substance used to treat illness or injury
  • Synonym: narcotic - a drug affecting mood or behavior, often used illicitly

Etymology

From Old French *drogue* (medicine, chemical), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ drʌɡ / (AmE), / drʌɡ / (BrE)
to give a person or animal a drug, especially in order to affect their behavior or mental state, often secretly or illegally

Examples

The theatre was hot and they were drugged with boredom.

Synset Examples

They drugged the kidnapped tourist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dose - to administer a measured amount of medicine
