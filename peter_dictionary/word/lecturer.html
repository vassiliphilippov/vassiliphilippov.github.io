lecturer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
A person who gives lectures, especially as a profession at a college or university.

Examples

Thanks to Spoken Arts Records, history buffs may hear Lincoln's "most memorable speeches and letters "in a two disc set, interpreted by Lincoln authority and lecturer Roy P. Basler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: professor - senior academic instructor at a college or university
  • Synonym: instructor - person who teaches, especially in a college or university

Etymology

From lecture + -er, meaning one who lectures.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃərə / (BrE)
a person who gives lectures, especially as an academic job at a university or college

Examples

The new century opened with Oersted beginning his professional career in charge of an apothecary shop in Copenhagen and as lecturer at the university.
He continued as a popular lecturer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: professor - a senior academic teacher or researcher, especially at a university
  • Degree Lower: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school

Etymology

From lecture + -er, meaning 'one who lectures'.
