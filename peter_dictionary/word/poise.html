poise

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪz / (AmE), / pɔɪz / (BrE)
A unit of dynamic viscosity in the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) system equal to the viscosity of a fluid in which a force of one dyne per square centimeter maintains a velocity of one centimeter per second.

Examples

However, there are photographs of suspended drops of cyclohexanol phthalate (viscosity 155 poises) suspended in corn syrup of 71 poises in a paper by Mason and Bartok.
However, there are photographs of suspended drops of cyclohexanol phthalate (viscosity 155 poises) suspended in corn syrup of 71 poises in a paper by Mason and Bartok.

Etymology

Named after French physicist Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille (1797–1869).

2. VERB

/ pɔɪz / (AmE), / pɔɪz / (BrE)
to be or remain motionless, especially in a balanced or suspended position, as if ready to move or act

Examples

Innumerable motels from Tucson to New York boast swimming pools ("swim at your own risk "is the hospitable sign poised at the brink of most pools).
The Free Chinese know that the situation on the Mainland is in flux, and are poised to strike.
It landed on Roberts' sprawled right thigh, poised precariously, then slid off to the ground.

Synset Examples

The bird poised for a few moments before it attacked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hover - to remain floating or suspended in one place
  • Synonym: perch - to rest or settle in a raised position

Etymology

From Old French *pois* (weight), from Latin *pensum* (weight, something weighed), root of 'pendere' (to weigh, to hang).

3. VERB

/ pɔɪz / (AmE), / pɔɪz / (BrE)
to prepare oneself mentally or physically for something difficult, unpleasant, or challenging

Examples

Yesterday it offered "Poised for Violence ", by Jean Reavey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace - to prepare oneself for something difficult or unpleasant
  • Antonym: relax - to become less tense or prepared

Etymology

From Old French *pois* (weight), from Latin *pensum* (something weighed), influencing the sense of balance or readiness.

4. VERB

/ pɔɪz / (AmE), / pɔɪz / (BrE)
to hold or balance something steadily, especially in a suspended or ready position

Examples

One white hand poised a stick above his desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance - keep something steady so it does not fall
  • Antonym: unbalance - to make unstable or likely to fall

Etymology

From Old French 'pois' (weight), from Latin 'pensum' (something weighed), from 'pendere' (to weigh).
