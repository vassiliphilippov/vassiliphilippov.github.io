draught

1. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
an act of swallowing a large amount of liquid at once, especially in a single, hurried gulp

Examples

He took a long but carefully controlled draught.

Synset Examples

he finished it at a single gulp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - an act of swallowing quickly or in large mouthfuls

Etymology

From Old English 'dræht', related to Old Norse 'dráttr' (pull, drawing; extended sense to 'swallowing drink').
