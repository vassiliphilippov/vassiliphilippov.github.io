intensive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Involving or requiring a great deal of effort, energy, or concentration; characterized by a high degree or intensity.

Examples

Another case may be given in illustration of a successful use of analysis, and also of the employment of a procedure for intensive analysis.

Synset Examples

the questioning was intensive
intensive care
research-intensive
a labor-intensive industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intense - having a strong degree or force; extreme

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'intensivus', from Latin 'intensus' (stretched, strained).
