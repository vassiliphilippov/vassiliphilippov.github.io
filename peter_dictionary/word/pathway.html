pathway

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæθˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθˌweɪ / (BrE)
a path or track that has been made by people walking, especially through grass or undergrowth; a trodden path

Examples

Also three other direct pathways of alveolar bronchial arterial supply were noted: via the pleura; through the interlobular septa; and along the terminal bronchiole (figs. 14, 17, 18, 19).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: path - a way or track laid down for walking or made by continual treading

Etymology

From Middle English ‘pathwei’, formed from ‘path’ + ‘way’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæθˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθˌweɪ / (BrE)
A bundle of nerve fibers in the brain or nervous system that carries signals along a specific route.

Examples

Facilitatory processes take place between neocortex and hypothalamus via ascending and descending pathways.
This tendency can be broken either by restoring hypothalamic excitability directly or via cortico-hypothalamic pathways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tract - A bundle of nerve fibers having a common origin and destination in the central nervous system
