slap

1. VERB

/ slæp / (AmE), / slæp / (BrE)
to hit someone or something with a flat object or an open hand, usually making a sharp sound

Examples

The sharp wind slapped at him and his feet felt like ice as the snow penetrated the holes of his shoes, his only ones, now patched with folded parchment.
He slapped the reins on the back of the powerful gray horse and held on as the sulky's wheels hit a pothole and came out with a jolt and went on.
Show her the new swimming pool and she would say, slapping her ankle, "I suppose this must be where you breed your gigantic mosquitoes ".
They had slapped their thighs.
She slapped the receiver into its holder and stepped away.

Synset Examples

The impatient teacher slapped the student
a gunshot slapped him on the forehead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - to strike sharply with an open hand or a flat object
  • Antonym: punch - to strike with a closed fist
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly, often with fingers or a light object

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound produced by a flat blow.

2. NOUN

/ slæp / (AmE), / slæp / (BrE)
A quick blow delivered with an open hand, usually producing a sharp sound.

Examples

Is that why you slapped my brother around in public?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - a sharp blow with the flat part of the hand or another flat object
