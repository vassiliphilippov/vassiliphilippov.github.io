untouched

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (BrE)
not influenced, affected, or changed by something; remaining in its original condition.

Examples

The society is likely to be characterized by having a fairly modernized urban sector and a relatively untouched rural sector, with very poor communications between the two.

Synset Examples

stewed in its petty provincialism untouched by the brisk debates that stirred the old world
unswayed by personal considerations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: affected - influenced or changed by something
  • Synonym: unspoiled - not altered or damaged; remaining in original condition
  • Synonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled

Etymology

From un- (not) + touched (from Middle English touchen).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌtʃt / (BrE)
(of food or drink) not eaten, drunk, or used; left in its original, complete condition.

Examples

He got no relief from drink because, though sometimes Precious would buy himself a drink if he went out with us in the evening, he 'd leave it on the table untouched.

Synset Examples

an untouched cocktail in her hand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consumed - used up, eaten, or drunk

Etymology

From un- (not) + touched (past participle of 'touch').
