stockade

1. NOUN

/ stɑˈkeɪd / (AmE), / stɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
A defensive barrier or enclosure made of strong posts or timbers set upright in the ground, often used as a fortification or prison.

Examples

Here they built huts and a stockade named Fort Daer after Selkirk's barony in Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palisade - a fence or wall made from iron or wooden stakes, used as a defensive structure

Etymology

From French 'stockade', from Spanish 'estacada' (fence made of stakes), from 'estaca' (stake).
