edition

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular form or version of a published text, such as a book, magazine, or newspaper

Examples

Whether considered alone or in relation to other editions, COLH 40 is a document of prime importance.
The first substantially complete stereo Giselle (and the only one of its scope since Feyer's four-sided LP edition of 1958 for Angel), this set is, I'm afraid, likely to provide more horrid fascination than enjoyment.
This 1930 edition also had songs in it by Vernon Duke and Ira Gershwin, by E. Y. Harburg and Duke, and by Harry Myers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: issue - a particular publication of a periodical or magazine

Etymology

From Latin 'editio' (publication), from 'edere' (to bring forth, publish).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a set of identical copies of a publication or product made available to the public at the same time

Examples

The Glazer-Fine Arts edition (Concert-Disc) is a model of lucidity and organization.
The Hephzibah Menuhin-Amadeus Quartet (Angel) and Victor Babin-Festival Quartet (RCA Victor) editions give us superlative string playing (both in symphonic style) crippled by unimaginative piano playing.

Synset Examples

the first edition appeared in 1920
it was too late for the morning edition
they issued a limited edition of Bach recordings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: printing - the total number of copies of a publication printed at one time

Etymology

From Latin 'editio' (publication, bringing forth), from 'edere' (to bring forth, publish).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular issue of a newspaper, published at a specific time or for a specific audience

Examples

If I bring something to you, can we make the evening edition?

Synset Examples

he read it in yesterday's edition of the Times

Etymology

From Latin 'editio(n-)' meaning 'production, publication', from 'edere' meaning 'to put forth'.
