outclass

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈklæs / (AmE), / aʊtˈklɑːs / (BrE)
to be much better than others in ability, quality, or performance; to surpass completely

Examples

"The Pirates showed they could outclass the field last year.

Synset Examples

The Yankees outclassed Cincinnati

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outshine - to be much better or more impressive than someone or something
  • Synonym: surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
  • Synonym: excel - to be exceptionally good at or proficient in an activity

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'out-' meaning 'beyond' and 'class', originally referring to competitive divisions.
