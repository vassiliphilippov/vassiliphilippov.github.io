delude

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlud / (AmE), / dɪˈluːd / (BrE)
to make someone believe something that is not true; to deceive or mislead.

Examples

And therein, I feel, many Northerners delude themselves about the South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: mislead - to cause someone to believe something that is incorrect

Etymology

From Latin 'deludere', from de- 'down' + ludere 'to play' (i.e., 'to play false').
