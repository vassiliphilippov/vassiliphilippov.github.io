rapture

1. NOUN

/ ˈræptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈræptʃə / (BrE)
A feeling of intense pleasure, joy, or enthusiasm; the state of being carried away by overwhelming emotion.

Examples

I am sure he is in raptures ".
I'm not pleading for the heart that leaps up when it beholds a rainbow in the sky, or for the heart that with rapture fills and dances with the daffodils.

Synset Examples

listening to sweet music in a perfect rapture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness or joyful excitement
  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or satisfaction
  • Antonym: misery - a state or feeling of great distress or discomfort

Etymology

From Medieval Latin raptura (seizure, snatching), from Latin raptus, past participle of rapere (to seize).
