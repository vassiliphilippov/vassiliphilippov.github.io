fractional

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrækʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to or being a part or fraction of a whole; not complete or entire.

Examples

Yet it is the accumulation of distortion, the fitting together of fractional bits until the total reaches the threshold of our awareness, that makes records sound like records.

Synset Examples

a fractional share of the vote
a partial dose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partial - existing only in part; incomplete

Etymology

From Latin 'fractio' meaning 'breaking', via 'fraction' + '-al'.
