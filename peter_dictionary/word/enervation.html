enervation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of being drained of energy or vitality; physical, mental, or emotional exhaustion.

Examples

A feeling of futility, an enervation of mind greater than any fatigue he had ever known, seeped through him.

Synset Examples

an enervation of mind greater than any fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - the state or condition of lacking strength or energy
  • Antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy

Etymology

From Latin enervare (to weaken), from enervis (without sinews).
