waive

1. VERB

/ weɪv / (AmE), / weɪv / (BrE)
to refrain from insisting on or using (a right or claim); to choose not to demand something you are entitled to

Examples

The filing of an original or amended declaration, otherwise due on January 15, 1962, is also waived, if you file your Form 1040 for 1961 and pay the full tax by January 31, 1962.

Synset Examples

We are dispensing with formalities
relinquish the old ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily give up something
  • Degree Lower: forfeit - to lose or give up something as a penalty

Etymology

From Old French 'weiver' (to abandon) and Anglo-Norman 'waiver', ultimately of Germanic origin.
