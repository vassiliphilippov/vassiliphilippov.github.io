personage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənɪdʒ / (BrE)
an important person or a character in a literary work; sometimes used as a formal or slightly humorous term for a person whose identity is unspecified or unimportant

Examples

A comedy in Three Acts ", in which, under "Personages ", Henrietta appeared as "A Schoolmarm ", and Bertha, who was only a trifle less brilliant in high school than Henrietta had been, appeared as "Dummkopf ".

Synset Examples

a strange personage appeared at the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figure - a person of importance or influence
  • Synonym: character - a person in a novel, play, or story

Etymology

From Old French 'personnage', from 'personne' (person), from Latin 'persona'.
