premonition

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
A strong feeling or sense that something, especially something unpleasant, is about to happen.

Examples

The following morning, as John entered the Place Molard on his way to visit a sick refugee, he had a premonition of danger.
The kiss outraged our friends but it was done and meanwhile had released in me all the remote, exciting premonitions of lust, all the mysterious sensations that I had imagined a truly consummated kiss would convey to me.

Synset Examples

a steadily escalating sense of foreboding
the lawyer had a presentiment that the judge would dismiss the case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreboding - a feeling that something bad will happen
  • Synonym: presentiment - an intuitive feeling about the future, especially something bad
  • Degree Higher: doom - sense of unavoidable negative fate

Etymology

From Latin praemonitio (a forewarning), from prae- (before) + monere (to warn).
