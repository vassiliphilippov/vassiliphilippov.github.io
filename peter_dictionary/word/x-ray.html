X-ray

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks reɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛks reɪ / (BrE)
A form of electromagnetic radiation with very short wavelengths, produced when high-speed electrons strike a solid target, used particularly in medical imaging and material analysis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gamma ray - higher-energy electromagnetic radiation than X-rays
  • Degree Lower: ultraviolet - lower-energy electromagnetic radiation than X-rays

Etymology

From 'X', denoting an unknown quantity, and 'ray', after its discovery by Wilhelm Röntgen in 1895.
