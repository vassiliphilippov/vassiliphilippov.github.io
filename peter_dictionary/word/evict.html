evict

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvɪkt / (AmE), / ɪˈvɪkt / (BrE)
to officially remove someone from a property, especially by legal process

Examples

She says she'’s been evicted for no good reason.

Synset Examples

The landlord evicted the tenants after they had not paid the rent for four months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to force someone to leave a place
  • Synonym: eject - to force someone to leave a place or position

Etymology

From Latin evincere (to overcome, expel), via Latin evictus (past participle of 'evincere').
