anaplasmosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəplæzˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəplazˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease of cattle, similar to Texas fever, transmitted by cattle ticks and caused by infection with Anaplasma bacteria.

Examples

To aid in the prevention of anaplasmosis, feed not less than 0.5 milligram per pound of body weight daily.

Etymology

From New Latin Anaplasma (genus name of the bacteria) + -osis (disease condition).
