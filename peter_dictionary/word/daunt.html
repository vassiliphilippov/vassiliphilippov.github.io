daunt

1. VERB

/ dɔnt / (AmE), / dɔːnt / (BrE)
to make someone feel intimidated or discouraged; to cause someone to lose courage or confidence

Examples

Claire was bitterly disappointed but determined not to let the rebuff daunt her purpose.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, especially to make them do what one wants
  • Synonym: discourage - cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'danter', from Latin 'domitare' (to tame, subdue), frequentative of 'domare' (to tame, subdue).
