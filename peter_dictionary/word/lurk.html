lurk

1. VERB

/ lɝk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to remain hidden so as to wait in ambush or to move stealthily, often with a secretive or sinister intent

Examples

If there is a moral lurking among the shenanigans, it is hard to find.
Somewhere, beyond, where shadows lurked, must be the yawning pit of which Papa preached and the dreadful Lake of Fire.
He had had the need to understand what life lurked behind the mask of flesh, behind the oath, the banter, the sadness.
Demagogues of this sort found communist bogeys lurking behind any new idea that would run counter to stereotyped notions.
How was he to suspect that an assassin had been lurking somewhere across the street waiting for just such a chance?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - to put or keep out of sight
  • Synonym: skulk - to keep out of sight, typically with a sinister or cowardly motive
  • Antonym: appear - to become visible or come into view

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English lorken (to frown, look sullen).

2. VERB

/ lɝk / (AmE), / lɜːk / (BrE)
to stay or wait in a secret or hidden way, often with the intention of doing something wrong or unpleasant

Examples

Just as Holmes the eccentric stands behind Holmes the detective, so Holmes the potential criminal lurks behind both.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loiter - stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: skulk - keep out of sight, typically with sinister or cowardly motive

Etymology

Probably from Middle English lurken, of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian lurka (to sneak).
