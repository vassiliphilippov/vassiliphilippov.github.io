demarcate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑrˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːkeɪt / (BrE)
to set the boundaries or limits of something; to separate clearly from something else, often by defining or marking boundaries

Examples

Eventually such incidents became more sporadic, and more sharply demarcated from her day-after-day behavior, and in one particular session, after several minutes of such behavior - which, as usual, went on without any accompanying words from her - she asked, eagerly, "Did you see Granny "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delimit - to set the limits or boundaries of something precisely
  • Synonym: define - to state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of

Etymology

From Spanish *demarcar*, from de- (from) + marcar (to mark), influenced by French *démarquer*; ultimately from Latin *marcare* (to mark).
