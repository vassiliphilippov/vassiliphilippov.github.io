pomp

1. NOUN

/ pɑmp / (AmE), / pɒmp / (BrE)
Ceremonial display and splendor, especially involving elaborate or grand proceedings.

Examples

And all his pomps? - I do renounce them.

Synset Examples

entered with much eclat in a coach drawn by eight white horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendor - brilliant or impressive appearance; grandeur
  • Synonym: pageantry - elaborate display or ceremony
  • Antonym: austerity - plainness or simplicity, especially relating to lack of adornment or luxury

Etymology

From Latin 'pompa' (procession, display) via Old French 'pompe'.
