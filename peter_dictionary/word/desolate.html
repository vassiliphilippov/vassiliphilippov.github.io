desolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsələt / (BrE)
(of a place) empty, providing no shelter or sustenance; barren and bleak.

Examples

A site may be a rundown slum or a desolate piece of desert in appearance today but have excellent potentials for the future with a little development or water.
It was one with the desolate rocks and the alien water on those days when she hated the sea.
It is a lonely, rather desolate region, but full of legendary and historic associations.
He doubted if a man could wade as far as the desolate, dry hills that rimmed the valley.

Synset Examples

bare rocky hills
barren lands
the bleak treeless regions of the high Andes
the desolate surface of the moon
a stark landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barren - land too poor to produce much or any vegetation
  • Synonym: bleak - lacking vegetation and exposed to the elements
  • Antonym: fertile - (of land) able to produce abundant crops or vegetation

Etymology

From Latin desolatus, past participle of desolare (to abandon, leave alone).
