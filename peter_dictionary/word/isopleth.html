isopleth

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.səˌplɛθ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.səˌplɛθ / (BrE)
a line on a map that connects points where a measured value, such as temperature or rainfall, is the same

Examples

From these dosage isopleths it can be seen that an area of over 34000 square miles was covered.
As can be seen from these dosage isopleths, approximately 100 square miles was covered within the area sampled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isogram - a line on a map connecting points with equal value of a given variable
  • Synonym: contour line - a line on a map joining points of equal elevation

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'plēthos' (quantity).
