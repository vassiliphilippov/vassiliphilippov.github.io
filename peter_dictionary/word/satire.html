satire

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætaɪr / (AmE), / ˈsætaɪə / (BrE)
the use of humour, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize people's stupidity or vices, especially in the context of contemporary politics and other topical issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: irony - the use of words to express something opposite to their literal meaning
  • Synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt

Etymology

From Latin 'satira' (medley, poetic form), altered from earlier 'satura'.
