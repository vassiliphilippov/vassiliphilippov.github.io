silicate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkət / (BrE)
a salt or ester containing the anion SiO4 or derived from silicic acid, commonly found in minerals and used in manufacturing

Examples

Hydroxides, orthophosphates, borates, carbonates, and silicates are important inorganic ingredients of hard surface cleaners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonate - a salt or ester of carbonic acid

Etymology

From French *silicate*, from Latin *silica* (flint, hard stone).
