nocturnal

1. ADJ

/ nɑkˈtɝːnəl / (AmE), / nɒkˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
Active or occurring at night rather than during the day.

Examples

The dark forms moved like mourners on some nocturnal pilgrimage, their dirge unsung for want of vocal chords.

Synset Examples

nocturnal animals are active at night
nocturnal plants have flowers that open at night and close by day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diurnal - active during the day

Etymology

From Latin nocturnalis, from nocturnus ('of night'), from nox ('night').
