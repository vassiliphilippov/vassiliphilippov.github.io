investigator

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to examine facts in order to discover the truth about something, especially as a professional activity.

Examples

This, of course, depends on the character of the site itself, the previous experience of the investigator, and the number of factors needed to arrive at a good decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose occupation is to investigate crimes

Etymology

From Latin 'investigātor', from 'investigāre' (to trace out, search after).
