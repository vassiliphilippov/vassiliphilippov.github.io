symptom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmptəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmptəm / (BrE)
A physical or mental feature that is regarded as indicating a condition of disease, especially such a feature that is apparent to the patient.

Examples

This is a common symptom and the cause usually is pressure on the nerve leading to the affected hand.

Although in both emotions sympathetic symptoms are present, different autonomic somatic patterns underlie aggression and anxiety, respectively, as indicated by the rate of the excretion of the catecholamines, the state of the muscle tone, and the Mecholyl test.
Eight or ten years ago, a couple of French hoods stole a priceless Khmer head from the Musee Guimet, in Paris, and a week later crawled into the Salpetriere with unmistakable symptoms of leprosy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - an indication of a medical condition observed by a doctor

Etymology

From Greek 'sýmptōma' (a happening, accident, symptom), from 'sympiptein' (to befall).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmptəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmptəm / (BrE)
A sign or indication of the existence of something, especially something undesirable or problematic.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - an indication of the existence or occurrence of something
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From Greek *sýmptōma* (a happening, accident), from *sympíptō* (to happen, befall).
