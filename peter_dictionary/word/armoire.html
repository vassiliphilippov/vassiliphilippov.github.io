armoire

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈmwɑr / (AmE), / ɑːˈmwɑː / (BrE)
A large, often ornate wardrobe or movable cabinet, originally used for storing weapons but now typically used for storing clothes or household items.

Examples

She thrust the envelope back in the bag; there was no point in locking it up in the armoire now, it was like locking the barn after the horse was stolen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wardrobe - a large, tall cupboard or cabinet in which clothes may be hung or stored
  • Synonym: cabinet - a piece of furniture with shelves, drawers, or compartments for storage

Etymology

From French armoire, from Old French armarie (cupboard), from Latin armarium (closet for storing arms or tools).
