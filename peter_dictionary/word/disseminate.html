disseminate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to spread or distribute something, especially information, ideas, or substances, widely so that it reaches many people or places

Examples

In the first trial an inert substance was disseminated from a boat travelling some ten miles off shore under appropriately selected meteorological conditions.
Zinc cadmium sulfide in particles of 2 microns in size were disseminated.
Four hundred and fifty pounds was disseminated while the ship was traveling a distance of 156 miles.
In this connection it should be capable of being disseminated without excessive destruction.
These things have been disseminated by other means, but always in the wake of extensive publication of analytic results.

Synset Examples

spread information
circulate a rumor
broadcast the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to open or distribute something over a surface; to make widely known
  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit or spread widely, especially through the media
  • Antonym: concentrate - to gather or focus in one place (opposite of spread out)

Etymology

From Latin disseminare (to scatter, spread about), from dis- (apart) + seminare (to sow, plant).
