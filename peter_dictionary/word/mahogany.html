mahogany

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
Hard reddish-brown wood from a tropical tree, widely used for high-quality furniture and cabinetwork.

Examples

In the room next to theirs was a huge cradle, of mahogany, ornately carved and decorated with gold leaf.
Looking around slowly, he saw a marble fireplace, a desk, a low bookcase of mahogany with criss-crossed brass wire instead of glass panes in the doors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - hardwood used for furniture and flooring

Etymology

From Spanish 'mahogany', from Taino (West Indies indigenous language).
