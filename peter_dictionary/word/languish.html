languish

1. VERB

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
To become weak or lose vitality, especially as a result of grief, neglect, or hardship.

Examples

But the liaison successfully started in the last days of autumn was now languishing.

Synset Examples

After her husband died, she just pined away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine away - to waste away from grief or longing
  • Antonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful

Etymology

From Old French *languiss-,* stem of *languir*, from Latin *languēre* (to be faint or weak).
