supernatant

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpɚˈneɪtənt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈneɪtənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) floating above a sediment or precipitate, especially after separation by centrifugation or settling.

Examples

After dialysis the sample was centrifuged and the supernatant placed on a * * f cm column of DEAE-cellulose equilibrated with starting buffer.

Synset Examples

the supernatant fat was skimmed off

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sediment - solid material that settles to the bottom of a liquid

Etymology

From Latin supernatantem, present participle of supernatāre (to float above), from super- (above) + natāre (to swim, float).
