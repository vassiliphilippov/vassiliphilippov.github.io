Americanism

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkənɪzəm / (BrE)
loyalty or allegiance to the United States and its institutions

Examples

Mexico, Panama, and Venezuela are displaying open sympathy for Castroism, and there is no country - save the Dominican Republic whose funeral services we recently arranged - where Castroism and anti Americanism does not prevent the government from unqualifiedly espousing the American cause.

Etymology

From 'American' + '-ism', patterned after similar terms denoting ideology or allegiance.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A custom, practice, or characteristic that is distinctive or peculiar to the United States or its citizens.

Examples

In the hopes of clearing my family name and in the sincere desire to give my children their fair share of the American way of life, without a blemish on their name and background, I have appeared before this committee and given it all the cooperation in my power.

Etymology

Formed from 'American' + '-ism', denoting a distinctive custom or practice.
