coarse

1. ADJ

/ kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
Lacking refinement or good taste; rude or uncultivated in manners or style.

Examples

These narratives of coarse action and crude language appeared first in local newspapers, as a rule, and later found their way between book covers, though rarely into the planters' libraries beside the morocco bound volumes of Horace, Mr. Addison, Mr. Pope, and Sir Walter Scott.

Synset Examples

he had coarse manners but a first-rate mind
behavior that branded him as common
an untutored and uncouth human being
an uncouth soldier--a real tough guy
appealing to the vulgar taste for violence
the vulgar display of the newly rich

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined - showing or having good taste, manners, or style
  • Synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners, refinement, or grace
  • Synonym: vulgar - lacking sophistication or good taste

Etymology

From Middle English 'cors', from Old French 'cors' (rough, coarse), from Latin 'corsus', variant of 'grossus' (thick, coarse).

2. ADJ

/ kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
Having a rough texture or composed of relatively large particles.

Examples

Jess's coarse features twisted in a surprised grin which was smashed out of shape by Curt's fist.

Synset Examples

coarse meal
coarse sand
a coarse weave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fine - having a smooth texture or consisting of small particles

Etymology

From Middle English cours, from Old French cours, meaning 'ordinary' or 'common'.
