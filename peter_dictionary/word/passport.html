passport

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːspɔːt / (BrE)
an official document issued by a government that certifies a person's identity and citizenship, allowing them to travel abroad and re-enter their home country

Examples

His passport'’s been invalidated, except for return to the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visa - an endorsement on a passport granting permission to enter, leave, or stay for a specified period of time in a country

Etymology

From French 'passeport', from passer (to pass) + port (port, gate).
