antithetical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈθɛt̬.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈθet.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Directly opposed or mutually incompatible; sharply contrasted in character or purpose.

Examples

This is true for two reasons: because Communism is both doctrinally, and in practice, antithetical to these conditions; and because Communists have the will and, as long as Soviet power remains intact, the capacity to prevent their realization.

Synset Examples

practices entirely antithetical to her professed beliefs
hope is antithetic to despair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - completely different; being in direct opposition
  • Synonym: contrary - opposite in nature, direction, or meaning

Etymology

From Greek antithetikos (setting in opposition), from antitithenai 'to set against'.
