stub

1. NOUN

/ stʌb / (AmE), / stʌb / (BrE)
the short section of a tree trunk or stem remaining after a branch or main part has been cut or broken off

Examples

A second and also good practice is to shear off the tops, leaving an inch high stub with just a leaf or two on each branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stump - the part of a tree trunk left projecting from the ground after the rest has fallen or been cut down

Etymology

From Old English *stybb* (stump, stub).
