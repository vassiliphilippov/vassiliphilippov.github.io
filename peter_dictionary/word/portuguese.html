Portuguese

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrtʃəˈɡiz / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃʊˈɡiːz / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken primarily in Portugal and Brazil

Examples

But the firm has recognized the tight dollar and the tourist's desire to visit the "smaller, less traveled and relatively inexpensive countries ", and is now prepared to teach modern Greek and Portuguese through recordings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and much of Latin America
  • Synonym: French - the Romance language spoken in France and other countries

Etymology

From Portuguese *português*, from *Portugal* + *-ês* (suffix forming language and nationality names).
