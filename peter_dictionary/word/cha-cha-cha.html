cha-cha-cha

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑ tʃɑ ˈtʃɑ / (AmE), / tʃɑː tʃɑː ˈtʃɑː / (BrE)
A lively ballroom dance of Latin American origin, characterized by small steps and swaying hip movements, danced to music in 4/4 time with a rhythmic 'cha-cha-cha' pattern.

Examples

That Freddie does a mean cha-cha-cha so watch out later on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a Latin American dance with similar origins
  • Synonym: samba - another type of lively Latin ballroom dance

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish, imitative of the sound of the rhythm played on the güiro instrument.
