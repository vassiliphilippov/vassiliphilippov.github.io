ephemeral

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɛmərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛmərəl / (BrE)
Lasting for a very short time; fleeting or transitory.

Examples

The second timely part of this sketch of literature and the search for identity has to do with the difference between good and enduring literary works and the ephemeral mass culture products of today.

Synset Examples

the ephemeral joys of childhood
a passing fancy
youth's transient beauty
love is transitory but it is eternal
fugacious blossoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeros (lasting only a day).
