confessor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛsər / (AmE), / kənˈfɛsə / (BrE)
A priest who hears confessions and gives absolution.

Examples

He made a point of frowning, of acting out the part of the fond father confessor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: penitent - A person who confesses sins and seeks absolution

Etymology

From Latin 'confessor', meaning a person who confesses or bears witness, later applied to priests hearing confessions.
