prefix

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈpriːfɪks / (BrE)
A letter or group of letters added to the beginning of a word to change its meaning or form a new word.

Examples

The many linguistic techniques for reducing the amount of dictionary information that have been proposed all organize the dictionary's contents around prefixes, stems, suffixes, etc..

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suffix - An affix added to the end of a word

Etymology

From Latin 'praefixum', from 'prae-' (before) + 'figere' (to fasten).
