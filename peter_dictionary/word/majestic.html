majestic

1. ADJ

/ məˈdʒɛstɪk / (AmE), / məˈdʒɛstɪk / (BrE)
Having or displaying great dignity, grandeur, or nobility; impressive and stately.

Examples

The ball went off in a majestic arc, an out-of-bounds slice.

Synset Examples

a gallant pageant
lofty ships
majestic cities
proud alpine peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand - impressive in size or appearance; splendid
  • Synonym: stately - having a dignified, unhurried, and grand manner
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French *majeste* (majesty), from Latin *maiestas* (greatness, dignity).

2. ADJ

/ məˈdʒɛstɪk / (AmE), / məˈdʒɛstɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing great dignity, grandeur, or stateliness; superior to ordinary matters.

Examples

I was having lunch not long ago (apologies to N. V. Peale) with three distinguished historians (one specializing in the European Middle Ages, one in American history, and one in the Far East), and I asked them if they could name instances where the general mores had been radically changed with "deliberate speed, majestic instancy " (Francis Thompson's words for the Hound of Heaven's pursuit) by judicial fiat.

Synset Examples

his majestic presence
olympian detachment
olympian beauty and serene composure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand - magnificent in appearance or style
  • Synonym: regal - suitable for a king or queen; royal or stately
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin 'majesticus', from 'majestas' meaning 'greatness, dignity'.
