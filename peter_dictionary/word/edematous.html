edematous

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈdɛmətəs / (AmE), / ɪˈdiːmətəs / (BrE)
(of tissue) swollen due to an excessive accumulation of fluid.

Examples

The mucosa of the jejunum and ileum showed similar changes, and in some areas the submucosa was edematous and contained considerable numbers of neutrophils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - expanded or enlarged by or as if by fluid

Etymology

From edema + -ous, following the pattern of medical adjectives.
