emboldened

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈboʊldənd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbəʊldənd / (BrE)
Feeling or made more confident and courageous; given the confidence to act boldly.

Examples

There is no evidence that these Milquetoasts became suddenly emboldened when they crossed the threshhold of the master bedroom.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bold - showing courage or confidence
  • Synonym: courageous - having or characterized by courage
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From the verb 'embolden', from 'en-' (cause to) + 'bold'.
