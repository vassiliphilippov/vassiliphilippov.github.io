vinegar

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡər / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡə / (BrE)
a sour-tasting liquid, typically obtained by fermenting wine, cider, or other alcoholic liquids, used as a condiment or food preservative

Examples

A Pennsylvania soldier wrote that "they were the hardest looking set of men that Ever i saw they Looked as if they had been fed on vinegar and shavings ".

Etymology

From Old French 'vinaigre' (sour wine), from 'vin' (wine) + 'aigre' (sour), from Latin 'vinum' (wine) and 'acer' (sour).
