inflect

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈflɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkt / (BrE)
(of a word) to change form according to grammatical rules, such as tense, case, or number.

Examples

Instead of inflecting a verb or using an unattached particle to indicate the past or future, Siddo used an entirely different word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjugate - to give the different forms of a verb according to tense, person, etc.

Etymology

From Latin inflectere (to bend, change).
