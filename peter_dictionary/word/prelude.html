prelude

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛl.jud / (AmE), / ˈprɛl.juːd / (BrE)
An action or event serving as an introduction to something more important; a preliminary or opening event.

Examples

All this, though, is simply a prelude, a curtain-raiser, for what ensues, and I doubt whether any Occidental could accurately forecast it.

Synset Examples

training is a necessary preliminary to employment
drinks were the overture to dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preliminary - something that precedes and prepares for the main event
  • Synonym: overture - an introduction or opening to something more important

Etymology

From Latin 'praeludium', from 'prae-' (before) + 'ludere' (to play).
