motel

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈtɛl / (AmE), / məʊˈtɛl / (BrE)
A roadside hotel designed primarily for motorists, typically having direct access to rooms from the parking area.

Examples

Even the loss of hotel business to the outskirt's motel has been relatively painless; the hotel-motel demarcation is becoming harder to find every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotel - an establishment providing lodging, meals, and other guest services

Etymology

Blend of 'motor' and 'hotel', coined in the US in the 1920s.
