timidity

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈmɪdədi / (AmE), / tɪˈmɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being shy, lacking confidence, or showing fear of the unknown or unfamiliar.

Examples

It was at that party that, finally overcoming my timidity, inspired by tales only half understood and overheard among older boys, I asked Jessie to spend New Year's Eve with me.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one; bravery
  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: boldness - willingness to take risks and act innovatively; confidence

Etymology

From Latin timiditas, from timidus (timid).
