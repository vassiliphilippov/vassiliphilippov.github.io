rephrase

1. VERB

/ riˈfreɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfreɪz / (BrE)
to express something again using different words, especially to make it clearer or easier to understand

Examples

It has been endlessly rephrased, but I may here put it thus: at what point do the tolerant find themselves obliged to become intolerant?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphrase - restate a text or passage in different words

Etymology

From re- (again) + phrase.
