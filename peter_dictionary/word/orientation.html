orientation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of positioning something or someone in a particular direction or relative to the points of a compass; the process of becoming familiar with a new situation or environment.

Examples

We found no obvious effects due to preferred orientation of the crystallites in this sample nor would we expect to on the basis of the shape found from electron microscopic examination.
The supposed tactual sense of spatial location and orientation in the patient and his ability to specify the location of a member, as well as the direction and scope of a movement, passively executed (with one of his members), proved to have been, on the contrary, very considerably affected ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alignment - the arrangement in a straight line or in correct relative positions

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *orientatio* (a positioning to the east), from Latin *orientare* (to orient).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a person's or group's general attitude, beliefs, or preferences towards something, often forming a consistent point of view or approach

Examples

Ceylon has moved from a pro Western orientation to a neutralism openly hostile to the West.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standpoint - a position from which things are considered or judged
  • Synonym: position - a person's point of view or attitude toward something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin orientatio (the action of orienting).
