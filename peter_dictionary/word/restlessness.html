restlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstlɪsnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unable to remain at rest or inactive; constant motion or activity.

Examples

Today's earthquakes are most numerous in belts where the earth's restlessness is presently concentrated, but scars of the past show that there is no part of the earth that has not had them.

Synset Examples

the restlessness of the wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being still, peaceful, or undisturbed

Etymology

From 'restless' + '-ness', with 'restless' from Middle English, combining 'rest' (repose) and '-less' (without).
