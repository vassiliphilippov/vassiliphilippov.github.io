musicianship

1. NOUN

/ mjʊˈzɪʃənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / mjʊˈzɪʃənʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill, artistry, or ability that a person shows in performing or creating music

Examples

Thereafter the audience waxed applause happy, but discriminating operagoers reserved judgment as her singing showed signs of strain, her musicianship some questionable procedure and her acting uncomfortable stylization.
If his scholarship and formal musicianship were not all they might have been, Mercer demonstrated at an early age that he was gifted with a remarkable ear for rhythm and dialect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musicality - sensitivity to, knowledge of, or talent for music
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of ability or skill

Etymology

From 'musician' + '-ship', denoting state or skill.
