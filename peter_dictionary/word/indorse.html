indorse

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to support, approve, or back (an idea, proposal, person, etc.)

Examples

City Finance Director Richard J. McConnell indorsed the higher fees, which, he said, had been under study for more than a year.

Synset Examples

He plumped for the Labor Party
I backed Kennedy in 1960

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorse - to declare public approval or support for

Etymology

Variant of 'endorse', from Latin 'indorsare' (to write on the back).
