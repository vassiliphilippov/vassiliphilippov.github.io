Hegelian

1. ADJ

/ həˈɡeɪliən / (AmE), / hɪˈɡeɪliən / (BrE)
Relating to the German philosopher Hegel or his system of dialectical philosophy.

Examples

National identification was reflected jurisprudentially in law theories which incorporated this Hegelian abstraction and saw law, domestic and international, simply as its formal reflection.
Hidden behind Hegelian abstractions were more practical reasons for a changing jurisprudence.
The theory did not require, though it unfortunately might acquire, a Hegelian mystique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialectical - relating to the art of investigating or discussing the truth of opinions, often associated with Hegel's philosophy

Etymology

From the name of German philosopher Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel (1770–1831) + -ian.
