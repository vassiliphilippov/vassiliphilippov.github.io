involvement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɑlv mənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv mənt / (BrE)
A sense of concern with or curiosity about someone or something; interest or participation.

Examples

Anyone's identification with an international struggle, whether warlike or peaceful, requires absurd oversimplification and intense emotional involvement.

Synset Examples

an interest in music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest - a feeling of curiosity or concern about something

Etymology

From involve + -ment, from Latin *involvere* (to wrap up) plus the suffix *-mentum* indicating result or means.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɑlvmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlvmənt / (BrE)
the state of being included in or connected with something; participation or association.

Examples

Kate tried to contain her curiosity and foreboding at what the letter portended, at what involvement existed for Juanita.

Synset Examples

he escaped involvement in the accident
there was additional involvement of the liver and spleen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participation - the act of taking part in something
  • Antonym: exclusion - the state of being left out or not included

Etymology

From involve + -ment, originating in Late Middle English from Latin 'involvere' (to enfold, entangle).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɑlvmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlvmənt / (BrE)
the act of taking part in or being connected with an activity or group

Examples

Artistic mimesis under Christian influence records the involvement of all persons, however humble, in a divine drama.
I have chosen to use the word "mimesis "in its Christian rather than its classic implications and to discover in the concrete forms of both art and myth powers of theological expression which, as in the Christian mind, are the direct consequence of involvement in historical experience, which are not reserved, as in the Greek mind, only to moments of theoretical reflection.
Foreign policy is the total involvement of the American people with peoples and governments abroad.
Apparently the feeling is that anything more would be involvement in technical abstrusenesses of possible pedantic interest but of no visible significance in practical affairs.

Synset Examples

the teacher tried to increase his students' engagement in class activities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participation - the act of taking part in something
  • Antonym: detachment - the state of being objective or uninvolved

Etymology

From involve + -ment; 'involve' from Latin involvere (to entangle, involve).
