onrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
A sudden, forceful attack or advance, especially by a group or in battle.

Examples

But it is an answer which opens the door wide to an onrush of objections and denials.
Their onrush and retreat, he reported, was accompanied by a great hissing, roaring and rattling.

Synset Examples

the attack began at dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - a sudden violent attack
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing after an attack

Etymology

From 'on' + 'rush', first recorded in the early 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
A sudden, forceful forward movement, rush, or flow, especially of people, things, or natural forces.

Examples

The riotous onrush of industrialism after the War for Southern Independence and the general secular drift to the Religion of Humanity, however, prepared the way for a reception of the French Revolution's socialistic offspring of one sort of another.

Synset Examples

from the bow she stared at the mesmerising onrush of the sea where it split and foamed
the explosion interrupted the wild onrush of her thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - A sudden powerful forward or upward movement, often of liquid
  • Synonym: flood - An overwhelming flow or rush, especially of water

Etymology

From 'on' + 'rush', first recorded in the mid-19th century.
