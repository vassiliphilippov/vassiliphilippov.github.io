strop

1. VERB

/ strɑp / (AmE), / strɒp / (BrE)
to sharpen (a razor or similar blade) by rubbing it on a strop, typically a strip of leather

Examples

He found tepid water in a pitcher and a last bit of soap, and he lathered his face and stood stropping the razor on his broad leather belt, its buckle held firm by a knob of the bedpost.
He stropped the razor slowly; what mattered was that a new concept of Americans was being born.

Synset Examples

strop razors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hone - to sharpen a blade, usually with a stone

Etymology

From strop, the noun; probably from Old English *strop* (band, strap), from Latin *stroppus* (cord, band).
