prowess

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraʊ.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈpraʊ.ɪs / (BrE)
great skill or ability in a particular activity or field, especially as a result of practice and study.

Examples

In fact, insofar as science generates any fear, it stems not so much from scientific prowess and gadgets but from the fact that new unanswered questions arise, which, until they are understood, create uncertainty.

Synset Examples

the art of conversation
it's quite an art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • Antonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability

Etymology

From Old French 'prowesse' (bravery, skill), from 'preu' (valiant, brave).
