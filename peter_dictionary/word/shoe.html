shoe

1. NOUN

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
A covering for the foot, typically made of leather, plastic, or other material, with a flexible upper part and a sole and heel, worn to protect and comfort the foot.

Examples

That is why it is important to invest in comfortable, airy types of shoes.
Stacked heels are also popular on dressy or tailored shoes.
Designed for summer comfort are the shoes illustrated.
In a lacy open weave shoes have a luster finish, braided collar and bow highlight on the squared throat.
"Doctor "Lee asked her to lie down on a bed and remove her shoes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boot - footwear covering the foot and ankle, and often the lower leg
  • Degree Lower: slipper - a light, low shoe for indoor wear

Etymology

From Old English *sceōh*, from Proto-Germanic *skōhaz*, referring to a covering for the foot.
