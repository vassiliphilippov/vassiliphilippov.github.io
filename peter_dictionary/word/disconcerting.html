disconcerting

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskənˈsɜrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈsɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing one to feel unsettled, confused, or disturbed.

Examples

Her entrance in Scene 2, Act 1, brought some disconcerting applause even before she had sung a note.
What had been an unmanageably powerful introject was now, despite its continuing charge of energy disconcerting to me, sufficiently within control of her ego that she could use it to show me what this introjected mother was like.

Synset Examples

his disconcerting habit of greeting friends ferociously and strangers charmingly
an upsetting experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsettling - causing anxiety or unease
  • Synonym: upsetting - causing distress or worry
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried

Etymology

From the verb 'disconcert' + -ing. 'Disconcert' from French 'déconcerter', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'concertare' (to bring together, contend).
