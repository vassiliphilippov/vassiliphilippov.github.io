vitamin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.tə.mɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɪ.tə.mɪn / (BrE)
Any of a group of organic substances that are essential in small quantities for normal growth and metabolism and must be obtained from the diet, as they are not synthesized in sufficient amounts by the body.

Examples

Aside from this, the average portion contains some protein, an appreciable amount of vitamins and C - about one-tenth of the minimum daily requirement, and about a third of the official vitamin E requirement.
A Schilling test demonstrated normal absorption of vitamin * * f.
This article assumes that the rations you are feeding your beef, dairy cattle, and sheep are adequately balanced with protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutrient - substance that provides nourishment essential for growth and life
  • Synonym: mineral - inorganic substance essential for health
  • Synonym: protein - class of essential nutrients made of amino acids

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) + 'amine', originally thought to be amines essential for life.
