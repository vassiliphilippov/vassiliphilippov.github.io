venerable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛnərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛnərəbəl / (BrE)
Accorded a great deal of respect, especially because of age, wisdom, or character.

Examples

There is an ancient and venerable tradition in the church (which derives, however, from the heritage of the Greeks rather than from the Bible) that God is completely independent of his creation and so has no need of men for accomplishing his work in the world.

Synset Examples

revered holy men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: august - respected and impressive; revered
  • Synonym: revered - deeply respected or admired
  • Antonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable in character or appearance

Etymology

From Latin venerabilis (worthy of reverence), from venerari (to revere).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛn(ə)rəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛn(ə)rəbəl / (BrE)
deserving of respect, especially because of age, character, or wisdom

Examples

I called the other afternoon on my old friend, Graves Moreland, the Anglo-American literary critic - his mother was born in Ohio - who lives alone in a fairy-tale cottage on the Upson Downs, raising hell and peacocks, the former only when the venerable gentleman becomes an angry old man about the state of literature or something else that is dwindling and diminishing, such as human stature, hope, and humor.

Synset Examples

a venerable sage with white hair and beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revered - deeply respected or admired
  • Synonym: respected - regarded with respect; esteemed
  • Antonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable; having a bad reputation

Etymology

From Latin venerabilis, from venerari meaning 'to revere, worship'.
