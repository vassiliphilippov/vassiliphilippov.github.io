preoccupy

1. VERB

/ priˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ / (AmE), / priˈɒkjəˌpaɪ / (BrE)
(of a thought or activity) to dominate or engross the mind of (someone) to the extent of excluding other concerns.

Examples

The husband is usually a well-educated professional, preoccupied with his job - often an organization man whose motto for getting ahead is: "Do n't rock the boat ".

Synset Examples

His work preoccupies him
The matter preoccupies her completely--she cannot think of anything else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorb - to hold fully the attention or interest of someone
  • Antonym: distract - to divert attention from something; to prevent focus

Etymology

From Latin praeoccupare, meaning 'to seize beforehand.'
