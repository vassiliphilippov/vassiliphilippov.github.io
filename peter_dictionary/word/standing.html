standing

1. ADJ

/ ˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstandɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an object) having a base or support that enables it to stand upright on its own.

Examples

As strength improves start in a standing position with legs wide apart and upper body bent forward.
Return to the standing position.

Synset Examples

a standing lamp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixed - firmly in position; stationary

2. NOUN

/ ˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstandɪŋ / (BrE)
the status, reputation, or rank of a person or organization within a social, financial, or professional context

Examples

This anomalous status of the educator cannot fail to lower his standing in the eyes of the students.
At first glance, not much of a motive for a man of his standing; but for all his air of affluence, who could tell what his private financial picture was?
It looked to me as though he had everything an artist could want, joy in his work, standing in the profession, a large and steady income.

Synset Examples

of equal standing
a member in good standing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reputation - the beliefs or opinions generally held about someone or something
  • Synonym: status - a person's social or professional position in relation to others

Etymology

From Old English 'standan' (to stand), via the participle 'standing'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a rule, order, invitation, etc.) continuing or permanent; not created for a specific occasion.

Examples

After all, the henpecked husband with his shrewish wife is a comic figure of long standing, in literature and on the stage, as Dr. Schillinger points out.

Synset Examples

a standing committee

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent
