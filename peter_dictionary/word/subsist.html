subsist

1. VERB

/ səbˈsɪst / (AmE), / səbˈsɪst / (BrE)
to maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level; to continue to exist, often on limited resources

Examples

A pitiful few of them are left now, to subsist mainly on the tourist trade and to sing their ancient tribal chants, which have the same haunting sadness as the laments of the American Indians.

Synset Examples

he could barely exist on such a low wage
Can you live on $2000 a month in New York City?
Many people in the world have to subsist on $1 a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survive - continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship
  • Synonym: live - to remain alive; to continue to have life

Etymology

From Latin 'subsistere' (to stand firm, exist, remain).
