unparalleled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpærəˌlɛld / (AmE), / ʌnˈparəlɛld / (BrE)
Having no equal; unique in quality, extent, or degree.

Examples

The attempt to enforce that belief ushered in a reign of bootleggers, racketeers, hijackers and gangsters that led to a breakdown of law unparalleled in our history.

Synset Examples

he is alone in the field of microbiology
this theory is altogether alone in its penetration of the problem
Bach was unique in his handling of counterpoint
craftsmen whose skill is unequaled
unparalleled athletic ability
a breakdown of law unparalleled in our history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unequaled - not matched or surpassed; having no equal
  • Synonym: unique - being the only one of its kind; unlike anything else
  • Synonym: peerless - without equal; unrivaled
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From un- (not) + parallel, after French 'imparallèle' or formed in English in the early 17th century.
