prise

1. VERB

/ praɪz / (AmE), / praɪz / (BrE)
To use force or leverage in order to open or move something, especially by using a tool.

Examples

Now he was certain: the lock had not yielded to Muller's collection of keys; fresh scars showed that the door had been prized open.

Synset Examples

The burglar jimmied the lock
Raccoons managed to pry the lid off the garbage pail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pry - to move or force open using leverage (especially with a lever or crowbar)

Etymology

From Middle French *preisier* (to press or force), influenced by Old French *prisier* (to value).
