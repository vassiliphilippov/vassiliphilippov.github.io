buffet

1. NOUN

/ bəˈfeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfɪt / (BrE)
a sideboard or cabinet in a dining room, with shelves and drawers for holding dishes, silverware, and table linens.

Examples

Her quarters were on the right as you walked into the building, and her small front room was clogged with heavy furniture - a big, round, oak dining table and chairs, a buffet, with a row of unclaimed letters inserted between the mirror and its frame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideboard - a piece of dining room furniture with cupboards and drawers for storing tableware

Etymology

From French 'buffet' (sideboard, small table).

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌf.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌf.ɪt / (BrE)
(of winds, waves, or other forces) to strike repeatedly and with force; to batter or pummel.

Examples

Now big forces buffet food.
Buffeted by swirling winds, the little green biplane struggled northward between the mountains beyond Northfield Gulf.
He mumbled at her but let himself be led off inside the house, shuffling mightily to make it clear how weak and aged he was and how he was buffeted about by those who still had their wicked strength.

Synset Examples

Winds buffeted the tent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - to strike repeatedly with hard blows
  • Synonym: pummel - to strike repeatedly, typically with fists

Etymology

From Old French *bufet* (a blow), diminutive of *bufe* (a blow), of imitative origin.
