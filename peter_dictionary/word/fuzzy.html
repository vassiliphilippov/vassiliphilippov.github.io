fuzzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌzi / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi / (BrE)
Covered with fine, soft, light hairs or fibers.

Examples

He thought he saw - it awakened and, for a moment, interested him - that Elizabeth held a leash in her hand and that a round fuzzy puppy was on the end of the leash.
Fuzzy caterpillars, snails with their sensitive horns, struggling grasshoppers held by their long hind legs and commanded to "spit tobacco, spit ".
What say there, Fuzzy Britches?

Synset Examples

his head fuzzed like a dandelion gone to seed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairy - covered with hair
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not hairy or rough

Etymology

Probably from 'fuzz' (short, soft fibers) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌzi / (AmE), / ˈfʌzi / (BrE)
(of an image, outline, or shape) indistinct or blurred in appearance; not clear or sharp.

Examples

Of course, there must be clarity: a single distinct impression is more valuable than many fuzzy ones.

Synset Examples

a landscape of blurred outlines
the trees were just blurry shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurry - lacking clarity or distinctness; blurred
  • Antonym: sharp - distinct and clear in outline or image

Etymology

Probably from 'fuzz' (fine fibers or fluff) + '-y'.
