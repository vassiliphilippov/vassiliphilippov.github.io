anachronistically

1. ADV

/ əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪkli / (AmE), / əˌnækrəˈnɪstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is out of its proper time period; in an anachronistic manner.

Examples

In Plato's Republic communism is - to speak anachronistically - a communism of Janissaries.

Synset Examples

let's look at this phenomenon anachronistically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anachronistic - belonging to a period other than that being portrayed

Etymology

From 'anachronistic' + '-ally'; ultimately from Greek 'ana-' (against) + 'chronos' (time).
