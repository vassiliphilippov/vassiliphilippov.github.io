connoisseur

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.nəˈsɜr / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.əˈsɜː / (BrE)
An expert judge in matters of taste, especially in the fine arts, food, or drink.

Examples

One part of her audience was totally engaged, the connoisseur witnessing a peculiarly fine performance of some ancient classic, the other part, the guest of the connoisseur, attentive as one who must take an intelligent interest in that which he does not fully understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - person with extensive knowledge or skill in a field

Etymology

From French 'connaisseur', from Old French 'conoisseor' (one who knows), from Latin 'cognoscere' (to know).
