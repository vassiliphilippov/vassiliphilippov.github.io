candidly

1. ADV

/ ˈkændɪdli / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that is honest and straightforward; frankly.

Examples

Quite candidly, fellows, I would n't be in your shoes for all the rice in China.

Synset Examples

honestly, I don't believe it
candidly, I think she doesn't have a conscience
frankly, my dear, I don't give a damn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankly - in a frank or direct manner
  • Synonym: honestly - in a truthful or sincere way

Etymology

From candid + -ly; 'candid' from Latin candidus (white, pure, honest).
