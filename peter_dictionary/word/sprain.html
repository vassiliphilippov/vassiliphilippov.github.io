sprain

1. VERB

/ spreɪn / (AmE), / spreɪn / (BrE)
to injure a joint by suddenly twisting it, causing damage to the ligaments without dislocating the bone

Examples

Halfback Bud Priddy, slowed for almost a month by a slowly mending sprained ankle, joined TCU's workout Monday.

Synset Examples

wrench one's ankle
The wrestler twisted his shoulder
the hikers sprained their ankles when they fell
I turned my ankle and couldn't walk for several days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - to injure a muscle or tendon by overstretching

2. NOUN

/ spreɪn / (AmE), / spreɪn / (BrE)
an injury to a joint in which the ligaments are stretched or torn, typically caused by a sudden twist or wrench, resulting in pain and swelling

Examples

There was young Master Bruce... ...leading his gray pony with me on its back... ...like a sack of old potatoes, covered in mud and nursing a sprained ankle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strain - an injury to muscle or tendon from overstretching

Etymology

Of unknown origin; first attested in the mid 16th century.
