perennial

1. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛniəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəl / (BrE)
(of a plant) living for several years; lasting or existing for a long or apparently infinite time, enduring or continually recurring.

Examples

If the content of faith is to be presented today in a form that can be "understanded of the people "- and this, it must not be forgotten, is one of the goals of the perennial theological task - there is no other choice but to abandon completely a mythological manner of representation.
"My beloved ward, my perennial gadfly ", said the whining voice.

Synset Examples

the common buttercup is a popular perennial plant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everlasting - lasting forever or for a very long time
  • Antonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
  • Antonym: annual - (of a plant) living for only one year or season
  • Degree Lower: biennial - (of a plant) living for two years or seasons

Etymology

From Latin *perennis* (lasting through the year; perpetual), from *per-* (through) + *annus* (year).
