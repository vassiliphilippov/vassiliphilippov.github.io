generalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of forming general principles or conclusions from specific instances or facts.

Examples

It is possible to make a few generalizations about the six giants themselves.
The results are too varied to allow generalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction - reasoning from specific cases to general rules
  • Antonym: deduction - reasoning from general principles to specific cases

Etymology

From general + -ization; modeled on French généralisation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of forming a general concept or principle by identifying common features among specific instances.

Examples

Still, we must remember that we cannot construct and justify generalizations of this sort unless we are ready to consider many special instances of influence moving between such areas as theology, philosophy, political thought, and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstraction - the process of considering something independently of its associations

Etymology

From general +‎ -ization; ultimately from Latin generalis (general).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement, idea, or principle that applies broadly rather than to specific cases.

Examples

Swadesh, and therefore Hoijer, felt compelled to omit all terms denoting species or even genera (fox, vulture, salmon, yellow pine, manzanita); their classes of animal and plant terms are restricted to generalizations or recurrent parts (fish, bird, tree, grass, horn, tail, bark, root).

Synset Examples

he spoke in broad generalities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: particularization - making something specific; the process of making general terms particular

Etymology

From general + -ization, modeled after French 'généralisation'.
