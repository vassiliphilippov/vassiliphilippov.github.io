rove

1. VERB

/ roʊv / (AmE), / rəʊv / (BrE)
to travel or move about aimlessly or without a fixed destination, often in search of something

Examples

However, a boy's lively eyes might rove.
Here, this happy, roving son of good fortune proved that he could accept the disciplines of a new social economic order fighting for its very existence and ideals in a truculent world.

Synset Examples

The gypsies roamed the woods
roving vagabonds
the wandering Jew
The cattle roam across the prairie
the laborers drift from one town to the next
They rolled from town to town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wander - to move from place to place without a fixed route or destination
  • Synonym: roam - to travel randomly or aimlessly, especially over a wide area

Etymology

Probably from Middle English roven, from Middle Dutch roven (to rob, wander).
