outstrip

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈstrɪp / (AmE), / aʊtˈstrɪp / (BrE)
to be or do something to a greater degree than someone or something else; to surpass or exceed

Examples

Competitors came to receive higher percentage of General Motors business in later years, but it is "likely "that this trend stemmed "at least in part "from the needs of General Motors outstripping du Pont's capacity.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surpass - to do or be better than; to go beyond
  • Synonym: exceed - to be greater in number or degree

Etymology

From out- (beyond) + strip (to move quickly), originally meaning to run faster than another.
