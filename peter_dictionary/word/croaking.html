croaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
A harsh, hoarse sound, especially the characteristic utterance of a frog or similar animal.

Examples

The ungainly bird thing ran away, and to David its croaking sounded like the crowing of a tormented rooster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croak - the characteristic harsh sound made by a frog or similar animal
  • Synonym: cawing - loud, harsh cry of a crow or similar bird

Etymology

From the verb 'croak', imitative in origin, representing a harsh sound.
