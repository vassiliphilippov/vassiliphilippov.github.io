careless

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛrləs / (AmE), / ˈkeələs / (BrE)
Not giving enough attention or thought to avoiding harm or mistakes; lacking attention, thoroughness, or consideration.

Examples

The careless writing was in keeping with his mood of savage discontent.
In the written language then can be underlined or italicized to guide the reader here, but much of the time the written language simply depends on the reader's alertness, and a careless reader will have to back up and reread.
Women and children can be careless, but not men.

Synset Examples

careless about her clothes
forgotten by some careless person
a careless housekeeper
careless proofreading
it was a careless mistake
hurt by a careless remark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: careful - giving attention and thought to avoiding harm or mistakes

Etymology

From Middle English 'careles', from 'care' + '-less', meaning 'without care'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɛr.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.ləs / (BrE)
(of movement, style, etc.) appearing effortless or unstudied; graceful in a way that seems natural and unconstrained.

Examples

In a bold, sometimes careless, form there is nothing academic; all is seen and felt and experienced, the observation is sharp and the imagination lively.

Synset Examples

an impression of careless elegance
danced with careless grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graceful - having or showing grace or elegance
  • Antonym: studied - deliberate and planned rather than spontaneous or natural

Etymology

From care + -less, originally meaning 'free from care' and later developing extended senses.
