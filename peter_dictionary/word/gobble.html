gobble

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɑb.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒb.əl / (BrE)
(of a turkey) to make a rapid gurgling or bubbling sound in the throat, characteristic of turkeys.

Examples

He stroked the hens and they responded with delighted clucks, he gobbled with the turkeys and they at once were all attention, he quacked with the ducks, and cackled with a pair of exceedingly flattered geese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - make a sharp, repeated sound (as a hen or goose)

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbəl / (BrE)
To eat quickly and in large mouthfuls, often without proper chewing.

Examples

And this is only natural - witness the haste with which modern man gobbles the latest "wonder drug ".

Synset Examples

Don't bolt your food!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devour - to eat hungrily or quickly
  • Antonym: nibble - to eat with small bites

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound made when eating greedily.
