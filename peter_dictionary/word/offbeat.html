offbeat

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔfˌbit / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌbiːt / (BrE)
Unusual or unconventional; not conforming to the ordinary or expected pattern.

Examples

The action centers about a group of outspoken and offbeat students sitting around a table in a cafeteria and their collective and ultimately fruitless search for a cup of hot coffee.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional forms or rules
  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange
  • Synonym: unorthodox - contrary to what is usual or accepted

Etymology

From 'off' (away from) + 'beat' (rhythm or regular movement), originally used in music for the weak or unaccented beat.
