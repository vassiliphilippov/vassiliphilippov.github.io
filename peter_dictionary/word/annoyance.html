annoyance

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɔɪəns / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪəns / (BrE)
A feeling of slight anger or irritation; the state of being annoyed.

Examples

Prickly twinges of annoyance ran through him.
One hebephrenic woman often became submerged in what felt to me like a somehow phony experience of pseudo emotion, during which, despite her wracking sobs and streaming cheeks, I felt only a cold annoyance with her.
At home he had been a clean orderly man, and now he had to hide his annoyance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - the state of feeling annoyed, impatient, or slightly angry
  • Degree Higher: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Degree Lower: calm - a state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Old French 'anoier' (to annoy), from Late Latin 'inodiare' (to make loathsome).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɔɪ.əns / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪ.əns / (BrE)
A feeling of slight anger or irritation caused by something perceived as bothersome or disturbing.

Examples

For another second or two he gave in to the annoyance that was directed at himself; then his mind moved on to be confronted by something far more serious, and as the thought expanded, the implications jarred him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - a feeling of being annoyed, irritated, or slightly angry
  • Degree Higher: anger - strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being tranquil or peaceful, free from agitation

Etymology

From Old French enoiance (vexation), from enoier (to annoy), from Latin inodiare (to make odious).
