diagnostician

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæɡ.nɑːˈstɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / daɪˌɒɡ.nɒˈstɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A doctor or medical professional who specializes in identifying diseases and disorders by examining symptoms and test results.

Examples

Only a radical change in the nature of the population in the central city would be likely to destroy this preference - and we must now turn our attention to the question of whether such a change, gloomily foreseen by so many urban diagnosticians, is actually upon us.

Etymology

From French *diagnosticien*, from Greek *diagnōsis* (discernment, distinguishing).
