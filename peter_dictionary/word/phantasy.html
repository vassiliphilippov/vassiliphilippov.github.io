phantasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
A belief held by many people that is false; an illusion or mistaken impression.

Examples

Some who have written on Utopia have treated it as "a learned diversion of a learned world ", "a phantasy with which More amused himself ", "a holiday work, a spontaneous overflow of intellectual high spirits, a revel of debate, paradox, comedy and invention ".

Synset Examples

they have the illusion that I am very wealthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief
  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression, especially in the face of contrary evidence
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

Alternative spelling of 'fantasy', from Greek 'phantasia' (appearance, imagination) via Latin and Old French.
