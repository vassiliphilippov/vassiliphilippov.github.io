gnash

1. VERB

/ næʃ / (AmE), / naʃ / (BrE)
to grind or strike together, especially of teeth, typically as a sign of anger or pain

Examples

A gnawing and gnashing within him.
Through the splash of the rising waters, they could hear the roar of the river as it raged through its canyon, gnashing big chunks out of the banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - reduce to small particles or powder by crushing; also to rub together harshly

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, possibly influenced by Middle English *gnasten* or Old Norse *gnastan* (to gnash the teeth).
