magpie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.pʌɪ / (BrE)
A long-tailed bird of the crow family with black-and-white plumage, known for its raucous chattering call.

Examples

There have been cooing doves, chattering magpies, thieving jackdaws, a proud peacock, a silly goose, and a harpy eagle - whom I was silly enough to mate with and who is now busy tearing at my vitals ".

Etymology

From Mag (nickname for Margaret) + pie (archaic term for magpie, from Latin pica).
