sabotage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæbətɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈsæbətɑːʒ / (BrE)
the deliberate destruction, damage, or obstruction of something, especially for political or military advantage

Examples

Biological agents are, moreover, suitable for delivery through enemy sabotage which imposes many problems in defense.
This is an enormous industry that is subject to sabotage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vandalism - deliberate destruction of or damage to property
  • Synonym: subversion - undermining the power and authority of an established system

Etymology

From French sabotage, from saboter (to sabotage), originally meaning to clatter with sabots (wooden shoes) and later to act clumsily, then to deliberately destroy.
