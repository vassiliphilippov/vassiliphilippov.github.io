liable

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(often followed by 'to') Likely to experience or suffer from (something, especially something undesirable)

Examples

Huge industries are involved also in the production of biological products, drugs and cosmetics which are liable to this type of attack.
What's most important to me... ...is that I have a guarantee.

Synset Examples

liable to diabetes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prone - Likely to suffer from or experience a particular problem
  • Synonym: susceptible - Likely to be influenced or affected by a particular thing
  • Antonym: immune - Protected or exempt, especially from something negative

Etymology

From Old French *liable*, from lier 'to bind', from Latin *ligare* 'to bind'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
legally responsible or subject to legal action or penalty

Examples

In the remainder of his speech Davis spoke of his admiration for Brown and warned those who took part in the meeting that they "are liable to the charge that they are supporting traitors and upholding men whom the laws have condemned ".

Synset Examples

liable to criminal charges

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immune - not subject to legal action or obligation; protected from liability
  • Synonym: accountable - required or expected to justify actions or decisions; responsible

Etymology

From Old French *liable*, from Latin *ligare* (to bind).

3. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
likely or prone to experience or suffer from something, especially something unpleasant or unwelcome.

Examples

Younger men usually do n't think of pensions as an important job benefit factor anyhow and they 're liable to change jobs several times before settling down.
A seeping coldness entered Holden's being; his nerves seemed frost-bitten down to the tips of his tingling fingers and his spine felt stiff and glass like, liable to break like an icicle at any moment.
At which time, Mr. Corleone, you may very well be subject to indictment for perjury.

Synset Examples

he is apt to lose
she is liable to forget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prone - likely or liable to suffer from or experience something, especially something unwelcome
  • Synonym: apt - having a tendency to do something; likely to
  • Antonym: immune - not likely or liable to be affected or harmed by something

Etymology

From Old French *liable* (bound), from Latin *ligare* (to bind).
