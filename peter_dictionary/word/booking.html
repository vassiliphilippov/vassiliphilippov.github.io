booking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
an arrangement to reserve a place, ticket, or the services of someone in advance

Examples

Remember how BY a series of booking absurdities Chicago missed seeing the Bolshoi Ballet?

Synset Examples

wondered who had made the booking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reservation - the act of reserving accommodation, a seat, etc. in advance

Etymology

From the verb 'book' (to reserve), originally from Old English 'bōc' (book), referring to entering something in a book or register.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
an engagement for performers or a performing group to appear or work for a limited period of time

Examples

Later the Hurok office made it Dec. 8 thru 17, a nice, long booking for the full repertory.
Allied Arts then notified us that the Kirov would cut short its Los Angeles booking, fly here to open Nov. 28, and close Dec. 2.
Shorter booking, but still a booking.
Shorter booking, but still a booking.
Then why not the juicy booking Hurok had held for us?

Synset Examples

the play had bookings throughout the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engagement - an arrangement to do something or go somewhere at a fixed time; here, especially for a performer

Etymology

From 'book' (to arrange or reserve), probably influenced by the practice of entering scheduled performances in a bookings ledger.
