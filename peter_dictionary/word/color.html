2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
The quality of an object or substance with respect to light reflected by it, usually described in terms of hue, lightness, and saturation.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hue - a particular shade or tint of a given color
  • Synonym: shade - a quality of a color that differs mainly in lightness or darkness
  • Antonym: monochrome - an image, object, or display rendered in shades of a single color

Etymology

From Old French *colour*, from Latin *color* (meaning 'color, hue').
