questioning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛs.tʃən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of asking questions, especially formally or officially to obtain information

Examples

It places certain values at least beyond questioning and tampering.
Rumors of the offer Tom Horn had made at the Stockgrowers' Association meeting had leaked out by then, and as a grand jury investigation of the murder got underway, the prosecuting attorney, a Colonel Baird, ordered that the tall stock detective be summoned for questioning.
Once again, Tom Horn was the first and most likely suspect, and he was brought in for questioning immediately.
"Well ", Heiser ventured, "why do n't we hold an investigation with questioning and "-

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogation - the act of questioning someone thoroughly, often by authority

Etymology

From the verb 'question' (Middle English: from Old French 'question', from Latin 'quaestio', meaning 'a seeking, inquiry, examination').

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɛstʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɛstʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
showing or expressing doubt, curiosity, or puzzlement, particularly as if unsure or seeking an explanation.

Examples

Rev's face was suddenly a little fixed and questioning.

Synset Examples

he had a quizzical expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quizzical - expressing puzzlement or mild amusement
  • Synonym: curious - eager to know or learn something
