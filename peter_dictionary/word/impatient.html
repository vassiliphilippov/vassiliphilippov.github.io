impatient

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
Easily annoyed by delays or unwilling to wait for something or someone.

Examples

So they parted when she was in an impatient humor.
The two little bangs meant that he was getting impatient to have a crowd of customers waited on and that if he had to he would jerk open the door and drag out, by the opposite door handle which she would be clutching, whichever-the-hell clerk it was who thought she could waste so much store time on the pot.

Synset Examples

impatient with the slower students
impatient of criticism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patient - able to accept or tolerate delays without getting angry or upset

Etymology

From Latin 'impatientem', from 'in-' (not) + 'patientem' (bearing, suffering).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃnt / (BrE)
eager for something to happen, especially so that one finds it hard to wait; full of eagerness (usually followed by 'to').

Examples

There are hordes of nubile young women there who, prodded by their impatient mothers, are determined to marry.
The bands were impatient, but they were the only ones.
I can't wait to hear the explanation.

Synset Examples

impatient to begin
raring to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - wanting to do or have something very much
  • Antonym: patient - able to wait calmly for something or endure

Etymology

From Latin 'impatientem' (not able to endure or remain calm).
