torture

1. VERB

/ ˈtɔrtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
To cause intense emotional or mental suffering to someone; to torment emotionally or mentally.

Examples

It is into this household, one eroded by irritations that have tortured the souls out of its people, that George Dillon enters at the beginning of the play.
It slips away, it burns and tortures me.
Even today range riders will come upon mummified bodies of men who attempted nothing more difficult than a twenty-mile hike and slowly lost direction, were tortured by the heat, driven mad by the constant and unfulfilled promise of the landscape, and who finally died.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe suffering of mind or body
  • Antonym: comfort - to soothe or relieve from pain or distress

Etymology

From Latin 'tortura', from 'torquere' (to twist, torment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
Severe mental or emotional anguish; extreme psychological suffering.

Examples

He and Mary would no longer have to tear and rend at each other; their mutual torture would be over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anguish - severe mental or physical pain or suffering
  • Synonym: misery - a state of great distress or discomfort of mind or body
  • Antonym: bliss - perfect happiness; great joy (opposite of mental anguish)

Etymology

From Latin tortura (twisting, torment), from torquere (to twist).

3. VERB

/ ˈtɔrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
to inflict severe physical or mental pain on someone, especially to force information, punish, or for cruelty

Examples

But when a board of inquiry was called to look into the charges of cowardice made against him, the men who had seen Reno leave the battlefield and the officer who had heard Reno suggest that the wounded be left to be tortured by the Sioux, refused to say a harsh word against him.

Synset Examples

The sinners will be tormented in Hell, according to the Bible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to inflict severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Latin 'torquere' (to twist), via Old French 'torture'.
