ginmill

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪnˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪnˌmɪl / (BrE)
(informal) A bar or tavern where alcoholic drinks are served, especially one considered cheap or disreputable.

Examples

We moved down Broadway from ginmill to ginmill.
We moved down Broadway from ginmill to ginmill.

Synset Examples

he drowned his sorrows in whiskey at the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barroom - a room where alcoholic drinks are served over a counter
  • Synonym: pub - public house; a bar or tavern

Etymology

Blend of 'gin' (alcoholic drink) and 'mill' (place of production or activity), likely originally referring to places where gin was made or served.
