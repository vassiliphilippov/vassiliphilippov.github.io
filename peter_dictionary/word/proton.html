proton

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tɒn / (BrE)
A stable subatomic particle with a positive electric charge equal in magnitude to that of an electron, found in the nucleus of atoms.

Examples

Further, the corpuscular radiation, i.e., the solar wind protons, must sputter away the surface atoms of the dust and cause a slow diminution in size, with a resultant increase in both the Poynting-Robertson effect and the ratio of the repulsive force to the gravitational force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutron - an uncharged subatomic particle found in the atomic nucleus
  • Antonym: electron - a subatomic particle with a negative charge

Etymology

From Greek 'prōtos' (first), reflecting its central role in atomic structure.
