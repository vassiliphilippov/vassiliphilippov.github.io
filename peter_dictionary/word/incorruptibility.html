incorruptibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəˌrʌptəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˌrʌptəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being incapable of corruption, decay, or moral corruption; incorruptness.

Examples

They speak of the work of Christ as the bestowal of incorruptibility, which can mean (though it does not have to mean) deliverance from time and history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corruptibility - the quality of being corruptible
  • Synonym: incorruptible - incapable of corruption; not corruptible

Etymology

From Latin 'incorruptibilis' (not corruptible), from 'in-' (not) + 'corruptibilis' (corruptible).
