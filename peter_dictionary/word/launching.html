launching

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔn(t)ʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving a newly built ship or vessel into the water for the first time

Examples

A news item described the launching of a ship: "Completing the ceremony, the beautiful movie star smashed a bottle of champagne over her stern as she slid gracefully down the ways into the sea ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to set a newly built vessel afloat for the first time

Etymology

From the verb 'launch', which comes from Old French 'lancher', from Late Latin 'lanceare' (to wield a lance).
