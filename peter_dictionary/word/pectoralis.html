pectoralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛktəˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛktəˈrælɪs / (BrE)
either of the two large muscles located on each side of the human chest, responsible for movement of the shoulder joint

Examples

These changes were most marked in the gastrocnemius and biceps and less evident in the pectoralis, diaphragm, and transversus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pectoralis major - the larger, uppermost chest muscle
  • Degree Lower: pectoralis minor - the smaller, deeper chest muscle beneath the major

Etymology

From Latin pectoralis, meaning 'of the chest', from pectus (chest).
