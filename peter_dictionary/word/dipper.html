dipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
A large ladle with a cup-shaped bowl and a long handle, used for dipping liquids.

Examples

He unlashed the dipper and drew water from a barrel.
He replenished the dipper and handed it to his young wife riding the hurricane deck.
She set the dipper on the edge of the deck, leaving it for him to stretch after it while she looked on scornfully.
He stared at the dipper, turning it over and over in his wide, calloused hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladle - a large, deep spoon with a long handle, used for serving soup or other liquids

Etymology

From Middle English dipper, from dip (to immerse).
