interconnect

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to connect or be connected with each other so that things or parts function together or are woven together

Examples

Subgroups of various types, interconnected by relational norms.

Synset Examples

The bones are interconnected via the muscle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connect - to join or link together

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'connect' (join together).
