narcotize

1. VERB

/ ˈnɑrkəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkətaɪz / (BrE)
to administer a narcotic drug to; to cause to become affected with or as if with narcotics

Examples

In moderate doses, alcohol narcotizes the appestat and enhances appetite (the original reason for the cocktail); but because liquor has a high caloric value - 100 calories per oz. - the heavy drinker is seldom hungry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedate - to calm or induce sleep by administering a sedative
  • Antonym: stimulate - to encourage activity or increased function (opposite effect)

Etymology

From French 'narcotiser', from 'narcotique' (narcotic), ultimately from Greek 'narkōtikos' (making numb).
