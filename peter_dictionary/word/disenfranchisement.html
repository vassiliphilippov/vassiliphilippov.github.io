disenfranchisement

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz.mənt / (BrE)
the act of depriving a person, group, or entity of a right, privilege, or franchise, especially the right to vote or operate a business

Examples

Du Pont, he said, had proposed disenfranchisement of its General Motors stock along with other restrictions on the du Pont-General Motors relationship.
The submission of detailed plans would place the issues before the court more readily than would discussion of divestiture or disenfranchisement in the abstract.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enfranchisement - the act of granting rights or privileges, especially the right to vote or operate a business

Etymology

From disenfranchise + -ment; 'franchise' from Old French, ultimately from Medieval Latin 'franchisia'.
