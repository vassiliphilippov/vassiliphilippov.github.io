chill

1. VERB

/ tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
To make someone feel suddenly afraid, uneasy, or discouraged; to depress the spirit or mood.

Examples

The gentle whir of each footstep left him more naked than before, until he felt his unprotected flesh tremble, chilled by each new sound.
Instantly, he chilled.

Synset Examples

The news of the city's surrender chilled the soldiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: hearten - to make someone feel more cheerful or encouraged

Etymology

From Old English 'ciele' (cold, chill), related to Old Norse 'kylja' (to chill).

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
A sudden feeling of coldness, often accompanied by shivering, that typically marks the onset of an illness, especially with fever.

Examples

He loathed the idea of drying up swamps or the chills of malaria or of leaving his natural birthright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiver - an instance of trembling, often due to cold or fear

3. NOUN

/ tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
A feeling or quality of coldness, especially from the environment; a moderate but penetrating cold.

Examples

It would take the chill from the shelter in the beginning.
Beaten with fear and sound and wet and chill, they crawled to the hurricane deck and looked out haggardly at a world of water that reached clear to the surrounding hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - a low temperature, especially in the atmosphere; the opposite of heat
  • Antonym: warmth - the quality, state, or sensation of being warm; moderate heat
  • Degree Higher: frost - a thin layer of ice that forms on surfaces when the temperature drops below freezing

Etymology

From Old English 'ciele' (cold, chilliness), related to 'ceald' (cold).

4. NOUN

/ tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
A sudden brief feeling of fear, excitement, or goosebumps, often experienced as a shiver.

Examples

"We 've got to eat ", Sarah said, curtly, because a chill crawled over her again.
You laughed and then your chest swelled and you felt you could cry for a little bit, and then a feeling hit you like a chill in your stomach and the goose bumps rippled along your arm.

Synset Examples

a frisson of surprise shot through him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frisson - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or fear; shiver; thrill
