lease

1. NOUN

/ lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
a piece of property or real estate that is leased or rented out

Examples

A big-league municipal stadium at Flushing Meadow Park is in the works, and once the lease is signed the local club will be formally recognized by Commissioner Ford C. Frick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rental - property or item that is rented

Etymology

From Old French 'lais', from laisser (to let, leave), and Medieval Latin 'laxa', meaning 'a letting to farm, lease'.

2. NOUN

/ lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
a legal contract by which one party conveys property, land, services, etc., to another for a specified time, usually in return for periodic payment

Examples

acquire secret processes, technical data, inventions, patent applications, patents, licenses, land and interests in land (including water rights), plants and facilities, and other property or rights by purchase, license, lease, or donation;

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rental agreement - a contract outlining terms under which one party agrees to rent property from another party
  • Synonym: tenancy - the occupancy or holding of property under a lease
  • Antonym: deed - a legal document that is a record of an agreement or transaction, especially to transfer property

Etymology

From Old French *lais* (a letting), from laisser (to let, leave), from Latin *laxare* (to loosen).

3. VERB

/ lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
to grant the use of property, land, or goods to another for a specified period in exchange for payment

Examples

In order to attract new industries, 15 states or more are issuing tax free bonds to build government owned plants which are leased to private enterprise.
You do n't want to lease a place way out in the sticks.

Synset Examples

We rented our apartment to friends while we were abroad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to allow someone to use something in exchange for payment

Etymology

From Old French *laisser* (to let, leave), from Latin *laxare* (to loosen, release).
