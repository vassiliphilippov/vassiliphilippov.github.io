cleric

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪk / (BrE)
a member of the clergy; a person in religious orders, especially a priest or minister

Examples

Since the Protestant clergy for the most part wear gray or some variant from the wholly black suit, my Roman collar and black garb usually identify me in England as a Roman Catholic cleric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy
  • Antonym: layperson - a non-ordained member of a church; not a cleric

Etymology

From Medieval Latin clericus (clergyman, priest), from Greek klerikos (of the clergy).
