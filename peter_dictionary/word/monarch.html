monarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmɒnək / (BrE)
A nation's ruler or head of state, especially one who holds this position by hereditary right.

Examples

Although the monarch had frequently asserted that the elections were to be without party significance, his action was an implicit admission that party identifications were a factor.
However, the sovereign was not Hobbes' absolute monarch but rather the parliamentary sovereign of Austin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sovereign - supreme ruler, especially a monarch
  • Antonym: dictator - ruler with total power, often not hereditary
  • Antonym: subject - person under the authority of a monarch

Etymology

From Late Latin monarcha, from Greek monarkhos (sole ruler).
