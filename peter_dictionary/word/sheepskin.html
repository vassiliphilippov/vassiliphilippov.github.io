sheepskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃiːp.skɪn / (BrE)
the tanned skin of a sheep with the wool still attached, used especially for clothing and other items

Examples

He left his home the day after New Year's wearing a mackinaw and sheepskin mittens and without a hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lambskin - skin of a young sheep prepared similarly

Etymology

Sheep + skin; referring literally to the skin of a sheep.
