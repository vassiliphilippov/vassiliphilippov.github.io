invisible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɪzəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪzɪbl̩ / (BrE)
Impossible or nearly impossible to see; not perceptible by the eye.

Examples

The senses in each counterpart bear the impression only of phenomena that share its own frequency, whereas those upon all other frequencies are invisible, inaudible and intactible to them.
Sometimes he would grunt softly to some invisible onlooker beside him, sometimes he would look stern and moralistic as his pencil did what he disapproved.
Like he had on an invisible coat that would shield him from this place.

Synset Examples

the invisible man
invisible rays
an invisible hinge
invisible mending

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen; perceptible by the eye
  • Synonym: unseeable - impossible to see; not visible

Etymology

From Latin invisibilis, from in- (not) + visibilis (visible).
