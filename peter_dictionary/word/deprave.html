deprave

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpreɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpreɪv / (BrE)
To make someone morally corrupt or wicked.

Examples

For it is such a distinguished place, with such fine works of art and such a big library, that there can be little doubt but that the owner has become depraved by all this culture.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to make someone or something dishonest or immoral
  • Synonym: debauch - to lead someone into immoral or excessive behavior
  • Antonym: edify - to instruct or improve someone morally or intellectually

Etymology

From Latin 'depravare' (to distort, pervert).
