zinc

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋk / (AmE), / zɪŋk / (BrE)
a bluish-white metallic element, brittle at ordinary temperatures but malleable when heated, used in various alloys and in galvanizing iron; chemical symbol Zn

Etymology

From German *Zink*, first used in the late 17th century.
