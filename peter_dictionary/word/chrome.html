chrome

1. NOUN

/ kroʊm / (AmE), / krəʊm / (BrE)
a thin, shiny coating of chromium, especially as applied decoratively or protectively to metal surfaces, such as on cars or appliances

Examples

Still nursing anger I listlessly thumbed a car that was slowly approaching, its pre-war chrome nearly blinding me.
A big car was approaching, its chrome teeth grinning.
Two cars came over a crest, their chrome and glass flashing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromium - the chemical element used to create chrome finishes

Etymology

Shortened from chromium, referencing the metallic element used in plating.
