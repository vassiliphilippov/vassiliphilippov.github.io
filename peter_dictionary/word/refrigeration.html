refrigeration

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of cooling or freezing something, especially food, in order to preserve it.

Examples

None of these values is destroyed, not significantly altered by refrigeration storage.
Ultracentrifugation was then carried out in a Spinco model L ultracentrifuge at 40000 rpm for 125 to 150 min, refrigeration being used throughout the run.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: freezing - The process of turning something into ice or a solid by cold; more extreme than refrigeration
  • Degree Lower: cooling - The act or process of making or becoming less warm; less intensive than refrigeration

Etymology

From Latin refrigeratio, from refrigerare (to make cold).
