outmaneuver

1. VERB

/ ˌaʊt.məˈnu.vɚ / (AmE), / ˌaʊt.məˈnjuː.və / (BrE)
To gain an advantage over someone by using greater skill, cleverness, or strategy, especially in a competitive or conflict situation.

Examples

He had been a corps commander during the disastrous defeat and retreat of 1942 when the ill-prepared, ill-equipped British forces "were outmaneuvered, outfought and outgeneraled ".

Synset Examples

The English troops outmaneuvered the Germans
My new supervisor knows how to outmaneuver the boss in most situations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsmart - to gain an advantage over by being more clever
  • Synonym: outwit - to cleverly defeat or trick someone
  • Antonym: underperform - to perform less well than others, often leading to being outmaneuvered

Etymology

From 'out-' (beyond) + 'maneuver', patterned after French 'manœuvrer'.
