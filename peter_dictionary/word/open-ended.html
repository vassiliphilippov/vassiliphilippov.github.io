open-ended

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊpənˈɛndɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊpənˈɛndɪd / (BrE)
Having no definite limit, restriction, or conclusion; allowing for future changes or continuations.

Examples

Also, beware of open end policies, especially in the medical field.

Synset Examples

an open-ended discussion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: limited - having fixed bounds or restrictions

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊpənˈɛndɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊpənˈɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of a question or activity) allowing for a free, unscripted, or unlimited response; not having a fixed limit or predetermined answer.

Examples

An interview schedule of open-ended questions and a multiple-choice questionnaire were prepared, and one parent of each of the sample children was seen in the home.

Synset Examples

an open-ended question

Related Terms

  • Antonym: closed-ended - (of a question) requiring a specific, limited response

Etymology

Formed from 'open' + 'ended', indicating that the end is not fixed.
