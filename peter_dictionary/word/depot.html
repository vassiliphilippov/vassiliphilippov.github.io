depot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːˌpoʊ, ˈdɛˌpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɛpəʊ, ˈdiːpəʊ / (BrE)
A station where transport vehicles, such as trains or buses, load or unload passengers or goods.

Examples

Houses winked as the cars rolled beside a little depot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - a place where vehicles regularly stop to load or unload passengers or goods

Etymology

From French dépôt (storehouse, deposit), from Latin depositum (deposit).
