heart

1. NOUN

/ hɑrt / (AmE), / hɑːt / (BrE)
The central or most important part of a person's feelings and emotions; regarded as the source of love, compassion, courage, or intuition.

Examples

Of the two cherished achievements the elderly Spahn's hitless pitching probably reached the most hearts.
Open your heart to Him and pray, Stephen, pray!
Back in Bavaria he had seen that gesture, and at that sight his heart had always died within him.
His heart suddenly opened to joy.
He thought: Only in my heart can I make the world hang together.

Synset Examples

in your heart you know it is true
her story would melt your bosom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bosom - the chest as the seat of emotions or affection; inner feelings
  • Synonym: spirit - the non-physical part of a person regarded as their true self, seat of emotions
  • Antonym: mind - the seat of thought and intellect, as contrasted with heart as emotion

Etymology

From Old English heorte, from Proto-Germanic *hertō, from Proto-Indo-European *ḱḗr (heart).

4. NOUN

/ hɑrt / (AmE), / hɑːt / (BrE)

Examples
