providential

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɑː.vəˈdɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.vɪˈdɛn.ʃəl / (BrE)
Occurring at a favorable time, as if by divine intervention; fortunate or opportune.

Examples

Even death, therefore, has a providential as well as a punitive function.

Synset Examples

a heaven-sent rain saved the crops
a providential recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunate - Receiving good fortune or luck
  • Synonym: lucky - Having good luck
  • Antonym: unlucky - Not having good luck; unfortunate

Etymology

From Latin 'providentia' (foresight), via French 'providence'.
