ad lib

1. ADV

/ æd lɪb / (AmE), / ad lɪb / (BrE)
to speak or perform without prior preparation; spontaneously or improvisationally.

Examples

The night we saw it, a rather unpleasant situation arose when the soloist refused to approach the armadillo, complaining - in ad-lib - that "it smelled ".

Synset Examples

he spoke ad lib

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvise - create or perform spontaneously without preparation

Etymology

From Latin *ad libitum*, meaning 'at one's pleasure'.
