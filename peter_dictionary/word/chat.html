chat

1. VERB

/ tʃæt / (AmE), / tʃat / (BrE)
to talk in a friendly and informal way, usually about unimportant things

Examples

She sees that there is a cup of steaming hot coffee awaiting him and the two chat informally as she presents the rules of the center and explains procedures.
He would come home in the evening tired and discouraged - in no frame of mind to play with their three children, or spend much time chatting with his wife.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talk - to speak in order to give information or express ideas or feelings
  • Degree Higher: gossip - to talk about other people's private lives, often in an unkind or disapproving way

Etymology

From Middle English 'chatten', imitative of the sound of conversation.
