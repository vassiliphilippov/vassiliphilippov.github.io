dissemble

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛm.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛm.bəl / (BrE)
to hide your real motives, feelings, or beliefs by pretending or putting on a false appearance

Examples

But we are to believe that Lindemann actively supported radar outside the Tizard Committee, and dissembling, discounted it inside?

Synset Examples

He feigned that he was ill
He shammed a headache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: feign - to fake or give a false appearance of something
  • Antonym: reveal - to make something known that was secret or hidden

Etymology

From Old French 'dissimuler', from Latin 'dissimulo' (to conceal, disguise).
