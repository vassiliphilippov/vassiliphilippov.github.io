closeup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊs.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈkləʊs.ʌp / (BrE)
A photograph or film shot taken at very close range, showing detail or a subject's face.

Examples

Cameras stared at soloists' faces in extreme closeups, then considerately pulled back for full views of ensemble work.
Samples zoomed into closeup range in regular succession, like telephone poles passing on the highway, while representative music reinforced the mood of the late teens and 1920's.
As a finale is appended a close-up of one of the band taking aim and firing his revolver straight at the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close shot - A shot film taken at a close distance (showing the subject in detail)
  • Antonym: long shot - A film or photograph taken from a considerable distance, showing a broad view

Etymology

From 'close' (near) + 'up'. Term popularized in motion picture and photography contexts in the early 20th century.
