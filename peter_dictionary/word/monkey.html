monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized primate with a long tail, typically having a prehensile tail and living in trees, found mainly in tropical regions.

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German 'monnik' or Old Italian 'monicchio', diminutive of 'monna' (a woman, nun), origin unclear.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
A person who behaves in a playfully mischievous or cheeky way.

Examples

I owe that monkey my life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person

Etymology

From Middle Low German *monnik(e)* or French *monne* (ape), origin uncertain.
