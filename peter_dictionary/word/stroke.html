stroke

1. NOUN

/ stroʊk / (AmE), / strəʊk / (BrE)
An act of striking at or hitting a ball with a club, racket, bat, cue, or the hand, especially in sports such as golf, tennis, or cricket.

Examples

Each of the four wayward shots cost him two strokes.
So he picked up a stroke with the provisional ball and won the tournament by the margin of that stroke.

4. VERB

/ stroʊk / (AmE), / strəʊk / (BrE)
to move one's hand gently and repeatedly over a surface, especially as a sign of affection or comfort

Examples

The music arrived, taking him, its rhythm stroked him, snaked all through him, the lyrics lifted him, took him from one magic isle to another, stopping briefly at each.
He stroked the hens and they responded with delighted clucks, he gobbled with the turkeys and they at once were all attention, he quacked with the ducks, and cackled with a pair of exceedingly flattered geese.

Synset Examples

He stroked his long beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently in a loving or affectionate manner
  • Synonym: pat - to touch lightly and repeatedly, usually with the palm or fingers

Etymology

From Old English *strācian* (to pass lightly over, stroke), related to German *streichen* (to stroke).
