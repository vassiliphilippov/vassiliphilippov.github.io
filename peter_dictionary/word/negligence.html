negligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Failure to take proper care or to act with the caution that a reasonable person would in the same circumstances, sometimes resulting in harm or damage.

Examples

I do n't think his diligence mitigates your negligence, Matson ".
"Negligence, hell "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or care
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent effort or care

Etymology

From Latin negligentia (carelessness), from negligere (to neglect).
