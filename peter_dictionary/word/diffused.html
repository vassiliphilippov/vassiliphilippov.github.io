diffused

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfjuzd / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
(of light) spread out and softened by being scattered, typically by passing through a translucent material or reflecting from an uneven surface; not direct or concentrated.

Examples

The sharp ray was absorbed by a cloud, then reflected to the earth in a softer, diffused radiance.

Synset Examples

diffused light

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - (of light) proceeding in a straight line without being scattered

Etymology

Past participle of 'diffuse', from Latin 'diffusus' (spread out).
