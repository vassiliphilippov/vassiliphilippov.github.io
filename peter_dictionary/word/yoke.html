yoke

1. NOUN

/ joʊk / (AmE), / jəʊk / (BrE)
A fitted piece of fabric at the top of a dress, shirt, or similar garment, usually shaped to fit the shoulders or hips and to which other sections of the garment are attached.

Examples

She was wearing a brown cotton dress, cut across the hips in a way that was supposed to make her look slimmer, a yoke set into the skirt and flaring pleats below.

Etymology

From Old English *geoc* (yoke), extended metaphorically from the wooden bar for joining oxen, referring to the shape of the garment section.
