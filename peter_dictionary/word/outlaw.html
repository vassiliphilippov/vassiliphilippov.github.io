outlaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɔː / (BrE)
a person who has committed a crime or has been declared an outlaw by legal authority; a criminal, especially one living outside the protection of the law.

Examples

Each scene is shot straight through, as had been the universal custom, from a camera fixed in a single position, but in the outdoor scenes, especially in the capture and destruction of the outlaws, Porter's camera position breaks, necessarily, with the camera position standard until then, which had been, roughly, that of a spectator in a center orchestra seat at a play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - a person who obeys the law

Etymology

From Old English *ūtlaga* (outlaw), from Old Norse *útlagi* (outlawed person).

2. VERB

/ ˈaʊtˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌlɔː / (BrE)
to declare illegal; to ban or forbid by law

Examples

Eighteenth-century England, upon whose customs our common law was built, had outlawed unions as monopolies and conspiracies.
"Use of such weapons has been outlawed by the general opinion of civilized mankind.

Synset Examples

Marijuana is criminalized in the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - officially or legally prohibit
  • Antonym: legalize - make legal; allow by law

Etymology

From Old English *ūtlaga* (banished person), from Old Norse *útlagi*.
