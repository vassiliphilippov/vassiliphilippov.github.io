tar

1. NOUN

/ tɑr / (AmE), / tɑː / (BrE)
a dark, thick, sticky substance obtained as a residue from the distillation of coal, petroleum, or wood, used in road making and for waterproofing

Examples

Ludie chewed roofer's tar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch - another term for a dark, sticky, resinous material

Etymology

Old English *teoru*, related to Old Norse *tjara* and Dutch *teer*.

2. VERB

/ tɑr / (AmE), / tɑː / (BrE)
To cover or coat something with tar, especially surfaces like roads or roofs.

Examples

Hell, we could have been tarring the roof of one of our own houses.

Synset Examples

tar the roof
tar the roads

Etymology

From Old English 'teoru' (tar), related to Old Norse 'tjara'.
