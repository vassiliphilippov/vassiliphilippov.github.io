crib

1. NOUN

/ krɪb / (AmE), / krɪb / (BrE)
A small bed for a baby or young child with high sides to prevent them from falling out.

Examples

"He found the Pedersen kid by the crib ".
"Nothing to steal from the crib ".
"I ai n't hid nothing in the crib ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cot - British term for a baby's bed with high sides

Etymology

From Old English *cribb* (manger, stall), related to Old High German *krippa* (manger).
