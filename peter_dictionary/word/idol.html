idol

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪd(ə)l / (BrE)
A person who is admired or loved very much, often to an excessive or unquestioning degree.

Examples

The idol of the hackers, of course, is Ray Ainsley, who achieved a 19 in the United States Open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • Synonym: icon - a person or thing regarded as a representative symbol or as worthy of veneration

Etymology

From Greek 'eidōlon' (image, form, phantom) via Latin 'idolum'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪd(ə)l / (BrE)
an image or object representing a deity, spirit, or other being, worshipped as a god or as sacred

Examples

Stevie saw no idols; it troubled him that he could n't always see what Papa saw.
One morning, we discovered not only that the pennies were missing from the idol but that a cigarette had been stubbed out in its lap.

Synset Examples

thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image
money was his god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icon - a devotional painting, especially in Eastern Christian tradition
  • Degree Higher: god - a deity or divine being

Etymology

From Greek eidōlon (image, phantom), via Latin idolum.
