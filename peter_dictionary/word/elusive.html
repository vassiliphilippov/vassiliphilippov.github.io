elusive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
difficult to catch or find, especially because of cleverness or skill at avoiding capture.

Examples

Beaten, bruised and exhausted, he pursues the elusive killer through the demi-monde of high society and low morals, always alone, always despised.

Synset Examples

a cabal of conspirators, each more elusive than the archterrorist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasive - tending to avoid capture or commitment

Etymology

From Latin 'elus-', past participle stem of 'eludere' (to elude).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
Difficult to find, catch, or define; hard to describe or understand clearly.

Examples

the aspect of learning the internal organization of obvious natural genetic groups of languages as well as their more remote and elusive external links; of classification first, with elapsed age merely a by-product; of acquiring evidential knowledge of what happened in Athabascan, in Yokuts, in Uto-Aztecan in the last few thousand years as well as forecasting what more anciently may have happened between them.

Synset Examples

a haunting elusive odor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly
  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe
  • Antonym: tangible - clear and definite; real

Etymology

From Latin 'elus-', past participle stem of 'eludere' meaning 'to evade, escape from'.
