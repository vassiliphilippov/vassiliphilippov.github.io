misrepresent

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to give a false or misleading account of the nature of something or someone; to describe inaccurately or dishonestly

Examples

The absurd notion that demythologization entails the expurgation of all mythological concepts completely misrepresents Bultmann's intention.
The two major newspapers in Providence continued, throughout the crisis, to accuse each other of misrepresenting the facts and attempting to falsify history.

Synset Examples

This statement misrepresents my intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distort - give a misleading or false account or impression
  • Synonym: falsify - to alter information in order to mislead
  • Antonym: clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand
  • Antonym: accurately represent - to portray truthfully or correctly

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + represent, from Latin repraesentare (to present, show).
