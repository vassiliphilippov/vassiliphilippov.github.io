strained

1. ADJ

/ streɪnd / (AmE), / streɪnd / (BrE)
showing signs of mental or emotional tension; appearing uneasy, nervous, or under stress

Examples

It was a strained, silent lunch.

Synset Examples

her voice was strained as she asked the question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tense - showing nervousness or anxiety
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety

Etymology

From the verb 'strain', from Old French *estreindre* (to bind tightly), from Latin *stringere* (to draw tight).

2. ADJ

/ streɪnd / (AmE), / streɪnd / (BrE)
(of a manner, speech, or situation) not relaxed or natural; showing signs of tension or effort.

Examples

Within this framework, what followed was strained, even macabre.

Synset Examples

a labored style of debating

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - not forced, relaxed, or easy

Etymology

Past participle of 'strain', from Old French 'estreindre' (to squeeze, press), from Latin 'stringere' (to draw tight).
