ill-fated

1. ADJ

/ ɪlˈfeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪlˈfeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having or likely to have bad luck; destined to fail or suffer misfortune.

Examples

After the collapse of that desperate and ill-fated campaign the character of the king degenerated for a time into a futility that was not merely pitiable but often ridiculous.

Synset Examples

their business venture was doomed from the start
an ill-fated business venture
an ill-starred romance
the unlucky prisoner was again put in irons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doomed - certain to fail or experience misfortune
  • Synonym: ill-starred - destined to encounter bad luck or misfortune
  • Antonym: fortunate - having good luck or a favorable outcome

Etymology

Formed from 'ill' (bad) + 'fated' (destined by fate).
