grind

1. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd / (BrE)
to press, crush, or rub something together, often producing a harsh, scraping, or crunching noise

Examples

He ground his teeth together.
Horse smell was very strong, and he could hear the crunch of grain being ground between strong jaws.
When B' dikkat cut the head from Mercer's thigh, he felt the knife grinding against the cartilage which held the head to his own body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it is damaged or breaks into pieces
  • Degree Higher: powder - to reduce to fine particles by crushing, beating, or grinding

Etymology

Old English *grindan* (to crush, to rub together), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd / (BrE)
(of two people) to dance by rotating the pelvis in an erotically suggestive way, often while in close contact with a partner.

Examples

"We grind, too ", he said.

3. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd / (BrE)
To work hard, especially at something monotonous or laborious.

Examples

The legislative mills have been grinding ever since, and when its cumbersome processes were no longer adequate to the task, a limited legislative authority was delegated in one form or another, to the executive.

Synset Examples

She was digging away at her math homework
Lexicographers drudge all day long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudge - to do hard, menial, or monotonous work
  • Synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly

Etymology

Old English grindan (to crush, rub together). Sense of 'to work hard' arose from the notion of repetitive effort, like grinding grain.

4. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd / (BrE)
To reduce something to small pieces or powder by crushing, pounding, or rubbing it.

Examples

The highway system is an agency of government, and when it grinds up 40000 Americans every year the government is destroying its own taxpayers, which is obviously a silly thing for any government to do.

Synset Examples

grind the spices in a mortar
mash the garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or is damaged
  • Synonym: pulverize - to crush into powder or dust
  • Synonym: mash - to crush or smash into a soft or pulpy mass

Etymology

Old English *grīndan* (to rub, crush), related to Old Norse *grinda*.

5. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd / (BrE)
to make a harsh or grating sound by rubbing two surfaces together

Examples

Today whole grains are freshly ground every day and baked into bread.
Once the grains are ground, vitamin E begins to deteriorate immediately and half of it is lost by oxidation and exposure to the air within one week.
He dropped it and carefully ground it to nothing with the sole of his heavy shoe.

Synset Examples

grate one's teeth in anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grate - to make a harsh rasping sound by rubbing

Etymology

Old English *grindan* (to grind), from Proto-Germanic *grindanan*.
