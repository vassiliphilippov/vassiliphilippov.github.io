differential

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
A mathematical expression representing the instantaneous rate of change of one quantity with respect to another; the result of differentiation, such as df(x)/dx.

Examples

The actual change * * f caused by a shear field is calculated by multiplying the pressure differential times the volume, just as it is for any gravitational or osmotic pressure head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derivative - function representing the rate of change in calculus

Etymology

From Latin differentia (difference), via French différentiel.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Relating to or showing a difference; depending on or involving distinctions between things.

Examples

Differential thermal analysis showed a very small endothermic reaction at 340 ` C and a large endothermic reaction at 470 ` C.

Synset Examples

differential treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinctive - serving to distinguish or set apart; having a specific difference
  • Antonym: identical - exactly alike; showing no difference

Etymology

From Medieval Latin differentialis, from Latin differentia (difference).
