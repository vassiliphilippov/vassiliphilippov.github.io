buckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl / (BrE)
A metal or plastic fastener for joining two ends of a belt, strap, or similar item, typically consisting of a frame and a movable prong.

Examples

Martha Schuyler, old, slow, careful of foot, came down the great staircase, dressed in her best lace-drawn black silk, her jeweled shoe buckles held forward.
He found tepid water in a pitcher and a last bit of soap, and he lathered his face and stood stropping the razor on his broad leather belt, its buckle held firm by a knob of the bedpost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clasp - a fastener used to hold two things together, often by means of a catch or hook
  • Synonym: buckle up - to fasten a seat belt using a buckle

Etymology

From Old French *bocle* (buckle, boss), from Latin *buccula* (cheek strap of a helmet), diminutive of *bucca* (cheek).

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl / (BrE)
(of a structure or body part) to bend, fold, or give way suddenly, typically under pressure or as a result of weakness.

Examples

Watson turned away and did not see the man's knees buckle and his body sag.

Synset Examples

His knees buckled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: fold - to bend or give way under force

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl / (BrE)
to fasten or secure (something) with a buckle or buckles

Examples

He at once cancels the celebrations and, buckling on his scimitar, stumbles blindly from the house, where he is hit and killed by a passing oxcart.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbuckle - to open or undo a buckle; to release something fastened with a buckle

Etymology

From Old French *bocle*, from Latin *buccula* (cheek strap of a helmet).

4. VERB

/ ˈbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl / (BrE)
(of a structure or material) to bend or give way out of shape, usually as a result of pressure or heat.

Examples

The roof of the command post began to buckle.

Synset Examples

The highway buckled during the heat wave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warp - to bend or twist out of shape, especially from heat or moisture
  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight; to remove bends or curves
