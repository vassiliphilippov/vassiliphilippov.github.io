tan

1. NOUN

/ tæn / (AmE), / tæn / (BrE)
a light yellowish-brown colour, resembling the colour of topaz

Examples

Spectators in white crush textures dip toe and heel in smooth black, navy and taffy tan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Higher: brown - a dark color, the color of wood or earth
  • Degree Lower: cream - a pale yellowish-white color

Etymology

From Old English *tannian* (to tan hide), referring to the brown colour produced by tanning leather.

2. NOUN

/ tæn / (AmE), / tæn / (BrE)
a brown or golden color of the skin that develops after exposure to sunlight

Examples

She had had a dignity about her, even barefoot and almost too tan.
That luscious tan... ... those ruby lips... ... and hair color so natural, only your undertaker knows for sure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunburn - reddening of the skin due to overexposure to sunlight

Etymology

From Old English 'tannian' (to tan hide), influenced by Old French 'tanner' and Medieval Latin 'tannare'.
