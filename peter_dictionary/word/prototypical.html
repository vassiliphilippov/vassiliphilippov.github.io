prototypical

1. ADJ

/ ˌproʊtəˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
representing the most typical or original example of a particular kind or group; serving as a model after which others are formed

Examples

Freeman, Cameron and McGhie, in their description of the disturbances of thinking found in chronic schizophrenic patients, say, in regard to condensation, that "the lack of adequate discrimination between the self and the environment, and the objects contained therein in itself is the prototypical condensation ".

Synset Examples

archetypal patterns
she was the prototypal student activist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetypal - very typical of a certain kind or class; serving as a perfect example

Etymology

From 'prototype' (from Greek prototypon, 'a first or original form') + '-ical'.
