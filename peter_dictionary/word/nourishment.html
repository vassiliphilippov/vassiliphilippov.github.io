nourishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɝːrɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈnʌrɪʃmənt / (BrE)
The food or substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition.

Examples

After Larkin has been persuaded to restock his tangled acres with pheasants, he poaches only what he needs for the nourishment of his family and local callers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutrition - the process of providing or obtaining the food necessary for health and growth
  • Antonym: starvation - suffering or death caused by lack of food

Etymology

From Old French 'nourissement', from 'nourir' (to nourish), from Latin 'nutrire' (to feed, nourish).
