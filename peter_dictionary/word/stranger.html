stranger

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒə / (BrE)
A person who is not known or is not familiar in a particular place or among a particular group of people.

Examples

His name is Praisegod Piepsam, and he is rather fully described as to his clothing and physiognomy in a way which relates him to a sinister type in the author's repertory - he is a forerunner of those enigmatic strangers in "Death in Venice ", for example, who represent some combination of cadaver, exotic, and psychopomp.
Extraordinary precautions were taken so that no stranger be allowed in the city and no citizen within the enclosure surrounding the scaffold.
Sensing the unseen presence of the other men in the patrol, he felt mutely united to these nine near strangers sharing this pinpoint of being with him.
In France he had puzzled the meaning of the great stone monuments men had thrown up to the sky, and always as he wandered, he felt a stranger to their exultation.
But the stranger was nimbler still.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreigner - a person from another country or place
  • Antonym: native - a person belonging to a place by birth or long association

Etymology

From Old French 'estrangier' (foreigner), from Latin 'extraneus' (from outside).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person whom one does not know or is not familiar with

Examples

I was very happy that this house never went to strangers.
They'’re saps because they risk their lives for strangers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly but who is not a close friend
  • Synonym: foreigner - a person from another country; an outsider

Etymology

From Old French estrange (stranger, foreign), from Latin extraneus (foreign, external).
