eclectic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈklɛktɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (BrE)
selecting or using what seems best from various sources, styles, or ideas rather than adhering to a single approach

Examples

If the record buyer's tastes are somewhat eclectic or even the slightest bit esoteric, he will find them satisfied on educational records.
If he is a traditionalist, he is an eclectic traditionalist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different

Etymology

From Greek eklektikos (selective), from eklegein (to select), from ek- (out) + legein (to choose).
