hurdle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝdəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdl / (BrE)
a light, movable barrier over which competitors must jump in a race, used especially in track and field events

Examples

The largest hurdle the Republicans would have to face is a state law which says that before making a first race, one of two alternative courses must be taken:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - a thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress; general sense

Etymology

From Old English *hyrdel* (framework, hurdle), related to German *Hürde*.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdl / (BrE)
to jump over a hurdle or barrier, especially in a race or while moving quickly

Examples

This time more of them hurdled the barrier.
Above me a dark rider was whipping his pony with a quirt in an attempt to hurdle the bales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump - to leap into the air or over something
  • Synonym: clear - to successfully jump over an obstacle

Etymology

From Old English *hurd* (frame or structure of interwoven twigs), related to barriers used in races.
