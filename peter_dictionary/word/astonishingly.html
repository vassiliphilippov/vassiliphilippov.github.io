astonishingly

1. ADV

/ əˈstɑnɪʃɪŋli / (AmE), / əˈstɒnɪʃɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes great surprise or amazement.

Examples

Besides showing no inclination, apparently, to absent himself from his native region even for short periods, and in addition writing a shelf of books set in the region, he has handled in those books an astonishingly complete list of matters which have been important in the South during the past hundred years.

Synset Examples

amazingly, he finished medical school in three years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazingly - in a way that causes great surprise or wonder

Etymology

From ‘astonishing’ + ‘-ly’, with ‘astonishing’ from ‘astonish’ (from Old French *estoner*, from Latin *extonare*, meaning ‘to thunder out’).
