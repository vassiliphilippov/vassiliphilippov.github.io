cozy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
giving a feeling of comfort, warmth, and relaxation, often in a small or sheltered space; providing or enjoying a warm, comfortable atmosphere

Examples

Wo n't you step into the living room, where it's cozier "?
At the same time, there is a good deal of self congratulation at attending a good college - they are even inclined to exaggerate its not inconsiderable virtues - and they express pleasure at the cozy in-group feeling that the college generates.

Synset Examples

a cozy nook near the fire
snug in bed
a snug little apartment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snug - comfortable, warm, and sheltered; fitting closely or tightly
  • Synonym: comfortable - providing physical ease and relaxation

Etymology

From Middle English 'cosy', probably from Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'koselig' (pleasant, cosy).
