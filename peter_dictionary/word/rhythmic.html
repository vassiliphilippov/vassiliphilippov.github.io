rhythmic

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪð.mɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.mɪk / (BrE)
Having or relating to a regular, repeating pattern of sounds, movements, or events, especially in music or poetry.

Examples

Schnabel's emphasis on structural clarity, his innate rhythmic vibrancy, and impetuous intensity all tend to stamp his reading as a symphonic one.
His sense for rhythmic variety and timing is impeccable.
Mercer's lyrics are characterized by an unerring ear for rhythmic nuances, a puckish sense of humor expressed in language with a colloquial flair.

Synset Examples

the rhythmic chiming of church bells
rhythmical prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythmical - having or characterized by rhythm; variant form

Etymology

From rhythm + -ic, with -ic from Greek -ikos forming adjectives.
