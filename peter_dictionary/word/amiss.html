amiss

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɪs / (AmE), / əˈmɪs / (BrE)
not quite right; out of order, or not functioning properly

Examples

The hosts never seem to recognize that something is amiss, so that the compartment afterward is sealed normally.

Synset Examples

something is amiss
has gone completely haywire
something is wrong with the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrong - not correct or not as it should be
  • Synonym: faulty - not working correctly; having defects
  • Antonym: right - correct or functioning properly

Etymology

From Middle English 'amys', from Old Norse 'á mis' meaning 'out of order, improperly'.

2. ADV

/ əˈmɪs / (AmE), / əˈmɪs / (BrE)
In a mistaken, improper, or wrong way; not as it should be.

Examples

What's wrong?

Synset Examples

if you think him guilty you judge amiss
he spoke amiss
no one took it amiss when she spoke frankly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrongly - in an incorrect or mistaken way
  • Antonym: correctly - in a correct or proper way

Etymology

From Middle English 'amys', from Old Norse 'amis' (wrongly).

3. ADV

/ əˈmɪs / (AmE), / əˈmɪs / (BrE)
In an imperfect, wrong, or faulty way.

Examples

What's wrong with you?

Synset Examples

The lobe was imperfectly developed
Miss Bennet would not play at all amiss if she practiced more

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrongly - in a wrong or incorrect manner
  • Antonym: correctly - in a correct or proper manner

Etymology

From Middle English amys, from Old French amis, from Vulgar Latin *admīssum (‘missed, mistaken’).
