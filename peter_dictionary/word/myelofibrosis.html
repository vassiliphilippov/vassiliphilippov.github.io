myelofibrosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəloʊfaɪˈbroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪələʊfɪˈbrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition involving the formation of fibrous tissue in the bone marrow, leading to impaired blood cell production.

Examples

Splenomegaly was first noted in 1956, and a sternal marrow biopsy at that time showed "scattered foci of fibrosis "suggestive of myelofibrosis.

Etymology

From Greek myelo- (marrow) + fibrosis (formation of fibrous tissue).
