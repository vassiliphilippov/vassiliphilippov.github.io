campfire

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmpˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæmpˌfaɪə / (BrE)
An outdoor fire built at a campsite, typically for warmth, light, or cooking food.

Examples

They brought to it all the odors that clung to men like themselves, that of their own sweat, of campfire smoke, of horses and cattle.

Etymology

Compound of 'camp' and 'fire.'
