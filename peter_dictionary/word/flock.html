flock

1. NOUN

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
A group of people under the spiritual care and guidance of a religious leader, especially a pastor or priest.

Examples

To be sure, when this is pointed out, a common response among certain churchmen is to fulminate about "the little flock "and "the great crowd "and to take solace from Paul's castigation of the "wisdom of the wise "in the opening chapter of First Corinthians.
He was stern and overbearing with his flock, but obsequious and conciliatory with the whites, especially the rich who partly supported the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congregation - an assembly of people for religious worship

Etymology

From Old English 'flocc' (group of people or animals), related to Middle Dutch 'vlok' (group, crowd).

2. VERB

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
(of people, animals, etc.) to move or gather together in a large group, often in the same direction or to the same place.

Examples

These were the years when people flocked to Manchester not only to play golf, which had come into vogue, but also to witness the Ekwanok Country Club tournaments.
Watch people flock to amusement houses, cocktail lounges, and night clubs that advertise continuous entertainment, which means an endless flow of noise and frivolity by paid entertainers who are supposed to perform in those incredible ways which are designed to give men a few hours of dubious relaxation - watch them and you can tell that many of them are running away from something.

Synset Examples

Tourists flocked to the shrine where the statue was said to have shed tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - come together in one place or group
  • Antonym: disperse - to break up and move in different directions

Etymology

From Old English 'flocc' meaning 'a group of people or animals'.

3. NOUN

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
a group of birds, especially when they are moving or feeding together

Examples

The thin gray figures raised a hoarse, cawing cry like the call of a bird flock.
Three quarters to 1 acre of good land is enough for raising fruits and vegetables for home use, and for a small flock of chickens, a cow, and two pigs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - a group of animals, especially hoofed mammals, that live or are kept together
  • Synonym: school - a large group of fish swimming together

Etymology

From Old English flocc (a group of people or animals); related to Old Norse flokkr (crowd, troop).

4. NOUN

/ flɑk / (AmE), / flɒk / (BrE)
a group of animals, especially birds, or people moving or gathered together in an orderly way

Examples

Already the jonquils were blooming in a flock by the front gate, and the periwinkles were coming on, blue by the porch steps.

Synset Examples

a troop of children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - a large group of animals, especially hoofed mammals, kept or living together
  • Synonym: troop - a group of people or animals assembled together
  • Degree Higher: crowd - a large number of people gathered together, typically in a disorganized way

Etymology

From Old English *flocc* (a band, troop), of Germanic origin.
