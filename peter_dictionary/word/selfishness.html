selfishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlfɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being chiefly concerned with one's own personal profit or pleasure, often at the expense of others.

Examples

What bad grace, what incredible selfishness he and Mark had shown.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: altruism - concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

From 'selfish' + '-ness', formed in English around the 17th century.
