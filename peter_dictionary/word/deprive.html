deprive

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪv / (BrE)
to prevent someone from having or enjoying something; to take something away from someone

Examples

Until we translate this gospel into a language that enlightened men today can understand, we are depriving ourselves of the very resources on which the continued success of our witness most certainly depends.
This subsection shall not be so construed as to deprive the owner of any background patent relating thereto of such rights as he may have thereunder.
This deprived him of liberty in Hong Kong, but he told Boats McCafferty that Hong Kong was a book he had read before, and the Navy would always bring him there again, some day.
You won'’t be deprived of anything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deny - to refuse to give or allow something
  • Synonym: strip - to remove something from someone, often forcefully or unjustly
  • Antonym: grant - to give something formally or officially

Etymology

From Latin deprivare, from de- (removal) + privare (to deprive, rob).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪv / (BrE)
to prevent someone from having or keeping something, especially something they need or value

Examples

Depicted, Cubist flatness is now almost completely assimilated to the literal, undepicted kind, but at the same time it reacts upon and largely transforms the undepicted kind - and it does so, moreover, without depriving the latter of its literalness; rather, it underpins and reinforces that literalness, re-creates it.
It's a matter of boredom, and the subconscious feeling that she is entitled to something, because she's being deprived of something else ".
She had to get away from here before this demoniac possession swallowed up the liquid of her eyes and sank into the fibers of her brain, depriving her of reason and sight.

Synset Examples

The Nazis stripped the Jews of all their assets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - to take something away from someone, especially in a sudden or forceful way

Etymology

From Latin privare (to bereave, deprive), via Old French priver.

3. VERB

/ dɪˈpraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪv / (BrE)
to take something away from someone or prevent them from having something, especially something necessary or desirable

Examples

Not long after the Warden deprived us of his company, I got a postcard in the mail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rob - to take property from a person or place illegally, often by force
  • Synonym: strip - to remove something from someone or something, often forcibly
  • Antonym: grant - to give or allow (something requested)

Etymology

From Latin 'deprivare' meaning 'to deprive, rob, or strip of'.
