flair

1. NOUN

/ flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
a natural ability to do something well, especially in a way that is stylish or original

Examples

One might have expected that such a violent epoch of transition would have destroyed the creative flair of a composer, especially one whose works were so fluent and spontaneous.
Because of the authentic homogeneity of his early Nationalistic materials, and his flair for orchestrations - his brilliant Petruchka, his savage Sacre du Printemps, his incisive Les Noces - the world kept hoping that he could recapture the historical direction for which his native talents were predisposed.

Synset Examples

he has a flair for mathematics
he has a genius for interior decorating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Degree Higher: genius - exceptional creative or intellectual power or aptitude
  • Synonym: aptitude - a natural ability to do something

Etymology

From French 'flair' (sense of smell, instinct), from Old French 'flairer' (to sniff).

2. NOUN

/ flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
A natural ability to do something with distinctive and stylish elegance; a sense of originality or panache.

Examples

Mercer's lyrics are characterized by an unerring ear for rhythmic nuances, a puckish sense of humor expressed in language with a colloquial flair.

Synset Examples

he wooed her with the confident dash of a cavalry officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panache - distinctive and stylish flair or confidence

Etymology

From French *flair* (sense of smell, instinct), from Old French *flair* (odor, scent), from Latin *flagrare* (to smell).
