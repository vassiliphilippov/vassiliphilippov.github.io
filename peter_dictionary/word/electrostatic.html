electrostatic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛk.troʊˈstæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəʊˈstæt.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by static electricity; concerned with electric charges at rest rather than in motion.

Examples

The association between electric (both electrostatic and voltaic) forces and magnetic forces had been recognized by investigators for many decades.
In the late 1700's Beccaria and van Marum, among others, had magnetized iron by sending an electrostatic charge through it.

Synset Examples

an electrostatic generator produces high-voltage static electricity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electromagnetic - relating to or caused by the interaction of electric and magnetic fields

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + static (from Greek statikos, 'causing to stand').
