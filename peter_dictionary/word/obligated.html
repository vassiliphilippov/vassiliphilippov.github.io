obligated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑblɪˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒblɪˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
required by law, conscience, or sense of duty to do something; compelled to act in a certain way

Examples

Indeed, of all that is his every man is by nature and reason and therefore by conscience obligated to regard himself as a custodian.
You may deduct these payments even though your child uses the money to purchase his own clothing or other necessities which you are normally obligated to furnish him, and even though you may be entitled to his services.

Synset Examples

felt obligated to repay the kindness
was obligated to pay off the student loan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliged - required or compelled, often by moral duty or social etiquette

Etymology

Past participle of 'obligate', from Latin 'obligatus' (bound, obliged).
