gauge

1. VERB

/ ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
To estimate or make a judgment about the amount, size, quality, or condition of something.

Examples

Not seeing her dark intelligent face, one would have gauged the voice as that of a Westwood Village matron, ten years out of Iowa.
From what I was able to gauge in a swift, greedy glance, the figure inside the coral colored boucle dress was stupefying.

Synset Examples

I estimate this chicken to weigh three pounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of something

Etymology

From Old French *jauge* (gauge, measure), perhaps of Frankish origin.

2. NOUN

/ ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring or indicating the amount, level, or contents of something, such as the thickness of a material or the amount of a liquid.

Examples

"I 've adjusted the gauge so that the pump cuts out before the water gets too low ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - device for measuring quantities

Etymology

From Old French 'jauge' (measuring rod) of uncertain origin.
