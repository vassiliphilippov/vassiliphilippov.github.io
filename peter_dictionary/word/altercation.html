altercation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔltɚˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːltəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A noisy or heated argument, especially in public.

Examples

The altercation in the coffee house had done little to dampen his spirits, but he was still a little wary around Rector for they had not yet discussed the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement
  • Synonym: argument - a disagreement, typically verbal
  • Degree Higher: fight - a violent confrontation or struggle
  • Degree Lower: discussion - conversation about a subject, especially more calm

Etymology

From Latin 'altercatio' (dispute), from 'altercari' (to argue).
