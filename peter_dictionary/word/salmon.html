salmon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ən / (BrE)
A large fish found in northern waters, known for migrating from saltwater to freshwater to spawn, and valued for its edible flesh.

Examples

Swadesh, and therefore Hoijer, felt compelled to omit all terms denoting species or even genera (fox, vulture, salmon, yellow pine, manzanita); their classes of animal and plant terms are restricted to generalizations or recurrent parts (fish, bird, tree, grass, horn, tail, bark, root).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a closely related freshwater fish, often similar in appearance to salmon

Etymology

From Old French *saumon*, from Latin *salmo*, *salmonis* (salmon).
