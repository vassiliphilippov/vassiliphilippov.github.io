stump

1. NOUN

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
The base part of a tree left standing after the tree has been cut down.

Examples

An area may have been partially logged and requires removal of stumps or clean up.
But he did not notice, and was already backing the car down to the road, saying "Toot-toot "! to the stump of a tree as he passed it, the same stump which had impaled the car of many a guest in the past thirty years and which he refused to have removed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trunk - main stem of a tree

Etymology

From Middle English 'stump', from Old English 'stump' (meaning piece or part), related to Middle Low German 'stump' (stump, end).

2. NOUN

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
The part of a limb or tooth that remains after the rest has been cut off or lost.

Examples

He took the pen in his stumps and began to write.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amputation - the removal of a limb by surgery or trauma

Etymology

From Middle English stompe, from Middle Dutch stomp (stump, stub).

3. VERB

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
to confuse or perplex (someone), especially by a question or problem that is difficult to answer

Examples

Questions like this, framed in verbal fog, are perhaps the only kind that have ever stumped an experienced politician.

Synset Examples

This problem stumped her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confound - to cause surprise or confusion in someone, especially by acting against their expectations
  • Synonym: perplex - to make (someone) feel completely baffled

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'stump' (noun), meaning a stub or part left after removal, alluding to being stopped short or unable to proceed.
