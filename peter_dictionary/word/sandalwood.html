sandalwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændlwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsændlwʊd / (BrE)
A close-grained, fragrant yellowish wood from certain trees, used in carving, cabinetmaking, and for its scent.

Examples

Rummaging through a stack of drawers nearby, you unearthed an antique French chess set in ivory and sandalwood, which, along with two box Kodaks, you added to your haul.

Etymology

From Sanskrit *candana* via Persian and Medieval Latin.
