trustworthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌstˌwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstwɜːði / (BrE)
Able to be relied on as honest and responsible in carrying out obligations.

Examples

Someone reasonably trustworthy.

Synset Examples

trustworthy public servants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliable - Consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted
  • Antonym: untrustworthy - Not able to be relied on as honest or responsible

Etymology

From 'trust' + 'worthy'; 'trust' from Old Norse 'traust' (confidence), 'worthy' from Old English 'weorþig' (deserving).
