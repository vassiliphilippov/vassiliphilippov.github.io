triangular

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈæŋgjələr / (AmE), / traɪˈæŋgjʊlə / (BrE)
Having three angles and three sides; shaped like a triangle.

Examples

New York lay bleaching in the summer sun, and the morning fish hawk, flying in the heated air, saw below him the long triangular wedge of Manhattan Island.

Synset Examples

a triangular figure
a triangular pyrimid has a triangle for a base

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trilateral - having three sides; involving three parties or groups
  • Degree Higher: quadrilateral - a shape with four sides and four angles

Etymology

From Latin 'triangulus' (three-cornered), from 'tri-' (three) and 'angulus' (angle).
