incursion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɜːʃən / (BrE)
an act of entering a place or area, especially in a sudden or hostile way; an invasion or intrusion

Examples

Gun set the captain's fifth of Hiram Walker inside the safe before he reported to Lt. Killpath, though he knew that Killpath's ulcer prevented him from making any untoward incursion on Herman Wolff's gift.

Synset Examples

the incursion of television into the American living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasion - an instance of invading a country or region with an armed force
  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of forcing oneself into a place or situation where one is not wanted or invited

Etymology

From Latin incursio (a running in, invasion), from incurrere (to run into).
