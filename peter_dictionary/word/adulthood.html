adulthood

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltˌhʊd / (AmE), / əˈdʌlthʊd / (BrE)
The period of life after a person has finished growing and is considered fully developed; the state or condition of being an adult.

Examples

The lower portion provides a rating at any stage between infancy and adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childhood - the period of being a child
  • Degree Lower: adolescence - the period of life between childhood and adulthood
  • Degree Higher: old age - the later stage of life after adulthood

Etymology

From 'adult' + '-hood'; from Latin 'adultus' (grown to maturity) and Old English '-hād' (state, condition).
