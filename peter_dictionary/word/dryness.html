dryness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being dry, especially lacking moisture or water.

Examples

He spoke of the season of dryness and dust, brought by the monsoon from the northeast, in harmony with the season of rain and mud, brought by the monsoon from the southwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aridity - the quality of being extremely dry, especially climate or soil
  • Antonym: moisture - slight wetness, especially considered as an amount or cause of dampness
  • Antonym: humidity - the state of being humid; moisture in the air

Etymology

From 'dry' + '-ness', modelled after Old English drīġness (dryness).
