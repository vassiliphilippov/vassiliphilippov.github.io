reprehensible

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnsəbl / (BrE)
deserving strong criticism or disapproval; morally wrong or blameworthy

Examples

A vague feeling that Anthony Payne had had it coming was hardly a thought and was, in any event, reprehensible.

Synset Examples

a criminal waste of talent
a deplorable act of violence
adultery is as reprehensible for a husband as for a wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplorable - deserving strong condemnation
  • Antonym: commendable - deserving praise or approval

Etymology

From Latin reprehensibilis, from reprehendere (to blame, to hold back).
