proletariat

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.ləˈtɛr.i.ət / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈteə.ri.ət / (BrE)
the class of people in society who do manual work or work for wages, especially in contrast to those who own capital or property

Examples

And it is clearly argued by Lord Percy of Newcastle, in his remarkable long essay, The Heresy of Democracy, and in a more general way by Voegelin, in his New Science of Politics, that this same Rousseauan idea, descending through European democracy, is the source of Marx's theory of the dictatorship of the proletariat.

Synset Examples

there is a shortage of skilled labor in this field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working class - social class of people employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work

Etymology

From Latin proletarius (citizen of the lowest class), from proles (offspring, progeny).
