disown

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈoʊn / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊn / (BrE)
to deliberately prevent someone from inheriting, especially by excluding them from a will

Examples

With a pardonable irony Shelley wrote to the father who had publicly disowned his daughter:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinherit - to prevent someone legally from receiving an inheritance

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + own; first used in 17th century.
