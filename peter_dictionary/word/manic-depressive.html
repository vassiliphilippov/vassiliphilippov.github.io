manic-depressive

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌmanɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
a person suffering from manic-depressive illness (bipolar disorder), characterized by alternating periods of mania and depression

Examples

This is the force, in our time and in every other time, that urges the paranoiac and the manic-depressive to become head of a state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bipolar disorder - a mental health condition marked by extreme mood swings, including mania and depression

Etymology

From 'manic' (relating to mania) and 'depressive' (relating to depression).
