make-work

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkˌwɜːk / (BrE)
work or activity that is created or assigned chiefly to keep someone busy, without serving any productive purpose

Examples

Would early retirement of non-productive, disabled employees reduce the number of make-work jobs?

Synset Examples

while he was waiting he filled the days with busywork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busywork - work that keeps someone busy without being useful

Etymology

Formed from 'make' + 'work', originally North American usage from the early 20th century.
