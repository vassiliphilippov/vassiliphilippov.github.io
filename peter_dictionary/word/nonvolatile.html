nonvolatile

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈvɑl.əˌtaɪl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance) not easily evaporating or vaporizing at normal temperatures; not readily converted into vapor.

Examples

Another recent achievement was the successful development of a method for the complete combustion in a bomb calorimeter of a metal in fluorine when the product is relatively non-volatile.

Synset Examples

a nonvolatile acid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: volatile - easily vaporized at normal temperatures

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'volatile' (from Latin 'volatilis', flying, fleeting).
