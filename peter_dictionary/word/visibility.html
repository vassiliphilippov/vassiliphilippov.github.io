visibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɪzəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The state or degree of being visible; the ability to be seen, especially under certain conditions, such as weather or lighting.

Examples

Visibility continued to be limited, and Greg was never able to get above a thousand feet.

Synset Examples

low visibility caused by fog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invisibility - the state of not being visible; inability to be seen
  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque; not allowing light to pass through
  • Degree Higher: clarity - the quality of being clear; clearness

Etymology

From Latin 'visibilitas' (ability to be seen), from 'visibilis' (visible).
