pan

1. VERB

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
to wash earth or gravel in a pan in order to separate out gold or other valuable minerals

Examples

An old shipwreck, a high dam, an old covered bridge, a place to find agates or other semi-precious stones or a place to pan gold, etcetera may be of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prospect - to search for precious metals, especially gold, by excavation or washing

2. NOUN

/ pæn / (AmE), / pan / (BrE)
A shallow, usually metal container with a flat bottom and low sides, used for cooking food over heat.

Examples

He saw Mose squatting by the hearth, breaking up hardtack into a pan.
He dumped the pan of crumbled hardtack into the boiling pot of lobscouse.
Argiento made him lie down on the bed, brought a pan of hot water, dipped some clean white linen cloth and applied it to extract the splinter.
He found the pan where he had dropped it and carried it back down to the stream.
He scrubbed absent-mindedly at the pans and reflected on how things had turned out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - deep round container for cooking, often with handles and a lid

Etymology

From Old English 'panna', of Latin origin 'panna' (bread pan, dish), possibly from a borrowing from another language.

3. VERB

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
(of a camera) to move so as to follow an object or to sweep across a scene from side to side or up and down.

Examples

After a few tortuous moments of wondering who "he "is, the camera pans across the room to the plaster statue, and we realize that Neitzbohr is trying to redeem himself in the eyes of a mute piece of sculpture.

Synset Examples

The camera panned across the room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tilt - to move a camera up or down, as opposed to side to side

Etymology

Shortened from 'panorama'; related to the use in cinematography.
