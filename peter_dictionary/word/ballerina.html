ballerina

1. NOUN

/ bæləˈrinə / (AmE), / bæləˈriːnə / (BrE)
A female ballet dancer, especially a principal or solo dancer in a ballet company.

Examples

Ask any group of ballerinas to do ten push-ups or three chin-ups and the results, considering the amount of physical training they have had, will be very disappointing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: danseur - a male ballet dancer

Etymology

From Italian 'ballerina', feminine of 'ballerino', from 'ballare' (to dance).
