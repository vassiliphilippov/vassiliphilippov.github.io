exhumation

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˌsjuːˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌsjuːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of digging up something that has been buried, especially a corpse.

Examples

Besides, he can hardly avoid musing on the instability of death which, what with exhumations and rehabilitations, seems to match that of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground

Etymology

From Latin exhumare ('to dig out of the ground'), from ex- ('out of') + humus ('ground, earth').
