supreme

1. ADJ

/ səˈprim / (AmE), / suːˈpriːm / (BrE)
Highest in rank or authority; ultimate or final, especially the last in a series or process.

Examples

At the same time that religion binds the individual helpfully to the supernatural and gives him cosmic peace and a sense of supreme fulfillment, it also has great therapeutic value for him.
The answer the authors give to it, therefore, is of supreme importance.
With a supreme effort, he broke it off.
The supreme object of their lives is now fulfilled, says the wife, her husband has achieved immortality.

Synset Examples

the supreme sacrifice
the supreme judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimate - being or happening at the end; final
  • Synonym: paramount - more important than anything else; supreme
  • Antonym: inferior - lower in rank, status, or quality

Etymology

From Latin supremus (highest, uppermost), superlative of superus (situated above).
