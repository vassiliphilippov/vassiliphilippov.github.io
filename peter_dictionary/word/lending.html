lending

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving money or property to someone with the expectation that it will be returned, usually with interest; the practice of making loans

Examples

SBA makes loans to individual small business firms, providing them with financing when it is not otherwise available through private lending sources on reasonable terms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: borrowing - the act of receiving money, property, or something else from someone, with the obligation to return it
  • Synonym: loan - a sum of money that is borrowed, often with interest to be paid

Etymology

From the verb 'lend', from Old English 'lǣnan' (to lend).
