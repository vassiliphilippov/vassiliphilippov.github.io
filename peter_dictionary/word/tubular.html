tubular

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuːbjələr / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbjʊlə / (BrE)
having the shape or form of a tube; consisting of or containing hollow tubes through which fluids can pass

Examples

Microscopically, both kidneys showed many small cortical scars in which there was glomerular and interstitial fibrosis, tubular atrophy, and an infiltration of lymphocytes and plasma cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cylindrical - having the shape of a cylinder

Etymology

From Latin 'tubulus' (a small tube), diminutive of 'tubus' (tube).
