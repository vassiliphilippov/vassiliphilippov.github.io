squashed

1. ADJ

/ skwɑʃt / (AmE), / skwɒʃt / (BrE)
(of an object or substance) pressed or crushed with force so that it becomes flat, misshapen, or compacted.

Examples

Dead fledgling birds, their squashed looking nakedness and the odor of decay that clung to the hand when they had been buried in our graveyard in front of the purple flags.

Synset Examples

the squashed looking nakedness of the fledgling birds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crushed - pressed or squeezed with force so that it is damaged or flattened

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'squash', from Middle English 'squaschen', from Old French 'esquasser'.
