deaden

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛdən / (AmE), / ˈdɛdən / (BrE)
to make something less distinct or clear, or to reduce the intensity or visibility of something (such as a sound or image)

Examples

During the night, a phone company technician had deadened the bells and installed red blinkers on the phones.

Synset Examples

muffle the message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dampen - to make less intense or strong
  • Synonym: muffle - to make a sound quieter or less distinct

Etymology

From Old English 'dǣdan', derived from 'dead' + '-en'.
