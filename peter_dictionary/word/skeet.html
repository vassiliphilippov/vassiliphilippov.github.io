skeet

1. NOUN

/ skit / (AmE), / skiÀêt / (BrE)
A sport in which participants shoot at clay targets thrown into the air to simulate the flight of birds.

Examples

Through the efforts of SAAMI's shooting development program these shooting activities, and many others, including assists in the development of public and privately financed shooting parks, trap and skeet leagues, rifle and pistol marksmanship programs have been promoted, to mention only a few.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapshooting - sport of shooting at clay targets launched from a single machine
  • Synonym: sporting clays - a variation of clay pigeon shooting simulating hunting conditions

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin, possibly related to Norwegian dialect *skyte* (to shoot).
