continuously

1. ADV

/ kənˈtɪnjuəli / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjuəsli / (BrE)

Related Terms

From 'continuous', which comes from Latin 'continuus' (uninterrupted, joined together).

/ kənˈtɪnjuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjʊəsli / (BrE)

Examples

  • Antonym: intermittently - in an irregular or sporadic manner, not continuously

Etymology

From 'continuous' + '-ly'; 'continuous' from Latin 'continuus' (uninterrupted, continuous) + '-ous'.
