isotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈtroʊ.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈtrɒp.ɪk / (BrE)
Having identical properties in all directions; not varying with direction.

Examples

An isotropic resonance shift of **f to lower applied fields indicates a weak isotropic hyperfine contact interaction.
An isotropic resonance shift of f to lower applied fields indicates a weak isotropic** hyperfine contact interaction.
Electron microscopic examination of the f sample showed it to be composed of nearly isotropic** particles about 0.3 lm in diameter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anisotropic - having properties that vary according to direction

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + tropos (way, direction), meaning 'the same in every direction'.
