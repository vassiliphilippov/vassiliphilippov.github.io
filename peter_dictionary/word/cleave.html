cleave

1. VERB

/ kliːv / (AmE), / kliːv / (BrE)
To split or cut something, especially with a sharp tool or instrument.

Examples

He heard the words: "Rock of Ages, cleft for me, Let me hide myself in Thee!

Synset Examples

cleave the bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - to divide or break into parts
  • Synonym: chop - to cut into pieces with repeated sharp blows
  • Antonym: join - to put or bring together

Etymology

Old English 'clēofan', from Proto-Germanic *kleuban.
