argument

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
A brief summary of the plot or subject of a literary work, such as a poem, play, or novel, often provided at the beginning.

Examples

Willie's big day revived the running argument about the relative merits of Mays and Mickey Mantle.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Latin argumentum (evidence, proof, topic, subject).

A statement, fact, or reason put forward as evidence or proof that something is true or valid.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
A discussion in which people express different opinions, often with reasons, for and against a particular idea or proposal.

Examples
