urbanized

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜrbəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɜːbənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an area or population) made more urban in character, typically by the growth of cities or towns and the development of infrastructure, buildings, and services.

Examples

Morrison points out that since our country is more urbanized than the Soviet Union or Red China, it is the most vulnerable of the great powers - Europe of course must be written off out of hand.
This carried over into the more urbanized late 19 th and early 20 th centuries, when the man ruled the roost in the best bull roaring Life With Father manner.

Synset Examples

the urbanized Eastern states

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rural - characteristic of the countryside rather than the town

Etymology

From 'urban' + '-ized', a suffix forming adjectives meaning 'to make or become something'; from Latin 'urbanus' (city) + '-ize'.
