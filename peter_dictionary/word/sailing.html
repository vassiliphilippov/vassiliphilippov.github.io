sailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
The sport or activity of traveling in a boat that is propelled by sails.

Examples

The two of them had developed into a remarkable sailing team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yachting - The sport or activity of sailing yachts

Etymology

From sail + -ing, denoting the act of using sails.

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or event of a ship or vessel departing from a port.

Examples

The sailing in the spring of 1610 was Hudson's fourth in four years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: departure - the act of leaving, especially from a place or starting point

Etymology

From sail + -ing; first recorded in Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity or sport of traveling by boat or ship using sails; the work or practice of operating a sailing vessel.

Examples

Even Hudson, experienced in Arctic sailing and determined as he was, must have had qualms as he slid down the Thames.
After three weeks' swift sailing, however, the ship entered an area of shallow marshes and river deltas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boating - the activity of traveling by boat
  • Synonym: navigation - the process or activity of accurately ascertaining one's position and planning and following a route

Etymology

From sail + -ing, originating in Middle English.
