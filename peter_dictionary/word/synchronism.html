synchronism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.krəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The occurrence or arrangement of events at the same time; simultaneity.

Examples

The video signal is amplified and then switched, in synchronism with the three ultraviolet light sources which are sequenced by the rotating mirror so that during one-twentieth of a second only one wavelength, corresponding to red, green, or blue, is seen.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneity - the state of occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronismos', from 'synchronos' (contemporary, simultaneous) + '-ism'.
