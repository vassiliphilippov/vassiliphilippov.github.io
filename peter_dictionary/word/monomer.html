monomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəmər / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəmə / (BrE)
A simple chemical compound whose molecules can join together to form a polymer.

Examples

(1) Below the critical micelle concentration, monomers and some dimers are present.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polymer - a large molecule made from many monomers joined together

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single, alone) and '-meros' (part).
