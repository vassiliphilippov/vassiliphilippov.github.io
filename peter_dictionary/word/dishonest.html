dishonest

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɑnɪst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnɪst / (BrE)
Not honest; likely to lie, cheat, or deceive others.

Examples

Do patriots everywhere know enough about how the persecution of the Jews in Germany and later in the occupied countries contributed to terrorizing the populations, splitting apart individuals and groups, arousing the meanest and most dishonest impulses, pulverizing trust and personal dignity, and finally forcing people to follow their masters into the abyss by making them partners in unspeakable crimes?
I'’ll do business with you, but the fact is that I despise your masquerade, the dishonest way you pose yourself.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • Synonym: fraudulent - characterized by fraud; deceitful
  • Synonym: deceptive - intended to mislead or trick

Etymology

From dis- (not) + honest, modeled after French 'malhonnête' but using the English negative prefix.
