nun

1. NOUN

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
a member of a religious community of women, typically one who lives under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience, often in a convent

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monk - a man who is a member of a religious order living under vows

Etymology

From Latin 'nonna' (tutor, nun), later adopted into Old English via Church Latin.
