walrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔl.rəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːl.rəs / (BrE)
A large marine mammal found in Arctic regions, with long ivory tusks, whiskers, and thick skin overlying tough blubber.

Examples

"The time has come ", the walrus said, "To speak of manic things, Of shots and shouts, and sealing dooms Of commoners and kings ".

Etymology

From Dutch 'walrus' or perhaps Norwegian 'hvalros', literally 'whale horse'.
