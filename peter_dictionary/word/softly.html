softly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑːftli / (AmE), / ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
In a way that is quiet or has a low volume.

Examples

Scotty murmured, "No, thanks ", so softly his father had to bend his gaunt height across the table and turn a round brown ear to him.
Scotty gazed out at ugly gray slums and said softly, "Look at those stupid kids ".
When he was asked a question or addressed in such a way that some response was inescapable, he would answer; if, as often happened, he had to repeat because he had spoken too softly, he would repeat his words in the same way, without emphasis or impatience, only a little louder.
Scotty would reply softly and his father, apologetically, would ask him to repeat.
Mose asked softly.

Synset Examples

speak softly but carry a big stick
she spoke quietly to the child
the radio was playing softly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quietly - in a quiet manner; with little noise
  • Antonym: loudly - with a lot of volume; noisily

Etymology

From Middle English soft, from Old English sōfte (gently, easily); -ly adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɔftli / (AmE), / ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
In a gentle way, with little weight or force.

Examples

The man wheezed weakly, his fetid breath beating softly against Watson's neck.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gently - in a kind or soft manner

Etymology

From Old English *sōfte* (gentle, soft) + -ly.

3. ADV

/ ˈsɔftli / (AmE), / ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
In a gentle and pleasing manner to the senses.

Examples

The girl's eyes were softly shining as she reached out and touched Casey's hand.
Softly.

Synset Examples

she smiled softly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gently - in a gentle manner
  • Antonym: harshly - in a harsh manner

Etymology

From 'soft' + '-ly'.
