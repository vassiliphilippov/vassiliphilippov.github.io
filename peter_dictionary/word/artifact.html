artifact

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtəˌfækt / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪˌfækt / (BrE)
an object made by a human being, typically of cultural or historical interest

Examples

Some areas may provide archeological values such as ancient Indian village sites or hunting areas, caves, artifacts, etcetera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artefact - alternative British spelling for 'artifact', same meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'arte' (by skill) + 'factum' (something made)
