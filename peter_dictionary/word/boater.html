boater

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊtər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊtə / (BrE)
a stiff straw hat with a flat crown and a straight brim, typically worn in summer or for rowing

Examples

He crossed the tracks to Delaware House, where ladies in gay dresses and men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah.

Etymology

From 'boat', as such hats were traditionally worn for boating and rowing in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
