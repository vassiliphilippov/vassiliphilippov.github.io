reluctant

1. ADJ

/ rəˈlʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt / (BrE)
Not willing or eager to do something; disinclined to become involved in a particular activity.

Examples

They were reluctant to appoint sheriffs to protect the property, thus running the risk of creating disturbances such as that on the Vermejo, and yet the cowboys protested that they got no salary for arresting cattle thieves and running the risk of being shot.
Bankers who had been reluctant to lend without better security than the house itself got that security from the U. S. government; householders who had been unable to pick up the burden of short-term high interest mortgages found they could borrow for twenty-five years at 4 per cent, under government aegis.

Synset Examples

they were usually reluctant to socialize
reluctant to help

Related Terms

  • Antonym: willing - ready, eager, or prepared to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'reluctari', meaning 'to struggle against'.

2. ADJ

/ rəˈlʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt / (BrE)
Unwilling and hesitant; not eager or willing to do something.

Examples

Giselle was reluctant but Alex succeeded in persuading her to come back in five minutes and the door was shut again.
Did men become perfectionists as they grew older, polishing, polishing, reluctant to let go?

Synset Examples

a reluctant smile
loath to admit a mistake

Related Terms

  • Antonym: willing - ready or prepared to do something; having no hesitation
  • Antonym: eager - showing keen interest or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: loath - unwilling or reluctant to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'reluctari' (to struggle against).

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈlʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlʌktənt / (BrE)
Unwilling and hesitant; not eager to do something.

Examples

Sleepily, as if half reluctant to break the silence, he lifted his voice: "Rhu-beb ni ice fresh rhu-beb today "!

Synset Examples

foreigners stubbornly reluctant to accept our ways
fresh from college and reluctant for the moment to marry him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: willing - ready, eager, or prepared to do something
  • Synonym: hesitant - unsure or slow in acting or speaking

Etymology

From Latin reluctari (to struggle against), from re- (against) + luctari (to struggle).
