knife

1. NOUN

/ naɪf / (AmE), / naɪf / (BrE)
An instrument consisting of a sharp-edged blade fixed in a handle, used for cutting or as a weapon.

Examples

The physical film is cut with a knife at the end of one complete sequence, and the cut edge is joined physically, by cement, to the cut edge of the beginning of the next sequence.
A knife would do.
From childhood he had known all about knives.
When the knife went into his chest, he went down at once.
There was a little blood on the hem of her dress, for the assassin had slashed Miss Harris's companion, Major Rathbone, with a knife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - short knife with a pointed and edged blade, used as a weapon
  • Synonym: scalpel - small and extremely sharp bladed instrument used in surgery
  • Antonym: fork - implement with two or more prongs used for eating or serving food

Etymology

From Old English *cnīf* (knife, dagger), of Germanic origin.
