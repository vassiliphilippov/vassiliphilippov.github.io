rectify

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to abandon a wrong or immoral course of action and adopt a better one

Examples

We had an argument, so I had to straighten him **out**.

Synset Examples

The Church reformed me
reform your conduct

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - to improve someone by removing faults or rectifying conduct

Etymology

From Latin rectificare (to make right), from rectus (right) + facere (to make).
