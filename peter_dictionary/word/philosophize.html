philosophize

1. VERB

/ fəˈlɑːsəˌfaɪz / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfaɪz / (BrE)
To reason or speculate about philosophical subjects; to think or talk about profound or abstract matters in a philosophical way.

Examples

As they waited for supper they sat by the fire, glasses in hand, while Byron philosophized as much for his own entertainment as hers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reason - to think, understand, and form judgments logically
  • Degree Lower: conjecture - to form an opinion or theory without sufficient evidence

Etymology

From French philosophaiser, from Greek philosophos (philosopher) + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
