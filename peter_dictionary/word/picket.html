picket

1. VERB

/ ˈpɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt / (BrE)
to take part in or organize a protest outside a workplace or other location, especially to persuade others not to enter during a dispute

Examples

Employes of Pawtucket's garbage and rubbish collection contractor picketed the firm's incinerator site yesterday in the second day of a strike for improved wages and working conditions.

Synset Examples

picket a business to protest the layoffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to refuse to work as a form of protest

Etymology

From French *piquet* (a pointed stake); the sense of a line of protesters dates from the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt / (BrE)
to fasten (an animal, especially a horse) with a rope or chain to a post or stake driven into the ground

Examples

The sentry's saddled horse stood picketed nearby, having been kept handy in case of need.

Synset Examples

picket the goat
