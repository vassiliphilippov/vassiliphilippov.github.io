improbable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːbəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒbəbəl / (BrE)
Not likely to be true or happen; unlikely.

Examples

Moreover, it should not be so fastidious in its growth requirements as to make production on a militarily significant scale improbable.

Synset Examples

legislation on the question is highly unlikely
an improbable event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true
  • Antonym: probable - likely to happen or be true

Etymology

From Latin 'improbabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'probabilis' (provable, likely).
