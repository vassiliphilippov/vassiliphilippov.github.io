Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊ.zəˌvɛlt, -vəlt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.zəˌvɛlt, -vəlt / (BrE)
Franklin D. Roosevelt, the 32nd President of the United States (1882–1945), noted for instituting the New Deal to combat the Great Depression and for leading the country during World War II.

Examples

The socialism implicit in the slogan of the Roosevelt Revolution, freedom from want and fear, seems a far cry from the individualism of the First Amendment to the Constitution, or of the Jacksonian frontier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Franklin D. Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States (1882–1945)
  • Synonym: Theodore Roosevelt - 26th President of the United States, distant cousin of Franklin D. Roosevelt

Etymology

American family name, ultimately of Dutch origin ('roos' meaning 'rose' and 'veld' meaning 'field').
