overhang

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɚˌhæŋ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.və.hæŋ / (BrE)
A part of something that extends or projects over and beyond another part.

Examples

If a branch extended out too far, each man held it back for the next, and if they met a low overhang, each warned the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - something that juts out from a surface

Etymology

From over- (above) + hang (to suspend), meaning something that hangs or projects over another.
