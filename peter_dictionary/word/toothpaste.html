toothpaste

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuθˌpeɪst / (AmE), / ˈtuːθpeɪst / (BrE)
A soft, often flavoured paste used in cleaning and polishing teeth.

Examples

We hold safe little jobs illustrating tooth-paste ads or the salacious incidents in trivial novels, and most of our easel painting is nothing but picking the fluff out of the navel so it can be contemplated in greater purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dentifrice - a substance used for cleaning teeth

Etymology

From 'tooth' + 'paste', first recorded in the late 19th century.
