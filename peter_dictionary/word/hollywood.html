Hollywood

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːliˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌwʊd / (BrE)
The film industry of the United States, especially as centered in and associated with the area of Hollywood in Los Angeles.

Examples

Many Hollywood films manage somehow to be authentic, but not realistic.
Almost every film bearing the imprimatur of Hollywood is physically authentic - in fact, impeccably so.
Earlier this month Edward R. Murrow, director of the United States Information Agency, came to Hollywood and had dinner with more than 100 leaders of the motion picture industry.
Yet for much of the globe, Hollywood is just that - prime, if not sole, source of knowledge.
If a man totally ignorant of America were to judge our land and its civilization based on Hollywood alone, what conclusions do you think he might come to?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showbiz - the entertainment industry, especially television, film, and theater
  • Synonym: film industry - the business of producing and distributing films

Etymology

From the district of Hollywood in Los Angeles, California, known as the center of the U.S. film industry since the early 20th century.

2. PROPN

/ ˈhɑliˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌwʊd / (BrE)
A district of Los Angeles, California, known as the historic center of the American film industry.

Examples

Rest, and in a month this Hollywood big shot will give you what you want.

Etymology

Named after 'holly' (the shrub) and 'wood' by early developers in the late 19th century.
