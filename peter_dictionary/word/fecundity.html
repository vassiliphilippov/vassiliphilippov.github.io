fecundity

1. NOUN

/ fəˈkʌndɪti, fiˈkʌndɪti / (AmE), / fɪˈkʌndɪti / (BrE)
the ability to produce many new and original ideas; intellectual productivity or creative power

Examples

Why did Prokofieff expand in stature and fecundity, while Stravinsky (who leaped into fame like a young giant) dwindled in stature and fruitfulness?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertility - the ability to produce abundantly, especially offspring or vegetation
  • Antonym: sterility - lack of ability to produce offspring or ideas; barrenness

Etymology

From Latin 'fecunditas' (fruitfulness), from 'fecundus' (fruitful).
