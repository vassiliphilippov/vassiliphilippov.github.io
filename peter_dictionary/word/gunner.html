gunner

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnə / (BrE)
A member of the military who operates large guns, especially in an artillery unit or on a military aircraft or ship.

Examples

The voice was that of Johnson, tail gunner off another crew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artilleryman - a soldier who serves in the artillery

Etymology

From Middle English gunnere, from gun, influenced by 'gunne' for cannon or fire-arm.
