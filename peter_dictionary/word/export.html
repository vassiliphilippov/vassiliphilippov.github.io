export

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spɔːt / (BrE)
goods or services sold and sent to another country

Examples

It could stop Cuban re export of guns and propaganda materials to South America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: import - goods or services bought from another country

Etymology

From Latin 'exportare' (to carry out).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɔrt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to send goods or services to another country for sale

Examples

They export also.
They export cotton ginning machinery.
About half of these are exported.
If they have trouble exporting, international bill for their support will grow larger than it otherwise would.

Synset Examples

we export less than we import and have a negative trade balance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: import - to bring goods or services into a country from abroad for sale

Etymology

From Latin 'exportare', from 'ex-' (out) + 'portare' (to carry).
