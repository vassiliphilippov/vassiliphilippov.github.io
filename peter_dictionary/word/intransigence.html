intransigence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrænzɪdʒəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəns / (BrE)
the refusal to change one's views or to agree about something; stubborn unwillingness to compromise

Examples

Or the city can be a graveyard monument to Western intransigence, if that is what the West wants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being determined not to change one's attitude or position
  • Antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or compromise

Etymology

From French intransigeance, from Spanish intransigente, from Latin in- (not) + transigere (to come to an agreement).
