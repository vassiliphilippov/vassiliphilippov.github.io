suggestive

1. ADJ

/ səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
tending to suggest or imply something rather than stating it directly

Examples

It is, however, highly suggestive and agrees well with our own findings in which we also failed to demonstrate normally occurring bronchial artery pulmonary artery shunts in certain species, especially the dog.

Synset Examples

artifacts suggestive of an ancient society
an implicative statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implicative - tending to imply or suggest

Etymology

From Latin 'suggestivus' (prompting, suggestive), from 'suggerere' (to suggest).
