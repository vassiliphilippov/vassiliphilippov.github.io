refinement

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
a small improvement or modification made to something, especially to increase its quality or effectiveness

Examples

A refinement of this technique has been described by Drs. Zworykin and Farrar and Mr. Berkely of the Medical Electronics Center of the Rockefeller Institute.

Synset Examples

he described a refinement of this technique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the process of making something better

Etymology

From French *raffinement*, from raffiner (to refine), from Old French *refiner*, based on Latin *refinare* ('re-' expressing intensive force + 'finare' to make fine).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the quality of being highly developed, polished, or perfected; elegance and sophistication in manner, style, or workmanship

Examples

There is evidence to suggest, in fact, that many authors of the humorous sketches were prompted to write them - or to make them as indelicate as they are - by way of protesting against the artificial refinements which had come to dominate the polite letters of the South.

Synset Examples

they performed with great polish
I admired the exquisite refinement of his prose
almost an inspiration which gives to all work that finish which is almost art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - a high degree of refinement, smoothness, or elegance
  • Antonym: crudeness - the quality of being rough, lacking sophistication or finish

Etymology

From French 'raffinement', from raffiner meaning 'to refine'.
