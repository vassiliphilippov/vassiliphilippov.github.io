indecision

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
The inability to make a decision quickly due to uncertainty between alternatives.

Examples

This is given some expression in Beardsley's notion of harmony and the resolution of indecision.

Synset Examples

his indecision was only momentary but the opportunity was lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitation - pause or delay due to uncertainty
  • Antonym: decisiveness - ability to make decisions quickly and firmly

Etymology

From Latin indecisionem, from in- (not) + decisionem (decision).
