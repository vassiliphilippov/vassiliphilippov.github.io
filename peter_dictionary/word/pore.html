pore

1. NOUN

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
A tiny opening in the skin or outer surface of an animal through which substances such as sweat or oil can pass.

Examples

No, other than cleaning out the pores and making the sweat glands work harder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follicle - a small cavity or sac in the skin, such as a hair follicle

Etymology

From Latin 'porus', from Greek 'poros' meaning passage or pore.

2. NOUN

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
A very small hole in a surface, especially one that allows liquid or gas to pass through.

Examples

The graphite was National Carbon NC 60, which has a porosity of 50 % and an average pore size of 30 This small pore size was required to ensure uniformity of the flow leaving the anode.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'porus' (a passage, pore), from Ancient Greek 'poros' (passage, pore).

3. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to read or examine something carefully and attentively, especially for a long time

Examples

Maggie and Stuart pored over figures every night, trying to find how they could squeeze out a few pennies more.

Synset Examples

Please focus on your studies and not on your hobbies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - to apply oneself to learning or knowledge
  • Antonym: scan - to look over something quickly rather than intently

Etymology

Probably from Old French *porer* (to peer at), of uncertain origin.
