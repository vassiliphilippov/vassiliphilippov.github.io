muted

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjutɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪd / (BrE)
(of sound, color, or expression) made softer or less intense; not loud, bright, or forceful.

Examples

The doctor's mind was working at a great speed; he rose to put his greatcoat on and addressed Alex in a muted voice.

Synset Examples

hushed voices
muted trumpets
a subdued whisper
a quiet reprimand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hushed - quiet or silenced, especially so as not to be heard
  • Synonym: subdued - softened or restrained in force, effect, or color
  • Antonym: loud - producing a great deal of noise

Etymology

Past participle of 'mute', from Latin 'mutus' (silent).
