concretely

1. ADV

/ kənˈkriːtli / (AmE), / kənˈkriːtli / (BrE)
In a clear, specific, and definite way; in terms that are concrete or material rather than abstract.

Examples

This meant, concretely, that the patient could not read at all without making writing like movements of the head or body, became easily confused by "hasher marks "inserted between hand-written words and thus confused the mark for one of the letters, and could recognize a simple straight line or a curved one only by tracing it.

Synset Examples

concretely, this meant that he was broke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstractly - In a way that is theoretical or not concrete; in terms that are abstract

Etymology

From 'concrete' + '-ly', with 'concrete' from Latin 'concretus' (grown together, solid).
