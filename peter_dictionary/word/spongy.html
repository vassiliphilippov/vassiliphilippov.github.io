spongy

1. ADJ

/ ˈspʌndʒi / (AmE), / ˈspʌndʒi / (BrE)
soft and easily squashed; having a texture like a sponge, with pores or spaces that make it compressible

Examples

Arnold's iron shot from the tee burrowed into the bunker guarding the green, an embankment that had become soft and spongy from the rains, thereby bringing local rules into force.
His soil was "nothing special ", just prairie land, but he had harrowed in compost until it was loose, spongy and brown black.

Synset Examples

spongy bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soggy - wet and soft
  • Synonym: porous - having small holes that let substances pass through

Etymology

From sponge + -y, meaning 'like a sponge'.
