exalted

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɔltɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːltɪd / (BrE)
(of a person, idea, or style) regarded as having high moral, intellectual, or artistic value; elevated in nature or tone.

Examples

Even Rector himself was prey to this spirit of competition and he knew it, not for a more exalted office in the hierarchy of the church - his ambitions for the bishopry had died very early in his career - but for the one clear victory he had talked about to the colonel.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - of imposing height; of a high moral or intellectual level
  • Synonym: noble - having or showing fine personal qualities or high moral principles
  • Antonym: base - lacking moral principles; ignoble

Etymology

From Latin 'exaltare' meaning 'to raise high'.
