shine

1. VERB

/ ʃaɪn / (AmE), / ʃaɪn / (BrE)
to appear bright or glossy, as if wet or polished; to glisten or glitter

Examples

Directed by Grigory Kozintsev in a tempo that is studiously slow, he develops a sense of a high tradition shining brightly and passing gravely through an impious world.
The great spire shone as if the lightning had polished it.
He could n't see the grin that split her mouth; the teeth that shone into the phone were like a shark's.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glisten - to shine with a sparkling light, especially as if something wet
  • Synonym: glitter - to shine brightly with reflected light
  • Synonym: gleam - to shine softly or briefly

Etymology

Old English *scīnan* (to emit light, be radiant), related to German *scheinen*, Dutch *schijnen*.

2. VERB

/ ʃaɪn / (AmE), / ʃaɪn / (BrE)
to be bright by reflecting or casting light

Examples

Each brass handle and hinge shone for his reward, and he knew how to get at the dust in the china flowers and how to take down the long glass drops which hung from the chandelier.
Well, what did that matter when the sun was shining and there were dreams to dream about?
The large municipal hall was ablaze with color, which shown out from the bright array of chic ballgowns worn by those participating in the "maskers' dances ".
Mercer saw the sun rise on occasion, closed his eyes briefly, and opened them to see stars shining.

Synset Examples

Drive carefully--the wet road reflects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or intermittent light
  • Synonym: glow - to give out a steady light
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny; lacking brightness

Etymology

Old English *scīnan* (to emit rays of light).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - shine with a steady but subdued light
  • Synonym: glow - give out steady light without flame
  • Antonym: dim - to become or make less bright

Etymology

Old English *scīnan* (to emit light, be radiant), from Proto-Germanic *skīnaną*.

4. VERB

/ ʃaɪn / (AmE), / ʃaɪn / (BrE)
(of the face or eyes) to appear bright with a healthy color or emotion, such as excitement or happiness.

Examples

The girl's eyes were softly shining as she reached out and touched Casey's hand.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - (of the face) to appear red or warm with health or emotion
  • Degree Lower: blush - (of the face) to become redder, typically from embarrassment or emotion

Etymology

Old English *scīnan* (to emit rays, be radiant), of Germanic origin.
