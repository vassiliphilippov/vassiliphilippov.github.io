accusative

1. ADJ

/ əˈkjuːzətɪv / (AmE), / əˈkjuːzətɪv / (BrE)
(of a grammatical case) denoting the object of a verb or certain prepositions.

Examples

At this moment I feel no love for you at all.

Synset Examples

objective case
accusative endings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nominative - denoting the case used for a noun or pronoun which is the subject of a verb
  • Degree Lower: dative - denoting the case used for a noun or pronoun which is the indirect object
  • Synonym: objective - relating to the case of a noun or pronoun that is the object of a verb or preposition in English grammar

Etymology

From Latin accusativus, from accusare (to accuse), used by Roman grammarians to translate Greek ‘aitiatikē’ (the case of that which is caused).
