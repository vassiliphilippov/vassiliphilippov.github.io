expansiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being friendly, open, and willing to talk; effusiveness

Examples

There is also the possibility, among higher religions, of experiencing consistent meaning in life and enjoying guidance and expansiveness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reserve - quality of being restrained or not openly expressive
  • Synonym: effusiveness - the quality of expressing feelings in an unrestrained or heartfelt way

Etymology

From 'expansive' + '-ness', with 'expansive' from Latin 'expandere' (to spread out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being expansive; largeness of scale, scope, or extent, or a tendency to expand.

Examples

Out of the church and into his big car, it tooling over the road with him driving and the headlights sweeping the pike ahead and after he hit college, his expansiveness, the quaint little pine board tourist courts, cabins really, with a cute naked light bulb in the ceiling (unfrosted and naked as a streetlight, like the one on the corner where you used to play when you were a kid, where you watched the bats swooping in after the bugs, watching in between your bouts at hopscotch), a room complete with moths pinging the light and the few casual cockroaches cruising the walls, an insect Highway Patrol with feelers waving.

Synset Examples

the expansiveness of their extravagant life style was soon curtailed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expansion - the act or process of becoming larger or more extensive
  • Antonym: contractiveness - the quality of becoming smaller or more limited

Etymology

From expansive + -ness; 'expansive' from Latin 'expandere' (to spread out).
