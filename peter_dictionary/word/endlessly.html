endlessly

1. ADV

/ ˈɛndləsli / (AmE), / ˈɛndləsli / (BrE)
In a way that continues forever without ever stopping; infinitely or without limit.

Examples

Cause of all this commotion: squat, pug-nosed, balding, hopelessly ugly Jean-Pierre Bravado, a Bogartian figure, who plays a sadistic, amoral, philosophic Tasti-Freeze salesman in old New-Waver Fredrico de Mille Rossilini's endlessly provocative film, A Sour Sponge.
And the valley stretched endlessly out ahead, scorched and baked and writhing in its heat, until it vanished into the throbbing wall of fiery orange brown haze.

Synset Examples

there are infinitely many possibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinitely - without limits or end
  • Synonym: ceaselessly - without stopping; constantly
  • Synonym: eternally - existing or continuing forever
  • Antonym: finitely - having limits; with an end

Etymology

From 'endless' + '-ly', forming the adverb from the adjective 'endless'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛndləsli / (AmE), / ˈɛndləsli / (BrE)
With persistent determination; in a manner that does not give up or tire.

Examples

Otherwise, we go on endlessly trying to draw the line, color and other, as to which kind of man we wish to see dominate.

Synset Examples

dance inspires him ceaselessly to strive higher and higher toward the shining pinnacle of perfection that is the goal of every artiste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceaselessly - without stopping; with unflagging resolve
  • Synonym: persistently - in a determined, continual way

Etymology

From 'endless' + '-ly', where 'endless' is from Old English 'endeleas' (having no end).
