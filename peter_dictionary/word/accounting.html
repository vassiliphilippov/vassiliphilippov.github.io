accounting

1. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of recording, analyzing, and reporting financial transactions and information for an organization or individual.

Examples

Accounting procedures which continue to charge a disproportionate overhead and warehouse expense to the main store make the branches seem more profitable than they are.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookkeeping - the recording of financial transactions
  • Synonym: auditing - examination of financial records

Etymology

From 'account' + '-ing', with 'account' from Old French 'aconter', from Latin 'computare' (to calculate).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
The profession or activity of keeping financial records, auditing accounts, and preparing financial statements for a business or organization.

Examples

Have the insurance company or your own accounting department break down the cost of your insurance package periodically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountancy - the profession of accounting

Etymology

From the verb 'account' + -ing, originally from Old French 'acompter,' based on Latin 'computare' (to calculate).

3. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntɪŋ / (BrE)
A clear and convincing explanation or justification, often revealing the underlying causes of something.

Examples

Only across a distance of time could the epic accounting begin.

Synset Examples

he was unable to give a clear accounting for his actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - statement that makes something clear or understandable
  • Synonym: justification - reason given to explain or defend something

Etymology

From account + -ing.
