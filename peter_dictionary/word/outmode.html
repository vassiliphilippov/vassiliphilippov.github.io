outmode

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈmoʊd / (AmE), / aʊtˈməʊd / (BrE)
to make something unfashionable, outdated, or obsolete; to cause to fall out of use or favor

Examples

Just as modern transportation has outmoded the early Studebaker covered wagon, the demand of today's sportsmen and women has necessitated changes in their equipment.

Synset Examples

Modern ways of cooking have outmoded the hearth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date
  • Antonym: update - to bring up to date; modernize

Etymology

From prefix 'out-' + 'mode' (meaning manner, fashion)
