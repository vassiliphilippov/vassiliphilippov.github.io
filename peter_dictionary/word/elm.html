elm

1. NOUN

/ ɛlm / (AmE), / ɛlm / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree of the genus Ulmus, with serrated leaves and often used for timber or shade.

Examples

Maple and ash, beech and elm, one hundred to win on Three in the fourth, fifty to win on Six in the third, one hundred to win on Two in the eighth.

Etymology

Old English 'elm', from Proto-Germanic *elmaz.
