advertise

1. VERB

/ ˈæd.vɚˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.taɪz / (BrE)
To make something known or draw attention to it, often publicly or through promotion.

Examples

In the household market, there are also low-sudsing detergent formulations based on nonionic actives with about the same amount of phosphate builder; light-duty synthetic detergents with much less builder; and the dwindling built soap powders as well as soap flakes and granules, none of which are now nationally advertised.

Synset Examples

Please don't advertise the fact that he has AIDS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicize - to make something widely known to the public

Etymology

From Latin 'advertere' (to turn toward), via Old French 'advertir' and Middle English 'advertisen'.
