antagonize

1. VERB

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to become hostile or unfriendly; to provoke hostility in someone

Examples

He seemed to antagonize everyone.

Synset Examples

Don't antagonize your boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke - to stimulate or give rise to a reaction or emotion
  • Antonym: appease - to pacify or placate by acceding to demands

Etymology

From Greek antagōnizesthai, from anti- (against) and agōnizesthai (to contend).
