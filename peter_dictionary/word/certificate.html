certificate

1. NOUN

/ sərˈtɪf.ɪ.kət / (AmE), / səˈtɪf.ɪ.kət / (BrE)
An official document that states or confirms certain facts, such as completion of an achievement, a qualification, or the truth of a statement.

Examples

Instead, retailers would sign a certificate of correctness, violation of which would carry a penalty of one to five years in prison, plus a $ 1000 fine.
"I 'll give you a medical certificate, framed, if you like ", Miss Ada had said.
On the second voyage, he had turned back at the frozen island of Novaya Zemlya and meekly given the crew a certificate stating that he did so of his own free will - which was obviously not the case.
He had all the proper ID, driver's license, birth certificate, social security card.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diploma - a document conferring a qualification, such as a degree
  • Synonym: license - an official document giving permission to do something
  • Synonym: deed - a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially one regarding ownership of property

Etymology

From Old French certificat, from Medieval Latin certificatum (a certified document), from Latin certificare (to certify).
