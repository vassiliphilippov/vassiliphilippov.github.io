beech

1. NOUN

/ bitʃ / (AmE), / biːtʃ / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree with smooth grey bark, spreading branches, and small edible triangular nuts, native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

Examples

But the shelter is as much a part of my landscape as the beech and horse-chestnut trees that grow on the ridge.
Maple and ash, beech and elm, one hundred to win on Three in the fourth, fifty to win on Six in the third, one hundred to win on Two in the eighth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a large deciduous tree with lobed leaves and acorns
  • Synonym: birch - a slender tree with peeling bark, of temperate regions

Etymology

Old English *bēce*, from Proto-Germanic *bōkō*; related to German *Buche*, Dutch *beuk*.
