crisp

1. ADJ

/ krɪsp / (AmE), / krɪsp / (BrE)
(of images, sounds, or textures) clearly defined, sharp, and distinct.

Examples

They come in crisp basket weaves in natural honey hues, along with lacy open weaves with a lustre finish in natural, white, black and a whole range of colors.
At the left is a pair of dressy straw pumps in a light, but crisp texture.
He also drew precise crisp spots, which he sold to various literary and artistic journals, The New Yorker, for instance, or Esquire.

Synset Examples

a sharp photographic image
the sharp crack of a twig
the crisp snap of dry leaves underfoot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp - clearly defined in outline or detail

Etymology

From Old English *crisp* (curly, wavy), from Latin *crispus* (curled).

2. NOUN

/ krɪsp / (AmE), / krɪsp / (BrE)
(British) A thin, crisp slice of potato that has been deep-fried and eaten as a snack; a potato chip.

Examples

"I might have starved, but at least I would n't be fried to a crisp and soaked with dirt "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato chip - thin slice of potato fried until crisp (American English)
