juice

1. NOUN

/ dʒus / (AmE), / dʒuːs / (BrE)
the liquid that can be extracted from plant or animal tissue, typically by squeezing or cooking

Examples

This was a broth of a boy, our Felix, and nothing was more obvious than the joy he took in demonstrating how agile he was and how full of juice and spirit.
That's not getting all the juice out of the orange, now is it "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broth - liquid in which meat, fish, or vegetables have been cooked
  • Synonym: sap - fluid, chiefly water with dissolved sugars and mineral salts, that circulates in a plant

Etymology

From Old French jus, from Latin jus (broth, soup, juice).
