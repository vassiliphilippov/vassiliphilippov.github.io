boatswain

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊsən / (AmE), / ˈbəʊs(ə)n / (BrE)
A ship's officer in charge of equipment and the crew who handle it, especially on the deck of a vessel.

Examples

The important result, however, was that Juet and Francis Clemens, the deposed boatswain, became Hudson's sworn enemies.
Only Cromwell, the giant boatswain, was mild-mannered and respectful.
The boatswain was as guilty as any.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bosun - alternative, informal spelling of 'boatswain'

Etymology

From Middle English 'boswayne', from Old English 'bat' (boat) + Old Norse 'sveinn' (boy, servant).
