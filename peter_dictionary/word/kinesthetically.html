kinesthetically

1. ADV

/ kɪnəsˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / kɪnɛsˈθɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to the sensation of body movement or position; by means of kinesthesia.

Examples

If the patient can perceive figure kinesthetically when he cannot perceive it visually, then, it would seem, the sense of touch has immediate contact with the spatial aspects of things in independence of visual representations, at least in regard to two dimensions, and, as we shall see, even this much spatial awareness on the part of unaided touch is denied by the authors.

Synset Examples

he can perceive shapes kinesthetically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinesthetic - relating to the sensation of movement or position in the body

Etymology

From 'kinesthetic' + '-ally'. 'Kinesthetic' from Greek 'kinesis' (movement) + '-esthetic' (relating to sensation).
