streaked

1. ADJ

/ stɹikt / (AmE), / stɹiːkt / (BrE)
Marked with stripes or long, thin lines, especially of a different color.

Examples

She said without turning her head, "After that rain beating in atop the dust, there is n't a thing that won n't be streaked ".

Synset Examples

streaked hair
fat legs and dirty streaky faces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streaky - marked with streaks or irregular lines

Etymology

From the verb 'streak' (to mark with lines), which derives from Middle English 'streken' (to go, to stretch) and Old English 'strīcan' (to stroke, to go).
