irrationally

1. ADV

/ ɪˈræʃənəli / (AmE), / ɪˈræʃənəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is not based on reason or logic.

Examples

Many Americans reacted irrationally to the challenge of Russia and turned to the repression of ideas by force.

Synset Examples

they acted irrationally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rationally - in a logical or reasonable manner

Etymology

Formed from 'irrational' + '-ly'; 'irrational' from Latin irrationalis (not endowed with reason).
