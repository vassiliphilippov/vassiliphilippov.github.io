seismograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪzməˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzməˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
An instrument that detects and measures the intensity, direction, and duration of ground movements, especially those caused by earthquakes.

Examples

This center also receives prompt reports on earthquakes from four Coast Survey stations in the Pacific which are equipped with seismographs.
At 17: 07 that afternoon (Greenwich time) the shock was recorded by the seismograph alarm in Honolulu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seismometer - an instrument for measuring ground motion, often used interchangeably with seismograph

Etymology

From Greek 'seismos' (earthquake) + '-graph' (writing, recording).
