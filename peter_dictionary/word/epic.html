epic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk / (BrE)
a long narrative poem, typically recounting the deeds of a legendary or historical hero

Examples

At the least, however, one may conclude that Geometric potters sensed a logical order; their principles of composition stand very close to those which appear in the Homeric epics and the hexameter line.
We shall not be able entirely to pass over these connections to the East as we consider Ripe Geometric pottery, the epic and the myth, and the religious evolution of early Greece; the important point, however, is that these magnificent achievements, unlike those of later decades, were only incidentally influenced by Oriental models.
His next major work, completed in 1892, was a long fantastic epic in prose, entitled Hans Alienus, which Professor Bo ^ o ^ k describes as a monument on the grave of his carefree and indolent youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saga - a long, involved story, especially a traditional tale of heroic exploits

Etymology

From Latin epicus, from Ancient Greek epikos (pertaining to a poem or song), from epos (word, story, poem).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or suggestive of a literary epic, especially in scope or style.

Examples

Though it is not easy to apply the evidence of the Iliad to any specific era, this marvelous product of the epic tradition had certainly taken definitive shape by 750.

Synset Examples

epic tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroic - relating to heroes or heroism, especially as in epic poetry

Etymology

From Latin 'epicus', from Ancient Greek 'epikos' (of or for a word, song, or poem), from 'epos' (word, story, poem).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛpɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk / (BrE)
Very impressive or remarkable, especially in size, scope, or achievement; surpassing the ordinary.

Examples

Only across a distance of time could the epic accounting begin.
This epic effort to secure seed for the colony cost Selkirk 1040.

Synset Examples

an epic voyage
of heroic proportions
heroic sculpture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroic - having the characteristics of a hero or being highly impressive
  • Synonym: grand - magnificent or very large in scale

Etymology

From Latin *epicus*, from Greek *epikos* (of or for epic poetry).
