starboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːrbərd / (AmE), / ˈstɑːbəd / (BrE)
the right side of a ship or aircraft as seen by someone on board and facing the front (bow or nose)

Examples

To starboard was a cape a thousand feet high, patched with ice and snow, populated by thousands of screaming sea birds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: port - the left side of a ship or aircraft as seen by someone facing the front

Etymology

From Old English 'stēorbord', from 'stēoran' (to steer) + 'bord' (side of a ship).
