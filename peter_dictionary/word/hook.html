hook

1. VERB

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
(in golf or other sports) to hit a ball so that it curves sharply to the left (for a right-handed player), usually due to spin.

Examples

He hooked the next two out of bounds on the opposite side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slice - hit a ball so that it curves sharply to the right (in golf, for a right-handed player)

2. NOUN

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
A curved piece of metal or other material used to catch, hold, or latch something, especially for locking a door.

Examples

There was no lock on the door, only an iron hook which he unfastened.

Etymology

Old English hoc (hook, angled piece of metal), related to Old Norse hok and Middle Dutch hoke.

3. VERB

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
To fasten or secure with a hook or to attach by means of a hook.

Examples

Curt followed, reaching behind him to shut the door and hook it.
Frank followed her into the bedroom, hooked her dress up the back.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unhook - to remove from a hook or to detach something from a hook

Etymology

Old English *hoc* (hook), of Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
(informal) To cheat or overcharge someone; to ask for an unreasonable price.

Examples

' City Hooked for $ 172000 "'?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip off - to cheat someone, especially financially
  • Antonym: undercharge - to charge less than the correct price

5. VERB

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
To make needlework, such as crochet or braid rugs, by interlocking and looping thread or yarn with a hooked needle.

Examples

Then again, there's always that lovely old pastime of hooking or braiding rugs.

Synset Examples

She sat there crocheting all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crochet - to make needlework by interlocking loops with a hooked needle

6. NOUN

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
a curved or bent piece of metal or other durable material used for suspending, catching, or pulling things

Examples

Tom, can you get me off the hook?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clasp - a fastening device with a bent part holding something together
  • Synonym: hanger - an object from which something is hung
  • Antonym: eyelet - a small hole or loop for securing a hook

Etymology

Old English *hōc* (hook), of Germanic origin.
