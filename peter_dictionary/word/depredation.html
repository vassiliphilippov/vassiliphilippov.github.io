depredation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
an act or instance of attacking, plundering, or destroying; the act of preying upon or ravaging.

Examples

Word reached the company that the man behind these depredations was Manuel Gonzales, a man with many followers, including a number who were kept in line through fear of him.
As you 've doubtless forgotten the circumstances in the press of more recent depredations, permit me to recapitulate them briefly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundering - the act of stealing goods, typically by force during wartime or civil disorder
  • Synonym: pillage - the act of looting or plundering, especially in war

Etymology

From Latin 'depraedatio' (a plundering), from 'depraedari' (to plunder).
