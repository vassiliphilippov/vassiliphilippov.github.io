assassin

1. NOUN

/ əˈsæs.ɪn / (AmE), / əˈsæs.ɪn / (BrE)
A person who murders an important or prominent figure, usually for political or religious reasons, often by surprise or stealth and sometimes for payment.

Examples

Finding him dead, Cook caught Russell's horse and rode to the cattle foreman's house to report the incident and request bloodhounds to trail the assassins.
There was a little blood on the hem of her dress, for the assassin had slashed Miss Harris's companion, Major Rathbone, with a knife.
How was he to suspect that an assassin had been lurking somewhere across the street waiting for just such a chance?
Privately, he created and magnified an image of himself as a hired assassin.
Ross had no intention of searching for the assassin.

Synset Examples

his assassins were hunted down like animals
assassinators of kings and emperors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killer - a person or thing that kills
  • Synonym: murderer - someone who commits murder

Etymology

From Arabic ḥashshāshīn, name for members of a medieval sect reputed for political murders; entered European languages via French and Italian.
