cusp

1. NOUN

/ kʌsp / (AmE), / kʌsp / (BrE)
A point or pointed end formed by the intersection of two arcs, especially as seen in the design of Gothic arches or similar architectural features.

Examples

Their meditations were approaching a violent cusp that would shape Martian art for many millennia.

Etymology

From Latin 'cuspis' (point, tip).
