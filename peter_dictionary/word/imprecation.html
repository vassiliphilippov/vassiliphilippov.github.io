imprecation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.prəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A spoken curse or the act of invoking evil upon someone.

Examples

For example, a writer in a recent number of The Queen hyperbolically states that "of the myriad imprecations the only one which the English Catholics really resent is the suggestion that they are' un English "'.

Synset Examples

he suffered the imprecations of the mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke a supernatural power to inflict harm or punishment

Etymology

From Latin imprecātiō (a calling down/curse), from imprecari (to invoke, curse).
