senseless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnsləs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsləs / (BrE)
(of an action, behavior, etc.) lacking good sense or reason; absurd or pointless.

Examples

"Argiento, this is senseless ", he complained, not liking to work on the wet floors, particularly in cold weather.
This was senseless - he had no idea what to look for.

Synset Examples

mindless violence
reasonless hostility
a senseless act

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reasonable - guided by reason or logic
  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable

Etymology

From Middle English 'senseles', from 'sense' + '-less'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnsləs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsləs / (BrE)
Temporarily unable to feel or respond to stimulation; unconscious or insensible.

Examples

There was no way to know, no way to guess whether any one of them was surprised at Sarah's appearance, believing her to be drugged and senseless - and just possibly dead.

Synset Examples

he lay insensible where he had fallen
drugged and senseless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not awake; unable to respond to stimuli
  • Synonym: insensible - without feeling or awareness; unconscious
  • Antonym: conscious - aware of and able to respond to one's surroundings
