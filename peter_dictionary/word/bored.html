bored

1. ADJ

/ bɔrd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
feeling weary and impatient because one is unoccupied or lacks interest in one's current activity

Examples

But he was not bored at all; he had found his style quite early in his career and he thought it quite wonderful that the world admired it, and he could not imagine why he should alter it.

Synset Examples

bored with life
strolled through the museum with a bored air

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interested - showing curiosity or attention
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment
  • Synonym: weary - feeling or showing tiredness

Etymology

Past participle of 'bore', from Old French 'bourrer' (to annoy, to bore), with figurative sense developing in English in the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ bɔrd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
Feeling uninterested or weary because of frequent exposure to or overindulgence in something.

Examples

Could it be that it speeded up for the aged in some mysterious way, as if a bored universe were skipping through the end of the chapter just to get it over with?

Synset Examples

his blase indifference
a petulant blase air
the bored gaze of the successful film star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blase - unimpressed or indifferent due to overexposure or excess
  • Antonym: interested - showing curiosity or concern about something
