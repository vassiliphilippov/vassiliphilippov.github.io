expressiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsprɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of conveying thoughts, feelings, or ideas effectively; the ability to express emotion or meaning clearly

Examples

In method as well as in theme this little anecdote with its details selected as much for expressiveness and allegory as for "realism ", anticipates a kind of musical composition, as well as a kind of fictional composition, in which, as Leverku^hn says, "there shall be nothing unthematic ".
Such speech differences made him acutely aware of the richness and expressivness of language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the act of conveying thoughts or feelings; a word or phrase used to convey an idea
  • Antonym: inexpressiveness - lack of ability to convey emotion or meaning

Etymology

From 'expressive' + '-ness', where 'expressive' derives from Latin 'expressus' (clearly presented).
