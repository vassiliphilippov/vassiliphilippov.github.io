bony

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊni / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni / (BrE)
(of a person or part of the body) very thin, with bones that are prominent, especially as a result of illness, hunger, or cold.

Examples

He treats this deep-eyed, bearded, bony crackpot with tangible affection and respect.
Occasionally if I pushed him too far he 'd give me a look out of narrowed eyes and the hard cruel bony skull would show through that smooth face of his.

Synset Examples

emaciated bony hands
a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially due to illness or lack of food
  • Synonym: skeletal - so thin that the bones show through the skin
  • Antonym: plump - having a full, rounded shape; slightly fat
