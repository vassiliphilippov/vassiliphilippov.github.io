overgrown

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈɡroʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) covered with plants that have grown in an uncontrolled way or to excessive size.

Examples

In the Delaware River, three long islands were overgrown with greening trees and underbrush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild - (of plants) growing without cultivation or control

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'grown', past participle of 'grow'.
