conformity

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔːrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmɪti / (BrE)
the state of being in agreement or correspondence in form, style, or appearance with something else

Examples

The accelerated pace of metropolitan changes has accentuated the drive to conformity in congregations of the major denominations.
This conformity represents a desperate attempt to stabilize a hopelessly unstable environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniformity - the quality or state of being uniform; sameness

Etymology

From Latin conformitas, from conformis (of like shape).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the practice of following accepted beliefs or standards, especially in religion, morals, or philosophy

Examples

An understanding of the new role of residential association in an industrial society serves to illuminate the forces which have fashioned the iron cage of conformity which imprisons the churches in their suburban captivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformity - failure or refusal to follow accepted beliefs or standards
  • Synonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice

Etymology

From Late Latin conformitas (similarity, correspondence), from Latin conformare (to shape, form).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the practice of behaving or thinking in accordance with accepted rules or standards

Examples

The parent was asked to describe the child's typical behavior in certain standard situations in which there was an opportunity to observe tendencies toward perfectionism in demands upon self and others, irrational conformity to rules, orderliness, punctuality, and need for certainty.
Children scoring high in compulsivity were those who gave evidence of tension or emotionality in situations where there was lack of organization or conformity to standards and expectations, or who made exaggerated efforts to achieve these goals.

Synset Examples

their financial statements are in conformity with generally accepted accounting practices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformity - refusal or failure to conform to accepted rules or standards
  • Synonym: compliance - the action of complying with a command or wish

Etymology

From Latin conformitas, from conformis (of like form), from conformare (to shape to).
