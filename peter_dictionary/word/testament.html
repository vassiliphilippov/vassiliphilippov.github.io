testament

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
a legal document declaring a person's wishes regarding the disposal of their property after death; especially used in the phrase 'last will and testament'

Examples

He made a will and last testament before we left Paterson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will - a legal document giving instructions as to what should be done with one's money and property after death

Etymology

From Latin testamentum (a will, testament), from testari (to testify, to bear witness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
A statement or profession of a person's beliefs, principles, or convictions.

Examples

The contributors to this testament were all well-known:
But it is the need to undertake these testaments that I would submit here as symptom of the common man's malaise.

Synset Examples

he stated his political testament

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - A formal statement or declaration, especially in court; evidence given as a witness

Etymology

From Latin testamentum (a will, testament), from testari (to be a witness).
