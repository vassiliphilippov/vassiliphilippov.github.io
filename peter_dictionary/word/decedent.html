decedent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsidənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːdnt / (BrE)
A person who has died, especially one whose estate is being handled in legal or financial proceedings.

Examples

If a taxpayer dies, the executor, administrator, or legal representative must file the final return for the decedent on or before the 15 th day of the 4 th month following the close of the deceased taxpayer's normal tax year.
The return for a decedent may also serve as a claim for refund of an overpayment of tax.

Synset Examples

I wonder what the dead person would have done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceased - a person who has died
  • Synonym: testator - a person who has made a will

Etymology

From Latin decedens, decedentis (departing), present participle of decedere (to depart, to die).
