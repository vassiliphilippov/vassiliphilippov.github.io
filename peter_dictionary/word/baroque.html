baroque

1. ADJ

/ bəˈroʊk / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊk / (BrE)
characterized by elaborate and intricate decoration or ornamentation, typically in a symmetrical or flamboyant style, especially related to art and architecture of the 17th and early 18th centuries.

Examples

Inside, as soon as Mr. Skopas had disclosed - in a hoarse whisper - the detective's errand, his family gathered in a huddle, forming a mass of dark flesh on and around a brocaded sofa which stood at one side of a baroque fireplace.

Synset Examples

the building...frantically baroque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated
  • Antonym: simple - free from elaborate ornamentation or decoration

Etymology

From French baroque, from Portuguese barroco (irregular pearl).
