wig

1. NOUN

/ wɪɡ / (AmE), / wɪɡ / (BrE)
A covering for the head made of real or synthetic hair, typically worn to conceal baldness or for decorative purposes.

Examples

He was proud that he had never worn a wig.

Etymology

Likely from shortened form of 'periwig', from French 'perruque'.
