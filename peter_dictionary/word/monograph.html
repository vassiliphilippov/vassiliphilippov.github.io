monograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A scholarly written work on a single specialized subject, usually detailed and thoroughly documented.

Examples

Forty other figures similar to 5 and 6 and the original data used in the construction of all figures and tables in this monograph have been included in the Appendix.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'graphein' (to write).
