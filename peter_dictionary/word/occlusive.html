occlusive

1. ADJ

/ əˈkluːsɪv / (AmE), / əˈkluːsɪv / (BrE)
Tending to close, block, or obstruct a passage or opening, especially in a medical context.

Examples

If this be true, the possibility exists that an occlusive lesion of the bronchial arteries might cause widespread degeneration of supportive tissue similar to that seen in generalized emphysema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructive - tending to obstruct or block

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere' (to close up).
