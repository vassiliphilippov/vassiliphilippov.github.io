taboo

1. ADJ

/ təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
Considered forbidden or prohibited from use, mention, or association due to social or cultural rules.

Examples

The Commission seems to represent the viewpoint of what I would call the unconscious liberal, but not unconscious enough, to invoke the now taboo symbolism of socialism.

Synset Examples

forbidden fruit
in our house dancing and playing cards were out
a taboo subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbidden - not allowed; not permitted
  • Antonym: permitted - allowed; given permission

Etymology

From Tongan tabu (sacred, prohibited), introduced to English by Captain James Cook in the late 18th century.
