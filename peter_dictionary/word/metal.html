metal

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
a class of chemical elements that are typically shiny, solid at room temperature, ductile, malleable, and good conductors of heat and electricity

Etymology

From Latin metallum (mine, quarry, metal), from Greek metallon (mine, metal).
