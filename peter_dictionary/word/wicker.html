wicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
Thin, flexible branches or twigs, especially of willow, used for making baskets and furniture.

Examples

Then she went back to the wicker chair and resolutely adjusted her eyes to the glare on the water.
It swayed with the wicker swings and screeched with the rusted hinges of screen doors.

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'wicker' (twig, branch).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
work or material made by weaving slender branches, especially willow, into a rigid pattern used for furniture or baskets.

Examples

He must have been the King of the Wicker People.

Etymology

Perhaps from Scandinavian; compare Swedish *vikker* (willow twig).
