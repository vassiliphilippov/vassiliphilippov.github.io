self-flagellation

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf flædʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf flædʒɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of punishing oneself by whipping, especially as a form of religious discipline or penance

Examples

Each wore the monkish scourge at his waist but this, it seems, was not employed for self-flagellation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penance - voluntary self-punishment as an expression of repentance

Etymology

From Latin 'flagellum' (whip) and 'self-', meaning oneself.
