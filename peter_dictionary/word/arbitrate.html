arbitrate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːrbɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to act as an impartial judge in a dispute in order to settle differences between two or more parties

Examples

Then the choreographer must arbitrate.

Synset Examples

He interceded in the family dispute
He mediated a settlement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediate - intervene to bring about agreement or reconciliation

Etymology

From Latin arbitrare (to judge, give a decision), from arbiter (judge, umpire).
