vouch

1. VERB

/ vaʊtʃ / (AmE), / vaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to give personal assurance or guarantee the truth or reliability of something or someone

Examples

They visited the shipyards at Brest and Pierre had to sign the register, vouching for the integrity of the visiting foreigner.

Synset Examples

Will he vouch for me?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - promise to ensure something will happen or be true
  • Synonym: attest - bear witness to the truth or genuineness of something

Etymology

From Old French *vocher* (to call, summon), from Latin *vocare* (to call).
