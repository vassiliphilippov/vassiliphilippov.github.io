smothered

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌðɚd / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðəd / (BrE)
(of a sound, laugh, feeling, etc.) suppressed or held back with difficulty; not fully expressed or released.

Examples

Presently he heard footsteps crossing the yard, and Jess's smothered curses.

Synset Examples

a smothered cough
a stifled yawn
a strangled scream
suppressed laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifled - suppressed or held back with effort
  • Synonym: suppressed - kept from being expressed or known
  • Synonym: strangled - stopped or restricted, especially of sound or emotion

Etymology

Past participle of 'smother,' from Middle English smotheren (to suffocate or choke).
