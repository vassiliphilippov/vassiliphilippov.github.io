oxidised

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑksɪˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzd / (BrE)
Having combined with oxygen or undergone a chemical reaction involving the addition of oxygen.

Examples

The mechanism of action of these drugs has not been completely worked out, but certain of them appear to act by reducing the oxidised form of iodine before it can iodinate thyroglobulin (Astwood, 1954).

Synset Examples

the oxidized form of iodine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reduced - having undergone a chemical reaction to gain electrons (opposite of oxidised)

Etymology

From 'oxide', which is from French 'oxyde', from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid) + '-ide'.
