contemptible

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛmptəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptəbəl / (BrE)
Deserving of contempt; so bad or low that one feels strong dislike or disrespect.

Examples

Here in the God-forsaken place, the westerly end of nowhere, Tom Lord looked almost insignificant, almost contemptible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despicable - deserving to be despised; extremely unpleasant or evil
  • Antonym: admirable - deserving admiration or respect

Etymology

From Latin 'contemptibilis', from 'contemnere' (to despise).
