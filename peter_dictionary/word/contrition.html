contrition

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
Sorrow for one's sins or wrongdoings, especially when arising from fear of divine punishment.

Examples

Not until the words had been spoken did Abel suddenly see the old house and the insistent sea, and feel his contrition blotted out in one shameful moment of covetousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: repentance - the action of repenting; sincere regret or remorse
  • Antonym: impenitence - lack of regret or remorse for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin contritio, from contritus, past participle of conterere 'to grind down, wear away'.
