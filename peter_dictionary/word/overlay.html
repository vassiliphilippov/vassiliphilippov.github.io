overlay

1. VERB

/ oʊvərˈleɪ / (AmE), / əʊvəˈleɪ / (BrE)
to cover the surface of something with a layer of another material

Examples

An alternate roof, perhaps more within do-it-yourself reach, could be constructed of heavy wooden roof beams, overlaid with boards and waterproofing.

Synset Examples

cover the meat with a lot of gravy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or upon, as for protection or concealment

Etymology

From Old English oferlecgan, with 'ofer' meaning 'over' and 'lecgan' meaning 'to lay'.
