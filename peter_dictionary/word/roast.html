roast

1. VERB

/ roʊst / (AmE), / rəʊst / (BrE)
To cook (food) using dry heat, especially in an oven or over an open fire.

Examples

I want to roast the whole thing, and have it for the boys ".
Their ham butts, cured in oak-log smoke, were also esteemed when roasted or boiled, and served with this original sauce.
Roast on grill over coals 15 - 20 minutes.

Synset Examples

roast the turkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bake - cook food by dry heat in an oven, typically for bread or cakes
  • Synonym: grill - cook food using direct heat from below, often on a grill
  • Antonym: boil - cook by immersing in boiling water

Etymology

From Old French *rostir*, from Frankish *hraustjan* (to cook on a grill).
