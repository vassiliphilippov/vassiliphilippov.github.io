unconsciously

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəsli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəsli / (BrE)
Without being aware; in a way that is not conscious.

Examples

Perhaps it was his misfortune, or good fortune, whichever way one looked at it, to belong to the former group, and he was struggling unconsciously to build up pressure in a world which demanded none, which was positively antagonistic to it.

Synset Examples

she jumped up unconsciously when he entered the room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consciously - with awareness; in a deliberate or intentional way

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'conscious' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
