pin

1. NOUN

/ pɪn / (AmE), / pɪn / (BrE)
A decorative piece of jewelry that is fastened or pinned to a garment, often for ornamental purposes.

Examples

In the vases this spirit may perhaps at times bore or repel one in its internal self-satisfaction, but the best of the Geometric pins have rightly been considered among the most beautiful ever made in the Greek world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brooch - an ornamental clasp or pin for clothing

2. VERB

/ pɪn / (AmE), / pɪn / (BrE)
to hold someone or something in place so that they cannot move or escape, especially by pressing or trapping them tightly

Examples

The Koreans fell back, but regrouped at the top of the hill and pinned down the cavalrymen with a screen of fire.
Because of this diversionary attack the main group that had been pinned down on the hill was able to surge forward again.

Synset Examples

The child was pinned under the fallen tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: release - to set someone or something free; allow to move

3. VERB

/ pɪn / (AmE), / pɪn / (BrE)
to attach or fasten something with pins, or to attribute something (such as blame) to a person

Examples

Pin right sides of pieces together, keeping edges even and matching rows or patterns.
And he's the one they pinned it on.

Synset Examples

pin the needle to the shirt
pin the blame on the innocent man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fasten - to attach firmly or securely in place
  • Antonym: unfasten - to detach or undo something that is fastened

Etymology

From Old English 'pinn' (peg, pin), from Latin 'pinna' (point, peak).
