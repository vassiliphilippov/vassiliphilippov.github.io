pastor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstə / (BrE)
a Christian minister, especially in charge of a Protestant church or congregation

Examples

The Deacon Board, headed by a black man named Carlson, had practically taken over as the pastor grew old, and had its way with the support of the Amen corner.
He sympathized with them on the loss of their old pastor.
The audience did not think much of the new pastor, and what the new pastor thought of the audience he did not dare at the time to say.

Synset Examples

clergymen are usually called ministers in Protestant churches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches
  • Synonym: priest - an ordained minister in Catholic, Orthodox, or Anglican churches

Etymology

From Latin 'pastor' (shepherd), from 'pasco' (to feed).
