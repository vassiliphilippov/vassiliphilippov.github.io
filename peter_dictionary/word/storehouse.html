storehouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːrˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈstɔː.haʊs / (BrE)
A building or place where goods are stored for future use; a warehouse.

Examples

"Not that there's much use in locking up the smokehouse and the storehouse now.
"We 'll go to the storehouse ".
Glendora dropped a chicken and a flurry of feathers, and went with her through the drizzle, to the storehouse.

Synset Examples

storehouses were built close to the docks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warehouse - large building for storing goods, typically commercially

Etymology

From Middle English 'storhous', from 'store' + 'house'.
