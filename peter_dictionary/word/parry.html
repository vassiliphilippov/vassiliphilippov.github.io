parry

1. VERB

/ ˈpæri / (AmE), / ˈpæri / (BrE)
to ward off or block (an attack or movement), especially with a countermove

Examples

The guard instinctively parried the blow with his rifle.

Synset Examples

block an attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to stop something from reaching its target
  • Synonym: deflect - to cause something to change direction, especially to avoid being hit
  • Synonym: dodge - to move quickly to avoid something

Etymology

From French *parer* (to ward off), from Italian *parare* (to prepare, defend), from Latin *parare* (to prepare).
