sear

1. VERB

/ sɪr / (AmE), / sɪə / (BrE)
to quickly brown the surface of food by exposing it to intense heat, often to seal in the juices

Examples

Sear on both sides then cover meat loosely with heat reflecting foil for juiciest results.
If necessary to replace both halves on grill, sear cuts and allot extra time.

Synset Examples

The heat scorched the countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorch - to burn the surface of something slightly
  • Degree Higher: char - to burn the surface of something so as to blacken it

Etymology

From Old English *sēarian* ('to wither'), related to Old High German *sôrēn*.
