marketplace

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪtˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtpleɪs / (BrE)
The area, sphere, or system in which commercial dealings and the buying and selling of goods and services occur.

Examples

Based on our experience with clients, we see 14 major problems which fall into three broad groups - the market place itself, marketing methods, and marketing management.
Will you be out in the market place with some of these sales building new products?
Driven from the marketplace by the course of history, our hero disguises himself as a private detective.

Synset Examples

without competition there would be no market
they were driven from the marketplace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an open place or a system for buying and selling goods or services

Etymology

Compound of 'market' (from Latin mercatus) and 'place', attested in English since the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪtˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtˌpleɪs / (BrE)
An open area or building in a town where people gather to buy and sell goods; a place where a market is held.

Examples

Some date it from woman suffrage, others from when women first began to challenge men in the marketplace, still others from the era of the emancipated flapper and bathtub gin.
If any had escaped expulsion by hiding, they certainly would not frequent the market-place.

Etymology

From Middle English market place, from Old French marché (market) + place (place).
