patriotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpætriətɪzəm / (BrE)
love for and devotion to one's country, often involving a willingness to support and defend it.

Examples

1) Most of the legends that are created to fan the fires of patriotism are essentially propagandistic and are not folk legends at all.
Naturally, such scholarly facts are of little concern to the man trying to make money or fan patriotism by means of folklore.

Synset Examples

they rode the same wave of popular patriotism
British nationalism was in the air and patriotic sentiments ran high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalism - strong identification with and support for one's nation, sometimes to the exclusion of others
  • Antonym: cosmopolitanism - the ideology that all human beings belong to a single community beyond national borders

Etymology

From French 'patriotisme,' from Late Latin 'patriota' (fellow countryman), from Greek 'patriōtēs' (countryman).
