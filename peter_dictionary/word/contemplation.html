contemplation

1. NOUN

/ kɑn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kɒn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action of thinking about something deeply and for a long time

Examples

During the rest of the summer my scholarly mania for making plaster casts and spatter prints of Catskill flowers and leaves was all but surpassed by the constantly renewed impressions of Jessica that my mind served up to me for contemplation and delight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Synonym: meditation - deep thought or focus, often for relaxation or spiritual reasons

Etymology

From Latin contemplatio, from contemplari (to observe, consider).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.təmˈpleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of observing or thinking about something for a long time in a thoughtful manner

Examples

His eyes went back to contemplation of the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meditation - the act of deep thought, especially as a spiritual practice

Etymology

From Latin contemplatio (act of looking at or observing).
