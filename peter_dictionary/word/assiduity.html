assiduity

1. NOUN

/ ˌæs.ɪˈdu.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪˈdjuː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
Constant and careful attention; persistent diligence in carrying out a task or duty.

Examples

The show was colorful, indeed, exuberant, but the press for all its assiduity could detect no note of a fateful rendezvous with destiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort
  • Synonym: industriousness - habitual hard work and effort
  • Antonym: laziness - the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy

Etymology

From Latin 'assiduitas', from 'assiduus' (constant, persistent).
