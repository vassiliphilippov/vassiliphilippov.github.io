bawl

1. VERB

/ bɔl / (AmE), / bɔːl / (BrE)
to shout or cry out loudly and unrestrainedly

Examples

The peddler came bawling his wares and told them of the convention in Wheeling, Which had formed a new state government by declaring the government at Richmond in the east illegal because they were traitors.
The bodies in the truck threshed and bawled without human speech when the dromozoa struck them.
They bawled questions that were not answered in the uproar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly, typically in anger, pain, or excitement
  • Synonym: scream - to give a long, loud, piercing cry
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, using the breath rather than the vocal cords

Etymology

Probably imitative. Recorded from late Middle English.

2. VERB

/ bɔl / (AmE), / bɔːl / (BrE)
to cry or weep loudly and noisily

Examples

[BAWLING] Money?

Synset Examples

Don't bawl in public!

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sob - to cry noisily with convulsive gasps
  • Synonym: wail - to make a prolonged, high-pitched cry of pain or grief
  • Degree Lower: whimper - to make low, feeble cries expressing pain or fear

Etymology

Of imitative origin, representing loud wailing cries.
