attractive

1. ADJ

/ əˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
Pleasing to the eye or mind, especially because of beauty or charm.

Examples

Big Band Percussion (SP 44002) seemed one of the least attractive discs - the arrangements just did n't have so much character as the others.
A site may also be attractive just through the beauty of its trees and shrubs.
The quantity of water flow may be critical; a stream or pond which is attractive in the springtime may become stagnant or dry in late summer.
Drunk or sober she was the most attractive woman in the world for me.
This is attractive, that is not.

Synset Examples

a remarkably attractive young man
an attractive personality
attractive clothes
a book with attractive illustrations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unattractive - not pleasing to the eye or mind; lacking beauty or charm
  • Degree Higher: beautiful - possessing beauty; very pleasing to the senses
  • Synonym: charming - delightfully pleasing or attractive

Etymology

From Latin 'attrahere' (to draw towards), via Medieval Latin 'attractivus'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / əˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
having qualities that arouse interest, appeal, or admiration; pleasing or appealing to the senses, emotions, or intellect

Examples

One of the most attractive things about avocados is that they do not require processing of any kind.
Religion provides the most attractive rewards, either in this world or the next, for those who not merely abide by its norms, but who engage in good works.
Irish Catholicism is not attractive, but it is deeper.

Synset Examples

an attractive opportunity
the job is attractive because of the pay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interesting - arousing curiosity or interest
  • Antonym: unattractive - not appealing or arousing interest

Etymology

From Latin 'attrahere' (to draw toward).
