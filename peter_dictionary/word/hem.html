hem

1. NOUN

/ hɛm / (AmE), / hɛm / (BrE)
the edge of a piece of cloth, especially the part that is folded under and sewn down to prevent unravelling

Examples

There was a little blood on the hem of her dress, for the assassin had slashed Miss Harris's companion, Major Rathbone, with a knife.
Martha picked up the hem of her gown and with eyes closed she slowly began to dance a stately minuet around the ballroom.
"Why, I 'm not fit to touch the hem of her garment ".

Synset Examples

the hem of her dress was stained
let down the hem
he stitched weights into the curtain's hem
it seeped along the hem of his jacket

Etymology

Old English 'hem', related to Dutch 'hem' (edge, hem) and German 'Saum'.

2. NOUN

/ hɛm / (AmE), / hɛm / (BrE)
A vocal sound uttered to attract attention, indicate hesitation, or fill a pause in speech.

Examples

Uh, Rocco?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ahem - sound made to attract attention or express hesitation
  • Synonym: uh - hesitation sound in speech
  • Synonym: um - hesitation sound in speech
