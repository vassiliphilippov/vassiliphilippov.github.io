detractor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtræktər / (AmE), / dɪˈtræktə / (BrE)
A person who disparages or belittles the worth or value of someone or something.

Examples

William, he was called, in honor of the man who was at once Shelley's pensioner and his most bitter detractor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - someone who expresses adverse or disapproving comments or judgments
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who approves of and encourages someone or something

Etymology

From Latin detrahere (to take away), via Late Latin detractare (to disparage).
