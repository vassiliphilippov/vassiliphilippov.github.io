havoc

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæv.ək / (AmE), / ˈhæv.ək / (BrE)
widespread destruction, disorder, or confusion, often caused suddenly and violently

Examples

Mr. Bates shows us how this mutual education spreads its inevitable havoc.
It can put an end to marginal claims which play havoc with your insurance rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying something
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged properly
  • Antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or quiet

Etymology

From Old French havot, alteration of havoc, a cry in the Middle Ages meaning 'plunder',' destroy'.
