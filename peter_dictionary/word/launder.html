launder

1. VERB

/ ˈlɔndɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndə / (BrE)
to convert money or assets obtained illegally into forms that appear legal

Examples

I'd be as indictable as you for laundering money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to free from dirt, marks, or unwanted matter

Etymology

From the literal sense 'to wash' (from Old French 'lavander'), applied metaphorically to money in the 20th century.
