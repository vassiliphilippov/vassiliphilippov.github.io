long-distance

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
(of a telephone call) made to or from a distant place, especially one outside a local area.

Examples

So, this big-shot lawyer calls me long-distance from Texas.

Synset Examples

a long-distance call
a long-distance transmission line
a long-distance operator

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local - referring to telephone calls within a limited geographic area

Etymology

From 'long' (great in distance) and 'distance' (Latin distantia). Refers to the extended range over which communication is made.
