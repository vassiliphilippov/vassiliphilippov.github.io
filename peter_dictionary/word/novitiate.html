novitiate

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈvɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / nəʊˈvɪʃɪət / (BrE)
The period during which someone is a novice, especially in a religious order, undergoing training and evaluation before taking full vows.

Examples

And the monastic communities were supposed to be made up of volunteers selected only after a novitiate which would test their religious aptitude for monastic rigors, their spiritual athleticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - someone who is new to a field or activity, especially in a religious order

Etymology

From Medieval Latin novitiatus (state of being a novice), from Latin novicius (newcomer, novice).
