collusion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːʒən / (AmE), / kəˈluːʒən / (BrE)
Secret or illegal cooperation or conspiracy, especially in order to deceive others.

Examples

"If there was collusion between an outside murderer and a member of the household it would be an elementary precaution to check on the door later.
He might have been in collusion with Johnston on the fraud; he might be Mrs. Meeker's murderer or have played some part in her death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful
  • Synonym: collaborate - to work together, especially in a secretive or illicit way
  • Synonym: complicity - the state of being involved with others in an illegal activity or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin 'collusio', from 'colludere' (to collude, to play together).
