parlay

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːrˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːleɪ / (BrE)
to use money, winnings, or resources gained to achieve greater returns or success, especially by staking winnings from one bet on another wager

Examples

But the one that upset the financially wise was the professional dancer who related in a book how he parlayed his earnings into a $ 2000000 profit on the stock market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - to risk money on the outcome of a game, contest, or event
  • Synonym: invest - to put money into something to try to earn more money

Etymology

From French 'parier' (to wager) via alteration influenced by 'parley.'
