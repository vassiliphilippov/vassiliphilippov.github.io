needle

1. NOUN

/ ˈnidəl / (AmE), / ˈniːdəl / (BrE)
A small, thin, sharp-pointed implement, usually made of steel, used for sewing, knitting, or other tasks requiring piercing or threading.

Examples

A quarter inch this way or that for the hardbake, or the toffee, or the barley sugar, or the sardines, or the bitters, or the condensed milk, or the stationery, or the needles - what could it mean?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin - a small, slender, pointed piece of metal used for fastening
  • Synonym: syringe - an instrument with a needle used for injections

Etymology

Old English 'nǣdl', from Proto-Germanic *næthlō.

2. VERB

/ ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
to intentionally provoke or annoy someone, especially through persistent criticism or teasing

Examples

You would n't have me throw the poor boy out on the street ", Eileen said when I needled her about it.

Synset Examples

He needled her with his sarcastic remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goad - to provoke or annoy someone into action
  • Synonym: taunt - to mock or provoke in a teasing way
  • Antonym: compliment - to praise or express approval

Etymology

Figurative use from the sharpness or sting of a needle.

3. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
a slender, pointed indicator on the scale of a measuring instrument, such as a compass or gauge

Examples

Electrical literature contained numerous references to lightning that had magnetized iron and had altered the polarity of compass needles.
Oersted's own earlier experiments were unimpressive, possibly because he had, like other experimenters, laid the conducting wire across the compass needle instead of parallel with it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointer - indicator or hand on a measuring instrument
