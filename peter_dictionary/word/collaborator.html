collaborator

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who assists in an unlawful or secret plan, often one involving wrongdoing or conspiracy

Examples

But if there was evidence at the Trial that aimed over Eichmann's head at his collaborators in the societies where he functioned, the press seems to have missed it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • Synonym: conspirator - a person who takes part in a secret plan to do something harmful

Etymology

From Latin collaborare (to work together), via French collaborateur.
