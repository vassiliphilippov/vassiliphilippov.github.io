slow

1. ADJ

/ sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
Moving, happening, or doing something at a low speed; not quick.

Examples

Carey singled on a slow bouncing ball to short which Robinson cut across to field and threw wide to first.
After this has become easy, use slower and slower rhythms, kicking higher and higher.

In some areas, the progress is slower than in others.

Synset Examples

a slow walker
the slow lane of traffic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fast - moving or happening at high speed
  • Degree Lower: sluggish - moving more slowly than usual or desired
  • Degree Lower: leisurely - acting or done without haste
  • Degree Higher: brisk - quick and energetic
  • Degree Higher: rapid - moving or happening very quickly

Etymology

From Old English 'slāw' meaning 'sluggish, lazy'; related to Old High German 'slēo' (blunt, dull).

3. ADJ

/ sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
(of music or movement) performed at a low speed or tempo; not fast.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fast - performed at a high speed or tempo
  • Degree Higher: moderate - (of tempo) at a medium speed
  • Degree Lower: largo - (in music) at a very slow tempo

Etymology

From Old English 'slāw' (slow, sluggish).
