reproduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
The process by which living organisms produce new individuals, or offspring.

Examples

Reduces losses from stomach, hookworm, and nodular worms by interfering with reproduction of the female worm by reducing the number of eggs laid and essentially rendering all laid eggs sterile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procreation - the act or process of producing offspring
  • Antonym: sterility - the inability to reproduce offspring

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to bring forth, to produce).
