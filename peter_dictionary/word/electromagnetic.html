electromagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊmægˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmægˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the interaction of electric currents or fields and magnetic fields, or by magnetism produced by moving electric charges

Examples

The solar electromagnetic - and corpuscular-radiation pressure and the associated Poynting-Robertson effect increase in effectiveness as the particle size decreases and modify the distribution and limit sizes to larger than a few microns.

Synset Examples

electromagnetic energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic - relating to or exhibiting magnetism
  • Synonym: electrical - relating to electricity

Etymology

From electro- (electric) + magnetic, referring to the combined effects of electricity and magnetism.
