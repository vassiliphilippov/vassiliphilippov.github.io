capitalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an economic system in which industries, businesses, and property are privately owned and operated for profit, rather than by the state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: socialism - an economic system in which the means of production are owned and regulated by the community or state
  • Antonym: communism - a political and economic system in which all property is publicly owned

Etymology

From French *capitalisme*, from *capital* (wealth), ultimately from Latin *caput* (head).
