egalitarianism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌɡælɪˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌɡalɪˈtɛːrɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief in or advocacy of the equality of all people, especially in political, economic, or social life.

Examples

As it is, they consider that the North is now reaping the fruits of excess egalitarianism, that in spite of its high standard of living the "American way "has been proved inferior to the English and Scandinavian ways, although they disapprove of the socialistic features of the latter.
French Egalitarianism had had only nominal influence in this country before the days of Popularism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • Antonym: inequality - the state of not being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities

Etymology

From 'egalitarian' + '-ism'. 'Egalitarian' from French 'égalitaire', from 'égal' (equal), from Latin 'aequalis'.
