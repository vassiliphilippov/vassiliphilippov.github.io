self-government

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
The government of a country, region, or group by its own people, especially after having been a colony or under external control.

Examples

However, the regionally differentiated results, which appear below in tables, are interesting evidence of the problems of developing self-government under even the most favorable circumstances.
King Muhammad /5, was known to be most sympathetic to the formation of local self-government and made the first firm promise of elections on May Day, 1957.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomy - self-directing freedom and especially moral independence; self-government or self-rule
  • Synonym: independence - the condition of a nation or state in which its residents and population exercise self-government, and usually sovereignty, over the territory
  • Antonym: colonization - control over a territory or people by a foreign power; the opposite of self-government

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'government', first attested in the 17th century.
