boring

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
Not interesting; making you feel tired and impatient.

Examples

He was tired, he had his business worries, and the sight of his wife arranging pork chops in the broiler only seemed like an extension of a boring day.
Usually, this was done when attention was diverted by someone else's long, boring story.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedious - long and tiresome; boring
  • Synonym: dull - not interesting or exciting; boring
  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness; the opposite of boring
