sunder

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌndər / (AmE), / ˈsʌndə / (BrE)
to break or split something apart, especially with force or violence

Examples

Although it is not possible to sunder old and new in this era, I shall consider in the present chapter primarily the first decades of the eighth century and shall interpret them as an apogee of the first stage of Greek civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart; to divide
  • Synonym: split - to divide or cause to divide into parts or elements
  • Antonym: unite - to join together; to make or become one

Etymology

Old English *syndrian* (to separate), from Germanic roots.
