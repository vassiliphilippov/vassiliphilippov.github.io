lid

1. NOUN

/ lɪd / (AmE), / lɪd / (BrE)
either of the folds of skin that cover and uncover the eyeball; an eyelid

Examples

He breathed now with his mouth open, showing a whitely curving section of lower teeth; he kept his eyes, with their puffed blurred lids, always lowered, though not, apparently, focusing.
Two clotted balls the color of mucus rolled between fiery lids.
Killpath peered through half closed lids at his reflection, thrust up his chin in a gesture of satisfaction and about-faced.

Synset Examples

his lids would stay open no longer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelid - the movable skin fold covering the eye

Etymology

From Old English hlid (cover, lid, gate).

2. NOUN

/ lɪd / (AmE), / lɪd / (BrE)
A movable cover or top, typically hinged or separate, used to close the opening of a container such as a box, chest, jar, or pan.

Examples

Until we find out, keep a lid on it.

Synset Examples

he raised the piano lid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that serves as a top or protection for an opening

Etymology

From Old English 'hlid' (cover, lid), related to German 'Lid' (eyelid).
