eternity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
A state of endless or timeless existence, especially as believed in many religions to characterize the afterlife.

Examples

Existence is created and willed by God and is not the consequence of a pre-existent rebellion or of a cosmic descent from eternity into history.
Eternity is no time for recriminations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immortality - the ability to live forever; eternal life
  • Antonym: temporality - the state of existing within or having some relationship with time

Etymology

From Latin 'aeternitas' (endlessness, eternity), from 'aeternus' (eternal).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɜrnəti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
Infinite or unending time; a state of existence outside time or beyond time's limits.

Examples

If it was, then it must have been God's intention to translate him at a certain point from time to eternity.
This theory makes it possible for any event throughout eternity to be continuously available at any moment to consciousness.
Men were slaughtered every day, tumbled into eternity like so many torn parcels flung down a portable chute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinity - limitless or endless in space, extent, or size; similar to eternity in temporal sense
  • Antonym: temporality - state of existing within or having some relationship with time; opposite of eternity
  • Synonym: immortality - the ability to live forever, eternal life

Etymology

From Latin 'aeternitas' (eternity), from 'aeternus' (eternal).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈtɝːnəti / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
A period of time that feels endless or is perceived as lasting forever, especially while waiting or experiencing something tedious.

Examples

And yet - a year to a child is an eternity, and in the memory that phase of one's being - a certain mental landscape - will seem to have endured without beginning and without end.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moment - a very short period of time
  • Synonym: age - a very long period of time

Etymology

From Latin aeternitas (eternity, timelessness).
