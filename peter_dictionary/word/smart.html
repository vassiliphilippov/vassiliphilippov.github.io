smart

1. ADJ

/ smɑrt / (AmE), / smɑːt / (BrE)
(of clothes or appearance) elegant and stylish in a fashionable way.

Examples

Rachel wore a smart hat and, because she had been warned recently about smoking, puffed at her cigarettes through a long ivory holder stained with lipstick.

Synset Examples

chic elegance
a smart new dress
a suit of voguish cut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chic - fashionably elegant and stylish
  • Synonym: stylish - fashionably elegant; having style
  • Antonym: scruffy - untidy or shabby in appearance

2. ADJ

/ smɑrt / (AmE), / smɑːt / (BrE)
having or showing quick intelligence, mental alertness, and resourcefulness

Examples

Oh he was smart.
A smart, shrewd and ambitious young man, well connected, and with a knack for getting in the good graces of important people, he was bound to go far.
"Mr. Skyros too smart a fellow want to get rid of me ", he said.
She 'd be smart about it, get him to give it to her in little bills so's nobody would suspect - maybe could n't get it until Monday account of that, the banks - But that was n't really long to wait.
He had found Curt's weakness, or what to Jess was a weakness, and was smart enough to take advantage of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence, especially of a high level
  • Synonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas
  • Antonym: dull - slow to understand; lacking intelligence

Etymology

From Old English 'smeart' (painful, stinging), later evolving to mean 'quick-witted'.

4. VERB

/ smɑrt / (AmE), / smɑːt / (BrE)
