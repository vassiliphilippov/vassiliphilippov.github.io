italic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
a style of handwriting or typeface in which the letters slant to the right, often used for emphasis

Examples

Here again, in the written language it is possible to help the reader get his stresses right by using underlining or italics, but much of the time there is simply reliance on his understanding in the light of context.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roman - upright style of typeface as opposed to italic
  • Synonym: oblique - slanted type style, often similar to italic

Etymology

From Latin 'Italic(us)' meaning 'of Italy', originally referring to the style's Italian origins.
