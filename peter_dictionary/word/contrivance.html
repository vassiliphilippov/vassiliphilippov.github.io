contrivance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtraɪvəns / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪv(ə)ns / (BrE)
the skill or faculty of inventing or devising clever solutions or devices

Examples

Through 1911 and 1912, as the Cubist facet-plane's tendency to adhere to the literal surface became harder and harder to deny, the task of keeping the surface at arm's length fell all the more to eye-undeceiving contrivances.

Synset Examples

his skillful contrivance of answers to every problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuity - the quality of being clever, original, and inventive

Etymology

From contrive + -ance, ultimately from Old French 'contreuvance' meaning 'invention'.
