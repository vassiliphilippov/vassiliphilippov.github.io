gallbladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælˌblædər / (AmE), / ˈɡælˌblædə / (BrE)
a small, pear-shaped muscular sac beneath the liver that stores bile produced by the liver until it is needed for digestion

Examples

The gallbladder contained about 40 cc. of green brown bile and 3 smooth, dark-green calculi measuring up to 1 cm. in diameter.

Etymology

From 'gall' (bile) + 'bladder' (a sac or pouch), originally Middle English.
