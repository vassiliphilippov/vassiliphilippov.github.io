besmirch

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsmɝrtʃ / (AmE), / bɪˈsmɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to damage the reputation of someone or something; to attack the good name and standing of a person, often unfairly or maliciously

Examples

He evolved programs that plumbed new depths of bathos and besmirched whole networks, and quickly superseded his boss.
Would we gain by keeping alive his memory and besmirching today's Roman Catholics by saying he had a Catholic heart?
I challenge this committee to produce any witness or evidence against me and if they do not, I hope they will have the decency to clear my name with the same publicity with which they have besmirched it.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defame - to harm someone's reputation by making false statements
  • Synonym: slander - to make false spoken statements that damage someone's reputation
  • Synonym: sully - to damage the purity or integrity of something

Etymology

From Middle English smirch (to soil), with be- prefix; related to Old English 'smeorchian' (to smear).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈsmɝtʃ / (AmE), / bɪˈsmɜːtʃ / (BrE)
To damage the reputation of someone or something; to make something dirty or stained, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

On the whole, however, one must wonder at just what it is that forces a beloved artist to besmirch her own reputation as time marches inexorably on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defame - to damage someone's reputation by making false or unjustified statements
  • Synonym: tarnish - to make someone or something less respected or valued

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + smirch (to soil, to stain), probably from Middle English.
