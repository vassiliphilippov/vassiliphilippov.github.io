Harlem

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑrləm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːləm / (BrE)
A district in the northern part of Manhattan, New York City, known for its significant African-American cultural and historical influence.

Examples

A New York kid, a refugee from one of the Harlem gangs, made fun of Trig's accent, and drew a knife.
Before the fight was over, the Harlem boy had a concussion and Trig was cut up badly.
Niggers are having a good time with our policy banks in Harlem.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch city Haarlem.
