mottle

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɒt.əl / (BrE)
to mark or cover with spots or blotches of different colors or shades

Examples

The firm red spleen weighed 410 gm., and its surface was mottled by discrete, small patches of white material.
The liver weighed 2090 gm., was brown in color, and the cut surface was mottled by irregular pale areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speckle - to mark with a large number of small spots or patches of color
  • Synonym: streak - to cover or mark with long, thin lines of color

Etymology

Probably from Middle English motle (a kind of cloth with varied coloring).
