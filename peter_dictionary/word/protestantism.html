Protestantism

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːtəstənˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɒtɪstəntɪzəm / (BrE)
The branch of Christianity comprising churches and individuals who separated from the Roman Catholic Church during the Reformation, characterized by differing doctrines and practices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Catholicism - branch of Christianity governed by the Pope; related term
  • Synonym: Reformation - 16th-century movement leading to Protestantism

Etymology

From French *protestantisme*, from *protestant* (follower of the Reformation) + -ism.
