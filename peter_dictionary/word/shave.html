shave

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪv / (AmE), / ʃeɪv / (BrE)
To remove hair from the face or body using a razor or similar tool.

Examples

And that was why, on the day of the performance, when a carriage from the royal stables called to take him to the palace, he did not bother to shave.
David began to shave with great sweeping strokes.
He drove sensual patterns off, carefully shaving his long upper lip.
David finished shaving, washed his face clean of lather, and combed and retied his hair.
Did it take a man of sixty-five longer to write a letter, shave, clean out a barn, read a newspaper, than a man of thirty?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - to make something neater by cutting small amounts (hair, beard, etc.)

Etymology

Old English *sceafan* (to scrape, shave), from Proto-Germanic *skabaną*.

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪv / (AmE), / ʃeɪv / (BrE)
To cut or trim something very closely, typically to make a surface smooth or to reduce to a minimal thickness.

Examples

The bristles are a Fitch 2 and a one-half inch brush shaved to a sharp chisel edge.

Synset Examples

trim my beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - To make something neat by cutting away irregular parts; similar in meaning to cut closely.

Etymology

Old English *scafan* (to scrape, polish), related to Old Norse *skafa* (to scrape).

3. NOUN

/ ʃeɪv / (AmE), / ʃeɪv / (BrE)
the act of removing hair from the body, especially the face, using a razor

Examples

Give him a shave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaving - the act of shaving hair, especially facial hair

Etymology

From Old English *sceafan* (to scrape, shave).
