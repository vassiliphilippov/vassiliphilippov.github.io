liner

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a batted ball that travels sharply and directly in a straight line, usually close to the level of the ground.

Examples

Lemon was on with his fourth single of the game, a liner to center.

Synset Examples

the batter hit a liner to the shortstop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line drive - a batted baseball that travels in a nearly straight line

Etymology

From 'line' (referring to a straight trajectory) + '-er', denoting something characterized by a line.
