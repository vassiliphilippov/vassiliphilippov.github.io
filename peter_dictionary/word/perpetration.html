perpetration

1. NOUN

/ pɜːrpəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / pɜːpɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of committing a crime or other wrongful deed.

Examples

This nettled the children into the revelation of exact truth, a sacrifice of their secret superiority over grown people, but a victory in the wide fields of perpetration and illegitimate accomplishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commission - the act of committing or carrying out (especially a crime)

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetratio', from 'perpetrare' (to accomplish, to commit).
