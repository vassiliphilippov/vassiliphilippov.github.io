swarm

1. VERB

/ swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or insects) to move somewhere in large numbers, often in a busy or uncontrolled way.

Examples

"If the day should ever come that foreign invaders swarm ashore along the Gulf Coast ", the account reads, "they can count on heavy opposition from a group of commando trained telephone employees - all girls.
Flies swarmed around the bowl of lentils in which he mopped thick black bread.
The guerrillas were swarming from their bivouac at the west end of the enclosure.

Synset Examples

people were pouring out of the theater
beggars pullulated in the plaza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pour - move in large numbers (often used figuratively, especially for people)
  • Synonym: throng - to move or crowd together in large numbers

Etymology

Old English swarma (noun), related to Old Norse svarmr (noisy crowd, swarm).

2. NOUN

/ swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
a large group of insects or other small creatures moving together in the air or on the ground; also, any large, moving crowd or mass.

Examples

But suddenly those hot honey eyes seemed to have everything but swarms of bees in them.

Synset Examples

a swarm of insects obscured the light
clouds of blossoms
it discharged a cloud of spores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horde - a large group of people or animals, often moving in a crowd
  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds or animals that move together

Etymology

From Old English 'swearm', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'zwerm' and German 'Schwarm'.

3. VERB

/ swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
to move in or be present in large numbers, especially in a way that is busy or crowded

Examples

As things happened, Morgan was installed in the Nob Hill residence of a magnate friend, whose kitchen swarmed with cooks of approved talent.
Colonel Frederick W. Lander, impersonated, will again make his break-neck ride down the steep declivity of Talbott's (now College) Hill and thunder across the bridge to join Colonel Benjamin F. Kelley's (West) Virginia Infantry, then swarming through the streets in pursuit of the retreating Confederates.

Synset Examples

The garden was swarming with bees
The plaza is teeming with undercover policemen
her mind pullulated with worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teem - to be full of or swarming with

Etymology

Old English 'swearm' (a swarm of bees), of Germanic origin.
