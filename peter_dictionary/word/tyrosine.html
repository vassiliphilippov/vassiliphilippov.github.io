tyrosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.rəˌsin / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.rəˌsiːn / (BrE)
an amino acid found in most proteins, serving as a precursor to several important hormones such as dopamine and thyroxine

Examples

If tyrosine and a system generating hydrogen peroxide are added to a cell-free homogenate of the thyroid, large quantities of free mono-iodotyrosine can be formed (Alexander, 1959).
In the thyroid gland it appears that proteins (chiefly thyroglobulin) are iodinated and that free tyrosine and thyronine are not iodinated.
Iodination of tyrosine, however, is not enough for the synthesis of hormone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - organic compound containing both amino and carboxyl groups, building block of proteins
  • Degree Lower: phenylalanine - an essential amino acid related to tyrosine

Etymology

From German 'Tyrosin', from Greek 'tyros' (cheese), after its discovery in cheese.
