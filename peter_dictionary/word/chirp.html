chirp

1. VERB

/ tʃɝːp / (AmE), / tʃɜːp / (BrE)
(of a small bird or insect) to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound.

Examples

Birds chirped and chattered in the trees and the sun, all dewy-eyed and soft, caressed her shoulders warmly from time to time.

Synset Examples

the birds were chirping in the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweet - to make a short, high-pitched sound (of a small bird)
  • Synonym: cheep - to make a short, high-pitched sound, especially as a young bird
  • Synonym: warble - to sing with trills or quavers, typically of birds

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, similar to 'cheep'.
