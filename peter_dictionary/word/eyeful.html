eyeful

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.fʊl / (BrE)
a full or satisfying view of something; a good look

Examples

Get a good look.

Synset Examples

they wanted to see violence and they got an eyeful

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glimpse - a brief or partial view

Etymology

Formed from 'eye' + 'ful', modeled after similar constructions, first recorded in the late 19th century.
