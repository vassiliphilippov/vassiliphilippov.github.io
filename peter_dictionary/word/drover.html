drover

1. NOUN

/ ˈdroʊvər / (AmE), / ˈdrəʊvə / (BrE)
a person who drives cattle, sheep, or other livestock from place to place, especially to market

Examples

Hercules L. Dousman, fur trader and merchant at Prairie du Chien, contracted to supply Selkirk's people with some 300 head of cattle, and Alexis Bailly and Francois Labothe were hired as drovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herder - a person who tends or drives herds of animals
  • Synonym: shepherd - a person who tends and herds sheep

Etymology

From drive + -er, referring to one who drives animals.
