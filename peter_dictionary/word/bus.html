bus

1. NOUN

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
A large road vehicle designed to carry many passengers, typically operating on a fixed route as part of a public transport system.

Examples

The tulips and the big pink peonies had been blooming along the drive, and he had walked up from the bus almost singing.
When you pass a church on an Irish bus, all the hands flurry in the sign of the cross.

Synset Examples

he always rode the bus to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - large motor vehicle for carrying passengers, usually long distances
  • Degree Lower: minibus - small bus for fewer passengers

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'omnibus,' from Latin 'omnibus' (for all).

2. VERB

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
to remove used dishes from tables in a restaurant or similar establishment

Examples

I got me this job one time bussing tables at a country club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - to remove objects, especially from a surface like a table

Etymology

From the noun 'busboy,' originally referring to a restaurant worker who clears tables.
