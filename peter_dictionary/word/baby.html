baby

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
A very young child, especially one who has not yet begun to walk or talk.

Etymology

From Middle English 'babey' or 'babie', a reduplication of 'baba' imitative of infantile speech.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
the youngest member of a group, especially within a family or organization, regardless of actual age

Examples

"She married our baby boy, Bobby Joe, this summer ".

Synset Examples

the baby of the family
the baby of the Supreme Court

Etymology

From Middle English 'babie', possibly imitative of infantile speech.
