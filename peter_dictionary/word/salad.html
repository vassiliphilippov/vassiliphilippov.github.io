salad

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæ.ləd / (AmE), / ˈsæl.əd / (BrE)
A cold dish made from a mixture of raw or cooked vegetables, usually seasoned with oil, vinegar, or other dressing, and often including greens.

Examples

A salad with greens and tomato is a popular and wonderfully healthful addition to a meal, but add an avocado and you have something really special.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coleslaw - a salad consisting mainly of shredded raw cabbage and dressing
  • Degree Lower: fruit salad - a salad made of mixed pieces of fruit, sometimes with syrup or dressing

Etymology

From Old French salade, from Italian insalata, from Latin salata, 'salted' (from sal, 'salt').
