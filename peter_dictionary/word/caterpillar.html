caterpillar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬ɚˌpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkætəˌpɪlə / (BrE)
The larval stage of a butterfly or moth, typically with a soft, wormlike body, often brightly colored or covered with bristles.

Examples

Fuzzy caterpillars, snails with their sensitive horns, struggling grasshoppers held by their long hind legs and commanded to "spit tobacco, spit ".

Etymology

From Middle English 'catirpel', probably from Anglo-Norman 'catirpel', based on Old French 'chatepelose' (hairy cat) due to its fur-like appearance.
