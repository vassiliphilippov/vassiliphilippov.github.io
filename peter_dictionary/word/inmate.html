inmate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌmeɪt / (BrE)
A person who is serving a sentence in a jail or prison.

Examples

Whatever the unhappy inmates of such camps are like today, Dostoevsky's companions were about as grim a lot as can be imagined.
Our inmates, properly supervised, will be put to work outside these walls, performing all manner of public service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - person kept in prison as punishment or while awaiting trial
  • Synonym: convict - person found guilty and serving a criminal sentence
  • Antonym: warder - person in charge of prisoners (antonym by role)

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (in) + Middle English 'mate' (companion); originally meant a person sharing quarters.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnmeɪt / (BrE)
a person who lives in an institution such as a prison, psychiatric hospital, or similar facility, especially someone who is confined by law or circumstance

Examples

"It's a house of prostitution for white men with white girls as inmates.
The inmates were white and from out of town, avoiding local friction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - person held in prison
  • Degree Lower: patient - person under medical care, esp. in hospital

Etymology

From early modern English, literally 'one who shares a house', from 'in' + 'mate'.
