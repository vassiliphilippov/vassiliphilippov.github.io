inhospitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌhɑːˈspɪtəbl / (AmE), / ɪnˌhɒsˈpɪtəbl / (BrE)
(of a place or environment) harsh and difficult to live in; unfriendly to life or growth.

Examples

The name "alkali bee "indicates that one has to look for them in rather inhospitable places.

Synset Examples

the barren inhospitable desert
inhospitable mountain areas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hospitable - friendly and welcoming to guests or life; the opposite meaning

Etymology

From Late Latin inhospitabilis (not hospitable), from Latin in- (not) + hospitabilis (hospitable).
