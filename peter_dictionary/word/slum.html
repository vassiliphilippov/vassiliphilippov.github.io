slum

1. NOUN

/ slʌm / (AmE), / slʌm / (BrE)
an area of a city where poor people live, with bad housing and often in very dirty or unsafe conditions

Examples

A site may be a rundown slum or a desolate piece of desert in appearance today but have excellent potentials for the future with a little development or water.
Scotty gazed out at ugly gray slums and said softly, "Look at those stupid kids ".
There are slums in the country as well as in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghetto - a part of a city, especially a slum area, occupied by a minority group or groups

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect slumre (to slumber) in the sense of a place that is neglected or in decline.
