track

1. NOUN

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
a narrow path or mark left by a person, animal, or vehicle as it moves over the ground, or the course or route taken by something that moves

Examples

A bright sun and brisk wind had the track in a fast condition for the first time this week and 8280 St. Patty Day celebrants bet $ 842617 on the well prepared program.
There were tracks of cattle all over his six hundred and forty acres.
There had been no sign of a rifleman and no track or trace to show that anyone had been near.
There were no tracks of either hoofs or boots.
A man knocked the roulette ball about idly in its track, and another dozed at one of the card tables.

Synset Examples

the hurricane demolished houses in its path
the track of an animal
the course of the river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: path - a route or track between one place and another
  • Synonym: trail - a mark, sign, or series of marks left behind by the passage of someone or something
  • Degree Higher: road - a wide way leading from one place to another

Etymology

From Middle French *trac*, from Old French *tracier* (to trace), ultimately from Latin *tractus* (a dragging, drawing out).

2. NOUN

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
a clue, mark, or piece of evidence that may lead to a possible solution, especially in an investigation

Examples

If we manage to keep track of a Bombus queen after she has left her feeding place, we may discover the snug little hideout which she has fixed up for herself when she woke up from her winter sleep.

Synset Examples

the police are following a promising lead
the trail led straight to the perpetrator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - a clue or piece of information for solving something
  • Synonym: trail - a series of signs or clues leading to someone or something

3. NOUN

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
a pair of parallel rails that forms a path for trains or other wheeled vehicles

Examples

He crossed the tracks to Delaware House, where ladies in gay dresses and men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway - a system of tracks along which trains run
  • Synonym: railroad track - track specifically for railroads

Etymology

Probably from Middle French *trac* (mark, footprint) or Middle Dutch *tracke* (track, path).

4. NOUN

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
a course, typically oval-shaped, prepared for races such as horse racing, car racing, or athletics

Examples

During the summer he had tried to repair some of his losses at the track, and the bare trees reminded him that his pari-mutuel tickets would still be lying, like leaves, in the gutters near Belmont and Saratoga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racetrack - a course over which races are run, especially for horses or automobiles
  • Synonym: course - an area where a race is held

Etymology

From Old French trac, from tracier (to track), from Vulgar Latin *tractiare (to pull, drag), from Latin tractus (a dragging, track).

5. VERB

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
To observe, follow, or record the movements or course of something, especially for the purpose of monitoring or detecting it.

Examples

To round out the blockading force, submarines would be needed - to locate, identify and track approaching vessels.

Synset Examples

track a missile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor - To observe and check the progress or quality of something over a period of time
  • Synonym: trace - To find or follow the course or track of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'trak', from Old French 'trac', of Germanic origin, meaning 'course, path'.

6. VERB

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to carry dirt or other material on the feet and deposit it somewhere, especially by walking into a place.

Examples

They tracked mud on the oaken floor, on the carpet.
Night after night he stayed with Gunny in the dead of winter, rubbing her with quarts of expensive liniment, fussing over her bran mash as the cook did over charlotte russe, tracking manure on the pretty new carpet when he did come to the house.

Synset Examples

track mud into the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trail - to leave a mark or line of something being drawn along a surface

7. VERB

/ træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
to follow the movements or trail of someone or something, especially in order to catch or find them

Examples

I did not rest until I had tracked the mystery down.

Synset Examples

The policeman chased the mugger down the alley
the dog chased the rabbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch or catch up with
  • Synonym: pursue - to follow in order to catch or overtake

Etymology

From Old French trac (track, footprint), from Germanic origin.
