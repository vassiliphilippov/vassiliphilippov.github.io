reconstructed

1. ADJ

/ ˌrikənˈstrʌktɪd / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) having changed one's views or behavior to adapt to new social or economic circumstances, especially after a period of holding contrary or outdated beliefs.

Examples

My definition of this much abused adjective is that a reconstructed rebel is one who is glad that the North won the War.

Synset Examples

a reconstructed feminist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reformed - having been changed for the better, especially in character or belief

Etymology

Formed from 'reconstruct' + '-ed', especially popularized in reference to the American South after the Civil War (Reconstruction).
