chop

1. VERB

/ tʃɑp / (AmE), / tʃɒp / (BrE)
to cut something into pieces with repeated sharp blows, especially using an ax, knife, or similar tool.

Examples

Harnessing a team to a buckboard, they drove out to a willow lined creek about a half-mile off, then climbed down and began chopping.
Coney Island sauce: finely chop several onions and add enough catsup to moisten well; add prepared mustard to suit taste.

Synset Examples

Chop wood
chop meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to make an opening, incision, or wound in (something) with a sharp-edged tool or object

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'choppen' (to strike, knock, or cut).
