uneasy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈizi / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːzi / (BrE)
Feeling or causing discomfort, worry, or lack of security; not relaxed or reassured.

Examples

Television has yet to work out a living arrangement with jazz, which comes to the medium more as an uneasy guest than as a relaxed member of the family.
There is no occasion to feel uneasy or disturbed about these people.
Yet often fear persists because, even with the most rigid ritual, one is never quite free from the uneasy feeling that one might make some mistake or that in every previous execution one had been unaware of the really decisive act.
Yet we no longer feel uneasy.
The Secretary was uneasy about the visit.

Synset Examples

farmers were uneasy until rain finally came
uneasy about his health
gave an uneasy laugh
uneasy lies the head that wears the crown
an uneasy coalition government
an uneasy calm
an uneasy silence fell on the group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety

Etymology

From un- (not) + easy; 'easy' from Old French aisié, past participle of aisier (to ease).
