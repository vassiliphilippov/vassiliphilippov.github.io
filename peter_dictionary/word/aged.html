aged

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
having lived for a long time; advanced in years; old, especially as an adjective describing people or things

Examples

Above the tongue, an aged yellow eye, sallow and time-cast, encrusted within a sphere of marbleized pink skin, stared unfalteringly at him.

Synset Examples

aged members of the society
elderly residents could remember the construction of the first skyscraper
senior citizen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderly - people who are old; advanced in age
  • Antonym: young - having lived or existed for only a short time

Etymology

From age + -ed, used as an adjective meaning 'having a specified age or aged in general'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒd / (BrE)
people who are old collectively; elderly people regarded as a group

Examples

Could it be that it speeded up for the aged in some mysterious way, as if a bored universe were skipping through the end of the chapter just to get it over with?

Synset Examples

special arrangements were available for the aged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderly - people who are old collectively or an old person
  • Antonym: youth - young people collectively

Etymology

From the adjective 'aged', ultimately from Latin 'aetas' (age, lifetime).
