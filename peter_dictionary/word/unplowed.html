unplowed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈplaʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈplaʊd / (BrE)
(of land or soil) not turned over or prepared with a plow.

Examples

For less than a dozen miles from the unplowed land of the dead man lived another settler who had ignored the warnings that his existence might be foreclosed on - a blatant and defiant rustler named Fred Powell.

Synset Examples

unplowed fields
unbroken land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plowed - (of land) turned over with a plow

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not', and 'plowed', the past participle of 'plow'.
