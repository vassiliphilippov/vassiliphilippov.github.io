blustery

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌstəri / (AmE), / ˈblʌstəri / (BrE)
(of the weather) characterized by strong, gusty, and often noisy winds.

Examples

It was a cold, windy day, the day after Kitti's death, but Stanley Gilborn paid no attention to the blustery October wind.

Synset Examples

blustering (or blusterous) winds of Patagonia
a cold blustery day
a gusty storm with strong sudden rushes of wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - free from wind or storm; tranquil
  • Synonym: gusty - characterized by sudden, strong bursts of wind

Etymology

From the verb 'bluster', meaning to blow in strong gusts, likely of Middle Dutch or Low German origin.
