sclerotic

1. ADJ

/ skləˈrɑːtɪk / (AmE), / skləˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or affected by sclerosis; abnormally hardened, especially of body tissue or organs.

Examples

The coronary arteries were sclerotic and diffusely narrowed throughout their courses, and the right coronary artery was virtually occluded by a yellow atheromatous plaque 1.5 cm. distal to its origin.

Synset Examples

a sclerotic patient

Etymology

From Greek 'sklērōtikos' (hardening), from 'sklēroun' (to harden).
