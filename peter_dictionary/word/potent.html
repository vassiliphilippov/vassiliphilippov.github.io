potent

1. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
having a strong physiological or chemical effect; very powerful or effective, especially in reference to drugs, chemicals, or drinks.

Examples

Albert (1949) has concluded that the most active preparations of TSH made during this period, from 1931 to 1945, were probably about 100 to 300 times as potent as the starting material.

Synset Examples

a potent toxin
potent liquor
a potent cup of tea
a stiff drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great power or effect
  • Antonym: weak - lacking strength or potency, especially of drugs or chemicals

Etymology

From Latin potent-, potens (being able, powerful), present participle of posse (to be able).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
Having great power or influence.

Examples

More potent a charm to bring back that time of life than this record of a few pictures and a few remembered facts would be a catalogue of the minutiae which are of the very stuff of the mind, intrinsic, because they were known in the beginning not by the eye alone but by the hand that held them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great power or strength
  • Synonym: influential - having the ability to influence people or events
  • Antonym: weak - lacking power or influence

Etymology

From Latin *potens*, *potentis* (powerful), present participle of *posse* (to be able).

3. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
having great power, influence, or effect; able to exert strong force or authority

Examples

The fact that such threat is potent in the beginning reading lessons is thought to be a vital factor in the continued pattern of failure or under-achievement these children exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impotent - lacking power or ability

Etymology

From Latin 'potentem' (powerful), present participle of 'posse' (to be able).
