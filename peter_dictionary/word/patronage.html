patronage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənɪdʒ / (BrE)
the support, encouragement, or financial aid given by a patron or supporter to a person, organization, or cause

Examples

Good wages, patronage and subscription of various kinds stopped open protest from Negroes.
By springtime, he was supported by a rich merchant syndicate under the patronage of Henry, Prince of Wales.
The fact that he has cast over those materials the light of a skeptical mind does not make him any the less Southern, I rather think, for the South has been no more solid than other regions except in the political and related areas where patronage and force and intimidation and fear may produce a surface uniformity.

Synset Examples

his vigorous backing of the conservatives got him in trouble with progressives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or backing given to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'patronatus' (protection, sponsorship), from 'patronus' (patron).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənɪdʒ / (BrE)
the business or support given by customers to a shop, restaurant, or other commercial establishment

Examples

I must say, I'm sorry to be losing your business.

Synset Examples

even before noon there was a considerable patronage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - the commercial exchange or business conducted by a shop or industry
  • Synonym: custom - the regular patronage given to a business, especially by returning customers

Etymology

From Latin patronagium, from patronus (protector, master).
