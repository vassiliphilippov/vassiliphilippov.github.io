Bolshevik

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlʃəˌvɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɒlʃəˌvɪk / (BrE)
A member of the Russian Social-Democratic Workers' Party that seized power in the October Revolution of 1917 and later became the Communist Party of the Soviet Union; also, an extreme radical or revolutionary.

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Menshevik - member of the moderate faction opposed to the Bolsheviks in the Russian socialist movement

Etymology

From Russian 'bol'shevik', from 'bol'shoy' meaning 'greater' (referring to the majority faction).
