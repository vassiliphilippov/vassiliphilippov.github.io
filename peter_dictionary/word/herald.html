herald

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛr.əld / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.əld / (BrE)
to praise or announce publicly and enthusiastically, often as a sign of future success or greatness

Examples

He was heralded as "Sportsman of the Year "by Sports Illustrated, and last night was acclaimed in Rochester as the "Professional Athlete of the Year ", a distinction that earned for him the $ 10000 diamond - studded Hickok Belt.

Synset Examples

The critics hailed the young pianist as a new Rubinstein

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaim - to praise enthusiastically and publicly
  • Synonym: hail - to greet or acclaim enthusiastically

Etymology

From Old French 'herault', from Medieval Latin 'heraldus', meaning a messenger or official at a tournament.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛrəld / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəld / (BrE)
to be a sign that something is about to happen; to foreshadow or presage.

Examples

During the Dorr trial the Democratic press condemned the proceedings and heralded Dorr as a martyr to the principles of the Declaration of Independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foretell - to predict or forecast something before it happens
  • Synonym: predict - to say that an event will happen before it occurs

Etymology

From Old French 'herault', from Germanic origins, related to announcing.
