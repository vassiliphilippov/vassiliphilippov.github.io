casebook

1. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪsˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsbʊk / (BrE)
used to describe something that is a typical or classic example of a particular kind, especially as described in textbooks or casebooks

Examples

Landesco thought him "just a superior sort of plugugly "but he was, in fact, with his aggression and hostility, and nerveless indifference to risking or administering pain, a casebook psychopath.

Synset Examples

a casebook schizophrenic
a textbook example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: textbook - typical; characteristic of a classic example

Etymology

From 'casebook' (noun), referring to a book with records of cases for study.
