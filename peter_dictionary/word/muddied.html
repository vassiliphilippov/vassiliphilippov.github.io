muddied

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌdid / (AmE), / ˈmʌdid / (BrE)
(of color) having lost brightness or clarity due to the presence of impurities; not clear or vivid.

Examples

Underneath him the sea was a dark and muddied gray.

Synset Examples

dirty
a dirty (or dingy) white
the muddied grey of the sea
muddy colors
dirty-green walls
dirty-blonde hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - (of color) dull or dirty in appearance
  • Antonym: clear - free from impurities; bright or easily seen

Etymology

Past participle of 'mud' (from Middle English 'mudde').
