sergeant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
A noncommissioned officer in the armed forces, ranking above a corporal.

Examples

This is not unlike the order received by the sergeant of an army motor pool: "Four trucks to Fort Mason gym, 7: 30 tonight, for hauling girls to dance.
A Yankee sergeant gave the following description of his sweetheart: "My girl is none of your one-horse girls.
Even the mess sergeant, Bill Brown, a dapper, cocky transfer from an airborne division, went out on the range.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: corporal - a military rank below sergeant
  • Degree Higher: staff sergeant - a military rank directly above sergeant

Etymology

From Old French 'sergent', from Latin 'servientem' (servant, soldier).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
A police officer holding the rank of sergeant, typically responsible for supervising lower-ranking officers and overseeing particular units or shifts.

He put his chin on his kneecap, stretching his neck like that of a turkey on a chopping block, and stared wordlessly at his sergeant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: officer - a person holding a position of authority in the police or armed forces
  • Degree Higher: lieutenant - a police officer of a rank above sergeant

Etymology

From Old French *sergent* (servant, officer), from Latin *serviens* (servant).
