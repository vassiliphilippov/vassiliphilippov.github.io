punster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌnstər / (AmE), / ˈpʌnstə / (BrE)
A person who often makes puns, especially habitually or to excess.

Examples

Joseph Jastrow, the younger son of the distinguished rabbi, Marcus Jastrow, was a friendly, round-faced fellow with a little mustache, whose field was psychology, and who was also a punster and a jolly tease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pun - a play on words exploiting different possible meanings

Etymology

From 'pun' (a play on words) + suffix '-ster' (denoting a person engaged in an activity).
