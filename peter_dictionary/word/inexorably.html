inexorably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɛksərəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛks(ə)rəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be stopped or changed; relentlessly or unyieldingly.

Examples

It is like a mysterious epidemic which, starting first with Abigail and Parris, spreads inexorably with a dreadfully growing virulence through the whole town until all have been infected by it.

Synset Examples

time marches on inexorably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentlessly - in a way that does not lessen in intensity or severity; persistently
  • Synonym: unstoppably - in a way that cannot be stopped
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or soft manner; not harshly or relentlessly
  • Antonym: mercifully - in a way that shows mercy, as opposed to being harsh or unyielding

Etymology

From 'inexorable', from Latin 'inexorabilis' (that cannot be moved by entreaty), with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
