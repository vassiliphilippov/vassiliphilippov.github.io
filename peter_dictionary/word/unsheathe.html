unsheathe

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈʃið / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃiːð / (BrE)
To draw (a weapon or other object) from its sheath or covering, especially a sword or knife.

Examples

In her first song she waved away one encroaching photographer who dared approach the throne unbidden and thereafter the boys with the cameras had to unsheathe their 300 mm. lenses and shoot at extreme range.

Synset Examples

the knight unsheathed his sword

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sheathe - to put (a weapon or blade) into its sheath

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not, opposite) + 'sheathe' (to put in a sheath).
