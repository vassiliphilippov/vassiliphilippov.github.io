outrun

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈrʌn / (AmE), / aʊtˈrʌn / (BrE)
To run faster than someone or something; to surpass in running speed or distance.

Examples

If we cluster together, the redcoats can make an advantage out of it, but there's not a blessed thing they can do with two or three of us except chase us, and we can outrun them ".
Reputedly it could outrun, underwater, the fastest destroyers.

Synset Examples

in this race, I managed to outran everybody else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outpace - to move or develop faster than; to surpass in speed
  • Synonym: overtake - to catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction

Etymology

From out- (a prefix meaning 'beyond') + run, formed in Middle English.
