thermostatics

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜɹmoʊˈstætɪks / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈstætɪks / (BrE)
the branch of thermodynamics that deals with systems in thermal equilibrium

Examples

In the above development we have applied the thermodynamics of equilibrium (referred to by some as thermostatics) to the steady state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermodynamics - the branch of physics concerned with heat and temperature and their relation to energy and work

Etymology

From thermodynamics, modeled after static (relating to equilibrium).
