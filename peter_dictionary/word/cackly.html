cackly

1. ADJ

/ ˈkækli / (AmE), / ˈkækli / (BrE)
(of a sound) resembling the sharp, broken cry or squawk of a hen, especially after it has laid an egg.

Examples

The events of the last quarter of an hour, mysterious to any bird accustomed only to the predictable life of coop and barnyard, had overcome the doctor's hen and she gave out a series of cackly wails, perhaps mourning her nest, but briefly enjoyed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - a sharp, noisy cry; esp. of a hen

Etymology

From 'cackle' + '-y', meaning having the quality of a cackle.
