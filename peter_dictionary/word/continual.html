continual

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əl / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əl / (BrE)
Occurring repeatedly or frequently; recurring at short intervals over a prolonged period, though not necessarily uninterrupted.

Examples

Since there is a continual loss of micrometeoritic material in space because of the radiation effects, there must be a continual replenishment: otherwise, micrometeorites would have disappeared from interplanetary space.

Synset Examples

the continual banging of the shutters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuous - without interruption or break
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'continuel', from Latin 'continuus' (uninterrupted, continuous).
