diligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
careful and persistent effort or attention to a task; conscientiousness in performing one's duties

Examples

I do n't think his diligence mitigates your negligence, Matson ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: industriousness - persistent, hard-working effort
  • Antonym: negligence - failure to take proper care

Etymology

From Latin 'diligentia' (attentiveness, carefulness), from 'diligere' (to value highly, take delight in).
