totalistic

1. ADJ

/ toʊtəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / təʊtəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Related to the principles of totalitarianism, especially where the state, group, or ideology seeks to regulate every aspect of life.

Examples

Observers can see a person engaged in spontaneous behavior, and watch him operating in a totalistic fashion.

Synset Examples

totalitarian theory and practice
operating in a totalistic fashion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalitarian - advocating absolute centralized control by the state

Etymology

From 'total' + '-istic', modeled after 'totalitarian'.
