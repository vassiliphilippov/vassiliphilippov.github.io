embolden

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈboʊldən / (AmE), / ɪmˈbəʊldən / (BrE)
to give someone the courage or confidence to do or say something

Examples

This co-operation has emboldened other Southern whites to add their voices to demands for peaceable accommodation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encourage - to give someone support, confidence, or hope
  • Antonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or hopeful

Etymology

From Old French 'emboldir', from 'en-' (make) + 'bold'.
