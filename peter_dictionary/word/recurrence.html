recurrence

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɜr.əns / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜː.rəns / (BrE)
The act or fact of happening again, especially at regular intervals.

Examples

Not a circle, then, nor a straight line, but a spiral represents the shape of death as Irenaeus sees it; for a spiral has motion as well as recurrence.

Synset Examples

the return of spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - the act of coming or going back
  • Synonym: repetition - the action of repeating something
  • Antonym: cessation - the process of ending or being brought to an end

Etymology

From Latin recurro (to run back), via Medieval Latin recurrentia.
