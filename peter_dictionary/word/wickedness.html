wickedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being wicked; great evil or moral wrongdoing.

Examples

It is not enough in accounting for this feeling to analyze it into the wickedness of individual people added together to produce a cumulative effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality and wickedness

Etymology

From Old English *wicednes*, from *wicede* ('wicked') + *-ness* (noun suffix).
