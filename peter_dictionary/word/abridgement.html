abridgement

1. NOUN

/ əˈbrɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əˈbrɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
A shortened version of a book, article, or other written work, keeping the essential content.

Examples

Reader's Digest condensed books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensation - the act or product of making something shorter, especially a written work
  • Synonym: abridged - (adjective) having been shortened while keeping main ideas

Etymology

From Middle English 'abregement', from Old French 'abregement', from 'abregier' (to shorten), from Latin 'abbreviare'.
