whir

1. NOUN

/ wɝː / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
a continuous, low, vibrating sound caused by something moving rapidly

Examples

The gentle whir of each footstep left him more naked than before, until he felt his unprotected flesh tremble, chilled by each new sound.

Synset Examples

whir of a bird's wings
the whir of the propellers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirr - continuous, resonant sound as of machinery or wings in motion

2. VERB

/ wɜr / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
(of a machine or object) to make a soft, continuous swishing or buzzing sound.

Examples

The sounds issuing from beyond - winches whirring, men shouting - indicated great activity and excited me.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whizz - move or cause to move quickly with a whistling or whooshing sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound described.
