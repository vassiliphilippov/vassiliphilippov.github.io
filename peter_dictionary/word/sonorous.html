sonorous

1. ADJ

/ səˈnɔrəs / (AmE), / səˈnɒrəs / (BrE)
(of a person's voice or other sound) imposingly deep and full; having a rich, clear, and loud tone.

Examples

As he declaimed the sonorous measures, it was as much as Claire could do to restrain herself from bursting out with her dramatic tidings.

Synset Examples

heavy sounds
a herald chosen for his sonorous voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonant - (of sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • Synonym: mellifluous - (of a voice or words) sweet or musical; pleasant to hear
  • Antonym: shrill - (of a sound) high-pitched and piercing

Etymology

From Latin 'sonorus' (resounding), from 'sonor' (sound).
