panic

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk / (BrE)
A sudden, uncontrollable feeling of fear or anxiety, often causing wildly unthinking behavior.

Examples

He was n't troubled with the ordinary, rank-and-file fear that overcomes and paralyzes and sends individual soldiers and whole companies under fire running in panic.
By the second day Michelangelo began to worry; and by the second night he was in a state of panic: he could see nothing out of the afflicted eye.
A rush of panic caught Sarah.
Later, she apologized for the long scratch across his face, tried to explain she could n't help herself, that the panic arose in her unwanted.
He fought the panic of vertigo.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: terror - intense, overpowering fear
  • Degree Lower: alarm - sudden fear or anxiety, usually less severe than panic

Etymology

From Greek *panikos* (pertaining to Pan, god of woods and fields), as sudden fear was believed to be caused by the god Pan.

2. VERB

/ ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk / (BrE)
To cause someone to feel sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety.

Examples

It was not exactly panic they gave way to, but they could not just sit there.

Synset Examples

The mere thought of an isolation cell panicked the prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrify - to frighten someone greatly
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful and free from fear

Etymology

From Greek *panikos*, meaning 'pertaining to Pan', the god believed to cause sudden fear.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk / (BrE)
A sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often affecting a large group of people and typically arising from a perceived threat.

Examples

In blind panic of grief she accepted Jonathan's dictum, and believed in her desperation that she had been cursed by God.
It could be argued that any fellowship which centers in residential neighborhoods is doomed to become an expression of the panic for stable identity among the middle classes.

Synset Examples

panic in the stock market
a war scare
a bomb scare led them to evacuate the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terror - intense fear; extreme fright
  • Antonym: calm - absence of fear or anxiety

Etymology

From Greek 'panikos', literally 'pertaining to Pan', from the belief that the god Pan caused sudden fear.

4. VERB

/ ˈpænɪk / (AmE), / ˈpanɪk / (BrE)
to be suddenly overcome by uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often resulting in unthinking or frantic behavior.

Examples

The rhinos would panic, screaming' Help'!
- Don't panic. - I won't panic.
- Don't panic. - I won't panic.

Synset Examples

The students panicked when told that final exams were less than a week away

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or fear

Etymology

From Greek 'panikos' (of Pan), referring to sudden fear attributed to the god Pan.
