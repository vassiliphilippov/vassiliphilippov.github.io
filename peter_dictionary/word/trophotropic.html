trophotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtroʊfoʊˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrɒfəˈtrəʊpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from a response involving trophotropism; often used to describe the physiological state or nervous system activity associated with rest, digestion, or energy conservation.

Examples

The feeding pain antagonism seems to be based on this reciprocal relation between the tropho - and ergotropic systems.

Etymology

From Greek 'trophē' (nourishment) + 'tropos' (turn) + '-ic'.
