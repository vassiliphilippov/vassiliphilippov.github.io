Pulitzer

1. PROPN

/ ˈpʊlɪtsər / (AmE), / ˈpʊlɪtsə / (BrE)
Joseph Pulitzer (1847–1911), a Hungarian-born American newspaper publisher who established the Pulitzer Prizes.

Examples

- No. What about our Pulitzer Prize?

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pulitzer Prize - a prestigious annual award in journalism, literature, and music

Etymology

From the surname of Joseph Pulitzer, after whom the prizes are named.
