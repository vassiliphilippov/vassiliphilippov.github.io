handy

1. ADJ

/ ˈhændi / (AmE), / ˈhændi / (BrE)
(of objects) easy to reach or conveniently located.

Examples

"Have you got our keys handy "?

Synset Examples

found a handy spot for the can opener

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convenient - suited to one's comfort, needs, or purpose; easy to use or get to

Etymology

From hand + -y, meaning 'within easy reach of the hand'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhændi / (AmE), / ˈhændi / (BrE)
(of an object) convenient and easy to use or handle.

Examples

"Just you wait ", said Arlene again, as though she were discovering the pleasantly tingling insinuations of that handy little sturdy statement.

Synset Examples

a handy gadget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convenient - fitting in well with a person's needs or plans; easy to use
  • Antonym: awkward - difficult to use or handle

Etymology

From Old English 'handig' (dexterous, ready at hand), from 'hand' + -y.
