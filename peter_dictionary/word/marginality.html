marginality

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrdʒɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdʒɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the state or condition of being on the edge or fringes of a group, society, or area; lack of central or mainstream status

Examples

In upper teen Jewish life, the non college group tends to have a sense of marginality.
They have a sense of marginality at being denied that special badge of status, the out-of-town school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mainstream - the ideas, attitudes, or activities that are regarded as normal or conventional; the dominant trend
  • Antonym: centrality - the quality of being central or essential

Etymology

From 'marginal' + '-ity', patterned after similar formations such as 'centrality'.
