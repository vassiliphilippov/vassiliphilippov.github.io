conspire

1. VERB

/ kənˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / kənˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to secretly plan with others to do something illegal or harmful

Examples

"It is not necessary that a defendant actually have conspired to use the U. S. mails to defraud as long as there is evidence of a conspiracy, and the mails were then used to carry it out ".
In 1920, as the startling news that the 1919 White Sox had conspired to lose the World Series leaked out, fans grew disillusioned and disinterested in baseball.
Other FBI men, talking with the pilot from the tower, conspired with him to delay the proposed flight to Havana.

Synset Examples

They conspired to overthrow the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - to make a secret plan, especially to do something illegal or harmful
  • Synonym: connive - to secretly allow or be involved in wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin conspirare (to breathe together, agree, plot), from con- (together) + spirare (to breathe).

2. VERB

/ kənˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / kənˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to make secret plans jointly with others to commit an unlawful or harmful act

Examples

After all, it goes back to the days in which sedition was not un-American, the days in which the Sons of St. Tammany conspired to overthrow the government by force and violence - the British government that is.
Refrigeration, automated processing and packaging conspire to defy season and banish spoilage.

Synset Examples

The two companies conspired to cause the value of the stock to fall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collaborate - work jointly on an activity, especially in a positive sense
  • Synonym: plot - to make secret plans to carry out something illegal or harmful
  • Synonym: scheme - make secret or underhanded plans

Etymology

From Latin conspirare (to breathe together, agree, plot).
