awed

1. ADJ

/ ɑd / (AmE), / ɔːd / (BrE)
Filled with a feeling of respect, fear, or wonder; inspired by awe.

Examples

At that moment Kipling was overwhelmed with awed amazement, suddenly recalling that these identical details of scene, action and word had occurred to him in a dream six weeks earlier.
Andy's co-workers kept their distance, awed by the tragedy.

Synset Examples

awed by the silence
awful worshippers with bowed heads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazed - filled with great surprise or astonishment
  • Synonym: reverent - showing deep respect and admiration
  • Antonym: unmoved - not affected or influenced emotionally

Etymology

From the noun "awe," which comes from Old Norse "agi" (fear, terror).
