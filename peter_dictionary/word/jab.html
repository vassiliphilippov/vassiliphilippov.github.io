jab

1. VERB

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒæb / (BrE)
to strike or poke with a quick, short, and sudden blow

Examples

Through the swathings of terror, she jabbed deceit's sharp point - Amy would be reborn, a new child, with new parents, living under new circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to prod or push with something pointed
  • Synonym: thrust - to push suddenly or violently in a specified direction

2. VERB

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒab / (BrE)
to poke or thrust something sharply or abruptly

Examples

Intelligence jabbed at him accusingly.
But the Indian was jabbing another bottle toward Johnson.

Synset Examples

he jabbed his finger into her ribs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - to push something with a finger or a sharp object
  • Degree Higher: stab - to push a pointed object into someone or something

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; perhaps imitative of a sharp blow, or related to 'job' (poke, stab) in dialect.

3. NOUN

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒæb / (BrE)
a quick, short, straight punch, especially in boxing

Examples

Cobb unleashed a single powerful jab that sent Gyp reeling wildly and crashing down with a whining groan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hook - a punch in boxing delivered with a bent arm and a swinging motion
  • Synonym: uppercut - an upward punch in boxing directed at an opponent's chin

4. NOUN

/ dʒæb / (AmE), / dʒæb / (BrE)
A quick, sharp hand movement or thrust, especially with a pointed finger or an object.

Examples

Suddenly the Spanish became an English in which only one word emerged with clarity and precision, "son of a bitch ", sometimes hyphenated by vicious jabs of a beer bottle into Johnson's quivering ribs.

Synset Examples

he warned me with a jab with his finger
he made a thrusting motion with his fist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poke - a quick push with something pointed
  • Synonym: thrust - a sudden or forceful forward movement
