crook

1. NOUN

/ krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a part of something that is bent or curved, especially the inside angle of a joint such as the elbow or knee

Examples

She fell asleep leaning on her hand, hearing the house creaking as though it were a living a private life of its own these two hundred years, hearing the birds rustling in their cages and the occasional whirring of wings as one of them landed on the table and walked across the newspaper to perch in the crook of her arm.

Synset Examples

a bend in the road
a crook in the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - a curved section or part
  • Antonym: straight - having no bends or curves

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *krókr* (hook, bend).

2. NOUN

/ krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is dishonest or a criminal, especially one who engages in theft or fraud

Examples

Ace of spades - a widow, that was what they called a widow, these low-class crooks remembered Mr. Skyros distractedly.
An' that could mean trouble with a fella that's workin' for crooks.
Not for an old crook like me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: felon - a person who has been convicted of a serious crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding - observing or obeying the law

Etymology

Originally meant 'hook' or 'bend' (from Old Norse 'krókr'), possibly alluding to the bent nature of dishonesty.
