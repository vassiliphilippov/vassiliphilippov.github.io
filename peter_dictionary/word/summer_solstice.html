summer solstice

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ ˈsɒl.stɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə ˈsɒl.stɪs / (BrE)
The time at which the sun reaches its highest position in the sky as seen from the Northern Hemisphere, occurring around June 21 and marking the longest day of the year.

Examples

The final step was a vote for a $ 230000 bond issue for the construction of a sewage system by the 1959 town meeting, later confirmed by a two-thirds vote at a special town meeting June 21, 1960.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winter solstice - when the sun is at its southernmost point, around December 21

Etymology

From Latin 'solstitium', from 'sol' (sun) + 'stitium' (a stoppage).
