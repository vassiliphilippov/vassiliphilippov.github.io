appease

1. VERB

/ əˈpiz / (AmE), / əˈpiːz / (BrE)
to calm or pacify someone by acceding to their demands or by making concessions, especially so as to gain favor or maintain peace

Examples

Was not this dropped primarily in order to "appease "the Chinese - especially after "Khrushchev's "humiliating "surrender to the West in canceling the German peace-treaty deadline of December 31?
The Abernathy furnace consumed fuel like a giant ravenous maw that had to be appeased by hurling tons of coal into its evil red depths, and no matter how much coal they put in the house remained cold.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacify - to bring peace or calm to a person or situation
  • Synonym: mollify - to soothe or calm the anger or feelings of someone
  • Antonym: provoke - to anger or incite someone; to make a situation worse or more intense

Etymology

From Old French *apaiser*, based on Latin *ad-* (to) + *pax* (peace).

2. VERB

/ əˈpiz / (AmE), / əˈpiːz / (BrE)
To make (someone) less angry or hostile, especially by giving in to demands; to bring about a state of peace or calm.

Examples

But he rejects, perhaps a little too sweepingly, the theory that disloyal and pro-Communist influences may have contributed to the policy of appeasing Stalin which persisted until after the end of the war and reached its high point at the Yalta Conference in February, 1945.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placate - to make someone less angry or hostile
  • Antonym: antagonize - to make someone angry; do the opposite of appease

Etymology

From Old French apaisier, based on Latin ad- (to) + pax (peace).

3. VERB

/ əˈpiz / (AmE), / əˈpiːz / (BrE)
to reduce or satisfy a feeling, demand, or need; to bring to a state of peace or calm

Examples

To the Zen monk the universe is still populated with "spiritual beings "who have to be appeased.

Synset Examples

quell my hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - make someone quiet or less agitated
  • Synonym: soothe - gently reduce pain or agitation
  • Antonym: aggravate - make a situation or feeling worse

Etymology

From Old French *apaisier* (to pacify), from Vulgar Latin *adpacificare*, from Latin *ad* (to) + *pacem* (peace).
