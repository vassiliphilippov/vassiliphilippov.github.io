slant

1. VERB

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
(of a line, surface, or object) to lie or extend at an angle; to slope or lean in a particular direction.

Examples

And he was handsome, despite the long thin scar that slanted across his cheek.

Synset Examples

A scar slanted across his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • Synonym: lean - to incline or bend from a vertical position
  • Antonym: level - to make or become horizontal, having no slope

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse slǫndr (sloping).

2. NOUN

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
The slope or degree of deviation from a horizontal or level position, especially of a surface.

Examples

Yet everywhere else sameness was stucco and wood in square blocks - like fortresses perched against the slant of the hill, rising with the hill to the top where the church was and beyond that to the cemetery.

Synset Examples

the roof had a steep pitch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slope - an inclined surface or line; angle of deviation from a horizontal line
  • Synonym: incline - an upward or downward slant or slope
  • Synonym: tilt - a sloping position or movement

Etymology

From Middle English slenten (to slope), of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect slenta (to slope).

3. NOUN

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
a particular point of view or way of presenting information, especially one that is biased

Examples

Are you experimenting with different selling slants in developing new customers?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - an inclination or prejudice for or against something

Etymology

From Old Norse slanta (to slope). The meaning of 'point of view' derives from the idea of sloping away from directness or neutrality.

4. VERB

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to present information in a way that shows bias or supports a particular viewpoint

Examples

In another approach to the same procedure, the content of the readings is analyzed so as to see how the particular medium is likely to slant her statements.

Synset Examples

He biased his presentation so as to please the share holders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - to influence in a particular, often unfair, direction
