jimmy

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪmi / (BrE)
to force open (a door, window, or lock) with a crowbar or similar lever.

Examples

Sometime on Saturday evening, August 22nd, while my family and I were dining at the Hostaria dell 'Orso, in Rome, you jimmied a window of our home in Bucks County, Pennsylvania, and let yourselves into the premises.

Synset Examples

The burglar jimmied the lock
Raccoons managed to pry the lid off the garbage pail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pry - to use force in order to move or open something

Etymology

From the noun 'jimmy' (a short crowbar used by burglars), origin unknown, first attested early 19th century.
