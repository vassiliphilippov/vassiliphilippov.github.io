undress

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to remove one's clothes, particularly in preparation for bed, a bath, or a change of attire

Examples

The other players were undressing quietly.
They lived mainly in the kitchen; they moved Maggie's bed and the baby's basket there, and the rest of them undressed by the stove and ran groaning and shivering to the upper polar regions and plunged into icy beds.

Synset Examples

please don't undress in front of everybody!
She strips in front of strangers every night for a living

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dress - to put on clothes or to clothe oneself
  • Synonym: strip - to remove all of one's clothing or to remove someone's clothing completely

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + dress; attested since the late 16th century.
