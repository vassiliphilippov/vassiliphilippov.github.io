soar

1. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to rise rapidly or increase quickly to a high level

Examples

She soared over the new pastor like an avenging angel lest he stray from the path and not know all the truth and gossip of which she was chief repository.
Soaring to stardom along with him, Margo Felicity Brighetti, a luscious and curvaceously beguiling Italian starlet, turns in a creditable performance as an airplane mechanic.
Some gracefully soared from the backs of their wounded, screaming mounts to make one last defiant charge before the lead split their hearts or tore their guts.

Synset Examples

the dollar soared against the yen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • Synonym: rocket - to rise very quickly and suddenly

Etymology

From Middle English 'soren', from Old French 'essorer' (to soar, to fly upward).

2. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to rise or increase rapidly to a high level

Examples

They 'd peddled the soap virtually alone, and without much success, until about a year ago, when - with the addition of "SX-21 "to their secret formula and the inauguration of a high-powered advertising campaign - sales had soared practically into orbit.

Synset Examples

The stock market soared after the cease-fire was announced

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • Synonym: rocket - to rise dramatically and quickly

Etymology

From Old French 'essorer', from Late Latin 'exaurare' (to rise into the air).

3. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
To fly or glide high in the air, especially by means of a glider or hang glider.

Examples

His spare time is spent in soaring gliders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously through air or over a surface

Etymology

From Old French 'esser', from Latin 'exsurgere' (to rise up).

4. VERB

/ sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to fly or rise high in the air

Examples

I tell you, those voices soared... higher and farther than anybody in a grey place dares to dream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascend - to move upward
  • Antonym: descend - to move downward

Etymology

From Old French 'essaurer', based on Latin 'ex-' (out) + 'aurum' (air).
