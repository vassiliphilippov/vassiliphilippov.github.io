opportunism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpərˈtunɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of taking advantage of opportunities as they arise, often with little or no regard for ethical principles or the consequences for others.

Examples

fearful of being mistaken for a Jew, he seeks protection in his Nazi uniform; clinging to the enemy Jew idea, he is forced to overcome habits of politeness and neighborliness; once in power he begins to give vent to a criminal opportunism that causes him to alternate between megalomania and envy of those above him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-interest - regard for one's own advantage or welfare
  • Antonym: altruism - unselfish concern for the welfare of others

Etymology

Formed from 'opportunity' + '-ism', modeled on French opportunisme or directly from Latin opportunus (opportune).
