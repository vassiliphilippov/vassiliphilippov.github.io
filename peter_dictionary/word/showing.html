showing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
an event or occasion where something is displayed or presented to the public, such as a performance, exhibition, or screening.

Examples

Treat them right and they 'll make a showing every month in the year except the frigid ones.

Synset Examples

the museum had many exhibits of oriental art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhibit - a public display of works of art or items of interest
  • Synonym: presentation - the act of showing something to an audience
  • Synonym: performance - an act of presenting a play, concert, or other form of entertainment

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'show' + the suffix '-ing', denoting the act or result of showing.

/ ˈʃoʊ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.ɪŋ / (BrE)

Related Terms
