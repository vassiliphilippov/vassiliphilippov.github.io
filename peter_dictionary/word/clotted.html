clotted

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɑtɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɒtɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) thickened or formed into soft, thick lumps; having formed clots or masses.

Examples

Two clotted balls the color of mucus rolled between fiery lids.

Synset Examples

clotted blood
seeds clogged together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulated - changed into a soft thick mass, especially by chemical process
  • Antonym: fluid - not thickened or coalesced; able to flow easily

Etymology

From the verb 'clot', from Old English *clott* (lump, mass).
