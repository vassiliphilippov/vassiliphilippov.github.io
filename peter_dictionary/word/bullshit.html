bullshit

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊlˌʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊlʃɪt / (BrE)
to speak insincerely, deceptively, or without regard for facts or truth, often to mislead or impress

Examples

All he does is to bullshit with his squad, and they are the stupidest bastards around.
Yeah, well, let'’s cut out the bullshit.

Synset Examples

The politician was not well prepared for the debate and faked it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to say something untrue with intent to deceive
  • Degree Lower: fib - to tell a small or harmless lie
  • Synonym: deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true

Etymology

From 'bull' (nonsense) + 'shit' (excrement); originally US slang, 20th century.
