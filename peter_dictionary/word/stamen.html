stamen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪmən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪmən / (BrE)
The male reproductive organ of a flower, typically consisting of a filament and an anther where pollen is produced.

Examples

It is not difficult to see that the stamens of the catkin are always arranged in pairs, and that each individual flower is nothing but one such pair standing on a green, black tipped little scale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pistil - the female reproductive organ of a flower

Etymology

From Latin 'stamen' (thread, warp), referring to the threadlike shape.
