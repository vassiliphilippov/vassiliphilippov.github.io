enrollee

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈroʊli / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊli / (BrE)
A person who is enrolled in a course, program, or institution.

Examples

A training program in a depressed area may have few enrollees unless there is some apparent prospect for better employment opportunities afterwards, and the prospect may be poor if the training is aimed solely at jobs in the local community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: registrant - a person who registers for something, such as an event or competition

Etymology

From French *enrôler* (to enroll), influenced by English enroll + -ee.
