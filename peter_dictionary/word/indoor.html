indoor

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌdɔː / (BrE)
Situated, intended for, or taking place within a building rather than outside.

Examples

The crowd at the twenty-first annual K. of C. Games, final indoor meet of the season, got a thrill a few minutes earlier when a slender, bespectacled woman broke the one week old world record in the half-mile run.
Also, perhaps, table-tennis and other indoor sports to keep them fit and contented.

Synset Examples

indoor activities for a rainy day
an indoor pool

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outdoor - located, suited for, or taking place outside or in open air

Etymology

Formed from 'in' + 'door', indicating 'within the doors' of a building.
