ranging

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
(of thoughts, discussions, etc.) moving or wandering freely over a wide area or range, without restraint or fixed direction.

Examples

He had not even thought about her much except once or twice at night in bed when his slowly ranging thoughts would abruptly, almost accidentally, encounter her.
These examples underscore the importance of even more searching evaluations of new major development programs and even more penetrating and far ranging analyses of the potentialities of future technology.

Synset Examples

at night in bed...his slowly ranging thoughts...encountered her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering - moving aimlessly or without fixed course
  • Antonym: focused - concentrated on a specific point or subject

Etymology

Present participle of 'range', from Old French *rengier* (put in order), from *renc* (row, rank).
