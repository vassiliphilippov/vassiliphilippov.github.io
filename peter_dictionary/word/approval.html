approval

1. NOUN

/ əˈpruvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
Official permission or agreement for something to happen or proceed.

Examples

Committee approval of Gov. Price Daniel's "abandoned property "act seemed certain Thursday despite the adamant protests of Texas bankers.
The proposal would have to receive final legislative approval, by two-thirds majorities, before March 1 to be printed on the April 4 ballot, Roberts said.
Furnishes complete cost estimates for clients approval.
Interest rates are determined by the board of directors of the bank with the approval of the Farm Credit Administration.
Though, to be sure, we gave Kennedy no very positive approval in the margin of his preferment.

Synset Examples

he gave the project his blessing
his decision merited the approval of any sensible person

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disapproval - the feeling or act of not approving; refusal to accept or agree
  • Synonym: authorization - the action of giving official permission
  • Synonym: endorsement - an act of giving one's public approval or support

Etymology

From approve + -al; 'approve' from Old French 'aprover', from Latin 'approbare' (to assent to, to confirm).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpruːvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
a positive feeling or attitude toward something or someone, indicating that you think it is good or acceptable

Examples

He holds that goodness and badness lie in feelings of approval or disapproval.
There is therefore nothing about them to which an attitude of approval or condemnation could be fitting.
That, however, may also bring the police, if the thinking does not meet with social approval.

Synset Examples

although she fussed at them, she secretly viewed all her children with approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disapproval - a negative feeling or attitude toward something or someone

Etymology

From approve + -al, modeled on similar formations.

3. NOUN

/ əˈpruvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
official acceptance of something as satisfactory or suitable

Examples

It is far more difficult in many communities to obtain admission to an apprentice program which involves union approval than to get into the most selective medical school in the nation.

Synset Examples

he bought it on approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disapproval - refusal to accept something as satisfactory

Etymology

From approve + -al; 'approve' from Old French 'aprover', from Latin 'approbare' (to assent to, to approve).
