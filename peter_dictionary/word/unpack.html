unpack

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈpæk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpak / (BrE)
to remove the contents from a suitcase, box, or container; to take things out of their packaging

Examples

Those who had driven hundreds of miles for the burial would not go home, for she might die any time; but they might as well unpack their suitcases, for she might linger on.

Synset Examples

unpack the presents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pack - to put things into a suitcase or container

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (reversal) + 'pack' (to put into a container or package).
