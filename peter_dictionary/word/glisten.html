glisten

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlɪsən / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsən / (BrE)
to shine with a soft and reflected or sparkling light, often as if wet or moist

Examples

The fountain had brimmed over, the cannon were wet, the soldiers' monument glistened.
Vernon wore a gray tall hat, a gardenia, and maroon Wellington boots that glistened like currant jelly.
I could see their faces glistening with sweat and bear grease, their mouths open, shouting their spine-chilling cries.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glitter - to shine with a bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • Synonym: gleam - to shine with a steady light
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny or bright

Etymology

From Middle English glisnen, from Old English glisnian, related to glisten and gleam.
