chime

1. VERB

/ tʃaɪm / (AmE), / tʃaɪm / (BrE)
(of a bell, clock, or similar object) to make a clear ringing sound, often to indicate the time or as a signal.

Examples

[DOORBELL CHIMING] [SINGING SOFTLY IN ITALIAN] [BABY CRYING] [CONTINUES CRYING]

Synset Examples

bells and gongs chimed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - to produce a clear resonant or vibrating sound, as a bell
  • Synonym: toll - to sound slowly and repeatedly, as a bell (often for a funeral)

Etymology

From Old French *chimer* (to play upon bells), from Late Latin *camena* (song), from Latin *canere* (to sing).
