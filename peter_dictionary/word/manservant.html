manservant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌsɝːvənt / (AmE), / ˈmænˌsɜːvənt / (BrE)
A male servant who works in someone's household, especially in a historical or formal context.

Examples

She was told by the manservant who opened the door that his lordship was engaged on work from which he had left strict orders he was not to be disturbed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maidservant - female household servant

Etymology

Formed from 'man' and 'servant'.
