subscription

1. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an agreement to support or participate in something, typically formalized by signing one's name to a document

Examples

Good wages, patronage and subscription of various kinds stopped open protest from Negroes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement

Etymology

From Latin 'subscriptio' (a signing, signature), from 'subscribere' (to write underneath).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an arrangement to receive and pay for issues of a newspaper or magazine regularly for a specified period

Examples

An item in the letters column of a newspaper renewed a subscription, adding: "I personally enjoy your newspaper as much as my husband ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: subscription - the act of agreeing to receive and pay for a service (e.g. streaming, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin 'subscriptio', meaning 'something written underneath', from 'subscribere' (to write underneath, sign).
