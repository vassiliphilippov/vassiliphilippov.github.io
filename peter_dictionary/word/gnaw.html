gnaw

1. VERB

/ nɔ / (AmE), / nɔː / (BrE)
to bite or chew something repeatedly with the teeth, especially making it gradually smaller

Examples

Clumps of brush that they passed were so many enchained demons straining in anger to tear and gnaw on his bones.
Pa had his head up, glaring, his teeth gnawing at the place where he 'd grown a mustache once.

Synset Examples

gnaw an old cracker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew - to crush food or an object with the teeth

Etymology

From Old English *gnagan*, related to Old High German *gnagan* (to gnaw), from Proto-Germanic *gnaganą*.

2. VERB

/ nɔ / (AmE), / nɔː / (BrE)
(of an emotion, feeling, or force) to gradually wear away or erode something; to cause persistent or gradual deterioration.

Examples

A gnawing and gnashing within him.

Synset Examples

Her confidence eroded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erode - to gradually wear away or be worn away

Etymology

Old English gnagan, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch knagen and German nagen.
