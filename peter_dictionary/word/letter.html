Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French 'lettre', from Latin 'littera' (letter of the alphabet, message).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
(of a student) to be awarded an athletic letter, usually for playing on a varsity sports team.

Examples

Moritz is listed on the Longhorn roster as a right halfback, the position at which he lettered on the 1956 team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: athletic letter - a cloth letter awarded for achievement in school sports

Etymology

From the practice of awarding a cloth letter to high-achieving student athletes, especially in the US.
