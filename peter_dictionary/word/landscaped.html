landscaped

1. ADJ

/ ˈlænd.skeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈlæn.skeɪpt / (BrE)
(of land or an area) having been improved and made more attractive by planting trees, flowers, or shrubs, or by altering the design of the land.

Examples

He lived in an apartment house not over three or four years old, a reclaimed island of landscaped brick and glass on the fringe of the business district.

Synset Examples

carefully landscaped gardens

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimproved - (of land) not improved by landscaping or development

Etymology

From the verb 'landscape', which comes from Dutch 'landschap' (region, landscape).
