tidings

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdɪŋz / (BrE)
news or information about recent or important events, especially as reported or announced

Examples

As he declaimed the sonorous measures, it was as much as Claire could do to restrain herself from bursting out with her dramatic tidings.
The Boulevard de la Madeleine, the Boulevard Malesherbes, and the Rue Royale ran to it with graceful flattery, bearing tidings of the Age of Reason.

Synset Examples

they awaited news of the outcome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news - new information about recent events

Etymology

From Old English 'tīdung' (announcement, event), related to 'tīd' (time, occasion).
