belated

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈleɪtɪd / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪtɪd / (BrE)
Happening or arriving later than expected or desired; delayed.

Examples

The flavor of Baltimore's Florida Grapefruit League news ripened considerably late today when the Orioles were advised that Ron Hansen has fulfilled his obligations under the Army's military training program and is ready for belated spring training.
I think it'’s too late for changes, Michael.

Synset Examples

a belated birthday card
I'm late for the plane
the train is late
tardy children are sent to the principal
always tardy in making dental appointments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late - occurring or coming after the expected time
  • Antonym: timely - occurring at the expected or right time
  • Antonym: early - happening before the expected or usual time

Etymology

From be- (prefix) + late (Middle English, from Old English 'læt').
