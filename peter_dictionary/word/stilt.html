stilt

1. NOUN

/ stɪlt / (AmE), / stɪlt / (BrE)
a long post or column of wood, steel, or concrete that is set upright in the ground to support a building above the surface or water

Examples

The Palace was an elaborate establishment, built practically on stilts in front, with long flights of wooden steps running up to the porch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pile - a heavy post driven into the ground to support a structure above it

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'stilt', meaning 'crutch' or 'support', of uncertain origin.
