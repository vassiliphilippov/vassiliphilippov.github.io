wildly

1. ADV

/ ˈwaɪldli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldli / (BrE)
To an extreme or exaggerated degree; in a very uncontrolled or intense way.

Examples

The storyline, in sort, is wildly unrealistic.
It was nothing, though his heart was thumping wildly.
He stood very still, his heart thumping wildly.
He looked wildly around, at the now empty street, at the mud, at the rain.
Watson gesticulated wildly.

Synset Examples

the storyline is wildly unrealistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Antonym: mildly - to a small or slight degree, not extreme

Etymology

From 'wild' + '-ly', formed in Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈwaɪldli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldli / (BrE)
In an uncontrolled or unrestrained manner; without restraint or discipline.

Examples

He slapped the buckskin again and it kicked wildly, its hoofs rattling the side of the stall.

Synset Examples

He gesticulated wildly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiercely - in a forceful or intense manner
  • Synonym: recklessly - without thinking about risks or consequences
  • Antonym: calmly - in a controlled or tranquil manner
  • Antonym: carefully - in a way that is controlled or with intention
