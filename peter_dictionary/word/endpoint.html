endpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɛndpɔɪnt / (BrE)
The point at which something ends; the final point or limit of a process, line, or structure.

Examples

Indeed, the spiral f, with the two endpoints** connected by a straight line possesses only one inscribed square.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: startpoint - the place or point where something begins
  • Synonym: termination - the act of ending or concluding something

Etymology

Formed from 'end' + 'point', both of Germanic origin.
