plowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplaʊɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of turning over and breaking up soil with a plow, especially in preparation for planting crops.

Examples

You will probably want to hire someone to do the plowing, however.
These tractors are not entirely satisfactory for plowing, particularly on heavier soils, so you may still want to hire someone to do the plowing.

Synset Examples

he hired someone to do the plowing for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilling - the preparation and cultivation of land for growing crops

Etymology

From the verb 'plow', ultimately from Old English *plōg* (plow, plowing implement).
