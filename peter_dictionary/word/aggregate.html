aggregate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡ.rə.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡət / (BrE)
A total amount formed by combining separate units or items; the whole sum or entirety.

Examples

Throughout the history of the program, state government expenditures in the aggregate have usually matched or exceeded the Federal expenditures, while local districts all together have spent more than either Federal or state governments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum - the total amount resulting from the addition of two or more numbers, amounts, or items
  • Synonym: total - the whole number or amount
  • Antonym: part - a piece or segment that forms a portion of something

Etymology

From Latin aggregatus (added to), past participle of aggregare, from ad- (to) + grex, greg- (a flock).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡət / (BrE)
formed by the collection of separate units into a mass or whole; total or combined

Examples

We are concerned with aggregate demand for the industry's product.
In any given time period, the aggregate demand for the industry's product is determined by two things: the price charged by the industry, and the level of GNP.

Synset Examples

aggregate expenses include expenses of all divisions combined for the entire year
the aggregated amount of indebtedness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: total - comprising the whole amount; complete sum

Etymology

From Latin aggregatus, past participle of aggregare (to add to), from ad- (to) + grex (flock).
