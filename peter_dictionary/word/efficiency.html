efficiency

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
The ability to do something well, successfully, and without wasting time or resources.

Examples

However, the jury said it believes "these two offices should be combined to achieve greater efficiency and reduce the cost of administration ".
Thus technical efficiency is achieved at the expense of actual experience.

(2) The patient attained an astonishing efficiency in a new trade.

Synset Examples

she did the work with great efficiency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficiency - lack of efficiency; wasteful use of resources or time
  • Synonym: effectiveness - degree to which something is successful in producing a desired result

Etymology

From Latin efficientia (efficacy, power to accomplish), from efficere (to accomplish).
