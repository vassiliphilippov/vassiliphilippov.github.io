blatancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈbleɪtənsi / (BrE)
The quality of being extremely obvious and often offensively conspicuous.

Examples

This dinner was the start of a new blatancy in the relationship between the gangs and the politicians, which, prior to 1924, says Pasley, "had been maintained with more or less stealth ", but which henceforth was marked by these ostentatious gatherings, denounced by a clergyman as "Belshazzar feasts ", at which "politicians fraternized cheek by jowl with gangsters, openly, in the big downtown hotels ".

Synset Examples

the blatancy of his attempt to whitewash the crime was unforgivable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blatant - very obvious and offensive; flagrant
  • Antonym: subtlety - the quality of being subtle, not obvious

Etymology

From 'blatant', coined in the 17th century (possibly by Edmund Spenser, influenced by Latin 'blatire', to babble).
