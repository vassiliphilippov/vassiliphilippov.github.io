technology

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / tɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes, especially in industry; the branch of knowledge concerned with engineering or applied sciences.

Examples

Just as present technology had to await the explanations of physics, so one might expect that social invention will follow growing sociological understanding.
Others invoked technology and common sense.

Synset Examples

he had trouble deciding which branch of engineering to study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineering - the branch of science and technology concerned with the design, building, and use of engines, machines, and structures

Etymology

From Greek *tekhnē* (art, skill) + *-logia* (study of).
