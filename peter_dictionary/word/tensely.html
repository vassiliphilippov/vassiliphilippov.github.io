tensely

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛn.sli / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.sli / (BrE)
In a manner showing tension, nervousness, or anxiety.

Examples

The colonel crouched tensely on one of the folding chairs, methodically tearing at his thumbnail.
"We 're lost and burning up already ", she bit out tensely.

Synset Examples

he sat down tensely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervously - in a nervous manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm manner

Etymology

From 'tense' + '-ly', where 'tense' ultimately comes from Latin 'tensus', past participle of 'tendere' (to stretch).
