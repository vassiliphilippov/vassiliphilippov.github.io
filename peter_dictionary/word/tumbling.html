tumbling

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
The performance of acrobatic and gymnastic movements such as somersaults, flips, and rolls, often performed on the floor or mats.

Examples

It is made up of tumbling, which might be said to start with a somersault, run through such stunts as headstands, handstands, cartwheels, backbends, and culminate in nearly impossible combinations of aerial flips and twists and apparatus work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrobatics - the performance of spectacular gymnastic feats

Etymology

From 'tumble' (Middle English, from Old English 'tumbian'), meaning to roll or turn over.
