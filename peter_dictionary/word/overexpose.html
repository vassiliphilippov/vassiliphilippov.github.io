overexpose

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose film, photographic paper, or a sensor to too much light, resulting in a faded or washed-out image

Examples

Be careful here not to overexpose this subject since they are extremely bright and light-reflecting.

Synset Examples

the photographic film was overexposed and there is no image

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underexpose - to expose photographic material to too little light

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'expose'.
