reconciling

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛk.ən.saɪ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
Tending to restore friendly relations or bring into harmony.

Examples

Something indirect, mixed, reconciling, tensional might well be the stratagem, the devious technique by which a poet indulged in all kinds of talk about love and anger and even in something like "expressions "of these emotions, without aiming at their incitement or even uttering anything that essentially involves their incitement ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciliatory - intended or likely to placate or pacify
  • Antonym: alienating - causing hostility or estrangement

Etymology

From the verb 'reconcile', which is from Old French 'reconcilier', from Latin 'reconciliare' (to bring together again, make friendly).
