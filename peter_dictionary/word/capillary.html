capillary

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri / (BrE)
A thin tube with a very small internal diameter, often used in scientific experiments to hold liquids by capillary action.

Examples

Spectra were also obtained from a sample in a spherical container which was made by blowing a bubble on the end of a capillary glass tube.
The bubble was filled to the top and special precautions were taken to prevent any sample from remaining in the capillary.

Etymology

From Latin 'capillaris' (of hair), from 'capillus' (hair), referring to the hair-like thinness of the tube.
