exertion

1. NOUN

/ ɪgˈzɜrʃən / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɜːʃən / (BrE)
The use of physical or mental energy; vigorous effort or hard work.

Examples

In their first two years in high school, Jewish boys in this town make strenuous exertions to win positions on the school teams.

Synset Examples

he got an A for effort
they managed only with great exertion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - the physical or mental energy used to do something; attempt
  • Antonym: relaxation - the state of being free from tension and effort; rest

Etymology

From Latin 'exsertio' (a putting forth, exertion), from 'exserere' (to put forth).
