become

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm / (BrE)
To begin to be; to undergo a change or development that results in being or appearing as something else.

Examples

He will be succeeded by Ivan Allen Jr., who became a candidate in the Sept. 13 primary after Mayor Hartsfield announced that he would not run for reelection.
When the gubernatorial campaign starts, Caldwell is expected to become a campaign coordinator for Byrd.

They plan to become county people who know the proper way to terminate a fox's life on earth.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn - to change into a different state or condition
  • Antonym: remain - to stay in the same state without changing

Etymology

From Old English becuman, from be- (prefix) + cuman (to come).
