highlight

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪlaɪt / (BrE)
to make something stand out or attract attention by emphasizing it

Examples

In a lacy open weave shoes have a luster finish, braided collar and bow highlight on the squared throat.
Was n't this meaning of Eichmann's experience in various countries worth highlighting?

Synset Examples

The introduction highlighted the speaker's distinguished career in linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
  • Antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant

Etymology

From 'high' + 'light', originally referring to areas of brightness in painting.
