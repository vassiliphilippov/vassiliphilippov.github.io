reversibility

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌvɜrsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be reversed or returned to a previous state or condition.

Examples

An important observation of Pavlov served as a guide post to achieve such a reversibility by physiological means.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreversibility - the quality of not being reversible

Etymology

From reversible + -ity, from Latin reversus (turned back) + -ibilis (able) + -ity.
