rive

1. VERB

/ raɪv / (AmE), / raɪv / (BrE)
to tear or split apart violently; to wrench open or asunder

Examples

Let the water and the blood From Thy riven side ".

Synset Examples

The curtain ripped from top to bottom
pull the cooked chicken into strips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or divide by force; rip
  • Synonym: rend - to tear apart violently
  • Synonym: split - to break or cause to break forcibly into parts

Etymology

From Old English rīfan (to tear, split), related to Old Norse rīfa (to tear).
