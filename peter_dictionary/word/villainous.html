villainous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪlənəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪlənəs / (BrE)
Extremely wicked or characteristic of a villain.

Examples

Not a man on the brig, loyal or villainous, could be unaffected by the sight of seven men involved in the crime of mutiny.

Synset Examples

nefarious schemes
a villainous plot
a villainous band of thieves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nefarious - (of an action) wicked, criminal
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Middle English *villaineus*, from Old French *villainos* ‘of or pertaining to a servant or farmhand’.
