warm-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrm ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːm ˌʌp / (BrE)
a set of light exercises or activities done to prepare for more strenuous physical activity or performance

Examples

"This is a warm-up ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cool-down - gentle exercise or activity to help the body return to a normal state after strenuous activity

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'to warm up' (to prepare by making warm), adapted into a noun in the early 20th century.
