payable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of an amount of money) required to be paid or able to be paid, especially on a particular date or to a specified person.

Examples

Shares of capital stock at $ 15 each in the latter company were payable at the Bank of Manchester or at various other Vermont banks.
All checks and money orders should be made payable to Internal Revenue Service.

Synset Examples

a collectible bill
a note payable on demand
a check payable to John Doe

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receivable - (of money) able or to be received, often used in finance as the opposite of payable

Etymology

From pay + -able, patterned after Latin *solvendus* (to be paid).
