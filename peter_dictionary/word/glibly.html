glibly

1. ADV

/ ˈɡlɪbli / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪbli / (BrE)
In a smooth, easy, and often insincere or superficial manner; with superficial plausibility.

Examples

And when psychology explains glibly, "but the subconscious mind is able to produce it "it refers to a mental region so vaguely identified that it may embrace the entire universal mind as conceivably as part of the individual mind.
Continuing glibly in this vein, he paused to comfort her:

Synset Examples

he talked glibly

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: smoothly - in a smooth or easy manner, possibly with skill or charm
  • Antonym: sincerely - in a genuine or heartfelt manner

Etymology

From 'glib' + '-ly'. 'Glib' from Middle Low German 'glibberich' (slippery).
