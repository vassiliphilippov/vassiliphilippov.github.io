specification

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a detailed and exact statement of the requirements, dimensions, materials, or standards for something to be built or supplied, especially as a document for a contract or design

Examples

These problems frequently arise where a firm is making items for the Government not directly along the lines of its normal civilian business or where the Government specifications require operations that the firm did not understand when it undertook the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requirement - something that is necessary or demanded in a specification or contract
  • Synonym: blueprint - a detailed plan or outline, especially for construction or engineering
  • Degree Lower: outline - a general description without detailed specifics

Etymology

From Latin 'specificatio', from 'specificare' (to mention specifically).
