barony

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəni, ˈbærəni / (AmE), / ˈbɑːrəni / (BrE)
the rank or estate belonging to a baron, including the land, title, and certain privileges; a territory ruled by a baron

Examples

Here they built huts and a stockade named Fort Daer after Selkirk's barony in Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baron - a member of the lowest rank of British nobility

Etymology

From Old French baronie, from baron (baron) + -ie (suffix denoting rank or state).
