anonymous

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɑnɪməs / (AmE), / əˈnɒnɪməs / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) having no known name, identity, or source; not identified by name.

Examples

It was marked by controversy, anonymous midnight phone calls and veiled threats of violence.
During the election campaign, both candidates, Davis and Bush, reportedly received anonymous telephone calls.
Ordinary Williams said he, too, was subjected to anonymous calls soon after he scheduled the election.
The author of the anonymous notes seemed to be all-knowing.
He had received both first and second anonymous notices, and each time he had accused his neighbors of writing them.

Synset Examples

anonymous authors
anonymous donors
an anonymous gift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nameless - having no name; not identified by name
  • Antonym: identified - having the known identity; named or recognized

Etymology

From Greek anōnymos (without a name), from an- (not) + onoma (name).

2. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːnɪməs / (AmE), / əˈnɒnɪməs / (BrE)
lacking individual features or characteristics; having no distinctive or identifying qualities

Examples

Moreover the centralization of our economy during the 1920 s, the dislocations of the Depression, the common ethos of Materialism everywhere, all contributed in various ways to the face-lifting that replaced Mike Fink and the Great Gatsby with the anonymous physiognomy of the Little People.

Synset Examples

brown anonymous houses
anonymous bureaucrats in the Civil Service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featureless - lacking distinctive features or characteristics
  • Antonym: identifiable - able to be recognized or distinguished

Etymology

From Greek anonymos, from an- (without) + onoma (name).
