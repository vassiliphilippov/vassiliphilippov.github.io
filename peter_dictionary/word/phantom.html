phantom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.təm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm / (BrE)
Something that you imagine you see or feel but that does not really exist; an apparition or illusion without physical presence.

Examples

He's a phantom, an apparition.

Synset Examples

seemed to hear faint phantom bells
the amputee's illusion of a phantom limb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparition - a ghost or ghostlike image of a person
  • Synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
  • Antonym: reality - the world or the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek phantasma (apparition, phantom) via Latin and Old French.
