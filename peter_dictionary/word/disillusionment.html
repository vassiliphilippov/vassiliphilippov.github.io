disillusionment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒənmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of disappointment resulting from the realization that something is not as good or valuable as one initially believed.

Examples

In 1952, it will be remembered, the G. O. P. without positive program campaigned on the popular disillusionment with liberal leadership and won overwhelmingly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disenchantment - loss of belief in the value or magic of something

Etymology

Formed from 'disillusion' + '-ment', where 'disillusion' is from French 'désillusion' and ultimately from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'illudere' (to mock, deceive).
