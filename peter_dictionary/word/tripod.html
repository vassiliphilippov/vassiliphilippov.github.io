tripod

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈtraɪpɒd / (BrE)
A stand or support with three legs, used to hold something steady such as a camera, a survey instrument, or a weapon.

Examples

The beaming rok was carrying a thirty caliber machine gun; another man lugged the tripod and a box of ammunition.

Etymology

From Greek tripous, tripodos, from tri- (three) + pous, podos (foot).
