candy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændi / (AmE), / ˈkændi / (BrE)
A sweet food made from sugar or syrup, often mixed with fruit, nuts, or chocolate, and formed into various shapes.

Examples

Virginia treated him with attention and tried to tempt his appetite with special food: biscuits, cookies, candies - the result of devoted hours in the tiled kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confectionery - sweet foods collectively, especially candy and chocolates
  • Synonym: sweet - (British) a small piece of candy
  • Antonym: savory - not sweet in flavor; contrasting taste profile

Etymology

From Arabic *qandī* (made of sugar), via Old French *çucre candi*.
