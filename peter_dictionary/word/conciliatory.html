conciliatory

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪliəˌtɔri / (AmE), / kənˈsɪliətəri / (BrE)
intended or likely to placate or pacify; showing willingness to make concessions or reduce hostility.

Examples

He was stern and overbearing with his flock, but obsequious and conciliatory with the whites, especially the rich who partly supported the church.

Synset Examples

loneliness tore through him...whenever he thought of...even the compromising Louis du Tillet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromising - willing to make concessions or yield in conflict
  • Antonym: intransigent - unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree

Etymology

From Latin conciliāre (to bring together, unite).
