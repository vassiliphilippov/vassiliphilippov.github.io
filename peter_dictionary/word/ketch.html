ketch

1. NOUN

/ kɛtʃ / (AmE), / kɛtʃ / (BrE)
a sailing vessel with two masts, with the smaller mizzen mast located forward of the rudderpost

Examples

These were the ships of His Majesty's Navy, herding the hulks of the East Indies merchants and the yachts and ketches of the loyalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schooner - a sailing vessel with two or more masts, with the foremast shorter than the mainmast

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English cache, possibly influenced by Old French coche (boat).
