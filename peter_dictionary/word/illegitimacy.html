illegitimacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪdʒɪˈtɪməsi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪdʒɪˈtɪməsi / (BrE)
The state or condition of being born to parents who are not legally married to each other.

Examples

Research projects as soon as possible on the causes and prevention of dependency and illegitimacy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legitimacy - the status of being born to legally married parents

Etymology

From illegitimate + -acy; 'illegitimate' from Latin illegitimus (not lawful).
