champ

1. NOUN

/ tʃæmp / (AmE), / tʃamp / (BrE)
an informal way of referring to a champion, especially someone who has won first place in a competition

Examples

With Mickey Mantle and Yogi Berra both out of action due to injuries, the American League champs still mounted a 15 - hit attack against a parade of eight Cincinnati pitchers, the most ever used by one team in a series game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - a person who has won first place in a competition

Etymology

Shortened form of 'champion,' first attested in the 18th century.
