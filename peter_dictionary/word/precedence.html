precedence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdəns / (BrE)
The condition of being considered more important than something else; priority in importance, order, or rank.

Examples

He must think about which of these principles must take precedence.
Quality and combat readiness must take precedence over mere numbers.

Synset Examples

...its precedence as the world's leading manufacturer of pharmaceuticals
national independence takes priority over class struggle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - the fact or condition of being regarded or treated as more important

Etymology

From Latin praecedentia, from praecēdere (to go before).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdəns / (BrE)
The condition of being considered more important than someone or something else; the fact of coming before in time, order, or rank, especially in ceremonies or formal contexts.

Examples

And you give your loyalty to a Jew before your own blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priority - the fact or condition of being regarded or treated as more important

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *praecedentia*, from Latin *praecedens* (going before).
