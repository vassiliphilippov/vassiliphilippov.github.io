antipathy

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪpəθi / (AmE), / ænˈtɪpəθi / (BrE)
A strong and deep-seated feeling of dislike or aversion toward someone or something.

Examples

The South's antipathy to Northern civilization includes such charges as poor manners, harsh accents, lack of appreciation of the arts of living like gastronomy and the use of leisure.
In she has it in for George dominant stress will ordinarily be on in, where the notion of stored up antipathy seems to center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Synonym: enmity - the state or feeling of being actively opposed or hostile
  • Antonym: sympathy - feelings of pity and sorrow for someone's misfortune

Etymology

From Greek antipatheia (opposition of feeling), from anti- (against) + pathos (feeling).
