jolt

1. NOUN

/ dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
A sudden, forceful shock or movement that shakes or jars something.

Examples

He slapped the reins on the back of the powerful gray horse and held on as the sulky's wheels hit a pothole and came out with a jolt and went on.

Synset Examples

the door closed with a jolt
all the jars and jolts were smoothed out by the shock absorbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden, intense disturbance or impact
  • Synonym: jerk - a quick, sharp movement

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly imitative or related to dialectal English 'jowl' (to shake).

2. VERB

/ dʒoʊlt / (AmE), / dʒəʊlt / (BrE)
to move or cause someone or something to move suddenly and roughly; to shock or surprise someone into action

Examples

"Governments apparently do not feel obligated to make the people adequately aware of this danger; therefore we need guardians to demonstrate against the ghastly stupidity of nuclear weapons and jolt the people out of their complacency ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly back and forth or up and down with force
  • Synonym: jar - to shake or cause to shake harshly or unpleasantly

Etymology

Of unknown origin, possibly imitative of the sound or action.
