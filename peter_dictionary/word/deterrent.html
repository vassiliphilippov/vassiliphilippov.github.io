deterrent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɜr.ənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛr.ənt / (BrE)
A thing that discourages or is intended to discourage someone from taking an action or proceeding with something.

Examples

- The deterrent power of our Armed Forces comes from both their nuclear retaliatory capability and their capability to conduct other essential operations in any form of war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction to something or someone
  • Synonym: impediment - something that makes progress, movement, or achieving something difficult or impossible
  • Antonym: encouragement - something that gives support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From Latin deterrent-, deterrens, present participle of deterrēre (to deter).
