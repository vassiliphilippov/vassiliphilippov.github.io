arch

1. NOUN

/ ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
A curved structure, especially one spanning an opening and supporting the weight above it, often used in buildings and bridges.

Examples

At the peak of the arch, tip the head back and bend the knees in an effort to touch toes to head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - an arched structure forming a roof or ceiling

Etymology

From Latin 'arcus' (bow, arch).

2. VERB

/ ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to form or cause to form a curved or arched shape

Examples

Arch the back upwards to make a bridge.
Straighten the arms slowly, this arches the back.
These are traversed by another line of vaults, and thus rooms, arched on all four sides, are formed.

Synset Examples

her back arches
her hips curve nicely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curve - to bend or form into a curved shape

Etymology

From Old French arche, from Latin arcus (bow, arch).

3. NOUN

/ ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the curved structure of bone in the foot that supports the body and helps movement; also, any similar curved bony structure in the body supporting or enclosing organs

Examples

Her tanned foot, whose arch swept high and white, pointed artfully toward tapering toes - toes like fingers, whose tips glowed white.
