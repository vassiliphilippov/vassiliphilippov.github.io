adamantly

1. ADV

/ ˈæd.ə.mənt.li / (AmE), / ˈæd.ə.mənt.li / (BrE)
In a firm and unyielding way; refusing to change one's mind or position.

Examples

Today the Nasser and Kassem governments are adamantly hostile to the West, are dependent for their military power on Soviet equipment and personnel; in almost every particular follow the Kremlin's foreign policy line.

Synset Examples

adamantly opposed to the marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - with steadfast determination or strength
  • Antonym: flexibly - in a way that is easily changed or adapted

Etymology

From 'adamant', which comes from Latin 'adamant-', from Greek 'adamant-' (untamable, invincible).
