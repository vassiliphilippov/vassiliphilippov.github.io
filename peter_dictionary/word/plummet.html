plummet

1. VERB

/ ˈplʌmət / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪt / (BrE)
(of something) to fall or drop suddenly and steeply, especially from a great height or value.

Examples

He tramped out of the Miners Rest with his hopes plummeting, and headed doggedly for the Palace Saloon, the last place of any consequence on this side of the street.

Synset Examples

The stock market plummeted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - to move downward, typically rapidly and freely without control
  • Antonym: soar - to rise rapidly

Etymology

From Middle English 'plomet', from Old French 'plommet' (a sounding lead), diminutive of 'plom' (lead). Originally referred to dropping a lead weight, hence the figurative sense.
