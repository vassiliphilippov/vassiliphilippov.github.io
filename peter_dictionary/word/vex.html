vex

1. VERB

/ vɛks / (AmE), / vɛks / (BrE)
to make someone feel annoyed, worried, or frustrated, especially by minor issues or repeated actions

Examples

Instead - and not just to prove my objectivity - I hasten to report that it's a highly amusing film which probably does a fairly accurate job of reporting on the Easter vacation shenanigans of collegians down in Fort Lauderdale, and that it seems to come to grips quite honestly with the moral problem that most commonly vexes youngsters in this age group - that is to say, sex.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoy - to make someone a little angry or irritated
  • Synonym: irritate - to make someone feel annoyed or impatient
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone feel more relaxed and less upset

Etymology

From Old French *vexer*, from Latin *vexare* (to shake, disturb).
