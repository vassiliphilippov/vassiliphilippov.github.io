weaning

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of gradually introducing an infant or young mammal to food other than its mother's milk

Examples

A tranquilizer fed to cattle (other than lactating dairy cows) prior to their being subjected to stress conditions such as vaccinating, shipping, weaning calves, and excessive handling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wean - to accustom a young mammal to food other than its mother's milk

Etymology

From the verb 'wean,' from Old English *wenian* (to accustom), of Germanic origin.
