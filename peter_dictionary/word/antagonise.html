antagonise

1. VERB

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to act in opposition to; to counteract or work against something

Examples

The antithyroid drugs of the thiouracil type, however, are not antagonised by such means.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counteract - to act against something to reduce its force or neutralize it
  • Antonym: support - to back up, help, or act in favor of

Etymology

From Greek antagonizesthai (to struggle against), from anti- (against) + agonizesthai (to contend for a prize).
