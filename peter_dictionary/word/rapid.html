rapid

1. ADJ

/ ˈræpɪd / (AmE), / ˈrapɪd / (BrE)
Happening or done very quickly; taking place in a short period of time.

Examples

But it has been during the last two centuries, during the scientific revolution, that our independence from the physical environment has made the most rapid strides.

Synset Examples

a rapid rise through the ranks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - moving or happening quickly
  • Synonym: quick - done with speed; speedy
  • Antonym: slow - happening or done without much speed

Etymology

From Latin rapidus (seizing, swift), from rapere (to seize).
