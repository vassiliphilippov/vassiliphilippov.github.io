college

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
The collective body of faculty and students at a college.

Examples

A normal year's work in college is 30 semester hours.
The remainder of the 4 - year college requirement would be in general subjects.
College teachers in Texas are not required to have the Education courses.
During his college career, Dr. Clark was captain of his basketball team and was a football letterman.
The largest of these organizations at present denies to the full time educator any vote on the conduct and standards of the group and, indeed, refuses him even the right to attach the customary initials after his name in the college catalog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher education offering courses beyond college

Etymology

From Latin 'collegium' (society, guild, college).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
an institution of higher education where students can study for a degree, often forming part of a university

Examples

Did you go to college to get stupid?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher education and research, typically comprising multiple colleges

Etymology

From Latin 'collegium' (community, society, guild), from 'collega' (partner, colleague).
