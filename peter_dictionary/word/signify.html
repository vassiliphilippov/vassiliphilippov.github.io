signify

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡnəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to be a sign of; to mean or represent something

Examples

Charity as she knew it was complex and reciprocal, and almost every roof she saw signified charity.

Synset Examples

These words mean nothing to me!
What does his strange behavior signify?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - to have as a meaning; to signify
  • Synonym: denote - to be a sign of; to indicate
  • Synonym: imply - to suggest without being stated directly

Etymology

From Latin significare (to indicate, mean), from signum (sign) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to be a sign or indication of; to mean or represent something

Examples

Mrs. Hewlitt led the birthcontrol league, Mrs. Ryerson was arthritis, and way in the distance could be seen the slate roof of Ethel Littleton's house, a roof that signified gout.
Though they are often heard clairaudiently, as if a voice were speaking them, in other cases they are apprehended visually as symbols: a slope to signify the name "Hill ", for instance.
This rate of increase does not signify anything in itself.

Synset Examples

`maison' means `house' in French
An example sentence would show what this word means

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - to have a particular sense or significance
  • Synonym: denote - to be a sign or indication of
  • Synonym: connote - to imply in addition to the literal meaning

Etymology

From Old French signifier, from Latin significare (to indicate, mean), from signum (sign) + facere (to make).
