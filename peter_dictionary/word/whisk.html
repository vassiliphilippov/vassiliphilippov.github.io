whisk

1. VERB

/ wɪsk / (AmE), / wɪsk / (BrE)
to take or move someone or something quickly and suddenly from one place to another

Examples

They are accused of whisking secrets out of naval strongrooms over which they kept guard.

Synset Examples

The President was whisked away in his limo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with speed
  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *viska* (quick movement, brush).
