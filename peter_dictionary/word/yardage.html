yardage

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdɪdʒ / (BrE)
the total distance measured in yards, especially as scored or recorded in sports or activities such as golf or football

Examples

In their first three games, the Longhorns have had the ball 41 times and scored 16 times, or 40 per cent their total passing yardage in three games, 447 on 30 completions in 56 attempts, is only 22 yards short of their total passing yardage in 1959, when they made 469 on 37 completions in 86 tries.

Synset Examples

what is the yardage of this golf course?

Etymology

From 'yard', referring to the unit of length, plus the suffix '-age' (indicating a collection or amount).
