crazily

1. ADV

/ ˈkreɪzɪli / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a wild, uncontrolled, or insane manner.

Examples

The words ran crazily in his head: Mollie the Mutton is scratching her nose in the rain.
Lips pursed mournfully, he stared down at its crazily sagging left side.

Synset Examples

she behaved insanely
he behaves crazily when he is off his medication
the witch cackled madly
screaming dementedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanely - in an insane or very wild manner
  • Synonym: madly - in a wild or frenzied manner
  • Antonym: rationally - in a reasonable or sane manner

Etymology

Formed from 'crazy' + '-ly'. 'Crazy' from English 'craze' (to shatter), possibly of Scandinavian origin.
