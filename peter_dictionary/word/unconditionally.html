unconditionally

1. ADV

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ən.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ən.əli / (BrE)
In a way that is not limited by or subject to any conditions; without reservations or limitations.

Examples

If the demand for demythologization is unavoidable and so must be accepted by theology unconditionally, the position of the "right "is clearly untenable.

Synset Examples

he accepted the offer unconditionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditionally - in a way that depends on specific conditions

Etymology

From 'unconditional' + '-ly', with 'unconditional' deriving from 'un-' (not) + 'conditional'.
