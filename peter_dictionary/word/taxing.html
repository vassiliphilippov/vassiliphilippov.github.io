taxing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæksɪŋ / (BrE)
Demanding great effort or energy; physically or mentally exhausting.

Examples

"One of the things which makes our lives drab and empty and which leaves us, at the end of the day, fatigued and deflated spiritually is the pressure of the taxing, practical, utilitarian concern of common-sense objects.

Synset Examples

the burdensome task of preparing the income tax return
my duties weren't onerous; I only had to greet the guests
a taxing schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdensome - not easily carried; causing difficulty or stress
  • Synonym: onerous - involving a great deal of effort or difficulty
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; causing little or no trouble

Etymology

From the verb 'tax' (to make demands upon), ultimately from Latin 'taxare' (to assess, estimate).
