crutch

1. NOUN

/ krʌtʃ / (AmE), / krʌtʃ / (BrE)
A support, typically made of wood or metal, that fits under a person's armpit and extends to the ground, used to help someone walk when they have difficulty using their legs.

Examples

Muscle weakness did not improve, and the patient needed first a cane, then crutches.
At the King Arthur one guest had had his head heavily bandaged, and another had a bandaged foot and had walked with crutches.

Etymology

From Old English 'crycce', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ krʌtʃ / (AmE), / krʌtʃ / (BrE)
A thing or person that provides support or reassurance, especially when relied on excessively or as a substitute for dealing with difficulties directly.

Examples

He finally fell asleep around six in the morning with the aid of a sleeping capsule, a crutch he rarely used, and did n't awaken until early afternoon.

Synset Examples

he uses drugs as a psychological crutch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that gives help or assistance
  • Synonym: prop - something used for support

Etymology

From Old English 'crycc', related to Middle Low German 'krucke' (crutch, staff).
