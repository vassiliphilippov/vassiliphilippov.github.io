exhaustive

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɔstɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːstɪv / (BrE)
Including everything possible; very thorough and complete.

Examples

An exhaustive survey was made of the literature, and a primary reference file of approximately 600 references was catalogued.

Synset Examples

an exhaustive study
made a thorough search
thoroughgoing research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorough - done with great care so that nothing is missed

Etymology

From Latin 'exhaurire' meaning 'to draw out or drain', via the verb 'exhaust'.
