conditioning

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a person's or animal's behavior is shaped or modified in response to specific stimuli, especially as a result of repeated exposure or association in psychology.

Examples

In a conditioning experiment, he demonstrated the antagonism between feeding and pain.
It is the urge to obtain a list as free of geographical and cultural conditioning as possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habituation - decrease in response to a stimulus after repeated presentations
  • Synonym: reinforcement - the action or process of strengthening a response in conditioning

Etymology

From the verb 'condition' + -ing; 'condition' from Latin 'condicio' (agreement, situation).
