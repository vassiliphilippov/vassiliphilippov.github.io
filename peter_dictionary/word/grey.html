grey

1. ADJ

/ ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
Of a colour between black and white; having a shade that is intermediate between the extremes of black and white.

Examples

Michelangelo found the rabbi in the room of study, a gentle old man with a white beard and luminous grey eyes, robed in black gabardine with a skullcap on his head.
I tell you, those voices soared... higher and farther than anybody in a grey place dares to dream.

Synset Examples

the little grey cells
gray flannel suit
a man with greyish hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - US spelling of 'grey', the colour between black and white
  • Degree Higher: white - the colour of pure snow; the opposite end of the black-grey-white scale
  • Degree Lower: black - the darkest colour, resulting from the absence of light

Etymology

From Old English grǣg, related to Dutch grijs and German grau.
