Lucifer

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːsɪfər / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪfə / (BrE)
(in Judeo-Christian and Islamic traditions) the chief spirit of evil, adversary of God, and ruler of Hell; often identified with Satan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Satan - the chief evil spirit and enemy of God; also known as the Devil
  • Synonym: devil - the supreme spirit of evil in many religions, especially in Christianity

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucifer', meaning 'light-bringer', used as a name for the morning star and later for a fallen angel.
