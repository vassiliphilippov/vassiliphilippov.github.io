deficient

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
not having enough of a specified quality or ingredient; inadequate in amount or degree.

Examples

For one thing, although considerable numbers of men have been trained, bureaucracies are still deficient in many respects; even the famed Indian Civil Service is not fully adequate to the tremendous range of tasks it has undertaken.

Synset Examples

a deficient education
deficient in common sense
lacking in stamina
tested and found wanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficient - not enough; inadequate
  • Synonym: inadequate - not sufficient; not enough
  • Antonym: sufficient - enough; adequate

Etymology

From Latin deficient-, past participle stem of deficere 'to fail, fall short'.
