peck

1. VERB

/ pɛk / (AmE), / pɛk / (BrE)
(of a bird) to eat by picking up small amounts of food with the beak.

Examples

"The news of their experiments reach the farmers who, forgetting that birds are the most efficient natural enemies of insects and rodents, are encouraged to try to get rid of all birds that occasionally peck their grapes or their blueberries ", Buchheister told the delegates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nibble - to take small bites, especially repeatedly

Etymology

From Middle English peken, of imitative origin, referring to the quick motions of a beak.

2. VERB

/ pɛk / (AmE), / pɛk / (BrE)
to strike or pick at something lightly and repeatedly, typically with a beak or similar motion

Examples

She was a large woman with a frizzled gray poodle cut and a pencil clamped like a bit between her teeth while she hunted and pecked on an old typewriter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pick - to hit or pull at something with short, repeated movements

Etymology

From Middle English *pecken*, of imitative origin.
