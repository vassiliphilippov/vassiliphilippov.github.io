doze

1. VERB

/ doʊz / (AmE), / dəʊz / (BrE)
to sleep lightly or for a short time, especially during the day or when you are supposed to be awake

Examples

He was dozing, perhaps the only sleep he 'd gotten.
Pam realized, to her surprise, that she had been almost dozing.
A man knocked the roulette ball about idly in its track, and another dozed at one of the card tables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - a short sleep, especially during the day
  • Degree Higher: sleep - to be in a natural state of rest in which consciousness is lost
  • Synonym: slumber - sleep, especially lightly

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *dosen*, of unknown origin.
