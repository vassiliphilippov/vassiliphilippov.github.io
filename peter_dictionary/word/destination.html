destination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the place to which someone or something is going or being sent, especially the end point of a journey or route

Examples

The Gabriel pointed towards her destination and, under one gee acceleration, began to build up towards her ultimate velocity, 99.1 percent of the speed of light.
And, in the half year left before reaching their destination, the men would carry out whatever preparations were needed.

Synset Examples

a crowd assembled at the finish
he was nearly exhausted as their destination came into view

Related Terms

  • Antonym: origin - the point at which something begins or is created

Etymology

From Latin destinatio (purpose, design), from destinare (to make firm, establish).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the purpose or end goal for which something is intended or carried out

Examples

After leaving Conrad, Gilborn had no destination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - an aim or desired result
  • Synonym: objective - something that one's efforts are intended to attain
  • Synonym: aim - a purpose or intention; a desired outcome

Etymology

From Latin destinatio (the act of appointing), from destinare (to determine, appoint).
