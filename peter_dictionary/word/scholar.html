Etymology

From Old French *escoler*, from Latin *scholaris* (of a school, scholar), from *schola* (school).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɒlə / (BrE)
A person who is learning, especially a child attending school; a pupil or student.

Examples

"We have nine hundred and eleven members in our charge ", Mama announced, "and three hundred and eighty Sunday-school scholars ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person who is studying at a school or college
  • Antonym: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school

Etymology

From Latin 'scholarius' (of a school), from 'schola' (school).
