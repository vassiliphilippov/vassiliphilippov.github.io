malevolency

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛvələnsɪ / (AmE), / məˈlɛvələnsɪ / (BrE)
The quality or state of threatening evil or harm.

Examples

Looming over all, Papa-san leered down at him, threatening a hundred hidden malevolencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malevolence - the state or quality of wickedness or ill will
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin malevolentia (ill will), with suffix -cy forming nouns of quality or state.
