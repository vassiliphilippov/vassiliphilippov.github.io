fortified

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.təˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or building) strengthened with defensive works such as walls or bastions to protect against attack.

Examples

MacPherson boldly approached the fortified adobe house and demanded entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored - protected by armor or defensive covering
  • Antonym: unprotected - not defended or strengthened against attack

Etymology

From Latin 'fortificare', from 'fortis' (strong) and 'facere' (to make), via Old French and Middle English.
