jockey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
A person who rides horses in races, especially as a professional.

Examples

Toying with her field in the early stages, Garden Fresh was asked for top speed only in the stretch by Jockey Philip Grimm and won by a length and a half in 1.24 3 - 5 for the 7 furlongs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rider - a person who rides animals, particularly horses, more generally
  • Degree Lower: horseman - a skilled rider or trainer of horses; less specific to racing

Etymology

Probably from the Scottish diminutive of the given name Jack.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
to handle or maneuver someone or something skillfully, often using trickery, in order to gain advantage or achieve a goal

Examples

In the House, the Southern-Republican coalition is expected to make another major stand in opposition to the Administration's housing bill, while more jockeying is expected in an attempt to advance the aid-to-education bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuver - to move skillfully or carefully
  • Synonym: outwit - to defeat by being more clever
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage

Etymology

Originally from 'jockey' (noun, a person who rides in horse races), from a diminutive of the given name Jock (a variant of Jack), later extended metaphorically to clever maneuvering.
