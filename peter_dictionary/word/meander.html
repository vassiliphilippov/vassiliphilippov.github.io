meander

1. VERB

/ miˈændɚ / (AmE), / miˈændə / (BrE)
(of a river, road, or path) to follow a winding or indirect course; (of a person or animal) to move slowly and aimlessly in a winding or circuitous route.

Examples

He meandered down Pike Street, past the First National Bank with its green window shades.

Synset Examples

the river winds through the hills
the path meanders through the vineyards
sometimes, the gout wanders through the entire body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind - to follow a twisting or turning course
  • Synonym: wander - to move about without a fixed course, aim, or goal

Etymology

From Greek 'maiandros', the name of the winding Maeander River in Phrygia (now Turkey).
