intensely

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɛnsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsli / (BrE)
In a manner showing great strength, degree, or focus; extremely or with marked intensity.

Examples

Miss Stuart is as intensely vibrant as one could wish, almost an icy shriek threatening to explode at any moment.
In some tumor sections small spherical bodies, possibly inclusion bodies (Littau and Black, 1952) stained more intensely than the rest of cytoplasm and probably contained more antigen.
Now she was intensely alive, anguished; her son was dead.

Synset Examples

he worked intensely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strongly - in a strong or forceful manner
  • Antonym: mildly - in a mild or moderate way

Etymology

From 'intense' + '-ly', where 'intense' ultimately derives from Latin 'intensus' (stretched, strained).
