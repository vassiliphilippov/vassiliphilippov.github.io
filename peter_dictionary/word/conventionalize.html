conventionalize

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɛnʃəˌnəlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃ(ə)nəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something conform to accepted conventions or established practices

Examples

Similarly experience itself can be conventionalized so that people react to certain preconceived clues for behavior without awareness of the vitality of their experiential field.

Synset Examples

conventionalized behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normalize - to make or become normal; to bring into conformity with a norm
  • Synonym: standardize - to cause something to conform to a standard

Etymology

From 'conventional' + '-ize', ultimately from Latin 'conventio' (agreement, assembly).
