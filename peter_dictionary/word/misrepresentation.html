misrepresentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of giving a false or misleading account of the nature of something.

Examples

There is no justification for such misrepresentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

From mis- (expressing negation) + representation; patterned after represent and representation.
