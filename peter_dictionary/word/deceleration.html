deceleration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːsɛləˈreɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌdiːsɛlɪˈreɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
a reduction in the speed or rate of something; the process of slowing down.

Examples

Many years later, the photon counting apparatus in the nose of the ship would determine that the star was close enough to actuate deceleration.
Then, after slowing the vessel considerably, the drive would adjust to a one gee deceleration.

Synset Examples

the deceleration of the arms race

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceleration - increase in the speed or rate of something

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + acceleration, modeled after acceleration.
