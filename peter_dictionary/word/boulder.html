boulder

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldə / (BrE)
A large rounded mass of rock detached from its original location, often found on the ground or in a riverbed.

Examples

Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rock - solid mineral material forming part of the surface of the earth
  • Degree Lower: pebble - a small, rounded stone

Etymology

From Middle English 'bulder', possibly from Scandinavian origins (cf. Danish 'buldre', to make a loud noise).
