fortunate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.tʃə.nət / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.tʃən.ət / (BrE)
Having exceptionally good luck or being favored by circumstances; supremely favored.

Examples

They should know simple exercises that could prepare less fortunate children for the sports we will demand be taught.

Synset Examples

golden lads and girls all must / like chimney sweepers come to dust

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfortunate - not favored by luck; unlucky
  • Synonym: lucky - having good luck; fortunate

Etymology

From Latin fortunatus (prospered, lucky), from fortuna (fortune).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
Having unexpected good fortune; resulting from or characterized by luck or good chance.

Examples

Anyone fortunate enough to have one of those early phones advertised the fact along with the telephone number in the Manchester Journal.
This was fortunate, as the Vail plant burned in 1905.
He was fortunate, and proud.
There are, after all, fortunate souls who hear everything, but only know how to listen to what is good for them, and Stowey was, as things go, a fortunate man.

Synset Examples

other, less fortunate, children died
a fortunate choice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfortunate - having or marked by bad fortune; unlucky
  • Synonym: lucky - having good luck; favored by fortune

Etymology

From Latin fortunatus, from fortuna (chance, luck).
