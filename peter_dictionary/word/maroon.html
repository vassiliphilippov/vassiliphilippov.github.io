maroon

1. VERB

/ məˈrun / (AmE), / məˈruːn / (BrE)
to leave someone in a place from which they cannot escape, with little hope of being rescued

Examples

They hurled a cement barge against a freighter in Honolulu Harbor, knocked down telephone lines, marooned automobiles, flooded lawns, killed six cows.

Synset Examples

the travellers were marooned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strand - to leave someone or something stuck in a place without means of leaving

Etymology

Possibly from the Spanish 'cimarrón' (wild, untamed), originally referring to escaped slaves or animals.

2. ADJ

/ məˈrun / (AmE), / məˈruːn / (BrE)
a dark brownish-red or purplish-red color

Examples

On the third floor one of the two windows was lighted; it was framed in maroon drapes, and no faces were visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgundy - a dark red color similar to maroon
  • Degree Lower: crimson - a strong, bright, deep red color
  • Degree Lower: scarlet - a bright red color

Etymology

From French 'marron' (chestnut), referring to the color of chestnuts.
