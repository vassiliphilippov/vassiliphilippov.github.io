retardation

1. NOUN

/ ɹɪˌtɑɹˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɹɪˌtɑːˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A decrease in the speed, rate, or progress of something; the act or process of slowing down.

Examples

The Poynting-Robertson effect (Robertson, 1937; Wyatt and Whipple, 1950), which is a retardation of the orbital motion of particles by the relativistic aberration of the repulsive force of the impinging solar radiation, causes the dust to spiral into the sun in times much shorter than the age of the Earth.

Synset Examples

the deceleration of the arms race

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceleration - increase in rate or speed; opposite of retardation
  • Synonym: deceleration - the act or process of slowing down; synonym for retardation in scientific contexts

Etymology

From Latin retardatio, from retardare (to slow down).
