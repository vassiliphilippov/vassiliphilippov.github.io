lampoon

1. VERB

/ læmˈpun / (AmE), / læmˈpuːn / (BrE)
to publicly criticize or ridicule someone or something using satire

Examples

A crowd of 1400 is expected for the ceremonies, which will be followed by the show in which the writers will lampoon baseball personalities in skit, dance and song.

Synset Examples

The writer satirized the politician's proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satirize - to criticize using satire
  • Synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful way

Etymology

From French 'lampoon', from 'lampon', meaning 'let us drink!', used as a refrain in satirical drinking songs.
