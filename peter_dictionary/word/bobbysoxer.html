bobbysoxer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑbiˌsɑksər / (AmE), / ˈbɒbiˌsɒksə / (BrE)
A teenage girl in the United States during the 1940s, noted for wearing bobby socks and often associated with enthusiastic fandom of popular music stars.

Examples

The film they did after his return was an inconsequential bit of nothing titled Out of This World, a satire on the Sinatra bobby-soxer craze.

Etymology

From 'bobby sock' + '-er', referring to the style of ankle socks popular with teenage girls in the 1940s.
