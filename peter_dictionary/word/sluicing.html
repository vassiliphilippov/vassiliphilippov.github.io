sluicing

1. ADJ

/ ˈsluːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsluːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of rain, water, etc.) pouring down forcefully or in great quantities, as if from a sluice.

Examples

He was only vaguely aware of the sluicing rain.

Synset Examples

the sluicing rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pouring - (of rain or liquid) falling heavily or in large quantities

Etymology

From the verb "sluice," ultimately from Old French *escluse*, from Latin *excludere* (to shut out).
