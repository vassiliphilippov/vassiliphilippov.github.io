spectacles

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktəkəlz / (AmE), / ˈspɛktəklz / (BrE)
a pair of lenses set in a frame, worn on the nose and ears to correct or aid defective eyesight

Examples

When Papa's slender fingers removed the spectacles, there were red indentations on the bridge of the strong nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glasses - a common term for spectacles

Etymology

From Latin spectaculum (a show, spectacle), from spectare (to look at).
