skullcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈskʌlˌkæp / (BrE)
A small, rounded, brimless cap that fits tightly to the crown of the head, often worn for religious or ceremonial reasons.

Examples

Michelangelo found the rabbi in the room of study, a gentle old man with a white beard and luminous grey eyes, robed in black gabardine with a skullcap on his head.

Etymology

Formed from 'skull' + 'cap', referring to a cap that covers the skull.
