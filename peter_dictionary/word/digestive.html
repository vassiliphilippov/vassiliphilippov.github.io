digestive

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈdʒɛstɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪv / (BrE)
relating to or having the ability to promote digestion in the body

Examples

The duration of the digestive process varies, the pathologist says, but the empty stomach and the findings in the upper gastrointestinal tract indicate that Mrs. Meeker died several hours after her seven o' clock dinner.

Synset Examples

digestive juices
a digestive enzyme
digestive ferment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digestive system - the organs involved in digestion
  • Synonym: digestive enzyme - an enzyme that aids digestion
  • Antonym: indigestible - not able to be digested

Etymology

From Latin 'digestivus', from 'digestus' (to distribute, dissolve, digest).
