palpably

1. ADV

/ ˈpæl.pəb.li / (AmE), / ˈpal.pəb.li / (BrE)
In a way that is easily noticed or obvious; perceptibly or clearly.

Examples

To be passive, to be girlishly shy was palpably absurd.

Synset Examples

she was palpably nervous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood
  • Antonym: imperceptibly - in a way that is impossible or difficult to perceive

Etymology

From palpable + -ly; ultimately from Latin 'palpabilis', from 'palpare' (to touch, feel).
