parisology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈsɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpærɪˈsɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the use of ambiguous or equivocal words, especially in speech or writing

Examples

There are many types of ambiguity and many of them have been described by rhetoricians under such names as amphibology, parisology, and other ologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphibology - ambiguous grammatical construction; double meaning

Etymology

From Greek 'parisologia', from 'para' (beside) + 'logos' (word, speech).
