constriction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstrɪkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪkʃən / (BrE)
a narrowing of a passage or channel that reduces or restricts the flow through it

Examples

Sarah swallowed past another kind of constriction in her throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrowing - the process or result of becoming narrower

Etymology

From Latin 'constrictionem', meaning a contraction or binding together.
