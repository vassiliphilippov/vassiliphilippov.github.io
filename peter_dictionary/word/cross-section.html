cross-section

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑs ˌsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs ˌsɛkʃən / (BrE)
a representation or view showing what is revealed by a plane cut at right angles to the length of something, especially in diagrams or drawings

Examples

By submerging the patient in a tub and rotating the transducer while the scanning goes on, they have been able to get cross-section views of the neck, as shown in Fig. 7, as well as many other hitherto impossible insights.

Synset Examples

cross-section views of the neck

Etymology

From 'cross', meaning across, and 'section', from Latin 'sectio' (a cutting).
