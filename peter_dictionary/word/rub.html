rub

1. VERB

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
to move something over a surface with pressure, especially using repeated or circular motions

Examples

Adam watched his own hands make the caressing, anxious movement that, when rain falls and nobody comes, and ruin draws close like a cat rubbing against the ankles, has been the ritual of stall vendors, forever.
The wraith-like events of the last few days flooded David's mind and he rubbed his unshaved chin and felt again the ache in his kidneys caused by his saddle odyssey from Boston.
Hans is rubbing him with snow.
He was rubbing snow on the kid's legs and feet.
Hans rubbed the kid with more snow & & & rubbed & & & rubbed.

Synset Examples

rub my hands
rub oil into her skin

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stroke - to move one's hand gently over a surface
  • Degree Higher: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *rubben*, origin uncertain, perhaps related to Old French *rober* (to scrape, rob).

2. VERB

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
to move something over a surface with pressure and repeated motion, causing friction

Examples

For almost 3000 years Europe and Asia have rubbed shoulders in its streets.

Synset Examples

my sweater scratches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - to drag or pull a hard or sharp object across a surface
  • Antonym: stroke - to move one’s hand gently over a surface

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *rubben*, of uncertain origin.
