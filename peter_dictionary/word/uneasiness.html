uneasiness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˈiːzinəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈiːzɪnɪs / (BrE)
A feeling of anxiety, worry, or discomfort; a state of being tense or unsettled.

Examples

I am usually filled with an uneasiness that through some unwitting slip all hell may break loose.
Much of the former extreme uneasiness associated with visions and hallucinations and with death has disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calmness - state of being free from anxiety or agitation

Etymology

Formed from "uneasy" (not comfortable, troubled) + noun suffix "-ness".
