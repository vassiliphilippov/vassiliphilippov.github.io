unexplained

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪkˈspleɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈspleɪnd / (BrE)
(of an event or situation) not accounted for or clarified; for which no explanation has been given or found.

Examples

The opening paragraph of the chapter titled The Theory Of Representative Perception, in the book Philosophies Of Science by Albert G. Ramsperger says, "passed on to the brain, and there, by some unexplained process, it causes the mind to have a perception ".
In 1953 the patient developed an unexplained anemia for which 15 blood transfusions were given over a period of 4 years.

Synset Examples

accomplished by some unexplained process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexplicable - impossible to explain or account for

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) + explained (from explain, from Latin *explanare*, meaning 'make plain').

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪkˈspleɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈspleɪnd / (BrE)
not having the reason or cause made clear or known; without an understandable cause

Examples

Absent-minded, subject to unexplained tears.

Synset Examples

an unexplained error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexplicable - impossible to explain or understand

Etymology

From un- (not) + explained, past participle of explain.
