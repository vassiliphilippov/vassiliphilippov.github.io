Caesar

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːzər / (AmE), / ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
Julius Caesar (100–44 BC), Roman general, statesman, and dictator, conqueror of Gaul and master of Italy.

Examples

But, like Caesar, he has only one joke, so far as I can find out.

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesar', a family name of the Julian gens.
