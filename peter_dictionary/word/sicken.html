sicken

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪkən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkən / (BrE)
to become ill or affected by sickness; to fall sick

Examples

Watson turned away, sickened for the first time in many months.

Synset Examples

She fell sick last Friday, and now she is in the hospital

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recover - to return to health after being sick

Etymology

From Old English 'siccan', from 'sice' (sickness), related to 'sick'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪkən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkən / (BrE)
to make someone feel great disgust or strong moral aversion

Examples

He was angry, sickened.
In the brief moment I had to talk to them before I took my post on the ring of defenses, I indicated I was sickened by the methods men employed to live and trade on the river.

Synset Examples

The pornographic pictures sickened us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - to cause a strong feeling of dislike or repulsion
  • Synonym: revolt - to make someone feel strong disgust or repugnance
  • Antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or satisfaction (opposite of sicken)

Etymology

From Middle English siken, sicknen, related to 'sick' + '-en' (verbal suffix).
