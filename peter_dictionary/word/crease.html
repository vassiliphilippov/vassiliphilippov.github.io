crease

1. VERB

/ kris / (AmE), / kriÀês / (BrE)
to make a line or fold in the surface of something, especially so that it is no longer smooth

Examples

He had a round, frank Irish face, creased in a jovial grin that stayed bleakly in place even when he was pumping bullets into someone's body.

Synset Examples

The dress got wrinkled
crease the paper like this to make a crane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - to make small lines or folds in a surface, especially the skin

Etymology

Probably from Old French 'crece' (a crease, fold).
