accustom

1. VERB

/ əˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / əˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
to make someone or oneself familiar with or used to something; to habituate or adapt to new conditions or practices

Examples

The events of the last quarter of an hour, mysterious to any bird accustomed only to the predictable life of coop and barnyard, had overcome the doctor's hen and she gave out a series of cackly wails, perhaps mourning her nest, but briefly enjoyed.
Hague had never accustomed himself to Kodyke.
First you hate 'em... ..then you get used to 'em.

Synset Examples

She became habituated to the background music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habituate - to make or become used to something through frequent exposure
  • Synonym: adapt - to adjust to new conditions
  • Antonym: unaccustom - to make no longer used to something

Etymology

From Old French *acostumer*, from Latin *accostumare* (to accustom), from *ad-* (to) + *consuetudo* (custom).
