gather

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to come together or bring together in one place; to collect or assemble

Examples

The Orioles once again performed at the plate in powderpuff fashion, gathering only seven blows off the offerings of three Kansas City pitchers.

Much information has been gathered relative to quantitative sampling and assessment techniques.

Synset Examples

gather some stones
pull your thoughts together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to bring or come together into one place or group
  • Antonym: disperse - to go or cause to go in different directions or to spread widely

Etymology

Old English 'gadrian', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'gaderen', German 'gattern'.

Synset Examples
