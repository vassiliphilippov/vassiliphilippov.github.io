peacock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈpiːkɒk / (BrE)
the male peafowl, known for its crested head and large fan-shaped tail decorated with iridescent eye-like spots

Examples

My host went over and stared out the window at his peacocks; then he turned to me.
Moreland went over to stare at his peacocks again, and then came back and sat down, restively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peahen - female peafowl

Etymology

From Middle English peacock, from Old English pāwa (peafowl) + coc (cock, male bird).
