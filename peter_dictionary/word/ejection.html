ejection

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of forcing something out or expelling it from a place, especially suddenly or with force

Examples

However, one must expect that vaporization and ejection of material by hypervelocity impacts would cause a deviation from a linear relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the action of forcing someone or something out
  • Antonym: injection - the act of inserting forcefully into something

Etymology

From Latin ejectio, from ejicere (to throw out).
