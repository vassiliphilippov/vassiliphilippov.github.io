brig

1. NOUN

/ brɪɡ / (AmE), / brɪɡ / (BrE)
A two-masted sailing vessel with square rigs on both masts.

Examples

Not a man on the brig, loyal or villainous, could be unaffected by the sight of seven men involved in the crime of mutiny.

Etymology

Shortened from 'brigantine', from Italian 'brigantino', meaning a small ship.
