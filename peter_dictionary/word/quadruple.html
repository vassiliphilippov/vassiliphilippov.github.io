quadruple

1. VERB

/ kwɑˈdruːpəl / (AmE), / kwɒˈdruːpəl / (BrE)
To increase or make something become four times as great in number, amount, or size.

Examples

Our respective families looked as if they had quadrupled.

Synset Examples

His stock earning quadrupled

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - to make or become twice as much
  • Degree Lower: triple - to make or become three times as much

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplus' (fourfold).
