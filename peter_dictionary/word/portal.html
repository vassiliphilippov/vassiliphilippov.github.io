portal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl / (BrE)
a large and impressive entrance to a building, or a doorway or gate, especially one that is grand or imposing; also used metaphorically to mean any point of entry

Examples

A number of unique medical problems might be created when man is exposed to an infectious agent through the respiratory route rather than by the natural portal of entry.
Some agents have been shown to be much more toxic or infectious to experimental animals when exposed to aerosols of optimum particle size than by the natural portal.

Synset Examples

the portals of the cathedral
the portals of heaven
the portals of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gate - a movable barrier at an opening for entry or exit
  • Synonym: doorway - an opening into a room or building through which one can enter or leave

Etymology

From Old French *portal* (gateway, entrance), from Latin *portale* (city gate), from *porta* (gate).
