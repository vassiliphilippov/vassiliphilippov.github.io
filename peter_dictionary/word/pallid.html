pallid

1. ADJ

/ ˈpælɪd / (AmE), / ˈpælɪd / (BrE)
(of light or color) lacking in intensity or brightness; pale, dim, or feeble.

Examples

Above, the glowing ivory baton of their searchlight pointed at the clouds, diluting the valley's dark to a pallid light.

Synset Examples

the pale light of a half moon
a pale sun
the late afternoon light coming through the el tracks fell in pale oblongs on the street
a pallid sky
the pale (or wan) stars
the wan light of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wan - (of light) pale and weak
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; shining

Etymology

From Latin pallidus (pale).
