pastime

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstaɪm / (BrE)
An activity that someone does regularly for enjoyment rather than work; a hobby or leisure pursuit.

Examples

Both, of course, were remarkable feats and further embossed the fact that baseball rightfully is the national pastime.
[TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] PRESIDENT: Mr. Michael Corleone of Nevada, [TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] representing our associates in tourism and leisure activities.

Synset Examples

sailing is her favorite pastime
his main pastime is gambling
he counts reading among his interests
they criticized the boy for his limited pursuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobby - an activity done regularly for pleasure in one's leisure time
  • Synonym: occupation - activity in which a person is engaged, especially casually
  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort done in order to achieve a result

Etymology

From Middle English 'pastime', from pass + time, originally meaning to pass the time.
