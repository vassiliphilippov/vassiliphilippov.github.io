bloc

1. NOUN

/ blɑk / (AmE), / blɒk / (BrE)
A group of countries, political parties, or organizations that form an alliance to pursue shared interests or goals.

Examples

At times, three ships a day from the Soviet bloc are unloading in Cuban ports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations

Etymology

From French 'bloc', meaning 'block' or 'group'.
