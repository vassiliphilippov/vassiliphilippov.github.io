fullness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊlnəs / (BrE)
a quality of being rich, deep, and pleasing, especially as a property of sound, music, or other sensations

Examples

In the fullness of her vocal splendor, however, she could sing the famous scene magnificently.

Synset Examples

the music had a fullness that echoed through the hall
the cheap wine had no body, no mellowness
he was well aware of the richness of his own appearance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: richness - the quality of being deep, strong, or pleasing (as in a sound or color)
  • Antonym: thinness - lacking strength or richness (esp. in sound or appearance)

Etymology

From Middle English *fulnesse*, from Old English *fullnes*, from *full* + *-ness*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being complete and comprehensive in scope or detail.

Examples

They were poems in a strange language, of which he could barely touch a meaning - enough to make his being ache with the desire for the fullness he sensed there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completeness - the state of being complete or entire
  • Antonym: emptiness - the state of being empty or lacking content

Etymology

From Old English 'fullnes' (state of being full), from 'full' + '-ness'.
