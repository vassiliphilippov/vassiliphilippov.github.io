exude

1. VERB

/ ɪgˈzud / (AmE), / ɪgˈzjuːd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or other substance) to discharge or release slowly and steadily, often in drops or small quantities.

Examples

As he chewed his gum and exuded wheezing breath, Marty smelt the reek of bad whiskey.

Synset Examples

exude sweat through the pores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ooze - to pass or flow slowly as if through small openings

Etymology

From Latin 'exudare', from 'ex-' (out) + 'sudare' (to sweat).
