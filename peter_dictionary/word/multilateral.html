multilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.tɪˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
Involving or having many sides, parts, or participants, especially in reference to agreements or discussions between multiple countries or parties.

Examples

Should the action be unilateral or multilateral?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unilateral - relating to, or involving only one side or party

Etymology

From Latin 'multilateralis', from 'multi-' (many) + 'latus' (side).
