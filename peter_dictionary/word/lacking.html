lacking

1. ADJ

/ ˈlækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlakɪŋ / (BrE)
not having enough of a particular quality or thing; insufficient or deficient in amount or degree.

Examples

The City Purchasing Department, the jury said, "is lacking in experienced clerical personnel as a result of city personnel policies ".
Benington recalled that he once told Hartweger that he doubted Gordon would ever play much for him because he seemed to be lacking in all of the accepted basketball skills.
Certainly in the matter of principals there is nothing lacking.
The assisting musicians from the Vienna Octet are somewhat lacking in expertise, but their contribution is rustic and appealing.
I'’m short of money right now.

Synset Examples

a deficient education
deficient in common sense
lacking in stamina
tested and found wanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deficient - not having enough of a specified quality or ingredient
  • Synonym: wanting - lacking in a required or necessary quality
  • Antonym: sufficient - having enough of something; adequate
  • Antonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities

Etymology

Present participle of 'lack', from Middle English 'lacken'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlækɪŋ / (BrE)
Not present or absent; not available or existing.

Examples

"Don't leave home without it."

Synset Examples

the thumb is absent
her appetite was lacking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent - not present in a place, at an occasion, or as part of something
  • Synonym: missing - not able to be found or not present where it should be

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'lack', from Middle English 'lakken'.
