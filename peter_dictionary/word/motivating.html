motivating

1. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.tɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone to feel a reason or incentive to act or take action; encouraging or stimulating motivation.

Examples

The insuperable reward systems that most religions embody have great motivating effects.
Fear of the competition - always a great motivating force in the American economy - makes retailers who do not have suburban operations exaggerate both the volume and the profitability of their rival's shiny new branches.

Synset Examples

it may well be that ethical language has primarily a motivative function
motive pleas
motivating arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiring - giving someone enthusiasm or motivation to do something

Etymology

From 'motivate' + '-ing', with 'motivate' from Latin 'motivus' (moving).
