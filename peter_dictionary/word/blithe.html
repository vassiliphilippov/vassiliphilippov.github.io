blithe

1. ADJ

/ blaɪð / (AmE), / blaɪð / (BrE)
Showing a casual and cheerful indifference considered to be improper or lacking due concern.

Examples

The cyclist, by contrast, blond and blue-eyed, is simply unreflective, unproblematic Life, "blithe and carefree ".
Southern Liberals (there are a good many) - especially if they 're rich - often exhibit blithe insouciance.

Synset Examples

spoke with blithe ignorance of the true situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from worries or responsibilities
  • Synonym: heedless - showing a reckless lack of care or attention

Etymology

From Old English 'blīthe' (cheerful, kind), related to Old Norse 'blīthr', Old Saxon 'blīdi'.
