parenthesis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛnθəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrɛnθəsɪs / (BrE)
either of a pair of curved marks ( ) used in writing to enclose extra information, an explanation, or words that are not part of the main statement

Examples

In some instances, the trade name is shown in parentheses following the chemical name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracket - rectangular punctuation marks [ ] used to enclose words
  • Synonym: parentheses - the plural form, referring to both punctuation marks together

Etymology

From Greek *parenthesis* (a putting in beside), from *para-* (beside) + *en* (in) + *thesis* (placing).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛnθəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrɛnθəsɪs / (BrE)
An interruption or remark that digresses from the main subject in speech or writing; a digression.

Examples

I mean, for real innocent?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • Synonym: aside - a remark not intended to be heard by everyone or a digressive comment
  • Antonym: main subject - the primary topic under discussion or consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'parenthesis', from Greek 'parenthesis' (insertion).
