perusal

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːzəl / (AmE), / pəˈruːzəl / (BrE)
the act of reading or examining something carefully and thoroughly, especially with intent to remember it

Examples

Similarly, in presenting still photographs of early jazz groups, the program allowed no time for a close perusal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reading - the act of interpreting written or printed matter

Etymology

From peruse + -al, with peruse from Middle English perusen (to examine carefully) likely from Latin per- (thoroughly) + use (to use).
