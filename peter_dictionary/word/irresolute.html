irresolute

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛzəˌlut / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛzəluːt / (BrE)
Unable to decide or act firmly; lacking resolution or determination.

Examples

The effect was as depressing as a gravestone, the applause irresolute and short-lived.

Synset Examples

the committee was timid and mediocre and irresolute

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resolute - determined; firm in purpose or belief
  • Synonym: indecisive - not able to make decisions quickly or effectively

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + resolutus (determined, resolved).
