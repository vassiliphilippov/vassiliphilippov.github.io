table

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈteɪbəl / (BrE)
An arrangement of data or information set out in rows and columns for easy reference or comparison.

Examples

Radio observations of the moon have been made over the range of wave lengths from 4.3 mm to 75 cm, and the results are summarized in Table 1.
The f values are listed in Table** 1 together with the measured surface temperatures and arc voltages.

Data on such comparisons together with data on the effect of light intensity are given in Table /1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - A visual display of information, often using symbols or graphics
  • Synonym: grid - A network of intersecting lines forming squares; used to organize data

Etymology

From Old French 'table', from Latin 'tabula' (a board, plank, writing tablet).
