receipts

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːts / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːts / (BrE)
the total amount of money received, especially before any deductions (such as expenses or taxes) are made; gross income

Examples

All moneys received from dispositions under this section shall be paid into the Treasury as miscellaneous receipts)
All amounts so deducted shall be covered into the Treasury to the credit of miscellaneous receipts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenue - income, especially that of a company or government
  • Antonym: expenditure - the action of spending funds; outflow opposed to income

Etymology

From Old French 'recete', from Latin 'recepta', past participle of 'recipere' (to receive).
