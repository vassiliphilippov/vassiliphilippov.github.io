physiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfɪziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of biology that deals with the normal functions of living organisms and their parts.

Examples

In conclusion, these findings suggest the need for a comparative physiology, pathology, and histology of mammalian lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the branch of science concerned with bodily structure
  • Antonym: pathology - study of causes and effects of diseases

Etymology

From Latin 'physiologia', from Ancient Greek 'phusiologia' meaning 'natural science, study of nature'.
