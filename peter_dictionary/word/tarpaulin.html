tarpaulin

1. NOUN

/ tɑrˈpɔlɪn / (AmE), / tɑːˈpɔːlɪn / (BrE)
a heavy, waterproofed cloth, typically made of canvas or plastic, used to cover and protect things from rain or moisture

Examples

Here and there on work table or pedestal stood a shape with a sheet or a tarpaulin draped over it.
He furled the slashed sides of the canvas tarpaulins, leaving the ribs and wagon open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarp - informal abbreviation for tarpaulin

Etymology

Probably from 'tar' + 'palling' (covering), originally referring to tarred canvas used as a waterproof covering.
