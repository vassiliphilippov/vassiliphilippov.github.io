wharf

1. NOUN

/ wɔrf / (AmE), / wɑːf / (BrE)
A flat structure built out from the shore and supported by piles, used for loading and unloading ships and boats.

Examples

The portwatchers were retired longshoremen and small time seafarers off towboats and barges who acted as watchmen on the wharves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pier - a platform extending from the shore into the water, used as a landing place for ships or boats
  • Synonym: dock - area of water for ships to load, unload, or be repaired

Etymology

From Old English 'hwearf' (bank, shore, wharf), of Germanic origin.
