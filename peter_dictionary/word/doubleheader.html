doubleheader

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlˈhɛdər / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈhɛdə / (BrE)
A sporting event in which two games are played by the same teams on the same day, especially in baseball.

Examples

The Bears are set to play at Harris Teachers College at 3:30 tomorrow and have a doubleheader at Quincy, Ill., Saturday.

Etymology

From 'double' + 'header', originally used in railroading for a train pulled by two engines, later applied to sports.
