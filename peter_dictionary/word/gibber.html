gibber

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪbər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪbə / (BrE)
to speak rapidly and unintelligibly, often in a way that is difficult to understand due to excitement, fear, or confusion

Examples

[GIBBERING NERVOUSLY IN ITALIAN] I wish I could stay longer!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to speak quickly and incoherently, making little sense
  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly about trivial matters

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, resembling the sound of rapid, unintelligible speech.
