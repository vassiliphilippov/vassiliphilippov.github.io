favored

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərd / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvəd / (BrE)
Regarded with special preference or treated with partiality; preferred above others.

Examples

The name presumably derives from the French royal house which never learned and never forgot; since Bourbon whiskey, though of Kentucky origin, is at least as much favored by liberals in the North as by conservatives in the South.
But apart from racial problems, the old unreconstructed South - to use the moderate words favored by Mr. Thomas Griffith - finds itself unsympathetic to most of what is different about the civilization of the North.

Synset Examples

the favored child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preferred - regarded with greater liking or priority
  • Antonym: disfavored - regarded with less preference or support

Etymology

From the verb 'favor' + -ed, meaning 'having favor'.
