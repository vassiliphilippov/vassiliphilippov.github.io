acquaintance

1. NOUN

/ əˈkweɪntəns / (AmE), / əˈkweɪntəns / (BrE)
Knowledge or familiarity with someone or something gained by experience or contact.

Examples

Ordinary politeness may have militated against this opinion being stated so badly but anyone with a wide acquaintance in both groups and who has sat through the many round tables, workshops or panel discussions - whatever they are called - on this subject will recognize that the final, boiled down crux of the matter is education.
His denials of extensive reading notwithstanding, it is no doubt safe to assume that he has spent time schooling himself in Southern history and that he has gained some acquaintance with the chief literary authors who have lived in the South or have written about the South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiarity - close knowledge of something or someone, often through repeated contact

Etymology

From Old French *acointance* (friendship, acquaintance), from *acointier* (to make known), from Late Latin *accognitare*, from Latin *cognoscere* (to know).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkweɪntəns / (AmE), / əˈkweɪntəns / (BrE)
A relationship with someone you know slightly, but who is not a close friend.

Examples

Presumably a cocktail party is expected to fulfill the host's desire to get together a number of people who are inadequately acquainted and thereby arrange for bringing the level of acquaintance up to adequacy for future cooperative endeavors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: friendship - a close and trusting relationship between friends
  • Degree Lower: stranger - a person with whom one has no relationship or knowledge

Etymology

From Old French *acointance* (acquaintance, familiarity), from *acointier* (to make known).

3. NOUN

/ əˈkweɪn.təns / (AmE), / əˈkweɪn.təns / (BrE)
A person whom one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend.

Examples

He was also, if Pam North was right, a closer acquaintance of Lauren Payne's than she, now, was inclined to admit.
I know a couple of big old bull queers that would just love to make your acquaintance.

Synset Examples

I have trouble remembering the names of all my acquaintances
we are friends of the family

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection
  • Degree Lower: stranger - a person whom one does not know at all

Etymology

From Old French 'acointer', from Latin 'accognitare' (to make known), related to 'cognoscere' (to know).
