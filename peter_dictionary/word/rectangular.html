rectangular

1. ADJ

/ rɛkˈtæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / rɛkˈtæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
Having the shape of a rectangle; having four right angles.

Examples

As he applied the applicator extending from the machine - which consisted of seven differently colored neon tubes superimposed on a rectangular base - to the supposedly diseased portions of Mrs. Shaefer's body, Lee kept up a steady stream of pseudo scientific mumbo-jumbo.

Synset Examples

a rectangular figure twice as long as it is wide

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: square - a shape with four equal sides and four right angles
  • Degree Higher: oblong - having an elongated rectangular or oval shape

Etymology

From Latin 'rectangulus', from 'rectus' (right) + 'angulus' (angle).

2. ADJ

/ rɛkˈtæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / rɛkˈtæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a system, especially coordinate axes or a grid) having axes or lines that are mutually perpendicular, meeting at right angles.

Examples

In the C-plane we construct a set of rectangular Cartesian coordinates u, v with the origin at Q and such that both C and **f have finite slope at Q.

Synset Examples

wind and sea may displace the ship's center of gravity along three orthogonal axes
a rectangular Cartesian coordinate system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthogonal - (of lines, axes, or vectors) at right angles; perpendicular

Etymology

From Latin 'rectangulus' (having right angles), from 'rectus' (right) + 'angulus' (angle).
