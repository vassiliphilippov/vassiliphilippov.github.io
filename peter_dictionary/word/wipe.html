wipe

1. VERB

/ waɪp / (AmE), / waɪp / (BrE)
to clean or dry a surface by rubbing, typically with a cloth or hand, in a circular or back-and-forth motion

Examples

In the kitchen Mama was wiping the cupboards.
I wanted to wipe my flint, but I did n't dare to, the state my hands were in, just as I did n't dare to do anything about the priming.
Isaac Pitt, one of the men from Lincoln, had taken a musket ball in his belly; and though he had found the strength to run with us, now he collapsed and lay on the ground, dying, the Reverend holding his head and wiping his hot brow.
Off and on, all day, someone would be wiping at the powdery gray dust that settled over everything.
He wiped the blood from his cheek.

Synset Examples

wipe the blackboard
He passed his hands over the soft cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard, usually with a brush
  • Synonym: polish - to make something smooth and shiny by rubbing

Etymology

From Old English wīpian (to wipe, cleanse, remove).

2. NOUN

/ waɪp / (AmE), / waɪp / (BrE)
an act of cleaning or drying by rubbing with a cloth, hand, etc.

Examples

I don'’t feel I have to wipe everybody out, Tom.

Synset Examples

he gave the hood a quick rub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rub - an act of rubbing something (especially cleaning or smoothing)

Etymology

From Old English *wīpian* (to wipe).
