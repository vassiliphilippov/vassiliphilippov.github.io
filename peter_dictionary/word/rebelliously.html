rebelliously

1. ADV

/ rɪˈbɛliəsli / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛliəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows resistance to authority or control; in a rebellious manner.

Examples

When he told her God had called the child to Him, she rejected his words rebelliously.

Synset Examples

he rejected her words rebelliously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obediently - in a manner showing willingness to comply

Etymology

From 'rebellious' + suffix '-ly'. 'Rebellious' from Latin 'rebellis', from 're-' (again) + 'bellum' (war).
