gage

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring and indicating a specific quantity, such as thickness, diameter, or other physical property

Examples

The direct evidence on the micrometeorite environment near the Earth is obtained from piezoelectric sensors (essentially microphones) and from wire gages; these instruments are installed on rockets, satellites, and space probes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - measuring instrument or standard (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Old French 'jauge' (a measuring rod, standard), later influenced by Middle English spelling variants.

2. NOUN

/ ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
(slang) Marijuana; cannabis, especially as used recreationally.

Examples

Now, you no doubt regard the preceding as pap; you 're tooling around full of gage in your hot rods, gorging yourselves on pizza and playing pinball in the taverns and generally behaving like Ubermenschen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - cannabis, especially when smoked as a drug

Etymology

Origin of this slang use is uncertain, but recorded in American English from early 20th century.
