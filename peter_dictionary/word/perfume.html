perfume

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfjuːm / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fjuːm / (BrE)
A fragrant liquid or substance, typically made from essential oils, used to give a pleasant smell to the body or to objects.

Examples

Exclusive of esthetic values, such as high - or low foam level, perfume content, etc., the requirements for the organic active used in washing textiles are high.
Three of these only were protected from us by stern commandment: the roses, whose petals might not be collected until they had fallen, to be made into perfume or rose tea to drink; the peonies, whose tight sticky buds would be blighted by the laying on of a finger, although they were not apparently harmed by the ants that crawled over them; and the poppies.
But hairspray mixed with lipstick and perfume will be toxic, and traceable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell; a substance emitting such a smell
  • Synonym: cologne - a lightly scented toiletry or perfume, especially for men
  • Antonym: stink - a strong, unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'perfumare' (to smoke through), from 'per-' (through) + 'fumus' (smoke).

2. NOUN

/ pɚˈfjum / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːm / (BrE)
a pleasant and distinctive smell, especially a sweet or fragrant scent

Examples

Halfway across the house, he could have smelled her morning perfume.
Cool air moving slowly through the open or smashed out side windows hinted of blooming roadside vegetation, and occasionally a faint fragrance of perfume swirled from the back seat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Synonym: aroma - a distinctive, typically pleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'per fumum' (through smoke), referring to aromatic substances burned for their fragrance.
