high-spirited

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪˈspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪˈspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
Full of energy and enthusiasm; lively and exuberant.

Examples

One thing Papa had not taught Henrietta was how to handle a young man as high-spirited and opinionated as herself.
But both were high-spirited and vivacious, both had tempers to control, both loved languages, especially English and German, both were good teachers and wrote for publication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebullient - cheerful and full of energy
  • Synonym: vivacious - attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful

Etymology

Formed from 'high' + 'spirited', where 'spirit' refers to liveliness or energy of character.
