underpin

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈpɪn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpɪn / (BrE)
to support, strengthen, or form the basis of something from beneath; to provide a foundation or backing for something, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

Depicted, Cubist flatness is now almost completely assimilated to the literal, undepicted kind, but at the same time it reacts upon and largely transforms the undepicted kind - and it does so, moreover, without depriving the latter of its literalness; rather, it underpins and reinforces that literalness, re-creates it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to bear or hold up from below; keep from falling, as a synonym for giving backing
  • Synonym: reinforce - to strengthen or support physically or mentally

Etymology

From under- (beneath) + pin (to fasten or secure); originally in engineering, meaning to support a structure from below.
