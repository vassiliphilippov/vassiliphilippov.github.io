assured

1. ADJ

/ əˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / əˈʃʊəd / (BrE)
Confidently guaranteed or protected against risk or uncertainty; having certainty or security.

Examples

He could no longer build anything, whether a private residence in his Pennsylvania county or a church in Brazil, without it being obvious that he had done it, and while here and there he was taken to task for again developing the same airy technique, they were such fanciful and sometimes even playful buildings that the public felt assured by its sense of recognition after a time, a quality of authentic uniqueness about them, which, once established by an artist as his private vision, is no longer disputable as to its other values.

Synset Examples

a tiny but assured income
we can never have completely assured lives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - protected against danger or risk; safe
  • Antonym: uncertain - not known or definite; lacking assurance or security

Etymology

Past participle of 'assure', from Old French 'asseurer', from Latin 'assecurare' (make sure).

2. ADJ

/ əˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / əˈʃɔːd / (BrE)
Confident and certain in manner or action.

Examples

But Blanche had been able to maintain a serene and assured composure in the face of her widowed mother's continued carping, had been able to resist her urgings to date anyone who offered the slightest possibility of matrimony.
Less assured than the tall, wide shouldered man in the lead, Cobb followed alertly, a hand on his gun butt.

Synset Examples

she paints with an assured hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confident - feeling or showing assurance or certainty
  • Antonym: hesitant - uncertain or lacking confidence

Etymology

From assure + -ed; assure from Old French assurer, from Latin ad- + securus (secure).
