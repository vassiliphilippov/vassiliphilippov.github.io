physicalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəlnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being physical or having material substance

Examples

It bulks under a veil of thin, new grass, like some embarrassing fact of physicalness, and I think Mrs. Pastern set out the statuary to soften its meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: materiality - the quality of being made of matter; physical existence
  • Antonym: immateriality - the quality of not being physical; lacking material substance

Etymology

From 'physical' + '-ness', following the standard pattern for forming nouns of quality.
