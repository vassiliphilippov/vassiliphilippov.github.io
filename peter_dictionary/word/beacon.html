beacon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːkən / (AmE), / ˈbiːkən / (BrE)
a fire or light set up in a high or prominent position as a signal, warning, or guide, especially on a hill or tower

Examples

He says: "Beside the Protestant philosophy of Progress, as expressed in radical or conservative millenarianism, should be placed the doctrine of the democratic faith which affirmed it to be the duty of the destiny of the United States to assist in the creation of a better world by keeping lighted the beacon of democracy ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an event or object that serves to convey information or instructions

Etymology

Old English *béacn* (signal, sign), related to Dutch *baken*, German *Baken*.
