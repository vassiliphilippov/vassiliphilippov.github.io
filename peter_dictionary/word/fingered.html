fingered

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚd / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəd / (BrE)
(of an object or part) having or resembling fingers; often used in combination to describe appearance, form, or possession of finger-like projections.

Examples

The landscape of Greek history broadens widely, and rather abruptly, in the eighth century B.C., the age of Homer's "rosy fingered Dawn ".

Synset Examples

the fingered roots of giant trees
rosy-fingered
three-fingered cartoon characters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fingerless - lacking fingers or finger-like projections
