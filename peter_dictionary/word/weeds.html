weeds

1. NOUN

/ widz / (AmE), / wiːdz / (BrE)
(archaic) Black clothing, especially a dress, worn by a widow as a sign of mourning.

Examples

She usually wore weeds, and a stranger watching her board a train might have guessed that Mr. Pastern was dead, but Mr. Pastern was far from dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourning - the expression of sorrow for someone's death, often shown by wearing black

Etymology

From Old English 'wǣd' (garment, clothing).
