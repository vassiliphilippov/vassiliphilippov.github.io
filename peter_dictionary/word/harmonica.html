harmonica

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈmɑnɪkə / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A small, rectangular musical instrument played by blowing or drawing air through a row of holes, each of which produces a different note.

Examples

Well, I played a mean harmonica, as a younger man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth organ - another term for harmonica

Etymology

From Italian *armonica*, short for *armonica a bocca* (mouth harmonica), influenced by 'harmony'.
