unabated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnəˈbeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnəˈbeɪtɪd / (BrE)
not weakened in strength, intensity, or force; continuing at full strength or intensity.

Examples

According to the theory proposed, this is a consequence of the severe condition of perceived threat that persists unabated for the anxious child in an ambiguous sort of school environment.

Synset Examples

the winds are unabated
the popularity of his books among young people continued unabated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abated - reduced in strength, intensity, or force

Etymology

From un- (not) + abated (past participle of 'abate', from Old French abatre, from Latin abattere 'to beat down').
