encumbrance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
A burden or impediment; something that makes movement, progress, or life more difficult or complicated.

Examples

This rather detached attitude toward life's encumbrances has seemed to be the dominant trait in Mercer's personality ever since.

Synset Examples

the burden of responsibility
that's a load off my mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a load, especially a heavy one or a source of difficulty
  • Synonym: load - something that is carried, often emotionally or literally
  • Synonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction
  • Antonym: help - something that aids or assists rather than hinders

Etymology

From Old French encombrer (to hinder, block), from Late Latin incombrare.
