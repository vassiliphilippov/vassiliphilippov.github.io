towboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌbəʊt / (BrE)
A small but powerful boat used to tow or push larger vessels, usually in harbors, rivers, or canals.

Examples

The portwatchers were retired longshoremen and small time seafarers off towboats and barges who acted as watchmen on the wharves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tugboat - a small powerful boat for towing or pushing larger ships, especially in harbors

Etymology

From 'tow' (to pull by rope) + 'boat'.
