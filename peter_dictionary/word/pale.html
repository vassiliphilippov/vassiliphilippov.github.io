4. VERB

/ peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
(of a person, their face, or a thing) to become lighter in color, especially from fear, shock, illness, or lack of blood.

Examples

She looked at the girl speculatively from eyes which had paled with the years; from the early evening lights of them which had first startled Izaak to look at her in an uncousinly way, they had faded to a near absence of color which had, possibly from her constant looking at the water, something of the light of the sea in them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blanch - become pale, especially from shock or fear
  • Antonym: flush - become red in the face from embarrassment, heat, or exertion

Etymology

From Old French *pale*, from Latin *pallidus* (pale, wan).

5. NOUN

/ peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
A wooden stake or strip, typically one of a series, forming part of a fence.

Examples

One lithe leg straddled the railing and swung loosely before the creaking, torn pales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picket - a pointed stake or strip of wood forming part of a fence

Etymology

From Latin palus (stake) via Old French pal.

6. ADJ

/ peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
(of a person or their face) having less color than usual, often because of illness, shock, or fear.

Examples

Her face was pale but set and her dark eyes smoldered with blame for Ben.
The pale blob of the woman disappeared.

Synset Examples

the pallid face of the invalid
her wan face suddenly flushed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallid - lacking color, especially in the face, often suggesting sickness
  • Synonym: wan - pale and giving the impression of illness or exhaustion
  • Antonym: flushed - having a reddish or pink color, especially in the face

Etymology

From Latin pallidus (pale, wan).
