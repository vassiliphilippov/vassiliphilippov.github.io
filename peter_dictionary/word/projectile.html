projectile

1. NOUN

/ prəˈdʒɛk.taɪl / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛk.taɪl / (BrE)
an object, especially a weapon, that is thrown or fired through the air towards a target but is not self-propelled

Examples

As time has passed and science has progressed, the speed of military vehicles has increased, the range of missiles has been extended, the use of target-hunting noses on the projectiles has been adopted, and the range and breadth of message sending has increased.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile - an object or weapon that is fired, thrown, dropped, or otherwise projected at a target

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'projectile', from Latin 'proicere' (to throw forward).
