plain

1. ADJ

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
not decorated or adorned; lacking embellishment or ornamentation.

Examples

He then sold her minerals to cure her kidney ailment, a can of sage "to make her look like a girl again ", and an application of plain mud to take her wrinkles away.

Synset Examples

a plain hair style
unembellished white walls
functional architecture featuring stark unornamented concrete

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unadorned - not decorated or embellished
  • Antonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated

Etymology

From Old French *plain* (simple, clear), from Latin *planus* (flat, clear).

2. ADJ

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
(of food, drink, material, etc.) not mixed with anything else; in a pure or simple state.

Examples

Greasy soils are hardly removed by washing in plain water; and natural waters, in addition, often contain impurities such as calcium salts which can react with soils to make them more difficult to remove.

Synset Examples

plain water
sheer wine
not an unmixed blessing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmixed - not mixed with anything else
  • Synonym: pure - free from anything that contaminates, clean or unadulterated

Etymology

From Old French *plain* ‘flat, smooth, clear’, from Latin *planus* ‘flat, level’.

3. ADJ

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
(of a color or surface) having no patterns, designs, or decorations; simple and uniform in appearance.

Examples

Painters left less and less of a vase in a plain dark color; instead they divided the surface into many bands or covered it by all-over patterns into which freehand drawing began to creep.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patterned - having a decorative design or pattern

4. ADJ

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
not decorated, elaborate, or complicated; simple in style or character

Examples

It is plain as hell Johnson is no Reactionary.

Synset Examples

plain food
stuck to the plain facts
a plain blue suit
a plain rectangular brick building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simple - easy to understand or not complicated
  • Antonym: elaborate - detailed and complicated in design and planning
  • Antonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated

Etymology

From Old French *plain* (simple, clear), from Latin *planus* (flat, even, clear).

5. NOUN

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
a large area of flat land with few trees, often covered with grass

Examples

On June 24 more than 400 families started the three month trip across the plains to the Mississippi.

Synset Examples

they emerged from the woods onto a vast open plain
he longed for the fields of his youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie - large open area of grassland, especially in North America
  • Antonym: plateau - an area of high, flat land
  • Antonym: valley - a low area of land between hills or mountains

Etymology

From Latin 'planus' (flat, level), via Old French 'plain'.

6. ADJ

/ pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
easily seen, understood, or recognized; clear or obvious to the mind or senses.

Examples

The examples were plain.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unclear - not easy to see, understand, or recognize

Etymology

From Old French *plain* (clear, evident), from Latin *planus* (flat, clear).
