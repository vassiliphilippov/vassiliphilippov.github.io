homogenize

1. VERB

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something uniform or homogeneous by mixing its different elements together thoroughly

Examples

"How the Kooks Crumble "features an amusingly accurate take-off on sneaky announcers who attempt to homogenize radio - TV commercials, and "The Watchers of the Night "is a veritable waking nightmare.

Synset Examples

homogenize the main ingredients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standardize - make uniform or consistent
  • Antonym: diversify - make more varied or diverse

Etymology

From French homogénéiser, from homogène (homogeneous), ultimately from Greek homos (same) + genos (kind, race).
