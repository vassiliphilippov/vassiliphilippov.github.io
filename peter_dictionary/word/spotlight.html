spotlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑtˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A focus of public attention or interest.

Examples

Nevertheless, like any other human being upon whom the spotlight of the world plays continually, Khrushchev, the anti personality cultist, has become a comic actor, or thinks he has.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed being in the limelight
when Congress investigates it brings the full glare of publicity to the agency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limelight - a focus of public attention; public notice
  • Synonym: glare - intense public scrutiny or attention

Etymology

Originally from the physical light used in theaters to highlight performers; used figuratively from the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈspɑtˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to make (something or someone) the center of attention; to draw public notice to; highlight

Examples

Show spotlights the Kings - George Worth, Bill Kay, Frank Ciciulla and Gene Wilson, flanked by Dave Grossman and Ron Stevens.

Synset Examples

The introduction highlighted the speaker's distinguished career in linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make something stand out

Etymology

From the noun 'spotlight' (a strong, focused light), used figuratively as a verb since the early 20th century.
