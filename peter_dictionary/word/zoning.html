zoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing an area, especially a city or town, into zones reserved for different purposes such as residence, business, or manufacturing

Examples

Few rural areas are protected by zoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rezoning - the process of changing the zoning classification of an area

Etymology

From 'zone' + '-ing'; 'zone' from French 'zone', from Latin 'zona', from Greek 'zōnē' (belt, girdle).
