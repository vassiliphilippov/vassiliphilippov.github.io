inhibited

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd / (BrE)
Held back, restrained, or prevented from acting, developing, or expressing oneself freely.

Examples

If, for instance, such a change is produced by one or a few insulin comas or electroshocks, previously inhibited conditioned reactions reappear.

Synset Examples

in certain conditions previously inhibited conditioned reactions can reappear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uninhibited - expressing oneself freely and without restraint

Etymology

From Latin 'inhibitus', past participle of 'inhibere' (to hold in, check).
