accentuate

1. VERB

/ ækˈsɛntʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛntjueɪt / (BrE)
to make something more noticeable or prominent; to emphasize

Examples

It is accentuated by the need for pre selling goods, and private label competition.
The accelerated pace of metropolitan changes has accentuated the drive to conformity in congregations of the major denominations.

Synset Examples

Dr. Jones emphasizes exercise in addition to a change in diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something in speaking or writing
  • Synonym: underline - to emphasize or stress (something)
  • Antonym: downplay - to make something appear less important

Etymology

From Medieval Latin accentuatus, past participle of accentuare (to accent), from Latin accentus (accent).
