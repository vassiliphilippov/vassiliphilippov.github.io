petite

1. ADJ

/ pəˈtit / (AmE), / pəˈtiːt / (BrE)
(of a woman) attractively small and dainty in figure; small and slender.

Examples

Petite, lovely Yvette Chadroe plays the nymphomaniac engagingly.

Synset Examples

diminutive in stature
a lilliputian chest of drawers
her petite figure
tiny feet
the flyspeck nation of Bahrain moved toward democracy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminutive - very small or tiny in size, especially in a charming way
  • Degree Higher: tiny - very small in size
  • Antonym: large - of considerable or relatively great size

Etymology

From French 'petite', feminine of 'petit' meaning 'small'.
