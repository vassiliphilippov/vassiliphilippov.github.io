protozoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
Microscopic single-celled organisms, often considered a subkingdom or superphylum in biological classification, including flagellates, ciliates, sporozoans, amoebas, and related forms.

Examples

Routine microscopic examinations were made of the mixed liquor as indicated by McKinney and Gram for the various types of protozoa.
Very little protozoa activity existed below 40 ` F.
When the temperature reached 32 ` F all protozoan activity ceased; but as the temperature rose, the numbers of protozoa increased rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protist - any of a diverse group of mostly single-celled eukaryotic organisms, including protozoa and algae
  • Antonym: bacteria - microscopic single-celled organisms lacking a nucleus

Etymology

From Greek prôtos (first) + zōon (animal): 'first animal'.
