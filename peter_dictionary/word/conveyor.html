conveyor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪər / (AmE), / kənˈveɪə / (BrE)
A moving belt or mechanism that transports objects from one place to another, especially in a factory or industrial setting.

Examples

There are three principal feed bunk types for dairy and beef cattle: (1) Fence-line bunks - cattle eat from one side while feed is put in from the opposite side of the fence by self unloading wagons; (2) Mechanized bunks - they sit within the feed lot, are filled by a mechanical conveyor above feeding surface; (3) Special bunks - as discussed here, they permit cattle to eat from all sides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyor belt - A continuous moving band used for transporting objects, especially in factories.

Etymology

From 'convey' (to transport), ultimately from Old French 'convoier'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪər / (AmE), / kənˈveɪə / (BrE)
A person who conveys or transmits something, such as information, objects, or feelings, from one person or place to another.

Examples

He is the conveyor of a sacred reality by which he has been grasped.

Synset Examples

the conveyer of good tidings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messenger - a person who carries messages or information

Etymology

From convey + -or, formed after pattern of Latin agent nouns.
