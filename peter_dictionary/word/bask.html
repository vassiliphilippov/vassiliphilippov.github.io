bask

1. VERB

/ bæsk / (AmE), / bɑːsk / (BrE)
to take pleasure or derive enjoyment from something, often in a warm or satisfied way

Examples

This boosted local telegraph business and Manchester basked in all the free advertising.

Synset Examples

She relished her fame and basked in her glory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relish - to enjoy greatly or take pleasure in
  • Synonym: savor - to enjoy something completely, especially by dwelling on it

Etymology

From Old Norse *bask* (to bathe), later influenced by association with warmth and pleasure.

2. VERB

/ bæsk / (AmE), / bɑːsk / (BrE)
to lie or relax in a pleasant warmth, especially from the sun, or to derive enjoyment from being admired or praised

Examples

Port Jervis, basking in the foothills, was the city of God.
The cold settled like a tangible pall over the Mile High City, locking it in an icy grip that harshened its outlines and altered its physical appearance; it had a look of grim stark realism, resembling other cities whose habitual climate was cold, instead of the sprawling bumptious open-handed greedy Western city basking in eternal sunshine at the foot of mountains stored with endless riches and resources.

Synset Examples

The seals were basking in the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunbathe - to expose oneself to sunlight for relaxation or tanning

Etymology

From Middle English *basken* (to bathe oneself), of unknown origin.
