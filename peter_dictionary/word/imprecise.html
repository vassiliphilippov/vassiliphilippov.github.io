imprecise

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprisaɪs / (AmE), / ɪmˈprisaɪs / (BrE)
not exact or accurate; lacking in precision or detail.

Examples

There is no justification for systematizing the random statements of Irenaeus about the image of God beyond this, nor for reading into his imprecise usage the later theological distinction between the image of God (humanity) and the similitude of God (immortality).

Synset Examples

imprecise astronomical observations
the terms he used were imprecise and emotional

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precise - exact and accurate

Etymology

From Latin 'imprecīsus', from 'in-' (not) + 'precīsus' (precise).
