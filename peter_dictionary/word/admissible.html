admissible

1. ADJ

/ ədˈmɪsəbl / (AmE), / ədˈmɪsɪbl / (BrE)
acceptable or valid, especially in a court of law; able to be admitted or allowed.

Examples

Most of this testimony may have been legally admissible as bearing on the corpus delicti of the total Nazi crime but seemed subject to question when not tied to the part in it of the defendant's Department of Jewish Affairs.

Synset Examples

admissible evidence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inadmissible - not allowable or not accepted, especially in court

Etymology

From Latin 'admissibilis', from 'admittere' (to admit).
