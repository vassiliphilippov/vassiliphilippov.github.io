pragmatic

1. ADJ

/ præɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / præɡˈmætɪk / (BrE)
Dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a way that is based on practical considerations rather than theoretical ones.

Examples

Furthermore, religion tends to integrate the whole range of values from the highest or ultimate values of God to the intermediary and subordinate values; for example, those regarding material objects and pragmatic ends.
Most of the teen-agers I interviewed rejected it on pragmatic grounds.

Synset Examples

a matter-of-fact (or pragmatic) approach to the problem
a matter-of-fact account of the trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice
  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with abstract ideas and theories rather than practical use

Etymology

From Greek *pragmatikos* (relating to fact or deed), from *pragma* (deed, act).
