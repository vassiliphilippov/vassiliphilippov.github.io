crupper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʌpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌpə / (BrE)
A strap that runs from the back of a saddle under a horse's tail to prevent the saddle from sliding forward.

Examples

She is well-educated and refined, all wildcat and fur, and Union from the muzzle to the crupper ".

Etymology

From Old French croupiere, from croupe (rump of a horse).
