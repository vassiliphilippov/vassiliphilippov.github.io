Sabbath

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæb.əθ / (AmE), / ˈsæb.əθ / (BrE)
A day of religious observance and abstinence from work, kept by Jews from Friday evening to Saturday evening, and by most Christians on Sunday; also observed by some Muslims on Friday.

Examples

This leads Irenaeus to the somewhat startling notion that Adam and Eve died on the same day that they disobeyed, namely, on a Friday, as a parallel to the death of Christ on Good Friday; he sees a parallel also to the Jewish day of preparation for the Sabbath.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'shabbāth' meaning 'rest.'
