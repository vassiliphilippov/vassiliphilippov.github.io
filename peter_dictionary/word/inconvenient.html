inconvenient

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt / (BrE)
Causing trouble, difficulty, or discomfort; not suited to one's comfort, needs, or purpose.

Examples

She concluded by asking him to name another hour should this one be inconvenient.
Since I am innocent of this crime, sir, I find it decidedly inconvenient that the gun was never found.

Synset Examples

it is inconvenient not to have a telephone in the kitchen
the back hall is an inconvenient place for the telephone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenient - suitable or fitting for comfort, needs, or purpose

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + convenient (suitable), from convenire (to come together, be fitting).
