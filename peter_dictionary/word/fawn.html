fawn

1. NOUN

/ fɔn / (AmE), / fɔːn / (BrE)
a light yellowish-brown color

Examples

He had put on the gray jacket and the dark gray slacks and the fawn colored shirt he had worn that first night in Rome when he had encountered her on the street.

Synset Examples

she wore dun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - a pale tone of brown
  • Synonym: beige - a light grayish-brown color

Etymology

From Old French faon (young animal, especially a deer), originally referring to the color of a young deer.
