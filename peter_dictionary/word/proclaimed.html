proclaimed

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈkleɪmd / (AmE), / prəˈkleɪmd / (BrE)
Declared publicly; made widely known or officially announced.

Examples

And thus torn between his desire to be known as the composer of a successful opera and the necessity of remaining true to his proclaimed desire for anonymity, Rousseau suffered through several painful weeks.
Johnson's fat hand, another bottle were protruding from the truck cab, and that self proclaimed Baptist teetotaler, had a bottle at his own lips.

Synset Examples

their announced intentions
the newspaper's proclaimed adherence to the government's policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announced - made known publicly or openly
  • Antonym: secret - kept hidden or not made public

Etymology

From the verb 'proclaim,' which comes from Latin 'proclamare' (to shout out, declare), from 'pro-' (forth) + 'clamare' (to shout).
