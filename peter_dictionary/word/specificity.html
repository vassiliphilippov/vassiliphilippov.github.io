/ spəˌsɪˈfɪdəti / (AmE), / spəˌsɪˈfɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being specific rather than general; the extent to which something is clearly defined or identified

2. NOUN

/ spəˌsɪfɪˈsɪɾi / (AmE), / spɪˌsɪfɪˈsɪti / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being specific to a particular organism or group, especially in biology or medicine, as in the ability of a parasite or antibody to affect only certain hosts or targets

Examples

In attempting to improve specificity of staining, the fluorescein labeled antisera used in both direct and indirect methods were treated in one of several ways: (1) They were passed through Dowex-2-chloride twice and treated with acetone insoluble powders (Coons, 1958) prepared from mouse liver or from healthy sweet clover stems or crown gall tissue produced by Agrobacterium tumefaciens (E. F. Smith + Townsend) Conn, on sweet clover stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selectivity - the capacity of a substance or process to affect one type of cell, organism, or object over others

From specific + -ity, patterned after French spécificité.
