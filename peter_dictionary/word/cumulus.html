cumulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
A type of cloud that is dense, white, and has a rounded, fluffy appearance, often seen in fair weather.

Examples

He saw the Starbird as she lay, her slender mast up and gently turning, its point describing constant languid circles against a cumulus sky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stratus - a type of low, uniform cloud in a flat layer
  • Antonym: cirrus - a type of high, wispy cloud

Etymology

From Latin *cumulus* (heap, pile, mass).
