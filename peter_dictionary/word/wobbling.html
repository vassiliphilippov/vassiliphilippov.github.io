wobbling

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑb.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒb.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of sound) fluctuating or varying unsteadily in pitch, volume, or tone.

Examples

She stood up, pulled the coat from her shoulders and started to slide it off, then let out a high-pitched scream and I let out a low-pitched, wobbling sound like a muffler blowing out.

Synset Examples

a low-pitched wobbling sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsteady - not firm or stable; liable to shake or fluctuate
  • Antonym: stable - steady, constant and not likely to change or fluctuate
