internal

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
situated or existing inside something; happening or arising within the limits or boundaries of an object, organization, or system

Examples

(B) That because of the low viscosity of the fluid, the internal pressure is the same in all directions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external - situated on or relating to the outside; opposite of internal
  • Synonym: inner - further inside; closer to the center

Etymology

From Latin 'internus' meaning 'inward, internal'.
