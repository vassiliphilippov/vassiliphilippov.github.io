rim

1. NOUN

/ rɪm / (AmE), / rɪm / (BrE)
the outer, often raised edge or border of a circular object

Examples

The mean temperature of this region was approximated by the temperature measured halfways between the edge of the hot spot and the rim of the plug.
Sarah had begun to tell Lucien of Emile, she had begun to question and a little draft had crept across the room from the bedroom door, open barely enough to show a rim of blackness in the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge - the border or boundary of a surface or object
  • Antonym: center - the middle point or part of something

Etymology

From Old English *rima* (edge, border).

2. VERB

/ rɪm / (AmE), / rɪm / (BrE)
to form or run along the outer edge or border of something; to surround or edge.

Examples

He doubted if a man could wade as far as the desolate, dry hills that rimmed the valley.

Synset Examples

Sugar rimmed the dessert plate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: border - to form the boundary or edge of something
  • Synonym: surround - to be all around the edge of something

Etymology

From Old English 'rima' (edge, border).
