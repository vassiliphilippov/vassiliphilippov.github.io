2. NOUN

/ taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
A situation in a contest or game where two or more participants have the same score or result, with no winner or loser.

Examples

The victory was the first of the season for the Billikens after nine defeats and a tie.
The tie was against Southeast Missouri last Friday.
In those vocational programs organized with Smith-Hughes money, there may be a close tie between the labor union and a local employer on the one hand and the vocational teacher on the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draw - a contest ending with no winner or loser

Etymology

From Old English 'tīgan', related to the verb 'to tie' in the sense of 'bind', extended to mean 'draw in a contest'.
