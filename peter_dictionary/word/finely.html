finely

1. ADV

/ ˈfaɪn.li / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn.li / (BrE)
In a manner that is elegant, stylish, or refined.

Examples

There are other good representations of peasants and people of the court by actors who are finely costumed and magnificently photographed in this last of the Russian films to reach this country in the program of joint cultural exchange.

Synset Examples

finely costumed actors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegantly - in an elegant or refined manner

Etymology

From 'fine' + '-ly', forming the adverb from the adjective.

2. ADV

/ ˈfaɪnli / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnli / (BrE)
into very small pieces; in a way that results in small, thin, or delicate particles or fragments.

Examples

Dirt, which is here defined as particulate material which is usually inorganic and is very often extremely finely divided so as to exhibit colloidal properties.
The surfaces were coarsely and finely granular and punctuated by clear, fluid filled cysts measuring up to 3 cm. in diameter.

Synset Examples

the surfaces were finely granular

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coarsely - in large pieces; not finely divided
  • Synonym: minutely - in extremely small detail or degree

Etymology

From fine + -ly; 'fine' from Middle English, from Old French 'fin' (delicate, pure).
