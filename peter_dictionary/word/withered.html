withered

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪðərd / (AmE), / ˈwɪðəd / (BrE)
Lean and wrinkled, often as a result of age, illness, or lack of moisture.

Examples

But when he called for his withered, wrinkled sister Rose to care for him and the children, had he guessed that all he would remember of his woman was the memory of her climbing into that streetcar?

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrivelled - shrunken and wrinkled, especially due to dryness or age
  • Synonym: wizened - shrunken and wrinkled from age or illness
  • Antonym: plump - having a full and rounded shape, the opposite of withered

Etymology

From the verb 'wither' (Middle English 'widren', probably of Scandinavian origin).
