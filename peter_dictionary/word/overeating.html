overeating

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of eating more food than the body needs, especially to a harmful extent.

Examples

As an aid in reducing losses due to enterotoxemia (overeating disease), feed a complete ration containing not less than 20 and not more than 50 grams of Aureomycin per ton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating
  • Antonym: undereating - eating less food than needed

Etymology

From 'overeat' (to eat too much) + '-ing'.
