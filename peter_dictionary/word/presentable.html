presentable

1. ADJ

/ prəˈzɛntəbəl / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntəbəl / (BrE)
clean, tidy, or attractive enough to be seen in public or to make a good impression

Examples

The Macmillan government might be willing to let him go, but he has been dead seventy-eight years and even the Soviet morticians could not make him look presentable.
He felt cheerful again, refreshed; presentable in his wide-cut brown suit, the well-made riding boots.

Synset Examples

presentable clothes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpresentable - not suitable to be seen; not fit in appearance
  • Synonym: tidy - neat and orderly in appearance
  • Antonym: scruffy - untidy or dirty in appearance

Etymology

From French presenter (to present) + -able, meaning 'able to be presented'.
