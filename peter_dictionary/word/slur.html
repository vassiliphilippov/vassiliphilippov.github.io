slur

1. NOUN

/ slɝː / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
an insulting or damaging remark about someone or something

Examples

But before I leave, I do want to say this, that these hearings on the Mafia are in no way whatsoever a slur upon the great Italian people.

Synset Examples

in the 19th century any reference to female sexuality was considered a vile aspersion
it is difficult for a woman to understand a man's sensitivity to any slur on his virility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an offensive or disrespectful remark
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English 'sluren' (to drag through mud), related to 'slur' meaning to soil or stain.
