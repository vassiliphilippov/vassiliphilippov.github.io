afloat

1. ADJ

/ əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
Drifting aimlessly without direction or purpose, either literally on water or figuratively in a situation.

Examples

The concert opened with another big romantic score, Schumann's Overture to Manfred, which suffered fate, this time with orchestral thrusts to the Byronic point to keep it afloat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrift - floating or moving without being anchored or guided
  • Antonym: grounded - in a stable or secure position, not drifting

Etymology

From 'a-' (on) + 'float', Middle English rel. to Old Norse floti (raft).
