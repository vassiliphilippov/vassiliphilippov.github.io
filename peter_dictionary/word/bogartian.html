Bogartian

1. ADJ

/ boʊˈɡɑːrtiən / (AmE), / bəʊˈɡɑːtiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Humphrey Bogart or his distinctive style, especially in film or demeanor.

Examples

Cause of all this commotion: squat, pug-nosed, balding, hopelessly ugly Jean-Pierre Bravado, a Bogartian figure, who plays a sadistic, amoral, philosophic Tasti-Freeze salesman in old New-Waver Fredrico de Mille Rossilini's endlessly provocative film, A Sour Sponge.

Etymology

From the name Humphrey Bogart (1899–1957), American film actor, with the English adjectival suffix -ian.
