grammatically

1. ADV

/ ɡrəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmatɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to grammar or conforms to the rules of grammar.

Examples

And the more complex the morphophonemic system is in relation to the phonemic base, the less easily a phonemic system will be analysed without close attention to the morphophonemics - at least, the less satisfying will a phonemic statement be if it cannot be related through morphophonemic rules to grammatically meaningful structures.

Synset Examples

this child already speaks grammatically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ungrammatically - in a way that does not conform to the rules of grammar

Etymology

From 'grammar' + '-ically', modeled on other adverbs ending in '-ally'.
