instrumentalist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.strəˈmen.t̬ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strəˈmen.tə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who plays a musical instrument, especially as a profession

Examples

The drama in the theater and the concert in the hall both have a fixed time, but the time is fixed by the director and the players, the conductor and the instrumentalists, subject, therefore, to much variation, as record collectors well know.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musician - a person who is skilled in playing, composing, or conducting music

Etymology

From 'instrument' + '-alist'; directly related to 'instrument' (from Latin 'instrumentum', tool).
