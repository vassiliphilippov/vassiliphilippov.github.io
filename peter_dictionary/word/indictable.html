indictable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdaɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaɪtəbəl / (BrE)
(of an offense or person) liable to be indicted; able to be charged with a crime, especially one that is more serious.

Examples

I'd be as indictable as you for laundering money.

Synset Examples

the suspect was chargeable
an indictable offense

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-indictable - not liable to be indicted; not subject to prosecution for a serious crime

Etymology

From indict + -able; 'indict' from Latin 'indictare' (to proclaim).
