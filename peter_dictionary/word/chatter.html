chatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃætər / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
noisy, trivial, or rapid talk, often that is continuous and considered distracting or unimportant

Examples

Keep the chatter to a minimum, short answers, one word, if possible.
[EXHALES DEEPLY] [INDISTINCT CHATTER] [DOOR OPENING] [MUSIC PLAYING] GEARY: ...and in my judgment you'’ll continue to get those because I don'’t believe that President Eisenhower would ever pull out of Cuba...

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - incoherent or foolish talk; rapid, continuous talk
  • Synonym: prattle - talk in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of sound or talk

Etymology

From Middle English 'chateren', probably of imitative origin, suggesting the sound of rapid talking.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃætər / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
(of a machine or tool) to vibrate rapidly so as to make uneven cuts or leave a rough surface.

Examples

It's good for cutting all the planking because it cuts with a bit-like blade at high rpm and does not chatter the plywood like a saber saw.

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃæt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæt.ə / (BrE)
(of teeth) to click together repeatedly and uncontrollably, typically as a result of cold or fear.

Examples

Her teeth chattered so that she made three attempts at speech before she became intelligible.

Synset Examples

Chattering teeth

4. VERB

/ ˈtʃætər / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
to talk quickly and continuously, especially about unimportant things; to engage in light, informal conversation

Examples

Maggie was looking much happier already, clearing a space on the table and chattering about how she could put up a typewriter right there, and be brushing up on her typing so Eugenia would n't be ashamed of it.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about other people's private lives
  • Antonym: discuss - to talk about something seriously or in detail

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of rapid talking or animal noises.

5. VERB

/ ˈtʃæt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæt.ə / (BrE)
to talk quickly and continuously about unimportant things; to converse rapidly and often noisily, especially about trivial matters

Examples

Birds chirped and chattered in the trees and the sun, all dewy-eyed and soft, caressed her shoulders warmly from time to time.
Hey, Mr. Einstein... [CHATTERING]

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Synonym: gab - to talk at length, typically about trivial matters

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of rapid speech or animal noises.
