alderman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔl.dɚ.mən / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.də.mən / (BrE)
A member of a municipal legislative body, such as a city council, typically representing a district or ward.

Examples

On the occasion of his 1922 indictment the $ 10000 bond was furnished by an alderman, and the charge was nolle prossed.

Synset Examples

aldermen usually represent city wards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: councilor - member of a municipal council

Etymology

From Old English 'ealdorman' (elder, chief man), originally meaning a person of high rank, from 'ealdor' (elder) + 'man'.
