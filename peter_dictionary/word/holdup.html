holdup

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldʌp / (BrE)
A robbery, especially one committed at gunpoint.

Examples

Unlike the Sicilians, he additionally conducted holdups, robberies, and safe cracking expeditions, and refused to touch prostitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the act of stealing from someone using force or threats

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'hold up', meaning to rob while threatening with a weapon.
