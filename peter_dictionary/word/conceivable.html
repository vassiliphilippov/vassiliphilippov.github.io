conceivable

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsiːvəbl̩ / (AmE), / kənˈsiːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be imagined or believed; possible to conceive of.

Examples

There would be no conceivable sense in going to the opposite extreme of selecting items whose forms are the most unstable.

Synset Examples

that is one possible answer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconceivable - impossible to imagine or believe

Etymology

From conceive + -able; ultimately from Latin concipere (to take in, conceive) and -abilis (able to be).
