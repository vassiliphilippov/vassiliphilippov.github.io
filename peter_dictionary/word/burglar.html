burglar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrɡlər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡlə / (BrE)
A person who illegally enters a building, especially by force, with the intention of stealing.

Examples

It is, I am reliably given to understand, the technical argot for those who engage in your particular branch of the boost; i.e., burglars who rob while the tenants are absent, in contrast to hot slough prowlers, those who work while the occupants are home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals; steals in general, not limited to breaking in
  • Synonym: robber - a person who takes property by force or threat of force

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *burgler*, from Old French *burg* (town) + Late Latin *latro* (robber).
