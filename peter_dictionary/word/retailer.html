retailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌteɪlər / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪlə / (BrE)
A person or business that sells goods to the public in relatively small quantities for use or consumption rather than for resale.

Etymology

From retail + -er, with 'retail' from Old French 'retaillier' (to cut off, clip, divide up).
