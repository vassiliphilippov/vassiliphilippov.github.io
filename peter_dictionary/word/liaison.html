liaison

1. NOUN

/ liˈeɪ.zɑn / (AmE), / liˈeɪ.zɒn / (BrE)
a person or means that facilitates communication or cooperation between groups or organizations

Examples

The horrifying humor, the specifically sexual embarrassment of the joke gone wrong, the monstrous image of the fat man dressed up as a whore dressing up as a baby; the epiphany of that quivering flesh; the bringing together around it of the secret liaison between indolent, mindless sensuality and sharp, shrewd talent, cleverness with an occasional touch of genius (which, however, does not know "how to attack the problem of suffering "); the miraculous way in which music, revelation and death are associated in a single instant - all this seems a triumph of art, a rather desperate art, in itself; beyond itself, also, it evokes numerous and distant resonances from the entire body of Mann's work.

Synset Examples

he provided a liaison with the guerrillas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermediary - a person who acts as a link between people in order to try to bring about an agreement
  • Synonym: mediator - a person who mediates between parties to resolve a conflict or facilitate communication

Etymology

From French 'liaison', meaning 'a binding', from 'lier' (to bind), from Latin 'ligare'.

2. NOUN

/ liˈeɪ.zɒn, liˈeɪ.zɑn / (AmE), / liˈeɪ.zɒn, ˈliː.eɪ.zɒn / (BrE)
a secret or illicit romantic or sexual relationship, especially outside marriage

Examples

But the liaison successfully started in the last days of autumn was now languishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affair - a romantic or sexual relationship, often secret or illicit

Etymology

From French 'liaison' meaning 'a bond or connection', from 'lier' (to bind).
