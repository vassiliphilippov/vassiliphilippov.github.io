bittersweet

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪdɚˌswit / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌswiːt / (BrE)
causing or expressing feelings of happiness and sadness at the same time; both pleasant and tinged with sadness

Examples

For the last two years, this frontier of the arts has produced a number of so-called "non dramas "which have left indelible, bittersweet impressions on the psyche of this veteran theatregoer.

Synset Examples

a movie with a bittersweet ending

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: melancholy - a feeling of sadness, often with no obvious cause
  • Synonym: poignant - evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret

Etymology

From 'bitter' + 'sweet', originally used of tastes and later of emotions (16th century).
