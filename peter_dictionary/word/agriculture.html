agriculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡ.rɪˌkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɡ.rɪˌkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
The science, art, or practice of cultivating the land, growing crops, and raising livestock.

Examples

In agriculture, for example, despite the advances in biology, elaborate rituals tend to persist along with a continued sense of the imminence of some natural disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farming - the practice of cultivating land or raising animals for food or other products

Etymology

From Latin 'agricultura', from 'ager' (field) + 'cultura' (cultivation).
