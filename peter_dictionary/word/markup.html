markup

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkˌʌp / (BrE)
an amount added to the cost price of goods to cover overhead and profit when setting the selling price

Examples

My normal mark-up's 20 per cent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margin - the difference between the cost and selling price, often as a percentage

Etymology

From 'mark up' (phrasal verb), meaning to increase the price by marking it higher.
