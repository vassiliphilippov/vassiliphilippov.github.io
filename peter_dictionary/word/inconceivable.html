inconceivable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbl / (BrE)
Impossible to imagine or believe; extremely unlikely.

Examples

Remember that in seeking the modern in Utopia we do not deny the existence of the medieval and the Renaissance there; we do not even need to commit ourselves to assessing on the same inconceivable scale the relative importance of the medieval, the Renaissance, and the modern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impossible - not able to occur or be done
  • Synonym: unthinkable - impossible to imagine or accept
  • Antonym: conceivable - capable of being imagined or believed

Etymology

From Late Latin inconcipibilis, from Latin in- (not) + concipere (to conceive).
