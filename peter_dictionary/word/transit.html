transit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræn.zɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
a surveying instrument with a telescope mounted on a tripod for measuring horizontal and vertical angles

Examples

"Have you ever operated a transit "? he said.
You 'll find an old transit in the basement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theodolite - surveying instrument for measuring angles in the horizontal and vertical planes

Etymology

From Latin transitus (a passing over), from transīre (to go across); technical use for the instrument dates to the 19th century.
