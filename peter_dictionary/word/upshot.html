upshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌʃɒt / (BrE)
the final result or outcome of a process, discussion, or series of events

Examples

The upshot of the evening was that I got the address of Pendleton's studio - or rather, of the studio in which he gave his classes, for he did n't work there himself - and joined the life class, which met every Tuesday and Thursday from ten to twelve in the morning.

Synset Examples

the magnetic effect was greater when the rod was lengthwise
his decision had depressing consequences for business
he acted very wise after the event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence or outcome
  • Synonym: consequence - something that logically or naturally follows from an action or condition
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From earlier sense meaning 'a final shot in an archery match'.
