backbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈbæk.bəʊn / (BrE)
The most important or central part that gives strength or support to something; the main support or foundation.

Examples

The New York Central notes: "The freight traffic most susceptible to raiding by the C. + O. - B. + O. provides the backbone of Central's revenues.
They are the salt of the earth, and they'’re one of the backbones of this country.

Synset Examples

faith is his anchor
the keystone of campaign reform was the ban on soft money
he is the linchpin of this firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main part of something
  • Synonym: cornerstone - an important quality or feature on which a particular thing depends or is based
  • Synonym: keystone - the central, topmost stone of an arch, metaphorically the central or most important part

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈbakbəʊn / (BrE)
Strength of character; courage and determination to do what is right or necessary.

Examples

Somebody in this town must still have some backbone ".

Synset Examples

he didn't have the guts to try it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guts - courage, bravery, determination
  • Synonym: spine - strength of character or resolve
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage or bravery

Etymology

From 'back' + 'bone'; figurative use based on the backbone as the central support of the body.
