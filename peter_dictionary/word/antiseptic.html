antiseptic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.tɪk / (BrE)
Free from or able to destroy disease-causing organisms; thoroughly clean and preventing infection.

Examples

John said: "I have some antiseptic salve with me, but it's too late for that ".

Synset Examples

doctors in antiseptic green coats
the antiseptic effect of alcohol
it is said that marjoram has antiseptic qualities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sterile - free from living organisms, especially bacteria
  • Synonym: disinfected - having had most germs or viruses removed
  • Antonym: contaminated - having harmful substances or germs; the opposite of antiseptic

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + septikos (putrefactive) via French antiséptique.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.təˈsɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.tɪk / (BrE)
(figurative) free from corruption or dishonesty; scrupulously clean or honest in conduct or practices.

Examples

It ignores the sordid financial aspects (quite conveniently, too, for his audience, who could indulge in moral indignation without visible, or even conscious, discomfort, their money from the transaction having been put away long ago in a good antiseptic brokerage).

Synset Examples

antiseptic financial practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • Antonym: corrupt - morally debased; dishonest

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + sēptikos (putrefactive), originally referring to preventing infection; used figuratively for moral 'cleanliness'.
