modern

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːdərn / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən / (BrE)
Relating to the present or recent times, as opposed to the remote past; characteristic of the current or most recent historical period.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporary - happening or existing at the same time as the present; belonging to the same period
  • Antonym: ancient - belonging to the distant past, especially before the end of the Western Roman Empire (AD 476)

Etymology

From Late Latin modernus (of today), from Latin modo (just now, recently).
