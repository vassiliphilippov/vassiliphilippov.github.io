loyalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
the quality of being loyal; a strong feeling of support or allegiance to someone or something.

Examples

Its folklore and legend, usually disguised as history, are allowed to account for group actions, to provide a focal point for group loyalty, and to become a cohesive force for national identification.
So instead of being tests of the South's loyalty, the Spanish War, the two World Wars, and the Korean War all served to overcome old grievances and cement reunion.
The Federals is making everybody take the oath of loyalty around these parts too ", he crowed.
O' Banion accepted his platinum watch and the tributes to his loyalty, and proceeded with the bigger and better Republican deal.
"What matters here is family loyalty; faith in the Buddha and staying at peace with the phis, the spirits; and to live in harmony with nature ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegiance - commitment or loyalty to a person, group, or cause
  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being faithful or loyal
  • Antonym: betrayal - the act of being disloyal
  • Antonym: disloyalty - lack of loyalty

Etymology

From Old French 'loiauté', based on 'loial' (loyal), from Latin 'legalis' (legal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl.ti / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.əl.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being strongly committed or devoted to someone, a group, or a cause, often expressed through supportive actions or steadfast allegiance

Examples

To forestall any change of allegiance, the Democrats hastily organised a testimonial banquet for O' Banion, as public reward for his past services and as a reminder of where his loyalties lay.

Synset Examples

his long commitment to public service
they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause
  • Antonym: betrayal - the act of being disloyal or breaking trust

Etymology

From Old French *loiauté*, from Latin *legalitas* (legality, lawfulness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl.ti / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.əl.ti / (BrE)
A strong feeling of support or allegiance to a person, group, cause, or principle.

Examples

When I pressed for a purely religious definition, I encountered the familiar blend of liberal piety, interfaith good will, and a small residue of ethnic loyalty.
And you give your loyalty to a Jew before your own blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause
  • Antonym: disloyalty - lack of loyalty; faithlessness
  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being loyal or faithful

Etymology

From Old French 'loiauté', from Latin 'legalitas' (legality, lawfulness).
