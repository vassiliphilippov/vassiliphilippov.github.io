Nashville

1. PROPN

/ ˈnæʃˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ.vɪl / (BrE)
The capital city of Tennessee, located in the north central part of the state on the Cumberland River; widely recognized as a center for country music.

Examples

Occasionally gay young blades would write vividly to boon companions at home about their amorous exploits in Richmond, Petersburg, Washington, or Nashville.

Etymology

Named after Francis Nash, an American Revolutionary War general.
