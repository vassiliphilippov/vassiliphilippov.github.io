anguished

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡwɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪʃt / (BrE)
experiencing or showing severe mental or emotional pain or distress

Examples

Now she was intensely alive, anguished; her son was dead.

Synset Examples

an anguished conscience
a small tormented schoolboy
a tortured witness to another's humiliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressed - feeling or showing extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Synonym: tormented - experiencing or characterized by severe physical or mental suffering
  • Antonym: content - in a state of peaceful happiness

Etymology

From 'anguish' + '-ed'; 'anguish' from Old French 'anguisse', from Latin 'angustia' (distress, narrowness).
