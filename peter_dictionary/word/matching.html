matching

1. ADJ

/ ˈmætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmatʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Having the same or a coordinated appearance, especially in color, design, or style, often because chosen to be similar to each other.

Examples

Matching photographs in oval frames of Mme Bonenfant and an elderly man who must be Alix's grandfather.

Synset Examples

curtains and walls were color coordinated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinated - having parts combined in an orderly or harmonious way
  • Antonym: contrasting - differing in a striking way, especially in color or appearance

2. ADJ

/ ˈmætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmatʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of two or more things) having the same appearance, colour, pattern, or other characteristics as each other; forming a pair or set because they are identical or very similar.

Examples

Each time a dictionary form matches a text form, the information cell of the matching text form is saved.
If cell * * f is an information cell, it and any information cells in the Y-region that have been linked to * * f each contain an address in the W-region where a potentially matching form is stored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identical - exactly the same in every detail
  • Degree Lower: similar - having a resemblance in appearance or nature but not identical
