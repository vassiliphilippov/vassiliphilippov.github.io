primates

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmeɪts / (AmE), / ˈprɪː.meɪts / (BrE)
An order of mammals that includes lemurs, tarsiers, monkeys, apes, and humans, typically characterized by large brains, forward-facing eyes, and grasping hands.

In addition, the neocortical hypothalamic relations play a great role in primates, as Mirsky's interesting experiment on the "communication of affect "demonstrates.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'primates', plural of 'primas' (principal, of the first rank), used in taxonomy to mean 'of the first order'.
