native

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪt̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈneɪtɪv / (BrE)
a person born in a particular place, especially an original inhabitant or indigenous person of an area

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country or place

Etymology

From Latin 'nativus' (innate, produced by birth), from 'nasci' (to be born).

3. ADJ

/ ˈneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to the original inhabitants of a place or region; characteristic of people who have inhabited a place from the earliest times.
