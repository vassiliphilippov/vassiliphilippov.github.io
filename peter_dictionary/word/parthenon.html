Parthenon

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑːrθəˌnɑːn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθənɒn / (BrE)
the ancient temple dedicated to Athena, situated on the Acropolis of Athens, considered a masterpiece of Greek Doric architecture

Examples

It celebrates the unknown architect who designed the temple of Neptune at Paestum, next to the Parthenon the noblest example of Grecian classic style now in existence.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Παρθενών (Parthenōn), meaning 'of the maiden', referring to the goddess Athena Parthenos.
