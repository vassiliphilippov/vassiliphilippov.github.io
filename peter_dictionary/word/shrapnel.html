shrapnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃræpnəl / (AmE), / ˈʃræpnəl / (BrE)
A type of shell filled with metal balls or other fragments that explodes in the air, scattering its contents; also, fragments from such an explosive shell.

Examples

The injured German veteran was a former miner, twenty-four years old, who had been wounded by shrapnel in the back of the head.
Thus he wired J. P. Lower and Sons of Denver: "Have you any percussion hand grenades for throwing in a house or across a well loaded with balls or shrapnel shot?

Etymology

Named after British army officer Henry Shrapnel (1761–1842), who invented the shell.
