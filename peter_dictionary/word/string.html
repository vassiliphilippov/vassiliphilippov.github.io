string

1. NOUN

/ strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
a series of similar things, events, or ideas arranged in a row or following one another, especially when each is related to the next

Examples

He ran his string of successful conversions this season to 13 straight before one went astray last Saturday night in the 41 - 8 slaughter of Washington State.

Synset Examples

a string of islands
train of mourners
a train of thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: series - a number of similar or related things coming one after another

Etymology

Old English *streng* (line, cord), from Proto-Germanic *strangaz*; sense of 'series' evolved from earlier meaning of 'line'.

2. NOUN

/ strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
a tightly stretched wire, cord, or gut on a musical instrument that makes sound when plucked, struck, or bowed

Examples

For a serious young man who plays golf with a serious intensity, Palmer has such an inherent sense of humor that it relieves the strain and keeps his nerves from jangling like banjo strings.
Some time in 1912, Picasso cut out and folded a piece of paper in the shape of a guitar; to this he glued and fitted other pieces of paper and four taut strings, thus creating a sequence of flat surfaces in real and sculptural space to which there clung only the vestige of a picture plane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wire - thin flexible strand of metal

Etymology

Old English streng (cord, string), from Proto-Germanic *strangaz (rope, cord).

3. NOUN

/ strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
the section of an orchestra or ensemble consisting of musicians who play bowed stringed instruments, such as violins, violas, cellos, and double basses.

Examples

From a technical standpoint, the string playing is good, but the Pro Arte people fail to enter into the spirit of things here.
The twenty-five year old recording offers rather faded string tone, but the balance between the instruments is good and the transfer is very quiet.
The Hephzibah Menuhin-Amadeus Quartet (Angel) and Victor Babin-Festival Quartet (RCA Victor) editions give us superlative string playing (both in symphonic style) crippled by unimaginative piano playing.

Synset Examples

the strings played superlatively well

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woodwind - section of an orchestra made up of woodwind instruments
  • Antonym: brass - section of an orchestra made up of brass instruments

Etymology

From Old English *streng* (cord, bowstring), of Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
To arrange or connect things, especially ideas or events, in a sequence.

Examples

"String them onto some sort of little plot, and you 'll have a delightful operetta ".

Synset Examples

string these ideas together
string up these songs and you'll have a musical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - to pass something through or arrange things in a series

5. NOUN

/ strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
Thin cord, typically made of twisted fibers, used for tying, binding, or fastening.

Examples

And the stiffly regal look of them, she saw grimly, lacked the quaver of age which, thwarting the efforts of her amazing will, ran through her spoken words like a thin ragged string.
The preparations were elaborate: flowers, candles, incense sticks, rice wine, dozens of delicacies, and pieces of white cotton string.
The strings were draped around flowers in tall silver bowls (page 261).
Then, one after another, the villagers tied the waiting cotton strings around our wrists.
And I refused to be a fool, dancing on a string held by all those big shots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twine - strong thread or light string made by twisting two or more strands together
  • Degree Higher: rope - strong thick line made of fibers twisted together

Etymology

Old English 'streng' (cord, bowstring), from Proto-Germanic *strangaz.

6. NOUN

/ strɪŋ / (AmE), / strɪŋ / (BrE)
a thin cord or lace, especially one used to draw together and secure the opening of a garment, bag, or other item

Examples

Her hat had come off and fallen behind her shoulders, held by the string, and he could see her face more clearly than he had at any time before.
That's my life, but I thought that... ...that when it was your time, you would be the one to hold the strings.

Synset Examples

he pulled the drawstring and closed the bag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawstring - a cord threaded through a hem for closing a bag or garment

Etymology

Old English *streng* (cord, rope), from Proto-Germanic *strangaz* (cord, rope), related to strength.
