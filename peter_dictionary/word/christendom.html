Christendom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.tən.dəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.tən.dəm / (BrE)
All Christian people or nations collectively, especially with reference to Europe and Western civilization throughout history.

Examples

And if he did stand on the margins of modernity, it was not in dying a martyr for such unity as Papal supremacy might be able to force on Western Christendom.

Synset Examples

for a thousand years the Roman Catholic Church was the principal church of Christendom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christianity - The religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Old English 'cristendōm' (Christianity, the Christian world), from 'cristen' (Christian) + '-dom' (domain, condition).
