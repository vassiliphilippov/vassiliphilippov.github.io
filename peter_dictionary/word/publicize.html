publicize

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make information about something widely known; to give publicity to

Examples

Proposed property sales of general interest to small business concerns are publicized through SBA regional news releases, and by "flyers "directed to the small business concerns.
Have you publicized the cents per hour value of the company's share of insurance premiums?

Synset Examples

Please don't advertise the fact that he has AIDS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertise - to draw attention to a product, service, or event publicly
  • Synonym: announce - to make a public declaration about something
  • Antonym: conceal - to prevent something from being known

Etymology

From public + -ize, modeled after French 'publiciser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌblɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something known to the public; to give publicity to something, especially through the media

Examples

The effects of earthquakes on civilization have been widely publicized, even overemphasized.
I believe the situation is improving, but the success stories need to be heavily publicized.
From next New Year's Day let us keep careful account of each successive fatality on the highways, publicizing it on all media of communication.

Synset Examples

She aired her opinions on welfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announce - to make a public statement about something
  • Synonym: advertise - to make something known publicly, often for commercial purposes
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From 'public' + '-ize', following the model of French 'publier' and American English formation (early 19th century).
