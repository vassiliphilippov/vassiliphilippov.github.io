volley

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːli / (AmE), / ˈvɒli / (BrE)
In tennis and other sports, a shot in which the ball is hit before it touches the ground.

Examples

They are definitely in the same room with you, but your head starts to swing as though you were sitting on the very edge of a tennis court watching a spirited volley.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: groundstroke - a tennis shot made after the ball bounces

Etymology

From Old French 'volée' (a flight), from Latin 'volatilis' (flying).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːli / (AmE), / ˈvɒli / (BrE)
A simultaneous or rapid series of shots or discharges, especially from firearms.

Examples

As they were riding along this winding road on the bench of land between the two bluffs, a volley of rifle fire suddenly crashed around the two officers.
A shot caught him and straightened him up in screaming pain; a following volley of shots shattered glass, ripped the ceiling, and sent him lurching heavily from the table.

Synset Examples

our fusillade from the left flank caught them by surprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusillade - a series of shots fired or missiles thrown all at the same time or in quick succession

Etymology

From Middle French 'volée', from 'voler' meaning 'to fly' or 'flight'.
