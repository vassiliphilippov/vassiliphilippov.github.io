malinger

1. VERB

/ məˈlɪŋɚ / (AmE), / məˈlɪŋɡə / (BrE)
pretend to be ill in order to escape duty or work

Examples

"This is a very modest proposal cut to meet absolutely essential needs ", he said, "and with sufficient' deductible' requirements to discourage any malingering or unnecessary overcrowding of our hospitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shirk - avoid work or duty, often by deliberate evasion

Etymology

From French 'malingre' (sickly), probably from Old Provençal 'mal' (badly) + 'hegre' (weak, thin).
