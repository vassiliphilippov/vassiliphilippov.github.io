palpable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpæl.pə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a feeling, atmosphere, or sensation) so intense or noticeable as to seem almost tangible; able to be touched or felt.

Examples

The street that is full now of traffic and parked cars then and for many years drowsed on an August afternoon in the shade of the curbside trees, and silence was a weight, almost palpable, in the air.

Synset Examples

a barely palpable dust
felt sudden anger in a palpable wave
the air was warm and close--palpable as cotton
a palpable lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangible - perceptible by touch; clear and definite
  • Antonym: intangible - unable to be touched or grasped; not having physical presence

Etymology

From Late Latin palpabilis, from Latin palpare (to touch gently).
