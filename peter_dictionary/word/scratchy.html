scratchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrætʃi / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃi / (BrE)
(of a surface or material) causing irritation or slight injury to the skin due to its roughness.

Examples

Also fine are Sue Lawless, as a mother more protective and belligerent than a female spider and just as destructive, Harold Cherry, as her scratchy spouse, and Hildy Weissman, as a vegetable in human form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasive - causing irritation or wear by rubbing; rough
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough

Etymology

From scratch + -y, denoting 'having the quality of causing scratching.'
