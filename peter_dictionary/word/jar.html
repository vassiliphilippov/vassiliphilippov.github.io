jar

1. VERB

/ dʒɑr / (AmE), / dʒɑː / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with a sudden, jerky, or jarring motion, often as the result of an impact or shock

Examples

The Texans made themselves a comforting break on the opening kickoff when Denver's Al Carmichael was jarred loose from the ball when Dave Grayson, the speedy halfback, hit him and Guard Al Reynolds claimed it for Dallas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jolt - to push or shake abruptly and roughly
  • Synonym: shake - to move with quick, short movements up and down or from side to side

Etymology

From Middle English 'jarren' (to make a grating noise), of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɑr / (AmE), / dʒɑː / (BrE)
A wide-mouthed, cylindrical container, typically made of glass or pottery, used for storing food or other substances and usually without handles.

Examples

"Why, Winston ", she 'd cry, "I just now saw you eating it out of the jar "!
He found a jar of preserved tomatoes and one of eggs that they had meant to save.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle - a narrow-necked container for liquids
  • Synonym: jug - a container for liquids, typically with a handle and a spout

Etymology

From Old Provençal *jarra*, from Arabic *jarra* (earthenware vessel).

3. VERB

/ dʒɑr / (AmE), / dʒɑː / (BrE)
(of things or ideas) to be incompatible or in conflict, often causing a disturbing effect.

Examples

For another second or two he gave in to the annoyance that was directed at himself; then his mind moved on to be confronted by something far more serious, and as the thought expanded, the implications jarred him.

Synset Examples

These colors clash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clash - come into conflict; be incompatible

Etymology

Probably from Old French 'jarrer' (to quarrel), of uncertain origin.
