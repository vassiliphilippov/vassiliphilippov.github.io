vibrant

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪ.brənt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.brənt / (BrE)
full of energy, life, and enthusiasm; vigorous and animated.

Examples

Miss Stuart is as intensely vibrant as one could wish, almost an icy shriek threatening to explode at any moment.
When consciousness deserts the sleeping body and the wakeful world, it continues in the myriad progressions of the ever-present past and future, in a life as vibrant and real as the one left when the body tired and required sleep.

Synset Examples

a vibrant group that challenged the system
a charming and vivacious hostess
a vivacious folk dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: vivacious - attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or energy

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrare' (to move rapidly to and fro).
