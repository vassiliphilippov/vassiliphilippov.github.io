repayable

1. ADJ

/ ɹiˈpeɪəbəl / (AmE), / ɹɪˈpeɪəbl / (BrE)
(of a sum of money or loan) required or able to be paid back.

Examples

Business loans generally are repayable in regular installments - usually monthly, including interest at the rate of 5 - 1/2 percent per annum on the unpaid balance - and have a maximum maturity of 10 years; the term of loans for working capital is 6 years.

Synset Examples

business loans are usually repayable in regular installments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonrepayable - not able or not required to be paid back

Etymology

From 'repay' + '-able', following the productive English adjective-forming suffix indicating possibility or susceptibility.
