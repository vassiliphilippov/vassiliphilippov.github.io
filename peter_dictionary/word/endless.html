endless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛndləs / (AmE), / ˈɛndləs / (BrE)
Seeming to have no end; very long or continuing forever, especially to a tiresome or overwhelming degree.

Examples

To both the amateur and the commercial progandist the analyst lacks a soul, lacks appreciation with his endless probings and classifications.
He is not lost in the abyss of endless time; he has endless being.
He is not lost in the abyss of endless time; he has endless being.
Journalism supplies us with an endless run of such slips.
The long minute before he reached effective cover seemed endless.

Synset Examples

endless debates
an endless conversation
the wait seemed eternal
eternal quarreling
an interminable sermon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting or existing forever; having no end
  • Synonym: interminable - endless or apparently endless (often used about something tiresome)
  • Antonym: brief - lasting only a short time; not long
  • Antonym: finite - having limits or bounds

Etymology

Formed from 'end' + '-less', meaning 'without end'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛndləs / (AmE), / ˈɛndləs / (BrE)
(of something) very great in number; so numerous as to seem without limit.

Examples

The "wooden-leg "gag of vaudeville, another standby of this sort, had endless variations.

Synset Examples

endless waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted; innumerable
  • Synonym: innumerable - too many to be counted
  • Antonym: finite - having limits or bounds; not endless

Etymology

Formed from 'end' + -less; from Old English 'end(e)' (end) and '-less' (without).
