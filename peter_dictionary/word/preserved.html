preserved

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈzɝvd / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
Kept in its original or an unchanged state; maintained in a particular condition, usually to prevent decay or deterioration.

Examples

Oversized monsters are never brought home either alive or preserved, and field measurements are obviously open to doubt because of the universal tendency to exaggerate dimensions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - not preserved; in a natural, unprocessed state

Etymology

Past participle of 'preserve', from Old French 'preserver', from Latin 'praeservare' (to guard beforehand).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈzɝvd / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
(of food or other organic matter) prevented from decaying or spoiling and prepared for future use, typically by being treated to resist decomposition.

Examples

He found a jar of preserved tomatoes and one of eggs that they had meant to save.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - not preserved; recently produced and not spoiled

Etymology

Past participle of 'preserve', from Latin 'praeservare' (to guard beforehand).
