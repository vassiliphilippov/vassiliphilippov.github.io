offender

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛndər / (AmE), / əˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a person who breaks a law or rule; someone who commits an illegal act or wrongdoing

Examples

Now, under the impact of his wife's disclosures, he was brought suddenly to the realization that there was a limit to tolerance, however brilliant, however far-famed the offender might be.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: wrongdoer - someone who does morally or legally wrong acts
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed by the actions of an offender

Etymology

From offend + -er, based on Latin offendere (to strike against, offend).
