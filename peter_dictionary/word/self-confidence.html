self-confidence

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈkɑnfɪdəns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈkɒnfɪdəns / (BrE)
A feeling of trust in one's abilities, qualities, and judgment; assurance in oneself.

Examples

In the past year there had been too many examples of his euphoric self-confidence and self aggrandisement for their liking.
Just a big pile of self-confidence in an almost teensy package.

Synset Examples

his assurance in his superiority did not make him popular
after that failure he lost his confidence
she spoke with authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - belief in one's abilities or someone else's; feeling of trust
  • Synonym: self-assurance - confidence in one's own abilities or worth
  • Antonym: self-doubt - lack of confidence in oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to oneself) + 'confidence' (from Latin 'confidentia').
