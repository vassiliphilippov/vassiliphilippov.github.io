qualified

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑː.ləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪˌfaɪd / (BrE)
having the necessary skills, experience, or knowledge to do a particular job or activity; meeting the required standards or requirements for a position or task

Examples

For site planning work, it is best to have a qualified and experienced park planner to carry through the study.
Men qualified for the broader task of marketing manager are even more scarce due to the demanding combination of qualifications called for by this type of management work.

Synset Examples

many qualified applicants for the job

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unqualified - not having the necessary qualifications or requirements
  • Synonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills

Etymology

From Latin 'qualificare' (to make of a certain quality), through Old French 'qualifier'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑl.əˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
(of approval, support, or another quality) limited or moderate; not complete or absolute.

Examples

These conditions are unobtainable - are not even approachable in the qualified sense I have indicated - without the prior defeat of world Communism.

Synset Examples

gave only qualified approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unqualified - without restrictions or limitations; absolute

Etymology

From qualify + -ed, ultimately from Late Latin qualificare 'to attribute a quality'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑ.ləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
Officially recognized as having the necessary training, documentation, or credentials to perform a particular job or function.

Examples

Provided, That if the total award is not over $ 500 and there is no qualified executor or administrator, payment may be made to the person or persons found by the Comptroller General of the United States to be entitled thereto, without the necessity of compliance with the requirements of law with respect to the administration of estates;

Synset Examples

a registered pharmacist
a registered hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certified - officially recognized as having certain qualifications or authority

Etymology

From Latin *qualificare* (to make of a certain quality), via French *qualifier*.
