gracious

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪʃəs / (BrE)
Showing charm, kindness, generosity of spirit, or good manners, especially toward others; courteous and considerate.

Examples

In spite of the increase in numbers and prestige brought about by the conversions of Newman and other Tractarians of the 1840's and 1850's, the Catholic segment of England one hundred years ago was a very small one (four per cent, or 800000) which did not enjoy a gracious hearing from the general public.

Synset Examples

gracious even to unexpected visitors
gracious living
he bears insult with gracious good humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courteous - polite, respectful, or considerate in manner
  • Antonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From Old French gracieux, from Latin gratiosus (enjoying favor, agreeable), from gratia (favor, kindness).
