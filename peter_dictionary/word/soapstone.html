soapstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A soft, heavy, compact rock consisting mainly of talc, with a soapy feel, used for hearths, tabletops, and ornaments.

Examples

Heywood, that isn't soapstone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talc - a soft mineral often found in soapstone

Etymology

From 'soap' + 'stone', due to its greasy or soapy feel.
