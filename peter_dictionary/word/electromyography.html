electromyography

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊmaɪˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmaɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A medical technique for diagnosing neuromuscular disorders by recording the electrical activity produced by muscles using an electromyograph.

Examples

Electromyography revealed no evidence of lower motor neuron disease.

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + myo- (Greek 'mys', muscle) + -graphy (writing/recording).
