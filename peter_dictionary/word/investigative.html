investigative

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛstəˌɡeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪɡətɪv / (BrE)
intended to examine, discover, or research facts, especially as part of an official process; relating to or engaged in investigation.

Examples

At another phase in the therapy, when a pathogenic mother introject began to emerge more and more upon the investigative scene, she muttered in a low but intense voice, to herself, "I hate that woman inside me "!

Synset Examples

a fact-finding committee
investigative reporting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploratory - involving or related to exploration or investigation
  • Synonym: research - systematic investigation into and study of materials and sources

Etymology

From investigate + -ive, ultimately from Latin investigare (to search after).
