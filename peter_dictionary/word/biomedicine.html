biomedicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛd.ɪ.sən / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊˈmɛd.ɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medical science that applies biological and physiological principles to clinical practice, especially in the development of treatments and diagnostics

Examples

The benefits electronics can bring to bio-medicine may be greater by far than any previous medical discovery.
We use the term "bio-medicine "because of the close interrelation between biology and medical research.

Etymology

From bio- (relating to life) + medicine.
