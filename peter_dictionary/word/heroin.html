heroin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛroʊ.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəʊ.ɪn / (BrE)
an illegal, highly addictive drug made from morphine, often used recreationally and known for its powerful narcotic effects

Examples

And one of your stars has just moved from marijuana to heroin.
In Sicily he has plants to process them into heroin.

Etymology

From German Heroin, trade name coined by Bayer in the 1890s, based on 'heroisch' (heroic).
