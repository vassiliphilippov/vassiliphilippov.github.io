pleased

1. ADJ

/ plizd / (AmE), / pliːzd / (BrE)
feeling or showing satisfaction or happiness about something

Examples

Scotty was pleased.
On the contrary, he was pleased that his face showed a neglect of several days.
He drew back, embarrassed and pleased.
She was terribly pleased.
In the meantime he was pleased with the warm night and the still more pleasing company of his water brother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfied - contented, feeling that one's desires or needs have been fulfilled
  • Antonym: displeased - feeling unhappy or dissatisfied

Etymology

From Old French 'plaisant', present participle of 'plaire' (to please), from Latin 'placere' (to please).

2. ADJ

/ plizd / (AmE), / pliːzd / (BrE)
feeling proud or satisfied with oneself or with one's own achievements

Examples

Dr. Isaacs was so pleased with the quality of her biographical study of Sara Sullam that he considered submitting it to the Century Magazine or Harper's but he decided that its Jewish subject probably would not interest them and published it in The Messenger, "so our readers will be benefited instead ".
He seemed very pleased with himself, as though some intricate scheme was working out exactly as he had planned.
All evening she was eloquent and pleased with herself.

Synset Examples

proud of their child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of one's own achievements
  • Synonym: self-satisfied - excessively satisfied with oneself or one's own achievements
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
