expert

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.spɝt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spɜːt / (BrE)
a person who is very knowledgeable about or skillful in a particular area

Examples

Mankowski, the ball-hawking defensive expert, was cited for his performance against Bradley in St. Louis U.'s nationally televised victory.
The problems confuse even the experts.
For that matter, the experts themselves are a mixed breed.
Our technical assistance to these countries should place special emphasis on inducing the central governments to assume the role of advisor and guide which at an earlier stage foreign experts assumed in dealing with the central governments.
And with each sigh, like a whip in the hand of an expert, the grass stripped something from Warren.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialist - a person who concentrates primarily on a particular subject or activity
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation

Etymology

From Latin 'expertus' (tried, experienced), past participle of 'experiri' (to try, test).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛkspɚt / (AmE), / ˈɛkspɜːt / (BrE)
having or showing a high level of knowledge, skill, or aptitude in a particular area

Examples

It has a fast pace, excellent music, expert direction, and not only a good comedian, but an appealing person in his own right, Mr. Berman.
He was an expert florist, tenderly dextrous in the arrangement of bouquets and wreaths.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient at something
  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing or using something
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation
  • Antonym: inexpert - lacking knowledge or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From Latin 'expertus', past participle of 'experiri' (to try, experience).
