default

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔlt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt / (BrE)
a loss or defeat in a game, contest, or legal proceeding because one party fails to appear or perform

Examples

He was not going to lose the mission by default, and whatever reason Kayabashi had for bringing his little sight-seeing group to the mission, he was going to be in for a surprise.

Synset Examples

he lost the game by default

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forfeit - loss or giving up of something as a penalty, especially in sport

Etymology

From Old French *defaute* (fault, lack), from Vulgar Latin *defallita*, past participle of Latin *deficere* (to fail).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈfɔlt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt / (BrE)
to fail to pay money that you owe at the time you should

Examples

West Virginia toll bonds have defaulted in interest for months, and, despite recent improvement in revenues, holders of the bonds are faced with more of the same.

Etymology

From Old French 'defaulte' (lack, failure), from 'defaillir' (to fail).
