resistor

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstə / (BrE)
an electrical component that limits or regulates the flow of electrical current in a circuit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conductor - a material or device that allows electric current to flow easily
  • Antonym: insulator - a material or device that resists the flow of electric current almost completely

Etymology

From 'resist' + '-or', meaning 'one that resists'; first used in the context of electricity in the late 19th century.
