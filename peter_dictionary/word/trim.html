trim

1. NOUN

/ trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
a decorative item or bordering added to a garment, especially on the edge or surface, for embellishment.

Examples

Contrast trim provides other touches of color.

Synset Examples

the trimming on a hat
the trim on a shirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decor - the furnishing and decoration of a room or article
  • Synonym: adornment - something that adorns, decorates, or embellishes

2. ADJ

/ trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
(of a person or their body) neat and slim in appearance; thin and fit.

Examples

The maid was very black and very energetic, trim in a yellow pique uniform.

Synset Examples

the spare figure of a marathon runner
a body kept trim by exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slim - slender in an attractive way
  • Antonym: plump - having a full and rounded shape

3. NOUN

/ trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
a good or neat state of order, condition, or readiness

Examples

He was crouched over his anvil in the courtyard getting his chisels into trim, when a splinter of steel flew into his eye and imbedded itself in his pupil.

Synset Examples

in good trim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - the state of being arranged properly or neatly
  • Antonym: disorder - lack of order or neatness

4. VERB

/ trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or number of something, especially by making small cuts or adjustments

Examples

But if all alternatives should be clearly blocked off, it can be expected the Davis administration will take steps to trim spending at the spring session of the state Legislature.

Synset Examples

reduce your daily fat intake
The employer wants to cut back health benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduce - to make something smaller in amount, degree, or size
  • Synonym: cut back - to reduce the amount of something, especially money or use

5. VERB

/ trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
to decorate something by adding ornaments or embellishments, especially around the edges

Examples

Mrs. Peter Feringa Jr., last year's Achaeans' queen, chose an eggshell white filmy lace short dress made with a wide decolletage trimmed with an edging of tulle.

Synset Examples

trim the christmas tree
trim a shop window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items to it
  • Synonym: embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details

6. VERB

/ trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
to cut away the edges or unwanted parts to make something the desired size or shape

Examples

Trim excess clay away from outer edges.
Trim excess clay from around lip of mold and set aside while assembling lid.
Check fit of lid on jar; if inner lid is too big, trim to fit, allowing room for thickness of glaze.
Trim excess clay from rim of mold.
Trim away excess clay; reinforce seam with a coil of clay.

Synset Examples

pare one's fingernails
trim the photograph
trim lumber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pare - to carefully remove a thin layer from the surface of something (especially peel from fruit or vegetable)
  • Synonym: clip - to cut something with scissors or shears
  • Synonym: prune - to remove unwanted parts, especially from plants

Etymology

From Old English *trymman* (to arrange, strengthen, make ready).
