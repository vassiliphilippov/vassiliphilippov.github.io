dump

1. VERB

/ dʌmp / (AmE), / dʌmp / (BrE)
to dispose of something by throwing it away, especially as refuse or unwanted material

Examples

Why could n't they have dumped him off on someone else?
He dumped the pan of crumbled hardtack into the boiling pot of lobscouse.
Once he went after Hans and dumped his pot over the banister.
Or maybe poling up the south fork of the Forked Deer River braving the wastes dumped in it?
The city sewer maintenance division said efforts will be made Sunday to clear a stoppage in a sewer connection at Eddy and Elm Streets responsible for dumping raw sewage into the Providence River.

Synset Examples

No dumping in these woods!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw something away or get rid of it as no longer useful
  • Synonym: dispose of - to get rid of by throwing away or destroying
  • Antonym: retain - to keep in possession or use

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'dumba' (to fall suddenly).

2. VERB

/ dʌmp / (AmE), / dʌmp / (BrE)
to end a romantic relationship or association with someone suddenly and without much care or consideration

Examples

He dumped me in solitary twice ".

Synset Examples

The company dumped him after many years of service
She dumped her boyfriend when she fell in love with a rich man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break up - to end a romantic relationship
  • Synonym: ditch - to abandon or leave someone

3. VERB

/ dʌmp / (AmE), / dʌmp / (BrE)
to drop or deposit something in a heap or messy pile, often in a careless or hasty way

Examples

At midnight, I will dump 20 million in cash... ... on the crowd.

Synset Examples

The truck dumped the garbage in the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - to pour out or empty something by tilting or overturning it

Etymology

Possibly of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish dumpe (to fall suddenly) and Norwegian dumpe (to fall with a thud).
