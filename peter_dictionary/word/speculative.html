speculative

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtɪv, spɛkˈjuːlətɪv / (AmE), / spɛkˈjuːlətɪv, ˈspɛkjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
involving a high risk of loss or not financially safe or secure, often in the hope of substantial gain

Examples

This is a highly speculative venture.

Synset Examples

a bad investment
high risk investments
anything that promises to pay too much can't help being risky
speculative business enterprises

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - not involving risk of loss; protected from financial danger
  • Antonym: prudent - acting with or showing care and thought for the future, especially financially cautious
  • Synonym: risky - involving the possibility of loss or danger

Etymology

From Latin speculativus (contemplative), from speculari (to observe, spy out).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkjələtɪv, ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / spɛkˈjuːlətɪv / (BrE)
based on conjecture rather than knowledge, fact, or investigation; theoretical rather than certain or proven

Examples

But the farther out he moves, the thinner will be his hold on conclusive evidence, and the larger the speculative component in his inferences.

Synset Examples

a notional figure of cost helps in determining production costs
speculative knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
  • Antonym: factual - based on or restricted to facts

Etymology

From Latin 'speculativus' (contemplative), from 'speculari' (to spy out, observe).
