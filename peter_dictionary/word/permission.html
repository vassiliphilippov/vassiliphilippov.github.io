permission

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the official approval or authorization to do something, often given formally or in writing

Examples

Skorich was considered the logical choice after the club gave Norm Van Brocklin permission to seek the head coaching job with the Minnesota Vikings, the newest National Football League entry.
We'’d like your blessing, and your permission to start work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or sanction
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding by law or authority

Etymology

From Latin permissio (allowance, leave), from permittere (to let through, allow).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
The official or formal approval to do something.

Examples

But I said I would first have to get the author's permission.
Having received permission to give a camp reunion Halloween party, she asked that I come and be her date.
The control tower gave him immediate take-off permission, and the clean roar of the engine that took him off the rough strip spoke well of the skill of Donovan.
I must ask your permission.

Synset Examples

he asked permission to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - the official power or right to do something
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by authority

Etymology

From Latin 'permissio', from 'permittere' (to allow).
