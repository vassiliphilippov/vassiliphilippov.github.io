rehabilitate

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.həˈbɪl.ə.teɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.həˈbɪl.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to help someone return to a normal life or to restore someone or something to a former state of health, good standing, or condition

Examples

Rehabilitated?

Synset Examples

The prisoner was successfully rehabilitated
After a year in the mental clinic, the patient is now rehabilitated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restore - to bring back to a former or original state
  • Antonym: punish - to subject someone to a penalty for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *rehabilitare* (to make able again), from Latin *re-* (again) + *habilitare* (to enable).
