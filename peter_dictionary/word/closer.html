closer

1. ADV

/ ˈkloʊsər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsə / (BrE)
at a shorter distance; more near than something else

Examples

Bobby Joe had been sitting close to her, touching her actually, and holding her hand from time to time, but it seemed at once that Howard sat much closer.
It raced closer and they could see a woman with white hair, sitting astride an upright branch.
CICCIO: Have him come closer.

Synset Examples

come closer, my dear!
they drew nearer
getting nearer to the true explanation

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: close - near in space or time
  • Degree Higher: nearest - most near; superlative of 'near' or 'close'
  • Synonym: nearer - comparative of 'near'; at a shorter distance

Etymology

Formed by adding the comparative suffix '-er' to 'close'.
