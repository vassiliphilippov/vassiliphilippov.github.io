logistics

1. NOUN

/ ləˈdʒɪstɪks / (AmE), / ləˈdʒɪstɪks / (BrE)
the detailed organization and implementation of a complex operation, especially the coordination of the movement, supply, and maintenance of resources, personnel, and equipment

Examples

Furthermore, the network in Figure 3 is only the basic net through which other networks pertaining to logistics and the like are interlaced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply chain - the sequence of processes involved in the production and distribution of a commodity

Etymology

From French 'logistique', from Greek 'logistikos' (skilled in calculating).
