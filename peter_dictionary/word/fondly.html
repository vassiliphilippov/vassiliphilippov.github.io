fondly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɑndli / (AmE), / ˈfɒndli / (BrE)
In a way that shows affection or love.

Examples

"Oh that's Linda Kay ", Mama Albright said fondly.
She glanced at her companion fondly.
"But it do n't matter a-tall ", Lord supplied fondly.

Synset Examples

she spoke to her children fondly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affectionately - in a way that shows affection
  • Antonym: coldly - in an unemotional or unfriendly manner

Etymology

From 'fond' + '-ly', Middle English 'fond' meaning foolish or loving.
