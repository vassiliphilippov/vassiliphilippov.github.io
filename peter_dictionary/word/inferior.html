inferior

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɪriər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
of lower rank, status, or importance; not as good as something else

Examples

As it is, they consider that the North is now reaping the fruits of excess egalitarianism, that in spite of its high standard of living the "American way "has been proved inferior to the English and Scandinavian ways, although they disapprove of the socialistic features of the latter.
Since the latter obviously require an audacity you do not possess, you may perhaps suppose that I am taunting you as socially inferior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior - of higher rank, status, or quality

Etymology

From Latin inferior (lower, farther down), comparative of inferus (low, below).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɪriər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
of low quality or value; not as good as something else of the same kind

Examples

The underlying assumption, of course, is that only sight and touch enable us, in any precise and fully dependable way, to locate objects in space beyond us, the other senses being decidedly inferior, if not totally inadequate, in this regard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior - of high quality or value; better than average or than others of the same class
  • Synonym: substandard - below the usual or required standard

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior', comparative of 'infra' (below).
