guidepost

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪdˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdˌpəʊst / (BrE)
a rule or principle that serves as guidance for behavior or decision-making

Examples

An important observation of Pavlov served as a guide post to achieve such a reversibility by physiological means.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signpost - something that serves as a guide or indication

Etymology

Formed from 'guide' + 'post', originally referring to a post that gives direction, later used metaphorically.
