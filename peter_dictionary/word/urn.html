urn

1. NOUN

/ ɝn / (AmE), / ɜːn / (BrE)
a large vase, typically with a rounded body, a narrow neck, and a pedestal or feet, used for decoration or holding the ashes of the dead

Examples

Flanked by marble urns and alabaster lamps, they seemed to be posing for a tribal portrait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vase - an open container used for holding flowers or as an ornament

Etymology

From Latin 'urna' (jar, vessel for ashes).
