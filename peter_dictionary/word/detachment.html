detachment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of separating something from something to which it is attached; removal or disengagement

Examples

He can diagnose detachment of the retina where conventional methods indicate blindness due to glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart; the process of separating
  • Antonym: attachment - the act of attaching or being attached; connection

Etymology

From French 'détachement', from détacher (to detach), from Old French destachier.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
the state of being objective or emotionally uninvolved; lack of emotional attachment or interest

Examples

The calmness and detachment of his tone suggested unawareness of how implicit was his own guilt in the words he had used to defend Cromwell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From French *détachement*, from *détacher* (to detach) and ultimately from Old French *destachier* (to unfasten).
