equip

1. VERB

/ ɪˈkwɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪp / (BrE)
to provide a person, group, or thing with the necessary items for a particular purpose

Examples

The purified product was stored at - 78 ` in a tube equipped with a break seal.
The radiochlorine was stored at - 78 ` in a tube equipped with a break seal.
Purified inactive chlorine was then added from one of the tubes described above and the mixture frozen out and sealed off in a flask equipped with a break seal.
Direct information has been obtained from rockets and satellites equipped with impact sensors.
Stained or unstained sections were examined under dark field illumination in a Zeiss fluorescence microscope equipped with a mercury vapor lamp (Osram HBO 200).

Synset Examples

The expedition was equipped with proper clothing, food, and other necessities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furnish - to supply or provide with what is needed
  • Synonym: supply - to make available or provide for use
  • Antonym: deprive - to take something away from someone or prevent from having

Etymology

From French 'équiper', probably from Old Norse 'skip' (ship), originally 'to fit out a ship'.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈkwɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪp / (BrE)
to provide someone with the abilities, skills, or knowledge needed to do something

Examples

Good field techniques will not only equip linguists for better work, but also help them overcome negative attitudes.

Synset Examples

She was never equipped to be a dancer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prepare - to make ready for a particular purpose or activity

Etymology

From Old French 'equiper', possibly from Old Norse 'skip' (ship), originally referring to outfitting a ship.
