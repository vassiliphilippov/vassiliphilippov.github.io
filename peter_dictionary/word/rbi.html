RBI

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.biˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑː.biːˈaɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a run that is scored as a result of a batter's action, credited to the batter.

Examples

Mantle, the bull-necked blond switch-hitter, had one sensational triple-crown season, 1959, when he batted .365 and also led the American League in home runs, 52, and RBIs, 130.

Synset Examples

he had more than 100 rbi last season

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'run batted in', from American baseball terminology.
