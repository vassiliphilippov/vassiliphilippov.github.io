pavement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvmənt / (BrE)
A hard, paved surface, especially one forming a road, street, or walkway.

Examples

Sizzling temperatures and hot summer pavements are anything but kind to the feet.
It was all over the pavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidewalk - a paved path for pedestrians at the side of a road (American usage)
  • Degree Higher: roadway - the part of a road intended for vehicles
  • Degree Lower: track - a path or rough route, typically unpaved

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *pavement*, from Latin *pavimentum* (a floor beaten or rammed down), from *pavire* (to beat, to strike).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvmənt / (BrE)
Material, such as concrete or stone, used to cover the surface of roads, walkways, or other areas to make them suitable for walking or driving.

Examples

I learned, for example, that he made a practice of yapping at dogs he encountered and, in winter, of sprinkling salt on the icy pavement to scarify their feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphalt - a mixture of dark bituminous pitch used for paving roads
  • Synonym: concrete - building material made from a mixture of cement, water and aggregate
  • Synonym: stone - natural rock used as building or paving material

Etymology

From Latin 'pavimentum' (a floor beaten or rammed down), from 'pavire' (to beat, ram).
