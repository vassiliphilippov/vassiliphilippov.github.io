catalog

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈkætəlɒg / (BrE)
a book or booklet containing a systematic list of items, often with descriptions or illustrations, especially for sale or exhibition

Examples

An examination of some forty catalogs of schools offering courses in interior design, for the most part schools accredited by membership in the National Association of Schools of Art, and a further "on the spot "inspection of a number of schools, show their courses adhere pretty closely to the recommendations.
Working in a vacuum of minimal information can result only in show pieces that look good in exhibitions and catalogs and may please the public relations department but have little to do with the essence of interior design.

Synset Examples

he found it in the Sears catalog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brochure - a small booklet or pamphlet containing information or advertising
  • Synonym: inventory - a complete list of items, especially in stock or owned by an organization

Etymology

From Greek katalogos (a list, register), from katalegein (to list, enroll).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬.əl.ɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæt.əl.ɒɡ / (BrE)
a complete list of items, typically one that is systematically arranged and may include descriptive details

Examples

The largest of these organizations at present denies to the full time educator any vote on the conduct and standards of the group and, indeed, refuses him even the right to attach the customary initials after his name in the college catalog.

Synset Examples

it does not pretend to be a catalog of his achievements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a detailed list of items such as property, goods in stock

Etymology

From Greek 'katalogos' (a list, register), via Latin 'catalogus' and Old French 'catalogue'.
