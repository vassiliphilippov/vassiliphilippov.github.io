barbecue

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
to cook (food, especially meat) outdoors over an open fire or on a barbecue grill, typically using charcoal or wood for flavor.

Examples

Do build a wall of glowing coals six to eight inches in front of meat that is barbecued on an electric spit.
1 pound ground beef 2 teaspoons grated onion Dash of pepper 1 2 teaspoon salt 1 2 cup chopped walnuts 1 4 cup ice cold bourbon Combine ingredients; form into patties and barbecue 5 minutes on each side.

Synset Examples

let's barbecue that meat
We cooked out in the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - to cook (food) on a grill over direct heat
  • Degree Lower: roast - to cook food, especially meat, using dry heat, especially in an oven or over an open fire

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', from Taino 'barabicu' (wooden frame on posts).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbɪˌkju / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbɪˌkjuː / (BrE)
An outdoor event or gathering where food, especially meat, is cooked over an open fire or on a grill, often featuring a whole animal roasted on a spit.

Examples

We'll have us a little book barbecue in the yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookout - an informal outdoor meal where food is cooked and eaten outdoors

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', itself from Taíno.
