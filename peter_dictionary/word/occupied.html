occupied

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a space, seat, room, etc.) being used by someone; not available because someone is using it.

Examples

The doctor shot down to the lavatory and turned the doorknob, but to no effect: the lavatory was occupied.
Do patriots everywhere know enough about how the persecution of the Jews in Germany and later in the occupied countries contributed to terrorizing the populations, splitting apart individuals and groups, arousing the meanest and most dishonest impulses, pulverizing trust and personal dignity, and finally forcing people to follow their masters into the abyss by making them partners in unspeakable crimes?
Like I said, in prison, a man will do most anything to keep his mind occupied.

Synset Examples

she keeps her time well occupied
the wc is occupied

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vacant - (of a space) not filled, empty, or available

Etymology

From occupy + -ed, from Latin 'occupare' (to seize, take possession of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
Busy and involved with a particular activity, so unable to do other things or pay attention to something else.

Examples

Keep them occupied.

Synset Examples

she keeps herself fully occupied with volunteer activities
deeply engaged in conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busy - Having a lot to do; engaged in activity
  • Antonym: free - Not occupied; available

Etymology

Past participle of 'occupy', from Latin occupare (to seize, take possession of).
