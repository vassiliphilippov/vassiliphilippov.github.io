embargo

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
An official order by a government that prohibits trade with a particular country or the exchange of specific goods.

Examples

On April 25, the White House reported that a total embargo of remaining U. S. trade with Cuba was being considered.
We should have expected it after the oil embargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • Synonym: sanction - an official measure taken against a country to force compliance

Etymology

From Spanish 'embargo' (seizure, hindrance), from 'embargar' (to impede), from Latin 'imbarricare' (to bar in).
