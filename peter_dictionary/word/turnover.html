turnover

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌoʊ.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌəʊ.və / (BrE)
The rate at which employees leave a workforce and are replaced within a certain period.

Examples

Do you measure its relation to reduced absenteeism, turnover, accidents, and grievances, and to improved quality and output?
Turnover rates of personnel went up, production dropped, and morale was visibly reduced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attrition - gradual reduction of workforce through resignation and retirement

Etymology

From the phrase 'to turn over' with the sense of replacement or renewal in staff.
