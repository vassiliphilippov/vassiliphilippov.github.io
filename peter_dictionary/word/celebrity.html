celebrity

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛbrɪti / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛbrɪti / (BrE)
A famous person, especially in entertainment or sport, who is widely recognized by the public.

Examples

Once, Andrus walked by it, hastily scanned the bold black headline and the five column lead of the article (by Duane Bosch, staff correspondent - age not given), and muttered: "We a buncha national celebrities ".

Synset Examples

he was a baseball celebrity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star - a very famous or talented person in entertainment or sport
  • Antonym: unknown - a person not known to the public

Etymology

From Latin 'celebritas' (fame, crowd, celebration), from 'celeber' (frequented, famous).
