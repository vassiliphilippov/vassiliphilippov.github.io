enthalpy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnˌθælpi / (AmE), / ˈɛnˌθalpi / (BrE)
(in thermodynamics) A quantity equal to the total internal energy of a system plus the product of its pressure and volume, representing the total heat content.

Examples

Assuming thermal equilibrium between the anode surface and the transpiring argon, the gas enthalpy rise through the anode was calculated according to the relation **f whereby the specific heat of argon was taken as **f.

Synset Examples

enthalpy is the amount of energy in a system capable of doing mechanical work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat energy - the total energy of molecular motion in a substance

Etymology

From Greek 'en-' (in) + 'thalpein' (to heat).
