specialization

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular area of work, study, or expertise in which someone specializes, especially as their main career focus

Examples

There's more specialization and a narrower purpose in two albums recently issued by Dover Publications.
Considerable specialization in teaching subjects such as architecture, furniture design, textiles and color is also desirable.

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialty - a person's particular field of expertise
  • Antonym: generalization - the act of making broad or general statements or having a broader range of skills

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specialisatio, from Latin specialis (special) + -ation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of focusing on a particular subject, activity, or skill, or making something suitable for a specific purpose

Examples

This comes not alone from high-set, high-rep training, but from certain definition specialization exercises which the champion selects for himself with the knowledge of exactly what works best for him.
The exercise I shall discuss in this - the first of a new series of articles on muscle definition specialization of a particular body part - is the One Leg Lunge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalization - the process of making something more general or less specific

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specializatio (making special), from Latin specialis (special).
