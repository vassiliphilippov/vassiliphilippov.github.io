kingdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋ.dəm / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋ.dəm / (BrE)
A domain, field, or sphere in which something dominates or is most important.

Examples

So impressive were those serious years of study at the university that Hans later wrote, "to be perfectly free, the young man must revel in the great kingdom of thought and imagination; there is a struggle there, in which, if he falls, it is easy for him to rise again, there is freedom of utterance there, which draws after it no irreparable consequences on society.
The wailing, guitar strumming minstrels of the cattle kingdom made up songs about him.

Synset Examples

the untroubled kingdom of reason
a land of make-believe
the rise of the realm of cotton in the south

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realm - A field or domain of activity or interest
  • Synonym: sphere - An area of activity, interest, or expertise

Etymology

From Old English cyningdōm, from cyning (king) + -dōm (domain, state, condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋ.dəm / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋ.dəm / (BrE)
A country or territory ruled by a king or queen; a monarchy.

Examples

Bertha Szold was more like Meg, the eldest March girl, who "learned that a woman's happiest kingdom is home, her highest honor the art of ruling it, not as a queen, but a wise wife and mother ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: republic - a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives

Etymology

From Old English 'cyningdōm' (realm of a king), from 'cyning' (king) + '-dōm' (domain, jurisdiction).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋdəm / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋdəm / (BrE)
A country or territory ruled by a king or queen.

Examples

He has traversed provinces and kingdoms, but he has not consolidated them behind him, nor does he control them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: republic - a country where the ruler is not a monarch

Etymology

Old English *cyningdom* (realm of a king), from *cyning* (king) + *-dom* (domain, judgment).
