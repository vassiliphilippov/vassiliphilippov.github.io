tollgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊlˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtəʊlˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
A gate or barrier across a road or bridge, especially one at which a toll is collected to allow passage.

Examples

His being and His will - Stevie could not divide God from his Papa - illumined every parish face, turned the choir into a band of angels, and the pulpit into the tollgate to Heaven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tollbooth - a booth for collecting tolls at a tollgate

Etymology

From 'toll' (a fee for passage) + 'gate' (barrier or entrance).
