subsistence

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the minimum amount of resources, such as food, money, or necessities, needed to support life or basic living

Examples

"We must solve the problems which have forced these people to depend upon ADC for subsistence ", Martin said.
There is little evidence that existing public or private training programs have any great difficulty getting students to enroll in their programs, even though they must pay tuition, receive no subsistence payments, and are not guaranteed a job.
This probably would require some support for subsistence as well as for tuition, but the total would be no greater than for the proposals of unemployment compensation or a Youth Conservation Corps.

Synset Examples

social security provided only a bare subsistence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: livelihood - means of securing the necessities of life
  • Degree Higher: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Late Latin subsistentia (existence, substance), from Latin subsistere (to exist, to stand firm).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the means by which someone sustains life, especially at a minimal level; the resources or activities necessary for survival

Examples

We consider a rural community as an assemblage of inhabited dwellings whose configuration is determined by the location and size of the arable land sites necessary for family subsistence.

Synset Examples

farming is a hard means of subsistence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: livelihood - means of securing the necessities of life
  • Antonym: starvation - lack of food or means of subsistence

Etymology

From Latin subsistentia (state of remaining, existence), from subsistere (to stand firm, to exist).
