marsh

1. NOUN

/ mɑrʃ / (AmE), / mɑːʃ / (BrE)
An area of low-lying, wet land with grassy vegetation, typically found as a transition zone between land and water.

Examples

After three weeks' swift sailing, however, the ship entered an area of shallow marshes and river deltas.

Synset Examples

thousands of acres of marshland
the fens of eastern England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp - an area of very wet, soft land, often with trees
  • Synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with decaying vegetation

Etymology

From Old English 'mersc' (marsh, swamp), related to Old High German 'marsc'.
