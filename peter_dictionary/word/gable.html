gable

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪbəl / (BrE)
the part of a wall that encloses the end of a pitched roof, forming a triangular section between the edges of intersecting roof pitches

Examples

Square corner - and gate posts were an open-work pattern of cast-iron foliage; they were topped by steeples complete in every detail: high pitched roof, pinnacle, and narrow gable.

Etymology

From Old French *gable*, from Old Norse *gafl* (gable, fork, partition).
