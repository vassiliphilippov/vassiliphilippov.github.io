zigzag

1. VERB

/ ˈzɪɡˌzæɡ / (AmE), / ˈzɪɡzæɡ / (BrE)
to move or travel by taking a series of sharp turns or angles alternately to the left and right

Examples

No sooner had I started drinking than the driver started zigzagging the truck.
The Angels in the nursery were zigzagging wildly in all directions, seemingly at random.

Synset Examples

The river zigzags through the countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to turn aside sharply from a straight course

Etymology

From French 'zigzag', possibly of imitative origin, first used in the 18th century.
