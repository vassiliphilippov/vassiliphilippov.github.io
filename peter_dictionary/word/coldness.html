coldness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
lack of warmth or affection in attitude, manner, or response; aloofness or indifference

Examples

A seeping coldness entered Holden's being; his nerves seemed frost-bitten down to the tips of his tingling fingers and his spine felt stiff and glass like, liable to break like an icicle at any moment.

Synset Examples

a distressing coldness of tone and manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warmth - friendliness or affection in manner or feeling
  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Old English 'ceald' (cold) + '-ness' (state, quality).
