custody

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstədi / (AmE), / ˈkʌstədi / (BrE)
the state of being kept in detention, especially by the police or authorities

Examples

While he was in custody his wife divorced him.

Synset Examples

his detention was politically motivated
the prisoner is on hold
he is in the custody of police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detention - the act or state of keeping someone in custody

Etymology

From Latin custodia (guard, keeping).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstədi / (AmE), / ˈkʌstədi / (BrE)
the state of being kept under guard, especially by the police or other authorities

Examples

Mr. Roth, you understand I'’ll have to take you into custody.

Synset Examples

the suspect is in custody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detention - the act of keeping someone in official custody
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from custody

Etymology

From Latin 'custodia' (guarding, keeping), from 'custos' (guardian).
