deplorably

1. ADV

/ dɪˈplɔːrəbli / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔːrəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that deserves strong condemnation or is extremely bad or unfortunate.

Examples

I have heard people talk with contempt about the British regulars, but that only proves that a lot of people talk about things of which they are deplorably ignorant.

Synset Examples

he was sadly neglected
it was woefully inadequate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woefully - in a way that is very bad or serious
  • Synonym: lamentably - in a way that is deserving of regret or sorrow
  • Antonym: commendably - in a deserving or praiseworthy manner (opposite meaning)

Etymology

From 'deplorable' + '-ly', ultimately from French 'déplorer' and Latin 'deplorare' (to lament).
