ash

1. NOUN

/ æʃ / (AmE), / æʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of the genus Fraxinus, with pinnate leaves, often grown for ornament or timber.

Examples

Maple and ash, beech and elm, one hundred to win on Three in the fourth, fifty to win on Six in the third, one hundred to win on Two in the eighth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fraxinus - the botanical genus for ash trees

Etymology

From Old English æsc, related to Old Norse askr, meaning 'ash tree'.

2. NOUN

/ æʃ / (AmE), / æʃ / (BrE)
the powdery residue left after the burning of a substance, especially wood, coal, or a body

Examples

And, therefore, being in disgrace, they would not be cremated and their ashes flung to the winds in public ceremony.
Sameness lined the streets with two story houses the color of ash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinder - a small piece of partly burned coal or wood that has stopped giving off flames but still has combustible matter in it

Etymology

Old English æsce, from Proto-Germanic *askō.
