overture

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərtʃər / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətjʊə / (BrE)
an orchestral piece played at the beginning of an opera or oratorio, introducing the main themes of the work

Examples

After a "busy "overture, the curtain rises on a farm scene - the Ranavan Valley in northern Maine.

Etymology

From French 'ouverture' (opening), from Old French 'overt', from Latin 'aperire' (to open).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌtjʊə / (BrE)
a proposal or action made to begin a discussion or to form a relationship, often as an initial approach

Examples

After every money argument, she rebuffed her husband's overtures until the matter was settled in her favor.

Synset Examples

she rejected his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a suggestion or offer put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration

Etymology

From French *ouverture* (opening), from Latin *apertura* (opening).
