jewel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəl / (BrE)
A precious or semiprecious stone, such as a diamond, ruby, or emerald, that is typically cut and polished for use in jewelry.

Examples

Repeating it like a student telling himself that the jewel is in the lotus he sank into nirvana.
Lighted windows glowed jewel bright through the downpour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a precious or semiprecious stone, especially when cut and polished

Etymology

From Old French 'jouel' (ornament, jewel), from Medieval Latin 'jocale' (plaything, jewel).
