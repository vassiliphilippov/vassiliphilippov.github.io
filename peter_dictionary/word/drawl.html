drawl

1. NOUN

/ drɔl / (AmE), / drɔːl / (BrE)
a way of speaking in which the vowel sounds are stretched out, producing a slow and lazy effect, often associated with southern American English

Examples

He was a wiry, inscrutable, silent country boy from the red clay of rural Alabama, and he spoke with the broad drawl that others normally make fun of.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twang - a nasal or resonant speech quality, also associated with regional accents

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *dralen* (to linger) or related to 'draw' (to drag).

2. VERB

/ drɑl / (AmE), / drɔːl / (BrE)
to speak slowly with long, drawn-out vowel sounds

Examples

"Might get there faster walkin "', Lord drawled, "seein' as how I got a busted front spring.

Synset Examples

drawl one's vowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enunciate - to pronounce words clearly and distinctly
