anyway

1. ADV

/ ˈɛniˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛniweɪ / (BrE)
Used to introduce a statement that supports or explains a previous statement, or to change the subject.

Examples

Anyway, a number of them meet here in devastating collisions.
All pricks like Coughlin run it anyway, one way or another.
"He 'll choke anyway ".
He had little time to himself, anyway.
What do you expect to get tonight, anyway?

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyhow - used to introduce a statement supporting a previous statement
  • Synonym: in any case - used to state that something remains true whatever the situation
  • Synonym: at any rate - used to emphasize something is true despite other things
  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; however
