lavishly

1. ADV

/ ˈlævɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈlævɪʃli / (BrE)
In a way that involves spending, giving, or using a large amount of something, often wastefully or extravagantly.

Examples

The drama itself - and this seems to be lavishly true of Biblical drama - often has hardly any relationship with authenticity at all.
They went downstairs, loaded a tray lavishly.

Synset Examples

the United States, up to the 1920s, used fuel lavishly, mainly because it was so cheap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagantly - in a way that spends or uses resources freely or recklessly
  • Antonym: frugally - in a way that is careful or sparing in the use of resources

Etymology

From 'lavish' (from Middle English 'lavasse', from Old French, ultimately from Latin *lavare* 'to wash').
