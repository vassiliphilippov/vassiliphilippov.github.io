berg

1. NOUN

/ bɜrɡ / (AmE), / bɜːɡ / (BrE)
a large mass of ice floating at sea, especially one that has broken off from a polar glacier; an iceberg

Examples

As the bergs grew larger, Hudson was forced to turn south into what is now Ungava Bay, an inlet of the great strait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iceberg - a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier or ice shelf

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'berg' (mountain), from Dutch 'berg'.
