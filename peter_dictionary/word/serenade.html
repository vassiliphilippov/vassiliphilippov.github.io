serenade

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈneɪd / (BrE)
a light, often romantic musical composition in several movements, typically performed in the evening, for a small orchestra or ensemble

Examples

Moreover, the piece is written in five movements, rather than the conventional four of most quintets, and this gives the opus a serenade or divertimento flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divertimento - a light and entertaining musical composition, typically for a small ensemble

Etymology

From Italian 'serenata', from 'sereno' (serene, evening).

2. VERB

/ ˌsɛrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈneɪd / (BrE)
to sing or play music for someone, especially in the open air and often as a display of romantic affection

Examples

Cardboard noisemakers, substitutes for the unavailable tin models, were being hawked and bought at makeshift stands every few yards along Broadway, and one's ears were continually serenaded by the horns' rasps and bleats.

Synset Examples

She was serenaded by her admirers

Etymology

From Italian 'serenata', from 'sereno' (calm, evening), related to the practice of singing in the evening.
