dizzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪzi / (AmE), / ˈdɪzi / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking seriousness and given to silliness or frivolity; scatterbrained.

Examples

But the book is written around a somewhat dizzy cartoonist, and it has to be that way.

Synset Examples

a dizzy blonde
light-headed teenagers
silly giggles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatterbrained - careless and unable to concentrate; disorganized
  • Synonym: silly - showing a lack of good sense or judgment; frivolous
  • Antonym: serious - thoughtful, solemn, and not funny or trivial

Etymology

From Old English *dysig* (foolish, stupid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪzi / (AmE), / ˈdɪzi / (BrE)
having or causing a sensation of spinning around and losing one's balance; feeling as if about to fall

Examples

Or you could hope the parachute would n't open just so you could say you saw it not open, not because you meant any harm to Starkey Poe in his suit of red underwear, but mainly because you were tired of being an old maid - a thing which cannot admit when it thinks it might be pregnant, but must stand the dizzy feeling all alone and go on like everything is all right instead of being able to say to somebody in a normal voice: "I think I 'm pregnant ".
I'll knock you dizzy.

Synset Examples

had a dizzy spell
a dizzy pinnacle
had a headache and felt giddy
a giddy precipice
feeling woozy from the blow on his head
a vertiginous climb up the face of the cliff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giddy - feeling as if you are spinning and likely to fall
  • Synonym: vertiginous - causing a feeling that you are about to fall, especially from a great height

Etymology

From Old English 'dysig' (foolish, foolishly behaving, mad).
