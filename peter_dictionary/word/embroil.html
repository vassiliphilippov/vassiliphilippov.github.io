embroil

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbrɔɪl / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪl / (BrE)
to involve someone or something deeply in an argument, conflict, or difficult situation

Examples

Immediately, the religious groups of the city were embroiled in an angry dispute over the alleged invasion of a man's right to freedom of religious belief and conscience.

Synset Examples

They were swept up by the events
don't drag me into this business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entangle - to twist together or involve in complications
  • Synonym: involve - to include or engage as a necessary part or result
  • Antonym: extricate - to free from an entanglement or difficulty

Etymology

From French 'embrouiller' (to confuse), from 'en-' (in) + 'brouiller' (to mix up, confuse).
