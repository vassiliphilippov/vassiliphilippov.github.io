spout

1. VERB

/ spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
(of liquid or another substance) to gush or flow out with force in a sudden stream or jet.

Examples

As the messages from Himmler, Heydrich, and Eichmann came in through Alfred Funk, Lublin's fountainhead spouted.

Synset Examples

water gushed forth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gush - to flow out suddenly and forcefully
  • Degree Lower: dribble - to flow slowly in drops or a thin stream
  • Synonym: jet - to spout out in a strong, focused stream

Etymology

Likely from Middle English *spouten*, from Middle Dutch *spouten* (to spout, spurt).

2. VERB

/ spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
to talk loudly, excitedly, or at length, especially in a boastful or pompous way

Examples

The terms are fairly safe to use on this side of the ocean, but before you start spouting them to your date, it might be best to find out if he was a member of Major Pockmanster's Delhi Regiment, since resentment toward the natives was reportedly very high in that outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rant - to speak or shout at length in a wild or impassioned way
  • Synonym: pontificate - to speak in a pompous or dogmatic way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English spoten, related to Middle Dutch spuiten (to spout, squirt).
