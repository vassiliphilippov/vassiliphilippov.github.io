sunbaked

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌnˌbeɪkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbeɪkt / (BrE)
Dried out or baked by the heat of the sun; exposed to or hardened under intense sunlight.

Examples

The town was about what Wilson expected: one main street with its rows of false-fronted buildings, a water tower, a few warehouses, a single hotel; all dusty and sunbaked.

Synset Examples

a vast desert all adust
land lying baked in the heat
parched soil
the earth was scorched and bare
sunbaked salt flats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - extremely dry, especially due to heat
  • Synonym: scorched - burned or dried out by intense heat
  • Antonym: moist - slightly wet; opposite of dry or parched

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'baked', meaning baked by the sun.
