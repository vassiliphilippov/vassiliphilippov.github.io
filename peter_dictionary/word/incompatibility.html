incompatibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kəmˌpæt.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəm.pæ.tɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or condition of being incompatible; a relationship between things, ideas, or statements that cannot exist or be true at the same time.

Examples

The psychological incompatibility of these emotional states seems to be reflected in, or based on, this marked difference.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compatibility - the state of being able to exist or occur together without conflict

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incompatibilitas, from incompatible (from Latin incompatibilis) + -ity.
