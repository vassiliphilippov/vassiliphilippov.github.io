skeptical

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
having or expressing doubt about the truth or value of something, especially religious or accepted beliefs; not easily convinced.

Examples

However, many herpetologists remain skeptical and would prefer a tentative maximum of about 30 feet.
It is to say rather, I believe, that he has brought to bear on the history, the traditions, and the lore of his region a critical, skeptical mind - the same mind which has made of him an inveterate experimenter in literary form and technique.
The fact that he has cast over those materials the light of a skeptical mind does not make him any the less Southern, I rather think, for the South has been no more solid than other regions except in the political and related areas where patronage and force and intimidation and fear may produce a surface uniformity.

Synset Examples

a skeptical approach to the nature of miracles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cynical - believing that people are motivated purely by self-interest; distrustful of motives

Etymology

From Greek *skeptikos* (inquirer), via Latin *scepticus*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
Not easily convinced; having doubts or reservations about something.

Examples

Some musicians were enthusiastic, some skeptical.
Faulkner has found it useful, but he has employed it with his habitual independence of mind and skeptical outlook.

Synset Examples

a skeptical attitude
a skeptical listener

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubtful - feeling unsure or uncertain about something
  • Antonym: convinced - completely certain about something

Etymology

From Greek 'skeptikos' (inquirer, doubter), via Latin 'scepticus' and Old French 'sceptique'.
