absentminded

1. ADJ

/ ˌæbsəntˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˌæbsəntˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Tending to be distracted by one's own thoughts and not pay attention to what is happening around one.

Examples

Absent-minded, subject to unexplained tears.
"That's Johnny ", sighed the bank president, "the best hearted boy in the world, but absent-minded ".
He also had, at times, an uncanny absent-minded air like a sleepwalker; he would look right through you while you were talking to him, and if you said, "For Christ's sake, Donald, you 've got Prussian blue all over your shirt ", he would smile, and nod, and an hour later the paint would be all over his pants as well.

Synset Examples

an absent stare
an absentminded professor
the scatty glancing quality of a hyperactive but unfocused intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because of mixed attentions
  • Synonym: preoccupied - completely engrossed in thought
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention to something

Etymology

Formed from 'absent' + 'minded', first attested in the 18th century.
