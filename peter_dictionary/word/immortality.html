immortality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmɔrˈtæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌmɔːˈtælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of living forever; eternal life or existence without death or end.

Examples

He does not mean to say that Adam lost the similitude of God and his immortality through the fall; for he was created not exactly immortal, nor yet exactly mortal, but capable of immortality as well as of mortality.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin immortalitas ('immortality'), from in- ('not') + mortalitas ('mortality').
