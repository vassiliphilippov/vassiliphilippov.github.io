nonequivalence

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʌn.ɪˈkwɪv.ələns / (AmE), / ˌnʌn.ɪˈkwɪv.ələns / (BrE)
The state or property of being not equivalent; lack of equivalence or interchangeability.

Examples

However, any oxygen nonequivalence would shorten either the already extremely short **f interlayer distance of 2.55 A or the non hydrogen bonded **f interlayer interactions which are already quite short at 2.58 A.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equivalence - the state of being equal or interchangeable

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'equivalence' (state of being equal in value or meaning), ultimately from Latin 'aequivalentia'.
