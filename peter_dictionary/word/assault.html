assault

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt / (BrE)
a violent and forceful attack, especially in military operations, often referring to close fighting during the culmination of an attack

Examples

In this manner, he seeks to expunge from his own soul the guilt pangs caused by his personal assaults against the English at Dunkirk.
But he plunges into yet another, this time with Norway, and is killed in an assault on the fortress of Fredrikshall, being only thirty-six years of age when he died.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an act of using force to try to harm or defeat someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'asaut' (attack), from Vulgar Latin '*assaltus*', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'saltus' (jump).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɔlt / (AmE), / əˈsɔːlt / (BrE)
the act of threatening or attempting to physically harm someone, especially when it appears the attacker is capable of causing injury

Examples

O' Banion's first conflict with the police came in 1909, at seventeen, when he was committed to Bridewell Prison for three months for burglary; two years later he served another three months for assault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - actual physical attack or beating
  • Antonym: defense - act of defending from an assault or attack

Etymology

From Old French *asaut* (attack) and Latin *assultus* (a leap upon, attack).
