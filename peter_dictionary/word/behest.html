behest

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈhɛst / (AmE), / bɪˈhɛst / (BrE)
an authoritative order or command; a strong request or directive, often from someone in power

Examples

You kill people at the, uh, at the behest of your superiors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - an order given with authority
  • Synonym: order - an instruction or direction given by someone in authority
  • Degree Lower: request - an act of asking for something to be given or done

Etymology

From Old English ‘behǣs’ (a vow or command), related to ‘behatan’ (to promise).
