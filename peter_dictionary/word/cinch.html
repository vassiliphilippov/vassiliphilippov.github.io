cinch

1. NOUN

/ sɪntʃ / (AmE), / sɪntʃ / (BrE)
A task or activity that is very easy to do; something that requires little effort.

Examples

If Rose Mallory's killer acted this way, catching up with him was going to be a cinch.
Baseball's no cinch.

Synset Examples

marketing this product will be no picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doddle - a task that is very easy to do (primarily British English)
  • Synonym: snap - something easily done; a simple task
  • Synonym: breeze - something that is easy to do
  • Antonym: challenge - something difficult, the opposite of an easy task

Etymology

Originally American English, from Spanish 'cincha' (a saddle girth or belt), metaphorically indicating something secure or assured.
