reward

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈwɔrd / (AmE), / rɪˈwɔːd / (BrE)
an action or system of actions intended to reinforce or encourage approved behavior, often by offering positive outcomes for desirable actions

Examples

The insuperable reward systems that most religions embody have great motivating effects.
Religion provides the most attractive rewards, either in this world or the next, for those who not merely abide by its norms, but who engage in good works.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punishment - an action to discourage or penalize undesirable behavior

Etymology

From Old French 'rewarder' (to regard, take notice of, reward), from 're-' (again) + 'warder' (to guard, watch).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈwɔrd / (AmE), / rɪˈwɔːd / (BrE)
A payment or benefit given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement.

Examples

Each brass handle and hinge shone for his reward, and he knew how to get at the dust in the china flowers and how to take down the long glass drops which hung from the chandelier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - something given as a prize or recognition
  • Antonym: punishment - a penalty imposed for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old French 'reguard', from 'regarder' (to take notice of), ultimately from Latin 'regardare'.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈwɔrd / (AmE), / rɪˈwɔːd / (BrE)
A thing given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement, or as a return for good or bad actions.

Examples

So Prokofieff was able to cultivate his musical talents and harvest a rich reward from them.
To forestall any change of allegiance, the Democrats hastily organised a testimonial banquet for O' Banion, as public reward for his past services and as a reminder of where his loyalties lay.
In the end, he gets his man, but no one seems to care; virtue is its own and only reward.
One did one's best and if fortune smiled, there was a reward.
Turn them over to friends, as a reward for the friends he'’s had and to make sure that everything goes well after he'’s gone.

Synset Examples

the wages of sin is death
virtue is its own reward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punishment - a penalty for wrongdoing
  • Synonym: prize - something given as a reward for success or achievement

Etymology

From Old French *rewarder* (to regard, consider, reward), from re- (again) + warder (to look, guard).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈwɔrd / (AmE), / rɪˈwɔːd / (BrE)
to give someone something in recognition of their service, effort, or achievement

Examples

They were rewarded with splendid, exciting, singing.

Synset Examples

Today we honor our soldiers
The scout was rewarded for courageous action

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punish - to inflict a consequence for wrongdoing; opposite of reward

Etymology

From Old French *rewarder*, from re- (again) + *warder* (to watch, guard), originally 'to look at, regard, reward'.
