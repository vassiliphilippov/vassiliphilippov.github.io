whirl

1. VERB

/ wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
to move or cause to move rapidly around and around in a circle or similar curve, especially with a twisting or spinning motion

Examples

Instead, he whirled and ran to his house for a gun forcing them to kill him, Cook reported.
A tune began to whirl inside his head.
Jesus, imagine, the crummy bastards, they 'll get em, they 'll get what's comin to em * * h He whirled about suddenly.
It made no sense to him * * h He whirled around, suddenly hot all over, finding the man who had been standing before him a few moments back, nailing him to the spot on which he now stood, open-mouthed -
Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.

Synset Examples

The leaves swirled in the autumn wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twirl - to spin or cause to spin quickly and lightly around, especially repeatedly
  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly on a central point
  • Synonym: rotate - to move or cause to move in a circle around an axis or center

Etymology

Middle English *whirlen*, frequentative of *whir* (to fly, move rapidly).

2. VERB

/ wɝl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to move or flow in a circular motion; to form an eddy or vortex.

Examples

Nearly twenty-five years before, Captain John Davis had noted, as he sailed near the Arctic Circle, "a very great gulf, the water whirling and roaring, as it were the meeting of tides ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eddy - (of water, air, or smoke) to move in a circular way; a circular current
  • Synonym: swirl - to move in a twisting or spiraling pattern, especially of water or air

Etymology

From Old Norse 'hvirfla' (to turn about).
