silence

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
A complete absence of sound or noise.

Examples

What followed the outburst brought almost breathless silence as Miss Sutherland revealed her mastery of a voice probably unique among sopranos today.
The beehive voices, for no one could bear silence, drowned out the sound of Mrs. Lincoln's weeping.
John lifted his hand for silence.
She did this now, comfortably aware of the mist running down the windows, of the silence outside, of the dark afternoon it was getting to be.
Otherwise, the silence was complete.

Synset Examples

he needed silence in order to sleep
the street was quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiet - the state of having little or no noise
  • Antonym: noise - unwanted or disturbing sound

Etymology

From Old French 'silence', from Latin 'silentium' (state of being silent, a keeping quiet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
the act of deliberately not speaking, especially when speech is expected or required

Examples

There seems to be almost a conspiracy of silence veiling it.
Perhaps it would be better to speak to him, since silence could not exorcise his form.
We sat for a while drinking in silence.
There was a long silence.
They had suffered, in sulky silence, the sight of his sharp practice in Cicero.

Synset Examples

his silence about my contribution was surprising

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticence - the quality of being reserved or not revealing one's thoughts readily

Etymology

From Latin 'silentia' (quietness), from 'silere' (to be quiet).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
The practice or trait of deliberately keeping things secret or withholding information.

Examples

As the first collective confrontation of the Nazi outrage, the Trial of Eichmann represents a recovery of the Jews from the shock of the death camps, a recovery that took fifteen years and which is still by no means complete (though let no one believe that it could be hastened by silence).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or the state of being kept secret

Etymology

From Latin silentia (being silent, silence), from silēre (to be quiet).

4. VERB

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to stop speaking or making noise; to make quiet

Examples

The slugs flattened ten tires and silenced one of the inboard engines; the plane slowed to a halt.
To silence extremists, to warn the Axis, President Roosevelt issued this statement for the Allies in August:
Mike silenced them.
Next to the Blackwells, Titus had owned the island most, and she and Adelia had often stood in front of him, silenced by his terrible years - a scanty man with a thin beard and very deep-set blue eyes like a mariner, more aged than possible.

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiet - to make quiet; to calm or silence
  • Synonym: hush - to make or become silent; to quiet

Etymology

From Latin 'silentium' (a being silent), related to 'silere' (to be quiet, to be silent).

5. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
A state or situation in which there is no sound or no one is speaking.

Examples

Not a word.

Synset Examples

there was a shocked silence
he gestured for silence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noise - sound, especially when loud, unpleasant, or unwanted
  • Synonym: quiet - the quality of being calm and without much noise

Etymology

From Old French 'silence', from Latin 'silentium' (a being silent).
