merry

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɛri / (BrE)
Cheerful and lively; full of high-spirited joy or festivity.

Examples

"I have heard stories ", he writes, "of the most terrible, the most unnatural actions, of the most monstrous murders, told with the most spontaneous, childishly merry laughter ".
If goodness and badness lie in attitudes only and hence are brought into being by them, those men who greeted death and misery with childishly merry laughter are taking the only sensible line.
Rabbi Melzi smiled at him with infinitely old but merry eyes.
- Okay, Pop. Merry Christmas.

Synset Examples

when hearts were young and gay
a poet could not but be gay, in such a jocund company
the jolly crowd at the reunion
jolly old Saint Nick
a jovial old gentleman
have a merry Christmas
peals of merry laughter
a mirthful laugh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyful - feeling or expressing great happiness
  • Synonym: jovial - cheerful and friendly
  • Antonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Old English 'myrge' (pleasant, agreeable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɛri / (BrE)
Full of fun and lively enjoyment; offering cheerfulness and gaiety.

Examples

A friend of mine in New Mexico said the Court order had caused no particular trouble out there, that all had gone as merry as a marriage bell.

Synset Examples

a festive (or festal) occasion
gay and exciting night life
a merry evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: jolly - happy and lively

Etymology

From Old English *myrge* (pleasant, agreeable, sweet), related to Old High German *murg* (short, brisk).
