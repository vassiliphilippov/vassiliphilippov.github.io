abnormality

1. NOUN

/ æbˌnɔrmˈæləti / (AmE), / æbˌnɔːmˈælɪti / (BrE)
A physical condition or feature that differs from the typical structure or function of the body, especially as a result of genetic or developmental factors.

Examples

The authors insist, however, that these abnormalities in the sense of touch were due absolutely to no organic disorders in that sense faculty but rather to the injuries which the patient had sustained to the sense of sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malformation - a condition in which part of the body is misshapen or malformed
  • Synonym: defect - a fault or imperfection in a physical structure
  • Antonym: normality - the condition of being normal (not abnormal)

Etymology

From abnormal + -ity; abnormal from Latin ab- (away from) + normālis (according to rule).
