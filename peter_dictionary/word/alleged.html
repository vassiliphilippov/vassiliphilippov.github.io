alleged

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɛdʒd / (AmE), / əˈlɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a fact, event, or person) claimed to be true or real, but not yet proven; doubtful or suspect.

Examples

Paradise lost to the alleged water needs of Texas' big cities Thursday.

Synset Examples

these so-called experts are no help

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supposed - assumed to be true, without proof
  • Antonym: confirmed - proven to be true; verified

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *alegge* and Old French *alléguer* (to allege); from Latin *allegare* (to state as a reason or excuse).

2. ADJ

/ əˈlɛdʒd / (AmE), / əˈlɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of an incident, fact, or person) stated to have happened or to be the case, but without proof or yet to be proven.

Examples

Immediately, the religious groups of the city were embroiled in an angry dispute over the alleged invasion of a man's right to freedom of religious belief and conscience.

Synset Examples

alleged abuses of housing benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supposed - believed to be true based on what is claimed, but not verified
  • Antonym: proven - established as true by evidence

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *alegger*, variant of *alleguer*, from Latin *allegare* (to declare).
