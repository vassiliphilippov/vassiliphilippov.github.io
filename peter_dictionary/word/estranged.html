estranged

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈstreɪndʒd / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒd / (BrE)
No longer close or affectionate to someone; alienated in feeling or relationship.

Examples

Nor is it necessary to look for such evidence in the great urban centers of our culture that are admittedly almost entirely secularized and so profoundly estranged from the conventional forms in which the gospel has been communicated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alienated - emotionally separated or isolated
  • Antonym: reconciled - brought back into a friendly or close relationship

Etymology

From Old French *estranger* (to treat as a stranger), from Latin *extraneare* (to treat as a stranger), from *extraneus* (foreign, strange).
