fictive

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɪktɪv / (BrE)
Not real; invented or adopted, especially in order to deceive.

Examples

All this expands the oscillation between surface and depth so as to encompass fictive space in front of the surface as well as behind it.

Synset Examples

an assumed name
an assumed cheerfulness
a fictitious address
fictive sympathy
a pretended interest
a put-on childish voice
sham modesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fictitious - not real or true, invented for a purpose
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring in reality

Etymology

From Latin fictivus (feigned), from fingere (to shape, invent).
