usher

1. VERB

/ ˈʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə / (BrE)
to guide someone to their seat or destination, especially in a theater, auditorium, or similar setting.

Examples

When Joe identified himself, he nodded, unsmiling, and ushered him into a sedate living room.
We gently usher them to an island of tables and chairs strategically placed on the far side of the pool where they can amuse each other until we get ready to merge sides.

Synset Examples

The usher showed us to our seats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - to accompany someone somewhere, especially for protection or guidance
  • Synonym: show - to guide or lead someone to a place
  • Synonym: lead - to guide or direct a person by going in front

Etymology

From Old French 'ussier', from Latin 'ustiarius' (doorkeeper).
