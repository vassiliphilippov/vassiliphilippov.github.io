sorbed

1. ADJ

/ sɔːrbd / (AmE), / sɔːbd / (BrE)
(of a substance) taken up and retained by another substance

Examples

In the cleaning process, sorbed greasy soils become coated in this manner with an oriented film of surfactant.
Then during washing, the greasy soil rolls back at the edges so that emulsified droplets can disengage themselves from the sorbed oil mass, with the aid of mechanical action, and enter the aqueous phase.

Synset Examples

the sorbed oil mass
large volumes of occluded hydrogen in palladium

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adsorbed - (of a substance) taken up and held on the surface of another substance
  • Degree Higher: absorbed - (of a substance) taken in and assimilated into the structure of another substance

Etymology

Formed from 'sorb' (from Latin sorbere, 'to suck in, absorb') with the past participle ending '-ed'.
