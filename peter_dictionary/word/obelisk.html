obelisk

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.bə.lɪsk / (AmE), / ˈɒb.ɪ.lɪsk / (BrE)
A tall, four-sided, narrow, tapering monument with a pyramidal top, typically made of stone.

Examples

He had never heard so many bells, and as he lay there listening, he thought of her scolding him for his remarks when he had looked up at the obelisk and the church at the top of the Spanish Steps.

Etymology

From Greek 'obeliskos', diminutive of 'obelos' (spit, pointed pillar), via Latin 'obeliscus'.
