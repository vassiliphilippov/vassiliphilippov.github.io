collectively

1. ADV

/ kəˈlɛktɪvli / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvli / (BrE)
As a group; together; in combination with others.

Examples

International law had to fit the conditions of Europe, and nothing that could not fit this system, or the interests of the great European nations collectively, could possibly emerge as law in any meaningful sense.

Synset Examples

our salaries put together couldn't pay for the damage
we couldn't pay for the damages with all our salaries put together

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individually - Separately; one by one, not as a group

Etymology

Formed from 'collective' + '-ly'; 'collective' from Latin 'collectivus'.
