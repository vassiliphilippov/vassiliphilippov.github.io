triviality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌtrɪvɪˈæləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unimportant, petty, or insignificant.

Examples

It is the obverse of triviality, shallowness, emotional anaesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importance - the quality of being significant or valuable

Etymology

From Latin 'trivialis' (commonplace, ordinary), from 'trivium' (crossroads, a place where three roads meet).
