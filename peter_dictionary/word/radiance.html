radiance

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəns / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəns / (BrE)
The amount of electromagnetic radiation emitted, reflected, transmitted, or received by a surface, measured per unit area per unit solid angle.

Examples

The sharp ray was absorbed by a cloud, then reflected to the earth in a softer, diffused radiance.

Etymology

From Latin radiāns (shining), from radiāre (to emit rays), from radius (ray, spoke).
