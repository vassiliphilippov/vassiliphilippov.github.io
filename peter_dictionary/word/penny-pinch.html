penny-pinch

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛni pɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛni pɪntʃ / (BrE)
to spend money very frugally or try to save as much as possible, often to an extreme degree

Examples

Less than half the sum has been spent, since the Interama board pinched pennies during that period of painstaking negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrimp - to be thrifty or parsimonious, especially by limiting expenses
  • Synonym: economize - to reduce expenses; spend less
  • Antonym: spendthrift - someone who spends money recklessly

Etymology

From 'penny' (smallest unit of currency) + 'pinch' (to squeeze), originally literal, now figurative.
