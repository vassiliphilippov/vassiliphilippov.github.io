durability

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʊrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌdjʊərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The ability of something to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; the quality of lasting for a long time without deterioration.

Examples

Or the exception may be due to a particular durability peculiar to the Athabascan verb.

Synset Examples

they advertised the durability of their products

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fragility - quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From Latin durabilis (lasting), from durare (to last).
