mesmerize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛzməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛzməraɪz / (BrE)
To capture and hold the complete attention of someone; to fascinate or enthrall.

Examples

Gaylor's obsession and Cunningham's chimera-chasing reminiscences had mesmerized him into thinking of Lila and Lilac, separately or together, as a legend.

Synset Examples

She magnetized the audience with her tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascinate - To attract and hold the attention of someone intensely
  • Antonym: bore - To make someone feel uninterested or weary

Etymology

From the name of Franz Mesmer (1734–1815), an Austrian physician who theorized about animal magnetism.
