parade

1. NOUN

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
A continuous succession or sequence of people or things, often in an impressive or showy manner.

Examples

With Mickey Mantle and Yogi Berra both out of action due to injuries, the American League champs still mounted a 15 - hit attack against a parade of eight Cincinnati pitchers, the most ever used by one team in a series game.

Synset Examples

a parade of strollers on the mall
a parade of witnesses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - an organized group or line of people or vehicles moving forward in an orderly way
  • Synonym: series - a number of things or events that happen one after another

Etymology

From French 'parade', from Italian 'parata' (a show, display), from 'parare' (to prepare).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈɹeɪd / (AmE), / pəˈɹeɪd / (BrE)
A public procession, especially one that is ceremonial and involves people marching along a street, often to celebrate a special day or event.

Examples

Brief snips of actual events were shown: parades, dances, street scenes.
Now the yard looked wet and bald, the trees bare under their buds, but in a while Miss Ada's flowers would bloom like a marching parade.
I don't care if this festival is in debt, I want a parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - a number of people or vehicles moving forward in an orderly fashion, especially as part of a ceremony or festival

Etymology

From French *parade*, from Italian *parata* (a show, a display), from *parare* (to prepare).

3. VERB

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to walk or move about in a way that is meant to attract attention or show off

Examples

In a few minutes she was making the ten foot hike unaided; soon she was parading around the house, flaunting her new skill.

Synset Examples

She parades her new husband around town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaunt - to display ostentatiously; to show off

Etymology

From French *parade* (show, display), from Spanish *parada* (a stopping place), from parar (to stop).

4. VERB

/ pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to march or walk in a formal procession, often as part of a ceremony or for display

Examples

Russian tanks and artillery parading through the streets of Havana, Russian intrigue in the Congo, and Russian arms drops in Laos (using the same Ilyushin transports that were used to carry Communist agents to the Congo) made it plain once more that the cold war was all of a piece in space and time.

Synset Examples

the veterans paraded down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - to walk with regular steps in a group, usually in a formal or ceremonial way

Etymology

From French 'parade', from Spanish 'parada' (a stopping place, parade), from 'parar' (to stop)
