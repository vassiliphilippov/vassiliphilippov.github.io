cryptic

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪptɪk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptɪk / (BrE)
having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure; difficult to understand or interpret.

Examples

Similarly, when a reporter once questioned Lincoln in cryptic fashion, Lincoln refused to make any further statement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to understand, not ambiguous

Etymology

From Greek kryptikos, from kryptos (hidden).
