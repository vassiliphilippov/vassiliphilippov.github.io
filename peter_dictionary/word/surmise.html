surmise

1. VERB

/ sərˈmaɪz / (AmE), / səˈmaɪz / (BrE)
to suppose that something is true without having conclusive evidence; to infer from incomplete or limited information

Examples

As one would surmise, the procedure, however, could be repeated with the same object or with the same type of object often enough, so that the corresponding visual blots and the merest beginning of the tracing movement would provide clues as to the actual shape, which the patient then immediately could determine by a kind of inference.
Russell had reached the house as Cook surmised, dismounted, but just as the old trapper opened the door to receive him, he fell into the trapper's arms - dead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - to estimate or suppose without sufficient information
  • Synonym: infer - to deduce or conclude information from evidence and reasoning rather than from explicit statements

Etymology

From Old French *surmis*, past participle of *surmettre* (to accuse), from Latin *supermittere* (to send over)

2. NOUN

/ sərˈmaɪz / (AmE), / səˈmaɪz / (BrE)
an idea or opinion formed without sufficient evidence; a conjecture or guess

Examples

I do n't propose to go into their history, but I have one or two surmises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - an estimate or opinion made without sufficient information
  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion based on incomplete information

Etymology

From Middle French 'surmis', past participle of 'surmettre' (to suppose), from Latin 'supermittere'.

3. VERB

/ sərˈmaɪz / (AmE), / səˈmaɪz / (BrE)
to suppose that something is true without having evidence to confirm it; to guess or infer.

Examples

First of all, as we had surmised, the highly compulsive children in the structured setting score significantly better (* * f) on achievement than do similar children in the unstructured schools.

Synset Examples

I suspect he is a fugitive
I surmised that the butler did it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - estimate or suppose something without sufficient information
  • Synonym: suspect - have an idea or impression of the existence, presence, or truth of something without certain proof

Etymology

From Old French *surmis*, past participle of *surmettre* (to accuse, allege), from Latin *supermittere* (to send upon).
