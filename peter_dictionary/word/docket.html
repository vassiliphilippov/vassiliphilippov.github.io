docket

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkɪt / (BrE)
to enter (a case or matter) on a court's schedule for legal action or consideration.

Examples

The judge became ill just as the Colfax District Court convened, no substitute was brought in, no criminal cases heard, only 5 out of 122 cases docketed were tried, and court adjourned sine die after sitting a few days instead of the usual three weeks.

Synset Examples

Only 5 of the 120 cases docketed were tried

Etymology

From Middle English, possibly from Anglo-Norman French *doquet* (document, summary).
