narrowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛˌroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊnəs / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being narrow; having little width.

Examples

Someone said that while we were standing here and arguing about it, the British would be gone; but Cousin Simmons said he had watched them marching west early in the morning, and moving at a much brisker pace it had still taken half an hour for their column to pass, what with the narrowness of the road and their baggage and ammunition carts.

Synset Examples

the narrowness of the road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrow - having little width; not wide
  • Antonym: width - the distance from side to side
  • Antonym: breadth - the distance or measurement from side to side; width

Etymology

From 'narrow' + '-ness', a suffix forming nouns denoting a quality or state.
