straggle

1. VERB

/ ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡl / (BrE)
to wander or move away from a direct or straight course, often spreading out in a scattered or irregular way

Examples

Even before the benches had dried, the Civil War veterans were straggling back to their places.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidetrack - to divert from the main topic or course
  • Synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly
  • Antonym: stay - to remain on course

Etymology

From Middle English straglen, probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian stragla (to stroll, wander).

2. VERB

/ ˈstræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræɡəl / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or things) to move, spread, or be arranged in an irregular, scattered, or untidy way.

Examples

One high-up camera shot is magnificent, as the Germans straggle from a cathedral, dotting a huge, cobblestone square, and drop their weapons.

Synset Examples

Branches straggling out quite far

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cluster - to come together in a group or bunch

Etymology

From Middle English straglen, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dialect stragla (to stroll, roam).
