confiscate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.fɪ.skeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.skeɪt / (BrE)
to take or seize property, especially by legal authority, often as a penalty

Examples

They could be sent to U. S. ports for rulings whether cargo should be confiscated.
They fought hard, but they were forgiving to former foes, and sought to prevent vindictive legislatures from confiscating Tory property in violation of the Treaty of 1783.

Synset Examples

The FBI seized the drugs
The customs agents impounded the illegal shipment
The police confiscated the stolen artwork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
  • Synonym: impound - to seize and hold in the custody of the law
  • Antonym: release - to set free or allow something to return after being held

Etymology

From Latin confiscare, from con- (together) + fiscus (treasury, purse).
