atypical

1. ADJ

/ eɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / eɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
not representative of a group, class, or type; not typical or characteristic.

Examples

Thus the student of literature may sometimes find it helpful to classify a poem or an essay as being in idea or in ideal content or subject matter typical or atypical of its period.

Synset Examples

a group that is atypical of the target audience
a class of atypical mosses
atypical behavior is not the accepted type of response that we expect from children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typical - having the distinctive qualities of a particular type

Etymology

From prefix 'a-' meaning 'not' + 'typical'.
