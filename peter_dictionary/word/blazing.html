blazing

1. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
shining with great intensity; very bright or brilliant

Examples

Arnold Palmer has been a blazing figure in golf over the past twelve months.
For three days, their stolid oxen had plodded up a blazing valley as flat and featureless as a dead sea.

Synset Examples

the blazing sun
blinding headlights
dazzling snow
fulgent patterns of sunlight
the glaring sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzling - shining extremely brightly; extremely impressive
  • Antonym: dim - not bright; lacking in brightness

Etymology

From the verb 'blaze' (to burn brightly or fiercely), which is of Middle English origin, from Old English 'blæse'.
