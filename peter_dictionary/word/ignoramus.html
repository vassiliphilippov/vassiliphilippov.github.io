ignoramus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪɡnəˈreɪməs / (AmE), / ˌɪɡnəˈreɪməs / (BrE)
A person who lacks knowledge or awareness; an ignorant person.

Examples

An Alabamian wrote: "Col. Henry is [ an ignoramus ] fit for nothing higher than the cultivation of corn ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: know-it-all - a person who acts as if they know everything

Etymology

From Latin 'ignoramus' (we do not know), used as a legal term and later as a personal designation.
