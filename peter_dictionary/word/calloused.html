calloused

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæl.əst / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əst / (BrE)
(of skin or a body part) made tough and thick, usually by repeated work, friction, or pressure, so that it is covered with calluses.

Examples

Calloused fingers, caressed only by the smoothness of polished rosaries, had swayed excitedly beneath puckered chins where tiny black hairs sprouted, never to be tweezed away.
He stared at the dipper, turning it over and over in his wide, calloused hands.

Synset Examples

calloused skin
with a workman's callous hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others; also the related form of skin being thickened

Etymology

From the noun 'callus', from Latin 'callum' (hard skin).
