cavalryman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlriˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈkævəlriˌmæn / (BrE)
a soldier who serves in a cavalry unit, especially one operating armored vehicles or, formerly, on horseback

Examples

The Koreans fell back, but regrouped at the top of the hill and pinned down the cavalrymen with a screen of fire.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infantryman - a soldier in an infantry unit

Etymology

From 'cavalry' (soldiers on horseback, from Italian 'cavalleria'), plus 'man'.
