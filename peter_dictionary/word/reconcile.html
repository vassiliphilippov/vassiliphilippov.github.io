reconcile

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛkənˌsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈrek.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
To accept or be at peace with a situation or decision, especially after initial opposition or difficulty.

Examples

Charles had died two weeks before, in early November, without being reconciled to the Church.

Synset Examples

After some discussion we finally made up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accept - consent to receive or come to terms with something
  • Synonym: resign oneself - accept something unpleasant that one cannot change

Etymology

From Latin 'reconciliare' (to bring together again, make friendly).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
to make two or more ideas, facts, or situations compatible or consistent with each other

Examples

But to me Beckett's writing had seemed permeated with love for human beings and with a kind of humor that I could reconcile neither with despair nor with nihilism.
The Constitution of the Southern "Confederation "differed from that of the Federal Union only in two important respects: It openly, defiantly, recognized slavery - an institution which the Southerners of 1787, even though they continued it, found so impossible to reconcile with freedom that they carefully avoided mentioning the word in the Federal Constitution.

Synset Examples

The scientists had to accommodate the new results with the existing theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonize - to bring into agreement or concord
  • Antonym: conflict - to be incompatible or at odds

Etymology

From Latin reconciliare (to make good again, to bring together) via Old French reconciler.

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
to accept or come to terms with something unpleasant or inevitable

Examples

This gets down to the heart of our problem, for it reconciles us with God, whom we fear most of all because we have sinned against Him.

Synset Examples

He resigned himself to his fate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • Synonym: resign - to accept something undesirable as inevitable
  • Antonym: resist - oppose or refuse to accept

Etymology

From Latin conciliāre (to bring together) via Old French reconcilier.

4. VERB

/ ˈrɛkənˌsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.saɪl / (BrE)
to bring different ideas, facts, or situations into agreement or harmony; to make compatible

Examples

This picture of extreme self-reliant individuation is difficult to reconcile with such Zendo formulas as: "O you, demons and other spiritual beings, I now offer this to you, and may this food fill up the ten quarters of the world and all the demons and other spiritual beings be fed therewith.
And Zen Buddhism, though it is extremely difficult to understand how these internal contradictions are reconciled, helps them in their struggle to achieve personal salvation through sexual release.

Synset Examples

harmonize one's goals with one's abilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonize - to bring into harmony or agreement
  • Antonym: conflict - a state of disagreement or opposition

Etymology

From Old French reconcilier, from Latin reconciliāre (to bring together again, win over).
