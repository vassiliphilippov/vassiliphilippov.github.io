overshadow

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
to be more important or significant than someone or something else, often making them seem less important or noticeable

Examples

Then, if the middle number is activated to its greatest potential in terms of this square, through multiplying it by the highest number, 9 (which is the square of the base number), the result is 45; and the latter is the total sum of all the numbers in the square, by which all the other numbers are overshadowed and in which they may be said to be absorbed.

Synset Examples

the tragedy overshadowed the couple's happiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eclipse - to make something less important or noticeable by comparison
  • Synonym: outshine - to be much better or more impressive than someone or something else

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (above) + 'shadow', meaning to cast a shadow over; figurative use dates from the early 17th century.
