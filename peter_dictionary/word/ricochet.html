ricochet

1. VERB

/ ˈrɪkəˌʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪkəʃeɪ / (BrE)
(of a bullet, shell, or other projectile) to bounce off a surface one or more times after hitting it; to rebound after impact.

Examples

A second bullet ricocheted off Max's belt buckle, leaving him unhurt but in some distress.

Synset Examples

The rubber ball bounced
These particles do not resile but they unite after they collide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounce - to rebound after hitting a surface; a more general term for moving back after impact

Etymology

From French *ricochet* (the skipping or rebounding of a projectile).
