ruddy

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌdi / (AmE), / ˈrʌdi / (BrE)
(of a person's face or complexion) having a healthy red color, often associated with outdoor life or robust health.

Examples

His face was ruddy and heavy and unlined, and when he laughed he showed his teeth, which were big and white and strong and unquestionably home-grown.

Synset Examples

a ruddy complexion
Santa's rubicund cheeks
a fresh and sanguine complexion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosy - having a pink or red color, especially in the cheeks
  • Antonym: pale - having little or no color in the face

Etymology

Old English *rudig*, from *rud* (redness).
