puff

1. VERB

/ pʌf / (AmE), / pʌf / (BrE)
To inhale or draw in air, smoke, or vapor in short breaths, especially while smoking.

Examples

Rachel wore a smart hat and, because she had been warned recently about smoking, puffed at her cigarettes through a long ivory holder stained with lipstick.

Synset Examples

draw a deep breath
draw on a cigarette

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhale - to breathe in air, smoke, or vapor
  • Antonym: exhale - to breathe out air, smoke, or vapor

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound of a sudden breath or a short burst of air.

2. VERB

/ pʌf / (AmE), / pʌf / (BrE)
to inhale and exhale smoke strongly while smoking, especially a cigarette, cigar, or pipe

Examples

He was puffing on a cigar, and he was turning up his coat collar against the rain.
Not long ago an acquaintance, a slick-headed water rat of a lad up from the maw of the city, stood on the balcony puffing his first cigarette in weeks.

Synset Examples

puff a cigar
whiff a pipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke - to draw into the mouth and exhale the smoke of tobacco

Etymology

Possibly imitative of the sound of a sudden expulsion of breath.

3. VERB

/ pʌf / (AmE), / pʌf / (BrE)
to breathe quickly and noisily, especially after physical exertion or when out of breath

Examples

By then one begins to notice the middle-age spread; the gray hairs, the eyeglasses, bodies that are too thin or too heavy; the fading signs of old beauty; the athlete of by-gone years who wears a size 46 suit and puffs when he has finished a sentence of any length; then, it is time to break it up and move on.

Synset Examples

The runners reached the finish line, panting heavily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pant - to breathe quickly and with difficulty, often after exertion
  • Synonym: gasp - to catch one's breath with an open mouth, as in surprise or after exertion
  • Synonym: wheeze - to breathe with a whistling or rattling sound in the chest
