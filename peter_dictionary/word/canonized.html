canonized

1. ADJ

/ ˈkænəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkanəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
Regarded as sacred, exemplary, or officially recognized as having special status, especially in a religious context.

Examples

Barstow concluded that as Rhode Island's John Brown became a canonized hero, if not a saint, so would it be with John Brown of Harper's Ferry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beatified - officially declared blessed, especially in Christian tradition

Etymology

From Medieval Latin canonizare, meaning 'to canonize;' from canon, rule or standard.
