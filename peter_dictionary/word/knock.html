knock

1. VERB

/ nɑk / (AmE), / nɒk / (BrE)
to hit or collide with something forcefully, often causing an impact or noise

Examples

He broke that boy (Air Force fullback Nick Arshinkoff) in two and knocked him loose from the football ".
Hoopla (Tar Heel-Holiday Hanover), a filly that wanted to trot, knocked herself October 31 and November 1 fighting the hopples.

Synset Examples

My car bumped into the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bump - to collide with something, often accidently
  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly
  • Synonym: collide - to come into forceful contact

Etymology

Old English *cnocian* (to knock, rap); of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ nɑk / (AmE), / nɒk / (BrE)
An act of negative criticism or disparaging comment.

Examples

And that's meant as a boost, not a knock.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compliment - an expression of praise or admiration

3. VERB

/ nɑk / (AmE), / nɒk / (BrE)
to deliver a sharp blow or push to someone or something, often causing movement or damage

Examples

I heard of some that tried it back in the States, and he 'd knock them clear across the room.
Another Yankee became so disgusted as to state: "I wish to God one half of our officers were knocked in the head by slinging them against [ the other half ] ".
An inch lower and it would have knocked him out.
The younger boy said the blast knocked him out of bed and against the wall.
Powers knocked his arm aside.

Synset Examples

He knocked the glass clear across the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike something with force
  • Degree Lower: tap - to touch lightly, especially with a slight sound
  • Degree Higher: bash - to strike with great force

Etymology

From Old English *cnocian*, imitative of the sound.

to strike a surface, especially a door, with a sharp blow using the knuckles in order to attract attention

Before he left the apartment he knocked on their door and asked if there was anything he could do for them.
The eternal Christ may knock at your soul's door, calling you to give up sin and prepare for heaven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rap - to strike a surface quickly, especially for making a noise
  • Synonym: tap - to hit lightly, producing a small noise, especially with the fingers or knuckles

5. NOUN

/ nɑk / (AmE), / nɒk / (BrE)
A sharp, abrupt sound made by striking something hard, especially on a door to attract attention.

Examples

Time elapsed but the doctor was obviously unconscious of its passage until an unwelcome knock on the door interrupted the processes of nature.
The doctor sat down rather wearily, caressing the hen and remarking that the city was not the place for a poultry loving man, but no sooner was the remark out than a knock at this door obliged him to cover the hen with his greatcoat once more.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rap - a quick, sharp knock or tap

Etymology

From Middle English knokken, from Old English cnocian (to knock).
