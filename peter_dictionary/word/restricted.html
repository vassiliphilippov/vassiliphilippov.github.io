restricted

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
Limited in extent, amount, or scope; subject to specific rules or conditions that limit its use or availability.

Examples

A restricted frontage may be too crowded an area for public use.
In some (the Ewe type mentioned above) interaction of tone and intonation is restricted to the ends of intonation spans.

Synset Examples

of restricted importance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrestricted - not limited or controlled in any way

Etymology

From the verb 'restrict,' from Latin 'restrictus,' past participle of 'restringere' (to bind back, restrain).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
Limited in scope, extent, or meaning; not broad or general.

Examples

The description of the Siddo syntax was very restricted.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broad - having a wide range or scope

Etymology

From Latin 'restringere' (to bind fast, confine), past participle 'restrictus'.
