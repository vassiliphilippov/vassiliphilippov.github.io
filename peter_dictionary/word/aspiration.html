aspiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A strong desire to achieve something or to succeed; ambition or a motivating goal.

Examples

To achieve this goal of balanced development, communications between the central government and the local communities must be such that the needs and aspirations of the people themselves are effectively taken into account.
By her eighteenth birthday her bent for writing was so evident that Papa and Mamma gave her a Life of Dickens as a spur to her aspiration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambition - a strong desire for success or achievement; drive
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern; absence of aspiration

Etymology

From Latin 'aspiratio' (a breathing upon, eager desire), from 'aspirare', meaning 'to breathe upon; to aspire'.
