dishearten

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈhɑːrtən / (AmE), / dɪsˈhɑːtən / (BrE)
Cause someone to lose confidence, hope, or enthusiasm.

Examples

This is a very common experience and should in no way discourage or dishearten either husband or wife.
In other municipalities the difficulties in overcoming the financial burden have been sufficiently great to dishearten proponents of fiscal year changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Synonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm

Etymology

From prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'hearten' (to encourage), hence 'to take away heart'.
