expletive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.splə.tɪv, ɪkˈspli.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛk.splɪ.tɪv, ɪkˈspliː.tɪv / (BrE)
a word or phrase, often profane or obscene, used to express strong emotion such as anger or surprise

Examples

What the Christ is this happy horse shit?

Synset Examples

expletives were deleted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - an offensive word or phrase expressing anger or annoyance
  • Synonym: swear word - an offensive word used especially to express anger or frustration

Etymology

From Latin expletivus (serving to fill out), from explere (to fill out).
