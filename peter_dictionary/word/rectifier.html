rectifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an electrical device that converts alternating current (AC) into direct current (DC)

Examples

The power source was a commercial D.C. rectifier.
Because of the falling characteristic of the rectifier, no ballast resistor was required for stability of operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inverter - device that converts direct current into alternating current

Etymology

From rectify + -er, meaning 'one who or that which rectifies'. 'Rectify' comes from Latin 'rectificare' (to make right).
