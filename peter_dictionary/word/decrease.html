decrease

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkris / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːs / (BrE)
to become or make something smaller in amount, size, number, or degree

Examples

The volume of ADC cases will decrease, Martin reported, when the community is able to deal effectively with two problems: Relatively limited skills and discrimination in employment because of color.

Synset Examples

The amount of homework decreased towards the end of the semester

Related Terms

  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in amount, number, or degree

Etymology

From Old French *decreistre*, from Latin *decrescere* (to grow less, diminish), from *de-* (down) + *crescere* (to grow).

Etymology
