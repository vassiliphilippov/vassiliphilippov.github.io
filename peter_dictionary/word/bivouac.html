bivouac

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪvuˌæk / (AmE), / ˈbɪvuːæk / (BrE)
A temporary camp or shelter, especially one used by soldiers or mountaineers, typically without tents or permanent structures.

Examples

A weapons carrier took Greg, Todman, Belton, Banjo Ferguson, and Walters and the others the two miles from the bivouac area to the strip.

Synset Examples

wherever he went in the camp the men were grumbling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encampment - a place with temporary accommodations, especially for soldiers

Etymology

From French 'bivouac', probably from Swiss German 'biwacht' (night watch).
