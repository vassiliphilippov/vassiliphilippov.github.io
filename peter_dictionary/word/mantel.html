mantel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tl̩ / (BrE)
A shelf that projects from the wall above a fireplace, often used for decorative objects.

Examples

The clock on the mantel piece was scandalized and ticked so loudly that he glanced at it over his shoulder and then quickly left the room.
In the same way he pantomimed grasping a mantel and bouncing cleanly off that, pressing his hands against the floor and bouncing cleanly off that.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a mantel a chimneypiece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimneypiece - ornamental structure around a fireplace

Etymology

From Old French mantel (cloak, mantel), from Latin mantellum (cloak).
