legislate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪt / (BrE)
to make or enact laws, or to bring something into effect by means of laws or legislation

Examples

Here in 1815 the great nations assembled to legislate not merely for Europe, but for the world.

Synset Examples

They passed the amendment
We cannot legislate how people spend their free time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enact - to make into law; to pass legislation

Etymology

From Latin 'legis' (law) + 'latus', past participle of 'ferre' (to carry), via Latin 'legislat-' (proposed a law).
