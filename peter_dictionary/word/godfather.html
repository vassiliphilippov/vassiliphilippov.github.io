godfather

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
A man who sponsors a child at baptism, often assuming responsibility for the child's religious education and welfare.

Examples

And you stood godfather to our baby.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: godmother - A woman who sponsors a child at baptism.

Etymology

From Old English 'godfæder', meaning 'spiritual father', combining 'god' and 'father'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
A man who sponsors a child at baptism and promises to oversee their religious education and upbringing; by extension, a male sponsor, mentor, or influential supporter.

Examples

Godfather, I have a daughter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: godmother - female sponsor at baptism

Etymology

From Old English 'godfæder,' combining 'god' (in the sense of spiritual) and 'father'.
