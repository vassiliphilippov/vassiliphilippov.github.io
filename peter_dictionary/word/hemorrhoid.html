hemorrhoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.əˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.ərɔɪd / (BrE)
a swollen and inflamed vein in the rectum or anus, often causing pain, itching, or bleeding

Examples

The terminal hospital admission on June 27, 1959, was necessitated by continued weakness and debility complicated by urinary retention and painful thrombosed hemorrhoids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piles - another term for hemorrhoids

Etymology

From Greek *haimorrhoides* (veins liable to discharge blood), from *haima* (blood) + *rhein* (to flow).
