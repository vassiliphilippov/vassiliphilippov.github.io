oscillator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.sɪˌleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒs.ɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
an electrical device or generator that produces oscillations, such as sound waves or alternating currents, typically by repeatedly converting energy from one form to another

Examples

This original capsule contained a battery and a transistor oscillator and was about 1 cm. in diameter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generator - a machine that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy
  • Synonym: resonator - a device or system that naturally oscillates at certain frequencies

Etymology

From Latin 'oscillare' (to swing), referring to repetitive motion.
