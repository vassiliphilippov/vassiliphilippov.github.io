democratize

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become more democratic, especially with regard to political systems or organizations

Examples

American policy should press constantly the view that until these governments demand efficiency and effectiveness of their bureaucracies there is not the slightest hope that they will either modernize of democratize their societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalize - make laws or systems more open or less strict
  • Antonym: authoritarianize - make or become more authoritarian

Etymology

From democracy + -ize, patterned after French démocratiser.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to introduce or bring about democratic reforms in an organization or country; to make (a system, process, or institution) more democratic

Examples

In municipal systems we tend to view what is called positivism as fundamentally a movement to democratize policy by increasing the power of parliament - the elected representatives - at the expense of the more conservative judiciary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalize - make (something) more liberal or open
  • Antonym: authoritarianize - make (a system) more authoritarian

Etymology

From French 'démocratiser', from 'démocratie' (democracy) + '-iser' (-ize).
