dreadful

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrɛd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəl / (BrE)
Causing fear, horror, or terror; extremely unpleasant or shocking.

Examples

Somewhere, beyond, where shadows lurked, must be the yawning pit of which Papa preached and the dreadful Lake of Fire.
The horse shied at the dreadful thing and flared its nostrils.
His eyes had the same dreadful rigid stare as Dr. Grimesby Roylott's when he was found before his open safe wearing the speckled band.

Synset Examples

the awful war
an awful risk
dire news
a career or vengeance so direful that London was shocked
the dread presence of the headmaster
polio is no longer the dreaded disease it once was
a dreadful storm
a fearful howling
horrendous explosions shook the city
a terrible curse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - causing fear, horror, or distress
  • Synonym: horrible - causing horror or disgust
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

Formed from 'dread' + '-ful', meaning 'full of dread'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdrɛd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fəl / (BrE)
Extremely bad, unpleasant, or causing strong dislike or discomfort.

Examples

Counsel for the defense, however, shrewdly allowing himself to be swept by the current of dreadful recollections, rarely raised an objection.

Synset Examples

atrocious taste
abominable workmanship
an awful voice
dreadful manners
a painful performance
terrible handwriting
an unspeakable odor came sweeping into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - shockingly bad or lacking in quality
  • Synonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad or serious
  • Synonym: horrible - very unpleasant or disagreeable
  • Antonym: delightful - giving great pleasure; very pleasing

Etymology

From dread + -ful, formed in Middle English.
