slovenly

1. ADJ

/ ˈslʌvənli / (AmE), / ˈslʌvənli / (BrE)
(of a person or appearance) habitually untidy, dirty, or careless in dress, habits, or work.

Examples

"But in such a slovenly condition ".
"What is slovenly about me "?
The same month that Alastor was published, Murray sold twenty thousand copies of The Siege of Corinth, a slovenly bit of Byronism that even Shelley's generosity rebelled at.

Synset Examples

filled the door with her frowzy bulk
frowzy white hair
slovenly appearance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neat - tidy, clean, and orderly in appearance or habits
  • Synonym: unkempt - not combed; poorly groomed or untidy
  • Antonym: tidy - keeping things clean and orderly

Etymology

From Middle English *slovenly*, from 'sloven' (a dirty or careless person) + '-ly'.
