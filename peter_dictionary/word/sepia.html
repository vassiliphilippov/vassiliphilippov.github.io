sepia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˈsiː.pi.ə / (BrE)
A reddish-brown color, often resembling the ink once used by artists, and commonly associated with old photographs.

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reddish-brown - a color that is brown with a tinge of red

Etymology

From Italian 'seppia' (cuttlefish), from Latin 'sepia', from Greek 'sēpía', named for the ink of the cuttlefish.
