swim

1. VERB

/ swɪm / (AmE), / swɪm / (BrE)
to move through water by moving your body, especially using your arms and legs

Examples

Henri has always had shapely legs from swimming and water skiing and really does n't have to work them very much.
But where do people go swimming in Paris?
But if he has time to go swimming, he had time to be with us ".
"Warm enough to swim - real Indian summer.
I swam like mad, got out of the pool, grabbed my robe, and ran to the car.

Synset Examples

We had to swim for 20 minutes to reach the shore
a big fish was swimming in the tank

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: float - to rest on the surface of a liquid without sinking

Etymology

Old English *swimman* (to move in water), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ swɪm / (AmE), / swɪm / (BrE)
to remain afloat or suspended on or below the surface of a liquid without sinking

Examples

To swim is true, and to sink is true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid without sinking
  • Antonym: sink - to go down below the surface of a liquid; to not stay afloat

Etymology

Old English 'swimman' (to move in water, float), of Germanic origin.
