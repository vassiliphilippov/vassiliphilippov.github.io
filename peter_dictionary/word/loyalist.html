loyalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who remains loyal to a government or ruler, especially in times of revolt

Examples

These were the ships of His Majesty's Navy, herding the hulks of the East Indies merchants and the yachts and ketches of the loyalists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rebel - person who opposes authority or government, especially during a revolt

Etymology

From 'loyal' + '-ist', from Old French 'loial', from Latin 'legalis' (legal).
