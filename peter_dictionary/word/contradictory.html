contradictory

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈdɪk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈdɪk.təri / (BrE)
(Of words or propositions) mutually exclusive so that both cannot be true and both cannot be false at the same time.

Examples

There have been contradictory reports from 1958 Delta 2, and the data quoted here are believed to be the more reliable.

Synset Examples

`perfect' and `imperfect' are contradictory terms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - Opposite in meaning but not necessarily mutually exclusive
  • Antonym: compatible - Able to exist or occur together without conflict

Etymology

From Latin *contradictorius* (contradictory), from *contra-* (against) + *dictorius* (pertaining to speaking).
