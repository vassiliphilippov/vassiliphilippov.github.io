rabble

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.əl / (AmE), / ˈræb.əl / (BrE)
A large, disorderly crowd of people; a mob, especially regarded as noisy or unruly.

Examples

On the outskirts of the rabble the Camaret brothers and Gaspard Favre shook their fists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - a large crowd of people, especially one that is disorderly or unruly
  • Synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people or animals

Etymology

From Middle English 'rabel', probably from Old French 'rabel' (pack, crowd).
