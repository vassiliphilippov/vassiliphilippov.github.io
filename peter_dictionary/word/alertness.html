alertness

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɝːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈlɜːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being quick to notice and respond to potential danger or problems; the state of being attentive and prepared

Examples

He snapped to alertness at Andy's entrance.
In the written language then can be underlined or italicized to guide the reader here, but much of the time the written language simply depends on the reader's alertness, and a careless reader will have to back up and reread.

Synset Examples

wakefulness, watchfulness, and bellicosity make a good hunter
vigilance is especially susceptible to fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: inattention - lack of attention; distraction or negligence

Etymology

From 'alert' + '-ness'; 'alert' from Italian 'all'erta' (on the lookout).
