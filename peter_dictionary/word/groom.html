groom

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
a man on his wedding day or just before or after the event

Examples

At five o'clock that night it was already dark, and behind my closed door I was dressing as carefully as a groom.
The bride, the groom and the angel... -

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bride - a woman on her wedding day or just before or after the event

Etymology

From Middle English *grome* (boy, youth, man servant).

2. VERB

/ ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
to prepare or train someone for a particular role, responsibility, or position, especially over a period of time

Examples

Some of my fellow workers were grooming me for an office in the Socialist Party.

Synset Examples

He is grooming his son to become his successor
The prince was prepared to become King one day
They trained him to be a warrior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - to teach the skills for a particular job or activity
  • Synonym: prepare - to make someone ready for a particular purpose

Etymology

Originally a noun for 'servant' or 'boy', from Middle English 'grome'; verb sense influenced by the idea of caring for or preparing someone.

3. VERB

/ ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
to clean and brush (an animal, especially a horse or dog) and make its appearance neat and tidy

Examples

The horses were groomed to a high gloss; departing, they stepped solemnly with knees lifted to the jaw, for they had been trained to drag at important funerals.

Synset Examples

groom the dogs
dress the horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - to use a brush to clean or smooth hair or fur

Etymology

From Middle English *grome* (boy, servant), of unknown origin.
