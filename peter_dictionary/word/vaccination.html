vaccination

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæksəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvæksɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of giving someone a vaccine to protect them against a particular disease.

Examples

For prevention of these diseases during periods of stress such as shipping, excessive handling, vaccination, extreme weather conditions: 350 milligrams per head per day for 30 days only.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunization - the process of becoming protected against a disease, often by vaccination

Etymology

From Latin 'vacca' (cow) via Latin 'vaccinatio', originally relating to cowpox used in early vaccines.
