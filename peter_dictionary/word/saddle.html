saddle

1. VERB

/ ˈsæd.əl / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl / (BrE)
to give someone a difficult responsibility, duty, or burden, usually something unwelcome or difficult to deal with

Examples

One of those capital-gains ventures, in fact, has saddled him with Gore Court.

Synset Examples

He charged her with cleaning up all the files over the weekend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load with a heavy responsibility or duty
  • Antonym: relieve - to free someone from a responsibility or burden

Etymology

Originally from Old English *sadol* (seat or saddle), figurative sense of 'burdening' from the image of loading a horse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.əl / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl / (BrE)
A seat fastened on the back of a horse, camel, or similar animal for a rider to sit on.

Examples

I have observed that being up on a horse changes the whole character of a man, and when a very small man is up on a saddle, he 'd like as not prefer to eat his meals there.
The redcoat officer collapsed like a punctured bolster, and the horse reared and threw him from the saddle, except that one booted foot caught in the stirrup.
One moment there was a man in the saddle; the next a headless horror on a horse that bolted through the redcoat ranks, and during the next second or two, we all of us fired into the suddenly disorganized column of soldiers.
Stranger, Uncle Randolph began riding home nights with a jug strapped to his saddle, drunkenly singing "Old Dan Tucker "at the top of his voice.
The wraith-like events of the last few days flooded David's mind and he rubbed his unshaved chin and felt again the ache in his kidneys caused by his saddle odyssey from Boston.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bareback - riding without a saddle

Etymology

Old English *sadol*, from Proto-Germanic *sadalaz* (saddle).

3. VERB

/ ˈsædɫ̩ / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl / (BrE)
to burden someone with a difficult or unwelcome responsibility, task, or situation

Examples

Otherwise, you may be saddled with a good size milk bill by milk drinkers.

Synset Examples

he saddled me with that heavy responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load someone with a heavy responsibility or duty
  • Synonym: encumber - to weigh someone down with a difficult duty or responsibility

Etymology

From Old English 'sadol', referring first to a seat for a rider, later used figuratively to mean 'to burden'.

4. VERB

/ ˈsæd.əl / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl / (BrE)
To put a saddle on a horse or other animal.

Examples

I 'll saddle the horses and bring them round.

Synset Examples

saddle the horses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaddle - to remove the saddle from (a horse, etc.)

Etymology

From Old English *sadol*, related to German *Sattel* and Dutch *zadel*.
