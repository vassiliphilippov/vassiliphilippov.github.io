foolishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfuːlɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unwise, lacking good sense, or acting without careful thought

Examples

Can the church risk assuming that the "folly "of men is as dear to God as their "wisdom ", or, as is also commonly implied, that "the foolishness of God "and "the foolishness of men "are simply two ways of talking about the same thing?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wisdom - soundness of judgement; the opposite quality to foolishness
  • Synonym: folly - lack of good sense; a foolish act

Etymology

From 'foolish' + '-ness', with 'fool' from Old French *fol* and Latin *follis* (bellows, windbag, fool).
