leisure

1. NOUN

/ ˈliʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛʒə / (BrE)
Time when one is not working and can relax or enjoy enjoyable activities; freedom to choose how to spend one's free time.

Examples

The South's antipathy to Northern civilization includes such charges as poor manners, harsh accents, lack of appreciation of the arts of living like gastronomy and the use of leisure.
Perhaps it is only an analogy, but one of the most obvious differences between cheap fiction and fiction of an enduring quality is the development of a theme or story with leisure and anticipation.

Synset Examples

he lacked the leisure for golf

Related Terms

  • Antonym: work - activity involving effort for pay or duty
  • Synonym: recreation - activity done for enjoyment in one's free time
  • Synonym: free time - time when not working or occupied

Etymology

From Old French *leisir* (capacity, ability, liberty), from Latin *licēre* (to be allowed).
