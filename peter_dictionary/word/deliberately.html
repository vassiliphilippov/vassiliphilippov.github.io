deliberately

1. ADV

/ dəˈlɪbɚətli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərətli / (BrE)
In a way that is done on purpose; intentionally.

Examples

Even in areas where political connotations are (deliberately?)
Many potters clung to the past the more determinedly as they were confronted with radically new ideas; the poet of the Iliad deliberately archaized.
In the end, Picasso and Braque plumped for the representational, and it would seem they did so deliberately.
He thought about it briefly, then deliberately turned the talk to something else.
Neither was Henrietta hoydenish like Jo, who frankly wished she were a boy and had deliberately shortened her name, which, like Henrietta's, was the feminine form of a boy's name.

Synset Examples

he used that word intentionally
I did this by choice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentionally - in a way that is planned or meant; on purpose
  • Antonym: accidentally - in a way that is not planned or intended

Etymology

From Latin deliberatus (considered carefully), past participle of deliberare.

2. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪbɚətli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərətli / (BrE)
In a slow, careful, and unhurried manner; with thought and intention, not rushed.

Examples

She began it deliberately, so that none of her words would be lost on him.
Halting, Pat turned to survey him deliberately.

Synset Examples

she was working deliberately

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hurriedly - in a quick or rushed manner

Etymology

From Latin deliberatus (considered carefully), past participle of deliberare (to ponder).
