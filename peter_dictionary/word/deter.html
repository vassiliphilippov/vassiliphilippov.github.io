deter

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtɝː / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː / (BrE)
to make someone decide not to do something, especially by making them realize it will have bad results

Examples

The danger lay not in believing that our own A-bombs would deter Russia's use of hers; that theory was and is sound.

Synset Examples

We should discourage this practice among our youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or eager to do something
  • Antonym: encourage - to give someone the confidence or desire to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'deterrere', from 'de-' (away) + 'terrere' (to frighten).
