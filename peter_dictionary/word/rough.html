rough

1. ADJ

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
(of a period, experience, or situation) difficult, full of hardship or trouble.

Examples

One of the roughest was the TV quiz shows, which gave him inferiority complexes.
Things may get rough with the move.

Synset Examples

the rocky road to success
they were having a rough time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard - difficult or challenging; not easy
  • Synonym: tough - difficult or demanding; requiring great effort
  • Antonym: smooth - free from difficulties or obstacles

2. ADJ

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
Having an uneven or irregular surface; not smooth or level.

Examples

One day when he attended a war memorial ceremony in Westminster Abbey his view was obstructed by a stout man on his left, his attention turned to the irregular pattern of the rough slab flooring and someone, clasping him by the arm, whispered, "I want a word with you, please ".
The particles appeared rough and undoubtedly the single crystal domains are smaller than this.
A rough attempt was made to characterize the vertical profile of the cloud by taking samples from outside the windows on the first, ninth, and fifteenth floors of a Government office building.
(1) When an object was placed in the patient's hand, he had no difficulty determining whether it was warm or cold, sharp or blunt, rough or smooth, flexible, soft, or hard; and he could tell, simply by the feel of it, whether it was made of wood, iron, cloth, rubber, and so on.
Even so, he could not ease the tension of his body; the rough surface of the earth itself seemed to resist every attempt on his part to relax.

Synset Examples

trees with rough bark
rough ground
rough skin
rough blankets
his unsmooth face

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough

Etymology

From Old English rūh (rough, hairy), related to Dutch ruw, German rauh.

3. ADJ

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
Not exact or precise; given as an estimate or approximation rather than as a definite value.

Examples

There seems to be a rough correlation between the initial and ultimate lengths, starting with the smallest (boa constrictor) and ending with the largest (anaconda).
Yet he never allowed these models to become fixed in his mind; they remained rough starting points.

Synset Examples

the approximate time was 10 o'clock
a rough guess
a ballpark estimate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approximate - close to the actual, but not completely accurate or exact
  • Antonym: exact - completely accurate or correct

4. ADJ

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
(of weather or sea) violently agitated, turbulent, or stormy.

Examples

Once, after the Discovery lay for a week in rough weather, Hudson ordered the anchor raised before the sea had calmed.

Synset Examples

boisterous winds and waves
the fierce thunders roar me their music
rough weather
rough seas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - (of weather or sea) free from agitation or disturbance

Etymology

From Old English 'ruh' (rough, shaggy, hairy), related to German 'rauh' and Dutch 'ruw'.

Examples

If that goombah tries any rough stuff, tell him I'm no bandleader.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gentle - kind or mild in temperament or behavior

Etymology

From Old English 'ruh' (rough, shaggy), related to Dutch 'ruw' and German 'rau'.

6. ADJ

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) unpleasantly harsh or grating.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - (of a voice or sound) pleasant and free from harshness or grating

7. ADJ

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) lacking gentleness, refinement, or sophistication; coarse or unpolished.

Examples

One handled the salesman in a friendly manner, another in a rough manner, and the third in a hesitating manner.
Oil-field workers were a rough tough lot.
I can be theatrical... ... and maybe even a little rough.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coarse - lacking refinement, sophistication, or delicacy (of people or manners)
  • Synonym: unrefined - lacking refinement or sophistication (of people or manners)
  • Antonym: polished - showing refinement and elegance (of people or manners)

Etymology

From Old English *ruh* (rough, unruly, shaggy).
