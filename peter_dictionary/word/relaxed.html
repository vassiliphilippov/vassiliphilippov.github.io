relaxed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈlækst / (AmE), / rɪˈlakst / (BrE)
free from tension or anxiety; calm and at ease

Examples

Television has yet to work out a living arrangement with jazz, which comes to the medium more as an uneasy guest than as a relaxed member of the family.
His hands, which had been as quick as a pair of fluttering birds, were now neither active nor really relaxed.
I want him completely relaxed and confident in our friendship.

Synset Examples

gave the impression of being quite relaxed
a relaxed and informal discussion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tense - feeling nervous or strained
  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or anxiety

Etymology

From the past participle of 'relax', from Latin 'relaxare' (to loosen, open, stretch out).
