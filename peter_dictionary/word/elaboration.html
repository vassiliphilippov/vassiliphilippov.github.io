elaboration

1. NOUN

/ əˌlæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlæbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the addition of extra material, detail, or information to clarify or expand on something

Examples

In analyzing the watercolors of Roy Mason, the first thing that comes to mind is their essential decorativeness, yet this word has such a varied connotation that it needs some elaboration here.
I could evoke no further elaboration from her about this; but a few seconds later she was standing directly across the room from me, looking me in the eyes and saying in a scathingly condemnatory tone, "Your father despises you "!

Synset Examples

a few remarks added in amplification and defense
an elaboration of the sketch followed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplification - the act of adding details or expanding on a statement
  • Antonym: simplification - the act of making something simpler by removing detail

Etymology

From Latin elaboratio (a working out), from elaborare (to work out)
