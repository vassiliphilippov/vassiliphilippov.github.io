pretend

1. VERB

/ prəˈtɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to behave as if something is true when it is not, especially in order to deceive or for the purposes of play or imagination

Examples

At the same time the multiple transvestitism involved - the fat man as girl and as baby, as coquette pretending to be a baby - touches for a moment horrifyingly upon the secret sources of a life like Jacoby's, upon the sinister dreams which form the sources of any human life.
No, she would not pretend modesty, but neither must she be crudely bold.
One might pretend never to have seen one before, or, to more purpose, that there would never be another like it.
They were n't sleeping, of course, but they thought they were doing him a favor by pretending.
He read Henry James and used to pretend profundity through eye-beamings at people.

Synset Examples

He feigned that he was ill
He shammed a headache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feign - to pretend to be affected by a feeling, state, or injury
  • Synonym: fake - to falsely present something as real or true
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic, real; not pretended or false

Etymology

From Old French 'pretendre' (to assert, allege), from Latin 'praetendere' (to stretch forth, put forward).

2. VERB

/ prɪˈtɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to behave as if something is true or real when it is not; to put on a false appearance or act affectedly

Examples

Ludie inquired every evening, pretending that he did not care.
The furor was such that people who could not possibly have squirmed their way into the rehearsals were pretending that they were intimate with the whole affair and that it would be sensational.
Now times have changed, and I must pretend that hair does n't grow on my face.
The clapping they made on the concrete interrupted him in the ecstatic pleasure he knew, so that he quickly released his hold on the goat and pretended to be examining its haunches for ticks.
There was the revolution in Tibet which we pretended did not exist.

Synset Examples

She's just acting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - to behave in a way that is not genuine or natural, to perform a role
  • Synonym: dissemble - to conceal one's true motives or feelings, to put on a false appearance
  • Antonym: be genuine - to act in a sincere and natural way

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + tendere (to stretch), via Old French prétendre (to claim).
