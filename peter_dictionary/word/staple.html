staple

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.pəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.pəl / (BrE)
A basic or principal item, especially a food or product that is regularly consumed and in constant demand.

Examples

No one who has studied the radical Right can suppose that words are their sole staple in trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - something that is needed or essential

Etymology

From Middle English stapel, meaning 'market-place, emporium', of Germanic origin.
