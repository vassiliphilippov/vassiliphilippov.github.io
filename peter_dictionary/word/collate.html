collate

1. VERB

/ kəˈleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈleɪt / (BrE)
to compare critically different texts or versions in order to note differences or similarities, especially for editorial purposes

Examples

When some thirteen records of newly and recently born individuals are collated, little or no correlation between length and distribution can be detected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compare - to examine similarities and differences

Etymology

From Latin 'collatum', past participle of 'conferre' (to bring together, compare).
