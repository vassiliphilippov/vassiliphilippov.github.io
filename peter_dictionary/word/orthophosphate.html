orthophosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθoʊˈfɑːsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of orthophosphoric acid, commonly found as a simple phosphate compound in chemistry

Examples

Hydroxides, orthophosphates, borates, carbonates, and silicates are important inorganic ingredients of hard surface cleaners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - a salt or ester of phosphoric acid in general

Etymology

From Greek 'ortho-' (straight, correct) + 'phosphate', describing the simplest form of phosphate.
