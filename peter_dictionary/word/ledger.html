ledger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒə / (BrE)
a book or other record in which financial accounts are kept, especially one used by businesses to record debits and credits

Examples

The moral aridity of merchants made them loyal usually to their ledgers.
There was little enough time to contemplate them, however; in an instant the doctor was stalking across the room with an antique ledger in his hands, thoroughly eared and big as a table top.
"This is my hen ledger ", he informed him in an absorbed way.
The ledger was full of most precise information:
Where there is a left-hand entry in the ledger, there is a right-hand one, he remembered from his school days.

Synset Examples

they got a subpoena to examine our books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: account book - a book in which financial accounts are recorded
  • Degree Lower: journal - a daily record of transactions in accounting

Etymology

From Middle English leger (book lying permanently in one place), from Old French legier, from Old Dutch *legheri (lying, remaining).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒə / (BrE)
a book or other physical record in which financial accounts are recorded and kept

Examples

Despite this danger, however, we are informed on every hand that ideas, not machines, are our finest tools; they are priceless even though they cannot be recorded on a ledger page; they are the most valuable of commodities - and the most salable, for their demand far exceeds supply.

Synset Examples

he bought a new daybook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daybook - a book in which transactions are recorded daily before being transferred to the ledger

Etymology

From Middle English 'leger' (book lying permanently in one place), from Old French, from Old Dutch 'legger' (a lie, layer).
