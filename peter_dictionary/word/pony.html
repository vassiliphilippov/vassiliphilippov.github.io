pony

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
(informal) A racehorse; used especially in the context of horse racing and betting.

Examples

Rep. Berry, an ex gambler from San Antonio, got elected on his advocacy of betting on the ponies.

Synset Examples

he liked to bet on the ponies

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
A small horse, especially one used as a range horse in the western United States.

Examples

They could hear the pony's feet on the dry leaves for a while, then the sound faded out.
Leading his pony, he hurried that way, not remounting till he was well below the level of the surrounding range.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: horse - large domesticated mammal for riding or carrying loads

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic *ponaidh*, possibly via French *poulenet* (little foal).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
A small breed of horse, usually less than five feet (about 1.5 meters) high at the shoulder, known for being gentle and sturdy.

Examples

There was young Master Bruce... ...leading his gray pony with me on its back... ...like a sack of old potatoes, covered in mud and nursing a sprained ankle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: horse - large domesticated animal used for riding and work

Etymology

From Scots and Scottish Gaelic *ponaidh*, probably from French *poulenet* (diminutive of *poulain*, foal).
