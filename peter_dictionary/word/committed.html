committed

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtɪd / (BrE)
Dedicated or bound to a cause, activity, or belief, often by a pledge or strong conviction.

Examples

The country is committed to the doctrine of security by military means.
Since American life is committed above all to productivity and a higher standard of economic life, the countervailing forces of residential and religious exclusiveness have fought a desperate, rearguard action against the expanding interdependence of the metropolis.
Thus committed, action might follow.
It seems to me I already got my boys committed elsewhere.

Synset Examples

committed church members
a committed Marxist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedicated - devoted to a particular purpose or cause
  • Antonym: uncommitted - not pledged or dedicated to a particular cause or course

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'commit', from Latin 'committere' (to bring together, to entrust).
