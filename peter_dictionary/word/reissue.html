reissue

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˈɪʃu / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
a new publication, recording, or release of a work that has been published or released before, typically without changes

Examples

The occasion for this marathon: Angel's long awaited reissue in its "Great Recordings of the Century "series of the Schnabel-Pro Arte version.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edition - a particular version or release of a published work

Etymology

From re- (again) + issue (to publish, release).

2. VERB

/ riˈɪʃu / (AmE), / riˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
To produce and publish something again, especially a book, record, or film, often with changes or updates.

Examples

Returning once again to the Schnabel reissue, I am beguiled anew by the magnificence of this pianist's musical penetration.

Synset Examples

They never reprinted the famous treatise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: republish - to publish again
  • Synonym: reprint - to print again a published work

Etymology

From re- (again) + issue, meaning to put out again.
