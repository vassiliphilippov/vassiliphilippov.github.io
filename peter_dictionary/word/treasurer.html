treasurer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒərər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒ(ə)rə / (BrE)
an official responsible for receiving, keeping, and disbursing money for an organization, such as a club, company, or government body

Examples

B. J. Connell is the present treasurer and manager.
Operating in 1887 was the "Valley Telegraph Line ", officers of which were E. C. Orvis, president; H. K. Fowler, vice-president and secretary; J. N. Hard, treasurer; F. H. Walker, superintendent; H. S. Walker, assistant superintendent.
The officers were John Marsden, president; John C. Blackmer, vice-president; George Smith, treasurer; and William F. Orvis, secretary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashier - person in charge of payments or receipts in a business, especially a bank or shop
  • Synonym: accountant - person responsible for keeping or inspecting financial accounts

Etymology

From Middle English 'tresorer', from Old French 'tresorier', from 'tresor' (treasure).
