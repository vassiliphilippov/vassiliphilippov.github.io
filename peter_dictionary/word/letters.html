letters

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtɚz / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz / (BrE)
written works, literary culture, or scholarship; the art or practice of literature.

Examples

There is evidence to suggest, in fact, that many authors of the humorous sketches were prompted to write them - or to make them as indelicate as they are - by way of protesting against the artificial refinements which had come to dominate the polite letters of the South.

Synset Examples

this book shows American letters at its best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literature - written works, especially those considered to have artistic merit
  • Antonym: illiteracy - lack of ability to read or write; absence of literary culture

Etymology

From Latin 'litterae' meaning 'letters (of the alphabet), literature'.
