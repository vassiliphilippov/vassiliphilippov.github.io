dramatically

1. ADV

/ drəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is intended to create a strong effect or impression; in a manner full of excitement or emotion.

Examples

Technically it was fascinating, aurally spell-binding, and dramatically quite realistic.
Of course, the crowning event that has dramatically upset the traditional pattern of English religious history was the friendly visit paid by Dr. Fisher, then Anglican Archbishop of Canterbury, to the Vatican last December.

Synset Examples

he confessed dramatically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrically - in a theatrical or showy manner

Etymology

From 'dramatic' + -ally.

2. ADV

/ drəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is striking, impressive, or likely to attract attention; to a great or significant degree.

It represents a punishment that he knows he deserves, but it also symbolizes most dramatically that he lives his life within the process of time.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strikingly - in a striking or noticeable manner
  • Antonym: subtly - in a delicate or not obvious way

Etymology

From 'dramatic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'dramatikos' (pertaining to drama).

3. ADV

/ drəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪkli / (BrE)
In terms of dramatic quality or value; regarding how something fits the standards or qualities of drama.

Examples

The first half of The Charles Men, ending on the climax of the battle of Poltava in 1709, is more dramatically coherent than the second.

Synset Examples

the play was dramatically interesting, but the direction was bad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrically - in a manner relating to the theatre or drama; as in theatrical performance
