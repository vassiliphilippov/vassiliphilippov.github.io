premises

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛmɪsɪz / (AmE), / ˈprɛmɪsɪz / (BrE)
the land and the buildings on it, especially used in reference to a property or business location

Examples

Sometime on Saturday evening, August 22nd, while my family and I were dining at the Hostaria dell 'Orso, in Rome, you jimmied a window of our home in Bucks County, Pennsylvania, and let yourselves into the premises.

Synset Examples

bread is baked on the premises
the were evicted from the premises

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praemissa', meaning 'things mentioned before', later specialized to mean 'property' in legal usage.
