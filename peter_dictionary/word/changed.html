changed

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒd / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒd / (BrE)
(of a person, thing, or situation) made or become different in nature, form, or appearance.

Examples

Since his seizure, Scotty had had little appetite; yet his changed appearance, surprisingly, was one of plumpness.
The next time we saw him, he was a changed person; he had aged thirty years, and his face, the color of tallow, was crisscrossed with wrinkles, as though it had been wrapped in chicken wire.

Synset Examples

changed attitudes
changed styles of dress
a greatly changed country after the war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unchanged - not altered, remaining the same
  • Synonym: altered - having been changed, usually slightly

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒd / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒd / (BrE)
Having become different from a previous form, condition, or state.

Examples

In this changed world, Prokofieff settled to find himself, and to create for large national purpose.
And I can honestly say I'm a changed man.

Synset Examples

he's an altered (or changed) man since his election to Congress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altered - having been made different
  • Antonym: unchanged - remaining the same as before
