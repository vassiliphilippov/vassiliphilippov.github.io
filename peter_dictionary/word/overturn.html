overturn

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈtɜrn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to officially change or reverse a decision or judgment, especially by a higher court or authority

Examples

As was said in Gonzales, "it is the Appeal Board which renders the selective service determination considered' final' in the courts, not to be overturned unless there is no basis in fact.

Synset Examples

The Republicans were overruled when the House voted on the bill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uphold - to support or confirm a previous decision or judgment

Etymology

From over- + turn; first attested in the 14th century, originally meaning to turn upside down.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈtɜrn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɜːn / (BrE)
(of an object) to turn over so that the top is on the bottom or no longer in its normal position; to tip or capsize.

Examples

An auto overturned, skidding into a stopped tractor-trailer and burst into flames near Snellville, the patrol said.

Synset Examples

The big vase overturned
The canoe tumped over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip over - to fall or make something fall onto its side
  • Synonym: capsize - to overturn a boat in water

Etymology

From over- + turn; 'turn' from Old English 'tyrrnan' (to turn).

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈtɜrn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to cause something to tip over from an upright or normal position

Examples

Eugenia suspected her of deliberately overturning the heater because she was getting tired of dragging it back and forth and still wanted her own way, but Hope said if Grandma would n't have the heater nobody would have it, so Grandma had to give in.

Synset Examples

The cat knocked over the flower vase
the clumsy customer turned over the vase
he tumped over his beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip over - to turn something onto its side or upside down
  • Synonym: upset - to knock over or overturn something

Etymology

From over- + turn.
