recorded

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɔrdɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdɪd / (BrE)
(of music, sound, or information) set down or registered in a permanent form, especially on audio or video media, for reproduction.

Examples

There's more reading and instruction to be heard on discs than ever before, although the spoken rather than the sung word is as old as Thomas Alva Edison's first experiment in recorded sound.
Some of them are obvious, such as the fact that we associate recorded and live music with our responses and behavior in different types of environments and social settings.

Synset Examples

recorded music

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live - (of music or performance) delivered or heard at the time of its occurrence, not recorded

Etymology

From 'record' + '-ed', indicating past participle/adjective form.
