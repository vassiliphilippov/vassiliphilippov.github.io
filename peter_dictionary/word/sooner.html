sooner

1. ADV

/ ˈsuːnər / (AmE), / ˈsuːnə / (BrE)
at a time earlier than expected or than something else; comparative of 'soon'. Often used in the phrase 'no sooner... than'.

Examples

The sooner you act like an angel the quicker you 'll feel angelic.
No sooner had I started drinking than the driver started zigzagging the truck.

Synset Examples

Come a little sooner, if you can
came earlier than I expected

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: soon - in or after a short time
  • Degree Higher: soonest - at the earliest possible time; superlative form of 'soon'
