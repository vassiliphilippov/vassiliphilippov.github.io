disallow

1. VERB

/ dɪsəˈlaʊ / (AmE), / dɪsəˈlaʊ / (BrE)
to officially refuse to allow something; to reject a claim, request, or action, especially by authority

Examples

The Revenue Service disallowed the claim, invoking a law provision that generally bars deductions for expenses incurred in connection with what it said was tax-exempt income.

Synset Examples

I forbid you to call me late at night
Mother vetoed the trip to the chocolate store
Dad nixed our plans

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allow - to permit something to happen; to let someone do something

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman disalouer, from Old French des- (expressing reversal) + alouer (to allow).
