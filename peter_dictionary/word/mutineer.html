mutineer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːtəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who rebels openly against authority, especially a member of the armed forces or a crew who participates in a mutiny

Examples

He saw the dangers, not the glories of being identified as a mutineer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition or armed resistance

Etymology

From French *mutinier* (to rebel), from *mutin* (rebellious)
