bespeak

1. VERB

/ bɪˈspiːk / (AmE), / bɪˈspiːk / (BrE)
To be a sign of or indicate something; to show or suggest clearly.

Examples

His red face, his coarse gestures, and his lustful stares bespeak his sensuality.

Synset Examples

These symptoms indicate a serious illness
Her behavior points to a severe neurosis
The economic indicators signal that the euro is undervalued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicate - to be a sign of; to show
  • Synonym: demonstrate - to show or make evident

Etymology

From Old English 'besprecan' (to speak about, accuse), later influenced by the sense 'to indicate'.
