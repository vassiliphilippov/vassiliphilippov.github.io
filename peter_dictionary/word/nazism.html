Nazism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːtsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɑːtsɪzəm / (BrE)
The political principles and practices of the Nazi Party, characterized by dictatorial power, extreme nationalism, racism, and expansionism, especially as practiced in Germany under Adolf Hitler (1933–1945).

Examples

So it is too with many other spirits which we all know: the spirit of Nazism or Communism, school spirit, the spirit of a street corner gang or a football team, the spirit of Rotary or the Ku Klux Klan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascism - authoritarian, nationalist right-wing system of government and social organization
  • Antonym: communism - political theory advocating class war and leading to a society in which all property is publicly owned
  • Degree Lower: socialism - political and economic theory advocating social ownership of production

Etymology

From German Nazismus, derived from 'Nazi' (short for Nationalsozialist, member of the National Socialist German Workers' Party) + -ism.
