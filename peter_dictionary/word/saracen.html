Saracen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsærəsən / (AmE), / ˈsærəsən / (BrE)
(historically) a member of a nomadic people from Syria and the Arabian deserts at the time of the Roman Empire, later used in medieval Europe to refer to Muslims, especially those opposed to the Crusaders.

Examples

Suddenly, a messenger arrives and, just before collapsing dead at his feet, informs him that the Saracens have invaded Silesia, the home province of his affianced.

Etymology

From Late Latin Saracenus, from Greek Sarakēnos, of uncertain origin.
