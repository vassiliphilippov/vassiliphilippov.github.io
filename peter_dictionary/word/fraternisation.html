fraternisation

1. NOUN

/ fræˌtɝːnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / frəˌtɜːnɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of associating with others in a friendly or brotherly way, especially with an enemy or opposing side

Examples

It was an interesting fraternisation of ex convicts, union racketeers, ward heelers, sold-out officials, and gunmen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternize - to associate in a friendly way, especially with the enemy

Etymology

From French 'fraternisation', from 'fraterniser' (to fraternise), from Latin 'frater' (brother).
