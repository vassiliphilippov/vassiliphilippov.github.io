worry

1. VERB

/ ˈwɝːi / (AmE), / ˈwʌri / (BrE)
to feel anxious or concerned about something; to be troubled or uneasy.

Examples

We 'd be losing at halftime to a good team and Hartweger would say,' Do n't worry, Coach - we 'll get 'em all right "'.
But do n't worry.
"Do n't you worry.
But as long as you can have treatment from my machine you have nothing to worry about.
By the second day Michelangelo began to worry; and by the second night he was in a state of panic: he could see nothing out of the afflicted eye.

Synset Examples

I worry about my job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fret - to be visibly anxious or worried
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling worried or anxious

Etymology

From Old English *wyrgan* (to strangle), later influenced by the meaning 'to harass or torment,' then evolved to 'to feel unease.'
