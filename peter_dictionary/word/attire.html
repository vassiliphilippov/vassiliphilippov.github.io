attire

1. NOUN

/ əˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / əˈtaɪə / (BrE)
clothes, especially those worn for a particular purpose or occasion, or which have a distinctive style

Examples

In these readings, the double bass is either kept discreetly in the background, or it is dressed in clown's attire - the musical equivalent of a bull in a china shop.
They keep their wings and feet pressed tightly against their bodies, and in spite of their often colorful attire you may very well mistake them for lumps of dirt.

Synset Examples

formal attire
battle dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothing - items worn to cover the body; clothes in general
  • Synonym: costume - a set of clothes characteristic of a particular period, place, or occasion
  • Synonym: garb - distinctive or special clothing, especially of a particular type or style

Etymology

From Old French atirier (to equip, attire), from a- (to) + tirer (to arrange, prepare).
