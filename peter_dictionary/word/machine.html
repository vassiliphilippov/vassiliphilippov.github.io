machine

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃin / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A device with moving parts, often powered by electricity or another source, that performs or assists with human tasks by transmitting or modifying energy.

Examples

He knew just the thing for her - a treatment from his "cosmic light ozone generator "machine.
As he applied the applicator extending from the machine - which consisted of seven differently colored neon tubes superimposed on a rectangular base - to the supposedly diseased portions of Mrs. Shaefer's body, Lee kept up a steady stream of pseudo scientific mumbo-jumbo.
Yes, the ozone from his machine would cure practically everything, he assured her.
This machine will cure your cancer ridden body ".
But as long as you can have treatment from my machine you have nothing to worry about.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device - piece of equipment made for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: apparatus - complex device or set of equipment for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'machina', from Greek 'mēkhanē' (device, contrivance).

2. NOUN

/ məˈʃin / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A device that applies force at one point to overcome resistance at another, especially to perform work or transmit mechanical power.

Examples

But the machine quack can cause far more than just suffering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanism - a device designed to transform input forces into a specific output, especially in machinery
  • Synonym: engine - a machine with moving parts that converts power into motion

Etymology

From Latin machina, from Greek mēkhanē (device, contrivance, engine).

3. NOUN

/ məˈʃin / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A person who works or performs tasks with exceptional efficiency, relentlessness, or consistency, especially in a specific activity.

Examples

A man can be an effective fighting machine throughout the incubation period of most infectious diseases.

Synset Examples

the boxer was a magnificent fighting machine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robot - person who behaves like a machine, lacking emotion or variation
