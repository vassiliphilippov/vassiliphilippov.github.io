tick

1. NOUN

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
a short, light, metallic tapping sound, especially one made regularly by a clock or watch

Examples

With these gadgets - impressive to the gullible because of their flashing light bulbs, ticks, and buzzes - he then carries out a vicious medical con game, capitalizing on people's respect for the electrical and atomic wonders of our scientific age.
"I paint the nothing ", he said once to Franz Kline and myself, "the nothing that is behind the something, the inexpressible, unpaintable' tick' in the unconscious, the' spirit' of the moment resting forever, suspended like a huge balloon, in non time ".

Synset Examples

he counted the ticks of the clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click - a short, sharp sound, especially as made by a switch or lock
  • Degree Higher: tock - the second, deeper sound made by a clock; paired with tick

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound itself.

2. VERB

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
(of a clock or similar device) to make a short, light, regularly repeated sound.

Examples

The clock on the mantel piece was scandalized and ticked so loudly that he glanced at it over his shoulder and then quickly left the room.

Synset Examples

the clocks were ticking
the grandfather clock beat midnight

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by a clock or watch.

3. NOUN

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
A small parasitic arachnid with a barbed mouthpart, feeding on the blood of warm-blooded animals.

Examples

The clapping they made on the concrete interrupted him in the ecstatic pleasure he knew, so that he quickly released his hold on the goat and pretended to be examining its haunches for ticks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - tiny arachnid similar to ticks but usually not parasitic on warm-blooded animals

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *tik* (same meaning), related to Low German *tick* and Dutch *teek*.

4. VERB

/ tɪk / (AmE), / tɪk / (BrE)
(of a clock or similar mechanism) to make a short, sharp, regular sound.

Examples

It ticked over smoothly, idling while Fogg exchanged mails with the armed messenger from Burlington at Fort Ethan Allen, and one from Montpelier and Barre at the St. John field.
In the command centers there are special clocks ready to tick off the minutes elapsed since "E hour ".

Synset Examples

The clock ticked away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click - to make a short, sharp sound as of a small object striking another

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a clock or watch.
