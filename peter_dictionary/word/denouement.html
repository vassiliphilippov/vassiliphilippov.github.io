denouement

1. NOUN

/ deɪˈnuːmɑnt / (AmE), / deɪˈnuːmɒ̃ / (BrE)
the final outcome or resolution of a complex sequence of events, especially in a narrative or drama

Examples

The spectacle progressed towards a denouement which was obviously still remote; the audience attended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusion - the end or finish of an event or process
  • Degree Higher: climax - the most intense point in the development of something

Etymology

From French 'dénouement', from 'dénouer' (to untie), from Latin 'nodus' (knot).
