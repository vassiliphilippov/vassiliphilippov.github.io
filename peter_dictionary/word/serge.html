serge

1. NOUN

/ sɜrdʒ / (AmE), / sɜːdʒ / (BrE)
A strong, twilled woolen fabric, typically used for making suits and uniforms.

Examples

Then Rector, attired in his best blue serge suit, sat in a chair out on the lawn, in the shade of a tree, smoking a cigarette and waiting.

Etymology

From Old French *serge*, from Latin *serica* (silken goods, from 'Seres', the Chinese).
