thundering

1. ADJ

/ ˈθʌndərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərɪŋ / (BrE)
Making a loud, deep, rumbling sound like thunder.

Examples

Emerson - Platonist, idealist, doctrinaire - sounded a high Transcendental note in his "Boston Hymn ", delivered in 1863 in the Boston Music Hall amidst thundering applause: "Pay ransom to the owner and fill the bag to the brim.

Synset Examples

the thundering herd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booming - making a loud, deep, resonant sound
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
