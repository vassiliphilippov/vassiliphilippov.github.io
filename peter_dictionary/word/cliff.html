cliff

1. NOUN

/ klɪf / (AmE), / klɪf / (BrE)
A steep, high face of rock, often at the edge of the sea, a river, or on a mountain.

Examples

The first series of Sherlock Holmes adventures ends with Holmes and Moriarty grappling together on the edge of a cliff.
The ceiling stayed solid above them at about eight hundred feet, and at times the sheer cliffs seemed about to close in.

Synset Examples

he stood on a high cliff overlooking the town
a steep drop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precipice - a very steep or vertical face of rock, often extremely high
  • Synonym: bluff - a high, steep bank or cliff, often beside a river or coast

Etymology

From Old English 'clif' (steep slope, cliff); related to Old Norse 'klif'.
