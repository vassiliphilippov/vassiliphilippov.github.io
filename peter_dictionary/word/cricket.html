cricket

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
A small leaping insect related to the grasshopper, the male of which makes a characteristic chirping sound by rubbing its forewings together.

Examples

Scotty's father sat sprawled in his chair, angular, alert as a cricket, looking about at the huge stainless-steel appointments of the room with an expression of proprietorship.

Etymology

From Old French *criquet*, of imitative origin.
