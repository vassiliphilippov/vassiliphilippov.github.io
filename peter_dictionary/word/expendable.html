expendable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛndəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛndəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) suitable to be used up or sacrificed, especially because it is considered less important or easily replaced.

Examples

Present conservation practices regard wildlife, not as an expendable natural resource, but as an annual harvest to be sown and also reaped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispensable - able to be done without or not essential
  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary or indispensable

Etymology

From Latin expendere (to weigh out, pay) via expend + -able.
