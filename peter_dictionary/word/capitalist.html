capitalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or believes in capitalism, especially one who owns or controls capital or wealth for investment

Examples

Hired, hard lackeys of the Warmongering Capitalists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: socialist - a person who supports socialism

Etymology

From late Latin 'capitalista', from 'capitale' (stock, property), from Latin 'caput' (head).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
Relating to capitalism, capitalists, or the economic principles of capitalism.

Examples

The world exploded when Fascism challenged all concepts of peace and liberty, and the outraged, freedom loving peoples of the Capitalist and Socialist worlds combined forces to stamp Fascist tyranny into cringing submission.
They were not diplomats or jazz musicians, or even organizers of reading-rooms and photo montage displays, but rugged capitalist entrepreneurs like Henry Ford, Hugh Cooper, Thomas Campbell, the International Harvester Co., and David W. Griffith.
Perhaps the public's present attitude toward business stems from the fact that the "rugged capitalist entrepreneur "no more exists in America.

Synset Examples

a capitalist nation
capitalistic methods and incentives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalistic - Relating to capitalism or its economic principles; another adjective form
  • Antonym: socialist - Relating to socialism or socialists

Etymology

From capital + -ist, originally in reference to someone who owns capital.
