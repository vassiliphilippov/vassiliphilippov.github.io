soap

1. NOUN

/ soʊp / (AmE), / səʊp / (BrE)
A substance used for washing and cleaning, made from the salts of vegetable or animal fats.

Examples

Polyphosphates gave renewed life to soap products at a time when surfactants were a threat though expensive, and these same polyphosphates spelled the decline of soap usage when the synergism between polyphosphates and synthetic detergent actives was recognized and exploited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detergent - a cleaning agent, especially a synthetic one, used instead of soap

Etymology

From Old English sāp (soap), of West Germanic origin; related to Dutch zeep and German Seife.
