hypoactive

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈaktɪv / (BrE)
Having less than normal activity; abnormally inactive, especially in a medical context.

Examples

At this time a detailed neuromuscular examination revealed diffuse muscle atrophy that was moderate in the hands and feet, but more marked in the shoulders, hips, and pelvic girdle, with hypoactive deep tendon reflexes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyperactive - abnormally or excessively active

Etymology

From prefix hypo- (under, below normal) + active.
