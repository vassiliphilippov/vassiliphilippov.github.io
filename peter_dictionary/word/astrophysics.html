astrophysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
The branch of astronomy that deals with the physical and chemical properties and processes of celestial bodies and the universe as a whole.

Examples

In addition to the basic programs in wavelength standards, spectroscopy, solid state physics, interactions of the free electron and atomic constants which are necessary to provide the foundation for technological progress, the Bureau has strengthened its activities in laboratory astrophysics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomy - the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole

Etymology

From Greek 'astro-' (star) + 'physics', from Greek 'physis' (nature).
