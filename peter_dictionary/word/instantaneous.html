instantaneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.stənˈteɪ.ni.əs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stənˈteɪ.ni.əs / (BrE)
happening or done immediately, without any delay; occurring at once.

Examples

A fire had just been lighted, he saw, and things had been set out for drinks, and, like any stray, his response to these comforts was instantaneous.

Synset Examples

relief was instantaneous
instant gratification

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediate - happening or done at once, without delay
  • Antonym: delayed - happening or done after a period of waiting

Etymology

From Latin 'instantaneus', from 'instans, instantis' (present, urgent).
