enclave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnkleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒŋklɑːv / (BrE)
A portion of territory surrounded by a larger territory whose inhabitants are culturally or ethnically distinct from those of the surrounding area.

Examples

Old attitudes are held more tenaciously in the Tidewater than the Piedmont; so that a line running down the length of the South marking the upper limits of tidewater would roughly divide the Old South from the new, but with, of course, important minority enclaves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exclave - a portion of territory that is politically attached to a larger region but not physically conterminous with it because of surrounding foreign territory

Etymology

From French 'enclaver', from Medieval Latin 'inclavare' (to enclose).
