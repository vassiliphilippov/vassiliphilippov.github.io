nude

1. ADJ

/ nud / (AmE), / njuːd / (BrE)
not wearing any clothes; completely unclothed.

Examples

"A miracle, a revelation, it was like a curtain suddenly torn away to reveal something nude ".
A few yards beyond the group of men, a man's nude body lay face down on a patch of thick green dichondra.

Synset Examples

bare bodies
naked from the waist up
a nude model

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked - without any clothes; bare
  • Antonym: clothed - wearing clothes

Etymology

From Latin 'nudus' (bare, naked).

2. NOUN

/ nud / (AmE), / njuːd / (BrE)
a painting, sculpture, or other artistic representation of a naked human figure

Examples

Hanging over the bar was an oil painting of a nude Al had accepted from a student at the Corcoran Gallery who needed to eat and drink and was broke.
The nude was small and black-haired and elfin, and was called "Eloise ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nude - a person who is naked

Etymology

From Latin 'nudus' (naked).

3. NOUN

/ nud / (AmE), / njuːd / (BrE)
the condition of being naked; a state of undress, especially with no clothes on

Examples

If you want me to pose nude, you'll need a long lens.

Synset Examples

they swam in the nude

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clothed - wearing clothes; covered by clothing
  • Synonym: naked - without clothes; not covered by clothing

Etymology

From Latin nudus (bare, naked).
