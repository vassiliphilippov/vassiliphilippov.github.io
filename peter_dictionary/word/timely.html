timely

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪmli / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmli / (BrE)
happening at the most suitable or appropriate time; occurring when most needed or expected

Examples

The second timely part of this sketch of literature and the search for identity has to do with the difference between good and enduring literary works and the ephemeral mass culture products of today.

Synset Examples

a timely warning
with timely treatment the patient has a good chance of recovery
a seasonable time for discussion
the book's publication was well timed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untimely - happening at an unsuitable or premature time
  • Synonym: opportune - well-chosen or particularly favorable or appropriate

Etymology

Formed from 'time' + '-ly'; attested since the Middle English period.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪmli / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmli / (BrE)
Done or occurring at a suitable or opportune moment; done before a deadline or when required.

Examples

Petitioner first contends that the Department denied him procedural due process by not giving him timely opportunity, before its final recommendation to the appeal board, to answer the statement of the local board as to his claim of devoting 100 hours to actual preaching.
(1) your reasons for requesting an extension, (2) whether you filed timely income tax returns for the 3 preceding years, and (3) whether you were required to file an estimated return for the year, and if so whether you did file and have paid the estimated tax payments on or before the due dates.
Any failure to file timely returns or make estimated tax payments when due must be fully explained.

Synset Examples

the timely filing of his income tax return

Related Terms

  • Antonym: late - after the proper or expected time

Etymology

From Middle English timeli, from time + -ly.

3. ADV

/ ˈtaɪmli / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmli / (BrE)
At an opportune or suitable time; in good time.

Examples

Since April 15, 1962, is on Sunday your return for the calendar year 1961 will be timely filed if it is filed on or before Monday, April 16, 1962.

Synset Examples

your letter arrived apropos

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untimely - at an inappropriate or unsuitable time

Etymology

From time + -ly, originally an adjective, used adverbially since the 16th century.
