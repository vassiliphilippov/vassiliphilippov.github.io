trigger-happy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ ˌhæpi / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə ˌhæpi / (BrE)
having a tendency to use firearms or resort to violence irresponsibly or without sufficient cause; eager to act without restraint.

Examples

That's to say, he was trigger happy.
"Your trigger-happy brother is n't in the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsive - acting without careful thought; rash
  • Synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers

Etymology

Formed from 'trigger' (the lever that activates a gun) and 'happy' (eager or inclined), emerging in 20th-century American English.
