hinder

1. VERB

/ ˈhɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪndə / (BrE)
to make it more difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen; to obstruct or delay progress

Examples

The Old Ones neither helped nor hindered this plan; time was not yet.
Presently, the intense absorption of ultra-violet rays in the earth's atmosphere seriously hinders ground observation.

Synset Examples

She is impeding the progress of our project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impede - to slow down or prevent the progress of
  • Synonym: obstruct - to block or get in the way of
  • Antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier
  • Antonym: help - to make it easier for someone to do something

Etymology

From Old English *hindrian* (to injure or harm), related to *hinder* (back, behind).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪndə / (BrE)
to make it difficult for someone or something to progress or succeed; to obstruct or impede

Examples

Then came this decision, which sped the process of gaining equality (or perhaps hindered it; only historical evolution will determine which):

Synset Examples

His brother blocked him at every turn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impede - to delay or prevent by obstructing
  • Synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening
  • Antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easy or easier
  • Antonym: promote - to help or encourage to exist or flourish

Etymology

From Old English hindrian (to harm, injure, keep back).

3. VERB

/ ˈhɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪndə / (BrE)
to make it difficult for someone or something to do something or for something to happen

Examples

This light did not penetrate very far back into the hall, and my eyes were hindered rather than aided by the dim daylight entering through the fan vents when I tried to pick out whatever might be lying, or squatting, on the floor below.

Synset Examples

The brace I have to wear is hindering my movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impede - to slow down or prevent the progress of
  • Synonym: obstruct - to block or get in the way of
  • Antonym: help - to make it easier or possible for something to happen

Etymology

From Old English hindrian (to injure, check, repress).
