fluorescent

1. ADJ

/ fluˈrɛsənt / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) emitting visible light when exposed to external radiation, especially ultraviolet light.

Examples

"Now ", he said, "we got to put in some outlets for them power tools; then a couple of fluorescent lamps over the workbench an' I guess we 're about through down here ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phosphorescent - emitting light after exposure to radiation, continuing after the source is removed

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flow) + '-escent' (becoming), coined after 'fluorescence.'
