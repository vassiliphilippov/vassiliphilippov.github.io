peril

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛrəl / (BrE)
serious and immediate danger; a situation presenting risk of harm or loss.

Examples

The two men sat for some time, savoring the pleasure of escape from peril and the relief such escape brings, before they got up and left the hotel, the doctor to go to the conference house and Alex to go to the main post office.

Synset Examples

drinking alcohol is a health hazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - a source of danger or risk
  • Antonym: safety - freedom from danger or risk
  • Synonym: risk - a situation involving exposure to danger

Etymology

From Old French *peril* (danger), from Latin *periculum* (trial, danger).
