uptown

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʌptaʊn / (BrE)
The residential or non-commercial part of a city or town, typically situated away from the central business district.

Examples

I raised some kale by hocking the good clothes I had left over from my respectable uptown life, but when that was gone I did n't have a cent.
Among us, we three handled quite a few small commissions, from spot drawings for advertising agencies uptown to magazine work and quick lettering jobs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downtown - the central business district of a city

Etymology

From 'up' + 'town', originally referring to areas of a city higher in elevation or farther from the city center.
