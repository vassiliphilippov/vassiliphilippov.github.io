regularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊˈlarəti / (BrE)
(Geometry) The property of a shape, especially a polygon, of having all sides and all angles equal.

Examples

They stick to their principles, insist upon their due, but grow and bloom with dependable regularity if given it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irregularity - the state of not having equal sides and angles (in geometry)

Etymology

From Latin regularitas, from regularis meaning 'regular'.
