anachronism

1. NOUN

/ əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnakrənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially something conspicuously old-fashioned; something that is out of its proper time period

Examples

As for states' rights, they have never counted in the thinking of my liberal friends except as irritations of a minor and immoral nature which exist now only as anachronisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anachronistic - belonging to a period other than that being portrayed

Etymology

From Greek anachronismos, from ana- (against) + khronos (time).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnækrənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A thing or artifact that belongs to a period other than that in which it exists, especially something outdated or old-fashioned for its current context.

Examples

Today a battle cry may seem an anachronism, for in the modern Army, esprit de corps has been sacrificed to organizational charts and tables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique - a collectable object with high value because of its considerable age
  • Antonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern

Etymology

From Greek anachronismos, from ana- (against) + khronos (time).
