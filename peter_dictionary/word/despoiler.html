despoiler

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɔɪlər / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a person who takes goods by force, especially in war; a plunderer or looter

Examples

The only performance which was too soft for me was that of Yvette Mimieux, but since someone had to become the victim of despoilers, just to emphasize that such things do happen at these fracases, I suppose this was the attitude the part called for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunderer - a person who steals goods, especially during war
  • Synonym: looter - a person who steals goods, typically during chaos

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'despoiller', from Latin 'despoliare' (to strip, rob).
