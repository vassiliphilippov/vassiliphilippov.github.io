optimistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑp.tɪˈmɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒp.tɪˈmɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Expecting that good things will happen or that a situation will turn out well.

Examples

During the next five years the leaders of the Fair Deal reluctantly backed down from the optimistic expectations of the New Deal.
It was, too, an optimistic philosophy, and, though it separated law from morality, it was by no means an immoral or amoral one.

Synset Examples

in an optimistic mood
optimistic plans
took an optimistic view

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst possible outcome
  • Synonym: hopeful - having or showing hope; expecting a positive outcome
  • Synonym: positive - having a positive or constructive attitude

Etymology

From Latin 'optimus' (best) via French 'optimiste'.
