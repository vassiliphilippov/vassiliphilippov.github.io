crew

1. NOUN

/ kruː / (AmE), / kruː / (BrE)
a group of people who work together, especially all the workers on a ship, aircraft, or in a particular job or task

Examples

Over his shoulder he could see Prevot with the machine gun crew.
He did not see Sparling, or DeGroot, or Ringel, or any of the feverish crew that had so harassed him twelve hours ago.
I will get you inside the guard camp as a member of my crew.
The rest of the crew offsaddled their mounts and turned them into the remuda.
And that's how it came to pass... that on the second-to-last day of the job, the convict crew that tarred the plate-factory roof in the spring of '49... wound up sitting in a row at ten o'clock in the morning, drinking icy-cold, Bohemia-style beer... courtesy of the hardest screw that ever walked a turn at Shawshank State Prison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: team - a group of people working together to achieve a common goal

Etymology

From Middle French 'creue' (increase, augmentation), from Old French 'creu', from Latin 'crescere' (to grow).

2. NOUN

/ kru / (AmE), / kru / (BrE)
all the people who work on and operate a ship, aircraft, or other large vehicle.

Examples

Some time later, after a check had been made of all automatic equipment, the crew would join the others.
And the crew would be automatically brought out of their suspended animation.
She carried, besides her captain, a crew of twenty-one and provisions for a voyage of exploration of the Arctic waters of North America.
He had obtained and provisioned a veteran ship called the Discovery and had recruited a crew of twenty-one, the largest he had ever commanded.
On the second voyage, he had turned back at the frozen island of Novaya Zemlya and meekly given the crew a certificate stating that he did so of his own free will - which was obviously not the case.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passenger - a person who is riding in a vehicle but not part of the crew

Etymology

From Middle French *creue* (increase, growth), from *croître* (to grow), originally referring to a group of people growing together into a team.
