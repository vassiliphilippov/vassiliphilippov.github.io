taken

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪkən / (AmE), / ˈteɪkən / (BrE)
understood or interpreted in a particular way

Examples

A word taken in its dictionary meaning, a photographic image of a recognizable object, the mere picturing of a "scene "tends to lose experiential vividness and to connote such conventional abstractions as to invite neutral reception without the incitement of value feelings.

Synset Examples

a word taken literally
a smile taken as consent
an open door interpreted as an invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreted - explained or understood in a specified way

Etymology

Past participle of 'take', from Old English *tacan*, from Old Norse *taka* (to take).
