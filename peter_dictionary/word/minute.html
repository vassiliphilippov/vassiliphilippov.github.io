minute

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪt / (BrE)
A unit of time equal to 60 seconds or one sixtieth of an hour.

Examples

The game players saw the Air Force film Monday, ran for 30 minutes, then went in, while the reserves scrimmaged for 45 minutes.
The Union Pacific Railroad streamliner, City of San Francisco, stopped in Ogden, Utah, for a few minutes.

Synset Examples

he ran a 4 minute mile

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: second - the base unit of time, 1/60th of a minute
  • Degree Higher: hour - a unit of time equal to 60 minutes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *minuta*, meaning 'small part'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪt / (BrE)
A very short period of time; a moment or instant.

Examples

A taxi took him back to the bar and grill where he had left his car, and a few minutes later he found a parking place across the street from his apartment.
Mike, can I have a minute?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - an indefinitely short period of time
  • Degree Lower: second - a short but slightly longer period of time; also a precise unit

Etymology

From Latin 'minuta', meaning 'small part'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnət / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪt / (BrE)
A precise point in time; a particular moment.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - a very brief period of time; an exact point in time

Etymology

From Latin 'minuta' (small part), via Old French 'minute'.
