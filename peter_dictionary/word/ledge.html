ledge

1. NOUN

/ lɛdʒ / (AmE), / lɛdʒ / (BrE)
a narrow, flat, projecting surface, especially on a cliff or underwater, forming a ridge or shelf

Examples

A detailed scouring of the entire area revealed nothing beyond a ledge of rocks that might have been the rifleman's hiding place.

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'lecche', related to Old English 'liccian' (to lick), referring to the way a ledge juts out.
