preaching

1. NOUN

/ ˈpri.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriː.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A sermon or religious address delivered publicly, usually as part of a church service, intended to instruct or inspire the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermon - a speech on a religious or moral subject, especially one given during a church service

Etymology

From Middle English preching, from Old French prechier, from Latin praedicare (to proclaim, preach).
