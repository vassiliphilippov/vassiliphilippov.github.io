alkaline

1. ADJ

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪn / (BrE)
Containing, relating to, or having the properties of an alkali; having a pH greater than 7.

Examples

Hard surface cleaning in household application is represented by two classes of alkaline products: (1) the formulations made expressly for machine dishwashers, and (2) the general-purpose cleaners used for walls and woodwork.
Sometimes, although by no means always, these are indeed alkaline.

Synset Examples

alkaline soils derived from chalk or limestone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidic - having a pH less than 7; opposite of alkaline

Etymology

From French alcalin, from 'alkali.'
