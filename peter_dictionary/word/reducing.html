reducing

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdusɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsɪŋ / (BrE)
In chemistry, relating to or involving the addition of electrons to an atom or ion, usually by removing oxygen or adding hydrogen; always occurs together with oxidation.

Examples

On the other hand, there are a few antithyroid drugs of this same general type, such as resorcinol, possessing no reducing activity and possibly acting through formation of a complex with molecular iodine.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oxidizing - relating to the loss of electrons in a chemical reaction

Etymology

From the verb 'reduce', from Latin 'reducere' (to lead back), in chemistry meaning to decrease the oxidation state by electron gain.
