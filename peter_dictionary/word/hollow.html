hollow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
A depression or cavity in a surface or solid object, often naturally formed.

Examples

Most species seem to prefer a ready-made hollow such as a deserted mouse nest, a bird house, or the hole made by a woodpecker; some show a definite liking for making their nest in moss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - an empty space within a solid object
  • Synonym: hole - an opening or gap in or through something

Etymology

From Old English 'holh' (a hollow place), related to 'hole'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
A space or cavity inside something; a depression or sunken area.

Examples

The doctor, he noticed, was attempting a transverse movement towards the stairs, but before the movement could be completed a distinct and audible cluck ruffled the air in the hollow of the stair-well.

Synset Examples

hunger had caused the hollows in their cheeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - an empty space within a solid object
  • Synonym: concavity - a hollowed or rounded inward shape
  • Antonym: protrusion - a thing that protrudes; the opposite of a hollow or depression

Etymology

From Old English 'holh', meaning 'a hollow place or cave'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
having an empty space or cavity inside; not solid throughout

Examples

Solid blocks are recommended because hollow blocks would have to be filled with concrete to give effective protection.

Synset Examples

a hollow wall
a hollow tree
hollow cheeks
his face became gaunter and more hollow with each year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - completely filled or whole, not hollow

Etymology

From Old English 'holh' (a hollow place, hole), related to 'hole'.

4. ADJ

/ ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
(of words, actions, or achievements) lacking real value, meaning, or sincerity; empty or insincere.

Examples

One finds, for example, that a terse and stereotyped verbal expression, seeming at first to be a mere hollow convention, reveals itself over the months of therapy as the vehicle for expressing the most varied and intense feelings, and the most unconventional of meanings.

Synset Examples

empty promises
a hollow victory
vacuous comments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty - lacking value or substance
  • Antonym: meaningful - having real significance or sincerity

5. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
A small, sheltered valley, especially one between hills or mountains.

Examples

Walls of water rushed down the slopes and filled the hollows like the crests of flash floods.

Synset Examples

he built himself a cabin in a hollow high up in the Appalachians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valley - a low area of land between hills, often with a river
