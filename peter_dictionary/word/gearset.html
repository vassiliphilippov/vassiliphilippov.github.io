gearset

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪrˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪəˌsɛt / (BrE)
a set of gears that work together in a machine, especially to transmit power or change speed in vehicles and mechanical devices

Examples

Bicycle gear-sets he had once used as the basis of the design for the Camden Cycly Company plant hung on a rope in one corner, and over his desk, next to several old and dusty hats, was a clean pair of roller skates which he occasionally used up and down front of his house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gearbox - mechanism housing gears, especially in vehicles

Etymology

From 'gear' (mechanical part) + 'set' (group of things).
