abnormally

1. ADV

/ əbˈnɔrməli / (AmE), / əbˈnɔːməli / (BrE)
In a way that is not normal or usual; unusually or exceptionally.

Examples

The importance of knowing in what chemical forms the hormone may exist is accentuated by the recent observation that there exists an abnormally long-acting TSH in blood drawn from many thyrotoxic patients (Adams, 1958).

Synset Examples

they were behaving abnormally
his blood pressure was abnormally low

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normally - in a normal or usual way

Etymology

Formed from 'abnormal' + '-ly', with 'abnormal' from Latin 'ab-' (away from) + 'normalis' (according to rule).
