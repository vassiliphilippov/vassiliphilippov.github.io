commandeer

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑː.mənˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.mənˈdɪə / (BrE)
to take possession or control of something, especially for military or official use, often without the owner's consent and by force or authority

Examples

Commandeering a passing car, Kimmell pursued the fleeing vehicle, but lost it in traffic.

Synset Examples

The Cubans commandeered the plane and flew it to Miami

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
  • Synonym: requisition - officially demand the use of property, especially by a public authority

Etymology

From French 'commander' (to command), influenced by 'commandeer' in Afrikaans, itself from Dutch 'commandeeren'.
