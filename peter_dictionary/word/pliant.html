pliant

1. ADJ

/ ˈplaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈplaɪənt / (BrE)
easily influenced or shaped; flexible in character or attitude

Examples

The teacher was a pliant fool.

Synset Examples

the plastic minds of children
a pliant nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleable - easily influenced or changed
  • Antonym: inflexible - not easily changed or influenced

Etymology

From Old French 'pliant', present participle of 'plier' (to bend), from Latin 'plicare' (to fold).
