crevice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛvəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛvɪs / (BrE)
A narrow crack or opening, especially in a rock or surface.

Examples

In addition, it is small enough to get into crevices, jacket and crown margins, malposed anteriors, and the back teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or line of breakage
  • Synonym: crack - a narrow opening or break, especially in a hard surface
  • Synonym: gap - a break or opening in an object or between two objects

Etymology

From Old French *crevace*, from Latin *crepare* (to crack).
