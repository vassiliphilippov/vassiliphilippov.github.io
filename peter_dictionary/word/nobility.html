nobility

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɪləti / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having high moral principles, ideals, or elevated character.

Examples

Nikolai Cherkasov, the Russian actor who has played such heroic roles as Alexander Nevsky and Ivan the Terrible, performs the lanky Don Quixote, and does so with a simple dignity that bridges the inner nobility and the surface absurdity of this poignant man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtue - moral excellence; goodness
  • Antonym: baseness - lack of moral principles; being low or dishonorable

Etymology

From Old French *nobilité*, from Latin *nobilitas* (fame, nobility, high birth).

2. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɪləti / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The class of people in society who hold hereditary titles or belong to the aristocracy.

Examples

On his father's side he was of German descent, on his mother's he came of the old Swedish nobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest social class, often holding hereditary titles
  • Antonym: commoner - a person without hereditary or noble rank

Etymology

From Latin nobilitas (nobility, fame, high rank).
