bake

1. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
to cook food by dry heat in an oven or on a heated surface, especially without direct exposure to a flame

Examples

Bake slowly about one hour at 250 - 275 F. until lightly brown.
Bake slowly at least one-half hour longer.
While this is baking, saute mushrooms, fresh or canned, in butter.
Today whole grains are freshly ground every day and baked into bread.
The bread baked from this mixture is light in color and fragrant in aroma.

Synset Examples

bake the potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roast - cook food, especially meat, by prolonged exposure to heat in an oven or over a fire
  • Antonym: fry - cook food in hot fat or oil
  • Antonym: grill - cook food over direct heat or flame

Etymology

From Old English 'bacan' (to bake), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
to cook food by dry heat, especially in an oven

Examples

Mrs. Reese baked her cookies for only the third time in the Bake-off finals.

Synset Examples

bake a cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roast - to cook food by exposing it to dry heat, typically in an oven or over a fire
  • Antonym: boil - to cook food in water or other liquid at boiling point
  • Antonym: fry - to cook food in hot fat or oil

Etymology

Old English *bacan* (to bake), from Proto-Germanic *bakaną*.

3. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
(of the sun, weather, etc.) To make something very hot by exposing it to intense heat, especially from a natural force.

Examples

The Santa Cruz mountains sprawl over three counties, and the roads twist through sky-tapping redwoods down whose furrowed columns ripple streams of rain, even when heat bakes the Santa Clara valley below at the left.

Synset Examples

The sun broils the valley in the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broil - to become very hot from exposure to intense heat, especially the sun
