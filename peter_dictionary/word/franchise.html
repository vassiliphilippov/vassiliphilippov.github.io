franchise

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrænˌtʃaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
An official authorization granted by a company, government, or organization allowing a person or group to carry out specified commercial activities, especially to sell or distribute its goods or services in a particular area.

Examples

When that failed, he enlisted Branch Rickey's aid in the formation of a third major league, the Continental, with New York as the key franchise.
The New York franchise is headed by Mrs. Charles Shipman Payson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - official permission to do something, especially in business

Etymology

From Old French *franchise* (freedom, right), from Medieval Latin *francitia*, from Latin *francus* (free).
