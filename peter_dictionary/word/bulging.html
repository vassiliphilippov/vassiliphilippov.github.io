bulging

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌldʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌldʒɪŋ / (BrE)
curving outward or protruding as if swollen; sticking out in a rounded shape

Examples

They were further stripped of old wives' tales by seeing the slender, lovely Russian girls performing feats requiring tremendous strength - and with not one bulging muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged or expanded by or as if by internal pressure
  • Antonym: sunken - curving inward; hollowed or depressed below the surrounding area

Etymology

Present participle of 'bulge', from Old French *bouge* (swelling, bag) from Latin *bulga* (leather bag).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌldʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌldʒɪŋ / (BrE)
swelling outward or protruding in a rounded shape

Examples

How in the world had he formerly found time to build up a business, raise a family, be on half a dozen boards, work actively on committees and either go out in the evening or plow through the contents of a bulging brief case?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convex - curving outward like the exterior of a sphere
  • Antonym: concave - curving inward like the interior of a sphere

Etymology

From 'bulge' (verb) + -ing, from Middle French 'bouge' (wallet, swelling).
