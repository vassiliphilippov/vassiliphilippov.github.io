diachronic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɑn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the study of something, especially language, as it changes over time.

Examples

But consideration of the frequency of stems per constant meaning seems to be established as having significance in comparative situations with diachronic and classificatory relevance; and Gleason presumably is on the way with a further contribution in this area.

Synset Examples

diachronic linguistics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronic - Relating to the study of something at a particular point in time, rather than through its historical development.

Etymology

From Greek dia- (through) + chronos (time).
