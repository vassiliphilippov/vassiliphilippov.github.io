croon

1. VERB

/ kruːn / (AmE), / kruːn / (BrE)
to sing or hum in a soft, gentle, and low voice, especially in a sentimental manner

Examples

When Juanita awoke, Kate was still rocking the dead child, still crooning in disbelief, "No, no, oh, no! "
As she worked she absentmindedly crooned, producing a curious sound.
The two women babbled and crooned at him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sing - to produce musical tones with the voice

Etymology

Originated in Scots, likely of Middle Dutch origin 'kronen' (to lament, murmur).
