paradoxical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpær.əˈdɑks.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpær.əˈdɒk.sɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Seeming impossible or self-contradictory, but possibly true.

Examples

That would certainly be paradoxical.
We enjoyed a paradoxical freedom when we were still too young for school.

Synset Examples

it is paradoxical that standing is more tiring than walking

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contradictory - mutually opposed or inconsistent

Etymology

From Greek paradoxos (contrary to expectation), via Late Latin paradoxalis.
