homefolk

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌfəʊk / (BrE)
the people from one's home, locality, or family, especially regarded collectively or with affection

Examples

Shortly after the beginning of Sherman's Georgia campaign, an ailing Yank wrote his homefolk: "The surgeon insisted on Sending me to the hospital for treatment.

Synset Examples

he wrote his homefolk every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: family - a group of people related by blood or marriage

Etymology

Formed from 'home' + 'folk', meaning people from one's home.
