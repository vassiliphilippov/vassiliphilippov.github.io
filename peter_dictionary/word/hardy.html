hardy

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑr.di / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.di / (BrE)
Strong and able to endure difficult conditions or hardship; tough and resilient.

Examples

By the time Felix turned up it was early afternoon, which, one would think, would be late enough so that by then, except for small children and a few hardy souls who had not yet sobered up, it could have been expected that people would no longer be having any sort of active interest in the previous night's noisemakers and paper hats.

Synset Examples

hardy explorers of northern Canada
proud of her tall stalwart son
stout seamen
sturdy young athletes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; hardy
  • Synonym: sturdy - physically strong and solid
  • Antonym: frail - weak and delicate; not hardy

Etymology

From Old French *hardi* (bold, daring), from hardir (to make hard or bold), from Frankish *hardjan* (to make hard).
