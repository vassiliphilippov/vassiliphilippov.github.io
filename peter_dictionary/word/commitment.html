commitment

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the quality of being dedicated or steadfast in purpose or action

Examples

We are left helpless to cope with it because we do not dare speak of it as anything real for fear that to do so would imply a commitment to that which has already been discredited and proved false.
"Perhaps "stands in place of commitment.

Synset Examples

a man of energy and commitment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedication - the quality of being devoted to a task or purpose
  • Synonym: devotion - profound dedication; earnest attachment to a cause or person
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From commit + -ment, patterned after French *engagement* and Latin *commitmentum*.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the state or act of pledging or binding oneself to a course of action, duty, or person, often with a sense of dedication or obligation

Examples

They must in their planning be able to count on at least tentative commitments of foreign capital assistance over periods of several years.
But he takes his bearings from the great guidelines of policy, well established precedents, the commitments of the United States under international charters and treaties, basic statutes, and well understood notions of the American people about how we are to conduct ourselves, in policy literature such as country papers and National Security Council papers accumulated in the Department.

Synset Examples

his long commitment to public service
they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligation - something one is bound or compelled to do
  • Synonym: responsibility - a duty or task one is required or expected to do
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern, absence of commitment

Etymology

From commit +‎ -ment, from Latin committere (to unite, connect, entrust).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
a contractual obligation involving a financial responsibility or engagement, especially in business or legal contexts

Examples

Sipping their coffee, discussing the weather, the day's shopping, Fritzie's commitments at the network (all of which he would cancel), they avoided the radio, the morning TV news show, even the front page of the Santa Luisa Register, resting on the kitchen bar.

Synset Examples

his business commitments took him to London

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligation - something that one is bound to do, especially legally or morally
  • Synonym: liability - a thing for which someone is responsible, especially a debt or financial obligation

Etymology

From commit + -ment, influenced by Old French 'cometement'.
