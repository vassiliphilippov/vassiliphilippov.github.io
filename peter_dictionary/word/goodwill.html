goodwill

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊdˌwɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdˌwɪl / (BrE)
An intangible asset representing the value of a business's reputation, customer relationships, or other non-physical elements that give it a competitive advantage, especially as shown in its value above tangible assets during a sale.

Examples

It was professedly worth three thousand dollars in stock and good will, and the name was written in gold in foot high letters across each of the two display windows.

Etymology

From 'good' + 'will', originally meaning benevolence; in business use since the 18th century to denote intangible value acquired by reputation.
