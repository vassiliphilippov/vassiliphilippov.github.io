hypnotize

1. VERB

/ ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪpnətaɪz / (BrE)
to put someone into a state of hypnosis, usually by using techniques of focused attention, suggestion, or relaxation

Examples

But I questioned, also, professional soldiers, who would not easily be hypnotized by a septuagenarian's dreamy irredentism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesmerize - to hold the complete attention of someone or to hypnotize them
  • Antonym: dehypnotize - to bring someone out of a hypnotic trance

Etymology

From French hypnotiser, from Greek hupnos (sleep) + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
