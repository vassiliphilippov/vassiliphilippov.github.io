lustily

1. ADV

/ ˈlʌstɪli / (AmE), / ˈlʌstɪli / (BrE)
In a strong, vigorous, or energetic manner; with healthy force or enthusiasm.

Examples

They germinate quickly, the tiny plants appearing in a week, and grow along lustily.

Synset Examples

the young plants grew lustily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigorously - in a way that involves physical strength, effort, or energy

Etymology

From "lusty" (vigorous, healthy, energetic) + -ly (adverbial suffix). "Lusty" is from Middle English, from Old English *lustig* (pleasant, desirous).
