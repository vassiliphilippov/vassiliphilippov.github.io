lecturing

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛktʃɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of delivering a formal talk or discourse on a subject, especially as a method of teaching

Examples

He was in his mid fifties at this time, long past the establishment of his name and the wish to be lionized yet once again, and it was almost a decade since he had sworn off lecturing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - a formal talk on a subject, especially for teaching
  • Synonym: teaching - the act or profession of instructing

Etymology

From 'lecture' (from Latin 'lectura', a reading), plus '-ing', forming a noun indicating the action or process.
