love

1. VERB

/ lʌv / (AmE), / lʌv / (BrE)
to feel a strong affection or deep attachment to someone or something

Examples

If his mother loves him, he clings to that love as a ballast.
"I just love them ", he said.
The dramatic construction of his stories characteristically turns on a situation in which someone is simultaneously compelled and forbidden to love.
The release, the freedom, involved in loving another is either terribly difficult or else absolutely impossible; and the motion toward it brings disaster.
The Vice President said with a slight bluster, "There is n't anyone who loves the President more than I do.

Synset Examples

I love French food
She loves her boss and works hard for him

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: like - to find agreeable or enjoyable
  • Degree Higher: adore - to love intensely or deeply
  • Antonym: hate - to feel intense dislike

Etymology

From Old English 'lufian' (to love); related to 'lufu' (love) and German 'liebe'.

2. NOUN

/ lʌv / (AmE), / lʌv / (BrE)

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Degree Higher: adoration - deep love and respect
  • Degree Lower: fondness - affection or liking for someone or something
  • Antonym: hate - intense dislike or aversion

Etymology

From Old English lufu, related to Old High German luba (love) and Gothic lubō (love).
