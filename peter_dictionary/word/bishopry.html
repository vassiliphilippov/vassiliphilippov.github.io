bishopry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪʃəpri / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəpri / (BrE)
The office, rank, or dignity of a bishop.

Examples

Even Rector himself was prey to this spirit of competition and he knew it, not for a more exalted office in the hierarchy of the church - his ambitions for the bishopry had died very early in his career - but for the one clear victory he had talked about to the colonel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bishopric - the district or diocese under a bishop's jurisdiction; also the office or rank of a bishop

Etymology

From bishop + -ry (denoting office or rank).
