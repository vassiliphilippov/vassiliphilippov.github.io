nitrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrəˌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrədʒən / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol N), a colorless, odorless, tasteless gas that forms about 78% of the Earth's atmosphere and is essential to all living things.

Related Terms

Etymology

From French 'nitrogène', from Greek 'nitron' (native soda) + French '-gène' (producing), meaning 'nitre-forming'.
