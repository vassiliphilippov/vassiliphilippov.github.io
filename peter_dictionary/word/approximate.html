approximate

1. VERB

/ əˈprɑksɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / əˈprɒksɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to judge or estimate something in a way that is nearly but not exactly correct; to make a close calculation or estimate

Examples

The mean temperature of this region was approximated by the temperature measured halfways between the edge of the hot spot and the rim of the plug.
Studies of the intensity data indicate that they may be converted to approximate transition probabilities.

Synset Examples

I estimate this chicken to weigh three pounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of something

Etymology

From Latin 'approximatus', past participle of 'approximare' (to come near), from 'ad-' (to) + 'proximus' (nearest).
