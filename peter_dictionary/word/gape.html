gape

1. VERB

/ ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
to look in surprise or amazement, typically with the mouth open

Examples

Arnold Palmer, the defending champion, lost his title on the 72 nd hole after a few minutes of misfortune that left even his fellow pros gaping in disbelief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gawk - to stare openly and stupidly
  • Synonym: goggle - to stare with wide eyes, typically in amazement or wonder

Etymology

From Old Norse *gapa* (to open the mouth wide).
