catastrophe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtæstrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəfi / (BrE)
A sudden event causing great and usually irreversible damage, destruction, or suffering; a disaster or total ruin.

Examples

Finally, the theatrical (and perversely erotic) notions of dressing up, cosmetics, disguise, and especially change of costume (or singularity of costume, as with Cipolla), are characteristically associated with the catastrophes of Mann's stories.

Synset Examples

lack of funds has resulted in a catastrophe for our school system
his policies were a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or loss
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and sudden damage or distress
  • Synonym: tragedy - an event causing great suffering or destruction

Etymology

From Greek katastrophē (overturning, disaster), from kata- (down) + strephein (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtæstrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəfi / (BrE)
an event causing sudden and great damage or suffering; a disaster

Examples

He is confronted with the recurrent crises, such as great natural catastrophes and the great transitions of life - marriage, incurable disease, widowhood, old age, the certainty of death.
He was trembling, a strange feeling upon him, fully expecting some catastrophe to strike him dead on the spot.
There's no greater catastrophe in the universe, she reflected dourly, impaling tender green beans on the silver fork, than the dwindling away of a family.

Synset Examples

the whole city was affected by the irremediable calamity
the earthquake was a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - an event causing great damage or hardship
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great distress or misfortune
  • Antonym: blessing - something beneficial, opposite of a disaster

Etymology

From Greek 'katastrophē' (overturning, sudden turn), from 'kata-' (down) + 'strephein' (to turn).
