layoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ɒf / (BrE)
the act of dismissing employees from their jobs, especially temporarily or for economic reasons

Examples

leaves of absences, illnesses, and layoffs.
But to the cattlemen who had been facing bankruptcy from rustling losses and to the cowboys who had been faced with lay-offs a few years earlier, he was becoming a vastly different type of legendary figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from employment
  • Synonym: redundancy - the state of being no longer needed at work, especially in British English

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'lay off', meaning to dismiss (someone) from employment.
