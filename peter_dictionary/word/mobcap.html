mobcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑbˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈmɒbˌkæp / (BrE)
a large, high, often frilly cap with a full, gathered crown, formerly worn indoors by women in the 18th and early 19th centuries

Examples

I would not want to be one of those writers who begin each morning by exclaiming, "O Gogol, O Chekhov, O Thackeray and Dickens, what would you have made of a bomb shelter ornamented with four plaster-of-Paris ducks, a birdbath, and three composition gnomes with long beards and red mobcaps "?

Etymology

From 'mob', a term for a woman's cap in the 18th century, possibly from 'mob' meaning a slovenly woman, plus 'cap'.
