document

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑkjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkjʊmənt / (BrE)
A piece of written, printed, or electronic matter that provides information or serves as an official record, or any written material representing a person's thoughts.

Examples

Whether considered alone or in relation to other editions, COLH 40 is a document of prime importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - A written account or document preserving information
  • Synonym: manuscript - A handwritten or typed document, especially a draft or original text

Etymology

From Latin 'documentum' (example, proof, lesson), from 'docēre' (to teach).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.kjə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.jʊ.mənt / (BrE)
An official or written paper that provides information or evidence.

Examples

There were liberal provisions for dispensation where documents or records were lacking.
(The common misconception that he was Dutch and that his first name was Hendrik stem from Dutch documents of his third voyage.)
The trial judge decided that the administrative procedures of the Act were fully complied with and refused to require the production of such documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a written account or entry of information, especially for official purposes
  • Synonym: certificate - an official document attesting a fact
  • Synonym: letter - a written or printed message addressed to a person or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'documentum' (example, proof), from 'docere' (to teach).

3. VERB

/ ˈdɑːkjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkjʊmənt / (BrE)
to record something in detail, especially in writing or with evidence, so that it can be referred to later or verified

Examples

The breakdown of classical structures of meaning in all realms of western culture has given rise to several generations of artists who have documented the disintegrative processes.
Each state advisory committee documented its own activity.
Though there is obviously great need for continued experimentation with various types of short-term intervention to further efforts in developing an operational definition of prevention at the secondary - or perhaps, in some instances, primary - level, the place of short-term intervention has already been documented by a number of investigators in a wide variety of settings.

Synset Examples

The parents documented every step of their child's development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to set down information in writing or other form
  • Synonym: note - to notice or record something for future reference

Etymology

From Latin 'documentum' (example, proof, lesson), from 'docere' (to teach).

4. VERB

/ ˈdɑːkjəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkjʊmɛnt / (BrE)
to support or supply (a claim, statement, etc.) with evidence, references, or documentation

Examples

Internal national responsibility, now a truism, need not be documented.

Synset Examples

Can you document your claims?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantiate - to provide evidence to support or prove the truth of something
  • Synonym: verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true, accurate, or justified

Etymology

From Latin 'documentum', meaning ‘lesson, example, proof’
