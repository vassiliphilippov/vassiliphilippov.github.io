universalistic

1. ADJ

/ juˌnɪvərsəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / juːˌnɪvəsəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or supporting the idea of universalism, especially the belief that certain values, principles, or beliefs are universally applicable.

Examples

In America also all of our major religious bodies officially sanction a universalistic ethic which is reflective of our common religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universalist - a person or doctrine advocating universalism

Etymology

From 'universalism' + '-istic', patterned after similar English adjective forms.
