trustfully

1. ADV

/ ˈtrʌst.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈtrʌst.fʊl.i / (BrE)
In a trusting manner; with trust or confidence in someone or something.

Examples

He was seeing, somehow, the face of a young boy, the boy Simms Purdew must once have been, a boy with sorrel hair, and blue eyes dancing with gaiety, and the boy mouth grinning trustfully among the freckles.

Synset Examples

she looked at her father trustingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustingly - in a way that shows trust; with trust
  • Antonym: distrustfully - in a way that shows lack of trust

Etymology

From 'trustful' + '-ly', modeled after other adverbs from adjectives.
