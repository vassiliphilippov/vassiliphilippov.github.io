Indian

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
relating to the indigenous peoples of the Americas (American Indians), their cultures, or their languages.

Examples

Some areas may provide archeological values such as ancient Indian village sites or hunting areas, caves, artifacts, etcetera.

Synset Examples

Native American religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Native American - a member of the indigenous peoples of the Americas

Etymology

From the mistaken application of 'India' by European explorers who thought they had reached South Asia when arriving in the Americas.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
A member of the indigenous peoples of North, Central, or South America; especially those encountered by Europeans when they arrived in the Americas. This term is now considered outdated or offensive by many people, and 'Native American,' 'First Nations,' or specific tribal names are preferred.
