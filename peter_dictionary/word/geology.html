geology

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the Earth's physical structure, history, and the processes that have shaped it, as revealed by rocks and other physical materials.

Examples

The key to the world of geology is change; nothing remains the same.
Geology is the study of pressure and time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geologist - a person who studies geology

Etymology

From Greek geōlogia: geō- (earth) + -logia (study)
