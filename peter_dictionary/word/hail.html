hail

1. NOUN

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
Small hard balls of ice that fall from the sky, especially during a storm.

Examples

With the first reports, Russell's horse wheeled to the right and ran towards the buildings while Cook, followed by a hail of bullets, raced towards the arroyo of Salyer's Canyon immediately in front of him, just reaching it as his horse fell.
Drought, hail, disease, and insects take their toll of crops.
The heavens opened, pelting them with hail the size of walnuts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: snow - soft white frozen precipitation as flakes
  • Antonym: rain - liquid water drops falling from clouds

Etymology

From Old English hægel (hail, hailstorm), related to Old High German hagal and Latin calamitas (damage), with Indo-European roots.

2. VERB

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
to come from or have been born in a particular place; to be a native of

Examples

They hail from Travancore, a state in the subcontinent where Kali, the goddess of death, is worshiped.

Synset Examples

She hails from Kalamazoo

3. VERB

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
to praise or acknowledge someone or something enthusiastically or publicly

Examples

Prokofieff's Classical Symphony was hailed as an ingenious work from a naturally gifted and well trained musician still in his twenties.
Those who backed a similar plan last year hailed the message.
More, the U. S. action was hailed by a principal opposition leader, Dr. Juan Bosch, as having saved "many lives and many troubles in the near future ".
In fact, the antipathy to outward ceremonies hailed by modern exponents as so uniquely characteristic of the "direct thinking "Zennist was a feature of Taoism.

Synset Examples

The critics hailed the young pianist as a new Rubinstein

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaim - to praise enthusiastically and publicly
  • Synonym: celebrate - to publicly acknowledge a significant or happy day or event with praise
  • Antonym: criticize - to express disapproval or find fault with someone or something

Etymology

From Old Norse 'heill' (health, good luck), originally used as a greeting; later extended to acclamation or enthusiastic praise.

4. VERB

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
to signal or call to attract the attention of, especially to stop a vehicle such as a taxi or bus

Examples

We talked after I hailed his Jeep marked with the U. N. flag.

Synset Examples

hail a cab

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flag down - to signal for a vehicle to stop

Etymology

From Old Norse heilla (to greet), related to hail (to greet or salute).

5. VERB

/ heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
to greet or acclaim someone enthusiastically or joyfully

Examples

Too often, unless he hails them, they pass him by.
FREDO: Hail Mary, full of grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salute - to greet with respect or admiration
  • Synonym: welcome - to greet someone with pleasure or kindly courtesy
  • Antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone

Etymology

From Old Norse 'heill' (health, prosperity), Old English 'hǣl' (health, greeting).
