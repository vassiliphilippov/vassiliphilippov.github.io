Highness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
a title used in addressing or referring to a royal person such as a prince or princess (e.g. His Highness, Her Highness, Your Highness)

Examples

His Highness had decided only two hours ago to go out of town, and he was eager to be off.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Majesty - a higher title used to address a king or queen

Etymology

From Middle English 'heynesse', from Old English 'hēanes', from 'hēah' (high) + '-ness'. Title usage evolved in English royal courts.
