toweling

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaʊəlɪŋ / (BrE)
fabric, usually of cotton or linen, used for making towels

Examples

He hesitated for a moment when Michelangelo asked him to disrobe, so Michelangelo gave him a piece of toweling to wrap around his loins, led him to the kitchen to take off his clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towel - a piece of absorbent cloth or paper for drying

Etymology

From towel + -ing, meaning material suitable for making towels.
