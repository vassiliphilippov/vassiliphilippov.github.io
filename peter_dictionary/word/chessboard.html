chessboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛsˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛsˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A square board divided into 64 squares of alternating colors, used for playing chess.

Examples

I'll keep an eye out for you and the chessboard ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: checkerboard - board marked with a pattern of squares, used in chess and draughts/checkers

Etymology

Formed from "chess" (the game) + "board".
