blunt

1. ADJ

/ blʌnt / (AmE), / blʌnt / (BrE)
(of a knife, blade, or similar object) having a worn-down edge or point; not sharp.

Examples

(1) When an object was placed in the patient's hand, he had no difficulty determining whether it was warm or cold, sharp or blunt, rough or smooth, flexible, soft, or hard; and he could tell, simply by the feel of it, whether it was made of wood, iron, cloth, rubber, and so on.

Synset Examples

a blunt instrument

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sharp - having an edge or point that is able to cut or pierce easily

Etymology

From Old French *blunt* or *blont* (dull), likely of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ blʌnt / (AmE), / blʌnt / (BrE)
(of a person or manner of speaking) uncompromisingly direct, plain, or frank, often seeming rude or insensitive.

Examples

Somehow, the pictures and stories of Soviet T-34 tanks on Cuban beaches and Russian Mig jet fighters strafing rebel troops has brought home to all of us the stark, blunt truth of what it means to have a Russian military base 90 miles away from home.
Well, it'’s perfectly all right with me but I should tell you that I am a blunt man and I intend to speak very frankly to you.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting
a blunt New England farmer
I gave them my candid opinion
forthright criticism
a forthright approach to the problem
tell me what you think--and you may just as well be frank
it is possible to be outspoken without being rude
plainspoken and to the point
a point-blank accusation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Synonym: forthright - direct and outspoken; straightforward and honest
  • Antonym: diplomatic - having or showing skill in dealing with people or sensitive matters

3. VERB

/ blʌnt / (AmE), / blʌnt / (BrE)
to make something less sharp, strong, or intense

Examples

The net result is a uniquely satisfying sense of comradeship, the kind of comradeship which sparks enthusiasm and blunts the cutting edge of sacrifice and hardship.

Synset Examples

blunted emotions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - to make less sharp or acute
  • Antonym: sharpen - to make sharper or more intense

Etymology

From Middle English 'blont', possibly of Scandinavian origin.
