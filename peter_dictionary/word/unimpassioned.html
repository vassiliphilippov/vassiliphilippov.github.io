unimpassioned

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪmˈpæʃənd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpæʃənd / (BrE)
not showing strong emotion; calm, rational, or detached in manner or thought.

Examples

Aristotle also tended to stratify all aspects of human nature and activity into levels of excellence and, like Plato, he put the pure and unimpassioned intellect on the top level.

Synset Examples

answered with an unimpassioned defense
the unimpassioned intellect

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impassioned - showing or filled with strong emotion

Etymology

Prefix "un-" (not) + "impassioned" (from passion, derived from Latin "passio" meaning suffering or feeling).
