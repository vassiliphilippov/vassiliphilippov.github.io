clerking

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɜrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɑːkɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity or occupation of keeping records of business transactions; performing clerical work such as bookkeeping or filing.

Examples

The position of receptionist was opened in a large office and an announcement was made to the other girls already working that they could apply for this job which had higher prestige and slightly higher salary than typing and clerking positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookkeeping - the activity of keeping financial records

Etymology

From 'clerk' (Middle English, from Old French 'clerc', from Latin 'clericus', meaning clergyman or scribe) + '-ing'.
