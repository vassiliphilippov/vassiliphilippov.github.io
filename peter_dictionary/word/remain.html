remain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
to continue to be in a particular place, position, or situation without leaving or changing

Examples

He remained there for four years before moving to Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, N. Y..
But except for a vague adherence to the basic storyline - i.e., that Ruth remained with Naomi and finally wound up with Boaz - the film version has little to do with the Bible.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to continue to be in a particular state or place
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place or position

Etymology

From Latin remanēre (to stay behind), from re- (back) + manēre (to stay).
