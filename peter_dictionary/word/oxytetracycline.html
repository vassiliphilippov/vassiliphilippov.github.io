oxytetracycline

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksiˌtɛtrəˈsaɪklin / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌtɛtrəˈsaɪkliːn / (BrE)
A yellow crystalline antibiotic, used to treat various bacterial and rickettsial infections, especially in animals; trademarked as Terramycin.

Examples

As an aid in reducing incidence and severity of bloat, provide 75 milligrams of oxytetracycline hydrochloride per animal daily.
To reduce incidence of liver abscesses, supply 75 milligrams of oxytetracycline activity per head daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetracycline - a group of broad-spectrum antibiotics structurally related to oxytetracycline

Etymology

Formed from oxy- (related to oxygen) and tetracycline (from tetra- 'four' and cycl- 'cycle, ring'), describing its chemical structure.
