dustbin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌstˌbɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʌst.bɪn / (BrE)
A container, usually with a lid, used for holding household rubbish until it is collected.

Examples

"If you are trying to get us out of the brothel, the dustbin, the kitchen sink, and the tawdry living-room, you are probably wasting your time ", Moreland told me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trash can - container for holding waste (American English)
  • Synonym: garbage can - container for holding household waste (American English)

Etymology

From 'dust' + 'bin'. 'Dust' in British English formerly referred to waste or rubbish.
