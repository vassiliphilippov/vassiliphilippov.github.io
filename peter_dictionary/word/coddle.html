coddle

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑd.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.əl / (BrE)
to treat someone with excessive care or indulgence; to be overprotective or too gentle with

Examples

Pansies do n't have to be coddled; they 'd rather have things rugged, with only moderate protection on the coldest days.
The boats of America's trailer sailors in 1961 will be coddled on clouds as they are hauled to new horizons.

Synset Examples

grandparents often pamper the children
Let's not mollycoddle our students!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pamper - to treat with excessive care or indulgence
  • Synonym: spoil - to harm the character of a child by giving too much care or attention

Etymology

Probably from Latin *calidulus* (slightly warm), diminutive of *calidus* (warm), originally referring to gentle cooking.
