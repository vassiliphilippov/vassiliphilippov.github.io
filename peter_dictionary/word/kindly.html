kindly

1. ADJ

/ ˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
Showing or motivated by sympathy, understanding, or generosity.

Examples

He did not mind the useless, kindly questions.

Synset Examples

was charitable in his opinions of others
kindly criticism
a kindly act
sympathetic words
a large-hearted mentor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charitable - showing generosity and sympathy for others
  • Antonym: cruel - showing a lack of kindness or sympathy

Etymology

From Middle English 'kindeli', from 'kind' + '-ly', meaning natural, compassionate.

2. ADV

/ ˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
In a kind or considerate manner; with kindness.

Examples

Mrs. Roebuck very kindly let me drive through Sante Fe to a road which would, she said, lead us to Taos and then Raton and "eventshahleh "out of New Mexico.
Mr. Corleone, would you kindly identify for the committee the gentleman sitting to your left?

Synset Examples

He spoke kindly to the boy
she kindly overlooked the mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gently - in a gentle manner
  • Antonym: harshly - in a harsh manner; with cruelty or severity

Etymology

From Middle English kindeli, from kind (nature, type) + -ly.
