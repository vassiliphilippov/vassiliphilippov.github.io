maple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
The hard, close-grained wood of any of various maple trees, especially the sugar maple, used for furniture, flooring, and similar purposes.

Examples

Maple and ash, beech and elm, one hundred to win on Three in the fourth, fifty to win on Six in the third, one hundred to win on Two in the eighth.
A hay wagon moved slowly along the gutter, the top of it swept by the low boughs of the maple trees, and loose straws were left hanging tangled among the leaves.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: maple - any tree or shrub of the genus Acer

Etymology

From Old English 'mapultrēow', from 'mapul' (maple) + 'trēow' (tree).
