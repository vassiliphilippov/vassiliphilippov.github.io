occupancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkjəpənsi / (AmE), / ˈɒkjəpənsi / (BrE)
The act or state of living in or using a place, especially as a tenant.

Examples

The Lenin tomb is obviously adequate for double occupancy, Moscow is a crowded city, and the creed of communism deplores waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenancy - the period or act of renting or leasing property

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *occupantia*, from Latin *occupare* (to seize, occupy).
