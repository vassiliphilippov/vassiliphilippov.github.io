twilight

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪlaɪt / (BrE)
the period of soft, diffused light from the sky when the sun is just below the horizon, especially following sunset.

Examples

I went to Israel because I wish to live there as a Jew, in the twilight of my life.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight, just before nightfall
  • Antonym: dawn - the period of twilight in the morning, just before sunrise

Etymology

From Middle English 'twilite', from Old English 'twī-' (two, signifying 'half') + 'lēoht' (light).
