decertify

1. VERB

/ diˈsɜːr.tɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / diːˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to officially withdraw approval or certification from someone or something, especially by an authority or governing body

Examples

Employes of Montgomery Ward + Co. at The Dalles, in a National Labor Relations Board election Thursday voted to decertify Local 1565, Retail Clerks International Association, AFL - CIO, as their collective bargaining agent.

Synset Examples

Carter derecognized Taiwan in 1979 after the U.S. recognized the People's Republic of China

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certify - to officially recognize or approve; to attest as true or meeting a standard

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (indicating removal) and certify.
