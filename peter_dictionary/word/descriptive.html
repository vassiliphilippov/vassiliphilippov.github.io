descriptive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪptɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv / (BrE)
serving to describe or give information about something rather than explain or judge it

Examples

Tables 1 and 2 present the results of the statistical analysis of the data when compulsivity is used as the descriptive variable.
It exhibits much the same descriptive technique and is open to much the same criticisms.

Synset Examples

the descriptive variable
a descriptive passage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explanatory - serving to explain rather than merely describe

Etymology

From Medieval Latin descriptivus, from Latin describere (to describe).
