sustenance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌstə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌstɪ.nəns / (BrE)
Food and drink regarded as a source of strength and nourishment; something that sustains life or health.

Examples

He does n't think that potting them from a deck chair on the south side of the house with a quart glass of beer for sustenance is entirely sporting.
Still, his finale is put together with taste and a most sensitive projection of that pale sustenance, despair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nourishment - food or other substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition
  • Synonym: nutrition - the process of providing or obtaining the food necessary for health and growth

Etymology

From Old French *sustenance*, from Latin *sustinēre* (to hold up, support).
