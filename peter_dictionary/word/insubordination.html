insubordination

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˌbɔr.dəˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˌbɔː.dɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
refusal to obey orders or show respect to someone in authority

Examples

"I should n't like to have to write you up for insubordination as well as dereliction of duty ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - the act of doing what someone in authority tells you to do
  • Synonym: defiance - open resistance against authority

Etymology

From Latin 'insubordinatio', from 'in-' (not) + 'subordinatio' (subordination).
