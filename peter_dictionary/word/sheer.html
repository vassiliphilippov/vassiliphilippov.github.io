sheer

1. ADJ

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the complete or pure quality of something; utter or absolute.

Examples

For sheer ambition, take the Decca series titled modestly "Wisdom ".
Despite the sheer beauty and spectacle of numerous documentaries, art films, and travelogues, despite the impressive financial success of such a recent development as Cinerama, the movies are at heart a form of fiction, like the play, the novel, or the short story.
Centrally, however, the administrative problem was more complex and the sheer prestige of office was very likely an unfair advantage.
He had a mean, unbroken sheer bastard in his outfit, and someone invented the name Trig for him.
They overran the 7 th Cav's forward machine-gun positions through sheer weight of numbers, over piles of their own dead.

Synset Examples

absolute freedom
an absolute dimwit
a downright lie
out-and-out mayhem
an out-and-out lie
a rank outsider
many right-down vices
got the job through sheer persistence
sheer stupidity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute - complete; without restriction or qualification
  • Synonym: utter - complete; total; absolute

Etymology

From Middle English schere (thin, bright, pure), probably from Old English scīr (bright, clear).

2. ADJ

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
(of a substance or quality) not mixed with anything else; pure.

Examples

Not altogether a successful play, "Epitaph for George Dillon "overcomes through sheer vitality and power what in a lesser work might be crippling.
Sheer plumpness, he knew, is not a vital part of the body and has no procreative functions.

Synset Examples

plain water
sheer wine
not an unmixed blessing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pure - not mixed with anything else; uncontaminated
  • Antonym: adulterated - having been made impure by adding inferior substances

Etymology

From Middle English schere, from Old English scīr (clear, pure).

3. ADJ

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
(of a cliff, wall, or surface) very steep; nearly vertical or perpendicular.

Examples

The ceiling stayed solid above them at about eight hundred feet, and at times the sheer cliffs seemed about to close in.

Synset Examples

a bluff headland
where the bold chalk cliffs of England rise
a sheer descent of rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - having a sharp inclination; not flat or gradual (of a slope)

Etymology

From Middle English 'schere', meaning 'thin' or 'pure', later influenced in sense by the idea of abruptness or steepness.

4. VERB

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
To turn sharply or change direction abruptly, especially to avoid something.

Examples

She instinctively sheered away from personal discussions but she filed the comment away for further meditation.

Synset Examples

The car cut to the left at the intersection
The motorbike veered to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swerve - to turn aside abruptly from a straight course
  • Synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly or abruptly

Etymology

From Middle English sheren (to deviate), related to Old English scieran (to shear, cut).
