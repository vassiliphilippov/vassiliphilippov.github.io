crush

1. VERB

/ krʌʃ / (AmE), / krʌʃ / (BrE)
to defeat an opponent or team overwhelmingly and decisively, especially in a competition or contest

Examples

The powerful New York Yankees won their 19th world series in a 5 - game romp over outclassed Cincinnati, crushing the Reds in a humiliating 13 - 5 barrage Monday in the loosely played finale.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trounce - to defeat thoroughly or decisively
  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over; overcome
  • Synonym: thrash - to beat decisively in a contest

3. VERB

/ krʌʃ / (AmE), / krʌʃ / (BrE)
to press or squeeze something with force so that it breaks, is damaged, or changes shape, especially into smaller pieces

Examples

But men willing to sail at all into waters where wooden ships could be crushed like eggs were hard to find.
He has already devoured huge areas of the world, putting men behind concrete walls and iron curtains and barbed wire, reducing them to slavery, systematically crushing not only their bodies but their souls, and shooting them to death if they try to escape their prison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - to break something into very small pieces or powder, especially by rubbing it between two hard surfaces
  • Synonym: smash - to break something into pieces violently

Etymology

From Old French *croissir*, of Germanic origin.
