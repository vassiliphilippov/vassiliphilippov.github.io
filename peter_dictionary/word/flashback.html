flashback

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌbak / (BrE)
A scene in a film, novel, or other work that interrupts the chronological order of the narrative to depict an earlier event.

Examples

For example, when the film is only four minutes old, Neitzbohr refers to a small, Victorian piano stool as "Wilhelmina ", and we are thereupon subjected to a flashback that informs us that this very piano stool was once used by an epileptic governess whose name, of course, was Doris (the English equivalent, when passed through middle Gaelic derivations, of Wilhelmina).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foreshadowing - A literary device in which a future event is hinted at earlier in the narrative

Etymology

Formed from 'flash' + 'back', indicating a sudden return to an earlier time.
