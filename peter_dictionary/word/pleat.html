pleat

1. NOUN

/ plit / (AmE), / pliÀêt / (BrE)
A fold in cloth made by doubling the material back on itself and securing it in place by pressing or stitching, especially as a decorative or functional feature in clothing.

Examples

She was wearing a brown cotton dress, cut across the hips in a way that was supposed to make her look slimmer, a yoke set into the skirt and flaring pleats below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - a crease produced by folding

Etymology

From Middle English 'plet', from Old French 'pleit' (fold), from Latin 'plicare' (to fold).
