mattress

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætrəs / (AmE), / ˈmætrəs / (BrE)
A large, thick pad filled with resilient material such as foam or springs, placed on a bed frame and used for sleeping or resting.

Examples

Paulie, go down 39th Street, pick up 18 mattresses and bring me the bill.

Etymology

From Arabic *maṭraḥ* (place where something is thrown), via Italian and Middle English.
