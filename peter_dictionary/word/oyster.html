oyster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
A marine mollusk with a rough, irregular shell, found on the sea bed, especially in coastal waters, some species of which are edible and harvested for food or pearls.

Examples

Thus the image of God is that which makes a man a man and not an oyster; the similitude of God, by contrast, is that which makes a man a child of God and not merely a rational creature.

Etymology

From Old French *oistre*, from Latin *ostrea*, from Greek *ostreon* (shellfish).
