expurgation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspɚˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing objectionable or inappropriate parts from a book, play, or other work before publication or presentation

Examples

The absurd notion that demythologization entails the expurgation of all mythological concepts completely misrepresents Bultmann's intention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of parts of books, films, or news

Etymology

From Latin *expurgatio* (a cleansing), from *expurgare* (to cleanse out).
