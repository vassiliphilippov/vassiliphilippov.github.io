lobster-backed

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑbstɚ bækt / (AmE), / ˈlɒbstə bækt / (BrE)
(historical, often derogatory) Of or relating to British soldiers, especially during the American Revolutionary War, so named because of their bright red uniforms.

Examples

Waited for more ships, more lobster-backed infantry, and asked what was to be done with a war of rebellion?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redcoat - British soldier during the American Revolutionary War

Etymology

Derived from 'lobster' (referring to the red shell) and 'backed', likening the soldiers' red coats to the shell of a cooked lobster.
