droop

1. VERB

/ druːp / (AmE), / druːp / (BrE)
to bend or hang down limply, especially from weakness or fatigue

Examples

Juanita drooped about the place, wearing a haunted, brooding look, which Kate attributed to the baby's death, until the day a letter came for her addressed to "Miss Juanita Fitzroy ", bearing a Grafton postmark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sag - to sink, droop, or bend downward under weight or pressure
  • Synonym: wilt - to lose strength and begin to bend down; often used for plants
  • Antonym: perk up - to become more lively or cheerful; to straighten up after drooping

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *droupen* (to droop), of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse *drúpa* (to stoop, droop).
