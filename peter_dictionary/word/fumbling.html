fumbling

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
Showing lack of skill, clumsy, or making mistakes when trying to do something.

Examples

The result has been neglect, fumbling efforts, or superficial treatment.

Synset Examples

a bungling workman
did a clumsy job
his fumbling attempt to put up a shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking skill or dexterity; awkward
  • Synonym: bungling - prone to making mistakes or being incompetent
  • Antonym: skillful - having or showing skill

Etymology

Present participle of 'fumble', from Middle English 'fomblen', probably frequentative of 'fumblen'.
