buff

1. NOUN

/ bʌf / (AmE), / bʌf / (BrE)
a person who is very interested in and knowledgeable about a particular subject; an enthusiast.

Examples

Thanks to Spoken Arts Records, history buffs may hear Lincoln's "most memorable speeches and letters "in a two disc set, interpreted by Lincoln authority and lecturer Roy P. Basler.
The jazz buff could hardly ask for more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiast - someone who is very interested in a particular activity or subject
  • Synonym: fan - an enthusiastic admirer

Etymology

Originally American English slang (late 19th century), probably from 'buff' in the sense of a buff-colored coat formerly worn by New York volunteer firemen, who were ardent followers of fires.

2. VERB

/ bʌf / (AmE), / bʌf / (BrE)
to polish (a surface) and make it shine, typically by rubbing with a cloth or a tool

Examples

Buffed those shoes to a high mirror shine.

Synset Examples

buff the wooden floors
buff my shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make something smooth and shiny by rubbing

Etymology

Originally from French 'bufle' (buffalo), referring to buff leather, which was used for polishing.
