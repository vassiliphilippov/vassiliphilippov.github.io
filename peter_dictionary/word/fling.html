fling

1. VERB

/ flɪŋ / (AmE), / flɪŋ / (BrE)
to throw something or someone with force, often in a careless or impulsive way

Examples

Cousin Simmons grabbed one of them by the shoulder and flung him away.
The head was wrapped in a turban and on top of the turban rode a great hamper across which a piece of poncho had been flung.
They spun and flung their rifles up.
In war, on a night like this, it was only the outward emotions that mattered, what could be flung out into the darkness to damage others.
Men were slaughtered every day, tumbled into eternity like so many torn parcels flung down a portable chute.

Synset Examples

fling the frisbee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurl - to throw with great force
  • Degree Lower: toss - to throw lightly or carelessly
  • Synonym: pitch - to throw or toss, especially with force
  • Degree Lower: lob - to throw in a high arc, usually gently

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *flengja* (to strike, beat).

2. VERB

/ flɪŋ / (AmE), / flɪŋ / (BrE)
to move suddenly or forcefully, especially by throwing oneself or something in an abrupt or headlong manner

Examples

It went right on creaking under his own considerable weight, and all it needed, Harold thought, was for somebody to fling himself back in a fit of laughter and that would be the end of it.

Synset Examples

He flung himself onto the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw - to propel something with force through the air by a movement of the arm and hand

Etymology

From Old Norse *flengja* (to whip, beat), related to Norwegian flenga (to dash, cut).
