unfasten

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈfæsn̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɑːsən / (BrE)
to make something that is fastened or closed become open or loose

Examples

There was no lock on the door, only an iron hook which he unfastened.

Synset Examples

unfasten your belt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fasten - to close or secure something firmly

Etymology

From 'un-' (prefix meaning not or opposite of) + 'fasten' (from Old English 'fæstnian').
