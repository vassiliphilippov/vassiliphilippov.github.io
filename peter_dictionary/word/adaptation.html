adaptation

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadapˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A written work that has been altered to fit a new form, such as a novel turned into a play, movie, or another genre.

Examples

The latest and, significantly, greatest fruit of this theatrical vine is The, an adaptation of Basho's classic frog haiku by Roger Entwhistle, a former University of Maryland chemistry instructor.

Synset Examples

the play is an adaptation of a short novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: version - a particular form of something differing from an earlier form or other forms
  • Antonym: original - the work from which an adaptation is derived

Etymology

From Latin adaptatio (a fitting to), from adaptare (to fit to).
