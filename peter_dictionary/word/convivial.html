convivial

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɪviəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɪviəl / (BrE)
(of a person or atmosphere) cheerful, sociable, and fond of the pleasures of good company.

Examples

I think I was what you might call a convivial man, and yet it was when I was alone in my studio, doing my work, that I really felt alive.

Synset Examples

a convivial atmosphere at the reunion
a woman of convivial nature
he was a real good-time Charlie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociable - willing to engage with others; friendly and outgoing
  • Antonym: unsociable - not enjoying or seeking the company of others; not friendly to socializing

Etymology

From Latin 'convivium' (banquet), from 'con-' (together) + 'vivere' (to live).
