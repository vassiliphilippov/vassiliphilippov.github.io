self-interest

1. NOUN

/ sɛlfˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / sɛlfˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
the practice of seeking personal advantage or gain without regard for the consequences for others; opportunism

Examples

The tendency to treat elections as an instrument of self-interest rather than an instrument of national interest had two important effects on electoral planning in Morocco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunism - taking advantage of opportunities without regard for others
  • Antonym: altruism - the belief in or practice of selfless concern for others
  • Synonym: selfishness - lack of consideration for others; concern only for oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'interest', both of Old English origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
regard for one's own advantage or welfare; concern for one's own interests above those of others

Examples

So let'’s just say that you'’ll pay me because it's in your interest to pay me.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: selflessness - lack of concern for one's own interests; putting others first
  • Synonym: egoism - the habit of valuing everything only in reference to one's own personal interest

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (oneself) + 'interest'; recorded since the 17th century.
