outspoken

1. ADJ

/ aʊtˈspoʊkən / (AmE), / aʊtˈspəʊkən / (BrE)
Expressing opinions or feelings freely and openly, often in a direct or forceful way.

Examples

The action centers about a group of outspoken and offbeat students sitting around a table in a cafeteria and their collective and ultimately fruitless search for a cup of hot coffee.

Synset Examples

outspoken in their opposition to segregation
a vocal assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Antonym: reserved - slow or reluctant to express one’s opinion or feelings

Etymology

From 'out' + 'spoken', meaning 'spoken out'.
