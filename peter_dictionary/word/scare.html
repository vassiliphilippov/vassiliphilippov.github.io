scare

1. VERB

/ skɛr / (AmE), / skɛə / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel frightened or afraid

Examples

He had been pretty scared himself, wondering what the hell was coming off.
Pa's scared to hell of axes ".
Maybe he only intended to scare the blackmailer, whoever he was, in which case an unloaded gun would be good enough.
I wish you luck when you try scaring that kid ".
I left it noisy, so it scares away any pain-in-the-ass innocent bystanders.

Synset Examples

The stranger who hangs around the building frightens me
Ghosts could never affright her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frighten - to make someone afraid or anxious
  • Degree Higher: terrify - to cause intense fear
  • Synonym: startle - to surprise and alarm suddenly

Etymology

Possibly from Old Norse *skirra* (to frighten off, prevent), or related to Old English *scerran* (to shun).

2. VERB

/ skɛr / (AmE), / skeə / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel frightened or to lose courage

Examples

"Scared the hell out of him ", Kahler grinned.
He, Casey, had been scared for a while, but that had come mostly from the fact that he felt responsible.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frighten - to make someone afraid or anxious
  • Synonym: daunt - to make someone feel intimidated or apprehensive
  • Antonym: encourage - to give someone confidence or courage

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *skirra* (to frighten, shun).
