pucker

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌkər / (AmE), / ˈpʌkə / (BrE)
to gather or contract into small wrinkles or folds, usually by tightening or drawing together

Examples

Pat swung into the saddle, yet still he delayed, his brows puckered.
Not one of them born... whose asshole wouldn't pucker up tighter than a snare drum when you asked them for funds.

Synset Examples

She puckered her lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - to make small lines or folds in something, usually skin or fabric
  • Antonym: smooth - to make something flat or even, removing wrinkles or folds

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *pokeren* (to poke, thrust), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌkər / (AmE), / ˈpʌkə / (BrE)
to contract or draw together into wrinkles or small folds, especially of the skin or cloth

Examples

Not one of them born... whose asshole wouldn't pucker up tighter than a snare drum when you asked them for funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to draw or bring together; cause to come together
  • Antonym: smooth - to make something flat or free from wrinkles

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly related to Dutch 'pokken' (to pock, to pit).
