reveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛvələ / (BrE)
A person who takes part in loud or lively celebrations, especially one who enjoys noisy partying.

Examples

Heavy coated, severe looking policemen sat astride noble horses along the curbside to prevent the revellers from spilling out in front of the crawling traffic.

Synset Examples

the clubs attract revelers as young as thirteen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrant - a person who participates in a celebratory event

Etymology

From Old French *reveler* (to make merry), from Latin *rebellare* (to rebel, revolt).
