rerun

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈrʌn / (BrE)
to broadcast or show (a television program, film, etc.) again after its original airing

Examples

It also happened with the Inauguration, which was not re-run at all during the evening hours, and I wrote to the TV editor of the Times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to broadcast a TV or radio program again

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + run (to show/play, as a performance or broadcast).
