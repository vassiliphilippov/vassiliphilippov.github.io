telepathy

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛpəθi / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛpəθi / (BrE)
the supposed communication of thoughts or ideas from one person to another without using the traditional five senses

Examples

Dreams present many mysteries of telepathy, clairvoyance, prevision and retrovision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clairvoyance - supposed ability to gain information about an object or event through extrasensory perception

Etymology

From French *télépathie*, from Greek *tele-* (far) and *-patheia* (feeling, perception).
