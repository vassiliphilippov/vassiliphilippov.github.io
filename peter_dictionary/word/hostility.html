hostility

1. NOUN

/ hɑˈstɪləti / (AmE), / hɒˈstɪləti / (BrE)
a very unfriendly or antagonistic attitude or feeling; the state of being hostile

Examples

Landesco thought him "just a superior sort of plugugly "but he was, in fact, with his aggression and hostility, and nerveless indifference to risking or administering pain, a casebook psychopath.
The image of the world tends to reflect the hostility and indifference of man or else to dissolve into empty spaces and overwhelming mystery.

Synset Examples

he could not conceal his hostility

Related Terms

  • Antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly; opposite of hostility
  • Synonym: animosity - strong dislike or hatred; synonym of hostility
  • Synonym: antagonism - active hostility or opposition

Etymology

From Latin 'hostilitas', from 'hostilis' (of an enemy), from 'hostis' (enemy).
