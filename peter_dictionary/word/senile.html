senile

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsiːnaɪl / (BrE)
(of a person) having or showing the weaknesses or diseases of old age, especially a loss of mental faculties.

Examples

Definite information on the growth of senile individuals is lacking.

Synset Examples

his mother was doddering and frail

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vigorous - strong and healthy, especially in older age

Etymology

From Latin *senilis* (of old age), from *senex* (old man).
