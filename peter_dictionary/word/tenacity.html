tenacity

1. NOUN

/ təˈnæsəti / (AmE), / tɪˈnæsəti / (BrE)
The quality of being very determined and persistent in achieving something despite difficulties or obstacles.

Examples

The foregoing, aided by several clues I 'll withhold to keep you on your toes, will pursue you with a tenacity worthy of Inspector Javert, but before they close in, gird yourselves, I repeat, for a vengeance infinitely more pitiless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perseverance - continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties
  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being firm in achieving a goal
  • Synonym: resilience - the ability to recover quickly from difficulties
  • Antonym: indecision - inability to make up one's mind
  • Synonym: persistence - firm or obstinate continuance in a course of action

Etymology

From Latin tenacitas, from tenax (holding fast, tenacious), from tenere (to hold).
