sanity

1. NOUN

/ ˈsænəti / (AmE), / ˈsænɪti / (BrE)
The state of having a healthy and sound mind; the ability to think and behave in a normal and rational manner.

Examples

I would have stood there and died there if left to myself, but Cousin Simmons grabbed my arm in his viselike grip and fairly plucked me out of there; and then I came to some sanity and plunged away with such extraordinary speed that I outdistanced Cousin Simmons by far.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insanity - the state of being seriously mentally ill; extreme foolishness

Etymology

From Latin sanitas (health, sanity), from sanus (healthy).
