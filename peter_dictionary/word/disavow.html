disavow

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈvaʊ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈvaʊ / (BrE)
to deny any responsibility for, knowledge of, or connection with something

Examples

I had nothing to do with it.

Synset Examples

Her husband disavowed her after 30 years of marriage and six children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deny - to state that something is not true or to refuse to accept or admit something

Etymology

From Old French desavouer, from des- (dis-) + avouer (to avow).
