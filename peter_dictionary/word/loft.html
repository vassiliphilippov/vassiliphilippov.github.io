loft

1. NOUN

/ lɔft / (AmE), / lɒft / (BrE)
an open space or room just below the roof of a house or building, often used for storage or as living space.

Examples

The children had to play away from the house (in the barn loft or the pasture behind the barn), to maintain a proper quietness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attic - a space or room just below the roof of a building, often used for storage

Etymology

From Old Norse 'lopt' (air, upper room); related to Old English 'lyft' (air).

2. NOUN

/ lɑft / (AmE), / lɒft / (BrE)
a large, open floor space above a factory, warehouse, or other commercial building, often used for storage, work, or converted into living space

Examples

It was a district of small factories and loft buildings and occasional tenements, and he could see the police radio car as he rounded the corner and slammed on the brakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attic - space or room just below the roof of a building, often used for storage

Etymology

From Old Norse 'lopt', meaning 'air, sky, upper room'.
