dishonour

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈɑːnər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnə / (BrE)
to bring shame or disgrace upon someone or something

Examples

When Mayor Dever heard of the banquet he summoned Hughes for an explanation of why he had been dishonouring the police department by consorting with these felons and fixers.
I gave her freedom, but I taught her never to dishonour her family.

Synset Examples

he dishonored his family by committing a serious crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - to cause someone to lose reputation or respect
  • Antonym: honour - to show respect or bring pride to someone or something
  • Synonym: shame - to cause someone to feel ashamed

Etymology

From Old French 'deshonorer', from Latin 'dehonorare' (to deprive of honor).
