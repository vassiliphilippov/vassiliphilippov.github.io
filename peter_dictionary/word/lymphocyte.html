lymphocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪm.foʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪm.fə.saɪt / (BrE)
A type of white blood cell that is part of the immune system, usually making up about a quarter of the total white blood cell count and increasing in response to infection.

Examples

Microscopically, both kidneys showed many small cortical scars in which there was glomerular and interstitial fibrosis, tubular atrophy, and an infiltration of lymphocytes and plasma cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukocyte - a white blood cell

Etymology

From Greek 'lympha' (water) and 'kytos' (cell).
