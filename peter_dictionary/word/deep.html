deep

1. ADJ

/ dip / (AmE), / diːp / (BrE)
far from the outer edge or surface; situated at a great distance inward, backward, or from a starting point in space or time.

Examples

If he were to go with White, he would be out there two days, not just listening in the dark at some point between here and Papa-san, but moving ever deeper into enemy land - behind Papa-san itself.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shallow - not far from the surface or edge

Etymology

From Old English dēop, of Germanic origin.
