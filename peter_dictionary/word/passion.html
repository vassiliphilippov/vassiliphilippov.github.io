passion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ən / (BrE)
An intense, irrational drive or obsession that motivates a belief or action, often overriding reason.

Examples

This passion brings on a fit which proves fatal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mania - an excessive enthusiasm or obsession; mental illness marked by hyperactivity or impulse
  • Synonym: obsession - an idea or thought that continually preoccupies or intrudes on a person's mind

Etymology

From Latin *passio* (suffering, emotion), from *patī* (to suffer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən / (BrE)
A strong enthusiasm, interest, or devotion for something or someone.

Examples

Music could not be Eugene's passion.

Synset Examples

the theater was her first love
he has a passion for cock fighting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: love - a strong feeling of affection and care toward something or someone
  • Synonym: devotion - strong loyalty or dedication to a cause, person, or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'passio' (suffering, feeling), from 'patī' (to suffer).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈpæʃ.ən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of sexual desire or attraction

Examples

Mauve colored mouths that had never known anything sweeter than the taste of new wine and the passion of man's tongue had not smiled, but had condemned again and again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lust - strong sexual desire
  • Degree Lower: infatuation - intense but short-lived passion or admiration

Etymology

From Latin *passio* (suffering, feeling), from *patior* (to suffer, feel).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpæʃən / (AmE), / ˈpæʃən / (BrE)
A very strong feeling or emotion, such as love, anger, or enthusiasm.

Examples

Being somewhat delicate in health, at the age of sixteen he was sent to Southern Europe, for which he at once developed a passion, so that he spent nearly all of the following ten years abroad, at first in Italy, then in Greece, Egypt, Asia Minor, and Palestine.
I submit that this was not a hot-blooded crime of passion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Synonym: emotion - a strong feeling such as love, anger, joy, hate, or fear
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'passio' (suffering, feeling), from 'pati' (to suffer, endure).
