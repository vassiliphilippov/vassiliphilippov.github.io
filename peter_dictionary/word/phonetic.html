phonetic

1. ADJ

/ fəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the sounds of human speech or their representation.

Examples

Some phonetic features, for example glottal catch or murmur, are sometimes to be assigned to segmental phonemics and sometimes to accentual systems.
Phonemes connected by a morphophonemic rule commonly show a good bit of phonetic similarity, possible because of the several dimensions of contrast in the system.

Synset Examples

phonetic transcription

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonological - relating to the systems and patterns of sounds in language
  • Antonym: orthographic - relating to the written spelling of words

Etymology

From Greek phōnētikos (vocal, relating to sound), from phōnē (voice, sound).
