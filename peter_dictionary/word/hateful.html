hateful

1. ADJ

/ ˈheɪt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪt.fəl / (BrE)
showing or motivated by hatred or malice; full of hate or animosity.

Examples

Ludie could be hateful.

Synset Examples

a hateful thing to do
in a mean mood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm; spiteful
  • Antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature

Etymology

From Middle English hateful, from hate + -ful.

2. ADJ

/ ˈheɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪtfəl / (BrE)
Arousing hatred or deserving to be hated.

Examples

She smiled at Winston, and he saw the hateful hard glitter in her eyes.
I think you 're mean and hateful and stupid, and - louder "? said Joyce.

Synset Examples

no vice is universally as hateful as ingratitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detestable - deserving intense dislike or hatred
  • Synonym: loathsome - causing hatred or disgust
  • Antonym: adorable - deserving adoration or affection

Etymology

From Middle English 'hatful', from 'hate' + '-ful'.
