Marxist

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑɹksɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɑːksɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or following the political and economic theories of Karl Marx (and typically Friedrich Engels), often advocating class struggle and the abolition of private property.

Examples

The long road that had taken liberals in this country into the social religion of democracy, into a worship of man, led logically to the Marxist dream of a classless society under a Socialist State.
And by the time the war ended, liberal leadership in this country was spiritually Marxist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - Relating to or supporting communism
  • Antonym: capitalist - Relating to capitalism or capitalists

Etymology

From the name Karl Marx (1818–1883), German philosopher and economist, plus the suffix -ist.
