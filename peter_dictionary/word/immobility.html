immobility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmoʊˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪməʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of not moving or being unable to move.

Examples

Where their sharp edges seemed restless as sea waves thrusting themselves upward in angry motion, Papa-san sat glacier like, his smooth solidity, his very immobility defying all the turmoil about him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobility - lack of movement or ability to move
  • Antonym: mobility - ability to move freely or easily
  • Synonym: stillness - the absence of movement or sound

Etymology

From Latin 'immobilitas', from 'immobilis' (not movable).
