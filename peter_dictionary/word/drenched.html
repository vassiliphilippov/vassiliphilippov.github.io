drenched

1. ADJ

/ drɛntʃt / (AmE), / drɛn(t)ʃt / (BrE)
Soaked or thoroughly covered with liquid or another substance; often used figuratively to describe being filled with something.

Examples

Mynheer, Sir Francis, the valley society, the very smell of the river on his right purling along to the bay past fish weirs and rocks, and ahead the sleepy ribbon of moon drenched road.

Synset Examples

drenched in moonlight
moon-drenched meadows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaked - very wet; saturated
  • Antonym: dry - not wet or moist

Etymology

From Old French *drenchier* (to drench), from Proto-Germanic *drankijaną* (to make drink, to drown).
