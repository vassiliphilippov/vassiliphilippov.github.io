snuggle

1. VERB

/ ˈsnʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsnʌɡl / (BrE)
to move into or arrange oneself in a warm, comfortable, or affectionate position, often close to someone or something

Examples

He snuggled into the blanket * * h Brandon dreamed.
"I think I 'll sleep in this morning ", she said drowsily, and as she snuggled against him, he wondered if she ever went to church.

Synset Examples

We cuddled against each other to keep warm
The children snuggled into their sleeping bags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuddle - to hold close in one's arms for warmth or affection
  • Synonym: nestle - to settle or lie comfortably within or against something

Etymology

Probably a frequentative formation based on 'snug' (cosy, comfortable).
