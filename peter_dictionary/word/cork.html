cork

1. NOUN

/ kɔrk / (AmE), / kɔːk / (BrE)
the outer bark of the cork oak tree, typically used for making bottle stoppers and other products

Examples

In the casual field straws feature wedge heels of cork or carved wood in a variety of styles.

Etymology

From Spanish and Portuguese 'corcho', from Latin 'quercus' (oak).

2. NOUN

/ kɔrk / (AmE), / kɔːk / (BrE)
a cylindrical or tapered stopper used to seal the mouth of a bottle, traditionally made from the bark of the cork oak tree

Examples

[CHAMPAGNE CORK POPPING] [PEOPLE LAUGHING] [SHRIEKS] [PEOPLE CHEERING] [MUSIC PLAYING] [SOFTLY]

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper - an object used to block or seal the opening of a container
  • Synonym: cap - a cover or lid for closing a container

Etymology

From Old Spanish *corcho* via Latin *corticem* (bark, especially of the cork oak).
