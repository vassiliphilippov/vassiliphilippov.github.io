Muse

1. PROPN

/ mjuːz / (AmE), / mjuːz / (BrE)
(in Greek and Roman mythology) Any of the nine goddesses, daughters of Zeus and Mnemosyne, presiding over the arts and sciences.

Examples

"I asked her why she could n't do it tomorrow, but it seems the muse is working good tonight and she's afraid to let it go ".

Etymology

From Greek *Mousa* (Μοῦσα), name of the goddesses of the arts.
