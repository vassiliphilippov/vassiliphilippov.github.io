bursitis

1. NOUN

/ bɝˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / bɜːˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of a bursa, typically causing pain and swelling, especially in joints such as the shoulder or elbow.

Examples

Recently many doctors have installed ultrasonic vibration machines for deep massage of bruises, contusions, and simple bursitis.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from 'bursa' (Latin for 'purse', meaning 'sac' in anatomy) + '-itis' (Greek, denoting inflammation).
