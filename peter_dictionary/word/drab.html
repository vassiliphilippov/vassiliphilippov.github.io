drab

1. ADJ

/ dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
Dull and lacking in brightness or color; not interesting or cheerful.

Examples

Burly leathered men and wrinkled women in drab black rags carried on in a primitive way, almost unchanged from feudal times.
It was like some beautiful bird flapped into our drab little cage and made those walls dissolve away.
But still... the place you live in is that much more drab and empty that they're gone.

Synset Examples

drab faded curtains
sober Puritan grey
children in somber brown clothes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; vivid in color
  • Antonym: colorful - having much or varied color; lively in appearance

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French *drabe* (dirty, scabby), or possibly of Scandinavian origin.

2. ADJ

/ dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
dull and lacking brightness, liveliness, or interest; not interesting or exciting.

Examples

"One of the things which makes our lives drab and empty and which leaves us, at the end of the day, fatigued and deflated spiritually is the pressure of the taxing, practical, utilitarian concern of common-sense objects.
I take the central meaning here to be the contrast between the drab empty quality of life without literature and a life enriched by it.

Synset Examples

her drab personality
life was drab compared with the more exciting life style overseas
a series of dreary dinner parties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; boring
  • Synonym: dreary - dull, bleak, and lifeless; depressing
  • Antonym: colorful - full of interest; lively and exciting
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images; lively

Etymology

Possibly from Middle Dutch *drabbe* (mud, dregs), originally meaning 'muddy' or 'dull-colored'.
