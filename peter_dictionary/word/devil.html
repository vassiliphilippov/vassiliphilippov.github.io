devil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
An evil supernatural being, especially one believed to be the enemy of God and humanity in various religions.

Examples

Aside from a quaint concern with witches and devils which provides the immediate problem in the opening scene, it is a quite normal community.
Instead, the audience can sit back at ease and, from the perspective of an enlightened time which no longer believes in such things, enjoy the dead seriousness with which the characters in the play take the witches and devils which are under discussion.
I wore a new double-breasted brown worsted suit with a faint herringbone design and wide lapels like a devil's ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demon - an evil spirit or supernatural being
  • Antonym: angel - a benevolent supernatural being

Etymology

From Old English 'deofol', from Latin 'diabolus' (devil), from Greek 'diabolos' (slanderer, accuser).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl / (BrE)
In Judeo-Christian and Islamic belief, the chief spirit of evil and enemy of God; the tempter of humankind and ruler of Hell.

(used in exclamations) an intensifier expressing surprise, confusion, or annoyance, often in the phrases 'what the devil', 'why the devil', etc.
