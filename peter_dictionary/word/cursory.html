cursory

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɝː.sɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.səri / (BrE)
Done quickly with little attention to detail; not thorough.

Examples

From even a cursory inspection of its many aspects, however, the historian can deduce several fundamental conclusions about the progress of the Aegean world down to 800 B.C.

Synset Examples

a casual (or cursory) inspection failed to reveal the house's structural flaws
a passing glance
perfunctory courtesy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thorough - done carefully and completely
  • Synonym: perfunctory - carried out with minimum effort or reflection

Etymology

From Medieval Latin cursorius (of running), from Latin currere (to run).
