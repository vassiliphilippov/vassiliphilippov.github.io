bipartisan

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈpɑːr.tɪ.zən / (AmE), / baɪˈpɑː.tɪ.zæn / (BrE)
Involving the agreement, cooperation, or support of two opposing political parties.

Examples

You can see it, for example, in the extensive efforts President Kennedy has made to enlist solid bipartisan support for his actions toward both Cuba and Laos; efforts, as I see it, which are being directed, by the way, toward support for future actions, not for those already past.

Synset Examples

a two-way treaty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partisan - strong supporter of a party, cause, or person

Etymology

From bi- meaning 'two' and 'partisan', originally from Middle French or Italian roots.
