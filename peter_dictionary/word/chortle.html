chortle

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɔːr.təl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔː.təl / (BrE)
to laugh in a joyful, gleeful, or quietly restrained way

Examples

He heard himself chortling.
"Those lovely whorls ", he chortled.
The good man chortled appreciatively and decided the trip was worth his time.
The police captain chortled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously

Etymology

Coined by Lewis Carroll in 'Through the Looking-Glass' (1871), likely a blend of 'chuckle' and 'snort'.
