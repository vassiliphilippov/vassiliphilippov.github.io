awhile

1. ADV

/ əˈwaɪl / (AmE), / əˈwaɪl / (BrE)
For a short period of time.

Examples

But I want this to sink in awhile.

Synset Examples

sit down and stay awhile
they settled awhile in Virginia before moving West
the baby was quiet for a while

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefly - for a short time
  • Synonym: momentarily - for a very short time; at any moment

Etymology

From Middle English a while, literally 'a while', influenced by use as a single word.
