snag

1. VERB

/ snæɡ / (AmE), / snæɡ / (BrE)
(of an object or fabric) to become caught or torn on something sharp or rough.

Examples

The lock insures that the points are thrown all the way with no chance that a wheel flange will snag on a partly thrown point.
Draw file the edges, square up the ends and put a slight chamfer on the edges so they will not snag in the frame.

Synset Examples

I snagged my stocking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - to become entangled or held fast
  • Degree Higher: tear - to damage by pulling apart or causing a hole
