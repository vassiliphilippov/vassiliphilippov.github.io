clump

1. NOUN

/ klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)
A compact group or cluster of things, such as trees, bushes, or other similar items, growing or located close together.

Examples

At times, clumps of 10 to 15 closely packed nuclei were also observed.
Clumps of brush that they passed were so many enchained demons straining in anger to tear and gnaw on his bones.
Prevot came up "Take that spot over there ", he whispered, pointing to a small clump of blackness.
Lying in the grass behind the brush clump, Warren looked about.
There was a clump of trees that appeared to provide cover right up to the road, and the shouting and gunfire never slackened.

Synset Examples

a bunch of trees
a cluster of admirers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluster - a group of similar things or people positioned or occurring closely together
  • Synonym: bunch - a number of things of the same kind, growing or held together

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound or shape of a mass or lump.

2. NOUN

/ klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)
a compact mass of something, especially of earth, mud, or hair, forming a lump or cluster

Examples

Clumps of brush rode down the ribbon.

Synset Examples

a ball of mud caught him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass of a substance, especially one without a definite or regular shape
  • Synonym: cluster - a group of similar things or people positioned or occurring closely together

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, referring to a heavy sound or mass.
