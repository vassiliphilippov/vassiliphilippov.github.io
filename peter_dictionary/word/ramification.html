ramification

1. NOUN

/ ˌræməfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of branching out or forming branches; division into branches or subdivisions

Examples

Some of this may prove to be true, or even considerable of it, whether by genetic ramification or by diffusion and coalescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branching - the action of separating into branches

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ramificatio, from Latin ramus (branch) + facere (to make).
