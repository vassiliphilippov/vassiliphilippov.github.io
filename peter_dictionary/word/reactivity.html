reactivity

1. NOUN

/ riˌækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / riːˌækˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance to undergo a chemical reaction, or the degree to which it reacts chemically

Examples

Even here there is room for some variation, for metal surfaces vary in smoothness, absorptive capacity, and chemical reactivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inertness - lack of reactivity; resistance to chemical change
  • Synonym: reactive - having a tendency to react chemically

Etymology

From react + -ivity, modeled on words like 'activity'.
