rot

1. NOUN

/ rɑt / (AmE), / rɒt / (BrE)
the process of decay or decomposition of organic matter, typically caused by bacteria or fungi

Examples

First of all there was the parsonage, an utterly impossible place for civilized people to live in, originally poorly conceived, apparently not repaired for years, with no plumbing or sewage, with rat-holes and rot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
  • Synonym: putrefaction - decomposition of organic matter, especially producing a foul smell
  • Antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh, not decayed

Etymology

From Old English rotian (to rot), related to Old Norse rot (decay) and Old High German roz (decay).

2. VERB

/ rɑt / (AmE), / rɒt / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decay or decompose, especially as a result of the action of bacteria or fungi.

Examples

* * h Automatically, Finnegan started going over today's lesson * * h Capitalism rots from the core.
A successful businessman recently prefaced his address to a luncheon group with the statement that all economists should be sent to the hospitals for the mentally deranged where they and their theories might rot together.
Diving boards must have non-skid surfaces (coco matting takes an awful beating from chlorine and rots quickly, but grit-impregnated paints are excellent).

Synset Examples

The bodies decomposed in the heat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decompose - to break down into simpler substances, especially through decay
  • Synonym: putrefy - to decay or rot and produce a foul smell
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something from decomposing or decaying

Etymology

From Old English *rotian* (to decay), related to Old Norse *rotna* (to rot) and Middle Dutch *roten*.

3. NOUN

/ rɑt / (AmE), / rɒt / (BrE)
A state of decay, typically with an unpleasant smell, caused especially by the action of bacteria or fungi on organic matter.

Examples

Even the stable garage, which housed nothing now but the scent of rot, had a lawn before it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - gradual decomposition of organic matter
  • Synonym: putrefaction - decomposition of body tissue producing a foul smell

Etymology

From Middle English 'rot', from Old Norse 'rot', related to 'rotna' (to rot).
