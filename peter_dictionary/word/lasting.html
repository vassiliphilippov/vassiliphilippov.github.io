lasting

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
Continuing or enduring for a long time without significant change.

Examples

It's not too much to assume that you will have a more lasting interest in paper hats than will Mr. Average Citizen.
They come prepared for family fun because Americans in ever growing numbers are learning that here is the way to a fine economical vacation that becomes a family experience of lasting importance.

Synset Examples

permanent secretary to the president
permanent address
literature of permanent value

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
  • Synonym: enduring - continuing or long-lasting
  • Antonym: transient - lasting only for a short time

Etymology

From Middle English lasten (to endure), from Old English læstan (to perform, follow).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
continuing to exist or have an effect for a long time; enduring

Examples

Though they would produce some very memorable and lasting songs, Arlen and Mercer were not given strong material to work on.

Synset Examples

hopes for a durable peace
a long-lasting friendship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
  • Synonym: enduring - continuing or long-lasting
  • Degree Higher: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Middle English 'lasten' (to endure, continue), from Old English 'lǣstan'.
