rasp

1. NOUN

/ ræsp / (AmE), / rɑːsp / (BrE)
a harsh, grating sound, especially one made in an irritated or rough tone

Examples

Cardboard noisemakers, substitutes for the unavailable tin models, were being hawked and bought at makeshift stands every few yards along Broadway, and one's ears were continually serenaded by the horns' rasps and bleats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - a low, rough, harsh sound

Etymology

From Old French *rasper* (to scrape), from a Germanic root meaning 'to scrape'.
