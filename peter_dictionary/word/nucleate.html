nucleate

1. VERB

/ ˈnuːkliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliˌeɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance or cell) to form a nucleus or cause to form a nucleus.

Examples

Some of the sinusoids contained large numbers of nucleated red cells, and cells of the granulocytic series were found in small numbers.

Synset Examples

Some cells had nucleated

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel).
