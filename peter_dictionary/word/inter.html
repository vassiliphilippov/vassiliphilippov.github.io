inter

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜr / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː / (BrE)
to place a dead body in a grave or tomb; to bury

Examples

In this true lover's tomb interred A world of love lies sepulchred ".

Synset Examples

Stalin was buried behind the Kremlin wall on Red Square
The pharaohs were entombed in the pyramids
My grandfather was laid to rest last Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bury - to place a dead body in the ground
  • Synonym: entomb - to place in a tomb

Etymology

From Latin 'interrare' (to put in the earth), from 'in-' (in) + 'terra' (earth).
