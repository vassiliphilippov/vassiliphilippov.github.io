weatherstrip

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛðərˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌstrɪp / (BrE)
To fit a door, window, or other opening with a strip that seals out air, water, or dust.

Examples

Insulate, weatherstrip, double-glaze to the maximum.

Synset Examples

The window must be weatherstripped before the cold weather sets in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to close tightly so that air, liquid, etc., cannot get in or out

Etymology

From 'weather' + 'strip,' referring to a strip used to counteract weather effects.
