reinstall

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to install something again, especially after it has been removed or repaired

Examples

When he was back on his feet again and about to reinstall the fuses, however, he hesitated.

Synset Examples

She reinstalled the washer after it had been repaired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: install - to put a piece of equipment in place and make it ready to use

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + install.
