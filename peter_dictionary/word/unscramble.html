unscramble

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈskræmbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈskræmbəl / (BrE)
to restore something to a clear, intelligible, or organized state; to make sense of something that is confused or jumbled

Examples

Until now, Lilac Gaylor and Lila Kingsley had been like an anagram which he could unscramble at his own pace and choosing.

Synset Examples

Can you unscramble the message?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decode - to convert a coded message into intelligible language
  • Antonym: scramble - to mix up or jumble

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'scramble'.
