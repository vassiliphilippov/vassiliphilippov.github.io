gilt

1. ADJ

/ ɡɪlt / (AmE), / ɡɪlt / (BrE)
having a golden or gold-colored appearance, especially with a slightly brownish tone

Examples

And a gray-haired man whose glance - direct, lifelike, and mildly accusing - was contradicted by the gilt and black frame.
The gilt sterns of the men-of-war becoming clearer to him, the sides of the wooden sea walls alternately painted yellow and black, the bronze cannon at the ports.

Synset Examples

long aureate (or golden) hair
a gold carpet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden - having the color or shine of gold

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'gild' (to cover with a thin layer of gold), from Old English 'gyldan'.
