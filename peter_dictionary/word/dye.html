dye

1. VERB

/ daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
to change the color of something, especially fabric or hair, by using a special substance called dye

Examples

Her hair was dyed, and her bloom was fading, and she must have been crowding forty, but she seemed to be one of those women who cling to the manners and graces of a pretty child of eight.

Synset Examples

Please dye these shoes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bleach - to remove color from something (opposite of dye)
  • Synonym: stain - to color a surface with a substance, often resulting in a spot or discoloration

Etymology

From Old English deāgian, from deāg (dye, color).
