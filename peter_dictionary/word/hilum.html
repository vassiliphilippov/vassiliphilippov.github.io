hilum

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈhɪləm / (BrE)
A small depression or fissure in an organ where blood vessels, nerves, or ducts enter or leave.

Examples

The pulmonary vein, however, without the limiting supportive tissue septa as in type 1,, follows a more direct path to the hilum and does not maintain this close relationship (figs. 8, 22).

Synset Examples

the hilus of the kidney

Etymology

From Latin 'hilum' meaning a small thing or trifle, used medically for 'a notch or opening in an organ'.
