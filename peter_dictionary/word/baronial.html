baronial

1. ADJ

/ bəˈroʊniəl / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊniəl / (BrE)
Impressive and grand in appearance, often resembling or characteristic of the style associated with barons or the nobility.

Examples

They ate the cafeteria food with its orange sauces and Scotty gazed without interest at his food, the teachers, the heroic baronial windows, and the bright ranks of college banners.

Synset Examples

a baronial mansion
an imposing residence
a noble tree
severe-looking policemen sat astride noble horses
stately columns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stately - having a dignified, unhurried, and grand manner or appearance
  • Synonym: noble - having or showing fine personal qualities or high moral principles; impressive in appearance
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; of low social, administrative, or political rank

Etymology

From French 'baronial', from 'baron' (nobleman).
