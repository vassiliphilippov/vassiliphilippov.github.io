Mafia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæf.i.ə / (BrE)
an organized criminal society originating in Sicily, Italy, known for secrecy, violence, and illicit activities; often extended to similar criminal groups.

Examples

That no proof linking me to any criminal conspiracy whether it is called Mafia or Cosa Nostra or whatever other name you wish to give has ever been made public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cosa Nostra - the Sicilian Mafia; another name for the organization

Etymology

From Italian 'mafia', of uncertain origin; first used in Sicily

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæf.i.ə / (BrE)
A secret criminal organization, especially one based in Italy or the United States, involved in organized crime such as extortion, racketeering, and illegal activities, structured in families.

Examples

He has stated that you are head of the most powerful Mafia family in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - an organized group of criminals; synonym for Mafia in American English

Etymology

From Italian 'mafia', of uncertain origin, first used in reference to Sicilian criminal groups.
