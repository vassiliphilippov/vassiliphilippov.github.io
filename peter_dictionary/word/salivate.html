salivate

1. VERB

/ ˈsælɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈsælɪveɪt / (BrE)
to produce saliva, especially in anticipation of food or as a physical response

Examples

For example, probably very few people know that the word "visrhanik "that is bantered about so much today stems from the verb "bouanahsha ": to salivate.

Synset Examples

We salivated when he described the great meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - to let saliva flow outside the mouth, often unintentionally

Etymology

From French *saliver*, from Latin *saliva* (saliva, spittle).
