peripherally

1. ADV

/ pəˈrɪfɚəli / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfərəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is situated at the edge or outer parts of something, or in a less important or minor role.

Examples

In type 3, this general relationship is maintained peripherally but not centrally where the pulmonary vein follows a more independent path to the hilum as is the case throughout the lung in type 2,.
Further, the gland is able to re-use a larger fraction of the thyroid hormone de-iodinated peripherally.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centrally - in or at the center; in a main or important role

Etymology

From 'peripheral' + '-ly', where 'peripheral' comes from Greek 'peripheria' (circumference).
