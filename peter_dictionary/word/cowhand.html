cowhand

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌhænd / (BrE)
a person employed to tend cattle, especially on horseback, on a ranch

Examples

Such ranchers as Coble and Clay and the Bosler brothers carried him on their books as a cowhand even while he was receiving a much larger salary from parties unknown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a man who tends cattle and performs ranch work on horseback
  • Synonym: ranch hand - a hired worker on a ranch

Etymology

Formed from 'cow' (cattle) and 'hand' (worker, helper).
