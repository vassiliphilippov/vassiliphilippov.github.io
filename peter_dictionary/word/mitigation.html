mitigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪt̬ɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making an offense or wrongdoing appear less serious or less blameworthy.

Examples

There will be no mitigation of these offences until all art schools, whether independent or attached to universities have separate accreditation - as do medical schools - by an art accreditation group such as the "National Association of Schools of Art ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extenuation - the act of making an offense seem less serious

Etymology

From Latin mitigatio (a softening), from mitigare (to soften).
