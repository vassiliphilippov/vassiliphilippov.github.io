placement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
The act or process of placing something, or the way in which things are arranged or positioned in a particular space.

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of finding a job or position for someone, especially through an agency or program

Examples

Up to this time and for the next eight years, the services provided disabled persons consisted mainly of training, counseling, and placement on a job.
Under P. L. 113, 78 th Congress, the Federal Government assumed responsibility for 100 % of necessary State expenditures in connection with administration and the counseling and placement of the disabled, and for 50 % of the necessary costs of providing clients with rehabilitation case services.

Synset Examples

the agency provided placement services

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employment - the condition of having paid work
  • Synonym: recruitment - the process of finding people to work for a company or become a member of an organization
  • Antonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from employment

Etymology

From the French *placement*, from placer (to place).
