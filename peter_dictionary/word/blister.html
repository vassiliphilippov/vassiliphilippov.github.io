blister

1. VERB

/ ˈblɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə / (BrE)
(of skin or a surface) to form blisters; to develop raised, fluid-filled spots as a result of heat, friction, or injury.

Examples

Being blistered for curbs has delayed his work somewhat.
Blistered for curbs and laid off three weeks, he is coming along fine and looks like a pacer to me.

Synset Examples

Her feet blistered during the long hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister - a small bubble on the skin, filled with liquid, caused by friction, burning, etc.
