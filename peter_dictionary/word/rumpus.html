rumpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmpəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌmpəs / (BrE)
A noisy commotion or disturbance, often involving confusion or disorder.

Examples

When a witness at court was asked if he had been kicked in the ensuing rumpus, he replied, "No, it was in the stomach ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of noise or disturbance
