operative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːpərətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
(of a law, rule, system, or principle) having effect; in force or in action.

Examples

The spirit of the Marine Corps was present and operative before any of the present members of it came into it.

Synset Examples

operative regulations
the major tendencies operative in the American political system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effective - successfully producing the intended result; in effect
  • Synonym: active - having an effect; currently in operation
  • Antonym: inactive - not currently in force or effect

Etymology

From Latin *operativus* (active, effective), from *operari* (to work).
