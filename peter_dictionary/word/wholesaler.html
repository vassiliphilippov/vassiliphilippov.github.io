wholesaler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊlˌseɪlər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪlə / (BrE)
A person or company that buys goods in large quantities from producers or manufacturers and sells them to retailers or other businesses, rather than to individual consumers.

Examples

SBA loans, which may be made to small manufacturers, small business pools, wholesalers, retailers, service establishments and other small businesses (when financing is not otherwise available to them on reasonable terms), are to finance business construction, conversion, or expansion; the purchase of equipment, facilities, machinery, supplies, or materials; or to supply working capital.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retailer - a person or business that sells goods to the final consumer

Etymology

Formed from "whole" + "sale" + "-er", indicating one who deals in wholesale goods.
