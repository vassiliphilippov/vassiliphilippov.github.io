unification

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of joining or combining two or more things into one entity; the process of making or becoming united or whole

Examples

National identification was not new, but it was accelerating in intensity and scope throughout Europe as new unifications occurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the act of joining two or more things together to form a single entity
  • Synonym: fusion - the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity
  • Antonym: division - the action or process of separating into parts or groups

Etymology

From French *unification*, from Medieval Latin *unificatio* (making into one), from Latin *unus* (one) + *facere* (to make).
