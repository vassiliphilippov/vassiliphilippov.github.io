pimp

1. NOUN

/ pɪmp / (AmE), / pɪmp / (BrE)
a person who controls sex workers and arranges clients for them, usually taking a portion of the earnings

Examples

The place smelt of some kind of hair lotion these pimp like characters use.
I 'd thought him a pimp or procurer but he did n't seem to be.
Greene was in actuality a young ruffian from Kent, who had broken with his parents in order to keep the company he preferred - pimps, panders and whores.
Tattaglia's a pimp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procurer - person who procures clients for sex workers
  • Synonym: madam - a woman who manages a brothel

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'pimpern' (to dress up elegantly), or from French 'pimpant' (alluring, seductive).
