coalesce

1. VERB

/ koʊəˈlɛs / (AmE), / kəʊəˈlɛs / (BrE)
(of separate elements) to come together and form one mass or whole; to fuse or unite.

Examples

As cells coalesced into organisms, they built new "unnatural "and internally controlled environments to cope even more successfully with the entropy increasing properties of the external world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merge - to combine or blend into a single entity
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to set or keep apart

Etymology

From Latin 'coalescere', from 'co-' (together) + 'alescere' (to grow).

2. VERB

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɛs / (BrE)
to come together and form one mass or whole; to join or blend to form a single entity

Examples

When the smaller facet-planes of Analytical Cubism were placed upon or juxtaposed with the large, dense shapes formed by the affixed materials of the collage, they had to coalesce - become "synthesized "- into larger planar shapes themselves simply in order to maintain the integrity of the picture plane.
There was considerable evidence of a tacit rapprochement with Castro in Cuba, previously a bete noire to Trujillo - thus illustrating the way in which totalitarianism of the right and left coalesces.

Synset Examples

The colors blend well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix two or more substances together so that they combine
  • Synonym: merge - to combine or cause to combine to form a single entity
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be moved apart

Etymology

From Latin 'coalescere' (to grow together): 'co-' (together) + 'alescere' (to grow).
