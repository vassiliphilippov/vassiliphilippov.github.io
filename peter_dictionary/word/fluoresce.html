fluoresce

1. VERB

/ flʊəˈrɛs / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɛs / (BrE)
to emit light while exposed to radiation such as ultraviolet light, typically by absorbing energy and re-emitting it as visible light

Examples

This material fluoresces under ultraviolet light which facilitates its sampling and assessment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphoresce - to emit light after exposure to radiation, continuing after the radiation stops

Etymology

From 'fluorescence', which is from the mineral name 'fluorite' plus '-escence'.
