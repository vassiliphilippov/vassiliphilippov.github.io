malign

1. ADJ

/ məˈlaɪn / (AmE), / məˈlaɪn / (BrE)
Having or showing an evil or harmful nature or influence.

Examples

You have unwittingly set in motion forces so malign, so vindictive, that it would be downright inhumane of me not to warn you about them.

Synset Examples

prompted by malign motives
believed in witches and malign spirits
gave him a malign look
a malign lesion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benign - having a kindly or harmless nature or influence
  • Synonym: malignant - dangerously harmful; malevolent

Etymology

From Latin malignus (wicked, malicious), from malus (bad) + -genus (born).
