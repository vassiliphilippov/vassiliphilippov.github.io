out-of-bounds

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv baʊndz / (AmE), / aʊt əv baʊndz / (BrE)
(in sports) outside the area of play or beyond the permitted boundary lines.

Examples

The ball went off in a majestic arc, an out-of-bounds slice.
He tried again and once more sliced out of bounds.
He hooked the next two out of bounds on the opposite side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in-bounds - within the permitted playing area (sports)

Etymology

Formed from 'out of' + 'bounds', where 'bounds' means boundaries or limits.
