completion

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpliʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
(In American football) a forward pass that is successfully caught by an eligible receiver.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpliʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of finishing something; a concluding action that makes something complete

Examples

With completion of filling, net-like anastomoses were noted to be present between these separately derived branches.
If the distant patron of the suburban branch has been frightened away from downtown by traffic problems, however, the city store can only pressure the politicians to do something about the highways or await the completion of the federal highway program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - the end or final part of something
  • Antonym: incompletion - the state of not being finished or complete

Etymology

From Latin completio (a filling up, fulfillment), from complere (to fill up, complete).
