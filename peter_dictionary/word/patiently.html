patiently

1. ADV

/ ˈpeɪʃəntli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃəntli / (BrE)
In a patient manner; showing patience or endurance, especially while waiting or dealing with difficulties.

Examples

He had to teach himself patiently that these traps were not for him.
Patiently, he explained what he knew about their course and their objectives.
"We can get it if we dig ", he said patiently.

Synset Examples

he patiently played with the child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impatiently - in an impatient manner; without patience

Etymology

From 'patient' + '-ly', with 'patient' from Latin 'patientem' (suffering, enduring).
