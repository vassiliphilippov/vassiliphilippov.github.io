telegram

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡræm / (BrE)
A message sent by telegraph and delivered in written or printed form.

Examples

By 1871 L. C. Orvis, manager of the "Western Union Telegraph Company ", expressed willingness to send emergency telegrams on Sundays from his Village drugstore.
The phone had been disconnected but telegrams came for him and notes by special messenger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cablegram - a message sent by submarine cable

Etymology

From French 'télégramme', based on Greek 'tēle' (far) + 'gramma' (something written).
