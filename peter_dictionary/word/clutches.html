clutches

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈklʌtʃɪz / (BrE)
a person’s (often controlling or harmful) grasp or power over someone or something; the act of seizing or holding tightly

Examples

"So I just scooted out of his clutches.

Synset Examples

he released his clasp on my arm
he has a strong grip for an old man
she kept a firm hold on the railing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - a firm hold; strong grasp
  • Synonym: grasp - a firm hold or grip
  • Antonym: release - the act of letting go

Etymology

From Middle English 'clocchen' (to clutch), related to Old English 'clyccan'.
