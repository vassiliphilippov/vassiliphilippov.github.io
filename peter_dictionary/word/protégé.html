protégé

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʒeɪ / (BrE)
a person who is supported and guided by an influential mentor, especially in their career or personal development

Examples

With Hudson looking on, his protege Greene picked a fight with the ship's surgeon, Edward Wilson.
Johnny Fontane ruined one of Woltz International's most valuable protégés.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mentor - an experienced or trusted adviser who supports and guides another

Etymology

From French protégé, past participle of protéger (to protect), from Latin protegere.
