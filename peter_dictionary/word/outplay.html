outplay

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈpleɪ / (AmE), / aʊtˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to perform better than an opponent in a game, sport, or contest; to defeat through superior skill or play

Examples

He outplayed Palmer all around the course and finished with a tremendous 65 to Palmer's 71.

Synset Examples

The Knicks outplayed the Lakers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a game, contest, or battle

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'to surpass') + 'play'.
