latitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬ɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈlætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the angular distance north or south of the earth's equator, measured in degrees and shown as lines parallel to the equator on maps and globes; similar distances on other heavenly bodies.

Examples

These contour diagrams also suggest a rather rapid falloff in the radio brightness with latitude.
Within about an hour with the help of reports from seismic stations in Alaska, Arizona and California, the quake's epicenter was placed at 51 degrees North latitude and 158 degrees East longitude.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: longitude - angular distance east or west of the prime meridian

Etymology

From Latin 'latitudo' (breadth, width), from 'latus' (wide).
