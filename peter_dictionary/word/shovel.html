shovel

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl / (BrE)
a tool with a broad scoop and typically a long handle, used for digging, lifting, and moving loose material such as soil, gravel, or snow

Examples

I took the pail and shovel and went out on the porch.
We send shovels, cement, nails, and corrugated iron for roofs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spade - a similar digging tool with a flat blade, used for cutting into earth

Etymology

From Old English 'scofl', related to 'scufan' (to thrust or push).

2. VERB

/ ˈʃʌvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvəl / (BrE)
to dig, move, or remove material using a shovel or a motion resembling the use of a shovel

Examples

Clerks and postmasters shoveled muck out of their offices - those who still had offices - and wondered how to move the mail.

Synset Examples

shovel sand
he shovelled in the backyard all afternoon long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - to pick up and move something with a scoop or a sweeping motion

Etymology

From Middle English 'shovel', from Old English 'scofl', related to 'sceofan' (to shove).
