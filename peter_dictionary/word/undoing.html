undoing

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈduːɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈduːɪŋ / (BrE)
an act or event that cancels or reverses the effect of a previous action

Examples

And because fortune had favored neither the prisoner nor the red haired captain, they would be each other's undoing.

Etymology

From Old English 'undōn' ('un-' meaning 'reversal' + 'do').
