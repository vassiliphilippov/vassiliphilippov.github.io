can

1. NOUN

/ kæn / (AmE), / kæn / (BrE)
A sealed metal container, typically cylindrical, used to store food, beverages, paint, or other substances.

Examples

He then sold her minerals to cure her kidney ailment, a can of sage "to make her look like a girl again ", and an application of plain mud to take her wrinkles away.
He considered opening a can of beer but vetoed that idea too.
I said, "Can of peas, my ass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin - sealed metal container (especially in British English)

Etymology

From Middle English 'canne', from Old English 'canne', meaning 'container, cup'.

2. VERB

/ kæn / (AmE), / kæn / (BrE)
(informal) To dismiss someone from their job; to fire.

Examples

Let her call Crosson if she wanted to, let Crosson raise the roof or even can him, he did n't care.

Synset Examples

The boss fired his secretary today
The company terminated 25% of its workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss someone from a job
  • Synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a position or job
  • Antonym: hire - to give someone a job

Etymology

Probably from the idea of putting someone 'in the can' (i.e., in jail or out, like garbage); first recorded early 20th century American slang.

3. VERB

/ kæn / (AmE), / kan / (BrE)
to preserve food by sealing it in a can or jar, usually after heating to prevent spoilage

Examples

She was ready to kill the beef, dress it out, and with vegetables from her garden was going to can soup, broth, hash, and stew against the winter.
And one of the best services available to the people who try to raise and can meat, to plant, grow vegetables and put them up.

Synset Examples

tinned foods are not very tasty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserve - to prevent food from spoiling

Etymology

Shortened from 'canning', derived from 'can' (noun), referring to the container.
