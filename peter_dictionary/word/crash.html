crash

1. NOUN

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
a serious accident in which a vehicle or vehicles are damaged, often involving injury or death

Examples

But at least the pessimists who believed that the world organization had plunged to its death in that plane crash in the Congo have been proved wrong.
She was the widow of a writer who had died in an airplane crash, and Mickie had found her a job as head of the historical section of the Treasury.
If Hal Yarrow had been killed in a crash, he must have wanted it that way.

Synset Examples

they are still investigating the crash of the TWA plane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collision - an instance of one moving object striking violently against another
  • Synonym: accident - an unfortunate incident, especially one causing injury or damage

Etymology

Probably imitative; related to words like 'crash' imitating the sound of breaking or smashing.

2. VERB

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
to fall, strike, or come down with sudden, forceful impact, often making a loud noise

Examples

As they were riding along this winding road on the bench of land between the two bluffs, a volley of rifle fire suddenly crashed around the two officers.
The old woman, stubbornly reigning in the house above the crashing waters took on an ominous reality.
The seemingly quiet ocean had crashed a wall of water from 10 to 100 feet high upon beaches crowded with bathers, drowning thousands of them and flattening villages along the shore.
Just as they reached the shore, the first huge wave crashed upon them.
The waves are separated by intervals of 15 minutes to an hour or more (because of their great length), and this has often lulled people into thinking after the first great wave has crashed that it is all over.

Synset Examples

The branch crashed down on my car
The plane crashed in the sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collide - to come together with solid or direct impact
  • Synonym: smash - to break into pieces suddenly, typically with noise and force
  • Antonym: land - to come down through the air to the ground, especially smoothly (contrast to falling violently)

Etymology

Imitative origin, possibly influenced by Middle English 'crasschen' (to smash).

3. VERB

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
to make or move with a loud, crashing noise

Examples

Thunder crashed; barrels tumbled down the mountainsides, and bounced and bounced till their own fury split them open.
He had not covered a hundred yards before a gun crashed from somewhere behind.
The explosion of the rifle had crashed against the walls of the stockade and the deep echoes were still rolling in the hills.

Synset Examples

The car crashed through the glass door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bang - to make a sudden loud noise
  • Synonym: boom - to make a deep, resonant sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound of a crash.

4. VERB

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
To move suddenly and violently, especially so as to break through or force one’s way past something.

Examples

Half crazed by the weight dragging, the dust, and the heat, the horse leaped our wall, dashing out the rider's brains against it, and leaving him lying there among us - while the horse crashed away through the brush.

Synset Examples

The terrorists crashed the gate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - to break something into pieces violently or forcefully

Etymology

Of imitative origin, echoing the sound of a violent impact or breakage.

5. NOUN

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
a sudden and severe fall in business activity or stock prices, often causing widespread damage to the economy

Examples

On that date the Musicians Emergency Fund, organized to furnish employment for musicians unable to obtain engagements during the depression and to provide relief for older musicians who lost their fortunes in the stock market crash, observed its 30 th anniversary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slump - a prolonged period of falling economic activity
  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth or rising prices

6. VERB

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
to break violently or with a loud noise; to smash into pieces suddenly and noisily

Examples

We flew in rickety planes so overloaded that we wondered why they did n't crash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shatter - to break suddenly into many small pieces
  • Synonym: smash - to break into pieces violently and noisily

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made when something breaks or collapses suddenly.

7. NOUN

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
A violent collision or the act of hitting something with force, often resulting in noise or damage.

Examples

The Indian's arm whipped sidewise - there was a flash of amber and froth, the crash of the bottle shattering against the side of the first car.

Synset Examples

his crash through the window
the fullback's smash into the defensive line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - a violent and noisy collision or impact
  • Degree Lower: bump - a light or less forceful collision

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, referring to the sound of something breaking.

8. NOUN

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
A sudden, loud, resonant noise produced by a violent impact or collision, often repeating or echoing.

Examples

He was interrupted by a crash from the deck and sprang toward the ladder, with Gansevoort and Heiser behind him.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant, metallic sound
  • Synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise, like an explosion or blow
  • Degree Lower: thud - a dull sound of impact

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of a collision or impact.

9. VERB

/ kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or aircraft) to be damaged or destroyed when it hits something with force; to collide violently.

Examples

The car door crashed shut.

Synset Examples

the plane crashed into the ocean
The car crashed into the lamp post

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collide - to come into violent contact; to crash together
  • Synonym: wreck - to destroy or severely damage, especially in an accident

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a violent collision; first attested in the 16th century.
