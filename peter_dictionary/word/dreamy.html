dreamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɹimi / (AmE), / ˈdɹiːmi / (BrE)
having a quality or mood that is vague, gentle, or pleasantly absent-minded, as if lost in thought or in a dream

Examples

"Well, that's your business, Mr. Skyros ", said Angie, and his dreamy eyes moved past Mr. Skyros' shoulder to gaze vaguely out the ground glass window.
It's like a flame, I guess ", she said in a dreamy tone.

Synset Examples

a woolgathering moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent-minded - lacking attention to immediate surroundings or details
  • Synonym: pensive - engaged in or reflecting deep or serious thought
  • Antonym: alert - fully aware and paying close attention

Etymology

From 'dream' + '-y', formed by adding the adjective suffix to the noun 'dream'.
