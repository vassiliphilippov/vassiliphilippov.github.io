transformed

1. ADJ

/ trænsˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
Having been changed completely in form, appearance, or character.

Examples

In any inquiry into the way in which great literature affects the emotions, particularly with respect to the sense of harmony, or relief of tension, or sense of "a transformed inner nature "which may occur, a most careful exploration of the particular feature of the experience which produces the effect would be required.

Synset Examples

shocked to see the transformed landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altered - changed in some respect, but not necessarily completely
  • Antonym: unchanged - remaining the same, not changed

Etymology

From the verb 'transform,' itself from Latin 'transformare' (to change in shape).
