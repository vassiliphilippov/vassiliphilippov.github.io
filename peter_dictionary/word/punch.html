punch

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to drive or force (something) suddenly or forcefully, especially as if with a physical punch

Examples

He dropped down to five hundred feet, swinging a little north of the city of Tacloban, and punched into the opening that showed against the mountain.

Synset Examples

the nail punched through the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust - push (something or someone) suddenly or violently in the specified direction
  • Synonym: jab - poke (someone or something) roughly or quickly, especially with something sharp or pointed

Etymology

From Middle English 'punchen' (to thrust, prod), probably from Old French 'poulchier' (to poke, prick).

2. NOUN

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
A quick, forceful blow with the fist, especially in boxing or fighting.

Examples

He jumped to his feet and started to throw punches.

Synset Examples

I gave him a clout on his nose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow - a sudden impact or strike, especially with the hand or a weapon
  • Degree Lower: jab - a quick, straight punch with the lead hand in boxing

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English punchen ‘to push, strike’, from Old French poinchonner.

3. VERB

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to strike or hit someone or something quickly and forcefully with a closed fist

Examples

Player then had the choice of punching the ball safely out of the woods to the 9 th fairway and settling for a bogey 5, or gambling.

Synset Examples

he punched me in the stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike with a hand or tool
  • Degree Lower: slap - to hit with an open hand
  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English punchen (to thrust, prod), from Old French 'ponchonner' (to stamp, imprint).
