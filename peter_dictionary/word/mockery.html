mockery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːkəri / (AmE), / ˈmɒkəri / (BrE)
The act of ridiculing or scornfully imitating someone or something; derision or contemptuous laughter.

Examples

The sound mockery of sheep is like the salubrious horse laugh ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - a rude remark or sound intended to mock or scorn
  • Synonym: taunt - a remark made to anger, wound, or provoke someone
  • Antonym: flattery - insincere praise intended to please

Etymology

From Old French mocquerie (mockery, derision), from mocquer (to mock).
