tingle

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to experience or cause a slight stinging or prickling sensation, often as a result of excitement, emotion, or physical stimulus

Examples

The Percussive Twenties (SP 44006) stirs pleasant memories with well-known songs of that day, and Johnny Keating's Kombo gives forth with tingling jazz in Percussive Moods (SP 44005).
He was tingling within.
He was again tingling with pleasure, seeing himself clearly in Slater's shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickle - to experience or cause a slight, sharp sensation like tiny points pressing on the skin

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, referring to the sensation itself.
