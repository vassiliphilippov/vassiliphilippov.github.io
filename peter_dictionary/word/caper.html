caper

1. VERB

/ ˈkeɪ.pər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.pə / (BrE)
to jump or move about in a lively or playful way

Examples

For ten minutes they ran beneath the squall, raising their arms and, for the first time, shouting and capering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: skip - to move lightly and quickly with small jumps
  • Degree Lower: jump - to push oneself off the ground and into the air by using the muscles in one's legs and feet
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly and energetically

Etymology

From Italian *capriolare* ('to leap like a goat'), from Latin *capreolus* ('wild goat').
