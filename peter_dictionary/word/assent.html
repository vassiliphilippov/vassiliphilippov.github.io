assent

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
official agreement or approval, especially after careful consideration of an idea, plan, or request

Examples

"We were brought up that way "was one statement which won general assent.
Cobb's assent was tight.

Synset Examples

he gave his assent eagerly
a murmur of acquiescence from the assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consent - permission or agreement for something to happen
  • Antonym: dissent - refusal to agree; difference of opinion

Etymology

From Latin assentire, from ad- ‘toward’ + sentire ‘to feel, think’.

2. VERB

/ əˈsɛnt / (AmE), / əˈsɛnt / (BrE)
to agree or express approval, especially formally or after careful consideration

Examples

Yes, the Maestro assented.

Synset Examples

The Maestro assented to the request for an encore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consent - to give permission or approval
  • Antonym: dissent - to disagree or withhold approval

Etymology

From Latin 'assentārī' (to agree with), from 'ad-' (to) + 'sentīre' (to feel, think).
