recreational

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənl / (AmE), / ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənl / (BrE)
relating to or provided for the purpose of recreation or leisure activities.

Examples

Personnel assigned through the shooting development program have proudly participated in over 53 state and regional workshops, at which hundreds of school administrators, teachers, professors, and recreational leaders have been introduced to Outdoor Education.

Synset Examples

a recreational area with a pool and ball fields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisure - free time when one is not working

Etymology

From 'recreation' + '-al', using the Latin root 'recreatio' (refreshment, recovery).

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
Engaged in or relating to activities done for enjoyment, relaxation, or as a pastime rather than for work or professional reasons.

Examples

Some persons consider the work on a farm recreational.

Synset Examples

an amateur painter
gained valuable experience in amateur theatricals
recreational golfers
reading matter that is both recreational and mentally stimulating
unpaid extras in the documentary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amateur - engaged in as a pastime, not as a profession
  • Antonym: professional - done as a profession rather than for recreation

Etymology

From recreation + -al, formed on the noun 'recreation' (from Latin recreatio, 'restoration').
