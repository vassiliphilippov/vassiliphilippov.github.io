reprint

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈprɪnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to print a book, article, or other text again, especially when copies have run out or a new edition is required

Examples

On November 7, 1859, the Providence Daily Journal reprinted a letter sent to John Brown from "E. B. ", a Quaker lady in Newport.

Synset Examples

They never reprinted the famous treatise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: print - to produce text or images on paper by a mechanical process

Etymology

From re- (again) + print; modelled after 'print'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈriː.prɪnt / (BrE)
a publication, such as a book or article, that has been printed and issued again without changes

Examples

I did book jackets and covers for paperback reprints: naked girls huddling in corners of dingy furnished rooms while at the doorway, daring the cops to take him, is the guy in shirt sleeves clutching a revolver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edition - a particular version or release of a published text

Etymology

From re- (again) + print, meaning to print again.
