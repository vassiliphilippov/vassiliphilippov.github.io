fault

1. NOUN

/ fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
an imperfection or defect in an object, machine, or system that prevents it from working correctly

Examples

After the coach listed all the boy's faults, Hartweger said, "Coach before I leave here, you 'll get to like me ".
Look at the physical features of the land to determine how desirable it is for use, what can be done to correct the faults, and what it will cost to make the area meet your needs in comparison to other sites.
Whatever their faults, they are not hypocrites.
She was a living doll and no mistake - the blue-black bang, the wide cheekbones, olive flushed, that betrayed the Cherokee strain in her Midwestern lineage, and the mouth whose only fault, in the novelist's carping phrase, was that the lower lip was a trifle too voluptuous.

Synset Examples

a flaw caused the crystal to shatter
if there are any defects you should send it back to the manufacturer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaw - a mark, fault, or other imperfection that mars a substance or object
  • Synonym: defect - a shortcoming, imperfection, or lack
  • Antonym: virtue - a good or desirable quality
  • Antonym: perfection - the condition of being free from all faults or defects

Etymology

From Old French faute (lack, defect), from Vulgar Latin *fallita (a deception, failure).

2. VERB

/ fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
To criticize or blame someone or something for a mistake or shortcoming.

Examples

"Chicago and All That Jazz "could not be faulted on the choice of artists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'faute' (lack, fault), from Latin 'fallere' (to deceive).

3. NOUN

/ fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
a mistake or error resulting from bad judgment, ignorance, or carelessness

Examples

And the fault, of course, was Rameau's.
The fault was Rameau's and that of the whole culture of this Parisian age.
Not his poor mother's fault.
It was n't his fault that these things were so.

Synset Examples

he made a bad mistake
she was quick to point out my errors
I could understand his English in spite of his grammatical faults

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong or misguided
  • Synonym: error - a mistake or inaccuracy
  • Degree Higher: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake

Etymology

From Old French *faute* (lack, deficiency), from Vulgar Latin *fallita*, from Latin *fallere* (to deceive, disappoint).

4. NOUN

/ fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
The responsibility for a mistake, failure, or bad situation; the state of being to blame for something undesirable.

Examples

It was my fault.

Synset Examples

it was John's fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • Synonym: responsibility - being accountable for something
  • Antonym: credit - responsibility for a good situation; opposite of fault

Etymology

From Old French faulte, from Latin fallita (deceived, failed).
