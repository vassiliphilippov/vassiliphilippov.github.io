capability

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪ.pəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkeɪ.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the ability or power to do something, especially to perform a particular task or function; the quality of being capable physically, intellectually, or legally

Examples

Companies of all types have made great advances in production capabilities and efficiencies - in modern equipment and new processes, enlarged R + D facilities, faster new product development.

Synset Examples

he worked to the limits of his capability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ability - the power or skill to do something
  • Antonym: incapacity - lack of ability; inability to do something
  • Synonym: potential - latent qualities or abilities that may be developed

Etymology

From capable + -ity, ultimately from Latin capabilis (able to hold or take) + -itas.
