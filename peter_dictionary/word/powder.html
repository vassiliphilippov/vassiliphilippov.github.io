1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A dry, bulk solid composed of tiny, loose particles, typically created by crushing or grinding a substance.

Etymology

From Middle English *poudre*, from Old French *poudre*, from Latin *pulvis* (dust).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A mixture of potassium nitrate, charcoal, and sulfur, used especially as an explosive in firearms, fireworks, and time fuses; also known as gunpowder.

Examples

We were less than a quarter of a mile from the road, and we could trace its shape from the ribbon of powder smoke and dust that hung over it.
Despite Company threats, duly carried through, to cut off supplies of powder, ball, and thread for fishing nets, about 350 persons stayed in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunpowder - explosive powder used in firearms and fireworks

Etymology

From Middle English 'poudre' via Old French, from Latin 'pulvis' (dust, powder).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
a cosmetic or medicinal substance in fine, dry particles, often applied to the skin

Examples

More and more of the colonials were wearing their own hair and not using powder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talcum powder - a soft, white powder used as a cosmetic or for medicinal purposes

Etymology

From Middle English poudre, from Old French poudre (dust), from Latin pulvis (dust, powder).
