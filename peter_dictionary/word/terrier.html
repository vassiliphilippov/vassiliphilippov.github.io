terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
A breed of small, energetic, short-bodied dog originally bred for hunting animals living underground, such as foxes and badgers.

Examples

He would lay in wait in the garage, and when the terrier came scratching around, he 'd let fly with a cherry bomb.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dog - domesticated canid, commonly kept as a pet
  • Degree Higher: hound - a type of hunting dog, generally larger than terriers

Etymology

From Old French *terrier* (from Latin *terra*, meaning 'earth'), referring to their ability to hunt underground.
