retaliation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌtæliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌtæliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of doing something harmful or unpleasant to someone because they have done something harmful or unpleasant to you; revenge or reprisal

Examples

It is not an entirely happy book, as Mrs. Fink soon becomes jealous of Alicia and, in retaliation, refuses to continue to scrape the algae off her glass.
For three years, since the liquor territorial conference, Torrio had, with his elastic patience, and because he knew that retaliation could cause only violent warfare and disaster to business, tolerated O' Banion's impudent double-crossing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the action of hurting or harming someone in return for an injury or wrong
  • Synonym: reprisal - an act of retaliation, especially in war
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the action of forgiving or being forgiven; the opposite of retaliation

Etymology

From Late Latin 'retaliatio' (return of like for like), from Latin 'retaliare' (to pay back in kind).
