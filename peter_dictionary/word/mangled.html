mangled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəld / (BrE)
(of an object or body) having edges that are torn, jagged, or mutilated, typically as a result of injury or violence.

Examples

His head hung over the boards of Katie's stall; before it was sprawled the mangled corpse of the bearded stranger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacerated - torn or deeply cut
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired

Etymology

From Middle English 'manglen', from Middle Dutch 'mangelen', meaning to mutilate or maim.
