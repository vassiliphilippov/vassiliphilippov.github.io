landslide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌslaɪd / (BrE)
an election victory in which one side wins by an exceptionally large margin

Examples

The result was a landslide for the Republican candidates.

Synset Examples

Roosevelt defeated Hoover in a landslide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweep - an overwhelming win in an election
  • Antonym: narrow victory - a win by a small margin

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌslaɪd / (BrE)
a mass of earth or rock that moves down a slope, often suddenly and rapidly, due to gravity

Examples

When a second quake came, they dashed back to the beach, fearing that they might be buried under landslides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mudslide - a landslide consisting mainly of mud
  • Synonym: avalanche - a large mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside
  • Synonym: rockfall - an instance of rocks falling down a slope or cliff

Etymology

From 'land' + 'slide', meaning land that slides down.
