brotherhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðəhʊd / (BrE)
The relationship or bond between brothers; the state of being brothers.

Examples

All of this happening in a time of their lives when their youth and their brotherhood knitted them together as no other time or circumstance could.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sisterhood - the relationship between sisters

Etymology

From Middle English 'brotherhod', from 'brother' + '-hood'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðəhʊd / (BrE)
A feeling of kinship and mutual support among people, like that between brothers.

Examples

The Prince visited the hospital of Operation Brotherhood, supported by the Junior Chamber of Commerce of the Philippines, and fed rice to two pet elephants he kept at his residence at Pak Song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternity - group formed for mutual support, especially among men

Etymology

From Middle English brotherhod, from brother + -hood.
