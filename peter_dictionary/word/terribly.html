terribly

1. ADV

/ ˈterəbli / (AmE), / ˈterəbli / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the degree of something; very or extremely.

Examples

It is awfully talky, for instance, and not all of the talk is terribly impressive.
It grows terribly and unavoidably in power and leaves in its wake a trail of misery, moral disintegration, and destruction.
The release, the freedom, involved in loving another is either terribly difficult or else absolutely impossible; and the motion toward it brings disaster.
"I am terribly sorry to keep you waiting ", she said, "but won't you make yourself a little drink while you wait?
She was terribly pleased.

Synset Examples

terribly interesting
I'm awful sorry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awfully - used as an intensifier, meaning very or extremely
  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small extent or degree

Etymology

From 'terrible' + -ly, originally meaning 'in a terrible manner', later generalized as an intensifier.

2. ADV

/ ˈtɛrəbli / (AmE), / ˈterəbli / (BrE)
In a very bad, unpleasant, or severe manner; to an extreme or intense degree.

Examples

He's young, not terribly bright.

Synset Examples

she sings terribly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badly - in a bad or unsatisfactory way
  • Antonym: wonderfully - in a wonderful or extremely good way

Etymology

From 'terrible' + -ly; 'terrible' from Latin 'terribilis' (frightful).
