disabling

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to become unable to function normally, especially as a result of injury, illness, or condition; incapacitating.

Examples

The case described in this paper is that of an older man who developed disabling muscular weakness while receiving a variety of steroids for a refractory anemia.
This patient was a 65 - year old white male accountant who entered the New York Hospital for his fourth and terminal admission on June 26, 1959, because of disabling weakness and general debility.

Synset Examples

a crippling injury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crippling - causing severe damage or limitation; incapacitating
  • Synonym: incapacitating - making someone unable to function in the usual way

Etymology

From the verb 'disable', itself from Old French 'desabler', from Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'habilis' (able).
