singsong

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋˌsɒŋ / (BrE)
A way of speaking or singing in which the pitch rises and falls regularly and often monotonously.

Examples

The lazy sing-song was spaced in time like the drone of a bumble-bee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotone - a continuous, unchanging sound or intonation

Etymology

Formed from the words "sing" and "song," imitating the repetitive musical quality.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋˌsɒŋ / (BrE)
to speak, chant, or recite in a singsong manner, with a rhythmic, repetitive rise and fall in pitch

Examples

They singsonged the portion at the teacher, who accompanied them in an off-key baritone and spurred them on with the stick.

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'singsong' (rhythmic chant), itself from 'sing' + 'song'.
