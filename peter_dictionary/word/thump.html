thump

1. VERB

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
(of the heart or a similar organ) to beat or move with a heavy, rhythmic sound, often loudly or forcefully.

Examples

It was nothing, though his heart was thumping wildly.
He stood very still, his heart thumping wildly.
Her heart was thumping painfully; the unknown was so much worse than - what dangers lay ahead for her?

Synset Examples

Her heart was beating fast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - (of the heart) to beat rapidly and forcefully
  • Degree Lower: flutter - (of the heart) to beat rapidly or irregularly

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound made by a heavy blow or beat.

2. VERB

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
to make a dull, heavy sound, typically by striking something with force

Examples

At 7: 25 two hotel doormen came thumping down the steps, carrying a saw-horse to be set up as a barricade in front of the haberdashery store window next to the entranceway, and as I watched them in their gaudy red coats that nearly scraped the ground, their golden, fringed epaulets and spic, red visored caps, I suddenly saw just over their shoulders Jessica gracefully making her way through the crowd.

Synset Examples

the knocker thudded against the front door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thud - to make a dull, heavy sound
  • Degree Higher: bang - to make a sudden, loud noise
  • Degree Lower: tap - to make a light, gentle sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by a heavy blow; first attested in the 16th century.

3. VERB

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
to hit something or someone hard with the hand, fist, or a heavy object, producing a dull sound

Examples

I'll thump your skull for you.

Synset Examples

the salesman pounded the door knocker
a bible-thumping Southern Baptist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - to strike repeatedly with heavy blows
  • Synonym: bang - to strike something forcefully and noisily

Etymology

Imitative origin, reflecting the sound produced by the action.
