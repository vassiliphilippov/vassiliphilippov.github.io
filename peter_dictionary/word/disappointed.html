disappointed

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
Feeling sad or displeased because something has failed to fulfill one's hopes or expectations.

Examples

Papa was disappointed that none of the brothers had heard the Call.
Claire was bitterly disappointed but determined not to let the rebuff daunt her purpose.
They expected greater things from him, regardless of how trying the circumstances, and they were disappointed.
He is utterly disappointed in himself and in the desultory life he has been leading.

Synset Examples

disappointed expectations and thwarted ambitions
their foiled attempt to capture Calais
many frustrated poets end as pipe-smoking teachers
his best efforts were thwarted

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfied - pleased because expectations have been met
  • Synonym: frustrated - feeling annoyed or less hopeful because of being unable to achieve something

Etymology

From 'disappoint' + '-ed'; 'disappoint' from Old French desapointer, from des- (reverse) + appointer (appoint).
