corrosive

1. ADJ

/ kəˈroʊsɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊsɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to destroy or damage other substances gradually by chemical action, especially acids or strong chemicals.

Examples

Precise pressure volume temperature measurements on corrosive gases are dependent on a sensitive yet rugged pressure transducer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - able to burn or corrode organic tissue by chemical action

Etymology

From Medieval Latin corrosivus, from Latin corrodere (to gnaw to pieces).
