blurt

1. VERB

/ blɝt / (AmE), / blɜːt / (BrE)
to say something suddenly and impulsively, often without careful thought

Examples

Out of compulsion to say something cheery, Ben Prime blurted, "Well, we were lucky to be on soft ground when the first floodheads hit.

Synset Examples

He blurted out the secret
He blundered his stupid ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclaim - to cry out suddenly and loudly

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly imitative or related to 'blare' or Middle English 'blarten' (to cry out).
