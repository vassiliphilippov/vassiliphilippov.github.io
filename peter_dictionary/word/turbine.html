turbine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbʌɪn / (BrE)
a rotary mechanical device in which the kinetic energy of a moving fluid, such as water, steam, or air, is converted into mechanical energy by passing over blades attached to a rotor

Examples

Recent studies by Weston and Stack had indicated that a turbine aerator could be added to an oxidation pond to increase the rate of oxygen transfer.
The pilot plant was equipped with a 3 - hp turbine aerator (Figure 2).
The raw sewage was introduced directly under the turbine aerator to insure maximum mixing of the raw sewage with the aeration tank contents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine - machine that converts energy into mechanical force

Etymology

From French *turbine*, from Latin *turbo* (whirl, whirlpool).
