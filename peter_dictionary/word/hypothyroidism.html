hypothyroidism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈθaɪrɔɪdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈθʌɪərɔɪdɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by an underactive thyroid gland, resulting in insufficient production of thyroid hormones.

Examples

Any of the antithyroid drugs, of either type, if given in large enough doses for a long period of time will cause goitre, owing to inhibition of thyroid hormone synthesis, with production of hypothyroidism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyperthyroidism - an overactive thyroid gland, producing excessive thyroid hormones

Etymology

From hypo- (under) + thyroid (thyroid gland) + -ism (condition).
