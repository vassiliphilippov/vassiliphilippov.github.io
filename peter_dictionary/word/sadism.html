sadism

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈseɪdɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency to derive sexual pleasure from inflicting pain or humiliation on others.

Examples

His hubris, deficiency of taste, and sadism carried him straightaway to the top.
The gruesome humor of the Nazis was not forgotten - the gas chamber with a sign on it with the name of a Jewish foundation and bearing a copper Star of David - nor the gratuitous sadism of SS officers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masochism - sexual pleasure from experiencing pain oneself

Etymology

From French sadisme, named after the Marquis de Sade (1740–1814), noted for his writings about such practices.
