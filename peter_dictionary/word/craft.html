craft

1. NOUN

/ kræft / (AmE), / krɑːft / (BrE)
A skilled activity, especially one involving making things by hand, or the occupation of persons engaged in such work.

Examples

He must be capable of designing for and supervising the manufacture of any craft materials needed in the furnishings ".
These opportunities are to be found in certain cities in such crafts as auto mechanics, carpentry, drafting, electrical work, tool-and-die work, and sheet-metal work.
"I do n't think I 've reached the point, yet, where I can say I know everything I ought to know about the craft.

Synset Examples

he learned his trade as an apprentice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - a skilled job, typically manual, requiring special training
  • Degree Higher: profession - a paid occupation, especially one requiring advanced education or training
  • Degree Lower: job - a paid position of regular employment, not necessarily skilled

Etymology

Old English 'cræft' (strength, skill, ability), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ kræft / (AmE), / krɑːft / (BrE)
a vehicle designed for travel on water, in air, or through space, such as a boat, ship, airplane, or spacecraft

Examples

He stalked into the water and fell heavily over the side of the flat-bottomed barge, his weight nearly swamping the craft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vessel - a ship or large boat

Etymology

From Old English 'cræft' (strength, skill); the vehicle sense evolved from the idea of skillful making.
