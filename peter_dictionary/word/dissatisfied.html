dissatisfied

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsˈsætɪsˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˈsætɪsfaɪd / (BrE)
Not pleased or content; feeling that something is not as good as it should be.

Examples

But he was totally dissatisfied with his upper body.
One of the obvious conclusions we can make on the basis of the last election, I suppose, is that we, the majority, were dissatisfied with Eisenhower conservatism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfied - pleased because you have achieved something or because something is as you want it
  • Degree Lower: content - a state of being happy and satisfied
  • Synonym: disgruntled - angry or dissatisfied

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + satisfied, from Latin satisfacere (to satisfy).
