implicated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Involved in a criminal or morally wrong act, typically in a way that suggests guilt or responsibility.

Examples

The Final Solution proved that the Jew-baiter of any variety exposes himself as being implicated in the criminality and madness of others.

Synset Examples

all those concerned in the bribery case have been identified
named three officials implicated in the plot
an innocent person implicated by circumstances in a crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involved - Having a part in something; connected to an activity or event, especially in a negative sense
  • Antonym: exonerated - Freed from guilt or blame, especially after being considered involved in wrongdoing

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'implicate', from Latin implicatus, past participle of implicare ‘to entwine, entangle’.
