regale

1. VERB

/ rɪˈɡeɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪl / (BrE)
to entertain or lavishly provide someone with choice or abundant food and drink

Examples

He regaled Claire with an account of the mock mass performed by the cassocked bloods, which he had had at firsthand from old Bud Dodington, one of the leaders of the so-called "Order ".

Synset Examples

Don't worry about the expensive wine--I'm treating
She treated her houseguests with good food every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat - to provide with food, drink, or entertainment

Etymology

From French *régalér* (to entertain), from Old French *regale* (a treat, feast), likely from Latin *regalis* (royal).
