studiously

1. ADV

/ ˈstuːdiəsli / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows careful and deliberate attention, often involving a great deal of study or effort.

Examples

Directed by Grigory Kozintsev in a tempo that is studiously slow, he develops a sense of a high tradition shining brightly and passing gravely through an impious world.

Synset Examples

she examined the data studiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefully - with great attention; taking care
  • Antonym: carelessly - in a way that shows lack of care or attention

Etymology

From 'studious' + '-ly'; 'studious' from Latin 'studiosus' (eager, zealous).
