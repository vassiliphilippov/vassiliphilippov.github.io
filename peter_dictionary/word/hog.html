hog

1. VERB

/ hɑg / (AmE), / hɒg / (BrE)
to take or use more than one's share of something, often in a greedy or selfish way

Examples

Although today's trucks are as fast as passenger cars, a truck driver has to be a sensible person and guard against hogging the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monopolize - to have or take complete control of something to the exclusion of others
  • Antonym: share - to use or enjoy something jointly with others

Etymology

From Old English 'hogg', referring to a young pig, later extended metaphorically to greedy behavior.
