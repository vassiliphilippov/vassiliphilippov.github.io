louse

1. NOUN

/ laʊs / (AmE), / laʊs / (BrE)
A small, wingless, usually flattened insect that lives as a parasite on warm-blooded animals and feeds on their blood.

Examples

He thought of Simms Purdew snoring on his bunk while Pullen James crouched by the hearth, skirmishing an undershirt for lice, and a wet log sizzled.
A Confederate observed that the Yankees were: "thicker than lise on a hen and a dam site ornraier ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small, wingless, jumping insect that feeds on blood of mammals and birds
  • Synonym: tick - a small arachnid parasite living on the skin of warm-blooded animals

Etymology

From Old English lūs, related to Old High German lūs and Old Norse lús.
