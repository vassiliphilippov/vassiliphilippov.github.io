reconvert

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈvɝːt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to convert something back to its former state or condition

Examples

In the forthcoming The Conquering Hero and Carnival, Broadway is not even adapting books, but reconverting old movies (Hail the Conquering Hero and Lili).

Synset Examples

Hollywood is reconverting old films

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convert - to change something into a different form, substance, or state

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) and convert, from Latin convertĕre (to turn around).
