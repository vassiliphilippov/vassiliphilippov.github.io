nymph

1. NOUN

/ nɪmf / (AmE), / nɪmf / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) a minor female nature deity, typically depicted as a beautiful and youthful maiden who inhabits rivers, woods, or other natural places.

Examples

"Mars and Venus, Bacchus and Ariadne, Jupiter and Io, Byron and the nymph of the owl's nest.

Synset Examples

the ancient Greeks believed that nymphs inhabited forests and bodies of water

Etymology

From Greek nymphē (bride, nymph).

2. NOUN

/ nɪmf / (AmE), / nɪmf / (BrE)
an immature form of certain insects that undergo incomplete metamorphosis, resembling the adult but lacking fully developed wings and reproductive organs

Examples

In the other hemisphere it is growing colder and nymphs, those who stayed alive through the summer, are being brought into nests for quickening and more growing ".

Etymology

From Greek numphē (bride, young woman, nymph); sense extended to biology for immature insect stages in 19th century.
