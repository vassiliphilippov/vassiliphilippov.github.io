meekly

1. ADV

/ ˈmikli / (AmE), / ˈmiːkli / (BrE)
In a quiet, gentle, and submissive manner; without protest or resistance.

Examples

On the second voyage, he had turned back at the frozen island of Novaya Zemlya and meekly given the crew a certificate stating that he did so of his own free will - which was obviously not the case.

Synset Examples

he humbly lowered his head

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous way

Etymology

From 'meek' + '-ly'; 'meek' from Middle English meke, from Old Norse mjúkr (soft, gentle).

2. ADV

/ ˈmik.li / (AmE), / ˈmiːk.li / (BrE)
In a submissive, quiet, or gentle manner; without resistance or assertiveness.

Examples

Not possible, he thought; the prime minister knew who his enemy was here; he was n't going to allow himself to be led meekly to the slaughter.

Synset Examples

meekly bowed to his wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissively - in a manner showing a willingness to submit to others
  • Antonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous way

Etymology

From 'meek', from Middle English 'meke', from Old Norse 'mjǫkr' (gentle, soft).
