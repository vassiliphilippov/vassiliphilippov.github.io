reverberation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌvɝːbɚˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜːbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a repeated sound that is heard after the original sound has been reflected off a surface, like an echo

Examples

Command has achieved the ideal amount of reverberation.

Synset Examples

she could hear echoes of her own footsteps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - a sound or series of sounds caused by the reflection of sound waves from a surface back to the listener

Etymology

From Latin reverberare (to beat back, to reflect) via Late Latin reverberatio.
