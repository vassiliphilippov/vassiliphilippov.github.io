descent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
an act or process of moving downward from a higher to a lower place or position

Examples

Existence is created and willed by God and is not the consequence of a pre-existent rebellion or of a cosmic descent from eternity into history.
Listed as present at the Descent were Mary, Mary's sister, Mary Magdalene, John, Joseph of Arimathea, Nicodemus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascent - an act or process of rising or going upward

Etymology

From Old French *descente*, from Latin *descensus* (a going down), from *descendere* (to go down).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
an act or process of moving downward; a downward movement from a higher to a lower place or position

Examples

When they can take ten seconds to accomplish the descent, they will have the strength to chin up.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascent - an upward movement; the act of rising up

Etymology

From Old French *descente*, from Latin *descensus* (a going down), from the verb *descendere* (to go down).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the origin or background of a person in terms of family or nationality; ancestry or lineage

Examples

And the great majority of these people are of Anglo-Saxon or Celtic descent.
On his father's side he was of German descent, on his mother's he came of the old Swedish nobility.
For years now, a growing number of my constituents have been of Italian descent.

Synset Examples

he comes from good origins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - family descent or lineage
  • Synonym: origin - a person's social or family background
  • Synonym: lineage - lineal descent from an ancestor

Etymology

From Old French *descente*, from descendre ‘to descend’, from Latin *descendere* ‘to go down’.
