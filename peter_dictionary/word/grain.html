grain

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ɡreɪn / (BrE)
A small, hard particle of a substance, such as sand, salt, or sugar.

Examples

It lacks pollen baskets and possesses only a large number of long, branched hairs on its legs, on which the pollen grains will collect.
The rice was n't dosed with opium, indeed it had no taste at all, not a grain of salt.

Synset Examples

a grain of sand
a grain of sugar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particle - a very small piece or part; a tiny portion or speck

Etymology

From Old French *grain* (seed, grain), from Latin *granum* (grain, seed).

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ɡreɪn / (BrE)
A foodstuff made from the harvested seeds of cereal grasses, such as wheat, corn, or oats, used as animal feed or for human consumption.

Examples

Horse smell was very strong, and he could hear the crunch of grain being ground between strong jaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cereal - any grass cultivated for the edible components of its grain
  • Synonym: feed - food given to animals, especially livestock

Etymology

From Latin granum (seed, grain).
