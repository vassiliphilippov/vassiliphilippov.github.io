appreciation

1. NOUN

/ əˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability to recognize and enjoy the good qualities or aesthetic value of something; sensitive understanding or taste

Examples

To both the amateur and the commercial progandist the analyst lacks a soul, lacks appreciation with his endless probings and classifications.

Synset Examples

arrogance and lack of taste contributed to his rapid success
to ask at that particular time was the ultimate in bad taste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taste - the ability to discern what is aesthetically pleasing or of good quality
  • Synonym: discernment - the ability to judge well or show good judgement

Etymology

From Latin *appretiatio*, from *appretiare* ‘to set a price to, value’, from *ad-* ‘to’ + *pretium* ‘price’.

3. NOUN

/ əˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of grateful recognition or favorable opinion; an expression of admiration or enjoyment.

Examples

He drank slowly with due appreciation.

Synset Examples

a small token in admiration of your works

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admiration - respectful approval or warm regard
  • Synonym: gratitude - thankfulness or appreciation
  • Antonym: disapproval - an unfavorable opinion or feeling

Etymology

From Late Latin appreciatio (estimation, valuation), from Latin appretiare (to appraise, value).

4. NOUN

From Late Latin ‘appretiatio’ (valuation), from ‘appretiare’ (to value).
