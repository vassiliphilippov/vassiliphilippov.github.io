sedentary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛdn̩ˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɛdn̩təri / (BrE)
(of a lifestyle, job, or person) involving much sitting and little physical activity.

Examples

Have not our physical abilities already deteriorated because of the more sedentary lives we are now living?

Synset Examples

forced by illness to lead a sedentary life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - involving energetic movement or activity

Etymology

From Latin sedentarius (sitting), from sedere (to sit).
