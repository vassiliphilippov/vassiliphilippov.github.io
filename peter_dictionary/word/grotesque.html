grotesque

1. ADJ

/ ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
comically or repulsively ugly or distorted; abnormal and unnatural in shape or appearance

Examples

Splendid, too, is the performance of Yuri Tolubeyev, one of Russia's leading comedians, as Sancho Panza, the fat, grotesque "squire ".
For the present it is enough to note that in the grotesque figure of Jacoby, at the moment of his collapse, all these elements come together in prophetic parody.
Piepsam is grotesque, a disturbing parody; his end is ridiculous and trivial.
It was a grotesque hen, five or six feet tall.

Synset Examples

tales of grotesque serpents eight fathoms long that churned the seas
twisted into monstrous shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hideous - extremely ugly or unpleasant
  • Synonym: monstrous - having the ugly or frightening appearance of a monster
  • Antonym: normal - usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From French 'grotesque', from Italian 'grottesca' (from 'grotta' meaning 'cave'), referring to cave paintings with fantastical forms.

2. NOUN

/ ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
a style of decorative art featuring fantastical combinations of human, animal, and plant forms, often producing a bizarre or absurd effect

Examples

Then the figures which held his attention became a group of shattered trees, standing like the grotesques of a medieval damnation scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arabesque - decorative design featuring flowing lines, often floral or foliage motifs

Etymology

From Italian grottesca, from grotta (grotto), referring to cave paintings with such designs.

3. ADJ

/ ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
Strangely or comically distorted, bizarre in appearance or style; ludicrously odd.

Examples

Again the student of evolutionary biology will find a fascinating, if to our minds grotesque, anticipation of the theory of chance variations and the natural elimination of the unfit in Lucretius, who in turn seems to have borrowed the concept from the philosopher Empedocles.

Synset Examples

Hamlet's assumed antic disposition
fantastic Halloween costumes
a grotesque reflection in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual, especially so as to cause interest or amusement
  • Synonym: fantastic - remarkable or imaginative in an odd, fanciful way
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From French *grotesque*, from Italian *grottesca* (cave painting), referring to fanciful designs found in Roman grottoes.
