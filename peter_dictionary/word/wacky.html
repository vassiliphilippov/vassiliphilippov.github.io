wacky

1. ADJ

/ ˈwæki / (AmE), / ˈwæki / (BrE)
Funny or eccentric in a silly or foolish way; amusingly bizarre or unconventional.

Examples

Expecting something more-than average wacky, I was surprised by what we found.

Synset Examples

gave me a cockamamie reason for not going
wore a goofy hat
a silly idea
some wacky plan for selling more books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goofy - silly or foolish
  • Synonym: silly - lacking in seriousness or sense; foolish
  • Antonym: serious - not joking or playful; solemn

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'whacky', from 'whack', meaning a blow or a state of being out of one's senses.
