ponderous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑndərəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəs / (BrE)
Slow and heavy; moving with great effort due to weight or bulk.

Examples

Between the ponderous hulk and himself, in the valley over which Papa-san reigned, men had hidden high explosives, booby traps, and mines.

Synset Examples

the heavy tread of tired troops
moved with a lumbering sag-bellied trot
ponderous prehistoric beasts
a ponderous yawn

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light - not heavy; easy to lift or move
  • Synonym: lumbering - moving in a slow, heavy, awkward way

Etymology

From Latin 'ponderosus' (heavy), from 'pondus' (weight).
