remembrance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɛm.brəns / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛm.brəns / (BrE)
The act or ability of remembering or recalling past events, people, or experiences.

Examples

We get some clue from a few remembrances of childhood and from the circumstance that we are probably not much more afraid of people now than man ever was.
She had even steeled herself to keep Juanita upstairs in the nurse's room off the empty nursery, although the girl tried to insist on moving back to the quarters to spare Kate remembrance of the baby's death.
The name fell with lazy affectionate remembrance from her lips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the quality of being unable to remember things

Etymology

From Old French 'remembrance', from remembrer (to remember), from Latin 'rememorari' (to remember again).
