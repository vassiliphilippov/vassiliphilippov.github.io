poor

1. ADJ

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
Having little money or few possessions; characterized by poverty.

Examples

"This is a poor boy's bill ", said Chapman.
Central city areas have become progressively poorer locales for the major denominations since the exodus of middle-class people from most central cities.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rich - having a great deal of money or resources

Etymology

From Old French *povre*, from Latin *pauper* (poor).
