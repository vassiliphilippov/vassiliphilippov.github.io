magical

1. ADJ

/ ˈmædʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmadʒɪkəl / (BrE)
Having, using, or connected with supernatural powers or magic.

Examples

Not through fear of disobeying orders, as Eichmann kept trying to explain, but through a peculiar giddiness that began in a half acceptance of the vicious absurdities contained in the Nazi interpretation of history and grew with each of Hitler's victories into a permanent light mindedness and sense of magical rightness that was able to respond to any proposal, and the more outrageous the better, "Well, let's try it ".
"He has the magical eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magic - the power of apparently influencing events by mysterious or supernatural forces
  • Synonym: wizardly - possessing the qualities or powers of a wizard; related to magic
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking any magical or supernatural quality; ordinary

Etymology

From magic + -al, with magic from Old French magique, from Latin magicus, from Greek magikos.
