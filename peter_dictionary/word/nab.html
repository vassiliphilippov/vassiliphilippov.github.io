(informal) to catch or seize someone, especially a criminal, often suddenly or unexpectedly; to take into custody.

Examples

Six radiomen told how, twice on two days after the ring was nabbed, a transmitter near Moscow was heard calling, using signals, times and wavelengths specified on codes found hidden in cigaret lighters in Lonsdale's apartment and the Krogers' house and also fastened to the transmitter lid.

Synset Examples

the police nabbed the suspected criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrest - to take into legal custody
  • Synonym: capture - to catch and take control of someone or something
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free after having been caught or detained

Etymology

Probably originally a dialectal or slang alteration of nabbe (to seize), of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.
