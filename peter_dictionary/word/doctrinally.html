doctrinally

1. ADV

/ ˈdɑktrənəli / (AmE), / ˈdɒktrɪnəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is based on doctrine; according to established or official teaching.

Examples

This is true for two reasons: because Communism is both doctrinally, and in practice, antithetical to these conditions; and because Communists have the will and, as long as Soviet power remains intact, the capacity to prevent their realization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrinal - relating to doctrine or principles

Etymology

From 'doctrinal' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'doctrina' (teaching).
