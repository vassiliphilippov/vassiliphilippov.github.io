flex

1. VERB

/ flɛks / (AmE), / flɛks / (BrE)
to contract or tighten a muscle, especially to show its shape or strength

Examples

Should these giants really flex their competitive muscles, they would become the only survivors in the industry.

Synset Examples

flex a muscle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relax - to make or become less tense or rigid (of muscles)

Etymology

From Latin 'flectere' meaning 'to bend'.
