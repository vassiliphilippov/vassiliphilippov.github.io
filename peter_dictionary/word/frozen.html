frozen

1. ADJ

/ ˈfroʊzən / (AmE), / ˈfrəʊzən / (BrE)
Turned into ice or affected by freezing or long and severe cold.

Examples

These cells were used rather than square Pyrex tubing because of the tendency of the latter to shatter when thawing frozen carbon tetrachloride.
On the second voyage, he had turned back at the frozen island of Novaya Zemlya and meekly given the crew a certificate stating that he did so of his own free will - which was obviously not the case.

Synset Examples

the frozen North
frozen pipes
children skating on a frozen brook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze - to turn into ice; to become solid because of cold
  • Antonym: thawed - no longer frozen; having returned to a liquid state after freezing

Etymology

Past participle of 'freeze', from Old English *freosan* (to freeze), of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfroʊzən / (AmE), / ˈfrəʊzən / (BrE)
Motionless or unable to move, typically as a result of shock, fear, or intense emotion.

Examples

I must state that the faster things happened, the slower they happened; the passage and rhythm of time changed, and when I remember back to what happened then, each event is a separate and frozen incident.
Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.

Synset Examples

frozen with horror
they stood rooted in astonishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionless - not moving; still
  • Synonym: paralyzed - unable to move, usually due to injury or shock
  • Synonym: rooted - unable to move from a spot, often from emotion

Etymology

Past participle of 'freeze,' from Old English 'frēosan' (to freeze).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfroʊzən / (AmE), / ˈfrəʊzən / (BrE)
(of a person's attitude, expression, or manner) lacking warmth or friendliness; showing unfriendliness or disdain.

Examples

O' Banion was a complex and frightening man, whose bright blue eyes stared with a kind of frozen candour into others'.

Synset Examples

a frigid greeting
got a frosty reception
a frozen look on their faces
a glacial handshake
icy stare
wintry smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosty - (of a manner or reception) unfriendly or unwelcoming
  • Synonym: frigid - lacking warmth, especially in manner or emotion
  • Antonym: cordial - warm and friendly; heartfelt

Etymology

Past participle of 'freeze', from Old English *frēosan* (to freeze).
