glory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːri / (BrE)
Brilliant radiant beauty that inspires admiration or wonder.

Examples

He suggests that Gore Court embodies all the glories of Tudor splendor.
The classical lines of the church which Napoleon thought of as a Temple of Glory, dominated all the scene where we sat.
Equally a master of prose and verse, he recreates the glory of Sweden in the past and continues it into the present.
As a boy in a local school he was shy and solitary, absorbed in his fondness for nature and his visions of Sweden's ancient glory.

Synset Examples

the glory of the sunrise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendor - magnificent and splendid appearance
  • Synonym: magnificence - impressively beautiful or elaborate appearance
  • Antonym: dullness - lacking brightness or beauty

Etymology

From Latin 'gloria' (fame, renown).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡlɔri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːri / (BrE)
to take great pride or pleasure in something; to rejoice proudly in.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exult - to show or feel elation; to rejoice greatly
  • Synonym: boast - to speak with excessive pride about oneself or achievements
  • Antonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret

Etymology

From Latin 'gloria' (fame, renown, praise).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔɹi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːri / (BrE)
High honor, praise, or distinction achieved by notable accomplishments or reputation.

Examples

That glory, craved for so long, was now forbidden to him.
He thought of Simms Purdew rising up, fearless in glory.
They get all the glory and most of the pay and do n't earn ten cents apiece on the average, the drunken rascals ".
He saw the dangers, not the glories of being identified as a mutineer.

Synset Examples

he valued glory above life itself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - high respect or esteem
  • Synonym: fame - being known by many people
  • Antonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect

Etymology

From Latin 'gloria' (fame, renown).
