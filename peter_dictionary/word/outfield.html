outfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌfiːld / (BrE)
the part of a baseball field beyond the infield, extending to the boundary fence, where outfielders are positioned

Examples

The Bears added their last run in the sixth on Alusik's double and outfield flies by Porter and Wert.
The infield was well flooded but the expanded outfield was much too dark.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infield - the area of a baseball field enclosed by the bases

Etymology

Formed from 'out' + 'field', referring to the area outside the infield.
