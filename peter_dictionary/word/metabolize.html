metabolize

1. VERB

/ məˈtæbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈtæbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to subject to metabolism; to process (a substance) within a living organism to obtain energy or build tissue

Examples

The vegetables were not readily metabolized by the bacteria in the aeration unit and tended to float on the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catabolize - to break down (substances) in metabolism for energy
  • Synonym: anabolize - to build up (substances) in metabolism for growth or repair

Etymology

From French métaboliser, from Greek metabolē (change, exchange) + -ize.
