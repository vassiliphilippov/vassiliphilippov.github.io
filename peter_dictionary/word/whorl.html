whorl

1. NOUN

/ wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
A pattern or formation in the shape of a series of concentric circles or spirals, often seen in leaves, flower petals, or fingerprints.

Examples

"Those lovely whorls ", he chortled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coil - a series of loops or spirals

Etymology

From Middle English 'whorle', probably related to 'whirl'.
