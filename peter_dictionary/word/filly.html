filly

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪli / (AmE), / ˈfɪli / (BrE)
a young female horse, typically under four years old

Examples

Gaining her second straight victory, Norman B. Small, Jr.'s Garden Fresh, a 3 - year old filly, downed promising colts in the $ 4500 St. Patrick's Day Purse, featured seventh race here today, and paid $ 7.20 straight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: colt - a young male horse, typically under four years old
  • Degree Higher: mare - an adult female horse
  • Degree Lower: foal - a young horse of either sex, especially under one year old

Etymology

From Middle English 'fili', probably from Old Norse 'fylja' (filly), diminutive of 'foli' (foal).
