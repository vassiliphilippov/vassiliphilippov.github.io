diplomacy

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈploʊməsi / (AmE), / dɪˈpləʊməsi / (BrE)
Skillful and tactful management or handling of people or situations.

Examples

He conducted it with less diplomacy and more spontaneous violence than the Sicilians, but he had his huge North Side portion to exploit and he made a great deal of money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of tact or skill in handling situations or people

Etymology

From French *diplomatie* (diplomacy), from Greek *diplōma* (folded paper, letter of recommendation).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈploʊməsi / (AmE), / dɪˈpləʊməsi / (BrE)
The practice of managing negotiations and relations between countries.

Examples

For, if so, the path leads through a complex process of parliamentary diplomacy which adds still another dimension to the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement

Etymology

From French 'diplomatie', from 'diplomate' (diplomat), ultimately from Greek 'diplōma' (folded paper).
