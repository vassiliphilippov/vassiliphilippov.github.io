doctrinal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑktrənl / (AmE), / ˈdɒktrɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to, involving, or concerned with doctrine or a system of beliefs, especially in religion or ideology.

Examples

A century ago, Newman saw that liberalism (what we now might call secularism) would gradually but definitely make its mark on English Protestantism, and that even high Anglicanism would someday no longer be a "serviceable breakwater against doctrinal errors more fundamental than its own ".

Synset Examples

quibbling over doctrinal minutiae

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogmatic - characterized by the assertion of unproved or unprovable principles as true

Etymology

From Late Latin doctrinalis, from Latin doctrina (teaching, doctrine).
