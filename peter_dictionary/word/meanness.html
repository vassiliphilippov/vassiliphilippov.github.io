meanness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːn.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being deliberately unkind, spiteful, or cruel.

Examples

cruelty, meanness, self-will.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate infliction of pain or suffering

Etymology

From mean + -ness, with 'mean' from Old English 'gemǣne' (common, shared) later acquiring the sense of 'ungenerous, nasty.'
