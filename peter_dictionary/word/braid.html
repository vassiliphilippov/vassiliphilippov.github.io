braid

1. NOUN

/ breɪd / (AmE), / breɪd / (BrE)
A hairstyle formed by weaving together three or more strands of hair.

Examples

She was wearing her dark hair in two, thick braids to attain an "American Girl "effect she thought was appropriate to Halloween.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plait - a hairstyle formed by intertwining strands of hair (chiefly British)

Etymology

From Middle English 'breyden' (to move quickly, to braid), from Old English 'bregdan' (to move quickly, intertwine).

2. VERB

/ breɪd / (AmE), / breɪd / (BrE)
to form something by interlacing three or more strands, such as hair, thread, or fabric

Examples

Then again, there's always that lovely old pastime of hooking or braiding rugs.

Synset Examples

lace a tablecloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plait - to form by interlacing strands, especially hair; chiefly British

Etymology

From Old English *bregdan* (to move quickly, weave), related to German *flechten* (to braid) and Dutch *breien* (to knit).
