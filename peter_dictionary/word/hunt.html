2. VERB

/ hʌnt / (AmE), / hʌnt / (BrE)
to pursue or chase relentlessly, especially to track down something or someone

Examples

The collector enjoys the contact with rural life; he hunts folklore for the very "field and stream "reasons that many persons hunt game; and only rarely is he acutely concerned with the meaning of what he has located.
She was a large woman with a frizzled gray poodle cut and a pencil clamped like a bit between her teeth while she hunted and pecked on an old typewriter.

Synset Examples

The hunters traced the deer into the woods
the detectives hounded the suspect until they found him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursue - to follow or chase after in order to catch or accomplish something
  • Synonym: chase - to follow rapidly in order to catch something or someone

Etymology

Old English huntian (to chase, capture), related to German 'hunden' (to hunt) and possibly from a Proto-Germanic root.

3. NOUN

/ hʌnt / (AmE), / hʌnt / (BrE)
An occasion when people pursue and kill or capture wild animals, regarded as a sport.

Examples

Did you have a good hunt?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunting - the activity or practice of pursuing and killing wild animals

Etymology

Old English *huntian* (to chase, capture), related to Old Norse *hunta* (to hunt).
