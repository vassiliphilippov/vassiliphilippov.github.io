promotion

1. NOUN

/ prəˈmoʊ.ʃən / (AmE), / prəˈməʊ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of raising someone to a higher rank or position in an organization, job, or other hierarchy

Examples

Digby was not pleased with his promotion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demotion - the act of lowering someone to a lower rank or position

Etymology

From Latin promotio, from promovere (to move forward) via Old French promotion.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / prəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the activity of publicizing or advertising a product, service, idea, or organization to increase sales or public awareness

Examples

Many companies have upgraded their sales manpower and tested new selling, distribution, and promotion techniques to gain a bigger competitive edge.
Display merchandising, backed by pre selling through advertising and promotion, will continue to make strides in the sixties.
From the manufacturer's point of view, the increasing cost of advertising and promotion is a very real problem to be faced in the sixties.
Have you evaluated the proper place of advertising and all phases of promotion in your total marketing program - from the standpoint of effort, money, and effectiveness?

Synset Examples

the packaging of new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertising - the activity or business of producing advertisements for commercial products or services
  • Synonym: publicity - the giving out of information about a product, person, or company for advertising or promotional purposes

Etymology

From Latin promotio (a moving forward, advancement), from promovere (to move forward).
