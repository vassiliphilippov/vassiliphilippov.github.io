guess

1. VERB

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
to suppose or believe something is probably true or likely, often without definite proof; to assume or expect

Examples

Edison could hardly have guessed, however, that Sophocles would one day appear in stereo.
Kahler continued: "I fixed his dog the other day and I guess he's sore, so I expected him to come barging in ".
"Call the Vouillemont, I guess ".
"I guess ".
When I was in liquor I rode him pretty hard I guess.

Synset Examples

I imagine she earned a lot of money with her new novel
I thought to find her in a bad state
he didn't think to find her in the kitchen
I guess she is angry at me for standing her up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppose - to think that something is likely to be true
  • Synonym: imagine - to form an idea or picture of something in your mind; to suppose
  • Synonym: expect - to regard something as likely or probable

2. VERB

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
to form an opinion or give an answer about something without being certain of all the facts; to estimate or suppose, often in the absence of complete evidence

Examples

If most of them were n't exactly specific - well, that's the way it is in life, I guess.
He guessed by their appearance that they were Rumanians.
She usually wore weeds, and a stranger watching her board a train might have guessed that Mr. Pastern was dead, but Mr. Pastern was far from dead.
But when he called for his withered, wrinkled sister Rose to care for him and the children, had he guessed that all he would remember of his woman was the memory of her climbing into that streetcar?
Youngish man on the make, Madden labeled him, and was ready to guess that in a correct, not too pushing fashion, the junior partner of the firm had political ambitions; that Mrs. Garth would be impeccably suitable as the wife of a rising young lawyer; that there were three children, two boys and a girl; that she was active in the Woman's Club and he in Lions, Rotary, and Jaycee; and finally, that neither of them had harbored an unorthodox opinion since their wedding day.

Synset Examples

I am guessing that the price of real estate will rise again
I cannot pretend to say that you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly judge or calculate the value or amount of something
  • Synonym: suppose - to assume something to be true without certain proof
  • Antonym: know - to be certain or aware of something based on facts or information

Etymology

From Middle English *gessen*, perhaps from Middle Dutch *gessen* or Middle Low German *gessen* (to guess).

3. NOUN

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
An estimate made without sufficient information or on little evidence.

Examples

One cannot make a very satisfactory guess about the micrometeorite flux in space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information
  • Synonym: estimate - a rough calculation or judgment of the value, number, quantity, or extent of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'gessen', probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian 'gissa' (to guess).

4. VERB

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
To form an opinion or estimate about something without sufficient information or relying on intuition rather than certain knowledge.

Examples

If, in a certain part of the range, it starts life 1 foot longer than do any of the other (relatively large) giants, and reaches maturity at, let us guess, 18 inches longer than the others, a quadrupling of the maturity length would result in a maximum of (nearly) 40 feet.
"Well, I guess I know enough to turn you inside out, you sockdologizing old mantrap "! said Trenchard, otherwise Hawk.
Thirty-five, I guess.
She doled out what Glendora vaguely guessed were the right amounts of dried peas, eggs, cornmeal, a little salt.
She said, "I guess the Lord looks out for fools, drunkards, and innocents ".

Synset Examples

I estimate this chicken to weigh three pounds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - form an approximate judgment or calculation
  • Antonym: calculate - determine mathematically

Etymology

From Middle English ges(s)en, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Middle Dutch gissen (to guess, surmise).

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
to arrive at the correct answer or solution without sufficient information, often by making an estimate or conjecture

Examples

Often he seems even to have been able to guess correctly, without the tracing motions, solely on the basis of qualitative differences among the blot like things which appeared in his visual experience.
As you have doubtless guessed already, the plot is plastered with Freudian, Jungian, and Meinckian theory.

It also weakened our diplomatic stance, because Russia could easily guess we did not desire a nuclear war except in the ultimate extremity.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of
  • Antonym: deduce - to arrive at a fact or conclusion by reasoning

Etymology

From Middle English gesen, gessen, possibly of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse geta (to guess, get, obtain).

6. NOUN

/ ɡɛs / (AmE), / ɡɛs / (BrE)
an opinion, estimate, or answer made without sufficient evidence or certainty

Examples

You read various guesses on how many Americans are camping.
Still, these guesses about the outcome of the struggle cannot be as important as the actual power relationship between the Soviet Union and ourselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - an approximate calculation or judgment

Etymology

From Middle English 'gessen', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'geta' (to get, to guess).
