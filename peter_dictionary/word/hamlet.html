hamlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmlət / (AmE), / ˈhæmlət / (BrE)
A small settlement, typically one smaller than a village and without its own church.

Examples

Rep. James Cotten of Weatherford insisted that a water development bill passed by the Texas House of Representatives was an effort by big cities like Dallas and Fort Worth to cover up places like Paradise, a Wise County hamlet of 250 people.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: village - a clustered human settlement or community larger than a hamlet but smaller than a town

Etymology

From Old French *hamelet*, diminutive of *hamel* (small village), from Old Frankish *haim* (home, village).
