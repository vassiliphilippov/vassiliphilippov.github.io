threshing

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of separating grain or seeds from their husks and straw, typically using a flail or threshing machine

Examples

He may have to depend upon custom service for specialized operations, such as spraying or threshing, and for these, he may have to wait his turn.

Synset Examples

they used to do the threshing by hand but now there are machines to do it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresh - to separate grain from husks and straw by beating

Etymology

From the verb 'thresh', from Old English 'þerscan' (to thresh, beat, thrash).
