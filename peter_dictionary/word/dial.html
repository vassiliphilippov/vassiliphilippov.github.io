dial

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪəl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əl / (BrE)
to select a telephone number by turning a dial or pressing buttons on a telephone

Examples

He dialed it and listened to it ring ten times before he hung up.
The thin man moved swiftly to the phone and dialed a number.
On a shelf in the office behind the counter was a small radio dialed permanently on a station which broadcast only vulgar commercials and cheap popular music.

Synset Examples

You must take the receiver off the hook before you dial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to make a telephone call to someone
  • Antonym: hang up - to end a telephone call

Etymology

From the noun 'dial', originally referring to the face of a timepiece or instrument, later applied to the rotary mechanism on telephones.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the flat, circular face of a clock, watch, or similar device, marked to show the time or measurements

Examples

He looked at the luminous dial of his wrist watch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - the surface of a clock showing the time

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'dialis' (daily), from Latin 'dies' (day).
