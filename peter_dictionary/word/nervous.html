nervous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrvəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs / (BrE)
easily agitated or anxious, especially as a result of stress or apprehension

Examples

Sarah Gross found that the treatments given her for a nervous ailment by the masseur were not helping her.
I went and, mum and nervous, all but made a fool of myself.
But this time she was nervous: she was open.
If anyone had asked her, she would have described herself only as nervous and worried.

Synset Examples

a nervous addict
a nervous thoroughbred

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety

Etymology

From Latin nervosus (sinewy, vigorous) from nervus (nerve).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɜrvəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs / (BrE)
showing or characterized by anxiety, worry, or unease

Examples

Kind of nervous from the trip.

Synset Examples

spent an anxious night waiting for the test results
cast anxious glances behind her
those nervous moments before takeoff
an unquiet mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease
  • Antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or anxiety

Etymology

From Latin nervosus (sinewy, vigorous), from nervus (nerve).
