pundit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌn.dɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌn.dɪt / (BrE)
A person who is an expert in a particular subject or field and is often called on to give opinions about it to the public.

Examples

But it is to say that this need is far more important for such infatuation than most of the pundits seem to have suspected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - A person with comprehensive knowledge in a field
  • Synonym: authority - A person with recognized knowledge in a subject area
  • Antonym: novice - A person new to a field or activity

Etymology

From Sanskrit *paṇḍita* (learned, scholar).
