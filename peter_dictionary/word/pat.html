pat

1. VERB

/ pæt / (AmE), / pæt / (BrE)
To touch someone or something lightly and repeatedly with the hand, especially in a gentle, fond, or playful manner.

Examples

Drenched and shaking, he stood near the sweet-smelling stall and dared to pat her muzzle.
One day, Ching had told him (smiling, patting him on the back) as they walked to the weekly conference of squad leaders, "Keep it up, your squad is good, one of the best, keep it up, keep up the good work ".
He patted the eye Joel had had blackened in a fight over being Rebel at the crossroads some days back.
Light sticks of fingers, the tips gummy with dark earth, patted at Watson's throat.
"I 've seen you, though, before ", she said with enormous coyness, patting his knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - to move one's hand with gentle pressure over a surface, typically repeatedly; to caress
  • Antonym: slap - to hit quickly with the flat part of the hand or an object

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, representing the sound of a light stroke.

2. VERB

/ pæt / (AmE), / pæt / (BrE)
to touch or hit someone or something lightly and repeatedly with your hand, usually as a sign of affection or to make something smooth

Examples

He had reached the three passive guards; he crept in an incertain manner, patting the ground before him.
At the doorway he squinted up at the gray morning overcast and patted his jacket pockets for the cigarettes, remembering then that he 'd left them at the Doughnuttery.

Synset Examples

pat him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap - to strike lightly, often to get attention
  • Degree Higher: slap - to hit with an open hand, often more forcefully than a pat

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, suggesting the sound or feel of a light tap.
