casino

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsinoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈsiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A public building or room where gambling games and entertainment are provided.

Examples

Charlie Marble was back and forth on several occasions, first to confer with Andy on the advisability of cancelling the Las Vegas engagement - they decided it was wise - and later to announce that a prominent comedian, also an agency client, had agreed to fill the casino's open date.
The Nacionale will go to the Lakeville Road Boys, the Capri to the Corleone family, the Sevilla Biltmore also, but Eddie Levine of Newport will bring in the Pennino brothers, Dino and Eddie, for a piece and also to handle the actual casino operations.

Etymology

From Italian 'casino', meaning 'little house', originally denoting a small country villa or social gathering place.
