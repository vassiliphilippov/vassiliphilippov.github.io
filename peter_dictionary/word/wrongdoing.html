wrongdoing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔŋˌduɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋˌduːɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or practice of doing something that is morally or ethically wrong; an immoral or illegal act.

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act that breaks the law
  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or illegal behavior
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From 'wrong' + 'doing', formed in English by compounding.
