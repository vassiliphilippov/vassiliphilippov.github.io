subtend

1. VERB

/ səbˈtɛnd / (AmE), / səbˈtɛnd / (BrE)
(of a side or object) to be opposite to and mark off (an angle or arc), especially in geometry; to extend under or form the limits of an angle subtended at a particular point.

Examples

The received radiation is compared with the radiation from a hypothetical black-body which subtends the same solid angle as the visible disk of the planet.
The half-intensity diameter of the antenna beam was about 9', and the angle subtended by the moon included the entire main beam and part of the first side lobes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppose - to be set in opposition to

Etymology

From Latin subtendere, from sub- (under) + tendere (to stretch).
