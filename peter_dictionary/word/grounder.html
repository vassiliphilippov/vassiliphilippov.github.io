grounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A ball that is hit so that it rolls or bounces along the ground.

Examples

Chico Ruiz made a spectacular play on Alusik's grounder in the hole in the fourth and Wert came up with some good stops and showed a strong arm at third base.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fly ball - (baseball) a ball hit high into the air
  • Antonym: line drive - (baseball) a ball hit hard in a nearly straight line through the air
