evangelist

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith, especially by public preaching of the gospel.

Examples

a former Democratic candidate for President, a New Deal poet, the magazine's chief editorial writer, two newspaper columnists, head of a national broadcasting company, a popular Protestant evangelist, etc..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preacher - person who gives religious sermons

Etymology

From Greek euangelistēs (bringer of good news), from euangelion (good news, gospel).
