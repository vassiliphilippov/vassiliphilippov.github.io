illusionary

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈluːʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒənəri / (BrE)
marked by or producing illusion; not real and intended to deceive or mislead

Examples

Melies, however, out of his professional instincts as a magician, discovered and made use of a number of illusionary techniques that remain part of the vocabulary of film.

Synset Examples

illusionary stage effects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusory - not real; based on illusion

Etymology

From 'illusion' + '-ary', after Latin 'illusio' (mockery, deception).
