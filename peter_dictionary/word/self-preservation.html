self-preservation

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˌprɛzɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌprɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The instinctive or natural act of protecting oneself from harm or danger.

Examples

The significant things in it, at the higher religious levels, are the inner emotional, mental, and spiritual occurrences that fill the pressing human needs of self-preservation, self pacification, and self completion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-defense - the act of defending oneself from physical harm
  • Antonym: altruism - the practice of selfless concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'preservation'.
