wallow

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləʊ / (BrE)
To indulge oneself completely in a particular state, emotion, or activity, especially one that is pleasurable or comforting, often to an excessive or self-absorbed degree.

Examples

Thirty years ago, while the nation was wallowing in economic depression, the prevailing philosophy of government was to stand aside and allow "natural forces "to operate and cure the distress.
She said to Maggie that it was one thing to meet an emergency and another to wallow in it, and it was beginning to look at if this one was going to last forever.

Synset Examples

Wallow in luxury
wallow in your sorrows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxuriate - to enjoy oneself without restraint; take great pleasure
  • Synonym: revel - to take great pleasure or delight in something
  • Antonym: restrain - to hold oneself back; to limit indulgence

Etymology

Old English *wealwian* (to roll about, wallow).
