blackberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈblækˌbəri / (BrE)
A large, sweet, black or very dark purple edible fruit made up of small drupelets, produced by various bushes of the genus Rubus.

Examples

Whenever she found time, she went blackberry picking with him, and they would come home together, mouths purple, arms and faces scratched, tired enough to forget grief for another day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a similar aggregate fruit, typically red or yellow, also from bushes of the genus Rubus

Etymology

From Old English blæcberie ('blæc' meaning 'black' + 'berie' meaning 'berry').
