monkish

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌŋkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkɪʃ / (BrE)
Relating to or typical of monks, especially in showing self-denial or simplicity.

Examples

Each wore the monkish scourge at his waist but this, it seems, was not employed for self-flagellation.

Etymology

From 'monk' + '-ish', forming adjectives meaning 'like a monk'.
