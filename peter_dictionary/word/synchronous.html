synchronous

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋkrənəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrənəs / (BrE)
occurring or operating at the same time or having the same period or phase.

Examples

Thus, a low intensity of hypothalamic cortical discharges prevails in sleep and a high one during wakefulness, resulting in synchronous EEG potentials in the former and asynchrony in the latter condition.

Synset Examples

recovery was synchronous with therapy
a synchronous set of clocks
the synchronous action of a bird's wings in flight
synchronous oscillations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneous - existing, happening, or done at the same time
  • Antonym: asynchronous - not occurring or existing at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos', from 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).
