meddling

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛd.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of interfering in a situation without invitation or necessity, often in a way that is improper or unwelcome.

Examples

All Southerners agree that slavery had to go; but many historians maintain that except for Northern meddling it would have ended in states like Virginia years before it did.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - the act of interfering or intervening
  • Synonym: intrusion - the act of entering or taking part without invitation or welcome

Etymology

From the verb 'meddle', ultimately from Old French 'medler' (to mix), from Vulgar Latin *misculare.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdlɪŋ / (BrE)
Interfering in other people's affairs in an intrusive or unwelcome manner.

Examples

Why, oh why, does n't he stick to preaching the Gospel, instead of meddling in civic affairs, politics, economics, and social issues that are no concern of the Church "?
Furthermore, Bern decrees that we must do as we are ordered by the Council, preach only the word of God and stop meddling in politics "!

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interfering - involving oneself in a situation without invitation or necessity
  • Synonym: officious - asserting authority in an annoyingly domineering way

Etymology

From verb 'meddle', from Old French 'medler' (to mix), from Latin 'miscēre' (to mix).
