gross

1. ADJ

/ ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
Lacking fine distinctions or detail; not nuanced or precise.

Examples

The gross details of the structure appear reasonable.

Synset Examples

the gross details of the structure appear reasonable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approximate - close to the actual, but not completely accurate or exact
  • Synonym: rough - not exact or precise; approximate
  • Antonym: detailed - characterized by great attention to detail

2. ADJ

/ ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
(of a person) extremely or unpleasantly fat.

Examples

It was amazing how they had herded together for protection: an enormous matriarch in a quilted silk wrapper, rising from the breakfast table; a gross boy in his teens, shuffling in from the kitchen with a sandwich in his hands; a girl in her twenties, fat and sullen, descending the marble staircase; then all four gathering on the sofa to face the inquisitor.

Synset Examples

a bald porcine old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obese - very overweight, medically fat
  • Degree Lower: overweight - above a weight considered normal or healthy
  • Antonym: slim - thin in an attractive way

3. ADJ

/ ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
(of an amount, especially of money) total before any deductions, such as taxes or expenses.

Examples

The total is the probable gross income from farming.
To find estimated net farm income, subtract estimated annual farming expenditures from probable gross income from farming.
Some deductions are subtracted from Gross Income to determine Adjusted Gross Income.
To compute your adjusted gross income you total all items of income.
Sick pay, if included in your Gross Income, is deducted in arriving at Adjusted Gross Income.

Synset Examples

gross income

Related Terms

  • Antonym: net - remaining after deductions, especially from total income or profit

Etymology

From Old French *gros* (large, thick), from Latin *grossus* (thick, coarse).

4. NOUN

/ ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
the total amount of income or revenue before any deductions, such as taxes or expenses, are made

Examples

Plus a monthly payment of five percent of the gross of all four hotels, Mr., uh, [EMPHASIZES ITALIAN PRONUNCIATION] Corleone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: net - income or amount remaining after all deductions have been made

Etymology

From Old French *gros* (large, thick) and Latin *grossus* (thick, coarse).
