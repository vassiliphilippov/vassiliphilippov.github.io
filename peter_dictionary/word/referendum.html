referendum

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛf.əˈrɛn.dəm / (AmE), / ˌrɛf.əˈrɛn.dəm / (BrE)
A general vote by the electorate on a single political question that has been referred to them for a direct decision.

Examples

An adverse vote of 81 to 65 kept in the State Affairs Committee a bill which would order the referendum on the April 4 ballot, when Texas votes on a U.S. senator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plebiscite - a direct vote by the electorate on an important public question

Etymology

From Latin 'referendum', neuter gerundive of 'referre' (to bring back, refer).
