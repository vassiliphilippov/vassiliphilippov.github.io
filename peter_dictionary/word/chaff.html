chaff

1. VERB

/ tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
to tease or make fun of someone in a good-natured or playful way

Examples

When they stood about his tent, chaffing each other, exchanging their obscenities, cursing command or weather, he had studied their faces.

Synset Examples

After we relaxed, we just kidded around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kid - to tease or joke with someone playfully
  • Synonym: banter - to exchange playful remarks

Etymology

Probably from verb use of 'chaff' (nonsense talk), derived from 'chaff' (husks), suggesting something light and trivial.
