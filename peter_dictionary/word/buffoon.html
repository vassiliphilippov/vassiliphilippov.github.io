buffoon

1. NOUN

/ bəˈfun / (AmE), / bəˈfuːn / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a ridiculous or foolish way, often intended to entertain others; a clown or fool.

Examples

It is really as though the Russians have seen in this character the oftentimes underlying vitality and courage of supposed buffoons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a comic entertainer or foolish person
  • Synonym: jester - a professional joker or fool at a medieval court
  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment; a jester

Etymology

From French 'bouffon', from Italian 'buffone', from 'buffa' (jest, puff of wind), related to Latin 'buffare' (to puff).
