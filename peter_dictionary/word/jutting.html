jutting

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
extending out above or beyond a surface or boundary

Examples

The legation was generously set back from the building line; if the angle of fire were too great the jutting buildings on either side would interfere.
It speared up into the air, then sinking back, the up jutting branch turned slowly.

Synset Examples

the jutting limb of a tree
massive projected buttresses
his protruding ribs
a pile of boards sticking over the end of his truck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projecting - extending outward beyond a surface
  • Antonym: recessed - set back or indented from a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'jut', probably from Middle English 'jouten', of uncertain origin.
