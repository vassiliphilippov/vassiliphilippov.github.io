suite

1. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
A set of connected rooms, especially in a hotel, used as a living unit.

Examples

The place was evidently a familiar haunt and Claire wondered what other illicit loves had been celebrated in the comfortable rooms to which they were shown.
I did book jackets and covers for paperback reprints: naked girls huddling in corners of dingy furnished rooms while at the doorway, daring the cops to take him, is the guy in shirt sleeves clutching a revolver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment - a set of rooms for living in, usually on one floor of a building
  • Degree Lower: room - a separate part of a building, usually used for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'suite' (sequence), from Old French 'siute', from Latin 'sequī' (to follow).

2. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
A group of attendants or followers accompanying an important person.

Examples

As the afternoon sped toward evening, the suite saw a steady procession of Paxton aides pass in and out, each with his own special problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retinue - a group of advisers, assistants, or others accompanying an important person
  • Synonym: entourage - a group of people attending or surrounding an important person
  • Synonym: cortege - a solemn procession, especially for a funeral; can indicate a group following an important person

Etymology

From French 'suite', from Old French, from 'suir' (to follow).

3. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
A musical composition consisting of a set of several short pieces or movements, usually in the same key and intended to be performed together.

Examples

We attended the premieres of his concertos, symphonies, and suites; we studied, taught, and performed his piano sonatas, chamber music, gavottes, and marches; we bought his records and played them in our schools and universities.
By September 1940 the suite had developed into a collection of six songs, "four spirituals, a dream, and a lullaby ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonata - a composition typically for a solo instrument with several movements
  • Synonym: concerto - a musical work composed for orchestra and solo instrument(s)

Etymology

From French *suite* (sequence), from Old French, from Latin *sequere* (to follow).

4. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
A set of connected rooms used as a living or working unit, especially in a hotel or office building.

Examples

What had been the ambassador's suite was now jagged walls of blackened brick.
Promptly at one-thirty he entered Hohlbein and Garth's elegant suite of offices in Medfield's newest professional building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment - a set of rooms for living in, usually on one floor of a building
  • Degree Lower: room - a part or division of a building enclosed by walls, floor, and ceiling

Etymology

From French *suite* (a sequence, suite), from Old French *siute*, from *suivre* (to follow).
