runner

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
In baseball, a player of the team at bat who is on a base or attempting to reach one.

Examples

Then Robinson slammed a long double to left center to score both runners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - a player who is hitting in baseball

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who smuggles goods into or out of a country without paying customs duties, often used in phrases such as 'blockade runner' or 'rum runner'

Examples

One of the beer runners telephoned O' Banion - on a line tapped by the detective bureau - and reported the situation.
But in the meantime the beer runner, unhappy with this solution, telephoned Torrio and returned to O' Banion with the message: "Say, Dionie, I just been talking to Johnny, and he said to let them cops have the three hundred.
Blockade runners can be stopped - by gunfire, if necessary - searched and held, at least temporarily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - a person who illegally transports goods to avoid customs duties

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who runs, especially as a sport or as part of their job

Examples

Alexander the Great, who used runners as message carriers, did not have to worry about having every officer in his command hear what he said and having hundreds of them comment at once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinter - a runner who runs short distances at high speed
  • Antonym: walker - a person who travels on foot by walking

Etymology

From Middle English *runnen* (to run) + -er (agent noun suffix).

4. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɚ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to deliver messages or documents, especially within an office or between locations

Examples

Years ago this was true, but with the replacement of wires or runners by radio and radar (and perhaps television), these restrictions have disappeared and now again too much is heard.

Synset Examples

he sent a runner over with the contract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messenger - a person who carries messages or does an errand
