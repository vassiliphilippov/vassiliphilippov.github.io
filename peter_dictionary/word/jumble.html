jumble

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)
A confused mixture or collection of different things.

Examples

Warren tried to shake off the jumble of his fears by looking at the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clutter - a collection of things lying about in an untidy mass

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *jumble*, variant of *jumble* (to mix confusedly), origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to mix or be mixed together in a confused or untidy manner

Examples

Her words jumbled together and she all but ran from the office and from the question in Rev's face.

Synset Examples

His words jumbled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend into one mass or mixture
  • Synonym: tangle - to twist together in a confused mass
  • Antonym: sort - to arrange systematically; to separate into categories

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *jumble* (to move in a confused way), related to 'jumble' (noun).
