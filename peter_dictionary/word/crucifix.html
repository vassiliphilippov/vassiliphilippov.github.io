crucifix

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːsɪˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfɪks / (BrE)
A cross with a representation of Jesus' body on it, used especially in the Christian Church as a symbol of faith.

Examples

When we got there, Trig and the Negro were quarreling over possession of a gold crucifix around the neck of a wounded Filipino.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cross - A Christian symbol consisting of an upright post with a transverse bar

Etymology

From Latin crucifixus, past participle of crucifigere (to crucify), from crux (cross) + figere (to fix).
