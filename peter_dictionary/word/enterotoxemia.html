enterotoxemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəroʊtɑkˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌɛntərəʊtɒkˈsiːmɪə / (BrE)
A disease of cattle and sheep caused by absorbing toxins from the intestines, often resulting in sudden death.

Examples

As an aid in reducing losses due to enterotoxemia (overeating disease), feed a complete ration containing not less than 20 and not more than 50 grams of Aureomycin per ton.

Etymology

From 'entero-' (relating to the intestines) + 'toxemia' (presence of toxins in the blood).
