tearfully

1. ADV

/ ˈtɪr.fəli / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows you are crying or about to cry; with tears in your eyes.

Examples

Though there was an occasional good-natured chuckle about Marvin Goulding, the Jewish officer from Chicago, singing tearfully about the ould sod, no one really thought it was strange.

Synset Examples

the man confessed tearfully to having beaten his wife

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy and cheerful manner
  • Synonym: tearful - showing or inclined to tears; full of tears

Etymology

From 'tearful' + '-ly', forming an adverb from the adjective 'tearful'.
