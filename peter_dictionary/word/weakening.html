weakening

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or result of becoming weaker or less strong.

Examples

My interviews with teen-agers confirmed this portrait of the weakening of religious and ethnic bonds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strengthening - the process of becoming stronger

Etymology

From 'weaken' (to make or become weak) + '-ing', forming a noun from the verb.
