gravel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævəl / (AmE), / ˈɡravəl / (BrE)
Small, loose rock fragments and pebbles, often used for making paths, driveways, or as a building material.

Examples

The space between them is filled with pit-run gravel or earth.
The roof shown here (fig. 9) is a 6 - inch slab of reinforced concrete, covered with at least 20 inches of pit-run gravel.
It would have to be covered with at least 28 inches of pit-run gravel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sand - fine granular material resulting from the disintegration of rocks
  • Degree Lower: pebble - a small, smooth, rounded stone, especially at the edge of water
  • Degree Higher: shingle - coarse, loose, waterworn pebbles that make up a beach

Etymology

From Old French *gravele*, diminutive of *grave* ('gravel, sand'), related to the Celtic root.
