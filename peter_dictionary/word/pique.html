pique

1. NOUN

/ piˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpiːkeɪ / (BrE)
A tightly woven fabric, usually of cotton, with raised parallel cords or ribs, often used for shirts, dresses, and uniforms.

Examples

The maid was very black and very energetic, trim in a yellow pique uniform.

Etymology

From French 'piqué', past participle of 'piquer' (to prick, stitch).
