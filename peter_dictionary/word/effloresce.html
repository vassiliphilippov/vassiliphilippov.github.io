effloresce

1. VERB

/ ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) to bloom or blossom; (figurative) to reach an optimal stage of development or come into full flower.

Examples

In time, these minor manifestations will multiply and effloresce, riddling you with frambesia, the king's evil, sheep rot, and clonic spasm, until your very existence becomes a burden and you cry out for release.

Synset Examples

These manifestations effloresced in the past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossom - (of a tree or plant) to produce flowers or masses of flowers

Etymology

From Latin efflorescere, from ex- (out) + florescere (begin to bloom), from flos (flower).
