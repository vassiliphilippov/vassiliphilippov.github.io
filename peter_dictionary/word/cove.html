cove

1. NOUN

/ koʊv / (AmE), / kəʊv / (BrE)
A small sheltered bay or inlet along a coast.

Examples

The red glow from the cove had died out of the sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bay - a broader indentation in the coastline, larger than a cove

Etymology

From Old English *cofa* (chamber, den, cave), related to Old Norse *kofi* (hut), of Germanic origin.
