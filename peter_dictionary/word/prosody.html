prosody

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsədi / (AmE), / ˈprɒsədi / (BrE)
the patterns of rhythm, stress, and intonation in speech

Examples

This restates the already widely known facts in terms of prosodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of pitch in speech
  • Degree Lower: meter - the rhythmic structure in poetry

Etymology

From Latin 'prosodia', from Ancient Greek 'prosōidía' (song sung to music, intonation).
