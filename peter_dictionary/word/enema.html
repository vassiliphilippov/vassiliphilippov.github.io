enema

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnəmə / (AmE), / ˈɛnəmə / (BrE)
A procedure involving the injection of liquid into the rectum through the anus, usually to stimulate evacuation of the bowels or for diagnostic purposes.

Examples

This town needs an enema!

Etymology

From Greek enienai ‘send or put in’, from en- ‘in’ + hienai ‘to send’.
