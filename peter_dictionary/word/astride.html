astride

1. ADV

/ əˈstraɪd / (AmE), / əˈstraɪd / (BrE)
with one leg on each side of something; straddling

Examples

It raced closer and they could see a woman with white hair, sitting astride an upright branch.

Synset Examples

she sat astride the chair

Etymology

From Middle English, prefix a- (on) + stride (to stride), meaning 'on the stride.'
