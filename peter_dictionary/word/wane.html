wane

1. VERB

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
to decrease gradually in size, amount, intensity, or degree; to become weaker or less important

Examples

Cobb watched this with hunted eyes, his desperate hope waning by the moment.

Synset Examples

Interest in his novels waned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminish - to become or cause to become less
  • Antonym: increase - to become larger, greater, or more
  • Synonym: decline - to become smaller, weaker, or less important

Etymology

From Old English 'wanian' (to lessen, decrease), related to 'wan' (deficient).

2. VERB

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
(of a feeling, power, or quality) to decrease gradually in size, amount, intensity, or degree.

Examples

His adventurous spirit had waned; he studied the pistol exhibition that Hurrays featured as an added attraction.

Synset Examples

Interest in the project waned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminish - to become or make less
  • Antonym: increase - to become greater in size, amount or degree
  • Antonym: wax - to grow or become larger (especially used of the moon; opposite of 'wane')

Etymology

From Old English 'wanian', meaning to lessen or decrease.
