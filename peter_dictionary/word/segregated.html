segregated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛɡ.rəˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.rɪˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of people, groups, or institutions) separated or kept apart from others, especially on the basis of race, religion, or other differences.

Examples

They are segregated businesses, combining again on one site the factory and the office, drawing their work force from segregated communities.
They are segregated businesses, combining again on one site the factory and the office, drawing their work force from segregated communities.

Synset Examples

a segregated school system
a segregated neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrated - not separated; combined into a whole, especially referring to groups of people

Etymology

From Latin segregare, meaning 'to separate from the flock'; from se- (apart) + grex (flock).
