marinate

1. VERB

/ ˈmærɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmærɪneɪt / (BrE)
to soak food, especially meat or fish, in a seasoned liquid before cooking to add flavor or tenderize

Examples

- An unusual flavor can be achieved by marinating the franks in French dressing or a mixture of honey, lemon juice and brown sugar prior to the picnic.
She always let it be known that there was wine in the pot roast or that the chicken had been marinated in brandy, and that Koussevitzky's second cousin was an intimate of theirs.

Synset Examples

marinade herring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to immerse in liquid for a period of time

Etymology

From Spanish 'marinar' (to pickle in brine), from 'marino' (marine), related to preserving food with salt water.
