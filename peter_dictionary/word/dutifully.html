dutifully

1. ADV

/ ˈduːtɪfli, ˈdjuːtɪfli / (AmE), / ˈdjuːtɪfli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows a sense of duty or obligation; performing actions because they are expected or required.

Examples

That I served my country faithfully and honorably in World War II and was awarded the Navy Cross for actions in defense of my country.

Synset Examples

he dutifully visited his mother every Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithfully - in a loyal or conscientious manner
  • Antonym: reluctantly - in a hesitant or unwilling manner

Etymology

From 'dutiful' + '-ly', formed from 'duty' (from Old French 'deu', from Latin 'debitum', meaning 'something owed').
