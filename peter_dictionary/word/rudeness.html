rudeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrud.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruːd.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being rude; offensive or insulting behavior or manners.

Examples

Eugene was not entirely silent, or openly rude - unless asking Harold to move to another chair and placing himself in the fauteuil that creaked so alarmingly was an act of rudeness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courtesy - polite behavior or manners
  • Antonym: politeness - the quality of being polite
  • Synonym: discourtesy - lack of courtesy; rudeness

Etymology

From Middle English 'rudnesse', from Old English 'rūdnys', from 'rūd' (rude) + '-ness'.
