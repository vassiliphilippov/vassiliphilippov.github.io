satin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætn̩ / (AmE), / ˈsætɪn / (BrE)
A smooth, glossy fabric, usually made of silk or a synthetic fiber, with a shiny front and a dull back.

Examples

With a tiny fur-piece wrapped around her shoulders, she wiggled her satin covered buttocks down the street before him and did n't stop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk - fiber or fabric made from silkworms, often with a glossy finish
  • Synonym: taffeta - crisp, smooth fabric often used for dresses
  • Antonym: matte - having a non-glossy, dull finish

Etymology

From Old French *satin*, possibly from Arabic *zaytūnī* (from Zaytun, old name for Quanzhou in China, a trading center for silk).
