twinge

1. NOUN

/ twɪndʒ / (AmE), / twɪndʒ / (BrE)
A sudden, sharp feeling of pain or emotion.

Examples

Prickly twinges of annoyance ran through him.
I looked over their faces and felt a twinge: they all looked so much more knowing than I.

Synset Examples

pangs of regret
she felt a stab of excitement
twinges of conscience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pang - a sudden sharp feeling of pain or distress
  • Synonym: stab - a sudden, sharp pain or feeling

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps a backformation from Middle English 'twengen' (to pinch or squeeze).
