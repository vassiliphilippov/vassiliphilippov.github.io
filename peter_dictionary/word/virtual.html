virtual

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
Almost or nearly as described, but not completely or according to strict definition; existing in effect though not in fact.

Examples

Since the hero, a sterling and upright fellow, is a rich Brown senior, while two Yalies are cast as virtual rapists, I suppose I should disqualify myself from sitting in judgment on "Where the Boys are ", but I shall do nothing of the sort.

Synset Examples

a practical failure
the once elegant temple lay in virtual ruin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actual - real and existing in fact, not just in appearance or effect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin virtualis, from Latin virtus (virtue, power).
