claret

1. NOUN

/ ˈklærət / (AmE), / ˈklærət / (BrE)
A dark purplish-red color, resembling that of claret wine.

Examples

Suddenly he was interrupted in his daydreaming by a warm wetness lapping against his chin, and his eyes opened wide and long at the sight of a goat's claret tongue, feasting against the salt taste of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgundy - a deep red color, similar to that of Burgundy wine
  • Degree Lower: maroon - a dark brownish-red color
  • Degree Higher: crimson - a rich deep red color inclining to purple

Etymology

Named after claret wine, from French 'clairet', a type of Bordeaux wine.
