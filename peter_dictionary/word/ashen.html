ashen

1. ADJ

/ ˈæʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈæʃ.ən / (BrE)
Pale or grayish in color, typically from illness, shock, or strong emotion.

Examples

John stood above him, his face ashen.

Synset Examples

a face turned ashen
the invalid's blanched cheeks
tried to speak with bloodless lips
a face livid with shock
lips...livid with the hue of death
lips white with terror
a face white with rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - light in color or lacking normal color, especially of the face
  • Synonym: livid - having a discolored or bruised appearance, sometimes used for pale from shock
  • Antonym: flushed - reddened, especially of the face (opposite of ashen in color)

Etymology

From ash + -en, meaning 'made of ash' or 'ash-colored'.
