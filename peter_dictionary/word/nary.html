nary

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛɚi / (AmE), / ˈneəri / (BrE)
(used with a singular count noun, especially in the phrase 'nary a') Not a single; not any.

Examples

The police dragged that river for three days, and nary a gun was found.

Synset Examples

heard nary a sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: none - not any; not one
  • Synonym: not a - phrase meaning not one; no

Etymology

Contraction of 'ne'er a' (never a), with roots in Middle English 'ne'er' (never) and 'a' (one).
