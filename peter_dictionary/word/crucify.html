crucify

1. VERB

/ ˈkruːsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
To treat someone with extreme cruelty, harsh criticism, or torment, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

He really crucified him.

Synset Examples

The children tormented the stuttering teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to inflict severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Latin crucifigare, meaning 'to fasten to a cross.'

2. VERB

/ ˈkruːsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to kill (someone) by nailing or binding them to a cross, especially as a method of execution in ancient times

Examples

A fellow came up to me, a Senator, I do n't have to tell you his name, and he told me,' I love the President like a brother, but God damn it, he's crucifying me.
Two thieves are crucified with Christ, one saved and the other damned.

Synset Examples

Jesus Christ was crucified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execute - to put someone to death as a legal or official punishment
  • Degree Lower: torture - to inflict severe pain as punishment or to force information

Etymology

From Latin crucifīgāre (to fasten to a cross), from crux (cross) + figere (to fasten).
