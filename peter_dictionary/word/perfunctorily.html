perfunctorily

1. ADV

/ pərˈfʌŋktərəli / (AmE), / pəˈfʌŋkt(ə)rəli / (BrE)
In a way that is done quickly and without giving enough attention or effort.

Examples

Heading for the batwings, Cobb glanced perfunctorily through the nearest window, and suddenly dodged aside.

Synset Examples

they answered my letter pro forma
he kissed her cheek perfunctorily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficially - without thoroughness or depth; on the surface
  • Antonym: thoroughly - in a detailed and careful way

Etymology

From Latin 'perfunctorius' (done carelessly), from 'perfungi' (to discharge, get through with).
