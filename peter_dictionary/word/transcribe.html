transcribe

1. VERB

/ trænˈskaɪb / (AmE), / trænˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to write or print a word or text in a different script or alphabet

Examples

In representing part of this new picture, I will be recounting some of my own personal experiences, reactions and judgments; but my primary aim is to transcribe what Englishmen themselves are saying and writing and implying about the Roman and Anglican Churches and about the present religious state of England.

Synset Examples

The Sanskrit text had to be transliterated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transliterate - to represent words or letters in the characters of another alphabet

Etymology

From Latin transcribere, from trans- (across) + scribere (to write).

2. VERB

/ trænˈskraɪb / (AmE), / trænˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to make a written copy of spoken words, notes, or another text; to write out or record in writing what has been spoken or recorded

Examples

Related to written literature, and often remaining temporarily frozen in written form, it loses its vitality when transcribed or removed from its oral existence.

Synset Examples

Transcribe the oral history of this tribe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to capture spoken words or sounds in a permanent form
  • Antonym: translate - to express the meaning of speech or text in a different language (sometimes confused, but distinct process)

Etymology

From Latin transcribere, from trans- (across) + scribere (to write).
