ebb

1. VERB

/ ɛb / (AmE), / ɛb / (BrE)
(of the tide) to flow back or recede; to diminish or lessen gradually.

Examples

Harmony, melody, counterpoint symphonic structure are there; and as this music ebbs and flows, there is an antiphonal chorus from all the atoms outside, in fact from the atoms of the entire universe.

Synset Examples

the tides ebbed at noon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wane - to decrease in power or vigor, as in the moon or a feeling
  • Antonym: flow - to move steadily and continuously in one direction (opposite of ebb)

Etymology

From Old English *ebbian* (to ebb), related to Old Norse *efja* (to ebb), from Proto-Germanic *abjan*.
