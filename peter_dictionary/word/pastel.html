pastel

1. ADJ

/ pæˈstɛl / (AmE), / ˈpæstl̩ / (BrE)
(of a color) delicate and pale rather than bright or strong.

Examples

While white is the coolest summer shade, there are lots of pastel hues along with tintable fabrics that will blend with any wardrobe color.

Synset Examples

pastel pink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - light in color or shade; lacking intensity of color
  • Antonym: vivid - (of a color) intensely deep or bright

Etymology

From French *pastel* (crayon made from powdered pigment), ultimately from Late Latin *pastellus* (pastel, woad paste).

2. NOUN

/ pæˈstɛl / (AmE), / paˈstɛl / (BrE)
a pale or light shade of color

Examples

Orange and lemon are considered important as are such pastels as blue and lilac.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - light in color or shade; lacking intensity

Etymology

From French 'pastel', originally referring to a plant-based blue dye.

3. ADJ

/ pæˈstɛl / (AmE), / ˈpæstl / (BrE)
(of a color, sound, style, or quality) lacking in body or vigor; pale or subdued in character.

Examples

The already faded pastel charms of the naive music itself vanish entirely in Fistoulari's melodramatic contrasts between ultra vehement brute power and chilly, if suave, sentimentality.

Synset Examples

faded pastel charms of the naive music

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vivid - strong, intense, or powerful in color or character

Etymology

From French 'pastel' (crayon for drawing), ultimately from Late Latin 'pastellus'.
