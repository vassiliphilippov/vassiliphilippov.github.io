railing

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
A barrier made of one or more horizontal bars supported by posts, often used alongside stairs, balconies, or platforms for safety or as a boundary.

Examples

One lithe leg straddled the railing and swung loosely before the creaking, torn pales.
Griffith had confronted Hoag on the building's front steps - Hoag had been permitted no further - and backed him against a wrought-iron railing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fence - a barrier enclosing or bordering a field, yard, etc.
  • Synonym: balustrade - a railing supported by balusters, especially on a balcony, bridge, or terrace

Etymology

From rail + -ing, originally Middle English; 'rail' from Old French reille (bar of a window), from Latin regula (straight stick, bar).
