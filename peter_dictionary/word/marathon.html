marathon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærəˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈmærəθən / (BrE)
A long and difficult activity or process, especially one requiring great effort and endurance.

Examples

The occasion for this marathon: Angel's long awaited reissue in its "Great Recordings of the Century "series of the Schnabel-Pro Arte version.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordeal - a very difficult or unpleasant experience
  • Antonym: sprint - a short, fast, intense effort

Etymology

From the Greek Marathon, the site of a battle in 490 BC, and the legendary long run to Athens. Used figuratively since the 20th century.
