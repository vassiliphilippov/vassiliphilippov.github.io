knuckleball

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌkl̩ˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈnʌk.l̩.bɔːl / (BrE)
a baseball pitch thrown so as to minimize spin, making its path unpredictable

Examples

Skinny Brown and Hoyt Wilhelm, the Flock's veteran knuckleball specialists, are slated to oppose the American League champions in tomorrow's 8 P.M. contest.

Etymology

From 'knuckle' + 'ball', reflecting the grip used to throw the pitch.
