dexamethasone

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛksəˈmɛθəˌsoʊn / (AmE), / ˌdɛksəˈmiːθəsəʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic corticosteroid drug used to treat allergies, inflammation, and certain types of cancer.

Examples

From March 3 to May 1, 1949, the patient was maintained on dexamethasone, 3 to 6 mg. daily.
In May 1959, prednisone, 30 mg. daily, replaced the dexamethasone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prednisone - a synthetic corticosteroid drug used as an anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressant

Etymology

From 'deca-' (ten) and 'methasone' (structural group in corticosteroids), formed to indicate chemical structure.
