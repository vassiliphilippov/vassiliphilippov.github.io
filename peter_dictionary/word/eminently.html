eminently

1. ADV

/ ˈɛmɪnəntli / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnəntli / (BrE)
To a notable or outstanding degree; extremely.

Examples

Research, on the other hand, has shown many stepmothers to be eminently successful, some far better than the real mothers.

Synset Examples

two subjects on which he was eminently qualified to make an original contribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree; extremely
  • Synonym: notably - in a notable or remarkable manner
  • Degree Lower: moderately - to a moderate extent or degree; not very or extremely

Etymology

From 'eminent' + '-ly', where 'eminent' is from Latin 'eminens' (standing out, prominent).
