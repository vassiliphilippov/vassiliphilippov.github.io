archaize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːr.keɪˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɑː.kɪ.aɪz / (BrE)
to give something an archaic appearance or character; to make appear old-fashioned or antique

Examples

Many potters clung to the past the more determinedly as they were confronted with radically new ideas; the poet of the Iliad deliberately archaized.

Synset Examples

archaized craftwork

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modernize - to give a modern character or appearance to

Etymology

From Greek 'archaios' (ancient) via French or directly from Medieval Latin 'archaizare'.
