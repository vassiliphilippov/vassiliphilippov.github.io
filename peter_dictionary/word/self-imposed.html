self-imposed

1. ADJ

/ sɛlf ɪmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / sɛlf ɪmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
(of a task, duty, or restriction) chosen or imposed by oneself rather than by an external authority.

Examples

Surrounded by crime and violence everywhere, the "hardboiled "private eye can retain his purity only through a life of self-imposed isolation.

Synset Examples

self-imposed exile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voluntary - done, given, or acting of one's own free will
  • Antonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; compulsory

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'imposed', meaning imposed by oneself.
