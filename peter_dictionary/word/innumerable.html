innumerable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnuːmərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːmərəbəl / (BrE)
Too many to be counted; countless.

Examples

Hastening to the attic, the temperature of which was easily hotter than the Gold Coast, you proceeded to mask the windows with a fancy wool coverlet, some khaki pants, and the like, and to ransack the innumerable boxes and barrels stored there.

Synset Examples

incalculable riches
countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars
untold thousands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too numerous to be counted; innumerable
  • Synonym: myriad - a countless or very great number
  • Antonym: few - a small number
  • Antonym: numerable - able to be counted

Etymology

From Latin 'innumerabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'numerabilis' (that can be numbered).
