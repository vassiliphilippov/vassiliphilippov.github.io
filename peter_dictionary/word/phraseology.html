phraseology

1. NOUN

/ freɪziˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfreɪziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The particular choice and arrangement of words in speaking or writing; wording or style of expression.

Examples

Heydrich, in opening the Conference, followed the reasoning and even the phraseology of the order issued earlier by Goering which authorized the Final Solution as "a complement to "previous "solutions "for eliminating the Jews from German living space through violence, economic strangulation, forced emigration, and evacuation.

Synset Examples

use concise military verbiage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wording - the words used to express something
  • Synonym: verbiage - excessive or over-elaborate wording
  • Synonym: diction - choice and use of words in speech or writing

Etymology

From Greek phrasis (way of speaking) + -logy (study of); via Late Latin phraseologia.
