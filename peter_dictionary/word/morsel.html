morsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrsəl / (AmE), / ˈmɔːsəl / (BrE)
a small piece or amount of food, or a small quantity of something

Examples

When the telephone rang on the day after Hino went down to the village, Rector had a hunch it would be Hino with some morsel of information too important to wait until his return, for there were few telephones in the village and the phone in Rector's office rarely rang unless it was important.

Synset Examples

a morsel of paper was all he needed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or quantity
  • Synonym: scrap - a small piece or amount, especially of food
  • Degree Higher: chunk - a large or thick piece

Etymology

From Old French *morsel* (a bite, a small piece), from Vulgar Latin *morsellum*, diminutive of Latin *morsus* (a bite).
