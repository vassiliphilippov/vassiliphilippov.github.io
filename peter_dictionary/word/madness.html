madness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædnəs / (AmE), / ˈmædnəs / (BrE)
reckless or foolish behavior; the quality of being rash and lacking good judgment

Examples

Most new fish come close to madness the first night.

Synset Examples

trying to drive through a blizzard is the height of folly
adjusting to an insane society is total foolishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - foolish or senseless behavior
  • Antonym: wisdom - the ability to make sensible and prudent decisions

Etymology

From Old English 'gemǣdnes' (state of being mad), from 'gemǣd' (mad).
