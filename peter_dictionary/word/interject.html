interject

1. VERB

/ ɪn.tɚˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to say something abruptly, especially as an interruption or in the middle of another person's remarks

Examples

"I think they 're dead ", Ekstrohm interjected quietly.

Synset Examples

She interjected clever remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop someone by saying or doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'interjectus', past participle of 'intericere' (to throw between), from 'inter-' (between) + 'iacere' (to throw).
