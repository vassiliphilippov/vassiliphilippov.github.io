longhand

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ.hænd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.hænd / (BrE)
written by hand in full, rather than in shorthand or by typing; handwriting using the normal alphabetic script

Examples

At his trial, petitioner sought to secure through subpoena duces tecum the longhand notes of the Department's hearing officer, Evensen, as well as his report thereon.

Synset Examples

longhand writing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shorthand - a method of rapid writing by using symbols or abbreviations

Etymology

Formed from 'long' + 'hand', referring to fully written text by hand rather than in shorthand.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌhænd / (BrE)
handwriting in which letters are written out fully and connected in cursive style, as opposed to printing or shorthand.

Examples

He stared at the report in Killpath's hand, sure it was written by Accacia - just as sure as if he 'd submitted it in his scrawled longhand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shorthand - handwriting using abbreviations, symbols, and rapid forms for quick note-taking

Etymology

Formed from 'long' + 'hand' to contrast with 'shorthand'.
