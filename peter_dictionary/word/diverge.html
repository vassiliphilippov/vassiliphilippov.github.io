diverge

1. VERB

/ daɪˈvɝdʒ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(of lines, paths, or things) to separate and go in different directions from a common point; also, to develop differences and become different from each other.

Examples

There are more stems per item in Athabascan, which expresses the fact that the Athabascan languages have undergone somewhat more change in diverging from proto Athabascan than the Yokuts languages from proto Yokuts.

Synset Examples

The two paths diverge here

Related Terms

  • Antonym: converge - to come together from different directions; meet

Etymology

From Latin 'divergere', from 'dis-' (apart) + 'vergere' (to turn, incline).
