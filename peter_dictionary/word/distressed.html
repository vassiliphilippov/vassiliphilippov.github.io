distressed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛst / (BrE)
(of a person, organization, or area) experiencing financial trouble or hardship.

Examples

An extension of this program into the other distressed areas should be undertaken.

Synset Examples

distressed companies need loans and technical advice
financially hard-pressed Mexican hotels are lowering their prices
we were hard put to meet the mortgage payment
found themselves in a bad way financially

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosperous - having financial success or well-being

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'distress', from Old French 'destresser', from Vulgar Latin *districtiare.
