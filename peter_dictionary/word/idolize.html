idolize

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪ.dəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.dəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to admire or love someone or something very much, often to an excessive or uncritical degree; to regard as an idol

Examples

"You 're afraid to talk to a guy you idolize ".

Synset Examples

Many teenagers idolized the Beatles

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: admire - to regard with respect or warm approval
  • Synonym: adore - to love and respect someone deeply
  • Antonym: despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for

Etymology

From idol + -ize, based on the word idol (from Latin idolum, from Greek eidolon, 'image, idol').
