residue

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌdu / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdjuː / (BrE)
the small amount of something that remains after the main part has been removed or used up

Examples

Miscellaneous soils, which primarily include sticky substances and colorless liquids which evaporate to leave a residue.
Will mere debate on that proposition, even though it be free and untrammeled, remove the dross and leave a residue of refined gold?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a small part or trace remaining
  • Synonym: remainder - what is left over; the rest

Etymology

From Latin residuum (that which remains), from residere (to remain).
