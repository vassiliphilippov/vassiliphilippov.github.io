sitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of assuming or maintaining a seated position.

Examples

Serum potassium at this time was 3.8 mEq. per liter, and the hemoglobin was 13.9 gm. By Dec. 1, 1958, the weakness in the pelvic and quadriceps muscle groups was appreciably worse, and it became difficult for the patient to rise unaided from a sitting or reclining position.
Mr. Corleone, would you kindly identify for the committee the gentleman sitting to your left?

Synset Examples

he read the mystery at one sitting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standing - the act of being upright on the feet
  • Antonym: reclining - the act of lying back in a relaxed position

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'sit', from Old English 'sittan'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
A period during which someone poses for a photograph, portrait, or similar artistic representation.

Examples

But at the end of the sitting, when Michelangelo showed him the quick, free drawings, with the mother roughed in, holding her son, the model grasped what Michelangelo was after, and promised to speak to his friends.

Synset Examples

he wanted his portrait painted but couldn't spare time for the sitting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: session - a period devoted to a particular activity, e.g., a meeting or class session

Etymology

From the verb 'sit', from Old English 'sittan', meaning to be seated.
