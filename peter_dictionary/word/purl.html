purl

1. VERB

/ pɜrl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
(of a stream or liquid) to flow with a gentle, swirling or rippling motion, often making a soft, murmuring sound.

Examples

Where Cuddleback Brook purled into the Neversink was a magnificent swimming hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eddy - move in a circular pattern (of water, air, or smoke)

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the murmuring sound of flowing water.

2. VERB

/ pɝl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
(of water) to flow with a gentle murmuring or rippling sound.

Examples

Mynheer, Sir Francis, the valley society, the very smell of the river on his right purling along to the bay past fish weirs and rocks, and ahead the sleepy ribbon of moon drenched road.

Synset Examples

the water was purling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - (of water) to make a continuous, gentle, pleasant sound
  • Synonym: gurgle - to make a hollow bubbling sound, like water flowing

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound itself.

3. VERB

/ pɜrl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
to knit with a purl stitch, passing the yarn in front of the needle to create a raised stitch on the fabric's surface

Examples

Photograph shows the wrong side of work with light strand being picked up under dark strand in position to be purled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knit - to make fabric by interlocking loops of yarn with knitting needles

Etymology

Probably imitative of the soft, curling movement of thread in early usage.
