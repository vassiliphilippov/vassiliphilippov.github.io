teasing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtizɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of making fun of or provoking someone in a playful or sometimes malicious way, often by persistent jokes or annoyances.

Examples

Scotty did not mind the doctor's unsmiling teasing as he used to.

Synset Examples

he ignored their teases
his ribbing was gentle but persistent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taunting - mocking or making fun of someone in a cruel way
  • Degree Higher: bullying - the act of intimidating or mistreating someone repeatedly

Etymology

From the verb 'tease', which originates from Old English *tǣsan* (to pluck, pull apart).
