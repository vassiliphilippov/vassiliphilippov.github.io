dissolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of breaking up or separating into component parts; disintegration.

Examples

But He set a bound to his [ state of ] sin, by interposing death, and thus causing sin to cease, putting an end to it by the dissolution of the flesh, which should take place in the earth, so that man, ceasing at length to live in sin, and dying to it, might live to God ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disintegration - the process of losing cohesion or strength, breaking into parts
  • Synonym: decomposition - the process of decaying or breaking down into basic parts
  • Antonym: formation - the action of coming together or creating something new (opposite process)

Etymology

From Latin dissolutio(n-), from dissolvere ‘to loosen or dissolve’.
