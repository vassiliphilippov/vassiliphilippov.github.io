swig

1. NOUN

/ swɪɡ / (AmE), / swɪɡ / (BrE)
A large, hurried or greedy swallow of a drink.

Examples

Mr. Gorboduc took a swig of his sherry.

Synset Examples

he finished it at a single gulp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - a large and hurried swallow, especially of liquid
  • Antonym: sip - a small mouthful of liquid

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the 18th century.
