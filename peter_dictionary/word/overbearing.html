overbearing

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
(especially of a person or their manner) expecting unquestioning obedience; domineering or excessively controlling.

Examples

The posse's retreat encouraged the Mexicans to be overbearing and impudent.
He was stern and overbearing with his flock, but obsequious and conciliatory with the whites, especially the rich who partly supported the church.

Synset Examples

the timid child of authoritarian parents
insufferably overbearing behavior toward the waiter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domineering - asserting one's will over others in an arrogant way
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + bear (to carry, conduct oneself), originally meaning 'domineering' in the 17th century.
