slop

1. NOUN

/ slɑp / (AmE), / slɒp / (BrE)
Wet feed for pigs, consisting mainly of kitchen waste mixed with water or milk.

Examples

And hit squealed for slop ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swill - liquid kitchen refuse fed to pigs

2. VERB

/ slɑp / (AmE), / slɒp / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to spill or overflow from a container, often carelessly or in a messy way.

Examples

And then came the water - not rain, but solid sheets that sluiced down like water slopping from a bucket.
Rourke hastily slopped whiskey into his glass on top of half-melted ice-cubes.

Synset Examples

spill the milk
splatter water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to cause or allow liquid to flow over the edge of its container

Etymology

From Old English *slopp* (mud, slop), related to the idea of wet, messy substances.

3. VERB

/ slɑp / (AmE), / slɒp / (BrE)
to walk through mud or slush, producing a squelching or splashing sound

Examples

They cringed under sodden covers, listening to the waves slop against the bottom.

Synset Examples

We had to splosh across the wet meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squelch - to walk noisily in wet, soft earth or mud
  • Synonym: trudge - to walk with slow, heavy steps, often through mud or difficult terrain
