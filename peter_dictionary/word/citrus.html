citrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of fruits from the genus Citrus, including oranges, lemons, limes, and grapefruits, characterized by a thick rind and juicy pulp, typically grown in warm climates.

Examples

The citrus tones popular in clothing are also to be found afoot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow rind
  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow oval citrus fruit with thick skin and sour juice
  • Synonym: lime - a small green citrus fruit with acidic juice

Etymology

From Latin citron-, citrus (‘citron tree’).
