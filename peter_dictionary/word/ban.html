ban

1. NOUN

/ bæn / (AmE), / bæn / (BrE)
an official order that forbids something to be done or used

Examples

He thereby precipitated a bitter controversy involving Protestants, Jews and Roman Catholics that continued for two months, until the city's Board of Hospitals lifted the ban on birth-control therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - the act of formally forbidding something

Etymology

From Old Norse 'bann' (proclamation, prohibition), via Old English 'bann' (proclamation); related to banish.

2. VERB

/ bæn / (AmE), / bæn / (BrE)
to officially prohibit something, especially by law or social pressure

Examples

Mr. Schaefer also recommended that the snow emergency route plan, under which parking is banned on key streets and cars are required to use snow tires or chains on them, should be "strictly enforced ".
All bottles must be kept a safe distance away from the pool and drinking glasses are banned in favor of plastic or metal cups.

Synset Examples

Smoking is banned in this building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibit - to formally forbid by law, rule, or other authority
  • Antonym: allow - to permit or let happen

Etymology

From Old Norse *banna* (to curse, prohibit) via Old English *bannan*.

3. VERB

/ bæn / (AmE), / bæn / (BrE)
To officially forbid something, especially the public distribution or use of something such as a movie, book, or product.

Examples

And to keep athletes' stomachs from getting jumpy under physical duress, he bans all highly flavored condiments.
Probably the best answer to this kind of entering wedge is congressional action requiring the Federal Communications Commission to ban such advertising through its licensing power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibit - formally forbid by law or authority
  • Antonym: allow - permit, let something happen or be available

Etymology

From Old Norse 'banna' (to curse, prohibit), related to Old English 'bannan' (to summon, proclaim).
