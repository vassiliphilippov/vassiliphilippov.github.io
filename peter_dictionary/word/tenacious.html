tenacious

1. ADJ

/ təˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / tɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
Holding firmly to an idea, purpose, or course of action; persistent and determined, especially in the face of difficulty or opposition.

Examples

She is quite tenacious.

Synset Examples

dogged persistence
dour determination
the most vocal and pertinacious of all the critics
a mind not gifted to discover truth but tenacious to hold it
men tenacious of opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion
  • Antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; easily influenced

Etymology

From Latin tenax (tenacious), from tenere (to hold).
