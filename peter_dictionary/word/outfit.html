outfit

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
a group of people working together as an organized unit, especially a military company or a team

Examples

Heavily armed and mobilized as a fast moving Civil Defense outfit, 23 operators and office personnel stand ready to move into action at a minute's notice ".
He had a mean, unbroken sheer bastard in his outfit, and someone invented the name Trig for him.
The terms are fairly safe to use on this side of the ocean, but before you start spouting them to your date, it might be best to find out if he was a member of Major Pockmanster's Delhi Regiment, since resentment toward the natives was reportedly very high in that outfit.
But of course the paterollers won n't be of any help, not with everything so upset and that Yankee cavalry outfit they say is running around, God knows where ".
The Brannon outfit - known as the Slash-B because of its brand - reached Hondo Creek before sundown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - a group forming part of a larger organization, especially in the military
  • Synonym: company - a military unit, especially an army unit
  • Synonym: team - a group of people who work together

Etymology

Originally from out + fit, meaning 'equipment' or 'fitting out', later extended to mean 'group equipped for a purpose'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
a set of articles or equipment, especially for a particular purpose or activity

Examples

"I suppose ", he muttered, "I can sell the outfit for enough to send you home to your folks, once we find a settlement ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kit - set of tools or equipment for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: gear - equipment needed for a particular purpose

Etymology

From German *Ausrüstung* (equipment), borrowed into English in the late 18th century via nautical slang.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fɪt / (BrE)
A set of clothes worn together, often including accessories.

Examples

"And I do n't know why you want to go on wearing that outfit ", she said, making a face.
Nice outfit.

Synset Examples

his getup was exceedingly elegant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: getup - a set of clothes, especially one with a distinctive style

Etymology

Originally US slang, from 'outfit' (equipment, provision), adapted to clothing in the mid-19th century.
