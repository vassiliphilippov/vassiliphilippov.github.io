brokerage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊkərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkərɪdʒ / (BrE)
A business or company of a broker, especially one that acts as an intermediary in buying and selling securities, and the fee charged for such services.

Examples

It ignores the sordid financial aspects (quite conveniently, too, for his audience, who could indulge in moral indignation without visible, or even conscious, discomfort, their money from the transaction having been put away long ago in a good antiseptic brokerage).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commission - a fee paid for a service, especially in sales or brokerage

Etymology

From 'broker' + '-age', Middle English 'brocour' from Old French 'broceur' (small trader).
