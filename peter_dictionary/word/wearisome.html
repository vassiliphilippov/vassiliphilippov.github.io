wearisome

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪr.i.səm / (AmE), / ˈwɪə.rɪ.səm / (BrE)
Causing boredom or tiredness due to monotony or lack of interest.

Examples

True, the pattern and flow of the drama have strong literary qualities that are a bit wearisome in the first half, before Don Quixote goes to the duke's court.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
  • Synonym: tiresome - causing one to feel bored or annoyed
  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

From Middle English 'werysom', from 'weary' + '-some'.
