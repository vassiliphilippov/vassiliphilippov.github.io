calmly

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑmli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmli / (BrE)
In a way that shows self-control or does not show agitation, especially in difficult, stressful, or emotional situations.

Examples

In fact it has caused us to give serious thought to moving our residence south, because it is not easy for the most objective Southerner to sit calmly by when his host is telling a roomful of people that the only way to deal with Southerners who oppose integration is to send in troops and shoot the bastards down.
But Sojourner was not easily excited or upset and said quite calmly: "Let's go and see what it's like ".
Calmly he opened the Bible and read of the woman at the well.

Synset Examples

he spoke calmly to the rioting students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quietly - in a way that is not loud or agitated
  • Antonym: anxiously - in a worried or nervous way

Etymology

From the adjective 'calm' + '-ly'. 'Calm' from Old French *calme*, from Italian *calma*, from Late Latin *cauma* (heat of the day, rest during the heat of the day), from Greek *kauma* (burning heat).
