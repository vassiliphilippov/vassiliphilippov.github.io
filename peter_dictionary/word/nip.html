nip

1. VERB

/ nɪp / (AmE), / nɪp / (BrE)
to squeeze or pinch sharply, especially with the fingers or teeth

Examples

He liked to nip ear lobes of unsuspecting visitors with his needle-sharp teeth.

Synset Examples

He pinched her behind
She squeezed the bottle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinch - to squeeze tightly between the fingers or in a device
  • Synonym: squeeze - to press something firmly, especially with the fingers

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English nippen, from Old English hnippan (to nip, pinch).
