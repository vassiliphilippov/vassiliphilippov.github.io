neither

1. DET

/ ˈniðər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪðə / (BrE)
Not one nor the other of two people or things; used to show that a negative statement applies to both parts of a sentence.

Examples

- Neither can they.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: either - one or the other of two

Etymology

From Old English nāhwæþer (not either).
