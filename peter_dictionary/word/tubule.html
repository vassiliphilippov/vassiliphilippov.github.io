tubule

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.bjuːl / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.bjuːl / (BrE)
A small tube, especially one forming part of a biological structure such as a gland or organ.

Examples

Occasional tubules contained hyaline casts admixed with neutrophils.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tube - a long, hollow cylinder used to convey fluids or as a part of a structure

Etymology

From Latin 'tubulus', diminutive of 'tubus' (tube).
