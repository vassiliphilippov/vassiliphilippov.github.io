prince

1. NOUN

/ prɪns / (AmE), / prɪns / (BrE)
a male member of a royal family, especially the son of a king or queen and not the ruler himself

Examples

The Prince visited the hospital of Operation Brotherhood, supported by the Junior Chamber of Commerce of the Philippines, and fed rice to two pet elephants he kept at his residence at Pak Song.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: princess - a female member of a royal family, especially the daughter of a sovereign
  • Degree Higher: king - male ruler of an independent state, especially a hereditary monarchy
  • Degree Lower: duke - a man of noble rank below a prince or king

Etymology

From Old French *prince*, from Latin *princeps* (first, chief)
