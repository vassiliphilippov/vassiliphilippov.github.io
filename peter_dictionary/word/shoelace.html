shoelace

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃuː.leɪs / (BrE)
A cord or string used for fastening a shoe by tying it.

Examples

When he was going to town, nothing was good enough - he had cursed at Winston once for leaving a fleck of polish on his shoelace.
Not for shoelaces or a stick of gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoestring - a narrow cord or lace for fastening shoes

Etymology

From 'shoe' + 'lace', meaning a lace for a shoe.
