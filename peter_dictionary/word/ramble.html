ramble

1. VERB

/ ˈræmbəl / (AmE), / ˈræmbəl / (BrE)
to talk or write at length in a confused or aimless way, often with many digressions and without clear structure

Examples

For the most part, his writing rambles and jogs, preventing easy access by the reader to his true thoughts.
The talk would not be in code, but neither would it ramble.

Synset Examples

This novel rambles on and jogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digress - leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing
  • Synonym: meander - to proceed aimlessly or with little purpose, especially in speech or writing
  • Antonym: focus - keep speech or writing concise and on the main topic

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *ramblen* (to wander), perhaps related to Middle Dutch *rammelen* (to wander about).
