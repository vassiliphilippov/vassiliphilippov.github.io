pretended

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈtɛndɪd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛndɪd / (BrE)
Not real; made to appear true, especially in order to deceive.

Examples

This was delivered in a forthright way, without coyness and over pretended interest - an admirable way with children.
Then, with a shrug of pretended indifference, she took a compact from her purse and went through the motions of fixing her make-up.

Synset Examples

an assumed name
an assumed cheerfulness
a fictitious address
fictive sympathy
a pretended interest
a put-on childish voice
sham modesty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genuine - truly what it is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; counterfeit or sham
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From pretend + -ed, meaning 'made to seem'.
