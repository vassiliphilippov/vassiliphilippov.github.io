Ceylon

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈlɒn / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒn / (BrE)
the former name (used until 1972) of the island nation now known as Sri Lanka, located in the Indian Ocean off the southeastern coast of India

Examples

Southern China, all of southeast Asia, southern India and Ceylon, these states of the Malay Federation spoke Bazaar.
Ceylon has moved from a pro Western orientation to a neutralism openly hostile to the West.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sri Lanka - current name of the island nation formerly known as Ceylon

Etymology

From Portuguese Ceilão, from Tamil இலங்கை (Ilaṅkai).
