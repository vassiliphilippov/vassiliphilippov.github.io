consolidation

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɑːlɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɒlɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of combining multiple parts or elements into a single, solid or unified mass

Examples

The epic poems, the consolidation of the Greek pantheon, the rise of firm political units, the self-awareness which could permit painted and sculptured representations of men - all these had to await the progress of following decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregation - the act of collecting into a whole
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into pieces

Etymology

From Latin consolidatio (making firm), from consolidare (to make firm).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌsɑlɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɒlɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something stronger or more solid by combining separate parts into a single, unified whole

Examples

To interpret, indeed, the era from 1000 to 800 as a period mainly of consolidation may be a necessary but unfortunate defect born of our lack of detailed information; if we could see more deeply, we probably would find many side issues and wrong turnings which came to an end within the period.

Synset Examples

a consolidation of two corporations
after their consolidation the two bills were passed unanimously
the defendants asked for a consolidation of the actions against them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combination - the act of joining or merging separate things
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into separate parts

Etymology

From Latin consolidatio (strengthening), from consolidare (to make firm).
