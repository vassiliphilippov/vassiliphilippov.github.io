glass

1. NOUN

/ ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A container, typically made of glass, used for holding drinks.

Examples

He does n't think that potting them from a deck chair on the south side of the house with a quart glass of beer for sustenance is entirely sporting.
The suburban branch is thereby credited with a sale which would have been made even if its glass doors had never opened.

Laura Keene brushed by him with the glass of water.
As they waited for supper they sat by the fire, glasses in hand, while Byron philosophized as much for his own entertainment as hers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup - A small open container used for drinking liquids, typically with a handle
  • Synonym: mug - A drinking vessel, typically with a handle and not made of glass, used for hot drinks
  • Synonym: goblet - A drinking vessel with a stem and base, often used for wine
  • Antonym: bottle - A container with a narrow neck, used for storing liquids

Etymology

Old English glæs, from Proto-Germanic *glasam, related to the material glass.

3. NOUN

/ ɡlæs / (AmE), / ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A serving of a drink held in a glass container, especially referring to alcoholic beverages such as wine or champagne.

Examples

Lord Byron poured himself another glass of wine and held it up to the candle flame admiring the rich color.
Here and there she stayed to visit and drink a glass of sherry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup - serving of drink in a cup

Etymology

From Old English glæs, related to Dutch glas and German Glas, originally referring to the material and later to the vessel and the portion it holds.
