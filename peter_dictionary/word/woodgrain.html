woodgrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌɡreɪn / (BrE)
the pattern or texture produced by the alignment and arrangement of fibers in wood, visible on its surface

Examples

It was for this reason, and no other that I can see, that in September 1912, Braque took the radical and revolutionary step of pasting actual pieces of imitation woodgrain wallpaper to a drawing on paper, instead of trying to simulate its texture in paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain - the arrangement or texture of fibers in a material, especially wood
  • Synonym: texture - the feel, appearance, or consistency of a surface

Etymology

From 'wood' (the hard substance of trees) + 'grain' (the arrangement of fibers or particles).
