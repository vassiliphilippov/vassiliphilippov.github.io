timbre

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈtæmbə / (BrE)
The quality of a musical sound or voice that distinguishes different types of sound production, such as voices and musical instruments, apart from pitch and loudness.

Examples

The timbre remains that of the instruments unclouded by resonance.
The voice is light in timbre, with a rough edge that corresponds to his visage.

Synset Examples

the timbre of her soprano was rich and lovely
the muffled tones of the broken bell summoned them to meet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone - quality or character of a musical or vocal sound
  • Antonym: pitch - the degree of highness or lowness of a sound

Etymology

From French 'timbre' (quality of a sound), originally meaning 'bell' or 'stamp'.
