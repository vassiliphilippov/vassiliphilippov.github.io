abridge

1. VERB

/ əˈbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
To shorten a text, speech, or document by omitting parts while retaining its essential meaning.

Examples

Field Marshal Slim has abridged it for the benefit of "those who, finding not so great an attraction in accounts of military moves and counter-moves, are more interested in men and their reactions to stress, hardship and danger ".

Synset Examples

The manuscript must be shortened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorten - make or become shorter
  • Synonym: condense - make (a text) more concise
  • Antonym: expand - make longer or more detailed

Etymology

From Old French 'abregier', from Latin 'abbreviare' (to shorten).
