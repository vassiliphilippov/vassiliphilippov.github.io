psychophysiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌfɪziəˈlɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌfɪziəˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of psychology that studies the physiological basis of psychological processes and behavior.

Examples

As in the theory of perception, established in psycho-physiology, the eye is recognized as an integral part of the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physiological psychology - The study of the relationship between physiology and psychological processes

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) and 'physiologia' (study of nature/natural functions).
