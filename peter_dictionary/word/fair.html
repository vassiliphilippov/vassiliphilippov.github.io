fair

1. ADJ

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
treating people equally without favoritism or bias; following the rules or standards of justice and honesty

Examples

In 1864 Newman professedly had to write his Apologia with his keenest feelings in order to be believed and to command a fair hearing from English readers.
It is probably fair to say that the idea of death is more profound in Irenaeus than the idea of sin is.
It is only fair to demand that teachers of courses in English, history, psychology and so on be as well informed in matters of art, especially interior design, as are the art teachers educated in the academic subjects.
The measure, for federal income tax purposes, of the dividend to individual stockholders would be the fair market value of the shares at the time of each annual distribution.
In the case of taxpaying corporate stockholders, the measure would be the lesser of the fair market value of the shares or du Pont's tax basis for them, which is approximately $ 2.09 per share.

Synset Examples

a fair referee
fair deal
on a fair footing
a fair fight
by fair means or foul

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - not taking sides; unbiased
  • Antonym: unfair - not just; treating people unequally or with bias

Etymology

From Old English fæger (beautiful, pleasing), later developing the sense of 'just' or 'equitable'.

2. ADJ

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
Moderately large in amount, size, or degree; not excessive or extreme.

Examples

Therefore, n is inversely proportional to the radius cubed and in fair agreement with the inverse 7 2 power derived from 1958 Alpha and 1959 Eta data.
Like a fair number of bootleggers he disliked alcohol.
Fair enough.

Synset Examples

a fairish income
reasonable prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - not extreme or excessive in amount or degree
  • Synonym: reasonable - not too much; acceptable in amount or degree
  • Antonym: excessive - too much; more than is necessary, permitted, or desirable

3. NOUN

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
A traveling entertainment event with rides, games, and side shows, often held temporarily in a town or city.

Examples

It was n't like getting cheated at the fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnival - a traveling amusement show with rides and games
  • Degree Lower: festival - an organized series of events or entertainment, often local or themed

4. VERB

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
(technical) To join or shape parts so that their external surfaces blend smoothly together.

Examples

The battens are carefully fastened in place after some necessary fairing on all frames.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - to make a surface flat or even
