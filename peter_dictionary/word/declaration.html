declaration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal or emphatic statement, often made openly or publicly

Examples

In short, both his own declarations and his figural blindness, when he looked at objects, seem to present undeniable evidence that he had simply no visual memory at all.
John's reply was like a declaration of war.
But what a super Herculean task it is to winnow anything of value from the mud beplastered arguments used so freely, particularly since such common use is made of cliches and stereotypes, in themselves declarations of intellectual bankruptcy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statement - a definite or clear expression in speech or writing
  • Synonym: announcement - a public or formal notice of something
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin declaratio (an explanation, proclamation), from declarare (to make clear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
An unsworn written statement made for use as evidence in a legal proceeding or transaction.

Examples

The filing of an original or amended declaration, otherwise due on January 15, 1962, is also waived, if you file your Form 1040 for 1961 and pay the full tax by January 31, 1962.

Synset Examples

his declaration of innocence

Etymology

From Latin 'declaratio', from 'declarare' (to declare).
