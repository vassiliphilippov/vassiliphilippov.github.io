disgusting

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely unpleasant or offensive; causing strong feelings of aversion or revulsion.

Examples

Bravado has been alternately described as "a symbol of the new grandeur of France and myself " (De Gaulle) and "a decadent, disgusting slob "!

Synset Examples

a disgusting smell
distasteful language
a loathsome disease
the idea of eating meat is repellent to me
revolting food
a wicked stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolting - causing intense disgust; repulsive
  • Synonym: repulsive - arousing intense distaste or aversion
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses

Etymology

From the verb 'disgust' + '-ing'. 'Disgust' from French 'dégouter', from 'des-' (expressing reversal) + 'gouter' (to taste).
