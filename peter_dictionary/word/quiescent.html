quiescent

1. ADJ

/ kwiˈɛsənt / (AmE), / kwɪˈɛsənt / (BrE)
In a state of inactivity or dormancy; not currently active or in motion.

Examples

The quiescent level of centimeter wave-length solar radiation would increase the average disk brightness temperature by less than 1 ` K.

Synset Examples

the quiescent level of centimeter wave-length solar radiation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaged in action; currently operating or functioning

Etymology

From Latin 'quiescens', present participle of 'quiescere' (to rest, be quiet).
