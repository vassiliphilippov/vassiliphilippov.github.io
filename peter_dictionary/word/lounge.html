lounge

1. VERB

/ laʊndʒ / (AmE), / laʊndʒ / (BrE)
To sit, lie, or stand in a relaxed or lazy way, typically in a comfortable or casual position.

Examples

Vernon would tilt his hat over one ear as he lounged with his feet on the dashboard, indulging in a huge cigar.
Here, in the old days - when they had come to see the moon or displays of fireworks - sat the king and his court while priests, soldiers, and other members of the party lounged in the smaller alcoves between.

Synset Examples

He was lounging on the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recline - to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
  • Synonym: sprawl - to sit or lie with limbs spread out awkwardly or carelessly

Etymology

Of obscure origin, possibly from French 's'allonger' meaning 'to stretch oneself out.'
