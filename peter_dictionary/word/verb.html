verb

1. NOUN

/ vɜrb / (AmE), / vɜːb / (BrE)
A word used to describe an action, state, or occurrence, forming the main part of the predicate of a sentence and conveying what is being done, experienced, or exists.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun - word class for people, places, things, or ideas
  • Antonym: adjective - word class that modifies nouns
  • Antonym: adverb - word class that modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' meaning 'word'.
