salute

1. VERB

/ səˈlut / (AmE), / səˈluːt / (BrE)
(formal) To propose or drink a toast in honor of someone or something.

Examples

Palmer, golf's leading money winner in 1960, and Snead will be saluted as the winning team in the Canada Cup matches last June in Dublin.

Synset Examples

Let us toast the birthday girl!
Let's drink to the New Year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toast - to drink in honor of someone or something

2. VERB

/ səˈlut / (AmE), / səˈluːt / (BrE)
to greet someone in a friendly or respectful way

Examples

A fresh breeze saluted him.

Synset Examples

I meet this men every day on my way to work and he salutes me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greet - to give a polite word or sign of welcome or recognition

Etymology

From Latin 'salutare' (to greet, wish well), from 'salus' (health).

3. VERB

/ səˈlut / (AmE), / səˈluːt / (BrE)
to express respect, praise, or admiration for someone or something

Examples

So we are the more prepared to give Parker the credit for having taken the right side in an unnecessary controversy, to salute his courage, and to pass on, happily forgetting both him and the entire episode.

Synset Examples

I salute your courage!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration
  • Synonym: commend - express approval of formally or officially
  • Antonym: criticize - express disapproval or point out faults

Etymology

From Latin salutare (to greet), from salus (health, welfare).
