staunch

1. ADJ

/ stɑntʃ, stɔntʃ / (AmE), / stɔːntʃ, stɑːntʃ / (BrE)
firm and loyal in your support of a person or cause; dependable and steadfast, especially in loyalty.

Examples

The opposition to this point of view has its staunchest support in the work of Miller (' 50).
From actions aboard, it is easy to guess that Spencer's boast of twenty staunch followers was a modest estimate ".

Synset Examples

a steadfast ally
a staunch defender of free speech
unswerving devotion
unswerving allegiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
  • Synonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • Antonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially in loyalty or affection

Etymology

From Old French *estanchier* (to stop, make watertight) and Latin *stagnare* (to block up).
