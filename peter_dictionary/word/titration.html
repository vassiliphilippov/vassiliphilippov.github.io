titration

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / taɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
a process in chemistry in which a solution of known concentration is used to determine the concentration of another solution, by gradually adding it until the reaction is complete

Examples

After titration of the liberated * * f with * * f, aliquots of the aqueous and of the organic phase were counted in a solution type Geiger tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titrate - to determine the concentration of a solution by titration

Etymology

From French *titration*, from *titre* (proportion of a substance in a mixture), ultimately from Latin *titulus* (title, label).
