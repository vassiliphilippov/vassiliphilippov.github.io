setting

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsetɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement of scenery and objects on a stage or set to represent the location where a play, film, or other dramatic work takes place

Examples

Directed by Michael Murray; settings by William David Roberts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scenery - painted backdrops and structures used to represent a place in a theater or film

Etymology

From English 'set' + '-ing', originally meaning the act of placing or arranging.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the place or environment in which something occurs or develops; the surroundings or circumstances of an event, story, or activity

Examples

Some of them are obvious, such as the fact that we associate recorded and live music with our responses and behavior in different types of environments and social settings.
The general setting of the Moroccan election may also encourage the deterioration of local party organization.

Synset Examples

you can't do that in a university setting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environment - the surroundings or conditions in which a person, animal, or plant lives or operates
  • Synonym: context - the circumstances that form the setting for an event or idea
  • Synonym: background - the circumstances or situation prevailing at a particular time or underlying a particular event

Etymology

From Middle English *setting*, from setten (to set) + -ing.
