saga

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑɡə / (AmE), / ˈsɑːɡə / (BrE)
a long story of heroic events or adventures, especially a prose narrative from medieval Iceland, or any long, involved story resembling such accounts

Examples

Would we want a future-day Gibbon or Macaulay recounting the saga of America with movies as his prime source of knowledge?
As he had longed to be, he became the echo of a saga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - a long narrative poem or story celebrating heroic feats
  • Synonym: chronicle - a factual written account of important or historical events in the order of their occurrence

Etymology

From Old Norse *saga* (story, tale, narrative).
