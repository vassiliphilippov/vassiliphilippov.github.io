mountainous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊn.tɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪ.nəs / (BrE)
(of a region, area, or terrain) having many mountains or crags; very hilly or rugged.

Examples

These observations indicate that the lunar maria heat up more rapidly and also cool off more rapidly than do the mountainous regions.

Synset Examples

hilly terrain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hilly - having many hills

Etymology

From mountain + -ous, from Old French montagne and Latin montanus (pertaining to a mountain).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊntənəs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪnəs / (BrE)
Extremely large or impressive in size or appearance, like a mountain; massive or imposing.

Examples

When McFeeley was halfway to the door, the proprietor emerged - a mountainous, dark man, his head thick with resiny black hair, his eyes like two of the black olives he imported in boatloads.
They usually range from 20 to 60 feet in height, but when they pour into a V shaped inlet or harbor they may rise to mountainous proportions.

Synset Examples

mountainous waves
a mountainous dark man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size or amount
  • Synonym: gigantic - very large, like a giant
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size

Etymology

From mountain + -ous, meaning 'full of mountains' or resembling a mountain; derived from Old French 'montaine' and Latin 'montanus.'
