pearl

1. NOUN

/ pɝl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
A hard, lustrous, rounded object produced within the soft tissue of a living shelled mollusk, valued as a gemstone.

Examples

The man wore a vest and a tie, the woman had on a dark green dress and three strands of pearls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a precious or semi-precious stone, especially when cut and polished

Etymology

From Old French *perle*, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Latin *perna* (a type of shellfish).

2. NOUN

/ pɜrl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
A shade of white resembling the lustrous, pale color of a pearl.

Examples

The velvet smoking jackets, pearl gray, wine, and blue, which Miss Ada had bought him hung brushed and unworn in the closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory - a creamy white color resembling ivory
  • Degree Lower: cream - a pale yellowish-white color
  • Degree Higher: white - the color of pure snow; the lightest color

Etymology

From Old French 'perle', of uncertain origin, referring originally to the pearl gem.
