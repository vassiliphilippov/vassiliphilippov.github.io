forswear

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈswɛr / (AmE), / fɔːˈswɛə / (BrE)
To formally renounce or abandon a formerly held belief, claim, or practice, usually as a public or solemn declaration.

Examples

No religious group, he declared in an interview, will receive Peace Corps funds unless it forswears all proselytizing on the project it proposes.

Synset Examples

He retracted his earlier statements about his religion
She abjured her beliefs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abjure - to renounce a belief, cause, or claim, especially formally or solemnly

Etymology

From Old English 'forswerian', from 'for-' (away, completely) + 'swerian' (to swear).
