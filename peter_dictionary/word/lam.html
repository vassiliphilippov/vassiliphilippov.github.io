lam

1. VERB

/ læm / (AmE), / læm / (BrE)
to escape or flee quickly, especially from the authorities

Examples

Then, having wrapped the lot in an afghan my dog customarily slept on, you lammed out the front door, considerately leaving it open for neighbors to discover.
There's no way to take him in now and keep those other two - Wister and the one who hired the two of them - from finding out about Roberts and lamming out.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - to move quickly on foot; to flee
  • Synonym: escape - to get away from confinement or control
  • Antonym: surrender - to give oneself up to an authority

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from slang 'on the lam', American English c. 1900, meaning 'in flight (from the law)'.
