frolic

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪk / (BrE)
A playful or lively activity done for amusement; a light-hearted recreational event.

Examples

The party at Floyd's penthouse gave the "chorines "a chance for a nostalgic frolic through all those hackneyed routines which have become a classic choreographic statement of the era's nonsense.

Synset Examples

it was all done in play
their frolic in the surf threatened to become ugly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: play - activity for enjoyment and recreation rather than a serious or practical purpose
  • Synonym: romp - energetic play or movement; lively frolic
  • Degree Higher: game - an activity that one engages in for amusement or fun

Etymology

From Dutch vrolic (merry, cheerful), from vroolijk.

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪk / (BrE)
to play or move about cheerfully, energetically, or boisterously

Examples

A year ago today, when the Democrats were fretting and frolicking in Los Angeles and John F. Kennedy was still only an able and ambitious Senator who yearned for the power and responsibility of the Presidency, Theodore H. White had already compiled masses of notes about the Presidential campaign of 1960.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romp - to play in a lively or boisterous way
  • Synonym: gambol - to skip or jump about playfully

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *vrolyc* (merry, joyful).
