grease

1. NOUN

/ ɡris / (AmE), / ɡriːs / (BrE)
A thick, oily or fatty substance, typically used to lubricate machinery, reduce friction, or as a protective coating.

Examples

In addition, dirt particles can be held onto a soiled surface by sticky substances or by the surface tension of liquids, including liquid greases.
Greases, stains, and miscellaneous soils are usually sorbed onto the soiled surface.
Obviously hydrophobic (oleophilic) substances such as greases, oils, or particles having a greasy or oily surface are more at home in the center of a micelle than in the aqueous phase.
And then you wiped up some grease ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil - a viscous liquid derived from petroleum, especially for lubrication
  • Synonym: lubricant - a substance used to minimize friction between surfaces

Etymology

From Old French 'graisse', from Latin 'crassus' (thick, fat).

2. VERB

/ ɡris / (AmE), / ɡriːs / (BrE)
To apply grease or a similar lubricant to reduce friction between surfaces.

Examples

One could shave in the reflection of its French-polished panels, and its axles were greased like those of roulette wheels.

Synset Examples

grease the wheels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lubricate - to make slippery or smooth, often with oil or grease

Etymology

From Old French 'gresse' (fat, grease), from Latin 'crassus' (thick, fat).
