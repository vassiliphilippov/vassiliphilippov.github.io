Mah-Jongg

1. NOUN

/ mɑ ˈdʒɔŋ / (AmE), / mɑː ˈdʒɒŋ / (BrE)
A Chinese game for four players played with 144 tiles, involving skill, strategy, and chance.

Examples

Opera in the Grand Tradition, along with mah-jongg, seems to be staging a well deserved comeback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahjong - Alternate spelling of the same game

Etymology

From Chinese (Cantonese) máh-jéung, meaning 'sparrow', referring to the noise of the tiles.
