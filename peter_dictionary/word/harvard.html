Harvard

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑrvərd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvəd / (BrE)
A prestigious university located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA, renowned for academic excellence and historical significance.

Examples

In general, however, the timetable of travel lags considerably behind that of the student at Harvard or Smith.
Applying the techniques developed at Harvard for generating a paradigm from a representative form and its classification, we can add all forms of a word to the dictionary at once.
And then Andy says to Mr. Dekins, "Do you want your sons to go to Harvard, - or Yale?" -

Etymology

Named after John Harvard (1607–1638), an English clergyman and early benefactor.
