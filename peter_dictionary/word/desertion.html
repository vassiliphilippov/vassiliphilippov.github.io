desertion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝːʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of abandoning a person, cause, or organization, especially when there is a duty or responsibility to remain

Examples

The report called racial discrimination in employment "one of the most serious causes of family breakdown, desertion, and ADC dependency ".

Synset Examples

his abandonment of his wife and children left them penniless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandonment - the act of leaving someone or something behind

Etymology

From Latin 'desertio' (an abandoning), from 'deserere' (to desert, forsake).
