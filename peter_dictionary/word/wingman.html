wingman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋ.mæn / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋ.mæn / (BrE)
a pilot whose aircraft flies beside and slightly behind the leader in a flying formation to provide support and protection

Examples

Out of the corner of his eye, he watched his wingman move out a bit and shoot up with him.
Wingman, stay clear, he prayed.

Etymology

From 'wing' + 'man', referring to the supporting position on the wing of the lead aircraft.
