septic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɛptɪk / (BrE)
(of a wound, environment, or substance) containing or resulting from disease-causing organisms, especially bacteria.

Examples

In the summer of 1960 the oxidation pond became completely septic and emitted obnoxious odors.
During this study septic conditions developed in the oxidation pond in the spring when the ice melted.
Shortly after this study ended septic conditions resulted which required the addition of sodium nitrate.

Synset Examples

a septic sore throat
a septic environment
septic sewage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aseptic - free from disease-causing organisms

Etymology

From Greek sēptikos (making rotten), from sēpein (to make rotten).
