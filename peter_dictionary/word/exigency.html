exigency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪdʒ(ə)nsi / (BrE)
An urgent need or demand; a pressing or critical situation that requires immediate action.

Examples

The District Courts, in the framing of equitable decrees, are clothed' with large discretion to model their judgments to fit the exigencies of the particular case'.

Synset Examples

the health-care exigency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgency - the quality of being very important and needing immediate attention

Etymology

From Latin exigentia (urgency, necessity), from exigere (to demand).
