tamper

1. VERB

/ ˈtæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtæmpə / (BrE)
to interfere with something secretly or dishonestly, often causing damage or making unauthorized changes

Examples

When the writer uses material does he tamper with it to improve its commercial effect or does he leave it pure?

Synset Examples

Someone tampered with the documents on my desk
The reporter fiddle with the facts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiddle - to alter something dishonestly, especially information or records

Etymology

Possibly from Middle French 'tamper' (to stamp, press), of uncertain origin.
