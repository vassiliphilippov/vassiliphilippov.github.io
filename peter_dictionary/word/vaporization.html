vaporization

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪpɚɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveɪpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of converting a substance into vapor, especially by the application of heat.

Examples

However, one must expect that vaporization and ejection of material by hypervelocity impacts would cause a deviation from a linear relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaporation - the process of a liquid turning into vapor, usually at temperatures below boiling
  • Antonym: condensation - the process by which vapor becomes liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam) + '-ization' (forming nouns indicating action or process).
