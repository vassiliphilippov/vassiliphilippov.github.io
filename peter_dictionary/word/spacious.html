spacious

1. ADJ

/ ˈspeɪ.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈspeɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
Having a large amount of space; very extensive or expansive in area or scope.

Examples

Not the noblest performance we have heard him play, or the most spacious, or even the most eloquent.
in his motherland; in the spacious hunting grounds of "Uncle Sam "; in the exciting salons of his lovely, brilliant Paris - mistress of gaiety - excess and abandon - world theatre of new found freedoms in tone, color, dance, design, and thought.

Synset Examples

a broad lawn
the wide plains
a spacious view
spacious skies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broad - wide in extent from side to side
  • Synonym: wide - measuring a great distance from side to side
  • Antonym: narrow - having a small width; limited in extent

Etymology

From Latin 'spatiosus' (roomy, vast), from 'spatium' (space).
