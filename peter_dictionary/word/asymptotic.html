asymptotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæsɪmˈtɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæsɪmpˈtɒtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or approaching an asymptote; in mathematics, describing a line or curve that gets closer to another curve as a variable moves towards infinity, but never meets it.

Examples

(Some suspensions break up before they are near to the direction of shear, and some become asymptotic to it without breakup.)

Synset Examples

an asymptotic function

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asymptote - a straight line that a curve approaches but never meets

Etymology

From Greek asymptōtos (not falling together), from a- (not) + sympiptein (to fall together).
