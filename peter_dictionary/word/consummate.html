consummate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑnsəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsjʊmeɪt / (BrE)
to make a marriage or relationship complete by having sexual intercourse.

Examples

Unless her husband also desires sex, the act cannot be consummated.
Extreme cases are on record in which the doctor has had to use instruments to cut through the hymen to permit marital relations to be consummated.

Synset Examples

consummate a marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - to make whole or bring to a finished state

Etymology

From Latin consummatus, past participle of consummare (to complete, finish).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.səˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sjʊ.meɪt / (BrE)
to make complete or perfect; to bring to a state of perfection

Examples

Taking off his hat and signaling the driver with it, Mr. Podger stepped into the road, lifted the surprised turtle and consummated its road-crossing with what must have been a breath-taking suddenness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfect - to make something free from faults, to improve to the highest standard

Etymology

From Latin consummare (to sum up, complete), from con- (together) + summa (sum, total).
