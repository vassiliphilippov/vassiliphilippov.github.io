avenge

1. VERB

/ əˈvɛndʒ / (AmE), / əˈvɛndʒ / (BrE)
to inflict harm or punishment in return for an injury or wrong, especially as an act of justice or retribution

Examples

In any case but the last, such a course is sure to avenge itself upon the individual; the moral powers no more than the physical and mental, can bear overstraining.

Synset Examples

He wants to avenge the murder of his brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - to retaliate or seek satisfaction for a wrong or injury
  • Antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense

Etymology

From Old French avengier, from Vulgar Latin *vindicare (to vindicate, punish).
