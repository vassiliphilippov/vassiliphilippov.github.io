surprise

1. NOUN

/ sərˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
An event or occurrence that is unexpected or not anticipated.

Examples

The rangy, Albany (Cal.) native, a surprise slugging sensation for the Flock last year as well as a defensive whiz, set "improved fielding "as his 1961 goal.
He was not going to lose the mission by default, and whatever reason Kayabashi had for bringing his little sight-seeing group to the mission, he was going to be in for a surprise.
They had protested that that was n't any surprise.
But his first few days with Troop H were full of surprises, beginning with First Sergeant Robert Early.
It had come as no great surprise to Matson that the hot water in the showers did n't work, that Loren Severe had thrown up all over the stairs, or that some thieving bastard of a cop had walked off with his cigarettes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or startling event or experience

Etymology

From Old French *surprise* (an overtake), from *surprendre* (to overtake, to surprise), from Latin *super* (over) + *prehendere* (to seize).

2. NOUN

/ səˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
A feeling of astonishment or amazement caused by something unexpected happening.

Examples

The young banker looked at him with a certain surprise, and then he said flatly: "I'm afraid I can't tell you anything in particular about Kent House.
To Voltaire's surprise, however, their host gives them fresh clothes to put on, opens his purse to lend them money and sits them down before a good dinner.
She looked at him in surprise.
He saw the surprise in her face, and laughed as though it were the funniest expression he had ever seen.
She showed her surprise by tightening the reins and moving the gelding around so that she could get a better look at his face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishment - a very great surprise; amazement
  • Degree Higher: shock - a sudden upsetting or surprising event; or the feeling resulting from it
  • Antonym: expectation - the state of thinking something will happen (opposite of surprise)

Etymology

From Old French 'surprise', from 'surprendre' meaning 'to overtake', from Latin 'super-' (over) + 'prehendere' (seize).

4. VERB

/ sərˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to come upon someone or something unexpectedly or take someone unawares

Examples

She started back for the house, saw a light in the office, opened the door and surprised a domestic little scene which was far outside the dark realm of murder or attempted murder.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - to discover or come upon unexpectedly

Etymology

From Old French *surprendre*, from Latin *superprehendere* (‘to seize, catch unawares’).

5. NOUN

/ səˈpraɪz / (AmE), / səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
The act of causing someone to feel unexpected wonder or astonishment.

Examples

Tom, you know, you surprise me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishment - a feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Antonym: expectation - the state of expecting something to happen

Etymology

From Old French 'surprise' (an unexpected event), from 'surprendre' (to overtake, surprise).
