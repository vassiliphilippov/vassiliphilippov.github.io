semipublic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
Partly public; having some characteristics of a public institution or open to the general public to a limited extent.

Examples

This is brought out in the common religious ethos that prevails even in the denominationally diverse audiences at many secular semi-public and public occasions in the United States; and it is evidenced in the prayers offered, in the frequent religious allusions, and in the confirmation of points on religious grounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: private - restricted to or intended only for a particular person or group
  • Degree Higher: public - open to or concerning all the people as a whole

Etymology

From semi- (meaning ‘half’ or ‘partly’) + public.
