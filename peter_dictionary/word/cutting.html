cutting

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of selecting, arranging, and assembling scenes to create the final version of a film; film editing.

Examples

Cutting, of course, takes place automatically in the creation of a film.
The chase in itself is a narrative; it presumes both speed and urgency and it demands cutting - both from pursued to pursuer and from stage to stage of the journey of both.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editing - the process of preparing film, video, or text by selecting and arranging material

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of dividing something into parts with a sharp tool or instrument.

Examples

The technique of cutting sections was essentially the same as that described by Coons et al. (1951).

Synset Examples

his cuts were skillful
his cutting of the cake made a terrible mess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - the act of cutting

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
a piece of a plant, such as a stem, leaf, or root, removed and used to grow a new plant by rooting or grafting

Examples

Fujimoto had a pile of cuttings near one side of the lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - a cutting taken from a plant for propagation
