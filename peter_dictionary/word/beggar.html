beggar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə / (BrE)
a person who lives by asking people for money or food, typically on the street

Examples

"I scanned the world through printed symbol swart, And through the beggar's rags I strove to see The inner man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panhandler - person who asks strangers for money, especially in a public place (North American term)
  • Synonym: pauper - very poor person; destitute individual

Etymology

From Middle English beggere, from beggen (to beg).
