astringent

1. ADJ

/ əˈstrɪndʒənt / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənt / (BrE)
Having a sharp, sour, or bitter taste.

Examples

Hindemith's joust with Weber tunes was a considerably more serious misfortune, for it demands translucent textures, buoyant rhythms, and astringent wit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acerbic - tasting sour or bitter
  • Antonym: sweet - having the pleasant taste characteristic of sugar or honey

Etymology

From Latin 'astringens', present participle of 'astringere' (to bind fast).
