keenly

1. ADV

/ ˈkiːnli / (AmE), / ˈkiːnli / (BrE)
In a way that shows strong interest, enthusiasm, or sharp perception.

Examples

Third, the significance of the vocational courses is that those enrolled are keenly interested in the work; they realize the relevance of what they are learning to their future careers, and this sense of purpose is carried over to the academic courses which they are studying at the same time.

Synset Examples

he was keenly aware of his own shortcomings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensely - in an intense or extreme manner
  • Synonym: vividly - in a way that is very clear, powerful, and detailed in the mind

Etymology

From 'keen' + '-ly', where 'keen' comes from Middle English 'kene', from Old English 'cēne' (bold, brave).
