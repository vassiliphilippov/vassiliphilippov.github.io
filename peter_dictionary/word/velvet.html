velvet

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvət / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪt / (BrE)
A soft, smooth fabric with a short, dense pile, usually woven from silk, cotton, or synthetic fibers, often used for clothing and furnishings.

Examples

One of the drawing-room shutters was partly open and he made out the shapes of chairs and sofas, which seemed to be upholstered in brown or russet velvet.
The velvet smoking jackets, pearl gray, wine, and blue, which Miss Ada had bought him hung brushed and unworn in the closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velveteen - a cotton fabric made in imitation of velvet
  • Degree Lower: corduroy - a thick cotton fabric with raised ribs, similar to velvet

Etymology

From Old French veluotte, from Vulgar Latin *vellūtum, from Latin vellus (fleece).
