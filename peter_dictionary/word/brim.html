brim

1. NOUN

/ brɪm / (AmE), / brɪm / (BrE)
The top edge of a container, such as a cup, bowl, or glass.

Examples

Emerson - Platonist, idealist, doctrinaire - sounded a high Transcendental note in his "Boston Hymn ", delivered in 1863 in the Boston Music Hall amidst thundering applause: "Pay ransom to the owner and fill the bag to the brim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rim - outer edge or border of something, especially a circular object

Etymology

Probably from Old English 'brymme' (edge, bank); related to Old High German 'bram' (edge).
