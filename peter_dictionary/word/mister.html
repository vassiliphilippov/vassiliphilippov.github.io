mister

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstər / (AmE), / ˈmɪstə / (BrE)
A polite or formal form of address for a man, used before a surname or alone when speaking to or about an adult male.

Examples

GEARY: You'’ve got a bet, mister.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sir - polite form of address for a man, especially in direct address

Etymology

From earlier English 'master', influenced by pronunciation and spelling change.
