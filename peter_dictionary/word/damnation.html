damnation

1. NOUN

/ dæmˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / damˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of being condemned to eternal punishment or suffering, especially in hell, as a result of sin or wrongdoing.

Examples

Then the figures which held his attention became a group of shattered trees, standing like the grotesques of a medieval damnation scene.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salvation - deliverance from sin and its consequences

Etymology

From Latin 'damnatio' (condemnation, sentence), from 'damnare' (to condemn).
