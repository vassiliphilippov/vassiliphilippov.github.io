infiltrate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.fɪlˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪl.treɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to enter or permeate (a material or tissue) by passing through its small spaces or interstices, especially gradually or stealthily.

Examples

In addition, other sections contained focal areas of recent myocardial necrosis that were infiltrated with neutrophils.
The submucosa was focally infiltrated with neutrophils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permeate - to spread or seep throughout

Etymology

From Medieval Latin infiltratus, past participle of infiltrare, from in- 'into' + filtrare 'to filter'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌfɪlˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪl.treɪt / (BrE)
to secretly enter a group or organization in order to gather information or exert influence, especially for spying purposes

Examples

"To prevent hoodlums from infiltrating the state as they did in the Republican administration in the early 1940 s ".

Synset Examples

The student organization was infiltrated by a traitor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - to secretly observe or gather information, often illicitly

Etymology

From Latin 'infiltrare', from 'in-' (into) + 'filtrum' (felt, filter).
