goad

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊd / (BrE)
A pointed stick or other instrument used to prod animals or urge them forward.

Examples

Ben Prime extended his high-stepped stride until he could lay his goad across the noses of the oxen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prod - a pointed implement used to poke or urge

Etymology

Old English *gād* (spear, goad), related to Old Norse *geitr* (spur).

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊd / (BrE)
to provoke or urge someone to take action, especially by annoying or challenging them

Examples

He hollered hoarsely, "Hang on "! and goaded the oxen as he yelled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prod - to poke or urge someone or something into action
  • Degree Lower: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone to do something
  • Synonym: spur - to incite or stimulate someone to take action
  • Antonym: deter - to discourage or prevent someone from taking action

Etymology

From Old English 'gād' (a pointed instrument for driving cattle).

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊd / (BrE)
to encourage or urge someone to take action, often by provoking or inciting them

Examples

Had it not been for such private enterprise, diocesan authorities might of course have been goaded into establishing institutions subsidized by diocesan funds and parish collections and staffed by religious as paid employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spur - to urge or encourage someone to take action

Etymology

Old English *gād* (goad, point), related to Old Norse *gaddr* (spike).
