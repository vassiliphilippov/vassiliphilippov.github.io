footage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtɪdʒ / (BrE)
recorded film, especially as material for editing or broadcast

Examples

If, as a home movie maker, you shoot the inevitable footage of your child taking its first steps, you have merely recorded an historical event.

Synset Examples

they had stock footage of lightning, tornados, and hurricanes
he edited the news footage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - motion picture material recorded on celluloid or digital media
  • Synonym: video - recorded moving visual images

Etymology

From 'foot' + '-age', originally referring to the length of film measured in feet.
