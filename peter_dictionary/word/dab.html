dab

1. VERB

/ dæb / (AmE), / dæb / (BrE)
to touch or hit something lightly, usually in a quick or gentle way, often with a soft object

Examples

"It was Brenner's idea ", Jess mumbled, dabbing at his nose.

Synset Examples

pat him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pat - to touch someone or something gently with your hand several times

Etymology

Probably from Middle English dabben (to strike lightly).

2. VERB

/ dæb / (AmE), / dæb / (BrE)
to touch or apply a substance to a surface with quick, light motions, especially a liquid; to pat gently

Examples

He saw the child-face grimace as the head was cut; he felt the far, cool flash of unimportant pain, as B' dikkat dabbed the wound with a corrosive antiseptic which stopped all bleeding immediately.
She clung to him, talking to him, and dabbing at her eyes.

Synset Examples

dab the wall with paint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pat - to touch or strike lightly and repeatedly, often as a sign of affection
  • Antonym: smear - to spread or apply a substance by rubbing or pressing

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, representing the sound or action of a light, quick touch.
