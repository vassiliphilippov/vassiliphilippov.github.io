disclosure

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of making new or secret information known to others

Examples

Now, under the impact of his wife's disclosures, he was brought suddenly to the realization that there was a limit to tolerance, however brilliant, however far-famed the offender might be.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revelation - the act of revealing or making something known

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French, from 'disclos', past participle of 'discloser' (to disclose), from Latin 'disclausus', past participle of 'disclaudere' (to open).
