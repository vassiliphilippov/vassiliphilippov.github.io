parody

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.di / (BrE)
A literary, musical, or artistic work that imitates the characteristic style of an author, artist, or genre in a humorous or satirical way.

Examples

For the present it is enough to note that in the grotesque figure of Jacoby, at the moment of his collapse, all these elements come together in prophetic parody.
Piepsam is grotesque, a disturbing parody; his end is ridiculous and trivial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satire - the use of humor or exaggeration to criticize people or ideas, especially in literature or art

Etymology

From Greek parōidia (a satirical poem), from para- (beside) + oide (song).

2. VERB

/ ˈpærədi / (AmE), / ˈpærədi / (BrE)
to imitate the style of someone or something in a humorous or satirical way, often to comment or mock

Examples

Another Yank parodied the familiar bedtime prayer:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satirize - to use satire to criticize or ridicule
  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something

Etymology

From Greek parōidia (a burlesque song or poem).
