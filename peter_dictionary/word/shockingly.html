shockingly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃɑkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkɪŋli / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the extreme degree of something, particularly something considered surprising or upsetting.

Examples

Augustus Baldwin Longstreet, a preacher and a college and university president in four Southern states, published the earliest of these backwoods sketches and in the character Ransy Sniffle, in the accounts of sharp horse-trading and eye gouging physical combat, and in the shockingly unliterary speech of his characters, he set an example followed by many after him.

Synset Examples

teachers were shockingly underpaid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to an extreme degree
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree

Etymology

From 'shocking' + '-ly'. 'Shocking' from the verb 'shock', originally from French 'choquer' (to strike).
