skyscraper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌskreɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌskreɪpə / (BrE)
A very tall building with many floors, typically found in cities.

Examples

(Los Angeles in 1957 finally bowed to the skyscraper.)

Etymology

From sky + scraper, first used in the late 19th century to describe tall buildings that seemed to 'scrape the sky'.
