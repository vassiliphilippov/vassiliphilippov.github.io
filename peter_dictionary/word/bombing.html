bombing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒmɪŋ / (BrE)
An attack made by dropping bombs from aircraft, typically during wartime.

Examples

I recall the startling, vivid realism of a dream in which I lived through the horror of the bombing of a little Korean town.
The low clouds made bombing difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air raid - an attack by aircraft, especially by bombers

Etymology

From 'bomb' + -ing, based on the verb 'to bomb'. 'Bomb' from French 'bombe', from Italian 'bomba', from Latin 'bombus', meaning 'a booming sound'.
