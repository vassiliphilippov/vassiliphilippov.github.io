mobilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of assembling and preparing troops or resources for war or an emergency.

Examples

This section prevents the military departments and the Office of Civil and Defense Mobilization from carrying out certain transactions involving real property unless they come into agreement with the Committees on Armed Services of the Senate and the House of Representatives.

Synset Examples

mobilization of the troops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demobilization - the process of standing down armed forces from combat-ready status

Etymology

From French mobilisation, from mobiliser ('to make mobile') + -ation.
