succession

1. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
A number of people or things following one after another in order or sequence.

Examples

Samples zoomed into closeup range in regular succession, like telephone poles passing on the highway, while representative music reinforced the mood of the late teens and 1920's.

Synset Examples

he played the trumps in sequence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which related things follow each other
  • Synonym: series - a number of things or events of the same class coming one after another

Etymology

From Latin 'successionem' (a following after), from 'succedere' (to follow after).

2. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
A number of people or things coming one after another; a sequence or series.

Examples

From maturity one looks back at the succession of years, counts them and makes them many, yet cannot feel length in the number, however large.

Synset Examples

a succession of stalls offering soft drinks
a succession of failures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which related things follow each other
  • Synonym: series - a number of things or events of the same class coming one after another

Etymology

From Latin successio (the act of following after), from succedere (to follow after).
