reinterpret

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈtɚprət / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈtɜːprɪt / (BrE)
to interpret something again, especially from a different viewpoint or with a new understanding

Examples

On the contrary, the old plays are continually being reinterpreted, and each new production of a classic has only a brief history at the Comedie.
So far as I know, the Comedie has never put Moliere's people in the costumes of the 20 th century, but they do reinterpret plays and characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpret - to explain the meaning of something

Etymology

Prefix re- (again) + interpret, from Latin interpretari (to explain, translate).
