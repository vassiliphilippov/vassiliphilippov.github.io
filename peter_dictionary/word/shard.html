shard

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrd / (AmE), / ʃɑːd / (BrE)
A sharp piece of broken glass, pottery, or other brittle material.

Examples

Hardly had Mrs. Roebuck driven off when a rusty pick-up truck, father or grandfather of Senor "Moriarty's "Ford sedan, came screeching to a dust swirling stop, and a brown face appeared, its nose threatened by shards of what had once been the side window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or separated from something
  • Synonym: splinter - a small, thin, sharp piece of wood, glass, or similar material broken off from a larger piece

Etymology

From Old English sceard (a gap, notch, broken piece).
