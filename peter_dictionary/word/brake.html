brake

1. NOUN

/ breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
A device for slowing down or stopping a vehicle by applying friction.

Examples

The speed is controlled by pressing on the two brake buttons located where the index finger and thumb are placed when holding the motor.
It was a district of small factories and loft buildings and occasional tenements, and he could see the police radio car as he rounded the corner and slammed on the brakes.
[BRAKES SQUEALING]

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accelerator - device that increases the speed of a vehicle
  • Synonym: brake pedal - pedal used to operate a vehicle's brakes

Etymology

From Middle English 'brake' (instrument for crushing), later influenced by the verb 'to break'.
