presto

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛstoʊ / (AmE), / ˈprɛstəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) at a very fast tempo.

Examples

The Presto ma non assai of the first trio of the scherzo is taken literally and may shock you, as the real Allegro con spirito of the finale is likely to bring you to your feet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: allegro - fast tempo (music)
  • Degree Lower: vivace - lively, brisk tempo (music)
  • Degree Higher: prestissimo - as fast as possible (music)

Etymology

From Italian 'presto' meaning 'quick' or 'prompt'.

2. ADV

/ ˈprɛstoʊ / (AmE), / ˈprɛstəʊ / (BrE)
suddenly, as if by magic; used to indicate something occurs very quickly or instantaneously.

Examples

Then, by squeezing her foot three times, he came up - presto - with a different diagnosis with each squeeze.

Synset Examples

Presto! begone! 'tis here again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instantly - immediately, without delay
  • Synonym: immediately - at once, without waiting
  • Antonym: slowly - not quickly, over a long period

Etymology

From Italian 'presto' (quick, prompt), used in English since the 18th century, especially in conjuring acts.
