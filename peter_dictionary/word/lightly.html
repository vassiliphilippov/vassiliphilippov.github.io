lightly

1. ADV

/ ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that involves little weight, force, or pressure; gently or delicately.

Examples

When the front knee is straight and locked, allow it to bend again until you feel the bar come lightly into contact with the sides of the Power Stands.
When Kitti was alive - and he remembered the pressure of her hand resting lightly on his arm - she had been the center of his life.

Synset Examples

experienced travellers travel light

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heavily - with great force or weight

Etymology

From Middle English 'lyghtly', from Old English 'lēohtlīċe' (easily, lightly), from 'lēoht' (light in weight).

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heavily - to a great degree; with excess or force

Etymology

From Middle English 'lyghtly', from Old English 'līhtlīce', from 'līht' (light) + '-līce' (-ly).

3. ADV

/ ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that shows indifference or lack of seriousness; without being troubled or dejected.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriously - with earnestness or gravity; with deep concern
  • Synonym: casually - in an unconcerned or informal manner

Etymology

From Middle English lightli (easily, cheerfully), from light (not heavy, not serious) + -ly.
