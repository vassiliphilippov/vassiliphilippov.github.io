bunker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkə / (BrE)
A hollow area filled with sand or a similar material, serving as a hazard on a golf course.

Examples

Arnold's iron shot from the tee burrowed into the bunker guarding the green, an embankment that had become soft and spongy from the rains, thereby bringing local rules into force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - an obstacle or danger on a golf course

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkə / (BrE)
(in golf) to hit a ball into a bunker (sand trap) on the course.

Examples

The latter involved hitting a full four-wood out to the first fairway and toward the clubhouse, hoping to slice it back to the deeply bunkered 9 th green.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand trap - a depression on a golf course filled with sand, designed as a hazard
