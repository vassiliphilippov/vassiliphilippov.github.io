reunion

1. NOUN

/ riˈjuːniən / (AmE), / riˈjuːniən / (BrE)
the act of coming together again after being apart

Examples

So instead of being tests of the South's loyalty, the Spanish War, the two World Wars, and the Korean War all served to overcome old grievances and cement reunion.

Synset Examples

monetary unification precipitated the reunification of the German state in October 1990

Related Terms

  • Antonym: separation - the state of being apart or divided

Etymology

From French 'réunion', from 'réunir' (to reunite).

2. NOUN

/ riˈjuniən / (AmE), / riˈjuːniən / (BrE)
A social gathering of people who have previously been associated, such as former classmates, colleagues, or family members, coming together after a period of separation.

Examples

Having received permission to give a camp reunion Halloween party, she asked that I come and be her date.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get-together - an informal gathering or meeting

Etymology

From French 'réunion', from 'réunir' (to reunite), from Latin 're-' (again) + 'unire' (to unite).
