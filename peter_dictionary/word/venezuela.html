Venezuela

1. PROPN

/ ˌvɛnəˈzweɪlə / (AmE), / ˌvɛnɪˈzweɪlə / (BrE)
A country in northern South America, bordering the Caribbean Sea, known for achieving independence from Spain in 1811 and for its significant oil reserves.

Examples

Mexico, Panama, and Venezuela are displaying open sympathy for Castroism, and there is no country - save the Dominican Republic whose funeral services we recently arranged - where Castroism and anti Americanism does not prevent the government from unqualifiedly espousing the American cause.

Etymology

From Spanish Venezuela, meaning 'Little Venice', named by explorers who thought stilted indigenous houses resembled Venice.
