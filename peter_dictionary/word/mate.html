mate

1. NOUN

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
a person who is a fellow member of a team, work group, or shared activity, especially in sports

Examples

Harvey Haddix, set back by the flu this season, will start against his former Cardinal mates, who might be playing without captain Kenny Boyer in tonight's game at Busch Stadium.
But our two Jeep mates - Keo Viphakone from Luang Prabang and John Cool from Beaver, Pennsylvania - were beaming under their coatings of dust.

Synset Examples

it was his first start against his former teammates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teammate - a member of the same team as someone else
  • Synonym: colleague - a person with whom one works in a profession or business
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who competes against or opposes another

Etymology

From Middle Low German *māt(e)* (comrade), originally 'eating companion'.

2. VERB

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
(of animals) to copulate; to engage in sexual intercourse, especially for the purpose of producing offspring.

Examples

I must add at once that these animals are what we call "queens ", young females that have mated in the previous summer or autumn.
The first worker bees do not mate or lay eggs; males and mating females do not emerge until later in the season.
The young queens and drones leave the nest and mate, and after a short period of freedom, the fertilized young queens will begin to dig in for the winter.
After having mated, an Andrena female digs a hole straight down into the ground, forming a burrow about the size of a lead pencil.
After the male and female andrenas have mated, the cycle is repeated.

Synset Examples

Birds mate in the Spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copulate - to engage in sexual intercourse, especially in a biological context
  • Synonym: breed - (of animals) to mate and produce offspring

Etymology

From Middle Low German or Middle Dutch *maten* (to pair), related to 'mate' meaning companion.

3. NOUN

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
an animal's partner, especially a sexual partner in mating

Examples

The best way to determine the correct figure (in captives) is by direct observation of pairs isolated from birth, a method that produced surprising results: maturing of a male Indian python in less than two years, his mate in less than three; data on the boa constrictor about match this.

Synset Examples

he loved the mare and all her mates
camels hate leaving their mates

Etymology

From Middle Low German *matte* (comrade) or Middle Dutch *mate* (companion); related to Old English *gemætta* (guest at the same table).

4. NOUN

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
one of a pair of items that are exactly alike and meant to be used together

Examples

She put the slipper neatly by its mate at the foot of the bed.

Synset Examples

when a match is found an entry is made in the notebook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pair - two corresponding or matching things
  • Synonym: match - an item that corresponds to another

Etymology

From Middle Low German *gemate* (comrade), via Middle English *mate* (companion; match).

5. NOUN

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
a husband, wife, or other partner in marriage

Examples

She would have a year in which to make up her mind, to choose a mate from a list selected by her gapt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spouse - a husband or wife
  • Synonym: partner - a person with whom one shares a relationship

Etymology

From Middle Low German *māt* (companion, housemate); related to Old English *gemætta*.

6. NOUN

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
An officer ranking below the master (or captain) on a commercial ship, responsible for various duties in navigation and ship management.

Examples

The captain was gone, and the mate was gone.
The mate, Robert Juet, who had kept the journal on the half Moon, was experienced - but he was a bitter old man, ready to complain or desert at any opportunity.
Hudson's reply was to accuse the mate of disloyalty.
The new mate was Robert Bylot, talented but inexperienced.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: captain - the master and highest-ranking officer of a ship

Etymology

From Middle Low German *māt(e)* (companion), related to sharing food or duties.

7. VERB

/ meɪt / (AmE), / meɪt / (BrE)
to bring two things together so that they fit closely or correspond, or to pair people or ideas for a purpose

Examples

All matching surfaces should be checked frequently and mated on a cut and fit basis.

Synset Examples

This fact is coupled to the other one
Matchmaker, can you match my daughter with a nice young man?
The student was paired with a partner for collaboration on the project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pair - to put two people or things together, especially for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: match - to bring two things together because they are compatible or fit well

Etymology

From Middle English *mat* (to join in marriage), ultimately from Latin *māta* (a partner).
