fallow

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfæləʊ / (BrE)
(of farmland) left unplowed and unseeded for a period to restore its fertility as part of a crop rotation or to avoid surplus production.

Examples

The small half-heartedly tended fields of men who 'd spent more time rustling cattle than farming were lying fallow.

Synset Examples

fallow farmland

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cultivated - (of land) prepared and used for growing crops

Etymology

From Old English *fealgian* (to break up land for sowing).
