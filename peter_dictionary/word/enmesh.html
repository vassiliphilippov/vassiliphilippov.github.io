enmesh

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈmɛʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈmɛʃ / (BrE)
to entangle or catch as if in a net or mesh, especially in a complicated or difficult situation

Examples

The American liberal may, in the world of to-day, have a strong case; but he presents it publicly so enmeshed in hypocrisy that it is not an honest one.
There we see a basically simple phonemic system enmeshed in a very complex and puzzling morphophonemic system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entangle - to twist together or intertwine so as to be difficult to separate; get caught up in

Etymology

From 'en-' (to put into) + 'mesh' (network of threads or wires); first recorded in the early 17th century.
