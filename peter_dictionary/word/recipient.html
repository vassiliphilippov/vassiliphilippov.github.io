recipient

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪpiənt / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪpiənt / (BrE)
a person who receives something, especially a gift, award, or payment

Examples

The monthly cost of ADC to more than 100000 recipients in the county is 4.4 million dollars, said C. Virgil Martin, president of Carson Pirie Scott + Co., committee chairman.
Among the recipients of the Nobel Prize for Literature more than half are practically unknown to readers of English.
Life's been good to me.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: donor - a person who gives something
  • Synonym: beneficiary - a person who receives benefits or advantages

Etymology

From Latin recipiens, recipient-, present participle of recipere (to receive).
