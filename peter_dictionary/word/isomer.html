isomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.sə.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sə.mə / (BrE)
A compound that has the same molecular formula as another compound but a different arrangement of atoms in the molecule and different properties.

Examples

In the over-all process, it is difficult to assign a "pure "role to each constituent of a built detergent formulation; and, indeed, there is no more reason to separate the interrelated roles of the active, builder, antiredeposition agent, etc. than there is to assign individual actions to each of the numerous isomers making up a given commercial organic active.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomerism - the phenomenon of compounds existing as isomers
  • Degree Higher: enantiomer - an isomer that is a mirror image of another

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + meros (part).
