hypocorism

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑːkərɪzəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkərɪzəm / (BrE)
A name or term of endearment, especially one formed using a diminutive suffix or by shortening a proper name, such as 'Billy' for 'William'.

Examples

Everything'’s gonna be okay, Frankie, don'’t worry.

Synset Examples

`Billy' is a hypocorism for `William'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminutive - A word or suffix indicating smallness or affection
  • Synonym: pet name - An affectionate or informal name for someone

Etymology

From Greek hypokorisma (term of endearment), from hypokorizesthai (to use pet names).
