friction

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)
Conflict or tension between people or groups, often resulting from disagreement or incompatibility.

Examples

The inmates were white and from out of town, avoiding local friction.
And it is precisely in this poorer economic class that one finds, and has always found, the most racial friction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord among people

Etymology

From Latin 'frictio' (a rubbing), from 'fricare' (to rub).
