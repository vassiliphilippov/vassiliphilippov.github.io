3. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
(of law or rules) formally laid down or imposed by authority, rather than arising naturally or by custom.

Examples

He is a trustee for the common good, however feeble the safeguards which the positive or municipal law of property provides against his misuse of that share of the common fund, wisely or unwisely, entrusted to his keeping.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - (of law) existing in or derived from nature, not from human enactment

Etymology

From Latin positivus ('settled by agreement, positive'), from ponere ('to place').

From Latin positivus (settled by agreement, positive), from ponere (to place).
