consistency

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪstənsi / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənsi / (BrE)
The quality or state of a substance that makes it thick, firm, or able to hold its shape.

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.
He improvised as he went along, completing a life-size clay figure, then bought yards of an inexpensive material from a draper, wet the lightweight cloth in a basin and covered it over with clay that Argiento brought from the bank of the Tiber, to the consistency of thick mud.

Synset Examples

wool has more body than rayon
when the dough has enough consistency it is ready to bake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: texture - the feel, appearance, or consistency of a surface or substance

Etymology

From Latin 'consistentia' (standing together, solidarity), from 'consistere' (to stand together).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪstənsi / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənsi / (BrE)
The quality of having uniformity or agreement among parts or elements.

Examples

The improvement was most noticeable in the greater consistency among reaction cells prepared as a group on the same manifold.
Finally, it gives meaning to much social endeavor, and logic, consistency, and meaning to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherence - the quality of being logical and consistent
  • Antonym: contradiction - a combination of statements, ideas, or features which are opposed to one another

Etymology

From Latin consistentia (standing together, standing firm), from consistere (to stand together, stop).
