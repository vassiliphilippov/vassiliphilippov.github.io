showerhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaʊərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊəhɛd / (BrE)
The part of a shower from which water sprays, typically a detachable or fixed nozzle with small holes.

Examples

The doctor's wits had not left him, however, for all his sixty-eight years, and the wails were almost immediately lost in the sound of water rushing out from the showerhead.

Etymology

Formed from 'shower' and 'head', indicating the head or outlet part of a shower.
