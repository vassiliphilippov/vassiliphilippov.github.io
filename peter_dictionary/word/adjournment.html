adjournment

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒɝnmənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒɜːnmənt / (BrE)
the formal suspension or ending of a meeting or session, especially of a legislative body

Examples

Before adjournment Monday afternoon, the Senate is expected to approve a study of the number of legislators allotted to rural and urban areas to determine what adjustments should be made.
Gov. Vandiver is expected to make the traditional visit to both chambers as they work toward adjournment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjourn - to suspend or end a meeting temporarily or formally

Etymology

From Old French *ajournement* (postponement), from *ajourner* (to adjourn), from Latin *ad* (to) + *diurnus* (daily).
