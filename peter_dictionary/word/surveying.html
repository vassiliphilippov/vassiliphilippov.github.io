surveying

1. NOUN

/ sərˈveɪɪŋ / (AmE), / səˈveɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The science and practice of measuring land angles and distances to determine land boundaries, features, and to create maps.

Examples

In November 1900 surveying was done under John Marsden on the east mountains to ascertain if it would be possible to get sufficient water and fall to operate an electric power plant.

Synset Examples

he studied surveying at college

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartography - the science or practice of drawing maps

Etymology

From 'survey', ultimately from Old French 'surveoir' (to oversee), from Latin 'supervidere'.
