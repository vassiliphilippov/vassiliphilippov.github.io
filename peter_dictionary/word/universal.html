universal

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəl / (BrE)
existing or true in all cases; applicable everywhere or to all people.

Examples

One of the most widespread features of contemporary thought is the almost universal disbelief in the reality of spirit.
The death of a man is unique, and yet it is universal.
Christianity declares that in the life and death of Jesus Christ the unique and the universal concur.
Perhaps no church father saw this concurrence of the unique and the universal as clearly, or formulated it as precisely, as Irenaeus.
Christ is both unique and universal.

Synset Examples

an issue of cosmopolitan import
the shrewdest political and ecumenical comment of our time
universal experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: global - relating to the whole world; worldwide
  • Synonym: worldwide - extending or reaching throughout the world
  • Antonym: local - relating to a particular area, not widespread

Etymology

From Latin universalis (of or belonging to all).

2. ADJ

/ ˌjunɪˈvɜrsəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəl / (BrE)
existing or occurring everywhere or involving everyone; applicable to or common to all members of a group or set.

Examples

Six wardens have been through here in my tenure, and I've learned one immutable, universal truth.

Synset Examples

the play opened to universal acclaim
rap enjoys universal appeal among teenage boys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: general - including or involving all or nearly all elements or cases
  • Antonym: particular - relating to one member or subset rather than all

Etymology

From Latin universalis (general, universal), from universus (whole, entire).
