rotated

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊteɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊteɪtɪd / (BrE)
turned around a central point or axis; having undergone rotation.

Examples

The set of intersections of f, the rotated** curve, with the original curve C consists of just the set of forward corner points on C corresponding to the vertex at **f, plus the vertex itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twisted - forced out of a natural or proper shape; contorted, as by twisting
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position; not rotated or tilted

Etymology

From Latin 'rotatus', past participle of 'rotare' (to rotate).
