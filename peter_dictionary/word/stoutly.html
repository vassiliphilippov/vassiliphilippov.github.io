stoutly

1. ADV

/ ˈstaʊtli / (AmE), / ˈstaʊtli / (BrE)
In a resolute and determined manner; with courage or firmness.

Examples

That after all his years of effort to become a composer, he should now, now when he was still stoutly replying to the critics of his Discourse on the Arts and Sciences, be so close to a success in music and have to reject it.

Synset Examples

he was stoutly replying to his critics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolutely - in a determined and unwavering manner
  • Antonym: timidly - in a way that shows a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From the adjective 'stout' (meaning brave or resolute), with adverbial suffix '-ly'.
