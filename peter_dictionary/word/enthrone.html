enthrone

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈθroʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrəʊn / (BrE)
to give someone power or authority; to install someone in a position of prominence, especially formally or officially

Examples

The Court's opinion dogmatically asserts that the husband wife conspiracy doctrine does not in fact protect this relationship, and that hence the doctrine "enthrone [ s ] an unreality into a rule of law ".

Synset Examples

They vested the council with special rights

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dethrone - to remove someone from power or authority

Etymology

From Old French 'entroner', from en- (in) + trone (throne), ultimately from Greek 'thronos'.
