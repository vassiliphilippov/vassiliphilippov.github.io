stratagem

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræt̬.ə.dʒəm / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ə.dʒəm / (BrE)
a clever scheme or maneuver designed to achieve a specific end, especially in a game, conversation, or contest

Examples

Something indirect, mixed, reconciling, tensional might well be the stratagem, the devious technique by which a poet indulged in all kinds of talk about love and anger and even in something like "expressions "of these emotions, without aiming at their incitement or even uttering anything that essentially involves their incitement ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploy - a cunning plan or action designed to turn a situation to one's own advantage
  • Synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive someone; a trick
  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term goal

Etymology

From Latin 'stratagema', from Greek 'stratēgēma' (artifice, from 'stratēgein' to be a general).
