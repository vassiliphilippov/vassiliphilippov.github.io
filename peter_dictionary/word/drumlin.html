drumlin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmlɪn / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmlɪn / (BrE)
a low, oval mound or small hill of compacted glacial debris, shaped by the movement of glaciers

Examples

From the outside, it seemed no more than a low drumlin, a lump on the dark earth.

Etymology

From Irish *druim* (ridge) or Scottish Gaelic *druim* (hill, ridge).
