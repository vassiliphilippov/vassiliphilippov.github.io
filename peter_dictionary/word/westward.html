westward

1. ADV

/ ˈwɛst.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈwɛst.wəd / (BrE)
In the direction of or toward the west.

Examples

This time he turned westward, to the middle Atlantic coast of North America.
For the first three weeks, the ship skirted up the east coast of Great Britain, then turned westward.
But each mile westward, she had hated him the deeper.

Synset Examples

they traveled westward toward the setting sun

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eastward - toward the east
  • Antonym: northward - toward the north
  • Antonym: southward - toward the south

Etymology

From Old English 'westweard' (toward the west), from 'west' + '-ward'.
