axiomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæksiəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌæksiəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
self-evident or unquestionable; obvious without needing proof or demonstration

Examples

The terms are generally taken for granted as though they referred to direct and axiomatic elements in the common experience of all.

Synset Examples

an axiomatic truth
we hold these truths to be self-evident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-evident - obvious without needing evidence or proof
  • Synonym: unquestionable - cannot reasonably be doubted or disputed

Etymology

From Greek axiomatikos (fit to be considered an axiom), from axioma (that which is thought worthy).
