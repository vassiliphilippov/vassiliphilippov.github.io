elapsed

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlæpst / (AmE), / ɪˈlæpst / (BrE)
(of time) having already passed or gone by; no longer remaining.

Examples

the aspect of learning the internal organization of obvious natural genetic groups of languages as well as their more remote and elusive external links; of classification first, with elapsed age merely a by-product; of acquiring evidential knowledge of what happened in Athabascan, in Yokuts, in Uto-Aztecan in the last few thousand years as well as forecasting what more anciently may have happened between them.
As time goes by and his position becomes stronger, will he attempt any individual vendetta?

Synset Examples

elapsed time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past - having taken place in a time before now
  • Antonym: remaining - left over; still present

Etymology

From the verb "elapse", from Latin elapsus (having slipped away), past participle of elabi.
