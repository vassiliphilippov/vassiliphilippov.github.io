flinch

1. VERB

/ flɪntʃ / (AmE), / flɪntʃ / (BrE)
To make a quick, sudden movement of the body as a reaction to fear, pain, or surprise.

Examples

He took it without flinching.

Synset Examples

she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wince - make a slight involuntary grimace or shrinking movement of the body out of pain or distress
  • Synonym: cower - crouch down in fear
  • Antonym: face - confront bravely or without flinching

Etymology

Probably from Old French *flenchir* (to turn aside).
