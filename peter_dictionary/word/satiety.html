satiety

1. NOUN

/ səˈtaɪəti / (AmE), / səˈtaɪəti / (BrE)
The state of being satisfactorily full, especially after eating; a feeling of having had enough of something.

Examples

And while her Adonis did not suffer from inexperience, satiety might well be an equal handicap.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hunger - the state of feeling a need or desire for food
  • Synonym: fullness - the state of being full, especially after eating

Etymology

From Latin satietas, from satis (enough).
