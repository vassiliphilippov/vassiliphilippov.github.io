Siberia

1. PROPN

/ saɪˈbɪəriə / (AmE), / saɪˈbɪəriə / (BrE)
a vast region in northern Asia, forming part of Russia, known for its harsh climate and long, cold winters

Examples

It was night on this hemisphere, but the light blazed from the megalopolises of Australia, Japan, China, Southeast Asia, India, Siberia.
Siberia spoke Icelandic.
Maybe I would beat old Herry to Siberia after all.

Etymology

From Russian Sibir, possibly from a Tatar or Mongol origin, referring to the medieval khanate of Sibir.
