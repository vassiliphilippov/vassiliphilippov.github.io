motherland

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðərˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəlænd / (BrE)
the country where you were born or which you feel a deep emotional connection to as your native land

Examples

in his motherland; in the spacious hunting grounds of "Uncle Sam "; in the exciting salons of his lovely, brilliant Paris - mistress of gaiety - excess and abandon - world theatre of new found freedoms in tone, color, dance, design, and thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatherland - one's native country, especially as associated with nationalism or ancestry
  • Synonym: homeland - the country where you were born or feel you belong

Etymology

Modelled on similar compounds in other languages (e.g. German Mutterland).
