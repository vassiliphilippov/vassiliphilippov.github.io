subdued

1. ADJ

/ səbˈdud / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːd / (BrE)
(of sound, color, or manner) quiet, soft, or restrained; not loud or intense.

Examples

As Andy reached the finale of his act, a subdued commotion backstage drew his attention to the wings.

Synset Examples

hushed voices
muted trumpets
a subdued whisper
a quiet reprimand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muted - softened or quieted in tone
  • Synonym: hushed - very quiet, as if intended not to be heard

Etymology

Past participle of 'subdue,' from Latin 'subduco' meaning 'to bring under'.
