rampart

1. NOUN

/ ˈræm.pɑrt / (AmE), / ˈræm.pɑːt / (BrE)
A defensive embankment or wall built around a place, especially a fort or city, to protect it against attack.

Examples

He had peered through the darkness at the rampart.
He turned slowly and began to crawl back up the bank toward the rampart.

Synset Examples

they stormed the ramparts of the city
they blew the trumpet and the walls came tumbling down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony
  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification
  • Synonym: wall - a continuous vertical brick or stone structure that encloses or divides an area

Etymology

From Old French 'rempart', from remparer (to fortify), from re- (again) + emparer (to take possession of).
