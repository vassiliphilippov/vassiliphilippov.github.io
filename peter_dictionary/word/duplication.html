duplication

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdjuːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
An exact copy of something; something that corresponds precisely to its original.

Examples

They went to the Flea Market, expecting to find the treasures of Europe, and found instead a duplication of that long double row of booths in Tours.

Synset Examples

he made a duplicate for the files

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - something made to be the same as another; a reproduction
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest form of something, not a copy

Etymology

From Latin duplicatio, from duplicare 'to double'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌduːpləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdjuːplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of making an exact copy or copies of something

Examples

Not only is this kind of duplication wasteful, but it gives the combined system the ability to take freight traffic away from the New York Central and other railroads serving the area.

Synset Examples

this kind of duplication is wasteful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copying - the action of making a copy of something

Etymology

From Latin 'duplicatio', from 'duplicare' (to double).
