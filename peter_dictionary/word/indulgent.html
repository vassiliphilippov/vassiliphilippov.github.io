indulgent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (BrE)
Having a tendency to be overly generous or lenient with someone; willing to allow excessive freedom or satisfy desires.

Examples

The violinist, in particular, is very indulgent with swoops and slides, and his tone is pinched and edgy.

Synset Examples

indulgent grandparents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lenient - showing tolerance or permissiveness; not strict
  • Antonym: strict - demanding that rules are closely followed; not indulgent

Etymology

From Latin indulgent-, present participle of indulgere (to be kind or lenient).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (BrE)
Willing to allow someone to have or enjoy something even though it may not be proper, healthy, appropriate, etc.; showing a readiness to be overly generous or lenient.

Examples

she was too indulgent a lover of dogs and grandchildren.

Synset Examples

indulgent parents risk spoiling their children
too soft on the children
they are soft on crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lenient - tolerant; not strict or severe
  • Antonym: strict - enforcing rules closely; not lenient or indulgent
  • Synonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'indulgent-', from 'indulgere' meaning 'to be kind or tender'.
