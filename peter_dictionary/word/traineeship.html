traineeship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪniˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtreɪniːʃɪp / (BrE)
a period of training or a program in which someone gains practical experience and skills for a particular job, often accompanied by financial support

Examples

The 1954 Amendments completely changed the financing of the vocational rehabilitation program, providing for a three part grant structure - for (1) basic support; (2) extension and improvement; and (3) research, demonstrations, training and traineeships for vocational rehabilitation - and in addition for short-term training and instruction.

Synset Examples

the bill provided traineeships in vocational rehabilitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprenticeship - a period of practical training under supervision, especially in a trade
  • Synonym: internship - a period of work experience offered by an organization for a limited time

Etymology

Formed from 'trainee' + '-ship', paralleling terms like 'studentship'.
