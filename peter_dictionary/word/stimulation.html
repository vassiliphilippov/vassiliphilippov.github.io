stimulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing someone or something to become more active or alert; the process of arousing activity, interest, or energy in an organism or system

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitation - the act of increasing the activity of an organ or part; arousal
  • Antonym: inhibition - the act of restraining or suppressing activity; prevention of action

Etymology

From Latin 'stimulatio' (a goading, incitement), from 'stimulare' (to goad, spur).
