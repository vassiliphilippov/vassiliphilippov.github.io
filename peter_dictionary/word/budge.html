budge

1. VERB

/ bʌdʒ / (AmE), / bʌdʒ / (BrE)
to move slightly or to make someone or something move slightly, often used in the negative to mean 'refuse to move or change one's position'

Examples

Well, Dietrich won't budge from McCormick Place.
But Myra would n't budge without an express invitation.

Synset Examples

He shifted in his seat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to change position; to go from one place to another
  • Synonym: shift - to change position or place very slightly
  • Synonym: yield - to give way under pressure or force

Etymology

From Middle French bouger, from Latin bullicāre (to bubble, move).
