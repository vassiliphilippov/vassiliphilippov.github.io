ineptly

1. ADV

/ ɪˈnɛptli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛptli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of skill or competence; in a clumsy or incompetent manner.

Examples

In a properly ordered society the massive force of public law performs the function which in natural law theory ineptly is left altogether to a small voice so often still.

Synset Examples

he performed his functions ineptly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsily - in a way that shows a lack of coordination or grace; awkwardly
  • Antonym: competently - in a capable or skilled manner
  • Antonym: skillfully - in an expert or proficient manner

Etymology

From 'inept' (from Latin 'ineptus', meaning unsuitable, absurd) + '-ly'.
