whodunit

1. NOUN

/ huːˈdʌnɪt / (AmE), / huːˈdʌnɪt / (BrE)
A story, such as a novel, play, or film, that centers on the investigation of a crime, typically a murder, and challenges the audience to solve the mystery before the solution is revealed.

Examples

In the modern English "whodunnit ", this insinuation of latent criminality in the detective himself has almost entirely disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective story - a story featuring a detective investigating a crime
  • Synonym: mystery - a story focused on solving a puzzling event or crime

Etymology

A blend of the words 'who done it?', originating in early 20th-century American usage.
