imitative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪməˌteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪtətɪv / (BrE)
Tending to copy or reproduce the style or behavior of someone or something else.

Examples

More often than not I have found easy excuse to leave my own work and stand at a respectable distance where I could watch this man transform raw nature into a composed, not imitative, painting.

Synset Examples

acting is an imitative art
man is an imitative being

Related Terms

  • Antonym: original - not derived from something else; fresh and inventive
  • Synonym: mimetic - relating to or characterized by imitation or mimicry

Etymology

From Latin imitativus, from imitari (to imitate).
