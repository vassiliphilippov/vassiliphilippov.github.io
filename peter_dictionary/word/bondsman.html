bondsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndzmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzmən / (BrE)
(archaic) A male slave; a man in bondage or servitude.

Examples

He also spoke of "the wealth piled by the bondsman's two hundred and fifty years in unrequited toil ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them
  • Antonym: freeman - a person who is not a slave or a serf

Etymology

From 'bond' (in sense of 'bound') + 'man'; Middle English 'bondeman'.
