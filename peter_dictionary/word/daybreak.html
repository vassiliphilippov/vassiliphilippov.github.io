daybreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌbreɪk / (BrE)
The time in the morning when light first appears; dawn.

Examples

Arriving at daybreak, they found Julio in his corral and demanded that he surrender.

Synset Examples

we got up before dawn
they talked until morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn - the first appearance of light in the sky before sunrise
  • Degree Higher: sunrise - the time when the sun first appears above the horizon in the morning
  • Antonym: nightfall - the time when it becomes dark and night begins

Etymology

Formed from 'day' + 'break', referring to the breaking or arrival of the day.
