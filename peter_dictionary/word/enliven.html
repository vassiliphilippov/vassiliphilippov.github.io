enliven

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪvən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪvən / (BrE)
To make something more lively, interesting, or exciting.

Examples

Over the years, enlivened chiefly by disputes about the relative merits of Maine and Idaho potatoes, the menu has pursued its drab all-American course.

Synset Examples

These paintings exalt the imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - give life or spirit to; make lively
  • Synonym: exhilarate - make someone feel very happy or animated
  • Synonym: inspire - fill someone with urge or ability to do or feel something
  • Antonym: dull - make or become less lively, intense, or sharp

Etymology

From en- (make) + live, after Middle English enlivener.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈlaɪvən / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪvən / (BrE)
To make something more lively, interesting, or cheerful.

Examples

A brisk, amusing man, apparently constructed on an ingenious system of spring-joints attuned to the same peppery rhythm as his mind, Smith began his academic career teaching speech to Barnard girls - a project considerably enlivened by his devotion to a recording about "a young rat named Arthur, who never could make up his mind ".

Synset Examples

let's liven up this room a bit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - give life or energy to
  • Synonym: liven up - make or become more lively
  • Antonym: dull - make less lively or interesting

Etymology

Formed from 'en-' (make) + 'liven' (to make lively).
