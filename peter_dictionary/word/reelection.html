reelection

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of being elected to a position again, especially after already holding it.

Examples

He will be succeeded by Ivan Allen Jr., who became a candidate in the Sept. 13 primary after Mayor Hartsfield announced that he would not run for reelection.

Synset Examples

he did not run for reelection

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: election - the act of choosing or electing, especially by vote

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'election', from Latin 'eligere' (to choose).
