Oxford

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑks.fərd / (AmE), / ˈɒks.fəd / (BrE)
A city in southern England, northwest of London, known as the site of the University of Oxford.

Examples

An Anglican clergyman in Oxford sadly but frankly acknowledged to me that this is true.

Etymology

From Old English 'Oxnaford', meaning 'ford of the oxen'.
