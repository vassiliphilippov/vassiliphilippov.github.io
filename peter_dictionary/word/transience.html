transience

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrænʃiəns / (AmE), / ˈtrænziəns / (BrE)
The state or quality of lasting only for a short time; the fact of being temporary or not permanent, often suggesting the inevitability of ending or dying.

Examples

Death reminds man of his sin, but it reminds him also of his transience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanence - the state or quality of lasting or remaining unchanged permanently

Etymology

From Latin transiens (present participle of transīre, 'to go across, pass by').
