tired

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəd / (BrE)
feeling in need of rest or sleep; lacking strength or energy due to physical or mental exertion

Examples

He felt tired and full and calm.
"You are looking tired and there you can rest.
Violence always made him tired, but he was not frightened.
Through the open window they heard sounds below in the street: cartwheels, a tired horse's plodding step, voices.
He was tired, he had his business worries, and the sight of his wife arranging pork chops in the broiler only seemed like an extension of a boring day.

Synset Examples

tired mothers with crying babies
too tired to eat

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: exhausted - extremely tired; completely worn out
  • Antonym: rested - having had enough rest; not tired

Etymology

From Old English *tīred*, past participle of *tīerian* (to weary).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəd / (BrE)
(of a remark, phrase, idea, etc.) dull and unoriginal because it has been used too often; banal or hackneyed.

Examples

I'm tired of being afraid all the time.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons
his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor `hard as nails'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banal - so lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring
  • Synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) having been overused; unoriginal and trite
  • Antonym: fresh - (of ideas or expressions) new or original
