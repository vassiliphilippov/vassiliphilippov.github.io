weightlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪt.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪt.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The property or condition of having very little or no weight; the state of being extremely light or appearing to lack weight.

Examples

Many styles have perforations and an almost weightlessness achieved via unlined leathers.
My unscientific friend does not believe that human stature is measurable in terms of speed, momentum, weightlessness, or distance from earth, but is a matter of the development of the human mind.

Synset Examples

the lightness of balsa wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightness - state or quality of being light in weight
  • Antonym: heaviness - state or quality of being heavy in weight

Etymology

Formed from 'weightless' + '-ness'; 'weightless' from 'weight' (from Old English 'wiht') + '-less'.
