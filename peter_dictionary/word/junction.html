junction

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
A place where two or more things, such as roads, railway lines, or rivers, come together or meet.

Examples

They found it at the junction between Troops H and G, and prepared to counterattack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intersection - the point where two lines or roads cross
  • Synonym: confluence - the junction of two rivers

Etymology

From Latin 'junctio' (a joining), from 'jungere' (to join).
