rafter

1. NOUN

/ ˈræftɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːftə / (BrE)
one of a series of parallel sloping beams that support a roof

Examples

She took Glendora to the smokehouse, unlocked it and saw with satisfaction there was still a quantity of hams and sides of bacon, hanging from the smoke stained rafters.

Etymology

From Old English *ræfter*, related to Old Norse *raptr* (beam, rafter).
