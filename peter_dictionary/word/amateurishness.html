amateurishness

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmətɚˈrɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˌamətəˈrɪʃnəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking professional skill or competence; the state or condition of being amateurish.

Examples

Although the play does show a certain structural amateurishness (there are eleven acts varying in length from twenty-five seconds to an hour and a half), the statement it makes concerning the ceaseless yearning and searching of youth is profound and worthy of our attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: professionalism - the competence or skill expected of a professional
  • Synonym: incompetence - inability to do something successfully or as it should be done

Etymology

From 'amateurish' + '-ness'; 'amateur' from French 'amateur', ultimately from Latin 'amator' (lover).
