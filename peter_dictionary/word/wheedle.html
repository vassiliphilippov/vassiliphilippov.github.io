wheedle

1. VERB

/ ˈwiːdəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːdəl / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something or give you something by using flattery or gentle, persistent words

Examples

Eileen must have wheedled a little out of Precious.

Synset Examples

He palavered her into going along

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coax - to gently persuade someone to do something
  • Synonym: cajole - to persuade someone by flattery or promises
  • Antonym: bully - to force someone to do something by intimidation or threats

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *wedilan* (to beg, command), or possibly of Germanic origin, but exact origin uncertain.
