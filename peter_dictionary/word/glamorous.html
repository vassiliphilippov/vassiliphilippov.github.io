glamorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlæmərəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlamərəs / (BrE)
Attractive in an exciting and special way, often suggesting allure, romance, or sophistication.

Examples

The opinion continues here that with a 162 - game schedule, pitching spread thin through a 10 - team league and a most inviting target in Los Angeles' Wrigley Field Jr., Mantle just might break the most glamorous record on the books, Babe Ruth's 60 homers of 1927.

Synset Examples

glamorous movie stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - very attractive or tempting; seductive
  • Antonym: unattractive - not appealing or pleasing to the senses

Etymology

Formed from 'glamour' and the suffix '-ous'; 'glamour' from Scots/English, originally meaning 'magic, enchantment.'
