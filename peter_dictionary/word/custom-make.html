custom-make

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌstəmˌmeɪk / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəmˌmeɪk / (BrE)
to make or produce something according to a customer's specific requirements or instructions

Examples

If he decides to proceed, he will custom-make for Susie an appliance consisting of bands, plastic plates, fine wires, and tiny springs.

Synset Examples

I had this desk custom-made for me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom-built - built to individual specifications
  • Antonym: mass-produce - to manufacture goods in large quantities, not individually customized

Etymology

From 'custom' (meaning special order) + 'make'. The use reflects the act of making something to order.
