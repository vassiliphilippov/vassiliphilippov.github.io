occluded

1. ADJ

/ əˈkluːdɪd / (AmE), / əˈkluːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) absorbed, trapped, or retained within another substance, especially in a solid or liquid.

Examples

The small reaction occurring at 337 ` C is probably caused by decomposition of occluded nitrates, and perhaps by a small amount of some hydrous material other than **f.

Synset Examples

the sorbed oil mass
large volumes of occluded hydrogen in palladium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbed - taken in or soaked up by another substance
  • Synonym: adsorbed - (of a substance) collected on the surface of another material

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere' (to shut up, close up).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkluːdɪd / (AmE), / əˈkluːdɪd / (BrE)
(especially in medical contexts) blocked or closed off, so that passage is prevented, as in a blood vessel or duct.

Examples

In particular, the orifices of the right renal and celiac arteries were virtually occluded, and both calcified common iliac arteries were completely occluded.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - not blocked or closed off

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere' meaning 'to close up'.
