pillar

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlə / (BrE)
a fundamental principle, idea, or practice that is central to the existence or functioning of something

Examples

On these pillars rested that solid basis for life and thought which was soon to be manifested in the remarkably unlimited ken of the Iliad.

Synset Examples

science eroded the pillars of superstition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - an underlying basis or principle for something

Etymology

From Old French *piler*, from Latin *pila* (pillar, pier).
