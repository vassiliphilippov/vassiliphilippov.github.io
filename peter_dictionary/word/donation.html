donation

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dəʊˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A voluntary gift of money, services, or goods to a person, organization, or cause, especially for charitable purposes.

Examples

Money for its construction will be sought later on but in the meantime the State Hospital board can accept gifts and donations of a site.
The red cells were used within 2 days after donation and were washed with large amounts of saline before use.
"In addition, the Library District has generously responded with a charitable donation of used books and sundries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contribution - something given to help achieve or provide something
  • Degree Lower: bequest - a gift of personal property by will

Etymology

From Latin 'donatio', from 'donare' meaning 'to give'.

2. NOUN

/ doʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dəʊˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
An act of giving money, goods, or help to a person or organization, especially to a charity or for a common cause.

Examples

acquire secret processes, technical data, inventions, patent applications, patents, licenses, land and interests in land (including water rights), plants and facilities, and other property or rights by purchase, license, lease, or donation;

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contribution - act of giving something (especially money) to help achieve a purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'donatio' (a presenting, giving), from 'donare' (to give).
