Whig

1. NOUN

/ wɪɡ / (AmE), / wɪɡ / (BrE)
a member of a British political party active from the late 17th to the 19th century that supported social reform and opposed the Tories

Examples

Here he was, suddenly caught up in the delirium of a war, in the spite and calumny of Whigs and Tories.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Tory - a member of the opposing British political party to the Whigs

Etymology

Originally a term of abuse for Scottish Presbyterians; later applied to political factions opposing the monarchy in 17th-century England.
