rumen

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmən / (AmE), / ˈruːmən / (BrE)
the first compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals, where partly digested food collects before being regurgitated as cud for further chewing

Examples

Stimulates rumen activity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: reticulum - the second compartment of a ruminant's stomach

Etymology

From Latin *rumen* (throat, gullet).
