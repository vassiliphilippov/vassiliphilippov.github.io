nonintervention

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
the principle or policy of not becoming involved in the internal affairs or conflicts of other countries

Examples

Now, the American public believe in non-intervention...

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intervention - the act of becoming involved in a situation to influence the outcome

Etymology

From non- (not) + intervention (from Latin intervenire 'come between')
