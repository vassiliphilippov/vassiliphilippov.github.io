opposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the person or group that you are competing against in a contest or battle

Examples

Dewey Lawrence, a Tyler lawyer representing the Texas Bankers Association, sounded the opposition keynote when he said it would force banks to violate their contractual obligations with depositors and undermine the confidence of bank customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - a person or group competing with another
  • Synonym: adversary - an opponent in a contest, conflict, or dispute

Etymology

From Latin 'oppositio(n-)', from opponere, meaning 'to set against'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
The action of resisting or contending against someone or something, especially when groups or individuals are in disagreement or conflict.

Examples

"If the day should ever come that foreign invaders swarm ashore along the Gulf Coast ", the account reads, "they can count on heavy opposition from a group of commando trained telephone employees - all girls.
The opposition to this point of view has its staunchest support in the work of Miller (' 50).
But when this proposal was made to his Deacon Board, he met unanimous opposition.

Synset Examples

the government was not ready for a confrontation with the unions
the invaders encountered stiff opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confrontation - a hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties
  • Synonym: resistance - the refusal to accept or comply with something; the attempt to prevent something by action or argument
  • Antonym: support - the action of backing or aiding a person or cause

Etymology

From Latin 'oppositio', from 'opponere' (to set against).

From Latin oppositionem (a setting against), from opponere (to set against).
