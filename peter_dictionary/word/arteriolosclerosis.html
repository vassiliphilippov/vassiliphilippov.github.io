arteriolosclerosis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪriəloʊskləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriələʊskləˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
Hardening and thickening of the walls of the small arteries (arterioles), often leading to reduced blood flow.

Examples

There were slight fibrosis and marked arteriolosclerosis.
Throughout, there were marked arteriolosclerosis and hyalinization of afferent glomerular arterioles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - thickening and hardening of the arteries in general
  • Synonym: atherosclerosis - build-up of fats and cholesterol in and on artery walls

Etymology

From Greek 'arteriolo-' (diminutive of 'arteria', artery) + 'sclerosis' (hardening).
