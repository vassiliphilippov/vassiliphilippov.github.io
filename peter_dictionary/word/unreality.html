unreality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnriˈæləti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unreal; something that is not true or does not exist in reality.

Examples

The Court's opinion dogmatically asserts that the husband wife conspiracy doctrine does not in fact protect this relationship, and that hence the doctrine "enthrone [ s ] an unreality into a rule of law ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist; opposite of unreality

Etymology

From un- (not) + reality, modeled on the formation of 'reality'.
