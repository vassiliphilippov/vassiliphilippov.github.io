misbranded

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪsˈbrændɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈbrændɪd / (BrE)
(of a product, especially a drug or food) falsely labeled or marked in violation of legal requirements.

Examples

The charge: violation of the California Medical Practices Act by practicing medicine without a license and selling misbranded drugs.

Synset Examples

confiscated the misbranded drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulterated - containing substances that make it impure or unsafe (especially in legal or regulatory contexts)

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrong, ill) and 'branded' (marked or labeled).
