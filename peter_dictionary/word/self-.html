self-

1. ADJ

/ sɛlf / (AmE), / sɛlf / (BrE)
(used as a combining form) relating to oneself; by, for, or of oneself.

Examples

Johnson's fat hand, another bottle were protruding from the truck cab, and that self proclaimed Baptist teetotaler, had a bottle at his own lips.

Synset Examples

self-knowledge
self-proclaimed
self-induced

Etymology

From Old English 'self', meaning 'same, oneself', related to Old High German 'selb'.
