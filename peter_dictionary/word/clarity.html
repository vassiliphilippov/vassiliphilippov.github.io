clarity

1. NOUN

/ ˈklær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈklær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear, easy to understand, or free from ambiguity

Examples

Such fascinating novelties in the score as the fugual treatment of "On the Side of the Angels "and "Politics and Poker "were handled splendidly, and I thought Rudy Bond and his band of tuneful ward-heelers made "Little Tin Box "even better than it was done by the New York cast; all the words of its clever lyrics came through with perfect clarity.
Schnabel's emphasis on structural clarity, his innate rhythmic vibrancy, and impetuous intensity all tend to stamp his reading as a symphonic one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being hard to understand or not clear
  • Synonym: precision - the quality of being exact or accurate

Etymology

From Latin 'claritas' (clearness), from 'clarus' (clear, bright).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrəti / (AmE), / ˈklærɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear and transparent, especially of liquid or expression in someone's eyes

Examples

Then she fell asleep again as suddenly as a person with fever, and when she awoke it was dark outside and the clarity was back in her eyes.

Synset Examples

when she awoke the clarity was back in her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearness - the state or quality of being clear
  • Antonym: opacity - the state of being opaque, not clear or transparent

Etymology

From Latin claritas (clearness, brightness), from clarus (clear).
