flux

1. NOUN

/ flʌks / (AmE), / flʌks / (BrE)
(physics, engineering) The rate of flow of a property—such as energy, particles, or fluid—through a surface or substance.

Examples

The high heat fluxes existing at the electrode surfaces of electric arcs necessitate extensive cooling to prevent electrode ablation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flow - the movement of a fluid or the quantity that moves
  • Degree Lower: influx - an inflow, especially of something substantial
  • Degree Higher: efflux - the flowing out of a substance or property

Etymology

From Latin 'fluxus' meaning 'a flow', from 'fluere' (to flow).
