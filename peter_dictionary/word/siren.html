siren

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən / (BrE)
a device that makes a loud, often wailing sound to signal a warning or attract attention, especially on emergency vehicles or as an alarm

Examples

[MAN ON MEGAPHONE SPEAKING SPANISH] [EXPLOSION] [PEOPLE SHOUTING] [CAR HONKING] [SIREN WAILING] MICHAEL: Fredo!

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Seirēn (mythological creatures), later used for mechanical warning devices due to their piercing sound.
