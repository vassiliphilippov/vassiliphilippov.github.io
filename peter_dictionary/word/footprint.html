footprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.prɪnt / (BrE)
a mark left on a surface by a foot or shoe

Examples

The hoofmarks of cattle and the prints of bare feet in the mud or in the dust were as numerous as the traces of shod horses.
We have footprints, tire tracks, bullets strewn on the ground which bear his fingerprints, a broken bourbon bottle, likewise with fingerprints.

Synset Examples

the police made casts of the footprints in the soft earth outside the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a mark left by a person, animal, or vehicle
  • Synonym: handprint - a mark left on a surface by a hand

Etymology

From foot + print; 'print' from Latin 'premere' (to press).
