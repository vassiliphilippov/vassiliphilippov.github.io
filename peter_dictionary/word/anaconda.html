anaconda

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈkɑndə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈkɒndə / (BrE)
A very large, non-venomous snake found in tropical South America, especially the green anaconda, which is among the largest snakes in the world.

Examples

There seems to be a rough correlation between the initial and ultimate lengths, starting with the smallest (boa constrictor) and ending with the largest (anaconda).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boa constrictor - large non-venomous snake of tropical America

Etymology

From Latin American Spanish, perhaps from Tupi or Sinhalese origin.
