splinter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntə / (BrE)
A small, thin, sharp piece of wood, glass, metal, or similar material broken off from a larger object.

Examples

He was crouched over his anvil in the courtyard getting his chisels into trim, when a splinter of steel flew into his eye and imbedded itself in his pupil.
Argiento made him lie down on the bed, brought a pan of hot water, dipped some clean white linen cloth and applied it to extract the splinter.
He assumed he could blink the splinter out.

Synset Examples

he got a splinter in his finger
it broke into slivers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sliver - a small, thin, narrow piece of something cut or split off

Etymology

From Middle English, possibly from Middle Dutch 'splinter' (splinter, sliver, splint).

2. VERB

/ ˈsplɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪn.tə / (BrE)
(of a group or organization) to break away or separate into smaller groups, often due to disagreement.

Examples

The statement also points to a classic paradox: The more men turn toward God, who is not only in himself the paradigm of all unity but also the only ground on which human unity can ultimately be established, the more men splinter into groups and set themselves apart from one another.

Synset Examples

After the break up of the Soviet Union, many republics broke away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secede - to formally withdraw from membership in a group or organization
  • Antonym: unite - to come together or join into a single group or body
