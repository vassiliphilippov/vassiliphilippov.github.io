coach

1. NOUN

/ koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A person who trains and directs athletes or a sports team, giving instruction and guidance to improve performance.

Examples

Baltimore's bulky spring training contingent now gradually will be reduced as Manager Paul Richards and his coaches seek to trim it down to a more streamlined and workable unit.
But ask coach Darrell Royal what position he plays and you 'll get the quick response, "place kicker ".

"We 're just real happy for the players ", Coach Bill Meek said of the 9 - 7 victory over the Air Force Academy.
Assistant coach John Cudmore described victory as "a good feeling, I think, on the part of the coaches and the players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trainer - person who teaches or conditions athletes
  • Antonym: player - person who participates as an athlete (not a coach)

Etymology

From French *coche* (carriage), later extended to 'instructor' from acting as a tutor (as one who 'carries' students to knowledge).

2. VERB

/ koʊtʃ / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to teach and train someone, especially in sports; to give guidance or instruction to help someone improve their skills

Examples

A good feeling prevailed on the SMU coaching staff Monday, but attention quickly turned from Saturday's victory to next week's problem: Rice University.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - to teach the skills required for a particular job or activity
  • Synonym: instruct - to teach someone how to do something
  • Synonym: mentor - to advise or train someone, especially over a period of time

Etymology

From French *coche* (carriage), later used for tutors or instructors, metaphorically as someone who 'carries' another to a goal.
