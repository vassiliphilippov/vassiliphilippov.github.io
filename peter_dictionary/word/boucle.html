boucle

1. NOUN

/ buːˈkleɪ / (AmE), / buːˈkleɪ / (BrE)
A fabric featuring a looped or knotted surface, made from yarn with a rough, curly or knobby texture.

Examples

From what I was able to gauge in a swift, greedy glance, the figure inside the coral colored boucle dress was stupefying.

Etymology

From French boucle, meaning 'curled' or 'ringed'.
