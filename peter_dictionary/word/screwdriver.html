screwdriver

1. NOUN

/ ˈskruˌdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈskruːˌdraɪvə / (BrE)
A hand tool with a shaped end designed to fit into the head of a screw for turning it.

Examples

Miss Pulova has a voice that Maria Callas once described as "like chipping teeth with a screw driver ", and her round, opalescent face becomes fascinatingly reflective of the emotions demanded by the role of Rosalie.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spanner - tool for gripping and turning nuts or bolts (British)

Etymology

From 'screw' + 'driver', referring to a tool that drives (turns) screws.
