apologize

1. VERB

/ əˈpɑl.əˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒl.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
to express regret for something that you have done wrong, especially to acknowledge and ask forgiveness for a fault or offense

Examples

Winston apologized and quickly set the hat right.
Later, she apologized for the long scratch across his face, tried to explain she could n't help herself, that the panic arose in her unwanted.
You'’re my brother, Fredo, you don'’t have to apologize to me.

Synset Examples

I apologized for being late
He apologized for the many typoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - to make an explanation or seek to lessen blame
  • Antonym: blame - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong

Etymology

From Greek 'apologeisthai' (to give an account, defend oneself), through Late Latin and French.
