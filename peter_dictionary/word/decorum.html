decorum

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkɔrəm / (AmE), / dɪˈkɔːrəm / (BrE)
behavior in keeping with good taste, propriety, or accepted standards of conduct.

Examples

All four types of message listed in Table 1 are permitted, although decorum and cocktail tradition require holding the commands to a minimum, while exclamations having complimentary intonations are more than customarily encouraged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - the state or quality of conforming to conventionally accepted standards of behavior or morals

Etymology

From Latin 'decorum' (that which is seemly), from 'decus' (beauty, propriety).
