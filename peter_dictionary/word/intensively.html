intensively

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that involves a lot of effort, attention, or concentration over a short period of time.

Examples

The biological and physical properties of infectious particles have been studied intensively during the past fifteen years.

Synset Examples

he studied the snake intensively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensely - in a way that is very strong or extreme

Etymology

From 'intensive' + '-ly', where 'intensive' is from Latin 'intensus' (stretched, strained).
