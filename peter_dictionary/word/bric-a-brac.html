bric-a-brac

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪk ə ˌbræk / (AmE), / ˈbrɪk ə ˌbræk / (BrE)
Small decorative objects or ornaments, often of little value, collected for their sentimental or decorative appeal.

Examples

In any given period piece the costumes, bric-a-brac, vehicles, and decor, bear the stamp of unimpeachable authenticity.

Etymology

From French 'bric-à-brac', originally an exclamation meaning 'at random, any old how'.
