phosphorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːsfəˌrəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒsfərəs / (BrE)
A chemical element (symbol P), a nonmetal of the nitrogen family found in phosphate rocks and all living cells, highly reactive and existing in several allotropic forms.

Examples

Calcium, phosphorus and iron are present in worthwhile amounts, and eleven other minerals also have been found in varying trace amounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances; chemical element
  • Degree Lower: iron - a metallic element, another essential mineral

Etymology

From Greek 'phōsphoros' meaning 'light-bringing'.
