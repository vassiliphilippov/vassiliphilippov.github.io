transpose

1. VERB

/ trænˈspoʊz / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to transfer something from one place, context, or period to another

Examples

The Istiqlal found that the spontaneous solidarity of the independence struggle was not easily transposed to the more concrete, precise problems of internal politics.

Synset Examples

The ancient Greek story was transplanted into Modern America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - to move from one place to another

Etymology

From Latin transponere, transpositum, meaning 'to put across' (trans- 'across' + ponere 'to put').

2. VERB

/ trænˈspoʊz / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to change the order or arrangement of; to exchange the positions of two or more things

Examples

In order to be saved, plasticity had had to be isolated; and as the aspect of the subject was transposed into those clusters of more or less interchangeable and contour obliterating facet-planes by which plasticity was isolated under the Cubist method, the subject itself became largely unrecognizable.
He had written out the license number of his car but had transposed the last two figures, an old dodge which is still good.

Synset Examples

Dyslexics often transpose letters in a word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permute - to change the order or arrangement of
  • Synonym: rearrange - to change the arrangement or order
  • Synonym: reverse - to turn something the opposite way or direction

Etymology

From Latin transponere, transposit-, from trans- (across) + ponere (to place).

3. VERB

/ trænˈspoʊz / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to cause two or more things to change places with each other

Examples

One frequently has the feeling that the order of their movement combinations could be transposed without notable loss of effect, there is too little suggestion of organic relationship and development.

Synset Examples

interchange this screw for one of a smaller size

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchange - exchange or swap places

Etymology

From Latin transponere, from trans- (across) + ponere (to place).
