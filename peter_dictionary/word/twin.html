twin

1. ADJ

/ twɪn / (AmE), / twɪn / (BrE)
Used before a noun to describe two things that are exactly the same in appearance, size, or nature and are paired or closely related.

Examples

Mark asked, and Abel lifted his eyes from the double lines in the middle of the road, the twin white ribbons which the car swallowed rapidly as it ascended the crest of the hill and came down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - consisting of two equal, identical, or similar parts

Etymology

From Old English twinn (double, twin), related to 'two'.

2. NOUN

/ twɪn / (AmE), / twɪn / (BrE)
Either of two children or animals born at the same birth to the same mother.

Examples

Conchita kept an eye on the twins and little Elena, trying to keep them from falling into the creek by which they persisted in playing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triplet - one of three siblings born in the same birth
  • Degree Lower: singleton - an only child born at a single birth (not a twin or multiple)

Etymology

From Old English twinn (double, twofold).
