lullaby

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlʌləˌbaɪ / (BrE)
A gentle song sung to calm a child and help them fall asleep.

Examples

By September 1940 the suite had developed into a collection of six songs, "four spirituals, a dream, and a lullaby ".
Shut the fuck up, or I'll sing you a lullaby!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cradle song - another term for a song sung to lull a child to sleep

Etymology

From early 16th century, imitative of a sound used to lull a child to sleep, possibly from 'lulla' (to lull) + 'by' (as in 'bye-bye').
