megalopolis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈlɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈlɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
A very large urban area, typically consisting of several cities and towns that have grown together to form one continuous, heavily populated region.

Examples

It was night on this hemisphere, but the light blazed from the megalopolises of Australia, Japan, China, Southeast Asia, India, Siberia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conurbation - a large urban area formed by the merging of several towns or cities

Etymology

From Greek mega- (large) + polis (city).
