mausoleum

1. NOUN

/ mɔsəˈliəm / (AmE), / mɔːsəˈliːəm / (BrE)
A large, stately building housing a tomb or tombs, typically above ground, built to contain the remains of one or more deceased people.

Examples

The removal of Stalin's body from the mausoleum he shared with Lenin to less distinguished quarters in the Kremlin wall is not unprecedented in history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomb - a grave or vault for the dead
  • Synonym: crypt - an underground burial chamber

Etymology

From Latin Mausōlēum, from Ancient Greek Μαυσωλεῖον (Mausōleîon), the tomb of Mausolus, ruler of Caria.
