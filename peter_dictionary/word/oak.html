oak

1. NOUN

/ oʊk / (AmE), / əʊk / (BrE)
The hard, durable wood of an oak tree, used especially for making furniture and flooring.

Examples

When he had stored his stock in the great oak chest, locked the two big hasps and secured the additional chain, tied the fly of the tent, and picked up the cash box, he moved up the darkening street.
Her quarters were on the right as you walked into the building, and her small front room was clogged with heavy furniture - a big, round, oak dining table and chairs, a buffet, with a row of unclaimed letters inserted between the mirror and its frame.
The door of the lockup was of oak planks and banded with strap iron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak tree - a tree of the genus Quercus, source of oak wood

Etymology

From Old English *āc*, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch *eik* and German *Eiche*.

2. NOUN

/ oʊk / (AmE), / əʊk / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree of the genus Quercus, typically having lobed leaves and producing acorns.

Examples

We went there for a picnic and made love under that oak.

Synset Examples

great oaks grow from little acorns

Etymology

Old English *āc*, related to Old Norse *eik* and German *Eiche*.
