typhus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈtɪ.fəs / (BrE)
an infectious disease caused by rickettsial bacteria, transmitted by lice or fleas, and marked by high fever, headache, and a skin rash

Examples

Let us assume that it would be possible for an enemy to create an aerosol of the causative agent of epidemic typhus (Rickettsia prowazwki) over City A and that a large number of cases of typhus fever resulted therefrom.
Lousiness is a prerequisite for epidemic typhus.

Etymology

From Greek typhos (smoke, stupor; fever), referring to the fever's delirious state.
