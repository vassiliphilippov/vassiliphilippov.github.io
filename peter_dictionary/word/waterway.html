waterway

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a river, canal, or other stretch of water that is wide and deep enough for ships or boats to travel through

Examples

Hudson now proposed to sail all the way through and test the seas beyond for the long sought waterway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canal - an artificial waterway constructed to allow passage of boats or ships

Etymology

From 'water' + 'way', denoting a way or route by water.
