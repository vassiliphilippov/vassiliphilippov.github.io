wallet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːlɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɒlɪt / (BrE)
A small, flat case, typically made of leather or fabric, used for carrying paper money, credit cards, and other personal items.

Examples

My camp made leather wallet, bulky with twisted, raised stitches around the edges, I stuffed with money I had been saving.
I felt for my wallet.
Check his wallet.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *walet* (bag, knapsack), of uncertain origin.
