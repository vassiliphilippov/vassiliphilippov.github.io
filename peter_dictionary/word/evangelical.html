evangelical

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪk.əl / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒɪˈlɪk.əl / (BrE)
Relating to a branch of Christianity or to Christian churches that emphasize personal conversion, the authority of the Bible (especially the Gospels), and often evangelism.

Examples

He points out that from the time of Jackson on through World War 1,, evangelical Protestantism was a dominant influence in the social and political life of America.
He terms this early enthusiasm "Romantic Christianity "and concludes that its similarity to democratic beliefs of that day is so great that "the doctrine of liberty seems but a secular version of its counterpart in evangelical Protestantism ".

Synset Examples

evangelical Christianity
an ultraconservative evangelical message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentalist - strictly adhering to traditional Christian doctrine, especially biblical literalism
  • Antonym: liberal - favoring more flexible, non-literal interpretation of Christian teachings

Etymology

From Medieval Latin evangelicus, from Greek euangelion (good news, gospel).
