neutralize

1. VERB

/ ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something ineffective or harmless by applying an opposite force or effect

Examples

A solid immunity is the one effective circumstance whereby attack by a specific agent can be neutralized.

Synset Examples

Her optimism neutralizes his gloom
This action will negate the effect of my efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counteract - to act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralize it
  • Synonym: negate - to make ineffective or null

Etymology

From French neutraliser, from neutre (neutral) + -iser (-ize).

2. VERB

/ ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a country or area politically neutral, especially by international agreement or treaty, so that it does not take sides in a conflict

Examples

Thus, typically, the first stage of a Communist takeover is to "neutralize "a country.
A nation such as Switzerland could be neutralized by agreement and could be relied upon to protect its neutrality; more doubtful, but possible, (with an assist from the North) was the neutralization of the Latin American countries; out of the question was the neutralization of Asia and Africa.
U. S. willingness to accept a neutral Laos may have led Premier Khrushchev to believe that other areas could be "neutralized "on Soviet terms.

Synset Examples

The treaty neutralized the small republic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demilitarize - to remove military forces from (an area)

Etymology

From French neutraliser, from neutre (neutral) + -iser (-ize).
