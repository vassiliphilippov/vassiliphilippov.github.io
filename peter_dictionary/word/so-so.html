so-so

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊ ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ ˌsəʊ / (BrE)
Only average in quality; neither particularly good nor bad.

Examples

The '49 team was off to a so-so 5 - 5 beginning, then fell as low as 12 - 17 on May 23 before finishing with 96 victories.
This clergyman should have referred to Shakespeare's dictum: "So-so is a good, very good, very excellent maxim.
It is but so-so ".

Synset Examples

an indifferent performance
a gifted painter but an indifferent actor
her work at the office is passable
a so-so golfer
feeling only so-so
prepared a tolerable dinner
a tolerable working knowledge of French

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good
  • Degree Higher: excellent - extremely good; outstanding
  • Degree Lower: poor - of low quality; bad

Etymology

From the repetition of 'so' to indicate moderate degree or middling quality; first recorded in the 16th century.
