sternocleidomastoid

1. NOUN

/ stɚˌnoʊˌklaɪdoʊˈmæstɔɪd / (AmE), / stɜːˌnəʊˌklaɪdəʊˈmæstɔɪd / (BrE)
A thick muscle located in the side of the neck, running from the sternum and clavicle to the mastoid process; it turns the head to the opposite side and, when both act together, flexes the neck or extends the head.

Examples

And they could n't have entrusted Henri to better hands because "le professeur "knows his muscles from the sterno-cleido mastoideus of the neck right down to the tibialis anticus of the leg - and better still, he knows just what exercises work best for them and what Weider principles to combine them with for fast, fast muscle growth.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'sterno-' (sternum), 'cleido-' (clavicle), and 'mastoides' (mastoid process).
