amphibology

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fəˈbɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈbɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
A phrase or sentence that is ambiguous due to its grammatical structure, allowing more than one interpretation.

Examples

There are many types of ambiguity and many of them have been described by rhetoricians under such names as amphibology, parisology, and other ologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguity - uncertainty or inexactness of meaning in language

Etymology

From Late Latin amphibologia, from Greek amphibolos (ambiguous) + -logia (speaking).
