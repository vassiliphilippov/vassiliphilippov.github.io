furlong

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːlɒŋ / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 220 yards (about 201 meters), formerly used in Britain and the US, especially in horse racing.

Examples

Toying with her field in the early stages, Garden Fresh was asked for top speed only in the stretch by Jockey Philip Grimm and won by a length and a half in 1.24 3 - 5 for the 7 furlongs.

Etymology

From Old English 'furlang', from 'furh' (furrow) + 'lang' (long).
