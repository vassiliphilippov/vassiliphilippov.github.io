uncanny

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkæni / (AmE), / ʌnˈkani / (BrE)
Strange or mysterious, especially in a way that makes you feel uneasy or suggests supernatural influences.

Examples

Their appearance, next spring, coincides in an almost uncanny way with the flowering of their host plants.

Synset Examples

an eldritch screech
the three weird sisters
stumps...had uncanny shapes as of monstrous creatures
an unearthly light
he could hear the unearthly scream of some curlew piercing the din

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eerie - strange and frightening; uncanny
  • Antonym: normal - ordinary and expected; not strange or uncanny

Etymology

From Scots 'uncannie', meaning 'unsafe, unlucky', from 'un-' (not) + 'canny' (safe, pleasant).
