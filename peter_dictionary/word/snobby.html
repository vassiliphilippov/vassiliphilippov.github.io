snobby

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnɑbi / (AmE), / ˈsnɒbi / (BrE)
Having or showing the attitude of people who think they are better than others; socially exclusive and dismissive of those considered inferior.

Examples

I could see why some of the boys took him for snobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
  • Synonym: elitist - believing that only people with special status or quality deserve influence or privilege
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's own importance

Etymology

From "snob" (person who despises those considered to be of lower social status) + -y (adjective suffix).
