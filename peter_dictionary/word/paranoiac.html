paranoiac

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈnɔɪˌæk / (AmE), / ˌparəˈnɔɪak / (BrE)
A person suffering from paranoia, especially someone exhibiting excessive suspicion or mistrust of others.

Examples

This is the force, in our time and in every other time, that urges the paranoiac and the manic-depressive to become head of a state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranoid - person afflicted with paranoia

Etymology

Formed from 'paranoia' (from Greek 'para-' beside + 'noos' mind) + '-ac' (forming adjectives or nouns).
