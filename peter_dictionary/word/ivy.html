ivy

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
A climbing evergreen plant with lobed leaves and small black berries, commonly found on walls and trees, especially in Europe and Asia.

Examples

There was the slate roof of the church; ivy climbed the red brick walls like a green scaled monster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeper - plant that grows by climbing or spreading

Etymology

From Old English īfig; related to German Efeu, Dutch ijf.
