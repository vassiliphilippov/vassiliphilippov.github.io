embodiment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑdiˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒdɪmənt / (BrE)
a person or thing that represents or is a perfect example of an idea, quality, or concept

Examples

By virtue of his self-reliance, his individualism and his freedom from external restraint, the private eye is a perfect embodiment of the middle class conception of liberty, which amounts to doing what you please and let the devil take the hindmost.

Synset Examples

the embodiment of hope
the incarnation of evil
the very avatar of cunning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarnation - the manifestation or embodiment of a quality or concept in a bodily form
  • Synonym: personification - the representation of an abstract quality or idea in human form

Etymology

From embody + -ment; 'embody' from Old French 'embodie', from 'em-' (in) + 'body'.
