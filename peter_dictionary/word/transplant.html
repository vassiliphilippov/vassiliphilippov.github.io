transplant

1. VERB

/ trænˈsplænt / (AmE), / trænˈsplɑːnt / (BrE)
to move a plant or organism from one place and set it in another so that it may continue growing or living; to transfer to a new situation or environment

Examples

Every time you transplant a pansy you cause its flowers to become smaller.
The moral is: do n't transplant it any oftener than you must.
Eldest of the seven, Benjamin Franklin, a New Englander transplanted to Philadelphia, wrote the most dazzling success story in our history.

Synset Examples

Transplant the young rice plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replant - to plant something again in a new place

Etymology

From Latin 'transplantare' (to plant across), from 'trans-' (across) + 'plantare' (to plant).
