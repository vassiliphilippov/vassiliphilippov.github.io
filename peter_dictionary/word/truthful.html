truthful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtruθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtruːθ.fəl / (BrE)
Saying or representing what is true; conforming to fact or reality.

Examples

If he wanted to know anything, he would end up asking about it point-blank, but in this guileless manner he would probably receive more truthful answers than if he tried to get them by indirection.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't have told you this if it weren't so
a truthful statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - telling the truth; not lying or cheating
  • Antonym: dishonest - not telling the truth; lying or deceitful

Etymology

Formed from 'truth' + '-ful' (full of); 'truth' from Old English 'trēowþ'.
