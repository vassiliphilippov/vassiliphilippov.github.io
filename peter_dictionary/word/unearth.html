unearth

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈɝθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːθ / (BrE)
to discover or bring something to light that was hidden or unknown, especially by investigation or by digging up from the ground

Examples

Both parties and the Ministry of the Interior were busily at work after the elections trying to unearth the political affiliations of the successful candidates and, thereby, give the elections a confidential but known degree of national political significance.
Rummaging through a stack of drawers nearby, you unearthed an antique French chess set in ivory and sandalwood, which, along with two box Kodaks, you added to your haul.

Synset Examples

The CIA unearthed a plot to kill the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discover - to find something for the first time; to become aware of something formerly hidden
  • Synonym: expose - to make visible; to reveal or uncover something hidden
  • Antonym: bury - to put into the ground and cover with earth, or to conceal

Etymology

From un- (not) + earth (to cover with earth), originally meaning to dig up from the ground.
