ironshod

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪərnˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌʃɒd / (BrE)
Fitted or covered with iron, especially at the base, as in hooves, wheels, or footwear.

Examples

He could feel his own feet, iron-shod, striking repeatedly until the body was limp.

Synset Examples

ironshod hooves
ironshod wheels

Etymology

From 'iron' + 'shod', past participle of 'shoe', meaning to fit with a shoe or covering.
