warble

1. VERB

/ ˈwɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːbəl / (BrE)
to sing or play a note with a series of rapid changes in pitch, producing a trilling effect

Examples

He's been warbling in severe pain; a medico's injection inflamed a nerve, and Johnny can barely walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trill - to sing or play a note alternating rapidly with another

Etymology

From Old French *werbler* (to trill), of Frankish origin, related to Old High German *wirbilon* (to whirl).
