restock

1. VERB

/ riˈstɑk / (AmE), / riːˈstɒk / (BrE)
to supply with new stock; to replenish goods, supplies, or animals that have been used or depleted

Examples

After Larkin has been persuaded to restock his tangled acres with pheasants, he poaches only what he needs for the nourishment of his family and local callers.

Synset Examples

He restocked his land with pheasants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replenish - to fill something up again or restore to a former level or condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + stock (to supply with goods or animals).
