baptise

1. VERB

/ ˈbæp.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈbæp.taɪz / (BrE)
to perform the Christian ceremony of baptism, which involves admitting someone into the Christian Church, usually by sprinkling or immersing them in water

Examples

Michael Rizzi, will you be baptised?

Synset Examples

The parents had the child baptized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: christen - give a Christian name at baptism

Etymology

From Greek 'baptizein' (to immerse, dip), via Late Latin 'baptizare' and Old French 'baptiser'.
