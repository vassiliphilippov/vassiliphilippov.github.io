sprawling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsprɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
An ungainly or loosely spread posture in which the arms and legs are stretched out awkwardly.

Examples

Mr. McKinley, for all his sprawling and his easy familiarity, was completely alert to his son, eyes always on the still face, jumping to anticipate Scotty's desires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprawl - to sit or lie with arms and legs spread out awkwardly

Etymology

From the verb 'sprawl', of uncertain origin, possibly of Scandinavian origin (cf. Norwegian 'sprawle').
