phosgene

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊzdʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈfɒzdʒiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous, colorless gas with a smell similar to freshly cut hay, used especially in the production of chemicals and formerly as a chemical warfare agent

Examples

When typical reaction cells to which 0.3 of an atmosphere of oxygen had been added were illuminated, chlorine and phosgene were produced.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'gene' (producing), because it was originally made by exposing carbon monoxide and chlorine to sunlight.
