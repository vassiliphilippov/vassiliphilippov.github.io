manufacture

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃə / (BrE)
The organized process of making goods, especially on a large scale, for sale.

Examples

He must be capable of designing for and supervising the manufacture of any craft materials needed in the furnishings ".
The city was a center of manufacture, especially in textiles, and also because of the beauty of some of its surroundings, a residence for many owners of the great industries in north Alabama.

Synset Examples

American industry is making increased use of computers to control production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: production - the process of making or manufacturing from components or raw materials
  • Antonym: consumption - the using up of goods and services

Etymology

From Latin 'manu factus', meaning 'made by hand'.

2. VERB

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃə / (BrE)
to make or produce goods, especially on a large scale, using machinery or industrial processes

Examples

Mr. Flannagan manufactured plastic tongue depressors.
Modern psychology has shown that paralleling "the authoritarian personality "is "the bigoted personality "in which insecurity, inferiority, suspicion, and distrust combine to provide a target for antagonism so indispensable that it will be manufactured if it does not exist naturally.
Providing these means are about ninety companies which manufactured the estimated 1800000 boat trailers now in use.

Synset Examples

the company fabricates plastic chairs
They manufacture small toys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to make or create, especially on a large scale
  • Synonym: fabricate - to invent or manufacture, especially with regard to objects or products
  • Antonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it

Etymology

From Latin 'manu factus', meaning 'made by hand', from 'manus' (hand) + 'facere' (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃə / (BrE)
to invent or fabricate (a story, excuse, etc.), especially with the intent to deceive; to make up something untrue or artificial

Examples

Those named in the Greek paper were manufacturing reasons to steal aft under pretence of some call of duty, so as to be near Spencer, watching an opportunity to communicate with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabricate - to invent or concoct (something), typically with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: concoct - to create or devise (a story or plan), often with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: falsify - to make false or untrue by adding or changing information

Etymology

From Latin 'manu factus' (made by hand), via French 'manufacture'.
