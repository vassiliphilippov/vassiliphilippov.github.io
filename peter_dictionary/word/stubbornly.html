stubbornly

1. ADV

/ ˈstʌb.ɚn.li / (AmE), / ˈstʌb.ən.li / (BrE)
In a manner that shows determination not to change one's attitude or position, even in the face of reason or persuasion.

Examples

God would not do that, Kate thought stubbornly.
The old woman, stubbornly reigning in the house above the crashing waters took on an ominous reality.
It might be contended, of course, that Eichmann in stubbornly denying anti-Semitic feelings was lying or insisting on a private definition of anti-Semitism.

Synset Examples

she remained stubbornly in the same position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinately - in a way that refuses to change one's opinion

Etymology

From 'stubborn' + '-ly'; 'stubborn' from Middle English 'stibourne' (obstinate).
