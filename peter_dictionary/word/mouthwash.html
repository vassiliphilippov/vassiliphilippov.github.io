mouthwash

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
A liquid preparation used for rinsing the mouth and throat to clean or freshen them, often containing antiseptic or medicated ingredients.

Examples

Cologne, mouthwash, underarm deodorant?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gargle - liquid used to rinse the mouth and throat

Etymology

Formed from 'mouth' + 'wash'; first recorded in the late 19th century.
