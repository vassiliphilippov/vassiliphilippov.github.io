bathos

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪθɑs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪθɒs / (BrE)
(in writing or speech) an effect of anticlimax created by a sudden transition from the lofty or serious to the trivial or ridiculous, often producing unintentional humor or insincerity.

Examples

He evolved programs that plumbed new depths of bathos and besmirched whole networks, and quickly superseded his boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticlimax - a disappointing end or sudden drop in tone after a build-up

Etymology

From Greek báthos (depth), used in English from the 18th century to describe sudden descent in style.
