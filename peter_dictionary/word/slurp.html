slurp

1. VERB

/ slɝp / (AmE), / slɜːp / (BrE)
to eat or drink something with a loud sucking noise

Examples

Grabski slurped his way to the bottom of the soup bowl.
[SLURPING]

Synset Examples

He slurped his soup

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sip - to drink (something) by taking small mouthfuls
  • Degree Higher: gulp - to swallow food or drink quickly or in large mouthfuls, often noisily
