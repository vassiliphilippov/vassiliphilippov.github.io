dispassionate

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈpæʃənət / (AmE), / dɪsˈpæʃənət / (BrE)
Not influenced by strong emotion or personal feelings; able to be objective and impartial.

Examples

Instead of emotional recrimination, loaded phrases and sloganeering, we need a dispassionate study of the facts, a better understanding of the opposite viewpoint and a more serious effort to extend the areas of agreement until a solution is reached.

Synset Examples

a journalist should be a dispassionate reporter of fact

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - not biased or partial; fair
  • Synonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings
  • Antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • Antonym: partial - favoring one side; biased

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'passionatus' (full of passion).
