index

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.dɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɛks / (BrE)
to list or arrange items in an index for easy reference

Examples

Under the sponsorship of the Office of Naval Research and the Advanced Research Projects Agency, a data center was established to gather and index all published information on atomic transition probabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalog - to make a systematic list of items

Etymology

From Latin 'index' (sign, list, pointer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.dɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɛks / (BrE)
a numerical scale used to compare one value, variable, or amount against another or against a reference value, often to show changes over time or differences between groups

Examples

The criterion score used in the statistical analysis is an index of over - or under-achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator - something that shows or points out; a sign or signal

Etymology

From Latin 'index' meaning 'forefinger, pointer, indicator'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
A number or statistical value, typically derived from a series of measurements, that indicates changes or levels in a variable over time or in comparison to others.

Examples

Thomas also presents a simple equation for deriving an index of persistence, which weights not only the number of stems (' roots') per meaning, but their relative frequency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator - something that indicates or points to a value or condition
  • Synonym: gauge - an instrument or device for measuring a value

Etymology

From Latin 'index' (one who points out, sign, indicator).
