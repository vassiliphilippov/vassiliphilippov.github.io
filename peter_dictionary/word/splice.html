splice

1. VERB

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
to join the ends of two pieces of rope, film, or other material by weaving, gluing, or otherwise fastening them together

Examples

It is over this piece that the laminated stem and keelson are spliced.

Synset Examples

splice film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to bring or put together
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *splissen* (to splice ropes).
