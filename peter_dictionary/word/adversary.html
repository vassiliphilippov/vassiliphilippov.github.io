adversary

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vərˌsɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.sə.ri / (BrE)
A person, group, or force that opposes or attacks; an opponent or enemy.

Examples

The traditional strategy of the South has been to expose the vices of the North, to demonstrate that the North possessed no superior virtue, to "show the world that " (as James's Christopher Newman said to his adversaries) "however bad I may be, you 're not quite the people to say it ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opponent - a person who competes against or fights another in a contest
  • Synonym: enemy - a person actively opposed or hostile to someone or something
  • Antonym: ally - a person or group that cooperates with or helps another
  • Antonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Latin adversarius (opponent, rival), from advertere (to turn toward).
