Zionist

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪənɪst / (AmE), / ˈzaɪənɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports Zionism, a movement for the development and protection of a Jewish nation, originally in Palestine and now chiefly in Israel.

Examples

He had sat beside Alexander Brandel at the rostrum of a congress of Zionists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti-Zionist - a person who opposes Zionism

Etymology

From Zion, a biblical term for Jerusalem, combined with -ist (a person who supports a particular doctrine or movement).
