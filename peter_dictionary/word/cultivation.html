cultivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of developing or improving one's mind, manners, or social abilities through education or training; refinement.

Examples

As Yinger has pointed out, the "reliance on symbols, on tradition, on sacred writings, on the cultivation of emotional feelings of identity and harmony with sacred values, turns one to the past far more than to the future ".

Synset Examples

her cultivation was remarkable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refinement - the improvement of something by making it more polished or cultured
  • Antonym: crudeness - the quality of being unrefined or lacking social polish

Etymology

From Latin 'cultivatio', from 'cultivare' (to cultivate), derived from 'cultus' (care).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl.təˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The activity of preparing and using land to grow crops, especially on a large scale.

Examples

An Alabamian wrote: "Col. Henry is [ an ignoramus ] fit for nothing higher than the cultivation of corn ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farming - the activity or business of growing crops and raising animals
  • Synonym: agriculture - the practice of cultivating the land or raising stock

Etymology

From Latin *cultivatio* (a tilling, cultivation), from *cultivare* (to cultivate), from *cultus* (cultivated, cared for).
