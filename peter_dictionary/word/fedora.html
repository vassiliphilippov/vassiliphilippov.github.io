fedora

1. NOUN

/ fəˈdɔɹə / (AmE), / fɪˈdɔːrə / (BrE)
A soft felt hat with a curled brim and a creased crown, typically worn by men.

Examples

My eyes were so bleary I could barely see him but there he was, a little smooth olivefaced guy in a new spring overcoat and a taffy colored fedora.

Etymology

From the title of the 1882 play 'Fédora' by Victorien Sardou, in which the main character wore such a hat.
