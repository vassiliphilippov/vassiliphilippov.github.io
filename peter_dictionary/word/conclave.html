conclave

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.kleɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kleɪv / (BrE)
A private or secret meeting, especially one held by a group for confidential discussion or decision-making.

Examples

Fosterite bishops, after secret conclave, announced the Church's second Major Miracle: Supreme Bishop Digby had been translated bodily to Heaven and spot-promoted to Archangel, ranking with-but-after Archangel Foster.

Etymology

From Latin 'conclavem', from 'con-' (together) + 'clavis' (key), originally meaning 'a room that can be locked.'
