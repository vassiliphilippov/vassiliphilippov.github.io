alight

1. VERB

/ əˈlaɪt / (AmE), / əˈlaɪt / (BrE)
(of a bird, insect, or airborne object) to come down from the air and settle or land gently on something.

Examples

After baptism Jesus came up out of the water at once, and at that moment heaven opened; he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove to alight upon him ";

Synset Examples

Misfortune lighted upon him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - (of an aircraft, bird, or flying object) to come down onto the ground or another surface

Etymology

From Old English *on līhtan* ('to descend, get down'), related to 'light' (to dismount or land).
