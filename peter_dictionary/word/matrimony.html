matrimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈmætrɪməni / (BrE)
The state of being married; marriage, especially as a formal or religious institution.

Examples

But Blanche had been able to maintain a serene and assured composure in the face of her widowed mother's continued carping, had been able to resist her urgings to date anyone who offered the slightest possibility of matrimony.

Synset Examples

a long and happy marriage
God bless this union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the state of being a married couple
  • Antonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage

Etymology

From Latin matrimonium, from mater (mother).
