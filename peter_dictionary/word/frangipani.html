frangipani

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrændʒɪˈpæni / (AmE), / ˌfrændʒɪˈpæni / (BrE)
a tropical American shrub or small tree of the genus Plumeria, with milky sap and fragrant, variously colored flowers

Examples

Shy, slender-waisted girls at the loom in harmony with the frangipani by the wayside.

Etymology

From Italian frangipane, the name of a 16th-century nobleman whose family produced a perfume, later associated with the plant's scent.
