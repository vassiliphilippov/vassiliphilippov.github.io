hydroxymethyl

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksɪˈmɛθɪl / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksɪˈmɛθɪl / (BrE)
A chemical group consisting of a methyl group in which one hydrogen atom has been replaced by a hydroxyl group (-CH2OH).

Examples

This buffer, pH 8.6, was 0.005 M in * * f and 0.039 M in tris (hydroxymethyl) - aminomethane (Tris).

Etymology

From hydroxy- (containing a hydroxyl group) + methyl (from Greek methy, 'wine', and hylē, 'wood').
