suction

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
a force that draws an object or substance into a space or causes it to move, created by a difference in pressure between two areas

The maximum suction was 3.25 "of test fluid measured from the top of the block, and steady states were apparently reached with these fluids.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'suctio', from 'sugere' (to suck).
