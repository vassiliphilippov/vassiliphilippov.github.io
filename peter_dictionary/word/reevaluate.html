reevaluate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪˈvæljuːˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈvæljʊˌeɪt / (BrE)
to assess or judge something again, especially with the possibility of changing a previous decision or opinion

Examples

A steering committee of students was organized on the first day whose duty it was to be alert and constantly evaluate and re-evaluate the direction and pace the class was taking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaluate - to judge or assess the value, quality, or significance of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + evaluate (from French 'évaluer', based on 'value').
