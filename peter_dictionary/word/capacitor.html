capacitor

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpæsɪtɚ / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪtə / (BrE)
An electrical component that stores energy in an electric field, used to store and release electrical charge in a circuit.

Examples

In this novel arrangement the "pill "is much smaller and contains only a resonant circuit in which the capacitor is formed by a pressure sensing transducer.
The opening action is obtained when a capacitor, charged to high voltage, is suddenly discharged through the foil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condenser - Older term for a capacitor, an electrical device that stores charge

Etymology

From Latin 'capacitas' (capacity), referring to the device's ability to store charge.
