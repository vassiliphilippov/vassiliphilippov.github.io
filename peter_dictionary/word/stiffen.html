stiffen

1. VERB

/ ˈstɪfən / (AmE), / ˈstɪfən / (BrE)
to become or make something become stiff or more rigid

Examples

John stiffened in anger.
When the cloth dried and stiffened, he saw what adjustments had to be made.
The thin legs twitched convulsively once, then Kate felt the little body stiffening in her arms and heard one strangled sound.
I stiffened.
Lolotte looked up and stiffened.

Synset Examples

He stiffened when he saw his boss enter the room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relax - to become less stiff or tense

Etymology

From 'stiff' + '-en' suffix, Middle English 'stiffen'

2. VERB

/ ˈstɪfən / (AmE), / ˈstɪfən / (BrE)
to make or become stiff or stiffer; to cause something to lose flexibility or pliancy

Examples

The Pathet Lao, stiffened by Communist Veterans from neighboring North Viet Nam, were supplied by Soviet aircraft.
When I hold my son he stiffens his whole body in my arms until he is as straight and stiff as a board.

Synset Examples

Stiffen the cream by adding gelatine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loosen - to make less tight or less stiff

Etymology

From 'stiff' + -en, a common English verb-forming suffix.
