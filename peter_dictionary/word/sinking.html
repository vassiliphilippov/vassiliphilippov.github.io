sinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of descending or being submerged in water or another liquid.

Examples

And he knew in that moment, with a cold sinking of despair, a dying of old hopes, that Mae had spread some kind of word there among the neighbors.

Synset Examples

they still talk about the sinking of the Titanic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submersion - the act of being submerged or put under water or another liquid
  • Antonym: floating - the act of remaining on the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From the verb 'sink', from Old English *sincan* (to sink, become lower).
