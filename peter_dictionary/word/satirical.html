satirical

1. ADJ

/ səˈtɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / səˈtɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
Using irony, sarcasm, or ridicule to criticize or mock human foolishness or vice, often in literature or art.

Examples

It required an energy he no longer possessed to be satirical about his father.

Synset Examples

a persistent campaign of mockery by the satirical fortnightly magazine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Synonym: ironic - conveying meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite
  • Antonym: serious - not using irony or ridicule; earnest

Etymology

From satire + -ical; ultimately from Latin 'satira' (mixture, medley).
