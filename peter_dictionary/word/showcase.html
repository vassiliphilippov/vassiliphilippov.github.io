showcase

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌkeɪs / (BrE)
An event, setting, or situation in which something or someone is displayed or presented to impressive advantage.

Examples

By trying to be both a serious survey of a bygone era and a showcase for today's artists, the program turned out to be a not quite perfect example of either.
The career of Eichmann made the Trial a potential showcase for anti-Semitic demoralization:

Synset Examples

it was a showcase for democracy in Africa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhibition - an event at which objects such as paintings are shown to the public
  • Synonym: window - a shop window or display case where goods are shown

Etymology

From 'show' + 'case', originally referring to a glass case for displaying items.
