basketball

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæs.kɪtˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːs.kɪtˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A game played between two teams of five players each on a rectangular court, in which points are scored by throwing a ball through an elevated hoop at each end.

Examples

He played basketball there while working toward a law degree.
Benington recalled that he once told Hartweger that he doubted Gordon would ever play much for him because he seemed to be lacking in all of the accepted basketball skills.
The parallel bars, horse, buck, springboard, horizontal bar, rings, and mats formerly in the school gyms were replaced by baseball, volleyball, basketball and football.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: netball - a similar team game, popular in Commonwealth countries, without dribbling or running with the ball

Etymology

From 'basket' and 'ball', describing the original use of a peach basket as a goal.
