insolent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.sələnt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sələnt / (BrE)
Showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect.

Examples

A tall lady, with a ruffled collar very low on her bosom, turned insolent green eyes upon him.

Synset Examples

a flip answer to serious question
the student was kept in for impudent behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinent - not showing proper respect; rude
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness or deference

Etymology

From Latin insolens (immoderate, arrogant), from in- (not) + solēns (accustomed).
