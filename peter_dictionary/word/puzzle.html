puzzle

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌzəl / (BrE)
to confuse or perplex someone; to make someone feel uncertain or unable to understand

Examples

He seemed puzzled by my question.
Rather than being deceived, the eye is puzzled; instead of seeing objects in space, it sees nothing more than - a picture.
In France he had puzzled the meaning of the great stone monuments men had thrown up to the sky, and always as he wandered, he felt a stranger to their exultation.
Peasants puzzled Andrei.
Her first day at work she was puzzled by an entry in the doctor's notes on an emergency case.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me--I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand
  • Synonym: perplex - to make someone feel completely baffled
  • Antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject

Etymology

From Old French *pusle* (bewilder), of uncertain origin.
