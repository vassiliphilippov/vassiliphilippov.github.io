tee

1. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / ti / (BrE)
the area from which the first stroke of each hole is played on a golf course

Examples

Arnold's iron shot from the tee burrowed into the bunker guarding the green, an embankment that had become soft and spongy from the rains, thereby bringing local rules into force.

Synset Examples

they were waiting on the first tee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tee box - the designated area where the first shot of a hole is taken in golf

Etymology

Possibly from Scottish 'teaz', meaning a small mound; related to the device used for holding a golf ball.
