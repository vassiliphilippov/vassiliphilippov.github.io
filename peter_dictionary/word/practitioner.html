practitioner

1. NOUN

/ prækˈtɪʃənər / (AmE), / prækˈtɪʃənə / (BrE)
A person actively engaged in a profession, especially one requiring special training, such as medicine or law.

Examples

Mr. A., her fellow townsman, also experienced a nervous breakdown just as soon as he discovered that he had been bilked of his life savings by the limited practitioner who had been treating his wife - a woman suffering from an incurable disease, multiple sclerosis - and himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: professional - a person engaged or qualified in a profession

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'practicionarius', from 'practicus' (practical).
