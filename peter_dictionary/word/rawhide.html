rawhide

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɔːˌhaɪd / (BrE)
untanned animal hide, especially from cattle, used in strips for making whips, ropes, or similar items

Examples

A third wrote that it was "raining like poring peas on a rawhide ".

Etymology

From 'raw' + 'hide', referring to untreated animal skin.
