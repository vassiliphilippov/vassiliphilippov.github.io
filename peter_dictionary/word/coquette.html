coquette

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / kɒˈkɛt / (BrE)
A woman who flirts or behaves in a teasing or playful way to gain the attention and admiration of men, often without sincere affection.

Examples

At the same time the multiple transvestitism involved - the fat man as girl and as baby, as coquette pretending to be a baby - touches for a moment horrifyingly upon the secret sources of a life like Jacoby's, upon the sinister dreams which form the sources of any human life.
One is so accustomed to think of men as the privileged who need but ask and receive, and women as submissive and yielding, that our sympathies are usually enlisted on the side of the man whose love is not returned, and we condemn the woman as a coquette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - a person who behaves playfully or amorously to attract attention

Etymology

From French 'coquette', feminine of 'coquet' meaning 'flirt', originally a diminutive of 'coq' (rooster).
