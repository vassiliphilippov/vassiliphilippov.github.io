retaliate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtæliˌeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈtælɪeɪt / (BrE)
to do something harmful or unpleasant to someone because they have done something harmful or unpleasant to you; to take revenge

Examples

And so it was over the weekend what with 40 - year old Warren Spahn pitching his no-hit masterpiece against the Giants and the Giants' Willie Mays retaliating with a record tying 4 - homer spree Sunday.

Synset Examples

He wants to avenge the murder of his brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avenge - to inflict harm in return for (an injury or wrong)
  • Antonym: forgive - to stop feeling anger or resentment towards someone for an offense

Etymology

From Latin retaliatus, past participle of retaliare (to pay back in kind).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtæliˌeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈtælieɪt / (BrE)
to do something harmful to someone because they have harmed you first; to make a counterattack, especially by returning harm for harm

Examples

Well, the odious little toad went along chivying animals and humans who could n't retaliate, and in due course, as was inevitable, overreached himself.

Synset Examples

The Empire strikes back
The Giants struck back and won the opener
The Israeli army retaliated for the Hamas bombing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenge - the act of harming someone in return for harm received
  • Antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or to stop seeking revenge for a wrong

Etymology

From Latin retaliatus, past participle of retaliare (to repay in kind).
