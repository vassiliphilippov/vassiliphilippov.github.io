leapfrog

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːpˌfrɔg / (AmE), / ˈliːpˌfrɒg / (BrE)
A sudden or significant advancement, especially by surpassing obstacles or competitors, as in the children's game of leapfrog.

Examples

It is merely the latest example of the leapfrog growth which formed the pattern of virtually all American cities.
The "leapfrog "was a phenomenon of the railroad and the steam turbine, and the time when the belts of residence surrounding the old factory area were not yet blighted.

Synset Examples

the company still believes the chip is a leapfrog in integration and will pay huge dividends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtake - to catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction
  • Synonym: outstrip - to move faster than and leave behind

Etymology

From the children's game 'leapfrog', in which players jump over each other.
