laundry

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔndɹi / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndɹi / (BrE)
clothes, sheets, and other items that need washing, are being washed, or have been washed

Examples

Argiento had been trained so rigorously by the Jesuits that Michelangelo was unable to change his habits: up before dawn to scrub the floors, whether they were dirty or not; water boiling on the fire for washing laundry every day, the pots scoured with river sand after each meal.
To keep you happy and doing the laundry.

Etymology

From Old French 'lavanderie', from 'laver' (to wash).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔn.dɹi / (AmE), / ˈlɔːn.dɹi / (BrE)
A business or facility where clothes and linens are washed and often ironed.

Examples

Get my stuff down to the laundry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launderette - a self-service laundry facility
  • Synonym: dry cleaner - a shop where clothes are cleaned with chemicals rather than water

Etymology

From French 'laver' (to wash), influenced by Middle English 'lavendrie'.
