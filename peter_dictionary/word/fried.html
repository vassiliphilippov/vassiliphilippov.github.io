fried

1. ADJ

/ fraɪd / (AmE), / fraɪd / (BrE)
(of food) cooked in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan.

Examples

In Keng Kok, the City of Silkworms, the Prime Minister bought fried chickens and fried cicadas, and two notebooks for me.
In Keng Kok, the City of Silkworms, the Prime Minister bought fried chickens and fried cicadas, and two notebooks for me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fried - cooked by frying in fat or oil
  • Antonym: baked - cooked by dry heat in an oven
  • Antonym: grilled - cooked on a grill or over direct heat
  • Antonym: boiled - cooked in boiling water

Etymology

Past participle of 'fry', from Old French *frire*, from Latin *frigere* (to roast, fry).
