reverend

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvərənd / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərənd / (BrE)
A member of the Christian clergy; a minister or priest.

Examples

Isaac Pitt, one of the men from Lincoln, had taken a musket ball in his belly; and though he had found the strength to run with us, now he collapsed and lay on the ground, dying, the Reverend holding his head and wiping his hot brow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergyman - A male member of the clergy; a religious leader in Christian churches.

Etymology

From Latin *reverendus* meaning 'to be revered', from *revereri* (to respect).
