scouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cleaning a surface by vigorously rubbing it, typically with a brush, soap, and water

Examples

Many scouring powders now also contain phosphates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubbing - the act of cleaning something by rubbing it hard, especially with a brush

Etymology

From the verb 'scour,' from Middle English 'scouren,' ultimately of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of moving over an area thoroughly in order to search for something.

Examples

A detailed scouring of the entire area revealed nothing beyond a ledge of rocks that might have been the rifleman's hiding place.

Synset Examples

scouring the entire area revealed nothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - the act of looking for something

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'scour' (to search), which may derive from Middle English 'scouren'.
