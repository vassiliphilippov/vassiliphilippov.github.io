frostbite

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔs(t)ˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstˌbaɪt / (BrE)
Injury to body tissues caused by exposure to extreme cold, typically resulting in numbness, tingling, blisters, and, in severe cases, tissue death.

Examples

Heat's bad for frostbite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilblain - painful inflammation of small blood vessels in the skin resulting from exposure to cold air

Etymology

From frost + bite, referring to cold 'biting' the flesh; first recorded in the late 18th century.
