miscalculate

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to judge, estimate, or calculate something incorrectly

Examples

At Fort Ethan Allen the ever-present wind off Lake Champlain could readily flip a puny man-made thing like an airplane if the pilot miscalculated.

Synset Examples

I had misestimated his determination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misjudge - to judge wrongly or incorrectly
  • Antonym: calculate - to determine mathematically

Etymology

From Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'calculate', from 'calculare' (to reckon, compute).
