repetitive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpɛt̬ətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈpetɪtɪv / (BrE)
involving repeated actions or sounds, especially in a way that becomes monotonous or persistent

Examples

To them he could have been the broken bell in the church tower which rang before and after Mass, and at noon, and at six each evening - its tone, repetitive, monotonous, never breaking the boredom of the streets.

Synset Examples

the bluejay's insistent cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotonous - lacking in variety and interest; tediously repetitive

Etymology

From Latin repetere (to repeat) via repetition + -ive.
