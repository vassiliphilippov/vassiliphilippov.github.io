blues

1. NOUN

/ bluz / (AmE), / bluÀêz / (BrE)
A style of music that originated among African Americans in the early 20th century, characterized by its melancholic tone and frequent use of blue notes.

Examples

Eliminating the patter and the upbeat numbers left little but blues and other songs of equal melancholy.
Johnny Mercer practically grew up with the sound of jazz and the blues in his ears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz - a genre of music with origins in African American communities, often associated with improvisation and syncopation

Etymology

From 'blue devils' (melancholy), later shortened to 'blues' and associated with the musical style.
