hissing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A sharp sibilant sound, especially made as an expression of disapproval, or by escaping steam, gas, or similar sources.

Examples

Woodbury's remarks were applauded by a portion of the audience several times and once there was hissing.
Their onrush and retreat, he reported, was accompanied by a great hissing, roaring and rattling.

Synset Examples

the performers could not be heard over the hissing of the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booing - shouting 'boo' to express disapproval
