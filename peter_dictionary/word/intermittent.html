intermittent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈmɪtənt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪtənt / (BrE)
Occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady.

Examples

Somehow he had forgotten what he must have been told, that combat was an intermittent activity.

Synset Examples

intermittent rain showers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - happening without interruption; unbroken

Etymology

From Latin *intermittere* (to leave off, interrupt), from *inter-* (between) + *mittere* (to send).
