bilharziasis

1. NOUN

/ bɪlˌhɑrˈzaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / bɪlˌhɑːˈzaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A tropical disease caused by infestation with parasitic worms of the genus Schistosoma, resulting in symptoms depending on the area of the body infected.

Examples

Additionally, since you 're going to be hors de combat pretty soon with sprue, yaws, Delhi boil, the Granville wilt, liver fluke, bilharziasis, and a host of other complications of the hex you 've aroused, you must n't expect to be lionized socially.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schistosomiasis - parasitic disease caused by Schistosoma worms

Etymology

From the name of German pathologist Theodor Bilharz, who first described the disease, plus '-iasis' (disease condition).
