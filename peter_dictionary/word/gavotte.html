gavotte

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈvɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈvɒt / (BrE)
a lively French dance in quadruple time, popular in the 17th and 18th centuries, or a piece of music composed for such a dance

Examples

We attended the premieres of his concertos, symphonies, and suites; we studied, taught, and performed his piano sonatas, chamber music, gavottes, and marches; we bought his records and played them in our schools and universities.

Etymology

From French 'gavotte', from Old Provençal 'gavoto', a type of dance from Gap, France.
