vineyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnjɚd / (AmE), / ˈvɪnjəd / (BrE)
A farm or plantation where grapevines are cultivated, especially for producing wine.

Examples

Here, Prokofieff became a workman in the vineyards of Socialism - producing music for the masses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winery - a place where wine is produced, often located at a vineyard

Etymology

From Old French 'vignard', from 'vigne' (vine), from Latin 'vinea' (vineyard).
