reorganize

1. VERB

/ riˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / riːˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or structure something in a new or different way, especially to improve efficiency or adapt to new circumstances

Examples

But it would greatly strengthen any Mayor's executive powers, remove the excuse in large degree that he is a captive of inaction in the Board of Estimate, increase his budget-making authority both as to expense and capital budgets, and vest in him the right to reorganize city departments in the interest of efficiency and economy.
In 1959, the Yacht Safety Bureau was reorganized by the National Association of Engine and Boat Manufacturers and a group of insurance underwriters to provide a testing laboratory and labeling service for boats and their equipment.
The whole system was again reviewed and reorganized in 1933.

Synset Examples

We must reorganize the company if we don't want to go under

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organize - to arrange or structure systematically

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'organize', from French 'organiser' and Medieval Latin 'organizare'.

2. VERB

/ riˈɔrɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / riˈɔːɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or structure something again, especially after disruption or to improve its functioning

Examples

Far up the valley I could see the Rees circling and reorganizing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organize - to arrange systematically; to put in order

Etymology

From re- (again) + organize, from French organiser, ultimately from Latin organum (instrument, tool).
