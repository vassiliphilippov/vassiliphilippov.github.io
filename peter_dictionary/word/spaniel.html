spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæniəl / (AmE), / ˈspæniəl / (BrE)
A dog of various breeds, typically small to medium-sized, with a long silky coat and long, drooping ears, bred originally as a gun dog for flushing game.

Examples

Mr. Skyros was not a man who thought very much about moral principles; he found money much more interesting; but all the same he thought now, uneasily, of the way in which Angelo earned his living - and paid for his own stuff - and eyed the soft smile, and the spaniel like dark eyes, and he felt a little ill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retriever - a type of gun dog bred to retrieve game

Etymology

From Old French 'espaigneul' (Spanish dog), from 'Espagne' (Spain), referring to the breed's supposed Spanish origin.
