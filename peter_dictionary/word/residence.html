residence

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
A place where a person lives, especially one which someone occupies for a significant period.

Examples

In fact it has caused us to give serious thought to moving our residence south, because it is not easy for the most objective Southerner to sit calmly by when his host is telling a roomful of people that the only way to deal with Southerners who oppose integration is to send in troops and shoot the bastards down.
Both men and women of twenty-one years of age could register and vote upon presenting proof of residence and identification.
as new homes were built they were connected so that all residences south of School Street are served by it.
The "leapfrog "was a phenomenon of the railroad and the steam turbine, and the time when the belts of residence surrounding the old factory area were not yet blighted.
Will the house on any part-time farm you are considering make a satisfactory full-time residence?

Synset Examples

a person can have several residences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home - the place where one lives permanently, especially as a member of a family or household
  • Synonym: dwelling - a place of residence or habitation

Etymology

From Latin *residentia*, from *residēre* meaning 'to remain, to stay'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
the official home of an important person, such as a sovereign or president

Examples

The city was a center of manufacture, especially in textiles, and also because of the beauty of some of its surroundings, a residence for many owners of the great industries in north Alabama.
Above all, disabuse yourselves of any thought that I propose to vent moral indignation at your rifling my residence, to whimper over the loss of a few objets d'art, or to shame you into rectitude.

Synset Examples

he refused to live in the governor's residence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Synonym: palace - official residence of a sovereign or high-ranking dignitary

Etymology

From Latin residentia (abiding), from residēre (to remain).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
A large and impressive house, typically used as a home.

Examples

He could no longer build anything, whether a private residence in his Pennsylvania county or a church in Brazil, without it being obvious that he had done it, and while here and there he was taken to task for again developing the same airy technique, they were such fanciful and sometimes even playful buildings that the public felt assured by its sense of recognition after a time, a quality of authentic uniqueness about them, which, once established by an artist as his private vision, is no longer disputable as to its other values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Synonym: manor - a large country house with lands

Etymology

From Latin residentia (act of remaining, dwelling), from residēre (to reside).

Examples
