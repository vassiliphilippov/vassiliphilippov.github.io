dramatic

1. ADJ

/ drəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
characteristic of or resembling drama; exciting, vivid, or striking as though in a play or performance.

Examples

Perhaps the Pirate who will be the unhappiest over the news that Musial probably will sit out most of the series is Bob Friend, who was beaten by The Man twice last season on dramatic home runs.

Yet such is the dramatic power of his writing that the audience is nevertheless left in the grip of the terrible power and potency of that which came over Salem.

Synset Examples

a dramatic entrance in a swirling cape
a dramatic rescue at sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrical - resembling theater or drama; exaggerated or showy
  • Antonym: undramatic - not exciting or theatrical; lacking drama

Etymology

From Late Latin dramaticus, from Greek dramatikos (‘pertaining to drama’).
