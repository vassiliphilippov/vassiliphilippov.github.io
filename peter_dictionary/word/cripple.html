cripple

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpl / (BrE)
To damage or weaken severely, making something or someone unable to function properly or effectively.

Examples

The Hephzibah Menuhin-Amadeus Quartet (Angel) and Victor Babin-Festival Quartet (RCA Victor) editions give us superlative string playing (both in symphonic style) crippled by unimaginative piano playing.
We would be crippled by reduced output, industrial decline, widespread unemployment ".

Synset Examples

This measure crippled our efforts
Their behavior stultified the boss's hard work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disable - to make unable to function
  • Synonym: paralyze - to make unable to move or act
  • Synonym: hamper - to hinder or restrict the progress of
  • Antonym: restore - to bring back to former strength or capability

Etymology

From Old English *crypel*, related to 'creopan' (to creep) and 'crip', referring to being disabled or unable to walk.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpəl / (BrE)
(offensive, archaic) A person who is unable to walk normally due to injury or disability affecting the legs or back.

Examples

The only one who would have him was his cripple, the strange unhappy woman who became his wife.

Etymology

Old English *cryppel*, related to 'creep', meaning one who limps or is disabled in the limbs.
