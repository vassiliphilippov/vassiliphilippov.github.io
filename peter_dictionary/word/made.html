made

1. ADJ

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
(of goods or objects) produced by a manufacturing process rather than by hand or occurring naturally.

Examples

(1) When an object was placed in the patient's hand, he had no difficulty determining whether it was warm or cold, sharp or blunt, rough or smooth, flexible, soft, or hard; and he could tell, simply by the feel of it, whether it was made of wood, iron, cloth, rubber, and so on.
They were carpeted, but made for pumps and congress gaiters, not the great clodhoppers he wore.
My camp made leather wallet, bulky with twisted, raised stitches around the edges, I stuffed with money I had been saving.
But, that aside... ...Iet me say that I swear... ...on the souls of my grandchildren, that I will not be the one to break the peace we've made here today.

Synset Examples

bought some made goods at the local store; rope and nails

Related Terms

  • Antonym: handmade - made by hand rather than by a machine
  • Synonym: manufactured - produced in large numbers in a factory

2. ADJ

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
(of a bed) arranged with sheets and blankets set neatly in order.

Examples

"This is moving day ", Winston reminded her, "and I bet you left things every which way upstairs, your clothes all over the floor and the bed not made.

Synset Examples

a neatly made bed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmade - (of a bed) not arranged, with sheets and blankets left disorderly
