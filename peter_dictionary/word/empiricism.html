empiricism

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪrɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪərɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
(in philosophy) the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience, as opposed to relying solely on reason or intuition.

Examples

Neither is primary experience understood according to the attitude of modern empiricism in which nothing is thought to be received other than signals of sensory qualities producing their responses in the appropriate sense organs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rationalism - the doctrine that reason is the chief source of knowledge

Etymology

From French *empirisme*, from Greek *empeiria* (experience).
