hangover

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌəʊvə / (BrE)
An unpleasant physical condition, such as headache or nausea, experienced after consuming too much alcohol.

Examples

We both had hangovers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intoxication - state of being affected by alcohol or drugs

Etymology

From the phrase 'hang over', originally meaning a thing remaining from earlier; first used in this sense in the early 20th century.
