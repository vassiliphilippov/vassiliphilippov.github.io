counteroffensive

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərəˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a large-scale military attack launched by a defending force to regain the initiative from an attacking enemy

Examples

In a book review of "The Soviet Cultural Offensive ", he says, "Long before the State Department organized its bureaucracy into an East-West Contacts Staff in order to wage a cultural counter-offensive within Soviet borders, the sharp cutting-edge of American culture had carved its mark across the Russian steppes, as when the enterprising promoters of' Porgy and Bess' overrode the State Department to carry the contemporary' cultural warfare' behind the enemy lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offensive - a military campaign of attack
  • Degree Lower: counterattack - a smaller, immediate attack in response to an assault
  • Synonym: onslaught - a strong or destructive attack

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'offensive' (military attack), first used in early 20th century military contexts.
