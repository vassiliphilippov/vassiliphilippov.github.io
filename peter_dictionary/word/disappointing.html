disappointing

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpɔɪn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpɔɪn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Not as good as expected; failing to meet hopes or expectations.

Examples

Ask any group of ballerinas to do ten push-ups or three chin-ups and the results, considering the amount of physical training they have had, will be very disappointing.

Synset Examples

a disappointing performance from one who had seemed so promising

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfying - giving pleasure or fulfillment by meeting expectations
  • Synonym: frustrating - causing annoyance because of unfulfilled expectations

Etymology

From the verb 'disappoint', which comes from Middle French 'desappointer' (to deprive of an appointment).
