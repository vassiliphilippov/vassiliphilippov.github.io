half-heartedly

1. ADV

/ ˌhæfˈhɑrtɪdli / (AmE), / ˌhɑːfˈhɑːtɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows little enthusiasm or effort.

Examples

The small half-heartedly tended fields of men who 'd spent more time rustling cattle than farming were lying fallow.

Synset Examples

she tried half-heartedly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enthusiastically - with great energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From 'half-hearted' (lacking enthusiasm) + the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
