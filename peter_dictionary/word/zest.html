zest

1. NOUN

/ zɛst / (AmE), / zɛst / (BrE)
great enthusiasm and excitement; vigorous and enthusiastic enjoyment

Examples

Hercule Poirot and Lord Peter Whimsey (the respective creations of Agatha Christie and Dorothy Sayers) have retained Holmes' egotism but not his zest for life and eccentric habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gusto - vigorous and enthusiastic enjoyment
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From French *zeste* (orange or lemon peel, figuratively 'something that adds flavor').
