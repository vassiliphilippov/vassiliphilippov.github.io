quotation

1. NOUN

/ kwoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kwəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A note or reference acknowledging the source of information or a quoted passage.

Examples

After she had served the detectives coffee and toast (they politely declined eggs, uncomfortable about their tenancy), she settled down with a morning newspaper and began reading the stock market quotations.

Synset Examples

the student's essay failed to list several important citations
the acknowledgments are usually printed at the front of a book
the article includes mention of similar clinical cases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citation - a reference to a published or unpublished source

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'quotationem', from Latin 'quotare' (to mark the number of, to cite).
