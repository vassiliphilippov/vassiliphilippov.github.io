wasteland

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪsˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstˌlænd / (BrE)
An area of land that is barren, uninhabited, and considered useless for cultivation or human habitation.

Examples

The name of it is Gore Court, and it is surrounded by a wasteland that would impress T. S. Eliot.
She studied him hopefully, yearningly; against the limitless background of sky and wasteland it was easy to confirm her analysis.

Synset Examples

the barrens of central Africa
the trackless wastes of the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert - A dry, barren area of land, often covered with sand and having little or no vegetation.
  • Synonym: wilderness - An area of land where there are no people living; an uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable region.
  • Antonym: fertile land - Land capable of producing abundant vegetation or crops.

Etymology

From 'waste' (barren, desolate) + 'land', attested since the 13th century.
