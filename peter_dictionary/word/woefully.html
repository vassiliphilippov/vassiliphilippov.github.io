woefully

1. ADV

/ ˈwoʊ.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈwəʊ.fʊ.li / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely bad, inadequate, or unfortunate; in a deplorable manner.

Examples

No one will deny that such broad developments and transitions are of great intrinsic interest and the study of ideas in literature would be woefully incomplete without frequent reference to them.

Synset Examples

he was sadly neglected
it was woefully inadequate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadly - in a way that is unfortunate or regrettable
  • Synonym: miserably - in an extremely unhappy or unfortunate manner

Etymology

From 'woeful' (full of woe) + '-ly' (adverb suffix).
