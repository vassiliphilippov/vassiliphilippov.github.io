banquet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkwət / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkwɪt / (BrE)
A formal meal for many people, often to celebrate a particular event or as a public or ceremonial occasion.

Examples

To forestall any change of allegiance, the Democrats hastily organised a testimonial banquet for O' Banion, as public reward for his past services and as a reminder of where his loyalties lay.
When Mayor Dever heard of the banquet he summoned Hughes for an explanation of why he had been dishonouring the police department by consorting with these felons and fixers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feast - a large, elaborately prepared meal, often for many people; elaborate celebration
  • Synonym: reception - a formal social occasion held to welcome someone or to celebrate an event
  • Antonym: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals or instead of a meal

Etymology

From Old French 'banquet', diminutive of 'banque' (bench, long table), from Italian 'banchetto'.
