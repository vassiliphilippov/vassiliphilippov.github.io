primeval

1. ADJ

/ praɪˈmiːvəl / (AmE), / prɪˈmiːvəl / (BrE)
(especially of the world or a natural environment) belonging to the earliest ages; ancient and unchanged since the earliest times.

Examples

The first turn of the spiral is the primeval history of humanity in Adam.
It is difficult to reconstruct the primeval fears of man.
There was the smell of the coast, like a primeval memory, composed of equal parts salt water, clams, seaweed and northern air.

Synset Examples

aboriginal forests
primal eras before the appearance of life on earth
the forest primeval
primordial matter
primordial forms of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primitive - relating to an early stage of development; simple or basic
  • Synonym: primordial - existing at or from the very beginning; primeval

Etymology

From Latin 'primaevus', from 'primus' (first) + 'aevum' (age, lifetime).
