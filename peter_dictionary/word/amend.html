amend

1. VERB

/ əˈmɛnd / (AmE), / əˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to make formal changes or improvements to a law, document, statement, etc., often in order to correct or update it

Examples

The third amended the enabling act for creation of the Lamar county Hospital District, for which a special constitutional amendment previously was adopted.
Section 8 (b) (2) of the Small Business Act, as amended, authorizes the SBA to make a complete inventory of the productive facilities of small business concerns.

Synset Examples

amend the document

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modify - to make partial changes to something
  • Synonym: correct - to make something free from errors
  • Antonym: repeal - to revoke or annul a law or act officially

Etymology

From Old French 'amender', from Latin 'emendare' (to correct, to free from fault).
