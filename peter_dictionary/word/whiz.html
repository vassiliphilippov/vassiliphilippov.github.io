whiz

1. NOUN

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
A person who is extremely skilled or talented in a particular area or field.

Examples

The rangy, Albany (Cal.) native, a surprise slugging sensation for the Flock last year as well as a defensive whiz, set "improved fielding "as his 1961 goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace - a person who excels at a particular sport or activity
  • Synonym: expert - someone with a high level of knowledge or skill in a field
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

2. VERB

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
(of an object) to move very quickly, making a soft, continuous, swishing sound.

Examples

Sometimes soldiers wrote letters while bullets were whizzing about their heads.
Autos whizzed past.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - move quickly with a rushing or swirling sound

Etymology

Imitative in origin, echoing the sound made by something moving swiftly through the air.
