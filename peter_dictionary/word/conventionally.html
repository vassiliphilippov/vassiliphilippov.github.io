conventionally

1. ADV

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəli / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəli / (BrE)
In a conventional manner; following what is generally done or accepted by custom or tradition.

Examples

In the upper center of Braque's first collage, Fruit Dish (in Douglas Cooper's collection), a bunch of grapes is rendered with such conventionally vivid sculptural effect as to lift it practically off the picture plane.

Synset Examples

he usually behaves rather conventionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconventionally - in an unconventional manner

Etymology

From 'conventional' + '-ly'. 'Conventional' from Latin 'conventionem' (assembly, agreement).
