outskirt

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌskɝt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌskɜːt / (BrE)
the outer part of a city or town, far from its central areas

Examples

Even the loss of hotel business to the outskirt's motel has been relatively painless; the hotel-motel demarcation is becoming harder to find every year.

Synset Examples

they built a factory on the outskirts of the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suburb - an outlying district of a city, especially a residential one

Etymology

From 'out' + 'skirt', in the sense of the border or edge.
