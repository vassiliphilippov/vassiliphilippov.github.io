explain

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspleɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈspleɪn / (BrE)
to make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving information about it

Examples

"If you kick too much, your leg gets kinda dead ", he explained.
"He's looking a lot better, and he's able to run ", Meek explained.
"We always like to keep the ball as much as we can against Denver because they have such an explosive attack ", explained Stram.

Synset Examples

He explained the laws of physics to his students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarify - make less confused and more comprehensible
  • Antonym: obscure - make unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'explanare' (to make level, make clear), from 'ex-' (out) + 'planus' (flat, level, clear).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspleɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈspleɪn / (BrE)

Synset Examples

Your need to sleep late does not excuse your late arrival at work
Her recent divorce may explain her reluctance to date again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justify - to provide good reasons for something that others disapprove of
  • Synonym: excuse - to provide a reason for something to lessen blame

Etymology

From Latin explicare (to unfold, to explain).
