adequacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.ɪ.kwə.si / (AmE), / ˈæd.ɪ.kwə.si / (BrE)
the quality of being sufficient or satisfactory for a particular purpose

Examples

I fingered it and had the feeling of adequacy that comes with the right texture, tilth and body.

Synset Examples

he questioned the sufficiency of human intelligence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insufficiency - the quality of not being sufficient or adequate
  • Synonym: sufficiency - the quality or state of being adequate or enough

Etymology

From Latin adaequatus (made equal or comparable), from adaequare (to make equal) + -cy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ˈædɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of being sufficient to satisfy a requirement or need

Examples

Presumably a cocktail party is expected to fulfill the host's desire to get together a number of people who are inadequately acquainted and thereby arrange for bringing the level of acquaintance up to adequacy for future cooperative endeavors.
the magnitude of effort and costs of each of these possible phases of an effective safety program for metal and nonmetallic mines (excluding coal and lignite mines); and the scope and adequacy of State mine safety laws applicable to such mines and the enforcement of such laws.

Synset Examples

he questioned the adequacy of the usual sentimental interpretation of the Golden Rule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficiency - the state of being sufficient
  • Antonym: inadequacy - the state of being insufficient or not enough

Etymology

From Latin adequatus (made equal, proportioned), from ad- (to) + aequare (to make equal).
