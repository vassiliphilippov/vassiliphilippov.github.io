scant

1. ADJ

/ skænt / (AmE), / skænt / (BrE)
Barely sufficient in amount; less than the correct, required, or full amount, often deliberately so.

Examples

The scant flesh grew cool beneath her frantic hands.
A scant half mile away Shelley and Mary were doubtless sitting on their diminutive terrace, the air about them scented with stock, and listening to the nightingale who had nested in the big lime tree at the foot of the garden.

Synset Examples

a light pound
a scant cup of sugar
regularly gives short weight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarce - in short supply; not plentiful
  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: ample - more than enough; plentiful
  • Antonym: abundant - present in great quantity

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skamt' (short, scanty).
