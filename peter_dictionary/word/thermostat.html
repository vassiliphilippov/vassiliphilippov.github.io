thermostat

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜr.moʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.mə.stæt / (BrE)
A device that automatically regulates temperature by activating or deactivating a heating or cooling system.

Examples

The light was filtered by the soft glass window of the thermostat thus ensuring that only light absorbed by the chlorine and not by the carbon tetrachloride could enter the reaction cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulator - device that controls a system or process

Etymology

From Greek 'thermo-' (heat) + 'stat' (stationary, standing).
