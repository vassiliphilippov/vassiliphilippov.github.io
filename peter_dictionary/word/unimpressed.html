unimpressed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪmˈprɛst / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪmˈprɛst / (BrE)
not feeling admiration, respect, or excitement about someone or something

Examples

Jean Jacques asked, striving to appear unimpressed.
He behaved publicly with a cocky, swaggering truculence that offended their vulpine Latin minds, and behaved towards them personally with an unimpressed insolence that enraged them beneath their blandness.

Synset Examples

trying to appear unimpressed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impressed - feeling admiration or respect for someone or something

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'impressed' (influenced or affected); based on Latin roots 'in-' (into) and 'premere' (to press).
