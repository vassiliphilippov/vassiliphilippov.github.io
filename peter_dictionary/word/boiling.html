boiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of applying heat to a liquid so that it changes into a gas; the act of heating something until it boils

Examples

The brushes can be cleaned and sterilized by boiling and are detachable so that every member of the family can have his own.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: evaporation - the process of turning from liquid into vapor without boiling
  • Degree Lower: simmering - cooking a liquid just below its boiling point

Etymology

From the verb 'boil', from Old French *boillir*, from Latin *bullīre* (to bubble, to boil).
