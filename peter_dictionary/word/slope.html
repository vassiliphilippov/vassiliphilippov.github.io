slope

1. NOUN

/ sloʊp / (AmE), / sləʊp / (BrE)
the side or face of a hill, mountain, or other elevated piece of land that rises at an angle

Examples

Check the elevation of the ground, degree and direction of slopes, drainage, rock outcrops, topsoil types and quality, as well as subsoil.
The side slopes were coated with fiberglas matting coated with asphalt to prevent erosion.
On the forward slope in front of his own post stretched two rows of barbed wire.
At the slope's base coils of concertina stretched out of eye range like a wild tangle of children's hoops, stopped simultaneously, weirdly poised as if awaiting the magic of the child's touch to start them all rolling again.
They crept down the muddy slope toward the waiting boats.

Synset Examples

he climbed the steep slope
the house was built on the side of a mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incline - a surface that has a slant or slope
  • Antonym: decline - a downward slope

Etymology

From Middle English, probably related to Old Norse *slupra* ('to slip'), possibly influenced by the verb 'slip'.

2. NOUN

/ sloʊp / (AmE), / sləʊp / (BrE)
The degree to which a line or surface inclines from the horizontal; the rate of change in position or value, usually expressed as a ratio or angle.

Examples

Synset Examples

a five-degree gradient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incline - a slope or slanted surface
  • Synonym: gradient - the degree of a slope; rate of ascent or descent
  • Synonym: steepness - the quality of being steep; degree of slope
  • Antonym: flatness - the state of being level or without slope

Etymology

From Middle English 'sloope', probably a variant of 'slip' (to slide) or influenced by Scandinavian languages.
