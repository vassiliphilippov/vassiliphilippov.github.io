nihilism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.ɪˌlɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈnɪ.ɪˌlɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that advocates the destruction of existing social or political institutions, often for its own sake, without regard for replacing them with a new system.

Examples

But to me Beckett's writing had seemed permeated with love for human beings and with a kind of humor that I could reconcile neither with despair nor with nihilism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchism - political philosophy advocating abolition of government and organization of society on voluntary, cooperative basis
  • Antonym: constructivism - theory or practice focused on building up social or political systems

Etymology

From Latin nihil (nothing) + -ism, coined in the 19th century.
