deduction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdəkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
an amount or percentage subtracted from a total, especially from income or a bill

Examples

The Secretary of the Treasury is authorized and directed out of the sums covered into any of the funds pursuant to subsection (b) of this section, and after making the deduction provided for in section 7 (b) of this Title -

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - amount of something permitted or granted, especially from a total
  • Antonym: addition - the process of adding or the amount added to a total

Etymology

From Latin 'deductio', from 'deducere' meaning 'to lead away or subtract'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
An amount that is subtracted from your total income, especially for tax purposes, to arrive at the amount on which tax is calculated.
