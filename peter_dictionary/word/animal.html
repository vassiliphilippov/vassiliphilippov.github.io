animal

1. ADJ

/ ˈænəməl / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl / (BrE)
(of feelings or desires) relating to physical appetites or passions, especially those of the body rather than the mind; carnal or sensual.

Examples

There are some passages in the writings of Irenaeus where the image of God and the similitude are sharply distinguished, so most notably in the statement: "If the [ Holy ] Spirit is absent from the soul, such a man is indeed of an animal nature; and, being left carnal, he will be an imperfect being, possessing the image [ of God ] in his formation, but not receiving the similitude [ of God ] through the Spirit ".

Synset Examples

animal instincts
carnal knowledge
fleshly desire
a sensual delight in eating
music is the only sensual pleasure without vice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnal - relating to physical, especially sexual, needs and activities
  • Synonym: sensual - relating to gratification of the senses or physical pleasure

Etymology

From Latin animalis (living being).

2. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməl / (AmE), / ˈænɪməl / (BrE)
A living creature that is not a plant, often characterized by voluntary movement and responding quickly to stimuli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creature - a living being, especially an animal

Etymology

From Latin animal (living being), from anima (breath, soul).
