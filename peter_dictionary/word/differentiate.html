differentiate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪf.əˈrɛn.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪf.əˈrɛn.ʃieɪt / (BrE)
to recognize or express the difference between things; to make or show a distinction

Examples

What is noteworthy about this large group of teen-agers is that, although their attitudes hardly differentiate them from their gentile counterparts, they actually lead their lives in a vast self enclosed Jewish cosmos with relatively little contact with the non Jewish world.
Let us differentiate a few of these ideas.
There is much research evidence to validate the use of the instrument in differentiating individuals who are likely to manifest anxiety in varying degrees.
In my experience, a great many of the patient's more puzzling verbal communications are so for the reason that concrete meanings have not become differentiated from figurative meanings in his subjective experience.

Synset Examples

We distinguish several kinds of maple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinguish - to recognize or point out the differences between things
  • Antonym: confuse - to mistake one thing for another, to fail to recognize differences

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'differentiare' (to make different), from Latin 'differentia' (difference).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪt / (BrE)
to serve as a distinctive feature or trait that marks someone or something as different; to distinguish by a specific characteristic

Examples

Only Co differentiated between the two groups at less than the 5 % level (* * f).

Synset Examples

His modesty distinguishes him from his peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinguish - to recognize or show the difference between; to make different or distinct

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *differentiare*, from Latin *differentia* (difference).
