alarmingly

1. ADV

/ əˈlɑrmɪŋli / (AmE), / əˈlɑːmɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes fear, concern, or worry; in a manner likely to startle or disturb.

Examples

Eugene was not entirely silent, or openly rude - unless asking Harold to move to another chair and placing himself in the fauteuil that creaked so alarmingly was an act of rudeness.

Synset Examples

It grew alarmingly fast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbingly - in a way that causes worry or unease
  • Antonym: reassuringly - in a way that removes concern or fear

Etymology

From 'alarming' + '-ly', where 'alarming' derives from 'alarm' (from Italian 'all'arme' meaning 'to arms').
