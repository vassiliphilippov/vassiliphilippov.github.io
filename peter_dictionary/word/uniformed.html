uniformed

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuː.nəˌfɔrmd / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪˌfɔːmd / (BrE)
Wearing an official uniform, often as part of one's job or role (such as a police officer, soldier, or attendant).

Examples

In the railway station at Berlin, a uniformed attendant was chanting,' Foreigners this way!
At the top a uniformed officer blocked further progress.
Two uniformed officers, a couple of plain-clothesmen I knew, and two other men stood on a gray cement area next to the pool on my left.

Synset Examples

uniformed policemen lined the President's route

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plainclothes - not wearing an official uniform, especially police in ordinary clothes
