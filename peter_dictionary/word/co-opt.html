co-opt

1. VERB

/ koʊˈɑpt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpt / (BrE)
to choose or elect someone as a member of a group, committee, or organization, often to fill a vacancy or gain support

Examples

The task of a congregation would be defined, according to economic integration, as the work of co-opting individuals and families of similar social and economic position to replenish the nuclear core of the congregation.

Synset Examples

The church members co-opted individuals from similar backgrounds to replenish the congregation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appoint - to assign a job or role
  • Synonym: elect - to choose someone for a position by voting

Etymology

From Latin 'cooptare', meaning to choose together, from 'co-' (together) + 'optare' (to choose).
