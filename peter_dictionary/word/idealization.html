idealization

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of regarding or representing something as perfect or better than in reality.

Examples

The thoroughgoing idealization of the planter society did not come, however, until after the Civil War when Southern writers were eager to defend a way of life which had been destroyed.

Synset Examples

the idealization of rural life was very misleading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticization - the act of making something seem more appealing or ideal than it really is
  • Antonym: realism - the practice of accepting a situation as it is and being prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From Medieval Latin idealis (ideal) + -ization (forming nouns indicating process or result).
