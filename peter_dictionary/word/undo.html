undo

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈduː / (BrE)
to cancel, annul, or reverse an action or its effect

Examples

The bodice beneath was buttoned and, withdrawing his lips from hers, he set her upright on his knee and started to undo it, unhurriedly as if she were a child.
Emerging from the two centuries of colonial domination, the Afro-Asian world is aflame with a nationalism that has undone empires.
There was a divine justice in one wrong thus undoing another.

Synset Examples

I wish I could undo my actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to cause something to go in the opposite direction or to be undone
  • Synonym: annul - to declare invalid, to cancel officially
  • Antonym: redo - to do again after undoing or canceling something

Etymology

From Old English 'undōn' (to unfasten, open), from 'un-' (reversal) + 'dōn' (do).

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈduː / (BrE)
To reverse or remove the effect of; to make as if not done; to unfasten or unmake.

Examples

Carefully, he undid the bow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: do - to perform an action
  • Antonym: fasten - to close or secure something

Etymology

Old English 'undōn', from 'un-' (reversal) + 'dōn' (do).
