gum

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
the firm, pink tissue in the mouth that covers the jaws and surrounds the bases of the teeth

Examples

The bristles are soft enough to massage the gums and not scratch the enamel.

Etymology

From Middle English *gomme*, from Old French *gomme*, from Latin *gummi* (gum, resin). Present sense extended to oral tissue in English by analogy with plant gums.

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
A sweetened, flavored substance, often made from chicle or synthetic materials, that is chewed for enjoyment but not swallowed.

Examples

The safe at Ingleside District Station stands next to the gum machine in a narrow passageway that leads to Captain Harris's office (to the left), the lieutenant's office (farther along and to the left) and the janitor's supply closet (straight ahead).
Chewing gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chewing gum - a sweetened preparation for chewing; not meant to be swallowed

Etymology

From Old English 'guma', from Latin 'gummi', from Greek 'kommi', originally referring to plant resin.
