concessionaire

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɛʃəˈnɛr / (AmE), / kənˌsɛʃəˈneə / (BrE)
A person or company that operates a business on property belonging to another, typically under a concession or license.

Examples

The jury did not elaborate, but it added that "there should be periodic surveillance of the pricing practices of the concessionaires for the purpose of keeping the prices reasonable ".
If a concessionaire runs the cafeteria, keep an eye out for quality and price.
If the soup tastes like dishwater, your employees won n't blame the concessionaire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licensee - a person or company given official permission to do something, especially to operate a business

Etymology

From French 'concessionnaire', from 'concession' (grant of rights or privileges).
