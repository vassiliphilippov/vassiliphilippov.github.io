tame

1. ADJ

/ teɪm / (AmE), / teɪm / (BrE)
Lacking excitement or interest; dull or uninspiring.

Examples

The casual seduction of a seventeen year old bluestocking seemed tame by comparison.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Synonym: dull - not interesting; lacking excitement

2. VERB

/ teɪm / (AmE), / teɪm / (BrE)
to make something less wild or difficult to control, especially by discipline or training

Examples

It is therefore not surprising that they resist the lure of marriage and the trap of domesticity, for like cats they are determined not to tame their sexual energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • Synonym: discipline - to train or control by instruction and punishment
  • Antonym: enrage - to make very angry, the opposite of making calm or docile

Etymology

From Old English 'tamian', from 'tam' (tame, gentle), of Germanic origin.
