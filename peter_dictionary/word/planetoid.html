planetoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.ɪ.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæn.ɪ.tɔɪd / (BrE)
a small celestial body that orbits the sun, especially one of the many found between Mars and Jupiter; a minor planet or asteroid

Examples

There is still the remote possibility of planetoid collision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asteroid - a small rocky body orbiting the sun, mostly found between Mars and Jupiter
  • Degree Higher: planet - a celestial body moving in orbit around a star, large enough to be rounded by its gravity

Etymology

From Greek 'planētēs' (wanderer) + '-oid' (like, resembling).
