magazine

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡəˈzin / (AmE), / ˌmæɡəˈziːn / (BrE)
a periodical publication containing articles, stories, photographs, and advertisements, usually on a particular topic or for a particular group of readers

Examples

Ritchie walked up to him at the magazine stand.
If he can't play with Mommy's magazines, he should have some old numbers of his own.
Friends, a picture magazine distributed by Chevrolet dealers, describes a paramilitary organization of employees of the Gulf Telephone Company at Foley, Alabama.
Pat had been worried as hell ever since she 'd lost her job on that fashion magazine.
"It was only the other day that I saw something of yours, about something or other, in some magazine ".

Synset Examples

it takes several years before a magazine starts to break even or make money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a magazine or newspaper that deals with a particular subject or professional activity
  • Degree Lower: newspaper - a printed publication, usually issued daily or weekly, containing news, articles, and advertisements

Etymology

From French 'magazine', meaning 'storehouse', originally from Italian 'magazzino', from Arabic 'makhzan' (storehouse).

2. NOUN

/ məˈɡæz.iːn / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.əˈziːn / (BrE)
A periodical publication containing articles, photographs, and advertisements, usually printed on glossy paper and issued at regular intervals.

Examples

If there is time after the warning, the basement shielding could be improved substantially by blocking windows with bricks, dirt, books, magazines, or other heavy material.
He peered in the boxes themselves; all were empty except one, and that one was jammed with letters and magazines.

Synset Examples

tripped over a pile of magazines

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: journal - a periodical publication with scholarly articles
  • Degree Lower: newspaper - a daily or weekly publication reporting news and events
  • Synonym: periodical - a publication issued at regular intervals

Etymology

From French 'magasin' meaning 'storehouse', from Italian 'magazzino', ultimately from Arabic 'makhzan' (storehouse).
