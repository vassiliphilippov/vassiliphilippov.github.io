erosion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊʒən / (BrE)
The gradual wearing away of soil, rock, or land, especially by the action of water, wind, or ice.

Examples

The side slopes were coated with fiberglas matting coated with asphalt to prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathering - the breaking down of rocks, soils, and minerals in place by environmental factors
  • Antonym: deposition - the geological process in which sediments, soil, and rocks are added to a landform

Etymology

From Latin 'erosio', from 'erodere' meaning 'to gnaw away'.
