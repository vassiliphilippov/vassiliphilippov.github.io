officially

1. ADV

/ əˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a formal or authorized way; as recognized or declared by an authority or public body

Examples

In America also all of our major religious bodies officially sanction a universalistic ethic which is reflective of our common religion.
First thing I did after my twenty-first birthday was go into court and have it officially changed, and this is something I do n't tell everybody.

Synset Examples

officially, he is in charge
officially responsible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unofficially - in a not formal or authorized way

Etymology

From 'official' + '-ly'; 'official' from Latin 'officialis' (of or belonging to duty or service).

2. ADV

/ əˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that is authorized, approved, or recognized by a person or body in authority.

Examples

Cows receiving drug may not be officially tested under breed registry testing programs.

Synset Examples

the club will be formally recognized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formally - in a manner following established conventions or official requirements

Etymology

From 'official', from Latin 'officialis' (attendant to a magistrate), + '-ly'.
