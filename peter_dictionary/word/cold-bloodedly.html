cold-bloodedly

1. ADV

/ ˌkoʊldˈblʌdɪdli / (AmE), / ˌkəʊldˈblʌdɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows no emotion or pity; in a deliberately cruel or ruthless manner.

Examples

Philip Spencer had cold-bloodedly planned the murder of his captain, yet it seemed in order to chide him for a lapse of proper address.

Synset Examples

he cold-bloodedly planned the murder of his boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthlessly - without pity or compassion; mercilessly
  • Synonym: cruelly - in a way that causes pain or suffering
  • Antonym: mercifully - in a compassionate or forgiving manner

Etymology

Formed from 'cold-blooded' + '-ly', with 'cold-blooded' originating in the late 17th century, metaphorically referring to lack of emotion.
