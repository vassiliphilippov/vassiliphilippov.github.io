subtlety

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌt̬.əl.ti / (AmE), / ˈsʌt.əl.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being difficult to detect, understand, or analyze; delicacy or nuance in thought, perception, or expression.

Examples

And the subtleties of the dialogue are most helpfully conveyed.

Synset Examples

you had to admire the subtlety of the distinctions he drew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle difference in or shade of meaning, expression, or sound

Etymology

From Old French 'subtilite', from Latin 'subtilitas', from 'subtilis' (fine, delicate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌt̬lti / (AmE), / ˈsʌtlti / (BrE)
a small or delicate difference in meaning, opinion, or attitude; a fine or nuanced distinction

Examples

These are, if the research is done with subtlety and skill, researchable topics, but the research is missing.

Synset Examples

without understanding the finer nuances you can't enjoy the humor
don't argue about shades of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle or slight difference in meaning, feeling, or tone

Etymology

From Old French 'subtilite', from Latin 'subtilitas' (fineness, delicacy).
