macroscopically

1. ADV

/ ˌmæ.krəˈskɑ.pɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəˈskɒ.pɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that does not require the use of a microscope; on a scale visible to the naked eye.

Examples

the tubes were then centrifuged at 1000 rpm for 1 min and examined macroscopically for agglutination.

Synset Examples

The tubes were examined macroscopically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microscopically - using or as seen through a microscope

Etymology

Formed from 'macroscopic' + '-ally'; 'macroscopic' from Greek makros (large) + skopein (to look).
