compliance

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪəns / (BrE)
The act of conforming to rules, standards, or laws.

Examples

Provided, That if the total award is not over $ 500 and there is no qualified executor or administrator, payment may be made to the person or persons found by the Comptroller General of the United States to be entitled thereto, without the necessity of compliance with the requirements of law with respect to the administration of estates;

Synset Examples

their financial statements are in conformity with generally accepted accounting practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conformity - compliance with accepted standards, rules, or norms
  • Antonym: noncompliance - failure or refusal to comply with rules or standards

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compliantia', from Latin 'complere' (to fill up, fulfill).
