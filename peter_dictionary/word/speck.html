speck

1. NOUN

/ spɛk / (AmE), / spɛk / (BrE)
A very small spot, mark, or particle of a substance.

Examples

He noticed a speck of dirt on the sill and swiped at it with his finger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture from the surrounding area
  • Synonym: fleck - a small patch or spot of color, or a tiny piece
  • Degree Higher: stain - a mark or spot that is difficult to remove

Etymology

From Middle English spekke, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch spek (bacon, spot), German Speck (bacon).

2. NOUN

/ spɛk / (AmE), / spɛk / (BrE)
A very small amount of something; a tiny quantity.

Examples

Looks like we might be in for a speck of trouble ".

Synset Examples

this dish could use a touch of garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount
  • Synonym: touch - a slight but appreciable amount
  • Synonym: smidgen - a small amount or bit
  • Synonym: bit - a small amount

Etymology

From Middle English specke, from Old English specca (spot, speck).
