cloudy

1. ADJ

/ ˈklaʊdi / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdi / (BrE)
(of an idea, situation, or statement) vague, unclear, or lacking definite form or limits.

Examples

The remote, cloudy, possible has values of its own - values of scope, stimulus, potential, and imagination.

Synset Examples

gropes among cloudy issues toward a feeble conclusion
nebulous distinction between pride and conceit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nebulous - (of an idea, statement, etc.) not clear or definite; vague
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Old English 'cludig' (cloudy, clouded), from 'clud' (cloud or mass of rock).
