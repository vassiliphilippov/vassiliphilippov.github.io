hitch

1. NOUN

/ hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a period of time spent in military service

Examples

Pitcher Steve Barber joined the club one week ago after completing his hitch under the Army's accelerated wintertime military course, also at Fort Knox, Ky..

Etymology

Originally meaning 'a temporary interruption or difficulty'; use for a military term possibly from the idea of a 'stint' or 'turn' at a task.

2. VERB

/ hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
to fasten or attach (an animal or object) to something else with a hook, rope, or similar device

Examples

After pushing an accumulation of vines and weeds from the moldboard, I lifted the lines from the dust and found hitched to that plow the finest team I ever held a rein on.
She was then trained on the trot until December 29, hitched to a breaking cart once around the half-mile track and hoppled again.

Synset Examples

One foot caught in the stirrup

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unhitch - to release from attachment; to unfasten
  • Synonym: attach - to join or fasten (something) to another thing
  • Synonym: fasten - to close or secure something using a device or means

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps related to Middle English hytchen (to move jerkily), possibly of Scandinavian origin.

3. NOUN

/ hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
an unexpected difficulty or obstacle that temporarily prevents progress

Examples

Despite a few hitches, the boys came through in fine style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
  • Synonym: snag - a minor problem or difficulty
  • Synonym: difficulty - a state of having trouble or problems
