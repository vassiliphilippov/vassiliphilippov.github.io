usurp

1. VERB

/ juːˈsɜrp / (AmE), / juːˈzɜːp / (BrE)
to take a position of power or control unlawfully or by force, especially without the right to do so

Examples

Mostly the scene was crowded with mourners, such as the dramatic Dell'Arca Lamentation in Bologna, where the grief-stricken spectators had usurped Mary's last poignant moment.
Though Walter Ulbricht, by grace of Soviet tanks, may be head man in East Germany, that does not give him any right to usurp the government of East Berlin or to absorb that semi-city into the Soviet zone.

Synset Examples

He assumed to himself the right to fill all positions in the town
he usurped my rights
She seized control of the throne after her husband died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
  • Synonym: assume - to take on a role or responsibility, sometimes without permission
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up possession or control

Etymology

From Latin *usurpare* (to take possession of, to use).
