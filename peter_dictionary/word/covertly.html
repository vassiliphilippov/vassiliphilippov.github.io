covertly

1. ADV

/ ˈkoʊvɚtli / (AmE), / ˈkʌvətli / (BrE)
In a secretive or concealed manner; without being openly acknowledged or displayed.

Examples

Once covertly looking at Simms Purdew, the only man in the world whom he hated, he had seen the heavy, slack, bestubbled jaw open and close to emit the cruel, obscene banter, and had seen the pale blue eyes go watery with whisky and merriment, and suddenly he was not seeing the face of that vile creature.

Synset Examples

he did it covertly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - without others knowing; in a secret manner
  • Antonym: openly - in a manner that is not secret; publicly

Etymology

From Middle English 'covert', from Old French, from Latin 'coopertus', past participle of 'cooperire' (to cover).
