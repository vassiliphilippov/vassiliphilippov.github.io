legendary

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdʒ.ən.der.i / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒ.ənd.əri / (BrE)
famous or celebrated to such an extent that it is regarded as almost mythical or the subject of legend.

Examples

It is a lonely, rather desolate region, but full of legendary and historic associations.
Some of the earlier episodes have touches of the supernatural, as suited to the legendary background.
But to the cattlemen who had been facing bankruptcy from rustling losses and to the cowboys who had been faced with lay-offs a few years earlier, he was becoming a vastly different type of legendary figure.

Synset Examples

the legendary exploits of the arctic trailblazers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythical - existing only in stories or legends; not real
  • Degree Lower: famous - widely known and recognized

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legendarius, meaning relating to legends.
