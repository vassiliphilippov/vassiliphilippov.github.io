woolgather

1. VERB

/ ˈwʊlˌɡæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌɡæðə / (BrE)
to engage in aimless daydreaming or indulge in idle fancies

Examples

They looked so formidable, however, so demanding, that I found myself staring at them in dismay and starting to woolgather again, this time about Francesca and her husband.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - to be lost in pleasant thoughts that distract one from the present

Etymology

From the practice of gathering loose tufts of wool from bushes, metaphorically referring to indulging in idle or fanciful thoughts.
