rathole

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈræt.həʊl / (BrE)
A hole in a building or other structure made by rats.

Examples

First of all there was the parsonage, an utterly impossible place for civilized people to live in, originally poorly conceived, apparently not repaired for years, with no plumbing or sewage, with rat-holes and rot.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈrætˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small, dirty, and uncomfortable room or place, often used to describe substandard living quarters

Examples

Roberto, the one who owns those ratholes.

Etymology

From 'rat' + 'hole', originally referring to a hole made by a rat, later used metaphorically for unpleasant spaces.
