grinding

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
Small particles or fragments produced by grinding something, often used in the plural, e.g., coffee grindings, vegetable grindings.

Examples

The large amount of vegetable grindings from the grocery store in the shopping center created a suspended solids problem.

Synset Examples

vegetable grindings clogged the drain

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: powder - fine, dry particles produced by crushing or grinding substances
  • Degree Lower: dust - fine, dry particles of matter

Etymology

From the verb 'grind' + -ing (forming nouns from verbs).
