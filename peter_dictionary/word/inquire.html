inquire

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪr / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to ask for information about something

Examples

We simply find ourselves in the position of having no means for inquiring into the structure and meaning of this range of our experience.
"I beg to inquire if the back is now safe for travelers ", he said.
Byron inquired.
Jubal deduced what had happened but did not know with whom - and did n't want to inquire.
The puzzled saleslady inquired, "How large is your house, Madam "?

Synset Examples

I asked about their special today
He had to ask directions several times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ask - to put a question; seek information
  • Synonym: question - to ask something; to inquire

Etymology

From Latin inquirere, from in- (into) + quaerere (to seek).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
to ask for information about something; to seek to know by questioning

Examples

Wilson inquired about it.
Ludie inquired every evening, pretending that he did not care.
After all, when one has asked whatever became of old Joe and Charlie; when one has inquired who it was Sue Brown married and where it is they now live; when questions are asked and answered about families and children; and old professors; when the game and its probable outcome has been exhausted; that does it.
Sir - People continue to inquire the reason for the race for outer space.
[INQUIRING IN ITALIAN]

Synset Examples

He wondered who had built this beautiful church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ask - to put a question to someone
  • Synonym: query - to express doubt or ask a question about something
  • Synonym: question - to ask about something; to interrogate

Etymology

From Latin inquirere, from in- (into) + quaerere (to seek).
