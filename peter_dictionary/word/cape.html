cape

1. NOUN

/ keɪp / (AmE), / keɪp / (BrE)
A sleeveless outer garment that hangs loosely from the shoulders and covers the back, typically shorter than a cloak.

Examples

As he neared the square, a round figure muffled in a long, black cape whisked by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak - a long, loose outer garment similar to a cape but usually with a fastener and sometimes with sleeves

Etymology

From Latin 'cappa' (hooded cloak).

2. NOUN

/ keɪp / (AmE), / keɪp / (BrE)
A narrow piece of land that sticks out into the sea or a lake.

Examples

To starboard was a cape a thousand feet high, patched with ice and snow, populated by thousands of screaming sea birds.
Hudson named the capes Digges and Wolstenholme, for two of his backers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: peninsula - land almost surrounded by water or projecting far into water

Etymology

From Latin 'caput' meaning 'head', via Old French 'cape'.
