polysyndeton

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈsɪndətən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsɪndɪtən / (BrE)
The use of several conjunctions in close succession, especially where they could be omitted, for rhetorical effect.

Examples

And what makes my head go round and round ♪

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asyndeton - omission of conjunctions where they would normally appear

Etymology

From Greek polysyndeton, from poly- (many) + syndeton (bound together).
