cartridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
A cylindrical case containing an explosive charge and a bullet, designed to be fired from a rifle or handgun.

Examples

Shot six or eight times the body was draped with Russell's pistol, belt, and cartridges.
Not even an empty cartridge case could be found.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - a casing containing an explosive charge for firearms or artillery

Etymology

From French 'cartouche', from Italian 'cartoccio' (roll of paper), from 'carta' (paper).
