feverish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (BrE)
showing or caused by intense excitement, activity, or agitation, resembling the rapid energy associated with a fever.

Examples

In an effort to fortify himself against the unforeseen upsets sure to arise in the future, Herbert A. Leggett, banker editor of the Phoenix "Arizona Progress ", reflects upon a few of the depressing experiences of the feverish fifties.
He did not see Sparling, or DeGroot, or Ringel, or any of the feverish crew that had so harassed him twelve hours ago.

Synset Examples

worked at a feverish pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - marked by wild or hurried activity
  • Antonym: calm - not showing agitation or excitement

Etymology

From 'fever' + '-ish', from Old French 'fievre', from Latin 'febris' (fever).
