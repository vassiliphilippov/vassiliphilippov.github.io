flight

1. NOUN

/ flaɪt / (AmE), / flaɪt / (BrE)
a journey made by air in an aircraft; the act or process of flying from one place to another by airplane

Examples

Their flight was delayed, Dunn said, when a boarding ramp inflicted some minor damage to the wing of the plane.
She grasped the chair arms and brought her thin body upright, like a bird alert for flight.
But Mary would soon no longer be living there, for she would be notified in a few days that her husband had died in an accident while on a flight to Tahiti.

It is expected that in 1963 two prototype aircraft will be available for flight testing.

Synset Examples

flying was still an exciting adventure for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - a journey or excursion, especially for pleasure
  • Synonym: voyage - a long journey involving travel by sea or in space
  • Antonym: drive - a journey made by car
  • Antonym: cruise - a journey by ship for pleasure

Etymology

From Old English *flyht* (action or manner of flying), related to *flēogan* (to fly).
