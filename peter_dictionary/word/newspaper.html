newspaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a printed publication, usually issued daily or weekly, containing news, feature articles, advertisements, and other items of general interest

Examples

Synset Examples

he read his newspaper at breakfast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations, often on a particular subject or aimed at a particular readership
  • Synonym: journal - a newspaper or magazine that deals with a particular subject or professional activity

Etymology

From 'news' + 'paper', first used in 17th century English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
A set of large sheets of printed paper containing news, articles, advertisements, and other information, published and distributed at regular intervals.

Examples

A newspaper open at stock-market reports lay on one of them.

Synset Examples

when it began to rain he covered his head with a newspaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations
  • Degree Lower: tabloid - a smaller-sized, condensed newspaper

Etymology

From 'news' + 'paper', indicating a paper containing news; first attested late 17th century.
