extraneousness

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈstreɪniəsnəs / (AmE), / ɛkˈstreɪnɪəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being extraneous; irrelevance or lack of connection to the subject at hand.

Examples

By its greater corporeal presence and its greater extraneousness, the affixed paper or cloth serves for a seeming moment to push everything else into a more vivid idea of depth than the simulated printing or simulated textures had ever done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the quality of not being connected with or relevant to something
  • Antonym: pertinence - the state of being relevant or applicable

Etymology

From extraneous + -ness; extraneous from Latin extraneus, meaning 'from outside'.
