light-headed

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪt ˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Not serious or sensible; tending to be frivolous or silly.

Examples

His rage assumes a religious form; that is, on the basis of his own sinfulness and abject wretchedness, Piepsam becomes a prophet who in his ecstasy and in the name of God imprecates doom on Life - not only the cyclist now, but the audience, the world, as well: "all you light-headed breed ".

Synset Examples

a dizzy blonde
light-headed teenagers
silly giggles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - lacking in seriousness or common sense; foolish
  • Antonym: serious - thoughtful or earnest in manner; not playful

Etymology

From 'light' meaning 'not heavy' + 'head', combined to suggest someone whose thoughts are insubstantial.

2. ADJ

/ ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
feeling dizzy and slightly faint, as if you might lose consciousness

Examples

He felt light-headed and sick.

Synset Examples

suddenly felt faint from the pain
was sick and faint from hunger
felt light in the head
a swooning fit
light-headed with wine
light-headed from lack of sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you or everything around you is spinning
  • Synonym: faint - close to losing consciousness
