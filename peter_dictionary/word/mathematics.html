mathematics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæθ.əˈmæt.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌmæθ.əˈmæt.ɪks / (BrE)
The abstract science of number, quantity, and space, either as pure mathematics or as applied to other disciplines such as physics and engineering.

Examples

From the town surveyor, Hans learned drawing and mathematics and, from a university student, some academic subjects.
He not only knew soldiering, but mathematics, history and literature as well.
So, too, is the mathematical competence of a college graduate who has majored in mathematics.
This is an unsolved problem which probably has never been seriously investigated, although one frequently hears the comment that we have insufficient specialists of the kind who can compete with the Germans or Swiss, for example, in precision machinery and mathematics, or the Finns in geochemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: math - short, informal form for 'mathematics' (mainly American English)

Etymology

From Latin 'mathematica', from Greek 'mathēmatikē (tekhnē)' (mathematical art), from 'mathēmatikos' (fond of learning).
