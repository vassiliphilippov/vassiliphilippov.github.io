imposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of establishing or applying something, such as a rule, tax, or restriction, by authority or force

Examples

Among the chief victims of such an ill conceived imposition would be religion itself.
Mr. Speaker, in my latest newsletter to my constituents I urged the imposition of a naval blockade of Cuba as the only effective method of preventing continued Soviet armaments from coming into the Western Hemisphere in violation of the Monroe Doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enforcement - the act of compelling observance of or compliance with a law, rule, or obligation
  • Antonym: removal - the act of taking something away; opposite of imposing

Etymology

From Latin impositio (a laying upon), from imponere (to impose).
