disrepute

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsrɪˈpjut / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈpjuːt / (BrE)
The state of being regarded with disfavor or low esteem; loss of a good reputation.

Examples

Mary's super critical attitude toward Byron had nothing to do with his moral disrepute.
It would be a shame, Mr. Chairman, if we allowed a few rotten apples to give a bad name to the whole barrel.

Synset Examples

your actions will bring discredit to your name
because of the scandal the school has fallen into disrepute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discredit - loss or lack of reputation or respect
  • Antonym: repute - the state of being highly regarded or having a good reputation

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'reputare' (to think over).
