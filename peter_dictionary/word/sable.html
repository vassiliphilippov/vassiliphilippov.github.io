sable

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəl / (BrE)
a fine artist's brush made from the hair of a sable, valued for its softness and spring

Examples

"My brushes are different from those used by most watercolorists, for I combine the sable and the bristle.
The red sables are 8; two riggers, 6 and 10; and a very large, flat wash brush.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bristle - an artist's brush made with coarse animal hair, often from pigs

Etymology

From the name of the sable, a small mammal (Martes zibellina), whose hair is used for fine brushes.
