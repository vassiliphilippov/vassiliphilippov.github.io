goddamn

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌdæm / (BrE)
Used to express anger, annoyance, or emphasis, often considered vulgar or offensive.

Examples

That goddamn redheader was the worst.
I hated the goddamn army from the first day I got in anyhow.
Why should I be spinning just because the goddamn log is spinning?
"Mind your own goddamn business ", Mike Deegan said.
Goddamn it, we had him!

Synset Examples

oh, damn (or goddamn)!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - used as an expletive to express anger or emphasis

Etymology

From 'God damn', invoking the curse 'may God damn (something)'. Considered blasphemous.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒd.dæm / (BrE)
Used to express strong anger, annoyance, or emphasis, especially as an intensifier; regarded as vulgar or offensive by some.

Examples

SONNY: You'’re goddamn right, that'’s Pop talking!

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot
it's a blamed shame
a blame cold winter
not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - used as an expletive to express anger, emphasis, or annoyance
  • Degree Lower: blasted - used informally as a mild intensifier, less vulgar than 'goddamn'

Etymology

Formed from 'God' and 'damn' as an expletive phrase, attested since the 19th century.
