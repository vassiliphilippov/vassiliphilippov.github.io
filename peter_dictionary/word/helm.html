helm

1. NOUN

/ hɛlm / (AmE), / hɛlm / (BrE)
the steering apparatus of a ship or boat, especially the wheel or tiller by which it is steered

Examples

Ralph Houk, successor to Casey Stengel at the Yankee helm, plans to bring the entire New York squad here from St. Petersburg, including Joe Dimaggio and large crowds are anticipated for both weekend games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiller - lever used to turn a boat's rudder

Etymology

Old English *helma* (rudder, handle), related to Old High German *helmo* (tiller).

2. NOUN

/ hɛlm / (AmE), / hɛlm / (BrE)
a position of leadership or control, especially in an organization or government

Examples

With Skorich at the helm, the Eagles are expected to put more emphasis on running, rather than passing.

Synset Examples

the President is at the helm of the Ship of State

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - control or authority over a group or organization
  • Synonym: leadership - the action or position of leading a group or organization
  • Antonym: subordinate - a lower or less powerful position under authority

Etymology

From Old English helpm (rudder, steering handle), related to Proto-Germanic *helmaz.
