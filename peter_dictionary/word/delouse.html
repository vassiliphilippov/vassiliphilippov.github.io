delouse

1. VERB

/ diˈlaʊs / (AmE), / diˈlaʊs / (BrE)
To remove lice from (a person, animal, or place) by cleaning or applying special chemicals.

Examples

They march you in, naked as the day you were born, skin burning and half-blind from that delousing shit they throw on you.

Synset Examples

They deloused the prisoners after they liberated the camps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small parasitic insect

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (remove) and 'louse' (a parasitic insect).
