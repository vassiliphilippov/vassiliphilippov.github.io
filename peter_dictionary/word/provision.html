provision

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a condition or clause that is specified or stipulated, especially in a legal document or agreement

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clause - a distinct section of a legal document
  • Synonym: condition - a stipulation that must be met

Etymology

From Latin 'provisio' (a foreseeing, provision), from 'providere' (to foresee, provide).

4. VERB

/ prəˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
to supply with food, equipment, or other necessary items, especially for a journey or expedition

Examples

He had obtained and provisioned a veteran ship called the Discovery and had recruited a crew of twenty-one, the largest he had ever commanded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply - to provide what is needed or required
  • Synonym: equip - to provide with necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'provisionem' (a providing beforehand), via Old French 'provision'.
