steadfastly

1. ADV

/ ˈstɛdˌfæstli / (AmE), / ˈstɛdˌfɑːstli / (BrE)
In a resolute, firm, or unwavering manner; with steady determination.

Examples

Denouncing the view that the sexual union is an end in itself, the Conference declared: "We steadfastly uphold what must always be regarded as the governing considerations of Christian marriage.

Synset Examples

we firmly believed it
you must stand firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - in a strong, steady, or resolute way
  • Synonym: resolutely - in an admirably purposeful, determined way

Etymology

From Middle English 'stedfast', from Old English 'stedefæst' (firm, fixed) + -ly.
