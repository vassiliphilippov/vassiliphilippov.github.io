nuisance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.səns / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.səns / (BrE)
A person, thing, or circumstance causing inconvenience or annoyance; in law, an act or condition that interferes with the use or enjoyment of property or endangers health or safety.

Examples

The location of the oxidation pond in a high value residential area makes odor nuisances a sensitive problem for the developer.
There would be all the nuisance of contacting someone else to take over.
And Mr. Skyros did n't like Angie, but what with Prettyman and three of his boys inside, and not likely to come out - And Angie such a valuable salesman, Prettyman said - All the nuisance and danger of getting in touch with practically a whole new bunch of boys - Why did everything have to happen at once?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - something that causes irritation or bother
  • Synonym: problem - something that causes difficulty or trouble
  • Antonym: convenience - the state of being able to proceed with little effort or difficulty; opposite of nuisance in some contexts

Etymology

From Old French 'nuire' (to harm) from Latin 'nocere' (to harm).
