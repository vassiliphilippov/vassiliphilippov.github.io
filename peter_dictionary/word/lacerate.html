lacerate

1. VERB

/ ˈlæsəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæsəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to cut or tear something, especially flesh, with jagged or irregular edges

Examples

If he backed against the fence, one of the cars would brush him as it passed, and he would be cruelly lacerated by the wire.
"Why lacerate the - congregation "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or make a hole in by force
  • Synonym: slash - to cut with a sweeping or forceful stroke
  • Synonym: mangle - to injure severely by crushing, cutting, or tearing

Etymology

From Latin laceratus, past participle of lacerare (to tear, mangle), from lacer (torn, mangled).
