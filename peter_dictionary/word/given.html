given

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
Something that is assumed to be true or accepted as a fact without proof.

Examples

It is for them a given which they and they alone possess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true without proof
  • Synonym: postulate - a statement assumed without proof as a basis for reasoning

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
Assuming or accepting something as a fact for the purposes of discussion or argument.

Examples

We first define a function b (t) as follows: given the set of squares such that each has three corners on C and vertex at t, b (t) is the corresponding set of positive parametric differences between t and the backward corner points.
Given this kind of business climate, what competitive marketing problems will your company face in the next 10 yr.?
And, given probable public attitudes - about which reasonably good estimates can be made - what action is called for to insure necessary support?
The manner in which this is shared among firms is taken as given.
For the industry of this model, the effect of such public pressures in the past has been to hold the price well below the short-run profit maximizing price (given the wage rate and the level of GNP), and even below the entry limited price (but not below average cost).

Synset Examples

given the engine's condition, it is a wonder that it started

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assuming - Accepting something as true for the purposes of argument
  • Synonym: provided - On the condition that; if

Etymology

From the past participle of 'give', from Old English 'giefan'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') having a tendency or inclination toward a particular habit, action, or condition.

Examples

I answer to Grissom, not to psychos.

Synset Examples

he is apt to ignore matters he considers unimportant
I am not minded to answer any questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prone - having a tendency or inclination to do something
  • Synonym: apt - having a natural tendency to do something

Etymology

Past participle of 'give', from Old English 'giefan'.
