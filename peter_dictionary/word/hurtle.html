hurtle

1. VERB

/ ˈhɝːtəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtəl / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with great speed, often making a loud noise

Examples

The truck was hurtling forward.
In a fraction of a second the pickup truck hurtled by on the other side.

Synset Examples

The cars hurtled by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Synonym: speed - to move quickly

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'hurten' (to knock), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɝːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move at great speed, often in a forceful or uncontrolled way

Examples

To the German pilot in the bomber the rocket became a faint black speck, hurtling through the sky at the then incredible speed of 420 m. p. h..
He glanced up in time to see Roberts hurtling down on him from above, literally flying through the air, his bloody face twisted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move quickly with force
  • Synonym: bolt - to move suddenly and quickly

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *hurlen* (to hurl, rush), frequentative of *hurl*.
