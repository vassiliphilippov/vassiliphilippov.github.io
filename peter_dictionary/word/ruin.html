ruin

1. VERB

/ ˈruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
to damage something so badly that it loses all value, usefulness, or enjoyment; to destroy completely or irreparably

Examples

But in our case - and neither my wife nor I have extreme views on integration, nor are we given to emotional outbursts - the situation has ruined one or two valued friendships and come close to wrecking several more.
It is true, of course, the uncertainties of life being what they are, that as now and then the Christian killed the lion, homecoming days have been ruined by a visiting team.
They had ruined the radar warning system with their window, they had made themselves invisible above their flares.
"You 're working up to something, and if you do n't watch out you 'll ruin your whole life one of these days just to prove that the Abernathy family is superior to everything, even a depression ".
And your family will be ruined.

Synset Examples

You have ruined my car by pouring sugar in the tank!
The tears ruined her make-up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • Synonym: wreck - to severely damage or destroy something
  • Degree Lower: spoil - to diminish or destroy the value or quality of

Etymology

From Old French *ruiner*, from Latin *ruinare* (to destroy), from *ruina* (collapse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
A complete and irreparable state of destruction or devastation.

Examples

Adam watched his own hands make the caressing, anxious movement that, when rain falls and nobody comes, and ruin draws close like a cat rubbing against the ankles, has been the ritual of stall vendors, forever.
He tried defiance and openly flaunted his devotion to his half sister, but he soon saw, as did she, that this course if persisted in would involve them in a common ruin.
Each failed catastrophically in an invasion of Russia and each brought ruin on the country that worshipped him.

Synset Examples

you have brought ruin on this entire family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying or the state of being destroyed
  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage; desolation
  • Antonym: salvation - preservation or deliverance from harm or ruin

Etymology

From Old French *ruine*, from Latin *ruina* (a collapse, downfall).

3. NOUN

/ ˈruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
A building or structure that has fallen into a state of decay or collapse, often as a result of age, neglect, or destruction.

Examples

They looked at the ruins of the old Roman wall on the lower Via Veneto, then they went to the Farnese Gardens.

Synset Examples

they explored several Roman ruins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remains - what is left after something is destroyed or decays
  • Synonym: wreck - a badly damaged or destroyed building or object
  • Antonym: structure - a building (usually intact), contrasted with ruin

Etymology

From Latin 'ruina' (a collapse, downfall), from 'ruere' (to fall, rush).

4. VERB

/ ˈruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
to do great harm or damage to something so that it is no longer good, useful, or successful

Examples

It will ruin your already useless reputation.

Synset Examples

This behavior will ruin your chances of winning the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Synonym: wreck - to severely damage or destroy something
  • Antonym: save - to rescue from harm or destruction

Etymology

From Old French 'ruiner', from Latin 'ruinare' (to fall, collapse), from 'ruina' (a collapse).
