vaccine

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
A substance containing weakened or killed microorganisms, or pieces of them, that is injected or otherwise administered to stimulate the body's immune response and protect against specific diseases.

Examples

The merits of the Salk anti polio vaccine were not established on the forensic platform or in newspaper editorials, but in the laboratory and by tests in the field on thousands of children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunization - the process of making someone immune, especially by vaccination
  • Synonym: inoculation - the act of introducing a vaccine or antigenic substance into the body

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccinus' (from cows), after early vaccines were derived from cowpox.
