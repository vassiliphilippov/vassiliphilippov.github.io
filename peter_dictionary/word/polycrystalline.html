polycrystalline

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑliˈkrɪstəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈkrɪstəlʌɪn / (BrE)
(of a material) consisting of multiple small crystals joined together, as opposed to a single crystal.

Examples

A proton magnetic resonance study of polycrystalline **f as a function of magnetic field and temperature is presented.
The general theory of resonance shifts is used to derive a general expression for the second moment f of a polycrystalline** paramagnetic sample and is specialized to **f.

Synset Examples

polycrystalline metals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocrystalline - consisting of a single crystal

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'crystal' (clear ice, crystal).
