redevelopment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
The process of improving an area by demolishing old buildings and constructing new ones, or by renovating existing structures.

Examples

Located in a bad slum area now undergoing redevelopment, this school and its program are especially tailored to the vocational aims of its students.

Synset Examples

they are pursuing a general program of renovation to the entire property
a major overhal of the healthcare system was proposed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the process of restoring something to a better state
  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its former condition
  • Antonym: demolition - the act of destroying, especially as part of rebuilding

Etymology

Formed from 're-' (again) + 'development'.
