/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (BrE)
the state or feeling of taking pleasure in something

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
  • Synonym: delight - extreme pleasure or satisfaction

Etymology

From Old French *enjois* (delight), from *enjoir* (to give joy), from Late Latin *en- + gaudium* (joy, delight).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (BrE)
The taking of pleasure in something; the state or process of deriving happiness or satisfaction from an activity or experience.

Examples

As the generations move on, clothes become more suitable for the enjoyment of outdoor sports.
Later, riding in for some lusty enjoyment of the liquor and professional ladies of Cheyenne, he laid claim to the killing with the vague insinuations he made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
  • Antonym: suffering - the state of undergoing pain, distress, or hardship

Etymology

From French *enjoie* or from enjoy + -ment, modeled after French.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (BrE)
(Law) The action or right of using and deriving benefit from property.

Examples

Moreover, as Communist power increases, the enjoyment of these conditions throughout the world diminishes pro rata and the possibility of their restoration becomes increasingly remote.

Synset Examples

we were given the use of his boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: use - the act of using something (legal right)

Etymology

From Old French *enjoir* (to enjoy), from Latin *en-*, intensive prefix, + *gaudere* (to rejoice).
