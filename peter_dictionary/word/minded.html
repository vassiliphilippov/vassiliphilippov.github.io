minded

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Inclined or disposed to do something (usually used after 'to').

Examples

I'm of a mind to make some mooky.

Synset Examples

he is apt to ignore matters he considers unimportant
I am not minded to answer any questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likely - having a tendency or inclination
  • Antonym: unwilling - not willing or inclined to do something

Etymology

From 'mind' + '-ed', meaning 'having the mind inclined (to)'.
