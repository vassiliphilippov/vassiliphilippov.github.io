introjected

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdʒɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʒɛktɪd / (BrE)
(in psychology) unconsciously incorporated into one's own psyche, especially referring to attitudes or values that originate from another person.

Examples

What had been an unmanageably powerful introject was now, despite its continuing charge of energy disconcerting to me, sufficiently within control of her ego that she could use it to show me what this introjected mother was like.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internalized - incorporated (an idea or attitude) within oneself

Etymology

From the verb 'introject', from Latin intro- (inward) + jacere (to throw).
