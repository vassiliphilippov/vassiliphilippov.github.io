suburbanized

1. ADJ

/ səˈbɝː.bə.naɪzd / (AmE), / səˈbɜː.bə.naɪzd / (BrE)
Having been developed with or surrounded by suburbs; having the characteristics of a suburb.

Examples

The fact seems to be that very many large branch stores are uneconomical, that the choice of location in the suburbs is as important as it was downtown, and that even highly suburbanized cities will support only so many big branches.

Synset Examples

a highly suburbanized city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanized - made more urban, with city-like characteristics

Etymology

From 'suburb' + '-anize' (form of '-ize', to make or become), meaning to become like suburbs or be filled with suburbs.
