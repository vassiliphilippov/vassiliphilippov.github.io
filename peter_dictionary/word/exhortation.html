exhortation

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
An address or communication that strongly encourages someone to do something.

Examples

We perform elaborate international exhortations and ceremonies with virtually no understanding of social cause and effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence to do something
  • Synonym: admonition - a firm warning or reprimand

Etymology

From Latin 'exhortatio', from 'exhortari' meaning to encourage or urge.
