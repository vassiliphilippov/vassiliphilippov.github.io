catatonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) characterized by a marked lack of movement, activity, or expression, especially as a result of catatonia; showing muscular rigidity or stupor.

Examples

Holmes is addicted to the use of cocaine and other refreshing stimulants; he is prone to semi catatonic trances induced by the playing of the vioiln; he is a recluse, an incredible egotist, a confirmed misogynist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catatonia - a state of psycho-motor immobility and behavioral abnormality

Etymology

From Greek katatonos (under tension), from kata- (down) + tonos (tone).
