subscribe

1. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
(of a person or organization) to apply for and agree to purchase shares in a company or a new issue of securities.

Examples

"We subscribe 500 shares of Morton Foods of Texas.
This paper was signed by forty-five persons, subscribing a total of two hundred shares.

Synset Examples

The broker subscribed 500 shares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invest - to put money into shares, property, or a business to earn a profit

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (write underneath, sign).

2. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to agree with or support an opinion, theory, or belief

Examples

We know that in the early part of the century many Protestant congregations took positive action against members who transgressed the ethical codes to which the majority subscribed.

Synset Examples

I subscribe to your view on abortion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorse - to publicly support or approve of something
  • Antonym: oppose - to disagree with or resist a belief, idea, or plan

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (to write underneath, sign), from sub- (under) + scribere (to write).

3. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to sign one's name at the end of a document as a witness or as a participant

Examples

"We, the Subscribers, do agree, that as soon as a convenient Number of Persons have subscribed to this, or a similar Writing, We will present a petition to the Hon' ble General Court of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, praying for an Act incorporating into a Body politic the subscribers to such Writing with Liberty to build such a Bridge, and a Right to demand a Toll equal to that received at Malden Bridge, and on like Terms, and if such an Act shall be obtained, then we severally agree each with the others, that we will hold in the said Bridge the several shares set against our respective Names, the whole into two hundred shares being divided, and that we will pay such sums of Money at such Times and in such Manners, as by the said proposed Corporation, shall be directed and required ".

Synset Examples

She signed the letter and sent it off
Please sign here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - write one's signature on a document

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (to write underneath, sign), from sub- (under) + scribere (to write).
