kidnapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdˌnæpər / (AmE), / ˈkɪdˌnapə / (BrE)
A person who unlawfully seizes and holds someone captive, often demanding a ransom for their release.

Examples

Kidnapper Spurns Ransom; Amy Still Missing.
It's just barely possible with this crowd that the kidnapper was n't able to get a table.
Or have a sign on his car that says,' Here Comes the Paxton Kidnapper "'?
The crime showed too much planning, the kidnappers appeared too proficient to be caught by a checklist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abductor - a person who forcibly takes someone away

Etymology

Formed from 'kidnap' (from 'kid' + 'nap' meaning 'to snatch'), with the agentive suffix '-er'.
