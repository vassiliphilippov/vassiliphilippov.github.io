notwithstanding

1. ADV

/ ˌnɑtwɪθˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒtwɪðˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
Nevertheless; in spite of this. Used to indicate that something happens or is true in spite of another fact or situation.

Examples

His denials of extensive reading notwithstanding, it is no doubt safe to assume that he has spent time schooling himself in Southern history and that he has gained some acquaintance with the chief literary authors who have lived in the South or have written about the South.
Now she can'’t stay even if she gets rid of it.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; nevertheless
  • Synonym: however - nevertheless; in spite of that
  • Synonym: despite - without being affected by; in spite of

Etymology

From Middle English, from the phrase 'not withstanding' (not resisting or opposing).
