accusation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.jəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.jʊˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A statement or claim that someone has done something wrong or illegal; the act of accusing someone.

Examples

Upon receiving the news, Northern writers, editors, and clergymen heaped accusations of murder on the Southern states, particularly Virginia.
And another time, without accusation: "You never wore that scarf I bought you ".
What was only a vague suspicion in the case of Sherlock Holmes now appears as a direct accusation: the private eye is in danger of turning into his opposite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charge - an official statement accusing someone of a crime
  • Antonym: denial - the act of stating that something is not true, especially an accusation

Etymology

From Latin accusatio (a charging, indictment), from accusare (to accuse).
