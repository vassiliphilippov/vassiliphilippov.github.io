self-destructive

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌk.tɪv / (BrE)
tending to cause harm or danger to oneself or one's own interests

Examples

the crude sketch of Piepsam contains, in its critical, destructive and self-destructive tendencies, much that is enlarged and illuminated in the figures of, for instance, Naphta and Leverku^hn.

Synset Examples

suicidal impulses
a suicidal corporate takeover strategy
a kamikaze pilot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-harming - deliberately injuring oneself
  • Antonym: self-preserving - tending to protect oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to oneself) and 'destructive' (causing destruction), patterned after similar self-compounds.
