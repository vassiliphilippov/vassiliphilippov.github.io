overtime

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətaɪm / (BrE)
time spent working in addition to one's normal working hours, or the pay received for this extra work.

Examples

Are you watching work schedules for boiler operators, guards, and other 24 - hour day, 7 - day week operations in order to minimize overtime?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensatory time - time off given instead of overtime pay

Etymology

From over- (above, beyond) + time; first attested in late 19th century referring to time worked in addition to normal hours.
