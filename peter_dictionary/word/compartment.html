compartment

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑːrtmənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
a separate section, chamber, or enclosed space within something larger, divided off by a partition

Examples

Sports Writer Ensign Ritchie of the Ogden Standard Examiner went to his compartment to talk with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: section - a distinct part or subdivision of a larger whole
  • Synonym: chamber - an enclosed space or compartment

Etymology

From French 'compartiment', from Italian 'compartimento', from 'compartire' (to divide with).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑːrtmənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
A separate section or space within a larger enclosed area, divided off for a particular purpose.

Examples

On this, she builds an "egg compartment "or "egg cell "which is filled with that famous pollen and nectar mixture called beebread.
Then, a group of eggs is deposited in a cavity in the beebread loaf and the egg compartment is closed.
When she succeeds, she usually manages to slip in unobtrusively, to deposit an egg on a completed loaf of beebread before the bumblebees seal the egg compartment.
The hosts never seem to recognize that something is amiss, so that the compartment afterward is sealed normally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: section - a distinct part or subdivision of something
  • Synonym: division - an act or instance of dividing; partition

Etymology

From French 'compartiment', from Italian 'compartimento', from 'compartire' (to divide).
