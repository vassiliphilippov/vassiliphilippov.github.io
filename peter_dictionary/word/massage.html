massage

1. VERB

/ məˈsɑʒ / (AmE), / maˈsɑːʒ / (BrE)
to rub and knead the muscles of someone’s body, usually to relieve tension or pain or to promote relaxation

Examples

The bristles are soft enough to massage the gums and not scratch the enamel.

Synset Examples

She massaged his sore back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knead - to work or massage by pressing and folding, usually dough or muscles
  • Synonym: rub - to move one object against another with pressure

Etymology

From French 'massage', from 'masser' (to massage), from Arabic 'massa' (to touch, to feel).

2. NOUN

/ məˈsɑːʒ / (AmE), / məˈsɑːʒ / (BrE)
The act of rubbing and kneading parts of the body with the hands, usually to relieve tension, increase circulation, and promote relaxation.

Examples

Recently many doctors have installed ultrasonic vibration machines for deep massage of bruises, contusions, and simple bursitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubdown - vigorously rubbing the body to warm or relax it

Etymology

From French 'massage' (friction of kneading), from 'masser' (to massage) and Italian 'massaggio'.

3. VERB

/ məˈsɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈmæsɑːʒ / (BrE)
to rub and press someone's body with the hands, usually to relieve pain, relax muscles, or improve circulation

Examples

Conrad hung up and sat on the small telephone-booth bench, massaging his right leg.

Synset Examples

She rubbed down her child with a sponge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knead - to work and press something (such as dough or muscles) with the hands

Etymology

From French 'massage', from 'masser' (to massage), ultimately from Arabic 'massa' (to touch, feel).
