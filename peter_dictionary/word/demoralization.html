demoralization

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌmɔɹələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒrələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of undermining or destroying the moral foundations of a doctrine, policy, or system.

Examples

The career of Eichmann made the Trial a potential showcase for anti-Semitic demoralization:

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior; process of making someone or something morally depraved
  • Synonym: demoralize - to undermine the morals of a person or group

Etymology

From French *démoralisation*, from *démoraliser* (to corrupt morals).
