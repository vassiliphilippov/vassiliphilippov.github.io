fairway

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈfeə.weɪ / (BrE)
the area of a golf course between the tee and the putting green where the grass is cut short, making it easier to play the ball

Examples

On the narrow fairway of a 508 - yard hole, Arnold whipped into his second shot.
His first shot in the Open last year landed in a brook that flowed along the right side of the fairway.

Etymology

From 'fair' (clear, uncluttered) + 'way' (path), referring to the clear path on a golf course.
