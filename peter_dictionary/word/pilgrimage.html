pilgrimage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɡrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɡrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
A journey undertaken to a sacred place as an act of religious devotion; sometimes used figuratively for any significant journey with a worthy purpose.

Examples

The dark forms moved like mourners on some nocturnal pilgrimage, their dirge unsung for want of vocal chords.
She lost not a second, picking herself up and continuing her pilgrimage to Laura.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another

Etymology

From Old French 'pelerimage', from Late Latin 'pelegrinaticum', from Latin 'peregrinus' (foreigner, pilgrim).
