variant

1. ADJ

/ ˈværiənt / (AmE), / ˈvɛːriənt / (BrE)
Differing from the standard, norm, or regular form.

Examples

Since the Protestant clergy for the most part wear gray or some variant from the wholly black suit, my Roman collar and black garb usually identify me in England as a Roman Catholic cleric.

Synset Examples

a variant spelling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: standard - conforming to established usage or accepted norm
  • Antonym: regular - conforming to a usual or prescribed standard

Etymology

From Latin 'variant-', present participle of 'variare' (to change, vary).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / ˈveəriənt / (BrE)
something that differs from others or from a standard; an instance or event that is unusual or unexpected.

Examples

The reader, observing this process, might ask "why not be different "? and find in the answer a license to be a variant of the human species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviation - a departure from a standard or norm
  • Synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard or expected
  • Antonym: norm - a standard or typical example

Etymology

From Latin 'varians', present participle of 'variare' (to change), from 'varius' (various).
