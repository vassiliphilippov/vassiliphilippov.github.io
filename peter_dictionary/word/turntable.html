turntable

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌteɪbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
a circular, horizontal platform that rotates a phonograph record so it can be played, especially on a record player or DJ equipment

Examples

Everything from poetry to phonetics, history to histrionics, philosophy to party games has been adapted to the turntable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record player - device that plays phonograph records

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'table', referring to a table that turns or rotates.
