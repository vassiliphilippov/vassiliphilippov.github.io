diaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.pə / (BrE)
A piece of absorbent material, often folded cloth or disposable material, worn by babies or those incontinent to catch urine and feces.

Examples

His chelas were required to assume the matsyendra posture dressed in hand-woven diapers while he read aloud from Rig-Veda and an assistant guru examined their purses in another room - nothing was stolen; the purpose was less immediate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nappy - British term for a baby's absorbent undergarment

Etymology

From Old French diapre (ornamental fabric), later used for linen cloth; later specialized in English for babies' cloths.
