contraction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The shortening or tightening of a muscle or muscle fiber, often resulting in movement or increased tension.

Examples

It's a complete thigh contraction extension exercise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxation - release of tension in a muscle
  • Degree Higher: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction

Etymology

From Latin 'contractio' (a drawing together), from 'contrahere' meaning 'to draw together'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtræktʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a word made by shortening and combining two or more words, usually by omitting certain letters or sounds (e.g., 'can't' from 'cannot')

Examples

KAY: I didn'’t even know he had a brother.

Synset Examples

`won't' is a contraction of `will not'
`o'clock' is a contraction of `of the clock'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbreviation - shortened form of a word or phrase

Etymology

From Latin contractionem, from contrahere (to draw together).
