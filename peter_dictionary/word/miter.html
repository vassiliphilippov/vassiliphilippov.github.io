miter

1. VERB

/ ˈmaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə / (BrE)
To bevel the edges of a piece, especially wood, usually at a 45-degree angle, so they fit together to form a miter joint.

Examples

Cut beveled edge on the long sides of clay at a 45 ` angle to miter corners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bevel - to cut or shape an edge at an angle

Etymology

From Old French mitre (headband, turban), from Latin mitra, from Greek mitra (headband, girdle). The tool/joinery sense is by analogy with a bishop's miter, due to the shape formed by the joint.
