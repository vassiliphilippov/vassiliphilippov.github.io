gallantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləntri / (AmE), / ˈɡæləntri / (BrE)
exceptional courage or heroism, especially in battle or facing danger

Examples

Adam stared at the door and remembered that Simms Purdew had been awarded the Medal of Honor for gallantry at Antietam.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroism - exceptional bravery or courageous actions, often in dangerous or challenging situations
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage to face danger or difficulty

Etymology

From French *galanterie* (gallant behavior); ultimately from Old French *galant* (courteous, brave).
