diplomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to diplomacy or foreign affairs, or characteristic of diplomats

Examples

"The discontinuity can either be that of war to destruction, or that of diplomatic policy ".
Fearful of inviting a military showdown with the West which they could not win, the Communists seek to undermine Western power where the nuclear might of the West is irrelevant - in backwoods guerrilla skirmishes, in mob uprisings in the streets, in parliaments, in clandestine meetings of undercover conspirators, at the United Nations, on the propaganda front, at diplomatic conferences - preferably at the highest level.

Synset Examples

diplomatic immunity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomat - an official representing a country abroad

Etymology

From French diplomatique, from diplomate (diplomat), from Greek díplōma (folded paper, document).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (BrE)
having or showing skill and tact in dealing with sensitive matters or people; tactful.

Examples

When, as a diplomatic gesture of amity and in payment for the loan of gunmen in the April election, Torrio had given O' Banion a slice of Cicero, the profits from that district had been $ 20000 a month.

Synset Examples

the hostess averted a confrontation with a diplomatic chenage of subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactful - having or showing sensitivity and skill in dealing with others or with difficult issues
  • Antonym: indiscreet - lacking good judgment, especially concerning sensitive matters

Etymology

From French diplomatique, from New Latin diplomaticus, relating to documents conferring privileges (diploma).
