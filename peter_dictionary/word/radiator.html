radiator

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.di.eɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.di.eɪ.tə / (BrE)
an object or surface that emits energy, especially in the form of heat or electromagnetic waves; in physics, any body that radiates energy.

Examples

For the case of Jupiter, the radio emission spectrum is definitely not like the spectrum of a black-body radiator, and it seems very likely that the radiation reaching the earth is a combination of thermal radiation from the atmosphere and non thermal components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emitter - an object that emits something (such as radiation, sound, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin *radiare* (to emit rays) + -or (agent suffix).
