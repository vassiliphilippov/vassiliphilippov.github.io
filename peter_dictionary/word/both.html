both

1. DET

/ boʊθ / (AmE), / bəʊθ / (BrE)
used to refer to two people or things together; the two considered together.

Examples

Tomorrow you both go talk to Fanucci.

Synset Examples

both girls are pretty

Etymology

From Old English *bā* (both), related to Old Norse *báðir*, Gothic *bai*, and Latin *uterque* (each of two).
