descending

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
moving or directed downward, or going from a higher to a lower position, level, or amount.

Examples

Facilitatory processes take place between neocortex and hypothalamus via ascending and descending pathways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascending - moving or directed upward

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'descend', from Latin 'descendere' (to climb down).
