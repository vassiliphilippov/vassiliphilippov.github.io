Occidental

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of the western part of the world, especially Europe or the Americas.

Examples

All this, though, is simply a prelude, a curtain-raiser, for what ensues, and I doubt whether any Occidental could accurately forecast it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Oriental - a native inhabitant of the Orient (East)

Etymology

From Latin occidentalis (western), from occidens (the west, sunset).
