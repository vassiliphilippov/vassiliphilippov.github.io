routine

1. NOUN

/ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
A short performance, such as a dance or comedy act, that forms part of a longer entertainment program.

Examples

The party at Floyd's penthouse gave the "chorines "a chance for a nostalgic frolic through all those hackneyed routines which have become a classic choreographic statement of the era's nonsense.
When radio came in, it continued the misplaced modifier in its routines as a standard device.

Synset Examples

he did his act three times every evening
she had a catchy little routine
it was one of the best numbers he ever did

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - a short performance forming part of a show
  • Synonym: number - a performance or item in a theatrical show

Etymology

From French *routine* (habit, usual course), from *route* (road, way).

2. ADJ

/ ruˈtin / (AmE), / ruːˈtiːn / (BrE)
happening as a normal part of daily life; usual or ordinary, not special or unusual

That Prokofieff's harmonies and forms sometimes seem professionally routine to our ears, may or may not indicate that he was less of an "original "than we prefer to believe.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everyday - ordinary; seen or used every day
  • Synonym: quotidian - commonplace; occurring daily
  • Antonym: exceptional - unusual; not typical or routine

Etymology

From French routine, from route (road, path), originally meaning a regular or habitual course.
