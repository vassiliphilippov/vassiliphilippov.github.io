unwisely

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈwaɪzli / (AmE), / ʌnˈwaɪzli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows poor judgment or lack of good sense.

Examples

He is a trustee for the common good, however feeble the safeguards which the positive or municipal law of property provides against his misuse of that share of the common fund, wisely or unwisely, entrusted to his keeping.

Synset Examples

He acted foolishly when he agreed to come

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wisely - in a way that shows good judgment
  • Synonym: foolishly - in a way that lacks good sense or judgment

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'wise' (showing good judgment) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
