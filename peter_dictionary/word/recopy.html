recopy

1. VERB

/ riˈkɑpi / (AmE), / riˈkɒpi / (BrE)
to make a copy of something again, especially after an earlier version was unsatisfactory or lost

Examples

Originally recorded by hand, these words have been copied and recopied, translated and retranslated through the ages.

Synset Examples

The child had to recopy the homework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - to make a duplicate of something

Etymology

From the prefix re- (again) and copy.
