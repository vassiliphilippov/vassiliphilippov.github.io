wedge

1. NOUN

/ wɛdʒ / (AmE), / wɛdʒ / (BrE)
A piece or shape that is thick at one end and tapers to a thin edge at the other, typically triangular in cross section.

Examples

New York lay bleaching in the summer sun, and the morning fish hawk, flying in the heated air, saw below him the long triangular wedge of Manhattan Island.

Etymology

Old English wecg, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch wig and German Wek.

2. VERB

/ wɛdʒ / (AmE), / wɛdʒ / (BrE)
to force or fit something tightly into a narrow space

Examples

Then there was a bad delay in getting Mort Lindsey's 30 - piece orchestra wedged into its chairs.

Synset Examples

lodge a bullet in the table
stick your thumb in the crack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - to squeeze or pack tightly into a space
