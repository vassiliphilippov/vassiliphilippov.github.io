spherule

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪr.juːl / (AmE), / ˈsfɪə.rjuːl / (BrE)
a small sphere, especially one occurring as a distinct particle or structure, often in scientific or geological contexts

Examples

Most meteoritic material, by the time it reaches the Earth's surface, has been reduced to dust or to spherules of ablated material in its passage through the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sphere - a perfectly round geometrical object in three dimensions
  • Synonym: globule - a small round drop or ball of a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'sphaerula', diminutive of 'sphaera' (sphere).
