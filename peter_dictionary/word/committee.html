committee

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A group of people officially appointed to perform a specific function, typically by a larger body or organization.

Examples

Committee approval of Gov. Price Daniel's "abandoned property "act seemed certain Thursday despite the adamant protests of Texas bankers.
Under committee rules, it went automatically to a subcommittee for one week.
He told the committee the measure would merely provide means of enforcing the escheat law which has been on the books "since Texas was a republic ".
A House committee which heard his local option proposal is expected to give it a favorable report, although the resolution faces hard sledding later.
The Senate quickly whipped through its meager fare of House bills approved by committees, passing the three on the calendar.

Synset Examples

a committee is a group that keeps minutes and loses hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panel - a small group of people brought together to discuss or decide on a particular matter
  • Synonym: board - a group of people who manage or oversee an organization

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *committe* (person to whom something is committed), from Latin *committere* (to entrust, commit).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪti / (AmE), / kəˈmɪti / (BrE)
A group of people who organize themselves to advance a cause or goal, especially without being formally appointed by an authority.

Examples

Named by Mayor Wagner three years ago to head a committee that included James A. Farley, Bernard Gimbel and Clint Blume, Shea worked relentlessly.
This committee owes an apology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commission - an official group appointed for a specific function
  • Synonym: board - a group of people who manage or oversee an organization

Etymology

From early modern English, from Anglo-Norman commité, past participle of commettre (‘to commit’).
