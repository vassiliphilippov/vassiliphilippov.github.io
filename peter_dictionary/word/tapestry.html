tapestry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæpəstri / (AmE), / ˈtapɪstri / (BrE)
A complex or intricate combination of things, events, or elements, likened to a richly detailed woven fabric.

Examples

Into the texture of this tapestry of history and human drama Henrietta, as every artist delights to do, wove strands of her own intuitive insights into human nature and - especially in the remarkable story of the attraction and conflict between two so disparate and fervent characters as this pair - into the relations of men and women: "In their relations, she was the giver and he the receiver, nay the demander.

Synset Examples

the tapestry of European history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web - something intricately constructed; a complex arrangement
  • Synonym: fabric - the structure or framework of something

Etymology

From Old French *tapisserie*, from *tapisser* (to cover with carpet), from *tapis* (carpet).
