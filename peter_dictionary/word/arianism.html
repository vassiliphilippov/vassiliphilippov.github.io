Arianism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛːrɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The Christian doctrine, considered heretical, that teaches the Son is subordinate to the Father, originally proposed by Arius in the 4th century.

Examples

"You are to be accused of Arianism to confuse the religious who remain loyal ".

Etymology

From Arius, a 4th-century Christian presbyter, plus -ism.
