conspicuously

1. ADV

/ kənˈspɪk.ju.əs.li / (AmE), / kənˈspɪk.ju.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is easily seen or attracts attention; noticeably.

Examples

The usual congratulatory crowd was conspicuously absent; the place had the air of a morgue.
White shirted and conservatively cravated drivers stared conspicuously toward the eastern horizon and past my supplicating and accusing gaze.

Synset Examples

there have been plenty of general declarations about willingness to meet and talk, but conspicuously no mention of time and place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noticeably - in a way that is easy to see or recognize
  • Antonym: unobtrusively - in a way that does not attract attention

Etymology

From 'conspicuous' + '-ly'. 'Conspicuous' is from Latin 'conspicuus' (visible), from 'conspicere' (to look at).

2. ADV

/ kənˈspɪkjuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈspɪkjuəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is very easy to see or notice; so as to attract attention.

Examples

But the most notable thing about the incantation of these ex liberals was that the one-time shibboleth of socialism was conspicuously absent.
Each was her mother's assistant and confidante; and each stood out conspicuously in the family picture.

Synset Examples

the new car was prominently displayed in the driveway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prominently - in a way that is very noticeable or important

Etymology

From Latin 'conspicuus' (visible, striking) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
