hate

1. VERB

/ heɪt / (AmE), / heɪt / (BrE)
To feel intense or passionate dislike for someone or something.

Examples

I hated the goddamn army from the first day I got in anyhow.
Once covertly looking at Simms Purdew, the only man in the world whom he hated, he had seen the heavy, slack, bestubbled jaw open and close to emit the cruel, obscene banter, and had seen the pale blue eyes go watery with whisky and merriment, and suddenly he was not seeing the face of that vile creature.
In that moment of vision Adam heard the voice within himself saying: I must not hate him, I must not hate him or I shall die.
In that moment of vision Adam heard the voice within himself saying: I must not hate him, I must not hate him or I shall die.
I hate Warsaw, he said to himself.

Synset Examples

I hate Mexican food
She detests politicians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detest - to feel intense dislike or hatred for
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection or liking for someone or something
  • Degree Lower: dislike - to not like someone or something

Etymology

From Old English 'hatian' (to hate), related to Old High German 'hazan.'

2. NOUN

/ heɪt / (AmE), / heɪt / (BrE)
A very strong feeling of dislike or aversion towards someone or something, often accompanied by a desire to avoid, reject, or harm.

Examples

The word also made him feel hate, sincere hate, for those so labeled.
The word also made him feel hate, sincere hate, for those so labeled.
He swung toward the front to give the news to Hettie, then stopped, barred from her by the vehemence of her blame and hate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: love - an intense feeling of deep affection or attachment
  • Degree Lower: dislike - a feeling of mild or moderate aversion

Etymology

From Old English *hatian* (to hate, treat as an enemy), related to Old Norse *hata*.
