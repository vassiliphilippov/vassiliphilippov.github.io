hunker

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə / (BrE)
to squat close to the ground, especially by bending the knees and keeping the heels close to or on the ground

Examples

As for Andy, he spent that break hunkered in the shade... a strange little smile on his face, watching us drink his beer.

Synset Examples

In some cultures, the women give birth while squatting
The children hunkered down to protect themselves from the sandstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - to crouch or sit with the knees bent and the heels close to or touching the buttocks or the back of the thighs

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly of Scottish origin, recorded from the early 18th century.
