espionage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛspiəˌnɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛspɪənɑːʒ / (BrE)
The practice of secretly gathering information about a government, organization, or country, especially for political or military advantage.

Examples

Our weapons production, our world prestige, our ideas of democracy, our actions of trust or stubbornness or secrecy or espionage?
I say we break into the place... ...trash the office... ...and make off with the records and say it was industrial espionage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spying - the act of secretly collecting information; synonym for 'espionage'

Etymology

From French 'espionnage', from 'espionner' (to spy), from 'espion' (spy).
