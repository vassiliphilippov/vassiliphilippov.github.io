interpretation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɚprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of explaining, understanding, or assigning meaning to something; a particular explanation or way of understanding

Examples

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɚprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular way of performing a piece of music, a role in a play, or another artistic work, reflecting the artist's own style or understanding

Examples

Other interpretations present the music as an essentially intimate creation.
This is a serious minded interpretation, but it is never strait-laced.
Every detail in his interpretation has been beautifully thought out, and of these I would especially cite the delicious landler touch the pianist brings to the fifth variation (an obvious indication that he is playing with Viennese musicians), and the gossamer shading throughout.

Synset Examples

her rendition of Milton's verse was extraordinarily moving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendition - a performance or interpretation, especially of music or a dramatic role

Etymology

From Latin interpretatio (explanation, exposition), from interpretari (explain, expound).

an explanation of the meaning of something, especially something that is not immediately obvious

Examples

As an example of the interpretation of an arrow in the figure which exceeds four months in shaft length in conjunction with its position in the figure: girl 2 had a delayed Onset and further delayed Completion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: misinterpretation - an incorrect or mistaken explanation or understanding

Etymology

From Latin interpretatio (an explanation), from interpretari (to explain).
