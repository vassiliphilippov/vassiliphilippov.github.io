lineman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnmæn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnmən / (BrE)
a player positioned on the line of scrimmage in American football or similar sports, responsible for blocking, tackling, or protecting teammates

Examples

Skorich, who is 39 years old, played football at Cincinnati University and then had a three year professional career as a lineman under Jock Sutherland with the Pittsburgh Steelers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: linebacker - defensive player positioned behind the line of scrimmage

Etymology

From 'line' + 'man'—referring to a player who plays on the line.
