forensic

1. ADJ

/ fəˈrɛnsɪk / (AmE), / fəˈrɛnsɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or suitable for public debate or formal argument.

Examples

The merits of the Salk anti polio vaccine were not established on the forensic platform or in newspaper editorials, but in the laboratory and by tests in the field on thousands of children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetorical - relating to the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'forensis' (of a forum or public place).
