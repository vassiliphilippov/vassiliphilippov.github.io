lipped

1. ADJ

/ lɪpt / (AmE), / lɪpt / (BrE)
having a lip or lips; used especially in compounds to describe the shape or form of the lips or similar structures.

Examples

The mouth was thin lipped and wide, the long cleft in the upper lip like a slide.

Synset Examples

a lipped bowl
a virgin purest lipped

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lipless - lacking lips or a lip

Etymology

Formed from 'lip' + '-ed', meaning 'having a lip or lips'.
