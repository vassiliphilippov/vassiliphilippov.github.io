disintegrate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈtɪɡɹeɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtɪɡɹeɪt / (BrE)
To break into parts or components, or to lose integrity, unity, or cohesion; to fall apart.

Examples

If he sees the heroic in a Sartoris or a Sutpen, he sees also - and he shows - the blind and the mean, and he sees the Compson family disintegrating from within.
In my estimation, we definitely do; and the sad part of it is that we had one, which was rounding into excellent shape, and we let it disintegrate and die.

Synset Examples

The material disintegrated
the group disintegrated after the leader died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - break into smaller pieces
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or fall apart suddenly
  • Antonym: integrate - to bring together or combine into a whole

Etymology

From Latin disintegratus, past participle of disintegrare (to destroy the integrity of), from dis- (apart) + integer (whole).
