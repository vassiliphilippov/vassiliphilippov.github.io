henchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛntʃ.mən / (AmE), / ˈhɛntʃ.mən / (BrE)
A faithful follower or supporter, especially one who assists in wrong or criminal activities.

Examples

CICCI: A button, you know, Senator, come on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • Synonym: confederate - an associate in wrongdoing or a partner in crime
  • Degree Higher: ringleader - the leader of a group involved in unlawful activities

Etymology

From Old English hengestman (groom, attendant; literally 'horse servant'), from hengest (stallion, horse) + man (man).
