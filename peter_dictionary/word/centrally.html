centrally

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛntrəli / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəli / (BrE)
in, at, or near a center or in a central position; in a way that is most important or plays a central role

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peripherally - at or near the edge or periphery, as opposed to centrally

Etymology

From 'central', formed from Latin 'centralis', plus '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
