vestibule

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstəˌbjuːl / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪbjuːl / (BrE)
an entrance hall or lobby next to the outer door of a building, often serving as a reception area

Examples

He climbed the steps of the first and opened the door to the vestibule.
He went on to the next building and found what he expected - the mingled cooking aromas of a public vestibule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foyer - an entrance hall or other open area in a building, typically used as a reception area
  • Synonym: lobby - a room providing a space out of which one or more other rooms or corridors lead, typically near the entrance of a public building

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance court).
