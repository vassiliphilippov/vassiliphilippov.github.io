covet

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌvət / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪt / (BrE)
to strongly desire or wish for something, especially something belonging to someone else

Examples

The good ones are not motivated to seek vainly, nor are they disposed to covet comfort, or they would have been led to fields that offer comfort and feed vanity.

Synset Examples

She covets her sister's house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - to want something strongly
  • Synonym: crave - to have an intense desire for something
  • Synonym: envy - to wish you had what someone else has

Etymology

From Old French *covetër*, from Latin *cupere* (to desire).
