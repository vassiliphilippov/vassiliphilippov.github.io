ethic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.ɪk / (BrE)
A set of moral principles or values held by an individual or group, guiding what is considered right and wrong behavior.

Examples

In America also all of our major religious bodies officially sanction a universalistic ethic which is reflective of our common religion.
Harold Clurman is right to say that "Waiting for Godot "is a reflection (he calls it a distorted reflection) "of the impasse and disarray of Europe's present politics, ethic, and common way of life ".

Synset Examples

the Puritan ethic
a person with old-fashioned values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - the branch of philosophy dealing with moral principles; often used as a plural noun for 'moral principles'
  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
  • Antonym: immorality - the state of not conforming to accepted standards of morality

Etymology

From Greek *ēthikos*, relating to character or morals.
