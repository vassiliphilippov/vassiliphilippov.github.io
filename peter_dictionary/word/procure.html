procure

1. VERB

/ proʊˈkjʊr / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊə / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially with effort or by special means

Examples

Hub, nosing about, spotted reporters in the lobby, so Andy was hustled away quietly through the hotel's service entrance in a strange car which Hub had procured somewhere.
In the coming fiscal year additional quantities of Atlas, Titan, and Polaris missiles also will be procured.
It was not very reasonable to believe that Lucien had procured unprocurable opium and come back to Honotassa with a formed plan to murder her.

Synset Examples

He procured extra cigarettes even though they were rationed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquire - to come into the possession of something; obtain
  • Synonym: secure - to obtain something, especially after effort
  • Antonym: lose - to no longer have something because you do not know where it is or you no longer possess it

Etymology

From Latin 'procurare' (to take care of, obtain), via Old French 'procurer'.
