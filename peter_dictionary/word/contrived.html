contrived

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtraɪvd / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvd / (BrE)
deliberately created rather than arising naturally or spontaneously; artificial or forced, often lacking in authenticity or naturalness because of being planned or manipulated.

Examples

Southern California gasped and blinked under an autumn hot spell, drier, more enervating, more laden with man's contrived impurities than the worst days of the summer past.

Synset Examples

a novel with a contrived ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - not natural, made by humans rather than occurring naturally
  • Synonym: forced - not done voluntarily or naturally; produced by compulsion
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humans

Etymology

From the verb 'contrive,' from Old French 'controver' (devise, invent), ultimately from Latin 'contrōvērtere' (to turn against).
