awkward

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkwərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwəd / (BrE)
(of a situation, time, etc.) causing difficulty or inconvenience; not ideal or unsuitable.

Examples

What were they to do, then, with these awkward judgments of value?
It was awkward: very awkward.
It was awkward: very awkward.
It's awkward, you see that, is n't it "?
This is awkward for everybody, is n't it, we all got to put up with inconvenience sometimes.

Synset Examples

they arrived at an awkward time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconvenient - causing difficulty or not suitable
  • Antonym: convenient - easy, suitable or causing no trouble

Etymology

From Middle English awkeward, from awk (meaning 'backhanded, clumsy') + -ward.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔk.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔːk.wəd / (BrE)
Feeling or causing social discomfort; unsure and constrained in manner.

Examples

I feel awkward enough as it is.

Synset Examples

awkward and reserved at parties
ill at ease among eddies of people he didn't know
was always uneasy with strangers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfortable - free from social discomfort or awkwardness

Etymology

From Middle English awkeward, from 'awk' (in the wrong direction) + '-ward'.
