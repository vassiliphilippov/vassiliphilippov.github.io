reception

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
a formal social gathering, especially one held after a ceremony such as a wedding or to welcome someone

Examples

An exhibition of Evelyn Cibula's paintings will open with a reception Nov. 5 at the Evanston Community center, 828 Davis st..
The reception was held in a private dining room of the Webster Hotel on Lincoln Park West.
These were the early departures; in half an hour the reception would be over.
Later on in the evening we'’re all invited to the Presidential Palace reception to bring in the New Year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests

Etymology

From Latin 'receptio' (a receiving), from 'recipere' (to receive).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the way in which someone or something is received or greeted by others

Examples

Even Joan Sutherland may not have anticipated the tremendous reception she received from the Metropolitan Opera audience attending her debut as Lucia in Donizetti's "Lucia di Lammermoor "Sunday night.
The monk who opened the door immediately calmed his worries about his reception: "I speak English ", the old man said, "but I do not hear it very well ".
The riotous onrush of industrialism after the War for Southern Independence and the general secular drift to the Religion of Humanity, however, prepared the way for a reception of the French Revolution's socialistic offspring of one sort of another.

Synset Examples

she did not expect the cold reception she received from her superiors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcome - an instance or manner of greeting someone in a friendly way
  • Antonym: rebuff - an unkind or unfriendly reception; rejection
  • Synonym: greeting - the act or manner of welcoming someone

Etymology

From Latin receptio (a receiving), from recipere (to receive).

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
The quality or fidelity of the signal received by a radio, television, or other broadcasting device.

Examples

Radio reception.
Radio reception is cut down by the shielding necessary to keep out radiation.
As soon as the shelter is completed a radio reception check must be made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - an electromagnetic or electrical transmission conveying information
  • Antonym: transmission - the act of sending a signal or broadcast

Etymology

From Latin receptio (a receiving) via Old French réception.
