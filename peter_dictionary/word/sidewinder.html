sidewinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌwaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌwaɪndə / (BrE)
A small pale-colored rattlesnake of the deserts of the southwestern United States, known for its unique sideways, S-shaped movement.

Examples

She had jumped away from his shy touch like a cat confronted by a sidewinder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - any of various venomous New World snakes having a series of horny interlocking segments at the end of the tail which when vibrated produce a rattling sound

Etymology

Named for its characteristic sideways movement ('sidewinding') across sand.
