balustrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæləˌstreɪd / (AmE), / ˌbæl.əˈstreɪd / (BrE)
A railing supported by balusters, typically found at the edge of a staircase, balcony, or terrace, to prevent people from falling.

Examples

In the Presidential box someone leaned over the balustrade and yelled: "He has shot the President "!
John leaned upon the stone balustrade.
He climbed, as quickly as he could urge his body, up the two unbroken flights to the third floor, pulling himself along on a delicate balustrade, all that remained of the building's beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banister - the handrail of a staircase; synonym for balustrade

Etymology

From French 'balustrade', from Italian 'balaustrata'.
