leakage

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈliːkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the accidental or unintended escape of a liquid or gas from a container or system

Examples

Because of light leakage from one ultraviolet source to another, the lights are switched by a commutator like assembly rotated by a synchronous motor.

Synset Examples

they tried to stop the escape of gas from the damaged pipe
he had to clean up the leak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leak - an unintended hole or crack where liquid or gas escapes

Etymology

From 'leak' + '-age', first seen in English in the 17th century.
