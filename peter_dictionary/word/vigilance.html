vigilance

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdʒələns / (AmE), / ˈvɪdʒɪləns / (BrE)
the action or state of keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties

Examples

Most Jewish mothers are determined to exercise vigilance over the social and sexual lives of their daughters by keeping them home.

Synset Examples

wakefulness, watchfulness, and bellicosity make a good hunter
vigilance is especially susceptible to fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchfulness - the state of closely observing or being alert to danger
  • Synonym: alertness - the quality of being alert or ready to act
  • Antonym: negligence - failure to give proper attention or care

Etymology

From Latin 'vigilantia' (wakefulness, watchfulness), from 'vigilant-' (keeping awake).
