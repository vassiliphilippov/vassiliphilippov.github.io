overburden

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɜrdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdn̩ / (BrE)
to give someone or something more work, responsibility, or weight than they can manage

Examples

The simple mechanical strain of overweight, says New York's Dr. Norman Jolliffe, can overburden and damage the heart "for much the same reason that a Chevrolet engine in a Cadillac body would wear out sooner than if it were in a body for which it was built ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or give a heavy responsibility
  • Synonym: overload - to put too much load, work, or responsibility on
  • Antonym: relieve - to remove a burden from someone

Etymology

From over- (above, too much) + burden (from Old English byrthen, meaning load or weight).
