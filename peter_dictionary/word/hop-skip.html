hop-skip

1. VERB

/ hɑp skɪp / (AmE), / hɒp skɪp / (BrE)
to move forward by making a series of short, light jumps; to jump lightly and quickly

Examples

Within half an hour I jumped a six-point buck that hop-skipped through a rhododendron thicket, and I caught him just behind the left foreleg at 60 yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip - to move along lightly, stepping from one foot to the other with a hop or bounce
  • Degree Higher: leap - to jump or spring a long way, to a great height, or with great force
  • Synonym: hop - to make a short jump or series of jumps on one foot or both feet
