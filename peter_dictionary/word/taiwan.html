Taiwan

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈwɑn / (AmE), / taɪˈwɑːn / (BrE)
An island country in East Asia, officially known as the Republic of China, established a separate government in 1949 after the Chinese civil war.

Examples

At a third stage in the modernization process are such countries as India, Brazil, the Philippines, and Taiwan, which are ready and committed to move into the stage of self-sustaining growth.

Etymology

From Chinese 臺灣 (Táiwān), referring to its indigenous and historical names.
