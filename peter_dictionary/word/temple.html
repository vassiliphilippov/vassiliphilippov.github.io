temple

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl / (BrE)
A building or place dedicated to the worship of a deity or deities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - a Christian place of worship
  • Synonym: mosque - a Muslim place of worship
  • Synonym: synagogue - a Jewish place of worship

Etymology

From Latin 'templum' (a space marked out for observation of auguries, building for worship).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəl / (BrE)
A building or place dedicated to a special or exalted purpose, such as celebrating glory, art, or another high ideal.

Examples

The classical lines of the church which Napoleon thought of as a Temple of Glory, dominated all the scene where we sat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a sacred or safe place, especially for worship or refuge

Etymology

From Latin templum (shrine, sacred space).
