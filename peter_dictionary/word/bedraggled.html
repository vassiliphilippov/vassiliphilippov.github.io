bedraggled

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈdræɡəld / (AmE), / bɪˈdræɡəld / (BrE)
Untidy, dirty, and limp as if having been dragged through mud or water.

Examples

When finally the two bedraggled men reach their friend's home, Voltaire's fears are once again aroused.

Synset Examples

the beggar's bedraggled clothes
scarecrows in battered hats or draggled skirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheveled - untidy or disordered in appearance (especially of hair or clothing)
  • Synonym: unkempt - having an untidy or disheveled appearance
  • Antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean and tidy

Etymology

From prefix 'be-' (thoroughly) + 'draggle' (to make wet and dirty by dragging).
