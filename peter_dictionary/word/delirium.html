delirium

1. NOUN

/ dəˈlɪriəm / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəm / (BrE)
A state of wild excitement, violent agitation, or intense confusion, often accompanied by disordered thinking or hallucinations.

Examples

Here he was, suddenly caught up in the delirium of a war, in the spite and calumny of Whigs and Tories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mania - an excessively intense enthusiasm or desire; frenzy
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and tranquility, free from agitation

Etymology

From Latin delirium (madness), from delirare (to be deranged), originally 'to go off the furrow' (de- + lira, furrow).
