signature

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnətʃə / (BrE)
A person's name written in their own handwriting, typically as a form of identification or authorization.

Examples

In the Blue Ridge meeting, the audience was warned that entering a candidate for governor would force it to take petitions out into voting precincts to obtain the signatures of registered voters.
Grissom's signature is legitimate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autograph - a person's own signature, especially that of a celebrity, written as a memento
  • Antonym: forgery - a false signature or document made to deceive

Etymology

From Medieval Latin signatura (a mark, sign).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡnətʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnətʃə / (BrE)
A distinctive quality, style, or characteristic by which something or someone may be identified.

Examples

However, in all of Prokofieff's music, young or mature, we find his profile - his "signature "- his craftsman's attitude.

Synset Examples

this room needs a woman's touch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallmark - a mark or feature indicating quality or origin
  • Synonym: trademark - a distinctive characteristic or feature

Etymology

From Medieval Latin signatura (a marking), from Latin signare (to sign).
