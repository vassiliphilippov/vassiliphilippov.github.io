Homeric

1. ADJ

/ hoʊˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / həʊˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Homer, his era, or the literary works attributed to him, especially the Iliad and Odyssey.

Examples

At the least, however, one may conclude that Geometric potters sensed a logical order; their principles of composition stand very close to those which appear in the Homeric epics and the hexameter line.

Homeric Greek

Etymology

From Latin Homericus, from Ancient Greek Ὁμηρικός (Homērikós), from Ὅμηρος (Homēros, 'Homer').
