hustle

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
to make someone move quickly and quietly, often in a secretive or hurried manner

Examples

Hub, nosing about, spotted reporters in the lobby, so Andy was hustled away quietly through the hotel's service entrance in a strange car which Hub had procured somewhere.

Synset Examples

The secret service agents hustled the speaker out of the amphitheater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or cause to move with haste
  • Synonym: rush - to move or cause to move with urgent speed

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch husselen (to shake, toss), frequentative of hussen (to shake).

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
to move or cause to move in a hurried or energetic way

Examples

Hustle "!

Synset Examples

The cheerleaders bustled about excitingly before their performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustle - move in an energetic or busy manner

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'husselen' (to shake, toss) or related to 'hustle' as a variant of 'hustle' (push, crowd).
