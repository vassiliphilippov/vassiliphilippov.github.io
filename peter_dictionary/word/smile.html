smile

1. NOUN

/ smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
A facial expression in which the corners of the mouth turn up, usually indicating pleasure, amusement, or friendliness.

Examples

Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.
Teachers - men who wore brown suits and had gray hair and pleasant smiles - came to their table to talk shop and to be introduced to Scotty and Rachel.
Jane nodded with a pleasant smile.
"Connections "was all he would say with that smooth hurt smile when I put leading questions.
"Your voice is delightful ", he approved with a warm smile.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frown - a facial expression in which the eyebrows are brought together, often indicating disapproval or concentration

Etymology

From Middle English *smilen*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Middle Low German *smilen* (to smile).

2. VERB

/ smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
to form one's features into a pleased, kind, or amused expression, typically by turning up the corners of the mouth

Examples

This happy, always smiling lad with the sunny disposition is our new Junior Mr. Canada - Henri de Courcy.
He never smiled.
Smiling at his quixotic thoughts, Warren turned back from the opening and lit a cigarette before sitting down.
He smiled and stuck a large finger with white hairs sprouting on it into his ear as though that might help.
Smiling at Warren's protestations, the old monk took his grip from him and led him down a corridor to a small parlor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frown - to lower the corners of the mouth to show displeasure
  • Degree Higher: grin - to smile broadly, showing the teeth

Etymology

From Middle English smilen, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse smila (to smile), Swedish smila.

3. NOUN

/ smaɪl / (AmE), / smaɪl / (BrE)
A facial expression in which the corners of the mouth are turned up, usually expressing pleasure, amusement, or friendliness.

Examples

"You have made the labor worth while ", he said to her, smiling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frown - a facial expression in which the eyebrows are brought together, showing displeasure or concern

Etymology

From Old English *smīelan* (to smile), related to Middle Low German *smilen*.
