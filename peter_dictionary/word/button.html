button

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtən / (AmE), / ˈbʌt(ə)n / (BrE)
A small device that you press to operate a machine or piece of equipment, such as an electrical switch.

Examples

The speed is controlled by pressing on the two brake buttons located where the index finger and thumb are placed when holding the motor.
We are worried about what people may do with them - that some crazy fool may "push the button ".
Greg pushed the radio button on his throttle.
He pushed the radio button.
Greg pushed the radio button again.

Synset Examples

the elevator was operated by push buttons
the push beside the bed operated a buzzer at the desk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - A device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit
  • Antonym: lever - A device operated by moving a bar or handle

Etymology

From Old French 'boton,' from 'boter' (to push).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtən / (AmE), / ˈbʌt(ə)n / (BrE)
A small, usually round object made of plastic, metal, or other material, sewn onto clothing and pushed through a slit or loop to fasten it.

Examples

But dully glinting on the dark form were the buttons and badge of a policeman.
The night we first met, at one of Mrs. Monmouth's giant parties, he was wearing a brown cashmere jacket with silver buttons and a soft pink Viyella shirt; instead of a necktie he wore a leather bolo drawn through a golden ring in which was set a lump of pale pure jade.
[GAGGING] [SCREAMING] BUTTON MAN: Get his head in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - a fastener that closes by pressing two parts together
  • Degree Higher: zipper - a fastening device using interlocking teeth and a slider

Etymology

From Old French *boton* (a bud, button), from bouter (to thrust, push), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬.ən / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən / (BrE)
A small device that, when pressed, releases or activates part of a mechanism.

Examples

When the boss says "push a button" on a guy, I push a **button**.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit
  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to operate a mechanism

Etymology

From Old French 'bouton' (knob, stud), from 'bouter' (to push).
