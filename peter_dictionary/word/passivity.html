passivity

1. NOUN

/ pæsˈɪvəti / (AmE), / pæˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
A lack of initiative or action; the quality of remaining inactive or submissive.

Examples

But her personality was such that each affair lasted only until that lover, too, had been conquered and reduced to passivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inactivity - the state of being inactive or idle
  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: initiative - the ability to assess and start things independently
  • Synonym: reactivity - the quality of being reactive rather than proactive

Etymology

From French *passivité*, from Late Latin *passivitas*, from *passivus* (suffering, passive).
