hammer

1. VERB

/ ˈhæmər / (AmE), / ˈhamə / (BrE)
to hit or strike something repeatedly and forcefully, especially with a heavy object or as if using a hammer

Examples

Gaines hammered the ball over the left fence for the third Indianapolis run in the ninth.
In the third Frank Robinson hammered a long home run deep into the corner of the bleachers in right center, about 400 feet away, with two men on.
He started to reach for his gun, but apparently hammer it.
Carpenters all wanted steady work and at the moment every mother's son for twenty miles around that could hammer nails for twenty-five dollars a day was working on the school job.
Or maybe you want to hammer somebody's skull.

Synset Examples

hammer the metal flat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - to strike repeatedly with force
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly or gently
  • Degree Higher: smash - to break or crush with violent force

Etymology

Old English *hamor* (hammer), related to Old Norse *hamarr* (hammer, rock),

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæmə / (BrE)
a hand tool with a heavy head attached to a handle, used for hitting nails or breaking things by delivering blows

Examples

Likewise the ivory Chinese female figure known as a "doctor lady " (provenance Honan); a friend of mine removing her from the curio cabinet for inspection was felled as if by a hammer, but he had previously drunk a quantity of applejack.
Lolotte held a patch of leather, Rev steadied something, a tiny brad, waiting for George's poised hammer.
All they found of him was a muddy set of prison clothes, a bar of soap and an old rock hammer damn near worn down to the nub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallet - a tool similar to a hammer but with a large, usually wooden head, used for striking softer materials

Etymology

Old English *hammor*, from Proto-Germanic *hamaraz* (stone, hammer).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhamə / (BrE)
the part of a gunlock that strikes the percussion cap or firing pin when the trigger is pulled, causing the firearm to discharge

Examples

As he crossed to the side of the stall, Curt drew his gun and clicked back the hammer.
