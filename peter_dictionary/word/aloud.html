aloud

1. ADV

/ əˈlaʊd / (AmE), / əˈlaʊd / (BrE)
In a way that can be heard; using the voice, not silently or in the mind.

Examples

"I soon came back to my senses ", he said, aloud, to the young blizzard, proudly, drawing himself up, as if making a report to some important superior.
"I told him ", he said aloud.
"Really something ", he said, aloud.
"Bastards ", he said aloud, spitting on them.
Watson snorted and then laughed aloud.

Synset Examples

please read the passage aloud
he laughed out loud

Related Terms

  • Antonym: silently - without making any sound, not aloud

Etymology

From Middle English 'aloude', combining 'a-' (on, in) with 'loud'.
