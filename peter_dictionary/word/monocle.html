monocle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.kəl / (BrE)
A single round lens used to correct or enhance vision in one eye, held in place by the muscles around the eye socket, often associated with old-fashioned or formal appearance.

Examples

A story, no doubt apocryphal, for Mercer himself denies it, has him sporting a monocle in those Village days.
One day in a bar, so the legend goes, someone put a beer stein with too much force on the monocle and broke it.
The innocent malfeasant, filled with that supreme sense of honor found in bars, insisted upon replacing the destroyed monocle - and did, over the protests of the former owner - with a square monocle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: eyeglasses - pair of lenses for correcting or assisting defective vision, worn on the face
  • Synonym: contact lens - thin lens placed directly on the surface of the eye to correct vision

Etymology

From French *monocle*, from Greek *monos* (single) + *-cle* from Latin *oculus* (eye).
