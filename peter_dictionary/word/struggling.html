struggling

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstrʌɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
experiencing difficulty or hardship, especially in financial or professional terms; trying hard to succeed or survive despite obstacles

Examples

Like many others, he had to work hard, long hours in a struggling family business which, though it was allied to art of a kind - the design and production of engraved seals - bore no relation to the painting of pictures.
Fuzzy caterpillars, snails with their sensitive horns, struggling grasshoppers held by their long hind legs and commanded to "spit tobacco, spit ".
No Jew was on hand to boycott his financially struggling theater.

Synset Examples

a financially struggling theater
struggling artists

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thriving - prosperous and successful
  • Antonym: prosperous - flourishing financially

Etymology

Present participle of 'struggle,' from Middle English *struglen* (to strive or struggle).
