batwing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbætˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
one of a pair of swinging doors, especially those found at the entrance to a western saloon

Examples

Heading for the batwings, Cobb glanced perfunctorily through the nearest window, and suddenly dodged aside.

Etymology

From 'bat' + 'wing', so called for the resemblance of the door's shape or motion to a bat's wing.
