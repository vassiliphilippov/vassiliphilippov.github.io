loon

1. NOUN

/ lun / (AmE), / luÀên / (BrE)
A large diving bird of the northern regions, with webbed feet set far back on the body, known for its distinctive calls and fish-eating habits; related to the grebes.

Examples

The Zeitgeist is not crazy as a loon or mad as a March hare; it is manic as a man ".

Etymology

From Scottish *loon* (wild fowl), exact origin uncertain.
