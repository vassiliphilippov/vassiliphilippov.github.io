microphoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkɹoʊˌfoʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪk.rəʊˌfəʊ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of converting sound waves into electrical signals using a microphone, especially as done in recording or broadcast engineering.

Examples

London explains that the very distinct directional effect in the Phase 4 series is due in large part to their novel methods of microphoning and recording the music on a number of separate tape channels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microphone - an instrument for converting sound waves into electrical energy

Etymology

From microphone + -ing, denoting the process or action involving microphones.
