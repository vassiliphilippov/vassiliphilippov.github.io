certitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.təˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
the state of being completely certain or confident about something

Examples

However, a growing intellectual sophistication and the new certitudes imparted by courses in psychology and anthropology make the students increasingly critical of their somewhat provincial and overprotective parents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the state of being certain; something that is certain
  • Antonym: uncertainty - the state of being uncertain or unsure

Etymology

From Late Latin certitudo (certainty), from Latin certus (sure, certain).
