astronomy

1. NOUN

/ əˈstrɑnəmi / (AmE), / əˈstrɒnəmi / (BrE)
The scientific study of celestial bodies such as stars, planets, and galaxies, and the universe as a whole.

Examples

While Hans devoted himself to the sciences of medicine, physics, and astronomy, his brother studied law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrophysics - branch of astronomy dealing with the physical properties of celestial bodies
  • Synonym: cosmology - the science of the origin and development of the universe
  • Synonym: physics - the natural science that studies matter and energy

Etymology

From Greek 'astronomia', from 'astron' (star) + 'nomos' (law).
