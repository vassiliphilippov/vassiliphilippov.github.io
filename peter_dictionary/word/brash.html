brash

1. ADJ

/ bræʃ / (AmE), / bræʃ / (BrE)
Confident in an aggressive or rude way that is likely to offend people; offensively bold.

Examples

These booze customers had until then been buying their supplies from the Sheldon, Saltis-McErlane, and Druggan-Lake gangs, and now they were competing for trade with the Torrio-Capone saloons; once again O' Banion's brash recklessness had caused a proliferation of ill will.

Synset Examples

a brash newcomer disputed the age-old rules for admission to the club
a nervy thing to say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bold - Willing to take risks; confident and courageous
  • Synonym: pushy - Excessively or unpleasantly self-assertive
  • Antonym: timid - Easily frightened; lacking self-confidence

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Scottish brash (attack, onset), or related to Old Norse brask (haste, impetuosity).
