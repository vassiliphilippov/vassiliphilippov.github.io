parrotlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈpærətˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈpærətˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or behavior) mechanically imitated or repeated without thought or understanding.

Examples

One learns to become alert to changes in his vocal tone - to his voice's suddenly shifting to a quality not like his usual one, a quality which sounds somehow artificial or, in some instances, parrotlike.

Synset Examples

a mere parrotlike word-calling process
a voice quality sounding parrotlike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanical - (of behavior or speech) done without thinking or feeling

Etymology

Formed from 'parrot' + 'like', patterned after the behavior of parrots imitating without understanding.
