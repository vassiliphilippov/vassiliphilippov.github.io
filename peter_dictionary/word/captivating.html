captivating

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæp.tɪˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Charming, holding people's attention as if by magic; extremely interesting or attractive.

Examples

His creative development of melodic designs of Slavic dance tunes and love songs is captivating: witty, clever, adroit, and subtle.

Synset Examples

bewitching smile
Roosevelt was a captivating speaker
enchanting music
an enthralling book
antique papers of entrancing design
a fascinating woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanting - charming in a magical or delightful way
  • Synonym: fascinating - extremely interesting or charming
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'captivus' (captive), from 'capere' (to seize, take).
