phonemics

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈnimɪks / (AmE), / fəˈniːmɪks / (BrE)
The study of the sound system of a specific language, focusing on the analysis and classification of its phonemes.

Examples

Some phonetic features, for example glottal catch or murmur, are sometimes to be assigned to segmental phonemics and sometimes to accentual systems.
His orthographic recommendations are no simplistic acceptance of phonemics on the one hand or of superficiality on the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of sound systems in language
  • Antonym: phonetics - the study of the physical sounds of speech

Etymology

Formed from 'phoneme' (from French *phonème*, from Greek *phōnēma*, sound) + -ics.
