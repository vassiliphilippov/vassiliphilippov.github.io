sickly

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪkli / (AmE), / ˈsɪkli / (BrE)
Having an unhealthy appearance, often looking pale or weak as if suffering from illness.

Examples

It is no harder to raise big, healthy, blooming plants than weak, sickly little things; in fact it is easier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - having good health and not showing signs of illness
  • Synonym: pale - having a lighter or whitish complexion, often due to illness

Etymology

From Middle English sikly, from sik(e) (sick) + -ly.
