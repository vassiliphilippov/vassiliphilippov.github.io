peremptory

1. ADJ

/ pəˈɹɛmptəri / (AmE), / pəˈrɛmpt(ə)ri / (BrE)
(of a person's manner or actions) insisting on immediate attention or obedience, especially in a brusquely authoritative way; offensively self-assured or dictatorial.

Examples

Still existing on a "Northern Union "telegraph form is a typical peremptory message from Peru grocer J. J. Hapgood to Burton and Graves' store in Manchester - "Get and send by stage sure four pounds best Porterhouse or sirloin steak, for Mrs. Hapgood send six sweet oranges ".

Synset Examples

an autocratic person
autocratic behavior
a bossy way of ordering others around
a rather aggressive and dominating character
managed the employees in an aloof magisterial way
a swaggering peremptory manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - exercising absolute power or authority
  • Synonym: bossy - fond of giving people orders; domineering
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority of others; meek
  • Synonym: authoritative - commanding and self-confident; likely to be respected

Etymology

From Latin 'peremptorius', from 'perimere' (to take entirely, destroy), with the sense shifting to 'decisive, absolute'.
