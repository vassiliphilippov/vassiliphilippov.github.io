shackle

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃækəl / (AmE), / ˈʃækəl / (BrE)
A metal fastening, usually one of a pair, for securing the wrists or ankles of a prisoner; often used figuratively to refer to something that limits freedom.

Examples

This tragic lad had forged his own shackles.
It was fantastic to turn from the seven men in shackles to the wardroom, where a class of apprentices awaited him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain - a series of linked metal rings used for restraining or fastening

Etymology

From Old English sceacol (fetter, chain), related to Middle Dutch schakel (link, shackle).
