gild

1. VERB

/ ɡɪld / (AmE), / ɡɪld / (BrE)
to cover with or as if with a thin layer of gold; to give a golden or attractive appearance to

Examples

Set the apples in the pastry-lined tin, spread over them three tablespoonfuls of softened butter, with as much brown sugar, a sprinkling of nutmeg, and a fresh bay leaf, then lay on a cover of pastry, and gild it with beaten yolk of egg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold-plate - to cover a surface with a thin layer of gold

Etymology

From Old English gyldan, from gold (gold) + -an (verbal suffix).
