expulsion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌlʃən / (BrE)
The act of forcing someone or something to leave a place, organization, or position.

Examples

If any had escaped expulsion by hiding, they certainly would not frequent the market-place.

Synset Examples

the ejection of troublemakers by the police
the child's expulsion from school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ejection - the act of being forced out
  • Antonym: admission - the act of allowing entry

Etymology

From Latin expulsio (driving out), from expellere (to drive out).
