sardine

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdin / (AmE), / sɑːˈdiːn / (BrE)
a small, oily fish of the herring family, commonly canned and eaten as food

Examples

A quarter inch this way or that for the hardbake, or the toffee, or the barley sugar, or the sardines, or the bitters, or the condensed milk, or the stationery, or the needles - what could it mean?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - a small, strongly flavored fish, often preserved and used as food
  • Synonym: herring - a larger, silvery fish related to the sardine

Etymology

From French 'sardine', originally from Latin 'sardina', from Sardinia, an island in the Mediterranean where these fish were once abundant.
