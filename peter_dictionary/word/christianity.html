Christianity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstʃiˈænəti / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstiˈænəti / (BrE)
The religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ, emphasizing belief in one God and following the Bible, particularly the New Testament, as its sacred scripture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Islam - the religion of Muslims, based on the teachings of Muhammad
  • Antonym: Judaism - the monotheistic religion of the Jewish people

Etymology

From Latin 'Christianitas' (Christian character, faith), from 'Christianus' (Christian).
