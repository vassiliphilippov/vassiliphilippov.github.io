purloin

1. VERB

/ pərˈlɔɪn / (AmE), / pəˈlɔɪn / (BrE)
to steal something, especially in a secret or sneaky way

Examples

Less respect for the legal conventions was displayed by Castro's right hand man, Che Guevara, who edified the Inter-American Economic and Social council meeting in Montevideo by reading two secret American documents purloined from the United States embassy at Caracas, Venezuela.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
  • Synonym: pilfer - to steal things of little value, especially repeatedly

Etymology

From Old French 'purloigner' (to remove, to put at a distance), from 'pur-' (away) + 'loing' (far), from Latin 'longus' (long).
