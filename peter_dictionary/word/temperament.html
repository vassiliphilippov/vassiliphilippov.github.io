temperament

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmprəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
A person's usual mood or disposition, especially as it affects their behavior or response to situations.

Examples

As for himself, he just did n't have the temperament for it.

Synset Examples

he has a happy disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disposition - a person's inherent qualities of mind and character
  • Synonym: personality - the combination of characteristics or qualities forming an individual's character

Etymology

From Latin temperamentum (proper mixture), from temperare (to mix in due proportion).
