learner

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnə / (BrE)
A person who is learning a subject or skill, especially through study or experience.

Examples

Second, vocational courses should be provided in grades 11 and 12 and not require more than half the student's time in those years; however, for slow learners and prospective dropouts these courses ought to begin earlier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person formally engaged in learning, especially in a school or college

Etymology

From Middle English 'lerner', from 'learn' + -er.
