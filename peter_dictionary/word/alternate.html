alternate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔl.tɚ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.neɪt / (BrE)
(of two or more things) occurring or arranged in turns; every other (such as every second day or item in a sequence).

Examples

As you can see, in this Push-Pull Super Set the entire chest back shoulder area is vigorously exercised in alternate sectors by alternate exercises, so the complete torso remains pumped-up all the time!

Squat style lifters and leg split lifters would both benefit enormously by practicing those variations providing that they remember to make alternate sets with the left and right leg to the front.

Synset Examples

the cleaning lady comes on alternate Wednesdays
jam every other day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: every other - every second one in a sequence
  • Antonym: successive - following one after the other (not skipping)

Etymology

From Latin 'alternatus', past participle of 'alternare' (to do by turns), from 'alternus' (one after the other).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔl.tɚ.nət / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.nət / (BrE)
(of two things) happening or following one after the other repeatedly, taking turns; each happening or appearing by turns.

Examples

That keeps in the cold, retains moisture and prevents the heaving of alternate freezing and thawing.

Synset Examples

alternating feelings of love and hate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternating - occurring in succession, one after the other

Etymology

From Latin alternatus, past participle of alternare 'to do by turns', from alternus 'one after the other'.

3. VERB

/ ˈɔl.tɚˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.təˌneɪt / (BrE)
to go back and forth between two or more states, conditions, or actions, often in a repeated pattern

Examples

Microscopically, there were many areas of hypercellularity alternating with areas of hypocellularity.
fearful of being mistaken for a Jew, he seeks protection in his Nazi uniform; clinging to the enemy Jew idea, he is forced to overcome habits of politeness and neighborliness; once in power he begins to give vent to a criminal opportunism that causes him to alternate between megalomania and envy of those above him.
Dances alternated with sung or spoken verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillate - to move or swing back and forth in a regular rhythm
  • Synonym: fluctuate - to rise and fall irregularly in number or amount

Etymology

From Latin alternare, from alternus (one after the other).

4. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚnət / (AmE), / ˈɔːltənət / (BrE)
(of something) serving as a replacement or substitute; able to be used in place of another.

Examples

An alternate roof, perhaps more within do-it-yourself reach, could be constructed of heavy wooden roof beams, overlaid with boards and waterproofing.
An alternate hatchway entrance, shown on page 25, would reduce the cost of materials $ 50 to $ 100.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From Latin 'alternatus', past participle of 'alternare' (to do by turns), from 'alternus' (one after the other).
