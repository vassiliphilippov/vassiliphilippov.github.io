grapevine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪpˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpˌvaɪn / (BrE)
an informal means of spreading information, gossip, or rumors by word of mouth

Examples

Love and hatred and fear were one here, shaded only by fig trees and grape vines.
But as November 1924 drew close the Democratic hierarchy was sorely troubled by grapevine reports that O' Banion was being wooed by the opposition, and was meeting and conferring with important Republicans.
Not that he gave a damn what the grapevine sent out about Killpath's little speech on the comportment of platoon commanders.

Synset Examples

the news of their affair was spread by word of mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumor - unverified information spread informally
  • Synonym: gossip - informal communication about other people, often personal or sensational

Etymology

Originally referred to the plant; figurative use derives from the Civil War era phrase 'heard it through the grapevine', alluding to the twisted, indirect nature of such communication.
