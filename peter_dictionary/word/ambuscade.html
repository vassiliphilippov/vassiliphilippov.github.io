ambuscade

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbəˈskeɪd / (AmE), / ˌambʊˈskeɪd / (BrE)
an act of lying in wait and concealing oneself in order to make a surprise attack; ambush

Examples

His friends advised that it would be only a question of time until either the Mexicans killed him by ambuscade or he would be compelled to kill them in self-defense, perpetuating the troubles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - an act of attacking unexpectedly from a concealed position

Etymology

From French *ambuscade*, from Italian *imboscata* (ambush).
