maddening

1. ADJ

/ ˈmædənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmadənɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely annoying; making someone feel very angry or frustrated.

Examples

Otherwise, she would be baited into a tantrum - teased and provoked until she lost control of herself, and thus lost still another battle in the maddening struggle of Tom Lord Vs. Joyce Lakewood.

Synset Examples

his cavelier curtness of manner was exasperating
I've had an exasperating day
her infuriating indifference
the ceaseless tumult of the jukebox was maddening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exasperating - causing intense irritation or annoyance
  • Synonym: infuriating - making someone extremely angry
  • Degree Lower: annoying - causing irritation or bother

Etymology

From verb 'madden' (to make mad) + adjective-forming suffix '-ing'.
