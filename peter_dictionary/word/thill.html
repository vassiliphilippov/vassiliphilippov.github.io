thill

1. NOUN

/ θɪl / (AmE), / θɪl / (BrE)
one of the two shafts extending on either side of a vehicle, such as a cart or carriage, to which a draft animal is harnessed

Examples

They were held together by pegs and withes and in later times drawn by a single ox in thills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaft - a long, narrow part or handle of something, especially part of a vehicle

Etymology

From Old English *þille* (the pole or shaft of a vehicle).
