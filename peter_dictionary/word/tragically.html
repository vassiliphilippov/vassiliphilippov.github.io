tragically

1. ADV

/ ˈtrædʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that involves or causes great sadness, suffering, or disaster; with tragic consequences.

Examples

Max, in a fit of despair, takes Alicia and runs off for two marvelous weeks in Burbank (Fink calls it "the most wonderful and lovely fourteen days in my whole life "), at the end of which Alicia tragically contracts Parkinson's disease and dies.

Synset Examples

the adventure ended tragically
tragically, she contracted AIDS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadly - in a sad or sorrowful manner
  • Antonym: comically - in a comical or humorous manner

Etymology

From 'tragic' + '-ally'; 'tragic' from Latin 'tragicus', from Greek 'tragikos'.
