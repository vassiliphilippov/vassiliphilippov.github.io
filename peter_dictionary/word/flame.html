flame

1. NOUN

/ fleɪm / (AmE), / fleɪm / (BrE)
The visible, hot, glowing part of a fire, produced by the combustion of materials and emitting heat and light.

Etymology

From Old French *flame*, from Latin *flamma* (flame, blaze).
