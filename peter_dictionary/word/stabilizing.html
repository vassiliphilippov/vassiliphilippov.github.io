stabilizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
having the effect of making something firm, steady, or unlikely to change suddenly; causing to become stable.

Examples

Religion usually exercises a stabilizing conserving function.

Synset Examples

the family is one of the great stabilizing elements in society

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destabilizing - making something unstable or less steady

Etymology

From 'stabilize' + '-ing', derived from French *stabiliser*, from Latin *stabilis* (stable, firm).
