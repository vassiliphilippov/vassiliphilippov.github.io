wryly

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪli / (AmE), / ˈraɪli / (BrE)
In a way that shows dry, especially mocking, humor; with an expression of irony or sardonic amusement.

Examples

"So that's sculpture ", commented Argiento wryly, when he had sluiced down the floor for a week, "making mud pies ".
A fourteen year old girl from the Middle West observed wryly that, in her community, religion inconveniently interfered with religious activities - at least with the peripheral activities that many middle class Jews now regard as religious.

Synset Examples

`I see,' he commented wryly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironically - in an ironic manner; with irony
  • Synonym: sardonically - in a sardonic manner; grimly mocking or cynical

Etymology

Formed from 'wry' (twisted, distorted) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Wry' from Old English 'wrīgian'.
