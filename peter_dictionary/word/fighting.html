fighting

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of military forces, units, personnel, etc.) engaged in or equipped and ready for battle or combat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of engaging in physical conflict or struggle; violent confrontation or combat between individuals or groups.

Examples

Trig and a very black colored boy from Detroit had killed or put out of action ten guerrillas by grenades and hand-to-hand fighting.
From then on the Fighting Seventh was in the thick of the bitterest fighting in Korea.
Phil Rossoff and two other Riverside players did not go out on the field when the fighting started.
"Listen, wise guy, if you think I 'm gonna do all the fighting for this ball club you 're crazy ".

Synset Examples

a fight broke out at the hockey game
there was fighting in the streets
the unhappy couple got into a terrible scrap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combat - fighting between armed forces
  • Synonym: battle - a sustained fight between large organized armed forces
  • Antonym: peace - absence of conflict or fighting

Etymology

Present participle of 'fight', from Old English 'feohtan' (to fight), of Germanic origin.
