gun

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌn / (AmE), / ɡʌn / (BrE)
a weapon that fires bullets or other projectiles from a tube by the force of an explosive or compressed gas

Examples

Many local citizens feared that there would be irregularities at the polls, and Williams got himself a permit to carry a gun and promised an orderly election.
As the civic temper rises, the more naive citizens begin to play soldier - but the guns are real.
Instead, he whirled and ran to his house for a gun forcing them to kill him, Cook reported.
That was when the gun went off.
Guns were going off all over Washington City these days, because of the celebrations, and the theatre was not soundproof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firearm - a portable gun, especially one using cartridges
  • Degree Lower: pistol - a small gun held in one hand
  • Degree Higher: rifle - a long gun designed to be fired from the shoulder
  • Synonym: weapon - an object used to inflict harm or damage

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'gonne', possibly related to the female given name 'Gunnhildr' and later associated with cannons.
