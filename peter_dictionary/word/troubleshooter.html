troubleshooter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to identify and solve problems, especially in machinery, equipment, or organizational systems

Examples

George Woodcock was manager and troubleshooter; Elizabeth Way was the first operator; and a night operator was also employed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixer - a person who solves problems, especially by intervention

Etymology

Formed from 'trouble' + 'shooter', originally used in the context of telecommunications and engineering in the early 20th century.
