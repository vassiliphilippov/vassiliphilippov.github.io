verisimilitude

1. NOUN

/ vɛrɪˈsɪmɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪsɪˈmɪlɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the quality of appearing to be true or real; the appearance of truth in something, such as a work of fiction or art

Examples

Although lacking absolute verisimilitude, they supply the ear and the imagination with all necessary materials for re-creation of the original.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - representation of things in a way that is true to life

Etymology

From Latin verisimilitudo, from verus (true) + similitudo (likeness).
