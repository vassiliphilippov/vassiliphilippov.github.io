acquired

1. ADJ

/ əˈkwaɪɚd / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪəd / (BrE)
(of a characteristic, trait, or condition) developed or obtained after birth as a result of external influences, rather than being inherited or innate.

Examples

First, the student trains on his hapless parents the heavy artillery of his newly acquired psychological and sociological insights.

Synset Examples

acquired characteristics (such as a suntan or a broken nose) cannot be passed on

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inherited - (of a trait or condition) existing at birth, passed genetically from parents

Etymology

From acquire + -ed, meaning that which has been obtained.
