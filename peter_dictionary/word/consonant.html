consonant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
A speech sound produced by partly or completely obstructing the flow of air through the vocal tract, unlike a vowel.

Examples

It is obvious enough that linguists in general have been less successful in coping with tone systems than with consonants or vowels.
We should expect that general phonologic theory should be as adequate for tone as for consonants and vowels, but it has not been.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vowel - a speech sound produced without significant constriction of airflow

Etymology

From Latin 'consonans', present participle of 'consonare' (to sound together).
