basophilic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbeɪsəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌbeɪsəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
Staining readily with basic dyes, especially in biological tissue samples.

Examples

Many of the myocardial fibers were hypertrophied and had large, irregular, basophilic nuclei.
Occasionally there were small basophilic fibers that were devoid of myofibrillae and contained many vesicular nuclei with prominent nucleoli (Fig. 5).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidophilic - staining readily with acid dyes

Etymology

From base + -philic, from Greek philia (loving).
