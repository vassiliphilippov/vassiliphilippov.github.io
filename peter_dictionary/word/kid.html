kid

1. NOUN

/ kɪd / (AmE), / kɪd / (BrE)
a child or young person

Examples

"I think the big thing about the game was that our kids for the third straight week stayed in there pitching and kept the pressure on.
Stengel probably preached too much in the early days when the kid wanted to pop his bubble gum and sow his oats.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: child - a young person not yet an adult
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown person

Etymology

Originally meant 'young goat,' from Old Norse 'kith'. Later transferred to mean 'child' in early 16th century slang.

  • Synonym: tease - make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful way

Originally slang, probably from earlier slang noun 'kid' (child), with the sense of fooling or playing tricks as children do.
