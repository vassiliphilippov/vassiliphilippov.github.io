elongated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɔŋɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having a shape that is much longer than it is wide; long and slender

Examples

Styles run the gamut from slender and tapered with elongated toes to a newer squared toe shape.

Synset Examples

an elongate tail tapering to a point
the old man's gaunt and elongated frame

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: long - measuring a great distance from end to end

Etymology

From Latin 'elongatus', past participle of 'elongare' (to lengthen, to remove to a distance), from 'e-' (out) + 'longus' (long).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɔŋˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒŋˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having been made longer or extended in length; stretched out.

Examples

I looked back at pale ovals framed in the elongated oval of the car's rear window.

Synset Examples

Picasso's elongated Don Quixote
lengthened skirts are fashionable this year
the extended airport runways can accommodate larger planes
a prolonged black line across the page

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthened - made longer
  • Synonym: extended - made longer or larger in space or time
  • Synonym: prolonged - extended in space or time beyond the usual

Etymology

From Latin 'elongare' (to remove to a distance, lengthen), from 'e-' (out) + 'longus' (long).
