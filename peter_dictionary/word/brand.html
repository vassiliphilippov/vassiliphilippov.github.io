brand

1. NOUN

/ brænd / (AmE), / brænd / (BrE)
a mark burned into the skin of an animal or (historically) a person, used as an identification or as a sign of ownership or punishment

Examples

She held Jonathan's letter, his words burning like a brand, and knew suddenly that the bonds between them were severed.
The Brannon outfit - known as the Slash-B because of its brand - reached Hondo Creek before sundown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - a visible impression or stain on a surface

Etymology

From Old English *brand* (fire, torch), related to Old Norse *brandr* (firebrand).

2. NOUN

/ brænd / (AmE), / brænd / (BrE)
A name given to a product or service, especially one produced by a particular company, used to identify and distinguish it from competitors.

Examples

In recent years gagwriters have discovered this brand of blunder and thus the misplaced modifier has acquired a new habitat in the gagline.
As mass dealer and distributor organizations grow in size, there is every reason to expect them to try to share in the manufacturer's as well as the distributor's profits - which is, in effect, what the sale of private brands tends to do.

3. NOUN

/ brænd / (AmE), / brænd / (BrE)
a particular kind, type, or variety of something, especially one that can be distinguished from others

Examples

Another brand of indefinite reference arises out of the use of the double verb.
Salesmanship is still necessary, but it's a different brand of salesmanship.
Jury, judge and executioner were riding the range in the form of a single unknown figure that could materialize anywhere, at any time, to dispense an ancient brand of justice the men of the new West had believed long outdated.

Synset Examples

there's a new brand of hero in the movies now
what make of car is that?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type - a category of people or things having common characteristics
  • Synonym: sort - a category or kind
  • Synonym: make - brand or manufacturer (as in cars)
  • Synonym: variety - a different kind, a sort

Etymology

From Old English 'brand' (burning, sword), later extended to mean 'mark', then figuratively as 'type'.

4. VERB

/ brænd / (AmE), / brænd / (BrE)
to mark an animal, object, or sometimes a person with a hot iron to indicate ownership or identify

Examples

He had the black numerals on his arm, so he had been branded in a concentration camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - to mark or damage the skin permanently

Etymology

From Old English *brand* (a burning, firebrand, sword), from Proto-Germanic *brandaz* (fire, burning, sword).
