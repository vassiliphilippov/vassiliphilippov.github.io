hack

1. NOUN

/ hæk / (AmE), / hæk / (BrE)
A person who does routine or uninspired work, especially one who works hard at dull or menial tasks; often implies lack of originality or creativity.

Examples

Nor will the hack, the Jack-of-all-trades, still found in some of the smaller art schools, suffice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudge - someone who does tedious, menial, or unpleasant work
  • Synonym: grind - a person who works hard at boring or routine tasks, especially a student
  • Antonym: innovator - a person who introduces new ideas or methods

Etymology

Originally from Old English *haccian* (to hack, cut); the figurative sense as a dull worker arose in the 18th century.

2. VERB

/ hæk / (AmE), / hæk / (BrE)
to cut or chop forcefully with repeated irregular blows, especially using a heavy tool

Examples

Payne hacked at their arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chop - to cut into pieces with a single sharp blow
  • Synonym: slash - to cut forcefully with a sweeping stroke
  • Antonym: dab - to touch lightly and quickly

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *haccian* (to hack, chop).
