marrowbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛroʊˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈmærəʊˌbəʊn / (BrE)
A bone containing edible marrow, often used as food or to flavor soups and stews.

Examples

Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone marrow - the soft fatty substance inside bones

Etymology

marrow + bone; from Old English 'mærh', meaning marrow, and 'bān', meaning bone.
