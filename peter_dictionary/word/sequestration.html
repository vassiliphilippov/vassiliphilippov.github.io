sequestration

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːkwəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsiːkwɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of isolating or separating someone or something from others; segregation

Examples

The doctor was wearing a long New England greatcoat, hardly necessary in the June weather but a garment which proved well adapted to the sequestration of hens.

Synset Examples

sequestration of the jury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: segregation - the act of separating people or things from a larger group
  • Antonym: integration - the act of combining or mixing people or things previously set apart

Etymology

From Latin sequestratio, from sequestrare (to set aside, surrender).
