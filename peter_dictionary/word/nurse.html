nurse

1. VERB

/ nɜrs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
to take care of an illness or injury, treating it carefully to help it heal

Examples

Ramsey has a thing or two to mutter about himself, for the Dallas defensive unit turned in another splendid effort against Denver, and the Texans were able to whip the dangerous Broncs without the fullbacking of a top star, Jack Spikes, though he did the team's place-kicking while nursing a knee injury.

Synset Examples

He nursed his cold with Chinese herbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treat - to try to cure or alleviate a health problem through medical attention

Etymology

From Old French *nourice*, based on Latin *nutrire* (to nourish).

2. NOUN

/ nɝs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
a woman, especially in the past, employed to look after children in a household

Examples

She had even steeled herself to keep Juanita upstairs in the nurse's room off the empty nursery, although the girl tried to insist on moving back to the quarters to spare Kate remembrance of the baby's death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanny - a woman employed to take care of children in a household

Etymology

From Old French *nurrice*, from Late Latin *nutricia* (nurse, wet nurse), from Latin *nutrix* (nurse, wet nurse).

3. NOUN

/ nɝs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
A person trained and skilled in caring for the sick, injured, or young people, usually under the supervision of a doctor.

Examples

Can your plant nurse be replaced by a trained first-aid man who works full-time on some other assignment?

4. VERB

/ nɝs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
To hold onto or foster a feeling, idea, or grievance, often over a period of time.

Examples

Still nursing anger I listlessly thumbed a car that was slowly approaching, its pre-war chrome nearly blinding me.

Synset Examples

bear a grudge
entertain interesting notions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor - to keep (a thought or feeling) in one's mind, especially secretly
  • Synonym: entertain - to keep a thought or idea in one's mind
  • Antonym: let go - to stop holding onto a feeling or idea

Etymology

From Old French *norrice*, from Late Latin *nutricia* (nurse), from Latin *nutrire* (to nourish).

5. VERB

/ nɝs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
to care for something attentively in order to promote its well-being or recovery

Examples

Unless you want to make your wife a pool widow and to spend a great many of your leisure hours nursing your pool's pristine purity, its care and feeding - from pH content to filtering and vacuuming - is best left to a weekly or bi-monthly professional service.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tend - to look after or care for something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for something properly

Etymology

From Old French *nourice*, based on Latin *nutrire* (to nourish).

6. VERB

/ nɝs / (AmE), / nɜːs / (BrE)
to care for someone who is ill or injured, especially as a nurse in a professional or nurturing capacity

Examples

Related Terms
