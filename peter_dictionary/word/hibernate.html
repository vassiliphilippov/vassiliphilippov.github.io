hibernate

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪbərˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪbəneɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to spend the winter in a dormant or inactive state to conserve energy.

Examples

Apart from the honeybee, practically all bees and bumblebees hibernate in a state of torpor.
The life history of the alkali bee is similar to that of Andrena, but the first activity of the adults does not take place until summer, and the individuals hibernate in the prepupal stage.

Synset Examples

Bears must eat a lot of food before they hibernate in their caves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: estivate - to spend a hot or dry period in a dormant state

Etymology

From Latin hibernare (to spend the winter), from hībernus (of winter).
