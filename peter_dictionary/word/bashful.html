bashful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæʃ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈbæʃ.fəl / (BrE)
Shy and easily embarrassed; self-consciously timid.

Examples

Seems like she's willing, but the male just flops around all day like the bashful boy who took Jeannie May behind the barn and then did n't know what to do, and the people at the zoo have n't got any vulture chicks to show for their trouble.

Synset Examples

I never laughed, being bashful; lowering my head, I looked at the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others
  • Antonym: bold - showing courage or confidence; not afraid to take risks

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *bæscan* (to be abashed) + -ful.
