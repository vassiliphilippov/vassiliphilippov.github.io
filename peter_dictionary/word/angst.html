angst

1. NOUN

/ ɑŋst / (AmE), / æŋst / (BrE)
A deep, often philosophical anxiety or dread, especially about the human condition or one's personal freedom.

Examples

In our present era of Science and Angst, the heart has been downgraded, to use one of our popular retrogressive verbs ".
In our age of Science and Angst it seems to me more brave to stay on Earth and explore inner man than to fly far from the sphere of our sorrow and explore outer space ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - A feeling of worry or unease, typically about an imminent event or something with an uncertain outcome
  • Synonym: worry - A state of being anxious or troubled about actual or potential problems

Etymology

From German 'Angst' meaning 'fear' or 'anxiety.'
