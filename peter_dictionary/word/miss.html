Miss

1. NOUN

/ mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
A title or form of address for an unmarried woman or girl.

Examples

I said, "Do we know each other, Miss "?
Man, you rang - it was in color, too, Miss, and & & & Miss?
Man, you rang - it was in color, too, Miss, and & & & Miss?

Synset Examples

a young lady of 18

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ms - title for a woman, regardless of marital status
  • Antonym: Mrs - title for a married woman

Etymology

From Middle English 'mistress', later shortened to 'Miss'.

2. NOUN

/ mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
A title used before the surname or full name of an unmarried woman.

Examples

Well, Miss Vale... ...another rooster in the hen-house?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ms - title for a woman regardless of marital status
  • Antonym: Mrs - title for a married woman

Etymology

From French 'mademoiselle' (young lady), shortened form of 'mistress'.
