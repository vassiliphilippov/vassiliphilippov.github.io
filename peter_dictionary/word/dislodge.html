dislodge

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈlɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒ / (BrE)
to remove or force something or someone from a fixed or settled position

Examples

The figure was close enough now for him to see the nose twitching to dislodge the drop clinging there.
Only in one instance have we moved purposively and effectively to dislodge existing Communist power:

Synset Examples

The dentist dislodged the piece of food that had been stuck under my gums
He finally could free the legs of the earthquake victim who was buried in the rubble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from its place or position
  • Synonym: displace - to move something or someone from its usual or proper place

Etymology

From Old French *deslogier* (to drive from one's dwelling), from *des-* (dis-) + *logier* (to lodge).
