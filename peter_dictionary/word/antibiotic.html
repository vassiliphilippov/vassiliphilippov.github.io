antibiotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.baɪˈɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.baɪˈɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of antibiotic drugs, especially in describing substances, actions, or properties involving antibiotics.

Examples

It is possible that certain mutational forms may be produced such as antibiotic resistant strains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug used to treat bacterial infections

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + bios (life) + -tic, as in 'antibiotic' (noun).
