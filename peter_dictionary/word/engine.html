engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A person, organization, or thing that acts as a driving force or instrument to bring about a result or achieve a particular purpose.

Examples

Historically, religion has also functioned as a tremendous engine of vindication, enforcement, sanction, and perpetuation of various other institutions.

Synset Examples

an engine of change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instrument - a means by which something is achieved

Etymology

From Old French engin (skill, device), from Latin ingenium (innate quality, talent, device).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
(historical or technical) A railway vehicle consisting of a self-propelled engine used to pull trains along railway tracks; a locomotive.

Examples

He was torn between the excitement in the sun inflamed waters and a little engine chugging northward on the Monticello Branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - A powered railway vehicle used for pulling trains

Etymology

From Latin 'ingenium' (invention, device, engine), via Old French and Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A machine that converts energy, especially thermal energy, into mechanical work, such as the device in vehicles that makes them move.

Examples

Finding the key under his shoe, he started the engine, and while it warmed up he turned to her standing there in the dripping fog, and said, "Defrost the refrigerator ".
However, I am recommending that development work on the B-70 air-frame and engines be continued.
At the prearranged time, Greg started the engine and taxied out.
The control tower gave him immediate take-off permission, and the clean roar of the engine that took him off the rough strip spoke well of the skill of Donovan.
Greg slammed his throttle to the fire wall and rammed up the RPM, and the engine responded as if it had been waiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motor - machine that produces motion or power for doing work

Etymology

From Old French 'engin' (skill, device), from Latin 'ingenium' (native quality, invention).
