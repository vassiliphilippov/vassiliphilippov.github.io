numb

1. ADJ

/ nʌm / (AmE), / nʌm / (BrE)
Deprived of physical sensation or the ability to feel, typically as a result of cold or injury.

Examples

writes: What makes my hands numb when sewing?
Anita put out the remaining candles with a long snuffer, and in the smell of scented candlewick, the comforting awareness of each other's bodies, the retained pattern of dancers and guests remembered, their minds grew numb and then empty of images.

Synset Examples

my foot is asleep
numb with cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asleep - having temporarily lost sensation (as of a limb)
  • Antonym: sensitive - having a quick or strong physical response to stimuli

Etymology

From Middle English *nome* (taken, seized), past participle of *nim* (to take), from Old English *niman*.
