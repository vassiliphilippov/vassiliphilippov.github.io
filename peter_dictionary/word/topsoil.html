topsoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑpˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
The uppermost layer of soil, rich in organic material and nutrients, in which most plants grow.

Examples

Check the elevation of the ground, degree and direction of slopes, drainage, rock outcrops, topsoil types and quality, as well as subsoil.

Etymology

From 'top' + 'soil', describing its position as the upper layer.
