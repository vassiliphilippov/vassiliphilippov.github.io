duke

1. NOUN

/ duk / (AmE), / djuÀêk / (BrE)
A male nobleman of the highest hereditary rank, especially in the British peerage system, ranking just below the monarch.

Examples

True, the pattern and flow of the drama have strong literary qualities that are a bit wearisome in the first half, before Don Quixote goes to the duke's court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchess - the wife or widow of a duke or a woman holding the rank of duke in her own right
  • Degree Lower: marquess - a nobleman ranking below a duke and above an earl

Etymology

From Old French *duc*, from Latin *dux* (leader, commander).
