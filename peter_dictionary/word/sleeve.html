sleeve

1. NOUN

/ sliːv / (AmE), / sliːv / (BrE)
The part of a piece of clothing that covers all or part of the arm.

Examples

She wiped it off with the sleeve of her coat.
When it was worn in the breast, or even on the sleeve, we at least knew where it was ".

Etymology

Old English *slēfe*, related to Old Norse *slīfa* (sleeve), of Germanic origin.
