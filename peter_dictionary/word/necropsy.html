necropsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛˌkrɑpsi / (AmE), / ˈniːkrɒpsi / (BrE)
An examination and dissection of a dead body, especially to determine the cause of death or the effects of disease.

Examples

Although biopsies have shown structural changes in some of the reported cases of steroid induced weakness, this case provides the only example known to us in which necropsy afforded the opportunity for extensive study of multiple muscle groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autopsy - examination of a dead body to determine cause of death

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body) + -opsy (to view).
