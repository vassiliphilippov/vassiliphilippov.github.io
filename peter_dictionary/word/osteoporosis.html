osteoporosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiəpəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊpəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which bones become weak and brittle due to a loss of bone tissue, often linked to a deficiency of calcium, and most common in postmenopausal women.

Examples

Extensive osteoporosis with partial collapse of D8 was found.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek osteon (bone) + poros (pore) + -osis (condition).
