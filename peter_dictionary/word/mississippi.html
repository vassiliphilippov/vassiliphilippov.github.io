Mississippi

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi / (BrE)
the major river of the United States, flowing from northern Minnesota south to the Gulf of Mexico

Examples

Etymology

From Ojibwe 'Misi-ziibi,' meaning 'Great River.'

2. PROPN

/ ˌmɪs.ɪˈsɪp.i / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ɪˈsɪp.i / (BrE)
A state in the southern United States, bordering the Gulf of Mexico, known for its historical significance and as one of the Confederate States during the American Civil War.

Examples

A Mississippi soldier wrote: "Our General Reub Davis is a vain, stuck-up, illiterate ass ".

Etymology

From French 'Mississippi,' from an Ojibwa (Algonquian) word 'Misi-ziibi' meaning 'great river.'
