nubbin

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌb.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈnʌb.ɪn / (BrE)
A small lump or projection; especially, an undeveloped fruit or ear, such as in corn.

Examples

After selecting a sheet and inspecting it for flaws (even the best sometimes has foreign' nubbins' on its surface), I sponge it thoroughly on both sides with clean, cold water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nub - a small lump or protuberance

Etymology

Probably from 'nub' (small lump) plus diminutive '-in'.
