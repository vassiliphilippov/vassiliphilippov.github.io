slate

1. VERB

/ sleɪt / (AmE), / sleɪt / (BrE)
to schedule or designate (someone or something) for a particular job, event, time, or purpose, especially in an official or planned way

Examples

Skinny Brown and Hoyt Wilhelm, the Flock's veteran knuckleball specialists, are slated to oppose the American League champions in tomorrow's 8 P.M. contest.
Possibly responsible for this is the incoming trend toward multicolor schemes in rooms, which seems slated to replace the one-color look to which we have been accustomed.

Synset Examples

He slated his talk for 9 AM
She was slated to be his successor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - to arrange or plan for something to take place at a particular time
  • Synonym: appoint - to assign a job or role to someone
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide that something planned will not take place

Etymology

Originally from Old French *esclate* (splinter), later used for lists of candidates recorded on a slate tablet.
