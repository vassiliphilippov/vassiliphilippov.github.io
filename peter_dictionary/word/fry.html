fry

1. VERB

/ fraɪ / (AmE), / fraɪ / (BrE)
to be extremely hot, especially to the point of discomfort

Examples

"I might have starved, but at least I would n't be fried to a crisp and soaked with dirt "!

Synset Examples

If the children stay out on the beach for another hour, they'll be fried

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bake - to be or become very hot, especially due to heat from the sun or environment

2. VERB

/ fraɪ / (AmE), / fraɪ / (BrE)
to cook food in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan

Examples

Fry in butter a small minced onion, rub with a tablespoonful of flour, add half a cup of cream, six beaten eggs, pepper, celery salt, a teaspoonful of minced chives, a dash of cayenne, and a pinch of nutmeg.
Throw in some tomatoes, tomato paste, fry it and make sure it doesn't stick.

Synset Examples

fry the pancakes

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: deep-fry - to cook food by immersing it completely in hot fat or oil
  • Degree Lower: sauté - to fry quickly in a small amount of fat
  • Antonym: bake - to cook by dry heat in an oven

Etymology

From Old French *frire*, from Latin *frigere* (to roast, fry).
