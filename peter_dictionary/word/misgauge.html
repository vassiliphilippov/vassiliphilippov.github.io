misgauge

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
To judge or estimate the amount, value, or extent of something incorrectly.

Examples

The acoustics of the small hall had been misgauged by the artists, so that for the first half of the program, when the piano was partially open, Mr. Jenni's playing was too loud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underestimate - to judge something to be smaller or less important than it really is
  • Synonym: overestimate - to judge something to be larger or more important than it really is

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'gauge' (to measure).
