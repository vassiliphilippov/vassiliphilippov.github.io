displeased

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈplizd / (AmE), / dɪsˈpliːzd / (BrE)
feeling or showing annoyance or dissatisfaction; not pleased

Examples

It was Giselle, the fille de chambre, come to clean the room, and while she stood before him with ears pricked up and regard all curiosity, explaining her errand, Alex could see from the corner of his eye the doctor doing all he could to calm the displeased bird.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfied - pleased or contented
  • Degree Higher: angry - feeling strong annoyance or displeasure
  • Antonym: pleased - feeling or showing satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'pleased', from Old French 'plaisir' (to please).
