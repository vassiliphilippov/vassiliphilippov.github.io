compiling

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
The action or process of collecting things, such as information or documents, into a single book, file, or list.

Examples

The first is compiling a list of text forms, assigning an information cell to each, and replacing text occurrences with the information cell assigned to the form of each occurrence.

Synset Examples

the job of compiling the inventory took several hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compilation - the act or process of compiling something

Etymology

Present participle of 'compile', from Latin 'compilare' (to plunder, to compile).
