puncher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃə / (BrE)
(chiefly US, regional) A cowboy or ranch worker who tends cattle, especially on horseback.

Examples

The seventh man was Red Hogan, a wiry little puncher with a wild streak and a liking for hell raising.
He fought down the impulse to rush in and collar the vicious puncher on the spot.
The truth was, the puncher was both bewildered and dismayed by his own mixed luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a person who tends cattle on horseback, especially in the western US

Etymology

From the verb 'to punch' (to drive cattle), with '-er' suffix forming an agent noun.
