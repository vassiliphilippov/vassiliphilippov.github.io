pry

1. VERB

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
to use force, often with a lever or similar tool, to move, lift, or open something, especially something that is difficult to move or open

Examples

The area adjacent to one edge of a piece of affixed material - or simply of a painted in form - will be shaded to pry that edge away from the surface, while something will be drawn, painted or even pasted over another part of the same shape to drive it back into depth.
Then he looked at his finger, at the wrinkled, heavy knuckle and the thick nail he used like a knife to pry up, slit, and open.
As the morning wore on and a blazing West Texas sun wiped the shadows off the Franklin Mountains, police got close enough to the plane to pry into the baggage compartment.
You'll have to pry it **up** to see.

Synset Examples

The burglar jimmied the lock
Raccoons managed to pry the lid off the garbage pail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jimmy - to force open, especially a lock, with a crowbar or similar tool

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French *praier* (to press), or related to Old English *prēon* (to press).

2. VERB

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
to inquire too closely into someone's private affairs, especially in a meddlesome or intrusive way

Examples

They surged around him, fingers pointing, eyes prying.

Synset Examples

This guy is always nosing around the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snoop - to look for private information about someone or something, especially in a sneaky way
  • Synonym: intrude - to put oneself deliberately into a place or situation where one is unwelcome
  • Antonym: mind your own business - to not interfere with other people's private matters

Etymology

Probably from Old French *prier* (to ask) or related to 'peer', meaning to look closely.

3. VERB

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
To inquire too closely into someone's private affairs; to be nosy or intrusive.

Examples

He would tell her not to pry into grownups' affairs - as though she were a little kid like Elena!
I do n't mean to pry, but do they hide the swimsuit with the bubbles?

Synset Examples

Don't pry into my personal matters!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • Synonym: butt in - to interrupt or intrude into a conversation or situation
  • Antonym: mind one's own business - to not interfere or inquire into others' affairs

Etymology

Possibly from Old Norse *prýja* (to look closely, inspect).
