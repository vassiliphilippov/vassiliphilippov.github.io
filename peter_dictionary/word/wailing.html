wailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
Loud, prolonged cries or sounds made as an expression of grief, pain, or distress.

Examples

With shout and slow dance, with tears and song, with scream and contortion, the corner group was beset by hysteria and shivering, wailing, shouting, possession of something that seemed like an alien and outside force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeping - shedding tears; crying as an expression of emotion
  • Synonym: lamentation - passionate expression of grief or sorrow

Etymology

From verb 'wail' + -ing; 'wail' from Old Norse *væla* (to cry, lament).
