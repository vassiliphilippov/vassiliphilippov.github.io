pothole

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːthoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒthəʊl / (BrE)
A hole or depression in a road surface, formed by wear or weathering, especially where traffic or weather has caused the surface to break up.

Examples

He slapped the reins on the back of the powerful gray horse and held on as the sulky's wheels hit a pothole and came out with a jolt and went on.
It was a rough long ride through the mud and pot holes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crater - large hole or depression, often from impact or explosion

Etymology

From 'pot' + 'hole', likening the cavity to a pot; first attested in the mid 19th century for road holes.
