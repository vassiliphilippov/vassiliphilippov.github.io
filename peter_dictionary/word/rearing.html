rearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of caring for and educating a child as they grow up

Examples

Circumstances gave her almost undisputed sway over child rearing, money handling and home maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upbringing - the treatment and training received while growing up
  • Synonym: nurture - the care and encouragement of growth or development

Etymology

From the verb 'rear', meaning to raise up or bring up.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of bringing up and caring for a child or young person until they are fully grown and able to function as members of society

Examples

These values give direction to the life of the middle-class man or woman, dictating the methods of child rearing, determining the pattern of community participation, setting the style for the psychiatric treatment of middle-class illness, and informing the congregational life of the major denominations.

Synset Examples

they debated whether nature or nurture was more important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upbringing - the treatment and instruction received by a child from its parents throughout its childhood
  • Synonym: parenting - the process of raising and educating a child by parents or guardians
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to provide proper care for someone, especially a child

Etymology

From the verb 'rear', meaning to bring up or raise, of Old English origin.
