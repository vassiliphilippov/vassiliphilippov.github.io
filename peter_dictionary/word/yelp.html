yelp

1. NOUN

/ jɛlp / (AmE), / jɛlp / (BrE)
A sharp, high-pitched cry or bark, especially one made by a dog or other animal.

Examples

Deliberately, she ignored the yelp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark - a sharp sound made by a dog
  • Degree Higher: howl - a long, loud wailing cry (often of a dog or wolf)

Etymology

Perhaps of imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ jɛlp / (AmE), / jɛlp / (BrE)
to give a short, sharp, high-pitched cry or bark, especially as a dog does, or to utter a similar cry in pain or excitement

Examples

"I forgot "! she yelped.
Then they had always been romping around him on these walks, yelping with delight, dashing off into the bushes on fruitless hunting expeditions, returning to jump up on him triumphantly with muddy paws.

Synset Examples

the puppies yelped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark - to make the sharp sound typical of a dog
  • Degree Higher: howl - to make a long, loud, wailing sound, especially by a dog or wolf
  • Degree Lower: whimper - to make a series of low, feeble sounds expressive of fear, pain, or unhappiness

Etymology

Possibly of imitative origin, reflecting the sound itself.
