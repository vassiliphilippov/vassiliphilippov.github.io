defecate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛfəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdiːfɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to discharge feces from the bowels; to have a bowel movement

Examples

"The devil himself must have defecated you into my classroom "!

Synset Examples

The dog had made in the flower beds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evacuate - to empty the bowels
  • Antonym: urinate - to discharge urine

Etymology

From Latin defaecare (to clear of dregs, purify), from de- (removal) + faex (dregs, sediment).
