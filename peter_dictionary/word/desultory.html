desultory

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛsəltɔri / (AmE), / ˈdɛlsətri / (BrE)
lacking a clear plan, purpose, or enthusiasm; moving or jumping from one thing to another in a disorganized way

Examples

He is utterly disappointed in himself and in the desultory life he has been leading.

Synset Examples

desultory thoughts
the desultory conversation characteristic of cocktail parties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambling - talking or moving from one topic or place to another without clear direction
  • Synonym: random - without method or conscious decision
  • Antonym: methodical - done according to a systematic or established procedure

Etymology

From Latin desultorius (leaping, skipping about), from desilire (to leap down).
