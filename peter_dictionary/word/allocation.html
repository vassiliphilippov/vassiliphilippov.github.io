allocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of distributing or assigning resources, responsibilities, or items according to a plan

Examples

Have you considered the implications of continuing geographic shifts in terms of sales force allocation, strength of distributor organizations, and even plant location?

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apportionment - the act of dividing and distributing according to a plan
  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among recipients

Etymology

From Latin ‘allocatio’ (an assigning), from ‘allocare’ (to assign, allot).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an amount of something that is given or allocated to someone for a particular purpose

Examples

The greatest impact of the matching fund principle has been in initially encouraging the poorest states and school districts to spend enough to obtain their full allocation of outside funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allotment - a portion that is allotted

Etymology

From Latin 'allocatio', from 'allocare' meaning 'to assign' or 'to allot'.
