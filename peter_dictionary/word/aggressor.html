aggressor

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsər / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsə / (BrE)
A person, group, or country that attacks first or initiates hostilities.

Examples

An aggressor would use an agent against which there was a minimal naturally acquired or artificially induced immunity in a target population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - a person who attacks another
  • Antonym: defender - a person who defends against attack

Etymology

From Latin aggressor, from aggredi (to attack).

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsər / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsə / (BrE)
A person who confidently and assertively initiates action, often as an instigator in a particular context.

Examples

One such man once confided to Dr. Theodor Reik, New York psychiatrist, that he preferred to have his wife the sexual aggressor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instigator - person who initiates something, esp. trouble

Etymology

From Latin aggressor (attacker), from aggredi (to attack).
