proposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A statement or proposal put forward for consideration, discussion, acceptance, or rejection.

Examples

The very proposition was sacrilege.
"Exterminatin' cow thieves is just a business proposition with me ", he 'd blandly announce.
He wants us to send Michael to hear the proposition.

Synset Examples

it was a suggestion we couldn't refuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a plan or suggestion put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: offer - a statement that one is willing to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio', from 'proponere' (to set forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(logic) A statement that affirms or denies something and is either true or false.

Examples

This conclusion is based on two propositions: that man by the use of his reason can ascertain God's purpose in the universe and that God makes known His purpose by certain "given "physical arrangements.
When Peter B. Kyne (Pride of Palomar, 43) informed us in 1921 that we had an instinctive dislike for the Japanese, did the heated debates of the Californians settle the truth or falsity of the proposition?
Will mere debate on that proposition, even though it be free and untrammeled, remove the dross and leave a residue of refined gold?
One would need to test this proposition carefully; after all, the large (and probably unreliable) Reader's Digest literature on the "most unforgettable character I ever met "deals with village grocers, country doctors, favorite if illiterate aunts, and so forth.
But still, the proposition is worth examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statement - a definite or clear expression in speech or writing
  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio' (a setting forth), from 'proponere' (to set forth).

3. VERB

/ ˌprɑpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to make a sexual advance to someone, especially in a direct or unsolicited way

Examples

She looked well-fed and prosperous, but he did n't get the impression he was being propositioned the way he 'd been hoping.

Synset Examples

She was propositioned by a stranger at the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solicit - to ask for or try to obtain something from someone, especially in a sexual context

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio', meaning an assertion or putting forward (via French). The sexual sense developed in the 20th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an offer or suggestion, especially one involving a private or often sexual request

Examples

I'll make him an offer he can't refuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - an act of offering or suggesting something for acceptance

Etymology

From Latin 'propositio', from 'proponere' (to put forth)
