boggle

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbɒɡl / (BrE)
(of a person or their eyes) to be startled or show amazement, usually with wide eyes or in a way that is clearly visible.

Examples

His eyes boggled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: amaze - to fill with wonder

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *bugge* (a frightening thing, bugbear).
