indelicate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (BrE)
Lacking in sensitivity or good taste; verging on being offensive or indecent.

Examples

There is evidence to suggest, in fact, that many authors of the humorous sketches were prompted to write them - or to make them as indelicate as they are - by way of protesting against the artificial refinements which had come to dominate the polite letters of the South.

Synset Examples

an indelicate remark
an off-color joke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: delicate - having or showing refined taste or tact

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + delicate, from delicatus (dainty, pleasing).
