superficially

1. ADV

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈfɪʃ.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.əli / (BrE)
In a way that is only concerned with or affects the surface or appearance, without going into depth.

Examples

The Catholic priest, though somewhat superficially drawn, easily came out the best.

Synset Examples

he was superficially interested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallowly - in a way that lacks depth or thoroughness
  • Antonym: thoroughly - in a way that is complete and with great attention to detail

Etymology

From Latin 'superficialis' (of or pertaining to the surface), itself from 'superficies' (surface).
