paging

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of calling out the name of a person, especially over a loudspeaker system.

Examples

Commonly used electronic devices which are found in practically every hospital are closed-circuit TV and audio systems for internal paging and instruction, along with radiation counters, timers, and similar devices.

Synset Examples

the public address system in the hospital was used for paging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summoning - calling someone to appear or attend
  • Degree Lower: announcement - a public statement communicated to people

Etymology

From the verb 'page', meaning to call or summon, which originated from the noun 'page' (servant who carried messages).
