spread-eagle

1. VERB

/ ˈsprɛdˌiɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɛdˌiːɡl / (BrE)
to stretch (someone or something) out with arms and legs extended outward, resembling the position of an eagle with wings spread; often used to describe forcing someone into this position.

Examples

I found a trooper once the Apache had spread-eagled on an ant hill, and another time we ran across some teamsters they 'd caught, tied upside down on their own wagon wheels over little fires until their brains was exploded right out o' their skulls.

Synset Examples

They spread-eagled him across the floor

Etymology

From the image of an eagle with wings spread wide, in reference to the American bald eagle on the Great Seal of the United States.
