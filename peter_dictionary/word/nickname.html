nickname

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.neɪm / (BrE)
a familiar or informal name given to a person, often a shortened form of their given name or a name describing their character, appearance, or other quality

Examples

Because of this he was known as Gimpy (but, as with Capone and his nickname of Scarface, never in his presence).
It's your uncle Bingo.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Etymology

From Middle English 'ekename' (an additional name), later reanalyzed as 'nickname' by false division; 'ekename' from Old English 'eaca' (an addition) + 'name'.

2. VERB

/ ˈnɪkˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.neɪm / (BrE)
to give a nickname to someone or something

Examples

The youngsters in the boys' class had nicknamed Dick "Bugs Bunny "because his teeth protruded.

Etymology

From Middle English 'ekename' (also spelled 'nekename'): 'eke' meaning 'also' and 'name'. The phrase 'an ekename' became 'a nekename', leading to 'nickname'.
