honour

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
A special award, medal, or title given to recognize achievement or distinction.

Examples

As Sir Giles Overreach (how often had he had to play that part, who did not believe a word of it), he raised his arm and declaimed: "Where is my honour now "?

Synset Examples

an award for bravery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - a prize or other mark of recognition given for achievement
  • Synonym: distinction - an honor or award recognizing excellence or achievement

Etymology

From Old French *honor*, from Latin *honor* (respect, dignity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
The state of being given special respect or recognition.

Examples

Hughes said that he had understood the party was to be in honour of Jerry O' Connor, the proprieter of a Loop gambling house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - American spelling of 'honour' (state of being honored)

Etymology

From Old French *honor*, from Latin *honor* (esteem, dignity).

3. VERB

/ ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
to show great respect for someone by giving them an award, title, or public recognition

Examples

In fact, O' Connor was honoured during the ceremony with the presentation of a $ 2500 diamond stickpin.

Synset Examples

Today we honor our soldiers
The scout was rewarded for courageous action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - to show great respect for someone by giving an award, orthographic variant (US spelling)

Etymology

From Old French *honorer*, from Latin *honorare* ('to honor'), from *honor* ('honor, respect').

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
(archaic) A woman's virtue or chastity.

Examples

She resisted, she kept her honour.

Etymology

From Old French *honor*, from Latin *honor* (honour, respect).
