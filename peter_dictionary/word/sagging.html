sagging

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsæɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Hanging down loosely, especially from weakness, exhaustion, or lack of support.

Examples

Lips pursed mournfully, he stared down at its crazily sagging left side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drooping - hanging or bending downward
  • Antonym: firm - solid, not hanging or bending downward

Etymology

From the verb 'sag', of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian 'sagga' (to let sink).
