riboflavin

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪboʊˈfleɪvɪn / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəʊˈfleɪvɪn / (BrE)
A water-soluble B vitamin (vitamin B2) essential to human health, found in food and used by the body to help convert nutrients into energy.

Examples

The B vitamins are well represented, especially thiamin and riboflavin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B2 - another term for riboflavin
  • Degree Lower: thiamine - another B vitamin (vitamin B1)
  • Degree Higher: niacin - another B vitamin (vitamin B3)

Etymology

From ribose (a type of sugar) + flavin (from Latin 'flavus', meaning yellow, due to its color).
