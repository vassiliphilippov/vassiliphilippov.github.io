unhitch

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈhɪtʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɪtʃ / (BrE)
To unfasten or release (something) from being attached or secured, especially to detach an animal or vehicle.

Examples

He unhitched his horse, walked it away, mounted, and spurred it on.
Billie had unhitched the mules from both Tom Brannon's and his father's wagon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hitch - to fasten or attach with a hook, rope, or similar device

Etymology

From un- (a prefix meaning 'reverse action') + hitch ('to fasten or tie')
