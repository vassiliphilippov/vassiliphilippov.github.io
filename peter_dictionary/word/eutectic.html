eutectic

1. NOUN

/ juːˈtɛktɪk / (AmE), / juːˈtɛktɪk / (BrE)
A mixture of substances that has the lowest possible melting point among all possible mixtures of those components, so that it melts or solidifies at a single temperature.

Examples

In addition to the method described in the section above, chlorine and radiochlorine were prepared by the electrolysis of a * * f eutectic on the vacuum line, and by exchange of * * f with molten * * f.

Etymology

From Greek 'eutēktos' meaning 'easily melted', from 'eu-' (well, easily) + 'tēkein' (to melt).
