converge

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɝːdʒ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(of lines, paths, or objects) to come together at a single point or to move toward a common point.

Examples

Department stores congregated in the "one hundred per cent location ", where all the transit lines converged.

Synset Examples

The lines converge at this point

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diverge - to go in different directions from a common point; to separate

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'convergere', from Latin 'com-' (together) + 'vergere' (to bend, turn).
