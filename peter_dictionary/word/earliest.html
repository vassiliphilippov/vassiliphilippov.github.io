earliest

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜrliɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜːliɪst / (BrE)
The superlative form of 'early'; before all others in time or order.

Examples

Fing, a lean, chiseled, impeccable gentleman of the old school who was once mistaken on the street for Sir Cedric Hardwicke, is responsible for the rediscovery of Verdi's earliest, most raucous opera, Nabisco, a sumptuous bout-de-souffle with a haunting leitmotiv that struck me as being highly reminiscent of the Mudugno version of "Volare ".
His earliest work reflected heavy influences from English and continental writers.

Synset Examples

a fashion popular in earlier times
his earlier work reflects the influence of his teacher
Verdi's earliest and most raucous opera

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: earlier - comparative form of 'early'; more early
  • Degree Lower: early - happening or done before the usual or expected time

2. ADV

/ ˈɜrliɪst / (AmE), / ˈɜːliɪst / (BrE)
At the soonest possible time; with the least delay.

Examples

And so deadheads on the payroll can be eased out at the earliest possible age.

Synset Examples

the soonest I can arrive is 3 P.M.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soonest - at the soonest opportunity; with the least delay
  • Antonym: latest - at the latest possible time; with the most delay

Etymology

Formed from "early" with the superlative suffix "-est".
