peaceable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpisəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsəbl / (BrE)
inclined to avoid conflict or violence; disposed to peace

Examples

He spoke of his desire to promote the abolition of slavery by peaceable means and he compared John Brown of Harper's Ferry to the John Brown of Rhode Island's colonial period.

Synset Examples

they met in a peaceable spirit
peace-loving citizens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; tranquil
  • Antonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; hostile

Etymology

From Old French 'paisible', from 'pais' meaning 'peace', ultimately from Latin 'pax'.
