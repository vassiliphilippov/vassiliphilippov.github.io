obliteration

1. NOUN

/ əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of completely destroying or removing something so that nothing remains.

Examples

Probably less than one percent of our previsions escape final obliteration before we wake.
Such swollen fibers were deeply eosinophilic, contained a few pyknotic nuclei, and showed loss of cross striations, obliteration of myofibrils, and prominent vacuolization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annihilation - the act of completely destroying something
  • Synonym: destruction - the act of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists

Etymology

From Latin oblitteratio, from oblitterare (to blot out).
