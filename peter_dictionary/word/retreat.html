retreat

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtrit / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːt / (BrE)
(military) The act of withdrawing troops from enemy forces after a defeat or to a more advantageous position.

Examples

The posse's retreat encouraged the Mexicans to be overbearing and impudent.
Their onrush and retreat, he reported, was accompanied by a great hissing, roaring and rattling.

Synset Examples

the disorderly retreat of French troops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advance - an onward movement toward the enemy or a goal in a military context

Etymology

From Old French 'retret', from Latin 'retrahere' (to draw back).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtrit / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːt / (BrE)
to move back or withdraw, especially from danger, threat, or an advancing force

Examples

Figures seemed to crouch in the surrounding dark; in the distance he saw a band of men who seemed to advance and retreat even as he watched.
Nevertheless so short was the supply of seed that the settlers were forced to retreat to Fort Daer for food.
He retreated a step and holstered his own.
The Aricaras broke under the devastating fire, wheeled and retreated.
Several people passed in the hall and stared as he slowly retreated, trying to close the door a little, and she slowly leaned toward him and raised her voice.

Synset Examples

The enemy withdrew
The limo pulled away from the curb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdraw - to move back or remove oneself from a place or situation
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or proceed

Etymology

From Old French *retret* (retreat, withdrawal), from Latin *retrahere* (to draw back).
