eloquent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛl.ə.kwənt / (AmE), / ˈɛl.ə.kwənt / (BrE)
expressing yourself in a clear, persuasive, and effective manner; able to use language well to move, inspire, or impress others

Examples

Not the noblest performance we have heard him play, or the most spacious, or even the most eloquent.
Compare the vast difference in scope and beauty between his neat and witty little Classical Symphony and his big, muscular, passionate, and eloquent Fifth Symphony; or the Love for Three Oranges (gay as it is) with the wonderful, imaginative, colorful, and subtle tenderness of the magnificent ballet, The Stone Flower.
All evening she was eloquent and pleased with herself.

Synset Examples

able to dazzle with his facile tongue
silver speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - able to express ideas clearly and effectively in speech or writing
  • Antonym: inarticulate - unable to express oneself clearly or effectively
  • Synonym: persuasive - good at convincing others

Etymology

From Latin 'eloquens', present participle of 'eloqui' (to speak out).
