protectorate

1. NOUN

/ prəˈtɛktərət / (AmE), / prəˈtɛktərət / (BrE)
A country or territory that is partly controlled and protected by a stronger state, while retaining some autonomy in internal matters, often established by treaty.

Examples

The police were disqualified along with certain categories of naturalized citizens, criminals and those punished for Protectorate activities.

Etymology

From protector + -ate, first used in the 18th century to denote a protected state.
