prickly

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪkli / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli / (BrE)
Easily irritated or annoyed; quick to take offense.

Examples

Prickly twinges of annoyance ran through him.

Synset Examples

bristly exchanges between the White House and the press
he became prickly and spiteful
witty and waspish about his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Synonym: touchy - easily upset or offended
  • Antonym: calm - not easily upset or annoyed

Etymology

From Middle English 'prikly', from 'prik' (prick) + '-ly'.
