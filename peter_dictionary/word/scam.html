scam

1. NOUN

/ skæm / (AmE), / skæm / (BrE)
a dishonest scheme or fraud, especially one intended to make money illegally

Examples

Get someone else to run your scams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - intentional deception for personal gain
  • Synonym: swindle - a fraudulent scheme or action to cheat someone
  • Antonym: legitimate business - a business operating legally and honestly

Etymology

Origin uncertain; popularized in American slang in the 1960s, possibly from "scamp."
