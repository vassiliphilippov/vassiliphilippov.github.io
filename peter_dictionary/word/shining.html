shining

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something smooth and shiny by rubbing or waxing it, typically to improve its appearance

Examples

Early in the war it was not uncommon for planters' sons to retain in camp Negro "body servants "to perform the menial chores such as cooking, foraging, cleaning the quarters, shining shoes, and laundering clothes.

Synset Examples

the shining of shoes provided a meager living
every Sunday he gave his car a good polishing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polishing - the act of making a surface smooth and shiny

Etymology

From the verb 'shine'; formed by adding the suffix '-ing'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
Exceptionally good, outstanding, or admirable; serving as a model or example to others.

Examples

And the fact remains that there are today few shining examples of Negroes in positions of intellectual leadership.

Synset Examples

had shining virtues and few faults
a shining example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplary - serving as a desirable model; representing the best of its kind
  • Synonym: outstanding - remarkably good; standing out from others
  • Antonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good

Etymology

Present participle of 'shine', originally from Old English 'scīnan' (to emit light).
