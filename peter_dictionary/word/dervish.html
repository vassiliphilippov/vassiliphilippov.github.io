dervish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɜrvɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɜːvɪʃ / (BrE)
A member of a Muslim religious order known for devotional practices, especially dancing, whirling, or other bodily movements as a form of worship; sometimes referred to as a Muslim ascetic or monk.

Examples

A bunch of amateur dervishes!

Etymology

From Turkish 'derviş', from Persian 'darvīsh' (poor, mendicant).
