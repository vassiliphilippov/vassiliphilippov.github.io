rigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to fit the rigging (ropes, cables, etc.) and other equipment on a ship

Examples

Finally come those little flicks of a rigger brush and the job is done.

Etymology

From rig + -er, meaning one who rigs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
A long, slender, pointed sable brush used by artists, especially for painting fine lines and details.

Examples

The red sables are 8; two riggers, 6 and 10; and a very large, flat wash brush.

Etymology

Possibly so called from its use for painting rigging on ship pictures.
