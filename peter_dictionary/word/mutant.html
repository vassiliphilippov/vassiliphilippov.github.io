mutant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːtənt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtənt / (BrE)
an organism, cell, or gene that has developed characteristics as a result of a genetic or chromosomal alteration

Examples

Mutants may also be developed with changes in biochemical properties that are of importance in identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutation - the process or result of changing genetic material

Etymology

From Latin 'mutans', present participle of 'mutare' (to change).
