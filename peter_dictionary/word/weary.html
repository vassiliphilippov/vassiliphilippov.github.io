weary

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪri / (AmE), / ˈwɪəri / (BrE)
Feeling or showing tiredness, especially as a result of excessive exertion or lack of sleep; physically or mentally fatigued.

Examples

If it could be shown that judgments of good and bad were not judgments at all, that they asserted nothing true or false, but merely expressed emotions like "Hurrah "or "Fiddlesticks ", then these wayward judgments would cease from troubling and weary heads could be at rest.
I have never asked for an easy task, but I am weary of the strife ".
He sank back on his thin haunches like a weary hound.
Alex returned to the hotel, rather weary and with no new prospects of a role, in the late afternoon, but found the doctor in an ebullient mood.
He had been weary; now he felt light and gay and clear-headed, ready for the many actions he saw spreading out before him.

Synset Examples

`aweary' is archaic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tired - in need of rest or sleep
  • Synonym: fatigued - experiencing tiredness or exhaustion
  • Antonym: energized - full of energy
  • Antonym: alert - quick to notice or react

Etymology

From Old English *wērig* (tired, exhausted).

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪri / (AmE), / ˈwɪəri / (BrE)
To exhaust or cause someone to become tired, especially by overuse or prolonged effort.

Examples

What was the matter with him that they all wearied him?

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatigue - extreme tiredness or weariness
  • Antonym: refresh - to make someone less tired or more energetic

Etymology

From Old English *wērig* (tired), related to Old High German *wuorag* (intoxicated, tired).
