double

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl / (BrE)
(Baseball) A hit that allows the batter to reach second base safely.

Examples

Three were doubles, Brooks Robinson getting a pair and Marv Breeding one.
In the ninth, Robinson led off with his second double of the night, a blast off the fence 375 feet deep into left.
Then Robinson slammed a long double to left center to score both runners.
"Take a ride on this one ", Brooks Robinson greeted Hansen as the Bird third sacker grabbed a bat, headed for the plate and bounced a third inning two run double off the left centerfield wall tonight.
It was the first of two doubles by Robinson, who was in a mood to celebrate.

Synset Examples

he hit a double to deep centerfield

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: single - base hit on which the batter stops safely at first base (baseball)
  • Degree Higher: triple - base hit on which the batter stops safely at third base (baseball)
  • Degree Higher: home run - base hit on which the batter circles all bases, scoring a run (baseball)

2. VERB

/ ˈdʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl / (BrE)
(of a batter) to hit a two-base hit in baseball.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple - to hit a three-base hit in baseball
