inaccuracy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈækjərəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈækjʊərəsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being inaccurate; the presence of mistakes or errors.

Examples

He did receive a resume of it - the same that was furnished the appeal board - and he made no claim of its inaccuracy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accuracy - the quality of being correct or precise

Etymology

From prefix in- (not) + accuracy, after Latin accuratus (careful, accurate).
