earnings

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝnɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪŋz / (BrE)
money obtained in return for labor or services, or profits from investments.

Examples

But the one that upset the financially wise was the professional dancer who related in a book how he parlayed his earnings into a $ 2000000 profit on the stock market.
A minor is subject to tax on his own earnings even though his parent may, under local law, have the right to them and might actually have received the money.

Synset Examples

wages were paid by check
he wasted his pay on drink
they saved a quarter of all their earnings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wage - fixed regular payment, typically paid on a daily or weekly basis, made by an employer to an employee
  • Synonym: salary - fixed, regular payment, typically paid monthly and expressed as an annual sum, made by an employer
  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments

Etymology

From 'earn' + '-ing', meaning what has been earned.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrnɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪŋz / (BrE)
Money received as profit or net income, especially by a business, after subtracting costs and expenses over a period of time.

Examples

The record of past earnings and prospects for the future must indicate it has the ability to repay the loan out of current and anticipated income.
Sickness or loss of some of the livestock may cut into the owner's earnings, even into his capital.
Next, estimate the value of possible earnings of the farm.
The annual dividends paid to du Pont stockholders in shares of General Motors stock would be taxable as ordinary income to the extent of du Pont's earnings and profits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments
  • Synonym: profit - financial gain, especially the difference between the amount earned and the amount spent
  • Antonym: loss - money lost by a business; the opposite of earnings

Etymology

From 'earn' + '-ing' + plural '-s', with 'earn' from Old English 'earnian' (to gain as a result of labor).
