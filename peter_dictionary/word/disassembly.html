disassembly

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəli / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəli / (BrE)
the act or process of taking something apart, especially a machine or device

Examples

It is presumed that this negative head was associated with some geometric factor of the assembly, since different readings were obtained with the same fluid and the only apparent difference was the assembly and disassembly of the apparatus.

Synset Examples

Russia and the United States discussed the dismantling of their nuclear weapons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assembly - the act of putting parts together to make a whole

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + assembly (the act of putting together).
