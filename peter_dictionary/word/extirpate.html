extirpate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛk.stərˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.stɪˌpeɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or remove something completely; to root out entirely

Examples

It has not been extirpated by ten years of Communist depersonalization.
China never tried to integrate Tibet by extirpating the people's religion and institutions.

Synset Examples

the vestiges of political democracy were soon uprooted
root out corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eradicate - to destroy or put an end to something completely
  • Synonym: eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
  • Synonym: root out - to find and remove something completely

Etymology

From Latin extirpatus, past participle of extirpare, from ex- (out) + stirps (root, stock).
