blue-collar

1. ADJ

/ bluː ˈkɑːlər / (AmE), / bluː ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
Relating to manual work or workers, especially in industry, who are typically paid by the hour or by the job rather than on a salary basis.

Examples

The blue-collar worker thus knows his insurance package, for example, costs $ 227.72.

Synset Examples

party of the propertyless proletariat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white-collar - relating to office or professional work rather than manual labor

Etymology

Refers to the colored shirts traditionally worn by manual workers to distinguish them from white-collar (office) workers.
