sheep

1. NOUN

/ ʃip / (AmE), / ʃiːp / (BrE)
A domesticated, typically woolly, ruminant mammal (Ovis aries) kept for its wool, meat, and milk, usually having horns and closely related to the goat.

Examples

Type 1, is represented by the cow, sheep, and pig; type 2,, by the dog, cat, and monkey; type 3,, by the horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goat - domesticated ruminant mammal related to the sheep

Etymology

Old English 'scēap' (sheep), of Germanic origin.
