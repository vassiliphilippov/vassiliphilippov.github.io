digestible

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈdʒɛstəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪbəl / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) able to be broken down and absorbed by the body through the digestive system.

Examples

The morning hawk, hungry for any eatable, killable, digestible item, kept his eyes on the ring of anchored ships that lay off the shores in the bay, sheltered by the Jersey inlets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indigestible - not able to be digested by the body

Etymology

From Latin 'digestibilis', from 'digere' (to separate, arrange).
