loop

1. NOUN

/ luːp / (AmE), / luːp / (BrE)
a small metal ring sewn into or lining a hole, used to reinforce the opening and allow the passage or attachment of cords or lines

Examples

A match flared, and he reached above his head to light a lantern which hung from a wire loop.

2. VERB

/ luːp / (AmE), / luːp / (BrE)
to move or form into a loop or series of loops, especially by following a curved or circular route

Examples

Ten thousand twisty miles of shoreline frame the 30 - odd lakes in the vast Tennessee River system that loops in and out of seven states.

Synset Examples

The bicycle looped around the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circle - to move all the way around something in a circular motion
  • Antonym: straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From Middle English loupe, possibly from Old French loupe (protuberance, lump).

3. VERB

/ luːp / (AmE), / luːp / (BrE)
To make one or more loops in (something), typically by forming it into a curved or circular shape.

Examples

Clayton looped the reins in a knot over the veranda post and patted the warm flesh of his neck.

Synset Examples

loop a rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - to tie or fasten with a knot

Etymology

From Middle English 'loupe', from Old French, of Germanic origin.
