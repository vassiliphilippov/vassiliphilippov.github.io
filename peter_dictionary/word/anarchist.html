anarchist

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɚkɪst / (AmE), / ˈanəkɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes in or tries to bring about the abolition of government and the organization of society on a voluntary, cooperative basis without recourse to force or compulsion.

Examples

He is an individualist but not an anarchist; he is a public servant but not a cop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nihilist - person who rejects all religious and moral principles, advocating that life is meaningless

Etymology

From French *anarchiste*, from Greek *anarkhos* ('without rulers').
