anomie

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌmi / (AmE), / ˈænəmi / (BrE)
A condition of instability or personal disorientation arising from a breakdown of standards and values or a lack of social norms.

Examples

He has to cope with frustration and other emotional disturbance and anomie.

Etymology

From French 'anomie', from Greek 'anomia' (lawlessness), from 'a-' (without) + 'nomos' (law).
