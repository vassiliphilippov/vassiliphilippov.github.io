wrathful

1. ADJ

/ ˈræθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒθ.fəl / (BrE)
Full of or characterized by intense anger.

Examples

He was Papa's chosen; therefore, nothing but good could happen to him, even in God's wrathful storms.

Synset Examples

they trembled before the wrathful queen
but wroth as he was, a short struggle ended in reconciliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong displeasure
  • Degree Higher: furious - extremely angry
  • Antonym: calm - not showing anger or agitation

Etymology

From 'wrath' + '-ful', with 'wrath' from Old English 'wrāththu' (anger).
