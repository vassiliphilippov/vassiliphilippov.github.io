existing

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
present or found in a specified place or thing; currently in existence.

Examples

Check the quantity and quality of all of the recreation interests already existing at the site.
The problem for the city apartment dweller is primarily to plan the use of existing space.

Synset Examples

depletion of the oxygen existing in the bloodstream

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonexistent - not existing or not present, especially where something might be expected

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
having existence; currently present or in operation

Examples

Until internal combustion became cheap, he had to be near a railroad siding and a trolley line or an existing large community of lower-class homes.
Only in one instance have we moved purposively and effectively to dislodge existing Communist power:
She was the sun, he the closest planet orbiting around her, the rest of the world existing and visible yet removed.

Synset Examples

an attempt to refine the existent machinery to make it more efficient
much of the beluga caviar existing in the world is found in the Soviet Union and Iran

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonexistent - not existing; not real or present
  • Synonym: extant - still in existence; surviving

Etymology

From Latin 'existere' meaning 'to stand out, exist.'
