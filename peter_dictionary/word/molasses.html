molasses

1. NOUN

/ məˈlæsɪz / (AmE), / məˈlæsɪz / (BrE)
A thick, dark syrup obtained by refining sugar, especially from boiled sugar cane or sugar beet juice.

Examples

During Prohibition we ran molasses into Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treacle - a thick, sweet, dark syrup made during sugar refining (chiefly British)

Etymology

From Portuguese 'melaço', from Latin 'mel' (honey).
