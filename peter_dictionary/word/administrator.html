administrator

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪn.əˌstreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪn.ɪ.streɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person responsible for managing the affairs, operations, or policies of a business, organization, or institution.

Examples

The jury also commented on the Fulton ordinary's court which has been under fire for its practices in the appointment of appraisers, guardians and administrators and the awarding of fees and compensation.
The Bathyrans ran a check on Globocnik and had only to conclude that he was in a tug of war with Hans Frank and the civilian administrators.

Despite the fact that he was regarded as an outstanding engineer, he seemed to be a very poor administrator, although no one quite knew what was wrong with him.
Brian Thayer was a thoroughly honest and competent administrator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person in charge of part of an organization; directs or controls staff and projects
  • Synonym: executive - person with senior managerial responsibility in an organization

Etymology

From Latin administrātor (manager, director), from administrare (to manage, control).

2. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪnəˌstreɪtər / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person legally appointed by a court to manage and distribute the estate of someone who dies without a will or without naming an executor

Examples

Related Terms
