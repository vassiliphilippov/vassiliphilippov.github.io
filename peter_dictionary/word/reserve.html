reserve

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
a supply of something that is kept back or stored for future use or for a special purpose

Examples

Garden Fresh, the result of a mating of Better Self and Rosy Fingered, seems to improve with each start and appeared to win the St. Patrick's Day Purse with some speed in reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - a supply of something kept for use as needed
  • Synonym: store - an accumulated supply reserved for future use
  • Synonym: hoard - an accumulation of something kept secret or for future use

Etymology

From Latin 'reservare', meaning to keep back, retain.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
A sports player who does not usually play in the starting lineup and is available to replace another player if needed.

Examples

The game players saw the Air Force film Monday, ran for 30 minutes, then went in, while the reserves scrimmaged for 45 minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a player who replaces another on a team
  • Antonym: starter - a player who starts a game on the field or court

Etymology

From French *réserve*, from Latin *reservare* (to keep back).
