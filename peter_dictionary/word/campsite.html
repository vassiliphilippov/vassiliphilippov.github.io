campsite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmp.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæmp.saɪt / (BrE)
An area where people can set up a tent or park a camper, especially when on holiday.

Examples

The National Park Service hopes by 1966 to have 30000 campsites available for 100000 campers a day - almost twice what there are at present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campground - an area equipped for campers, often with facilities

Etymology

From 'camp' + 'site', indicating a place designated for camping.
