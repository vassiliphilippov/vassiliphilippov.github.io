despondency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɑndənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒndənsi / (BrE)
A state of low spirits caused by loss of hope or courage; feeling downcast and disheartened.

Examples

He gripped the wheel hard to fight the despondency of defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dejection - A sad and depressed state; low spirits
  • Synonym: despair - The complete loss or absence of hope
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - The state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin 'despondentia', from 'despondere' (to give up, lose hope).
