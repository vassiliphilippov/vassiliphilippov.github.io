inscrutability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌskruːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌskruːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible to understand or interpret; the state of being mysterious or difficult to investigate.

Examples

If life and death did not both present themselves to us, there would be no inscrutability.

Synset Examples

the inscrutability of the future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being difficult to understand or explain
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of being easy to perceive or detect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'inscrutabilis' (not examinable, mysterious), from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'scrutari' (to search, examine).
