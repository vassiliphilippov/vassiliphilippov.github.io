lucid

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪd / (BrE)
(of language or writing) clearly expressed and easy to understand; transparent in meaning.

Examples

This is precisely what makes lucid, straightforward music so difficult to compose - the clarity must be new, not old ".
When Prokofieff forged his new clarity of "lucid, straightforward music, so difficult to compose ", he shaped his talents to his purpose.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret; unambiguous
  • Antonym: obscure - not clearly expressed or easily understood

Etymology

From Latin 'lucidus', from 'lucere' meaning 'to shine'.
