disservice

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
An action or behavior that unintentionally causes harm or has negative effects, especially when meant to help.

Examples

In one way the Institutes and Societies do a disservice to the schools.

Synset Examples

he did them a disservice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: service - an act of helping or doing work for someone
  • Synonym: harm - physical injury, damage, or negative effect

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'dis-' (Latin: apart, away) to 'service'.
