contradiction

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
(logic) A statement that is necessarily false because it contains mutually exclusive or incompatible propositions.

Examples

But this is a contradiction, for we know that the multiplicity of f (t) is odd for every t.

Synset Examples

the statement `he is brave and he is not brave' is a contradiction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradox - a statement that seems self-contradictory or absurd but may be true
  • Antonym: tautology - a statement that is true in every possible interpretation (logic)

Etymology

From Latin contrādictiōn-em (opposition, objection), from contrādīcere (to speak against).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.trəˈdɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈdɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
A situation in which two or more statements, ideas, or actions are opposed to each other or cannot all be true at the same time.

Examples

A contradiction?
Bah, what was a contradiction in one's life?
"Mr. Jones, you may recall that we printed last week your denial of having retracted the contradiction of your original statement.
Even on the fictional level, however, the contradictions which give rise to the mystery story are not fully resolved.
It is the gradual unfolding and deepening of this contradiction which creates the inner dialectic of the evolution of the mystery story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradox - a seemingly absurd or self-contradictory statement or proposition
  • Antonym: consistency - the absence of contradiction among statements or facts

Etymology

From Latin contradictio (a denial or opposition), from contradicere (to speak against).
