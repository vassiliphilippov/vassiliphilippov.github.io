glaring

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡleərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of light) extremely bright or dazzling; shining intensely.

Examples

Pansies are supposed to like it cool, but those great velvety flowers were healthy and perky in the glaring sun.
Pierre Ameaux, the gaming-card manufacturer said, his little pig eyes glaring.

Synset Examples

the blazing sun
blinding headlights
dazzling snow
fulgent patterns of sunlight
the glaring sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blazing - shining very brightly or fiercely
  • Synonym: dazzling - so bright as to blind temporarily
  • Antonym: dim - not shining brightly or clearly

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡleərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fault or problem) highly obvious and often shockingly bad or reprehensible.

Examples

Anyone who tried to remedy some of the most glaring defects in our form of democracy was denounced as a traitorous red whose real purpose was the destruction of our government.

Synset Examples

a crying shame
an egregious lie
flagrant violation of human rights
a glaring error
gross ineptitude
gross injustice
rank treachery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egregious - extremely bad in a way that is obvious or shocking
  • Synonym: flagrant - (of something considered wrong or immoral) conspicuously bad
  • Degree Lower: minor - less significant or serious in degree (of an error or fault)
