phonology

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of linguistics concerned with the systematic organization of sounds in languages, including the analysis and classification of phonemes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonetics - the study of speech sounds and their physical properties

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) + -logy (study of).
