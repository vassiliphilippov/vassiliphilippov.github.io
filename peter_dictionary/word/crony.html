crony

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊni / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊni / (BrE)
a close friend, especially one who is a companion in activities or associates closely with another, often with negative or informal connotations

Examples

He claimed to be visiting the waterfront saloon at the crossroads to play cards and drink with his cronies, but Kate had not smelled brandy on him since Mrs. Lattimer's funeral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buddy - a close friend; companion
  • Degree Lower: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly

Etymology

Probably from Greek *khronios* (long-lasting) via 17th-century university slang.
