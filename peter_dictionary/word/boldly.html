boldly

1. ADV

/ ˈboʊldli / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldli / (BrE)
In a bold or courageous manner; showing confidence and a willingness to take risks.

Examples

Our central goal should be to provide the greatest positive incentive for these societies to tackle boldly the tasks which they face.
MacPherson boldly approached the fortified adobe house and demanded entrance.

Synset Examples

we must tackle these tasks boldly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageously - in a way that shows courage
  • Antonym: timidly - in a shy or lacking courage manner

Etymology

From 'bold' + '-ly'; 'bold' from Old English *beald* or *bald* (brave, confident).
