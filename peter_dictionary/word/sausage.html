sausage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɒsɪdʒ / (BrE)
A food made of seasoned minced meat, often pork or beef, stuffed into a casing and usually shaped into links or patties.

Examples

His broad flat fingers, petrified into massive sausages by years of bricklaying, snatched the bills clumsily.
You get it to a boil, you shove in all your sausage and meatballs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankfurter - a type of smoked sausage, also known as a hot dog
  • Synonym: banger - British term for a sausage

Etymology

From Old French 'saussiche', from Latin 'salsicia', from 'salsus' (salted).
