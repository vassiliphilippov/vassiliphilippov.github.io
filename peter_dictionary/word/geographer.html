geographer

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑɡrəfər / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
A person who studies or is an expert in geography.

Examples

In 1609, the Dutch East India Company hired Hudson, gave him two learned geographers, fitted him out with a ship called the Half Moon, and supplied him with Dutch sailors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartographer - a person who makes maps

Etymology

From Greek geographos (one who writes about the earth), from geo- (earth) and -grapher (writer).
