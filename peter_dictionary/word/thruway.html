thruway

1. NOUN

/ ˈθruˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈθruː.weɪ / (BrE)
A wide, high-speed road designed for fast traffic, especially one with limited access and no intersections.

Examples

And wherever the new thruways go up their banks are lined by neat glass and metal and colored brick light industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressway - a broad highway designed for high-speed traffic
  • Synonym: motorway - a high-speed road, especially in the UK
  • Synonym: highway - a main road for public travel

Etymology

A simplified spelling of 'throughway', modeled after similar terms like 'subway'.
