rummage

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to search through something in a hurried or disorderly way, especially by moving things around or turning them over

Examples

Rummaging through a stack of drawers nearby, you unearthed an antique French chess set in ivory and sandalwood, which, along with two box Kodaks, you added to your haul.
"Rummaging in the dew ", said Rosa coldly.

Synset Examples

We rummaged through the drawers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look carefully through or for something

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *rommelen* (to make a noise, move things about), influenced by obsolete French *arrumer* (to stow cargo).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thorough search for something, often resulting in disorder or confusion

Examples

Tossing cells was just an excuse.

Synset Examples

he gave the attic a good rummage but couldn't find his skis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - the act of looking for something
  • Synonym: ransack - to search thoroughly and often roughly

Etymology

From Middle French *arrumer* (to stow cargo) and obsolete French *romage*, originally referring to arranging cargo in a ship.
