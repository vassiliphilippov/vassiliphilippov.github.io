attentive

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɛn.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈten.tɪv / (BrE)
Paying close attention to something; being careful to notice or listen, or showing care for the needs of others.

Examples

One part of her audience was totally engaged, the connoisseur witnessing a peculiarly fine performance of some ancient classic, the other part, the guest of the connoisseur, attentive as one who must take an intelligent interest in that which he does not fully understand.

Synset Examples

attentive to details
the nurse was attentive to her patient
an attentive suitor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention; heedless
  • Synonym: observant - quick to notice or perceive things

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'attentif', from Latin 'attentivus' (heedful).
