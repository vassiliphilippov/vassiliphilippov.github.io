circumstantially

1. ADV

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈstænʃəli / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstænʃəli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to or depending on the particular circumstances; as far as the circumstances are concerned.

Examples

As far as they're concerned, there's only three ways to spend money when it comes to prisons: more walls, more bars and more guards.

Synset Examples

the account was circumstantially accurate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditionally - depending on certain conditions

Etymology

From circumstantial + -ly, ultimately from Latin circumstantia ('surrounding condition').
