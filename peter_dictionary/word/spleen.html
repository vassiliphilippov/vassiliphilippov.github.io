spleen

1. NOUN

/ spliːn / (AmE), / spliːn / (BrE)
A large, dark-red organ situated on the left side of the abdomen, near the stomach, that plays a role in the immune system by producing and removing blood cells.

Examples

The firm red spleen weighed 410 gm., and its surface was mottled by discrete, small patches of white material.
In addition to the heart and aorta, successful measurements of liver and spleen have also been made by this technique.
It was a spleen crushing day.

Etymology

From Old French 'esplen', from Latin 'splen', from Greek 'splēn'.
