circulate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɝːkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjəleɪt / (BrE)
to cause information, ideas, or rumors to become widely known or to spread widely among people

Examples

Only a few years ago a middle western college circulated a request for a teacher of interior design.
Such was the impromptu that Voltaire gave to howls of laughter at Sans Souci and that was soon circulated in manuscript throughout the literary circles of Europe, to be printed sometime later, but with the name of Timon of Athens, the famous misanthrope, substituted for that of Rousseau.

Synset Examples

spread information
circulate a rumor
broadcast the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to extend or distribute over a larger area or among more people (information, news, etc.)
  • Synonym: broadcast - to make information, news, etc. known to a wide audience

Etymology

From Latin circulare, from circulus meaning 'circle'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjəleɪt / (BrE)
(of information, news, or rumors) to become widely known and passed on from person to person; to spread widely.

Examples

Indeed, from the moment the reports of the coming issue first started circulating in Dallas last January, the inquiries and demand for the stock started building up.
This month a million Guideposts will circulate all over the world.

Synset Examples

the rumor spread
the story went around in the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to affect or become known by more people; to extend over a larger area

Etymology

From Latin circulare (to make circular), from circulus (circle).

3. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjəleɪt / (BrE)
to move continuously around a closed system or area, often returning to the starting point

Examples

And if you should step inside of such a magnified atom, according to the physics of forty years ago, you would see circulating over your head, down at the sides, and under your feet, some twenty luminous balls about the size of footballs.

Synset Examples

Blood circulates in my veins
The air here does not circulate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flow - to move along in a steady and continuous way

Etymology

From Latin 'circulare' (to make circular), from 'circulus' (circle).

4. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjəleɪt / (BrE)
to cause something, such as information, a document, or a fluid, to move around or be distributed within a system or group so that it reaches different places or people

Examples

For cooling, chilled water is circulated instead of hot water.

Synset Examples

This letter is being circulated among the faculty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribute - to give out or spread over an area or among many people

Etymology

From Latin *circulare* (to make round, circulate), from *circulus* (circle).
