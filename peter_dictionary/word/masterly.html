masterly

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæstərli / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəli / (BrE)
Showing or done with great skill or mastery.

Examples

The beautiful, the satisfying part of his disintegration, however, was the masterly way the Buddha polished him off.

Synset Examples

a consummate artist
consummate skill
a masterful speaker
masterful technique
a masterly performance of the sonata
a virtuoso performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterful - having or showing great skill or power
  • Antonym: clumsy - lacking skill or grace

Etymology

From Middle English 'maisterly', from 'maister' (master) + '-ly'.
