silently

1. ADV

/ ˈsaɪləntli / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləntli / (BrE)
In a quiet manner; without making any sound or speaking.

Examples

He watched the half-breed as he turned silently.
The Old Man silently fed upon these streets.
As we stepped out into the sunlight, a man came up to John Cool and silently showed him his hand.

Synset Examples

he sat mutely next to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quietly - with little or no noise; not loudly
  • Antonym: noisily - with much noise or loudness

Etymology

From silent + -ly. 'Silent' from Latin 'silens', present participle of 'silere' (to be quiet).
