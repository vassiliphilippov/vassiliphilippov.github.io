hospitable

1. ADJ

/ hɑˈspɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / hɒˈspɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of an environment or climate) favorable to life, growth, or development.

Examples

But that did not prevent him from writing more long letters, or from coming to spend his Christmas vacations with the hospitable, lively Szolds in their pleasant house on Lombard Street.

Synset Examples

soil sufficiently hospitable for forest growth
a hospitable environment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhospitable - (of an environment) harsh and unfriendly to life or growth

Etymology

From Latin 'hospitalis' (of a guest or host), from 'hospes' (guest, host).
