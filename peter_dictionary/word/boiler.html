boiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ.lə / (BrE)
A closed vessel in which water is heated and converted into steam, often used to power machines or provide heating.

Examples

Are you watching work schedules for boiler operators, guards, and other 24 - hour day, 7 - day week operations in order to minimize overtime?

Etymology

From 'boil' (to heat liquid until it bubbles) + '-er' (agent noun suffix), attested from the 18th century.
