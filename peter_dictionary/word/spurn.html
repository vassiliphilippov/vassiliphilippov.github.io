spurn

1. VERB

/ spɝn / (AmE), / spɜːn / (BrE)
to reject someone or something with disdain or contempt

Examples

Kidnapper Spurns Ransom; Amy Still Missing.
Everyone had heard of the American contractor who had spurned an escort.

Synset Examples

She spurned his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, use, or believe someone or something
  • Antonym: accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered

Etymology

From Old English 'spurnan' (to kick, reject).
