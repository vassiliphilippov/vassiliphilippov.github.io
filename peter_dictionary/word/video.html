video

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
A recording that contains both visual and audio elements, especially one of a movie, television program, or similar content.

Examples

This dissatisfaction led to Howsam's request that the video not be terminated before the end of the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audiovisual - involving both hearing and sight

Etymology

Shortened from 'videotape', from Latin 'videre' (to see).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
The visual component of a television broadcast or transmission, as opposed to the audio or sound portion.

Examples

The video signal is amplified and then switched, in synchronism with the three ultraviolet light sources which are sequenced by the rotating mirror so that during one-twentieth of a second only one wavelength, corresponding to red, green, or blue, is seen.

Synset Examples

they could still receive the sound but the picture was gone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: audio - the sound component of a television broadcast

Etymology

From Latin 'video' (I see), adopted in 1930s-40s for moving images technology.
