working

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɝkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a theory, plan, or document) adopted temporarily as a basis for further work, and subject to change or refinement.

Examples

At least this seemed to be the working hypothesis for "Chicago and All That Jazz ", presented on NBC - TV Nov. 26.

Synset Examples

a working draft
a working hypothesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provisional - existing or accepted for the present, possibly to be changed later
  • Antonym: definitive - final and not subject to change

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
serving to permit or facilitate further work or activity; designed or intended for practical use while activity continues

Examples

It follows that any effective policy toward the underdeveloped countries must have a realistically long working horizon.

Synset Examples

discussed the working draft of a peace treaty
they need working agreements with their neighbor states on interstate projects

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a machine, system, etc.) Performing or capable of performing its intended function; operating properly.

Examples

This circumstance in the patient's case plus the fact that his tactual capacity remained basically in sound working order constitutes its exceptional value for the problem at hand since the evidence presented by the authors is overwhelming that, when the patient closed his eyes, he had absolutely no spatial (that is, third-dimensional) awareness whatsoever.

Synset Examples

in running (or working) order
a functional set of brakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - in working order; operating as intended
  • Antonym: broken - not working; damaged and not operational

Etymology

Present participle of 'work', from Old English 'wyrcan'.

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of knowledge, a definition, majority, etc.) adequate for practical use; sufficient for a particular purpose.

Examples

Similarly, a girl who graduates with a good working knowledge of stenography and the use of clerical machines and who is able to get a job at once may wish to improve her skill and knowledge by a year or two of further study in a community college or secretarial school.
