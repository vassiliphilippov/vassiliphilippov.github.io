dash

1. NOUN

/ dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
A short, quick run, especially in a race or to reach something quickly.

Examples

This is n't surprising when we consider that over 29 percent of the 11 - year old boys in America cannot chin themselves once, and that English school girls outdo them in almost every test (even dashes and endurance).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprint - a short, fast run, especially in a race

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *daschen*, to strike or move violently.

2. VERB

/ dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
to run or move quickly and suddenly, often with urgency or excitement

Examples

She dashes around in alarm.
That absence of an urgent self-indulgence dashed them awake like a pail of water.
When a second quake came, they dashed back to the beach, fearing that they might be buried under landslides.
He dashed madly for the next elbow turn in the draw, and made it.
Then they had always been romping around him on these walks, yelping with delight, dashing off into the bushes on fruitless hunting expeditions, returning to jump up on him triumphantly with muddy paws.

Synset Examples

She dashed into the yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dart - move or run somewhere suddenly or rapidly
  • Synonym: rush - move with urgent haste
  • Antonym: saunter - walk in a slow, relaxed manner
  • Antonym: stroll - walk in a leisurely way

Etymology

From Middle English daschen, perhaps from Middle French dacher (to strike, hit, impel suddenly).

3. VERB

/ dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
to break into pieces by striking or knocking against something forcefully

Examples

Half crazed by the weight dragging, the dust, and the heat, the horse leaped our wall, dashing out the rider's brains against it, and leaving him lying there among us - while the horse crashed away through the brush.
I did n't offer any advice, but I certainly did not want to go back to where the officer lay with his brains dashed out.

Synset Examples

Smash a plate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smash - to break into pieces violently
  • Synonym: shatter - to break suddenly into many small pieces
  • Synonym: crush - to compress or break with force

Etymology

From Middle English dashen, likely of imitative origin, suggesting a forceful impact.

4. NOUN

/ dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
stylish and spirited elegance in manner or appearance; flair

Examples

Then epistolatory me was a foreign correspondent dispatching exciting cables and communiques, full of dash and wit and glamor, quoting from the books I read, imitating the grand styles of the authors recommended by a teacher in whose special, after school class I was enrolled.

Synset Examples

he wooed her with the confident dash of a cavalry officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flair - stylishness and originality; natural elegance
  • Synonym: panache - distinctive and stylish elegance
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of excitement or flair

Etymology

From Middle English dachen (to strike), of imitative origin. Sense of 'energy, flair' developed in the 19th century.
