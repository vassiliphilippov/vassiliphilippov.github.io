hood

1. NOUN

/ hʊd / (AmE), / hʊd / (BrE)
(informal, US slang) A young person, especially a man, who is part of a gang or regularly commits violent or criminal acts.

Examples

Eight or ten years ago, a couple of French hoods stole a priceless Khmer head from the Musee Guimet, in Paris, and a week later crawled into the Salpetriere with unmistakable symptoms of leprosy.
I thought all you Guinea hoods were locked up! -

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoodlum - a violent criminal or troublemaker
  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal
  • Synonym: gangster - a member of a gang involved in crime

Etymology

Shortened from 'hoodlum', originated in American English slang in the 19th century.
