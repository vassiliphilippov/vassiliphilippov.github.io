baptism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæp.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The Christian sacrament of admittance to the church, usually involving the ritual use of water as a sign of spiritual cleansing and rebirth.

Examples

After the baptism.

Synset Examples

most churches baptize infants but some insist on adult baptism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: christening - the ceremony of baptizing, especially for infants

Etymology

From Greek *baptisma* (immersion, washing), from *baptizein* (to immerse, to dip).
