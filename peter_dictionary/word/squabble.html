squabble

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɒbəl / (BrE)
a noisy quarrel about something trivial or petty

Examples

In all the bitter in-fighting, the squabbles over election procedures, the complicated numbers game that East and West played on the assistant secretaries' theme, the gentleman from Burma showed himself both as a man of principle and a skilled diplomat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicker - to argue about unimportant matters
  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement
  • Degree Higher: fight - a violent confrontation or struggle
  • Degree Higher: dispute - an argument or disagreement

Etymology

Probably a frequentative of 'squab' (young bird), perhaps imitating the sound of squawking.
