abide

1. VERB

/ əˈbaɪd / (AmE), / əˈbaɪd / (BrE)
to remain or continue in a particular state, place, or condition

Examples

Before He left them He promised that His peace would be their portion to abide in their hearts and minds.
He who does not love abides in death.

Synset Examples

You can stay with me while you are in town
stay a bit longer--the day is still young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remain - to continue to exist, especially after other parts have gone or been removed
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place or person

Etymology

Old English abīdan (remain, wait for), from ā- (prefix) + bīdan (to bide, wait).
