underbid

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈbɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbɪd / (BrE)
to offer to do work or provide goods at a price lower than that offered by a competitor, especially in an auction or contract bidding process

Examples

This pool of slave labor you've got, you can underbid any contractor in town.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outbid - to offer a higher price than another in a bidding process

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'less than') and 'bid'.
