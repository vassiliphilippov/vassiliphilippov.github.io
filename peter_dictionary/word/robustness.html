robustness

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbʌst.nəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈbʌst.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being strong, healthy, and resilient in constitution.

Examples

William Gilmore Simms, sturdy realist that he was, pleaded for a natural robustness such as he found in his favorites the great Elizabethans, to vivify the pale writings being produced around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • Antonym: frailty - the condition of being weak and delicate

Etymology

From 'robust', from Latin robustus (strong, hardy), from robur (oak, strength).
