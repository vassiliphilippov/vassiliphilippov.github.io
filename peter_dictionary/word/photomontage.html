photomontage

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊmɒnˈtɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊmɒnˈtɑːʒ / (BrE)
A picture or image created by combining several photographs, often to produce a surreal or artistic effect.

Examples

They were not diplomats or jazz musicians, or even organizers of reading-rooms and photo montage displays, but rugged capitalist entrepreneurs like Henry Ford, Hugh Cooper, Thomas Campbell, the International Harvester Co., and David W. Griffith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collage - an artistic composition made of various materials, sometimes including photographs

Etymology

From French 'photomontage', combining 'photo' (photograph) and 'montage' (mounting, assembling).
