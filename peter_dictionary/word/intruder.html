intruder

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtruːdər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːdə / (BrE)
A person who enters a place without permission, especially to commit a crime or disturb privacy.

Examples

Thus, the larvae of the intruder can develop at the expense of the rightful inhabitants and the store of beebread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespasser - someone who enters property without permission

Etymology

From Latin 'intrudere' meaning 'to thrust in'.
