median

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiən / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiən / (BrE)
Relating to or constituting the middle value in an ordered set of numbers, or the average of the middle two when there is an even number of values.

Examples

The battery median grade equivalent was used in data analysis in this study.

Synset Examples

the median value of 17, 20, and 36 is 20
the median income for the year was $15,000

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mean - average value of a set, obtained by dividing the sum by the number of terms
  • Antonym: mode - value that appears most frequently in a data set

Etymology

From Latin 'medianus' (of the middle), from 'medius' (middle).
