annual

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn.ju.əl / (AmE), / ˈæn.ju.əl / (BrE)
(of a plant) living or lasting only for one year or season; completing its full life cycle within a single year.

Examples

The crowd at the twenty-first annual K. of C. Games, final indoor meet of the season, got a thrill a few minutes earlier when a slender, bespectacled woman broke the one week old world record in the half-mile run.

Synset Examples

a border of annual flowering plants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perennial - (of a plant) living for more than two years
  • Antonym: biennial - (of a plant) completing its life cycle in two years

Etymology

From Latin 'annuus' (yearly), from 'annus' (year).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæn.ju.əl / (AmE), / ˈæn.ju.əl / (BrE)
Happening or payable once every year.

Examples

Appropriations Committee voted an annual payment of $500 just to shut him up.

Synset Examples

an annual trip to Paris
yearly medical examinations
annual (or yearly) income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yearly - occurring every year
  • Degree Lower: biennial - occurring every two years
  • Degree Higher: perennial - lasting or existing for a long or apparently infinite time (not limited to a year)

Etymology

From Latin 'annualis', from 'annus' (year).
