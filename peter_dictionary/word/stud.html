stud

1. VERB

/ stʌd / (AmE), / stʌd / (BrE)
to scatter or cover (a surface or area) with objects, features, or embellishments resembling dots or studs

Examples

But then, Mario Lanza was no common singer, and his whole career, public and non-public, was studded with the kind of unconventional happenings that terminate with the appearance of his first "recital "only when he has ceased to be a living voice.
It may be a big hole in the ground filled with salt hay, or an ice floe studded with logs.
Travelers entering from the desert were confounded by what must have seemed an illusion: a great garden filled with nightingales and roses, cut by canals and terraced promenades, studded with water tanks of turquoise tile in which were reflected the glistening blue curves of a hundred domes.

Synset Examples

Hills constellated with lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dot - to mark with a small round spot or spots
  • Synonym: speckle - to mark or cover with a large number of small spots or patches

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'studu' (post, pillar); verbal use from the sense of 'placing studs on'.

2. NOUN

/ stʌd / (AmE), / stʌd / (BrE)
An adult male horse kept for breeding purposes.

Examples

I'm going to put him out to stud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stallion - an uncastrated adult male horse

Etymology

From Old English 'stod' (herd of horses, stud), reinforced by Old Norse 'stoð' (herd of horses).
