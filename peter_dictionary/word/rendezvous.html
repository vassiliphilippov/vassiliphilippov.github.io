rendezvous

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːndɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒndɪvuː / (BrE)
A meeting arranged for a specific time and place; the act or an instance of coming together by agreement.

Examples

He walked toward the center of the city to keep his rendezvous with Styka.
In December I wrote her with authority that we would meet on the steps of the Hotel Astor, a rendezvous spot that I had learned was the most sophisticated.
The show was colorful, indeed, exuberant, but the press for all its assiduity could detect no note of a fateful rendezvous with destiny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - an arrangement to meet at a particular time

Etymology

From French "rendez-vous" meaning ‘present yourselves!’; imperative of "se rendre" (to present oneself) + "vous" (you, plural).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːn.deɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.deɪˌvuː / (BrE)
A secret meeting arranged, especially between lovers.

Examples

I got a date.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tryst - A secret meeting, especially between lovers

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', meaning 'present yourselves', from 'rendez' (present, imperative of 'rendre'), and 'vous' (you, plural).
