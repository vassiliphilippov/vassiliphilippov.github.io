baste

1. VERB

/ beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
to pour melted fat, meat juices, or other liquid over food while it is cooking, typically to keep it moist or enhance flavor

Examples

Do n't forget to buy a plastic pastry brush for basting with sauces.
You will discover you keep the sauce for basting meat in one, use six for drinks, serve soup or coffee in another half-dozen - and need one more to mix the salad dressing.

Synset Examples

baste a roast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basting - the act of moistening food with liquid while cooking

Etymology

From Middle English *basten* ('to moisten'), from Old French *bastir* ('to build, prepare').
