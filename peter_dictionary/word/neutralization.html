neutralization

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːtrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The action or process of making a country politically neutral, especially by agreement, or of excluding it from involvement in a war or conflict.

Examples

A nation such as Switzerland could be neutralized by agreement and could be relied upon to protect its neutrality; more doubtful, but possible, (with an assist from the North) was the neutralization of the Latin American countries; out of the question was the neutralization of Asia and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict

Etymology

From French 'neutralisation', from 'neutraliser' (to neutralize), ultimately from Latin 'neutralis' (neutral).
