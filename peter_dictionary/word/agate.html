agate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡ.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈæɡ.ət / (BrE)
A semi-precious stone consisting of banded chalcedony, a variety of quartz, typically used as a gemstone or in decorative items.

Examples

An old shipwreck, a high dam, an old covered bridge, a place to find agates or other semi-precious stones or a place to pan gold, etcetera may be of interest.

Etymology

From Greek 'agathos' via Latin 'achates', originally named after the river Achates in Sicily where agate was found.
