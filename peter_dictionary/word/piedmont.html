Piedmont

1. PROPN

/ ˈpiˌædmɑnt / (AmE), / ˈpiːdmɒnt / (BrE)
A plateau region between the coastal plain and the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States, extending from New Jersey to Alabama, including parts of Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, and Alabama.

Examples

Old attitudes are held more tenaciously in the Tidewater than the Piedmont; so that a line running down the length of the South marking the upper limits of tidewater would roughly divide the Old South from the new, but with, of course, important minority enclaves.

Etymology

From Italian 'Piemonte', meaning 'foot of the mountain'.
