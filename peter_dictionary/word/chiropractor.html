chiropractor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪrəˌpræktər / (AmE), / ˈkaɪərəˌpræktə / (BrE)
A practitioner who treats disorders of the musculoskeletal system, especially the spine, by manipulating and adjusting the body, often without the use of drugs or surgery.

Examples

Sarah Gross, a dress shop proprietor, paid $ 1020 to a masseur, and Mr. A., a laborer, paid $ 4200 to a chiropractor for treatment with two fake health machines - the "radioclast "and the "diagnometer ".
"Neither me nor my wife were helped by that chiropractor's treatments ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteopath - a medical professional treating muscular and skeletal issues, often by manipulation
  • Synonym: physiotherapist - a healthcare professional treating injury or dysfunction by physical methods

Etymology

From Greek *kheir* (hand) + *praktikos* (practical), via English 'chiropractic'.
