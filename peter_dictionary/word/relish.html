relish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪʃ / (BrE)
vigorous and enthusiastic enjoyment or appreciation of something

Examples

But the brigadier dines on the birds with relish.
The jaded amorist conjured up pictures of the blasphemous rites with relish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gusto - vigorous and enthusiastic enjoyment
  • Synonym: zest - eager and enthusiastic enjoyment

Etymology

From Old French *reles* (remainder, taste left behind), from relaisser (to leave behind), from Latin *relinquere* (to leave behind).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪʃ / (BrE)
to enjoy or take great pleasure in something

Examples

She 'll relish the sight of a friendly face.

Synset Examples

She relished her fame and basked in her glory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enjoy - to take delight or pleasure in
  • Synonym: savor - to taste and enjoy completely

Etymology

From Old French *reles* (remainder, taste left in mouth), from relaisser (to leave behind), ultimately from Latin *relinquere* (to leave behind).
