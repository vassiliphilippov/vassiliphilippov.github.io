hubris

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːbrɪs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːbrɪs / (BrE)
Excessive pride or self-confidence, often leading to downfall.

Examples

His hubris, deficiency of taste, and sadism carried him straightaway to the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - overbearing pride; haughtiness
  • Antonym: humility - modest view of one's importance
  • Degree Lower: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements

Etymology

From ancient Greek ὕβρις (hybris), meaning 'wanton violence, insolence, pride'.
