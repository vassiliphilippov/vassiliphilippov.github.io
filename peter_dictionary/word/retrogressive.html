retrogressive

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
Characterized by or relating to movement from a better to a worse state; tending to revert to an earlier and less advanced condition.

Examples

In our present era of Science and Angst, the heart has been downgraded, to use one of our popular retrogressive verbs ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - characterized by advancing or improvement

Etymology

From Latin retrogressus (having gone back), from retrogradi (to go backward).
