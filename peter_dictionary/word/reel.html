reel

1. NOUN

/ ril / (AmE), / riːl / (BrE)
a roll of photographic film containing a sequence of frames for projection in a movie projector

Examples

The Great Train Robbery is a one reel film.
One reel - from eight to twelve minutes - became the standard length from the year of Robbery, 1903, until Griffith shattered that limit forever with Birth of a Nation in 1915.
The reel itself became and still is the standard of measure for the movies.

Etymology

From Middle English *reel* (winding device), from Old English *hrēol* (reel, winding device).

2. VERB

/ riːl / (AmE), / riːl / (BrE)
to spin or revolve quickly and repeatedly around one's own axis

Examples

Cobb unleashed a single powerful jab that sent Gyp reeling wildly and crashing down with a whining groan.

Synset Examples

The dervishes whirl around and around without getting dizzy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirl - to move or cause to move rapidly around and around
  • Synonym: spin - to turn around and around quickly

Etymology

From Middle English *relen* (to whirl), possibly related to Old Norse *rulla* (to roll).

3. VERB

/ ril / (AmE), / riːl / (BrE)
To wind or unwind something onto or off a reel.

Examples

They don't quit till they reel someone in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind - to turn or twist something flexible around and around a central point or object

Etymology

From Middle English *rele*, from Old English *hrēol* (a reel, spool).

4. NOUN

/ ril / (AmE), / riːl / (BrE)
a round object or device on which thread, film, tape, or similar material is wound for storage or use

Examples

- I gotta change the reel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spool - a cylindrical object on which thread or wire is wound
  • Synonym: bobbin - a cylinder or spindle on which thread or yarn is wound, typically used in sewing machines

Etymology

From Old English *hrēol* (reel, winding spool).
