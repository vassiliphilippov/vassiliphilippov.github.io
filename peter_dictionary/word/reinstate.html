reinstate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to restore someone or something to a previous position, status, or condition

Examples

After a flood of protests, they were reinstated at the beginning of 1953.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restore - to return something to its former condition or position

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'instaurare' (to restore).
