clock

1. NOUN

/ klɑk / (AmE), / klɒk / (BrE)
A device used for measuring and indicating the time of day, typically by means of hands moving on a dial or by digital display.

Examples

Although he never gets to play while the clock is running, he gets a big kick - several every Saturday, in fact - out of football.
On the clock given him was the inscription, "For Outstanding Contribution to Billiken Basketball, 1960 - 61 ".
The clock which had struck presented an innocent face.
And the loudest clock in the whole world "!
The clock you heard strike - it's really the town clock - was installed last April by Mrs. Shorter, on her birthday ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - A small timepiece worn typically on the wrist
  • Synonym: timer - A device that measures time intervals

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *clocke* (bell, clock), from Medieval Latin *clocca* (bell).

2. VERB

/ klɑk / (AmE), / klɒk / (BrE)
to measure or record the time taken by something or someone, especially in a race or event

Examples

- The best 2 - year-old pacing mile up to date at Ben White Raceway has been that of Mary Liner (Mainliner-Highland Ellen), a member of the Dick Williams stable, who was clocked 2: 25.

Synset Examples

he clocked the runners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time - to measure the time required for something

Etymology

From the noun 'clock', originally from Middle Dutch 'clocke' (bell, clock), via Old French.
