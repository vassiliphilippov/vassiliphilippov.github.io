understatement

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
A statement that makes something seem less important, serious, or severe than it really is, often for ironic effect.

Examples

This, for the liberals I know, would be an understatement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overstatement - statement made more strongly than justified
  • Synonym: euphemism - mild or indirect word or phrase substituted for one considered too harsh

Etymology

From 'understate' + '-ment', meaning the action or result of understating.
