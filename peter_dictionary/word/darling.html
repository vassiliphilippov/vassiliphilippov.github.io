darling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who is very much loved or especially dear, often used as a term of endearment

Examples

"Darling, I love that photograph.
So young Prokofieff was the darling of success:
"Come on, darling.
Hello, darling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dear - a loved or cherished person; term of endearment

Etymology

From Middle English derling, from Old English dēorling (favorite, darling), from dēore (dear) + -ling (diminutive suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
Loved or cherished very much; dearly loved.

Examples

"Mike darling "- They kissed, and went on kissing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beloved - dearly loved
  • Synonym: dear - loved or valued very much

Etymology

From Old English 'deorling' (darling, favourite, lover), from 'deore' (dear).
