non-resistant

1. NOUN

/ nɑn rɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / nɒn rɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
A person who advocates or practices non-resistance, especially someone who believes in achieving reform through passive resistance rather than force.

Examples

In reference to Brown's raid she wrote, "though we are non-resistants and religiously believe it better to reform by moral and not by carnal weapons we know thee was anemated [ sic ] by the most generous and philanthropic motives ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifist - a person who believes that war and violence are unjustifiable
  • Antonym: resister - a person who opposes something or someone

Etymology

Formed from non- (not) + resistant, from Latin resistere (to withstand).
