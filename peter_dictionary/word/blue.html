blue

1. ADJ

/ bluː / (AmE), / bluː / (BrE)
Of the color between green and violet in the spectrum; having the color of a clear unclouded sky.

Examples

Orange and lemon are considered important as are such pastels as blue and lilac.
In a brighter nautical vein is Ile de France blue.
Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.
The human body - he pointed out, for example - required 33 units of blue light.
For that reason, he informed her, the Lord made the sky blue.

Synset Examples

October's bright blue weather
a blue flame
blue haze of tobacco smoke

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: green - of the color between blue and yellow in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: violet - of the color at the end of the visible spectrum, between blue and ultraviolet
  • Synonym: azure - bright blue in color, like a cloudless sky

Etymology

From Old French *bleu*, probably of Germanic origin (compare Old High German *blāo* ‘blue’).

2. NOUN

/ bluː / (AmE), / bluː / (BrE)
A color intermediate between green and violet in the spectrum, resembling the clear sky on a sunny day.

Examples

She swung her eyes up to the blue of the window, her jaws gently mashing the bitter beans.
The velvet smoking jackets, pearl gray, wine, and blue, which Miss Ada had bought him hung brushed and unworn in the closet.
Already the jonquils were blooming in a flock by the front gate, and the periwinkles were coming on, blue by the porch steps.
Miss Ada was looking fine; she had on her Easter suit, blue, with lavender binding.
I hope the Pacific is as blue as it has been in my dreams.

Synset Examples

he had eyes of bright blue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: red - the color resembling blood or a ripe tomato
  • Degree Lower: green - the color between blue and yellow in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: violet - a color at the end of the spectrum, between blue and ultraviolet

Etymology

From Old French *bleu*, of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ blu / (AmE), / blu / (BrE)
a member of an organization, especially a military force, whose uniforms or badges are blue, sometimes used specifically for Union soldiers during the American Civil War

Examples

Indeed, no richer humor is to be found in the whole of American literature than in the letters of the semi-literate men who wore the blue and the gray.

Synset Examples

the Union army was a vast blue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gray - a member of an organization, especially a military force, whose uniforms or badges are gray, esp. Confederate soldier
