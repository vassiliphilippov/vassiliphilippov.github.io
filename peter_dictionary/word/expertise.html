expertise

1. NOUN

/ ɛk.spɚˈtiz / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɜːˈtiːz / (BrE)
Expert skill or knowledge in a particular field or subject.

Examples

The assisting musicians from the Vienna Octet are somewhat lacking in expertise, but their contribution is rustic and appealing.
Its truth is illustrated by the skill, sensitivity, and general expertise of the English professor with whom one attends the theatre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well
  • Synonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • Antonym: inexperience - lack of experience or skill

Etymology

From French 'expertise' (assessment, expert opinion), from 'expert' (expert).
