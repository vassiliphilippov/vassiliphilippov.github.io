inscribe

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to carve, cut, or etch words, designs, or marks into a surface, typically for the purpose of recording or commemorating something

Examples

In the following paper it is shown that in a certain definite sense, exactly an odd number of squares can be inscribed in every such curve which does not contain an infinite number of inscribed squares.
Inscribed around its base is a charm in Balinese, a dialect I take it you do n't comprehend.

Synset Examples

engrave a pen
engraved the trophy cupt with the winner's
the lovers scratched their names into the bark of the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrave - to cut or carve a text or design on the surface of a hard object

Etymology

From Latin inscribere, from in- ‘in’ + scribere ‘to write’.
