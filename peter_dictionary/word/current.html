1. ADJ

/ ˈkɝː.ənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
happening or existing now; belonging to the present time.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporary - existing or happening in the same period of time as something else, especially the present
  • Antonym: past - relating to a time that has gone by; completed or finished
  • Antonym: future - relating to a time that is to come

Etymology

From Latin 'currens', present participle of 'currere' (to run), via Old French 'courant'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːrənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
a continuous, steady flow of water, air, or another fluid, especially in a particular direction

Examples

The flat-bottomed boat swung slowly to the pull of the current.
The dangerous current upon the prairie ceased, but the water stood and kept on rising.
She was watching a tree ride wildly down that roiling current.

Related Terms
