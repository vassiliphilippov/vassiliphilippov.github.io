exonerate

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɑːnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒnəreɪt / (BrE)
To officially declare that someone is not guilty of a particular crime or wrongdoing; to clear from blame or responsibility.

Examples

In order to exonerate himself, he is compelled to find the real criminal, who happens to be his girl friend.

Synset Examples

The suspect was cleared of the murder charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquit - to officially declare not guilty of a crime
  • Antonym: convict - to find or declare guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Latin exonerare (to unburden, discharge), from ex- (out) + onus (burden).
