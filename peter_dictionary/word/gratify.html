gratify

1. VERB

/ ˈɡræt̬.ə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to give pleasure or satisfaction to (someone); to make happy or contented

Examples

A need so deeply planted, asking for direction, so to speak, is likely to be gratified by the vivid examples and heroic proportions of literature.
Washington castigated his critic, General Conway, as being capable of "all the meanness of intrigue to gratify the absurd resentment of disappointed vanity ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfy - fulfil the desires or needs of (someone)
  • Synonym: please - to make someone feel happy or contented
  • Antonym: displease - to cause someone to feel unhappy or annoyed

Etymology

From Latin gratificari (give as a favor, oblige), from gratus (pleasing).
