anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌænɪsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Loss of bodily sensation, with or without loss of consciousness, especially as induced by medical procedures.

Examples

It is the obverse of triviality, shallowness, emotional anaesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - American spelling of anaesthesia

Etymology

From Greek 'anaisthēsia' (lack of sensation), from 'an-' (without) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
