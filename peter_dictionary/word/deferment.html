deferment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɝːmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜːmənt / (BrE)
The action of delaying or postponing something to a later time.

Examples

I didn'’t ask for deferment, I didn'’t want it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the action of delaying something

Etymology

From the verb 'defer' + -ment; 'defer' from Latin 'deferre' (to put off, postpone).
