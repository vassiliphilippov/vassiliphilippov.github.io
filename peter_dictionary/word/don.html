don

1. VERB

/ dɑn / (AmE), / dɒn / (BrE)
to put on a piece of clothing

Examples

Evenings, he was frequently to be seen at restaurants like Enrico + Paglieri's or Peter's Backyard drunkenly donning ladies' hats and singing "O Sole Mio ".
Those who had slickers donned them.
Mike stripped these from him and donned them.

Synset Examples

What should I wear today?
He put on his best suit for the wedding
The princess donned a long blue dress
The queen assumed the stately robes
He got into his jeans

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doff - to take off a piece of clothing
  • Synonym: put on - to place clothing or accessories onto one's body

Etymology

From Middle English 'don', a contraction of 'do on' (put on).

2. NOUN

/ dɑn / (AmE), / dɒn / (BrE)
the head of an organized crime family, especially in the Mafia

Examples

You'’re going to be the Don.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boss - leader of a criminal organization

Etymology

From Italian 'don', an honorific title meaning 'lord' or 'sir'.

3. NOUN

the head of an organized crime family, especially in the Mafia

Examples

Did you serve under Caporegime, Peter Clemenza, and under Vito Corleone, also known as the Godfather?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godfather - the head of a criminal organization, especially the Mafia

From Italian 'don' (title of respect), from Latin 'dominus' (lord, master).
