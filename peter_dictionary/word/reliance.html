reliance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
Firm dependence or confidence in someone or something, especially based on past experience.

Examples

As Yinger has pointed out, the "reliance on symbols, on tradition, on sacred writings, on the cultivation of emotional feelings of identity and harmony with sacred values, turns one to the past far more than to the future ".

Synset Examples

he wrote the paper with considerable reliance on the work of other scientists
he put more trust in his own two legs than in the gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability of someone or something
  • Synonym: dependence - state of relying on something or someone for support
  • Antonym: doubt - feeling of uncertainty or lack of confidence

Etymology

From Old French relais, from relier ‘to bind fast’, influenced by rely.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
The state of depending on or trusting in something or someone.

Examples

Here again, in the written language it is possible to help the reader get his stresses right by using underlining or italics, but much of the time there is simply reliance on his understanding in the light of context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by something else
  • Antonym: independence - the state of not relying on others

Etymology

From Old French 'reliance', from 'relier' (to bind together), from Latin 'religare'.
