gallows

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæloʊz / (AmE), / ˈɡaləʊz / (BrE)
A structure, typically of two upright posts and a crossbeam, used for executing people by hanging.

Examples

The head was then fixed on a pole at Westminster, and the rest of the body was buried under the gallows.

Etymology

From Old English *galga* (gallows, cross), related to Old Norse *galgi* (gallows).
