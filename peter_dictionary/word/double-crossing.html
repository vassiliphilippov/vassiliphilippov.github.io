double-crossing

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlˈkrɑsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of betraying someone, especially after previously pretending to be loyal.

Examples

For three years, since the liquor territorial conference, Torrio had, with his elastic patience, and because he knew that retaliation could cause only violent warfare and disaster to business, tolerated O' Banion's impudent double-crossing.

Synset Examples

he gave us the old double cross
I could no longer tolerate his impudent double-crossing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayal - the act of betraying someone or something
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal; the opposite of betrayal

Etymology

From the phrase "double cross," originally referring to marking a bet with two crosses to indicate cheating or deception (early 20th century).
