par

1. NOUN

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
(in golf) the number of strokes a skilled golfer is expected to take to complete a particular hole or the entire course.

Examples

A couple of weeks ago, he scored a monstrous 12 on a par 5 hole.
The world's best golfer, shooting below par, came to the last hole of the opening round of the Los Angeles open with every intention of delivering a final crusher.

Synset Examples

a par-5 hole
par for this course is 72

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: birdie - a score of one stroke under par on a hole (golf)
  • Degree Higher: bogey - a score of one stroke over par on a hole (golf)

Etymology

From Latin par (equal). Adopted in golf in the late 19th century to mean the standard score for a hole.

2. NOUN

/ pɑr / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
The state of being equal or equivalent to someone or something; the same level or standard as another.

Examples

Its growth continues steadily on a par with past growth; and no limitation is in evidence.

Synset Examples

on a par with the best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal - being the same in quantity, size, degree, or value

Etymology

From Latin 'par' (equal).
