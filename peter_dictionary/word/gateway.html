gateway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪtˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪtweɪ / (BrE)
An entrance or opening that can be closed by a gate, especially one forming part of a wall or fence.

Examples

On August 3, two massive headlands reared out of the mists - great gateways never before, so far as Hudson knew, seen by Europeans.

Etymology

From Middle English 'gat' (gate) + 'wey' (way), meaning a way through a gate.
