piezoelectricity

1. NOUN

/ piˌɛzoʊˌɛlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (AmE), / piˌɛtsəʊˌɪlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (BrE)
Electricity produced when mechanical pressure is applied to certain crystals, or a change in the shape of a crystal when an electrical voltage is applied.

Examples

The x-ray data did not permit Douglass to determine uniquely the space group, but a negative test for piezoelectricity led him to assume a center of symmetry.

Etymology

From Greek 'piezein' (to press) and 'electricity'.
