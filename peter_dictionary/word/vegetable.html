vegetable

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒtəb(ə)l / (BrE)
A plant or part of a plant used as food, typically as an accompaniment to meat or fish, such as leaves, roots, stems, and non-sweet fruits.

Also fine are Sue Lawless, as a mother more protective and belligerent than a female spider and just as destructive, Harold Cherry, as her scratchy spouse, and Hildy Weissman, as a vegetable in human form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fruit - edible, typically sweet product of a plant containing seeds

Etymology

From Old French 'vegetable' (living, growing), from Latin 'vegetabilis' (animating, enlivening), from 'vegetare' (to enliven).
