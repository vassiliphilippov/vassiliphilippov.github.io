uneven

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
not level, smooth, or regular in surface, shape, or quality; lacking uniformity or equality

Examples

Somewhat uneven in interest for an average reader, eight or ten of these are among the finest of their kind in literature.

Synset Examples

an uneven color
uneven ground
uneven margins
wood with an uneven grain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: even - level, smooth, or regular in surface, shape, or quality

Etymology

from un- (not) + even, Old English 'efen' (level, equal)

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
(of a contest or competitors) not fairly matched; having a significant difference in strength, ability, or chance of success.

Examples

The totals for these five categories are not too uneven, namely 20, 15, 11, 16, 9 respectively.

Synset Examples

vaudeville...waged an uneven battle against the church

Related Terms

  • Antonym: even - (of a contest) fairly matched; equal in strength or ability

Etymology

From un- (not) + even, where 'even' comes from Old English 'efen' meaning level or equal.
