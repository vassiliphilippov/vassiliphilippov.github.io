symptomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪmp.təˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmptəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
showing symptoms of a particular disease or indicative of a specific condition or problem.

Examples

The flood is not as great - only 700 a week according to one apparently conservative account - but it is symptomatic.

Synset Examples

a diagnostic sign of yellow fever
a rash symptomatic of scarlet fever
symptomatic of insanity
a rise in crime symptomatic of social breakdown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnostic - characteristic or indicative of a disease or condition
  • Antonym: asymptomatic - showing no symptoms of disease

Etymology

From Medieval Latin symptomāticus, from Greek symptomatikos, from symptom.
