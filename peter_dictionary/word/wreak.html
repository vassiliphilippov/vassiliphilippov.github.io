wreak

1. VERB

/ rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
to cause something harmful or damaging to happen

Examples

Almost febrile in intensity, the principle has become worldwide in application - unfortunately at the very time that nationalist fervors can wreak greatest harm.

Synset Examples

I cannot work a miracle
wreak havoc
bring comments
play a joke
The rain brought relief to the drought-stricken area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflict - to cause something unpleasant or painful to be suffered by someone

Etymology

Old English *wrecan* (to drive out, avenge), related to German *rächen* (to avenge).
