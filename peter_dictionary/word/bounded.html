bounded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndɪd / (BrE)
Having fixed limits or boundaries; confined within certain bounds.

Examples

This theorem is similar to the theorem of Kakutani that there exists a circumscribing cube around any closed, bounded convex set in **f.
Then every component of the graph of f must be defined over a bounded sub-interval.
This is true of all components which have such a bounded support.

Synset Examples

a delimited frontier through the disputed region

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbounded - without limits or boundaries

Etymology

From the past participle of 'bound', from Middle English 'bounden', from Old French 'bonder' (to set boundaries).
