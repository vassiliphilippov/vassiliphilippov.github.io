perky

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝːki / (AmE), / ˈpɜːki / (BrE)
cheerful, lively, and full of energy or lightheartedness.

Examples

Sparrow size Virginia Gibson, with sparkling blue eyes and a cheerful smile, made a suitably perky Amy, while Melisande Congdon, as the real aunt, was positively monumental in the very best Gibson Girl manner.
Pansies are supposed to like it cool, but those great velvety flowers were healthy and perky in the glaring sun.

Synset Examples

buoyant spirits
his quick wit and chirpy humor
looking bright and well and chirpy
a perky little widow in her 70s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buoyant - cheerful and optimistic
  • Synonym: chirpy - cheerful and lively, especially in manner or mood
  • Antonym: downcast - unhappy or lacking in spirits
  • Antonym: dejected - sad or dispirited

Etymology

Perhaps from dialect perky (lively, spirited), probably from perke (perk, to perch up).
