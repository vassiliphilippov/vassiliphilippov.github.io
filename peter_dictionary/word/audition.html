audition

1. VERB

/ ɔˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɔːˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to perform or present a trial piece in order to obtain a role as an actor, singer, musician, etc.

Examples

"I 'd love to audition for you ", she gushed.
It seemed as though she were always auditioning.

Synset Examples

She auditioned for a role on Broadway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try out - to attempt to qualify for a role by performing

Etymology

From Latin 'auditio' (a hearing), via French 'audition'.
