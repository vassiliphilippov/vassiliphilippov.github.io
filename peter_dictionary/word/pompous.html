pompous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːmpəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpəs / (BrE)
Having or showing an excessive sense of self-importance or dignity; marked by arrogance or ostentatious display.

Examples

"The past I leave to historians ", Felix intoned, demonstrating that he could be pompous as well as happy.

Synset Examples

a grandiloquent and boastful manner
overblown oratory
a pompous speech
pseudo-scientific gobbledygook and pontifical hooey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandiloquent - using extravagant or pompous language
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble view of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French 'pompeux', from Latin 'pomposus' (stately, magnificent), from 'pompa' (procession).
