mimetic

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈmɛtɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈmɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by imitation, especially in art or literature; imitative.

Examples

As in experience one is seized by given entities and their interrelations and is forced to respond in value feelings to them, so one is similarly seized in the mimetic presentation of images.
The mimetic character of the imaginative consciousness tends to express itself in the presentation of artistic forms and materials.

Synset Examples

a mimetic dance
the mimetic presentation of images

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - copying or following a model or example

Etymology

From Greek mimētikos (able to imitate), from mimeisthai (to imitate).
