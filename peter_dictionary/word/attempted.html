attempted

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɛmptɪd / (AmE), / əˈtɛmptɪd / (BrE)
(of a crime or action) tried but not successfully completed.

Examples

An attempted middle course might lead to devices like a 5000 - word alphabetized dictionary from which every fiftieth word was selected.
She started back for the house, saw a light in the office, opened the door and surprised a domestic little scene which was far outside the dark realm of murder or attempted murder.

Synset Examples

attempted murder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: successful - accomplished in achieving a desired result

Etymology

From the verb 'attempt', from Old French 'atempter' (to try, attempt), from Latin 'attemptare' or 'adtemptare' (to test, try).
