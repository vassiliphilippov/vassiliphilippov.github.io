demineralization

1. NOUN

/ diˌmɪnɚələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The abnormal loss of mineral salts from a substance, especially the loss of calcium or other minerals from bone or teeth.

Examples

X-ray films of the vertebral column showed progression of the demineralization.

Etymology

From de- (removal) + mineral + -ization (action or process).
