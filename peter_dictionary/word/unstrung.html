unstrung

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstrʌŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrʌŋ / (BrE)
Emotionally disturbed or upset; unable to think or act calmly due to shock or anxiety.

Examples

Oh, dear, I 'm all unstrung ".

Synset Examples

the incident left him unstrung and incapable of rational effort

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composed - calm and in control of oneself
  • Synonym: distressed - suffering from anxiety or sorrow

Etymology

Past participle of 'unstring', meaning to remove strings or to make slack, used figuratively for loss of composure.
