trivial

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪviəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪviəl / (BrE)
Of little value or importance; insignificant.

Examples

Piepsam is grotesque, a disturbing parody; his end is ridiculous and trivial.
He would remain in the tent, waiting impatiently, occupied by some trivial task.
Linguists have not always been more enlightened than "practical people "and sometimes have insisted on incredibly trivial points while neglecting things of much greater significance.

Synset Examples

a fiddling sum of money
a footling gesture
our worries are lilliputian compared with those of countries that are at war
a little (or small) matter
a dispute over niggling details
limited to petty enterprises
piffling efforts
giving a police officer a free meal may be against the law, but it seems to be a picayune infraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

From Latin *trivialis* (commonplace, ordinary), from *trivium* (a place where three roads meet, hence a public place).
