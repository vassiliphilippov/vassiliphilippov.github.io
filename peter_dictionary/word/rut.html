rut

1. NOUN

/ rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
A settled and monotonous routine that is hard to change or escape.

Examples

Wilson stressed the fact that clear as this was, they must have a better church, a more business-like conduct of the church organization, and an effort to get this religious center out of its rut of wild worship into a modern church organization.

Synset Examples

they fell into a conversational rut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groove - a fixed, regular way of doing something, often uninteresting
  • Synonym: routine - a regular way of doing things, especially one that is dull or unchanging
  • Synonym: habit - a settled or regular tendency or practice
  • Antonym: break the mold - do something in a completely new way, escape the usual

Etymology

Probably from Middle French 'rut' (track made by a wheel), from Latin 'ruta', related to 'ruere' (to fall, rush down).

2. NOUN

/ rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
A long, narrow groove or track in the ground, especially one made by the repeated passage of the wheels of vehicles.

Examples

We rode over roads so rough that our Jeep came to rest atop the soil between ruts, all four wheels spinning uselessly.

Etymology

From Old French rute (track, way), from Latin rupta (broken way), from rumpere (to break).
