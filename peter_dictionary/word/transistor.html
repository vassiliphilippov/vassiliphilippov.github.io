transistor

1. NOUN

/ trænˈzɪstər / (AmE), / trænˈzɪstə / (BrE)
An electronic component made of semiconductor material that can amplify or switch electronic signals.

Examples

This original capsule contained a battery and a transistor oscillator and was about 1 cm. in diameter.

Etymology

From transfer + resistor, referring to its function as a transfer resistor in electronics.
