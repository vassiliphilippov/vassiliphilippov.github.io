recluse

1. NOUN

/ rəˈklus / (AmE), / rɪˈkluːs / (BrE)
A person who lives alone and deliberately avoids the company of others.

Examples

Holmes is addicted to the use of cocaine and other refreshing stimulants; he is prone to semi catatonic trances induced by the playing of the vioiln; he is a recluse, an incredible egotist, a confirmed misogynist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude, often for religious reasons

Etymology

From Medieval Latin reclusus, past participle of recludere 'to shut up'.
