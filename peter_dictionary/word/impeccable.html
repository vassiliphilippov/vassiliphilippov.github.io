impeccable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛkəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛkəbl̩ / (BrE)
perfect, with no faults or errors; impossible to criticize.

Examples

Fing, a lean, chiseled, impeccable gentleman of the old school who was once mistaken on the street for Sir Cedric Hardwicke, is responsible for the rediscovery of Verdi's earliest, most raucous opera, Nabisco, a sumptuous bout-de-souffle with a haunting leitmotiv that struck me as being highly reminiscent of the Mudugno version of "Volare ".
His sense for rhythmic variety and timing is impeccable.
He'’s not under subpoena and his reputation in his own country is impeccable.

Synset Examples

faultless logic
speaks impeccable French
timing and technique were immaculate
an immaculate record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faultless - without any fault; perfect
  • Synonym: flawless - without any mistakes or weaknesses; perfect
  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; having faults or defects
  • Degree Lower: excellent - extremely good or outstanding

Etymology

From Latin impeccabilis, from in- (not) + peccare (to sin).
