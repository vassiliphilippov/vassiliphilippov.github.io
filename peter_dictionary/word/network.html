network

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛt.wɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɛt.wɜːk / (BrE)
An interconnected system or group of people or things that communicate or work together.

Examples

This year, I am told, the CBS network will continue to abide by the rule but NBC will play to a conclusion here.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web - a complex network of interconnected elements
  • Synonym: system - an organized set of connected things or parts
  • Antonym: individual - a single person or thing, not part of a network

Etymology

From net + work, originally referring to a net-like structure; later generalized to systems of interconnected people or things.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɛt.wɜːk / (BrE)
A group of radio or television stations that broadcast the same programs at the same time, often under one management or affiliation.

Still, the network's willingness to experiment in this musical field is to be commended, and future essays happily anticipated.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcasting - The transmission of radio or television programs to a dispersed audience

Etymology

From net + work; in this sense from the 20th century as a term for broadcast organizations linked by communications technology.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
An open fabric or mesh made from string, rope, or wire woven together at regular intervals.

Examples

A network of sampling stations had been set up on shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: net - a woven or knotted material used for catching things or as a mesh

Etymology

From net + work, first recorded in the 16th century.
