volcano

1. NOUN

/ vɑlˈkeɪnoʊ / (AmE), / vɒlˈkeɪnəʊ / (BrE)
An opening in the Earth's crust through which molten lava, ash, and gases are expelled, often forming a mountain or hill.

Examples

Where it is not actually erupting, it rumbles and smolders in sullen resentment like a volcano, ready to explode at any moment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: geyser - A natural hot spring that intermittently ejects water and steam.

Etymology

From Italian 'vulcano', from Latin 'Vulcanus', the Roman god of fire.
