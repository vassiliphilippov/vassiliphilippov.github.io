constantly

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑnstəntli / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstəntli / (BrE)
Continuously over a period of time; without interruption.

Examples

It is a tremendous book, lively, constantly moving, and the Matunuck cast does well by it.

Synset Examples

the world is constantly changing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continually - repeatedly or very frequently over time
  • Antonym: intermittently - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuously

Etymology

From Latin constans, constantis (standing firm) via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈkɑːn.stənt.li / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that happens all the time or very often, without interruption or change.

Examples

Mythological concepts may by all means still be used, but they can be used responsibly only as "symbols "or "ciphers ", that is, only if they are also constantly interpreted in non mythological (or existential) terms.
Both watched Scotty constantly, Rachel without seeming to, Virginia openly, her eyes filled with concern.

Synset Examples

constantly kind and gracious
he always arrives on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuously - without stopping or interruption
  • Antonym: occasionally - not happening often or regularly

Etymology

From Latin 'constans, constantis' (standing firm).
