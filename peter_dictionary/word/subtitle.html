subtitle

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌbˌtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌtaɪtəl / (BrE)
to provide subtitles for (a film, television program, etc.); to supply with written translations or transcriptions of speech appearing on the screen.

Examples

Published annually by William Hord Richardson, the 1962 edition, subtitled Society Register of Southern California, is scheduled to arrive with Monday morning's postman.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dub - to provide a film with a soundtrack in a different language

Etymology

From sub- (under) + title, originally in publishing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌtaɪtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.taɪ.təl / (BrE)
Text displayed at the bottom of a film or television screen that translates or transcribes the dialogue, especially when in a foreign language.

Examples

Subtitle Credits - P.NARASIMHA HOPE U ENJOYED :) THE SUBTITLE.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caption - text on a screen that conveys dialogue and sounds for the hard-of-hearing

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) and 'titulus' (title).
