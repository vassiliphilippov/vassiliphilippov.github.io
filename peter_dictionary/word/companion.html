companion

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpænjən / (BrE)
A person who frequently accompanies, associates with, or shares time with another, especially as a friend.

Examples

One day Maeterlinck, coming with a friend upon an event which he recognized as the exact pattern of a previous dream, detailed the ensuing occurrences in advance so accurately that his companion was completely mystified.
Whatever the unhappy inmates of such camps are like today, Dostoevsky's companions were about as grim a lot as can be imagined.
There was a little blood on the hem of her dress, for the assassin had slashed Miss Harris's companion, Major Rathbone, with a knife.
This place was hatred to him, just as hatred was his only companion in his aloneness.
A Texan wrote to a male companion at home: "What has become of Halda and Laura?

Synset Examples

drinking companions
comrades in arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection
  • Degree Lower: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend
  • Degree Higher: partner - a person with whom one has an intimate or exclusive relationship

Etymology

From Old French compagnon, from Late Latin companio (someone who eats bread with another), from Latin com- (together) + panis (bread).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjən / (AmE), / kəmˈpanjən / (BrE)
a person who travels with or accompanies another

Examples

In town after town my companion pointed out the Negro school and the White school, and in every instance the former made a better appearance (it was newer, for one thing).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel companion - a person who travels with another
  • Degree Higher: traveler - a person who travels (especially long distances)

Etymology

From Old French compagnon, from Latin companio (fellow or messmate), from com- (together) + panis (bread).
