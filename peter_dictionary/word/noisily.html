noisily

1. ADV

/ ˈnɔɪzɪli / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that makes a lot of noise; loudly or with much unpleasant sound.

Examples

- and flounced to the sink, where she began noisily to wash her hands.

Synset Examples

he blew his nose noisily

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quietly - in a quiet or silent manner

Etymology

From 'noisy' + '-ly'; 'noisy' from French *noisif* ('harmful'), from Latin *noxia* ('hurt, damage').
