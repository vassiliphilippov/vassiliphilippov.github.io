habitual

1. ADJ

/ həˈbɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / həˈbɪtʃu.əl / (BrE)
Done, used, or experienced regularly; usual or customary.

Examples

Faulkner has found it useful, but he has employed it with his habitual independence of mind and skeptical outlook.

Synset Examples

his accustomed thoroughness
took his customary morning walk
his habitual comment
with her wonted candor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customary - usual, according to custom; established by habit
  • Antonym: occasional - occurring only at times, not regular or habitual

Etymology

From Latin habituālis, from habitus (custom, habit).
