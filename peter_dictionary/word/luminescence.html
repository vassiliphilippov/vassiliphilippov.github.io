luminescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːməˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌluːmɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the emission of light by a substance that has not been heated, as in fluorescence and phosphorescence; light produced at low temperatures and not caused by incandescence

Examples

The figure was wreathed in an extraordinary luminescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorescence - the emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light, ceasing almost immediately when the excitation source is removed
  • Synonym: phosphorescence - the emission of light without perceptible heat, persisting after exposure to radiation

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light) and the suffix '-escence' indicating a process or state.
