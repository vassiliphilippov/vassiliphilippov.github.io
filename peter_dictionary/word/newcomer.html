newcomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuˌkʌm.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌkʌm.ə / (BrE)
a person who has recently arrived in a place, joined a group, or started participating in an activity

Examples

"Chicago and All That Jazz "may have wound up satisfying neither the confirmed fan nor the inquisitive newcomer.
Negroes, Puerto Ricans, and rural newcomers are slowly making their way into the cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginner - a person just starting to learn or do something

Etymology

From late Middle English, from new + comer (one who comes).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuˌkʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌkʌmə / (BrE)
a person who has recently arrived in a place or joined a group, especially one who is new to a community, organization, or situation

Examples

For the occasion on which everyone already knows everyone else and the host wishes them to meet one or a few honored newcomers, then the "open house "system is advantageous because the honored guests are fixed connective points and the drifting guests make and break connections at the door.
What's with all the new faces?

Synset Examples

he's a newcomer to Boston

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veteran - someone with long experience in a place or group
  • Synonym: rookie - a person new to an activity or organization, especially sport or work

Etymology

From Middle English 'newcomer', from 'new' + 'comer'.
