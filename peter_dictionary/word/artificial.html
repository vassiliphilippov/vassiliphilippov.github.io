artificial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːr.t̬əˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
Made by human skill rather than occurring naturally; produced by humans as a copy or imitation of something natural.

Examples

The useful suggestion of Professor David Hawkins which considers culture as a third stage in biological evolution fits quite beautifully then with our suggestion that science has provided us with a rather successful technique for building protective artificial environments.
Much of the older experimental work on respiratory infections was accomplished by very artificial procedures.
The freeway with narrowly spaced interchanges concentrates and mitigates the access problem, but it also acts inevitably as an artificial, isolating boundary.
In March, 1931, 22 out of 28 members of a committee of the Federal Council of Churches ratified artificial methods of birth control.

Synset Examples

artificial flowers
artificial flavoring
an artificial diamond
artificial fibers
artificial sweeteners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made by humankind

Etymology

From Latin artificialis, from artificium meaning 'craftsmanship, artifice'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːɹ.təˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
(of a manner, behavior, or style) lacking naturalness or spontaneity; contrived or excessively formal.

Examples

There is evidence to suggest, in fact, that many authors of the humorous sketches were prompted to write them - or to make them as indelicate as they are - by way of protesting against the artificial refinements which had come to dominate the polite letters of the South.
One learns to become alert to changes in his vocal tone - to his voice's suddenly shifting to a quality not like his usual one, a quality which sounds somehow artificial or, in some instances, parrotlike.

Synset Examples

that artificial humility that her husband hated
contrived coyness
a stilted letter of acknowledgment
when people try to correct their speech they develop a stilted pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrived - deliberately created rather than arising naturally or spontaneously; unnatural
  • Synonym: stilted - stiff and self-conscious or unnatural (especially of a manner of talking or writing)
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humankind
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic or sincere

Etymology

From Latin artificialis, from artificium (craft, artifice).
