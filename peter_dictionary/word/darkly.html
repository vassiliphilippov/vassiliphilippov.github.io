darkly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkli / (BrE)
In a manner without light or brightness; in darkness.

Examples

He had crossed the river which now, beyond the woods yonder, was sliding darkly under the mist.

Synset Examples

the river was sliding darkly under the mist

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dimly - in a faint or weak light; not brightly
  • Antonym: brightly - with a lot of light or brightness

Etymology

From the adjective 'dark' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
