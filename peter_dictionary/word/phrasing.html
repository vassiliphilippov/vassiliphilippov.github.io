phrasing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the way in which musical phrases are grouped and articulated in a melodic line

Examples

Not only was Haumd's intonation and phrasing without flaw, but he seemed to take every tonal eccentricity in stride.

Etymology

From phrase + -ing, modelled after 'phrase' (from Greek 'phrasis', 'speech, expression').
