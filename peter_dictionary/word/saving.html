saving

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of rescuing or preserving someone or something from harm, danger, or loss.

Examples

The TEA estimated there would be 182 scholastics to attend the day school in Dallas County, saving them from coming to Austin to live in the state deaf school.

Synset Examples

work is the deliverance of mankind
a surgeon's job is the saving of lives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - the act of saving someone from a dangerous or distressing situation
  • Synonym: deliverance - the action of being rescued or set free
  • Antonym: endangerment - the act of putting someone or something in danger

Etymology

From Old French sauver, from Latin salvare (to save).

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
an act or instance of reducing expenditure or cost; the amount of money or resources conserved

Examples

Operating budget for the day schools in the five counties of Dallas, Harris, Bexar, Tarrant and El Paso would be $ 451500, which would be a savings of $ 157460 yearly after the first year's capital outlay of $ 88000 was absorbed, Parkhouse told the Senate.
This is true because of savings in utility lines and the fact that your buildings have a useful radius equal in all directions.

Synset Examples

it was a small economy to walk to work every day
there was a saving of 50 cents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economy - careful management of resources to avoid waste
  • Antonym: expenditure - the act of spending money or resources

Etymology

From Middle English 'saven' (to save) + -ing.

3. ADJ

/ ˈseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of faith, grace, etc.) bringing about salvation or redemption from sin; leading to spiritual deliverance.

Examples

Gannon contributed saving plays on the Falcons' aerial thrusts in the late stages.

Synset Examples

saving faith
redemptive (or redeeming) love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redemptive - serving to deliver from sin or error; bringing about salvation
  • Antonym: damning - bringing about condemnation or spiritual ruin

Etymology

From save + -ing, in the sense of 'bringing about salvation'.
