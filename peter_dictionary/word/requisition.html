requisition

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to make a formal request or demand for something, especially for official or military use.

Examples

- Fetch me the copies of everything B and C companies have requisitioned in the last six months.
He did say he was out of cans, the No .3's, but "I requisitioned 22000 ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to ask for something formally or politely

Etymology

From Latin requisitio (a searching, seeking); see also the related noun 'requisition'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
an official or formal demand, especially by a public authority or military, to take or use something, often temporarily

Examples

Oh, you can submit a requisition for a miracle if you want to make a fool of yourself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - an act of asking for something formally or politely
  • Synonym: demand - an insistent and peremptory request

Etymology

From Latin requisitio (a searching, demanding), from requirere (to seek, ask for).
