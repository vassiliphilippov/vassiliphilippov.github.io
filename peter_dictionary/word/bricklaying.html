bricklaying

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪkˌleɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪk.leɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The work or skill of building with bricks, especially as a trade.

Examples

His broad flat fingers, petrified into massive sausages by years of bricklaying, snatched the bills clumsily.
They were learning trades as diverse as shoe repairing, bricklaying, carpentry, cabinet making, auto mechanics, and airplane mechanics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masonry - the craft of working with stone, brick, or concrete in construction

Etymology

From 'brick' (from Middle English 'bryk') and 'laying' (from 'lay', Old English 'lecgan').
