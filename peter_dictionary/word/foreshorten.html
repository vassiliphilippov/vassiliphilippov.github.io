foreshorten

1. VERB

/ ˌfɔrˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈʃɔːtən / (BrE)
To reduce something in length, scope, or time while retaining its essential elements.

Examples

Finally, just as no different issues are posed for thoughtful analysis by the foreshortening of time that may yet pass before the end of human life on this earth, but only stimulation and alarm to the imagination, the same thing must be said in connection with the question of what we may perhaps already be doing, by human action, to accelerate this end.

Synset Examples

The manuscript must be shortened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abridge - reduce in scope without removing essentials
  • Synonym: shorten - make shorter in length or time
  • Antonym: lengthen - make longer in length or time

Etymology

From Italian *forzare* (to force) + *scorciare* (to shorten).
