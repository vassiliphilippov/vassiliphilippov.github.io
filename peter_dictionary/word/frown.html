frown

1. VERB

/ fraʊn / (AmE), / fraʊn / (BrE)
to wrinkle one's forehead, typically as a sign of anger, disapproval, or concentration

Examples

She was frowning.
Frowning, Miss Ada studied the list.
Hal waited: Macneff paced some more, frowning.
She stood frowning and chewing her lip.
Killpath leaned forward; his foot slipped off the chair and he put it back again, frowning now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glower - to have an angry or sullen look on one's face

Etymology

From Middle English 'frounen', probably from Old French 'froignier' (to snort, frown).
