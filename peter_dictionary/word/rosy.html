rosy

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
Having a color or tint like that of a rose; pink or blush-colored, especially used to describe a healthy or attractive pink complexion or light.

Examples

The landscape of Greek history broadens widely, and rather abruptly, in the eighth century B.C., the age of Homer's "rosy fingered Dawn ".

Synset Examples

blushful mists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink - having a pale red color
  • Degree Lower: pale - lacking strong color
  • Degree Higher: red - having the color of blood or ripe strawberries; intense hue

Etymology

From rose + -y, meaning 'like a rose'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
Having a pinkish or reddish color, especially as a sign of good health.

Examples

She was so beautiful with her rosy mouth and haughty air that she had to be wicked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flushed - having a red or pink color, especially in the face
  • Antonym: pale - lacking in color or intensity, especially in the face

Etymology

From rose (the flower), referencing its color.

3. ADJ

/ ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
hopeful and showing or expecting good things; optimistic.

Examples

However one looks at it, therefore, I 'd say that your horoscope for this autumn is the reverse of rosy.

Synset Examples

a rosy future
looked at the world through rose-colored glasses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst; tending to see the negative side
  • Synonym: optimistic - expecting good things; hopeful about the future

Etymology

From rose + -y, referring to the color of a rose, with transferred meaning of brightness or cheerfulness.
