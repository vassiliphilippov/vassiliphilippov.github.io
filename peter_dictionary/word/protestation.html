protestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.t̬əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒt.əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a formal and emphatic declaration of objection, disapproval, or dissent, often stated publicly or officially

Examples

Smiling at Warren's protestations, the old monk took his grip from him and led him down a corridor to a small parlor.

Synset Examples

they finished the game under protest to the league president
the senator rose to register his protest
the many protestations did not stay the execution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection, disapproval, or dissent

Etymology

From Latin protestatio (a public declaration), from protestari (to declare publicly).
