forename

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.neɪm / (BrE)
A person's given name, especially the name that comes before their family name.

Examples

MICHAEL: That'’s right.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surname - the family name that follows the forename
  • Synonym: first name - a person's given name; the name that precedes the surname

Etymology

From Old English 'fore' (before) + 'name'.
