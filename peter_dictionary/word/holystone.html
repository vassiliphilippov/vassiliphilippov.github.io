holystone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊ.liˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊ.liˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A type of soft sandstone used for scrubbing and cleaning the decks of ships, especially in historical or naval contexts.

Examples

Heiser, breathless and wild-eyed, brought the chilling news that the handspikes, heavers and holystones had been mysteriously removed from their customary places.

Etymology

From the practice of sailors kneeling while cleaning the decks, as if in prayer ('holy'), combined with 'stone'.
