emasculate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmæskjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmæskjʊleɪt / (BrE)
To make someone or something weaker or less effective; to deprive of strength, vigor, or effectiveness, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

The courts, by interpretation, emasculated the act.

Synset Examples

The Senate emasculated the law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaken - make or become weaker
  • Antonym: strengthen - make or become stronger

Etymology

From Latin 'emasculatus', past participle of 'emasculare', from 'e-' (away) + 'masculus' (male).
