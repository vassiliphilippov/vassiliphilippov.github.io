deadhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (BrE)
An unproductive or unenterprising person, especially one who does not contribute their fair share or is paid without working.

Examples

And so deadheads on the payroll can be eased out at the earliest possible age.

Synset Examples

the deadheads on the payroll should be eased out as fast as possible

Etymology

From 'dead' + 'head'; originally U.S. slang for a person given free or unearned admission, later generalized.
