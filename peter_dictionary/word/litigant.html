litigant

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtɪɡənt / (BrE)
A person, group, or organization involved as a party in a lawsuit.

Examples

"Litigants and witnesses were put to the expense and inconvenience of going long distances to transact business; public money spent; justice delayed; nothing accomplished, and the whole distribution of justice in this county seems to be an absolute farce ".

Synset Examples

plaintiffs and defendants are both litigants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
  • Synonym: defendant - an individual, company, or institution sued or accused in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin litigant-, stem of litigans, present participle of litigare (to dispute, to sue in law).
