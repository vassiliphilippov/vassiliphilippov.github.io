unavoidably

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnəˈvɔɪdəbli / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈvɔɪdəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be prevented or avoided; inevitably.

Examples

It grows terribly and unavoidably in power and leaves in its wake a trail of misery, moral disintegration, and destruction.

Synset Examples

the situation slid inescapably toward disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitably - In a way that cannot be avoided; as is certain to happen

Etymology

Formed from 'unavoidable' + '-ly'.
