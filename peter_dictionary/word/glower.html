glower

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with an angry, sullen, or menacing expression

Examples

Our man stopped dead and glowered at Felix.
The sky glowered down at them.

Synset Examples

The girl glared at the man who tried to make a pass at her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glare - to stare in an angry or fierce way
  • Antonym: smile - to look at someone with a friendly, happy facial expression

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *glowren* (to stare), perhaps of Scandinavian origin.
