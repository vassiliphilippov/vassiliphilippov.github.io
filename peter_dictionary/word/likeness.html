likeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪk.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪk.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being similar in appearance, character, or nature to someone or something else

Examples

"Fellowship by likeness "and "mission by friendly contact "form the iron cage of denominational religion.

Synset Examples

man created God in his own likeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - the state of being similar; resemblance
  • Synonym: resemblance - a way in which two or more things are alike
  • Antonym: difference - the quality of being unlike or dissimilar

Etymology

From Old English licnes, from lic (body, form) + -ness.
