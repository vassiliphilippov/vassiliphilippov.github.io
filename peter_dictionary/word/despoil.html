despoil

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɔɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔɪl / (BrE)
to steal or take valuables from a place, often by force and in a destructive way; to plunder

Examples

The savage barbarian hordes of red Russian Communism descended on the Athens that was mighty Metronome, sacking and despoiling with their Bolshevistic battle cry of "Soak the rich'!

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • Synonym: loot - to steal goods, especially in war or disorder
  • Synonym: pillage - to rob a place using violence, especially in wartime

Etymology

From Old French despoiller, from Latin despoliare (to strip, plunder).
