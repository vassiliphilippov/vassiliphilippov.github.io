discouraging

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɝɹɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing a loss of confidence, enthusiasm, or hope; making someone less likely to continue something.

Examples

It is discouraging for students to realize that the societies do not truly uphold the standards for which they are supposed to stand.

Synset Examples

where never is heard a discouraging word

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraging - giving someone support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From 'discourage' + '-ing'; 'discourage' from Old French 'descouragier', from 'des-' (expressing reversal) + 'corage' (courage).
