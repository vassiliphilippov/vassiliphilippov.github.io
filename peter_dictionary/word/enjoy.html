1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪ / (BrE)
to take pleasure in something or get satisfaction from it

Examples

Synset Examples

She relished her fame and basked in her glory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relish - to take great pleasure in something
  • Antonym: dislike - to not enjoy or take pleasure in something
  • Degree Higher: love - to like or enjoy something very much (greater degree)
  • Degree Lower: like - to find something pleasant or satisfactory (less intense than 'enjoy')

Etymology

From Old French *enjoir*, from en- (make) + joir (rejoice), from Latin *gaudēre* (to rejoice).
