obsidian

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɪdiən / (AmE), / əbˈsɪdiən / (BrE)
A hard, dark, glass-like volcanic rock formed by the rapid solidification of lava without crystallization.

Examples

First of all, the six figures of the Buddha you heisted - four Siamese heads, a black obsidian statuette in the earth touching position, and a large brass figure of the Dying Buddha on a teakwood base.

Etymology

From Latin obsidianus (lapis), mistakenly after Obsius, alleged discoverer.
