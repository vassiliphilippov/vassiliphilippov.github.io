directional

1. ADJ

/ dəˈrɛkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃənl̩ / (BrE)
Pointing or operating in a particular direction; relating to or indicating direction, especially in space.

Examples

London explains that the very distinct directional effect in the Phase 4 series is due in large part to their novel methods of microphoning and recording the music on a number of separate tape channels.

Synset Examples

a directional microphone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: omnidirectional - operating equally in all directions

Etymology

From direction + -al, meaning 'pertaining to direction'.
