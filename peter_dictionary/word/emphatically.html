emphatically

1. ADV

/ ɛmˈfætɪkli / (AmE), / ɪmˈfætɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is clear and without any doubt; unmistakably or decidedly.

Examples

I do n't remember ever seeing teeth that were quite so white and at the same time quite so emphatically not dentures.

Synset Examples

it was decidedly too expensive
she told him off in spades
by all odds they should win

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decidedly - without any doubt; unmistakably
  • Synonym: unquestionably - without any doubt

Etymology

From emphatic + -ally, ultimately from Greek emphatikos (significant, forceful).
