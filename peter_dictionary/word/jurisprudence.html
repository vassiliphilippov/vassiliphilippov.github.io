jurisprudence

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʊr.ɪsˈpruː.dəns / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊə.rɪsˈpruː.dəns / (BrE)
the study of law and the principles or philosophy of legal systems

Examples

Hidden behind Hegelian abstractions were more practical reasons for a changing jurisprudence.
The displacement (at least to a considerable extent) of the ethical jurisprudence of the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries by positivism reshaped both international law theory and doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law - the system of rules recognized by a country or community
  • Synonym: legal philosophy - the philosophical study of law and legal systems

Etymology

From Latin 'jurisprudentia' (knowledge of law), from 'jus' (law) and 'prudentia' (knowledge).
