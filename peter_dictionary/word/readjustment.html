readjustment

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (BrE)
the act or process of adjusting something again, especially to new or changed circumstances

Examples

Casey heard the voice distinctly and he knew who it was, but it took him a while to make the mental readjustment and control the disturbance inside his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - the process of making something more suitable or effective

Etymology

From re- (again) + adjustment, formed after adjust.
