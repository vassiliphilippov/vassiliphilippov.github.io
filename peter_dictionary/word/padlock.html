padlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈpædˌlɒk / (BrE)
a detachable lock with a hinged shackle that can be passed through an opening and snapped shut

Examples

It was secured by an oversized padlock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock - a device used to fasten something such as a door or container

Etymology

From pad (origin uncertain) + lock; attested since the 17th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈpædˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈpadlɒk / (BrE)
to fasten or secure (something) with a padlock

Examples

He was uttering threats in a low but savage voice when they closed and padlocked the door.
