creedal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkridəl / (AmE), / ˈkriːdəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a creed or system of beliefs.

Examples

It is always a temptation for a religious organization, especially a powerful or dominant one, to impose through the clenched fist of the law its creedal viewpoint upon others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrinal - relating to doctrine, set of beliefs taught by a group

Etymology

From 'creed' + '-al', modeled after Latin adjective form.
