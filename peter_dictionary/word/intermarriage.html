intermarriage

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmærɪdʒ / (BrE)
The custom or practice of marriage between people belonging to different social, ethnic, or religious groups, especially as required or regulated by custom or law.

Examples

Intermarriage, which is generally regarded as a threat to Jewish survival, was regarded not with horror or apprehension but with a kind of mild, clinical disapproval.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endogamy - marriage within one's own group
  • Synonym: exogamy - marriage outside one's social group

Etymology

From inter- (between) + marriage; modeled after Latin roots.
