afflicted

1. ADJ

/ əˈflɪktɪd / (AmE), / əˈflɪktɪd / (BrE)
Severely affected by pain, suffering, disease, or misfortune.

Examples

By the second day Michelangelo began to worry; and by the second night he was in a state of panic: he could see nothing out of the afflicted eye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaffected - not influenced or changed by something, especially disease or suffering

Etymology

Past participle of 'afflict', from Latin afflictus, past participle of affligere (to strike down).
