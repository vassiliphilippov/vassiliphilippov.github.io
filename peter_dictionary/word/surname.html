surname

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈsɜːˌneɪm / (BrE)
A family name passed down from generation to generation, typically inherited and used to identify the members of a family, as distinct from a given or first name.

Examples

QUESTADT: Mr. Pentangeli, you, uh...

Related Terms

  • Synonym: last name - synonym meaning family name; usually used in American English
  • Antonym: given name - the forename or first name, as opposed to surname

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'surnom' (additional name), from 'sur-' (over) + 'nom' (name).
