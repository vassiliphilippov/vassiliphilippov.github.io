poster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə / (BrE)
a large printed picture or notice, often with words, that is displayed in a public place to advertise something, give information, or for decoration

Examples

This can be done by stories in your house organs, posters, special publications, letters to workers' homes as well as by word of mouth through your chain of command.
That and a big goddamn poster.

Synset Examples

a poster advertised the coming attractions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placard - a sign for public display; often for notices or protests

Etymology

From post (to display) + -er (agent suffix), referring to something that is posted up.
