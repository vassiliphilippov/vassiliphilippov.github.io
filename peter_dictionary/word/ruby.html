ruby

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbi / (AmE), / ˈruːbi / (BrE)
A precious, transparent red gemstone made of corundum that has been cut and polished for use in jewelry.

Examples

Then O' Banion was presented with a platinum watch set with rubies and diamonds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapphire - a precious blue gemstone, also a variety of corundum

Etymology

From Latin 'rubinus' (red), from 'rubeus' (red).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruː.bi / (AmE), / ˈruː.bi / (BrE)
A deep and vivid red color, resembling that of a ruby gemstone.

Examples

That luscious tan... ... those ruby lips... ... and hair color so natural, only your undertaker knows for sure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a deep, purplish-red color
  • Degree Lower: scarlet - a brilliant red color with a slightly orange tinge

Etymology

From Latin 'rubinus' (red, ruby), from 'rubeus' (red), referring originally to the gemstone.
