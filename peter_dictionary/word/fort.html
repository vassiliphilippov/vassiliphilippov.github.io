fort

1. NOUN

/ fɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːt / (BrE)
A strong building or group of buildings used for defending an area, where troops are stationed.

Examples

The chevaux de frise, those sharp stakes and barriers around the fort at the Battery, pointed to a conflict between the town and sea power rolling in glassy swells as the tide came in.
Below the fort, high bluffs extended uninterruptedly for six miles along the Mississippi River.
Fort Hancock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garrison - a body of troops stationed in a fort or town
  • Degree Higher: castle - a large fortified building or set of buildings

Etymology

From French 'fort', from Latin 'fortis' (strong).

2. NOUN

/ fɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːt / (BrE)
A strong building or group of buildings used for military defense against attack.

Examples

On Big Stone Lake near the headwaters of the Red River, Robert Dickson, Superintendent of the Western Indian Department of Canada, had a trading post and planned in 1818 to build a fort to be defended by twenty men and two small artillery pieces.
Fort Hancock, Texas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortress - a large fortified place, often including a town

Etymology

From Latin 'fortis' (strong) via French 'fort' (stronghold, fort).
