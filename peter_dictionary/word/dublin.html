Dublin

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʌblɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʌblɪn / (BrE)
The capital and largest city of the Republic of Ireland, located on the east coast at the mouth of the River Liffey.

Examples

Palmer, golf's leading money winner in 1960, and Snead will be saluted as the winning team in the Canada Cup matches last June in Dublin.

Etymology

From Irish 'Dubh Linn', meaning 'black pool'.
