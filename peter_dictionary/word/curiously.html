curiously

1. ADV

/ ˈkjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is unusual, unexpected, or strange.

Examples

He felt curiously sleepy, the world seemed far away; he knew he should get to Cap, but he did n't know how.
For a man of his mass he was curiously short.
"But we now find writers obsessed by the nooks and crannies of their ivory towers, and curiously devoted to the growing obscurity and complexity of poetry and non poetry.

Synset Examples

had a curiously husky voice
he's behaving rather peculiarly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peculiarly - in a way that is unusual or strange

Etymology

From 'curious' + '-ly'; 'curious' from Latin 'curiosus' (careful, inquisitive).

2. ADV

/ ˈkjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows curiosity; in a manner eager to know or learn something.

Examples

Watson stared at them curiously.

Synset Examples

the baby looked around curiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitively - in a way that shows an eager desire to know or learn
  • Antonym: indifferently - without interest or concern

Etymology

From 'curious' + '-ly', originating from Latin 'curiosus' (careful, inquisitive).
