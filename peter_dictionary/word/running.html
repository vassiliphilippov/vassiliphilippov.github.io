running

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or sport of moving rapidly on foot, usually at a speed faster than walking

Examples

"We 'll let him do a lot of running this week, but I do n't know if he 'll be able to play ".
He would sit inside the coffee shop and pound a gloved fist upon the table and a girl would hear him and come running, bowing with her running, calling out in her bowing, "At your service ".
A lovely beast like that running around... ...could put steam in a man's strides.

Synset Examples

he broke into a run
his daily run keeps him fit

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jogging - running at a slow, steady pace
  • Degree Higher: sprinting - running at full speed over a short distance

Etymology

From the verb 'run' + -ing.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a subject, joke, argument, etc.) continually recurring or repeated over a period of time.

Examples

Willie's big day revived the running argument about the relative merits of Mays and Mickey Mantle.

Synset Examples

a running joke among us

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
(In American football) a play or the strategy of advancing the ball by carrying it rather than passing it.

Examples

With Skorich at the helm, the Eagles are expected to put more emphasis on running, rather than passing.
Skorich, however, is a strong advocate of a balanced attack - split between running and passing.

Synset Examples

the defensive line braced to stop the run
the coach put great emphasis on running

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passing - (football) advancing the ball by throwing it to a teammate

4. ADJ

/ ˈrʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fluid) flowing or issuing in a continuous stream.

Examples

He doubted whether she heard him, over the running water.

Synset Examples

as mountain stream with freely running water
hovels without running water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowing - moving smoothly and continuously, especially of liquids
