furnished

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɜrnɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a room, apartment, or building) provided with furniture or the necessary equipment.

Examples

I did book jackets and covers for paperback reprints: naked girls huddling in corners of dingy furnished rooms while at the doorway, daring the cops to take him, is the guy in shirt sleeves clutching a revolver.

Synset Examples

a furnished apartment
a completely furnished toolbox

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfurnished - not provided with furniture or equipment

Etymology

Past participle of 'furnish', from Old French *fournir* (to furnish, provide), of Germanic origin.
