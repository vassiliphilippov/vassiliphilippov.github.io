rhyme

1. NOUN

/ raɪm / (AmE), / raɪm / (BrE)
A short poem, especially one for children, or a piece of poetry in which lines end with similar sounds.

Examples

It has come to mean myths, legends, tales, songs, proverbs, riddles, superstitions, rhymes and such literary forms of expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verse - a line of poetry or a poem
  • Synonym: poem - a piece of writing in verse, often with rhyme or rhythm

Etymology

From Old French *rime*, from Late Latin *rithmus*, from Greek *rhythmos* (measured flow, rhythm).

2. NOUN

/ raɪm / (AmE), / raɪm / (BrE)
the similarity or correspondence of sounds at the ends of words or lines of poetry, especially the final sounds

Examples

But there is every reason to regard deal as a monosyllable, and because of the fact that / l / commonly has the quality of / / when it follows vowel sounds, deal seems to be a perfectly satisfactory rhyme with real.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: assonance - resemblance of vowel sounds between words

Etymology

From Old French *rime*, from Medieval Latin *rithmus*, from Greek *rhythmos* (measured motion, rhythm).

3. VERB

/ raɪm / (AmE), / raɪm / (BrE)
to compose lines or verses in which the final sounds of some words correspond in a regular pattern

Examples

Rhyme' em up cute - Arcilla, Flotilla ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verse - compose verses in poetry

Etymology

From Old French *rime* (verse, rhyme), from Late Latin *rithmus* (measured rhythm), from Greek *rhythmos* (measured flow, rhythm).
