siege

1. NOUN

/ siːdʒ / (AmE), / siːdʒ / (BrE)
A military operation in which an armed force surrounds a place and cuts off essential supplies, aiming to compel the surrender of those inside.

Examples

And now, in March, all Laos suffered a state of siege.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockade - the act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving
  • Degree Higher: assault - a direct or sudden attack, especially of a military kind
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing troops from an enemy or from a battle

Etymology

From Old French 'siege', from Latin 'sedere' (to sit), referring to 'a sitting down', i.e., a prolonged military encampment.
