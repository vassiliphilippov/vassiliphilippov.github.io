manhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈmæn.hʊd / (BrE)
The state or period of being a man rather than a boy; qualities traditionally associated with men, such as courage or strength.

Examples

Marcellus, the hero, is beset from all sides by the problems of approaching manhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boyhood - the state or period of being a boy

Etymology

From Middle English 'manhod', from Old English 'mann' (man) + '-hod' (state, condition).
