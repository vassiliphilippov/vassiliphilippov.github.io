sloganeering

1. NOUN

/ sloʊɡəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / sləʊɡəˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of slogans, especially in a manipulative, superficial, or empty way to promote a cause or persuade others

Examples

Instead of emotional recrimination, loaded phrases and sloganeering, we need a dispassionate study of the facts, a better understanding of the opposite viewpoint and a more serious effort to extend the areas of agreement until a solution is reached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propaganda - biased or misleading information used to promote a political cause or point of view

Etymology

From 'slogan' + '-eering', patterned after 'pioneering' or similar formations.
