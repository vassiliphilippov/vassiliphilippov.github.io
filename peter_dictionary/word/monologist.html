monologist

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / məˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who performs monologues, especially as a form of entertainment

Examples

Berman, whose fame has rested in recent years on his skills as a night club monologist, proved himself very much at home in musical comedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monologue - a long speech by one person in a play, movie, or as entertainment

Etymology

Formed from 'monologue' + '-ist', modeled after similar constructs for performer titles.
