cake

1. NOUN

/ keɪk / (AmE), / keɪk / (BrE)
A block or compact mass of a solid substance, such as soap or ice, formed into a rectangular or round shape.

Examples

Several sections of a loaf of dark bread; butter; jam; a tiny cake of ice.
Their product had been endorsed by Good Housekeeping, the A. M. A., and the Veterinary Journal, among other repositories of higher wisdom, and before much longer if you did n't have a cake of their soap in the john, even your best friends would think you did n't bathe.

Synset Examples

a bar of chocolate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a block of solid substance, especially made of metal or soap

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kaka' (cake, loaf), originally meaning a mass formed by baking or pressing.

2. NOUN

/ keɪk / (AmE), / keɪk / (BrE)
A sweet baked food made from a mixture of flour, sugar, eggs, and fat, often with added flavorings or fillings, usually served as a dessert.

Examples

Here'’s the cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry - baked food made from dough, often sweet or savory
  • Synonym: pie - dish of pastry with sweet or savory filling
  • Degree Lower: bread - baked food made mainly from flour and water

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kaka' (cake).
