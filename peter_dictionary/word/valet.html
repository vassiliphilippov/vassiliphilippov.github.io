valet

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælɪt, vəˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈvælɪt, ˈvæleɪ / (BrE)
A male servant who acts as a personal attendant, especially to help with clothes, grooming, and personal needs.

Examples

Still he let Eileen treat him like a valet.
He had only agreed with Miss Ada about getting the valet, but he had actually suggested the photograph to Mr. Jack.

Synset Examples

Jeeves was Bertie Wooster's man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butler - the chief male servant of a household, usually in charge of dining and wine

Etymology

From Old French *valet* (servant, young man) and Medieval Latin *vassellittus* (young nobleman, squire).
