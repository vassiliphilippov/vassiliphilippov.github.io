defiance

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfaɪəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪəns / (BrE)
open resistance or bold disobedience; a hostile or challenging attitude toward authority or opposition

Examples

In defiance, a chinless reprobate, Jake Camaret, marched down the aisle in St. Peter's one Sunday morning, followed by one of the women from the Bordel, whose dress and walk plainly showed the lack of any shame.
If the detective insists upon retaining his personal standards, he must now do so in conscious defiance of his society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with orders or requests; opposite of defiance
  • Synonym: rebellion - open resistance to authority; similar to defiance but on a larger scale

Etymology

From Old French 'defiance' (challenge), from 'desfier' (to challenge), based on Latin 'dis-' (reverse) + 'fidere' (to trust).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfaɪəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪəns / (BrE)
open resistance or bold disobedience; a daring or challenging attitude toward authority or opposition

Examples

He tried defiance and openly flaunted his devotion to his half sister, but he soon saw, as did she, that this course if persisted in would involve them in a common ruin.
Again, Henley's attitude of defiance which colors his ideal of self mastery is far from characteristic of a Stoic thinker like Marcus Aurelius, whose gentle acquiescence is almost Christian, comparable to the patience expressed in Milton's sonnet on his own blindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - an act of violent or open resistance to authority
  • Antonym: obedience - the act of complying with a command or request

Etymology

From Old French defier (to challenge) and defiance (challenge, resistance) from Latin dis- (away) + fidare (to trust).
