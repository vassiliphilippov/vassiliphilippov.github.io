skepticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛptɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
A skeptical attitude; doubt as to the truth of something.

Examples

None of this showed in his face, and he tried to keep his skepticism in hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: credulity - a tendency to be too ready to believe that something is real or true

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'skeptikos' (inquisitive, doubting) via Latin 'scepticus'.
