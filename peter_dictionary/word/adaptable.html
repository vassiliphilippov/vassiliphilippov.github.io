adaptable

1. ADJ

/ əˈdæptəbəl / (AmE), / əˈdaptəbl / (BrE)
able to adjust to new conditions or to be modified for a new use or purpose.

Examples

SBA serves also as a clearing house for information on products and processes particularly adaptable for exploitation by small firms.

Synset Examples

to succeed one must be adaptable
the frame was adaptable to cloth bolts of different widths

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - able to bend or be changed easily
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to change or be adapted

Etymology

From 'adapt' + '-able', with 'adapt' from Latin 'adaptāre' (to fit to).
