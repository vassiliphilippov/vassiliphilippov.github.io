ward

1. NOUN

/ wɔrd / (AmE), / wɔːd / (BrE)
A person, especially a minor, who is under the protection or custody of a guardian or a court.

Examples

"These actions should serve to protect in fact and in effect the court's wards from undue costs and its appointed and elected servants from unmeritorious criticisms ", the jury said.
"My beloved ward, my perennial gadfly ", said the whining voice.
Love to you, my ward ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guardian - a person who is legally appointed to care for another

Etymology

From Old English 'weard' (guard, protection), related to Old High German 'wart'.

2. NOUN

/ wɔrd / (AmE), / wɔːd / (BrE)
a district within a city or town, especially one designated for administrative or electoral purposes

Examples

He was also personally active in ward politics, and by 1924 O' Banion had acquired sufficient political might to be able to state: "I always deliver my borough as per requirements ".
There was a question and answer gag that went around at that time: Q. "Who 'll carry the Forty-second and Forty-third wards "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district - an area of a country, city, or town that has official boundaries or is considered as a unit for administrative purposes

Etymology

From Old English 'weard' meaning 'guard, protection', later used for district under a guardian's control.

3. NOUN

/ wɔrd / (AmE), / wɔːd / (BrE)
a division or large room in a hospital, usually accommodating several patients who require similar care or treatment

Examples

Another patient, a paranoid woman, for many months infuriated not only me but the ward personnel and her fellow patients by arrogantly behaving as though she owned the whole building, as though she were the only person in it whose needs were to be met.

Synset Examples

they put her in a 4-bed ward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - a section of a hospital dedicated to a specific type of care (e.g., intensive care unit)

Etymology

From Old English 'weard' (protection, guardian), originally referring to guardianship and later extended to mean areas under supervision.

4. NOUN

/ wɔrd / (AmE), / wɔːd / (BrE)
a section or division within a prison that usually contains several cells

Examples

Tier 1 North... - Tier 1 South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cellblock - a division of a prison consisting of cells

Etymology

From Old English weard (guard, protection); in this sense, developed to mean a section under supervision.
