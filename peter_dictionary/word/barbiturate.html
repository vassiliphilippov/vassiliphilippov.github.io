barbiturate

1. NOUN

/ bɑːrˈbɪtʃuˌreɪt / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɪtjʊrət / (BrE)
A type of drug derived from barbituric acid, used chiefly as a sedative or hypnotic; excessive use can lead to dependence or fatal overdose.

Examples

On the other hand, the temporary reduction in hypothalamic excitability through the injection of a barbiturate into the posterior hypothalamus causes a lessening in frequency and amplitude of cortical strychnine spikes until the hypothalamic excitability is restored.

Etymology

From barbituric acid, itself named from German Barbitursäure (Barb- + uric acid).
