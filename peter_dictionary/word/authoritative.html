authoritative

1. ADJ

/ əˈθɔr.ə.teɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
Having or showing the confident quality of someone who is respected or obeyed as an authority; commanding and reliable.

Examples

The people of the 17 th District of New York, and I as their Representative in Congress, take great pride in the New York Times as one of the great and authoritative newspapers of the world.

Synset Examples

an important official
the captain's authoritative manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commanding - having a powerful or imposing quality
  • Synonym: influential - having the power to influence others
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others

Etymology

From Medieval Latin auctoritativus, from Latin auctoritas (authority).
