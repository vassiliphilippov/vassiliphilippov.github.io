tax-exempt

1. ADJ

/ tæks ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / tæks ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
(of income, goods, or organizations) not subject to taxation by the government.

Examples

"Sometimes we 'd have trouble persuading her to make tax-exempt charitable contributions, and I 've known her to quarrel with a plumber over a bill for fixing a faucet; the next moment she 'd put another half million into the scholarship fund or thirty thousand into something as impractical as this unfortunate Johnston affair.

Synset Examples

tax-exempt bonds
an untaxed expense account

Related Terms

  • Antonym: taxable - subject to being taxed

Etymology

Formed from 'tax' and 'exempt' (from Latin exemptus, past participle of eximere 'take out').
