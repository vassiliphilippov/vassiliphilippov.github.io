concurrence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkɜːrəns / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrəns / (BrE)
The simultaneous occurrence or acting together of events, agents, or circumstances.

Examples

Perhaps no church father saw this concurrence of the unique and the universal as clearly, or formulated it as precisely, as Irenaeus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincidence - occurrence of events at the same time
  • Synonym: collaboration - working together for a common purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrentia': a coming together, from 'concurrere' (to run together, coincide).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈkɜrəns / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrəns / (BrE)
agreement in opinion, decision, or result; the state of agreeing with someone or something

Examples

The Secretary of the Treasury, upon the concurrence of the Secretary of State, is authorized and directed, out of the sum covered into the Yugoslav Claims Fund pursuant to subsection (b) of this section, after completing the payments of such funds pursuant to subsection (c) of this section, to make payment of the balance of any sum remaining in such fund to the Government of the Federal People's Republic of Yugoslavia to the extent required under article 1 (c) of the Yugoslav Claims Agreement of 1948.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion or feeling
  • Antonym: dissent - the holding or expression of opinions at variance with those commonly or officially held

Etymology

From Latin concurrentia (a running together, agreement), from concurrere (to run together, coincide).
