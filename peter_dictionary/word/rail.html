rail

1. NOUN

/ reɪl / (AmE), / reɪl / (BrE)
(short for railway) the system or service of transporting people and goods by train.

Examples

The railroad siding is still important - it is usually, though not always, true that long-haul shipment by rail is cheaper than trucking.
Grabski's shanty was beyond the bridge over the River Bystrzyca near the rail center.
Though the number of people flowing in and out of our metropolitan areas each day has increased tremendously since World War 2,, total annual rail commutation dropped 124 million from 1947 to 1957.

Synset Examples

he traveled by rail
he was concerned with rail safety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway - track and system for trains
  • Synonym: train - vehicle running on rails for carrying passengers or freight

Etymology

Shortened from 'railway'.

2. NOUN

/ reɪl / (AmE), / reɪl / (BrE)
a long, narrow bar, usually made of metal or wood, that acts as a barrier or support, typically forming part of a fence, balcony, or staircase

Examples

But the only answer that reached him was the screeching of the porch rail from her leg moving against it.
Reaching the porch rail beyond view of the bar windows, he feverishly scanned the busy street below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railing - a barrier made of rails; a fence or balustrade

Etymology

From Old French *reille* (iron bar), from Latin *regula* (straight stick, rule).
