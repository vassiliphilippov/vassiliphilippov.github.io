miscegenation

1. NOUN

/ mɪˌsɛdʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌsɛdʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the reproduction or interbreeding of people considered to belong to different racial groups, especially between a white person and a person of another race; formerly used especially regarding laws and social attitudes in the United States.

Examples

Southern resentment has been over the method of its ending, the invasion, and Reconstruction; their fears now are of miscegenation and Negro political control in many counties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interracial marriage - marriage between people of different races
  • Antonym: endogamy - marriage within a specific group or social category

Etymology

From Latin miscere (to mix) + genus (race, kind) with suffix -ation.
