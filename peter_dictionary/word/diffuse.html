diffuse

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(of a substance, energy, etc.) to spread or pass through a substance or area gradually and widely.

Examples

moreover, diffuses through structures in much the same manner as a gas.

Synset Examples

An atmosphere of distrust has permeated this administration
music penetrated the entire building
His campaign was riddled with accusations and personal attacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permeate - to spread through something and be present in every part of it

Etymology

From Latin diffundere, meaning 'to pour out.'

2. VERB

/ dɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to spread or move outward from an area of high concentration to an area of lower concentration, often gradually.

Examples

The thyroxine and tri-iodothyronine released by proteolysis and so escaping de-iodination presumably diffuse into the blood stream.

Synset Examples

The soldiers fanned out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disperse - to spread out or scatter over a wide area
  • Antonym: concentrate - to focus or gather in one place or area

Etymology

From Latin 'diffundere' (to pour out, spread out).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːs / (BrE)
Spread over a large area and not concentrated in one place.

Examples

At this time a detailed neuromuscular examination revealed diffuse muscle atrophy that was moderate in the hands and feet, but more marked in the shoulders, hips, and pelvic girdle, with hypoactive deep tendon reflexes.

Synset Examples

a large diffuse organization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentrated - gathered together in one place

Etymology

From Latin diffusus, past participle of diffundere (to pour out, spread out).

4. VERB

/ dɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to cause information, ideas, or knowledge to be spread widely among people

Examples

Even so, Gannett judiciously argued, the Association could legitimately decide that Parker "should not be encouraged nor assisted in diffusing his opinions by those who differ from him in regard to their correctness ".

Synset Examples

spread information
circulate a rumor
broadcast the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disseminate - to spread widely, especially information or ideas
  • Synonym: broadcast - to make information known widely; to spread widely
  • Antonym: gather - to collect information rather than spread it

Etymology

From Latin diffundere (to pour out, scatter).
