aspirant

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspərənt / (AmE), / ˈæspɪrənt / (BrE)
A person who has ambitions to achieve something, especially a particular job, career, or position.

Examples

As the historic processes of modernization gradually gain momentum, their cohesion will be threatened by divisive forces, the gaps between rulers and subjects, town and country, will widen; new aspirants for power will emerge whose ambitions far exceed their competence; old rulers may lose their nerve and their sense of direction.

Synset Examples

a lofty aspirant
two executive hopefuls joined the firm
the audience was full of Madonna wannabes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candidate - a person who applies for a job or is nominated for election
  • Synonym: contender - a person who competes for something, especially a position of power
  • Antonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position; opposite of aspirant

Etymology

From Latin aspirantem, present participle of aspirare (to strive for, to seek to reach).
