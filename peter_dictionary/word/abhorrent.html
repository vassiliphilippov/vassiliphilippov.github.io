abhorrent

1. ADJ

/ əbˈhɔrənt / (AmE), / əbˈhɒrənt / (BrE)
Inspiring disgust and loathing; repugnant or extremely offensive to the mind or morals.

Examples

Reading the Old Testament would have shown the dramatist that the ideas attributed to Shylock were abhorrent to the Jews.

Synset Examples

an abhorrent deed
the obscene massacre at Wounded Knee
morally repugnant customs
repulsive behavior
the most repulsive character in recent novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repugnant - extremely distasteful or unacceptable
  • Synonym: repulsive - arousing intense distaste or disgust
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality and decency

Etymology

From Latin 'abhorrens', present participle of 'abhorrēre' meaning 'to shrink back from, shudder at'.
