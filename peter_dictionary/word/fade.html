fade

1. VERB

/ feɪd / (AmE), / feɪd / (BrE)
to become less clear, visible, or strong and gradually disappear

Examples

As the first scene begins to fade, the succeeding scene begins to appear.
I waved with discretion and moderation to the vague golden faces fading through rising dust and the distortions of the back window glass.
As church membership burgeoned, such measures faded into desuetude.

Synset Examples

The scene begins to fade
The tree trunks are melting into the forest at dusk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanish - to disappear completely and suddenly
  • Synonym: dissolve - to fade away gradually, especially in film or television scenes
  • Synonym: disappear - to cease to be visible; to go out of sight

Etymology

From Middle English 'faden', from Old French 'fader' (to fade, weaken), probably from Vulgar Latin *fatidare.

2. VERB

/ feɪd / (AmE), / feɪd / (BrE)
to gradually disappear or become less distinct, strong, or clear

Examples

And suppose that the circumstances have now so changed that the feeling with which we made the remark in the first place has faded.

Synset Examples

The pain eventually passed off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
  • Synonym: dissipate - to scatter or disappear gradually
  • Synonym: evaporate - to fade away or disappear gradually, often by turning into vapor

Etymology

From Old French 'fader' (to become weak), from Latin 'fatuus' (foolish, insipid).

3. VERB

/ feɪd / (AmE), / feɪd / (BrE)
to gradually lose freshness, vigor, or vitality

Examples

Her hair was dyed, and her bloom was fading, and she must have been crowding forty, but she seemed to be one of those women who cling to the manners and graces of a pretty child of eight.
Then he played nine imaginary holes of golf, choosing his handicap, his irons, his stance, his opponents, and his weather in detail, but the green of the links seemed faded in the light of his business worries.
She looked at the girl speculatively from eyes which had paled with the years; from the early evening lights of them which had first startled Izaak to look at her in an uncousinly way, they had faded to a near absence of color which had, possibly from her constant looking at the water, something of the light of the sea in them.

Synset Examples

Her bloom was fading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wither - to become dry and shriveled, especially of plants or flowers
  • Synonym: wilt - to lose strength or freshness, often due to lack of water, especially of plants
  • Antonym: revive - to regain life or strength

Etymology

From Old French 'fader' (to become weak) and Latin 'fatuus' (foolish, insipid).
