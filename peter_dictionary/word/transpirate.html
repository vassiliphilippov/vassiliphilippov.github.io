transpirate

1. VERB

/ ˈtræn.spəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.spɪˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of a gas or vapor) to pass through the pores or interstices of a substance, especially as in the movement of gas through tissue or membrane.

Examples

The dependence of the arc voltage upon the mass flow velocity of the transpirating gas was investigated for various arc lengths and currents between 100 Amp and 200 Amp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transpire - (of a liquid or gas) pass through pores or a membrane

Etymology

Formed from 'transpire', patterned after other scientific verbs (e.g., 'respirate').
