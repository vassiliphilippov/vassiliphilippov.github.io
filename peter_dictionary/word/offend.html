offend

1. VERB

/ əˈfɛnd / (AmE), / əˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel hurt, angry, or upset, often by being rude or showing a lack of respect

Examples

He behaved publicly with a cocky, swaggering truculence that offended their vulpine Latin minds, and behaved towards them personally with an unimpressed insolence that enraged them beneath their blandness.
And to offend the dead meant to incur their wrath, and thus provoke the unleashing of countrywide disasters.

Synset Examples

Her tactless remark offended me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak or treat with disrespect or scorn
  • Antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied

Etymology

From Latin *offendere* (to strike against, displease).

2. VERB

/ əˈfɛnd / (AmE), / əˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel hurt, angry, or upset, especially by something you say or do

Examples

I apologise if I offended you.

Synset Examples

She hurt me when she did not include me among her guests
This remark really bruised my ego

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak or act in a way that is rude or offensive to someone
  • Antonym: please - to give satisfaction or pleasure to someone; to make someone happy

Etymology

From Latin 'offendere' (to strike against, displease).
