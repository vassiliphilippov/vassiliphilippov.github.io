gingham

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪŋəm / (AmE), / ˈɡɪŋəm / (BrE)
A lightweight, plain-woven cotton cloth, typically patterned with checks or stripes, used for clothing and household items.

Examples

From above one could only occasionally catch a glimpse of life on the floor of this green sea: a neighbor's gingham skirt flashing into sight for an instant on the path beneath her grape-arbor, or the movement of hands above a clothesline and the flutter of garments hung there, half-way down the block.
She drank and pushed back her gingham bonnet to wet a kerchief and wipe her face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaid - fabric with a crisscrossed pattern, usually of multicolored stripes
  • Degree Lower: muslin - lightweight plain-woven cotton fabric, often unpatterned

Etymology

From Malay 'genggang' (striped), adopted via Dutch or French.
