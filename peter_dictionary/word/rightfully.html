rightfully

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪtfəli / (AmE), / ˈraɪtfʊli / (BrE)
In a way that is morally or legally right; by right.

Examples

Both, of course, were remarkable feats and further embossed the fact that baseball rightfully is the national pastime.
The aspects of physical development that catch the judges' eyes and which rightfully influence their decisions are symmetry and that hallmark of the true champion - superior definition of the muscles.

Synset Examples

baseball rightfully is the nation's pastime

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wrongfully - in a way that is not morally or legally right
  • Synonym: justly - in a fair, morally right, or lawful manner

Etymology

From 'rightful' + '-ly', where 'rightful' is from 'right' (in accordance with justice or law) + '-ful'.
