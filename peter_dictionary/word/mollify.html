mollify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑːlɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone less angry or upset; to calm or soothe their feelings or temper

Examples

And yet Mr. Kennedy persists in trying to mollify the intransigents of the right with apologies and promises of "tightening up "and "economizing ".

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placate - to make someone less angry or hostile
  • Synonym: appease - to calm or pacify, especially by acceding to demands
  • Antonym: enrage - to make someone very angry

Etymology

From Latin mollificare, from mollis (soft) + facere (to make).
