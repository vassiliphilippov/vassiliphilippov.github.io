regime

1. NOUN

/ rəˈʒim / (AmE), / rɛˈʒiːm / (BrE)
A government, especially an authoritarian one, or the system or method by which a government is organized.

Examples

"It is not an individual that is in the dock at this historical trial "- said Ben Gurion, "and not the Nazi regime alone - but anti-Semitism throughout history ".
There would be changes made, and Signor Raymond should understand that when the Pope died it was like the end of a regime in Rome.
You were around the old-timers, who dreamed of how the families should be organized, basing them on the old Roman legions, and call '’em "regimes," the "capos" and "soldiers."

Synset Examples

the government reduced taxes
the matter was referred to higher authorities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the group of people with authority to govern a country or state

Etymology

From French 'régime' (system of rule), from Latin 'regimen' (rule, direction).

2. NOUN

/ rəˈʒim / (AmE), / rɛˈʒiːm / (BrE)
A systematic plan of therapy, often involving diet, exercise, or medication, followed to improve or maintain health.

Examples

So we are faced with a vast network of amorphous entities perpetuating themselves in whatever manner they can, without regard to the needs of society, controlling society and forcing upon it a regime representing only the corporation's needs for survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regimen - a prescribed course of medical treatment or way of life

Etymology

From French régime (system of order, rule), from Latin regimen (guidance, rule).
