rascal

1. NOUN

/ ˈræskəl / (AmE), / ˈrɑːskəl / (BrE)
A person who behaves badly or dishonestly, often in a playful or mischievous way; sometimes used affectionately for someone who causes trouble.

Examples

They get all the glory and most of the pay and do n't earn ten cents apiece on the average, the drunken rascals ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: rogue - a dishonest or unprincipled man
  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'rascaile' (rabble, mob), from Old French 'rascaille'.
