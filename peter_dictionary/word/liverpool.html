Liverpool

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪvərˌpuːl / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəpuːl / (BrE)
A large city and major port in northwestern England, known for its maritime history, cultural heritage, and as the birthplace of The Beatles.

Examples

A notable example of this was the discussion of Christian unity by the Catholic Archbishop of Liverpool, Dr. Heenan, and the Anglican Archbishop of York, Dr. Ramsey, recently appointed Archbishop of Canterbury.

Etymology

From Old English 'Liferpul', first recorded in the 13th century.
