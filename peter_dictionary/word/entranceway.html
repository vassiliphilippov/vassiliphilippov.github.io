entranceway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.trənsˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.trəns.weɪ / (BrE)
A passage, door, or entry giving access to a place, especially into a building or room.

Examples

At 7: 25 two hotel doormen came thumping down the steps, carrying a saw-horse to be set up as a barricade in front of the haberdashery store window next to the entranceway, and as I watched them in their gaudy red coats that nearly scraped the ground, their golden, fringed epaulets and spic, red visored caps, I suddenly saw just over their shoulders Jessica gracefully making her way through the crowd.

Synset Examples

they waited at the entrance to the garden
beggars waited just outside the entryway to the cathedral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrance - a door, gate, or passage for entering a place
  • Synonym: entryway - an entrance or passage into a room or building
  • Antonym: exit - a way out of a place

Etymology

From 'entrance' + 'way', both ultimately from Old French and Middle English roots.
