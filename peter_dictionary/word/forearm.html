forearm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌɑːm / (BrE)
the part of the arm between the elbow and the wrist

Examples

The horse walked, the reins were slack, the huckster rode with bowed shoulders, his forearms across his knees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper arm - the part of the arm between the shoulder and the elbow

Etymology

From Old English 'for-' (before) + 'arm'.
