elasticity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlæˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / ɪˌlæˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
The ability of a body or material to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed.

Examples

The elasticity as a parameter of fluids which is not subject to simple measurement at present, and it is a parameter which is probably varying in an unknown manner with many commercial materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plasticity - the quality of being easily shaped or molded, without necessarily returning to the original shape
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of resisting deformation and not returning to the original shape

Etymology

From French *élasticité*, from *élastique*, from Greek *elastos* (ductile, flexible).
