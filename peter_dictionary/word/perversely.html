perversely

1. ADV

/ pərˈvɜrsli / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsli / (BrE)
In a way that deliberately behaves unreasonably, contrary, or against what is expected or accepted.

Examples

Finally, the theatrical (and perversely erotic) notions of dressing up, cosmetics, disguise, and especially change of costume (or singularity of costume, as with Cipolla), are characteristically associated with the catastrophes of Mann's stories.
Philip Staffe, the ship's carpenter, was a good worker, but perversely independent.

Synset Examples

his perversely erotic notions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrarily - in a contrary or opposing way

Etymology

From "perverse" + "-ly"; perverse from Latin perversus (turned away, contrary).
