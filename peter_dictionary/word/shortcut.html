shortcut

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtkʌt / (BrE)
a route that is quicker and shorter than the usual way to get somewhere

Examples

Suspecting an ambush, the two deputies decided to ride up a side canyon taking a short cut into Catskill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: detour - a longer or roundabout route taken to avoid something or visit somewhere along the way

Etymology

From 'short' + 'cut', indicating a cutting of distance or time.
