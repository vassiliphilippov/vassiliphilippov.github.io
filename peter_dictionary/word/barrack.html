barrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærək / (AmE), / ˈbærək / (BrE)
A building or set of buildings built to house soldiers or other military personnel.

Examples

Maybe yes, maybe no, but you can observe everything from the roof of a barrack we are building ".
I live in an army barracks with the FBI guys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracks - plural form commonly used for military housing

Etymology

From French *baraque* (hut, cabin), likely from Catalan or Italian, ultimately of unknown origin.
