crucial

1. ADJ

/ ˈkruːʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃəl / (BrE)
Extremely important, especially in determining the outcome of a situation; decisive or critical.

Examples

Love is the crucial dilemma of experience for Mann's heroes.
size at which life is started and at which sexual maturity is reached; time required to reach maturity; rate of growth both before and after this crucial stage; and maximum length, with confirmation or amplification of Oliver's figures.
The foregoing remarks imply that the hypothalamic balance plays a crucial role at the crossroads between physiological and pathological forms of emotion.
He felt himself now, as he himself says in his Confessions, at a crucial point of his life.
A crucial question, therefore, is what evangelism and mission actually mean in metropolitan Protestantism.

Synset Examples

a crucial moment in his career
a crucial election
a crucial issue for women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - of decisive importance
  • Synonym: vital - absolutely necessary or essential
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From French 'crucial' (decisive), from Latin 'crux' (cross), originally referring to something at a 'crossroads', i.e., a critical point.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkruːʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃəl / (BrE)
Of great importance; decisive or critical, especially in determining the outcome of something.

Examples

How this was accomplished may be described, since this sometimes is a crucial problem.

Synset Examples

crucial to the case
relevant testimony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - of great importance or decisive; crucial
  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From French *crucial* (decisive), from Latin *crux* (cross), originally referring to a cross-shaped test or trial.
