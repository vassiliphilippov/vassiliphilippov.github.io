gravitational

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the force of gravity or gravitation.

Examples

The actual change * * f caused by a shear field is calculated by multiplying the pressure differential times the volume, just as it is for any gravitational or osmotic pressure head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the force that attracts objects toward the center of the earth or another physical body having mass
  • Synonym: gravitational pull - the attraction exerted by gravity on a body

Etymology

From 'gravitation' (from French gravitation, from Latin gravitas meaning 'weight, heaviness') plus the adjective suffix '-al'.
