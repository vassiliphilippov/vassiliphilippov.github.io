horn

1. NOUN

/ hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
A hard, pointed, bony projection that grows on the heads of certain animals, such as cows, goats, and sheep, often used for defense or display.

Examples

"Since 1692 a great but superficial change has wiped out God's beard and the Devil's horns, but the world is still gripped between two diametrically opposed absolutes.

Swadesh, and therefore Hoijer, felt compelled to omit all terms denoting species or even genera (fox, vulture, salmon, yellow pine, manzanita); their classes of animal and plant terms are restricted to generalizations or recurrent parts (fish, bird, tree, grass, horn, tail, bark, root).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antler - branched bony growth on the heads of deer and similar animals

Etymology

Old English *horn*, from Proto-Germanic *hurnaz*, related to horn-shaped objects.

3. NOUN

/ hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
a hard, pointed or rounded projection growing from the head of certain animals, or any similar protuberance on an organism

Examples

Fuzzy caterpillars, snails with their sensitive horns, struggling grasshoppers held by their long hind legs and commanded to "spit tobacco, spit ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antler - a bony growth on the head of a deer, typically branched

Etymology

Old English *horn*, from Proto-Germanic *hurnaz*; related to German *Horn* and Dutch *hoorn*.

4. NOUN

/ hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
a brass musical instrument with a tube and a flared bell, typically played by means of valves, producing a bright, resonant tone

Examples

Thoroughly modern in treatment, they are at the same time, full of simple sincerity which invariably characterizes genuine Negro folk-music and are by no means to be confused with the average' Broadway Spirituals' which depend for their racial flavor upon sundry allusions to the' Amen Corner',' judgement day',' Gabriel's horn', and a frustrated devil - with a few random' Hallelujahs' thrown in for good measure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornet - a similar brass musical instrument with a more compact shape and mellower tone
  • Synonym: trumpet - a brass musical instrument similar in shape and range but with a brighter, more piercing sound

Etymology

From Old English *horn*, of Germanic origin, originally meaning 'animal horn', later extended to musical instruments made from or resembling animal horns.

6. NOUN

/ hɔrn / (AmE), / hɔːn / (BrE)
a device in a vehicle that produces a loud sound as a warning or signal

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: klaxon - a type of loud electric horn

Etymology
