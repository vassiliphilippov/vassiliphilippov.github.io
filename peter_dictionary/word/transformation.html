transformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræns.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræns.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A complete or major change in someone or something, especially in form, appearance, or character.

Examples

Thus the transformation of Adam Smith's ideal entrepreneur into a mythological detective coincides closely with the decline of the real entrepreneur in economic life.
A fourth view is the transformation of emotion, as in Housman's fine phrase on the arts:
The student of ideas and their place in history will always be concerned with the patterns of transition, which are at the same time patterns of transformation, whereby ideas pass from one area of activity to another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - the act or process of becoming different
  • Synonym: conversion - the process of changing from one form or use to another
  • Synonym: transition - the process of changing from one state or condition to another

Etymology

From Latin 'transformatio', from 'transformare' meaning 'to change in shape or form'.
