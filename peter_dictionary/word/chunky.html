chunky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌŋki / (BrE)
having or containing small thick pieces or lumps, often referring to texture in foods or substances.

Examples

He poured the water off the sourdough and off the flour, salvaging the chunky, watery messes for biscuits of a sort.

Synset Examples

the dumplings were chunky pieces of uncooked dough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpy - having lumps; not smooth

Etymology

From 'chunk' + '-y', meaning 'full of chunks'.
