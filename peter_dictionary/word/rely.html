rely

1. VERB

/ rɪˈlaɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to depend on someone or something confidently; to have trust or faith in

Examples

Mr. Khrushchev is known to rely heavily on his instincts about his adversaries and to be a shrewd judge of men.
Do you have faith in my judgement?

Synset Examples

We can trust in God
Rely on your friends
bank on your good education
I swear by my grandmother's recipes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depend - to be controlled or determined by; to rely on
  • Synonym: trust - to believe that someone or something is reliable, good, honest, etc.
  • Antonym: distrust - to have no confidence or trust in someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *relier* (to fasten, bind together), from Latin *religare* (to bind fast).
