whet

1. VERB

/ wɛt / (AmE), / wɛt / (BrE)
To make (a feeling, desire, or appetite) keener or more intense.

Examples

But one wishes, when the appetite is whetted, as it was in the case of the all-too-brief excerpt from the Blomdahl opera, that further opportunity would be provided both for hearing the works in their entirety and for a closer analytical look at the sense and nature of the compositions.

Synset Examples

whet my appetite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpen - make sharper or more acute (literal or figurative)
  • Antonym: blunt - make less sharp, weaken a feeling or effect

Etymology

From Old English *hwettan* (to sharpen), related to *hwat* (sharp, bold).
