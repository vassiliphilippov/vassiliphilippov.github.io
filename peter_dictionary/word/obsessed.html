obsessed

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsɛst / (AmE), / əbˈsɛst / (BrE)
Having or showing an excessive or compulsive concern with someone or something.

Examples

"But we now find writers obsessed by the nooks and crannies of their ivory towers, and curiously devoted to the growing obscurity and complexity of poetry and non poetry.

Synset Examples

became more and more haunted by the stupid riddle
was absolutely obsessed with the girl
got no help from his wife who was preoccupied with the children
he was taken up in worry for the old woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preoccupied - absorbed or engrossed in something to the exclusion of other things

Etymology

From past participle of 'obsess', which derives from Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidēre' (to besiege).
