codification

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.də.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.dɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of arranging laws, rules, or other items into a systematic code or body

Examples

First was the period of codification of existing law: the Code Napoleon in France and the peculiar codification that, in fact, resulted from Austin's restatement and ordering of the Common Law in England.
Codification was followed in all countries by a growing amount of legislation, some changing and adjusting the older law, much dealing with entirely new situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematization - the process of organizing according to a system

Etymology

From Latin 'codex' (book of laws) + English suffix '-fication'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.dɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.dɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A systematic set of written rules, principles, or laws, often forming a formal code.

Examples

First was the period of codification of existing law: the Code Napoleon in France and the peculiar codification that, in fact, resulted from Austin's restatement and ordering of the Common Law in England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code - a systematic collection of laws or regulations

Etymology

From French 'codification', from Latin 'codex' (book of laws).
