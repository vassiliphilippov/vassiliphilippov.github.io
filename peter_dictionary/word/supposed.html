supposed

1. ADJ

/ səˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
Believed or assumed to be true, often mistakenly or without sufficient proof.

Examples

It is really as though the Russians have seen in this character the oftentimes underlying vitality and courage of supposed buffoons.
Well, I was under the impression that you and I would talk alone.

Synset Examples

the supposed existence of ghosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alleged - stated or claimed to be the case, but without proof
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or true, not imagined

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'supposen', from Old French 'supposer', from Latin 'supponere' (to put under, assume).

2. ADJ

/ səˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
expected or required to do or be something, often by rules, instructions, or circumstances.

Examples

I'’m supposed to stay in my house?

Synset Examples

I'm supposed to be there at ten
he was supposed to go to the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliged - required or compelled to do something; having a duty
  • Antonym: permitted - allowed to do something; not forbidden

Etymology

From Middle English *supposen*, from Old French *supposer*, from Latin *supponere* (to put under, substitute).

3. ADJ

/ səˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
assumed to be true or real, often without proof or adequate evidence

Examples

Hey, how the hell was I supposed to know?

Synset Examples

theories about the extinction of dinosaurs are still highly conjectural
the supposed reason for his absence
suppositious reconstructions of dead languages
hypothetical situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjectural - based on conjecture rather than certain knowledge
  • Synonym: hypothetical - based on or serving as a hypothesis
  • Antonym: proven - demonstrated by evidence or argument as true

Etymology

Past participle of 'suppose', from Old French *supposer* (to assume, presume), from Latin *supponere* (to put under, to assume).
