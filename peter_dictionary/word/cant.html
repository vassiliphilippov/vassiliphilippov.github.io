cant

1. NOUN

/ kænt / (AmE), / kænt / (BrE)
language or stock phrases used repeatedly, often to the point of losing meaning or sincerity; jargon or platitudes particular to a group or profession.

Examples

It was still a very big world, despite all the modern cant to the contrary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular group
  • Synonym: platitude - a remark or statement that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful

Etymology

Probably from Latin 'cantare' (to sing), via Middle English meaning 'chant, singing'.
