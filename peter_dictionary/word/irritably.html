irritably

1. ADV

/ ˈɪr.ɪ.təb.li / (AmE), / ˈɪr.ɪ.tə.bli / (BrE)
In a way that shows irritation or annoyance; in an irritable manner.

Examples

He had noticed how formal and irritably exact Rachel had grown.

Synset Examples

she had become irritably exact

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angrily - in a manner showing anger
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm manner, without irritation or anger

Etymology

From 'irritable' + '-ly'. 'Irritable' from Latin 'irritabilis' (easily excited).

2. ADV

/ ˈɪr.ɪ.tə.bli / (AmE), / ˈɪr.ɪ.tə.bli / (BrE)
In a manner showing irritation or annoyance; with annoyance or impatience.

Examples

"People automatically assume that I 'm in college ", a nineteen year old machinist observed irritably.

Synset Examples

he said testily; `Go away!'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testily - in a way showing irritation or impatience
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or unruffled manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'irritable' + the adverb suffix '-ly'. 'Irritable' comes from Latin 'irritabilis' (easily excited).
