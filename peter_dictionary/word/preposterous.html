preposterous

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈpɑstərəs / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒstərəs / (BrE)
Completely ridiculous or absurd; contrary to reason or common sense.

Examples

It's a wonder, really, to how much mendacious trouble Larkin puts himself to sell the Jerebohms that preposterous manse.
Downtown Los Angeles is already two-thirds freeway, interchange, street, parking lot and garage - one of those preposterous "if "statistics has already come to pass.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate
  • Synonym: ludicrous - so foolish, unreasonable, or out of place as to be amusing
  • Antonym: sensible - in accordance with reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin 'praeposterus' (having the last first, absurd), from 'prae' (before) + 'posterus' (coming after).
