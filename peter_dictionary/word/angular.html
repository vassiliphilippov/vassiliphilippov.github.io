angular

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a quantity) measured by an angle or by the rate of change of an angle, especially in reference to motion or size in mathematics, physics, or astronomy.

Examples

The maximum angular diameter of any planetary disk as observed from the earth is about 1 minute of arc.

Synset Examples

angular momentum

Etymology

From Latin angularis, from angulus (angle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
having angles or sharply defined edges or shapes

Examples

Scotty's father sat sprawled in his chair, angular, alert as a cricket, looking about at the huge stainless-steel appointments of the room with an expression of proprietorship.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rounded - having a smooth, curved shape; without angles or sharp corners

Etymology

From Latin 'angularis', from 'angulus' meaning 'angle'.
