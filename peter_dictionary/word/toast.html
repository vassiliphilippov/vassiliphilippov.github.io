toast

1. NOUN

/ toʊst / (AmE), / təʊst / (BrE)
Slices of bread that have been browned by exposure to heat.

Examples

After she had served the detectives coffee and toast (they politely declined eggs, uncomfortable about their tenancy), she settled down with a morning newspaper and began reading the stock market quotations.
She had a cup of something steaming, coffee perhaps, in one hand, a fresh piece of toast in the other.
She stood there, watching Holden come in, and she put the piece of toast in her mouth and bit off one corner with a huge chomp of her white teeth.
"I 've been thinking ", she said, swallowing the toast.
She took another bite of toast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bread - a staple food made of flour and water

Etymology

From Old French *toster* (to toast, roast), from Latin *tostare*, frequentative of *torrēre* (to parch, burn).

3. VERB

/ toʊst / (AmE), / təʊst / (BrE)
to make (bread, buns, etc.) brown and crisp by heating, especially in a toaster or over an open flame

Examples

The nations's number one picnic treat is the skinless frankfurter - toasted over a bonfire on the beach or, more sedately, charcoal broiled on a portable grill.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown - to cook until browned
  • Antonym: burn - to char or scorch by excessive heat

Etymology

From Old French *toster* (to grill or roast), from Latin *tostare*, frequentative of *torrere* (to parch, roast).

4. NOUN

/ toʊst / (AmE), / təʊst / (BrE)
An act of raising and drinking a glass in honor of someone or something, often accompanied by a spoken wish or tribute.

Examples

Salud!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salutation - a gesture or utterance made as a greeting or acknowledgment

Etymology

From Old French 'toste' (toasted bread), which was placed in wine, later becoming associated with the act of drinking in someone's honor.
