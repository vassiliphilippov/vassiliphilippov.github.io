blackness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæknəs / (AmE), / ˈblaknɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being black in color; the property of having the darkest shade, with little or no light.

Examples

Prevot came up "Take that spot over there ", he whispered, pointing to a small clump of blackness.
They were climbing the hill in the night when the headlights abruptly probed solid blackness, became two parallel luminous tubes which broadened out into a faint mist of light and ended.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whiteness - the quality or state of being white in color

Etymology

From 'black' + '-ness', forming a noun to denote the state or quality of being black.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblæknəs / (AmE), / ˈblaknəs / (BrE)
total darkness; the complete absence of light

Examples

Or the surging whirling sounds of bats at night, when their black bodies dived into the blackness above and below the amber street lights.
Sarah had begun to tell Lucien of Emile, she had begun to question and a little draft had crept across the room from the bedroom door, open barely enough to show a rim of blackness in the hall.

Synset Examples

they fumbled around in total darkness
in the black of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkness - absence of light
  • Degree Lower: gloom - partial or subdued darkness
  • Antonym: brightness - quality of being full of light

Etymology

From 'black' + suffix '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being black.
