nightmarish

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtˌmɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtmɛərɪʃ / (BrE)
Extremely frightening, alarming, or resembling a nightmare.

Examples

If we look at the situation this way, we can get an idea of Khrushchev's nightmarish worries - or, at least, of the worries he might have if his enemies were disposed to exploit their advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrifying - causing great fear or terror
  • Synonym: alarming - causing fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: calming - making someone feel relaxed and not afraid

Etymology

Formed from 'nightmare' + '-ish', meaning 'like a nightmare'.
