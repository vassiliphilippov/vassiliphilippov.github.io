utterance

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌtərəns / (AmE), / ˈʌtərəns / (BrE)
the act of speaking or expressing something in words; spoken words, statements, or vocal sounds used to communicate

Examples

So impressive were those serious years of study at the university that Hans later wrote, "to be perfectly free, the young man must revel in the great kingdom of thought and imagination; there is a struggle there, in which, if he falls, it is easy for him to rise again, there is freedom of utterance there, which draws after it no irreparable consequences on society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech - the expression of thoughts and feelings by spoken words; spoken language
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of sound or speech

Etymology

From Middle English 'utteraunce', from Old French 'uterance' (the action of uttering), from 'uter', meaning 'outer' (in reference to putting forth speech).
