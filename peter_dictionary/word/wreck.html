wreck

1. NOUN

/ rɛk / (AmE), / rɛk / (BrE)
A person or thing that has suffered severe damage, ruin, or dilapidation.

Examples

But he's a welfare case now - a human wreck - thanks to this modern witch doctor.

Synset Examples

the house was a wreck when they bought it
thanks to that quack I am a human wreck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - a person or thing that is irreparably damaged or destroyed
  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage; something or someone badly ruined

Etymology

From Old English *wrecan* (to drive out; avenge), later influenced by Old Norse *wrek* (wreck, driftwood).
