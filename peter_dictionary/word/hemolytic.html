hemolytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to, involving, or causing the destruction of red blood cells (hemolysis).

Examples

In an attempt to reverse the downhill trend by stimulating the bone marrow and controlling any hemolytic component, triamcinolone, 16 mg. daily, was begun on Sept. 26, 1958, and continued until Feb. 18, 1959.

Synset Examples

hemolytic anemia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysis - the breakdown of red blood cells

Etymology

From hemolysis, from Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'lysis' (loosening, dissolving), plus the suffix '-ic' (of, relating to).
