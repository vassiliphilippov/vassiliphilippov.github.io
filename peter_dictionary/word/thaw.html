thaw

1. NOUN

/ θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
A period or process during which ice, snow, or something frozen melts, especially due to a rise in temperature.

Examples

Whenever there is a thaw or a few sunny days, you 'll be likely to find a brave little blossom or two.

Synset Examples

the power failure caused a refrigerator melt that was a disaster
the thawing of a frozen turkey takes several hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freeze - process of turning from liquid to solid due to cold
  • Synonym: melt - process of becoming liquid from solid due to heat

Etymology

From Old Norse 'þeyja' (to thaw), related to Old English 'þawian'.

2. VERB

/ θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially something frozen) to become soft or liquid as a result of warming, or to cause something frozen to do this.

Examples

These cells were used rather than square Pyrex tubing because of the tendency of the latter to shatter when thawing frozen carbon tetrachloride.
These cells were thawed at 37 ` C for 30 min and were deglycerolized by alternately centrifuging and mixing with descending concentrations of glycerol solutions (20, 18, 10, 8, 4 and 2 %).
The sections were then thawed by placing a finger under the slide and dried under a fan for 30 minutes; until used they were stored for as long as 2 weeks.
He's got to thaw slow.
Insects there were on the world, but they were frozen solid during the night, only to revive and thaw in the morning sun.

Synset Examples

The sun melted the ice
the ice thawed
the ice cream melted
The heat melted the wax
The giant iceberg dissolved over the years during the global warming phase
dethaw the meat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freeze - to become hardened into ice or another solid by cold; the opposite of thaw

Etymology

From Old English thawian (to thaw, become liquid).

3. NOUN

/ θɑ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
A period of weather when temperatures rise sufficiently to melt snow and ice after a freeze.

Examples

In April came a rapid thaw that produced high waters which did not recede until mid-June.

Synset Examples

they welcomed the spring thaw

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freeze - a period of extremely cold weather when water turns to ice
