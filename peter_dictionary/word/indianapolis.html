Indianapolis

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪndiəˈnæpəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪndiəˈnæpəlɪs / (BrE)
The capital and largest city of Indiana, United States; known for its major commercial activities and as the site of the annual Indianapolis 500 motor race.

Examples

Rookie Ron Nischwitz continued his pinpoint pitching Monday night as the Bears made it two straight over Indianapolis, 5 - 3.
A major portion of the credit should also go to flautist Haumd for his rendering of the almost impossible "Indianapolis "movement in the Baslot.

Etymology

From Indiana (the state) + -polis (Greek for 'city').
