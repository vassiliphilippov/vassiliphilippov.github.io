de-iodinase

1. NOUN

/ diː ˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / diː ˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that removes an iodine atom or group from a compound, especially in the context of thyroid hormone metabolism

Examples

A microsomal de-iodinase with a pH optimum of around 8, and requiring reduced triphosphopyridine nucleotide for activity, has been identified in the thyroid (Stanbury, 1957).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a catalyst in biochemical reactions

Etymology

From 'de-' (removal) and 'iodine' with enzyme suffix '-ase'.
