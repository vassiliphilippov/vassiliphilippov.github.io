rodeo

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.di.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.di.əʊ / (BrE)
A public event in which people compete in displays of cowboy skills such as riding broncos, roping cattle, and bull riding.

Examples

No man's name brought more cheers when it was announced in a rodeo.
In the cow camps, Tom Horn was regarded as a hero, as the same kind of champion he was when he entered and invariably won the local rodeos.

Etymology

From Spanish *rodeo* (a round-up), from *rodear* (to surround).
