subterfuge

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbtɚˌfjuːdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbtəˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
A trick or dishonest way, especially one used to hide the true nature of an activity or to achieve a goal by deception.

Examples

How could the rich, for whom life was made so simple, ever understand the subterfuges, the lies, the frauds, the errors, sins and even crimes to which the poor were driven in their efforts to overcome the great advantages the rich had in the race of life?

Synset Examples

he wasn't sick--it was just a subterfuge
the holding company was just a blind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving; misleading another
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free of deceit
  • Synonym: ruse - a clever trick used to deceive someone

Etymology

From Latin subterfugium (an escape), from subter (secretly) + fugere (to flee).
