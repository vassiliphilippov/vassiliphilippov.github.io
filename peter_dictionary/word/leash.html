leash

1. NOUN

/ liʃ / (AmE), / liːʃ / (BrE)
A long, narrow strap, rope, or chain attached to an animal, especially a dog, and held by a person to keep the animal under control.

Examples

He thought he saw - it awakened and, for a moment, interested him - that Elizabeth held a leash in her hand and that a round fuzzy puppy was on the end of the leash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - a British term for a leash used for animals, especially dogs

Etymology

From Old French *les*, *lais* (a strap, thong), from Latin *laxare* (to loosen).
