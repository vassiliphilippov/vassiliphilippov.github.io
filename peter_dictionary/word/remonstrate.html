remonstrate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmɑnstreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒnstreɪt / (BrE)
to protest or argue forcefully against something; to make a strong objection or disapproval

Examples

The part of the mind that preserves dates and events may remonstrate, "It could have been like that for only a little while "; but true memory does not count nor add: it holds fast to things that were and they are outside of time.
"That's hardly a Christian approach ", she remonstrated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - to express strong objection

Etymology

From Medieval Latin remonstratus, past participle of remonstrare (to point out, show).
