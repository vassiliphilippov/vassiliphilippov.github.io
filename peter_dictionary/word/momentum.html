momentum

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / məˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
The quantity of motion an object has, measured as the product of its mass and velocity; also, the driving force or impetus gained by movement or by a course of events.

Examples

The Russian experimenters claim that only a small fraction of the impulse from the sensors is caused by the incident momentum with the remainder being momentum of ejected material from the sensor.
