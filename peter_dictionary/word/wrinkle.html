wrinkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
A small line or fold in something smooth, especially in skin, fabric, or another surface.

Examples

He then sold her minerals to cure her kidney ailment, a can of sage "to make her look like a girl again ", and an application of plain mud to take her wrinkles away.
The boy shuddered at the deathly pale countenance with its wrinkles and gray hair.
"Not a line, not a wrinkle.
The next time we saw him, he was a changed person; he had aged thirty years, and his face, the color of tallow, was crisscrossed with wrinkles, as though it had been wrapped in chicken wire.

Synset Examples

his face has many lines
ironing gets rid of most wrinkles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced on paper or cloth by folding, pressing, or crushing

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'wrinklen', of Scandinavian origin; related to Norwegian 'vrangla' (to twist).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
to make small lines or folds on the surface of something, especially something smooth, by pressing, folding, or contracting it

Examples

"You 're boiling milk, ai n't you "? soothing it with his hand, knowing the whiskered jowls and the swollen smoothness of teats that wrinkled expectantly to his touch.

Synset Examples

The dress got wrinkled
crease the paper like this to make a crane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crease - to make a line or fold in something by crushing or folding it
  • Antonym: smooth - to make a surface free from wrinkles or roughness

Etymology

From Old English *wrinclian* or *wrincle* (a twist, fold), related to Old Norse *hrukka* (wrinkle).

3. VERB

/ ˈrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
to contract or gather together into wrinkles or folds; to pucker

Examples

Maude's long nose unexpectedly wrinkled up.
Her little brown face wrinkled up, her brown eyes gleamed, and with her little gestures she said all the courteous things.

Synset Examples

purse ones's lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pucker - to gather into wrinkles or small folds
  • Antonym: smooth - to make flat or free from wrinkles

Etymology

From Old English 'wrencan' (to twist, turn).
