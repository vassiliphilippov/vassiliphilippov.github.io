perforation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɜːrfəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːfəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A row of small holes made in a sheet of material, especially paper, for easy tearing along the line.

Examples

Many styles have perforations and an almost weightlessness achieved via unlined leathers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hole - an opening or gap in a solid body or surface

Etymology

From Latin perforatio, from perforare ‘to bore through’.
