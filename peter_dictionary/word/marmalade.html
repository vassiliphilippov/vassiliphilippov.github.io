marmalade

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrməˌleɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːməleɪd / (BrE)
A preserve made from the pulp and rind of citrus fruits, especially oranges, boiled with sugar.

Examples

They passed the marmalade, the bread, the black-market butter, back and forth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a sweet spread or preserve made from fruit and sugar
  • Synonym: jelly - a clear, sweet preserve made from fruit juice and sugar

Etymology

From Portuguese 'marmelada', from 'marmelo' meaning 'quince'.
