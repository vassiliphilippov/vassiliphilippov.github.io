overachievement

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərəˈtʃivmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈtʃiːvmənt / (BrE)
the act of performing better than expected, especially as predicted by intelligence or aptitude tests

Examples

The criterion score used in the statistical analysis is an index of over - or under-achievement.
If a child with an I. Q. of 120 scored 5.5 in achievement, his discrepancy score would be + .7, representing .7 of one year of over-achievement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underachievement - performance below what is expected or predicted by measures such as intelligence tests

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (above or beyond) and 'achievement'.
