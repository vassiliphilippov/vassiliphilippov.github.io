chin-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪn ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪn ʌp / (BrE)
An exercise in which you pull yourself up with your arms while hanging from a bar until your chin is level with the bar.

Examples

Ask any group of ballerinas to do ten push-ups or three chin-ups and the results, considering the amount of physical training they have had, will be very disappointing.
Those children who can chin themselves should be told to do one chin up each time they pass under it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull-up - an arm exercise where you pull yourself up on a bar

Etymology

From 'chin' (noun, the lower part of the face) + 'up', describing the upward movement of the chin during the exercise.
