toxic

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
Containing or caused by a poison or toxin; harmful or capable of causing illness or death by chemical means.

Examples

Biological warfare is the intentional use of living microorganisms or their toxic products for the purpose of destroying or reducing the military effectiveness of man.
Some agents have been shown to be much more toxic or infectious to experimental animals when exposed to aerosols of optimum particle size than by the natural portal.
Botulinal toxin, for example, is several thousand-fold more toxic by this route than when given per os.
But hairspray mixed with lipstick and perfume will be toxic, and traceable.

Synset Examples

suffering from exposure to toxic substances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisonous - containing or producing poison
  • Antonym: nontoxic - not containing or caused by poison; not harmful

Etymology

From Greek toxikon (poison for arrows), from toxikos (of or for a bow).
