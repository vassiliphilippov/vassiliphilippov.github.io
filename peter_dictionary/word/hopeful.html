hopeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊp.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊp.fəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing hope that something desired will happen or be the case.

Examples

Many people seem hopeful, yet it is difficult to predict whether or not there will be any more real attainment of Christian unity in 1961 than there was in 1861.
He could think of nothing else to tell them: no assurances, no hopeful hints at great discoveries that day.

Synset Examples

a line of people hopeful of obtaining tickets
found a hopeful way of attacking the problem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hopeless - having or showing no hope; despairing
  • Synonym: optimistic - expecting the best; hopeful about the future

Etymology

From hope +‎ -ful.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpfəl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfʊl / (BrE)
Giving cause for hope; showing signs of future success or promise.

Examples

But it was a hopeful sign, he told himself.

Synset Examples

had a bright future in publishing
the scandal threatened an abrupt end to a promising political career
a hopeful new singer on Broadway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promising - showing signs of future success
  • Antonym: unpromising - not likely to be successful

Etymology

From hope + -ful (suffix forming adjectives).
