headlinese

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdlaɪnˌiz / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlaɪnˌiːz / (BrE)
The distinctive, abbreviated style of language commonly used in newspaper headlines.

Examples

Apart from misplaced modifiers and headlinese, journalism contributes a wide variety of comic ambiguities in both editorial and advertising matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journalese - the characteristic style of language used in journalism

Etymology

Formed from 'headline' + '-ese', the suffix denoting a type of language variety.
