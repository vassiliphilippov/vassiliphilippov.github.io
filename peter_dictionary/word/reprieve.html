reprieve

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpriv / (AmE), / rɪˈpriːv / (BrE)
a temporary relief from something harmful, unpleasant, or difficult

Examples

So Linda Kay gave up asking, and accepted her reprieve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respite - a short period of rest or relief from something difficult

Etymology

From Old French *reprendre* (to take back), from Latin *reprehendere* (to hold back, restrain).
