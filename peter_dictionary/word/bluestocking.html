bluestocking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌstɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌstɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
A woman with strong literary or intellectual interests, especially one regarded as having such interests in a period when this was considered unusual for women.

Examples

The casual seduction of a seventeen year old bluestocking seemed tame by comparison.

Etymology

Originally from 18th-century Blue Stocking Society in England, where women and men met for literary discussions. The name referred to the blue worsted stockings worn by some members.
