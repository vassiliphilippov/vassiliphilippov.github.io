tape

1. NOUN

/ teɪp / (AmE), / teɪp / (BrE)
a recording of sound or images made on magnetic tape, especially for audio or video playback or archival purposes.

Examples

London explains that the very distinct directional effect in the Phase 4 series is due in large part to their novel methods of microphoning and recording the music on a number of separate tape channels.
In some of the numbers the instrumental parts have even been recorded at different times and then later combined on the master tape to produce special effects.
I want to get this on tape.

Synset Examples

the several recordings were combined on a master tape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassette - a small plastic case containing magnetic tape, for audio or video recording
  • Synonym: reel-to-reel - an open spool magnetic tape recording format
  • Antonym: digital recording - a recording made using digital technology, as opposed to magnetic tape

Etymology

From Old English *tæppe* (narrow strip of cloth); extended to magnetic recording media in the mid-20th century.
