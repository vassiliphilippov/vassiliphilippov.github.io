seafarer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌfɛrər / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌfeərə / (BrE)
A person who works or travels on ships at sea, especially as a sailor.

Examples

The portwatchers were retired longshoremen and small time seafarers off towboats and barges who acted as watchmen on the wharves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariner - a person who navigates or assists in navigating a ship; sailor
  • Synonym: sailor - a person whose job is working as a crew member on a ship

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'farer' (one who travels), modeled after 'wayfarer'.
