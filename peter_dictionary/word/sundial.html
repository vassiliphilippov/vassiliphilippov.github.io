sundial

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdaɪəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.daɪ.əl / (BrE)
An instrument that shows the time of day by the position of a shadow cast by a gnomon on a marked plate.

Examples

And his eyes - those miniature sundials of variegated yellow - had not altered their expression or direction.

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'dial', first recorded in the 16th century.
