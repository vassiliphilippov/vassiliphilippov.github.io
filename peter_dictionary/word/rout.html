rout

1. VERB

/ raʊt / (AmE), / raʊt / (BrE)
to defeat an opponent or group decisively and force them to retreat or flee in disorder

Examples

Johnny Blanchard, Mantle's replacement, slammed a 2 - run homer as the Yankees routed loser Joey Jay in a 5 - run first inning.

Synset Examples

rout out the fighters from their caves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone or something
  • Synonym: trounce - to defeat thoroughly
  • Antonym: lose - to be defeated in a contest or battle

Etymology

From Middle English 'routen', possibly from Old French 'rute' (a defeat, retreat) or Latin 'rupta' (broken ranks, from 'rumpere', to break).
