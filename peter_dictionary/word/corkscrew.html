corkscrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrkˌskru / (AmE), / ˈkɔːk.skruː / (BrE)
A tool with a spiral metal rod used for drawing corks from bottles.

Examples

A chicken with six tits and a tail lak a corkscrew.

Etymology

From 'cork' (the material) + 'screw' (tool for turning); attested from the late 17th century.
