scepticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛp.tɪ.sɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈskɛp.tɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The attitude of doubting or questioning the possibility of absolute or ultimate knowledge; philosophical disbelief in any claims of ultimate knowledge.

Examples

As long as perception is seen as composed only of isolated sense data, most of the quality and interconnectedness of existence loses its objectivity, becomes an invention of consciousness, and the result is a philosophical scepticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agnosticism - the belief that ultimate knowledge is impossible; philosophical uncertainty about ultimate truths
  • Antonym: dogmatism - tendency to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true; positive assertion of opinions as truths

Etymology

From Greek *skepsis* (inquiry, doubt), via Latin *scepticus* and French *scepticisme*.
