abortion

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
The deliberate termination of a pregnancy, resulting in the removal or expulsion of an embryo or fetus before it can survive outside the uterus.

Examples

The raped valley was a pregnant womb awaiting abortion.
To reduce vibrionic abortion in breeding sheep, feed 80 milligrams per head daily.
In general, the means (excluding abortion) that prove most effective are considered the most ethical.
It was an abortion, Michael.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: miscarriage - spontaneous loss of pregnancy before the fetus can survive outside the uterus

Etymology

From Latin abortio (miscarriage), from aboriri (to miscarry, to pass away).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbɔɹʃən / (AmE), / əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
A complete failure or ruin, especially of a plan or attempt.

Examples

Just like our marriage is an abortion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success
  • Antonym: success - achievement of a desired result

Etymology

From Latin abortio, from aboriri (to miscarry).
