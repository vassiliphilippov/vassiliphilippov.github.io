flour

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
A fine powder made by grinding cereal grains or other starchy food substances, used in baking and cooking.

Examples

The bottle was filled up with flour.
Early in 1822 he was at Fort Garry offering to bring in pork, flour, liquor and tobacco.
He poured the water off the sourdough and off the flour, salvaging the chunky, watery messes for biscuits of a sort.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: meal - coarsely ground grain, less finely milled than flour

Etymology

From Old French 'fleur' (flower; finest part of meal) and Latin 'flos' (flower).
