rebuke

1. VERB

/ rɪˈbjuk / (AmE), / rɪˈbjuːk / (BrE)
to express sharp or severe disapproval or criticism of someone because of their actions

Examples

He further called upon the people of Providence to rebuke the meeting and avoid disgrace.
Interruptions came largely from the bench, which numerous times rebuked the Attorney General for letting his witnesses run on, though it, too, made no serious effort to choke off the flow.
But her mother would rebuke her if she mentioned it, and say that it was none of her concern.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprimand - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: scold - to speak angrily or criticize someone for their behavior
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French *rebuchier*, meaning 'to check, chide', originally from re- (expressing opposition) + buschier (to strike).
