transmute

1. VERB

/ trænzˈmjut / (AmE), / trænzˈmjuːt / (BrE)
to change or alter in form, appearance, or nature, often into a different or higher state

Examples

In this process the Minoan Mycenaean inheritance had been transmuted or finally rejected; the Aegean world which had existed before 1000 differed from that which rises more clearly in our vision after 800.
Individuals possessing unusual gifts and great personal power were transmuted at death into awesome spirits; they were almost immediately worshipped for these newer, even more terrible abilities.

Synset Examples

He transformed into a monster
The salesman metamorphosed into an ugly beetle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change in form, appearance, or character
  • Synonym: metamorphose - to undergo or cause to undergo a change in form or nature

Etymology

From Latin transmutare, from trans- (across) + mutare (to change).

2. VERB

/ trænzˈmjut / (AmE), / trænzˈmjuːt / (BrE)
to change or alter in form, appearance, or nature; to transform into a different state or substance

Examples

Thus jazz is transmuted into something holy, the sacred road to integration of being.

Synset Examples

This experience transformed her completely
She transformed the clay into a beautiful sculpture
transubstantiate one element into another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transform - to change something completely, especially in a marked or dramatic way
  • Synonym: convert - to change into a different form or function

Etymology

From Latin transmutare, from trans- ‘across’ + mutare ‘to change’.
