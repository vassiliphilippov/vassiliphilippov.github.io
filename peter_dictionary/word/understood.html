understood

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈstʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstʊd / (BrE)
Not stated openly but implied or assumed; tacit.

Examples

It was at that party that, finally overcoming my timidity, inspired by tales only half understood and overheard among older boys, I asked Jessie to spend New Year's Eve with me.
I understood he was coming.

Synset Examples

gave silent consent
a tacit agreement
the understood provisos of a custody agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tacit - understood or implied without being stated
  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈstʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstʊd / (BrE)
Clearly known or agreed by all parties, even if not directly stated; fully comprehended in purpose or meaning.

Examples

But he takes his bearings from the great guidelines of policy, well established precedents, the commitments of the United States under international charters and treaties, basic statutes, and well understood notions of the American people about how we are to conduct ourselves, in policy literature such as country papers and National Security Council papers accumulated in the Department.

Synset Examples

the understood conditions of troop withdrawal were clear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt

Etymology

Past participle of 'understand', from Old English 'understandan'.
