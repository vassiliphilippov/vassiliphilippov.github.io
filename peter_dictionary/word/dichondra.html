dichondra

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkɑndrə / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒndrə / (BrE)
a creeping perennial herb with hairy stems and rounded or kidney-shaped leaves, often used as a grass substitute in warm climates

Examples

A few yards beyond the group of men, a man's nude body lay face down on a patch of thick green dichondra.

Etymology

From New Latin Dichondra, from Greek di- (two) + chondros (grain, lump), referring to the fruit's two-lobed shape.
