certification

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.tə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially recognizing someone or something as having a particular status or meeting certain standards, often by issuing a formal document

Examples

The element of public danger which enters so largely into architectural certification, however, would demand a prolonged study of structure.
Not only should every educator above the rank of instructor be expected to be a member of one of the professional organizations, but his first qualification for membership as an educator should be so sharply scrutinized that membership would be equivalent to certification to teach the subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - formal permission to do something, often by a government or authority
  • Synonym: authorization - the official act of giving approval or permission
  • Antonym: revocation - the official act of rescinding or withdrawing an official status or right

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'certificatio', from Latin 'certificare' (to certify).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.t̬ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
official confirmation or attestation of a fact, statement, or status, typically provided in writing

Examples

Such certification shall be final and conclusive and shall not be subject to review by any other official or department, agency, or establishment of the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate - an official document attesting a fact, qualification, or status

Etymology

From Medieval Latin certificatio, from Latin certificare (to make certain).
