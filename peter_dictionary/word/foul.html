foul

1. VERB

/ faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
(in baseball and related sports) to hit a ball so that it lands outside the designated fair area, resulting in a foul ball.

Examples

John Powell, batting for Adair, fanned after fouling off two 2 - and 2 pitches, and Buddy Barker, up for Stepanovich, bounced out sharply to Jerry Lumpe at second to end the 2 - hour and - 27 - minute contest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fair - (of a ball) landing within the designated fair area in sports

2. VERB

/ faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
to make something dirty or impure; to pollute

Examples

Their presence fouled the elegance of that room.

Synset Examples

The industrial wastes polluted the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollute - to make dirty or impure, especially by adding harmful substances
  • Antonym: cleanse - to make clean or pure

Etymology

From Old French 'fuler', from Latin 'fullo' (person who cleans cloth), related to Old English 'fūl' (foul, dirty).

3. ADJ

/ faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
(of actions or behavior) violating accepted standards or rules; dishonest or unfair, especially in a sporting or competitive context.

Examples

How's it strike you, foul or fair "?

Synset Examples

a dirty fighter
used foul means to gain power
a nasty unsporting serve
fined for unsportsmanlike behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fair - in accordance with rules or standards; honest
  • Synonym: unsporting - not showing sportsmanship or fairness
  • Synonym: cheating - acting dishonestly to gain an advantage

Etymology

From Old English *fūl* (dirty, sinful, foul).
