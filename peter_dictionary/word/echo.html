echo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
A sound or series of sounds that is reflected off a surface and heard again after the original sound has stopped.

Examples

"Nae, man ", said Willie, "ye must be countin' the echoes ".
a dull boom and a throbbing echo.
he shouted, and was aghast at the echoes.
Our readers are familiar with the principles of sonar where sound waves are sent out in water and the echoes then indicate submerged objects.
As he had longed to be, he became the echo of a saga.

Synset Examples

she could hear echoes of her own footsteps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverberation - prolongation of a sound; continued echo
  • Antonym: silence - absence of sound or noise

Etymology

From Latin 'echo', from Ancient Greek 'ēkhō' (ἠχώ, sound).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛk.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛk.əʊ / (BrE)
to repeat or imitate what someone else has said, often to express agreement or reinforce the statement

Examples

"A public of one ", Mary echoed reprovingly.
"Just you wait ", advised Arlene, echoing the dialogue in a recent British movie.
"Sportin "'! he echoed again in soft wonder.
"What in the world ", echoed others, those come with the beans, potatoes, the tomatoes, "will any of us do "?
The staircase itself seemed still to be echoing the heavy footfalls of many men.

Synset Examples

followers echoing the cries of their leaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say something again, either exactly or in a similar way

Etymology

From Greek 'ēkhō', meaning 'sound' or 'echo'.

3. VERB

/ ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be repeated or reflected after being produced, as when sound bounces back from a surface.

Examples

How lightly her "eventshah-leh "passed into the crannies where I was storing dialect material for some vaguely dreamed opus, and how the word would echo.
The weird, insane sound of the Rebel yell reverberated again and echoed from the distant hills.

Synset Examples

the hall resounded with laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverberate - (of sound) to be repeated several times as an echo
  • Synonym: resound - to fill a place with sound, to echo loudly

Etymology

From Latin 'echo', from Greek 'ēkhō' (sound, echo).
