spoken

1. ADJ

/ ˈspoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈspəʊkən / (BrE)
expressed or communicated by speaking rather than writing; uttered by speech.

Examples

There's more reading and instruction to be heard on discs than ever before, although the spoken rather than the sung word is as old as Thomas Alva Edison's first experiment in recorded sound.
He was smooth and civil spoken but it seemed to me there was something tough under his self-effacing manner.

Synset Examples

a spoken message
the spoken language
a soft-spoken person
sharp-spoken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbal - relating to spoken words rather than written
  • Antonym: written - expressed in writing rather than by speech

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'speak', from Old English 'specan' or 'sprecan'.
