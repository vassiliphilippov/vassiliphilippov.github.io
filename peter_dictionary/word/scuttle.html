scuttle

1. VERB

/ ˈskʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈskʌt.l̩ / (BrE)
to move quickly with short, hurried steps, especially to escape or hide

Examples

And if it was not an individual who was in the dock, why was the Trial, as we shall observe later, all but scuttled in the attempt to prove Eichmann a "fiend "?
They are huge areas which have been swept by winds for so many centuries that there is no soil left, but only deep bare ridges fifty or sixty yards apart with ravines between them thirty or forty feet deep and the only thing that moves is a scuttling layer of sand.
He scuttled in shadow along the east wall of the stockade and then followed the south wall until he was at the rear of the two frame buildings.

Synset Examples

so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurry - to move hurriedly with short steps

Etymology

Perhaps from Scandinavian (cf. Danish 'skutte' to run, hurry), or related to an old northern English dialect word meaning 'to run quickly.'
