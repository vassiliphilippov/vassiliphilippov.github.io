crunch

1. VERB

/ krʌntʃ / (AmE), / krʌntʃ / (BrE)
to make a crushing or crackling noise, especially when being pressed, stepped on, or chewed

Examples

A carriage crunched by, its dim lights filtering through the gloom.

Synset Examples

his shoes were crunching on the gravel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackle - to make a series of small sharp noises
  • Synonym: snap - to make a sudden sharp cracking noise
  • Degree Lower: squelch - to make a soft, sucking sound, often in mud

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced during crushing or grinding, possibly influenced by 'crush' and 'crackle'.

2. NOUN

/ krʌntʃ / (AmE), / krʌntʃ / (BrE)
A loud, crisp sound produced when something is crushed or pressed, especially underfoot or between teeth.

Examples

Horse smell was very strong, and he could hear the crunch of grain being ground between strong jaws.

Synset Examples

he heard the crunch of footsteps on the gravel path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackle - a series of small sharp snapping noises
  • Synonym: crush - to compress or squeeze forcibly so as to break, damage, or distort

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by crushing or grinding.
