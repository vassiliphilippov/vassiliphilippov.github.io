streamliner

1. NOUN

/ ˈstriːmlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈstriːmlaɪnə / (BrE)
A passenger train with a streamlined design, typically built for high speed and efficiency, popular in the mid-20th century.

Examples

The Union Pacific Railroad streamliner, City of San Francisco, stopped in Ogden, Utah, for a few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - a series of connected vehicles on a railway

Etymology

From 'streamline' (to design with smooth, flowing lines) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
