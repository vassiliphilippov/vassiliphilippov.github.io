counterchallenge

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
To issue a challenge in response to a previous challenge; to challenge in turn, especially in the context of authentication or verification processes.

Examples

Additional codes can be used to challenge and counterchallenge the authentications.

Synset Examples

The authentication was counterchallenged

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'challenge'; patterning on verbs like 'counterattack'.
