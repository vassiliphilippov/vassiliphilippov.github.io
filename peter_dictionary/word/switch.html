switch

1. VERB

/ swɪtʃ / (AmE), / swɪtʃ / (BrE)
to exchange or replace one thing with another; to substitute or give something in exchange for something else

Examples

He attended New York University before switching to Georgetown University in Washington.
He told once of how he switched his style of playing to jazz after listening to two old Armstrong records he bought in the Flea Market in Paris.
Moreover, it allows the present management to reassign vehicles so that mileage will be more uniformly distributed throughout the fleet; for example, if one driver puts on 22000 miles per year and another driver 8000 miles per year, their cars will be switched so that both cars will have 30000 miles after two years, rather than 44000 miles (and related higher maintenance costs) and 16000 miles respectively ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - to give something and receive something else in return
  • Synonym: swap - to exchange one thing for another

Etymology

Originates from Middle English 'swichen', perhaps of Low German origin; influenced by form and sense of 'switche', meaning whip or rod.

2. NOUN

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitution - the act of putting one thing in place of another
  • Synonym: replacement - the act of replacing one thing with another

3. NOUN

/ swɪtʃ / (AmE), / swɪtʃ / (BrE)
a device used to open or close an electrical circuit, turning a current or device on or off

Examples

She went into the living room and turned on three lamps, then back into the kitchen where she turned on the ceiling light and the switch that lit the floods on the barn, illuminating the driveway.
Putting the key into the switch, pressing the accelerator with his foot, putting the car into reverse, seemed vast endeavors almost beyond the ability of his shaking body.
Hoag stretched his left hand to the wall and fumbled for the switch: evil flourishes in the dark.
Greg slapped his hand across the switches that turned on the guns and gun camera and gun sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuit breaker - a safety device that automatically stops the flow of electricity if a fault develops
  • Synonym: toggle - a switch with a lever that moves back and forth

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *swicte* (a whip) or obsolete Dutch *swits*, related to switching as striking or moving quickly.

4. VERB

/ swɪtʃ / (AmE), / swɪtʃ / (BrE)
to change from one thing to another; to stop using or doing one thing and start using or doing another

Examples

The video signal is amplified and then switched, in synchronism with the three ultraviolet light sources which are sequenced by the rotating mirror so that during one-twentieth of a second only one wavelength, corresponding to red, green, or blue, is seen.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - to make or become different; alter
  • Antonym: switch back - to return to a previous choice or state

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'swetsen' (to strike, swish).
