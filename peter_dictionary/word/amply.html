amply

1. ADV

/ ˈæmpli / (AmE), / ˈampli / (BrE)
to a sufficient or more than adequate degree; fully or generously.

Examples

And she felt amply rewarded for her suffering when the evidence of Lee's quack shenanigans, gathered by the tape recorder under her friend's clothing, proved adequate in court for convicting Franklin D. Lee.
Results from this approach amply confirm the direct observations:

Synset Examples

these voices were amply represented
we benefited richly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adequately - to a satisfactory or sufficient degree
  • Synonym: richly - in a way that is more than sufficient; abundantly

Etymology

From 'ample' (Latin 'amplus', meaning large or spacious) + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
