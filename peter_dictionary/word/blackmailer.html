blackmailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌmeɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌmeɪlə / (BrE)
a person who demands money from someone by threatening to reveal embarrassing, damaging, or incriminating information about them

Examples

The important thing was that Burton had gone somewhere to meet a blackmailer with a gun in his pocket.
Maybe he only intended to scare the blackmailer, whoever he was, in which case an unloaded gun would be good enough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extortionist - a person who obtains something through force or threats

Etymology

From 'blackmail' (originally meaning 'protection money'), + '-er' (agent noun suffix). 'Blackmail' is from 16th-century Scottish black (black) + mail (payment/rent).
