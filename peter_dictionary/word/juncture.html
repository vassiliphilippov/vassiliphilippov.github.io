juncture

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
A particular point in time, especially one which is critical or important.

Examples

Sometimes, as at this juncture, he did not.

Synset Examples

at such junctures he always had an impulse to leave
it was needed only on special occasions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Synonym: turning point - a time at which a decisive change in a situation occurs

Etymology

From Latin *junctura* (a joining), from *jungere* (to join).
