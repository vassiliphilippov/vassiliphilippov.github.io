sushi

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuʃi / (AmE), / ˈsuːʃi / (BrE)
A Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as raw fish, vegetables, and wrapped in seaweed or formed into rolls.

Examples

When he had given the call a few moments thought, he went into the kitchen to ask Mrs. Yamata to prepare tea and sushi for the visitors, using the formal English china and the silver tea service which had been donated to the mission, then he went outside to inspect the grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sashimi - Japanese dish of thinly sliced raw fish, served without rice

Etymology

From Japanese 寿司 (sushi), originally referring to fermented rice and fish dishes.
