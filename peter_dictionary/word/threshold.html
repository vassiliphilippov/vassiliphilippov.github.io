threshold

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃ.həʊld / (BrE)
the level at which a stimulus is strong enough to be detected or to produce a response

Examples

Yet it is the accumulation of distortion, the fitting together of fractional bits until the total reaches the threshold of our awareness, that makes records sound like records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limen - a technical synonym for sensory threshold

Etymology

From Old English 'þrescwald', of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
The strip of wood, stone, or metal forming the bottom of a doorway and crossed when entering a room or building.

Examples

There is no evidence that these Milquetoasts became suddenly emboldened when they crossed the threshhold of the master bedroom.

Etymology

From Old English *þrescold* (threshold, door-sill).
