wad

1. VERB

/ wɑd / (AmE), / wɒd / (BrE)
to compress or form into a wad or a small, compact mass

Examples

Johnson unwired the right hand door, whose window was, like the left one, merely loosely taped fragments of glass, and Johnson wadded himself into a narrow seat made still more narrow by three cases of beer.

Synset Examples

wad paper into the box

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - to fill tightly or compress into a container or space

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Middle English 'wad' (a bundle of fibrous material).
