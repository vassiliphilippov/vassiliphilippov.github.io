frustrating

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing annoyance or discouragement by preventing success or progress.

Examples

An argument with Orville Torrence Killpath was as frustrating and as futile as a cap pistol on a firing range.
It was frustrating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discouraging - causing someone to lose confidence or hope
  • Antonym: encouraging - giving someone support or confidence

Etymology

From the verb 'frustrate', which comes from Latin frustrari (to deceive, disappoint).
