dressed

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrɛst / (AmE), / ˈdrɛst / (BrE)
wearing clothes, especially of a particular type or in a particular style; sometimes used in combination (e.g., formal-dressed, well-dressed)

Examples

Another man approached, this one fully dressed.
The figure was so theatrically dressed, that it was as though a character from some other play had blundered into this one.
Both were dressed rather formally.
Martha Schuyler, old, slow, careful of foot, came down the great staircase, dressed in her best lace-drawn black silk, her jeweled shoe buckles held forward.
It was the doctor, dressed and ready for the expedition to the market, and Alex was obliged to prepare himself in haste.

Synset Examples

the elegantly attired gentleman

went about oddly garmented
professors robed in crimson
tuxedo-attired gentlemen
crimson-robed Harvard professors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothed - wearing clothes
  • Antonym: naked - not wearing any clothes
  • Antonym: undressed - not wearing any clothes
  • Degree Higher: well-dressed - wearing neat, elegant, or stylish clothes
  • Degree Lower: shabbily dressed - wearing worn or old clothes

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'dress', from Old French *dresser* (arrange, prepare), from Vulgar Latin *directiare* (set straight).
