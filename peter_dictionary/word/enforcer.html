enforcer

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːsə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to carry out unpleasant or violent tasks, often on behalf of someone in authority, such as enforcing threats, punishment, or illegal orders

Examples

What about the theory that the Batman is a Mob enforcer?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: henchman - a faithful follower or supporter, especially one prepared to engage in crime or violence by way of service
  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal

Etymology

From enforce + -er, meaning one who enforces or compels.
