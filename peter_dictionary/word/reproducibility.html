reproducibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːprəˌduːsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˌdjuːsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be reproduced, especially under the same conditions in scientific or technical contexts.

Examples

Contrary to these expectations we have found it impossible to obtain the degree of reproducibility one would wish, even with extensive efforts to prepare especially pure reagents.
The reproducibilities of helium vapor-pressure thermometers have been investigated in conjunction with a "constant temperature "liquid helium bath from 4.2 to 1.8 * * f.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeatability - the degree to which a process or experiment can be exactly duplicated

Etymology

From "reproducible" + "-ity", patterned after similar English nouns denoting quality or state.
