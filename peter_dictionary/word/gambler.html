gambler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.blɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.blə / (BrE)
a person who risks money on the outcome of a game, race, or other unpredictable event

Examples

They were disturbed by his idiotic bravado - as, when his bodyguard, Yankee Schwartz, complained that he had been snubbed by Dave Miller, a prize-fight referee, chieftain of a Jewish gang and one of four brothers of tough reputation, who were Hirschey, a gambler politician in loose beer running league with Torrio and O' Banion, Frank, a policeman, and Max, the youngest.
We figure that entertainment would draw gamblers to the casino.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bettor - a person who places bets, especially on the outcome of sporting events
  • Synonym: punter - a person who gambles or places bets, especially in British English

Etymology

From gamble (to play games of chance) + -er (agent suffix).
