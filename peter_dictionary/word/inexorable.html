inexorable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛksərəbl / (BrE)
impossible to stop or prevent; not moved by pleas, persuasion, or requests.

Examples

The process of flattening seemed inexorable, and it became necessary to emphasize the surface still further in order to prevent it from fusing with the illusion.

Synset Examples

he is adamant in his refusal to change his mind
Cynthia was inexorable; she would have none of him
an intransigent conservative opposed to every liberal tendency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstoppable - impossible to stop or prevent
  • Synonym: relentless - continuing in a determined way without stopping or yielding
  • Antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; flexible or giving in

Etymology

From Latin inexorabilis (not to be moved by prayer), from in- (not) + exorabilis (inclined to entreaties).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to stop or prevent; not to be persuaded, moved, or appeased by entreaty.

Examples

As Broadway itself becomes increasingly weighted down by trite, heavy-handed, commercially successful musicals and inspirational problem dramas, the American theatre is going through an inexorable renaissance in that nebulous area known as "off-Broadway ".

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - unceasingly intense or harsh, persistent
  • Synonym: unstoppable - impossible to stop or prevent
  • Antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; responsive to persuasion

Etymology

From Latin inexorabilis, from in- (not) + exorabilis (able to be entreated).
