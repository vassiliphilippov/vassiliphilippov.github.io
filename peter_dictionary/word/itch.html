itch

1. VERB

/ ɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪtʃ / (BrE)
to have or experience an uncomfortable skin sensation that causes a desire to scratch

Examples

his fear lest in this closed hall he should suddenly itch to relieve himself.

Synset Examples

I'm itching--the air is so dry!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scratch - to rub one's skin with the nails, often to relieve itching

Etymology

From Old English *giccan* (to itch).

2. VERB

/ ɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪtʃ / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) to have an uncomfortable sensation on the skin that causes a desire to scratch or rub.

Examples

"I 'm starting to itch ", she said.
"Itch "?
Mmmm, it sure itches ".

Synset Examples

Don't scratch your insect bites!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to rub the skin with nails or a sharp object, often to relieve itching

Etymology

Old English *giccan* (to itch).
