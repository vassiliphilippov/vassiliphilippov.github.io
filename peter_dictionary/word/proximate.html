proximate

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑksəmət / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪmət / (BrE)
(formal) Closest or nearest in degree, order, time, or space, especially in a sequence of events or causes.

Examples

It follows that victory over Communism is the dominant, proximate goal of American policy.
Proximate in the sense that there are more distant, more "positive "ends we seek, to which victory over Communism is but a means.

Synset Examples

news of his proximate arrival
interest in proximate rather than ultimate goals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ultimate - furthest or final in a sequence, opposite of 'proximate'
  • Synonym: immediate - occurring or done at once; closest in time or relationship; close in meaning to 'proximate'

Etymology

From Latin proximatus, past participle of proximare ‘to approach’, from proximus ‘nearest’.
