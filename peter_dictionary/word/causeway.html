causeway

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔzˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzˌweɪ / (BrE)
A raised road or track across low or wet ground, often built over water or marshland.

Examples

They shot Sonny on the causeway.

Etymology

From Middle English 'cauceswey', from Old Northern French 'caucie' (paved road) + 'wei' (way).
