pledge

1. VERB

/ plɛdʒ / (AmE), / plɛdʒ / (BrE)
to make a solemn and formal promise, especially publicly or officially

Examples

Black said COAHR "hoped to be able to integrate the theaters without taking direct action, but we are pledged to using every legal and nonviolent means at our disposal ".
Buchheister pledged the land would be an "inviolate "sanctuary for all birds, animals and plants.
In exchange for higher price supports, growers pledged reduction in planted acreage.
I pledge my ever-ending loyalty.

Synset Examples

I pledge that I will honor my wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promise - to make a declaration that one will do something
  • Synonym: vow - to make a solemn promise or assertion, often religious
  • Antonym: forswear - to renounce or reject solemnly (as an antonym, to pledge not to)

Etymology

From Middle English 'plegge', from Old French 'plege', from Late Latin 'plebium' (security, bail).
