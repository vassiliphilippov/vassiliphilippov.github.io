asphalt

1. NOUN

/ ˈæs.fɔlt / (AmE), / ˈas.fɑːlt / (BrE)
A black, sticky substance mixed with gravel or sand and used for paving roads, covering roofs, etc.

Examples

The side slopes were coated with fiberglas matting coated with asphalt to prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitumen - a viscous mixture of hydrocarbons, also used in road surfacing

Etymology

From Late Latin *asphaltum*, from Greek *ásphaltos* (asphalt, bitumen).
