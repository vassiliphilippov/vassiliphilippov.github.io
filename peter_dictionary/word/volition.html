volition

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈlɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / vəˈlɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the faculty or power of using one's will; the act of making a conscious choice or decision

Examples

It was practically the last move that McBride made of his own volition.

Synset Examples

the exercise of their volition we construe as revolt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will - the faculty by which a person decides on and initiates action

Etymology

From Latin 'volitio', from 'velle' (to wish, will).
