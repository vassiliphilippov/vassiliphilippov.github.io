outsmart

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈsmɑrt / (AmE), / aʊtˈsmɑːt / (BrE)
to defeat or gain an advantage over someone by using greater intelligence or cunning

Examples

It no longer mattered that Burton had outsmarted him.
But the farmers outsmarted Washington by shortening the distance between the rows and pouring on the fertilizer.

Synset Examples

I beat the traffic
She outfoxed her competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outwit - defeat by being more clever or resourceful
  • Synonym: outfox - defeat or trick someone by being more cunning

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (beyond, surpass) + 'smart' (clever) in the sense of intelligence or quickness of mind.
