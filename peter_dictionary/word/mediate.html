mediate

1. VERB

/ ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to try to bring about an agreement or reconciliation between two or more parties in dispute by acting as an intermediary

Examples

Within the membership of this group, as has been found true of men in other professional or trade associations, the most ready portrayal of oneself to "the public "is that of a neutral agent simply serving the interests of a seller or buyer and mediating between them.

Synset Examples

He interceded in the family dispute
He mediated a settlement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrate - to settle a dispute by acting as an arbitrator
  • Synonym: intervene - to become involved in a situation to change what is happening
  • Antonym: escalate - to increase or intensify a conflict

Etymology

From Latin mediatus, past participle of mediare (to be in the middle), from medius (middle).
