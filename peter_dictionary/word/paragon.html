paragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈparəɡən / (BrE)
a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality; a model of excellence or perfection

Examples

If such a paragon of perfection as Palmer could commit such a scoring sacrilege, there was hope left for all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplar - a typical or excellent model
  • Synonym: model - a person or thing regarded as an excellent example
  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; having faults or flaws

Etymology

From Middle French *paragon*, from Italian *paragone* (touchstone), from *paragonare* (to compare).
