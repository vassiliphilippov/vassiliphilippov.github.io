venerate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
to regard someone or something with great respect and reverence; to honor as sacred or highly esteemed

Examples

The English saints are widely venerated, quite naturally, and now there is great hope that the Forty Martyrs and Cardinal Newman will soon be canonized.

Synset Examples

Fear God as your father
We venerate genius

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revere - to feel deep respect or admiration for something or someone
  • Synonym: worship - to show reverence and adoration for a deity

Etymology

From Latin 'venerari' (to worship, revere), from 'venus' (love, desire).
