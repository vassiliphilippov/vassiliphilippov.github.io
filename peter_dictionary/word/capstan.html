capstan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.stən / (AmE), / ˈkap.stən / (BrE)
A revolving device with a vertical axis, used on ships for moving heavy weights such as anchors or sails by winding a cable or rope around it.

Examples

Michael Butt and Adame Moore were thrown off the capstan and badly injured.

Etymology

From Old French 'cabestan', from Old Provençal, from Latin 'capistrum' (halter, rope), from 'capere' (to hold).
