perturb

1. VERB

/ pərˈtɜrb / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːb / (BrE)
to disturb or worry someone; to make uneasy or anxious

Examples

"It was a nice round figure, that 12 ", he said as he headed for the clubhouse, not too much perturbed.

Synset Examples

She was rather perturbed by the news that her father was seriously ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturb - to interrupt the normal state or activity; to bother
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone tranquil; to soothe

Etymology

From Latin perturbare, from per- (through) + turbare (to disturb).
