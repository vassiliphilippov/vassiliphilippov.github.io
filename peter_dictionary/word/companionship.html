companionship

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kəmˈpænjənʃɪp / (BrE)
the pleasant feeling or state of being with someone and enjoying their company

Examples

She wanted to be with him, to give him the comfort and companionship she knew he needed.

Synset Examples

he missed their company
he enjoyed the society of his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - the condition of being with someone; social interaction
  • Antonym: isolation - the state of being alone or separated from others

Etymology

From companion + -ship, with companion from Old French compagnon, from Late Latin companio (one who eats bread with another).
