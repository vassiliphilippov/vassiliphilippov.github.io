capacitance

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpæsɪtəns / (AmE), / kəˈpæsɪtəns / (BrE)
The ability of a system, such as a capacitor, to store an electric charge, measured as the ratio of the change in electric charge to the corresponding change in electric potential.

Examples

A small pressure unbalance displaces the diaphragm and changes the capacitance between the diaphragm and an electrically insulated plate spaced 0.001 in. apart (for * * f).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacity - the maximum amount that something can contain or produce; also used in technical contexts as a synonym

Etymology

From capacity + -ance, modeled on conductance and other technical terms.
