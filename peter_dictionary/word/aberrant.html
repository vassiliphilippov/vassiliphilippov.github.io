aberrant

1. ADJ

/ əˈbɛrənt / (AmE), / əˈbɛr(ə)nt / (BrE)
markedly different from what is normal or accepted; deviating from the usual or natural type

Examples

Shakespeare does not tell us that Shylock was an aberrant individual.

Synset Examples

aberrant behavior
deviant ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviant - deviating from usual standards, especially in social or sexual behavior
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'aberrant-', from 'aberrare' (to wander away, stray).
