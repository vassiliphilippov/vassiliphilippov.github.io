craze

1. NOUN

/ kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
an enthusiasm or interest that is taken up with great passion, often for a short time; a fad

Examples

The film they did after his return was an inconsequential bit of nothing titled Out of This World, a satire on the Sinatra bobby-soxer craze.

Synset Examples

he always follows the latest fads
it was all the rage that season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fad - an intense and widely shared enthusiasm for something, especially one that is short-lived
  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • Antonym: tradition - a long-established custom or belief

Etymology

Probably from Middle English crasen (to break, shatter), later associated with 'crack-brained' due to excessive enthusiasm.
