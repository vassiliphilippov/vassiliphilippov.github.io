charter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
a formal document that creates an institution such as a company, city, or university and sets out its rights and privileges

Examples

Accordingly, though the practice violated the no trading provision of the Selkirk charter which reserved all such activity in merchandise and furs to the Hudson's Bay Company, some settlers went into trade.
But he takes his bearings from the great guidelines of policy, well established precedents, the commitments of the United States under international charters and treaties, basic statutes, and well understood notions of the American people about how we are to conduct ourselves, in policy literature such as country papers and National Security Council papers accumulated in the Department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - a written document outlining the fundamental principles or established precedents of an organization or government
  • Synonym: articles of incorporation - a legal document that establishes a corporation and its basic structure

Etymology

From Old French *charte* (document), from Latin *charta* (paper, card).

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
to hire or lease (a vehicle, such as a plane, bus, or boat) for private use

Examples

In the past, the men and women have chartered planes to Las Vegas and Jamaica, buses to Mineral Wells and Kerrville and private railway coaches to Shreveport and Galveston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to pay someone for the use of something, especially property, vehicle, or equipment
  • Synonym: lease - to grant the use of something under the terms of a lease

Etymology

From Old French *chartre*, from Latin *charta* (paper, document).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːɹtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
an arrangement to hire or lease a vehicle, aircraft, or boat for private use

Examples

Charter fishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract to rent property or equipment
  • Synonym: hire - an arrangement to pay for temporary use of something

Etymology

From Old French *chartre*, from Latin *charta* (paper, document).
