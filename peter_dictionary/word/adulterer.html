adulterer

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌltərər / (AmE), / əˈdʌltərə / (BrE)
A person who commits adultery; someone who is unfaithful to their spouse by having a sexual relationship outside of marriage.

Examples

It would be well to show the populace how we deal with adulterers ".
Before them stalked the beadle, proclaiming as he went, "Thus the Council deals with those who break its laws - adulterers, thieves, murderers, and lewd persons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulteress - a woman who commits adultery

Etymology

From Latin adulterātor, from adulterāre (to commit adultery).
