filthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪlθi / (AmE), / ˈfɪlθi / (BrE)
Extremely dirty or polluted; disgustingly unclean.

Examples

I hear the boot of Lucifer, I see his filthy face "!
And there I was shacked up with Eileen in that filthy fourth floor attic on Hudson Street.

Synset Examples

as filthy as a pigsty
a foul pond
a nasty pigsty of a room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - not clean
  • Antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from dirt

Etymology

From Old English 'fȳlþ' (filth) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪl.θi / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.θi / (BrE)
containing or characterized by obscenity; using or expressing language or content considered morally offensive or indecent.

Examples

She has a filthy mouth, this Guinea brat.

Synset Examples

had a filthy mouth
foul language
smutty jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smutty - containing obscene or indecent language or content
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards of morality and decency
  • Antonym: clean - free from obscenity or indecency (re language or jokes)

Etymology

From Old English *fylþ* (filth) + -y.
