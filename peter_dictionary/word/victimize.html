victimize

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to punish or treat someone unfairly, making them a victim, especially by unjust or harsh actions

Examples

He merely became victimized by a form of athletics that respects no one and aggravates all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persecute - to subject someone to hostility and ill-treatment, especially because of race, political or religious beliefs
  • Synonym: oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by the unjust exercise of authority

Etymology

From Latin 'victima' (sacrificial animal) + '-ize' (forming verbs).

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone a victim, especially by treating them unfairly or exploiting them

Examples

Female individuals of Psithyrus look deceptively like the workers and queens of the bumblebees they victimize.
The Providence Sunday Journal article (Jan. 29) asking whether American taxpayers are being victimized by a gigantic giveaway to pay for the care of war veterans who have non-service-connected disabilities sounds as though The Providence Journal is desperate for news.

Synset Examples

I was victimized by this con-man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to take advantage of or use unfairly for one's own advantage
  • Synonym: persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, especially because of their beliefs

Etymology

From victim + -ize, first attested in the early 19th century.
