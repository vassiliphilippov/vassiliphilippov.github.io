wildlife

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld.laɪf / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld.laɪf / (BrE)
Animals and plants living and growing in natural conditions, not tamed or cultivated by humans.

Examples

It also cools the air in summer and nourishes the trees and wildlife.
If a human figure or wild life are to be part of the projected final picture, I try to place them in the initial sketch.
Present conservation practices regard wildlife, not as an expendable natural resource, but as an annual harvest to be sown and also reaped.

Synset Examples

chemicals could kill all the wildlife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauna - the animals of a particular region, habitat, or period
  • Synonym: flora - the plants of a particular region, habitat, or period

Etymology

Formed from 'wild' + 'life', meaning life found in the wild.
