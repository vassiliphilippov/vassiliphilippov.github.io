inequality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌniːˈkwɑlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌniːˈkwɒlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being unequal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities.

Examples

It is at least as important as the more dramatic attempts to break down barriers of inequality in the South.

Synset Examples

the growing inequality between rich and poor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities

Etymology

From Old French 'inequalité', from Latin 'inaequalitas', from 'inaequalis' (unequal).
