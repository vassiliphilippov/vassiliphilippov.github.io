ethereal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈθɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪəriəl / (BrE)
Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world; heavenly or insubstantial.

Examples

Digby heaved a deep ethereal sigh.

Synset Examples

figures light and aeriform come unlooked for and melt away
aerial fancies
an airy apparition
physical rather than ethereal forms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: earthly - relating to this world rather than a spiritual or heavenly realm

Etymology

From Latin 'aetherius' (of the upper air), from Greek 'aithēr' (upper air, pure, bright).
