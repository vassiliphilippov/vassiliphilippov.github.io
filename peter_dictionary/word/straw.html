straw

1. NOUN

/ strɑ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
the dried stalks of cereal plants after the grain and chaff have been removed, used as material for weaving, animal bedding, or fodder

Examples

Do n't overlook the straws this year.
In the casual field straws feature wedge heels of cork or carved wood in a variety of styles.
At the left is a pair of dressy straw pumps in a light, but crisp texture.
The plants took zero nights in their stride, with nothing but a mat of straw over the glass to protect them.
He crossed the tracks to Delaware House, where ladies in gay dresses and men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah.

Etymology

Old English *streaw*, from Proto-Germanic *strawam* (that which is strewn).

2. ADJ

/ strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
Pale yellow in color, like straw; straw-colored.

Examples

And the cast shell of a locust, straw colored and transparent, weighing nothing, fragile but entire, with eyes like bubbles and a gaping slit down its back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blond - light yellowish-brown color, similar to straw
  • Degree Higher: golden - bright yellow or gold in color
  • Degree Higher: lemon - a more vivid yellow color

Etymology

From Old English 'streaw', meaning dried stalks of grain, referencing the typical color of straw.

3. NOUN

/ strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
The dry stalks of cereal plants after the grain and chaff have been removed, used as fodder, bedding, or for weaving.

Examples

A hay wagon moved slowly along the gutter, the top of it swept by the low boughs of the maple trees, and loose straws were left hanging tangled among the leaves.
He shook loose straw out of the action, and placed the gun in his holster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hay - grass that has been mown and dried for use as fodder

Etymology

From Old English 'streaw', related to Old High German 'stro', from Proto-Germanic '*strawam'.

4. NOUN

/ strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
A thin tube of paper or plastic used for sucking liquids into the mouth.

Examples

To my knowledge, he lived out the rest of his days drinking his food through a straw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drinking straw - another name for a straw used to suck liquids

Etymology

Originally from Old English *streaw* (stalk, straw); the drinking implement is so named from its early use of hollow plant stems.
