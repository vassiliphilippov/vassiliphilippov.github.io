roundup

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd.ʌp / (BrE)
an event or activity in which livestock such as cattle are gathered together for counting, branding, or sale

Examples

He wisely decided that it would be foolish to create a disturbance during the coming roundup, particularly since the Mexicans were on their guard.
He made their spreads his headquarters, and he helped out in their roundups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle drive - movement of cattle over long distances, typically to market

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'round up', meaning to gather together, especially livestock, in the late 19th century American West.
