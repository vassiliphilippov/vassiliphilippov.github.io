disability

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
A physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities, making them unable to do certain things as others can.

Synset Examples

reading disability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impairment - a loss or abnormality of psychological, physiological or anatomical structure or function
  • Antonym: ability - possession of the means or skill to do something

Etymology

From Old French 'deshabilite', from Latin 'dis-' (opposite of) + 'habilitas' (ability).
