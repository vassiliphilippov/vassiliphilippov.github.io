caution

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
care taken to avoid danger or mistakes; the quality of being cautious.

Examples

Also, for the present, great caution should be exercised in the choice of an experimental animal for pulmonary studies if they are to be applied to man.

Synset Examples

a man of caution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefulness - the quality of being cautious and attentive to potential danger
  • Antonym: recklessness - lack of caution; acting without considering danger or consequences
  • Synonym: heed - careful attention to something, especially advice or warnings

Etymology

From Latin caūtio (care, caution), from cavēre (to beware).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
Careful thought and attention in order to avoid danger, mistakes, or harm.

Examples

The registration figures given in Table 2 must be interpreted with caution since the estimate for eligible electors were made without the benefit of a reliable census.

Synset Examples

he exercised caution in opening the door
he handled the vase with care

Related Terms

  • Synonym: care - serious attention or consideration taken to avoid harm
  • Antonym: recklessness - lack of concern for danger or consequences; opposite of caution

Etymology

From Latin 'cautio' (care, precaution), from 'cavēre' (to beware).

3. VERB

/ ˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
to warn someone strongly, especially to take care or avoid possible danger or mistakes

Examples

He handed Harold a key to the front door, and cautioned him against leaving it unlocked while they were out of the apartment.
He cautioned that "the greater danger in this recovery may be excessive stimulation by government which could bring moderate inflation ".
The executive paid tribute to research and development and technology for their great contributions in the past, but he also cautioned industry that they tend to be great equalizers because they move at a fairly even pace within an industry and fail to give it the short-term advantage which it often needs.
A call for action "before it is too late "has alarming implications when it comes from a man who, in his previous reports on the schools, cautioned so strongly against extreme measures.
In fact, they went so far as to caution the writer that if he attempted to design a section exclusively for married students there should be, at the beginning, some "hind sight "study; but they all hastened to add that certainly less time was needed on it than presently spent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warn - to inform someone in advance of possible danger, problem, or other unpleasant situation

Etymology

From Latin 'cautio' (a taking of precautions), from 'cavere' (to beware).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
a warning or advice given to prevent someone from making a mistake or undertaking a risky action

Examples

As a word of caution, we should be aware that in actual practice no message is purely one of the four types, question, command, statement, or exclamation.
May I add a caution on this particular point?

Synset Examples

a caveat against unfair practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - a statement or event that indicates a possible or impending danger, problem, or other unpleasant situation
  • Degree Lower: recommendation - a suggestion or proposal as to the best course of action

Etymology

From Latin 'cautio' (care, wariness), from 'cavere' (to beware).
