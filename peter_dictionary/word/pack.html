pack

1. NOUN

/ pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
a set or collection of similar things considered as a group

Examples

They now lead Louisville by a full game on top of the American Association pack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set - group or collection of things considered as a unit
  • Synonym: bundle - collection of things fastened together

Etymology

From Middle English *pak*, probably from Middle Dutch *pak* (bundle, pack).

2. VERB

/ pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
to arrange or place (objects) carefully into a container for storage, transportation, or protection

Examples

It is brought to packing houses, cleaned and graded as to size and quality, and packed in protective excelsior.
He began to pack the saddlebags.
Inside, carefully packed in straw, were six eggs, but the eye of a poultry psychologist was required to detect what scientifically valuable specimentalia lay inside; to Alex they were merely six not unusual hens' eggs.
He sat for a while with his hands on his knees, watching the bend of her back as she gathered up her things - a comb, a bottle of aspirin - to take upstairs and pack.
She 'd say she did n't feel good on Sunday, could n't go to church - there 'd be a little argument, but she could be stubborn - and when the old woman had gone, quick pack the things she 'd need to take, all but the dress she 'd wear Monday, and take the bag down to that place in the station where you could put things in a locker overnight, for a dime.

Synset Examples

pack the books into the boxes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpack - to remove items from a container
  • Synonym: stuff - to fill a container or space tightly with something

Etymology

From Middle English pakken, likely of Low German or Dutch origin.

3. NOUN

/ pæk / (AmE), / pak / (BrE)
a large group or number of animals, people, or things, especially ones considered together and sharing some association

Examples

I felt superior to the neighborhood friends I was leaving behind, felt older than my years, and was full of compliments for myself as I headed into the subway that was carrying its packs of passengers out of that dull borough and into the unstable, tantalizing excitement of Manhattan.

Synset Examples

a battalion of ants
a multitude of TV antennas
a plurality of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together
  • Synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things
  • Synonym: swarm - a large group of insects or people moving together

Etymology

From Middle English 'pak', probably from Middle Dutch, meaning a bundle or group.

4. VERB

/ pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
to carry something, especially on one's back or by means of pack animals

Examples

In 1802 Henry referred to "our new carts "as being about four feet off the ground and carrying five times as much as a horse could pack.
It was payday for Highlands, and he was packing a lot of money back into the oil fields.

Synset Examples

Pack your tents to the top of the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carry - to support and move something from one place to another

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French *pac*, from Middle Dutch *pak* (bundle).

5. VERB

/ pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
to compress or press something into a tight mass or wad

Examples

Narrow four story buildings ran the length of the block like books tightly packed on a shelf.
It was only about the size of a baseball; but packed in that metallic ball there was the explosive force of 20000 tons of TNT.

Synset Examples

wad paper into the box

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compress - to press or squeeze something together to make it smaller or more compact
  • Synonym: cram - to force something into a space that is too small

6. NOUN

/ pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
A small, convenient package or parcel containing a set quantity of items sold together, such as cigarettes or film.

Examples

He could pick up another pack on his way home, if he were going home.
It only cost us a pack of smokes per man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packet - a small package or container, especially of small items

7. VERB

/ pæk / (AmE), / pak / (BrE)
to fill (a place) completely, especially with people

Examples

There were two possible methods of breaching the conservative barriers around the Rules Committee: 1) to pack it with additional liberals and break the conservative-liberal deadlock, or 2) to remove one of the conservatives - namely Mississippi's 14 - term William Meyers Colmer (pronounced Calmer).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empty - to remove contents from; to make vacant

8. VERB

/ pæk / (AmE), / pak / (BrE)
(of a leader or group) to fill a committee or body with supporters, in order to unfairly influence its decisions or outcomes.

Examples

I fought like a tigress but by the time I appealed my case to the Supreme Court (1937), Mr. Roosevelt and his "henchmen "had done their "dirty work "all too well, even going so far as to attempt to "pack "the highest tribunal in the land in order to defeat little me.

Synset Examples

9. VERB

/ pæk / (AmE), / pæk / (BrE)
to treat a part of the body by filling or wrapping it with material such as gauze, blankets, or ice, typically to protect, apply therapy, or stop bleeding

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
