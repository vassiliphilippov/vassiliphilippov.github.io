impairment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeəmənt / (BrE)
the process or result of being damaged, weakened, or made worse; the state of being impaired.

Examples

Some investigators have found a parallelism between remissions and return of the sympathetic reactivity of the hypothalamus to the normal level as indicated by the Mecholyl test and, conversely, between clinical impairment and increasing deviation of this test from the norm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs the value or function of something
  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming progressively worse
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better

Etymology

From Latin 'impair', influenced by Old French 'empeirer' (to make worse).
