extrude

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstruːd / (AmE), / ɪkˈstruːd / (BrE)
to shape or form (a material) by forcing it through an opening, often creating objects with a particular cross-section

Examples

The affixed elements of collage were extruded, as it were, and cut off from the literal pictorial surface to form a bas-relief.

Synset Examples

extrude steel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inject - to force a substance into something, especially by using a syringe or similar device

Etymology

From Latin extrudere, from ex- (out) + trudere (to push, thrust).
