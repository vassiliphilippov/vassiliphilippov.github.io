waylay

1. VERB

/ weɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / weɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
To stop someone unexpectedly, often in order to attack or detain them.

Examples

With the neutralists maintaining pressure for one of their own to succeed Mr. Hammarskjold, U Thant emerged as the only possible candidate unlikely to be waylaid by a veto.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - to attack by surprise from a hidden place
  • Synonym: accost - to approach and address someone boldly or aggressively

Etymology

From Middle English waylayen, from 'way' + 'lay'.
