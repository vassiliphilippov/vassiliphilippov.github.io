sad

1. ADJ

/ sæd / (AmE), / sæd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing unhappiness or sorrow.

Examples

He knew the faces of all, hairy or shaven, old or young, fat or thin, suffering or hardened, sad or gay, good or bad.
She made him sad some days, and he was never sure why; it was something to do with her back, the thinness of it, and the quick, jerky way she bent.
For the first time, he let himself be sad about the move.
"Of the humans we left at the equator, one has discorporated and the others are sad ".
"They should not be sad.

Synset Examples

feeling sad because his dog had died
Better by far that you should forget and smile / Than that you should remember and be sad

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment
  • Synonym: unhappy - experiencing or showing unhappiness
  • Degree Higher: depressed - in a state of unhappiness or low spirits
  • Degree Lower: disappointed - feeling let down or sad due to unmet expectations

Etymology

From Old English sæd (sated, weary; later 'sad' in sense of sorrowful), influenced by Old Norse sætr (satiated).
