grief-stricken

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡriːf ˌstrɪkən / (AmE), / ˈɡriːf ˌstrɪkən / (BrE)
Overwhelmed by deep sorrow, especially due to loss or bereavement.

Examples

Mostly the scene was crowded with mourners, such as the dramatic Dell'Arca Lamentation in Bologna, where the grief-stricken spectators had usurped Mary's last poignant moment.

Synset Examples

bereft of hope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bereaved - suffering the loss of a loved one
  • Synonym: heartbroken - experiencing intense emotional pain from loss
  • Antonym: consolable - able to be comforted after loss

Etymology

From 'grief' (deep sorrow) + 'stricken' (affected by, past participle of 'strike').
