byname

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪneɪm / (BrE)
A familiar or informal name for a person, often a shortened or affectionate version of their proper name; a nickname.

Examples

I'’m your older brother, Mike, and I was stepped over!

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - a familiar or humorous name given instead of or as well as the real name

Etymology

From Middle English 'byname', from 'by' + 'name'.
