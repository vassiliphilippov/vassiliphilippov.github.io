proteolysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtiˈɑləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtiˈɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the breakdown of proteins into peptides and amino acids by the action of enzymes that cleave peptide bonds

Examples

The thyroxine and tri-iodothyronine released by proteolysis and so escaping de-iodination presumably diffuse into the blood stream.
Similar results might be expected from the influence of drugs or pathological conditions that limit iodide trapping, or organification, or accelerate thyroglobulin proteolysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protein hydrolysis - the chemical breakdown of proteins by reaction with water

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'prōteios' (of primary importance, from 'prōtos' first) + 'lysis' (loosening, dissolution).
