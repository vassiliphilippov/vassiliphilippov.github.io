bellhop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌhɑp / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌhɒp / (BrE)
A person employed in a hotel to carry guests' luggage and run errands.

Examples

Shrugs met that, from room clerks, from bellhops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porter - person employed to carry luggage, especially in a hotel or at a railway station

Etymology

Formed from 'bell,' referring to hotel desk bells, and 'hop,' US slang for 'run quickly.'
