distraught

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔːt / (BrE)
Deeply upset and agitated, especially due to strong emotion or worry.

Examples

Then Rudy Bond was simply grand as Ben, the distraught Republican Party district chieftain.

Synset Examples

distraught with grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • Antonym: calm - not agitated or excited; composed

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'distraughten', variant of 'distract', from Latin 'distractus' (drawn apart, distracted).
