photon

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.tɒn / (BrE)
A quantum of electromagnetic radiation, regarded as a particle of light or other electromagnetic energy.

Examples

Many years later, the photon counting apparatus in the nose of the ship would determine that the star was close enough to actuate deceleration.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs', 'phōt-' meaning 'light', with the suffix -on (used in names of subatomic particles).
