cadence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈkeɪdəns / (BrE)
(Prosody) the accent or rhythmic rise and fall in a metrical foot of verse.

Examples

Not a thought nor a cadence was missed in her summary of appreciation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the rhythmic structure of verse

Etymology

From Latin 'cadentia' (a falling), from 'cadere' (to fall).
