bottomland

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəmˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəmˌland / (BrE)
Low-lying, fertile land near a river, often subject to flooding and typically with rich soil.

Examples

Uncle Randolph and Joel had replanted the bottom lands with difficulty, for more of the slaves, including Annie, had sneaked off when the soldiers broke camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodplain - flat area of land next to a river that regularly floods

Etymology

From 'bottom' (lowest part; valley) + 'land' (ground, soil).
