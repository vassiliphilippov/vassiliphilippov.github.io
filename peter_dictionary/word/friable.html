friable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
easily broken into small pieces or reduced to powder; crumbly.

Examples

The intimal surface of the aorta was covered with confluent, yellow-brown, hard, friable plaques along its entire course, and there was a marked narrowing of the orifices of the large major visceral arteries.
On the surface of the right kidney there were also 2 yellow, firm, friable raised areas measuring up to 2 cm. in diameter.

Synset Examples

friable sandstone
friable carcinomatous tissue
friable curds formed in the stomach
crumbly cookies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crumbly - easily breaking into small fragments or particles

Etymology

From Late Latin 'friabilis' (easily crumbled), from Latin 'friare' (to crumble).
