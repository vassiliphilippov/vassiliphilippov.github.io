pheasant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈfɛznt / (BrE)
A large, long-tailed bird native to Eurasia, often with brightly colored plumage, now introduced elsewhere and commonly hunted as game.

Examples

They are willing to settle, however, in anything that offers pheasants to shoot at and peasants to work at.
After Larkin has been persuaded to restock his tangled acres with pheasants, he poaches only what he needs for the nourishment of his family and local callers.

Etymology

From Middle English *fesaunt*, from Old French *fesan*, from Latin *phasianus*, from Ancient Greek *phasianos* (bird of the river Phasis, in Colchis).
