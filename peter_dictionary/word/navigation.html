navigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or activity of planning and directing the route or course of ships, aircraft, or other vehicles from one place to another.

Examples

In September 1822 two companies of infantry arrived at the mouth of the St. Peter's River, the head of navigation on the Mississippi, and began construction of Fort St. Anthony which, upon completion, was renamed in honor of its commander, Colonel Josiah Snelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilotage - the act of navigating a ship or aircraft, especially by visual reference to landmarks

Etymology

From Latin 'navigatio' (a sailing, voyage), from 'navigare' (to sail).
