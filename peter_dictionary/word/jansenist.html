Jansenist

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæn sən ɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒan sɪn ɪst / (BrE)
A follower or advocate of Jansenism, a Catholic theological movement emphasizing original sin, human depravity, and the necessity of divine grace.

Examples

Within this notion clarity is possible, but for us who are neither Greek nor Jansenist there is not such clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinist - follower of Calvinism, another Christian theological movement emphasizing predestination and grace

Etymology

From French 'janséniste', from Cornelius Jansen, Dutch theologian (1585–1638), whose doctrines inspired the movement.
