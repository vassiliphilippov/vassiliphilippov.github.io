stitch

1. NOUN

/ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A single loop, link, or knot of thread, yarn, or similar material made by hand or machine in sewing, knitting, crocheting, or embroidery.

Examples

My camp made leather wallet, bulky with twisted, raised stitches around the edges, I stuffed with money I had been saving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seam - a line where two pieces of fabric are sewn together

Etymology

From Old English *stice* (a puncture, stitch), related to *stician* (to prick, pierce).

2. VERB

/ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / stɪtʃ / (BrE)
To join or fasten two pieces of fabric or material together by passing a needle and thread through them; to sew.

Examples

Gray Eyes attacked our camp just as the first pink threads stitched together the hills and the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sew - to join with a needle and thread

Etymology

From Old English *styccean* (to prick or pierce), related to 'stick'.
