determined

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɝːmɪnd / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
having a firm intention to do something, showing strong resolve or willpower

Examples

They figured prominently in the Balafrej government of May, 1958, which the King was reportedly determined to keep in office until elections could be held.
Claire was bitterly disappointed but determined not to let the rebuff daunt her purpose.
And determined not to show it.
He was determined to hold out.
Even Hudson, experienced in Arctic sailing and determined as he was, must have had qualms as he slid down the Thames.

Synset Examples

a struggle against a determined enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • Synonym: unwavering - not changing or becoming weaker in determination or purpose
  • Antonym: hesitant - uncertain or lacking determination

Etymology

Past participle of 'determine', from Latin 'determinare' (to limit, fix, decide).
