shading

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
graded areas of light and dark in a drawing, painting, or other artwork, typically used to show depth, form, or shadow

Examples

At the same time, the wallpaper strips themselves seem to be pushed into depth by the lines and patches of shading charcoaled upon them, and by their placing in relation to the block capitals; and these capitals seem in turn to be pushed back by their placing, and by contrast with the corporeality of the woodgraining.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highlight - area of strong light in art
  • Synonym: shadow - dark area caused by obstruction of light

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a subtle difference or gradation between similar things, such as grades, tones, or types

Examples

In spite of the shading of one type of course into another, I believe it is useful to talk about vocational courses as apart from academic courses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle or slight degree of difference or distinction
  • Synonym: gradation - a minute or gradual transition from one thing to another

Etymology

From ‘shade’ + -ing, originally referring to gradations of darkness in drawing or color.
