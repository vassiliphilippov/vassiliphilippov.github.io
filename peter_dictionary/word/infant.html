infant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fənt / (BrE)
A very young child, especially one who is not yet able to walk or talk; typically under one year of age.

Examples

Most Europeans have been exercising newborn infants for centuries.)
The infant, in white terry-cloth bathrobe, her face intense and purposeful, had essayed a few wobbly steps toward her father.
It could have been anyone's infant, for it had not survived the bassinet.

Synset Examples

the baby began to cry again
she held the baby in her arms
it sounds simple, but when you have your own baby it is all so different

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby - a very young child, especially one newly or recently born

Etymology

From Latin 'infans' (unable to speak), from 'in-' (not) + 'fari' (to speak).
