clink

1. VERB

/ klɪŋk / (AmE), / klɪŋk / (BrE)
to make a high, ringing sound, especially by striking glass or metal objects together

Examples

He took his glass, clinked it against mine, and said, "Toujours gai, what the hell "! borrowing a line from Don Marquis' Mehitabel.

Synset Examples

champagne glasses clinked to make a toast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clank - to make a loud, sharp sound like pieces of metal hitting together
  • Synonym: clang - to make a resonant metallic sound
