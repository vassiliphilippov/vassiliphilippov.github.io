erode

1. VERB

/ ɪˈroʊd / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊd / (BrE)
to gradually wear away or diminish, especially by natural forces or through a process of deterioration

Examples

It is into this household, one eroded by irritations that have tortured the souls out of its people, that George Dillon enters at the beginning of the play.

Synset Examples

Her confidence eroded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrode - to destroy or weaken gradually, especially by chemical action
  • Synonym: deteriorate - to become worse or of less value
  • Antonym: build up - to increase or strengthen gradually

Etymology

From Latin erodere, from e- (out) + rodere (to gnaw).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈroʊd / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊd / (BrE)
(of wind, water, or other natural agents) gradually wear away soil, rock, or land.

Examples

His lack of success steadily eroded his interest.

Synset Examples

Rain eroded the terraces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weather - to wear away or change by exposure to the weather

Etymology

From Latin 'erodere', from 'e-' (away) + 'rodere' (to gnaw).
