bathtub

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθ.tʌb / (AmE), / ˈbɑːθ.tʌb / (BrE)
A large, fixed container in a bathroom for holding water in which a person can wash their body.

Examples

Many Shapes in Bathtubs.
"The place had no sink or washbasin, only a bathtub ", his mother discovered when she visited him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bath - the act or process of washing oneself or soaking in water; also the container for bathing
  • Antonym: shower - a device that sprays water for washing the body, typically standing up

Etymology

Formed from 'bath' + 'tub'; 'bath' from Old English 'bæth', 'tub' from Middle Dutch 'tubbe'.
