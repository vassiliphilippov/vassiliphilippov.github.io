dodge

1. NOUN

/ dɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɒdʒ / (BrE)
a quick and sudden movement to avoid someone or something

Examples

Just count each blot a dodge and add in a few for I do n't dodge every time ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidestep - a step to one side, especially to avoid an obstacle or attack

2. VERB

/ dɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɒdʒ / (BrE)
to make a sudden quick movement in order to avoid someone or something

Examples

A Reb stationed near Petersburg informed his mother: "I need not tell you that I dodge pretty often, for you can see that very plainly by the blots in this letter.
Just count each blot a dodge and add in a few for I do n't dodge every time ".
Heading for the batwings, Cobb glanced perfunctorily through the nearest window, and suddenly dodged aside.

Synset Examples

The child dodged the teacher's blow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
  • Synonym: duck - to lower the head or body quickly to avoid something
  • Antonym: confront - to face or deal with something directly

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from the Middle English 'dogge' (to follow closely), or from dialectal English.

3. VERB

/ dɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɒdʒ / (BrE)
to avoid answering, dealing with, or fulfilling (a question, issue, or responsibility), especially by being clever or evasive
