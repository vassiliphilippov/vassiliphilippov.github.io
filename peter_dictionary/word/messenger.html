messenger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛs.ɪn.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmes.ɪn.dʒə / (BrE)
a person who carries and delivers messages, letters, or information to others, especially as a job

Examples

During summers, business was so brisk that Mrs. Wilcox had two assistants and a messenger.
He would send on by trusted messenger the dispatches with their electrifying news.
The phone had been disconnected but telegrams came for him and notes by special messenger.
Suddenly, a messenger arrives and, just before collapsing dead at his feet, informs him that the Saracens have invaded Silesia, the home province of his affianced.
Your father did business with Hyman Roth, your father respected Hyman Roth, but your father never trusted Hyman Roth or his Sicilian messenger boy, Johnny Ola.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courier - a person who carries messages, packages, or mail, especially as part of a job

Etymology

From Old French messager, from Late Latin missātārius (message-bearer).
