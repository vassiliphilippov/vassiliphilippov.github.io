dismay

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmeɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈmeɪ / (BrE)
A sudden and complete loss of courage or resolution due to unexpected trouble; a feeling of shock and discouragement, especially when faced with difficulties.

Examples

He could only watch with a sort of gentle dismay while his body did these quick, appalling, and efficient things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discouragement - loss of confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: hope - a feeling of expectation or desire

Etymology

From Old French *esmaier* (to dismay), from Vulgar Latin *exmagare* (to lose power).
