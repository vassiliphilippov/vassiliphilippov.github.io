violet

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪələt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪəlɪt / (BrE)
A small, low-growing plant of the genus Viola, typically having purple, blue, or white flowers.

Examples

At State College, he had no time to walk among the violets on the water's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pansy - a type of viola with larger, showy flowers

Etymology

From Old French *violete*, diminutive of *viole*, from Latin *viola* (the violet flower).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.əlɪt / (BrE)
A color at the end of the visible spectrum between blue and ultraviolet, resembling the hue of the violet flower.

Examples

He looked out through windowpanes turned a faint violet by sun and weather, looked out at King's Bridge toward Westchester.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue
  • Degree Lower: blue - a primary color between green and violet in the spectrum

Etymology

From Old French 'violete', from Latin 'viola' (the violet flower), referring to its characteristic color.
