clannishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklænɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈklænɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The tendency of people to associate closely with a particular group, often to the exclusion of outsiders.

Examples

Those O'Dwyers had that Irish clannishness that made them stick together in spite of politics and everything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusivity - the state of being limited to a particular group
  • Antonym: inclusiveness - the quality of being open to all groups or people

Etymology

From 'clannish' + '-ness', where 'clannish' derives from 'clan', originally from Gaelic 'clann' (children, offspring, family).
