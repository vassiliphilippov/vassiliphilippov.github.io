vocalize

1. VERB

/ ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to produce sounds or utter words using the voice; to speak or sing by using the vocal cords

Examples

Earlier, this woman had been so filled with a chaotic variety of introjects that at times, when she was in her room alone, it would sound to a passerby as though there were several different persons in the room, as she would vocalize in various kinds of voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak - to utter words with the voice; to talk
  • Synonym: sing - to make musical sounds with the voice
  • Degree Lower: whisper - to speak very softly using breath rather than the vocal cords

Etymology

From Latin vocalis (vocal), from vox (voice).

2. VERB

/ ˈvoʊ.kə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to produce sounds using the voice, especially speech sounds

Examples

Come on... [VOCALIZING] [PIANO PLAYING] You!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak - to say words; to talk
  • Synonym: articulate - to pronounce words clearly and distinctly
  • Synonym: sing - to produce musical tones with the voice

Etymology

From Medieval Latin vocalizare, from Latin vocalis (vocal).
