re-explore

1. VERB

/ ˌri ɪkˈsplɔɚ / (AmE), / ˌriː ɪkˈsplɔː / (BrE)
to explore again or anew, typically in order to gain new insight or updated understanding

Examples

"Do you think we need a new Henry James to re-explore the Anglo-American scene "? he asked.

Synset Examples

We need to re-explore Colonialism

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: explore - to investigate or travel through in order to learn about or familiarize oneself with it

Etymology

Formed by prefixing re- (again) to explore.
