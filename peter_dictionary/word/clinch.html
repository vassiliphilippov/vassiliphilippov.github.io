clinch

1. VERB

/ klɪntʃ / (AmE), / klɪntʃ / (BrE)
to secure or fasten (something) by flattening or bending the ends, especially of a nail, bolt, or rivet

Examples

After the preliminary business affair was finished Depew arose and delivered the convincing speech that clinched the nomination for Roosevelt.
American history should clinch the case when Congress is asked to approve.
The Greek evidently fell for her, "Monsieur X "recounted, and to clinch what he thought was an affair in the making he gave her 100000 francs (about $ 300) and led her to the roulette tables.

Synset Examples

The girder was clinched into the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fasten - to attach or secure firmly
  • Antonym: loosen - to make less tight or secure

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English clinchen, related to Dutch 'klinken' (to rivet).

2. NOUN

/ klɪntʃ / (AmE), / klɪntʃ / (BrE)
(in boxing) the act of one boxer holding onto their opponent to avoid being hit or to rest momentarily.

Examples

Mike was of legal age and presumed able to defend himself in the clinches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapple - a close struggle or wrestle; the act of holding and struggling with someone
  • Antonym: break - the act of separating boxers in a clinch (in boxing)
