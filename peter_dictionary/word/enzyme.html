enzyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnˌzaɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛn.zaɪm / (BrE)
a substance, usually a protein produced by living cells, that acts as a catalyst to speed up specific biochemical reactions without being changed itself

Examples

Similar findings have been noted in a patient with congenital absence of the organification enzymes, whose thyroid tissue could only concentrate iodide.
Besides proteolytic enzymes the thyroid possesses de-iodinating enzymes.

Bacterial and fungal enzymes.

Etymology

From Medieval Greek *enzymos* (leavened), from Greek *en* (in) + *zyme* (leaven).
