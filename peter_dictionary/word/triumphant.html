triumphant

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈʌmfənt / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmfənt / (BrE)
Feeling or expressing great joy or pride as a result of a victory, achievement, or success.

Examples

Religion at its best is out in front, ever beckoning and leading on, and, as Lippman put it, "mobilizing all man's scattered energies in one triumphant sense of his own infinite importance ".
He wanted desperately to see Kayabashi defeated, the Communists in the village rooted out, the mission standing triumphant, for in the triumph of the Lord he himself would be triumphant, too.

Synset Examples

rejoicing crowds filled the streets on VJ Day
a triumphal success
a triumphant shout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victorious - having won a victory; triumphant
  • Antonym: defeated - having been beaten in a contest or conflict

Etymology

From Latin triumphans, triumphant-, present participle of triumphare (to triumph).

2. ADJ

/ traɪˈʌmfənt / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmfənt / (BrE)
Feeling or showing a sense of victory or accomplishment; experiencing triumph.

Examples

He wanted desperately to see Kayabashi defeated, the Communists in the village rooted out, the mission standing triumphant, for in the triumph of the Lord he himself would be triumphant, too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victorious - having won a victory; being the winner
  • Antonym: defeated - having been beaten in a contest or battle

Etymology

From Latin triumphans, triumphantis, present participle of triumphare (‘to triumph’).
