shipwreck

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌrɛk / (AmE), / ˈʃɪprɛk / (BrE)
A ship that has been destroyed or badly damaged in an accident at sea, or a part of such a ship.

Examples

An old shipwreck, a high dam, an old covered bridge, a place to find agates or other semi-precious stones or a place to pan gold, etcetera may be of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreck - the remains of something that has been badly damaged or destroyed, especially a ship

Etymology

From ship + wreck; 'wreck' from Old English 'wrecan' (to drive, expel).
