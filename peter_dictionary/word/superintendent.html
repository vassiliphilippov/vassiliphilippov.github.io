superintendent

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛndənt / (BrE)
A person who directs and manages an organization or operation, especially a school, building, or department.

Examples

Operating in 1887 was the "Valley Telegraph Line ", officers of which were E. C. Orvis, president; H. K. Fowler, vice-president and secretary; J. N. Hard, treasurer; F. H. Walker, superintendent; H. S. Walker, assistant superintendent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person who manages or controls a business or organization
  • Synonym: overseer - person who supervises others, especially workers
  • Antonym: subordinate - person under the authority of another

Etymology

From Latin superintendēns, present participle of superintendere (to oversee), from super- (over) + intendere (to direct).
