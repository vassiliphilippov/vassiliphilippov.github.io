tap

1. VERB

/ tæp / (AmE), / tæp / (BrE)
to draw upon, make use of, or access a resource, supply, or source, especially for something helpful or valuable

Examples

Or does he sincerely want to tap the real springs of American attitude and culture regardless of how unpopular and embarrassing they may be?
Adios ", I said, exhausting my Spanish vocabulary on my host and exchanging one of a scarcely tapped store of smiles with my host's daughters.
The possibility has to be ruled out that the medium's ESP may tap the memory of the sitter, and to do this, the two central characters in this drama must be separated.

Synset Examples

tap one's memory
tap a source of money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draw on - to make use of a supply or resource
  • Synonym: exploit - to make full use of a resource

2. VERB

/ tæp / (AmE), / tæp / (BrE)
to strike lightly with a quick or gentle action

Examples

He would sit, slim-waisted and spare, on the edge of Scotty's bed, his legs crossed so elaborately that the crossed foot could tap the floor.
The girl tapped the quirt impatiently against her knee and glared at him.
The children rushed off to get rid of their sweaters; and Arlene began tapping the kitchen door open.

Synset Examples

He tapped me on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knock - to strike a surface noisily to attract attention
  • Degree Higher: bang - to strike something loudly or forcefully

Etymology

Of imitative origin, reflecting the sound of a light blow.

3. NOUN

/ tæp / (AmE), / tæp / (BrE)
A light, gentle sound produced by a quick touch or knock.

Examples

"Arlene's a good girl ", my uncle remarked to us; but he said it too soon, for it came out just before the tap to which the door responded.
That tap had a slight bang ish quality.
There was a tap at the door and Oliver entered with the word that Heiser wished to see the Captain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knock - a sharp sound made by striking a surface
  • Degree Higher: bang - a sudden loud noise
  • Synonym: pat - a gentle touch or stroke

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound produced; related to Middle English 'tappen' (to strike).

4. VERB

/ tæp / (AmE), / tæp / (BrE)
to secretly listen in on telephone or telegraph conversations, especially by connecting electronic equipment to the line without the knowledge of those using it

Examples

One of the beer runners telephoned O' Banion - on a line tapped by the detective bureau - and reported the situation.

Synset Examples

The FBI was tapping the phone line of the suspected spy
Is this hotel room bugged?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiretap - to listen secretly to telephone communications by connecting a device
