transcription

1. NOUN

/ trænˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a written or printed copy of something, especially one made from dictation or from one medium to another

Examples

There has been a tendency on the part of many American linguists to assume that a phonemic transcription will automatically be the best possible orthography and that the only real problem will then be the social one of securing acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - an identical or near-identical reproduction of a document or image

Etymology

From Latin 'transcriptio', from 'transcribere' (to transcribe).
