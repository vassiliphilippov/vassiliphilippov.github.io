colonize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.lə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to settle among and establish political control over the indigenous people of an area, typically by sending settlers from a foreign country

Examples

To many a Frenchman - they came 95 years ago, colonized, and stayed until Laos became independent in 1953 - the land had been even more delightfully tranquil than Tahiti.

Synset Examples

Europeans colonized Africa in the 17th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle - to move to a new area and establish a community; to make one's home
  • Antonym: decolonize - to withdraw from a colony, leaving it independent

Etymology

From Latin 'colōnizāre', from 'colōnia' meaning colony.
