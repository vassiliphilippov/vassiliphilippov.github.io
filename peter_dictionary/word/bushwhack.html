bushwhack

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊʃˌwæk / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwæk / (BrE)
to ambush someone; to lie in wait and attack by surprise, especially from a concealed position

Examples

"Tom ", a friend asked him once, "how come you bushwhacked them rustlers?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - to attack unexpectedly from a concealed position

Etymology

Originally American English, from 'bush' (wild or uncleared country) and 'whack' (to strike), first used in the 19th century for guerrilla fighting in wooded terrain.
