lobscuse

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑbˌskus / (AmE), / ˈlɒbˌskjuːs / (BrE)
A stew made with meat, vegetables, and hardtack, traditionally eaten by sailors.

Examples

Had to put my foot on it to hole it down while I cut it up fer the lob-scuse ".
"Good ole lob-scuse ", he mumbled, and stirred the pot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobscouse - another spelling for the same sailors' stew

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'lobscouse', itself of uncertain origin.
