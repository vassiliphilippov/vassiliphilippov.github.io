operationally

1. ADV

/ ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌɒp.ərˈeɪ.ʃən.əli / (BrE)
In relation to the way something works or is put into operation.

Examples

I would propose, first, an abandonment of attempts at a universal lexical list, as intrinsically unachievable, and operationally inadequate in proportion as it is achieved.

Synset Examples

reported the machine operationally satisfactory
a well-trained staff that is operationally adequate

Etymology

From 'operational' + '-ly', where 'operational' derives from 'operation' (from Latin 'operatio', meaning 'work, effort, operation').
