3. ADJ

/ kloʊzd / (AmE), / kləʊzd / (BrE)
(of the eyes, mouth, etc.) shut; not open.

Examples

Jesus' inert body would be passive, his eyes closed.
When painting, Mason's physical eyes are half closed, while his mind's eye is wide open, and this circumstance accounts in part for the impression he wishes to convey.
Killpath peered through half closed lids at his reflection, thrust up his chin in a gesture of satisfaction and about-faced.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - (of the eyes, mouth, etc.) not shut; allowing passage or view
  • Synonym: shut - (of the eyes, mouth, etc.) firmly closed or made not open

Etymology

Past participle of 'close', from Middle English 'closen', from Old French 'clos', from Latin 'clausus' (shut, closed).
