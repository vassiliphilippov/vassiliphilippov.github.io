oblivion

1. NOUN

/ əˈblɪvi.ən / (AmE), / əˈblɪvi.ən / (BrE)
The state of being forgotten or ignored; a condition in which something or someone is no longer remembered or recognized.

Examples

Procreation, expansion, proliferation - these are the laws of living things, with the penalty for not obeying them the ultimate in punishments: oblivion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgetfulness - the state of being unable to remember
  • Antonym: remembrance - the act of remembering or honoring

Etymology

From Latin 'oblīvīo', meaning 'forgetfulness'.
