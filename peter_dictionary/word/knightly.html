knightly

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtli / (BrE)
Relating to the qualities, time, or traditions of knights or chivalry, especially during the Middle Ages.

Examples

The sentimental pure heart of Galahad is gone with the knightly years, but I still believe in the heart of the George Meredith character that was not made of the stuff that breaks ".

Synset Examples

chivalric rites
the knightly years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalric - relating to chivalry or the qualities of knights

Etymology

From Middle English knyghtly, from knyght (knight) + -ly (adjectival suffix).
