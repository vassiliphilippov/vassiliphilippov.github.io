literalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.ɚ.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
The doctrine or practice of portraying subjects in art or literature in a strictly realistic or literal manner, without embellishment or interpretation.

Examples

Mimesis here is not to be confused with literalism or realism in the conventional sense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - the practice of representing things as they are; artistic or literary fidelity to actual life
  • Antonym: abstractionism - the practice of representing things in a non-literal or non-realistic way in art or literature

Etymology

From 'literal' + '-ism', indicating a doctrine or practice.
