2. VERB

/ ˈʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌtə / (BrE)
to make a sound with one's voice, especially not involving words; to express audibly

Examples

The man uttered a weird cry, spun about, and collapsed in the sand.
He was uttering threats in a low but savage voice when they closed and padlocked the door.

Synset Examples

She let out a big heavy sigh
He uttered strange sounds that nobody could understand

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: speak - to say words; to use spoken language
  • Degree Lower: murmur - to say something in a soft, quiet voice

Etymology

From Middle English *uttren*, from Old English *ūtera* (outer), sense extended to 'outward expression'.
