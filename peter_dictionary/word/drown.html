drown

1. VERB

/ draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
(of a sound, feeling, etc.) to completely cover or obscure something so that it cannot be seen, heard, or felt; to overwhelm or make imperceptible.

Examples

He opened it a crack and in doing so made as much shuffling, coughing, and scraping noise as possible in order to drown emanations from the hen who had begun to protest.
At once my ears were drowned by a flow of what I took to be Spanish, but - the driver's white teeth flashing at me, the road wildly veering beyond his glistening hair, beyond his gesticulating bottle - it could have been the purest Oxford English I was half hearing; I would n't have known the difference.
Yes, Blanche was very, very sweet - being in her company was like being drowned in warm, melted marshmallows.

Synset Examples

I was drowned in work
The noise drowned out her speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overwhelm - to overpower or completely cover
  • Synonym: submerge - to go under and cover completely, especially with water
  • Antonym: expose - to make visible or leave uncovered

Etymology

From Old English 'druncnian' (to be drowned), related to 'drink'.

2. VERB

/ draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
to kill (a person or animal) by holding them under water or another liquid so that they cannot breathe

Examples

The seemingly quiet ocean had crashed a wall of water from 10 to 100 feet high upon beaches crowded with bathers, drowning thousands of them and flattening villages along the shore.

Synset Examples

He drowned the kittens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocate - to kill by preventing breathing
  • Antonym: rescue - to save someone from drowning

Etymology

From Old English druncnian (to be swallowed up by water), influenced by Old Norse drukna (to drown).

3. VERB

/ draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
to die through submersion in and inhalation of water; to die by suffocating in water or another liquid

Examples

The PM might show he drowned instead, but that's what the once-over-lightly gives us.
The horse and men were saved, but the oxen drowned.

Synset Examples

The child drowned in the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air or inability to breathe

Etymology

From Old English druncnian ('to be drowned'), partly from Proto-Germanic *druknaz ('to sink') and related to 'drink'.

4. VERB

/ draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
To try to get rid of or forget something (such as sorrow or trouble), especially by immersing yourself in an activity, usually drinking alcohol.

Examples

Pleasure, fame and fortune, drowning your troubles with a drink, and "living it up "with the gang are like candy bars when you 're hungry: they may ease your hunger temporarily, but they 'll never take the place of a satisfying, mouth-watering steak.
I finally went downstairs to the bar off the main lobby where most of the cast were drowning their sorrows over the untimely passing of Ask Tony.

Synset Examples

She drowned her trouble in alcohol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drown one's sorrows - try to forget one's problems by drinking alcohol
