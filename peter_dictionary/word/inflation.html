inflation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
a general and continuous increase in the prices of goods and services across an economy, leading to a decrease in the purchasing power of money

Examples

The entry limiting price will also be raised for potential domestic competition, but unless general inflation permits profit margins to increase proportionately throughout the economy, we might expect the public limit price to approach the entry limit price.

Synset Examples

in inflation everything gets more valuable except money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deflation - a general decline in prices, opposite of inflation
  • Degree Higher: hyperinflation - extremely rapid or out of control inflation

Etymology

From Latin 'inflare' (to blow into, inflate), via French 'inflation'. First used in economics in the mid-19th century to refer to an increase in currency supply leading to price rises.
