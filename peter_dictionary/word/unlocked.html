unlocked

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɑkt / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɒkt / (BrE)
(of a door, window, etc.) not fastened or secured with a lock; able to be opened without a key.

Examples

He handed Harold a key to the front door, and cautioned him against leaving it unlocked while they were out of the apartment.
"Through a door conveniently unlocked ", Madden supplemented.

Synset Examples

an unbarred door
went through the unlatched gate into the street
an unlocked room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: locked - fastened with a lock; secured

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'locked' (secure with a lock).
