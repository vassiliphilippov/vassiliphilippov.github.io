sorrel

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔrəl, ˈsɑrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
of a light brownish or reddish-brown color, especially used to describe hair or the coat of a horse

Examples

He was seeing, somehow, the face of a young boy, the boy Simms Purdew must once have been, a boy with sorrel hair, and blue eyes dancing with gaiety, and the boy mouth grinning trustfully among the freckles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - reddish-brown color, often used for horse coats

Etymology

From Old French *sorel*, diminutive of *sor* (yellowish-brown), ultimately of Germanic origin.
