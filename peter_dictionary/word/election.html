election

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a formal process in which people vote to choose a person or group for a political or official position

Examples

The jury further said in term end presentments that the City Executive Committee, which had over-all charge of the election, "deserves the praise and thanks of the City of Atlanta "for the manner in which the election was conducted.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poll - a process of voting in an election
  • Synonym: referendum - a general vote by the electorate on a single political question

Etymology

From Latin 'electionem' (a choosing out), from 'eligere' (to choose).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally choosing or selecting someone or something, especially for a position, honor, or membership

Examples

He worked in oil for years before beginning his work in watercolor, and his first public recognition and early honors, including his election to the Academy, were for his essays in the heavier medium.

Synset Examples

her election of medicine as a profession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selection - the act of choosing something or someone from a group

Etymology

From Latin electio (a choice, selection), from eligere (to select).
