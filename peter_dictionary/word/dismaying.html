dismaying

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈmeɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈmeɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a feeling of shock, worry, or disappointment

Examples

He seemed to remember reading somewhere that Abyssinians had large litters, and suffered a dismaying vision of the apartment overrun with a dozen kittens.

Synset Examples

appalling conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarming - causing worry or fear
  • Antonym: comforting - causing a feeling of comfort or reassurance

Etymology

From the verb 'dismay', ultimately from Old French 'esmaier' (to frighten), from Vulgar Latin *exmagare.
