singular

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
Very unusual or remarkable; exceptionally good or noteworthy.

Examples

This is our singular goal.

Synset Examples

a remarkable sight
such poise is singular in one so young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remarkable - Unusual or worthy of attention or notice
  • Antonym: ordinary - Normal, not unusual or special

Etymology

From Latin singularis (alone of its kind; extraordinary), from singulus (single).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
denoting a single, individual person or thing; separate or one in number.

Examples

At the Hotel Dumont there had, at the time in issue, been twenty-three overnighters, counting couples as singular.

Synset Examples

can the singular person be understood apart from his culture?
every fact in the world might be singular...unlike any other fact and sole of its kind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individual - single, distinct person or thing
  • Antonym: plural - referring to more than one person or thing

Etymology

From Latin singularis (alone, single), from singulus (one, single).
