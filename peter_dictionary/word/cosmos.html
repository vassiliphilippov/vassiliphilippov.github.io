cosmos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑzmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɒs / (BrE)
the universe seen as a well-ordered and harmonious system; all existing matter and space considered as a whole

Examples

The achievements which dispelled our fears of the cosmos took place three centuries ago.

Synset Examples

they study the evolution of the universe
the biggest tree in existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universe - all of space and everything in it; the totality of existence
  • Antonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion, especially as the antithesis of the ordered cosmos

Etymology

From Greek 'kosmos' (order, world, universe).
