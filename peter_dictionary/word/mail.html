mail

1. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
a vehicle, formerly a coach or train, that transports letters and packages sent by the postal system

Examples

On Thursday evening we may go out of town together by some stage or mail about the distance of ten or twelve miles.

Etymology

From Middle English *male* (traveling bag), later referring to bag of letters and its conveyance.

2. VERB

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
to send a letter, package, or other item using the postal service

Examples

She signed the letters quickly, stamped them, and placed them on the hall table for Raphael to mail in town.
The check had been mailed from Chicago, the envelope bore no return address, and the check was not signed.
The Commission is authorized and directed to mail to each claimant in proceedings before the Commission notice of the provisions of this subsection.
If you mail a return or tax payment, you must place it in the mails in ample time to reach the district director on or before the due date.
If you file Form 1040 A and the District Director computes your tax, any refund to which you are entitled will be mailed to you.

Synset Examples

I'll mail you the check tomorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - send (a letter or package) by mail, chiefly British usage

Etymology

From Middle English 'mail(e)' (letter bag), from Old French 'male' (wallet, bag), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
letters and packages sent or delivered by the postal service collectively; the system or the items carried.

Examples

I lived to see an envelope of hers in the morning mail and to lock myself in my room in the afternoon to reread her letter for the tenth time and finally prepare an answer.
And most of all it is not having the only man you could love, whether he drives a bread truck or delivers the mail or checks the berry crates down at the sheds, or owns seventeen oil wells and six diamond mines, for if you are anybody what he is or does makes no difference if he is the one.
Not long after the Warden deprived us of his company, I got a postcard in the mail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - letters and packages sent or delivered by the postal system

Etymology

From Middle English male (bag, pouch), from Old French male (bag), of Germanic origin.

4. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
The system for sending letters and parcels from one place to another, typically operated by a public postal service.

Examples

Postmaster General Burleson set about to protect the American people against radical propaganda that might be spread through the mails.
If you mail a return or tax payment, you must place it in the mails in ample time to reach the district director on or before the due date.

Synset Examples

the mail handles billions of items every day
he works for the United States mail service
in England they call mail `the post'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - the mail system in British English

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman 'male' (bag), later referring to letters carried in bags.

5. NOUN

/ meɪl / (AmE), / meɪl / (BrE)
A collection of letters and packages that are delivered together, especially to a particular person or address.

Examples

Would you add this to your outgoing mail?

Synset Examples

your mail is on the table
is there any post for me?
she was opening her post

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - letters and parcels sent or delivered

Etymology

From Middle English *male* (traveling bag, wallet), from Old French *male* (bag, pack), of Germanic origin.
