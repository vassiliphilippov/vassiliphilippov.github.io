hyperfine

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪ.pɚˌfaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.pəˌfaɪn / (BrE)
Extremely fine or minute; in physics and chemistry, denoting minute structure or splitting of spectral lines due to interactions such as those between nuclear spins and electrons.

Examples

An isotropic resonance shift of f to lower applied fields indicates a weak isotropic hyperfine** contact interaction.

Synset Examples

hyperfine structure

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fine - of very high quality or very thin

Etymology

From hyper- (meaning ‘over, beyond’) + fine.
