imbibe

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbaɪb / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaɪb / (BrE)
to absorb or take in, especially liquids, or to assimilate ideas or knowledge

Examples

Micelles can imbibe and hold a considerable amount of oleophilic substances so that the micelle volume may be increased as much as approximately two-fold.

Synset Examples

The sponge absorbs water well
She drew strength from the minister's words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorb - to take in or soak up (a liquid, energy, or other substance) by chemical or physical action

Etymology

From Latin 'imbibere' (to drink in), from 'in-' (in) + 'bibere' (to drink).
