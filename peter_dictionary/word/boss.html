boss

1. NOUN

/ bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
a person who is in charge of a worker or organization; someone who exercises control or authority over others

Examples

Let's throw a little nuclear hardware at them and show them who's boss ".
Show them who's boss "!
The Boss wants performance, not gripes.
That was the day that he had practically mopped up the main street of Big Sands with Aaron McBride, field boss for the Highlands Oil + Gas Company.
Now, Mis-ter McBride ", said Lord, and he laid a firmly restraining hand on the field boss's arm.

Synset Examples

if you want to leave early you have to ask the foreman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervisor - person overseeing workers or activity
  • Antonym: employee - someone who works for a boss
  • Antonym: subordinate - person below another in rank or authority

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'baas' meaning 'master' or 'overseer'.

2. NOUN

/ bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
A person who is in charge of a worker or organization, especially one with the authority to hire and manage employees.

Examples

It all seemed - if one could have peeked in at him through one of his windows - as though this broken nosed man with the muscular arms and wrestler's neck was merely the caretaker trying his hand at the boss's work.
He evolved programs that plumbed new depths of bathos and besmirched whole networks, and quickly superseded his boss.
the failure of the boss's wife and son to join the outfit.
The hired man ran over to help his boss.

Synset Examples

the boss hired three more men for the new job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - person in charge of part of an organization
  • Synonym: supervisor - person overseeing and directing others
  • Antonym: subordinate - person under the authority of a boss or manager

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *baas* (master, overseer).

3. NOUN

/ bɑs / (AmE), / bɒs / (BrE)
A person who is in charge of a worker, group, or organization and has the authority to give orders and make decisions.

Examples

But there is little doubt that in many marriages the wife is boss of the marital bed.
Are you driving yourself, Boss?

Synset Examples

he is his own boss now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • Synonym: supervisor - a person who supervises others, especially workers; overseer
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority or control of another within an organization

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'baas' meaning 'master' or 'foreman'.
