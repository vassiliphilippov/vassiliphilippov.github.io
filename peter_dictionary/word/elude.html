elude

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlud / (AmE), / ɪˈluːd / (BrE)
to escape from or avoid (someone or something), especially by being quick, clever, or tricky; to fail to be attained or understood by someone

Examples

Billy Gardner's line double, which just eluded the diving Minnie Minoso in left field, drove in Jim Lemon with the winning run with two out in the last of the ninth to give the Minnesota Twins a 6 - 5 victory over the Chicago White Sox Monday.
They succeeded in eluding the curious at the hotel, but there was no chance of avoiding them at the nightclub.

Synset Examples

The thief eluded the police
This difficult idea seems to evade her
The event evades explanation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evade - to escape or avoid, especially in a clever or tricky way
  • Antonym: capture - to catch or hold something that is trying to escape

Etymology

From Latin 'eludere' meaning 'to escape from or parry', from 'e-' (out of) + 'ludere' (to play').

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlud / (AmE), / ɪˈluːd / (BrE)
(of an idea or fact) to fail to be understood, remembered, or achieved by someone; to escape someone's grasp or comprehension.

Examples

What you were looking for (unless you make a hobby of collecting old tennis rackets and fly screens) eludes me, but to judge from phonograph records scattered about a fumed oak Victrola, you danced two tangos and a paso doble, which must have been fairly enervating in that milieu.

Synset Examples

What you are seeing in him eludes me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evade - avoid or escape by cleverness or trickery
  • Antonym: understand - to grasp the meaning, significance, or nature of something

Etymology

From Latin *eludere* (to escape, evade, mock).
