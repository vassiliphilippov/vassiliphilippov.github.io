Greenland

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡriːnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd / (BrE)
the world's largest island, situated between the North Atlantic and Arctic Oceans, an autonomous territory within the Kingdom of Denmark

Examples

Hal saw in his mind the rest of Earth: Iceland, Greenland, the Caribbean Islands, and the eastern half of South America.
Early in June, the Discovery passed "Desolation " (southern Greenland) and in mid-June entered the "Furious Overfall ".

Etymology

From Old English Grēnland, meaning 'green land', reportedly so named by Norse settlers.
