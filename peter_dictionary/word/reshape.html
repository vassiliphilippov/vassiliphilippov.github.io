reshape

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈʃeɪp / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃeɪp / (BrE)
to shape or form something again or in a different way

Examples

The displacement (at least to a considerable extent) of the ethical jurisprudence of the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries by positivism reshaped both international law theory and doctrine.

Synset Examples

The new foreign minister reshaped the foreign policy of his country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remodel - to change the structure or form of something
  • Synonym: alter - to change or make different

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + shape.
