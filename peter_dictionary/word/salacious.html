salacious

1. ADJ

/ səˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of writing, remarks, pictures, etc.) containing or conveying undue or inappropriate interest in sexual matters.

Examples

But if Joyce got involved in murder or salacious scandal, the role would probably go to the sponsor's wife, Mrs. Oatnut Grits.

Synset Examples

lewd whisperings of a dirty old man
an indecent gesture
obscene telephone calls
salacious limericks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lewd - crude and offensive in a sexual way
  • Synonym: indecent - not conforming with accepted standards of morality; improper
  • Synonym: obscene - offensive or disgusting by accepted standards, especially sexual matters

Etymology

From Latin *salax* (lustful) via Latin *salacis* (of leaping), related to *salire* (to leap).

2. ADJ

/ səˈleɪ.ʃəs / (AmE), / səˈleɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
Having or conveying undue or inappropriate interest in sexual matters; lustful or lecherous.

Examples

We hold safe little jobs illustrating tooth-paste ads or the salacious incidents in trivial novels, and most of our easel painting is nothing but picking the fluff out of the navel so it can be contemplated in greater purity.

Synset Examples

eluding the lubricious embraces of her employer
her sensuous grace roused his lustful nature
prurient literature
prurient thoughts
a salacious rooster of a little man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prurient - having or encouraging an excessive interest in sexual matters
  • Synonym: lecherous - showing excessive or offensive sexual desire

Etymology

From Latin 'salax', 'salacis' meaning 'lustful'.
