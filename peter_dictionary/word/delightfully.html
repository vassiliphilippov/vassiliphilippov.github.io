delightfully

1. ADV

/ dɪˈlaɪtfəli / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪtfəli / (BrE)
In a way that gives great pleasure or enjoyment; in a delightful manner.

Examples

To many a Frenchman - they came 95 years ago, colonized, and stayed until Laos became independent in 1953 - the land had been even more delightfully tranquil than Tahiti.
They comprise a great variety of scene and interest: grim episodes of war, idyllic interludes, superb canvases of world-shaking events, and delightfully humorous sketches of odd characters.

Synset Examples

the farm house, though in itself a small one, is delightfully situated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasantly - in a way that is enjoyable or pleasing
  • Antonym: disagreeably - in a manner that is unpleasant or displeasing

Etymology

From 'delightful' + '-ly', with 'delightful' from Middle English, ultimately from Latin 'delectare' (to please).
