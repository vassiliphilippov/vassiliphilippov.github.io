suspect

1. VERB

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to believe or imagine that something is likely to be true or the case, often without certain proof

Examples

There is a break in continuity just before the fourth variation in the "Forellen "movement, and I suspect that this is due to imperfect splicing between sides of the original SPs.
She 'd be smart about it, get him to give it to her in little bills so's nobody would suspect - maybe could n't get it until Monday account of that, the banks - But that was n't really long to wait.
Requests for substantiation, the Service indicated, can be especially expected in cases where it suspects the donor received some material benefit in return, such as tickets to a show.
There are times when one suspects that the songs that are dropped from musical shows before they reach Broadway may really be better than many of those that are left in.

Synset Examples

I suspect he is a fugitive
I surmised that the butler did it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - to form an opinion or answer about something without sufficient evidence
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about; to suspect that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin suspectare (to mistrust), frequentative of suspicere (to look up to, mistrust).

2. ADJ

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
Regarded with suspicion or distrust; not as expected or possibly not genuine.

Examples

That this abandonment takes place on a stage, during an' artistic' performance, is enough to associate Jacoby with art, and to bring down upon him the punishment for art; that is, he is suspect, guilty, punishable, as is anyone in Mann's stories who produces illusion, and this is true even though the constant elements of the artist nature, technique, magic, guilt and suffering, are divided in this story between Jacoby and La^utner.
Since f is ferromagnetic, we felt that any results obtained from the magnetically contaminated f would be **suspect**.

Synset Examples

there was something fishy about the accident
up to some funny business
some definitely queer goings-on
a shady deal
her motives were suspect
suspicious behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspicious - having or showing distrust or doubt
  • Antonym: trustworthy - deserving of trust or confidence

Etymology

From Old French suspec, from Latin suspectus, past participle of suspicere (to mistrust, look up at).

3. VERB

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to regard someone or something as untrustworthy or likely to be guilty; to have doubts about the honesty or reliability of someone or something

Examples

He suspected something underhanded and furtive about him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trust - to have confidence in or believe in someone or something
  • Synonym: distrust - to have no trust or confidence in someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'suspectare', frequentative of 'suspicere' (to look up at, mistrust).

4. VERB

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to believe that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing without certain proof

Examples

She wanted the police to notice him, suspect him.
Commissioner Gordon informs me that he has targeted those businesses... ... suspected of fronting for the syndicate in this city.

Synset Examples

The U.S. suspected Bin Laden as the mastermind behind the terrorist attacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distrust - to doubt the honesty or reliability of someone or something
  • Antonym: trust - to believe that someone is honest or reliable

Etymology

From Latin suspectare, frequentative of suspicere 'to mistrust'

5. NOUN

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
a person thought to be guilty of a crime or offense

Examples

Once again, Tom Horn was the first and most likely suspect, and he was brought in for questioning immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culprit - a person responsible for a crime or misdeed
  • Synonym: accused - a person charged with a crime

Etymology

From Latin suspectus, past participle of suspicere (to look up to, suspect).
