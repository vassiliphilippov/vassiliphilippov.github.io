torpid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔːrpɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːpɪd / (BrE)
Mentally or physically inactive; sluggish or apathetic.

Examples

Or was he now taking the role - the gesture and the suffering - because it was the only way to affirm his history and identity in the torpid, befogged loneliness of this land.

Synset Examples

she was fat and inert
a sluggish worker
a mind grown torpid in old age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluggish - slow-moving or inactive
  • Synonym: lethargic - lacking energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

From Latin 'torpidus' (numb, sluggish), from 'torpere' (to be numb or inactive).
