stethoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛθ.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈstɛθ.ə.skəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument used by doctors and healthcare professionals for listening to sounds generated within the body, such as the heart and lungs.

Examples

His stethoscope was on the table by Scotty's bed and he picked it up and wagged it at Scotty.
The stethoscope glinted silver in the darkening room.

Etymology

From Greek *stēthos* (chest) and *-scope* (an instrument for viewing).
