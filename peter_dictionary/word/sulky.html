sulky

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlki / (AmE), / ˈsʌlki / (BrE)
a light, two-wheeled horse-drawn vehicle for one person, typically used in harness racing or for personal transport

Examples

Now, driving the horse and sulky borrowed from Mynheer Schuyler, he felt as if every bone was topped by burning oil and that every muscle was ready to dissolve into jelly and leave his big body helpless and unable to move.
He slapped the reins on the back of the powerful gray horse and held on as the sulky's wheels hit a pothole and came out with a jolt and went on.

Etymology

From 'sulk' plus the suffix '-y', possibly referring to the single, solitary seat designed for one person.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlki / (AmE), / ˈsʌlki / (BrE)
Sullen, moody, and unwilling to be cheerful or cooperative.

Examples

They had suffered, in sulky silence, the sight of his sharp practice in Cicero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullen - gloomy, silently resentful or depressed
  • Synonym: moody - often changing mood, especially to being sullen or irritable
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: amiable - friendly and pleasant

Etymology

Probably from English dialect sulk (to be silent, ill-humored) + -y.
