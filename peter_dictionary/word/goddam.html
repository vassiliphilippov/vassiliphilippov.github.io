goddam

1. ADJ

/ ɡɑdˈdæm / (AmE), / ɡɒdˈdæm / (BrE)
(informal, vulgar) Used to emphasize annoyance, anger, or surprise, often before a noun, as an intensifier.

Examples

"Nothing's free in the whole goddam world ", was all I could think of to say.
It was more a matter of tact, and also it was none of Killpath's goddam business.
I would have been negligent and a goddam lousy cop to boot, if I 'd sat around this station all night when somebody got away with murder in my district.

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot

not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - (informal, vulgar) Intensifier expressing annoyance or anger
  • Synonym: goddamned - (informal, vulgar) Intensifier expressing annoyance or anger

Etymology

Contraction of 'God damn', used as an expletive intensifier.
