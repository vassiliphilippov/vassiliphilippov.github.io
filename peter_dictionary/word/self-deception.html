self-deception

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf dɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of deceiving oneself by refusing to accept the truth or by believing comforting falsehoods.

Examples

In America, such self-deception has served a particularly useful purpose.
He hoped he was free of self-deception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or opinion held despite evidence
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or free from deceit

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'deception.'
