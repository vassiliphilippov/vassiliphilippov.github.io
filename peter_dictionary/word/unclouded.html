unclouded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
Free from anything that dulls, dims, or obscures, especially of sound or color; clear and pure.

Examples

The timbre remains that of the instruments unclouded by resonance.

Synset Examples

efforts to obtain a clean bass in orchestral recordings
clear laughter like a waterfall
clear reds and blues
a light lilting voice like a silver bell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - free from cloudiness, obstruction, or impurity; easy to perceive
  • Antonym: clouded - dimmed, dulled, or made less clear

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'clouded' (obscured, dimmed).
