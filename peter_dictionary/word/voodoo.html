voodoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈvuː.du / (AmE), / ˈvuː.duː / (BrE)
A charm or object believed, especially superstitiously, to embody magical powers and bring about particular effects.

Examples

Our collective policies, group and national, are similarly based on voodoo, but here we often lack even the empirically successful rituals and are still engaged in determining them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amulet - an ornament or small piece of jewelry thought to give protection against evil, danger, or disease
  • Synonym: talisman - an object, typically an inscribed ring or stone, thought to have magic powers and bring good luck

Etymology

From Louisiana Creole French 'voudou', from West African (Ewe/Fon) 'vodun' (spirit, deity).
