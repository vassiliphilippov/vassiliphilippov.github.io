untruth

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtruθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruːθ / (BrE)
A statement that is not true; a lie or falsehood.

Examples

And though it was logical that a man who could plot mass murder would not hesitate to speak an untruth, still it was difficult to understand why Spencer spoke only for Cromwell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - a deliberately false statement
  • Antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with reality

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'truth'.
