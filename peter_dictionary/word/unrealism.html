unrealism

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈriː.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The quality or practice of representing things in a manner that does not refer to concrete objects or specific examples, especially in art or literature.

Examples

It is the abstractionism, the unrealism, of the pure idealist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realism - the attitude or practice of accepting a situation as it is and dealing with it accordingly; representation of things as they actually are
  • Synonym: abstractionism - the practice of abstract art or thought; representing ideas rather than concrete things

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'realism'.
