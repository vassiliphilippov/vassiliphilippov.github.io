tall

1. ADJ

/ tɔl / (AmE), / tɔːl / (BrE)
Of greater than average height, especially in relation to others of the same kind; high in stature or vertical extent.

Examples

Now when Henri was just 12 he was only 4' 10 "tall and weighed an astounding 72 pounds, and his greatest desire was to pack on some weight.
These were heroes nine feet tall to him ".
The Dutch Reformed Church, with two steeples and its own school was on Main Street; the Episcopal Church was one block down Sussex Street; the Catholic Saint Mary's Church, with an even taller steeple and a cross on top, stood on Ball Street.
A tall lady, with a ruffled collar very low on her bosom, turned insolent green eyes upon him.
She was taller than Aggie.

Synset Examples

tall people
tall buildings
tall trees
tall ships

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short - of less than average height
  • Synonym: high - of great vertical extent or distance from base to top
  • Degree Higher: gigantic - extremely large and tall
  • Degree Lower: short - of less than average height

Etymology

Old English 'getæl' (quick, prompt) and Middle English forms; current meaning from Middle English 'tal' (noble, excellent, later 'high' or 'lofty').
