shucks

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌks / (AmE), / ʃʌks / (BrE)
(informal) Something of little or no value.

Examples

He is not worth shucks.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - something of no value or worth
  • Synonym: red cent - a very small amount of money; something of little value

Etymology

Originally American English, possibly from the literal meaning of 'shuck', meaning the husk of corn or shell of an oyster.

2. NOUN

/ ʃʌks / (AmE), / ʃʌks / (BrE)
Used to express mild disappointment, embarrassment, or annoyance.

Examples

KAY: Oh, no...

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darn - used as a mild expletive to express annoyance or disappointment
  • Synonym: shoot - an exclamation used to express mild annoyance or surprise
  • Synonym: drat - used to express mild irritation or frustration
