humming

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making a musical sound with closed lips, producing a continuous tune or note

Examples

Two hours of watching his serenely happy face, listening to his soft humming as he bent over his penciled figures, and Blanche had to leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - to make a low, continuous musical sound with closed lips

Etymology

From the verb 'hum', which imitates the sound produced.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
A low, continuous sound, like that made by bees or distant traffic.

Examples

Over the rattling of fenders, humming of tires and chattering of gears there was a charming melody of whispers and tiny giggles.

Synset Examples

the hum of distant traffic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hum - a low, steady, continuous sound
