Related Terms

  • Synonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort done to achieve a result
  • Synonym: employment - the state of having paid work
  • Antonym: leisure - free time when not working

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə / (BrE)
The social class consisting of people who do manual work or work for wages, especially as opposed to those who own businesses or hire workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working class - social group consisting of wage earners in manual or industrial occupations
  • Antonym: capital - the class owning or controlling capital or means of production, contrasted with 'labor'

Etymology

From Latin labor (toil, work).

3. VERB

/ ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
to strive and make a great effort to achieve something, especially through hard or sustained work

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly
  • Synonym: strive - to make great efforts to achieve or obtain something
  • Antonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax or recover strength

Etymology

From Latin 'laborare' (to work), from 'labor' (toil, work).
