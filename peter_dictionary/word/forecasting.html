forecasting

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making statements about what is likely to happen in the future, especially using analysis or data

Examples

Boys 32, 23, 31, 17, 30, 19, and 24 had "same schedule "at Onset and Completion; thus early forecasting of the pubescent stage would appear possible for only seven boys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prediction - the act of saying what will happen in the future
  • Synonym: projection - an estimate or forecast of a future situation

Etymology

From 'forecast' (to predict), originating from Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'cast' (to throw, suppose).
