myocardium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈkɑr.di.əm / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈkɑː.di.əm / (BrE)
the thick, muscular middle layer of the wall of the heart, responsible for its contractions

Examples

The myocardium of the posterior base of the left ventricle was replaced by gray scar tissue over a 7.5 cm. area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: endocardium - the thin inner lining of the heart chambers
  • Degree Higher: epicardium - the outermost layer of the heart wall

Etymology

From Greek myo- (muscle) + kardia (heart) + -ium (neuter noun ending).
