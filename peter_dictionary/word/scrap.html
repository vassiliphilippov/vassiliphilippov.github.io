scrap

1. VERB

/ skræp / (AmE), / skræp / (BrE)
to get rid of (something no longer needed or wanted); to discard or dispose of as useless or obsolete

Examples

It seemed to me that the liberals had scrapped the balanced polarity and reposed both liberty and the fundamental law in the common man.

Synset Examples

trash these old chairs
junk an old car
scrap your old computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of something
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or continue to have possession of something

Etymology

Probably a back-formation from 'scraps' (small pieces left over), ultimately from Old Norse 'skrap' (scraps).

2. NOUN

/ skræp / (AmE), / skræp / (BrE)
a small piece or fragment of something, especially something left over or discarded after the main part has been used or removed

Examples

Mr. Mills had done some figuring on a scrap of paper and given him the various kinds of boards and two-by-fours which, properly handled, would, he had assured him, turn into a workbench.

Synset Examples

a bit of rock caught him in the eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or quantity of something
  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or separated from something
  • Antonym: whole - something complete, with no parts missing or broken

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'scrap' (piece, fragment), perhaps related to Old Norse 'skrap' (scrapings).
