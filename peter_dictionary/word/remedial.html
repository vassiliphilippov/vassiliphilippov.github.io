remedial

1. ADJ

/ rəˈmidiəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
Intended to correct or improve something that is wrong or deficient, especially in education or health.

Examples

If any official operation abroad begins to go wrong, we shall look to the ambassador to find out why and to get suggestions for remedial action.

Synset Examples

a remedial reading course
remedial education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrective - intended to correct or counteract

Etymology

From Latin remediālis, from remedium (remedy).
