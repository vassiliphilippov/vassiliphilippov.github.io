massacre

1. VERB

/ ˈmæsəkər / (AmE), / ˈmæsəkə / (BrE)
to kill a large number of people indiscriminately and brutally

Examples

Parker could weep - and he wept astonishingly often and on the slightest provocation - but the psychology of those tears was entirely compatible with a remorseless readiness to massacre his opponents.
Look how they massacred my boy.

Synset Examples

The Hutus massacred the Tutsis in Rwanda

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughter - to kill in a violent or brutal manner, especially in large numbers
  • Synonym: exterminate - to kill a whole group of people or animals, usually deliberately
  • Antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm

Etymology

From Middle French 'massacre' (slaughter, carnage), possibly from Old French 'macacre' (slaughter of animals).
