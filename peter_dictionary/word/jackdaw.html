jackdaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌdɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.dɔː / (BrE)
A small, black-and-grey Eurasian bird of the crow family, known for its chattering calls and habit of taking shiny objects.

Examples

There have been cooing doves, chattering magpies, thieving jackdaws, a proud peacock, a silly goose, and a harpy eagle - whom I was silly enough to mate with and who is now busy tearing at my vitals ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - large, black passerine bird closely related to the jackdaw

Etymology

From Middle English *jakdaw*, combining 'Jack' (a masculine forename often used in animal names) and 'daw' (an old word for this species).
