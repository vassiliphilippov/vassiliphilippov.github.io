loyal

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
firm and unwavering in support, allegiance, or duty to a person, cause, organization, or country

Examples

Charlotte Fairchild was excellent as the loyal Marie, who became the second Mrs. LaGuardia, singing and acting with remarkable conviction.
"You are to be accused of Arianism to confuse the religious who remain loyal ".
They won't never git over inter loyal western Virginia, them traitors!
The moral aridity of merchants made them loyal usually to their ledgers.
Maybe that was why they were cordial and loyal towards the unpredictability of Arlene.

Synset Examples

loyal subjects
loyal friends stood by him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithful - remaining true and loyal to a person or cause
  • Antonym: disloyal - not loyal; lacking allegiance or fidelity
  • Degree Higher: devoted - very loving and loyal
  • Antonym: unfaithful - not remaining loyal or true

Etymology

From Old French 'loial', from Latin 'legalis' (legal, lawful).
