chattering

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬ɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætərɪŋ / (BrE)
A rapid, repetitive series of noises made by moving parts of a machine.

Examples

Over the rattling of fenders, humming of tires and chattering of gears there was a charming melody of whispers and tiny giggles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattling - a rapid succession of short, sharp sounds (often by machinery)
  • Synonym: clattering - a loud, continuous rattling or banging noise

Etymology

From the verb 'chatter', likely imitative of rapid, repetitive noise.
