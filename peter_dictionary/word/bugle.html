bugle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbjuːɡl̩ / (BrE)
A brass musical instrument with no valves, typically used for military calls and fanfares.

Examples

An old gentlemen next to me held a Boy Scout bugle to his lips and blasted away at every fourth step and during the interim shouted out, "V for Victory "!
Good morning, Portland Daily Bugle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpet - brass instrument with valves or slides
  • Synonym: cornet - small brass instrument similar to a trumpet, with valves

Etymology

From Latin 'buculus' (young bull), originally referring to a horn made from a bull's horn.
