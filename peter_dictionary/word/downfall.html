downfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnfɔːl / (BrE)
A loss of power, position, or reputation, often as a result of failure or misfortune.

Examples

He evidently could n't foresee that it might be his downfall in the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - complete destruction or collapse
  • Synonym: collapse - sudden failure or breakdown
  • Antonym: triumph - great victory or achievement

Etymology

From Middle English 'downfal', from down + fall.
