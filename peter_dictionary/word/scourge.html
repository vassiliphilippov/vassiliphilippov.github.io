scourge

1. NOUN

/ skɝdʒ / (AmE), / skɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a whip used for inflicting punishment, especially as an instrument of religious discipline

Examples

Each wore the monkish scourge at his waist but this, it seems, was not employed for self-flagellation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - a strip of leather or cord used for flogging or punishment

Etymology

From Old French *escorgier* (to whip), from Vulgar Latin *excorrigiare* (to whip), from Latin *corrigia* (thong, whip).
