grudge

1. NOUN

/ ɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / ɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
A persistent feeling of ill will or resentment, especially one that results from a past insult or injury.

Examples

Grudges simply do not keep that well in a sane mind.
"I know you 've got a grudge against me, and maybe I can n't blame you.
But there were other homesteaders who passed the Lewis murder off as a personal grudge killing, the work of one of his neighbors.

Synset Examples

holding a grudge
settling a score

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentment - a feeling of bitter indignation at having been treated unfairly

Etymology

Probably from Old French 'grouchier' (to grumble or complain).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
to harbor persistent feelings of resentment or ill will against someone

Examples

No hard feelings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone
  • Synonym: resent - to feel bitter or indignation at a circumstance, action, or person

Etymology

From Middle English 'grucchen' (to murmur, grumble), from Old French 'grouchier'.
