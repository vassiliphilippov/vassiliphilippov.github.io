reproach

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A mild expression of disapproval or criticism.

Examples

Considerable technical skill was used and the administration of the elections was generally above reproach.

Synset Examples

words of reproach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French reproche, from reprochier (to bring back close), from Latin re- (back) + prope (near).
