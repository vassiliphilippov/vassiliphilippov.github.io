foliage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊliɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊlɪɪdʒ / (BrE)
the leaves of a plant or trees; leaves collectively

Examples

The day will come, in midsummer, when you find your plants becoming "leggy ", running to tall-growing foliage at the expense of blossoms.
Children walking home from school would scuff through what seemed to be his foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf - a flat, usually green structure attached to a stem, main organ of photosynthesis

Etymology

From Old French 'feuillage', from 'feuille' meaning 'leaf', from Latin 'folia'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊliɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊliɪdʒ / (BrE)
An architectural ornament or pattern resembling leaves, used especially in decorative elements of buildings.

Examples

Square corner - and gate posts were an open-work pattern of cast-iron foliage; they were topped by steeples complete in every detail: high pitched roof, pinnacle, and narrow gable.

Etymology

From Old French 'foillage', from Latin 'folium' (leaf).
