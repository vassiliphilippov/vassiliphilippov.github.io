refuel

1. VERB

/ riˈfjuəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfjuːəl / (BrE)
to supply a vehicle, aircraft, ship, or machine with more fuel

Examples

It could, reputedly, go 70000 miles without refueling and stay down more than a month.
Usually back in Concord by noon, there was just time to get partially thawed out, refuel, and grab a bit of Mrs. Fogg's hot broth before starting the second trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - to supply with fuel
  • Antonym: defuel - to remove fuel from

Etymology

From re- (again) + fuel; first recorded in early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ riˈfjuəl / (AmE), / riˈfjuːəl / (BrE)
to put more fuel into a vehicle, aircraft, or machine so it can continue operating

Examples

But, he told the "skyjackers ", the 707 did n't carry enough fuel to reach Havana; they would have to refuel at El Paso.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - to supply or power with fuel

Etymology

From re- (again) + fuel, first attested in the early 20th century.
