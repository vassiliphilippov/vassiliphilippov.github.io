poplin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːplɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɒplɪn / (BrE)
A strong, plain-woven fabric with a ribbed texture, used for clothing and upholstery.

Examples

His hand went to her shoulder and pushed aside the knotted scarf that surmounted the striped poplin gown; then, to better purpose, he took hold of the knot and with dextrous fingers, untied it.

Etymology

From French *popeline*, from Italian *papalina* (papal): originally made at Avignon, residence of the pope in the 14th century.
