thermistor

1. NOUN

/ θɚˈmɪstɚ / (AmE), / θəˈmɪstə / (BrE)
A semiconductor device whose electrical resistance changes in response to temperature, commonly used for measuring or regulating temperature in circuits.

Examples

It uses both an ultrasonic dimensioning arrangement of the heart and a catheter carrying a thermistor inserted into the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistor - an electronic component that resists current, of which a thermistor is a type

Etymology

Blend of 'thermal' and 'resistor'.
