wheeze

1. VERB

/ wiz / (AmE), / wiːz / (BrE)
to breathe with difficulty, often producing a whistling or rattling sound in the chest

Examples

The man wheezed weakly, his fetid breath beating softly against Watson's neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - to inhale suddenly with mouth open, out of pain or astonishment
  • Synonym: pant - to breathe quickly and often loudly, usually after exertion

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, reflecting the sound made when wheezing.

2. NOUN

/ wiːz / (AmE), / wiːz / (BrE)
A breath or breathing characterized by a husky or whistling sound, often due to difficulty in breathing.

Examples

"You do "? he asked, between wheezes of laughter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stridor - a harsh, grating breathing sound, typically higher-pitched than a wheeze

Etymology

From Old English 'hwēosan' (to breathe hard, whistle).
