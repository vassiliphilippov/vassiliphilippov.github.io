stupid

1. ADJ

/ ˈstuːpɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpɪd / (BrE)
Lacking intelligence or common sense; showing a marked lack of intellectual acuity.

Examples

Secondly, to find a learned diversion and a pleasing joke in More's account of the stupid brutalities of early sixteenth century wars, of the anguish of the poor and dispossessed, of the insolence and cruelty of the rich and powerful requires a callousness toward suffering and sin that would be surprising in a moral imbecile and most surprising in More himself.
Scotty gazed out at ugly gray slums and said softly, "Look at those stupid kids ".
No one is stupid.
All he does is to bullshit with his squad, and they are the stupidest bastards around.
To confess with a canvas chair as a prie-dieu, gouging at his heart until a rough and stupid hand bade him rise and go?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: dull - slow to learn or understand
  • Antonym: smart - having or showing a quick-witted intelligence
  • Antonym: intelligent - having intelligence, especially of a high level

Etymology

From Latin 'stupidus' (amazed, stunned), from 'stupere' (to be numb or amazed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
Lacking intelligence or common sense; slow to learn or understand.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unintelligent - lacking intelligence
  • Synonym: dull - lacking brightness or quickness of mind
  • Antonym: smart - having or showing a quick-witted intelligence
  • Antonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence, especially of a high level

Etymology

From Latin 'stupidus' (amazed, dull), from 'stupere' (to be amazed, be numbed).
