forerunner

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who precedes or announces the approach of someone else, especially one who comes before to prepare the way

Examples

His name is Praisegod Piepsam, and he is rather fully described as to his clothing and physiognomy in a way which relates him to a sinister type in the author's repertory - he is a forerunner of those enigmatic strangers in "Death in Venice ", for example, who represent some combination of cadaver, exotic, and psychopomp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precursor - a person or thing that comes before another of the same kind

Etymology

From Middle English forerunner, from fore- (before) + runner.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrˌrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːrʌnə / (BrE)
A person or thing that precedes the coming or development of someone or something; an early indicator or sign.

Examples

Lublin generally was the forerunner of what would happen elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbinger - a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another

Etymology

From Old English forerennere, from fore- (before) + runner.
