unsuitable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsuːtəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsuːtəbəl / (BrE)
not right or appropriate for a particular purpose or occasion

Examples

The incubation period of infectious disease, plus a variable period of illness even before a lethal effect, render this weapon unsuitable for hand-to-hand encounter.

Synset Examples

a solvent unsuitable for use on wood surfaces

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suitable - right or appropriate for a particular purpose

Etymology

From un- (not) + suitable, from Late Latin *sūitābilis*, from Latin *suitare* (to make fit).
