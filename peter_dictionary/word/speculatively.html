speculatively

1. ADV

/ spɛk.jəˈleɪ.tɪv.li / (AmE), / spɛk.jʊˈleɪ.tɪv.li / (BrE)
In a way that involves or expresses speculation or conjecture rather than certainty.

Examples

She looked at the girl speculatively from eyes which had paled with the years; from the early evening lights of them which had first startled Izaak to look at her in an uncousinly way, they had faded to a near absence of color which had, possibly from her constant looking at the water, something of the light of the sea in them.

Synset Examples

he looked at her speculatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjecturally - In a way based on conjecture rather than definite knowledge

Etymology

Formed from 'speculative' + '-ly'. 'Speculative' from Latin 'speculatus', past participle of 'speculari' (to observe).
