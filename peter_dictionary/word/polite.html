polite

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlaɪt / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪt / (BrE)
having or showing good manners and respect for other people; courteous in behavior, speech, or attitude.

Examples

Rachel was polite, Scotty indifferent.
They did; though contemptuous, they were still polite.
just as p' lite an - an cohnfidunt, an Ah says' Uh coahse you cai n't', but he says' Whah nawt, you ah hevin one'?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courteous - having or showing good manners, polite
  • Antonym: rude - impolite or disrespectful in manners or speech

Etymology

From Latin 'politus' (polished, refined), past participle of 'polire' (to polish).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈlaɪt / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪt / (BrE)
Having or showing behavior that is refined and socially correct; marked by good manners and consideration for others.

Examples

There is evidence to suggest, in fact, that many authors of the humorous sketches were prompted to write them - or to make them as indelicate as they are - by way of protesting against the artificial refinements which had come to dominate the polite letters of the South.

Synset Examples

cultivated speech
cultured Bostonians
cultured tastes
a genteel old lady
polite society

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered
  • Synonym: courteous - polite, respectful, or considerate in manner
  • Synonym: civilized - marked by taste and manners; refined

Etymology

From Latin 'politus' (polished), past participle of 'polire' (to polish, refine).
