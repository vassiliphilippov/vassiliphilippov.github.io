inadequately

1. ADV

/ ɪˈnædɪkwətli / (AmE), / ɪˈnædɪkwətli / (BrE)
In a way that is not sufficient or not good enough for a particular purpose.

Examples

Presumably a cocktail party is expected to fulfill the host's desire to get together a number of people who are inadequately acquainted and thereby arrange for bringing the level of acquaintance up to adequacy for future cooperative endeavors.

Synset Examples

the temporary camps were inadequately equipped

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adequately - in a satisfactory or sufficient manner

Etymology

Formed from 'inadequate' + '-ly'. 'Inadequate' from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'adaequatus' (equalized).
