low-down

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.daʊn / (BrE)
(informal, slang) Secret or inside information about someone or something, especially information that is not generally known.

Examples

This was one place where Moonan could go for a drink in a back booth without anyone noticing him, or at least coming up and hanging around and wanting to know all the low-down.
A freshman girl's father not too long ago called a dean at Brooklyn College and demanded the "low-down "on a boy who was going out with his daughter.

Synset Examples

is that the straight dope?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dope - slang for inside information

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌdaʊn / (BrE)
Extremely contemptible or despicable; deserving of strong disapproval or scorn.

Examples

I never felt such a lowdown hound in my life.

Synset Examples

abject cowardice
a low stunt to pull
a low-down sneak
his miserable treatment of his family
You miserable skunk!
a scummy rabble
a scurvy trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving of contempt; despicable
  • Synonym: despicable - deserving hatred and contempt
  • Antonym: admirable - worthy of admiration; inspiring approval
