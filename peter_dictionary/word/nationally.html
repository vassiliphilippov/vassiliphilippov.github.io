nationally

1. ADV

/ ˈnæʃənəli / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or affects a whole nation; throughout or by the entire country.

Examples

Mankowski, the ball-hawking defensive expert, was cited for his performance against Bradley in St. Louis U.'s nationally televised victory.
This program is now nationally known as "Teen Hunter Clubs ".

Synset Examples

a nationally uniform culture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: locally - in a way that relates to a particular local area

Etymology

From 'national' + '-ly'. 'National' from French 'national', from nation.

2. ADV

/ ˈnæʃənəli / (AmE), / ˈnaʃnəli / (BrE)
In a way that extends throughout or is applicable to an entire nation; across a whole country.

Examples

In the household market, there are also low-sudsing detergent formulations based on nonionic actives with about the same amount of phosphate builder; light-duty synthetic detergents with much less builder; and the dwindling built soap powders as well as soap flakes and granules, none of which are now nationally advertised.

Synset Examples

nationally advertised
it was broadcast nationwide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationwide - happening or existing across the whole nation

Etymology

From 'national' + '-ly', with 'national' derived from nation (from Latin 'natio', meaning 'birth, nation').
