fiendish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːn.dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːn.dɪʃ / (BrE)
Extremely evil or cruel; resembling a fiend or demon in wickedness.

Examples

This would make anyone crafty and cruel, capable of fiendish revenge.

Synset Examples

something demonic in him--something that could be cruel
fires lit up a diabolic scene
diabolical sorcerers under the influence of devils
a fiendish despot
hellish torture
infernal instruments of war
satanic cruelty
unholy grimaces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - extremely evil or wicked
  • Synonym: satanic - of or characteristic of Satan; wicked
  • Antonym: angelic - resembling an angel in goodness or purity; exceptionally kind or innocent

Etymology

From 'fiend' + '-ish'; 'fiend' from Old English 'fīend', meaning 'enemy, devil'.
