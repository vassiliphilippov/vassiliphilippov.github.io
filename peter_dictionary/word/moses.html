Moses

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊzɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊzɪz / (BrE)
A Hebrew prophet in the Old Testament who led the Israelites out of Egypt and received the Ten Commandments from God on Mount Sinai.

Examples

"E. B. "compared John Brown to Moses in that they were both acting to deliver millions from oppression.

Etymology
