recondition

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
to repair or renovate something so that it is in good or improved condition again

Examples

He did this by the charming practice of buying up used electric blankets for $ 5 to $ 10 from survivors of patients who had died, reconditioning them, and selling them at $ 185 each.

Synset Examples

He reconditioned the old appliances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refurbish - to renovate and redecorate something, typically a building or furniture
  • Synonym: restore - to return something to a former condition or position

Etymology

From re- (again) + condition; first used in early 20th century.
