noticeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊ.tɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.tɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
Able to be easily seen, heard, or noticed; clear or obvious.

Examples

The penalty for this is noticeable in the big, bold, brilliant, but brassy piano sounds in Melody and Percussion for Two Pianos (SP 44007).

Synset Examples

after a noticeable pause the lecturer continued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detectable - able to be detected or discovered
  • Antonym: imperceptible - impossible or difficult to perceive

Etymology

From 'notice' + '-able', modeled after French 'noticable'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊtɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtɪsəbəl / (BrE)
Easily seen, noticed, or perceived; obvious or worthy of attention.

Examples

The improvement was most noticeable in the greater consistency among reaction cells prepared as a group on the same manifold.
This was particularly noticeable in group A and group B sera, in which cases activity in Regions 1 and 2 was usually not detectable without prior concentration and occasionally could not be detected at all.

Synset Examples

noticeable shadows under her eyes
noticeable for its vivid historical background
a noticeable lack of friendliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: conspicuous - standing out so as to be clearly visible
  • Antonym: unnoticeable - not easy to see or perceive
  • Synonym: visible - able to be seen

Etymology

From notice + -able, first recorded in English in the late 17th century.
