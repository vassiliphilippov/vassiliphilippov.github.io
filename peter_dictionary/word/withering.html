withering

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪðərɪŋ / (BrE)
devastating or fiercely destructive; capable of inflicting complete destruction or overwhelming force

Examples

He went down like a wet rag and the attackers hit the dirt in the face of the withering enemy fire.

Synset Examples

possessing annihilative power
a devastating hurricane
the guns opened a withering fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastating - causing great damage or destruction
  • Synonym: annihilative - completely destroying or obliterating

Etymology

Present participle of 'wither,' meaning to dry up or shrivel; figuratively extended to mean intensely destructive.
