batter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætər / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a player who is currently at bat and attempting to hit the ball pitched by the pitcher.

Examples

Then Dick Hyde, submarine ball hurler, entered the contest and only five batters needed to face him before there existed a 3 - to 3 deadlock.
Lumpe worked a walk as the first batter to face Hyde and romped around as Siebern blasted Hyde's next toss 415 feet over the scoreboard in right center.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pitcher - the baseball player who throws the ball to the batter
  • Synonym: hitter - baseball player whose main role is to hit the ball

2. VERB

/ ˈbæɾɚ / (AmE), / ˈbætə / (BrE)
to strike repeatedly with hard blows; to hit against something forcefully, often over and over

Examples

The waves of a 1923 tsunami in Sagami Bay brought to the surface and battered to death huge numbers of fishes that normally live at a depth of 3000 feet.
They had winged over the Adriatic, they had taken Bari by complete surprise and now they were battering her, attacking with deadly skill.

Synset Examples

Winds buffeted the tent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffet - to strike repeatedly, especially with blows or impacts
  • Synonym: pound - to hit something repeatedly with force

Etymology

From Middle English 'bateren', from Old French 'batre', from Latin 'battuere' (to beat).
