correctness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛktnəs / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being in accordance with fact, truth, or accepted standards; accuracy.

Examples

Instead, retailers would sign a certificate of correctness, violation of which would carry a penalty of one to five years in prison, plus a $ 1000 fine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accuracy - the quality of being correct or precise
  • Antonym: incorrectness - the state of being not correct or accurate

Etymology

From correct + -ness, where 'correct' is from Latin 'correctus', past participle of 'corrigere' (to make straight, set right).
