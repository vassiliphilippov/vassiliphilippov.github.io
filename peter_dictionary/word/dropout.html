dropout

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑpˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈdrɒpˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who leaves school or college before completing a course of study or graduating

Examples

Second, vocational courses should be provided in grades 11 and 12 and not require more than half the student's time in those years; however, for slow learners and prospective dropouts these courses ought to begin earlier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graduate - a person who has completed a course of study at a school or college
  • Synonym: truancy - the action of staying away from school without good reason

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'drop out', meaning to withdraw from participation; used as a noun since the mid-20th century.
