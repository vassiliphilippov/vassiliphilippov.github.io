pulling

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of applying force to move something toward oneself or in a specific direction.

Examples

Mr. Claude is a specialist in torso development and he has long favored the now famous Weider Push-Pull Super-Set technique in which one exercise of the Super-Set is a pressing or "pushing "movement which accents one sector of a muscle group in a specific way, followed by a "pulling "exercise which works the opposing sector of the same muscle group.

Synset Examples

the pull up the hill had him breathing harder
his strenuous pulling strained his back

Related Terms

  • Antonym: push - the act of applying force to move something away from oneself
  • Synonym: dragging - pulling something slowly or with effort along a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'pull' + -ing suffix, forming a noun denoting the action of pulling.
