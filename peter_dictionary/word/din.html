din

1. NOUN

/ dɪn / (AmE), / dɪn / (BrE)
a loud, continuous, and often unpleasant noise or commotion

Examples

The din was successful, too, for just before the moon disappeared, the frightened toad had begun to spit it out again, which meant good luck all around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • Synonym: uproar - a state of commotion and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: silence - the complete absence of sound

Etymology

From Old English 'dyne' (noise), related to Old Norse 'dynr'.
