repentant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpɛntənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛntənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing sincere regret or remorse for wrongdoing; contrite.

Examples

In each of the last, the trial marked the beginning of a new course: in Moscow the liquidation of the Old Bolsheviks and the tightening of Stalin's dictatorship; in the United States the initiation of militant anti Communism, with the repentant ex Communist in the vanguard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrite - feeling or expressing remorse or penitence; affected by guilt
  • Synonym: remorseful - full of remorse; sorry
  • Antonym: unrepentant - not feeling or showing regret for one's actions

Etymology

From Latin 'paenitentem' (regretting, repenting) via Old French 'repentant'.
