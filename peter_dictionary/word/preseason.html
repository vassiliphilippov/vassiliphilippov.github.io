preseason

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
The period before the start of a regular sports season, devoted to training and preparation.

Examples

The Texans have two more road games - at Buffalo and Houston - before they play for the home folks again, and it looks as if coach Hank Stram's men will meet the Bills just as they are developing into the kind of team they were expected to be in pre-season reckonings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offseason - the period between the end of one season and the beginning of the next
  • Antonym: postseason - the period after the regular season, especially for playoffs

Etymology

From 'pre-' (before) + 'season' (period for sports competition).
