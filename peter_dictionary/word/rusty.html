rusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti / (BrE)
having the reddish-brown color of rust

Examples

A. armata is much more beautiful in color, at least in the female of the species: a rich, velvety, rusty red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auburn - reddish-brown in color, especially of hair
  • Synonym: chestnut - of a dark reddish-brown color, especially referring to hair or horses
  • Degree Higher: scarlet - bright red color

Etymology

From Old English 'rūstig', from 'rūst' (rust).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti / (BrE)
covered with or consisting of rust; having a surface corroded by oxidation, typically taking on a reddish-brown appearance.

Examples

Opposite every gate was a hitching post or a stone carriage step, set with a rusty iron ring for tying a horse.
Hardly had Mrs. Roebuck driven off when a rusty pick-up truck, father or grandfather of Senor "Moriarty's "Ford sedan, came screeching to a dust swirling stop, and a brown face appeared, its nose threatened by shards of what had once been the side window.
On the truck bed there was nothing smaller than a piece of rusty machinery; with more time I could have loosened a small burr or cotter pin -

Synset Examples

a rusty machine
rusty deposits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroded - damaged by a chemical reaction, especially by oxidation
  • Antonym: shiny - having a smooth, glossy, or polished surface; not rusted or corroded

Etymology

From Old English 'rustig', from 'rust' (reddish-brown coating on iron or steel).
