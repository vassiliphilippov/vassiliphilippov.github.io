sherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈʃɛri / (BrE)
A fortified wine, originally from the Jerez region of southern Spain, ranging from dry to sweet and usually served as an aperitif.

Examples

Here and there she stayed to visit and drink a glass of sherry.
Mr. Gorboduc took a swig of his sherry.
He was so long thinking that my mother had time to inspect her sherry for dregs.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Jerez', the name of the city in Spain where the wine originates.
