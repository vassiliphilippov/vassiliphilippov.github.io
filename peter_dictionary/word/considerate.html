considerate

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪd.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / kənˈsɪd.ər.ət / (BrE)
thoughtful toward the needs, rights, or feelings of others

Examples

He wants to be called sweet, good, considerate and mother's little helper.
As for the night being warm, he would have been comfortable naked on Mount Everest but his water brothers had little tolerance for changes in temperature and pressure; he was considerate of their weakness, once he learned of it.
You'’re a wise and considerate young man.

Synset Examples

friends considerate enough to leave us alone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtful - showing consideration or attention to the needs of others
  • Antonym: inconsiderate - not showing concern for the feelings or needs of others

Etymology

From Latin consideratus, past participle of considerare (to consider).
