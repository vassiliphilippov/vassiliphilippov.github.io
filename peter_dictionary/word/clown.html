clown

1. NOUN

/ klaʊn / (AmE), / klaʊn / (BrE)
a person who entertains others by behaving in a comical or silly way, often by performing tricks, jokes, or pranks

Examples

In these readings, the double bass is either kept discreetly in the background, or it is dressed in clown's attire - the musical equivalent of a bull in a china shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffoon - a person who amuses others by clowning, jesting, or ridiculous behavior
  • Synonym: jester - a professional joker or 'fool' at a medieval court

Etymology

From early modern English, likely from Middle Low German 'klönne' (clumsy person).

2. NOUN

/ klaʊn / (AmE), / klaʊn / (BrE)
an insulting term for a person who behaves in a foolish, rude, or contemptible way

Examples

How could he exert authority over them - make them toe the line, as he had to - if he knuckled under to this small-town clown?
If this clown could touch Grissom, I'd have handed him his lungs by now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who behaves in a silly or stupid way
  • Synonym: buffoon - a ridiculous but amusing person; a clown
