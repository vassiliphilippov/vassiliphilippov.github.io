widowhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊˌhʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being a widow; the condition or time in which a woman remains after her husband's death

Examples

He is confronted with the recurrent crises, such as great natural catastrophes and the great transitions of life - marriage, incurable disease, widowhood, old age, the certainty of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widowerhood - the state or period of being a widower

Etymology

From Middle English 'widowhod', from 'widow' + '-hood' (state or condition).
