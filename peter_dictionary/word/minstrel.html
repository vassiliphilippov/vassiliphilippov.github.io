minstrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnstrəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəl / (BrE)
a singer or musician who performed songs or recited poetry, especially one who traveled from place to place in the Middle Ages

Examples

The wailing, guitar strumming minstrels of the cattle kingdom made up songs about him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bard - a poet and singer, especially one reciting epics in ancient times

Etymology

From Old French 'menestrel', from Late Latin 'ministralis' (servant or entertainer).
