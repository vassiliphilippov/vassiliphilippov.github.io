flaw

1. NOUN

/ flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
A fault or imperfection in an object, material, or machine that detracts from its quality or functionality.

Examples

Not only was Haumd's intonation and phrasing without flaw, but he seemed to take every tonal eccentricity in stride.
After selecting a sheet and inspecting it for flaws (even the best sometimes has foreign' nubbins' on its surface), I sponge it thoroughly on both sides with clean, cold water.

Synset Examples

a flaw caused the crystal to shatter
if there are any defects you should send it back to the manufacturer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defect - a shortcoming or imperfection in something
  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance of something

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse flaga (slab, spot, patch).
