curtly

1. ADV

/ ˈkɜrtli / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtli / (BrE)
In a way that is curt, abrupt, and often discourteous.

Examples

"We 've got to eat ", Sarah said, curtly, because a chill crawled over her again.

Synset Examples

he told me curtly to get on with it
he talked short with everyone
he said shortly that he didn't like it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptly - in a sudden or brusque manner
  • Antonym: politely - in a courteous and respectful manner

Etymology

From 'curt' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Curt' from Latin 'curtus' (short).
