petulance

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtʃʊləns / (AmE), / ˈpɛtjʊləns / (BrE)
the quality of being childishly sulky, irritable, or bad-tempered

Examples

In "The King's Ride ", Charles breaks out of a long period of petulance and inertia, regains his old self, escapes from Turkey, and finally reaches his own land after an absence of eighteen years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed or made angry
  • Antonym: placidity - the quality of being calm and peaceful

Etymology

From Latin 'petulantia' (immodesty, impudence).
