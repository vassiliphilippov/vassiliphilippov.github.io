desecrate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪkrɛɪt / (BrE)
To treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect; to violate its sanctity.

Examples

He had belonged to this land and, perhaps, had desecrated it - and this was the only material symbol that remained of him.

Synset Examples

desecrate a cemetery
violate the sanctity of the church
profane the name of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profane - to treat something sacred with irreverence
  • Antonym: consecrate - to declare or make something sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'desecratus', past participle of 'desecrate' meaning 'to make unholy', from 'de-' (do the opposite of) + 'sacrare' (to make holy).
