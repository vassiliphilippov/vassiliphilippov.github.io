idleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdlnəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪdlnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being idle; lack of occupation, work, or activity

Examples

End Gene Raesz, who broke a hand in the Owl's game with LSU, was back working out with Rice Monday, and John Nichols, sophomore guard, moved back into action after a week's idleness with an ankle injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active; engagement in action or work

Etymology

From Middle English idelnesse, from idle + -ness.
