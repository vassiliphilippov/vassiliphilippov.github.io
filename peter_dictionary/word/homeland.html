homeland

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌlænd / (BrE)
the country where you were born or consider as your native land

Examples

A dominant motive is the poet's longing for his homeland and its boyhood associations: "Not men folk, but the fields where I would stray, The stones where as a child I used to play ".
He is driven back by his yearning to the wintry homeland of his fathers in the forest of Tiveden ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatherland - one's native country, especially when emphasizing ancestral ties
  • Synonym: motherland - one's native country, often used similarly to 'homeland'

Etymology

Formed from 'home' + 'land', dating from the late 19th century.
