submission

1. NOUN

/ səbˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of accepting or yielding to a superior force or to the will or authority of another person

Examples

The world exploded when Fascism challenged all concepts of peace and liberty, and the outraged, freedom loving peoples of the Capitalist and Socialist worlds combined forces to stamp Fascist tyranny into cringing submission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - the act of giving up resistance to an opponent or authority
  • Antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply with something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'submissio(n-)', from Latin 'submittere' meaning 'to lower, let down'.

2. NOUN

/ səbˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
something that is presented or handed in for consideration, especially to a person or group in authority, such as a manuscript, proposal, or claim.

Examples

The Commission shall certify to the Secretary of State, upon his request, copies of the formal submissions of claims filed pursuant to subsection (b) of section 4 of this Act for transmission to the foreign government concerned.
Counsel for du Pont indicated a preference for the submission of detailed plans by both sides at an early date.
The submission of detailed plans would place the issues before the court more readily than would discussion of divestiture or disenfranchisement in the abstract.

Synset Examples

several of his submissions were rejected by publishers
what was the date of submission of your proposal?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a plan or suggestion put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: application - a formal request, often written, for something such as a job or permission

Etymology

From Latin submissio(n-), from submittere ‘send under, submit’.
