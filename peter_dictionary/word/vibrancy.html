vibrancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪbrənsi / (AmE), / ˈvaɪbrənsi / (BrE)
The quality of being resonant, having a loud, deep, and rich sound.

Examples

Schnabel's emphasis on structural clarity, his innate rhythmic vibrancy, and impetuous intensity all tend to stamp his reading as a symphonic one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality in a sound of being deep, full, and reverberating

Etymology

From 'vibrant' + '-cy', ultimately from Latin 'vibrare' (to move quickly to and fro).
