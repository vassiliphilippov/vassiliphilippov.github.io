preconception

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
An idea or opinion formed before having enough evidence, especially one that is unreasonable or unsupported.

Examples

As an American Catholic of Irish ancestry, I came with certain preconceptions and expectations; being intellectually influenced by Newman and the general 19th century literature of England, I knew only a Protestant dominated country.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason
  • Synonym: bias - inclination or prejudice for or against something

Etymology

From Latin præ- (before) + conception (from Latin conceptio, conception).
