notoriously

1. ADV

/ noʊˈtɔriəsli / (AmE), / nəˈtɔːriəsli / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something is well known, especially for being bad or undesirable.

Examples

In his teens O' Banion was enrolled in the vicious Market Street gang and he became a singing waiter in McGovern's Cafe, a notoriously low and rowdy dive in North Clark Street, where befuddled customers were methodically looted of their money by the singing waiters before being thrown out.

Synset Examples

European emigres, who notoriously used to repair to the British Museum to write seditious pamphlets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famously - in a way that is well known, often for something notable or positive

Etymology

From 'notorious' + '-ly'. 'Notorious' from Medieval Latin 'notorius' (well-known).
