self-satisfaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˌsætɪsˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌselfˌsætɪsˈfakʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of being pleased or content with oneself and one's achievements, sometimes to the point of complacency.

Examples

In the vases this spirit may perhaps at times bore or repel one in its internal self-satisfaction, but the best of the Geometric pins have rightly been considered among the most beautiful ever made in the Greek world.
Along with this self-satisfaction, however, Joyce sensed a growing tension.

Synset Examples

his complacency was absolutely disgusting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complacency - a feeling of quiet pleasure or security, often while unaware of danger or defect
