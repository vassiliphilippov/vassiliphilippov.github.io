fetch

1. VERB

/ fɛtʃ / (AmE), / fɛtʃ / (BrE)
to go and bring someone or something back from where they are

Examples

- Fetch me the copies of everything B and C companies have requisitioned in the last six months.
We 'll have oystchers - couple bar' l oystchers 'll fetch in a crowd any time.
Get on back and fetch us up some.

Synset Examples

Get me those books over there, please
Could you bring the wine?
The dog fetched the hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring - to take or carry someone or something to a place
  • Synonym: retrieve - to get something and bring it back

Etymology

Old English 'feccan', of Germanic origin, possibly related to 'fetch' in Middle Dutch 'vetsen'.

2. VERB

/ fɛtʃ / (AmE), / fɛtʃ / (BrE)
(of an item) to be sold for a particular price, especially at auction or in a sale.

Examples

What's an item like this usually go for?

Synset Examples

The painting brought $10,000
The old print fetched a high price at the auction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring - (of an item) to be sold for a certain price
