vapor

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə / (BrE)
A visible suspension in the air of very small particles of liquid or solid, such as water or mercury.

Examples

Stained or unstained sections were examined under dark field illumination in a Zeiss fluorescence microscope equipped with a mercury vapor lamp (Osram HBO 200).
It was with the assistance of one of the members of this expedition, Lauritz Esmarch, that Oersted succeeded in producing light by creating an electric discharge in mercury vapor through which an electric current was made to flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mist - fine droplets of water suspended in air
  • Synonym: steam - water vapor produced by heating water
  • Degree Higher: gas - substance in a gaseous state
  • Antonym: condensation - the process of turning vapor into a liquid or solid

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, heat, vapor).
