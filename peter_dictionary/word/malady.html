malady

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæl.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈmæl.ə.di / (BrE)
a disease or disorder of the body; an illness, especially one that is serious or chronic

Examples

One of the most common of camp maladies was diarrhoea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illness - a state of poor health; sickness
  • Synonym: disease - a particular abnormal condition negatively affecting an organism
  • Antonym: health - the state of being free from illness or injury

Etymology

From Old French *maladie* (illness), from *mal* (bad) + *-ie* (noun suffix), ultimately from Latin *male* (badly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmælədi / (AmE), / ˈmælədi / (BrE)
a serious illness or a serious problem affecting a person or a society

Examples

As these maladies overlap, so must the cure.

Synset Examples

what maladies afflict our nation?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disease - a disorder of structure or function in a living organism
  • Synonym: affliction - something that causes pain or suffering

Etymology

From Old French *maladie* (disease), from *mal* (bad) + *-ie* (suffix).
