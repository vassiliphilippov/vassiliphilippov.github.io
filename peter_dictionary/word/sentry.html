sentry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntri / (AmE), / ˈsɛntri / (BrE)
a soldier or guard stationed to keep watch and prevent unauthorized access or unexpected events

Examples

He 'd shoot at anything if it was the rear end of a horse or his own sentry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who keeps watch or protects a place or person
  • Synonym: lookout - a person employed to keep watch for some anticipated event

Etymology

From Italian 'sentinella' (sentry, sentinel), likely from a verb meaning 'to perceive, watch'.
