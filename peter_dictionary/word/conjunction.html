conjunction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the state of two or more things occurring at the same time or being used or considered together

Examples

But for purely definition purposes - used in conjunction with your regular Squatting, Leg Curling, Leg Extensor programs - a heavy weight is not needed.
As an example of the interpretation of an arrow in the figure which exceeds four months in shaft length in conjunction with its position in the figure: girl 2 had a delayed Onset and further delayed Completion.
The reproducibilities of helium vapor-pressure thermometers have been investigated in conjunction with a "constant temperature "liquid helium bath from 4.2 to 1.8 * * f.

Synset Examples

the interval determining the coincidence gate is adjustable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincidence - the occurrence of events at the same time

Etymology

From Latin conjunctio (a joining together), from conjungere (to join).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a word such as 'and', 'but', or 'because' that is used to connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences

Examples

All the other parts of speech: nouns, pronouns, adjectives, adverbs, and conjunctions operated under the same system as the verbs.
Well, if you don'’t feel like that, why don'’t you just quit college and go to join the army!

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: coordinate conjunction - a conjunction that joins words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance (e.g., and, but, or)
  • Degree Higher: subordinating conjunction - a conjunction that introduces a subordinate clause (e.g., because, although, if)

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctio' (a joining together), from 'conjungere' (to join together).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
The act or state of two or more things being joined together; a combination or union.

Examples

The efforts of various interest groups to control or influence governmental decisions, particularly when taken in conjunction with the impact of industrialization, led to a concentration of attention on the legislative power and the means whereby policy could be formulated and enforced as law through bureaucratic institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the action of joining together or the fact of being joined together
  • Antonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart; division

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctio', from 'conjungere' (to join together).
