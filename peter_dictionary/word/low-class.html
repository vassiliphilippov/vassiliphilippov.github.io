low-class

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ ˌklæs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌklɑːs / (BrE)
Belonging to or characteristic of the lowest social or economic group in society.

Examples

Faulkner's low-class characters had but few counterparts in earlier Southern novels dealing with plantation life.
Ace of spades - a widow, that was what they called a widow, these low-class crooks remembered Mr. Skyros distractedly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper-class - belonging to the highest social or economic group
  • Synonym: working-class - belonging to the social group that is employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
