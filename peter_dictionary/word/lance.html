lance

1. NOUN

/ læns / (AmE), / lɑːns / (BrE)
A long, pointed weapon used in fighting, especially in the past by soldiers on horseback; also, a tool resembling such a weapon.

Examples

The company which performed the Pulitzer Prize musical here last night and will repeat it twice today is full of bounce, the politicians are in fine voice, the chorines evoke happy memories, and the Little Flower rides to break a lance again.
It could never happen as long as God was alert and the Drew steeple stood guard with its peaked lance.
Batman's head on a lance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spear - a weapon with a long shaft and a pointed tip, used for thrusting or throwing

Etymology

From Old French *lance* (spear), from Latin *lancea* (light spear).
