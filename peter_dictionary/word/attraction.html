attraction

1. NOUN

/ əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / əˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a place, event, or activity that is intended to entertain people and draws visitors, especially as part of a public entertainment

Examples

Areas should be large enough to include the attractions, have ample space for the use of facilities needed, and have room around the edges to protect the values of the area from encroachment by private developments.
Shape of the area is also related to the use attractions and needs of the development.
We figure that entertainment would draw gamblers to the casino.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entertainment - an event, performance, or activity designed to amuse or interest people
  • Degree Lower: distraction - something that draws attention away

Etymology

From Latin attractio(n-), from attrahere (to draw towards).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / əˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
A quality or feature that provides pleasure and draws interest or attention.

Examples

Movement itself was the chief and often the only attraction of the primitive movies of the nineties.

Synset Examples

flowers are an attractor for bees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully attractive or fascinating
  • Antonym: repulsion - a feeling of intense distaste or aversion, the opposite of attraction

Etymology

From Latin 'attractio', from 'attrahere' meaning 'to draw towards'.
