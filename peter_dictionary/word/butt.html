butt

1. NOUN

/ bʌt / (AmE), / bʌt / (BrE)
the thick or blunt end of a handle, especially of a weapon or tool

Examples

Reversing it, he smashed the butt down on Frederick Seward's head, over and over again.
Less assured than the tall, wide shouldered man in the lead, Cobb followed alertly, a hand on his gun butt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hilt - the handle of a sword or dagger

Etymology

From Old French *but* (end, target), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ bʌt / (AmE), / bʌt / (BrE)
to place or join the ends of two objects together so that they touch, usually without overlapping

Examples

The first piece is glued and screwed to the frames and transom and the piece butted between frames one and two.

Synset Examples

The frames must be butted at the joints

3. VERB

/ bʌt / (AmE), / bʌt / (BrE)
(of one object or surface) to be positioned so as to touch or be adjacent to another object or surface, typically forming a joint.

Examples

All frames are butted at the joints and 3 8 - inch plywood gussets are glued and nailed on each side of each joint, again using the one-inch, No. 12 nails.
After the frames and transom are set up on the jig and temporarily braced, a piece of three-inch-wide mahogany (only widths will be given since the 13 16 - inch thickness is used throughout) is butted between frames one and two below the line of the keelson.
The exception is where the flooring butts.

Synset Examples

Canada adjoins the U.S.
England marches with Scotland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjoin - to be next to or joined with
  • Synonym: abut - to be next to or touch at one end or side

4. NOUN

/ bʌt / (AmE), / bʌt / (BrE)
The fleshy part of the human body that you sit on; the buttocks (informal).

Examples

Move your butts!

Synset Examples

he deserves a good kick in the butt
are you going to sit on your fanny and do nothing?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttocks - the two rounded parts of the human body that form the bottom
  • Synonym: rear end - informal term for the buttocks

Etymology

Originates as a shortened form of 'buttocks'.
