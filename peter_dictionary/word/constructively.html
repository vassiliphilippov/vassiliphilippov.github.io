constructively

1. ADV

/ kənˈstrʌktɪvli / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that is helpful, positive, or intended to produce good results rather than causing problems.

Examples

Together they had probably done more than any other men to help push Laos toward the 20 th century - constructively.
But while the corporation has all the disadvantages of the socialist form of organization (so cumbersome it cannot constructively do much of anything not compatible with its need to perpetuate itself and maintain its status quo), unluckily it does not have the desirable aspect of socialism, the motivation to operate for the benefit of society as a whole.

Synset Examples

it is my task to look critically and constructively at the flaws and the failures

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destructively - in a way that causes damage or harm; opposite of constructively

Etymology

From 'constructive' + '-ly'; 'constructive' from Latin 'constructus', past participle of 'construere' (to heap up, build).
