abjection

1. NOUN

/ æbˈʤɛkʃən / (AmE), / æbˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
A state of being extremely degraded, humiliated, or cast down; the condition of being abject.

Examples

It is this modulation which reveals to Jacoby his own frightful abjection and, simultaneously, his wife's infidelity.

Synset Examples

each confession brought her into an attitude of abasement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasement - state of being degraded or humbled
  • Synonym: humiliation - feeling or state of being humiliated

Etymology

From Late Latin 'abjectio', from Latin 'abicere' (to throw away).
