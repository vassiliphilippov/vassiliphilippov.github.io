unfair

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɛr / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛə / (BrE)
Not just, unbiased, or honest; marked by injustice, partiality, or deceit.

Examples

Therefore, he decided he was unfair to the young man and should make an effort to understand and sympathize with his point of view.

Synset Examples

used unfair methods
it was an unfair trial
took an unfair advantage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fair - just, impartial, and honest; free from discrimination or dishonesty
  • Synonym: inequitable - unfair; not equitable
  • Antonym: just - acting with fairness and justice

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fair', meaning not fair.
