ram

1. VERB

/ ræm / (AmE), / ræm / (BrE)
To force something into or from a state or action with great energy or determination, often used both literally and figuratively.

Examples

Greg slammed his throttle to the fire wall and rammed up the RPM, and the engine responded as if it had been waiting.
He rammed his mind into focus and said to the two ladies who lay pinkly nude beside him in the desert, "That was a good bite.

Synset Examples

She rammed her mind into focus
He drives me mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force - to make someone do something against their will or put strong pressure
  • Synonym: shove - to push someone or something roughly

Etymology

From Old English 'ramman' (to strike, push), related to Old Norse 'ramma' (to ram).

2. VERB

/ ræm / (AmE), / ræm / (BrE)
to hit or forcefully push something or someone with great strength or impact

Examples

All Deegan had to do was slide, fall away, but instead, he rammed into the catcher.
"You did n't have to ram him ".

Synset Examples

ram the gate with a sledgehammer
pound on the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bash - to hit something hard and violently
  • Degree Lower: bump - to hit or knock against with a degree of force
  • Degree Higher: slam - to push or hit something with force, producing a loud noise

Etymology

From Old Norse *rammr* (strong), related to the noun meaning 'male sheep' used as a battering symbol.

3. VERB

/ ræm / (AmE), / ræm / (BrE)
to forcefully hit or push something with great force, especially deliberately; to crash into something violently

Examples

Deegan had no business ramming into that kid out there.

Synset Examples

the plane crashed into the ocean
The car crashed into the lamp post

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to collide with something with force and cause damage
  • Degree Lower: bump - to hit something lightly or accidentally

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *ramma* (strong, mighty).
