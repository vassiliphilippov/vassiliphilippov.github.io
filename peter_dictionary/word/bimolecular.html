bimolecular

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.moʊˈlɛk.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.məˈlɛk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
(of a reaction or process) involving or relating to two molecules, especially as reactants.

Examples

The rate of the gas phase exchange reaction appears to be proportional to the first power of the absorbed light intensity indicating that the radical intermediates are removed at the walls or by reaction with an impurity rather than by bimolecular radical combination reactions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: unimolecular - involving a single molecule, especially in a reaction

Etymology

From bi- (two) + molecular (from molecule, from French molécule, from Latin moles ‘a mass’).
