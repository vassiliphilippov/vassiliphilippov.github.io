grub

1. NOUN

/ ɡrʌb / (AmE), / ɡrʌb / (BrE)
a soft, thick, wormlike larva of certain beetles and other insects, often found in soil or decaying wood

Examples

Effectively controls cattle grubs which damage hides and can reduce gains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larva - the immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect's life cycle

Etymology

Probably from Old English *grybban* (to scratch, dig).
