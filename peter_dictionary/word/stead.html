stead

1. NOUN

/ stɛd / (AmE), / stɛd / (BrE)
the place, role, or position that is filled by another person or thing, especially as in the phrase 'in someone's stead' meaning 'in place of someone else'

Examples

These 1750 cases were carted off in a one night operation by the O' Banion men, who left in their stead the same number of barrels filled with water.
In his stead is a milquetoast version known as "the corporation ".

Synset Examples

can you go in my stead?
took his place
in lieu of

Related Terms

  • Synonym: place - the position or role taken by someone or something
  • Synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting in place of another

Etymology

From Old English stede (place, position), related to German Stelle (place).
