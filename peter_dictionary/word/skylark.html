skylark

1. VERB

/ ˈskaɪ.lɑrk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪ.lɑːk / (BrE)
to play or run about in a lively, boisterous, or carefree manner; to frolic or romp

Examples

Robinson told Policemen James Jones and Morgan Lloyd of the Wabash avenue district that 10 youths boarded his south bound express bus in front of Dunbar Vocational High school, 30 th street and South Park way, and began "skylarking ".

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly and energetically
  • Synonym: gambol - to run or jump about playfully

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'skylark' (the bird); perhaps from the idea of larking about as freely as a skylark flies.
