longitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑndʒəˌtuːd, -ˌtjud / (AmE), / ˈlɒndʒɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the angular distance east or west on the earth's surface, measured in degrees from the prime meridian at Greenwich

Examples

Within about an hour with the help of reports from seismic stations in Alaska, Arizona and California, the quake's epicenter was placed at 51 degrees North latitude and 158 degrees East longitude.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: latitude - the angular distance north or south of the Earth's equator, measured in degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'longitudo' (length), from 'longus' (long).
