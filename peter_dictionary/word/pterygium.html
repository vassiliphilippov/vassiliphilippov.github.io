pterygium

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɪdʒ.i.əm / (AmE), / təˈrɪdʒ.i.əm / (BrE)
A growth of thickened, triangular tissue on the conjunctiva of the eye that may extend onto the cornea, often caused by irritation.

Examples

The body was that of a well developed, somewhat debilitated white man weighing 108 lb. There were bilateral pterygia and arcus senilis, and the mouth was edentulous.

Etymology

From Greek pterygion, diminutive of pteryx (wing), referring to the wing-shaped appearance.
