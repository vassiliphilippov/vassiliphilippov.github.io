redress

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdrɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈdrɛs / (BrE)
money or other compensation given to make up for a loss or injury; reparation.

Examples

The belief in immortality, where held, functions as a redress for the ills and disappointments of the here and now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury
  • Synonym: damages - money claimed or awarded in compensation for a loss or an injury
  • Synonym: reparation - the making of amends for a wrong done, by paying money or otherwise helping

Etymology

From Old French 'redresser', based on Latin 'redirectus' (straightened again).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈdrɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to put right (an unfair situation or a wrong); to make amends for or remedy

Examples

The glass may seem trivial but Communist official hooliganism feeds on such incidents unless they are redressed.

Synset Examples

right a wrongs done to the victims of the Holocaust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remedy - to correct or solve a problem
  • Synonym: compensate - to make up for a loss or damage
  • Antonym: wrong - to do harm or treat unjustly

Etymology

From Old French redresser, from re- (again) + dresser (to straighten, arrange).
