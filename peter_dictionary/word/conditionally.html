conditionally

1. ADV

/ kənˈdɪʃənəli / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəli / (BrE)
In a way that depends on certain conditions being met; subject to a condition.

Examples

If you'’re happy, I'm happy.
And that there was something in it for me if I could help him out.

Synset Examples

he accepted the offer conditionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditionally - without conditions or limitations

Etymology

From 'condition' + '-ally', formed from Latin 'condicionem' (agreement, condition).
