etch

1. VERB

/ ɛtʃ / (AmE), / ɛtʃ / (BrE)
to cut or carve a design or image on a surface, especially by using acid or a pointed tool; to make an etching of

Examples

Six red lines etched their way into the gray and vanished.

Synset Examples

He etched her image into the surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrave - to cut or carve words, pictures, or patterns into the surface of something
  • Synonym: inscribe - to write or carve words or symbols on something

Etymology

From Dutch *etsen* (to etch), from German *ätzen* (to eat, corrode).
