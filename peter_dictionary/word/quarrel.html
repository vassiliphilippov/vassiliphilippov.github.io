quarrel

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəl / (BrE)
To argue angrily or have a disagreement, often repeatedly or about something unimportant.

Examples

Few will quarrel with the aim of the schools or with the wording of their curriculum.
This strange person quarrels with a cyclist because the latter is using the path rather than the highroad.
But the eagerly sought "homogeneity "of the Balafrej Council of Government was never achieved as the Istiqlal quarreled over foreign policy, labor politics and economic development.
This is an assumption with which few would be disposed to quarrel.
When we got there, Trig and the Negro were quarreling over possession of a gold crucifix around the neck of a wounded Filipino.

Synset Examples

We quarreled over the question as to who discovered America
These two fellows are always scrapping over something

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argue - to give reasons or cite evidence in support of an idea; to discuss or debate
  • Degree Higher: fight - to engage in a violent struggle or heated argument
  • Degree Lower: bicker - to argue about petty and trivial matters
  • Antonym: agree - to have the same opinion; to be in harmony

Etymology

From Old French 'querele', from Latin 'querela' (complaint).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrəl / (BrE)
A heated argument or disagreement, typically one marked by anger and raised voices.

Examples

On the third voyage, a near mutiny rising from a quarrel between Dutch and English crew members on the Half Moon had almost forced him to head the ship back to Amsterdam in mid Atlantic.

Synset Examples

they had a quarrel
they had words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - an exchange of diverging or opposite views, typically heated or angry
  • Degree Higher: fight - a violent confrontation or struggle; can also mean a heated dispute
  • Degree Lower: discussion - a conversation about something; less heated than a quarrel
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion or feeling; the opposite of a quarrel

Etymology

From Old French 'querele' (complaint, dispute), from Latin 'querela' (complaint).
