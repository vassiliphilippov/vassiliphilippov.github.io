inflexible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈflɛksəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛksəbl / (BrE)
unable or unwilling to change or adapt; not flexible.

Examples

Convinced of the wisdom of their own actions, and reassured by the promises of their economic development programs, governments may fail to push outward to win more and more people to the national effort, becoming instead rigid and inflexible in their policies.

Synset Examples

a man of inflexible purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigid - not able to be changed or adapted; stiff
  • Antonym: flexible - able to change or adapt easily

Etymology

From Latin 'inflexibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'flexibilis' (flexible).
