pick

1. VERB

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
to gather or collect (flowers, fruit, plants, etc.) by searching for and removing them by hand

Examples

Pick the flowers, keep the soil dampened, and each of the pegged-down branches will take root and become a little plant and go on blooming for the rest of the season.
One soft evening - that marvelous sea blessed time when the sun's departing warmth lingers and a smell of spume and wrack haunts everything - Amy had picked herself off the floor and begun to walk.
Whenever she found time, she went blackberry picking with him, and they would come home together, mouths purple, arms and faces scratched, tired enough to forget grief for another day.
They had located the runway of a colony of ants and as the ants came out of the ground, the boys picked them up, one at a time, and pinched them dead.
They're going to pick you up outside Jack Dempsey's in an hour and a half.

Synset Examples

pick mushrooms
pick flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - bring together or collect, especially from scattered locations
  • Synonym: harvest - to gather crops from the fields

Etymology

From Old English *pician* (to prick, pierce, or pick), related to Middle Dutch *picken* (to peck).

2. VERB

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
to move carefully, selecting each step, especially to avoid danger or obstacles

Examples

Soon they were picking their way along the edge of the stream which glowed in the night.
Warren thought of all the men out that night who, like himself, had left their protective ridge and - fear working at their guts - picked their way into the area beyond.
All through July the Discovery picked her way along the 450 - mile long strait, avoiding ice and rocky islands.
Two of the new hands, a Mexican named Jose Amado and a kid known only as Laredo, were picked for the first trick of riding night herd.
"I 'll pick murder.

Synset Examples

She finally picked her successor
He picked his way carefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - to make one's way carefully through (a difficult path)

3. VERB

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
To deliberately provoke or instigate (especially a fight, quarrel, or other trouble).

Examples

With Hudson looking on, his protege Greene picked a fight with the ship's surgeon, Edward Wilson.

Synset Examples

pick a fight or a quarrel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke - to stimulate or incite (someone) to do or feel something, especially by arousing anger
  • Synonym: incite - to encourage or stir up (violent or unlawful behavior)

4. VERB

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
To remove unwanted parts or substances from something, especially feathers from poultry or pits from fruit.

Examples

In the kitchen Glendora was despairingly picking chickens.

Synset Examples

Clean the turkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make something clean by removing unwanted substances

5. NOUN

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
a person or thing that has been chosen or selected from a group

Examples

Accident, murder, suicide - take your pick ".

Synset Examples

he was my pick for mayor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - the act of choosing or the thing chosen
  • Synonym: selection - the action or fact of carefully choosing someone or something as being the best or most suitable

Etymology

From Old English *pician* (to prick, pierce), but this noun sense is related to the verb 'pick' in the sense of 'choose'.

6. VERB

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
to remove something in small pieces or bits, often using the fingers or a tool

Examples

We hold safe little jobs illustrating tooth-paste ads or the salacious incidents in trivial novels, and most of our easel painting is nothing but picking the fluff out of the navel so it can be contemplated in greater purity.

Synset Examples

pick meat from a bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - to remove something (especially hairs or feathers) by pulling it out sharply

Etymology

From Old English *pician* "to prick, pick," of West Germanic origin.

7. NOUN

/ pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
an act of choosing or selecting from a number of options

Examples

So I pick out this guy, go in one night and do his place.

Synset Examples

you can take your pick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - the act of choosing or selecting
  • Synonym: selection - the act of selecting among options

Etymology

From Old English 'pician' (to prick, pierce), later extended to mean 'select'.
