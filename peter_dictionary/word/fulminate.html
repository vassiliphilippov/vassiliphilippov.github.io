fulminate

1. VERB

/ ˈfʊl.məˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈfʌl.mɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to express vehement protest or criticism; to denounce forcefully

Examples

To be sure, when this is pointed out, a common response among certain churchmen is to fulminate about "the little flock "and "the great crowd "and to take solace from Paul's castigation of the "wisdom of the wise "in the opening chapter of First Corinthians.

Synset Examples

He fulminated against the Republicans' plan to cut Medicare
She railed against the bad social policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail - to complain or protest strongly and persistently
  • Synonym: denounce - to publicly declare something wrong or evil

Etymology

From Latin 'fulminare' (to hurl lightning), from 'fulmen' (lightning).
