thriller

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɪlər / (AmE), / ˈθrɪlə / (BrE)
a book, film, or play that tells an exciting story, especially one about crime or espionage, intended to keep the audience in suspense

Examples

A similar tone of underlying futility and despair pervades the spy thrillers of Eric Ambler and dominates the most famous of all American mystery stories, Dashiell Hammett's The Maltese Falcon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - story involving a crime or puzzle to be solved
  • Degree Lower: drama - serious fictional story, often not focused on suspense

Etymology

From English 'thrill' (to excite), with '-er' denoting something that thrills.
