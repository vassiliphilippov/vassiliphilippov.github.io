revert

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to return to a previous state, condition, or way of behaving or thinking

Examples

When I try to work out my reasons for feeling that this passage is of critical significance, I come up with the following ideas, which I shall express very briefly here and revert to in a later essay.
But suppose that a week later we revert to the incident in thought and make our statement again.
If a child had a single drop of Negro blood, he would revert to the ancestral line which, except as slaves under a superior race, had not made one step of progress in 3000 years.

Synset Examples

We reverted to the old rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially after improvement
  • Synonym: regress - to return to a less developed or less advanced state
  • Antonym: progress - to move forward to a more advanced state

Etymology

From Latin 'revertere' (to turn back, return) via Old French 'reverter'.
