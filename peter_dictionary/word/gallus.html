gallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.əs / (BrE)
(usually plural) elastic straps that pass over the shoulders to hold up trousers; braces or suspenders.

Examples

The medical device pirate of today, of course, is a far more sophisticated operator than his predecessor of yesteryear - the gallus snapping hawker of snake oil and other patent medicines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braces - elastic straps used to hold up trousers (UK term)
  • Synonym: suspenders - elastic straps used to hold up trousers (US term)

Etymology

Probably from Scottish dialect, related to 'gallows' from Old Norse 'galgi' (pole, gallows).
