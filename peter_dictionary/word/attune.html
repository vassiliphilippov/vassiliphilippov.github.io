attune

1. VERB

/ əˈtun / (AmE), / əˈtjuːn / (BrE)
to adjust or adapt so as to be in harmony or accord with something; to make receptive or responsive

Examples

A brisk, amusing man, apparently constructed on an ingenious system of spring-joints attuned to the same peppery rhythm as his mind, Smith began his academic career teaching speech to Barnard girls - a project considerably enlivened by his devotion to a recording about "a young rat named Arthur, who never could make up his mind ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonize - to bring into agreement or harmony
  • Synonym: adjust - to alter or move something slightly to achieve the desired fit or result

Etymology

From Middle English 'atune', from 'a-' (on, at) + 'tune'.
