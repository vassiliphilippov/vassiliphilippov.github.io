macabre

1. ADJ

/ məˈkɑb / (AmE), / məˈkɑːb / (BrE)
Involving or depicting death or injury in a way that is disturbing or horrifying; gruesome.

Examples

Within this framework, what followed was strained, even macabre.

Synset Examples

ghastly wounds
the grim aftermath of the bombing
the grim task of burying the victims
a grisly murder
gruesome evidence of human sacrifice
macabre tales of war and plague in the Middle ages
macabre tortures conceived by madmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruesome - causing horror or disgust
  • Synonym: ghastly - shockingly frightful or dreadful
  • Antonym: cheerful - happy and not disturbing

Etymology

From French 'macabre', likely from 'danse macabre' (dance of death), possibly from Medieval Latin 'chorea Machabaeorum' (dance of the Maccabees).
