reconsideration

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.kənˌsɪ.dəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.kənˌsɪd.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of considering something again, especially with the possibility of changing an earlier decision

Examples

Pelham said Sunday night there was research being done on whether the "quickie "vote on the increase can be repealed outright or whether notice would have to first be given that reconsideration of the action would be sought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an act of examining or assessing something again
  • Synonym: deliberation - careful consideration or discussion before a decision

Etymology

From re- (again) + consideration, influenced by Latin roots.
