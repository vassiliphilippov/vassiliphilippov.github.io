propagation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːpəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of spreading a belief, practice, or idea to new areas or among more people

Examples

Resolved that his wrongs and bereavements in Kansas, occasioned by the violence and brutality of those who were intent on the propagation of slavery in that territory, call for a charitable judgment upon his recent efforts in Virginia to undermine the despotism from which he had suffered, and commend his family to the special sympathy and aid of all who pity suffering and reverence justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissemination - the act of spreading something, especially information, widely

Etymology

From Latin propagatio (reproduction, spreading), from propagare (to propagate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of producing offspring or increasing in number by reproduction

Examples

Thus, man can readily deduce that the primary objective end of the conjugal act is procreation, the propagation of the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproduction - the process of producing offspring

Etymology

From Latin propagatio (reproduction, propagation), from propagare (to propagate).
