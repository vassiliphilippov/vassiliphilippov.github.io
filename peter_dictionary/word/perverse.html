perverse

1. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜrs / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːs / (BrE)
Deliberately behaving in a way that is unreasonable or likely to cause trouble, especially by opposing what is expected or desired.

Examples

As if it were perverse to do so!
Then he thought me more perverse than ever.

Synset Examples

took perverse satisfaction in foiling her plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - opposing or resisting what is expected or desired
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or action

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned away, contrary), past participle of pervertere (to turn around, corrupt).
