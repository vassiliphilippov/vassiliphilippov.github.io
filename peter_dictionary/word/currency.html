currency

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːrənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
The state of being widely accepted, used, or in general circulation, especially in reference to ideas, opinions, or practices.

Examples

The popularity of local color literature before the Spanish-American War, the steady currency of the Lincoln myth, the increased emphasis on the frontier west in our mass media are cases in point.

Synset Examples

the currency of ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popularity - The state of being liked or supported by many people
  • Synonym: prevalence - The fact or condition of being widespread
  • Antonym: obsolescence - The state of no longer being in common use

Etymology

From Middle English 'curraunt' (in circulation), from Old French 'corant', present participle of 'courre' (to run), from Latin 'currere'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːrənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the system of money in general use in a particular country, especially in the form of coins and banknotes

Examples

Inside the crown, stuffed behind the stained sweatband, could be seen thin, crumpled wads of currency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money - general term for means of exchange or payment
  • Degree Lower: coin - metal piece used as money
  • Degree Lower: banknote - paper money issued by a bank

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'currens' (running), from Latin 'currere' (to run), originally referring to something in circulation.
