sovereignty

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑvərnti / (AmE), / ˈsɒv(ə)rɪnti / (BrE)
The authority of a state to govern itself or another state; freedom from external control or influence.

Examples

On the one hand, the major European nations had to maintain vis-a-vis each other an emphasis upon sovereignty, independence, formal equality - thus insuring for themselves individually an optimal freedom of action to maintain the "flexibility of alignment "that the system required and to avoid anything approaching a repetition of the disastrous Napoleonic experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomy - independence or self-government, especially of a state
  • Antonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under domination or control

Etymology

From Old French 'souveraineté', from 'souverain' (sovereign), from Latin 'superanus' (chief, principal).
