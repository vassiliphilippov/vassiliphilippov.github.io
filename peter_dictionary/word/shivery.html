shivery

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəri / (BrE)
Very cold; cold enough to cause shivers.

Examples

He came home overheated, ran straight to the ice-chest, and gulped shivery cold water.

Synset Examples

felt all shivery
shivery weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilly - uncomfortably cold
  • Degree Higher: icy - extremely cold, covered with ice
  • Degree Lower: cool - moderately cold

Etymology

From 'shiver' (to shake with cold or fear) + -y (adjective suffix).
