hemosiderin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˈsɪdərɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˈsɪdərɪn / (BrE)
a brownish granular pigment containing iron, formed from the breakdown of hemoglobin in the body

Examples

The endothelial cells lining the sinusoids were prominent, and many contained large quantities of hemosiderin.
Some liver cord cells contained vacuolated cytoplasm, while others had small amounts of brown hemosiderin pigment.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + International Scientific Vocabulary 'sider-' (iron) + '-in' (substance).
