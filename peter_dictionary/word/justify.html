justify

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
To show that something is reasonable, right, or necessary, or to provide good reasons or adequate grounds for it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrant - justify or necessitate (a certain course of action)
  • Synonym: excuse - try to remove blame from; justify with reasons
  • Antonym: condemn - express complete disapproval of; censure

Etymology

From Old French *justifier*, from Latin *iustificare* (to make just).
