silenced

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪlənst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənst / (BrE)
Made silent, especially by force or authority; prevented from speaking or expressing opinions.

Examples

The last two writers introduce strong political bias into their works, and not unlike the union leaders that we will discuss soon, see folklore as a reservoir of protest by a downtrodden and publically silenced mass.

Synset Examples

the silenced crowd waited expectantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quieted - made quieter or calm; subdued
  • Antonym: outspoken - expressing opinions freely and openly

Etymology

From the verb 'silence', itself from Old French 'silence', from Latin 'silentium' (a being silent).
