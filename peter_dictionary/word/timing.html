timing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
The choice, control, or assessment of when something happens, especially in relation to achieving the best result.

Examples

Place kicking is largely a matter of timing, Moritz declared.
Practice helps you to get your timing down.
"It's kind of like golf - if you do n't swing a club very often, your timing gets off ".
The gymnast must develop strength, flexibility, coordination, timing, rhythm, courage, discipline, persistence and the desire for perfection.
His sense for rhythmic variety and timing is impeccable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - A plan for carrying out a process or procedure, giving lists of intended events and times
  • Antonym: mistiming - The occurrence of something at the wrong time

Etymology

From 'time' + -ing, first used in the late 19th century.
