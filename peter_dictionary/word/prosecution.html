prosecution

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːsɪˈkjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒsɪˈkjuːʃən / (BrE)
The act of officially charging someone with a crime and conducting legal proceedings against them in a court of law.

Examples

The ten or more dangerous parties singled out for prosecution were still at large, and Pels realized that if these men entrenched themselves in their adobe houses, defending themselves through loopholes, it would be most difficult to capture them.
This is a prosecution for refusal to be inducted into the armed services, in violation of the provisions of the Universal Military Training and Service Act, 62 Stat. 604, 622, 50 U. S. C. App. 462 (a).
Upon return of the file to the local board, petitioner was again ordered to report for induction and this prosecution followed his failure to do so.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the act of representing the accused against charges in court

Etymology

From Latin prosecutio (a pursuing, continuation), from prosequi (to pursue).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.səˈkjuː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒs.ɪˈkjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the lawyers who conduct the case against a person accused of a crime on behalf of the state or the people

Examples

These questions touch the root of confusion in the prosecution's case.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the lawyers representing the accused person in court

Etymology

From Latin prosecutio (a following, pursuing), from prosequi (to pursue).
