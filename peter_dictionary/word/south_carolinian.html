South Carolinian

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ ˌkæ.rəˈlɪn.i.ən / (AmE), / saʊθ ˌkær.əˈlɪn.i.ən / (BrE)
A native or resident of the state of South Carolina, USA.

Examples

Probably a larger percentage of Virginians and South Carolinians remain unreconstructed than elsewhere, with Georgia, North Carolina, and Alabama following along after them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: South Carolinian - adjective relating to South Carolina or its people

Etymology

From South Carolina, the name of the U.S. state, plus the suffix '-ian' forming demonyms.
