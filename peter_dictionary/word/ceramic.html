ceramic

1. ADJ

/ səˈræmɪk / (AmE), / səˈræmɪk / (BrE)
made of or relating to a hard, brittle material produced by firing clay or similar substances, typically used for pottery, tiles, and other objects.

Examples

For this period, as for earlier centuries, pottery remains the most secure source; the ceramic material of the age is more abundant, more diversified, and more indicative of the hopes and fears of its makers, who begin to show scenes of human life and death.

Synset Examples

a ceramic dish

Etymology

From Greek keramikos, from keramos (potter's clay, pottery).
