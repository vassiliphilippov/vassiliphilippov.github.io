physique

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈzik / (AmE), / fɪˈziːk / (BrE)
the form, size, and development of a person's body

Examples

Undoubtedly you have read the case histories of some of his prize winning pupils (every pupil has a physique title of some kind or other).
Physique contests are rarely won on muscle size alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: build - the physical structure of a person's body
  • Synonym: figure - the shape or form of a person's body

Etymology

From French 'physique' (natural constitution), from Latin 'physica' (natural science).
