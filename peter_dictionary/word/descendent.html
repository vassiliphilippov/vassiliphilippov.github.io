descendent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who is descended from a particular ancestor or group of ancestors

Examples

The latter's real descendents were unable to take root in England; they fled from the Victorian parlor and made their way across the stormy Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • Synonym: offspring - a person's child or descendant

Etymology

From Latin 'descendentem', present participle of 'descendere' meaning 'to descend'.
