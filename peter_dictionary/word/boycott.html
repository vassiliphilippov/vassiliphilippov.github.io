boycott

1. VERB

/ ˈbɔɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪkɒt / (BrE)
to refuse to buy, use, or take part in something as a way of protesting

Examples

No Jew was on hand to boycott his financially struggling theater.
It met a serious rebuff in New Orleans, where the two schools selected for the first moves toward integration were boycotted by white parents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: support - to give approval or encouragement to

Etymology

From Captain Charles Cunningham Boycott (1832–1897), an English land agent in Ireland who was socially ostracized as part of a protest.
