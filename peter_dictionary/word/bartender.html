bartender

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrˌtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːˌtɛndə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to mix and serve alcoholic drinks at a bar.

Examples

Two men murmured with their heads together at the end of the bar, while the sleek headed bartender absently polished a glass.
The bartender measured this situation with heavy eyes and decided he wanted no part of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barman - male bartender, especially in British English
  • Synonym: barmaid - female bartender
  • Antonym: customer - person being served by the bartender

Etymology

From English 'bar' + 'tender' (one who tends or serves), first attested in the 19th century.
