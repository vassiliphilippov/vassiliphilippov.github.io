egotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡəˌtɪzəm, ˈɛɡəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊˌtɪzəm, ˈɛɡəʊˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of talking and thinking about oneself excessively because of an exaggerated sense of one's own importance.

Examples

The two men resemble each other closely in their cunning, their egotism, their relentlessness.
Hercule Poirot and Lord Peter Whimsey (the respective creations of Agatha Christie and Dorothy Sayers) have retained Holmes' egotism but not his zest for life and eccentric habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-importance - having or showing an exaggerated sense of one's own value or importance
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of not being proud or thinking you are better than others

Etymology

From Latin ego (I) + -ism, influenced by French égotisme.
