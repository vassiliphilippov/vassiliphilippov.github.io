opportunistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːpərtuːˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒpəːtjuːˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Taking immediate advantage of any situation, often without regard for ethics or principles.

Examples

Essentially this imposed two conditions: First, international law had to recognize and be compatible with an international political system in which a number of states were competitive, suspicious, and opportunistic in their political alignments with one another; second, it had to be compatible with the value system that they shared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unscrupulous - having or showing no moral principles
  • Antonym: principled - acting in accordance with morality or rules

Etymology

From 'opportunist' (from French opportuniste), ultimately from Latin 'opportunus' (fitting, advantageous).
