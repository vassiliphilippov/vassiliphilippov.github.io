GOP

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː oʊ ˈpiː / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː əʊ ˈpiː / (BrE)
the Grand Old Party; a commonly used acronym for the Republican Party, one of the two major political parties in the United States

Examples

Robert Snodgrass, state GOP chairman, said a meeting held Tuesday night in Blue Ridge brought enthusiastic responses from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Republican Party - one of the two major political parties in the United States
  • Antonym: Democratic Party - the other major political party in the United States

Etymology

Acronym for 'Grand Old Party', a nickname for the Republican Party dating from the late 19th century.
