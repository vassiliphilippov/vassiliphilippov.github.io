shod

1. ADJ

/ ʃɑd / (AmE), / ʃɒd / (BrE)
Wearing footwear, or fitted with a shoe or shoes (especially of horses or other animals).

Examples

The hoofmarks of cattle and the prints of bare feet in the mud or in the dust were as numerous as the traces of shod horses.
Again he stood in the darkness listening, but there was only the scrape of a shod hoof on a plank floor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: barefoot - not wearing any footgear; without shoes

Etymology

Past participle of 'shoe', from Old English 'sceod'.
