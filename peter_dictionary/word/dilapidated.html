dilapidated

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlæpəˌdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a building or object) in a state of disrepair or ruin as a result of age or neglect.

Examples

A second reported that the dilapidated houses in Virginia "look like the latter end of original sin and hard times ".

Synset Examples

a street of bedraggled tenements
a broken-down fence
a ramshackle old pier
a tumble-down shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramshackle - badly constructed or maintained; in disrepair
  • Synonym: tumbledown - falling or likely to fall into ruin
  • Antonym: intact - undamaged, in perfect condition

Etymology

From Latin dilapidare (to scatter like stones), from lapis (stone).
