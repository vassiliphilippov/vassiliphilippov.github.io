astute

1. ADJ

/ əˈstut / (AmE), / əˈstjuːt / (BrE)
having or showing an ability to accurately assess situations or people and turn this to one's advantage; shrewd or clever

Examples

Even so astute a commentator as Harold Clurman of The Nation has said that "Waiting for Godot "is "the concentrate of the contemporary European mood of despair ".

Synset Examples

a smart businessman
an astute tenant always reads the small print in a lease
he was too shrewd to go along with them on a road that could lead only to their overthrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrewd - having sharp judgment; astute
  • Synonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas; astute
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment; easily fooled

Etymology

From Latin *astutus* (crafty, shrewd), from *astus* (craft, cunning).
