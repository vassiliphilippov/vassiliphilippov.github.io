rapidity

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / rəˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being rapid; great speed or swiftness.

Examples

Although the theological forms of the past continue to exist in a way they do not in a more secularized situation, the striking thing is the rapidity with which they are being reduced to a marginal existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
  • Antonym: slowness - the quality or state of being slow

Etymology

From French *rapidité*, from Latin *rapiditas*, from *rapidus* (swift).
