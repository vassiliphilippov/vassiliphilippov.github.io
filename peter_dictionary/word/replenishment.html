replenishment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈplɛnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈplɛnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of refilling or restoring something that has been used up or depleted

Examples

Since there is a continual loss of micrometeoritic material in space because of the radiation effects, there must be a continual replenishment: otherwise, micrometeorites would have disappeared from interplanetary space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refill - to fill something again
  • Antonym: depletion - the reduction or exhaustion of resources

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'repleniss-', stem of 'replenir', from Latin 'replēre' (to fill again).
