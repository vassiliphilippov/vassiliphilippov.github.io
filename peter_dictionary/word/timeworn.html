timeworn

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪmwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmwɔːn / (BrE)
(of a phrase, idea, custom, etc.) having lost freshness or originality because of long use or repetition; stale or overfamiliar.

Examples

The content of his words may lapse back into monotonous repetition, as if a phonograph needle were stuck in one groove; only seldom is it so simple as to be a matter of his obviously parroting some timeworn axiom, common to our culture, which he has evidently heard, over and over, from a parent until he experiences it as part of him.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons
his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor `hard as nails'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackneyed - lacking originality; overused
  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
  • Antonym: fresh - new or original, not stale or overused

Etymology

From 'time' + 'worn', past participle of 'wear', meaning worn by the effects of time.
