unconcerned

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈsɝnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
not worried or anxious; calm and at ease, especially when there is reason to be concerned

Examples

After all, the average American as he lies and waits for the enemy in Korea or as she scans the newspaper in some vain hope of personal contact with the front is unconcerned that his or her plight is the result of a complex of personal, economic and governmental actions far beyond the normal citizen's comprehension and control.

Synset Examples

the prisoner seems entirely unconcerned as to the outcome of the examination

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worried - feeling anxious or troubled about something
  • Antonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'concerned', meaning 'not concerned'. 'Concerned' from 'concern', ultimately from Medieval Latin 'concernere'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈsɝnd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
not interested in or worried about something; showing a lack of concern.

Examples

Again among those jubilantly reunited bunkmates, I was shy with Jessie and acted as I had during those early Saturday mornings when we all seemed to be playing for effect, to be detached and unconcerned with the girls who were properly our dates but about whom, later, in the privacy of our bunks, we would think in terms of the most elaborate romance.
She leaned unconcerned against the broken porch fence, brushing and drying her wet, gilded hair in the sun.
The low compulsive child was one who appeared relatively unconcerned about such matters.

Synset Examples

the average American...is unconcerned that his or her plight is the result of a complex of personal and economic and governmental actions...beyond the normal citizen's comprehension and control
blithely unconcerned about his friend's plight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: concerned - worried or interested; feeling concern
  • Synonym: nonchalant - feeling or appearing casually calm and relaxed; not displaying anxiety, interest, or enthusiasm

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'concerned'.
