proceeding

1. NOUN

/ prəˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
An action taken in a court of law; a series of steps or events in a legal process.

Examples

During the Dorr trial the Democratic press condemned the proceedings and heralded Dorr as a martyr to the principles of the Declaration of Independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawsuit - a claim or dispute brought to a court of law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *procedere* (to proceed).
