outwardly

1. ADV

/ ˈaʊt.wɚd.li / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.wəd.li / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the external appearance or surface, not to what is inside or hidden.

Examples

In direct contrast to the other Rhode Island editors, Samuel S. Foss of the Woonsocket Patriot outwardly condemned the trial as being completely unfair.

Synset Examples

outwardly, the figure is smooth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparently - as far as one knows or can see, seemingly
  • Antonym: inwardly - in the mind or spirit rather than outward appearance

Etymology

From 'outward' + '-ly', with 'outward' from Middle English, from Old English ūtw(e)ard.
