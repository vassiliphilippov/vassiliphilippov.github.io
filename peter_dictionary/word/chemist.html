chemist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
A scientist who studies or specializes in chemistry.

Examples

If the volume is the molal volume, then * * f is obtained on a molal basis which is the customary terminology of the chemists.
In Berlin he published his views of the chemical laws of nature in German and this was issued in French translation (Paris, 1813) under the title Recherches sur l' identite des forces chimiques et electriques, a work held in very high esteem by the new generation of research chemists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacist - person who prepares and dispenses medicinal drugs (British English usage)

Etymology

From French *chimiste*, from *chimie* (chemistry), from Medieval Latin *alchimia* (alchemy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪst / (BrE)
(British) A person whose job is to prepare and sell medicines, especially in a pharmacy.

Examples

The idea had come from Herold, who had once been a chemist's clerk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacist - A person qualified to prepare and dispense medicinal drugs

Etymology

From Latin 'chemista', referring to a practitioner of alchemy or chemistry.
