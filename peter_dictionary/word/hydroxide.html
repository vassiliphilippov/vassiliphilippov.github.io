hydroxide

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the hydroxyl ion (OH−), often formed when an oxide reacts with water

Examples

Hydroxides, orthophosphates, borates, carbonates, and silicates are important inorganic ingredients of hard surface cleaners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxide - a compound of oxygen and another element

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water) and oxide (compound of oxygen).
