bosom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊzəm / (AmE), / ˈbʊzəm / (BrE)
the chest or breasts of a person, especially a woman

Examples

A tall lady, with a ruffled collar very low on her bosom, turned insolent green eyes upon him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest - the front surface of a person's or animal's body between the neck and the abdomen
  • Synonym: breast - either of the two soft, rounded parts on a person's chest

Etymology

From Old English bosm (breast, chest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊzəm / (AmE), / ˈbʊzəm / (BrE)
the part of a garment that covers the chest or breasts

Examples

Joel came and sat mutely with her, sharing her pain and anguish, averting his eyes from the ice packs on her bosom.

Etymology

From Old English 'bosm', related to Old High German 'buosam' (chest, breast).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʊzəm / (AmE), / ˈbʊzəm / (BrE)
A situation or place characterized by close affection and protection; a state of being warmly accepted and cared for.

Examples

Nevertheless, Prokofieff was much influenced by Paris during the Twenties: the Paris which was the artistic center of the Western World - the social Paris to which Russian aristocracy migrated - the chic Paris which attracted the tourist dollars of rich America - the avant-garde Paris of Diaghileff, Stravinsky, Koussevitzky, Cocteau, Picasso - the laissez-faire Paris of Dadaism and ultramodern art - the Paris sympathique which took young composers to her bosom with such quick and easy enthusiasms.

Synset Examples

his willing embrace of new ideas
in the bosom of the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - the act of accepting or supporting willingly and enthusiastically

Etymology

From Old English 'bosm' (breast, bosom), related to German 'Busen'.
