bundle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.dl̩ / (BrE)
A collection of things fastened or wrapped together into a single package for carrying or storing.

Examples

Mrs. Pastern had been given sixteen names, a bundle of literature, and a printed book of receipts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parcel - a package wrapped up for posting or carrying

Etymology

From Middle English 'bondel', from Old French 'bundel', diminutive of 'bunde' (bundle, sheaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌnd(ə)l / (BrE)
a collection of things, such as clothes or papers, fastened or wrapped together

Examples

Here "- She thrust a bundle of keys strung on a thick red cord into Sarah's hand.
They 've found some sort of new evidence, a bundle of clothes or something, and it must link the kid even stronger to the crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - an object or group of objects wrapped in paper or packed in a box

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French *bundle*, perhaps of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌnd(ə)l / (BrE)
to gather or tie things together into a bundle

Examples

Wires whined as a cold November blast rocked the silver wings, but the engine roar was reassuring to the pilot bundled in the open cockpit.
Each morning at five Fogg crawled out of bed to bundle into flying togs over the furnace register of his home.

Synset Examples

he bundled up his few possessions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - to put things into a container for transport or storage
  • Antonym: unwrap - to remove the covering or packaging from something

Etymology

From Middle English 'bondel', diminutive of 'bund' (a bundle, sheaf), probably from Middle Dutch.
