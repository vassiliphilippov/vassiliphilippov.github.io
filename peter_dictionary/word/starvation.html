starvation

1. NOUN

/ stɑrˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / stɑːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
extreme hunger caused by a prolonged lack of food or essential nutrients, often resulting in severe weakness or death.

Examples

Inevitably, Mrs. Hull died of starvation and tuberculosis, weighing 60 pounds.
When she reached port, she was found to have on board only eight men, all near starvation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malnutrition - poor nutrition caused by insufficient or unbalanced diet

Etymology

From starve + -ation, modeled after Latin roots.
