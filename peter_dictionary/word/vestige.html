vestige

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small remaining part or sign of something that once existed; a trace or remnant

Examples

Some time in 1912, Picasso cut out and folded a piece of paper in the shape of a guitar; to this he glued and fitted other pieces of paper and four taut strings, thus creating a sequence of flat surfaces in real and sculptural space to which there clung only the vestige of a picture plane.

Synset Examples

there wasn't a trace of evidence for the claim
a tincture of condescension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount; a sign remaining from the past
  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'vestigium' (footprint, trace, track).
