regal

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈriːɡəl / (BrE)
Of, resembling, or suitable for a king or queen; royal; majestic.

Examples

And the stiffly regal look of them, she saw grimly, lacked the quaver of age which, thwarting the efforts of her amazing will, ran through her spoken words like a thin ragged string.

Synset Examples

golden age of imperial splendor
purple tyrant
regal attire
treated with royal acclaim
the royal carriage of a stag's head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal - relating to or belonging to a king or queen
  • Synonym: imperial - relating to or characteristic of an empire or emperor
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; not proud or majestic

Etymology

From Latin *regalis* (royal), from *rex* (king).
