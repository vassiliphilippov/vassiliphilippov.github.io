mash

1. VERB

/ mæʃ / (AmE), / mæʃ / (BrE)
to crush or squeeze something into a soft, pulpy, or flattened state, typically by applying pressure

Examples

She swung her eyes up to the blue of the window, her jaws gently mashing the bitter beans.

Synset Examples

crush an aluminum can
squeeze a lemon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - to press or squeeze something so hard that it is damaged or loses its shape
  • Synonym: squash - to press or squeeze something with force so that it becomes flat or loses its shape
  • Synonym: grind - to reduce to small particles by crushing

Etymology

From Middle English 'mashe', probably from Old English 'masc', related to the action of crushing grain.
