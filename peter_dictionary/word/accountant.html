accountant

1. NOUN

/ əˈkaʊntənt / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntənt / (BrE)
A person whose job is to keep or inspect financial accounts, especially for businesses or organizations.

Examples

This patient was a 65 - year old white male accountant who entered the New York Hospital for his fourth and terminal admission on June 26, 1959, because of disabling weakness and general debility.
Our accountants are looking into it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookkeeper - person who records the financial transactions of a business
  • Synonym: auditor - person who formally inspects financial accounts

Etymology

From Old French *acompte* (account) and the agent suffix *-ant*, from Latin *computare* (to calculate).
