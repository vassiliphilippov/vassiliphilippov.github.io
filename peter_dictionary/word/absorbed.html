absorbed

1. ADJ

/ əbˈzɔrbd / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbd / (BrE)
(of energy, such as light or radiation) taken in by a material without being reflected.

Examples

The rate of the gas phase exchange reaction appears to be proportional to the first power of the absorbed light intensity indicating that the radical intermediates are removed at the walls or by reaction with an impurity rather than by bimolecular radical combination reactions.

Synset Examples

the absorbed light intensity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflected - (of light, etc.) thrown back from a surface

Etymology

From the verb 'absorb,' from Latin absorbere (to swallow up).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈsɔrbd, æbˈsɔrbd / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbd, æbˈzɔːbd / (BrE)
Deeply interested or involved; giving all of one's attention to something.

Examples

"This is my hen ledger ", he informed him in an absorbed way.
As a boy in a local school he was shy and solitary, absorbed in his fondness for nature and his visions of Sweden's ancient glory.

Synset Examples

that engrossed look or rapt delight
then wrapped in dreams
so intent on this fantastic...narrative that she hardly stirred
rapt with wonder
wrapped in thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrossed - deeply involved or occupied
  • Synonym: preoccupied - lost in thought; deeply absorbed
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate; having attention diverted

Etymology

From Latin absorbere (to swallow up), via Middle French absorber.
