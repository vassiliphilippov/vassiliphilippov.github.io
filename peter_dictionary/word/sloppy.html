sloppy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɑpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
Untidy, careless, or lacking neatness and order.

Examples

She was personally sloppy, and when she had colds would blow her nose in the same handkerchief all day and keep it, soaking wet, dangling from her waist, and when she gardened she would eat dinner with dirt on her calves.
Was she just naturally sloppy about everything but her physical appearance?
He got sloppy.

Synset Examples

a sloppy room
sloppy habits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messy - untidy or disorderly
  • Antonym: neat - tidy and orderly

2. ADJ

/ ˈslɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
Covered in or smeared with spilled liquid or a moist substance.

Examples

I owe that boy a great big, sloppy kiss when I see him.

Synset Examples

a sloppy floor
a sloppy saucer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messy - untidy or dirty, especially as a result of spilled material
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

3. ADJ

/ ˈslɑpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
Showing a lack of care, attention, or effort; careless or untidy.

Examples

Says the neighborhood'’s getting sloppy.

Synset Examples

a most haphazard system of record keeping
slapdash work
slipshod spelling
sloppy workmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - failing to give sufficient attention or thought; negligent
  • Antonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
