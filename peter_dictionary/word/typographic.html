typographic

1. ADJ

/ taɪ.pəˈɡræf.ɪk / (AmE), / taɪ.pəˈɡræf.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the style, appearance, or arrangement of printed matter; of or pertaining to typography.

Examples

The result of this attitude has been the domination of many orthography conferences by such considerations as typographic' esthetics', which usually turns out to be nothing more than certain prejudices carried over from European languages.

Synset Examples

the typographic art
a typographical error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typographical - relating to typography (alternative form)

Etymology

From French 'typographique', from Greek 'typos' (impression, mark, type) + 'graphia' (writing).
