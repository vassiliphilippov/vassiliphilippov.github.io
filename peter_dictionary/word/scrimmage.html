scrimmage

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in American football) a practice game or match between two squads of the same team.

Examples

"We 'll work hard Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday ", Meek said, "and probably will have a good scrimmage Friday.

Etymology

Alteration of 'skirmish'.

2. VERB

/ ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to play a practice game, especially in sports, as preparation or training

Examples

The game players saw the Air Force film Monday, ran for 30 minutes, then went in, while the reserves scrimmaged for 45 minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - to perform an activity repeatedly or regularly to improve one's skill

Etymology

From earlier 'scrimmage', a variant of 'skirmish', from Old French 'eskirmir' (to fight with a sword).
