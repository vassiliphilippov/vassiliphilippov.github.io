secrete

1. VERB

/ sɪˈkrit / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːt / (BrE)
(of a cell or gland) to produce and release a substance, such as a hormone or enzyme, especially as part of a biological process.

Examples

There it seems that the goitrogen ingested by dairy animals is itself inactive but is converted in the animal to an active goitrogen, which is then secreted in the milk.

Synset Examples

secrete digestive juices
release a hormone into the blood stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excrete - to eliminate waste material from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'secretus', past participle of 'secernere' (to separate, distinguish).
