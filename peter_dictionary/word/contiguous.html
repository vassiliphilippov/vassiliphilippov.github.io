contiguous

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪɡjuəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪɡjuəs / (BrE)
sharing a common border or touching; very close or connected in space or time.

Examples

Substances other than detergent actives also tend to be strongly sorbed from aqueous media onto surfaces of other contiguous condensed phases.

Synset Examples

contiguous events
immediate contact
the immediate vicinity
the immediate past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else
  • Antonym: separate - not touching or connected; distinct

Etymology

From Latin 'contiguus', from 'contingere' meaning 'to touch, be in contact with'.
