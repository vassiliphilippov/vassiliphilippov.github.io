noun

1. NOUN

/ naʊn / (AmE), / naʊn / (BrE)
A word that refers to a person, place, thing, or idea, and can function as the subject or object of a verb, the object of a preposition, or be used in apposition.

Examples

All the other parts of speech: nouns, pronouns, adjectives, adverbs, and conjunctions operated under the same system as the verbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verb - word class describing actions or states
  • Synonym: pronoun - word class that substitutes for nouns

Etymology

From Latin 'nomen' (name).

2. NOUN

/ naʊn / (AmE), / naʊn / (BrE)
A word that is used to identify a person, place, thing, quality, or idea.

Examples

In the second sentence if drinking water is a gerundial clause and without drinking water is roughly equivalent in meaning to unless I drink water, there will be stronger stress on water than on drinking; but if drinking is a gerundial noun modifying water and without drinking water is equivalent to without water for drinking, there will be stronger stress on drinking than on water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verb - word used to describe an action, state, or occurrence
  • Antonym: adjective - word that describes a noun
  • Antonym: pronoun - word that takes the place of a noun

Etymology

From Latin 'nomen' (name) via Old French 'nom'.
