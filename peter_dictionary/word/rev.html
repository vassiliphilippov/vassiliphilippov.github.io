rev

1. VERB

/ rɛv / (AmE), / rɛv / (BrE)
(of an engine or its operator) to increase the speed of rotation, especially by pressing the accelerator.

Examples

Once the soldiers from the barracks had to hold the ship from blowing away while Fogg revved the engine and got the tail up.

Synset Examples

rev up an engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accelerate - to increase speed

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'revolution' (in the sense of one complete turn of a shaft).
