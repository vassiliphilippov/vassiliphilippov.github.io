unbalance

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈbæləns / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæl(ə)ns / (BrE)
A lack of balance; a state in which equilibrium is disturbed.

Examples

A small pressure unbalance displaces the diaphragm and changes the capacitance between the diaphragm and an electrically insulated plate spaced 0.001 in. apart (for * * f).

Synset Examples

a hormonal imbalance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imbalance - lack of balance or symmetry; state of disequilibrium
  • Antonym: balance - a condition in which different elements are equal or in the correct proportions

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'balance', meaning not balanced.
