harrow

1. VERB

/ ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhærəʊ / (BrE)
to break up and smooth out the surface of soil using a harrow after ploughing

Examples

His soil was "nothing special ", just prairie land, but he had harrowed in compost until it was loose, spongy and brown black.
For larger plantings, you 'll need some kind of power for plowing, harrowing, disking, and cultivating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plough - to turn over the earth with a plough

Etymology

From Old English *hergian* (to ravage), later influenced by Old Norse *herfi* (a rake, harrow).
