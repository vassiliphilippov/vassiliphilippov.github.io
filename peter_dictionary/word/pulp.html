pulp

1. NOUN

/ pʌlp / (AmE), / pʌlp / (BrE)
the soft, moist, edible part inside a fruit or vegetable, often surrounding the seeds

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flesh - The soft substance of a fruit or vegetable, similar in meaning to pulp

Etymology

From Latin pulpa (flesh, soft part).
