mud

1. NOUN

/ mʌd / (AmE), / mʌd / (BrE)
Soft, wet earth or soil, often formed after rain or near water, that is sticky or slippery.

Examples

Because of its important game with Arkansas coming up Saturday, Baylor worked out in the rain Monday - mud or no mud.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: clay - fine-grained natural soil material that is plastic when wet
  • Degree Higher: sludge - thick, soft, wet mixture or deposit; often viscous mud

Etymology

From Middle English *mudde*, of unknown origin.
