switchboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪtʃbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈswɪtʃbɔːd / (BrE)
a board or panel used for connecting telephone circuits manually, by plugging and unplugging wires; historically used in telephone exchanges

Examples

A small single switchboard was installed in the Village over Woodcock's hardware store (later E. H. Hemenway's).

Etymology

From 'switch' (to change the direction of a circuit) + 'board' (panel).
