deformational

1. ADJ

/ dɪˌfɔrˈmeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˌfɔːˈmeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to or causing change in the shape or size of a material body or geometric figure.

Examples

This has an interesting analogy with the assumption stated by Philippoff that "the deformational mechanics of elastic solids can be applied to flowing solutions ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformation - the act or process of changing shape or form

Etymology

From 'deformation' + '-al'. 'Deformation' from Latin 'deformatio' (a disfiguring).
