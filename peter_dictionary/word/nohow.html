nohow

1. ADV

/ ˈnoʊˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌhaʊ / (BrE)
In no way; not at all; by no means.

Examples

But before I leave, I do want to say this, that these hearings on the Mafia are in no way whatsoever a slur upon the great Italian people.

Synset Examples

We could nohow make out his handwriting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nowise - not at all; in no way

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'how', using 'no' as a negative prefix; attested since the 17th century.
