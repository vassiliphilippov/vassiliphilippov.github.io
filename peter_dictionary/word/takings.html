takings

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɪŋz / (BrE)
money received as income, especially from sales or business transactions; revenue or proceeds

Examples

The revenue from O' Banion's Cicero territory went up still higher, until the yield was more than the Torrio-Capone takings from the far bigger trade area of Chicago's South and West Sides.

Synset Examples

the average return was about 5%

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenue - income, especially of a company or organization
  • Synonym: gross - total income before deductions
  • Degree Lower: profit - financial gain after expenses

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'take' with the agent noun suffix '-ing' and plural ending, indicating things that have been taken (i.e., received as income).
