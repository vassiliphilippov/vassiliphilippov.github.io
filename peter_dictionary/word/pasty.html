pasty

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪsti / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsti / (BrE)
(of a person's face or skin) pale and unhealthy in appearance; resembling paste in color or texture.

Examples

Under the trees, there was a dead redcoat, a young boy with a pasty white skin and a face full of pimples, who had taken a rifle ball directly between the eyes.

Synset Examples

he looked pasty and red-eyed
a complexion that had been pastelike was now chalky white

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallid - pale, typically because of poor health
  • Synonym: wan - pale and giving the impression of illness or exhaustion
  • Antonym: florid - having a red or flushed complexion

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪsti / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsti / (BrE)
having a sticky or adhesive consistency, like paste or glue

Examples

Water from the snow and water from the towels had run off the kid to the table where the dough was, and the dough was turning pasty, sticking to the kid's back and behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticky - having a thick, glue-like texture; tending to adhere

Etymology

From paste + -y, meaning 'resembling paste'.
