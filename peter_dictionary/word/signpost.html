signpost

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnpəʊst / (BrE)
a post by the side of a road or path, with a sign giving information or directions

Examples

Whole platoons were taking up new positions on the steps, arriving and departing, while I stayed glued, like a signpost, to one spot.
And there isn'’t even a plaque, or a signpost or statue of him in that town.

Etymology

From sign + post; 'sign' (from Latin signum) and 'post' (from Latin postis, doorpost).
