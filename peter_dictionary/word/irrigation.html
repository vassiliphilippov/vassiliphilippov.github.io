irrigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
The artificial application of water to land to assist in the growth of crops.

Examples

- In addition to its recreation interests, water is needed for drinking, sanitation, and irrigation.
Lack of rainfall and extreme temperatures may call for the development of shade and irrigation of a site to make it useable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watering - supplying water to plants

Etymology

From Latin 'irrigatio', from 'irrigare' (to water, to moisten).
