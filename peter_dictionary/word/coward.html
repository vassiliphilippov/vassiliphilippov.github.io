coward

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊərd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəd / (BrE)
a person who lacks courage or is excessively afraid of danger or pain

Examples

He had come because he could not live out his life feeling that he had been a coward.
"Just let me meet up with that damned bushwhackin' coward face-to-face "! he exploded.
"Does this make it any easier, coward "?
This dirty coward just admitted killing Arbuckle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brave - someone showing courage or fearlessness
  • Antonym: hero - a person noted for courageous acts or nobility of character

Etymology

From Old French 'couard', from 'coe' (tail), implying someone who turns tail and flees.
