entomb

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtuːm / (AmE), / ɪnˈtuːm / (BrE)
to place a dead body in a tomb or grave, often with a sense of enclosing or sealing it

Examples

In the clay are entombed millions of pollen grains and spores which came from plants growing in the region at the time.

Synset Examples

Stalin was buried behind the Kremlin wall on Red Square
The pharaohs were entombed in the pyramids
My grandfather was laid to rest last Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bury - to put a dead body into the ground
  • Antonym: exhume - to remove a body from the ground or from a tomb

Etymology

From Old French 'entomber', from 'en-' (in) + 'tombe' (tomb).
