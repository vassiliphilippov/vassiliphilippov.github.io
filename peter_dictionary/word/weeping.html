weeping

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of shedding tears, often with audible sounds such as sobbing; crying.

Examples

The beehive voices, for no one could bear silence, drowned out the sound of Mrs. Lincoln's weeping.

Synset Examples

I hate to hear the crying of a child
she was in tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying - the act of shedding tears, typically with sound

Etymology

Present participle of 'weep', from Old English *wēpan* (to weep).
