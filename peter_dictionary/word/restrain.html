restrain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from doing something; to hold back or control.

Examples

He may respect too much the Italian tradition of letting singers hold on to their notes, but to restrain them in a singers' opera may be quite difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhibit - to hold back or restrain an impulse, action, or person
  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to the activities or existence of; to hold back
  • Antonym: release - to set free or allow to move, act, or flow freely

Etymology

From Old French *restreindre*, from Latin *restringere* (to bind fast, check, restrain).
