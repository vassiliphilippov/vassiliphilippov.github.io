recess

1. VERB

/ rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
to set something into a hollow space or niche so that it is sunken below or within a surface

Examples

Recess base slightly to allow room for stopper.
Her permanent titanium shell was recessed behind an even more indestructible barrier in the central shaft of the scout ship.

Synset Examples

recess lights

Etymology

From Latin recessus (retreat, withdrawal), from recedere (to go back).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriː.sɛs, rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / ˈriː.sɛs, rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
A temporary suspension or break in proceedings, especially during a meeting, court, or legislative session.

Examples

The committee will stand in recess until 10:00 a.m. Monday morning, at which time the committee will produce a witness who'’ll corroborate the charges which are made against you today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjournment - a suspension or postponement of proceedings to another time or place

Etymology

From Latin recessus (a going back, a retreat), from recedere (to go back).
