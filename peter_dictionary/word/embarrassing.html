embarrassing

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbæɹəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing discomfort, difficulty, or shame; awkward or hard to deal with, especially in a way that makes someone feel self-conscious or uneasy.

Examples

Or does he sincerely want to tap the real springs of American attitude and culture regardless of how unpopular and embarrassing they may be?
Fortunately, the embarrassing questions raised by objects do not need to be answered, or we would all have to go sleep in the open fields.
Complicity is an embarrassing word.

Synset Examples

awkward (or embarrassing or difficult) moments in the discussion
an awkward pause followed his remark
a sticky question
in the unenviable position of resorting to an act he had planned to save for the climax of the campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - causing or feeling embarrassment or inconvenience
  • Synonym: difficult - not easy; requiring much effort or skill
  • Antonym: comfortable - giving ease or relaxation; free from embarrassment or pain

Etymology

From French *embarrasser* (to block, hamper) and Italian *imbarazzare*; related to 'embarrass'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəsɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to feel self-conscious, ashamed, or awkward.

Examples

It bulks under a veil of thin, new grass, like some embarrassing fact of physicalness, and I think Mrs. Pastern set out the statuary to soften its meaning.
Griffith said, "Hoag told me to tell you "- he waited until they were close; it was hideously embarrassing - "not to let the prime minister be alone with Mahzeer ".

Synset Examples

the embarrassing moment when she found her petticoat down around her ankles
it was mortifying to know he had heard every word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mortifying - causing great embarrassment or shame
  • Synonym: awkward - causing difficulty, embarrassment, or discomfort
  • Antonym: comforting - making one feel less worried or upset
  • Antonym: consoling - giving comfort or solace

Etymology

From French *embarrasser* (to hamper, make difficult), from Italian *imbarazzare*, from *bara* (bar, obstacle).
