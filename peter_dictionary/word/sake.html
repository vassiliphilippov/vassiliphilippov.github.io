sake

1. NOUN

/ seɪk / (AmE), / seɪk / (BrE)
the purpose or reason for which something is done, or in whose interest something is done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created
  • Synonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From Old English *sacu* (a cause, lawsuit, dispute), related to Old Norse *sǫk* (cause, case).
