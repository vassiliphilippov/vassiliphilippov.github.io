proportionally

1. ADV

/ prəˈpɔrʃənəli / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənəli / (BrE)
In a way that corresponds in size, amount, or degree to something else; in proportion.

Examples

What we have left as reasonably comparable are four classes: (1) body parts and products, which with a proportionally nearly even representation (51 terms out of 253, 25 out of 100) come out with nearly even ratios; 2.6 and 2.7; (2) Nature (29 terms against 17), ratios 3.3 versus 4.1; (3) adjectives (16, 15 terms), ratios 3.9 versus 4.7; (4) verbs (9, 22 terms), ratios 4.0 versus 3.4.

Synset Examples

your salary will rise proportionately to your workload

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionately - to a proportionate degree; in due proportion

Etymology

Formed from 'proportional' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin proportio (proportion).
