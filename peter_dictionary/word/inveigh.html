inveigh

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈveɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪ / (BrE)
to speak or write with great hostility about something; to complain strongly and bitterly

Examples

So, while we properly inveigh against the new poisoning, history is not likely to justify the pose of righteousness which some in the West were so quick to assume when Mr. Khrushchev made his cynical and irresponsible threat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail - to complain or protest strongly and persistently
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'invehi' (to attack with words), from 'in-' (against) + 'vehi' (to ride, carry).
