tender

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛndər / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
showing gentleness, sympathy, or affection; easily moved by feelings of compassion or emotion.

Examples

These little songs, however, were sweet nothings from the heart, tender memories of his childhood, little melodies that anyone could hum and that would make one want to weep.

Synset Examples

a tender heart
a tender smile
tender loving care
tender memories
a tender mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament
  • Synonym: soft-hearted - easily moved to pity or compassion; tender in nature
  • Antonym: callous - emotionally insensitive; showing a lack of sympathy or concern for others
  • Antonym: hard-hearted - lacking in sympathy or kindness; unfeeling

Etymology

From Middle English 'tendre', from Old French, from Latin 'tener' (delicate, tender).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
(of food) easy to cut or chew; not tough.

Examples

There's no greater catastrophe in the universe, she reflected dourly, impaling tender green beans on the silver fork, than the dwindling away of a family.

Synset Examples

tender beef

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tough - difficult to cut or chew (especially food)

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) painful or sensitive to touch; easily hurt.

Examples

Our Northern brethren also I believe felt a little tender under those censures; for though their people had very few slaves themselves, yet they had been pretty considerable carriers of them to others ".

Synset Examples

the tender spot on his jaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sore - painful or aching; tender to touch

Etymology

From Old French tendre, from Latin tener (soft, delicate, tender).

4. VERB

/ ˈtɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
to formally offer or present something for acceptance, especially in a legal or official context

Examples

What a joy to realize that we, too, can claim this promise tendered by the Lord during His earthly ministry to a group of men who were very dear to Him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - to present or proffer something for acceptance
  • Synonym: submit - to present for consideration or judgment

Etymology

From Old French 'tendre', from Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, extend).
