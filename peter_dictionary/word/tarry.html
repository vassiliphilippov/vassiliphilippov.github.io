tarry

1. VERB

/ ˈtæri / (AmE), / ˈtæri / (BrE)
to delay leaving a place; to stay or linger somewhere, especially when unnecessary or unexpected

Examples

Ensign Vesole decided that he would not tarry until he heard the whispering of the bombs, and when night began to fall, he put Seaman 2 / c Donald L. Norton and Seaman 1 / c William A. Rochford on the guns and told them to start shooting the moment they saw an enemy silhouette.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary, similar in meaning to tarry
  • Antonym: depart - to leave a place, the opposite action to tarry

Etymology

From Middle English tarien, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old French tarier (to delay).
