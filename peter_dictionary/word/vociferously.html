vociferously

1. ADV

/ voʊˈsɪfərəsli / (AmE), / vəʊˈsɪfərəsli / (BrE)
In a loud, forceful, and insistent manner, often expressing strong feelings or demands.

Examples

Styka nodded and sniffed and blew his nose vociferously.

Synset Examples

he complained vociferously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudly - in a loud manner

Etymology

From Latin 'vociferari' (to shout), from 'vox' (voice) + 'ferre' (to bear).
