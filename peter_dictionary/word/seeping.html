seeping

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid, gas, or other substance) leaking or flowing out slowly through small openings or porous material.

Examples

A seeping coldness entered Holden's being; his nerves seemed frost-bitten down to the tips of his tingling fingers and his spine felt stiff and glass like, liable to break like an icicle at any moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oozing - leaking out slowly, especially as a thick liquid

Etymology

Present participle of 'seep', from Middle English 'sepen', of uncertain origin.
