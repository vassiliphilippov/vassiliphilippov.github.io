bust

1. VERB

/ bʌst / (AmE), / bʌst / (BrE)
(informal) To break, damage, or ruin something completely.

Examples

He do n't care at all, and I do n't care to get my head busted neither.

Synset Examples

He busted my radio!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain

2. VERB

/ bʌst / (AmE), / bʌst / (BrE)
to break open or apart suddenly and forcefully; to smash

Examples

Hadley busted his head up pretty good.

Synset Examples

The dam burst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to separate or cause to separate into pieces
  • Synonym: smash - to break into pieces violently
  • Synonym: burst - to break open suddenly from internal pressure

Etymology

Originally American slang, variant of 'burst.'
