squawk

1. VERB

/ skwɑk / (AmE), / skwɔːk / (BrE)
(of a bird or person) to make a loud, harsh, abrupt scream or cry.

Examples

Could n't take them near a river, though, or they 'd squawk like a turkey cock the day before Thanksgiving ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - utter a high-pitched loud scream
  • Synonym: scream - give a long, loud cry
  • Antonym: murmur - speak or make a quiet, gentle sound

Etymology

Imitative in origin, resembling the sound itself.
