utilitarian

1. ADJ

/ juˌtɪlɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / juːˌtɪlɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
Designed to be useful or practical rather than attractive.

Examples

"One of the things which makes our lives drab and empty and which leaves us, at the end of the day, fatigued and deflated spiritually is the pressure of the taxing, practical, utilitarian concern of common-sense objects.

Synset Examples

utilitarian steel tables

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice

Etymology

From utilitarian, coined in English (late 18th century) from Latin utilis (useful) + -arian.
