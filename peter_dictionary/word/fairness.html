fairness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfeə.nəs / (BrE)
Impartial and just treatment or behavior in accordance with rules or standards.

Examples

Those who feared "emotionalism "at the Trial showed less understanding than Dr. Servatius of the route by which man achieves the distance necessary for fairness toward enemies.

Synset Examples

the judge recognized the fairness of my claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justice - quality of being fair and reasonable
  • Synonym: impartiality - equal treatment of all rivals or disputants
  • Antonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against something

Etymology

From Middle English 'feirnesse', from 'fair' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɛənəs / (BrE)
the quality of making judgments that are free from bias, discrimination, or dishonesty

Examples

So in all fairness, this statement should be heard!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the quality of treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • Antonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group

Etymology

From Middle English 'feyrnesse', from Old English 'fæġernes', meaning beauty or fairness.
