skid

1. VERB

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or person) to slide uncontrollably, especially sideways, on a slippery surface.

Examples

At Jenks street, Simms said, the car skidded completely around, just missed two parked cars, and sped east in Jenks.
An auto overturned, skidding into a stopped tractor-trailer and burst into flames near Snellville, the patrol said.
Dollar bills skidded off my hands and they tipped their caps politely.

Synset Examples

the car skidded in the curve on the wet road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian sources; related to Old Norse 'skíða' (to make a skid, to slide).
