hyalinization

1. NOUN

/ haɪəˌlɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪəˌlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A process in which tissue becomes glassy and translucent due to the accumulation of certain proteins within cells or the intercellular space, often seen in pathological conditions.

Examples

Throughout, there were marked arteriolosclerosis and hyalinization of afferent glomerular arterioles.

Synset Examples

the patient's arterioles showed marked hyalinization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyaline - glassy, translucent substance found in tissues

Etymology

From hyaline + -ization, indicating the process of becoming glass-like.
