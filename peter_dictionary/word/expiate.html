expiate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛkspiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkspɪeɪt / (BrE)
to show remorse or make amends for guilt or wrongdoing; to atone for

Examples

What was he expiating?

Synset Examples

expiate one's sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atone - to make amends or reparation for a wrongdoing
  • Antonym: condone - to accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong

Etymology

From Latin expiare (to atone for, to purify by sacrifice), from ex- (out) + piare (to appease).
