convoy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
A group of vehicles, especially motor vehicles or ships, traveling together for protection or convenience.

Examples

A further example of the incompatible difference in personalities was when two policemen held up a Torrio beer convoy on a West Side street and demanded $ 300 to let it through.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravan - a group of vehicles or people traveling together, especially across a desert

Etymology

From French 'convoyer', from Late Latin 'conviare' (to accompany on the way).
