marvel

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑrvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvəl / (BrE)
to be filled with wonder or astonishment at something; to be amazed

Examples

We marvel at their blindness for not seeing this.
Motorists like myself who can remember the old "tourists accommodated "signs on farm houses and village homes before World War 2, can only marvel at the great size and the luxury of the relatively new and fast-grossing motel business.
There can be no doubt that the American Catholic accomplishment in the field of higher education is most impressive: our European brethren never cease to marvel at the number and the size of our colleges and universities.
Chaplin, 71, who met K. when the Soviet boss visited England in 1956, confided that he hopes to visit Russia some time this summer because "I have marveled at your grandiose experiment and I believe in your future ".

Synset Examples

We marvelled at the child's linguistic abilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - to feel surprise or admiration; to marvel
  • Synonym: gape - to stare with open mouth in astonishment; often with amazement
  • Synonym: admire - to regard with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Old French 'merveiller', from 'merveille' (wonder, marvel), from Latin 'mirabilia' (wonderful things), from 'mirari' (to wonder).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑrvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːvəl / (BrE)
to express great surprise or admiration about something

Examples

A Protestant woman marveled to me over the large crowds going in and out of the Birmingham Oratory (Catholic) Church on Sunday mornings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - to feel or express surprise, curiosity, or doubt

Etymology

From Old French *merveiller*, from Latin *mirari* (to wonder at).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑr.vəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.vəl / (BrE)
A person or thing that causes great surprise or admiration; something wonderful.

Examples

She found this a marvel because, as she said, only six per cent of English people are churchgoers.

Synset Examples

the wonders of modern science

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - something surprising or remarkable
  • Synonym: miracle - an extraordinary and welcome event believed to be caused by a divine power

Etymology

From Old French *merveille* (wonder), from Latin *mirabilia* (wonderful things).
