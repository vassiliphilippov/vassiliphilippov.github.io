leafhopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːfˌhɑpər / (AmE), / ˈliːfˌhɒpə / (BrE)
A small, leaping insect that feeds on plant sap and can transmit plant diseases.

Examples

Wound tumor virus is a leafhopper transmitted virus not easily transmissible by mechanical inoculation (Black, 1944; Brakke et al., 1954).

Etymology

From 'leaf' + 'hopper', indicating its habit of jumping on leaves.
