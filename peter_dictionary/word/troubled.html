troubled

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌbəld / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəld / (BrE)
Experiencing or marked by unrest, disorder, or disturbance.

Examples

The Szolds, like the Marches, enjoyed and loved living together, even in troubled times; and, as in the March home, any young man who called on the Szolds found himself confronted with a phalanx of femininity which made it rather difficult to direct his particular attention to any one of them.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance, calm
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion

Etymology

From Middle English 'troublen', from Old French 'troubler', from Vulgar Latin *turbulare.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌbəld / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəld / (BrE)
Experiencing or showing distress, worry, or difficulty; beset by problems or danger.

Examples

Troubled, he continued along the corridor, poking his head into the next office for a careful look around.

Synset Examples

troubled areas
fell into a troubled sleep
a troubled expression
troubled teenagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressed - feeling or showing anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Antonym: untroubled - not feeling or affected by trouble or distress

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'trouble' + past participle ending '-ed'. 'Trouble' from Old French *troubler*, from Late Latin *turbulare*.
