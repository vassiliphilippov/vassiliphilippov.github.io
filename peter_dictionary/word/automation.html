automation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The use of machines and advanced technology to control processes and operate equipment, often replacing human labor.

Examples

With greater investments in plant facilities, with automation growing, you can n't switch around, either in volume or in product design, as much as was formerly possible - or at least not as economically.
New computer and automation techniques were applied to these spectra with considerable success.

Synset Examples

automation replaces human workers by machines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robotics - the technology dealing with the design, construction, and operation of robots
  • Antonym: manual - done by hand or human effort rather than by machine

Etymology

From automate + -ion, first used in the mid-20th century.
