depletion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpliːʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
the reduction in the number or quantity of something, especially through consumption, use, or loss

Examples

If you are a life tenant, you deduct allowable depreciation and depletion.
If you are an income beneficiary of property held in trust or an heir, legatee, or devisee, you may deduct allowable depreciation and depletion, if not deductible by the estate or trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • Antonym: augmentation - the act of increasing something

Etymology

From Latin 'deplere' (to empty, reduce) via Late Latin 'depletio'.
