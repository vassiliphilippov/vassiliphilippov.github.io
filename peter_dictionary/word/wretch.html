wretch

1. NOUN

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
A person in a very unhappy or unfortunate state, often deserving of pity or sympathy.

Examples

And what of that poor tarred and feathered wretch he had seen on the road driving down from Schuyler's?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserable - feeling very unhappy or uncomfortable; pitiable
  • Synonym: unfortunate - person suffering from bad luck

Etymology

From Old English wrecca (exile, stranger), related to 'wreck'.
