philanthropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪlənˈθrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪlænˈθrɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or action) seeking to promote the welfare of others, especially by donating money or resources to good causes.

Examples

In reference to Brown's raid she wrote, "though we are non-resistants and religiously believe it better to reform by moral and not by carnal weapons we know thee was anemated [ sic ] by the most generous and philanthropic motives ".

Synset Examples

a benevolent contributor
eleemosynary relief
philanthropic contributions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charitable - generous in giving to those in need
  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more, especially money or resources, than is necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Greek philanthrōpikos, from philanthrōpos (‘human-loving’).
