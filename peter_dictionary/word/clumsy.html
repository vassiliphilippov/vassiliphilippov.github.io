clumsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈklʌmzi / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzi / (BrE)
Moving or doing things in a way that is awkward, lacking grace or skill, often resulting in accidents or mistakes.

Examples

Certainly there would be less anxiety, fewer accidents (it is the clumsy child who sustains the worst injuries), and higher scholastic averages, since alert children work better.
They were clumsy, but they were beginning to catch on.

Synset Examples

a gawky lad with long ungainly legs
clumsy fingers
what an ungainly creature a giraffe is
heaved his unwieldy figure out of his chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill in movement
  • Antonym: graceful - showing elegance and smoothness in movement

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian; compare Old Norse *klumsa* (to become stiff), Swedish *klunsig* (clumsy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈklʌmzi / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzi / (BrE)
Lacking elegance or grace in expression; awkward or inept in speech or writing.

Examples

But it is a clumsy and wasteful process: it can produce negative results but not much that is positive.

Synset Examples

an awkward prose style
a clumsy apology
his cumbersome writing style
if the rumor is true, can anything be more inept than to repeat it now?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - not graceful or elegant in movement or expression
  • Antonym: graceful - showing elegance or smoothness in movement or style

Etymology

Possibly from Scandinavian origin, compare Swedish 'klumpsig' (clumsy).
