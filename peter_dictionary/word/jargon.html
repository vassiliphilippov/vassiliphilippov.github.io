jargon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑrɡən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
special words or expressions used by a particular profession or group that are difficult for others to understand

Examples

Rudyard Kipling's scorn for the "jargon "of psychical research was altered somewhat when he wondered "how, or why, had I been shown an unreleased roll of my life film "?

Synset Examples

they don't speak our lingo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slang - very informal language used by particular groups
  • Synonym: lingo - informal language or vocabulary used by a particular group
  • Synonym: argot - the jargon or slang of a particular group

Etymology

From Old French 'jargon' (chattering, jabbering), ultimately of imitative origin.
