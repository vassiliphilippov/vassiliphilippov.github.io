ceaseless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːsləs / (AmE), / ˈsiːsləs / (BrE)
Continuing without stopping; seemingly endless or uninterrupted.

Examples

Although the play does show a certain structural amateurishness (there are eleven acts varying in length from twenty-five seconds to an hour and a half), the statement it makes concerning the ceaseless yearning and searching of youth is profound and worthy of our attention.

Synset Examples

the ceaseless thunder of surf
in constant pain
night and day we live with the incessant noise of the city
the never-ending search for happiness
the perpetual struggle to maintain standards in a democracy
man's unceasing warfare with drought and isolation
unremitting demands of hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unending - having or seeming to have no end
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady
  • Synonym: incessant - continuing without pause or interruption

Etymology

From 'cease' + '-less' (meaning 'without ceasing').
