standstill

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌstɪl / (AmE), / ˈstandˌstɪl / (BrE)
A situation or condition in which movement, progress, or activity comes to a complete stop.

Examples

Political theoretical understanding, although almost at a standstill during this century, did develop during the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries, and resulted in a flood of inventions which increased the possibility for man to coexist with man.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation where progress is impossible, especially in a dispute or negotiation
  • Synonym: impasse - a situation in which no progress is possible, especially because of disagreement
  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which further action or progress is impossible

Etymology

From the phrase 'stand still', meaning to stop moving.
