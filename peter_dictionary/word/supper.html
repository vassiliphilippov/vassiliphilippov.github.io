supper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌp.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.ə / (BrE)
a light evening meal, often eaten instead of dinner in the late evening or after an earlier main meal

Examples

However, the litter remained, augmented by several dozen lunchroom suppers.
Kate went back and reminded the kitchen women of the supper preparations.
A cold supper was ordered and a bottle of port.
As they waited for supper they sat by the fire, glasses in hand, while Byron philosophized as much for his own entertainment as hers.
The light supper over, Claire went to him and, slipping an arm about his shoulder, sat on his knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinner - the main meal of the day, eaten either in the middle of the day or in the evening
  • Antonym: breakfast - the first meal of the day, taken in the morning
  • Antonym: lunch - a meal eaten in the middle of the day

Etymology

From Old French 'souper', from 'souper' (to eat the evening meal), from 'soup'.
