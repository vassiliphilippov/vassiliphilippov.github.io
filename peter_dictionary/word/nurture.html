nurture

1. VERB

/ ˈnɝː.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.tʃə / (BrE)
To encourage the growth or development of someone or something, especially by providing care, support, or encouragement.

Examples

It is difficult to say what can be done about them except that we must learn to recognize when it is they, rather than pretexts for them, that are causing the trouble, and do everything possible to nurture the healthy personalities that will prevent the development of such deficiencies.

Synset Examples

nurture his talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foster - to encourage the development or growth of something
  • Antonym: discourage - to try to prevent the development or growth of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'nourture', from Old French, from Latin 'nutrire' (to nourish).
