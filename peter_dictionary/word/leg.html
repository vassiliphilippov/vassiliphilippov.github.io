leg

1. NOUN

/ lɛg / (AmE), / lɛg / (BrE)
Either of the two limbs of the human body from the hip down, especially referring to the whole limb, but sometimes specifically to the part between the knee and ankle.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old Norse 'leggr' (leg, bone, limb).

2. NOUN

/ lɛg / (AmE), / lɛg / (BrE)
a limb used for support and movement in humans and many animals

Examples

It lacks pollen baskets and possesses only a large number of long, branched hairs on its legs, on which the pollen grains will collect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arm - a limb used for manipulation and holding in humans and some animals
  • Degree Lower: foot - the lower extremity of the leg used for standing or walking

Etymology

From Old English 'legge', of uncertain origin; possibly from Old Norse 'leggr' (leg, bone of the leg).

3. NOUN

/ lɛɡ / (AmE), / lɛɡ / (BrE)
each of the supports on which a piece of furniture stands, such as a table, chair, or bed.

Examples

A workbench had a heavy top and sturdy legs, but how did you attach sturdy legs to a heavy top so that the whole thing did n't wobble like a newborn calf and ultimately collapse when you leaned on it?
