rhubarb

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.bɑːrb / (AmE), / ˈruː.bɑːb / (BrE)
The long, thick, reddish or pinkish sour stalks of a plant, typically cooked and sweetened as food.

Examples

Sleepily, as if half reluctant to break the silence, he lifted his voice: "Rhu-beb ni ice fresh rhu-beb today "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pieplant - another term for rhubarb stalks used as food

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *reubarbarum*, literally ‘foreign rhubarb’.
