self-defeating

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf.dɪˈfitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌself.dɪˈfiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing problems or failure by acting in a way that defeats one's own aims or purposes.

Examples

The identification of the basic unit of religious organization - the parish or congregation - with a residential area is self defeating in a modern metropolis, for it simply means the closing of an iron trap on the outreach of the Christian fellowship and the transmutation of mission to co-optation.

Synset Examples

it is self-defeating...to ignore the progress of events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterproductive - having the opposite of the desired effect

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'defeating', indicating an action that defeats oneself.
