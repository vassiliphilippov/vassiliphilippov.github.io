salvo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælˌvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsælvəʊ / (BrE)
A simultaneous or successive discharge of firearms or artillery, often as a signal or salute, or as an attack.

Examples

The crowd staged its own mad scene in salvos of cheers and applause and finally a standing ovation as Miss Sutherland took curtain call after curtain call following a fantastic "Mad Scene "created on her own and with the help of the composer and the other performers.

Synset Examples

our fusillade from the left flank caught them by surprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volley - simultaneous discharge of a number of bullets or arrows
  • Synonym: fusillade - rapid simultaneous firing of firearms

Etymology

From Italian 'salva', from Latin 'salvāre' (to save) in the sense of safety or salute; related to 'salute'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsælvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsalvəʊ / (BrE)
A sudden and vigorous outburst resembling the discharge of firearms or release of bombs, often used metaphorically for a burst of sound, emotion, or activity.

Examples

Generally the first salvo of a tsunami is a rather sharp swell, not different enough from an ordinary wave to alarm casual observers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid, concentrated outpouring (e.g. of questions), often compared to artillery fire

Etymology

From Italian 'salva' (discharge of guns), from Latin 'salvāre' (to save), related to the military practice of firing a salute.
