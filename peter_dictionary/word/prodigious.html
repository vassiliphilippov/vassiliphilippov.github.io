prodigious

1. ADJ

/ prəˈdɪdʒəs / (AmE), / prəˈdɪdʒəs / (BrE)
Remarkably or impressively great in size, extent, or degree; extraordinary.

Examples

His thirteenth chapter includes many exciting accounts of huge serpents with prodigious strength, but these seem to be given to complete his picture, not to be believed.
His energy was prodigious; sometimes he would be up before dawn, clad as a garbage collector and hurling pails into areaways to exasperate us, and thereafter would hurry to the Bronx Zoo to grimace at the lions and press cigar butts against their paws.

Synset Examples

colossal crumbling ruins of an ancient temple
has a colossal nerve
a prodigious storm
a stupendous field of grass
stupendous demand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colossal - extremely large or great
  • Synonym: massive - large and heavy or solid
  • Synonym: gigantic - very large; enormous
  • Synonym: huge - extremely large in size or amount
  • Antonym: tiny - very small

Etymology

From Latin 'prodigiosus' (marvelous, portentous), from 'prodigium' (omen, portent).
