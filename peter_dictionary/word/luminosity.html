luminosity

1. NOUN

/ luːməˈnɑsɪti / (AmE), / luːmɪˈnɒsɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of emitting or reflecting light; brightness or radiance, especially as measured in scientific contexts.

Examples

The luminosity of the water is now believed to have been caused by the stimulation of vast numbers of the luminescent organism Noctiluca miliaris by the turbulence of the sea.

Synset Examples

its luminosity is measured relative to that of our sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightness - the quality or state of being bright
  • Synonym: radiance - the quality of being radiant or glowing

Etymology

From Latin 'luminosus' (bright) + '-ity' forming nouns of quality.
