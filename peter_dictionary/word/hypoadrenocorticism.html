hypoadrenocorticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊəˌdrɛnəˈkɔrtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊəˌdriːnəʊˈkɔːtɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by insufficient production of hormones by the adrenal cortex, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, anemia, and darkening of the skin; also called Addison's disease.

Examples

These reactions were interpreted as being manifestations of hypoadrenocorticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Addison's disease - disorder caused by failure of adrenal cortex function

Etymology

From hypo- (deficient), adreno- (adrenal gland), and corticism (related to the cortex).
