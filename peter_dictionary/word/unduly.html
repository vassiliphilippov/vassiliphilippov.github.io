unduly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈduːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuːli / (BrE)
to an excessive, unjustified, or inappropriate degree; more than is reasonable or proper.

Examples

All too often our language is unduly harsh.

Synset Examples

she was unduly pessimistic about her future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a greater degree than is necessary or appropriate
  • Synonym: overly - to an excessive degree
  • Antonym: duly - in a proper or appropriate manner

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'duly' (properly, as due)
