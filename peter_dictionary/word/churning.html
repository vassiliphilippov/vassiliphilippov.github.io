churning

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) moving with great turbulence or agitation.

Examples

On the shores north and south, the fishers and mooncursers - smugglers - lived along the churning Great South Bay and the narrow barrier of sand, Fire Island.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - (of a liquid or gas) characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; not controlled or calm
  • Antonym: calm - (of water or air) free from motion or disturbance

Etymology

Present participle of 'churn'.
