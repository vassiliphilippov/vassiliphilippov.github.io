fierce

1. ADJ

/ fɪrs / (AmE), / fɪəs / (BrE)
Showing intense and ruthless competitiveness; aggressively determined to win or succeed, often disregarding fairness or mercy.

Examples

Yet he remains the fiercest of competitors.

Synset Examples

cutthroat competition
bowelless readiness to take advantage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion; merciless
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion, especially to an opponent

Etymology

From Middle English *fers*, from Old French *fers*, from Latin *ferus* (wild, untamed).

2. ADJ

/ fɪrs / (AmE), / fɪəs / (BrE)
Having or showing strong feelings, intensity, or energy, often to a violent or passionate degree.

Examples

It was arranged that he would board in the home of one of the old members of the church, a woman named Catt who, as Wilson afterward found, was briefly referred to as The Cat because of her sharp tongue and fierce initiative.
Fierce anger surged through Russ.

Synset Examples

fierce loyalty
in a tearing rage
vehement dislike
violent passions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vehement - showing strong feeling; forceful, passionate, or intense
  • Synonym: violent - showing strong, often destructive force or emotion
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions; peaceful

Etymology

From Middle English 'fers', from Old French 'fers', from Latin 'ferus' (wild).
