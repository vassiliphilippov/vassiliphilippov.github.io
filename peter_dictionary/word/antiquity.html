antiquity

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪkwəti / (AmE), / ænˈtɪkwɪti / (BrE)
the period of history before the Middle Ages, especially relating to ancient Greece and Rome

Examples

What that spirit and attitude were we can best understand if we see more precisely how it contrasts with the communist tradition with the longest continuous history, the one which reached Christianity by the way of Stoicism through the Church Fathers of Late Antiquity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Middle Ages - period of European history from about the 5th to the late 15th century
  • Synonym: ancient history - history of the distant past, especially of classical civilizations

Etymology

From Latin antiquitas ‘ancient times’, from antiquus ‘ancient’.
