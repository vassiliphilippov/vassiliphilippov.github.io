Prohibition

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌhɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌhɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
(in the US) the period from 1920 to 1933 when the manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages was forbidden by law under the Eighteenth Amendment.

Examples

Consider what happened during World War 1,, when the Protestant churches united to push the Prohibition law through Congress.
To try to oppose the general religious and moral conviction of such a majority by a legislative fiat would be to invite the same breakdown of law and order that was occasioned by the ill-starred Prohibition experiment.
Prohibition was the law of the land, but it was unpopular (how many of us oldsters took up drinking in prohibition days, drinking was so gay, so fashionable, especially in the sophisticated Northeast!)

Synset Examples

in 1920 the 18th amendment to the Constitution established prohibition in the US

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding or banning something by law or authority (general sense)

Etymology

From Latin *prohibitiō* (a forbidding), from *prohibēre* (to forbid).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈhɪbɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˈhɪbɪʃən / (BrE)
The period from 1920 to 1933 in the United States when the manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages was banned by constitutional amendment.

Examples

Prohibition was the law of the land, but it was unpopular (how many of us oldsters took up drinking in prohibition days, drinking was so gay, so fashionable, especially in the sophisticated Northeast!)
During Prohibition we ran molasses into Canada.

Etymology

From Latin 'prohibitio' (a forbidding), but capitalized as a historical term for the US constitutional alcohol ban.
