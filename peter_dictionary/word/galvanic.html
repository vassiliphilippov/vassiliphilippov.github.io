galvanic

1. ADJ

/ ɡælˈvænɪk / (AmE), / ɡælˈvanɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or producing electric current by chemical action, especially in batteries.

Examples

The form of galvanic activity is halfway between the magnetic form and the electrical form.

Synset Examples

a galvanic cell
a voltaic (or galvanic) couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltaic - producing electricity by chemical action (especially in batteries)

Etymology

From Italian galvanico, from Luigi Galvani (1737–1798), Italian physician and physicist.
