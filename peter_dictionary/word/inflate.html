inflate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪt / (BrE)
To increase or expand something, especially figures or claims, beyond their true value, often for effect or advantage.

Examples

Since the constitution forbids introduction of a tax bill at a fiscal session, the administration will either have to cut down expenses or inflate its estimates of anticipated revenues.

Synset Examples

The charges were inflated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as greater than it actually is
  • Antonym: understate - to represent something as less important or smaller than it really is

Etymology

From Latin inflare, meaning 'to blow into; to puff up'.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to fill something with gas or air so that it becomes larger or more expanded

Examples

A tire must be inflated to its normal hot operating pressure and the car must be loaded to its operating weight when this measurement is made.

Synset Examples

inflate a balloons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deflate - to release air or gas from something

Etymology

From Latin inflare, from in- (into) + flare (to blow).
