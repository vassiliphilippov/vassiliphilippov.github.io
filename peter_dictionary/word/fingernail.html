fingernail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəneɪl / (BrE)
The hard, slightly curved covering of keratin at the tip of a finger.

Examples

The son and heir of a prominent family marries a girl who has tell-tale shadows on the half-moons of her finger nails.
Find out what he's got under his fingernails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toenail - the nail at the end of a toe

Etymology

From Middle English 'fyngernail', from Old English 'finger' + 'nægel' (nail).
