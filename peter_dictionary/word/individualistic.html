individualistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪvɪdʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪvɪdʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or expressing individuality; independent and self-reliant in actions or thought

Examples

All such imitations of negative quality have given rise to a compensatory response in the form of a heroic and highly individualistic humanism: if man can neither know nor love reality as it is, he can at least invent an artistic "reality "which is its own world and which can speak to man of purely personal and subjective qualities capable of being known and worthy of being loved.

Synset Examples

an individualistic way of dressing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - free from outside control; not depending on another's authority
  • Antonym: conformist - behaving or thinking like everyone else, rather than being different

Etymology

From individual + -istic, after French individualistique.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˌvɪdʒuəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdjʊəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Favouring minimal restrictions on commercial freedom; advocating for or characteristic of free enterprise and lack of governmental control in economic affairs.

Examples

Its refrain was: "Let us return to the individualistic democracy of our forefathers for our salvation ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal government interference in economic affairs
  • Antonym: collectivist - favoring collective or governmental control over commerce

Etymology

From 'individual' + '-istic', formed from Latin 'individuus' (indivisible).
