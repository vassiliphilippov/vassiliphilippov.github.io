carbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
a nonmetallic chemical element (symbol C), found in all organic compounds, and occurring naturally in the forms of graphite, diamond, and amorphous carbon

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: diamond - a crystalline form of carbon, the hardest natural material
  • Degree Lower: graphite - a soft, black, crystalline form of carbon used in pencils and lubricants

Etymology

From Latin 'carbo' (charcoal).
