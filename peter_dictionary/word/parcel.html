parcel

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːr.səl / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.səl / (BrE)
to divide something into parts or sections, especially for distribution

Examples

New Jersey folk need not be told of the builder's march to the sea, for in a single generation he has parceled and populated miles of our shoreline and presses on to develop the few open spaces that remain.

Synset Examples

The developers parceled the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • Synonym: split - to divide or cause to divide into parts

Etymology

From Old French *parcela* (a small part), from Latin *particella*, diminutive of *pars* (part).
