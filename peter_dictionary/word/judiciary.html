judiciary

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈdɪʃ.iˌɛr.i / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃ.ə.ri / (BrE)
The judges and courts that collectively have the authority to interpret and apply the law.

Examples

In municipal systems we tend to view what is called positivism as fundamentally a movement to democratize policy by increasing the power of parliament - the elected representatives - at the expense of the more conservative judiciary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal established to administer justice

Etymology

From Latin 'judiciarius', from 'judicium' (judgment).
