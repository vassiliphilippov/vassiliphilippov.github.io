Roman Catholicism

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊmən kəˈθæθəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌrəʊmən kəˈθætəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
The beliefs, practices, and system of the Roman Catholic Church, which recognizes the authority of the Pope in Rome.

Examples

Further evidence that Roman Catholicism enjoys a more favorable position today than in 1861 is the respectful attention given to it in the mass media of England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholicism - the beliefs and practices of the Catholic Church in general

Etymology

From 'Roman Catholic' (relating to Rome and the universal church) + -ism (doctrine or system).
