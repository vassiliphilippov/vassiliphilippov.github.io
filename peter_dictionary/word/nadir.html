nadir

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.dɪə / (BrE)
the lowest point or most unsuccessful moment in a situation or process; an extreme state of adversity

Examples

At the nadir of that circuit is death.
Nevertheless, even at the nadir of the circuit the spiral of history belongs to God, and he still rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zenith - the highest point; peak or culmination

Etymology

From Arabic *naẓīr* (opposite), via French *nadir* and Medieval Latin *nadir* (point opposite the zenith).
