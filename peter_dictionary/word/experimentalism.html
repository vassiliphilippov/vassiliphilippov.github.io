experimentalism

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛrəməntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪmɛntəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of relying on experiment and observation, especially as opposed to theory or tradition; advocacy of experimental methods or principles, especially in science, art, or philosophy

Examples

Prokofieff might well emerge as a cultural hero, who, by the force of his creative life, helped preserve the main stream of tradition, to which the surviving idioms of current experimentalism may be eventually added and integrated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience

Etymology

From experiment + -al + -ism, modeled after similar doctrines (e.g. realism, idealism).
