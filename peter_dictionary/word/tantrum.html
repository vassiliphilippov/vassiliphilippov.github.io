tantrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.trəm / (AmE), / ˈtæn.trəm / (BrE)
An uncontrolled outburst of anger or frustration, typically by a young child.

Examples

Otherwise, she would be baited into a tantrum - teased and provoked until she lost control of herself, and thus lost still another battle in the maddening struggle of Tom Lord Vs. Joyce Lakewood.

Synset Examples

he had a fit
she threw a tantrum
he made a scene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fit - a sudden outburst of emotion, especially anger
