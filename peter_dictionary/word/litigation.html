litigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪt̬.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪt.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of taking legal action in a court of law to resolve a dispute or enforce a right.

Examples

Admiralty law, the law merchant, and the host of problems which arise in private litigation because of some contact with a foreign country were all severed from the older Law of Nations and made dependent on the several national laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawsuit - a claim or dispute brought to a court of law for adjudication

Etymology

From Latin litigatio (a dispute), from litigare (to dispute, quarrel).
