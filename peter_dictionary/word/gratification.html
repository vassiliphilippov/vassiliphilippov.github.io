gratification

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræt̬.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡræt.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of pleasure or satisfaction, especially when something desired or needed is fulfilled.

Examples

Meek expressed particular gratification at the defensive performances of end Happy Nelson and halfback Billy Gannon.
It is at least possible that the capacity to postpone gratification is developed as well as expressed in a continuous and guided exposure to great literature.

Synset Examples

dull repetitious work gives no gratification
to my immense gratification he arrived on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfaction - fulfillment of one's wishes, expectations, or needs
  • Antonym: disappointment - a feeling of dissatisfaction when expectations are not met

Etymology

From Latin gratificatio (the act of rendering pleasing or doing a favor).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡræt̬əfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of satisfying a desire or need

Examples

In B. M. Spinley's portrayal of the underprivileged and undereducated youth of London, a salient finding was the inability to postpone gratification, a need to satisfy impulses immediately without the pleasure of anticipation or of savoring the experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfaction - the fulfillment of a need or desire

Etymology

From Latin gratificatio (an act of showing favor), from gratificari (to do a favor).
