Phoenix

1. PROPN

/ ˈfiː.nɪks / (AmE), / ˈfiː.nɪks / (BrE)
The state capital and largest city of Arizona, located in south-central Arizona in a formerly desert area now prosperous due to irrigation.

Examples

In an effort to fortify himself against the unforeseen upsets sure to arise in the future, Herbert A. Leggett, banker editor of the Phoenix "Arizona Progress ", reflects upon a few of the depressing experiences of the feverish fifties.

Etymology

Named after the mythical bird 'Phoenix', symbolizing renewal and rebirth, as the city was founded on the ruins of ancient Hohokam civilization canals.
