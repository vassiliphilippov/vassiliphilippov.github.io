opener

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpənər / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənə / (BrE)
the first event in a series, such as a sports game, concert, or theatrical performance, especially at the start of a season

Examples

Rice has not played since injuring a knee in the opener with Maryland.
The Bears took the lead in the first inning, as they did in Sunday's opener, and never lagged.
Producer John Holmes has chosen a delightful comedy for his season's opener at Matunuck in Jean Kerr's "King of Hearts ".

Synset Examples

she played Chopin for her opener
the season's opener was a game against the Yankees

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finale - the last event in a series, such as a final performance or match

Etymology

From 'open' + '-er', meaning 'that which opens' or 'begins'.
