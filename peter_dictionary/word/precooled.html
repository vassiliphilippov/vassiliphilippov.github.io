precooled

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriˈkuːld / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkuːld / (BrE)
Cooled beforehand, especially in preparation for a particular use or process.

Examples

The reactants for the gas phase experiments were first frozen out in a side arm attached to the manifold and then allowed to distill slowly into the manifold of pre-cooled reaction cells before sealing off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilled - made cool or cold, typically by refrigeration

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pre-' (meaning 'before') and 'cooled'.
