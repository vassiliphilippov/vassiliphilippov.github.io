softening

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒfənɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of becoming softer or making something softer.

Examples

This cooling does not change the avocado in any way, it just delays the natural softening of the fruit until a grove like temperature (room temperature) is restored.

Synset Examples

refrigeration delayed the softening of the fruit
he observed the softening of iron by heat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardening - the process of becoming harder
  • Synonym: soften - to make or become soft

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɔfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒfənɪŋ / (BrE)
Having a soothing or emollient effect, especially making something, such as skin, less hard or rough.

Examples

But Carla; eyes were on Agnese whose glowing face and softening eyes gave her a look of warmth and happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emollient - having the quality of softening or soothing the skin
