blank

1. ADJ

/ blæŋk / (AmE), / blæŋk / (BrE)
(Of a surface) not written, printed, or marked on; empty or unfilled.

Examples

He piled everything neatly in a corner of the cellar and turned to stare at the blank stone wall.
It was blank but the postmark said...

Synset Examples

blank pages
fill in the blank spaces
a clean page
wide white margins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: filled - (Of a surface) written or printed on; not blank

Etymology

From Old French 'blanc' (white, shining, empty), from Frankish or another Germanic source.
