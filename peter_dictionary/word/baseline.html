baseline

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪslaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪslaɪn / (BrE)
A standard or level used as a reference for comparison or measurement.

Examples

On the other hand, even when the binomial model does not describe well the physical phenomenon being studied, the binomial model may still be used as a baseline for comparative purposes; that is, we may discuss the phenomenon in terms of its departures from the binomial model.

Synset Examples

the established a baseline for the budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard point of reference for measuring or judging quality or performance
  • Synonym: standard - something established as a measure of quality or degree

Etymology

Formed from 'base' (foundation) + 'line'; originally used in surveying and sports to mean a real or imaginary reference line.
