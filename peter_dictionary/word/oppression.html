oppression

1. NOUN

/ əˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / əˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
Cruel or unjust use of authority or power to control or subjugate people.

Examples

"E. B. "compared John Brown to Moses in that they were both acting to deliver millions from oppression.

Synset Examples

the tyrant's oppression of the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persecution - persistent harassment or cruel treatment, often due to beliefs or identity
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from oppression or imprisonment
  • Synonym: tyranny - cruel and oppressive government or rule

Etymology

From Latin oppressio (a pressing down), from opprimere (to press against, to overwhelm).
