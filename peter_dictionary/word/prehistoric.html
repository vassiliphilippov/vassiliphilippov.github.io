prehistoric

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːhɪˈstɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriːhɪˈstɒrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or belonging to the period before written history.

Examples

It is not through space nor time that the time machine most approved by science fiction must travel for a visit to the permanent prehistoric past, or the ever existent past fantasy future.

Synset Examples

prehistoric settlements
prehistoric peoples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: historic - famous or important in history, or having happened in the past

Etymology

From pre- (before) + historic, ultimately from Greek 'proistoria' via Latin.
