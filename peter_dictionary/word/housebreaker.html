housebreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
A person who unlawfully enters a house with the intention of committing theft; a burglar.

Examples

Oddly enough, this is an amulet against housebreakers, presented to the mem and me by a local rajah in 1949.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglar - a person who breaks into buildings to commit theft

Etymology

Formed from 'house' (dwelling) + 'breaker' (one who breaks in), first recorded in late 16th century English.
