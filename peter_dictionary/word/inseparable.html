inseparable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛp(ə)rəbl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛp(ə)rəbl / (BrE)
(of people or things) unable to be separated or parted; always together or firmly joined.

Examples

But people can n't be made to integrate, socialize (the two are inseparable by Southern standards) by law.

Synset Examples

inseparable pieces of rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indivisible - impossible to divide or separate
  • Antonym: separable - able to be separated

Etymology

From Latin inseparabilis, from in- (not) + separabilis (able to be separated).
