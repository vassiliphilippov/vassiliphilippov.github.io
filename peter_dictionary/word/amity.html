amity

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A friendly and peaceful relationship; mutual harmony and goodwill.

Examples

When, as a diplomatic gesture of amity and in payment for the loan of gunmen in the April election, Torrio had given O' Banion a slice of Cicero, the profits from that district had been $ 20000 a month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enmity - the state or feeling of being actively opposed or hostile to someone

Etymology

From Old French amité, from Latin amicitia (friendship), from amicus (friend).
