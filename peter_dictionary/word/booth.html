booth

1. NOUN

/ buːθ / (AmE), / buːθ / (BrE)
A seating area in a restaurant or bar consisting of a table surrounded by two high-backed benches.

Examples

This was one place where Moonan could go for a drink in a back booth without anyone noticing him, or at least coming up and hanging around and wanting to know all the low-down.
We have a table waiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table - a piece of furniture with a flat top for eating or working at
  • Synonym: stall - a small enclosed space for selling goods or seating

Etymology

From Middle English 'bothe', from Old Norse 'búð' (dwelling, stall).

2. NOUN

/ buːð / (AmE), / buːθ / (BrE)
A small, enclosed or partly enclosed area set apart for a particular purpose, such as selling goods at a market or providing a service.

Examples

They went to the Flea Market, expecting to find the treasures of Europe, and found instead a duplication of that long double row of booths in Tours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - a compartment for the sale of goods in a market
  • Synonym: kiosk - a small open-fronted hut or cubicle

Etymology

From Middle English bothe, from Old Norse búð (dwelling, stall).
