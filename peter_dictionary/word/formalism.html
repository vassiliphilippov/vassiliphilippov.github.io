formalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrməˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlɪzəm / (BrE)
The theory or approach that values formal structure, style, or rules over content or meaning, especially in art, literature, or philosophy.

Examples

These polar concerns (imitation vs. formalism) reflect a philosophical and religious situation which has been developing over a long period of time.
We need a doctrine of imitation to save us from the solipsism and futility of pure formalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imitation - emphasis on replicating content or subject matter rather than form

Etymology

From French 'formalisme', from Latin 'forma' (form).
