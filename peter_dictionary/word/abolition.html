abolition

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈlɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈlɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of officially ending or abolishing a law, system, or practice, especially slavery.

Examples

He spoke of his desire to promote the abolition of slavery by peaceable means and he compared John Brown of Harper's Ferry to the John Brown of Rhode Island's colonial period.

Synset Examples

the abolition of capital punishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abolish - to formally put an end to something

Etymology

From Latin 'abolitio' (a putting an end to), from 'abolere' (to abolish).
