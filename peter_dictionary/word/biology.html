biology

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / baɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of living organisms, including their structure, function, growth, evolution, and interactions.

Examples

In agriculture, for example, despite the advances in biology, elaborate rituals tend to persist along with a continued sense of the imminence of some natural disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life science - any of the sciences that deal with living organisms
  • Degree Lower: zoology - the scientific study of animals
  • Degree Lower: botany - the scientific study of plants
  • Degree Lower: biochemistry - the branch of science concerned with the chemical processes within living things

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -logia (study of).
