inboard

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.bɔːd / (BrE)
Located within the hull or nearest the central line (midline) of a vessel or aircraft.

Examples

Just as it was being hauled inboard, a sea hit the ship.

Synset Examples

the inboard flaps on the wing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outboard - located outside the hull or farthest from the midline of a vessel/aircraft

Etymology

From 'in' + 'board', originally referring to being inside the side boards (hull) of a ship.
