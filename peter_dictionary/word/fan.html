fan

1. VERB

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
(in baseball) to strike out a batter, especially by making them swing and miss.

Examples

With one down in the eighth, Marv Throneberry drew a walk and stole second as Hyde fanned Tuttle.
John Powell, batting for Adair, fanned after fouling off two 2 - and 2 pitches, and Buddy Barker, up for Stepanovich, bounced out sharply to Jerry Lumpe at second to end the 2 - hour and - 27 - minute contest.
Nischwitz fanned six and walked only Charley Hinton in the third inning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike out - to be put out by failing to hit three pitched balls in baseball

Etymology

From the motion of swinging wildly, likened to the movement of a hand-held fan.

2. NOUN

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
a person who is very enthusiastic about a particular sport, team, or athlete

Examples

He came all the way around on Gardner's hit before 5777 fans.
This is an issue which boils down to a matter of opinion, depending on whether you 're an American or National fan and anti or pro Yankee.
You would be surprised how many fans purposely stayed away from Bears Stadium last year because of the television policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a sports team
  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is highly interested in a particular activity or subject

Etymology

Shortened from 'fanatic', originally from Latin 'fanaticus' (inspired by a god, frenzied).

3. NOUN

/ fæn / (AmE), / fan / (BrE)
a person who has a strong interest in and admiration for a particular person, group, activity, or thing

Examples

So the Kirov will fly back to Russia, minus a Chicago engagement, a serious loss for dance fans - and for the frustrated bookers, cancellation of one of the richest bookings in the country.
"Chicago and All That Jazz "may have wound up satisfying neither the confirmed fan nor the inquisitive newcomer.
Once you become an avocado fan, you will look forward to the season each year with eager anticipation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - a person who admires someone or something
  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is very interested in a particular activity or subject
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses an unfavorable opinion

Etymology

Short for 'fanatic', from Latin 'fanaticus' (of a temple, inspired by orgiastic rites).

4. VERB

/ fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
to make an emotion, feeling, or situation stronger or more intense; to stir up or incite

Examples

1) Most of the legends that are created to fan the fires of patriotism are essentially propagandistic and are not folk legends at all.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incite - to encourage or stir up (usually something negative)
  • Antonym: temper - to moderate or calm (an emotion or situation)

Etymology

From Old English 'fann', meaning a device for winnowing; the figurative sense stems from the action of fanning flames to make them burn more fiercely.
