introduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act of starting something new or bringing something into use for the first time

Examples

For the most part, this discussion will be confined to results obtained since the introduction of the reference standard.
Too often it is thought of at the last moment of new product introduction.

Synset Examples

they looked forward to the debut of their new product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debut - the first time something is presented or performed
  • Synonym: launch - the act of beginning or introducing something new

Etymology

From Latin introductio, from introducere 'to lead in'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntrəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
The first section of a book, speech, piece of music, or other communication, which gives an overview or preparatory information.

Examples

The introduction of the United States Pharmacopoeia reference standard in 1952 and the redefinition and equating of the USP and international units of thyroid stimulating activity have made it possible to compare results published by different investigators since that time.
He waved at Fox to cut off the finale introduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preface - introduction to a book, usually by the author
  • Synonym: foreword - short introductory section in a book, usually by someone other than the author
  • Antonym: conclusion - the final section of a communication

Etymology

From Latin introductio (a leading in), from introducere (to lead in).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally making a person known to another or to the public

Examples

Admirably written, it is a perfect introduction to Swedish history for readers of other countries.
I mean, what do I got to do, do I have to get a letter of introduction to get a sit-down?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presentation - the act of presenting someone formally

Etymology

From Latin introductio, from introducere (to lead in).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
a text or book that provides basic information or a preliminary explanation about a subject, intended for beginners or learners

Examples

The paper has a certain value as a comparatively easy introduction to this approach, particularly since it treats a fairly simple and straightforward phenomenon where it is possible to compare it with a more traditional (though not structural) statement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primer - a first book of instruction in a subject

Etymology

From Latin introductio (a leading in), from introducere (to lead in, introduce).
