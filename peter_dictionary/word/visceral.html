visceral

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪsərəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl / (BrE)
relating to the internal organs of the body, especially those within the chest and abdomen, such as the heart, lungs, liver, and intestines.

Examples

The intimal surface of the aorta was covered with confluent, yellow-brown, hard, friable plaques along its entire course, and there was a marked narrowing of the orifices of the large major visceral arteries.

Synset Examples

visceral bleeding
a splanchnic nerve

Etymology

From Latin visceralis, from viscera (internal organs).
