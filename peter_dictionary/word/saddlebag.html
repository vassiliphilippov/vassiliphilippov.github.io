saddlebag

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəlˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl.bæɡ / (BrE)
a large bag or a pair of bags attached to a saddle for carrying belongings, typically used on horses, motorcycles, or bicycles

Examples

David reached for the pair of pistols in the saddlebags at his feet.
From the saddlebags, hung on a Hitchcock chair, David took out a good English razor, a present from John Hunter.
He began to pack the saddlebags.

Etymology

From saddle + bag, indicating a bag used with a saddle.
