refute

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfjut / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːt / (BrE)
To prove that a statement, argument, or theory is false or incorrect.

Examples

Again the homely, everyday details of daily living refuted a vicious attempt to frighten her - or to murder her.
Who's gonna prove me wrong?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disprove - Show something is not true or valid
  • Antonym: prove - Show something is true or valid
  • Synonym: contradict - Assert the opposite of a statement

Etymology

From Latin refutare (to drive back, repel, refute).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈfjut / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːt / (BrE)
To prove that a statement, idea, or claim is wrong or false by argument or evidence.

Examples

Sprinkel strongly refuted the current neo-stagnationist thesis that we are facing a future of limited and slow growth, declaring that this pessimism "is based on very limited and questionable evidence ".
At one time I became disturbed in the faith in which I had grown up by the apparent inroads being made upon both Old and New Testaments by a "Higher Criticism "of the Bible, to refute which I felt the need of a better knowledge of Hebrew and of archaeology, for it seemed to me that to pull out some of the props of our faith was to weaken the entire structure.

Synset Examples

The speaker refuted his opponent's arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebut - to argue against a claim showing it is false
  • Synonym: disprove - to show that something is not true
  • Antonym: prove - to demonstrate the truth or existence of something

Etymology

From Latin refutare (to drive back, rebut).
