wild-eyed

1. ADJ

/ waɪld aɪd / (AmE), / waɪld aɪd / (BrE)
Having a look of extreme agitation, fear, or madness in one’s eyes.

Examples

Heiser, breathless and wild-eyed, brought the chilling news that the handspikes, heavers and holystones had been mysteriously removed from their customary places.

Synset Examples

crowded the wild-eyed animals into a truck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling agitation; peaceful
