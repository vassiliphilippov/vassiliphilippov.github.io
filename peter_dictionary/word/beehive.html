beehive

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiˌhaɪv / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌhaɪv / (BrE)
A place where many people are working very busily, resembling the activity of bees in a hive.

Examples

The beehive voices, for no one could bear silence, drowned out the sound of Mrs. Lincoln's weeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hive - a busy place filled with activity

Etymology

From 'bee' + 'hive', originally referring to the structure where bees live and work; figurative use attested since the 17th century.
