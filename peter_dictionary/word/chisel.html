chisel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzl / (BrE)
A hand tool with a flat steel blade and a cutting edge, used for shaping wood, stone, or metal by cutting or carving.

Examples

But contest definition - that dramatic muscular separation of every muscle group that seems as though it must have been carved by a sculptor's chisel - is something quite different.
He was crouched over his anvil in the courtyard getting his chisels into trim, when a splinter of steel flew into his eye and imbedded itself in his pupil.
The bristles are a Fitch 2 and a one-half inch brush shaved to a sharp chisel edge.
They look as if they had been sculptured with an unsharpened chisel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gouge - a chisel with a curved blade for carving hollows

Etymology

From Old French *cisel* (cutting tool), from Vulgar Latin *cisellum*, from Latin *caesellus*, diminutive of *caedere* (to cut).

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzəl / (BrE)
to cheat or swindle someone, especially by gaining small amounts through deceit or trickery

Examples

Barzini's people chisel my territory and we do nothing about it!

Synset Examples

The con-man beat me out of $50
This salesman ripped us off!
we were cheated by their clever-sounding scheme
They chiseled me out of my money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to deceive or trick, depriving someone of something
  • Synonym: swindle - to obtain money or property by fraud or deceit
  • Synonym: defraud - to illegally deprive someone of money or property by deception

Etymology

Metaphoric use of 'chisel', originally meaning a tool for cutting, extended to mean 'cutting in' to someone's share or rights.
