stuffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstʌfi / (AmE), / ˈstʌfi / (BrE)
(of a room or place) lacking fresh air and unpleasantly warm or close.

Examples

There was the suggestion of ice water, and - in spite of the protest "We 're not really thirsty "- Linda Kay, to escape the stuffy air and the smothering soft voices, hurried to the kitchen.

Synset Examples

a dusty airless attic
the dreadfully close atmosphere
hot and stuffy and the air was blue with smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airless - lacking air; having no fresh air
  • Antonym: ventilated - supplied with fresh air

2. ADJ

/ ˈstʌfi / (AmE), / ˈstʌfi / (BrE)
(of a person, group, or activity) very conventional, old-fashioned, and lacking imagination or excitement.

Examples

It was in order to avoid the stuffy routine of middle class life that Holmes became a detective in the first place.

Synset Examples

why is the middle class so stodgy, so utterly without a sense of humor?
a stodgy dinner party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stodgy - dull, excessively conventional or old-fashioned
