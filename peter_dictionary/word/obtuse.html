obtuse

1. ADJ

/ əbˈtus / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs / (BrE)
slow to understand or perceive things; lacking quickness of intellect or perception

Examples

Obtuse.

Synset Examples

so dense he never understands anything I say to him
never met anyone quite so dim
although dull at classical learning, at mathematics he was uncommonly quick
dumb officials make some really dumb decisions
he was either normally stupid or being deliberately obtuse
worked with the slow students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking intelligence or quickness of mind
  • Synonym: dim - not quick to understand; slow-witted
  • Antonym: acute - quick-witted; mentally sharp

Etymology

From Latin 'obtusus' meaning 'blunted, dull', from ob- (against) + tundere (to beat).
