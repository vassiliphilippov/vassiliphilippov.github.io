singe

1. VERB

/ sɪndʒ / (AmE), / sɪndʒ / (BrE)
to burn the surface of something slightly, especially so that it is only superficially damaged

Examples

Molten glare singed their eyelids an angry crimson; suffocating air sapped their strength and strained their nerves to snapping; dust choked their throats and lay like acid in their lungs.

Synset Examples

I singed my eyebrows

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burn - to damage or destroy by fire or heat

Etymology

From Old French 'sengier', of Germanic origin; related to German 'sengen' (to singe).
