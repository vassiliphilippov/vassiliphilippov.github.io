taffy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæfi / (AmE), / ˈtæfi / (BrE)
A soft, chewy candy made by boiling sugar or syrup until thick and then pulling it until it becomes glossy.

Examples

Spectators in white crush textures dip toe and heel in smooth black, navy and taffy tan.
My eyes were so bleary I could barely see him but there he was, a little smooth olivefaced guy in a new spring overcoat and a taffy colored fedora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toffee - a hard, chewy candy made by boiling sugar, often with butter and sometimes nuts

Etymology

Probably from a dialect variant of 'toffee.'
