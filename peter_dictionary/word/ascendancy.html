ascendancy

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛndənsi / (AmE), / əˈsɛndənsi / (BrE)
A position of power, strength, or control over others.

Examples

Many English Catholics are proud of their Catholicism and know that they are in a new ascendancy.

Synset Examples

her apparent dominance of her husband was really her attempt to make him pay attention to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominance - the state of having power or control over others
  • Antonym: subordination - the state of being lower in rank or position

Etymology

From English 'ascendant' (rising, dominant) + '-cy'. Ultimately from Latin 'ascendere' (to climb).
