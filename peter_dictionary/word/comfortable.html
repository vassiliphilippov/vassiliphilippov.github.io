comfortable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfɚtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbl / (BrE)
providing physical ease or relaxation; giving a sense of well-being or relief from pain or discomfort

Examples

That is why it is important to invest in comfortable, airy types of shoes.
(Music often sounds best to me when I can dress informally and sit in something more comfortable than a theatre seat.)
So choosing a good tree, he clambered up into it, found a comfortable notch, and curled up in it to sleep, like the tousled bear he was, with his hands across his chest, as though surfeited with honey.
The place was evidently a familiar haunt and Claire wondered what other illicit loves had been celebrated in the comfortable rooms to which they were shown.
After they had left, some of the people moved around, to find more comfortable places to sit.

Synset Examples

comfortable clothes
comfortable suburban houses
made himself comfortable in an armchair
the antihistamine made her feel more comfortable
are you comfortable?
feeling comfy now?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncomfortable - causing or feeling slight pain, physical discomfort, or unease
  • Synonym: comfy - informal version meaning comfortable

Etymology

From Old French confortable, from conforter (to comfort), from Latin confortare (to strengthen).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbl / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbl / (BrE)
free from worry or stress; feeling mentally at ease or secure

Examples

Thus shielded, we played many foolish games in comfortable unselfconsciousness; even when the fences became a part of the game - when a vine embowered gate-post was the Sleeping Beauty's enchanted castle, or when Rapunzel let down her golden hair from beneath the crocketed spire, even then we paid little heed to those who went by on the path outside.
Stern faced, he inspected the prisoners, satisfying himself that they were clean, well fed and comfortable within reason.

Synset Examples

was settled in a comfortable job, one for which he was well prepared
the comfortable thought that nothing could go wrong
was comfortable in his religious beliefs
she's a comfortable person to be with
she felt comfortable with her fiance's parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - free from worry; feeling safe or confident
  • Synonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety
  • Antonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness
  • Antonym: uneasy - feeling worried or unsure

Etymology

From Old French *confortable* (providing comfort), from Latin *confortare* (to strengthen).
