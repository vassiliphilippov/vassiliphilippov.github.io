graph

1. NOUN

/ ɡræf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a diagram showing the relationship between two or more quantities, typically represented as points, lines, or bars on a set of axes

Examples

By the use of various weights, data for a force rate of shear graph can be obtained.
We consider now the graph of the function f (t) on **f.
We will refer to the plane of C and f as the C-plane and to the plane of the graph** as the f-plane.
The graph, as a set, may have a finite number of components.
Each point with abscissa t on the graph represents an intersection between C and **f.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - a visual representation of data, often in the form of a table or diagram
  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the structure or workings of something

Etymology

From Greek 'graphē' (writing, drawing), from 'graphein' (to write, draw).

2. VERB

/ ɡræf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to represent (data or information) by means of a graph

Examples

After every session, the check marks were totaled up and graphed, and in this way the supervisor's progress was charted.

Synset Examples

chart the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - to represent information visually using a chart
  • Synonym: plot - to mark or illustrate points or data on a graph or chart

Etymology

From noun 'graph', originally short for graphic, influenced by words like 'photograph' and 'telegraph'.
