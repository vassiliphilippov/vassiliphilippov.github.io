devilish

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlɪʃ / (BrE)
Showing cunning, ingenuity, or wickedness characteristic of a devil; extremely clever or mischievous.

Examples

There was a sniper's nest in a mountain cave, and it was picking off our men with devilish accuracy.

Synset Examples

devilish schemes
the cold calculation and diabolic art of some statesmen
the diabolical expression on his face
a mephistophelian glint in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - extremely evil or cruel; wicked as a devil
  • Antonym: angelic - having the nature of an angel; exceptionally kind or innocent

Etymology

From 'devil' + '-ish', meaning 'like a devil'.
