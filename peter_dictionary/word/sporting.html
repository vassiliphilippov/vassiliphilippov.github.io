sporting

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
Showing fairness, honesty, and respect for opponents in a contest or situation; displaying sportsmanship or fair play.

Examples

He does n't think that potting them from a deck chair on the south side of the house with a quart glass of beer for sustenance is entirely sporting.
"But, well, it just do n't seem sportin' somehow "!
"Sportin "'!
"Sportin "'! he echoed again in soft wonder.
But there's one thing I never seen or heard of, one thing I just do n't think there is, and that's a sportin' way o' killin' a man "!

Synset Examples

a clean fight
a sporting solution of the disagreement
sportsmanlike conduct

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsporting - not showing fairness or respect for opponents; lacking sportsmanship
  • Synonym: sportsmanlike - exhibiting the qualities of a good sportsman, such as fairness and respect

Etymology

From 'sport' + '-ing', meaning pertaining to sport or conduct as in sport.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
involving a willingness to take risk or accept uncertain outcomes, especially for the sake of fair play or excitement

Examples

Say three minutes to make it sporting.

Synset Examples

a sporting chance
sporting blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventurous - willing to take risks or try out new experiences
  • Synonym: daring - brave and willing to take risks

Etymology

From 'sport', originally meaning 'game' or 'play'.
