intangible

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
An asset that has no physical substance but is of value, such as goodwill, patents, or trademarks.

Examples

Intangibles entered, then - hunches which felt like facts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tangible - a physical asset that can be touched or measured

Etymology

From Medieval Latin intangibilis: in- (not) + tangibilis (that can be touched).
