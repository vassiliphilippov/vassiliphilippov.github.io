maddened

1. ADJ

/ ˈmædnd / (AmE), / ˈmædnd / (BrE)
extremely angry or made wild with rage or emotion

Examples

Fear maddened, fleeing the lengthening shadow of death, he scrambled to his feet again.

Synset Examples

the enraged bull attached
furious about the accident
a furious scowl
infuriated onlookers charged the police who were beating the boy
could not control the maddened crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enraged - extremely angry
  • Synonym: furious - feeling or expressing intense anger
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger; peaceful

Etymology

From the verb 'madden', derived from 'mad' (Old English *gemǣdde*) + -en (verb-forming suffix) + -ed (past participle).
