expository

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɑːzɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪtəri / (BrE)
intended to explain or describe something; serving to expound or set forth ideas or information clearly.

Examples

For expository purposes, this is best treated as a model which spells out the conditions under which an important industry affected with the public interest would find it profitable to raise wages even in the absence of union pressures for higher wages.

Synset Examples

clean expository writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanatory - serving to explain something

Etymology

From Late Latin expositorius, from Latin exponere (to set forth).
