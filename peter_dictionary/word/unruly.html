unruly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈruːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈruːli / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or things) difficult to control or manage; disorderly or lacking discipline.

Examples

Unruly hair goes straight up from his forehead, standing so high that the top falls gently over, as if to show that it really is hair and not bristle.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorderly - lacking organization or control
  • Synonym: chaotic - completely confused or disordered
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests
  • Antonym: manageable - capable of being controlled or dealt with

Etymology

From un- (not) + obsolete English 'reuly' (obedient, from Old French 'reule', rule).
