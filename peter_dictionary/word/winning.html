winning

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or fact of achieving victory, especially in a competition or struggle.

Examples

It was Nischwitz' third straight victory of the new season and ran the Grizzlies' winning streak to four straight.

Synset Examples

winning is not everything

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victory - the act of defeating an opponent or enemy
  • Antonym: loss - the fact or process of failing to win

Etymology

From the verb 'win', Old English 'winnan' (to strive, struggle, win).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
Having achieved victory in a contest or competition; that has won.

Examples

Palmer, golf's leading money winner in 1960, and Snead will be saluted as the winning team in the Canada Cup matches last June in Dublin.
This ignorant estimate, I repeat, is not of any interest in itself; but it becomes very important if foreign peoples react the way human beings typically do - namely, by taking steps to end up on what appears to be the winning side.
But I sure do love that winning horse of mine, though.

Synset Examples

the victorious entry
the winning team

Related Terms

  • Antonym: losing - having been defeated; that has lost

Etymology

Present participle of 'win', Old English 'winnan' (to struggle, achieve, win).
