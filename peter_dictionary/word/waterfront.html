waterfront

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌfrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌfrʌnt / (BrE)
the part of a city, town, or area that is situated along the edge of a body of water, such as a river, lake, or sea, often used for business, recreation, or residential purposes.

Examples

He claimed to be visiting the waterfront saloon at the crossroads to play cards and drink with his cronies, but Kate had not smelled brandy on him since Mrs. Lattimer's funeral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor - a place on the coast where vessels may find shelter
  • Synonym: dockyard - an area where ships are built, maintained, or repaired
