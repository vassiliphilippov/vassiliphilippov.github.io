coconut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkəˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkənʌt / (BrE)
The firm white edible flesh found inside a coconut shell, often used shredded in cooking and baking.

Examples

There had been some coconut in it, for I remember my mother's taking a quick glance at a stringy bit of this nut on the cheek of one of them and then putting down her radish with a shiver.
Possibly, the coconut containing dessert had brought up bitter problems of administration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copra - dried coconut meat used to extract coconut oil

Etymology

From Portuguese and Spanish coco (meaning coconut), likely from 'coco' meaning 'grinning face', due to the markings on the shell.
