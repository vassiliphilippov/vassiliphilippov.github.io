polished

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːlɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
Made smooth and shiny by rubbing; (of style, manners, etc.) refined and perfected.

Examples

Calloused fingers, caressed only by the smoothness of polished rosaries, had swayed excitedly beneath puckered chins where tiny black hairs sprouted, never to be tweezed away.
Though versatile and capable of turning out a ballad lyric with the best of them, Mercer's forte is a highly polished quasi folk wit.

Synset Examples

his polished prose
in a freshly ironed dress and polished shoes
freshly polished silver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - shiny and smooth; reflecting light
  • Synonym: refined - improved to be free from flaws; elegant
  • Antonym: rough - not smooth, having an uneven surface
  • Antonym: unfinished - not brought to a state of completion or refinement

Etymology

From late Middle English, past participle of 'polish', from Old French 'polir', from Latin 'polire' (to smooth, polish).
