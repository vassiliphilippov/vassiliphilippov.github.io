ineffectively

1. ADV

/ ɪn.ɪˈfɛktɪv.li / (AmE), / ɪn.ɪˈfɛktɪv.li / (BrE)
In an ineffective manner; without producing the intended effect or result.

Examples

During the nineteenth century these views were protested by virtually all the Latin American writers, though ineffectively, just as the new nations of Africa and Asia protest them, with more effect, today.

Synset Examples

he dealt with the problem rather ineffectively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effectively - in an effective manner; successfully producing the intended result

Etymology

From "ineffective" + "-ly"; ultimately from Latin "in-" (not) + "effectivus" (effective).
