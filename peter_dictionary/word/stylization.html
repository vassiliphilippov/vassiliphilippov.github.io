stylization

1. NOUN

/ ˌstaɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstaɪləˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of representing something according to a particular style, especially in art, literature, or performance, rather than attempting to depict it realistically.

Examples

Thereafter the audience waxed applause happy, but discriminating operagoers reserved judgment as her singing showed signs of strain, her musicianship some questionable procedure and her acting uncomfortable stylization.
A tongue-in-cheek stylization of 18 th Century ideas; a trial balloon to test the aesthetic climate of the times; a brilliant piece de resistance?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realism - the quality or practice of representing things as they actually are; opposite of stylization

Etymology

From 'stylize' + '-ation'. 'Stylize' from style (from Latin 'stilus', writing instrument).
