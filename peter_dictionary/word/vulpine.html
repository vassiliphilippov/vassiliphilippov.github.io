vulpine

1. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈvʌlpaɪn / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of a fox, especially in cunning or craftiness.

Examples

He behaved publicly with a cocky, swaggering truculence that offended their vulpine Latin minds, and behaved towards them personally with an unimpressed insolence that enraged them beneath their blandness.

Synset Examples

vulpine cunning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxy - sly or cunning, like a fox

Etymology

From Latin 'vulpinus', from 'vulpes' meaning 'fox'.
