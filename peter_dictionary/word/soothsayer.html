soothsayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːθˌseɪər / (AmE), / ˈsuːθˌseɪə / (BrE)
a person who claims to be able to predict what will happen in the future, especially by using special knowledge or powers

Examples

It is being fought, moreover, in fairly close correspondence with the predictions of the soothsayers of the think factories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune-teller - person who claims to predict the future
  • Synonym: prophet - person regarded as inspired to predict the future

Etymology

From Middle English 'soth(sayer)', from 'sooth' (truth) + 'sayer' (one who says).
