altruism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæltruˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæltruːɪzəm / (BrE)
the principle or practice of unselfish concern for the welfare of others, even at a cost to oneself

Examples

A romantic is one who thinks the world is divinely inspired and all he has to do is find the right key, and then divine justice and altruism will appear.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: selfishness - concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure
  • Synonym: selflessness - the quality of unselfish concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

From French 'altruisme', from Italian 'altrui' (of others), from Latin 'alter' (other).
