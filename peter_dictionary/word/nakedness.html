nakedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪkədnəs / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪdnəs / (BrE)
The state of being without clothes; the condition of being unclothed or exposed.

Examples

Dead fledgling birds, their squashed looking nakedness and the odor of decay that clung to the hand when they had been buried in our graveyard in front of the purple flags.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudity - the state of being naked, especially in art or public

Etymology

From Old English *nacodnes*, from *nacod* (naked) + *-ness* (state, quality).
