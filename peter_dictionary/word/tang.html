tang

1. NOUN

/ tæŋ / (AmE), / tæŋ / (BrE)
a sharp, distinctive, and often somewhat tart flavor, smell, or quality

Examples

As a boy Roy Mason began the long process of extracting the goodness of the out-of-doors, its tang of weather, its change of seasons, its variable moods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zest - great enthusiasm or a piquant flavor
  • Antonym: blandness - lacking strong flavor or character

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse tangi (a point of land, prong, or spike).
