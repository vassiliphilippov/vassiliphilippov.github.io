smothering

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌðɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of air, heat, or atmosphere) making breathing difficult by being excessively hot, stuffy, or lacking in fresh air; oppressively close or stifling.

Examples

There was the suggestion of ice water, and - in spite of the protest "We 're not really thirsty "- Linda Kay, to escape the stuffy air and the smothering soft voices, hurried to the kitchen.

Synset Examples

the choking June dust
the smothering soft voices
smothering heat
the room was suffocating--hot and airless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocating - making it hard to breathe; stifling
  • Synonym: stifling - very hot and uncomfortable; making breathing difficult
  • Synonym: airless - lacking fresh air; stuffy

Etymology

From the verb 'smother' (early 14th century, from Middle English 'smother', meaning 'stifling smoke' or 'to suffocate').
