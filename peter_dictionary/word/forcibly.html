forcibly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɔrsəbli / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsəbli / (BrE)
In a manner involving the use of force; by using physical power or coercion.

Examples

Bull queers take by force.

Synset Examples

keep in mind the dangers of imposing our own values and prejudices too forcibly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - with great force or intensity
  • Antonym: gently - in a gentle or soft manner

Etymology

From 'forcible' + '-ly', with 'forcible' from Old French 'force' (force; strength).
