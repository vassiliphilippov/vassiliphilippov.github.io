empire

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmˌpaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
A group of countries or regions ruled by a single monarch, especially an emperor or empress.

Examples

Since the land he desired lay within the great northern empire of the Hudson's Bay Company, he purchased great blocks of the Company's stock with the view to controlling its policies.
Selkirk persuaded eighty men and four officers to go to Red River where they were to serve as a military force to protect his settlers from the hostile Northwest Company which resented the intrusion of farmers into the fur traders' empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - a country, state, or territory ruled by a king or queen

Etymology

From Latin *imperium* (command, authority, empire).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.paɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
A group of businesses or companies under common ownership and managed as a single organization.

Examples

We have here, finally, a witness that will further testify to Michael Corleone'’s rule over a criminal empire that controls all gambling in this country and perhaps in other countries.

Etymology

From Latin 'imperium' (rule, command, authority), through Old French 'empire'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpaɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
A group of countries or regions that are controlled by one ruler or government.

Examples

The Corleone family was like the Roman Empire.

Synset Examples

the British created a great empire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - a country ruled by a king or queen
  • Antonym: republic - a state in which supreme power is held by the people or their elected representatives

Etymology

From Latin imperium (command, power).
