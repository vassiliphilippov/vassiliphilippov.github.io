escapade

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskəˌpeɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛskəpeɪd / (BrE)
an exciting, daring, or mischievous adventure, especially one that is reckless or unconventional

Examples

More than a beautiful visualization of the illustrious adventures and escapades of the tragi-comic knight-errant and his squire, Sancho Panza, in seventeenth century Spain, this inevitably abbreviated rendering of the classic satire on chivalry is an affectingly warm and human exposition of character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventure - an unusual, daring, or exciting experience or activity

Etymology

From French 'escapade', from Spanish 'escapada' (a getaway), from 'escapar' (to escape).
