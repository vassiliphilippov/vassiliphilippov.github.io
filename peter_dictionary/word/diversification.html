diversification

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvɜrsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌvɜːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of adding variety, especially by investing in different types of assets or offering a range of products or services, to reduce risk or increase opportunities.

Examples

This may be helpful in improving the competitive position of established firms through diversification and expansion or through more economical utilization of plant capacity.

Synset Examples

my broker recommended a greater diversification of my investments
he limited his losses by diversification of his product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse; having differences

Etymology

From diversify + -cation; ultimately from Latin diversificare (to make diverse).
