randomization

1. NOUN

/ ˌrændəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrændəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something random, especially by arranging items or performing actions in a way determined by chance rather than by a set pattern, often to avoid bias in experiments or studies

Examples

A randomization of "ups "and "downs "is more likely than ordered "ups "and "downs "in position (3) since the hydrogen atoms are well separated and so the position of one could hardly affect the position of another, and also since ordered "up "and "down "implies a larger unit cell, for which no evidence exists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomness - the quality or state of lacking a predictable order or plan

Etymology

From random + -ization, based on randomize.
