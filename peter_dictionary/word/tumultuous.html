tumultuous

1. ADJ

/ təˈmʌltʃuəs / (AmE), / tjuːˈmʌltʃuːəs / (BrE)
involving a lot of disturbance, confusion, or disorder; full of noisy commotion or uproar

Examples

That tumultuous, painful and costly experience shows clearly that a law expressing a moral judgment cannot be enforced when it has little correspondence with the general view of society.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance or disorder

Etymology

From Latin tumultuosus, from tumultus (commotion, uproar).
