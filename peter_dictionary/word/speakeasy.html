speakeasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiˌkizi / (AmE), / ˈspiːkiːzi / (BrE)
(historical, US) An illicit bar or nightclub selling alcoholic beverages, especially during the Prohibition era in the United States.

Examples

In six months O' Banion had boosted the profits to $ 100000 a month - mainly by bringing pressure to bear on fifty Chicago speak-easy proprietors to shift out to the suburb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind pig - another term for an illicit bar during Prohibition

Etymology

From the phrase 'speak easy', referring to the need to speak quietly about such establishments to avoid police detection.
