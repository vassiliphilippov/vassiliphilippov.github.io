plumbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
the occupation or trade of installing and repairing pipes and fixtures for water, gas, or sewage in buildings

Examples

The interior designer, then, must first be an artist but also understand carpentry and painting and lighting and plumbing and finance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumber - a person whose job is to install and repair pipes and fixtures

Etymology

From Middle English 'plummynge', related to 'plumber'; ultimately from Latin 'plumbum' (lead), as pipes were originally made of lead.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of pipes, fixtures, and other apparatus in a building for the distribution of water or gas and disposal of waste.

Examples

First of all there was the parsonage, an utterly impossible place for civilized people to live in, originally poorly conceived, apparently not repaired for years, with no plumbing or sewage, with rat-holes and rot.
If the house is not wired adequately for electricity or if plumbing or a central heating system must be installed, check into the cost of making these improvements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipework - system of pipes collectively
  • Degree Lower: sewage - wastewater and waste material carried away by sewers

Etymology

From the verb 'plumb' (to work as a plumber) and the suffix -ing; ultimately from Latin 'plumbum' (lead), as lead was formerly used in pipes.
