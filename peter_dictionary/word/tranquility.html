tranquility

1. NOUN

/ træŋˈkwɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A state of being calm, peaceful, and free from disturbance or stress.

Examples

From the time he had been at college he had achieved a certain tranquility and composure by accepting the fact that there were certain things he could never know.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenity - a state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From Latin *tranquillitas*, from *tranquillus* (calm, quiet).
