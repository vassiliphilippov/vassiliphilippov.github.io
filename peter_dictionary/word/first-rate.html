first-rate

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɝːstˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌfɜːstˈreɪt / (BrE)
Of the highest possible quality; excellent.

Examples

But then, after the little operetta had been given its feeble amateur rendering, everyone insisted that it was too good to be lost forever, and that the Royal Academy of Music must now have the manuscript in order to give it the really first-rate performance it merited.

Synset Examples

an ace reporter
a crack shot
a first-rate golfer
a super party
played top-notch tennis
an athlete in tiptop condition
she is absolutely tops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top-notch - of the highest quality; excellent
  • Antonym: second-rate - of mediocre or lesser quality; not the best

Etymology

From the naval rating system in the 17th century, where 'first-rate' referred to the largest and most powerful warships.
