convulse

1. VERB

/ kənˈvʌls / (AmE), / kənˈvʌls / (BrE)
(of a person) to be overcome with sudden, uncontrollable laughter or emotion, often causing involuntary movements of the body.

Examples

This happy bulletin convulsed Mr. Gorboduc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse with laughter - to laugh so much that one loses control

Etymology

From Latin convulsus, past participle of convellere (to tear loose).
