blaspheme

1. VERB

/ blæsˈfim / (AmE), / blɑːsˈfiːm / (BrE)
to speak about God or sacred things in a profane or irreverent way; to utter profanities or obscenities

Examples

Dice rolled, prostitutes plied their trade, thieves stole, murderers stabbed, and the ungodly blasphemed.

Synset Examples

The drunken men were cursing loudly in the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to utter offensive words, swear
  • Synonym: swear - to use offensive language, especially profanities

Etymology

From Greek blasphēmein (to speak evil of).
