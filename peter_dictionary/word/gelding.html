gelding

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛl.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛl.dɪŋ / (BrE)
A male horse that has been castrated, often to make it more docile and suitable for riding or work.

Examples

She showed her surprise by tightening the reins and moving the gelding around so that she could get a better look at his face.
He side-stepped her blow and she fell, stumbling against the gelding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stallion - an uncastrated adult male horse
  • Antonym: mare - an adult female horse

Etymology

From Old Norse *gelda* (to castrate), related to the act of castration.
