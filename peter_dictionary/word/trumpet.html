trumpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
A brass musical instrument with a bright, penetrating tone, having a slender tube, three valves, and a flared bell, played by blowing air through closed lips to produce vibration.

Examples

With her son evidencing so strong a musical bent his mother could do little else but get him started on the study of music - though she waited until he was ten - beginning with the piano and following that with the trumpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornet - a brass instrument similar to the trumpet but with a mellower tone

Etymology

From Old French 'trompette' (diminutive of 'trompe'), from a Germanic root related to 'trump', meaning to sound.

2. VERB

/ ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
to announce or proclaim something loudly and publicly, often with emphasis or self-importance

Examples

Is it after all possible that no matter how the liberals trumpet their confidence in human dignity they are exposed to a contagion of fear more insidious than any conservative has ever to worry about?

Synset Examples

Liberals like to trumpet their opposition to the death penalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announce - to make something known publicly
  • Synonym: broadcast - to publicly communicate or spread information

Etymology

From the noun 'trumpet', referring to the brass musical instrument; figurative sense derives from the loud, attention-grabbing quality of the instrument.
