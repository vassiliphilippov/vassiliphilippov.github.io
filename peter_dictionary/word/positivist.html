positivist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːzətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪtɪvɪst / (BrE)
A person who holds the philosophical view that knowledge should be based only on observable phenomena, rejecting metaphysical speculation.

Examples

This is the course the positivists took.
The positivist tells us that when we say this we are only expressing our present emotion.
Consider what follows from the positivist view.
So far as I can see, there is only one way out for the positivist.
This way of escape is theoretically possible, but since it has grave difficulties of its own and has not, so far as I know, been urged by positivists, it is perhaps best not to spend time over it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricist - someone who emphasizes observation and experience as the source of knowledge
  • Antonym: metaphysician - someone who studies or believes in metaphysics, often contrasted with positivist

Etymology

From French *positiviste*, from *positif* 'positive', ultimately from Latin *positivus*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑzətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪvɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of positivism, especially in philosophy or social science; based on the view that only factual knowledge gained through observation and experience is meaningful.

Examples

It removes our present expression and shows that the badness we meant would not be affected by this, whereas on positivist grounds it should be.
It ideally removes the past event, and shows that this would render false what we mean to say, whereas on positivist grounds it should not.
On the positivist theory, everything I sought to express by calling it evil in the first case is still present in the second.
The positivist theory would here distort our meaning beyond recognition.

Synset Examples

positivist thinkers
positivist doctrine
positive philosophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic

Etymology

From French *positiviste*, from *positif* (positive), based on Latin *positivus* (settled by agreement).
