monotony

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːtəni / (AmE), / məˈnɒtəni / (BrE)
the quality of being dull, unvarying, and repetitive, making something tedious or boring

Examples

To break the monotony it may be necessary to invent tasks that will keep the family busy.

Synset Examples

he had never grown accustomed to the monotony of his work
he was sick of the humdrum of his fellow prisoners
he hated the sameness of the food the college served

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedium - the state of being tedious or boring
  • Antonym: variety - the quality of being different or diverse
  • Antonym: diversity - the state of being diverse; showing a great deal of variety

Etymology

From Greek monotonia, from monos (single) + tonos (tone).
