delineation

1. NOUN

/ dəˌlɪniˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌlɪnɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A graphic or vivid verbal description of something; the act of clearly representing or outlining in words.

Examples

Rather than from a first-hand study of Jewish people, his delineation of Shylock stems from a collection of Italian stories, Il Pecorone, published in 1558, although written almost two centuries earlier.

Synset Examples

too often the narrative was interrupted by long word pictures
the author gives a depressing picture of life in Poland
the pamphlet contained brief characterizations of famous Vermonters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the act of representing something in words or pictures
  • Synonym: description - a statement that represents something in words
  • Synonym: characterization - a description of distinctive characteristics

Etymology

From Latin *delineatio* (sketch, outline), from *delineare* (to sketch out).
