vocal

1. ADJ

/ ˈvoʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəl / (BrE)
(of a person or group) expressing opinions or feelings freely, loudly, or insistently; outspoken.

Examples

They are less vocal now, when it is the West Berliners who are migrating.

Synset Examples

outspoken in their opposition to segregation
a vocal assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outspoken - expressing opinions or feelings freely and openly
  • Antonym: reticent - not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily

Etymology

From Latin vocalis, from vox (voice).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvoʊ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the singing voice or to the use of the voice in producing music or sounds

Examples

One learns to become alert to changes in his vocal tone - to his voice's suddenly shifting to a quality not like his usual one, a quality which sounds somehow artificial or, in some instances, parrotlike.

Synset Examples

vocal technique
the vocal repertoire
organized a vocal group to sing his compositions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instrumental - relating to musical instruments rather than the voice

Etymology

From Latin vocalis (of or pertaining to the voice), from vox (voice).
