bond

1. NOUN

/ bɑnd / (AmE), / bɒnd / (BrE)
an official certificate promising to pay back borrowed money with interest at a fixed time, issued by a government or company

Examples

The Georgia Legislature will wind up its 1961 session Monday and head for home - where some of the highway bond money it approved will follow shortly.
Vandiver likely will mention the $ 100 million highway bond issue approved earlier in the session as his first priority item.
Meanwhile, it was learned the State Highway Department is very near being ready to issue the first $ 30 million worth of highway reconstruction bonds.
The bond issue will go to the state courts for a friendly test suit to test the validity of the act, and then the sales will begin and contracts let for repair work on some of Georgia's most heavily traveled highways.
A Highway Department source said there also is a plan there to issue some $ 3 million to $ 4 million worth of Rural Roads Authority bonds for rural road construction work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debenture - unsecured loan certificate issued by a company, backed by general credit rather than assets
  • Antonym: stock - a share representing ownership in a company or corporation

Etymology

From Middle English 'bond', variant of 'band', from Old Norse 'band' (something that binds). The financial sense developed in the 17th century.

5. NOUN

/ bɑnd / (AmE), / bɒnd / (BrE)
(Law) A sum of money paid as a guarantee that a person accused of a crime will appear for trial; forfeited if the person fails to appear.

Examples

On the occasion of his 1922 indictment the $ 10000 bond was furnished by an alderman, and the charge was nolle prossed.

Synset Examples

the judge set bail at $10,000
a $10,000 bond was furnished by an alderman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bail - money paid as security for the release of an accused awaiting trial

Etymology

From Middle English 'bond' (binding agreement), from Old English 'bonda' (householder, farmer), influenced by Old Norse 'bondi', and from Old English 'bindan' (to bind).
