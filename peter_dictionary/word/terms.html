terms

1. NOUN

/ tɝmz / (AmE), / tɜːmz / (BrE)
the state of the relationship or understanding between people or groups, especially as expressed in certain conditions or the way they interact (often used with prepositions such as 'on...terms')

Examples

In many cases that statement "We break even on our downtown operation and make money on our branches "would be turned around if the cost analysis were recalculated on terms less prejudicial to the old store.
"All right, Crouch; we 're on even terms.

Synset Examples

on good terms with her in-laws
on a friendly footing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relationship - the way in which two or more people or groups regard and behave toward each other
  • Synonym: standing - status, reputation, or position in relation to others

Etymology

Plural of 'term', from Latin 'terminus' (end, boundary); figurative use for relational standing dates from early 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ tɜrmz / (AmE), / tɜːmz / (BrE)
the amount of money required to be paid for something, or the conditions under which a financial transaction occurs

Examples

When it was proposed to rebuild the church, Wilson found that the terms for a new mortgage were very high.

Synset Examples

the price of gasoline
he got his new car on excellent terms
how much is the damage?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: price - the amount of money expected, required, or given in payment for something
