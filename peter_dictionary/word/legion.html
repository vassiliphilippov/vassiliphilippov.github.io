legion

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈliːdʒən / (BrE)
a large military unit, especially in ancient Rome, typically made up of several thousand soldiers; also used for some modern military groups.

Examples

You were around the old-timers, who dreamed of how the families should be organized, basing them on the old Roman legions, and call '’em "regimes," the "capos" and "soldiers."

Synset Examples

the French Foreign Legion

Etymology

From Latin 'legio' (meaning 'military levy, legion'), from 'legere' (to choose, gather).
