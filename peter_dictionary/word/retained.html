retained

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈteɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnd / (BrE)
kept in one's possession, use, or memory; not lost or discarded.

Examples

Anita put out the remaining candles with a long snuffer, and in the smell of scented candlewick, the comforting awareness of each other's bodies, the retained pattern of dancers and guests remembered, their minds grew numb and then empty of images.

Synset Examples

in...the retained pattern of dancers and guests remembered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserved - kept safe or in its original state
  • Antonym: relinquished - given up or let go

Etymology

From retain + -ed, from Latin retinere (to hold back).
