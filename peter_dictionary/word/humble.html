humble

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose pride or feel less important; to lower someone's dignity or status

Examples

Poor where they had once been rich, humbled where they had been arrogant, having no longer any hope of sharing in the leadership of the nation, the rebels who would not surrender in spirit drew comfort from the sympathy they felt extended to them by the mother country.

Synset Examples

He humiliated his colleague by criticising him in front of the boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • Antonym: exalt - to raise to a higher status or position; to praise highly

Etymology

From Latin humilis (low, lowly, humble), related to humus (ground).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌm.bəl / (BrE)
(of people or things) having a low social, administrative, or financial rank; not important or grand.

Examples

And knowing its humble place in the scale of things, why did he, at this time of life, seem almost ready to sell his soul for plumpness?
Artistic mimesis under Christian influence records the involvement of all persons, however humble, in a divine drama.
It seems time to consider a revision of operational procedures for lexicostatistic studies on a more humble, solid, and limited basis.

Synset Examples

a humble cottage
a lowly parish priest
a modest man of the people
small beginnings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lowly - of low rank or status
  • Synonym: modest - unassuming in status or rank
  • Antonym: exalted - of high rank or status
  • Antonym: grand - impressive in rank or status

Etymology

From Latin *humilis* (low, humble), from *humus* (ground).

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to cause someone to become less proud or to realize their limitations; to make modest or unpretentious

Examples

On the other hand, you may seek His favor, humble yourself before Him and beg His mercy, implore His forgiveness, forsake your sins, and abandon your whole life to Him.

Synset Examples

This experience will humble him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • Antonym: exalt - to raise in rank or dignity; to praise highly

Etymology

From Old French *humiler*, from Latin *humiliare* (to humble), from *humilis* (low, humble).
