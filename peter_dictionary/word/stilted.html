stilted

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈstɪltɪd / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or manner) stiff, formal, and unnatural, often because of an attempt to appear correct or dignified.

Examples

The complexities of communication have been considerably abetted in this case by appropriately stilted English language that has been excellently dubbed in place of the Russian dialogue.

Synset Examples

that artificial humility that her husband hated
contrived coyness
a stilted letter of acknowledgment
when people try to correct their speech they develop a stilted pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - not natural or real; made or produced by people
  • Antonym: natural - as found in nature; not artificial or forced

Etymology

From 'stilt' (a long pole for walking above the ground), implying stiffness or unnatural height.
