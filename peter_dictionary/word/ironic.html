ironic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈrɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒnɪk / (BrE)
Using words that mean the opposite of what you really think, often in a humorous or mocking way; characterized by or expressing irony.

Examples

It is truly odd and ironic that the most handsome and impressive film yet made from Miguel de Cervantes' "Don Quixote "is the brilliant Russian spectacle, done in wide screen and color, which opened yesterday at the Fifty-fifth Street and Sixty-eighth Street Playhouses.

Synset Examples

dry humor
an ironic remark often conveys an intended meaning obliquely
an ironic novel
an ironical smile
with a wry Scottish wit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Antonym: sincere - expressing genuine or honest feelings

Etymology

From French ironique, from Latin ironia (irony), from Greek eirōneía (simulated ignorance).
