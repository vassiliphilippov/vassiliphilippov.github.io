involuntary

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˈvɑlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒləntri / (BrE)
(of an action or movement) done without conscious control or as a result of an automatic response, not subject to the control of the will.

Examples

Her action was involuntary.

Synset Examples

involuntary manslaughter
involuntary servitude
an involuntary shudder
It (becoming a hero) was involuntary. They sank my boat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or free will, not forced

Etymology

From Latin 'involuntarius', from 'in-' (not) + 'voluntarius' (voluntary, of one's free will).
