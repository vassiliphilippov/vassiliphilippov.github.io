wolf-whistle

1. VERB

/ ˈwʊlf ˌwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwʊlf ˌwɪsəl / (BrE)
(of a man) to whistle in a distinctive way at a woman to express sexual attraction or approval, typically regarded as rude or inappropriate.

Examples

[MAN WOLF-WHISTLING] [CROWD CHEERING AND APPLAUDING]

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catcall - to make a whistle or shout of sexual interest or derision at someone, especially a woman

Etymology

Formed from 'wolf' (from the animal's stereotypical association with lustful behavior) and 'whistle.'
