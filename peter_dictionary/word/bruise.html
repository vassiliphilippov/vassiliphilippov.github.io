bruise

1. NOUN

/ bruːz / (AmE), / bruːz / (BrE)
An injury appearing as an area of discolored skin on the body, caused by a blow or impact that does not break the skin.

Examples

Recently many doctors have installed ultrasonic vibration machines for deep massage of bruises, contusions, and simple bursitis.
Every so often, Andy would show up with fresh bruises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contusion - medical term for a bruise

Etymology

From Old French *bruisier* (to break, shatter), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ bruz / (AmE), / bruːz / (BrE)
to injure the underlying soft tissue or bone, causing a discolored area on the skin without breaking it

Examples

We press him to conform to our comfortable conceptions and not to bruise our satisfactions with his word, and God's.
In spite of her attempt to preserve her balance, she fell, bruising her arm on a naked stone.

Synset Examples

I bruised my knee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contuse - to injure tissue without breaking the skin

Etymology

From Old French 'bruisier' (to break), from Medieval Latin 'brusiare'.
