steam

1. VERB

/ stiːm / (AmE), / stiːm / (BrE)
(of a ship, train, or other vehicle) to travel using steam power.

Examples

Other factors increasing operating costs include the higher unit cost of each flying hour, up 11 % in two years, and of each steaming hour, up 15 %.
Cutting short a holiday at Hong Kong, the aircraft carriers Lexington and Bennington steamed off into the South China Sea, accompanied by a swarm of destroyers, plus troopships loaded with marines.

Synset Examples

The ship steamed off into the Pacific

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sail - to travel in a boat or ship by means of sails or navigation
  • Degree Higher: motor - to travel in a motor vehicle

Etymology

From Old English *stēam* (vapor, fume), later used as a verb meaning to move by means of steam.

2. VERB

/ stim / (AmE), / stiːm / (BrE)
to emit or give off steam as vapor, often as a result of heat

Examples

She had a cup of something steaming, coffee perhaps, in one hand, a fresh piece of toast in the other.

Synset Examples

The rain forest was literally steaming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke - to emit smoke as from burning
  • Synonym: vaporize - to emit vapor, turn to vapor

Etymology

From Old English *stēam* (vapor, fume).
