variegated

1. ADJ

/ ˈver.i.əˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈveər.ɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Having a variety of colors, especially as irregular patches or streaks; marked by different colors.

Examples

And his eyes - those miniature sundials of variegated yellow - had not altered their expression or direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - Having many colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - Having only one color

Etymology

From Latin 'variegatus', from 'variare' (to change, to vary) + '-atus' (-ed).
