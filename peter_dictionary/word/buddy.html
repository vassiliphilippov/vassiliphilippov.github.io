buddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdi / (AmE), / ˈbʌdi / (BrE)
A close friend or companion; a person who shares activities and spends time with another.

Examples

When he was in the war, he was in Law or Supplies or something like that, and an old buddy of his told me he would come down on Sundays to the Pentagon and read the citations for medals - just like the one we sent in for Trig - and go away with a real glow.
How long, buddy "?
"Do n't Christsake me, buddy!
"Get in, buddies.
"In back, buddy ", the driver said to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pal - a close friend; mate
  • Synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and has a bond of mutual affection
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile; antonym of friend

Etymology

Probably from 'brother', later influenced by other terms for friend in American English.
