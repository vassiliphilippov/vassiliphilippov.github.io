sunlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌlaɪt / (BrE)
the light and energy that comes from the sun

Examples

For example, bright sunlight is rapidly destructive for living microorganisms suspended in air.

Then the darkness thinned, and there was light again, and then bright sunlight.

Synset Examples

the shingles were weathered by the sun and wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunshine - the shining of the sun; direct sunlight

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'sun' + 'light'.
