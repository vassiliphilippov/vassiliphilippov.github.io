leaden

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛd.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɛd.ən / (BrE)
Feeling or appearing heavy, sluggish, or weighted down, especially due to fatigue or weariness.

Examples

He could not grasp that Lord had withdrawn from the fight minutes ago, and that his leaden arms were flailing at nothing but the air.

Synset Examples

his leaden arms
weighted eyelids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy - having great weight; difficult to lift or move
  • Synonym: sluggish - slow or lacking energy

Etymology

From Old English 'lēaden', from 'lead' (the metal) + '-en' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛdən / (AmE), / ˈlɛdn̩ / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) dark and dull, covered with heavy clouds; resembling the grey color of lead.

Examples

Then he noticed the clouds racing upon them - heavy, ominous, leaden clouds that formed even as they sliced over the crests of the surrounding hills.

Synset Examples

a dark day
a dull sky
the sky was leaden and thick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening (of sky)
  • Antonym: sunny - bright with sunlight (of sky or weather)

Etymology

From Old English 'lēaden', from 'lead' (the metal) + '-en'.
