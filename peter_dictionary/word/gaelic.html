Gaelic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈɡælɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪlɪk, ˈɡælɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the Celts, especially to their languages, culture, or peoples.

Examples

For example, when the film is only four minutes old, Neitzbohr refers to a small, Victorian piano stool as "Wilhelmina ", and we are thereupon subjected to a flashback that informs us that this very piano stool was once used by an epileptic governess whose name, of course, was Doris (the English equivalent, when passed through middle Gaelic derivations, of Wilhelmina).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celtic - relating to the Celts or their languages

Etymology

From Latin 'Gadēlīcus', from Old Irish 'Goídelc' (the Gaelic language).
