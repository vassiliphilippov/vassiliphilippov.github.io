pepper

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
to attack or bombard repeatedly, as with missiles or questions

Examples

Along the main thoroughfares hardly a house had not been peppered.

Synset Examples

pelt the speaker with questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelt - to attack by repeatedly throwing things at

Etymology

From Old English 'pipor', from Latin 'piper' (pepper, the spice); figurative use from the action of scattering pepper.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
To add pepper to (food or a dish) as seasoning.

Examples

Salt and pepper each breast.

Synset Examples

pepper the soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt - to add salt to food as seasoning

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
The edible fruit of plants of the genus Capsicum, including sweet and hot varieties such as bell peppers and chili peppers, used as a vegetable or spice.

Examples

That'’s a Ritz cracker and chopped liver!" [LAUGHING] Bring out the peppers and sardines!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - the hot-tasting pod of a type of Capsicum plant, often used as a spice
  • Synonym: bell pepper - a mild, sweet variety of Capsicum, usually green, red, or yellow

Etymology

From Old English 'pipor', from Latin 'piper' (black pepper); extended to Capsicum fruits in modern use.
