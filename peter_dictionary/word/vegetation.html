vegetation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvedʒəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
all the plants or plant life in a particular area or period, especially considered as a collective group

Examples

They dislike dense vegetation.
Cool air moving slowly through the open or smashed out side windows hinted of blooming roadside vegetation, and occasionally a faint fragrance of perfume swirled from the back seat.

Synset Examples

Pleistocene vegetation
the flora of southern California
the botany of China

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flora - the plants of a particular region, habitat, or period
  • Antonym: fauna - the animals of a particular region, habitat, or period

Etymology

From Latin 'vegetationem' (growth, act of growing), from 'vegetare' (to enliven, quicken).
