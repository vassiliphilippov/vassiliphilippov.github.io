alpha

1. NOUN

/ ˈælfə / (AmE), / ˈalfə / (BrE)
the first letter of the Greek alphabet (Α, α), used in science, mathematics, and as a symbol for various concepts

Examples

Below these particulars was a series of alpha beta gammas connected by arrows and crosses which denoted the lineage of the breed.

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha', from Phoenician 'aleph'.
