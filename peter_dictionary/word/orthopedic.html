orthopedic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔrθəˈpidɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈpiːdɪk / (BrE)
relating to the branch of medicine concerned with the correction or prevention of deformities or disorders of the bones, joints, and muscles.

Examples

A year later in Albany, N. Y., a Roman Catholic hospital barred an orthopedic surgeon because of his connection with the Planned Parenthood Association.

Synset Examples

orthopedic shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthopaedic - relating to orthopedics (alternative British spelling)

Etymology

From French orthopédique, from Greek orthos (straight) + paidion (child).
