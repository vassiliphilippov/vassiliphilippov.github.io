deliverance

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvərəns / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvərəns / (BrE)
The act of being rescued or set free from danger, suffering, or confinement.

Examples

They speak of the work of Christ as the bestowal of incorruptibility, which can mean (though it does not have to mean) deliverance from time and history.

Synset Examples

work is the deliverance of mankind
a surgeon's job is the saving of lives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue - The act of saving someone from a dangerous or distressing situation
  • Synonym: liberation - The act of setting someone free from imprisonment or oppression
  • Antonym: captivity - The condition of being imprisoned or confined

Etymology

From Old French 'delivrance', from Latin 'deliberare' (to set free).
