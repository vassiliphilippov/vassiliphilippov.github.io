riot

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪət / (AmE), / ˈraɪət / (BrE)
A violent public disturbance caused by a group of people, typically involving damage to property and confrontation with authorities.

Examples

J. Wheaton Smith, editor of the Warren Telegraph stated that "the ends of justice must be satisfied, a solitary example must be set, in order that all those misnamed philantropists [ sic ], who, actuated by a blind zeal, dare to instigate riot, treason, and murder, may heed it and shape their future course accordingly ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - an event causing disorder or confusion in public
  • Degree Lower: protest - a public demonstration expressing strong objection
  • Degree Higher: insurrection - a violent uprising against authority

Etymology

From Old French *riote* (dispute, quarrel), from *rioter* (to quarrel, dispute).
