thrifty

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɪfti / (AmE), / ˈθrɪfti / (BrE)
Careful and prudent in the use of money or other resources; economical.

Examples

When they have 4 to 6 leaves and are thrifty little plants, it's time to set them out where they are to remain.
It would have been unwise policy, for instance, to apply the pound of flesh characterization to the thrifty Scotchman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frugal - using money or resources sparingly; not wasteful
  • Antonym: wasteful - using resources carelessly or extravagantly

Etymology

From Old English 'thrift' (prosperity, savings) + '-y'.
