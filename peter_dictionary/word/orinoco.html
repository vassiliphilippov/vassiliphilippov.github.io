Orinoco

1. PROPN

/ ˌɔːrɪˈnoʊkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪˈnəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
a large river in northern South America, about 2,410 km (1,500 miles) long, flowing from the Guiana Highlands in Venezuela to the Atlantic Ocean.

Examples

R. R. Mole and F. W. Urich give approximately 20 inches as the average length of a brood of thirty from the region of the Orinoco estuaries.

Etymology

From Spanish Orinoco, likely from an indigenous (Guarani or Piaroa) word meaning 'a place to paddle'.
