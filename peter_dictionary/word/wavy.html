wavy

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪvi / (AmE), / ˈweɪvi / (BrE)
(of hair) having waves or undulating form, not straight or curly.

Examples

In litters of eight mice from similar parents, the number of mice with straight instead of wavy hair is an integer from 0 to 8.
We classify mice as "straight haired "or "wavy haired ", but a hairless mouse appears.

Synset Examples

she had long wavy hair

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: curly - (of hair) having curls or ringlets
  • Antonym: straight - (of hair) having no waves or curls

Etymology

Formed from 'wave' + '-y', denoting 'having the qualities of waves.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪvi / (AmE), / ˈweɪvi / (BrE)
Having a series of smooth curves or undulations; not straight or flat.

Examples

Wavy lines, feather like patterns, rosettes of indefinitely floral nature, birds either singly or in stylized rows, animals in solemn frieze bands (see Plates 11 - 12) - all these turned up in the more developed fabrics as preliminary signs that the potters were broadening their gaze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curly - having curls or curved shapes
  • Antonym: straight - not having curves or bends

Etymology

From wave +‎ -y, meaning 'having the quality of waves'.
