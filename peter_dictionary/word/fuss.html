fuss

1. VERB

/ fʌs / (AmE), / fʌs / (BrE)
to worry unnecessarily or pay excessive attention to minor details, often in an anxious or agitated way

Examples

"Fuss, fuss, old man ".
"Fuss, fuss, old man ".
He opened the inner door; the cooking odors were stronger - all over the city, at this hour, housewives would be fussing over stoves.

Synset Examples

don't fuss too much over the grandchildren--they are quite big now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something; to worry or concern oneself
  • Synonym: fret - to be visibly anxious or distressed; to worry
  • Antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to; to disregard

2. NOUN

/ fʌs / (AmE), / fʌs / (BrE)
a display of unnecessary or excessive excitement, activity, or worry about something.

Examples

He had made such a fuss about buying that second car that he knew he was vulnerable.
"I doubt they'll kick up any fuss.

Synset Examples

he was in a dither
there was a terrible flap about the theft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness or tranquility; opposite of fuss

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the 18th century.

3. VERB

/ fʌs / (AmE), / fʌs / (BrE)
to show excessive or unnecessary concern or care for someone or something, often in a way that suggests anxiety or inconvenience

Examples

Night after night he stayed with Gunny in the dead of winter, rubbing her with quarts of expensive liniment, fussing over her bran mash as the cook did over charlotte russe, tracking manure on the pretty new carpet when he did come to the house.

Synset Examples

She fusses over her husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dote - to show excessive fondness or attention
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly (opposite sense)
