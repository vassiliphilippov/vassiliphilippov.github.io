fibrocalcific

1. ADJ

/ ˌfaɪbroʊkælˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪbrəʊkalˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of tissue) involving or resulting from the calcification of fibrous tissue, typically as a result of disease or chronic inflammation.

Examples

The parenchyma was slightly hyperemic in the apex of the left lung, and there were several firm, gray, fibrocalcific nodules measuring as large as 3 mm..

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (fiber) + 'calcificus' (making into lime, calcifying).
