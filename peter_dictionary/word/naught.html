naught

1. NOUN

/ nɔt / (AmE), / nɔːt / (BrE)
Nothing; zero; a quantity of no importance.

Examples

Furthermore - and this, to me, strikes an especially warming note - it shall avail the vandals naught to throw away or dispose of their loot.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; zero
  • Synonym: nil - zero; nothing

Etymology

From Old English nāwiht (no thing).
