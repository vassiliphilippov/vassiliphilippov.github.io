keen

1. ADJ

/ kin / (AmE), / kiːn / (BrE)
Having a sharp intelligence or the ability to notice and understand subtle details; perceptive.

Examples

In 1864 Newman professedly had to write his Apologia with his keenest feelings in order to be believed and to command a fair hearing from English readers.
Occasionally, you may come across one or two bumblebees in the cold season, when you are turning over sods in your garden, but you have to be a really keen observer to see them at all.

Synset Examples

an acute observer of politics and politicians
incisive comments
icy knifelike reasoning
as sharp and incisive as the stroke of a fang
penetrating insight
frequent penetrative observations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • Synonym: incisive - showing clear and analytical thinking
  • Antonym: dull - slow to understand or notice things

Etymology

From Middle English kene, from Old English cēne (bold, brave, clever).
