irritability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrətəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easily annoyed or made angry; a tendency to become irritable

Examples

He felt her irritability did not concern him, yet he knew he would not care even if it did.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temper - a person's state of mind in terms of being angry or calm
  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being peaceful or not easily disturbed

Etymology

From Latin irritabilis (easily excited, irritable), from irritare (to excite, provoke) + -ity.
