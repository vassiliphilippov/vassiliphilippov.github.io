wangle

1. VERB

/ ˈwæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwæŋɡəl / (BrE)
to achieve or obtain something by cleverness or devious methods, often by manipulating a situation

Examples

Drafted into the Austrian army, he rebelliously rejected discipline, wangled a Vienna billet, went on painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finesse - to achieve something by skillful handling or manipulation

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly related to dialectal English 'wankle' (unsteady, tricky), or influenced by 'waggle'.
