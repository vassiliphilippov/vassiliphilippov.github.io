individualism

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdɪvɪˈdʒuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɪvɪˈdʒuːəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The quality of being independent and self-reliant; the habit or principle of being an individual and valuing personal independence over conformity.

Examples

This restless individualism found its answer when he returned to live nearly all the rest of his life in Sweden.
For this reason, he appears as an independent and self-reliant figure, whose rugged individualism need not be pressed into the mold of a 9 to 5 routine.
It is this curious blend of rugged individualism and public service which accounts for the great appeal of the mythological detective.
By virtue of his self-reliance, his individualism and his freedom from external restraint, the private eye is a perfect embodiment of the middle class conception of liberty, which amounts to doing what you please and let the devil take the hindmost.
In the mystery story, as in The Wealth of Nations, individualism and the social good are two sides of the same benevolent coin.

Synset Examples

so absorbed by the movement that she lost all sense of individuality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individuality - the qualities that make a person or thing different from others
  • Antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual

Etymology

From French 'individualisme', from 'individuel' (individual).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine that government should not interfere in commercial affairs; economic policy emphasizing minimal state involvement.

Examples

However, it is important to trace the philosophy of the French Revolution to its sources to understand the common democratic origin of individualism and socialism and the influence of the latter on the former.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laissez-faire - policy of minimal government interference in economic affairs

Etymology

From French individualisme, from individu (individual), from Medieval Latin individuus.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that each person is independent and self-reliant, and that individual interests and rights are more important than those of the group or state

Examples

The socialism implicit in the slogan of the Roosevelt Revolution, freedom from want and fear, seems a far cry from the individualism of the First Amendment to the Constitution, or of the Jacksonian frontier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual

Etymology

From individual + -ism, influenced by French individualisme.
