heading

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
A line of text at the top of a page or section introducing or summarizing the content that follows.

Examples

Under the heading of it's an ill wind, et cetera ".

Synset Examples

the heading seemed to have little to do with the text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the name of a book, composition, or other artistic work
  • Degree Lower: subtitle - a secondary or subordinate title giving additional information
  • Synonym: headline - the heading at the top of an article or page, especially in a newspaper

Etymology

From 'head' + '-ing', meaning something that comes at the head (top) of a section.
