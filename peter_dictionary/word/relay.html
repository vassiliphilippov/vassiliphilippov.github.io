relay

1. VERB

/ rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
to receive and then pass on (information, a message, etc.) to someone else

Examples

A point like p gets information directly from n, but all information beyond n is indirectly relayed through n.
(b) A message runs too great a risk of being distorted if it is to be relayed more than about six consecutive times.

Synset Examples

Please relay the news to the villagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmit - to send or pass something from one person or place to another
  • Synonym: forward - to send something onward to another destination or recipient

Etymology

From French relayer (to replace, to relieve), from Old French relaier.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪˌleɪ / (BrE)
an act of passing something, especially information or an object, from one person or group to another in a sequence

Examples

Phil Rossoff cut over to center from left field to get the relay.

Synset Examples

the relay was successful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handover - the act of transferring something to another person
  • Synonym: transmission - the act of sending something from one place or person to another

Etymology

From Old French *relai* (fresh supply, rest), from *relaier* (to change, to relay).
