collective

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of actions, ideas, or attributes) done by or involving people acting together as a group; shared or undertaken jointly by a number of individuals.

Examples

They include both individual fears and collective ones.
Our collective policies, group and national, are similarly based on voodoo, but here we often lack even the empirically successful rituals and are still engaged in determining them.
As he points out, a religious group cannot exist without a collective credo, and the more extensive the credo, the more unified and strong is the group.
The action centers about a group of outspoken and offbeat students sitting around a table in a cafeteria and their collective and ultimately fruitless search for a cup of hot coffee.
Everyone with a personal or group tragedy to relate had to be given his day in court as in some vast collective dirge.

Synset Examples

a joint identity
the collective mind
the corporate good

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individual - done by or relating to a single person

Etymology

From Latin collectīvus, from colligere ‘gather together’.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
A group of people acting together in a cooperative enterprise, especially an organization owned and managed jointly by its members.

Examples

The collective by which I address you in the title above is neither patronizing nor jocose but an exact industrial term in use among professional thieves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperative - an organization owned and operated by a group for their mutual benefit

Etymology

From Latin 'collectivus', from 'collectus', past participle of 'colligere' (to gather together).
