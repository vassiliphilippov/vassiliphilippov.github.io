mutilate

1. VERB

/ ˈmjuːtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
To severely damage or injure, especially by cutting off or destroying a body part; to disfigure or make imperfect by such injury.

Examples

Before him, mutilated, bleeding to death, they lay.

Synset Examples

The madman mutilates art work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maim - to wound or injure someone so that part of the body is permanently damaged
  • Synonym: disfigure - to spoil or damage the appearance of something or someone

Etymology

From Latin *mutilare* (to cut off, to maim), from *mutilus* (maimed, mutilated).
