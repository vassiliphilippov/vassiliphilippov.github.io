eulogize

1. VERB

/ ˈjuː.ləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.ləˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
To praise someone or something formally and eloquently, especially in a speech or written tribute.

Examples

They came together in the huge, high-ceilinged Council Chamber to hear the late leader eulogized.
Those writers known collectively as the "Southern school "have received accolades from even those critics least prone to eulogize; according to many critics, in fact, the South has led the North in literature since the Civil War, both quantitatively and qualitatively.

Synset Examples

The dead woman was eulogized at the funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of
  • Antonym: criticize - indicate the faults of someone or something in a disapproving way

Etymology

From Greek eulogein (to speak well of) via Late Latin eulogizare.
