humility

1. NOUN

/ hjuˈmɪləti / (AmE), / hjuːˈmɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance; the state of being humble.

Examples

One might say it combines the man; own pride and humility.
For he has the pride that comes of self acceptance and the humility, perhaps of the same genesis, not to impose himself upon another.

Synset Examples

not everyone regards humility as a virtue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction from one's achievements; often contrasted with humility

Etymology

From Latin *humilitas* (lowness, humility), from *humilis* (humble, low).
