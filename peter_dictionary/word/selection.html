selection

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
A person or thing that has been chosen from a group.

Examples

The selection had been expected.

Synset Examples

he was my pick for mayor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - a person or thing that is chosen
  • Synonym: pick - something or someone that is chosen

Etymology

From Latin selectio (a picking out, choosing), from seligere (to select).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of choosing someone or something from among a group

Examples

This is particularly true in site selection.
With such a program you can make constructive selections of the best sites available.
The quantity and quality of water sources is often a big factor in site selection.
There is a broad spectrum of organisms from which selection for a specified military purpose might be made.
It may be assumed that differences in ratings due to selection of growth centers from specific regions of the body will be small, according to existing tables of onset age and completion age for centers throughout the body.

Synset Examples

your choice of colors was unfortunate
you can take your pick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - the act of selecting or making a decision when faced with two or more possibilities
  • Antonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept, choose, or believe

Etymology

From Latin selectio (a choosing out), from seligere (to select).

3. NOUN

/ səˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a range or variety of things from which a choice can be made

Examples

But whenever a major purchase was contemplated forty years ago - a new bedroom set or a winter coat, an Easter bonnet, a bicycle for Junior - the family set off for the downtown department store, where the selection would be greatest.
Tone analysis will continue to be difficult and unsatisfactory until a more representative selection of systems is familar to every practicing field linguist.
By the year Kennedy was shot, Andy had transformed a storage room smelling of rat turds and turpentine into the best prison library in New England, complete with a fine selection of Hank Williams.

Synset Examples

the store carried a large selection of shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - the act of choosing or a thing chosen
  • Synonym: array - an impressive display or range of a particular type of thing
  • Synonym: collection - a group of things considered as a whole

Etymology

From Latin selectio (a choosing out), from seligere (to select).
