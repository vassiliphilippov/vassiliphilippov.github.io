humorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈhjuːmərəs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmərəs / (BrE)
Full of or characterized by humor; funny or amusing.

Examples

But it is characteristic of him, we are told, "his little artifice ", to be able to introduce "into a fairly vulgar and humorous piece of hackwork a sudden phrase of genuine creative art ".
"Now drive carefully, for God's sake "! she called, trying to attain a half humorous resentment at his departure.
This masterpiece has gaiety, too, but it is the gaiety of dancing people: earthy, salty and humorous.
He gives us an account of this in his lively and humorous poem, "The Happy Artists ".
They comprise a great variety of scene and interest: grim episodes of war, idyllic interludes, superb canvases of world-shaking events, and delightfully humorous sketches of odd characters.

Synset Examples

humorous stories
humorous cartoons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funny - causing laughter or amusement
  • Synonym: comic - intended to make people laugh; humorous
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or not intended to be funny

Etymology

From Latin humorosus (moist, full of fluid), from humor (moisture; later 'mood, wit'). The sense relating to wit or fun comes from the ancient theory of bodily 'humors' affecting mood.
