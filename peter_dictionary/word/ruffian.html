ruffian

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfiən / (AmE), / ˈrʌfiən / (BrE)
A violent or brutal person, especially one who behaves in a cruel, bullying, or lawless manner.

Examples

Greene was in actuality a young ruffian from Kent, who had broken with his parents in order to keep the company he preferred - pimps, panders and whores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable
  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of good manner and gentle, civilized behavior

Etymology

From Old French *rufian* (scoundrel), possibly from Italian *ruffiano* (pimp, flatterer).
