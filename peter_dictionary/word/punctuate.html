punctuate

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌŋktʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to insert punctuation marks such as commas, periods, or question marks into a piece of writing to clarify meaning

Examples

The surfaces were coarsely and finely granular and punctuated by clear, fluid filled cysts measuring up to 3 cm. in diameter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punctuation - the marks used in writing to clarify meaning

Etymology

From Medieval Latin punctuationem (marking with points), from Latin punctus (point).
