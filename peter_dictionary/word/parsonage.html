parsonage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsənɪdʒ / (BrE)
A house provided by a church for its parson, vicar, or rector to live in.

Examples

First of all there was the parsonage, an utterly impossible place for civilized people to live in, originally poorly conceived, apparently not repaired for years, with no plumbing or sewage, with rat-holes and rot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manse - a house provided for a minister, especially in Presbyterian churches
  • Synonym: rectory - the residence of a rector of a church
  • Synonym: vicarage - the residence of a vicar

Etymology

From Old French *personage*, meaning 'benefice of a parson', and ultimately from Medieval Latin *personāticum* (residence of a parson).
