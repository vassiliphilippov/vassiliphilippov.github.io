lodging

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
a place to stay temporarily, especially for sleeping or living, such as a hotel, inn, or rented room

Examples

The AAA then splits it down this way: $ 10.50 for meals, $ 9.50 for lodging, $ 7 for gas and oil, and $ 2 for tips and miscellaneous.
The $ 9.50 for lodging they save.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place to stay or live, often temporarily

Etymology

From Middle English 'loggyng', from Anglo-Norman 'loger' (to lodge).
