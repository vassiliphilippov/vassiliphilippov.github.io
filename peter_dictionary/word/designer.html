designer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzaɪnər / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who plans and creates the interior spaces of buildings, including the selection of furnishings and decorative elements

Examples

The American Institute of Interior Designers has published a recommended course for designers and a percentage layout of such a course.
This kind of irresponsibility toward their students can scarcely build a strong professional attitude in the future designer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interior decorator - person who decorates the interiors of buildings

Etymology

From design + -er, meaning a person who designs.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈzaɪnɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who creates plans or drawings for something to be built, made, or produced, such as buildings, clothes, machines, or other objects

Examples

Stowey Rummel was internationally famous, a crafter of a genuine Americana in foreign eyes, an original designer whose inventive childishness with steel and concrete was made even more believably sincere by his personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architect - a person who designs buildings
  • Synonym: planner - someone who makes detailed arrangements for projects

Etymology

From design + -er, influenced by French 'designer'.
