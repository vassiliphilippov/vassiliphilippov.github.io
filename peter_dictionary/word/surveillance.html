surveillance

1. NOUN

/ sərˈveɪ.ləns / (AmE), / sɜːˈveɪ.ləns / (BrE)
The careful watching of a person or group, especially by the police or other authorities, to gather information or prevent crime.

Examples

The jury did not elaborate, but it added that "there should be periodic surveillance of the pricing practices of the concessionaires for the purpose of keeping the prices reasonable ".
Not discussed here are some military problems of modern times such as undersea warfare, where the surveillance, sending, transmitting, and receiving are all so inadequate that networks and decision making are not the bottlenecks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitoring - the regular observation and recording of activities or data
  • Synonym: observation - the action or process of closely observing something or someone

Etymology

From French 'surveillance' (oversight), from 'surveiller' (to watch over), from Latin 'super' (over) + 'vigilare' (to watch).
