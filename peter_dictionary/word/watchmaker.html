watchmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
A person who makes or repairs watches, especially as a profession.

Examples

In 1846 Matthew B. Goodwin, jeweler and watchmaker, became the town's first telegrapher in a dwelling he built for himself and his business "two doors north of the Equinox House "or "one door north of the Bank, Manchester, Vermont ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clockmaker - a person who makes or repairs clocks

Etymology

watch + maker: from 'watch' (timepiece) and 'maker' (one who makes).
