ritualize

1. VERB

/ ˈrɪtʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃuəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something into a ritual or give it the characteristics of a ritual.

Examples

Often it is recognized that all the details of the pattern may not be essential to the outcome but, because the pattern was empirically determined and not developed through theoretical understanding, one is never quite certain which behavior elements are effective, and the whole pattern becomes ritualized.

Synset Examples

The growing up of children has become ritualized in many cultures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritual - a set of fixed actions performed regularly, often as a ceremony

Etymology

From 'ritual' + '-ize', formed in English after similar patterns.
