nonoccurrence

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːn.əˈkɝː.əns / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əˈkɜː.rəns / (BrE)
The fact of not happening or taking place; the absence of an event or incident.

Examples

On the theory before us, such relief would be groundless, for in that suffering itself there was nothing bad at all, and hence in its nonoccurrence there would be nothing to be relieved about.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occurrence - the fact of something happening or taking place

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'occurrence' (something that happens); 'occurrence' from Latin 'occurrere' (to happen, to occur).
