boxed

1. ADJ

/ bɑkst / (AmE), / bɒkst / (BrE)
(of text or an element) enclosed in or set off by a border or box, especially in print or display.

Examples

There are boxed proclamations in the newspaper of watchings, pinnings, ringings, engagements, and marriages in a scrupulously graded hierarchy of felicity.

Synset Examples

boxed sections of the report
boxed announcements in the newspaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framed - enclosed or surrounded by a frame

Etymology

Formed from 'box' + '-ed', indicating something provided with a box or enclosure.
