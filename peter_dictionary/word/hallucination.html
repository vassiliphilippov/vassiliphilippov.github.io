hallucination

1. NOUN

/ həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
An experience in which a person sees, hears, feels, or senses something that does not exist outside their mind, typically as a symptom of a mental disorder or as an effect of drugs.

Examples

Much of the former extreme uneasiness associated with visions and hallucinations and with death has disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief held despite evidence to the contrary
  • Degree Lower: illusion - a perception that misrepresents physical reality

Etymology

From Latin hallucinatio (wandering of the mind), from hallucinari (to wander in mind, dream).
