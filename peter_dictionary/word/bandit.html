bandit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪt / (AmE), / ˈbændɪt / (BrE)
an armed thief, especially one who is a member of a group and attacks travelers or settlements

Examples

"You must be a very learned man ", says Voltaire to one of the bandits.
"A learned man "? the bandit laughs in his face.
He refuses to believe that the bandit chief never attended a higher institution.
Just some lousy bandits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robber - a person who steals, especially by force or threat
  • Synonym: outlaw - a person who has broken the law and is not protected by it
  • Antonym: policeman - a member of the police force

Etymology

From Italian *bandito* (outlaw), past participle of *bandire* (to banish).
