flooded

1. ADJ

/ ˈflʌdəd / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or area) covered or submerged by water, typically as a result of heavy rain, overflow, or flooding.

Examples

The infield was well flooded but the expanded outfield was much too dark.

Synset Examples

the main deck was afloat (or awash)
the monsoon left the whole place awash
a flooded bathroom
inundated farmlands
an overflowing tub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inundated - flooded or overwhelmed, especially with water
  • Antonym: dry - not wet or covered with water

Etymology

From the verb 'flood', from Old English *flōd* (a flowing of water, inundation).
