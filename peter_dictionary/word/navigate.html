navigate

1. VERB

/ ˈnæv.ɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈnæv.ɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to travel or direct the course of a ship, boat, or other vessel on water, especially using maps, instruments, or knowledge of the area

Examples

The rancher was navigating his way across the flatland.
"Do you always navigate like this "?

Synset Examples

The QE2 will sail to Southampton tomorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sail - to travel in a boat or ship, usually by wind power
  • Synonym: pilot - to steer or guide a ship or boat

Etymology

From Latin navigare (to sail, to travel by ship), from navis (ship) + agere (to drive).
