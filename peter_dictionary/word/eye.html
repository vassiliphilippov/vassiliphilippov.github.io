eye

1. NOUN

/ aɪ / (AmE), / aɪ / (BrE)
The organ of sight in humans and animals; the part of the body that detects light and enables vision.

Examples

If, in Larkin's eyes, they are nothing but Piccadilly farmers, he has as much to learn about them as they have to learn about the ways of truly rural living.

As in the theory of perception, established in psycho-physiology, the eye is recognized as an integral part of the brain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ear - the organ of hearing and balance

Etymology

From Old English *ēage*, from Proto-Germanic *augō*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₃ekʷ- (to see).
