arraign

1. VERB

/ əˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈreɪn / (BrE)
to accuse or criticize someone, especially publicly or formally, for a wrongdoing or fault

Examples

He arraigned the citizens in language of so little courtesy that they had to respond with, at the least, resentment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accuse - to charge someone with an offense or crime
  • Synonym: criticize - to point out the faults of someone or something
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *araisnier*, from Vulgar Latin *arrationare* (to address in a speech), from Latin *ad-* (to) + *rationem* (reason, account).

2. VERB

/ əˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈreɪn / (BrE)
to formally call someone before a court of law to answer a criminal charge

Examples

The latter in turn assured him that "were I arraigned at the bar, and you my judge, I should expect to stand or fall only by the merits of my cause ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indict - to formally accuse of or charge with a serious crime
  • Antonym: acquit - to officially declare not guilty of a criminal charge

Etymology

From Old French 'areigner' (to address in court), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'rationem' (account, reason).
