enduringly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdʊrɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that continues or lasts for a long time; in an enduring manner.

Examples

Everywhere within the common pattern, however, one finds local diversity; Greek history and culture were enduringly fertilized, and plagued, by the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors.

Synset Examples

Roman culture was enduringly fertilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts for a long time without change
  • Antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

From 'enduring' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Enduring' from Latin 'indurare' (to harden).
