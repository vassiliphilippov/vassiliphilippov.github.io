confession

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
a statement in which a person admits to committing a crime, wrongdoing, or fault

Examples

The tensions accompanying a repressive consciousness of wrongdoing or sinning or some tormenting secret are relieved for the less self-contained or self-sufficient by confession, repentance, and penance.
Refugees poured in, signing the Confession and rules in order to remain, and then disregarding them.
Done in the modern style of a "confession ", Fink tells in exquisite detail how he came to know, and, more important, love his mother's pet salamander, Alicia.
Do you deny this confession, and do you realize what will happen as a result of your denial?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - an act of acknowledging something as true; often used for admitting facts or faults
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or did not happen; the refusal to admit a fact or fault

Etymology

From Latin confessio (acknowledgment), from confiteri (to confess).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the act of admitting one's sins to a priest in the sacrament of penance, with the intention of receiving absolution.

Examples

It went to church on Sunday and one Saturday a month went to confession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penance - a sacrament in which a member of the Church confesses sins to a priest and is given absolution

Etymology

From Latin confessio (acknowledgment, confession).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
a written statement in which someone admits to having committed a crime or offense, typically signed by the person admitting guilt

Examples

He wondered how long it would be before they had a signed confession from Lionel Black.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - statement acknowledging something is true, especially a crime
  • Antonym: denial - statement saying that something is not true, often in contrast to a confession

Etymology

From Latin 'confessiō' (acknowledgment, admission).
