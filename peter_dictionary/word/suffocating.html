suffocating

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌfəˌkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfəkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of air, heat, or a place) making it difficult to breathe, especially because of lack of fresh air or excessive heat.

Examples

Molten glare singed their eyelids an angry crimson; suffocating air sapped their strength and strained their nerves to snapping; dust choked their throats and lay like acid in their lungs.

Synset Examples

the choking June dust
the smothering soft voices
smothering heat
the room was suffocating--hot and airless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stifling - oppressively hot or stuffy; making it hard to breathe
  • Synonym: oppressive - (of weather or a place) uncomfortably hot or close

Etymology

From suffocate + -ing; ultimately from Latin suffocare (to choke).
