incarnate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑrnət / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑːnət / (BrE)
(of a quality or idea) embodied in physical form; made flesh.

Examples

The wife, Amra, and her lover are both savagely portrayed, she as incarnate sensuality, "voluptuous "and "indolent ", possibly "a mischief maker ", with "a kind of luxurious cunning "to set against her apparent simplicity, her "bird like brain ".

Synset Examples

what seemed corporal melted as breath into the wind
an incarnate spirit
`corporate' is an archaic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodied - expressed or represented in a tangible or visible form

Etymology

From Latin incarnatus, past participle of incarnare (to make flesh) from in- (into) + caro (flesh).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɑrnət, ɪnˈkɑrneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑːnət, ɪnˈkɑːneɪt / (BrE)
to embody or represent a quality, idea, or principle in a tangible or visible form; to make concrete or real

Examples

Insofar as these nations claim to incarnate traditions and ways of life which constitute ultimate, trans-political justifications for their existence, such people are inevitably led to emphasize the ways in which these traditions and ways are theirs rather than someone else's.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embody - to give form to an idea, feeling, or quality

Etymology

From Late Latin incarnare, from Latin in- (into) + caro, carnis (flesh).
