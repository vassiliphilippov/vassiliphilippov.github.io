tiger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
A person who is particularly fierce, energetic, or audacious.

Examples

The meekest, most submissive wife of today is a tiger by her mother's or grandmother's standards.

Synset Examples

he's a tiger on the tennis court
it aroused the tiger in me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lion - a person regarded as brave or courageous

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
A large wild cat native to Asia, with a tawny coat and black stripes, known for its strength and predatory skills; now endangered in the wild.

Examples

Ambushes are laid by those who doubt themselves, as any man may against a tiger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lion - large tawny wild cat native to Africa and India

Etymology

From Old French 'tigre', from Latin 'tigris', from Greek 'tigris', ultimately of Iranian origin.
