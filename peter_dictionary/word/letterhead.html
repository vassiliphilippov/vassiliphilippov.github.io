letterhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəˌhɛd / (BrE)
A sheet of paper with the name, address, and other details of an organization printed at the top, used for official correspondence.

Examples

Not only do these quacks assume impressive titles, but represent themselves as being associated with various scientific or impressive foundations - foundations which often have little more than a letterhead existence.

Etymology

From 'letter' (written message) + 'head' (top part), referring to the header area of stationery.
