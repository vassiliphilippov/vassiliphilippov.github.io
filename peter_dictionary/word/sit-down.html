sit-down

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.daʊn / (BrE)
A form of protest or strike in which workers or demonstrators refuse to leave the workplace or protest area until their demands are addressed.

Examples

"Speaking of nonism: the other day, in a story about a sit-down demonstration, the Paris Herald Tribune wrote,' The non violence became noisier'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - an organized refusal to work as a form of protest

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'sit down', meaning to adopt a seated position, + noun usage to name the act of group sitting as protest (first recorded in the 1930s, in labor union contexts).
