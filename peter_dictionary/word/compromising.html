compromising

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpraɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈprʌɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to make concessions or to yield, especially during a disagreement; inclined to settle differences by mutual agreement

Examples

Loneliness tore through him like a physical pain whenever he thought of Peter Robert, Nerien, Nicholas Cop, Martin Bucer, and even the compromising Louis du Tillet.

Synset Examples

loneliness tore through him...whenever he thought of...even the compromising Louis du Tillet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciliatory - intended or likely to placate or pacify
  • Antonym: unyielding - not giving way to pressure, stubborn or inflexible

Etymology

From the verb 'compromise,' from Middle French 'compromis,' from Latin 'compromissum' (mutual promise).
