restrictive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪv / (BrE)
imposing limitations or controls; preventing or limiting freedom, action, or development.

Examples

The monitoring is the highest and most restrictive of any organization in existence.

Synset Examples

teenagers eager to escape restrictive home environments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictivus,' from 'restringere' meaning 'to bind fast.'

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪv / (BrE)
(of a tariff or law) designed to protect national interests by limiting or controlling imports.

Examples

As I have repeatedly stated, this provision is much more restrictive than the general law, popularly known as the Buy American Act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protective - (of a tariff) designed to shield domestic industries from foreign competition
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior or policy

Etymology

From Latin *restrictivus*, from *restringere* (to bind fast, restrict).
