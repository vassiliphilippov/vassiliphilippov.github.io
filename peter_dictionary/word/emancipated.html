emancipated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmænsɪˌpeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
freed from traditional social restraints, especially regarding behavior or roles

Examples

Some date it from woman suffrage, others from when women first began to challenge men in the marketplace, still others from the era of the emancipated flapper and bathtub gin.

Synset Examples

an emancipated young woman pursuing her career
a liberated lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberated - freed from traditional social or moral restrictions

Etymology

From Latin 'emancipatus', past participle of 'emancipare' (set free).
