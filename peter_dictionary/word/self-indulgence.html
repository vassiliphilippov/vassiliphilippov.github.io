self-indulgence

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌlʤəns / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdʌl.dʒəns / (BrE)
the act of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasures or satisfy desires, often excessively or without restraint

Examples

That absence of an urgent self-indulgence dashed them awake like a pail of water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: self-control - restraint exercised over one's own impulses and desires
  • Synonym: hedonism - the pursuit of pleasure as a principle or way of life

Etymology

From self- + indulgence; 'indulgence' from Latin indulgentia (leniency, favor).
