vibration

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
the rapid movement back and forth of an object or substance, or the act of vibrating

Examples

Recently many doctors have installed ultrasonic vibration machines for deep massage of bruises, contusions, and simple bursitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillation - repeated movement from side to side or up and down

Etymology

From Latin 'vibratio' (a shaking or brandishing), from 'vibrare' (to shake).
