gristmill

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪstˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪstˌmɪl / (BrE)
A mill for grinding grain, especially one where grain brought by customers is ground into flour or meal.

Examples

The first mention of an electric plant in Manchester seems to be one installed in Reuben Colvin's and Houghton's gristmill on the West Branch in Factory Point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mill - a building equipped with machinery for grinding grain or other materials

Etymology

From 'grist' (grain to be ground) + 'mill'.
