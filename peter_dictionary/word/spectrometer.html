spectrometer

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈtrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / spɛkˈtrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure and analyze the properties of light or particles, especially by separating them into a spectrum; often used to identify the composition or mass of a sample

Examples

The magnetic resonance absorption was detected by employing a Varian model f broad line spectrometer** and the associated 12 - inch electromagnet system.
The spectrometer was adjusted to minimize the amount of dispersion mode mixed in with the absorption signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mass spectrometer - an instrument for measuring the masses and relative concentrations of atoms and molecules
  • Synonym: spectroscope - an instrument for producing and observing spectra

Etymology

From spectrum (Latin: appearance, image) + -meter (Greek: measure).
