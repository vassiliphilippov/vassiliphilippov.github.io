weakness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknəs / (BrE)
A fault or defect in someone's character or something that makes them less effective or successful; a weak point.

Examples

If we return to them today, we have no difficulty spotting their weaknesses but we find them still pleasing.
Although biopsies have shown structural changes in some of the reported cases of steroid induced weakness, this case provides the only example known to us in which necropsy afforded the opportunity for extensive study of multiple muscle groups.
The case described in this paper is that of an older man who developed disabling muscular weakness while receiving a variety of steroids for a refractory anemia.
This patient was a 65 - year old white male accountant who entered the New York Hospital for his fourth and terminal admission on June 26, 1959, because of disabling weakness and general debility.
In september, 1958, the patient developed generalized weakness and fatigue which was concurrent with exacerbation of his anemia; the hemoglobin was 10.6 gm..

Synset Examples

he was quick to point out his wife's failings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong or having strong qualities

Etymology

From Old English 'wācness', derived from 'wāc' (weak) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of lacking physical or mental strength; susceptibility to failure under pressure or stress.

Examples

Muscle weakness is now recognized as an uncommon though serious complication of steroid therapy, with most of the synthetic adrenal corticosteroids in clinical use.

Synset Examples

his weakness increased as he became older
the weakness of the span was overlooked until it collapsed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong

Etymology

From Old English wāc (weak) with the suffix -ness forming a noun of state or quality.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknɪs / (BrE)
The state or condition of being unable to act or lacking power; a lack of strength or ability that leaves one helpless or incapable.

Examples

For the oyabun to make such a trip was either a sign of great weakness or an indication of equally great confidence, and from all the available information it was probably the latter.
As for the night being warm, he would have been comfortable naked on Mount Everest but his water brothers had little tolerance for changes in temperature and pressure; he was considerate of their weakness, once he learned of it.

Synset Examples

in spite of their weakness the group remains active

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strength - the state of being strong or having power or ability
  • Synonym: helplessness - the state of being unable to help oneself; lack of ability or power

Etymology

From Old English wēacness, from wēac (weak) + -ness.

4. NOUN

/ ˈwiːknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknəs / (BrE)
a strong liking or affection for something, especially something not good for you

Examples

I have a sentimental weakness for my children, and I spoil them.

Synset Examples

he has a weakness for chocolate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fondness - affection or liking for something
  • Synonym: craving - intense desire for something, especially food or drink
  • Antonym: aversion - strong dislike for something

Etymology

From Old English wācnes (weakness), from wāc (weak).
