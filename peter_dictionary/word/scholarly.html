scholarly

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɑːlərli / (AmE), / ˈskɒləlɪ / (BrE)
involving or characteristic of serious academic study, research, or scholarship; showing learning or knowledge

Examples

Naturally, such scholarly facts are of little concern to the man trying to make money or fan patriotism by means of folklore.
During the rest of the summer my scholarly mania for making plaster casts and spatter prints of Catskill flowers and leaves was all but surpassed by the constantly renewed impressions of Jessica that my mind served up to me for contemplation and delight.

Synset Examples

scholarly pursuits
a scholarly treatise
a scholarly attitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic - relating to education or scholarship
  • Synonym: learned - having or showing much knowledge from study
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

Formed from 'scholar' + '-ly', meaning 'in the manner of a scholar'.
