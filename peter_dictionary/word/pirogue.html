pirogue

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈroʊɡ / (AmE), / pɪˈrəʊɡ / (BrE)
A type of canoe made by hollowing out and shaping a large log, traditionally used in parts of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Americas.

Examples

The slim pirogues in harmony with the majestically meandering Mekong River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugout - a canoe made from a hollowed tree trunk

Etymology

From French 'pirogue', from Spanish 'piragua', ultimately from Carib 'piraua'.
