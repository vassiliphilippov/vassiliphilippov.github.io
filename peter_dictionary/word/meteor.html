meteor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiɚ / (AmE), / ˈmiːtiə / (BrE)
A piece of rock or metal from space that enters the Earth's atmosphere and typically burns up, producing a streak of light visible from the ground.

Examples

A meteor could fall on San Francisco.
In addition, the size distribution obtained from visual and radar observations of meteors may be extrapolated to the micrometeorite domain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteoroid - a small body moving in the solar system that would become a meteor if it entered the Earth’s atmosphere
  • Synonym: meteorite - a meteor that survives passage through the atmosphere and reaches the Earth's surface

Etymology

From Greek meteōros (raised, high up), later applied to atmospheric phenomena.
