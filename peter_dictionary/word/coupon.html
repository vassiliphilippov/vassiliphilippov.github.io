coupon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːˌpɑn, ˈkuːˌpən / (AmE), / ˈkuːpɒn / (BrE)
a small piece of material taken as a representative sample for testing, especially in metallurgy and materials science.

Examples

In contrast, a metal coupon * * f in size would have a magnitude from 100000 to a million less.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuː.pɑn, ˈkuː.pɒn / (AmE), / ˈkuː.pɒn / (BrE)
A piece of paper or electronic code that may be used to obtain a discount, rebate, or a particular item when exchanged or redeemed.

Examples

They went shopping in the neighborhood, and bought two loaves of bread with the ration coupons they had been given in Blois, and some cheese, and a dozen eggs, and a bag of oranges from a peddler in the Place Redoute - the first oranges they had seen since they landed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voucher - a document that can be exchanged for goods or services

Etymology

From French 'coupon', from 'couper' meaning 'to cut'.
