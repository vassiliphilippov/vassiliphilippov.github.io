morose

1. ADJ

/ məˈroʊs / (AmE), / məˈrəʊs / (BrE)
having a sullen, gloomy, or ill-tempered manner or disposition; showing brooding ill humor.

Examples

Spencer's potential followers were openly sullen and morose, missing muster without excuse, expressing in ominous tones their displeasure at the prisoners being kept in irons, communicating with the three by glance and signal.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullen - bad-tempered, sulky, and gloomy
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin morosus (peevish, fretful), from mos (habit, manner).
