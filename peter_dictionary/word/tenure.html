tenure

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.jɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjə / (BrE)
the period of time during which someone holds a particular office, job, or position

Examples

He is not one to remain more comfortably and unquestioningly within a body of social, cultural, or literary traditions than he was within the traditions - or possibly the regulations - governing his tenure in the post office at Oxford, Mississippi, thirty-five years ago.
Tenure, too.
Six wardens have been through here in my tenure, and I've learned one immutable, universal truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incumbency - the period during which an official holds office

Etymology

From Latin 'tenura' (a holding), from 'tenere' (to hold) via Old French.
