crawl

1. VERB

/ krɔl / (AmE), / krɔːl / (BrE)
to move forward slowly with the body close to the ground, especially using the hands and knees

Examples

From the east to the west coast of the Korean peninsula was a strip of land in which fear filled men were at that same moment furtively crawling through the night, sitting in sweaty anticipation of any movement or sound, or shouting amidst confused rifle flashes and muzzle blasts.
The crawling men tried to rise and fell again.
The third crawling man forced himself erect.
Then he began to crawl again.
If only there was a clock for him to crawl against.

Synset Examples

The crocodile was crawling along the riverbed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly with the body close to the ground
  • Degree Higher: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting one foot after another

Etymology

From Middle English 'craule', of Scandinavian origin; related to Swedish 'kravla' and Danish 'kravle'.

2. VERB

/ krɔl / (AmE), / krɔːl / (BrE)
(of the skin) to feel as if insects are moving on the surface, typically as a result of fear, disgust, or unease.

Examples

Her skin crawled: Lolotte had told Maude that she was in the hall and the door was open.

Synset Examples

My skin crawled--I was terrified

3. NOUN

/ krɔl / (AmE), / krɔːl / (BrE)
a very slow rate of movement, especially when progress is impeded or sluggish

Examples

The car lurched along at a snail's crawl, the left front mudguard banging and scraping against the tire, occasionally scraping against the road itself.

Synset Examples

the traffic advanced at a crawl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully, typically in order to avoid being noticed

4. NOUN

/ krɔl / (AmE), / krɔːl / (BrE)
a slow movement in which a person or animal advances on hands and knees or by dragging the body close to the ground

Examples

Andy Dufresne, who crawled through a river of shit and came out clean on the other side.

Synset Examples

a crawl was all that the injured man could manage
the traffic moved at a creep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and quietly close to the ground
