inventor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛn.tə / (BrE)
A person who creates or designs something that has not existed before.

Examples

Manchester's unusual interest in telegraphy has often been attributed to the fact that the Rev. J. D. Wickham, headmaster of Burr and Burton Seminary, was a personal friend and correspondent of the inventor, Samuel F. B. Morse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovator - someone who introduces new ideas or methods

Etymology

From Latin 'inventor' (contriver, deviser), from 'invenire' (to come upon, find).
