backdrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A painted cloth or set piece hung at the back of a stage to provide background scenery; more generally, the setting or background for an event or scene.

Examples

Out of this background of hunting and fishing, it was only natural that Roy first painted subjects he knew best: hunters in the field, fishermen in the stream, ducks and geese on the wing - almost always against a vast backdrop of weather landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: background - the part of a scene or picture that forms a setting for the main figures or objects

Etymology

From back + drop, originally theatre jargon for a scenic curtain hung at the rear of a stage.
