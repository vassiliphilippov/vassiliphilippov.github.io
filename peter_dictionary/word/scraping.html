scraping

1. NOUN

/ ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A harsh or grating noise produced by something being scraped.

Examples

He opened it a crack and in doing so made as much shuffling, coughing, and scraping noise as possible in order to drown emanations from the hen who had begun to protest.

Synset Examples

the scrape of violin bows distracted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - a harsh sound produced by scraping

Etymology

From the verb 'scrape' + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskreɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
A small piece or fragment that has been scraped off a surface, typically collected for analysis or examination.

Examples

Scrapings from the bed linen identical with the lint and thread found in the nasal and oral cavities.

Synset Examples

they collected blood scrapings for analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaving - a thin slice or piece removed from a surface, typically with a tool

Etymology

From the verb 'scrape' + '-ing', indicating something resulting from the action of scraping.
