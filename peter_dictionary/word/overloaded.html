overloaded

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
having been loaded beyond the usual or legal limit; carrying more than can be safely handled.

Examples

We flew in rickety planes so overloaded that we wondered why they did n't crash.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underloaded - not loaded to full or normal capacity

Etymology

From 'over-' meaning excessively + 'loaded', past participle of 'load'.
