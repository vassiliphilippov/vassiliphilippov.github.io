conceptually

1. ADV

/ kənˈsɛptʃuəli / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptʃuəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to ideas or abstract concepts rather than to concrete facts or objects.

Examples

The law of nature governed sovereigns in their relationship to their own citizens, to foreigners, and to each other in a conceptually unified system.

Synset Examples

he can no longer think conceptually
conceptually, the idea is quite simple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstractly - in an abstract manner; not concrete

Etymology

From 'concept' (from Latin 'conceptus') + '-ually', an adverbial suffix.
