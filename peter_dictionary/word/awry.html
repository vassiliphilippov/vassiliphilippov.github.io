awry

1. ADV

/ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / əˈraɪ / (BrE)
away from the correct, expected, or intended course; not as planned or intended

Examples

There was only one place where Jake Carwood's description had gone badly awry: the peace and quiet.

Synset Examples

something has gone awry in our plans
something went badly amiss in the preparations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiss - not correct; wrong; out of place

Etymology

From Middle English awrye, from a- (on) + wry (twisted or turned).
