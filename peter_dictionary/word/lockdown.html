lockdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌdaʊn / (BrE)
A situation in which prisoners are confined to their cells, typically to restore order or ensure security.

Examples

All prisoners report for lockdown.

Etymology

From 'lock' (secure with a lock) + 'down' (direction), first used in U.S. prisons in the 1970s.
