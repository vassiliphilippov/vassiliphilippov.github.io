obsequious

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsiːkwiəs / (AmE), / əbˈsiːkwɪəs / (BrE)
Excessively eager to please or obey someone important; showing servile deference or flattery towards those in authority.

Examples

He was stern and overbearing with his flock, but obsequious and conciliatory with the whites, especially the rich who partly supported the church.
At the inn, which was situated close to a broad weir, Byron was greeted by the landlord with obsequious deference and addressed as "milord ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Synonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
  • Synonym: fawning - displaying exaggerated flattery or affection, typically to gain favor
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

From Latin obsequiosus (compliant, obliging), from obsequium (compliance, dutiful service).
