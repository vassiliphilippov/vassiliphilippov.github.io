bloodshed

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌʃɛd / (BrE)
killing or injury in which blood is spilled, especially during violent conflict or war

Examples

Cook Bennett Mathues said Juet had predicted bloodshed on the ship.
Alexander guessed that they had planned confusion and turmoil, thinking it the ideal climate in which to begin battle and bloodshed.

Synset Examples

he avenged the bloodshed of his kinsmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt or kill
  • Synonym: slaughter - the killing of many people or animals in a cruel or violent way
  • Antonym: peace - a state in which there is no war or violence

Etymology

From 'blood' + 'shed', meaning the spilling of blood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdʃɛd / (BrE)
the killing or wounding of people, especially in a violent or brutal way; the act or result of causing blood to be lost in conflict

Examples

I am resigning from office to avoid further bloodshed.

Synset Examples

a bloodbath took place when the leaders of the plot surrendered
ten days after the bloodletting Hitler gave the action its name
the valley is no stranger to bloodshed and murder
a huge prison battue was ordered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - the killing of many people, especially those who cannot defend themselves
  • Synonym: carnage - the killing of a large number of people
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from violence or war

Etymology

From blood + shed, meaning 'to cause blood to flow' (shed: Old English *sceadan*, to separate, divide).
