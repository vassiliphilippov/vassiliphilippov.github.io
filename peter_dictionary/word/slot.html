3. NOUN

/ slɑt / (AmE), / slɒt / (BrE)
A narrow opening or slit, especially one designed for inserting coins, letters, or similar items.

Examples

As he went out he told Freddie the dinner was perfect, and when he got his hat and coat from Nancy Parks and put a fifty-piece piece in the slot, he told her to be sure that it went toward her dowry.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French esclot (track, hoofprint), of Germanic origin.
