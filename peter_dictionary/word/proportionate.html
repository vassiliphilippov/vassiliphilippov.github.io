proportionate

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpɔrʃənət / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənət / (BrE)
Corresponding in size, amount, or degree; in keeping with something else in terms of quantity or extent.

Examples

The jurors said they realize "a proportionate distribution of these funds might disable this program in our less populous counties ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disproportionate - not corresponding in size or degree; out of proportion

Etymology

From Latin 'proportionatus', past participle of 'proportionare' (to proportion).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈpɔrʃənət / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənət / (BrE)
Corresponding in size, amount, or degree to something else; in due proportion.

Examples

Traditionally, rates of pay for retired military personnel have been proportionate to current rates of pay for active personnel.
The 1958 military pay act departed from this established formula by providing for a 6 % increase rather than a proportionate increase for everyone retired prior to its effective date of June 1, 1958.

Synset Examples

proportionate representation of a minority group

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disproportionate - not in correct or equal proportion
  • Synonym: commensurate - corresponding in size or extent; adequate; proportional

Etymology

From Latin 'proportionatus', past participle of 'proportionare' (to proportion).
