adjusted

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
changed in order to fit particular requirements or to achieve a desired result or condition.

Examples

The pre 1960 rate of Federal participation with respect to any State's base allotment, as well as the adjusted rate in effect during the 1960 - 1962 period, is designated by the statute as that State's "adjusted Federal Share ".

Synset Examples

an adjusted insurance claim

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaltered - not changed or modified
  • Synonym: modified - changed somewhat, usually to improve or make more suitable

Etymology

From the verb 'adjust', from Old French 'ajuster', from Late Latin 'adjuxtare' (to bring near).
