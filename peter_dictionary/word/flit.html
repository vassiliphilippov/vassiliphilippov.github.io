flit

1. VERB

/ flɪt / (AmE), / flɪt / (BrE)
to move lightly and quickly from one place to another, often in an apparently random or fleeting way

Examples

Stravinsky, nearing the age of eighty, is like a lost and frantic bird, flitting from one abandoned nest to another, searching for a home.

Synset Examples

The hummingbird flitted among the branches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dart - move or run somewhere suddenly or rapidly
  • Synonym: skim - move quickly and lightly over a surface

Etymology

From Middle English flitten, from Old Norse flytja (to move, convey).
