infect

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to pass a disease to a person, animal, or plant, causing them to become ill

Examples

It is like a mysterious epidemic which, starting first with Abigail and Parris, spreads inexorably with a dreadfully growing virulence through the whole town until all have been infected by it.
The concentration and apparent localization of the WTV antigen in pseudophloem tissue of the tumor may indicate that the virus preferentially multiplies in the phloem and may need to be directly placed in this tissue in order to infect plants.

Synset Examples

Your children have infected you with this head cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminate - to make something impure or harmful by contact with something unclean or infectious

Etymology

From Latin 'infectus' (stained, tainted), past participle of 'inficere' (to put in, stain, infect).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to contaminate (a person, animal, or thing) with a disease, microorganism, or virus; to cause to become diseased.

Examples

It was so cold and so wretched that a sort of desperate gaiety infected all of them, like people stormbound or shipwrecked or caught in some other freak of circumstance so that time stood still and minor anxieties fell away and the only important thing was to cling together and survive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminate - to make something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • Synonym: expose - to subject to risk of infection, harm, or danger
  • Antonym: sterilize - to make (something) free from bacteria or other living microorganisms
  • Antonym: cleanse - to remove dirt, infection, or toxins from something

Etymology

From Latin infectus, past participle of inficere 'to dye, taint, corrupt'.
