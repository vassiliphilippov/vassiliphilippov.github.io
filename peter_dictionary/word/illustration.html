illustration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪl.əˈstreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪl.əˈstreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an instance or example that helps explain or clarify a general statement, idea, or concept

Examples

The embarrassment of these theories over the naturalness of death is an illustration of the thesis that death cannot be only a punishment, for some termination seems necessary in a life that is lived within the natural order of time and change.

Synset Examples

this patient provides a typical example of the syndrome
there is an example on page 10

Related Terms

  • Synonym: example - a representative case used to illustrate a point
  • Synonym: instance - a particular occurrence of something, used to illustrate a point

Etymology

From Latin illustratio (an illuminating, making clear), from illustrare (to illuminate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a picture, diagram, or other artwork used to explain, decorate, or clarify a text

Examples

Seeing so many illustrations and reading so many testimonials to the value of Quick-Wate and Super-Protein, those two wonder-working Weider food supplements, he decided to try them and see what they could do for him.
The illustrations in fig. 8 show how to lay a concrete block wall.
The illustration (fig. 11) shows this shelter with the roof at ground level and mounded over.
The book could be The Brothers Karamazov, but it would still have the same jacket illustration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something
  • Synonym: photograph - a picture made using a camera

Etymology

From Latin illustratio (a making clear, enlightening), from illustrare (to make bright, illustrate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪləˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a picture, diagram, or other visual representation used to explain, decorate, or clarify something in a text or presentation

Examples

Hino talked very little about himself except for the infrequent times when he used a personal illustration in connection with another subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something
  • Synonym: picture - a painting, drawing, or photograph
  • Antonym: text - written or printed words, contrasted to images or illustrations

Etymology

From Latin illustratio (enlightenment, explanation), from illustrare (to illuminate, make clear).

Examples

We assume for this illustration that the size of the land plots is so great that the distance between dwellings is greater than the voice can carry and that most of the communication is between nearest neighbors only, as shown in Figure 2.

Another case may be given in illustration of a successful use of analysis, and also of the employment of a procedure for intensive analysis.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin illustratio (enlightenment, explanation), from illustrare (to make light, explain).
