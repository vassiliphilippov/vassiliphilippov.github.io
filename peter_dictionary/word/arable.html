arable

1. ADJ

/ ˈær.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈær.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of land) suitable for growing crops; able to be farmed productively.

Examples

We consider a rural community as an assemblage of inhabited dwellings whose configuration is determined by the location and size of the arable land sites necessary for family subsistence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertile - (of land) capable of producing abundant crops
  • Antonym: infertile - (of land) not able to support the growth of crops

Etymology

From Latin *arabilis* (that can be plowed), from *arare* (to plow).
