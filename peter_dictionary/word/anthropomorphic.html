anthropomorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of a non-human thing) described or thought of as having human form or characteristics, especially ascribing human traits to animals, objects, or deities.

Examples

The dominant pottery of the century was Geometric; political organization revolved about the basileis; trade was just beginning to expand; the gods who protected the Greek countryside were only now putting on their sharply anthropomorphic dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personification - attribution of human qualities to abstract ideas or things

Etymology

From Greek anthropomorphos, from anthrōpos (human) + morphē (form).
