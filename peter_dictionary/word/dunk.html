dunk

1. VERB

/ dʌŋk / (AmE), / dʌŋk / (BrE)
to immerse something briefly in a liquid, especially so as to wet, coat, or saturate it

Examples

No bottle pool is tolerated - bottle pool being our lingo for those who come to swim and sink into our bar while protesting that they can only dunk and run.

Synset Examples

dip the garment into the cleaning solution
dip the brush into the paint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dip - to put something into a liquid quickly and then take it out
