polyelectrolyte

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliɪˈlɛktroʊˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɒliɪˈlɛktrəʊˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A polymer containing ionizable groups that make it behave as an electrolyte in solution; typically a high molecular weight compound whose repeating units bear electrolyte groups.

Examples

This is particularly true of highly charged ions, especially those ions which fall into the class of polyelectrolytes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electrolyte - a substance that conducts electricity when dissolved in water

Etymology

From poly- (many) + electrolyte, indicating a substance with many electrolyte groups.
