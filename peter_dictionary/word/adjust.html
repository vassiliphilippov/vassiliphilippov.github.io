adjust

1. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to change or regulate something slightly in order to improve it, make it more accurate, or conform to a particular standard

Examples

Place your Power Stands in position and adjust their height so that this will correspond to the height of your shoulders when you are in a deep leg split as for a heavy Clean.
This tube could be adjusted in its axial direction by an electric drive to establish the required electrode spacing.
The spectrometer was adjusted to minimize the amount of dispersion mode mixed in with the absorption signal.
"I 've adjusted the gauge so that the pump cuts out before the water gets too low ".
The shutter aperture may be made larger or smaller by changing the foil area and adjusting the electrical energy input to the foil.

Synset Examples

Adjust the clock, please
correct the alignment of the front wheels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regulate - to control or maintain the rate or speed of a machine or process
  • Synonym: calibrate - to adjust or mark so as to be precise for a measurement
  • Antonym: misadjust - to adjust incorrectly

Etymology

From Old French *ajoster* 'to join, adjust', from Latin *ad* 'to' + *iungere* 'to join'.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: align - to arrange in a line or bring into alignment
  • Synonym: straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From Old French ajuster, from Late Latin adjuxtare, frequentative of Latin juxta (near).

4. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to change something so that it becomes more appropriate or fits a particular standard, condition, or situation

Examples
