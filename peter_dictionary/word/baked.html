baked

1. ADJ

/ beɪkt / (AmE), / beɪkt / (BrE)
Dried out or hardened by heat or excessive exposure to sunlight.

Examples

And the valley stretched endlessly out ahead, scorched and baked and writhing in its heat, until it vanished into the throbbing wall of fiery orange brown haze.

Synset Examples

a vast desert all adust
land lying baked in the heat
parched soil
the earth was scorched and bare
sunbaked salt flats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - dried out, especially from heat
  • Synonym: scorched - burned and dried by intense heat
  • Synonym: sunbaked - dried and hardened by exposure to the sun

Etymology

From bake (verb), ultimately from Old English *bacan* (to bake).
