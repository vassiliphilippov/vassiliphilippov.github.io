froth

1. NOUN

/ frɑθ / (AmE), / frɒθ / (BrE)
A mass of small bubbles formed in or on the surface of a liquid.

Examples

The Indian's arm whipped sidewise - there was a flash of amber and froth, the crash of the bottle shattering against the side of the first car.

Synset Examples

the beer had a thick head of foam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles on or in liquid

Etymology

From Old Norse 'froða' (froth, foam), related to Old English 'āfrēothan' (to froth up).
