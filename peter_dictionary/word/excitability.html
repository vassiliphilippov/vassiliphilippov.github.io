excitability

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsʌɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being abnormally or excessively responsive to stimuli, especially in an organ or body part.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - abnormal or excessive responsiveness to stimulation (esp. in physiology)

Etymology

From Latin excitare (to rouse, excite) + English -bility (from -bility, forming nouns of capability).
