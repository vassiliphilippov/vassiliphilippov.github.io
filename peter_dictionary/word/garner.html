garner

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɑrnər / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnə / (BrE)
to obtain or collect something, especially information, support, or approval, through effort or achievement

Examples

Mr. Papp has directed a performance that has verve and pace, although he has tolerated obvious business to garner easy laughs where elegance and consistency of style would be preferable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earn - to receive as return for effort or service
  • Synonym: acquire - to come into possession of; obtain
  • Antonym: lose - to be deprived of, or cease to have

Etymology

From Middle English *garneren*, from Old French *garnir* (to equip, provide), of Germanic origin.
