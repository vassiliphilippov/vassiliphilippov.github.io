hatchway

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhatʃweɪ / (BrE)
an opening in a ship or building, especially one between decks, that is fitted with a hatch or cover

Examples

An alternate hatchway entrance, shown on page 25, would reduce the cost of materials $ 50 to $ 100.
Plans for the shelter, with either a stairway or hatchway entrance, are shown on pages 26 and 27.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatch - a door or cover for an opening in a ship or building

Etymology

Formed from "hatch" (a cover or door) + "way" (a passage).
