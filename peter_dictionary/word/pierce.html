pierce

1. VERB

/ pɪrs / (AmE), / pɪəs / (BrE)
to force a way through or into something; to penetrate or pass through.

Examples

Ever since he had first begun to study music and to teach it, Rousseau had dreamed of piercing through to fame as the result of a successful opera.
It was pierced by a wagon gate built of two wings.

Synset Examples

the knife cut through the flesh
The path pierced the jungle
Light pierced through the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penetrate - to enter or force a way into or through something
  • Synonym: perforate - to pierce and make a hole or series of holes

Etymology

From Old French *percer*, of uncertain origin, possibly from Vulgar Latin *pertusiare* (to bore through).
