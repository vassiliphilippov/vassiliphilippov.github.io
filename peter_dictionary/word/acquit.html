acquit

1. VERB

/ əˈkwɪt / (AmE), / əˈkwɪt / (BrE)
to officially declare in a court of law that someone is not guilty of a criminal charge

Examples

On one of his 1921 ventures he was actually come upon by a Detective Sergeant John J. Ryan down on his knees with a tool embedded in a labour office safe in the Postal Telegraph Building; the jury wanted better evidence than that and he was acquitted, at a cost of $ 30000 in bribes, it was estimated.

Synset Examples

The suspect was cleared of the murder charges

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convict - declare someone guilty of a criminal offense

Etymology

From Old French 'aquiter' (to pay, settle), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'quietare' (to quiet, settle).
