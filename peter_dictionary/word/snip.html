snip

1. NOUN

/ snɪp / (AmE), / snɪp / (BrE)
A small piece of something, especially one that has been cut off with scissors or shears.

Examples

Brief snips of actual events were shown: parades, dances, street scenes.

Etymology

Imitative; from the verb 'snip', likely of imitative origin, suggesting a sharp cutting sound.
