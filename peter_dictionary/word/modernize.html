modernize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑːdərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪz / (BrE)
to make changes or improvements to something so that it is up to date or more suitable for present needs

Examples

It recommended that Fulton legislators act "to have these laws studied and revised to the end of modernizing and improving them ".
How much will it cost to do any necessary modernizing and redecorating?
He also expanded and modernized the radio system with a central control station.

Synset Examples

You should overhaul your car engine
overhaul the health care system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - to bring something up to date
  • Synonym: renovate - to restore or improve by repairing or remodeling
  • Antonym: antiquate - to make old-fashioned or obsolete

Etymology

From modern + -ize, meaning 'to make modern'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑdɚˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪz / (BrE)
to become or cause to become technologically advanced or up to date

Examples

American policy should press constantly the view that until these governments demand efficiency and effectiveness of their bureaucracies there is not the slightest hope that they will either modernize of democratize their societies.

Synset Examples

Many countries in Asia are now developing at a very fast pace
Viet Nam is modernizing rapidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - make something more modern or up to date

Etymology

From modern + -ize, formed in English in the early 19th century.
