vanity

1. NOUN

/ ˈvænəti / (AmE), / ˈvænɪti / (BrE)
Excessive pride in or admiration of one's own appearance, abilities, or achievements.

Examples

On the street outside, Hieronymus envisions a holocaust of the vanities of this world, such a burning of artistic and erotic productions as his namesake actually brought to pass in Florence, and prophetically he issues his curse: "Gladius Dei super terram cito et velociter ".
"You seem to have read so much, you have a natural gift for words ", he added, trying to flatter her vanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French vanité, from Latin vanitas ('emptiness, futility, pride'), from vanus ('empty').
