yonder

1. ADJ

/ ˈjɑndər / (AmE), / ˈjɒndə / (BrE)
at some distance in the direction indicated; distant but within sight.

Examples

He had crossed the river which now, beyond the woods yonder, was sliding darkly under the mist.

Synset Examples

yonder valley
the hills yonder
what is yon place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: there - in that place or position

Etymology

From Middle English yonder, from Old English geond (beyond, over there).
