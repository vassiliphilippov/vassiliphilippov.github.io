restrained

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈstreɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnd / (BrE)
Showing control of one's feelings or behaviour; behaving in a cool, formal, or unemotional way.

Examples

They are n't "frigid "and they are n't homosexual; they 're just restrained in all of life.
Owing to the restrained usages characteristic of 19 th century America, these letters usually were stereotyped and revealed little depth of feeling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotion or opinions; shy or restrained in manner
  • Antonym: unrestrained - not controlled or held back; uninhibited

Etymology

From restrain + -ed, based on Latin restringere 'bind fast, hold back'.

2. ADJ

/ ɹɪˈstreɪnd / (AmE), / ɹɪˈstreɪnd / (BrE)
showing control or moderation; not excessive or flamboyant

Examples

The Ivy League enjoys no easy dominion here, and the boys are as likely to dress in rather foppish Continental fashion, or even in nondescript working class manner, as they are in the restrained, button-down Ivy way.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrestrained - not controlled or held back; excessive or free

Etymology

From restrain + -ed, from Old French restreindre (to hold back), from Latin restringere (to bind fast).
