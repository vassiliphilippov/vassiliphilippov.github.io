lunatic

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːnətɪk / (AmE), / ˈluːnətɪk / (BrE)
Insane; mentally ill, especially as formerly believed to be influenced by the phases of the moon. Now chiefly archaic or offensive.

Examples

Public relations strategists everywhere, watching the reaction of the German press, the liberal press, the lunatic fringe press, listening to their neighbors, studying interviews with men and women on the street, cried out: Too much, too much - the mind of the audience is becoming dulled, the horrors are losing their effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; deranged
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not insane

Etymology

From Latin 'lunaticus' (moonstruck), from 'luna' (moon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːnəˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈluːnətɪk / (BrE)
an offensive and old-fashioned term for an insane person

Examples

JOHNNY: Those rebels, you know, they'’re lunatics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madman - a man who is insane; synonym for lunatic
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy; antonym for lunatic

Etymology

From Latin 'lunaticus' (moonstruck), from 'luna' (moon), due to the belief that the moon caused madness.
