academicianship

1. NOUN

/ əˌkædəˌmɪʃənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / əˌkadɪˌmɪʃənʃɪp / (BrE)
the status or position of being an academician, especially as a member of an honorary academy

Examples

He was elected to the National Academy of Design as an Associate in the oil class in 1931 (after receiving his first Ranger Fund Purchase Prize at the Academy in 1930), and elevated to Academicianship in 1940.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academician - a member of an honorary academy

Etymology

From academician + -ship (denoting status, office, or skill).
