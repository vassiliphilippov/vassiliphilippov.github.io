pH

1. NOUN

/ pi eɪtʃ / (AmE), / piː eɪtʃ / (BrE)
A measure of the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, defined as the logarithm of the reciprocal of hydrogen ion concentration on a scale from 0 (very acidic) to 14 (very basic), with 7 being neutral.

Examples

Fahey and Morrison used a single, continuous gradient at constant pH for the fractionation of anti - A and anti - B agglutinins from preisolated | g-globulin samples.

Etymology

Abbreviation of German 'Potenz' (power) + H (for hydrogen ion concentration).
