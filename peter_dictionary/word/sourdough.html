sourdough

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊrˌdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊədəʊ / (BrE)
a mixture of flour and water containing wild yeast and bacteria, used as a leavening agent in baking bread

Examples

He poured the water off the sourdough and off the flour, salvaging the chunky, watery messes for biscuits of a sort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - microscopic fungus used in bread making

Etymology

From Middle English ‘sour dough’, indicating dough that has gone sour through fermentation.
