shed

1. VERB

/ ʃɛd / (AmE), / ʃɛd / (BrE)
to get rid of or discard something, especially something no longer wanted or needed; to cast off.

Examples

God knew his tears were his to shed if he so desired, for it had not been with an egotist's rage for fame that he had held precious his naval career.
How far that pedimented and pillared style has shed its influence Mr. Sansom reminds us thus:
When shed, they leave rust marks.
Clyde Miller was crying softly to himself, shedding his striped suit and fumbling into the nondescript butternut pants, the worn brown shirt.

Synset Examples

he shed his image as a pushy boss
shed your clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of something
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

Etymology

Old English *sceadan* (to separate, divide).

2. NOUN

/ ʃɛd / (AmE), / ʃɛd / (BrE)
a small, simple building, usually in a garden or yard, used for storage or as shelter for animals, tools, or equipment

Examples

And most of all it is not having the only man you could love, whether he drives a bread truck or delivers the mail or checks the berry crates down at the sheds, or owns seventeen oil wells and six diamond mines, for if you are anybody what he is or does makes no difference if he is the one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small simple dwelling or shelter, usually single-storied
  • Degree Higher: barn - a large farm building for storing grain, hay, or livestock

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *shedde*, related to Old English *sceadan* (to divide, separate).

3. VERB

/ ʃɛd / (AmE), / ʃɛd / (BrE)
to allow or cause (a liquid or other substance) to flow out in drops or small amounts; to pour forth gradually

Examples

Because he copiously shed his own tears, and yielded himself up as a living sacrifice to the impersonalized conscience of New England, he was not disturbed by the havoc he worked in other people's consciences.
America, America, God shed His grace on thee, and crown thy good with brotherhood from sea to shining sea ".

Synset Examples

shed tears
spill blood
God shed His grace on Thee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - cause or allow (liquid) to flow over the edge of its container

Etymology

Old English *scēadan* (to separate, divide, part, scatter).
