quixotic

1. ADJ

/ kwɪkˈsɑtɪk / (AmE), / kwɪkˈsɒtɪk / (BrE)
Unrealistically idealistic; exceedingly impractical in pursuit of ideals, especially in a way that disregards practicality.

Examples

Smiling at his quixotic thoughts, Warren turned back from the opening and lit a cigarette before sitting down.
The sound discoveries of this quixotic genius were so diluted by those of fantasy that the prize was never awarded to him.

Synset Examples

as quixotic as a restoration of medieval knighthood
a romantic disregard for money
a wild-eyed dream of a world state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealistic - Believing in very high or noble goals, often impractical
  • Antonym: pragmatic - Dealing with things realistically and sensibly

Etymology

From Don Quixote, the title character of the novel by Miguel de Cervantes, known for his impractical, idealistic quests.
