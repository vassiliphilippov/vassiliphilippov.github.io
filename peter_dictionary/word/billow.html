billow

1. VERB

/ ˈbɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪləʊ / (BrE)
(of smoke, cloud, or fabric) to rise or swell outward in large, rolling waves.

Examples

Behind him billowed a small pungent cloud of smoke.

Synset Examples

smoke billowed up into the sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward

Etymology

From Old Norse *bylgja* (a wave).
