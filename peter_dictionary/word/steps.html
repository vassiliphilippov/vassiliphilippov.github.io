steps

1. NOUN

/ stɛps / (AmE), / stɛps / (BrE)
the path or course taken by someone as they walk, often referring to the trace or sequence of movements made by a person

Examples

If, as a home movie maker, you shoot the inevitable footage of your child taking its first steps, you have merely recorded an historical event.

Synset Examples

I followed in his steps
he retraced his steps
his steps turned toward home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footsteps - the marks or impressions left by feet or the act of walking in someone's path

2. NOUN

/ stɛps / (AmE), / stɛps / (BrE)
A set of stairs; a series of flat surfaces, one above the other, that lead from one level to another.

Examples

They turned at the bottom of Kate's steps and moved off in the direction of the park.
Quickening his steps, John entered the vast church and climbed the tower steps to the bells.
The slow tapping of a cane on the stone steps coming up to the tower interrupted his reverie.
He started down the steps to meet the near-blind preacher, who had been one of the early Gospelers in Paris.
She came to the ballroom and stood on the two carpeted steps that led down to it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stairs - a set of steps leading from one floor to another
  • Synonym: staircase - a structure containing a set of stairs
