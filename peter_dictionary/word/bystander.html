bystander

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌstændər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌstandə / (BrE)
A person who is present at an event or incident but does not take part.

Examples

I left it noisy, so it scares away any pain-in-the-ass innocent bystanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onlooker - a person who watches something that is happening but is not involved
  • Antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something

Etymology

From by + stander, meaning one who stands by.
