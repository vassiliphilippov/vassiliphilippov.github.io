prolific

1. ADJ

/ prəˈlɪfɪk / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfɪk / (BrE)
Producing many works, results, or ideas; highly productive, especially in a creative or intellectual field.

Examples

If the plants are cared for and protected over the winter, the second year is more prolific than the first.

Synset Examples

a prolific writer
a fecund imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fecund - producing many new ideas or creations
  • Antonym: unproductive - not producing much; not creative or fruitful

Etymology

From French prolifique, from Latin proles (offspring) + facere (to make).
