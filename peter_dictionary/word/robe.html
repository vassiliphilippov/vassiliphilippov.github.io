robe

1. NOUN

/ roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
a long, loose outer garment, often reaching to the ankles and worn for ceremonial, official, or informal purposes

Examples

He was able to discern the body lines of the Roman women under their robes.
I swam like mad, got out of the pool, grabbed my robe, and ran to the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gown - a woman's dress or a long, elegant garment

Etymology

From Old French *robe* (booty, spoil, later 'garment'), from Frankish *rauba* (spoils), related to 'rob'.

2. VERB

/ roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
to dress someone or oneself in formal or ceremonial clothing, especially robes worn for religious or official occasions

Examples

Michelangelo found the rabbi in the room of study, a gentle old man with a white beard and luminous grey eyes, robed in black gabardine with a skullcap on his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes on oneself or someone else, especially in a particular style
  • Antonym: disrobe - to remove one's clothes; to undress

Etymology

From Old French *robe* (long outer garment), from Frankish *rouba* (spoils, plunder).
