aerodynamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛroʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the properties of an object that affect how it moves through the air, especially with minimal resistance.

Examples

For example, the importance of the Regulus 2,, a very promising aerodynamic ship to surface missile designed to be launched by surfaced submarines, was greatly diminished by the successful acceleration of the much more advanced Polaris ballistic missile launched by submerged submarines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streamlined - having a shape designed to move easily through air or water
  • Antonym: drag - resistance by air to the movement of an object

Etymology

From aero- (air) and dynamic (from Greek dynamikos, powerful).
