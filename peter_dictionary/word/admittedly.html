admittedly

1. ADV

/ ədˈmɪtɪdli / (AmE), / ədˈmɪtɪdli / (BrE)
Used to acknowledge a fact or point, often before stating something that contrasts with or qualifies it.

Examples

Nor is it necessary to look for such evidence in the great urban centers of our culture that are admittedly almost entirely secularized and so profoundly estranged from the conventional forms in which the gospel has been communicated.

Synset Examples

true, she is the smartest in her class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concededly - used to admit a point, often reluctantly

Etymology

From 'admit' + '-edly', modeled after similar adverb formations.
