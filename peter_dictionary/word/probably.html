probably

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑbəbli / (AmE), / ˈprɒbəbli / (BrE)
Used to say that something is likely to happen or to be true, though not certain.

Examples

Cox argued that the bill is "probably unconstitutional "since, he said, it would impair contracts.
"We 'll work hard Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday ", Meek said, "and probably will have a good scrimmage Friday.
You 'll probably get a ball bat on the head.
Of the two cherished achievements the elderly Spahn's hitless pitching probably reached the most hearts.
That means Stan Musial probably will ride the bench on the seventh anniversary of his record five home run day against the Giants.

Synset Examples

He is probably out of the country
in all likelihood we are headed for war

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: possibly - used to indicate that something might happen or be true, but with more doubt than 'probably'
  • Degree Higher: certainly - used to indicate that something is sure or definite
  • Antonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true; the opposite of probable

Etymology

From Latin probabilis (provable, credible), via Middle French probable and English 'probable' + '-ly'.
