gadgetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædʒətri / (AmE), / ˈɡædʒɪtri / (BrE)
Small mechanical or electronic devices considered collectively; gadgets as a group.

Examples

If anything may be predicted in the quicksilver world of retailing, it seems likely that the suburban branch will come to dominate children's clothing (taking the kid downtown is too much of a production), household gadgetry and the discount business in big-ticket items.

Synset Examples

laborsaving gadgetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gadget - a small mechanical or electronic device with a practical use

Etymology

Formed from 'gadget' (origin uncertain) + suffix '-ry' indicating a collection or group.
