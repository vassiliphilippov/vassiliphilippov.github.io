preconceived

1. ADJ

/ priːkənˈsivd / (AmE), / priːkənˈsiːvd / (BrE)
(of an idea or opinion) formed before having evidence or experiencing something, especially in a way that is influenced by prejudice or assumptions.

Examples

Similarly experience itself can be conventionalized so that people react to certain preconceived clues for behavior without awareness of the vitality of their experiential field.

Synset Examples

certain preconceived notions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust based on preconceived ideas

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'concipere' (to take in, conceive).
