half-time

1. ADV

/ ˈhæf taɪm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf taɪm / (BrE)
For less than the standard number of hours, especially in reference to work or study schedules.

Examples

In both cases the student attends school half-time and works in a regular job the other half.

Synset Examples

he works part-time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: part-time - working or studying for only part of the standard hours

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'time', referring to half the usual period.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæfˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌtaɪm / (BrE)
Involving or working for only half the standard or customary amount of time for an activity or job.

Examples

To be sure, in tool-and-die work and in the building trades, the first job must be often on an apprentice basis, but two years of halftime vocational training enables the young man thus to anticipate one year of apprentice status.

Synset Examples

he had two years of half-time training

Related Terms

  • Synonym: part-time - Working fewer hours than full-time but not necessarily half
  • Antonym: full-time - Involving the whole of the usual time or hours

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'time', indicating half the usual duration.
