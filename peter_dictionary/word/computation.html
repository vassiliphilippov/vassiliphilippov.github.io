computation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or an act of calculating something, especially using mathematical or logical methods

Examples

(For each State, make all computations set forth in items 1 to 8 above, and then add the results obtained for each State in item 8.)
The Stanford Achievement Test, Form J, was administered by classroom teachers, consisting of a battery of six sub tests: Paragraph Meaning, Word Meaning, Spelling, Language, Arithmetic Computation, and Arithmetic Reasoning.
All of these sub-tests involve reading except Arithmetic Computation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using numbers to find out something; the act of calculating

Etymology

From Latin computatio (a calculation), from computare (to compute).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpjuˈteɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpjʊˈteɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The use of mathematical methods or calculation to solve problems involving numbers or quantities.

Examples

If your computation on Form 1040 or Form 1040 A shows you owe additional tax, it should be remitted with your return unless you owe less than $ 1, in which case it is forgiven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using numbers or mathematics to figure out an answer

Etymology

From Latin 'computatio' (a reckoning, calculation), from 'computare' (to calculate).
