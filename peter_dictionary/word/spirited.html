spirited

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
full of energy, enthusiasm, or determination; showing animation or liveliness.

Examples

They are definitely in the same room with you, but your head starts to swing as though you were sitting on the very edge of a tennis court watching a spirited volley.
No group of officers came in for more spirited denunciation than the doctors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: animated - full of life or excitement; lively
  • Synonym: vigorous - strong, healthy, and full of energy
  • Antonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From the noun 'spirit' + '-ed', meaning 'possessing spirit or liveliness'.
