preferment

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈfɝːmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈfɜːm(ə)nt / (BrE)
the act of preferring one person or thing over another; the action of showing a preference

Examples

Though, to be sure, we gave Kennedy no very positive approval in the margin of his preferment.

Synset Examples

the preferment went to the younger candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman French *preferment*, from Old French *prefferer* (to prefer).
