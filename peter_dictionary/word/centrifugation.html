centrifugation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəfjuˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrɪfjʊˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating substances of different densities using a centrifuge

Examples

A very fine, gray solid (about 15 g) is formed, water-washed by centrifugation, and dried at 110 ` C.
The saline and albumin tests were performed as described for the ABO samples except that the mixture was incubated for 1 hr at 37 ` C before centrifugation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrifuge - a machine that spins substances to separate components of different densities

Etymology

From French 'centrifugation', from 'centrifuger' (to spin in a centrifuge); ultimately from Latin 'centrum' (center) and 'fugare' (to drive away).
