thieving

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθiːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or practice of stealing; theft

Examples

It was responsible and sometimes dangerous work because the thieving is awful in the port of New York.
The influence of Mass was less pervasive than that of the congested, slum tenements among the bawdy houses, honkytonks, and sawdust saloons of his birthplace; he ran wild with the child gangs of the neighbourhood, and went through the normal pressure-cooker course of thieving, police dodging, and housebreaking.

Synset Examples

the thieving is awful at Kennedy International

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the act of taking another person's property without permission
  • Synonym: larceny - the crime of unlawfully taking the personal goods of another
  • Synonym: robbery - the action of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From Middle English theuinge, from the verb 'thieve.'
