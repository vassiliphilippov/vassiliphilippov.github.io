exaggerated

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzadʒəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
regarded or represented as greater, larger, or more extreme than is actually the case

Examples

He demonstrated by playing an imaginary piano, doing a staccato passage with a broadly exaggerated attack.
Children scoring high in compulsivity were those who gave evidence of tension or emotionality in situations where there was lack of organization or conformity to standards and expectations, or who made exaggerated efforts to achieve these goals.

Synset Examples

an exaggerated opinion of oneself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstated - expressed in too strong or exaggerated terms
  • Antonym: understated - described as smaller or less important than is actually the case

Etymology

From the verb 'exaggerate', from Latin 'exaggerare' (to heap up, enlarge).
