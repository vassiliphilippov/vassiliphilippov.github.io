readapt

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈdæpt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈdæpt / (BrE)
to adapt again or anew, especially after a change or disturbance

Examples

And the authors give numerous instances of calculated guessing on the patient's part to show how large a role it played in his process of readapting himself and how proficient he became at it.

Synset Examples

He readapted himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adapt - to adjust to new conditions

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 're-' (again) to 'adapt' (from Latin 'adaptare', to fit to).
