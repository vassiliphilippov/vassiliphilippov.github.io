crazy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkreɪ.zi / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.zi / (BrE)
Mentally deranged, especially as manifested in wild or aggressive behavior; affected with madness or insanity. Sometimes used informally to describe wildly foolish or irrational actions.

Examples

We are worried about what people may do with them - that some crazy fool may "push the button ".
There was something maimed and crazy about its motion that disturbed them.
But now what crazy twists and turns of his emotions!
A dozen cows mooed sadly and regarded us as if we were insane, as perhaps we were at that moment, with the crazy excitement of our first encounter, the yelling and shooting still continuing up at the road, and the thirst of some of the men, which was so great that they waded into the muddy water and scooped up handfuls of it.
The model quite plainly thought Michelangelo crazy; only the instructions from his rabbi kept him from bolting.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; deranged
  • Synonym: mad - mentally ill; crazy; wild
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps from Middle English *craze* (to shatter).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
Extremely enthusiastic or excited about something; acting with excessive excitement.

Examples

It was just me and Eileen getting drunk together like we used to in the old days, and me staring at her across the table crazy to get my hands on her partly because I wanted to wring her neck because she was so ornery but mostly because she was so wonderful to touch.

Synset Examples

the crowd went crazy
was crazy to try his new bicycle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild - showing lack of restraint or control, very excited

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *craze* (to shatter), ultimately of uncertain origin.

3. ADJ

/ ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
(of an idea, plan, or person) extremely foolish, absurd, or lacking in good sense.

Examples

Just do n't tell Ferguson that crazy opinion of yours ".
"Listen, wise guy, if you think I 'm gonna do all the fighting for this ball club you 're crazy ".
You're crazy.

Synset Examples

a crazy scheme
half-baked ideas
a screwball proposal without a prayer of working

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensible - having or showing good sense

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'craze' (to shatter, derange), of uncertain origin.

4. ADJ

/ ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
very enthusiastic about or extremely interested in someone or something; obsessed.

Examples

These Dagos are crazy when it comes to their wives.

Synset Examples

crazy about cars and racing
he is potty about her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mad - extremely enthusiastic or obsessed (British usage)
  • Synonym: obsessed - completely preoccupied with or focused on

Etymology

Probably from Middle English ‘craisy’, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old French ‘craisié’ (cracked, damaged).
