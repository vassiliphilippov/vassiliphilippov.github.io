articulate

1. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈtɪkjʊlət / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
able to express thoughts and ideas easily and clearly; expressed in a clear and effective way

Examples

A borderline schizophrenic young man told me that to him the various theoretical concepts about which he had been expounding, in a most articulate fashion, during session after session with me, were like great cubes of almost tangibly solid matter up in the air above him; as he spoke I was reminded of the great bales of cargo which are swung, high in the air, from a docked steamship.

Synset Examples

articulate speech
an articulate orator
articulate beings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
  • Antonym: inarticulate - unable to express oneself clearly

Etymology

From Latin articulatus (distinct, jointed), past participle of articulare.

2. VERB

/ ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjəleɪt / (BrE)
to pronounce or express words, syllables, or sounds clearly and distinctly; to enunciate.

Examples

Immediately thereafter, the patient fractures her rehearsed story, veering into an oversoft, breathy, sloppily articulated, "I do n't feel like talking right now ".

Synset Examples

She pronounces French words in a funny way
I cannot say `zip wire'
Can the child sound out this complicated word?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enunciate - to say or pronounce clearly; to enunciate

Etymology

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare (to divide into joints, utter distinctly).

3. VERB

/ ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to connect by a joint; to provide with a joint so that movement is possible

Examples

Where boundary maintenance describes the boundaries or limits of the group, systemic linkage is defined "as the process whereby one or more of the elements of at least two social systems is articulated in such a manner that the two systems in some ways and on some occasions may be viewed as a single unit.

Synset Examples

the carpenter jointed two pieces of wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - to join or connect (pieces) by a joint

Etymology

From Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare (to divide into joints).

4. VERB

/ ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express an idea or feeling clearly in words

Examples

Though there is obviously nothing new about these techniques, they do challenge the worker's skill to articulate them precisely on the spot and on the basis of quick and accurate diagnostic assessments.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret; not express

Etymology

From Latin articulatus (distinct, jointed), past participle of articulare (to divide into joints).
