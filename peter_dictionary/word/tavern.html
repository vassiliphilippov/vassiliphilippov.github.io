tavern

1. NOUN

/ ˈtævərn / (AmE), / ˈtævən / (BrE)
An establishment where alcoholic drinks are served to customers, often also providing food; similar to a bar or pub.

Examples

Now, you no doubt regard the preceding as pap; you 're tooling around full of gage in your hot rods, gorging yourselves on pizza and playing pinball in the taverns and generally behaving like Ubermenschen.
A tavern, filling station, junk yard, rendering plant, or some other business may go up near enough to hurt your home or to hurt its value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pub - a public house; a place where alcoholic drinks are served, especially in Britain
  • Synonym: bar - an establishment where alcoholic drinks are sold and served

Etymology

From Old French 'taverne', from Latin 'taberna' (shop, inn).
