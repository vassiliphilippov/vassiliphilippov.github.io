hypocellularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˌsɛljuˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˌsɛljʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
A condition or state in which there are abnormally few cells within a tissue or sample.

Examples

Microscopically, there were many areas of hypercellularity alternating with areas of hypocellularity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypercellularity - the state of having abnormally many cells

Etymology

From hypo- (Greek, 'under' or 'less') + cellularity (relating to cells).
