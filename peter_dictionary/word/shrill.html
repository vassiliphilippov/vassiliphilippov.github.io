shrill

1. ADJ

/ ʃrɪl / (AmE), / ʃrɪl / (BrE)
(of a sound) having a high-pitched and piercing quality; sharp and loud.

Examples

It began invariably in low tones, almost conversational, and then gradually worked up to high, shrill appeals to God and man.
The first shrill ring of the telephone brought Harold out into the hall.
He expected Concetta's thin hand to reach down to grasp the boy, and her shrill, impetuous voice to sound against the rotundity of his disfigured flesh that was never sure of hearing anything.

Synset Examples

a shrill whistle
a shrill gaiety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piercing - (of a sound) very loud and high or shrill
  • Antonym: mellow - (of a sound) pleasantly smooth or soft; not harsh or shrill

Etymology

From Middle English *shrille*, possibly of imitative origin or related to Middle Low German *schrille* (shrill).

2. VERB

/ ʃrɪl / (AmE), / ʃrɪl / (BrE)
to make or utter a high-pitched, piercing sound or cry

Examples

Other Indians were running at the ponies, shrilling and waving blankets.
The little boys shrilled out a Yiddish translation or interpretation of the Five Books of Moses, which they had previously chanted in Hebrew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - to utter a loud, sharp, high-pitched cry

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrille' (verb), from 'shrille' (adj.), likely of imitative origin.
