cowpuncher

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌpʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌpʌntʃə / (BrE)
A person, especially in the western United States, whose job is to tend cattle and perform duties on horseback; a cowboy.

Examples

Russ ran through the bills and named an amount it was highly unlikely any cowpuncher would come by honestly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a man hired to tend cattle and do ranch work on horseback

Etymology

American English; from 'cow' + 'puncher', a person who herds cattle by prodding.
