occlusion

1. NOUN

/ əˈkluʒən / (AmE), / əˈkluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the blockage or closing of a passage or vessel, especially in a medical context

Examples

A careful search failed to show occlusion of any of the mesenteric vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or closes a passage

Etymology

From Latin 'occludere' meaning to shut up, close off.
