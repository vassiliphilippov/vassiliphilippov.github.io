biophysicist

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈfɪs.ɪ.sɪst / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈfɪs.ɪ.sɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies biological processes using the principles and methods of physics

Examples

By combining the talents of a medical man, Dr. Aterman, a biophysicist, Mr. Berkely, and an electronics expert, Dr. Zworykin, this novel technique has been developed which promises to open broad avenues to understanding life processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biophysics - the science of applying the principles of physics to understand biological systems

Etymology

From bio- (relating to life) + physicist (one who studies physics).
