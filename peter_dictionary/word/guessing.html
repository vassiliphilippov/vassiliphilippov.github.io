guessing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of forming an estimate or opinion without sufficient information or certainty.

Examples

And the authors give numerous instances of calculated guessing on the patient's part to show how large a role it played in his process of readapting himself and how proficient he became at it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Synonym: estimation - a rough calculation or judgment of the value, number, quantity, or extent of something
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is known for sure
  • Antonym: deduction - the process of reaching a conclusion based on available facts

Etymology

From the verb 'guess', ultimately of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English 'gessen'.
