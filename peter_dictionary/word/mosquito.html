mosquito

1. NOUN

/ məˈskiːtoʊ / (AmE), / məˈskiːtəʊ / (BrE)
A small flying insect in which the female has a long, slender mouthpart for piercing skin and sucking the blood of humans or animals, often transmitting diseases.

Examples

He scratched his mosquito plagued neck.
Show her the new swimming pool and she would say, slapping her ankle, "I suppose this must be where you breed your gigantic mosquitoes ".

Etymology

From Spanish 'mosquito', diminutive of 'mosca' (fly), from Latin 'musca'.
