embitter

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɪtər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɪtə / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel bitter, resentful, or angry, often as a result of unfair treatment or disappointment

Examples

For the hero of this work by John Osborne and Anthony Creighton is a chap embittered by more than the lack of beer during a jam session.

Synset Examples

These injustices embittered her even more

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soften - to make (someone) gentler or less harsh in feeling
  • Antonym: console - to comfort someone and reduce bitterness or resentment

Etymology

From Old French 'embetirer', from 'es-' (intensive prefix) + 'amer' (bitter), influenced by 'bitter'.
