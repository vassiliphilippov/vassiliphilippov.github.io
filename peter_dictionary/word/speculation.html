speculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an investment involving considerable risk but offering the chance of substantial profits

Examples

"Uncle Sam "was, indeed, a rich uncle to Prokofieff, in those opulent, post-war victory years of peace and prosperity, bold speculations and extravaganzas, enjoyment and pleasure: "The Golden Twenties ".

Synset Examples

he knew the stock was a speculation when he bought it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - an action or undertaking involving risk, similar to speculation

Etymology

From Latin 'speculatio' (observation, spying), from 'speculari' (to observe), later influenced in meaning by business jargon.
