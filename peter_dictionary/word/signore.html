signore

1. NOUN

/ siˈnɔreɪ / (AmE), / siˈnɔːreɪ / (BrE)
an Italian title of respect for a man, equivalent to 'sir', used without a name

Examples

Filippo Rossi, that's what he was called in the old country; but here he was just Signore or the Old Man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sir - title used before a man's name as a form of respect or address

Etymology

From Italian 'signore' meaning 'lord' or 'gentleman'.
