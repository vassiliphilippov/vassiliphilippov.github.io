stare

1. VERB

/ stɛr / (AmE), / steə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with fixed, wide-open eyes, often with curiosity, surprise, or disbelief

Examples

Cameras stared at soloists' faces in extreme closeups, then considerately pulled back for full views of ensemble work.
There is a pause in the merriment as your friends gaze at you, wondering why you are staring, open-mouthed in amazement.
Certain this menace was only imaginary, he yet stared in fascinated horror, his hand sticky against the stock of his weapon.
Lawrence stared a minute at the lighted ribbon of traffic, hoping that a clue to his dislike of the Vice President would appear.
Wilson stared at her.

Synset Examples

The students stared at the teacher with amazement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - to look steadily and intently, especially in admiration or thought
  • Degree Higher: glare - to stare in an angry or fierce way
  • Degree Lower: glance - to look quickly or briefly

Etymology

From Middle English *steren*, from Old English *stieran* (to steer, look fixedly), influenced by Middle Low German *staren* (to stare).

2. NOUN

/ stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə / (BrE)
A long, fixed look with the eyes wide open, often showing surprise, curiosity, or intense focus.

Examples

He was able, now, to sit for hours in a chair in the living room and stare out at the bleak yard without moving.
He worked standing, with his left hand in his pocket as though he were merely stopping for a moment, sketching with the surprised stare of one who was watching another person's hand.
His eyes had the same dreadful rigid stare as Dr. Grimesby Roylott's when he was found before his open safe wearing the speckled band.
The old man gave the room a stare in leaving; under the scraggly brows the pale old eyes burned with a bitter memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - a steady and intent look
  • Synonym: glare - an angry or fierce stare
  • Antonym: glance - a quick look

Etymology

From Middle English *staren*, from Old English *staran* (to stare).

3. VERB

/ stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with eyes wide open and fixed attention, often for a long time or with intensity

Examples

He sat beside Kate's bed with the others throughout the morning, talking, talking of God's will, while Kate lay staring angrily at him.

Synset Examples

The ancestor in the painting is staring down menacingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - look steadily and intently, especially in admiration or thought
  • Degree Higher: glare - look at someone or something in an angry or fierce way
  • Degree Lower: glance - take a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Old English 'starian', related to Dutch 'staren' and German 'starren', meaning 'to gaze fixedly'.
