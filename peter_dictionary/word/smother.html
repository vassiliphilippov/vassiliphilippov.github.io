smother

1. VERB

/ ˈsmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðə / (BrE)
to suppress or conceal (a feeling, impulse, expression, etc.), to prevent from being seen or expressed

Examples

Tooling through Sydney on his way to race in the New Zealand Grand Prix, Britain's balding Ace Driver Stirling Moss, 31, all but smothered himself in his own exhaust of self-crimination.

Synset Examples

smother a yawn
muffle one's anger
strangle a yawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to forcibly; to restrain or prevent the expression of something
  • Synonym: stifle - to restrain or stop oneself from acting on an emotion or impulse
  • Antonym: reveal - to make known; to show or disclose openly

Etymology

From Middle English 'smotheren', related to 'smother' (noun), probably from Old English 'smorian' (to suffocate).

2. VERB

/ ˈsmʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌðə / (BrE)
To cover or envelop completely, often preventing exposure or access.

Examples

A truth-revealing crisis erupted in Katanga for a couple of days this month, to be quickly smothered by the high pressure verbal fog that is kept on tap for such emergencies.

Synset Examples

smother the meat in gravy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or on top of; to envelope

Etymology

From Middle English smotheren, related to 'smother' meaning 'thick smoke' or 'suffocating matter', likely from Old English 'smorian' (to suffocate).
