emergence

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒəns / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
The process of becoming visible or coming into view.

Examples

In the Sacramento valley in California, for instance, it has been observed that there was not one day's difference between the emergence of the andrenas and the opening of the willow catkins.

Synset Examples

not a day's difference between the emergence of the andrenas and the opening of the willow catkins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appearance - the act of becoming visible
  • Antonym: disappearance - the act of ceasing to be visible

Etymology

From Latin emergere (to rise up, come forth).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒəns / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the process of coming into existence or prominence; the act of gradually becoming visible, important, or recognized

Examples

Figurines and simple chapels presage the emergence of sculpture and architecture in Greece; objects in gold, ivory, and bronze grow more numerous.

Synset Examples

figurines presage the emergence of sculpture in Greece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appearance - the act of coming into view or becoming visible
  • Antonym: disappearance - the act of ceasing to be visible or present

Etymology

From Medieval Latin emergentia (an arising), from Latin emergere (to rise out)
