foolproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈfuːlˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈfuːlˌpruːf / (BrE)
(of a plan, device, or method) designed to be easy to use or understand and not likely to fail, even when used by someone with no experience or knowledge.

Examples

He put the basket down distastefully, muttering softly and thoroughly disgusted with himself and his plan that had seemed so foolproof.

Synset Examples

a foolproof identification system
the unfailing sign of an amateur
an unfailing test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfailing - always effective; never failing

Etymology

Formed from 'fool' + 'proof', originally meaning 'proof against fools' (i.e., even a fool could not fail using it).
