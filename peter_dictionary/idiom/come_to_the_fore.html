come to the fore

1. VERB

/ kʌm tə ðə fɔɹ / (AmE), / kʌm tə ðə fɔː / (BrE)
to become prominent or noticeable; to take a leading or active role

Examples

To perpetuate wealth control led by small groups of individuals who played no role in its creation prevents those with real initiative from coming to the fore, and is basically anti democratic.

Synset Examples

Young people should step to the fore and help their peers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step forward - to move or come forward to offer help or assume responsibility
  • Antonym: take a back seat - not to play a prominent or leading role
