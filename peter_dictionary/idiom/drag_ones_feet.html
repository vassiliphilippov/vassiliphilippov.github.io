drag one's feet

1. VERB

/ dræɡ wʌnz fit / (AmE), / dræɡ wʌnz fiːt / (BrE)
to be deliberately slow or reluctant to act or make progress, especially to delay something that needs to be done

Examples

Bill Mazeroski with .179 and Hal Smith with .143 were the only Pirates dragging their feet.
But the Latin American republics who have been rather inclined to drag their feet on taking action against Castro also reacted swiftly last week by finally throwing Cuba off the Inter-American Defense Board.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastinate - to delay or postpone action; put off doing something
  • Antonym: hasten - to do something quickly or speed up; to move or act quickly
