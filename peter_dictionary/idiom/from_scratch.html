from scratch

1. ADV

/ frəm skrætʃ / (AmE), / frəm skrætʃ / (BrE)
Starting from the very beginning, without using anything that already exists or any prepared materials.

Examples

And without high density in the core, rapid-transit systems cannot be maintained economically, let alone built from scratch at today's prices.

Synset Examples

he baked the torte from scratch
she built her business up from scratch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: from the ground up - starting completely from the beginning
  • Synonym: from nothing - from a state of having no resources or advantage

Etymology

Possibly from the sporting sense of 'scratch' (mark indicating the starting line in a race or contest), dating from the 18th century.
