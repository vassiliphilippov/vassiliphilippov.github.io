on the face of it

1. ADV

/ ɒn ðə feɪs əv ɪt / (AmE), / ɒn ðə feɪs əv ɪt / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something appears to be true based on the available evidence, although it may not be on closer examination.

Examples

On the face of it, it is because he employs deductive techniques alien to official police routine.

Synset Examples

irrigation often produces bumper crops from apparently desert land
the child is seemingly healthy but the doctor is concerned
had been ostensibly frank as to his purpose while really concealing it
on the face of it the problem seems minor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparently - as far as one knows or can see; seemingly
  • Synonym: ostensibly - as appears or is stated to be true, but not necessarily so
  • Synonym: seemingly - so as to appear to be; apparently
