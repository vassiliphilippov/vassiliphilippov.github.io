silver spoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˌspun / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌspuːn / (BrE)
Used to refer to inherited wealth or privilege, especially from birth; often in the phrase 'born with a silver spoon in one's mouth.'

Examples

That tall drink of water with the silver spoon up his ass.

Synset Examples

he was born with a silver spoon in his mouth
she is the daughter of old money from Massachusetts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old money - families with inherited wealth over several generations
  • Antonym: nouveau riche - people who have recently acquired wealth, contrasted with those born into it

Etymology

From the literal meaning of an expensive silver utensil symbolizing wealth; the idiomatic phrase dates back to the 18th century.
