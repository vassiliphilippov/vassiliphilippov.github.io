free rein

1. NOUN

/ friː reɪn / (AmE), / friː reɪn / (BrE)
Freedom to act or express oneself without restraint or limitation.

Examples

Some renditions are of symphonic dimensions, with the contrabass given free rein.

Synset Examples

he gave free rein to his impulses
they gave full play to the artist's talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full play - freedom for something to develop fully
  • Antonym: restraint - a measure or condition that keeps someone or something under control
  • Synonym: license - freedom to act however one wishes, especially without restraint

Etymology

From horse riding, where 'rein' refers to the straps used to control a horse; giving a horse 'free rein' allows it to move as it wishes.
