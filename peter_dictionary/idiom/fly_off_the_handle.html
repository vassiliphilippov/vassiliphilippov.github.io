fly off the handle

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ɑf ðə ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / flaɪ ɒf ðə ˈhændl / (BrE)
To suddenly become very angry or lose one's temper.

Examples

He flies off the handle sometimes, but we're good friends.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose one's temper - to suddenly become angry
  • Antonym: keep one's cool - to remain calm and not get angry

Etymology

Originated in mid-19th century America, likely referring to an axe head unexpectedly detaching from its handle, metaphorically representing an unexpected loss of control.
