out of the question

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ðə ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ˈkwes.tʃən / (BrE)
not possible or not worth considering; impossible

Examples

A nation such as Switzerland could be neutralized by agreement and could be relied upon to protect its neutrality; more doubtful, but possible, (with an assist from the North) was the neutralization of the Latin American countries; out of the question was the neutralization of Asia and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impossible - not able to occur or be done
  • Antonym: possible - able to be done or achieved

2. ADJ

/ aʊt əv ðə ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
not possible or not allowed to be considered; impossible

Examples

Now why don'’t you tell him that marriage is out of the question, and you don'’t want to see him any more.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impossible - not able to occur or be done
