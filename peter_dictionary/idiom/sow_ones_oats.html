sow one's oats

1. VERB

/ soʊ wʌnz oʊts / (AmE), / səʊ wʌnz əʊts / (BrE)
(of a young man) to behave promiscuously or indulge in youthful pleasures, especially before settling down.

Examples

Stengel probably preached too much in the early days when the kid wanted to pop his bubble gum and sow his oats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow one's wild oats - to indulge in youthful pleasures or behave promiscuously before settling down

Etymology

From the agricultural practice of sowing oats, idiomatically meaning to spread one's seed before marriage.
