curry favour

1. VERB

/ ˈkɜːri ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌri ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to seek to gain someone's approval or advantage by flattery or obsequious behaviour

Examples

You could argue he'd done it to curry favor with the guards.

Synset Examples

This employee is currying favor with his superordinates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatter - to praise insincerely to gain favor
  • Synonym: butter up - to flatter someone for personal gain

Etymology

From Middle English 'curry favell', referring to currying (grooming) a horse named Fauvel, symbolizing deceit; 'favell' later altered to 'favour'.
