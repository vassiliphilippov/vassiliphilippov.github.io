go a long way

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ə lɔŋ weɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ə lɒŋ weɪ / (BrE)
To be very helpful or effective; to suffice or have a significant impact, especially in achieving a particular goal or result.

Examples

These scientists are convinced that a telescope unclouded by the earth's gases will go a long way toward bolstering or destroying cosmic theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to make it easier for someone to do something or for something to happen
  • Synonym: contribute - to help to cause an event or situation
