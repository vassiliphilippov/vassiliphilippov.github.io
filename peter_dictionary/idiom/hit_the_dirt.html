hit the dirt

1. VERB

/ hɪt ðə dɝt / (AmE), / hɪt ðə dɜːt / (BrE)
To quickly fall to the ground, usually in order to avoid danger or harm.

Examples

He went down like a wet rag and the attackers hit the dirt in the face of the withering enemy fire.

Synset Examples

The soldiers hit the dirt when they heard gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - to jump or move quickly downward
