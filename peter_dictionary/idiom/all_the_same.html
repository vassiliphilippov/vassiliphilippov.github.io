all the same

1. ADV

/ ɔl ðə seɪm / (AmE), / ɔːl ðə seɪm / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something remains true despite what has just been said; nevertheless; in spite of everything

Examples

Mr. Skyros was not a man who thought very much about moral principles; he found money much more interesting; but all the same he thought now, uneasily, of the way in which Angelo earned his living - and paid for his own stuff - and eyed the soft smile, and the spaniel like dark eyes, and he felt a little ill.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; notwithstanding
  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
