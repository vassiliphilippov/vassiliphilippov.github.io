take the Fifth

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə fɪfθ / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fɪfθ / (BrE)
to refuse to answer questions in a court of law or other setting by invoking the right under the Fifth Amendment not to incriminate oneself

Examples

A ten-to-one shot he would take the Fifth, and I'’d lose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plead the Fifth - invoke the Fifth Amendment right not to self-incriminate

Etymology

From the Fifth Amendment to the United States Constitution, which protects against self-incrimination.
