to boot

1. ADV

/ tə buːt / (AmE), / tə buːt / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is added to what has already been mentioned; moreover; in addition

Examples

I would have been negligent and a goddam lousy cop to boot, if I 'd sat around this station all night when somebody got away with murder in my district.

Synset Examples

he serves additionally as the CEO

Related Terms

  • Synonym: besides - in addition; as well
  • Synonym: furthermore - in addition; moreover

Etymology

From Old English 'bōt' (advantage, remedy, addition).
