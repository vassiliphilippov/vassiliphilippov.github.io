by all means

1. ADV

/ baɪ ɔl minz / (AmE), / baɪ ɔːl miːnz / (BrE)
used to emphasize that someone is welcome to do something, or to give permission; certainly; definitely.

Examples

Mythological concepts may by all means still be used, but they can be used responsibly only as "symbols "or "ciphers ", that is, only if they are also constantly interpreted in non mythological (or existential) terms.

Synset Examples

Visit us by all means

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - used to emphasize agreement or permission
  • Antonym: by no means - not at all; in no way
