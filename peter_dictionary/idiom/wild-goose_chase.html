wild-goose chase

1. NOUN

/ waɪld ɡus tʃeɪs / (AmE), / waɪld ɡuːs tʃeɪs / (BrE)
A search or pursuit that is hopeless or pointless because the sought-after thing cannot be found or does not exist.

Examples

Took him on a wild goose chase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool's errand - a task or journey that is foolish and pointless

Etymology

From the hunting of wild geese, which is extremely difficult and often fruitless. The phrase dates back to the late 16th century, first appearing in English literature.
