break one's back

1. VERB

/ breɪk wʌnz bæk / (AmE), / breɪk wʌnz bæk / (BrE)
to work extremely hard at a task, often physically or with great effort

Examples

In the unfertile land in Palestine humans broke their backs pushing will power to the brink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work one's fingers to the bone - to work extremely hard
  • Antonym: take it easy - to relax; not work hard
