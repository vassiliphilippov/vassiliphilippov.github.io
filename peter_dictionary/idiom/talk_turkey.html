talk turkey

1. VERB

/ tɔk ˈtɝki / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
to speak frankly and directly, especially about business or serious matters

Examples

Let's talk business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak plainly - to express oneself in a clear and straightforward way
  • Antonym: beat around the bush - to avoid getting to the point; to speak indirectly

Etymology

Phrase originated in early 19th-century America, possibly from negotiations between settlers and Native Americans involving turkey and corn.
