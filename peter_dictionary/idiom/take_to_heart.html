take to heart

1. VERB

/ teɪk tə hɑrt / (AmE), / teɪk tə hɑːt / (BrE)
To consider something very seriously and be deeply affected or influenced by it, especially advice or criticism.

Examples

John Adams took to heart the advice given him by his legal mentor, Jeremiah Gridley, to "pursue the study of the law, rather than the gain of it ".

Synset Examples

Attend to your duties, please

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something; to disregard
