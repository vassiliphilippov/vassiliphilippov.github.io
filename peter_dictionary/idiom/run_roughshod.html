run roughshod

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈrʌfˌʃɑd / (AmE), / rʌn ˈrʌfˌʃɒd / (BrE)
to act without regard for the feelings or rights of others; to treat people harshly or inconsiderately

Examples

"All too frequently ", points out James O' Gara, managing editor of Commonweal, "Catholics run roughshod over Protestant sensibilities in this matter, by failure to consider the reasoning behind the Protestant position and, particularly, by their jibes at the fact that Protestant opinion on birth control has changed in recent decades ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldoze - to force one's way aggressively or ruthlessly; act with disregard for others
  • Synonym: steamroll - to proceed ruthlessly or forcibly, crushing opposition

Etymology

From the literal term 'roughshod', meaning 'having shoes with projecting nails or points', originally used to describe horses, hence figuratively 'to trample over'.
