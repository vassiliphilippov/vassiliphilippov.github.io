and then some

1. ADV

/ ænd ðɛn sʌm / (AmE), / ənd ðɛn sʌm / (BrE)
used to emphasize that an amount or degree is even greater than what has just been mentioned; and much more in addition.

Examples

Whatever mistakes I've made, I've paid for them and then some.

Synset Examples

it cost me a week's salary and then some

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: more - a greater or additional amount or degree
