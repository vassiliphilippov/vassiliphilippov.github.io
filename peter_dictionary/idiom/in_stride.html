in stride

1. ADV

/ ɪn straɪd / (AmE), / ɪn straɪd / (BrE)
to deal with something calmly and without being disturbed or upset

Examples

The plants took zero nights in their stride, with nothing but a mat of straw over the glass to protect them.
Not only was Haumd's intonation and phrasing without flaw, but he seemed to take every tonal eccentricity in stride.

Synset Examples

she took all his criticism in stride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take in stride - to accept or deal with something without difficulty
