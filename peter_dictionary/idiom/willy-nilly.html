willy-nilly

1. ADV

/ ˌwɪliˈnɪli / (AmE), / ˌwɪliˈnɪli / (BrE)
In a random or haphazard manner; without order or planning.

Examples

On the other we have the flag wavers and the national sentimentalists who have been willing to use any patriotic, "frontier western "or colonial material willy-nilly.
Are you following competition willy-nilly - trying to match dollar for dollar - or are you experimenting with new means for reaching and influencing consumers?

Synset Examples

the houses were randomly scattered
bullets were fired into the crowd at random

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - without method or conscious decision
  • Antonym: orderly - in an organized or methodical way

Etymology

Probably from the phrase 'will he, nill he' meaning 'whether he wants to or not' (from Old English *nyllan*, not to want).

2. ADV

/ ˌwɪliˈnɪli / (AmE), / ˌwɪliˈnɪli / (BrE)
Without having a choice or whether one likes it or not.

Examples

Who is involved willy nilly?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsorily - by force or requirement, not by choice
  • Antonym: voluntarily - done by one's own choice, willingly

Etymology

Possibly a contraction of 'will ye, nill ye' (whether you want to or not), from Old English 'willan' (to will) and 'nyllan' (to be unwilling).
