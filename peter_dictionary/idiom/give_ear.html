give ear

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv ɪr / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɪə / (BrE)
to listen attentively to; to pay heed to someone or something

Examples

His power was so great that he even promoted and demoted gods according to whether they had given ear or been deaf to petitions.

Synset Examples

The children in the audience attended the recital quietly
She hung on his every word
They attended to everything he said

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listen - to give attention with the ear; to hear with thoughtful attention
  • Antonym: ignore - to refuse to take notice of; disregard

Etymology

From the literal act of offering one's ear to hear; used since at least the 16th century in English.
