pull strings

1. VERB

/ pʊl ˈstrɪŋz / (AmE), / pʊl ˈstrɪŋz / (BrE)
To use one's personal influence or connections, often in a secretive or unofficial way, to achieve a desired outcome.

Examples

Pop had to pull a lot of **strings** to get you a deferment.

Synset Examples

He manipulated public opinion in his favor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull wires - use personal connections to achieve something (especially in US English)

Etymology

Refers to puppeteers controlling puppets by pulling strings, metaphorically using one's power to manipulate situations or people.
