cheek by jowl

1. ADV

/ tʃik baɪ dʒaʊl / (AmE), / tʃiːk baɪ dʒəʊl / (BrE)
(of people or things) very close together; in close proximity.

Examples

Dives, carefully repaired huts, and nicely painted and ornamented cottages were jumbled together cheek by jowl with little distinction.

Synset Examples

the houses were jumbled together cheek by jowl

Etymology

From the literal reference to cheeks and jaws being next to each other; recorded since the 16th century.
