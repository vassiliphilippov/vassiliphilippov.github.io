matter of course

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætɚ əv kɔrs / (AmE), / ˈmætə əv kɔːs / (BrE)
something that is expected to happen or be done as a normal part of a process, without special attention or need for justification.

Examples

Extensions are not granted as a matter of course, and the reasons for your request must be substantial.
