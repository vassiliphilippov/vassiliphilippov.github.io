cut corners

1. VERB

/ kʌt ˈkɔrnɚz / (AmE), / kʌt ˈkɔːnəz / (BrE)
to do something in the easiest, quickest, or cheapest way, often by ignoring rules or leaving out important steps, usually resulting in lower quality or unsafe results

Examples

Not a corner has been visibly cut in this one.

Synset Examples

Cut corners to make a cheaper product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrimp - to use as little money or materials as possible
  • Synonym: skimp - to use too little of something; to deal with something carelessly or superficially
