clean slate

1. NOUN

/ klin sleɪt / (AmE), / kliːn sleɪt / (BrE)
an opportunity to start again without being affected by past mistakes or difficulties

Examples

But we cannot start off with a clean slate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fresh start - an act of beginning something again or differently
  • Synonym: new beginning - a completely new chance to begin something

Etymology

Originates from the practice of writing with chalk on slates, which could be wiped clean to begin anew.
