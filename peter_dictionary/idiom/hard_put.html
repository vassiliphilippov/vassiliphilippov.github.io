hard put

1. ADJ

/ hɑrd pʊt / (AmE), / hɑːd pʊt / (BrE)
Experiencing difficulty or trouble in doing something; having a hard time managing or accomplishing a task.

Examples

Department stores were built on dry goods, especially ladies' fashions, and in this area, in the long run, the suburban branches will be hard put to compete against downtown.

Synset Examples

distressed companies need loans and technical advice
financially hard-pressed Mexican hotels are lowering their prices
we were hard put to meet the mortgage payment
found themselves in a bad way financially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard-pressed - under strain or pressure; having difficulty managing due to lack of resources
  • Synonym: at a loss - unable to know what to do or say; confused or uncertain
