throw in the towel

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊəl / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn ðə ˈtaʊəl / (BrE)
To admit defeat or give up, especially after a struggle or when success seems impossible.

Examples

But so convinced of communism's inevitable triumph is Prince Sihanouk that he is ready to throw in the towel.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give up - to stop trying; to cease making an effort
  • Synonym: quit - to stop or discontinue doing something
  • Antonym: persevere - to persist in an activity or belief
  • Antonym: keep going - continue an activity or effort despite difficulties

Etymology

Originates from boxing, where a trainer would throw a towel into the ring to signal surrender.
