know the score

1. VERB

/ noʊ ðə skɔr / (AmE), / nəʊ ðə skɔː / (BrE)
To be fully aware of the real situation or of all the important facts.

Examples

Knows the score.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in the know - to be well-informed; to have special knowledge

Etymology

Originally from sports terminology, especially gambling, referring to knowing the actual score of a game and thus the real state of affairs.
