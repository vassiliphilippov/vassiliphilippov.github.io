out of the blue

1. ADV

/ aʊt əv ðə blu / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə bluː / (BrE)
Happening unexpectedly and without warning.

Examples

He had gone into the Japanese navy, had been trained as an officer, had participated in one or two battles - he never went into detail regarding his military experience - and at the age of twenty-five, quite as a bolt out of the blue, he had walked into the mission as if he belonged here and had become a Christian.

Synset Examples

unanticipated and disconcerting lines of development
unforeseen circumstances
a virtue unlooked-for in people so full of energy
like a bolt out of the blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - In a way that is not expected; without warning
  • Synonym: suddenly - Quickly and without warning

Etymology

Originates from the phrase 'a bolt out of the blue,' referring to a sudden lightning bolt from a clear, blue sky (19th century).
