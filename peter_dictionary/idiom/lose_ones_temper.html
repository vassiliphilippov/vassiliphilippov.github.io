lose one's temper

1. VERB

/ luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry and unable to control oneself

Examples

Don't lose that famous **temper** of yours, Sonny.

Synset Examples

The professor combusted when the student didn't know the answer to a very elementary question
Spam makes me go ballistic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: keep one's temper - to remain calm and not get angry
  • Synonym: blow one's top - to suddenly become extremely angry
  • Synonym: lose it - to lose control of one's emotions, especially anger
