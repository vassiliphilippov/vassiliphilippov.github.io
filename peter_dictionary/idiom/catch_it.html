catch it

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɪt / (AmE), / kætʃ ɪt / (BrE)
To be punished, scolded, or reprimanded, especially after having done something wrong.

Examples

You'll take a vacation, nobody knows where, and we'll catch the **hell**.

Synset Examples

I really caught it the other day!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get it - to be scolded or punished
  • Antonym: be let off - to avoid punishment or reprimand
