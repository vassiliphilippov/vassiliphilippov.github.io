dead letter

1. NOUN

/ dɛd ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / dɛd ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
A law, rule, or agreement that is no longer in effect or enforced, though it has not been officially repealed.

Examples

Mr. Justice Taney's Dred Scott decision in 1857 was unpopular in the North, and soon became a dead letter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolete - no longer used or produced; out of date
  • Degree Higher: law - system of rules that a particular country or community recognizes
