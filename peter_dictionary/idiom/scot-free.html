scot-free

1. ADV

/ skɑt fri / (AmE), / skɒt friː / (BrE)
Without suffering any punishment, harm, or penalty; completely unpunished.

Examples

He, McBride, would be cited as in the wrong, and he, Lord, would go scot-free, an officer who had only done his duty, though perhaps too energetically.

Synset Examples

he went scot-free

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untouched - not affected or harmed
  • Antonym: punished - subjected to penalty for an offense

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'scot' (a tax or payment), meaning 'free from payment or penalty'.
