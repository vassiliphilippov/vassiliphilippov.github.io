in sight

1. ADJ

/ ɪn saɪt / (AmE), / ɪn saɪt / (BrE)
within a distance where something can be seen; visible or able to be seen

Examples

Even industrial development leaders find it hard to win local support for training unless a new industry is in sight and requests it.
If Ferguson's car had been in sight, Holden would have walked directly to it.
Stevens was nowhere in sight.

Synset Examples

not a policeman in sight
kept the monkey in view

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of sight - not able to be seen; hidden or beyond visible range
