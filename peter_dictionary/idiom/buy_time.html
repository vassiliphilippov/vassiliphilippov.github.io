buy time

1. VERB

/ baɪ taɪm / (AmE), / baɪ taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately delay something in order to gain an advantage or prepare for something

Examples

He wanted to buy time to strengthen the U. S. and its allies and to define and begin to implement his foreign policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to delay taking action or making a decision
  • Synonym: delay - to postpone or put off an action
