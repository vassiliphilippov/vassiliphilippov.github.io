by a long shot

1. ADV

/ baɪ ə lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / baɪ ə lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
By a large amount; by far (used to emphasize a difference or degree). Often used in negative constructions to mean 'not at all' or 'by no means.'

Examples

Subways improved land values without taking away land; freeways boost valuation less (because the garages they require are not prime buildings by a long shot), and reduce the acreage that can be taxed.

Synset Examples

he is the best by a long shot
his labors haven't ended there--not by a long shot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by far - to a great degree; by a large margin

Etymology

Originally from shooting competitions, where a long shot is more difficult and less likely to succeed, emphasizing the magnitude of the difference.
