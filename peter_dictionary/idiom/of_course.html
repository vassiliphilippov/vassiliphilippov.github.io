of course

1. ADV

/ əv kɔrs / (AmE), / əv kɔːs / (BrE)
used to say that something is expected, obvious, or naturally the case

Examples

Both, of course, were remarkable feats and further embossed the fact that baseball rightfully is the national pastime.
The idol of the hackers, of course, is Ray Ainsley, who achieved a 19 in the United States Open.
And now, of course, the hue and cry for counter escalation is being raised on our side.
Morrison points out that since our country is more urbanized than the Soviet Union or Red China, it is the most vulnerable of the great powers - Europe of course must be written off out of hand.
There is Karl Marx, of course, buried in London.

Synset Examples

naturally, the lawyer sent us a huge bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naturally - used to say something is obvious or expected
  • Synonym: certainly - used to express agreement or certainty

2. ADV

/ əv ˈkɔrs / (AmE), / əv ˈkɔːs / (BrE)
Used to show that something is expected, obvious, or naturally the case.

Examples

Of course Signora Colombo can stay!

Synset Examples

naturally, the lawyer sent us a huge bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naturally - as might be expected; in a natural manner or way
  • Synonym: certainly - used to express complete agreement or affirmation
  • Antonym: of course not - used to strongly deny or reject a suggestion
