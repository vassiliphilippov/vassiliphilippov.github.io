turn the tables

1. VERB

/ tɝn ðə ˈteɪbəlz / (AmE), / tɜːn ðə ˈteɪbəlz / (BrE)
to reverse the situation so that you gain the advantage over someone who previously had it over you

Examples

Suppose, he says, that the tables were turned, and we were in the Soviets' position: "There would be more than 2000 modern Soviet fighters, all better than ours, stationed at 250 bases in Mexico and the Caribbean.

Synset Examples

The tables are turned now that the Republicans are in power!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse the situation - to change the circumstances so that the opposite occurs

Etymology

Originates from the 17th-century custom of turning positions at a gaming table to reverse roles or fortunes.
