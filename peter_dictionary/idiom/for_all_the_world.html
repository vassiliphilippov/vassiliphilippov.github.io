for all the world

1. ADV

/ fɔr ɔl ðə wɝld / (AmE), / fə ɔːl ðə wɜːld / (BrE)
(used with negative or hypothetical statements) Under any circumstances; by any means.

Examples

A somewhat less fragmented hebephrenic patient of mine, who used to often seclude herself in her room, often sounded through the closed door - as I would find on passing by, between our sessions - for all the world like two persons, a scolding mother and a defensive child.

Synset Examples

she wouldn't give up her pets for love or money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: for love or money - by any means whatsoever; under any circumstances
