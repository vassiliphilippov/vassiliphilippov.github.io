sweep under the rug

1. VERB

/ swip ˈʌndɚ ðə rʌɡ / (AmE), / swiːp ˈʌndə ðə rʌɡ / (BrE)
To try to hide or conceal something embarrassing, unpleasant, or damaging in the hope that it will not be noticed or discovered.

Examples

The whole thing, his manner conveyed, was so far outside the normal routine of Hohlbein and Garth that it practically demanded being swept under the rug.

Synset Examples

The president tried to sweep the embarrassing incident under the rug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover up - to hide the truth about something
  • Antonym: bring to light - to reveal or disclose something hidden
