take time by the forelock

1. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm baɪ ðə ˈfɔrlɑk / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm baɪ ðə ˈfɔːlɒk / (BrE)
To seize an opportunity at once, acting quickly and decisively without hesitation.

Examples

I would like to take this opportunity to thank United Telephone and Telegraph for their lovely Christmas gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seize the day - to make the most of the present moment

Etymology

Refers to the ancient allegorical figure of 'Occasion' or 'Opportunity,' depicted with a lock of hair on the forehead to be grasped quickly as she passes.
