go far

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fɑɹ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fɑː / (BrE)
to achieve great success or progress, especially in a career or particular field

Examples

These proposals would go far toward creating the economic atmosphere favoring growth of the individual, who, in turn, would help us to cope with runaway technology.
A smart, shrewd and ambitious young man, well connected, and with a knack for getting in the good graces of important people, he was bound to go far.

Synset Examples

After he published his book, he had arrived
I don't know whether I can make it in science!
You will go far, my boy!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result; to be successful
  • Synonym: make it - to achieve success; to reach a desired position
  • Antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal
