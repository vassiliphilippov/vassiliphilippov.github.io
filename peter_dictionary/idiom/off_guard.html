off guard

1. ADJ

/ ɑf ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒf ɡɑːd / (BrE)
Not prepared or not paying attention; unready to respond to a sudden event or surprise.

Examples

She had caught him off guard, no preparation, nothing certain but that ahead lay some kind of disaster.
And their arrival caught John Clayton and Charles Ansley off guard.

Synset Examples

the blow caught him napping
caught in an off-guard moment
found him off his guard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprepared - not ready for something; not prepared
  • Antonym: alert - watchful and ready to respond
