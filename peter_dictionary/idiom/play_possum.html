play possum

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ˈpɑsəm / (AmE), / pleɪ ˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
To pretend to be dead or to feign ignorance, especially in order to avoid attention or danger.

Examples

I knew the only way I could beat you was to play possum, but it was a good try, kid, and I appreciate it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not

Etymology

From the behavior of the opossum, which is known to feign death as a defense mechanism.
