off the hook

1. ADJ

/ ɑf ðə hʊk / (AmE), / ɒf ðə hʊk / (BrE)
Having been freed from blame, responsibility, or an unpleasant situation.

Examples

Tom, can you get me off the hook?

Synset Examples

I let him off the hook with a mild reprimand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the clear - no longer at risk of blame or harm
  • Antonym: on the hook - responsible for something or under obligation
