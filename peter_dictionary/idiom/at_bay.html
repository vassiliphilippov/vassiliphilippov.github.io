at bay

1. ADV

/ æt ˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ət ˈbeɪ / (BrE)
Unable to escape and forced to face attackers or difficulties; kept from advancing or moving forward.

Examples

It has held them at bay.
Really he had Mr. Skyros at bay.

Synset Examples

a stag at bay
she had me cornered between the porch and her car
like a trapped animal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornered - forced into a position where escape is impossible

Etymology

From the hunting term 'bay', referring to an animal that is cornered by hounds and forced to turn and face them. 'Bay' in this sense comes from Old French 'abai', meaning 'bark'.
