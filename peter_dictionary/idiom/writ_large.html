writ large

1. ADV

/ ɹɪt lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɹɪt lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
used to indicate that something is shown or expressed in a clearer, more obvious, or exaggerated way

Examples

In this essay, we are, along with most historians, interested in the more general or more inclusive ideas, that are so to speak "writ large "in history of literature where they recur continually.

Synset Examples

the effect of...his irregular life could be seen writ large on his gaunt features

Etymology

From 'writ' (archaic past participle of 'write') plus 'large', originally meaning 'written in large letters to be easily seen.'
