to the hilt

1. ADV

/ tə ði hɪlt / (AmE), / tə ði hɪlt / (BrE)
To the maximum extent possible; completely or thoroughly.

Examples

Against all expectation, Carmer was inside, clearly enjoying himself to the hilt and already so tipsy that it seemed unlikely he was bothering to note anything or anyone about him.

Synset Examples

you are in this to the hilt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - entirely; totally
  • Synonym: thoroughly - in a thorough manner; fully

Etymology

Originally from the imagery of plunging a sword or dagger all the way up to its hilt (handle), indicating the fullest possible extent.
