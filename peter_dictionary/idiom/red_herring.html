red herring

1. NOUN

/ rɛd ˈhɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / rɛd ˈhɛrɪŋ / (BrE)
An idea, fact, or argument introduced into a discussion as a distraction from the main issue or subject.

Examples

And it makes a very poor red herring for an inside job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distraction - something that diverts attention

Etymology

Originally referred to strong-smelling smoked fish used to mislead hunting dogs during training or to divert them from the correct scent trail.
