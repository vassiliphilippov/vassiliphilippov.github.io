outside door

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtsaɪd dɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtsaɪd dɔː / (BrE)
A door that provides entry to or exit from the outside of a building.

Examples

The 7: 45 bell rang and he could hear the outside doors bang shut, closing in the assembled day watch.
Pausing in the outside door to glance behind him, Pat looked his unspoken warning and stepped out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior door - a door located inside a building, not leading directly outside
