endothermic reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈθɜrmɪk riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈθɜːmɪk riˈækʃən / (BrE)
A chemical reaction that absorbs heat from its surroundings.

Examples

Differential thermal analysis showed a very small endothermic reaction at 340 ` C and a large endothermic reaction at 470 ` C.
Differential thermal analysis showed a very small endothermic reaction at 340 ` C and a large endothermic reaction at 470 ` C.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exothermic reaction - a chemical reaction that releases heat

Etymology

From Greek 'endo-' (within) and 'thermos' (heat), plus 'reaction'.
