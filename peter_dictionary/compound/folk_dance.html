folk dance

1. NOUN

/ foʊk dæns / (AmE), / fəʊk dɑːns / (BrE)
A traditional style of dancing that originates among the common people of a region or country, usually passed down through generations.

Examples

LaGuardia's multi lingual rallies, when he is running for Congress, are well staged, and wind up in a wild Jewish folk-dance that is really great musical theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk music - traditional music of the common people

Etymology

From 'folk', meaning common people, and 'dance'; widely attested in English since the 19th century.
