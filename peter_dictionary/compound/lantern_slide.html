lantern slide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæntɚn slaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæntən slaɪd / (BrE)
an image transparency mounted in a frame, designed to be projected onto a surface using a magic lantern or slide projector

Examples

Even the "history of furniture "can hardly be taught exclusively from photographs and lantern slides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - a transparent image for projection with a projector

Etymology

From 'lantern' (originally referring to a 'magic lantern,' an early type of image projector) and 'slide' (from sliding the image into the projector).
