topgallant mast

1. NOUN

/ tɑpˌɡæl.ənt mæst / (AmE), / tɒpˌɡæl.ənt mɑːst / (BrE)
the mast attached above the topmast on a square-rigged sailing vessel, supporting the topgallant sails

Examples

A glance revealed that the main topgallant mast had been carried away.
And it was clear that Adrien was not mistaken, for both Small and Cromwell took no step toward aiding in the sending up of the new topgallant mast till Philip Spencer had given the signal to obey.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mainmast - the principal mast of a ship
  • Degree Lower: topmast - the mast above the lower mast, supporting the topgallant mast

Etymology

From 'topgallant', originally referring to the uppermost sails ('gallant' indicating something splendid or lofty), and 'mast'.
