liberal arts

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚəl ɑrts / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəl ɑːts / (BrE)
Academic subjects such as literature, philosophy, mathematics, and social and natural sciences, which are intended to give general knowledge and to develop intellectual skills rather than occupational or professional skills.

Examples

It has been the custom for most universities to stretch the blanket of accreditation for their liberal arts school to cover the shivering body of their fine arts department.

Synset Examples

the college of arts and sciences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanities - academic disciplines studying aspects of human culture

Etymology

From Latin 'liberales artes', meaning 'the skills befitting a free person'.
