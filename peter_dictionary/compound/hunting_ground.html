hunting ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A place or environment where many opportunities for a particular activity are available.

Examples

in his motherland; in the spacious hunting grounds of "Uncle Sam "; in the exciting salons of his lovely, brilliant Paris - mistress of gaiety - excess and abandon - world theatre of new found freedoms in tone, color, dance, design, and thought.

Etymology

Based on the literal use for areas traditionally used for hunting animals.
