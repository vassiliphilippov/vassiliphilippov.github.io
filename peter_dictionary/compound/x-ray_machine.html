X-ray machine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks ˌreɪ məˈʃin / (AmE), / ˈɛks ˌreɪ məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A device used to produce X-rays, especially for medical imaging or inspection.

Examples

Electronics has been applied to medicine for many years in the form of such familiar equipment as the x-ray machine, the electrocardiograph, and the diathermy machine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: X-ray - electromagnetic radiation used for imaging

Etymology

From 'X-ray', named after X-radiation discovered by Wilhelm Röntgen, combined with 'machine'.
