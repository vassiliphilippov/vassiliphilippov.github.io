high explosive

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / haɪ ɪksˈpləʊsɪv / (BrE)
A powerful chemical explosive that produces gases rapidly and with great force, often used in munitions and demolition.

Examples

They do not destroy physical structures as is true of high explosives.
Between the ponderous hulk and himself, in the valley over which Papa-san reigned, men had hidden high explosives, booby traps, and mines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low explosive - an explosive that burns rather than detonates, producing gas at a lower rate
