tetrasodium pyrophosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˌsoʊdiəm ˌpaɪroʊˈfɒsfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˌsəʊdiəm ˌpaɪrəˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a sodium salt of pyrophosphoric acid, commonly used as a builder in soaps and detergents

Examples

These widely advertised products, which are used primarily for washing clothes, are based on high-sudsing, synthetic organic actives (sodium alkylbenzenesulfonates) and contain up to 50 % by weight of sodium tripolyphosphate or a mixture of sodium tripolyphosphate and tetrasodium pyrophosphate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium tripolyphosphate - a related sodium salt used in detergents

Etymology

Formed from tetra- (four), sodium, and pyrophosphate.
