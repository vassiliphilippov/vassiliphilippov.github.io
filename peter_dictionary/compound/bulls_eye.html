bull's eye

1. NOUN

/ bʊlz aɪ / (AmE), / bʊlz aɪ / (BrE)
an achievement or result that exactly reaches its intended target or objective; something that is exactly right or successful

Examples

People who take us for suckers are like the Westerner who had on exhibit his superior marksmanship in the form of a number of bull's-eye achievements.

Synset Examples

the new advertising campaign was a bell ringer
scored a bull's eye
hit the mark
the president's speech was a home run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit the mark - to achieve exactly what was intended
  • Antonym: miss the mark - to fail to achieve the intended result

Etymology

Originally from archery and shooting sports, where the 'bull's eye' is the center of a target, representing the most valuable spot.
