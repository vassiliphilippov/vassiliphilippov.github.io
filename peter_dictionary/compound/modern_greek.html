Modern Greek

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑdɚn ˈɡrik / (AmE), / ˌmɒd(ə)n ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the Greek language as spoken and written in the present day, especially since the late medieval period

Examples

But the firm has recognized the tight dollar and the tourist's desire to visit the "smaller, less traveled and relatively inexpensive countries ", and is now prepared to teach modern Greek and Portuguese through recordings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ancient Greek - the Greek language as used during classical antiquity
  • Synonym: Greek - the language of the Greek people, encompassing all historical stages

Etymology

Modern from Latin 'modernus' (of the present time) + Greek, referring to the Hellenic language; distinguishes it from Ancient and Medieval Greek.
