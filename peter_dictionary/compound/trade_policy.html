trade policy

1. NOUN

/ treɪd ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / treɪd ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government's official set of rules and regulations that manage and direct foreign trade.

Examples

We cannot regard foreign policy as something left over after defense policy or trade policy or fiscal policy has been extracted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreign policy - a government's strategy in dealing with other nations
  • Degree Lower: protectionism - the theory or practice of shielding a country's domestic industries from foreign competition
  • Degree Higher: free trade - international trade left to its natural course without tariffs or other restrictions
