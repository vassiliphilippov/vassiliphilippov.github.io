subconscious mind

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈkɑn.ʃəs maɪnd / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒn.ʃəs maɪnd / (BrE)
The part of the mind that influences actions and feelings without conscious awareness.

Examples

And when psychology explains glibly, "but the subconscious mind is able to produce it "it refers to a mental region so vaguely identified that it may embrace the entire universal mind as conceivably as part of the individual mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - the part of the mind not accessible to conscious thought
  • Antonym: conscious mind - the part of the mind involving active awareness and deliberate thought

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'conscious' (from Latin 'conscius', knowing with others or in oneself).
