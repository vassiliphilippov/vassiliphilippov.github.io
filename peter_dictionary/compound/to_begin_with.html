to begin with

1. ADV

/ tə bɪˈɡɪn wɪθ / (AmE), / tə bɪˈɡɪn wɪð / (BrE)
used to introduce the first reason or point in a series, or to refer to the situation as it was at the start.

Examples

To begin with, what is an interior designer?
The meaning of the word is quite physical, to begin with.
Although it had seemed a good reason, to begin with: no couple could afford to have children.

Synset Examples

why didn't you tell me in the first place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the first place - at the beginning; as the first reason
