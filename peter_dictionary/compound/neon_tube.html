neon tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːɑn tuːb / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn tjuːb / (BrE)
A sealed glass tube containing neon gas at low pressure, which emits light when an electric current passes through it; commonly used for signs and decorative lighting.

Examples

As he applied the applicator extending from the machine - which consisted of seven differently colored neon tubes superimposed on a rectangular base - to the supposedly diseased portions of Mrs. Shaefer's body, Lee kept up a steady stream of pseudo scientific mumbo-jumbo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neon lamp - an electric lamp using a tube of neon gas that glows when electricity is applied

Etymology

Neon from Greek 'neos' (new), referring to the element discovered in 1898; 'tube' from Latin 'tubus' (pipe).
