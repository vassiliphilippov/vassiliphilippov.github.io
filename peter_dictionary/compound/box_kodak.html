box Kodak

1. NOUN

/ bɑks ˈkoʊdæk / (AmE), / bɒks ˈkəʊdæk / (BrE)
A simple, box-shaped camera made by Kodak, typically easy to use and popular in the early 20th century.

Examples

Rummaging through a stack of drawers nearby, you unearthed an antique French chess set in ivory and sandalwood, which, along with two box Kodaks, you added to your haul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box camera - a simple box-shaped camera

Etymology

Kodak is a trademarked name coined in the 19th century by George Eastman; 'box' refers to the camera's shape.
