free trader

1. NOUN

/ friː ˈtreɪdər / (AmE), / friː ˈtreɪdə / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices the policy of unrestricted international trade, free from government tariffs or restrictions

Examples

With their customary source of supply cut off, the Fort Garry free traders engaged three men to cart goods to them from the Mississippi country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protectionist - a person who favors government restrictions and tariffs on imports

Etymology

Formed from 'free' + 'trader', relating to the policy of free trade.
