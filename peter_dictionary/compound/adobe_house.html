adobe house

1. NOUN

/ əˈdoʊbi haʊs / (AmE), / əˈdəʊbi haʊs / (BrE)
A house built from adobe bricks, typically constructed with sun-dried clay and straw and laid in horizontal courses.

Examples

MacPherson boldly approached the fortified adobe house and demanded entrance.
The ten or more dangerous parties singled out for prosecution were still at large, and Pels realized that if these men entrenched themselves in their adobe houses, defending themselves through loopholes, it would be most difficult to capture them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sod house - house made of blocks of sod, used especially in the North American prairies

Etymology

From Spanish *adobe*, originating from Arabic *aṭ-ṭūb* (the brick).
