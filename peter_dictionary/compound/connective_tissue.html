connective tissue

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛktɪv ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪv ˈtɪʃuː / (BrE)
The tissue in animals and humans composed of fibers and various cells, such as collagen and fibroblasts, that supports, connects, or separates different organs and structures, and includes tendons, ligaments, and fat.

Examples

This maybe due to the heavy interlobular connective tissue barriers present.
