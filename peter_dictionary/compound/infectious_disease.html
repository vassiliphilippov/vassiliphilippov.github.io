infectious disease

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease caused by pathogenic microorganisms, such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites, that can be spread directly or indirectly from one person to another.

Examples

It is the exploitation of the inherent potential of infectious disease agents by scientific research and development, resulting in the production of BW weapons systems.
The incubation period of infectious disease, plus a variable period of illness even before a lethal effect, render this weapon unsuitable for hand-to-hand encounter.
A man can be an effective fighting machine throughout the incubation period of most infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious disease - disease spread directly from person to person
  • Antonym: noninfectious disease - disease not caused by pathogens or spread by infection

Etymology

From Latin *infectio* (to taint, infect) and Latin *disease*, from Old French *desaise* (lack of ease).
