garden heliotrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdn ˈhɛliəˌtroʊp / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdn ˈhɛliəˌtrəʊp / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant (Valeriana officinalis) with fragrant flowers and underground stems (rhizomes) used in traditional medicine

Examples

My great grandmother, I have been told, made her garden her great pride; she cherished rare and delicate plants like oleanders in tubs and wall-flowers and lemon verbenas in pots that had to be wintered in the cellar; she filled the waste spots of the yard with common things like the garden heliotrope in a corner by the woodshed, and the plantain lilies along the west side of the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valerian - the plant Valeriana officinalis, known for its medicinal properties

Etymology

Heliotrope: from Greek 'helios' (sun) + 'tropos' (turning), though in this context, the name refers to the plant's fragrance, not botanical relation to true heliotropes.
