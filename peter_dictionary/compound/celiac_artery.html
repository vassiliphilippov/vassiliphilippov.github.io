celiac artery

1. NOUN

/ siˈliː.æk ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / siˈliː.æk ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
A major artery that branches from the abdominal aorta below the diaphragm and supplies blood to the stomach, liver, and spleen.

Examples

In particular, the orifices of the right renal and celiac arteries were virtually occluded, and both calcified common iliac arteries were completely occluded.

Etymology

From Greek 'koiliakos' (of the belly); 'artery' from Greek 'arteria'.
