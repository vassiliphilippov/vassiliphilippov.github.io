oral cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrəl ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˈkævəti / (BrE)
The mouth, especially as the anatomical space within the jaws where food is taken in and sounds are produced.

Examples

Scrapings from the bed linen identical with the lint and thread found in the nasal and oral cavities.

Synset Examples

he stuffed his mouth with candy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouth - the opening in the face through which food is taken in and speech is produced

Etymology

From Latin 'os' (mouth) and 'cavitas' (cavity, hollow).
