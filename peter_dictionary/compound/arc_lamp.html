arc lamp

1. NOUN

/ ɑrk læmp / (AmE), / ɑːk læmp / (BrE)
A lamp that produces light by creating an electric arc between two electrodes.

Examples

Hold it! - You need a little more heat on that arc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon arc lamp - a type of arc lamp using carbon electrodes

Etymology

Arc from Latin 'arcus' (bow), referring to the arc-shaped discharge; lamp from Greek 'lampas' (torch).
