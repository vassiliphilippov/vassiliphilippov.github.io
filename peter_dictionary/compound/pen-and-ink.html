pen-and-ink

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn ən ˈɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌpɛn ən ˈɪŋk / (BrE)
A drawing or illustration made using a pen and ink, typically characterized by fine detail and strong contrast.

Examples

George did wonderful complicated pen-and-ink drawings like something out of a medieval miniature: hundreds of delicate details crammed into an eight by ten sheet and looking as if they had been done under a jeweler's glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing - a picture or diagram made with a pencil, pen, or crayon rather than paint
