sign of the cross

1. NOUN

/ saɪn əv ðə krɔs / (AmE), / saɪn əv ðə krɒs / (BrE)
a gesture made by moving the right hand to form a cross, especially as an act of Christian devotion or a profession of faith, commonly used by Catholics and some other Christian denominations

Examples

When you pass a church on an Irish bus, all the hands flurry in the sign of the cross.
