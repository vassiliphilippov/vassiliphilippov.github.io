deputy sheriff

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛpjəti ˈʃɛrɪf / (AmE), / ˈdɛpjʊti ˈʃɛrɪf / (BrE)
A law enforcement officer appointed to assist or act in place of a sheriff, especially during emergencies or in the sheriff's absence.

Examples

On May 19, a deputy sheriff's posse of eight men left Maxwell City and rode thirty-five miles up the Vermejo where they were joined by Juan Jose Martinez.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sheriff - chief law enforcement officer of a county

Etymology

Deputy from Old French 'deputé' (appointed), sheriff from Old English 'scīrgerefa' (shire-reeve, official of a shire).
