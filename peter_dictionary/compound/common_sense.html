common sense

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmən sɛns / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən sens / (BrE)
the ability to make sensible decisions and judgments based on practical reasoning and everyday knowledge

Examples

Others invoked technology and common sense.
He tried to ignore what his own common sense told him, but it was n't possible; her motives were too blatant.

Synset Examples

Common sense is not so common
he hasn't got the sense God gave little green apples
fortunately she had the good sense to run away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good sense - sound judgment and reasoning
  • Synonym: practicality - the quality of being practical or realistic
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sensus communis' (common feeling, general sense).
