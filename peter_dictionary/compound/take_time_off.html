take time off

1. VERB

/ teɪk taɪm ɔf / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to stop working temporarily, especially in order to rest or deal with personal matters

Examples

I kept saying, "If I could just build up a reputation for myself, make some real money, get to be well known as an illustrator - like Peter Askington, for instance - then I could take some time off and paint ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - a period of permitted absence from work
  • Synonym: vacation - a period of time spent away from work or school for rest or recreation
  • Antonym: work - be engaged in physical or mental activity; the opposite of taking time off
