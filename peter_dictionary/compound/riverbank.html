riverbank

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvərˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈrɪvəˌbaŋk / (BrE)
the land along the edge of a river

Examples

Below he could see the bright torches lighting the riverbank.
If he failed to reach the riverbank in five minutes, say, then the skiffs would pull away and leave him groping in the mud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bank - sloping side of a river or body of water

Etymology

Formed from 'river' + 'bank', both of Old English origin.
