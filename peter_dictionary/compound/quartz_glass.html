quartz glass

1. NOUN

/ kwɔrts ɡlæs / (AmE), / kwɔːts ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A colorless and transparent glass made from almost pure silica (silicon dioxide), known for its high resistance to heat and chemicals, often used in laboratory and optical equipment.

Examples

The microscope and orthicon are both selected to operate well into the ultraviolet spectrum, which means that all lenses must be quartz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fused silica - glass made from pure silicon dioxide, similar to quartz glass

Etymology

Formed from 'quartz' (the mineral) and 'glass'; quartz from German 'Quarz,' originally of Slavic origin.
