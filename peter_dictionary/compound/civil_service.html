civil service

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl ˌsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl ˌsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
The permanent professional branches of a government administration, excluding military and judicial branches, whose employees are usually hired on the basis of competitive examination.

Examples

The civil services in such societies are generally inadequate to deal competently with the problems facing them; and their members often equate a government career with security and status rather than with sacrifice, self-discipline, and competence.
The Istiqlal was still firmly united in 1957, but the P.D.I. (Parti Democratique de l'Independance), the most important minor party at the time, objected to the Istiqlal's predominance in the civil service and influence in Radio Maroc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public service - service provided by the government to people; often overlaps with 'civil service' but can include broader activities

Etymology

From 'civil' (relating to citizens) + 'service' (work or duty), used in the 19th century to distinguish government administration from military service.
