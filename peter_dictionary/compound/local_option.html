local option

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl ˈɑpʃən / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
the right of a local government to decide by popular vote whether a particular law, especially one that is controversial, will apply in its area

Examples

A House committee which heard his local option proposal is expected to give it a favorable report, although the resolution faces hard sledding later.
