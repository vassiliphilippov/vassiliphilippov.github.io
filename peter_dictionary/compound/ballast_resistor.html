ballast resistor

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.əst rɪˈzɪs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əst rɪˈzɪs.tə / (BrE)
An electrical resistor placed in a circuit to stabilize current by compensating for changes such as temperature fluctuations.

Examples

Because of the falling characteristic of the rectifier, no ballast resistor was required for stability of operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistor - an electrical component that limits or regulates electric current

Etymology

From 'ballast' meaning stabilizing weight, plus 'resistor' in electronics.
