do work

1. VERB

/ duː wɜrk / (AmE), / duː wɜːk / (BrE)
to be employed or to perform tasks or duties, typically as part of a job or occupation

Examples

"The nuns there do a wonderful work ", the President commented.

Synset Examples

Is your husband working again?
My wife never worked

She works as a waitress to put herself through college

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work - be employed or perform a job
  • Antonym: rest - to cease work in order to relax or recover strength
