ignition key

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnɪʃən kiː / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɪʃən kiː / (BrE)
a key used to operate the ignition switch of a motor vehicle, starting the engine

Examples

When he was bent over behind the wheel of the station wagon, feeling in his trouser cuffs for the ignition key which he had dropped a moment before, she came out of the house with an enormous Rumanian shawl over her head, which she had bought in that country during one of their trips abroad, and handed him a clean handkerchief through the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition switch - the switch in a vehicle that controls the ignition system and starts the engine
