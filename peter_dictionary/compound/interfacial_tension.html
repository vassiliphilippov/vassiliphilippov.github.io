interfacial tension

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfeɪʃəl ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfeɪʃl ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
The surface tension occurring at the interface separating two non-miscible liquids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface tension - the elastic tendency of a fluid surface which makes it acquire the least surface area possible

Etymology

From 'interface' (from Latin inter- ‘between’ + facies ‘appearance, face’) and 'tension' (from Latin tensio ‘a stretching’).
