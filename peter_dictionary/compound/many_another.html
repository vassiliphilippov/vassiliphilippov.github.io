many another

1. DET

/ ˈmɛni əˈnʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeni əˈnʌðə / (BrE)
Used before a singular noun to mean 'many other' of that kind; each of a great number of others.

Examples

Though it was a great relief when the big brains on these shows turned out to be frauds and phonies, it did irreparable damage to the ego of the editor and many another intelligent, well-informed American.

Synset Examples

many a man
many another day will come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: many a - used to mean 'many' with a singular noun for emphasis
