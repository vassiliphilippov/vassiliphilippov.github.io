surface noise

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfɪs nɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs nɔɪz / (BrE)
The background noise caused by the friction of a record player's stylus moving over the surface of a vinyl record.

Examples

All of the releases, however, are recorded at a gratifyingly high level, with resultant masking of any surface noise.
