double standard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbl ˈstændɚd / (AmE), / ˈdʌbl ˈstændəd / (BrE)
A rule or set of principles applied differently and usually more severely to one group of people than to another, often unfairly.

Examples

A kind of double standard exists here for Jewish boys and girls as it does in the realm of sex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group
  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
