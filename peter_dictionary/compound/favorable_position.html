favorable position

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvɚrəbəl pəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbəl pəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A situation or condition that gives someone or something a competitive advantage over others.

Examples

Further evidence that Roman Catholicism enjoys a more favorable position today than in 1861 is the respectful attention given to it in the mass media of England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantage - a condition that gives a greater chance of success
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable or inferior position
