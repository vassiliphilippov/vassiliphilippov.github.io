tibialis anticus

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌbaɪəlɪs ænˈtaɪkəs / (AmE), / tɪˌbaɪælɪs ænˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
a muscle of the lower leg that runs from the tibia to the first metatarsal and cuneiform bones, involved in dorsiflexing the foot

Examples

And they could n't have entrusted Henri to better hands because "le professeur "knows his muscles from the sterno-cleido mastoideus of the neck right down to the tibialis anticus of the leg - and better still, he knows just what exercises work best for them and what Weider principles to combine them with for fast, fast muscle growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tibialis anterior - muscle at the front of the lower leg; modern term for tibialis anticus

Etymology

From Latin 'tibialis' (of the tibia) and 'anticus' (in front).
