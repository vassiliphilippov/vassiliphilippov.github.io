public utility

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌblɪk juːˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪk juːˈtɪləti / (BrE)
A company that provides an essential service, such as water, electricity, or gas, and is regulated by the government.

Examples

Natural gas public utility companies would be given the right of eminent domain, under a bill by Sen. Frank Owen /3, of El Paso, to acquire sites for underground storage reservoirs for gas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utility company - a company that supplies public services such as water, electricity, or gas
