millimeter of mercury

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌmitɚ əv ˈmɝkjʊˌri / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə əv ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
A unit of pressure equal to the pressure exerted by a column of mercury 1 millimeter high at standard gravity; approximately 133.322 pascals.

Examples

Over a temperature range from 25 to 200 * * f and at pressures up to 250 atm, an overload of 300 psi, applied for a period of one day, results in an uncertainty in the pressure of, at most, one millimeter of mercury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torr - unit of pressure equal to 1 mm Hg
  • Synonym: pascal - SI unit of pressure

Etymology

Named for the use of millimeters to measure the height of a column of mercury; 'mercury' from Latin 'mercurium'.
