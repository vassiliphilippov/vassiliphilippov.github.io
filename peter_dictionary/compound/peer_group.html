peer group

1. NOUN

/ pɪr ɡruːp / (AmE), / pɪə ɡruːp / (BrE)
A group of people of similar age, status, or interests, considered as a unit for purposes of comparison or social influence.

Examples

The congregation is first and foremost an economic peer group; it is secondarily a believing and worshiping fellowship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohort - a group of people with a shared characteristic, especially age
