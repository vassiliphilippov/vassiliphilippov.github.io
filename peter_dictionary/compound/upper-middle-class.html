upper-middle-class

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌpɚ ˌmɪdəl ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˌʌpə ˌmɪdl ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
belonging to the upper part of the middle socioeconomic class, typically with higher income, education, and social status than the average middle class

Examples

The unstructured schools were in a large suburban community, predominantly middle - to upper-middle class, but fringed by an industrial area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: middle-class - belonging to the socioeconomic group between the lower and upper classes
  • Degree Lower: lower-middle-class - relating to the lower end of the middle socioeconomic class

Etymology

Formed from 'upper', 'middle', and 'class'.
