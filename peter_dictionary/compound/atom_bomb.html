atom bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬əm bɑm / (AmE), / ˈætəm bɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon that releases immense energy by the fission of heavy atomic nuclei, such as uranium-235 or plutonium-239.

Examples

the atom bomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear bomb - a bomb whose explosive power is due to nuclear reactions

Etymology

From Greek 'atomos' (indivisible) and 'bomb'; coined in English in the 20th century.
