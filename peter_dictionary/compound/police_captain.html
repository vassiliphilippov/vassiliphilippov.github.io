police captain

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˈkæptɪn / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈkæptɪn / (BrE)
A police officer of the rank in charge of a precinct or station, ranking above lieutenant and below the chief or commissioner in many police departments.

Examples

From this side he could see farther into the legation's third story window, but he saw no faces; the room's occupants were still seated or they had been called into the hallway by an alarmed police captain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - an officer ranking above lieutenant
  • Degree Higher: police chief - the head of a police department
  • Degree Lower: police lieutenant - a lower-ranking police officer, below captain

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˌkæp.tɪn / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˌkæp.tɪn / (BrE)
a police officer of high rank who is typically in charge of a police precinct or district

Examples

CHAIRMAN: The witness has testified that you are personally responsible for the murder of a New York City police captain in 1947 and with him a man named Virgil Sollozzo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - an officer holding a position of authority or command
