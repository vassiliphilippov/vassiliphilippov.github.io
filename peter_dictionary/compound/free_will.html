free will

1. NOUN

/ fri wɪl / (AmE), / friː wɪl / (BrE)
the ability to make choices freely and independently, without being controlled by outside forces

Examples

On the second voyage, he had turned back at the frozen island of Novaya Zemlya and meekly given the crew a certificate stating that he did so of his own free will - which was obviously not the case.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: determinism - the belief that all events are caused by preceding factors and not by individual choice
