fishing rod

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃɪŋ rɑd / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ rɒd / (BrE)
A long, thin pole used in angling to cast and control the fishing line.

Examples

They bring their fishing rods and binoculars and bathing suits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rod - a long, thin straight bar or stick of wood, metal, or other material
