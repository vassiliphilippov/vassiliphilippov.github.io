renal artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.nəl ˈɑːr.tɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈriː.nəl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
Either of a pair of arteries branching from the abdominal aorta that supply blood to the kidneys, adrenal glands, and ureters.

Examples

In particular, the orifices of the right renal and celiac arteries were virtually occluded, and both calcified common iliac arteries were completely occluded.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: renal vein - vein that carries blood away from the kidney
  • Synonym: artery - blood vessel carrying blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'renalis' (of the kidney) and Greek 'arteria' (windpipe, artery).
