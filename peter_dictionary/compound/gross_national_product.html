gross national product

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊs ˈnæʃənəl ˈprɑːdʌkt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs ˈnæʃənəl ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
a former measure of a country's total economic output, representing the total market value of goods and services produced by all citizens and capital of a nation in a given period, usually one year

Examples

The industry with which this model is concerned is a basic industry, producing a substantial share of gross national product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gross domestic product - total value of goods and services produced within a country's borders
