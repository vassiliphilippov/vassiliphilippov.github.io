Noctiluca miliaris

1. NOUN

/ nɑk.tɪˈluː.kə mɪl.iˈɛər.ɪs / (AmE), / nɒk.tɪˈluː.kə mɪl.iˈeə.rɪs / (BrE)
A species of large bioluminescent marine protozoan found in ocean waters, known for producing visible light.

Examples

The luminosity of the water is now believed to have been caused by the stimulation of vast numbers of the luminescent organism Noctiluca miliaris by the turbulence of the sea.

Etymology

From Latin: 'noctiluca' meaning 'night-shiner' ('nocti-' night + 'luc-' light') and 'miliaris' meaning 'millet-like,' referring to its appearance.
