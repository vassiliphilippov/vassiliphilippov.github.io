tax return

1. NOUN

/ tæks rɪˈtɜrn / (AmE), / tæks rɪˈtɜːn / (BrE)
an official document in which a person or company gives details of their income and the tax they have paid, used by tax authorities to assess tax liability

Examples

He imagined they were the kind whose tax returns were never examined (if they were, they were never penalized), whose children had no unhappy romances, whose names never knew scandal.
Their United States tax returns are due April 16, 1962.

Synset Examples

his gross income was enough that he had to file a tax return

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax form - form for reporting income to the tax authorities

2. NOUN

/ tæks rɪˈtɝn / (AmE), / tæks rɪˈtɜːn / (BrE)
an official document submitted to a tax authority that states a taxpayer's income and the amount of tax owed or refunded for a specific period

Examples

The following April, Andy did tax returns for half the guards at Shawshank.

Synset Examples

his gross income was enough that he had to file a tax return

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax form - official form for reporting income to tax authorities
