white heat

1. NOUN

/ waɪt hiːt / (AmE), / waɪt hiːt / (BrE)
A temperature so high that a heated object glows white; also used figuratively to describe intense passion or emotion.

Examples

There was about that song something incandescent, for this Brahms was Milstein at white heat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red heat - the temperature at which something glows red when heated
