science fiction

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəns ˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪəns ˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
A genre of literature or film that explores imaginative concepts such as advanced technology, space exploration, time travel, and the impact of science on society, often set in the future or on other worlds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - literature featuring fictional worlds and magical elements
  • Synonym: speculative fiction - fiction genre including science fiction, fantasy, and related genres
  • Antonym: nonfiction - factual writing as opposed to fiction

Etymology

Formed from 'science' + 'fiction', first recorded in early 20th century.
