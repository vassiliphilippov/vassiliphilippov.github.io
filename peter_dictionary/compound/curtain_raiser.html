curtain raiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtn̩ ˌreɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtn ˌreɪzə / (BrE)
an event or activity that takes place before the main event, serving as an introduction or prelude

Examples

All this, though, is simply a prelude, a curtain-raiser, for what ensues, and I doubt whether any Occidental could accurately forecast it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelude - an action or event that serves as an introduction to something more important
  • Synonym: prologue - an introductory section of a literary or musical work
  • Antonym: main event - the principal or most important event

Etymology

Originally from the theatre, referring to a short piece performed before the main play to 'raise the curtain'.
