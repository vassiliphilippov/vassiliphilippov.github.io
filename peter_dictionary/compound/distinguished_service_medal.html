Distinguished Service Medal

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt ˈsɜːrvɪs ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt ˈsɜːvɪs ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
a United States military decoration awarded for exceptionally meritorious service to the government in a duty of great responsibility, typically during wartime

Examples

Mr. Christiansen knows that a soldier will get the Distinguished Service Medal for conduct that would land him in prison for life or the electric chair as a civilian.
