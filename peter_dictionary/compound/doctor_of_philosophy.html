Doctor of Philosophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv fɪˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
an advanced academic degree awarded by universities for original research in any subject, typically abbreviated as PhD

Examples

He completed his training in pharmacy also, taking his degree with high honors in 1797, and in 1799 was awarded the degree of Doctor of Philosophy along with a prize for an essay in medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PhD - abbreviation for Doctor of Philosophy

Etymology

From Latin 'philosophiae doctor', meaning 'teacher (doctor) of philosophy'.
