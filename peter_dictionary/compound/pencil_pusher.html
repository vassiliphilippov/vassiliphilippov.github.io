pencil pusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsəl ˌpʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsl ˌpʊʃə / (BrE)
an office worker who does mostly routine paperwork, often seen as boring or unimportant

Examples

If the acting captain wanted his acting lieutenant to sit on his ass around the station all night, Killpath would just have to go out and drag Gun back by the heels once an hour; because he 'd be damned if he was going to be a mid-watch pencil-pusher just to please his ulcerated pro-tem captain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penpusher - a clerk or office worker who does routine paperwork
  • Synonym: paper-pusher - a person whose job involves excessive paperwork

Etymology

Derived from 'pencil' (writing instrument) and 'pusher' (one who moves or works rapidly), referring to someone who pushes a pencil around on paper rather than doing practical or physical work.
