radio observation

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ ˌɑbzɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˌɒbzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
An observation or measurement made using a radio telescope, typically to study celestial objects by detecting their radio emissions.

Examples

Radio observations of Venus and Jupiter have already supplied unexpected experimental data on the physical conditions of these planets.
Radio observations of the moon have been made over the range of wave lengths from 4.3 mm to 75 cm, and the results are summarized in Table 1.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio astronomy - the study of celestial objects using radio waves
