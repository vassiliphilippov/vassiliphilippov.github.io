line of work

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv wɜrk / (AmE), / laɪn əv wɜːk / (BrE)
a particular type of job or profession that someone does to earn a living

Examples

Because of his brain injury and the extreme damage suffered to his sight, the patient had to train himself for a new line of work, that of a portfolio maker, an occupation requiring a great deal of precision in the making of measurements and a fairly well developed sense of form and contour.

Synset Examples

he's not in my line of business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupation - a person's regular work or profession
  • Synonym: career - an occupation undertaken for a significant period of a person's life
  • Synonym: profession - a type of job that requires special education, training, or skill
