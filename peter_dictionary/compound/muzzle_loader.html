muzzle loader

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌzəl ˌloʊdər / (AmE), / ˈmʌzəl ˌləʊdə / (BrE)
a firearm loaded through the open end of the barrel (the muzzle), rather than from the rear, used before the invention of breech-loading guns

Examples

The American firearms and ammunition manufacturers through diligent research and technical development have replaced the muzzle loader and slow firing single shot arms with modern fast firing autoloaders, extremely accurate bolt, lever, and slide action firearms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: breechloader - a firearm loaded through the rear of the barrel (the breech)

Etymology

From 'muzzle', meaning the open end of the gun barrel, and 'loader', meaning one that loads.
