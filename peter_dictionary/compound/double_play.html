double play

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəl pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl pleɪ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which two players are put out as a result of continuous action.

Examples

Then Throneberry rapped into a fast double play.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triple play - a play in baseball where three players are put out as a result of continuous action
  • Degree Lower: single out - to put out one player in baseball during a play
