power plant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊɚ ˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
A facility where electricity is produced, especially on a large scale for supply to a region or network.

Examples

In November 1900 surveying was done under John Marsden on the east mountains to ascertain if it would be possible to get sufficient water and fall to operate an electric power plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power station - another term for a facility generating electricity
