infectious hepatitis

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs hɛpəˌtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs hɛpəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A mild but contagious form of viral hepatitis (hepatitis A) caused by an RNA virus, typically spread by ingesting food or drink contaminated with fecal matter; the virus does not persist in the blood serum.

Examples

It was the day to canvass for infectious hepatitis.
"Infectious hepatitis ", he shouted heartily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatitis A - A form of viral hepatitis transmitted by contaminated food or water; synonymous with infectious hepatitis.
  • Antonym: serum hepatitis - A more serious form of hepatitis (hepatitis B), usually transmitted by blood or body fluids.

Etymology

From Latin *infectio* (infection) and Greek *hēpar* (liver) + *-itis* (inflammation).
