Ptolemaic system

1. NOUN

/ ˌtoʊləˈmeɪɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌtɒlɪˈmeɪɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The astronomical model developed by Ptolemy in which the Earth is at the center of the universe and all planets and stars revolve around it.

Examples

This and other fears of the solar system have disappeared gradually, first, with the Ptolemaic system and its built-in concept of periodicity and then, more firmly, with the Newtonian innovation of an universal force that could account quantitatively for both terrestrial and celestial motions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heliocentric model - astronomical model with the Sun at the center

Etymology

Named after the astronomer Claudius Ptolemy (c. AD 100–170), who formalized the geocentric model.
