seal of approval

1. NOUN

/ siːl əv əˈpruːvəl / (AmE), / siːl əv əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
an official statement or sign showing that something has been formally approved or recognized for quality or safety

Examples

In addition, the motor has the seal of approval of the Underwriters Laboratories, which means it is safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - the act of giving official approval or support to something

Etymology

From 'seal' meaning a stamp of authentication and 'approval' meaning sanction or acceptance. The expression alludes to the physical seal formerly used to endorse documents.
