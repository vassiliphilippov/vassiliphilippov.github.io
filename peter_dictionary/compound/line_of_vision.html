line of vision

1. NOUN

/ laɪn əv ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / laɪn əv ˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
An imaginary straight line along which an observer is looking; the direct path between an observer's eye and what they are viewing.

Examples

At the same time he watched carefully to see how one attached pegboards to stone walls, but Mr. Blatz was usually standing in his line of vision and it all seemed so simple that he did n't like to disclose his ignorance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line of sight - the straight line along which an observer looks
