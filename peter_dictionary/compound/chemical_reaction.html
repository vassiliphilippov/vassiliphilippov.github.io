chemical reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl riˈakʃən / (BrE)
a process in which substances interact to form new substances with different properties

Examples

Argon was used as a blowing gas to exclude any effects of dissociation or chemical reaction.

Synset Examples

there was a chemical reaction of the lime with the ground water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reaction - the process of changing due to the action of another substance

Etymology

From 'chemical', relating to chemistry (from French chimique), and 'reaction', from Latin reactio (a doing again, response).
