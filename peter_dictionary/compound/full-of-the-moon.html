full-of-the-moon

1. NOUN

/ fʊl əv ðə mun / (AmE), / fʊl əv ðə muːn / (BrE)
The period or moment when the Moon is fully illuminated as seen from Earth; the time of the full moon.

Examples

It was full-of-the-moon (or a little past), and nearly light as day.

Synset Examples

the moon is at the full

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full moon - The phase of the Moon when it is fully illuminated
