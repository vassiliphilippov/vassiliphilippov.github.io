tattletale grey

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæt̬lˌteɪl ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈtætlteɪl ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
A pale greyish-white color, especially as a dingy shade appearing in fabrics after washing.

Examples

A good formulation will keep the clothes clean and white after many washings; whereas, with a poor formulation, the clothes exhibit a build-up of "tattle-tale grey "and dirty spots - sometimes with bad results even after the first wash.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gray - the color between black and white
  • Degree Higher: white - the lightest color, opposite of black

Etymology

From 'tattletale' (someone who betrays secrets) + 'grey', referring to the color 'telling' on fabric.
