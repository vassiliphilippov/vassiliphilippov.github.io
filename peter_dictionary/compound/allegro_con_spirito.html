allegro con spirito

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛɡroʊ kɒn spɪˈritoʊ / (AmE), / əˈlɛɡrəʊ kɒn ˈspɪrɪtəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) played in a lively and spirited manner (with quick tempo and energy).

Examples

The Presto ma non assai of the first trio of the scherzo is taken literally and may shock you, as the real Allegro con spirito of the finale is likely to bring you to your feet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: allegro - a musical direction meaning 'lively, fast tempo'
  • Degree Higher: presto - a musical direction meaning 'very fast, quicker than allegro'

Etymology

Italian, literally 'cheerful with spirit.'
