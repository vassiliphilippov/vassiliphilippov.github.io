capital loss

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl lɔs / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl lɒs / (BrE)
The amount by which the purchase price of an asset exceeds its selling price, resulting in a realized loss when the asset is sold.

Examples

You also deduct 50 % of the excess of net long-term capital gains over net short-term capital losses in determining Adjusted Gross Income.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capital gain - profit from selling an asset for more than its purchase price
