amethystine python

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˌθɪstiːn ˈpaɪθɑn / (AmE), / ˌæməˌθɪstiːn ˈpaɪθən / (BrE)
A large nonvenomous python species with a purplish iridescent sheen, native to Indonesia, Papua New Guinea, and northern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: python - large constricting snake of the family Pythonidae

Etymology

From 'amethyst', referring to its purplish coloration, and 'python', from Greek mythology (Πύθων, a serpent).
