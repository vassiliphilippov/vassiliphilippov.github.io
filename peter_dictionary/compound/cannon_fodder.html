cannon fodder

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænən ˌfɑdər / (AmE), / ˈkænən ˌfɒdə / (BrE)
Soldiers who are regarded as expendable and are sent into dangerous situations with little chance of survival, especially in war.

Examples

First came the cannon fodder, white clad civilians being driven into death as a massive human battering ram.

Etymology

From 'cannon' (artillery gun) and 'fodder' (food for livestock), figuratively meaning people treated as disposable resources.
