out loud

1. ADV

/ aʊt laʊd / (AmE), / aʊt laʊd / (BrE)
In a way that can be heard; using the voice and not silently.

Examples

(he asked this out loud, but no one heard it over the other noise in the hut).
"What's the matter with me "? he demanded out loud.

Synset Examples

please read the passage aloud
he laughed out loud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloud - in a voice that can be heard; not silently

2. ADV

/ aʊt laʊd / (AmE), / aʊt laʊd / (BrE)
In a way that is spoken or audible, not silently; using the voice.

Examples

Our people with the New York detectives said he was half dead, scared stiff and talking out loud that you'’d turned on him.

Synset Examples

please read the passage aloud
he laughed out loud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloud - spoken audibly; not silently
