black hole

1. NOUN

/ blæk hoʊl / (AmE), / blak həʊl / (BrE)
a region in space with a gravitational field so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it, thought to be formed when a massive star collapses at the end of its life cycle.

Examples

They had let this black hole of death in Warsaw's heart exist without a cry of protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singularity - point in space with infinite density, at the center of a black hole

Etymology

From 'black' + 'hole', describing the object's invisibility and gravitational absorption.
