tomato paste

1. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ peɪst / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ peɪst / (BrE)
A thick, concentrated purée made from cooked tomatoes, used as a base or flavoring in cooking.

Examples

Yet if he were not there, they would have missed him, as they would have missed the sounds of bees buzzing against the screen door in early June; or the smell of thick tomato paste - the ripe smell that was both sweet and sour - rising up from aluminum trays wrapped in fly dotted cheesecloth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tomato purée - a less concentrated form of cooked and strained tomatoes

2. NOUN

/ təˈmeɪɾoʊ peɪst / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ peɪst / (BrE)
A thick, concentrated paste made from cooked and strained tomatoes, used as a base or flavoring in cooking.

Examples

Throw in some tomatoes, tomato paste, fry it and make sure it doesn't stick.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tomato purée - cooked and strained tomatoes, less concentrated than paste
  • Degree Lower: tomato sauce - sauce made from tomatoes, thinner than paste or purée
