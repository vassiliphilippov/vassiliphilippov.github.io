mass culture

1. NOUN

/ mæs ˈkʌlt͡ʃɚ / (AmE), / mæs ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
Culture that is produced for and disseminated to large populations through mass media, often characterized by its widespread availability and appeal to general tastes.

Examples

The second timely part of this sketch of literature and the search for identity has to do with the difference between good and enduring literary works and the ephemeral mass culture products of today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popular culture - culture based on the tastes of ordinary people rather than an educated elite
