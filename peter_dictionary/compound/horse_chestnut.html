horse chestnut

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈtʃes.nʌt / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree with palmate leaves, clusters of white or red flowers, and brown, shiny, inedible seeds; native to southeastern Europe and widely planted as an ornamental tree.

Examples

But the shelter is as much a part of my landscape as the beech and horse-chestnut trees that grow on the ridge.

Etymology

So named for supposed resemblance or association with horses and its large seeds similar to sweet chestnut.
