French horn

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ hɔrn / (AmE), / frentʃ hɔːn / (BrE)
a brass musical instrument with a coiled, conical tube and rotary valves, producing a mellow, rounded tone

Examples

A biting, pithy parable of the all pervading hollowness of modern life, the piece has been set by Mlle Lagoon to a sumptuous score (a single motif played over and over by four thousand French horns) by existentialist hot-shot Jean-Paul Sartre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horn - general term for brass instrument with a curved shape

Etymology

The name refers to the instrument's historical association with France and its horn-like shape.
