modern man

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdɚn mæn / (AmE), / ˈmɒdn mæn / (BrE)
A member of the subspecies Homo sapiens sapiens; a human being as distinguished from earlier forms of humans.

Examples

When we look at countries like Iran, Iraq, Pakistan, and Burma, where substantial progress has been made in creating a minimum supply of modern men and of social overhead capital, and where institutions of centralized government exist, we find a second category of countries with a different set of problems and hence different priorities for policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: early man - prehistoric humans or hominins preceding modern man
  • Synonym: human - a person; a member of the species Homo sapiens

Etymology

From Latin modernus (modern) plus Latin homo (man).
