big-ticket

1. ADJ

/ bɪɡ ˈtɪkət / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈtɪkɪt / (BrE)
(of an item) costing a large amount of money; very expensive.

Examples

If anything may be predicted in the quicksilver world of retailing, it seems likely that the suburban branch will come to dominate children's clothing (taking the kid downtown is too much of a production), household gadgetry and the discount business in big-ticket items.

Synset Examples

big-ticket items like cars and furs
a big-ticket government program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expensive - costing a lot of money
  • Antonym: affordable - not costing much; reasonably priced
  • Antonym: cheap - low in price; costing little

Etymology

Formed from 'big' (large) + 'ticket', implying a large price indicated on a ticket; popularized in the mid-20th century.
