buffer zone

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌfər zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə zəʊn / (BrE)
An area of land lying between two rival powers or hostile regions, intended to reduce the risk of conflict between them by serving as a neutral or demilitarized space.

Examples

The family had a lot of buffers!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demilitarized zone - area where military forces are not permitted

Etymology

From 'buffer' (meaning something that lessens shock or protects) + 'zone'. 'Buffer' from Middle French 'bouffeur', from Old French 'bouffer' (to puff, to blow).
