alveolar bed

1. NOUN

/ ælˈvioʊlɚ bɛd / (AmE), / ælˈviːələ bɛd / (BrE)
The part of lung tissue that consists of a dense area of alveoli where gas exchange takes place.

Examples

Very small particles, however, in a size range of 1 to 4 microns in diameter are capable of passing these impinging barriers and entering the alveolar bed of the lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alveolus - a small air sac in the lungs where gas exchange occurs

Etymology

From 'alveolar', relating to alveoli (air sacs in the lungs), and 'bed', meaning a layer of tissue.
