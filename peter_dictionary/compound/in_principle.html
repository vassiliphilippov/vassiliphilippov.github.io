in principle

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ɪn ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something is true or possible as a basic idea, although the details or practical aspects may differ.

Examples

Sintered porous metals should be usable in principle.
The men in power are committed in principle to modernization, but economic and social changes are proceeding only erratically.

Synset Examples

in principle, we agree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in theory - used to say that something should be true or possible according to theory
