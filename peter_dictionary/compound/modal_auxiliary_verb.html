modal auxiliary verb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊdəl ɔːgˈzɪl.iː.ɛri vɜːrb / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dəl ɔːgˈzɪl.i.ər.i vɜːb / (BrE)
A verb such as 'can', 'could', 'may', 'might', 'must', 'shall', 'should', 'will', or 'would', used with another verb to show possibility, necessity, permission, or ability.

Examples

A lovely beast like that running around... ...could put steam in a man's strides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modal verb - a verb used with another verb to express possibility, likelihood, etc.
  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used in forming tenses, moods, and voices of other verbs

Etymology

From 'modal' (from Latin 'modus', mode) plus 'auxiliary verb' (from Latin 'auxilium', help).
