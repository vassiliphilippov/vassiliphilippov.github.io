master's degree

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚz dɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəz dɪˌɡriː / (BrE)
An academic degree awarded by a university or college upon completion of study beyond a bachelor's degree and below a doctor's degree.

Examples

"A person with a master's degree in physics, chemistry, math or English, yet who has not taken Education courses, is not permitted to teach in the public schools ", said Grover.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bachelor's degree - undergraduate academic degree awarded by colleges and universities
  • Degree Higher: doctorate - highest level academic degree, such as PhD or EdD

Etymology

From 'master' (as in Latin magister, teacher) and 'degree' (from Old French degré, from Latin gradus 'step').
