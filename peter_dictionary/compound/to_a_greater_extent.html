to a greater extent

1. ADV

/ tə ə ˈɡreɪtər ɪkˈstɛnt / (AmE), / tə ə ˈɡreɪtə ɪkˈstɛnt / (BrE)
used to indicate that something applies or occurs more than in a previous instance or in comparison with something else; forming comparative expressions for adjectives and adverbs.

Examples

Observations of the radio emission of a planet which has an extensive atmosphere will probe the atmosphere to a greater extent than those using shorter wave lengths and should in some cases give otherwise unobtainable information about the characteristics of the solid surface.

Synset Examples

more interesting
more beautiful
more quickly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to a lesser extent - expressing a lower degree or comparison
  • Synonym: more - comparative form, indicating greater degree or quantity
