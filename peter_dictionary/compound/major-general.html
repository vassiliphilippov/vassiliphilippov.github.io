major-general

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪdʒɚ ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌmeɪdʒə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
A senior military rank above brigadier general and below lieutenant general, typically commanding a division or similar unit.

Examples

He finished the war as a major general, commanding a full division, and at 25 was the youngest major general in the history of the U. S. Army.
He finished the war as a major general, commanding a full division, and at 25 was the youngest major general in the history of the U. S. Army.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brigadier general - a military officer ranking below major-general
  • Degree Higher: lieutenant general - a military officer ranking above major-general

Etymology

From 'major' (senior officer) + 'general' (high military rank); first used in late 17th century.
