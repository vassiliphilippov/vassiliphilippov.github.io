ultraviolet spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
the range of electromagnetic wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays

Examples

The microscope and orthicon are both selected to operate well into the ultraviolet spectrum, which means that all lenses must be quartz.
Many of the cells and microorganisms which are transparent to visible light, absorb or reflect the much shorter wavelengths of the ultraviolet spectrum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: visible spectrum - range of wavelengths visible to the human eye
  • Degree Lower: infrared spectrum - range of electromagnetic wavelengths longer than those of visible light

Etymology

From Latin 'ultra' (beyond) + 'violet', referring to wavelengths beyond violet light; 'spectrum' from Latin (appearance, image).
