complex number

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛks ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛks ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
a number of the form a + bi, where a and b are real numbers and i is the square root of -1, used in mathematics to extend the concept of real numbers

Examples

Let V be a finite dimensional vector space over an algebraically closed field F, e.g., the field of complex numbers.
If we are discussing differentiable complex valued functions, then * * f and V are complex vector spaces, and * * f may be any complex numbers.
We now write * * f where * * f are distinct complex numbers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: real number - number without an imaginary part

Etymology

Formed by combining 'complex' (from Latin *complexus*, 'embraced, entwined') and 'number' (from Latin *numerus*).
