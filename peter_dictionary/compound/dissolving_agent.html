dissolving agent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɑlvɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlvɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A liquid substance capable of dissolving other substances, especially in forming a solution; a solvent.

Examples

The washing process whereby soils are removed consists basically of applying mechanical action to loosen the dirt particles and dried matter in the presence of water which helps to float off the debris and acts, to some extent, as a dissolving and solvating agent.

Synset Examples

the solvent does not change its state in forming a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a liquid that dissolves another substance to form a solution
