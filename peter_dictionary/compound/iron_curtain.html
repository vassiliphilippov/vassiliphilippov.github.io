Iron Curtain

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn ˈkɝːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˈkɜːtn̩ / (BrE)
A notional barrier separating the former Soviet bloc and the West during the Cold War, associated with secrecy, censorship, and restricted communication.

Examples

We have had opportunities - clear invitations to plant our influence on the other side of the Iron Curtain.

Etymology

Popularized by Winston Churchill in 1946; from 'iron' (symbolizing strength and impenetrability) + 'curtain' (a barrier).
