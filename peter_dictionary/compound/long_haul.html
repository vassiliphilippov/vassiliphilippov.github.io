long haul

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ hɔl / (AmE), / lɒŋ hɔːl / (BrE)
a journey or transportation over a long distance, especially by road, air, or rail

Examples

The railroad siding is still important - it is usually, though not always, true that long-haul shipment by rail is cheaper than trucking.

Synset Examples

it's a long haul from New York to Los Angeles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short haul - a journey or transport over a short distance
