pop fly

1. NOUN

/ pɑp flaɪ / (AmE), / pɒp flaɪ / (BrE)
A ball hit high into the air but not far, giving fielders an easy chance to catch it, typically in baseball or softball.

Examples

The White Sox had taken a 5 - 4 lead in the top of the sixth on a pair of pop fly hits - a triple by Roy Sievers and single by Camilo Carreon - a walk and a sacrifice fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly ball - a ball hit high into the air in baseball
