hillbilly music

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪlˌbɪli ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɪlbɪli ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A style of country music that originated in the mountainous regions of the southern United States, especially in the early to mid-20th century.

Examples

Publishers want books that will sell, recording studios want discs that will not seem strange to ears used to hillbilly and jazz music, grade and high schools want quaint, but moral, material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country music - a genre of popular music from rural southern U.S.

Etymology

Formed from 'hillbilly', an informal term for people from rural mountainous regions, especially in the southern U.S., and 'music'.
