state prison

1. NOUN

/ steɪt ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / steɪt ˈprɪzn / (BrE)
A prison operated and maintained by a U.S. state government, used for the incarceration of people convicted of crimes under state law.

Examples

No sympathy or admiration for Brown could be found in the Providence Daily Post, for the editor claimed that there were a score of men in the state prison who were a thousand times more deserving of sympathy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federal prison - A prison operated by the U.S. federal government
  • Antonym: county jail - A local detention facility operated by a county government
