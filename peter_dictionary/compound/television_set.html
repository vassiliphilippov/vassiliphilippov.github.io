television set

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌvɪʒən sɛt / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən sɪt / (BrE)
an electronic device that receives television broadcasts and displays them on a screen

Examples

The spaciousness of the Tudor cooking areas, for example, will provide needed space for the extra television sets required by modern butlers, cooks and maids.
You then descended one story, glommed a television set from the music room - the only constructive feature of your visit, by the way - and, returning to the ground floor, entered the master bedroom.

Synset Examples

the British call a tv set a telly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: television - medium of broadcasting moving visual images
  • Synonym: TV - abbreviation for television set

Etymology

From 'television' (from Greek 'tele' meaning 'far' and Latin 'visio' meaning 'seeing') + 'set', meaning a device or piece of apparatus.
