horn fly

1. NOUN

/ hɔrn flaɪ / (AmE), / hɔːn flaɪ / (BrE)
A small black European fly (Haematobia irritans) that sucks blood from cattle, especially at the base of the horn, and is a pest of livestock.

Examples

Also, aids in the control of horn flies by preventing them from hatching in the droppings.
