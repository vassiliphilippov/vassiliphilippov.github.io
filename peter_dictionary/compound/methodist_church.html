Methodist Church

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛθədɪst tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɛθədɪst tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination founded on the teachings of John Wesley and Charles Wesley, emphasizing personal faith, social justice, and outreach.

Examples

And for him to leave this job now without accomplishing anything would mean practically the end of his career in the Methodist church, if not in all churches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Presbyterian Church - Another Protestant denomination governed by elders
  • Synonym: Baptist Church - Protestant Christian denomination emphasizing baptism by immersion

Etymology

From Methodist, referring to the followers of John and Charles Wesley, and church, meaning a Christian place of worship or its organization.
