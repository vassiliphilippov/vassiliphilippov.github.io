physical property

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
a characteristic of matter or energy, such as mass, color, density, or boiling point, that can be measured or observed without changing its composition

Examples

The biological and physical properties of infectious particles have been studied intensively during the past fifteen years.
The most active preparations obtained by these two groups of investigators appear to be similar in potency, composition and physical properties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chemical property - a property of matter that becomes evident during or after a chemical reaction
