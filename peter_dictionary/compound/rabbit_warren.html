rabbit warren

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌwɔrən / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌwɒrən / (BrE)
A densely crowded and confusing residential area, especially one characterized by narrow, winding passageways or poorly-planned streets and housing.

Examples

The rest of Black Bottom was a rabbit warren of homes in every condition of neglect, disrepair and careful upkeep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a squalid and overcrowded urban street or district inhabited by very poor people

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on the literal sense of 'warren', originally denoting a network of interconnected burrows created by rabbits.
