drying agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪɪŋ ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A substance used to remove moisture from another material, often by absorbing water.

Examples

Calcium hydride was substituted for * * f as a drying agent for carbon tetrachloride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desiccant - a substance that promotes drying by absorbing water
