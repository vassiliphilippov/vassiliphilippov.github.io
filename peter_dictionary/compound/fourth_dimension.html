fourth dimension

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrθ dɪˈmɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ dɪˈmɛnʃən / (BrE)
The hypothetical spatial or temporal dimension beyond the three known spatial dimensions, used in mathematics, physics, and science fiction.

Examples

A "mental image "subconsciously impressing us from beneath its language symbols in wakeful thought, or consciously in light sleep, is actually not an image at all but is comprised of realities, viewed not in the concurrent sensory stream, but within the depths of the fourth dimension.
If the fourth dimension is a physical concept and not purely metaphysical, through what medium does it extend?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dimension - a measurable extent of a particular kind, such as length, breadth, depth, or height
  • Synonym: space-time - the four-dimensional continuum of space and time in which events occur

Etymology

From 'fourth' (ordinal form of 'four') + 'dimension', denoting an extension beyond the three-dimensional space.
