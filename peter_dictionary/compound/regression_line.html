regression line

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛʃən laɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən laɪn / (BrE)
A straight line drawn through a set of data points in regression analysis, showing the relationship between two variables and used for predicting values.

Examples

Children whose reading scores were at least one standard deviation below the regression line of each total third grade school population were considered under-achievers for the purposes of sample selection.
Over-achievers were at least one standard deviation above the regression line in their school system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line of best fit - a straight line that best represents the data on a scatter plot

Etymology

Regression: from Latin regressio (a going back); statistical sense from 19th century statistics. Line from Latin linea (linen thread, line).
