literary study

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərɛri ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
the scholarly or academic examination of literature, its forms, history, and interpretation

Examples

I am not aware of great attention by any of these authors or by the psychotherapeutic profession to the role of literary study in the development of conscience - most of their attention is to a pre-literate period of life, or, for the theologians of course, to the influence of religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literature - written works considered as art or intellectual achievement
  • Synonym: literary criticism - evaluation and interpretation of literary works
