first name

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːst ˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌneɪm / (BrE)
The name given to a person at birth or baptism that comes before the family name; given name.

Examples

(The common misconception that he was Dutch and that his first name was Hendrik stem from Dutch documents of his third voyage.)

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surname - family name or last name
  • Synonym: given name - a person's first name; the name given at birth
  • Antonym: last name - family name, usually appearing after the given name

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌneɪm / (BrE)
The personal name given to someone at birth or baptism, placed before the family name.

Examples

Carter, James...

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surname - family name, inherited and placed after the first name
  • Synonym: given name - the personal name given to someone at birth or baptism
