X-ray film

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛks reɪ ˌfɪlm / (AmE), / ˈɛks reɪ ˌfɪlm / (BrE)
a sheet of photographic film that records images produced by X-rays, used especially in medical imaging

Examples

X-ray films of the vertebral column showed progression of the demineralization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiograph - an image produced on photographic film by the passage of X-rays through an object or body
