heat of condensation

1. NOUN

/ hit əv kɑn.dɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv kɒn.dənˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The amount of heat released by a unit mass of a gas at its boiling point as it condenses to a liquid.

Examples

Heat of condensation (work function) plus kinetic energy of the electrons impinging on the anode.

Synset Examples

the heat of condensation is equal to the heat of vaporization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heat of vaporization - the amount of heat absorbed when a liquid turns into a gas at its boiling point
