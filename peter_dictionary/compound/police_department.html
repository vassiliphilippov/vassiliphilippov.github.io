police department

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis dɪˌpɑrtmənt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs dɪˌpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
A government organization responsible for enforcing the law and preventing crime in a particular area, especially a city or town.

Examples

The jury praised the administration and operation of the Atlanta Police Department, the Fulton Tax Commissioner's Office, the Bellwood and Alpharetta prison farms, Grady Hospital and the Fulton Health Department.
When Mayor Dever heard of the banquet he summoned Hughes for an explanation of why he had been dishonouring the police department by consorting with these felons and fixers.
He could throw himself on the mercy of the Police Department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police force - an organized body of police officers

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlis dɪˌpɑrtmənt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs dɪˌpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
The division of a local government responsible for enforcing laws, maintaining public order, and preventing crime.

Examples

Police Department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police force - a body of police officers organized to enforce the law
  • Antonym: fire department - the division of a local government responsible for firefighting
