man-of-war

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn əv ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌmæn əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
A heavily armed warship, especially one from the 16th to 19th centuries, intended for combat.

Examples

The gilt sterns of the men-of-war becoming clearer to him, the sides of the wooden sea walls alternately painted yellow and black, the bronze cannon at the ports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warship - a ship equipped for fighting in war

Etymology

From 'man' (person) + 'of war' (of combat), originally meaning 'man serving in war', later applied to ships.
