biological warfare

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːrfɛr / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːfɛə / (BrE)
the use of living organisms, such as bacteria, viruses, or toxins, as weapons to harm or kill people, animals, or crops, often during war

Examples

Biological warfare is the intentional use of living microorganisms or their toxic products for the purpose of destroying or reducing the military effectiveness of man.
Biological warfare is considered to be primarily a strategic weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemical warfare - the use of chemical substances as weapons
  • Synonym: germ warfare - warfare involving the use of microorganisms or biological toxins as weapons
