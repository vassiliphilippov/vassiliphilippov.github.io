cast iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæst ˌaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːst ˌaɪən / (BrE)
A hard, brittle alloy of iron with a high carbon content, used for casting into shapes but not easily wrought or forged.

Examples

Square corner - and gate posts were an open-work pattern of cast-iron foliage; they were topped by steeples complete in every detail: high pitched roof, pinnacle, and narrow gable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wrought iron - a tough, malleable form of iron suitable for forging or rolling but not for casting
  • Synonym: steel - an alloy of iron with carbon and usually other elements, less brittle than cast iron

Etymology

From 'cast' meaning 'to form by pouring into a mold' and 'iron'; refers to iron shaped by being cast.
