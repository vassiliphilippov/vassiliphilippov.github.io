forced sale

1. NOUN

/ fɔrst seɪl / (AmE), / fɔːst seɪl / (BrE)
A sale of property, typically under a court order or other legal compulsion, to satisfy an unpaid debt or obligation.

Examples

The forced sale of the General Motors stock owned by or allocable to Christiana, Delaware, and the stockholders of Delaware, and deposited with the trustee, would result in a tax to those parties at the capital gains rate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auction - a public sale at which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
  • Antonym: voluntary sale - a sale undertaken freely by the owner, without legal compulsion
