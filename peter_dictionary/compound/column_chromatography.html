column chromatography

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləm kroʊˌmætəˈɡræfi / (AmE), / ˈkɒləm krəʊˌmætəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
a laboratory method of separating substances in a mixture by passing them through a column filled with an adsorbent material, which selectively retains different compounds

Examples

These include low-temperature ethanol (Cohn) fractionation, electrophoresis, ultracentrifugation and column chromatography on ion exchange celluloses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatography - technique for separating mixtures based on differential absorption

Etymology

From 'column', referencing the tubular container used, and 'chromatography', from Greek 'chroma' (color) and 'graphein' (to write), originally referring to color-based separations.
