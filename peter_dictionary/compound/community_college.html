community college

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunədi ˈkɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪti ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A nonresidential junior college that offers programs and courses designed to meet the educational and vocational needs of the local community.

Examples

Similarly, a girl who graduates with a good working knowledge of stenography and the use of clerical machines and who is able to get a job at once may wish to improve her skill and knowledge by a year or two of further study in a community college or secretarial school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junior college - a college offering courses for two years beyond high school, either as a terminal degree or as preparation for college-level work
