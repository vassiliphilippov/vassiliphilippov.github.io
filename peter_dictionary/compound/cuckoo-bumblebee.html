cuckoo-bumblebee

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊku ˈbʌmbəlˌbi / (AmE), / ˈkʊku ˈbʌmbəlˌbi / (BrE)
A type of bee that lays its eggs in the nests of bumblebees, allowing its larvae to be raised by the host bees.

Examples

Bumblebees can thus suffer severely from the onslaughts of Psithyrus, the "cuckoo bumblebee "as it is called in some European countries.

Etymology

Named for its parasitic behavior, resembling that of the cuckoo bird, which lays eggs in other birds' nests.
