calcium lactate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsiəm ˈlækˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪəm ˈlakteɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt made by reacting lactic acid with calcium carbonate, used in food as a baking powder and as a calcium supplement in medicine

Examples

A high protein diet, calcium lactate supplements, and norethandrolone failed to change the skeletal complaint or the severe muscle weakness.

Etymology

Formed from 'calcium' (named for Latin calx 'lime') and 'lactate' (from Latin lactis 'milk').
