direct current

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈrɛkt ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
An electric current that flows in only one direction continuously, as opposed to alternating current.

Examples

The power source was a commercial D.C. rectifier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alternating current - An electric current that reverses direction periodically

Etymology

Formed from 'direct' (straight, without deviation) and 'current' (flow of electricity).
