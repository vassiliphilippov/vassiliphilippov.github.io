grade school

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪd skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd skuːl / (BrE)
A school for young children, typically covering the first six to eight years of formal education.

Examples

Publishers want books that will sell, recording studios want discs that will not seem strange to ears used to hillbilly and jazz music, grade and high schools want quaint, but moral, material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elementary school - a school for the first years of a child's education, usually ages 5-11
  • Synonym: primary school - a school for children between the ages of about 5 and 11
