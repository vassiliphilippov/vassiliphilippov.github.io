psychic phenomenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkɪk fəˈnɑːməˌnɑːn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkɪk fɪˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
A phenomenon that appears to defy physical laws and suggests influence or causation by mental or supernatural processes.

Examples

Every dream, and this is true of a mental image of any type even though it may be readily interpreted into its equivalent of wakeful thought, is a psychic phenomenon for which no explanation is available.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranormal - Events or phenomena beyond scientific explanation
  • Synonym: supernatural - Attributed to forces beyond natural laws
  • Antonym: natural phenomenon - An observable event explainable by natural laws

Etymology

From Greek 'psychikos' (of the soul, psychic) and 'phainomenon' (that which appears, phenomenon).
