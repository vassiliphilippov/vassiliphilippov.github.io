Leiden jar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪdən dʒɑɹ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪdən dʒɑː / (BrE)
a glass jar coated inside and out with metal foil, used as an early form of capacitor for storing static electricity, invented in the 18th century

Examples

Thus, 1745 corresponded to the invention of the "Leiden "jar by Kleist, 1764 that of the electrophorus by Wilcke, 1782 produced the condenser of Volta, and 1801 the voltaic pile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacitor - an electrical component for storing energy electrostatically

Etymology

Named after the city of Leiden in the Netherlands, where it was invented in the 18th century.
