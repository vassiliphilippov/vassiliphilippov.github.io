light beam

1. NOUN

/ laɪt bim / (AmE), / laɪt biːm / (BrE)
A narrow column or stream of light, typically from a lamp, laser, or other directed source.

Examples

The round cells were reproducibly positioned in the light beam which entered the thermostated mineral oil bath through a window.
The light beam from the lamp was filtered through a half standard thickness Corning 1840 filter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a narrow beam of light; synonym for 'light beam'
  • Antonym: darkness - the absence of light or illumination
