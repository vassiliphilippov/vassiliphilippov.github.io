fallout shelter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔl.aʊt ˌʃɛl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.aʊt ˌʃel.tə / (BrE)
a covered structure designed to protect people from radioactive fallout in the event of a nuclear explosion

Examples

An outdoor, aboveground fallout shelter also may be built with concrete blocks.
They form effective fallout shelters when mounded over with earth, as shown in figure 10.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunker - an underground shelter, often used during war
  • Synonym: bomb shelter - a shelter built to protect occupants from bombs

Etymology

From 'fallout' (radioactive particles falling after a nuclear explosion) and 'shelter'.
