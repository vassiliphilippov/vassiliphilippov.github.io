royal brace

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪəl breɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪəl breɪs / (BrE)
A rope or line used to secure or control the royal mast on a sailing ship.

Examples

"Small violently jerked the weather royal brace with full intention to carry away the mast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace - a rope for controlling the yard of a sail

Etymology

From 'royal', referring to the topmost sail or mast on a square-rigged ship, and 'brace', meaning a rope used for controlling sails.
