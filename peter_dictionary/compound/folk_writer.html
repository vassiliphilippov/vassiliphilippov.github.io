folk writer

1. NOUN

/ foʊk ˈraɪtər / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈraɪtə / (BrE)
A writer who composes or records folktales or traditional stories.

Examples

In the second place, a large number of writers, making a more direct claim than Frost to being "folk writers "of one sort or another, clearly make no distinctions between genuine and bogus material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folklorist - a person who studies or collects folklore
  • Synonym: storyteller - a person who tells or writes stories
