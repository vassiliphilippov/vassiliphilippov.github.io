personal pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.sən.əl ˈproʊ.naʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.sən.əl ˈprəʊ.naʊn / (BrE)
A pronoun that refers to a particular person or thing and shows distinctions of person (such as I, you, he, she, it, we, they).

Examples

The personal pronouns and substitute one are normally unstressed because they refer to what is prominent in the immediate context.
the personal pronoun has dominant stress because "he "has not been mentioned previously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word used in place of a noun

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənl proʊˈnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənl prəˈnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun that refers to a specific person or thing, typically expressing distinctions such as first, second, or third person, and often number and gender

Examples

Johnny Ola told me about this place.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pronoun - a word that stands in for a noun

Etymology

Formed from 'personal' and 'pronoun'; 'pronoun' from Latin 'pronomen' (standing for a noun).
