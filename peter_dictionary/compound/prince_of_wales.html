Prince of Wales

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɪns əv weɪlz / (AmE), / ˈprɪns əv weɪlz / (BrE)
the title given to the male heir apparent of the British monarch

Examples

By springtime, he was supported by a rich merchant syndicate under the patronage of Henry, Prince of Wales.

Etymology

Title in use since the 14th century, originally conferred by the English monarchy after the conquest of Wales.
