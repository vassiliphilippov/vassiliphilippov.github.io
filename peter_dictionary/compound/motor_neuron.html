motor neuron

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtər ˈnʊroʊn / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˈnjʊərəʊn / (BrE)
a nerve cell that transmits impulses from the brain or spinal cord to muscles or glands, enabling movement

Examples

Electromyography revealed no evidence of lower motor neuron disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensory neuron - a nerve cell transmitting impulses toward the brain or spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin 'motor' (mover) and Greek 'neuron' (nerve).
