psychoactive drug

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈæktɪv drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈæktɪv drʌɡ / (BrE)
A drug that affects mental processes, producing changes in mood, perception, or consciousness.

Examples

The former comprise analeptic and psychoactive drugs, the latter the tranquilizers.
The emotional states produced by drugs influence the cortical potentials in a characteristic manner; synchrony prevails in the EEG of the experimental animal after administration of tranquilizers, but asynchrony after application of analeptic and psychoactive drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a drug that causes hallucinations
  • Synonym: stimulant - a substance that increases physiological or nervous activity
  • Synonym: depressant - a drug that reduces functional or nervous activity

Etymology

Formed from 'psycho-' (mind) and 'active', referring to an agent that acts on the mind; 'drug' from Old French ‘drogue’.
