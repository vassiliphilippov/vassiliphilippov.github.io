thyrotrophic hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊfɪk ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪrəˈtrɒfɪk ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the activity of the thyroid gland.

Examples

The hormone has also been called thyrotrophin or thyrotrophic hormone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyrotropin - another name for thyrotrophic hormone

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreo-' (shield, referring to the thyroid gland) and 'trophe' (nourishment); '-ic' forming adjectives; 'hormone' from Greek 'hormōn' (that which sets in motion).
