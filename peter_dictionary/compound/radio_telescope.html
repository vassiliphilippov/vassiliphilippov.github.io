radio telescope

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ təˈlɛskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ tɪˈlɛskəʊp / (BrE)
An astronomical instrument that collects and detects radio waves from space to study celestial objects.

Examples

High sensitivity in radio telescopes is achieved by reducing the bandwidth of the receiver; therefore, only with precise foreknowledge of the line frequencies is an astronomical search for the radio spectra of these molecules feasible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optical telescope - telescope that collects visible light to observe celestial objects

Etymology

Formed from 'radio', referring to radio-frequency electromagnetic waves, and 'telescope', from Greek 'tēle' (far) and 'skopein' (to look).
