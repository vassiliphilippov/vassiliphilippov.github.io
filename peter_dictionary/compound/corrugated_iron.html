corrugated iron

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəˌɡeɪtəd ˈaɪərn / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəˌɡeɪtɪd ˈaɪən / (BrE)
Sheet iron or steel, typically galvanized, pressed into parallel ridges and hollows to make it stronger, commonly used for roofing and walls.

Examples

We send shovels, cement, nails, and corrugated iron for roofs.
