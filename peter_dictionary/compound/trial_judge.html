trial judge

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪəl ˌdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl ˌdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
A judge who presides over a court trial and makes rulings during the proceedings.

Examples

The trial judge decided that the administrative procedures of the Act were fully complied with and refused to require the production of such documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - a public official who decides cases in a court of law
