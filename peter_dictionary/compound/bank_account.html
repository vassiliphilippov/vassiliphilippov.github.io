bank account

1. NOUN

/ bæŋk əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / baŋk əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
An arrangement with a bank in which a customer deposits and withdraws money and from which the bank keeps a record of transactions.

Examples

It permits the state to take over bank accounts, stocks and other personal property of persons missing for seven years or more.

Synset Examples

he moved his bank account to a new bank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: account - a record or arrangement for keeping financial transactions, especially at a financial institution

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋk əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈbaŋk əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
An arrangement with a bank whereby one deposits and withdraws money and manages funds electronically or by other means.

Examples

The man with the bank accounts.

Synset Examples

he moved his bank account to a new bank

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: savings account - an account intended for storing savings, often earning interest
  • Degree Lower: checking account - a bank account from which withdrawals can be made using checks or debit cards

Etymology

Formed from 'bank' (from Italian 'banca', meaning bench or moneylender's table) and 'account' (from Old French 'aconter', to reckon).
