face-saving

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪs ˌseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs ˌseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
an action or measure taken to avoid embarrassment or a loss of dignity or prestige

Examples

And he could no longer think of face-saving, of honor, but only of escape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humiliation - state of being made to feel ashamed or losing dignity
  • Antonym: embarrassment - feeling or state of being embarrassed

Etymology

From the phrase 'save face', meaning to preserve one's dignity; 'face' in this sense is translated from the Chinese concept of honor or social standing.
