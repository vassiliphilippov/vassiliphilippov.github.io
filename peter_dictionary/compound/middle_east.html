Middle East

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪdəl ˈist / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ˈiːst / (BrE)
A region around the eastern Mediterranean, typically including countries from Turkey and Egypt to Iran, noted for its historical significance and cultural influence.

Examples

In the Middle East, Iraq, Syria and Egypt were, a short while ago, in the Western camp.

Synset Examples

the Middle East is the cradle of Western civilization

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' and 'east', referring historically to lands east of Europe but west of South and East Asia; in use since the early 20th century.
