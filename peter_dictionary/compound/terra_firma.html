terra firma

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈfɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌtɛərə ˈfɜːmə / (BrE)
dry land; solid ground, especially as distinct from the sea or air.

Examples

But time is running out, and many of Stravinsky's admirers begin to fear that he will never find terra firma.

Synset Examples

the plane turned away from the sea and moved back over land
the earth shook for several minutes
he dropped the logs on the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: land - the solid surface of the earth
  • Antonym: sea - the expanse of salt water covering much of the earth's surface
  • Synonym: ground - the solid surface of the earth

Etymology

From Latin terra firma (solid earth).
