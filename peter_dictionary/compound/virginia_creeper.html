Virginia creeper

1. NOUN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə ˈkripər / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪniə ˈkriːpə / (BrE)
A common North American vine with compound leaves and bluish-black berrylike fruit, often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Examples

Side fences were hidden beneath lilacs and hundred leaf roses; front fences were covered with Virginia creeper or trumpet vines or honeysuckle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeper - a plant that grows along the ground or up surfaces by means of extending stems

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Virginia and 'creeper' for its climbing habit.
