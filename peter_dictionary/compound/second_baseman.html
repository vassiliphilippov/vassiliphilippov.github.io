second baseman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈbeɪsmən / (AmE), / ˈsekənd ˈbeɪsmən / (BrE)
(in baseball) a player who defends the area near second base and is responsible for fielding balls hit to that area.

Examples

Rookie southpaw George Stepanovich relieved Hyde at the start of the ninth and gave up the A's fifth tally on a walk to second baseman Dick Howser, a wild pitch, and Frank Cipriani's single under Shortstop Jerry Adair's glove into center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortstop - player at the position between second and third base in baseball
  • Synonym: first baseman - player at first base in baseball
