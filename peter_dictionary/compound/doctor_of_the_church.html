Doctor of the Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv ðə tʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ðə tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A title given by the Roman Catholic Church to certain saints who have made a significant contribution to theology through their teachings and orthodoxy.

Examples

The grounds for the Church's position are Scriptural (Old Testament), the teachings of the fathers and doctors of the early Church, the unbroken tradition of nineteen centuries, the decisions of the highest ecclesiastical authority and the natural law.

Synset Examples

the Doctors of the Church greatly influenced Christian thought down to the late Middle Ages

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor' meaning 'teacher', and 'ecclesia' meaning 'church'.
