synchronous motor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˈmoʊ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˈməʊ.tə / (BrE)
An electric motor in which the rotational speed is directly proportional to the frequency of the alternating current power supply.

Examples

Because of light leakage from one ultraviolet source to another, the lights are switched by a commutator like assembly rotated by a synchronous motor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asynchronous motor - an electric motor whose speed is not directly proportional to the supply frequency

Etymology

From 'synchronous' (from Greek 'synchronos', meaning 'occurring at the same time') + 'motor'.
