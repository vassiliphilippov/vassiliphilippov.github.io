Aerobacter aerogenes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.oʊ.bæk.tər ˌɛr.əˈdʒiːniːz / (AmE), / ˌɛərəʊˈbæktə ˌɛərəˈdʒiːniːz / (BrE)
a species of Gram-negative, aerobic bacteria that produce gas and acid from sugars and are sometimes involved in the souring of milk

Examples

Cultures taken from the jejunum yielded Monilia albicans, Pseudomonas pyocanea, Aerobacter aerogenes, and Streptococcus anhemolyticus.

Etymology

From Greek 'aero-' (air), Latin 'bacter' (rod), and Latin 'aerogenes' (produced by air).
