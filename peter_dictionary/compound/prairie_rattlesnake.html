prairie rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri ˈræt̬lˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈræt(ə)lˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a venomous rattlesnake (Crotalus viridis) native to the grasslands of North America, distributed between the Mississippi River and the Rocky Mountains

Examples

Other herpetologists have ascertained that in the northern United States the prairie rattlesnake may not give first birth until it is four or even five years old, and that the young may be born every other year, rather than annually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - any of various venomous New World snakes notable for their rattle-tipped tail

Etymology

From 'prairie', referring to grassland habitat, and 'rattlesnake', from the snake's characteristic tail rattle.
