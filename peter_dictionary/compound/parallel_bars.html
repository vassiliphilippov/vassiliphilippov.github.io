parallel bars

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌlɛl bɑrz / (AmE), / ˈpærəlɛl bɑːz / (BrE)
a piece of gymnastic equipment consisting of two parallel wooden or metal bars fixed horizontally at the same height on uprights, used for exercises and competitions

Examples

The parallel bars, horse, buck, springboard, horizontal bar, rings, and mats formerly in the school gyms were replaced by baseball, volleyball, basketball and football.
