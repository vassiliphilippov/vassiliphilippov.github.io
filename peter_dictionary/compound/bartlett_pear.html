Bartlett pear

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrtlət pɛr / (AmE), / ˈbɑːtlət peə / (BrE)
A variety of pear with a juicy, sweet yellow flesh, commonly eaten fresh or canned.

Examples

It is roughly shaped like a large pear, and when properly ripened, its dark green skin covers a meaty, melon like pulp that has about the consistency of a ripe Bartlett pear, but oily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Williams pear - the same pear variety known as 'Williams' in Britain and other countries

Etymology

Named after Enoch Bartlett, an American nurseryman who popularized the variety in the early 19th century.
