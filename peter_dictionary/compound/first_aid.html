first aid

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɝst ˈeɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈeɪd / (BrE)
help and emergency medical treatment given to an injured or sick person before full medical care is available

Examples

Can your plant nurse be replaced by a trained first-aid man who works full-time on some other assignment?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency care - urgent medical treatment in emergencies

Etymology

From 'first' (earliest) and 'aid' (help); in use since the late 19th century.
