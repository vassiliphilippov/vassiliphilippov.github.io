railroad station

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɑd ˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊd ˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
A terminal where trains stop to pick up or drop off passengers or goods.

Examples

A band of robbers enters a railroad station, overpowers and ties up the telegraph operator, holds up the train and escapes.
