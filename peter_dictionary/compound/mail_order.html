mail order

1. NOUN

/ meɪl ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / meɪl ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A system of buying and selling goods where orders are made by mail and products are delivered to the customer by post.

Examples

The Postmaster General recently reported that mail order frauds - among which fake therapeutic devices figure prominently - are at the highest level in history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: online shopping - purchasing goods or services over the Internet
  • Antonym: in-store purchase - buying goods directly from a physical shop
