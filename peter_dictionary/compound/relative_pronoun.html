relative pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv proʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun (such as 'that', 'which', or 'who') that introduces a relative clause and refers to an antecedent mentioned earlier in the sentence

Examples

Indeed, I can proudly say that some of my very best friends are Italian-Americans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that substitutes for a noun or noun phrase

Etymology

From 'relative', from Latin 'relativus' (relating to), and 'pronoun', from Latin 'pronomen'.
