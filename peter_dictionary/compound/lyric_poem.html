lyric poem

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪrɪk ˌpoʊəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪk ˌpəʊəm / (BrE)
A short poem expressing the personal thoughts or feelings of a single speaker, often in a songlike form.

Examples

An idea, of the sort that we have in mind, although of necessity readily available to imagination, is more general in connotation than most poetic or literary images, especially those appearing in lyric poems that seek to capture a moment of personal experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ode - A type of lyric poem characterized by elaborate or stately style
  • Antonym: ballad - A narrative poem, often set to music

Etymology

From Greek 'lyrikos' (singing to the lyre) and 'poem' from Greek 'poiēma' (a thing made).
