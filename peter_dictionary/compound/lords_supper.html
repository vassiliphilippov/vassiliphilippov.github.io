Lord's Supper

1. NOUN

/ lɔrdz ˈsʌpɚ / (AmE), / lɔːdz ˈsʌpə / (BrE)
A Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with his disciples, typically involving the consecration and sharing of bread and wine.

Examples

Bern demands that the Lord's Supper be administered here as it used to be, with unleavened bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Communion - another term for the Christian sacrament of sharing bread and wine
  • Synonym: Eucharist - the central Christian rite commemorating the Last Supper
