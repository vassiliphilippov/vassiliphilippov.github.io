movie camera

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvi ˌkæmərə / (AmE), / ˈmuːvi ˌkæmərə / (BrE)
A camera used to record a sequence of photographs on film or digital media that, when viewed rapidly, create the illusion of moving images.

Examples

After a few tortuous moments of wondering who "he "is, the camera pans across the room to the plaster statue, and we realize that Neitzbohr is trying to redeem himself in the eyes of a mute piece of sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: video camera - camera used for recording moving images electronically
