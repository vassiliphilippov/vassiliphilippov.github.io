arcus senilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkəs səˈnaɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈɑːkəs sɪˈnaɪlɪs / (BrE)
A whitish or grayish arc-shaped deposit sometimes seen at the margin of the cornea, commonly associated with aging.

Examples

The body was that of a well developed, somewhat debilitated white man weighing 108 lb. There were bilateral pterygia and arcus senilis, and the mouth was edentulous.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'arch of old age'.
