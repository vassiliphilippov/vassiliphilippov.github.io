ion exchange

1. NOUN

/ aɪ.ɑn ɪkˈsʧeɪndʒ / (AmE), / aɪ.ɒn ɪksˈʧeɪndʒ / (BrE)
a chemical process in which ions are swapped between a solution and an insoluble solid (typically a resin), commonly used in industrial and laboratory applications

Examples

These include low-temperature ethanol (Cohn) fractionation, electrophoresis, ultracentrifugation and column chromatography on ion exchange celluloses.

Etymology

From 'ion' (Greek 'ion', going) and 'exchange' (Old French 'eschangier')
