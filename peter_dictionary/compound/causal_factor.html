causal factor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔzəl ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzəl ˈfæktə / (BrE)
A factor that directly causes or contributes to a particular result or outcome.

Examples

A broader concept of imitation is needed, one which acknowledges that true invention is important, that the artist's creativity in part transcends the non artistic causal factors out of which it arises.

Synset Examples

education is an important determinant of one's outlook on life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinant - something that determines or influences an outcome
