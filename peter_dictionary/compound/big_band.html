big band

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ bænd / (AmE), / bɪɡ band / (BrE)
A large group of musicians, especially in jazz or dance music, typically playing arranged pieces with sections for improvised solos.

Examples

A few years ago a "Timex All Star Jazz Show "offered a broad range of styles, ranging from Lionel Hampton's big band to the free-wheeling Dukes of Dixieland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jazz band - a musical group specializing in jazz music
  • Degree Higher: orchestra - a large group of instrumental musicians
