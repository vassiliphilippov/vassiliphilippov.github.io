sense organ

1. NOUN

/ sɛns ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / sɛns ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
An organ of the body that receives and responds to external or internal stimuli, such as the eyes, ears, nose, tongue, or skin.

Examples

Neither is primary experience understood according to the attitude of modern empiricism in which nothing is thought to be received other than signals of sensory qualities producing their responses in the appropriate sense organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptor - a specialized cell or group of nerve endings that responds to sensory stimuli
