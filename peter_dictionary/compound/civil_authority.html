civil authority

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvəl əˈθɔrəti / (AmE), / ˈsɪv(ə)l ɔːˈθɒrəti / (BrE)
A person or organization with the power to govern or make decisions over civilian, non-military matters.

Examples

Only confusion, failure and anarchy result when the effort is made to impose upon the civil authority the impossible task of policing private homes to preclude the possibility of sin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: military authority - person or body exercising power over armed forces

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to citizens) and 'auctoritas' (influence, command).
