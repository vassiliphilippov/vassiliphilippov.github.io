auto limitation

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːtoʊ lɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊ lɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(chiefly legal or philosophical) The act of self-imposed limitation, especially by a government, authority, or individual as an expression of self-will or general consent.

Examples

In the international community this reduced law to Jellinek's auto limitation.

Etymology

From Greek 'auto' meaning 'self' and Latin 'limitatio' (limitation).
