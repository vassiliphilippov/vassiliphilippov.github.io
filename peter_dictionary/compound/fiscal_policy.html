fiscal policy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪskl ˈpɑːləsi / (AmE), / ˈfɪskl ˈpɒlɪsi / (BrE)
The use of government spending, taxation, and borrowing to influence a country's economy.

Examples

We cannot regard foreign policy as something left over after defense policy or trade policy or fiscal policy has been extracted.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monetary policy - government regulation of the money supply and interest rates

Etymology

From Latin 'fiscus' (treasury) + Greek 'politikē' (affairs of state).
