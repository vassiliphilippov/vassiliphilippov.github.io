North Pole

1. NOUN

/ nɔrθ poʊl / (AmE), / nɔːθ pəʊl / (BrE)
the northernmost point on the Earth's surface, situated at the end of the Earth's axis of rotation in the Arctic region

Examples

In 1607 and 1608, the English Muscovy Company had sent him northward to look for a route over the North Pole or across the top of Russia.
The ship's compass was useless because of the nearness of the magnetic North Pole.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: South Pole - the southernmost point of Earth's axis
