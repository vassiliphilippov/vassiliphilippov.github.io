lined snake

1. NOUN

/ laɪnd sneɪk / (AmE), / laɪnd sneɪk / (BrE)
A small, secretive snake native to central United States, often found in city dumps, parks, prairies, and open woods, primarily feeding on earthworms.

Examples

These three were much alike: lined snake (Tropidoclonion), one year and nine months; red-bellied snake (Storeria), two years; cottonmouth (Ancistrodon), two years.
