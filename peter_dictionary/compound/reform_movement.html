reform movement

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrm ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
A social or political campaign or movement aimed at introducing changes or improvements to existing systems, laws, or institutions, especially to promote greater fairness or humanitarian goals.

Examples

Young Morris, who, while attending the University of Pennsylvania, also taught and edited a paper, found time to write Henrietta twenty page letters on everything that engaged his interest, from the acting of Sarah Bernhardt in Philadelphia to his reactions to the comments of "Sulamith "on the Jewish reform movement being promulgated by the Hebrew Union College in Cincinnati.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conservative movement - a movement opposed to or reversing reforms, advocating traditional values

Etymology

From 'reform', from Latin reformare (to form again), and 'movement', from Old French movement (movement, motion), from Latin movimentum.
