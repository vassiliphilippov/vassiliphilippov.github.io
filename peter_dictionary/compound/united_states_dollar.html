United States dollar

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪtɪd steɪts ˈdɑlər / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtɪd steɪts ˈdɒlə / (BrE)
the official currency and basic unit of money in the United States of America, represented by the symbol $.

Examples

a special fund created for that purpose pursuant to subsection (a) of this section any amounts hereafter paid, in United States dollars, by a foreign government which has entered into a claims settlement agreement with the Government of the United States as described in subsection (a) of section 4 of this Title.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dollar - the basic monetary unit of several countries, including the US, Canada, Australia, and others

Etymology

From United States (country name) + dollar, from German Thaler (a large silver coin).
