well-kept

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛl kɛpt / (AmE), / ˈwɛl kɛpt / (BrE)
(of a place or object) maintained in good condition and kept neat and tidy.

Examples

The females like to burrow in the short turf of well-kept lawns, where their little mounds of earth often appear by the hundreds.
On the other side of the church was a quiet, well-kept house with shutters and recently painted.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neglected - not given proper care; poorly maintained

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (in good, satisfactory way) + 'kept' (past participle of 'keep').
