Purple Heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpl hɑːt / (BrE)
A United States military decoration awarded to members of the armed forces who are wounded or killed in action.

Examples

"He recommended both of them for the DSM and the Detroit fellow for the Purple Heart, too, for a combat inflicted wound.

Etymology

Named after the heart-shaped purple badge established by General George Washington in 1782.
