prison camp

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən kæmp / (AmE), / ˈprɪz(ə)n kamp / (BrE)
A camp where prisoners of war or political prisoners are detained, often under harsh conditions.

Examples

In his novel The House of the Dead, Dostoevsky tells of his experiences in a Siberian prison camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration camp - camp where civilians, political prisoners, and minorities are confined, often without trial
  • Synonym: internment camp - camp for detaining people, especially during wartime
  • Antonym: refugee camp - temporary camp for people forced to flee their homes
