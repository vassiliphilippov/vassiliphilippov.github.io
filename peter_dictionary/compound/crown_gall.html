crown gall

1. NOUN

/ kraʊn ɡɔl / (AmE), / kraʊn ɡɔːl / (BrE)
A bacterial disease of plants, especially fruit trees, grapes, and roses, characterized by the formation of tumor-like growths (galls) near the stem base, caused by Agrobacterium tumefaciens.
