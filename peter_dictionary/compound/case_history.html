case history

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs ˌhɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs ˌhɪstəri / (BrE)
A detailed written account of the background and circumstances of a person or group, especially as used for study, diagnosis, or treatment in fields such as medicine or psychology.

Examples

Undoubtedly you have read the case histories of some of his prize winning pupils (every pupil has a physique title of some kind or other).
