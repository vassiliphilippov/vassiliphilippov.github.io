direct evidence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈrɛkt ˈɛv.ə.dəns / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈɛv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
Evidence that directly proves a fact in question, such as the testimony of a witness who saw an event happen.

Examples

The direct evidence on the micrometeorite environment near the Earth is obtained from piezoelectric sensors (essentially microphones) and from wire gages; these instruments are installed on rockets, satellites, and space probes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: circumstantial evidence - evidence that relies on inference to connect it to a fact
