biochemical mechanism

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈkɛm.ɪ.kəl ˈmɛk.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈkɛm.ɪ.kəl ˈmɛk.əˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
A chemical process or sequence of chemical reactions involved in vital activities within living organisms.

Examples

At the present time we do not know by what biochemical mechanism TSH acts on the thyroid, but for bio-assay of the hormone there are a number of properties by which its activity may be estimated, including release of iodine from the thyroid, increase in thyroid weight, increase in mean height of the follicular cells and increase in the thyroidal uptake of * * f.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanism - a system of parts working together in a machine; a process by which something takes place

Etymology

From 'biochemical', relating to the chemistry of living things (bio- + chemical), and 'mechanism', from Greek mēkhanē (machine).
