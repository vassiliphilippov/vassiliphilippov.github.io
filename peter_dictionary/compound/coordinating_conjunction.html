coordinating conjunction

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɔrdɪˈneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction, such as 'and' or 'or', that joins two words, phrases, or clauses of equal grammatical rank

Examples

And it worked.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinating conjunction - a conjunction that introduces a dependent clause (e.g., 'because', 'although')

Etymology

From Latin 'coordinare' (to arrange) + 'conjunctio' (joining together).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌɔrdɪˈneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɔːdɪˈneɪtɪŋ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction that connects words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance in a sentence, such as 'and', 'but', or 'or'

Examples

MICHAEL: I thought you were going to tell me that you were moving your family to Vegas and that you'’d been offered the, uh, Vice Presidency of the Houstan hotels there.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinating conjunction - a conjunction that introduces a subordinate clause, such as 'because', 'although', or 'if'

Etymology

From 'coordinate' (to arrange or join on an equal footing) + 'conjunction' (from Latin 'coniunctio', joining together).
