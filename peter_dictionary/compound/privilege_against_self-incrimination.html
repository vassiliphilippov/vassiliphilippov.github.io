privilege against self-incrimination

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ əˈɡɛnst ˌsɛlf ɪnˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ əˈɡɛnst ˌsɛlf ɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the legal right to refuse to answer questions or give testimony that could incriminate oneself, especially as protected by the Fifth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution

Examples

If you give up the right to remain silent, anything you say will be held against you in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right to remain silent - the right not to answer questions or give information to police or in court
  • Synonym: self-incrimination - the act of implicating oneself in a crime or exposing oneself to prosecution
