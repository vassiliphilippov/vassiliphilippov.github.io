high society

1. NOUN

/ haɪ səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / haɪ səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
The group of people in a community who are considered to be the most fashionable, wealthy, or socially prominent.

Examples

And this in addition to his usual fear of being among people of high society.
Beaten, bruised and exhausted, he pursues the elusive killer through the demi-monde of high society and low morals, always alone, always despised.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upper class - the social group with the highest status and privilege
  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, often holding hereditary titles
  • Antonym: lower class - the social group with the least wealth and status
