diversionary attack

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvɝʒəˌnɛri əˈtæk / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːʃənəri əˈtæk / (BrE)
A military tactic involving an attack intended to distract or mislead the enemy and draw their defenses away from the main target.

Examples

Because of this diversionary attack the main group that had been pinned down on the hill was able to surge forward again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feint - a deceptive or pretended blow, thrust, or other movement in battle
  • Antonym: main attack - the primary or principal attack in a military operation
