stained-glass window

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪnd ɡlæs ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪnd ɡlɑːs ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A window made of pieces of colored glass held together with strips of lead, often forming decorative designs, commonly found in churches and other significant buildings.

Examples

The stained-glass windows may have developed unpremeditated patinas, the paneling may be no more durable than the planks in a political platform.

Etymology

From 'stained glass' (colored glass) + 'window'.
