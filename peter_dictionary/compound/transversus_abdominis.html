transversus abdominis

1. NOUN

/ trænzˌvɜrsəs æbˈdɑmɪnɪs / (AmE), / tranzˌvɜːsəs æbˈdɒmɪnɪs / (BrE)
a flat muscle of the abdominal wall with horizontal fibers, forming part of the anterior and lateral walls of the abdomen

Examples

Microscopic studies of the gastrocnemius, pectoralis major, transversus abdominis, biceps brachii, and diaphragm showed atrophy as well as varying degrees of injury ranging from swelling and vacuolization to focal necrosis of the muscle fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectus abdominis - a long, flat muscle of the front of the abdomen
  • Synonym: external oblique - a broad, thin muscle on each side of the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'transversus' (turned across) and 'abdominis' (of the abdomen).
