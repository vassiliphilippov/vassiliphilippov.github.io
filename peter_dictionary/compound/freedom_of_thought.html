freedom of thought

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdəm əv θɔt / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm əv θɔːt / (BrE)
The right or liberty to think whatever one chooses, especially to hold beliefs or ideas that may be unpopular or controversial.

Examples

We are reminded, however, that freedom of thought and discussion, the unfettered exchange of ideas, is basic under our form of government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedom of speech - the right to express opinions without censorship
  • Antonym: censorship - the suppression of free expression
