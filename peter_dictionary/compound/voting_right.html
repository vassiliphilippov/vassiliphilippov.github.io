voting right

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.tɪŋ raɪt / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.tɪŋ raɪt / (BrE)
the legal right to participate in a vote, especially the right of a shareholder to vote on company matters in person or by proxy

Examples

Christiana and Delaware would, in turn, be required to pass on the voting rights to the General Motors shares allocable to them to their own stockholders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: franchise - the right to vote in public elections
