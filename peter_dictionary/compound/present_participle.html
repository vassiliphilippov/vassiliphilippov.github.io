present participle

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzənt pɑːrˈtɪsɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɛzənt pɑːˈtɪsɪpəl / (BrE)
A verb form that expresses an ongoing action, usually formed in English by adding -ing to the base verb, and used in continuous tenses and as an adjective.

Examples

Would you be willing to swear before a judge and jury, having placed your hand on the Good Book and taken an oath before Almighty God himself?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participle - a non-finite verb form used as an adjective or to form verb tenses
  • Antonym: past participle - a participle expressing completed action, often ending in -ed or -en

Etymology

From Latin 'participium praesens', literally 'present participle'.
