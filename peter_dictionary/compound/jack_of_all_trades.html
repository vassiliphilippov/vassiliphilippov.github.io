Jack of all trades

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæk əv ɔl ˈtreɪdz / (AmE), / ˌdʒæk əv ɔːl ˈtreɪdz / (BrE)
A person who is competent at many different types of work but not necessarily outstanding in any one area.

Examples

Nor will the hack, the Jack-of-all-trades, still found in some of the smaller art schools, suffice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specialist - a person highly skilled in a specific field

Etymology

From the given name Jack, used generically for 'man' or 'common fellow', combined with 'trades' meaning occupations or skills. The phrase dates to the 17th century.
