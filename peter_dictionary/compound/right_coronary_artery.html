right coronary artery

1. NOUN

/ raɪt ˈkɔːrnɛri ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / raɪt ˈkɒrənəri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
the main artery arising from the right aortic sinus that supplies blood to the right side of the heart

Examples

The coronary arteries were sclerotic and diffusely narrowed throughout their courses, and the right coronary artery was virtually occluded by a yellow atheromatous plaque 1.5 cm. distal to its origin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left coronary artery - the main artery arising from the left aortic sinus that supplies blood to the left side of the heart

Etymology

coronary from Latin 'coronarius', relating to a crown, due to the crown-like arrangement around the heart.
