prairie dog

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛri dɒɡ / (AmE), / ˈpreəri dɒɡ / (BrE)
a small North American burrowing rodent of the squirrel family, noted for its shrill barking call and complex colonies known as 'towns'

Examples

Prairie dogs were popping up and popping down.

Etymology

Named for its habitat (prairie) and barking dog-like call.
