natural gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃ(ə)rəl ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ɡas / (BrE)
A fossil fuel in the gaseous state, used chiefly as a fuel for heating, cooking, and generating electricity.

Examples

Natural gas public utility companies would be given the right of eminent domain, under a bill by Sen. Frank Owen /3, of El Paso, to acquire sites for underground storage reservoirs for gas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: propane - a component of natural gas, used as a fuel
  • Degree Lower: methane - the primary component of natural gas
  • Synonym: petroleum - another major fossil fuel, typically liquid
