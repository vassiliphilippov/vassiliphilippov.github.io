The Star-Spangled Banner

1. NOUN

/ ði stɑr ˌspæŋgəld ˈbænɚ / (AmE), / ði stɑː ˌspæŋgəld ˈbænə / (BrE)
the national anthem of the United States, originally a poem by Francis Scott Key set to music and officially adopted in 1931

Examples

[BAND PLAYING MUSIC] [BAND PLAYING THE STAR-SPANGLED BANNER] [SPEAKING ITALIAN]

Etymology

From 'star-spangled' (decorated with stars) and 'banner' as used in the poem by Francis Scott Key (1814).
