golden glow

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldən ɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ɡləʊ / (BrE)
a tall North American perennial plant (Rudbeckia laciniata) with branching stems and large, bright yellow, double flower heads, often grown in gardens

Examples

Alley fences were made of solid boards higher than one's head, but not so high as the golden glow in a corner or the hollyhocks that grew in a line against them.
