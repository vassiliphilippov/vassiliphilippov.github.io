telescopic sight

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛskəpɪk saɪt / (AmE), / tɛlɪˈskɒpɪk saɪt / (BrE)
an optical gun sight that uses a telescope to improve aiming accuracy by magnifying the target

Examples

Like, for example, a man carrying a twenty-two rifle, probably with a telescopic sight attached?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scope - short form for telescopic sight, especially in firearms context

Etymology

From "telescopic" (from telescope, itself from Italian "telescopio") and "sight" (from Old English "siht").
