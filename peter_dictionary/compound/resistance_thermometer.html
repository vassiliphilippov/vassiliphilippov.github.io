resistance thermometer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstəns θərˈmɑmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A thermometer that measures temperature by detecting changes in the electrical resistance of a metal, typically a platinum wire spiral.

Examples

As a first step toward this goal, arrangements were worked out for comparing the scales now in use through circulation of a group of standard platinum resistance thermometers for calibration by each national laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermistor - temperature sensor whose resistance varies with temperature, usually semiconductors
