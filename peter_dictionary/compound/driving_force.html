driving force

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvɪŋ fɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ fɔːs / (BrE)
A force that causes something to move or progress; a motivating influence behind an action or process.

Examples

But perhaps this was a part of the eternal plan, that man's ambition when linked with God would be a driving, indefatigable force for good in the world.

Synset Examples

after reaching the desired velocity the drive is cut off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impetus - something that encourages a process or activity to develop more quickly
  • Synonym: motivation - the reason or reasons for acting or behaving in a particular way
  • Antonym: obstacle - something that blocks or impedes progress
