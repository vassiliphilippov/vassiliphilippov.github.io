court of law

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt əv lɑ / (AmE), / kɔːt əv lɔː / (BrE)
A formal tribunal, presided over by a judge or magistrate, where legal cases are heard and justice is administered according to the law.

Examples

You made a good living, had police protection and there were courts of law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal for the administration of justice
