voting precinct

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊ.tɪŋ ˈpriː.sɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈvəʊ.tɪŋ ˈpriː.sɪŋkt / (BrE)
A district within a city or town established for electoral purposes, each containing a designated polling place.

Examples

In the Blue Ridge meeting, the audience was warned that entering a candidate for governor would force it to take petitions out into voting precincts to obtain the signatures of registered voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polling place - a specific location where people go to vote in an election
