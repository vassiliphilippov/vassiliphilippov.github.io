absolute temperature

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.səˈluːt ˈtɛmp.ɚ.ə.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌæb.səˈluːt ˈtɛmp.ər.ə.tʃə / (BrE)
The temperature of an object measured from absolute zero on the thermodynamic (Kelvin) scale; temperature measured from a fixed, non-arbitrary starting point, typically in kelvins.

Examples

Such an interferometer provides a means of absolute temperature measurement, and may be used as an alternative to the gas thermometer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kelvin - SI base unit of absolute temperature
  • Degree Lower: Celsius - temperature scale with 0 °C as freezing point of water

Etymology

Formed from 'absolute' (from Latin absolutus, freed or unrestricted) and 'temperature' (from Latin temperatura, moderation, mixture). Refers to measurement from an absolute reference point (absolute zero).
