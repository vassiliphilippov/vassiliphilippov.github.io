Staten Island

1. PROPN

/ ˈsteɪtən ˈaɪlənd / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtən ˈaɪlənd / (BrE)
A borough of New York City located on an island southwest of Manhattan, known for its suburban character and proximity to the Staten Island Ferry.

Examples

Below in the open bay facing Manhattan was Staten Island, gritty with clam shells and mud flats behind which nested farms, cattle barns, and berry thickets.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch parliament, the States General, as 'Staaten Eylandt' by early Dutch settlers.

2. PROPN

/ ˌstætən ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˌstæt.ən ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
One of the five boroughs of New York City, located in the southwest part of the city.

Examples

And from Staten Island we have with us Victor Strachi.

Etymology

Named after the States General (Staten-Generaal) of the Netherlands.
