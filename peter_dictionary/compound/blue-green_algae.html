blue-green algae

1. NOUN

/ bluː ɡriːn ˈældʒi / (AmE), / bluː ɡriːn ˈælɡi / (BrE)
a group of mainly photosynthetic bacteria (cyanobacteria) that contain a blue pigment in addition to chlorophyll, found in a variety of aquatic and terrestrial habitats

Examples

It was possible to maintain aerobic conditions in the pond by regular additions of sodium nitrate until the temperature decreased and the algae population changed from blue-green to green algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanobacteria - photosynthetic bacteria that are the scientific name for blue-green algae
