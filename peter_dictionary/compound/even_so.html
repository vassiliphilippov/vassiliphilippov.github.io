even so

1. ADV

/ ˈivən ˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːvən ˈsəʊ / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with what has just been said, meaning 'despite that' or 'nevertheless'.

Examples

Even so, he could not ease the tension of his body; the rough surface of the earth itself seemed to resist every attempt on his part to relax.
They looked, even so.
Even so, Madden's dislike of the suave, correct lawyer deepened.
Even so, he generally listened and was usually reasonable to those who voiced their objections properly.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; nonetheless
  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with the preceding one
