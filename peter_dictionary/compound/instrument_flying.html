instrument flying

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈflaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnstrəmənt ˈflaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or technique of navigating and flying an aircraft solely by reference to instruments rather than by visual cues.

Examples

He possessed the fighter pilot's horror of bad weather and instrument flying, and he wondered, if the ceiling did drop, whether he and the other flights would be able to find their way back in this unfamiliar territory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visual flying - Flying an aircraft using outside visual references
