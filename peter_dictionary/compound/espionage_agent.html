espionage agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛspiəˌnɑːʒ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɛspɪənɪdʒ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A person who is employed to spy on another country or business competitor in order to obtain secret information.

Examples

It was a ridiculous situation and Rector knew it, for Hino, frankly partisan, openly gregarious, would make a poor espionage agent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - person who secretly collects information for a government or competitor
  • Degree Higher: double agent - a spy who pretends to serve one government while actually serving another

Etymology

From French *espionnage* (spying) and English *agent* (representative).
