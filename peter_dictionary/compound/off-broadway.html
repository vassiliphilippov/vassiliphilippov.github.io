off-Broadway

1. NOUN

/ ɑf ˈbrɔdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ɒf ˈbrɔːdˌweɪ / (BrE)
Theaters or theatrical productions in New York City that are smaller and less commercial than those on Broadway, typically with lower budgets and often located outside the Broadway theater district.

Examples

As Broadway itself becomes increasingly weighted down by trite, heavy-handed, commercially successful musicals and inspirational problem dramas, the American theatre is going through an inexorable renaissance in that nebulous area known as "off-Broadway ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Broadway - major commercial theater district in New York City

Etymology

From 'off' (meaning apart from or away from) + 'Broadway', the famous theater district in Manhattan.
