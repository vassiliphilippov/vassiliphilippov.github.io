winter solstice

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚ ˈsɑl.stɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tə ˈsɒl.stɪs / (BrE)
The time, usually around December 21 or 22 in the Northern Hemisphere, when the sun is at its southernmost point, resulting in the shortest day and longest night of the year.

Examples

It is curious that even centuries of repetition of the yearly cycle did not induce a sufficient degree of confidence to allow people to abandon the ceremonies of the winter solstice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: summer solstice - The time, usually around June 21 or 22, when the sun is at its northernmost point, resulting in the longest day of the year.

Etymology

From Latin 'solstitium' (solstice), from 'sol' (sun) + 'sistere' (to stand still).
