Payne's gray

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪnz ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˌpeɪnz ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
a dark bluish-gray pigment used in painting, producing a greyish to dark greyish blue color

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Etymology

Named after William Payne (c. 1760–1830), English watercolorist who developed and popularized the color.
