farm club

1. NOUN

/ fɑrm klʌb / (AmE), / fɑːm klʌb / (BrE)
A minor-league sports team that is owned by or affiliated with a major-league team, especially in baseball, and serves to develop players for the major league.

Examples

Ryne Duren and Roland Sheldon, a rookie righthander who posted a 15 - 1 record last year for the Yanks' Auburn (N.Y.) farm club of the Class - D New York-Pennsylvania League, are the probable rival pitchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farm team - minor-league team affiliated with a major-league team

Etymology

From 'farm' (in the sense of developing talent) and 'club' (organization or team); developed in early 20th-century American baseball slang.
