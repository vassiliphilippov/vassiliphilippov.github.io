Roman collar

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈkɑː.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈkɒl.ə / (BrE)
a stiff, white band worn as a collar by members of the clergy, typically with no opening in the front, signifying their religious office

Examples

Since the Protestant clergy for the most part wear gray or some variant from the wholly black suit, my Roman collar and black garb usually identify me in England as a Roman Catholic cleric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerical collar - a stiff white band worn around the neck by clergy

Etymology

Named for its association with the Roman Catholic Church.
