Delhi boil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛli bɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɛli bɔɪl / (BrE)
A form of leishmaniasis affecting the skin, marked by ulcerative lesions.

Examples

Additionally, since you 're going to be hors de combat pretty soon with sprue, yaws, Delhi boil, the Granville wilt, liver fluke, bilharziasis, and a host of other complications of the hex you 've aroused, you must n't expect to be lionized socially.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutaneous leishmaniasis - medical term for Delhi boil; skin infection caused by Leishmania parasites
  • Synonym: Oriental sore - another common term for cutaneous leishmaniasis

Etymology

Named after Delhi, India, where the disease has been prevalent; 'boil' refers to the appearance of the skin lesion.
