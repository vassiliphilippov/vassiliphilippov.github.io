executive branch

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
the branch of government responsible for implementing and enforcing laws, typically headed by a president or equivalent executive leader

Examples

If there is anything which we can do in the executive branch of the Government to speed up the processes by which we come to decisions on matters on which we must act promptly, that in itself would be a major contribution to the conduct of our affairs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legislative branch - the branch of government responsible for making laws
  • Antonym: judicial branch - the branch of government responsible for interpreting laws
