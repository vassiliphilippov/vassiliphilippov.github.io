stream of consciousness

1. NOUN

/ striːm əv kənˈʃɑnsnəs / (AmE), / striːm əv ˈkɒnʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
A literary style or technique that depicts the continuous flow of a character's thoughts, feelings, and reactions, often in an unstructured or associative manner.

Examples

Evidence is plentiful that early and later also he has been indebted to the Gothic romancers, who deal in extravagant horror, to the symbolists writing at the end of the preceding century, and in particular to the stream-of-consciousness novelists, Henry James and James Joyce among them.

Etymology

Figurative use of 'stream' and 'consciousness'; popularized in early 20th-century literary criticism after William James's psychological term.
