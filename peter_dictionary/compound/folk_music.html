folk music

1. NOUN

/ foʊk ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music that originates in traditional popular culture or is written in such a style, typically passed down orally and associated with the life and customs of a community.

Examples

Thoroughly modern in treatment, they are at the same time, full of simple sincerity which invariably characterizes genuine Negro folk-music and are by no means to be confused with the average' Broadway Spirituals' which depend for their racial flavor upon sundry allusions to the' Amen Corner',' judgement day',' Gabriel's horn', and a frustrated devil - with a few random' Hallelujahs' thrown in for good measure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditional music - music passed down through generations, characteristic of a specific culture or community
  • Antonym: classical music - music written in a Western classical tradition, usually formal and often not of anonymous origin

Etymology

From 'folk' (Old English folc, people) + 'music' (from Greek mousikē, art of the Muses).
