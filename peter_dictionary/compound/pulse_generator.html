pulse generator

1. NOUN

/ pʌls ˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / pʌls ˈdʒɛnəreɪtə / (BrE)
A device that produces single or multiple voltage pulses, usually with adjustable rate, used in electronics and signal processing.

Examples

A sonar or radar type of pulse generator and time-delay measuring system is required for body tissue evaluation.
