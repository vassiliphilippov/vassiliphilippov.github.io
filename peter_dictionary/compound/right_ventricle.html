right ventricle

1. NOUN

/ raɪt ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / raɪt ˈvɛntrɪkl̩ / (BrE)
the chamber of the heart located on the right side, which receives deoxygenated blood from the right atrium and pumps it into the pulmonary artery

Examples

The heart weighed 510 gm., and at the outflow tracts the left and right ventricles measured 19 and 3 mm., respectively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: left ventricle - the chamber of the heart on the left side pumping blood into the aorta

Etymology

From Latin 'ventriculus' (small belly); 'right' from Old English 'riht'.
