four times

1. ADV

/ fɔr taɪmz / (AmE), / fɔː taɪmz / (BrE)
By a factor of four; multiplied by four.

Examples

For example, the BBB has reported it was receiving four times as many inquiries about quack devices and 10 times as many complaints compared with two years ago.
Note that the mass threshold is four times that of 1958 Alpha and that the flux is one fifth as large.

Synset Examples

the price of gasoline has increased fourfold over the past two years

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three times - by a factor of three; multiplied by three
  • Degree Higher: five times - by a factor of five; multiplied by five
