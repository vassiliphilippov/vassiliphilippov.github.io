in fact

1. ADV

/ ɪn fækt / (AmE), / ɪn fakt / (BrE)
Used to emphasize the truth of a statement, often introducing additional information or a clarification; in reality, actually.

Examples

"These actions should serve to protect in fact and in effect the court's wards from undue costs and its appointed and elected servants from unmeritorious criticisms ", the jury said.
Although he never gets to play while the clock is running, he gets a big kick - several every Saturday, in fact - out of football.
"In fact our whole defensive unit did a good job ".
It is, in fact, a relatively mild chastisement of the dead.
One of those capital-gains ventures, in fact, has saddled him with Gore Court.

Synset Examples

in fact, it was a wonder anyone survived
painters who are in fact anything but unsophisticated
as a matter of fact, he is several inches taller than his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actually - used to emphasize the real or true situation
  • Synonym: in reality - in truth as opposed to what is imagined or supposed
