code of conduct

1. NOUN

/ koʊd əv ˈkɑndʌkt / (AmE), / kəʊd əv ˈkɒndʌkt / (BrE)
A set of rules or principles outlining the standards of behavior expected of members of a group, organization, or profession.

Examples

The individualism and public service of the private detective both stem from his dedication to a personal code of conduct: he enforces the law without being told to do so.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: code - a system of principles or rules
