senior high school

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːniɚ haɪ ˌskuːl / (AmE), / ˈsiːniə haɪ ˌskuːl / (BrE)
A public secondary school that typically includes the final years of schooling, usually grades 9 through 12, for students aged about 14 to 18.

Examples

Rep. Henry C. Grover, who teaches history in the Houston public schools, would reduce from 24 to 12 semester hours the so-called "teaching methods "courses required to obtain a junior or senior high school teaching certificate.
Indeed, one school superintendent in a large city objects to the use of the term comprehensive high school for the senior high schools in his city, because these schools do not offer strictly vocational programs.

Synset Examples

he goes to the neighborhood highschool

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: high school - a school for children between elementary and college, usually grades 9–12
  • Degree Lower: junior high school - a school for children between elementary and senior high, usually grades 7–9
