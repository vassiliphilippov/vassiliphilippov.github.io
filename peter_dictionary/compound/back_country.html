back country

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæk ˌkʌntri / (AmE), / ˈbæk ˌkʌntri / (BrE)
A remote, undeveloped, and sparsely inhabited region far from towns or cities.

Examples

They have an ancestry extending back, however, at least to 1728, when William Byrd described the Lubberlanders he encountered in the back country of Virginia and North Carolina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilderness - uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable area
  • Synonym: backwoods - remote, wooded, sparsely inhabited area
  • Antonym: city - large and densely populated urban area
