stage right

1. NOUN

/ steɪdʒ ˈraɪt / (AmE), / steɪdʒ ˈraɪt / (BrE)
The part of a theater stage that is to the right of an actor facing the audience.

Examples

According to the myth, Old Order then vanishes at stage left and reappears at extreme stage right, but director Shuz skillfully sidesteps the rather gooshey problem of stage effects by simply having Miss Arapacis walk across the stage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stage left - the part of the stage on the actor's left as the actor faces the audience
