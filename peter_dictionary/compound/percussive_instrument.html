percussive instrument

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌsɪv ˌɪnstrəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / pəˈkʌsɪv ˌɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
A musical instrument that produces sound by being struck, shaken, or scraped, such as drums, cymbals, or xylophones.

Examples

Drums, xylophones, castanets, and other percussive instruments are reproduced remarkably well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion instrument - Alternative, more common form of 'percussive instrument'

Etymology

From Latin percussio (a striking), from percutere (to strike).
