nuclear resonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuˌkliɚ ˈrɛzəˌnəns / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə ˈrɛzənəns / (BrE)
A phenomenon in which a nucleus absorbs a gamma ray at a specific frequency, identical to the frequency at which it previously emitted the gamma ray.

Examples

It is important to consider and experimentally verify this influence since quantitative nuclear resonance is becoming increasingly used in investigations of structure.

Etymology

From 'nuclear', relating to the nucleus, and 'resonance', from Latin 'resonantia' (echo, resounding).
