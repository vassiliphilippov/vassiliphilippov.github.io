beacon light

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːkən laɪt / (AmE), / ˈbiːkən laɪt / (BrE)
A tower or structure equipped with a light used as a warning or navigational aid to ships, especially to mark dangerous areas such as shoals.

Examples

The great ultimate ends of religion have served as magnificent beacon lights that lured people toward them with an almost irresistible force, mobilizing energies and inducing sacrifices; for example, the Crusades, mission efforts, just wars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lighthouse - a tower with a powerful light to guide or warn ships

Etymology

From Middle English 'beken' (beacon) and 'light', referring to a signal fire or light for guidance.
