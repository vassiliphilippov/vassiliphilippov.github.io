quarter mile

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrdɚ maɪl / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtə maɪl / (BrE)
A unit of length equal to one fourth of a mile, equivalent to 440 yards or about 402 meters.

Examples

Kerr, who set the world record earlier this month in New York with a clocking of 1.09.3, wiped out Mills's early pace and beat the young Big 10 quarter-mile king by 5 yards.
Still a quarter-mile away, the fresh clap of guns only served to increase his speed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,760 yards (about 1,609 meters)
  • Degree Lower: yard - a unit of length equal to 3 feet, used in the UK and US
