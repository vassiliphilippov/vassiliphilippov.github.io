sawdust saloon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːˌdʌst səˈluːn / (AmE), / ˈsɔːˌdʌst səˈluːn / (BrE)
A bar or saloon with a floor covered in sawdust, historically common in the United States, especially in the 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

The influence of Mass was less pervasive than that of the congested, slum tenements among the bawdy houses, honkytonks, and sawdust saloons of his birthplace; he ran wild with the child gangs of the neighbourhood, and went through the normal pressure-cooker course of thieving, police dodging, and housebreaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a place where alcoholic drinks are served
  • Synonym: saloon - a public room or building for the sale and consumption of alcoholic drinks
