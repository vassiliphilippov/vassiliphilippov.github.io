display window

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspleɪ ˈwɪn.doʊ / (AmE), / dɪˈspleɪ ˈwɪn.dəʊ / (BrE)
A large window at the front of a store, facing the street, where merchandise is arranged to attract customers.

Examples

It was professedly worth three thousand dollars in stock and good will, and the name was written in gold in foot high letters across each of the two display windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shop window - window at the front of a shop displaying merchandise
