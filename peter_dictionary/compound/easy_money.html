easy money

1. NOUN

/ ˈizi ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈiːzi ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money earned with little or no effort, often implying that it is obtained more easily than is usual or expected

Examples

Every man who dabbles in the market to make a little easy money on the side and suffers losses could at the time hardly face his wife who was wondering how her husband could be so dumb.
He rather wished he had never got into the business, and still - scarcely to be resisted, a nice little profit with not much work involved, easy money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - an unexpected amount of money received
