justice of the peace

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌstɪs əv ðə pis / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪs əv ðə piːs / (BrE)
A magistrate, usually in a local area, who has limited judicial powers such as handling minor legal matters and civil cases.

Examples

A Mexican justice of the peace had issue a writ against Chavez for taking part in the "murder "of Manuel Gonzales so he and his father were anxious to be taken out of danger.

Etymology

From the concept of 'justice' (from Latin justitia) and 'peace' (from Latin pax), referring to a magistrate charged with keeping the peace.
