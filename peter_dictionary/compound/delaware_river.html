Delaware River

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛləˌwɛr ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɛləwɛə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in the eastern United States that flows from southeastern New York, along the borders of Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Delaware, and empties into Delaware Bay.

Examples

In the Delaware River, three long islands were overgrown with greening trees and underbrush.

Etymology

Named after Thomas West, 3rd Baron De La Warr, an early colonial governor of Virginia.
