home plate

1. NOUN

/ hoʊm pleɪt / (AmE), / həʊm pleɪt / (BrE)
(Baseball) the base that a batter stands beside and must be touched by a runner to score a run.

Examples

After Al Paschal grounded out, Jay Cooke walked and Jim McDaniel singled home McAuliffe.
Bobby Lowe of Boston was the first to hit four at home and Gil Hodges turned the trick in Brooklyn's Ebbetts Field.
By the time the fielder got his hands on the ball Deegan was rounding third base and heading for home.

Synset Examples

he ruled that the runner failed to touch home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home base - another term for home plate in baseball

2. NOUN

/ hoʊm pleɪt / (AmE), / həʊm pleɪt / (BrE)
In baseball, the flat, five-sided slab of rubber at one corner of the diamond, where the batter stands and which a runner must touch to score a run.

Examples

The Anniston catcher was straddling home plate.
He went back to touch home plate, turned and walked to the dugout without looking back.

Synset Examples

he ruled that the runner failed to touch home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: base - one of the four stations that must be reached by a runner in baseball
