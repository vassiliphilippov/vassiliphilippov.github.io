rank and file

1. NOUN

/ ˌræŋk ən ˈfaɪl / (AmE), / ˌræŋk ənd ˈfaɪl / (BrE)
The ordinary members of an organization or group, especially as opposed to its leaders or officers.

Examples

He was n't troubled with the ordinary, rank-and-file fear that overcomes and paralyzes and sends individual soldiers and whole companies under fire running in panic.
The soldiers who comprised the rank and file of the Civil War armies were an earthy people.

Synset Examples

the strike was supported by the union rank and file
he rose from the ranks to become a colonel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leadership - the leaders of an organization or group

Etymology

Originally military, referring to soldiers arranged by rank (rows) and file (columns).
