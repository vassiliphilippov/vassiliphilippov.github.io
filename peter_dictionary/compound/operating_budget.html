operating budget

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑpəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒpəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
A budget that covers an organization's ongoing expenses, such as salaries, maintenance, and supplies, as opposed to expenditures for major projects or capital improvements.

Examples

Operating budget for the day schools in the five counties of Dallas, Harris, Bexar, Tarrant and El Paso would be $ 451500, which would be a savings of $ 157460 yearly after the first year's capital outlay of $ 88000 was absorbed, Parkhouse told the Senate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capital budget - a budget for major, long-term investments or improvements
