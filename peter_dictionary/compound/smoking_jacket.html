smoking jacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkɪŋ ˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkɪŋ ˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
A man's comfortable, often velvet jacket with a tie belt, traditionally worn at home for leisure or while smoking.

Examples

The velvet smoking jackets, pearl gray, wine, and blue, which Miss Ada had bought him hung brushed and unworn in the closet.

Etymology

From 'smoking' (referring to the act of smoking) + 'jacket'; term originated in the 19th century when such jackets were worn to absorb smoke and protect clothing.
