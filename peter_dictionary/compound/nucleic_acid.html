nucleic acid

1. NOUN

/ nuˈkliɪk ˈæsɪd, njuˈkliɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / njuːˈkliːɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A complex organic substance present in living cells, consisting of long chains of nucleotides; the principal types are DNA and RNA, which carry genetic information.

Examples

Will advances in human sciences help us build social structures and governments which will enable us to cope with people as effectively as the primitive combination of protein and nucleic acid built a structure of molecules which enabled it to adapt to a sea of molecular interaction?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DNA - deoxyribonucleic acid, type of nucleic acid carrying genetic information
  • Synonym: RNA - ribonucleic acid, type of nucleic acid involved in protein synthesis

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel) and acid, referring to acidic substances found in cell nuclei.
