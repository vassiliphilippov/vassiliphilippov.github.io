gas jet

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs dʒɛt / (AmE), / ɡæs dʒɛt / (BrE)
A device or nozzle from which combustible gas emerges to form a steady flame, especially used in burners or for lighting before electrical illumination was common.

Examples

The heat transfer to the anode in free burning arcs is enhanced by a hot gas jet flowing from the cathode towards the anode with velocities up **f.
The pressure gradient producing the jet is due to the nature of the magnetic field in the arc (rapid decrease of current density from cathode to the anode).
It was exposed to a high velocity gas jet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bunsen burner - laboratory gas burner producing a single open gas flame

Etymology

From 'gas' (from Greek 'khaos' via Dutch) + 'jet' (from Old French 'jet', a throw, a gush).
