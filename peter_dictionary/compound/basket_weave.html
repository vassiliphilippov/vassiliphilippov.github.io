basket weave

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæskɪt wiːv / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt wiːv / (BrE)
A weaving pattern in which two or more threads are interlaced in a way that resembles the weave of a basket, typically used for fabrics and textiles.

Examples

They come in crisp basket weaves in natural honey hues, along with lacy open weaves with a lustre finish in natural, white, black and a whole range of colors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plain weave - basic weaving pattern with single threads alternately interlaced
