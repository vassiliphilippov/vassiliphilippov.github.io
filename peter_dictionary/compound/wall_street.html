Wall Street

1. PROPN

/ wɔl strit / (AmE), / wɔːl striːt / (BrE)
A street in lower Manhattan, New York City, known as the historical location of the New York Stock Exchange and financial institutions; often used as a symbol of the American financial industry.

Examples

It was thickly settled by fifteen thousand citizens and laid out into pig infested streets, mostly around the Battery, going bravely north to Wall Street, but giving up and becoming fields and farms in the region of Harlem Heights.
The Mercers took up residence in Brooklyn, and Mercer found a regular job in Wall Street "misplacing stocks and bonds ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - the management of large amounts of money, especially by governments or large companies
