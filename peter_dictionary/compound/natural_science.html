natural science

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ˈsʌɪəns / (BrE)
A branch of science that studies the physical world and its phenomena, such as physics, chemistry, biology, and earth sciences.

Examples

In the field of the natural sciences, scientifically verified data are quite readily available and any discussion can be shortened with good results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physical science - sciences dealing with non-living systems such as physics and chemistry
  • Antonym: social science - sciences studying human society and relationships

Etymology

From Latin 'natura' (nature) and 'scientia' (knowledge).
