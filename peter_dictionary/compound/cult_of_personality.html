cult of personality

1. NOUN

/ kʌlt əv pɚˌsɑːnəˈlɪɾi / (AmE), / kʌlt əv pəˌsɒnəˈlɪti / (BrE)
a situation in which a public figure, such as a political leader, is deliberately presented to the public as an idealized and heroic figure, often using mass media and propaganda, resulting in intense or unquestioning devotion

Examples

After Gagarin became the Greatest Man in the World, for a nation that does not believe in the cult of personality or in careerism, Moreland wrote me a letter in which he said: "I am not interested in how long a bee can live in a vacuum, or how far it can fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personality cult - excessive admiration for a leader; synonym for cult of personality
