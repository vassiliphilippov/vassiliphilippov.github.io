civic center

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪvɪk ˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪk ˌsɛntə / (BrE)
A public building or complex used for community gatherings, social, or cultural activities in a town or city.

Examples

He skimmed over the millions that went to Meeker Park, Medfield Hospital, the civic center, the Public Health Nursing Association, the library, and so on, pausing when he came to the scholarship fund.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community center - a place for community members to gather for activities and events

Etymology

From 'civic' (relating to a city or town) + 'center.'
