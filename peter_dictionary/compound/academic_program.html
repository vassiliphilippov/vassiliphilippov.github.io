academic program

1. NOUN

/ əˌkædəˈmɪk ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / əˌkadəˈmɪk ˈprəʊɡram / (BrE)
A course of study offered by a school, college, or university, typically in the liberal arts or sciences and leading towards a degree or qualification.

Examples

First and foremost, vocational courses should not replace courses which are essential parts of the required academic program for graduation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curriculum - the subjects comprising a course of study in a school or college
