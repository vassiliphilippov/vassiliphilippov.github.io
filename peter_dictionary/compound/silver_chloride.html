silver chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪlvər ˈklɔːrˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪlvə ˈklɔːrˌaɪd / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound (AgCl) used chiefly in photographic emulsions and in certain chemical processes.

Examples

To prepare the latter, silver chloride was precipitated from a solution containing * * f obtained from the Oak Ridge National Laboratory.
The silver chloride was fused under vacuum in the presence of aluminum chips with the resultant product of * * f which was sublimed into a flask on the vacuum line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver bromide - a silver halide used in photographic materials

Etymology

Formed from silver + chloride, indicating its composition: silver (Ag) and chlorine (Cl).
