prose poem

1. NOUN

/ proʊz poʊəm / (AmE), / prəʊz pəʊəm / (BrE)
A piece of writing in prose that has the concentrated, imaginative qualities or heightened emotional effects of poetry.

Examples

This is brought out in the next to last chapter of the book, "A Hero's Funeral ", written in the form of an impassioned prose poem.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: poem - a piece of writing that expresses ideas or emotions in a style that often involves rhythm and imagery; typically written in verse
  • Degree Lower: prose - written or spoken language in its ordinary form, without metrical structure
