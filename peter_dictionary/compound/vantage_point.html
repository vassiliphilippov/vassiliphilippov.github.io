vantage point

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæn.tɪdʒ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A position or place that provides a strategic or advantageous view of something.

Examples

The sniper, whether psychopathic marksman or murderer by intent, would hardly have walked to his vantage point with rifle over shoulder, whistling a marching tune.

Synset Examples

from that vantage point he could survey the whole valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viewpoint - a particular attitude or way of considering a matter

Etymology

From 'vantage', meaning advantage (Middle English, from Old French 'avantage'), plus 'point'.
