alizarin crimson

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzərɪn ˈkrɪmzən / (AmE), / əˈlɪzərɪn ˈkrɪmzən / (BrE)
a deep red pigment or dye derived from alizarin, used especially in painting and to dye fabrics.

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - a red dye originally obtained from madder root

Etymology

From 'alizarin' (a red dye originally made from madder root, from Arabic 'al-‘uṣāra', juice) and 'crimson' (from Old Spanish 'cremesin', from Arabic 'qirmiz', kermes).
