winding-clothes

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪndɪŋ ˌkloʊðz / (AmE), / ˈwaɪndɪŋ ˌkləʊðz / (BrE)
Clothes or a sheet in which a dead body is wrapped for burial; a burial shroud.

Examples

They took Jesus's body, then, and wrapped it in winding-clothes with the spices; that is how the Jews prepare a body for burial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shroud - a cloth used to wrap a body for burial

Etymology

From 'winding' (to wrap) + 'clothes', referring to cloth wound around a body.
