trisodium orthophosphate

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌsoʊdiəm ˌɔrθoʊˈfɑsfeɪt / (AmE), / traɪˌsəʊdiəm ˌɔːθəʊˈfɑːsfeɪt / (BrE)
A compound (Na3PO4) used as a cleaning agent and water softener in soaps and detergents.

Examples

In addition, many of the hard surface cleaners used for walls and woodwork had their genesis in trisodium orthophosphate, which is still the major ingredient of a number of such products.

Etymology

Formed from 'tri-' (three) + 'sodium' + 'orthophosphate' (from Greek 'ortho-' meaning 'straight' and 'phosphate').
