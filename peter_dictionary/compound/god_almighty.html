God Almighty

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑd ɔlˈmaɪti / (AmE), / ɡɒd ɔlˈmaɪti / (BrE)
A title for the Judeo-Christian God, denoting supreme power and authority.

Examples

Would you be willing to swear before a judge and jury, having placed your hand on the Good Book and taken an oath before Almighty God himself?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: God - the creator and ruler of the universe in monotheistic religions
  • Synonym: the Almighty - another title for God signifying omnipotence

Etymology

Derived from 'God', from Old English 'God', and 'Almighty', from Old English 'ælmīhtig' (all-powerful).
