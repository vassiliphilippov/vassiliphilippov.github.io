womb-to-tomb

1. ADJ

/ wuːm tə tuːm / (AmE), / wuːm tə tuːm / (BrE)
Extending or lasting throughout an entire lifetime, from birth to death.

Examples

Why, in the first place, call himself a liberal if he is against laissez-faire and favors an authoritarian central government with womb-to-tomb controls over everybody?

Synset Examples

a lifelong friend
from lifelong habit
his lifelong study of Greek art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifelong - Lasting or continuing through all or most of a person's life

Etymology

Formed from 'womb' (the organ where a baby develops before birth) and 'tomb' (a place where a dead body is buried), expressing the span from birth to death.
