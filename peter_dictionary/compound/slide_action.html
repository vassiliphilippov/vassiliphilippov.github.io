slide action

1. NOUN

/ slaɪd ˈækʃən / (AmE), / slaɪd ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a firearm mechanism in which a sliding lever is moved back and forth to eject the spent shell, cock the weapon, and load a new cartridge

Examples

The American firearms and ammunition manufacturers through diligent research and technical development have replaced the muzzle loader and slow firing single shot arms with modern fast firing autoloaders, extremely accurate bolt, lever, and slide action firearms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pump action - mechanism in a firearm operated by a sliding fore-end (synonym)
