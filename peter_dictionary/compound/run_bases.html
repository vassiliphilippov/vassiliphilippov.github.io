run bases

1. VERB

/ rʌn ˈbeɪsɪz / (AmE), / rʌn ˈbeɪsɪz / (BrE)
(in baseball) to advance around all the bases on the field after batting, with the aim of scoring a run.

Examples

Or even the way you run bases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal a base - to advance to the next base while the pitcher is delivering the ball, without a hit
