submachine gun

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.məˈʃin ˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.məˈʃiːn ˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a lightweight, portable automatic firearm that typically uses pistol ammunition, capable of rapid fire

Examples

Even if it did not, how would this little world of gentle people cope with its new reality of grenades and submachine guns?
He walked up to the lieutenant's office, leaned wearily against the gun rack that housed four rifles and a gas gun nobody remembered having used and a submachine gun that was occasionally tried out on the Academy Range.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: machine gun - fully automatic firearm designed for sustained rapid fire

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (meaning 'under, below') and 'machine gun', first used in early 20th century military terminology.
