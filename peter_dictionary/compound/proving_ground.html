proving ground

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruvɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpruːvɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place or environment where new equipment, methods, or ideas are tested and evaluated

Examples

So escalation proceeds, ad infinitum or, more accurately, until the contestants begin dropping them on each other instead of on their respective proving grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test site - site where experiments or tests are conducted
  • Synonym: laboratory - a place equipped for scientific experiments and research

Etymology

From 'prove' (test) + 'ground' (place, area); originally used for military testing grounds.
