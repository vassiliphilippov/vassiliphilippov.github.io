test suit

1. NOUN

/ tɛst suːt / (AmE), / tɛst suːt / (BrE)
A legal action brought to establish a precedent, typically to clarify a law's validity or application.

Examples

The bond issue will go to the state courts for a friendly test suit to test the validity of the act, and then the sales will begin and contracts let for repair work on some of Georgia's most heavily traveled highways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test case - a legal case serving as a precedent
