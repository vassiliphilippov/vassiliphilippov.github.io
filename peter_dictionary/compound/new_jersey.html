New Jersey

1. PROPN

/ nu dʒɝːzi / (AmE), / njuː ˈdʒɜːzi / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, located on the Atlantic coast; one of the original thirteen colonies.

Examples

South of Laurel Grove Cemetery, and below the junction of the Neversink and the Delaware, was the Tri-State Rock, from which Stevie could spy New Jersey and Pennsylvania, as well as New York, simply by spinning around on his heel.
The morning hawk, hungry for any eatable, killable, digestible item, kept his eyes on the ring of anchored ships that lay off the shores in the bay, sheltered by the Jersey inlets.

Etymology

Named after the island of Jersey in the English Channel.

A state in the northeastern United States, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean; one of the original thirteen colonies.

Examples

Don Barzini, I want to thank you for helping me organise this meeting, and the other heads of the five families from New York and New Jersey.
