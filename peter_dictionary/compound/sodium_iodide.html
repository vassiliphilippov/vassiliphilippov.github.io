sodium iodide

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊdiəm ˈaɪəˌdaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈaɪədaɪd / (BrE)
A white, crystalline, water-soluble salt (NaI) used in medicine and chemistry, especially in photographic emulsions and as a source of iodine.

Examples

Following reaction the cells were scratched with a file and opened under a 20 % aqueous sodium iodide solution.
Carrier * * f was added and the aqueous and organic phases were separated (cells containing gaseous reactants were immersed in liquid air before opening under sodium iodide).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium iodide - a similar salt, potassium compound with iodide ion

Etymology

Formed from 'sodium', from English naming of the element, and 'iodide', from iodine, which is from Greek 'ioeides' (violet-like) due to its color.
