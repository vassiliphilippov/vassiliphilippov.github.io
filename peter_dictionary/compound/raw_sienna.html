raw sienna

1. NOUN

/ rɔ siˈɛnə / (AmE), / rɔː siˈenə / (BrE)
a yellowish-brown pigment or color, typically a medium to dark tan, used in painting

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: burnt sienna - a reddish-brown pigment or color produced by heating raw sienna

Etymology

From Italian 'terra di Siena' (earth of Siena), named after Siena, Italy, where the pigment was originally sourced.
