agglutinating activity

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡluːtəˈneɪtɪŋ ækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / əˌɡluːtəˈneɪtɪŋ ækˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the process by which small suspended particles clump together to form larger masses, often resulting in precipitation, especially in a biological or chemical context

Examples

Not all these regions exhibited equal agglutinating activity, as evidenced by titer and the extent of the active areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agglutination - the process or result of particles clumping together, especially in a biological context

Etymology

From 'agglutinate', from Latin 'agglutinatus' (glued to) + 'activity'.
