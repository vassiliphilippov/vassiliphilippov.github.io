sweet shrub

1. NOUN

/ swit ʃrʌb / (AmE), / swiːt ʃrʌb / (BrE)
A hardy shrub native to the southeastern United States, known for its clove-scented wood and fragrant reddish-brown flowers.

Examples

In the center of any open space remaining our grandfathers had planted syringa and sweet shrub, snowball, rose-of-Sharon and balm-of-Gilead.
