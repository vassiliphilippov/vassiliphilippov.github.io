back door

1. NOUN

/ bæk dɔr / (AmE), / bæk dɔː / (BrE)
a secret or indirect way of gaining access to a place, position, or opportunity, especially by circumventing normal procedures or rules

Examples

No one can deny that these "back door "admissions to membership provisions have been seriously abused nor that they have not resulted in the admission of downright incompetents to membership in supposedly learned societies.

Synset Examples

he got his job through the back door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loophole - a means of avoiding a rule without directly breaking it

2. NOUN

/ bæk dɔr / (AmE), / bak dɔː / (BrE)
a door or entrance located at the rear of a building

Examples

The cops caught him sneaking TV sets out the back door of a JC Penney.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front door - the principal entrance at the front of a building
