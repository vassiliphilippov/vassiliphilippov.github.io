gospel truth

1. NOUN

/ ˈgɑspəl truθ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspəl truːθ / (BrE)
A statement or fact that is accepted as completely true, or not to be doubted.

Examples

God's honest truth.

Synset Examples

his word was gospel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • Synonym: axiom - a self-evident or accepted truth
  • Antonym: falsehood - a lie or untrue statement

Etymology

From 'gospel' meaning 'good news' in Old English *gōdspel*, traditionally referring to the teachings of the Christian gospels, denoting truth and authority.
