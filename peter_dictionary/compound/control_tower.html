control tower

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtroʊl ˌtaʊər / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl ˌtaʊə / (BrE)
A tall building at an airport from which air traffic controllers manage the movement of aircraft on the ground and in the airspace near the airport.

Examples

The control tower gave him immediate take-off permission, and the clean roar of the engine that took him off the rough strip spoke well of the skill of Donovan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air traffic control - the activity or service of managing aircraft movements, especially at airports
