losing streak

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːzɪŋ strik / (AmE), / ˈluːzɪŋ striːk / (BrE)
A period during which a person or team experiences a series of consecutive losses, especially in sports or games.

Examples

If the Orioles are to break their losing streak within the next two days, it will have to be at the expense of the American League champion New York Yankees, who come in here tomorrow for a night game and a single test Sunday afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winning streak - a series of consecutive wins
