epic poem

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪk ˈpoʊəm / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪk ˈpəʊəm / (BrE)
A long narrative poem, typically detailing the heroic deeds and adventures of legendary or historical figures.

Examples

The epic poems, the consolidation of the Greek pantheon, the rise of firm political units, the self-awareness which could permit painted and sculptured representations of men - all these had to await the progress of following decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epic - relating to or characteristic of an epic poem
  • Degree Lower: ballad - a narrative poem, often shorter and focused on folk tales or legends
  • Degree Higher: saga - a long story of heroic achievement, sometimes prose but often compared to epics

Etymology

From 'epic', from Latin 'epicus', from Greek 'epikos' (word, song, poem), and 'poem' from Greek 'poiēma' (something made, poem).
