sweet orange

1. NOUN

/ swit ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A variety of orange (Citrus sinensis) with sweet, juicy pulp, commonly eaten fresh and widely cultivated worldwide.

Examples

Still existing on a "Northern Union "telegraph form is a typical peremptory message from Peru grocer J. J. Hapgood to Burton and Graves' store in Manchester - "Get and send by stage sure four pounds best Porterhouse or sirloin steak, for Mrs. Hapgood send six sweet oranges ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bitter orange - a sour or bitter variety of orange (Citrus aurantium)
  • Synonym: orange - the fruit of any citrus tree of the genus Citrus, especially Citrus sinensis

Etymology

‘Orange’ from Old French ‘orenge’, from Arabic ‘nāranj’, from Persian ‘nārang’; ‘sweet’ as opposed to bitter.
