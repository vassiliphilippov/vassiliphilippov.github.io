in any case

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈɛni keɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ˈeni keɪs / (BrE)
used to indicate that something remains true or relevant despite other circumstances or regardless of what has just been mentioned

Examples

In any case, I have always been treated with the utmost courtesy by Englishmen, even in Devonshire and Cornwall, where anti Catholic feeling has supposedly existed the strongest and longest.
In any case, though they had been promised immortality if they ate of the tree, they obtained mortality instead.
However, readers who accept Freud's findings and believe that he has solved completely the mystery of dreams, should ponder over the following words in his Interpretation Of Dreams, Chapter 1,: "as a matter of fact no such complete solution of the dream has ever been accomplished in any case, and what is more, every one attempting such solution has found that in most cases there have remained a great many components of the dream the source of which he has been unable to explain, nor is the discussion closed on the subject of the mantic or prophetic power of dreams ".
"Well, there's time, in any case.
And in any case, answers may clarify but they do not change anything.

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyhow - used to indicate that a statement supports or explains a previous one or is true regardless
  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement regardless of other things
  • Synonym: at any rate - used to add a statement that reduces the effect of what has just been said

2. ADV

/ ɪn ˈɛni keɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ˈeni keɪs / (BrE)
used to add another reason or to say that something remains true despite other factors; anyway; regardless

Examples

But anyway...

Synset Examples

I don't want to go to a restaurant; besides, we can't afford it
she couldn't shelter behind him all the time and in any case he wasn't always with her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a point despite other considerations
  • Synonym: besides - used to make an additional point; moreover
