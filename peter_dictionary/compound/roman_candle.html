Roman candle

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmən ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmən ˈkændl / (BrE)
A cylindrical firework that projects a series of colored balls of fire one after another.

Examples

Ludie had a cigar box full of marbles and shooters, and a Roman candle from last Fourth of July.

Etymology

From the name of the city Rome; origin refers to the style of early fireworks used in Italy.
