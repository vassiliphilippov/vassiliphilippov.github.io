world council

1. NOUN

/ wɝːld ˈkaʊn.səl / (AmE), / wɜːld ˈkaʊn.səl / (BrE)
an international council composed of representatives from different nations, typically convened to discuss global issues or coordinate international policies

Examples

Constitutional government, popular vote, trial by jury, public education, labor unions, cooperatives, communes, socialized ownership, world courts, and the veto power in world councils are but a few examples.
