livery stable

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvəri ˌsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri ˌsteɪbəl / (BrE)
A place where horses and vehicles are kept for hire.

Examples

When several minutes had passed and Curt had n't emerged from the livery stable, Brenner re-entered the hotel and faced Summers across the counter.
Curt's visit to the livery stable had been merely a precaution in case anyone should be watching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stable - building where animals, especially horses, are kept

Etymology

From 'livery' (provision of horses for hire) and 'stable' (building for horses).
