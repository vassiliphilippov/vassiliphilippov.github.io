High Anglicanism

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈæŋɡlɪˌkænɪzəm / (AmE), / haɪ ˈæŋɡlɪˌkænɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A doctrine and practice within the Church of England that emphasizes the Catholic tradition, especially in ritual and theology.

Examples

A century ago, Newman saw that liberalism (what we now might call secularism) would gradually but definitely make its mark on English Protestantism, and that even high Anglicanism would someday no longer be a "serviceable breakwater against doctrinal errors more fundamental than its own ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Low Church - Anglican faction emphasizing Protestant and evangelical elements
  • Degree Lower: Broad Church - Anglican group emphasizing a middle path between High and Low Church practices
  • Synonym: Anglo-Catholicism - Doctrine within Anglicanism emphasizing Catholic tradition and liturgy

Etymology

From 'High', denoting ritualistic practices, and 'Anglicanism', derived from Medieval Latin 'Anglicanus' (English).
