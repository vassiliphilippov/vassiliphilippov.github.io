cobalt blue

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌbɔlt bluː / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌbɒlt bluː / (BrE)
a deep vivid blue colour, especially as made from a cobalt-based pigment

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerulean blue - a bright blue pigment used in painting
  • Degree Lower: ultramarine - a deep blue pigment and color

Etymology

From cobalt, reflecting the pigment's origin from compounds of the element cobalt.
