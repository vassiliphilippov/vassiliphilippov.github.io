absentee ballot

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.sənˈti ˈbæl.ət / (AmE), / ˌæb.sənˈti ˈbæl.ət / (BrE)
A ballot sent in by a voter who is unable to be present at their designated polling station on election day, often by mail.

Examples

I came home to vote in the presidential election because they wouldn'’t give me an absentee ballot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mail-in ballot - a ballot that is cast by mail rather than in person, often synonymous in US context
