snare drum

1. NOUN

/ snɛr ˈdrʌm / (AmE), / snɛə ˈdrʌm / (BrE)
A small cylindrical drum with a set of wires (snares) stretched across the bottom head, used in orchestras, bands, and drum kits to produce a sharp, crisp sound.

Examples

Not one of them born... whose asshole wouldn't pucker up tighter than a snare drum when you asked them for funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side drum - another term for snare drum
