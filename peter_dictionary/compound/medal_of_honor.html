Medal of Honor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəl əv ˈɑnɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəl əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
the highest military decoration awarded by the United States government for acts of remarkable bravery and valor in action

Examples

Adam stared at the door and remembered that Simms Purdew had been awarded the Medal of Honor for gallantry at Antietam.
