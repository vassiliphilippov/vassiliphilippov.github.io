semiminor axis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmɪˈmaɪnɚ ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmaɪnə ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
Half of the minor axis of an ellipse, measured from the center to the edge along the shortest diameter.

Examples

The Poynting-Robertson effect causes the semi major axis of orbits to diminish more rapidly than the semi-minor axis, with a consequent tendency toward circular orbits as the particles move toward the sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: major axis - the longest diameter of an ellipse
  • Antonym: semimajor axis - half the major axis of an ellipse

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half') and minor axis (the shorter diameter of an ellipse).
