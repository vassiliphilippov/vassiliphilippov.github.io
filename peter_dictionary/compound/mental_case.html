mental case

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl keɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl keɪs / (BrE)
An informal or outdated term for a person suffering from a mental disorder, especially neurosis. Now often considered inappropriate or offensive.

Examples

Sherlock Holmes is not merely an individualist; he is very close to being a mental case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotic - a person affected by neurosis
