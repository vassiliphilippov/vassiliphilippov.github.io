modal verb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.dəl vɜrb / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dəl vɜːb / (BrE)
an auxiliary verb, such as 'can', 'may', or 'will', used with another verb to express possibility, necessity, obligation, or permission

Examples

REPORTER: Can you give us your reaction to the High Court of Israel'’s ruling?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used in forming tenses, moods, and voices of other verbs

Etymology

From 'modal', relating to mode, and 'verb', from Latin 'verbum' (word).
