Judgement Day

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒmənt deɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒm(ə)nt deɪ / (BrE)
(in Christianity and some other religions) the day at the end of the world when God judges all humans according to their deeds, deciding their fate for eternity.

Examples

Thoroughly modern in treatment, they are at the same time, full of simple sincerity which invariably characterizes genuine Negro folk-music and are by no means to be confused with the average' Broadway Spirituals' which depend for their racial flavor upon sundry allusions to the' Amen Corner',' judgement day',' Gabriel's horn', and a frustrated devil - with a few random' Hallelujahs' thrown in for good measure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Last Judgment - the final judgement of all humans by God at the end of the world

Etymology

From 'judgement' (decision, verdict) + 'day', referring to the day of God's final judgement in Christian theology.
