Red China

1. PROPN

/ rɛd ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / rɛd ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
A historical term referring to the People's Republic of China, especially emphasizing its communist government.

Examples

Morrison points out that since our country is more urbanized than the Soviet Union or Red China, it is the most vulnerable of the great powers - Europe of course must be written off out of hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: People's Republic of China - the official name for the country of China since 1949

Etymology

So named to distinguish communist-controlled mainland China (red for communism) after 1949.
