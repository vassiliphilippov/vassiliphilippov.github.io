good faith

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd feɪθ / (AmE), / ɡʊd feɪθ / (BrE)
honest or sincere intention; the state of acting with honesty and without deception or fraud

Examples

His claim was therefore "so highly exaggerated ", the Department concluded, that it "cast doubt upon his veracity and, consequently, upon his sincerity and good faith ".

Synset Examples

he acted in good faith
doubt was expressed as to the good faith of the immigrants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad faith - intent to deceive or act dishonestly
