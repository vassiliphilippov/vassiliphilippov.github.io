speed of light

1. NOUN

/ spiːd əv laɪt / (AmE), / spiːd əv laɪt / (BrE)
the constant speed at which light travels in a vacuum, exactly 299,792,458 meters per second

Examples

The Gabriel pointed towards her destination and, under one gee acceleration, began to build up towards her ultimate velocity, 99.1 percent of the speed of light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: c - symbol for the speed of light in physics
