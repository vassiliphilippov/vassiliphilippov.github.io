spectator pump

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈteɪtɚ pʌmp / (AmE), / spɛkˈteɪtə pʌmp / (BrE)
A women's shoe with a medium heel, typically featuring contrasting colors on the toe and heel.

Examples

Spectators in white crush textures dip toe and heel in smooth black, navy and taffy tan.

Etymology

Named for its distinctive appearance, thought suitable for social or sporting events where one is a spectator.

2. NOUN

/ spɛkˈteɪtɚ pʌmp / (AmE), / spɛkˈteɪtə pʌmp / (BrE)
A woman's dress shoe with a medium heel, typically featuring contrasting colors on the toe and heel, often used for formal or semi-formal wear.

Examples

So you sit in the car and listen to the air run out and listen to the rain and see the mud in front of the headlights, waiting for you, for your new spectator pumps, waiting for you to squat by yourself out there in your tight skirt, crying and afraid and trying to get that damned son-of-a-bitch tire off, because that is being an old maid too, if you happen to drive a car, it is changing the tire yourself in the night, and in the mud and the rain, hating to get out in it but afraid to stay and afraid to try to walk out for help.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pump - a woman's plain, heeled shoe, usually without a fastening

Etymology

From 'spectator' (suggesting being seen or on show) and 'pump' (a type of low-heeled shoe); popularized in the early 20th century for spectator sports and events.
