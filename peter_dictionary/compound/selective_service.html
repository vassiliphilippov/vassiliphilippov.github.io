Selective Service

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktɪv ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
The system in the United States through which men are registered for possible compulsory military service; also refers to the federal agency that administers this process.

Examples

As was said in Gonzales, "it is the Appeal Board which renders the selective service determination considered' final' in the courts, not to be overturned unless there is no basis in fact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscription - the compulsory enlistment of people into military service
  • Synonym: draft - the system of ordering people into military service
