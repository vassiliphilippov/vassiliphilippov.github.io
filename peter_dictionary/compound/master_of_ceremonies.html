master of ceremonies

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚ əv ˈsɛrəˌmoʊniz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə əv ˈsɛrɪˌməʊniz / (BrE)
a person who acts as host at a formal event, introducing speakers and keeping the proceedings orderly

Examples

Furthermore, Garry Moore makes an ideal master of ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emcee - person who acts as host at an event

Etymology

From 'master' (person in charge) + 'ceremonies' (formal events or rituals)
