black-market

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæk ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
Sold or distributed illegally and without government approval, especially to avoid taxes or regulations.

Examples

They passed the marmalade, the bread, the black-market butter, back and forth.

Synset Examples

the black economy pays no taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - not allowed by law
  • Synonym: contraband - goods that are imported or exported illegally

Etymology

Formed from 'black' (here meaning illicit) + 'market'; the use of 'black' to imply illegality dates to the 18th century.
