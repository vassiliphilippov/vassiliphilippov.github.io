prisoner of war

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzənər əv wɔr / (AmE), / ˈprɪzənə əv wɔː / (BrE)
a person, typically a soldier, who is captured and held by an enemy during a war

Examples

Sixty thousand Jewish prisoners of war disappeared into Lublin's web.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: POW - abbreviation for prisoner of war
