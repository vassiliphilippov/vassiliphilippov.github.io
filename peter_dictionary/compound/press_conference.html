press conference

1. NOUN

/ prɛs ˈkɑːn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / prɛs ˈkɒn.fər.əns / (BrE)
an event at which reporters are invited to ask questions of a politician, celebrity, or organization representative, usually for news coverage

Examples

He did not want to bring the Andruses to the station house too early - Rheinholdt had summoned a press conference, and he did n't want them subjected to the reporters again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news conference - an event held to provide information to the news media and allow questions

Etymology

Formed from 'press' (news media) and 'conference' (formal meeting).

2. NOUN

/ prɛs ˈkɑːn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / prɛs ˈkɒn.fər.əns / (BrE)
an event where journalists are invited to ask questions of a public figure, such as a politician or celebrity, and receive official information

Examples

- Aren't you covering the press conference?
