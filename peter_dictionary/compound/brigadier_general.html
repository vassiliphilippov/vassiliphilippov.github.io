brigadier general

1. NOUN

/ brɪˌɡeɪdiːɚ ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / brɪˌɡeɪdiːə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
A military officer of high rank, ranking immediately below a major general.

Examples

During the Civil War, Custer, who achieved a brilliant record, was made brigadier general at the age of 23.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: major general - a general officer ranking above a brigadier general and below a lieutenant general
  • Degree Lower: colonel - a commissioned officer rank below brigadier general
