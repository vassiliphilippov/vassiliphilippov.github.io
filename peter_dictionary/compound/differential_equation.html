differential equation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃl ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
An equation that involves derivatives of a function, representing how the function changes and often used to model physical phenomena.
