water development

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər dɪˌvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə dɪˌvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
The process of making an area of water or water resources more useful, such as by construction, management, or improvement for purposes like irrigation, supply, or flood control.

Examples

Rep. James Cotten of Weatherford insisted that a water development bill passed by the Texas House of Representatives was an effort by big cities like Dallas and Fort Worth to cover up places like Paradise, a Wise County hamlet of 250 people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water management - the process of controlling and moving water resources
