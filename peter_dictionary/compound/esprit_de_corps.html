esprit de corps

1. NOUN

/ eˈspri də ˈkɔr / (AmE), / eˈspri də ˈkɔː / (BrE)
A feeling of pride, loyalty, and togetherness shared by members of a group, motivating them to work towards common goals and group success.

Examples

They have little "esprit de corps ".
Today a battle cry may seem an anachronism, for in the modern Army, esprit de corps has been sacrificed to organizational charts and tables.
One night on the Naktong River, Mel Chandler called on that fabled esprit de corps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morale - confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a group

Etymology

French, literally ‘spirit of the body (group)’
