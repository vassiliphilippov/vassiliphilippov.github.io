inferiority complex

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfɪriˈɔrəti kəmˈplɛks / (AmE), / ɪnˌfɪəriˈɒrəti kəmˈplɛks / (BrE)
A persistent feeling of being less worthy or capable than others, often leading to low self-esteem and insecurity.

Examples

One of the roughest was the TV quiz shows, which gave him inferiority complexes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superiority complex - an attitude of superiority that conceals actual feelings of inferiority

Etymology

Formed from 'inferiority' (from Latin 'inferior' meaning 'lower') and 'complex' (from Latin 'complexus', meaning 'composed of parts'). Term popularized by psychologist Alfred Adler in the early 20th century.
