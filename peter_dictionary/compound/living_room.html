living room

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ ruːm / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ ruːm / (BrE)
A room in a house used for relaxing, entertaining guests, and general family activities.

Examples

He was able, now, to sit for hours in a chair in the living room and stare out at the bleak yard without moving.
When Joe identified himself, he nodded, unsmiling, and ushered him into a sedate living room.
Wo n't you step into the living room, where it's cozier "?
She went into the living room and turned on three lamps, then back into the kitchen where she turned on the ceiling light and the switch that lit the floods on the barn, illuminating the driveway.
The women, keeping their voices low as they worked around the house or sat in the living room, sounded like chickens shut up in a coop for the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sitting room - a room in a house for sitting and socializing; parlour
  • Synonym: parlour - a room in a private house for receiving guests
  • Synonym: drawing room - a formal living room, especially in a large house

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪŋ ruːm / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪŋ ruːm / (BrE)
A room in a house where people sit, relax, and entertain guests.

Examples

Living room, dining room, arsenal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sitting room - room for sitting and socializing (synonym)
  • Synonym: drawing room - room in a home for entertaining (British synonym)
  • Synonym: parlor - old-fashioned term for living room
