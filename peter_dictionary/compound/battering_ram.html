battering ram

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ ræm / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ər.ɪŋ ræm / (BrE)
A heavy beam used to break open doors or walls, especially in siege warfare.

Examples

First came the cannon fodder, white clad civilians being driven into death as a massive human battering ram.

Etymology

From Middle English 'ram' (male sheep), used metaphorically from the animal's headbutting; 'batter' from Old French 'batre' (to beat) and Latin 'battuere'.
