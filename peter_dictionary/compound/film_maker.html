film maker

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlm ˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlm ˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
A person who produces or directs motion pictures; a movie producer or director.

Examples

In about seven years Griffith either invented or first realized the possibilities of virtually every resource at the disposal of the film maker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - person who supervises the making of a film
  • Synonym: producer - person responsible for overseeing film production

Etymology

From 'film' (motion picture) + 'maker' (person who creates).
