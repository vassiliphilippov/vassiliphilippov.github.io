town house

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊn ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtaʊn ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house that is one of a row of similar houses joined by shared side walls and typically located in a city or town.

Examples

He had assumed that all these buildings had been divided into apartments, but this one, from a glance at the hall furnishings, was obviously still a functioning town house, and its owners were in residence; that made it doubtful as the hiding place of a man whose plans had to be made in advance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: row house - A house in a row of similar ones joined by common sidewalls
  • Degree Lower: semi-detached house - A house joined to another on one side only
