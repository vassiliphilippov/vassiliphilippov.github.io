thermodynamics of equilibrium

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɝː.moʊ.daɪˈnæm.ɪks ʌv ˌiː.kwɪˈlɪb.ri.əm / (AmE), / ˌθɜː.məʊ.daɪˈnæm.ɪks ɒv ˌiː.kwɪˈlɪb.ri.əm / (BrE)
the branch of thermodynamics that studies systems in thermal equilibrium, also known as thermostatics

Examples

In the above development we have applied the thermodynamics of equilibrium (referred to by some as thermostatics) to the steady state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermostatics - the study of thermodynamic systems in equilibrium
  • Degree Higher: thermodynamics - the science of the relations between heat, work, temperature, and energy

Etymology

thermodynamics: from Greek thermē (heat) + dynamis (power); equilibrium: from Latin aequilibrium (equal balance)
