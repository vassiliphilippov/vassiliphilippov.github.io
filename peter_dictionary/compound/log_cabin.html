log cabin

1. NOUN

/ lɔg ˈkæb.ɪn / (AmE), / lɒg ˈkæb.ɪn / (BrE)
A small, simple house or shelter built from logs, typically found in rural or wilderness areas.

Examples

Beyond that misty gray of the rain, he saw the stretching hutment, low diminutive log cabins, chinked with mud, with doorways a man would have to crouch to get through, with roofs of tenting laid over boughs or boards from hardtack boxes, or fence rails, with cranky chimneys of sticks and dried mud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabin - a small, simple house or shelter

Etymology

From 'log' (tree trunk) and 'cabin' (small shelter); the term dates to early American frontier days.
