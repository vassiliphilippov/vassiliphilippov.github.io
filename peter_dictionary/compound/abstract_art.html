abstract art

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbˌstrækt ɑrt / (AmE), / ˈæb.strækt ɑːt / (BrE)
Art that does not attempt to represent reality accurately but uses shapes, colors, forms, and gestural marks to achieve its effect.

Examples

Cubism, in its 1911 - 1912 phase (which the French, with justice, call "hermetic ") was on the verge of abstract art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: representation art - art that depicts recognizable subjects

Etymology

From 'abstract' (from Latin 'abstractus', drawn away) + 'art'. The term came into use in the early 20th century.
