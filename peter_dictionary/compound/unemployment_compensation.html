unemployment compensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.ɪmˈplɔɪ.mənt ˌkɑːm.pənˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪmˈplɔɪ.mənt ˌkɒm.pənˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a payment made by a government agency to unemployed individuals who meet certain eligibility requirements, typically provided to support them while they are seeking new employment

Examples

This probably would require some support for subsistence as well as for tuition, but the total would be no greater than for the proposals of unemployment compensation or a Youth Conservation Corps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unemployment benefit - money paid by the government to people who are unemployed
