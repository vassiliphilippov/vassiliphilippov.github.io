camera lens

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmərə lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkæmərə lɛnz / (BrE)
the lens in a camera that focuses light to form an image on the film or sensor

Examples

The fast opening of the shutter consists of a piece of aluminum foil (approximately * * f) placed directly in front of the camera lens so that no light may pass into the camera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lens - a piece of glass or other transparent material with curved sides for concentrating or dispersing light rays
