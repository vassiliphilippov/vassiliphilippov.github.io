rock and roll

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑk ənd ˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌrɒk ən ˈrəʊl / (BrE)
A style of popular music that originated in the 1950s, combining elements of rhythm and blues with country and western.

Examples

Mr. Rock'n'roll.

Synset Examples

rock is a generic term for the range of styles that evolved out of rock'n'roll.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rock music - Popular music genre that evolved from rock and roll

Etymology

From 'rock', a style of rhythmic music, and 'roll', referencing movement, both used in African-American music slang; the phrase popularized in the 1940s and 1950s.
