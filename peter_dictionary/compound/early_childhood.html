early childhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrli ˈtʃaɪldˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈɜːli ˈtʃaɪldhʊd / (BrE)
The period in a person's life from about the end of infancy to the beginning of school years, typically regarded as ages two to five.

Examples

The Maturity Chart for each sex demonstrates clearly that Onset is a phenomenon of infancy and early childhood whereas Completion is a phenomenon of the later portion of adolescence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: infancy - the earliest stage of childhood, typically the first year of life
  • Degree Higher: childhood - the state or period of being a child
