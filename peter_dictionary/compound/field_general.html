field general

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ˌdʒɛnɚəl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
A quarterback; the player who directs the offensive play of a football team.

Examples

Rabb, the former Louisiana State field general, came off the bench for his debut with the Bills Sunday and directed his new team to a 22 - 12 upset victory over the Houston Oilers, defending league champions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarterback - player who directs the offensive play in football

Etymology

From 'field' (sports playing area) and 'general' (military leader), referring to tactical leadership on the field.
