light year

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪt ˌjɪr / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌjɪə / (BrE)
A unit of distance in astronomy equal to the distance light travels in one year in a vacuum, approximately 5.88 trillion miles or 9.46 trillion kilometers.

Examples

I can n't tell you now the distance in light years or what star this was, though you can see it with the naked eye at night in this hemisphere.

Etymology

Formed from 'light' (as in light waves) and 'year' (time), first used in the mid-19th century in astronomical contexts.
