inner city

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɚ ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈɪnə ˈsɪti / (BrE)
the central part of a city, often characterized by older buildings, dense population, and social or economic challenges

Examples

The breakdown of social homogeneity in inner city areas and the spread of inner city blight account for the decline of central city churches.
