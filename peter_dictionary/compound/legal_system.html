legal system

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɡəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The set of laws and procedures, along with the institutions and people who interpret and enforce them, that govern how justice is administered in a particular country or community.

Examples

In the first place the new doctrine brought a formal separation of international from municipal law, rejecting the earlier view that both were parts of a universal legal system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justice system - the overall system for administering justice in a society
  • Degree Lower: criminal justice system - the system dealing specifically with crime and legal punishment
