antitrust case

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈtrʌst keɪs / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈtrʌst keɪs / (BrE)
A legal action taken against individuals or organizations accused of restricting free competition in the marketplace, typically in violation of antitrust laws.

Examples

No previous antitrust case, he said, had involved interests of such magnitude or presented such complex problems of relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antitrust suit - A lawsuit filed to challenge anti-competitive practices under antitrust law

Etymology

From 'antitrust' (from anti- + trust, referring to legislation against business monopolies) and 'case' (from Latin 'casus', event or occurrence).
