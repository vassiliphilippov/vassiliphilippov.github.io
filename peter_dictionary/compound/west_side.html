west side

1. NOUN

/ wɛst saɪd / (AmE), / wɛst saɪd / (BrE)
the side of something that faces or is towards the west

Examples

My great grandmother, I have been told, made her garden her great pride; she cherished rare and delicate plants like oleanders in tubs and wall-flowers and lemon verbenas in pots that had to be wintered in the cellar; she filled the waste spots of the yard with common things like the garden heliotrope in a corner by the woodshed, and the plantain lilies along the west side of the house.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east side - the side that is on the east

2. NOUN

/ wɛst saɪd / (AmE), / wɛst saɪd / (BrE)
The side or part of an area, building, or town that faces or lies toward the west.

Examples

Try 309 West 43rd Street. - Know a good spot on the West Side? - I'll think about it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: east side - the side or part of an area that lies toward the east
