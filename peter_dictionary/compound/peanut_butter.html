peanut butter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːnət ˌbʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌt ˌbʌtə / (BrE)
A smooth or crunchy spread made from ground roasted peanuts, commonly eaten on bread or used in cooking.

Examples

There were times it was n't right to make a person happy, like the times she came in the kitchen and asked for a peanut butter sandwich.
"You know we do n't keep peanut butter in this house ", he always told her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nut butter - a spread made from ground nuts (other than peanuts)

Etymology

Formed from 'peanut' and 'butter', indicating a butter-like spread made from peanuts; first used in the late 19th century in North America.
