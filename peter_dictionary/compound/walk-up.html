walk-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌʌp / (BrE)
An apartment in a building that has no elevator, requiring residents to use stairs to reach upper floors.

Examples

It was a walk up on Hudson Street.

Etymology

From 'walk' + 'up', referencing the need to walk upstairs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk.ʌp / (BrE)
A building, especially an apartment building, with no elevator, so residents must use stairs to reach upper floors.

Examples

But Mercer hung on, living, after a fashion, in a Greenwich Village fourth flight walk-up.
