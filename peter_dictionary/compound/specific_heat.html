specific heat

1. NOUN

/ spəˈsɪfɪk hiːt / (AmE), / spɪˈsɪfɪk hiːt / (BrE)
the amount of heat energy required to raise the temperature of one gram of a substance by one degree Celsius

Examples

Assuming thermal equilibrium between the anode surface and the transpiring argon, the gas enthalpy rise through the anode was calculated according to the relation f whereby the specific heat** of argon was taken as **f.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat capacity - total amount of heat needed to change a substance's temperature

Etymology

Formed from 'specific' (from Latin 'specificus', 'of a kind or species') + 'heat'.
