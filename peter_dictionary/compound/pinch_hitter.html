pinch hitter

1. NOUN

/ pɪntʃ ˈhɪtər / (AmE), / pɪntʃ ˈhɪtə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A player who substitutes for another batter, especially in a critical situation.

Examples

Then three consecutive pinch hitters failed to produce.

Etymology

From 'pinch' (a critical situation) + 'hitter', used since early 20th century American baseball.
