second moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
The expected value of the square of the deviations of a random variable from the point of origin, used in statistics and physics to characterize the spread of a distribution.

Etymology

From 'second' (ordinal number) and 'moment' (statistical term from Latin 'momentum', meaning importance or force).
