sugar maple

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊɡɚ ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
A North American maple tree (Acer saccharum) valued for its hardwood and as the principal source of maple syrup and maple sugar.

Examples

Since the hazards of poor communication are so great, p can be justified as a habitable site only on the basis of unusual productivity such as is made available by a waterfall for milling purposes, a mine, or a sugar maple camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - any tree or shrub of the genus Acer, typically with lobed leaves
