ipso facto

1. ADV

/ ˌɪpsoʊ ˈfæktoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪpsəʊ ˈfæktəʊ / (BrE)
By that very fact or as a direct consequence; by the fact itself.

Examples

It is a characteristic of thoughts that, in re-thinking them we come, ipso facto, to understand why they were thought ".

Synset Examples

ipso facto, her innocence was established

Related Terms

  • Synonym: per se - by itself; intrinsically

Etymology

From Latin 'ipso facto', meaning 'by the fact itself'.
