hand grenade

1. NOUN

/ hænd ɡrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / hænd ɡrəˈneɪd / (BrE)
A small bomb designed to be thrown by hand, typically exploding after a short time delay.

Examples

Thus he wired J. P. Lower and Sons of Denver: "Have you any percussion hand grenades for throwing in a house or across a well loaded with balls or shrapnel shot?
All sizes: big ones, some wired to set off a whole field, little ones, hand grenade size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenade - general term for a small bomb

Etymology

From 'hand' (Old English, meaning 'the end part of the arm') + 'grenade' (French 'grenade', from Spanish 'granada', meaning 'pomegranate', likening the weapon's shape to the fruit).
