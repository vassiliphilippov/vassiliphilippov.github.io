meteorological conditions

1. NOUN

/ ˌmit̬i.ɚ.əˈlɑdʒ.ɪ.kəl kənˈdɪʃ.ənz / (AmE), / ˌmiː.tɪə.rəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl kənˈdɪʃ.ənz / (BrE)
the state of the atmosphere at a particular time, especially as it affects or predicts the weather

Examples

Neutral or inversion meteorological conditions are necessary for a cloud to travel along the surface.
In the first trial an inert substance was disseminated from a boat travelling some ten miles off shore under appropriately selected meteorological conditions.

Etymology

From meteorological, relating to meteorology, which derives from Greek meteōrologia (discussion of atmospheric phenomena).
