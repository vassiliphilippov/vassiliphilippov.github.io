vote of confidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊt əv ˈkɑnfɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈvəʊt əv ˈkɒnfɪdəns / (BrE)
a formal or informal expression showing that a group or individual approves of and supports someone or something, often used to demonstrate trust in a leader or decision

Examples

A splendid vote of confidence in Thayer, Madden reflected.

Synset Examples

they gave the chairman a vote of confidence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vote of no confidence - a formal vote showing that a person or group no longer has support
