carboxymethyl cellulose

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˌbɑksimeθɪl ˈsɛljəˌloʊs / (AmE), / kɑːˌbɒksimeθɪl ˈsɛljʉːləʊs / (BrE)
An acid derivative of cellulose used as a thickener, stabilizer, and emulsifier in food and industrial products.

Examples

Abelson and Rawson, using a stepwise elution scheme, fractionated whole sera containing ABO and Rh antibodies on diethylaminoethyl DEAE cellulose and carboxymethyl cellulose.

Etymology

Formed from 'carboxymethyl' (a chemical group) and 'cellulose'.
