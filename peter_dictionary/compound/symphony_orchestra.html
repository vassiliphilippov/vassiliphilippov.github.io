symphony orchestra

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.fə.ni ɔɹˈkɛs.trə / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.fə.ni ɔːˈkɛs.trə / (BrE)
a large ensemble of musicians, typically with strings, woodwinds, brass, and percussion, that performs symphonic and other orchestral music

Examples

Of course, it must not be forgotten that in achieving this historical feat, Prokofieff had the vast resources of his people behind him; time and economic security; symphony orchestras, opera and ballet companies; choruses, chamber music ensembles; soloists; recordings; broadcastings; television; large and eager audiences.

Synset Examples

we heard the Vienna symphony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestra - large group of instrumentalists playing together

Etymology

Symphony from Greek *symphonia* (agreement or concord of sound); orchestra from Greek *orchestra* (a place for dancing in ancient theaters).
