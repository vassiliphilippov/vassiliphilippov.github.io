hard times

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd taɪmz / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪmz / (BrE)
A period of difficulty, especially involving financial hardship or adversity.

Examples

A second reported that the dilapidated houses in Virginia "look like the latter end of original sin and hard times ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosperity - a period of financial success or thriving
  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being very poor; lack of means
