coco palm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.koʊ pɑm / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.kəʊ pɑːm / (BrE)
A tall tropical palm tree (Cocos nucifera) producing large edible coconuts; widely cultivated in tropical regions.

Examples

An elephant or a fox or a swan or a cocopalm or a banana possess in unusually high degree this quality of obvious, common-sense, indubitable identity, as do an eye or tooth or nail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coconut palm - another common name for the same species (Cocos nucifera)

Etymology

From Spanish and Portuguese 'coco' (coconut) and 'palm', referring to the palm tree bearing coconuts.
