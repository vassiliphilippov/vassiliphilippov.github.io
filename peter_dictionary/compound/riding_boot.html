riding boot

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdɪŋ buːt / (AmE), / ˈraɪdɪŋ buːt / (BrE)
A long, sturdy boot without laces, typically extending to the knee, worn for riding horses as part of equestrian attire.

Examples

He felt cheerful again, refreshed; presentable in his wide-cut brown suit, the well-made riding boots.
They were all three bent over a shabby riding boot; George had a tack hammer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: boot - a type of footwear covering the foot and lower leg
