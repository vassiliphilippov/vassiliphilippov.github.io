tug-of-war

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
A difficult struggle or contest between groups or individuals equally matched in strength, influence, or determination.

Examples

The Bathyrans ran a check on Globocnik and had only to conclude that he was in a tug of war with Hans Frank and the civilian administrators.
It purported to be a reasonably serious attempt at a treatment of jazz musicians, their aims, their problems - the tug-of-war between the "pure "and the "commercial "- and seemed a promising vehicle, for the two men shared a common interest in jazz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - forceful or violent effort to get free of restraint or resist attack
  • Synonym: contest - competition or struggle for victory or superiority
  • Antonym: cooperation - the process of working together to the same end

Etymology

Originally from the literal contest involving teams pulling on opposite ends of a rope; 'tug' (pull) + 'war'.
