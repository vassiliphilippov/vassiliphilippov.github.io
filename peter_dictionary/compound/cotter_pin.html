cotter pin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtər pɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtə pɪn / (BrE)
A metal fastener consisting of a split pin that is inserted through a hole and secured by bending the ends apart.

Examples

On the truck bed there was nothing smaller than a piece of rusty machinery; with more time I could have loosened a small burr or cotter pin -

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split pin - a metal fastener with two legs that are bent to secure it

Etymology

From 'cotter', a metal pin, likely from Old French 'coter' (to wedge), and 'pin'.
