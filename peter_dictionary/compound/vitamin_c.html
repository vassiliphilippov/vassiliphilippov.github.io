vitamin C

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪtəmɪn ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˈsi / (BrE)
an essential vitamin found in fresh fruits (especially citrus fruits) and vegetables, necessary for healthy body function and the prevention of scurvy

Examples

Aside from this, the average portion contains some protein, an appreciable amount of vitamins and C - about one-tenth of the minimum daily requirement, and about a third of the official vitamin E requirement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascorbic acid - the chemical name for vitamin C

Etymology

The 'vitamin' part from Latin 'vita' (life) and 'amine' (originally thought to contain an amine group); 'C' denotes its position as the third vitamin discovered.
