balance of payments

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.əns əv ˈpeɪ.mənts / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns əv ˈpeɪ.mənts / (BrE)
A record of all economic transactions between one country and the rest of the world over a specific period, typically one year.

Examples

Finally, a balance must be effected among project finance, utilization of agricultural surpluses, and general balance of payments support.

Synset Examples

a favorable balance of payments exists when more payments are coming in than going out

Etymology

From 'balance' (financial statement of credits and debits) + 'of payments'; used in economic contexts since the 18th century.
