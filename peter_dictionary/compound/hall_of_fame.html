Hall of Fame

1. NOUN

/ hɑl əv feɪm / (AmE), / hɔːl əv feɪm / (BrE)
A building or institution that honors individuals who have achieved distinction in a particular field, often by displaying their names, awards, or memorabilia.

Examples

Other items on the agenda during the meetings, which are expected to continue through Saturday, concern television, rules changes, professional football's hall of fame, players' benefits and constitutional amendments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: museum - a building where objects of historical, scientific, or artistic interest are kept and shown
  • Synonym: shrine - a place regarded as holy because of its associations with a notable person or event

Etymology

First used in the early 20th century, named after the 'Hall of Fame for Great Americans' founded in New York in 1900.
