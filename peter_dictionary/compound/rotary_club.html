Rotary Club

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri ˌklʌb / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri ˌklʌb / (BrE)
an international service organization of business and professional people, founded to promote humanitarian projects and goodwill among nations

Examples

Youngish man on the make, Madden labeled him, and was ready to guess that in a correct, not too pushing fashion, the junior partner of the firm had political ambitions; that Mrs. Garth would be impeccably suitable as the wife of a rising young lawyer; that there were three children, two boys and a girl; that she was active in the Woman's Club and he in Lions, Rotary, and Jaycee; and finally, that neither of them had harbored an unorthodox opinion since their wedding day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lions Club - another international service organization of business people
  • Synonym: Kiwanis - an international service club similar in purpose to Rotary Club

Etymology

From 'rotary' (meaning rotating or revolving) because original meetings rotated among members' places of business.
