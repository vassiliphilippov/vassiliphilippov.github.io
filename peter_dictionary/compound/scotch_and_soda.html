Scotch and soda

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ ənd ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ənd ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
A drink consisting of Scotch whisky mixed with club soda, typically served over ice.

Examples

She stopped at the Surcliffes' after dusk, and had a Scotch-and-soda.

Etymology

Scotch refers to Scotch whisky, produced in Scotland; 'soda' refers to soda water or club soda.
