Roman nose

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmən noʊz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmən nəʊz / (BrE)
A nose with a prominent, slightly curved (aquiline) bridge.

Examples

At the door she turned back, her Roman nose looking very long now and satiric.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aquiline nose - a nose that is curved like an eagle's beak, more pronounced curve than Roman nose

Etymology

Refers to the classical association with sculptures and portraits of ancient Romans, who were often depicted with such noses.
