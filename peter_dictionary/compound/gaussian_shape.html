Gaussian shape

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaʊsiən ʃeɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊsiən ʃeɪp / (BrE)
A symmetrical, bell-shaped curve representing the normal distribution, commonly used in statistics and physics.

Examples

The half-intensity diameter of the main lobe of the antenna was about 18' .5, and the brightness temperature was reduced by assuming a Gaussian shape for the antenna beam and a uniformly bright disk for the moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normal curve - the symmetrical bell-shaped curve of a normal distribution

Etymology

Named after Carl Friedrich Gauss (1777–1855), German mathematician, whose work on the normal distribution inspired the term.
