capital punishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl ˈpʌnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
The legally authorized killing of someone as punishment for a crime; the death penalty.

Examples

The editor of the Newport Advertiser could discover no evidence of extenuating circumstances in the Brown trial which would warrant making an exception to the infliction of capital punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death penalty - Execution of a person by the state as punishment for a crime

Etymology

From Latin 'capitalis' (relating to the head, hence to life) and 'punishment'.
