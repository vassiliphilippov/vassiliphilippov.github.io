Great Britain

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən / (BrE)
the island that comprises England, Scotland, and Wales, situated to the northwest of mainland Europe

Examples

For the first three weeks, the ship skirted up the east coast of Great Britain, then turned westward.

Etymology

From the Latin 'Britannia', with 'Great' used to distinguish it from Brittany (France).

2. PROPN

/ ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈbrɪt.ən / (BrE)
An island off the northwest coast of continental Europe, comprising the countries of England, Scotland, and Wales; often used loosely to refer to the entire United Kingdom, including Northern Ireland.

Examples

The latter, members of two regiments of Swiss mercenaries transported by Great Britain to Canada to fight the Americans in the War of 1812, had settled in Montreal and Kingston at the close of the war in 1815.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United Kingdom - the sovereign country consisting of Great Britain and Northern Ireland

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Britannia' (Britain), with 'Great' distinguishing it from Brittany in France.
