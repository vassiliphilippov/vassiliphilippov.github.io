trade route

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪd ˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd ˌruːt / (BrE)
A customary course or path, especially at sea, regularly used by commercial vessels for transporting goods.

Examples

The route which he had traveled and which he believed might develop into a trade route was followed by his settlers earlier than he might have expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipping lane - a regularly used route at sea for ships

Etymology

From 'trade' (commerce) + 'route' (path or course), both from Middle English via Old French.
