pneumatic tire

1. NOUN

/ nuˈmætɪk ˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / njuːˈmætɪk ˈtaɪə / (BrE)
A tire made of reinforced rubber and filled with compressed air, used on motor vehicles, bicycles, and similar vehicles.

Etymology

From Greek pneuma (air, breath); 'pneumatic' refers to air or gases.
