stainless steel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪn.ləs stil / (AmE), / ˈsteɪn.ləs stiːl / (BrE)
A type of steel containing chromium, making it resistant to rust and corrosion.

Examples

Scotty's father sat sprawled in his chair, angular, alert as a cricket, looking about at the huge stainless-steel appointments of the room with an expression of proprietorship.

Etymology

From 'stainless' (without stains) and 'steel'; developed in the early 20th century from advances in metallurgy.
