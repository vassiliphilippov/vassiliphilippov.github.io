phonological system

1. NOUN

/ fəˌnɑːləˈdʒɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / fəˌnɒləˈdʒɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
The complete set of sounds and sound rules used in a particular language.

Examples

But no other two phonologic systems are as difficult to disentangle as are tone and intonation in some languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - study of the sound system of languages

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) + -logical, relating to the study of these sounds.
