deadly sin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdli sɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli sɪn / (BrE)
A sin regarded, especially in Christian theology, as grave enough to result in a complete loss of divine grace if unrepented; traditionally, one of the seven sins deemed particularly serious.

Examples

It resembles, too, pictures such as Du^rer and Bruegel did, in which all that looks at first to be solely pictorial proves on inspection to be also literary, the representation of a proverb, for example, or a deadly sin.

Synset Examples

theologians list seven mortal sins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: venial sin - a relatively minor sin not resulting in complete loss of grace
  • Synonym: mortal sin - another term for deadly sin; a sin causing complete loss of grace
