road game

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd ˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd ˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
A sporting event played at an opponent's venue, rather than at the team's own home venue.

Examples

The Texans have two more road games - at Buffalo and Houston - before they play for the home folks again, and it looks as if coach Hank Stram's men will meet the Bills just as they are developing into the kind of team they were expected to be in pre-season reckonings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: home game - a game played at a team's own venue

Etymology

road refers to travel away from home; compound of 'road' + 'game'.
