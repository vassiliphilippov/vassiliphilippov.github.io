iron cage

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn keɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən keɪdʒ / (BrE)
A situation or system from which there seems to be no escape, typically one that restricts individual freedom and is difficult or impossible to break free from.

Examples

"Fellowship by likeness "and "mission by friendly contact "form the iron cage of denominational religion.
An understanding of the new role of residential association in an industrial society serves to illuminate the forces which have fashioned the iron cage of conformity which imprisons the churches in their suburban captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - place or condition in which someone is confined
  • Synonym: trap - a situation from which escape is difficult

Etymology

Metaphorical use based on a literal cage made of iron; notably used by sociologist Max Weber in early 20th century.
