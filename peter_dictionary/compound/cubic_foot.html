cubic foot

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjuː.bɪk ˈfʊt / (AmE), / ˌkjuː.bɪk ˈfʊt / (BrE)
A unit of volume equal to that of a cube measuring one foot on each side, used in US customary and British imperial systems.

Examples

It should provide not less than 5 cubic feet per minute of air per person.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cubic inch - a unit of volume equal to a cube one inch per side
  • Degree Higher: cubic yard - a unit of volume equal to a cube one yard per side
  • Synonym: liter - metric unit of volume

Etymology

From Latin *cubus* (cube) and Old English *fōt* (foot).
