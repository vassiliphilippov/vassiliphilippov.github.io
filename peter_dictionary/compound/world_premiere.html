world premiere

1. NOUN

/ wɝld prəˈmɪr / (AmE), / wɜːld prəˈmɪə / (BrE)
the first public performance or showing of a play, film, or musical work anywhere in the world

Examples

Chicago was also a welcome host: there, in 1921, Prokofieff conducted the world premiere of the Love for Three Oranges, and played the first performance of his Third Piano Concerto.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - the first public performance of a play, film, etc.
