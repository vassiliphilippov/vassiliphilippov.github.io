vocational training

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈkeɪʃənəl ˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / vəʊˈkeɪʃənəl ˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
Education or instruction that prepares people for specific trades, crafts, or occupations.

Examples

Was it supposed, perchance, that A + M (vocational training, that is) was quite sufficient for the immigrant class which flooded that part of the New England world in the post Civil War period, the immigrants having been brought in from Southern Europe, to work in the mills, to make up for the labor shortage caused by migration to the West?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprenticeship - period of training with a skilled worker for a trade

Etymology

From 'vocation' (from Latin 'vocatio', a calling) + 'training' (from Old French 'trainer', to drag or draw).
