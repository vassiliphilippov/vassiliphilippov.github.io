heart disease

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrt dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːt dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
Any condition or disorder that affects the structure or function of the heart.

Examples

In such diseases as cancer, tuberculosis, and heart disease, early diagnosis and treatment are so vital that the waste of time by the patient with Doctor Fraud's cure-all gadget can prove fatal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiovascular disease - disease affecting the heart and blood vessels
  • Degree Higher: stroke - sudden loss of brain function due to blood supply issues
