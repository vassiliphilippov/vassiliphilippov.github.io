better off

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛtər ɔf / (AmE), / ˈbɛtə ɒf / (BrE)
In a more fortunate or prosperous situation than before or compared to others.

Examples

He may or may not be "better off "than his classmate who went on to a college and professional school.
Are we better off for having Shakespeare's idea of Shylock?
She would have been better off if she had stuck to her Bible.

Synset Examples

she would have been better off if she had stuck with teaching
is better off than his classmate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worse off - in a less fortunate or poorer situation
