dwelling house

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwɛlɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdwɛlɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house that is used as a residence; a building or part of a building for people to live in.

Examples

In November 1887 a line connecting several dwelling houses in Dorset was extended to Manchester Depot.

Synset Examples

he built a modest dwelling near the pond
they raise money to provide homes for the homeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residence - a place where someone lives
  • Synonym: dwelling - a place where people live
  • Synonym: house - a building for human habitation

Etymology

Compound of 'dwelling' (from Old English 'dwellan', to delay, abide) and 'house' (from Old English 'hūs').
