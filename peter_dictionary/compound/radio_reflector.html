radio reflector

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdioʊ rɪˈflɛktər / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ rɪˈflɛktə / (BrE)
A large parabolic antenna used to detect and focus radio waves from astronomical sources.

Examples

In spite of this, the very large radio reflectors and improved amplifying techniques which are now becoming available should make it possible to observe the radio emission of most of the planets in a few years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio telescope - an astronomical telescope that detects radio waves from space
