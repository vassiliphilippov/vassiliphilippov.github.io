value orientation

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæljuː ˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvæljuː ˌɒriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a person's or group's beliefs about what is important in life, especially regarding principles of right and wrong

Examples

This is demonstrated especially when there is awareness of radically different value orientation elsewhere; for example Americans rally to Christian values vis-a-vis those of atheistic communism.

Synset Examples

the Puritan ethic
a person with old-fashioned values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethic - a set of moral principles or values
