wide screen

1. NOUN

/ waɪd skrin / (AmE), / waɪd skriːn / (BrE)
A projection or display screen that is significantly wider than it is high, typically used in cinema or television formats.

Examples

Color was delayed until 1935, the wide screen until the early fifties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widescreen - another form referring to the same screen aspect ratio
