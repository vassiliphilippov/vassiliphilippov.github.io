flagellated protozoan

1. NOUN

/ fləˈdʒɛl.eɪ.tɪd proʊˈtoʊ.zoʊ.æn / (AmE), / fləˈdʒɛl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd prəʊˈtəʊ.zəʊ.ən / (BrE)
A single-celled organism of the protozoan group that moves using one or more whip-like appendages called flagella; some species are free-living while others can cause diseases in humans and animals.

Examples

Initially, the flagellated protozoa predominated, but they soon gave way to the free swimming ciliated protozoa.

Etymology

From Latin 'flagellum' (whip) + protozoan, from Greek 'protos' (first) + 'zoon' (animal).
