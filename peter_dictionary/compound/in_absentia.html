in absentia

1. ADV

/ ɪn æbˈsɛnʃə / (AmE), / ɪn æbˈsentiə / (BrE)
When someone is not present; used especially when someone is involved in a formal process, such as a trial or graduation, while not physically present.

Examples

His casual, dreamlike working methods, often as not in absentia, were an abrupt change from Harburg's, so that Arlen had to adjust again to another approach to collaboration.

Synset Examples

he was sentenced in absentia

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'in absence.'
