Hooker's green

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkərz ɡriːn / (AmE), / ˈhʊkəz ɡriːn / (BrE)
a green pigment made by blending Prussian blue with gamboge, widely used by artists for its distinct green tone

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viridian - a bright green pigment used in painting

Etymology

Named after William Hooker (1779–1832), English botanical illustrator.
