/ ʌp tu / (AmE), / ʌp tə / (BrE)

I 'd think that you, even more than I, would be wondering what they 're up to.

2. ADJ

/ ʌp tu / (AmE), / ʌp tə / (BrE)
Having the necessary qualities, ability, or courage for (a particular task or challenge).

Examples

Now it would be up to me to keep the little girl out of mischief.
Over a temperature range from 25 to 200 * * f and at pressures up to 250 atm, an overload of 300 psi, applied for a period of one day, results in an uncertainty in the pressure of, at most, one millimeter of mercury.

Synset Examples

equal to the task
the work isn't up to the standard I require

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal to - having the necessary ability or qualities for
  • Synonym: fit for - suitable or qualified for

3. ADJ

/ ʌp tʊ / (AmE), / ʌp tuː / (BrE)
having the necessary ability, strength, or quality to do or achieve something; capable or adequate for a particular task or standard

Examples

Because from the time of the great Christopher Columbus up through the time of Enrico Fermi, right up to the present day, Italian-Americans have been pioneers in building and defending our great nation.

Synset Examples

equal to the task
the work isn't up to the standard I require

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal to - having the necessary ability or quality for something
  • Synonym: fit for - suitable or adequate for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: not up to - not having the necessary qualities or standard
