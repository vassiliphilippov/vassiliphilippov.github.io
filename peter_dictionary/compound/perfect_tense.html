perfect tense

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.fɪkt tɛns / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt tɛns / (BrE)
A verb tense used to describe actions that have been completed or have relevance to the present, past, or future.

Examples

Thus, the masculine animate infinitive dabhumaksanigalu'ahai, meaning to live, was, in the perfect tense, ksu'u'peli'afo, and, in the future, mai'teipa.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simple tense - verb tense indicating an action without reference to completion or duration
  • Antonym: progressive tense - verb tense indicating ongoing or continuous action

Etymology

Formed from 'perfect', from Latin 'perfectus' (completed), and 'tense', from Latin 'tempus' (time).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.fɪkt tɛns / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt tɛns / (BrE)
A verb tense used to express an action that has been completed at the time of speaking or at some relevant time in the past, present, or future.

Examples

Where have you been?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfective aspect - grammatical aspect denoting a completed action

Etymology

From Latin 'perfectum' (completed) and Old French 'tens' (time), via Middle English.
