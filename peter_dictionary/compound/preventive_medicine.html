preventive medicine

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈvɛntɪv ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪv ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
The branch of medicine that focuses on preventing disease and promoting health, rather than treating illnesses after they arise.

Examples

They argue further (and somewhat contradictorily) that our knowledge and resources in preventive medicine would make it possible to control such an outbreak of disease.

Synset Examples

the medical establishment doesn't profit from preventive medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public health - the science of protecting and improving the health of people and communities

Etymology

From Latin *praeventus* (to prevent) and Latin *medicina* (medicine).
