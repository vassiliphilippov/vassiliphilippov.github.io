atomic physics

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɑmɪk ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies the structure and behavior of atoms, especially their energy levels, electron arrangements, and interactions, but not the atomic nucleus

Examples

In the physical sciences, these achievements concern electricity, chemistry, and atomic physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear physics - branch of physics dealing with atomic nuclei

Etymology

From 'atomic' (relating to atoms), from Greek 'atomos' (indivisible), and 'physics', from Greek 'physis' (nature).
