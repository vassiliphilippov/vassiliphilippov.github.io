expense account

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛns əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛns əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account used by employees, such as salespersons or executives, to record travel, entertainment, or other business-related expenses so they may be reimbursed by their employer

Examples

In Dunston the rent would run close to two hundred a month; in Medfield, perhaps twenty-five less, not all of it paid by Thayer, who could charge off one room on his expense account.
