political science

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
the study of the systems of government, the analysis of political activity and behavior, and the theory and practice of politics

Examples

The titles he could read easily through the criss-crossed wires: works on theology, astral physics, history, biology, political science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: politics - the activities associated with governance; the subject political science studies

Etymology

From Latin 'politicus' (of citizens or the state) and Latin 'scientia' (knowledge).
