Red Scare

1. NOUN

/ rɛd skɛr / (AmE), / rɛd skeə / (BrE)
A period of widespread fear or panic about the potential rise of communism, especially in the United States during the early and mid-20th century.

Examples

During the last years of Woodrow Wilson's administration, a red scare developed in our country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: McCarthyism - practice of making accusations of subversion or treason without proper evidence, especially during the 1950s in the US

Etymology

From 'red,' a term for communism (from the color of the Soviet flag), and 'scare,' meaning a sudden fear.
