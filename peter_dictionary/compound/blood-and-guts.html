blood-and-guts

1. ADJ

/ ˌblʌd ən ˈɡʌts / (AmE), / ˌblʌd ənd ˈɡʌts / (BrE)
Marked by violence, extreme physical action, or fierce enthusiasm, especially in military, sports, or fiction contexts.

Examples

Brevet Major General George Armstrong Custer was the regiment's first permanent commander and, like such generals as George S. Patton and Terry de la Mesa Allen in their rise to military prominence, Custer was a believer in blood and guts warfare.

Synset Examples

real blood-and-guts fiction
blood-and-guts football

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violent - using or involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill
  • Synonym: zealous - having or showing great energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: mild - not severe, intense, or violent
