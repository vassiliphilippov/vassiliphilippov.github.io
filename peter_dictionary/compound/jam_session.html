jam session

1. NOUN

/ dʒæm ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / dʒam ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
an informal or impromptu gathering of musicians, especially jazz musicians, who play improvised music together

Examples

For the hero of this work by John Osborne and Anthony Creighton is a chap embittered by more than the lack of beer during a jam session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of creating music spontaneously

Etymology

From 'jam' (slang for improvising music, especially jazz) and 'session' (meeting or period of activity); originated in jazz culture of the early 20th century.
