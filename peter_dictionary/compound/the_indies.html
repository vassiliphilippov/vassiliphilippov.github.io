the Indies

1. PROPN

/ ði ˈɪn.diz / (AmE), / ði ˈɪn.diːz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the Caribbean Sea, situated between North and South America, also known as the West Indies

Examples

A century of exploration had established that a great land mass, North and South America, lay between Europe and the Indies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: West Indies - the islands in the Caribbean, between North and South America

Etymology

From Latin 'Indiae', plural of 'India', originally used by Europeans to refer to South and Southeast Asia; later applied to Caribbean islands by explorers who believed they had reached India.
