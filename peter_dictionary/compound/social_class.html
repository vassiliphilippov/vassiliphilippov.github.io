social class

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.ʃəl klæs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ʃəl klɑːs / (BrE)
A group of people within a society who share similar economic, social, or educational status.

Examples

(1) central cities tend to be areas of residence for lower social classes; (2) central cities tend to be more heterogeneous in social composition.
