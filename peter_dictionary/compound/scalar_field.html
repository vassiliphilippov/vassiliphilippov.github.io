scalar field

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪlər fiːld / (AmE), / ˈskeɪlə fiːld / (BrE)
A mathematical function or region in which each point is associated with a scalar value, often used in physics and mathematics to describe quantities with magnitude but no direction.

Examples

We can do this through the characteristic values and vectors of T in certain special cases, i.e., when the minimal polynomial for T factors over the scalar field F into a product of distinct monic polynomials of degree 1.
First, T may not have a single characteristic value; this is really a deficiency in the scalar field, namely, that it is not algebraically closed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vector field - a field in which each point is associated with a vector

Etymology

From 'scalar' (from Latin 'scalaris', relating to a ladder or scale) and 'field' (mathematical sense from German 'Feld').
