infrared spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd ˈspɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd ˈspɛk.trəm / (BrE)
The range of wavelengths in the electromagnetic spectrum that are longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves, corresponding to infrared radiation.

Examples

An infrared spectrum, obtained by H. A. Benesi and R. G. Snyder of this laboratory, showed bands in the positions found by Jones.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: visible spectrum - portion of the electromagnetic spectrum visible to the human eye
  • Degree Higher: ultraviolet spectrum - portion of the electromagnetic spectrum with wavelengths shorter than visible light

Etymology

From 'infrared', meaning 'below red' (from Latin 'infra' meaning 'below' and English 'red'), and 'spectrum' from Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image).
