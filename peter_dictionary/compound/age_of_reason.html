Age of Reason

1. NOUN

/ eɪdʒ əv ˈriːzən / (AmE), / eɪdʒ əv ˈriːzən / (BrE)
A period in European history (c. 1650–1800) characterized by the emphasis on reason, individualism, and scientific thought, rather than tradition or established doctrine; also known as the Enlightenment.

Examples

The Boulevard de la Madeleine, the Boulevard Malesherbes, and the Rue Royale ran to it with graceful flattery, bearing tidings of the Age of Reason.

Synset Examples

the Enlightenment brought about many humanitarian reforms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Enlightenment - the European intellectual movement emphasizing reason over tradition

Etymology

Refers to the period (c. 17th–18th centuries) when reason was seen as the primary source of authority and legitimacy; also called the Enlightenment from French 'siècle des Lumières'.
