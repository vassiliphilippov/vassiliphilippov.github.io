junior high school

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːniɚ haɪ ˌskuːl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə haɪ ˌskuːl / (BrE)
A school for students typically in the seventh and eighth grades, between elementary and high school.

Examples

Rep. Henry C. Grover, who teaches history in the Houston public schools, would reduce from 24 to 12 semester hours the so-called "teaching methods "courses required to obtain a junior or senior high school teaching certificate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middle school - school for students in grades between elementary and high school, often grades 6–8
  • Degree Higher: high school - school for older students, generally grades 9–12
  • Degree Lower: elementary school - school for young children, typically grades K–6
