diurnal variation

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɝː.nəl ˌvɛr.iˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈɜː.nəl ˌveə.riˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A fluctuation or change that occurs in a regular pattern during each day, often relating to scientific phenomena such as temperature, biological processes, or other measurable variables.

Examples

The data show daily and diurnal variations.
The data have not yet been analyzed for diurnal variations.
The diurnal variation in the observed flux may be partly due to the dependence of the detector sensitivity on the incident velocity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circadian rhythm - biological processes showing a 24-hour cycle

Etymology

From Latin 'diurnalis' (daily) and 'variatio' (change).
