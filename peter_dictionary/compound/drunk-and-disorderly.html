drunk-and-disorderly

1. NOUN

/ drʌŋk ənd dɪsˈɔrdərli / (AmE), / drʌŋk ənd dɪsˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
A person who has been arrested or charged with the offense of being drunk and behaving in a disorderly manner in public.

Examples

Gun knew it was Car 12, the wagon, returned from delivering Ingleside's drunk-and-disorderlies to the City Jail.

Synset Examples

they delivered the drunk-and-disorderlies to the county jail

Etymology

Formed from 'drunk' and 'disorderly', reflecting the legal phrase 'drunk and disorderly' (offense).
