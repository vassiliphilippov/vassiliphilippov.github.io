rheumatic fever

1. NOUN

/ ruˈmæt̬ɪk ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ruːˈmatɪk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A serious disease, mainly affecting children, characterized by painful inflammation of the joints and often causing heart valve damage, typically following a streptococcal throat infection.

Examples

Sadie, like Beth March, suffered ill health - got rheumatic fever and had to be careful of her heart - but that never dampened her spirits.

Etymology

From 'rheumatic', relating to rheumatism, and 'fever.'
