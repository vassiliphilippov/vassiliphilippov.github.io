T-square

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌskwɛr / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌskweə / (BrE)
A ruler shaped like the letter 'T', used by draftsmen to draw straight and parallel lines, especially on technical drawings or blueprints.

Examples

He also bought a huge square of pegboard for hanging up his tools, and lumber for his workbench, sandpaper and glue and assorted nails, levels and T squares and plumb lines and several gadgets that he had no idea how to use or what they were for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set square - a triangular instrument used in technical drawing

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the capital letter 'T'.
