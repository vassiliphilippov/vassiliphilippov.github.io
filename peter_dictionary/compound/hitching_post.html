hitching post

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪtʃɪŋ poʊst / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃɪŋ pəʊst / (BrE)
A strong upright post, usually with a metal ring, to which horses are tied to prevent them from wandering away.

Examples

Opposite every gate was a hitching post or a stone carriage step, set with a rusty iron ring for tying a horse.

Etymology

From 'hitch', meaning to fasten or tether, and 'post', meaning an upright support.
