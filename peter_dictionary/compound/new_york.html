New York

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈjɔrk / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk / (BrE)
the largest city in the United States, located in southeastern New York State at the mouth of the Hudson River; a major financial, cultural, and population center

Examples

My partner in America, in New York.

Etymology

Named after York, a city in northern England; 'New' added when the Dutch colony of New Amsterdam was taken by the English in 1664 and renamed.

2. PROPN

/ nuː ˈjɔrk / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk / (BrE)
One of the original thirteen British colonies that formed the United States, later becoming a state.

Examples

He must be somewhere in New York.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York State - the modern US state formed from the colony of New York

Etymology

Named after the Duke of York (later King James II of England) when the English took the colony from the Dutch in 1664.
