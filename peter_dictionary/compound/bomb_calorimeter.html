bomb calorimeter

1. NOUN

/ bɑm kəˈlɒrəmətər / (AmE), / bɒm kəˈlɒrɪmiːtə / (BrE)
a strong, sealed container used to measure the heat produced during the combustion of a substance

Examples

Another recent achievement was the successful development of a method for the complete combustion in a bomb calorimeter of a metal in fluorine when the product is relatively non-volatile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calorimeter - device for measuring quantities of heat

Etymology

From 'bomb' (for sealed vessel under pressure) and 'calorimeter' (from Latin calor, meaning 'heat', and -meter).
