dangling modifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæŋɡlɪŋ ˈmɑdɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈdæŋɡlɪŋ ˈmɒdɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A word or phrase in a sentence that appears to modify the wrong word or no word at all, usually because of its incorrect placement.

Examples

There are many grammatical misconstructions other than dangling modifiers and anatomicals which permit two different interpretations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misplaced modifier - a modifier incorrectly separated from the word it modifies

Etymology

From "dangling" (hanging loosely) and "modifier" (grammar term for a word or phrase that restricts or qualifies another word).
