service entrance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪs ˈɛntrəns / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs ˈɛntrəns / (BrE)
an entrance to a building used by staff, delivery personnel, or for taking out refuse, rather than for the general public

Examples

Hub, nosing about, spotted reporters in the lobby, so Andy was hustled away quietly through the hotel's service entrance in a strange car which Hub had procured somewhere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main entrance - the principal entrance for general public use
