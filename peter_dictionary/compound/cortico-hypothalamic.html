cortico-hypothalamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔrtɪkoʊ haɪpoʊθəˈlæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊ haɪpəʊθəˈlæmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving both the cerebral cortex and the hypothalamus, especially concerning neural pathways or functional interactions.

Examples

After this brief discussion of neo -, paleocortical, and cortico-hypothalamic relations, let us return once more to the problem of hypothalamic balance and its physiological and pathological significance.
This tendency can be broken either by restoring hypothalamic excitability directly or via cortico-hypothalamic pathways.

Etymology

From Latin cortex (bark, outer layer) and hypothalamus (from Greek hypo- 'under' + thalamos 'chamber').
