terminal velocity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrmənl vəˈlɑsəti / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnəl vəˈlɒsəti / (BrE)
the constant maximum speed that a falling object achieves when the force of gravity is balanced by the resistance of the air or fluid it is moving through

Examples

After it has reached terminal velocity, the time for the tape to travel a known distance is recorded.

Etymology

From 'terminal' (ending or final) + 'velocity' (speed), referring to the final constant speed reached.
