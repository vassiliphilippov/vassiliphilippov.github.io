war crime

1. NOUN

/ wɔr kraɪm / (AmE), / wɔː kraɪm / (BrE)
A serious violation of the laws and customs of war, constituting a criminal offense under international law, such as mistreatment of prisoners or targeting civilians.

Examples

It is not that the facts of the persecution were unavailable; most of the information elicited in Jerusalem had been brought to the surface by the numerous War Crimes tribunals and investigating commissions, and by reports, memoirs, and survivors' accounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act punishable by law; an offense
