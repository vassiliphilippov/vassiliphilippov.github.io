flash flood

1. NOUN

/ flæʃ flʌd / (AmE), / flæʃ flʌd / (BrE)
A sudden and severe local flood, typically caused by heavy rain, with a large volume of water appearing and receding in a short period of time.

Examples

Walls of water rushed down the slopes and filled the hollows like the crests of flash floods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of a large amount of water beyond its normal limits

Etymology

Compound of 'flash' (sudden) and 'flood'. 'Flash' from Middle English, from Old French *flachir* (to bend, abate); 'flood' from Old English *flōd*.
