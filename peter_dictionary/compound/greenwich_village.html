Greenwich Village

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡrɛnɪtʃ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛnɪtʃ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A chiefly residential neighborhood in Manhattan, New York City, historically known as a center for artists, writers, and bohemian culture, especially in the 20th century.

Examples

But Mercer hung on, living, after a fashion, in a Greenwich Village fourth flight walk-up.

Etymology

Named after the village of Greenwich in London, England.
