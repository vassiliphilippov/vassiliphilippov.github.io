money order

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni ˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ˌɔːdə / (BrE)
a printed document issued by a bank or post office, used as a safe way to send a specified sum of money to a named person

Examples

All checks and money orders should be made payable to Internal Revenue Service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postal order - a payment order similar to a money order, issued by a postal service
