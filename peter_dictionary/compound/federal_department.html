federal department

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdɚəl dɪˈpɑːrtmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl dɪˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
A department or administrative division of the federal government of the United States, responsible for specific areas of public policy, government function, or service.

Examples

coordinate, correlate, and publish information with a view to advancing the development of low-cost saline water conversion projects; and cooperate with other Federal departments and agencies, with State and local departments, agencies, and instrumentalities, and with interested persons, firms, institutions, and organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government department - a part of government responsible for a particular area of activity
