central heating

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl ˈhiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
A system for heating a whole building in which air or water is warmed at a central point and distributed through pipes or vents to radiators or outlets in each room.

Examples

If the house is not wired adequately for electricity or if plumbing or a central heating system must be installed, check into the cost of making these improvements.
