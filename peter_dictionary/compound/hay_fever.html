hay fever

1. NOUN

/ heɪ ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / heɪ ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An allergy causing sneezing, a runny or blocked nose, and watery eyes, especially in spring and summer, triggered by pollen.

Examples

He milks the latest scientific advances, translating them into his own special Buck Rogers vocabulary to huckster his fake machines as a cure-all for everything from hay fever to sexual impotence and cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allergic rhinitis - inflammation of the nasal passages caused by allergies

Etymology

From the association with hay harvest season and fever-like symptoms, first used in the early 19th century.
