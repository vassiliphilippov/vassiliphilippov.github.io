accentual system

1. NOUN

/ ækˈsɛn.tʃu.əl ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ækˈsɛn.tju.əl ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
The system by which stress or accent is assigned to syllables or words in a particular language.

Examples

Some phonetic features, for example glottal catch or murmur, are sometimes to be assigned to segmental phonemics and sometimes to accentual systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosodic system - system governing rhythm, stress, and intonation in language

Etymology

From 'accentual', relating to accent, and 'system', from Greek systēma (organized whole).
