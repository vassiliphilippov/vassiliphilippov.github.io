short-lived

1. ADJ

/ ʃɔrtˈlɪvd, ʃɔrtˈlaɪvd / (AmE), / ʃɔːtˈlɪvd, ʃɔːtˈlaɪvd / (BrE)
Lasting for only a brief period; not enduring for long.

Examples

The comfort was short-lived, yet she found herself returning to the assurance whenever her imagination forced images on her too awful to contemplate without the prop of illusion.
The effect was as depressing as a gravestone, the applause irresolute and short-lived.

Synset Examples

the ephemeral joys of childhood
a passing fancy
youth's transient beauty
love is transitory but it is eternal
fugacious blossoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely

Etymology

Compound of 'short' + 'lived', with 'lived' referring to duration of life or existence.
