magnetic attraction

1. NOUN

/ məgˈnɛtɪk əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / magˈnɛtɪk əˈtrakʃən / (BrE)
The force by which a magnet attracts iron or other magnetic materials, a phenomenon associated with magnetic fields generated by electric currents or magnets.

Examples

The great resemblance between electrical and magnetic attractions and repulsions and the similarity of their laws necessarily would bring about this comparison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces
  • Antonym: magnetic repulsion - the force by which like poles of magnets repel each other
