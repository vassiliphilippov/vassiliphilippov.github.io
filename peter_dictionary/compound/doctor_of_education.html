Doctor of Education

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ əv ˌɛdjuˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə əv ˌɛdjʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A doctoral degree awarded for advanced study and original research in the field of education.

Examples

Dr. Clark holds an earned Doctor of Education degree from the University of Oklahoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PhD - the highest academic degree; Doctor of Philosophy
  • Synonym: EdD - abbreviation of Doctor of Education

Etymology

From Latin 'doctor' (teacher) and 'education'. Modeled after similar degree naming conventions.
