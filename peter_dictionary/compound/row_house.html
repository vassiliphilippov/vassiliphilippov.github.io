row house

1. NOUN

/ roʊ haʊs / (AmE), / rəʊ haʊs / (BrE)
A house that is one of a row of identical or similar houses, situated side by side and sharing common walls.

Examples

It was a Negro section of peeling row houses, store-front churches and ragged children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: townhouse - a tall, narrow traditional terraced house, generally having three or more floors
