barrel roll

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəl roʊl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl rəʊl / (BrE)
an aerial maneuver in which an aircraft makes a complete rotation around its longitudinal axis while following a helical or spiral flight path

Examples

If the turn was too tight, a barrel roll would bring them out.
A hell of an altitude for a barrel roll, but it could be done.

Etymology

The term likens the aircraft's motion to the rolling of a barrel along its length.
