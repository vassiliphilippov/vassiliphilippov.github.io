the Virgin

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈvɝːdʒɪn / (AmE), / ði ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
Mary, the mother of Jesus, especially venerated in Christian tradition and often referred to as the Virgin Mary.

Examples

His image of the Virgin had always been that of a young woman, even as had his memory of his mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgin Mary - Mary, the mother of Jesus in Christian theology

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' (maiden, virgin), used as a title for Mary.
