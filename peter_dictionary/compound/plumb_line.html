plumb line

1. NOUN

/ plʌm laɪn / (AmE), / plʌm laɪn / (BrE)
A cord with a metal weight attached to one end, used to determine the exact vertical direction (the plumb) from a specific point.

Examples

He also bought a huge square of pegboard for hanging up his tools, and lumber for his workbench, sandpaper and glue and assorted nails, levels and T squares and plumb lines and several gadgets that he had no idea how to use or what they were for.

Etymology

From Middle English 'plumb' (lead; weight), from Latin 'plumbum' (lead), referring to the lead weight used.
