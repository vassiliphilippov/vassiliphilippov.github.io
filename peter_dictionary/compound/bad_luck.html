bad luck

1. NOUN

/ bæd lʌk / (AmE), / bad lʌk / (BrE)
unfortunate chance; a situation or event caused by unpredictable and unfavorable outcomes

Examples

It took a piece of bad luck to show Michelangelo that the boy was devoted to him.

Synset Examples

if I didn't have bad luck I wouldn't have any luck at all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfortune - an unfortunate condition or event
  • Antonym: good luck - unpredictable outcome that is fortunate

2. NOUN

/ bæd lʌk / (AmE), / bæd lʌk / (BrE)
An unfortunate state or series of events caused by chance rather than one's own actions.

Examples

He wondered where the superstition had originated that it was bad luck for a crew chief to watch his plane take off on a combat mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfortune - unfortunate event or condition
  • Antonym: good luck - fortunate state resulting from favorable outcomes

3. NOUN

/ bæd lʌk / (AmE), / bæd lʌk / (BrE)
An unfortunate or unlucky situation; a state resulting from unfavorable circumstances or outcomes.

Examples

That's bad luck for me, and **bad luck** for you if you don't make that deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfortune - unfortunate event or circumstance
  • Antonym: good luck - favorable or lucky state or outcome
