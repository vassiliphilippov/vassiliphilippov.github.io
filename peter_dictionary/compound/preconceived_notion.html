preconceived notion

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd ˈnoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd ˈnəʊʃən / (BrE)
An opinion or belief formed before having enough evidence or experience.

Examples

The very firmness of her convictions and logical clearness of her arguments captivated and stimulated him to make greater efforts; usually, this is most exasperating to men, who expect every woman to verify their preconceived notions concerning her sex, and when she does not, immediately condemn her as eccentric and unwomanly.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - an unreasonable preconceived opinion or feeling
  • Synonym: bias - an inclination or prejudice for or against something

Etymology

From 'preconceived', meaning formed beforehand (from Latin 'prae-' meaning before + 'concipere' to conceive), and 'notion', from Latin 'notio' (idea, concept).
