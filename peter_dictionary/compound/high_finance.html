high finance

1. NOUN

/ haɪ fəˈnaɪns / (AmE), / haɪ faɪˈnɑːns / (BrE)
large-scale financial activities involving substantial sums of money, often considered complex or sophisticated and sometimes regarded as lacking transparency or morality.

Examples

After how many generations is such wealth (mounting all the while through the manipulations of high finance) purified of taint?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - management of large amounts of money, especially by governments or large companies
