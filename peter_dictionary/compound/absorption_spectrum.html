absorption spectrum

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrpʃən ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
The spectrum of electromagnetic radiation that has passed through a substance and has certain wavelengths missing because they have been absorbed.

Examples

It was possible to make estimates of the quantum yield by observing the extent of reduction of a uranyl oxalate actinometer solution illuminated for a known time in a typical reaction cell and making appropriate conversions based on the differences in the absorption spectra of uranyl oxalate and of chlorine, and considering the spectral distribution of the light source.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emission spectrum - The spectrum of electromagnetic radiation emitted by a source.

Etymology

absorption from Latin absorptio (a sucking in); spectrum from Latin spectrum (image, apparition).
