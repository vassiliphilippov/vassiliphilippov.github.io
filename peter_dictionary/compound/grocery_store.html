grocery store

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊsəri stɔr / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsəri stɔː / (BrE)
A shop or store where food and other daily necessities are sold.

Examples

Some retailing, of course, always spreads with the population - grocery stores, drugstores, local haberdasheries and dress shops, candy stores and the like.
The large amount of vegetable grindings from the grocery store in the shopping center created a suspended solids problem.

Synset Examples

the grocery store included a meat market

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supermarket - a large self-service store selling food and other goods
  • Degree Lower: corner shop - a small local shop selling groceries and other items, especially in the UK

Etymology

From 'grocery' (from Anglo-Norman *grosserie*), and 'store' (from Old French *estore*), meaning a shop for food and provisions.
