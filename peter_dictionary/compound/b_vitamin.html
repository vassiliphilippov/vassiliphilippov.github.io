B vitamin

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈvaɪtəmɪn / (AmE), / bi ˈvɪtəmɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of water-soluble vitamins that play important roles in cell metabolism, originally thought to be a single vitamin but now recognized as several distinct vitamins (such as B1, B2, B6, and B12)

Examples

The B vitamins are well represented, especially thiamin and riboflavin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin - an organic compound essential for normal growth and nutrition

Etymology

Named for their identification by letter, starting with vitamin B in scientific classification.
