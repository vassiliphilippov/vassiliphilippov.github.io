magnetic resonance

1. NOUN

/ məɡˌnɛtɪk ˈrɛzənəns / (AmE), / mæɡˌnɛtɪk ˈrɛzənəns / (BrE)
A phenomenon in which atomic nuclei or electrons respond to specific radiofrequency radiation in a magnetic field, widely used in physics, chemistry, and imaging technologies.

Examples

The magnetic moment of an unpaired electron associated nearby may have a tremendous influence on the magnetic resonance properties of nuclei.
The magnetic resonance absorption was detected by employing a Varian model **f broad line spectrometer and the associated 12 - inch electromagnet system.

Etymology

From 'magnetic' (relating to magnetism) and 'resonance' (from Latin 'resonantia', meaning echo or resounding).
