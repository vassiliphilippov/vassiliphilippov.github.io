Johnny Reb

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑni ˌrɛb / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒni ˌrɛb / (BrE)
A nickname used by Union soldiers to refer to a Confederate soldier during the American Civil War, especially one from the South.

Examples

Johnny Rebs from the deep South who were plagued with diarrhoea after transfer to the Virginia front often informed their families that they were suffering from the "the Virginia quickstep ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Yankee - Union soldier in the American Civil War (nickname)

Etymology

Nickname formed from the given name Johnny and 'Reb', a shortening of 'rebel', used by Union soldiers during the American Civil War.
