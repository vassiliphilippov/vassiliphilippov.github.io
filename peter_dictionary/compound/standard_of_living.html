standard of living

1. NOUN

/ ˌstændɚd əv ˈlɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌstændəd əv ˈlɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The degree of wealth, comfort, material goods, and necessities available to a person, group, or society.

Examples

As it is, they consider that the North is now reaping the fruits of excess egalitarianism, that in spite of its high standard of living the "American way "has been proved inferior to the English and Scandinavian ways, although they disapprove of the socialistic features of the latter.
Prosperity is a worthy objective; but if providing higher standards of living gets in the way of producing sufficient guns to resist Communist aggression, then material sacrifices and denials will have to be made.

Synset Examples

they enjoyed the highest standard of living in the country
the lower the standard of living the easier it is to introduce an autocratic production system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quality of life - the general well-being of individuals and societies
