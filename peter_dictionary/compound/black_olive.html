black olive

1. NOUN

/ blæk ˈɑːlɪv / (AmE), / blæk ˈɒlɪv / (BrE)
A ripe olive with a dark purplish-black color, typically cured and eaten as a food, often preserved in brine, oil, or canned.

Examples

When McFeeley was halfway to the door, the proprietor emerged - a mountainous, dark man, his head thick with resiny black hair, his eyes like two of the black olives he imported in boatloads.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: green olive - an unripe olive of green color, often pickled or brined
