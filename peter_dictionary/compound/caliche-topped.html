caliche-topped

1. ADJ

/ kəˈliːtʃi tɑpt / (AmE), / kəˈliːtʃi tɒpt / (BrE)
(of a road, surface, etc.) covered with a hard layer of caliche (calcium carbonate encrusted soil), often used to describe roads or ground in arid regions.

Examples

Driving along the caliche-topped road to town, Howard talked.

Etymology

From 'caliche', borrowed from Spanish, meaning a hard crust of calcium carbonate, and 'topped,' meaning covered on top.
