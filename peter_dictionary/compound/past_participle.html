past participle

1. NOUN

/ pæst pɑːrˈtɪsəpəl / (AmE), / pɑːst pɑːˈtɪsɪpl̩ / (BrE)
A verb form typically used to express a completed action or state, often used in perfect tenses and the passive voice.

Examples

He could have had his own family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present participle - verb form expressing ongoing action

Etymology

From Latin participium (participle), with 'past' indicating completed action.
