tabula rasa

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæbjələ ˈrɑsə / (AmE), / ˌtabjʊlə ˈrɑːsə / (BrE)
the mind in its hypothetical original state, before being influenced by experience; a blank slate

Examples

Experience is not seen, as it is in classical rationalism, as presenting us initially with clear and distinct objects simply located in space and registering their character, movements, and changes on the tabula rasa of an uninvolved intellect.
Gone is the tabula rasa of the mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blank slate - the idea of the mind as unaffected by experience; synonym for tabula rasa

Etymology

From Latin tabula rasa (scraped tablet), referring to a slate wiped clean for writing.
