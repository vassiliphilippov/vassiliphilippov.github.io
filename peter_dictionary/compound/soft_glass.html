soft glass

1. NOUN

/ sɔft ɡlæs / (AmE), / sɒft ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of glass that has a relatively low softening point compared to other glasses, making it easier to shape or melt at lower temperatures.

Examples

The light was filtered by the soft glass window of the thermostat thus ensuring that only light absorbed by the chlorine and not by the carbon tetrachloride could enter the reaction cell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hard glass - glass with a relatively high softening point
