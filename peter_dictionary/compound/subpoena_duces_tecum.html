subpoena duces tecum

1. NOUN

/ səˈpiː.nə ˌduːˌseɪs ˈtiː.kəm / (AmE), / səˈpiː.nə ˌdjuː.siːz ˈtiː.kəm / (BrE)
A writ issued by a court ordering a witness to produce documents or evidence in their possession at a trial or deposition.

Examples

At his trial, petitioner sought to secure through subpoena duces tecum the longhand notes of the Department's hearing officer, Evensen, as well as his report thereon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subpoena - a writ ordering a person to attend a court

Etymology

From Latin: 'subpoena' (under penalty) and 'duces tecum' (bring with you).
