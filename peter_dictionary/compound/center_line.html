center line

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚ ˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə ˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A line that divides a plane figure or object into two equal parts, especially along its length; a line through the center.

Examples

During the discharge the magnetic forces set up by the passage of current cause the edges of the foil to roll inward toward its center line, thus allowing light to pass into the camera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axis - an imaginary line about which a body rotates or is symmetrically arranged
  • Synonym: median - a line dividing a geometric figure into two equal parts, especially in mathematics
