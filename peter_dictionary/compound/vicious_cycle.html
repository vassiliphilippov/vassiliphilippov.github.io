vicious cycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃəs ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃəs ˈsʌɪkl / (BrE)
A situation in which one problem causes another problem, which then makes the original problem worse, creating a worsening loop that is hard to break.

Examples

Obviously, a vicious cycle develops.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virtuous circle - a situation in which one good thing leads to another that improves the first, in a beneficial loop
