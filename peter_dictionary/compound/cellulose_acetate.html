cellulose acetate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌloʊs ˈæs.ɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊˌləʊs ˈæs.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound derived from cellulose and acetic acid, used in making fibers, photographic film, and varnishes

Examples

A tape of cellulose acetate is pulled between the blocks and the tape pulls the fluid or paste with it between the parallel faces of the blocks.

Etymology

Formed from 'cellulose' (from Latin 'cellula', small cell) and 'acetate' (from Latin 'acetum', vinegar).
