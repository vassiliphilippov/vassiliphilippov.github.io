patent medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt ˈmɛdɪsən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt ˈmɛdɪsən / (BrE)
a medicine sold under a proprietary name, often without a prescription and not always scientifically proven in effectiveness, especially common in the past

Examples

The medical device pirate of today, of course, is a far more sophisticated operator than his predecessor of yesteryear - the gallus snapping hawker of snake oil and other patent medicines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprietary medicine - medicine sold under a brand name
  • Synonym: over-the-counter medicine - medicine available without prescription

Etymology

From 'patent' (exclusive legal right) and 'medicine'; originally referred to medicines with patented formulas.
