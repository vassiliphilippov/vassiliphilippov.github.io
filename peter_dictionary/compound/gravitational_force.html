gravitational force

1. NOUN

/ ɡræˌveɪʃənəl ˈfɔrs / (AmE), / ɡrævɪˈteɪʃənəl fɔːs / (BrE)
The force of attraction between all masses, especially the pull that the Earth exerts on objects near its surface.

Examples

Further, the corpuscular radiation, i.e., the solar wind protons, must sputter away the surface atoms of the dust and cause a slow diminution in size, with a resultant increase in both the Poynting-Robertson effect and the ratio of the repulsive force to the gravitational force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the natural force that causes things to fall towards the earth

Etymology

Formed from 'gravitational', from 'gravity' (from Latin gravitas, meaning 'weight, heaviness') and 'force'.
