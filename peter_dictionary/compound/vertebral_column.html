vertebral column

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝː.tə.brəl ˈkɑː.ləm / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɪ.brəl ˈkɒl.əm / (BrE)
the series of bones (vertebrae) forming the central axis of the skeleton in vertebrates, enclosing and protecting the spinal cord; the backbone or spine.

Examples

X-ray films of the vertebral column showed progression of the demineralization.

Synset Examples

the fall broke his back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spine - the backbone; the series of vertebrae extending from the skull to the lower back
  • Synonym: backbone - the column of bones within the body that supports the head and body and surrounds the spinal cord

Etymology

From Latin vertebra (joint of the spine) + columna (pillar, column).
