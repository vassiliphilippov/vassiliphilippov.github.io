lights-out

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪtsˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtsˈaʊt / (BrE)
The time at which lights must be switched off, especially as a signal that people should go to bed, typically in institutions such as the military, prisons, or camps.

Examples

He was in his cell at lights out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedtime - the time at which one goes to bed
