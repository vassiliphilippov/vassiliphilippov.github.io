ruling class

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːlɪŋ klæs / (AmE), / ˈruːlɪŋ klɑːs / (BrE)
The social group that holds power or authority in a society, especially by making and enforcing political or economic decisions.

Examples

Moreover, it is too readily forgotten that in the Republic what gave the initial impetus to Plato's excursus into the construction of an imaginary commonwealth with its ruling-class communism of goods, wives, and children, was his quest for a canon for the proper ordering of the individual human psyche; and it is to this problem that the Republic ultimately returns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: upper class - the social group with the highest status, often associated with wealth
  • Antonym: working class - the social group consisting of people employed in lower-paying jobs, often with less authority
