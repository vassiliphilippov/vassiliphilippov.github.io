Roman Empire

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈɛm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
the empire established by Augustus in 27 BC, which at its height controlled much of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, and was later divided into Western and Eastern (Byzantine) Empires

Examples

The Corleone family was like the Roman Empire.

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman) + 'imperium' (command, sovereignty).
