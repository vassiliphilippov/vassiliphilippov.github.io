dinner pail

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪnɚ ˌpeɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɪnə ˌpeɪl / (BrE)
A metal pail used by workers to carry their lunch or dinner, especially common in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

As the robbers leave the looted train, the film suddenly cuts back to the station, where the telegrapher's little daughter arrives with her father's dinner pail only to find him bound on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunchbox - a box for carrying a packed meal
