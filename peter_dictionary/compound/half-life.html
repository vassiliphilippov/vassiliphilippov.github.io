half-life

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌlaɪf / (BrE)
The time required for a quantity, especially the atoms in a radioactive substance, to decrease to half its original value.

Examples

The crowd consequently breaks up into temporary groups ranging in size from two to six, with a half-life for the cluster ranging from three to twenty minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of declining in quantity or quality
  • Degree Higher: lifetime - the duration of existence of something

Etymology

From 'half' + 'life', first used in physics to describe the decay rate of radioactive substances.
