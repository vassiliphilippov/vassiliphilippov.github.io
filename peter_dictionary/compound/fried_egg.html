fried egg

1. NOUN

/ fraɪd ɛɡ / (AmE), / fraɪd eɡ / (BrE)
An egg cooked by frying in oil or butter, typically with the yolk unbroken; it may be cooked on one or both sides.

Examples

The enormous plates which had held Mr. Jack's four fried eggs and five strips of bacon were still stacked in the sink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scrambled egg - egg beaten and cooked while stirring to form soft curds
  • Antonym: boiled egg - egg cooked in its shell by boiling
  • Antonym: poached egg - egg cooked without shell in simmering water
