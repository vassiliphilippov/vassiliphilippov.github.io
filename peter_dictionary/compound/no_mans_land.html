no man's land

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊˌmænz ˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌmænz ˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of land between the positions of opposing armies which is not controlled by either side and is often dangerous or unoccupied

Examples

This was no man's land, disputed by the Haijac Union and the Israeli Republic, a potential source of war for the last two hundred years.

Etymology

Originally used for land not claimed by any owner; popularized during World War I to describe the land between enemy trenches.
