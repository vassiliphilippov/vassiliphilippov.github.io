Guy Fawkes Day

1. NOUN

/ ɡaɪ ˈfɔks deɪ / (AmE), / ɡaɪ ˈfɔːks deɪ / (BrE)
An annual British celebration on November 5th commemorating the failure of the Gunpowder Plot of 1605, marked by fireworks and bonfires.

Examples

On my first Guy Fawkes Day here, I found Catholics as well as non Catholics celebrating with the traditional fireworks and bonfires, and was told that most Englishmen either do not know or are not concerned with the historical significance of the day.

Etymology

Named after Guy Fawkes, a conspirator in the Gunpowder Plot of 1605.
