calcium hydride

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əm haɪˈdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈkælsiəm haɪˈdraɪd / (BrE)
A saltlike binary compound (CaH₂) used as a reducing agent and as a source of hydrogen.

Examples

Calcium hydride was substituted for * * f as a drying agent for carbon tetrachloride.

Etymology

From 'calcium' (from Latin 'calx', limestone) and 'hydride' (from hydrogen).
