ice floe

1. NOUN

/ aɪs floʊ / (AmE), / aɪs fləʊ / (BrE)
A large, flat sheet of floating ice, typically found in the sea and smaller than an ice field.

Examples

It's just like that book your Northern friend wrote - except there are n't any ice floes to cross and no bloodhounds ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ice field - a large area of floating ice, bigger than an ice floe
