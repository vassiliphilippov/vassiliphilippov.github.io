typhus fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.fəs ˈfiː.vər / (AmE), / ˈtɪ.fəs ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease caused by rickettsial bacteria, transmitted by body lice, and marked by high fever and a widespread skin rash.

Examples

Let us assume that it would be possible for an enemy to create an aerosol of the causative agent of epidemic typhus (Rickettsia prowazwki) over City A and that a large number of cases of typhus fever resulted therefrom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhus - infectious disease caused by rickettsial bacteria

Etymology

From Greek *typhos* (smoke, stupor), referring to the delirium seen in severe infections, and 'fever' from Old French *fievre*, from Latin *febris*.
