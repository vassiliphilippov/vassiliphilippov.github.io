real estate broker

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəl əˈsteɪt ˈbroʊkər / (AmE), / ˌrɪəl ɪsˈteɪt ˈbrəʊkə / (BrE)
A person licensed and authorized to act as an agent in the buying, selling, or renting of real estate.

Examples

The other bill, by Sen. A. M. Aikin Jr. of Paris, would relieve real estate brokers, who pay their own annual licensing fee, from the $ 12 annual occupation license on brokers in such as stocks and bonds.

Synset Examples

in England they call a real estate agent a land agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realtor - a member of the National Association of Realtors, often acting as a real estate agent
  • Synonym: real estate agent - a person who acts as an intermediary in the buying and selling of real estate
  • Degree Lower: seller - a person who sells something, not specific to real estate
  • Degree Higher: real estate developer - a person or company that builds new houses or commercial property for sale
