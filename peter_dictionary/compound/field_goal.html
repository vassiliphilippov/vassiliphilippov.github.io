field goal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːld ɡoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ɡəʊl / (BrE)
A score made in American football by kicking the ball through the opponents' goalposts.

Examples

Moritz said Monday his leg feels fine and, as a result, he hopes to start practicing field goals this week.
It was the first time we 've been ahead this season (when John Richey kicked what proved to be the winning field goal) ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchdown - a score in American football, made by carrying the ball into the end zone

Etymology

From 'field' meaning the playing area, and 'goal' referring to the scoring structure, used since the early forms of football.
