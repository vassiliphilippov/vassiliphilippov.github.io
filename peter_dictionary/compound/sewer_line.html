sewer line

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.ɚ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsjʊə laɪn / (BrE)
a large pipe or conduit that carries waste water and sewage away from buildings or areas to a disposal or treatment site

Examples

The layout of the sewer lines was designed by Henry W. Taylor, who was the engineer for the Manchester Village disposal plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewer - an underground conduit for carrying off drainage water and waste matter
