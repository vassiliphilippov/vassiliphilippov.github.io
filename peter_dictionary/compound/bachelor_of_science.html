Bachelor of Science

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃələr ʌv ˈsaɪəns / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələ əv ˈsaɪəns / (BrE)
A bachelor's degree awarded for an undergraduate program in science or related fields.

Examples

He also received a Master of Science degree from Texas A+I College and a Bachelor of Science degree from Southwestern State College, Weatherford, Okla..

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Master of Science - a postgraduate degree in science

Etymology

bachelor from Latin baccalarius (young man, student); science from Latin scientia (knowledge).
