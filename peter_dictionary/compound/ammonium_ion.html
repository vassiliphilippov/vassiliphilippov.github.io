ammonium ion

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniəm aɪˌɑn / (AmE), / əˈməʊniəm aɪˌɒn / (BrE)
A positively charged ion (NH4+) formed from ammonia, commonly found in solutions and behaving like an alkali metal ion in many ways.

Examples

The precipitate was washed twice with an 80 % saturated solution of * * f, dissolved in a small quantity of 0.1 M neutral phosphate buffer, dialyzed against cold distilled water till free from ammonium ions, and lyophilized using liquid nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonia - A colorless gas (NH3) with a pungent smell, from which ammonium ion is derived
  • Synonym: cation - A positively charged ion

Etymology

From 'ammonium' (from ammonia) + 'ion.' 'Ammonia' comes from Latin 'ammoniacum', relating to the temple of Ammon in Egypt, where ammonium chloride was collected.
