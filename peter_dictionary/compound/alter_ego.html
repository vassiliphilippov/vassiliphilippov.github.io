alter ego

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔltər ˈiːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɔːltə ˈiːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A very close and trusted friend who seems like another self; a second self or different version of oneself.

Examples

Linked to Holmes even in death, Moriarty represents the alter-ego of the great detective, the image of what our hero might have become were he not a public servant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soulmate - a person ideally suited to another as a close friend or romantic partner
  • Antonym: nemesis - a long-standing rival; an archenemy, often considered an opposite or counterpart

Etymology

From Latin 'alter' (other) + 'ego' (I, self).
