arteriosclerosis obliterans

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪri.oʊskləˈroʊsɪs ɒb.lɪˈtɛr.ənz / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəri.əʊskləˈrəʊsɪs ɒblɪˈtɛərənz / (BrE)
A stage of arteriosclerosis characterized by the narrowing and closure of blood vessels, typically leading to impaired blood flow to organs or limbs.

Examples

Additional findings at this time included cardiomegaly, peripheral arteriosclerosis obliterans, and cholelithiasis.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'arteriosclerosis' (hardening of arteries) + Latin 'obliterare' (to erase), referring to closure or obliteration of the vessel.
