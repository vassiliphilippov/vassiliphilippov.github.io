Siamese cat

1. NOUN

/ saɪəˌmiz kæt / (AmE), / saɪəˌmiːz kæt / (BrE)
A breed of slender, short-haired cat with blue eyes and a pale coat featuring dark markings on the ears, face, paws, and tail.

Examples

When Siamese cats are intertwined it is difficult to tell where one leaves off and another begins.
Stilts and Shadow, on Pam's bed, appeared to be one cat - rather large, as Siamese cats go, and, to be sure, having two heads and two tails.

Etymology

Named after Siam (now Thailand), where the breed originated.
