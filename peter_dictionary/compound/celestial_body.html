celestial body

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛstʃəl ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛstiəl ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a natural object in space, such as a star, planet, or moon, that can be seen in the sky

Examples

We talk about national character in the same way that Copernicus talked of the compulsions of celestial bodies to move in circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomical object - an object in space, such as a planet, star, or galaxy, studied by astronomy

Etymology

From Latin caelestis (heavenly) and body.
