Holy Sacrament

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˈsækrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˈsækrəmənt / (BrE)
In Christianity, the sacrament of the Eucharist, commemorating the Last Supper by consecrating bread and wine.

Examples

The Holy Sacrifice of the Mass with commentary has been televised several times in recent months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Communion - the Christian rite of consecrated bread and wine; alternative name
  • Synonym: Eucharist - the Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper

Etymology

From Latin sacramentum (sacred oath or rite), with 'holy' from Old English hālig.
