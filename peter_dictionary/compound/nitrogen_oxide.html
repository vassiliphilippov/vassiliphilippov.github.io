nitrogen oxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.tɹə.dʒən ˈɑk.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.tɹɪ.dʒən ˈɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
Any of several chemical compounds consisting of nitrogen and oxygen atoms, some of which are found in car exhaust and contribute to air pollution.

Examples

Mass spectrometric analysis of gases evolved upon heating to 410 ` C indicated nitrogen oxides and water vapor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nitric oxide - a specific nitrogen oxide, chemical formula NO
  • Degree Higher: nitrogen dioxide - a specific nitrogen oxide, chemical formula NO₂

Etymology

Formed from 'nitrogen' and 'oxide' (from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid) and '-ide' (forming names of chemical compounds)).
