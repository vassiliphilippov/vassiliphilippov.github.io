vocal cord

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊkəl kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəl kɔːd / (BrE)
Either of two bands of muscle and mucous membrane in the larynx that produce sound when air passes through them.

Examples

The dark forms moved like mourners on some nocturnal pilgrimage, their dirge unsung for want of vocal chords.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocal fold - another term for vocal cord; also refers to the bands of tissue in the larynx
