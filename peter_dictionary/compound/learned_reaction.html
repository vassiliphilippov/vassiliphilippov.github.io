learned reaction

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪd riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd riˈakʃən / (BrE)
A reaction or response acquired through learning, rather than one that is innate or instinctive.

Examples

He showed convincingly that anxiety is a learned (conditioned) reaction and is the basis of experimental and clinical neuroses and assumed, therefore, that the neuronal changes which underlie the neuroses are functional and reversible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conditioned response - a learned response to a previously neutral stimulus
  • Antonym: innate reaction - a reaction present from birth, not learned
