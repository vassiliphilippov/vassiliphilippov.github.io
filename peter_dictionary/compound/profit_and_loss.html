profit and loss

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fɪt ənd lɔs / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ɪt ənd lɒs / (BrE)
An account or financial statement summarizing gross and net profits or losses over a set period, typically at the end of an accounting period.

Examples

Are there individuals in your organization who can shepherd a new product through to commercialization; who can develop reliable estimates of sales volume, production, and distribution costs; and translate the whole into profit and loss and balance sheet figures which management can act on with some assurance?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit and loss account - A British term for a financial statement showing profits or losses
  • Synonym: income statement - A U.S. term for a financial statement showing company earnings and losses
