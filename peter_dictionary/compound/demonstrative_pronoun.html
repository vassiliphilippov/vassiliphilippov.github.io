demonstrative pronoun

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑnstrətɪv ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv ˈprəʊˌnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun used to point out or identify specific people or things, such as 'this', 'that', 'these', and 'those'

Examples

JOHNNY: Those rebels, you know, they'’re lunatics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that takes the place of a noun

Etymology

From Latin demonstrativus (pointing out) and pronomen (pronoun).
