sacrifice fly

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæk.rəˌfaɪs flaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsæk.rɪˌfʌɪ flaɪ / (BrE)
(In baseball) a fly ball that enables a base runner to score after the catch is made, usually at the cost of the batter being put out.

Examples

The White Sox had taken a 5 - 4 lead in the top of the sixth on a pair of pop fly hits - a triple by Roy Sievers and single by Camilo Carreon - a walk and a sacrifice fly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice bunt - (baseball) a bunt allowing a runner to advance while the batter is put out

Etymology

From 'sacrifice' (giving up one's chance) and 'fly' (fly ball in baseball terminology).
