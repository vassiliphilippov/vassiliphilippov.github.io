benefactive role

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnəˌfæktɪv roʊl / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˌfæktɪv rəʊl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) the semantic role of a person or entity that benefits from the action described by the verb in a clause.

Examples

You'’ve done well for yourself.
If there'’s something I can do for you, you come, we talk, huh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recipient role - semantic role of the person who receives something

Etymology

From Latin benefactum (a good deed) + 'role', meaning function or part played.
