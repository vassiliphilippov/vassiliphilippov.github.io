Geiger tube

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪɡɚ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪɡə tjuːb / (BrE)
A tube containing an ionization chamber used in a Geiger counter to detect radiation.

Examples

After titration of the liberated * * f with * * f, aliquots of the aqueous and of the organic phase were counted in a solution type Geiger tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Geiger counter - an instrument for detecting and measuring ionizing radiation

Etymology

Named after Hans Geiger, German physicist, who co-invented the Geiger counter.
