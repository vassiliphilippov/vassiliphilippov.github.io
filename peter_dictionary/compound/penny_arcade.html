penny arcade

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ɑrˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ɑːˈkeɪd / (BrE)
An entertainment venue featuring a variety of coin-operated machines and games, popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

Motion-picture exhibitions took place in stores in a general atmosphere like that of the penny arcade which can still be found in such urban areas as Times Square.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcade - a place with many amusement machines and games

Etymology

From 'penny' (unit of currency) and 'arcade' (a passage or area with amusements and shops), referring to the original cost and nature of the entertainment.
