power company

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊər ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a company that supplies electricity to the public as a utility service

Examples

Manchester then had two competing power companies until 1904, when the Manchester Light and Power Company purchased the transmission system of the Vail Company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utility company - a company providing basic services like water, gas, or electricity
