English muffin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈmʌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈmʌfɪn / (BrE)
A small, round, flat bread roll, typically sliced horizontally, toasted, and eaten with butter or other toppings.

Examples

The innocence that they tried to conceal at the beginning is clearly destroyed forever when one of them, asking for a piece of lemon-meringue pie, gets a plate of English muffins instead.

Etymology

So named because the style of muffin originated in the United Kingdom.
