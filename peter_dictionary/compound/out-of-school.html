out-of-school

1. ADJ

/ ˌaʊt əv ˈskuːl / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈskuːl / (BrE)
Not attending school; used to describe children, youth, or others who are no longer or not currently enrolled in school.

Examples

Adult education courses, work-study programs of various sorts - these are all evidence of a continuing interest of the schools in furthering educational opportunities for out-of-school youth.

Synset Examples

opportunities for out-of-school youth

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: truant - a student who stays away from school without leave or explanation
