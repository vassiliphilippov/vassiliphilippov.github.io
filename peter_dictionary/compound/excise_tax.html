excise tax

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.saɪz tæks / (AmE), / ˈɛk.saɪz tæks / (BrE)
A tax levied on certain goods, services, or transactions and measured by the amount of business done, rather than on property or income.

Examples

One, by Sen. Louis Crump of San Saba, would aid more than 17000 retailers who pay a group of miscellaneous excise taxes by eliminating the requirement that each return be notarized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sales tax - a tax on sales or on the receipts from sales
  • Antonym: income tax - tax levied on income, especially on an individual's earnings

Etymology

From 'excise', ultimately from Dutch 'accijs' or Old French 'excise', meaning 'tax'.
