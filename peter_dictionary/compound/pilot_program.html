pilot program

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət ˌproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət ˌprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
A small-scale, preliminary study or project carried out to evaluate feasibility, duration, cost, and potential outcomes before full-scale implementation.

Examples

Other pilot programs were conducted by A + S, Babylon, New York; J. L. Hudson, Detroit; Joseph Horne, Pittsburgh.

Synset Examples

they funded a pilot project in six states

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial run - an experiment or test to assess feasibility or performance
  • Synonym: prototype - an original model or first version used as a basis for further development

Etymology

From 'pilot' (serving as a test) + 'program.' 'Pilot' in this sense comes from the idea of guiding or testing a course.
