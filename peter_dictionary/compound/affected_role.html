affected role

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛktɪd roʊl / (AmE), / əˈfɛktɪd rəʊl / (BrE)
In linguistics, the semantic role of an entity in a clause that is not the agent but is directly influenced or changed by the action of the verb.

Examples

Look what'’s happened to us, Mike.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agent - the semantic role of the entity that performs the action in a clause

Etymology

From 'affect', ultimately from Latin 'afficere' (to influence), and 'role', from French 'rôle' (part played).
