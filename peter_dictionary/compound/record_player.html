record player

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌpleɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌpleɪə / (BrE)
A machine used for playing vinyl records, in which a rotating turntable moves the record while a needle (stylus) tracks the grooves and the sound is amplified.

Examples

The portable record player with a pile of classical records beside it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turntable - rotating platform on which a record is played
  • Synonym: phonograph - original machine for recording and reproducing sound; early record player

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd ˌpleɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd ˌpleɪə / (BrE)
A machine for playing vinyl records, in which a rotating disc moves a stylus that produces sound, usually amplified electrically or acoustically.

Examples

So, they let you tote that record player down there, huh?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turntable - device on which a record is played; sometimes means the whole record player
  • Synonym: gramophone - early device for playing records, acoustic not electronic
