all-important

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔl ɪmˈpɔrtənt / (AmE), / ˌɔːl ɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
Extremely important; more important than anything else.

Examples

So all-important are ideas, we are told, that persons successful in business and happy in social life usually fall into two classes: those who invent new ideas of their own, and those who borrow, beg, or steal from others.
Merely having a mental image of some sort is not the all-important consideration.

Synset Examples

the all-important subject of disarmament
crucial information
in chess cool nerves are of the essence

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: important - of great significance or value
  • Synonym: crucial - decisive or critical, especially in success or failure
  • Synonym: vital - absolutely necessary or essential
