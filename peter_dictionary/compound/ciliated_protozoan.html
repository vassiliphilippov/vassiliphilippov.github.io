ciliated protozoan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlieɪtɪd proʊˈtoʊzoʊæn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlieɪtɪd prəʊˈtəʊzəʊæn / (BrE)
a protozoan characterized by having cilia (tiny hair-like structures) on its surface, used for movement and feeding

Examples

Initially, the flagellated protozoa predominated, but they soon gave way to the free swimming ciliated protozoa.
As the temperature decreased, the number of free swimming ciliated protozoa decreased.
The predomination of free swimming ciliated protozoa is indicative of a high bacterial population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ciliate - a protozoan with cilia

Etymology

From Latin 'cilium' (eyelash, small hair) and 'protozoan' from Greek 'protos' (first) + 'zoon' (animal).
