chevaux-de-frise

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˌvoʊ də ˈfriːz / (AmE), / ʃəˌvəʊ də ˈfriːz / (BrE)
A defensive barrier made of spikes or barbed wire fixed to a movable frame, used to obstruct the advance of cavalry or vehicles.

Examples

The chevaux de frise, those sharp stakes and barriers around the fort at the Battery, pointed to a conflict between the town and sea power rolling in glassy swells as the tide came in.

Etymology

From French 'chevaux de frise' ('Frisian horses'), originally referring to obstacles used in Frisia (now Friesland, Netherlands).
