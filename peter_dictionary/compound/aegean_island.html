Aegean island

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒiən ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / iːˈdʒiː.ən ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
an island located in the Aegean Sea, between Greece and Turkey

Examples

The potters of the Aegean islands thus stood apart from those of the mainland, and in Greece itself Argive, Corinthian, Attic, Boeotian, and other Geometric sequences have each their own hallmarks.

Etymology

Aegean: from Latin Aegaeus, from Greek Aigaios (of the Aegean Sea); island: Old English īegland (island).
