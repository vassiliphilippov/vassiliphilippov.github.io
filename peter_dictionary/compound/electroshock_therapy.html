electroshock therapy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈʃɑk ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈʃɒk ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
A medical treatment for certain psychiatric disorders in which a strong electric current is passed through the brain to induce a brief seizure.

Examples

This explains the beneficial effect of electroshock therapy in certain depressions and a shift in the reaction from hypo - to normal reactivity of the sympathetic system as shown by the Mecholyl test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - Formal term for electroshock therapy; same treatment method for psychiatric disorders

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + shock + therapy.
