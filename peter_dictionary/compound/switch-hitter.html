switch-hitter

1. NOUN

/ swɪtʃ ˈhɪtɚ / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ˈhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a player who is able to bat both right-handed and left-handed.

Examples

Mantle, the bull-necked blond switch-hitter, had one sensational triple-crown season, 1959, when he batted .365 and also led the American League in home runs, 52, and RBIs, 130.

Etymology

From 'switch', meaning to change, and 'hitter', someone who hits (in baseball).
