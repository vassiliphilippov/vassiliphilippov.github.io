major-league club

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdʒɚ liːg klʌb / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə liːg klʌb / (BrE)
A professional sports team that plays in a major league, especially in baseball or other organized sports leagues at the highest level.

Examples

The Orioles tonight retained the distinction of being the only winless team among the eighteen Major-League clubs as they dropped their sixth straight spring exhibition decision, this one to the Kansas City Athletics by a score of 5 to 3.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: major-league team - a team that plays in a major league
  • Antonym: minor-league club - a team that plays in a minor league

Etymology

From the combination of 'major league' (the highest level of professional sports competition) and 'club' (an organization or team).
