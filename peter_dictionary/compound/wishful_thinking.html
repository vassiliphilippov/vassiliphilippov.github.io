wishful thinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃfəl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃfəl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
The mistaken belief that one's desires or wishes reflect reality, rather than facts.

Examples

I suppose the reason is a kind of wishful thinking: do n't talk about the final stages of Reconstruction and they will take care of themselves.
The inspector knew as he left that this was wishful thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusion - false belief or impression, often despite evidence
