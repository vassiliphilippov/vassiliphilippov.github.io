Gothic romancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːθɪk roʊˈmænsər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk rəʊˈmɑːnsə / (BrE)
a writer of Gothic romances, especially active in the 18th and 19th centuries

Examples

Evidence is plentiful that early and later also he has been indebted to the Gothic romancers, who deal in extravagant horror, to the symbolists writing at the end of the preceding century, and in particular to the stream-of-consciousness novelists, Henry James and James Joyce among them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gothic novelist - writer of Gothic novels

Etymology

From Gothic (relating to a literary genre characterized by horror and romance) + romancer (one who writes romances).
