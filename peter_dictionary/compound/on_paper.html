on paper

1. ADV

/ ɑn ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
As written, printed, or formally recorded, rather than in reality or practice.

Examples

Steinberg seems to have gone directly back to the score, discounting tradition, and has built his performance on the intention to reproduce as faithfully as possible exactly what Brahms set down on paper.
(It makes no real difference that Braque's collage is on paper and eked out in charcoal, while Picasso's is on canvas and eked out in oil.)
To do this, set up a plan on paper for operating the farm.
A man with a sketch pad in hand sat with a large pink woman in a small office at the end of a long, dim corridor and made pencil lines on paper and said, "Is this more like it, Mrs. MacReady?

Synset Examples

this is exactly what the composer had set down on paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in theory - in a way that exists only as an idea, not in reality
  • Antonym: in practice - as it actually happens rather than as described

2. ADV

/ ɑn ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
in writing or according to written records, rather than in reality or practice

Examples

Until that moment, he didn't exist, except on paper.

Synset Examples

this is exactly what the composer had set down on paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in theory - as something is supposed or planned, but not always in practice
  • Antonym: in practice - in reality; how something actually happens
