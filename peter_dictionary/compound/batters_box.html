batter's box

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætərz ˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈbætəz ˌbɒks / (BrE)
an area marked on either side of home plate on a baseball field, within which the batter must stand when batting

Examples

He got back into the batter's box and on the next pitch dropped into the dirt again.
Mike was slow getting into the box this time.
He stood flat-footed in the box, but not very close to the plate now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on-deck circle - area where the next batter waits and warms up
