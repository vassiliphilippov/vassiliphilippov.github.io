left-of-center

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɛft əv ˈsɛntɚ / (AmE), / ˌlɛft əv ˈsɛntə / (BrE)
having political views that are moderately liberal or leaning toward the political left, but not extremely radical

Examples

This is puzzling to an outsider conscious of the classic tradition of liberalism, because it is clear that these Democrats who are left-of-center are at opposite poles from the liberal Jefferson, who held that the best government was the least government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-of-center - having political views that are moderately conservative or leaning toward the political right
