square root

1. NOUN

/ skwɛr rut / (AmE), / skweə ruːt / (BrE)
a number that, when multiplied by itself, gives a specified number

Examples

The greater the depth of the water, the greater is the speed of the wave; Lagrange's law says that its velocity is equal to the square root of the product of the depth times the acceleration due to gravity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cube root - a number that, when multiplied by itself twice, gives a specified number

Etymology

From 'square' (mathematical term for a number multiplied by itself) and 'root' (from Latin radix, meaning root or origin).
