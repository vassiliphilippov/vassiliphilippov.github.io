second person

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkənd ˈpɝsən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
The grammatical category of pronouns and verb forms used to address the person or people being spoken to, such as 'you' in English.

Examples

I swear I'’ll make it up to you.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first person - grammatical category referring to the speaker ('I', 'we')
  • Antonym: third person - grammatical category referring to someone other than the speaker/addressed ('he', 'she', 'they')

Etymology

From Latin 'secundus' (second) + 'persona' (person).
