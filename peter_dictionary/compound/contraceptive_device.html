contraceptive device

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈsɛp.tɪv dɪˈvaɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈsɛp.tɪv dɪˈvaɪs / (BrE)
A device or agent intended to prevent pregnancy by interfering with conception.

Examples

In New York City in 1958, the city's Commissioner of Hospitals refused to permit a physician to provide a Protestant mother with a contraceptive device.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraceptive - an agent or method to prevent conception

Etymology

From Latin 'contra' (against) + 'concipere' (to conceive).
