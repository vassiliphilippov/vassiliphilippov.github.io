synthetic cubism

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (BrE)
the later phase of Cubism, developed in the early 1910s, characterized by simpler shapes, brighter colors, and the use of collage elements

Examples

Analytical Cubism came to an end in the collage, but not conclusively; nor did Synthetic Cubism fully begin there.
Synthetic Cubism began with Picasso alone, late in 1913 or early in 1914; this was the point at which he finally took the lead in Cubist innovation away from Braque, never again to relinquish it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analytical cubism - early phase of Cubism focusing on breaking objects into components

Etymology

From 'synthetic' (combining elements), indicating the constructive aspect of this Cubist phase; 'Cubism' from French 'cubisme'.
