balm of Gilead

1. NOUN

/ bɑm əv ˈɡɪliæd / (AmE), / bɑːm əv ˈɡɪliæd / (BrE)
A medium-sized fir tree (Abies balsamea) native to northeastern North America, with aromatic leaves and resin, often used for pulpwood and as Christmas trees.

Examples

In the center of any open space remaining our grandfathers had planted syringa and sweet shrub, snowball, rose-of-Sharon and balm-of-Gilead.

Etymology

The name refers to the biblical region of Gilead, once famous for its healing balsam; used for North American fir due to its fragrant resin.
