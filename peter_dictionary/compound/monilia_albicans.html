Monilia albicans

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɪliə ælˈbaɪkænz / (AmE), / məˈnɪlɪə ælˈbeɪkænz / (BrE)
a species of parasitic fungus that can infect the mouth, skin, intestines, or vagina, causing candidiasis

Examples

Cultures taken from the jejunum yielded Monilia albicans, Pseudomonas pyocanea, Aerobacter aerogenes, and Streptococcus anhemolyticus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Candida albicans - current scientific name for Monilia albicans, a fungus causing candidiasis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Monilia (genus name, from Latin monile 'necklace') + specific epithet albicans (from Latin 'becoming white').
