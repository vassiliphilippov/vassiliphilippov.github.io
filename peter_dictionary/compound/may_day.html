May Day

1. NOUN

/ meɪ deɪ / (AmE), / meɪ deɪ / (BrE)
A holiday celebrated on May 1st in many countries, traditionally marking the coming of spring; also observed as International Workers' Day in some countries.

Examples

King Muhammad /5, was known to be most sympathetic to the formation of local self-government and made the first firm promise of elections on May Day, 1957.

Etymology

Named for the first day of May, with roots in ancient European spring festivals and later associated with labor movements in the late 19th century.
