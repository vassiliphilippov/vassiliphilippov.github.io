chemical substance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪkəl ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
A material with a distinct molecular composition that is produced by or used in a chemical reaction.

Examples

Napier's cleaning out Axis Chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound - a substance formed from two or more elements chemically united
  • Antonym: element - a pure chemical substance consisting of one type of atom

Etymology

Chemical from Medieval Latin *chemicalis* (relating to alchemy), substance from Latin *substantia* (being, essence).
