electrical shock

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl ʃɒk / (BrE)
A sudden bodily reaction caused by the passage of electric current through the body.

Examples

A mild electrical shock served as a conditioned stimulus and was followed by feeding.

Synset Examples

subjects received a small electric shock when they made the wrong response
electricians get accustomed to occasional shocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric shock - sudden bodily reaction to electric current
