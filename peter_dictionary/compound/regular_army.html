regular army

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələr ˈɑrmi / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
The permanent, professional military land forces of a nation or state, distinct from reserve or volunteer forces.

Examples

The troop received a new leader, Lieutenant Robert M. Carroll, fresh out of ROTC and bucking for Regular Army status.
One of the more seriously wounded was Lieutenant Carroll, the young officer bucking for the Regular Army.
Today he is a major - in the Regular Army.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: militia - a military force composed of ordinary citizens, not professional soldiers
  • Antonym: reserve forces - military units composed of part-time soldiers who can be mobilized when needed
