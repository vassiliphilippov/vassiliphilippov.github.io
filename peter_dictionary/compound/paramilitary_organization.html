paramilitary organization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈmɪlɪtəri ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A group of civilians organized in a military style, often to supplement or operate alongside regular armed forces.

Examples

Friends, a picture magazine distributed by Chevrolet dealers, describes a paramilitary organization of employees of the Gulf Telephone Company at Foley, Alabama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militia - a military force made up of ordinary citizens
  • Antonym: army - the regular armed forces of a country

Etymology

From 'paramilitary' (from Greek para- 'beside, resembling' + 'military') combined with 'organization'.
