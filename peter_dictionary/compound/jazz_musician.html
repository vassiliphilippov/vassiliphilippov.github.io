jazz musician

1. NOUN

/ dʒæz mjuˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒæz mjuˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A person who plays or composes jazz music, either as a performer or composer.

Examples

They were not diplomats or jazz musicians, or even organizers of reading-rooms and photo montage displays, but rugged capitalist entrepreneurs like Henry Ford, Hugh Cooper, Thomas Campbell, the International Harvester Co., and David W. Griffith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musician - person who plays a musical instrument or is musically talented
  • Synonym: jazz band - a group that performs jazz music
