hot-water heater

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑt ˈwɔtɚ ˌhitɚ / (AmE), / ˌhɒt ˈwɔːtə ˌhiːtə / (BrE)
A device consisting of a heater and storage tank used to supply heated water, typically to plumbing fixtures in a building.

Examples

He went down the hall to Eugene's bathroom, to turn on the hot-water heater, and on the side of the tub he saw a pair of blue wool swimming trunks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water heater - appliance for heating water
