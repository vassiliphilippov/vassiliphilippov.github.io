relative humidity

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv hjuːˈmɪdəti / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv hjuːˈmɪdəti / (BrE)
The amount of water vapor in the air, expressed as a percentage of the maximum amount the air could hold at a given temperature.

Examples

There was a slight lapse condition, a moderate fog, and 100 per cent relative humidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humidity - the amount of water vapor in the air

Etymology

From Latin 'relativus' (relative) and Latin 'humiditas' (moisture, dampness).
