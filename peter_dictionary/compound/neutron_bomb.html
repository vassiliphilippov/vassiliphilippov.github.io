neutron bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuˌtrɑn bɑm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrɒn bɒm / (BrE)
A nuclear weapon designed to release a large number of lethal neutrons with relatively little explosive blast, intended to maximize harm to living beings while minimizing structural damage.

Examples

So be it - then we must embark on a crash program for 200 - megaton bombs of the common or hydrogen variety, and neutron bombs, which do not exist but are said to be the coming thing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic bomb - a bomb that derives its destructive power from nuclear fission reactions
  • Degree Higher: hydrogen bomb - a nuclear weapon that uses fusion of isotopes of hydrogen

Etymology

Formed from 'neutron' (from Latin 'neutrum', meaning 'neither', and '-on', a particle suffix) and 'bomb' (from French 'bombe', from Italian 'bomba').
