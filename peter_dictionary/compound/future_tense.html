future tense

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuː.tʃɚ ˌtɛns / (AmE), / ˈfjuː.tʃə ˌtɛns / (BrE)
The grammatical tense used to describe actions or states that will happen after the present time.

Examples

The Nacionale will go to the Lakeville Road Boys, the Capri to the Corleone family, the Sevilla Biltmore also, but Eddie Levine of Newport will bring in the Pennino brothers, Dino and Eddie, for a piece and also to handle the actual casino operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present tense - verb tense for current actions or states
  • Antonym: past tense - verb tense for actions or states in the past

Etymology

From Latin 'futurus' (about to be) and 'tempus' (time, tense).
