electric discharge

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the flow of electricity through a gas, often resulting in the emission of light or other forms of energy, caused by the movement of charged particles under an electric field

Examples

It was with the assistance of one of the members of this expedition, Lauritz Esmarch, that Oersted succeeded in producing light by creating an electric discharge in mercury vapor through which an electric current was made to flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arc - a visible electric discharge between two electrodes
  • Synonym: spark - a short, bright electric discharge
