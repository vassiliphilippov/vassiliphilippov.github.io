ultraviolet illumination

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌɪl.jəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌɪl.jʊˈmɪə.ʃən / (BrE)
illumination provided by ultraviolet radiation; the use of ultraviolet light to make objects visible or to reveal properties not seen under visible light

Examples

Where the microscope under visible light may show only vague shadows or nothing at all, ultraviolet illumination and subsequent translation into a color TV picture reveal a wealth of detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultraviolet light - light with wavelengths shorter than visible light, but longer than X rays
  • Degree Lower: visible light - light visible to the human eye
  • Degree Higher: X-ray - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than ultraviolet light

Etymology

From ultraviolet ('beyond violet', from Latin 'ultra' meaning 'beyond' + 'violet') and illumination (from Latin 'illuminatio', from 'illuminare', 'to light up').
