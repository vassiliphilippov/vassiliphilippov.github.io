trompe l'oeil

1. NOUN

/ tɹoʊmp lɔɪ / (AmE), / tɹɒmp lɔɪ / (BrE)
a style of painting that is so realistic in detail as to create the illusion that depicted objects exist in three dimensions

Examples

The trompe-l'oeil illusion here is no longer enclosed between parallel flatnesses, but seems to thrust through the surface of the drawing paper and establish depth on top of it.

Etymology

From French 'trompe l'œil', literally 'deceives the eye'.
