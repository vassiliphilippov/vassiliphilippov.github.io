chuck wagon

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌk ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / tʃʌk ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A wagon equipped with cooking facilities and food supplies, used especially in the past to feed cowboys on cattle drives.

Examples

He 'd started a fire and put coffee on, and now was busy at the work board of his chuck wagon.

Etymology

From 'chuck' (slang for food) + 'wagon,' originating in the American West in the late 19th century.
