first moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
In statistics and mathematics, the arithmetic mean of a set of values; the sum of the values of a random variable divided by the number of values.

Examples

Field shifts were derived from the mean value of the resonance line, defined as the field about which the first moment is zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - the sum of values divided by the number of values
  • Antonym: median - the middle value in a list of numbers
  • Degree Higher: second moment - the expected value of the square of deviations; related to variance

Etymology

From the mathematical use of 'moment', meaning a quantitative measure, and 'first' indicating the order.
