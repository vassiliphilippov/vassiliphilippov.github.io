Orthodox Catholic Church

1. PROPN

/ ˌɔrθəˌdɑks ˈkæθəlɪk tʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˌdɒks ˈkæθəlɪk tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The branch of Christianity derived from the Byzantine tradition, comprising churches that follow the Byzantine rites and maintain continuity with the early church, commonly known as the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Examples

With the exception of the Roman Catholic and the Orthodox Catholic Churches, most churches make no moral distinction between rhythm and mechanical or chemical contraceptives, allowing the couple free choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - A synonym for Orthodox Catholic Church, the main Eastern Christian church
