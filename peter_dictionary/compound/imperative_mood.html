imperative mood

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrətɪv muːd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv mjuːd / (BrE)
The grammatical mood used to express commands, requests, or instructions.

Examples

Why don't you tell that nice girl you love her?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indicative mood - grammatical mood for statements and questions
  • Antonym: subjunctive mood - grammatical mood for hypothetical or non-real actions

Etymology

From Latin 'imperativus' (commanding), from 'imperare' (to command), and 'mood' from Old English 'mod' (frame of mind, grammar).
