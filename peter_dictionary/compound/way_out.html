way out

1. NOUN

/ weɪ aʊt / (AmE), / weɪ aʊt / (BrE)
An opening, passage, or means of departure, especially as a means of escape or exit from a place or situation.

Examples

So far as I can see, there is only one way out for the positivist.
But Duclos thought he saw a way out.

Synset Examples

he blocked the way out
the canyon had only one issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit - a way through which one can leave a place
  • Antonym: entrance - an opening or passage for entering a place

2. NOUN

/ weɪ aʊt / (AmE), / weɪ aʊt / (BrE)
an exit or opening that allows escape or leaving a place

Examples

It'’s the only way out of here tonight.

Synset Examples

he blocked the way out
the canyon had only one issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exit - a way out of a place; an opening for leaving
