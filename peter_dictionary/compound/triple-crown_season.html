triple-crown season

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl kraʊn ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəl kraʊn ˈsiːzən / (BrE)
A baseball season during which a player wins the triple crown by leading the league in batting average, home runs, and runs batted in (RBIs).

Examples

Mantle, the bull-necked blond switch-hitter, had one sensational triple-crown season, 1959, when he batted .365 and also led the American League in home runs, 52, and RBIs, 130.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triple crown - achievement of leading the league in batting average, home runs, and RBIs in a single season (baseball)

Etymology

From 'triple crown' (achievement of leading in three statistical categories), originally from horse racing, combined with 'season'.
