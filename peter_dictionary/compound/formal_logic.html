formal logic

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrməl ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməl ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
the branch of logic that studies the structures and principles of reasoning abstracted from content, focusing on criteria for consistency and validity

Examples

But the inner, formal logic of Cubism, as it worked itself out through the collage, had just as much to do with shaping their decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic logic - the branch of logic involving symbols and formal systems
  • Antonym: informal logic - the branch of logic concerning everyday reasoning and arguments

Etymology

formal from Latin 'formalis' (of form) + logic from Greek 'logikē' (science of reasoning)
