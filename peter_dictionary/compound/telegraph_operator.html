telegraph operator

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌɡræf ˈɑpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf ˈɒpəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who transmits and receives messages by telegraph, operating telegraph equipment to communicate over long distances

Examples

A band of robbers enters a railroad station, overpowers and ties up the telegraph operator, holds up the train and escapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegrapher - a person who operates a telegraph; synonym for telegraph operator
  • Degree Higher: radio operator - person who operates a radio communication system

Etymology

From 'telegraph' (Greek 'tele' meaning 'far' and 'graphein' meaning 'to write') + 'operator'.
