corpuscular-radiation pressure

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈpʌlskjələr reɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / kɔːˈpʌlskjʊlə reɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
The very small pressure exerted on a surface that is perpendicular to the direction of propagation of a wave, caused by the impact of particles (corpuscles) associated with the radiation.

Examples

The solar electromagnetic - and corpuscular-radiation pressure and the associated Poynting-Robertson effect increase in effectiveness as the particle size decreases and modify the distribution and limit sizes to larger than a few microns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation pressure - the pressure exerted by electromagnetic radiation on a surface

Etymology

From 'corpuscular', referring to particles, and 'radiation pressure', a physics term for the pressure exerted by electromagnetic or particle radiation.
