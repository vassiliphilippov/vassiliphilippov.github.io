trichloroacetic acid

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌklɔroʊəˈsiːtɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / traɪˌklɔːrəʊəˈsiːtɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
A strong, crystalline acid (C2Cl3O2H) produced by chlorinating acetic acid, used in biochemistry and medicine, for example as a reagent and cauterizing agent.

Examples

The first attempts to isolate TSH came a decade later, when Janssen and Loeser (1931) used trichloroacetic acid to separate the soluble TSH from insoluble impurities.

Etymology

Formed from 'tri-' (three) + 'chloro' (chlorine) + 'acetic acid', indicating three chlorine atoms in the acetic acid molecule.
