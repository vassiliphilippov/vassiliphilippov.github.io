gamma globulin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə ˈɡlɑbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡamə ˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
A plasma protein composed of immunoglobulins responsible for immune responses; often used in medicine to refer to antibodies in the blood.

Examples

Fahey and Morrison used a single, continuous gradient at constant pH for the fractionation of anti - A and anti - B agglutinins from preisolated | g-globulin samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - a class of proteins functioning as antibodies

Etymology

From Greek 'gamma' (the third letter of the Greek alphabet) + 'globulin,' a diminutive of Latin 'globulus' meaning small sphere.
