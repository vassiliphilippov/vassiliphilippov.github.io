grade-constructed

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪd kənˌstrʌktɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪd kənˌstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
(of a road or structure) built at ground level, rather than elevated or underground.

Examples

The new interchange among the four Los Angeles freeways, including the grade-constructed accesses, occupies by itself no less than eighty acres of downtown land, one-eighth of a square mile, an area about the size of Rockefeller Center in New York.

Synset Examples

grade-constructed accesses to the freeway

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elevated - (of roads/structures) raised above ground level

Etymology

From 'grade' (ground level) + 'constructed' (built).
