solitary confinement

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːləˌtɛri kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪtəri kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
The condition of being kept alone in a cell as a form of punishment, especially in prison, with little or no contact with other prisoners.

Examples

A week in the hole is like a year.

Synset Examples

he was held in solitary
