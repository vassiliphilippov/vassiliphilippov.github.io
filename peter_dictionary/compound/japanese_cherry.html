Japanese cherry

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæniz ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
An ornamental tree (Prunus serrulata), widely cultivated for its showy white or pink blossoms, particularly in Japan and elsewhere; its fruits are inedible.

Examples

And the dogwood in early May, for Miss Ada's alfresco party; and after that the Japanese cherries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sakura - Japanese term for the Japanese cherry tree or its blossoms
