vice versa

1. ADV

/ ˌvaɪs ˈvɜrsə / (AmE), / ˌvaɪs ˈvɜːsə / (BrE)
Used to indicate that the reverse of what has been said is also true.

Examples

Likewise, a charged particle will tend to stick to an uncharged surface and vice versa, and a charged particle will be very strongly attracted to a surface exhibiting an opposite charge.

Synset Examples

she hates him and vice versa

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'the position being reversed'.
