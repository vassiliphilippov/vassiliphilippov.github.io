old wives' tale

1. NOUN

/ oʊld waɪvz teɪl / (AmE), / əʊld waɪvz teɪl / (BrE)
a traditional belief, story, or idea that is commonly regarded as unscientific or false and passed down by word of mouth

Examples

They were further stripped of old wives' tales by seeing the slender, lovely Russian girls performing feats requiring tremendous strength - and with not one bulging muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - a traditional story or widely held but false belief
  • Synonym: superstition - an irrational belief usually founded on ignorance or fear

Etymology

Refers to traditional stories supposedly told by older women; attested in English since the 16th century.
