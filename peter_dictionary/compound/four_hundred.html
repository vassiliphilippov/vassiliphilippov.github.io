Four Hundred

1. NOUN

/ fɔr ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / fɔː ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
The most fashionable or socially prominent group of people in a particular city, especially referring to New York City's social elite in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

Four hundred and fifty pounds was disseminated while the ship was traveling a distance of 156 miles.

Etymology

Refers to the reputed number of people who made up New York City's social elite, as defined by Ward McAllister in the late 19th century.
