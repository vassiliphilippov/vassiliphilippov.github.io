bird shot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝd ˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːd ˌʃɒt / (BrE)
Small spherical pellets, usually made of lead, used in shotgun shells for shooting birds and other small game.

Examples

My Cousin Simmons carried a musket, but he had loaded it with bird shot, and as the officer came opposite him, he rose up behind the wall and fired.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: buckshot - larger shotgun pellets used for hunting big game
  • Synonym: shot - projectiles fired from guns, especially pellets in ammunition

Etymology

From 'bird' + 'shot', referring to its use in hunting birds.
