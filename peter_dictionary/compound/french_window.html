French window

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˈwɪndoʊ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈwɪndəʊ / (BrE)
A pair of glazed doors, usually opening onto a garden, balcony, or patio, set in an exterior wall.

Examples

In the ballroom below, the dark had given way to moonlight coming in through the bank of French windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French door - a pair of doors with glass panes extending for most of their length

Etymology

Named after 17th-century French architectural style, combining 'French' and 'window'.
