bubble gum

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌb.əl ˌɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl ˌɡʌm / (BrE)
A type of chewy, flavored gum designed to be blown into bubbles.

Examples

Stengel probably preached too much in the early days when the kid wanted to pop his bubble gum and sow his oats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chewing gum - sweetened gum for chewing, not meant for blowing bubbles
