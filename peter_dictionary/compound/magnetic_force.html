magnetic force

1. NOUN

/ məɡˈnɛɾɪk fɔrs / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk fɔːs / (BrE)
the force exerted by magnets or by electric currents that attracts or repels certain materials, especially iron, and is characterized by magnetic fields

Examples

Continuous motion of the arc contact area at the anode by flow or magnetic forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces between objects

Etymology

From 'magnetic', from Greek *magnetis* (lodestone), and 'force', from Latin *fortis* (strong).
