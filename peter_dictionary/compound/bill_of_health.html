bill of health

1. NOUN

/ bɪl əv hɛlθ / (AmE), / bɪl əv hɛlθ / (BrE)
an official certificate indicating that a departing ship's crew is free from contagious disease, to be presented at its port of arrival

Examples

For Mrs. Shaefer - who had been given a clean bill of health by her own physician at the time she visited Lee - and her friend were agents for the California Pure Food and Drug Inspection Bureau.

Etymology

From 'bill' (official document) and 'health'; refers to health certificates issued at ports.
