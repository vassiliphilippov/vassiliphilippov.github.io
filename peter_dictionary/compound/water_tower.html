water tower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚ ˌtaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌtaʊə / (BrE)
A tall structure with a large tank for storing water at a height, to provide pressure for a water supply system.

Examples

The town was about what Wilson expected: one main street with its rows of false-fronted buildings, a water tower, a few warehouses, a single hotel; all dusty and sunbaked.
