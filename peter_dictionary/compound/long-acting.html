long-acting

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋ ˌæktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ ˌæktɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a medicine, drug, or hormone) having an effect that lasts for a long period of time.

Examples

The importance of knowing in what chemical forms the hormone may exist is accentuated by the recent observation that there exists an abnormally long-acting TSH in blood drawn from many thyrotoxic patients (Adams, 1958).

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-acting - having an effect that lasts for a short period of time (especially of drugs)
