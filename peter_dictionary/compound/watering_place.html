watering place

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərɪŋ pleɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtərɪŋ pleɪs / (BrE)
A health resort or leisure destination located near a spring or by the seaside, frequented for rest, recreation, or health benefits.

Examples

But it should be pointed out that some of the new watering places - Fire Island, Nantucket, Westhampton, Long Island, for example - tend to be homogeneously Jewish.

Etymology

From the verb 'to water' (to supply with water), referring to a place to take water; later used figuratively for resorts by water sources.
