cerulean blue

1. NOUN

/ səˈruːliən bluː / (AmE), / sɪˈruːlɪən bluː / (BrE)
A light greenish-blue pigment, consisting essentially of oxides of cobalt and tin, used especially in painting.

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobalt blue - a bright blue pigment made from cobalt salts
  • Synonym: ultramarine - a vivid blue pigment originally made from lapis lazuli

Etymology

Cerulean from Latin 'caeruleus' meaning 'sky blue'.
