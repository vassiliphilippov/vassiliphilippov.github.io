all together

1. ADV

/ ɔl təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɔːl təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
All at the same time; all in one group or collectively.

Examples

Throughout the history of the program, state government expenditures in the aggregate have usually matched or exceeded the Federal expenditures, while local districts all together have spent more than either Federal or state governments.

Synset Examples

Let's say `Yes!' all at once

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altogether - completely; entirely

2. ADV

/ ɔl təˈgɛðər / (AmE), / ɔːl təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
used to indicate that everyone or everything in a group does something as a single unit or is in the same place at the same time, especially when 'all' and 'together' can be separated by other words.

Examples

I thought of getting a board together.

Synset Examples

they were herded all together
they were all herded together
the books lay all together in a heap
the books all lay together...

Related Terms

  • Antonym: altogether - completely; entirely
