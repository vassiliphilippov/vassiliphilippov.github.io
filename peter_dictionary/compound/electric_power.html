electric power

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈpaʊɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
The rate at which electrical energy is transferred by an electric circuit, calculated as the product of voltage and current.

Examples

Even if the electric power fails after an attack, any time that the heater has been used will make the shelter that much more comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrical energy - energy resulting from electric current or fields
