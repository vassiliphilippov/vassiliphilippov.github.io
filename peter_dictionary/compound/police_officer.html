police officer

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a member of a police force, especially one whose job is to prevent crime and enforce laws

Examples

This gentleman is a police officer.

Synset Examples

it was an accident, officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policeman - a male police officer
  • Synonym: policewoman - a female police officer

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlis ˌɑfəsər / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˌɒfɪsə / (BrE)
A member of the police force who is responsible for enforcing the law and maintaining public order.

Examples

If an unlucky accident should befall him, if he should be shot by a police officer, or if he should hang himself in his jail cell, or if he's struck by a bolt of lightning, then I'm going to blame some of the people in this room.

Synset Examples

it was an accident, officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policeman - male member of a police force
  • Synonym: policewoman - female member of a police force
  • Synonym: detective - police officer who investigates crimes

Etymology

From 'police' (French police, from Latin politia ‘civil administration’) + 'officer' (from Old French officier, from Latin officium ‘duty’).
