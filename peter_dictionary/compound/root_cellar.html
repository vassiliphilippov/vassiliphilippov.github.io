root cellar

1. NOUN

/ rut ˈsɛlər / (AmE), / ruːt ˈsɛlə / (BrE)
An underground room or excavation, often built into the side of a hill or covered with earth, used for storing root vegetables, fruits, and other produce to keep them cool and fresh.

Examples

It was a long, low room, like a root cellar, for it was banked up with soil, and vines had run rampant over that, too.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cellar - a room below ground level used for storage, especially of food or wine
