in the beginning

1. ADV

/ ɪn ðə bəˈɡɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
At the start of something; referencing the origin or first stage of an event or process.

Examples

That parallelism affects his picture of man's disobedience too; for as it was Christ, the Word of God, who came to rescue man, so it was disobedience to the word of God in the beginning that brought death into the world, and all our woe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at first - initially; in the first stage
  • Antonym: in the end - ultimately; finally

2. ADV

/ ɪn ðə bəˈɡɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪn ðə bɪˈɡɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
At the start; at the point when something first started happening or was first created.

Examples

More potent a charm to bring back that time of life than this record of a few pictures and a few remembered facts would be a catalogue of the minutiae which are of the very stuff of the mind, intrinsic, because they were known in the beginning not by the eye alone but by the hand that held them.

Synset Examples

why didn't you tell me in the first place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at first - in or at the beginning, initially
