ransom money

1. NOUN

/ ˈrænsəm ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm ˈmʌn.i / (BrE)
money paid or demanded for the release of a kidnapped person or property

Examples

He stalled for a half-hour longer, hoping to hear something from Vecchio about the ransom money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ransom - the sum or payment demanded or paid for the release of a prisoner

Etymology

From 'ransom' (from Old French 'raençon', from Latin 'redemptio') and 'money'.
