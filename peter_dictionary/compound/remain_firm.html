remain firm

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn fɜrm / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn fɜːm / (BrE)
To continue to be steadfast, resolute, or unyielding in an opinion, position, or situation, especially in the face of difficulty or opposition.

Examples

Only in Europe have our lines remained firm - and there only on the surface.
West Germany, with its industrial and military might, reaffirmed its democracy and remains firm with the free nations.

Synset Examples

I am standing my ground and won't give in!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand firm - refuse to change your mind or position
