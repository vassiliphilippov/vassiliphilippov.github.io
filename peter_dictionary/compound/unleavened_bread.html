unleavened bread

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈlɛvənd brɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɛvənd brɛd / (BrE)
Bread made without yeast or other leavening agents, often flat and brittle; especially used in Jewish Passover rituals and some Christian traditions.

Examples

Bern demands that the Lord's Supper be administered here as it used to be, with unleavened bread.
"It was always the spirit with Christ; matters such as leavened or unleavened bread are inconsequential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matzo - a type of unleavened bread eaten by Jews during Passover

Etymology

From 'unleavened' (not containing leaven, i.e., yeast) + 'bread'. 'Leaven' from Latin levare (to raise/levitate).
