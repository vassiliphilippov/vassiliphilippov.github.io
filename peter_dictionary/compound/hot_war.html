hot war

1. NOUN

/ hɑt wɔr / (AmE), / hɒt wɔː / (BrE)
A war in which there is active combat and open hostilities between opposing parties, as opposed to a period of tension without direct fighting.

Examples

The fact is due mainly to international wars, both hot and cold.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cold war - a conflict involving political and economic hostility but without open warfare
