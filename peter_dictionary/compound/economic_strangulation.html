economic strangulation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkɑnəmɪk ˌstræŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / iːˈkɒnəmɪk ˌstræŋɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of punishing or coercing a group by cutting off their commercial or financial dealings.

Examples

Heydrich, in opening the Conference, followed the reasoning and even the phraseology of the order issued earlier by Goering which authorized the Final Solution as "a complement to "previous "solutions "for eliminating the Jews from German living space through violence, economic strangulation, forced emigration, and evacuation.

Synset Examples

the economic strangulation of the Jews by the Nazi Party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economic sanction - official penalty restricting trade or finances
  • Synonym: boycott - refusal to have commercial dealings with a group as protest
