first base

1. NOUN

/ fɝst beɪs / (AmE), / fɜːst beɪs / (BrE)
the fielding position in baseball for the player stationed at first base (the first of four bases in the infield, counting counterclockwise from home plate)

Examples

Carey singled on a slow bouncing ball to short which Robinson cut across to field and threw wide to first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: second base - the fielding position at second base in baseball
  • Synonym: third base - the fielding position at third base in baseball
  • Synonym: first baseman - the player who occupies first base in baseball
