mining bee

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪnɪŋ bi / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnɪŋ biː / (BrE)
Any of various bees of the genus Andrena, which dig tunnels in the ground to nest.

Examples

It is almost certain that some of these, usually a trifle smaller than the honeybees, are andrenas or mining bees.

Etymology

Named for their nesting behavior, digging (mining) tunnels in the ground; from 'mine' (to dig) + 'bee'.
