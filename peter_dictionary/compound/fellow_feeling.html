fellow feeling

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ ˈfiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ ˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
sympathy and shared emotional understanding with another person, especially of their sorrow or difficulties

Examples

Instead of wanting to sock the poor bastard I found myself having a fellow-feeling for him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathy - feeling of sorrow or pity for someone's misfortune
  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share another's feelings
  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others

Etymology

From 'fellow' (companion, partner) + 'feeling'; highlights the idea of shared or mutual emotion.
