pelvic girdle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlvɪk ˈɡɜrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɛlvɪk ˈɡɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
the structure of bones in the lower torso that supports the legs in humans and the hind limbs or corresponding parts in other vertebrates

Examples

At this time a detailed neuromuscular examination revealed diffuse muscle atrophy that was moderate in the hands and feet, but more marked in the shoulders, hips, and pelvic girdle, with hypoactive deep tendon reflexes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvis - the large bony frame at the base of the spine

Etymology

From Latin 'pelvis' (basin) and Old English 'gyrdel' (belt, girdle).
