slave trade

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv treɪd / (AmE), / sleɪv treɪd / (BrE)
the buying and selling of enslaved people, especially the trade of Black Africans transported to the Americas from the 16th to 19th centuries

Examples

Lincoln was historian and economist enough to know that a substantial portion of this wealth had accumulated in the hands of the descendants of New Englanders engaged in the slave trade.
