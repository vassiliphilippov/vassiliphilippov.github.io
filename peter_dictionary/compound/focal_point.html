focal point

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
The point at which rays of light or other radiation converge or from which they diverge, especially in optics; also, the central or most important point of attention or activity.

Examples

Its folklore and legend, usually disguised as history, are allowed to account for group actions, to provide a focal point for group loyalty, and to become a cohesive force for national identification.
From the moment of the occupation Lublin became a focal point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the point where rays converge or the center of interest

Etymology

From 'focus' (from Latin focus, meaning 'hearth') + 'point'.
