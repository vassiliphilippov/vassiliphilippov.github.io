hit-and-run

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɪt ən ˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌhɪt ən ˈrʌn / (BrE)
involving or consisting of a sudden attack followed immediately by a rapid withdrawal or escape, especially to avoid capture or counterattack

Examples

Throughout the land their hit-and-run terrorists spread fear of ambush and death.

Synset Examples

hit-and-run units
tip-and-run assaults

Etymology

Formed from the verbs 'hit' and 'run', originally from military and sports usage.
