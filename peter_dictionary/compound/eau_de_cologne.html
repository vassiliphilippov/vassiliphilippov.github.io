eau de cologne

1. NOUN

/ oʊ də koʊˈlɔːn / (AmE), / əʊ də kəˈlɒn / (BrE)
A lightly scented perfumed liquid made from essential oils and alcohol, used for personal fragrance.

Examples

Cologne, mouthwash, underarm deodorant?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfume - A fragrant liquid typically used to give a pleasant smell to one's body

Etymology

French, literally 'water of Cologne.' Named after the German city of Cologne where it was first made.
