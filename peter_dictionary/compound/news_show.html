news show

1. NOUN

/ nuz ʃoʊ / (AmE), / njuːz ʃəʊ / (BrE)
A television or radio broadcast primarily dedicated to reporting and discussing current events, often featuring interviews and commentary.

Examples

Sipping their coffee, discussing the weather, the day's shopping, Fritzie's commitments at the network (all of which he would cancel), they avoided the radio, the morning TV news show, even the front page of the Santa Luisa Register, resting on the kitchen bar.

Synset Examples

we watch the 7 o'clock news every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: news program - a broadcast devoted to reporting current events
