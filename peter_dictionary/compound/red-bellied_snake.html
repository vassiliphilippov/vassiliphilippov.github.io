red-bellied snake

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdˈbɛlid ˈsneɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɛdˈbɛlɪd ˈsneɪk / (BrE)
A small, harmless woodland snake of the southeastern United States, distinguished by its reddish underside.

Examples

These three were much alike: lined snake (Tropidoclonion), one year and nine months; red-bellied snake (Storeria), two years; cottonmouth (Ancistrodon), two years.
