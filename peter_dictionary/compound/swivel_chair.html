swivel chair

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪvəl tʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈswɪvəl tʃɛə / (BrE)
A chair with a seat that can rotate horizontally on its base, often used in offices.

Examples

The truth is, however, that when Mel Chandler first reported to the regiment the only steed he had ever ridden was a swivel chair and the only weapon he had ever wielded was a pencil.
He sat stiff-backed in a chair that did not swivel, though it was obvious to Gun that Killpath felt his position as acting captain plainly merited a swivel chair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: office chair - a chair designed for use at a desk in an office, often adjustable and on wheels

Etymology

From 'swivel' (to turn around a point) and 'chair'. 'Swivel' is from Middle English *swivel*, from Old English *swifan* (to move in a sweeping manner).
