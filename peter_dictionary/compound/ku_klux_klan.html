Ku Klux Klan

1. PROPN

/ ku ˈklʌks klæn / (AmE), / kjuː ˈklʌks klæn / (BrE)
A secret extremist organization formed in the southern United States in the 19th century, known for advocating white supremacy and using terrorist tactics, especially to suppress Black people and resist civil rights.

Examples

So it is too with many other spirits which we all know: the spirit of Nazism or Communism, school spirit, the spirit of a street corner gang or a football team, the spirit of Rotary or the Ku Klux Klan.

Etymology

From Greek κύκλος (kyklos, 'circle'); the name is meant to evoke a sense of a secret society.
