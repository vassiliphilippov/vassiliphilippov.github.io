trading post

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdɪŋ poʊst / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdɪŋ pəʊst / (BrE)
a store established to supply goods to settlers, traders, or local inhabitants in sparsely populated areas, often serving as a center for trade with local people or indigenous groups

Examples

On Big Stone Lake near the headwaters of the Red River, Robert Dickson, Superintendent of the Western Indian Department of Canada, had a trading post and planned in 1818 to build a fort to be defended by twenty men and two small artillery pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: general store - a store selling a wide variety of goods, especially in a small community

Etymology

From 'trade' (to buy and sell goods) + 'post' (station or place); used since the 17th century for frontier trading locations.
