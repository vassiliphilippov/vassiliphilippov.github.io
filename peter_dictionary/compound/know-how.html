know-how

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌhaʊ / (BrE)
practical knowledge and skills needed to do something effectively

Examples

SBA offers Administrative Management Courses, which are designed to improve the management efficiency and "know-how "of small business concerns within a community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expertise - advanced skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • Synonym: skill - ability to do something well
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From 'know' (to have knowledge) and 'how' (the manner or way), first used in business English in the early 20th century.
