Mount Everest

1. PROPN

/ maʊnt ˈɛvɚɪst / (AmE), / maʊnt ˈɛvərɪst / (BrE)
the highest mountain peak in the world, located in the central Himalayas on the border between Tibet (China) and Nepal

Examples

As for the night being warm, he would have been comfortable naked on Mount Everest but his water brothers had little tolerance for changes in temperature and pressure; he was considerate of their weakness, once he learned of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Everest - alternative short name for Mount Everest

Etymology

Named after Sir George Everest, a British surveyor general of India.
