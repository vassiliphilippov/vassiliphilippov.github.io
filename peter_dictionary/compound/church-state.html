church-state

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ steɪt / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ steɪt / (BrE)
A state or government ruled by religious authorities or based on religious law.

Examples

When we see the steady and methodical inculcation into humanity of the idea of man's worthlessness - until redeemed - the necessity of the Devil may become evident as a weapon, a weapon designed and used time and time again in every age to whip men into a surrender to a particular church or church-state ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theocracy - a system of government in which priests rule in the name of God or a god

Etymology

From 'church' (Christian religious institution) and 'state' (organized political community); modeled after pairings like 'church-state relations'.
