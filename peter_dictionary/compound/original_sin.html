original sin

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdʒɪnəl sɪn / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəl sɪn / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) the tendency to evil allegedly innate in all human beings, inherited from Adam and Eve's transgression in the Garden of Eden.

Examples

A second reported that the dilapidated houses in Virginia "look like the latter end of original sin and hard times ".

Synset Examples

Adam and Eve committed the original sin when they ate the forbidden fruit in the Garden of Eden

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actual sin - a sin arising from a person's own deliberate act, contrasted with original sin

Etymology

From Latin peccatum originale, meaning 'original sin'.
