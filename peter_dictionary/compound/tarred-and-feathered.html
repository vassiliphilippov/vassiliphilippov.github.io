tarred-and-feathered

1. ADJ

/ tɑrd ənd ˈfɛðərd / (AmE), / tɑːd ənd ˈfɛðəd / (BrE)
(of a person) having been smeared with tar and covered with feathers as a form of public punishment or humiliation.

Examples

And what of that poor tarred and feathered wretch he had seen on the road driving down from Schuyler's?

Synset Examples

the poor tarred-and-feathered wretch

Etymology

From the practice of tarring and feathering as public humiliation in 18th–19th century America and Britain.
