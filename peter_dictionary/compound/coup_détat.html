coup d'état

1. NOUN

/ ku deɪˈtɑ / (AmE), / ku deɪˈtɑː / (BrE)
A sudden and often violent overthrow of an existing government by a small group, usually through illegal means or force.

Examples

And contrary to what has been said recently, we did not wait for "outside pressures "and "world opinion "to bring down that Communist government; we moved decisively to effect an anti Communist coup d' etat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order, often with mass participation

Etymology

From French 'coup d'État' meaning 'stroke of state'.
