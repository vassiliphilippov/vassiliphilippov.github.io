court order

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an official direction or command issued by a court of law, requiring someone to do or refrain from doing something

Examples

that the Court order does not unequally affect the Southern region?
A friend of mine in New Mexico said the Court order had caused no particular trouble out there, that all had gone as merry as a marriage bell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injunction - a court order that requires a party to do or refrain from specific acts
