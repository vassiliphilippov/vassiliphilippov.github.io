wage setter

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪdʒ ˌsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈweɪdʒ ˌsɛtə / (BrE)
an individual, group, or economic factor that determines wage rates

Examples

Thus, for non negative changes in the basic wage rate, the industry becomes the active wage setter, since any increase in the basic wage rate can occur only by reason of industry acquiescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employer - a person or organization that hires employees and often sets wages
  • Antonym: wage earner - a person who earns wages, sometimes contrasted with wage setter
