ground glass

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡraʊnd ˈɡlæs / (AmE), / ˌɡraʊnd ˈɡlɑːs / (BrE)
Glass with a roughened surface, produced by abrasion or etching, that diffuses light instead of letting it pass directly through.

Examples

"Well, that's your business, Mr. Skyros ", said Angie, and his dreamy eyes moved past Mr. Skyros' shoulder to gaze vaguely out the ground glass window.

Etymology

From 'ground', past participle of 'grind', referring to the process of roughening the glass surface.
