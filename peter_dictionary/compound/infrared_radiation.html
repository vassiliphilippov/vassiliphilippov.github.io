infrared radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
Electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than radio waves.

Examples

Piddington and Minnett explained their observations by pointing out that rocklike materials which are likely to make up the surface of the moon would be partially transparent to radio waves, although opaque to infrared radiation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ultraviolet radiation - Electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light

Etymology

From Latin 'infra' (below) and 'red', meaning 'below red' in terms of wavelength.
