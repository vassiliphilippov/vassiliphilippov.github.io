age-old

1. ADJ

/ eɪdʒ oʊld / (AmE), / eɪdʒ əʊld / (BrE)
Having existed or continued for a very long time; from a past era.

Examples

After this holocaust, a changing world occupied the minds of men; a world beset with new boundaries, new treaties and governments, new goals and methods, and the age-old fears of aggression and subjugation - hunger and exposure.

Synset Examples

age-old customs
the antique fear that days would dwindle away to complete darkness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient - belonging to the distant past
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
