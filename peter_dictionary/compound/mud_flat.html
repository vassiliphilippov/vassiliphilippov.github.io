mud flat

1. NOUN

/ mʌd flæt / (AmE), / mʌd flæt / (BrE)
A stretch of muddy land, typically near an estuary, that is covered by water at high tide and exposed at low tide.

Examples

Below in the open bay facing Manhattan was Staten Island, gritty with clam shells and mud flats behind which nested farms, cattle barns, and berry thickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tidal flat - an area of flat, muddy land alternately covered and uncovered by tides
