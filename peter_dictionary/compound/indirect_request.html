indirect request

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈrɛkt rɪˈkwɛst / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkt rɪˈkwɛst / (BrE)
A statement or question made to express a desire or ask for something without stating it directly, often to be more polite or subtle.

Examples

If there's nothing else, I'd like to go to my daughter's wedding.

Synset Examples

I could tell that it was his wish that the guests leave
his crying was an indirect request for attention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - an act of asking for something
  • Antonym: direct request - an explicit or straightforward request
