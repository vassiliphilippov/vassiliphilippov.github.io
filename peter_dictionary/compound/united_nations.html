United Nations

1. PROPN

/ juˈnaɪt̬ɪd ˈneɪʃənz / (AmE), / juːˈnaɪtɪd ˈneɪʃənz / (BrE)
an international organization founded in 1945 by independent states to promote peace, security, and cooperation among countries

Etymology

Formed from 'united' (joined together) and 'nations' (countries), coined in 1942 by Winston Churchill and Franklin D. Roosevelt and adopted as the organization's name at its founding in 1945.
