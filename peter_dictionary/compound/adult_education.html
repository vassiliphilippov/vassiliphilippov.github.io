adult education

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌlt ˌɛdʒuˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈdʌlt ˌɛdjuːˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
education designed for adults, typically involving courses, lectures, or correspondence for those not engaged in formal study.

Examples

Adult education courses, work-study programs of various sorts - these are all evidence of a continuing interest of the schools in furthering educational opportunities for out-of-school youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuing education - further education for adults after leaving formal schooling
  • Synonym: lifelong learning - pursuit of learning activities throughout life, often after formal education
