Nobel Prize

1. PROPN

/ noʊˈbɛl ˌpraɪz / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɛl ˌpraɪz / (BrE)
an annual international award given for outstanding achievements in physics, chemistry, medicine, literature, economics, or peace

Examples

Among the recipients of the Nobel Prize for Literature more than half are practically unknown to readers of English.

Etymology

Named after Alfred Nobel (1833–1896), Swedish chemist and inventor, who established the awards in his will.
