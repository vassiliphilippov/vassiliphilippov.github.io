pulmonary emphysema

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌlməˌnɛri ɛmˌfɪsiˈmə / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri ɛmˌfɪsiˈmə / (BrE)
A chronic lung disease characterized by abnormal enlargement of air spaces and destruction of lung tissue, resulting in reduced respiratory function.

Examples

In addition, a detailed interspecies survey of the incidence of generalized pulmonary emphysema in mammals would be interesting and pertinent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphysema - A general term for pathological air or gas in tissues; also refers specifically to the same pulmonary condition

Etymology

From Latin 'pulmonarius' (pertaining to the lungs) and Greek 'emphysēma' (inflation).
