meteor stream

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtiər striːm / (AmE), / ˈmiːtiə striːm / (BrE)
a group of meteoroids traveling together in space that, upon entering the earth's atmosphere, create a transient shower of meteors

Examples

The data for the first day indicate a meteor stream with a very high concentration of particles and may have led to the high estimates of micrometeorite flux.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteor shower - a period during which numerous meteors are observed to radiate from one point in the sky
