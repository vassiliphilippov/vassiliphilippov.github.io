glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase

1. NOUN

/ ɡluˈtæmɪk ˌɑksəloʊəˈsɛtɪk trænsˈæmɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / ɡluˈtæmɪk ˌɒksələʊəˈsɛtɪk trænsˈæmɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme found in the liver and other tissues, involved in the transfer of amino groups and measured in blood tests to help diagnose liver and heart problems

Examples

Serial serum potassium levels remained normal; the serum glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase was 10 units per ml. per min..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspartate aminotransferase - enzyme also known as glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase

Etymology

From the names of the substrates: glutamic (acid) and oxaloacetic (acid), plus 'transaminase', an enzyme transferring amino groups.
