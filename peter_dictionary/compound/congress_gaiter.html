congress gaiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋɡrəs ˈɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrɪs ˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
A type of ankle-high shoe with elastic sides, popular in the 19th century.

Examples

They were carpeted, but made for pumps and congress gaiters, not the great clodhoppers he wore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chelsea boot - ankle-high boot with elastic side panels, similar modern style

Etymology

Gaiter from French 'guêtre' (a protective covering for the ankle and lower leg); 'congress' likely refers to its popularity in the U.S. Congress during the 19th century.
