trade school

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪd skuːl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd skuːl / (BrE)
A school that provides education and training in skilled trades, such as carpentry, plumbing, or mechanics, typically at the secondary or post-secondary level.

Examples

There are a great number and variety of private commercial schools, trade schools and technical schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocational school - a school providing practical and job-oriented training in specific trades or vocations
