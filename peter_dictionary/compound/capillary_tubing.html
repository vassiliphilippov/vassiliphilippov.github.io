capillary tubing

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪləri ˈtuːbɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈpɪləri ˈtjuːbɪŋ / (BrE)
A very narrow tube that allows liquid to rise or be held inside by capillary action, commonly used in laboratories and scientific equipment.

Examples

The samples for liquid phase thermal reaction studies were prepared in Pyrex capillary tubing 2.5 mm. i.d. and about 15 cm. long.

Etymology

From Latin *capillaris* (hair-like) and English 'tubing'.
