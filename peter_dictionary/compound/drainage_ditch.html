drainage ditch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪnɪdʒ dɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnɪdʒ dɪtʃ / (BrE)
A ditch constructed to carry away excess water or sewage from land or roads.

Examples

The effluent was collected through two pipes and discharged to the Blue River through a surface drainage ditch.
The BOD in the drainage ditch receiving the pilot plant effluent averaged 12 mg / l.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ditch - a long narrow excavation in the ground, used for drainage or irrigation
