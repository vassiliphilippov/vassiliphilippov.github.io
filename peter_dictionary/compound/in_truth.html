in truth

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈtruθ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈtruːθ / (BrE)
used to emphasize the veracity of a statement; in fact; indeed.

Examples

In truth, we can say that this broke the power of Senator Joseph McCarthy, who was finally exposed in full light to the American people.

Synset Examples

in truth, moral decay hastened the decline of the Roman Empire
really, you shouldn't have done it
a truly awful book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeed - used to emphasize a statement or response
  • Synonym: in fact - used to emphasize the truth of an assertion
  • Synonym: truly - genuinely or to a great degree; actually
