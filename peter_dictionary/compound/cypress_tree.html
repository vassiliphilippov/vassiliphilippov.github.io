cypress tree

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪprəs tri / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪprəs triː / (BrE)
A type of evergreen conifer of the genus Cupressus, native to northern temperate regions, with dark, scalelike leaves and rounded cones.

Examples

But even before he started across the oiled road to his Plymouth, parked in the lot under the cypress trees across from the station, he knew that he was n't going home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - another coniferous tree with scalelike leaves, genus Juniperus

Etymology

From Old French cipres, from Latin cupressus, from Greek kyparissos.
