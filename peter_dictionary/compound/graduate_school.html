graduate school

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuət skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡrædjʊət skuːl / (BrE)
A division of a university or college offering studies and degrees beyond the bachelor's level, such as master's or doctorate programs.

Examples

The basic problem involved is that a college setting up a graduate school must have an entirely separate faculty for the advanced degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postgraduate - relating to studies after a bachelor's degree

Etymology

Graduate from Latin *gradus* (step, degree) + school; refers to school for those who have already graduated with a bachelor's degree.
