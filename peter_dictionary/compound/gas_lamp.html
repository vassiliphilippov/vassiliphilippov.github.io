gas lamp

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs læmp / (AmE), / ɡas lamp / (BrE)
A lamp that produces light by burning illuminating gas, often used in streets and homes before the widespread use of electricity.

Examples

Two gas lamps were no more than a misleading glow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil lamp - lamp that burns oil for light
  • Antonym: electric lamp - lamp that produces light using electricity
