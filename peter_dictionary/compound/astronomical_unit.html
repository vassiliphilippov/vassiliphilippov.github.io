astronomical unit

1. NOUN

/ əˌstrɑːnəmɪkəl ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / əˌstrɒnəmɪkəl ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A unit of length, equal to the mean distance from the Earth to the Sun, used for measuring distances within the solar system (about 93 million miles or 150 million kilometers).

Examples

At one astronomical unit from the sun (the Earth's distance) the dust orbits are probably nearly circular.

Etymology

From 'astronomical' (relating to astronomy) + 'unit' (a standard measure). Term formalized in scientific usage in the 19th-20th centuries.
