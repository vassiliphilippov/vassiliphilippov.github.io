phonograph record

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊnəˌɡræf ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊnəɡrɑːf ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
A flat disc that stores sound recordings in grooves, played back using a phonograph or record player.

Examples

What you were looking for (unless you make a hobby of collecting old tennis rackets and fly screens) eludes me, but to judge from phonograph records scattered about a fumed oak Victrola, you danced two tangos and a paso doble, which must have been fairly enervating in that milieu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinyl record - a synonym term modernly used for 'phonograph record'

Etymology

Phonograph: from Greek 'phōnē' (sound) + '-graph' (writing). Record: from Latin 'recordari' (to remember).
