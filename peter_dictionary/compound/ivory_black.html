ivory black

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvəri blæk / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri blæk / (BrE)
a deep black pigment made from the charred remains of ivory, traditionally used in painting and drawing.

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamp black - a black pigment made from soot
  • Synonym: bone black - a black pigment made from charring animal bones

Etymology

Named for its original method of production from burnt ivory.
