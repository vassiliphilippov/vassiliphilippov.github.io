voltaic cell

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈteɪɪk sɛl / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪɪk sɛl / (BrE)
An electric cell that generates electricity through an irreversible chemical reaction and cannot be recharged.

Examples

Together they also developed a new form of voltaic cell in which the wooden trough was replaced by one of copper, thereby producing stronger currents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanic cell - an alternative name for a voltaic cell; a non-rechargeable electric cell

Etymology

Named after Alessandro Volta (1745–1827), Italian physicist and inventor of the electric battery.
