district attorney

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstrɪkt əˈtɝːni / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrɪkt əˈtɜːni / (BrE)
The public official who represents the government in the prosecution of criminal offenses for a specific judicial district, particularly in the United States.

Examples

Three weeks later following his recovery, armed with a writ issued by the Catskill justice on affidavits prepared by the district attorney, Cook and Russell rode to arrest Martinez.
District Attorney M. W. Mills warned that he would vigorously prosecute persons caught committing these crimes or carrying arms - he just did n't catch anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosecutor - a legal official who brings charges against someone in a court of law
  • Antonym: defense attorney - an attorney who represents the accused in legal proceedings

Etymology

Combination of 'district' (from Latin 'districtus', meaning 'region of jurisdiction') and 'attorney' (from Old French 'atorné', meaning 'appointed').
