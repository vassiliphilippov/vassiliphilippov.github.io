line drive

1. NOUN

/ laɪn draɪv / (AmE), / laɪn draɪv / (BrE)
(Baseball) A batted ball that travels fast and straight in the air, usually parallel to the ground.

Examples

Alusik then moved Cooke across with a line drive to left.

Synset Examples

the batter hit a liner to the shortstop

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ground ball - a batted ball that rolls or bounces along the ground
  • Antonym: fly ball - a batted ball that is hit high into the air
