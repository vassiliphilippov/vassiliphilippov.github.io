rose of Sharon

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊz əv ˈʃɛɹən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊz əv ˈʃarən / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree (Hibiscus syriacus) native to Asia, with large, showy flowers in shades of rose, purple, or white, widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Examples

In the center of any open space remaining our grandfathers had planted syringa and sweet shrub, snowball, rose-of-Sharon and balm-of-Gilead.

Etymology

From a biblical reference to 'rose of Sharon' in the Song of Solomon, though not referring to the modern plant.
