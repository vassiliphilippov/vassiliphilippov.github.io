political platform

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈplætfɔrm / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈplætfɔːm / (BrE)
A formal statement of the aims and principles of a political party, often set out in a document used during an election campaign.

Examples

The stained-glass windows may have developed unpremeditated patinas, the paneling may be no more durable than the planks in a political platform.

Synset Examples

their candidate simply ignored the party platform
they won the election even though they offered no positive program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manifesto - a public declaration of policy and aims, especially before an election
