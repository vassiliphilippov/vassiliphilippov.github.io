cortical potential

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtɪkəl pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkəl pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
A rapid fluctuation of electrical voltage between parts of the cerebral cortex, especially as detected by an electroencephalograph.

Examples

The emotional states produced by drugs influence the cortical potentials in a characteristic manner; synchrony prevails in the EEG of the experimental animal after administration of tranquilizers, but asynchrony after application of analeptic and psychoactive drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainwave - a detectable fluctuating voltage between parts of the brain

Etymology

From cortex (Latin for 'bark', in anatomy the outer layer of an organ) + potential (from Latin potentia, meaning 'power, potentiality').
