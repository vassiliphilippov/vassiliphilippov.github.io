nasal cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪzəl ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəl ˈkævəti / (BrE)
Either of the two cavities inside the nose, between the floor of the cranium and the roof of the mouth, extending from the nostrils to the pharynx.

Examples

Scrapings from the bed linen identical with the lint and thread found in the nasal and oral cavities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oral cavity - the mouth cavity, the space inside the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'nasalis' (of the nose) and 'cavitas' (hollow, cavity).
