low-water mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ ˌwɔtɚ mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌwɔːtə mɑːk / (BrE)
The lowest or most unsuccessful point in a situation, activity, or person's life.

Examples

The lordly poet was at low-water mark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nadir - the lowest point; the moment of greatest adversity
  • Antonym: high-water mark - the highest point of achievement or success

Etymology

Originally a nautical term referring to the lowest level reached by water, later used figuratively.
