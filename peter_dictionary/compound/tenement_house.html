tenement house

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnəmənt ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnəmənt ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A large residential building divided into separate apartments, especially one that is old, run-down, and meeting only minimal standards of comfort and safety.

Examples

The structured schools were in an industrial city, with three family tenement houses typical of the residential areas, but with one rather sizable section of middle-class homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment building - a building containing several individual apartments
  • Degree Higher: slum - an overcrowded, poor, and run-down urban area

Etymology

From Old French *tenement* (dwelling), from Latin *tenementum* (holding; property).
