piano sonata

1. NOUN

/ piˈænoʊ səˈnɑːtə / (AmE), / piˈanəʊ səˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A piece of music in sonata form written for solo piano.

Examples

We attended the premieres of his concertos, symphonies, and suites; we studied, taught, and performed his piano sonatas, chamber music, gavottes, and marches; we bought his records and played them in our schools and universities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonata - a musical composition for one or more instruments, often in several movements

Etymology

From Italian 'sonata' (played) and 'piano' (soft; referring to the pianoforte instrument).
