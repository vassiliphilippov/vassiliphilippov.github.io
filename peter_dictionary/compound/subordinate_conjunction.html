subordinate conjunction

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɔrdɪnət kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / səˈbɔːdɪnət kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a conjunction that introduces a dependent (subordinate) clause, such as 'because', 'if', or 'that'

Examples

If anything in this life is certain, if history has taught us anything, it'’s that you can kill anyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coordinating conjunction - a conjunction that joins two elements of equal grammatical rank

Etymology

From sub- (Latin, under) and Latin ordinare (to order); relates to grammatical hierarchy.
