reflexive pronoun

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛksɪv ˈproʊnaʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪv ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
A pronoun such as myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, or themselves, used when the subject and the object of a verb refer to the same person or thing.

Examples

That's fine during the day, but at night he's got that cell all to himself.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: personal pronoun - a pronoun designating a particular person or thing

Etymology

From Latin reflexivus (turned back) and pronomen (pronoun).
