at-bat

1. NOUN

/ æt ˈbæt / (AmE), / æt ˈbat / (BrE)
A turn by a batter in baseball or softball resulting in an attempt to get a hit, typically recorded as a statistic.

Examples

Jensen got only six hits in 46 at-bats for a .130 batting average in the first 12 games.

Synset Examples

he was at bat when it happened
he got four hits in four at-bats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate appearance - each completed turn by a batter, whether or not it counts as an at-bat
