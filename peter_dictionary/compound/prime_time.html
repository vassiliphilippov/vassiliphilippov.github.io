prime time

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ˈpraɪm taɪm / (BrE)
The period, typically between 7 and 11 p.m., when television or radio audiences are at their highest and many broadcasters schedule their most popular programs.

Examples

There seems to be an unfortunate assumption that an hour of Chicago style jazz in prime evening time, for example, could not be justified without the trimmings of a portentous documentary.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-peak - periods with lower TV/radio audience
