dark field illumination

1. NOUN

/ dɑrk fiːld ˌɪl.əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / dɑːk fiːld ˌɪl.jʊˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A method of microscopic examination in which specimens are lit by scattered light, making them appear bright against a dark background.

Examples

Stained or unstained sections were examined under dark field illumination in a Zeiss fluorescence microscope equipped with a mercury vapor lamp (Osram HBO 200).
