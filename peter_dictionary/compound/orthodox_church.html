Orthodox Church

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrθəˌdɑks tʃɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɔːθədɒks tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The branch of Christianity comprising churches derived from the Byzantine tradition and adhering to Eastern (Byzantine) rites, especially the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Examples

Today, the Roman Catholic and Orthodox Churches stand virtually alone in holding that conviction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - the main body of Orthodox Christianity

Etymology

From Greek 'orthodoxos' meaning 'having the right opinion' + 'church' from Greek 'kyriakon' (Lord's house).
