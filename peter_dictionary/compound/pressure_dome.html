pressure dome

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃɚ doʊm / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə dəʊm / (BrE)
A dome-shaped structure that is kept at a higher-than-ambient air pressure to support human life or specific environments.

Examples

On Mars humans were building pressure domes for the male and female party that would arrive by next ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geodesic dome - a dome constructed of short struts following geodesic lines, often used for efficient enclosing structures

Etymology

From 'pressure' (force exerted per unit area) and 'dome' (rounded vault forming the roof of a building).
