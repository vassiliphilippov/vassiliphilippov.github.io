petroleum geologist

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊliəm dʒiˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊliəm dʒiˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the origin, occurrence, movement, accumulation, and exploration of oil and natural gas resources

Examples

The most convincing recent measurement of an anaconda was made in eastern Colombia by Roberto Lamon, a petroleum geologist of the Richmond Oil Company, and reported in 1944 by Emmett R. Dunn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geologist - a scientist who studies the Earth's physical structure and substances
