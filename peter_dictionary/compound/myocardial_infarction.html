myocardial infarction

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.oʊˈkɑr.di.əl ɪnˈfɑrk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈkɑː.di.əl ɪnˈfɑːk.ʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a part of the heart muscle is damaged or dies because its blood supply is blocked; commonly known as a heart attack.

Examples

In February, 1958, the patient suffered a myocardial infarction complicated by pulmonary edema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart attack - sudden blockage of blood flow to the heart muscle

Etymology

From Greek *myo-* (muscle) + *kardia* (heart) + Latin *infarctio* (stuffing, cramming).
