search warrant

1. NOUN

/ sɝtʃ ˈwɔɹənt / (AmE), / sɜːtʃ ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official legal document authorizing law enforcement officials to search a specified place for evidence or persons involved in a crime

Examples

Since the strength of the Mexicans had been underrated, too small a posse had been collected, and since the deputy had not been provided with search warrants, MacPherson and his men decided it was much wiser to withdraw.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: warrant - an official authorization, especially one issued by a judge or magistrate
