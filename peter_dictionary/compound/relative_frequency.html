relative frequency

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛlətɪv ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
The proportion of times a particular value or category occurs in a dataset, expressed as a ratio to the total number of observations.

Examples

Thomas also presents a simple equation for deriving an index of persistence, which weights not only the number of stems (' roots') per meaning, but their relative frequency.
