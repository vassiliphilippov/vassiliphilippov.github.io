down-and-out

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌdaʊn ən ˈaʊt / (BrE)
A person who is without money, home, or prospects; someone who is destitute.

Examples

He said he'’d get the police to throw us out on the street.

Synset Examples

he tried to help the down-and-out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place and lives by begging
  • Synonym: homeless - a person without a home
  • Synonym: destitute - lacking the basic necessities of life

Etymology

From the phrase 'down and out', originally referring to boxers who were knocked down and unable to continue; later extended to mean destitute.
