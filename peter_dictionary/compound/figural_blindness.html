figural blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡjɚəl ˈblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡjərəl ˈblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
A condition marked by the inability to perceive or recognize shapes and contours visually.

Examples

In short, both his own declarations and his figural blindness, when he looked at objects, seem to present undeniable evidence that he had simply no visual memory at all.

Etymology

From 'figural' (relating to figures or forms) + 'blindness.'
