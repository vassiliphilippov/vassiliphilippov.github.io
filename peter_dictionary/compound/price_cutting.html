price cutting

1. NOUN

/ praɪs ˈkʌt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / praɪs ˈkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of lowering prices, especially below the usual or advertised level, often to attract customers or compete with rivals.

Examples

A funeral parlor chain was indicted for price-cutting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discounting - the act of reducing prices from the regular rate
  • Antonym: price hike - an increase in the price of merchandise
