audio system

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiˌoʊ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A set of electronic devices designed for recording, amplifying, and reproducing sound.

Examples

Commonly used electronic devices which are found in practically every hospital are closed-circuit TV and audio systems for internal paging and instruction, along with radiation counters, timers, and similar devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound system - a set of equipment for amplifying or reproducing sound

Etymology

From Latin *audio* (I hear) + English *system*.
