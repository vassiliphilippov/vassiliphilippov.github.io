married couple

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærid ˈkʌpəl / (AmE), / ˈmærid ˈkʌpl / (BrE)
Two people who are married to each other.

Examples

Repeated polls have disclosed that most married couples are now using contraceptives in the practice of birth control.
While it is easy enough to ridicule Hawkins' pronouncement in Pleas of the Crown from a metaphysical point of view, the concept of the "oneness "of a married couple may reflect an abiding belief that the communion between husband and wife is such that their actions are not always to be regarded by the criminal law as if there were no marriage.

Synset Examples

his second marriage was happier than the first
a married couple without love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spouses - husband and wife collectively or people who are married to each other
  • Antonym: unmarried couple - two people who are in a relationship but not married
