glottal catch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑt̬.əl kætʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒt.əl kætʃ / (BrE)
a stop consonant produced by briefly blocking airflow at the glottis, as in the abrupt start of a vowel sound in some words

Examples

Some phonetic features, for example glottal catch or murmur, are sometimes to be assigned to segmental phonemics and sometimes to accentual systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glottal stop - a stop consonant formed at the glottis

Etymology

From 'glottal' (relating to the glottis, from Greek 'glōtta' meaning 'tongue') and 'catch' (to seize or capture).
