Pseudomonas pyocanea

1. NOUN

/ suːdəˈmoʊnəs paɪoʊˈkeɪniə / (AmE), / sjuːdəˈməʊnəs paɪəˈkeɪniə / (BrE)
A species of aerobic bacteria belonging to the genus Pseudomonas, known for occurring in various environments and sometimes associated with infections.

Examples

Cultures taken from the jejunum yielded Monilia albicans, Pseudomonas pyocanea, Aerobacter aerogenes, and Streptococcus anhemolyticus.

Etymology

From New Latin; the genus name 'Pseudomonas' means 'false unit' (Greek 'pseudo-' + 'monas'), and 'pyocanea' from Greek 'pyon' (pus) and 'kyanos' (blue).
