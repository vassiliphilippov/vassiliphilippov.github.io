red blood cell

1. NOUN

/ rɛd blʌd sɛl / (AmE), / rɛd blʌd sɛl / (BrE)
A type of blood cell, typically biconcave and lacking a nucleus, that contains hemoglobin and carries oxygen from the lungs to tissues throughout the body.
