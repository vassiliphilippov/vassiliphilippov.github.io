conditioned response

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃənd rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənd rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a learned reaction that occurs when a previously neutral stimulus elicits a response due to association with another stimulus

Examples

However, if these procedures are applied more often, conditioned emotional responses are temporarily abolished.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconditioned response - an automatic, natural reaction to a stimulus, not learned
