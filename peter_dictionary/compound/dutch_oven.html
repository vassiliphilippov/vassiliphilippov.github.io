Dutch oven

1. NOUN

/ dʌtʃ ˈʌvən / (AmE), / dʌtʃ ˈʌvən / (BrE)
A heavy, thick-walled cooking pot, usually made of cast iron, with a tight-fitting lid, used for slow-cooking or baking, especially in a fire or oven.

Examples

He was readying a batch of sourdough biscuits for the Dutch oven.

Etymology

Named for Dutch expertise in casting iron cookware; 'oven' refers to its use for baking and braising.
