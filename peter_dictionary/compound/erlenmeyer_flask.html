Erlenmeyer flask

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝːlənˌmaɪɚ flæsk / (AmE), / ˈɜːlənˌmaɪə flæsk / (BrE)
A glass laboratory flask with a conical body, wide flat base, and a narrow neck, used for mixing and heating liquids.

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Florence flask - a laboratory flask with a round body and long neck

Etymology

Named after German chemist Emil Erlenmeyer (1825–1909), who invented it in 1861.
