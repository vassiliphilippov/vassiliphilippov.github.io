sweat suit

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛt suːt / (AmE), / ˈswɛt sjuːt / (BrE)
A loose, comfortable outfit consisting of sweatpants and a sweatshirt, typically worn for exercise or casual wear.

Examples

- Held out of Texas Tech's sweat suits drill Monday at Lubbock was tackle Richard Stafford, who is undergoing treatment for a leg injury suffered in the Raiders' 38 - 7 loss to Texas A+M.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tracksuit - a suit of clothing consisting of sweatpants and a zippered jacket, worn especially for sports or casual wear

Etymology

From "sweat" + "suit", named for its original use during sweating in physical exercise.
