physical chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪzɪkəl ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌfɪzɪkəl ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
The branch of chemistry concerned with the study of the physical properties and phenomena of chemical substances and the physical changes they undergo.

Examples

Although there is no question but that the process of washing fabrics involves a number of phenomena which are related together in an extremely complicated way and that these phenomena and their interrelations are not well understood at the present, this section attempts to present briefly an up-to-date picture of the physical chemistry of washing either fabrics or hard surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemistry - the scientific study of the properties and reactions of substances

Etymology

Formed by combining 'physical' and 'chemistry'; 'physical' from Greek 'physikos' (natural), 'chemistry' from Arabic 'al-kīmiyā'.
