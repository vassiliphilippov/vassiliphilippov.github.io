rallying point

1. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A person, place, or idea that serves to unite people with shared interests, especially those who were previously divided or dispersed.

Examples

If he had been "liquidated "in some way, he would have become a martyr, a rallying point for people who shared his ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the center of interest or activity
  • Antonym: dividing line - something that separates or distinguishes conflicting groups
