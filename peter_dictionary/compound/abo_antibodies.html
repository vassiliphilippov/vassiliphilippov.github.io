ABO antibodies

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˌbiːˈoʊ ˈæn.tiˌbɑː.diz / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌbiːˈəʊ ˈæn.tiˌbɒd.iːz / (BrE)
Antibodies in the blood plasma that react against antigens of the ABO blood group system.

Examples

Abelson and Rawson, using a stepwise elution scheme, fractionated whole sera containing ABO and Rh antibodies on diethylaminoethyl DEAE cellulose and carboxymethyl cellulose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood group antibody - an antibody in the blood that reacts with an antigen on red blood cells

Etymology

ABO from the letters denoting human blood types (A, B, O); antibody from Greek anti- (against) + body.
