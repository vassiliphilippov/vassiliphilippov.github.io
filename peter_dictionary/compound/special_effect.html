special effect

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəl əˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃ(ə)l ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
an artificial effect created to simulate imagined events in film, television, or theater that cannot be achieved by normal means

Examples

In some of the numbers the instrumental parts have even been recorded at different times and then later combined on the master tape to produce special effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visual effect - techniques for creating imagery outside the live action shot
