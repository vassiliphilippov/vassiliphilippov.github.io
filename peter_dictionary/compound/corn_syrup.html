corn syrup

1. NOUN

/ kɔrn ˈsɪrəp / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈsɪrəp / (BrE)
A thick, sweet syrup made from corn starch, used as a sweetener in foods and drinks.

Examples

However, there are photographs of suspended drops of cyclohexanol phthalate (viscosity 155 poises) suspended in corn syrup of 71 poises in a paper by Mason and Bartok.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glucose syrup - a syrup containing glucose as the main sugar, often used as a sweetener
