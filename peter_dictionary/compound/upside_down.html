upside down

1. ADV

/ ˈʌp.saɪd ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌʌp.saɪd ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
with the upper side or part turned toward the ground or away from the usual position; in an inverted position or manner.

Examples

"I'm General Burnside's horse, upside down ", Arlene said, sort of gaspingly, for her: even she had to breathe kind of funny when she was in that position.
She made General Burnside's horse's belly do so funny when it was upside down.
I found a trooper once the Apache had spread-eagled on an ant hill, and another time we ran across some teamsters they 'd caught, tied upside down on their own wagon wheels over little fires until their brains was exploded right out o' their skulls.

Synset Examples

the box was lying on the floor upside down

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right side up - with the correct side or surface facing upward
