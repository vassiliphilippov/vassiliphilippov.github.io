tactual sensation

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tʃu.əl sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tʃu.əl sɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A physical feeling resulting from the stimulation of touch receptors in the skin, such as by pressure, texture, or vibration.

Examples

(2) Spatiality becomes part of the tactual sensation only by way of visual representations; that is, there is, in the true sense, only a visual space ".

Synset Examples

she likes the touch of silk on her skin
the surface had a greasy feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - the sense that allows perception of physical contact
  • Synonym: feeling - an experience or awareness of physical sensation

Etymology

From Latin 'tactilis' (of touch) + English 'sensation'.
