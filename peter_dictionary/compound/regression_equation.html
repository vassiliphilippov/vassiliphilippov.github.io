regression equation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛʃən ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
a mathematical equation expressing the relationship between one dependent variable and one or more independent variables, used to predict probable values of the dependent variable based on the independent variable(s)

Examples

For example, on the basis of the regression equation, a child with an I. Q. of 120 in this sample would be expected to earn an achievement test score of 4.8 (grade equivalent).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regression analysis - a statistical procedure for estimating the relationships among variables
  • Antonym: correlation - statistical relationship between two variables, not necessarily predictive

Etymology

From 'regression' (Latin regredi, to go back) and 'equation' (Latin aequare, to make equal), used in statistics since the late 19th century.
