shock therapy

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑk ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ʃɒk ˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a treatment for certain mental disorders in which shocks are administered to induce convulsions, used especially in psychiatric care

Examples

The shock therapies act likewise on the hypothalamic balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroconvulsive therapy - a type of shock therapy using electrical currents to induce seizures for psychiatric treatment
