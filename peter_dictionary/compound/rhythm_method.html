rhythm method

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪðəm ˌmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈrɪðəm ˌmɛθəd / (BrE)
A method of natural family planning that involves abstaining from intercourse during the estimated fertile days of a woman's menstrual cycle to avoid pregnancy.

Examples

The Roman Catholic Church sanctions only abstention or the rhythm method, also known as the use of the infertile or safe period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural family planning - a birth control method relying on knowledge of a woman's fertility cycle

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪðəm ˌmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈrɪðəm ˌmɛθəd / (BrE)
A method of natural family planning in which a woman avoids pregnancy by abstaining from sexual intercourse during the days she is most fertile, calculated based on her menstrual cycle.

Examples

With the exception of the Roman Catholic and the Orthodox Catholic Churches, most churches make no moral distinction between rhythm and mechanical or chemical contraceptives, allowing the couple free choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraception - Prevention of pregnancy by various methods
  • Synonym: natural family planning - Methods of avoiding pregnancy based on observation of natural fertility signs
