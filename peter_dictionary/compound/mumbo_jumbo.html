mumbo jumbo

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmboʊ ˈdʒʌmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʌmbəʊ ˈdʒʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
language, speech, or ritual that is confusing or meaningless, often intended to obscure the truth or impress people

Examples

As he applied the applicator extending from the machine - which consisted of seven differently colored neon tubes superimposed on a rectangular base - to the supposedly diseased portions of Mrs. Shaefer's body, Lee kept up a steady stream of pseudo scientific mumbo-jumbo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibberish - unintelligible or nonsensical talk or writing

Etymology

Originally a West African term referring to a masked dancer, adopted into English in the 18th century to refer to meaningless rituals and later generalized.
