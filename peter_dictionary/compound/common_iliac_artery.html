common iliac artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmən ˈɪli.æk ˈɑr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən ˈɪl.i.æk ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
either of the two large arteries that branch from the end of the abdominal aorta and supply blood to the pelvis and lower limbs.

Examples

In particular, the orifices of the right renal and celiac arteries were virtually occluded, and both calcified common iliac arteries were completely occluded.

Etymology

From Latin 'iliacus' (pertaining to the flanks or groin) and 'arteria' (artery).
