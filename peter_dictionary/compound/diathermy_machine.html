diathermy machine

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæθɚmi məˈʃin / (AmE), / daɪˈæθəmi məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a device used in medicine to locally heat bodily tissues, typically for therapeutic purposes such as relieving pain or improving circulation

Examples

Electronics has been applied to medicine for many years in the form of such familiar equipment as the x-ray machine, the electrocardiograph, and the diathermy machine.

Etymology

From 'diathermy', from Greek dia- (through) + thermē (heat), and 'machine'.
