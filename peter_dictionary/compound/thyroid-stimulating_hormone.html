thyroid-stimulating hormone

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪrɔɪd stɪˈmjuleɪtɪŋ ˈhɔrmoʊn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərɔɪd stɪˈmjuːleɪtɪŋ ˈhɔːməʊn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce its hormones

Examples

The name thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) has been given to a substance found in the anterior pituitary gland of all species of animal so far tested for its presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: TSH - abbreviation for thyroid-stimulating hormone
  • Synonym: thyrotropin - another name for thyroid-stimulating hormone

Etymology

From 'thyroid' (from Greek 'thyreoeides') and 'stimulating hormone', a standard term in endocrinology.
