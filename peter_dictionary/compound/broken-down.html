broken-down

1. ADJ

/ ˈbroʊ.kən ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.kən ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of things) in a state of disrepair or ruin; dilapidated or worn out through neglect or age.

Examples

That afternoon when they had pulled up in front of the broken-down ranch house, his hopes had been high.

Synset Examples

a street of bedraggled tenements
a broken-down fence
a ramshackle old pier
a tumble-down shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run-down - in bad condition due to neglect or age
  • Synonym: ramshackle - (of a building or vehicle) in a state of severe disrepair
  • Synonym: tumble-down - (of a building) in such poor condition as to look about to collapse
