reciprocal pronoun

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəl ˈproʊnaʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
A pronoun or pronominal phrase that indicates a mutual relationship or action between two or more people or things, such as 'each other' or 'one another'.

Examples

Talk to each other.

Synset Examples

The sentence `They cared for each other' contains a reciprocal pronoun

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reflexive pronoun - a pronoun referring back to the subject of the clause

Etymology

reciprocal: from Latin reciprocus (returning the same way); pronoun: from Latin pronomen (pronoun).
