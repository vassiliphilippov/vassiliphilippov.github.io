condensed milk

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɛnst mɪlk / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnst mɪlk / (BrE)
Milk from which most of the water has been removed, usually with sugar added, resulting in a thick, sweet product used in cooking and baking.
