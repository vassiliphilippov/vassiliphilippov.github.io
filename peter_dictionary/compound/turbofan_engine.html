turbofan engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːboʊˌfæn ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌfæn ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a type of jet engine in which a turbine-driven fan provides additional air to the burner, increasing thrust and efficiency

Examples

I am recommending additional acquisitions of the improved version of the B-52 (the B-52 H with the new turbofan engine) and procurement of the B-58 supersonic medium bomber, together with the supporting refueling tankers in each case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbojet - a jet engine in which all the air passes through the combustion chamber and turbine

Etymology

From turbo- (from Latin 'turbo', meaning 'whirlwind') + fan engine.
