falling off

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑlɪŋ ɔf / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlɪŋ ɒf / (BrE)
A noticeable decrease or decline in performance, quality, or number.

Examples

The Protestants themselves are the first to admit the great falling off in effective membership in their churches.

Synset Examples

the team went into a slump
a gradual slack in output
a drop-off in attendance
a falloff in quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slump - a sudden severe or prolonged fall in the value, amount, or level of something
  • Synonym: drop-off - a decline or reduction in amount, quality, or number
  • Antonym: increase - an instance of increasing, growth or rise
