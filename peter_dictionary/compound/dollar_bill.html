dollar bill

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlər bɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə bɪl / (BrE)
A piece of paper currency worth one dollar, especially used in the United States.

Examples

When we separated that evening Pat pushed a hundred dollar bill into Eileen's hand to help towards a layette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banknote - A piece of paper money issued by a bank, used as legal tender

Etymology

From 'dollar', referring to the monetary unit, and 'bill', meaning a note or piece of paper money.
