pilot light

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlət laɪt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət laɪt / (BrE)
A small gas flame that burns continuously to ignite a larger burner in an appliance such as a stove or heater.

Examples

He found the pilot light and turned on one of the burners for her.

Etymology

From 'pilot' (one who guides) + 'light', reflecting its role as a guiding or igniting flame.
