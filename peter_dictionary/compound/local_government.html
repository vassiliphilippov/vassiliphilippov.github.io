local government

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl ˈɡʌvərnmənt / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl ˈɡʌvənmənt / (BrE)
The administration of public affairs and services at a local, rather than national, level; the governing body of a city, town, county, or district.

Examples

The Federal Government is aiding local governments in several places to survey residential, commercial and industrial buildings to determine what fallout protection they would provide, and for how many people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: central government - the government of an entire nation as opposed to local government
