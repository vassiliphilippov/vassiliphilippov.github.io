sacred writing

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪkrɪd ˈraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrɪd ˈraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
A text or document regarded as holy and venerated in religious practice, considered the word or teaching of a deity or held to have spiritual authority.

Examples

As Yinger has pointed out, the "reliance on symbols, on tradition, on sacred writings, on the cultivation of emotional feelings of identity and harmony with sacred values, turns one to the past far more than to the future ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacred text - writing considered holy or authoritative in a religion
  • Synonym: scripture - the sacred writings of a religion
