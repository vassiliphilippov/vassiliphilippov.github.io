out of sight

1. ADJ

/ aʊt əv saɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv saɪt / (BrE)
not visible; not within the range of vision.

Examples

We heard him before he ever showed, and we heard him yelling after he was out of sight.
There was an artificial lake just out of sight in the first stand of trees, fed by a half dozen springs that popped out of the ground above the hillside orchard.
From the time the chocks were pulled until the plane was out of sight, he knew Donovan would keep his back to the strip.

Synset Examples

concealed (or hidden) damage
in stormy weather the stars are out of sight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; concealed
  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen
