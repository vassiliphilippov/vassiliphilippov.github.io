first person

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst ˌpɝsən / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
The form of verbs or pronouns used to refer to the speaker or writer themselves, such as 'I' or 'we'.

Examples

SONNY: "I don'’t feel that way."

Related Terms

  • Antonym: second person - grammatical person referring to the person addressed (e.g., 'you')
  • Antonym: third person - grammatical person referring to others besides the speaker and listener (e.g., 'he', 'she', 'they')

Etymology

From Latin 'persona prima' meaning 'first person'.
