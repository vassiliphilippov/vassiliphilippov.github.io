Holy Ghost

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊli ˌgoʊst / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˌgəʊst / (BrE)
The third person of the Christian Trinity, regarded as the spiritual presence of God active in the world.

Examples

Do you believe in the Holy Ghost, the Holy Catholic Church?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy Spirit - alternative term for the third person of the Trinity

Etymology

From Old English 'hāliġ gāst', translating Latin 'Spiritus Sanctus'.
