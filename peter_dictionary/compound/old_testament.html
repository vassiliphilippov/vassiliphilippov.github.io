Old Testament

1. PROPN

/ oʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / əʊld ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
the first part of the Christian Bible, consisting of the books that were originally written in Hebrew and form the sacred scriptures of Judaism

Examples

Here, for the most part, they were well treated, as a "reminder of the Old Testament heritage of Christianity "; many of their gifted members were prominent in the Vatican as physicians, musicians, bankers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible, dealing with the life and teachings of Jesus and his earliest followers

Etymology

From Latin 'vetus testamentum', translating Greek 'hē palaia diathēkē' (the old covenant/agreement).
