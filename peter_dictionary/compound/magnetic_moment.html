magnetic moment

1. NOUN

/ məgˈnɛtɪk ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / mægˈnɛtɪk ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
A measure of the strength and orientation of a magnet or dipole, representing the torque it experiences in a magnetic field.

Examples

The magnetic moment of an unpaired electron associated nearby may have a tremendous influence on the magnetic resonance properties of nuclei.

Etymology

From 'magnetic' (relating to magnets) + 'moment' (meaning a measure or quantity); 'moment' in physics comes from Latin 'momentum', meaning 'movement, importance'.
