minute of arc

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪt əv ɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪt əv ɑːk / (BrE)
A unit of angular measurement equal to one sixtieth (1⁄60) of a degree.

Examples

The maximum angular diameter of any planetary disk as observed from the earth is about 1 minute of arc.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: second of arc - A unit of angular measurement equal to 1⁄60 of a minute of arc
  • Degree Higher: degree - A unit of angle equal to 1⁄360 of a circle

Etymology

From Latin *pars minuta* meaning 'small part', used in medieval Latin for subdivisions of angles.
