patron saint

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.trən ˌseɪnt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.trən ˌseɪnt / (BrE)
A saint regarded as the special guardian or protector of a particular group, nation, place, or occupation.

Examples

Yet paradoxically my liberal friends continue to view Jefferson as one of their patron saints.
The resultant town, platted in 1847 and named for the patron of Father Galtier's mission, St. Paul, was to become an important center of the fur trade and was to take on a new interest for those Selkirkers who remained at Red River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protector - a person or thing that protects or defends
  • Synonym: guardian - someone who guards or watches over; protector

Etymology

From Latin 'patronus' (protector, defender) and 'sanctus' (holy, saint).
