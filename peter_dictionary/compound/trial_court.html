trial court

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.əl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əl kɔːt / (BrE)
A court of law where a case is first tried and the facts are determined.

Examples

This Court agreed with the trial court "that considerations of price, quality and service were not overlooked by either du Pont or General Motors ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appellate court - court that hears appeals against trial court decisions
