law of nature

1. NOUN

/ lɔ əv ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
A general rule or principle that describes consistent facts or events in the natural world, often seen as universally valid.

Examples

Because community not severalty of property is the law of nature no man can assert an absolutely unalterable right to what is his.
In Berlin he published his views of the chemical laws of nature in German and this was issued in French translation (Paris, 1813) under the title Recherches sur l' identite des forces chimiques et electriques, a work held in very high esteem by the new generation of research chemists.
Believing that God is the Author of this law and of all laws of nature, Roman Catholics believe that they are obliged to obey those laws, not frustrate or mock them.

Synset Examples

the laws of thermodynamics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural law - a philosophy asserting certain rights and moral values are inherent by nature
