hue and cry

1. NOUN

/ hjuː ənd kraɪ / (AmE), / hjuː ənd kraɪ / (BrE)
a loud and public outcry, especially as a reaction to something considered wrong or unfair

Examples

And now, of course, the hue and cry for counter escalation is being raised on our side.

Synset Examples

he ignored the clamor of the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially of people shouting

Etymology

From Middle English 'hue' (shouting) and 'cry' (call), originally a legal term for a public alarm raised to pursue a criminal.
