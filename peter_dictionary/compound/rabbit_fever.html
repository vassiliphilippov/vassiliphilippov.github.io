rabbit fever

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt ˌfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt ˌfiːvə / (BrE)
A highly infectious disease (tularemia) affecting mainly rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, and sometimes transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or handling infected animals.

Examples

In tularemia produced by aerosol exposure, one would not expect to find the classical ulcer of "rabbit fever "on a finger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tularemia - the medical term for rabbit fever, an infectious disease in rodents and humans

Etymology

Named for its frequent occurrence in rabbits; 'fever' refers to one of its main symptoms.
