chaulmoogra oil

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔlˈmuːɡrə ɔɪl / (AmE), / tʃɔːlˈmuːɡrə ɔɪl / (BrE)
An oil obtained from the seeds of chaulmoogra trees, used especially in the past to treat skin diseases and leprosy.

Examples

Hell's own amount of chaulmoogra oil did nothing to alleviate their torment; they expired amid indescribable fantods, imploring the Blessed One to forgive their desecration.

Etymology

From Hindi 'chaulmoogra', referring to the tree from which the oil is extracted.
