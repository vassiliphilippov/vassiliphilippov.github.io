Fort Worth

1. PROPN

/ fɔrt wɝθ / (AmE), / fɔːt wɜːθ / (BrE)
A large city in northeastern Texas, west of Dallas, known for its industry and cultural institutions.

Examples

Rep. James Cotten of Weatherford insisted that a water development bill passed by the Texas House of Representatives was an effort by big cities like Dallas and Fort Worth to cover up places like Paradise, a Wise County hamlet of 250 people.
"Dallas and Fort Worth can vote bonds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dallas - major city adjacent to Fort Worth in Texas

Etymology

Named after Major General William Jenkins Worth, a U.S. Army officer.
