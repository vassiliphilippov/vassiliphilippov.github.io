circulatory system

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kjəˌleɪ.tɔːr.i ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kjʊ.lə.tər.i ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the system of organs and tissues, including the heart, blood, and blood vessels, responsible for circulating blood and lymph throughout the body

Examples

Their main asset is an abundance of unsaturated fatty acids, so necessary for maintaining the good health of the circulatory system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiovascular system - the system comprising the heart and blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'circulare' (to move in a circle) and late Latin 'systema' (system, organized whole), via French and English scientific terminology.
