wrought iron

1. NOUN

/ rɔt ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / rɔːt ˈaɪən / (BrE)
A form of iron with a very low carbon content, known for its toughness and malleability, commonly used for decorative railings and gates.

Examples

Griffith had confronted Hoag on the building's front steps - Hoag had been permitted no further - and backed him against a wrought-iron railing.

Etymology

From 'wrought,' past participle of 'work,' meaning 'worked iron.'
