psychosomatic disorder

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊsoʊˈmætɪk dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊsəʊˈmætɪk dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A disorder in which psychological factors cause or significantly affect physical symptoms.

Examples

We pointed out that emotional excitement may lead to psychosomatic disorders and neurotic symptoms, particularly in certain types of personality, but it is also known that the reliving of a strong emotion ("abreaction ") may cure a battle neurosis.

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + somatic (relating to the body), originally from Greek sōma (body).
