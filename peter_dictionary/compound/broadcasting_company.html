broadcasting company

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæstɪŋ ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdkɑːstɪŋ ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
A company that operates television or radio stations and is responsible for broadcasting content to the public.

Examples

a former Democratic candidate for President, a New Deal poet, the magazine's chief editorial writer, two newspaper columnists, head of a national broadcasting company, a popular Protestant evangelist, etc..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcaster - person or company that transmits TV or radio programs
  • Synonym: station - a company or facility transmitting radio or TV programs
