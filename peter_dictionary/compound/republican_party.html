Republican Party

1. PROPN

/ rəˈpʌblɪkən ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪkən ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
one of the two major political parties in the United States, traditionally associated with conservative and right-leaning policies

Examples

The Providence Daily Journal answered the Daily Post by stating that the raid of John Brown was characteristic of Democratic acts of violence and that "He was acting in direct opposition to the Republican Party, who proclaim as one of their cardinal principles that they do not interfere with slavery in the states ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Democratic Party - the other major political party in the United States, traditionally associated with liberal and left-leaning policies

Etymology

From 'republican', relating to a republic, and 'party', meaning a political group; formed in the United States in the 1850s.
