but then

1. ADV

/ bʌt ðɛn / (AmE), / bʌt ðen / (BrE)
Used to introduce a contrasting point or an alternative perspective.

Examples

But then the so-called coffee was bad enough at best, cold it was all but undrinkable - especially that cup!

Synset Examples

on the other hand, she is too ambitious for her own good
then again, she might not go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the other hand - used to introduce a contrasting point of view
  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a contrast or contradiction

2. ADV

/ bət ðɛn / (AmE), / bət ðɛn / (BrE)
Used to introduce a contrasting point or an alternative perspective.

Examples

But I want to carve the pieces myself.

Synset Examples

on the other hand, she is too ambitious for her own good
then again, she might not go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the other hand - introducing a contrasting point of view
  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
