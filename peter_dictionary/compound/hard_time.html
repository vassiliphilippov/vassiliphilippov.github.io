hard time

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
A period or experience of difficulty or trouble that can be overcome with effort.

Examples

Aeschbacher's work is very much akin to Schnabel's, but the sound on his Decca disc is dated, and you will have a hard time locating a copy of it.

Synset Examples

we had a hard time getting here
analysts predicted rough sledding for handset makers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough patch - a difficult or troubled period
  • Antonym: easy time - a period or experience without difficulty

2. NOUN

/ hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
A prison sentence served in a maximum security facility, often involving severe conditions.

Examples

Hey, you've done a stretch in Cashman, right?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stretch - a period of time spent in prison
