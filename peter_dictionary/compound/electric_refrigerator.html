electric refrigerator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ɹɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk rɪˌfrɪdʒəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
A refrigerator powered by electricity in which the coolant is circulated by an electric motor, as opposed to older icebox models.

Examples

They had cleaned up an old ice box and begun to buy fifty pound blocks of ice in town, as the electric refrigerator came nowhere near providing enough ice for the crowds who ate and drank there.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: icebox - a non-electric refrigerator cooled by ice blocks
