thickly settled

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɪkli ˈsɛtld̩ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkli ˈsɛtld / (BrE)
Having a large number of people living close together; densely populated.

Examples

It was thickly settled by fifteen thousand citizens and laid out into pig infested streets, mostly around the Battery, going bravely north to Wall Street, but giving up and becoming fields and farms in the region of Harlem Heights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: populous - having a large population; densely inhabited
  • Antonym: sparsely populated - with few people living in the area; not densely settled
