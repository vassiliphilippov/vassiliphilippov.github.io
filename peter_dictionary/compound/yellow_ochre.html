yellow ochre

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a natural earth pigment containing hydrated iron oxide with clay and silica, typically yellowish-brown in color and used as a paint and coloring agent

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ochre - a natural earth pigment ranging from yellow to deep orange or brown

Etymology

From ochre, from Greek 'ōkhra' (yellow earth), with 'yellow' indicating its color.
