small voice

1. NOUN

/ smɔl vɔɪs / (AmE), / smɔːl vɔɪs / (BrE)
a quiet inner voice that guides or judges one's actions, especially morally; conscience

Examples

In a properly ordered society the massive force of public law performs the function which in natural law theory ineptly is left altogether to a small voice so often still.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscience - an inner sense of what is morally right or wrong
