burnt sienna

1. NOUN

/ bɝnt siˈɛnə / (AmE), / bɜːnt sɪˈenə / (BrE)
a deep reddish-brown color, commonly used as an artist's pigment

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw sienna - a yellowish-brown earth pigment, the unburnt form of sienna
  • Degree Lower: reddish brown - a color between red and brown; the broader color category burnt sienna belongs to

Etymology

From Italian 'terra di Siena' (earth of Siena), after the city of Siena in Italy; 'burnt' refers to the pigment being calcinated to achieve the color.
