image orthicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmɪdʒ ˈɔrθɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒ ˈɔːθɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
an early type of television camera tube in which a photoemissive surface produces electrons in response to light, used to convert optical images into electronic signals (now obsolete)

Examples

Instead of the observer's eye the image orthicon in the TV camera does the "looking ".

Etymology

From 'image' + 'orthicon', the latter from Greek 'orthos' (straight, correct) and 'icon' (image).
