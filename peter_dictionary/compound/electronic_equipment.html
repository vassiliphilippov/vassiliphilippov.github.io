electronic equipment

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk ɪˈkwɪpmənt / (BrE)
Devices or machinery that operate using the controlled movement of electrons, especially through gases, vacuums, or semiconductors.

Examples

The author has recently studied the field of medical electronics and has been convinced that, in this area alone, the application of electronic equipment has enormous possibilities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: electrical equipment - devices or machinery powered by electricity rather than electronics
  • Synonym: electronic device - a single item of equipment based on electronics
