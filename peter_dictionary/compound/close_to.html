close to

1. ADV

/ kloʊs tu / (AmE), / kləʊs tuː / (BrE)
used to show that a number or amount is almost, but not exactly, a particular value; nearly, approximately

Examples

The 6 - foot 3 inch Hansen checked in close to 200 pounds, 15 pounds lighter than his reporting weight last spring.
It was going to be another hot day; already the thermometer stood close to ninety.
In Dunston the rent would run close to two hundred a month; in Medfield, perhaps twenty-five less, not all of it paid by Thayer, who could charge off one room on his expense account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughly - approximately, not exactly
  • Antonym: exactly - precisely, without any deviation

2. ADV

/ kloʊs tu / (AmE), / kləʊs tə / (BrE)
Used before a number, amount, or measurement to mean approximately but not exactly; nearly.

Examples

Close to a million bucks.
