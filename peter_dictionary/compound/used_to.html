used to

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuːzd tə / (AmE), / ˈjuːzd tə / (BrE)
Accustomed or familiar with something through experience.

Examples

It is a flavor that might take a little getting used to - not because it is unpleasant, but because the flavor is hard to define in the light of our experience with other fruits.
Publishers want books that will sell, recording studios want discs that will not seem strange to ears used to hillbilly and jazz music, grade and high schools want quaint, but moral, material.
I'm used to all three, but I think the French have the healthiest attitude ".
Bern demands that the Lord's Supper be administered here as it used to be, with unleavened bread.
He had ridden hard from Boston, and he was not used to horseback.

Synset Examples

I am used to hitchhiking
you'll get used to the idea
...was wont to complain that this is a cold world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accustomed - familiar with something; in the habit of
  • Antonym: unaccustomed - not familiar with something; not in the habit of

2. VERB

/ ˈjuzd tu / (AmE), / ˈjuːzd tuː / (BrE)
expressing a past habit or state that no longer exists; indicating something that happened regularly or was true in the past

Examples

You know, Mama used to tease me.

Synset Examples

I am used to hitchhiking
you'll get used to the idea
...was wont to complain that this is a cold world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accustomed to - familiar with something so that it seems normal or usual
  • Synonym: be in the habit of - to regularly do something as a habit
