trade name

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪd ˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈtreɪd ˌneɪm / (BrE)
The name used by a company to market a product or service, especially as distinct from its chemical, technical, or generic name; a brand name.

Examples

The drug's chemical name is listed, since most states require feed processors to use this name instead of the trade name on the feed tag.
In some instances, the trade name is shown in parentheses following the chemical name.
This indicates that this drug is being marketed under one trade name only or state regulatory organizations have approved its use on the feed tag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand name - the name given to a product by the company that makes it
  • Antonym: generic name - the standard or nonproprietary name for a product

Etymology

Formed from 'trade' (business, commerce) + 'name'.
