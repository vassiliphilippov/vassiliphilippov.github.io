state of affairs

1. NOUN

/ steɪt əv əˈfɛrz / (AmE), / steɪt əv əˈfeəz / (BrE)
the general condition or combination of circumstances at a particular time

Examples

I use this term to mean three things: a search for the human significance of an event or state of affairs, a tendency to look at wholes rather than parts, and a tendency to respond to these events and wholes with feeling.

Synset Examples

the present international situation is dangerous
wondered how such a state of affairs had come about
eternal truths will be neither true nor eternal unless they have fresh meaning for every new social situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: situation - set of circumstances in which one finds oneself
