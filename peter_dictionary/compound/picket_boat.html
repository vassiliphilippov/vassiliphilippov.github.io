picket boat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkɪt boʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt bəʊt / (BrE)
a small naval boat used for patrolling, guarding, or serving as an outpost, typically to watch for enemy movements or provide security for larger vessels

Examples

The British ships rolled at anchor, sent out picket boats and waited for orders from London.

Etymology

From 'picket' (military term for a guard or patrol) and 'boat'.
