lead pencil

1. NOUN

/ lɛd ˈpɛnsl̩ / (AmE), / lɛd ˈpɛnsəl / (BrE)
A pencil with a core made of graphite, used for writing or drawing.

Examples

After having mated, an Andrena female digs a hole straight down into the ground, forming a burrow about the size of a lead pencil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pencil - an instrument for writing or drawing, usually containing graphite

Etymology

The term 'lead pencil' comes from a historical misunderstanding; early pencils were believed to use lead, but actually contain graphite.
