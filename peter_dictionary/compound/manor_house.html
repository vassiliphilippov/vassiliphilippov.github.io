manor house

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.ɚ haʊs / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ə haʊs / (BrE)
A large country house, typically the home of a lord or wealthy person, especially in former times.

Examples

They want to own a junior-grade castle, or a manor house, or some modest little place where Shakespeare might once have staged a pageant for Great Elizabeth and all her bearded courtiers.
From there it looked across at Westchester County and the Hudson River where the manor houses, estates, and big farms of the original (non Indian) landowners began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Degree Higher: castle - a large fortified building or set of buildings
  • Degree Lower: cottage - a small, simple house, typically in the countryside

Etymology

From Middle English manor (from Old French manoir, dwelling, from manoir, to dwell) + house.
