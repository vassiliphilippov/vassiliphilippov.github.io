gravitational attraction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrævɪˈteɪʃənl əˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃənl əˈtrakʃən / (BrE)
the force by which objects with mass attract one another, especially the force that causes objects on Earth to be drawn toward its center

Examples

Also, planetary gravitational attraction increases the dust concentration near the plane of the ecliptic as the sun is approached.

Synset Examples

the more remote the body the less the gravity
the gravitation between two bodies is proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them
gravitation cannot be held responsible for people falling in love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - the force that attracts a body toward the center of the earth, or toward any other physical body having mass
  • Synonym: gravitation - the action or process of moving toward or being attracted to a place or person; in physics, the force causing attraction between masses

Etymology

From 'gravity' (Latin gravitas, 'weight') and 'attraction' (Latin attrahere, 'to draw towards').
