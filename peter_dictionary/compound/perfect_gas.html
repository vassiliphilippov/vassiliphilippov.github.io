perfect gas

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrfɪkt ɡæs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪkt ɡas / (BrE)
A hypothetical gas composed of molecules of negligible size and no intermolecular forces, obeying the ideal gas laws exactly.

Examples

The energy for any isothermal work done by the perfect gas must come as thermal energy from its surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideal gas - a hypothetical gas that perfectly fits all the assumptions of the kinetic-molecular theory
