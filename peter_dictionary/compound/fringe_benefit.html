fringe benefit

1. NOUN

/ frɪndʒ ˈbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / frɪndʒ ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
an additional benefit supplementing an employee's salary, such as a company car, health insurance, or pension, regarded as part of the compensation package

Examples

Above all, do n't set up extravagant fringe benefits just to buy employee good will.
Unions stress fringe benefits, but the individual hourly worker prefers cash every time.

Synset Examples

a limousine is one of the fringe benefits of the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perk - an extra benefit or advantage, especially one given by an employer

Etymology

From 'fringe' (an outer edge, here meaning something secondary or additional) + 'benefit'; usage dates to the mid-20th century in employment context.
