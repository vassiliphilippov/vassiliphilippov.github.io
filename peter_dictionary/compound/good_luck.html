good luck

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd lʌk / (AmE), / ɡʊd lʌk / (BrE)
Favorable or fortunate circumstances; success brought by chance rather than through one's own actions.

Examples

The din was successful, too, for just before the moon disappeared, the frightened toad had begun to spit it out again, which meant good luck all around.
The expression was his trade-mark, his open sesame to good luck, and his prayer that pilot and plane would always return.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad luck - unfortunate or adverse circumstances; the opposite of good luck
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck, especially as affecting someone

2. NOUN

/ ɡʊd lʌk / (AmE), / ɡʊd lʌk / (BrE)
Success or good fortune resulting from chance rather than effort.

Examples

I wish all of you good luck.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bad luck - adverse fortune or misfortune
  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external, arbitrary force
