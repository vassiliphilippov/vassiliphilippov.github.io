implicit in

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈplɪsɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪsɪt ɪn / (BrE)
existing as an essential or inherent part of something, though not directly expressed or obvious

Examples

The socialism implicit in the slogan of the Roosevelt Revolution, freedom from want and fear, seems a far cry from the individualism of the First Amendment to the Constitution, or of the Jacksonian frontier.

Synset Examples

shortcomings inherent in our approach
an underlying meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherent in - existing as a permanent or essential characteristic of something
  • Antonym: explicit in - clearly and directly stated in something

Etymology

From Latin 'implicitus' (entangled, involved) + English 'in'.
