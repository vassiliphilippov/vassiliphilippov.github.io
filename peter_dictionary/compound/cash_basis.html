cash basis

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ ˈbeɪsɪs / (AmE), / kæʃ ˈbeɪsɪs / (BrE)
an accounting method in which transactions are recorded when cash is received or paid, rather than when they are incurred

Examples

"And you know, Angelo, Pretty, he always keeps it a strict cash basis, like they say "-

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accrual basis - an accounting method where transactions are recorded when they are incurred, not when cash is exchanged
