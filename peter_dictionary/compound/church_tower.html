church tower

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
A tall structure that forms part of a church building, often containing bells or a clock.

Examples

To them he could have been the broken bell in the church tower which rang before and after Mass, and at noon, and at six each evening - its tone, repetitive, monotonous, never breaking the boredom of the streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steeple - a tall tower on a church, often with a spire
