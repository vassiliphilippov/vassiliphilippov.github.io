carbon tetrachloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən ˌtɛtrəˈklɔraɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌtɛtrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, nonflammable liquid chemical (CCl₄) formerly used as a solvent and cleaning fluid, now limited due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloroform - volatile, colorless liquid once used as an anesthetic and solvent
  • Synonym: trichloroethylene - volatile, colorless liquid used as a solvent, similar applications

Etymology

Formed from 'carbon' and 'tetrachloride', indicating four chlorine atoms bonded to a carbon atom.
