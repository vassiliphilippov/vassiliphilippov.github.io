triphosphopyridine nucleotide

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌfɑsfoʊpɪˈrɪdəˌniːn ˈnuːkliˌoʊˌtaɪd / (AmE), / trɪˌfɒsfəʊpɪˈrɪdɪniːn ˈnjuːklɪəˌtaɪd / (BrE)
a coenzyme, also known as NAD, involved in redox reactions in cellular metabolism

Examples

A microsomal de-iodinase with a pH optimum of around 8, and requiring reduced triphosphopyridine nucleotide for activity, has been identified in the thyroid (Stanbury, 1957).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NAD - common abbreviation for nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide, synonym
  • Synonym: coenzyme - an organic non-protein compound that assists enzyme function, synonym

Etymology

From 'triphospho-' (having three phosphate groups), 'pyridine' (a nitrogenous base), and 'nucleotide' (a monomer of nucleic acids).
