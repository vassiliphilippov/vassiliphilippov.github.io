chromic acid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊmɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A powerful, unstable acid (H2CrO4) known only in solution or as chromate salts, used as an oxidizing agent and in cleaning metal surfaces.

Examples

Douglass prepared his sample of f by thermal decomposition of aqueous chromic acid** at 300 - 325 ` C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromate - a salt containing the chromate ion (CrO4²⁻)

Etymology

From 'chromic' (relating to chromium) + 'acid'.
