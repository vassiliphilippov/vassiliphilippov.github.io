French ultramarine blue

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ ˌʌltrəˈmɛrin blu / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˌʌltrəˈmɛəriːn bluː / (BrE)
a deep blue pigment made by artificial synthesis of ultramarine, used in painting and coloring materials

Examples

alizarin crimson, orange, raw sienna, raw umber, burnt sienna, sepia, cerulean blue, cobalt blue, French ultramarine blue, Winsor green, Hooker's green 2, cadmium yellow pale, yellow ochre, Payne's gray, charcoal gray, Davy's gray, and ivory black ".

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ultramarine - a blue pigment originally made from lapis lazuli

Etymology

Named for its origin in France, where the synthetic pigment was first produced in the 19th century; 'ultramarine' from Latin 'ultramarinus' (beyond the sea).
