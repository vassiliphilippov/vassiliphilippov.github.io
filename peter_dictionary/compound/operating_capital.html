operating capital

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑpəˌreɪtɪŋ ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈɒpəreɪtɪŋ ˈkæpɪtəl / (BrE)
The funds a business uses for its day-to-day operations, as opposed to investments in long-term assets or financial transactions.

Examples

Commodity loans, operating capital loans, and facility loans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: working capital - the capital of a business used in its day-to-day trading operations
