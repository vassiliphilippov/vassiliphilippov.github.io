hydrostatic head

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈstætɪk hɛd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈstætɪk hɛd / (BrE)
the pressure at a specific point in a liquid, expressed as the height of a column of the same liquid that would exert an equivalent pressure

Examples

A hydrostatic head correction has been neither necessary nor applicable in the determination of vapor pressures or temperatures for the bulk liquid helium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure head - height of a fluid column that corresponds to a given pressure

Etymology

From 'hydrostatic' (from Greek 'hydro-' meaning water and 'statikos' meaning causing to stand) and 'head' meaning the height of liquid above a point.
