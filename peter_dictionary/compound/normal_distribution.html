normal distribution

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrməl dɪˌstrɪbˈjuʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl dɪˌstrɪbˈjuːʃən / (BrE)
a probability distribution that is symmetrical about the mean, showing that data near the mean are more frequent in occurrence than data far from the mean; often represented as a bell-shaped curve

Examples

The final sample was not significantly different from a normal distribution in regard to reading achievement or intelligence test scores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaussian distribution - another term for normal distribution
  • Antonym: skewed distribution - a probability distribution that is not symmetric

Etymology

From 'normal' (from Latin 'norma', a carpenter's square) + 'distribution' (from Latin 'distributio', division into parts).
