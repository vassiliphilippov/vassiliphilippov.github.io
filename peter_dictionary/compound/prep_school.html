prep school

1. NOUN

/ prɛp skuːl / (AmE), / prɛp skuːl / (BrE)
A private secondary school that prepares students for college or higher education, especially in the United States and Britain.

Examples

It would be interesting to know how much "integration "there is in the famous, fashionable colleges and prep schools of New England.
He was always well groomed and well tailored, and he had that rich man's look which was authentic enough and came from two good prep schools and a proper university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparatory school - school preparing students for college or public school

Etymology

Shortened from 'preparatory school'.
