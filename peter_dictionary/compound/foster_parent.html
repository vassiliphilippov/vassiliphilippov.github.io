foster parent

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑstər ˌpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈfɒstə ˌpeərənt / (BrE)
a person who cares for and raises a child in place of the natural parents, usually temporarily and without legal adoption

Examples

Jubal said that his night-sight probably came from the conditions in which he had grown up, and Mike grokked this was true but grokked that there was more to it; his foster parents had taught him to see.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adoptive parent - a person who has legally adopted a child

Etymology

From 'foster', meaning to bring up or care for, and 'parent'. 'Foster' from Old English 'fōstrian' (to supply with food, nourish).
