degree of freedom

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡri əv ˈfridəm / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriː əv ˈfriːdəm / (BrE)
An independent variable or parameter that can be varied in a statistical calculation or a physical system without violating any constraints.

Examples

The truck and the car gave the manufacturer a new degree of freedom in selecting his plant site.

Etymology

From 'degree' (Latin 'gradus', step) and 'freedom' (Old English 'frēodōm', liberty).
