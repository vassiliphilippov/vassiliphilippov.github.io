Kamchatka Peninsula

1. PROPN

/ kæmˈtʃætkə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / kamˈtʃætkə pɪˈnɪnsjʊlə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in eastern Siberia, Russia, situated between the Bering Sea and the Sea of Okhotsk

Examples

On November 4, 1952, an earthquake occurred under the sea off the Kamchatka Peninsula.

Etymology

From Russian полуо́стров Камча́тка (poluostrov Kamchatka), from native Kamchatkan languages.
