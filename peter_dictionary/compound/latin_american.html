Latin American

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæt̬.ɪn əˈmer.ɪ.kən / (AmE), / ˌlæt.ɪn əˈmeə.rɪ.kən / (BrE)
A person who is a native or inhabitant of Latin America, especially a country in Central or South America where Romance languages are spoken.

Examples

But the Latin American republics who have been rather inclined to drag their feet on taking action against Castro also reacted swiftly last week by finally throwing Cuba off the Inter-American Defense Board.
During the nineteenth century these views were protested by virtually all the Latin American writers, though ineffectively, just as the new nations of Africa and Asia protest them, with more effect, today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: South American - a native or inhabitant of South America

Etymology

Formed from 'Latin' (referring to Latin languages) and 'American', indicating someone from the parts of the Americas where Latin-based languages are spoken.
