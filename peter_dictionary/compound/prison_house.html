prison house

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzən haʊs / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən haʊs / (BrE)
A place or situation in which someone feels trapped or confined, often used metaphorically for emotional or social restriction.

Examples

The breaking away from the prison house of Brooklyn is gradual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime
