middle distance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈmɪdl ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
The part of a scene, especially in art or photography, that lies between the foreground and the background.

Examples

Long observation has taught Mason that most landscape can be reduced to three essential planes: a foreground in sharp focus - either a light area with dark accents or a dark one with lights; a middle distance often containing the major motif; and a background, usually a silhouetted form foiled against the sky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foreground - the part of a scene nearest to the viewer
  • Antonym: background - the part of a scene farthest from the viewer
