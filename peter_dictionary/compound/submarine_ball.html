submarine ball

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbməˌrin ˈbɔl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbməˌriːn ˈbɔːl / (BrE)
A pitch in baseball thrown with a sidearm or underhand motion, instead of an overhead delivery.

Examples

Then Dick Hyde, submarine ball hurler, entered the contest and only five batters needed to face him before there existed a 3 - to 3 deadlock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidearm pitch - A baseball pitch thrown from the side rather than overhead
  • Antonym: overhand pitch - A baseball pitch thrown overhand, in contrast to sidearm

Etymology

From 'submarine' (referring to the low, underhand throwing motion) and 'ball' (the baseball being pitched).
