body of water

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdi əv ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi əv ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
a large area of water such as a river, lake, sea, or ocean; any significant accumulation of water on the Earth's surface

Examples

A body of water is usually the center of interest at parks which attract the greatest picnic and camping use.

Synset Examples

they invaded our territorial waters
they were sitting by the water's edge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterway - a river, canal, or other route for travel by water
