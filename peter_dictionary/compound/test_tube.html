test tube

1. NOUN

/ tɛst tuːb / (AmE), / tɛst tjuːb / (BrE)
A small glass tube closed at one end, used in laboratories for holding, mixing, or heating substances.

Examples

One drop of each sample was added to one drop of a 2 % suspension of group * * f or group B red cells in a small * * f test tube.

Etymology

From 'test' (as in experiment) and 'tube', referring to its use in scientific testing.
