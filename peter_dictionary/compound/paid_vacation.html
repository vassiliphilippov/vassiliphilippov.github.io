paid vacation

1. NOUN

/ peɪd vəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / peɪd vəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a period of time when an employee does not have to work but still receives their salary

Examples

Among them, according to the U. S. Department of Commerce, are: (1) shorter work weeks, (2) higher pay, (3) longer paid vacations, (4) better transportation, (5) earlier retirement, and (6) more education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paid leave - time away from work during which an employee continues to be paid
  • Antonym: unpaid vacation - vacation from work without pay
