biceps brachii

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪsɛps ˈbreɪkiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪsɛps ˈbrækɪaɪ / (BrE)
a large muscle at the front of the upper arm that bends the elbow and rotates the forearm

Examples

Microscopic studies of the gastrocnemius, pectoralis major, transversus abdominis, biceps brachii, and diaphragm showed atrophy as well as varying degrees of injury ranging from swelling and vacuolization to focal necrosis of the muscle fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biceps - the main muscle at the front of the upper arm

Etymology

From Latin: 'biceps' (two-headed) + 'brachii' (of the arm).
