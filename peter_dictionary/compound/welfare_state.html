welfare state

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌfɛr steɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɛlfeə steɪt / (BrE)
A system in which the government assumes primary responsibility for the social and economic welfare of its citizens, often through programs such as health care, housing, pensions, and unemployment benefits.

Examples

Perhaps the lesson we should take from these pages is that the welfare state in England still allows wild scope for all kinds of rugged eccentrics.
They have also led the nation in the direction of a welfare state.
