hypodermic syringe

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈdɜrmɪk səˈrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈdəːmɪk sɪˈrɪndʒ / (BrE)
A syringe fitted with a hypodermic needle, used to inject substances under the skin or withdraw fluids from the body.

Examples

Successive 1 - ml fractions were then drawn off with a hypodermic syringe, starting at the top of the tube, and tested for agglutinin activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syringe - instrument for taking up and ejecting liquid in a thin stream

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'derma' (skin), combined with 'syringe,' from Greek 'syrinx' (pipe).
