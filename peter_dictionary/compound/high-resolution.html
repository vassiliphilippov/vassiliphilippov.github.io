high-resolution

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪ ˌrɛzəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ ˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
(of an image or device) producing images that are sharp and show fine detail.

Examples

Such high-resolution observations as these are needed at several wave lengths in order that the radio emission of the moon can be properly interpreted.

Synset Examples

high-resolution photography
a high-resolution lens
high-resolution television

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-resolution - (of an image or device) producing images that are not sharp or lack fine detail
