run batted in

1. NOUN

/ rʌn ˌbætɪd ˈɪn / (AmE), / rʌn ˌbætɪd ˈɪn / (BrE)
(in baseball) a run scored as a direct result of a batter's action, such as a hit, walk, or sacrifice, which enables a runner to cross home plate.

Examples

It was Gardner's second run batted in of the game and his only ones of the year.

Synset Examples

he had more than 100 rbi last season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: RBI - abbreviation of 'run batted in', a baseball statistic
