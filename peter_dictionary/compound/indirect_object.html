indirect object

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.daɪˈrɛkt ˈɑb.dʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkt ˈɒb.dʒɪkt / (BrE)
the noun or pronoun in a sentence that receives the benefit of the action of the verb, typically positioned between the verb and the direct object, or introduced by 'to' or 'for'

Examples

Don'’t argue with him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct object - the noun or pronoun that directly receives the action of the verb

Etymology

From Latin 'objectum' (thing put before), with 'indirect' indicating not the direct receiver of the action.
