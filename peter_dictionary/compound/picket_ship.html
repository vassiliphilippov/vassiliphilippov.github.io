picket ship

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkət ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt ʃɪp / (BrE)
A naval ship assigned to act as a guard or lookout, providing warning or surveillance for a fleet or strategic area.

Examples

So would radar picket ships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patrol boat - small naval vessel used for patrolling waters

Etymology

From 'picket' (a soldier or small unit positioned as a guard) + 'ship'.
