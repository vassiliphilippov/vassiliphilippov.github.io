bridged-T

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪdʒd ˈti / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdʒd ˈtiː / (BrE)
an electronic circuit consisting of a T-network with an additional shunt connected across the two series arms

Examples

A bridged-T type of bridge was used in the 10 - 16 Mc / sec range.

Etymology

From 'bridged' (referring to the additional connection) and 'T', describing the circuit's shape.
