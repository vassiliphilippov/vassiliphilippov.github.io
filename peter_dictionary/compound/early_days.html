early days

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝli deɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜːli deɪz / (BrE)
The initial stage or period in the development or existence of something.

Examples

Indonesia, in the early days of the Republic, leaned toward the West.
In the early days of a homogeneous population, the public school was quite satisfactory.

Synset Examples

during the youth of the project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts
  • Antonym: end - the final stage or close of something
