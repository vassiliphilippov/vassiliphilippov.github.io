bush jacket

1. NOUN

/ bʊʃ ˈdʒæk.ɪt / (AmE), / bʊʃ ˈdʒæk.ɪt / (BrE)
A loose-fitting jacket with four patch pockets and a belt, resembling a shirt, often worn for outdoor activities in warm climates.

Examples

And so, after a flight southeast to Savannakhet, we found ourselves bouncing along in a Jeep right behind the Land-Rover of Prince Boun Oum of Champassak, a tall man of Churchillian mien in a bush jacket and a ten-gallon hat from Texas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safari jacket - a similar type of jacket worn for safaris or in hot climates

Etymology

From 'bush' (referring to wild or rural areas in colonial Africa, Australia, and elsewhere) + 'jacket'.
