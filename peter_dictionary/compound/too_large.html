too large

1. ADJ

/ tuː lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tuː lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
larger than is desirable, acceptable, or suitable; excessively large

Examples

He must deal with the question of how to manage a part when it cannot be handled without relation to the whole - when the whole is too large to grasp.
But at this the one too-large cat suddenly became two cats, stretching.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: large - of considerable or relatively great size, extent, or capacity
  • Degree Higher: huge - extremely large
  • Antonym: too small - smaller than is desirable, acceptable, or suitable
