ring-around-a-rosy

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪŋ əˈraʊnd ə ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˌrɪŋ əˈraʊnd ə ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
A children's game in which players hold hands and dance in a circle, then squat or fall down at a signal, often accompanied by a nursery rhyme.

Examples

It is a sort of academic ring-around-a rosy and you solve it.

Etymology

Probably from traditional English nursery rhyme first published in the 19th century.
