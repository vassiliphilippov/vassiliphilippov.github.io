occipital lobe

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɪpɪtəl loʊb / (AmE), / əkˈsɪpɪtəl ləʊb / (BrE)
the rearmost part of each cerebral hemisphere of the brain, responsible mainly for vision

Examples

This resulted in damage to the occipital lobe and very probably to the left side of the cerebellum also.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parietal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex lying behind the frontal lobe and above the temporal lobe
  • Synonym: temporal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex located at the sides of the brain, beneath the temples
  • Synonym: frontal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex at the front of the brain, involved in decision making and problem solving

Etymology

From Latin 'occiput' (back of the head) and 'lobe' (a roundish projection or division).
