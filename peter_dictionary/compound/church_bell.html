church bell

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ bɛl / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ bɛl / (BrE)
A large bell, typically hung in a church tower, used especially to summon people to church services or mark the time.

Examples

In many Northern towns and cities meetings were held and church bells were tolled.
And in the morning when he woke up at ten the church bells were ringing.

Synset Examples

church bells were ringing all over town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell - a hollow metal object that makes a ringing sound when struck

2. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ bɛl / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ bɛl / (BrE)
A large bell in a church tower, typically rung to signal the time or to summon people to worship.

Examples

[CHURCH BELL TOLLING] Quick!

Synset Examples

church bells were ringing all over town

Related Terms
