blood transfusion

1. NOUN

/ blʌd trænsˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / blʌd trænzˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which blood or blood plasma is introduced into a person's bloodstream through a vein or artery

Examples

In 1953 the patient developed an unexplained anemia for which 15 blood transfusions were given over a period of 4 years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfusion - the act of transferring fluid, especially blood, into the body

Etymology

From Latin 'transfusio' (a pouring from one to another), from 'trans-' (across) + 'fundere' (to pour); combined with 'blood'.
