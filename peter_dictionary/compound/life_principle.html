life principle

1. NOUN

/ laɪf ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / laɪf ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
A hypothetical force or agent thought to be responsible for the distinctive functions and qualities of living organisms.

Examples

The central city areas, in other words, exhibit the two characteristics which violate the life principle of congregations of the major denominations: they have too few middle-class people; they mix middle-class people with lower-class residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vital force - a hypothetical force regarded as a source of the functions and qualities of living beings
