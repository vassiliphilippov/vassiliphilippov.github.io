cheap money

1. NOUN

/ tʃip ˈmʌni / (AmE), / tʃiːp ˈmʌni / (BrE)
a situation in which loans or credit can be obtained at low rates of interest, usually as a result of government policy to stimulate economic activity

Examples

At the same time, Roosevelt's Federal Housing Administration, coupled with Henry Morgenthau's cheap-money policy, permitted ordinary lower-middle-class families to build their own homes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dear money - credit available at high interest rates
