deck chair

1. NOUN

/ dɛk tʃɛr / (AmE), / dɛk tʃɛː / (BrE)
a folding outdoor chair with a wooden frame and a strip of canvas for sitting, typically used on decks or patios

Examples

He does n't think that potting them from a deck chair on the south side of the house with a quart glass of beer for sustenance is entirely sporting.

Etymology

From 'deck', as in the deck of a ship, where such chairs were commonly used, and 'chair'.
