hard worker

1. NOUN

/ hɑrd ˈwɝːkɚ / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
A person who puts a lot of effort and care into their work.

Examples

The engineer had more than seven years of experience in the firm, was well trained, was considered a hard worker, was respected by his fellow engineers for his technical competence and was regarded as a "comer ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or puts in little effort
  • Degree Higher: workaholic - a person who compulsively works long and hard hours
