sleeping pill

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ pɪl / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ pɪl / (BrE)
A medicine in the form of a tablet or capsule taken to help induce sleep.

Examples

The old nightmare which had caused him so many wakeful hours came charging in on him once more, only this time he could n't pacify it with a sleeping pill and send it away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a drug taken to calm or induce sleep
  • Antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases energy and alertness
