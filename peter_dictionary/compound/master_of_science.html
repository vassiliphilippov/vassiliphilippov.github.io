Master of Science

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɚ əv ˈsaɪ.əns / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stər əv ˈsaɪ.əns / (BrE)
An advanced academic degree in science, typically awarded after one or two years of graduate study following a bachelor's degree.

Examples

He also received a Master of Science degree from Texas A+I College and a Bachelor of Science degree from Southwestern State College, Weatherford, Okla..

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bachelor of Science - undergraduate degree in science
  • Degree Higher: Doctor of Philosophy - advanced doctoral degree, usually beyond Master's

Etymology

From Latin 'magister' (master) and 'scientia' (knowledge, science).
