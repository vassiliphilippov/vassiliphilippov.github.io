road show

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd ʃəʊ / (BrE)
A performance, exhibition, or entertainment that tours from place to place, usually for promotional purposes or to reach a wider audience.

Examples

The sets are remarkably elaborate for a road-show that does n't pause long in any one place, and they are devised so that they shift with a minimum of interruption or obtrusiveness.
