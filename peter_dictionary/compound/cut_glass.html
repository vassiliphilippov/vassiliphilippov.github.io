cut glass

1. NOUN

/ kʌt ɡlæs / (AmE), / kʌt ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
Glassware decorated with a pattern of facets or designs that are cut or ground into its surface.

Examples

He observed the Florentine vase in the hall, the Renoir painting in the library, as well as the long shelves of well-bound volumes; the pattern of the Oriental rug, the delicate cut-glass chandelier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etched glass - glass decorated by engraving or etching the surface
