chamber of commerce

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbɚ əv ˈkɑːmɚs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə əv ˈkɒməs / (BrE)
an association of local businesspeople formed to promote and protect their commercial interests within a community

Examples

These short, "streamlined "meetings usually are sponsored by local banks, Chambers of Commerce, trade associations, or other civic organizations.
Interesting evidence can be found in the results of the Chamber of Commerce elections, which took place three weeks before national elections.

Etymology

From French chambre de commerce (business chamber), adopted into English in the 18th century.
