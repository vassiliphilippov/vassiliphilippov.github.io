Federal soldier

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl ˈsoʊldʒər / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəɫ ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
A soldier who served in the Union Army of the United States during the American Civil War (1861–1865).

Examples

One Federal soldier wrote: "The docters is no a conte.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Union soldier - Member of the Union Army (Northern states), American Civil War
  • Antonym: Confederate soldier - Soldier in the Confederate Army during the American Civil War

Etymology

Federal, from Latin 'foedus' (covenant); soldier from Old French 'souldier'.
