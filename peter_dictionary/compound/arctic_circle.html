Arctic Circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtɪk ˈsɜrkəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪk ˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
an imaginary line of latitude around the Earth, near the North Pole, marking the northernmost point at which the sun can be seen during the winter solstice and the southernmost point of continuous daylight in the summer solstice

Examples

Nearly twenty-five years before, Captain John Davis had noted, as he sailed near the Arctic Circle, "a very great gulf, the water whirling and roaring, as it were the meeting of tides ".

Etymology

From Latin 'Arcticus' (northern, of the bear constellation) and 'circle'.
