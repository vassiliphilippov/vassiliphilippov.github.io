vowel sound

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaʊəl saʊnd / (AmE), / ˈvaʊəl saʊnd / (BrE)
A speech sound produced with the vocal tract open, characteristic of vowels such as 'a', 'e', 'i', 'o', and 'u'.

Examples

But there is every reason to regard deal as a monosyllable, and because of the fact that / l / commonly has the quality of / / when it follows vowel sounds, deal seems to be a perfectly satisfactory rhyme with real.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonant sound - a speech sound produced with the vocal tract partially or completely closed
