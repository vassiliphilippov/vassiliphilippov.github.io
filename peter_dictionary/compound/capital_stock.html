capital stock

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪt̬l stɑːk / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪtl stɒk / (BrE)
The total number of shares a corporation is authorized to issue under its articles of incorporation.

Examples

Shares of capital stock at $ 15 each in the latter company were payable at the Bank of Manchester or at various other Vermont banks.
The line soon lived up to its name, as local messages of moderate length could be sent for a dime and the company was quickly able to declare very liberal dividends on its capital stock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorized share capital - maximum value of shares a company can legally issue
  • Degree Lower: outstanding shares - shares currently held by shareholders
  • Degree Lower: issued shares - shares that have been issued by the company

Etymology

From 'capital', meaning wealth or assets, and 'stock', referring to ownership shares in a company.
