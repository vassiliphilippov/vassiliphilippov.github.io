country doctor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌntri ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌntri ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
A doctor who practices medicine in rural areas, typically far from modern hospitals and urban centers.

Examples

One would need to test this proposition carefully; after all, the large (and probably unreliable) Reader's Digest literature on the "most unforgettable character I ever met "deals with village grocers, country doctors, favorite if illiterate aunts, and so forth.

Synset Examples

do country doctors still make house calls?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physician - a medical doctor, especially in contrast to one in a rural setting
  • Synonym: general practitioner - a physician providing general medical care rather than specializing, often found in rural areas
  • Antonym: specialist - a doctor with advanced training in a specific area, often contrasted with rural doctors
