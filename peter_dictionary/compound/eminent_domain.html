eminent domain

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪnənt doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪnənt dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
The right of a government to take private property for public use, with payment of compensation.

Examples

Natural gas public utility companies would be given the right of eminent domain, under a bill by Sen. Frank Owen /3, of El Paso, to acquire sites for underground storage reservoirs for gas.

Etymology

From Latin 'eminentia' (prominence) and Old French 'demeine' (domain, property).
