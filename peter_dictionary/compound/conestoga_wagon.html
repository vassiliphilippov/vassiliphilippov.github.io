Conestoga wagon

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈstoʊɡə ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnɪˈstəʊɡə ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A large, broad-wheeled wagon with an arched canvas top, used in the 19th century by United States pioneers to cross the prairies.

Examples

The six ton Conestoga began to whip and shake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covered wagon - a wagon with a canvas cover used by pioneers

Etymology

Named after Conestoga, a region in Pennsylvania, USA, where the wagon originated.
