coronary artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəˌneri ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the aorta and supplies blood to the heart muscle

Examples

The coronary arteries were sclerotic and diffusely narrowed throughout their courses, and the right coronary artery was virtually occluded by a yellow atheromatous plaque 1.5 cm. distal to its origin.
The intima of the larger coronary arteries was thickened by fibrous tissue containing fusiform clefts and mononuclear cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aorta - the main artery carrying blood from the heart to the rest of the body
  • Antonym: vein - blood vessel carrying blood to the heart

Etymology

From Latin *coronarius* ('of a crown') referring to the encircling nature of these arteries; 'artery' from Greek *artēria* ('windpipe, artery').
