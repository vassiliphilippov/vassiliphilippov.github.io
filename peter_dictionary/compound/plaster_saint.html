plaster saint

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstɚ seɪnt / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə seɪnt / (BrE)
A person regarded as being or pretending to be morally perfect and lacking human faults, often used disapprovingly or ironically.

Examples

I'm not a plaster saint.

Synset Examples

he's no plaster saint

Etymology

Alludes to statues of saints made of plaster, which appear flawless but are not genuinely alive.
