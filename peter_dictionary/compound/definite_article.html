definite article

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
A determiner, such as 'the' in English, that indicates specificity and refers to a particular person or thing already mentioned or easily identified.

Examples

Turn them over to friends, as a reward for the friends he'’s had and to make sure that everything goes well after he'’s gone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indefinite article - a determiner (such as 'a' or 'an') used to refer to non-specific persons or things

Etymology

From Latin 'definitus' (defined, specific) + 'article' from Latin 'articulus' (small joint or section).
