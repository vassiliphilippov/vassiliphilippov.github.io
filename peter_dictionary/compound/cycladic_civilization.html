Cycladic civilization

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈklædɪk ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈklædɪk ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The Bronze Age civilization that developed on the Cyclades islands in the southern Aegean Sea, flourishing from about 3000 to 1100 BC, noted for its distinctive art and architecture.

Examples

Though the spread of this type of decoration in the Aegean has not yet been precisely determined, it seems to appear first in the Cyclades, which were among the leading exporters of pottery throughout the century.

Etymology

'Cycladic' from Cyclades, the group of Greek islands; 'civilization' from Latin 'civilis'.
