kinetic energy

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / kɪˈnɛtɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
The energy that a body possesses due to its motion.

Examples

Heat of condensation (work function) plus kinetic energy of the electrons impinging on the anode.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: potential energy - energy stored in an object due to its position or condition

Etymology

From Greek 'kinētikos' (of motion) and 'energy', from Greek 'energeia' (activity).
