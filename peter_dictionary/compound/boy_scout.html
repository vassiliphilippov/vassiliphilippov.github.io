Boy Scout

1. NOUN

/ bɔɪ skaʊt / (AmE), / bɔɪ skaʊt / (BrE)
A boy who is a member of the Boy Scouts, an organization that teaches practical skills and promotes citizenship, outdoor activities, and community service.

Examples

An old gentlemen next to me held a Boy Scout bugle to his lips and blasted away at every fourth step and during the interim shouted out, "V for Victory "!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boy Scouts - the organization of which a Boy Scout is a member
  • Antonym: Girl Scout - a girl who is a member of the Girl Scouts

Etymology

Formed from 'boy' and 'Scout', with 'Scout' from the name of the scouting movement founded by Robert Baden-Powell in 1908.
