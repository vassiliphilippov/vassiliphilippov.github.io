contour language

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.tʊr ˌlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tjʊə ˌlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A tone language in which meaning is distinguished by variations in pitch within a syllable or word, rather than by static pitch levels.

Examples

By contrast, even experienced linguists commonly know no more of the range of possibilities in tone systems than the over-simple distinction between register and contour languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: register language - tone language using level pitch distinctions

Etymology

From 'contour', referring to the shape or profile of pitch, and 'language'.
