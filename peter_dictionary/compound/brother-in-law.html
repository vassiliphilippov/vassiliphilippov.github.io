brother-in-law

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʌðər ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / ˈbrʌðər ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
The brother of one's spouse or the husband of one's sibling or sibling-in-law.

Examples

When Fred Powell's brother-in-law, Charlie Keane, moved into the dead man's home, the anonymous letter writer took no chances on Charlie taking up where Fred had left off and wasted no time on a first notice:

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sister-in-law - female relative by marriage; either the sister of one's spouse or the wife of one's sibling or sibling-in-law

Etymology

Formed from 'brother' + 'in law', indicating a familial relationship established by marriage.
