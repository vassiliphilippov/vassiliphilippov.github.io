junior high

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːniər ˈhaɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːniə ˈhaɪ / (BrE)
A school for young adolescents, usually including 7th and 8th grades, and sometimes 6th or 9th, between elementary and high school.

Examples

Grover also would require junior senior high teachers to have at least 24 semester hours credit in the subject they are teaching.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: high school - A secondary school typically for grades 9–12
