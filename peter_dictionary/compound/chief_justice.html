chief justice

1. NOUN

/ tʃif ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / tʃiːf ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
The judge who presides over the supreme court of a country or state.

Examples

Under Formby's plan, an appointee would be selected by a board composed of the governor, lieutenant governor, speaker of the House, attorney general and chief justice of the Texas Supreme Court.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: justice - a judge in a court of law, especially a supreme court

Etymology

From 'chief' (leader) and 'justice' (judge), both from Latin roots.
