emission spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪʃ.ən ˈspɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪʃ.ən ˈspɛk.trəm / (BrE)
The spectrum of electromagnetic radiation emitted by a substance or source that is self-luminous, typically observed as lines or bands at characteristic wavelengths.

Examples

Emission spectra indicated **f calcium and all other impurities much lower.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorption spectrum - spectrum formed when a substance absorbs electromagnetic radiation at specific wavelengths

Etymology

From Latin 'emissio' (a sending out) and Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image).
