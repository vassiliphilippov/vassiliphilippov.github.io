All Souls' Day

1. NOUN

/ ɔl ˈsoʊlz deɪ / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈsəʊlz deɪ / (BrE)
A Christian festival on November 2 dedicated to the remembrance and prayer for all the souls of the dead, especially those in purgatory.

Examples

On Wednesday morning, November 2, 1859, the Providence Daily Journal stated that although Brown justly deserved the extreme penalty, no man, however criminal, ought to suffer the penalty without a fairer trial.

Etymology

From Middle English 'All Sowlen day', translating Medieval Latin 'Commemoratio Omnium Fidelium Defunctorum' (commemoration of all the faithful departed).
