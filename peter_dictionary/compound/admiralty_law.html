admiralty law

1. NOUN

/ ˈædmərəlti lɔ / (AmE), / ˈædmɪrəlti lɔː / (BrE)
The branch of law that governs maritime activities, including shipping, navigation, waters, and ocean fisheries.

Examples

Admiralty law, the law merchant, and the host of problems which arise in private litigation because of some contact with a foreign country were all severed from the older Law of Nations and made dependent on the several national laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maritime law - Law governing activities on the sea and other navigable waters

Etymology

From 'admiralty', referring to the office of the admiral and its jurisdiction over naval and maritime matters.
