commuter traffic

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjuːt̬ɚ ˈtræfɪk / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːtə ˈtræfɪk / (BrE)
the movement of vehicles or passengers traveling regularly between home and work, especially during peak hours

Examples

The New York Central today handles 60 percent of all southbound commuter traffic coming into New York City.
This is a $ 14 million operation involving 3500 employees who work on commuter traffic exclusively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush hour - the busy part of the day when commuter traffic is heaviest
  • Synonym: traffic jam - a line of vehicles stopped or slowing down due to heavy traffic
