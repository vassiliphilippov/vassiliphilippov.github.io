iodoamino acid

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.oʊ.doʊˌæm.ɪ.noʊ ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əʊ.dəʊˌæm.ɪ.nəʊ ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
An amino acid that has iodine atoms attached to its molecule, often occurring in thyroid chemistry.

Examples

In addition, the iodoamino acid formed in largest quantity in the intact thyroid is di-iodotyrosine.

Etymology

From 'iodo-' (relating to iodine) + 'amino acid'.
