ultraviolet radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəˈvaɪəlɪt ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈvaɪələt ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
Electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, lying in the ultraviolet range of the spectrum.

Examples

A completely new insight into living cells and their structure will be possible by use of a new technique which replaces visible light with ultraviolet radiation and combines a microscope with a color - TV system to view the results.
Three different wavelengths of ultraviolet radiation are selected by the variable filters placed in front of the three mercury xenon lights which serve as the ultraviolet sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultraviolet light - light with wavelengths in the ultraviolet spectrum
  • Antonym: infrared radiation - electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light

Etymology

From ultra- (beyond) + violet, indicating radiation beyond the violet end of visible light.
