van der Waal's forces

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæn dɚ ˈvɑlz ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / ˌvan də ˈvɑːlz ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
relatively weak forces of attraction between neutral atoms or molecules, caused by temporary polarization effects induced by the presence of nearby particles.

Examples

However, a major factor in binding dirt is the attraction between surfaces that goes under the name of van der Waal's forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: London dispersion forces - the weakest type of van der Waals forces, arising from instantaneous dipoles

Etymology

Named after Dutch physicist Johannes Diderik van der Waals (1837–1923), who first described these forces.
