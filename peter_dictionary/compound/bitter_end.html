bitter end

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚ ˌɛnd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˌɛnd / (BrE)
the final point of something, especially after a long struggle or unpleasant experience

Examples

This was the bitter end, and Hudson seemed to know he was destined to failure.

Synset Examples

he was determined to fight to the bitter end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - the end or conclusion of something
  • Antonym: beginning - the start or commencement of something

Etymology

Originally a nautical term, 'bitter end' referred to the end of a rope ('bitt' being a post on a ship), later extended metaphorically to mean the very end of a situation.
