narrow escape

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæroʊ ɪˈskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ ɪˈskeɪp / (BrE)
A situation in which someone avoids danger, harm, or trouble by a very small margin.

Examples

I could tell you of plenty narrow escapes, but we take no notice of them now ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close call - a situation where someone almost suffered harm, loss, or danger
  • Synonym: near miss - an event in which harm or disaster is narrowly avoided
