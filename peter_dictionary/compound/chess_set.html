chess set

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛs sɛt / (AmE), / tʃɛs sɛt / (BrE)
A set consisting of a chessboard and the full complement of pieces required to play chess.

Examples

Rummaging through a stack of drawers nearby, you unearthed an antique French chess set in ivory and sandalwood, which, along with two box Kodaks, you added to your haul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chess - a board game of strategic skill for two players
