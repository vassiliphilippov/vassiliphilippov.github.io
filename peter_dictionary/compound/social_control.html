social control

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəl kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the ways in which a society or group regulates or enforces standards of behavior, often through rules, norms, or collective pressure

Examples

Religion usually acts as a powerful aid in social control, enforcing what men should or should not do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: social regulation - regulation of behavior by rules or norms in a society
