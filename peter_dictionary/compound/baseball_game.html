baseball game

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔl ɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbɔːl ɡeɪm / (BrE)
A game played between two teams of nine players who take turns batting and fielding, using a bat and ball, with the aim of scoring runs.

Examples

St. Louis University found a way to win a baseball game.
He trailed Ludie to the baseball game in the lot on Kingston Street near the Dutch Reformed.

Synset Examples

he played baseball in high school
there was a baseball game on every empty lot
there was a desire for National League ball in the area
play ball!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseball - the sport of baseball as a whole
  • Degree Lower: softball game - a similar bat-and-ball game but with a larger ball on a smaller field

Etymology

From 'baseball' (originating in 18th-century North American English), and 'game' from Old English 'gamen' (amusement).
