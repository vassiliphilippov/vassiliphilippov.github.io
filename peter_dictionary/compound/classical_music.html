classical music

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A traditional genre of music characterized by formal structure and complexity, typically associated with Western art music from the late 18th to early 19th centuries, but also including earlier and later works conforming to established forms.

Examples

[CLASSICAL MUSIC PLAYING OVER P.A.] WOMAN: Famiglia! ALL: Cent'’ anni!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jazz - A music genre with complex rhythms and improvisation, originating in African-American communities
  • Antonym: popular music - Music intended for or appreciated by a wide audience, often contrasted with classical music

Etymology

From 'classical', meaning relating to the highest standards of excellence, and 'music'; 'classical' from Latin 'classicus' (belonging to a class/division/rank).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A genre of music following established forms and conventions, typically composed in the Western tradition from the late 18th to early 19th centuries, and regarded for its formal structure and serious artistic value.

Examples

[CLASSICAL MUSIC PLAYING OVER P.A.] WOMAN: Famiglia! ALL: Cent'’ anni!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: popular music - music intended for mass audiences, typically contemporary styles
