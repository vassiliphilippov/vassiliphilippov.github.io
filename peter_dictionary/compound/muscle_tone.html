muscle tone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl toʊn / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl təʊn / (BrE)
The continuous and passive partial contraction of the muscles, which helps maintain posture and readiness for action.

Examples

Although in both emotions sympathetic symptoms are present, different autonomic somatic patterns underlie aggression and anxiety, respectively, as indicated by the rate of the excretion of the catecholamines, the state of the muscle tone, and the Mecholyl test.

Synset Examples

exercise improves muscle tone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: muscle flaccidity - lack of muscle tone or firmness
  • Degree Higher: muscle rigidity - abnormally increased muscle tone
  • Degree Lower: muscle hypotonia - abnormally decreased muscle tone
