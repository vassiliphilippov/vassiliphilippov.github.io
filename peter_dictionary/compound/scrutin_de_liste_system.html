scrutin de liste system

1. NOUN

/ skruːˌtæn də ˈliːst ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / skruːˌtæn də ˈliːst ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
An electoral system based on proportional representation in which voters choose between party lists, and the number of candidates elected from each list is determined by the percentage of votes cast for each list.

Examples

At the central level the scrutin uninominal voting system was selected over some form of the scrutin de liste system, even though the latter had been recommended by Duverger and favored by all political parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportional representation - an electoral system in which parties gain seats in proportion to the number of votes cast for them

Etymology

From French 'scrutin de liste' ('list ballot'), referring to a system where votes are cast for party lists.
