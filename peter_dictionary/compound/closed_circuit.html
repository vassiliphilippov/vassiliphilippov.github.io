closed circuit

1. NOUN

/ kloʊzd ˈsɜːrkɪt / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
an electrical circuit in which the current flows continuously in a complete loop, allowing a signal or charge to circulate.

Examples

Commonly used electronic devices which are found in practically every hospital are closed-circuit TV and audio systems for internal paging and instruction, along with radiation counters, timers, and similar devices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open circuit - an electrical circuit in which the path is incomplete and current cannot flow
