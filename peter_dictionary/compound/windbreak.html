windbreak

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌbreɪk / (BrE)
A hedge, fence, or row of trees or shrubs planted to reduce the force of the wind and protect farmland or buildings from erosion and damage.

Examples

Moving around camp we saw all kinds of camp stoves, lanterns, coolers, bedding, games, fishing tackle, windbreaks and sunshades.
