inclusion body

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkluʒən ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
a small structure within a cell, often found in association with certain diseases or infections

Examples

In some tumor sections small spherical bodies, possibly inclusion bodies (Littau and Black, 1952) stained more intensely than the rest of cytoplasm and probably contained more antigen.

Synset Examples

an inclusion in the cytoplasm of the cell
