standard deviation

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændɚd dɪˈvieɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈstændəd dɪˈvɪəʃən / (BrE)
A measure of the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of values, calculated as the square root of the variance.

Etymology

From 'standard' (norm) + 'deviation' (amount by which something differs from a mean), popularized in statistics in the late 19th century.
