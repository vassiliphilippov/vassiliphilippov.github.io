cerebral palsy

1. NOUN

/ səˈriː.brəl ˈpɔl.zi / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl ˈpɔːl.zi / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by impaired muscle coordination (spastic paralysis) and other disabilities, typically caused by damage to the brain before or at birth.

Examples

The electric gadget is most helpful when there are many crowned teeth and in individuals who are elderly, bedfast with a chronic disease, or are handicapped by disorders such as cerebral palsy or muscular dystrophy.

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) and Greek 'palsia' (paralysis); 'palsy' from Old French 'paralisie'.
