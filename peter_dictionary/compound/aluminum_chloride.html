aluminum chloride

1. NOUN

/ əˈlumɪnəm klaɪˈraɪd / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪnɪəm klaɪˈraɪd / (BrE)
a white or yellowish chemical compound (AlCl₃) used as a catalyst in chemical reactions and as a wood preservative

Examples

Of several methods employed for tagging chlorine with radiochlorine, the exchange of inactive chlorine with tagged aluminum chloride at room temperature was found to be the most satisfactory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferric chloride - a similar chemical compound used as an etching agent and in water treatment

Etymology

From 'aluminum' and 'chloride', indicating it is a compound of aluminum and chlorine.
