fox terrier

1. NOUN

/ fɑks ˈtɛr.i.ər / (AmE), / fɒks ˈtɛr.i.ə / (BrE)
A small, lively breed of terrier, usually black and white, formerly bred to dig out foxes during hunts.

Piepsam calls the cyclist "cur "and "puppy "among other things, and at the crisis of his fit a little fox-terrier stands before him and howls into his face.
