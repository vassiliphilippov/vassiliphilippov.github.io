teaching certificate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtitʃɪŋ sərˌtɪfɪkət / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃɪŋ səˌtɪfɪkət / (BrE)
an official document stating that the holder is qualified to teach, especially in public schools

Examples

Rep. Henry C. Grover, who teaches history in the Houston public schools, would reduce from 24 to 12 semester hours the so-called "teaching methods "courses required to obtain a junior or senior high school teaching certificate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaching credential - official qualification to teach (used in some regions)
