left fielder

1. NOUN

/ lɛft ˈfiːldər / (AmE), / lɛft ˈfiːldə / (BrE)
the player whose position is in left field, the area of the outfield to the left from the perspective of home plate, in baseball or softball.

Examples

In the last of the sixth inning Mike Deegan got up to bat and hit a fast ball over the left fielder's head.
The left fielder threw and it was a good one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outfielder - a player positioned in the outfield (left, center, or right field) in baseball/softball
