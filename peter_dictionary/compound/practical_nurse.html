practical nurse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræktɪkəl nɝs / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəl nɜːs / (BrE)
A nurse who has received the required training to be officially licensed by a state to provide routine care for the sick.

Examples

The Health Amendment Act of 1956 added $ 5 million for practical nurse training.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: registered nurse - a nurse who has graduated from a nursing program and passed a national licensing exam
