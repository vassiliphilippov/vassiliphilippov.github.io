Oxford English

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑks.fɚd ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒks.fəd ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ / (BrE)
The variety of English associated with Oxford University, often considered by some to be particularly refined, affected, or pretentious.

Examples

At once my ears were drowned by a flow of what I took to be Spanish, but - the driver's white teeth flashing at me, the road wildly veering beyond his glistening hair, beyond his gesticulating bottle - it could have been the purest Oxford English I was half hearing; I would n't have known the difference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Received Pronunciation - the standard accent of Standard English in Britain

Etymology

Named after Oxford University, in Oxford, England.
