sea power

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
The strength or capability of a nation to project force or influence at sea, especially by means of its navy.

Examples

The chevaux de frise, those sharp stakes and barriers around the fort at the Battery, pointed to a conflict between the town and sea power rolling in glassy swells as the tide came in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naval power - military strength at sea; similar to sea power
  • Antonym: land power - military strength on land; contrasting with sea power
