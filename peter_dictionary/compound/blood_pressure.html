blood pressure

1. NOUN

/ blʌd ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / blʌd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the force exerted by circulating blood on the walls of the blood vessels, usually measured to assess health, particularly as systolic and diastolic pressures

Examples

It is also possible to utilize a pressure transducer, mounted at the end of a catheter which is inserted into the heart's left ventricle, to indicate the blood pressure in the heart itself.
Russ visited two places without result and his blood pressure was down to zero.

Synset Examples

adult blood pressure is considered normal at 120/80 where the first number is the systolic pressure and the second is the diastolic pressure
