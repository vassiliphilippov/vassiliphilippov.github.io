out-of-doors

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊt əv ˈdɔrz / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈdɔːz / (BrE)
the open air; the space outside buildings; the outdoors

Examples

As a boy Roy Mason began the long process of extracting the goodness of the out-of-doors, its tang of weather, its change of seasons, its variable moods.

Synset Examples

he wanted to get outdoors a little
the concert was held in the open air
camping in the open

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdoors - the open air; outside; not indoors

Etymology

From the phrase 'out of doors', meaning outside the doors; first recorded in the 17th century.
