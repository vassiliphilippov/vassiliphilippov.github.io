solvating agent

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑl.veɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.veɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
A substance that facilitates the formation of a solvate by surrounding and interacting with the molecules or ions of another substance.

Examples

The washing process whereby soils are removed consists basically of applying mechanical action to loosen the dirt particles and dried matter in the presence of water which helps to float off the debris and acts, to some extent, as a dissolving and solvating agent.

Etymology

From 'solvate' (from Latin 'solvere', to loosen, dissolve) + 'agent'.
