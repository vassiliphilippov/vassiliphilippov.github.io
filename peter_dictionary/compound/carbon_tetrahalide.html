carbon tetrahalide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən ˌtɛtrəˈhælˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˌtɛtrəˈhælaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical compound consisting of one carbon atom bonded to four halogen atoms.

Examples

Because of the simplicity of the molecules, isotopic exchange reactions between elemental halogens and the corresponding carbon tetrahalides would appear to offer particularly fruitful possibilities for obtaining unambiguous basic kinetic data.

Etymology

From 'carbon' (from Latin 'carbo', meaning coal) + 'tetra-' (Greek for four) + 'halide' (from 'halogen', elements forming salts).
