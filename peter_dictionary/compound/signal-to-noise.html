signal-to-noise

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɪɡnəl tə ˈnɔɪz / (BrE)
The ratio of the strength of a desired signal to the level of background noise, often used to measure clarity or quality in audio, electronics, and scientific data.

Examples

A modulation frequency of 40 cps with an amplitude as small as possible, commensurate with reasonably good signal-to-noise quality, was used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal-to-noise ratio - the numerical expression of the difference between signal and noise level

Etymology

Formed from 'signal' (from Latin signum, 'mark') and 'noise' (from Old French, ultimately from Latin nausea, 'sea sickness').
