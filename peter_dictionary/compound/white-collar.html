white-collar

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪt ˌkɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌkɒlə / (BrE)
Relating to salaried professional or clerical work, especially as opposed to manual labour.

Examples

For some white-collar workers it is a welcome change from the regular job, and a physical conditioner.
The motifs of impersonal interdependence and insulation of residential communities have polarized; the schism between central city and suburb, Negro and White, blue collar and white collar can be viewed as symptomatic of this deeper polarization of trends in the metropolis.

Synset Examples

the coal miner's son aspired to a white-collar occupation as a bookkeeper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blue-collar - relating to manual work or workers, especially in industry

Etymology

From the typical white shirts worn by office workers, as opposed to the blue collars of manual workers, first used in the 1930s in the United States.
