vicious circle

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃəs ˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃəs ˈsɜːkl̩ / (BrE)
A situation in which one problem causes another problem, which then makes the first problem worse, creating a cycle of difficulties.

Examples

Dominated by the vicious circle of the university promotion system, the analyst looks down on and gets along poorly with the other three groups, although he cannot deny his debt to the collector.
Rather we see here another vicious circle.
How can the vicious circle be broken?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch-22 - a dilemma or difficult circumstance from which there is no escape because of mutually conflicting conditions
  • Synonym: vicious cycle - a cycle in which problems become progressively worse
