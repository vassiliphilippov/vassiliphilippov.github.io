sales tax

1. NOUN

/ seɪlz tæks / (AmE), / seɪlz tæks / (BrE)
A tax imposed on the sale of goods and services, calculated as a percentage of the price and collected from the buyer at the point of purchase.

Examples

the suburbanite naturally calls a local rather than a central city number if both are listed in an advertisement, especially if the local call eliminates city sales tax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value-added tax - a tax levied on the added value of goods and services at each stage of production or distribution
