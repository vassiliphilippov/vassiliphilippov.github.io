Mary Magdalene

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛri ˈmæɡdəˌlin / (AmE), / ˌmɛːri ˈmaɡdəliːn / (BrE)
A follower of Jesus in the New Testament, traditionally depicted as a woman Jesus healed of evil spirits and who witnessed his crucifixion and resurrection.

Examples

Listed as present at the Descent were Mary, Mary's sister, Mary Magdalene, John, Joseph of Arimathea, Nicodemus.

Etymology

From Magdala, a town on the Sea of Galilee, traditionally identified as her place of origin.
