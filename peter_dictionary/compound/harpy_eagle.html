harpy eagle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrpi ˌiːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpi ˌiːɡəl / (BrE)
a large black-and-white crested eagle of tropical America, known for its powerful build and striking appearance

Examples

There have been cooing doves, chattering magpies, thieving jackdaws, a proud peacock, a silly goose, and a harpy eagle - whom I was silly enough to mate with and who is now busy tearing at my vitals ".

Etymology

From 'harpy', after the mythological creature, referring to its fierce appearance, and 'eagle'.
