first half

1. NOUN

/ fɝst hæf / (AmE), / fɜːst hɑːf / (BrE)
The first of two equal parts into which something is divided, especially the initial period of a game, event, or time span.

Examples

True, the pattern and flow of the drama have strong literary qualities that are a bit wearisome in the first half, before Don Quixote goes to the duke's court.
The pottery of the first half of the eighth century is commonly called Ripe Geometric.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: second half - the latter of two equal parts, especially in games or periods
