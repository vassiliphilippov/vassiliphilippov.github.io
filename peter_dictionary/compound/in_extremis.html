in extremis

1. ADV

/ ɪn ɪkˈstriːmɪs / (AmE), / ɪn ɪkˈstriːmɪs / (BrE)
At the point of death or in a very critical or desperate situation.

Examples

Not long ago, I rode down with him in an elevator in Radio City; he was talking to himself thirteen to the dozen and smoking two cigars at once, clearly a man in extremis.

Etymology

From Latin 'in extremis' meaning 'in the last moments'.
