intercontinental ballistic missile

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚkənˈtɪnəntəl bəˈlɪstɪk ˈmɪsaɪl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkɒnˈtɪnəntəl bəˈlɪstɪk ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
A ballistic missile with sufficient range to travel from one continent to another, typically used for delivering nuclear warheads.

Examples

Notable in this category are the Jupiter and Thor intermediate range ballistic missiles, which have been successfully developed, produced, and deployed, but the relative importance of which has diminished with the increasing availability of the Atlas intercontinental ballistic missile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ICBM - abbreviation for intercontinental ballistic missile

Etymology

Formed from 'intercontinental' (between continents), 'ballistic' (relating to projectiles), and 'missile' (projectile weapon).
