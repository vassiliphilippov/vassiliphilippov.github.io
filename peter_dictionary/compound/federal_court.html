federal court

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl kɔːt / (BrE)
A court established by the authority of a federal government, dealing with matters under federal jurisdiction.

Examples

"With my trial coming up in Federal Court next week I would n't want that picture published ".

Related Terms

  • Antonym: state court - a court established by individual states, handling matters under state laws
