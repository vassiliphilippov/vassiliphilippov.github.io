magnifying glass

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnəˌfaɪɪŋ ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈmaɡnɪfaɪɪŋ ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A handheld lens with a handle, used to make objects appear larger by means of magnification.

Examples

From the curio cabinet on its south wall and the bureaus beneath, you abstracted seventeen ivory, metal, wood, and stone sculptures of Oriental and African origin, two snuffboxes, and a jade handled magnifying glass.

Synset Examples

the magnifying glass was invented by Roger Bacon in 1250

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand lens - a small, simple magnifier held in the hand for examining objects closely
