hash mark

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæʃ mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈhæʃ mɑːk / (BrE)
A badge or stripe, typically sewn onto a uniform, used to indicate the number of years of service a person has completed, especially in the armed forces or police.

Examples

This meant, concretely, that the patient could not read at all without making writing like movements of the head or body, became easily confused by "hasher marks "inserted between hand-written words and thus confused the mark for one of the letters, and could recognize a simple straight line or a curved one only by tracing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service stripe - a stripe on a military or police uniform indicating years of service

Etymology

From 'hash', originally denoting the 'number sign' (#), and 'mark', meaning a sign or symbol.
