snake oil

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk ˌɔɪl / (BrE)
A substance with no real medicinal value sold as a cure-all, especially one promoted with exaggerated or fraudulent claims.

Examples

The medical device pirate of today, of course, is a far more sophisticated operator than his predecessor of yesteryear - the gallus snapping hawker of snake oil and other patent medicines.

Etymology

Originally referred to oil purportedly extracted from snakes, sold as a cure-all remedy in 19th-century America.
