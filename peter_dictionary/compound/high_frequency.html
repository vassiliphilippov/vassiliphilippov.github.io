high frequency

1. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
A pitch or sound that is perceived as higher compared to other pitches; often refers to sound waves with a high rate of oscillation, measured in hertz.

Examples

There is an extraordinary sense of presence in all of these recordings, apparently obtained at least in part by emphasizing the middle and high frequencies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low frequency - a pitch or sound perceived as lower than others; low rate of oscillation

2. NOUN

/ haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈfriːkwənsi / (BrE)
(in electronics) radio frequencies in the range of 3 to 30 megahertz, often used for communication signals.

Examples

A high frequency starter was used to start the arc.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: very high frequency - radio frequencies in the range of 30 to 300 megahertz
  • Degree Lower: medium frequency - radio frequencies in the range of 300 to 3000 kilohertz
