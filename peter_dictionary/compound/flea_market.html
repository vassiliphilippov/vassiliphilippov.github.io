flea market

1. NOUN

/ ˈfli ˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈfliː ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
An open-air market where secondhand or inexpensive goods are sold, often from stalls or booths.

Examples

They went to the Flea Market, expecting to find the treasures of Europe, and found instead a duplication of that long double row of booths in Tours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swap meet - informal event where people exchange secondhand goods

Etymology

From the French 'marché aux puces' (market of fleas), referring to secondhand goods that might harbor fleas.
