register language

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒɪstər ˈlæŋgwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstə ˈlaŋgwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A language in which variations in tone are distinguished by differences in voice pitch level (register) rather than by pitch movement (contour).

Examples

By contrast, even experienced linguists commonly know no more of the range of possibilities in tone systems than the over-simple distinction between register and contour languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contour language - a tone language where pitch movement distinguishes meaning

Etymology

From 'register' (referring to vocal pitch level) + 'language.'
