Roman numeral

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.mən ˈnuː.mə.rəl / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən ˈnjuː.mə.rəl / (BrE)
A symbol used in the ancient Roman system of writing numbers, in which letters like I, V, X, L, C, D, and M are used to represent numbers.

Examples

I don'’t like the C note, Rosato.

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman) and 'numeralis' (of a number).
