piezoelectric effect

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːzoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌpiːzəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
The generation of electric charge in certain crystals when subjected to mechanical pressure, or a change in their dimensions when exposed to an electric field.

Examples

Position (2) appears to us to be unlikely in view of the absence of a piezoelectric effect and on general chemical structural grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piezoelectricity - electricity produced by pressure on certain materials

Etymology

From Greek 'piezein' (to press) + 'electric', relating to electricity.
