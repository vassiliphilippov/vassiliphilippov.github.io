Alexander the Great

1. PROPN

/ əˌlɛksəndɚ ðə ˈgreɪt / (AmE), / əˌlɛksəndə ðə ˈgreɪt / (BrE)
King of Macedon (356–323 BC) who created one of the largest empires of the ancient world and is famed for his military genius and for founding many cities, including Alexandria.

Examples

Alexander the Great, who used runners as message carriers, did not have to worry about having every officer in his command hear what he said and having hundreds of them comment at once.

Etymology

From Alexander (Greek: Alexandros, meaning ‘defender of men’), with 'the Great' as an honorific.
