Earth's surface

1. NOUN

/ ɝːθs ˈsɝːfɪs / (AmE), / ɜːθs ˈsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
the outermost layer of the land and sea that forms the exterior of the planet Earth

Examples

Most meteoritic material, by the time it reaches the Earth's surface, has been reduced to dust or to spherules of ablated material in its passage through the atmosphere.

Synset Examples

earthquakes originate far below the surface
three quarters of the Earth's surface is covered by water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - the outside layer or top part of something
