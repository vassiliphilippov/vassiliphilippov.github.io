in order

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ɪn ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
arranged or organized properly; in a correct or satisfactory state for use or occurrence

Examples

He found Elizabeth in the parlor and asked her to make sure everything was in order in the residential hall, and then to take charge of the office while the party was here.

Synset Examples

everything is in order for their arrival

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of order - not working, not in proper arrangement or condition

2. ADJ

/ ɪn ˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
arranged or prepared correctly and ready for use or a particular purpose

Examples

I thought champagne would be in order, ma'am.

Synset Examples

everything is in order for their arrival

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - fully prepared for use or action
  • Synonym: organized - systematically arranged or structured
  • Antonym: out of order - not functioning or not correctly arranged
