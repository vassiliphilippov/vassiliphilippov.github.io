Fourth of July

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrθ əv dʒʊˈlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθ əv dʒuːˈlaɪ / (BrE)
The national holiday in the United States commemorating the adoption of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776.

Examples

Ludie had a cigar box full of marbles and shooters, and a Roman candle from last Fourth of July.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Independence Day - The official national holiday commemorating U.S. independence

Etymology

Refers to July 4th, the date the U.S. Declaration of Independence was adopted in 1776.
