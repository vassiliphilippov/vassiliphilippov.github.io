alkali bee

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.laɪ biː / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪ biː / (BrE)
A solitary bee species (Nomia melanderi) native to North America, valued for its role in pollinating alfalfa, especially in the western United States.

Examples

Closely related to the andrenas are the nomias or alkali bees.
The life history of the alkali bee is similar to that of Andrena, but the first activity of the adults does not take place until summer, and the individuals hibernate in the prepupal stage.
The name "alkali bee "indicates that one has to look for them in rather inhospitable places.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nomia melanderi - scientific name for the alkali bee

Etymology

From 'alkali', referring to the alkaline soils in which the species commonly nests, and 'bee'.
