pipe dream

1. NOUN

/ paɪp driːm / (AmE), / paɪp driːm / (BrE)
A hope or plan that is impossible or extremely unlikely to be realized; an unrealistic or fanciful aspiration.

Examples

Like Eliot, in my fantasies, I had a proud bearing and, with a skill that was vaguely continental, I would lead Jessica through an evening of dancing and handsome descriptions of my newest exploits, would guide her gently to the night's climax which, in my dreams, was always represented by our almost suffocating one another to death with deep, moist kisses burning with love.
"To me you 'll always be the girl o' my dreams, an' the sweetest flower that grows ".

Synset Examples

I have this pipe dream about being emperor of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - an idea with no basis in reality; an imagined wish or hope
  • Synonym: delusion - a persistent false belief held despite evidence
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they are, rather than as imagined

Etymology

From the image of dreams experienced while smoking opium pipes, popularized in the late 19th century.
