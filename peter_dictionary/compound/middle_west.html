Middle West

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪdəl ˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ˈwɛst / (BrE)
the north-central region of the United States, often referred to as the heartland or breadbasket of America

Examples

A fourteen year old girl from the Middle West observed wryly that, in her community, religion inconveniently interfered with religious activities - at least with the peripheral activities that many middle class Jews now regard as religious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Midwest - the north-central region of the United States; alternative name

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' and 'west', referring to its central location in the western part of historical US territories.
