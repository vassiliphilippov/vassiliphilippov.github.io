hot dog

1. NOUN

/ hɑt dɑɡ / (AmE), / hɒt dɒɡ / (BrE)
A cooked sausage, usually made of beef or pork, served in a long, soft roll and typically eaten hot.

Examples

I want hot dogs, balloons, everything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankfurter - a type of smoked sausage similar to a hot dog
  • Degree Lower: sausage - highly seasoned minced meat usually stuffed in casings

Etymology

American English, early 20th century, possibly from German 'dachshund sausage' due to the sausage's shape.
