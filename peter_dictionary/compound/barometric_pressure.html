barometric pressure

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛrəˈmɛtrɪk ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˌbarəˈmɛtrɪk ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by the atmosphere, as measured by a barometer

Examples

Jubal would have bet his life that if Anne were to witness the Second Coming, she would memorize date, time, personae, events, and barometric pressure without batting her calm blue eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmospheric pressure - the pressure exerted by the atmosphere at a given point

Etymology

From 'barometer' (Greek baros 'weight' + -meter 'measure') + 'pressure'.
