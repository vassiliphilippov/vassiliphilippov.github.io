good night

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈnaɪt / (BrE)
A conventional expression used to bid someone farewell in the evening or before going to bed.

Examples

In or out, should I kiss her good-night?
He had taken her upstairs to say good night.
Now, as he passed the open counter that divided the assembly room from the business office, he nodded and said good night to the station keeper and his clerks, not stopping to hear the day-watch playback of his chewing out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodbye - a conventional expression of farewell
  • Antonym: good morning - a conventional expression of greeting in the morning

2. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈnʌɪt / (BrE)
A conventional expression used to wish someone well before parting or going to bed at night.

Examples

Good night sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodbye - a conventional expression used when parting
  • Antonym: hello - a conventional greeting when meeting someone
