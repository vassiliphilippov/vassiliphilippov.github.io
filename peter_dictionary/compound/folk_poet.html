folk poet

1. NOUN

/ foʊk ˈpoʊɪt / (AmE), / fəʊk ˈpəʊɪt / (BrE)
A writer who composes poetry in the folk tradition, often reflecting the language, themes, and concerns of ordinary people.

Examples

This has not, however, prevented publishers from labeling him a "folk poet ", simply because he is a rural one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folk singer - singer who performs traditional folk songs
  • Degree Lower: poet - writer of poems
