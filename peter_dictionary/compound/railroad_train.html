railroad train

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌrɔd treɪn / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrəʊd treɪn / (BrE)
A series of connected railway cars pulled by a locomotive, used for transporting passengers or goods over a railway; especially in American English.

Examples

On ships at sea, on railroad trains, in summer hotels with mountain views, they always said, "I 've never done this before ".

Synset Examples

express trains don't stop at Princeton Junction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - a connected line of railway cars pulled by a locomotive
