shock absorber

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑk əbˈzɔrbɚ / (AmE), / ʃɒk əbˈzɔːbə / (BrE)
A device fitted to a vehicle or machinery to absorb and dampen shock impulses, usually by converting the kinetic energy of the shock into another form of energy (typically heat).

Examples

Then he hunkered down on the heels of his handmade boots, peered into the orderly chaos of axle, shock absorber, and spring.

Synset Examples

the old car needed a new set of shocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damper - a device that reduces vibration or oscillation
