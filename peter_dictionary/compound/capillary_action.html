capillary action

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪˈlɛri ˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪˈlæri ˈækʃən / (BrE)
the movement of liquid within narrow spaces due to the forces of adhesion, cohesion, and surface tension, often resulting in the liquid rising or falling in a thin tube

Examples

In most cases, these soils are taken up as liquids through capillary action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capillarity - the phenomenon of liquid movement in narrow spaces due to surface tension

Etymology

From 'capillary', from Latin 'capillaris' (hair-like), referring to thin tubes, and 'action'.
