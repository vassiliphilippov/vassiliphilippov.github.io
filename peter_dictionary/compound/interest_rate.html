interest rate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrəst reɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪntrɪst reɪt / (BrE)
the percentage charged or paid for the use of money, usually expressed as an annual percentage of the amount borrowed or invested.

Examples

Interest rates are determined by the board of directors of the bank with the approval of the Farm Credit Administration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rate of return - the gain or loss on an investment over a specified period
