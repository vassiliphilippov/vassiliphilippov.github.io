state of matter

1. NOUN

/ steɪt əv ˈmætər / (AmE), / steɪt əv ˈmætə / (BrE)
Any of the distinct physical forms in which matter can exist, primarily solid, liquid, and gas, each characterized by specific physical properties.

Examples

So we may conceive the coexistence of the infinite number of universal, apparently momentary states of matter, successive one after another in consciousness, but permanent each on its own basic phase of the progressive frequencies.

Synset Examples

the solid state of water is called ice

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: solid - a state of matter with fixed shape and volume
  • Degree Higher: liquid - a state of matter with fixed volume and adaptable shape
  • Degree Higher: gas - a state of matter with no fixed shape or volume
