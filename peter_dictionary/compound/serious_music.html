serious music

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəs ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəs ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music of a formal or classical tradition, conforming to established forms and usually intended for attentive or critical listening, as opposed to popular or light music.

Examples

No reference is made to the possibility of recording other than popular music in this manner, and it would not seem to lend itself well to serious music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical music - traditional Western art music composed during the Classical period and more generally music in this tradition
  • Antonym: popular music - music intended for a wide audience, often with a strong rhythm and simple structure
