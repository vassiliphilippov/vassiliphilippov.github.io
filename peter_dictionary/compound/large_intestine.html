large intestine

1. NOUN

/ lɑrdʒ ɪnˈtɛstɪn / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ ɪnˈtɛstɪn / (BrE)
The part of the intestine that extends from the cecum to the rectum, responsible for absorbing water from indigestible food matter and forming feces.

Examples

The small and large intestines were filled with gas, and the jejunum was dilated to about 2 times its normal circumference.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: small intestine - the part of the intestine between the stomach and the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin 'intestinum' (gut, entrails), with 'large' referring to its diameter compared to the small intestine.
