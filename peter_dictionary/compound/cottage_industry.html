cottage industry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.tɪdʒ ˌɪn.də.stri / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.tɪdʒ ˌɪn.də.stri / (BrE)
A small-scale business or manufacturing activity carried out at home, often by family members using their own equipment.

Examples

Andy was a regular cottage industry.

Etymology

From 'cottage' (a small house) + 'industry' (manufacturing activity), referring to manufacturing in domestic settings before industrialization.
