free lunch

1. NOUN

/ ˌfri ˈlʌntʃ / (AmE), / ˌfriː ˈlʌntʃ / (BrE)
something gained or received without effort, payment, or obligation; often used to express the idea that nothing is really free.

Examples

No free ride.

Synset Examples

there is no free lunch in politics or Hollywood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free ride - benefit or advantage gained without cost or effort

Etymology

Originally referred to a complimentary meal served in bars to attract patrons, especially in 19th-century America.
