hour angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊɚ ˌæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈaʊə ˌæŋɡl / (BrE)
In astronomy, the angular distance measured westward along the celestial equator from the observer's meridian to the hour circle passing through a celestial body.

Examples

The measurements made with the NRL 50 - foot reflector, which is altitude azimuth mounted, would have shown a systematic change with local hour angle in the measured intensities of Venus and Jupiter if a substantial part of the radiation had been linearly polarized.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: right ascension - celestial coordinate measuring angular distance eastward from the vernal equinox
  • Synonym: sidereal time - time measured by Earth's rotation relative to fixed stars, often calculated using hour angle

Etymology

From 'hour', referring to time, and 'angle', describing the angular measurement in astronomy.
