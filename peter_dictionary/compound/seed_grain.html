seed grain

1. NOUN

/ sid ɡreɪn / (AmE), / siːd ɡreɪn / (BrE)
Grain of good quality that is reserved and used for planting, rather than for consumption.

Examples

He sputtered back to his feet and scrambled madly to pull his bags of seed grain forward.
Of all their worldly belongings, next to the oxen and his gun, the seed grain had been the most treasured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed - a grain or ripened ovule used for sowing to produce new plants
