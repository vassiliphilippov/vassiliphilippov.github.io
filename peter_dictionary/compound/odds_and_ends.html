odds and ends

1. NOUN

/ ɑdz ənd ɛndz / (AmE), / ɒdz ənd endz / (BrE)
small, miscellaneous items of little value or importance; various things left over or gathered together

Examples

The machine quack makes his Rube Goldberg devices out of odds and ends of metals, wires, and radio parts.
This evening, they were pushed in from the breakfast room, with odds and ends of dessert distributed over them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bits and pieces - small individual items of various kinds; miscellaneous things

Etymology

From the pairing of 'odds' (remnants) and 'ends' (leftover pieces), originally referring to leftover or miscellaneous items.
