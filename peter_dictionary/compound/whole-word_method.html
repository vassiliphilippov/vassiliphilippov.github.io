whole-word method

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl wɝd ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl wɜːd ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method of teaching reading by encouraging beginners to recognize whole words as single units, associating their printed and spoken forms rather than analyzing them into phonetic parts

Examples

While we had expected that compulsive children in the unstructured school setting would have difficulty when compared to those in the structured, we were surprised to find that the achievement of the high compulsives within the schools where the whole-word method is used in beginning reading compares favorably with that of the low compulsives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phonics - method of teaching reading by correlating sounds with letters or groups of letters
