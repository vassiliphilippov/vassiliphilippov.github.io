bathtub gin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæθˌtʌb ˌdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɑːθˌtʌb ˌdʒɪn / (BrE)
Homemade gin, especially that made illegally, as during Prohibition in the United States.

Examples

Some date it from woman suffrage, others from when women first began to challenge men in the marketplace, still others from the era of the emancipated flapper and bathtub gin.

Etymology

From 'bathtub' + 'gin', referring to the practice of making illicit spirits at home, sometimes with makeshift equipment such as bathtubs, especially during 1920s American Prohibition.
