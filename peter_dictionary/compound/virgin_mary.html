Virgin Mary

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɜrdʒɪn ˈmɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn ˈmeəri / (BrE)
the mother of Jesus Christ, especially honored in Christianity; particularly venerated in the Roman Catholic Church as the mother of God

Examples

"O Blessed Virgin Mary, Mother of God and our most gentle queen and mother, look down in mercy upon England, thy "dowry ", and upon us all who greatly hope and trust in thee.
"Faith of our Fathers: Mary's prayers / Shall win our country back to thee ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mary - the mother of Jesus in Christianity

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo Maria', meaning 'Mary the virgin'.
