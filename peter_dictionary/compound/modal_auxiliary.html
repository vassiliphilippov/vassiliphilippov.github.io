modal auxiliary

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.dəl ɔːɡˈzɪl.iˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dəl ɔːɡˈzɪl.i.əri / (BrE)
a verb, such as 'can', 'may', or 'will', used with another verb to express possibility, necessity, permission, or ability

Examples

I wouldn'’t want it to get around that you held back the money because you had second thoughts about the rebels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary verb - a verb used to help form the tense, mood, or voice of another verb

Etymology

From 'modal', relating to mode or manner, and 'auxiliary', from Latin auxilium (help).
