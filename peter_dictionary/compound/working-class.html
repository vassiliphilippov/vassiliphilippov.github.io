working-class

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋ klæs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ klɑːs / (BrE)
Belonging to or characteristic of the social group whose members are employed in manual or industrial work, especially for wages and without significant property or wealth.

Examples

The Ivy League enjoys no easy dominion here, and the boys are as likely to dress in rather foppish Continental fashion, or even in nondescript working class manner, as they are in the restrained, button-down Ivy way.

Synset Examples

party of the propertyless proletariat

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: middle-class - belonging to the social group between working class and upper class
  • Degree Higher: upper-class - belonging to the highest social class
