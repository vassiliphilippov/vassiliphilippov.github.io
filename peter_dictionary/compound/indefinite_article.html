indefinite article

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət ˈɑːtɪkl / (BrE)
a word such as 'a', 'an', or 'some' used to refer to a person or thing that is not specifically identified

Examples

He can corroborate our charges on enough counts for this committee to recommend a charge of perjury against Michael Corleone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: definite article - a word such as 'the' used to refer to a specific person or thing

Etymology

From Latin 'indefinitus' (not defined) + 'article', from Latin 'articulus' (joint, part).
