frontier settlement

1. NOUN

/ frʌnˈtɪr ˈsɛtlmənt / (AmE), / ˈfrʌntɪə ˈsɛtlmənt / (BrE)
A community or settlement established on the edge of explored or developed territory, often implying primitive conditions and challenges of early colonization or expansion.

Examples

But Thorpe saw also the hardships of pioneer existence, the cultural poverty of the frontier settlements, and the slack morality which abounded in the new regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pioneer settlement - a settlement established by pioneers in unexplored or undeveloped territories
