electric light

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɪk laɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɪk laɪt / (BrE)
A device that produces light by using electricity, typically consisting of a glass housing with a filament that glows when current passes through it.

Examples

The plant was located west of the Battenkill and south of the location of the former electric light plant.
On June 14, 1900 the Manchester Journal reported that an electrical engineer was installing an electric light plant for Edward S. Isham at "Ormsby Hill ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light bulb - a glass bulb containing a wire filament that produces light when electric current passes through
