tax advantage

1. NOUN

/ tæks ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / tæks ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a benefit conferred by law that reduces the amount of tax due on certain activities or investments

Examples

Proprietorships should get the tax advantages now accruing to corporations, e. g. the chance to accumulate capital so vital for growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax break - a provision that reduces taxes for certain activities or groups
