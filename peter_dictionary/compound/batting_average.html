batting average

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæɾɪŋ ˌævɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ ˌævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the proportion of attempts that result in success, used especially to measure performance or effectiveness in activities such as sales or business

Examples

The batting average of one success out of seven increased to one out of three.

Synset Examples

the salesman's batting average was 7 out of 12

Etymology

Originally from baseball, where it measures how often a batter gets a hit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬ɪŋ ˈævərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbætɪŋ ˈævərɪdʒ / (BrE)
(Baseball) a statistic that shows how often a batter gets a hit, calculated by dividing the number of hits by the number of official times at bat.

Examples

My batting average & & & ".
"What difference does your batting average make?

Synset Examples

Ted Williams once had a batting average above .400

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - the result obtained by adding several quantities and then dividing by their number
