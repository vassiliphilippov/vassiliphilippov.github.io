Mississippi River

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪs.ɪˈsɪp.i ˈrɪv.ər / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ɪˈsɪp.i ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
the chief river of the United States, rising in northern Minnesota and flowing south for about 2,350 miles (3,780 km) to the Gulf of Mexico

Examples

For many years the Northwest Company had its southern headquarters at Prairie du Chien on the Mississippi River, some 300 miles southeast of present-day St. Paul, Minnesota.
Below the fort, high bluffs extended uninterruptedly for six miles along the Mississippi River.

Etymology

Named from the Ojibwe word 'Misi-ziibi', meaning 'Great River'.
