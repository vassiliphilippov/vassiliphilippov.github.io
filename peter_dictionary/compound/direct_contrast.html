direct contrast

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛkt ˈkɑntræst / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˈkɒntrɑːst / (BrE)
a situation in which two things are compared and are strikingly different or opposite to each other

Examples

In direct contrast to the other Rhode Island editors, Samuel S. Foss of the Woonsocket Patriot outwardly condemned the trial as being completely unfair.

Synset Examples

in contrast to
by contrast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else
