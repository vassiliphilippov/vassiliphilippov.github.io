World War II

1. NOUN

/ wɜrld wɑr tu / (AmE), / wɜːld wɔː tuː / (BrE)
The global war fought from 1939 to 1945 between the Allied and Axis powers, resulting in significant worldwide impact.

Examples

Though the number of people flowing in and out of our metropolitan areas each day has increased tremendously since World War 2,, total annual rail commutation dropped 124 million from 1947 to 1957.
While there may be several such industries to which the model of this paper is applicable, the authors make particular claim of relevance to the explanation of the course of wages and prices in the steel industry of the United States since World War 2,.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second World War - another common name for World War II
  • Degree Lower: World War I - the earlier global conflict from 1914 to 1918

Etymology

Formed from 'world war' and Roman numeral II to denote the second such global conflict; first used shortly after World War I.

2. NOUN

/ wɜrld wɔr tu / (AmE), / wɜːld wɔː tuː / (BrE)
The global war fought mainly between the Allies and the Axis powers from 1939 to 1945, widely regarded as the most extensive and deadly conflict in human history.

Examples

That I served my country faithfully and honorably in World War II and was awarded the Navy Cross for actions in defense of my country.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: World War I - the global war of 1914–1918

Etymology

Named as the second 'world war', following World War I (1914–1918).
