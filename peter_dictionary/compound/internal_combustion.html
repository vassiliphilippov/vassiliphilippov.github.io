internal combustion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːnəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː.nəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (BrE)
the process of burning fuel inside an engine cylinder to produce power, as in an internal-combustion engine

Examples

Until internal combustion became cheap, he had to be near a railroad siding and a trolley line or an existing large community of lower-class homes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external combustion - the burning of fuel outside the cylinder, as in a steam engine
