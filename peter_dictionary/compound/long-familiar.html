long-familiar

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ fəˈmɪljɚ / (AmE), / lɒŋ fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
Well known or frequently experienced for a long time; closely or intimately known over an extended period.

Examples

The meaning of this, as we shall see, is that he had no fund of visual memory-images of objects as objects; and, therefore, he could not recognize even long-familiar things upon seeing them again.

Synset Examples

a long-familiar face
a well-known voice reached her ears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: familiar - well known from long or close association
  • Synonym: well-known - known by many people or widely recognized
