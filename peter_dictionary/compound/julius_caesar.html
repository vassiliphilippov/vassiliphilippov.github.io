Julius Caesar

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːliəs ˈsiːzər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːliəs ˈsiːzə / (BrE)
(100–44 BC) Roman general, statesman, and dictator who played a critical role in the events that led to the demise of the Roman Republic and the rise of the Roman Empire.

Examples

His ideal was Alexander of Macedon, as Napoleon's was Julius Caesar.

Etymology

From Latin Gaius Iulius Caesar.
