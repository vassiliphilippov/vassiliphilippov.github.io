physical ability

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪkəl əˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl əˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The capacity to perform actions or tasks that require bodily strength, coordination, or movement, as opposed to mental ability.

Examples

Have not our physical abilities already deteriorated because of the more sedentary lives we are now living?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mental ability - the capacity to perform tasks that require thinking or intellect
