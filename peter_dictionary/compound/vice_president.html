vice president

1. NOUN

/ vaɪs ˈprɛz.əˌdɛnt / (AmE), / vaɪs ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (BrE)
A person in an organization or government who is second in rank to the president and authorized to act as president in their absence.

Etymology

From Latin 'vice' (in place of) and 'president'.
