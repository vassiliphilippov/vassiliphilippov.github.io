voltaic pile

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈteɪɪk paɪl / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪɪk paɪl / (BrE)
The earliest type of electric battery, consisting of a series of voltaic cells placed in sequence and devised by Alessandro Volta.

Examples

He was stirred by the announcement of Volta's discovery of chemical electricity and he immediately applied the voltaic pile to experiments with acids and alkalis.
Thus, 1745 corresponded to the invention of the "Leiden "jar by Kleist, 1764 that of the electrophorus by Wilcke, 1782 produced the condenser of Volta, and 1801 the voltaic pile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - a device that produces electricity

Etymology

Named after Alessandro Volta, Italian physicist, and from 'pile', meaning a stack or heap.
