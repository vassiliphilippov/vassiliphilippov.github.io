linguistic unit

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
A segment or element of language, such as a sound, word, morpheme, or phrase, considered as a component in the analysis of linguistic structure.

Examples

- Syllables are linguistic units centering in peaks which are usually vocalic but, as has been noted, are consonantal under certain circumstances, and which may or may not be combined with preceding and / or following consonants or combinations of consonants.

Etymology

linguistic from Latin lingua (tongue, language) + unit from Latin unitas (oneness, unity).
