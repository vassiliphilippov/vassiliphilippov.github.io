last rites

1. NOUN

/ læst raɪts / (AmE), / lɑːst raɪts / (BrE)
the final religious ceremony, especially in Roman Catholicism, in which a priest anoints and prays for someone who is dying

Examples

The canons, in a body, had tried to force him on his deathbed to let them give him the last rites of the Church, but he had died still proclaiming salvation by faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anointing of the sick - the Catholic sacrament involving anointing the dying or ill with oil
