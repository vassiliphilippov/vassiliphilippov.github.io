Uncle Sam

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌŋ.kəl sæm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋ.kl̩ sæm / (BrE)
the personification of the United States government or the United States as a political entity, often depicted as a tall, thin man with a goatee and top hat decorated in the American flag's colors

Examples

"Uncle Sam "was, indeed, a rich uncle to Prokofieff, in those opulent, post-war victory years of peace and prosperity, bold speculations and extravaganzas, enjoyment and pleasure: "The Golden Twenties ".

Etymology

Originated in early 19th century America, possibly from the initials 'U.S.' (United States), personified as 'Uncle Sam.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl sæm / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkəl sæm / (BrE)
A personification of the United States government, often depicted as a tall, thin man with a white goatee, dressed in a top hat and red, white, and blue clothing.

Examples

I tell you... Uncle Sam!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United States - a country in North America, often referred to as the U.S. or U.S.A.

Etymology

Probably originated from the initials 'U.S.' for United States, associated with Samuel Wilson, a meat packer who supplied the army during the War of 1812.
