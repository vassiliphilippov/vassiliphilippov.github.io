bit part

1. NOUN

/ bɪt pɑrt / (AmE), / bɪt pɑːt / (BrE)
A small acting role in a play, film, or television show, often with few or no lines.

Examples

And for his playmate, Francis Coletta of West Warwick, who has a bit part, Billy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leading role - the most important acting role in a production

Etymology

From 'bit' (meaning a small portion) and 'part' (a role in a performance).
