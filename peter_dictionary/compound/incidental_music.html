incidental music

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈdɛn.tl̩ ˈmju.zɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈden.təl ˈmjuː.zɪk / (BrE)
music composed to accompany the action of a play, film, or other performance, often played during scene changes or intervals

Examples

[MUSIC CONTINUES] [CROWD APPLAUDS]

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soundtrack - music accompanying a film, broadcast, or other presentation

Etymology

From 'incidental' (from Latin 'incidentalis', happening as a result of something else) and 'music'.
