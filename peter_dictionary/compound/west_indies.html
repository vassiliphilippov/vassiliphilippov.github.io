West Indies

1. PROPN

/ wɛst ˈɪndiz / (AmE), / wɛst ˈɪndiːz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in the Caribbean Sea, stretching from near Florida to Venezuela, including the Greater and Lesser Antilles.

Examples

Also, we should not even to-day discount the fact that a region such as the coastal lowlands centering on Charleston had closer ties with England and the West Indies than with the North even after independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caribbean - region of the Caribbean Sea, including the islands and surrounding coasts

Etymology

Named after the mistaken belief by early European explorers that these lands were part of the Indies (South and Southeast Asia) when reached by sailing west from Europe.
