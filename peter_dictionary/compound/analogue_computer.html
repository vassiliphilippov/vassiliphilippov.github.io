analogue computer

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌlɔg kəmˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈænəlɒg kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
A computer that processes data represented by continuously variable physical quantities, such as electrical voltages or mechanical motion, rather than by discrete digital signals.

Examples

Outputs of the two systems are measured by a pulse timing circuit and a resistance bridge, followed by a simple analogue computer which feeds a multichannel recorder.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: digital computer - a computer that processes data using discrete digital signals

Etymology

From 'analogue', meaning 'a thing comparable to another', and 'computer'. 'Analogue' from French 'analogue', from Latin 'analogus', from Greek 'analogos' (proportionate).
