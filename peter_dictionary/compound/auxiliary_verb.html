auxiliary verb

1. NOUN

/ ɔɡˈzɪljəri vɜrb / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈzɪljəri vɜːb / (BrE)
a verb used with another verb to show tense, mood, voice, or condition, such as 'be', 'have', 'do', or modal verbs like 'can' and 'will'

Examples

Tom, what do I do now?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modal verb - a type of auxiliary verb expressing necessity or possibility

Etymology

From Latin 'auxiliarius' (helping) + 'verb'.
