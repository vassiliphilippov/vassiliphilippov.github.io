big leaguer

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡ ˈliɡɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈliːɡə / (BrE)
A member of a major-league baseball team, especially in the United States.

Examples

And one of the Milwaukee rookies sighed and remarked, "Wish I was 40, and a top grade big leaguer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: major leaguer - member of a major-league team (esp. baseball)
  • Antonym: minor leaguer - member of a minor-league baseball team

Etymology

From 'big league', referring to the top professional leagues in sports, especially baseball, plus the agent suffix '-er'.
