primary election

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri ɪˈlɛkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A preliminary election in which voters select candidates or delegates for a subsequent general election.

Examples

The Fulton County Grand Jury said Friday an investigation of Atlanta's recent primary election produced "no evidence "that any irregularities took place.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general election - the main election in which voters make the final choice among candidates
