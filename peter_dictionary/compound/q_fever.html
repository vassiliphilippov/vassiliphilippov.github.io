Q fever

1. NOUN

/ kju ˈfi vɚ / (AmE), / kju ˈfiː və / (BrE)
An infectious disease caused by the bacterium Coxiella burnetii, characterized by flu-like symptoms such as fever, headache, and muscle pain.

Examples

Agents that are known to cause frequent infections among laboratory workers such as those causing Q fever, tularemia, brucellosis, glanders, coccidioidomycosis, etc., belong in this category.

Etymology

The 'Q' stands for 'query', denoting the disease's unknown cause when first identified.
