proton magnetic resonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.tɑn mæɡˈnɛt.ɪk rɪˈzɒ.nəns / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tɒn mæɡˈnɛt.ɪk rɪˈzɒ.nəns / (BrE)
A method of studying the structure of substances by measuring the resonance of protons in a magnetic field in response to electromagnetic radiation.

Examples

A proton magnetic resonance study of polycrystalline **f as a function of magnetic field and temperature is presented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear magnetic resonance - the resonance phenomenon of nuclei in a magnetic field; basis of NMR spectroscopy
