heat sink

1. NOUN

/ ˈhit sɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈhiːt sɪŋk / (BrE)
A device or material, typically made of metal, designed to absorb and dissipate heat from another object or system.

Examples

In other words, if an ideal gas is compressed and kept at constant temperature, the work done in compressing it is completely converted into heat and transferred to the surrounding heat sink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiator - a device for radiating heat, often used in engines or electronics

Etymology

From 'heat' (thermal energy) + 'sink' (something that absorbs or takes in).
