hydrogen peroxide

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrədʒən pərˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən pəˈrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, viscous liquid (H₂O₂) with strong oxidizing and bleaching properties, used as a mild disinfectant and, in concentrated form, as a propellant or industrial oxidizer

Examples

If tyrosine and a system generating hydrogen peroxide are added to a cell-free homogenate of the thyroid, large quantities of free mono-iodotyrosine can be formed (Alexander, 1959).

Etymology

Formed from 'hydrogen' and 'peroxide'; 'peroxide' from French *peroxyde*, from 'per-' (through) and 'oxide'.
