head up

1. VERB

/ hɛd ʌp / (AmE), / hɛd ʌp / (BrE)
to lead or be in charge of a group, organization, or activity

Examples

His maneuvering for the shot had placed him near the overcast, almost inverted and heading up into the clouds.
Miss Bouton headed up one of the four groups that went on simultaneous tours after the Gallery had closed at 5 p. m..

Synset Examples

This student heads the class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - to be in charge or command of
  • Antonym: follow - to come after or be guided by another
