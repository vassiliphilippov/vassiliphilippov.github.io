drop in

1. VERB

/ drɑp ɪn / (AmE), / drɒp ɪn / (BrE)
to visit someone informally and without arranging it beforehand

Examples

The Moiseyev Dance Company dropped in at Madison Square Garden last night for the first of four farewell performances before it brings its long American tour to a close.

Synset Examples

We frequently drop by the neighbors' house for a cup of coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop by - visit someone informally and without notice
  • Synonym: call on - make a short visit to someone, often for social reasons

2. VERB

/ drɑp ɪn / (AmE), / drɒp ɪn / (BrE)
To visit someone informally and without prior arrangement.

Examples

Feel free to drop in.

Synset Examples

We frequently drop by the neighbors' house for a cup of coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call in - to visit briefly, especially while on another errand
  • Synonym: stop by - to make a brief visit
