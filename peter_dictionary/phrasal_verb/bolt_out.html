bolt out

1. VERB

/ boʊlt aʊt / (AmE), / bəʊlt aʊt / (BrE)
to leave or exit a place suddenly and in a hurry

Examples

The buckskin bolted out of the stall.

Synset Examples

The listeners bolted when he discussed his strange ideas
When she started to tell silly stories, I ran out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run off - to leave a place or person quickly and suddenly
  • Synonym: dash out - to leave or go out quickly and suddenly

Etymology

From 'bolt' (to dash or spring suddenly) + out.
