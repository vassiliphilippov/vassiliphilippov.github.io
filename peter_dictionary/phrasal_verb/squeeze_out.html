squeeze out

1. VERB

/ skwiz aʊt / (AmE), / skwiːz aʊt / (BrE)
to extract liquid or substance from something by applying pressure and squeezing it

Examples

Somehow Dr. Charles Leale was forced through the mob and squeezed out into the dingy corridor.

Synset Examples

wring out the washcloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wring out - extract liquid by twisting and squeezing

2. VERB

/ skwiz aʊt / (AmE), / skwiːz aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something out of a position, place, or opportunity, typically by applying pressure or competition

Examples

This might be done to arouse those who have been squeezed out by the trims to exert pressure on the Legislature, so it would be more receptive to a tax proposal later in the year.

Synset Examples

Some employees were squeezed out by the recent budget cuts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oust - to force someone out of a position or place

3. VERB

/ skwiz aʊt / (AmE), / skwiːz aʊt / (BrE)
to obtain or produce something only with great difficulty or effort, especially when resources are scarce

Examples

Maggie and Stuart pored over figures every night, trying to find how they could squeeze out a few pennies more.

Synset Examples

He eked out a living as a painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eke out - to manage to make a supply last longer by using or adding small amounts
