fall off

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɑf / (AmE), / fɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
(of something attached or fixed) to become detached and drop from where it was attached.

Examples

When you see a needle in my hands you will know the family buttons have fallen off and I have to sew them back on, or get out the safety pins.

Synset Examples

This button had fallen off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: come off - become detached from a surface or place
