sleep in

1. VERB

/ slip ɪn / (AmE), / sliːp ɪn / (BrE)
to sleep until later in the morning than is usual, especially by choice or as a form of rest

Examples

"I think I 'll sleep in this morning ", she said drowsily, and as she snuggled against him, he wondered if she ever went to church.

Synset Examples

On Sundays, I sleep in

Related Terms

  • Antonym: get up early - to rise earlier than usual in the morning
