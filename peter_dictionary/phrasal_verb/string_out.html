string out

1. VERB

/ strɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / strɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or spread out in a line, series, or succession, especially over a distance

Examples

When they turned in the saddle they could see the men behind them, strung out on the prairie in a flat black line.

Synset Examples

the houses were strung out in a long row

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread out - to extend or arrange over a wider area
  • Antonym: cluster - to group or gather closely together
