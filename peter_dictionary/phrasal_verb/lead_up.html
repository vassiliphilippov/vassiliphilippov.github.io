lead up

1. VERB

/ lid ˈʌp / (AmE), / liːd ˈʌp / (BrE)
to be an event, action, or series of actions that prepares the way for something to happen, especially an important event

Examples

The campaign leading to the election was not so quiet, however.
The minister, describing the attacks which led up to the appeal, said that 60000 Communist North Vietnamese were fighting royal army troops on one front - near Thakhek, in southern-central Laos.
"Lead up!
Lead up!

Synset Examples

Hitler's attack on Poland led up to World War II

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precede - to come before in time or order
