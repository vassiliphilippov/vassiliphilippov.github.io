clear out

1. VERB

/ klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place, removing all of your possessions or making sure nothing is left behind

Examples

Just cleared out.
They just all cleared out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move out - to leave one’s home or place of residence permanently
  • Synonym: evacuate - to leave a place, often quickly or for safety reasons

2. VERB

/ klɪr aʊt / (AmE), / klɪə aʊt / (BrE)
to remove everything from a place or container so that it is completely empty

Examples

I want all this cleared out before the Warden gets back.

Synset Examples

We cleaned out all the drawers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empty - to remove all contents from something
