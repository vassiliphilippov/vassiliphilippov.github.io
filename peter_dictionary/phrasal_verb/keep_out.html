keep out

1. VERB

/ kip aʊt / (AmE), / kiːp aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from entering a place; to shut out

Examples

But now we can keep it out no longer, because we have come into a time when "it invades our experience at every moment.
Radio reception is cut down by the shielding necessary to keep out radiation.
"Keep out of this ", Purvis snarled.

Synset Examples

The trees were shutting out all sunlight
This policy excludes people who have a criminal record from entering the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclude - to deliberately prevent someone or something from entering or being included
  • Antonym: admit - to allow someone or something to enter
