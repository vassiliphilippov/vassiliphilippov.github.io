come around

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kʌm əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
To change your opinion or decision, especially after being persuaded.

Examples

Or else the North really believes that all Southerners except a few quaint old characters have come around to realizing the errors of their past, and are now at heart sharers of the American Dream, like everybody else.

Synset Examples

He came around to our point of view

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change one's mind - to adopt a different opinion or decision
  • Antonym: stick to one's guns - to refuse to change one's opinion or decision
