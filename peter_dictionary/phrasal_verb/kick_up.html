kick up

1. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something, especially dust or dirt, to rise into the air by striking the ground with one's feet or with another object

Examples

He came spurring and whooping down the road, his horse kicking up clouds of dust, shouting:

Synset Examples

kick up dust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir up - cause something, especially dust or trouble, to become active or disturbed

2. VERB

/ kɪk ʌp / (AmE), / kɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to cause or provoke (trouble, fuss, dust, etc.) to appear or increase suddenly

Examples

"I doubt they'll kick up any fuss.

Synset Examples

Her behavior provoked a quarrel between the couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir up - to cause or provoke (something, especially trouble or strong emotions)
  • Antonym: quiet down - to become or make less loud or agitated
