cave in

1. VERB

/ keɪv ɪn / (AmE), / keɪv ɪn / (BrE)
(of a structure, surface, or person) to fall down or collapse suddenly, either physically or figuratively.

Examples

At first the patient felt stronger, and the hemoglobin rose to 13.8 gm., but on Oct. 20, 1958, he complained of "caving in "in his knees.
The whole fucken sky's caved in!

Synset Examples

The wall collapsed
The business collapsed
The dam broke
The roof collapsed
The wall gave in
The roof finally gave under the weight of the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: give way - (of a structure or person) to yield or break under pressure
