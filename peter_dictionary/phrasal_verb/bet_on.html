bet on

1. VERB

/ bɛt ɑn / (AmE), / bɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to risk money by guessing the result of an event and stating in advance which outcome you think will occur, especially in gambling.

Examples

Rep. Berry, an ex gambler from San Antonio, got elected on his advocacy of betting on the ponies.
A bright sun and brisk wind had the track in a fast condition for the first time this week and 8280 St. Patty Day celebrants bet $ 842617 on the well prepared program.

Synset Examples

Which horse are you backing?
I'm betting on the new horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: back - to support or place a bet on (especially in horse racing)
  • Synonym: gamble - to risk money on an uncertain outcome

2. VERB

/ bɛt ɑn / (AmE), / bɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to risk money by predicting the outcome of a future event, such as which horse will win a race or which team will win a game

Examples

It's as good a thing to bet on as any, I guess.

Synset Examples

Which horse are you backing?
I'm betting on the new horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - risk money on the outcome of an event
  • Synonym: back - support a person or team by betting money on their success
