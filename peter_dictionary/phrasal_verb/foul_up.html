foul up

1. VERB

/ faʊl ʌp / (AmE), / faʊl ʌp / (BrE)
To make a mistake that ruins or spoils something; to mess up.

Examples

I give you my word I 'm not trying to pull some stupid kind of joke, or to deliberately foul up the expedition.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly; to ruin by clumsy action
  • Synonym: screw up - to make a serious mistake; to ruin something
