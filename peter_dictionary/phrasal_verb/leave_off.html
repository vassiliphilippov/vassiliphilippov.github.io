leave off

1. VERB

/ liv ɔf / (AmE), / liːv ɒf / (BrE)
to cease or stop doing something; to bring an action or activity to an end.

Examples

And they would n't leave off arguing and pleading until he had promised.
When Siamese cats are intertwined it is difficult to tell where one leaves off and another begins.

Synset Examples

the road leaves off at the edge of the forest
leave off where you started

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - to bring or come to an end
  • Synonym: cease - to bring or come to an end
  • Antonym: start - to begin doing something (opposite meaning)
  • Antonym: continue - to persist in an action or activity (opposite meaning)

2. VERB

/ liv ɔf / (AmE), / liːv ɒf / (BrE)
to omit or remove something from a group, list, or set; to not include

Examples

(Leave off the ham and you call it Chicken Pontiac, says Ramsey.)
Make same as salt and pepper shakers, leaving off top pieces.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclude - to deliberately not include something or someone
  • Antonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or set
