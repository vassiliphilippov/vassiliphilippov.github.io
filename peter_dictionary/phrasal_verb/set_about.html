set about

1. VERB

/ sɛt əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / sɛt əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to begin to do or deal with something, especially with determination

Examples

The authors set about answering this fundamental question through a detailed investigation of the patient's ability, tactually, (1) to perceive figure and (2) to locate objects in space, with his eyes closed (or turned away from the object concerned).
Postmaster General Burleson set about to protect the American people against radical propaganda that might be spread through the mails.
A former du Pont official became a General Motors vice president and set about maximizing du Pont's share of the General Motors market.

Synset Examples

approach a task
go about a difficult problem
approach a new project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tackle - to deal with or attempt to solve a problem or task
  • Synonym: undertake - to begin or take on a task or responsibility

2. VERB

/ sɛt əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / sɛt əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to begin or start doing something, especially a task or project

Examples

He had enlisted in the Army straight out of high school and had immediately set about learning his new trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embark on - to start or begin a project, venture, or activity

3. VERB

/ sɛt əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / sɛt əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to begin doing or undertaking a task, activity, or enterprise

Examples

And I've got to go to work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertake - to take on or begin a task or responsibility
  • Synonym: embark on - to start to do a new project or activity
  • Antonym: finish - to bring a task or activity to an end
