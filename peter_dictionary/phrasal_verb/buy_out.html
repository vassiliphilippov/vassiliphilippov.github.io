buy out

1. VERB

/ baɪ aʊt / (AmE), / baɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to purchase the entire ownership or controlling share of a company or a person's stake, often to gain full control

Examples

He called the Cuban tractor plan an outright blackmail action, and noted that in war "you can n't buy yourself out and that's what we 're trying to do ".
Twenty years before a group of Easterners had bought out the Haskell claims in the rocky hills south of Grass Valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take over - to assume control of something, especially a business

2. VERB

/ baɪ aʊt / (AmE), / baɪ aʊt / (BrE)
To purchase the entire share or interest in a company or organization, thereby gaining full ownership or control.

Examples

No, I buy you **out**.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take over - to gain control of, especially a business
