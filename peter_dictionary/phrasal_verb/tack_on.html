tack on

1. VERB

/ tæk ɑn / (AmE), / tæk ɒn / (BrE)
to add something as an extra part, especially in a way that seems incidental or not fully integrated

Examples

In all, the Senate signed a check for $ 46.7 billion, which not only included the extra $ 3.5 billion requested the week before by President Kennedy, but tacked on $ 754 million more than the President had asked for.

Synset Examples

append a charm to the necklace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: append - add as a supplement or attachment
  • Synonym: attach - join or fasten to something
  • Antonym: remove - take away or get rid of

Etymology

From the verb 'tack' (to fasten with tacks or attach), likely drawing on the literal sense of physically fastening something with a tack.
