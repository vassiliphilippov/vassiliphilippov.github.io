yield up

1. VERB

/ jiːld ʌp / (AmE), / jiːld ʌp / (BrE)
to surrender or give up something, especially as a result of pressure or force

Examples

Because he copiously shed his own tears, and yielded himself up as a living sacrifice to the impersonalized conscience of New England, he was not disturbed by the havoc he worked in other people's consciences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give up - to stop trying to do something; to surrender or relinquish
