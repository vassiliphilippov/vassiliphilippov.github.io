look out on

1. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt ɒn / (BrE)
(of a building, window, etc.) to face or have a view of something from a particular position.

Examples

The window looked out on the Place Redoute - it was the only window of the apartment that did.
Its front was windowless, but irregularities in the masonry might be an indication that windows, now blinded, had once looked out upon the street.

Synset Examples

The house looks out on a tennis court
The apartment overlooks the Hudson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - to have a view of from above
