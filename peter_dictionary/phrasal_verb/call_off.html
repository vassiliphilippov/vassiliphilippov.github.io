call off

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɔf / (AmE), / kɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
To cancel or stop something that was planned or scheduled.

Examples

Sunday New Orleans brunches continue at the Trade Winds but the daily French buffets have been called off.

Synset Examples

Call off the engagement
cancel the dinner party
we had to scrub our vacation plans
scratch that meeting--the chair is ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that an event will not take place

2. VERB

/ kɔl ˈɔf / (AmE), / kɔːl ˈɒf / (BrE)
To cancel or stop something that was planned or scheduled.

Examples

Maybe we should call it **off**.

Synset Examples

Call off the engagement
cancel the dinner party
we had to scrub our vacation plans
scratch that meeting--the chair is ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that something already planned will not happen
