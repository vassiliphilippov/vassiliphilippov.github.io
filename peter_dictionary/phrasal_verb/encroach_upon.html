encroach upon

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkroʊtʃ əˌpɑn / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃ əˌpɒn / (BrE)
To gradually intrude on or take over the rights, property, or territory of someone else.

Examples

He did build a framework of academic "respectability ", and one which did not encroach upon the "sacred sovereignty "of any other existing campus course.

Synset Examples

This new colleague invades my territory
The neighbors intrude on your privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrude on - to enter without permission or welcome
  • Synonym: invade - to enter forcefully or without permission

Etymology

From Anglo-French *encrochier* (to seize, fasten upon), from en- (in) + crochier (to catch with a hook).
