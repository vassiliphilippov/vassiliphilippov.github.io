camp out

1. VERB

/ kæmp aʊt / (AmE), / kæmp aʊt / (BrE)
to sleep outdoors or stay for a period in temporary shelter, such as a tent, especially for enjoyment or necessity

Examples

Each year he invites the boys to camp out on his estate for one of their big week ends of the year.

Synset Examples

Can we go camping again this summer?
The circus tented near the town
The houseguests had to camp in the living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - to stay in a tent or temporary shelter while traveling or for recreation
