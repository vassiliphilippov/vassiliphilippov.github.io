stamp down

1. VERB

/ stæmp daʊn / (AmE), / stæmp daʊn / (BrE)
to suppress or put an end to something, especially by using force or authority

Examples

More of this stamping down of human emotion as a young lawyer in New York.

Synset Examples

suppress a nascent uprising
stamp down on littering
conquer one's desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to something by force; to restrain
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to
