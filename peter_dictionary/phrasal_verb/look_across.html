look across

1. VERB

/ lʊk əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / lʊk əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to face or have a view in a particular direction, especially across an area or towards something

Examples

From there it looked across at Westchester County and the Hudson River where the manor houses, estates, and big farms of the original (non Indian) landowners began.
Pat paused there, looking across at the young fellow.

Synset Examples

The house looks out on a tennis court
The apartment overlooks the Hudson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - to have a view over something from above
