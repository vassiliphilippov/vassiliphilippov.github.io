talk into

1. VERB

/ tɑk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something, especially something they may not want to do at first

Examples

As Hartweger accepted his silver bowl, he said, "I want to thank coach's wife for talking him into letting me play ".
He had talked one other member of the group to stay with him, but that friend had tired of not eating regularly and returned to Savannah.
Somehow he talked Spencer into letting him use another name ".
Attending the life class was my idea - or rather, Askington's idea, but I was ripe for it, and the other two would n't have gone if I had n't talked them into it.
She had talked her "boy friend "into sending her to New York to take a screen test.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: talk out of - persuade someone not to do something
  • Synonym: persuade - convince someone to do something

2. VERB

/ tɔk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something, especially something they may be reluctant to do

Examples

Did Kay talk you into it?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: talk out of - to persuade someone not to do something
  • Synonym: persuade - to convince someone to do or believe something
