weight down

1. VERB

/ weɪt daʊn / (AmE), / weɪt daʊn / (BrE)
To burden or oppress by adding a heavy load, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

As Broadway itself becomes increasingly weighted down by trite, heavy-handed, commercially successful musicals and inspirational problem dramas, the American theatre is going through an inexorable renaissance in that nebulous area known as "off-Broadway ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or cause to feel oppressed
  • Antonym: lighten - to make less heavy or less burdensome
