step out

1. VERB

/ stɛp aʊt / (AmE), / stɛp aʊt / (BrE)
to go outside a room or building for a short time, especially briefly or unexpectedly

Examples

He stepped out into the hall, was delighted to encounter a water brother.
As we stepped out into the sunlight, a man came up to John Cool and silently showed him his hand.
"Probably just stepped out ", he said.
Pausing in the outside door to glance behind him, Pat looked his unspoken warning and stepped out.
Shayne opened the door on his side and stepped out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: step in - to enter a room or building

2. VERB

/ stɛp aʊt / (AmE), / stɛp aʊt / (BrE)
To leave a room or building briefly, especially to go outside for a short time.

Examples

They'll be out any second.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: step in - to enter a room, building, or situation briefly
  • Synonym: pop out - go out for a short time, often quickly or unexpectedly
