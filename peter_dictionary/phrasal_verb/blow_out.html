blow out

1. VERB

/ bloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
To become damaged or unusable suddenly, especially by bursting, breaking, or burning out (e.g. a lightbulb, fuse, or muffler).

Examples

She stood up, pulled the coat from her shoulders and started to slide it off, then let out a high-pitched scream and I let out a low-pitched, wobbling sound like a muffler blowing out.
There had been a good second or two during which my muffler had been blowing out, and now I was certain I 'd seen her somewhere before.

Synset Examples

The lightbulbs blew out
The fuse blew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn out - to stop working or become unusable due to burning or overheating
