farm out

1. VERB

/ fɑrm aʊt / (AmE), / fɑːm aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange for someone to do work or to be cared for by another person or organization, usually for payment and often temporarily; to assign or subcontract tasks or people to others.

Examples

You certainly could n't take them into the little apartment and if you tried to farm them out for two or three days every week they would become so confused that they would have nervous breakdowns.

Synset Examples

We rent out our apartment to tourists every year
He hired himself out as a cook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subcontract - employ another party to complete part of a task or service
  • Synonym: hire out - to allow to be used by others for payment
  • Antonym: retain - to keep or hold in one's own control, opposite of assigning to others

Etymology

From the 17th-century practice of 'farming' (leasing out) revenues or jobs; figurative use developed from the notion of leasing land or services for profit.

2. VERB

/ fɑrm aʊt / (AmE), / fɑːm aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange for work or tasks to be done by others, especially by contracting another person or company to do it

Examples

In a rare fit of anger and spite, I "farmed out "my own husband to a small and most undistinguished studio to make one picture as a form of punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subcontract - to contract work out to another person or company

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'farm' in the sense of leasing out land, extending to the sense of delegating work to others.
