thin out

1. VERB

/ θɪn aʊt / (AmE), / θɪn aʊt / (BrE)
to make something less dense by removing some parts, especially to reduce the number of plants, people, or items in a group

Examples

There never was a season before, but now they want to thin 'em out on account of the drouth ".

Synset Examples

thin out the young plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thin - to make or become less thick or dense
  • Antonym: densify - to make more dense or compact
