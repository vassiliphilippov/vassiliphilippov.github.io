bring forward

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
To move something or someone to a position further in front or ahead.

Examples

But a moment later he brought his horse forward into the light, and Wilson had a good look at him.

Synset Examples

Can you move the car seat forward?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - to move something forward, especially in position or time
  • Antonym: move back - to move something to a position further behind

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ ˈfɔr.wɚd / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔː.wəd / (BrE)
to present something for consideration or discussion; to raise a topic, issue, or proposal for attention

Examples

He and other Soviet leaders responsible for the document were proud of having brought forward some new formulas, such as the early replacement of the dictatorship of the proletariat by an "All People's State ", and also of having laid down the lines for a much greater "democratization "of the whole hierarchy of Soviets, starting with the Supreme Soviet itself.

Synset Examples

The case was called up in court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to bring up a matter for discussion
  • Synonym: table - to formally present for discussion or consideration (mainly in British or formal usage)
