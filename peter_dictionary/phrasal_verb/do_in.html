do in

1. VERB

/ du ɪn / (AmE), / duː ɪn / (BrE)
to kill someone; to cause the death of someone, often deliberately or violently

Examples

It was paired with a Darius Milhaud opera, "The Poor Sailor ", set to a libretto by Jean Cocteau, a kind of Grand Guignol by the sea, a sailor returns, unrecognized, and gets done in by his wife.

Synset Examples

The mafia liquidated the informer
the double agent was neutralized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kill - to cause the death of a person or animal
  • Synonym: murder - to kill someone deliberately and unlawfully
  • Antonym: save - to keep someone alive or prevent death
