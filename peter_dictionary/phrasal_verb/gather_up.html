gather up

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə ʌp / (BrE)
to collect or pick up several things and lift or bring them together, often in order to move them elsewhere

Examples

He sat for a while with his hands on his knees, watching the bend of her back as she gathered up her things - a comb, a bottle of aspirin - to take upstairs and pack.
Food, medical supplies, utensils, and equipment, if not already stored in the shelter, must be quickly gathered up and carried into it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to bring things together from different places
  • Synonym: pick up - to lift or take something in one's hand

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæðɚ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə ʌp / (BrE)
to collect or bring together people or things, often from different places or sources

Examples

The cops would gather up Connor and the foursome on the third floor and bring us those of them who would voluntarily submit to fingerprinting.

Synset Examples

You can get the results on Monday
She picked up the children at the day care center
They pick up our trash twice a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to bring items or people together; to gather
  • Synonym: accumulate - to gather or collect over time
