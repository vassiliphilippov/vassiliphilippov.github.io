perk up

1. VERB

/ pɜrk ʌp / (AmE), / pɜːk ʌp / (BrE)
to become or make more lively, cheerful, or energetic again; to gain or regain energy or alertness

Examples

Miss Jen was funny that way, funny that she did n't seem to take to his ideas and perk up.

Synset Examples

I picked up after a nap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liven up - to make or become more lively or cheerful
  • Synonym: cheer up - to become or make happier or more cheerful
  • Antonym: flag - to become tired or less energetic

Etymology

Possibly from dialect 'perk' (to look or to be lively), of uncertain origin.
