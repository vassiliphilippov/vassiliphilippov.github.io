get off the ground

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɑf ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
To begin or start successfully; to set something in motion, especially a project, idea, or activity.

Examples

The Continental League never got off the ground, but after two years it forced the existing majors to expand.
Since 1949, the only National League club that got off to a hot start and made a runaway of the race was the '55 Dodger team.

Synset Examples

the project took a long time to get off the ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - to begin doing something
  • Synonym: launch - to start or set in motion (an activity, enterprise, or project)
  • Antonym: finish - to bring to an end or completion
