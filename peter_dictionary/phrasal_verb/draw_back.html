draw back

1. VERB

/ drɔ bǽk / (AmE), / drɔː bæk / (BrE)
to move away from something or someone, or to pull something backward, usually suddenly or in response to emotion such as fear or surprise

Examples

He drew back, embarrassed and pleased.
He drew back his arm to slash the gunbarrel across Jess's face, but did n't finish the motion.
He backed Jess into a corner, grabbed a handful of the man's shirtfront, and drew back his right fist.
I drew back, drawing back my foot for a kick.
I drew back, drawing back my foot for a kick.

Synset Examples

The enemy withdrew
The limo pulled away from the curb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull back - to move back or withdraw, especially physically
