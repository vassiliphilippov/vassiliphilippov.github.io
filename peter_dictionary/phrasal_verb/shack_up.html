shack up

1. VERB

/ ʃæk ʌp / (AmE), / ʃæk ʌp / (BrE)
(informal) To live together and share a home as a couple without being married.

Examples

And there I was shacked up with Eileen in that filthy fourth floor attic on Hudson Street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohabit - to live together as a couple, especially when unmarried

Etymology

From 'shack' (a small, roughly built house) + 'up', originally American slang (early 20th century).
