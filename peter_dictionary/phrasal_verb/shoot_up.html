shoot up

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃuːt ʌp / (BrE)
to increase suddenly and rapidly in amount, value, or level

Examples

Out of the corner of his eye, he watched his wingman move out a bit and shoot up with him.
I shoot up through the roof, into the sky, past the clouds, through the stratosphere, out beyond the moon, out among the planets, until I am over a hundred and fifty million miles long.

Synset Examples

Prices shot up overnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soar - to rise very quickly to a high level
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall suddenly and quickly from a high level
  • Synonym: rise - to move upwards or increase
