dispense with

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɛns wɪθ / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛns wɪð / (BrE)
to manage without something; to get rid of or no longer use something that is not necessary

Examples

Some persons are so sensitive to this truth as to propose that we do away with institutions altogether; in the present context this amounts to the advice that while being religious may have a certain justification, we ought to dispense with churches.

Synset Examples

he asked if they could spare one of their horses to speed his journey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do without - to manage or survive without something
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or continue having something

Etymology

From Latin 'dispensare' (to distribute) + English 'with'.
