wear thin

1. VERB

/ wɛr θɪn / (AmE), / wɛə θɪn / (BrE)
gradually become weakened, diminished, or less effective through continued use, stress, or repetition, either physically or figuratively

Examples

Eugenia hated being cold worse than anything, and she was beginning to find the joys of poverty wearing thin.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wear out - to deteriorate completely through use
  • Synonym: fray - to become or make ragged or worn along the edges
