go steady

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈstɛdi / (BrE)
(of two people) to have a regular romantic relationship with each other over a period of time, often implying exclusivity.

Examples

In her sophomore year she had started going steady with Bobby Joe, who was a football player, Future Homemakers sweetheart, and president of Future Farmers.

Synset Examples

Did you know that she is seeing an older man?
He is dating his former wife again!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: date - to regularly spend time with someone you have a romantic interest in
