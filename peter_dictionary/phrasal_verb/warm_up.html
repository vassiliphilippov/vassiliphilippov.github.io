warm up

1. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to become more friendly, enthusiastic, or interested, especially after being reserved or distant at first

Examples

But he warmed up after a while.
The Brevard visitors had very little to say at the beginning of the tour but warmed up later.

Synset Examples

She warmed up after we had lunch together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open up - to become more open, communicative, or willing to talk about feelings
  • Synonym: thaw - (informal) to become more relaxed or friendly after being cold or reserved

2. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to make one's body more limber or supple by performing gentle exercises or stretches, typically to prepare for physical activity

Examples

Nervousness at the start must have caused the blemishes of her first scene, or she may warm up slowly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cool down - to become less physically active after exercise to help the body recover

3. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
(of an engine or machine) to run until it reaches normal working temperature.

Examples

Finding the key under his shoe, he started the engine, and while it warmed up he turned to her standing there in the dripping fog, and said, "Defrost the refrigerator ".
He found the key to the Jeep, got it started and warmed it up for five minutes.

Synset Examples

We warmed up the car for a few minutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat up - to make hotter or become hot

4. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to do or cause someone to do gentle exercises to prepare the body for more strenuous physical activity

Examples

He was eyeing the Anniston catcher warming up his pitcher before the inning began.

Synset Examples

The coach warmed up the players before the game

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cool down - to do gentle exercises after strenuous activity to help the body recover

5. VERB

/ wɔrm ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
to make or become warm or warmer, especially by heating or through movement; to prepare by increasing temperature or activity

Examples

Out at the airport each morning, Fogg's skilled mechanic Caleb Marston would have the Waco warmed up and running in the drafty hangar.

Synset Examples

The soup warmed slowly on the stove

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat up - to make hot or hotter
  • Antonym: cool down - to make or become cooler
