weed out

1. VERB

/ wid aʊt / (AmE), / wiːd aʊt / (BrE)
To remove unwanted or less desirable people or things from a group or collection.

Examples

Have you audited your program recently to weed out those phases that draw least participation?
Drunken truck drivers in the city departments should be weeded out.

Synset Examples

The company weeded out the incompetent people
The new law weeds out the old inequities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something

Etymology

Figurative use of 'weed' (remove unwanted plants).
