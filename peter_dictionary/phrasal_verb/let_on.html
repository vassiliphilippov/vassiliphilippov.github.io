let on

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɑn / (AmE), / lɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to reveal or disclose information that was intended to be kept secret; to admit or hint at something previously concealed.

Examples

And when they stood by the fountain in the piazza looking at Santa Maria he had to keep a straight face, not letting on he had been there with Alberto.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveal - to make something known that was previously secret or unknown
  • Synonym: divulge - to make private or sensitive information known to others
  • Antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • Antonym: keep quiet - to not reveal information
