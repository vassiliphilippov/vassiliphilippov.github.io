loosen up

1. VERB

/ ˈluːsən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ʌp / (BrE)
to make or become less blocked, tight, or restricted; to relieve congestion or stiffness

Examples

He started by making free sketches to loosen up his thinking so that images would appear on paper.

Synset Examples

The medicine unstuffed my nose in minutes!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction or block from

2. VERB

/ ˈluːsən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ʌp / (BrE)
To become less tense or anxious; to relax, especially after being nervous, stressed, or inactive.

Examples

Sonny, things are starting to loosen up.

Synset Examples

He relaxed in the hot tub
Let's all relax after a hard day's work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relax - to become less tense or anxious
  • Antonym: tense up - to become more tense or anxious

3. VERB

/ ˈluːsən ʌp / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ʌp / (BrE)
to become less tense, less formal, or less restrained, often adopting a more relaxed or friendly manner

Examples

So things are loosening up.

Synset Examples

our new colleague relaxed when he saw that we were a friendly group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • Antonym: tense up - to become or make oneself more tense
