let loose

1. VERB

/ lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to express or release something, especially a sound or exclamation, often suddenly or without restraint

Examples

Before you let loose a howl saying we announced its coming, not once but several times, indeed we did.

Synset Examples

She let out a big heavy sigh
He uttered strange sounds that nobody could understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utter - to say or make a sound
  • Synonym: let out - to express or emit (a sound, feeling, etc.)
