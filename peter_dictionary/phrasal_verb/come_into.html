come into

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈɪntu / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to acquire or obtain (something), especially by chance, inheritance, or as a result of an event

Examples

By the death of his father in 1888 he had come into possession of the family estate and had re-assumed its traditions.
Having whipped Mr. De Sapio in the primaries and thus come into control of Tammany Hall, they have changed the name to Chatham Hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherit - receive (money, property, or a title) as an heir at the death of the previous holder
  • Antonym: lose - to cease to have or no longer possess something
