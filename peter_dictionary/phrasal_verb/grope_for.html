grope for

1. VERB

/ ˈɡroʊp fər / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊp fə / (BrE)
to search for something with your hands, especially in a way that is uncoordinated or because you cannot see clearly.

Examples

For a moment she held her face to the empty doorway; then she snorted and groped for her fork.

Synset Examples

She groped for his keys in the dark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search for - to look for something

Etymology

From 'grope' (Middle English, from Old English 'grāpan' meaning 'to seize, touch') + 'for'.
