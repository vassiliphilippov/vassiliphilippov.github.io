mill about

1. VERB

/ mɪl əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / mɪl əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move around a place without any clear purpose or direction

Examples

The aimless milling about of what had been a well trained, well organized crew struck Alexander with horror.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loiter - stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: wander - move about without a fixed course or direction
  • Antonym: stand still - remain in one place without moving

Etymology

From 'mill' in the sense of move in a circle, and 'about'.
