look on

1. VERB

/ lʊk ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to watch something happen without participating, often as a bystander or observer

Examples

With Hudson looking on, his protege Greene picked a fight with the ship's surgeon, Edward Wilson.
She set the dipper on the edge of the deck, leaving it for him to stretch after it while she looked on scornfully.
When Mercer looked on helplessly, the half-man twisted over on his side, his pink dusty back turned to Mercer, and wept hoarsely and quietly to himself.

Synset Examples

They watched as the murderer was executed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - to observe attentively; to pay close attention to something
  • Antonym: take part - to participate or be involved in an activity

2. VERB

/ lʊk ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ɒn / (BrE)
to regard or consider in a particular way; to view someone or something with a particular feeling or attitude

Examples

He frequently cites intimate details that seem to come straight from the horse's mouth, from numerous insiders and from Mr. Kennedy himself; but never from Mr. Nixon, who looked on reporters with suspicion and distrust.

Synset Examples

she looked on this affair as a joke
He thinks of himself as a brilliant musician
He is reputed to be intelligent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regard - to think of someone or something in a particular way
  • Synonym: view - to consider or regard in a specified way
  • Degree Higher: esteem - to regard highly or with respect
  • Antonym: ignore - to fail to consider or deliberately disregard
