black out

1. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or obscure information, especially in printed or broadcast material, by censoring all or part of it

Examples

THE DENVER-AREA TV audience was privileged to see Mays' four home runs, thanks to a new arrangement made by Bob Howsam that the games are not to be blacked out when his Bears are playing at home.

Synset Examples

parts of the newspaper article were blacked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - to officially suppress or edit information
  • Antonym: reveal - to make previously hidden information known

2. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to make a place completely dark by turning off all lights or covering windows

Examples

Starting with the room completely blacked out, as it was when we came in, he unerringly fixed things so that the whole place was bathed in the maximum of light without at any point admitting even so much as a crack of glare.

Synset Examples

The dining room blackened out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darken - to make or become darker

3. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish or make a light or lights invisible, often intentionally for safety or concealment

Examples

After the first two were blacked out, the third light was abandoned by a terrified Italian crew, who left their light to shine for nine minutes like an unerring homing beacon until British MP's shot it out.

Synset Examples

Some life-forms were obliterated by the radiation, others survived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinguish - to cause (a light or fire) to cease to burn or shine

4. VERB

/ blæk aʊt / (AmE), / blæk aʊt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness suddenly, especially from shock, injury, or a lack of oxygen

Examples

Soon we were deep in a conversation that was interrupted many times by little things like Jennie's holding her breath and pretending to black out, Miranda's dumping the contents of the sugar bowl on the table, and various screeches, thuds, and giggles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - lose consciousness temporarily due to lack of blood flow to the brain
  • Synonym: pass out - become unconscious for a short time, often from shock or lack of oxygen
  • Antonym: come round - regain consciousness
