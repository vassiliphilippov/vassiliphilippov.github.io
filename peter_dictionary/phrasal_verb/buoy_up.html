buoy up

1. VERB

/ ˈbɔɪ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to make (someone) feel more cheerful or confident; to raise the spirits of

Examples

After Captain Docherty sent Arleigh Griffith for Hoag he was able to complete his detailed inspection of the third floor and to receive a report from his man covering the floors above before Griffith returned, buoyed up by a brief stop for another glass of champagne.

Synset Examples

after a glass of wine, he lightened up a bit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer up - to become or make happier

Etymology

From 'buoy', originally from Middle Dutch 'boeye', meaning a float, used metaphorically to mean 'to support or uplift'.
