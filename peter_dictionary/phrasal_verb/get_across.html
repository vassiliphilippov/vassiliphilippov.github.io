get across

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to successfully communicate an idea or message so that it is understood

Examples

The historical sign tells its story, but nothing gets interest across as well as some of the original historical items or places themselves which still have the character of the period covered.

Synset Examples

I couldn't get across the message
He put over the idea very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convey - to communicate or make known
  • Synonym: communicate - to transmit information or ideas

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt əˈkrɑs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to become understood or clear to someone; to be successfully communicated or comprehended

Examples

Moreover, even getting this across would be difficult.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn on - to become apparent or understood suddenly
