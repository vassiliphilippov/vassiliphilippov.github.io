extend to

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɛnd tu / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnd tʊ / (BrE)
to reach as far as a particular point, often used both literally and figuratively

Examples

Invitations have been extended to some Austin dignitaries including Gov. and Mrs. Price Daniel.
Her invitation from Premier Joseph Smallwood is reported to be the only one extended to a woman.
From early family portraits, painted before he entered the schools of the Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts, the chronology extends to a group of paintings executed in his last year (1951) and still part of his estate.
His authority extended to the far edge of the counter, no further.

Synset Examples

The sunlight reached the wall
Can he reach?
The chair must not touch the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reach - to stretch out as far as; to extend to a point

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɛnd tu / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnd tʊ / (BrE)
to stretch or continue as far as a particular place or point; to reach.

Examples

And the other associates that came as far as from California and Kansas City and all the other territories of the country.

Synset Examples

The sunlight reached the wall
Can he reach?
The chair must not touch the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reach - to arrive at or extend as far as a point
