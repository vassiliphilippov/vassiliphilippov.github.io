vote down

1. VERB

/ voʊt daʊn / (AmE), / vəʊt daʊn / (BrE)
to reject or defeat something, such as a proposal or motion, by voting against it

Examples

My plea was unanimously voted down and "thrown out ".

Synset Examples

kill a motion
he shot down the student's proposal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approve - to accept or agree to something by formal vote or decision
