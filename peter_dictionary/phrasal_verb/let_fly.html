let fly

1. VERB

/ lɛt flaɪ / (AmE), / lɛt flaɪ / (BrE)
to discharge or hurl something suddenly, especially as a weapon; to fire or shoot quickly and spontaneously

Examples

He would lay in wait in the garage, and when the terrier came scratching around, he 'd let fly with a cherry bomb.

Synset Examples

The soldiers let drive their bullets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to discharge a firearm or other weapon
