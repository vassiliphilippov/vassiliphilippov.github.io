snuff out

1. VERB

/ snʌf aʊt / (AmE), / snʌf aʊt / (BrE)
to extinguish a flame, light, or to put an end to something, especially a life

Examples

Curt snuffed out the match.

Synset Examples

The Nazis snuffed out the life of many Jewish children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning

Etymology

From 'snuff' meaning the burnt part of a candle wick, and 'out' meaning to finish or extinguish.
