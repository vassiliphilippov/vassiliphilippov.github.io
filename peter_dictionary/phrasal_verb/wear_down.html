wear down

1. VERB

/ wɛr daʊn / (AmE), / wɛə daʊn / (BrE)
to gradually make someone feel tired, weak, or less determined, especially through constant stress, effort, or pressure

Examples

Throughout "The Making of a President "Mr. White shows wonderfully well how the pressures pile up on candidates, how decisions have constantly to be made, how fatigue and illness and nervous strain wear candidates down, how subordinates play key roles.

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tire out - to make someone very tired
  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired

2. VERB

/ wɛr daʊn / (AmE), / wɛə daʊn / (BrE)
To make someone gradually become exhausted, less energetic, or less determined by continuous use, pressure, or strain.

Examples

And I say, starting with this anniversary festival... ... we run this city **into the ground**.

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wear out - to exhaust someone completely, or to become unusable through use
  • Antonym: refresh - to give new energy or vigor to someone
