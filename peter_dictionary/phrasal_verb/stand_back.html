stand back

1. VERB

/ stænd bæk / (AmE), / stænd bæk / (BrE)
to move a short distance away from something or someone, often to allow a better view or for safety

Examples

Then he stood back to look at Mr. Jack, who was pulling on his pigskin gloves.

Synset Examples

He stood back to look at her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step back - to move a pace or step backwards
  • Synonym: stand aside - to move to one side to make way

2. VERB

/ stænd ˈbæk / (AmE), / stænd ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move away from something or someone, typically to keep a safe distance, especially in response to a warning or out of caution

Examples

"Stand back, Miss Jen, she's oneasy of your scarf ".

Synset Examples

Keep your hands off my wife!
Keep your distance from this man--he is dangerous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step back - to move one or more steps away from something or someone
  • Antonym: approach - to come closer to someone or something
