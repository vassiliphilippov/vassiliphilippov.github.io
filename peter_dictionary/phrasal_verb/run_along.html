run along

1. VERB

/ rʌn əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / rʌn əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
(of a road, path, boundary, etc.) to extend in a line parallel to or beside something else.

Examples

The first sentence, with its platitudinous irony, announces an emblematic intent: "The way to the churchyard ran along beside the highroad, ran beside it all the way to the end; that is to say, to the churchyard ".

Synset Examples

trees line the riverbank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - to form a line along the edge of something (verb)
