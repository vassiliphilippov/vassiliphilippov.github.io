walk out

1. VERB

/ wɔk aʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
(of workers) to stop working and leave your place of work as a form of protest, especially to gain better pay or conditions.

Examples

Eileen got to dancing, just a little tiny dancing step to a hummed tune that you could hardly notice, and trying to pick up strange men, but each time I was ready to say to hell with it and walk out she 'd pull herself together and talk so understandingly in that sweet husky voice about the good times and the happiness we 'd had together and there I was back on the hook.
State and federal approval of right to walk out at any time when so voted by 51 per cent of the prisoners.

Synset Examples

The auto workers are striking for higher wages
The employees walked out when their demand for better benefits was not met

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - stop work as a form of protest

2. VERB

/ wɔk aʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
To leave a place suddenly and unexpectedly, especially as an act of protest or anger.

Examples

I waited until the parking attendant was busy with a customer, then slipped around the back of the car with license number JYM 114, attached the electronic bug to the rear bumper and walked out.

Synset Examples

The customer that was not served walked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm out - to leave a place angrily and suddenly
  • Antonym: walk in - to enter a place, especially casually or unexpectedly

3. VERB

/ wɑk aʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation suddenly, especially to show disapproval or as a protest

Examples

They'll stare at your face, so walk out fast, but don't run.

Synset Examples

She walked out on her husband and children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Synonym: storm out - to leave a place angrily and suddenly
