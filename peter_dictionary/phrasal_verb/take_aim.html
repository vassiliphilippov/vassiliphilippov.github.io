take aim

1. VERB

/ teɪk eɪm / (AmE), / teɪk eɪm / (BrE)
to prepare to point or direct a weapon or device at a target

Examples

As a finale is appended a close-up of one of the band taking aim and firing his revolver straight at the audience.

Synset Examples

Please don't aim at your little brother!
He trained his gun on the burglar
Don't train your camera on the women
Take a swipe at one's opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aim - to point or direct a weapon or object at a target
