fit out

1. VERB

/ fɪt aʊt / (AmE), / fɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to provide the necessary equipment, supplies, or furnishings for a particular purpose or activity, especially to prepare a vehicle, building, or person for a specific task or journey

Examples

In 1609, the Dutch East India Company hired Hudson, gave him two learned geographers, fitted him out with a ship called the Half Moon, and supplied him with Dutch sailors.

Synset Examples

The expedition was equipped with proper clothing, food, and other necessities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equip - to supply with the necessary items for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: outfit - to supply with clothing or equipment, especially for a specific purpose
