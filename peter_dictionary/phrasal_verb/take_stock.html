take stock

1. VERB

/ teɪk stɑk / (AmE), / teɪk stɒk / (BrE)
to carefully examine or consider a situation or one's position, especially in order to make a decision

Examples

We waited till he had finished laughing, and that gave us a few moments for taking stock of him.

Synset Examples

he scrutinized his likeness in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflect - to think carefully or deeply about something
  • Synonym: analyze - to examine in detail in order to understand or explain

Etymology

From the practice of counting and evaluating the stock (goods) in a shop, figuratively extended to evaluating one's situation.
