put up with

1. VERB

/ pʊt ʌp wɪð / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp wɪð / (BrE)
to accept or tolerate someone or something unpleasant without complaining

Examples

What I have to put up with!
This is awkward for everybody, is n't it, we all got to put up with inconvenience sometimes.

Synset Examples

I cannot bear his constant criticism
The new secretary had to endure a lot of unprofessional remarks
he learned to tolerate the heat
She stuck out two years in a miserable marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerate - to allow something unpleasant to exist or happen
  • Synonym: endure - to suffer patiently; to bear
  • Synonym: stand - to accept or tolerate something; often used in negative
  • Synonym: bear - to accept or tolerate something unpleasant
