pay off

1. VERB

/ peɪ ɔf / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to pay the whole amount of money owed on a debt, loan, or promissory note so that nothing more is owed

Examples

The department apparently intends to make the Rural Roads Authority a revolving fund under which new bonds would be issued every time a portion of the old ones are paid off by tax authorities.
As a matter of fact, Fogg and his plane did n't get beyond Pennsylvania in the race - an engine oil leak forced him down - but the flying service and school he started subsequently were first steps in paying off his wry-faced backers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repay - to pay back money that you have borrowed
  • Synonym: settle - to pay money owed (a bill or debt) in full

2. VERB

/ peɪ ɔf / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf / (BrE)
(of an action or effort) to result in success, benefit, or profit after a period of time.

Examples

A non contributory plan usually won n't pay off for the worker until he retires.
The battle of the Naktong River is just one example of how the battle cry and the spirit of The Fighting Seventh have paid off.
This sum spent for foreign economic aid, the peace corps, food for peace, or any other program to solve the problems of the underdeveloped countries would be an investment that would pay off in world peace, increased world trade, and prosperity for every country on the globe.
A little extra work here will pay off with a smooth, snug-fitting machine when you are finished.

Synset Examples

His efforts finally paid off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear fruit - to produce good or successful results
  • Antonym: backfire - to have the opposite result to what was intended

3. VERB

/ ˈpeɪ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ˌɒf / (BrE)
to settle a debt by making the final payment; to eliminate a debt by paying the total amount owed

Examples

He died before he could completely pay off his debts.
The suit against the union was successful and many workers lost their homes to pay off the judgment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidate - to settle a debt by paying the amount owed

4. VERB

/ peɪ ˈɔf / (AmE), / peɪ ˈɒf / (BrE)
to pay someone the money that is owed to them, especially as a final payment for work done or to settle a debt; to discharge an obligation by payment

Examples

We were paid off Tuesday morning and given tickets back to New York.

Synset Examples

Does she pay you for the work you are doing?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle up - to pay the amount you owe and finish an account

5. VERB

/ peɪ ɔf / (AmE), / peɪ ɒf / (BrE)
To give someone money, especially illegally, to persuade them to do something for you; to bribe.

Examples

He's never been... - I said take him in! - What's The Turk paying you? - Take a hold of him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - to persuade someone to act in one's favor by offering money or gifts
