rig up

1. VERB

/ rɪɡ ʌp / (AmE), / rɪɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to assemble or construct something quickly, often as a temporary or makeshift solution

Examples

Andy told him, "Bake, I wish you 'd talk to Skolman, see if some kind of p. a. system can be rigged up outside.

Synset Examples

Can he rig up a P.A. system?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation or as a makeshift
  • Synonym: assemble - to put together or construct by joining parts

Etymology

From 'rig' (to fit out), origin uncertain but likely nautical, combined with the adverb 'up'.
