get by

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to manage to survive or to cope, especially with only just enough of what is necessary

Examples

Nothing like the old days in the army, but I get by.
Actually, all a man in uniform has to do is to get by.
He may not rise to the heights, but he can get by, and eventually be retired.

Synset Examples

We got by on just a gallon of gas
They made do on half a loaf of bread every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manage - to succeed in surviving or in achieving something despite difficult circumstances
  • Synonym: make do - to manage with the limited or insufficient resources available

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
to move past or in front of someone or something, especially by passing them physically

Examples

There was, of course, no way for the other planes to get by them.

Synset Examples

Bride's Biscuit got by the other dogs to win the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtake - to move ahead of someone or something that is in front
  • Synonym: pass - move beyond or in front of

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt baɪ / (AmE), / ɡɛt baɪ / (BrE)
To avoid or escape the consequences of something, often by using cleverness or luck; to get away with a forbidden or questionable action.

Examples

"How did you get by the desk "? he asked curiously.

Synset Examples

She gets away with murder!
I couldn't get out from under these responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get away with - to succeed in avoiding punishment or negative consequences for something
