gang up

1. VERB

/ ɡæŋ ʌp / (AmE), / ɡæŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to join together as a group, usually to oppose, criticize, or attack someone or something

Examples

Jubal could n't reconstruct the crime from the way the girls behaved because patterns kept shifting - ABC vs D, then BCD vs A & & & or AB vs CD, or AD vs CB, through all ways that four women can gang up on each other.
"Why the hell did n't you come out when you saw them gang up on me "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborate - work jointly on an activity or project
  • Synonym: ally - to unite or join with others for a common purpose

Etymology

From 'gang' (meaning group) + 'up'; phrasal verb formation common in informal English.
