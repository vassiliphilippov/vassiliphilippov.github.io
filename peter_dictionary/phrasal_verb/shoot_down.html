shoot down

1. VERB

/ ʃuːt daʊn / (AmE), / ʃuːt daʊn / (BrE)
to move quickly and suddenly in a downward direction, or along a path, often with force or urgency

Examples

The doctor shot down to the lavatory and turned the doorknob, but to no effect: the lavatory was occupied.

Synset Examples

The car tore down the street
He came charging into my office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear down - move quickly and violently down or along

2. VERB

/ ʃut daʊn / (AmE), / ʃuːt daʊn / (BrE)
to fire at and cause an aircraft or similar object to fall to the ground

Examples

He has been in prison since May, 1958, when his aircraft was shot down over Moluccas.

Synset Examples

the enemy landed several of our aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring down - to cause something to come down, especially by force
