set back

1. VERB

/ sɛt bæk / (AmE), / sɛt bæk / (BrE)
To delay or postpone something to a later time.

Examples

Harvey Haddix, set back by the flu this season, will start against his former Cardinal mates, who might be playing without captain Kenny Boyer in tonight's game at Busch Stadium.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Synonym: defer - to put off to a future time
  • Antonym: hasten - to cause to happen sooner; to speed up

2. VERB

/ sɛt bæk / (AmE), / sɛt bæk / (BrE)
to delay the progress of someone or something; to cause a setback or hindrance

Examples

Hansen will be getting a late spring training start, which might very well set him back.

Synset Examples

His late start set him back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress
