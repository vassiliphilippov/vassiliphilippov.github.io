taper off

1. VERB

/ ˈteɪpɚ ɑf / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə ɒf / (BrE)
to decrease gradually until it stops or becomes less strong, intense, or frequent

Examples

We 'll work out about an hour on Saturday, then we 'll work Monday and Tuesday of next week, then taper off ".

Synset Examples

The music just petered out--there was no proper ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwindle - to become steadily less
  • Synonym: peter out - to diminish gradually and stop

Etymology

From 'taper', meaning to become gradually narrower, from Old French 'taper' (to taper), plus 'off'.
