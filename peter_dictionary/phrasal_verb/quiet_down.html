quiet down

1. VERB

/ ˈkwaɪət daʊn / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət daʊn / (BrE)
to become quiet or less noisy; to make less noise or commotion

Examples

Until the Bucs' bats quieted down a bit in Cincinnati over the weekend, the champions had eight men hitting over .300.

Synset Examples

The audience fell silent when the speaker entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm down - to become less agitated or excited
  • Synonym: settle down - to become quiet, calm, or orderly
  • Antonym: perk up - to become more lively or active
