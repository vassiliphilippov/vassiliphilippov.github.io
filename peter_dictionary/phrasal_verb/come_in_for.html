come in for

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɪn fɔr / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn fə / (BrE)
to receive or be subjected to something, especially criticism, blame, or scrutiny

Examples

No group of officers came in for more spirited denunciation than the doctors.
Charitable deductions come in for closer scrutiny by the I. R. S..

Synset Examples

The governor came in for a lot of criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attract - to draw (attention, criticism, etc.) to oneself
  • Antonym: escape - to avoid something, such as criticism or blame
