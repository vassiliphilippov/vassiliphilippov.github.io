drop out

1. VERB

/ drɑp aʊt / (AmE), / drɒp aʊt / (BrE)
To leave or withdraw from an activity, school, or society before completion, especially permanently or before achieving a qualification.

Examples

If the student drops out of high school, the break with the school is even more complete.
Many of them appear to drop out, for one reason or another.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quit - To stop participating in or doing something, especially permanently
  • Synonym: give up - To cease making an effort; stop trying
  • Antonym: persist - To continue firmly despite difficulty or opposition
  • Antonym: stay in - To remain a member of a course or organization and not leave before completion
