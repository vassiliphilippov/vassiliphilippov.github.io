mete out

1. VERB

/ mit aʊt / (AmE), / miːt aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something, especially punishment, justice, or criticism, in measured portions

Examples

If, however, it would help to intensify your anguish, I can delimit the powers of a few of the divinities you 've affronted and describe the punishment they meted out in one analogous instance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administer - to give or apply (a remedy, punishment, etc.)
  • Synonym: deal out - to distribute or give out (something)

Etymology

From Old English *metan* (to measure) + out.
