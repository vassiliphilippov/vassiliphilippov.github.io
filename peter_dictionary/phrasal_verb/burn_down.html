burn down

1. VERB

/ bɝn daʊn / (AmE), / bɜːn daʊn / (BrE)
to destroy or be destroyed completely by fire, especially a building

Examples

Not realizing the seriousness of the wound, the besiegers warned that if he did not surrender the house would be burned down around him.
Hengesbach has been living in Grand Ledge since his house and barn were burned down after his release in 1958.

Synset Examples

They burned the house and his diaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn up - to be destroyed or consumed by fire, often of smaller objects

2. VERB

/ bɝn daʊn / (AmE), / bɜːn daʊn / (BrE)
To be completely destroyed by fire, especially used of buildings or structures.

Examples

"You could burn down this whole mountainside with a fire that size.
Let it burn down.
The road maps of the region have changed since 1905; inns have burned down, moved elsewhere, or taken other names.

Synset Examples

The hut burned down
The mountain of paper went up in flames

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn up - to be entirely consumed by fire (often for smaller objects)
