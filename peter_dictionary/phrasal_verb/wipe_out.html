wipe out

1. VERB

/ waɪp aʊt / (AmE), / waɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to destroy something completely; to eliminate or eradicate.

Examples

Kerr, who set the world record earlier this month in New York with a clocking of 1.09.3, wiped out Mills's early pace and beat the young Big 10 quarter-mile king by 5 yards.
For example, it was a battalion of the 7 th Cavalry under Colonel George Armstrong Custer that had been wiped out at the Battle of The Little Big Horn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Synonym: eradicate - to completely remove or get rid of something; to wipe out
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original condition; to prevent from being destroyed

Etymology

From the verb 'wipe' (to clean or remove by rubbing) and the adverb 'out,' forming a phrasal verb with intensified sense since early 20th century.
