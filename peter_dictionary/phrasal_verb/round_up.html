round up

1. VERB

/ raʊnd ʌp / (AmE), / raʊnd ʌp / (BrE)
to collect or gather people, animals, or things together, often by searching for them

Examples

"When Konishi gets back with the jeep, I want you to round up two or three Japanese boys.
Armed with the Nixon opinion, the Senate liberals rounded up their slim majority and prepared to choke off debate on the filibuster battle this week.
We 'll still have the rifle, and I might be able to round up some more.
Then Little Billy began shouting orders to round up the ponies and fill the water buckets and for the cooks to hurry up with the meal.

Synset Examples

round up some loyal followers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring together or collect people or things
  • Antonym: disperse - to cause to break up and go in different directions

2. VERB

/ raʊnd ʌp / (AmE), / raʊnd ʌp / (BrE)
to gather together people or animals into one place, especially by searching for and collecting them

Examples

Our police have rounded up all of the Joker's men.

Synset Examples

round up some loyal followers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring together into one group
  • Antonym: disperse - to distribute or spread people or animals over a wide area
