prop up

1. VERB

/ prɑp ʌp / (AmE), / prɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to support something or someone by placing it against something solid, or to help a person, organization, or system stay in a stable condition, often artificially.

Examples

"Here, help me prop him up.
It is a desperate effort to prop up a sagging candidate who has proven he cannot answer any questions about New Jersey's problems.

Synset Examples

shore and buttress an old building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to hold up or bear the weight of something; to provide assistance

2. VERB

/ prɑp ʌp / (AmE), / prɒp ʌp / (BrE)
to support or prevent something from falling by placing something solid under or against it

Examples

He left a note propped on the desk asking her to call him sometime today, and drove home.

Synset Examples

shore and buttress an old building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to bear the weight of or hold up
  • Synonym: shore up - to support, especially with props or beams
