wear on

1. VERB

/ wɛr ɑn / (AmE), / weə ɒn / (BrE)
(of time) to pass slowly, often with a sense of tedium or reluctance.

Examples

As the morning wore on and a blazing West Texas sun wiped the shadows off the Franklin Mountains, police got close enough to the plane to pry into the baggage compartment.
The day wore on.

Synset Examples

The day wore on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag on - (of time) to pass very slowly and tediously
