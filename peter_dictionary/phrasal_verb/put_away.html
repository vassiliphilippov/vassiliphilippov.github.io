put away

1. VERB

/ pʊt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to place something in a secure or designated place where it is not easily accessible, or to confine someone so they cannot escape

Examples

It ignores the sordid financial aspects (quite conveniently, too, for his audience, who could indulge in moral indignation without visible, or even conscious, discomfort, their money from the transaction having been put away long ago in a good antiseptic brokerage).
Most children love the animated puppet faces and their flexible bodies, and they prefer to see them as though the puppets were in action, rather than put away in boxes.

Synset Examples

The parents locked her daughter up for the weekend
She locked her jewels in the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock up - to confine someone or something in a secure place
  • Antonym: take out - to remove something from storage

2. VERB

/ pʊt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to discard or get rid of something; to throw or cast aside

Examples

I now ever look for Christ acording to His promises and those of the Old Testament as well, to appear again in glory to put away all sin and to reign in righteousness over the whole earth.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something that is no longer wanted or useful
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something; not discard

3. VERB

/ pʊt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / pʊt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to place something in its proper place, out of sight, or to store it, often temporarily

Examples

Put that **away**.

Synset Examples

it's time for you to put away childish things
