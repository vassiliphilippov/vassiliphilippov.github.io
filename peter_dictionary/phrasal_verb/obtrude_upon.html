obtrude upon

1. VERB

/ əbˈtruːd əˈpɑn / (AmE), / əbˈtruːd əˈpɒn / (BrE)
To impose oneself or one's presence unwelcomely or intrusively on someone or something.

Examples

The engineering as such never obtrudes upon your consciousness.

Synset Examples

This new colleague invades my territory
The neighbors intrude on your privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrude on - to put oneself deliberately into a situation where one is unwelcome

Etymology

From Latin 'obtrudere' meaning to thrust upon.
