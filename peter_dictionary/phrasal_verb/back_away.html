back away

1. VERB

/ bæk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bæk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to withdraw from a commitment, activity, or previously agreed position; to retreat from involvement or responsibility.

Examples

The mare had backed away.

Synset Examples

We'll have to crawfish out from meeting with him
He backed out of his earlier promise
The aggressive investment company pulled in its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdraw - to remove oneself from participation or engagement
  • Synonym: retreat - to move away from a position or commitment
  • Antonym: commit - to pledge or be involved in an activity
