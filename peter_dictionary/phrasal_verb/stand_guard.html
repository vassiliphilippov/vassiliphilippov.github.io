stand guard

1. VERB

/ stænd ɡɑrd / (AmE), / stænd ɡɑːd / (BrE)
to remain in a place in order to protect someone or something from danger or harm

Examples

It could never happen as long as God was alert and the Drew steeple stood guard with its peaked lance.
Deputy Marshal Luke Harper still stood guard on the veranda, a forlorn, scarecrowish figure in the murky dark.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand sentinel - to keep watch or guard
  • Synonym: keep guard - to remain on guard to protect
