pop in

1. VERB

/ pɑp ɪn / (AmE), / pɒp ɪn / (BrE)
To visit or enter a place quickly or for a short period of time, often without prior arrangement.

Examples

Thornburg popped in to advise, "Andy, Skolman's sending up smoke signals.
For 25 c load up the cooler with ice and keep cool pop in the car.

Synset Examples

He popped in for two minutes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop by - visit someone informally and briefly
  • Synonym: stop in - make a short visit to a place
  • Antonym: stay - remain in a place for a longer period
