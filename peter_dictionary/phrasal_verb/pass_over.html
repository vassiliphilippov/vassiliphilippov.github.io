pass over

1. VERB

/ pæs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to ignore, skip, or not give attention to something or someone; to move past without dealing with or mentioning.

Examples

We shall not be able entirely to pass over these connections to the East as we consider Ripe Geometric pottery, the epic and the myth, and the religious evolution of early Greece; the important point, however, is that these magnificent achievements, unlike those of later decades, were only incidentally influenced by Oriental models.
Let me pass over the trip to Sante Fe with something of the same speed which made Mrs. Roebuck "wonduh if the wahtahm speed limit " (35 m. p. h.) "is still in ee-faket ".

Synset Examples

He skipped a row in the text and so the sentence was incomprehensible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skip - to move ahead without addressing something
  • Synonym: overlook - to fail to notice or ignore
  • Antonym: address - to deal with or discuss

2. VERB

/ pæs ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to travel across, over, or through something; to move across a surface or area

Examples

Although a look of alarm passed over his face, he did not arrest his movements but disappeared into the shower room just as the chambermaid emerged from number nine.

Synset Examples

The caravan covered almost 100 miles each day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traverse - to travel across or through

3. VERB

/ pæs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to move or travel across, above, or through something, especially without stopping

Examples

The Old Man near the corner let the shadow pass over him, sensing something portentous in it.

Synset Examples

The tourists moved through the town and bought up all the souvenirs;
Some travelers pass through the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to go across from one side to the other
  • Synonym: traverse - to travel across or through

4. VERB

/ pæs ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an aircraft or object) to fly above something without landing, or to move in the air above something.

Examples

A Yank writing from Vicksburg, May 28, 1863, stated "Not less than 50 balls have passed over me since I commenced writing.

Synset Examples

The plane passed over Damascus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly over - to travel through the air above something
