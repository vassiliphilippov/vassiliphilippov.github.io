come off

1. VERB

/ kʌm ɑːf / (AmE), / kʌm ɒf / (BrE)
to become detached or separated from something to which it was attached

Examples

Rabb, the former Louisiana State field general, came off the bench for his debut with the Bills Sunday and directed his new team to a 22 - 12 upset victory over the Houston Oilers, defending league champions.
Her hat had come off and fallen behind her shoulders, held by the string, and he could see her face more clearly than he had at any time before.

Synset Examples

His retina detached and he had to be rushed into surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall off - to drop from a place where it was attached

2. VERB

/ kʌm ɔf / (AmE), / kʌm ɒf / (BrE)
to happen or take place, especially in a particular way or as planned

Examples

He had been pretty scared himself, wondering what the hell was coming off.

Synset Examples

how did your talk go over?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happen - to occur or take place
  • Synonym: occur - to happen or come to pass
  • Synonym: take place - to happen or occur
