root out

1. VERB

/ rut aʊt / (AmE), / ruːt aʊt / (BrE)
to remove completely, especially by pulling up by the roots; to get rid of something unwanted thoroughly

Examples

He wanted desperately to see Kayabashi defeated, the Communists in the village rooted out, the mission standing triumphant, for in the triumph of the Lord he himself would be triumphant, too.

Synset Examples

uproot the vine that has spread all over the garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproot - to pull out of the ground by the roots
  • Synonym: eradicate - to destroy or get rid of completely

2. VERB

/ rut aʊt / (AmE), / ruːt aʊt / (BrE)
to find and remove completely; to eliminate or eradicate something harmful, undesirable, or hidden

Examples

As mayor, I promise to root out the source of this corruption... ...Boss Carl Grissom.

Synset Examples

the vestiges of political democracy were soon uprooted
root out corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eradicate - to destroy completely; put an end to
  • Synonym: stamp out - to eliminate or destroy completely, often used for bad practices or diseases
