cut through

1. VERB

/ kʌt θru / (AmE), / kʌt θruː / (BrE)
to pass through or travel across (an area, obstruction, etc.), often making a more direct route

Examples

long, domed, chalk-white rooms with daises of turquoise tile, their end walls cut through to the orchards and the sky by open arches.

Synset Examples

The caravan covered almost 100 miles each day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to move, pass, or extend across something
  • Synonym: traverse - to travel or move across, over, or through an area
