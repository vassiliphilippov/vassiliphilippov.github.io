team up

1. VERB

/ tim ʌp / (AmE), / tiːm ʌp / (BrE)
to join with another person or group in order to do something together

Examples

She teamed up with another beauty, whose name has been lost to history, and commenced with some fiddling that would have made Nero envious.
There were no depressingly serious cases: the ward doctor sometimes teamed up with the chaplain to serve as a marriage counselor - sometimes the Navy sent people back to the States to preserve a marriage - but mental health as a rule was very high.

Synset Examples

We teamed up for this new project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborate - to work together with others, especially on a project
  • Synonym: cooperate - to act jointly; work toward the same end
  • Antonym: compete - to strive against others to win or achieve something
