fill up

1. VERB

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to eat so much that one is completely full or satisfied

Examples

In Chicago, some time ago, Mr. H., age 27, a diabetic since he was six, stopped using insulin because he had bought a "magic spike "- a glass tube about the size of a pencil filled with barium chloride worth a small fraction of a cent - sold by the Vrilium Company of Chicago for $ 306 as a cure-all.

Synset Examples

He filled up on turkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuff oneself - to eat so much that you feel uncomfortably full
  • Synonym: eat one's fill - to eat until one is satisfied

2. VERB

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to make something completely full, either literally (by adding a substance until a container is full) or figuratively (by satisfying a feeling or need fully)

Examples

The bottle was filled up with flour.
This picture of extreme self-reliant individuation is difficult to reconcile with such Zendo formulas as: "O you, demons and other spiritual beings, I now offer this to you, and may this food fill up the ten quarters of the world and all the demons and other spiritual beings be fed therewith.

Synset Examples

fill a container
fill the child with pride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fill - to make full or occupy completely

3. VERB

/ fɪl ʌp / (AmE), / fɪl ʌp / (BrE)
to become full, especially of a space or container

Examples

So when the Big House filled up and ran over, the sisters-in-law found beds for everyone in their own homes.

Synset Examples

The pool slowly filled with water
The theater filled up slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fill - to make something full; to put as much of something into a container as it can hold
  • Antonym: empty - to remove all contents from; become empty
