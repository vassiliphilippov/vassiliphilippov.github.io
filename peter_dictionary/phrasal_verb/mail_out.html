mail out

1. VERB

/ meɪl aʊt / (AmE), / meɪl aʊt / (BrE)
to send something, especially to many people, by mail

Examples

They borrowed a typewriter, raised about $ 2000 in contributions, hired a secretary, persuaded a couple of young men to join them for almost no pay and began mailing out a collection of unstapled leaflets that they called Guideposts.

Synset Examples

The company mailed out the catalog to all potential customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: send out - to distribute or dispatch something to a number of people
  • Synonym: mail - to send something through postal service
