throw in

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to include something extra with something that is being sold or offered, without increasing the price; to add as a bonus or gratuity.

Examples

As a result, most people do n't have more than a vague idea what folklore actually is; they see it as a potpourri of charming, moral legends and patriotic anecdotes, with a superstition or remedy thrown in here and there.
Thoroughly modern in treatment, they are at the same time, full of simple sincerity which invariably characterizes genuine Negro folk-music and are by no means to be confused with the average' Broadway Spirituals' which depend for their racial flavor upon sundry allusions to the' Amen Corner',' judgement day',' Gabriel's horn', and a frustrated devil - with a few random' Hallelujahs' thrown in for good measure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: add - to put something with something else to increase the number or amount
  • Synonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or set

2. VERB

/ θroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to insert a remark, comment, or item into a conversation, discussion, or situation, often unexpectedly or as an addition

Examples

She concluded by asking for a brief interview - "to settle with you where "- and she threw in a tribute to his "gentle manners "and "the wild originality of your countenance ".

Synset Examples

She interjected clever remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interject - to say something abruptly, especially as an interruption
  • Synonym: add - to include something with others

3. VERB

/ ɜːr oʊ ɪn / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to stop trying or quit, especially because you admit defeat or have lost hope

Examples

It was strictly the deputy's game, but McBride had gone too far to throw in.

Synset Examples

In the second round, the challenger gave up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give up - to cease making an effort; resign oneself to failure
  • Antonym: persevere - to keep trying in spite of difficulties
