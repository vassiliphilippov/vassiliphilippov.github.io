move out

1. VERB

/ muːv aʊt / (AmE), / muːv aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place where you have been living or working, especially your home or office, and take your belongings with you

Examples

But I'’ve been instructed to tell you that if you move Klingman **out**, our friend in Miami will go along.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: move in - to begin living in a new house or residence
