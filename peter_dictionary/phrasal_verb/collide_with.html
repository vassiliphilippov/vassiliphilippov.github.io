collide with

1. VERB

/ kəˈlaɪd wɪð / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd wɪð / (BrE)
to crash into something or someone with force, usually unexpectedly

Examples

John Pezza, 69, of 734 Hartford Avenue, Providence, complained of shoulder pains after an accident in which a car he was driving collided with a car driven by Antonio Giorgio, 25, of 12 DeSoto St., Providence, on Greenville Avenue and Cherry Hill Road in Johnston yesterday.

Synset Examples

The car hit a tree
He struck the table with his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash into - to hit something with great force while moving (especially vehicles)
  • Antonym: avoid - to keep away from or prevent from happening (as in preventing collision)
