straighten out

1. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt(ə)n aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve a problem, misunderstanding, or confusion; to correct or improve the behavior or situation of someone or something

Examples

It could all be straightened out later.
Sam is having his troubles with Layton Hanover (Dean Hanover-Lucy Hanover), but hope to have him straightened out and going before long.

Synset Examples

we need to iron out our disagreements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron out - to resolve or smooth out problems or difficulties

2. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (BrE)
to improve one's behavior or situation; to reform or resolve problems, especially in one's life or conduct

Examples

And you wonder if that is why the little man lost his job and his car and stayed drunk about a year before he straightened out and moved to St. Louis, where he got to be a big unhappy success.

Synset Examples

The lazy student promised to reform
the habitual cheater finally saw the light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - to improve or correct one's behavior or habits
  • Antonym: relapse - to fall back into former bad habits or behavior

3. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtn aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve a confused, tangled, or problematic situation; to make something organized or orderly

Examples

But now he was happy she would let him straighten out her life and take care of her.

Synset Examples

Can you disentangle the cord?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untangle - to free from tangles or complications
  • Antonym: entangle - to cause to become twisted together or caught in

4. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən aʊt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt(ə)n aʊt / (BrE)
to settle or resolve a problem, misunderstanding, or confusion; to put something right

Examples

He says we can straighten out any of our problems.

Synset Examples

we need to iron out our disagreements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron out - to resolve or settle differences or problems
  • Synonym: untangle - to free from confusion or difficulties; to resolve a complicated situation
