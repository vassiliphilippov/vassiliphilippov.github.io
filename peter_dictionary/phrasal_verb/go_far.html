go far

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ fɑr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ fɑː / (BrE)
To have a wide influence, effect, or range; to achieve notable success or significance.

Examples

An adequately staffed and equipped State assessing office could apply uniform methods and standards which would go far toward producing equitable assessments on all properties throughout the State.

Synset Examples

His accomplishments go far

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have a wide reach - to affect or influence many people or things
  • Antonym: fall short - to not achieve the desired influence or success
