whisk by

1. VERB

/ wɪsk baɪ / (AmE), / wɪsk baɪ / (BrE)
to move past quickly and smoothly

Examples

As he neared the square, a round figure muffled in a long, black cape whisked by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zip by - move by very quickly
  • Synonym: rush by - move past quickly

Etymology

From 'whisk' meaning to move or sweep rapidly, combined with 'by' indicating movement past something.
