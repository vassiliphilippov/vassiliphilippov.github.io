keep to oneself

1. VERB

/ kip tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kiːp tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
To avoid socializing or sharing personal matters with others; to deliberately be solitary or private.

Examples

But he did n't play golf, did n't seem to belong to any local clubs - his work took him away a lot, of course - which probably accounted for his tendency to keep to himself.

Synset Examples

The new student keeps to herself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be reserved - to be slow to share feelings or thoughts with others
  • Antonym: be outgoing - to be sociable and inclined to seek the company of others

2. VERB

/ kip tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / kiːp tə wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to avoid interacting with others and prefer to be alone; to not share one's thoughts, feelings, or company with others

Examples

Andy kept pretty much **to himself** at first.

Synset Examples

The new student keeps to herself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdraw - deliberately move away from social interaction
  • Antonym: mingle - socialize and mix with others; opposite behavior
