weigh anchor

1. VERB

/ weɪ ˈæŋkər / (AmE), / weɪ ˈæŋkə / (BrE)
to lift an anchor from the bottom in preparation for sailing

Examples

On April 17, 1610, the sturdy little three masted bark, Discovery, weighed anchor in St. Katherine's Pool, London, and floated down the Thames toward the sea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anchor - to drop or secure an anchor to the bottom

Etymology

The verb "weigh" in this sense comes from Old English "wegan" (to lift, to carry), used nautically to mean 'raise (the anchor)'.
