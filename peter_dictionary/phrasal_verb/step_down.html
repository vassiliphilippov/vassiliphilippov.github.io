step down

1. VERB

/ stɛp daʊn / (AmE), / stɛp daʊn / (BrE)
to leave an important job or official position, often so that someone else can take over

Examples

A special presentation was made to Mrs. Geraldine Thompson of Red Bank, who is stepping down after 35 years on the committee.

Synset Examples

The Secretary of the Navy will leave office next month
The chairman resigned over the financial scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resign - to officially leave a job or position
  • Synonym: retire - to leave one's job and stop working, typically because of age
  • Antonym: step up - to take on a higher role or responsibility
