swear by

1. VERB

/ swɛr baɪ / (AmE), / swɛə baɪ / (BrE)
to have complete trust or confidence in someone or something as being effective or reliable

Examples

If I hear so much as a mouse fart in here the rest of the night, I swear by God and sonny Jesus, you will all visit the infirmary.

Synset Examples

We can trust in God
Rely on your friends
bank on your good education
I swear by my grandmother's recipes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - to believe that someone or something is reliable, good, or honest
  • Synonym: rely on - to depend on someone or something with full trust or confidence
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain or lack confidence in something or someone
