hold out

1. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to stretch out or extend something, especially a hand or object, towards someone or something

Examples

Young Frederick Seward held out his hand.
Also, when she had bitten off half of the small radish, she found the suspense unbearable; and she would snatch the finger held half of the radish out to where she could inspect it.
She held out her hand to show that she had money.
Straightening up, his eyes ablaze, he held out the battered Stetson.

Synset Examples

He held out his hand
point a finger
extend a hand
the bee exserted its sting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extend - to stretch out or make longer; to extend something
  • Synonym: offer - to present or hold something out for someone to take

2. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to continue to resist or refuse to yield to someone or something, especially for a period of time

Examples

He was holding out for something.
He was determined to hold out.
He held out a moment longer, then his nerve gave under the pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: give in - to stop resisting; to yield
  • Synonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to an opponent or authority

3. VERB

/ hoʊld aʊt / (AmE), / həʊld aʊt / (BrE)
to refuse to accept less than what one wants or demands, waiting until something desired is obtained

Examples

Vernon, however, held out for plain oyster roast, and plenty of it, unadorned by herbs or any seasoning but salt, though he did fancy a bit of lemon.

Synset Examples

He held out for the dessert and did not touch the cheeses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compromise - to accept less than one originally wanted to reach agreement
