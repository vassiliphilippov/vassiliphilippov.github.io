switch off

1. VERB

/ swɪtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to stop a device or machine from working by moving a switch to the off position

Examples

Mark stopped the car and switched off the lights and they sat looking at the water, which, there being no moon out, at first could be distinguished from the sky only by an absence of stars.
I reached over and switched off the electronic bugging device.

Synset Examples

Turn off the stereo, please
cut the engine
turn out the lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn off - to stop a device or machine from operating by switching it off
  • Antonym: switch on - to make a device or machine start working by moving its switch to the on position
