cool off

1. VERB

/ kul ɔf / (AmE), / kuːl ɒf / (BrE)
to become calmer or quieter, especially after being angry or agitated

Examples

These observations indicate that the lunar maria heat up more rapidly and also cool off more rapidly than do the mountainous regions.

Synset Examples

After the fight both men need to cool off.
It took a while after the baby was born for things to settle down again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm down - to become calmer or less excited
  • Antonym: heat up - to become more excited, agitated, or intense

2. VERB

/ kuːl ɔf / (AmE), / kuːl ɒf / (BrE)
To become less intense, active, or passionate; to lose strength or enthusiasm.

Examples

But even on these, the palette often takes over while the shotgun cools off!

Synset Examples

His enthusiasm cooled considerably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm down - to become less agitated or emotional
  • Antonym: heat up - to become more intense or excited
