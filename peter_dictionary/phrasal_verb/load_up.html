load up

1. VERB

/ loʊd ʌp / (AmE), / ləʊd ʌp / (BrE)
to fill or place a load onto (something); to put goods, supplies, or other items into or onto a container or vehicle

Examples

For 25 c load up the cooler with ice and keep cool pop in the car.

Synset Examples

load a car
load the truck with hay

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unload - to remove a load from something

2. VERB

/ loʊd ʌp / (AmE), / ləʊd ʌp / (BrE)
to fill a vehicle, container, or person with as much as possible; to place a load on something

Examples

Every evening, I load up the cart and make my round.

Synset Examples

load a car
load the truck with hay

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unload - to remove a load from something
