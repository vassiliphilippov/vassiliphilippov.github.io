set in

1. VERB

/ sɛt ɪn / (AmE), / sɛt ɪn / (BrE)
(of something undesirable or negative) to begin and become established, often gradually.

Examples

Dr. Bonnor supports the idea that the universe both expands and contracts, that in several billion years the expansion will slow up and reverse itself and that the contraction will set in.

Synset Examples

Laziness set in
After a few moments, the effects of the drug kicked in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kick in - to begin to have an effect
