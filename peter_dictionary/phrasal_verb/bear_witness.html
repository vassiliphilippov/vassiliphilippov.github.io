bear witness

1. VERB

/ bɛr ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / beə ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
to give evidence or testify, especially in support of the truth of something; to affirm or declare as evidence of a fact or event.

Examples

Fifteen years ago, troubled by the rising tide of materialism in the post-war world, a businessman and a minister asked themselves if there might not be a place for a small magazine in which men and women, regardless of creed or color, could set forth boldly their religious convictions and bear witness to the power of faith to solve the endless problems of living.

Synset Examples

The blood test showed that he was the father
Her behavior testified to her incompetence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testify - to give evidence as a witness, especially in a court of law

2. VERB

/ bɛr ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / beə ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
to give evidence or testimony, especially in a court of law, or to serve as evidence of something

Examples

Throughout the Adirondack region abandoned farm homes and wild orchards bear ghostly testimony that their owners met defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testify - to give evidence as a witness, especially in court
  • Synonym: attest - to provide or serve as clear evidence
