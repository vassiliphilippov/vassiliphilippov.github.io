put out feelers

1. VERB

/ pʊt aʊt ˈfiːlərz / (AmE), / pʊt aʊt ˈfiːləz / (BrE)
to make discreet or preliminary inquiries in order to gauge reactions or gather information

Examples

Feelers were put out last year to the City of Warwick, as reported in your newspapers, suggesting investigation of a common rubbish disposal area to service the Potowomut and Cowessett areas of Warwick along with East Greenwich.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound out - to attempt to discover opinions or intentions indirectly
