put to work

1. VERB

/ pʊt tə wɜrk / (AmE), / pʊt tə wɜːk / (BrE)
to cause (a person or thing) to begin working or being used; to employ or utilize for a purpose

Examples

Decide in the beginning to put your barbecue equipment to work.

Synset Examples

he is working his servants hard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employ - to make use of; to hire for work
  • Synonym: utilize - to make practical use of

2. VERB

/ pʊt tə wɜrk / (AmE), / pʊt tə wɜːk / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to begin working; to assign work to a person or thing

Examples

Our inmates, properly supervised, will be put to work outside these walls, performing all manner of public service.

Synset Examples

he is working his servants hard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employ - to give someone a job; to use someone for work
  • Antonym: lay off - to terminate someone's employment
