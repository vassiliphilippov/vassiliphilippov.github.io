muck up

1. VERB

/ mʌk ʌp / (AmE), / mʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to spoil, ruin, or make a mess of something; to do something badly

Examples

ExPe does n't like anybody mucking up primary evidence ".

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess up - to do something badly or make a mistake; to spoil something
  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly

Etymology

From 'muck' (dirt or waste) + 'up'; British English origin, influenced by the sense of making dirty or confused.
