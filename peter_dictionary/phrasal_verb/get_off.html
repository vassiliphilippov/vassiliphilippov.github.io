get off

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to have someone acquitted in a criminal case or to secure a lenient punishment, especially through legal assistance

Examples

When authorities convicted him of practicing medicine without a license (he got off with a suspended sentence of three years because of his advanced age of 77), one of his victims was not around to testify: He was dead of cancer.

Synset Examples

The lawyer got him off, even though there was no doubt in everybody's mind that he killed his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquit - to declare not guilty in a court of law
  • Antonym: convict - to find guilty in a court of law

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to avoid being punished or to escape the unpleasant consequences of an action; to manage not to be severely affected by something, especially something one deserves.

Examples

But did n't they get off too easy?

Synset Examples

She gets away with murder!
I couldn't get out from under these responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get away with - to avoid punishment for, or the consequences of, doing something wrong

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to send or dispatch something, especially quickly or with little delay

Examples

In their suburban cottage the crown charges, the Krogers received secrets from the mystery man, usually on the first Saturday evening of each month, and spent much of the week-end getting the secrets off to Moscow, either on a powerful transmitter buried under the kitchen floor or as dots posted over period marks in used books.

Synset Examples

The spy sent the classified information off to Russia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: send - to cause to go or be taken to a particular destination

4. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a vehicle, aircraft, or other form of transport.

Examples

Anybody who is expecting a joyride should, according to Mr. Shriver, get off the train right now.
I got off there, crossed the street, walked ahead with St. Sophia on my left, the Blue Mosque on my right, and in a moment came to the entrance of St. Sophia.
There I got off, crossed the square, and on the side directly opposite the gate found a good restaurant, hard to come by in this part of the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: get on - to board or enter a vehicle or form of transport

5. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf / (BrE)
to finish one's work or duties and be allowed to leave, typically for the rest of the day or a break

Examples

She gave a little pout and said, "I do n't get off work until eleven o' clock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to depart from a place or position, commonly after finishing one's duties

6. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈɔf / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈɒf / (BrE)
(especially in legal contexts) to help someone avoid punishment or being found guilty of a crime.

Examples

Tom, can you get me **off the hook**?

Synset Examples

The lawyer got him off, even though there was no doubt in everybody's mind that he killed his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquit - to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty
