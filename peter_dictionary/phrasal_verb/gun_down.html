gun down

1. VERB

/ ɡʌn daʊn / (AmE), / ɡʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to shoot and seriously injure or kill someone with a gun, especially deliberately

Examples

One ground of action certainly exists when fusillades of stray shots go over into West Berlin as Communist "vopos "try to gun down fleeing unarmed residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - to fire a gun at someone or something
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from harming or killing someone

2. VERB

/ ɡʌn daʊn / (AmE), / ɡʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to shoot someone, especially causing serious injury or death

Examples

Nobody has ever gunned down a New York police captain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - to fire a gun at someone or something
