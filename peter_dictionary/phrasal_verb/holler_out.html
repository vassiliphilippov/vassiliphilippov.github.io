holler out

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑlər aʊt / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə aʊt / (BrE)
To shout something loudly; to call out loudly.

Examples

We got to one house where there were five secessionist they brok + run and Arch holored out to shoot the ornery suns of biches and we all let go at them.

Synset Examples

He hollered out to surrender our weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - to shout loudly; to cry out loudly
  • Synonym: shout out - to call out loudly; to speak in a loud voice

Etymology

Holler: probably a variant of 'holler' (to shout), from 'holler' (shout).
