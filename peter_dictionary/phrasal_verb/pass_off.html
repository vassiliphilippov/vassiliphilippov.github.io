pass off

1. VERB

/ pæs ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
To treat something as unimportant or unworthy of attention; to disregard.

Examples

But there were other homesteaders who passed the Lewis murder off as a personal grudge killing, the work of one of his neighbors.

Synset Examples

She passed off the insult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismiss - to treat something as unworthy of consideration

2. VERB

/ pæs ˈɔf / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈɒf / (BrE)
to be accepted as something or someone else, especially by deceiving people; to pretend that someone or something is a particular person or thing, usually falsely.

Examples

If he does, it's still better than an even chance he won n't notice the transposition of the numbers, and if he should notice it, the thing can be passed off as an honest mistake.

Synset Examples

She passed off as a Russian agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pose as - to pretend to be someone else in order to deceive people

3. VERB

/ pæs ɔf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
to present oneself or something as someone or something else in order to be accepted, usually falsely

Examples

I don'’t like to see you come out to this clean country in your oily hair dressed up in those silk suits, trying to pass yourselves **off** as decent Americans.

Synset Examples

She passed off as a Russian agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impersonate - pretend to be another person
  • Synonym: masquerade - pretend to be someone one is not
