crack up

1. VERB

/ kræk ʌp / (AmE), / kræk ʌp / (BrE)
To have a nervous breakdown or lose mental stability, often due to stress or pressure.

Examples

One beatnik got the woman he was living with so involved in drugs and self-analysis and all-night sessions of sex that she was beginning to crack up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break down - to lose control of one's emotions or mental state

2. VERB

/ kræk ʌp / (AmE), / kræk ʌp / (BrE)
to begin laughing in an uncontrolled or loud way

Examples

Then he laughed himself silly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laugh - to make the spontaneous sounds and movements of the face and body that are the instinctive expressions of lively amusement
