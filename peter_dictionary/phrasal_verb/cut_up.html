cut up

1. VERB

/ kʌt ʌp / (AmE), / kʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to injure or damage someone or something severely, especially by cutting.

Examples

Before the fight was over, the Harlem boy had a concussion and Trig was cut up badly.

Synset Examples

The madman mutilates art work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutilate - to inflict serious damage or injury, especially by cutting

2. VERB

/ kʌt ʌp / (AmE), / kʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to divide something into smaller pieces by cutting; to chop into pieces.

Examples

Had to put my foot on it to hole it down while I cut it up fer the lob-scuse ".
"Miss Sarah, I can n't cut up no chicken.

Synset Examples

Father carved the ham

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chop up - to cut something into pieces, often with repeated sharp blows
  • Synonym: slice - to cut into thin, flat pieces
