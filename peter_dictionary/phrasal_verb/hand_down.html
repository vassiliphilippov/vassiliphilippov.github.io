hand down

1. VERB

/ hænd daʊn / (AmE), / hænd daʊn / (BrE)
to give or leave something to a younger person or to future generations, especially as an inheritance or tradition.

Examples

Chabrier's delightful music stands just at the point where the classical, rationalist tradition, (handed down to Chabrier largely in the form of operetta and salon music) becomes virtually neo-classicism.

Synset Examples

This ring was handed down through many generations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass on - to give something to someone else after one's death or retirement
  • Antonym: inherit - to receive property, money, or a title from someone after they die
