carry oneself

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈkæri wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to conduct or behave oneself in a specified manner, especially with regard to posture, attitude, or dignity

Examples

I have a distinct admiration for this man we honor today because of the humility with which he carries his greatness.

Synset Examples

She carried herself well
he bore himself with dignity
They conducted themselves well during these difficult times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct oneself - to behave in a particular way
  • Synonym: comport oneself - to behave or conduct oneself, especially in a formal manner
