swallow up

1. VERB

/ ˈswɑloʊ ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ ˈʌp / (BrE)
to enclose or envelop completely, often so that nothing remains visible or accessible

Examples

She seemed to have come such a long distance - too far for her destination which had wilfully been swallowed up in the greedy gloom of the trees.
She had to get away from here before this demoniac possession swallowed up the liquid of her eyes and sank into the fibers of her brain, depriving her of reason and sight.

Synset Examples

The huge waves swallowed the small boat and it sank shortly thereafter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engulf - to sweep over and cover completely
  • Synonym: consume - to use up or destroy completely
  • Antonym: release - to set free from confinement or engulfment
