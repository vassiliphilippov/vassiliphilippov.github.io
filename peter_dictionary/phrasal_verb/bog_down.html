bog down

1. VERB

/ bɑɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
(of a person or process) To become stuck, delayed, or unable to make progress, especially because of difficulties or complications.

Examples

Too many have bogged down in bickering.

Synset Examples

She bogged down many times while she wrote her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to stop making progress unexpectedly or abruptly
  • Antonym: progress - to move forward or make progress

Etymology

From 'bog' (a wet, spongy ground) and the metaphorical idea of becoming stuck as if in a bog.

2. VERB

/ bɑg daʊn / (AmE), / bɒg daʊn / (BrE)
to cause to become stuck, delayed, or unable to progress effectively

Examples

But because of the peculiar nature of the military situation in Laos, the Soviet leader must be tempted to let things ride - a course that would appear to cost him little on the spot, but would bog Washington in a tactical mess.

Synset Examples

The vote would bog down the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stall - to stop or cause to stop making progress
  • Synonym: impede - to hinder or obstruct progress
  • Antonym: free - to cause to no longer be stuck or delayed

Etymology

From 'bog' (a wet, muddy ground) + 'down'; metaphorical use since early 20th century.
