skim over

1. VERB

/ skɪm oʊvɚ / (AmE), / skɪm əʊvə / (BrE)
To read something quickly and superficially, paying attention only to the main points rather than details.

Examples

He skimmed over the millions that went to Meeker Park, Medfield Hospital, the civic center, the Public Health Nursing Association, the library, and so on, pausing when he came to the scholarship fund.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scan - to look through written material quickly to locate information
  • Antonym: study - to examine or read something thoroughly
