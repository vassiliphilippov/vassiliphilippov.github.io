get through

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt θru / (AmE), / ɡɛt θruː / (BrE)
to finish doing something, especially something difficult or time-consuming

Examples

The evidence in court was testimony about the interview, which for Holmes lasted an hour, although at least one white student at Georgia got through this ritual by a simple phone conversation.
The gallant company completed Act 4, and got through part of Act 5,.
How would you feel about it if I were to ask you for a date when I get through at Hanover "?
Before we get through he 'll have the Blackfeet hankerin' for our hair and our goods.

Synset Examples

I finally got through this homework assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - to finish making or doing something
  • Synonym: finish - to bring a task or activity to an end

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt θru / (AmE), / ɡet θruː / (BrE)
to spend or manage to cope with a period of time, especially one that is difficult, boring, or unpleasant

Examples

The one unifying note, if any, is sounded in the initial article entitled: "How to Get Through the Day ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make it through - to survive or endure until the end of something
  • Synonym: pass the time - to spend time, often while waiting or being bored

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈθru / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈθruː / (BrE)
to succeed in reaching a place, person, or result after overcoming difficulties or obstacles

Examples

And there would be no chance of signaling them - without the Nernst generator Jack could not send a call powerful enough to get through all the static, and by the time he could rebuild his fusion power the skiff would be gone.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reach - to arrive at a place, goal, or person; to succeed in contact
  • Antonym: fail - to be unsuccessful in reaching or achieving something

4. VERB

/ ɡɛt θruː / (AmE), / ɡɛt θruː / (BrE)
(of an idea, message, or feeling) to be understood or to reach someone's consciousness or emotions; to make someone aware of something, often after difficulty.

Examples

I like to think the last thing that went through his head, other than that bullet, was to wonder how the hell Andy Dufresne ever got the best of him.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn on - (of an idea) to become clear to someone
  • Synonym: sink in - to become fully understood
