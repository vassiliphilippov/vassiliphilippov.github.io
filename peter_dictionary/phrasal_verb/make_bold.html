make bold

1. VERB

/ meɪk boʊld / (AmE), / meɪk bəʊld / (BrE)
To act in a way that is presumptuous or takes liberties, often without permission or sufficient authority.

Examples

But what quarter could a poor defenseless woman expect from a dictator who would even make so bold as to close all of the banks in our great nation?

Synset Examples

How dare you call my lawyer?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venture - to dare to do something or go somewhere that may be dangerous or unpleasant
  • Synonym: dare - to have the courage to do something
