make do

1. VERB

/ meɪk duː / (AmE), / meɪk dʊ / (BrE)
to manage or cope with limited resources, using what is available rather than what is wanted or needed

Examples

The expedient thing - yes, very true, one must make do as one could, in some situations.

Synset Examples

We got by on just a gallon of gas
They made do on half a loaf of bread every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get by - to manage with what you have
  • Synonym: cope - to deal successfully with a difficult situation
