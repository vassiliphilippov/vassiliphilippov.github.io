shoo away

1. VERB

/ ʃu əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ʃu əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To make a person or animal go away, usually by waving your hands or saying 'shoo.'

Examples

The women said they had seen him wave an exhausted farewell; but he might have been shooing away the fleas that hopped from his yellow dog onto him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase away - drive someone or something away
  • Synonym: drive away - force to leave

Etymology

From the exclamation 'shoo', imitative of the sound used to drive something away, plus 'away.'
