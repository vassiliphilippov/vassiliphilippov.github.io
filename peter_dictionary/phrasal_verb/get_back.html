get back

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk / (BrE)
to recover something or someone that was lost, taken, or returned

Examples

"He should be getting back in the groove before long.
How about me trying to help her get her job back?
Now Hans had given Ma something of his - we both had when we thought she was going straight to Pa - something valuable; but since she did n't know we 'd given it to her, there was no easy way of getting it back.

Synset Examples

We got back the money after we threatened to sue the company
He got back his son from the kidnappers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retrieve - to get something back that was lost or left behind
  • Antonym: lose - to no longer have something because it has been taken or cannot be found

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk / (BrE)
to take revenge on someone or to retaliate for something done to you

Examples

The most articulate Republicans are those who, in their desire to get back at Mr. Kennedy, already have created the image of a Republican leadership which is reluctant to assist the distressed and the unemployed, and which is even more unwilling to help old people who need medical care.

Synset Examples

I cannot accept the defeat--I want to get even

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get even - to retaliate or take revenge in order to restore equality or balance
  • Antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt bæk / (AmE), / ɡɛt bæk / (BrE)
to recover possession of something or somebody that was lost or taken away

Examples

I'll talk to you guys when you get back.

Synset Examples

We got back the money after we threatened to sue the company
He got back his son from the kidnappers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to regain possession of something lost
  • Antonym: lose - to be deprived of or stop having something
