lose it

1. VERB

/ luːz ɪt / (AmE), / luːz ɪt / (BrE)
To become unable to control one's emotions, often resulting in an outburst of anger, distress, or hysteria.

Examples

He started to lose it.

Synset Examples

When she heard that she had not passed the exam, she lost it completely
When her baby died, she snapped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break down - to lose control of emotions and start to cry or become unstable
  • Antonym: keep it together - to remain calm and maintain control over one's emotions
