take the stand

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə stænd / (AmE), / teɪk ðə stænd / (BrE)
to go to the witness box in a court of law to give testimony

Examples

The first witness, Moses Winston Mardis, 5835 Michigan av., a real estate agent and former bail bondsman, took the stand after opening statements had been made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testify - to give evidence as a witness in a court of law
