turn in

1. VERB

/ tɝn ɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or driver) to leave a road by turning, especially to enter a driveway or side road.

Examples

Turning in at the Flannagans' driveway, he tried to remember if he had ever met them.
Her father's attention would be on the road ahead and it would n't deviate an inch until he crossed the bridge at the Falls and took the River Road to LaSalle and, finally, turned in at their own driveway at 387 Heather Heights.

Synset Examples

Turn in after you see the gate

2. VERB

/ tɝn ɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
to give or deliver (a performance, effort, or result), especially as a measure of achievement or quality

Examples

Soaring to stardom along with him, Margo Felicity Brighetti, a luscious and curvaceously beguiling Italian starlet, turns in a creditable performance as an airplane mechanic.

Synset Examples

They turned in a splendid effort
They turned in top jobs for the second straight game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perform - to carry out an action or task, especially in front of an audience

3. VERB

/ tɝn ɪn / (AmE), / tɜːn ɪn / (BrE)
to give something or someone to an authority, especially as required or expected

Examples

Fire Fighters Local 798, which is sponsoring the toy program for the 12 th straight year, issued a call for San Franciscans to turn in discarded toys, which will be repaired by off-duty firemen.

Synset Examples

the guard delivered the criminal to the police
render up the prisoners
render the town to the enemy
fork over the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand over - to give or deliver something to someone in authority
  • Synonym: give up - to surrender or relinquish something
