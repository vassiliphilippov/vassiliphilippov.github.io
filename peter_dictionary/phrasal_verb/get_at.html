get at

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt æt / (BrE)
to mean or intend to communicate something, especially indirectly; to imply

Examples

New, indeed, is Luther's perception, but not modern, as anyone knows who has ever tried to make intelligible to modern students what Luther was getting at.
What I'm getting at is that we were fully prepared for Felix's being an odd one.

Synset Examples

What are you driving at?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imply - to express or suggest indirectly
  • Synonym: hint - to suggest something without saying it directly

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt æt / (BrE)
to reach or gain access to something, especially something that is difficult to reach or obtain

Examples

Each brass handle and hinge shone for his reward, and he knew how to get at the dust in the china flowers and how to take down the long glass drops which hung from the chandelier.

Synset Examples

How does one access the attic in this house?
I cannot get to the T.V. antenna, even if I climb on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: access - to reach or make use of; to gain entry to

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt æt / (AmE), / ɡɛt æt / (BrE)
to reach or gain access to something, especially when it is difficult to do so

Examples

Synset Examples

How does one access the attic in this house?
I cannot get to the T.V. antenna, even if I climb on the roof

Related Terms
