clue in

1. VERB

/ klu ɪn / (AmE), / kluː ɪn / (BrE)
to provide someone with information or make someone aware of something that was previously unknown or unclear

Examples

In 1959, the State Senate finally clued into the fact they couldn't buy him off with just a $200 check.

Synset Examples

Can you clue me in?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inform - to give someone information about something

Etymology

From the noun 'clue' (variant of 'clew'), meaning a hint or piece of information, plus the preposition 'in'.
