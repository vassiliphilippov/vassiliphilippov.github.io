be on

1. VERB

/ bi ɑn / (AmE), / bi ɒn / (BrE)
to appear in a television or radio program, or to be broadcast.

Examples

Again, my name was on all the front pages.

Synset Examples

The news won't be on tonight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air - to be broadcast or shown on TV or radio
  • Antonym: be off - not to be broadcast or shown at a scheduled time

2. VERB

/ bi ɑn / (AmE), / bi ɒn / (BrE)
to appear on a show, television, or radio; to be broadcast

Examples

[MAN SPEAKING INDISTINCTLY ON TV] MAN: [ON TV] ...pick up of two by Holden.

Synset Examples

The news won't be on tonight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air - to be broadcast (as a program)
