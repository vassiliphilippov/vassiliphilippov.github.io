roll up

1. VERB

/ roʊl ʌp / (AmE), / rəʊl ʌp / (BrE)
To form something into a cylindrical shape by rolling it.

Examples

Andy rolled up the revised script he had been studying.

Synset Examples

Roll up the cloth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unroll - to open or spread out something that has been rolled up

2. VERB

/ roʊl ʌp / (AmE), / rəʊl ʌp / (BrE)
(informal) To arrive, especially in a vehicle or with a sense of occasion.

Examples

And, oh, my Lord, how the money rolled in.

Synset Examples

He rolled up in a black Mercedes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show up - to arrive at a place, especially unexpectedly
  • Synonym: turn up - to arrive at a place, especially unexpectedly or without prior arrangement
