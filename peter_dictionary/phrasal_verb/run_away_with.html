run away (with)

1. VERB

/ ˌrʌn əˈweɪ (wɪð) / (AmE), / ˌrʌn əˈweɪ (wɪð) / (BrE)
(of a process or situation) to escape control; to proceed rapidly and without restraint beyond proper limits.

Examples

Thus, if corporations are not to run away with us, they must become quasi governmental institutions, subject to public control and needs.

Synset Examples

Industry is running away with us all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get out of hand - to become unmanageable or uncontrollable
  • Synonym: spiral out of control - to escalate beyond control
