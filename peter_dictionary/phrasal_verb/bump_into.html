bump into

1. VERB

/ bʌmp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / bʌmp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to meet someone by chance, usually unexpectedly

Examples

One is always a little surprised to bump into such individual distinctions when it is unexpected.

Synset Examples

We find this idea in Plato
I happened upon the most wonderful bakery not very far from here
She chanced upon an interesting book in the bookstore the other day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run into - to meet someone unexpectedly
  • Synonym: encounter - to meet someone, especially unexpectedly

2. VERB

/ bʌmp ˈɪntu / (AmE), / bʌmp ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to collide with someone or something, usually accidentally and with some force

Examples

He must rearrange matters so that two performers do not bump into each other.

Synset Examples

I ran into the telephone pole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run into - to collide with someone or something accidentally
  • Degree Higher: crash into - to hit something hard while moving
  • Degree Lower: brush against - touch gently or lightly while passing
