cancel out

1. VERB

/ ˈkænsəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkænsəl aʊt / (BrE)
To neutralize or offset the effect of something, making it ineffective or void.

Examples

The shell, which served the strain so well at a relatively early stage in the evolutionary scheme, tended to cancel out the possibility of future development.
Although a similar situs for tangible property is mentioned in the statute, this is cancelled out by the provision that definite kinds of property "and all other tangible property "situated or being in any town is taxable where the property is situated.
The dromozoa fed him in their mysterious way; the drug canceled out his needs for cycles of the body.

Synset Examples

The new tax effectively cancels out my raise
The `A' will cancel out the `C' on your record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutralize - to make something ineffective by applying an opposite force
  • Synonym: counteract - to act against something in order to reduce its force or effect

2. VERB

/ ˈkæn.səl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or neutralize the effect of something by having an opposite effect; to make something no longer significant or effective

Examples

He cancels out what they did to Father. - We should listen to them.

Synset Examples

The new tax effectively cancels out my raise
The `A' will cancel out the `C' on your record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutralize - to make something ineffective by applying an opposite force or effect
  • Antonym: reinforce - to strengthen or increase the effect of something
