hold up

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ʌp / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to rob someone or something by threatening them with a weapon or other form of menace, especially at gunpoint

Examples

A band of robbers enters a railroad station, overpowers and ties up the telegraph operator, holds up the train and escapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rob - to steal from someone or somewhere, especially using force or threats

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ʌp / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to remain strong or continue to function or survive, especially during or after a difficult period or situation

Examples

But that is too simple, and won't hold up.
