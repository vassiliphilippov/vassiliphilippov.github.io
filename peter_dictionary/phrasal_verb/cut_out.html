cut out

1. VERB

/ kʌt aʊt / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to form or create something by cutting material into a particular shape

Examples

Some time in 1912, Picasso cut out and folded a piece of paper in the shape of a guitar; to this he glued and fitted other pieces of paper and four taut strings, thus creating a sequence of flat surfaces in real and sculptural space to which there clung only the vestige of a picture plane.

Synset Examples

Picasso cut out a guitar from a piece of paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carve - to shape something by cutting away material, especially wood or stone
  • Synonym: sculpt - to form or shape something as an artist does with clay or stone

2. VERB

/ kʌt aʊt / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or delete something, especially by cutting; to omit or eliminate.

Examples

"I 've adjusted the gauge so that the pump cuts out before the water gets too low ".
Today, in the era of the integrated musical when an individual song must contribute to the over-all development of the show, it is understandable that a song, no matter how excellent it may be on its own terms, is cut out because it does not perform the function required of it.

Synset Examples

Cut out the extra text
cut out the newspaper article

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delete - remove or erase written, printed, or recorded material
  • Antonym: add - put in or include (opposite action of remove)

3. VERB

/ kʌt aʊt / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt / (BrE)
To suddenly block or move in front of someone, especially another vehicle, causing them to slow down or stop.

Examples

In Chicago, the driver cut out would likely jam his gas pedal to the floor in an effort to force the other car back.

Synset Examples

The bicyclist was cut out by the van

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to block someone's path or stop them from continuing, especially in traffic

4. VERB

/ kʌt aʊt / (AmE), / kʌt aʊt / (BrE)
(of a machine or engine) to stop operating suddenly or unexpectedly.

Examples

Yeah, well, let'’s cut out the bullshit.

Synset Examples

The pump suddenly cut out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break down - (of a machine) to stop functioning
