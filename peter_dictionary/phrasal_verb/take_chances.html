take chances

1. VERB

/ teɪk ˈtʃænsɪz / (AmE), / teɪk ˈtʃɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
to do something risky in the hope of achieving a good result

Examples

The most frequent excuse for the prevalence of unoriginals and tested imports is increasing production expense - producers cannot afford to take chances.

Synset Examples

When you buy these stocks you are gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take risks - to do something that could have a bad result in hope of success
  • Antonym: play it safe - to avoid risky actions
