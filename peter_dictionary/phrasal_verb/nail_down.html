nail down

1. VERB

/ neɪl daʊn / (AmE), / neɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to succeed in obtaining or securing (a job, position, agreement, etc.)

Examples

Some brilliant rookies nailed them down, so that this spring just two spots, left and right field, are really up for grabs.

Synset Examples

He nailed down a spot at Harvard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - to succeed in obtaining or achieving something
  • Synonym: win - to succeed in achieving something sought after

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'nail down' (to fasten with nails), extended metaphorically to mean securing or fixing something firmly.
