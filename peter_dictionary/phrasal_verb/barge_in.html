barge in

1. VERB

/ bɑrdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / bɑːdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place or conversation suddenly and rudely, without being invited or asked

Examples

Kahler continued: "I fixed his dog the other day and I guess he's sore, so I expected him to come barging in ".

Synset Examples

let's crash the party!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrude - to enter somewhere without permission; synonym of 'barge in'
