write off

1. VERB

/ raɪt ɑːf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
To accept that someone or something is no longer useful, valuable, or likely to succeed; to dismiss as a loss or failure.

Examples

Morrison points out that since our country is more urbanized than the Soviet Union or Red China, it is the most vulnerable of the great powers - Europe of course must be written off out of hand.
This is the point on which so many people have written off the aircraft in favor of the missile.

Synset Examples

write it off as a loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismiss - to decide that someone or something is not important and not worth considering
  • Synonym: disregard - to ignore or pay no attention to something
  • Antonym: salvage - to save something from loss (opposite intent)

2. VERB

/ raɪt ɔf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
To compose or produce (something, such as a letter or note) quickly and without hesitation.

Examples

There she extracted two limp vellum sheets and wrote off the letters, one to Abel, one to Mark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash off - to write or do something quickly without much care

3. VERB

/ raɪt ɔf / (AmE), / raɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to cancel a debt, accepting that it will not be paid; to remove an amount of money from official records as a loss

Examples

You can write that **off**.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that something, especially a debt, no longer has effect
