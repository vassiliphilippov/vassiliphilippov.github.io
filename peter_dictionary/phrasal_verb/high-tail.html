high-tail

1. VERB

/ haɪˌteɪl / (AmE), / haɪˌteɪl / (BrE)
To leave, flee, or retreat quickly; to move away at high speed.

Examples

The luxury of Paris' most fashionable hotel, the George 5,, bored the beautifully-built blonde, so she high-tailed it to Rome.

Synset Examples

The actress high-tailed to her villa when reporters began to follow her to the restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw from danger or difficulty quickly

Etymology

Probably from the image of an animal, especially a deer, lifting its tail and running away rapidly.
