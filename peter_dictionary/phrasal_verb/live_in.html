live in

1. VERB

/ lɪv ɪn / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn / (BrE)
(of an employee) to reside at their place of work, especially in someone else's house.

Examples

First of all there was the parsonage, an utterly impossible place for civilized people to live in, originally poorly conceived, apparently not repaired for years, with no plumbing or sewage, with rat-holes and rot.

Synset Examples

our babysitter lives in, as it is too far to commute for her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live out - to reside away from one's place of work, especially as a domestic employee

2. VERB

/ lɪv ɪn / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn / (BrE)
(of an employee, especially a domestic worker) to reside at the place where one is employed, usually in the employer's house.

Examples

Can somebody tell me what kind of a world we live in... ...when a man dressed as a bat... ...gets all my press?

Synset Examples

our babysitter lives in, as it is too far to commute for her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live out - to not live at the place where one is employed, going home at the end of each day
