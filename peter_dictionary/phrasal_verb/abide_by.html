abide by

1. VERB

/ əˈbaɪd baɪ / (AmE), / əˈbaɪd baɪ / (BrE)
to accept and act according to a rule, decision, or instruction

Examples

This year, I am told, the CBS network will continue to abide by the rule but NBC will play to a conclusion here.
Religion provides the most attractive rewards, either in this world or the next, for those who not merely abide by its norms, but who engage in good works.

Synset Examples

He complied with my instructions
You must comply or else!
Follow these simple rules
abide by the rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comply with - act in accordance with a rule or instruction
  • Antonym: disobey - refuse to follow a rule or order
