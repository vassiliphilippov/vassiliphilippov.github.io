get together

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / ɡɛt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to bring people into a group; to assemble or gather people for a particular purpose or activity.

Examples

Presumably a cocktail party is expected to fulfill the host's desire to get together a number of people who are inadequately acquainted and thereby arrange for bringing the level of acquaintance up to adequacy for future cooperative endeavors.

Synset Examples

assemble your colleagues
get together all those who are interested in the project
gather the close family members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assemble - to bring people or things together; to gather
  • Synonym: gather - to collect or bring together

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / ɡɛt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to meet with others socially or for a specific purpose

Examples

After we got a script and the spots for the songs were blocked out, we 'd get together for an hour or so every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together in order to spend time, discuss, or do something

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / ɡɛt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to cooperate or work jointly with others, especially on a shared project or task

Examples

If only they and Fausto Cleva in the pit had got together a bit more.

Synset Examples

The soprano and the pianist did not get together very well
We joined forces with another research group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborate - to work jointly with others, especially in an intellectual endeavor

4. VERB

/ ɡɛt təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / ɡɛt təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to meet with other people socially or for a particular purpose

Examples

I thought of getting a board **together**.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together in the same place or for a common purpose
