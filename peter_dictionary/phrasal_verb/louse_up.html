louse up

1. VERB

/ laʊs ʌp / (AmE), / laʊs ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mess of; to spoil or ruin something through mistake or clumsiness

Examples

"They 're going to louse me up good.
The Man from Mars had interrupted him with his work half finished - and that stupid jackass Short was certain to louse it up.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: mess up - to make a mistake or ruin something
  • Synonym: screw up - to do something badly or make a mistake

Etymology

From 'louse' (the insect), used figuratively in American slang from early 20th century, meaning to spoil or ruin.
