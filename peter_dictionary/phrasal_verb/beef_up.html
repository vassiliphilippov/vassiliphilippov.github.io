beef up

1. VERB

/ biːf ʌp / (AmE), / biːf ʌp / (BrE)
to make something stronger, more effective, or more intense

Examples

Interpretation, naturally, remains the role of the therapist, but orientation - not only the patient's vocal giveaways of geographical and socio-economic background, but also vocal but non-verbal giveaways of danger spots in his relationship to people - can be considerably beefed up by the linguist.

Synset Examples

This exercise will strengthen your upper body
strengthen the relations between the two countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger
  • Synonym: reinforce - to strengthen or support

Etymology

From 'beef' (to increase muscle or strength), informal use dating from mid-20th century.
