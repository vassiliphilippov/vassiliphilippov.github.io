hide out

1. VERB

/ haɪd aʊt / (AmE), / haɪd aʊt / (BrE)
to stay in a secret place, usually to avoid danger or being found by the authorities

Examples

Prosecutor Baird immediately assumed he was hiding out there after the shooting and began preparing an indictment.

Synset Examples

Probably his horse would be close to where he was hiding
She is hiding out in a cabin in Montana

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hide - to put oneself or something out of sight
