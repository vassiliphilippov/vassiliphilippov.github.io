wear off

1. VERB

/ wɛr ɔf / (AmE), / wɛə ɔf / (BrE)
to become gradually removed or diminished as a result of use, stress, or friction

Examples

In spots such as the elbows and knees the second skin is worn off and I realized the aborigines were much darker than they appeared; as if the coating of sweat, dirt, and ashes were a cosmetic.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rub off - to be removed by rubbing
  • Synonym: wear away - to gradually erode or be eroded
