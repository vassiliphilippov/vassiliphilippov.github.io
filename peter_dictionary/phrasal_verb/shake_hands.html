shake hands

1. VERB

/ ʃeɪk hændz / (AmE), / ʃeɪk hændz / (BrE)
to take someone else's hand and move it up and down as a greeting, sign of agreement, or to congratulate them

Examples

"I think they played Hail to the Chief better than the Marine Corps Band, and we are grateful to them ", President Kennedy remarked after mounting the bandstand and shaking hands with conductor James Christian Pfohl.
As he left the bandstand to return to his office, the slender, sun-tanned Chief Executive paused along the way to shake hands with the members of the audience in wheel chairs forming the first row under the field tent set up for the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greet - to give a polite word or sign of welcome or recognition
  • Degree Higher: hug - to put your arms around someone as a sign of affection

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪk hændz / (AmE), / ʃeɪk hændz / (BrE)
to clasp and move someone's hand up and down in greeting, agreement, or congratulation

Examples

If we can't do business, we'll just shake hands and that'll be it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handshake - an act of shaking hands as a greeting or sign of agreement
