sound off

1. VERB

/ saʊnd ɔf / (AmE), / saʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
to express one's opinions openly, loudly, or without fear or hesitation

Examples

Emerson - Platonist, idealist, doctrinaire - sounded a high Transcendental note in his "Boston Hymn ", delivered in 1863 in the Boston Music Hall amidst thundering applause: "Pay ransom to the owner and fill the bag to the brim.

Synset Examples

John spoke up at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speak out - to express opinions freely and publicly
  • Antonym: keep quiet - to refrain from expressing one's opinions
