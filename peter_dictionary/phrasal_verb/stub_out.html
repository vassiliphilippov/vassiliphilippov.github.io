stub out

1. VERB

/ stʌb aʊt / (AmE), / stʌb aʊt / (BrE)
To extinguish (a cigarette, cigar, etc.) by pressing the end against a surface.

Examples

One morning, we discovered not only that the pennies were missing from the idol but that a cigarette had been stubbed out in its lap.

Synset Examples

stub out your cigar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put out - to extinguish (a light, fire, etc.)

Etymology

From 'stub' meaning the end part of something, especially what remains after the rest has been used, and 'out' suggesting completion of an action.
