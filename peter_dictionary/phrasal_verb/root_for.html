root for

1. VERB

/ rut fɔr / (AmE), / ruːt fə / (BrE)
To support or cheer for someone or something, especially in a competition or contest.

Examples

What are you a Jap-lover or something, you on their side?

Synset Examples

We all rooted for the home team
I'm pulling for the underdog
Are you siding with the defender of the title?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer for - to encourage or shout support for someone or a team
  • Synonym: support - to back or give approval to someone or something

Etymology

Possibly from earlier American usage 'to root' (to shout encouragement), origin uncertain but first recorded late 19th century in US sporting contexts.
