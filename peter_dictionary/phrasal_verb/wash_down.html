wash down

1. VERB

/ wɑʃ ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / wɒʃ ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
To drink something after eating in order to help swallow the food or to make it easier to eat.

Examples

He washed it down with beer and produced a deep-seated belch.
Steak House has such a run on beer to wash down that Mexican food "Tex "Burgess had to call the draft man twice in one day.

Synset Examples

She washed down her dinner with a bottle of red wine
He washes down his worries with a nightly glass of whisky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp down - to swallow food or drink quickly and in large amounts
  • Antonym: choke - have difficulty swallowing food; opposite in terms of smooth eating

2. VERB

/ wɑʃ daʊn / (AmE), / wɒʃ daʊn / (BrE)
(of liquid or an object) to flow freely or be carried along by a stream of water or other liquid.

Examples

It was partially cemented by ages and pressure, yet it crumpled before the onslaught of the powerful streams, the force of a thousand fire hoses, and with the gold it held washed down through the long sluices.

Synset Examples

The body washed down the river
