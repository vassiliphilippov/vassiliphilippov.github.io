go under

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈʌndə / (BrE)
to sink below the surface of water or another liquid and become submerged, especially referring to boats or objects.

Examples

They got it over the side and clambered aboard only a few minutes before their schooner went under.

Synset Examples

The raft sank and its occupants drowned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sink - to go down below the surface of water or another liquid
