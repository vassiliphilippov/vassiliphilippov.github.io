look out over

1. VERB

/ lʊk aʊt ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to have or offer a view in a particular direction, especially so as to overlook a place or scene.

Examples

Jefferson Lawrence was alone at the small, perfectly appointed table by the window looking out over the river.

Synset Examples

The house looks out on a tennis court
The apartment overlooks the Hudson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - to have a view of something from above
