chalk up

1. VERB

/ tʃɑk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or record (a score, debt, or achievement), especially in a way that is noted or remarked upon

Examples

If they can n't chalk up big business here then let's stop this noise about how hip we are, and stick to our community singing.

Synset Examples

he chalked up $100 in the course of the evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run up - to accumulate a debt rapidly

Etymology

From the practice of recording scores or debts with chalk on a board.

2. VERB

/ tʃɑk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ʌp / (BrE)
to record or score (a point, achievement, or result), especially in a contest or as part of a series

Examples

Conversely, if he gives a heavy rating to his own reading, and finds more accurate facts in it than in the others, a point is chalked up for the intrinsic, objective meaningfulness of this type of mediumistic material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: score - to gain a point in a game or contest
  • Synonym: record - to make an official note of something, especially an achievement or statistic

Etymology

From the practice of using chalk to keep scores or tallies on a board.
