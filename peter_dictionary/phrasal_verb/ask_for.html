ask for

1. VERB

/ æsk fəɹ / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə / (BrE)
to do something that makes it likely you will experience trouble, criticism, or a negative reaction

Examples

They 're asking for union trouble.
To the extent that he was ostracized or even reviled, we solace ourselves by saying he asked for it.
It was a bad place for Roberts to wind up, but Roberts had asked for it.
If he had married her, he 'd have been asking for trouble.

Synset Examples

ask for trouble
invite criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invite - to encourage or make likely (a consequence or response)
  • Synonym: court - to act in a way that is likely to provoke (trouble, danger, etc.)
