buy up

1. VERB

/ baɪ ʌp / (AmE), / baɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to purchase all or most of something, especially to gain control or prevent others from obtaining it, often used in the context of businesses, goods, or shares

Examples

He did this by the charming practice of buying up used electric blankets for $ 5 to $ 10 from survivors of patients who had died, reconditioning them, and selling them at $ 185 each.
He bought up Cezannes, Braques, Matisses, Legers, a splendid Picasso series, more than 70 Giacometti sculptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquire - to get or obtain something, especially by buying; general verb for obtaining ownership
  • Antonym: sell off - to sell something, especially quickly and cheaply, often to get rid of it; opposite commercial action
