make over

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / meɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to change something so that it has a different appearance or form; to remake or redesign

Examples

In any case, he refused to accept the implications of the analysis, that he needed to be made over.

Synset Examples

retread an old plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - to make something again or differently
  • Synonym: rework - to use again in an altered form

2. VERB

/ meɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / meɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to change or renovate something so that it appears new or different; to remodel or transform.

Examples

"Roots ", the new play at the brand-new Mayfair Theater on 46 th St. which has been made over from a night club, is about the intellectual and spiritual awakening of an English farm girl.

Synset Examples

She is remaking her image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - to make something again, especially with improvements or changes
  • Synonym: refurbish - to clean, decorate, and repair something to improve its condition
  • Synonym: restore - to bring something back to its original condition

3. VERB

/ meɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / meɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to give a new appearance, style, or character to someone or something; to remake or transform

Examples

Alicia's been made over in line with my new philosophy.

Synset Examples

She is remaking her image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - to make again or differently; to transform
  • Synonym: transform - to change completely in appearance or character
