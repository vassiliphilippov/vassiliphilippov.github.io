run down

1. VERB

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
(of a person, machine, etc.) to lose energy, strength, or power and gradually stop functioning or become exhausted.

Examples

Occasionally, when he had an unusual flower that he wanted more of he did; but pansy seeds, he told me, soon "run down ".
I was ready to jump but when Ma said she 'd get the whisky it surprised him like it surprised me, and he ran down.

Synset Examples

At the end of the march, I pooped out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tire out - to make someone very tired
  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone completely tired or to use up resources

2. VERB

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to move downward, especially by flowing or descending along a surface or path

Examples

Moisture ran down the cheeks, gathered at the tip of the nose, and at the chin.
Muffling an exclamation, Russ sprang to the nearest steps and ran down.

Synset Examples

The water ran down

3. VERB

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to investigate or trace the source of something, especially a piece of information or a lead

Examples

"A couple of tips we 're running down - nothing promising.
Only an occasional tip turned out to be a phony, and, like the police, Casey had made a point of running down all such suggestions and he did not hesitate this time.

Synset Examples

We are running down a few tips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track down - to search for and find someone or something
  • Synonym: trace - to find or discover by investigation

4. VERB

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to examine, review, or check something quickly or briefly

Examples

Before midnight he speeded his heart, resumed normal breathing, ran down his check list, uncurled and sat up.

Synset Examples

She scanned the newspaper headlines while waiting for the taxi

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scan - to examine quickly or superficially
  • Degree Higher: inspect - to look at closely and carefully

5. VERB

/ rʌn daʊn / (AmE), / rʌn daʊn / (BrE)
to injure or kill (someone or something) by hitting them with a vehicle and driving over them

Examples

When the Plymouth neared, it veered toward him and seemed about to run him down.
