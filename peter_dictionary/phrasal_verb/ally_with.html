ally with

1. VERB

/ əˈlaɪ wɪθ / (AmE), / əˈlaɪ wɪð / (BrE)
to unite formally with another group, country, or individual for a common purpose or cause

Examples

Man has the experience of being helpfully allied with what he cannot fully understand; he is a coordinate part of all of the mysterious energy and being and movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate with - to connect or partner with

Etymology

From Latin 'alligare' (to bind to), via French 'allier'.
