draw in

1. VERB

/ drɔ ɪn / (AmE), / drɔː ɪn / (BrE)
to attract or bring people or things towards oneself or itself, often by appealing features or influence

Examples

It seemed to her, as it seemed each night, that the gloom drew itself in and became densest at the table's empty chairs, giving her the frequent illusion that she dined with shadows.

Synset Examples

Her good looks attract the stares of many men
The ad pulled in many potential customers
This pianist pulls huge crowds
The store owner was happy that the ad drew in many new customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attract - to cause to come near or be drawn toward
  • Antonym: repel - to drive or force back; to discourage approach or interest

2. VERB

/ drɔ ɪn / (AmE), / drɔː ɪn / (BrE)
to pull something inward or towards a center or oneself

Examples

By a wrenching effort, he managed to hunch and draw in, to take the final fall on his back and shoulders rather than his head.

Synset Examples

The pilot drew in the landing gear
The cat retracted his claws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retract - to draw something back or in
