step in

1. VERB

/ stɛp ɪn / (AmE), / stɛp ɪn / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation, especially in order to help, mediate, or prevent something from happening

Examples

Here's where luck would normally step in.
The Japanese want to increase exports to the U. S. While they have been curbing shipments, they have watched Hong Kong step in and capture an expanding share of the big U. S. market.

Synset Examples

Why did the U.S. not intervene earlier in WW II?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intervene - become involved in a situation to change what is happening
  • Antonym: step back - withdraw or remove oneself from involvement
