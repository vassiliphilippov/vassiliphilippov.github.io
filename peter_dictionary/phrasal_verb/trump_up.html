trump up

1. VERB

/ trʌmp ʌp / (AmE), / trʌmp ʌp / (BrE)
to invent a false story, accusation, or excuse, especially for a dishonest purpose

Examples

Mother even went so far as to trump up for me matrimonial opportunities with Pasadena debs who had been educated abroad, and with those of the more lenient Los Angeles area where a debutante was a girl who had been to high school.

Synset Examples

trump up charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabricate - to invent or concoct (something), typically with deceitful intent
  • Synonym: forge - to make or produce (something false) in order to deceive
  • Synonym: concoct - to create or devise (a story or plan), often in a deceptive way
  • Antonym: exonerate - to officially absolve from blame; antonym as it is the act of clearing rather than making false claims
