map out

1. VERB

/ mæp aʊt / (AmE), / mæp aʊt / (BrE)
To plan, organize, or arrange something in detail before starting it.

Examples

The London Universe devoted its centenary issue last December 8 to mapping out various aspects of Catholic progress during the last one hundred years.

Synset Examples

map one's future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
  • Synonym: organize - to arrange systematically

Etymology

From 'map' (to represent or lay out) + 'out' (to make explicit or visible).
