take aback

1. VERB

/ teɪk əˈbæk / (AmE), / teɪk əˈbæk / (BrE)
To surprise or shock greatly; to cause (someone) to be very surprised.

Examples

He was taken aback.

Synset Examples

I was floored when I heard that I was promoted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: amaze - to fill with great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: shock - to cause someone to feel surprised and upset
