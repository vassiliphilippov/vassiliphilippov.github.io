pull off

1. VERB

/ pʊl ɔf / (AmE), / pʊl ɒf / (BrE)
To remove something by pulling it sharply or with force.

Examples

They had stripped him of his musket and equipment, and now they were pulling his boots and jacket off.
"You know what she likes, Winston ", he had said wearily, one evening in November when Winston was pulling off his overshoes.

Synset Examples

pluck the flowers off the bush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take off - to remove something, especially clothing

2. VERB

/ pʊl ɔf / (AmE), / pʊl ɒf / (BrE)
to cause (someone or something) to withdraw from a duty, job, or activity before completion

Examples

The announcement that the city would sue for recovery on the performance bond was made by City Solicitor David Berger at a press conference following a meeting in the morning with Wagner and other officials of the city and the PTC as well as representatives of an engineering firm that was pulled off the El project before its completion in 1959.

Synset Examples

We pulled this firm off the project because they overcharged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdraw - to remove or take away from a position or involvement

3. VERB

/ pʊl ɔf / (AmE), / pʊl ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by pulling it away from a surface or from where it is attached

Examples

I pulled them **off**.

Synset Examples

She placed the tray down and drew off the cloth
draw away the cloth that is covering the cheese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take off - to remove something, especially clothing, from the body
