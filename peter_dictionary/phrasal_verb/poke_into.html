poke into

1. VERB

/ poʊk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / pəʊk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter or go into a place briefly or casually, often out of curiosity

Examples

Sailing activity is slowed down by Texas northers, but power cruisers can move freely, poking into the San Jacinto, Trinity and Brazos rivers (fine tarpon fishing in the Brazos) or pushing eastward to the pirate country of Barataria.

Synset Examples

We poked into the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look in - to enter a place briefly, usually to see someone or something
