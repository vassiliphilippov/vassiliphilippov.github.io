hem in

1. VERB

/ hɛm ɪn / (AmE), / hɛm ɪn / (BrE)
to surround completely or restrict the movement, freedom, or action of someone or something

Examples

Hemmed in on the job and unable to assert himself, he uses the sex act so he can be supreme in at least one area.

Synset Examples

The Turks besieged Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: besiege - to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force surrender
  • Synonym: enclose - to surround on all sides

Etymology

From the verb 'hem', meaning to enclose or border, + 'in' as a particle indicating into an enclosed space.

2. VERB

/ hɛm ɪn / (AmE), / hɛm ɪn / (BrE)
to surround someone or something closely, especially so that movement is restricted

Examples

Instead of the usual straggling privet hedges and patches of bare dirt in most small-town squares, the building was hemmed in by a semitropical growth of camellias and azaleas and a smooth lawn the improbably bright-green shade of florist's grass.

Synset Examples

The building was hemmed in by flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclose - to surround on all sides; to shut in
  • Synonym: fence in - to surround with or as if with a fence
  • Antonym: free - to release from confinement or restriction

Etymology

From the verb 'hem' meaning to enclose or border, combined with 'in'.
