shut down

1. VERB

/ ʃʌt daʊn / (AmE), / ʃʌt daʊn / (BrE)
(of a machine, business, or system) to cease operating or to cause something to stop operating.

Examples

Spread your vacation period over the widest possible span of time or shut the plant down for two weeks.
An oversize unit will cool off your house quickly, then shut down for a long period.

Synset Examples

The owners decided to move and to close the factory
My business closes every night at 8 P.M.
close up the shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close down - to stop operating, especially permanently
  • Antonym: open up - to begin operating or become active
