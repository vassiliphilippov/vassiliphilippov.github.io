lead on

1. VERB

/ lid ɑn / (AmE), / liːd ɒn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to believe or do something, especially by giving them false hope or by enticing them when it is unwise or misleading

Examples

Religion at its best is out in front, ever beckoning and leading on, and, as Lippman put it, "mobilizing all man's scattered energies in one triumphant sense of his own infinite importance ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
