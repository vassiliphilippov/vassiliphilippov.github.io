while away

1. VERB

/ waɪl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / waɪl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to spend time in a relaxed or leisurely way, often to make a long period seem shorter or less tedious

Examples

She put the violin away and took out some linen, needles and yarn to while away the long, idle days in Budapest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass the time - to do something to make time seem to go by more quickly

Etymology

From obsolete sense of 'while' meaning 'to cause (time) to pass', traced to Old English *hwīl* ('period of time').
