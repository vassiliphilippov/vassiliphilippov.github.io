go forward

1. VERB

/ goʊ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move ahead or advance; to continue progress in time, space, or a process

Examples

Capital imports drawn from a number of sources must be employed and combined skillfully enough to permit domestic investment programming to go forward.
The old man beckoned with one finger and Clayton went forward to him.

Synset Examples

We proceeded towards Washington
She continued in the direction of the hills
We are moving ahead in time now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proceed - to continue or go forward, especially after a pause
  • Synonym: advance - to move forward, especially in position or progress
  • Synonym: move forward - to move or progress ahead
  • Antonym: fall back - to retreat or move backwards (opposite of go forward)
