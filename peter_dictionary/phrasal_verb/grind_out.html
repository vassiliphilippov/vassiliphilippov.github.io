grind out

1. VERB

/ ɡraɪnd aʊt / (AmE), / ɡraɪnd aʊt / (BrE)
to produce something, especially work or written material, in a dull, mechanical, or monotonous way, often with little inspiration or enthusiasm

Examples

The American Medical Association is once again grinding out its tear-soaked propaganda based upon the high cost of the Veterans Administration medical program to the American taxpayer.

Synset Examples

We have to crank out publications in order to receive funding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crank out - to produce something quickly and mechanically
