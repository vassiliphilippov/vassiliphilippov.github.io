allow in

1. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ ɪn / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to permit someone or something to enter a place

Examples

Extraordinary precautions were taken so that no stranger be allowed in the city and no citizen within the enclosure surrounding the scaffold.
It is there and it must be allowed in ".

Synset Examples

We cannot admit non-members into our club building
This pipe admits air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admit - to allow to enter; grant entry to
  • Synonym: let in - to allow to go or come into a place
  • Antonym: exclude - to deny entry to; keep out
