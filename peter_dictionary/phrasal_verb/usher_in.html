usher in

1. VERB

/ ˈʌʃər ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə ɪn / (BrE)
to mark the beginning of something new; to cause or introduce the start of a period, event, or development

Examples

The attempt to enforce that belief ushered in a reign of bootleggers, racketeers, hijackers and gangsters that led to a breakdown of law unparalleled in our history.

Synset Examples

The fall of the Berlin Wall ushered in the post-Cold War period

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduce - to bring something into use or operation for the first time

Etymology

From 'usher' (noun/verb meaning 'to lead or conduct'), influenced by the sense of leading something in.
