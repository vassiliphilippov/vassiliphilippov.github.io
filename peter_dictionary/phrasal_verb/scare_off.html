scare off

1. VERB

/ skɛr ɔf / (AmE), / skeə ɒf / (BrE)
To make someone or something go away or avoid something by frightening them.

Examples

The dog refused to be scared off, so Kahler had purchased some small firecrackers.
Starlings and blackbirds are scared off by cannon, from City Hall.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone into doing or not doing something
  • Synonym: daunt - to make someone feel intimidated or discouraged
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone
