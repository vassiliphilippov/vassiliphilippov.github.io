crowd together

1. VERB

/ kraʊd təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / kraʊd tʊˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to gather closely in large numbers, often in a confined space

Examples

Little boys crowded together on long wooden benches, and in the center of the room sat the teacher.

Synset Examples

men in straw boaters and waxed mustaches crowded the verandah

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluster - to come together in a small group
  • Antonym: scatter - to move or distribute apart in different directions
