knuckle under

1. VERB

/ ˈnʌkəl ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈnʌkəl ˈʌndə / (BrE)
To submit or yield to pressure or authority, especially unwillingly.

Examples

An aggressive woman wants a man to demand, not knuckle under.
How could he exert authority over them - make them toe the line, as he had to - if he knuckled under to this small-town clown?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: give in - to cease to resist; submit
  • Antonym: stand firm - to refuse to yield or submit, especially under pressure

Etymology

Originally American English, from the literal sense of putting a knuckle to the ground in submission (19th century).
