catch on

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / kætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand something, often after initial difficulty or confusion

Examples

They were clumsy, but they were beginning to catch on.
They also caught on a little bit on how to smile a lot without your lips trembling.
Moreover, the larger and more aggressive mass distribution outlets and chain stores have insisted on high quality - and the customer seems to have caught on.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasp - to understand something fully
  • Synonym: get it - to understand or comprehend
  • Antonym: misunderstand - fail to understand or interpret correctly

2. VERB

/ kætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / kætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
(of an idea, style, or activity) to become popular or fashionable.

Examples

But because it was a suspense gangster story of the Capone era, many of us felt that it might catch on for a run in Chicago, continue as a road company, and eventually become a movie.

Synset Examples

This fashion caught on in Paris

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take off - to suddenly become popular or successful

3. VERB

/ kætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / kætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something, often after some initial difficulty or hesitation

Examples

If they ever catch on, though, he's gonna wind up in here wearing a number himself.

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get it - to understand something
  • Synonym: grasp - to understand something fully
  • Synonym: catch - to understand (informal sense)
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to understand
