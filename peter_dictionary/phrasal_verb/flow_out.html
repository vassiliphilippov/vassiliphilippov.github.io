flow out

1. VERB

/ floʊ aʊt / (AmE), / fləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, gas, or substance) to move or pour out from a container, opening, or confined space.

Examples

Such liquids will rise to a considerable height above the surface around the chamber before they will flow out of the chamber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to cause liquid to accidentally flow out of a container
  • Synonym: leak - to escape or pass out through a hole or crack
