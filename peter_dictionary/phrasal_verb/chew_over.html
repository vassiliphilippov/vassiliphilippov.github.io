chew over

1. VERB

/ tʃuː ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / tʃuː ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to think carefully or at length about something; to consider or reflect on a subject deeply.

Examples

Chewing it over with his colleagues and in his own mind, he reaches a tentative identification of the question - tentative because it may change as he explores it further and because, if no tolerable answer can be found, it may have to be changed into one which can be answered.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponder - to think about something carefully before making a decision or reaching a conclusion
  • Synonym: mull over - to think about something deeply and at length
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention or respect to; ignore

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'chew' (to grind with the teeth) for mentally working over a subject, from Middle English 'chewen', Old English 'ceowan'.
