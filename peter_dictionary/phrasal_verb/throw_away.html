throw away

1. VERB

/ θɹoʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / θrəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to dispose of something as useless or unwanted; to discard

Examples

He ran for the sick room, found his pistol was broken, and threw it away.
"This is the sort of stuff I write and then throw away "!
Furthermore - and this, to me, strikes an especially warming note - it shall avail the vandals naught to throw away or dispose of their loot.
The North Koreans threw away their guns and fled across the rice paddies.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful or desirable
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

2. VERB

/ θroʊ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / θrəʊ əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To dispose of something as unwanted or useless; to get rid of by discarding.

Examples

She threw it all **away** just to make me look ridiculous!

Synset Examples

he shed his image as a pushy boss
shed your clothes
