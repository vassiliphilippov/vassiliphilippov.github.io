square up

1. VERB

/ skwɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skweə ʌp / (BrE)
to adjust or shape something so that it has square angles or straight edges; to make something square

Examples

"When working from one of my sketches I square it up and project its linear form freehand to the watercolor sheet with charcoal.
Draw file the edges, square up the ends and put a slight chamfer on the edges so they will not snag in the frame.

Synset Examples

Square the circle
square the wood with a file

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straighten - make or become straight

2. VERB

/ skwɛr ʌp / (AmE), / skwɛə ʌp / (BrE)
to settle a dispute or account; to resolve a matter conclusively

Examples

I'll square it **with** Mom, that you're not seeing her before you leave and...

Synset Examples

We finally settled the argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle - bring to an end, resolve (a dispute or account)

Etymology

From 'square' meaning to make even or settle accounts, plus the particle 'up' indicating completion.
