leech onto

1. VERB

/ liːtʃ ˈɑn.toʊ / (AmE), / liːtʃ ˈɒn.təʊ / (BrE)
To cling to or persistently follow someone, often seeking their attention or advantage, in a way that is considered dependent or exploitative.

Examples

I'm onto Batman.

Synset Examples

the groupies leeched onto the rock star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cling to - hold on tightly or stay very close to someone
  • Synonym: attach oneself to - to become closely connected with someone, often undesirably
  • Antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone

Etymology

From 'leech', originally an Old English word for the bloodsucking worm, used metaphorically here; 'onto' as the preposition.
