fool around

1. VERB

/ ful əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or physically noisy way, often instead of doing something more serious; to engage in horseplay

Examples

"I tell you, you want to leave it that way, I do n't fool around with it.
"Too much fooling around ", he said.

Synset Examples

Enough horsing around--let's get back to work!
The bored children were fooling about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse around - engage in boisterous or noisy play

2. VERB

/ ful əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a silly or playful way, often wasting time or not being serious

Examples

- Don't fool around.

Synset Examples

Enough horsing around--let's get back to work!
The bored children were fooling about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse around - to play or fool about in a noisy, mischievous way

3. VERB

/ ful əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or aimless way, often wasting time or not being serious

Examples

- Don't fool around.

Synset Examples

Enough horsing around--let's get back to work!
The bored children were fooling about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse around - to engage in playful or boisterous activity
  • Synonym: mess around - to spend time doing things that are not useful or serious
