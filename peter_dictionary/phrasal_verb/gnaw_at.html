gnaw at

1. VERB

/ nɔ æt / (AmE), / nɔː ət / (BrE)
(of a worry, feeling, etc.) to trouble or distress someone persistently over a period of time.

Examples

Day by day, week by week, month by month, the betrayal gnawed at Andrei's heart.

Synset Examples

Her confidence eroded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prey on - to cause persistent distress or worry (prey on someone's mind)
