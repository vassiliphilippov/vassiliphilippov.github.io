lay off

1. VERB

/ leɪ ˈɔf / (AmE), / leɪ ˈɒf / (BrE)
To stop doing something, especially for a period of time; to cease an activity or behavior.

Examples

Blistered for curbs and laid off three weeks, he is coming along fine and looks like a pacer to me.
The last-named is a fine-looking, large colt, who has been unfortunate to be laid off for some time due to injuries.
"Why do n't you bastards lay off for a while "?

Synset Examples

Quit teasing your little brother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quit - to stop doing something, especially permanently
  • Synonym: cease - to stop or bring to an end
  • Antonym: resume - to begin again or continue after a pause

2. VERB

/ leɪ ɔf / (AmE), / leɪ ɒf / (BrE)
To dismiss an employee from their job, usually temporarily and for economic reasons such as lack of work.

Examples

Worry had a great deal to do with it; Stuart had been laid off at the produce company and had to go back to sitting in his father's office, taking what salary his father could hand out to him.

Synset Examples

She was laid off together with hundreds of other workers when the company downsized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from their job
  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss from employment, especially as a punishment
  • Antonym: hire - to give someone a job; to employ
