deck out

1. VERB

/ dɛk aʊt / (AmE), / dɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to dress or decorate (someone or something) with special or elaborate clothes or adornments, often for a particular occasion or to enhance appearance

Examples

If one of Mr. Rodgers' melodies seemed to deserve a better fate than interment in Boston or the obscurity of a Broadway failure, Mr. Hart was likely to deck it out with new lyrics to give it a second chance in another show.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress up - to put on special or formal clothes

Etymology

Based on the noun 'deck', related to covering or decorating, with nautical origins in 'decking' (covering) a ship.
