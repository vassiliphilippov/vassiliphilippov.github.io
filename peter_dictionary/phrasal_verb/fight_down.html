fight down

1. VERB

/ faɪt daʊn / (AmE), / faɪt daʊn / (BrE)
to suppress or control (a feeling, urge, or impulse) with effort; to resist strongly and prevent from being expressed or acted upon.

Examples

He fought down the impulse to rush in and collar the vicious puncher on the spot.

Synset Examples

The senator said he would oppose the bill
Don't fight it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to forcibly put an end to (e.g., a feeling or action)
  • Antonym: give in - to cease fighting or resisting; yield
