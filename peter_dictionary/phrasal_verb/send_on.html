send on

1. VERB

/ sɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / sɛnd ɒn / (BrE)
to forward or dispatch something or someone to a further destination, often from an intermediate point in a journey or process

Examples

He would send on by trusted messenger the dispatches with their electrifying news.
By fall, 443 survivors of this arduous journey were clustered about Fort Snelling, but most of them were sent on to Galena and St. Louis, with a few going as far as Vevay, Indiana, a notable Swiss center in the United States.

Synset Examples

forward my mail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - to send (goods, mail, etc.) onward to another destination

2. VERB

/ sɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / sɛnd ɒn / (BrE)
to cause something, such as mail or a message, to be sent or delivered to a new address or onward destination, especially after reaching an intermediate location

Examples

You want anyone sent over here?

Synset Examples

forward my mail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - to send something onward to another location
