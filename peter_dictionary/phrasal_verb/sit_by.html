sit by

1. VERB

/ sɪt baɪ / (AmE), / sɪt baɪ / (BrE)
To remain inactive or indifferent while something happens, especially when action or intervention might be expected.

Examples

In fact it has caused us to give serious thought to moving our residence south, because it is not easy for the most objective Southerner to sit calmly by when his host is telling a roomful of people that the only way to deal with Southerners who oppose integration is to send in troops and shoot the bastards down.

Synset Examples

Don't just sit by while your rights are violated!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand by - to be present but take no action; remain uninvolved
  • Antonym: intervene - to become actively involved or take action, especially to change a situation
