make out

1. VERB

/ meɪk aʊt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to manage to see, hear, or understand something or someone, often with difficulty

Examples

He could n't make out what he was saying.
He could not make out, but he knew that again she was scratching her nose.
One of the drawing-room shutters was partly open and he made out the shapes of chairs and sofas, which seemed to be upholstered in brown or russet velvet.
He heard cries from behind him, but he could make out no words.
The night was so clear that Richardson had no difficulty making out the silhouette.

Synset Examples

The fleeing convicts were picked out of the darkness by the watchful prison guards
I can't make out the faces in this photograph

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discern - to perceive or recognize something clearly
  • Synonym: perceive - to become aware of or identify by means of the senses
  • Antonym: miss - fail to notice, hear, or understand

2. VERB

/ meɪk aʊt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to manage to see, hear, or understand something with difficulty

Examples

I could n't make out what his racket was.
The figures on the worksheet paper in front of her were jumping and waving around so badly it was all she could do to make them out clearly enough to copy them with the typewriter.

Synset Examples

I cannot make out what this politician is saying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discern - to recognize or identify as separate and distinct
  • Synonym: perceive - to become aware of through the senses
  • Synonym: grasp - to comprehend fully

3. VERB

/ meɪk aʊt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to manage or fare in a particular situation; to proceed, get along, or achieve a result in a specified context

Examples

In your sales force, will a smaller number of higher-priced, high quality salesmen serve you best, or can you make out better with a larger number of lower paid salesmen?

Synset Examples

How is she doing in her new job?
How are you making out in graduate school?
He's come a long way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get by - to manage with difficulty to survive or accomplish something
  • Synonym: cope - to deal effectively with a challenging situation
  • Degree Lower: struggle - to try very hard to do something difficult
  • Degree Higher: thrive - to prosper or do very well

4. VERB

/ meɪk aʊt / (AmE), / meɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to write and issue (a check, ticket, or other document) in a legally valid form

Examples

But Mercer's explanation was simple: "I made out the check and carried it around a few days unsigned - in case I lost it ".
When he remembered that he might have not signed the check, Mercer made out another for the same amount, instructing the bank to destroy the other - especially if he had happened to have absent-mindedly signed both of them.

Synset Examples

write out a check
cut a ticket
Please make the check out to me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: write out - to write in full (a document, such as a check or prescription)
