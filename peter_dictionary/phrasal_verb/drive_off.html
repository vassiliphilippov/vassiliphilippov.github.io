drive off

1. VERB

/ draɪv ɑf / (AmE), / draɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave, especially by deliberate action; to chase away

Examples

The music drove them off, or away, and he was free to walk on air in a very few moments, humming and jiving within, beating the rhythm within.
He drove sensual patterns off, carefully shaving his long upper lip.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase away - to force someone or something to leave by pursuing or scaring them off
  • Synonym: dispel - to make something, especially a feeling or belief, disappear
