hold down

1. VERB

/ hoʊld daʊn / (AmE), / həʊld daʊn / (BrE)
To keep or maintain a job, position, or responsibility, especially for a period of time.

Examples

Vitamins, eggs and milk begin to look like foods to hold down on (though mothers' milk is still the ticket).

Synset Examples

She manages to hold down two jobs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep - to continue to have or hold something
  • Antonym: lose - to fail to keep or maintain something
