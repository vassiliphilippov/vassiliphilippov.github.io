feed back

1. VERB

/ fid ˈbæk / (AmE), / fiːd ˈbæk / (BrE)
to return information or data to a program, system, or mechanism, often to influence future operation or control process

Examples

Gas injection through a porous anode (transpiration cooling) not only feeds back the energy transferred to the anode by the above mentioned processes, but also modifies the conditions in the arc itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feedback - information returned to a system for control or modification
