keep down

1. VERB

/ kip daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp daʊn / (BrE)
to prevent the level, amount, or number of something from increasing; to maintain at a low level

Examples

You can keep your machinery investment down by buying good secondhand machinery, by sharing the cost and upkeep of machinery with a neighbor, and by hiring someone with machinery to do certain jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduce - to make smaller or less in amount or size
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

2. VERB

/ kip daʊn / (AmE), / kiːp daʊn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from rising, developing, or succeeding, especially by using force or intimidation

Examples

Keep it **down**.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - forcibly put an end to something; to subdue
  • Synonym: repress - restrain or prevent the progress, expression, or development of something
  • Antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to (someone)
