give birth

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv bɝθ / (AmE), / ɡɪv bɜːθ / (BrE)
to produce a baby or young animal from the body; to bring forth offspring

Examples

Other herpetologists have ascertained that in the northern United States the prairie rattlesnake may not give first birth until it is four or even five years old, and that the young may be born every other year, rather than annually.
The female from Central America which gave birth to four was only 3 feet 11 inches long.

Synset Examples

My wife had twins yesterday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear - to give birth to (used formally or in passive)
  • Synonym: deliver - to give birth; specifically referring to the act of a woman giving birth to a child

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv ˈbɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈbɜːθ / (BrE)
to originate, create, or bring about (something such as an idea, movement, or project)

Examples

In both cases the stubborn fact remains: liberalism gave birth to two brilliant apostates, both legitimate offspring of its loins, and when brought to the test, it behaved shabbily.

Synset Examples

Marx and Engels gave birth to communism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: originate - to begin or give rise to something
  • Synonym: spawn - to produce or generate, often used figuratively

3. VERB

/ ɡɪv bɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɪv bɜːθ / (BrE)
to produce a baby or young animal from the body; to bring a child or offspring into the world.

Examples

I wouldn'’t bring another one of your sons **into this world**!

Synset Examples

My wife had twins yesterday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliver - to assist in the birth of a child
  • Antonym: be born - to come into existence as a baby or young
