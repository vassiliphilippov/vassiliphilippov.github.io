invite in

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvaɪt ɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪt ɪn / (BrE)
to ask someone to enter a place, especially your home or a room

Examples

Should I accompany her to the door of her home, or should I ask to be invited in?
She had invited him in for coffee.

Synset Examples

We invited the neighbors in for a cup of coffee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: let in - to allow someone to enter a place
