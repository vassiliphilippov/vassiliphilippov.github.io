go past

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ pæst / (AmE), / ɡəʊ pɑːst / (BrE)
to move beyond or pass someone or something while continuing in the same direction

Examples

Many high school students go past my house every day, and they look like perfect ladies and gentlemen.
I got a quick look at their faces as we went past.

Synset Examples

A black limousine passed by when she looked out the window
He passed his professor in the hall
One line of soldiers surpassed the other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass by - to go past a person or place
  • Synonym: overtake - to catch up with and move in front of
