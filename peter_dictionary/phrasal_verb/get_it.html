get it

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ɪt / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪt / (BrE)
to understand or comprehend something, especially after difficulty or confusion

Examples

These are mentalities which crave action - and they are beginning to get it, as Messrs. Salsich and Engh report on page 372.
"I got it, Andy.
Dazed, Phil said: "I do n't get it.
"I do n't get it ".

Synset Examples

She didn't know what her classmates were plotting but finally caught on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch on - to begin to understand or realize something
  • Synonym: grasp - to understand something, especially complicated
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to understand something

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɛt ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɛt ɪt / (BrE)
(informal) to be punished or scolded for something that one has done wrong.

Examples

God knows how, but I got it.

Synset Examples

You are going to get it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch it - be punished or scolded
