get down

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt daʊn / (AmE), / ɡɛt daʊn / (BrE)
To swallow or manage to swallow (food or drink), especially with difficulty.

Examples

Try to get that down ".

Synset Examples

Swallow the raw fish--it won't kill you!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - to cause food or drink to pass down the throat to the stomach

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt daʊn / (AmE), / ɡɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to dismount or alight from a horse, bicycle, or similar vehicle

Examples

Hogan got down from the saddle and had a look inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismount - to get off or down from a horse, bicycle, or similar

3. VERB

/ ɡɛt daʊn / (AmE), / ɡɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to lower yourself by bending your legs, especially so your knees are close to the ground or in a kneeling or crouching position

Examples

Would he have to clean up after her every day, clean the kitchen, the bathroom, and get down on his knees and scrub the kitchen floor, then hang up her dresses, pick up her stockings, make the bed while she lay around?
Had it done so, the blot on its escutcheon would have remained indelible, nor could the Harvard Divinity School assemble today to honor Parker's insurgence other than by getting down on its collective knees and crying "peccavi ".

Synset Examples

Get down on your knees!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kneel - to be or move into a position where your body is supported by your knees
  • Antonym: stand up - to rise to a standing position from sitting or lying
