call in

1. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to summon someone, especially an expert or professional, to carry out a particular job or activity

Examples

The Ifni crisis in the fall of 1957 postponed further consideration of elections, but French consultants were called in and notices of further investigation appeared from time to time.
Artillery and air strikes were called in to kill them by the hundreds.

Synset Examples

Experts were called in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summon - to officially order someone to come

2. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(in sports) to take a player out of a game in order to substitute another player.

Examples

He called in the pitcher who had been pitching, and a big, heavy, powerfully built right hander moved out to the mound for Anniston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - replace one player with another during a game

3. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to summon someone to enter a room or place, especially for a meeting, appointment, or consultation

Examples

If it was designed to put me on the spot, it would have to have been written before Peralta ever called me in on the case ".
I think her husband strongly suspects so, and that's why he called me in on the thing in direct defiance of his confederates and almost certainly without telling them why he was doing so.

Synset Examples

The nurse called in the next patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invite in - to ask someone to come into a place

4. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to contact a person, organization, or radio station by telephone, especially to provide information, ask a question, or participate in a discussion

Examples

If I could call in, they could check the story while we were on our way.

Synset Examples

call in to a radio station
call in sick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone in - to make a telephone call to a place, especially to contribute to a radio or TV program

5. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
To make a brief visit to a person or place, especially unexpectedly or informally.

Examples

Still she would probably have sense enough not to call in the local sheriff to find her boy friend who, apparently, had run away.

Synset Examples

The mayor likes to call on some of the prominent citizens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop by - to visit someone casually and unexpectedly
  • Synonym: call on - to pay a short visit, especially for a particular purpose

6. VERB

/ kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to make a phone call to a place, especially to report something or give information, such as telling your employer that you will be absent from work

Examples

Paulie called in sick this morning.

Synset Examples

call in to a radio station
call in sick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone in - to make a telephone call to a radio or TV program
