spit out

1. VERB

/ spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
To expel something from the mouth in a forceful or sudden manner.

Examples

The din was successful, too, for just before the moon disappeared, the frightened toad had begun to spit it out again, which meant good luck all around.
Frozen, they stared at it whirling down the valley, gouging and spitting out boulders and chunks of earth like a starving hound dog cracking marrowbones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spew - to expel something forcefully or violently
  • Antonym: swallow - to cause or allow something to pass down the throat

2. VERB

/ spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to say something quickly and angrily, often with contempt or disgust

Examples

He spent long hours before the TV spitting out promises of revenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - to speak abruptly or angrily
  • Synonym: shout - to say something loudly, often with anger
