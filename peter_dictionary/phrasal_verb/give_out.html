give out

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to emit or release something such as light, heat, a sound, or a smell.

Examples

The events of the last quarter of an hour, mysterious to any bird accustomed only to the predictable life of coop and barnyard, had overcome the doctor's hen and she gave out a series of cackly wails, perhaps mourning her nest, but briefly enjoyed.

Synset Examples

The ozone layer blocks some harmful rays which the sun emits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emit - to send forth or discharge (light, heat, etc.)
  • Synonym: give off - to produce and release (a substance, light, heat, etc.)

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to distribute something to several people

Examples

This procedure is much more effective than giving out a membership packet.

Synset Examples

The teacher handed out the exams

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand out - to distribute something to people
  • Antonym: collect - to gather things from people
