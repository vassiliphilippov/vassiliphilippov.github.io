cascade down

1. VERB

/ kæˈskeɪd daʊn / (AmE), / kæˈskeɪd daʊn / (BrE)
to flow or fall downward in large quantities or in a series, like a waterfall

Examples

One of the most beautiful buildings in Istanbul, it was constructed in the early years of the Seventeenth Century, with a huge central dome, two half domes that seem to cascade down from it, and smaller full domes around the gallery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pour down - to fall heavily in large amounts

Etymology

From 'cascade' (noun/verb, waterfall) + 'down' (direction). 'Cascade' from French 'cascade' (waterfall), from Italian 'cascata', from 'cascare' (to fall).
