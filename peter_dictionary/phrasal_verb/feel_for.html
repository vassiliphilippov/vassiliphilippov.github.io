feel for

1. VERB

/ fil fɔr / (AmE), / fiːl fə / (BrE)
to have sympathy for someone; to share in their suffering or emotional pain.

Examples

In point of fact, the race-drivers one knows are nearly always intelligent, healthy technicians who differ from other technicians only in the depth of the passion they feel for the work by which they live.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathize - to share or understand the feelings of another
  • Synonym: empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Antonym: be indifferent to - not showing concern or sympathy for someone
