shore up

1. VERB

/ ʃɔr ʌp / (AmE), / ʃɔːr ʌp / (BrE)
to support or strengthen something by placing something solid against it, or, figuratively, to reinforce or bolster an institution, system, or organization.

Examples

But for the United States and its SEATO allies to attempt to shore up a less tough, less combat-tested government army in monsoon-shrouded, road-shy, guerrilla-th' - wisp terrain is a risk not savored by Pentagon planners.
Operating side by side, together they helped shore up the nation-state.

Synset Examples

shore and buttress an old building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prop up - to support by placing something under or against

Etymology

From 'shore' meaning to support (from Middle English 'schore', a prop or support).
