fall in

1. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of a structure or organization) to collapse or cave in, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

He adjusted the hat, lifted the horn to his lips as though it were a flute, and fell in alongside our fingerprint expert to cavort with him.
A few years later the dome fell in.
The roof was about ready to fall in on Diane's little world, but it took nothing less than the Egyptian revolution to bring it down.

Synset Examples

The wall collapsed
The business collapsed
The dam broke
The roof collapsed
The wall gave in
The roof finally gave under the weight of the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - fall down or give way suddenly
  • Synonym: give way - to break or collapse under pressure
  • Synonym: fall down - to drop to the ground from a higher place

2. VERB

/ fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(Of soldiers) to form a line or take one's place in a military formation as commanded.

Examples

Greg rumbled down the rough metal taxi strip, and one by one the seven members of his flight fell in behind him.

Synset Examples

Troops fall in!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fall out - (Of soldiers) to leave a formation or cease being in formation

3. VERB

/ fɑl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of soldiers) to take one's place in a military formation or line, usually in response to a command.

Examples
