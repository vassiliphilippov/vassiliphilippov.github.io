take advantage (of)

1. VERB

/ teɪk ədˈvæntɪdʒ ʌv / (AmE), / teɪk ədˈvɑːntɪdʒ ɒv / (BrE)
to make unfair or excessive use of someone or something for one's own benefit, often to the detriment of others.

Examples

They made her drink whiskey and then they tried to take advantage of her.

Synset Examples

You are taking advantage of my good will!
She is trespassing upon my privacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to use someone or something selfishly or unethically for one's own benefit
