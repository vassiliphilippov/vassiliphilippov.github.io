gloss over

1. VERB

/ ɡlɑs ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡlɒs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to treat or mention something briefly or superficially so as to avoid dealing with its full details or seriousness

Examples

This is an ethical demand which cannot be evaded or glossed over by talking exclusively of weapon superiority or even of the evil of Communism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skirt - to avoid dealing with or discussing (something) directly
  • Synonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • Antonym: address - to deal with or discuss directly
