make off

1. VERB

/ meɪk ˈɔf / (AmE), / meɪk ˈɒf / (BrE)
to leave quickly, especially in order to escape or steal something

Examples

I say we break into the place... ...trash the office... ...and make off with the records and say it was industrial espionage.

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest
  • Synonym: flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
