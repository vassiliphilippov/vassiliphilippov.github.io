slip in

1. VERB

/ slɪp ɪn / (AmE), / slɪp ɪn / (BrE)
To insert or include something casually, unobtrusively, or without attracting attention.

Examples

When she succeeds, she usually manages to slip in unobtrusively, to deposit an egg on a completed loaf of beebread before the bumblebees seal the egg compartment.

Synset Examples

She slipped in a reference to her own work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak in - to enter or include something in a secretive or stealthy way
  • Synonym: slide in - to put or move something into position smoothly or quietly
