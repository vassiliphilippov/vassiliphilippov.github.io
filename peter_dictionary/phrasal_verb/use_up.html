use up

1. VERB

/ juz ˈʌp / (AmE), / juːz ˈʌp / (BrE)
to consume all of something so that none remains; to exhaust a supply or resource

Examples

A flame would use up air.
Such "depletion allowances ", in the form of percentages of sales are authorized by tax law for specified raw materials producers using up their assets.
"Thrifty of her to use it up.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consume - to use up a resource; to eat, drink, or use
  • Synonym: expend - to use up or spend resources
  • Synonym: deplete - to reduce the quantity of something by consuming it
  • Antonym: conserve - to use as little of something as possible to avoid waste
