depend on

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd ɒn / (BrE)
to be determined or influenced by something; to have as a condition or basis

Examples

In the past the club depended largely on Van Brocklin's aerials.
This, of course, depends on the character of the site itself, the previous experience of the investigator, and the number of factors needed to arrive at a good decision.
The amount which may be borrowed from the SBA depends on how much is required to carry out the intended purpose of the loan.
This energy transfer depends on the current, the temperature in the arc column, the anode material, and the conditions in the anode sheath.
The internal energy of an ideal gas depends on temperature only and is independent of pressure or volume.

Synset Examples

The outcomes rides on the results of the election
Your grade will depends on your homework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rely on - to depend confidently; to trust in
  • Synonym: hinge on - to depend entirely on

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd ɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈpend ɒn / (BrE)
to rely on someone or something for support, help, or maintenance

Examples

Enough time passes... you get so you depend on 'em.

Synset Examples

elderly parents often depend on their adult children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rely on - to trust or depend on someone or something
  • Synonym: count on - to rely on someone or something

Etymology

From Latin dependere, meaning 'to hang from' or 'be contingent on', via Old French dependre.
