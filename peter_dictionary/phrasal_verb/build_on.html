build on

1. VERB

/ bɪld ɑn / (AmE), / bɪld ɒn / (BrE)
To use something as a foundation or basis for further progress, development, or argument.

Examples

Department stores were built on dry goods, especially ladies' fashions, and in this area, in the long run, the suburban branches will be hard put to compete against downtown.
"You can n't build on your hit-and-miss five-seventeen ".

Synset Examples

What's this new evidence based on?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: base on - to use as the foundation for something
  • Synonym: depend on - to be determined or influenced by something
