break loose

1. VERB

/ breɪk lus / (AmE), / breɪk luːs / (BrE)
(of something or someone) to suddenly become free and uncontrollable, often resulting in chaos, violence, or noise.

Examples

I am usually filled with an uneasiness that through some unwitting slip all hell may break loose.
But then, at mention of that name, the Amen corner broke loose.

Synset Examples

His anger exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erupt - to burst out or break forth suddenly and violently
  • Synonym: break out - to start suddenly, especially something unwelcome such as trouble or violence

2. VERB

/ breɪk lus / (AmE), / breɪk luːs / (BrE)
to escape or get free from confinement or control

Examples

A bold line of violet broke loose from the high ridge of the mountains, followed by feathers of red that swept the last stars from the sky.

Synset Examples

The convicted murderer escaped from a high security prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place where you are being held or kept

3. VERB

/ breɪk lus / (AmE), / breɪk luːs / (BrE)
To escape from a place or situation where one is confined or restricted, often suddenly or forcefully.

Examples

If you go after Tattaglia, all hell will break loose.

Synset Examples

The convicted murderer escaped from a high security prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get free from a place where you are kept or held

4. VERB

/ breɪk lus / (AmE), / breɪk luːs / (BrE)
to suddenly become uncontrolled or violent; to be unleashed or escape with force or noise

Examples

If you go after Tattaglia, all hell will break loose.

Synset Examples

His anger exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break out - to begin suddenly and unexpectedly, especially of something unwelcome
