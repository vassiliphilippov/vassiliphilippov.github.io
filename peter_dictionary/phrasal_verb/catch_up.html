catch up

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ʌp / (AmE), / katʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to reach the point where you should be after having fallen behind; to bring yourself or your work up to date after a delay

Examples

Thus, the Span of its ossification was shortened and the center's ability to "catch up "in ossification is demonstrated.
No matter how well work is planned, bad weather or unexpected setbacks can cause extra work that must be caught up.

Synset Examples

I caught up on my homework

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fall behind - to fail to keep up with others or with a schedule
