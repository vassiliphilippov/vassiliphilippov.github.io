buck up

1. VERB

/ bʌk ʌp / (AmE), / bʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to become more cheerful, optimistic, or courageous; to encourage oneself or someone else to face difficulty with spirit.

Examples

I only hope my talking to you has helped you a little, anyway, because you need spiritual bucking-up ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take heart - to gain courage or confidence
  • Synonym: cheer up - to become or make someone happier
