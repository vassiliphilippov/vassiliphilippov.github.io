ease up

1. VERB

/ iz ʌp / (AmE), / iːz ʌp / (BrE)
to move slightly in order to make room for someone or something; to shift position to allow passage or access

Examples

A few minutes later, FBI agent Francis Crosby, talking fast, eased up the ramp to the plane, unarmed.

Synset Examples

The park gave way to a supermarket
`Move over,' he told the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move over - to move sideways to make space for someone or something

2. VERB

/ iz ʌp / (AmE), / iːz ʌp / (BrE)
(of something) to become less intense, severe, or strong.

Examples

Then, after emasculation, he was eased up for a couple of weeks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
