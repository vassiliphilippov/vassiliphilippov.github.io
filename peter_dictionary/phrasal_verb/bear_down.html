bear down

1. VERB

/ bɛr daʊn / (AmE), / beə daʊn / (BrE)
(of something heavy) to press down with force; to exert pressure by weighing heavily on something.

Examples

Floating ice bore down from the north and west.

Synset Examples

The snow bore down on the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press down - to push downwards with force
  • Synonym: weigh down - to make something heavier or cause it to sink by adding weight

2. VERB

/ bɛr daʊn / (AmE), / beə daʊn / (BrE)
to move quickly and forcefully towards someone or something, often in a threatening or determined manner

Examples

In the middle of the party Chandler looked up to see four smiling faces bearing down upon him, each beaming above the biggest, greenest shamrock he had ever seen.

Synset Examples

he saw Jess charging at him with a pitchfork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charge - to rush forward or attack suddenly
