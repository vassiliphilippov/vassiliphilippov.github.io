line up

1. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to form or arrange in a straight line or row; to be arranged in a line

Examples

Tanks lined up at the border will be no more helpful.
Only too often, however, you have the feeling that you are sitting in a room with some of the instruments lined up on one wall to your left and others facing them on the wall to your right.
"The county, though, seems more interested in those people who do n't even try, those who sit and draw welfare checks and line up for surplus food ".

Synset Examples

The buildings all line up neatly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - to form a line, especially of people waiting their turn
  • Antonym: scatter - to separate or spread things or people over an area

2. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to form or stand in a line or queue, especially to wait for something

Examples

In the villages people lined up to give us flowers.

Synset Examples

Customers lined up in front of the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queue - to form a line of people waiting for something
  • Degree Lower: wait - to stay in one place until an expected event happens

3. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to obtain or arrange something or someone for a particular purpose or event

Examples

In addition to the tickets to the movies, sporting events and concerts, Mrs. Marr lines up candy and cookies because alcoholics require a lot of sweets to replace the sugar in their system.

Synset Examples

I found this gadget that will serve as a bottle opener
I got hold of these tools to fix our plumbing
The chairman got hold of a secretary on Friday night to type the urgent letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange - to organize or make plans for something
  • Synonym: secure - to obtain something, often with effort

4. VERB

/ laɪn ʌp / (AmE), / laɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to place people or things in a straight line or in parallel order; to arrange so that they are level or aligned.

Examples

At one point late in the day, when Palmer was lining up a 25 - foot putt on the 16 th, a thunderous cheer from the direction of the 18 th green unmistakably announced that Player had birdied the final hole.

Synset Examples

align the car with the curb
align the sheets of paper on the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: align - to place or arrange things in a straight line
  • Synonym: straighten - to make or become straight
