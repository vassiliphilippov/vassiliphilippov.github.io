iron out

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪərn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈaɪən aʊt / (BrE)
to resolve or remove difficulties, differences, or problems, often through discussion or effort

Examples

Some predict the administration will settle down during 1961 and iron out the rough edges which it has had thus far.

Synset Examples

we need to iron out our disagreements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - to settle or find a solution to a problem

Etymology

Figurative use of 'iron' meaning to smooth out wrinkles, from ironing clothes.
