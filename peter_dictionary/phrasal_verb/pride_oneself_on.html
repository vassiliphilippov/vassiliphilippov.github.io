pride oneself on

1. VERB

/ ˈpraɪd wʌnˈsɛlf ɑn / (AmE), / ˈpraɪd wʌnˈsɛlf ɒn / (BrE)
to feel pleased and satisfied about something that you or someone you are associated with has done or achieved

Examples

There have even been serious errors in the U. S. Forest Service, whose officials pride themselves in their scientific training, he added.
It contained, for example, a number of curious admissions about the peasants, who enjoy no sickness benefits, no old-age pensions, no paid holidays; they still benefit far less than the "other "50 per cent of the nation from that "welfare state "which the Soviet Union so greatly prides itself on being.

Synset Examples

He prides himself on making it into law school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements
  • Antonym: humble oneself - to act modestly or lower one's pride

Etymology

From the noun 'pride', from Old French 'pride', from Latin 'proditum' (meaning proud). The reflexive verb use dates from the early 18th century.
