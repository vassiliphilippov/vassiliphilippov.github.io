snap at

1. VERB

/ snæp æt / (AmE), / snæp ət / (BrE)
to try to bite something quickly and suddenly; to bite at with a sudden movement

Examples

One wrote: "[ I am so hungry ] I could eat a rider off his horse + snap at the stirups ".

Synset Examples

The dog snapped off a piece of cloth from the intruder's pants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite at - to attempt to bite something
