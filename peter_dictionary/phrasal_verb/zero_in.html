zero in

1. VERB

/ ˈzɪəroʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈzɪərəʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to direct attention, aim, or focus precisely on a particular point or target, especially using navigational aids or guidance.

Examples

But now, for the first time since Lou Gehrig (with 47 home runs) spurred Ruth on in 1927, two men playing for the same team have zeroed in on 60.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aim - to direct efforts or attention towards a target
  • Synonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort on something

Etymology

From 'zero (in on),' alluding to adjusting an instrument or aim so that the target aligns with the 'zero' or center point; mid-20th century American English.
