cut back

1. VERB

/ kʌt bæk / (AmE), / kʌt bæk / (BrE)
(in film, television, or storytelling) to return to a previous point in time within the narrative, often by means of an abrupt shift or transition.

Examples

As the robbers leave the looted train, the film suddenly cuts back to the station, where the telegrapher's little daughter arrives with her father's dinner pail only to find him bound on the floor.

Synset Examples

the film cut back to an earlier event in the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash back - move to a previous event in a narrative

2. VERB

/ kʌt bæk / (AmE), / kʌt bæk / (BrE)
To reduce the amount or quantity of something, especially in order to save money or resources.

Examples

The prospect of cutting back spending is an unpleasant one for any governor.

Synset Examples

reduce your daily fat intake
The employer wants to cut back health benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduce - make smaller in amount or degree
