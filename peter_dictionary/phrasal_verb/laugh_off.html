laugh off

1. VERB

/ læf ɑf / (AmE), / lɑːf ɒf / (BrE)
To dismiss or treat a problem, criticism, or embarrassment as unimportant by making a joke or pretending to be amused by it.

Examples

He was laughed off the screen.

Synset Examples

She laughs away all these problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush off - to dismiss someone or something as unimportant
  • Synonym: shrug off - to treat something as unimportant or not serious
