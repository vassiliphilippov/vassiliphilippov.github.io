fall out

1. VERB

/ fɔl aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
(of two or more people) to have an argument or disagreement that damages a relationship.

Examples

Fourteen of the sculptures you took posses properties of a most curious and terrifying nature, as you will observe when your limbs begin to wither and your hair falls out in patches.

Synset Examples

We fell out over a trivial question

Related Terms

  • Antonym: make up - to become friends again after a quarrel
