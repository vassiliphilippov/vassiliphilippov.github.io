ride off

1. VERB

/ raɪd ɔf / (AmE), / raɪd ɒf / (BrE)
to leave a place riding a horse or another vehicle, especially so as to go away from someone or something

Examples

When Marsh called to his aide and the pair rode off down the River Road where the gentians burned blue, Juanita was shaken and trying not to cry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ride away - to go away from a place riding a horse or vehicle
