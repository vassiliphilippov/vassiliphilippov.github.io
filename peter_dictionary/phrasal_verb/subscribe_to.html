subscribe to

1. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb tu / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb tə / (BrE)
to arrange to receive or obtain something, such as a publication or service, regularly, usually by paying for it in advance

Examples

Additionally, on the many ships at sea and in the smaller naval stations, the availability of the Journal removed the necessity of subscribing to several additional journals of civilian origin over and above the quantity now authorized, in order to provide any reasonably comparable coverage.

Synset Examples

We take the Times every day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsubscribe from - to cancel a subscription to a service or publication

Etymology

From Latin 'subscribere' (to write underneath or sign), with modern sense influenced by newspaper and journal publishing.
