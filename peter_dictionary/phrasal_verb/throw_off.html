throw off

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ɔf / (AmE), / θrəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of something quickly, such as clothing or a feeling; to cast aside

Examples

He threw off the light blanket and swung his feet off the bunk, to the floor.

Synset Examples

he shed his image as a pushy boss
shed your clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to get rid of something, especially a feeling, quality, or attitude that is no longer wanted
  • Synonym: take off - to remove something, especially an item of clothing
