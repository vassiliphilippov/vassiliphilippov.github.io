plug in

1. VERB

/ plʌɡ ɪn / (AmE), / plʌɡ ɪn / (BrE)
to connect an electrical device to a power source by inserting its plug into a socket or outlet.

Examples

The unit consists of a small motor that goes on as soon as it is plugged in.
With a short-lived sigh of relief, Jack plugged the fuses back in and threw the switch.

Synset Examples

Please plug in the toaster!
Connect the TV so we can watch the football game tonight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplug - to disconnect an electrical device from a power source
