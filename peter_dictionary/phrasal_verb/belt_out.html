belt out

1. VERB

/ bɛlt aʊt / (AmE), / bɛlt aʊt / (BrE)
to sing (a song or piece of music) loudly and with great force

Examples

And time after time she really belted out her songs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: croon - to sing in a soft, low voice

2. VERB

/ bɛlt aʊt / (AmE), / bɛlt aʊt / (BrE)
to sing a song loudly and with great force

Examples

Elaine Stritch, who sings with a persuasively warm huskiness, belts some life into most of her songs, but the other members of the cast sound as lukewarm as Mr. Coward's songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sing - to produce musical tones with the voice
