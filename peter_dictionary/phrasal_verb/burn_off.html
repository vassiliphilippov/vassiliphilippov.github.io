burn off

1. VERB

/ bɝn ɑf / (AmE), / bɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
to clear land of its vegetation by setting fire to it

Examples

Most of the fingers on his left hand were burned off when he fell asleep with a cigarette.

2. VERB

/ bɝn ɑf / (AmE), / bɜːn ɒf / (BrE)
to use up or remove something, such as energy, calories, or a substance, through heat or physical activity

Examples

The proteins and fats are burned off, and the cholesterol is left behind.

Synset Examples

burn off calories through vigorous exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consume - to use up, especially entirely
