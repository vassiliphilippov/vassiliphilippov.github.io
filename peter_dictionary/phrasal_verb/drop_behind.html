drop behind

1. VERB

/ drɑp bəˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / drɒp bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to fall behind others in position, progress, or pace; to lag or linger behind

Examples

I could get up close to him where there was traffic but had to drop far behind when there was n't traffic.

Synset Examples

But in so many other areas we still are dragging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall behind - to fail to keep pace with others or a schedule
  • Antonym: catch up - to reach the same position or level as others
