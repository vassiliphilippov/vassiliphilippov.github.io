sweep through

1. VERB

/ swip θru / (AmE), / swiːp θruː / (BrE)
to succeed at something very easily or with little effort

Examples

He swept through the music with ease, in a non-sentimental and ultra-efficient manner.

Synset Examples

She sailed through her exams
You will pass with flying colors
She nailed her astrophysics course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeze through - to succeed easily or complete something quickly and with little effort
  • Synonym: sail through - to succeed easily, especially in a test or task
