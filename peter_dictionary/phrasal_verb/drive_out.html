drive out

1. VERB

/ draɪv aʊt / (AmE), / draɪv aʊt / (BrE)
To force someone or something to leave a place, or to make something disappear; used both literally and figuratively.

Examples

"Wherefore also He [ God ] drove him [ man ] out of Paradise, and removed him far from the tree of life, not because He envied him the tree of life, as some venture to assert, but because He pitied him, [ and did not desire ] that he should continue a sinner for ever, nor that the sin which surrounded him should be immortal, and evil interminable and irremediable.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: dispel - to make something (such as a feeling or belief) disappear

2. VERB

/ draɪv aʊt / (AmE), / draɪv aʊt / (BrE)
To force someone or something to leave a place, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

PRESIDENT: I assure you that, although the rebels have mounted an impressive campaign in Las Villas, [TRANSLATOR TRANSLATING] my staff indicates, with assurance, that we'’ll drive them **out** of the city of Santa Clara before the New Year.

Synset Examples

Drive away potential burglars
drive away bad thoughts
dispel doubts
The supermarket had to turn back many disappointed customers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: chase away - to force to leave, often used for people or animals
  • Antonym: welcome - to receive or accept someone willingly
