blaze away

1. VERB

/ bleɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bleɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to perform an acting passage or task with exceptional brilliance and rapidity

Examples

There is a fine second act, as an example, one in which Samuel Groom, as Dillon, has an opportunity to blaze away in one impassioned passage after another.

Synset Examples

Mr. Jones blazed away in one passage after another to loud applause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shine - to perform outstandingly or with distinction
  • Synonym: excel - to be exceptionally good at an activity

2. VERB

/ bleɪz əˈweɪ / (AmE), / bleɪz əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to fire a gun or guns rapidly and repeatedly

Examples

Several police cars, loaded with armed officers, raced alongside, blazing away at the tires of the big jet.

Synset Examples

He blazed away at the men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire away - to begin shooting or to start asking questions energetically (in gunfire sense, a synonym)
