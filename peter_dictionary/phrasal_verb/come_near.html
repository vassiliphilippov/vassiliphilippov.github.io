come near

1. VERB

/ kʌm nɪr / (AmE), / kʌm nɪə / (BrE)
to move closer to someone or something in space, time, or degree; to approach.

Examples

He was promised that no harm would befall him if he would come out, but he cursed and replied that he would shoot any man coming near the door.
Of course I had to give her Eileen's address, but she never came near us.

Synset Examples

We were approaching our destination
They are drawing near
The enemy army came nearer and nearer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come close to something or someone; to move nearer
  • Antonym: withdraw - to move away from something or someone

2. VERB

/ kʌm nɪr / (AmE), / kʌm nɪə / (BrE)
To almost do or experience something; to be close to a particular action or feeling without actually doing it.

Examples

They had cleaned up an old ice box and begun to buy fifty pound blocks of ice in town, as the electric refrigerator came nowhere near providing enough ice for the crowds who ate and drank there.

Synset Examples

She came near to screaming with fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly but not exactly or entirely
  • Synonym: nearly - closely approaching a state or condition
