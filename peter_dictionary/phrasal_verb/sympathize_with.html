sympathize with

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz wɪð / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪz wɪð / (BrE)
to share in the feelings or suffering of another, especially by expressing compassion or understanding for their misfortune

Examples

He sympathized with them on the loss of their old pastor.
It may appear that we were cruel and callous, but no one had time to spend sympathizing with poor Isaac - except the Reverend.
Her friends and professional associates would sympathize with her, not because she had lost a beloved husband, but because she had been married to a man who thought unrealistically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathize with - to understand and share the feelings of another

Etymology

From Greek sympathēs (having a fellow feeling), via Late Latin sympathizare + with.
