warm to

1. VERB

/ wɔrm tə / (AmE), / wɔːm tə / (BrE)
To become more enthusiastic or excited about something or someone, especially after initial hesitation.

Examples

Rector was warming to his over-all strategy by the time he got back to the residential hall.
Yet Andy plowed ahead, mouthing the inconsequential words as if they possessed real meaning, and gradually his listeners warmed to him.

Synset Examples

He warmed to the idea of a trip to Antarctica

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take to - to start liking someone or something
