help out

1. VERB

/ hɛlp aʊt / (AmE), / hɛlp aʊt / (BrE)
To assist someone, especially in a particular situation or when extra effort is needed.

Examples

He made their spreads his headquarters, and he helped out in their roundups.

Synset Examples

Can you help out tonight with the dinner guests?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to give assistance or support to

2. VERB

/ hɛlp aʊt / (AmE), / hɛlp aʊt / (BrE)
to assist someone, especially in a particular situation or by doing part of their work or task.

Examples

And that there was something in it for me if I could help him **out**.

Synset Examples

Can you help out tonight with the dinner guests?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assist - to help someone by doing part of their work or providing support
