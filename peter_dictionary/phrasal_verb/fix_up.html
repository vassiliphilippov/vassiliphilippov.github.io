fix up

1. VERB

/ fɪks ˈʌp / (AmE), / fɪks ˈʌp / (BrE)
to arrange, provide, or find something or someone for someone else; to organize or set up a meeting, date, job, or similar arrangement for someone

Examples

If we manage to keep track of a Bombus queen after she has left her feeding place, we may discover the snug little hideout which she has fixed up for herself when she woke up from her winter sleep.
Before he left town Pat saw to it that I was fixed up with a job.
My man, he won n't be around a little while, he just fixed me up with this stuff they took out of the Elite.
"Well ", he said, "got your man fixed up nice down there.

Synset Examples

I'll fix you up with a nice girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set up - to arrange or organize something for someone else (e.g., a meeting, date, or job)

2. VERB

/ fɪks ʌp / (AmE), / fɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to make arrangements for something or someone; to organize or prepare.

Examples

"If you can fix it up with the undertaker ", returned the politician, "it's all right with me ".
I go over to Castro and get fixed up there.

Synset Examples

Can you arrange a meeting with the President?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrange - to organize or make plans for something
  • Synonym: set up - to organize or prepare something
