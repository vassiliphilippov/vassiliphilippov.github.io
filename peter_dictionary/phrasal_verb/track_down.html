track down

1. VERB

/ træk daʊn / (AmE), / træk daʊn / (BrE)
to search for and find someone or something after a thorough investigation or pursuit

Examples

It was you that tracked it down anyway, Stevens ", he pursued strictly.
She 'd have made a great scientist dedicated to tracking down heredity and environment.
Tracking down Mrs. Calhoun was like trying to catch up with Paul Revere between Lexington and Concord.

Synset Examples

Goering often hunted wild boars in Poland
The dogs are running deer
The Duke hunted in these woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunt - to pursue and try to catch or kill (an animal or person)

Etymology

From 'track' (to follow traces) + 'down' (to completion); usage dates to the early 20th century.
