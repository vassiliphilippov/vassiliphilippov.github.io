cut away

1. VERB

/ kʌt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(in filmmaking or broadcasting) to switch quickly from one scene or subject to another.

Examples

Left in their previous atom-like smallness, they would have cut away too abruptly into depth; and the broad, opaque shapes of pasted paper would have been isolated in such a way as to make them jump out of plane.

Synset Examples

`cut away now!' the director shouted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut to - switch to a different scene or image (film/TV)
