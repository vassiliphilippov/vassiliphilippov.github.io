peter out

1. VERB

/ ˈpiːtər aʊt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə aʊt / (BrE)
to gradually become weaker or come to an end, especially after becoming less and less strong or frequent

Examples

Then the vein had petered out and the whole project had been abandoned.

Synset Examples

The music just petered out--there was no proper ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwindle - to gradually become smaller or less
  • Synonym: fade away - to slowly disappear or become weaker
  • Synonym: taper off - to become gradually smaller, fewer, or less

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from the use of 'peter' meaning to diminish in mining slang (mid-19th century).
