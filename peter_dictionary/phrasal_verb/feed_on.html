feed on

1. VERB

/ fid ɑn / (AmE), / fiːd ɒn / (BrE)
to be nourished, sustained, or supported by something, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

When the larvae hatch, they feed on the beebread, although they also receive extra honey meals from their mother.
The glass may seem trivial but Communist official hooliganism feeds on such incidents unless they are redressed.

Synset Examples

He fed on the great ideas of her mentor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsist on - to maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level, using something as food
  • Synonym: thrive on - to prosper or flourish as a result of something (often adversity or challenge)
