cart off

1. VERB

/ kɑrt ˈɔf / (AmE), / kɑːt ˈɒf / (BrE)
to take something or someone away, especially forcibly or in large quantities, typically using a vehicle

Examples

These 1750 cases were carted off in a one night operation by the O' Banion men, who left in their stead the same number of barrels filled with water.

Synset Examples

They carted off the old furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul away - to remove or transport something away, usually by vehicle

Etymology

From 'cart' (vehicle) + 'off', indicating removal or transport away.
