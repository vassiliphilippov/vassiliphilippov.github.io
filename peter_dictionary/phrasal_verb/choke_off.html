choke off

1. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ɒf / (BrE)
to stop or suppress something from continuing or developing, especially abruptly or forcefully

Examples

Interruptions came largely from the bench, which numerous times rebuked the Attorney General for letting his witnesses run on, though it, too, made no serious effort to choke off the flow.
Armed with the Nixon opinion, the Senate liberals rounded up their slim majority and prepared to choke off debate on the filibuster battle this week.

Synset Examples

He choked down his rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppress - to put an end to forcibly; to prevent something from continuing
  • Synonym: cut off - to stop the supply or movement of something; to interrupt or halt

2. VERB

/ tʃoʊk ɔf / (AmE), / tʃəʊk ɒf / (BrE)
to prevent or block the flow of something, especially by obstructing or constricting a passage

Examples

Or (more commonly, thinks Keys) the deposits themselves get so big that they choke off the artery's flow to the point that an infarct occurs: the heart muscle is suffocated, cells supplied by the artery die, and the heart is permanently, perhaps fatally injured.

Synset Examples

The leaves clog our drains in the Fall
The water pipe is backed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct the movement or progress of something
  • Antonym: unclog - to remove an obstruction; to clear, especially a passage or pipe

Etymology

From the verb 'choke' (to suffocate or block) + 'off,' meaning to separate or interrupt.
