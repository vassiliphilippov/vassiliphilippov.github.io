salt away

1. VERB

/ sɔlt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / sɒlt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to save or store something, especially money, for future use, often in a secretive or cautious manner

Examples

As for his finances, I was never privileged to know exactly how much money Letch had "salted away ".

Synset Examples

store grain for the winter
The bear stores fat for the period of hibernation when he doesn't eat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to put something away for future use
  • Synonym: save - to keep something, especially money, for future use

Etymology

Refers to the old practice of preserving food with salt for later use.
