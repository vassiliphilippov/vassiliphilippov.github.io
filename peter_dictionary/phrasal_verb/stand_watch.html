stand watch

1. VERB

/ stænd wɑtʃ / (AmE), / stænd wɒtʃ / (BrE)
to keep guard or stay on duty to observe and protect a place or people, especially in a military or nautical context

Examples

Since he could n't sleep anyway, he might as well stand their watches for them or write their reports.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keep watch - to stay awake and observe attentively as a form of protection
  • Synonym: stand guard - to be on guard duty; protect by watching
