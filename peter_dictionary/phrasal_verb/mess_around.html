mess around

1. VERB

/ mɛs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / mɛs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to spend time doing things that are not serious or useful; to engage in aimless or unplanned activity, or to interfere with something or someone, often annoyingly

Examples

It is blind, fundamentalist dogmatism to say, "Messing around with the King James version seems to us a perilous sport at best ".
He could n't afford to have anyone mess around with them, he said.

Synset Examples

The old lady is usually mucking about in her little house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool around - to behave in a silly or aimless way; to interfere thoughtlessly
  • Synonym: potter around - to do things or move about without any purpose or plan, especially in a relaxed way
