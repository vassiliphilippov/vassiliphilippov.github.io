make peace

1. VERB

/ meɪk pis / (AmE), / meɪk piːs / (BrE)
to stop fighting or arguing and become friendly again; to end a conflict or hostilities

Examples

She had come to make her peace with the past, and of that past this ancient of the earth was only a kind of shadow.

Synset Examples

The brothers who had been fighting over their inheritance finally made peace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconcile - to restore friendly relations
  • Antonym: make war - to begin or engage in hostilities or conflict

2. VERB

/ meɪk pis / (AmE), / meɪk piːs / (BrE)
to bring about an end to conflict or hostilities; to reconcile parties who have been in disagreement or fighting

Examples

It's up to you to make peace between me and Sonny.

Synset Examples

The brothers who had been fighting over their inheritance finally made peace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconcile - restore friendly relations between
  • Antonym: make war - begin or engage in hostilities
