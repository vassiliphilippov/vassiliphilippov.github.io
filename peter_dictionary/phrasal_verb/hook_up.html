hook up

1. VERB

/ hʊk ʌp / (AmE), / hʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to connect or link equipment, systems, or lines together so they can work as a unit

Examples

A telephone line had been hooked up to connect the ship with the Base exchange.

Synset Examples

hook up the houses to the gas supply line
Hook up the components of the new sound system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connect - to join together so as to provide access or communication
  • Antonym: disconnect - to detach or break the connection
