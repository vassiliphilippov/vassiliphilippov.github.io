drop off

1. VERB

/ drɑp ˈɔf / (AmE), / drɒp ˈɒf / (BrE)
to decrease in amount, quality, or activity; to diminish or decline.

Examples

Vernon was serviceable on the botanical field trips, but he could arrange no schedule with the cooks, and he was glad when the trips dropped off, and the botanists began to motor out by themselves.

Synset Examples

The number of students in this course dropped off after the first test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrease - to become smaller or less
  • Synonym: fall off - to decrease in amount, quality, or intensity
  • Antonym: increase - to become larger or greater in amount

2. VERB

/ drɑp ɔf / (AmE), / drɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to fall asleep, especially unexpectedly or unintentionally

Examples

The girl dropped off to sleep.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nod off - to fall asleep briefly or unintentionally, especially while sitting
  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly or nap
  • Antonym: wake up - to stop sleeping and become awake
