let in

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɪn / (AmE), / lɛt ɪn / (BrE)
To allow someone to be involved in an activity, group, or privilege; to permit entry, participation, or access.

Examples

The equation was simple: wealth brought them happiness, and their united front to the world was their warning that they meant to keep everything they had, let no one in on the secrets.

Synset Examples

admit someone to the profession
She was admitted to the New Jersey Bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admit - allow someone to enter or be part of a group or place

2. VERB

/ lɛt ɪn / (AmE), / lɛt ɪn / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to enter a place

Examples

Why won't you let me **in**?

Synset Examples

We cannot admit non-members into our club building
This pipe admits air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admit - to allow entry; to let in
  • Antonym: keep out - to prevent entry; to not let in
