sit out

1. VERB

/ sɪt aʊt / (AmE), / sɪt aʊt / (BrE)
To not take part in a particular activity, such as a dance, game, or event, often by choice or necessity.

Examples

Perhaps the Pirate who will be the unhappiest over the news that Musial probably will sit out most of the series is Bob Friend, who was beaten by The Man twice last season on dramatic home runs.

Synset Examples

He sat out the game

Related Terms

  • Antonym: participate - to take part in or become involved in an activity

2. VERB

/ sɪt aʊt / (AmE), / sɪt aʊt / (BrE)
To choose not to participate in a particular activity, event, or part of it, such as a dance, game, or match.

Examples

But there are reasons why you must have no part in what is going to happen.

Synset Examples

He sat out the game

Related Terms

  • Antonym: take part - to participate in an activity or event
