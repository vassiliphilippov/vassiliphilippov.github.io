turn around

1. VERB

/ tɝːn əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
To move so as to face in the opposite direction; to suddenly or decisively change position or outlook.

Examples

"He timed it just right and broke through there before the boy (halfback Terry Isaacson) had time to turn around.
I turned around with the percolator in my hand.
"I can n't be underfoot every time those cops turn around!
Summers pulled up short, and turned around.
Now turn around so I can see your face ".

Synset Examples

He turned around to face his opponent
My conscience told me to turn around before I made a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turn back - to return in the direction from which you have come
  • Synonym: face - to turn so as to be opposite

2. VERB

/ tɝn əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / tɜːn əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
To move so that you are facing the opposite direction, either physically or in attitude or approach.

Examples

Turn around and face the Warden.

Synset Examples

He turned around to face his opponent
My conscience told me to turn around before I made a mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face about - to turn so as to face in the opposite direction
  • Synonym: turn back - to reverse direction or movement
