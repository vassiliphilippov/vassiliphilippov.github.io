back out

1. VERB

/ bæk aʊt / (AmE), / bæk aʊt / (BrE)
to move backwards out of a space, especially in a vehicle or from a place

Examples

Then he backed out and swung around to the front drive.
I shuddered and backed out of the room.

Synset Examples

He backed out of the driveway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to move backwards, especially a vehicle

2. VERB

/ bæk aʊt / (AmE), / bæk aʊt / (BrE)
to move out of a space by reversing, especially backward (as with a vehicle or when leaving a room)

Examples

So I'm backing out the door, right?

Synset Examples

He backed out of the driveway
