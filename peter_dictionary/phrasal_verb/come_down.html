come down

1. VERB

/ kʌm ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / kʌm ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
(of rain, snow, etc.) to fall from the sky; to precipitate.

Examples

"Lookit that come down, willya ", said the man, scratching himself, yawning.

Synset Examples

rain, snow and sleet were falling
Vesuvius precipitated its fiery, destructive rage on Herculaneum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - to drop from a higher place to a lower one

2. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn / (BrE)
to move downward from a higher to a lower position, but not necessarily reaching the very bottom

Examples

We came down off the wall as if he had toppled all of us, and we crouched behind it.
Rank after rank of them came down the road, and the faces were all the same, and they walked in a sea of dust.
Martha Schuyler, old, slow, careful of foot, came down the great staircase, dressed in her best lace-drawn black silk, her jeweled shoe buckles held forward.
"Please come down as soon as you conveniently can ", the upright letters stalked from the broad nibbed pen, "I have an important matter to discuss with you ".
"My nephews will be coming down ", she said that evening as Angelina brought her dinner into the dining room, the whole meal on a vast linen covered tray.

Synset Examples

The temperature is going down
The barometer is falling
The curtain fell on the diva
Her hand went up and then fell again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: descend - to move or fall downward
  • Synonym: go down - to move to a lower level or position
  • Antonym: ascend - to move upward from a lower to a higher position

3. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn / (BrE)
(of an argument, issue, etc.) To be essentially concerned with or to be reduced to a single important point; to amount to.

Examples

Yet with all their skills, the appeal of Mantle and Maris in 1961 comes down to one basic: The home run.
The difference came down to this: The Southern States insisted that the United States was, in last analysis, what its name implied - a Union of States.

Synset Examples

The proposal boils down to a compromise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boil down - to reduce to the essential part or main point
  • Synonym: amount to - to be essentially equal to or result in

4. VERB

/ kʌm daʊn / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn / (BrE)
to become ill with a particular disease or sickness; to contract an illness

Examples

He was n't likely to come down with hot chills and puzzling striped fever.

Synset Examples

She fell sick last Friday, and now she is in the hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - to contract an illness
  • Antonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health after illness

/ kʌm daʊn / (AmE), / kʌm daʊn / (BrE)
to move downward from a higher to a lower position, but not necessarily all the way to the bottom

Examples

Don't make me come down there.

Synset Examples
