screw up

1. VERB

/ skruː ʌp / (AmE), / skruː ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mistake or do something badly; to spoil, ruin, or mess something up

Examples

I think your friend is screwed up.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: mess up - to make a mistake or do something badly
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or correct something that is wrong
