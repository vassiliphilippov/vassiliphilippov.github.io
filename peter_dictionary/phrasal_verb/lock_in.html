lock in

1. VERB

/ lɑk ɪn / (AmE), / lɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to put someone or something in a place and fasten the door or container so that they cannot leave or be removed

Examples

I lived to see an envelope of hers in the morning mail and to lock myself in my room in the afternoon to reread her letter for the tenth time and finally prepare an answer.

Synset Examples

The parents locked her daughter up for the weekend
She locked her jewels in the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock up - to lock someone or something inside so they cannot escape

2. VERB

/ lɑk ɪn / (AmE), / lɒk ɪn / (BrE)
to close or seal tightly so that nothing can escape or enter, especially to keep something (such as air, flavor, or moisture) inside

Examples

Be sure to press the additional clay firmly into place without locking in air bubbles.

Synset Examples

This vacuum pack locks in the flavor!
