come away

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌm əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to become detached or separated from something

Examples

When he felt the side of his head, his fingers came away covered with blood.
His hands snatched at an imaginary bucket, swooping down hard to grab it and coming away with equal snap like a ball that's been bounced hard.

Synset Examples

His retina detached and he had to be rushed into surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detach - to separate or remove something from something else
  • Antonym: stick - to become or remain attached or fixed to something

2. VERB

/ kʌm əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌm əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation with a particular feeling, impression, or condition

Examples

Dr. Conant has come away shocked and angry.

Synset Examples

She came away angry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
