get hold of

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ˈhoʊld əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ˈhəʊld ɒv / (BrE)
to take possession of something physically; to manage to obtain or acquire something, especially by reaching for it or securing it.

Examples

One should be able to get hold of the book at once.

Synset Examples

Take a cookie!
Can you take this bag, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obtain - to get or acquire something
  • Antonym: lose - to no longer have something; to fail to keep possession of

2. VERB

/ ɡɛt hoʊld ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
to take or obtain something in one's hands or possession; to physically acquire or grasp something

Examples

How did they get their hands on him?

Synset Examples

Take a cookie!
Can you take this bag, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take - to get into one's hands, possession, or control
