throw out

1. VERB

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place or group, especially suddenly or unceremoniously

Examples

Hieronymus, like Piepsam, makes his protest quite in vain, and his rejection, though not fatal, is ridiculous and humiliating; he is simply thrown out of the shop by the porter.
Mobutu promptly flung out the Russians, who have not since played any significant part on the local scene, although they have redoubled their obstructionist efforts at U. N. headquarters in New York.
To climax her Roman revels, she was thrown out of the swanky Hotel Excelsior after she had run naked through its marble halls screaming for help.

Synset Examples

He was expelled from his native country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: eject - to force someone or something to leave a place
  • Antonym: admit - to allow someone to enter a place or group

2. VERB

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to get rid of something by discarding or disposing of it, often by physically throwing it away

Examples

I opened the window and threw the radish out ".
He threw out some of Hutchins' more wildly experimental courses, raised sagging undergraduate enrollment to 2100, nearly doubled endowment to $ 139.3 million.
He threw out the hi-graders.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful or desirable
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a position or office; to remove from a job, group, or position of authority

5. VERB

/ θroʊ aʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of a court, committee, etc.) to dismiss or reject something, especially a legal case or suggestion, so it is no longer considered.

Examples
