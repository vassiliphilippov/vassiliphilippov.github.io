slip away

1. VERB

/ slɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / slɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of time) to pass gradually and often unnoticed.

Examples

It slips away, it burns and tortures me.

Synset Examples

three years elapsed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elapse - (of time) to pass or go by
  • Synonym: fly by - (of time) to seem to pass very quickly

2. VERB

/ slɪp əˈweɪ / (AmE), / slɪp əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To leave a place quietly and without being noticed, often to avoid attention.

Examples

Smarter than most gamblers, she slipped away from the casino, packed her bag and took the night train to Paris.

Synset Examples

The lecture was boring and many students slipped out when the instructor turned towards the blackboard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak out - leave a place secretly and quietly to avoid being noticed
  • Degree Lower: depart - to leave, especially a place
