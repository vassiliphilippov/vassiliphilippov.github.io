trick out

1. VERB

/ trɪk aʊt / (AmE), / trɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to decorate or dress something or someone in a fancy or elaborate way, especially to make it more attractive or eye-catching

Examples

So junior's bedroom was usually tricked out with heavy, nondescript pieces that supposedly could take the "hard knocks ", while the fine secretary was relegated to the parlor where it was for show only.

Synset Examples

She never dresses up, even when she goes to the opera
The young girls were all fancied up for the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress up - to put on formal or elaborate clothes
  • Synonym: overdress - to dress in too elaborate or formal a manner
  • Antonym: understate - to present or dress in a deliberately restrained or simple way
