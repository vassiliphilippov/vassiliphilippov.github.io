let up

1. VERB

/ lɛt ʌp / (AmE), / lɛt ʌp / (BrE)
to reduce the degree, pressure, or intensity of something, or to become less severe or intense.

Examples

I let up on the accelerator, only to gradually reach again the 60 m. p. h. which would, I hoped, overhaul Herry and the blonde, and as there were cars whose drivers apparently had something more important to catch than had I, Mrs. Major Roebuck settled down to practicing on Corporal Johnson the kittenish wiles she would need when making her duty call on Colonel and Mrs. Somebody in Sante Fe.

Synset Examples

he eased off the gas pedal and the car slowed down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ease off - to gradually reduce the force or intensity of something
  • Synonym: slacken - to make or become less active, intense, or fast
  • Antonym: intensify - to become or make more intense

2. VERB

/ lɛt ʌp / (AmE), / lɛt ʌp / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant or intense) to become less strong or to stop gradually.

Examples

A voice on the loudspeaker system announced that if the rain let up the performance would resume in ten minutes.

Synset Examples

The storm abated
The rain let up after a few hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abate - to become less strong or decrease in intensity
  • Synonym: ease off - to become less severe or intense
  • Antonym: intensify - to become stronger or more intense
