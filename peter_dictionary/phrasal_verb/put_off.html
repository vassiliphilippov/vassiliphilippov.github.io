put off

1. VERB

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
To cause someone to feel intense dislike or distaste; to make someone lose interest or enthusiasm.

Examples

It put you off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - to cause a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Antonym: attract - to cause interest or liking

2. VERB

/ pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
To delay doing something or arranging for something to happen until a later time.

Examples

To the manufacturer of the more convenient type product - the purchase of which can be switched, delayed, or put off entirely - the implications are important.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Antonym: bring forward - to arrange for something to happen sooner
