wall up

1. VERB

/ wɔl ʌp / (AmE), / wɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to enclose or block an opening or space by building a wall across it

Examples

The entrance to a church has been walled up, so that the congregation, most of which is in the western sector, cannot worship God there anymore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal up - to close something tightly so that nothing can pass through
  • Synonym: brick up - to block an opening by building a brick wall
