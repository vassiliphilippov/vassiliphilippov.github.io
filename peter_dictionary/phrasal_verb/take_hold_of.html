take hold of

1. VERB

/ teɪk hoʊld ʌv / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
to grasp or seize something or someone firmly with the hand, especially in order to control, move, or restrain it.

Examples

His hand went to her shoulder and pushed aside the knotted scarf that surmounted the striped poplin gown; then, to better purpose, he took hold of the knot and with dextrous fingers, untied it.

Synset Examples

Catch the ball!
Grab the elevator door!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to seize suddenly and roughly
  • Synonym: catch - to capture or seize, especially something moving
  • Antonym: release - to let go or set free something seized or held

2. VERB

/ teɪk hoʊld ʌv / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
To grasp or seize something firmly, often to restrain or stop its movement.

Examples

He's never been... - I said take him in! - What's The Turk paying you? - Take a **hold** **of** him.

Synset Examples

Catch the ball!
Grab the elevator door!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take or seize suddenly and roughly
  • Synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
