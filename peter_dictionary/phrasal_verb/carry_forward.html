carry forward

1. VERB

/ ˈkæri ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈkæri ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to transfer or continue something to a later time period, especially to move figures or tasks to the next stage or period

Examples

They began to meet in the evenings and carry forward various discussions they felt not fully enough covered in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carry over - to transfer or extend something to a later time or another context
