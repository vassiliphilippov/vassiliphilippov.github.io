check out

1. VERB

/ tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to examine or inspect something to determine its quality, accuracy, or condition

Examples

Motion picture cameras had been installed to film the audience, the reservation list was being checked out name by name, and a special detail was already at work in the parking lot scrutinizing automobiles for a possible lead.
Have you actually checked out (not just mentally tested) different selling approaches designed to counter the price competition problem?
Check the overnighters out.
Failing to find what was wanted, as was most likely, check out other guests, with special - but not exclusive - attention to those with rooms on the street.
To be checked out further.

Synset Examples

check the brakes
Check out the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully to assess its condition or quality
  • Synonym: examine - to look at or consider something carefully
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to look at or pay attention to something

2. VERB

/ tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to announce one's departure and settle one's bill when leaving a hotel

Examples

These included, as one, Mr. and Mrs. Anthony Payne, who had checked in a little after noon the day before, and had not checked out together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: check in - to register upon arrival at a hotel or similar accommodation

3. VERB

/ tʃɛk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɛk aʊt / (BrE)
to be verified as true or accurate; to pass inspection or scrutiny

Examples

Check him **out**. - It's really beautiful.

Synset Examples

These stories don't check!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold up - to remain valid under scrutiny or examination
