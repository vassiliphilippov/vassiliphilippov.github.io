take the field

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə fiːld / (AmE), / teɪk ðə fiːld / (BrE)
to set out for battle or begin military operations; to go to war or start a campaign

Examples

I insisted on takeing the field and prevailed - thinking that I had better die by rebel bullets than [ by ] Union quackery ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: go to war - to begin armed conflict with another group or nation
  • Antonym: lay down arms - to surrender or stop fighting
