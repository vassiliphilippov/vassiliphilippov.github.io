stretch forth

1. VERB

/ strɛtʃ fɔrθ / (AmE), / strɛtʃ fɔːθ / (BrE)
to extend or thrust something, especially a limb, outward or forward

Examples

Then Jennie closed her eyes, stretched forth her arms, and said: "Take my hand, Louise; I 'm a stranger in paradise ".

Synset Examples

He held out his hand
point a finger
extend a hand
the bee exserted its sting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extend - to cause something to reach out or continue forward
  • Antonym: pull back - to retract or withdraw something that was extended
