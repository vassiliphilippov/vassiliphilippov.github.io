make noise

1. VERB

/ meɪk nɔɪz / (AmE), / meɪk nɔɪz / (BrE)
to produce a sound or sounds, especially unintentionally or without specifying the exact type of noise

Examples

I shook him a little harder and made some noise.
He opened it a crack and in doing so made as much shuffling, coughing, and scraping noise as possible in order to drown emanations from the hen who had begun to protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: make a sound - to cause a sound to be audible
