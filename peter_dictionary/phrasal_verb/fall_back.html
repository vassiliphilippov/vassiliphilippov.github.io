fall back

1. VERB

/ fɔl bæk / (AmE), / fɔːl bæk / (BrE)
to move or drop backwards suddenly, often by losing balance or yielding under pressure

Examples

The things left unsaid they both felt deeply, and with a sigh they fell back on the well stuffed pillows.
The nice-looking young officer fell back on his heels, open-mouthed and blushing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recoil - to suddenly move back, especially from shock or force
  • Antonym: fall forward - to fall in a forward direction

2. VERB

/ fɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move or retreat to a position further back, especially after lagging behind or losing ground in movement, progress, or development

Examples

The Koreans fell back, but regrouped at the top of the hill and pinned down the cavalrymen with a screen of fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, especially from danger or difficulty
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress

3. VERB

/ fɔl ˈbæk / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move back or retreat, especially in the face of opposition or danger

Examples

They neither gained nor fell back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - to withdraw from an enemy or difficult situation
