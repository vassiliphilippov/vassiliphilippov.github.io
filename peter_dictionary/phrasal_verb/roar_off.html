roar off

1. VERB

/ rɔr ɑf / (AmE), / rɔːr ɒf / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or person) to leave or depart quickly and noisily, especially making a loud engine sound.

Examples

Now she kept herself protectively ready to laugh again and sure enough he pointed at her with his index finger and said "Toot "! once more and roared off into the fog, his foot evidently surprising him with the suddenness with which it pressed the accelerator, just as his hand did when he worked.

Synset Examples

The car roared off into the fog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoom off - depart quickly, usually making a buzzing or humming sound
  • Synonym: drive off - depart in a vehicle
  • Antonym: creep away - depart quietly and slowly
