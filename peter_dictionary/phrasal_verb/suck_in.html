suck in

1. VERB

/ sʌk ɪn / (AmE), / sʌk ɪn / (BrE)
to attract or involve someone or something by a strong or irresistible force; to draw in.

Examples

Watson ran up the ladder and stood for a second sucking in the cool air that smelled of mud and river weeds.

Synset Examples

The current boom in the economy sucked many workers in from abroad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draw in - to attract or cause to become involved
  • Synonym: pull in - to attract or draw in; to bring towards oneself

2. VERB

/ sʌk ɪn / (AmE), / sʌk ɪn / (BrE)
to draw (air, liquid, etc.) into the mouth or body with suction, as if by sucking

Examples

When he turned into the highway that led to the outskirts of the city and then rose toward home, he had to pull over to the curb and wait for a few minutes, sucking in air and squinting and blinking his eyes to clear them of tears.
Deputy coroner says it looks like he sucked in a big pile of those thick suds and strangled on' em.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhale - to breathe in air, smoke, or gas
  • Antonym: exhale - to breathe out air, smoke, or gas
