level off

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛvəl ɔf / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl ɒf / (BrE)
(of a surface, path, or progression) to become level, flat, or even after having been sloped or uneven.

Examples

The first part of the road was steep, but it leveled off after the second bend and curled gradually into the valley.
Greg's airspeed indicator was over 350 when he leveled off just above the trees.
And with passenger weight shifted forward, Hotei levels off for speed under power of a Merc 800.

Synset Examples

The ground levelled off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatten out - to become flat or flatter
