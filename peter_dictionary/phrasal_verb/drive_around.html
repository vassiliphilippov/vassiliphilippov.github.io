drive around

1. VERB

/ draɪv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / draɪv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to travel aimlessly or casually in a vehicle, especially for leisure or without a specific destination.

Examples

She 'd driven around for a while, Joyce said, then, thinking Louis Thor would have calmed down by that time, she 'd gone back to his home on Bryn Mawr Drive, parked in front, and walked toward the pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise - to drive around without a fixed destination, often for pleasure or relaxation
