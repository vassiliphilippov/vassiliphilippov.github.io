tell on

1. VERB

/ tɛl ɑn / (AmE), / tɛl ɒn / (BrE)
to inform someone in authority about another person's wrongdoing; to betray or report someone's actions, especially secretly

Examples

No one told on Ludie, not even when he slipped live grasshoppers into the mite-box.

Synset Examples

He told on his classmate who had cheated on the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitch - to secretly inform someone in authority about another person's wrongdoing
  • Synonym: inform - to communicate knowledge, especially about wrongdoing, to authority
  • Antonym: cover up - to conceal wrongdoing or incriminating information

2. VERB

/ tɛl ɑn / (AmE), / tɛl ɒn / (BrE)
to have a noticeable effect, often harmful or tiring, on someone or something

Examples

The heat and strain began to tell on the Beardens.

Synset Examples

Each step told on his tired legs
