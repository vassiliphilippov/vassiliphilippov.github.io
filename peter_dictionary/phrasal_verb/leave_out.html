leave out

1. VERB

/ liv aʊt / (AmE), / liːv aʊt / (BrE)
To fail to include something or someone; to omit, either intentionally or unintentionally.

Examples

It was left out of him at birth.

Synset Examples

How could I miss that typo?
The workers on the conveyor belt miss one out of ten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omit - to leave out or exclude
  • Synonym: exclude - to deliberately not include
  • Antonym: include - to make something part of a whole

2. VERB

/ liv aʊt / (AmE), / liːv aʊt / (BrE)
to fail to include someone or something; to omit or exclude from a group, list, activity, or consideration.

Examples

I leave out of account the question of the best interests of the children, the question of what their best interests really are.
Its drafters discussed this vital point but left it out of their Constitution.
"Have a party an' leave' em out, hon ", he suggested.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omit - to leave out or exclude, either intentionally or forgetfully
  • Antonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or list
