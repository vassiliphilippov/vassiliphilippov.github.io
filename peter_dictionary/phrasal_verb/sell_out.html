sell out

1. VERB

/ sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to sell all of one's business, property, or merchandise, often to cease operations or leave a place

Examples

General manager Pels even suggested that it might be wise to keep the Mexicans in suspense rather than accept their offers to sell out and move away, and try to have a few punished.
The easiest thing would be to sell out to Al Budd and leave the country, but there was a stubborn streak in him that would n't allow it.

2. VERB

/ sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to betray or compromise others, especially for personal advantage or gain

Examples

He sold out to Kruger's men.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betray - to deliver someone or something to an enemy by treachery
  • Antonym: stand by - to remain loyal or supportive

3. VERB

/ sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to betray someone by divulging compromising information about them, especially for personal gain.

Examples

If Luca sold out, we're in a lot of trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betray - to be disloyal or disclose information that harms someone
