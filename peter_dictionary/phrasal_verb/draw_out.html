draw out

1. VERB

/ drɔʊ aʊt / (AmE), / drɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to encourage someone to speak or express their thoughts, especially when they are reluctant or shy

Examples

She tried to find some way to draw him out, to help him.

Synset Examples

Can you draw her out--she is always so quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone; inspire with courage, spirit, or hope

2. VERB

/ drɔʊ aʊt / (AmE), / drɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to cause something to last longer than usual; to prolong in time

Examples

Modern writers, who are supposed to keep their fingers firmly upon the pulse of their subjects, insist upon drawing out this legend, prolonging its burial, when it well deserves a rest after the overexploitation of the past century.

Synset Examples

We prolonged our stay
She extended her visit by another day
The meeting was drawn out until midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolong - to make something last longer; to extend in duration
  • Antonym: shorten - to make something shorter in time or duration

3. VERB

/ drɔʊ aʊt / (AmE), / drɔː aʊt / (BrE)
to make something last longer than usual or necessary; to prolong in time

Examples

Alone in the dark with nothing but your thoughts, time can draw out like a blade.

Synset Examples

We prolonged our stay
She extended her visit by another day
The meeting was drawn out until midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolong - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: shorten - to make something shorter in duration
