break away

1. VERB

/ breɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / breɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To separate or remove a piece from a whole by breaking it off.

Examples

She could not count the times Herman had rapped on the door, just a couple of bangs that shook the whole damned closet and might, someday, break away the pipe connections from the wall.

Synset Examples

Her tooth chipped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chip off - to remove a small piece by striking or breaking
  • Synonym: break off - to separate a piece from something by breaking

2. VERB

/ ˌbreɪk əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌbreɪk əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to leave or escape suddenly from someone or something that restrains you; to become separated from a group, routine, or restriction

Examples

But her conscious need was to break away from constricting patterns of form, a need to let the experience shape itself.
But at long last came a time when I broke away from Mother and her society "chi-chi "in order to spend a cosy evening with Viola and her chaperon at her home.

Synset Examples

The horses broke from the stable
Three inmates broke jail
Nobody can break out--this prison is high security

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get free from something or someone
  • Synonym: break free - to escape or become free, especially suddenly
