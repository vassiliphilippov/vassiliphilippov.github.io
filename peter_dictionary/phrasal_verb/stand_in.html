stand in

1. VERB

/ stænd ɪn / (AmE), / stænd ɪn / (BrE)
to take the place of someone or something temporarily; to act as a substitute

Examples

This includes driers, cleaners, burr extractors, separators and piping that's located above gin stands in a complete gin.

Synset Examples

The young teacher had to substitute for the sick colleague
The skim milk substitutes for cream--we are on a strict diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to take the place of another; serve as a replacement
