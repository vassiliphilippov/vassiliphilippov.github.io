tangle with

1. VERB

/ ˈtæŋɡəl wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡl wɪð / (BrE)
to become involved in a conflict or difficulty with someone or something, often in a challenging or adversarial way.

Examples

In that case, listen to what befell another wisenheimer who tangled with our joss.
I 'm not afraid to tangle with the Republican nominee ".

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight with - to engage in physical or verbal conflict with
  • Synonym: clash with - to oppose or conflict with someone or something

2. VERB

/ ˈtæŋɡəl wɪð / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡl̩ wɪð / (BrE)
To become involved in a difficult, complicated, or troublesome situation, person, or organization.

Examples

A cop who got mixed up in the rackets and got what was coming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get involved with - become engaged in or a part of something
  • Synonym: mix up in - become entangled or involved in (often something problematic)

Etymology

From the verb 'tangle' (to twist together in a confused mass), with 'with' indicating involvement.
