come home

1. VERB

/ kʌm hoʊm / (AmE), / kʌm həʊm / (BrE)
(of a fact or realization) To become fully understood or deeply felt, especially after being obscure or unnoticed.

Examples

The miracle of democratic America comes home to one most strongly only when one has seen the endless Great Plains of the Midwest; the sky-reaching peaks of the Northwest mountains; the smoke-filled, art-filled, drama-filled life of the great cities of the East; the lush and historic charm of the South.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike home - to have the intended effect, especially emotionally; to be fully understood
  • Synonym: dawn (on someone) - (of an idea or realization) to become clear to someone

2. VERB

/ kʌm hoʊm / (AmE), / kʌm həʊm / (BrE)
(of a fact, realization, or emotion) to become fully clear or understood, especially with force or emotional impact.

Examples

I came home to vote in the presidential election because they wouldn'’t give me an absentee ballot.

Synset Examples

It dawned on him that she had betrayed him
she was penetrated with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dawn on - to become apparent or understood suddenly
