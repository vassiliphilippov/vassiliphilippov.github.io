get up

1. VERB

/ ɡɛt ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɛt ʌp / (BrE)
to rise from a sitting or lying position to a standing position

Examples

Bobby Joe and two or three of the other boys declared they had never been possum hunting, and Uncle Bill Farnworth (from Mama Albright's side of the family) said he would just get up from there and take them, right then.

She finally regained her balance and got up in the saddle.
Then, they had to get up and be General Burnside.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand up - to rise from a sitting position to stand on one's feet
  • Antonym: sit down - to move from a standing position into a sitting position
