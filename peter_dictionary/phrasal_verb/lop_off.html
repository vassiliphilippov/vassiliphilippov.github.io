lop off

1. VERB

/ lɑp ɑf / (AmE), / lɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to remove something by cutting it off, especially using a quick or heavy cut

Examples

Instead of quelling the dissension, as many captains of the era would have done (Sir Francis Drake lopped a man's head off under similar circumstances), Hudson decided to be reasonable.

Synset Examples

cut off the ear
lop off the dead branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut off - to remove something by cutting

Etymology

From the verb 'lop' (to cut off, especially branches), likely of Scandinavian origin.
