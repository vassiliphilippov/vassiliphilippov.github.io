persist in

1. VERB

/ pərˈsɪst ɪn / (AmE), / pəˈsɪst ɪn / (BrE)
to continue firmly or obstinately in a course of action or an opinion, despite difficulty or opposition

Examples

He tried defiance and openly flaunted his devotion to his half sister, but he soon saw, as did she, that this course if persisted in would involve them in a common ruin.

Synset Examples

We continued our research into the cause of the illness
The landlord persists in asking us to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persevere - to continue trying to do or achieve something despite difficulties
  • Antonym: give up - to stop trying to do something; to cease making an effort

Etymology

From Latin 'persistere' (to stand firm, to persevere); 'in' is a preposition of Latin origin.

2. VERB

/ pərˈsɪst ɪn / (AmE), / pəˈsɪst ɪn / (BrE)
to continue firmly or obstinately in an action or belief despite difficulty or opposition

Examples

The Sisters kept at him.

Synset Examples

We continued our research into the cause of the illness
The landlord persists in asking us to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persevere - to persist in a course of action despite difficulties
  • Antonym: give up - to cease making an effort; stop trying

Etymology

From Latin 'persistere' (to continue steadfastly), from 'per-' (through) + 'sistere' (to stand).
