drag a bunt

1. VERB

/ dræɡ ə bʌnt / (AmE), / dræɡ ə bʌnt / (BrE)
In baseball, to bunt the ball while running toward first base, making the ball travel a short distance in order to increase the chance of reaching base safely.

Examples

Both are good bunters: Maris once beat out eighteen of nineteen in the minor leagues; Mantle is a master at dragging a bunt toward first base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunt - (baseball) to gently tap the ball without swinging fully
