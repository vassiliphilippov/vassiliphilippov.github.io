butt against

1. VERB

/ bʌt əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / bʌt əˈɡenst / (BrE)
to lie directly next to something or touch it at the edge; to adjoin or be in physical contact along a boundary

Examples

The first two pieces butt against the inner member of the keelson and are glued and screwed to the brace between the first two frames.
The second two pieces lap over the inner member of the keelson and butt against the outer member.

Synset Examples

Canada adjoins the U.S.
England marches with Scotland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjoin - to be next to or joined with something

Etymology

From 'butt' meaning 'to strike against or rest against', and 'against' meaning 'in contact with'.
