break in

1. VERB

/ breɪk ɪn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a building or property illegally, especially by force, often in order to steal something

Examples

During the following week, six tons of hay belonging to one rancher were burned; some buildings, farm tools, two horses, plows, and hay owned by Bonito Lavato, a friendly interpreter for the company, and Pedro Chavez' hay were stolen or destroyed; and a store was broken into and robbed.
A 24 - year-old Atlanta man was arrested Sunday after breaking into the home of relatives in search of his wife, hitting his uncle with a rock and assaulting two police officers who tried to subdue him, police said.
I feel that few burglars would be prone to break and enter into someone's apartment if they were met with a good hardy growl that a dog would provide.

Synset Examples

Someone broke in while I was on vacation
They broke into my car and stole my radio!
who broke into my account last night?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgle - to enter a building illegally with intent to commit a crime
  • Synonym: break into - to enter a place forcibly or illegally
  • Antonym: break out - to escape from a place (especially prison)

2. VERB

/ breɪk ɪn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a building, car, or other property illegally, especially by force and usually with the intent to steal or commit another crime

Examples

He could not break in on an armed man.
Patrolman Robert J. Nunes, who investigated, said the thieves broke in through the back door.

Synset Examples

Someone broke in while I was on vacation
They broke into my car and stole my radio!
who broke into my account last night?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgle - to enter a building illegally and steal things
  • Synonym: break into - to enter a place, especially a building, by force, usually in order to steal something

3. VERB

/ breɪk ɪn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to interrupt a conversation or discussion, usually suddenly or unexpectedly

Examples

He was about to make a gas check on his flight when Todman's voice broke in: "Sweeneys!
"That would be worse than useless ", Alexander broke in.

Synset Examples

her husband always chimes in, even when he is not involved in the conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chime in - to join in or interrupt a conversation, especially to add a comment
  • Synonym: cut in - to interrupt someone who is speaking

4. VERB

/ breɪk ɪn / (AmE), / breɪk ɪn / (BrE)
To begin or enter upon a particular activity, enterprise, or role, especially for the first time.

Examples

As roommates, teammates, and home-run mates, Mantle, 29, who broke in with the Yankees ten years ago, and Maris, 26, who came to the Yankees from Kansas City two years ago, have strikingly similar backgrounds.
This colt arrived at the Raceway early last November, and immediately was put into harness and line-driven for a few days, and then put to cart and broken in very nicely, knowing nothing but trot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enter - to come or go into a place, situation, or activity
  • Synonym: start out - to begin a course, career, or journey

to enter a building, car, or other property illegally and often by force, especially with the intention of stealing or committing a crime

Examples
