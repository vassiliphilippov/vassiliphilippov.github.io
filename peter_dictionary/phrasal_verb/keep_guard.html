keep guard

1. VERB

/ kip ɡɑrd / (AmE), / kiːp ɡɑːd / (BrE)
to watch over someone or something in order to protect them from harm or danger

Examples

They are accused of whisking secrets out of naval strongrooms over which they kept guard.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand guard - to remain in a place to protect someone or something
  • Synonym: guard - to watch over in order to protect or control
