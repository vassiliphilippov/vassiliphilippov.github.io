whistle away

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪsəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
To dismiss, rid oneself of, or banish something (such as a worry, fear, or negative thought) easily or lightly, as if by whistling.

Examples

His bright, daylight mind would whistle away such images; they would not dare to face his scoffing.

Synset Examples

Her optimism whistled away these worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banish - to send something away, especially from one's presence or mind
  • Synonym: dispel - to drive away, especially an idea or feeling
