walk off

1. VERB

/ wɑk ˈɔf / (AmE), / wɔːk ˈɒf / (BrE)
to take something without permission; to steal something, often in a casual or unnoticed way

Examples

It had come as no great surprise to Matson that the hot water in the showers did n't work, that Loren Severe had thrown up all over the stairs, or that some thieving bastard of a cop had walked off with his cigarettes.

Synset Examples

he walked off with my wife!
The thief walked off with my gold watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
  • Antonym: give back - to return something to its rightful owner
