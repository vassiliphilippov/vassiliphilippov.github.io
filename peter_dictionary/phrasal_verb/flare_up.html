flare up

1. VERB

/ flɛr ʌp / (AmE), / flɛə ʌp / (BrE)
to begin or become much more intense or active suddenly, often after a period of calm; to erupt suddenly (used of emotions, conflict, illness, etc.)

Examples

It has flared up periodically on the front pages of newspapers in communities divided over birth prevention regulations in municipal hospitals and health and family welfare agencies.

Synset Examples

Unrest erupted in the country
Tempers flared at the meeting
The crowd irrupted into a burst of patriotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erupt - to break out or burst forth suddenly and dramatically
  • Antonym: die down - to become less intense or active after a period of high activity

2. VERB

/ flɛr ʌp / (AmE), / fleə ʌp / (BrE)
(of a fire or flame) to ignite or burn suddenly and intensely, especially after having died down.

Examples

Do keep the grill high enough above the fire so that when fat from meat drips down and flares up, flames cannot reach the meat.

Synset Examples

the fire flared up and died down once again

Related Terms

  • Antonym: die down - (of a fire or emotion) to become less strong or intense
