add up

1. VERB

/ æd ʌp / (AmE), / æd ʌp / (BrE)
to amount to a particular total, result, or outcome; to develop into something

Examples

We have 2500 such projects, and they add up to a lot more than just roads and wells and schools.
Even so, it adds up to impossible odds, except that the question arises, On whose side would the Mainland Chinese army fight?
What does it all add up to?
"How often do they add up to headlines?

Synset Examples

This idea will never amount to anything
nothing came of his grandiose plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amount to - to total, be equivalent to, or result in

2. VERB

/ æd ʌp / (AmE), / æd ʌp / (BrE)
to calculate the total of two or more numbers or amounts; to determine the sum of several items or figures

Examples

So each reading can be given a weight and each reading a score by adding up these weights.
All these evaluations are then totted up and tabulated, by adding up the Hits and Significants, with the weight placed on those in the sitter's own reading.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum up - to find the total by adding numbers; to summarize collectively
  • Antonym: subtract - to take one number or amount away from another
