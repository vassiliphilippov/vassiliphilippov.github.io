wear out

1. VERB

/ wɛr aʊt / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone very tired or exhausted, especially by overuse or great effort

Examples

Said you wore yourself out, cleaning all these empty rooms ".

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone very tired
  • Antonym: invigorate - to make someone feel more lively or energetic

2. VERB

/ wɛr aʊt / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt / (BrE)
(of an object or material) to become unusable or damaged through long or excessive use, strain, or age.

Examples

The simple mechanical strain of overweight, says New York's Dr. Norman Jolliffe, can overburden and damage the heart "for much the same reason that a Chevrolet engine in a Cadillac body would wear out sooner than if it were in a body for which it was built ".

Synset Examples

The lawn mower finally broke
The gears wore out
The old chair finally fell apart completely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break down - stop functioning due to malfunction or age
  • Synonym: fall apart - to disintegrate or break into pieces

3. VERB

/ wɛr aʊt / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt / (BrE)
(of an object or material) to become unusable or damaged because of being used too much or for too long.

Examples

Just dropping the baby's bottle and breaking it became a catastrophe, and Stuart wore out his shoes so fast that he was termed a major disaster.

Synset Examples

The constant friction wore out the cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wear down - to gradually damage or erode something, often making it thinner or weaker
