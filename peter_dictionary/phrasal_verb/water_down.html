water down

1. VERB

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə daʊn / (BrE)
To make something less strong, potent, or effective, often by adding water or by reducing its intensity or concentration.

Examples

Daniel personally led the fight for the measure, which he had watered down considerably since its rejection by two previous Legislatures, in a public hearing before the House Committee on Revenue and Taxation.

Synset Examples

water down the mixture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilute - make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent
  • Synonym: weaken - make or become less strong
  • Antonym: strengthen - make or become stronger
