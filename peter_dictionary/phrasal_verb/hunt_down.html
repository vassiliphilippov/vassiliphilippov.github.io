hunt down

1. VERB

/ hʌnt daʊn / (AmE), / hʌnt daʊn / (BrE)
to pursue relentlessly in order to capture or kill, especially a person or animal

Examples

Soon they will begin to hunt down the traitors they are assured are in our midst.
If we fail to develop the means to hunt down and destroy the enemy's military force with extreme care and precision, and if war comes in spite of our most ardent desires for peace, our choice of alternatives will be truly frightening.

Synset Examples

Goering often hunted wild boars in Poland
The dogs are running deer
The Duke hunted in these woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursue - to follow in order to catch or overtake
  • Synonym: track down - to search for and find after difficulty
  • Antonym: let go - to cease pursuit or let escape
