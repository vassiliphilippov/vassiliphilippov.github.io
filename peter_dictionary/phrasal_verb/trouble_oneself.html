trouble oneself

1. VERB

/ ˈtrʌbəl wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈtrʌb(ə)l wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to make the effort to do something, especially when it is inconvenient or unnecessary

Examples

They never troubled themselves about us while we were playing, because the fence formed such a definite boundary and "Do n't go outside the gate "was a command so impossible of misinterpretation.

Synset Examples

He did not trouble to call his mother on her birthday
Don't bother, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - to make the effort to do something; to take trouble
