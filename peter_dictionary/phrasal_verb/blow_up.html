blow up

1. VERB

/ bloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / bləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to cause something to burst violently and break apart with a sudden release of energy; to explode something

Examples

Only one of the flight scored a direct hit and the rest blew up jungle.
Just like the balloon would go up and you could sit all day and wish it would spring a leak or blow to hell up and burn and nothing like that would happen.
If anyone thought of the John Harvey, it was to observe that she was straddled by a pair of ships heavily laden with high explosive and if they were hit the John Harvey would likely be blown up with her own ammo and whatever else it was that she carried.
Ten years ago they blew up some of our ditches.

Synset Examples

We exploded the nuclear bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explode - to burst or cause to burst violently as a result of rapid combustion or decompression
  • Antonym: implode - to collapse inward violently

2. VERB

/ bloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / bləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to enlarge something, especially a photograph or image, so that it appears bigger

Examples

It seemed almost a magnification of the Galaxy itself, of the Milky Way, blown up by some master photographer.

Synset Examples

blow up an image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlarge - to make or become bigger; to increase in size

3. VERB

/ bloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / bləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
To cause something to burst violently with an explosion; to detonate.

Examples

You open this fucking bar and I'’ll blow your head **in**!

Synset Examples

We exploded the nuclear bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explode - to burst or cause to burst with force and noise
  • Antonym: defuse - to remove the triggering device from (a bomb or explosive)
