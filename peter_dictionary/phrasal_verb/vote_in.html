vote in

1. VERB

/ voʊt ɪn / (AmE), / vəʊt ɪn / (BrE)
to elect someone to a position or office through a voting process

Examples

We can vote in the UN against South African apartheid or Portuguese rule in Angola, but we cannot even introduce a motion on the Berlin Wall - much less, give the simple order to push the Wall down.

Synset Examples

They voted in Clinton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elect - choose someone for office by voting
