short-change

1. VERB

/ ʃɔrt ˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to give someone less money than they are owed, especially by mistake or in order to cheat; or to treat someone unfairly by giving or doing less than they deserve

Examples

We find too many sales and marketing executives so burdened with detail that they are short-changing planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception

Etymology

From 'short' (lacking) and 'change' (money returned after a purchase); originated in US English in the 19th century.
