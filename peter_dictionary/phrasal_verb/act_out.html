act out

1. VERB

/ ækt aʊt / (AmE), / ækt aʊt / (BrE)
to perform or represent (something, such as a scene or event) as if in a play; to express or demonstrate through actions rather than words.

Examples

To make it clearer he shifted to acting out, but with no change of manner, the killing of Rose Mallory.

Synset Examples

She reenacted what had happened earlier that day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reenact - to act out (a past event) again, especially on stage or for demonstration

2. VERB

/ ækt aʊt / (AmE), / ækt aʊt / (BrE)
to represent a particular incident, emotion, or role by physical action or performance, especially as part of a play or demonstration

Examples

He made a point of frowning, of acting out the part of the fond father confessor.

Synset Examples

She could act neurotic anxiety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enact - to perform or represent a story or event on stage
