ask for trouble

1. VERB

/ æsk fər ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ɑːsk fə ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is likely to cause problems or difficulties for oneself

Examples

I didn't want to get into trouble.

Synset Examples

He is asking for trouble with his behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bring trouble on oneself - to cause problems for oneself through one's own actions
  • Synonym: court disaster - to act in a way that makes disaster likely
