hold off

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ɔf / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf / (BrE)
To prevent someone or something from succeeding or advancing, especially by resisting or defending effectively until the threat is neutralized or time runs out.

Examples

Then Kerr, a graduate student from Illinois, moved past him on a straightaway and held off Mills's challenge on the final turn.
The Broncs did hit two quick strikes in the final period against the Texans, but Dallas had enough of a lead to hold them off.
The same can be said about the half-hearted Cuban invasion mounted by the administration last April, which, we trust, is not symptomatic of the methods to be invoked in holding off the felonious Khrushchev.

Synset Examples

Dallas had enough of a lead to hold the Broncos off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fend off - to defend oneself against, especially physically; synonym in the sense of resisting attack or challenge
  • Synonym: ward off - to prevent or repel something undesirable
