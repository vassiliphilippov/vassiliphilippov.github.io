give way

1. VERB

/ ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
to move aside or yield in order to make space for someone or something; to allow or be replaced by something else.

Examples

2. VERB

/ ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
to yield or stop resisting, especially under pressure or force

Examples

It was not exactly panic they gave way to, but they could not just sit there.

Synset Examples

The door yielded to repeated blows with a battering ram

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yield - to give in or surrender in the face of force or pressure
  • Antonym: resist - to refuse to give way or surrender

/ ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
