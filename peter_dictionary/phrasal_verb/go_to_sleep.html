go to sleep

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ tə sliːp / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə sliːp / (BrE)
to begin sleeping; to fall asleep

Examples

"Go to sleep ", he said.
He found that if he was tired enough at night, he went to sleep simply because he was too exhausted to stay awake.
I gave you a drink and then you went to sleep ".

Synset Examples

I usually turn in at midnight
He goes to bed at the crack of dawn

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wake up - to stop sleeping and become awake
  • Synonym: fall asleep - to start sleeping
