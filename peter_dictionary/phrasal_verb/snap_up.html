snap up

1. VERB

/ snæp ʌp / (AmE), / snæp ʌp / (BrE)
To obtain or take something quickly and eagerly, especially before anyone else can get it.

Examples

See, he's already snapping it up, the dumb jerk * * h Coughlin grinned, feeling supremely on top of things * * h He watched the snow once again.

Synset Examples

I snapped up all the good buys during the garage sale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take or seize something suddenly or quickly
  • Synonym: snatch - to quickly seize something in a forceful or sudden way
