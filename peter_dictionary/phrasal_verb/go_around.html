go around

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of news, stories, rumors, etc.) to circulate or become widely known by being passed from person to person.

Examples

There was a question and answer gag that went around at that time: Q. "Who 'll carry the Forty-second and Forty-third wards "?

Synset Examples

the rumor spread
the story went around in the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to become widely distributed or disseminated

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of something) to be sufficient for everyone in a group; to be enough to be shared or distributed among all concerned.

Examples

"They would n't have sold me in the first place if there 'd been food enough to go around ".

Synset Examples

There's not enough to go around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffice - to be enough or adequate
  • Antonym: fall short - to not be enough or to be insufficient

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of an object) to turn continuously in a circular direction around a central point or axis.

Examples

And what makes my head go round and round ♪

Synset Examples

The Earth revolves around the Sun
The lamb roast rotates on a spit over the fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolve - to move in a circle on a central axis
  • Synonym: rotate - to turn around a central point or axis
