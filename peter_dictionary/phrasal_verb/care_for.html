care for

1. VERB

/ kɛr fɔr / (AmE), / keə fə / (BrE)
to like or be fond of someone or something; to have a preference for

Examples

Skolman was n't the only one who did n't care for Andy's scheme.
On the surface, this seems a sound approach to Christian mission: members of the congregation show by their friendly attitudes that they care for new people; the new people respond in kind by joining the church.
John Vernon had had all the patronage he cared for - he had prospered, but he could not retire from horsedom.
Letch had made it abundantly clear that he did not care for the company of my own precious daughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - to find agreeable, enjoyable, or satisfactory
  • Antonym: dislike - to not like; to have a distaste for

2. VERB

/ ˈkɛr fɔr / (AmE), / ˈkeə fə / (BrE)
to look after or be responsible for (someone or something); to manage or attend to the needs of.

Examples

The U. S. Forest Service cares for hundreds of thousands of campers in its 149 National Forests and is increasing its facilities steadily.
Never rebuilt, the bridge was strengthened in 1938 by two extra piers, a concrete floor, and a walk-way along the upper side in order to care for modern traffic.
Mrs. Clark, as house manager, planned the menus and cared for the ordering.

Synset Examples

I can deal with this crew of workers
This blender can't handle nuts
She managed her parents' affairs after they got too old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look after - to take care of someone or something
  • Synonym: manage - to be responsible for controlling or running something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

3. VERB

/ kɛr fɔr / (AmE), / keə fə / (BrE)
to feel affection or fondness for someone or something

Examples

You fly around the world with men who don'’t care for you and use you like a whore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - to find someone or something pleasant or enjoyable
  • Degree Higher: love - to have deep affection for someone or something
  • Antonym: dislike - to not like someone or something
