come back

1. VERB

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
To achieve equality in the score after being behind, especially in a sports competition.

Examples

Jim Landis' 380 - foot home run over left in the first inning gave the Sox a 1 - 0 lead, but Harmon Killebrew came back in the bottom of the first with his second homer in two days with the walking Bob Allison aboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rally - to recover during a contest to close the gap or even the score

2. VERB

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
to return to a previous place, condition, or topic, or to remember something from earlier

Examples

Bob Carroll may not bear quite as close a physical resemblance to LaGuardia as Tom Bosley does, but I was amazed at the way he became more and more Fiorello as the evening progressed, until one had to catch one's self up and remember that this was n't really LaGuardia come back among us again.
The thought came back, the one nagging at him these past four days.

Synset Examples

This harks back to a previous remark of his

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revert - to return to a previous state or condition
  • Synonym: hark back - to recall or refer back to something earlier

3. VERB

/ kʌm ˈbæk / (AmE), / kʌm ˈbæk / (BrE)
to return to a previous state or condition, such as health, consciousness, or strength, after being absent or diminished

Examples

Soon came back to his senses.
"I soon came back to my senses ", he said, aloud, to the young blizzard, proudly, drawing himself up, as if making a report to some important superior.
Of course she was n't herself right now, but as her strength came back her spirits did n't seem to rise with it.

Synset Examples

Her old vigor returned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
  • Antonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially after improvement

4. VERB

/ kʌm bæk / (AmE), / kʌm bæk / (BrE)
to respond quickly or sharply to a remark; to reply, often in a witty or critical way

Examples

Then the Communese reply came back from many mouthpieces with striking consistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retort - to reply quickly, especially in a sharp or witty way
  • Synonym: answer - to reply to a question or remark

5. VERB

/ kʌm ˈbæk / (AmE), / kʌm ˈbæk / (BrE)
to return to a previous good condition, position, or state after a decline; to be restored

Examples

You fill the square with people, and businesses will come back.

Synset Examples

Her old vigor returned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength; regain
  • Synonym: return - to come or go back to a previous place, condition, or activity
  • Antonym: decline - to become worse in condition or quality (opposite sense)
