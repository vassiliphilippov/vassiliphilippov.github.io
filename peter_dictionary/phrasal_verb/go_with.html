go with

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð / (BrE)
(of two things) to occur together or be found together; to accompany each other or be regularly associated.

Examples

He dropped his earlier and delightful hamming, which is about the only way to handle the old war horse called "Charley's Aunt ", and let himself go with as an appealing an "Amy "as anybody could ask.

Synset Examples

The word 'hot' tends to cooccur with 'cold'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accompany - to go somewhere with someone or to exist or happen together
  • Synonym: coexist - to exist at the same time or in the same place

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð / (BrE)
to be present together with, or found in association with, a particular event, person, or entity

Examples

Three requirements go with a blockade: It must be proclaimed; the blockading force must be powerful enough to enforce it; and it must be enforced without discrimination.
