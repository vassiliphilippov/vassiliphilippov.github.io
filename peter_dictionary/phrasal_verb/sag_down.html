sag down

1. VERB

/ sæɡ daʊn / (AmE), / sæɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to sink or droop downward due to weight or lack of support; to cause to sag lower

Examples

"Now ", said Arlene, eventually, making them both sit in formation on a big root of a live oak, the sort of root that divided itself and made their bottoms sag down and feel comfortable.

Synset Examples

The children sagged their bottoms down even more comfortably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sag - to sink, droop or bend downward under weight or pressure
