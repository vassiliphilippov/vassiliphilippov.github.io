sort out

1. VERB

/ sɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or organize things or people into classes or categories; to separate or classify according to specific criteria

Examples

This meant sorting out press clippings and the like.
The problem in the policy officer's mind thus begins to take shape as a galaxy of utterly complicated factors - political, military, economic, financial, legal, legislative, procedural, administrative - to be sorted out and handled within a political system which moves by consent in relation to an external environment which cannot be under control.
As I have been intimating, in the schizophrenic - and perhaps also in the dreams of the neurotic; this is a question which I have no wish to take up - condensation is a phenomenon in which one finds not a condensed expression of various feelings and ideas which are, at an unconscious level, well sorted out, but rather a condensed expression of feelings and ideas which, even in the unconscious, have yet to become well differentiated from one another.

Synset Examples

How would you classify these pottery shards--are they prehistoric?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classify - to arrange or assign to particular categories or classes
  • Synonym: categorize - to place in a particular class or group

2. VERB

/ sɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
to deal with or resolve a problem, difficulty, or confusion; to clarify a situation

Examples

Sort things out, damn it.
Sort out the next move.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear up - make free from confusion or doubt; clarify
  • Antonym: confuse - to make unclear or difficult to understand

3. VERB

/ sɔrt aʊt / (AmE), / sɔːt aʊt / (BrE)
to deal with or clear up confusion, uncertainty, or problems; to resolve something that is unclear or disorganized

Examples

I will sort everything **out**.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarify - to make (something) clearer or easier to understand
  • Synonym: clear up - to make clear or understandable; to resolve a confusion
