fly open

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
(of a door, window, etc.) to open suddenly and quickly, often unexpectedly.

Examples

At that moment the bathroom door flew open and Eugene came out, with his face lathered for shaving, and strode down the hall, tying the sash of his dressing gown as he went.

Synset Examples

the doors flew open in the strong gust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst open - (of something) to break open or open suddenly and violently
