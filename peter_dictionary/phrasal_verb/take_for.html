take for

1. VERB

/ teɪk fər / (AmE), / teɪk fə / (BrE)
to regard or assume someone or something to be a particular kind of person or thing, often mistakenly

Examples

People who take us for suckers are like the Westerner who had on exhibit his superior marksmanship in the form of a number of bull's-eye achievements.

Synset Examples

take for granted
view as important
hold these truths to be self-evident
I hold him personally responsible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake for - to wrongly identify a person or thing as someone or something else

2. VERB

/ teɪk fɔɹ / (AmE), / teɪk fə / (BrE)
to regard or consider (someone or something) as being a particular type; to judge or assume to be.

Examples

I could see why some of the boys took him for snobby.

Synset Examples

take for granted
view as important
hold these truths to be self-evident
I hold him personally responsible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deem - to regard or consider in a specified way
  • Synonym: regard as - to consider or label someone or something as a particular kind
