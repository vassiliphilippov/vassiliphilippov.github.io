put back

1. VERB

/ pʊt bæk / (AmE), / pʊt bæk / (BrE)
to return something to its original place or position

Examples

Killpath leaned forward; his foot slipped off the chair and he put it back again, frowning now.
And put Cate's gun back ".

Synset Examples

replace the book on the shelf after you have finished reading it
please put the clean dishes back in the cabinet when you have washed them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replace - to put something back in its former place; to restore
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away from a place
