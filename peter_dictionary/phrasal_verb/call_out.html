call out

1. VERB

/ kɔl aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to shout or speak loudly, especially in order to attract someone's attention or to announce something

Examples

John recognized Ablard Corne and called out a greeting.
The concierge called out to them as they were passing through the foyer.
He looked around the room and then called out:
He would sit inside the coffee shop and pound a gloved fist upon the table and a girl would hear him and come running, bowing with her running, calling out in her bowing, "At your service ".
The day-watch platoon commander, Lt. Rinker, was calling out the beat assignments, but Matson could n't make the names mean anything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly

2. VERB

/ kɔl aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
to say something loudly or shout, often in order to attract attention or express emotion such as surprise, fear, or joy

Examples

She did not call out.
"Yes ", Owen called out.

Synset Examples

`I won!' he exclaimed
`Help!' she cried
`I'm here,' the mother shouted when she saw her child looking lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Synonym: cry out - to make a loud sound or speak loudly, especially when in pain or distress
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very quietly so that only someone near can hear you
