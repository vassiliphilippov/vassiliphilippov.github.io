single out

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl aʊt / (BrE)
to choose or distinguish one person or thing from a group for special attention, treatment, or consideration

Examples

The ten or more dangerous parties singled out for prosecution were still at large, and Pels realized that if these men entrenched themselves in their adobe houses, defending themselves through loopholes, it would be most difficult to capture them.

Synset Examples

She was singled out for her outstanding performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to choose someone or something from a group
  • Antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl aʊt / (BrE)
to choose or treat (someone or something) as different or special from others, often in a way that is unfair

Examples

"Why single me out on this permit deal "?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminate - to treat differently, especially on the grounds of race, sex, or age

Etymology

From 'single' meaning 'one, separate' and 'out' as an intensifier; in use since the late 19th century.
