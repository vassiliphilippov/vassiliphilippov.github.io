mess up

1. VERB

/ mɛs ʌp / (AmE), / mɛs ʌp / (BrE)
To spoil something or make a mistake that causes things to go wrong; to ruin or botch an activity, task, or situation.

Examples

"You are young, intelligent, have a whole lifetime before you to make something worth while of yourself, but you mess it up with whiskey, indifference, self-destructive attitudes.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: screw up - to make a serious mistake or do something badly (informal)
