listen in

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪsən ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪsən ɪn / (BrE)
To listen to a conversation or communication without participating, typically in a secretive or unobtrusive way.

Examples

Wisman, below, would listen in and act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eavesdrop - to secretly listen to a private conversation
