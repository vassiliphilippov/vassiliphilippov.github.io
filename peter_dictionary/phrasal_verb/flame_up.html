flame up

1. VERB

/ fleɪm ʌp / (AmE), / fleɪm ʌp / (BrE)
to burn or blaze brightly, often suddenly or with increased intensity

Examples

The gas flamed up two inches high.
Their autumn tints are all fairly low keyed compared with the fiery stabs of crimson, gold, purple, bronze, blue and vermilion that flame up in North America.

Synset Examples

Every star seemed to flare with new intensity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flare up - to suddenly burn or increase in brightness
