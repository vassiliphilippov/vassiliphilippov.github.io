cast down

1. VERB

/ kæst daʊn / (AmE), / kɑːst daʊn / (BrE)
To make someone feel sad or disheartened; to lower someone's spirits.

Examples

I'll pull you out of that one-bunk Hilton and cast you **down** with the Sodomites.

Synset Examples

These news depressed her
The bad state of her child's health demoralizes her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depress - to make someone feel sad or lacking in hope
  • Antonym: cheer up - to make happier or encourage
