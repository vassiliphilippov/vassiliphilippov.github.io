creep up

1. VERB

/ krip ʌp / (AmE), / kriːp ʌp / (BrE)
to approach or advance gradually and often unnoticed, sometimes in a stealthy or subtle way

Examples

Sometimes a burst of aggressiveness will sweep over a man - or his wife - because he or she feels age creeping up.

Synset Examples

Age creeps up on you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak up - to approach someone quietly and secretly, especially so as not to be noticed
