fall short

1. VERB

/ fɔl ʃɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːl ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to fail to reach the desired level, standard, or expectation; to not be sufficient or adequate

Examples

I think you fell short of the real truth in the matter: That the move is working out through the fine cooperation of the staff and all the people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meet expectations - to satisfy or fulfill what is hoped for or required
  • Synonym: come up short - to fail to achieve a goal or requirement
